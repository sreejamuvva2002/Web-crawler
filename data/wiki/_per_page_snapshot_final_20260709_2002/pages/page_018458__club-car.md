# Club Car®

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-12-23

**Source:** [Club Car Names Scanlon New CEO as Wagner Retires in 2025 | Intermountain Golf Cars](https://intermountaingolfcars.com/club-car-names-scanlon-new-ceo-as-wagner-retires-in-2025/)  ·  `page_018458`

## Facts

- Club Car is a global leader in golf cars, low-speed utility vehicles, and personal transportation.
- The company announced in March 2025 that President and CEO Mark Wagner would retire effective August 1, 2025, after 29 years with the company, including the last 8 as its top executive.
- In a letter to employees, Wagner reflected on the company’s achievements, stating: “We have achieved remarkable milestones, navigated historic challenges, and built a culture – a strong Black & Gold family.”
- Wagner emphasized the importance of a smooth transition amid ongoing product launches and growth initiatives.
- On June 9, 2025, Club Car named Craig Scanlon as the new President and Chief Executive Officer.
- Scanlon brings over two decades of executive leadership experience in powersports, automotive aftermarket, and industrial sectors.
- Scanlon began his career in 1998 selling Club Car Carryall vehicles at Ingersoll Rand, which was Club Car’s former parent company.
- Scanlon later served 18 years at Polaris Industries, rising to the position of Chief Marketing Officer.
- Most recently, Scanlon served as CEO of K&N Engineering before joining Club Car.
- Jared Hutchins, Principal at Platinum Equity (Club Car’s owner since 2021), praised Scanlon: “Craig is a proven leader with an impressive combination of operational expertise and deep industry experience. His success driving growth and innovation makes the ideal choice to lead the next chapter at Club Car.”
- Scanlon expressed enthusiasm about returning to the company, stating: “I’m honored to return to Club Car at such a pivotal time. The usage of low-speed vehicles is evolving, and I look forward to leveraging Club Car’s rich history and brand while embracing innovation that serves our channel partners and customers.”
- Intermountain Golf Cars has represented Club Car vehicles since October 1985, making it an important partner for 40 of the company's more than 65 years in business.
- Intermountain Golf Cars is a family-owned dealership with locations across Utah, Idaho, Nevada, and Arizona that provides expert sales, service, and support to golf enthusiasts, businesses, and communities throughout the Intermountain West.
- Club Car is headquartered in Augusta, Georgia.
- Club Car continues to lead the industry in golf, utility, and personal transportation vehicles.
