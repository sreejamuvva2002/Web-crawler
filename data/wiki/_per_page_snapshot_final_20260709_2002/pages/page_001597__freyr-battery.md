# Freyr Battery

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-02-07

**Source:** [Freyr Battery Factory in Georgia Bites the Dust - CleanTechnica](https://cleantechnica.com/2025/02/07/freyr-battery-factory-in-georgia-bites-the-dust/)  ·  `page_001597`

## Facts

- Freyr is based in Norway.
- Freyr was one of the first companies to jump into the US market with big battery plans due to the Inflation Reduction Act passed by Democrats in 2022.
- The author interviewed Freyr’s CEO, Tom Jensen, about the plans and the Inflation Reduction Act.
- Freyr announced that it is pulling the plug on a giant battery factory it had planned to build in Georgia.
- The canceled project was a $2.6 billion factory named “Giga America” (or prematurely named Giga America).
- The plant cancellation occurred right after Trump took office and said he was going to pull the plug on all of the renewable energy and electric vehicle policies Biden had a hand in.
- Jason Pearce, Freyr’s senior vice president of business development, stated that climbing interest rates, falling battery prices, and a change in leadership at the company all contributed to the decision.
- The Newnan Times-Herald reported on Jason Pearce's statement regarding the cancellation reasons.
- Higher interest rates were used to get inflation under control following factors related to the COVID-19 pandemic and the Russian invasion of Ukraine.
- Falling battery prices have dropped significantly in the past few years, making profitability an extra difficult challenge for battery companies trying to get a factory off the ground and profitable in the United States.
- Freyr was planning to produce batteries for stationary storage battery projects, not EV batteries, at this facility.
- The company letter making the announcement came on January 21, basically right after Trump took office.
- Freyr is going to return all grants and incentives linked to the project that the company received from Georgia.
- While the “Giga America” battery project is canceled, Freyr is now moving forward with a solar panel production facility in Texas.
- The Texas solar panel facility is nearly complete and Freyr has just acquired it.
- The solar panel factory will provide “a fast path to cash” for the company.
- The company prefers generating cash from the solar facility over burning cash for years on a battery factory that might never break even.
- Freyr reported a net loss of $27.5 million in the 3rd quarter of 2024.
- Freyr reported a net loss of $83 million through the first three quarters of 2024.
- Freyr's cash reserves (with no debt) were down to $184.1 million on September 30, 2024, from $275.7 million on September 30, 2023.
