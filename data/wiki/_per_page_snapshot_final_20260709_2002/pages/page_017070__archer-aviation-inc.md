# Archer Aviation Inc.

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-12

**Source:** [Press Release: Archer completes construction of manufacturing facilityRunway Girl](https://runwaygirlnetwork.com/2024/12/archer-high-volume-manufacturing-facility/)  ·  `page_017070`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation Inc. announced that it received the certificate of occupancy for its high-volume manufacturing facility, ARC, earlier this week.
- The ARC facility is connected to the Covington Municipal Airport in Georgia.
- Archer is working to complete the tooling load-in for the initial manufacturing line at the facility.
- Production is planned to begin in early 2025.
- Archer aims to ramp production to a rate of two aircraft per month by the end of the year.
- Archer built ARC in close partnership with Stellantis.
- The two companies are continuing to progress their plans to mass-produce Archer’s Midnight aircraft.
- Stellantis is contributing capital, advanced manufacturing technology and expertise, and experienced personnel to scale the facility to 650 aircraft annually by 2030.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp commented that the announcement marks the next step in a relationship reaffirming Georgia's commitment to creating quality jobs and opportunities for years to come.
- The manufacturing facility is located in Newton County, near Covington.
- Congressman Mike Collins of Georgia, who sits on the House Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure and Committee on Science, Space, and Technology, stated that the completion highlights Georgia’s leadership in innovation, advanced manufacturing, and job creation.
- Adam Goldstein, Archer’s founder and CEO, said the completion is a testament to the industry shifting from R&D into commercialization.
- Goldstein noted that with construction complete, the team is focused on execution and scaling after production starts early next year.
- Goldstein stated the facility was built fast and on budget.
- Arnaud Deboeuf, Stellantis Chief Manufacturing and Supply Chain Officer, commented that supporting Archer’s electric aircraft growth with deep manufacturing expertise is a great opportunity for both companies.
- ARC will produce Archer’s all-electric vertical take-off and landing aircraft, Midnight.
- The Midnight aircraft is designed to offer passengers a sustainable, low-noise, and safe alternative to ground transportation in congested cities around the world.
