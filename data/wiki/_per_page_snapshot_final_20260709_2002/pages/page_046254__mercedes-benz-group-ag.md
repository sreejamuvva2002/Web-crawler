# Mercedes-Benz Group AG

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Mercedes invests $7bn in US manufacturing with $4bn Tuscaloosa plant upgrade and new Atlanta R&D hub](https://www.automotivemanufacturingsolutions.com/strategy/mercedes-commits-7bn-to-itsus-manufacturing-future/2639955)  ·  `page_046254`

## Facts

- Mercedes-Benz commits $7 billion-plus to its US manufacturing future.
- $4 billion of the investment is directed specifically to the Tuscaloosa plant by 2030.
- Atlanta is established as Mercedes-Benz's North American headquarters, anchored by a new research and development hub.
- The new GLE and GLS world premieres occurred at the Tuscaloosa, Alabama facility on March 31, 2026.
- The event marked the assembly of the five-millionth vehicle since production began at the plant in 1997.
- The Tuscaloosa plant is operated by Mercedes-Benz US International (MBUSI).
- The plant opened in 1995 and began series production in 1997 with the M-Class SUV, marking the first major Mercedes-Benz plant located outside Germany.
- MBUSI directly employs approximately 5,800 people.
- Senator Katie Britt noted direct employment of around 6,100 MBUSI workers in Alabama and indirect employment of close to 60,000 Alabamians through supplier and services relationships.
- Ola Källenius, Chairman of the Board of Management of Mercedes-Benz Group AG, was part of the original team at the Alabama plant in the 1990s and subsequently served as its head.
- Current production rates at the Tuscaloosa plant are around 260,000 vehicles per year.
- The plant exports approximately 60% of its annual output to markets across the globe.
- Investment allocations will cover production system upgrades, automation, electrification-ready infrastructure, and capacity expansion.
- Mercedes-Benz sold 303,200 passenger cars and 40,000 vans in the United States in the most recent reporting period, making it the company's second-largest global market.
- The GLC SUV will join the Tuscaloosa production roster with US production scheduled for late 2027.
- Current Tuscaloosa production includes the GLE, GLS, GLE Coupe, AMG performance variants, Mercedes-Maybach GLS, EQE SUV, EQS SUV, and Mercedes-Maybach EQS SUV.
- The new GLE and GLS arrive with updated AI-driven technologies, refined powertrains, and premium connectivity features.
- The Mercedes-AMG GLE 53 HYBRID made its world debut at the event.
- MBUSA is moving up to 500 roles from various US locations into a new Atlanta R&D hub as part of a strategic multi-million-dollar investment.
- The Atlanta base centers on the existing '1MB' facility in Sandy Springs, which has housed approximately 800 Mercedes-Benz employees since 2018.
- The transition to add up to 500 further roles to the Atlanta headcount is expected to be complete by August 2026.
- The new R&D hub will leverage the engineering talent pool around the Georgia Institute of Technology and collaborate with the region's startup ecosystem and tech sectors.
- Mercedes-Benz operates approximately 400 US-based suppliers and sources its demand for steel and aluminum almost exclusively from within the country.
- The company supports an estimated 160,000 jobs across the US economy, encompassing 10,600 direct roles of which around 8,000 are in manufacturing.
- An additional EV infrastructure investment of approximately $1.2 billion is under way to expand a high-speed charging network across North America.
- The MBUSI plant at Vance, near Tuscaloosa, functions as the global hub for Mercedes-Benz SUV production, with two-thirds of its output leaving the country.
