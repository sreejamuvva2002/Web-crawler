# Kia Georgia, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-07-12

**Source:** [Kia to Invest Over $200 Million in EV9 Production Expansion | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-releases/2023/kia-invest-over-200-million-ev9-production-expansion)  ·  `page_000016`

## Facts

- Kia will invest more than $200 million to expand EV9 production at its West Point plant.
- The investment will create nearly 200 new jobs at the facility.
- The expansion accommodates assembly of the all-electric 2024 EV9 three-row SUV.
- EV9 assembly is expected to begin in the second quarter of 2024.
- The EV9 will become the fifth model assembled at Kia Georgia, joining the Telluride, Sorento, Sportage SUVs, and K5 mid-size sedan.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced the investment on July 12, 2023.
- The project aims to help propel Georgia toward its goal of becoming the e-mobility capital.
- Currently, more than 40 percent of Kia vehicles sold in the U.S. are manufactured at the West Point plant.
- The EV9 is Kia’s first three-row EV SUV and the first Kia EV assembled in North America.
- The EV9 serves as the flagship vehicle for Kia’s global Plan S strategy focused on sustainable mobility leadership.
- Sean Yoon, President & CEO of Kia Motors North America and Kia Motors America, stated the EV9 will be the most innovative vehicle Kia has ever built.
- Kia Georgia utilizes Georgia Quick Start workforce development program, which operates the Kia Georgia Training Center.
- Georgia Quick Start provides comprehensive, customized training services to prepare new employees for the new assembly line.
- Stuart Countess, President & CEO of Kia Georgia, highlighted partnerships with the Governor’s Office, Department of Economic Development, Georgia QuickStart, City of West Point, and Troup County.
- City of West Point Mayor Steve Tramell noted Kia has been an outstanding corporate citizen for seventeen years, providing employment opportunities and fostering economic growth in West Point.
- Amanda Fields, Senior Regional Project Manager, represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s Global Commerce team on the project.
- The project involved partnerships with the West Point Development Authority, City of West Point, Troup County, Georgia Center of Innovation’s Manufacturing and Energy teams, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated Kia has exceeded expectations since being recruited in 2006.
- To date, Kia has invested more than $1.9 billion in the state of Georgia.
- Kia Georgia is responsible for more than 14,000 plant and supplier jobs in the region.
- Governor Kemp and state lawmakers recognized January 31 as “Kia Day” earlier in 2023 to commend Kia’s manufacturing impact.
- The 2006 announcement to establish a vehicle assembly facility in West Point was one of the biggest single foreign investments in Georgia’s history.
- Kia ships vehicles from Georgia to global sales markets including the United States, Canada, Mexico, the Caribbean, Middle East, and Pacific nations.
- Since 2020, Georgia has attracted more than $22.7 billion in investment and created over 28,400 jobs along the entire EV supply chain.
- Kia Georgia has an annual production capacity of 340,000 units.
- The Kia Georgia facility is located on 2,200 acres in West Point, Georgia, and began mass production on November 16, 2009.
- Kia America is headquartered in Irvine, California, and operates a U.S. dealer network of over 750 locations.
- The K5, Sportage, Sorento, and Telluride are assembled in the United States from U.S. and globally sourced parts.
- The EV9 is expected to be assembled in the United States from U.S. and globally sourced parts.
