# Daimler Truck North America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Daimler Truck CFO says North American turnaround is second-half story | FleetOwner](https://www.fleetowner.com/equipment/news/55328912/daimler-truck-cfo-says-north-american-turnaround-is-second-half-story)  ·  `page_053533`

## Facts

- DTNA’s Q3 profits fell 65% and sales dropped 39%, showing ongoing pressure from tariffs and weaker North American demand.
- North American truck sales likely won’t rebound until the second half of 2026, keeping fleets cautious on new orders.
- Global performance shows Europe improving but Asia struggling, signaling fleet managers must navigate uneven regional demand.
- Daimler Truck North America’s profits fell more than 60% in the third quarter compared to a year earlier and will slide further this quarter, according to CFO Eva Scherer on November 7.
- The North American operations of Daimler Truck Holding AG comprise the Freightliner and Western Star truck brands as well as Thomas Built Buses.
- DTNA sold 30,225 units in Q3 (ended September 30), down 39% from the same period in 2024.
- Q3 revenues slid by a third to about $4.6 billion.
- Q3 earnings before interest and taxes fell 65% to $295 million.
- CFO Eva Scherer expects DTNA’s North American profitability to take another step down this quarter as the Trump administration’s tariffs on trucks and their parts work through the company’s business.
- Rival International, whose parent is Traton SE, could post a Q4 operating loss as tariffs compound falling orders and rising input costs.
- DTNA sales picked up in September and October relative to the two months prior, but remain far below desired numbers.
- Scherer stated that while the company hopes the market will pick up, it will not happen in Q1 and will probably occur towards the second half of the year.
- DTNA executives expect North American sales to finish 2025 closer to the bottom of their forecast of 135,000 to 155,000 than the top.
- Based on nearly 108,000 vehicles sold through the end of September, Q4 sales will be generally in line with Q3’s.
- DTNA sold about 191,000 trucks in 2024.
- The company cut about 2,700 jobs since this time last year in response to the order downturn.
- Daimler’s leaders have not yet decided whether to move more truck assembly work from Mexico to the U.S. due to an unclear tariff situation.
- It is premature to discuss DTNA imposing more surcharges to offset the effects of new tariffs.
- Globally, Daimler Truck posted a net profit of about $530 million on more than $13.2 billion in sales.
- Scherer noted that parts of Europe are showing signs of improving demand, but key Asian markets like Japan and Indonesia continue to struggle.
- The company sold a little more than 98,000 vehicles during Q3, a drop of 15% from the same period last year.
- Shares of Daimler (Ticker: DTG) fell slightly after the earnings announcement, leaving the company’s market value at about $20 billion over the past six months.
