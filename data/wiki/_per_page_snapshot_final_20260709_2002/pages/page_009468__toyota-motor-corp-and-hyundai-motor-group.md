## Facts

- Toyota and Hyundai are the world's number one and number three automakers by sales respectively.
- Both automakers are facing intensifying competition in electric vehicles from Tesla and Chinese carmakers such as BYD.
- The chairmen of Asia's two largest automakers made a rare joint public appearance at an event to showcase their companies' high-performance vehicles, underscoring the trend of rivals joining hands in the face of electrification.
- Toyota Motor Corp.'s chairman Akio Toyoda and Hyundai Motor Group's chairman Euisun Chung were at a racing festival in Yong-in, south of Seoul.
- Samsung Electronics Co. chairman Jay Y. Lee and Hankook & Company Co.'s chairman Hyunbum Cho attended the event to meet Toyoda and Chung.
- The event displayed rally cars from both automakers and several future-oriented models, including hydrogen-powered vehicles.
- Toyota chairman Akio Toyoda stated that 'Toyota and Hyundai are joining hands to create a better society and shape the future of mobility,' adding that he and Chung discovered a shared passion for racing.
- Sunday's festival marks the first time the two auto giants have teamed up to hold an event, raising the prospect of further collaboration.
- Earlier this month, Toyota's research unit and Hyundai's Boston Dynamics Inc. announced a plan to jointly develop humanoid robots with artificial intelligence.
- Global car companies are increasingly joining forces in production and research and development to lower costs and boost competitiveness.
- Nissan Motor Co. and Honda Motor Co. announced a joint research agreement in August to develop a platform and technologies for next-generation software-defined vehicles.
- Hyundai has also partnered with General Motors Co. around joint product development, manufacturing and future clean energy technologies.
- Hyundai showcased rally cars from its World Rally Championship program, including the i20 N Rally 1 Hybrid and i20 N Rally 2.
- Toyota's Gazoo racing unit presented its GR Yaris Rally 1 Hybrid, GR Yaris Rally 2, GR Supra, and GR86 among others.
