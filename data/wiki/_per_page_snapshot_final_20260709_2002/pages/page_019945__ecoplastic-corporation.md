# Ecoplastic Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-01

**Source:** [Longtime Kia and HMG Supplier to Build Manufacturing Facility in Georgia - Rome CEO](https://romeceo.com/news/2023/01/longtime-kia-and-hmg-supplier-build-manufacturing-facility-georgia/)  ·  `page_019945`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced on Friday, January 6th, 2023, that Ecoplastic Corporation will create 456 new jobs and invest approximately $205 million in a new manufacturing facility in Bulloch County.
- Ecoplastic Corporation is a Korea-based company established in 1984 that manufactures plastic automobile parts for original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) and other suppliers.
- Ecoplastic is a Tier-1 supplier for Hyundai Motor Group (HMG) and Kia.
- The company engages in the manufacturing of automotive interior and exterior plastic parts, including bumpers, consoles, trims, and plastic molds.
- Shang Han, President and CEO of Ecoplastic, stated the company is honored to establish a production facility for electric vehicle parts in Bulloch County.
- Ecoplastic’s new facility will be located at 4822 Highway 301 South Register near Statesboro.
- The new plant will be capable of injection molding and painting.
- The facility will produce exterior parts, bumpers and garnishes, interior parts, consoles, and trims for electric vehicles.
- Ecoplastic expects to begin operations by October 2024.
- The company will be hiring for HR, finance, parts development, quality control, facility maintenance, injection, and paint production positions.
- Billy Allen, Chair of the Development Authority of Bulloch County, welcomed Ecoplastic to the community.
- Alyce Thornhill, Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy, represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this project.
- The project was executed in partnership with the Development Authority of Bulloch County, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia EMC.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson noted that Ecoplastic has decades of experience as a supplier for Kia and Hyundai.
- Georgia is positioned as a hub for the electric mobility industry.
- Since 2020, more than 30 EV-related projects have announced more than $21 billion in investment and more than 26,300 jobs in Georgia.
- On October 25, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America officially broke ground on its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility in neighboring Bryan County.
- Offsite suppliers connected to the Hyundai project were predicted to invest approximately $1 billion in other locations around the state.
- Projects announced to date have already exceeded the $1 billion offsite supplier investment prediction.
