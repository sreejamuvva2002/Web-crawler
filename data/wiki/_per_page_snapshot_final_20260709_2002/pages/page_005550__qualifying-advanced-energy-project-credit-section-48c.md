# Qualifying Advanced Energy Project Credit (Section 48C)

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2025-07-04

**Source:** [Home](https://eco.energy.gov/48C/s/)  ·  `page_005550`

## Facts

- The One Big Beautiful Bill Act (P.L. 119-21) or Working Families Tax Cut was enacted on July 4, 2025.
- This act revised dates and provisions in the Internal Revenue Code that affect some or all of the tax credits described on the page.
- The Qualifying Advanced Energy Project Credit (§ 48C) program aims to strengthen U.S. industrial competitiveness and clean energy supply chains.
- As the nation builds a net-zero economy, the § 48C tax credit program aims to play a critical role to create high-quality jobs, reduce industrial emissions, and increase domestic production of critical clean energy products and materials.
- The Inflation Reduction Act provided $10 billion in new funding under § 48C(e).
- At least $4 billion is reserved for projects in certain energy communities with closed coal mines or retired coal-fired power plants.
- Credits are allocated to projects in three categories: clean energy manufacturing and recycling, greenhouse gas emission reduction, and critical materials refining, processing, and recycling.
- Registered applicants will be able to create, view, and manage their applications through the program's portal.
