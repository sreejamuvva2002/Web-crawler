# Hyundai Motor Group

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group has entered a significant power purchase agreement (PPA) with Spanish renewable energy company Matrix Renewables to power its electric vehicle (EV) plant in Georgia.
- The 147-megawatt deal represents the largest single PPA signed by a Korean company in the United States.
- The agreement involves four Hyundai companies: Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America, Hyundai Mobis North America, Hyundai Steel Georgia, and Hyundai Transys Georgia Powertrain.
- Together, they secured a joint purchase agreement for renewable energy, reducing costs compared to individual contracts.
- The renewable energy will come from a new 210-megawatt solar power plant named Stillhouse Solar, currently under construction in Texas.
- Hyundai Motor will obtain about 378 gigawatt-hours of power per year from this plant.
- The obtained power is sufficient to supply electricity to approximately 100,000 households annually.
- This renewable energy initiative is expected to lower carbon emissions by 140,000 tons annually.
- The emission reduction is comparable to the emissions from 84,000 mid-sized sedans.
- Hyundai Motor's EV-dedicated plant in Bryan County, Georgia, is set to begin operations in October.
- The plant will begin operations three months ahead of schedule.
- As Hyundai Motor Group continues its transition towards renewable energy, the company remains committed to efficiently securing clean power for its automotive production.
