# TDK Kabushiki-gaisha

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** power_electronics  ·  **Published:** 1935-12-07

**Source:** [TDK - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/TDK)  ·  `page_060200`

## Facts

- TDK Corporation is a Japanese multinational electronics corporation that manufactures electronic components, power supplies, and sensors.
- Since the 2000s, TDK has focused on the development, manufacture and sales of electronic components, HDD heads and suspension, and power supplies.
- Beginning in 2005, TDK has acquired many types of electronic device manufacturers including passive component developers, sensors manufacturers and power supply companies.
- TDK's portfolio includes multi-axis MEMS motion tracking devices and MEMS microphones from InvenSense.
- TDK acquired a point-of-load DC-DC converter technology from Faraday Semi.
- TDK acquired MEMS-based ultrasonic Time-of-Flight sensors from Chirp Microsystems intended for consumer electronics, AR/VR, robotics, drones, IoT, automotive and industrial market segments.
- Other areas of TDK's recent focus include power components for mobile devices, high-stability MEMS accelerometers from Tronics, miniaturized haptic actuators, and custom ASICs through its subsidiary ICsense.
- In 2017, TDK and GenCell began collaborating to develop and produce an ammonia-based fuel solution.
- In 2005, TDK acquired Amperex Technology Limited, a Lithium Polymer battery company based in Hong Kong.
- In 2005, TDK acquired the Lambda Power Division, a group of power supply businesses of London-based Invensys PLC.
- In 2017, TDK formed RF360 Holdings Singapore PTE Ltd., a joint venture with Qualcomm Inc.
- In October 2024, McLaren announced TDK as the official technology partner for the McLaren Formula E Team and the McLaren Shadow F1 Sim Racing Team.
- In 1980, TDK developed a multilayering technology to create chip capacitors and inductors used in personal computers, laptops, smartphones and other electronic devices.
