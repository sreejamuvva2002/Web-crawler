# Georgia Department of Economic Development

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2023-12

**Source:** [GDEcD: 50 Years of International Presence - Metro Atlanta CEO](https://metroatlantaceo.com/features/2023/12/50-years-japan-and-europe/)  ·  `page_017470`

## Facts

- The State of Georgia has become a center of international business, with Atlanta and Savannah serving as primary global gateways for people and products through air, sea, and rail.
- Decades of strategic decisions by state leaders are delivering generational investments that keep Georgia at the forefront of global trends and stretching into new markets.
- Fifty years ago in 1973, state leadership under then-Governor Jimmy Carter began establishing Georgia’s physical presence abroad to kickstart its trajectory as a global hub for business and innovation.
- Coryn Marsik serves as Director of International Investment at the Georgia Department of Economic Development.
- Coryn Marsik stated that Georgia having international representation for over 50 years has helped give its economy remarkable resiliency, noting that these offices recruit diverse investments to create jobs and help companies reach new global markets.
- Georgia established its first international office in Tokyo in 1973 to provide direct access to rapidly developing markets in East Asia.
- Japanese companies continue to be the top international employer in Georgia to this day.
- The state maintains active ties with Japan-Georgia organizations including the Japan-America Society of Georgia and the Japan External Trade Organization.
- Georgia serves as a charter member in the Southeast U.S.-Japan Alliance.
- The State of Georgia’s Europe Office was established shortly after the Tokyo office, with professionals serving the European market from Munich, Germany.
- Germany is one of Georgia’s top investor nations and has a robust mobility industry that anchors Georgia’s mission to attract innovative technologies from Europe.
- In 2020, Governor Brian Kemp and a State of Georgia delegation met with automotive companies headquartered in Germany, where electrification and alternative fuels were repeatedly discussed.
- Following the 2020 meetings, the state adopted a new goal to identify opportunities at every step of the mobility and battery supply chain.
- Just three years after the 2020 meetings, the mobility and battery supply chain in Georgia announced more than $25.5 billion in investments and an estimated 30,700 new jobs over the next few years.
- European-based companies enhancing Georgia’s mobility ecosystem include GEDIA Automotive, Aurubis AG, and Heliox.
- The success of Georgia’s mobility and clean tech industries led to representation at the 2023 World Economic Forum in Davos-Klosters, Switzerland.
- Governor Brian Kemp served on a panel at the 2023 World Economic Forum, stating that one can count on stability and a great economy and business environment in Georgia.
- Ellen Kraft serves as Managing Director of the Europe Office.
- Ellen Kraft stated that the European market is full of opportunity for attracting innovative companies, helping Georgia businesses connect with new customers, and establishing new ties, noting that relationships are expanding with examples like the North Rhine-Westphalia (NRW) office in Atlanta.
- International businesses operating in Georgia tap into university-backed research, innovative ecosystems, and solutions-oriented partnerships to stay up-to-date on business trends.
- Commissioner Pat Wilson explains how Georgia is reaping the benefits of strategic leadership decisions made decades ago.
