# Archer Aviation Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-12-19

**Source:** [Archer](https://news.archer.com/archer-completes-construction-of-high-volume-manufacturing-facility)  ·  `page_016943`

## Facts

- Archer received the certificate of occupancy for its ~400,000 square foot aircraft manufacturing facility—referred to as ARC—in Covington, GA and held a commemorative ribbon cutting ceremony.
- Tooling load-in is already underway ahead of the planned start of initial production beginning in early 2025, with the goal being to ramp production to a rate of two aircraft per month by the end of the year.
- Stellantis and Archer are working to finalize an agreement pursuant to which Stellantis would work with Archer as its exclusive contract manufacturer to mass produce Archer’s Midnight eVTOL aircraft at this facility.
- As part of the partnership, Stellantis will contribute capital, advanced manufacturing technology and expertise and experienced personnel with the goal of scaling this facility to 650 aircraft annually by 2030.
- ARC is connected to the Covington Municipal Airport in Georgia.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp commented that announcing Archer’s choice to build the new manufacturing facility in Newton County marked just the start of an incredible relationship and reaffirmed Georgia's commitment to creating quality jobs and opportunities for years to come.
- Congressman Mike Collins of Georgia, who sits on the House Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure Committee and Committee on Science, Space, and Technology, added that the completion of Archer's manufacturing facility in Covington highlights Georgia’s leadership in innovation, advanced manufacturing and job creation.
- Archer founder and CEO Adam Goldstein stated that the completion of the facility is a testament to the industry shifting from R&D into commercialization, praised the team and partners for getting construction done fast and on budget, and noted the focus is now on execution and scaling.
- Stellantis Chief Manufacturing and Supply Chain Officer Arnaud Deboeuf commented that supporting Archer’s electric aircraft growth with deep manufacturing expertise is a great opportunity for both companies and a significant step in taking clean mobility to the sky.
- ARC is where Archer plans to produce its all-electric vertical take-off and landing aircraft, Midnight, which is designed to offer passengers a sustainable, low-noise and safe alternative to ground transportation in congested cities around the world.
- Covington Mayor Fleeta Baggett, State Rep Tim Fleming, Newton County Development President Serra Hall, Newton County Chamber President Debbie Harper, Covington City Manager Tres Thomas, Newton County Commissioner Lanier Sims, and Archer VP of Operations Glen Burks were joined by several local leaders to commemorate the completion of construction with a ribbon-cutting ceremony.
- The press release contains forward-looking statements regarding Archer’s future business plans, aircraft performance, certification timelines, production ramp-up, and volume expectations, noting they are predictions subject to risks detailed in SEC filings.
