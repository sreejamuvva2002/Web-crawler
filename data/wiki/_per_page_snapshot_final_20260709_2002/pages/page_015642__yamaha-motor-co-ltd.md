# Yamaha Motor Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-03-10

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp: Yamaha to Move HQ to Georgia - SelectCobb](https://selectcobb.com/gov-kemp-yamaha-to-move-hq-to-georgia/)  ·  `page_015642`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp welcomed the announcement that Yamaha Motor Co., Ltd. has officially decided to move its U.S. headquarters to Kennesaw, Georgia.
- The company is relocating out of California following roughly 50 years of operation there.
- The decision follows a meeting between Governor Kemp and Yamaha executives in October of last year at the Southeast U.S./Japan (SEUS-Japan) annual gathering in Tokyo.
- Yamaha Motor Co., Ltd. manufactures ATVs, boat engines, personal watercraft, and other motorized products.
- The company already has a strong presence in Georgia, employing over 2,300 Georgians.
- The relocation of the Yamaha Motor Corp. U.S. headquarters will begin this year (2026) and is expected to continue through 2028.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated that they are honored and proud to welcome Yamaha’s American headquarters to the No. 1 state for business.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp noted that the move is a loud and clear testament to what Georgia offers job creators from around the world.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp invited other California-based companies looking for a better home to consider Georgia.
- The Yamaha Motor Company currently employs thousands of people across various facilities in Georgia.
- The 1.3-million-square-foot Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation of America factory in Newnan opened in 1986 and currently employs roughly 2,000 Georgians.
- Yamaha relocated its marine division to Kennesaw in 1999.
- The company expanded the Kennesaw facility with a 75,000-square-foot Marine Innovation Center in 2023.
- In 2019, the company moved its motorsports operations to Georgia to benefit from close proximity to existing facilities.
