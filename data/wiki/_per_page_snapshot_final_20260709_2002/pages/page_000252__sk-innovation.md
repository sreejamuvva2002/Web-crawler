# SK Innovation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2021-04-11

**Source:** [Deal reached to allow Georgia battery plant completion - POLITICO](https://www.politico.com/news/2021/04/11/georgia-battery-plant-deal-lg-sk-480842)  ·  `page_000252`

## Facts

- South Korean battery manufacturers LG and SK Innovation reached a settlement allowing SK to complete its $2.6 billion factory in Georgia.
- The settlement removes the need for President Joe Biden and U.S. Trade Representative Katherine Tai to decide whether to overturn an International Trade Commission (ITC) ruling that had prompted SK to threaten abandoning the plant and its 2,600 jobs.
- Sunday served as the deadline for the Biden administration to issue a decision on the ITC ruling.
- The $1.8 billion agreement enables SK Innovation to complete its Commerce, Georgia facility, which will supply batteries for a suite of Volkswagen vehicles and the electric Ford F-150.
- President Joe Biden publicly praised the settlement in a White House statement on Sunday.
- Biden stated that a strong, diversified, and resilient U.S.-based EV battery supply chain is needed to meet growing global demand, create good-paying domestic jobs, and lay the groundwork for future jobs.
- Biden added that the settlement is a positive step toward building the EV battery supply chain, bringing relief to Georgia workers and new opportunities nationwide.
- In February, the ITC ruled that SK Innovation stole trade secrets from LG and ordered a blockade on shipments of materials needed to make batteries at the Georgia plant.
- Despite an ITC grace period allowing automakers time to switch battery suppliers, SK Innovation threatened to abandon the project if the ruling was not overturned.
- The ITC ruling posed a potential setback to President Biden's ambitious electric vehicle manufacturing goals.
- The ITC granted Volkswagen two years and Ford four years to find alternative battery suppliers, but both automakers indicated it would take at least that long, risking a shift to imported batteries or delays in new model releases.
- Federal law grants the president and U.S. Trade Representative 60 days to overrule an ITC decision, prompting an intense lobbying campaign directed at the White House.
- SK Innovation hired former EPA Administrator Carol Browner and former Deputy Attorney General Sally Yates to lobby President Biden and USTR Katherine Tai to overturn the ruling.
- SK Innovation warned that abandoning the Georgia plant would allow Chinese battery suppliers to capture the resulting market demand.
- LG maintained that SK Innovation was bluffing about abandoning the project and would likely complete the factory regardless.
- LG argued that even if SK abandoned the facility, another supplier would quickly acquire it to meet automaker demand.
- Throughout the dispute, LG stated it was open to a settlement allowing SK to build its factory in exchange for compensation for stolen intellectual property.
- On Sunday, the companies announced that SK Innovation would pay LG $1.8 billion plus a running royalty on batteries produced at the Georgia facility.
- The companies did not disclose the size of the running royalty payments or their duration.
- In exchange, LG agreed to resolve the ITC dispute, a parallel D.C. Circuit Court case, and all related legal issues in Korea, while promising not to sue SK Innovation over the intellectual property disputes for 10 years.
- The settlement provides relief to the Biden White House by avoiding a difficult decision between protecting swing-state jobs and upholding U.S. trade laws.
- Had President Biden upheld the ITC ruling, Republicans were prepared to criticize the administration over potential job losses.
- Conversely, had Biden overturned the ruling, progressives would have argued for waiving intellectual property protections for COVID-19 vaccines, a stance the White House has resisted.
- USTR Katherine Tai pushed both companies to reach a settlement through more than a dozen phone calls with their CEOs and executives in recent weeks.
- Tai also consulted with senior Biden administration officials and Georgia lawmakers, prompting Biden to thank her for her "tireless work to resolve this dispute."
- Katherine Tai congratulated both companies on resolving their differences, stating it builds confidence in their reliability and responsibility as U.S. auto industry suppliers.
- Georgia's two newly elected Democratic senators welcomed the deal after publicly urging the companies to negotiate rather than pressuring the Biden administration to overturn the ruling.
- Senators Raphael Warnock and Jon Ossoff claimed credit for the settlement, noting they had repeatedly contacted both the battery company leaders and the Biden administration.
- Senator Raphael Warnock stated that negotiating a good-faith settlement was the best way to protect workers in Commerce, Georgia, and fulfill job promises made to Georgians.
