# Hyundai EV Assembly Plant

**Type:** Facility  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[vehicle-manufacturing|Vehicle Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** Ellabell, GA, Bryan County, Georgia, United States

## Overview

Hyundai Motor Group is constructing a $5.5 billion electric-vehicle assembly plant in Ellabell, GA, part of a nearly 3,000-acre megasite that will produce up to 300,000 EVs annually and use renewable energy to help the automaker reach carbon-neutral goals.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Primary vehicle manufacturing facility for Hyundai’s electric-vehicle lineup in Georgia.

## Key facts

- Hyundai’s $5.5 billion EV assembly plant is under construction in Ellabell, GA.
- The megasite spans nearly 3,000 acres and will use renewable energy to power mass production.
- Hyundai aims to achieve carbon neutrality by 2045, with the new plant contributing to that goal.
- Hyundai Industrial Co. is spending more than $24 million to retrofit a Newnan facility for armrests and headrests, creating 100 jobs.
- Hwashin plans a $176 million plant in Dublin, GA, expected to create over 460 jobs and begin production in late 2025.
- Daechang Seat Corp. USA is investing $72.5 million to build an assembly plant in Savannah, GA, creating more than 500 jobs.
- The combined supplier investments bring an estimated $2.5 billion to Georgia from 14 suppliers.
- The growing EV network associated with Hyundai’s plant is projected to create more than 6,000 jobs across the state.
