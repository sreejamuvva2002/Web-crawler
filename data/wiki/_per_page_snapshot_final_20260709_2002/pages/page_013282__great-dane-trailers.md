# Great Dane Trailers

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2013-02-04

**Source:** [2012 Is U.S. Trailer Makers’ Best Year Since ’06 - TT](https://www.ttnews.com/articles/2012-us-trailer-makers-best-year-06)  ·  `page_013282`

## Facts

- U.S. trailer makers took in more orders for new equipment in 2012 than in any other year since 2006.
- Trailer makers have a strong enough backlog to keep busy through the middle of 2013, even as the economy has cooled.
- A Jan. 28 report from ACT Research Co. said net December orders came in at 30,594, or 21.7% above the final month of 2011.
- The annual total rose to 239,219, up 1.1% from 236,589 in 2011.
- The record for trailer orders was 286,500 units in 2006, the same year that truck sales peaked.
- When the recession hit, trailer orders plunged to just 85,100 units in 2009.
- Since then, annual orders have nearly tripled, according to ACT figures.
- Shipments of finished trailers from factories rose by about 13% to more than 240,000 units last year from 213,000 in 2011.
- Frank Maly, ACT’s director of analysis and research, stated that all 10 tracked types of trailers grew in shipments on a year-over-year basis.
- December orders lay a strong foundation for 2013, with deals still rolling out in the pipeline.
- Chris Hammond, vice president at Great Dane Trailers, noted the Savannah, Ga., company’s order backlog is pushing well into the second quarter of 2013.
- Hammond cited a hangover effect from 2009 to 2010 as customers realign their trade-in cycles.
- Glenn Harney, senior vice president at Hyundai Translead, said quote and order volumes have remained steady.
- Production for vans at Hyundai Translead is fully booked through mid-May, and through mid-June for refrigerated trailers.
- Utility Trailer Manufacturing Co. made more trailers last year than ever before, with 2012 sales volume exceeding 2011 by 13%.
- Utility sold more than 18,000 refrigerated trailers in 2012, the second-highest amount in its history behind 2006.
- Stoughton Trailers customers for dry vans are bumping up against an informal “10-year rule” where shippers won’t load/unload trailers older than 10 years due to safety concerns.
- Sales observed by Stoughton Trailers are replacement purchases rather than growth in the nation’s total trailer fleet.
- With another year of modest growth, orders will be comparable to the 2004-2006 high-volume days, when orders rose from 245,000 in 2004 up to the 2006 peak.
- Fontaine Trailers management expects a solid year slightly off from 2012 levels.
- Alan Briley, vice president of sales and marketing for Fontaine’s commercial platform division, noted flatbed carriers were replacing equipment for the first time in several years.
- Fontaine’s backlog of unfilled orders dropped from nine to 12 months a year ago to three or four months currently.
- MAC Trailer Manufacturing entered the pneumatic bulk tank business in 2009 and benefited from sales to companies hauling sand for hydraulic fracturing in oil and gas fields.
- MAC Trailer Manufacturing had a record year for sales in 2012 after a record in 2011, with flatbed demand growing to compensate for dropped pneumatic bulk demand.
- Jeffrey Kauffman, stock analyst for Sterne, Agee & Leach, believes fleet managers are shifting investment incrementally from trucks to trailers.
- Orders for new trucks contracted year-over-year for most of 2012, and U.S. retail truck sales have shrunk since September, even though 2012 was better than 2011.
- Rence Oliphant, vice president of sales and marketing for suspension maker Hendrickson International, noted a separation between trucks and trailers, with trailer purchases held on longer than tractor buys.
- Fleet managers postponed trailer purchases longer than tractor buys, making the need to buy trailers more acute.
- A new heavy-duty tractor can cost about $125,000, leading fleet managers to postpone such major purchases in favor of trailers as a better bargain for capital spending.
- The dry van market is currently the hottest among trailer types.
- Wabash National Corp. declined comment but announced it has purchased assets from Beall Corp.
