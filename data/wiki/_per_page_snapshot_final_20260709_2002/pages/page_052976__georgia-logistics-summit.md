# Georgia Logistics Summit

**Type:** event  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Georgia Center of Innovation Hosts 2022 Logistics Summit In Macon, Georgia | AM 1180 Radio](https://chattooga1180.com/georgia-center-of-innovation-hosts-2022-logistics-summit-in-macon-georgia/)  ·  `page_052976`

## Facts

- The Georgia Center of Innovation, a strategic arm of the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD), hosted hundreds of attendees at the March 16 Georgia Logistics Summit.
- Experts offered insights on the digitalization of the logistics industry and harnessing needed talent.
- Created in 2009 to bring together all aspects of the logistics industry, the Georgia Logistics Summit has grown to become one of the Southeast’s most important logistics and supply chain-centered events.
- The total sales impact of Georgia’s logistics industries jumped to $83.4 billion in 2021, equaling approximately 7% of the state’s total sales, an increase from $60.7 billion (or 6%) in 2018.
- Georgia outpaced the nation in warehousing services growth at 92.8% growth since 2018.
- In 2018, one out of every 14 Georgia jobs owed its existence to logistics; today, that number has grown to one in every nine Georgia jobs.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that long-term investments have made Georgia’s logistics networks a gateway to the entire United States and driven record-breaking economic investment and trade year after year.
- Commissioner Wilson noted the summit was a sold-out program of hundreds of industry practitioners from business, government, and education who gathered in Macon to discuss best practices and position businesses for success.
- Two featured panels at the Summit were “Inside the Mind – Awakening Your Talent Search” and a keynote panel titled “A Vision for Supply Chain Digitalization in 2030.”
- Panelists included business leaders from Amazon, RaceTrac, Blue Bird Corporation, and Accenture who shared experiences on how technology is shaping logistics and fulfillment operations.
- The role of data and analytics in supply chain transformation was explored, including ways companies are approaching changes to make supply chains future-proof.
- Panelists emphasized that while digitalization offers tools, logistics remains a “people business” and stressed redundancy of operations as a key need for supply chain resiliency amid the COVID-19 pandemic.
- Panelists noted digitalization is increasingly utilized for data harvesting and demand signaling, with companies focusing on vertical integration through leading-edge technology and automation to streamline logistics sequences.
- It was noted that 50% of existing repetitive tasks can be automated through existing technology, but companies must also leverage human creativity, decision-making, problem-solving, and leadership skills.
- Amazon representatives focused on delivery speed and responsible resource use at the summit.
- Sean Holloway, General Manager of Sav 3 at Amazon in Macon, highlighted Amazon's electric delivery van fleet order with American startup Rivian to accommodate increasing demand for “last mile” deliveries.
- Holloway stated that companies will increasingly focus on being good stewards and adopting green technology moving into the future.
- The summit featured Macon Mayor Lester Miller and Irving Consumer Products, who highlighted Macon as a prime community for logistics success.
- Georgia Department of Transportation (GDOT) Planning Director Jannine Miller provided updates on strategic investments to maintain and improve Georgia’s top-ranked interstate highway system and state roads and bridges.
- Miller briefed attendees on GDOT’s strategic investments in local projects to help trucks move more efficiently, including updates on long-term projects like the I-75/I-16 Interchange in Macon.
- Center of Innovation Executive Director David Nuckolls stated that digitalization changes how business is done and why the Center fills a critical need for Georgia businesses.
- Nuckolls added that through events like the annual Georgia Logistics Summit, they continue to showcase industry best practices and make Georgia’s logistics industry more competitive.
- Educating the needed workforce was the focus of the event’s first panel, with representatives from secondary and higher education, along with the Technical College System of Georgia, taking a proactive approach to aligning education with industry needs.
- New career pathways in warehousing and distribution are preparing students for direct entry into the industry upon graduation, and in some cases while still in school.
- The availability and skill of Georgia’s large veteran population was discussed, including opportunities through the Georgia Veterans Education Career Resource (VECTR Center).
- The VECTR Center provides unique, accelerated programs in high-demand strategic industries that abbreviate post-secondary certificate and degree processes by recognizing veterans' military training.
- Panelists focused on developing students’ core skills, creating future leaders, and building partnerships with local schools and industrial/economic development authorities to foster industry-educator conversations.
- Georgia is home to 85% of third-party logistics companies and is ranked No. 1 for infrastructure and access to global markets by Area Development.
- The state is home to Hartsfield-Jackson International Airport, the busiest and most efficient airport in the world, and the Port of Savannah, the fastest-growing seaport in the U.S.
- Georgia offers robust rail and highway infrastructure, including more rail miles than any other Southeast state at 5,000 miles, 1,200 miles of interstate highways (I-75, I-85, I-95, and I-20), and 20,000 miles of federal and state highways.
- Many leading logistics companies have operations or headquarters in Georgia, including Amazon, The Coca-Cola Company, Delta Air Lines, The Home Depot, Norfolk Southern, and UPS.
- Additional information on the 2022 Georgia Logistics Summit and next year's plans is available through an official summit website.
