# Mollertech LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2013-03-27

**Source:** [Detroit area worker killed in auto parts plant - World Socialist Web Site](https://www.wsws.org/en/articles/2013/03/27/dead-m27.html)  ·  `page_057977`

## Facts

- A 42-year-old auto parts worker was killed at the Mollertech LLC plant in Shelby Township, Michigan when a five-ton piece of equipment fell on him.
- The incident occurred while the worker was reportedly working on an overhead crane and was trapped underneath by a five-ton die.
- Fire Chief James Swinkowski stated the cause is still under investigation regarding potential process failure.
- Mollertech produces injection molded parts and accessories, such as dashboards, for car and truck interiors.
- The plant opened in 1998 and operates as a non-union company employing approximately 140 people.
- The facility is owned by MollerGroup GmbH, based in Bielefeld, Germany.
- Mollertech supplies parts for Chrysler, BMW, Toyota, Honda, and Volkswagen.
- The plant is located about 30 miles north of Detroit in an industrial park near Chrysler’s Sterling Heights assembly plant.
- OSHA records show a single unannounced inspection opened in October 2006 citing four 'serious' violations and ten 'other' violations, with initial fines reduced from $3,600 to $1,800 after litigation.
- In a 2003 lawsuit, Mollertech fired former employee Paul Bennett for requesting a 40-hour workweek under the ADA due to Crohn’s disease, claiming the request was unreasonable.
- Bureau of Labor Statistics data shows automobile and light duty motor vehicle manufacturing workers experience higher injury and illness rates than most other industries, with a 2011 incidence rate nearly 40 percent higher than private industry overall.
- Auto parts plants supplying Chrysler, General Motors, Ford, and other automakers face worse conditions due to cost-cutting pressures and just-in-time delivery schedules from Detroit automakers.
- The 2009 auto industry restructuring under the Obama administration led to tens of thousands of job losses, dozens of factory shutdowns, and slashed wages, particularly for new hires.
- Factories are running at full tilt with scheduled 10-12 hour shifts, eliminated break times, and increased assembly line speeds.
- Cuts to OSHA under the Obama administration in 2011 included plans to sharply cut corporate regulations to eliminate constraints on profit margins.
- The UAW's role in abandoning basic safety includes isolating strikes by parts workers since the 1980s, notably betraying 450 members of UAW Local 14 at AP Parts in Toledo, Ohio in 1984-85 after wage slashes from $12 to $6 an hour.
- By 2000, auto parts workers earned 31 percent less than assembly workers at Big Three plants due to the rise of non-union plants and lowered wages for new hires.
