# Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution Battery Cell Manufacturing Joint Venture

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-05-26

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution to Establish Battery Cell Manufacturing Joint Venture in the U.S.](https://www.kiamedia.com/us/en/media/pressreleases/20690/hyundai-motor-group-and-lg-energy-solution-to-establish-battery-cell-manufacturing-joint-venture-in)  ·  `page_001254`

## Facts

- The new battery cell joint venture will be located in Bryan County, Savannah, Georgia, adjacent to Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America which is currently under construction.
- Total investment for the joint venture will exceed USD 4.3 billion (KRW 5.7 trillion).
- Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution will each hold a 50 percent stake in the joint venture.
- The joint venture aims to begin battery production at the end of 2025 at the earliest.
- Annual production capacity of the new facility will be 30 GWh.
- The facility's capacity will support the annual production of 300,000 EVs.
- Construction of the joint venture facility is planned to start in the second half of 2023.
- Hyundai Mobis will assemble battery packs using cells from the new plant.
- Hyundai Mobis will supply the assembled battery packs to Hyundai Motor Group’s U.S. manufacturing facilities for production of Hyundai, Kia and Genesis EV models.
- The new facility aims to create a stable regional battery supply and allow the group to respond fast to soaring EV demand in the U.S. market.
- This marks LGES's seventh battery plant currently operating or under construction in the U.S., with the company concentrating most of its resources on expanding local production capacity.
- Hyundai Motor Group and LGES have long been partners in electrification, having previously supplied EV batteries for vehicles including the Elantra Hybrid, Kona Electric, and IONIQ 6 dedicated EV.
- The Hyundai Elantra Hybrid, an LPi hybrid vehicle introduced in 2009, was the group’s first electrified model.
- In 2021, both companies started construction of a separate Indonesia battery cell joint venture set to start production in the first half of 2024.
- Hyundai Motor Group and LGES signed a memorandum of understanding for U.S. EV battery production on May 26, 2023, at LGES headquarters in Seoul.
- Jaehoon Chang, President and CEO of Hyundai Motor Company, and Youngsoo Kwon, CEO of LG Energy Solution, attended the signing ceremony.
- “Hyundai Motor Group is focusing on its electrification efforts to secure a leadership position in the global auto industry. We will create a strong foundation to lead the global EV transition through establishing a new EV battery cell plant with LG Energy Solution, a leading global battery producer and long-time partner,” said Jaehoon Chang, President and CEO of Hyundai Motor Company.
- “Two strong leaders in the auto and battery industries have joined hands, and together we are ready to drive the EV transition in America,” said Youngsoo Kwon, CEO of LG Energy Solution.
