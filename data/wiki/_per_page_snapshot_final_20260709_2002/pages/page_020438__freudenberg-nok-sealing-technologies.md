# Freudenberg-NOK Sealing Technologies

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2012-11-20

**Source:** [Freudenberg-NOK Sealing Technologies showcase nonwoven materials - Nonwovens Industry](https://www.nonwovens-industry.com/issues/2013-01/view_breaking-news/freudenberg-nok-sealing-technologies-showcase-nonwoven-materials)  ·  `page_020438`

## Facts

- Freudenberg-NOK Sealing Technologies (FNST) showcased a portfolio of products that enhance the safety, durability and performance of electric vehicles at the 2012 Battery Show in Detroit, MI, from Nov. 13-15.
- FNST is one of the largest producers of elastomeric seals and custom molded parts.
- FNST is pursuing advanced electric vehicle technologies as part of its commitment to sustainable products and processes.
- FNST displayed a variety of components at the show that leverage the expertise and products of its own divisions and other Freudenberg companies including Freudenberg-NOK Mechatronics and Freudenberg Nonwovens.
- Dr. Luis Lorenzo, vice president, Advanced Product Engineering, FNST, stated that the company is working in collaboration with customers to ensure that electric vehicle technologies evolve to the next level of safety and reliability.
- Developing alternative technological solutions that address the functional requirements of the system and the cost effectiveness of different approaches is one of FNST's key drivers, according to Dr. Lorenzo.
- With operations in the Americas, Europe and Asia-Pacific markets, FNST can tap a vast network of chemistry, design, product engineering and manufacturing experts to provide solutions for the global vehicle electrification industry.
- The company uses nearly two dozen polymer families and more than 2,000 raw materials to provide premium battery protection technologies.
- FNST provides products including battery pack gaskets, cell frame sealing, plug-and-seal technologies, compensation elements and liquid detectors.
- Breathable nonwoven elements were among the technologies displayed at The Battery Show.
- Breathable components produced from Freudenberg nonwoven materials optimize the ability of the battery system to repel water, oil, and dirt contamination.
- The structure of the fabric creates a “deep filter” affect in the battery unit that ensures the exchange of gases even when exposed to environmental contaminants.
- Juergen Mall, market segment manager, E-Mobility, FNST, stated that FNST engineers work on-site with auto manufacturers and their teams have been integrated into the vehicle electrification development process.
- FNST offers products capable of performing over a broad range of temperatures and environments.
- Customers rely on FNST's expertise in areas like material testing and product validation.
