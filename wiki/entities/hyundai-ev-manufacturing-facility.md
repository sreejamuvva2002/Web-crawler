# Hyundai EV Manufacturing Facility

**Type:** Facility  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[vehicle-manufacturing|Vehicle Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** Bryan County, Georgia, Bryan County, Georgia, United States

## Overview

Hyundai Motor Group will open its first fully dedicated electric vehicle (EV) and battery manufacturing facility in Georgia at the Bryan County Megasite. The $5.54 billion investment, plus about $1 billion from non-affiliated suppliers, will create roughly 8,100 jobs and have an annual production capacity of 300,000 EVs.

## EV supply-chain relevance

First fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility in the state, supporting Georgia's electric-mobility ecosystem and creating thousands of advanced-manufacturing jobs.

## Timeline

- **2021** — Strategic purchase of the Bryan County Megasite, the largest land acquisition in Georgia history.
- **January 2023** — Construction on the Hyundai EV and battery manufacturing facility scheduled to begin.
- **First half of 2025** — Full production expected to start, with annual capacity of 300,000 electric vehicles.
- **—** — Since 2020, Georgia has announced more than 20 EV-related projects totaling over $13.54 billion and creating nearly 18,000 jobs.

## Key facts

- Hyundai Motor Group will invest $5.54 billion to build a fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility in Georgia.
- Non-affiliated Hyundai suppliers are expected to contribute roughly $1 billion additional investment.
- The project will create approximately 8,100 new advanced-manufacturing jobs in the coastal region.
- Annual production capacity is planned at 300,000 fully electric vehicles.
- The facility will incorporate AI-equipped smart manufacturing and renewable energy to support Hyundai’s carbon-neutrality goals.
- Location offers direct access to Interstate 16, I-95, the Port of Savannah, and two Class I rail lines, facilitating global supply chains.
- Georgia is the southeastern leader in EV registrations per 1,000 vehicles and has the highest density of charging outlets per capita in the Southeast.
- The announcement was made at a signing ceremony with Governor Brian Kemp, highlighting Georgia’s commitment to electric mobility.
- The project underscores Georgia’s broader strategy to become a premier destination for quality companies creating jobs of today, tomorrow, and beyond.
