# Porsche Cars North America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Porsche's growing Atlanta HQ making efforts to go green(er) | Urbanize Atlanta](https://atlanta.urbanize.city/post/porsche-north-american-headquarters-solar-power-go-greener)  ·  `page_048830`

## Facts

- Porsche Cars North America has signed a deal with Georgia’s largest non-utility provider of solar energy to build and operate a solar-powered microgrid at One Porsche Drive.
- Solar panels will be installed soon on most new and existing buildings at the multifaceted complex neighboring Atlanta’s airport.
- The microgrid installed by Cherry Street Energy is expected to use solar panels to power “a significant portion” of electricity needs at a facility combining Porsche’s corporate headquarters with the expanding Porsche Experience Center Atlanta.
- Porsche’s first electric car, the Taycan, will be charged from renewable power in Atlanta, where a fleet is available for visitor driving experiences starting at $650 for 90 minutes.
- Kjell Gruner, PCNA’s president and CEO, stated that harnessing Georgia sunshine “makes perfect sense” and demonstrates Porsche’s commitment to sustainability.
- Porsche is aiming to be CO2 net neutral with its new vehicles by 2030.
- The PCNA campus and Experience Center, one of two in the U.S. and 10 around the world, is undergoing a $50-million expansion that includes a second driver development track for public use.
- Expansion features will include the Porsche Classic Factory Restoration facility, a new parking deck, and an already open Porsche Service Center South Atlanta.
- The project adds 33 acres to the existing 27-acre campus, expanding the footprint toward downtown Hapeville.
- Installation of the solar microgrid is expected to start next month and wrap up next year.
- Cherry Street Energy estimates the system will annually generate 2,050 megawatt hours of electricity, enough to power 191 average homes for a year.
- The solar setup will spare the air from about 3.2 million pounds of CO2 annually, equivalent to an average passenger car driving 3.6 million miles.
- Solar panels will be mounted on buildings throughout the complex, on a new 950-foot covered walkway extending off a parking garage to the headquarters building, and on the roofs of staging areas near the two race tracks.
- Cherry Street Energy will own, operate, and maintain the microgrid under a 25-year operation agreement while selling the generated power to PCNA.
- The campus expansion, straddling the Hapeville-Atlanta line, is being designed to achieve LEED Gold certification.
- The current One Porsche Drive campus opened seven years ago and previously earned LEED Gold certification.
- Porsche officials state the expansion is on track to finish construction in early 2023.
