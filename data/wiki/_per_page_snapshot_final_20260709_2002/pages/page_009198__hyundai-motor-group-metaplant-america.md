# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-18

**Source:** [Hyundai to invest $2.7 billion in Georgia plant expansion after immigration raid](https://www.wtoc.com/2025/09/18/hyundai-invest-27-billion-georgia-plant-expansion-after-immigration-raid/)  ·  `page_009198`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Company unveiled its roadmap to 2030 plan which includes an expansion for the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America in Ellabell.
- The announcement comes two weeks after Homeland Security raided the Hyundai megasite, which the agency called the largest single-site enforcement operation in its history.
- Phase 2 of the expansion is set to add production capacity of 200,000 units by 2028.
- The expansion will bring total production capacity to 500,000 cars a year.
- The expansion includes a $2.7 billion investment.
- The expansion will create 3,000 new jobs.
- Hyundai’s original contract with the State of Georgia stipulates it must create over 8,000 full-time jobs in the state by 2031 to receive promised billions of dollars in tax breaks.
- Hyundai Motor aims to produce more than 80% of cars sold in the U.S. domestically by 2030.
- The company made the announcement at its Investor Day.
- CEO José Muñoz addressed the immigration raid, which halted construction at the HL-GA Battery Company.
- HL-GA is owned jointly by Hyundai and LG, and is located on the same megasite as the EV factory.
- In the raid, 475 workers were detained by ICE.
- Around 300 of those detained were South Korean nationals, all but one of whom have been repatriated.
- Roughly 175 non-South Korean workers remain detained at an ICE facility in Folkston.
- CEO José Muñoz stated: “I want to restate our support to the workers that were involved. We’ve provided all the possible support to our colleagues from LG and their suppliers. But having said that, we obviously continue to see the US as the most important market outside of Korea to continue with the growth.”
- The production announcement is only for the EV plant, not HL-GA Battery Company, though the battery supplier exists to support the EV plant.
- The jobs of the people detained by ICE do not qualify as full-time, permanent positions that are contractually obligated to be created.
- The new jobs announced today do qualify as full-time, permanent positions for contractual obligations.
- CEO José Muñoz stated: “We need more capacity to be able to increase our localization. Our localization in North America is one of the lowest that we have globally.”
- CEO José Muñoz stated: “We’re very happy to have announced the additional 200,000 unit capacity, in total $2.7 billion of additional investment which is going to give more confidence to the State of Georgia and our partners while all this visa and immigration issues get resolved immediately.”
- Savannah Mayor Van Johnson released a statement saying: “this project will bring new opportunities for our workforce, bolster our infrastructure, and strengthen Savannah’s position as a hub for advanced manufacturing.”
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp released a statement to X following the announcement.
