## Facts

- Yancey Engineered Solutions will invest $5.7 million in a new manufacturing facility in Cordele, creating 300 new jobs over the next several years in Crisp County.
- Rinnai is expanding its operations in Griffin, marking its third facility in Georgia; the expansion doubles its space under roof and creates 50 jobs.
- Prime Inc will invest more than $160 million to establish a new Southeastern hub in Griffin, creating more than 120 full-time jobs in addition to hiring more than 50 truck drivers.
- Preciball USA plans to invest $17.6 million in a new production facility in Sylvania, creating 65 new jobs over the next several years; the company designs and manufactures precision and industrial balls for bearings, pumps, valves, swivel joints, and more.
- MGM Products plans to build a new manufacturing facility in Piedmont Regional Industrial Park in Monroe, representing an estimated $38.5 million investment expected to create between 150 and 175 new jobs within the first five years.
- Stellantis is investing more than $41 million in a new Mopar Parts Distribution Center in Forsyth; the nearly 422,000-square-foot facility strengthens the company’s U.S. parts distribution network for Chrysler, Dodge, Jeep, Ram, Alfa Romeo, and FIAT brands throughout the southeastern U.S.
- stow Group will invest $36 million to establish a new manufacturing presence in Calhoun, creating approximately 200 new jobs.
- EnergyTek Corporation plans to invest at least $275 million in establishing new manufacturing operations in Moultrie, expecting to create a total of 1,300 jobs at full operations following a multi-year ramp up.
- South Korean manufacturer Sejong opened a new plant in LaGrange to produce integrated charging control units for KIA vehicles.
- GE Appliances has added an additional $60 million to its already announced $180 million investment at its Roper Corporation cooking products plant in LaFayette, enabling purchase of state-of-the-art equipment, expanded production capacity, and accelerated launch of innovative new products.
- MollerTech invested $20 million to expand its operations in Elberton; the company is a globally recognized developer of specialized plastic components for automotives.
- Greyland Farms development under construction will add 900 kW of new electric load to Grantville’s system.
- ADS will invest $30 million to expand its existing facility to 117,000 sq. ft., creating 50 new jobs.
- Blue Bird is receiving $80 million from the federal government as part of a $1.7 billion effort to convert former auto factories into EV manufacturing and assembly plants; the investment is expected to create 428 manufacturing jobs and 250 construction jobs in Fort Valley.
- Hirano Tecseed Co., Ltd. will open its first U.S. office in Norcross, manufacturing advanced coating machines for electronics, medical, and aerospace industries.
- Lee Kum Kee Sauce Group will invest up to $288 million in a new manufacturing facility in LaGrange, creating up to 267 jobs in Troup County over the next several years to produce Asian sauces and condiments for future growth in the Americas.
- Brightmark plans to invest nearly $1 billion to build a 2.5 million sq. ft. facility in Thomaston that diverts 400,000 tons per year of plastic from landfills and turns used plastic into building blocks for new plastics; the facility will employ 200 workers.
- Imola Automotive USA is establishing a manufacturing plant on 195 acres in Fort Valley and will employ 7,500 people.
- Creative Flooring Solutions announced plans for a $151 million expansion to its Calhoun campus, increasing its footprint to more than 1.4 million square feet and employing about 500 people within the next five years.
- Hyundai Industrial will invest more than $24 million in a new manufacturing facility in Coweta County; the retrofitted building will produce arm and head rests for OEMs, including Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- Hanwha Advanced Materials Georgia Inc., a supplier of solar panel giant Qcells, will invest $147 million in a new Cartersville manufacturing facility creating 160 jobs; the factory supports Qcells’ planned 3.3-gigawatt plant in Cartersville, the first in the U.S. to build silicon solar panels from raw materials.
- Idea Nuova Inc., through its subsidiary American Home Manufacturing, will invest more than $19 million in a new manufacturing and distribution facility in Sylvania, creating an estimated 80 new jobs.
- Q Cells Co. Ltd. has opened a solar panel manufacturing plant in Cartersville that produces 16,000 solar panels a day, enough to generate 3.3 gigawatts of power; combined with its Dalton plant, the investment tops $2.5 billion and employs 4,000 Georgians.
- The Department of Energy awarded an $80 million grant to AVAPCO LLC to fund construction of a larger plant in Thomaston capable of producing 1.2 million gallons of jet fuel annually plus sustainable material for the rubber industry.
- Hyundai Motor Group (HMG) and SK On selected a site in Cartersville for a new electric vehicle (EV) battery manufacturing facility that will supply Hyundai Motor Group’s plants in the U.S.; stakeholders estimate it will create more than 3,500 new jobs through approximately $4-5 billion of investment in Bartow County.
- Green Georgia LLC will create over 170 jobs at its new headquarters in Thomaston and invest $59 million; the company designs and manufactures low-carbon materials for prefabricated buildings including sustainable factories.
- Troy Acoustics Corporation will invest almost $40 million in a new manufacturing facility in Thomas County, adding 88 new jobs while retaining 17 positions; the company engineers sound-wall systems used in numerous industries.
- PepsiCo Beverages North America will invest $8.5 million in a new 172,000-square-foot warehouse and distribution facility, creating 50 new jobs for the region.
- Mimecri USA LLC will invest $6 million, create 30 jobs, and establish its new U.S. headquarters in Thomasville; the company develops and manufactures efficient steel and aluminum parts with investment casting, MIM, and machining.
- 80 Acres Farms will create 150 new jobs through a $120 million investment for vertical farming to meet growing consumer demand for local fresh produce.
- Okabashi Brands Inc. will create approximately 340 new jobs as it reinvests in its existing Buford facility making comfort footwear that is 100 percent recyclable and contains on average 25 percent recycled content.
- Procter & Gamble Distributing LLC is planning to invest $205 million in a new distribution facility in Jackson, creating 350 new jobs in Butts County.
- Renewable Biomass Group will construct a $95-million wood pellet facility in Adel, initially creating 60 new jobs.
- Battery Resourcers will invest $43 million to build the largest battery recycling plant in North America; the lithium-ion battery recycling and engineered materials startup will open its first commercial-scale plant in the Covington area and create 150 jobs.
- SKC, a subsidiary of SK Group, announced it will invest more than $473 million in Newton County to manufacture glass-based substrates for semiconductor chips and create over 400 new jobs.
- Microsoft Corp. will develop the East Point Data Center, a 250,000-square-foot facility situated on a 125-acre site within Southmeadow Business Park, with an estimated project value of $420 million.
- Ecopol will invest more than $38 million and create 130 new jobs at its first North American facility in Griffin, manufacturing water-soluble and biodegradable film for consumer goods.
- Roper Corporation will invest $118 million in expanding its manufacturing operations in LaFayette, creating an additional 600 jobs.
- Remington Firearms is moving its global headquarters along with a new advanced manufacturing operation and research & development center; RemArms will invest $100 million and create 865 jobs in Troup County over a 5-year period.
- HelloFresh will establish its first distribution center in Newnan in a 208,930-square-foot facility, potentially bringing more than 750 jobs in meal distribution, assembly lines, and management roles.
- Georgia ranked #1 for 12 consecutive years in the Top States for Doing Business rankings by Area Development, placing in the top 10 in 14 categories and earning the No. 1 spot in seven classifications including Energy Availability and Costs, Overall Cost of Doing Business, Competitive Labor Market, Workforce Development Programs, Business Incentive Programs, Logistics and Infrastructure, and Cooperative and Responsive State Government.
