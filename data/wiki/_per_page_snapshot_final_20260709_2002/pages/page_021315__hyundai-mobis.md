# Hyundai Mobis

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2022-11-23

**Source:** [Hyundai invests nearly $1 billion to create another new plant in Bryan County  - Grice Connect](https://www.griceconnect.com/business/hyundai-invests-nearly-1-billion-to-create-another-new-plant-in-bryan-county-6506037)  ·  `page_021315`

## Facts

- Hyundai Mobis is investing nearly $1 billion to build an electric vehicle (EV) plant in Bryan County.
- The investment will create over 1,500 good-paying jobs.
- Governor Brian Kemp announced the Hyundai Mobis investment on Nov. 22.
- This is the second investment from Hyundai in less than a month.
- The Motor Group Metaplant America broke ground on an EV and battery manufacturing facility in late October.
- The facility will supply over 900,000 EV power electric systems annually.
- The facility will supply 450,000 Integrated Charging Control Units annually.
- The plant will be located at the Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development Certified Belfast Commerce Park in Richmond Hill.
- Jon Seagraves is the chairman of the development authority for Bryan County.
- H.S. Oh is the vice president of Hyundai Mobis Electric Powertrain Business Unit.
- The new plant will be an expansion of the company’s existing footprint in the state.
- Since 2009, Hyundai Mobis has operated a manufacturing facility in West Point.
- The Tier 1 supplier for Kia Georgia employs 1,200 employees.
- Since 2020, more than 30 EV-related projects have contributed over $13.3 billion in investment in Georgia.
- H.S. Oh stated that Hyundai Mobis’ investment project reflects an acceleration in the development of the EV supply chain in Georgia’s auto industry.
- H.S. Oh stated that the company will be a major production player in the EV market and expects to trigger more growth within the sector.
- H.S. Oh stated that Mobis is looking forward to providing high-quality work opportunities to the growing local workforce.
