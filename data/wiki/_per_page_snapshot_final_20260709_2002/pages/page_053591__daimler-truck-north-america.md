# Daimler Truck North America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-05-17

**Source:** [Daimler Staying Put in Oregon With Planned $40M Swan Island Expansion – Oregon Business](https://oregonbusiness.com/daimler-staying-put-in-oregon-with-planned-40m-swan-island-expansion/)  ·  `page_053591`

## Facts

- Daimler opted to expand its Portland footprint after considering two other sites in the greater Portland area.
- Daimler Truck North America announced plans to construct a $40 million engineering facility and a $3 million training center at its Swan Island headquarters.
- The expansion aims to bolster the company's electric truck manufacturing operations in Oregon.
- A Daimler representative stated the decision to expand on Swan Island was made to aid in collaboration.
- Construction on the engineering facility will begin in the third quarter of this year and is expected to open in 2025.
- The Electric Vehicle Supply Equipment training center is expected to open in 2026, according to DTNA spokeswoman Whitney Anderson.
- Daimler announced plans for a fully electric truck in 2019.
- The company unveiled the eCascadia electric truck prototype in 2022, featuring a range of about 230 miles suitable for short- and medium-range shippers.
- Sales of the truck had fallen short of expectations by the beginning of last year.
- The company secured larger orders for the electric truck from customers including UPS and Sysco.
- Company representatives told the Portland City Council last summer that the expansion project will bring 150 high-wage jobs to the area.
- In August, the council granted Daimler an enterprise zone tax-break exemption and a $1.5 million grant for the training facility.
- The engineering facility received a $700,000 state grant, one condition of which is maintaining the current employee headcount in Portland.
- DTNA employs around 3,000 people in the Portland area.
- The 110,000-square-foot engineering facility will house multiple research projects under one roof.
- Research projects at the new facility will include the development of battery electric and hydrogen fueled vehicles.
- In 1981, the German company purchased Freightliner, a Portland-based manufacturer of heavy duty trucks.
- Freightliner spun off from Daimler Trucks AG, the world’s largest manufacturer of commercial vehicles, in 2021.
- In 2016, Daimler opened a $150-million regional headquarters that received $20 million in tax incentives.
- The regional headquarters has become a hub for electric vehicle research in the years since its opening.
- The company operates its High Desert Proving Ground facility for new technologies in Madras.
- The 249-acre proving ground features 3.5 miles of test track and 14 types of road conditions for testing various technologies including autonomous operation.
- The test track is intended to help simulate a vehicle’s entire eight-year life cycle in only six months.
- The test track is located in an enterprise zone, one of 74 in the state, that offers tax incentives for companies that bring jobs to an area.
- Autonomous driving is thought to be a potential boon for companies facing low job retention among commercial drivers, especially long-haul commercial drivers.
- Some tech companies have lately scaled back ambitions to develop driverless freight trucks.
- The trucking industry in Oregon is affected by a labor shortage, though not as acutely as other industries, according to the latest Oregon Talent Assessment.
- Job reskilling on technologies like route optimization and lane-assisted driving can aid in hiring, according to the assessment developed by the state in partnership with consultancy ECONorthwest.
- Trucking employers report the top challenge with job reskilling is motivating employees to participate in training programs, followed by balancing training needs with current business operations.
