# Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Economic Development Partners | Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority](https://timber.jeffersoncountyalliance.com/about-us/economic-development-partners/)  ·  `page_055948`

## Facts

- The Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority maintains a structured network of economic development partners to support regional industrial and supply-chain projects.
- City of Dublin leadership includes Mayor Joshua Kight and City Manager Josh Powell.
- Laurens County Board of Commissioners is led by Chairman Jeff Davis and County Administrator Bryan Rogers.
- State of Georgia officials involved in regional economic development include Governor Brian P. Kemp, Lieutenant Governor Burt Jones, Secretary of State Brad Raffensperger, GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson, Senator Larry Walker III (District 20), and Representative Matt Hatchett (District 150).
- Regional state agencies supporting industrial development include the Department of Community Affairs, OneGeorgia Authority, Georgia Tech Enterprise Innovation Institute, and Heart of Georgia Altamaha Regional Commission.
- Local economic development is coordinated by the Dublin-Laurens County Chamber of Commerce under President & CEO Heath Taylor, alongside the Downtown Dublin Development Authority with directors Glenda Berry and Miriam Lewis.
- Workforce development partners for manufacturing and industrial training include Oconee Fall Line Technical College, QuickStart, and the Georgia Department of Labor.
- Transportation infrastructure partners supporting logistics and supply chain include Genesee & Wyoming, Inc. and the Georgia Ports Authority.
- Utility partners available for industrial site development include Georgia Power Company, Georgia EMC, Oconee EMC, Little Ocmulgee EMC, Tri-County EMC, and Electric Cities of Georgia.
