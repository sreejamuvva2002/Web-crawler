# Bridgestone Americas

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Press Release Details](https://www.bridgestoneamericas.com/en/press-release-details.en.2022.bridgestone-bandag-achieve-record-growth)  ·  `page_018226`

## Facts

- Bridgestone Americas is on pace to achieve one of its highest year-over-year results in its Bandag business with a 9% increase over 2021.
- The company forecasts year-end sales of Bandag retreads will reach more than 7 million units in North America.
- Bridgestone anticipates continued growth in 2023 with a $60 million investment in its Abilene, Texas retread plant to increase production capacity.
- Retreading extends the life of premium tire casings, reduces environmental impact of end-of-life tires, maximizes fleet uptime, and mitigates overall operating costs.
- Retreading is a critical component for fleets to unlock ESG and sustainability goals contributing to a circular economy.
- In 2022, Bandag retreads are forecast to deliver environmental benefits of approximately 105 million gallons of oil saved.
- In 2022, Bandag retreads are forecast to deliver environmental benefits of approximately 290 million pounds of landfill waste avoided.
- In 2022, Bandag retreads are forecast to deliver environmental benefits of approximately 207 million pounds of tire waste avoided.
- In 2022, Bandag retreads are forecast to deliver environmental benefits of approximately 5.6 billion KwH of energy saved, which is the equivalent of powering 500,000 U.S. homes for a full year.
- Jason Roanhouse serves as executive director of Bandag Operations at Bridgestone Americas.
- Bridgestone announced a new $60 million investment in its Bandag tread manufacturing plant in Abilene, Texas.
- The Abilene plant expansion will add 50,000 square-feet and additional production days to meet accelerating demand for retread products.
- The expansion will increase the plant's retread output by 16%.
- Bridgestone introduced the all-new Bridgestone Last Mile Tire System (LMTS) in the second half of 2022.
- The LMTS program will begin utilizing retread for small and medium-sized fleets in Q1 2023.
- The goal of the LMTS program is to extend the benefits of retreading to a broader population of fleet customers.
- The LMTS allows smaller fleets operating out of the same terminal to realize greater economies of scale in their tire operations.
- The LMTS provides access to Bandag retread solutions and Bridgestone Fleet Care products.
- The LMTS program was launched in summer 2022 at the Route Consultant Expo, where more than 500 fleet contractors expressed interest as potential early adopters.
- Bandag operates over 200 retread manufacturing plants in North America.
- Bandag has a network of more than 1,100 authorized dealer commercial locations.
- Bandag represents nearly one in every two retreaded truck tires on North American roads today.
- Bandag’s proprietary 10-step manufacturing process has kept an estimated 300 million tires out of the waste stream and saved over 4 billion gallons of oil over the years.
- Bandag’s vertical integration with Bridgestone helped to reduce supply chain challenges during the COVID-19 pandemic and beyond.
- The continued expansion of Bridgestone Bandag retreading aligns with the Bridgestone E8 Commitment, which consists of 8 values starting with “E” (Energy, Ecology, Efficiency, Extension, Economy, Emotion, Ease, and Empowerment).
