# U.S. Department of Energy Transportation Technologies Office

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** research_and_development  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [Batteries | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/cmei/vehicles/batteries)  ·  `page_029841`

## Facts

- Improving batteries for electric drive vehicles, including hybrid electric (HEV) and plug-in electric vehicles (PEV), plays a key role in the country's transportation future.
- Transitioning to a light-duty fleet of HEVs and PEVs could reduce U.S. foreign oil dependence by 30-60%, depending on the exact mix of technologies.
- Further improvements in batteries could make electric drive vehicles more affordable and convenient to consumers.
- Some heavy-duty manufacturers are pursuing hybridization of medium and heavy-duty vehicles to improve fuel economy and reduce idling.
- The Transportation Technologies Office (TTO) focuses on reducing the cost, volume, and weight of batteries, while simultaneously improving vehicle battery performance (power, energy, and durability) and tolerance to abuse conditions.
- Reaching TTO's goals in battery areas and commercializing advanced energy storage technologies will allow more people to purchase and use electric drive vehicles.
- TTO pursues Exploratory Battery Materials Research, which addresses fundamental issues of materials and electrochemical interactions associated with lithium and beyond-lithium batteries.
- This exploratory research attempts to develop new and promising materials, use advanced material models to predict battery failure modes, and employ scientific diagnostic tools to understand why materials and systems fail.
- Building on these findings, the exploratory research works to develop ways to mitigate those failures.
- TTO pursues Applied Battery Research, which focuses on optimizing next-generation, high-energy lithium-ion electrochemistries that incorporate new battery materials.
- This applied research emphasizes identifying, diagnosing, and mitigating issues that negatively impact the performance and lifespan of cells using advanced materials.
- TTO pursues Advanced Battery Development, System Analysis, and Testing, which focuses on developing robust battery cells and modules to significantly reduce cost, increase lifespan, and improve performance.
- This development research aims to ensure battery systems meet specific goals for particular vehicle applications.
- The DOE's battery research builds upon decades of work conducted by the department in batteries and energy storage.
- Research supported by TTO led to modern nickel metal hydride batteries, which were used in nearly all first-generation hybrid electric vehicles.
- The office's research also helped develop the lithium-ion battery technology used in the Chevrolet Volt, the first commercially available plug-in hybrid electric vehicle.
- This lithium-ion battery technology is now being used in a variety of hybrid and plug-in electric vehicles coming to market, including the Ford Focus EV.
- The batteries subprogram works extensively with national laboratories and universities.
- Within the DOE, the office collaborates with the Office of Science and ARPA-e (Advanced Research Projects Agency-Energy).
- Across the federal government, the subprogram works with the Interagency Advanced Power Group.
- Across the federal government, the subprogram works with the Environmental Protection Agency.
- Across the federal government, the subprogram works with the National Aeronautics and Space Administration.
- Across the federal government, the subprogram works with the National Science Foundation.
- Across the federal government, the subprogram works with the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (Department of Transportation).
- Across the federal government, the subprogram works with the U.S. Army Tank, Automotive Research and Development and Engineering Center (Department of Defense).
- The subprogram collaborates on international research with the International Energy Agency's Hybrid & Electric Vehicle Technology Collaboration Programme.
- The subprogram collaborates on international research with the Clean Energy Ministerial's Electric Vehicle Initiative.
- The subprogram collaborates on international research through the Clean Energy Research Center bilateral agreement between the U.S. and China.
- Much of the subprogram's research is conducted in sync with industry partners through the U.S. DRIVE Partnership, focusing on light-duty vehicles.
- Much of the subprogram's research is conducted in sync with industry partners through the 21st Century Truck Partnership, focusing on heavy-duty vehicles.
- Much of the subprogram's research is conducted in sync with industry partners through the United States Advanced Battery Consortium (USABC), a partnership between the DOE, Chrysler, Ford, and General Motors to develop and demonstrate advanced battery technologies for hybrid and electric vehicles, as well as benchmark test emerging technologies.
- TTO's Batteries and Energy Storage subprogram aims to research new battery chemistry and cell technologies that can reduce the cost of electric vehicle batteries to less than $100/kWh—ultimately $80/kWh.
- TTO's Batteries and Energy Storage subprogram aims to research new battery chemistry and cell technologies that can increase the range of electric vehicles to 300 miles.
- TTO's Batteries and Energy Storage subprogram aims to research new battery chemistry and cell technologies that can decrease charge time to 15 minutes or less.
