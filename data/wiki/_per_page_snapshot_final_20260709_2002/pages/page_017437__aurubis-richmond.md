# Aurubis Richmond

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- Aurubis has opened the first secondary smelter specializing in multimetal recycling in the US.
- The company made an around $800 million targeted investment in expanding its own recycling business.
- The state-of-the-art plant is located in Augusta (Richmond County) in the state of Georgia.
- The facility will process about 180,000 t of complex recycling materials annually.
- The plant supports Aurubis’ commitment to the circular economy and its goal of achieving carbon neutrality well before 2050.
- Construction on the site started in mid-2022.
- Aurubis Richmond celebrated a ribbon-cutting ceremony in September 2024.
- The first stage of the site will be gradually commissioned during fiscal year 2024/25.
- Completion of the second module is scheduled for 2026.
- The recycling system's technology and processing capabilities position Aurubis as a pioneer in sustainable multimetal recycling in the US.
- The facility uses sophisticated off-gas cleaning technologies, including post-combustion SNCR (Selective Non-Catalytic Reduction) and an off-gas scrubber, to safely destroy pollutants in the primary off-gas.
- The facility is designed to achieve zero effluent discharge.
- It will use onsite water treatment to recover wastewater and reuse the cleaned wastewater in various production processes.
- Rainwater from the factory premises will be collected and reused for production processes.
- Management systems are currently being developed in accordance with ISO 9001, ISO 14001, ISO 45001, and ISO 50001 standards to be implemented and certified.
