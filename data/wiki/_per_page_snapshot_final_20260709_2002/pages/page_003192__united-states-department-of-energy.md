# United States Department of Energy

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [Biden-Harris Administration Announces First Projects Receiving Clean Energy Manufacturing Investments in America's Industrial and Energy Communities | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/articles/biden-harris-administration-announces-first-projects-receiving-clean-energy-manufacturing)  ·  `page_003192`

## Facts

- The U.S. Department of Energy released details for 35 projects across 20 states that voluntarily shared they received a total of $1.93 billion in allocations of the Qualifying Advanced Energy Project Credit (48C).
- The 48C credit is an allocated tax credit funded by President Biden’s Investing in America agenda through the Inflation Reduction Act to accelerate clean energy manufacturing and recycling and reduce greenhouse gas emissions at industrial facilities.
- The announced projects address critical needs across grid components, electric vehicle components and chargers, solar components, clean steel, critical materials processing and recycling, and other clean energy products.
- Seven of the announced projects are located in traditional energy communities, which include communities with closed coal mines or coal plants.
- Projects receiving a 48C tax credit must meet prevailing wage and registered apprenticeship requirements to receive the full 30 percent investment tax credit.
- U.S. Secretary of Energy Jennifer M. Granholm stated the administration is executing an industrial strategy prioritizing rebuilding the domestic manufacturing sector and creating good-paying jobs across the country.
- U.S. Secretary of the Treasury Janet Yellen stated President Biden’s economic agenda ensures all communities benefit from clean energy growth by driving innovation and investment, strengthening energy security, and lowering energy costs for American families and small businesses.
- John Podesta, Senior Advisor to the President for International Climate Policy, stated the announcement represents a direct, targeted investment in energy communities and manufacturing across the nation.
- The Inflation Reduction Act expanded the Qualifying Advanced Energy Project Credit (48C) to provide an additional credit allocation of $10 billion, with $4 billion set aside for projects in designated energy communities.
- On March 29, 2024, the IRS allocated approximately $4 billion of 48C credits for over 100 projects across 35 states, with approximately $1.5 billion allocated to projects in designated energy communities.
- The 48C(e) program can release names and allocation amounts only after project certification, a process that can take up to two years.
- As of April 18, 2024, 35 projects voluntarily self-disclosed information to the DOE to share publicly ahead of certification.
- Highland Materials, Inc. in Surgoinsville, TN, located in an Energy Community, will initially produce 16,000 Metric Tons per year of solar grade polysilicon at less than standard cost with a 90% reduction in carbon emissions.
- At full capacity, the Highland Materials project will produce 20,000 MT/year, equivalent to 11 GW of solar cells.
- Highland Materials is working closely with Northeast State Community College to develop and implement workforce development including DOL certified apprenticeship programs and community support programs.
- The Highland Materials project received a credit amount of $255.6 Million.
- Mobis North America electrified Powertrain, LLC (MNAe) in Richmond Hill, GA, will construct three different electric vehicle component plants to provide parts for Hyundai and Kia electric vehicles.
- The awarded MNAe plant in Richmond Hill will specifically produce Power Electric Systems (PE System), which are integral to the production of electric vehicles.
- The MNAe project received a credit amount of $57.6 Million.
- Eaton Corporation in Waukesha, WI, will increase manufacturing capacity for three-phase transformers used for utility power grids, data centers, and large commercial and industrial applications.
- Eaton's equipment will provide critical support for electric grid resilience, renewable energy projects, and electric vehicle charging stations.
- Eaton works closely with Waukesha County Technical College to create education and training programs for manufacturing careers, including coil winding.
- The Eaton project received a credit amount of $1.33 Million.
- Prysmian in Williamsport, PA, will reequip and expand an existing facility to increase manufacturing capacity of advanced transmission conductors, enabling production of thousands of metric tons per year.
- Prysmian's conductors are expected to supply the Grain Belt Express project, a long-distance high-voltage direct-current transmission line delivering clean electricity to the Midwest.
- The Prysmian project received a credit amount of $3.89 Million.
- ArcelorMittal Calvert LLC in Calvert, AL, will build a new advanced manufacturing facility in an Energy Community to produce high-quality non-grain oriented electrical steel (NOES).
- NOES is a critical material for production of electric motors used to power battery electric vehicles, plug-in hybrid electric vehicles, hybrid vehicles, and other clean energy technologies.
- ArcelorMittal Calvert will partner with local educational and training institutions to develop tailored workforce development programs.
- The ArcelorMittal Calvert project received a credit amount of $280.5 Million.
- Nth Cycle Inc. in Fairfield, OH, will provide the nation’s first large-scale production of Mixed Hydroxide Precipitate (MHP) using recycled and scrap materials from Li-ion batteries, spent catalysts, and alloy scraps and slags.
- The Nth Cycle process will provide critical nickel and cobalt materials for EV battery production.
- The Nth Cycle project received a credit amount of $7.22 Million.
- Middlesex County Utilities Authority in Sayreville, NJ, will increase the efficiency of a solid waste handling process of wastewater treatment through a new Advanced Anaerobic Digestion Facility, increasing renewable energy use and reducing emissions by approximately 23%.
- The Middlesex County Utilities Authority construction contracts will include project labor agreements, support apprenticeships, and engage with businesses owned by minorities, women, veterans, and local small business owners.
- The Middlesex County Utilities Authority project received a credit amount of $40.5 Million.
- Topsoe SOEC Production US Inc. in Chester, VA, will significantly expand U.S. electrolyzer manufacturing capacity to meet growing demand for green hydrogen.
- Electrolyzers are key for decarbonizing energy-intensive industries like steel, mining, and long-distance transportation, which account for approximately 30% of global greenhouse gas emissions.
- The Topsoe SOEC Production US Inc. project received a credit amount of $135.9 Million.
- Projects are partnering with training and education organizations, such as community colleges and career technical centers, to develop a skilled workforce for the clean energy and manufacturing sectors.
- Community benefit agreements, project labor agreements, and impact agreements identifying specific benefits for workers and local communities are being utilized across the portfolio.
- The U.S. Department of Treasury and Internal Revenue Service anticipate issuing guidance for the second round of the 48C program in the coming weeks.
- A submission window for required concept papers for the second round is anticipated to open by summer.
- Companies may submit an opt-in form to the DOE to be publicly listed as recipients of the 48C program.
- The Qualifying Advanced Energy Project Credit (48C) is managed by the DOE’s Office of Manufacturing and Energy Supply Chains.
