# Hwashin Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-11-01

**Source:** [Auto Parts Supplier Hwashin to Bring 460 Jobs to Middle Georgia - AllOnGeorgia](https://allongeorgia.com/georgia-business/auto-parts-supplier-hwashin-to-bring-460-jobs-to-middle-georgia/)  ·  `page_055980`

## Facts

- Hwashin, an automotive parts company, will establish a new manufacturing facility in Dublin.
- The project will create over 460 new jobs.
- The project involves more than $176 million in investment in Laurens County.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated the goal to become the e-mobility capital of the nation is bringing opportunity to communities all across the state.
- Hwashin is the latest company to join Georgia's growing ecosystem of companies bringing good jobs in this industry, according to Governor Kemp.
- Hwashin stands as a Tier-1 supplier with a specialization in crafting chassis and body parts for Hyundai Motor Group, Kia, and international original equipment manufacturers (OEMs).
- The company operates from its headquarters in Korea and has established production facilities spanning the United States, Asia, and South America.
- Seo Jin Jung is CEO of Hwashin.
- Jung stated Hwashin aspires to shape a future global city focused on electric vehicles (EVs), drawing from 50 years of expertise and collaboration with Georgia and Laurens County.
- The alliance seeks to position the region at the forefront of the evolving automobile industry, according to Jung.
- Jung stated Hwashin will take on the role of a reliable partner to customers and an engaged contributor to the local community.
- Jung expressed belief in the company's capacity to contribute to community prosperity and the American auto parts industry, extending gratitude to the State of Georgia and Laurens County for ongoing cooperation and support throughout the project.
- Hwashin’s new Georgia facility is scheduled to begin production in late 2025.
- The facility will be located at the Georgia Highway 257 Rail Served Site, the first Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) Select site in the state.
- A “GRAD Certified Select” status indicates a site has met or exceeded more rigorous certification requirements.
- The facility will be primarily focused on manufacturing chassis components for OEMs, including Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America and Kia Georgia.
- Dublin Mayor Joshua Kight stated the City of Dublin is pleased to welcome Hwashin to the community.
- Mayor Kight praised the staff and board of the Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority for demonstrating why Dublin-Laurens County is a great place to do business.
- Mayor Kight stated the manufacturing facility will become an economic engine for the region, leading to more employment opportunities and quality growth.
- Jeff Davis is Chairman of the Laurens County Board of Commissioners.
- Chairman Davis welcomed Hwashin as the seventh international company to call Dublin and Laurens County home.
- Davis cited the partnership and “can-do attitude” between Laurens County, the City of Dublin, and the Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority.
- Davis stated the project will change the landscape for Dublin-Laurens County by providing new career opportunities for area residents while developing increased opportunities in housing and retail development.
- Griff Lynch is President and CEO of the Georgia Ports Authority.
- Lynch stated significant investments from Hwashin and other automotive suppliers are delivering career opportunities that will allow Georgians to thrive at home.
- Lynch expressed pride in supporting economic development throughout the state and welcomed Hwashin to the manufacturing community.
- Lynch stated the Georgia Ports Authority's mission is to efficiently connect companies like Hwashin to international markets, noting the announcement as evidence of Georgia’s position as a global business center.
- Alyce Thornhill is Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy at the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team.
- Thornhill represented GDEcD on this competitive project in partnership with the Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia EMC, and Georgia Quick Start.
- Pat Wilson is a GDEcD Commissioner.
- Commissioner Wilson stated Hwashin will have direct interstate access to at least two long-term automotive partners from its chosen site.
- Wilson noted the company can pursue an accelerated construction timeline by locating on a GRAD Select site.
- Wilson cited decades of elected leadership supporting infrastructure and workforce development as areas where Georgia excels against competition.
- Wilson praised local officials and teams for their role in attracting jobs of the future.
- For over a century, Georgia has fostered healthy industry practices, encouraged collaboration and innovation, and positioned itself as a leader in developing emerging technologies for the automotive and mobility industry.
- In fiscal year 2023, job creation in the automotive industry increased by 324 percent compared to FY21.
- Suppliers attracted by Georgia’s mobility OEMs resulted in over $2 billion in investment across the state.
- As the electric vehicle market continues to grow, Georgia has pursued the entire supply chain, creating more than $25.1 billion in investments and 29,000 jobs since 2020.
- Since its inception in 1975, Hwashin Co., Ltd. has held a prominent position in the Korean auto parts industry for nearly five decades.
- The company maintains a specialized focus on chassis and body parts and actively engages in research and development.
- Hwashin continuously expanded its product offerings to encompass lightweight aluminum components and eco-friendly solutions aligned with the global automotive industry’s eco-conscious direction.
- Hwashin Co., Ltd. employs more than 4,200 personnel worldwide.
