# Aurubis Richmond at Augusta Corporate Park

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Aurubis announces expansion of its Augusta copper smelting plant - The Augusta Press](https://theaugustapress.com/aurubis-announces-expansion-of-its-augusta-copper-smelting-plant/)  ·  `page_017300`

## Facts

- German copper producer Aurubis is expanding its Augusta facility even further.
- The company broke ground on its metal recycling plant, Aurubis Richmond at Augusta Corporate Park in June.
- The facility is an investment of about $340 million.
- Aurubis estimated that it will process some 90,000 tons of complex metals annually, including those used for printed circuit boards and copper cables.
- That number is doubling now as the company plans to add approximately an additional $265 million toward fast-tracking its expansion.
- Aurubis now projects this will increase throughput volume to some 180,000 tons of complex recycling materials per year.
- Earlier this year when construction began, the company touted 125 jobs the new facility would bring to Augusta.
- With the expansion, now it expects to bring about 200 employees to the facility once both phases of construction are complete.
- Aurubis expects the first phase of its Augusta site to come online in mid-2024.
- The company expects its expansion to be complete by 2026.
- With this expansion, and considering capital expenditure adjustment for infrastructure requirements and inflation, Aurubis aims to put about $640 million into its operations in Georgia.
- Aurubis expects to generate operative earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation and amortization of approximately $180 million starting in fiscal year 2026-27.
