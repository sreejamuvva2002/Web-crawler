# Georgia State Tax Credits and Incentive Programs

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2023-06

**Source:** [Maximizing Your Benefits: An Overview of Georgia Tax Credits and Incentives | SPARK Blog | ADP](https://www.adp.com/spark/articles/2023/06/maximizing-your-benefits-an-overview-of-georgia-tax-credits-and-incentives.aspx)  ·  `page_015345`

## Facts

- The state of Georgia offers tax credits and incentives for businesses that want to expand, relocate, or start operations in the Peach State.
- The Georgia Jobs Tax Credit (GAJTC) rewards job creation within the state by providing $750 - $5,250 per new full-time job created each year for up to five years, depending on location and eligibility requirements.
- Georgia counties and census tracts are ranked as economic tiers based on unemployment rate, per capita income, and percentage of residents whose incomes are below the poverty level.
- Businesses located in Tier 1 counties, military zones, opportunity zones, or less developed census tracts can use GAJTC to offset income tax or reduce payroll withholding obligations.
- Companies may be eligible for GAJTC if they create new full-time jobs in Georgia that are at least 35 hours a week, offer health insurance, and pay more than the lowest county average wage.
- GAJTC can offset 50% to 100% of state income tax liability with a 10-year carry-forward period.
- All industries are eligible for GAJTC in the 40 least-developed counties and other specifically designated zones.
- Beyond designated zones, employers generating jobs must operate in one of eight qualified industries: Manufacturing, Tourism, Processing, Telecommunications, Broadcasting, Warehousing and distribution, Elderly and disability services, or Research and development.
- The GAJTC county credit amount ranges from $750 to $4,000.
- Companies located in Tier 1 counties and specially designated areas can apply the base job tax credit against employee withholding taxes after corporate income tax has been eliminated.
- Georgia's Quality Jobs Tax Credit (GQJTC) provides a significant tax break for eligible companies across all industries if created jobs pay at least 10% above the average county wage and are considered high-paying jobs.
- GQJTC provides a 5-year credit for each qualifying job added, provided the jobs are maintained.
- GQJTC can be used to offset up to 100% of a company's total Georgia income tax liability.
- GQJTC can be applied against employee withholding after the income tax benefit has been exhausted.
- A one-year look back is available for GQJTC claims.
- Unused GQJTC credits can be carried forward for up to 10 years.
- GQJTC cannot be combined with the Georgia investment tax credit for the same project or used for the same jobs claimed under GAJTC, but may be used along with the Georgia retraining tax credit.
- The GQJTC equals $2,500 to $5,000 per job every year for five years, based on the increase in average jobs over the previous year's average.
- To qualify for GQJTC, a company must create at least 50 net new qualified quality jobs over a period of two years, exceeding the previous 12-month period's average.
- Acquiring employees through a merger does not count toward the GQJTC credit.
- For rural counties only, ten (10) new Tier 1 county quality jobs and 25 Tier 2 county new quality jobs must be created to qualify for GQJTC.
- GQJTC requires paying at least 110% of the average wage in the county where the jobs were created.
- Businesses that have claimed QJTC previously must create at least 50 net new qualified quality jobs over a 24-month period and make at least a $2.5 million investment in qualified investment property to start a new period of eligibility.
- The Georgia retraining tax credit (GARTC) enables businesses to offset their investment in employee training for new equipment, new technology, or competitive positioning.
- Any business that files a Georgia income tax return is eligible for GARTC if training programs are designed to enhance quality and productivity, teach certain software technologies, and are approved by the technical college system of Georgia.
- Eligible GARTC expenses include costs of instructors and teaching materials, employee wages during training, and reasonable travel expenses.
- GARTC provides a tax credit of 50 percent of direct training expenses.
- GARTC provides up to $500 credit per full-time employee per training program.
- The annual maximum for the GARTC amounts to $1,250 per employee.
- Unused GARTC credits can be carried forward for ten years.
- The investment tax credit applies to employers making an investment in certain types of manufacturing processes or equipment or providing telecommunications services.
- Depending on the county, the investment tax credit ranges from 1% to 5% of qualified investment expenses.
- To qualify for the investment tax credit, a company must have operated a manufacturing or telecommunications facility in Georgia for at least three years and make a minimum $100,000 investment.
- Qualifying businesses in rural counties that meet certain criteria can utilize the investment tax credit against payroll withholding.
- Eligible expenses for the investment tax credit include land acquisitions, improvements, buildings, machinery, and equipment.
- Companies in certain designated counties may also be able to apply excess investment tax credits to payroll withholding.
- The Georgia research & development (R&D) tax credit applies to facilities engaged in or headquartered in manufacturing, warehousing and distribution, tourism, telecommunications, broadcasting, or companies engaged in qualified R&D activities.
- The Georgia R&D tax credit equals 10% of qualified R&D expenditures over a base amount.
- The value of the R&D credit is determined based on qualifying expenditures each year along with a gross receipts test.
- Qualifying R&D expenditures include the purchase of materials and supplies, fees for Georgia-based contract work, and the portion of wages and salaries of personnel, direct management, and direct support dedicated to in-state R&D activities.
- The port tax credit bonus applies if a company increases imports or exports through a Georgia port by 10% and qualifies for GAJTC or the investment tax credit.
- To qualify for the port tax credit bonus, a company's base port traffic amount must equal at least 10 TEUs (twenty-foot equivalent units).
- The required 10% increase in port traffic can be measured against the previous year or base year.
- When paired with GAJTC, the port tax credit amounts to an additional $1,250 credit per new job per year for five years, as long as both the job and higher level of port traffic are maintained.
