## Facts

- Essex Furukawa released its second annual Sustainability Report on 31 Ogos 2022 related to Vision 2030: Masa Depan Lestari, Kuasa Penggerak Kita.
- The 29 muka surat (page) report emphasizes increased reliance on renewable energy and carbon reduction achievements.
- Progress is shown toward achieving Zero Waste to Landfill across the global manufacturing footprint.
- Future initiatives regarding Scope 3 emissions, supply chain data, and ESG projects are introduced.
- Austin Robinson, Global Corporate Sustainability Manager for Essex Furukawa, collected data with help from business unit heads and third-party verification partners.
- Robinson stated that achievements only happened with strong support, noting significant company-wide changes and continuous sharing and implementation of best practices.
- The company observed a significant increase in social sustainability as more employees recognized their role in the future environment and local community.
- Essex Furukawa created its holistic sustainability program with three core values: Disruptive Innovation, Green Manufacturing, and Agile Digitalization.
- These core values are designed to drive industry change and innovate the magnet wire industry while considering environmental impact.
- The report displays last year's results and how each impacts progress toward the holistic Vision 2030 program.
- Key goals revolve around achieving Zero Waste to Landfill, Energy Reduction, Renewable Energy adoption, and Carbon Reduction tied to Green Manufacturing.
- Essex Furukawa prioritizes Agile Digitalization while collaborating with customers and R&D leaders to advance Disruptive Innovation in the magnet wire industry.
- Daniel Choi, CEO of Essex Furukawa, stated that leadership in Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) requires the transparency offered by the report.
- Choi noted the ESG landscape is constantly evolving and the company feels responsible to stakeholders to be agile and accountable.
- Choi added that across all perspectives and metrics, they have succeeded and want to lead by example, set standards, and not just follow them.
