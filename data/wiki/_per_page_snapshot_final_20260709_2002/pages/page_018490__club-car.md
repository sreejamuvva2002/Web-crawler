## Facts

- Club Car is a global industry-leading manufacturer of small-wheel, zero emissions vehicles.
- Club Car celebrated the opening of the new Club Car Appling Manufacturing Facility with tours and a ribbon cutting ceremony.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp and First Lady Marty Kemp attended the facility opening event.
- State and local elected officials and area business leaders joined the tour of the new manufacturing facility.
- Attendees toured the facility, learned about the Club Car Urban vehicle, and participated in a ribbon cutting ceremony.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Club Car is bringing jobs into Georgia in the growing e-mobility sector and leading the way in compact utility EV manufacturing.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Club Car's growth and investment are a testament to efforts to make Georgia the number one state to do business.
- The Club Car Appling Manufacturing Facility provides 105,000 sq. ft. of flexible production and warehouse storage space.
- Club Car opened the new facility to improve customer experience as demand for the Club Car Urban vehicle increases in the United States.
- Club Car has committed nearly $8 million in capital investments into the facility over a two-year period.
- The new facility currently operates a state-of-the-art production line.
- The facility can produce up to 20 vehicles per day.
- Club Car President and CEO Mark Wagner stated that their innovative vehicles are impacting commercial fleet electrification, mini-mobility, and last mile delivery.
- Club Car has been a Georgia-based company for more than sixty years.
- Club Car President and CEO Mark Wagner stated the company is proud of the quality, safety, and reliability built into every Club Car product.
