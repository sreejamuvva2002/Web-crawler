# Georgia NEVI EV Charging Infrastructure Funding Program

**Type:** Government Program  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[charging-infrastructure|Charging Infrastructure]]  ·  **Location:** Georgia, United States

## Overview

The Georgia State Transportation Board awarded $24.4 million in federal NEVI funding to build 26 new fast-charging stations across the state, targeting rural and underserved corridors.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Funding to establish 26 new EV fast-charging stations, expanding Georgia’s charging network along major highways.

## Investment

- **$24.4 million** (20 November) — Establish 26 new EV fast-charging stations
  - Awarded by the Georgia State Transportation Board under the NEVI programme to support public-private partnership deployment.

## Timeline

- **20 November** — Georgia State Transportation Board announced $24.4 million award for 26 new EV fast-charging stations.
- **2025** — Planned start of installation for the 26 new charging stations.

## Key facts

- Georgia's multi-year plan focuses on rural and underserved areas where private investment in EV charging has lagged.
- The NEVI programme allocates up to 80% of project costs, with Georgia receiving approximately $135 million nationwide.
- The new stations will support the state's goal of reliable charging access every 50 miles along designated corridors.
- Public-private partnership model ensures long-term operation and maintenance for at least five years.
- Georgia Power has piloted vehicle-to-grid technology and smart grid integration, providing data for scaling EV infrastructure.
- Each station will feature multiple DC fast chargers compatible with various EV models and charging standards.
