# Yachiyo Industry Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2014-03-31

**Source:** [Yachiyo Industry Co., Ltd. Business Report FY ended 2014 - MarkLines Automotive Industry Portal](https://www.marklines.com/en/top500/cf/s500_209_hl2013)  ·  `page_015571`

## Facts

- Yachiyo Industry Co., Ltd. announced plans to set up a new plant in Georgia in the United States.
- The new Georgia facility will take over the fuel tank business from Yachiyo Manufacturing of Alabama, LLC (YMA).
- The Georgia plant will manufacture 400,000 plastic fuel tanks per year for Honda's facility in Alabama.
- The Georgia plant will include a sunroof production line with an annual capacity of 200,000 units to meet growing North American demand.
- Yachiyo announced it signed an agreement to sell the sheet metal components business of YMA to Unipres Corporation.
- Unipres is a Nissan-affiliated structural components manufacturer seeking to expand business with Honda by strengthening its North American operations.
- Yachiyo will expand its resin parts business, developing and marketing body panels and interior parts as a pillar of its midterm business plan starting in April 2014.
- Automakers are increasingly using resin materials for outer panels to develop lighter and fuel-efficient vehicles.
- Yachiyo will enter the hot stamped parts market by developing low-cost mass production technologies for high-tensile vehicle body frame parts with a tensile strength of 1,500MPa.
- The company will use an electrical heating method to form steel for hot stamped parts to reduce production costs.
- Yachiyo's decision to enter the hot stamped parts market was driven by Honda Motor Co., Ltd.'s introduction of these parts into mini vehicle production.
- Yachiyo is developing plastic liners for high-pressure hydrogen tanks for fuel cell vehicles (FCVs) to replace aluminum liners and lower production costs.
- The company plans to enter the FCV parts market with its plastic liner technology in anticipation of wider hydrogen tank adoption on vehicles.
- Yachiyo developed a plastic fuel tank system with very low fuel-permeability that meets California's new Low-Emission Vehicle (LEV) Program regulations.
- A new manufacturing method for the low-permeability fuel tanks, which installs valves and tubes before blow-molding, eliminates the need to create holes in the tank after molding.
- The innovative production technique for the new fuel tanks resulted in a 12 percent reduction in hydrocarbon permeability.
- Stricter LEV III emission guidelines are scheduled to be adopted in California in 2014.
- Yachiyo will accelerate marketing efforts for its new fuel tank by highlighting its environmental advantages.
- Planned capital investment for YMA (Alabama, USA) is 3,067 million yen from August 2013 to December 2014 to respond to new business and increase production capacity.
- Planned capital investment for US Yachiyo Inc. (Ohio, USA) is 486 million yen from April 2013 to March 2015 to renew facilities, reduce energy consumption, rationalize operations, and respond to new business.
