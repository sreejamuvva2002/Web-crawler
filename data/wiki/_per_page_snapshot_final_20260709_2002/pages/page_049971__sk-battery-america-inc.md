## Facts

- SK Battery America Inc. has laid off nearly 1,000 workers at its battery manufacturing plant in Commerce, Georgia.
- The layoffs coincide with automakers revising their electrification strategies and uncertainty around consumer demand for electric vehicles.
- A notice was filed under the Worker Adjustment and Retraining Notification Act (WARN) by human resources chief Chuck Moore.
- Friday marked the final working day for exactly 958 employees.
- The affected workers will continue to receive pay through May 6.
- SK Battery America Inc. opened its $2.6 billion plant in January 2022.
- The Commerce, Georgia plant supplies batteries for the electric pickup Ford F-150 Lightning produced by Ford Motor Company.
- In December, Ford Motor Company announced it would cancel the fully electric version of the F-150 model and instead introduce an extended-range variant.
- Industry observers note the decision reflects slower-than-expected growth in the U.S. electric vehicle market and shifts in federal policy.
- Under the administration of Donald Trump, the government has moved away from aggressive electrification targets while supporting the oil and gas sector.
- The Trump administration is proposing to ease emissions standards for automakers.
- SK Battery America Inc. and Ford Motor Company had previously announced a joint investment of $11.4 billion to build battery plants in the United States.
- The partnership between SK Battery America Inc. and Ford Motor Company ended in December.
- In 2020, SK Battery America Inc. revealed plans to invest $940 million to expand battery manufacturing in the Atlanta area.
- The 2020 Atlanta area expansion plan was expected to create around 600 jobs.
- SK Group and Hyundai Motor Company are continuing development of a $5 billion battery factory near Cartersville.
- The state of Georgia has attracted a $5 billion EV factory investment by Rivian.
- The state of Georgia has attracted a $7.6 billion EV manufacturing complex investment by Hyundai Motor Company.
- EV demand continues to grow but has fallen short of earlier projections.
- Electric vehicles accounted for about 8% of new vehicle sales in the United States in 2025.
- The 8% EV market share in 2025 was roughly unchanged from the previous year.
- Automakers such as Ford Motor Company, General Motors, and Stellantis have begun reassessing multibillion-dollar electrification strategies.
- These automakers are delaying or revising factory projects and investments due to shifting demand patterns.
- Hybrid and more fuel-efficient gasoline vehicles are gaining popularity among buyers concerned about driving range.
- Buyers are also concerned about the availability of charging infrastructure for electric vehicles.
- The U.S. government has removed tax credits of up to $7,500 for new and used EV purchases.
- The removal of EV tax credits could further affect the pace of EV adoption in the market.
