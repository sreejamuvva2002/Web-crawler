# Evatran

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** charging_infrastructure  ·  **Published:** 2011-06-01

**Source:** [The 2013 EV will recharge wirelessly thanks to Evatran, Yazaki](https://www.torquenews.com/397/2013-ev-will-recharge-wirelessly-thanks-evatran-yazaki)  ·  `page_015891`

## Facts

- The agreement commercializes the wireless charging system developed by Evatran, making it a factory or dealership option.
- Electric vehicles will park over a floor-mounted charging pad for wireless charging without needing a physical plug.
- Induction technology is utilized to create a convenient wireless charging system specifically designed for high-power vehicle applications.
- Plugless Power technology transfers up to 3.3kW of power, the level required by the typical electric vehicle.
- The wireless charging system operates at an efficiency level over 90 percent.
- The technology ensures vehicles recharge as quickly as with traditional methods.
- Rebecca Hough, executive vice president of Sales and Product Development at Evatran, stated that the partnership guarantees their technology will have the highest level of design reviews, performance testing, and vehicle integration engineering.
- Jim Romine, executive vice president of Engineering at Yazaki North America, stated that Yazaki is proud to be one of the first suppliers committed to assessing and commercializing new technologies for customers, with a specific focus on sustainable initiatives and hybrid electric vehicles.
- Jim Romine also stated that Yazaki looks forward to using its technical expertise in the automotive sector to bring this technology to the emerging electric vehicle market.
- Yazaki North America is a Tier 1 automotive supplier headquartered in Canton, Michigan.
- Yazaki North America is a subsidiary of the Yazaki Corporation, which is headquartered in Tokyo.
- Yazaki is the world's largest supplier of automotive electrical distribution systems (EDS).
- Yazaki has been at the forefront of the electric vehicle market since its inception.
- Yazaki has years of experience in high voltage system integration and development.
