# First Solar

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [First Solar cashes in with sale of IRA tax credits… | Canary Media](https://www.canarymedia.com/articles/climatetech-finance/first-solar-cashes-in-with-sale-of-ira-tax-credits-worth-up-to-700m)  ·  `page_045714`

## Facts

- First Solar is the biggest U.S.-based manufacturer of solar panels.
- First Solar entered into two deals to sell up to $700 million worth of tax credits.
- The deals were announced a week after the U.S. Treasury and IRS officially defined the scope of incentives available to domestic renewable energy equipment manufacturers.
- This transaction is the “first significant” tax-credit transfer in the solar manufacturing business, according to First Solar.
- Fully integrated domestic solar manufacturers can take advantage of tax credits of up to about 16 cents per watt for a solar module manufactured in a U.S. factory.
- This incentive levels the playing field with Chinese producers, whose modules flirt with prices of 10 cents per watt.
- The Inflation Reduction Act enables companies to sell earned tax credits on an open market to any company looking to reduce its tax bill.
- First Solar sold the credits to Fiserv, a global fintech and payments company, at a price of $0.96 per $1 of tax credits.
- Mark Widmar, First Solar’s CEO, stated: “This is the [Inflation Reduction Act] delivering on its intent, which is to incentivize high value domestic manufacturing by providing manufacturers with the liquidity they need to reinvest in growth and innovation.”
- In 2022, the U.S. produced a mere 5 gigawatts of solar modules, according to the National Renewable Energy Laboratory.
- In 2022, the U.S. imported 29 gigawatts of solar modules from Asian countries.
- China has invested tens of billions of dollars to command more than 80 percent of global solar manufacturing capacity, now exceeding 800 gigawatts.
- U.S. module production capacity is growing rapidly but currently stands at just about 11 gigawatts, which is far from enough to compete with China on the world market.
- First Solar has scaled a manufacturing business around an exotic cadmium telluride chemistry that no one else in the world has been able to tame.
- First Solar uses semiconductor deposition methods that can turn a sheet of glass into a solar panel in about four hours.
- First Solar has weathered global market ups and downs since 1999.
- First Solar is likely to be one of the leading beneficiaries of the 45X production tax credit.
- First Solar is investing more than $2 billion to build new manufacturing capacity in Alabama, Louisiana and Ohio.
- First Solar expects to have 14 gigawatts of vertically integrated U.S. solar manufacturing capacity by 2026.
- Vertically integrated solar manufacturer Qcells is another potential winner of the incentives.
- The South Korean firm Qcells is investing more than $2.5 billion to build and expand its solar-cell and module production line in Dalton, Georgia.
- Qcells' total production capacity will be 8.4 gigawatts when completed in 2024.
- Many other low-carbon technologies, like wind turbine equipment and battery production, are eligible for the 45X production tax credit and its transferability mechanism.
- The new process allows project developers and manufacturers to monetize the IRA’s generous tax credits without relying on traditional tax-equity markets.
- First Solar could have elected to receive a direct cash payment from the U.S. Department of Treasury over a five-year period as a refund from its annual tax return.
- First Solar chose the third-party transfer route, citing stronger than anticipated demand for credits and the opportunity to receive cash 12 to 18 months earlier than anticipated.
- First Solar plans to use the capital from the transfer to support its manufacturing and R&D investments.
- Jigar Shah, director of the DOE Loan Programs Office, wrote on X that the news is a “[g]reat data point showing that the Inflation Reduction Act is working. A well functioning market will take time, but [it’s a] great start.”
