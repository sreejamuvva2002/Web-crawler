# Georgia Clean Economy Summit

**Type:** event  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [E2 Georgia Clean Economy Summit: Opportunity Rising | E2](https://e2.org/gasummit/)  ·  `page_010168`

## Facts

- The Georgia Clean Economy Summit is scheduled for Thursday, February 15 from 2:00pm to 6:00pm.
- The event location is Tech Square ATL at 848 Spring St NW, Atlanta, GA 30308.
- Georgia has recently announced more than 25 major clean energy and clean transportation manufacturing projects, the most of any state in the country.
- These announced projects are set to bring nearly $15 billion of new private investments to Georgia.
- These announced projects are set to bring nearly 15,000 new clean economy jobs to Georgia.
- Georgia's statewide clean energy workforce already employs more than 78,000 Georgians.
- The summit features panel discussions, keynote speakers, and networking opportunities to connect business, government, and non-profit leaders driving the state's clean economy boom.
- Panel Discussion 1 (2:50pm – 3:35pm) is titled "Growing the Clean Economy: Georgia as a Cleantech Hub".
- Panel Discussion 1 is moderated by Michael Oxman, Managing Director of the Georgia Tech Ray C. Anderson Center for Sustainable Business.
- Scott McMurray, Deputy Commissioner of Georgia Quick Start, is a speaker on Panel Discussion 1.
- Tonya Hicks, President & CEO of Power Solutions Intl, is a speaker on Panel Discussion 1.
- Scott Moskowitz, Senior Director of Market Strategy & Industry Relations at Qcells North America, is a speaker on Panel Discussion 1.
- Emilie Oxel O’Leary, CEO/Owner of Green Clean Solar, is a speaker on Panel Discussion 1.
- Panel Discussion 2 (3:50pm – 4:40pm) is titled "Growing the Clean Economy: Building Off Success".
- Panel Discussion 2 is moderated by Pamela Fann, Principal Owner of Impact Energy.
- Dana Clare Redden, Founder of Solar Stewards and Board Member of Black Owners of Solar Services, is a speaker on Panel Discussion 2.
- Kristofor Anderson, Director of Energy Resources at the Georgia Environmental Finance Authority, is a speaker on Panel Discussion 2.
- Carrie Horrocks, COO of Radiance Solar and Board Member of the Georgia Solar Energy Association, is a speaker on Panel Discussion 2.
- Shalaya Morissette, Chief of the Minority Business & Workforce Division at the U.S. Department of Energy's Office of Energy Justice & Equity, is a speaker on Panel Discussion 2.
- Dr. Shelley Francis, Co-Founder & Managing Partner of EVNoire, is a speaker on Panel Discussion 2.
- Keynote Address (4:40pm – 5:00pm) is titled "Georgia’s Clean Economy Future is Now".
- Keynote Speaker Stuart Countess is President & CEO of Kia Georgia, Inc., and is introduced by Lloyd Avram, President & CEO of the Georgia Association of Manufacturers.
- Stuart Countess oversees all operational facets of Kia Georgia's four-model assembly plant in West Point, Ga.
- The Kia Georgia plant employs more than 3,200 team members.
- The Kia Georgia plant has a production capacity of 350,000 vehicles annually.
- Stuart Countess holds a degree in Electrical Engineering from the University of Alabama.
- Countess began his 30-year manufacturing career with Hughes Aircraft Corporation before moving to the Mercedes Benz assembly plant in Vance, Ala.
- In 2008, Countess was hired as Sr. Manager for Kia Georgia’s General Assembly shop and helped develop The Kia Way and the Georgia Production System.
- Countess previously held positions at Kia Georgia as Director of Quality, Vice President of Quality and Chief Administrative Officer, and Chief Operating Officer.
- Countess serves on the boards of the Georgia Chamber, LaGrange College, LaGrange Academy, Georgia State Workforce Development Board, The Ray, Georgia Association of Manufacturers, and The Carter Center.
- Countess was chair of a key committee in Georgia’s Electric Mobility and Innovation Alliance (EMIA).
- Countess has been included in GEORGIA 500 – The State’s Most Influential Leaders and received the Georgia Titan 100 Award in 2023.
- Scott McMurray previously served as Deputy Commissioner of Global Commerce for the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD), leading teams focused on job creation and investment recruitment.
- Tonya Hicks is the first woman to become a Journeyman Inside Wireman Electrician in IBEW Local 917 and the first Black Woman to become an IBEW Journeyman Electrician in Mississippi.
- Tonya Hicks founded Power Solutions, Inc. in 2000 at age 28; the company specializes in renewable energy, electrification, and EV charging solutions.
- During the pandemic, Tonya Hicks founded sheEV to provide safer EV charging locations for women, elderly, and disabled persons.
- In 2024, the launch of the sheEV Car Club will be the first of its kind, providing community support, educational resources, and trusted products.
- Emilie Oxel O’Leary’s previous company installed solar on companies including Amazon, L’Oreal, Target, Perry Ellis, and Blue Cross Blue Shield.
- Dana Clare Redden is a co-founder of BOSS: Black Owners of Solar Services and serves on the board of directors for the Solar Energy Industries Association (SEIA).
- Radiance Solar is a solar EPC headquartered in Atlanta, GA focused on the distributed generation market.
- Impact Energy is a certified Black women-owned energy services company focusing on energy efficiency project implementation and workforce development.
- Bob Keefe is the author of two books on climate change economics: “Climatenomics” (2022) and “Clean Economy NOW” (2024).
- Scott Moskowitz previously spent five years as a market analyst at GTM Research, a leading U.S. clean energy market intelligence firm.
- Kristofor Anderson leads GEFA programs including the Weatherization Assistance Program, State Energy Program, Fuel Storage Tank Program, and programs created by the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law and Inflation Reduction Act.
- At GEFA, Kristofor Anderson has managed millions of dollars of federal grants for energy efficiency, renewable energy, and energy resiliency.
- Dr. Shelley Francis founded EVHybridNoire, the nation’s largest network of diverse EV drivers and enthusiasts with thousands of members and chapters globally focused on increasing EV adoption in under-represented communities.
- Lloyd Avram joined the Georgia Association of Manufacturers from Chevron, where he served as National Manager of State Government Affairs after overseeing global Media Relations and issues management.
