# Archer Aviation

**Source:** [Archer Aviation’s (ACHR) Recent Turbulence Could Present a Buying Opportunity - TipRanks.com](https://www.tipranks.com/news/archer-aviations-achr-recent-turbulence-could-present-a-buying-opportunity)  ·  `page_016898`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation received a certificate of occupancy for a high-volume manufacturing facility in Covington, GA.
- The company aims to produce its all-electric vertical takeoff and landing aircraft, known as Midnight, at the Covington, GA facility.
- At the end of 2024, Archer completed the construction of its ARC aircraft manufacturing facility in Covington, GA.
- Production at the ARC facility will commence in early 2025.
- The company plans to produce two aircraft per month by year-end.
- Production capacity is planned to scale to 650 aircraft annually by 2030.
- Plans are underway with Stellantis to finalize an agreement making Stellantis Archer’s exclusive contract manufacturer for mass production of the Midnight eVTOL aircraft at the ARC facility.
- Archer and Anduril Industries formed an exclusive partnership to develop a hybrid propulsion vertical takeoff and landing (VTOL) aircraft for defense applications.
- Archer raised an additional $430 million in equity capital, bringing total capital raised to roughly $2 billion.
- The financing round included participation from existing partners Stellantis and United Airlines as well as new institutional investors.
- Archer has forged strategic alliances with Southwest Airlines and Signature Aviation to establish air taxi networks across the U.S., aiming to electrify 200 airports nationally.
- A consortium led by the Abu Dhabi Investment Office (ADIO) plans to launch commercial air taxi services in the UAE, with a targeted start date in Q4 2025.
- Archer partnered with Soracle Corporation, a joint venture between Japan Airlines and Sumitomo Corporation, to launch air mobility services in Japan.
- Soracle intends to pioneer operations using Archer’s Midnight aircraft in cities facing ground transportation challenges due to traffic or geographic limitations.
- Soracle possesses the rights to order up to 100 Midnight aircraft valued at up to $500 million.
- Archer and Soracle will coordinate with the Japanese Civil Aviation Bureau for necessary permissions and certifications.
- Archer is nearing the completion of the FAA’s four-phase type certification process for its eVTOL aircraft.
- The FAA recently released a powered-lift Special Federal Aviation Regulation (SFAR) to enable plans to commercialize eVTOL in the U.S.
