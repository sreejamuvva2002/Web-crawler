# Robert Bosch GmbH

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Bosch has set a course for the future in the difficult 2025 financial year - Bosch Media Service US](https://www.bosch-presse.de/pressportal/us/en/press-release-29632.html)  ·  `page_049221`

## Facts

- Bosch employs approximately 20,000 associates in the U.S., which includes high-quality, high-value jobs in engineering, manufacturing, software and research and development.
- In total, Bosch currently maintains 20 manufacturing locations in the U.S. in Arkansas, California, Florida, Illinois, Indiana, Kansas, Kentucky, North Carolina, Michigan, Minnesota, Oklahoma, Pennsylvania, South Carolina and Tennessee.
- Since 2023, Bosch has announced more than $6 billion in U.S.-based acquisitions, resulting in more than 5,000 U.S. jobs.
- Paul Thomas serves as president of Bosch in North America and president of Bosch Mobility, Americas.
- The structural shift toward electromobility and the extremely high price and competitive pressure in the global automotive industry is resulting in an annual cost gap of around 2.5 billion euros worldwide in the Mobility business sector alone.
- Bosch announced last year that it needed to cut about another 13,000 jobs globally due to the structural shift toward electromobility and competitive pressures.
- Last year, Bosch won customer orders for solutions for automated driving, the requisite sensor technology, and central vehicle computers worth 10 billion euros.
- Bosch's Vehicle Motion Management software provides central control of brakes, steering, powertrain, and chassis and is well received on the market.
- At 56 billion euros, sales revenue in the Mobility business sector was up slightly year on year by 0.3 percent (or 3.1 percent adjusted for exchange-rate effects).
- For 2025, preliminary figures indicate sales to third parties in North America were 18.7 billion USD, an increase of approximately eight percent year-over-year.
- Bosch now assumes that it will begin achieving its target margin of 7 percent in 2027 at the earliest, instead of in 2026.
- Stefan Hartung is chairman of the board of management of Robert Bosch GmbH.
- Markus Forschner is member of the board of management and chief financial officer of Robert Bosch GmbH.
- Increased tariffs negatively impacted results alongside lower sales.
- The company made considerable provisions for necessary structural adjustments and associated personnel measures.
- Bosch's Strategy 2030 stipulates that the company must be one of the three leading providers in its key markets in all regions of the world.
- By the end of 2027, the company plans to have invested a total of 2.5 billion euros in AI, which is already in use throughout the company.
