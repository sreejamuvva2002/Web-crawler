# National Transmission Needs Study

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [National Transmission Needs Study | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/oe/national-transmission-needs-study)  ·  `page_003747`

## Facts

- Published in October 2023, the National Transmission Needs Study is an assessment of existing data and current and near-term future transmission needs through 2040.
- The study assesses publicly available information and more than 120 recently published reports considering historic and anticipated future needs given electricity demand, public policy, and market conditions.
- It serves as the DOE’s triennial state of the grid report and fulfills a Congressional requirement to conduct assessments of national electric transmission capacity constraints and congestion at least once every 3 years.
- The expanded scope considers both historic and anticipated future capacity constraints and transmission congestion that could adversely affect consumers.
- Nearly all regions in the United States would gain improved reliability and resilience from additional transmission investments.
- Regions with historically high within-region congestion, particularly the Northwest, Mountain, Texas, and New York, need additional strategically placed transmission deployment to reduce congestion.
- The largest benefit in new interregional transfer capacity additions is between the three interconnections: Mountain and Plains, Texas and Delta, Southwest and Texas, Plains and Midwest, and Plains and Delta regions.
- Significant within-region transmission deployment is needed as soon as 2030 in the Plains, Midwest, and Texas regions.
- By 2040, large deployments will also be needed in the Mountain, Mid-Atlantic, and Southeast regions.
- In 2030, large relative interregional transmission deployments are needed between the Delta and Plains, Midwest and Plains, and Mid-Atlantic and Midwest regions.
- By 2040, there is a significant need for new interregional transmission between nearly all regions.
- DOE released a draft of the study to states, Tribes, and regional grid entities in October 2022, with a 30-day comment period.
- During the consultation period, DOE hosted six informational webinars and received nearly 180 unique comments from 20 consultation entities.
- An updated draft was released in February 2023 for a 45-day public comment period closing April 20, 2023.
- A public webinar providing an overview of the findings was held on November 8, 2023.
