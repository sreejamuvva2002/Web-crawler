# Lehigh Technologies

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Lehigh Technologies begins production of micronized rubber powder at Spain facility | Tire Technology International](https://www.tiretechnologyinternational.com/news/manufacturing-facilities/lehigh-technologies-begins-production-of-micronized-rubber-powder-at-spain-facility.html)  ·  `page_057118`

## Facts

- Lehigh Technologies is a specialty chemicals company that produces sustainable raw materials from end-of-life tires and post-industrial rubber.
- Lehigh Technologies has begun production at its new Murillo el Fruto micronized rubber powder (MRP) plant in Navarra, Spain.
- The Spanish factory is the company’s first facility outside the USA.
- The facility was constructed in partnership with Lehigh’s Spanish partner Hera Holding.
- The facility utilizes Lehigh’s cryogenic turbo mill technology.
- The plant has a capacity to produce 10,000 metric tons of MRP per year.
- Production will include Lehigh’s PolyDyne and MicroDyne product ranges.
- These product ranges are already used by customers in more than 18 countries around the world.
- The production line is co-located with Indugarbi NFU, a leading tire recycler in Spain operated by Hera Holding, to ensure efficient supply of granulate feedstock for the MRP manufacturing process.
- Construction of the Navarra plant was managed by Lehigh and executed by UK-based Jex Engineering, with support from the Dennis Group and Air Products.
- The new plant employs the same layout and equipment as Lehigh’s Tucker, Georgia, plant in the USA.
- Matching the Tucker, Georgia plant's layout and equipment ensures consistent quality and product specification across all locations.
- Jason Stravinski, Lehigh’s VP of operations, stated he is very proud of the international team that made the project successful.
- Engineers from Lehigh US, Lehigh Spain, and Hera Holding worked with the Jex team to complete the project on time and on budget.
- Lehigh expects the plant to be a crucial component to meeting the rising demand for green chemicals in Europe and beyond.
- Lluís Molina, Lehigh Spain general manager, said demand for Lehigh’s products is increasing rapidly and the new plant comes at the perfect time to support growth.
- The Murillo plant is the next step in Lehigh’s mission to commercialize MRP for customers in a wide range of performance-driven markets.
- Lehigh Technologies became a subsidiary of Michelin in late 2017.
- Lehigh serves as an important platform supporting Michelin’s goal that all new tires will contain 80% sustainable materials by 2048.
