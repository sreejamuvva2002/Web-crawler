# Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution Battery Cell Manufacturing JV

**Type:** Joint Venture  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[battery-cell-manufacturing|Battery Cell Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** Bryan County, Georgia, United States

## Overview

A battery-cell manufacturing joint venture between Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution located at the Metaplant site in Bryan County, Georgia. The JV received an additional $2 billion investment in August 2023, bringing total JV capital to over $4.3 billion and supporting a 30 GWh facility capable of supplying batteries for up to 300,000 electric vehicles per year.

## EV supply-chain relevance

The JV produces lithium-ion battery cells that power Hyundai, Kia and Genesis electric vehicles assembled in the United States.

- **Metaplant Battery Cell Manufacturing Facility** — The plant will produce lithium-ion cells for Hyundai Motor Group vehicles; Hyundai Mobis will assemble battery packs using these cells.

## Investment

- **$2 billion** (August 31, 2023) — Additional investment in the Metaplant battery cell manufacturing JV
  - Raises total JV investment to more than $4.3 billion; overall project value estimated at $7.59 billion.

## Jobs

- **400 new jobs**
  - Areas: battery cell manufacturing, assembly
  - The expansion will create 400 well-paying positions in the Savannah region.

## Timeline

- **May 2023** — Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution signed a memorandum of understanding establishing LGES as the partner for Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America’s onsite battery cell manufacturing JV.
- **August 31, 2023** — Governor Brian P. Kemp announced an additional $2 billion investment by Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution in the Metaplant battery cell manufacturing joint venture.

## Key facts

- The additional $2 billion investment raises the JV’s total capital to more than $4.3 billion, with an overall project value estimated at $7.59 billion.
- The 30 GWh Metaplant facility will support production of up to 300,000 electric vehicles per year when operating at full capacity.
- Expansion creates 400 new well-paying jobs in the Savannah region.
- Georgia has attracted over $25 billion in EV-related investments since 2018, creating more than 30,000 jobs; battery projects account for at least $11 billion of that investment.
- "These types of major investments ultimately go to hardworking Georgians in the form of paychecks, improved schools and infrastructure," said Governor Kemp.
- "This incremental investment in Bryan County reflects our continued commitment to create a more sustainable future powered by American workers," said José Muñoz.
- "Our dedication to driving America’s EV transition while bolstering the local economy through quality jobs" was emphasized by Dong-Myung Kim of LG Energy Solution.
- Pat Wilson highlighted Georgia’s goal to become the top location for EV manufacturing in the United States.
