# Denkai America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Denkai America will locate its new manufacturing facility and North American Headquarters in Richmond County.
- The company’s initial investment will be $150 million to establish an electrodeposited (ED) copper foil production facility focused on electric vehicle battery foil.
- The facility will be located on a 115-acre site.
- Denkai America aims to invest a total of $430 million and create 250 new jobs over the next five years.
- Governor Kemp stated that Georgia is proud to be selected as the home of Denkai America, further strengthening the state's status as a leader in emerging electric mobility and existing aerospace, defense, and technology industries.
- Governor Kemp emphasized Georgia's focus on bringing quality jobs to highly-trained Georgians and highlighted the state's top-ranking pro-business environment.
- Denkai America’s materials have traditionally been used in printed circuit board applications primarily for the aerospace and defense industries.
- The company's materials are now also used in lithium-ion batteries powering electric vehicles.
- Operating for more than 60 years, Denkai has team members worldwide with operations in Japan and North America.
- Heday Nakajima, Ph.D., Denkai Group President and CEO, stated the investment highlights their commitment to leading ED battery foil manufacturing in North America for many years to come.
- Nobu Masuda, Denkai America’s President and CEO, expressed thrill at expanding manufacturing presence into Augusta due to its skilled and talented workforce.
- Construction is expected to begin in September 2022 as the first part of a three-phase investment.
- The facility will be fully operational by December 2024.
- The company will be hiring for skilled industrial technicians, production managers, and engineers.
- Steven Kendrick, Chairman of the Augusta Economic Development Authority, welcomed Denkai America to the Augusta Corporate Park and noted they will bring high-skilled jobs to the growing list of companies in the area.
- The Augusta Commission and Augusta Utilities Department provided support to make the project happen.
- Barton Lowrey, Global Commerce Director of Corporate Solutions and Cyber Security at the Georgia Department of Economic Development, represented the state on this competitive project.
- The project is a partnership with the Augusta Economic Development Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- Georgia Quick Start is the Technical College System of Georgia’s nationally ranked workforce training program that has provided specialized training tailored to business needs for more than 40 years.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Denkai America plays a vital role in the EV ecosystem and highlighted Georgia's commitment to building lasting international relationships and recruiting the wider electric mobility value chain.
- Georgia’s prime location, extensive infrastructure, skilled workforce, and business-friendly climate have made it attractive for diverse industries focused on creating a sustainable future.
- Georgia is cultivating a vertically integrated supply chain to help companies increase efficiencies by reducing reliance on imported materials.
- EV-related projects have contributed more than $12.6 billion in investments and more than 17,800 new jobs to Georgia since 2020.
- Augusta Corporate Park is a Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) Certified park.
- To earn GRAD certification, a property must undergo due diligence and complete specific certification requirements prior to a prospective business visit.
- Denkai America is a leader in the manufacture of high-quality electrodeposited copper foils for printed circuit board, industrial, and energy storage applications.
- Denkai America delivers application-specific copper foils required by technology-leading companies, backed by a global presence and domestic manufacturing strength.
