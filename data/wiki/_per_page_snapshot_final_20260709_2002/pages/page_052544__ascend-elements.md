# Ascend Elements

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2022-10-26

**Source:** [Ascend Elements | Ascend Elements Secures $300 Million in Funding](https://ascendelements.com/ascend-elements-secures-300-million-in-funding/)  ·  `page_052544`

## Facts

- Ascend Elements secured $300 million in equity and debt financing.
- The funding includes $200 million in Series C equity investments from an international group of strategic and financial investors.
- The funding round was led by Fifth Wall Climate.
- SK ecoplant, the environmental unit of South Korean conglomerate SK Group, joined the funding round.
- New investors include Oman Investment Authority, Lithium Americas Corporation, GLy Capital Management’s New Mobility Fund, Mirae Asset Capital & LS and Shinhan GIB.
- Previous investors participated in the latest round, including Hitachi Ventures, Jaguar Land Rover’s InMotion Ventures, TDK Ventures, Orbia Ventures, At One Ventures, TRUMPF Venture, and Doral Energy-Tech Ventures.
- The funding is in addition to two recently awarded grants totaling $480 million from the Department of Energy.
- The funding will accelerate commercialization of Ascend Elements’ Hydro-to-Cathode™ direct precursor synthesis process.
- The proprietary process establishes a closed-loop, EV battery materials supply chain in North America.
- Ascend Elements plans to invest up to $1 billion to build a sustainable lithium-ion battery materials facility in Hopkinsville, Ky.
- The Kentucky facility, known as “Apex 1”, will produce enough lithium-ion battery pCAM and sustainable CAM to equip up to 250,000 electric vehicles per year.
- Ascend Elements is already generating revenue at its “Base 1” facility in Georgia.
- The Georgia facility processes end-of-life batteries and manufacturing scrap.
- The Georgia facility will have 30,000 metric tons of EV battery recycling capacity by the end of 2022.
- The Georgia facility will ultimately feed the Apex 1 facility in Kentucky.
- Ascend Elements expects to recycle more than 150,000 metric tons of lithium-ion batteries per year globally by 2026.
- The company transforms recycled batteries into high-performance, customized EV battery cathode active material precursor (pCAM) and cathode active materials (CAM).
- The produced pCAM and CAM meet or exceed performance requirements of top-tier battery manufacturers.
- CEO Mike O’Kronley stated: “Our investment partners understand the urgent need to produce sustainable, lithium-ion battery materials. It’s not enough to simply recycle lithium-ion batteries and recover metals for the global commodity markets. Our patented, closed-loop process goes beyond simple battery recycling. Instead, we produce sustainable, high-performance cathode active materials that can go directly back into new EV batteries.”
- Fifth Wall Partner Peter Gajdoš noted that securing recyclability of critical metals is essential to achieve energy independence from fossil fuels and supports creating sustainable batteries for automotive and stationary storage.
- SK ecoplant CEO Park Kyung-il stated the investment continues their strategic partnership and lays the foundation for SK ecoplant as the first shareholder to dominate the global battery recycling industry.
- Oman Investment Authority Director Ibrahim Al Eisri stated the investment aligns with OIA’s strategy and Vision 2040 priorities to develop and transform the lithium-ion battery recycling industry globally.
- Ascend Elements is based in Westborough, Mass., and is a leading provider of sustainable, closed-loop battery materials solutions.
- The company's proprietary technology produces new CAM from spent lithium-ion cells more efficiently than traditional methods, resulting in reduced cost, improved performance, and lowered GHG emissions.
