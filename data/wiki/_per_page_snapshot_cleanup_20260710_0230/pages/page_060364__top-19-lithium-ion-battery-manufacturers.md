# Top 19 Lithium Ion Battery Manufacturers

**Type:** supplier_relationship  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-02-06

**Source:** [Top 19 Lithium Ion Battery Manufacturer in 2026 - MANLY Battery](https://manlybattery.com/top-10-lithium-ion-battery-manufacturers/)  ·  `page_060364`

## Facts

- Global H1-2025 power-battery installs reached ~504.4 GWh (+37.3% YoY), with LFP holding ~61% share and NMC concentrating in high-performance EVs.
- China accounted for ~58.5% of global battery installs (~295.2 GWh, +48.8% YoY), while overseas markets reached ≈209.2 GWh (+23.8% YoY).
- Continued localization in the US/EU is expected under IRA/CBAM with tighter origin rules.
- Global lithium battery demand is projected to rise from ~471 GWh (2021) to ~3,939 GWh (2028E), implying a ~31% CAGR from 2023–2028.
- EV & traction power demand remains the largest segment at ~66–71% of total market share.
- Utility/Home-ESS demand is growing fastest, projected to reach 30% share (~1,182 GWh by 2028E).
- CATL produced 96.7 GWh out of a global 296.8 GWh in the referenced period, up 167.5% yearly.
- CATL introduced its Tener energy storage system on April 9, 2024, a standard 20-foot container-based LFP system claiming zero capacity/power loss for the first five years.
- CATL unveiled a second-generation sodium-ion battery with 200Wh/kg energy density in November 2024, expected to launch in 2025.
- LG Energy Solution is investing $5.5 billion in battery plants in the U.S.
- LG Energy Solution's Ultium Cells JV operates in the U.S., debuting NA-made LFP for ESS.
- LG Energy Solution expects tax breaks from the U.S. government to help achieve a 45 to 50 GWh production capacity.
- LG Energy Solution introduced its next-generation pouch-type high-voltage mid-nickel CTP battery in September 2024, offering up to 600 km range for large trucks.
- Panasonic secured the third spot globally outside China with 44.6 GWh supplied (14% market share) and plans to build four additional EV battery plants.
- Panasonic's Kansas EV cell plant has a Phase 1 target of ~30–40 GWh/yr.
- Samsung SDI announced 2023 sales of 22.71 trillion Korean won and is building a second U.S. battery plant in collaboration with Stellantis.
- Samsung SDI expects the power battery market to grow ~18% in 2024 to approximately $184.8 billion.
- Samsung SDI introduced the Samsung Battery Box (SBB) 1.5 on June 19, 2024, a 20-foot containerized LFP solution storing up to 5.26 MWh.
- Samsung SDI unveiled an LFP+ battery in September 2024 optimized for commercial EVs, enabling an 80% charge in 20 minutes.
- BYD announced plans to build a $1.2 billion EV battery plant in central China.
- Tesla operates Megafactories in the U.S. and Shanghai for utility storage, with global deployments.
- Tesla announced a Megapack 3 (~5 MWh class) in 2025.
- MANLY Battery exports to the US/EU from facilities in Shenzhen/Dongguan/Huizhou, China.
- SK On operates the BlueOval SK JV in the U.S. and announced an LFP ESS program for North America targeting mass production from 2H 2026.
- CALB Group has CALB USA & EU supply operations and launched a 392 Ah ESS cell and a 6.25 MWh liquid-cooled 20-ft container system.
- Gotion High-Tech targets 40 GWh cells + 10 GWh packs at its Manteno, IL site.
- Northvolt's EU assets were acquired by Lyten in August 2025, with a Poland BESS (~6 GWh) restart plan.
- A123 Systems has a US/CN/EU footprint and produces 12V/48V low-voltage automotive packs and utility/C&I platforms.
- SVOLT canceled planned Saarland/Lauchhammer factories in 2024 but continues exporting ESS/EV products to overseas markets.
