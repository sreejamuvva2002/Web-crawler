# Sewon America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-03-07

**Source:** [Another Hyundai supplier to expand near Georgia EV campus | Supply Chain Dive](https://www.supplychaindive.com/news/sewon-america-second-georgia-facility/644380/)  ·  `page_001380`

## Facts

- Auto parts manufacturer Sewon America is investing over $300 million toward a second facility in Georgia, according to a Feb. 21 press release.
- Sewon’s new facility will be located in Rincon and will produce EV body parts for original equipment manufacturers.
- One of the OEM customers for the Rincon facility is the Hyundai Metaplant near Savannah.
- Suppliers for the Hyundai Metaplant have now committed over $1.8 billion in investment in Georgia, at least $800 million higher than initial expectations.
- Sewon’s investment is expected to create 740 jobs.
- South Korea-based Sewon is another manufacturer on the growing list of Hyundai suppliers either establishing or expanding its operations near the incoming $5.5 billion Hyundai Meta plant.
- Sewon already has one facility in LaGrange, Georgia, and the second facility will be Rincon’s largest private investment.
- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp congratulated Chairman Kim on the company’s then-recent expansion during his first international trip as governor to Korea.
- Gov. Kemp stated that discussions with Chairman Kim led to this announcement as Sewon America continues to grow its Georgia operations.
- In November, Joon Georgia became the first supplier to announce operations, with plans to invest $317 million in a production facility in Bulloch County.
- Later that month, Hyundai Mobis became the second supplier to invest in the area, announcing it would spend $926 million on an EV Power Electric system plant in Bryan County.
- In January, Ecoplastic Corporation announced it is investing approximately $205 million in a new manufacturing facility in southeast Georgia to supply components to the incoming electric vehicle campus.
- Last month, Seoyon E-Hwa announced it will invest almost $76 million toward its second manufacturing facility in Georgia.
- Gov. Brian Kemp called the Hyundai Metaplant the “largest economic development project in state history.”
- The Hyundai Metaplant is estimated to create 8,100 jobs in Georgia’s coastal region.
- Hyundai began recruiting to fill those Metaplant jobs in November, according to a Savannah Harbor-Interstate I-16 Corridor Joint Development Authority press release.
