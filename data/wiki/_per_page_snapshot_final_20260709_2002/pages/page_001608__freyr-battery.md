# FREYR Battery

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-06

**Source:** [FREYR Battery Cancels $2.6 Billion Georgia Factory Amid Strategic Shift](https://evmagz.com/freyr-battery-cancels-2-6-billion-georgia-factory-amid-strategic-shift/)  ·  `page_001608`

## Facts

- FREYR Battery announced the cancellation of its planned $2.6 billion battery manufacturing facility in Coweta County, Georgia.
- The decision reflects a strategic pivot towards solar panel production following the company’s recent acquisition of a solar module manufacturing plant in Texas.
- In a letter dated January 21 to the Coweta County Development Authority, FREYR cited rising interest rates, declining battery prices, and changes in company leadership as factors influencing the decision.
- The January 21 letter stated, “This decision was made reluctantly, as the Company has realigned its near-term strategic goals.”
- The Georgia project was originally unveiled in 2022.
- The canceled Georgia project was expected to create over 700 jobs and significantly contribute to the state’s growing clean energy sector.
- Construction on the Georgia facility had not commenced prior to the cancellation announcement.
- FREYR has entered into an agreement to sell the 368-acre Georgia site for $50 million.
- The sale transaction is anticipated to close on February 15.
- Net proceeds from the site sale are expected to be approximately $22.5 million after repaying state and local grants.
- FREYR emphasized that the cancellation decision does not reflect any concerns with the support provided by Georgia or local partners.
- The company acknowledged the “extraordinary assistance and support” provided by state and local authorities regarding the project.
- This move aligns with FREYR’s broader strategy to establish a vertically integrated U.S. manufacturing presence, beginning with its Texas facility.
- FREYR plans to expand its solar manufacturing capabilities, including the development of a 5 GW solar cell facility.
- Construction for the planned 5 GW solar cell facility is slated to start in the second quarter of 2025.
