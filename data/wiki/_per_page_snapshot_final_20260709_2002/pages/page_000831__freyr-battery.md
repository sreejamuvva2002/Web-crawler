# Freyr Battery

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Two Battery Manufacturers Abandon Projects](https://www.constructconnect.com/construction-economic-news/two-battery-manufacturers-abandon-projects)  ·  `page_000831`

## Facts

- Freyr Battery has officially abandoned its plans for a $2.6 billion battery manufacturing facility in Coweta County, Georgia.
- The project was announced in 2022 under the name Giga America.
- The Georgia facility was expected to create over 700 jobs.
- The first phase of the Georgia facility was expected to deliver 34 GWh of battery capacity.
- Freyr is now focusing on solar module manufacturing in Texas after acquiring a 5 GW solar facility from Trina Solar for $340 million.
- Freyr selected Austin, Texas, to relocate its headquarters and sold the 368-acre Georgia property.
- The company stated that the announcement coincides with the ongoing ramp of production at FREYR’s G1 solar module facility in Wilmer, Texas.
- The G1 solar module facility in Wilmer, Texas, already employs more than 1,000 people across the Wilmer and Dallas communities.
- Daniel Barcelo, FREYR’s Chairman of the Board and Chief Executive Officer, stated that as the company moves forward with its strategy to build an integrated American solar + storage manufacturing network, it intends to bring more than 1,000 new American jobs to the Texas economy.
