# New Manufacturing Facilities Property Credit

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2025-01-01

**Source:** [New Manufacturing Facilities Property Credit | Department of Revenue](https://dor.georgia.gov/new-manufacturing-facilities-property-credit)  ·  `page_005131`

## Facts

- The New Manufacturing Facilities Property Credit is a tax incentive for a manufacturer who has operated a manufacturing facility in Georgia for at least 3 years and who spends $800 million on a new manufacturing facility in the state.
- Eligible manufacturers must have a number of full-time employees equal or exceed 1,800, though these jobs do not have to be new jobs to Georgia.
- An application must be filed which a panel must approve to receive the benefit.
- The benefit awarded is a credit against taxes equal to 6 percent of the cost of all qualified investment property purchased or acquired.
- The total credit allowed under the program is $50 million.
- The credit offsets income tax liability and any excess may be used to offset withholding taxes.
- There is a 15-year carry forward of any unused tax credit.
- For taxable years beginning on or after January 1, 2025, any credits generated but not used may be carried forward for 10 years.
- There are different provisions available for certain high-impact aerospace defense projects.
- The tax credit code for this incentive is 120.
