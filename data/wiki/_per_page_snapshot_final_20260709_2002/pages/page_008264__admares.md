# ADMARES

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2023-06-02

**Source:** [Finnish Company Picks Ware County for First U.S. Manufacturing Facility - Tennessee Star](https://tennesseestar.com/economy/finnish-company-picks-ware-county-for-first-u-s-manufacturing-facility/tcsquare/2023/06/02/)  ·  `page_008264`

## Facts

- ADMARES is from Turku, Finland and specializes in the industrialized manufacturing of buildings and homes.
- The company plans to build its first U.S. manufacturing facility in Ware County.
- ADMARES is relocating its headquarters to the United States.
- The company intends to create more than 1,400 jobs at its new Georgia location.
- ADMARES plans to invest $750 million in its Waycross facility.
- The facility will be a 2.5-million-square-foot build-to-suit greenfield site located on Highway 23 in Waycross.
- The Georgia manufacturing facility is expected to start production in late 2025.
- The facility will specialize in producing buildings for the housing sector.
- The company's buildings have been used in residential and hospitality applications.
- A Georgia Department of Economic Development representative confirmed that the project is still active.
- Georgia Quick Start is involved in the project.
- The Georgia Quick Start designation allows officials to keep the cost of incentives and taxpayer expenses secret until a deal is finalized.
- Georgia Quick Start is a division of the Technical College System of Georgia.
- Georgia Quick Start provides customized workforce training for companies creating jobs in Georgia.
- Mikael Hedberg is the founder and CEO of ADMARES.
- Mikael Hedberg stated that Waycross, Georgia is an ideal transportation hub with easy access to major highways and extensive rail connections.
- Mikael Hedberg noted that Waycross's proximity to the Port of Brunswick offers a competitive advantage for global trade.
- The Port of Brunswick is one of the busiest ports on the eastern seaboard.
- Georgia officials stated that the new ADMARES facility will help the state address workforce housing challenges.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson said the facility is filling a niche critical to economic development.
- Workforce housing is described as a growing national challenge that Georgia is also facing.
