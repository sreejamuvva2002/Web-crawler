# Ford-SK Innovation Battery Plants JV

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2021-09-27

**Source:** [Ford Is Building Three New EV Battery Plants With 129 GWh Capacity](https://interestingengineering.com/transportation/ford-is-building-three-new-ev-battery-plants-with-129-gwh-capacity)  ·  `page_060383`

## Facts

- Ford is building three new EV battery plants with one located in Tennessee and two in Kentucky.
- The project is an $11.4-billion deal between Ford and the South Korea-based company SK Innovation.
- The first Kentucky plant will complete assembly in 2025.
- A sister plant in Kentucky is slated for completion in 2026.
- Combined, the three new facilities will create 11,000 new jobs.
- The combined output of the three facilities will be 129 GWh.
- The 129 GWh capacity is enough to power 1 million all-electric vehicles.
- Ford committed $7 billion to the project.
- SK Innovation committed $4.4 billion to the project.
- The Tennessee facility is a $5.6-billion mega-campus located in Stanton, Tenn., called Blue Oval City.
- Blue Oval City will offer 6,000 new jobs.
- Blue Oval City will include community-designed technical training programs to strengthen the local community.
- Ford and SK Innovation plan to build a $5.8-billion battery manufacturing complex in Kentucky called BlueOvalSK Battery Park.
- BlueOvalSK Battery Park will require filling 5,000 new jobs.
- The new facilities are component plants for battery manufacturing, not automotive assembly plants.
- The battery manufacturing effort will run parallel with ambitions to build a more reliable means of recycling lithium-ion batteries at minimal cost to the environment.
- The aim is to develop a manufacturing line to supply Ford’s North American car assembly plants with locally assembled batteries.
- The batteries will be needed for future Ford and Lincoln EVs, including next-generation all-electric F-Series trucks.
- Bill Ford, Executive Chair of Ford, called the project a transformative moment where Ford will lead America’s transition to electric vehicles and usher in a new era of clean, carbon-neutral manufacturing.
- Bill Ford stated that with this investment and innovation, they can achieve mutually exclusive goals: protect the planet, build great electric vehicles Americans will love, and contribute to the nation's prosperity.
- The deal responds to strong demand for the Ford F-150 Lightning truck, E-Transit, and Mustang Mach-E vehicles.
- Ford has not single-handedly built an entirely new plant from the ground up since its first Kentucky plant was commissioned in 1969.
- Jim Farley, CEO and President of Ford, called it their biggest investment ever to help build a better future for America.
- Jim Farley stated they are moving to deliver breakthrough electric vehicles for the many rather than the few, creating good jobs supporting American families, an ultra-efficient carbon-neutral manufacturing system, and value for communities, dealers, and shareholders.
- This is Ford's largest single manufacturing investment in its 118 years of operation.
- The project is part of Ford's more-than-$30 billion investment in EVs through 2025.
- Ford aims to develop the country’s manufacturing ecosystem for the 21st century while committing to meet the Paris Climate Agreement deadline with zero carbon no later than 2050.
