# Hyundai Mobis

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** power_electronics  ·  **Published:** 2022-11-23

**Source:** [Second Automotive Supplier for Hyundai Metaplant To Create 1,500 Jobs | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-releases/2022/second-global-automotive-supplier-hyundai-metaplant-create-1500-jobs)  ·  `page_000039`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Hyundai Mobis plans to invest $926 million in an Electric Vehicle (EV) Power Electric system plant in Bryan County that will eventually employ at least 1,500 people.
- The EV Power Electric system plant expands Hyundai Mobis’ current footprint in Georgia.
- Hyundai Mobis has operated a manufacturing facility with almost 1,200 employees in West Point since 2009 as the largest Tier 1 supplier to Kia Georgia.
- The West Point plant supplies automotive manufacturers with complete cockpit modules, chassis modules, and bumper assemblies.
- H.S. Oh, Vice President of Hyundai Mobis Electric Powertrain Business Unit, stated the investment reflects an acceleration in the development of the EV supply chain in Georgia’s auto industry.
- H.S. Oh noted that Hyundai Mobis will become a major production player in the EV market and trigger more growth within the sector.
- Once it reaches full production, the 1,200,000-square-foot facility will be able to supply over 900,000 EV Power Electric systems and 450,000 Integrated Charging Control Units annually to the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) factory in Bryan County, the Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Alabama (HMMA) factory in Montgomery, and the Kia Georgia plant.
- Construction on the new facility is expected to begin as early as January 2023, and production is expected to begin in 2024.
- The new facility is located at the GRAD Certified Belfast Commerce Park in Richmond Hill.
- Jon Seagraves, Development Authority of Bryan County Chairman, welcomed Hyundai Mobis to the community.
- Jon Seagraves stated the site was purchased about two years ago and highlighted its proximity to available housing in nearby Heartwood, a top-performing public school system, interstate access, and rail.
- Jon Seagraves noted the project represents nearly $1 billion of investment and the creation of over 1,500 good-paying jobs.
- Jon Seagraves expressed gratitude to Bryan County, the City of Richmond Hill, and Raydient Places for their support and partnership on the project.
- Alyce Thornhill, Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy, represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project in partnership with the Development Authority of Bryan County, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia Power.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that suppliers who have been part of Georgia’s automotive industry are transitioning to the jobs of the future and becoming part of Georgia’s growing EV ecosystem.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that in May, they predicted non-affiliated HMGMA suppliers would invest an additional $1 billion into Georgia, a prediction already blown past in about two weeks.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson noted that with the support of partners like Georgia Quick Start and sister agencies, they are excited to prepare Georgia’s workforce of tomorrow for the jobs being created now.
- Since 2020, more than 30 EV-related projects have contributed over $13.3 billion in investment and announced more than 18,100 jobs in Georgia.
- On October 25, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America officially broke ground in Bryan County on its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility.
- Offsite suppliers connected to the HMGMA project are predicted to invest approximately $1 billion in other locations around the state.
- Georgia is positioned as a hub for the electric mobility industry.
