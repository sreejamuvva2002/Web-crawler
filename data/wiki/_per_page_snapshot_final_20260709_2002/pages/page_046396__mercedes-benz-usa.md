# Mercedes-Benz USA

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2015-01-13

**Source:** [Deal: Mercedes-Benz USA to create 800 jobs in Atlanta](https://www.wtoc.com/story/27837012/deal-mercedes-benz-usa-to-create-800-jobs-in-atlanta/)  ·  `page_046396`

## Facts

- Gov. Nathan Deal announced that Mercedes-Benz USA will relocate its headquarters to Atlanta.
- The relocation will create at least 800 jobs.
- Mercedes-Benz USA will invest approximately $74 million in the move.
- Georgia Governor Nathan Deal stated that Mercedes-Benz USA's decision to move to Georgia is a home run for the state due to its international name recognition and unmatched reputation for quality.
- Gov. Deal expressed looking forward to building upon the new partnership and adding Mercedes-Benz to the state's automotive sector.
- Gov. Deal noted that Georgia is home to many growing, globally connected businesses due to its top-ranked economic environment, making it the No. 1 state for business three times over.
- Gov. Deal stated that Mercedes-Benz will find in Georgia a state that will help its bottom line and people who will welcome its team and their families with open arms.
- Mercedes-Benz USA is currently headquartered in Montvale, N.J.
- Mercedes-Benz USA is responsible for the distribution, marketing, and customer service for all Mercedes-Benz products in the United States.
- Mercedes-Benz USA President and CEO Stephen Cannon stated that Atlanta provides a premier city foundation to write the next chapter of their success story in the U.S.
- Stephen Cannon noted that Atlanta offers employees strong quality of life, terrific schools, and wonderful cultural and recreational activities outside of work.
- Stephen Cannon expressed pride in calling Georgia their future home.
- The automotive industry, from headquarter operations to major automotive suppliers' manufacturing operations, is an important part of Georgia's economy.
- Georgia's automotive sector experienced a 149 percent increase in investment during FY 2014.
- Georgia's automotive sector experienced an 11 percent increase in jobs during FY 2014.
- Georgia's automotive industry exports grew 9.2 percent between 2012 and 2013.
- Atlanta has become a leading city for auto technology, hosting the GM Innovation Center, Panasonic Automotive Innovation Center, and the AT&T Drive Studio.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner Chris Carr stated that welcoming Mercedes-Benz to Georgia is an incredible victory for the state.
- Chris Carr credited the move to continued collaboration with economic development partners including the Metro Atlanta Chamber of Commerce, Georgia Power, Development Authority of Fulton County, City of Sandy Springs, MARTA, the Georgia Department of Transportation, the Georgia Department of Driver Services, and the Georgia Council for the Arts.
