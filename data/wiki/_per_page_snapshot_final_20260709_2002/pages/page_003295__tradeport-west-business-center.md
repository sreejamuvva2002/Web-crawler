# Tradeport West Business Center

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2026-04-27

**Source:** [Tradeport West Business Center in Liberty County Achieves ‘Georgia Rea | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-releases/2026/tradeport-west-business-center-liberty-county-achieves-georgia-ready-accelerated)  ·  `page_003295`

## Facts

- Tradeport West Business Center in Liberty County has earned “Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development” (GRAD) certification with “Select” status.
- The site’s new “GRAD Select” status provides an additional seal of approval that makes it especially attractive for future industrial development.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that achieving GRAD Select status is a powerful signal to companies and site selectors that a location is ready for investment and growth.
- Liberty County’s Tradeport West Business Center has received further support through state initiatives such as the Rural Site Development Initiative (RSDI).
- The 1,224-acre industrial site in Liberty County, Georgia, is located south of Midway on US 17, about one mile south of its intersection with US 84, with I-95 approximately five miles east.
- Planned main access to the site will be from US 17.
- The site borders the Riceboro Southern Railway.
- Portions of the property have been graded to accommodate access roads, while other areas remain undeveloped.
- Tradeport West is a major expansion priority for the Liberty County Development Authority (LCDA), accelerated by a $2 million OneGeorgia Rural Site Development Initiative grant.
- The $2 million grant will fund 7,000 feet of new access road, critical utility extensions, and site preparation across 170 acres of prime industrial property within the next year.
- State Representative and LCDA Board Chairman Al Williams stated that earning ‘GRAD Select’ certification reflects years of deliberate investment and close partnership with GDEcD to ensure the highest standards for speed-to-market and positioning the park to deliver higher-wage jobs as it develops.
- Donald Lovette, Chairman of the Liberty County Board of Commissioners and LCDA Board Member, stated that Liberty County is proud to have developed this asset and excited about its impact in strengthening the local economy.
- The industrial park has already attracted strong interest from large-scale manufacturing prospects.
- The designation reinforces Liberty County’s position as a premier destination, strategically located between the Ports of Savannah and Brunswick and supported by a strong workforce pipeline from Fort Stewart and across the region.
- Completing the GRAD certification program with GDEcD is a proactive way for community planners and landowners to help catalyze economic growth and industrial development in their regions.
- To qualify for GRAD status, available sites must meet the program’s due diligence standards, be reviewed by a third party, and earn the final approval of a board of advisors comprised of public and private sector economic development professionals.
- The “GRAD Select” status is an indication that a site has met or exceeded more rigorous certification requirements.
- The GRAD Program offers more than 70 industrial certified sites that are ready for fast-track industrial projects, thanks to comprehensive due diligence.
- Georgia is investing over $21 million to help rural communities prepare new industrial sites through the Rural Site Development Initiative.
- These grants support site improvements and the addition of new GRAD-certified sites.
