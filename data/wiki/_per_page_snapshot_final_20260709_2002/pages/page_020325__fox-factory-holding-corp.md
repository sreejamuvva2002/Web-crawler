# Fox Factory Holding Corp.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-05-07

**Source:** [Sales in Fox Factory's SSG unit down 9% in first quarter | Bicycle Retailer and Industry News](https://www.bicycleretailer.com/industry-news/2026/05/07/sales-fox-factorys-ssg-unit-down-9-first-quarter)  ·  `page_020325`

## Facts

- The company operates out of Duluth, Ga.
- Fox Factory’s Speciality Sports Group (SSG), which includes its bicycle-product business, reported sales down 8.7% in the first quarter compared to the same period in 2025.
- The decline in SSG sales reflects distributor and dealer inventory destocking and a difficult prior-year comparison due to the industry’s first half 2025 order pull-forward.
- The $10.5 million or 8.7% decrease applies to Specialty Sports Group (“SSG”) net sales.
- The SSG division includes Fox's bicycle business through the Fox, Marzocchi, Ride Concepts, RaceFace, Easton cycling and Lizard Skins brands.
- SSG also includes Fox's Marucci business, which sells baseball and softball gear.
- Company-wide, Fox’s first quarter sales were up 3.9% to $368.7 million.
- The company recorded a net loss for the quarter of $15 million, an improvement on its first quarter 2025 loss of $259.7 million.
- Company-wide gross margin was 28.9%, compared to 30.9% in the first quarter of fiscal 2025.
- The decrease in gross margin was primarily driven by the net impact of tariffs and shifts in product line mix.
- Fox CEO Mike Dennison stated that Q1 would be a tough column for SSG, particularly in bikes, given the strength seen in the first half of the prior year due to industry order pull-forward in 2025.
- Dennison noted the bike environment feels much like last year, with channel inventory improved but volatile, and demand signals remaining muted as consumers are cautious.
- Dennison highlighted progress on new customer relationships and product expansion, particularly in categories like e-bikes, where they see long-term opportunity.
- Dennison commented on the changing landscape in OEMs, stating it is both a challenge and an opportunity for Fox.
- Regarding new OEM customers in the bike market, Dennison cited volatility and significant declines among many players, forecasting stable to slightly up sales for Fox due to product diversification across premium categories between e-bikes and normal mountain bikes.
- Dennison also mentioned the development of new customers, noting a fairly significant rotation of new players versus traditional players, indicating disruption in the industry that Fox is benefiting from through relationships with new players and their new products.
- Dennison mentioned that the Middle East war has disrupted some of its SSG suppliers and customers, without sharing specifics, with impacts expected to be seen in the second-quarter financial report.
- Fox stated there is “potential” it will recover tariff costs paid under the International Emergency Economic Powers Act (IEEPA), but uncertainty about timing and size means it is not including potential recovery in forecasts.
