# 2022 UGA Electric Mobility Summit

**Type:** event  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [2022 UGA Electric Mobility Summit - Georgia Economic Developers Association](https://www.geda.org/events/2022-uga-electric-mobility-summit)  ·  `page_010157`

## Facts

- The 2022 UGA Electric Mobility Summit was held in Athens, GA.
- Georgia is fast becoming a hub of investment and activity in electric mobility.
- The state has a vibrant multi-modal transportation network that includes Atlanta-Hartsfield Jackson International Airport, the Port of Savannah, and an extensive road and rail network.
- The state is poised to be a leader in the adoption of electric mobility technologies for the benefit of the health and resilience of its communities.
- This inaugural meeting focused on how the integration of electric mobility in communities will change Georgia, the educational programs needed to support growth, and the future of electric mobility in Georgia and beyond its borders.
- The event took place on Thursday, April 28 and Friday, April 29.
- An E-Mobility Showcase and Reception was held from 6:00 to 7:30 p.m. at the Delta Innovation Hub, located at 210 Spring Street, Athens, GA.
- Commissioner Tim Echols of the Georgia Public Service Commission delivered opening remarks.
- The main sessions were held at Mahler Hall – Georgia Center, located at 1197 S Lumpkin Street, Athens, GA.
- A session on Opportunities for E-Mobility Technologies was scheduled for 8:30 a.m.
- Stephanie Gossman from Electric Transportation at Georgia Power Company spoke during the morning sessions.
- Russell McMurry, Commissioner of the Georgia Department of Transportation, spoke during the morning sessions.
- Roundtable 1 focused on Perspectives from the E-Mobility Industry and began at 9:30 a.m.
- Stuart Countess, President & CEO of Kia Georgia, Inc., participated in Roundtable 1.
- Joe George, President of Cox Automotive Mobility Solutions, participated in Roundtable 1.
- Steven Jahng, Director of External Affairs and Social Value at SK Battery America, participated in Roundtable 1.
- Lincoln Wood, Electrification Policy Manager at Southern Company, participated in Roundtable 1.
- A session on Education Pathways for E-Mobility: Existing Programs and Future Needs was scheduled for 11:00 a.m.
- Steven Ferguson, Chief Information Officer of the Technical College System of Georgia, spoke during the education pathways session.
- Art Recesso, Chief Innovation Officer of the University System of Georgia, spoke during the education pathways session.
- Roundtable 3, titled A New Mobility Era, began at 1:30 p.m.
- Anne Blair, Director of EV Policy at the Electrification Coalition, participated in Roundtable 3.
- Elyse Hart, Assistant Manager of EV Training and Integration at GM's EV Ecosystem, participated in Roundtable 3.
- Roger Lin, Vice President of Marketing & Government Relations at Ascend Elements, Inc., participated in Roundtable 3.
- Andrew Sunderman, Senior Manager of Product Management for Global Farming Solutions at AGCO Corporation, participated in Roundtable 3.
