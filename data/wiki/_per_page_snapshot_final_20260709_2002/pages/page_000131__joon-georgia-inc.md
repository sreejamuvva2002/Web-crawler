# Joon Georgia, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2022-11

**Source:** [First Hyundai Motor Group supplier to build facility in Bulloch County – Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority](https://savannahjda.com/2022/11/first-hyundai-motor-group-supplier-to-build-facility-in-bulloch-county/)  ·  `page_000131`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Joon Georgia, Inc., an automotive parts manufacturer, will create 630 new jobs and invest $317 million in Bulloch County.
- The company’s new manufacturing facility marks it as the first confirmed supplier for Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America in Bryan County.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that less than two weeks after Hyundai Motor Group's groundbreaking, the region is already seeing its impact.
- Governor Brian Kemp noted that Joon Georgia will be the first of many companies drawn to Georgia to support the Metaplant, creating jobs and opportunity for generations of Georgians.
- Joon Georgia is part of Ajin USA, a current supplier for multiple Hyundai Motor Group facilities.
- The company produces a variety of automotive body parts and electronics for original equipment manufacturers.
- Jung Ho Sea, CEO of Ajin USA, expressed excitement about building Joon Georgia's first plant in Georgia as one of the first Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America automotive suppliers in Bulloch County.
- Joon Georgia, Inc. will create approximately 630 new jobs with competitive benefits in Bulloch County and the State of Georgia.
- The company committed to being the best parts supplier for automotive and EV companies like Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America, as well as a top employer and corporate citizen in Bulloch County and the City of Statesboro.
- Joon Georgia’s new facility will be located at Bruce Yawn Commerce Park (previously known as Southern Gateway Commerce Park) near Statesboro and in close proximity to the Port of Savannah.
- Connections to the port will allow both Hyundai and Joon Georgia to access global markets quickly and efficiently.
- Operations at the Bruce Yawn Commerce Park are expected to begin in mid-2024, with hiring starting in mid-2023.
- Billy Allen, Chair of the Development Authority of Bulloch County, welcomed Joon Georgia to the Statesboro-Bulloch County community and region.
- Billy Allen noted that the site's proximity to Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America and local education resources like Georgia Southern University and Ogeechee Technical College will provide critical workforce support.
- Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy Alyce Thornhill represented the GDEcD Global Commerce team on the project in partnership with the Development Authority of Bulloch County, Georgia Quick Start, Georgia Ports Authority, and Georgia Power.
- Workforce development for the new facility will be supported by Quick Start, the top-ranked workforce training program in the nation.
- Partnering with the Technical College System of Georgia, Quick Start will help assess workers, train new employees on unique processes, and develop customized job-specific training using current techniques and media.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson called Joon Georgia's announcement a landmark moment for driving Georgia’s automotive industry into the future.
- Pat Wilson stated that Hyundai Motor Group, its suppliers, and the Savannah area JDA are creating generational opportunities through regional collaboration that shares benefits across county lines.
- Pat Wilson expressed excitement about welcoming the company to Georgia and looking forward to its future in Bulloch County.
- Georgia is positioned as a hub for the electric mobility industry due to assets that make the automotive industry successful.
- Since 2020, more than 30 EV-related projects have contributed over $13.3 billion in investment and announced more than 18,100 jobs in Georgia.
- On October 25, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America officially broke ground in neighboring Bryan County on its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility.
- Non-affiliated suppliers connected to the Hyundai project are predicted to invest approximately $1 billion in other locations around the state.
