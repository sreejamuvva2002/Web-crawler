# Ecoplastic Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-01-05

**Source:** [Kia and HMG Supplier to Build Manufacturing Facility in Bulloch County | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-release/longtime-kia-and-hmg-supplier-build-manufacturing-facility-bulloch-county)  ·  `page_000020`

## Facts

- Ecoplastic Corporation will invest approximately $205 million in a new manufacturing facility in Bulloch County.
- The project will create 456 new jobs.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced the investment and job creation on January 5, 2023.
- Ecoplastic Corporation is a Korea-based company established in June 1984 as Apollo Ind Co., Ltd. that manufactures plastic automobile parts for original equipment manufacturers and other suppliers.
- The company is a Tier-1 supplier for Hyundai Motor Group (HMG) and Kia.
- Shang Han serves as President and CEO of Ecoplastic Corporation.
- The new facility will be located at 4822 Highway 301 South Register near Statesboro.
- The plant will feature injection molding and painting capabilities.
- The facility will produce exterior parts, bumpers and garnishes, interior parts, consoles, and trims specifically for electric vehicles.
- Ecoplastic expects to begin operations by October 2024.
- The company will be hiring for HR, finance, parts development, quality control, facility maintenance, injection, and paint production positions.
- Billy Allen serves as Chair of the Development Authority of Bulloch County.
- Alyce Thornhill is the Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy at the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s Global Commerce team.
- The project was executed in partnership with the Development Authority of Bulloch County, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia EMC.
- Pat Wilson serves as Commissioner for the Georgia Department of Economic Development.
- Ecoplastic will serve its client HMGMA (Hyundai Motor Group Manufacturing America) from its new Statesboro location, which offers close proximity to Georgia's ports system.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated that the company will be able to serve the Hyundai Metaplant well from Statesboro due to its community and port access.
- Billy Allen expressed confidence that Bulloch County's location, workforce, and quality of life will help Ecoplastic succeed.
- Pat Wilson noted that Georgia is building a supplier network for the Hyundai metaplant in Bryan County and spreading suppliers across the region to provide well-paying, high-tech manufacturing jobs.
- Georgia is positioned as a hub for the electric mobility industry, with more than 30 EV-related projects announced since 2020 bringing more than $21 billion in investment and more than 26,300 jobs to the state.
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America officially broke ground on October 25 for its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility in neighboring Bryan County.
- Offsite suppliers connected to the Hyundai project were predicted to invest approximately $1 billion in other locations around Georgia.
- Ecoplastic established its first Technical Research Center in 1987 and was publicly listed on the Korean Stock Exchange in March 2000.
- The company currently employs more than 770 employees globally.
- Ecoplastic recorded sales revenue of more than $700 million in 2021.
