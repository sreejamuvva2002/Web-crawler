# Aurubis Richmond

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2022-06-18

**Source:** [Aurubis Richmond in Augusta will be the nation's first recycled-copper smelter](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/aurubis-richmond-augusta-nations-first-090058105.html)  ·  `page_001272`

## Facts

- Local, state, federal and German officials welcomed a $340 million copper recycling and smelting plant in Augusta.
- The project exemplifies greener industry and Georgia’s economic focus on the growing electric vehicle market.
- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp said “Welcome to the future” during remarks to about 100 people at Augusta Corporate Park for the ceremonial groundbreaking.
- Aurubis CEO Roland Harings stated the 125-employee facility is slated to start production in January 2024.
- Three copper smelting facilities already operate in the United States, with two located in Arizona and one in Utah.
- The existing U.S. smelting facilities process directly mined copper, whereas Augusta’s Aurubis facility will smelt copper extracted from items such as pipes, wiring, and computers.
- The Augusta facility will form the recycled copper into large sheets for manufacturers to use in their finished products.
- Traditional liquid-fuel cars typically contain 18-49 pounds of copper, according to the Copper Development Association.
- Hybrid electric vehicles contain about 85 pounds of copper, while plug-in hybrid EVs contain 132 pounds, according to the Copper Development Association.
- Pat Wilson, commissioner of the Georgia Department of Economic Development, visited Aurubis’ global headquarters in Hamburg, Germany, which helped steer the state’s decision to focus on the electrification of the auto industry.
- In the six months prior to the announcement, Georgia announced that EV manufacturers Rivian and Hyundai would open vehicle production plants in the state.
- Pat Wilson described Aurubis along with the Rivian and Hyundai investments as long-term strategic investments in the EV supply chain that will pay generational returns to their communities, the state, and the world.
- Gov. Brian Kemp stated that the recycling and supply chain component of companies like Aurubis is only going to grow, calling it a decades-long start to a successful business model.
- The Aurubis Richmond plant will process about 90,000 tons of complex recycling materials annually to extract about 35,000 tons of blister copper, according to Aurubis.
- Aurubis Richmond will be the company's second facility in the United States.
- Aurubis Buffalo employs about 650 workers at an upstate New York plant that produces copper and brass sheets for products such as roofing copper and brass hose nozzles.
- Copper is highly sought after globally due to its malleability, conductive properties, and role as a strengthening component in alloys.
- The Copper Alliance states that copper can be recycled repeatedly without any loss of performance and there is no quality difference between mined and recycled copper.
- Copper closed at $4.12 per pound in global market trading on the Thursday prior to publication.
