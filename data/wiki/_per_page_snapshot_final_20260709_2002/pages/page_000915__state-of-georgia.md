## Facts

- Meta has announced three new, large solar projects in Georgia as part of an agreement for Walton EMC to provide renewable energy equivalent to 100% of Meta's data center energy usage in Newton County.
- Democrat Sen. Jon Ossoff stated there is broad bipartisan support in Georgia for securing energy independence and ensuring a robust supply of affordable energy that is not destroying the environment.
- The all-Republican Georgia Public Service Commission tripled the budget for Georgia Power's Make Ready Program, putting $53 million toward upgrading electric vehicle charging stations over the next three years.
- Republican Commissioner Tim Echols provided a major push for the Georgia Public Service Commission's decision to triple the Make Ready Program budget.
- The Republican-controlled Georgia House of Representatives passed House Bill 406, expanding EV-charging opportunities across the state, by a vote of 161-0.
- QCells is building a $2.31 billion solar panel manufacturing plant in Cartersville, Georgia, located about 35 miles northwest of Atlanta.
- The QCells plant in Cartersville will hire 2,000 workers and possibly meet 30% of total U.S. solar panel demand by 2027.
- QCells announced the expansion of its site in Dalton, Georgia, bringing with it 510 more jobs.
- Republican Rep. Marjorie Taylor Greene praised QCells' expansion in Dalton, Georgia.
- The passage of the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law and the CHIPS Act was made possible by Georgia and the 2021 election of Sens. Raphael Warnock and Jon Ossoff.
- The Biden administration's Inflation Reduction Act and Justice 40 initiative have given all Americans a stake in the new green economy, with Georgia playing a key role.
- Nationally, data shows that over 60% of major projects receiving federal investment are taking place in districts represented by Republican policymakers who voted against the IRA.
- The Justice 40 initiative has set a goal that 40% of the overall benefits of federal investment will find their way to marginalized communities that have been historically underserved and overburdened by pollution.
- Recent polling from the Atlanta Journal-Constitution illustrates that 80% of Democrats, a slim majority of independents, and roughly half of Republicans support Georgia becoming an EV hub.
- Republican Gov. Brian Kemp declared in his State of the State address that by the end of his second term, Georgia will be 'the electric mobility capital of America.'
