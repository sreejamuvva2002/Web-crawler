# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-06-06

**Source:** [How the Georgia-Korea Connection Energizes Opportunities | Georgia](https://georgia.org/blogs/how-georgia-korea-connection-energizes-opportunities)  ·  `page_000221`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) announced a state-of-the-art vehicle manufacturing facility and battery joint venture two years ago.
- The HMGMA project represents an estimated $7.59 billion in investment.
- The HMGMA project will create 8,500 new jobs for the Savannah region over the next eight years.
- The HMGMA facility is on track to begin full production in fourth quarter 2024.
- Suppliers to HMGMA announced investments totaling more than $2.5 billion across 11 counties in Georgia as of July 2024.
- HMGMA supplier investments span west and middle Georgia.
- Kia Georgia's lineup expanded to include the EV9, which is the first electric car expected to roll off the line in Georgia.
- The Kia EV9 began rolling off the line at Kia Georgia's existing facility in West Point in May 2024.
- Kia Georgia has an annual production capacity of 350,000 units.
- Kia Georgia manufactures the Telluride SUV, Sorento SUV models, Sportage SUV models, and the all-electric EV9 SUV.
- The battery joint venture is a collaboration between Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solutions.
- The Hyundai-LG Energy Solutions battery JV will support production of 300,000 units of Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis EVs annually at full operations.
- SK Battery America operates existing facilities that support EV manufacturers across the Southeast.
- Qcells is a solar panel manufacturer located in northern Georgia.
- Georgia is advancing its position as a central hub for the "Battery Belt."
- The State of Georgia has hosted a Korean consular presence since 1971.
- The State of Georgia has maintained continuous diplomatic representation in Seoul since 1985.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp conducted his first economic development mission to Korea in 2019.
- The Korea Trade-Investment Promotion Agency (KOTRA) opened its Atlanta office in 2022.
- The State of Georgia received the James A. Van Fleet Award from The Korea Society in 2023.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development and the Korea International Trade Association (KITA) both received a 2023 Van Fleet Award.
- Metro Atlanta ranks among the top five U.S. metros for its Korean American community size.
- Korean communities are actively growing across the state of Georgia.
- The "Seoul of the South™" food tour, organized by Explore Gwinnett, fosters ties between Gwinnett County's hospitality industry and the local Korean community.
- The Seoul of the South™ food tour is featured in Taste of the South Magazine and regularly sells out.
- Six dates of the Seoul of the South™ food tour scheduled between May and September 2024 sold out by March 2024.
- Explore Gwinnett developed a DIY Seoul of the South™ guide to accommodate demand for experiencing Korean food and culture.
- Since 2022, Hyundai Motor Group has donated over $2 million to regional nonprofits in the Savannah area, focusing on safety and education.
- HMGMA engaged students from the Savannah College of Art and Design in multiple community projects.
- HMGMA student projects included designing a parade float for the 200th Anniversary of the St. Patrick's Day Parade and designing the Bryan County water tank.
- Hyundai Motor Group sponsored a two-week summer study abroad program to Korea for Columbus State University students in 2024.
- The Columbus State University summer study abroad program featured Korean history, culture, and its growing economic importance to Georgia and the United States.
- Daily direct flights operate between Atlanta and Seoul via Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport.
- The Delta Air Lines-Korean Air joint venture is celebrating its sixth year of operation.
- The Delta Air Lines-Korean Air joint venture connects travelers to more than 290 destinations in the Americas and 80 destinations in Asia.
- Georgia is the only U.S. state where tourism visitation from South Korea has recovered to and exceeded 2019 levels.
- Visitors from South Korea to Georgia totaled 33,500 in 2022, representing a year-over-year increase of more than 360% according to Tourism Economics data.
- Estimated spending by Korean visitors in Georgia in 2022 surpassed pre-pandemic 2019 levels.
