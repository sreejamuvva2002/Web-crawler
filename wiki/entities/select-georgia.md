## Timeline

- **2015** — Georgia attempted to recruit a major automotive OEM to Bryan County but lost the project; leaders formed a joint development authority and preserved land for future projects.
- **2018** — SK Battery America announced historic investment in Jackson County.
- **2019** — Hanwha Qcells opened its first Georgia facility.
- **2019** — Groundbreaking for SK Battery America plant, bringing 2,000 jobs and $1.67 billion investment.
- **Oct 2021** — Hyundai Metaplant America selected Georgia as site.
- **May 2022** — Hyundai Metaplant America agreement formally signed; LG Energy Solution joint venture added, raising total investment to >$7.5 billion.
- **Aug 2022** — Inflation Reduction Act signed into law, further accelerating EV investments in Georgia.
- **2021** — Rivian announced largest economic development project in Georgia’s history: 7,500 jobs and $5 billion investment.

## Key facts

- Georgia’s electric mobility ecosystem was deliberately built years before federal incentives.
- The state aligned workforce programs with industry to ensure a long-term talent pipeline.
- Early leadership positioned Georgia ahead of the Inflation Reduction Act, attracting companies like Hanwha Qcells and Hyundai before the law passed.
- Policy incentives accelerated existing momentum but companies chose Georgia because of its established ecosystem, proven workforce pipeline, and infrastructure planning.
- Long-term site readiness after a 2015 OEM loss helped secure later wins such as Hyundai’s Metaplant.
- Georgia Power extended EV charger rebate programs through 2028 and launched a vehicle-to-grid pilot using electric school buses-the first of its kind in the Southern Company system.
- The state is expanding EV charging infrastructure across urban and rural communities to alleviate range anxiety.
- Georgia’s flexible manufacturing approach allows OEMs like Hyundai, Kia, and Rivian to scale internal combustion or electric production based on consumer demand.
