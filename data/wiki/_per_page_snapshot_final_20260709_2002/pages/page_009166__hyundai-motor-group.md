# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** research_and_development  ·  **Published:** 2026-04-29

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Group and Georgia Tech Sign MOU to Further Collaborate on Hydrogen Mobility Developmen](https://www.conceptcarz.com/a60822/hyundai-motor-group-georgia-tech.aspx)  ·  `page_009166`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group announced a multi-year partnership with the Georgia Institute of Technology to advance hydrogen-powered transportation, applied research, education, and zero-emissions vehicles in Georgia.
- Hyundai Motor North America is donating four NEXO fuel cell electric SUVs exclusively for campus use at Georgia Tech.
- Hyundai Motor Group is donating a hydrogen electrolyzer project that will be installed at Georgia Tech's North Avenue Research Area (NARA).
- The donated NEXO SUVs and hydrogen electrolyzer will support campus operations and interdisciplinary research across engineering, sustainability, energy systems, and public policy.
- Ken Ramírez, Executive Vice President and Head of Global Energy and Hydrogen Business at Hyundai Motor Group, stated the collaboration combines Georgia Tech's research with Hyundai's global experience to drive the energy transition and inspire future mobility leaders.
- Ken Ramírez is a Georgia Tech alumnus (class of 1991) and currently serves on the Georgia Tech Advisory Board.
- Randy Parker, President and CEO of Hyundai Motor North America, stated the donation of NEXO fuel cell SUVs will expand the clean mobility footprint on campus and provide real-world experience with zero-emissions transportation technology.
- Ángel Cabrera, President of the Georgia Institute of Technology, stated the expanded partnership advances hydrogen research, reduces campus emissions, and strengthens Georgia's role in the future of clean mobility.
- The installation of the hydrogen electrolyzer at NARA positions Georgia Tech as one of the most visible real-world testbeds for hydrogen mobility in the U.S.
- Research focus includes exploring hydrogen-based systems and mobility solutions within Engineering.
- Research focus includes assessing environmental benefits of hydrogen technologies within Sustainability.
- Research focus includes understanding integration of hydrogen fuel cells into current infrastructure within Energy systems.
- Research focus includes evaluating regulatory and social implications of hydrogen adoption within Public policy.
- The initiative uses Georgia Tech as a living laboratory for clean transportation, with faculty and students studying real-world hydrogen technology performance, infrastructure requirements for large-scale deployment, and environmental impacts of hydrogen energy systems.
- Insights from the initiative aim to inform and accelerate widespread hydrogen technology use in campuses, fleets, cities, and freight corridors.
- The partnership supports Georgia Tech's strategic plan goal of expanding zero-emissions vehicles powered by sustainable energy sources.
- Beyond hydrogen energy, the collaboration aims to advance innovation in autonomous driving, electric vehicle batteries, charging infrastructure, materials science, and cybersecurity.
- Hyundai Motor Group's regional presence in Georgia includes the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) and HTWO Logistics, a clean logistics partnership operating Hyundai XCIENT fuel cell heavy-duty trucks near Savannah.
- The collaboration reinforces the link between education, research, and Hyundai Motor Group's long-term goal of achieving carbon neutrality by 2045.
- The partnership aims to generate workforce talent and position Georgia as a national leader in hydrogen and zero-emissions transportation.
- Additional announcements regarding research projects, educational programs, and vehicle deployment are expected in the coming months.
- A new initiative brings NEXO hydrogen fuel cell SUVs and fueling infrastructure to campus to advance research, workforce development, and zero-emissions logistics.
