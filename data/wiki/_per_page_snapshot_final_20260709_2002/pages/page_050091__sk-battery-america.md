# SK Battery America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-03-06

**Source:** [SK Battery America lays off nearly 1,000 workers at Georgia plant](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/sk-battery-america-lays-off-180100202.html)  ·  `page_050091`

## Facts

- SK Battery America is a subsidiary of South Korea-based SK On.
- SK Battery America is laying off hundreds of workers from its production facility in Commerce, Georgia.
- The layoffs are occurring as electric vehicle battery demand slows and federal incentives dry up.
- SK Battery America filed a Worker Adjustment and Retraining Notification on Friday.
- The notification informed Georgia of job cuts affecting 958 people.
- Affected workers will receive pay and benefits for the next 60 days.
- Friday, March 6, was the last day of work for the affected employees.
- The Commerce site previously had 2,566 workers, and the layoffs reduced the headcount to 1,600.
- Joe Guy Collier serves as SK Battery America's spokesperson.
- The company will work with affected workers during the transition.
- SK Battery America stated it remains committed to Georgia and building a robust U.S. supply chain for advanced battery manufacturing.
- The company is pursuing future customers in the Battery Energy Storage System arena.
- Many automakers and battery companies are making cutbacks due to slower sales and a pullback of federal incentives under the current administration, according to the Federal Reserve Bank of Dallas.
- Companies in the sector are looking to cater to growing data center demand for backup power and energy storage systems.
- In December, Ford Motor Co., a customer of SK Battery America, announced it is shifting its electrification plans to include hybrid vehicles and smaller, more affordable EVs.
- Ford also announced plans to grow its energy storage business.
- Ford expects to record a $19.5 billion write-down due to this shift.
- Ford dissolved its BlueOval SK battery joint venture with SK On as it shifts away from EVs.
- Ford canceled the electric F-150 Lightning due to sluggish sales.
- In 2023, SK Battery America announced plans to expand its Commerce site workforce by several hundred employees to meet rising EV demand.
- The expansion exceeded the company's previous goal of 2,600 employees.
- The Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA) fined SK Battery America twice for exposing workers to respiratory hazards.
- Law firm Strauss Borrelli announced an investigation into SK Battery America for allegedly failing to notify the 958 employees per the WARN Act.
- The WARN Act mandates that employers notify workers in writing at least 60 days before a plant closure or job cuts take effect.
- Spokesperson Joe Guy Collier stated that SK Battery America's workforce reduction complies with WARN Act guidelines.
