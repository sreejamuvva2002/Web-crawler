# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-11-24

**Source:** [Hyundai suppliers fined by OSHA following worker's fatal forklift accident - The Current](https://thecurrentga.org/2025/11/24/hyundai-suppliers-fined-by-osha-following-workers-fatal-forklift-accident/)  ·  `page_001226`

## Facts

- The federal workplace safety agency fined three companies approximately $27,000 for violations that contributed to the death of a Korean worker in March.
- The fines conclude one of the most gruesome chapters in the construction of the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- The fine amount is less than the retail price of a new electric vehicle made at the facility.
- The facility has developed a checkered reputation for safety.
- OSHA launched an investigation after Korean worker Sunbok You was killed by a forklift at the battery plant in March.
- A Bryan County Sheriff’s report described the incident as leaving a long bloody trail on the factory floor.
- The fatality occurred just days before the metaplant’s grand opening, where the chief executive and other senior Hyundai Motor Group officials extolled the company’s safety culture.
- Fines were assessed against Beyond Iron Construction LLC., which was helping construct the battery plant.
- Fines were assessed against SBY America, which employed Sunbok You.
- Fines were assessed against HL-GA Battery, a joint venture between Hyundai Motor Group and LG Battery Solutions.
- The battery plant was targeted in a September raid by U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) and other federal agencies.
- OSHA inspectors concluded Beyond Iron and SBY allowed forklift drivers to exceed speed limits and failed to ensure enough safety measures.
- HL-GA Battery was cited for failing to submit required reports after injuries.
- The original fines exceeded $160,000 but were reduced on appeal.
- Safety managers and documents suggest a culture of lax workplace safety at the site.
- A 2024 investigation by The Current GA found approximately 10 serious accidents had not been reported by Korean suppliers at the Ellabell facility between January 2023 and May 2024.
- One unreported incident involved a Korean engineer being crushed in a conveyor belt he was helping to install at the Hyundai Glovis facility in 2024.
- First responders flew the injured engineer to the hospital while he was still alive.
- It is unknown whether the engineer survived his injuries, which included a crushed torso.
- Three men have died on site during the construction of the metaplant in Ellabell.
- The first death was construction worker Victor Gamboa in 2023 after a fall due to faulty safety equipment.
- Gamboa's employer, Eastern Constructors Inc., was originally fined approximately $160,000 for allowing him to use a defective body harness with notable indicators of damage and deterioration.
- The fine against Eastern Constructors Inc. was reduced on appeal to approximately $20,000.
- HMGMA later severed ties with Eastern Constructors Inc.
- Another death occurred in May 2025 when an American worker was crushed by a load that fell from a forklift.
- In September, Hyundai announced it was installing a new official at the site to coordinate safety measures across all suppliers at the metaplant.
- This announcement came days after the OSHA fine against HL-GA Battery and the federal raid that resulted in more than 400 workers being detained and deported, including approximately 300 Koreans.
- At the time of the federal raid, the United Automobile Workers union issued a statement criticizing Hyundai for dangerous work conditions at the metaplant.
- Earlier this month, a nonprofit labor organization in California filed a lawsuit against Hyundai and Kia, its Montgomery, Ala. manufacturing facility, and its west Georgia supplier plant for alleged human trafficking and child labor abuses.
- The lawsuit claims these practices give Hyundai and its subsidiaries an unfair pricing advantage when bidding for state contracts.
- Last week, Hyundai Chief Executive José Muñoz said the White House phoned him to apologize for September’s immigration raid.
- Speaking at a business leaders conference in Singapore, Muñoz said Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp also called him.
- Muñoz quoted Kemp as saying, “I don’t know what happened. This is not state jurisdiction.”
- Citing the need to “bring in talent” to the U.S., President Donald Trump appeared earlier this month to condemn the raid.
- Trump told Fox News host Laura Ingraham there are not enough American workers to perform the “very complicated” and “dangerous” task of building car batteries.
