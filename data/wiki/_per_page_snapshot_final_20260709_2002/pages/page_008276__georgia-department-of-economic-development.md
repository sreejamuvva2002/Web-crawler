# Georgia Department of Economic Development

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2017-06-01

**Source:** [Georgia Continues to Lead in Economic Development - Expansion Solutions](https://www.expansionsolutionsmagazine.com/georgia-continues-to-lead-in-economic-development/)  ·  `page_008276`

## Facts

- Site Selection magazine ranked Georgia’s business climate No. 1 for the fourth consecutive year.
- Since Governor Nathan Deal took office in January 2011, the state has announced 2,380 projects, $30.7 billion investment and 178,641 jobs.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) goal is to create quality jobs and investment opportunities using an integrated approach that recruits traditional industrial development, supports creative industries, provides growth resources, and partners with universities and technical colleges for workforce development.
- Manufacturing remains one of Georgia’s strongest industries, with 10,162 manufacturing facilities employing 386,529 individuals.
- The annual manufacturing wage in Georgia is 12 percent higher than the overall average for jobs in the state.
- Georgia ranks No. 1 in workforce development and has one of the largest university systems in the U.S., housing more than 50,000 annual graduates from 31 public colleges and universities.
- Governor Deal created the High Demand Career Initiative in 2014 to connect GDEcD, the University System of Georgia, the Technical College System of Georgia, and key private-sector business leaders to meet future workforce needs.
- Exclusive Centers of Innovation provide over 1,500 businesses each year with tools to connect, compete and grow globally, covering six strategic industries: Aerospace, Agribusiness, Energy Technology, Information Technology, Logistics and Manufacturing.
- Ninety-nine point eight percent of registered establishments in Georgia are classified as small businesses, with more than 1,000 receiving one-on-one assistance from GDEcD’s entrepreneur and small business team in FY16.
- In 2016, total trade between Georgia and the world exceeded $121.7 billion and exports exceeded $35.5 billion, maintaining national rankings as the 11th largest exporting state and seventh largest importing state.
