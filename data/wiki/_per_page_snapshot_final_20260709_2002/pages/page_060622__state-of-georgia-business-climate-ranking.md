# State of Georgia Business Climate Ranking

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2018-11-05

**Source:** [World Trade Centers Association](https://www.wtca.org/news/ga-s-business-climate-no-1-for-6th-straight-year)  ·  `page_060622`

## Facts

- Gov. Nathan Deal announced that Georgia's business climate has been ranked No. 1 in the nation for the sixth consecutive year by Site Selection on November 5, 2018.
- Georgia is the first state to hold this top ranking for six consecutive years under leadership by the same governor.
- Gov. Nathan Deal stated that people from all over the country have come to Georgia's communities to find opportunity, jobs, hope, and prosperity.
- The six-year streak reflects the strength of Georgia's business-friendly environment and strategic investments made across the state.
- The ranking resulted from exceptional collaboration between public and private sector partners creating economic opportunities in Georgia's communities.
- Conservative policies have attracted more businesses to the state each year.
- Georgia has cultivated an economic environment that rewards hard work and innovation, generates investments in local communities, and improves quality of life for families.
- The state has cut taxes and made deeper investments in its workforce since first earning the distinction.
- Gov. Nathan Deal expressed pride in leading the nation in economic development and looking forward to Georgia retaining its position as an exceptional place to do business, work, learn, and call home.
- Site Selection releases rankings for the top states to do business each November.
- Site Selection's business climate rankings are based on a survey of corporate real estate executives and site consultants.
- The ranking index includes tax burden criteria from the Tax Foundation and KPMG's Location Matters analysis.
- The index also considers qualifying projects resident in the Conway Projects Database, which tracks new and expanded business facility activity worldwide.
- Georgia has been ranked among the top 10 states throughout the last decade.
- Georgia held the top spot for five years prior to this sixth consecutive year.
- Mark Arend, editor-in-chief of Site Selection, noted that a six-year top business climate winning streak is highly unusual.
- Arend attributed the streak to Governor Deal's commitment to making Georgia a competitive location for business early in his administration and every year since.
- Capital investors report that Georgia delivers fiscal soundness, logistics infrastructure, competitive business costs, and workers required today and in the future.
- Gov. Deal was joined at the news conference by Mark Arend, GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson, former GDEcD Commissioners Attorney General Chris Carr, and Chris Cummiskey.
- Pat Wilson stated that being recognized for having the best business climate in the nation for six years is a testament to the leadership of Governor Deal and the General Assembly.
- Wilson highlighted that the last eight years have been unprecedented for Georgia in terms of economic development from job creation to located projects and investment.
- Site Selection, published by Conway Data Inc., delivers expansion planning information to 48,000 executives of fast-growing firms.
- Site Selection is available via Site Selection Online and publishes e-newsletters including The Site Selection Dispatch, Life Sciences Report, Aerospace Report, Energy Report, Site Selection International, The FDI Report, TrustBelt.com, and the OnSITE Travel blog.
