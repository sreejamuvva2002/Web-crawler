# Archer Aviation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-04-17

**Source:** [Archer Aviation wants to help New Yorkers skip airport traffic with electric air taxis | TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2025/04/17/archer-aviation-wants-to-help-new-yorkers-skip-airport-traffic-with-electric-air-taxis/?trk=article-ssr-frontend-pulse_little-text-block)  ·  `page_016931`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation unveiled a proposed air taxi network for New York City in partnership with United Airlines that would allow passengers to add an Archer ride to traditional airline tickets.
- The proposed NYC network will feature nine core nodes connecting Manhattan to JFK, LaGuardia, Newark, Downtown Skyport, east and west side heliports, Teterboro, Westchester, and Long Island Republic.
- Archer's aircraft is a five-seat electric vertical takeoff and landing (eVTOL) vehicle named Midnight.
- The company is waiting on Federal Aviation Administration approval for the Midnight aircraft before it can begin testing planned routes.
- Archer has only flown the Midnight autonomously without humans inside, whereas competitors Joby Aviation and Beta Technologies have both conducted piloted flights.
- CEO Adam Goldstein expressed hope that Archer will achieve necessary FAA certifications in 2026 and plans to provide an update on piloted flights at its next earnings call.
- Archer went public in 2021 via a special-purpose acquisition merger and has raised $3.36 billion to date through public and private fundraises.
- United Airlines will assist with aircraft storage, maintenance, charging setup, and vertiport construction at airports for the NYC network.
- Archer partnered with fixed-base operators Atlantic Aviation, Signature, and Modern Aviation to operate Manhattan helipads, provide passenger access, and help establish charging infrastructure.
- CCO Nikhil Goel stated that New York is the No. 1 helicopter commuting city in the world outside of São Paulo, making it compelling for replacing helicopters with Archer aircraft due to existing routes, familiar air traffic patterns, and available FBO/landing facilities.
- Archer plans to initially deploy up to five aircraft to NYC and other cities to practice running routes before a full launch.
- The company's long-term goal is to operate hundreds of aircraft across several cities over the next 10 to 20 years.
- Archer began constructing Midnight aircraft at its production facility in Georgia last month.
- The Georgia production facility was built in partnership with strategic investor Stellantis.
- Archer plans to build 650 aircraft annually by 2030.
- Archer also plans to launch air taxi services in San Francisco and Miami, though timelines remain dependent on FAA certification.
- The company has not yet selected its first U.S. launch city.
- Archer plans to launch an air taxi service in Abu Dhabi later this year due to less strict regulations there.
