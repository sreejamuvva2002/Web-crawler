# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-03-26

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America Celebrates Grand Opening, Powering U.S. Economic Growth](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/hyundai-motor-group-metaplant-america-celebrates-grand-opening-powering-us-economic-growth-302412605.html)  ·  `page_002788`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America is the key pillar of the Group's $12.6 billion investment in Georgia.
- It is the largest economic development project in Georgia's history.
- The vehicle assembly and battery plant will produce up to 500,000 electric and hybrid vehicles annually for Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis brands.
- Hyundai Motor Group commits to an additional $21 billion investment from 2025 to 2028 to drive U.S. manufacturing growth.
- Since entering the U.S., Hyundai Motor Group has invested $20.5 billion in the U.S., creating directly and indirectly more than 570,000 American jobs.
- HMGMA hosted its Grand Opening celebration on March 26, 2025.
- The ceremony marks the completion of the project just two and a half years after breaking ground.
- Euisun Chung serves as Executive Chair of Hyundai Motor Group.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp attended the grand opening event.
- U.S. Representative Buddy Carter attended the grand opening event.
- Jaehoon Chang serves as Vice Chair of Hyundai Motor Group.
- José Muñoz is President and CEO of Hyundai Motor Company.
- Ho Sung Song is President and CEO of Kia Corporation.
- Governor Kemp highlighted collaborations between HMGMA and Georgia's universities and technical colleges, including Georgia Quick Start's on-site training facility.
- HMG entered a landmark agreement with the State of Georgia in May 2022 to establish the facility in Ellabell, Georgia.
- The site is located 20 miles outside of historic Savannah.
- HMGMA was officially formed by June 2022.
- Groundbreaking occurred within four months of the company's formation.
- On October 3, 2024, the first vehicle – a Hyundai IONIQ 5 – rolled off the assembly line.
- Hyundai's IONIQ 9 three-row electric SUV is now in production at HMGMA.
- The first Kia model to be built at the facility will join production in 2026.
- The plant initially has capacity to produce 300,000 vehicles annually.
- Investment in Georgia related to HMGMA and joint battery ventures with LG Energy Solution and SK On totals $12.6 billion.
- The project includes a total of 8,500 jobs at HMGMA by 2031.
- Total investments in Georgia are expected to create nearly 40,000 direct and indirect jobs.
- Expected $4.6 billion in individual earnings every year according to the Center for Automotive Research.
- As of the end of 2024, more than $2.5 billion in capital investment and approximately 6,900 jobs have been announced by HMGMA suppliers in 12 counties across the state.
- Supplier investments more than double the additional $1 billion investment originally anticipated.
- The Group announced a significant investment of $21 billion in the U.S. from 2025 to 2028.
- $9 billion will be invested to establish an annual production capacity in the U.S. of 1.2 million vehicles.
- The Group plans to expand HMGMA's annual production capacity to a total of 500,000 units, an increase of 200,000 units from the original plan.
- Existing U.S. production sites – Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Alabama and Kia Autoland Georgia – will also see improvements in production facilities.
- $6 billion will be allocated to enhance the Group's parts and logistics business.
- The Group is establishing an Electric Arc Furnace-based integrated steel mill in the U.S. through affiliate Hyundai Steel, with an annual steel production capacity of 2.7 million tons.
- The mill will supply high-quality automotive steel plates to HMGMA when commercial steel production begins in 2029.
- The Group will invest an additional $6 billion to drive innovation and expand strategic partnerships with U.S. companies in autonomous driving, robotics, artificial intelligence (AI), and advanced air mobility (AAM).
- Through the $21 billion commitment, the Group anticipates creating 14,000 new direct full-time jobs in the U.S. by 2028.
- The overall economic impact is expected to generate more than 100,000 direct and indirect job opportunities across related industries.
- HMGMA's architecture blends advanced technology with design elements reflecting Hyundai's brand identity and the natural beauty of the Savannah area.
- HMGMA integrated proof-of-concept manufacturing technologies from Hyundai Motor Group Innovation Center Singapore (HMGICS).
- All processes including order collection, procurement, logistics, and production are optimized using AI and real-time data.
- Cutting-edge robotics and vision systems ensure high-quality vehicle production.
- Automation in HMGMA's assembly shop is nearly double that of the Group's existing facilities.
- The facility uses automated guided vehicles for logistics, Boston Dynamic's Spot for exterior quality inspection in the weld shop, and parking robots in the assembly shop.
- Oscar Kwon serves as CEO of HMGMA.
- Employees at the plant are known as "Meta Pros".
- A solar parking lot at the front of the site will generate 5.2 megawatts of energy, accounting for roughly five percent of the Metaplant's total power consumption.
- Solar panels are installed above 1,878 parking spaces to provide shade and produce electricity.
- A 41-acre ecological park with trails, running tracks, sports fields, and picnic areas is under construction in front of the Metaplant for employees.
- HMGMA deployed Hyundai Motor XCIENT heavy-duty hydrogen fuel-cell electric trucks for clean logistics operations.
- A total of 21 XCIENT trucks are currently in operation at the facility.
- These trucks represent more than a third of the Glovis America truck fleet at HMGMA.
- The trucks transport vehicle parts from suppliers across the region to the 2,906-acre Megasite where HMGMA is located on a daily basis.
- Since 2022, Hyundai and Genesis have donated nearly $4.5 million to local organizations in Georgia through social responsibility initiatives.
- Kia's Autoland Georgia factory in West Point has provided over $22 million in support of Georgia communities since 2009.
- Kia's support includes more than $6 million for educational programs such as the THINC College & Career Academy and SAE's "A World in Motion."
- HMGMA is Hyundai Motor Group's first dedicated electrified vehicle mass-production plant located in Bryan County, GA.
