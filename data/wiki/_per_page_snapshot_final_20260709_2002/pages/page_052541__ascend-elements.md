# Ascend Elements

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Polish Investment and Trade Agency (PAIH) Names Ascend Elements’ Project Apex 2 Manufacturing Investment of the Year](https://www.01net.it/polish-investment-and-trade-agency-paih-names-ascend-elements-project-apex-2-manufacturing-investment-of-the-year/)  ·  `page_052541`

## Facts

- The Polish Investment and Trade Agency (PAIH) named Ascend Elements’ Project Apex 2 its Manufacturing Investment of the Year.
- Project Apex 2 is focused on developing the EU’s first fully integrated battery materials refining center.
- The facility will turn spent lithium-ion batteries into new, low-carbon cathode materials using Hydro-to-Cathode® technology.
- The Republic of Poland granted Ascend Elements USD 320 million (approximately 1.22 billion PLN) to advance construction of its first European pCAM and Lithium manufacturing facility.
- This grant is one of the largest ever made by Poland’s Ministry of Economic Development and Technology.
- The award was announced at the PAIH 2026 Investor’s Gala in Warsaw.
- Linh Austin serves as President and CEO of Ascend Elements.
- "Project Apex 2 is about more than a single facility; it is about proving that Europe can lead the world in a truly circular, low‑carbon battery economy," said Linh Austin.
- Paweł Pudłowski is Deputy Chairman of the Board at the Polish Investment and Trade Agency.
- "The most innovative companies in the global clean‑technology and battery industries are choosing Poland as a location for their investments, and Ascend Elements is further proof of this trend," said Paweł Pudłowski.
- Project Apex 2 will refine separated key cathode raw materials recovered from spent lithium-ion batteries, known as "black mass," into lithium carbonate battery-grade materials and precursor cathode active material (pCAM).
- These products are essential for the production of cathode active material (CAM), a core component of lithium-ion batteries used in electric vehicles and stationary energy storage systems.
- The facility will commercialize Ascend Elements’ Hydro-to-Cathode® hydrometallurgical technology in Europe to produce sustainable lithium and pCAM materials from recycled battery materials.
- The investment will help create an integrated, low-emission supply chain for lithium-ion batteries inside the EU, reducing dependence on imported metals and cathode materials from high-risk markets.
- Project Apex 2 aligns with the EU Critical Raw Materials Act (CRMA) and circular-economy objectives to ensure strategic metals are recovered and processed within the European Union.
- Michał Jaros is Secretary of State at the Polish Ministry of Economic Development and Technology.
- "Project Apex 2 is exactly the kind of strategic, high‑value investment Poland needs to solidify its position at the heart of Europe’s clean‑energy economy," said Michał Jaros.
- Ascend Elements already operates commercial-scale hydrometallurgical recycling and lithium recovery facilities in the United States.
- In 2025, Ascend Elements produced approximately 2,500 tonnes per year of battery-grade lithium carbonate (Li₂CO₃).
- The company plans to increase global lithium carbonate capacity to more than 15,000 tonnes per year by 2027.
- Project Apex 2 will transfer proven technologies to Poland to close Europe’s growing gap in cathode precursor supply and reinforce the EU’s strategic autonomy in critical battery materials.
- Ascend Elements is a North America- and Europe-based battery materials leader that converts spent lithium-ion batteries and manufacturing scrap into premium precursor cathode material (pCAM) and battery-grade lithium carbonate through its patented Hydro-to-Cathode® process.
