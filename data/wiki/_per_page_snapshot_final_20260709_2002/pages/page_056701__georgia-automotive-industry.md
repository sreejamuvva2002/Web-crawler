# Georgia Automotive Industry

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-04

**Source:** [A Century of Innovation and Counting: Georgia’s Automotive Edge - Savannah CEO](https://savannahceo.com/news/2026/04/century-innovation-and-counting-georgias-automotive-edge/)  ·  `page_056701`

## Facts

- Automotive News published a special 100th anniversary edition chronicleing the evolution of the automotive industry over the last century and Georgia’s role in it.
- The first hybrid 2027 Kia Tellurides will roll off the production line in West Point, Georgia.
- Early roots (1909-1920s) saw White Star Automobile in Atlanta and Savannah Blowpipe (now Great Dane) launch Georgia’s automotive presence.
- Blue Bird Corporation was established in 1927 during Georgia's foundational automotive growth period.
- Genuine Parts Company was established in 1928 during Georgia's foundational automotive growth period.
- Ford and General Motors invested in Georgia by 1947.
- The former GM plant in Doraville was repurposed into Assembly Studios.
- The Ford Hapeville facility transformed into the Porsche Experience Center Atlanta.
- Kia Georgia, established in 2006, along with its suppliers, has created over 14,000 jobs.
- Pirelli, Novelis, Honda Precision Parts, Kumho Tire USA, Toyo Tire, Cox Automotive, Porsche, Toyota Industries Corporation, and Mercedes-Benz have expanded their Georgia operations in the 21st century.
- Recent investments in Georgia focus on hybrid and electric vehicles, DC-to-DC production, e-mobility innovation corridors including The Ray and Curiosity Lab, and the $12.6 billion Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- Misti Martin serves as Deputy Commissioner at the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD).
- GDEcD connects automakers with communities, utilities, education partners, and industry leaders to accelerate long-term growth of integrated supply chains in a stable, business-friendly environment.
- Georgia Quick Start and the Technical College System of Georgia provide customized workforce training for rapid, efficient scaling.
- Facilities like Kia Georgia and Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America produce a mix of traditional electric and hybrid vehicles backed by a robust supplier network.
- The Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) program ensures industrial sites are prepped and primed for major projects.
- The Rural Site Development Initiative supports site readiness for industrial development in Georgia.
- Hyundai’s $12.6 billion Metaplant America is a major project supported by Georgia's site readiness programs.
- Automotive investments in Georgia spark job creation, charge educational partnerships, drive infrastructure growth and cross-cultural exchange, and shift local economies.
