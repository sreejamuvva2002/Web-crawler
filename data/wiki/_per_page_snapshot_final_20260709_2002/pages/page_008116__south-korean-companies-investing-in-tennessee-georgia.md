# South Korean Companies Investing in Tennessee & Georgia

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-25

**Source:** [Tennessee-South Korea trade ties strained by auto plant deportations](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/tennessee-south-korea-trade-ties-100318640.html)  ·  `page_008116`

## Facts

- Michael Kofoed, an assistant professor of economics at The University of Tennessee Knoxville, stated that the relationship between South Korea and the U.S. will never be the same.
- South Korea has opened an investigation into whether human rights violations were committed in the Sept. 4 raid at LG Energy Solution's joint battery plant with Hyundai Motor in Georgia.
- The raid detained 475 workers, more than 300 of whom were South Korean nationals.
- Korean employees at LG Energy Solution's Spring Hill, Tenn., operation left the U.S., citing growing concerns over visa status and legal uncertainty.
- LG Energy Solution told the Tennessean that its plant is still operating as usual.
- An LG Energy Solutions spokesperson stated the company remains committed to its U.S. projects and will continue to navigate circumstances with the aim to continue necessary investments and business.
- The same spokesperson noted the U.S. is an important market and, in the mid- to long-term, plans to locate half of its total global battery cell-making capacity in North America.
- South Korean President Lee Jae Myung stated at a Sept. 11 news conference that businesses will hesitate to make direct investments in the United States as things stand now.
- Many Georgia detainees held Electronic System for Travel Authorization visa waivers or B-1 temporary business traveler visas.
- Those visa documents prohibit foreign nationals from working in construction or equipment installation in the U.S.
- The trade rift could impact Tennessee, where more than 20 South Korean companies have collectively poured nearly $6 billion into the state as of Oct. 2024.
- The $6 billion investment figure comes from the Tennessee Department of Economic and Community Development.
- Michael Kofoed stated that injecting fear and uncertainty around LG is equivalent to raising their taxes and has raised the cost of doing business in the United States.
- U.S. Homeland Security Secretary Kristi Noem acknowledged the trade policy impact of the deportations.
- Noem said the deportations signal policy consistency that could ultimately bolster investor confidence.
- Much of South Korean investment in Tennessee revolves around auto industry second-tier suppliers supporting electric vehicle production for local OEMs like Ford and General Motors.
- Wisarut Suwanprasert, Middle Tennessee State University Associate Professor of Economics, stated that Korean investment has created thousands of jobs.
- Suwanprasert believes the dwindling Korean morale toward U.S. investment is a minor diplomatic issue.
- Suwanprasert suggested the Trump administration should provide a formal apology to make things smoother.
- Kofoed did not anticipate a U.S. apology nor suggest South Korea would try to restore the bond between the partners.
- Kofoed stated that given the current trajectory, there is less willingness for foreign partners to do foreign direct investment in the U.S.
- Amid shifting global dynamics and U.S. trade and immigration policies under President Donald Trump, countries like South Korea and Japan have increasingly explored economic ties with China.
- Kofoed stated that because the U.S. is receding as a business partner, South Korea and Japan are being drawn into the Chinese sphere of influence.
- Noem argued that tough actions mean there is no uncertainty about the Trump administration's policies.
- Noem claimed investors prefer a stable, rule-bound environment where immigration laws are enforced evenly against allies' workers on grey zone visas to protect American jobs long-term.
- Kofoed expects long-term trust problems between the U.S. and its foreign partners.
- Kofoed described the situation as a conflict between trusting the U.S. for investment and feeling that trust was betrayed.
- Ultium Cells is a joint venture between Korean-owned LG Energy Solutions and General Motors that manufactures low-cost electric vehicle battery cells known as lithium iron phosphate (LFP).
- Ultium Cells announced a $2.3 billion investment in July, located onsite at the GM manufacturing plant in Spring Hill.
- LG Chem is a global leading chemical company specializing in petrochemicals, advanced materials and life sciences, and is the parent company of LG Energy Solutions following a December 2020 diversification.
- In Nov. 2022, LG Chem announced it would invest roughly $3.2 billion to build a cathode materials plant for electric vehicle batteries in Clarksville.
- Dongwha Electrolyte USA is a subsidiary of Dongwha Enterprise Company that specializes in electrolytes used in lithium batteries.
- In 2023, Dongwha Electrolyte USA announced a $70 million investment into creating a Clarksville production facility.
- Enchem America invested $152.2 million in June 2023 to create an electrolyte manufacturing site for electric batteries in Brownsville.
- Enchem America is a subsidiary of Enchem Company, and its Tennessee operations supported its U.S. headquarters located in Commerce, Georgia.
- Hanon Systems specializes in ventilation and air conditioning and announced a $170 million expansion in August 2023 to create a production facility in Huntington Park.
- Hanon Systems' Tennessee operations supply thermal solutions for automakers across North America.
- Unitech announced in January 2024 it would invest roughly $30 million to debut its first U.S. facility in Tennessee.
- Unitech specializes in thermal adhesive and battery compression pad production for EV manufacturers across the Southeast, and also manufactures industrial adhesives and sealers.
- The Aluko Group announced a $36.3 million facility in December 2024 to produce aluminum battery casings for Ford's BlueOval City.
- The Aluko Group consists of Aluko, Hyundai Aluminum, Gogang Aluminum, Alutec, KPTU, Hyundai Aluminum Vina and Alutec Vina.
