# Aurubis Richmond LLC

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-02

**Source:** [Metal Recycling Offers Answer to Rising Copper, Battery Needs](https://www.industrialinfo.com/news/article/metal-recycling-offers-answer-to-rising-copper-battery-needs--358519)  ·  `page_001278`

## Facts

- Industrial Info Resources is tracking nearly 500 active and proposed metal-recycling projects worldwide, totaling more than US$45 billion of investment.
- The U.S. accounts for nearly US$5 billion of the global metal-recycling investment.
- China accounts for about 80% of investment at metal-recycling facilities worldwide, with the U.S. accounting for nearly 11%.
- China accounts for more than 94% of US$14.6 billion worth of projects deemed to have a high likelihood (81% or more) of beginning construction within the next 12 months, while the U.S. makes up only 3.1%.
- Strong demand from data-center and automotive markets is keeping global copper prices at elevated levels.
- Aurubis Richmond LLC, a subsidiary of German firm Aurubis AG, expects to finish construction later this year on an expansion of its copper scrap recycling and processing plant in Augusta, Georgia.
- The Augusta plant expansion has been under construction since 2022.
- The additional capacity from the Augusta plant expansion is expected to double the plant's output to 70,000 tons per year.
- Demand for the Augusta plant's output is fueled by data centers and EVs.
- Aurubis stated in a September 2025 press release that American industry currently needs about 1.8 million tons of copper per year.
- Experts estimate copper demand will rise nearly 30% in the next five years, according to Aurubis.
- As of the September 2025 announcement, the U.S. imports about half of the copper it processes.
- Multi-metal recycling can close the domestic copper supply gap quickly, according to Aurubis.
- China's copper-recycling market is experiencing a regional shortage of copper scrap raw materials, fueling sustained rallies in copper prices.
- Growing adoption of EVs is driving a boom in U.S.-based lithium-ion battery recycling, which accounts for most investment in U.S.-based metal-recycling projects under construction.
- Cirba Solutions, a subsidiary of environmental-services and heavy-construction firm The Heritage Group, has two lithium-ion battery-recycling projects set to be completed this year: a plant in Columbia, South Carolina, and an expansion in Lancaster, Ohio.
- The new South Carolina plant is expected to produce up to 500,000 EV batteries per year.
- The Ohio expansion is expected to boost output by more than 250,000 EV batteries per year.
- Ara Partners is proposing an expansion of its lithium-ion battery-recycling plant in Bartlesville, Oklahoma.
- The first phase of the Ara Partners facility completed construction in December and can process 14,000 tons per year of battery materials.
- The Ara Partners expansion is designed to elevate processing capacity to 50,000 tons per year.
