# Volvo Cars

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-03-05

**Source:** [Volvo Cars may move some production to US over Trump tariffs](https://www.usatoday.com/story/money/business/2025/03/05/volvo-cars-production-trump-tariffs/81643919007/)  ·  `page_014140`

## Facts

- Volvo Cars may move production of some of its models to the U.S. depending on tariffs.
- Volvo aims to stick to a plan to export its European-made EX30 SUV model to the United States later this year.
- Volvo Cars last year moved production of its EX30 electric vehicles from China to Europe to avoid European Union tariffs.
- The EX30 SUV should start production in Europe this quarter, and Volvo aims to increase production to serve both the European and U.S. markets in the second half of 2025.
- Exports of cars from Europe to the U.S. are only subject to a 2.5% tariff for now, but that is likely to change.
- CEO Jim Rowan stated that if tariffs go up to 10% each way, Volvo can cope, but if they go to 25%, it will be significantly more difficult from a profit perspective.
- Volvo Cars has enough capacity at its U.S. assembly plant to move more of its production to the United States.
- The company has space, paint shops, and buildings at the U.S. plant but needs to make a final decision on which models and platforms to move to the USA.
- Volvo produces its SUV EX90 in its Charleston, South Carolina plant, while also importing both hybrid and electric models from Europe at the moment.
- CEO Jim Rowan said Volvo could move its XC60 or XC90 models to the plant in Charleston, or look at putting models using different technology there.
- The final decision on relocating models will depend on waiting for tariff quotes.
- Volvo Cars is majority owned by China's Geely and seeks to replace most combustion engine cars in its line-up with fully electric vehicles by 2030.
