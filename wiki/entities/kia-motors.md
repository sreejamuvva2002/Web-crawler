# Kia Motors

**Type:** Company  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[vehicle-manufacturing|Vehicle Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** West Point, Georgia, Georgia, United States

## Overview

Kia announced the production of its first electric vehicle manufactured in Georgia, the EV9 SUV, at its expanded West Point plant after a $200 million investment that added 200 jobs and supports the state's emerging EV supply chain.

## EV supply-chain relevance

First electric vehicle produced in Georgia, expanding domestic EV manufacturing and contributing to the regional battery ecosystem.

## Key facts

- First electric vehicle manufactured in Georgia - the Kia EV9 SUV.
- $200 million investment by Kia to expand the West Point plant for EV production.
- Governor Brian Kemp drove the EV9 off the production line and said Georgia is working to become the e-mobility capital of the nation.
- U.S. Senator Jon Ossoff praised the milestone, calling Georgians proud of the cars produced in Georgia.
- The vehicle may qualify for up to a $7,500 federal tax credit under the Inflation Reduction Act.
- Battery warranty is 10 years/100,000 miles; federal regulation mandates a minimum eight-year warranty.
- Charging time is approximately eight hours on a 220 V Level 2 circuit.
- MSRP range for the EV9 is $55,000-$70,000, before incentives and trim adjustments.
- The EV9 has earned awards including World Car of the Year, World EV of the Year, and North American Utility Vehicle of the Year.
- Plant expansion added 200 jobs; 86 employees from Columbus work at Kia Georgia in West Point.
- Transportation accounted for 37.4 % of U.S. CO₂ emissions in 2022 according to EPA data.
- Kia will invest more than $200 million and create nearly 200 jobs at its West Point plant for EV9 production.
- The EV9 will be the fifth model assembled at the West Point plant since it opened in 2009.
- More than 40% of Kia vehicles sold in the United States are manufactured at the West Point facility.
- Kia has invested more than $1.9 billion in Georgia to date and supports over 14,000 plant and supplier jobs in the region.
- Governor Brian Kemp highlighted the project as part of Georgia’s goal to become the e-mobility capital.
- Sean Yoon described the EV9 as Kia’s most innovative vehicle and a potential change catalyst.
- The EV9 plant is expected to begin operations in the second quarter of next year.
