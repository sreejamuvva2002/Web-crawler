# RealTruck Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2025-05-14

**Source:** [RealTruck Inc. Outlook Revised To Negative On Tig | S&P Global Ratings](https://www.spglobal.com/ratings/en/regulatory/article/-/view/type/HTML/id/3369302)  ·  `page_049051`

## Facts

- RealTruck Inc.’s financial performance has been strained by weaker organic sales volumes, higher restructuring charges, and elevated capital expenditure (capex).
- The company experienced greater-than-expected negative free operating cash flow (FOCF) in 2024 and tightening liquidity.
- S&P Global Ratings revised RealTruck’s outlook to negative from stable and affirmed the 'B-' issuer credit rating.
- The negative outlook reflects the possibility of a downgrade over the next 12 months if EBITDA margins do not recover, combined with continued investment in working capital and elevated capex causing sustained negative FOCF.
- RealTruck’s top-line growth and margins are expected to remain weaker this year, leading to further cash flow deficits, reduced liquidity, and higher leverage.
- EBITDA margins have been weaker over the past several quarters due to lower organic sales volumes and increased restructuring related to the integration of recent acquisitions and expansion of manufacturing in its Mexico plants.
- Relocation of some manufacturing to Mexico from the U.S. resulted in better gross margins during 2024, but marketing costs remain elevated.
- Ongoing restructuring activity is expected to continue weighing on RealTruck's EBITDA margins.
- The company is confronting tariffs and potentially lower consumer demand.
- Direct tariff impacts are considered manageable assuming United States-Mexico-Canada Agreement (USMCA) parts remain exempt, given the company’s large North American manufacturing footprint.
- Tariffs are expected to strain U.S. consumer spending, particularly for some of RealTruck's most discretionary products.
- Capex has remained elevated as RealTruck ramped production at its facilities in Mexico.
- This contributed to four consecutive quarters of cash burn in 2024.
- S&P Global Ratings notes a high degree of unpredictability around U.S. administration policy implementation regarding tariffs and potential effects on economies, supply chains, and credit conditions.
- For 2025, organic revenues are assumed to decline 2%-5% due to weaker consumer demand.
- EBITDA margins are expected to improve to 16.3% from 15.3% in 2024 due to the margin accretive acquisition of Vehicle Accessories Group (VAI) and somewhat lower restructuring.
- These projected margins remain lower than historical margins and previous forecasts given assumed weaker sales and continued elevated restructuring costs.
- Further investment in working capital and high capital spending are expected as the company invests in its plants and likely stockpiles inventory ahead of tariffs.
- Debt to EBITDA is forecast to exceed 10x, with negative cash flow in 2025 of over $90 million.
- RealTruck has sufficient liquidity sources to service projected cash flow deficits over the next 12 months, though its cushion will contract.
- The company had balance sheet cash of $26.2 million as of year-end 2024 and $247.8 million of net asset-based loan (ABL) availability, for total liquidity of $274 million.
- RealTruck has no near-term debt maturities until its first-lien term loans expire in January 2028, followed by senior unsecured notes in January 2029.
- Demand for highly discretionary auto parts is expected to decline in a more protracted recession.
- A longer U.S. recession would reduce discretionary consumer spending and demand for RealTruck's products more than base-case assumptions.
- Ratings could be lowered if margins weaken further, working capital intensifies, capex remains elevated, weaker discretionary consumer spending leads to lower sales, or the company cannot pass through inflationary or tariff pressures to end customers.
- Outlook could be revised to stable if FOCF stabilizes to at least break-even on a sustained basis, contingent on sales volumes and EBITDA margins stabilizing.
