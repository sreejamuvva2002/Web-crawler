# Murata Electronics North America, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Murata Unveils Latest Additions To Its Temperature Sensing Thermistor Line](https://www.metrologyworld.com/doc/murata-unveils-latest-additions-to-its-0001)  ·  `page_047130`

## Facts

- Murata Electronics North America is located in Smyrna, GA.
- The company announced the addition of the PRF and NCP series to its temperature sensing thermistor line.
- Both series were developed to meet the design flexibility, reliability, standardization and cost savings needs expected by engineers in today's automotive market.
- The PRF PTC (positive temperature coefficient) thermistors are ideal for high accuracy overheat sensing.
- The NCP NTC (negative temperature coefficient) thermistors were created for temperature measurement with high accuracy over wide temperature ranges.
- The PRF series offers a quick and easy design for repeatable overheat sensing and provides resettable protection in the circuit because there is no hysteresis.
- The NCP series offers the same resistance and B constant in several different package sizes that allows for convenient redesign and cost reduction.
- Both series are surface mount with operating temperatures in the range of -40°C to +125°C for commercial grade and up to +150°C for automotive grade.
- Both series are suitable for lead free soldering.
- Both series meet ELV/UL and AEC-Q200 requirements.
- Both series utilize a very sturdy construction that make them resistant to mechanical vibrations and shock.
- Mitch Nozaki, Product Manager at Murata Electronics North America, stated the company is continuously developing fine ceramic materials and firing processes to produce the best products that meet designers' needs of today and tomorrow.
- Nozaki noted the company has provided the automotive design community with products needed to regulate detrimental temperature variances and secured the most market share in doing so.
