# Georgia Quick Start

**Type:** workforce_training  ·  **Role:** workforce_training  ·  **Published:** 2023-08-20

**Source:** [Quick Start: Meeting the Demands of Georgia’s Clean Energy Boom | Georgia](https://georgia.org/blog/quick-start-meeting-demands-georgias-clean-energy-boom)  ·  `page_005583`

## Facts

- Global corporations throughout the clean-energy sector are spending billions to build facilities to manufacture a wide array of solar, e-mobility, and innovative battery products.
- The State of Georgia ranks as a top-choice destination for these projects, which are literally creating tens of thousands of jobs in the state.
- Georgia Quick Start is a 56-year-old program providing company-specific workforce training.
- The program is routinely named the top state workforce development program in the country.
- Quick Start has helped prepare more than 1.8 million Georgians for new jobs since its creation in 1967.
- Companies benefiting from Quick Start include Rivian, Hyundai Motor Group, and SK Battery America.
- Quick Start customizes training and replicates the actual processes that will be used to create a workforce ready to start production on Day One.
- The program provides free-of-charge job training for qualified companies.
- While utilized by a diverse group of manufacturing sectors, the majority of Quick Start's recent clients are clean energy companies and their suppliers.
- In June 2023, the U.S. Secretary of Education spoke at Agnes Scott College and praised Quick Start as one of Georgia’s unique workforce-training assets.
- Robert Howey was hired as a 'tabber operator' at the Hanwha Qcells solar manufacturing plant in Dalton to watch over machines that solder silicon wafers together, an early stage in making a solar module.
- Before receiving Quick Start training, Howey felt nervous and uncertain about how his task fit into the overall process.
- Quick Start provided guidance during a week of on-ramping, walking him through all steps of making a solar panel to give him confidence for his new career.
- Howey described Quick Start as "a lifesaver."
- Quick Start currently operates five training centers.
- The program is expanding its advanced manufacturing center.
- Quick Start is designing three new facilities for clean energy manufacturers and suppliers.
- One new facility will be located in Bryan County, where Hyundai Motor Group is investing $7.59 billion in an electric vehicle (EV) and battery manufacturing "metaplant" with aggressive production timelines.
- The state-of-the-art Quick Start facility in Bryan County will train 300 to 400 new Hyundai hires a week.
