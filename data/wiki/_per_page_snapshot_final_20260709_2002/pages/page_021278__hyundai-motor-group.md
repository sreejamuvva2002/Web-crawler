# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** power_electronics  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-06

**Source:** [Hyundai invests $926M in 2nd EV plant in GA](https://www.teslarati.com/hyundai-2nd-ev-plant-ga/)  ·  `page_021278`

## Facts

- Hyundai Mobis will build a second electric vehicle plant in Georgia.
- Governor Brian Kemp announced the investment on Wednesday.
- Kemp’s office said that Hyundai is investing $926 million in an Electric Vehicle Power Electric system facility in Bryan County.
- The new facility will eventually employ 1,500 people.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that celebrating the groundbreaking of Hyundai’s new electric vehicle and battery manufacturing facility in Bryan County would unleash transformational job creation and investment in that entire region of the state.
- Governor Kemp noted they announced a second supplier in just two weeks to locate in that area, highlighting the growing list of other job creators that will soon follow.
- Construction on the new facility is expected to begin in January 2023.
- Production at the new facility will start sometime in 2024.
- In October, Hyundai Motor Group broke ground on its $5.5 billion EV manufacturing factory in Bryan County.
- The $5.5 billion factory is the first in the U.S. for Hyundai.
- Euisun Chung, executive chairman of Hyundai Motor Group, said that the manufacturing plant would be the envy of the industry.
- The first factory is expected to open in 2025.
- The first factory will employ 8,100 workers.
- The first factory will produce up to 300,000 EVs per year.
- Governor Kemp stated that record jobs and investment coming to all parts of the state, along with award-winning workforce development programs and infrastructure, are pushing the Peach State’s economy to new heights.
- Governor Kemp said their partnership with Hyundai Motor Group and the groundbreaking of this innovative facility exemplifies unprecedented success in improving lives and livelihoods.
- Governor Kemp expressed a long-term commitment to continuing the partnership and seeing its impact on both the community and the state as a whole.
