# State of Indiana

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2025-07-01

**Source:** [Alternative Fuels Data Center: Electricity Laws and Incentives in Indiana](https://afdc.energy.gov/fuels/laws/ELEC?state=in)  ·  `page_004741`

## Facts

- The Indiana Economic Development Corporation (IEDC) may designate an area as a certified technology park if criteria are met, including commitment from at least one business engaged in high technology activity creating significant jobs.
- High technology activities for certified technology parks include advanced vehicles technology, which covers electric vehicles, hybrid electric vehicles, alternative fuel vehicles, or their components.
- Each Indiana state entity must purchase or lease a clean energy vehicle unless the Department of Administration determines it is inappropriate or substantially more costly than a comparable non-clean vehicle.
- A clean energy vehicle is defined as operating on one or more alternative energy sources, including rechargeable energy storage systems, electricity, ethanol, biodiesel, hydrogen, natural gas, and propane.
- Before July 1, 2025, the Indiana Department of Administration must make recommendations to state entities regarding clean energy vehicle procurement.
- Each Indiana state entity must annually submit information to the Department of Administration regarding its use of clean energy vehicles.
- The EV Commission is established in Indiana to assess the EV market and labor force.
- The EV Commission must evaluate EV production facilities and capabilities within the state.
- The EV Commission must take inventory of skilled and non-skilled workers, training needs, and opportunities in the Indiana EV industry.
- The EV Commission must identify and leverage manufacturing competencies within the automotive industry to increase EV production.
- The EV Commission must identify opportunities for EV-related research and development industry in Indiana.
- EV owners in Indiana are required to pay an additional registration fee of $230.
- Plug-in hybrid and hybrid electric vehicle owners in Indiana are required to pay an additional registration fee of $77.
- Indiana EV registration fees will be updated annually based on the special fuel tax index factor.
- Indiana joined Michigan, Illinois, and Wisconsin in signing a memorandum of understanding (MOU) to create an EV charging corridor along the Lake Michigan coastline for seamless cross-state driving.
- Signatory states of the Lake Michigan EV Circuit are committed to coordinating regional EV charger siting and deployment strategies.
- Signatory states aim to achieve a consistent EV charger user experience across state lines.
- The Lake Michigan EV Circuit MOU aims to encourage EV tourism around Lake Michigan.
- The initiative focuses on removing barriers to EV adoption and reducing range anxiety.
- Signatory states will identify and develop best practices for EV charging, infrastructure optimization, information sharing, and electricity demand management.
- The corridor aims to provide reliable long-distance driving routes for EV drivers.
- A working group composed of senior leadership from each signatory state will meet and report on Lake Michigan EV Circuit actions.
- In Indiana, a person or joint agency that owns, operates, or leases EV chargers for public use is not defined as a public utility.
- Indiana joined Illinois, Michigan, Minnesota, and Wisconsin in signing the REV Midwest MOU to accelerate vehicle electrification in the Midwest.
- Signatory states of the REV Midwest plan are committed to accelerating medium- and heavy-duty fleet electrification.
- The REV Midwest initiative involves collaborating on regional EV charger siting and deployment analyses with a focus on commercial routes.
- States will standardize regulations, messaging, and customer experience related to EVs across state lines.
- The REV Midwest plan includes evaluating opportunities for workforce development.
- Signatory states will identify historically underserved communities for equitable EV charger development and EV adoption.
- The initiative aims to educate consumers and fleet owners to raise EV awareness, reduce range anxiety, and increase EV adoption.
- A coordination group composed of senior leadership from each REV Midwest signatory state will meet and report on actions.
- Electric utilities in Indiana may request approval from the Indiana Utility Regulatory Commission to implement a pilot program evaluating large-scale EV charger deployment for public-use EVs.
- Public-use EVs eligible under the utility pilot program include electric school buses, electric transit buses, and EVs used for public goods and services delivery.
- Eligible utility pilot proposals must install, own, operate, or make-ready EV chargers for public-use EVs and provide incentives or rebates for EV purchases and charger installations.
- Utility pilot program proposals must include plans to install EV chargers in underserved and diverse communities.
- The Indiana Utility Regulatory Commission has authority to promote transportation sector electrification by designating specific EV planning issues utilities must include in their next integrated resource plan.
- The Indiana Department of Environmental Management (IDEM) administers the DieselWise Indiana grant programs to reduce diesel emissions.
- DieselWise grants range from $50,000 to $250,000 for diesel vehicle, equipment, and engine retrofits or replacements, as well as idle reduction technologies.
- Private and public entities are eligible applicants for IDEM's DieselWise grant programs.
- The U.S. Department of Transportation’s NEVI Formula Program required the Indiana Department of Transportation (INDOT) to submit an annually updated EV Infrastructure Deployment Plan for fiscal years 2022 through 2026.
- INDOT's NEVI plans must describe how the state intends to distribute funds, outline physical and cybersecurity strategies, and include a Community Engagement Outcomes Report.
- The Indiana 21st Century Research and Technology Fund provides grants and loans to support economic development in high technology industry clusters.
- Eligible projects for the 21st Century Research and Technology Fund may include qualified alternative fuel technologies and fuel-efficient vehicle development and production.
