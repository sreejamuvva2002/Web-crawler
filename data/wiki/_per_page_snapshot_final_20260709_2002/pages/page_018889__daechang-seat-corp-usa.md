# Daechang Seat Corp. USA

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-10

**Source:** [Automotive seat frames supplier to invest $72.5M in GA facility](https://valdostatoday.com/news-2/region/2023/10/automotive-seat-frames-supplier-to-invest-72-5m-in-ga-facility/)  ·  `page_018889`

## Facts

- Daechang Seat Corp. USA will invest $72.5 million in a Georgia center creating more than 500 jobs.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Daechang Seat Corp. USA, a supplier specializing in automotive seat frames, will invest $72.5 million in the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center, in close proximity to Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA), creating over 500 jobs.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that the news of another HMGMA supplier coming to Georgia is a reminder of the impact projects like the Hyundai Metaplant have beyond their initial investment.
- Governor Brian Kemp noted that suppliers for the Hyundai Metaplant project alone reach at least nine counties, and they are excited to add Daechang Seat Corp. USA to their growing manufacturing base centered around Chatham County and the Savannah region.
- Daechang Seat Corp. (DSC) was established in 1979 in Kyungju, South Korea, and has become one of the largest seat frame producers in the world.
- Globally, DSC employs more than 5,000 people in seven countries.
- The company’s production process includes fully automatic lines with arc welding and spot-welding machines.
- Jinsuk Lee, CEO of Daechang Seat Savannah Corp., expressed appreciation to the State of Georgia, its local communities, and the City of Savannah for creating improved opportunities and employment prospects within Chatham County and the Savannah area.
- Jinsuk Lee stated DSC takes great pride in maintaining its steadfast partnership with the City of Savannah.
- Jinsuk Lee said their primary objective is to drive the growth of DSC through facility expansions, ultimately transforming DSC into a global entity.
- Jinsuk Lee noted that DSC currently holds a prominent partnership with Hyundai Transys and Hyundai Mobis at HMGMA.
- Jinsuk Lee expressed hope to collaborate with even more projects in the future.
- DSC’s new facility will be located at the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center.
- The company will be hiring for assembly operators, equipment technicians, mechanical engineers, and electrical engineers through local job fairs and website postings in the future.
- Griff Lynch, Georgia Ports Authority President and CEO, said DSC is only minutes from the Port of Savannah and is ideally located to take advantage of Georgia’s world-class connectivity.
- Griff Lynch stated GPA is all-in on providing the best global gateway for U.S. supply chains and is excited to welcome DSC as the latest job creator in Georgia’s automaking sector.
- Trip Tollison, Savannah Economic Development Authority President and CEO, announced that the third Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America supplier will be locating in the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center and bringing 500 new jobs and $72.5 million in capital investment in Chatham County.
- Trip Tollison described Daechang Seat Corp. USA as a well-established global company and expressed looking forward to working with them as they grow and become part of the community.
- Alyce Thornhill, Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy, represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project in partnership with the Savannah Economic Development Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson said countless partners are helping transform Georgia into a center of the automotive and mobility industry and welcomed DSC in Savannah.
- Pat Wilson noted Savannah is an established global gateway and Chatham County has leveraged that advantage to create a bustling community where people come to live, work, and enjoy the Hostess City of the South.
- As the market transitions to electric vehicles, Georgia has pursued the entire supply chain, creating more than $25 billion in investments and 30,000 jobs since 2018.
- In fiscal year 2023, job creation in the automotive industry increased by 324 percent when compared to FY21.
- Suppliers attracted by Georgia’s mobility original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) resulted in over $2 billion in investment across the state in FY23.
- DSC aims to be a top-tier company that respects humanity, leveraging its accumulated technology and customer trust.
- DSC has recently ventured into the electric vehicle sector and continues to expand its scope.
- With over 5,000 employees in seven countries worldwide and automated production lines in its processes, DSC anticipates greater opportunities for growth with the completion of new manufacturing facilities, providing larger installation spaces.
