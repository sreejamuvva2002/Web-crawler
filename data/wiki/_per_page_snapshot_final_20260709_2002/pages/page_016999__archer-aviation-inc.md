# Archer Aviation Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-11-07

**Source:** [Archer Aviation (ACHR) Raises $650M, Moves to Buy Hawthorne Airport for $126M; Shares Slip After Q3 Update — Nov. 7, 2025](https://ts2.tech/en/archer-aviation-achr-raises-650m-moves-to-buy-hawthorne-airport-for-126m-shares-slip-after-q3-update-nov-7-2025/)  ·  `page_016999`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation Inc. (NYSE: ACHR) unveiled a multi-pronged update tied to its third-quarter results, including a $650 million equity raise and a deal to acquire control of Los Angeles’ Hawthorne Airport for $126 million.
- The company signed definitive agreements to gain control of the 80-acre Hawthorne Airport (Jack Northrop Field), located just under three miles from LAX, for $126 million.
- Archer plans to use Hawthorne Airport to anchor its Los Angeles air-taxi network and serve as an AI operations testbed for AI-powered traffic and ground-ops management.
- The airport acquisition transaction remains subject to closing conditions and city approval.
- Archer will assume the master ground lease for the facility rather than buying the land outright, with the lease running through 2055.
- The company has listed additional capital projects at the site as part of an “airport of the future” build-out.
- Archer filed a 424B5 prospectus supplement for 81,250,000 new shares priced at $8.00 per share, generating gross proceeds of $650 million.
- Following the issuance, total Class A shares outstanding will be approximately 732.5 million.
- A separate prospectus covers up to approximately $27.6 million of additional shares to settle a contractual payment in stock.
- Archer reported a Q3 2025 GAAP net loss of $129.9 million and an adjusted EBITDA loss of $116.1 million.
- The company held $1.64 billion in cash, cash equivalents, and short-term investments at the end of the quarter.
- Archer guided for a Q4 adjusted EBITDA loss between $110 million and $140 million.
- Its Midnight eVTOL completed a 55-mile piloted flight lasting 31 minutes at speeds exceeding 126 mph.
- The Midnight aircraft also successfully completed a 10,000-foot altitude flight.
- Archer closed its acquisition of the Lilium patent portfolio, bringing its global IP assets to over 1,000.
- Hawthorne Airport's proximity to LAX, SoFi Stadium, the Intuit Dome, The Forum, and Downtown LA positions it as a strategic hub for short-hop urban missions ahead of the LA28 Olympic Games.
- Industry coverage indicates the airport will serve as the central node of Archer’s LA network and a staging area for 2028 operations, pending approvals.
- Archer cites lease renewal risk and capital expenditure obligations to transform the site as part of its master lease assumption.
- The company flagged execution and certification risks in its prospectus filings.
- Archer states it is largely through Phase 3 of the FAA type-certification program and is now focusing on Phase 4 implementation and formal TIA testing.
- The company estimates receiving approximately 15% of compliance verification documents for the final certification phase.
- Archer holds a Part 135 air carrier certificate.
- The company is currently producing six Midnight aircraft across U.S. facilities for testing, certification, and non-U.S. launch-edition programs.
- Archer highlighted test and demonstration flights in the UAE alongside new airline partnerships with Korean Air and a JAL/Soracle JV in Japan, though some agreements remain conditional.
- JPMorgan cut its price target for ACHR to $8, assigning a Neutral rating following the Q3 results.
- Needham reiterated a Buy rating but lowered its price target to $10.
- ARK Invest disclosed purchasing over 3 million ACHR shares on November 6 across multiple funds.
- Post-raise liquidity stands at greater than $2 billion, according to company statements.
- The Lilium patent acquisition added approximately 300 assets to Archer's intellectual property portfolio.
- Archer continues developing a Los Angeles network blueprint with ties to the LA28 Olympics and maintains partnerships with airlines like United Airlines to integrate urban air mobility into existing networks.
