# Blue Ridge Connector

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2026-01-22

**Source:** [Blue Ridge Connector Nears Completion, Expands Georgia Ports Rail Network in 2026 — Savannah Traffic Club](https://www.savannahtrafficclub.org/news/blue-ridge-connector-95-percent-complete)  ·  `page_017928`

## Facts

- Construction on the Blue Ridge Connector is now 95 percent complete.
- Georgia Ports’ latest addition to its inland rail network will open for operation later this year (2026).
- The development cost is $134 million.
- Connection of electrical power service is expected by the end of January.
- GPA will fully commission the terminal’s seven all-electric rubber tire gantry cranes in February.
- The RTGs are completely assembled and have been tested using generators.
- Georgia Ports President and CEO Griff Lynch expressed excitement about the progress at the new Gainesville facility and its impact on the State of Georgia.
- A rail connection in the region will enhance Northeast Georgia as an attractive location for businesses that rely on global logistics.
- Direct rail service via Norfolk Southern will connect Northeast Georgia and Savannah.
- The rail service provides a new option to replace a long-haul truck move of around 600 miles roundtrip.
- The project aims to reduce highway congestion, cut emissions, and avoid costly empty container moves to or from the coast.
- The rail terminal links directly to the Port of Savannah’s extensive global shipping network of 39 ships per week.
- Companies in Northeast Georgia, including poultry producers and manufacturers of heavy equipment and forest products, will be able to move goods to and from international markets with greater efficiency.
- Port officials expect the inland terminal to act as a magnet for job creation by bringing global market access to local businesses.
- Lynch stated that Georgia Ports' role is to support economic development and prosperity across the state.
- The Appalachian Regional Port has played a key role in attracting firms to Northwest Georgia based on logistics infrastructure, and the Blue Ridge Connector will do the same for Northeast Georgia.
- GPA contributed $4.8 million to Hall County projects to reduce the new railyard’s traffic impact to local residents.
- Hall County projects included eliminating an at-grade crossing, rerouting White Sulphur Road, and surfacing Cagle Road.
- The new White Sulphur route south of the inland terminal ensures free access for emergency vehicles and avoids traffic disruption from trains.
- Cagle Road resurfacing offers an improved alternative for residents.
- Both road improvement projects were completed in late summer 2025.
