# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-02

**Source:** [State Leaders Celebrate Hyundai Motor Group’s Historic Impact on Georgia - Savannah CEO](https://savannahceo.com/features/2024/02/state-leaders-celebrate-hyundai-motor-groups-historic-impact-georgia/)  ·  `page_060660`

## Facts

- Governor Brian Kemp and First Lady Marty Kemp joined President and Global COO of Hyundai Motor Company and President and CEO of Hyundai and Genesis Motor North America José Muñoz to celebrate Hyundai Motor Group’s historic impact on Georgia.
- An update was provided on the progress of Hyundai Motor Group's Metaplant America facility.
- The Metaplant America facility will officially start vehicle production in the fourth quarter of this year (2024).
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Hyundai has been an incredible partner to Georgia for decades and is helping recruit businesses, give back to communities, and work with colleges and universities to connect Georgians with opportunities.
- Hyundai and Genesis electric vehicles on display included the IONIQ 5, IONIQ 6, GV60, and Electrified GV70.
- The hydrogen-powered XCIENT Fuel Cell Truck will be used for logistics needs between Hyundai’s manufacturing facilities and suppliers.
- Governor Kemp officially declared February 26 as Hyundai Day throughout the state of Georgia.
- The Georgia General Assembly passed a Resolution commending Hyundai Motor Group’s dedication to the state and recognizing February 26 as Hyundai Day at the State Capitol.
- José Muñoz stated that Hyundai’s investments in Georgia will create tens of thousands of great-paying American jobs.
- Hyundai’s investments in Georgia are expected to bring tens of billions of dollars in economic impact for decades to come.
- The Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) project was originally announced in May 2022.
- LG Energy Solution was named as the joint venture partner in the manufacture of battery components for the project.
- An additional expansion of the LG Energy Solution battery facility was announced.
- Hyundai and SK On are building an EV battery manufacturing facility in Bartow County to supply Hyundai Motor Group’s U.S. plants.
- Hyundai Motor Group’s planned investment in Georgia has reached more than $12.6 billion.
- The project is bringing the creation of 12,000 direct, long-term jobs.
- Lieutenant Governor Burt Jones, House Speaker Jon Burns, legislative leaders, GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson, and officials from the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority attended the celebration.
- Lt. Governor Burt Jones applauded Governor Kemp, state agencies, and the University System of Georgia, especially Georgia Tech, for their partnership with Hyundai.
- These initiatives support Georgia's position as the number one state in which to do business.
- Up to 300,000 Hyundai, Genesis, and Kia EVs are expected to be produced annually at the Metaplant.
- Seventeen HMGMA suppliers have announced projects across 10 Georgia counties spanning beyond southeast Georgia.
- Further supplier announcements are expected.
- Supplier facilities are expected to create more than 6,700 new jobs and represent more than $2.5 billion in investment.
- Supplier facilities will be operational prior to the opening of HMGMA.
- Speaker of the House Jon Burns stated that Hyundai's Metaplant will have a generational impact as a force multiplier for investments in education, healthcare, housing, and transportation.
- The nearly 3,000-acre Metaplant site already has four building exteriors complete.
- Outfitting for internal operations is underway at the Metaplant site.
- The Metaplant’s architecture incorporates technologically advanced elements, design features representing the Savannah area, Hyundai’s brand identity, nature, and clean energy.
- HMGMA's full-time employees are referred to as "Meta Pros" and are being hired rapidly for training on machinery, office/clerical work, and other roles.
- HMGMA currently has 566 full-time employees.
- Approximately 75 percent of the current workforce is from the Greater Savannah area.
- An additional 37 employees are from Georgia but outside the local area.
- Georgia Quick Start broke ground on its customized training center at the Metaplant site on February 8, 2024.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Georgians will be trained in high-skill careers as Meta Pros at one of the most advanced vehicle assembly and battery plants in the world.
- HMGMA partnered with local technical colleges to prepare students for entry-level employment at the Metaplant.
- Electrical Vehicle Professional Technical Certificate of Credit (EVP TCC) training is available at Coastal Pines Technical College’s Baxley and Waycross campuses, Southeastern Technical College’s Vidalia campus, Savannah Technical College, and Ogeechee Technical College.
- In September 2023, Georgia Institute of Technology (Georgia Tech) and Hyundai Motor Company signed a memorandum of understanding to collaborate on research for sustainable mobility, including developing a hydrogen economy, workforce development, and smart city initiatives.
- U.S. News & World Report ranked Georgia Tech as the top public university and No. 3 nationally in energy and fuel research.
- Georgia Tech President Ángel Cabrera stated that the partnership will advance science for key sectors and produce the next-generation workforce needed for these industries.
- Since 1998, Hyundai has directed over $6 million towards local Georgia communities through philanthropic programs and partnerships.
- Hyundai is promoting STEM programs in local K-12 schools and hosting educational programs on alternative energy sources.
- Hyundai supports local organizations dedicated to affordable homeownership and focuses on environmental stewardship in the region.
- Trip Tollison of the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority stated that Hyundai has given more than $1 million to regional nonprofits.
- Hyundai has committed to $7.59 billion in capital investment in Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- The Metaplant project will create 8,500 jobs according to the Joint Development Authority.
- Beginning with mass production, the electricity used to power the Metaplant will come from 100 percent clean power sources.
- This aligns with Hyundai’s commitment to carbon neutrality by 2045.
- A 41-acre park will be featured in front of the plant, including trails, running tracks, environmental art displays, sports fields, and amenities for employee use.
