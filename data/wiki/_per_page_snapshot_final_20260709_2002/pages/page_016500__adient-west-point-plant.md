# Adient West Point Plant

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [2018 IW Best Plants Winner: Adient Takes a Back Seat to No One for High Performance | IndustryWeek](https://www.industryweek.com/talent/article/22027332/2018-iw-best-plants-winner-adient-takes-a-back-seat-to-no-one-for-high-performance)  ·  `page_016500`

## Facts

- Adient West Point is located in West Point, Ga., roughly halfway between Atlanta and Montgomery.
- The plant operates just down the road a couple miles from the Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia (KMMG) plant.
- Adient serves as a Tier 1 supplier to Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia.
- Adient's decision to operate in West Point mirrors the keiretsu tradition initiated by Japanese automakers and is now a standard best practice for automakers worldwide.
- Parent company Adient plc is the world’s largest supplier of automotive seats, responsible for providing roughly one out of every three automotive seats.
- Adient plc is a $17.4 billion company that spun off from Johnson Controls a couple years ago.
- The parent company has well over 200 manufacturing plants worldwide.
- Each plant follows the Adient Manufacturing System (AMS) approach to manufacturing, built on four foundations: customer focus, stable operations environment, zero tolerance for waste, and organizing around customer pull.
- Adient West Point is the first U.S. just-in-time (JIT) plant to achieve AMS Level 3.
- Achieving AMS Level 3 requires implementing and demonstrating evidence for 556 manufacturing objectives.
- The plant’s continuous improvement initiatives include the use of Six Sigma, lean manufacturing and Kepner & Tregoe problem-solving.
- The hourly workforce has been certified in “5 why” analysis to discover the root cause of any problem areas.
- Adient West Point adopted High-Performance Teams (HPTs) and became the benchmark plant for HPT within all seating plants in the United States.
- The West Point plant served as the pilot plant for HPTs for the company.
- It took a year to prepare the workforce for the HPT effort, which included lessons in teambuilding, building trust and teaching the hourly team the plant metrics.
- The HPT effort launched in 2017 with the full engagement of the hourly workers.
- Under the HPT initiative, the plant achieved significant improvements in safety, quality, delivery (with no customer disruptions in the past two years) and cost.
- To date, the company has achieved 526 days and counting with no OSHA recordable incidents, marking a new milestone for the plant.
- KMMG recognized Adient West Point as its best supplier in safety.
- KMMG asked Adient’s safety manager, Robert Brock, to train 29 other local suppliers in best practices in environmental health and safety (EHS).
- Plant manager Tracy Breeding set a basic goal for the facility: “To be the best damn JIT plant in the Americas.”
- Engineering manager Jason Martin has been at Adient and Johnson Controls for 22 years, noting that HPT was something new and Tracy’s enthusiasm for it was infectious.
- Operations manager Steve Perdue stated that floor workers are keeping leaders accountable by asking for the information they need to do their jobs better.
- Prior to HPTs, various departments at the plant functioned in silos, but now they work together as a team with improved communication.
- HR manager JR Smith noted that the word on the street is that Adient is a good place to work, which makes it easier to recruit new hires and circumvent talent finding difficulties.
- The plant's motto is “Safety first. Quality always. Excellence everyday.”
