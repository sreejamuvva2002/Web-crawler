# Constellium

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- Constellium operates a corporate office in downtown Baltimore, Maryland, located only a few minutes from the Inner Harbor.
- The Baltimore corporate office houses multiple teams including Finance, HR, IT, Legal and Communications.
- The Bowling Green, Kentucky plant produces flat rolled aluminium Auto Body Sheet products for the growing automotive market in North America.
- Coils supplied by the Bowling Green plant are used for a variety of automotive applications.
- Constellium Muscle Shoals is a major aluminium sheet supplier for the packaging and automotive markets.
- The Muscle Shoals plant has approximately 1,200 employees and more than 100 acres under roof.
- Plymouth, Michigan is home to Constellium's Research & Technology hub in North America.
- The Plymouth location serves as the sales office for Automotive Rolled Products.
- The Plymouth location serves as the sales and administrative office for Automotive Structures in the region.
- With over 1,100 employees, Constellium’s Ravenswood, West Virginia plant is one of the world’s largest rolled products facilities.
- The Ravenswood facility offers customers the most powerful plate stretchers in the world.
- The Ravenswood facility features one of the largest cold rolling mills.
- Constellium Van Buren is a state-of-the-art production facility which produces customized aluminium automotive structures and Crash Management Systems for OEM customers.
- The Van Buren facility features advanced development and prototyping capabilities.
- Constellium's plant in White, Georgia is dedicated to the production of advanced aluminium automotive structural components and Crash Management Systems.
- The White, Georgia facility is strategically located to supply automakers in the Southeast U.S.
- Constellium’s University Technology Center at Brunel University London is a center of excellence for the design, development, and prototyping of aluminium extrusions and automotive components.
- The Brunel UTC develops automotive components such as Crash Management Systems, body structure components, and battery enclosures for electric vehicles.
- The Constellium Technology Center (C-TEC) in France is an R&D facility with approximately 250 employees dedicated to aluminium and its alloys.
- C-TEC's full- and prototyping-scale facilities provide a venue to develop new solutions that meet customer needs while housing a team of international scientists and technicians.
- Constellium Engley (Changchun) Automotive Structures Co., Ltd. is a joint venture situated in the heart of China’s northern base for the auto industry.
- The Changchun joint venture provides global customers with aluminium Crash Management Systems and other structural components.
- The Děčín plant in the Czech Republic is part of Constellium's Hard Alloys & Specialties operating unit.
- The Decin plant features a cluster of three cast-houses, three extrusion units and extensive finishing capabilities in cold drawing, cutting and machining operations.
- The Decin plant is one of the biggest suppliers in Europe with capacity of 100,000 metric tons a year.
- Constellium Gottmadingen is a state-of-the-art production facility which produces customized aluminum automotive structural components and Crash Management Systems for automotive customers.
- The Gottmadingen plant features advanced development and prototyping capabilities and is ASI certified.
- Issoire is one of the world’s top two aerospace plate mills, with wingskin capabilities and the first casthouse dedicated to producing Airware® technology on an industrial scale.
- The Issoire facility employs 1,600 people and is a key actor in the aerospace industry, producing plates, sheets, and extrusions for various market segments.
- Neuf-Brisach is one of Constellium’s largest plants with a capacity of 450,000 metric tons.
- The Neuf-Brisach facility is a unique integrated aluminium rolling, finishing, and recycling facility that designs and produces a wide portfolio of coil and sheet products servicing customers in canstock and foodstock, automotive.
- Paris is home to Constellium's Corporate Headquarters located in the heart of Paris, just a street away from the Champs-Elysées.
- The Paris corporate office houses multiple teams including Finance, HR, IT, Legal, Marketing, Sustainability, Internal Audit, Communications and Public Affairs.
- Constellium’s Automotive Structures plant in San Luis Potosí produces customized aluminium Crash Management Systems and structural components for automakers in Mexico.
- The San Luis Potosí plant is centrally located to serve Mexico’s expansive auto industry.
- The San Luis Potosí plant features a state-of-the-art quality lab.
- Constellium’s plant at Singen is an aluminium manufacturing plant with over 1,500 employees.
- The Singen plant features an integrated hot/cold rolling line, high-grade cold mills, and one of the largest extrusion presses in the world.
- Sierre produces large profiles used in the transportation, industry, and general engineering sectors and has two of the largest extrusion presses in the world.
- The Sierre facility also manufactures high value-added aluminium plates in a variety of widths dedicated to industry, defense, and aerospace.
- Constellium Vigo is a state-of-the-art production facility which produces customized aluminium Crash Management Systems for OEM customers.
- The Vigo facility employs more than 60 employees and is part of a network of Automotive Structures facilities serving automakers across Europe.
- Zilina features advanced technologies for forming, machining, welding, and heat-treating aluminium automotive components along with a state-of-the-art quality lab to ensure products meet customer specifications.
- The Zilina plant has 160 employees.
- Zurich is home to a Corporate Office located in the Altstetten quarter in district 9 of Zurich.
- The Zurich corporate office houses multiple teams including Finance, HR, IT, Legal, Marketing, Purchasing, Sales, Strategy and Internal Audit.
