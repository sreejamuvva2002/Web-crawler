# Boogook Georgia, LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-06-28

**Source:** [Korean auto supplier Boogook Georgia enters US market with a $6.8 million facility in Macon](https://wgxa.tv/news/local/korean-auto-supplier-boogook-georgia-enters-us-market-with-a-68-million-facility-in-macon)  ·  `page_053104`

## Facts

- Boogook Georgia, LLC is a Korean automotive supplier known for its expertise in fluid-carrying systems for vehicles.
- The company announced its entry into the United States market with the establishment of its first facility in Macon.
- Boogook Georgia will undertake the redevelopment of an existing building located at 2600 Weaver Road within the Macon-Bibb County Industrial Authority (MBCIA) Industrial Park.
- The company plans to invest $300,000 in leasehold improvements and a total capital investment of $6.8 million to support its operations in the region.
- Joong Suk Oh serves as the Chief Executive Officer of Boogook Georgia, LLC.
- CEO Joong Suk Oh highlighted the strategic decision to establish operations in Macon to better serve Korean automotive manufacturing partners in the local market.
- The expansion positions the company to effectively cater to the Southeastern United States and contribute to enhanced regional operations.
- Boogook Georgia aims to build a successful partnership with the MBCIA as it embarks on this new chapter.
- The selection of Macon was motivated in part by its proximity to Hyundai Motor's Metaplant America in Savannah, a $5.5 billion facility.
- The location is also near Kia's West Point manufacturing facility.
- The 2600 Weaver Road site offers strategic highway access via I-16, situated within two miles of the company's new location.
- Mayor Lester M. Miller of Macon-Bibb County welcomed Boogook Georgia as the newest addition to the community and underscored Georgia's favorable business environment.
- Mayor Miller highlighted the area's convenient access to interstates and highways, positioning it as an ideal choice for industries seeking efficient product transportation.
- Upon its opening in 2024, Boogook Georgia is projected to generate 80 full-time jobs.
- Further job growth is anticipated in the future.
- The employment opportunities will provide professional roles spanning management, manufacturing, assembly, quality assurance, and delivery functions.
- Stephen Adams serves as the Executive Director of the MBCIA.
- Stephen Adams hailed Boogook's decision to establish a presence in Macon as a testament to the region's growing automotive industry.
- Adams emphasized Middle Georgia's appeal to companies in this sector, highlighting its competitiveness and potential to contribute significantly to future economic growth and job creation.
