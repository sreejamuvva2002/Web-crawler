# US Federal Electric Vehicle Tax Credit Proposal

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2021-08-18

**Source:** [US EV Tax Incentive Debate Heats Up In Congress - CleanTechnica](https://cleantechnica.com/2021/08/18/us-ev-tax-incentive-debate-heats-up-in-congress/)  ·  `page_012047`

## Facts

- President Biden has issued an executive order requiring the US government to purchase mostly electric vehicles for its massive fleet of vehicles.
- President Biden pledged to put federal muscle to work expanding the supply of public charging equipment for EVs.
- The Biden proposal aims to keep the existing $7,500 federal tax credit in place and revive it for brands that no longer qualify, including Tesla and GM.
- The proposal adds two new tax credits: $2,500 for electric cars manufactured in the US and another $2,500 for electric cars built in the US by union workers.
- Under the Biden plan, buyers could qualify for a federal tax credit of up to $12,500 if they choose to buy an electric car.
- The existing tax credit is only available on the first 200,000 eligible electric vehicles sold in America by any manufacturer.
- Tesla and General Motors have long since passed the 200,000 vehicle threshold, meaning their customers currently get no benefit from the federal tax credit.
- US senators proposed a non-binding resolution to limit the tax credit to families earning less than $100,000 a year and cut it off for any vehicle costing more than $40,000.
- Without reviving the extension for Tesla and GM buyers, only three EVs would be eligible under the proposed limits: the Hyundai Ioniq, the base Hyundai Kona EV with no options, and the Nissan Leaf.
- The base-level Chevy Bolt would be eligible except General Motors has already run through its allotment of EV tax credits.
- The base Tesla Model 3 starts at $39,990 but is not eligible because it falls under the exhausted manufacturer cap.
- Under NAFTA or its successor, vehicles assembled in Mexico and Canada using parts sourced from multiple foreign countries qualify as “American made.”
- Honda issued a statement saying production associates in Alabama, Georgia, Indiana, and Ohio deserve fair treatment and should not be penalized for their choice of workplace regarding the union provision.
- Toyota North America stated that the policy would unfairly discriminate against American autoworkers based on their choice of whether to unionize.
- Autos Drive America, an industry trade group representing foreign automakers, noted that half of all vehicles manufactured in the U.S. are built by Americans who have chosen not to join a union.
- All federal EV incentives are now part of the budget reconciliation process within Biden’s $3.5 trillion infrastructure and social justice plan.
- A 2019 study by researchers at the University of California Davis found that Black and Latino car buyers account for 41% of gas-powered vehicle purchases but only 12% of electric vehicle purchases.
