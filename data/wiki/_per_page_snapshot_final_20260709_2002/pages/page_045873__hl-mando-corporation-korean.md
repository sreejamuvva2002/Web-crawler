# HL Mando Corporation (Korean: 에이치엘만도 주식회사)

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2014-09-01

**Source:** [HL Mando - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HL_Mando)  ·  `page_045873`

## Facts

- HL Mando Corporation is a public company and the largest global Tier 1 Korean Original Equipment Manufacturer.
- The company supplies brake, steering, suspension systems, chassis, automotive components, and automotive safety and ADAS products to General Motors, Cadillac, Ford, Chrysler, Chevrolet, Nissan, Kia Motors Company, Fiat, Volkswagen, BMW, Suzuki, Hyundai Motor Company, and many other global automobile distributors.
- HL Mando has an annual profit topping over US$6 billion every year.
- The company is currently owned and run by the original founding parent company, Halla Group (also referred to as HL Group).
- In 2014, Mando was split into two companies, with the legal successor changing its name to Halla Holdings and a new entity named Mando being created to establish a holding company structure.
- Mando has three main business areas: steering parts, brake parts, and suspension parts.
- Mando recently opened a joint-venture company with Hella to produce ABS sensor software algorithm.
- The company is part of the Halla Group, which comprises automobile, logistics, investment banking, ground transportation, education, construction, and sports and entertainment companies with over 10,000+ employees around the world under one CEO.
- Mando was created by founder Chung In-Yung on October 1, 1962, initially named Hyundai International Inc., and changed its name to Mando Machinery Corporation in February 1980.
- In 1988, Mando opened the Brake System Plant in Pyeongtaek, and two years later opened the Munmak Steering System Plant (currently renamed Wonju operations).
- By 1995, Mando Machinery had a suspension system plant in Iksan as well as a dedicated R&D center.
- Mando America Corporation (MAC) was founded in 1996.
- In 1999, during the financial crisis, Mando was sold to Sunsage B.V., a daughter company of JP Morgan, and promptly renamed as Mando Corporation before Halla Group repurchased it.
- As of 2013, Mando Corporation has six affiliated locations within Michigan, Alabama, and Georgia in the United States.
- Its affiliates have a 1,300-employee presence in North and South America.
- It is projected that Mando's affiliates will employ roughly 2,400 employees in the United States alone by 2017.
- Worldwide, the manufacturer employs over 10,000+ workers and has an annual revenue of more than $6 billion.
- Mando products include brake systems (drum brake, master cylinder & booster, ABS, ESC, AHB, EPB, MoC), steering systems (steering gear system, manual/hydraulic power steering gear, intermediate shaft, steering column, electric power steering variants), suspension systems (damper spring module, shock absorber, suspension strut, self-levelizer, semi-active suspension), driver assistance systems (smart cruise control, lane keeping assist system, smart parking assist system, blind spot detection, autonomous emergency braking), and electronics-iron casting components (power pack, yaw & g sensor, ultrasonic/torque/torque angle sensors, 77GHz/24GHz radar, front camera module).
- In 2016, Mando was served with two separate patent infringement lawsuits from Bosch GmbH and Jaguar Land Rover Limited for multiple products.
- This was in addition to Nexteer Automotive Corporation v. Mando America Corporation alleging trade secret theft and other numerous claims.
- Mando has faced past lawsuits regarding various discrimination claims.
