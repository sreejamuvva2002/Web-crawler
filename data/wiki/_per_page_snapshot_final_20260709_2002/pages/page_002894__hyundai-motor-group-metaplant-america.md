# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-02

**Source:** [Governor Kemp, state and local leaders celebrate Hyundai Motor Group’s historic impact on Georgia – Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority](https://savannahjda.com/2024/02/governor-kemp-state-and-local-leaders-celebrate-hyundai-motor-groups-historic-impact-on-georgia/)  ·  `page_002894`

## Facts

- Governor Brian Kemp and First Lady Marty Kemp joined Hyundai Motor Company President/Global COO and Hyundai/Genesis Motor North America President/CEO José Muñoz to celebrate Hyundai Motor Group’s impact on Georgia and update progress on Metaplant America.
- The facility will officially start vehicle production in the fourth quarter of this year.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated Hyundai has been an incredible partner for decades, helping recruit businesses, giving back to communities, and working with colleges and universities to connect Georgians with opportunities.
- Hyundai and Genesis electric vehicles on display included the IONIQ 5, IONIQ 6, GV60, and Electrified GV70.
- A hydrogen-powered XCIENT Fuel Cell Truck will be used for logistics needs between Hyundai’s manufacturing facilities and suppliers.
- Governor Kemp officially declared February 26 as Hyundai Day throughout the state.
- The Georgia General Assembly passed a Resolution commending Hyundai Motor Group’s dedication to Georgia and recognizing February 26 as Hyundai Day at the State Capitol.
- José Muñoz stated Hyundai’s investments in Georgia will create tens of thousands of great-paying American jobs and bring tens of billions of dollars in economic impact for decades to come.
- Since the Metaplant America project was announced in May 2022, LG Energy Solution was named as the joint venture partner in the manufacture of battery components.
- An additional expansion of the LG Energy Solution battery facility was announced.
- Hyundai and SK On are building an EV battery manufacturing facility in Bartow County to supply Hyundai Motor Group’s U.S. plants.
- Hyundai Motor Group’s planned investment in Georgia has reached more than $12.6 billion.
- The project is bringing with it the creation of 12,000 direct, long-term jobs.
- Lieutenant Governor Burt Jones and House Speaker Jon Burns attended alongside legislative leaders, GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson, local elected officials from the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority, and company representatives.
- Lt. Governor Burt Jones applauded Governor Kemp, state agencies, and the University System of Georgia, especially Georgia Tech, for their partnership with Hyundai.
- Up to 300,000 Hyundai, Genesis, and Kia EVs are expected to be produced annually at the Metaplant.
- 17 HMGMA suppliers have announced projects across 10 Georgia counties spanning beyond southeast Georgia, with further announcements expected.
- Supplier facilities are expected to create more than 6,700 new jobs and more than $2.5 billion in investment.
- Supplier facilities will be operational prior to the opening of HMGMA.
- Speaker of the House Jon Burns credited Governor Kemp and the General Assembly for sound policy direction that enabled Hyundai’s investment and expansion in Southeast Georgia.
- The Metaplant will serve as a force multiplier for additional investments in education, healthcare, housing, and transportation.
- The nearly 3,000-acre Metaplant site is located in Bryan County, Georgia.
- Four building exteriors are complete at the site, and outfitting for internal operations is underway.
- The Metaplant’s architecture incorporates technologically advanced elements alongside design features representing the Savannah area, Hyundai’s brand identity, nature, and clean energy.
- HMGMA is hiring full-time employees, referred to as "Meta Pros," at a rapid pace for training and skills on machinery, office and clerical work, and numerous other employment roles.
- HMGMA currently has 566 full-time employees.
- Approximately 75 percent of the current workforce are from the Greater Savannah area.
- An additional 37 employees are from Georgia but outside of the local area.
- Georgia Quick Start broke ground on its customized training center at the Metaplant site on February 8.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated Georgia fulfilled one of its commitments to Hyundai Motor Group and Georgians with the training center groundbreaking.
- HMGMA partnered with local technical colleges to prepare students for entry-level employment at the Metaplant.
- Electrical Vehicle Professional Technical Certificate of Credit (EVP TCC) training is available at Coastal Pines Technical College’s Baxley and Waycross campuses, Southeastern Technical College’s Vidalia campus, Savannah Technical College, and Ogeechee Technical College.
- TCSG Commissioner Greg Dozier stated the Technical College System of Georgia is providing comprehensive training programs tailored to the growing electric vehicle industry in partnership with Hyundai.
- In September 2023, Georgia Institute of Technology (Georgia Tech) and Hyundai Motor Company signed a memorandum of understanding to collaborate on research and applications for sustainable mobility, hydrogen economy development, workforce development, and smarter cities.
- U.S. News & World Report ranked Georgia Tech as the top public university and No. 3 nationally in energy and fuel research.
- Georgia Tech President Ángel Cabrera stated the strategic partnership with Hyundai will advance science powering key sectors and produce the next-generation workforce needed for these industries.
- Since 1998, Hyundai has directed over $6 million towards local Georgia communities through philanthropic programs and partnerships.
- The company is promoting STEM programs in local K-12 schools and hosting educational programs on alternative energy sources.
- Hyundai is supporting local organizations dedicated to affordable homeownership and focusing on environmental stewardship.
- Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority’s Trip Tollison stated Hyundai has given more than $1 million to regional nonprofits and formed key partnerships with local educational institutions.
- Trip Tollison noted Hyundai committed to $7.59 billion in capital investment in Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America and will create 8,500 jobs.
- More than 550 Meta Pros have already been hired, with 75 percent coming from the Savannah region.
- Beginning with mass production, the electricity used to power the Metaplant will come from 100 percent clean power sources.
- This aligns with Hyundai’s commitment to carbon neutrality by 2045.
- A 41-acre park will be featured in front of the plant, including trails, running tracks, environmental art displays, sports fields, and other amenities for employee use.
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America is Hyundai Motor Group’s first dedicated Electric Vehicle mass-production plant.
- The new plant will boast a highly connected, automated, and flexible manufacturing system that organically connects all elements of the EV ecosystem.
- The Georgia facility will become an intelligent manufacturing plant where order collection, procurement, logistics, and production are optimized utilizing AI and data.
- The innovative manufacturing system will help create a human-centered work environment with robots assisting human workers.
- Hyundai Motor Group is a global enterprise with about 250,000 employees worldwide and mobility brands including Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis.
