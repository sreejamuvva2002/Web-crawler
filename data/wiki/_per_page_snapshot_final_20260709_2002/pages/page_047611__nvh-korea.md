# NVH Korea

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-06-22

**Source:** [Henry County Attracts $72M Investment From Korean Auto Parts Supplier  - Global Atlanta](https://www.globalatlanta.com/henry-county-attracts-72m-investment-from-korean-auto-parts-supplier/)  ·  `page_047611`

## Facts

- A Korean auto parts supplier is making its debut in the U.S. electric vehicle supply chain by setting up a new factory in Henry County.
- The company will hire 160 people for the new facility.
- The company will invest $72 million in the new facility.
- NVH Korea’s Locust Grove facility will make battery parts for Hyundai Mobis.
- Hyundai Mobis is tasked with assembling modules of batteries powering electric vehicles produced at the Hyundai Meta Plant near Savannah.
- NVH will sell directly to the Georgia plants of both Kia and Hyundai.
- The company is based in Ulsan, South Korea.
- The company was founded in 1984 to make components that manage heat and reduce noise and vibrations.
- For more than a decade, the company has operated a factory making floor mats, cargo mats, and cargo trays in Columbus through its subsidiary, AFS America LLC.
- According to a news release from Gov. Brian Kemp’s office, NVH has factories in Korea, Europe, and India.
- Georgia will be the first location where NVH places emphasis on electric vehicles.
- Ja Kyum Koo is the chairman and CEO of NVH Korea.
- Ja Kyum Koo stated that cooperation between state agencies and company staff helped guide them throughout the project.
- Ja Kyum Koo stated this is the first step of the company’s effort to contribute to the electrification of the mobility industry.
- Ja Kyum Koo expressed looking forward to joining the community of Locust Grove and Henry County.
- The new factory is located at Gardner Logistics Park on Colvin Drive.
- The new factory is expected to begin operations in the second quarter of 2024.
- The manufacturing win is a significant boost for Henry County, which has seen increased investment in warehouses and e-commerce fulfillment centers run by foreign companies in recent years.
- Henry County straddles Interstate 75 near Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport, making it a prime location for warehousing.
- Korean mattress maker Zinus USA invested $108 million to put a new factory in Henry County in 2020 to produce and distribute foam mattresses sold through various online platforms.
- NVH is listed on the KOSDAQ stock market.
