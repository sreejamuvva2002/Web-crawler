# Seoyon E-Hwa

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-02-03

**Source:** [Hyundai Supplier Picks Chatham County for $76M Investment, Plans 500 Jobs - Global Atlanta](https://www.globalatlanta.com/hyundai-supplier-picks-chatham-county-for-76m-investment-plans-500-jobs/)  ·  `page_001343`

## Facts

- Seoyon E-Hwa announced that it would spend $76 million to develop a new factory in Chatham County.
- The new facility will be the first supplier to land in Chatham County, which encompasses the city of Savannah.
- Production at the Seoyon plant is set to begin in October 2024 and will create 500 jobs.
- Counting indirect jobs from construction and operation spending, the state estimates the plant will lead to employment for more than 740 Georgians.
- Chatham County borders Bryan County, where Hyundai's new meta plant will be built.
- Chatham and Bryan counties were among four that partnered to create a joint development authority to market the 2,900-acre, state-owned mega-site in Ellabell.
- The Ellabell mega-site eventually won Hyundai’s first U.S. electric-vehicle plant.
- Seoyon E-Hwa makes door trim, headlining, seats, bumpers and other automotive parts through its factory network.
- Seoyon's factory network includes locations in North America, India, Europe and South Korea.
- Seoyon was formed in 2014 in a spinoff from Hanil E-hwa, an auto parts company founded in 1972.
- Hanil E-hwa set up a facility in Troup County in 2010 that is still in operation today as Seoyon E-hwa Georgia.
- The Seoyon E-hwa Georgia facility supplies the Kia plant in West Point.
- Building the Chatham County facility will nearly double Seoyon's workforce in the state.
- Korean investment serving Kia, Georgia’s first OEM car maker, continues to sprout new job growth more than a decade after it started operations.
- Operating as Hanil, Seoyon first invested $8 million in a factory that employed 173 people in Georgia.
- Seoyon invested $5.5 million more in a 2014 expansion that was projected to create 80 more jobs.
- The West Point operation has grown to 630 people, which Seoyon expects to retain even as it opens the new facility.
- The Hyundai Meta Plant near Savannah is part of a parade of suppliers receiving benefits from communities that partnered to win the initial $5.5 billion investment.
