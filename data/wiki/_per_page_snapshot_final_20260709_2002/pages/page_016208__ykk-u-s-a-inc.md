# YKK (U.S.A.) Inc.

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [page_016208](https://www.maconbibb.us/tag/ykk-usa/feed/)  ·  `page_016208`

## Facts

- YKK (U.S.A.) Inc. announced the 50th anniversary of its Macon Factory on November 9 with a Family Day dedicated to employees.
- The Macon Factory is located just south of Macon in the newly developed Ocmulgee East Industrial Park in Georgia.
- Japan-based YKK first entered the U.S. market in 1960 as a zipper distributor in New York City, strategically located near the garment district.
- Founder Tadao Yoshida’s vision extended beyond distribution to a model of vertical integration that epitomized the company’s “Cycle of Goodness” philosophy.
- Site selection teams explored locations in Tennessee, North and South Carolina, Alabama, and Georgia before Georgia emerged as a frontrunner due to its robust infrastructure, including four interstate highways, the deep-water port of Savannah, and a major international airport.
- The decision to settle in Georgia was clinched by a fortuitous meeting arranged by Cloyd Hall, a Macon native and Special Assistant to then-Governor Jimmy Carter.
- Under the leadership of Yoshinori Kitano, construction and operation strategies were crafted for the new plant.
- Newly hired engineers, including future YKK Corporation of America CEO Alex Gregory, were trained in Japan to ensure the transfer of specialized manufacturing techniques to the Macon facility.
- The new plant opened in 1974.
- The facility initially focused on producing zippers tailored to the specific needs of the American market, including varieties for clothing, furniture, and outdoor equipment.
- Over the decades, the factory has expanded dramatically, employing over 600 local residents and diversifying into advanced solutions for industries including automotive, government, and medical sectors.
- In 1977, YKK played a pivotal role in establishing a sister city relationship between Kurobe, Japan, and Macon, Georgia.
- Over the past four decades, this partnership has fostered cultural exchange with more than 1,100 citizens from both cities visiting each other’s countries.
- YKK has been a stalwart supporter of the Macon Cherry Blossom Festival, providing substantial sponsorships and active participation in festival activities.
- In 1990, YKK (U.S.A.) Inc. donated $300,000 towards the construction of a dedicated building for the festival organizers.
- The city of Macon complemented the donation by providing $60,000 worth of land for the festival building.
- Chairman Tadahiro Yoshida personally attended the groundbreaking ceremony for the festival building in 1990.
- YKK Corporation was founded in Tokyo in 1934 and continuously sets industry standards for quality, innovation, and sustainability in fastening products.
- The company produces zippers, plastic hardware, hook and loop fasteners, webbing tapes, and snap and buttons.
- YKK leverages its integrated production, service, and supply chain solutions across 70+ countries and regions.
- At the anniversary event, speeches were delivered by Mayor of Macon-Bibb County Lester Miller, Consul General of Japan Mio Maeda, YKK Corporation Chairman Masayuki Sarumaru, President of YKK Corporation of America Jim Reed, and President of YKK (U.S.A.) Inc. John Smith.
- John Smith highlighted the pivotal role of the Macon Factory in embodying the company’s philosophy and its enduring commitment to the community.
