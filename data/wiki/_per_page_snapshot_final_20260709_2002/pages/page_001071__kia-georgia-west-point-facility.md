# Kia Georgia (West Point Facility)

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Employer Spotlight: Speed Toward A Fulfilling Career at Kia Georgia - Unmudl](https://unmudl.com/blog/employer-spotlight-kia-georgia)  ·  `page_001071`

## Facts

- Kia Georgia's West Point facility has been operating since 2006.
- The facility sits on a sprawling 2,200-acre site with a $3.2 billion investment.
- The state-of-the-art manufacturing operation runs 24 hours a day.
- The facility builds the Telluride, Sorento, Sportage, and the all-electric EV9 and EV6 SUVs.
- Kia Georgia’s West Point facility and its suppliers have created more than 14,000 jobs across the region.
- The facility has produced over 4.6 million vehicles to date.
- The facility has an annual capacity exceeding 350,000 units.
- Nearly 40% of all Kia sales in the United States start at the West Point facility.
- Assembly specialists work on welding, stamping, and final vehicle assembly using advanced machinery while maintaining strict quality standards.
- Production group leaders oversee specific areas of the assembly line, motivate teams, troubleshoot, and take ownership of station quality.
- Maintenance technicians respond to production line breakdowns, diagnose problems, and handle preventative maintenance to ensure equipment runs safely and efficiently.
- Kia Georgia partners with West Georgia Technical College and Georgia Quick Start for specialized maintenance certification programs that help young students access industry-relevant training.
- Development specialists prepare requests for quotes, negotiate long-term agreements, and lead yearly cost reduction activities while building relationships with suppliers.
- Procurement specialists coordinate purchasing activities between Kia Georgia and automotive parts suppliers, managing contract negotiations and delivery schedules.
- Supply chain analysts ensure materials flow smoothly through the facility.
- Quality engineers analyze data to identify manufacturing process improvements and work with suppliers to address issues before they impact production.
- Quality engineering roles require expertise across multiple disciplines, including electrical, mechanical, and mechatronics.
- The facility operates around the clock with multiple shifts offering flexibility for different lifestyles and schedules.
- The 70,000-square-foot Kia Georgia Training Center provides customized, advanced industrial training to help employees keep up with current automotive technology.
- Every employee receives comprehensive safety training, and the facility maintains rigorous protocols that protect team members at all times.
- Employees are eligible for comprehensive health benefits on the first day of the month after their start date, including medical coverage, dental, and vision care.
- The Employee Assistance Program provides free access to counseling, health and wellness programs, financial consultation, and legal consultation for employees and their dependents.
- The company’s 401(k) plan becomes available 60 days following an employee's first day.
- Paid time off benefits include vacation days, sick leave, and personal days.
- Additional performance rewards and incentives include bonuses, profit-sharing, and recognition programs for employees who achieve specific goals or demonstrate outstanding performance.
- Kia Georgia provides tuition reimbursement for employees pursuing approved courses, degree programs, or certifications that align with their current role or future opportunities.
- Career development opportunities include training and workshops, mentorship programs, performance reviews, leadership development, job rotation, and networking events.
- Relocation assistance supports employees moving for work-related reasons, including financial aid for moving expenses, temporary housing, and help with home transitions.
- Kia Georgia supports veterans, active-duty service members, and military families through flexible learning options, career transition resources, and recognition of military experience.
- The company focuses on building a "Customer-Centric, People-Focused Culture" that emphasizes empathy, collaboration, transparency, innovation, and continuous improvement.
- Many employees begin in entry-level production roles and advance to team leadership, technical specialist positions, and management roles.
- Mentorship programs pair experienced employees with newer team members to facilitate knowledge transfer and professional development.
- Leadership development programs prepare high-potential employees for management positions by building skills in team motivation, strategic thinking, and operational excellence.
- Job rotation opportunities allow employees to gain experience across different areas of the facility, broadening their understanding of the entire manufacturing process.
- The company makes a deliberate effort to promote from within, rewarding employees who learn and grow.
- Manufacturing Georgia is a program developed through a strategic partnership between the Georgia Association of Manufacturers and Unmudl, designed specifically to connect workers with employers like Kia Georgia.
- Unmudl offers direct pathways to jobs with training aligned with skills Kia Georgia actively seeks, including mechatronics and automation training, manufacturing basics, and technical skills in electronics and industrial systems.
- Kia Georgia has been recognized as an outstanding example of international manufacturing success in Georgia, earning praise from state officials and industry leaders for its rapid growth and community impact.
