# PPG Industries, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- PPG Industries, Inc. is the largest coatings company in the world by revenue.
- The company operates a facility in East Point, Georgia that specializes in small batch production of water and oil-based coatings.
- The East Point plant pre-assembles, mixes, fills, packages, and ships 3 million gallons of coatings each year.
- The plant has the potential to produce above current levels and reduce its order delays.
- The facility's current customer satisfaction rate is below the company-wide target of 98%.
- PPG East Point identified pre-assembly (PA) as the process bottleneck of the plant.
- Pre-assembly is the first step in the paint manufacturing process, and delays in this stage contribute to 47% of delayed batches.
- The PA bottleneck is caused by limited indoor raw material storage capacity, which necessitates using external trailers for raw material storage.
- Frequently used raw materials are often stored in hard-to-access locations at the facility, leading to delays and errors.
- A Georgia Tech ISYE senior design capstone project was initiated to decrease the cycle time of the pre-assembly process and the plant's entire production chain.
- The project team conducted three site visits to assess the capacity of the current raw materials warehouse and identify additional storage locations.
- Increasing indoor storage capacity allows operators to access and retrieve materials quickly while reducing holding costs for existing inventory.
- The team used an optimization model to create storage guidelines based on each material’s frequency of use.
- Materials used most commonly are designated to be stored in the most easily accessible locations to reduce retrieval time and establish a consistent storage policy.
- The team used Simio software to create a simulation model of the raw material warehouse and pre-assembly process to validate design strategies.
- Three versions of the simulation were created: (1) the current system, (2) the proposed system after implementing storage redesign recommendations, and (3) the proposed system after additionally implementing an optimal storage policy.
- The simulation model was validated by comparing outputs from the current system to data collected from the plant.
- Key metrics compared across simulation versions included the time to pre-assemble a batch and the number of batch pre-assemblies completed per day.
- The team identified four locations within the warehouse that could serve as additional bay storage for raw materials, assigning each a specific number of pallets.
- Four separate maps were created detailing how to design each new location to hold its assigned pallet count and how to allocate materials from external trailers into the new rooms.
- The warehouse redesign eliminates time-consuming raw material pulls from external storage, thereby reducing pre-assembly time.
- An optimization tool was built to reallocate materials based on frequency of use using a cost-free, continuous Google Colaboratory model.
- The optimization tool inputs consist of Python code and an Excel sheet that can be updated with locations and materials.
- The optimization tool requires no technical knowledge to run and does not store client data on the cloud, mitigating privacy concerns.
- The optimization tool contributes to reducing pre-assembly time by decreasing the time operators spend searching for raw materials.
