# Hyundai Motor Group and SK On EV Battery Cell Production Joint Venture

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-04-25

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Group and SK On to Establish EV Battery Cell Production Joint Venture in US](https://www.hyundai.com/worldwide/en/newsroom/detail/hyundai-motor-group-and-sk-on-to-establish-ev-battery-cell-production-joint-venture-in-us-0000000237)  ·  `page_000149`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group announced plans to establish an EV battery cell production joint venture in the US with partner SK On.
- Affiliates Hyundai Motor Company, Kia Corporation, and Hyundai Mobis each approved the joint venture plan at their respective board meetings on April 25, 2023.
- In November 2022, Hyundai Motor Group signed a memorandum of understanding (MOU) with SK On to secure EV battery supply for North America.
- The joint venture's total investment is expected to be approximately USD 5 billion.
- Hyundai Motor Group and SK On will each hold a 50 percent stake in the joint venture.
- Both parties plan to establish an EV battery cell plant in Bartow County, Georgia.
- The new plant will be located close to Hyundai's U.S. production facilities: Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Alabama, Kia Georgia, and Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- The plant is expected to start manufacturing battery cells in the second half of 2025.
- The facility will have an annual production capacity of 35 GWh.
- This capacity is sufficient to support the production of 300,000 EVs.
- Hyundai Mobis will assemble battery packs using cells from the new plant.
- Hyundai Mobis will supply the assembled battery packs to the Group's U.S. manufacturing facilities for Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis EV models.
- The joint venture aims to accelerate Hyundai Motor Group's electrification efforts and bolster its position as an EV leader in the U.S. market through a stable battery supply.
- SK On has been a strategic partner for Hyundai Motor Group.
- Both companies have previously collaborated on the production of globally renowned EV models, including the Hyundai IONIQ 5, Kia EV6, and Genesis GV60.
