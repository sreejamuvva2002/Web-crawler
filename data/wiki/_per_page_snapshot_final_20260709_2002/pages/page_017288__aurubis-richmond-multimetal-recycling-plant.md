# Aurubis Richmond Multimetal Recycling Plant

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Aurubis celebrates first melt at Georgia multimetal recycling plant](https://www.euwid-recycling.com/news/business/aurubis-celebrates-first-melt-at-georgia-multimetal-recycling-plant-250925/)  ·  `page_017288`

## Facts

- Aurubis's new multimetal recycling plant in the United States began production one year after its official ribbon-cutting ceremony.
- The facility is located in Richmond County, Georgia.
- The plant recovers strategic metals such as copper, nickel, tin and precious metals from complex recycling materials.
- These recovered metals are central to the expansion of energy infrastructure, data centres, artificial intelligence applications, high-tech production and the defence industry.
- Aurubis is investing around €740m in the US project.
- The project will create more than 240 jobs.
- CEO Toralf Haag stated that with Aurubis Richmond, the company is the frontrunner in multimetal recycling and processing in the US, a position it already holds in Europe.
- Haag noted the US market offered "appealing prospects" due to high availability of recycling materials and good local conditions.
- The facility is located in the City of Augusta, the seat of Richmond County.
- The new recycling facility can currently process around 90,000 tonnes of complex recycling materials per year.
- Processed materials include printed circuit boards, copper cables and other metal-bearing products.
- Once the second construction phase is completed in 2026, capacity will double to 180,000 tonnes a year.
- Aurubis Richmond is designed as a flexible "one-stop shop" and will be supplied with a wide range of metal-bearing recycling materials from US suppliers.
- The US industry alone currently requires around 1.8 million tonnes of copper a year.
- Copper demand in the US is expected to grow by almost 30 per cent over the next five years.
- Around half of the metallic raw materials used by US manufacturers is imported.
- Multimetal recycling could quickly close the gap in domestic metallic raw material supply for US manufacturers.
- As the first and most technologically advanced secondary smelter, Aurubis Richmond strengthens the independence of US supply chains by processing complex recycling materials.
- Numerous high-ranking political and industry figures attended the "first melt" celebration.
- US Representative Rick W. Allen (R), who represents Georgia's twelfth district, described Aurubis Richmond as "strategic" for the region.
- Rep. Allen stated the plant reinforces America’s ability to supply itself with metals that help strengthen national security, infrastructure and technology leadership.
- Georgia Governor Brian P. Kemp said in a video message that the plant is "a significant employer for Georgia and plays a major role in the American multimetal supply chain."
