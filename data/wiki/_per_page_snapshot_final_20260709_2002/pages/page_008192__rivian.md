# Rivian

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Rivian boosts Georgia plant capacity to 300,000 vehicles annually](https://evwire.com/p/rivian-boosts-georgia-plant-capacity-300k-vehicles-annually)  ·  `page_008192`

## Facts

- Rivian is increasing planned production capacity at its Georgia plant to 300,000 vehicles annually.
- This represents a 50% increase from the facility's original 200,000-unit target.
- The capacity update aims to lower cost per unit.
- The update supports future expansion as Rivian scales its next-generation R2 platform.
- The project is backed by an updated U.S. Department of Energy loan of up to $4.5 billion.
- Rivian expects to begin drawing funds from the DOE loan in early 2027.
- Construction at the Georgia site is set to begin in 2026.
- Vehicle production at the facility is targeted for late 2028.
- CEO RJ Scaringe stated, “We’re very excited to partner with the U.S. Department of Energy to grow our manufacturing footprint in Georgia… R2 dramatically expands our market opportunity.”
- Rivian’s updated DOE loan totals up to $4.5 billion, including $4,006 million in principal and $494 million in capitalized interest.
- The company is preparing to transition into vertical construction.
- Work is currently underway on key areas such as the stamping press section.
- Activity at the Georgia site is expected to accelerate through 2026 as primary buildings begin to take shape.
- The Rivian R2 will start production at the company's Normal, Illinois plant.
- The Georgia facility will support new programs beyond core vehicle production.
- Rivian plans to build up to 50,000 vehicles at the Georgia plant for its robotaxi partnership with Uber.
- Robotaxi production at the Georgia plant is scheduled to start in late 2028.
- The R2 program anchors Rivian’s capacity expansion strategy.
- Rivian’s updated capacity plan ties directly to its R2 production strategy.
- At its 2024 Investor Day, Rivian outlined a current manufacturing capacity of up to 215,000 units annually at its Normal, Illinois plant.
- Roughly 155,000 of the Normal plant's annual capacity is allocated to the R2.
- The Georgia plant is expected to significantly expand Rivian's manufacturing footprint.
- Rivian estimates the Georgia site’s two production lines could support up to 400,000 vehicles annually.
- The Georgia production lines will manufacture the R2, R3, and R3X models.
- The recent update may allow the Georgia plant's output to exceed the originally planned 400,000 vehicles annually.
- One production line at the Georgia site will be upgraded to a capacity of 300,000 units per year.
- Rivian has begun ramping R2 production, including the addition of a night shift.
