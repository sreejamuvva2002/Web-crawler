# Superior Essex Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2008-05-01

**Source:** [page_003228](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1271193/000110465908029213/a08-13203_1ex99d1.htm)  ·  `page_003228`

## Facts

- Superior Essex Inc. reported first quarter 2008 financial results on May 1, 2008.
- First quarter 2008 revenues were $757 million with earnings of $0.37 per diluted share.
- Adjusted earnings per diluted share totaled $0.69 for the first quarter of 2008, a 50% increase over the prior year first quarter adjusted earnings per diluted share of $0.46.
- Core Business revenues at constant copper increased 12% in the first quarter of 2008 compared to the prior year quarter.
- Excluding acquisitions and currency impact, Core Business revenues at constant copper declined approximately 9%, as compared to the first quarter of 2007.
- Stephen M. Carter serves as chief executive officer of Superior Essex.
- The company's expanded energy/power product lines now exceed $600 million in annualized revenues.
- Superior Essex announced plans to consolidate its global Magnet Wire manufacturing base, with benefits expected to begin being realized in late 2008.
- The North American Magnet Wire factory consolidation project is expected to be completed by the end of 2008 with estimated annual cost savings of $7 to $9 million.
- The company closed its Magnet Wire manufacturing facility in Vincennes, Indiana, incurring $10.1 million in special items including $2.1 million in cash charges and $7.5 million in non-cash accelerated depreciation charges.
- Superior Essex is headquartered at 150 Interstate North Parkway, Atlanta, GA 30339.
- The company manufactures and supplies wire and cable products for the Communications, Energy, Automotive, Industrial, and Commercial & Residential end-markets.
- It is a leading manufacturer of magnet wire, fabricated insulation products, and copper and fiber optic communications wire and cable.
- Total debt at March 31, 2008, was $356 million, with net debt of approximately $290 million.
- Global Magnet Wire and Distribution operations reported adjusted EBITDA of $22.8 million for the first quarter of 2008, a 36% increase over the prior year.
- The Communications Cable segment revenues declined 11% on a constant copper basis in the first quarter of 2008 due to reduced demand from major telecommunications customers.
- Copper Rod business segment revenues for the first quarter of 2008 were reduced to $14 million as compared to $72 million in the first quarter of 2007, following a planned rationalization of production output.
- Peggy Reilly Tharp serves as Director of Investor Relations for Superior Essex.
