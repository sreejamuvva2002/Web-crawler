# Georgia Environmental Protection Division

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2010-06-03

**Source:** [Title V Permitting | Environmental Protection Division](https://epd.georgia.gov/air-protection-branch-technical-guidance-0/types-air-quality-permits/title-v-permitting)  ·  `page_003808`

## Facts

- Georgia EPD has introduced a new online Title V permit application.
- Title V permit applications are now required to be submitted by using the new Georgia Environmental Online System (GEOS).
- Training information for GEOS is available online.
- There is no ability for EPD to hold information submitted in the GEOS system confidential, and all information entered should be public.
- If there is a need to hold information confidential, facilities must contact Steve Allison in the Stationary Source Permitting Program at the Air Protection Branch.
- Data cannot be imported from the old Title V software database into GEOS, so all information must be entered from scratch in GEOS.
- A Responsible Official (RO) may delegate another representative to represent a facility in its Permit and Environmental Protection related reporting and filings with the Georgia EPD, including Emissions Fee Statements, Emission Inventory reports, and Title V/SIP Application submittals.
- 40 CFR Part 64 requires the submission of a Compliance Assurance Monitoring Plan (CAM Plan) by facilities that have equipment subject to CAM regulations, and the CAM Plan must be submitted with the Title V application.
- The Greenhouse Gas Tailoring Rule (40 CFR Part 70, amended June 3, 2010) makes Greenhouse Gases subject to regulation under Title V as of July 1, 2011 if facility potential-to-emit equals or exceeds 100,000 tons of CO2e per year.
- To address GHG regulations, EPD developed a GHG Part 70 Application Supplement that must be completed and submitted with any Title V application (or Title V modification application) as of July 1, 2011.
- An August 15, 2014 update states that due to a US Supreme Court Decision on June 23, 2014, the GHG Supplement form is no longer required to be submitted.
