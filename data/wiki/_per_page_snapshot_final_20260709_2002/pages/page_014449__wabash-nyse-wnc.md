# Wabash (NYSE: WNC)

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-25

**Source:** [Wabash Expands Southeast Footprint to Strengthen Customer Access and Service](https://finviz.com/news/176193/wabash-expands-southeast-footprint-to-strengthen-customer-access-and-service)  ·  `page_014449`

## Facts

- Wabash announced a significant expansion of its network in the Southeast to strengthen customer access and service in the greater Atlanta region.
- The expansion strategy includes opening a new Parts & Service center, expanding its dealer relationship with Fleetco, and adding CS Truck and Trailer to its Preferred Partner Network.
- Wabash’s new Atlanta-area Parts and Services center is strategically located at the heart of the Southeast’s trucking corridor.
- The new center provides faster access to parts, service, and truck body upfitting for customers.
- The facility will offer Wabash’s Ready-to-Mount (RTM) truck bodies for dry freight and platform applications with rapid turnaround times for in-stock chassis.
- The Atlanta site joins existing Wabash service centers located in California, Florida, Ohio, Pennsylvania, and Texas.
- Additional Wabash service center locations are planned beyond the current network.
- Doors to the new Atlanta Parts & Service center officially opened on September 18, 2025.
- Fleetco, a long-standing Wabash dealer, will now represent Wabash in the greater Atlanta market specifically for trailer sales.
- The Fleetco expansion broadens Wabash’s dealer footprint across the Southeast and provides customers with additional access to new and used equipment through a trusted partner.
- Fleetco president Bill Tirrill stated that the partnership allows them to expand their sales force across the region and provide immediate access to new and used trailers.
- Wabash added three CS Truck and Trailer locations to its Preferred Partner Network in Georgia.
- The Preferred Partner Network model delivers expert service, maintenance, and Wabash Genuine Parts to customers through authorized third-party dealerships.
- Dave Hill, vice president of Parts & Services at Wabash, stated that the Atlanta expansions mean faster turnaround, more coverage, and better reliability for fleets operating across Georgia, Alabama, South Carolina, and Tennessee.
- Wabash is headquartered in Lafayette, Indiana, and designs, manufactures, and services an extensive range of products including dry and refrigerated trailers, truck bodies, platform trailers, tank trailers, and structural composites.
- Customers gain access to a nationwide parts and service network, Trailers as a Service (TaaS)℠, and advanced operational tools through the Wabash Marketplace and Wabash Parts.
