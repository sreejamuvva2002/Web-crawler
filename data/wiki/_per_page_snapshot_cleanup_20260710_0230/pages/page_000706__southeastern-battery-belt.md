# Southeastern Battery Belt

## Facts

- The Biden administration is betting on renewing the American industrial machine to speed progress toward decarbonizing the U.S. electric grid by 2035.
- The White House aims to use clean electricity to power a mass transition to electric vehicles to tackle transportation greenhouse gases.
- Incentives in the Inflation Reduction Act spurred the emergence of a new Battery Belt spanning Michigan, Indiana, Ohio, Kentucky, Tennessee, Georgia, and the Carolinas.
- Former Michigan Governor Jennifer Granholm picked battery and EV production as a strategic growth industry over a decade ago to help Michigan recover from the Great Recession.
- Georgia leads the country in post-Inflation Reduction Act clean energy factory investments with over $17 billion, according to a Canary Media analysis.
- South Carolina ranks second with $9.3 billion in clean energy factory investments, followed by Michigan with $8.1 billion.
- The Southeast built an industrial foundation in chemicals and advanced manufacturing that now supports EV manufacturing growth.
- Matt Kisber, former economic development lead for Tennessee Governor Phil Bredesen, helped recruit Volkswagen to build its Chattanooga plant.
- Lithium mining history dates back to the Foote Mineral Company opening the Kings Mountain Mine in 1938 in the Carolina Tin-Spodumene Belt across Cleveland and Gaston counties.
- Lithium from the Carolina Tin-Spodumene Belt was used for ceramics, pharmaceuticals, and Manhattan Project thermonuclear research during the Cold War.
- The Kings Mountain lithium mine shut down in 1988 due to corporate cost-cutting and overseas manufacturing trends.
- Livent's Hallman-Beam Mine near Bessemer City shuttered in 1996.
- Albemarle, which now owns the Kings Mountain mine, is conducting economic-feasibility studies to revive the dormant site.
- Newcomer Piedmont Lithium hopes to open a new mine in the area, though its initial public outreach campaign faced opposition over concerns about mining explosions and water well destabilization.
- Global lithium suppliers Albemarle and Livent still process lithium near Charlotte, North Carolina.
- If regional mines reopen, they could close the loop on localized EV production by mining, refining, battery fabrication, vehicle assembly, and recycling within a couple hundred miles.
- Lithium markets expert Joe Lowry stated that reopening mines would complement hard-rock mining in Canada and extraction from brines and clay formations elsewhere in the U.S. to create a diversified North American supply chain.
- Energy Secretary Jennifer Granholm emphasized that while the U.S. should extract critical minerals domestically, new mining requires environmental reforms and respect for tribal communities.
- Former Charlotte Mayor Jennifer Roberts noted that state regulators often lack resources to police industrial polluters but highlighted Vulcan Materials' community engagement practices as a positive model.
- South Korea's SK Corporation opened a $2.6 billion battery factory in Commerce, Georgia, in early 2022 before the Inflation Reduction Act passed.
- The SK Battery America factory ships batteries to Volkswagen's electric ID.4 production site in Chattanooga and supplies Ford's F-150 Lightning.
- The facility produces enough batteries to power 300,000 EVs annually according to company statements.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp praised SK for hitting employment targets two years ahead of schedule, with 2,600 employees already and a target of 3,000 by year-end.
- Norwegian company Freyr is fast-tracking a second factory in Coweta County, southwest of Atlanta, to leverage Inflation Reduction Act benefits.
- Freyr is making a $2.6 billion investment in Georgia in partnership with Koch Industries.
- Freyr expects to produce 2.5 gigawatt-hours of batteries in 2025, ramping to 38 gigawatt-hours shortly thereafter.
- The Freyr plant will employ more than 700 people and licensed battery technology from MIT spinout 24M.
- SK Corporation is teaming up with Hyundai to build a $5 billion factory in northern Georgia.
- SK Corporation is also partnering with Ford to build $5.7 billion factories near Memphis and in southern Kentucky.
- Georgia appointed an economic development lead specifically for South Korea in 2006, with Yoonie Kim facilitating $11.5 billion of Korean investment in the state in 2022 alone.
- The Kemp administration utilizes tax incentives and the state-funded Quick Start worker training program to attract EV employers.
- Volkswagen's Chattanooga factory received its global board in July 2022 to commemorate the start of electric vehicle production.
- The VW ID.4 is largely sourced from within the U.S., with steel from Alabama and Ohio, interior parts from Indiana and South Carolina, electronics from Kentucky and North Carolina, and batteries from SK's Georgia base.
- Volkswagen ramped up ID.4 assembly last year by hiring 1,000 new employees and adding a third shift, with another 500 hires planned for the following year.
- Tennessee is home to approximately 900 auto parts suppliers, a network that increasingly includes batteries to reduce global shipping disruption risks.
- Roughly half the U.S. population lives within 650 miles of Nashville, providing Tennessee with a logistical advantage for distributing finished vehicles.
- Memphis serves as a historic logistics hub halfway up the Mississippi River and is a global air freight center due to FedEx.
- Ford is building its combined battery and EV mega-plant, BlueOval City, in Memphis.
- General Motors manufactures electric Cadillacs at a facility in Spring Hill, Tennessee.
- BMW added EV production to its plant in Spartanburg, South Carolina.
- Hyundai and Kia are building new manufacturing sites in Georgia.
- Rivian's proposed site outside Atlanta faced legal challenges as the state's incentive package was tied up in court by opposing residents.
- Central North Carolina attracted new factories from Toyota and Vietnamese firm VinFast.
- The Southeastern region is solidly right to work, a policy used to weaken union power, though Tennessee recently passed a constitutional amendment to perpetuate this status.
- United Auto Workers President Shawn Fain has advocated for ensuring union representation at new EV plants in the region.
- Ascend Elements opened the first operational battery recycler in the Southeastern Battery Belt in Covington, Georgia, this spring.
- Ascend Elements uses a two-story-tall wet shredder to process used batteries and scrap materials from nearby factories like SK's.
- The recycling facility separates aluminum, copper, magnetic bits, and black mass powder containing electrode metals by the 1,000-kilogram sack.
- Ascend Elements is breaking ground on a $1 billion factory in southern Kentucky to use its hydro-to-cathode technique for pulling exact element ratios for battery makers.
- Ascend Elements announced a $1 billion deal to supply an unnamed U.S. battery manufacturer.
- Redwood Materials, founded by Tesla co-founder JB Straubel, is building a $3.5 billion recycling plant near Charleston, South Carolina.
- By 2025, Redwood Materials plans to produce 100 gigawatt-hours per year of cathode active materials and anode foil across its U.S. operations, supplying approximately 1 million electric vehicles.
- Redwood Materials spokesperson Daniel Zotos stated that localizing anode and cathode production lowers manufacturing environmental footprints, mitigates supply-chain risks, and drives down EV costs.
- Recyclers acknowledge that domestic recycling will not meet soaring battery demand for at least the next decade, requiring fresh mining to sustain EV production.
- Representative Sean Casten (D-Illinois) emphasized the need to keep high-value-add manufacturing in America to maintain a competitive advantage.
- The Southeastern Battery Belt has become indispensable to the national quest to clean up transportation-related emissions by locking in value-added EV supply chain activities.
