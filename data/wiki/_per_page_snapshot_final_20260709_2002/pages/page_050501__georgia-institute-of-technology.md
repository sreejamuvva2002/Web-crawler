# Georgia Institute of Technology

**Type:** research_center  ·  **Role:** research_and_development  ·  **Published:** 2025-04-24

**Source:** [Georgia Tech And Stryten Energy Install Lead Battery Energy Storage System For Advanced Research - SolarQuarter](https://solarquarter.com/2025/04/24/georgia-tech-and-stryten-energy-install-lead-battery-energy-storage-system-for-advanced-research/)  ·  `page_050501`

## Facts

- Georgia Institute of Technology collaborated with Stryten Energy LLC to successfully complete the installation of a Lead Battery Energy Storage System (BESS) at the university’s Carbon Neutral Energy Solutions Laboratory (CNES).
- The CNES facility is located within the North Avenue Research Area of the Georgia Tech campus and houses the Strategic Energy Institute (SEI) along with research groups focused on renewable energy and energy infrastructure.
- The installation serves as a foundational step toward establishing a living-learning laboratory on campus to support academic research and real-world exploration of medium-duration energy storage technologies.
- The decision to use a lead battery system was driven by its affordability, high power discharge capability, proven recyclability, and extensive performance data.
- Christine Conwell, SEI’s interim executive director, stated that Georgia Tech’s strategic plan envisions the campus as a dynamic laboratory and experimental test bed where sustainable practices are integrated into operations, creating mini ecosystems with partners like Stryten to yield situational data for innovative energy research.
- Scott Childers, vice president of essential power at Stryten Energy, noted that while large utility-scale implementations were previously the focus for solar and renewables, this BESS highlights behind-the-meter applications getting their moment.
- Childers stated excitement about deploying the unit in commercial and industrial microgrids and paired with EV charging stations to help the U.S. achieve its energy goals.
- Childers praised Georgia Tech as a tremendous partner and highlighted the advantages of lead BESS regarding cost savings, technology, environmental impact, and safety perspectives.
- The newly installed BESS integrates renewable energy sources with the CNES lab's existing power infrastructure to offer reliable backup power and reduce grid dependence during high-demand periods.
- The system is equipped with smart software and hardware that support bi-directional energy flow, allowing it to store energy during off-peak periods and discharge it back to the grid or a microgrid when needed.
- Richard Simmons, Director of Research and Studies at the Strategic Energy Institute, emphasized the Stryten system as a key component in Georgia Tech’s broader Distributed Energy Resources (DER) research efforts.
- The lab can now manage the battery’s charge and discharge cycles in conjunction with an existing solar photovoltaic array and a recently added electric vehicle (EV) charging test bed.
- This setup enables researchers to explore methods for time-shifting solar energy to meet late afternoon building demands or store power for overnight vehicle charging.
- Simmons expressed hope that the lead BESS will serve as one of several living lab battery pilots at Georgia Tech, alongside regional partners exploring R&D and testing projects involving flow batteries for longer-duration storage.
- Researchers are also exploring lithium-ion BESS that may integrate second-life EV battery modules to improve grid resilience and drive advancements in sustainable energy research.
- The use of DERs like this BESS is a growing area of study as institutions and industries seek to enhance grid reliability, manage energy costs, and support the clean energy transition.
- This installation marks an important step in advancing Georgia Tech’s mission to contribute practical solutions and research to the evolving field of sustainable energy.
