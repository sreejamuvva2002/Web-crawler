# Aurubis AG

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2023-11-16

**Source:** [Aurubis to Invest $700 Million to Build Smelter in Augusta, Georgia | Mining Connection — The Link For All Your Mining Resources](https://miningconnection.com/surface/news/article/aurubis_to_invest_700_million_to_build_smelter_in_augusta_georgia/)  ·  `page_017307`

## Facts

- Aurubis AG welcomed a visit from First Lady Jill Biden at its new site in Augusta, Georgia.
- The company is investing around $700 million to build the first secondary smelter for complex recycling materials in North America.
- Commissioning of the secondary smelter is planned for the second half of the coming year.
- An expansion of the plant is already under construction.
- The expansion will allow Aurubis Richmond to process a total of 180,000 tons of complex recycling materials per year as of 2026.
- Complex recycling materials processed at the facility include printed circuit boards and insulated copper cable.
- Augusta is one of five focus regions known as Workforce Hubs where skilled worker jobs will be created due to significant federal and private-sector investments.
- The new recycling plant will create long-term, secure jobs for more than 200 people in an industry strategically important to the U.S.
- The metals returned to circulation are essential to the mobility and energy transition and contribute to the electrical vehicle ecosystem.
- This project aligns with Georgia’s Electric Mobility and Innovation Alliance initiative.
- Aurubis's Chief Operations Officer (COO) for Multimetal Recycling is Inge Hofkens.
- The Workforce Hub initiative supports the company's commitment to training workers for challenging tasks in the secondary smelter.
- Aurubis has pledged a community benefit agreement with technical colleges in Augusta and Aiken (South Carolina) as well as the Richmond County School System.
- As part of this agreement, Aurubis will invest more than $200,000 in scholarships and job training programs for young workers over the next five years.
- The company actively promotes women in the metal industry through its international Women4Metals initiative.
- Georgia offers ideal conditions for tapping the growth potential of the U.S. recycling market due to its pro-business climate and excellent infrastructure.
- Increasing awareness for sustainability and a significant jump in collection volumes generate roughly 6 million tons of recyclable, metal-bearing materials in the U.S. every year.
- This high volume of recyclable materials is a main driver for Aurubis's investment decision.
- The goal is to establish a reliable and sustainable supply chain by returning more recycling materials to local cycles.
- Experts estimate that recycling volume in North America will increase by an average of 5 percent annually in the coming years.
- Roland Harings serves as the chief executive officer of Aurubis.
- Georgia's logistics and stable energy supply at competitive prices provide perfect conditions for profitable growth.
- Support from Georgia state officials continues to present opportunities for contributing expertise and becoming a multi-metal recycling pioneer in the U.S.
- The Inflation Reduction Act offers potential to accelerate Aurubis's future U.S. growth and investment, positioning the Southeast as a leading ecosystem for EV and multi-metal recycling.
- Aurubis is in a strong financial position and ready to invest more in the U.S. in keeping with its 'Metals for Progress: Driving Sustainable Growth' strategy.
- Aurubis's metals create the foundation for technologies of the future, including renewable energies, electric vehicles, digitalization, and urbanization.
- Demand for metals will continue to rise as a result of these technological advancements.
- For more than 150 years, Aurubis has produced copper and other metals crucial for modernization and ongoing development.
- The company considers itself a real multimetal provider.
