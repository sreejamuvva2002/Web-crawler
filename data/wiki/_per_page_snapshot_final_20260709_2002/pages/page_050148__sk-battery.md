# SK Battery

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2021-02-11

**Source:** [Appeal or Deal: SK Battery has two choices to save 2,600 jobs | FOX 5 Atlanta](https://www.fox5atlanta.com/news/appeal-or-deal-sk-battery-has-two-choices-to-save-2600-jobs)  ·  `page_050148`

## Facts

- SK Battery is counting on the Biden Administration to reverse an order that threatens the future of its electric car battery plant in Jackson County.
- A rival Korean battery manufacturer says the only way to save 2,600 jobs is to pay up.
- On Wednesday, the United States International Trade Commission sided with LG Energy Solution, agreeing SK Battery’s parent company SK Innovation tried to hide evidence in a trade secrets dispute.
- As punishment, SK faces a 10-year ban from using the battery technology in the United States, including its Jackson County plant.
- SK had already lined up two big customers for its batteries: the new Ford Electric F-150 Pickup and Volkswagen’s ID.4 SUV being built in Chattanooga, TN.
- LG Energy Solution’s top intellectual property attorney Song Jung stated that LG's property and technology were stolen and that SK must adequately compensate LG Energy Solution under law.
- Already 250 people work inside the SK Battery plant, which is still under construction along I-85 in Jackson County.
- The plant was on track for mass production by next year, eventually employing 2,600 people.
- Parent company SK Innovation says it now has 'serious concerns' about the future of the plant.
- The state of Georgia agreed to $300 million in tax incentives to land the SK plant two years ago.
- Much of the $300 million in tax incentives won’t kick in unless the promised jobs appear.
- The ITC is temporarily allowing SK to build those batteries for a few years, giving Ford and VW time to find a new supplier.
- A Ford spokesman said the ITC decision supports their plans to bring the all-electric Ford F-150 to market in mid-2022, calling it an important part of their plan to revolutionize electrified vehicles and a top priority for the company.
- LG attorney Jung says SK should strike a financial deal with his company that would allow the technology to still be used and the plant still stay on schedule.
- SK is pursuing a strategy of appealing to President Joe Biden to reverse the decision.
- SK Battery stated they are hopeful that the Biden administration will take seriously the negative economic and environmental impact of this decision and reverse it so they can continue to operate.
- President Biden campaigned on an environmentally friendly platform, promising 10 million green jobs.
- LG attorney Jung said he doesn’t believe the ITC decision in any way hinders Biden's progress toward those green jobs.
- Governor Brian Kemp called on Biden to intervene, saying the decision puts the future of the Jackson County plant 'in peril.'
- Governor Kemp stated that litigation in these disputes is always complex, with several additional levels of review prior to a final resolution and the possibility of a settlement.
- Governor Kemp noted President Biden and his administration have the opportunity to support thousands of hardworking Georgians and their communities who would benefit from SK Innovation’s continued success in the state, and sincerely hopes Georgia’s congressional delegation will join him in advocating for swift presidential action.
- The White House had no comment on the situation.
- Presidential vetoes of ITC rulings are rare.
- Biden has 60 days to make a decision regarding the ITC ruling.
- LG says it is waiting for a call from SK to make them a 'viable' offer, while people in Jackson County are left to wait and worry.
- Some SK Battery construction workers have complained about safety issues at the plant.
- Federal authorities say they found Korean nationals illegally hired by subcontractors to help build the factory.
- SK Battery insists its worksite is safe and says contractors are punished if they bring illegal workers on the job site.
