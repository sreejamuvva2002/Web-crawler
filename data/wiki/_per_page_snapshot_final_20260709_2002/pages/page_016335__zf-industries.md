# ZF Industries

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [ZF Industries Plant Tours - Gainesville -  Georgia Manufacturing Alliance](https://www.georgiamanufacturingalliance.com/events/zf-industries-plant-tours---gainesville)  ·  `page_016335`

## Facts

- The event features two plant tours in one day at ZF Industries in Gainesville, Georgia.
- Plant 1 initially produced 5-speed manual transmissions and has evolved into a production site for three of ZF's divisions.
- Plant 1 produces axle drives and steering components for the passenger car industry.
- Plant 1 produces suspension and steering components for the heavy commercial vehicle industry.
- Plant 2 was originally designed to produce gearboxes for wind turbines.
- In 2013, Plant 2 introduced production of axles and transmissions for the construction industry.
- In 2013, Plant 2 added axle assembly for the bus and coach industry.
- In 2013, Plant 2 added the assembly of gearboxes for the rail industry.
- Tour attendees will observe production systems, precision machining centers, and assembly lines utilizing lean principles.
- The facility utilizes lean logistics with self-driven tugger trains and incorporates environmental considerations into its building footprint.
- The tour agenda includes registration from 7:30 to 8:00 am, a corporate presentation and safety briefing from 8:30 to 8:45 am, a Plant 1 tour from 8:45 to 10:00 am, carpooling to Plant 2 from 10:00 to 10:15 am, a Plant 2 tour from 10:15 to 11:30 am, and a wrap-up with Q&A from 11:30 to 11:45 am.
- Tour attendees are required to wear long pants and closed-toe shoes, with high heels prohibited.
