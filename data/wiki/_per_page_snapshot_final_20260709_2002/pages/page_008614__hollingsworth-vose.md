# Hollingsworth & Vose

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [VA: Hollingsworth & Vose To Expand Manufacturing In Floyd County, Creating 25 Jobs | Trade and Industry Development](https://www.tradeandindustrydev.com/region/virginia/news/va-hollingsworth-vose-expand-manufacturing-floyd-31254)  ·  `page_008614`

## Facts

- Hollingsworth & Vose will invest $40.2 million to expand its operation in Floyd County.
- The company will add over 28,000 square feet to its facility at 365 Christiansburg Pike Northeast.
- The expansion will accommodate new production equipment to meet increased demand.
- Virginia successfully competed with Georgia for the project.
- The project will create 25 new jobs.
- Governor Glenn Youngkin announced the investment and stated that Hollingsworth & Vose has generated positive economic impact and job opportunities in Floyd County for more than four decades.
- Secretary of Commerce and Trade Caren Merrick stated that Hollingsworth & Vose has played a role in the success of Virginia’s advanced materials industry over the past 46 years.
- Joe Turman, Chairman of the Floyd County Board of Supervisors, stated that the new investment is over three times bigger than the largest private investment in the county’s 191-year history.
- Delegate Marie March stated that the expansion will provide crucial jobs for Floyd families and enable them to keep more families working at home.
- Hollingsworth & Vose was incorporated in 1892 and is headquartered in East Walpole, Massachusetts.
- The company’s advanced materials contribute to a cleaner world through products that provide clean air, clean liquids, and energy storage.
- Hollingsworth & Vose has been family-owned for seven generations.
- In 1976, Hollingsworth & Vose established its first Virginia facility in Floyd County.
- The company currently employs over 200 workers at the Floyd County location.
- The Virginia Economic Development Partnership worked with Floyd County to secure the project for Virginia.
- Governor Youngkin approved a $558,700 grant from the Commonwealth’s Opportunity Fund to assist Floyd County with the project.
