# Hanon Systems Corp.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** thermal_management  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hanon to build $40 mn heat pump systems plant in US state of Georgia - KED Global](https://www.kedglobal.com/electric-vehicles/newsView/ked202305240019)  ·  `page_001391`

## Facts

- Hanon Systems Corp. is a South Korean thermal management systems provider for automakers.
- Hanon Systems Corp. is set to inject $40 million to establish a plant in the US state of Georgia.
- The new facility is located in Bulloch County, Georgia.
- The plant will assemble automotive thermal solutions and create 160 new jobs.
- The facility is set to begin operations in May 2024.
- The plant will also be expected to begin operations as early as the third quarter of 2024.
- The facility will accelerate Hanon’s supplies for electric vehicles (EVs) to Hyundai Motor Group, Ford Motor Co., and other global clients.
- There is a possible expansion in annual production at the facility to 500,000 EVs.
- The investment aligns with Hanon’s strategy to support the industry shift toward electrification while growing the company’s footprint in North America.
- Min Sung serves as the president and chief executive of Hanon.
- Hanon will intensify its heat management systems production in the US as global automakers increase manufacturing under the United States-Mexico-Canada Agreement (USMCA).
- The USMCA took effect in July 2020 to incentivize automotive parts made in North America.
- Hanon offers a variety of solutions in heating ventilation and air conditioning; powertrain cooling; compressor; fluid transport; and electronics and fluid pressure.
- Hanon was named Supplier of the Year by General Motors two months prior to the announcement.
- Hanon earned the Supplier of the Year title from GM for six consecutive years by exceeding requirements and providing innovative technologies with high quality.
- The office of Georgia Governor Brian Kemp announced the plant details on a Tuesday.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Georgia’s automotive and e-mobility industries continue to grow as they welcome Hanon Systems to Georgia.
- Governor Brian Kemp noted that Georgia is furthering its position as the EV capital of America and that new jobs and opportunities are coming to Georgians across the state.
- Other Korean car parts suppliers are also set to expand their production in the US as global automakers accelerate EV manufacturing in the region.
- Ajin Industrial Corp., which directly supplies auto parts such as fenders to Hyundai, will inject 440 billion won ($334.1 million) to establish an EV components plant in Georgia.
- Ecoplastic Corp., a leading supplier of plastic parts like exterior bumpers and interior consoles, will build a $205 million plant in the same state to supply Hyundai Motor’s EV and battery plant to be built nearby.
- Seoyon E-hwa Co., which provides car interior parts such as door panels, bumpers and seats to Hyundai Motor, announced it will invest $4.2 million to expand production in Alabama state.
