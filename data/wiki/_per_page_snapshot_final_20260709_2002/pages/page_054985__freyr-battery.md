# FREYR Battery

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-11-11

**Source:** [FREYR Battery Announces Plans for U.S. Gigafactory in Georgia - T1 Energy Inc](https://news.cision.com/t1-energy-inc/r/freyr-battery-announces-plans-for-u-s--gigafactory-in-georgia,c3665528)  ·  `page_054985`

## Facts

- FREYR has selected and purchased a site in Coweta County, Georgia for a multi-phase Giga America clean battery manufacturing project.
- The project is expected to be developed based on the next-generation SemiSolidTM Lithium-Ion Battery Technology platform developed by 24M Technologies Inc (“24M”) in Boston, MA.
- The initial phase of the project will be a battery cell production module of approximately 34 GWh.
- The initial phase is based on a preliminarily estimated capital investment of $1.7 billion.
- FREYR plans to undertake detailed plant engineering in the coming months to finalize plans for the initial Gigafactory module.
- The company and key stakeholders are evaluating additional value accretive upstream and downstream modules as well as a second cell production phase.
- Total capital investments for the project are expected to bring to more than $2.6 billion by 2029.
- Over the life of the project, FREYR and its partners expect to create more than 720 U.S. jobs for highly skilled workers in Coweta County.
- FREYR purchased land for development at the Bridgeport Industrial Park site in Coweta County, GA.
- The industrial zoned tract is 368 acres with options secured for further expansion.
- The site was selected through a rigorous evaluation process based on key operational, logistical, and financial criteria.
- The Giga America site already offers substantial room for additional upstream and downstream development projects to strengthen cost position and improve the carbon footprint of produced batteries.
- The development of Giga America positions FREYR to meet rapidly growing customer demand for Energy Storage Systems (ESS) applications with U.S.-based conditional offtake partners.
- FREYR is seeing very strong interest for increased volume commitments through ongoing customer dialogues.
- FREYR is accelerating its previously planned U.S. expansion based on strong tailwinds in U.S. renewable energy development, grid stability initiatives, and recently announced tax incentives associated with the Inflation Reduction Act.
- Projected U.S. demand is being augmented further by increased interest from existing and new customer dialogues in the commercial mobility and electric vehicle segments.
- FREYR is intensifying and broadening its financing efforts to support expected capital commitments for the Giga America project.
- Financing efforts are running parallel with the ongoing construction of Giga Arctic in Mo i Rana, Norway.
- The State of Georgia and Coweta County are collectively providing strong financial incentives for the Giga America project.
- FREYR intends to apply with the U.S. Department of Energy for packages that could potentially include a grant and/or direct loan to assist with development.
- FREYR is evaluating clean power supply solutions for Giga America with utility providers in the region, including potential development of a dedicated solar plus storage facility.
- Giga America will leverage 24M’s SemiSolidTM platform, which simplifies lithium-ion battery production and enables highly capital, labor, and energy efficient manufacturing at scale based on U.S. technology originating from MIT.
- FREYR’s U.S. project team intends to replicate major elements of the Giga Arctic development by maintaining a continuous technical dialogue with key personnel in Norway.
- The announcement was made from New York, Oslo, and Luxembourg on November 11, 2022.
- Upon completion of all contemplated construction phases, the Giga America complex is expected to be one of the largest battery cell manufacturing developments globally.
- FREYR’s Co-Founder and Chief Executive Officer Tom Einar Jensen stated that expanding into the U.S. has been a foundational aspect of the company's long-term strategy and expects U.S. demand for ESS, passenger EV, and other electric mobility applications to grow rapidly over the next decade due to the Inflation Reduction Act.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp stated that job creators bring operations to Georgia for success and welcomed FREYR as a valued addition to the state's No. 1 business ranking, looking forward to its impact on Coweta and the surrounding region.
- Coweta County Board of Commissioners Chairman Bob Blackburn stated that Coweta County is quickly becoming a premier destination for top-tier manufacturing industries and looks forward to the innovation and success FREYR will generate.
- The total package of financial incentives for Giga America includes robust assistance from The State of Georgia and Coweta County plus expected production tax credits associated with the Inflation Reduction Act.
- The U.S. energy storage market is already the largest in the world and is expected to experience exponential growth over the next several years.
- According to Rystad Energy, cumulative collective storage demand of 4.5 TWh from the U.S. and Europe will be required through 2030 to satisfy a 1.6-degree scenario.
- Under the same scenario, Rystad expects annual collective storage demand in the U.S. and Europe to reach 1.4 TWh by 2030, representing a 34-fold increase from projected corresponding annual 2022 demand.
- Jensen added that FREYR is deeply committed to decarbonizing transportation and energy sectors and expects to make meaningful investments to spur job creation and develop localized, decarbonized supply chains in the U.S.
- Giga America will be based on the SemiSolidTM technology of FREYR’s U.S.-based licensing partner, 24M.
- The SemiSolidTM platform enables capital and energy efficient production of lithium-ion batteries at scale.
- FREYR is currently evaluating clean power supply solutions for Giga America with U.S. Independent Power Producers (IPPs).
- The company’s long-term ambition is to replicate the strategy employed at Giga Arctic to produce next-generation batteries that are among the cleanest anywhere in the world.
- A launch event will be hosted live from Coweta County, Georgia at 10:00 am EST (16:00 CET).
- The webcasted presentation will be streamed online in English, with a recording made available after the presentation on FREYR’s website and YouTube channel.
- FREYR Battery aims to provide industrial scale clean battery solutions to reduce global emissions and is listed on the New York Stock Exchange under ticker FREY.
- The company has commenced building its first planned factory in Mo i Rana, Norway and announced potential development of industrial scale battery cell production in Vaasa, Finland, and the United States.
- FREYR intends to install 50 GWh of battery cell capacity by 2025, 100 GWh annual capacity by 2028, and 200 GWh of annual capacity by 2030.
- A cautionary statement notes forward-looking statements regarding the project's ability to deliver an initial battery cell production module of approximately 24 GWh, among other potential benefits and risks.
- Information about factors that could materially affect FREYR is set forth in its Registration Statement on Form S-3 filed with the SEC on September 1, 2022, and annual report on Form 10-K filed on March 9, 2022.
