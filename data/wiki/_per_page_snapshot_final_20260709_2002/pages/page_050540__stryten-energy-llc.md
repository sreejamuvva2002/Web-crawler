# Stryten Energy LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-03-31

**Source:** [Stryten Energy Unveils Plan to Add 10 Gigawatts of New U.S. Energy Storage Manufacturing Capacity | Nasdaq](https://www.nasdaq.com/press-release/stryten-energy-unveils-plan-add-10-gigawatts-new-us-energy-storage-manufacturing)  ·  `page_050540`

## Facts

- Stryten Energy LLC is a U.S.-based energy storage solutions provider.
- The company announced a new plan expected to expand its domestic manufacturing capacity to 24 Gigawatts.
- The plan focuses on increasing production across its 11 U.S. manufacturing and battery component plants.
- More than 2,500 people currently produce batteries at these facilities.
- Batteries are produced for multiple applications including military and government, data centers, grid storage, automotive and material handling.
- The expansion will increase the domestic supply of batteries for military, long-duration energy storage, transportation and material-handling applications.
- The new investment is supported by existing advanced manufacturing production tax credits.
- These tax credits contribute to the company's ability to accelerate capacity growth while supporting the domestic industrial base.
- Mike Judd serves as President and Chief Executive Officer of Stryten Energy.
- Mike Judd stated that Stryten Energy is committed to growing its domestic energy storage capacity to serve the needs of customers and partners in the United States.
- Mike Judd emphasized that American energy security must rely upon domestic companies like Stryten that manufacture batteries to keep supply chains running and critical infrastructure supplied with backup power.
- Mike Judd noted that investments to significantly increase gigawatt capacity across their footprint will help support growing energy demand and achieve the nation's goal of true energy resilience.
- Meeting increasing power demands will require scaling up the production of all reliable battery chemistries.
- Stryten is advancing energy storage in the U.S. with a unique suite of domestically manufactured battery solutions using advanced lead, lithium and vanadium redox flow battery technology.
- The company's plants are located in Kansas, Georgia, Iowa, Virginia and Arkansas.
- Plans for increasing gigawatt production capacity will bring hundreds of high-paying manufacturing jobs.
- The expansion will deliver a positive economic impact to the surrounding communities.
- Stryten operates a domestic, vertically integrated supply chain that provides battery components to its factories and the broader U.S. battery manufacturing industry.
- Plants in Pennsylvania, New York, Indiana, Missouri and Wisconsin ensure a reliable U.S. source for critical materials needed to manufacture batteries.
- The investment plan includes increasing domestic processing capacity of recycled plastic from spent batteries.
- Recycled plastic is used to produce new battery components for automobiles, forklifts and locomotives.
- This recycling effort contributes to the sustainable circular economy of the U.S. battery industry.
- Stryten recently opened a lithium battery assembly plant in Georgia.
- The Georgia plant supports military and industrial battery needs with proprietary lithium module technology.
- The company is partnering with Dragonfly Energy, headquartered in Reno, Nevada, to bring advanced lithium batteries to automotive aftermarket and battery distributor partners.
- Stryten announced the launch of Storion Energy LLC through its affiliate Stryten Critical E-Storage LLC.
- Storion Energy was formed with a subsidiary of Largo Inc. (NASDAQ, TSX: LGO) and Largo Clean Energy Corp. (LCE).
- Storion Energy is focused on removing barriers to entry for battery manufacturers to domestically source price-competitive electrolytes.
- These electrolytes are used in vanadium redox flow batteries (VRFB) for long-duration energy storage (LDES) applications in the U.S.
- Stryten Energy is headquartered in Alpharetta, Georgia.
- The company provides solutions across Essential Power, Motive Power, Transportation, Military and Government sectors.
- Stryten's energy storage technologies include advanced lead, lithium and vanadium redox flow batteries, intelligent chargers and energy performance management software.
- The company is backed by more than a century of expertise.
