# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-03-27

**Source:** [Not 300,000 cars a year, but 500,000 - Statesboro Herald](https://www.statesboroherald.com/local/not-300000-cars-a-year-but-500000/)  ·  `page_001447`

## Facts

- As of its public grand opening, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America directly employs a few more than 1,200 people.
- The promised total of 8,500 jobs at the main plant and its five onsite suppliers is expected to be realized by 2031.
- Company officials announced a future expansion of capacity from the original 300,000 to 500,000 cars a year.
- A top executive said the new goal will eventually boost direct employment at the site to 14,000 jobs.
- The 14,000 direct employment estimate includes two battery plants.
- The extra capacity at the Metaplant is located on the nearly 3,000-acre Mega Site in the Ellabell and Black Creek area of northern Bryan County.
- The expanded capacity will result mostly from the addition of a production line for hybrid vehicles.
- The plant will not be limited to manufacturing all-electric vehicles (EVs) as first announced.
- About 50 reporters and photographers were given a tour Wednesday in motorized carts through the massive complex, which is now mostly finished.
- A battery plant being built in a joint venture between Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution is still taking shape.
- A ceremony was attended by Gov. Brian Kemp, U.S. Rep. Buddy Carter, Republic of Korea Ambassador to the U.S. Hyundong Cho, and Hyundai corporate officials.
- The event celebrated the largest economic development project in Georgia’s history.
- Groundbreaking for the project occurred two and a half years prior to the ceremony.
- A couple hundred “Meta Pros” (employees of the Metaplant) wore blue company shirts and white HMG caps at the ceremony.
- The plant utilizes hundreds of robots, including small floor-level materials movers, dog-like quadrupeds, and giant robotic cranes.
- Robots and employees are currently assembling two all-electric vehicle types: the compact Ioniq 5 and the larger Ioniq 9 SUV.
- Hyundai Motor Company President and CEO Jose’ Muñoz indicated plans for other production lines during his remarks.
- HMG also makes vehicles with the Kia, Genesis, and Ioniq labels, as well as Hyundais.
- Hyundai Motor Group is investing an additional $21 billion in the United States, according to CEO Jose’ Muñoz.
- Nine billion of this additional investment is designated to expand the group’s automotive operations.
- Part of the new investment impacts the operation at HMGMA.
- The company is expanding HMGMA from 300,000 units annually to 500,000 units.
- The expanded production at the Ellabell plant will not account for all of the $9 billion additional investment in automotive operations.
- South Korea-based HMG’s planned $21 billion total new U.S. investment over four years will also include a $5.8 billion steel plant in Louisiana.
- Altogether, this new commitment doubles the $20.5 billion Muñoz said HMG has invested in production in the United States since first expanding to America in 1986.
- The corporation’s originally announced investment to establish the Metaplant was $7.5 billion.
- Expanding in second and third phases to 500,000 units annually represents a more than 66% increase in the production goal.
- CEO Jose’ Muñoz stated that adding 200,000 additional units is like building a new plant.
- The expansion announcement was made on the day of the plant's official opening.
- Governor Brian Kemp remarked that the new news requires another ribbon cutting and groundbreaking for the new plant.
- Kemp noted that hardworking Georgians will either need to be hired or see a lot of overtime to produce another 200,000 vehicles.
- CEO Jose’ Muñoz confirmed in a roundtable interview that the expansion would require a larger workforce.
- This confirmation led to the “14,000 direct employment” estimate.
- HMG officials do not expect to add any more buildings at the site to reach the new production goal.
- A substantial portion of the production will be of hybrid vehicles.
- Significant investment is required on equipment, with most dedicated to hybrids.
- Originally, the project was thought to be a 100% EV plant based on trends and information at the time.
- The company is now moving more into hybrids, with a minimum of one-third going to hybrids.
- The shift to hybrids requires additional significant investment on equipment, people, and systems, but no additional buildings.
- Euisun Chung, executive chair of Hyundai Motor Group, met with President Donald Trump on Monday at the White House.
- Louisiana Gov. Jeff Landry also met with Trump to announce plans for Hyundai’s steel plant in Louisiana and overall $21 billion U.S. investment.
- Trump called Hyundai’s plans clear evidence that “tariffs very strongly work.”
- Trump reportedly said that Hyundai’s products will not be subject to tariffs because of its level of U.S. investment.
- It remains to be seen whether the tariff exemption will actually apply to the company’s vehicles or materials imported from Korea.
- CEO Jose’ Muñoz stated it would be nice if President Trump’s statement was confirmed regarding tariff exemptions.
- In an email, Bianca Johnson, the Metaplant’s assistant manager for communications and public relations, said the plant currently has 1,232 employees.
- The current employee count does not include Korean expatriates.
- Among the 1,232 workers, 1,077 are from Georgia.
- Of the Georgia employees, 185 are Bulloch County residents.
- 895 employees are from what Hyundai calls the Greater Savannah area, a 60-mile radius from the plant.
- Bulloch County has the second-highest level of employment in the plant among area counties, with 185 Bulloch residents working there thus far, or 15% of the total.
- Chatham County is first with 413 employees at the plant, or 33% of its workforce.
- Effingham County is third, with 103 employees, or 8%.
- Bryan County is fourth, with 91 employees at the plant, or 7% of the HMGMA workforce.
- The initial commitment of HMG alone was for about 2,600 jobs.
- Right now, there are about 1,200 of that 2,600 for HMGMA, according to Chief Administrative Officer Brent Stubbs.
- The remainder of the 8,500 predicted jobs are with onsite suppliers and the battery joint venture.
- Hyundai broke ground on the site in October 2022.
- Production operations commenced at the plant last fall with the first vehicle, a Hyundai IONIQ 5, rolling off the assembly line Oct. 4.
- In a release Wednesday, Hyundai Motor Group said its investment in Georgia related to the Metaplant and joint battery ventures with LG Energy Solution and SK On is $12.6 billion.
- This is the largest single business investment in Georgia history.
- The company expects to reach its previously stated goal of 8,500 total jobs at the Metaplant by 2031.
- A report from the Center for Automotive Research cites that Hyundai’s total investments in Georgia are expected to create nearly 40,000 direct and indirect jobs.
- These investments are expected to generate $4.6 billion in individual earnings every year.
- As of the end of 2024, more than $2.5 billion in capital investment and approximately 6,900 jobs have been announced by Metaplant suppliers in 12 counties across the state.
- In Bulloch County, three tier-one, direct suppliers to Hyundai are currently operating or completing manufacturing plants.
- AJIN Georgia manufactures stamped-metal parts that comprise much of the framework of passenger vehicles.
- Hanon Systems is a maker of "automotive thermal and energy management" equipment, which includes air-conditioning components.
- Ecoplastic America Corporation will manufacture injection-molded plastic automotive body parts for Hyundai Motor Group vehicles.
