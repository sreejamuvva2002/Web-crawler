# Hyundai EV Plant - Bryan County

**Type:** Facility  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[vehicle-manufacturing|Vehicle Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** Near Savannah, Bryan County, Georgia, United States

## Overview

A $5.5 billion electric-vehicle assembly plant and adjacent battery factory being built on a nearly 3,000-acre site near Savannah in Bryan County, Georgia, with planned capacity of up to 500,000 vehicles per year and employment for about 8,100 workers.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Assembly plant for electric vehicles and adjacent EV battery factory in Georgia, representing the largest single economic-development project in the state’s history.

## Investment

## Jobs

- **8,100 workers (assembly plant and battery factory combined)** — Assembly facility expected to be operational around January 2025; Quick Start Center to train workforce as construction proceeds.
  - Areas: Vehicle assembly, Battery production, Supplier jobs

## Timeline

- **January 9** — Construction of Hyundai EV plant underway; clearing portion completed.

- **May (previous year)** — Initial announcement of Hyundai plant with plan to produce ~300,000 vehicles per year.
- **January 2025** — Projected start of vehicle assembly operations at the Bryan County plant.

## Key facts

- The Bryan County facility will be three times the size of Hyundai’s Montgomery, Alabama plant.
- Planned production capacity will increase to 500,000 vehicles per year, nearly double the initial estimate of 300,000.
- The project includes an adjacent EV battery factory, expanding Georgia’s electric-vehicle supply chain.
- Approximately 200 South Korean families are expected to relocate to the area, with about 100 already in Pooler.
- Quick Start Center will cost $70 million and serve as a training hub for the local workforce.
- Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center played a pivotal role in attracting suppliers such as Igneo Tech.
- Igneo Tech is adding 150 new jobs to the region, unrelated to Hyundai but part of the megasite development.
