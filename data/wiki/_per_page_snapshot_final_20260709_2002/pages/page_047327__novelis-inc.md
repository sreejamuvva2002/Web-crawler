# Novelis Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2022-01-19

**Source:** [Novelis Invests in New Recycling Center for Automotive - Light Metal Age Magazine](https://www.lightmetalage.com/news/industry-news/recycling-remelt/novelis-invests-in-new-recycling-center-for-automotive/)  ·  `page_047327`

## Facts

- Novelis Inc. plans to invest $365 million to build a highly advanced recycling center for automotive in North America, adjacent to its existing automotive finishing plant in Guthrie, Kentucky.
- The new facility is expected to support the expansion of Novelis’s automotive closed-loop-recycling programs and reduce carbon emissions by more than one million tons each year.
- Steve Fisher, president and CEO of Novelis, Inc., stated that Novelis aims to be the world’s leading provider of low-carbon, sustainable aluminum solutions advancing a circular economy.
- Through this investment, Novelis will increase recycled content in its products, reduce CO2 emissions, and move closer to carbon neutrality.
- The recycling center will have an annual casting capacity of 240,000 tons of sheet ingot.
- The facility will be equipped with industry-leading processes including advanced shredding and sorting technology.
- It will feature energy-efficient innovations to support Novelis’s sustainability goal to reduce energy intensity by 10% by 2026.
- Advanced technologies at the plant will enable growth of closed-loop-recycling programs for more automotive customers in North America.
- Through closed-loop recycling, Novelis takes back aluminum remaining after automotive parts are stamped from sheet and reprocesses it into the same product for new vehicle production.
- The center will also have the capability to process aluminum from vehicles at the end of their lifecycle.
- When fully operational, the expansion will add approximately 140 new jobs in Guthrie.
- Novelis has a 37-year history of creating jobs in Kentucky and currently employs 1,600 people in the state.
- Novelis recently held the grand opening ceremony of its new automotive finishing plant in Guthrie, which currently employs 150 people and will grow to 190 people over the next two years.
- The company also operates an aluminum beverage can recycling plant in Berea and the Logan Aluminum joint venture in Russellville, both in Kentucky.
- Tom Boney, executive vice president and president of Novelis North America, stated that the investment will help achieve sustainability goals and support the carbon-reduction targets of automotive customers.
- Tom Boney noted that the Commonwealth of Kentucky has a strong, highly skilled workforce to meet the growing need for high-strength, low-carbon aluminum.
- Groundbreaking on the new recycling facility is scheduled for early 2022, with commissioning expected in 2024.
