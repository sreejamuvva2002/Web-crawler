## Facts

- An electric vehicle company planning to hit huge production milestone figures could face a roadblock depending on the 2022 Georgia gubernatorial election results.
- A planned production plant for Rivian Automotive Inc (NASDAQ:RIVN) in Georgia has become a focal point in the primary battle between two Republican candidates for the state’s gubernatorial race.
- Former U.S. Sen. David Perdue is running against incumbent Gov. Brian Kemp for the Republican nomination in Georgia.
- Perdue claimed Kemp pledged tax dollars to lure a California company funded by George Soros to Georgia, calling it ‘economic development’.
- Perdue stated the factory announcement was done by Kemp to promote himself in an election year.
- Perdue asserted that real economic development should be straightforward and organic, with local community and leaders involved in the process.
- Perdue claimed many Georgia residents are against Rivian’s announced electric vehicle production factory in the state.
- Perdue accused Kemp of acting like a career politician who is ignoring locals’ concerns and cutting them out of the process.
- Perdue said he will prioritize growing Georgia jobs without the George Soros connection if elected governor.
- Soros Fund Management is an investor in Rivian and owns around 2% of the company’s shares, making it around the 10th-largest shareholder.
- Amazon.com Inc (NASDAQ:AMZN) and Ford Motor Company (NYSE:F) are also investors in Rivian, with larger stakes than Soros.
- A Kemp campaign spokesperson said the governor planned to make comments Tuesday regarding Perdue’s accusations.
- Rivian announced plans for its second plant in Georgia, consisting of nearly 20 million square feet of space on a 1,978-acre piece of land.
- The Georgia plant will include production, onsite vehicle delivery, an event center, training facility, and a charging station with 144 stalls.
- Rivian was making around 50 units a week in December.
- Rivian has plans to increase its annual capacity to 200,000 vehicles.
- The Georgia plant would add an annual capacity of 400,000 vehicles.
- Rivian plans to eventually have four assembly plants worldwide.
- Rivian aims to produce 1 million cars annually by 2030.
- Rivian produced 1,015 electric vehicles in 2021 and reported 920 deliveries, falling short of original company estimates.
- The winner of the GOP gubernatorial primary in Georgia is expected to face off against Democratic candidate Stacey Abrams.
- In the 2018 election, Kemp narrowly defeated Abrams with 50.2% of the popular vote versus 48.8%.
- Abrams has previously voiced support for the Rivian plant.
- Rivian could soon find itself rooting for a matchup of Kemp vs. Abrams for Georgia governor.
- Rivian shares are down 8.98% to $61.49 Tuesday afternoon.
- Rivian shares have traded between $50 and $179.46 since going public at an IPO price of $78 in November 2021.
