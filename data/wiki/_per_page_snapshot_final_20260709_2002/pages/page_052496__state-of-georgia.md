# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2023-07-01

**Source:** [Georgia Job Creation Remains Strong - AllOnGeorgia](https://allongeorgia.com/georgia-business/georgia-job-creation-remains-strong/)  ·  `page_052496`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp, in conjunction with the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD), announced sustained momentum for business recruitment and expansions in Fiscal Year 2024.
- Between July 1, 2023, and June 30 of this year, GDEcD’s Global Commerce team supported 429 facility expansions and new locations resulting in more than $20.3 billion in investment and the commitment of 26,900 new, private sector jobs statewide.
- 83% of expansions and new locations are directed to communities outside the 10-county Atlanta region, representing more than 18,300 new jobs and $12.5 billion in investment.
- Metro Atlanta attracted nearly $7.8 billion in investments as a logistics and business hub.
- Statewide, new locations account for $12.8 billion in investments and expansions are responsible for 9,800 new jobs.
- Lt. Governor Burt Jones emphasized ensuring businesses have the necessary workforce, citing the Technical College System of Georgia, University System of Georgia, College and Career Academies, and private-public partnerships building the workforce pipeline.
- Speaker of the House Jon Burns highlighted historic investments in education, workforce development, and infrastructure alongside tax cuts and regulatory relief.
- During FY2024, Governor and First Lady Kemp led economic development missions to Korea and Switzerland that focused on strengthening existing relationships with key investors like Hyundai Motor Group and its supplier network.
- The prior year, Governor and First Lady Kemp led similar missions to Israel, France, and Switzerland.
- Georgia maintained its position as a global gateway for business, landing more than 8,100 new jobs and $5.9 billion in investments from international companies expanding or locating in the U.S. market this past fiscal year.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson noted the state’s strong partnerships, diverse industry landscape, and range of locations providing quality-of-life options for companies.
- The State of Georgia celebrated more than 50 years of continuous representation in Europe and Japan in FY2024, which remain among the top sources of foreign direct investment.
- The coming fiscal year will mark 40 years of continuous representation in Korea, a partnership that earned GDEcD the James A. Van Fleet Award from the Korea Society.
- All markets where Georgia maintains representation in business and trade accounted for 29% of total new jobs and investments in FY2024.
- GDEcD Commissioner Kristi Brigman highlighted the state’s transition from agribusiness and manufacturing to mobility and technology, celebrating a decade of excellence and project milestones.
- Manufacturing, including the aerospace, automotive, and e-mobility sectors, accounted for over 50% of expansions or new locations in FY2024.
- Kia Georgia added the EV9 to its lineup in Troup County.
- Toyota Industries Corporation established a campus in Jackson County.
- StandardAero operates in Richmond County.
- International life sciences company Gerresheimer announced a recent expansion in Fayette County.
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America suppliers announced new locations in Bryan, Candler, Chatham, Coweta, Effingham, Harris, and Laurens County.
- SOLARCYCLE, an innovator in solar panel recycling, announced plans for a new location in Polk County to support Qcells’ operations.
- Investments in food processing and agribusiness totaled more than $1.3 billion in FY2024.
- Japanese probiotic beverage company Yakult located in Bartow County.
- Korean bakery CJ Foodville located in Hall County.
- Walmart will source milk from local dairy farmers at a future dairy processing facility in Lowndes County.
- Technology and corporate hubs are creating at least 4,400 new jobs as a result of wins in FY2024.
- Cargill Corporation is establishing a new office hub in the City of Atlanta.
- U.S. Soccer’s future headquarters and national training center will be located in Fayette County.
- Georgia earned the rank of No. 1 state for business from Area Development magazine for 10 consecutive years.
- The state earned the No. 1 spot in Site Selection magazine’s annual Prosperity Cup rankings for successful investment recruitment in 2023.
- Georgia was awarded a Gold Shovel by Area Development for achieving significant success in attracting new business investment and job creation in 2023.
- Business Facilities magazine ranked Georgia as the No. 1 state for EV investments as well as a top 10 state for food processing, cybersecurity, aerospace and defense, and AI growth hubs.
- GDEcD serves as the state’s sales and marketing arm and lead agency for attracting new business investment, encouraging industry expansion, and promoting the state.
