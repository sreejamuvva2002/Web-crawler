# Lehigh Technologies

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2017-10-20

**Source:** [Michelin acquires specialty materials company, Lehigh Technologies](https://www.tractionnews.com/michelin-acquires-specialty-materials-company-lehigh-technologies/)  ·  `page_057116`

## Facts

- Michelin announced that it has acquired Lehigh Technologies, a specialty materials company.
- Lehigh Technologies uses patented cryogenic turbo mill technology to transform rubber from end-of-life tires and industrial goods into materials for new tires and other products.
- The process reduces the amount of raw materials initially needed, such as elastomers and fillers from oil- and rubber-based sources.
- Pete Selleck, chairman and president of Michelin North America, stated that using high-technology recycled materials helps achieve safer and more sustainable mobility while consuming less finite natural resources.
- Christophe Rahier, senior vice president of strategic planning materials for the Michelin Group, noted the acquisition demonstrates Michelin’s strategic intent to extend its materials expertise beyond tires and foster advanced rubber recycled materials in tire and non-tire industries.
- Lehigh Technologies is based in Tucker, Ga., near Atlanta.
- The firm employs about 100 people.
- Lehigh produces highly engineered, versatile raw materials called micronized rubber powder (MRP).
- MRP is a low-cost, high-performance, sustainable material that substitutes for oil- and rubber-based materials used in manufacturing tires, plastics, asphalt, and construction materials.
- Lehigh's customers include some of the largest tire companies in the world, as well as companies in construction materials, asphalt modification, and other markets.
- Alan Barton, CEO of Lehigh Technologies, stated the acquisition provides an opportunity to continue growth with Michelin's backing to expand capabilities, execute a global growth strategy, and pursue new market opportunities.
- Michelin completed the acquisition on Oct. 13.
