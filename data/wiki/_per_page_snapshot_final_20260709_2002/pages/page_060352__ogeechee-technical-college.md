# Ogeechee Technical College

**Type:** workforce_training  ·  **Role:** workforce_training  ·  **Published:** 2024-11-14

**Source:** [Ogeechee Technical College breaks ground on Robotics Training Center - Statesboro Herald](https://www.statesboroherald.com/local/ogeechee-technical-college-breaks-ground-on-robotics-training-center/)  ·  `page_060352`

## Facts

- Ogeechee Technical College President Lori Durden views the Georgia Industrial Systems and Industrial Robotics Training Center as a gateway to advancement and catalyst for community growth rather than just another campus addition.
- The facility's groundbreaking ceremony took place Thursday at the corner of A.J. Riggs Road and Highway 301 South.
- Approximately 200 people attended the groundbreaking event.
- Event attendees included Technical College System of Georgia Commissioner Greg Dozier, Speaker of the Georgia House Jon Burns, State Sen. Billy Hickman, and Rep. Lehman Franklin.
- Ogeechee Technical College began offering industrial robotics training in 2019.
- Bulloch County Manager Tom Couch and Jan Moore, the college’s vice president for Economic Development, first outlined the vision for a full-scale robotics training center at Ogeechee Tech.
- Governor Brian Kemp approved $23 million, included by Commissioner Dozier in the system’s 2023 budget, to construct the 37,000-square-foot Robotics Training Center in Bulloch County.
- The center will provide training to employees of regional manufacturers and companies utilizing automation in supply and warehousing facilities.
- Bobby Jones, chairman of the Ogeechee Tech Board of Directors, stated the center will house cutting-edge technology and sophisticated programs to equip individuals with needed skills, creating economic growth and workforce readiness.
- In fiscal year 2023, Ogeechee Tech delivered more than 240,000 hours of industrial systems and robotics training.
- Once open, the Robotics Center will more than double the college's training capacity and significantly expand the depth of training available.
- Georgia Southern University Center for Business Analytics and Economic Research projects the center will yield an economic output of just over $9 million annually within a year of opening, growing to over $15 million within a few years.
- Demand for robotics training stems largely from Hyundai's 2,541-acre Bryan County Metaplant, which broke ground in October 2022 to manufacture electric vehicles.
- The Hyundai Metaplant is currently under construction just south of I-16 East near Blitchton and has rolled off its first production vehicle.
- The Hyundai Metaplant complex is projected to eventually employ 8,500 people on-site.
- More than a dozen manufacturing plants being built in southeast Georgia are directly related to the Hyundai Metaplant, including three in Bulloch County.
- Ajin USA, a supplier of metal auto body parts to Hyundai, opened its $317 million plant in July at Bruce Yawn Commerce Park next to I-16.
- Hanon Systems, a maker of automotive thermal and energy management equipment including air-conditioning components, is building a plant in Bulloch County as a supplier to the Metaplant.
- Ecoplastic America Corporation will manufacture injection-molded plastic automotive body parts for Hyundai Motor Group vehicles and is also building a plant in Bulloch County.
- The factories for Hanon Systems and Ecoplastic America are expected to come online soon.
- The training center has established a partnership between Ogeechee Tech and Southeastern Technical College in Vidalia.
- The center will house 12 industrial labs, classrooms, computer labs, and meeting spaces designed for innovative, state-of-the-art programs preparing trainees for current and future workforce demands.
- State Sen. Billy Hickman stated the center will serve nine counties, ensure regional manufacturers have access to state-of-the-art training, inspire young students, generate higher-paying skilled jobs, and attract future manufacturers by assuring workforce needs will be met.
