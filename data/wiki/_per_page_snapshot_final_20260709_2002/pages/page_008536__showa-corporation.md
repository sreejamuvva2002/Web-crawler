## Facts

- Showa Corporation was a globally recognized Japanese manufacturer specializing in high-performance shock absorbers, suspension systems, steering systems, and other critical components for automobiles, motorcycles, and power products.
- Showa Corporation was founded in 1938 and built a strong reputation for quality and innovation while supplying major Original Equipment Manufacturers (OEMs) worldwide.
- In January 2021, Showa Corporation merged with Keihin Corporation, Nissin Kogyo Co., Ltd., and Hitachi Automotive Systems, Ltd. to form Hitachi Astemo, Ltd.
- The merger integration aimed to create a new global mega-supplier in the automotive and motorcycle industry by combining strengths in powertrain, chassis, and safety systems.
- While the Showa brand and its technologies continue within Hitachi Astemo, Showa Corporation as an independent entity ceased operations upon this merger.
- The Gyoda headquarters served as the central hub for Showa's global operations, including corporate management, research and development, strategic planning, and oversight of its automotive and motorcycle components business.
- The Gyoda facility was known for advanced manufacturing capabilities and R&D centers focused on suspension, steering, and hydraulic technologies, often including proving grounds and testing facilities.
- Prior to its merger into Hitachi Astemo, Showa Corporation maintained an extensive global footprint with numerous manufacturing plants, R&D centers, and sales offices across Asia (Japan, Thailand, Indonesia, India, China, Vietnam), North America (USA, Canada, Mexico), South America (Brazil), and Europe (UK, Spain, Germany, Italy).
- These global facilities were dedicated to designing, manufacturing, and supplying high-quality shock absorbers, power steering systems, drive train components, and other critical parts for major automotive and motorcycle OEMs.
- Showa Corporation's headquarters address was 1 Chome-14-1 Fujiwara, Gyoda, Saitama Prefecture, Japan.
- A key US facility was located at 707 W. Cherry St., Sunbury, OH 43074, USA.
- North American operations catered directly to OEM clients like Honda and Harley-Davidson, managed regional sales, provided localized engineering support, and ensured just-in-time delivery.
- A key Asian facility was located at Rojana Industrial Park, Ayutthaya, Thailand, supplying components to Japanese and international automotive/motorcycle manufacturers in the ASEAN region.
- A key European facility was located at Showa Autoparts Spain, S.A.U., Martorelles, Barcelona, Spain, serving European automotive manufacturers with localized production and R&D support.
- Hitachi Astemo announced its participation in EICMA 2023 (Milan Motorcycle Show) on November 2, 2023, showcasing advanced suspension systems under the Showa brand for motorcycles.
- Hitachi Astemo Americas introduced the new SHOWA G²as (Groove² Gas) Series aftermarket rear cushion units for Harley-Davidson Touring models from 2014 onwards on October 26, 2022.
- Hitachi Astemo, Ltd. officially began operations on January 5, 2021, following the successful business integration of Hitachi Automotive Systems, Keihin Corporation, Showa Corporation, and Nissin Kogyo Co., Ltd.
- As of April 2025, there have been no executive new hires or exits specifically for Showa Corporation within the last 12 months due to its merger into Hitachi Astemo.
