# Vernay Laboratories

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2024-09-09

## Facts

- Vernay is a global leader in precision fluid control components.
- Vernay announced its participation in MEDevice Boston, scheduled for September 25-26, 2024, at the Boston Convention & Exhibition Center in Boston, MA, USA.
- The Vernay medical team will be located in Hall C, Booth 337 at the event to present products for the medical device industry.
- MEDevice Boston is an important gathering for engineers, innovators, and professionals in the MedTech field.
- Vernay will introduce precision-engineered fluid control components specifically developed to meet the unique demands of medical devices.
- Andy Woodward serves as Vice President of Sales & Marketing at Vernay.
- Andy Woodward stated that Vernay excels in providing custom-engineered fluid control solutions offering precision and reliability tailored to the medical industry's stringent requirements.
- Shawna Krummel serves as Global Medical Business Unit Manager at Vernay.
- Shawna Krummel noted MEDevice Boston is a critical opportunity to connect with partners and customers in the MedTech industry.
- Shawna Krummel emphasized commitment to delivering fluid control solutions that meet current and future medical challenges.
- Vernay invites attendees to visit Booth 337 to learn about its engineering, manufacturing, and testing capabilities for high-performance medical industry solutions.
- Vernay Laboratories has been a technology leader in the design, development, and manufacture of customized flow-control components since its founding in 1946.
- The company formulates its own compounds to address each specific application.
- Vernay’s products play a vital role in millions of medical devices, automobiles, and consumer products.
- The privately held firm produces more than 1 billion components per year.
- Vernay draws from a library of more than 33,000 elastomeric materials and compound formulations.
- The company operates six plants around the world, located in the U.S., the Netherlands, Italy, and China.
