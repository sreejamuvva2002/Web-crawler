# Brian P. Kemp

**Type:** person  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2025-01-17

**Source:** [Governor Kemp to Reinforce Economic Partnerships in Germany and Poland | Governor Brian P. Kemp Office of the Governor](https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases/2025-01-17/governor-kemp-reinforce-economic-partnerships-germany-and-poland)  ·  `page_005248`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced an international mission to Germany and Poland on January 17, 2025, to strengthen economic ties and develop new relationships for Georgians.
- Over the last six years prior to the announcement, international companies have invested over $38 billion in Georgia.
- These investments created more than 58,000 new job opportunities for Georgians across every corner of the state.
- Communities outside of metro Atlanta attracted over 80% of those international investment jobs.
- Germany is one of Georgia's largest trade partners and a major contributor to job creation and investment in the state.
- The mission will include meetings with firms already operating or under construction in Georgia, as well as diplomatic and relationship-building opportunities.
- Notable German companies on the itinerary include BASF, Gerresheimer, Carcoustics, KIRCHHOFF Automotive, and GEDIA Automotive Group.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that German companies excel in industries like life sciences and automotive manufacturing, and Georgia serves as a strategic gateway for them and other European businesses entering the U.S. market.
- Over the past two decades, German automotive firms alone have contributed approximately 2,200 jobs and $830 million in investments to Georgia communities.
- The growth of life sciences companies like Gerresheimer demonstrates how international businesses become integral to Georgia's economy.
- Exploring emerging markets such as Poland allows Georgia to stay ahead of global trends, build new partnerships, and support local companies seeking to expand internationally.
- The State of Georgia has maintained continuous economic representation in Europe since 1973.
- Germany is the fourth largest export and import market for the State of Georgia.
- More than $12.8 billion in total trade moved between Georgia’s global gateways and Germany in 2023.
- German companies have invested more than $3.9 billion in Georgia since 2010 through projects with state involvement.
- An estimated 650 German-owned facilities support approximately 42,000 jobs for Georgians across the state.
- Georgia-based companies maintaining operations in Germany include Alimera Sciences, Bridgestone Golf, Delta Air Lines, Eversheds Sutherland, Graphic Packaging Holding, Novelis, Porex Corporation, and WestRock.
- BASF’s manufacturing presence in Georgia includes operations in Attapulgus, Edgar, and Savannah.
- Gerresheimer first established a production facility in Peachtree City in 1993 and expanded in 2009 with the establishment of a Technical Competence Center (TCC).
- Gerresheimer announced additional expansions in Georgia in December 2023 and May 2024.
- In 2017, Carcoustics established Carcoustics USA South Inc. in Buford, Georgia, including a central research center and a manufacturing presence.
- KIRCHHOFF Automotive opened an assembly facility in Lawrenceville in 2023.
- In 2020, GEDIA Automotive Group announced an advanced manufacturing facility that will begin production with a focus on parts for electric vehicles in the greater Dalton area.
