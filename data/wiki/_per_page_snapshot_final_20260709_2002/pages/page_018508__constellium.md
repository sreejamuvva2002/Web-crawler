# Constellium

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Constellium Coming to Bartow County | WBHF](https://wbhfradio.org/constellium-coming-to-bartow-county/)  ·  `page_018508`

## Facts

- Gov. Nathan Deal announced that Constellium will establish a facility in Bartow County.
- The project represents the creation of 150 new jobs.
- The project represents an overall investment of $32 million in the local community.
- Gov. Nathan Deal stated that Georgia has a long-standing reputation for attracting leading manufacturers in the automotive industry and has seen particularly strong growth in this sector over the last year from heavy-hitting suppliers.
- Constellium creates lightweight vehicle solutions aiming to reduce vehicle mass while advancing safety, comfort and performance.
- The company’s solution portfolio includes advanced crash management systems, other safety components such as side impact beams, front-end structures, lower path energy absorption systems and additional automotive structures.
- Eric Krepps, Vice President and General Manager of Constellium Automotive Structures North America, stated that Georgia has a strong business climate and an advanced technical education network to provide a skilled workforce for operations.
- Constellium operates more than 20 manufacturing sites in North America, Europe and Asia.
- The company’s world-class technology center located in Voreppe, France, is the origin of its advanced aluminum solutions.
- The project represents a $20 million investment by Constellium and an additional $12 million investment by developer Seefried Properties, for a total project value of $32 million.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) senior project manager E. Jane Caraway is Georgia’s representative for the project and has worked closely with the Bartow-Cartersville Joint Development Authority, City of Cartersville Electric System, Georgia Power Company and local leadership.
- Bartow County Commissioner Steve Taylor welcomed Constellium to Bartow County and noted that the automotive sector is strategically important there in the midst of so many southeast original equipment manufacturers.
- The facility will be located in Highland 75 Corporate/Industrial Park.
- GDEcD Commissioner Chris Carr stated that Georgia offers an abundant workforce, well-connected logistics infrastructure and an overall business-friendly environment for the project.
- Constellium (NYSE and Euronext: CSTM) is a global sector leader that develops innovative, value added aluminum products for aerospace, automotive and packaging markets.
