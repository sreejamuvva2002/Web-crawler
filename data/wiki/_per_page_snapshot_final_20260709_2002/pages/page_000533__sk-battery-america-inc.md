# SK Battery America Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-03-06

**Source:** [Nearly 1,000 workers laid off at Georgia battery plant as companies cancel EVs and lose incentives | Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/03/06/sk-battery-america-laid-off-nearly-1000-workers-georgia-electric-vehicles/)  ·  `page_000533`

## Facts

- SK Battery America Inc. laid off nearly 1,000 workers at its manufacturing plant northeast of Atlanta on Friday amid automakers’ changing electrification plans and uncertain consumer demand for EVs.
- Friday marked the last working day for exactly 958 plant employees.
- The workforce reduction was reported via a Worker Adjustment and Retraining Notification (WARN) notice filed by human resources chief Chuck Moore.
- Impacted workers will be paid through May 6.
- SK Battery America opened its $2.6 billion battery plant in Commerce, Georgia, in January 2022.
- The Commerce plant notably supplied batteries for the Ford F-150 Lightning electric pickup truck.
- Ford announced plans to cancel the fully electric version of the F-150 Lightning in December.
- SK Battery America and Ford had together previously invested $11.4 billion in joint battery plants in the U.S.
- SK Battery America ended its joint venture with Ford in December.
- SK Battery America has invested significantly in Jackson County, Georgia, in recent years.
- In June 2020, SK Battery America announced plans to pour $940 million to expand its battery manufacturing presence in Atlanta.
- At the time, Gov. Brian Kemp’s office said the Atlanta expansion would create 600 jobs.
- SK Battery America and Hyundai are still jointly building a $5 billion battery factory near Cartersville, northwest of Atlanta.
- The state of Georgia has attracted other massive EV manufacturing investments, including Rivian’s $5 billion factory and Hyundai’s own $7.6 billion factory complex.
- EVs accounted for about 8% of new vehicle sales in the U.S. in 2025, much the same as a year earlier.
- Automakers including Ford, General Motors, Stellantis and others across the EV supply chain have reneged on factory, investment and product plans, laid off workers, and pivoted efforts to hybrid and plug-in hybrid electric vehicles.
- Under President Donald Trump, Congress has eliminated tax credits of up to $7,500 for consumers’ purchases of new or used EVs.
- The administration has announced plans to weaken fuel economy and greenhouse gas emissions rules for automakers, essentially eliminating any federal incentive for auto companies to make their vehicle fleets cleaner.
