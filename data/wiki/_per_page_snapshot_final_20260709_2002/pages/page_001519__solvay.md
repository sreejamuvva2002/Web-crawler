# Solvay

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** research_and_development  ·  **Published:** 2022-06-05

**Source:** [​Solvay opens new innovation lab in Alpharetta with HRH Princess Astrid of Belgium | Solvay](https://www.solvay.com/en/press-release/solvay-opens-new-innovation-lab-alpharetta-hrh-princess-astrid-belgium)  ·  `page_001519`

## Facts

- Solvay officially opened its New Applications Development Lab and 3D printing technology line at its Research & Innovation Center in Alpharetta, Ga.
- The Alpharetta site is part of the global headquarters of Solvay’s Materials business, which provides solutions enabling the future of electric vehicles, the hydrogen economy, aerospace, and other advancements supporting a more sustainable society.
- Over the last four years, Solvay has invested more than $30 million in the Alpharetta site, including the new lab.
- The new lab serves as a workshop for Solvay and its customers to develop advanced prototyping, virtual engineering validation, special testing, and secondary operations activities.
- Customers can engage in real-time with Solvay’s versatile engineering teams at the facility to co-create innovative creative solutions more efficiently than before.
- The opening marked the first stop of a seven-day Belgian Economic Mission visiting and recognizing Belgian companies that are growing in the U.S.
- The mission was led by Her Royal Highness Princess Astrid of Belgium, Representative of His Majesty the King.
- Attendees included Belgian Ministers, high-ranking Belgian officials, Deputy Commissioner Abby Turano of the Georgia Department of Economic Development, Solvay leadership and employees, and other guests.
- The event included a ribbon cutting and tour of the new lab as well as the decoration of Mike Finelli, President, Group Growth Initiatives and Chief North America Officer at Solvay, for services rendered to Solvay and to Belgium in its development in the US.
- Solvay CEO Ilham Kadri stated the state-of-the-art facility offers the perfect space for scientists to partner with customers to develop tailor-made solutions to meet their unique needs.
- Minister in charge of Trade David Clarinval noted the lab exemplifies innovative solutions from a strong business partnership between Belgium and the United States, reinforcing Belgium’s links with Georgia and contributing to economic relations between the two countries.
- Clarinval added that the U.S. is the number one export destination outside the EU for Belgium and the largest foreign investor back home.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson congratulated Solvay on the lab opening, noting Georgia continues to lead the U.S. in supporting the transition to e-mobility and celebrating Belgian investments that continue to reinvest and grow in Georgia.
- Other Belgian Economic Mission attendees included His Excellency David Clarinval (Minister for the Self-Employed, SMEs and Agriculture, Institutional Reforms and Democratic Renewal, in charge of Foreign Trade); His Excellency Willy Borsus (Vice-President of the Walloon Government, Minister of Economy, Foreign Trade, Research and Innovation, New Technologies, Agriculture and Urban and Spatial Planning); and His Excellency Pascal Smet (State Secretary of the Brussels-Capital Region, responsible for Urbanism and Heritage, European and International Relations, Foreign Trade, Firefighting and Emergency Medical Assistance).
- In 2021, North America represented 26% of Solvay’s net sales.
- More than 5,400 Solvay employees work in 25 states at 38 industrial sites, 3 R&I Centers, and 3 corporate offices, including the Alpharetta site where the company employs more than 450 full-time workers and contractors.
- Solvay is a science company with more than 21,000 employees in 63 countries that creates sustainable shared value through its Solvay One Planet roadmap focused on protecting the climate, preserving resources, and fostering a better life.
- The Group’s innovative solutions contribute to safer, cleaner, and more sustainable products found in homes, food and consumer goods, planes, cars, batteries, smart devices, health care applications, water and air purification systems.
- Founded in 1863, Solvay ranks among the world’s top three companies for the vast majority of its activities and delivered net sales of €10.1 billion in 2021.
- Belgian Economic Missions are organized jointly by the Belgian Foreign Trade Agency, the regional institutions Wallonia Foreign Trade & Investment Agency, Brussels Invest & Export by hub.brussels and Flanders Investment & Trade and FPS Foreign Affairs.
