# Panasonic Energy Co., Ltd.

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Panasonic Energy Begins Mass Production at New Automotive Lithium-ion Battery Factory in Kansas, Aiming for Annual Capacity of 32 GWh to Accelerate U.S. Local Production | Panasonic North America - United States](https://na.panasonic.com/news/panasonic-energy-begins-mass-production-at-new-automotive-lithium-ion-battery-factory-in-kansas-aiming-for-annual-capacity-of-32-gwh-to-accelerate-us-local-production)  ·  `page_012467`

## Facts

- The Kansas Factory aims for an annual capacity of 32 GWh.
- Panasonic Energy has begun mass production at its new automotive lithium-ion battery factory in Kansas.
- The Kansas Factory is built on a site spanning approximately 300 acres, equivalent to the size of more than 225 American football fields.
- This facility represents Panasonic Energy’s second EV battery production site in North America, following the Nevada Factory.
- The Nevada Factory has been operating since 2017 with a current annual capacity of approximately 41 GWh.
- Once fully operational, the Kansas Factory will bring Panasonic Energy’s total U.S.-based production capacity to approximately 73 GWh.
- The new plant aims to meet growing demand from automotive customers expanding their electric vehicle production.
- Panasonic Energy focuses on producing high-performance, high-quality cylindrical lithium-ion batteries to meet North American EV range, capacity, and safety demands.
- Starting with EV battery production in Japan, Panasonic Energy has expanded its advanced battery production to North America as an industry leader.
- The company has approximately 30 years of experience in lithium-ion battery manufacturing.
- Panasonic Energy has around 8 years of production expertise at its Nevada Factory in Sparks, Nevada.
- The Nevada facility employs over 4,000 people and has delivered more than 11 billion cells to date.
- By introducing labor-saving production lines, the Kansas Factory is expected to achieve approximately 20 percent higher productivity than the Nevada Factory.
- In the near future, Panasonic Energy plans to introduce products using advanced materials that will increase cell capacity by around five percent.
- The facility strengthens the domestic EV supply chain, enhances competitiveness in North American battery manufacturing, and supports sustainability commitments.
- As the largest economic development project in Kansas state history, the factory will create up to 4,000 direct jobs.
- The project will create approximately 8,000 total jobs, including those in supplier and related industries.
- Panasonic Energy is collaborating with the University of Kansas to promote long-term industry-academia partnerships for technological advancement and specialized talent development.
- The collaboration aims to contribute to the economic growth of De Soto and the surrounding Kansas region, the revitalization of the U.S. manufacturing sector, skilled workforce cultivation, and long-term economic strength.
- Kazuo Tadanobu, CEO of Panasonic Energy, stated that the opening of the Kansas Factory marks a major milestone in scaling advanced battery production in the United States.
- Tadanobu noted the achievement required strong support from local partners and the State of Kansas.
- The company is using the facility to accelerate the shift to electrification, reinforce regional supply chains, and nurture the next generation of battery talent.
- Tadanobu described the facility as a foundation for long-term collaboration and innovation in the U.S.
- Panasonic Energy’s high-capacity lithium-ion cells feature an industry-leading volumetric energy density of 800 Wh/L.
- The company's cells have powered approximately 3.7 million EVs, with around 19 billion cells supplied as of March 2025.
- The batteries have set a standard for safety and reliability, having caused no vehicle recalls to date.
- Panasonic Energy remains committed to supporting CO₂ emissions reduction through the widespread adoption of electric vehicles.
- The Kansas Factory is operated by Panasonic Energy Corporation of North America.
- The facility is located at 10301 Astra Parkway, De Soto, KS 66018.
- The factory opening is scheduled for July 2025.
- The plant will produce cylindrical lithium-ion batteries for automotive use, specifically 2170 cells.
- The total site area is approximately 300 acres (about 1.2 million m²).
- The building area is approximately 4.7 million ft² (about 440,000 m²).
- The facility will employ up to approximately 4,000 personnel.
- Executive leadership includes Chief Executive Officer Yasuaki Takamoto and President and Chief Operating Officer Allan Swan.
- Panasonic Energy Co., Ltd. was established in April 2022 as part of the Panasonic Group's switch to an operating company system.
- The company provides innovative battery technology-based products globally, including automotive lithium-ion batteries, storage battery systems, and dry batteries.
