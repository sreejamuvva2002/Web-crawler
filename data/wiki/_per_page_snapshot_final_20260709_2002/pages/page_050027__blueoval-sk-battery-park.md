# BlueOval SK Battery Park

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-03-03

**Source:** [BlueOval SK Battery Park spurs massive growth in Hardin County – Here’s what it means.](https://eifky.org/blueoval-sk-battery-park-spurs-massive-growth-in-hardin-county-heres-what-it-means/)  ·  `page_050027`

## Facts

- BlueOval SK Battery Park is a $5.8 billion joint venture project between Ford Motor Co. and South Korea-based SK On.
- The 1,500-acre site is located just off Interstate 65 south of Elizabethtown in Hardin County, Kentucky, specifically in the rural village of Glendale.
- The land was originally purchased and gifted to the county from the state in 2001 to become an industrial manufacturing hub.
- Former Kentucky Governor Paul Patton previously hoped the site would become home to Hyundai, but that deal fell through.
- Former Kentucky Governor Steve Beshear also had hopes for the site that never materialized.
- The project was announced in 2021 after more than two decades of local preparation.
- Construction is on pace with operations expected to start next year relative to the article's March 3, 2023 publication date.
- U.S. Secretary of Transportation Pete Buttigieg visited the BlueOval SK Battery Park facility in Glendale, Kentucky on March 3, 2023.
- The facility is expected to create 5,000 jobs for Kentuckians and generate a direct payroll of $265 million in 2026.
- In October, Ford announced it would delay production at the second battery plant on the site because EV market demand was not rising as quickly as initially anticipated.
- Andy Games, president and COO of the Elizabethtown-Hardin County Industrial Foundation, stated that the production delay gave them time to take a deep breath.
- The facility is described as the 10th largest manufacturing facility in the world and will house twin battery plants.
- Production at the site is expected to gear up in 2025.
- Within 18 months of the battery park announcement, 90% of the remaining available industrial park land managed by the foundation was sold to Korean supplier companies.
- Hardin County anticipates a population increase of more than 22,000 residents by 2030, with the majority moving to Elizabethtown and pushing it to become the fifth largest city in Kentucky.
- A study indicates the county will need more than 8,800 new housing units to support the growing population.
- To support construction workers on the battery park project, including more than 2,800 traveling workers, the county built several RV parks for temporary housing.
- In the areas immediately surrounding Glendale, six residential duplexes and 64 new single-family dwellings were permitted in the year of the article.
- There are seven new subdivisions in development across the county, including the county's largest with 419 units.
- In a typical year, the county sees less than 200 new residential developments.
- The city of Elizabethtown receives requests for roughly 150 housing units annually on average.
- From 2022 through the present, the city has seen around 2,000 new housing units permitted.
- Local officials are working to protect Historic Downtown Glendale, which is listed on the National Register of Historic Places.
- The county and state have developed better transportation routes for the twin battery plants, including roundabouts, additional funding, and projects to reduce commuter traffic effects on local roads.
- BlueOval SK Battery Park opened its job training center on Wednesday, May 8, 2024.
- Joe Reverman, director of planning and development for the city of Elizabethtown, stated that locals knew something was going on at the site for over 20 years and that the city had pieces in place to handle growth.
- Andy Games noted that developers from Michigan to Florida to South Korea are calling about ground due to the large influx of people expected.
- The city has entered a new phase of development with more commercial developments, restaurants, and grocery store interest.
- Andy Games remarked that "The United States of America has figured out where [Hardin County] is."
