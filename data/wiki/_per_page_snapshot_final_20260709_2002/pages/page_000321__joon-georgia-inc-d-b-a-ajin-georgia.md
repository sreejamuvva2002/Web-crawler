# Joon Georgia, Inc., d/b/a Ajin Georgia

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2022-11-07

**Source:** [Bulloch YES (AGAIN) | First Hyundai Metaplant America Supplier Chooses Bulloch  - Grice Connect](https://www.griceconnect.com/business/bulloch-yes-again-first-hyundai-metaplant-america-supplier-chooses-bulloch-6505859)  ·  `page_000321`

## Facts

- Georgia Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Joon Georgia, Inc., d/b/a Ajin Georgia, an automotive parts manufacturer, will create 630 new jobs and invest $317 million in Bulloch County.
- Plans for the company’s new manufacturing facility mark the first confirmed supplier for Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America in Bryan County.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that less than two weeks after the groundbreaking of Hyundai Motor Group’s innovative facility, they are already seeing regional impact.
- Governor Brian Kemp noted Joon Georgia will be the first of many companies drawn to Georgia to support the Metaplant, creating jobs and opportunity for generations of Georgians.
- Joon Georgia is part of Ajin USA, a current supplier for multiple Hyundai Motor Group facilities.
- The company produces a variety of automotive body parts and electronics for original equipment manufacturers (OEMs).
- Jung Ho Sea, CEO of Ajin USA, expressed excitement about being one of the first Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America automotive suppliers to build an automotive parts manufacturing facility in Bulloch County and their first plant in Georgia.
- Jung Ho Sea stated Joon Georgia will create approximately 630 new jobs with competitive benefits in Bulloch County and the State of Georgia.
- Jung Ho Sea committed to being the best parts supplier for automotive and EV companies like Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America, and the best employer and corporate citizen giving back to Bulloch County and the City of Statesboro.
- Joon Georgia’s new facility will be located at Bruce Yawn Commerce Park, previously known as the Southern Gateway Commerce Park at I-16 and Hwy. 301.
- Operations are expected to begin in mid-2024.
- The company will begin hiring mid-2023.
- Billy Allen, Chair of the Development Authority of Bulloch County, welcomed Joon Georgia to the Statesboro-Bulloch County community and region.
- Billy Allen stated that their site in Bruce Yawn Commerce Park and proximity to Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America will serve the company well.
- Billy Allen noted that education resources, including Georgia Southern University and Ogeechee Technical College, will provide critical workforce support for Joon Georgia.
- Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy Alyce Thornhill represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The GDEcD partnered with the Development Authority of Bulloch County, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia EMC for this project.
- Workforce recruitment for the new facility will be supported by Quick Start, the top-ranked workforce training program in the nation.
- By partnering with the Technical College System of Georgia, Quick Start helps assess workers, train new employees on unique processes, and develop customized job-specific training using current techniques and media.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson called Joon Georgia’s announcement a landmark moment driving Georgia’s automotive industry into the future.
- Pat Wilson stated Hyundai Motor Group, its suppliers, and the Savannah area JDA are creating generational opportunities for communities in Georgia.
- Pat Wilson emphasized that supporting tomorrow's jobs is a team effort and regional collaboration ensures benefits are shared across county lines.
- Georgia is positioned as a hub for the electric mobility industry.
- Since 2020, more than 30 EV-related projects have contributed over $13.3 billion in investment and announced more than 18,100 jobs in Georgia.
- On October 25, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America officially broke ground in neighboring Bryan County on its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility.
- Non-affiliated suppliers connected to the Hyundai project are predicted to invest approximately $1 billion in other locations around the state.
- Ajin Georgia will acquire the remaining land left in the Bruce Yawn Industrial park.
- They will join Aspen Aerogels whose plant is already under construction.
- The two-story Aspen plant construction can be seen from I-16.
- These two new industries could bring over 1,000 new, high demand jobs in Bulloch.
- This is in addition to the 8,500 new jobs that will be created at the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America in Bryan County which is just five miles from the Bulloch County line.
- In the November 1, 2022 Bulloch County Commissioners meeting they approved rezoning two tracks of land totaling over 100 acres to light industrial.
- During the presentation, Stephen Rushing, Bulloch County Development Authority attorney, said that multiple Hyundai suppliers are showing high interest in the land.
- There could be two to three new industries located on these sites.
