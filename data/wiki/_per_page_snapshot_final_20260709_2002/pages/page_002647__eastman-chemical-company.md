# Eastman Chemical Company

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2018-12-31

**Source:** [Document](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/915389/000091538919000009/emn2018123110k.htm)  ·  `page_002647`

## Facts

- Eastman Chemical Company is a global advanced materials and specialty additives company headquartered in Kingsport, Tennessee.
- The company operates 48 manufacturing facilities and holds equity interests in three manufacturing joint ventures across 14 countries.
- Kingsport, Tennessee hosts the company's headquarters and largest manufacturing facility.
- In 2018, Eastman reported sales revenue of $10.2 billion, earnings before interest and taxes (EBIT) of $1.6 billion, and net earnings attributable to Eastman of $1.1 billion.
- Diluted earnings per share attributable to Eastman were $7.56 in 2018.
- Cash provided by operating activities was $1.5 billion in 2018.
- Excluding non-core and unusual items, adjusted EBIT was $1.6 billion and adjusted diluted earnings per share attributable to Eastman were $8.20.
- Approximately 60 percent of 2018 sales revenue was generated from outside the United States and Canada region.
- The company produces Impera™ high performance resins for tires, which enable tire manufacturers to improve safety and handling, balance performance and fuel economy needs, and achieve superior tack levels for tire construction.
- Eastman manufactures Tetrashield™ performance polyester resins based on proprietary monomer technology for automotive coatings, industrial, and food packaging markets.
- The company recently introduced a 72-inch version of automotive paint protection film marketed under established trademarks.
- Eastman completed an expansion of its manufacturing facility in Kuantan, Malaysia for Crystex™ insoluble sulfur rubber additives in the second quarter of 2018, with commercial sales beginning in the first quarter of 2019.
- A capacity expansion at the Kingsport, Tennessee manufacturing facility for specialty ketones became fully operational in the second quarter of 2018.
- The Advanced Materials segment produces polymers, films, and plastics with differentiated performance properties for value-added end-uses in transportation, consumables, building and construction, durable goods, and health and wellness markets.
- Key technology platforms utilized by the company include cellulose esters, copolyesters, polyvinyl butyral (PVB), polyester films, insoluble sulfur, hydrocarbon resins, alkylamine derivatives, and propylene derivatives.
