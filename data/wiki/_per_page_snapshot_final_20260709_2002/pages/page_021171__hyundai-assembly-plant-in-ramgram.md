# Hyundai Assembly Plant in Ramgram

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hyundai’s Industrial Revolution Nepal From Importing Local](https://ictframe.com/hyundais-industrial-revolution-nepal/)  ·  `page_021171`

## Facts

- The Hyundai Assembly Plant is located in Ramgram, Nawalparasi.
- In 2024, Laxmi Motor Corporation (LMC), a subsidiary of Laxmi Group and the authorized distributor for Hyundai, inaugurated the nation’s first sophisticated passenger car assembly plant.
- In just two years, the facility has rolled out nearly 2,000 vehicles.
- The plant currently focuses on producing two of Nepal’s most popular SUVs: the Hyundai Venue and the Hyundai Creta.
- Demand for the Creta has outpaced the Venue, with production numbers for the Creta nearly doubling.
- By reducing import duties on fully built units (CBU), the Hyundai Venue price in Nepal dropped by approximately 400,000 NPR during its initial local launch phase.
- The plant utilizes semi-robotic equipment and adheres to the global quality standards of Hyundai Motor Company (HMC), Korea.
- The plant is operated by a 100% Nepalese workforce.
- Engineers and technicians, previously limited to sales and service roles, are now experts in precision quality assurance and process engineering.
- By assembling vehicles locally, Nepal is retaining internal resources and reducing the trade deficit.
- The success of Hyundai paved the way for potential “backward integration” involving the future production of local components like batteries, tires, and seats.
- As of March 2026, the “Made in Nepal” variants offer competitive pricing compared to their imported counterparts.
- The Hyundai Venue E (1.2L Petrol) variant is priced from 40.96 Lakh NPR.
- The Hyundai Venue SX variant is priced from 53.56 Lakh NPR.
- The Hyundai Creta 1.5 E MT (Local) variant is priced from 55.56 Lakh NPR.
- The Hyundai Creta 1.5 SX(O) IVT (Local) variant is priced from 80.56 Lakh NPR.
- With the government’s commitment to industrialization and the private sector’s proven capability, Nepal is positioning itself as a budding hub for automobile assembly in South Asia.
- Hyundai continues to expand its local lineup with rumors of electric vehicle (EV) assembly on the horizon.
