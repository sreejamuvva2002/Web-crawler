## Facts

- Archer Aviation has closed its business combination with Atlas Crest Investment.
- Upon the completion of the transaction, Atlas Crest changed its name to Archer Aviation Incorporated and became a publicly traded company.
- As its common stock and warrants commenced trading, Archer celebrated with the ceremonial ringing of the bell to open the New York Stock Exchange.
- The business combination generated approximately $857.6 million of gross proceeds.
- The gross proceeds include $600 million of proceeds from the PIPE.
- The proceeds will help fund Archer's vision of bringing an eVTOL to market and launching an urban air mobility network.
- Brett Adcock, co-founder of Archer, stated that completing the go-public transaction and having Archer trade on the New York Stock Exchange is a huge milestone for the business.
- Michael Spellacy, CEO of Atlas Crest, stated that with the proceeds from the transaction, Archer is entering the public markets from a position of financial and competitive strength to accomplish its mission of revolutionizing the future of urban transportation.
- Brett Adcock concluded that Archer's eVTOL aircraft can be the future of how people will travel in and around cities.
- Morgan Stanley projects the urban air mobility market to be worth $1 trillion by 2040.
