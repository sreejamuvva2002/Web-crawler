# Georgia Quick Start

**Type:** workforce_training  ·  **Role:** workforce_training  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Transforming the Coastal Empire - Georgia Quick Start](https://www.georgiaquickstart.org/showcase/living-up-to-its-name/)  ·  `page_000112`

## Facts

- Located in Pooler, Georgia.
- Designs, develops, and delivers customized, company-specific workforce training for qualified companies investing and creating jobs in Georgia.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated: 'It’s impressive. I think this facility just continues to give us something that we can offer to really any kind of company that would want to come do advanced manufacturing in Georgia.'
- The Advanced Manufacturing Training Center was built in 2015 on a site just outside Savannah, Georgia, directly astride Interstate 16.
- Built to address the vexing issue facing companies today: finding the skilled workforce able to perform successfully in an increasingly complex manufacturing environment.
- In 2022, the largest economic development project in the state’s history was announced.
- The center is currently providing training for Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) and other companies locating to supply HMGMA’s operation with parts needed to assemble electric vehicles and new products yet to come as the company pioneers future mobility solutions.
- Dedicated to supporting any qualified new or expanding company, continuing to provide training for distribution operations, food manufacturers, and the wide range of industries that make up the robust powerhouse that is the Coastal Empire.
- Hyundai Transys is currently training at the Advanced Manufacturing Training Center, focusing on one of the important aspects of automobile choice: comfort.
- Building a state-of-the-art 90,000-sq.ft. training center to help prepare the Meta Pros of tomorrow, as well as Hyundai affiliates.
- Quick Start and McKnight Construction Company celebrated the 'topping off' of the steel frame following a tradition dating back to the 600s that is still honored today.
- The design of the Quick Start Advanced Manufacturing Training Center pays homage to the spirit of Historic Savannah while meeting the needs of a 21st century smart factory.
- Expanding to make way for new trainees and new technologies as Georgia becomes a leader in clean technology.
- Develops company-specific training by conducting project studies complete with detailed analysis of materials, technology, and corporate culture.
