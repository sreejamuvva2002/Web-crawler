# TCSG Electric Vehicle Listening Session

**Type:** event  ·  **Role:** workforce_training  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [WGTC Hosts TCSG, Industry Partners for Electric Vehicle Listening Session – Winters Media](https://www.wintersmedia.net/wgtc-hosts-tcsg-industry-partners-for-electric-vehicle-listening-session/)  ·  `page_001075`

## Facts

- The Technical College System of Georgia (TCSG) held an electric vehicle listening session at West Georgia Technical College’s (WGTC) Callaway Conference Center in LaGrange.
- Leaders from Georgia’s emerging electrification industry along with technical college administrators and faculty attended the session to discuss the future of EV manufacturing and skilled workforce demand.
- Dr. Ray Perren serves as Deputy Commissioner for Technical Education at TCSG.
- Perren stated this was the third of four regional listening sessions across Georgia.
- Perren emphasized TCSG’s commitment to building a well-educated, globally competitive workforce through technical education.
- Perren said they are there to listen to business partners regarding the future of e-vehicles and ensure they provide the workforce EV partners need so Georgia can continue to be the best place to do business.
- Industry representatives from Kia Georgia, FREYR Battery, Hyundai-Transys, and Georgia Power spoke at the event.
- Latesha Bailey serves as Senior Manager of Kia Georgia.
- Bailey discussed labor market challenges in Georgia for attracting and retaining talent for the mobility industry.
- Bailey stated upskilling the current workforce is front and center of Kia's operation.
- Bailey noted the mobility industry faces additional challenges converting or diversifying existing skill sets to meet EV production needs.
- Bailey emphasized that today’s vehicle technology advances quickly, making upskilling the current workforce important for EV technology success.
- Ensuring existing and new workers are continually educated on fast-paced, changing technology is required.
- Kia discussed developing a certification course in EV structure components, manufacturing processes, high voltage safety, charge and repair, problem-solving, and troubleshooting.
- Creating a badge and database for employers of potential candidates would assist in building an EV workforce pipeline.
- Kia Georgia is preparing to launch its plant’s first electric vehicle.
- Bailey emphasized the importance of TCSG and WGTC on the front lines of building Georgia’s future workforce of technicians and skilled professionals for the auto industry's era of EVs.
- Jason Peace serves as Senior Vice President of Business Development and Supply Chain for FREYER Battery.
- Peace explained that FREYER focuses more on grid electrification and broader global electrification than specifically on EVs.
- Peace stated that when discussing electrifying the U.S., they are directly competing with China.
- Peace noted China has 95% of the battery production in the world and 95% production of the raw materials required for batteries.
- Peace said China got a 10- to 15-year head start, and catching up requires talent from the high school system to support future plants.
- FREYR Battery is a developer of clean, next-generation battery cell production capacity.
- FREYR recently announced the selection and purchase of a site in Coweta County for its planned Giga America battery plant.
- Peace challenged TCSG administrators and faculty to help educate high school students on future possibilities beyond traditional trades like welding or CDL.
- Peace stated painting a picture of where the world is headed is impactful, as being part of future industry opportunities is a rare chance that requires getting in front of high schoolers to share the story and passion.
