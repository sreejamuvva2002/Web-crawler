# DAS Corp.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-09-06

**Source:** [DAS Corp. To Bring 300 Automotive Jobs To Metter, Ga. | Textile World](https://www.textileworld.com/textile-world/2023/09/das-corp-to-bring-300-automotive-jobs-to-metter-ga/)  ·  `page_019095`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced on September 6, 2023 that DAS Corp. (DAS) will establish a new manufacturing facility in Metter.
- The new facility will create 300 new jobs and generate more than $35 million in investment in Candler County.
- The initiative will involve collaborative indirect investments.
- DAS was established in Korea in 1987 and specializes in designing and manufacturing seating systems, safety seating components, and seating structures.
- DAS is a key supplier for Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia (KMMG) and Hyundai Motor Group.
- The strategic location near prominent cities like Savannah and Macon facilitated the decision-making process.
- Metter was deemed an ideal location in anticipation of its role as a global electric vehicle production hub due to its close proximity to Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- The new DAS manufacturing facility will be located in the Candler County Industrial Park in Metter.
- The facility will primarily produce automotive seat structures.
- Operations are projected to begin in the latter half of 2024.
- Over the course of the next five years, DAS will recruit for assembly technicians.
- DAS intends to establish collaborations with local employment services as part of its hiring process.
- Metter Mayor Ed Boyd stated the impact on the community will be transformative, stimulating investment in housing, creating other business opportunities, and improving socioeconomic wellbeing.
- Glyn Thrift, Chairman of the Candler County Board of Commissioners, thanked DAS for choosing the area to spur more economic development.
- Griff Lynch, President and CEO of the Georgia Ports Authority, stated the authority is proud to support the growing automotive manufacturing presence in the state.
- Alyce Thornhill, Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy, represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The project was executed in partnership with the Candler County Industrial Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, Electric Cities of Georgia, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated Georgia is well-positioned for the arrival of Hyundai’s supplier network due to years of consistent investment in site preparation, workforce solutions, and connectivity.
- Since 2018, Georgia has pursued the entire EV supply chain, creating more than $25 billion in investments and 30,000 jobs.
- In fiscal year 2023, job creation in the automotive industry increased by 324 percent compared to FY21.
- Suppliers attracted by Georgia’s mobility original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) resulted in over $2 billion in investment across the state in FY23.
