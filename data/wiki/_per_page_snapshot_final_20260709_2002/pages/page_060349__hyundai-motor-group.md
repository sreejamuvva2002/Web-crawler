# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-07-06

**Source:** [The numbers behind Hyundai's metaplant in Bryan County  - Grice Connect](https://www.griceconnect.com/local-news/the-numbers-behind-hyundais-metaplant-in-bryan-county-7230747)  ·  `page_060349`

## Facts

- The Hyundai metaplant is under construction in Bryan County.
- Official announcements for the project include 8,100 jobs, at least $5.5 billion in construction spending, and suppliers planning to hire thousands more employees.
- Hyundai Motor Group states its arrival will create 8,100 new jobs at its site located at Interstate 16 and Georgia 280 in Bryan County when the electric vehicle and battery plant is fully operational.
- The metaplant's workforce size will place it among the largest private employers in the region.
- According to Georgia Department of Labor 2022 data, only 11 private companies employ more than 1,000 people across Chatham, Bryan, and Effingham counties.
- Gulfstream is the largest private employer in the area with roughly 11,500 workers around Savannah and is currently hiring.
- The Savannah-Chatham Public School System employs approximately 5,500 staff members.
- Approximately 3,500 civilians work at Fort Stewart and 640 civilians are employed at Hunter Army Air Field.
- Roughly 27,000 people work in the hospitality industry across Chatham, Bryan, and Effingham counties, spread among approximately 1,600 small businesses.
- Hyundai and the adjacent battery plant will pay an average of at least $58,105 per job, plus benefits.
- This wage level is required to qualify for state and local grants, tax breaks, and land valued at approximately $228,000 per job.
- Hyundai suppliers can negotiate separate pay, investment, and subsidy agreements.
- Ecoplastic's plant near Register must create 456 jobs over eight years when fully operational producing automotive plastic parts.
- Ecoplastic workers must be paid at least the county's average wage of $21.65 per hour as established in a February 2023 agreement with Bulloch County.
- A 40-hour workweek at that rate equals approximately $43,300 annually.
- In exchange for building in the county, Bulloch County provided Ecoplastic with a $2.3 million site purchase discount and a 15-year county property tax break.
- The property tax discount excludes school and fire district taxes.
- Ecoplastic may also receive a $1.6 million state grant.
- Joon Georgia has a similar agreement with Bulloch County and the state, matching the county average wage for 630 promised jobs at its new facility.
- Joon Georgia received a $3.1 million land discount near I-16, a $2.7 million state grant, and a 10-year county property tax break.
- The property tax abatement for Joon Georgia does not cover school and fire district rates.
- The Hyundai Metaplant site spans more than a mile of Black Creek, approximately three miles upstream from where it joins the Ogeechee River.
- Hyundai and its suppliers are locating near interstates and Ogeechee watershed waterways.
- A new wastewater treatment plant for the site and surrounding areas will discharge into the Ogeechee River.
- The wastewater treatment plant located across the interstate from Hyundai will process the facility's wastewater and north Bryan County's sewage before discharging it into the Ogeechee River.
- Environmentalists at Ogeechee Riverkeeper are tracking water quality in Black Creek and the Ogeechee River due to distrust of state pollution monitoring.
- Eight Hyundai suppliers will be located across Bryan, Bulloch, Chatham, Effingham, and Liberty counties.
- None of the supplier factories are currently served by public transportation lines.
- Hyundai production at the metaplant is scheduled to begin in the first half of 2025.
- The metaplant site is located on the south side of I-16, approximately 22 miles northwest of Savannah.
- Site clearance for the project is already visible via satellite imagery.
- Satellite imagery shows industrial site development in the area has been ongoing since 2005.
- Daniel Defense and ORAFOL facilities are located directly across the interstate from the metaplant site.
- State and local governments view the job influx as highly significant, comparing it to winning a College Football National Championship.
- The team that attracted Hyundai included state and county leaders plus the newly formed Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority.
- Georgia competed against other regional states, anticipating manufacturers would demand billions in subsidies.
- Georgia's Department of Economic Development highlighted its "Best State for Business" ranking in its offer letter to Hyundai, citing outstanding logistics, a well-trained workforce, low business costs, a pro-business climate, and high quality of life.
- Worker quality of life is not included in the "Best State for Business" rankings.
- Coastal Georgia laborers will quickly benefit from tens of millions of dollars in state-funded job training tailored to the new employers' needs.
