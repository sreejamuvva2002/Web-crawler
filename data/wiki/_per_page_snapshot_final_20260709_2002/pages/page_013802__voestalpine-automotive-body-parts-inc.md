# voestalpine Automotive Body Parts Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2016-08-16

**Source:** [Deal: Leading automotive supplier to create 150 jobs in Bartow County | WBHF](https://wbhfradio.org/deal-leading-automotive-supplier-to-create-150-jobs-in-bartow-county/)  ·  `page_013802`

## Facts

- Gov. Nathan Deal announced on August 16, 2016 that voestalpine Automotive Body Parts Inc. will expand its existing operation in Bartow County.
- The expansion project will create 150 new jobs.
- The company is investing $50 million in the local community as part of the expansion.
- voestalpine Automotive Body Parts Inc. serves as the supply base for the voestalpine Group, producing ultra-high strength body-in-white components for premium car manufacturers.
- Gov. Nathan Deal stated that the decision strengthens Georgia’s growing automotive industry and highlights the state’s extensive manufacturing capabilities.
- The newly created jobs will range from advanced manufacturing to hot-forming and assembly positions.
- The company currently employs approximately 100 Georgians at its facility in Bartow County.
- The facility offers extensive training opportunities, including an apprenticeship program with students of the Bartow County College & Career Academy in Cartersville.
- Philipp Schulz, managing director of voestalpine Automotive Body Parts Inc., stated the investment is an important step in the company’s U.S. growth strategy and positions it well in the center of the southern automotive hub.
- Philipp Schulz thanked state and local officials as well as the Georgia Department of Economic Development for their assistance in the project.
- voestalpine Automotive Body Parts Inc. is a subsidiary company of the Metal Forming Division of the German-based voestalpine Group.
- The Metal Forming Division produces highly refined sections, tubes and precision strip steel products, as well as ready-to-install components made of pressed, stamped and roll formed parts.
- Bartow County Commissioner Steve Taylor noted that voestalpine was the first company to build in Highland 75 and highlighted its multiple additional phases and continued investment.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) Senior Project Manager Wylly Harrison and Regional Project Manager Stephanie Scearce represented the Global Commerce Division in partnership with the Cartersville-Bartow County Department of Economic Development and Georgia Power.
- GDEcD Commissioner Chris Carr stated that global manufacturing companies choosing Georgia testifies to the state’s top-ranked business climate and international reputation.
- The voestalpine Group operates globally with around 500 Group companies and locations in more than 50 countries on all five continents.
- The voestalpine Group has been listed on the Vienna Stock Exchange since 1995.
- The voestalpine Group is a leading partner to the automotive and consumer goods industries in Europe and to the oil and gas industries worldwide.
- The voestalpine Group is the world market leader in turnout technology, special rails, tool steel, and special sections.
