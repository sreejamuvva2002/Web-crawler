# TI Fluid Systems plc

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- TI Fluid Systems plc is a global industry leader in highly engineered automotive fluid storage, carrying and delivery systems and thermal management products and systems.
- The Group announced its full year results for the period ended 31 December 2024 on 11 March 2025.
- Full year 2024 revenue was €3,360.3 million, a 4.4% decline year-on-year or 3.5% at constant currency.
- Adjusted EBIT for 2024 was €260.8 million, representing a 40 basis points margin expansion to 7.8%.
- Adjusted Free Cash Flow was €113.0 million, with a conversion rate of 29% of Adjusted EBITDA.
- Total bookings for 2024 remained strong at €2.7 billion, including €0.5 billion in BEV awards and €0.6 billion in HEV awards.
- Bookings demonstrated a clear shift to ICE vehicles, with ICE bookings representing over half of total awards in the period.
- The company estimates that BEVs require 4-5 times as many connectors and 2-4 times as many coolant lines versus a comparable ICE vehicle.
- TI Fluid Systems opened its fifth and final e-Mobility Innovation Centre (eMIC) in the US in the middle of 2024.
- The company completed the development of its electric coolant pump (eCP) and installed its first manufacturing line in China ahead of industrialisation and launch in 2025.
- TI Fluid Systems finalized and launched new product lines to underpin its EV strategy, including the SPT 2.0 fuel tank for plug-in hybrids.
- The Group closed three facilities and downsized others during 2024 as part of footprint optimisation.
- Restructuring costs increased to €37.4 million in 2024, largely related to headcount and footprint optimisation in response to softer industry volumes.
- An exceptional charge was recorded primarily due to the closure of the Group's manufacturing facility in Ligonier and the associated exit of the powertrain business at that location.
- The pending acquisition by ABC Technologies is expected to complete during H1 2025, subject to regulatory and court approvals.
- Hans Dieltjens serves as Chief Executive Officer and President of TI Fluid Systems plc.
- Julien Plenchette joined TI in April 2024 as President for the Americas region.
- Renee McLeod was promoted to Chief Human Resources Officer in June 2024, and Samantha Bellis was promoted to Vice President, Operational Excellence in September 2024.
- The company returned a total of €69.2 million to shareholders in 2024 via dividends and share buybacks, its largest single-year return since its 2017 IPO.
- TI Fluid Systems aims for a 2026 revenue target of €3.8-4.2 billion and a >€4.5 billion target by 2030.
- The company targets double-digit Adjusted EBIT margins in the mid-term and an Adjusted Free Cash Flow Conversion of 30%.
- TI Fluid Systems achieved a 28% reduction in Scope 1 & 2 carbon emissions compared to its 2021 baseline, with a target of 50% reduction by 2030.
- Demand for thermal products for EVs increased in the second half of 2024 as customers ramped up BEV production ahead of 2025 European CO2 emissions standards.
- In China, TI Fluid Systems secured eight new awards with the largest Chinese OEM (LOEM) for brake lines and tanks for BEV and PHEV platforms.
- The company launched circa 66 programmes during 2024, almost two thirds of which were with Local OEMs in China.
- Americas revenue declined 8.1% at constant currency in 2024, impacted by destocking from the region's largest OEM customer and a circa 350 basis points headwind from exiting an unprofitable product line.
- The Group maintains a propulsion agnostic portfolio to capture opportunities across ICE, PHEV, and BEV transitions.
