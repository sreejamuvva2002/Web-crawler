# Industrial Technology Innovation Advisory Committee

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [Meet the Industrial Technology Innovation Advisory Committee | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/cmei/ito/meet-industrial-technology-innovation-advisory-committee)  ·  `page_005534`

## Facts

- The Industrial Technology Innovation Advisory Committee (ITIAC) is a U.S. Department of Energy advisory body comprising experts in industrial decarbonization, clean energy technology, and manufacturing policy.
- Sharon Nolen serves as an Eastman fellow and program manager for Global Natural Resource Management at Eastman Chemical Company in Tennessee, with over 30 years of tenure overseeing efficiency and sustainability improvements.
- Sharon Nolen has earned the ENERGY STAR “Sustained Excellence” award, the ACC Exceptional Merit Energy Efficiency Award, and the DOE Better Projects Award for her work in energy efficiency and sustainability in the chemicals industry.
- Jeffrey Rissman serves as senior director of the industry program at Energy Innovation, where he founded and leads the organization’s industrial decarbonization program to research and brief policymakers on technologies and policies to cut industrial pollutant emissions.
- Jeffrey Rissman is the author of "Zero-Carbon Industry: Transformative Technologies and Policies to Achieve Sustainable Prosperity" (2024) and creator of the Energy Policy Simulator, an open-source model forecasting environmental, financial, and public health impacts of clean technology choices.
- Sunday Abraham serves as director of research and development for SSAB Americas, where he shapes sustainability efforts through the R&D of high strength steels and brings over 20 years of steel industry experience.
- Sunday Abraham has developed high performance high strengths steels that enhance performance in end use applications through increased payload, improved fuel efficiency, and extended life cycle performance.
- Cathy Choi serves as executive advisor at Knoxville Locomotive Works and on the Board of the Advanced Machine and Vehicle Innovation Center as a board member and managing partner.
- Cathy Choi previously served as chief sustainability officer at ClearFlame Engine Technologies and executive director at Cummins, where she oversaw technology strategy for energy and power solutions spanning 160 kilowatts to 16.8 megawatts.
- Sue Clark serves as deputy laboratory director for the Savannah River National Laboratory (SRNL) in South Carolina, stewarding a $400 million research enterprise overlapping ITIAC priorities like energy efficiency and smart manufacturing.
- Sue Clark is leading SRNL’s efforts to develop next-generation decarbonized manufacturing technology through the creation of the Advanced Manufacturing Collaboratory (AMC).
- Subodh Das serves as chief executive officer of Phinix, LLC in Clayton, Missouri, and brings over 45 years of experience in sustainability, energy efficiency, carbon neutrality, recycling, and waste utilization focused on aluminum.
- Elizabeth Dutrow has over 30 years of federal experience focusing on reducing greenhouse gas emissions through energy efficiency, innovation, benchmarking, and adoption of new technologies in industrial manufacturing.
- Elizabeth Dutrow serves as team leader for the ENERGY STAR Industrial Team, interfacing with more than 800 corporations across 32+ sectors to implement energy efficiency and decarbonization projects.
- Neal Elliott is director emeritus of the American Council for an Energy-Efficient Economy (ACEEE), where he founded the industrial program in 1993 and has researched industrial energy and decarbonization technologies and policies for three decades.
- Anna Fendley serves as director of regulatory and state policy at the United Steelworkers Union (USW), coordinating union policy positions on climate change, energy, and the environment intersecting with workers’ rights and manufacturing.
- Comas Haynes has been a faculty member at Georgia Tech for more than two decades and is a former joint faculty appointee at the Oak Ridge National Laboratory.
- Comas Haynes currently serves as branch head of the Georgia Tech Research Institute and is head of Georgia Tech’s Hydrogen Initiative, which coordinates activities related to hydrogen production, storage/transport, utilization, and equitable incorporation.
- Joe Powell is a principal advisor at ChemePD in Richmond, Texas, with over 30 years of prior tenure as chief scientist for Shell’s chemical engineering department, focusing on clean energy, hydrogen, and ammonia process development.
- Abigail Regitsky manages the U.S. Policy and Advocacy team for Breakthrough Energy, implementing climate policy priorities focused on industrial decarbonization, embodied carbon in buildings, clean electricity standards, and climate/trade.
- Akshay Sahni is general manager of strategy and technology for Chevron Technology Ventures in Houston, Texas, leading enterprise technology strategies to lower carbon emissions through carbon capture, renewable fuels, geothermal, offshore wind, energy storage systems, and the circular economy.
- Sridhar Seetharaman serves as Fulton Professor of Industrial Decarbonization at Arizona State University and principal investigator/chief executive officer of the Electrified Processes for Industrial Excellence (EPIXC) institute, DOE’s 7th Clean Energy Manufacturing Innovation Institute.
- Jolene Sheil manages the Focus on Energy Program, Wisconsin’s statewide renewable energy program, with nearly 30 years of experience planning, managing, and implementing renewable energy efforts across business, city planning, and environmental sustainability.
- Sasha Stashwick serves as director of federal affairs at Antora Energy, leading efforts to advance federal policy support for thermal storage as a key technological solution to decarbonize industrial heat and power with renewable electricity.
