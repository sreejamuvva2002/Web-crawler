# EcoPlastic America Corp

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-02-21

**Source:** [Commissioners Approve 100% Tax Abatement for New Manufacturer in Bulloch County • The Georgia Virtue](https://www.thegeorgiavirtue.com/bulloch-local-government/commissioners-approve-100-tax-abatement-for-new-manufacturer-in-bulloch-county/)  ·  `page_019887`

## Facts

- Bulloch County Commissioners voted to finalize a 15-year tax abatement for a manufacturer coming to Bulloch County as part of the Hyundai Meta Plant.
- EcoPlastic America Corp, a Korean based company, announced its intention to come to Bulloch County in January of this year.
- The new facility will offer 456 jobs to the community.
- The facility is located at 4822 Highway 301 South.
- The project is reportedly a $205 million investment.
- EcoPlastic America Corp is a supplier to Hyundai and Kia.
- According to Area Development, the company currently employs more than 770 employees and recorded sales revenue of more than $700 million in 2021.
- Commissioners approved a tax abatement structure of 100% for ten years followed by a 50% abatement for five years after that.
- The Bulloch County Development Authority agreed to provide a $2.28 million discount on the $4.312 million property.
- BCDA is slated to apply for a $1.6 million grant from the ONEGeorgia Edge Grant program for the project.
- The project includes $30,000 allocated for training future EcoPlastic America Corp employees, which must take place at Ogeechee Technical College.
- BCDA will provide a temporary office for EcoPlastic America Corp for a cost up to $15,000 if requested by the company.
- Prior to the company taking on the property, the Development Authority conducted a $56,565 wetlands delineation study.
- The company is not expected to reach its full 456 jobs until years 8-15 in Bulloch County.
- Employment for years one through seven will scale gradually from 80 employees to 435.
- The Development Authority entered into an MOU with EcoPlastic America Corp for the location of a manufacturing project in Bulloch County.
- The facility will manufacture automotive exterior and interior parts including bumpers, garnishes, consoles and trims for electric vehicles.
- The MOU parties are the Development Authority and EcoPlastic America Corp, with requests made to the Board of Commissioners and Board of Tax Assessors to acknowledge the MOU and agree to applicable provisions.
- The MOU includes a 100% abatement of County ad valorem taxes for 10 years and a 50% abatement for an additional 5 years as an inducement for location.
- The tax abatement excludes school taxes and taxes for the County’s fire district.
- County attorney Jeff Akins stated that the MOU is similar to incentives offered to other manufacturers recently.
- Bulloch County Development Authority Chair Billy Allen welcomed EcoPlastic, noting confidence that their location, workforce, and quality of life will help them succeed.
- Commissioners approved the abatement unanimously with no discussion after staff recommended approval.
