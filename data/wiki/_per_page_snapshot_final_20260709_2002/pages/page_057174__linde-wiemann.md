# Linde + Wiemann

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- Linde + Wiemann is expanding its manufacturing facility in Lavonia, Georgia.
- Wet soil remediation and stabilization had to be completed in expansion areas before the project began.
- Roads and parking were added earlier as part of site preparation.
- Soil conditions were exacerbated by the time of year and weather.
- Choate utilized soil cement to keep the project on schedule given the soil challenges.
- The team leveraged existing experience and trade partner expertise to develop a plan for using soil cement.
- The plant remained fully operational during the expansion and production realignment.
- Choate created a complex series of digital equipment scanning and modeling of both existing and new production components to maintain operations.
- This digital strategy allowed the team to reevaluate production flow prior to construction and plan for future manufacturing layout efficiencies without impacting operations.
- A proprietary piece of stamping equipment, specially made in Europe, was the impetus for the expansion.
- The machine’s shipping, arrival, and installation were crucial to the project’s successful completion.
- Installation required several equipment pits to be dug, some as deep as 10 feet.
- Design modifications made while the machine was crossing the Atlantic required additional pit construction.
- The equipment arrived in Lavonia in seven shipping containers, with one weighing 38,000 pounds.
- The entire facility space was dried-in prior to the stamping machine's arrival due to its centrality to the project.
- An interior overhead crane delivery post dry-in required an unorthodox installation method.
- Working with a crane supplier, small panels were removed in the roof to allow outside mobile cranes to access and lift the massive gantry system onto interior crane rails.
- This safe crane installation method kept the project on schedule and allowed for future maintenance access through roof curbs.
- Choate’s VDC team laser scanned, measured, and generated as-builts for existing elements to be moved to minimize downtime and ensure accuracy in equipment reinstallation.
- The team guaranteed the accuracy, squareness, and straightness of crane rail beams within a 1/8-inch tolerance across the entire 320 feet of travel.
- The team relied on laser scanning, measuring, and as-builting to ensure precision for the beams and rails.
