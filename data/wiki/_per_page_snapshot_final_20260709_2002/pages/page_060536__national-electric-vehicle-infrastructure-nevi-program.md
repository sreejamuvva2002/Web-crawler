# National Electric Vehicle Infrastructure (NEVI) program

## Facts

- The National Electric Vehicle Infrastructure (NEVI) program is set to accelerate its rollout of EV chargers across the United States in 2025.
- The NEVI program was established under the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law.
- Its goal is to create a national network of fast chargers for electric vehicles across the country.
- The program aims to facilitate the widespread adoption of EVs by alleviating range anxiety among potential users.
- By 2025, the program is expected to have significantly increased the number of charging stations available to the public.
- The NEVI program's updated strategy will focus on improving installation timelines and enhancing funding accessibility for local governments and private firms.
- This initiative follows a comprehensive review of the program's initial rollout phases, which faced delays due to regulatory hurdles and funding allocations.
- As part of these changes, NEVI plans to invest heavily in upgrading existing infrastructure to support a wider array of vehicle types.
- Many projects face delays due to complex local permitting processes.
- Despite federal support, not all regions have the financial resources to match funding requirements, which may lead to uneven charger distribution.
- Ongoing global supply chain issues continue to impact the availability of necessary components for EV charger installations.
- A well-distributed charging network will bolster consumer confidence, thus driving EV sales upwards.
- According to a recent report from Statista, increased charger availability is projected to boost EV market share to over 20% by 2025, compared to just 4% in 2020.
- Achieving these ambitious goals requires navigating regulatory and funding challenges that could slow deployment.
- Ongoing collaboration between federal, state, and local entities, alongside private investment, will be essential to ensuring the successful implementation of the NEVI program.
