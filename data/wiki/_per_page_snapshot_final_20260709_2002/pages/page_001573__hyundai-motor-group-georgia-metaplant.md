# Hyundai Motor Group Georgia Metaplant

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** workforce_training  ·  **Published:** 2026-05

**Source:** [Hyundai’s Georgia Metaplant uses Quick Start to train workers](https://www.ajc.com/news/2026/05/can-man-and-dog-robot-work-together-at-hyundai-plant-they-already-do/)  ·  `page_001573`

## Facts

- The Hyundai electric vehicle factory near Savannah is known as the Metaplant and is a 20-month-old manufacturing facility.
- Acclimation time with Spot, a doglike robot, is part of the onboarding process for trainees at the Metaplant.
- Susan Williams heads the factory’s training program through Quick Start, a state-funded workforce development organization.
- Spot would walk up beside new hires and startle them, which is why acclimation time is necessary to prevent screaming on the factory floor.
- The Hyundai Metaplant is currently seeking full-time and part-time factory employees.
- Open roles include automation, robotics, maintenance, logistics, production and quality positions.
- Susan Williams confirmed that the plant will be hiring for all of these listed positions.
- Quick Start is described as Georgia’s “secret weapon” for economic development.
- The training program follows a “train like you work” philosophy.
