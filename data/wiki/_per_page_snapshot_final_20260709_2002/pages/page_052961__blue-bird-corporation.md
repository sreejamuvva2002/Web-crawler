# Blue Bird Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [First Blue Bird redesigned All Americans delivered in Georgia | School Bus Fleet](https://www.schoolbusfleet.com/news/first-blue-bird-redesigned-all-americans-delivered-in-georgia)  ·  `page_052961`

## Facts

- Ten of Blue Bird Corp.'s 2014 All American buses were delivered on Wednesday to the Georgia Department of Corrections.
- The delivered buses will be placed into service immediately, replacing existing fleet buses that have each accumulated over 300,000 miles.
- The redesigned buses can seat 50 passengers, increasing seating capacity by four compared to previous models.
- The buses feature pass-through luggage compartments and interior storage bins to fully maximize storage.
- The buses include a custom onboard lavatory.
- The delivery event took place in Forsyth, Ga., with officials from Blue Bird Corp. and dealer Yancey Bus Sales and Service.
- The Georgia Department of Corrections’ buses are the first of Blue Bird’s redesigned 2014 All American buses to roll off the production line in Fort Valley, Ga.
- The buses feature Blue Bird’s exclusive purpose-built chassis.
- The buses feature an ergonomic driver’s cockpit for increased driver comfort.
- The buses feature a larger windshield that provides improved driver sightlines.
- The buses feature an excellent turning radius.
- The buses feature a larger entrance door.
- The buses feature enhanced serviceability.
- The buses feature excellent fuel economy.
- The buses feature greater parts commonality with other Blue Bird buses.
- Phil Horlock, president and CEO of Blue Bird Corp., stated that as a Georgia-based manufacturer for over 86 years and the second largest manufacturer in the automotive category in Georgia, Blue Bird is pleased to provide buses crafted by and for Georgians.
- Blue Bird’s manufacturing facilities are all based in Fort Valley, Ga.
- Blue Bird employs 1,500 Georgians from over 20 Georgia counties.
- Over 20% of the materials used to build Blue Bird buses are sourced from Georgia-based companies.
- A 2009 economic multiplier study found that for every $1 Georgia school districts and other customers spend on Blue Bird buses, that dollar is multiplied four times within the state.
- Every Blue Bird bus sold in Georgia provides a positive impact to the state gross domestic product by approximately $86,000.
- Commissioner Brian Owens of the Georgia Department of Corrections stated that their partnership with Blue Bird Corp. is a new beginning and expressed pleasure in owning buses designed and built by a Georgia-based company.
- Eric Arnold, vice president of power systems for Yancey Brothers Co. (parent company of Yancey Bus Sales and Service), noted the dealership is honored to provide buses to the Georgia Department of Corrections and looks forward to future opportunities for safe transportation throughout Georgia.
- Officials in attendance at the delivery event included state of Georgia Chief Operating Officer Bart Gobeil, Chris Cummiskey (commissioner of the Georgia Department of Economic Development), local elected officials, and representatives from the Monroe County Chamber of Commerce.
