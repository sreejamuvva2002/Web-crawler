# Ascend Elements

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2023-09-13

**Source:** [Ascend Elements develops cathode material for Freudenberg - electrive.com](https://www.electrive.com/2023/09/13/ascend-elements-develops-cathode-material-for-freudenberg/)  ·  `page_052601`

## Facts

- The US battery-materials company Ascend Elements has entered into a development partnership with Freudenberg e-Power Systems.
- Recycled battery materials could be used in heavy commercial vehicles and ships in the future as part of the agreement.
- Ascend Elements will develop an NMC cathode material that meets Freudenberg’s exact specifications.
- Freudenberg manufactures cells, packs and system solutions for heavy-duty vehicles and ships at the battery plant of its subsidiary Xalt Energy in Midland in the US state of Michigan.
- Max Kley, CEO of Freudenberg e-Power Systems, stated that integrating Ascend Elements’ recycled cathode material into their high-performance cell design reduces environmental impact while ensuring the highest product quality and exceptional durability.
- Max Kley also noted that Ascend Elements’ domestic manufacturing contributes to the stability of their supply chain.
- Freudenberg e-Power Systems tested the recycled materials in advance and found them to be good.
- Mike O’Kronley, CEO of Ascend Elements, stated that the contract is evidence that recycled battery metals can perform as well as virgin metals, noting that metals are essentially the same whether from a mine or a used lithium-ion battery depending on how the cathode material is engineered.
- To win the contract, Freudenberg tested Ascend Elements’ customized cathode product extensively and found it to have truly exceptional cycle-life results while achieving best-in-class safety.
- Ascend Elements raised $542 million in capital from investors a few days prior to the article's publication, equivalent to about €506 million.
- The fresh capital is designated for the completion of Ascend Elements' factory in Hopkinsville in the US state of Kentucky.
- The NMC cathode material for Freudenberg will be developed at Ascend’s research and development centre in Westborough, Massachusetts.
- In March 2023, Ascend Elements commissioned its first commercial-scale lithium-ion battery recycling plant in the US state of Georgia.
- The Georgia facility, referred to as ‘Base 1’, is North America’s largest recycling plant for electric vehicle batteries.
- Up to 30,000 tonnes of batteries can be processed at the Georgia facility annually, which is equivalent to around 70,000 e-vehicle batteries.
