# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-02-27

**Source:** [Leaders Celebrate Hyundai Motor Group’s Historic Impact on Georgia | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-releases/2024/state-leaders-celebrate-hyundai-motor-groups-historic-impact-georgia)  ·  `page_000042`

## Facts

- Governor Brian Kemp and First Lady Marty Kemp joined President and Global COO of Hyundai Motor Company and President and CEO of Hyundai and Genesis Motor North America José Muñoz to celebrate Hyundai Motor Group’s historic impact on Georgia.
- An update was provided on the progress of Hyundai Motor Group's Metaplant America facility.
- The facility will officially start vehicle production in the fourth quarter of 2024.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Hyundai has been an incredible partner to the state, helping recruit businesses, giving back to communities, and working with colleges and universities to connect Georgians with opportunities.
- Hyundai and Genesis electric vehicles on display included the IONIQ 5, IONIQ 6, GV60, and Electrified GV70.
- The hydrogen-powered XCIENT Fuel Cell Truck will be used for logistics needs between Hyundai’s manufacturing facilities and suppliers.
- Governor Kemp officially declared February 26 as Hyundai Day throughout the state.
- The General Assembly passed a Resolution commending Hyundai Motor Group’s dedication to Georgia and recognizing February 26 as Hyundai Day at the State Capitol.
- José Muñoz stated that Hyundai’s investments in Georgia will create tens of thousands of great-paying American jobs and bring tens of billions of dollars in economic impact for decades to come.
- The Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America project was announced in May 2022.
- LG Energy Solution was named as the joint venture partner in the manufacture of battery components.
- An additional expansion of the battery facility was announced.
- Hyundai and SK On are building an EV battery manufacturing facility in Bartow County to supply Hyundai Motor Group’s U.S. plants.
- Since the initial announcement, Hyundai Motor Group’s planned investment in Georgia has reached more than $12.6 billion.
- The planned investment will bring the creation of 12,000 direct, long-term jobs.
- Lieutenant Governor Burt Jones and House Speaker Jon Burns attended the celebration along with legislative leaders, GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson, and officials from the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority.
- Lt. Governor Burt Jones applauded Governor Kemp, state agencies, and the University System of Georgia, especially Georgia Tech, for their partnership with Hyundai.
- Lt. Governor Jones stated that stakeholder commitment enabled Hyundai's positive impact and creation of thousands of jobs, supporting Georgia's position as the number one state to do business.
- Up to 300,000 Hyundai, Genesis, and Kia EVs are expected to be produced annually at the Metaplant.
- 17 HMGMA suppliers have announced projects across 10 Georgia counties spanning beyond southeast Georgia.
- Further supplier announcements are expected.
- Supplier facilities are expected to create more than 6,700 new jobs and more than $2.5 billion in investment.
- Supplier facilities will be operational prior to the opening of HMGMA.
- Speaker Jon Burns stated that Hyundai's Metaplant will have a generational impact as a force multiplier for investments in education, healthcare, housing, and transportation.
- The Metaplant site is nearly 3,000 acres.
- Four building exteriors are complete and outfitting for internal operations is underway.
- The Metaplant’s architecture incorporates technologically advanced elements and design features representing the Savannah area, Hyundai’s brand identity, nature, and clean energy.
- HMGMA's full-time employees, called "Meta Pros," are being hired rapidly for training on machinery, office/clerical work, and other roles.
- HMGMA currently has 566 full-time employees.
- Approximately 75 percent of the workforce is from the Greater Savannah area.
- An additional 37 employees are from Georgia but outside the local area.
- Georgia Quick Start broke ground on its customized training center at the Metaplant site on February 8, 2024.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that the groundbreaking fulfilled a commitment to Hyundai and Georgians to train individuals in high-skill careers as Meta Pros.
- HMGMA partnered with local technical colleges to prepare students for entry-level employment at the Metaplant.
- Electrical Vehicle Professional Technical Certificate of Credit (EVP TCC) training is available at Coastal Pines Technical College’s Baxley and Waycross campuses, Southeastern Technical College’s Vidalia campus, Savannah Technical College, and Ogeechee Technical College.
- TCSG Commissioner Greg Dozier stated that technical colleges are providing comprehensive training programs tailored to the growing EV industry in Georgia.
- In September 2023, Georgia Tech and Hyundai Motor Company signed a memorandum of understanding to collaborate on research for sustainable mobility, including developing a hydrogen economy, workforce development, and smarter cities.
- U.S. News & World Report ranked Georgia Tech as the top public university and No. 3 nationally in energy and fuel research.
- Georgia Tech President Ángel Cabrera stated that the partnership will advance science powering key sectors and produce the next-generation workforce needed for these industries.
- Since 1998, Hyundai has directed over $6 million towards local Georgia communities through philanthropic programs and partnerships.
- The company is promoting STEM programs in local K-12 schools and hosting educational programs on alternative energy sources.
- Hyundai supports local organizations dedicated to affordable homeownership and focuses on environmental stewardship.
- Trip Tollison of the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority stated that Hyundai has given more than $1 million to regional nonprofits and formed partnerships with local educational institutions.
- Trip Tollison noted Hyundai committed to $7.59 billion in capital investment in HMGMA and will create 8,500 jobs.
- More than 550 Meta Pros have already been hired, with 75 percent coming from the Savannah region.
- Beginning with mass production, electricity used to power the Metaplant will come from 100 percent clean power sources.
- This aligns with Hyundai’s commitment to carbon neutrality by 2045.
- A 41-acre park will be featured in front of the plant, including trails, running tracks, environmental art displays, sports fields, and amenities for employees.
- HMGMA is Hyundai Motor Group’s first dedicated Electric Vehicle mass-production plant located in Bryan County, Georgia.
- The plant will utilize a highly connected, automated, and flexible manufacturing system that organically connects all elements of the EV ecosystem.
- All production processes including order collection, procurement, logistics, and production will be optimized utilizing AI and data.
- The innovative manufacturing system will create a human-centered work environment with robots assisting human workers.
- Hyundai Motor Group has about 250,000 employees worldwide and operates mobility brands including Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis.
