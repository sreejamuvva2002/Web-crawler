# Rivian Manufacturing Facility, Georgia (Stanton Springs)

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-16

**Source:** [Rivian Holds Kickoff Ceremony for Georgia Plant, Accelerating American Manufacturing](https://rivian.com/newsroom/article/rivian-holds-kickoff-ceremony-for-georgia-plant-accelerating-american-manufacturing-growth)  ·  `page_000193`

## Facts

- Rivian held a formal groundbreaking ceremony at its manufacturing site outside Social Circle, Georgia, on September 16, 2025.
- The new facility represents a multi-billion dollar investment.
- The plant is expected to create 7,500 jobs by 2030.
- The project will also create 2,000 construction jobs.
- Customer vehicle production at the Georgia facility is expected to begin in 2028.
- Rivian plans to manufacture the midsize, 5-seater R2 SUV and R3 crossover at the new plant.
- The facility will be built in two phases.
- Each phase will result in 200,000 units of annual production capacity.
- The total annual production capacity for the completed facility is expected to be up to 400,000 vehicles.
- The plant's output will support the sale of American EVs in international markets.
- An analysis conducted by IMPLAN estimates the project will generate an additional nearly 8,000 indirect jobs.
- Collectively, the 15,000+ direct and indirect jobs are expected to generate over $1 billion in labor income annually.
- The project is expected to support suppliers, vendors, and small businesses in Jasper, Morgan, Newton, and Walton Counties and the surrounding region.
- Billions in economic benefits are anticipated from the project across these counties.
- Rivian is developing partnerships with universities, technical colleges, and local, regional, and state institutions as it scales operations.
- The manufacturing site covers nearly 2,000 acres.
- The site will utilize modern construction techniques and advanced environmental management.
- The facility is intended to feature recreational trails for employees and customers, including a dedicated Rivian experience trail.
- On September 14, 2025, Rivian held a community event at the Stanton Springs Plant site welcoming nearly 1,000 future neighbors and friends.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp, First Lady Marty Kemp, and Georgia Speaker of the House Jon Burns attended the groundbreaking ceremony alongside other state and local officials.
- Rivian Founder and CEO RJ Scaringe stated the facility will support global expansion and provide scale for millions of future drivers in all-electric vehicles domestically and overseas.
- Jerry Silvio, Chairman of the Joint Development Authority of Jasper, Morgan, Newton & Walton Counties, noted the project aligns with the JDA's 25-year vision for high-tech manufacturing and good-paying jobs.
