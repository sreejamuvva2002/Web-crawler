# Ascend Elements

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Ascend Elements | Republic of Poland Offers Ascend Elements Up to USD $320 Million to Support Construction of Lithium-ion Battery Materials Plant in Poland](https://ascendelements.com/republic-of-poland-offers-ascend-elements-up-to-usd-320-million/)  ·  `page_052619`

## Facts

- The Republic of Poland offered Ascend Elements up to USD $320 million (1.22 billion Polish Zloty) to support construction of a precursor cathode active material (pCAM) manufacturing facility in Poland.
- The cash subsidy is one of the largest grants ever offered by Poland’s Ministry of Economic Development and Technology.
- As part of the E.U.’s Temporary Crisis and Transition Framework (TCTF), the grant is designed to support Europe’s transition to a net-zero economy.
- Ascend Elements has already identified a location in Poland for the construction of its first advanced battery materials (pCAM) manufacturing facility in Europe.
- CEO Linh Austin met with Poland’s Secretary of State Michał Jaros at the Polish Ministry of Economic Development and Technology in Warsaw on Wednesday to receive the grant offer.
- In 2024, Ascend Elements and Elemental Strategic Metals established a joint venture to own and operate the AE Elemental JV battery recycling facility in Zawiercie, Poland.
- The new pCAM facility in Poland will be solely owned and operated by Ascend Elements.
- Ascend Elements plans to commercialize its innovative technology for the manufacture of nickel, manganese, and cobalt (NMC) pCAM made from recycled lithium-ion battery materials at the new Polish facility.
- The company’s Hydro-to-Cathode® direct pCAM synthesis technology produces new NMC pCAM material from spent lithium-ion cells more efficiently than traditional methods, resulting in improved economics and lowered carbon emissions.
- A recently published life cycle assessment found the Hydro-to-Cathode® process is significantly cleaner than traditional battery recycling methods – producing 49% lower carbon dioxide equivalent (CO2e) emissions and 26% lower particulate matter 2.5 (PM 2.5) emissions.
- By 2030, Ascend Elements aims to achieve 86% lower CO2e emissions and 94% lower PM 2.5 emissions through additional decarbonization efforts.
- In the United States, Ascend Elements owns and operates one of North America’s largest battery recycling facilities in Covington, Georgia.
- A new lithium recovery line at the Georgia plant is expected to produce up to 3,000 metric tons of Lithium Carbonate (Li₂CO₃) annually.
- The new lithium recovery line makes Ascend Elements the first U.S. company to produce Li₂CO₃ from recycled content.
- In Hopkinsville, Kentucky, the company is building an advanced Hydro-to-Cathode® pCAM manufacturing facility to produce engineered battery materials for up to 750,000 electric vehicle batteries annually.
- When operational in late 2026, the Kentucky facility is expected to be North America’s largest NMC pCAM manufacturer.
- Linh Austin, President and CEO at Ascend Elements, said the offer of support marks a significant step forward in the company’s European growth strategy and that they are committed to building an efficient battery materials supply chain on both sides of the Atlantic.
- Pawel Pudlowski, Ph.D., Vice President of the Management Board at the Polish Investment and Trade Agency (PAIH), said battery sector projects are a key part of PAIH’s investment portfolio and the Ascend Elements project is one of the largest U.S. investments in recent years, strengthening Poland’s role in the battery supply chain.
- Tomasz Poznar, Ph.D., Senior Vice President of Commercial at Ascend Elements, said the company is deeply grateful for the support of the Polish government and thrilled to expand its presence in Europe.
