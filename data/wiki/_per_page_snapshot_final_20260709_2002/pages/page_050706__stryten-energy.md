# Stryten Energy

**Source:** [Stryten Energy Recognized by Kansas House for 50 Years of Manufacturing Excellence  - Professional Services](https://www.gulfoilandgas.com/webpro1/main/mainnews.asp?id=1113449)  ·  `page_050706`

## Facts

- The Kansas House of Representatives issued House Resolution No. 6036 honoring Stryten Energy for 50 years of manufacturing at its Salina, Kansas plant.
- The resolution acknowledges the company's long-standing contributions to the economic vitality, workforce development, and community strength of Salina and the State of Kansas.
- Stryten Energy has manufactured batteries in Salina for five decades.
- The company assembles batteries for key industry sectors including automotive, marine, recreation, heavy-duty truck, and lawn-and-garden for use across the United States.
- Stryten Energy currently employs more than 700 people in Saline County.
- The resolution highlights the company's civic engagement and volunteer service, including participation in the Smoky Hill River Festival, the downtown Salina Christmas Parade, the Parade of Lights, the Fe for a Cure Race, and contributions to Project Salina’s annual food drive.
- Stryten Energy maintains ongoing engagement with Kansas technical schools and universities to promote advanced manufacturing careers and workforce development.
- Rodger Meyer, Executive Vice President of Operations, stated the company is honored by the recognition and looks forward to continuing its mission of innovation, resilience, and leadership in energy storage technologies.
- Stryten Energy's contributions to the domestic energy supply chain include battery recycling and closed-loop manufacturing technologies.
- These contributions were recognized as important for strengthening U.S. energy security.
- Representative Steven Howe sponsored the resolution and formally presented it, noting the company's commitment to the community, domestic supply chain, and national energy and security independence.
- The Kansas House formally adopted House Resolution No. 6036 on March 11, 2026.
