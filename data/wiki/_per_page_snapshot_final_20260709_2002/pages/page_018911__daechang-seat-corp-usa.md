# Daechang Seat Corp. USA

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced on October 23 that Daechang Seat Corp. USA will invest $72.5 million in the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center in Chatham County, Georgia.
- The investment will create over 500 jobs at the new facility located in close proximity to Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA).
- Daechang Seat Corp. USA specializes in automotive seat frames and serves as a supplier for HMGMA.
- Daechang Seat Corp. (DSC) was established in 1979 in Kyungju, South Korea, and has become one of the largest seat frame producers in the world.
- Globally, DSC employs more than 5,000 people across seven countries.
- The company's production process includes fully automatic lines with arc welding and spot-welding machines.
- DSC recently ventured into the electric vehicle sector and continues to expand its scope.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that suppliers for the Hyundai Metaplant project alone reach at least nine counties, adding to a growing manufacturing base centered around Chatham County and the Savannah region.
- Jinsuk Lee, CEO of Daechang Seat Savannah Corp., expressed appreciation to the State of Georgia, local communities, and the City of Savannah for their support.
- DSC currently holds a prominent partnership with Hyundai Transys and Hyundai Mobis at HMGMA.
- The new facility will be located at the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center.
- The company will hire for assembly operators, equipment technicians, mechanical engineers, and electrical engineers through local job fairs and website postings in the future.
- Griff Lynch, President and CEO of the Georgia Ports Authority, noted that the site is only minutes from the Port of Savannah, providing ideal connectivity for U.S. supply chains.
- Trip Tollison, President and CEO of the Savannah Economic Development Authority, highlighted that DSC is the third Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America supplier to locate in the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center.
- Alyce Thornhill, Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy at the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s Global Commerce team, represented the department on this competitive project.
- The project was developed in partnership with the Savannah Economic Development Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that countless partners are helping transform Georgia into a center of the automotive and mobility industry.
- Since 2018, Georgia has created more than $25 billion in investments and 30,000 jobs as it pursues the entire electric vehicle supply chain.
- In fiscal year 2023, job creation in Georgia's automotive industry increased by 324 percent compared to fiscal year 2021.
- Suppliers attracted by Georgia’s mobility original equipment manufacturers resulted in over $2 billion in investment across the state in fiscal year 2023.
