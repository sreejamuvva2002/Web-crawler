# Ecoplastic Corp.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-01-05

**Source:** [Bulloch County lands second auto parts supplier for new Hyundai EV plant - The Augusta Press](https://theaugustapress.com/bulloch-county-lands-second-auto-parts-supplier-for-new-hyundai-ev-plant/)  ·  `page_000473`

## Facts

- Ecoplastic Corp. will build a new automotive parts plant in Bulloch County.
- The project represents a $205 million investment that will create 456 jobs, as announced by Gov. Brian Kemp on Thursday.
- The plant will supply the huge Hyundai electric vehicle manufacturing facility being built near Savannah.
- Ecoplastic was established in 1984 and produces plastic automobile parts for original equipment manufacturers and other suppliers.
- Products include bumpers, consoles, trims, and plastic molds.
- The Korean company expects to begin operations by October of next year.
- The facility will be filling positions in human resources, finance, parts development, quality control, facility maintenance, injection, and paint production.
- Gov. Brian Kemp stated: “We’re proud to welcome Ecoplastic to our growing ecosystem of manufacturers, logistics professionals, suppliers, and more, building on the record-breaking jobs and investments that are on their way to the Peach State.”
- Gov. Brian Kemp added: “This great company … will find a welcoming community of hardworking Georgians and close proximity to our ports system.”
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development worked with the Development Authority of Bulloch County, the Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia EMC, and the Technical College System of Georgia to land the project.
- Two months ago, auto parts manufacturer Joon Georgia announced it will invest $317 million in a new manufacturing plant in Bulloch County that will create 630 jobs, making it the new Hyundai EV plant’s first confirmed supplier.
