# Aspen Aerogels (NYSE: ASPN)

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** thermal_management  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp: Aspen Aerogels to Open Advanced Manufacturing Facility in Bulloch County](https://www.einpresswire.com/article/563418313/gov-kemp-aspen-aerogels-to-open-advanced-manufacturing-facility-in-bulloch-county)  ·  `page_052772`

## Facts

- Aspen Aerogels plans to construct an advanced manufacturing facility at Bulloch County’s Southern Gateway Commerce Park near Statesboro in southeast Georgia.
- The company intends to invest a minimum of $325 million in the new site.
- Aspen will create more than 250 advanced manufacturing jobs at the facility.
- Production at the new site is expected to begin in late 2023.
- Hiring for the project will commence in the second half of 2022 across skilled manufacturing operators, advanced process engineers, quality and maintenance technicians, and professional support roles.
- The planned state-of-the-art facility will be a 500,000-square-foot building situated on a 90-acre parcel.
- When combined with Aspen’s original East Providence, Rhode Island plant, the Georgia facility is designed to triple the company’s nameplate aerogel capacity.
- The expansion supports Aspen’s thermal barrier growth plans in the fast-growing electric vehicle market.
- Aspen aims to capitalize on increased demand for its Aerogel Technology Platform™ as EV OEMs adopt its PyroThin® aerogel thermal barrier solutions.
- The PyroThin materials plant will provide thermal management, mechanical stability, and fire protection properties specifically for EV battery systems.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced the project, stating that Aspen has put confidence in Georgia, its people, and the state’s growing electric mobility sector.
- Don Young, President and CEO of Aspen, noted that the Statesboro and Bulloch County region offers access to efficient ports, a strong available workforce, and secure low-cost utilities and critical raw materials.
- Billy Allen, Vice Chair of the Development Authority of Bulloch County, welcomed Aspen to the community and highlighted the region’s commitment to innovation within the electric mobility ecosystem.
- Georgia Southern University and Ogeechee Technical College are identified as key education resources that will provide critical workforce support for the project.
- Assistant Director Alyce Thornhill represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce division on the project in partnership with the Development Authority of Bulloch County, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia EMC.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Georgia employees will collaborate with Aspen’s network of scientists, engineers, and skilled workforce to address challenges in the changing mobility landscape.
- Southern Gateway Commerce Park holds Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) certification, which requires prospective businesses to complete due diligence and specific requirements prior to site visits.
- Aspen Aerogels is headquartered in Northborough, MA, and currently manufactures its products at facilities in East Providence, Rhode Island.
- The company’s carbon aerogel program aims to increase lithium-ion battery cell performance to help EV manufacturers extend driving range and reduce vehicle costs.
- Aspen’s PyroThin® thermal barrier products are designed to address thermal runaway challenges within the electric vehicle and energy storage markets.
