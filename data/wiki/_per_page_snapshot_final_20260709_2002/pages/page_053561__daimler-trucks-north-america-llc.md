# Daimler Trucks North America LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2015-06-25

**Source:** [Daimler Trucks North America Invests in OEM Partner Zonar, Changes Future of Telematics - Thomas Built Buses](https://thomasbuiltbuses.com/resources/news/daimler-trucks-north-america-invests-in-2015-06-25/)  ·  `page_053561`

## Facts

- Daimler Trucks North America (DTNA) and Zonar Systems, Inc. announced that DTNA has become a minority investor in Zonar.
- The investment marks a milestone in DTNA’s development of smarter connected vehicles and in Zonar’s continued role as a leader in telematics and connectivity services.
- The long-term strategic partnership aims to help DTNA and Zonar achieve their shared vision of optimal transportation logistics through intelligent connectivity.
- Zonar will continue to operate independently of DTNA, with Martin Daum, CEO of DTNA, becoming a member of its Board of Directors.
- Dr. Wolfgang Bernhard, Member of the Board of Management of Daimler AG responsible for Daimler Trucks and Buses, stated that telematics and connectivity are revolutionizing commercial vehicle operation with enormous opportunities for OEMs.
- Dr. Bernhard noted that DTNA made connectivity a dedicated part of its strategy and reached an important milestone to put connected services on the road with greater speed and variety.
- Martin Daum, President and CEO of Daimler Trucks North America, said the strategic partnership provides DTNA the ability to leverage Zonar’s agile development and rapid innovation cycles alongside DTNA’s engineering leadership.
- Daum added that the ultimate focus is on increasing efficiency and safety for everyone on the road.
- Zonar CEO Brett Brinton described the relationship as combining Zonar’s big data analytics and rapid technology development with DTNA’s vehicle engineering expertise.
- The companies have partnered for more than five years, starting with the launch of the Virtual Technician™ remote diagnostic system.
- Zonar’s products can be factory-installed in Freightliner Trucks, Western Star trucks, and Thomas Built Buses.
- DTNA and Zonar will continue to co-develop new products and expand Zonar’s product suite as an OEM and independently.
- The investment underlines the ongoing evolution of Zonar’s diagnostic, safety, and tracking systems integrated into all DTNA vehicles.
- The collective vision includes vehicles tailored for each driver, route, and payload.
- Every new DTNA vehicle coming off the line will benefit from this relationship, including vocational vehicles, school buses, and on-highway trucks.
- Zonar’s current solutions offer visibility into fleet operations, including fuel savings and increased safety and uptime across several market segments.
- DTNA’s expertise will enable Zonar to expand its present product line and build new technology solutions for additional connected vehicle systems.
- Zonar’s technology provides fleets with benefits such as reducing fuel usage, increasing uptime, and improving compliance.
- Drivers powered by Zonar see increased safety, easier HOS logging, and verified inspection records.
- Zonar’s 2020® tablet includes apps for HOS to meet the ELD mandate, EVIR®, driver behavior coaching, and more.
- DTNA’s complete telematics solution, branded Detroit Connect, was developed by and is powered by Zonar.
- Detroit Connect is used in more than 150,000 vehicles, accounting for billions of miles driven.
- Daimler Trucks North America LLC is headquartered in Portland, Oregon, and is the leading heavy-duty truck manufacturer in North America.
- DTNA produces and markets commercial vehicles under the Freightliner, Western Star, and Thomas Built Buses nameplates.
- DTNA is a Daimler company, the world’s leading commercial vehicle manufacturer.
- Zonar provides electronic fleet inspection, tracking, and operations solutions for public and private fleets.
- Zonar built the first Electronic Vehicle Inspection Report (EVIR) system for pre- and post-trip inspections mandated by state and federal law.
- Zonar has more than 400,000 telematics devices installed in commercial vehicles.
