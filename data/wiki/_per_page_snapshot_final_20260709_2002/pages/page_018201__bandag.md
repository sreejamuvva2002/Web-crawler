# Bandag

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Bandag to build state-of-the-art retreading factory in Georgia in collaboration with Tegeta Motors and Bridgestone](https://www.zawya.com/en/press-release/companies-news/bandag-to-build-state-of-the-art-retreading-factory-in-georgia-in-collaboration-with-tegeta-motors-and-bridgestone-d4rjbb4t)  ·  `page_018201`

## Facts

- Bandag has signed an agreement with Tegeta Motors to build a state-of-the-art Tegeta Bandag Factory in Rustavi, Georgia.
- The new facility is supported by an initial investment of USD 2 million.
- The plant aims to produce up to 5,000 retreaded tyres annually.
- Production capacity is expected to double within five years.
- The new facility will be the first Bandag retreading factory in the Caucasus region.
- Construction is scheduled to begin this year.
- Completion of the facility is targeted for late 2026.
- The project marks a significant milestone in Bandag’s commitment to advancing circular mobility and promoting environmental responsibility.
- The initiative strengthens Tegeta Motors’ position as a leading provider of advanced automotive services in the region.
- Jacques Fourie, President of Bridgestone Africa, Middle East Region, stated that the collaboration combines technical expertise, shared vision, and a deep commitment to sustainable mobility.
- The facility will initially retread tyres for buses and trucks.
- Every product produced will meet international safety and performance standards.
- Representatives from Bridgestone and Bandag will provide construction oversight to ensure the facility adheres to global quality standards.
- The plant will be equipped with state-of-the-art Bandag machinery sourced from Europe and the United States.
- Retreaded tyres cost 30-40% less than new tyres.
- Retreaded tyres deliver comparable performance to new tyres.
- Retreaded tyres provide a service life of up to five years.
- Bandag’s retreading technology requires less raw material and energy compared to manufacturing new tyres.
- The process extends a tyre’s life through multiple cycles, reduces waste generation, minimizes carbon emissions, and supports more efficient use of natural resources.
- Ekaterine Kavtaradze, CEO of Tegeta Motors, stated that the launch advances fleet solutions, promotes sustainable mobility, and strengthens their presence in the Caucasus region.
- Tegeta Motors will integrate the retreading operations into its existing ecosystem, which includes tyre services, oil and battery operations, and recycling activities.
- Bridgestone MEA continues to provide technical expertise and innovation support throughout the factory's development as Bandag’s supporting entity in the Caucasus.
