# Qcells

## Facts

- Vice President Kamala Harris visited an expanding solar panel factory in northwest Georgia on Thursday and announced an order for millions of Peach State-built panels.
- Harris toured the Qcells plant near I-75 in Whitfield County, one of two northwest Georgia locations where the South Korean company is expanding its solar panel production.
- The Qcells Dalton expansion and a new facility in Bartow County represent the largest ever investment in clean energy manufacturing in U.S. history, according to company leaders and elected officials.
- The $2.5 billion expansion will result in 2,500 additional jobs.
- This investment is part of the White House’s “Investing in America” tour promoting President Joe Biden’s legislative agenda ahead of a likely 2024 re-election campaign.
- Harris highlighted a historic commitment from Qcells and Virginia-based commercial solar company Summit Ridge Energy to deploy 1.2 gigawatts of community solar power, which is enough electricity to power 140,000 homes and businesses.
- Harris stated this deployment is the largest community solar purchase in U.S. history.
- Community solar projects involve residents, businesses, or groups pooling resources to deploy and connect to solar arrays built elsewhere, with participants buying or leasing access or receiving utility bill credits.
- There are several such community solar arrangements currently operating in Georgia.
- The Qcells and Summit Ridge Energy commitment requires the construction of 2.5 million solar panels.
- Harris noted this output represents roughly eight months’ worth of production from Qcells’ current Dalton facility.
- The bulk of these panels will be installed in community projects across Illinois, Maryland, and Maine, though specific project locations are still being assessed.
- Harris cited administration achievements including growing jobs, strengthening the economy, addressing climate change, boosting infrastructure spending, and encouraging domestic manufacturing.
- President Biden visited Minnesota on Monday to highlight how federal policies have revitalized blue-collar communities.
- Dalton is famous as the flooring capital of the world and is located in a deep-red corner of Georgia that supports former President Donald Trump and represents U.S. Rep. Marjorie Taylor Greene.
- White House National Climate Advisor Ali Zaidi noted the irony that the investment is taking place in a district represented by someone who did not vote for the responsible tax incentives but wants to repeal them.
- A Republican National Committee spokeswoman criticized the Biden and Harris agenda as “out-of-touch,” claiming Democrats are crushing Peach State families with higher prices, lower savings, and tighter incomes.
- President Biden won Georgia in 2020 by approximately 12,000 votes, and the state is expected to be a key battleground in 2024.
- Harris stated that communities like Dalton are full of people with incredible ambition who just need an opportunity to show what they can do.
- Georgia has become a leader across the clean tech sector, particularly in solar panel and electric vehicle industries.
- Hyundai Motor Group, Rivian, SK Innovation, Freyr Battery, and Qcells have announced billion-dollar facilities across Georgia over the past few years.
- Kia, a subsidiary of Hyundai, announced it will manufacture a new electric SUV at its existing West Point factory starting in 2024.
- President Biden stated Georgia is ground zero for building a clean energy economy and industries of the future, citing investments that create good-paying jobs, unleash private sector investments, and produce Made in Georgia and Made in America products.
- Solar panel production has historically been dominated by China.
- Qcells’ expansion makes it the largest solar panel manufacturing operation in the Western Hemisphere and brings the entire chain of panel production onto U.S. soil, according to experts.
- Georgia Republicans led by Gov. Brian Kemp and prominent statewide Democrats have each claimed credit for the state’s clean energy manufacturing surge.
- The Inflation Reduction Act included historic investments to fight climate change and promote domestic clean tech manufacturing.
- The legislation includes the Solar Energy Manufacturing for America Act, championed by U.S. Sen. Jon Ossoff, intended to boost solar panel production.
- Sen. Ossoff stated his solar manufacturing law continues to expand opportunity in Georgia and nationwide through this new partnership.
- State and local governments have aggressively incentivized clean technology companies with billions of dollars in tax abatements, job tax credits, and free infrastructure.
- Gulfstream Aerospace Corporation plans a $150 million expansion that will add 1,600 jobs to its workforce in Chatham County.
- Local governments provided financial incentives to Gulfstream to encourage the projects, according to officials at the Savannah Economic Development Authority.
