# Storion Energy LLC

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2024-12-19

**Source:** [Stryten Energy and Largo Launch Long-Duration Energy Storage Company](https://storion.com/stryten-energy-and-largo-launch-long-duration-energy-storage-company/)  ·  `page_050465`

## Facts

- Stryten Energy LLC, a U.S.-based energy storage solutions provider, announced the signing of agreements by its affiliate Stryten Critical E-Storage LLC with Largo Clean Energy Corp. (LCE) to form Storion Energy LLC.
- LCE is a subsidiary of Largo Inc., which trades on NASDAQ and TSX under the symbol LGO.
- The new company’s mission is to remove barriers for battery manufacturers to domestically source price competitive electrolyte used in vanadium redox flow batteries (VRFB) for long-duration energy storage (LDES) applications in the U.S.
- Storion utilizes the Earth to Energy™ model which combines access to high-quality vanadium supply from the only western hemisphere vanadium mine with domestic electrolyte production.
- This model establishes a fully integrated vertical supply chain for utility-scale flow battery LDES solutions.
- Storion will leverage Stryten’s proprietary reactor design and Largo Physical Vanadium Corp.’s (LPV) unique vanadium electrolyte leasing capabilities to deliver vanadium electrolyte at less than $0.02 per kilowatt-hour (kWh).
- This cost-effective domestic supply chain for vanadium electrolyte positions the U.S. as a leader in critical energy infrastructure and supports the Department of Energy’s Long Duration Storage Shot goal to reduce the levelized cost of storage (LCOS) to $0.05/kWh by the end of the decade.
- Mike Judd, President and CEO of Stryten Energy, stated that VRFB technology will completely replace lithium in the utility arena in the next five years to support deployment of renewables for data centers, AI, green hydrogen production, and commercial-industrial applications.
- Vanadium electrolyte typically constitutes a large portion of the overall VRFB system cost depending on market price.
- LPV provides a unique vanadium leasing model that allows investors to invest in physical vanadium assets while stored in VRFBs, aiming to alleviate acquisition costs and drive additional vanadium demand.
- Storion will have exclusive rights to offer LPV-owned vanadium to the market in the form of vanadium electrolyte.
- Francesco D’Alessio, President of LCE, noted that utilizing LPV’s leasing capabilities is expected to create a competitive VRFB pricing model to challenge lithium dominance for utility-scale deployments of 10 MWhs and greater.
- Following the installation of the largest VRFB in Europe by the LCE team, Storion’s cost-effective domestic supply chain presents an opportunity to rapidly grow adoption of this technology.
- VRFB technology provides long-duration energy storage greater than four hours to help ensure grid stability and facilitate increased utilization of renewables for businesses and consumers across the U.S.
- With proper maintenance, VRFBs can last more than 20 years without their electrolyte losing storage capacity.
- The electrolyte is infinitely recyclable, and the battery offers a near-limitless cycle life.
- These systems are independently scalable in power and capacity, with potential for substantial cost reduction as discharge time increases.
- Storion Energy will be a joint venture between a Stryten Energy affiliate and Largo Clean Energy Corp., a subsidiary of Largo Inc., one of the world’s largest and highest quality vanadium suppliers.
- Storion will have locations in Wilmington, Massachusetts and Alpharetta, Georgia.
- Stryten Energy is headquartered in Alpharetta, Georgia, and partners with major companies to meet demand for reliable and sustainable energy storage capacity.
- Stryten’s stored energy technologies include advanced lead, lithium and vanadium redox flow batteries, intelligent chargers, and energy performance management software.
- LPV aims to provide a secure, convenient, and exchange-traded investment alternative for direct exposure to physical vanadium, a metal essential to steel, aerospace, and energy storage industries.
- Vanadium is non-degrading and fully recyclable when used as electrolyte in VRFBs and offers carbon-reducing attributes when used in steel alloying applications.
- LPV’s strategy includes owning and actively supplying vanadium to end users of VRFBs to advance renewable energy integration in long duration storage.
- Largo is a globally recognized supplier of high-quality vanadium and ilmenite products sourced from its Maracás Menchen Mine in Brazil.
- As one of the world’s largest primary vanadium producers, Largo produces critical materials for steel, aerospace, defense, chemical, and energy storage sectors.
- Largo holds a 50% ownership stake in Storion Energy through its strategic investment in the clean energy storage sector.
