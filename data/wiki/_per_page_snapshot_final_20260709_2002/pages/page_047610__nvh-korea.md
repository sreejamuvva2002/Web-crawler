# NVH Korea

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-02

**Source:** [NVH Korea To Open New Facility In Georgia, Creating 160 Jobs And Investing $72 Million | Doc and the Block](https://docandtheblock.com/nvh-korea-to-open-new-facility-in-georgia-creating-160-jobs-and-investing-72-million/)  ·  `page_047610`

## Facts

- Republican Georgia Governor Brian Kemp announced that NVH Korea would be opening a new facility in Locust Grove.
- The project will create over 160 new jobs.
- The company will invest $72 million in Henry County.
- NVH Korea is an automotive parts manufacturer based in the City of Ulsan.
- The company was established in 1984.
- NVH Korea specializes in automotive noise, vibration, and heat-control parts.
- The company currently produces these parts in Korea, Europe, and India.
- NVH Korea already operates a manufacturing facility in Georgia that produces floor mats, cargo mats, and cargo trays.
- The new facility will be located at Gardner Logistics Park on Colvin Drive in Locust Grove.
- The new facility will supply battery parts for electric vehicles (EVs) in the United States.
- EV customers for the new facility include Hyundai Mobis, Kia Georgia, and Hyundai Motor Group.
- The new facility will produce EV battery system components that protect, connect, and sense the performance of battery cells.
- The facility is expected to begin operations in the second quarter of 2024.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Georgia's reliable infrastructure network connects global partners and its great workforce allows job creators to find success in every community.
- Governor Brian Kemp noted the incredible growth of the e-mobility and battery industries in Georgia supports large suppliers like NVH Korea.
- Governor Brian Kemp expressed pride in welcoming NVH Korea as a partner in making Georgia the epicenter of the industrial revolution of their time.
- Ja Kyum Koo, Chairman and CEO of NVH Korea, stated that Georgia was chosen as the company’s first EV battery component manufacturing site.
- Ja Kyum Koo noted that cooperation between state agencies and company staff helped guide the project.
- Ja Kyum Koo described the new facility as the first step in NVH Korea’s effort to contribute to the electrification of the mobility industry.
