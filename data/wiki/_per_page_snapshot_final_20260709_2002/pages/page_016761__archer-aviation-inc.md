## Facts

- Archer Aviation Inc. and Palantir Technologies Inc. announced a partnership to build the AI foundation for the future of next-gen aviation technologies.
- Archer will initially leverage Palantir Foundry and AIP to accelerate the scaling of its aircraft manufacturing capabilities.
- Archer and Palantir plan to work in parallel to develop software for the next-gen of critical aviation systems, including air traffic control, movement control and route planning.
- The partnership will be formalized later today during a signing ceremony between Palantir co-founder and CEO Alex Karp and Archer Founder and CEO Adam Goldstein at Palantir’s AIPCon.
- Archer and Palantir plan to leverage Palantir Foundry and AIP to accelerate the scaling of Archer’s aircraft manufacturing capabilities at its facilities in Georgia and Silicon Valley, with the intent to advance the development of software solutions to drive innovation across the entire value chain.
- The partnership will include the development of next-gen software utilizing AI to improve a range of aviation systems, including air traffic control, movement control and route planning, with the goal of improving efficiency, safety and affordability across the industry.
- Palantir co-founder and CEO Alex Karp stated that the partnership is redefining the future of flight, making it not only more efficient but also more accessible by integrating advanced AI capabilities with Archer’s innovative approach to aircraft manufacturing and operations.
- Archer Founder and CEO Adam Goldstein noted that while the aviation industry has an unmatched level of safety, much legacy technology has only incrementally advanced, and AI and software present an inflection point that will shape the future of aviation.
- Archer is designing and developing key enabling technologies and aircraft necessary to power the future of aviation.
