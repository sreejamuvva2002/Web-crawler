# Ascend Elements

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Ascend Elements, US Engineered Materials and Lithium-ion Battery Recycling Company, Secures $300 Million in Funding - Batteries News](https://batteriesnews.com/ascend-elements-us-engineered-materials-lithium-ion-battery-recycling-company-secures-300-million-funding/)  ·  `page_060507`

## Facts

- Ascend Elements is a U.S.-based engineered materials and lithium-ion battery recycling company.
- The company announced it has secured $300 million in equity and debt financing.
- This funding includes $200 million in Series C equity investments from an international group of strategic and financial investors.
- The funding round was led by Fifth Wall Climate.
- SK ecoplant, the environmental unit of South Korean conglomerate SK Group, joined the funding round.
- Other new investors include Oman Investment Authority, Lithium Americas Corporation, GLy Capital Management’s New Mobility Fund, Mirae Asset Capital & LS and Shinhan GIB.
- Many investors from previous funding rounds also participated in the latest round, including Hitachi Ventures, Jaguar Land Rover’s InMotion Ventures, TDK Ventures, Orbia Ventures, At One Ventures, TRUMPF Venture, and Doral Energy-Tech Ventures.
- This funding is in addition to two recently awarded grants totaling $480 million from the Department of Energy.
- The funding and grants will accelerate commercialization of Ascend Elements’ proprietary Hydro-to-Cathode™ direct precursor synthesis process.
- This process establishes a closed-loop, EV battery materials supply chain in North America.
- The announcement follows plans to invest up to $1 billion to build a sustainable lithium-ion battery materials facility in Hopkinsville, Ky.
- The first-of-its-kind manufacturing facility is known as “Apex 1”.
- Apex 1 will produce enough lithium-ion battery pCAM and sustainable CAM to equip up to 250,000 electric vehicles per year.
- Mike O’Kronley serves as the CEO of Ascend Elements.
- O’Kronley stated that their patented, closed-loop process goes beyond simple battery recycling to produce sustainable, high-performance cathode active materials for new EV batteries.
- Ascend Elements is already generating revenue and processing end-of-life batteries and manufacturing scrap at its “Base 1” facility in Georgia.
- The Base 1 facility will have 30,000 metric tons of EV battery recycling capacity by the end of 2022.
- The Base 1 facility will ultimately feed Apex 1 in Kentucky.
- The company expects to recycle more than 150,000 metric tons of lithium-ion batteries per year globally by 2026.
- Recycled materials will be transformed into high-performance, customized EV battery cathode active material precursor (pCAM) and cathode active materials (CAM).
- These materials meet or exceed the performance requirements of top-tier battery manufacturers.
- Peter Gajdoš is a Fifth Wall Partner and Co-Lead of the Climate Investment team.
- Gajdoš stated that securing recyclability of critical metals in batteries is essential to achieve energy independence from fossil fuels.
- Park Kyung-il serves as the CEO of SK ecoplant.
- Park stated the investment lays the foundation for SK ecoplant as the 1st shareholder to dominate the global battery recycling industry.
- Ibrahim Al Eisri is Director – Private Equity at Oman Investment Authority (OIA).
- Al Eisri stated the investment aligns with OIA’s strategy of investing in transformative companies complementing Oman’s Vision 2040 priorities.
- Ascend Elements is based in Westborough, Mass.
- The company is the leading provider of sustainable, closed-loop battery materials solutions.
- Its proprietary Hydro-to-Cathode™ direct precursor synthesis technology produces new CAM from spent lithium-ion cells more efficiently than traditional methods.
- This technology results in reduced cost, improved performance, and lowered GHG emissions.
- The announcement was made on October 26, 2022.
