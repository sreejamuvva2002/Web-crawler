# Georgia EV Market and Federal Clean Air Tax Credit Program

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-26

**Source:** [Race against time: Georgia EV buyers scramble for $7,500 tax credit](https://www.atlantanewsfirst.com/2025/09/26/georgia-ev-buyers-rush-beat-federal-tax-credit-deadline/)  ·  `page_009417`

## Facts

- Consumers have less than a week to claim the $7,500 federal tax credit for new clean air electric vehicles before the program ends.
- The $4,000 federal tax credit for qualifying used EVs also ends on Sept. 30.
- Local dealerships across Georgia are capitalizing on the countdown with targeted incentives and promotions.
- Ed Voyles Honda has created a dedicated EV webpage featuring available incentives and a map of nearby charging stations.
- Jim Ellis Volkswagen Kennesaw is highlighting which models still qualify for the credit, including the Volkswagen ID.4.
- Costco’s Auto Program is offering members an additional $1,000 to $3,000 incentive on top of the federal tax credit when purchasing an EV through participating dealerships.
- According to the U.S. Department of Energy, Georgia is home to approximately 92,000 electric vehicles, with the majority concentrated in the metro Atlanta area.
- The concentration of EVs has led to the development of approximately 1,700 charging stations in the metro Atlanta region and 2,300 statewide.
- Kia’s plant in West Point produces 40% of all Kia models sold nationwide, according to the Georgia Department of Economic Development.
- Hyundai’s new Metaplant will support the production of 500,000 Hyundai, Kia and Genesis electric and hybrid vehicles annually at full production.
- Last week, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America announced a $2.7 billion expansion that is set to create 3,000 more jobs.
- Rivian has confirmed a $5 billion investment in a plant near Covington, which is expected to create 7,500 jobs.
