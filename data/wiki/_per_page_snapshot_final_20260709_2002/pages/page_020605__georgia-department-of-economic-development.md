# Georgia Department of Economic Development

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2023-12

**Source:** [GDEcD: 50 Years of International Presence - Columbus CEO](https://thecolumbusceo.com/features/2023/12/50-years-japan-and-europe/)  ·  `page_020605`

## Facts

- The State of Georgia has become a center of international business, with Atlanta and Savannah serving as primary global gateways for people and products through air, sea, and rail.
- State leadership under then-Governor Jimmy Carter began establishing Georgia’s physical presence abroad fifty years ago (in 1973), kickstarting the state’s trajectory to becoming a global hub for business and innovation.
- Coryn Marsik serves as Director of International Investment at the Georgia Department of Economic Development, stating that over 50 years of international representation has given Georgia's economy remarkable resiliency by recruiting diverse investments that create jobs and help companies reach new global markets.
- The State of Georgia’s first international office was established in Tokyo in 1973 to provide direct access to rapidly developing East Asian markets.
- Japanese companies continue to be the top international employer in Georgia to this day, supported by an active community of organizations such as the Japan-America Society of Georgia and the Japan External Trade Organization.
- Georgia serves as a charter member in the Southeast U.S.-Japan Alliance.
- Joseph Huntemann serves as Managing Director of the State of Georgia’s Japan Office, providing insights on how the office supports jobs and opportunity for Georgians.
- The State of Georgia’s Europe Office was established shortly after the Tokyo office, with professionals serving the European market from Munich, Germany.
- Germany is one of Georgia’s top investor nations and has a robust mobility industry that anchors Georgia’s mission to attract innovative technologies from Europe.
- In 2020, Governor Brian Kemp and a State of Georgia delegation met with automotive companies headquartered in Germany, where electrification and alternative fuels were repeatedly discussed.
- Following the 2020 trip, the state set a new goal to identify opportunities at every step of the mobility and battery supply chain.
- Just three years after the 2020 delegation trip, the mobility and battery supply chain announced more than $25.5 billion in investments and an estimated 30,700 new jobs over the next few years.
- European-based companies enhancing Georgia’s mobility ecosystem include GEDIA Automotive, Aurubis AG, and Heliox.
- Governor Brian Kemp served on a panel at the 2023 World Economic Forum in Davos-Klosters, Switzerland, sharing that “one thing you can count on is stability and a great economy, a great business environment in the State of Georgia.”
- Ellen Kraft serves as Managing Director of the Europe Office, noting that the European market offers opportunities for attracting innovative companies, helping Georgia businesses connect with new customers, and establishing new ties like the North Rhine-Westphalia (NRW) office in Atlanta.
- Pat Wilson serves as Commissioner of the Georgia Department of Economic Development, explaining how decades of visionary leadership has allowed Georgia to reap long-term economic benefits.
- International businesses operating in Georgia tap into university-backed research, innovative ecosystems, and solutions-oriented partnerships across local, state, and industry levels.
