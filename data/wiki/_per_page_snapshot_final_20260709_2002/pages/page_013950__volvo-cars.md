# Volvo Cars

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-23

**Source:** [Volvo stock jumps on expanded US production, new hybrid model to counter tariffs](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/volvo-stock-jumps-on-expanded-us-production-new-hybrid-model-to-counter-tariffs-160422577.html?fr=sycsrp_catchall)  ·  `page_013950`

## Facts

- Sweden's Volvo Cars is expanding production at its US plant to localize production, deflect tariffs, and build more cars that appeal to buyers in one of its biggest markets.
- Volvo, wholly owned by China's Geely, announced it would bring a fourth vehicle to its production facility located in Ridgeville, S.C.
- The new vehicle is a next-generation hybrid model designed to meet the 'specific demands' of the US market.
- Volvo offered no details of the new vehicle, but others have surmised it will be a large crossover SUV of some sort.
- Volvo stock trading in Sweden jumped over 3% following the announcement.
- This summer, Volvo announced that it would bring its bestselling XC60 midsize SUV to the South Carolina production line starting in late 2026.
- The company currently builds the XC60 at its plant in Gothenburg, Sweden.
- The XC60 is an important car for Volvo stateside; the company has sold over 27,000 XC60s in the US in the first eight months of 2025, an increase of almost 20% year over year.
- The South Carolina plant currently produces the fully electric Volvo EX90 SUV and its sister car, the Polestar 3.
- The South Carolina plant has installed production capacity of 150,000 cars per year.
- Altogether, Volvo's investment at its US plant stands at $1.3 billion.
- Volvo Cars CEO Håkan Samuelsson stated: 'Our investment plans once again reinforce our long-term commitment to the US market and our manufacturing operations in South Carolina.'
- Samuelsson led Volvo from 2012 to 2022 and replaced prior CEO Jim Rowan in March of this year.
- Volvo has auto production plants in Gothenburg, Ridgeville, Chengdu, Daqing, Taizhou in China, as well as in Ghent, Belgium.
- Volvo said its regionalization efforts allow the company to 'better meet' the needs of its customers.
- The imposition of President Trump's tariffs on global imports is driving the expansion, making building in the US paramount.
- Samuelsson stated regarding expanding its US manufacturing footprint: 'The tariffs have accelerated this process, it would be fair to admit that.'
- Cars imported from Sweden and the EU will eventually face 15% tariffs, an improvement from the current 27.5% rate, but the deal is currently tentative.
- Importing vehicles from China plants, where the entry-level EX30 EV is produced, faces 100% tariffs, making it a non-starter.
- Volvo moved production of its EX30 from China to Ghent in response to tariff pressures.
- Polestar stopped selling its China-made Polestar 2 sedan in the US earlier this year.
