# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-02

**Source:** [State Leaders Celebrate Hyundai Motor Group’s Historic Impact on Georgia - Tifton CEO](https://tiftonceo.com/features/2024/02/state-leaders-celebrate-hyundai-motor-groups-historic-impact-georgia/)  ·  `page_009390`

## Facts

- Governor Brian Kemp and First Lady Marty Kemp joined President and Global COO of Hyundai Motor Company and President and CEO of Hyundai and Genesis Motor North America José Muñoz to celebrate Hyundai Motor Group’s historic impact on Georgia.
- An update was provided on the progress of Hyundai Motor Group's Metaplant America facility.
- The facility will officially start vehicle production in the fourth quarter of this year.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated Hyundai has been an incredible partner to the state for decades and highlighted the historic benefits a project like this brings to Georgians.
- Hyundai helps recruit businesses, gives back to communities, and works with world-class colleges and universities to connect Georgians with opportunities.
- Hyundai and Genesis electric vehicles on display included the IONIQ 5, IONIQ 6, GV60, and Electrified GV70.
- The hydrogen-powered XCIENT Fuel Cell Truck will be used for logistics needs between Hyundai’s manufacturing facilities and suppliers.
- Governor Kemp officially declared February 26 as Hyundai Day throughout the state.
- The General Assembly passed a Resolution commending Hyundai Motor Group’s dedication to Georgia and recognizing February 26 as Hyundai Day at the State Capitol.
- José Muñoz stated that Hyundai’s investments in Georgia will create tens of thousands of great paying American jobs.
- Hyundai’s investments in Georgia will bring tens of billions of dollars in economic impact for decades to come.
- The Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America project was announced in May 2022.
- LG Energy Solution was named as the joint venture partner in the manufacture of battery components.
- An additional expansion of the battery facility was announced.
- Hyundai and SK On are building an EV battery manufacturing facility in Bartow County to supply Hyundai Motor Group’s U.S. plants.
- Hyundai Motor Group’s planned investment in Georgia has reached more than $12.6 billion.
- The planned investment brings the creation of 12,000 direct, long-term jobs.
- Lieutenant Governor Burt Jones and House Speaker Jon Burns joined legislative leaders, GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson, local elected officials from the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority, and company representatives at the celebration.
- Lt. Governor Burt Jones applauded Governor Kemp, state agencies, and the University System of Georgia for their partnership with Hyundai.
- The commitment from all stakeholders has enabled Hyundai's positive impact and the creation of thousands of jobs.
- These initiatives support Georgia's position as the number one state in which to do business.
- Up to 300,000 Hyundai, Genesis, and Kia EVs are expected to be produced annually at the Metaplant.
- 17 HMGMA suppliers have announced projects across 10 Georgia counties spanning beyond southeast Georgia.
- Further supplier announcements are expected.
- Supplier facilities are expected to create more than 6,700 new jobs and more than $2.5 billion in investment.
- Supplier facilities will be operational prior to the opening of HMGMA.
- Speaker Jon Burns praised Governor Kemp and the General Assembly for policy direction supporting Hyundai's Southeast Georgia investment.
- The Metaplant will have a generational impact as a force multiplier for additional investments in education, healthcare, housing, and transportation.
- The nearly 3,000-acre Metaplant site has four building exteriors complete with outfitting for internal operations underway.
- The Metaplant’s architecture incorporates technologically advanced elements, design features representing the Savannah area, Hyundai’s brand identity, nature, and clean energy.
- "Meta Pros" are HMGMA’s full-time employees being hired rapidly for training and skills on machinery, office and clerical work, and numerous other employment roles.
- HMGMA currently has 566 full-time employees.
- Approximately 75 percent of the workforce is from the Greater Savannah area.
- An additional 37 employees are from Georgia but outside the local area.
- Georgia Quick Start broke ground on its customized training center at the Metaplant site on February 8.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Georgians will be trained in high-skill careers as Meta Pros at one of the most advanced vehicle assembly and battery plants in the world.
- HMGMA partnered with local technical colleges to prepare students for entry-level employment at the Metaplant.
- Electrical Vehicle Professional Technical Certificate of Credit (EVP TCC) training is available at Coastal Pines Technical College’s Baxley and Waycross campuses, Southeastern Technical College’s Vidalia campus, Savannah Technical College, and Ogeechee Technical College.
- TCSG Commissioner Greg Dozier stated the collaboration demonstrates a shared commitment to innovation, excellence, and the future of the automotive industry.
- The Technical College System of Georgia is providing comprehensive training programs tailored to the growing electric vehicle industry.
- In September 2023, Georgia Tech and Hyundai Motor Company signed a memorandum of understanding to collaborate on research and applications for sustainable mobility.
- Collaboration areas include developing a hydrogen economy, workforce development, and making cities smarter and more sustainable.
- U.S. News & World Report ranked Georgia Tech as the top public university and No. 3 nationally in energy and fuel research.
- Georgia Tech President Ángel Cabrera stated the partnership represents one of their most significant transformative corporate relationships.
- Through the agreement, they are working together to advance science for key sectors and emerging technologies while producing the next-generation workforce.
- Since 1998, Hyundai has directed over $6 million towards local Georgia communities through philanthropic programs and partnerships.
- The company is promoting STEM programs in local K-12 schools and hosting educational programs on alternative energy sources.
- Hyundai supports local organizations dedicated to affordable homeownership and focuses on environmental stewardship.
- Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority’s Trip Tollison stated Hyundai has given more than $1 million to regional nonprofits.
- Hyundai has committed to $7.59 billion in capital investment in Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- The project will create 8,500 jobs.
- More than 550 Meta Pros have already been hired, with 75 percent coming from the Savannah region.
- Beginning with mass production, the electricity used to power the Metaplant will come from 100 percent clean power sources.
- This aligns with Hyundai’s commitment to carbon neutrality by 2045.
- A 41-acre park will be featured in front of the plant, including trails, running tracks, environmental art displays, sports fields, and other amenities for employee use.
