# Hyundai Metaplant

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-03-26

**Source:** [Hyundai Metaplant holds grand opening](https://www.yahoo.com/news/hyundai-metaplant-holds-grand-opening-180021571.html)  ·  `page_017608`

## Facts

- The Hyundai Metaplant has officially opened its doors in Bryan County, Ga.
- The grand opening festivities mark the culmination of a project nearly three years in the making.
- The facility was originally announced in 2022.
- The Metaplant is expected to create 14,000 direct jobs in the area.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp and Representative Buddy Carter (GA-01) attended the opening of the over $7 billion facility alongside Hyundai executives.
- As of March 24, there are 1,232 employees at the plant, not counting Korean expats.
- Seventy-two percent of the current workforce is from the Greater Savannah area, within a 60-mile radius of the plant.
- The total number of Georgia employees is 1,077, which makes up 87% of the current workforce.
- One hundred fifty-five employees are from outside of the state.
- Eight hundred ninety-five employees are from the greater Savannah area.
- Four hundred thirteen employees (33%) are from Chatham County.
- Ninety-one employees (7%) are from Bryan County.
- One hundred eighty-five employees (15%) are from Bulloch County.
- One hundred three employees (8%) are from Effingham County.
- Joze Munoz, President and CEO of Hyundai Motor Company, announced plans to increase production capacity from 300,000 units per year to 500,000 units per year.
- The increased production target requires the workforce to grow closer to 14,000 employees instead of the originally planned 8,500.
- The plant will not require a new building construction but will need a significant investment in equipment to support the expansion.
- Current production stands at 250 cars per day.
- The Hyundai Metaplant is considered one of the biggest economic projects in Georgia's history.
