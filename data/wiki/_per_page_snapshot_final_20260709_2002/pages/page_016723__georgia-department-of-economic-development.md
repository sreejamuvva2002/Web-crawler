# Georgia Department of Economic Development

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2025-09

**Source:** [Why Georgia Stays on Top: GDEcD's Misti Martin on Growth, Talent, and What’s Next - Rome CEO](https://romeceo.com/features/2025/09/why-georgia-stays-top-misti-martin-growth-talent-and-whats-next/)  ·  `page_016723`

## Facts

- Georgia has been ranked the No. 1 state for business for a decade.
- Misti Martin serves as Deputy Commissioner of Global Commerce at GDEcD.
- Before joining GDEcD, Misti Martin led economic development in Cherokee County, one of metro Atlanta’s fastest-growing areas.
- The state’s economic development strategy focuses on job creation, smart expansion, and transformative investments from global companies including Hyundai Motor Group, Kia, Q CELLS, and Rivian.
- Georgia’s competitive edge is attributed to three pillars: a skilled workforce, well-planned infrastructure, and a consistent business-friendly climate.
- “We’ll accomplish our goals by delivering on our consistent and proven methods. Georgia’s collaborative approach and strong, solutions-based partnerships with our industries allow us to support their growth trajectories,” said Misti Martin.
- Georgia Quick Start is ranked as the No. 1 workforce training program in the nation.
- The Technical College System of Georgia (TCSG) experienced a 10.6% enrollment boost in 2024.
- Programs such as GEORGIA MATCH and Be Pro Be Proud Georgia are helping students connect with in-state higher education and training to strengthen the talent pipeline for Georgia employers.
- The state is currently focused on building out entire supply chains, ranging from logistics providers to advanced manufacturers, recyclers, and R&D firms.
- Recent infrastructure investments include billions of dollars allocated for highway, airport, and freight corridor improvements.
- GDEcD collaborates with local economic developers, utility partners, technical colleges, and employers to ensure projects align with regional goals.
- Supporting the expansion and innovation of Georgia-grown businesses is a top priority for the state.
- Georgia possesses two deepwater ports, the world’s busiest airport, and an integrated highway and rail system.
