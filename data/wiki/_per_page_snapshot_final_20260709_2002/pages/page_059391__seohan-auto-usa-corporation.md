# Seohan Auto USA Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2013-10-22

**Source:** [Seohan Auto to expand in Auburn, creating new jobs](https://www.wtvm.com/story/23750728/seohan-auto-to-expand-in-auburn-creating-new-jobs/)  ·  `page_059391`

## Facts

- Seohan Auto USA Corporation is a Korean-based company with American roots in Auburn planning to expand.
- The company is in its planning stages of building a second facility creating initially 50 jobs and investing millions of dollars into the local economy.
- Jobs will be in a manufacturing environment, including roles for machinery operators, machine setters, managers, and engineers, according to City of Auburn Director of Economic Development Phillip Dunlap.
- Seohan has been located in Auburn since 2008 and currently employs about 240 people.
- The company supplies front axles and rear carrier assemblies to several car manufacturers in the US including Hyundai and Kia.
- Seohan's second plant will focus on machining metal components for the auto industry and will represent an additional capital investment of nearly $25 million in Auburn's economy.
- Phillip Dunlap states that the investment is value-added manufacturing, more sophisticated manufacturing, and those jobs typically pay better.
- The new facility is to be built in Auburn's technology Park West and is planned to be completed by the end of 2014.
- Seohan Auto US President Chang-Dug Moon stated that the expansion will allow them to serve their new customer Mitsubishi Motors.
- Chang-Dug Moon attributed the success of their business in part to Auburn's strong workforce and the support of the City of Auburn and the State of Alabama.
- Phillip Dunlap states that creating those jobs creates a positive effect throughout the economy with more disposable income.
