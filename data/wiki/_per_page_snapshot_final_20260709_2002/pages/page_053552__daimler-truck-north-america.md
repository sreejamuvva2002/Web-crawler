# Daimler Truck North America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2023-11-30

**Source:** [Daimler Truck North America just deployed its electric semis in-house](https://electrek.co/2023/11/30/daimler-truck-north-america-electric-semis/)  ·  `page_053552`

## Facts

- Daimler Truck North America is deploying its own electric semi trucks to transport auto parts.
- The Portland, Oregon-based company launched its Freightliner eCascadia battery electric semi-trucks for customers in 2022.
- Daimler Truck North America is deploying an initial four eCascadias to support its production and aftermarket operations across North America.
- The four eCascadias will pick up parts from suppliers in the Pacific Northwest and deliver them to a consolidation center in Portland.
- Parts transported by the eCascadias will be shipped to Daimler's North American factories and aftermarket parts distribution centers serving customers in the US and Canada.
- The four eCascadias charge at Daimler’s “Electric Island,” a heavy-duty electric truck charging, development, and testing site that opened in 2021 at the company’s headquarters.
- The Freightliner eCascadia comes in 315 or 475kWh battery configurations.
- The eCascadia has up to 250 miles of range while carrying approximately a 65,000-pound gross vehicle weight.
- In 2020, the Portland truck factory where the eCascadia is built achieved carbon-neutral production with reduced energy consumption and the offset of onsite emissions.
- Daimler plans to incorporate carbon-neutral production at its remaining truck factories by 2025.
