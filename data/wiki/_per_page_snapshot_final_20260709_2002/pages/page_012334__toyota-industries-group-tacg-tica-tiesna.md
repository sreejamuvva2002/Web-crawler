# Toyota Industries Group (TACG, TICA, TIESNA)

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Toyota Industries Group: TACG-TICA-TIES in Pendergrass, GA](https://toyotacompressor.com/)  ·  `page_012334`

## Facts

- Toyota-Denso Automotive Compressor Georgia (TACG) and Toyota Industries Compressor Parts America (TICA) manufacture and supply vehicle air conditioning compressors for more than half of the North American auto industry.
- Toyota Industries Group is situated on a 150-acre campus off Valentine Industrial Parkway in Pendergrass, Ga., and distributes its energy-efficient products throughout North America.
- The company's compressors have become the industry standard and are used in over half of all cars, trucks, and vans manufactured in North America.
- Well-known automotive brands such as Ford, Honda, BMW, Lexus, Subaru, Fiat-Chrysler, and Toyota Motor vehicles rely on TACG and TICA compressors for their superior performance and reliability.
- The company assembles inverters that supply electronics to power the plug-in devices used in vehicles.
- TACG was established in 2004, specializing in the manufacturing of automobile air conditioning compressors.
- To meet increasing demand, TICA was built adjacent to TACG in 2012, expanding production capacity by manufacturing the component parts used in TACG’s AC compressors.
- In November 2020, TICA expanded its production capabilities to include DC-AC inverters.
- These DC-AC inverters enable the use of electronic devices such as cellphones, laptops, job-site tools, and small appliances in vehicles.
- Toyota Industries Electronics North America (TIESNA) was founded in Michigan in 2013 as a sales company for Toyota Industries’ electronics products.
- TIESNA is expanding its footprint into northeast Georgia while maintaining its position as a leader in the automotive industry.
- Breaking ground occurred in Pendergrass, Georgia, representing a significant milestone for the expansion.
- Production at the new TIESNA facility will commence starting July 2025.
- The new TIESNA facility will produce the DC-DC converter and other electronics products.
- Kotaro Nishimoto serves as President of TACG & TICA in Georgia.
- Sadanori Suzuki serves as President of TIESNA for Georgia & Michigan.
- Associates at the facilities receive highly competitive wages, outstanding benefits, and opportunities for growth in a strong and stable industry.
- The company is committed to environmental responsibility through research and development of eco-friendly automotive solutions for the automotive manufacturing industry.
