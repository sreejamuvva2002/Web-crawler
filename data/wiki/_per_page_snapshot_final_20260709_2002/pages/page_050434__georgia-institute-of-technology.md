# Georgia Institute of Technology

**Type:** research_center  ·  **Role:** research_and_development  ·  **Published:** 2025-05-05

**Source:** [Georgia Tech, Stryten Energy tap lead battery innovation - pv magazine USA](https://pv-magazine-usa.com/2025/05/05/georgia-tech-stryten-energy-tap-lead-battery-innovation/)  ·  `page_050434`

## Facts

- Georgia Tech and Stryten Energy are partnering to give new life to lead batteries, a technology with more than 160 years of history.
- Stryten Energy is headquartered in Alpharetta, Georgia, with manufacturing facilities located in Kansas.
- A new lead battery energy storage system (BESS) was unveiled last week and installed on Georgia Tech’s Atlanta campus.
- The BESS will serve as an experimentation site for advanced research on medium-duration energy storage solutions and applications.
- The installation is designed to complement Georgia Tech’s existing clean energy offerings, which include a previously-installed solar array and a new electric vehicle (EV) charging testbed.
- The lead BESS will enable bi-directional EV charging and load shifting of peak solar power generation.
- The system will provide backup power and support the school’s microgrid and grid-interactive efficient buildings program.
- The project creates a real-time, real-world testbed for evaluating lead battery performance in advanced energy applications within institutional environments.
- Lead batteries offer advantages over lithium-ion devices, including a proven safety record, recyclability, and domestic availability of materials.
- Scott Childers, vice president of essential power at Stryten Energy, stated that at comparable deployment costs, lead is ideal for smaller, behind-the-meter applications.
- Growing U.S. policy focus on building secure supply chains is making lead batteries increasingly appealing.
- The lead battery systems are 100% domestically built.
- Stryten Energy has institutionalized a 98%-plus closed-loop recycling process for its lead systems.
- Researchers from Georgia Tech’s Carbon Neutral Energy Solutions Laboratory and Strategic Energy Institute (SEI) will collect and analyze data on the system's performance, lifecycle impact, and grid interaction.
