# Vanguard Trailer

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [How Dade County’s Workforce and Low Cost of Doing Business Wooed Vanguard Trailer - Greater Chattanooga Economic Partnership](https://www.greaterchatt.com/success_stories/how-dade-countys-workforce-and-low-cost-of-doing-business-wooed-vanguard-trailer/)  ·  `page_013175`

## Facts

- Vanguard Trailer is an Indiana-based manufacturer of dry freight and refrigerated semi-trailer vans for the North American transportation industry.
- A subsidiary of CIMC (China International Marine Containers), Vanguard began considering building a second dry van manufacturing facility in 2014 to continue growth and address demand.
- Vanguard identified an opportunity to build the first dry van manufacturing plant in the Southeast, initially considering locations in Tennessee, Alabama, or Georgia.
- In 2015, Vanguard Trailer announced Dade County, Georgia as the home of its new $36 million, 350,000-square-foot manufacturing facility.
- The facility will employ 400 workers and produce 10,000 semi-trailers annually once operating at full capacity.
- Vanguard credits Dade County’s proximity to customers, availability of a talented workforce, and support of local and state governments for its decision to locate in Trenton, Georgia.
- Denny Williams serves as chief operating officer at CIMC USA, the holding company for Vanguard Trailer.
- Williams stated that Dade County fits well with their current and planned customer base, saving them a substantial amount on delivery costs for Southeast customers and dealers.
- Before settling, Vanguard met with the Georgia Department of Economic Development and the Dade County Industrial Development Authority to learn about available support.
- Available incentives included job-tax credits and Georgia’s state EDGE grant.
- Georgia offers a comprehensive workforce training program called Quick Start, which partners businesses with technical colleges to provide customized workforce training for free.
- The chosen site received the state’s GRAD certification, which handles due diligence such as environmental and geological reviews before property purchase.
- Williams noted that both the state of Georgia and Dade County were excellent at providing resources and information for their location decision.
- Ground broke on the project in September 2015, and plant operations began on August 15, 2016, meeting the original Q3 2016 start goal.
- To-date, the company has hired 130 employees with plans to exceed the originally planned 400 workers.
- Employees will fill roles in manufacturing, leadership, safety and human resources, materials, and accounting.
- Williams expressed satisfaction with operations in Dade County, stating both the county and state delivered on all their promises.
- Workers at Vanguard Trailer work four 10-hour shifts instead of five eight-hour days to allow more personal time outside of work.
