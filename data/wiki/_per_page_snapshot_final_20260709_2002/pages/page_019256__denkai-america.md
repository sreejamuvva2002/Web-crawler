# Denkai America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2022-07-20

**Source:** [Denkai America announces new North American Headquarters, ED Copper Foil Production Facility in Augusta, Georgia | Maxis Advisors LLC](https://www.maxisadvisors.com/blog/2022/07/20/denkai-america-announces-new-north-american-headquarters-ed-copper-foil-production-facility-in-augusta-georgia/)  ·  `page_019256`

## Facts

- Denkai America announced it will locate its new manufacturing facility and North American Headquarters in Richmond County.
- The company’s initial investment will be $150 million to establish an electrodeposited (ED) copper foil production facility.
- The facility will focus on electric vehicle (EV) battery foil.
- The project will take place on a 115-acre site.
- Denkai America aims to invest a total of $430 million and create 250 new jobs over the next five years.
- Maxis Advisors handled the multistate site selection and incentives advisory on behalf of Denkai America.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced the project, stating Georgia is proud to be selected as the home of Denkai America.
- Governor Kemp noted the selection strengthens Georgia’s status as a leader in the emerging electric mobility and existing aerospace, defense, and technology industries.
- Governor Kemp emphasized the state's focus on bringing quality jobs to highly-trained Georgians and fostering a top-ranking pro-business environment.
- Denkai America’s materials have traditionally been used in printed circuit board (PCB) applications for the aerospace and defense industries.
- The company's materials are now also used in lithium-ion batteries powering electric vehicles.
- Denkai has operated for more than 60 years with team members worldwide and operations in Japan and North America.
- Heday Nakajima, Ph.D., Denkai Group President and CEO, stated the investment highlights a commitment to leading ED battery foil manufacturing in North America for many years to come.
- Nobu Masuda, Denkai America’s President and CEO, said the company is thrilled to expand its manufacturing presence into Augusta due to its skilled and talented workforce.
- Construction is expected to begin in July 2022 as the first part of a three-phase investment.
- The facility will be fully operational by December 2024.
- The company will be hiring for skilled industrial technicians, production managers, and engineers.
- Steven Kendrick, Chairman of the Augusta Economic Development Authority, welcomed Denkai America to the Augusta Corporate Park and thanked the Augusta Commission and Augusta Utilities Department for their support.
- Barton Lowrey, Global Commerce Director of Corporate Solutions and Cyber Security at the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD), represented the department on this competitive project.
- The project was developed in partnership with the Augusta Economic Development Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- Georgia Quick Start is the Technical College System of Georgia’s nationally ranked workforce training program that has provided specialized training tailored to business needs for more than 40 years.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated the state is committed to harnessing the generational opportunity of electric mobility adoption to ensure Georgians in the automotive industry have access to future jobs at home.
- Commissioner Wilson noted Denkai America plays a vital role in the EV ecosystem and welcomed the innovative company to Augusta and Richmond County.
- The investment serves as evidence of Georgia’s commitment to building lasting international relationships that drive economic partnerships and recruit the wider electric mobility value chain.
- Georgia’s prime location, extensive infrastructure, skilled workforce, and business-friendly climate have made it attractive for diverse industries focused on creating a sustainable future.
- Georgia is cultivating a vertically integrated supply chain to help companies increase efficiencies by reducing reliance on imported materials.
- EV-related projects have contributed more than $12.6 billion in investments and more than 17,800 new jobs to Georgia since 2020.
- Augusta Corporate Park is a Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) Certified park.
- To earn GRAD certification, a property must undergo due diligence and complete specific requirements prior to a visit by a prospective business.
- Completing the GRAD certification program is a proactive way for community planners and landowners to help catalyze economic growth and industrial development in their regions.
