# Lithium-ion battery

## Facts

- In late 2024, global demand for lithium-ion batteries passed 1 terawatt-hour per year.
- As of late 2024, global production capacity for lithium-ion batteries was more than twice the demand.
- In 2010, global lithium-ion battery production capacity was 20 gigawatt-hours.
- By 2016, global production capacity reached 28 GWh, with 16.4 GWh in China.
- Global production capacity was 767 GWh in 2020, with China accounting for 75%.
- Predictions for lithium-ion battery production in 2023 range from 400 to 1,100 GWh.
- NMC (lithium nickel manganese cobalt oxide) and its derivatives are widely used in the electrification of transport.
- Lithium nickel cobalt aluminum oxide (NCA) is another high-energy lithium-ion chemistry commonly used in electric vehicle batteries.
- Three basic battery types are used in 2020s-era electric vehicles: cylindrical cells (e.g., Tesla), prismatic pouch (e.g., from LG), and prismatic can cells (e.g., from LG, Samsung, Panasonic).
- Overall, between 1991 and 2024, prices for all types of lithium-ion cells fell approximately 99%.
- From 1991 to 2005, the energy capacity per price of lithium-ion batteries improved more than ten-fold.
- Silicon anodes enhanced with graphene nanotubes allow for record-breaking battery energy density of up to 350 Wh/kg and lower EV prices to be competitive with ICEs.
- Standard lithium-ion batteries have a specific energy of 160–300 W·h/kg, with specialty versions reaching up to 450 W·h/kg.
- Standard energy density for lithium-ion batteries is 250–842 W·h/L, while specialty versions reach up to 1,100 W·h/L.
- The charge/discharge efficiency of lithium-ion batteries ranges from 80% to 90%.
- Cycle durability for lithium-ion batteries typically ranges from 400 to 1,200 cycles.
- The electrolyte salt in commercial lithium-ion cells is almost always lithium hexafluorophosphate (LiPF6).
- Copper is used as the current collector at the negative electrode, while aluminum is used for the positive electrode.
- Lithium-ion batteries have enabled portable consumer electronics, laptop computers, cellular phones, and electric cars.
- In the three decades since lithium-ion batteries were first sold in 1991, their volumetric energy density increased threefold while their cost dropped tenfold.
