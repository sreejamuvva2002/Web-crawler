# FREYR Battery

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-04-20

**Source:** [FREYR Battery announces land purchase for U.S. gigafactory in Georgia By Investing.com](https://www.investing.com/news/stock-market-news/freyr-battery-announces-land-purchase-for-us-gigafactory-in-georgia-432SI-2941942)  ·  `page_055001`

## Facts

- FREYR (NYSE:FREY), a German battery company, announced Friday that it selected and purchased a site in Coweta County, Georgia for a multi-phase Giga America clean battery manufacturing project.
- The Giga America project will be developed in multiple phases, beginning with an initial battery cell production module of approximately 34 GWh.
- The initial phase requires a preliminarily estimated capital investment of $1.7 billion.
- FREYR selected the Bridgeport Industrial Park site through a rigorous evaluation process based on operational, logistical, and financial criteria.
- The site was chosen for its suitability to co-locate modular upstream and downstream projects with the Giga America cell manufacturing facility.
- FREYR accelerated its U.S. expansion plans due to strong tailwinds in U.S. renewable energy development, a focus on grid stability initiatives, and recently announced tax incentives associated with the Inflation Reduction Act.
- FREYR Co-Founder and CEO Tom Einar Jensen stated that expanding into the U.S. is foundational to the company's long-term strategy and expects rapid growth in U.S. demand for ESS, passenger EV, and other electric mobility applications over the next decade following the passage of the Inflation Reduction Act.
- The Giga America project will utilize SemiSolid™ technology licensed from FREYR's U.S.-based licensing partner, 24M.
- The SemiSolid™ platform enables capital- and energy-efficient production of lithium-ion batteries at scale.
