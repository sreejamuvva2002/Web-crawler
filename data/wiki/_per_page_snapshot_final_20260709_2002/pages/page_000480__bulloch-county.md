# Bulloch County

**Type:** unknown  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2023-11-29

**Source:** [Statesboro | Bulloch County: Success Breeds Success - Georgia Trend Magazine](https://www.georgiatrend.com/2023/11/29/statesboro-bulloch-county-success-breeds-success/)  ·  `page_000480`

## Facts

- The development of the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant just over the county line in Bryan County is having a profound effect in the region, with Bulloch County seeing the most impact aside from Bryan County.
- Three Tier-one suppliers to the Hyundai plant are starting construction of facilities in southeast Bulloch near I-16, pacing to match or beat the Hyundai completion date of early 2025.
- A fourth automotive parts manufacturer, not directly related to Hyundai, has broken ground in Bulloch County.
- Joon Georgia, part of auto parts manufacturer Ajin USA, is expected to bring 630 jobs and a $317 million investment to Bulloch County.
- The Korean-owned company Joon Georgia will produce automotive body parts for cars for the Hyundai plant.
- Ecoplastic, a Korean-based company, will generate an anticipated 456 jobs with a $205 million investment in Bulloch County.
- Ecoplastic will produce plastic car parts such as bumpers for Hyundai.
- Hanon Systems will supply vehicle air conditioning systems for Hyundai and also for other manufacturers.
- Hanon Systems' $40 million investment is expected to generate 160 jobs.
- Aspen Aerogels makes insulation blankets for battery compartments of electric vehicles.
- Aspen Aerogels will hire an anticipated 250 employees with $325 million in investment in Bulloch County.
- The Aspen Aerogels project was announced ahead of the May 2022 Hyundai plant announcement, and although ground has been broken, construction is currently on hold.
- General Motors is a major customer of Aspen Aerogels, and the pause in construction came before the early fall UAW strikes against major automakers.
- German-based plastics recycler revalyu Resources (US) is building its first U.S. facility in Bulloch County to recycle PET plastic bottles, accounting for 121 jobs on a $210 million investment.
- Ogeechee Technical College (OTC) is expanding its education of industrial employees and currently training Amazon workers on industrial maintenance, with students traveling from across the country.
- OTC is constructing a new 37,000-square-foot Georgia Industrial Systems and Industrial Robotics Training Center to triple its training capacity.
- The $19.7 million OTC training facility is slated for completion in March 2025.
- The two-story training center is being built on land donated by the Development Authority of Bulloch County and will include labs for industrial systems, industrial robotics, and programmable logic controller (PLC) training.
- Georgia Southern University's research areas focus on direct impact in Southeast Georgia and include logistics and supply chain, advanced manufacturing, coastal resilience and sustainability, holistic wellness and fitness, and community vibrancy reflected in public health and safety.
- Georgia Southern is adding two new doctoral programs in high-demand areas of applied computing and engineering.
- The university is expanding the delivery of six graduate degree programs over the coming year to the Education Center at Fort Stewart and Hunter Army Airfield, including an executive MBA, a Master of Public Administration, a Master of Science in IT, a master's degree in professional communication and leadership, and two graduate certificates in cybersecurity and professional communication.
- New industries are concentrated in the southeastern portion of Bulloch County, closest to the Hyundai plant in Bryan County, impacting farmland use.
- Approximately 1,700 jobs are currently in the process of locating and building in Bulloch County, according to Roy Thompson, chair of the Bulloch County Board of Commissioners.
