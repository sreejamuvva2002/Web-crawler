# Hyundai Metaplant

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- Hundreds of federal officers raided the Hyundai Metaplant in Ellabell, Georgia.
- Federal officers arrested 475 people during the raid.
- Officials described the operation as the largest single-site enforcement action in Homeland Security Investigations history.
- Most of the detained individuals were Korean nationals.
- Other detained individuals included visa overstays and individuals without work authorization.
- Some workers attempted to flee by running into a sewage pond during the arrests but were captured.
- Hyundai stated that none of the detainees were direct employees of the company.
- Some of the detained individuals may have worked for contractors or subcontractors associated with the plant.
- Construction at the EV battery plant was halted following the raid.
- The Metaplant is located on a sprawling 2,900-acre site.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp has promoted the Metaplant as a major economic driver for the state.
- The project includes plans to create 8,500 jobs.
- Federal officials stated the raid targeted unlawful employment practices.
- Federal officials warned companies that exploit workers will be held accountable.
