# Sewon America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-02-21

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp: Sewon America Announces 740 New Jobs in Effingham County | Governor Brian P. Kemp Office of the Governor](https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases/2023-02-21/gov-kemp-sewon-america-announces-740-new-jobs-effingham-county)  ·  `page_000095`

## Facts

- Sewon America is a subsidiary of Korean-based Sewon Precision Industry Co.
- Sewon America will create 740 new jobs in Rincon.
- Sewon America will invest more than $300 million in a new Effingham County manufacturing facility.
- This project is the largest known private investment in the City of Rincon to-date.
- The announcement fulfills the commitment to create jobs and investment in all four Savannah Harbor Interstate-16 Joint Development Authority (JDA) counties.
- Hyundai's Metaplant location in Bryan County is described as the largest economic development project in state history.
- Suppliers for the Metaplant have now committed over $1.8 billion in investment, surpassing initial estimates by at least $800 million.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced the project on February 21, 2023.
- Governor Kemp discussed continuing to work with Sewon America during his first international trip as governor to Korea.
- Speaker of the House Jon Burns represents part of Effingham County and played a role in securing the project.
- Lt. Governor Burt Jones stated the investment builds on Georgia's commitment to remain the No. 1 state in which to do business while attracting a robust workforce.
- Sewon America operates one of the largest automobile body parts manufacturing plants in North America.
- The company opened a manufacturing facility in LaGrange in 2008, which has since doubled in size.
- Following the completion of the Rincon facility, Sewon is expected to support 1,600 jobs in Georgia.
- Vice Chairman Kim stated Sewon America was founded 15 years ago in west Georgia and is building another facility in east Georgia.
- Sewon’s new facility will be located at the Grande View industrial park in Rincon.
- The new manufacturing location will produce electric vehicle (EV) body parts for original equipment manufacturers (OEMs), including Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- Operations are expected to begin in 2025.
- Rincon Mayor Ken Lee welcomed Sewon America to the community and commended the Effingham County Industrial Development Authority for bringing the largest employment and private investment project in the city’s history.
- Chairman of the Effingham County Industrial Development Authority (IDA) Troy Smith stated the IDA selected Grande View industrial park as the best location for Sewon's significant investment.
- Executive Director of the Georgia Ports Authority Griff Lynch expressed excitement to support Sewon’s growth and noted the Hyundai announcement continues to bring new economic opportunities to Georgia.
- Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy Alyce Thornhill represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The GDEcD partnered with the Effingham County Industrial Development Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia EMC for this project.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson noted Sewon has integrated into and lent its own industry experience to Georgia’s automotive community through local colleges and Georgia Quick Start.
- On October 25, 2022, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America officially broke ground in neighboring Bryan County on its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility.
- Since 2020, more than 35 EV-related projects have announced more than $21 billion in investment and 26,700 jobs in Georgia.
- Sewon America was established in 2008 in LaGrange as a Georgia company.
- Sewon primarily operates in the Motor Vehicles and Car Bodies industry within the Transportation Equipment sector, producing stamped body components.
