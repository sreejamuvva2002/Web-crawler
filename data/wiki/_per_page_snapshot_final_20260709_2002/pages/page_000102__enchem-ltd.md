# Enchem Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2020-01-22

**Source:** [Enchem to Build Two Facilities in Jackson County, Create 300 Jobs | Governor Brian P. Kemp Office of the Governor](https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases/2020-01-22/enchem-build-two-facilities-jackson-county-create-300-jobs)  ·  `page_000102`

## Facts

- The announcement was made on January 22, 2020.
- Korean manufacturer Enchem Ltd. will build two new manufacturing plants in Jackson County, Georgia.
- The project will create more than 300 jobs.
- Enchem is investing $61.35 million in the project.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp welcomed Enchem to Georgia, noting the project builds on the success of SK Innovation in Jackson County.
- The project aligns with goals established during Governor Kemp's first international trip to Korea last June.
- The market for electric vehicles and batteries continues its rapid growth, with the industry building a hub in Georgia.
- Strong international partnerships between South Korea and Georgia will benefit hardworking Georgians and create opportunities throughout the state.
- Enchem specializes in the development and manufacturing of electrolytes for rechargeable batteries and electrostatic double-layer capacitors (ELDCs).
- Enchem is the first announced supplier for SK Innovation, which broke ground in Jackson County in March 2019 on its $1.67 billion battery manufacturing plant.
- Since its founding in 2012, Enchem has developed multiple new electrolytes and functional additives to improve and extend battery life.
- The company continues to increase its sales to major rechargeable battery and EDLC manufacturers worldwide.
- EnChem CEO Oh Jung Kang stated the company will benefit from Georgia's infrastructure, transportation, and excellent weather.
- Jackson County Board of Commissioners Chairman Tom Crow noted the county's thriving business climate continues to attract top companies from around the world.
- GDEcD Director of Korean Investment Yoonie Kim represented the Global Commerce Division on this competitive project.
- The project was developed in partnership with the Jackson County Development Authority, Georgia EMC, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson highlighted the department's strategic representation in Korea since 1985 and its ongoing commitment to the Korean market.
- Commissioner Wilson noted reliance on partnerships and programs like Georgia Quick Start to ensure workforce and company success.
- In fiscal year 2019, South Korea was a leader in both foreign direct investment and jobs in Georgia, investing $1.87 billion and creating 2,507 jobs.
- Enchem Ltd electrolytes are recognized for guaranteeing long battery life, high power, large capacity, high safety, and stability in rechargeable batteries and EDLCs.
- Enchem's electrolytes have been recognized for performance in high-voltage, high-capacity, and high-power rechargeable batteries used in leading edge IT and electrical vehicle applications.
- The rapid transition from small mobile technology rechargeable batteries to mid- and large-size hybrid and electric vehicle batteries (xES) and energy storage system (ESS) batteries has increased electrolyte consumption and customer requests.
- Increased demand for electrolytes prompted the creation of two new plants.
