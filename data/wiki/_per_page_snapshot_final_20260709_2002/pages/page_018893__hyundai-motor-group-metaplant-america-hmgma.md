# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA)

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Daechang Seat latest supplier to localise production with Hyundai in Georgia | Automotive Logistics](https://www.automotivelogistics.media/electric-vehicles/hyundai-supported-by-suppliers-in-race-to-localise-ev-production-in-us/44811.article)  ·  `page_018893`

## Facts

- Hyundai is building a new $7.6 billion EV assembly plant in the US state of Georgia.
- South Korean tier one supplier Daechang Seat has announced investment to localise production alongside Hyundai.
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) broke ground on its facility in October 2022.
- The HMGMA facility is located near the port of Savannah.
- Hyundai is building a battery plant alongside the production lines on the 1,200-hectare site.
- Construction has begun on all production-related buildings at the site.
- Oscar Kwon, CEO of HMGMA, stated: “The site is advancing every day as we work diligently to complete what will be one of the most advanced vehicle assembly and battery plants in the world.”
- Hyundai is on track to start production at the facility in early 2025.
- José Muñoz noted that production might start sooner than early 2025.
- In May, Hyundai announced a partnership with LG Energy Solution in a 50/50 joint venture for the battery cell manufacturing facility at the plant.
- The battery facility will involve an investment of more than $4.3 billion.
- The battery facility will have an annual production capacity of 30 GWh.
- The 30 GWh capacity will be able to supply annual production at the assembly plant of 300,000 EVs.
- Hyundai is eager to take advantage of incentives for localized EV manufacture offered by the US government’s Inflation Reduction Act (IRA).
- The IRA provides tax credits that save EV buyers up to $7,500 on EVs made in North America with locally supplied batteries.
- These incentives apply to pickup trucks and SUVs that sell for under $80,000 and sedans that sell for under $55,000.
- Hyundai is second only to Tesla in EV sales in the US but has yet to establish EV production in the country supplied by local battery and parts content.
- Hyundai currently benefits from IRA law allowing dealers to apply the credit to any leased electric vehicle regardless of where it is made.
- Hyundai accelerated its plant production schedule, with executives stating they are doubling down and trying to accelerate the project as much as possible.
- The original January 2025 production date may be pulled ahead by three months or more.
- Hyundai signed a partnership with Georgia Tech aimed at strengthening research into hydrogen-fuelled vehicles during a press conference in Atlanta.
- Supplier Daechang Seat committed $72.5 million to the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center, located in close proximity to HMGMA.
- Griff Lynch, president and CEO of the Georgia Ports Authority (GPA), stated that Daechang Seat is only minutes from the port of Savannah and ideally located to take advantage of Georgia’s connectivity.
- Daechang Seat already operates a plant in Phenix City, Alabama, which opened in 2019.
- Daechang Seat announced a $23.4m expansion at its Phenix City plant last year.
- Sewon Precision Industry is investing $300m in a metal stamping plant as part of Hyundai's localized production commitments in Georgia.
- Seoyon E-HWA plans a $76m plant to make interior and exterior trim parts for Hyundai in Georgia.
- Hyundai Mobis is investing $926m in a powertrain plant for the project.
- Joon Georgia is investing $317m in a plant to make vehicle frames.
- Ecoplastic is investing $205m in a plant to make interior and exterior plastic parts, including bumpers, consoles, trims, and plastic moulds.
- According to the Georgia Department for Economic Development, suppliers attracted by Georgia’s mobility original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) scheme resulted in over $2 billion in investment across the state in fiscal year 2023.
