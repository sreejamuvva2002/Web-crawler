# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-02-26

**Source:** [Hyundai Announces New Production Start Date at Metaplant America During Hyundai Day in Georgia - HMGMA](https://www.hmgma.com/2024/02/26/hyundai-announces-new-production-start-date-at-metaplant-america-during-hyundai-day-in-georgia/)  ·  `page_009377`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) has accelerated its official start of production to the fourth quarter of 2024.
- The grand opening celebration for HMGMA is targeted for the first quarter of 2025.
- Production was previously announced to begin in the first quarter of 2025.
- Georgia's General Assembly passed a Resolution commending Hyundai Motor Group and officially recognizing Feb. 26, 2024, as Hyundai Day at the State Capitol.
- Hyundai and Genesis electric vehicles on display included the IONIQ 5, IONIQ 6, GV60, and Electrified GV70.
- The hydrogen-powered XCIENT Fuel Cell Truck will be deployed for logistics needs between HMGMA and its suppliers.
- José Muñoz, president and global COO of Hyundai Motor Company and president and CEO of Hyundai and Genesis Motor North America, stated that Hyundai's investments in Georgia will create tens of thousands of great-paying American jobs.
- Muñoz added that these investments will bring tens of billions of dollars in economic impact to the state for decades to come.
- Governor Brian Kemp highlighted Hyundai as an incredible partner helping recruit businesses, give back to communities, and work with colleges and universities to connect Georgians with opportunities.
- Oscar Kwon, president and CEO of HMGMA, called the project the largest economic development project in Georgia's history.
- Kwon credited the hard work of Meta Pros and continued support from state and local partners, including the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Joint Development Authority.
- Lieutenant Governor Burt Jones and House Speaker Jon Burns attended the celebration alongside legislative leaders from both chambers.
- Department of Economic Development Director Pat Wilson joined local elected and economic officials from the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority at the event.
- Hyundai Motor Group is investing $7.59 billion to develop HMGMA.
- HMGMA will create 8,500 direct jobs.
- Hyundai and SK On are building a $5 billion EV battery cell plant in Bartow County, Georgia.
- The SK On joint venture plant is expected to employ 3,500 workers.
- A recent Center for Automotive Research study projects Hyundai's investments in Georgia will create nearly 40,000 jobs.
- The same study estimates $4.6 billion in individual earnings every year from Hyundai's Georgia investments.
- HMGMA has developed educational partnerships with area colleges to create a pipeline for future employment.
- The Electric Vehicle Professional Technical Certificate (EVP TCC) is being offered at four area technical colleges as part of this collaboration.
- Students who successfully complete the EVP TCC program receive preferred status when applying for a position at HMGMA.
- Hyundai is actively developing a hydrogen mobility value chain at HMGMA, as announced at CES 2024.
- Through the Clean Logistics Project in Georgia, the Metaplant will utilize XCIENT Fuel Cell tractors to manage logistics for the plant and its suppliers.
- The XCIENT is Hyundai's commercialized class 8 6×4 fuel cell electric model with more than 6.25 million accumulated miles globally.
- Hyundai is developing hydrogen refueling infrastructure needed to support fuel cell activity at HMGMA.
- This hydrogen collaboration is anticipated to stimulate economic growth and create job opportunities within the state.
- HMGMA is Hyundai Motor Group's first dedicated Electric Vehicle mass-production plant.
- The facility is located in Bryan County, Georgia.
- The plant will utilize a highly connected, automated, and flexible manufacturing system that organically connects all elements of the EV ecosystem.
- All production processes, including order collection, procurement, logistics, and production, will be optimized utilizing AI and data.
- The manufacturing system will create a human-centered work environment with robots assisting human workers.
- Hyundai's U.S. operations, combined with those of its 835 independent dealers, contribute $20.1 billion annually and 190,000 jobs to the U.S. economy.
