# Eaton Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2003-11-04

**Source:** [Eaton Corporation's Van Wert, Ohio, Facility Receives
Certification to New Automotive Supply Chain Standard](https://www.theautochannel.com/news/2003/11/04/172170.html)  ·  `page_019729`

## Facts

- Eaton Corporation's Van Wert, Ohio facility received certification to a new technical specification published by the International Organization for Standardization (ISO).
- The Van Wert facility is among the first facilities in North America and the second at Eaton Corporation to be certified.
- The certification covers the Manufacturing and Central Distribution Facility at Van Wert, Ohio.
- The facility was certified to ISO/TS 16949:2002, an international standard that defines quality system requirements in the automotive supply chain when coupled with customer-specific requirements.
- The certification also covers design functions at Eaton's Technical Center in Maumee, Ohio.
- The certification covers applications engineering, customer service, marketing, strategic purchasing, and supply resource management at Eaton Hydraulics Inc. headquarters in Eden Prairie, Minnesota.
- The Amtec Plant in Rivarolo, Italy is also certified to the standard within Eaton Corporation.
- ISO/TS 16949:2002 was jointly developed by International Automotive Task Force members to ensure global consistency of quality processes.
- The standard includes all requirements of ISO 9000 and combines AVSQ (Italian), EAQF (French), QS-9000 (North American), and VDA 6.1 (German) automotive requirements.
- For customers that still honor QS-9000, the new standard will replace it as of December 14, 2006.
- Employees at Eaton's Van Wert, Eden Prairie, and Maumee facilities began pursuing certification five months before the standard was officially released in April 2002.
- The pursuit of certification was led by Quality Assurance Engineer Karen Adkins and a team of internal auditors.
- Don Waggener serves as plant manager for Van Wert Manufacturing.
- Don Waggener stated that success depends on pursuing certification to the toughest industry standards to drive process and performance improvements.
- Don Waggener noted that Eaton is one of only a few companies in North America fulfilling ISO/TS 16949:2002 requirements at the time.
- Don Waggener expressed pride in Karen Adkins, the internal audit team, and all Van Wert employees for reaching high performance levels.
- Scott Etzler serves as plant manager for Van Wert Central Distribution Facility.
- Scott Etzler stated that the certification will bring improved processes and continuous improvement to operations.
- Scott Etzler noted that the certification will create additional customer opportunities as more customers adopt the automotive business model or require the certification in the future.
- DNV Certification, an independent certifying body, confirmed that Van Wert's quality systems link all functions and comply with customer-specific requirements.
- The certification process included a nine-day location audit where employees demonstrated results across eight clauses covering quality management system, resource management, product realization, and measurement, analysis, and improvement.
- Eaton Corporation is a global $7.2 billion diversified industrial manufacturer.
- Eaton is a leader in fluid power systems; electrical power quality, distribution, and control; automotive engine air management and fuel economy; and intelligent drive train systems for fuel economy and safety in trucks.
- Eaton employs 51,000 people and sells products in more than 50 countries.
