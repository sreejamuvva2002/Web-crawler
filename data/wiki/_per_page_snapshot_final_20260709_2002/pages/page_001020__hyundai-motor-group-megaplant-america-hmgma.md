# Hyundai Motor Group Megaplant America (HMGMA)

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-08-30

**Source:** [Hyundai’s Georgia EV plant clears environmental review](https://www.teslarati.com/hyundai-georgia-ev-plant-water-enviromental-review/)  ·  `page_001020`

## Facts

- Hyundai is constructing a $7.6 billion electric vehicle plant in Georgia that has cleared an environmental review concerning its water usage.
- In August, the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers agreed to revisit Hyundai Motor Group Megaplant America’s (HMGMA) permit standards regarding water usage after a conservation group threatened to sue.
- Last August, the Ogeechee Riverkeeper group pushed back against Hyundai’s permit, citing risks to the region’s drinking water from the plant’s 6.6 million daily gallons usage.
- After reviewing Hyundai’s water usage for months, the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers concluded that HMGMA’s permit required no changes.
- The Army Corps' memo stated that the impacts on the environment as a result of the permit were evaluated and found to be insignificant.
- The memo noted a "long-term minor effect" on the water supply, marking a shift from the "negligible" label used in 2022.
- Ogeechee Riverkeeper's legal director, Ben Kirsch, responded to the agency's findings regarding the permit decision.
- Ben Kirsch stated that they do not yet know what ramifications will come from the decision and cannot offer substantive comments until reviewing the decision document, but they will continue to monitor actions around the permit closely.
- Hyundai’s Georgia plant has experienced wastewater issues since it started operations.
- The City of Savannah issued a permit to accept HMGMA’s wastewater on August 30, 2024.
- The City of Savannah's water treatment facility stopped accepting the EV plant's wastewater by September 2024.
- The city noted that the wastewater was affecting biological processes and clarified that it did not stop taking the Hyundai factory’s wastewater due to Hurricane Helene.
- Hyundai received a Notice of Violation in October regarding the wastewater issue.
- The City of Savannah is working with Georgia’s EPD and Bryan County to resolve the wastewater issue.
- The environmental review clearance keeps Hyundai’s Georgia plant on track for its planned timeline.
- Despite HMGMA’s water usage issues and wastewater hiccups, the Army Corps ruling signals confidence in the project’s environmental footprint, though local oversight persists amid water supply concerns.
