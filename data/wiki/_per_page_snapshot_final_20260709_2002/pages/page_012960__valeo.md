# Valeo

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2022-07-27

**Source:** [Valeo posts profit beat amid 'improving' chip shortage, EV adoption accelerates | Euronews](https://www.euronews.com/next/2022/07/27/valeo-outlook)  ·  `page_012960`

## Facts

- French car parts maker Valeo posted slightly better-than-expected half-year core profit and reiterated its full-year guidance, citing a projected increase in global car output.
- Valeo's half-year earnings before interest, tax, depreciation and amortisation fell nearly 8% year-on-year to 1.11 billion euros ($1.12 billion), but beat a company-supplied analyst consensus forecast of 1.07 billion euros.
- CEO Christophe Perillat stated that the context has been difficult due to microchip shortages that have dogged the automotive sector, but said the chip situation is improving.
- Additional pressures on auto parts manufacturers include rising costs in tight supply chains required to produce components.
- Valeo forecast a 2022 core profit margin in a range of 11.8-12.3%, down from 13.4% the previous year.
- IHS Markit forecast global production of light vehicles would hit 81 million in 2022, up from about 77 million in 2021.
- Valeo reported being particularly pleased with its order intake, especially in vehicle electrification and advanced driving assistance systems.
- The European Union is pushing ahead with plans for net-zero emissions by 2050, prompting automakers and suppliers to ramp up investments in electric vehicles.
- Valeo signed a major contract with BMW to equip the German carmaker's upcoming EV platform with its advanced driving assistance systems (ADAS).
- In February, Valeo announced plans jointly with Renault to develop a new-generation electric-motor vehicle in France.
- Valeo unveiled its "Move Up" plan in February, which bets on the accelerated adoption of vehicle electrification and ADAS systems.
- Order intake for Valeo in the first six months of 2022 was 13.1 billion euros, and 2.9 billion for Valeo Siemens eAutomotive, putting the group perfectly in line with the Move Up plan.
