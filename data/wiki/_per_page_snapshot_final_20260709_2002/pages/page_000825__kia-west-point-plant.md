# Kia West Point Plant

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-07-12

**Source:** [Kia to bring EV manufacturing to West Point | News | times-herald.com](https://www.times-herald.com/news/kia-to-bring-ev-manufacturing-to-west-point/article_46c91018-20d0-11ee-a2bd-e3c34ad43934.html)  ·  `page_000825`

## Facts

- Kia will invest more than $200 million at its West Point plant.
- The investment will create nearly 200 jobs.
- The plant will produce the Korean automaker’s new electric vehicle sports-utility model, the EV9.
- Gov. Brian Kemp announced the project on Wednesday.
- The EV9 will become the fifth model assembled at the Georgia plant since it opened in 2009.
- The plant currently assembles the Telluride, Sorento and Sportage SUVs, and the K5 mid-size sedan.
- More than 40% of Kia vehicles sold in the U.S. are manufactured in West Point.
- Gov. Brian Kemp stated that Georgia’s longstanding partnership with Kia has led to generational job creation and growth for the West Point area.
- Kemp said the project will bring more opportunity to hardworking Georgians and help propel the state toward its goal of becoming the e-mobility capital.
- Sean Yoon, president & CEO of Kia Motors North America and Kia Motors America, stated that like the Telluride, the EV9 has the potential to be another change catalyst for Kia.
- Yoon added that the EV9 will be the most innovative vehicle Kia has ever built and a standout in the EV market and on the road.
- To date, Kia has invested more than $1.9 billion in Georgia.
- Kia Georgia is responsible for more than 14,000 plant and supplier jobs in the region.
- Hyundai is building a $5.5 billion plant west of Savannah that will generate 8,100 jobs.
- The Hyundai project is described as the biggest economic development project in Georgia’s history.
- Rivian has under construction a $5 billion EV plant in the Covington area expected to create 7,500 jobs.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development’s Global Commerce team worked on the Kia EV project with the West Point Development Authority.
- The project involved collaboration with the city of West Point and Troup County.
- The Georgia Center of Innovation’s manufacturing and energy teams assisted with the project.
- The Technical College System of Georgia’s Quick Start program was involved in workforce training for the project.
- The EV9 plant is expected to begin operations in the second quarter of next year.
