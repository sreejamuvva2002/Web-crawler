## Facts

- Stock quotes for Archer Aviation collapsed nearly a quarter in the past two weeks.
- Risk-tolerant investors are advised to look at Archer as long as the share price is below the recent high of $13.30 per share, reached on May 16.
- At the close on Friday, May 30, one share was trading 24% below that high, at $10.09.
- Before the open on June 2, Archer's stock had edged 0.4% lower.
- The company faces "hugely exciting" opportunities in 2025 as its eVTOL aircraft, Midnight, should make its first commercial flight.
- Archer stated that its Midnight aircraft could allow passengers to replace "one-to-two-hour drives with flights that take 5-15 minutes, potentially saving hours versus sitting in traffic."
- The company currently has no revenue, no profits, and no finished and certified product.
- For the first quarter, the company reported a net loss of $93.4 million.
- At the end of the first quarter, the company estimated it was only 15% of the way through the U.S. regulatory approval process.
- In 2025, Archer plans to be the first carrier to launch air taxi services in Abu Dhabi.
- A successful launch in the UAE would help assure investors that Archer's long-term plans are feasible and potential customers that air taxi flights are safe.
- Archer faced pressure from a short-seller campaign in May, with Culper Research publishing a report on May 20 alleging the company "over the past 12 months, has systematically misled, deceived, or outright lied to investors about virtually every supposed milestone related to its development and testing of its eVTOL aircraft, Midnight."
- Culper Research believed that Archer’s push for near-term commercialization is "not only premature, but reckless."
- On May 21, the day after the report came out, Archer shares fell 4% to $10.82 apiece.
- Since the beginning of the year, Archer stock is up a slight 3.5%, but over the last 12 months, it has more than tripled.
- Eight Wall Street analysts advise buying Archer ("buys" and "overweights"), while two others advise holding.
