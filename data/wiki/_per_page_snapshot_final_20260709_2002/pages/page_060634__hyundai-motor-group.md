# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** research_and_development  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hyundai, Georgia Tech To Invest $12.6bn To Boost Electric Mobility](https://www.saurenergy.com/solar-energy-news/hyundai-georgia-tech-to-invest-12-6bn-to-boost-electric-mobility)  ·  `page_060634`

## Facts

- Georgia Tech and Hyundai Motor Group signed a Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) to collaborate on research and applications to support the future of sustainable mobility.
- The collaboration includes developing a hydrogen economy, workforce development, and making cities smarter and more sustainable.
- Hyundai Motor Group is investing $12.6 billion in Georgia's new dedicated electric vehicle and battery manufacturing facilities.
- The investment includes the $7.59 billion Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) site located in Bryan County, Georgia.
- Hyundai Motor Group claims the HMGMA site will create more than 8,500 direct jobs.
- The group is also investing $5 billion in a battery manufacturing facility joint venture with SK On in Bartow County, Georgia.
- The SK On joint venture facility is expected to employ around 3,500 people.
- Proximity to Georgia Tech was cited as one of the important considerations for selecting the locations of these facilities.
- Hyundai's collaboration with Georgia Tech builds upon its long history in Georgia.
- Kia Georgia's West Point assembly plant began vehicle production in 2009.
- University System of Georgia Chancellor Sonny Perdue participated in the HMG-Georgia Tech MOU ceremony.
- Sonny Perdue was the Governor of Georgia at the time of the Kia Georgia plant's opening.
- A recent study by the Center for Automotive Research reveals that Hyundai Motor's automotive operations contributed $20.1 billion and 190,000 jobs to the US economy in 2021 alone.
- According to the study, Hyundai's investments in Georgia are expected to create nearly 40,000 jobs.
- The study projects that Hyundai's investments in Georgia will generate $4.6 billion in annual earnings.
- Georgia Tech President Ángel Cabrera stated that Hyundai is a global brand synonymous with quality, innovation, and a commitment to advancing technology to make a positive difference in the world.
- Cabrera noted that as they have gotten to know each other, the alignment of their values has become obvious.
- Jay Chang, President and CEO of Hyundai Motor Company, expressed thrill at creating a partnership with Georgia Tech covering research, sustainable mobility, hydrogen economy, workforce development, and smart cities.
- Chang stated that today is the beginning of a partnership that will last for decades.
- Chang cited this partnership as one of the reasons Hyundai chose Georgia for its EV investments.
- José Muñoz, President and Global COO of Hyundai Motor Company and President and CEO of Hyundai and Genesis Motor North America, said Hyundai's investments in Georgia will create tens of thousands of jobs and bring tens of billions of dollars in economic impact for decades to come.
- Muñoz stated that proximity to institutions like Georgia Tech was one of the many reasons Hyundai selected Georgia for its new EV manufacturing facility.
- Hyundai Motor Group is described as a global enterprise that has created a value chain based on mobility, steel and construction, logistics, finance, IT, and service.
