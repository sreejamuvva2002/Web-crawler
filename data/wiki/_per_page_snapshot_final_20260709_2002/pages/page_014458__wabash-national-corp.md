# Wabash National Corp.

**Source:** [Wabash National Refocuses Business On Cold Chain And e-commerce - Wabash National (NYSE:WNC) - Benzinga](https://www.benzinga.com/news/earnings/21/02/19489155/wabash-national-refocuses-business-on-cold-chain-and-e-commerce)  ·  `page_014458`

## Facts

- Wabash National Corp. (NYSE:WNC) is refocusing its business on cold chain, home delivery and parts and service.
- The company is pushing resources to refrigerated vans needed to move vaccines and food to a homebound nation.
- CEO Brent Yeagy stated that Wabash sees the growth in home delivery having staying power even as more Americans receive inoculations against COVID-19.
- New technology and products to keep goods refrigerated present significant organic growth prospects, according to CEO Brent Yeagy on the company's fourth-quarter earnings call Wednesday.
- Yeagy said, "We've never been faced with a market that has this much potential."
- Yeagy added, "All the ways that we could potentially grow are on the table right now."
- Growth strategies include expanding upfitting, acquiring technology or another manufacturing platform.
- Yeagy stated the company took action to streamline its portfolio by selling assets that do not offer strong strategic fit.
- As it finishes pruning its portfolio, Wabash is setting the stage to backfill by continuing diversification with expanded and new revenue streams in transportation, logistics and distribution markets.
- Wabash sold its Beall brand of tank trailer to MAC Trailer Enterprises in December.
- The company is holding onto its other tank trailer brands.
- Yeagy said the company is "absolutely repositioning resources to accelerate the organic efforts to create additional revenue in a way that has never been seen at Wabash National."
- Wabash sees expanded use of molded structural composite (MSC) technology beyond the 53-foot refrigerated van market.
- MSC weighs less and offers better thermal protection than other trailer construction material.
- Wabash worked on an MSC pilot project with C&S Wholesale Grocers in 2020.
- Using MSC technology, Wabash is able to make a 48-foot trailer 30% more thermally efficient than a traditional refrigerated trailer.
- With this technology, refrigeration units can run fewer hours and for those with all-electric units, fewer batteries are needed.
- Yeagy said, "We believe this is likely to be a rapidly expanding market segment where MSC continues to offer unique value to customers with its durability, reduced weight and improved thermal efficiency."
- Yeagy added that consistent feedback from interested parties indicates their technology offers benefits they have been unable to find elsewhere.
- Wabash is manufacturing Gruau refrigerated inserts for the Ford Transit van to serve the rapidly expanding food/grocery home delivery market.
- Traditionally constructed refrigerated cargo vans use spray foam insulation, which can release gas and allow mold to intrude.
- Gruau inserts fit specific van models with 30% to 50% greater thermal efficiency.
- Yeagy stated this is an important space for Wabash to participate in with its technology as it sets them up to better serve the smaller light-duty upfit market compared to larger, more traditional truck body product models.
- President Joe Biden's green agenda, the lingering pandemic, climate change and technology advances combine to create opportunity, according to Yeagy.
- Yeagy said at least for the next four years, it opens up a really interesting way that Wabash National can take ideas generated from an innovation standpoint and technology they have to bring sustainable solutions within those spaces.
- Traditional truck bodies are the main catalyst to pay for expansion.
- The value of Wabash's backlog of trailers awaiting construction rose to $1.5 billion at the end of Q4 compared to $1 billion at the end of the third quarter.
- Most of the work is dry vans that dominate the Commercial Trailer Products division.
- The average selling price was $27,000, flat with the prior year.
- Yeagy said generally shipments are in line with how they thought that would materialize in the fourth quarter.
- As they look at 2021, Wabash does not see anything that is a structural impediment moving forward.
- Wabash shipped 10,610 trailers in Q4 compared to 14,950 in the same period last year.
- Though Yeagy expressed concern during the third-quarter earnings call about being able to find workers to hire during the pandemic, Wabash added 600 hourly employees during the October-December period.
- The company is seeking 900 more new hires this quarter, according to Chief Financial Officer Mike Pettit.
- Net sales for the fourth quarter 2020 were $404.1 million.
- Operating income was $10 million or 2.5% of net sales.
- For the full year 2020, total revenue totaled $1.5 billion.
- Operating income for the full year was $22.5 million.
- Q4 net income was $5.5 million, or 10 cents per diluted share.
- For the full year of 2020, net income was $7.8 million, or 15 cents a share.
- Operating earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation and amortization (EBITDA) was $25.2 million, or 6.2% of net sales in Q4.
- Full-year operating EBITDA was $73.6 million, or 5% of net sales.
