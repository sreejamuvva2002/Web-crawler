# Kia Motors Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2006-11

**Source:** [New manufacturing plant to be the cornerstone of Kia's plans to more than double sales in North Amer - AI Online -](https://ai-online.com/2006/11/new-manufacturing-plant-to-be-the-cornerstone-of-kias-plans-to-more-than-double-sales-in-north-amer/)  ·  `page_010002`

## Facts

- Kia Motors Corporation (KMC) carried out the groundbreaking ceremony for its first-ever U.S. manufacturing facility in West Point, Troup County, Georgia.
- The automotive assembly and manufacturing plant represents a US$1 billion investment.
- The facility is central to Kia’s plans to increase North American sales (US and Canada) to around 650,000 units by 2010.
- The groundbreaking ceremony was attended by over 500 VIPs from Korea and the U.S., including Mong-Koo Chung (Chairman of Hyundai-Kia Automotive Group), Euisun Chung (President & CEO, Kia Motors Corporation), Sonny Perdue (Governor of Georgia), and high-ranking Korean government and State of Georgia officials.
- Mong-Koo Chung stated that the establishment of the U.S. plant will serve as a springboard to achieve Kia's ultimate goal of firmly establishing itself as a truly world-class global automaker.
- Chung added that Kia Motors will secure jobs and sustain financial growth in the area through transparent management and world-class quality assurance, striving to become a responsible corporate citizen contributing to the local community and preservation of the environment.
- Governor Sonny Perdue stated that the ceremony marks the beginning of a long-term partnership between Kia Motors and Georgia, anticipating years of cooperation as the new plant creates thousands of jobs and sets the standard for the automotive industry.
- The Georgia facility is named KMMG (Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia) and is scheduled to begin production in 2009.
- At full capacity, the plant is expected to produce 300,000 vehicles per year.
- The 224,000-square meter plant will be situated on a site covering 2.6 million square meters.
- The plant will employ approximately 2,500 local workers.
- Five to six suppliers have plans to set up operations in Troup County and its environs, creating an additional 2,000 jobs.
- A total of around 10 suppliers are eventually expected to locate in the vicinity of the plant.
- Alongside a second manufacturing plant in China scheduled for completion next year and the start of production at its first European facility in Slovakia this November, the U.S. plant will play a critical role in boosting Kia’s sales and brand development worldwide.
- The U.S. plant will bring Kia’s total annual overseas production capacity to around 1 million units.
- Kia Motors, along with Hyundai Motor, has invested more than US$300 million in the North American region since 2002.
- Past North American investments include an R&D center in Michigan, a custom-built vehicle proving ground in the Mojave desert, and the largest automotive design studio in California.
- Kia Motors America is currently constructing a new US$85 million, 22,000 square meter corporate headquarters building in Irvine, California.
- The Irvine corporate headquarters is scheduled for completion in December 2006 and occupancy in January 2007.
- The new California facility will also house a US$35 million, 9,400 square meter Kia-exclusive design center to be completed later in 2007.
- Following construction of the KMMG facility, Kia will have the capability to localize all areas of its business in the U.S., from R&D and production to marketing, sales, and after service.
- Since entering the North American market, Kia has expanded to a full product line of vehicles and enjoyed 12 years of consecutive sales increases.
- Kia sold 304,137 vehicles in 2005.
- For September 2006, Kia posted a year-on-year sales increase of 6.4% in the U.S. and Canada.
