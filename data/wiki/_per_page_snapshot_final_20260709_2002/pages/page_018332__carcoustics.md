# Carcoustics

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Deal: German car parts company creating 200 jobs in Buford :: WRAL.com](https://www.wral.com/story/deal-german-car-parts-company-creating-200-jobs-in-buford/16762791/)  ·  `page_018332`

## Facts

- Carcoustics, a German automotive acoustic parts supplier, plans to create 200 new jobs in Buford over the next half-decade.
- The Leverkusen, Germany-based company will invest $6 million in a new 130,000-square-foot manufacturing facility at 4620 Thompson Mill Road.
- Carcoustics focuses on developing and producing solution-oriented automotive parts that are both acoustically and thermally effective.
- Governor Nathan Deal stated that Georgia offers automotive industry suppliers like Carcoustics the resources necessary to compete worldwide.
- Gov. Deal noted that Carcoustics will benefit from a well-trained workforce, a thriving automotive industry, and the nation's top business environment in Georgia.
- Gov. Deal expressed pride in another German company locating in Georgia and welcomed Carcoustics to the state's growing community of auto industry suppliers.
- The project announcement resulted from a joint effort between the Georgia Department of Economic Development's Global Commerce Division, Partnership Gwinnett, the Metro Atlanta Chamber, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- Carcoustics will join numerous other international and German-based companies with locations in Gwinnett County.
- Partnership Gwinnett Project Manager Deven Cason stated that Carcoustics was chosen as a global innovation leader and its new Buford facility is ideally located to serve its rapidly growing worldwide customer base.
- Carcoustics will join nearly 100 other German companies operating in Gwinnett County, which also hosts 500 internationally owned and operated firms.
- Carcoustics Project Manager Stefan Vallentin cited the facility's strategic location near Southeast U.S. car plants as a key factor, specifically naming facilities owned by Kia, Mercedes, BMW, Honda, Volkswagen, Nissan, Hyundai, and Volvo.
- The proximity to these automotive production facilities provides Carcoustics with access to a large pool of potential customers.
- Stefan Vallentin stated that Georgia provides excellent international transport connections and is logistically positioned between BMW's plant in South Carolina and Mercedes' plant in Alabama.
- Vallentin noted that this logistical advantage offers new possibilities for Carcoustics' growth strategy, particularly in the U.S. market.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson highlighted continued economic development opportunities in Gwinnett County and noted Carcoustics joins a growing number of German automotive manufacturers in Georgia.
- Buford City Manager Bryan Kerlin called the project a great win for the city and expressed confidence that Carcoustics will find necessary local support and resources to remain globally competitive.
- Gwinnett County Board of Commissioners Chairwoman Charlotte Nash welcomed Carcoustics, noting it joins a strong base of international companies that found Gwinnett's mix of talent, resources, and location ideal.
- Chairwoman Nash expressed anticipation for Carcoustics' continued success and growth within the county.
