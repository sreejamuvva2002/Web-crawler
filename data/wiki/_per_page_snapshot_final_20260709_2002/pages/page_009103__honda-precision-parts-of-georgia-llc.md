# Honda Precision Parts of Georgia LLC

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2006-05-12

**Source:** [Honda transmission plant in Georgia celebrates production milestone](https://www.reliableplant.com/Read/26854/Honda-transmission-plant-Georgia)  ·  `page_009103`

## Facts

- Associates at Honda Precision Parts of Georgia LLC reached a production milestone on October 4 at 2:30 p.m. with the completion of the 1 millionth Honda transmission built in Georgia.
- Mass production at the facility began on May 12, 2006.
- The Tallapoosa, Ga. facility cost $150 million.
- Masaaki Miyaji, HPPG president, called the milestone a "happy and proud day for all HPPG associates, our Tallapoosa community, for Georgia and for Honda."
- Miyaji stated that HPPG has established a strong culture of stable operations, timely delivery, and production of high quality products for customers.
- Miyaji added that the foundation built at HPPG will be key to future success and exceeding customer expectations.
- HPPG currently employs 400 associates.
- The plant has a capacity to produce more than 300,000 automatic transmissions per year.
- Honda's strategy bases powertrain production near vehicle production for synchronous assembly.
- The Tallapoosa plant supports production of the Honda Odyssey minivan, Pilot sport utility vehicle, Ridgeline pickup truck, and Accord V6 sedan at Honda Manufacturing of Alabama LLC (HMA) in Lincoln, Ala.
- HMA is located 60 miles west of the Tallapoosa plant.
- HMA is the sole North American production source for the Pilot sport utility vehicle, Odyssey minivan, Ridgeline pickup, and the V6 engines that power each vehicle.
- HMA shares production of the Accord V6 Sedan with the Marysville Auto Plant in Marysville, Ohio.
- HMA has a capacity to produce 300,000 vehicles and V6 engines per year.
- Honda has manufactured more than 11 million transmissions in America since starting production of automatic transmissions in Ohio in 1989.
