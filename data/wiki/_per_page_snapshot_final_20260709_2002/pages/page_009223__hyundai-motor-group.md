# Hyundai Motor Group

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group officially opened its new Metaplant in Ellabell on March 27.
- Hyundai Motor Group will invest a total of $12.6 billion in Georgia.
- The facility will produce 500,000 electric and hybrid vehicles annually.
- The project was originally slated as a $5.54 billion investment with a capacity of 300,000 units per year.
- The new announcement marks a substantial increase in both scope and impact compared to the original plan.
- The Hyundai Metaplant is the largest economic development project in Georgia’s history.
- Hyundai’s roots in Georgia date back to 2005 with the construction of Kia’s manufacturing plant in West Point.
- Then-Governor Sonny Perdue welcomed Hyundai Motor Group’s first investment in the Peach State twenty years ago.
- Governor Brian Kemp announced both the opening of the Hyundai Metaplant and an expansion at the Bryan County facility this week.
- Tens of thousands of Georgians are employed in the automotive and electric vehicle (EV) industries.
- The automotive and EV industries generate billions in economic activity each year in Georgia.
- Georgia is now considered the unquestioned leader in advanced EV manufacturing following the announcement.
- The Georgia Chamber thanked Hyundai Motor Group for increasing its record-breaking investment in Georgia.
- The Georgia Chamber applauded Governor Brian Kemp for his tireless dedication to pro-growth policies that made the expansion possible.
- The Georgia Chamber thanked state and regional economic developers and other state leaders for their commitment to Georgia.
- The Georgia Chamber stated that Georgia is the preferred destination for firms to meet customer demand for automobiles for decades to come.
