# Ascend Elements

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2025-03-26

**Source:** [Ascend Elements Names New CEO | WKDZ](https://www.wkdzradio.com/2025/03/26/okronley-moves-on-ascend-elements-names-new-ceo/news-edge/)  ·  `page_052559`

## Facts

- Ascend Elements announced the appointment of Linh Austin as its new president and chief executive officer on March 20, replacing Mike O’Kronley at the post.
- Austin is considered a “seasoned energy industry executive” with three decades of experience.
- Austin has been on the Ascend Elements Board of Directors since September 2024 after previous stops at McDermott, BP and ARCO.
- Austin stated that Ascend Elements is “poised to become Europe and North America’s leading commercial-scale manufacturer of domestic, critical battery minerals.”
- The company utilizes a hydro-to-cathode direct cathode precursor synthesis process, which Austin described as the “pinnacle of technology” in the battery materials and recycling industry.
- Ascend Elements currently operates one of North America’s largest lithium-ion battery recycling facilities located in Covington, Georgia.
- The company is still expected to begin commercial-scale production of up to 3,000 metric tons of lithium carbonate in 2025.
- Ascend Elements continues to build North America’s only commercial scale pCAM manufacturing facility, Apex 1, in Christian County, Kentucky.
- The Kentucky facility remains partially funded by a $316 million U.S. Department of Energy grant.
- The company's operations and expansion “squarely align” with U.S. energy policy related to increasing domestic production of critical minerals and enhancing U.S. energy independence.
- Former CEO Mike O’Kronley announced his departure via LinkedIn, stating it was a difficult decision but that he has “full confidence” in the company’s continued growth and success.
