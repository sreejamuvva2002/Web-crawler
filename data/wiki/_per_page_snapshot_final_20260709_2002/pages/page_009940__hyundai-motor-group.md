# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-05

**Source:** [Homeland security official says 475 people were detained during an immigration raid in Georgia | National | smdailyjournal.com](https://www.smdailyjournal.com/news/national/homeland-security-official-says-475-people-were-detained-during-an-immigration-raid-in-georgia/article_7448b823-9bf9-4423-b086-0f0eb206f47a.html)  ·  `page_009940`

## Facts

- Immigration authorities detained 475 people during a raid on the sprawling manufacturing site in Georgia where Korean automaker Hyundai makes electric vehicles.
- The raid was led by Steven Schrank, lead Georgia agent of Homeland Security Investigations, and resulted from a monthslong investigation into allegations of illegal hiring at the site.
- The operation was described as the "largest single site enforcement operation" in the agency's two-decade history.
- The raid targeted one of Georgia's largest manufacturing sites where Hyundai Motor Group began manufacturing electric vehicles a year ago at a $7.6 billion plant.
- The site employs about 1,200 people in an area about 25 miles (40 kilometers) west of Savannah where bedroom communities bleed into farms.
- Gov. Brian Kemp and other officials have touted the site as Georgia's largest economic development project.
- Agents focused their operation on an adjacent plant still under construction where Hyundai has partnered with LG Energy Solution to produce batteries that power EVs.
- The South Korean government expressed "concern and regret" over the operation targeting its citizens.
- South Korean Foreign Ministry spokesperson Lee Jaewoong stated that business activities of investors and rights of nationals must not be unjustly infringed during U.S. law enforcement.
- The South Korean ministry is dispatching diplomats from its embassy in Washington and consulate in Atlanta to the site and planning to form an on-site response team.
- Immigration attorney Charles Kuck said two of his detained clients arrived from South Korea under a visa waiver program that enables them to travel for tourism or business for stays of 90 days or less without obtaining a visa.
- One detained client had been in the U.S. for a couple of weeks, while the other had been in the country for about 45 days.
- Schrank told reporters that while some detained workers illegally crossed the U.S. border, others had entered legally but had expired visas or had entered on a visa waiver that prohibited them from working.
- Some of those detained worked for the battery manufacturer, while others were employed by contractors and subcontractors at the construction site.
- Schrank said he didn't know precisely how many of the 475 detained were Korean nationals, but that they made up a majority.
- No one has yet been charged with any crimes, and the investigation is ongoing.
- Most of the detainees were taken to an immigration detention center in Folkston, Georgia, near the Florida state line.
- The Hyundai site sits on 3,000 acres (1,214 hectares) in a largely rural area of Bryan County, drawing in workers from several surrounding counties and communities including Savannah.
- Hyundai began producing electric vehicles at the site last September.
- Hyundai Motor Group Executive Chairman Euisun Chung credited President Trump with the company's decision to create more American jobs by building an EV factory in Georgia during a White House appearance.
- Chung stated the decision to invest in Savannah, Georgia, creating more than 8,500 American jobs, was initiated during his meeting with President Trump in Seoul in 2019.
- The battery plant operated by HL-GA Battery Co., a joint venture by Hyundai and LG Energy Solution, is slated to open next year.
- LG said it was closely monitoring the situation and gathering all relevant details, and couldn't immediately confirm how many of its employees or Hyundai workers had been detained.
- LG stated its top priority is always ensuring the safety and well-being of its employees and partners, and that it will fully cooperate with the relevant authorities.
- Operations at Hyundai's EV manufacturing plant were not interrupted by the raid, according to plant spokesperson Bianca Johnson.
- Hyundai Motor Company stated it was working to understand the specific circumstances of the raid and detentions.
- Hyundai Motor Company stated that as of the report date, none of those detained are directly employed by Hyundai Motor Company.
- HL-GA Battery Co. did not immediately respond to a request for comment but previously stated it's cooperating fully with appropriate authorities.
- The Democratic Party of Georgia condemned the raid, with chair Charlie Bailey calling it politically-motivated fear tactics designed to terrorize people who work hard for the economy and communities across Georgia.
- Kemp and other Georgia Republican officials issued statements saying all employers in the state were expected to follow the law.
