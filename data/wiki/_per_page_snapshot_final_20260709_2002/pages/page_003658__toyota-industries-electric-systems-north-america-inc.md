# Toyota Industries Electric Systems North America, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-11-14

**Source:** [Toyota Industries Corporation Expands Georgia Presence, Creating 250 N | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-releases/2023/toyota-industries-corporation-expands-georgia-presence-creating-250-new-jobs)  ·  `page_003658`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Toyota Industries Electric Systems North America, Inc., (TIESNA) will create more than 250 new jobs while expanding the Toyota Industries Corporation campus in Pendergrass with a new manufacturing facility valued at $69 million in investment.
- TIESNA broke ground at the site on November 14, 2023.
- Japanese Consul General Mio Maeda and local representatives joined the announcement.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated Georgia has distinguished itself as a leader in the automotive industry and thanked Toyota for choosing to expand there.
- TIESNA is a subsidiary of Toyota Industries Corporation, which is headquartered in Japan.
- Toyota Industries develops and manufactures automobile-related products, including car air-conditioning compressors and car electronics components for worldwide carmakers.
- The Toyota Industries Group has operated in Pendergrass since 2004.
- The group initially started with TD Automotive Compressor Georgia, LLC (TACG).
- The group added Toyota Industries Compressor Parts America, Co. (TICA) in 2012.
- TACG and TICA currently support more than 400 jobs in Jackson County.
- TIESNA’s new facility will be located at the 1700 Valentine Industrial Parkway campus in Pendergrass.
- The new facility will produce DC-to-DC converters, which convert high voltage to low voltage to supply power to auxiliary equipment.
- Operations at the new facility are expected to begin in 2025.
- County Commission Chair Tom Crow welcomed TIESNA as Toyota Industries' third related company to Jackson County.
- Griff Lynch, President and CEO of the Georgia Ports Authority, commented on the expansion adding to Georgia’s growing automaking sector.
- Griff Lynch stated significant investments from Toyota Industries and other automotive suppliers are delivering career opportunities for Georgians.
- Kristen Miller, Senior Regional Project Manager, represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on the project.
- The project was a partnership with Jackson County Area Chamber of Commerce, Georgia Power, the Georgia Ports Authority, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated Toyota Industries has been part of the Jackson County community fabric since 2004.
- Pat Wilson noted maintaining 50 years of representation in Japan allows direct communication with corporate HQs like Toyota Industries’.
- In fiscal year 2023, job creation in Georgia’s automotive industry increased by 324 percent compared to fiscal year 2021.
- Suppliers attracted by Georgia’s OEMs resulted in over $2 billion in investment across the state in FY23.
- Toyota Industries Corporation was established in 1926 in Japan for the manufacturing and sales of Automatic Looms.
- TICO has more than 280 consolidated subsidiaries around the world.
- TICO holds the largest world market share in three products: air-jet looms, car air conditioning compressors, and lift trucks.
- Toyota Industries Electric Systems North America, Inc. was established in 2013 in Novi, Michigan.
- TIESNA is a sales company handling automotive electronics components such as AC inverters, voltage stabilizers, and motor controllers.
- TIESNA’s customers include major OEMs in North America.
