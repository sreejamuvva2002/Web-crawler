# Hyundai Motor Company

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-06

**Source:** [‘Hyundai has become synonymous with technology’ - THE INVESTOR](https://m.theinvestor.co.kr/article/10410824)  ·  `page_009213`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor and Georgia Tech announced a multidecade partnership in August 2023 to work together in research and applications to support sustainable mobility, the hydrogen economy, and workforce development.
- About a year and a half after the partnership announcement, Georgia Tech President Angel Cabrera stated the collaboration has been a win-win situation for both sides.
- In an interview in Seoul on Jan. 23, Georgia Tech President Angel Cabrera said Hyundai has become synonymous with technology.
- Hyundai Motor decided to invest $12.6 billion in constructing a new electric vehicle-dedicated manufacturing plant and battery production sites in Georgia.
- The Georgia EV and battery facility construction is being done in partnerships with Korean battery firms LG Energy Solution and SK On.
- Georgia Tech President Angel Cabrera noted that Hyundai is already actively recruiting students, and Georgia Tech students want to work for innovative companies like Hyundai.
- Cabrera stated that Hyundai's advantage lies in its very exciting sense of purpose and project for the future.
- When asked about US President Donald Trump's pledge to remove government subsidies for electric vehicles, Georgia Tech President Angel Cabrera dismissed concerns that it would hinder EV sector growth.
- Cabrera said he does not think the new administration is anti-EV but may challenge subsidies, emphasizing the idea is to let the market develop technology and that the future of mobility will be electric.
- Georgia Tech President Angel Cabrera purchased an Ioniq 5, Hyundai's award-winning EV, for himself seven months ago as his first pure electric vehicle.
- Cabrera expressed confidence that customers will not want to return to internal combustion engine-powered cars once they experience an EV.
- Georgia Tech is currently exploring a co-location of Hyundai staff inside its facilities to conduct research there.
- There are currently about 10 ongoing joint research projects between Georgia Tech and Hyundai in areas such as EV and battery technologies.
- A Georgia Tech delegation visited Hyundai’s research sites and joint labs with top Korean schools including Seoul National University and Hanyang University to understand challenges Hyundai is facing.
- During their visit to Korea, the delegation met with officials from Samsung, Doosan, and Hanwha to discuss various topics.
- Georgia Tech has collaborated with Samsung and Doosan in research areas of software technology and hydrogen turbines.
- Hanwha is building a large solar panel production site in Dalton, Georgia, located about 145 kilometers away from Atlanta, which is home to Georgia Tech.
- Georgia Tech's mission to develop leaders who advance technology aligns with Hyundai’s "Progress for Humanity" vision, leading both to always strive for innovation.
- Cabrera emphasized that Hyundai is not just building traditional cars but is constantly trying to figure out how to build new vehicle technology.
- About 10 years ago, Georgia Tech began offering master’s programs online despite initial concerns that it would hurt the university's brand.
- Georgia Tech now offers three online master's courses in computer science, data analytics, and cybersecurity.
- There are about 20,000 students enrolled in remote classes at Georgia Tech, making up the largest master's programs in the world.
- Cabrera noted that Hyundai cars are now everywhere in the US and have become one of the biggest brands in the country.
