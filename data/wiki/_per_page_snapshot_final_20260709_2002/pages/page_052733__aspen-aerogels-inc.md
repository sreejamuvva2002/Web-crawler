# Aspen Aerogels Inc

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** thermal_management  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Aspen Aerogels Charts Cost-Cut Path Amid EV Slowdown - TipRanks.com](https://www.tipranks.com/news/company-announcements/aspen-aerogels-charts-cost-cut-path-amid-ev-slowdown)  ·  `page_052733`

## Facts

- Aspen Aerogels Inc (ASPN) held its Q4 earnings call to discuss financial performance and strategic outlook.
- Full-year 2025 revenue reached $271.1 million, split between $168.9 million from Thermal Barrier and $102.2 million from Energy Industrial.
- The company's business remains heavily tied to EV-related demand.
- Q4 2025 sales slowed to $41.3 million, with Energy Industrial contributing $25.3 million and Thermal Barrier $16.1 million.
- Management expects the Energy Industrial segment to grow about 20% in 2026.
- Energy Industrial growth drivers include a rising subsea project pipeline, more LNG projects, and pent-up maintenance work at refineries and petrochemical plants.
- LNG revenue alone is projected to roughly double versus 2025.
- The company highlighted growing traction with European automakers, including seven design wins and a recent award with Volvo Cars.
- Aspen sees a Europe-only pipeline of roughly $220 million tied to 2027 launches.
- The European pipeline is expected to expand to more than $450 million in 2028.
- These European programs are expected to add about $10–$15 million of revenue in 2026.
- Aspen ended 2025 with $158.6 million in cash and cash equivalents, providing a substantial liquidity buffer.
- The company amended its MidCap credit agreement to gain more covenant flexibility.
- Aspen generated $6.1 million of cash in Q4 2025.
- Management underscored structural fixed cash cost reductions of about $75 million annually.
- Adjusted EBITDA breakeven was reduced from roughly $330 million in 2024 to about $270 million in 2025.
- The adjusted EBITDA breakeven is targeted at around $200 million for 2026.
- Management aims for an adjusted EBITDA breakeven near $175 million in 2027 to improve operating leverage.
- For 2025, Aspen posted a gross profit of $46.3 million, translating into a 17% gross margin.
- Management expects gross margins to expand as volumes recover and the leaner cost structure takes effect.
- The company believes incremental revenue above breakeven could deliver 50%–60% EBITDA margins.
- Aspen is developing a battery energy storage systems segment that will leverage its existing technology and domestic manufacturing capacity.
- First revenue from the new battery energy storage segment is expected in 2026.
- Management identified selective growth opportunities in building and construction as well as defense markets.
- The company's 2026 capital expenditure plan is held to roughly $10 million.
- Scheduled debt amortization for 2026 is about $35 million, including $24 million of term-loan principal.
- Aspen expects to expand its net cash position to more than $70 million by the end of 2026.
- The company reported a GAAP net loss of $389.6 million for 2025 and a Q4 GAAP loss of $72.9 million.
- Adjusted EBITDA for 2025 was a modest $2.9 million, with Q4 adjusted EBITDA at negative $18 million.
- U.S. EV sales slumped in Q4 2025, and General Motors’ decision to ramp down EV production reduced Aspen’s manufacturing absorption, sharply compressing margins.
- Management expects EV demand to recover but now sees the production ramp as more measured.
- Gross margin in Q4 was significantly hurt by lower production volumes and several discrete year-end items that inflated expenses.
- Management noted temporarily elevated costs equal to 48% of revenue in the quarter, alongside multiple one-time adjustments.
- The quarter absorbed a $3 million bad debt expense tied to a customer solvency issue, highlighting credit risk from concentrated customer exposure in emerging EV markets.
- Energy Industrial revenue of $102.2 million in 2025 was mainly baseload maintenance work and limited LNG activity, marking a step down from the subsea-heavy record years of 2023 and 2024.
- Management estimated a $30–$35 million revenue gap versus earlier peaks for the Energy Industrial segment.
- Aspen guided to Q1 2026 as the seasonal low point, with revenue expected between $35 million and $40 million and adjusted EBITDA between negative $13 million and negative $10 million.
- For 2026, management expects sequential revenue growth driven by roughly 20% expansion in Energy Industrial, $10–$15 million from European OEM programs, normalization of GM EV volumes, and beginning to book battery energy storage revenue.
