# Atlanta Cylinders Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-04-18

**Source:** [Czech Investment Grows in Georgia as Cylinder Firm Opens in Hall County - Global Atlanta](https://www.globalatlanta.com/czech-investment-grows-in-georgia-as-cylinder-firm-opens-in-hall-county/)  ·  `page_051995`

## Facts

- Czech-owned provider of gas cylinders held a grand opening of a new distribution facility in Flowery Branch on Tuesday.
- Atlanta Cylinders Inc. is the subsidiary of Cylinders Holding a.s., whose facility in Ostrava, Czech Republic, is among the largest in the world for production of steel cylinders used to hold and transport technical gases, hydrogen, and compressed natural gas.
- The company arrived in Georgia through diplomatic urging and pull factors rather than standard local booster sales pitches.
- Atlanta Cylinders is importing its Czech-made cylinder bundles into a Hall County warehouse located under the same roof as Albaform Inc., a Czech-owned provider of seat frames to automotive suppliers around the South.
- The facility is located at 5405 Rafe Banks Drive in Flowery Branch, Georgia.
- Albaform CEO and owner Monika Vintrlikova, who also serves as the honorary consul of the Czech Republic in Georgia, welcomed attendees to the reception.
- Monika Vintrlikova and her husband Jan Vintrlik started Albaform’s U.S. journey at that brick building exactly 10 years ago.
- Czech trailblazers in Georgia like Albaform and Silon LLC in Peachtree City are helping sell the state back in their home country, prompting more companies to take a serious look.
- Monika Vintrlikova stated a goal of bringing more Czech direct investment to Hall County.
- Miloslav Stašek, Czech ambassador to the United States, made his fourth visit to Georgia in recent months and has become a proponent of the state’s business environment after meeting with Gov. Brian Kemp and others.
- The ambassador noted extremely friendly conditions make the metro area a prime hub for firms breaking into the U.S. and that they are searching for synergies among Czech companies.
- Albaform uses industrial gases in its robotic welding processes and supplies products to larger assembly plants, overlapping with Cylinder Holding as they explore a business partnership beyond warehouse renting.
- Cylinders Holding CEO and Chairman Jan Světlík said entering the U.S. market has been a long-held objective since the company started selling here in 1996.
- After a 2008 mishap in China where thieves stole the company’s designs, Cylinders Holding swore off that country and sought to break into the U.S. to boost market share and be closer to customers.
- Ambassador Stašek had known the company executives for years and began discussing opening doors for the Czech firm in Georgia before taking his posting.
- Consolidation in the gas cylinder industry has left remaining domestic players with little incentive to innovate, putting the U.S. behind on technology, according to Mr. Světlík.
- Atlanta Cylinders hopes to partner with domestic manufacturers to provide cylinders that are lighter and boast greater capacity, helping them save time and money on fuel and refilling.
- Mr. Světlík stated American manufacturers are not the competition; Chinese manufacturers with government-subsidized pricing are, emphasizing support for remaining U.S. steel cylinder manufacturers.
- The company sells DOT cylinders widely used in the U.S., as well as UN/ISO standard cylinders commonly used in Europe that can be imported into the U.S., which are usually larger and withstand higher internal pressures.
- Cylinder Holding sells these individually or in large modular arrays, adhering to the highest safety standards.
- Mr. Světlík emphasized security as his No. 1 priority to avoid unpleasant surprises.
- Vladislav Smrz, chief operations officer for Atlanta Cylinders, said the company wants to help the industry develop in the U.S. and bring the best technology to move it from the 20th century to the 21st century.
- In addition to cylinders, Atlanta Cylinder offers technology to track and remotely monitor containers with bundles of cylinders.
- While heavy steel extrusion will not happen in the U.S. in the short term, the company is looking to bring lighter manufacturing stateside, including racks that corral cylinders into bundles for transport.
- The company has begun talking with Georgia Tech about educational collaborations and is working closely with Hall County to build a local workforce.
- Tim Evans, vice president at the Greater Hall Chamber of Commerce, noted that the community benefits when companies like Albaform become de facto ambassadors in their home countries about growth experienced in Georgia.
- Hall County has about 60 international subsidiaries, ranging from small headquarters to Kubota Corp., which has 2,500 employees and plans to add 500 more.
- Atlanta Cylinders hopes to deepen its reach into Canada and Mexico from its local operation, serving not only the U.S. but also these neighboring countries.
