# YKK AP America Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Governor Kemp: Building Materials Manufacturer YKK AP America Expands Residential Window Production Capabilities with New Facility in Bibb County](https://www.einpresswire.com/article/586370427/governor-kemp-building-materials-manufacturer-ykk-ap-america-expands-residential-window-production-capabilities-with-new-facility-in-bibb-county)  ·  `page_016205`

## Facts

- YKK AP America Inc. will invest up to $125 million in an expansion that includes a new manufacturing facility and additional machinery and equipment in Bibb County.
- The new facility will create 100 new jobs in addition to the 250 jobs YKK AP currently supports in Macon, Georgia.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced the investment expansion on August 16.
- YKK AP is a technology-oriented manufacturer of commercial façade systems and residential windows and doors.
- YKK AP specializes in architectural products for commercial and residential buildings.
- YKK AP entered the U.S. market in 1991 with a vertically integrated, commercial products factory in Dublin, Georgia.
- YKK AP's headquarters are located in Atlanta.
- YKK AP and its Ontario-based subsidiary Erie Architectural Products employ over 1,200 employees in North America.
- Nearly 900 of YKK AP's employees are located within the State of Georgia.
- Oliver Stepe is the President of YKK AP America Inc.
- The new facility will be located at the Macon-Bibb I-75 Industrial Park.
- The site is a Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) certified site.
- After completion, the company will relocate its operations and employees from its current site on Ocmulgee Boulevard to the new facility.
- The Macon facility will become home to the company’s residential division, which manufactures vinyl windows and doors for builders and homeowners.
- The facility will serve as a model plant for future North American manufacturing facilities.
- Operations at the new facility are expected to begin in early 2024.
- The company is currently hiring for numerous positions.
- Governor Kemp stated that being the No. 1 state for business means ensuring companies already operating in Georgia can grow and thrive.
- Oliver Stepe stated the expansion will introduce further vertical integration of vinyl extrusion, glass, and window production to manufacture high-quality windows and doors.
- The investment builds toward YKK AP's long-term vision to be the leader in the residential window and door market.
- Macon-Bibb County Mayor Lester M. Miller commented on the company being a long-running business partner making another investment in the community.
- Senior Project Manager Candice Scott represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this project.
- The project was completed in partnership with the Macon-Bibb County Industrial Authority and Georgia Power.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson noted that YKK AP opened its first branch in Atlanta in 1986 and established its U.S. manufacturing presence in middle Georgia five years later.
- Companies in advanced manufacturing like YKK AP created the second most new jobs in Georgia last year, according to GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson.
- To earn GRAD certification, a property must undergo due diligence and complete specific certification requirements prior to a visit by a prospective business.
- Through the GRAD program, Georgia has more than 60 industrial certified sites that are ready for fast-track industrial projects.
