## Facts

- Sales reached 20,037 million euros in 2022, up by 16%.
- Accelerated growth in ADAS (up 29% LFL) and electrification (up 32 % LFL).
- Original equipment sales outperformed automotive production in all regions by 3 percentage points worldwide.
- Strong growth in aftermarket sales, up 9% LFL.
- EBITDA margin reached 12.0% of sales and EBIT margin reached 3.2 % of sales.
- Generated free cash flow of 388 million euros.
- Order intake increased by 48% to 32.6 billion euros, confirming growth prospects in electrification and ADAS.
- Reduced greenhouse gas emissions in line with the “CAP 50” carbon neutrality roadmap.
- Proposed a 2022 dividend of €0.38 per share to be presented at the next Shareholders’ Meeting.
- CEO Christophe Périllat stated that all financial objectives set at the beginning of 2022 were achieved despite high inflation, an electronic components shortage, lockdown measures in China, and the conflict in Ukraine.
- Growth in original equipment sales outperformed automotive production across all regions.
- EBIT and EBITDA margins aligned with guidance published in February 2022.
- Free cash flow generation of 388 million euros exceeded expectations.
- Order intake growth included several particularly strategic orders in electrification and ADAS.
- Performance confirms the successful first-year implementation of the Move Up strategic plan.
- Targeting strong year-on-year sales growth in 2023 by leveraging acceleration in electrification and ADAS megatrends.
- Aiming to curb inflation impact through productivity and cost reduction measures combined with price increases passed to customers.
- Targeting 2023 sales between 22 and 23 billion euros.
- Targeting an improved 2023 operating margin between 3.2% and 4.0%.
- Targeting 2023 free cash flow generation of over 320 million euros.
- Expecting a significant improvement in financial performance during the second half of 2023.
