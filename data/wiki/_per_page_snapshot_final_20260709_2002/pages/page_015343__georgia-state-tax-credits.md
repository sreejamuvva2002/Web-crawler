# Georgia State Tax Credits

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-02

**Source:** [Georgia State Tax Credits: Incentives for Business Growth](https://www.fusiontaxes.com/thought-leadership/blog/georgia-state-tax-credits/)  ·  `page_015343`

## Facts

- Georgia offers a wide range of business-friendly tax credits covering job creation, high-wage roles, R&D, film/TV, facility expansion, childcare, retraining, and port activity that can significantly reduce costs.
- Georgia maintains a 5.19% corporate tax rate with single-factor apportionment.
- Georgia has maintained AAA credit ratings for two decades.
- The Job Tax Credit (JTC) rewards job creation in tiered counties or zones, offering $750 to $4,000 per job annually for five years, with unused credits carryable forward up to 10 years and the ability to offset income tax.
- JTC Tier 1 provides ± $4,000 per job per year with a minimum requirement of 2 new jobs.
- JTC Tier 2 provides ± $3,000 for creating 10 jobs.
- JTC Tier 3 provides ± $1,750 for creating 15 jobs.
- JTC Tier 4 provides ± $1,250 for creating 25 jobs.
- The Quality Jobs Tax Credit targets high-value, higher-wage roles, providing $2,500 to $5,000 per quality job per year for up to five years, applying to 100% of corporate income tax with a 10-year carryforward.
- To qualify for the Quality Jobs Tax Credit, a business must create and maintain at least 50 new jobs paying at least 110% of the county’s average wage.
- The R&D Tax Credit encourages innovation by providing credits equal to a portion (usually 10%) of qualified R&D spending over a base amount, offsetting up to 50% of Georgia income tax liability with a 10-year carryforward.
- The Film/TV/Digital Entertainment credit rewards media production spend with up to a 30% credit including logo inclusion, starting with a base credit of 20% of qualified in-state production expenses exceeding $500,000, plus an additional 10% if the production includes the "Made in Georgia" promotional logo.
- The Investment Tax Credit (ITC) reduces upfront facility expansion costs, offering 1–5% for basic equipment and expansions, and 3–8% for recycling, pollution control, or defense-conversion investments, applying against 50% of income tax liability with a 10-year carryforward.
- The ITC is available to manufacturing or telecommunications firms making qualified investments equal to or exceeding $50K–$100K.
- The Child Care Credit allows employers providing or sponsoring childcare to claim 75% of direct costs over several years, with the ability to carry forward unused credits.
- The Retraining Credit supports workforce upskilling by covering half the training cost for approved programs, subject to per-employee limits.
- Businesses frequently fail to claim credits because they do not track qualifying expenses properly throughout the year.
- Claiming the wrong tier or miscalculating wage thresholds can invalidate an entire tax credit claim.
- Many Georgia state tax credits must be applied for on time, and missing documentation deadlines risks losing the credits permanently.
- Failing to map and integrate state incentives with federal benefits results in untapped savings for businesses.
