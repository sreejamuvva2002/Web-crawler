# Archer Aviation Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Archer Aviation Showcases Midnight Aircraft In Atlanta As It Nears Completion Of Its High-Volume Manufacturing Facility In Covington, Georgia - EIN Presswire](https://www.einnews.com/pr_news/732052331/archer-aviation-showcases-midnight-aircraft-in-atlanta-as-it-nears-completion-of-its-high-volume-manufacturing-facility-in-covington-georgia)  ·  `page_001671`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation Inc. provided an update on the construction progress of its high-volume manufacturing facility in Covington, GA.
- The company reaffirmed an expected completion date for the facility in 2024.
- Last week, Archer showcased its Midnight aircraft at Atlantic Aviation’s new terminal at DeKalb–Peachtree Municipal Airport in Atlanta.
- Guests at the showcase included government officials from Georgia’s Office of the Governor, Georgia’s Congressional Offices, the City of Atlanta, Georgia’s State Legislature, the Federal Aviation Administration, and other local governments.
- Archer completed the first build phase earlier this year, which included grading and foundation work.
- The company is nearing completion of phase two, which involves the erection of the exterior walls and roof.
- Phase three (interior fit out) and phase four (equipment load in and power on) are on track to be completed in the coming months.
- Once complete, the facility will be nearly 400,000 square feet.
- The facility will support Archer’s planned production of up to 650 Midnight aircraft annually.
- This production volume would make it one of the largest manufacturing facilities by volume in the aviation industry.
- Archer’s buildout is taking place in close partnership with Stellantis.
- Archer and Stellantis are continuing to progress their plans to mass-produce Archer’s Midnight aircraft together.
- Adam Goldstein, CEO and founder of Archer, stated that the team is tracking towards completing the manufacturing facility by the end of the year.
- Upon completion, Archer will focus on ramping production up to 650 aircraft a year.
- Archer aims to be the first in the eVTOL industry to achieve scaled manufacturing.
- Midnight is an electric vertical take-off and landing (eVTOL) aircraft designed around Archer’s proprietary electric powertrain.
- The Midnight aircraft is designed to offer passengers a sustainable, low-noise, and safe alternative to ground transportation.
- The aircraft travels at speeds up to 150 mph, turning hour-long ground commutes into minutes in the air.
- It is designed for back-to-back flights of 20-50 miles with minimal charge time in between.
- The Midnight aircraft is 100x quieter than a helicopter at cruising altitudes and nearly inaudible from the streets below.
- It features redundant systems across the aircraft, allowing Archer to target similar levels of safety as commercial airliners.
- The aircraft is designed to carry a pilot, up to four passengers, and carry-on luggage.
- Archer is designing and developing key enabling technologies and aircraft to power the next great transportation revolution.
- Archer's goal is for its proprietary technology to deliver unprecedented connectivity to people and places across the most congested cities in the world.
