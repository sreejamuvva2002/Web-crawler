# One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBBA) EV Loan Interest Deduction

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-30

**Source:** [Does the 2026 Tax Deduction for American-Made Vehicles Apply to EVs? - Clean Energy CU](https://www.cleanenergycu.org/resources/blog/obbba-ev-tax-deduction-2026-guide/)  ·  `page_015994`

## Facts

- The American-made tax credit applies to the 2026 EV car loan interest deduction.
- It replaces a one-time credit under the Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) with a recurring electric vehicle loan interest deduction under the One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBBA).
- The IRA offers a single tax credit of up to $7,500 for the purchase of EVs and hybrid vehicles, paid at the time of purchase.
- The new OBBBA tax benefit is a multi-year deduction of up to $10,000 annually that makes the financing of American-made vehicles more affordable.
- The $7,500 Point-of-Sale Credit was a feature of the IRA that allowed EV buyers to transfer their federal tax credit directly to a dealership at the time of purchase.
- This credit effectively reduced the price of an EV by $7,500 on the spot if you met the eligibility requirements (battery, income caps, MSRP limits and North American assembly).
- The credit provision was replaced on September 30, 2025.
- Applicable EVs purchased on or before that date can still claim the credit “retroactively” on 2025 tax returns (filed in 2026) using IRS Form 8936.
- The OBBBA interest deduction is an annual, above-the-line incentive that lowers taxable income by allowing owners to deduct up to $10,000 in loan interest each year through 2028.
- The “Car Loan Interest Deduction” applies to qualifying loans that originated between January 1, 2025, and December 31, 2028.
- To qualify for the deduction, the vehicle must be made in America, and purchased brand-new, after December 31, 2024.
- The deduction is intended for personal use vehicles of middle-income earners.
- Anyone with a modified adjusted gross income (MAGI) up to $100,000 (Single) or $200,000 (married filing jointly) are eligible for the full deduction.
- The benefit decreases by $200 for every $1,000 over these limits up to the ceiling of $150,000 (single) or $250,000 (joint).
- To qualify under the “made in America” rule, the vehicle must have its final assembly in the United States.
- The OBBBA encourages domestic manufacturing, so unlike the one-time POS credit under the IRA, the new deduction does not apply to vehicles manufactured in Canada or Mexico and there are no strict battery requirements.
- A Ford Mustang Mach-E assembled in Mexico qualified for previous credits, but the interest on its loan is not deductible under the OBBBA because it was not assembled in the U.S.
- The Tesla Model 3 is assembled in Fremont, California, so it does qualify.
- Full deduction if MAGI is under $100,000 for single filers; no deduction above $150,000.
- Full deduction if MAGI is under $200,000 for joint filers; no deduction above $250,000.
- The $10,000 cap reduces by $200 for every $1,000 earned over the threshold.
- Vehicle must be brand-new (original use must begin with you); used/CPO vehicles do not qualify.
- The vehicle must have final assembly in the United States.
- Gross Vehicle Weight Rating (GVWR) must be under 14,000 pounds (covers all light-duty EVs/SUVs).
- Must be used primarily for personal use (over 50%); business-only vehicles are excluded.
- Must be a first lien auto loan; leases and “balloon” financing usually do not qualify.
- Loan must have originated between Jan 1, 2025, and Dec 31, 2028.
- There are no MSRP limits for this deduction.
- The most definitive way to check a vehicle’s origin is the Vehicle Identification Number (VIN) on the Safety Compliance Certification Label (silver/white sticker on the driver’s side dashboard or doorframe).
- It explicitly lists the “Final Assembly Point.”
- To verify whether final assembly is in the United States check the NHTSA VIN Decoder or simply look for a VIN starting with 1, 4, 5 or 7.
- Vin numbers starting with 2 (Canada), 3 (Mexico), J (Japan), W (Germany), or S (South Korea), do not qualify for the OBBBA interest deduction.
- Ford qualifying VINs start with 1 (F-150 Lightning); non-qualifying start with 3 (Mach-E is Mexico); qualifying US plants in Dearborn, MI.
- Rivian qualifying VINs start with 7 (All R1 & R2 models); all U.S. built; qualifying US plants in Normal, IL.
- Lucid qualifying VINs start with 7 (All Air & Gravity); all U.S. built; qualifying US plants in Casa Grande, AZ.
- GM (Chevy/Cadillac) qualifying VINs start with 1 (Silverado, Blazer, LYRIQ); non-qualifying start with 3 (Equinox EV – Mexico); qualifying US plants in Detroit, MI; Spring Hill, TN.
- Toyota qualifying VINs start with 4 or 5 (Grand Highlander/Sienna); non-qualifying start with J (Japan-made imports); qualifying US plants in Princeton, IN.
- Honda / Acura qualifying VINs start with 1 or 4 (Prologue, MDX, etc.); non-qualifying start with J or 2 (Japan or Canada); qualifying US plants in Lincoln, AL; Marysville, OH.
- Mercedes-Benz qualifying VINs start with 4 (EQE / EQS SUVs); non-qualifying start with W (Germany); qualifying US plants in Vance, AL.
- Subaru qualifying VINs start with 4; non-qualifying start with J (Japan); qualifying US plants in Lafayette, IN.
- Volkswagen qualifying VINs start with 1 (ID.4); non-qualifying start with W (Germany); qualifying US plants in Chattanooga, TN.
- Hyundai / Kia qualifying VINs start with 5 (Ioniq 5, EV9, Ioniq 9); non-qualifying start with K (South Korea); qualifying US plants in Bryan County, GA; West Point, GA.
- Tesla qualifying VINs start with 1, 5, or 7; non-qualifying start with L (China); qualifying US plants in Fremont, CA; Austin, TX.
- At the dealership you can look for a Monroney sticker on the window of the vehicle, specifically the section titled “Parts Content Information.”
- It will explicitly state: “Final Assembly Point:” followed by the city and state (e.g., Normal, IL or Austin, TX).
- For the 2026 deduction, this must be a U.S. location.
- Required documents to claim the EV interest deduction include Mortgage Interest Statement, IRS Form 1098-V: Proof of the annual interest you paid.
- Your lender is required to send you this form by January 31st if you paid more than $600 in interest.
- Additional Deductions, IRS Form 1040, Schedule 1-A: Establishes vehicle identity.
- This is where you will list the deduction and your 17-digit Vehicle Identification Number (VIN) to qualify under the “Made in America” eligibility requirements.
- Monroney Sticker (Window Sticker): Proves the “Final Assembly” was in the USA. Keep a copy (or photo) of the original window sticker as the IRS may ask for this in an audit.
- Bill of Sale / Purchase Agreement: Confirms you are the original owner (first use) and that the vehicle was purchased brand-new.
- Signed Loan Agreement: Proves the loan is a first lien that was started after December 31, 2024.
- Title/Registration: Ownership documentation that proves the vehicle is registered for personal use.
- If you refinanced your EV loan in 2026, you would also need the closing statements for the original and the new loan.
- You can only deduct interest on the portion of the new loan used to pay off the original purchase principal.
- By prioritizing American-made vehicles, the OBBBA ensures that qualifying cars use domestic supply chains, making production more efficient and less vulnerable to global shipping disruptions.
- It was designed to incentivize “onshoring”—bringing manufacturing jobs back to American soil.
- By tying interest deduction to U.S.-assembled EVs, the government is essentially rewarding you for supporting the domestic clean energy economy.
