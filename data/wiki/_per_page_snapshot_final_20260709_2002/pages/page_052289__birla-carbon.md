# Birla Carbon

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- Birla Carbon is moving forward with a $1 billion plant in South Carolina to produce synthetic graphite.
- Synthetic graphite is an anode material for lithium-ion batteries.
- The Indian firm received a $150 million grant for the project from the US Department of Energy.
- The grant was awarded in September.
- Birla Carbon will use a new manufacturing process that consumes less energy than conventional graphite production methods.
- Firms in China produce nearly 70% of the world’s synthetic graphite, according to Benchmark Mineral Intelligence.
- Chinese firms process virtually all natural graphite materials, according to Benchmark Mineral Intelligence.
- The Chinese government imposed restrictions on graphite exports last year.
- Companies in the US produce about 5% of the world’s synthetic graphite.
- US companies produce no natural graphite.
- Several other firms are trying to increase US graphite production.
- Anovion Technologies began building an $800 million synthetic graphite plant in Georgia last year.
- Epsilon Advanced Materials is planning a $650 million synthetic graphite plant in North Carolina.
- Firms are investing in natural graphite processing plants in Alabama, Louisiana, and other locations.
