# UL Solutions

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** research_and_development  ·  **Published:** 2023-06-05

**Source:** [UL Solutions Breaks Ground at North America Advanced Battery Laboratory in Auburn Hills, Michigan | UL Solutions](https://www.ul.com/news/ul-solutions-breaks-ground-north-america-advanced-battery-laboratory-auburn-hills-michigan)  ·  `page_060400`

## Facts

- On June 5, 2023, UL Solutions officially began construction of its North America Advanced Battery Laboratory in Auburn Hills, Michigan.
- The facility is located near the center of the United States automotive industry.
- The laboratory will help enable electric vehicle and industrial battery original equipment manufacturers and their suppliers with shorter development cycles, faster time-to-market, and the ability to be more competitive in the global marketplace.
- Groundbreaking attendees included U.S. Sen. Debbie Stabenow (D-MI), U.S. Representative Haley Stevens (D-MI), Michigan Gov. Gretchen Whitmer, Quentin Messer, Jr. (chief executive and economic competitiveness officer – chair, Michigan Strategic Fund/Michigan Economic Development Corporation), David Coulter (county executive, Oakland County), and Kevin McDaniel (mayor of the City of Auburn Hills).
- Jennifer Scanlon, president and CEO of UL Solutions Inc., stated the company is thrilled to be part of the region’s innovation ecosystem and sit side-by-side with world’s top OEMs, electric vehicle and industrial battery product manufacturers, and high-tech companies focusing on the future of mobility and electrification.
- Sen. Stabenow stated Michigan workers are the best in the world and the investment will create jobs while helping solidify Michigan’s position at the forefront of advanced manufacturing and mobility.
- Rep. Stevens noted that once completed, the facility will be one of the most extensive battery testing and engineering laboratories in North America and expressed pride that it chose Michigan.
- Gov. Whitmer stated UL Solutions’ investment proves their work to grow Michigan’s economy, invest in people, and build the future of cars, chips, and batteries is paying off.
- Gov. Whitmer added the laboratory will test electric vehicle batteries being built in Michigan so they can safely go into Michigan-made cars and trucks.
- Gov. Whitmer stated Michigan won this investment over several other states because of its strong workforce and leading battery and automotive manufacturers.
- Mayor McDaniel stated the City of Auburn Hills, in coordination with the State of Michigan, has made new mobility, specifically the automotive and EV battery sectors, a priority driver for the local economy.
- Supply chain issues, energy independence, sustainability goals, and local innovation and manufacturing velocity are key factors driving an influx of public and private investment to establish the foundation of a new domestic supply chain for EV and industrial batteries.
- The U.S. government reported that EV suppliers and OEMs have announced $36 billion USD of investment to meet growing demand.
- The laboratory will house dedicated UL Solutions experts and equipment capable of providing thermal fire propagation, electrical, mechanical abuse, and environmental testing.
- Testing will be based on various UL and International Electrotechnical Commission standards, United Nations goals and initiatives, and Society of Automotive Engineers and OEM specifications.
- UL Solutions plans to open its North America Advanced Battery Laboratory in mid-2024.
- When completed, the facility will be one of North America’s most extensive battery testing and engineering laboratories.
- UL Solutions is a global leader in applied safety science that delivers testing, inspection, certification services, software products, and advisory offerings across more than 100 countries.
- Groundbreaking attendees also included Kathy Seegebrecht (regional vice president, Americas), Jeff Smidt (senior vice president, Industrial Testing, Inspection and Certification), Milan Dotlich (vice president and general manager, Energy and Industrial Automation), and Mary Joyce (vice president and general manager, New Mobility).
