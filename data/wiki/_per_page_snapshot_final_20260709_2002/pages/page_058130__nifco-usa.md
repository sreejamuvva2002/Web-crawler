## Facts

- OEMs favor plastic injection molding due to the ability to manufacture abundant plastic components with high-quality consistency.
- The company has over 50 years of experience in plastic injection molding.
- Nifco maintains dedicated tooling engineers for in-house designs and processing engineers to develop efficient processes for high-quality products.
- A diversified, cross-functional team manufactures components to individual customer needs.
- Twin Shot (also called dual-shot, double-shot, multi-shot, 2k, or twin-shot injection) is a process that runs different raw material resins in one press.
- Nifco utilizes two-shot injection molding technology to enhance product aesthetics and functionality while meeting stringent customer requirements for water sealing, automation, and multi-color products.
- Insert Molding involves shooting plastic over objects like metal rings or threaded bushings.
- Robotics and bowl feeders are used to automate the loading of inserts for high-quality products and decreased cycle times.
- Automated assembly is a key process in American manufacturing, and Nifco has mastered building and coding automation equipment in-house to ensure accuracy, adjustability, and quick turnaround.
- The engineering team possesses keen knowledge of manufacturing software and robot design.
- Automation capabilities enable high-quality checks, faster production, and human-free interaction.
- Nifco offers a comprehensive range of welding techniques including Vibration Welding, Ultrasonic Welding, Laser Welding, and Manual welding.
- Skilled engineers operate these diverse welding styles to ensure proficiency in their application.
- Manual assembly capabilities involve skilled technicians carefully joining mating components by hand after production to prioritize quality.
- A dedicated in-house design team works on custom designs for customers.
- Mold Flow simulation software is used to troubleshoot plastic flow problems and address manufacturing challenges like part warpage, cooling efficiency, and cycle time reduction.
- FEA (finite element analysis) is a simulated situation that helps engineers understand part stress and optimize the design process.
- An in-house lab provides capabilities to perform a wide variety of tests on products to create efficient products.
