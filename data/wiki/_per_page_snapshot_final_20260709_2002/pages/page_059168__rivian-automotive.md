# Rivian Automotive

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-05-03

**Source:** [Here's Why Rivian Stock Jumped Tuesday Morning | The Motley Fool](https://www.fool.com/investing/2022/05/03/heres-why-rivian-stock-jumped-tuesday-morning/)  ·  `page_059168`

## Facts

- Rivian stock jumped Tuesday morning on news related to a planned second manufacturing facility.
- In December 2021, Rivian announced it planned to build a $5 billion manufacturing facility -- its second -- in Georgia.
- Local residents initially pushed back against the facility plan.
- State and local officials have agreed to provide Rivian with $1.5 billion in incentives, including tax breaks, to move the project forward.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp stated the plant is expected to eventually employ more than 7,500 workers.
- Governor Kemp called the plant the largest economic development project ever undertaken in the state.
- Governor Kemp defended the incentive package by citing a 2006 offer to automaker Kia worth about $570 million in today's dollars that resulted in great-paying jobs in rural Georgia.
- Rivian is currently ramping up production and expects to produce only 25,000 vehicles this year due to supply chain challenges.
- Georgia included protections in the incentive offer requiring Rivian to succeed over the long term to realize the benefits.
- Rivian has until 2028 to fulfill 80% of its employment goal and investment plans.
- Rivian must maintain its employment goal and investment plans through 2047.
- Failure to meet the requirements will subject Rivian to state claw-back provisions.
- Rivian has yet to prove it can manufacture at scale and fully execute its plans.
