# BlueOval SK joint venture

**Type:** Joint Venture  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[battery-cell-manufacturing|Battery Cell Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** United States

## Overview

A U.S. battery manufacturing joint venture between South Korean battery maker SK On and Ford Motor Company, launched in 2022 to build large-scale EV battery capacity. In December 2025 the partners announced they will unwind the JV, with SK On taking full ownership of the Tennessee plant and Ford assuming the two Kentucky plants.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Battery manufacturing for electric vehicles in the United States, including facilities that affect Georgia's EV supply chain through SK On’s existing plant in Commerce, GA.

## Key facts

- The split will give SK On full ownership of the Tennessee plant and Ford full ownership of two Kentucky plants.
- SK On’s standalone battery production capacity in the US is expected to rise from 22 GWh to 67 GWh annually after the transfer.
- The Tennessee plant will become SK On’s second wholly owned battery factory in the US, alongside SK Battery America in Commerce, Georgia.
- Despite the dissolution of the joint venture, SK On and Ford will continue their supplier relationship, with the Tennessee plant supplying batteries for Ford’s next-generation electric vehicles.
- The original BlueOval SK project was announced as an $11 billion investment with up to 120 GWh annual capacity, sufficient for more than one million EVs per year.
- Ford postponed its investment in the second Kentucky plant in 2023 because of weaker-than-anticipated demand for EVs across the US.
- Nissan was allowed to use Ford’s Kentucky battery production earlier this year.
