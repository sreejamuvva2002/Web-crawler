# State of Georgia Economic Growth

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [McKinsey study flags threats to Georgia's economic growth](https://www.ajc.com/business/economy/mckinsey-study-flags-threats-georgia-economic-expansion/fRfJjbIFhu0QZt4q4doalO/)  ·  `page_013232`

## Facts

- A study by McKinsey & Company warns that Georgia cannot afford to ignore dangerous economic trends often masked by positive news.
- Georgia became the ninth-largest state economy by 2017, rising from 17th place in 1977.
- Atlanta fueled most of Georgia's recent expansion as one of 11 U.S. “superstar cities.”
- Atlanta experienced a 3.4% average annual economic growth rate between 2012 and 2017.
- The McKinsey study identifies transportation, education, and healthcare as the most pressing weaknesses threatening Georgia's continued economic growth.
- Steve Reis, a senior McKinsey partner in Atlanta and co-author of the study, stated that the past is not a guaranteed guide to the future and emphasized the need to address identified shortcomings.
- The report highlights workforce skills shortages and infrastructure gaps as critical threats to sustaining economic expansion.
