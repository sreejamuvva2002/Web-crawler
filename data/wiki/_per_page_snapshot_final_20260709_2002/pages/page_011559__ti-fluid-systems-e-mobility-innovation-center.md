# TI Fluid Systems e-Mobility Innovation Center

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** thermal_management  ·  **Published:** 2026-05-01

**Source:** [TI Fluid Systems opens new Michigan facility for development of thermal management systems | Automotive Powertrain Technology International](https://www.automotivepowertraintechnologyinternational.com/news/facility-development/ti-fluid-systems-opens-new-michigan-facility-for-development-of-thermal-management-systems.html)  ·  `page_011559`

## Facts

- TI Fluid Systems, a manufacturer of thermal management and fluid handling systems, has officially opened its new e-Mobility Innovation Center (eMIC) in Auburn Hills, Michigan.
- The 4,460m2 facility is designed to foster collaboration with customers and simplify the development of thermal management systems for hybrid, plug-in hybrid and battery-electric vehicles in North America.
- The Auburn Hills eMIC incorporates TI’s Core Competency model, integrating virtual engineering, designing, processing, prototyping, product testing and vehicle testing into a single location.
- The company states this integration will reduce the development time for prototype thermal management components and systems from six months to just two weeks.
- The facility can reportedly achieve system-weight reductions of up to 60%.
- The facility can reportedly achieve overall cost savings of up to 30%.
- The facility is equipped with technology for rapid prototyping, including 3D printing and injection molding.
- The facility includes two vehicle lifts and 42 electric vehicle charging stations.
- This setup aims to help customers navigate the complex transition to zero-emission vehicles.
- Hans Dieltjens serves as president and CEO of TI Fluid Systems.
- Hans Dieltjens stated that the all-new Auburn Hills eMIC represents a significant step forward in automotive thermal management development in North America, enabling faster development, collaboration, and innovation crucial for hybrid, plug-in hybrid, and battery-electric vehicles.
- Since 2022, TI Fluid Systems has opened five eMICs worldwide across key regions for automotive and e-mobility development: China, Germany, Japan, South Korea, and the USA.
- Dieltjens noted that constructing five eMICs in key e-mobility development regions underscores the company's commitment to supporting customer competitiveness in a fast-changing automotive landscape.
- He added that an innovative approach to thermal management impacts the efficiency, performance, and cost of electric vehicles.
