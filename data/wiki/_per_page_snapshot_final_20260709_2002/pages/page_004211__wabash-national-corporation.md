# Wabash National Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2017-08-08

**Source:** [Wabash National Corporation Announces Agreement to Acquire](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2017/08/08/1081949/0/en/Wabash-National-Corporation-Announces-Agreement-to-Acquire-Supreme-Industries-Inc.html)  ·  `page_004211`

## Facts

- Wabash National Corporation is a diversified industrial manufacturer and North America’s leading producer of semi-trailers and liquid transportation systems.
- Wabash National entered into a definitive agreement to acquire all outstanding shares of Supreme Industries, Inc. in a cash tender offer for $21 per share.
- The acquisition represents an equity value of $364 million and an enterprise value of $342 million.
- Supreme Industries was founded in 1974 and is the second largest U.S. manufacturer of truck bodies with 2016 sales of $299 million.
- Supreme primarily manufactures light- and medium-duty truck bodies at seven facilities throughout the United States.
- Dick Giromini, Wabash National’s chief executive officer, stated that Wabash formally entered the final mile space in 2015 with the launch of dry and refrigerated truck bodies.
- The acquisition combines Supreme’s medium- and light-duty commercial vehicle portfolio, distribution network, and regional manufacturing locations with Wabash National’s advanced composite technologies, lean manufacturing expertise, engineering proficiency, and supplier relationships.
- Supreme provides Wabash National with reduced dependence on dry van trailer demand, reduced cyclicality, and new segments for growth.
- Wabash National intends to preserve Supreme’s heritage of excellence in serving customers while accelerating its organic truck body growth initiative.
- Dick Giromini noted that combining with Supreme allows Wabash to accelerate organic growth with innovative DuraPlate®, honeycomb panel and molded structural composite (MSC) truck bodies, as well as provide a broader conventional product offering.
- Wabash National expects to deliver at least $20 million in annual run-rate cost synergies by 2021, primarily driven by corporate and procurement expenditures and operational improvement savings.
- Wabash National expects to achieve significant incremental revenue opportunities over time that neither company could obtain on a standalone basis.
- Mark Weber, Supreme Industries’ Chief Executive Officer, commented that combining with Wabash will enhance their ability to innovate more quickly and create more value for customers.
- Wabash formed an acquisition subsidiary, Redhawk Acquisition Corporation, to commence the tender offer.
- Following the tender offer, Wabash expects to consummate a merger of Redhawk Acquisition Corp. and Supreme, converting remaining shares into the right to receive the same cash price per share.
- The transaction is subject to customary closing conditions, including acquiring a majority of Supreme’s outstanding shares and expiration/termination of the Hart-Scott-Rodino Antitrust Improvements Act waiting period.
- The closing of the acquisition is expected to occur no later than the fourth quarter of 2017.
- The transaction is not subject to any financing condition, with Wabash obtaining committed bridge financing from Morgan Stanley Senior Funding, Inc. and Wells Fargo Bank.
- The purchase price is expected to be funded by a combination of notes and cash.
- Supreme’s board of directors approved the agreement and recommended that stockholders accept the offer.
- Baird acted as financial advisor to Supreme, and Haynes & Boone provided legal advice.
- Morgan Stanley & Co. LLC acted as financial advisor to Wabash, and Hogan Lovells provided legal advice.
- Wabash National will conduct a conference call to discuss the transaction on August 9, 2017, at 8:00 a.m. EDT.
- Wabash National Corporation was established in 1985 in Lafayette, Indiana.
- Wabash National manufactures dry freight and refrigerated trailers, platform trailers, bulk tank trailers, dry and refrigerated truck bodies, truck-mounted tanks, intermodal equipment, aircraft refueling equipment, structural composite panels and products, trailer aerodynamic solutions, and specialty food grade and pharmaceutical equipment.
- Wabash National’s products are sold under brand names including Wabash National®, Beall®, Benson®, Brenner® Tank, Bulk Tank International, DuraPlate®, Extract Technology®, Garsite, Progress Tank, Transcraft®, Walker Engineered Products, and Walker Transport.
- Supreme Industries’ customers include national rental fleets, national and regional leasing companies, truck dealers, and fleet operators.
