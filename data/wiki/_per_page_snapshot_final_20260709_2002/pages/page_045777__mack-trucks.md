# Mack Trucks

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-02-09

**Source:** [Mack Trucks to invest $14.5 million, add 51 jobs in Roanoke County - Cardinal News](https://cardinalnews.org/2024/02/09/mack-trucks-to-invest-14-5-million-add-51-jobs-in-roanoke-county/)  ·  `page_045777`

## Facts

- Mack Trucks is investing $14.5 million to expand its operations.
- The company is adding 51 new jobs at its Roanoke County location.
- The expansion adds 72,000 square feet to the Dixie Caverns-area factory.
- Gov. Glenn Youngkin announced the investment and job additions during a plant visit on Friday afternoon.
- Gov. Youngkin and his wife Suzanne Youngkin broke ground on the expansion alongside company and government officials.
- Mack Trucks is part of Volvo Group, which opened the Roanoke County location in 2020.
- Volvo operates a heavy-duty truck plant in Pulaski County.
- Gov. Glenn Youngkin stated that Mack committed to hiring 250 people four years prior and currently has 420 employees working at the site, with 51 more to be added.
- The expansion is expected to begin in April and conclude by late 2025.
- The plant's total size will increase to 352,000 square feet following the expansion.
- The project will add capacity for two existing medium-duty truck lines: MD-6 and MD-7.
- The project will also add capacity for Mack Trucks' emerging medium-duty electric truck line.
- Rich Million, Mack Trucks' senior vice president of strategy and business development, cited strong dealer and customer reception as the reason for the expansion.
- Million noted that customers want to buy more trucks and praised the local community partnership and talented workforce.
- Gov. Youngkin approved a $255,000 grant for the project from the Commonwealth’s Opportunity Fund.
- Roanoke County offered an incentive package totaling $842,420.
- The Virginia Jobs Investment Program will support employee training for the project.
- Mack is eligible to receive state benefits from the Major Business Facility Job Tax Credit for new full-time jobs created by the project.
- A Roanoke Regional Partnership analysis estimates the operational increase will add an annual economic impact of $72 million in Roanoke County and the surrounding region.
- Phil North, Roanoke County Board of Supervisors Chairman, credited county Administrator Richard Caywood and economic development director Megan Baker for securing the project.
- The Virginia Economic Development Partnership and the Roanoke Regional Partnership worked together to secure the project.
- Phil North stated the expansion reaffirms the county's strategy to foster high-value advanced manufacturing and showcases the region as an attractive destination for growing businesses.
