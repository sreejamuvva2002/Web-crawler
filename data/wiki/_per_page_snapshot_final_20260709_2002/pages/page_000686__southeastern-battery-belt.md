# Southeastern Battery Belt

## Facts

- Georgia leads the country in post-Inflation Reduction Act clean energy factory investments with over $17 billion, according to a Canary Media analysis.
- South Carolina ranks second with $9.3 billion in clean energy factory investments, followed by Michigan with $8.1 billion.
- The Biden administration is betting on renewing the American industrial machine to help decarbonize the U.S. electric grid by 2035 and power a mass transition to electric vehicles.
- Incentives in the Inflation Reduction Act spurred the emergence of a new Battery Belt spanning Michigan, Indiana, Ohio, Kentucky, Tennessee, Georgia, and the Carolinas.
- Former Michigan Governor Jennifer Granholm picked EV and battery production as a strategic growth industry over a decade ago to help Michigan recover from the Great Recession.
- The Kings Mountain Mine in North Carolina was opened by Foote Mineral Company in 1938, tapping the Carolina Tin-Spodumene Belt across Cleveland and Gaston counties.
- Historically, the Kings Mountain mine produced more hard-rock lithium than pretty much anywhere else in the world before shutting down in 1988.
- Livent's Hallman-Beam Mine near Bessemer City, North Carolina, shuttered in 1996.
- Albemarle, which now owns the Kings Mountain mine, is conducting economic-feasibility studies to determine whether to revive the long-dormant operation.
- Newcomer Piedmont Lithium hopes to open a brand-new mine in the area, but its initial public outreach campaign failed due to neighbor concerns over mining explosions and potential destabilization of local water wells.
- Global lithium suppliers Albemarle and Livent still process lithium near Charlotte, North Carolina.
- Energy Secretary Jennifer Granholm stated that the U.S. should do more to extract critical minerals domestically but emphasized that new mining operations need reforms for better environmental practices and respect for local residents, especially tribal communities.
- Former Charlotte Mayor Jennifer Roberts noted that state regulators often lack resources to police industrial polluters but highlighted Vulcan Materials as a good neighbor that set aside wildlife habitat and coordinated blasting with residents.
- South Korea's SK Corporation opened a $2.6 billion battery factory in Commerce, Georgia, in early 2022, before the Inflation Reduction Act was passed.
- SK ships batteries from its Georgia plant to Volkswagen's electric ID.4 production site in Chattanooga, Tennessee, and supplies Ford's F-150 Lightning.
- SK makes enough batteries at its Georgia facility to power 300,000 EVs annually, according to company statements.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp praised SK in January for hitting employment targets two years ahead of schedule, with 2,600 people already working there and the number expected to hit 3,000 by year's end.
- SK is one of the largest single-site employers in Georgia.
- Norwegian company Freyr opted to fast-track development of a second factory in Coweta County, southwest of Atlanta, to take advantage of Inflation Reduction Act benefits for U.S.-based battery production.
- Freyr is making a $2.6 billion investment in Georgia in partnership with Koch Industries.
- Freyr expects to produce 2.5 gigawatt-hours of batteries in 2025, ramping to 38 gigawatt-hours shortly thereafter, and should employ more than 700 people.
- Freyr licensed battery technology developed by MIT spinout 24M, which advertises a streamlined production technique that lowers factory construction costs.
- SK will team up with Hyundai to build a $5 billion factory in northern Georgia.
- SK is also partnering with Ford to build $5.7 billion factories near Memphis and in southern Kentucky.
- Georgia emerged as a hub of U.S. battery manufacturing by courting SK and other Korean investors, including appointing an economic development lead dedicated specifically to attracting South Korean business.
- Yoonie Kim started her job as Georgia's economic development lead for South Korea in 2006 and helped facilitate $11.5 billion of Korean investment in the state in 2022 alone.
- The Kemp administration courts EV employers with tax incentives and Georgia's state-funded Quick Start worker training program.
- Volkswagen ramped up ID.4 assembly last year, hiring 1,000 new employees and adding a third shift at its Chattanooga plant, with another 500 hires expected that year.
- The VW ID.4 electric SUV flagship is largely sourced from within the U.S., with steel from Alabama and Ohio, interior parts from Indiana and South Carolina, electronics from Kentucky and North Carolina, and batteries from SK's Georgia base.
- Tennessee is home to approximately 900 auto parts suppliers, a network that increasingly includes batteries to reduce global shipping disruption risks.
- Roughly half the U.S. population lives within 650 miles of Nashville, giving Tennessee a geographical logistics advantage.
- Memphis serves as a historic logistics hub halfway up the Mississippi River and has evolved into a railroad, trucking, and global air freight hub thanks to FedEx.
- GM makes electric Cadillacs at a facility in Spring Hill, Tennessee, near Ford's BlueOval City.
- BMW added EV production to its plant in Spartanburg, South Carolina.
- Hyundai and Kia are building new sites in Georgia.
- Electric truck startup Rivian looked to set up shop outside Atlanta, but the state's incentive package has been tied up in court due to a case brought by residents opposing the project.
- Central North Carolina has attracted new factories from Toyota and Vietnamese firm VinFast.
- Tennessee recently passed a constitutional amendment to perpetuate right-to-work status, with voters approving it by a nearly 40-point margin.
- Newly elected United Auto Workers President Shawn Fain has spoken about the need to ensure union representation at new EV plants in the region.
- The first operational battery recycler in the Southeastern Battery Belt, Ascend Elements, opened in spring 2023 in Covington, Georgia.
- Ascend Elements uses a two-story-tall wet shredder to process used battery modules and scrap materials from nearby factories like SK's.
- The wet shredder drops batteries through an airlock into an oxygen-purged space to prevent sparks from grinding metal while batteries contain flammable liquid electrolyte.
- Ascend cooks off the liquid electrolyte in a rotating kiln, condenses it, cleans it up, and sells it back to battery makers.
- Ascend separates remnants by density and particle size, producing aluminum, copper, magnetic bits, and black mass containing electrode metals in 1,000-kilogram sacks.
- Ascend sells its black mass back to battery manufacturers for processing into proprietary recipes.
- Ascend is breaking ground on a $1 billion factory in southern Kentucky that will use a hydro-to-cathode technique to pull out exact element ratios for each battery maker.
- Ascend just announced a $1 billion deal to supply an unnamed U.S. battery manufacturer.
- Redwood Materials, launched by Tesla co-founder JB Straubel, is building a $3.5 billion recycling plant near Charleston, South Carolina.
- Redwood plans to produce 100 gigawatt-hours per year of cathode active materials and anode foil annually across its U.S. operations by 2025, which would supply approximately 1 million electric vehicles.
- Nearly all anode and cathode production supplying U.S. battery cell manufacturers currently occurs overseas in Asia, according to Redwood Materials spokesperson Daniel Zotos.
- Localizing the supply chain lowers the environmental footprint of manufacturing batteries and helps mitigate supply-chain risks, which will drive down EV costs.
- Recycling cannot catch up to soaring battery demand for at least the next decade, so fresh mining will be needed to keep EV production going.
- The Southeastern Battery Belt already produces scrap from manufacturing lines and will generate waste from retired EV batteries, making recycling a solvable ecological opportunity.
- Southeastern states have become indispensable to the national quest to clean up transportation-related emissions by locking in value-add parts of the EV supply chain, including gigafactories, EV production locations, and advanced lithium-ion recycling leadership.
