# Kia Georgia, Inc.

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-07-12

**Source:** [Kia to Invest Over $200 Million in EV9 Production Expansion - AllOnGeorgia](https://allongeorgia.com/georgia-business/kia-to-invest-over-200-million-in-ev9-production-expansion/)  ·  `page_010167`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Kia will invest more than $200 million at its West Point plant.
- The investment will create nearly 200 new jobs.
- The expansion is to accommodate assembly of the company’s all-electric 2024 EV9 three-row SUV.
- The project involves a production line transformation at the facility.
- The EV9 will become the fifth model assembled at Kia Georgia.
- Existing models assembled at Kia Georgia include the Telluride, Sorento and Sportage SUVs, and the K5 mid-size sedan.
- EV9 assembly is expected to begin in the second quarter of 2024.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Georgia’s longstanding partnership with Kia has led to generational job creation and growth for the West Point area.
- Governor Kemp noted that the project will bring more opportunity to hardworking Georgians.
- Governor Kemp stated the project will help propel Georgia toward its goal of becoming the e-mobility capital.
- Currently, more than 40 percent of Kia vehicles sold in the U.S. are manufactured in West Point.
- The addition of the EV9 to the lineup will further increase the facility’s output.
- The EV9 is the brand’s first three-row EV SUV.
- The EV9 is the first Kia EV to be assembled in North America.
- The EV9 serves as the flagship vehicle for Kia’s global Plan S strategy focused on sustainable mobility leadership.
- Sean Yoon, President & CEO of Kia Motors North America and Kia Motors America, stated that the EV9 has the potential to be another change catalyst for Kia.
- Sean Yoon described the EV9 as the most innovative vehicle they have ever built.
- Sean Yoon noted the EV9 will be a standout in the EV market and on the road.
- Kia Georgia is a certified Georgia Made manufacturer.
- Kia Georgia will continue to utilize the services of Georgia Quick Start.
- Georgia Quick Start is described as the nation’s top-ranked workforce development program.
- Georgia Quick Start operates the Kia Georgia Training Center.
- Quick Start’s comprehensive, customized training services will prepare new employees with skills required for the new assembly line.
- Stuart Countess, President & CEO of Kia Georgia, stated that the plant is preparing to begin production of its first EV.
- Stuart Countess highlighted partnerships developed statewide with the Governor’s Office, the Department of Economic Development, and Georgia QuickStart.
- Stuart Countess highlighted local partnerships with the City of West Point and Troup County.
- City of West Point Mayor Steve Tramell stated that Kia has been an outstanding corporate citizen and community partner for seventeen years.
- Mayor Tramell noted Kia has helped improve livelihoods in West Point by providing employment opportunities and fostering economic growth.
- Amanda Fields, Senior Regional Project Manager, represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The GDEcD partnered with the West Point Development Authority, the City of West Point, Troup County, Georgia Center of Innovation’s Manufacturing and Energy teams, and Georgia Quick Start.
- Pat Wilson, Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner, stated that since Georgia recruited Kia in 2006, Kia has exceeded expectations as a partner by giving back to its adopted community.
- To date, Kia has invested more than $1.9 billion in the state of Georgia.
- Kia Georgia is responsible for more than 14,000 plant and supplier jobs in the region.
- Governor Kemp and state lawmakers recognized January 31 as “Kia Day” to commend Kia’s vehicle manufacturing and impact since the 2006 announcement of a state-of-the-art vehicle assembly facility in West Point.
- From Georgia, Kia ships vehicles to sales markets around the globe.
- Kia's global sales markets include the United States, Canada, Mexico, the Caribbean, Middle East and Pacific nations.
- Georgia has pursued job creation along the entire EV supply chain.
- This pursuit has resulted in more than $22.7 billion in investment and the creation of over 28,400 jobs since 2020.
- Kia Georgia, Inc. is the first manufacturing site in North America for Kia Corporation based in Seoul, Korea.
- Kia Georgia has an annual capacity of 340,000 units.
- Kia Georgia is located on 2,200 acres in West Point, Georgia.
- The facility began mass production on November 16, 2009.
- Kia America is headquartered in Irvine, California.
- Kia serves as the “Official Automotive Partner” of the NBA.
- Kia offers a range of gasoline, hybrid, plug-in hybrid and electrified vehicles sold through a network of over 750 dealers in the U.S.
- The K5, Sportage, Sorento, and Telluride (excludes HEV and PHEV models) are assembled in the United States from U.S. and globally sourced parts.
- The EV9 is expected to be assembled in the United States from U.S. and globally sourced parts.
