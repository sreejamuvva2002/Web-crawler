# TI Fluid Systems

**Type:** supplier_relationship  ·  **Role:** thermal_management  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [TI Fluid Systems Launched 2021 Hyundai Santa FE SUV Hybrid EV - Urja Daily](https://urjadaily.com/ti-fluid-systems-launched-2021-hyundai-santa-fe-suv-hybrid-ev/)  ·  `page_011418`

## Facts

- TI Fluid Systems has launched new fluid handling products for thermal coolant and fuel systems on the 2021 Hyundai Santa FE SUV hybrid electric vehicle (HEV).
- The Hyundai HEV application demonstrates the company’s ability to optimize systems with light weight solutions for improved EV mode efficiency.
- Battery, motor drive, and power electronics thermal management applications typically use complex rubber and aluminum line assemblies for coolant thermal loops.
- TI Fluid Systems has developed in-house a full range of multiple light-weight, multi-layer nylon plastic constructs with associated quick connections for all vehicle operating temperatures.
- In the Hyundai Santa Fe HEV application, TI Fluid Systems collaborated with the OEM to fully switch traditional chassis thermal loops to lightweight optimized materials.
- TI Fluid Systems’ Fuel Tank and Delivery Systems (FTDS) division launched their latest ‘Light Pressurized Tank’ (LPT) pressure resistant plastic fuel tank for the HEV.
- In a HEV, when electric propulsion runs, the engine shuts off and fuel vapor builds pressure internally in the tank.
- A pressure resistant fuel tank is required to maintain in-tank pressure and extend the electric-only driving range during this unique electric only mode.
- TI Fluid Systems’ LPT technology integrates mechanical pin columns during the blow molding manufacturing process to stiffen the upper and lower tank shells.
- Hans Dieltjens, Chief Operating Officer of TI Fluid Systems, stated that the new Hyundai Santa Fe HEV model and its related SUV platform models demonstrate an outstanding opportunity for increased product content per vehicle with light weight solutions for the hybrid electric vehicle segment.
- Hans Dieltjens also noted that TI Fluid Systems is a differentiated supplier that can leverage fluid know-how on the new thermal challenges of electrification.
