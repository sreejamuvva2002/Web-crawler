# Michelin

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-03-18

**Source:** [Michelin unveils new retread, trailer tire monitoring technologies - Truck News](https://www.trucknews.com/transportation/michelin-unveils-new-retread-trailer-tire-monitoring-technologies/1003211288/)  ·  `page_046607`

## Facts

- Michelin North America announced the launch of TreadVision by Michelin Retread Technologies, a new approach to the retread process using artificial intelligence and advanced data insights to enhance retread quality.
- The announcement was made at a press conference during the Technology & Maintenance Council’s annual meeting in Nashville, Tenn.
- Retread Brand Manager Emily Ledbetter said TreadVision is designed not to replace humans, but instead to reduce manual and repetitive tasks.
- A key feature of TreadVision is the TreadEye depth assessment tool, which consistently collects 1,200 measurement points, providing precise insights into tread wear and casing conditions.
- The TreadEye tool helps fleets identify pull points, protect casing integrity, and reduce downtime.
- TreadVision incorporates proprietary automated inspection powered by AI to help identify imperfections and anomalies that may not be detected by traditional methods.
- TreadVision integrates automated tire handling to streamline retread plant operations and help increase turnaround times.
- Janet Foster-Whitley, Michelin’s senior director of enterprise dealer and North America retreading, stated that retreading plays a vital role in helping fleets extend asset life and control operating costs.
- The evolved TreadVision process aims to deliver greater consistency, improved quality, and faster turnaround times.
- Also at the TMC meeting, Michelin Connected Fleet announced the launch of Smart Predictive Tire for trailers hauled by heavy-duty fleets.
- The predictive trailer tire monitoring solution helps fleets proactively manage tire health and improve uptime through real-time pressure and predictive maintenance insights.
- Pilot programs conducted in Europe showed that fleets using Smart Predictive Tire saw up to 80% fewer emergency roadside tire events.
- Fleets using the solution saw up to 9% longer tire life where chronic under-inflation was corrected.
- Fleets saw up to 4% lower fuel consumption when recommended pressures were maintained.
- Damon Newquist, Michelin’s vice president of sales, stated that emergency roadside service is a major pain point for fleets, especially with trailers, and improper inflation is the overwhelming root cause of tire-related events.
- The Smart Predictive Tire solution empowers fleet operators with tools and alerts to address inflation issues before they become critical.
