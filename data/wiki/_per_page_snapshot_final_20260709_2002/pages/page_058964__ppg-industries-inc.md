# PPG Industries Inc

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [PPG Announces $280 Million Facility Expansion in Delaware](https://www.thetranscriptmag.com/post/ppg-announces-280-million-facility-expansion-in-delaware)  ·  `page_058964`

## Facts

- PPG Industries Inc (formerly known as Pittsburgh Plate Glass) announced a $280 million expansion of its manufacturing operations.
- The expansion is located at 760 Pittsburgh Drive in Delaware.
- The project includes the construction of a new 100,000-square-foot facility.
- The expansion will create 100 new full-time jobs and add approximately $8 million in payroll to the local economy.
- New roles will include production operators, lab technicians, and engineers.
- PPG has been located in Delaware since 1965 and currently employs around 450 people.
- The new facility will focus on producing OEM coatings used by car manufacturers.
- The state of Ohio approved an eight-year 1.483 percent Job Creation Tax Credit to support the project.
- Construction is scheduled to begin in late 2026 and is expected to be completed by 2028.
- Alisha Bellezza, PPG's senior vice president of automotive and packaging coatings, stated that the expanded capabilities will help deliver next-generation high-quality, sustainably advantaged coatings technologies as the automotive industry evolves.
- Tom Stipkovich from One Columbus noted that companies like PPG reinvest in Delaware due to its skilled workforce and strategic location.
- The facility produces resins and coatings for car manufacturers, collision repair shops, and packaging companies.
- In August 2025, PPG celebrated its 60th anniversary in Delaware with local and state officials.
- The expansion aligns with the automotive industry's shift toward electric vehicles, which require coatings that cure at lower temperatures due to the absence of exhaust heat.
- PPG developed the ENVIRO PRIME EPIC 300 product for electric vehicles, which reduces bake temperature by 20 degrees Celsius, cuts energy use by up to 13 percent, and lowers greenhouse gas emissions.
- The Delaware facility will expand production capacity for ENVIRO PRIME EPIC 300 to meet increasing demand while maintaining sustainability goals.
- The Ohio Tax Credit Authority approved eight projects totaling approximately $853 million in investment, expected to create 1,320 new jobs and add over $80 million in payroll.
- Among the Ohio projects, AAA Cooper Transportation plans to create 210 jobs across Columbus, Richfield, and Toledo with $14.7 million in new payroll.
- Dainty USA will create 240 jobs in Batavia Township, contributing $15.8 million in payroll.
- The 100 new PPG jobs (including lab technicians, engineers, and business operations roles) present opportunities for Ohio Wesleyan University students majoring in STEM or Business.
- PPG has previously invited OWU students to tour the Delaware facility during National Manufacturing Day events.
- The OWU Career Connection office may expand internship and full-time job opportunities with PPG, though the new facility will not be operational until 2028.
- Construction activity prior to the 2028 opening will bring additional jobs and spending to the local economy.
