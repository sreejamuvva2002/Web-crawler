# Development Authority of Bulloch County

**Type:** Government Program  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[government-support|Government Support]]  ·  **Location:** Bulloch County, Georgia, United States

## Overview

The Development Authority of Bulloch County drives economic growth in the region by attracting and supporting Tier-one automotive suppliers for Hyundai’s Metaplant, facilitating workforce-training programs, and coordinating infrastructure projects that bolster Georgia’s electric-vehicle supply chain.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Facilitates the establishment of Tier-one suppliers for Hyundai’s Metaplant and supports EV battery-component manufacturing through infrastructure, land donation, and workforce training.

## Workforce & partners

- Georgia Industrial Systems and Industrial Robotics Training Center — Operated by Ogeechee Technical College; land donated by the Development Authority. 37,000-sq-ft facility, $19.7 million, slated for completion March 2025; will triple OTC’s training capacity.
- Amazon Industrial Maintenance Training — Training partnership with Ogeechee Technical College. OTC trains Amazon workers on industrial maintenance; trainees travel from across the country.

## Key facts

- Three Tier-one suppliers to the Hyundai Metaplant are starting construction in facilities in southeast Bulloch near I-16, pacing to match or beat the Hyundai completion date of early 2025.
- Joon Georgia is expected to bring 630 jobs and a $317 million investment, producing automotive body parts for the Hyundai plant.
- Ecoplastic will generate 456 jobs with a $205 million investment, making plastic car parts such as bumpers for Hyundai.
- Hanon Systems plans a $40 million investment creating 160 jobs, supplying vehicle air-conditioning systems for Hyundai and other manufacturers.
- Aspen Aerogels, which makes insulation blankets for battery compartments of electric vehicles, will hire an anticipated 250 employees with $325 million in investment; General Motors is a major customer.
- German-based plastics recycler revalyu Resources (US) is building its first U.S. PET-bottle recycling plant in Bulloch County, creating 121 jobs on a $210 million investment.
- Bulloch County has retained all existing industries over the past 20 years with no major shutdowns, highlighting a stable industrial base.
- The county’s agriculture remains significant, ranking 18th statewide in farm-gate value in 2021, with cotton and peanuts as leading crops.
- OTC’s new Georgia Industrial Systems and Industrial Robotics Training Center will triple training capacity and is slated for completion March 2025.
- Amazon sends industrial-maintenance trainees from across the country to Ogeechee Technical College for specialized training.
- Roy Thompson estimates approximately 1,700 jobs are being located and built in Bulloch County, reducing unemployment concerns.
- Bill Tyson notes that industrial development is converting farmland, with about 100,000 acres of former agricultural land now under consideration for factories.
- Statesboro Mayor Jonathan M. McCollar describes the Blue Mile project as a pedestrian-friendly corridor linking Georgia Southern University to downtown, enhancing community connectivity.
