# Club Car

## Facts

- Club Car has been producing small vehicles, from golf carts to cars, for over 65 years.
- The company transitioned to electric vehicles over the last two decades.
- A ribbon-cutting ceremony was held Thursday for a new manufacturing facility expansion.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp attended the facility's ribbon-cutting ceremony.
- According to Governor Brian Kemp, 90 percent of the world's golf and small vehicles are made in Georgia, with 85 percent of those being electric vehicles.
- Electric vehicles and renewable energy are on the rise across the nation, and Thursday marked a celebration for the expansion and new facility for Club Car.
- The new manufacturing plant is located on the outskirts of Columbia County in Appling, Georgia.
- CEO and President Mark Wagner stated that many customers are seeking products to meet green, sustainability, or carbon footprint reduction goals.
- Wagner noted that most sales of their utility vehicles involve replacing gas-powered vehicles.
- The new facility drives job growth in Appling and the CSRA region, with additional hiring planned for the future.
- Governor Kemp noted that Club Car was founded in 1958 and currently employs 1,700 people worldwide.
- Of Club Car's global workforce, 1,300 employees are located in Georgia.
- The project created 40 jobs from its inception to the current point.
- The facility required approximately eight million dollars of investment to reach this stage.
- Club Car has expansion plans inside the new Appling facility.
- The site can produce up to 20 electric vehicles per day.
- The first vehicle from the Appling manufacturing plant was expected to be completed on the day of the ribbon-cutting ceremony.
