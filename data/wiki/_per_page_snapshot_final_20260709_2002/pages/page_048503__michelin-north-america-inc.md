# Michelin North America Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Michelin raising North America prices for fourth time | European Rubber Journal](https://www.european-rubber-journal.com/article/2068729/michelin-raising-north-america-prices-for-fourth-time)  ·  `page_048503`

## Facts

- Michelin North America Inc. (MNA) is raising prices for the fourth time this year, announced on 1 Oct.
- MNA is based in Greenville, South Carolina.
- Effective 1 Dec, the company is increasing prices up to 4.9% for Michelin- and BFGoodrich-brand truck tires and Michelin Retread Technologies and Oliver-brand retread products in the US and Canada.
- Additional pricing details will be provided directly to MNA's North American fleet account customers.
- On 1 Sept, MNA increased prices on selected Michelin- and BFGoodrich-brand passenger and light-truck tires in the US and Canada by up to 5%.
- On 1 July, the South Carolina-based tire maker increased prices on Uniroyal-brand commercial tires and select Michelin-brand off-highway products by as much as 6%.
- The July increase followed a 1-Jan price hike of up to 5% on passenger and light-truck tire prices in the US and Canada.
- The January price increase also applied to Uniroyal-brand consumer tires and Michelin-brand two-wheeler tires sold in the US and Canada.
- Yokohama Tire Corp. is raising prices in the US by as much as 5% on its consumer tires, including passenger, high performance and light-truck tires, effective 1 Nov.
- Pirelli Tire North America LLC raised prices on replacement market passenger and light truck tires in the US on 1 Oct but declined to disclose the amount.
- Pirelli's price increase will vary by line and tire size and will be communicated directly with its customers.
- Pirelli also raised prices by an average of 6% on 1 Jan.
- Cooper Tire & Rubber Co. raised prices on its Cooper- and Roadmaster-brand truck tires to compensate for elevated US government import duties on truck tires from China.
- Cooper's price increases were effective after the start of the second quarter, though the company declined to disclose the scale of the increase.
