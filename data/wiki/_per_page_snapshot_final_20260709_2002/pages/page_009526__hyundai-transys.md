# Hyundai TRANSYS

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2020-12-02

**Source:** [Hyundai to Invest More Than $240 Million in Expansion in Georgia, Create 678 Job - AllOnGeorgia](https://allongeorgia.com/georgia-business/hyundai-to-invest-more-than-240-million-in-expansion-in-georgia-create-678-job/)  ·  `page_009526`

## Facts

- Governor Brian Kemp announced on Tuesday that Hyundai TRANSYS will invest more than $240 million to expand its operations in Georgia.
- The investment will create 678 full-time long-term positions at a new manufacturing facility in the West Point area.
- Hyundai TRANSYS is a world-renowned producer of automotive parts.
- Governor Brian Kemp cited Georgia's No. 1 business climate, robust logistics network, and top-ranked workforce training programs as key factors attracting and retaining global companies.
- Hyundai TRANSYS is the only automobile manufacturing company in the world that specializes in transmissions.
- The new manufacturing facility will be 620,000 square feet and located in West Point near the company's current location on the Kia campus.
- The plant will produce eight-speed transmissions for a global automotive leader.
- Hyundai TRANSYS Georgia Powertrain President Sangkil Jung stated that years of capital investment, employment, and training support in Georgia made it a natural choice for expansion.
- The expansion will bring hundreds of advanced manufacturing jobs, plus management and office staff positions, to the local community.
- The company plans to create 165 supplementary construction handling and inspection jobs.
- The expansion will bring Hyundai TRANSYS' total employment in Georgia to 1,741 jobs.
- West Point Mayor Steve Tramell expressed thrill over Hyundai TRANSYS' long-term investment and the creation of new jobs.
- Mayor Steve Tramell thanked the West Point Development Authority, the State of Georgia, and Troup County for their contributions to making the project a reality.
- Hyundai TRANSYS has been a community partner with West Point and Troup County for over a decade.
- Troup County Chairman Patrick Crews stated that Troup County was honored to be chosen as the location for the new manufacturing facility.
- The significant capital investment is expected to strengthen the local economy and create economic opportunities for residents.
- GDEcD Senior Project Manager Amanda Fields represented the agency's Global Commerce division on the project.
- The project was developed in partnership with the West Point Development Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that companies investing in Georgia become Georgia companies and expressed gratitude for Hyundai TRANSYS' continued investment.
- In 2020, Georgia's international relationships facilitated more than $1 billion in investment and over 2,000 jobs from South Korean companies.
- Georgia is home to more than 200 automotive-related facilities and remains a national leader in advanced manufacturing.
- Georgia has outpaced the United States in 10-year GDP growth in the manufacture of machinery, electrical equipment and components, and fabricated metals.
