# Honda Development & Manufacturing of America, LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-01-19

**Source:** [40 Years and 30 Million Vehicles Made in America:  How did we get here and where are we going?](https://hondanews.com/en-US/honda-corporate/releases/release-4e58b4e0fcd795affa5685a66a1d8bc7-40-years-and-30-million-vehicles-made-in-america-how-did-we-get-here-and-where-are-we-going)  ·  `page_009105`

## Facts

- Honda produced its 30 millionth vehicle in America in early 2023.
- The achievement was based on associates at eight auto manufacturing facilities in Alabama, Georgia, Indiana, and Ohio.
- Honda's first auto plant in America was the Marysville Auto Plant in Ohio, established 40 years ago.
- Honda's total investment in new facilities over the past four decades totals almost $20 billion.
- Honda's vision is to make battery-electric and fuel cell electric vehicles represent 100% of its vehicle sales in North America by 2040.
- Honda plans to produce those electrified vehicles in America within the next few years.
- Bob Schwyn joined Honda 35 years ago (in 1988) as an equipment engineer in the paint department at the Ohio plant, when cumulative production was less than 500,000 vehicles.
- Advanced technology is now the cornerstone of auto production, requiring vast amounts of computing power.
- Modern Honda vehicles contain more computing power in safety sensing and connected car technologies than the entire Ohio plant had when Schwyn joined.
- Honda opened a second auto plant in Ohio.
- Honda established a new auto and engine production facility in Alabama, with engineers from Ohio and Canada helping construct and launch it.
- Over the past 25 years, eight plants in Ohio, Alabama, and Indiana produce some 12 Honda and Acura vehicles, along with engines, transmissions, and hybrid-electric systems.
- The Alabama Auto Plant began production in 2001.
- Lamar Whitaker is a home-grown Honda associate who joined the company in 2001 and succeeded Schwyn as Plant Lead of the Alabama Auto Plant.
- Honda is establishing an EV Hub in Ohio with a $700 million investment to re-tool three plants for producing EVs and EV components.
- The EV Hub will include a new EV battery plant, which is a joint venture investment of $3.5 billion with LG Energy Solution to make batteries for EVs built in Honda auto plants.
- These investments focus on attracting and preparing the next generation of skilled associates for the future manufacturing workforce.
- Bob Schwyn holds the title of Senior Vice President at Honda Development & Manufacturing of America, LLC.
