# Daimler Truck North America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-07-14

**Source:** [Layoffs hit Daimler Truck workers in Gaston County NC | Charlotte Observer](https://www.charlotteobserver.com/news/business/article310622505.html)  ·  `page_053566`

## Facts

- Nearly 600 manufacturing Daimler Truck North America employees are temporarily losing their jobs in the Charlotte region.
- Daimler Truck North America is temporarily laying off 573 workers in Gaston County according to a N.C. Worker Adjustment and Retraining Notification Act report filed Friday with the North Carolina Department of Commerce.
- The mass layoffs will be effective Friday at the Custom Truck Services facility located at 1803 Main St.
- The mass layoffs will also affect the manufacturing plant at 1800 N. Main St.
- Daimler Truck North America spokesman Andrew Johnson stated that 546 employees will be temporarily laid off in Mount Holly.
- An additional 27 employees will be affected at Daimler’s components and logistics site in Gastonia.
- The Gastonia Components and Logistics facility is located at 1400 Tulip Drive.
- Andrew Johnson cited a notable slowdown in new truck orders, particularly in the medium-duty, on-highway, and electric vehicle segments.
- Daimler Truck North Carolina Human Resources Manager Steve Griffin stated the job cuts are due to a sustained reduction in orders and diminished build rate.
- The temporary layoffs are expected to last for an unknown duration.
- Affected employees will receive payment at their regular pay rate for the period between Friday and Sept. 9.
- The majority of affected jobs are truck assemblers, material handlers, office supervisors, technicians, and painters.
- There are about 1,400 employees at Daimler’s Mount Holly truck manufacturing plant.
- The company has approximately 6,500 employees across North Carolina.
- Daimler Truck North America implemented workforce reductions across several facilities to align with current market conditions.
- In total, about 2,000 employees at five sites will be impacted by the temporary layoffs.
- Other impacted locations include Detroit, Portland, Oregon, and Saltillo, Mexico.
- Daimler Truck North America designs, engineers, manufactures, and markets medium- and heavy-duty trucks, school buses, vehicle chassis, and their associated technologies and components under brands including Freightliner, Western Star, and Thomas Built Buses.
- The Mount Holly plant produces medium-duty Freightliner trucks.
- The Gastonia Components and Logistics facility provides stamping, metal fabrication, and sub-assembly of cab and chassis parts.
- Daimler has other plants in the Charlotte region, including Daimler Truck Remarketing Corp. at 2477 Deerfield Road in Fort Mill, S.C.
- Another regional facility is the Cleveland Truck Manufacturing Plant at 11550 Statesville Blvd. in Cleveland, N.C.
- In 2016, the company cut more than 900 jobs due to a diminished build rate.
- Around that same time, Daimler temporarily laid off about 700 employees at its Mount Holly facility and about 550 at its Cleveland plant.
- Daimler Truck North America is a subsidiary of Germany-based Daimler Truck Holding AG.
- The parent company operates 40 production sites globally with more than 100,000 employees.
