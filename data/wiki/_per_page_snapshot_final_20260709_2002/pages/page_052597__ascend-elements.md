# Ascend Elements

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-06

**Source:** [Ascend Elements, SK Ecoplant, and TES Collaborate to Build $65 Million Battery Recycling Plant in Kentucky](https://evmagz.com/ascend-elements-sk-ecoplant-and-tes-collaborate-to-build-65-million-battery-recycling-plant-in-kentucky/)  ·  `page_052597`

## Facts

- US battery-materials company Ascend Elements is teaming up with South Korean firms SK Ecoplant and its subsidiary TES to construct a cutting-edge battery recycling plant in Hopkinsville, Kentucky.
- The project has a budget of $65 million.
- Construction is scheduled to commence in November and anticipated for completion by January 2025.
- The recycling plant will possess the capacity to process up to 12,000 tons of black mass extracted from used EV batteries.
- Ascend Elements will utilize these recycled materials to bolster its battery production efforts.
- The Hopkinsville region is quickly emerging as a hub for advanced battery technologies.
- Ascend Elements is also in the process of constructing a battery materials facility, aptly named Apex 1, within the same area.
- Apex 1 is slated to become operational next year and is projected to produce cathode material and precursors for an impressive 750,000 electric vehicle batteries annually.
- The projected annual output of 750,000 EV batteries represents a threefold increase compared to the company’s 2022 estimates.
- SK Ecoplant holds a majority stake of 64 percent in the new battery recycling plant.
- Ascend Elements will possess a significant 25 percent share, while TES will account for the remaining eleven percent.
- Mike O’Kronley, CEO of Ascend Elements, stated that this venture marks the beginning of an entirely new industry in the United States.
- Mike O’Kronley noted that for every new EV battery gigafactory built, a new battery recycling facility will be needed to process manufacturing scrap and end-of-life batteries.
- Mike O’Kronley described the project as a capital-intensive endeavor where joint ventures between strategically aligned partners are an ideal way to fund new infrastructure projects.
- In March 2023, Ascend Elements marked a significant milestone by commissioning its first commercial-scale lithium-ion battery recycling plant in the US state of Georgia, named ‘Base 1.’
- ‘Base 1’ is regarded as North America’s largest recycling plant for electric vehicle batteries.
- ‘Base 1’ has the capacity to process up to 30,000 tonnes of batteries annually, equivalent to approximately 70,000 e-vehicle batteries.
