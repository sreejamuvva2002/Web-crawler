# Saft America Inc.

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Saft America Inc. - Jacksonville, Florida | Trade and Industry Development](https://www.tradeandindustrydev.com/industry/manufacturing/saft-america-inc-jacksonville-florida-2379)  ·  `page_059253`

## Facts

- In August 2009, Saft was awarded a $95 million grant from the U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) under the American Recovery and Reinvestment Act.
- The funding is designated for a high-volume manufacturing plant to build advanced lithium-ion cells and batteries.
- The manufactured batteries will serve military hybrid vehicles, aviation, smart grid support, broadband backup power, and energy storage for renewable energy applications.
- Alternative energy is a designated high-impact sector in Florida.
- The grant provided an opportunity to leverage federal funding with state and local resources to encourage growth and diversification of Florida’s alternative energy sector.
- Florida and Jacksonville officials worked with Saft long before the DOE grant was awarded and faced significant competition for the project.
- State and local partners secured the project by creating a package containing cash grants, tax incentives (credits, abatements, refunds and exemptions), and workforce training grants.
- Company officials identified Jacksonville's transportation infrastructure and workforce as well-suited for a major battery manufacturing plant.
- The facility is located at the Cecil Commerce Center, continuing the redevelopment of Cecil Field, a former Naval Air Station.
- The total estimated cost of the project is around $200 million.
- The project will bring roughly 279 jobs to the Jacksonville area over a six-year period.
- Construction was scheduled to be completed before the end of 2010.
- Governor Charlie Crist stated that Florida is stepping onto the world stage as a major marketplace for advanced energy technologies.
- Governor Charlie Crist noted that the state-of-the-art manufacturing facility ensures competitive battery solutions will be imminently available to U.S. and world markets from Jacksonville.
