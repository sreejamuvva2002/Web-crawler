# Duckyang Ind. Co. Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2021-05-26

**Source:** [Korean auto manufacturer to open facility in Braselton | News | mainstreetnews.com](https://www.mainstreetnews.com/braselton/news/korean-auto-manufacturer-to-open-facility-in-braselton/article_7f1e1d01-4f1e-5a24-a0c8-989dee93752b.html)  ·  `page_007755`

## Facts

- Duckyang recently announced it will invest $10 million in opening its first U.S. manufacturing facility in Jackson County.
- Duckyang is an electric mobility parts supplier of automotive battery modules and energy storage systems to SK Battery America (SKBA).
- Duckyang's new 230,000 sq. ft. facility will be located at 284 Broadway Ave. in Braselton.
- The facility will create 285 jobs in the area.
- Governor Brian Kemp made an announcement regarding the project on May 20.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Duckyang is creating high-paying, advanced automotive manufacturing jobs for the hardworking Georgians of Jackson County.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Georgia is home to the best workforce training programs in the nation and has remained the number one state for business for the past eight years.
- Duckyang is headquartered in Ulsan, Korea.
- Duckyang supplies cockpit modules to customers around the world including Hyundai, Kia, Renault Samsung Motors, Genesis and Mobis.
- Cockpit modules are integrated auto parts comprised of more than 130 sub-components found near the driver’s seat of a vehicle.
- Company leaders say that opening a facility in Georgia will allow Duckyang to better serve several of its automotive customers within the region.
- Dong-in Son is the CEO of Duckyang Ind. Co. Ltd.
- Dong-in Son stated that entering the U.S. electric vehicle battery market with SKBA will allow cooperation with many other automotive companies, including Hyundai and Kia Motors, that already have a manufacturing base in the U.S.
- It is expected that Duckyang will have business expansion opportunities to supply not only electric vehicle parts but also interior parts, including the cockpit module, door trim panel module, and other flagship parts to global automakers based in the United States.
- The company will be hiring for positions in production, maintenance, logistics and warehouse management.
- Braselton Town Manager Jennifer Scott stated that the Town of Braselton looks forward to welcoming Duckyang to their community and is delighted to see a new manufacturing facility located in their town.
- John Scott is the Director of Economic Development for the Jackson County Area Chamber of Commerce.
- John Scott stated that as the automotive industry continues to move toward electric, great companies like Duckyang are helping Jackson County and the State of Georgia secure a place in the industry’s future.
- Director of Korean Investment Yoonie Kim represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce division on this project.
- The GDEcD worked in partnership with the Jackson County Industrial Development Authority, Georgia Quick Start and Georgia EMC on this project.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Georgia is focused on growing the entire electric mobility supply chain.
- Pat Wilson noted that strong relationships with SK Battery America and other Korean companies, such as Duckyang, are helping grow the supply chain.
- Pat Wilson highlighted that this project is an example of how they help companies bring their suppliers to the state in order to secure their local supply of needed parts.
