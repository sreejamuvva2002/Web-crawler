# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-11-11

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor plays down missed IRA incentives for Georgia plant - The Korea Herald](https://www.koreaherald.com/article/3851604)  ·  `page_009418`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor missed out on United States tax incentives for its new $7.6-billion manufacturing plant -- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America -- in Bryan County, Georgia.
- The missed tax credit did not affect Hyundai Motor's investment in the plant.
- Industry sources reported that Hyundai Motor was not included in the second round allocations of the Qualifying Advanced Energy Project Credit (48C).
- The 48C program is an allocated tax credit funded by President Joe Biden’s Investing in America agenda through the Inflation Reduction Act.
- The Biden administration has assigned a total of $10 billion to the 48C program.
- Selected companies could receive up to a 30 percent tax credit for their investments on American soil.
- A local report estimated that Hyundai Motor could have received up to 480 billion won ($344 million) in tax incentives.
- Construction at the Georgia plant has proceeded smoothly, with early-stage production of electric vehicles beginning at HMGMA in October.
- Hyundai Motor is expected to start full operation of the new plant in the first quarter of next year.
- A Hyundai Motor official stated that while the company applied for the 48C program, it did not expect benefits because the incentives were intended for parts makers, not automakers.
- The same official noted that the 48C program was not a factor in the decision to invest in HMGMA.
- The US Department of Energy announced in April that 35 projects across 20 states were selected for $1.93 billion in the first round allocations of the 48C program.
- The first-round 48C projects were categorized into four areas: clean energy and clean vehicle manufacturing, critical minerals and materials, grid components and modernization, and industrial decarbonization.
- Mobis North America electrified Powertrain, a US subsidiary of Hyundai Motor Group’s auto parts maker Hyundai Mobis, was awarded $57.6 million in tax credits under the program.
- Mobis is set to construct three different EV component plants that will provide parts for the production of Hyundai and Kia electric vehicles.
