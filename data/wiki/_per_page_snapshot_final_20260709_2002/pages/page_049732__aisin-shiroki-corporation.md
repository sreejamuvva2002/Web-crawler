# AISIN SHIROKI CORPORATION

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2016-03-29

**Source:** [AISIN SHIROKI CORPORATION (Formerly Shiroki Corporation) - MarkLines Automotive Industry Portal](https://www.marklines.com/en/top500/aisin-shiroki)  ·  `page_049732`

## Facts

- The Company manufactures functional parts used in automotive interiors and car exterior products.
- Main products include seat recliners, adjusters, window regulators, door locks (functional interior parts), door sashes and moldings (car exterior parts).
- Aisin Seiki Co., Ltd. (currently Aisin) became the parent company in December 2015 via a stock transfer transaction that officially took effect in April 2016.
- Shiroki was delisted from the stock market on March 29, 2016.
- On April 1, 2023, the Company changed its name from Shiroki Kogyo to Aisin Shiroki Corporation.
- As of March 31, 2025, Aisin Corporation holds a 100.00% investment ratio as the sole shareholder.
- Main products include arm type window regulators (fan type and crescent gear type), wire-type window regulators, and guideless window regulators.
- Main products include front and rear doorframes (TNGA and basic types) and aluminum front doorframes.
- Main products include belt molding, arch molding, and frame molding.
- Main products include high-tensile bumpers, rocker EA materials, and touch sensors.
- Main products include power sliding doors and universal steps.
- Main products include luggage compartment door closers, door locks, fully open-door stoppers, door hinges, door checks, engine hood locks, back door locks, rear seatback locks, and floor locks.
- In March 1946, the company was established as Shiroki Kinzoku Kogyo Co., Ltd. with its head office and a factory in the Jujo Military Arsenal in Tokyo.
- In October 1950, headquarters moved to Shinagawa-ward, Tokyo.
- In July 1956, the Ex-Nagoya Plant started operations, later integrated into the Toyokawa Plant in June 1988.
- In December 1959, the Osaka Plant started operations, originally in Ikeda City and moved to Ibaraki City in August 1977.
- In November 1960, the Toyota-City Plant started operations in Toyota-City, Aichi Prefecture, renamed Toyota Plant in February 1985.
- In February 1964, the Musashino Plant started operations in Musashimurayama City, Tokyo, renamed Tokyo Plant in October 1969 and integrated into the Fujisawa Plant in December 1986.
- In January 1967, the Fujisawa plant started operations in Fujisawa City, Kanagawa Prefecture, where headquarters were transferred.
- The company was listed on the 2nd Section of the Tokyo Stock Exchange in May 1970 and the Nagoya Stock Exchange in October 1972.
- In August 1973, it was listed on the 1st Section of both the Tokyo and Nagoya Stock Exchange.
- In October 1980, the Toyokawa branch plant started operation in Ichinomiya-cho, Hoi-gun, Aichi Prefecture, renamed Toyokawa plant in August 1981.
- In September 1984, Shiroki Seiko Co., Ltd. was established.
- In December 1985, Shiroki Shoji Co., Ltd. was established.
- In May 1986, a two-headquarters system consisting of the Yokohama headquarters and the Toyokawa headquarters was launched.
- In September 1986, the Toyokawa Technical Center was established.
- In December 1986, Tokyo Plant operations were integrated into the Fujisawa Plant.
- In March 1988, Shiroki-Wickes Corporation was established in Tennessee, U.S.A., later renamed SW Manufacturing, Inc. in December 1989 and Shiroki North America, Inc. in January 2007.
- In June 1988, Nagoya Plant operations were integrated into the Toyokawa Plant.
- In September 1988, the corporate name was changed to Shiroki Corporation.
- In May 1989, Shiroki USA Corp. was established in Michigan, U.S.A.
- In April 1991, the Nagoya Plant started operations in Toyota City, Aichi Prefecture.
- In December 1993, Toyota Plant operations were integrated into the Nagoya Plant, establishing the Toyota Production Division.
- In June 1995, SWM Georgia LLC was established in Georgia, U.S.A., which adopted a new name, Shiroki Georgia LLC, in January 2007.
- In July 2000, the Yokohama headquarters was closed and operations shifted to a single headquarters at Toyokawa.
- In August 2001, Kyusyu Shiroki Corp. was established.
- In January 2002, Shiroki Thailand Co., Ltd. was established.
- In February 2002, Shiroki Brose Corp. was established in Toyohashi City, Aichi Prefecture, later moved to Toyota City in 2005.
- In October 2003, Guangzhou Shiroki Corp. was established in Guangzhou City, Guangdong Province, China.
- In June 2005, SWM-GT LLC was established in Gordonsville, Tennessee, USA, later renamed Shiroki GT LLC in January 2007.
- In December 2007, Production section 3 of the Nagoya Plant was integrated into the Toyokawa Plant as Production section 4.
- In December 2009, Production Section 4 of the Toyokawa Plant was consolidated into Production Section 3, and the vacated plant was renamed Toyota Depot.
- In March 2011, Matsumi Corporation's status was changed to a consolidated subsidiary.
- In August 2011, P.T. Shiroki Indonesia was established in West Java, Indonesia.
- In July 2012, Shiroki Asia Corporation Limited was established in Bangkok, Thailand.
- In July 2013, Tohoku Shiroki Co., Ltd. was established in Japan.
- In July 2014, Shiroki Technico India Pvt. Ltd. was established in Haryana, India.
- In May 2015, a contract was signed to transfer the seat structure business with Toyota Boshoku and Aisin Seiki.
- On April 1, 2016, the company became a wholly owned subsidiary of Aisin Seiki.
- In December 2017, Shiroki Auto Components India Private Limited was established in Gujarat, India.
- In August 2020, an additional 5% equity interest was acquired in the joint venture Shiroki Technico India Pvt. Ltd., making it a wholly owned subsidiary.
- In March 2021, Shiroki Auto Components India Pvt. Ltd. merged with Shiroki Technico India Pvt. Ltd. and ceased to exist.
- In June 2021, Shiroki Technico India Pvt. Ltd. changed its name to Shiroki Automotive India Pvt. Ltd.
- In June 2022, TOYOTA BOSHOKU CORPORATION, AISIN CORPORATION and car seats Concluded a production.
