# Hyundai Metaplant America

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-06

**Source:** [South Korea charters plane to fly home over 300 workers detained by ICE at Georgia Hyundai plant : NPR](https://www.npr.org/2025/09/06/nx-s1-5532604/hyundai-immigration-raid-georgia-south-korea)  ·  `page_047663`

## Facts

- South Korea's foreign minister is considering a trip to the U.S. to meet with the Trump administration after hundreds of South Korean nationals were arrested in Georgia at an electric vehicle battery plant.
- Foreign Minister Cho Hyun expressed deep concern and a heavy sense of responsibility over the arrests of South Korean nationals during an emergency meeting in Seoul.
- South Korean President Lee Jae Myung called for all-out efforts to swiftly resolve the matter, stressing that the rights and interests of South Korean nationals and the business operations of South Korean companies investing in the U.S. must not be infringed upon.
- U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement agents arrested 475 people, most of them South Korean nationals, in a raid Thursday at an electric vehicle battery plant under construction in Ellabell, Ga., near Savannah.
- The plant is a joint venture between Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution.
- On Sunday, Presidential chief of staff Kang Hoon-sik said that South Korea and the U.S. had finalized negotiations on the workers' release.
- South Korea plans to send a charter plane to bring more than 300 workers home as soon as remaining administrative steps are completed.
- ICE released video of what officials say is the largest single-site enforcement operation in the history of the Homeland Security Investigations, showing officers shackling workers and loading them onto Georgia prison buses.
- Steven Schrank, special agent in charge of homeland security investigations for Georgia and Alabama, stated the operation underscores a commitment to protecting jobs for Georgians and Americans.
- Schrank said the operation was not simply rounding up people but the result of a months-long investigation.
- According to Schrank, the workers were either illegally present in the U.S. or working unlawfully.
- The arrests add to tension with a key ally at a time when President Trump has been pushing for trade deals and more South Korean investment in the U.S.
- Hyundai also has an auto plant in Montgomery, Ala., and has announced plans to invest more than $5 billion in a steel plant in Louisiana.
- President Trump said when asked about the raid at the White House on Friday: "I would say that they were illegal aliens, and ICE was just doing its job."
- Democratic U.S. Sen. Raphael Warnock of Georgia says the raid raises questions about both the president's immigration strategy and how the firms were able to allegedly employ workers without proper documentation.
- Warnock stated: "Companies should not be able to get rich in Georgia by exploiting immigrant workers and putting them at risk of politically motivated raids and violations of due process."
- Hyundai Motor Co. says it's working to understand the specific circumstances of the arrests.
- Hyundai Motor Co. stated that based on their current understanding, none of those detained is directly employed by Hyundai Motor Company.
- Hyundai Motor Co. promised an internal investigation into employment compliance and stated it has zero tolerance for those who don't follow the law.
- LG Energy Solution suspended all business trips to the U.S. and told workers visiting the U.S. to stay in their accommodations or return to South Korea immediately.
- LG Energy Solution's chief human resources officer is heading to the U.S. to address the issues on site.
- LG Energy Solution stated it will make every effort to promptly resolve the situation, including ensuring the speedy and safe return of all employees.
- Georgia officials have touted the Hyundai Metaplant America site as a game changer for the state.
- Republican Gov. Brian Kemp has said the plant has provided "historic benefits" and new opportunities for workers.
- A spokesperson for Gov. Kemp's office stated that Georgia will always enforce the law, including all state and federal immigration laws, and noted that the state Department of Public Safety coordinated with ICE to support the operation.
- The Georgia National Guard is providing administrative support to ICE.
- Republican U.S. Senate candidate and Congressman Buddy Carter, whose district includes the plant site in Ellabell, Ga., applauded the Trump Administration and law enforcement officers for taking bold action to put American workers first.
