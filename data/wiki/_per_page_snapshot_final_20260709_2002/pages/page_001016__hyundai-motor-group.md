# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-06-21

**Source:** [Money Talks: The Electric Vehicle Revolution Trumps Red State Politics - CleanTechnica](https://cleantechnica.com/2024/06/21/money-talks-the-electric-vehicle-revolution-trumps-red-state-politics/)  ·  `page_001016`

## Facts

- The Hyundai Ioniq 5 SUV will be the first electric vehicle to roll off the production line of the company’s new $7.5 billion manufacturing campus in Georgia.
- The Ioniq series has been a hit in the US and globally, with the Ioniq 5 SUV pulling the rug out from under EV market naysayers despite some glitches in the US market last year.
- EV sales began to pick up in January 2024 and momentum has continued globally, with the Ioniq 5 serving as Exhibit A in the rebound.
- According to a Hyundai press release dated June 18, the IONIQ 5’s sales numbers prove its popularity, making it Hyundai’s highest volume EV with sales up 43% so far in 2024 and an increase of 82% in May alone.
- The new campus is named Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) and is located in Ellabell, Georgia.
- HMGMA will host the production of the 2025 Ioniq 5, which has yet to be officially unveiled for North America.
- HMGMA is currently training and preparing to begin production in the fourth quarter of this year (2024).
- The new plant will produce all trim lines except IONIQ 5 N and will eventually be the sole facility building IONIQ 5 models for the U.S. market.
- Hyundai is investing $7.59 billion to develop the campus, with the expectation of creating 8,500 new direct jobs.
- Hyundai and SK On are building a $5 billion EV battery cell plant in Bartow County, Georgia that will employ 3,500 workers.
- A study from the Center for Automotive Research calculated that Hyundai’s electric vehicle investments in Georgia will create almost 40,000 jobs, with an annual figure for individual earnings of an estimated $4.6 billion.
- The state of Georgia and local governments are on track to give $2.1 billion in tax breaks and other incentives to Hyundai Motor Group after the automaker announced an additional $2 billion investment at its EV complex last month (September).
- Associated Press calculations show projected incentives will rise by more than $290 million from the $1.8 billion deal originally announced last year, with only $2.75 million representing additional cash from the state and the rest coming from increases in tax breaks.
- Georgia has a longstanding school-to-factory worker training and hiring program that attracts manufacturers.
- Georgia is a “right to work” state under regulations passed in 1947, making it difficult for workers to unionize and allowing employers to enjoy a more compliant workforce.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) states that building on the assets that make the state’s automotive industry successful, Georgia is rapidly developing as the capital of EV manufacturing for the nation.
- In addition to Hyundai’s venture, GDEcD notes that the Kia facility in West Point will begin making the company’s new EV9 model this year (2024).
- Rivian announced plans for a $5 billion factory in Georgia back in 2021, but the project got well into the site preparation phase before switching gears to focus on its EV production facility in Illinois, though characterized as temporary.
- GDEcD notes that Georgia is rapidly expanding across multiple EV supply chain segments: cathode and anode manufacturing, chemical and mineral solutions, battery manufacturing, metals and aluminum manufacturing, and battery recycling.
- Energy storage startup Freyr Battery Inc. announced earlier this year that it is relocating its home base back to the US from Europe, citing global tax developments and U.S. incentive programs for battery manufacturers as a key advantage.
- Freyr’s new Giga America factory site selection was announced in 2022 in Coweta County, Georgia.
- The initial phase of Giga America is planned to be a cell production module of approximately 34 GWh based on the next-generation of 24M’s U.S.-based SemiSolidTM production platform at an initial projected capital cost of $1.7 billion.
- Freyr name-checked the Inflation Reduction Act as a key element in support of the project, stating that with its passage, they expect U.S. demand for ESS, passenger EV and other electric mobility applications to grow rapidly over the next decade.
- The total package of financial incentives for Giga America includes robust assistance collectively provided by The State of Georgia and Coweta County in addition to expected production tax credits associated with the recently passed Inflation Reduction Act.
- A corporate update earlier this year indicated that plans for the Giga America battery factory are moving forward.
- Georgia state legislators have been working to pass bills aimed at blocking financial firms that ascribe to ESG investing principles, even while their own economic development agencies invest millions of public dollars to attract new clean tech businesses.
