# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-06

**Source:** [Governor Kemp reinforces economic partnerships in Korea](https://valdostatoday.com/news-2/region/2024/06/governor-kemp-reinforces-economic-partnerships-in-korea/)  ·  `page_047706`

## Facts

- Governor Brian Kemp and First Lady Marty Kemp are leading a delegation of state officials on an international mission to the Republic of Korea to reinforce strategic economic development partnerships.
- Georgia’s connections to Korea go back decades, with this trip aiming to strengthen relationships that have a generational impact on the No. 1 state for business.
- The state delegation includes President Pro Tem John Kennedy, Representative Soo Hong, and Representative Lehman Franklin.
- The delegation also features representatives from the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD), Georgia Environmental Protection Division, and Georgia Ports Authority.
- Governor Kemp and the delegation will conduct meetings with existing industries operating or under construction in Georgia, specifically Hyundai Motor Group, Kia, CJ Foodville, LG Group, SK Group, and Hanwha Qcells.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that meeting with industry leadership at their headquarters provides a unique opportunity to learn about industry perspectives and position Georgia at the forefront of new opportunities.
- The State of Georgia has maintained continuous representation in Korea since 1985.
- Korea’s Consular Office, which holds jurisdiction across the Southeast, Puerto Rico, and U.S. Virgin Islands, has been located in Georgia since 1976.
- Korea has been one of the top sources of Foreign Direct Investment and international job creation in Georgia for the last three years.
- In fiscal year 2023 alone, Korean companies announced more than $10 billion in investments and the creation of 12,605 jobs across the state.
- Total trade between the State of Georgia and Korea totaled over $15.8 billion in calendar year 2023, accounting for nearly 10 percent of the state’s total trade.
