# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Korean Companies Invested 32 Trillion Won Over 10 Years... How Did Georgia Become a Hub for 'K-Manufacturing'? - The Asia Business Daily](https://www.asiae.co.kr/en/article/2023101608272441168)  ·  `page_008052`

## Facts

- Korean companies have invested $23 billion in Georgia over the past decade, amounting to about 32 trillion won.
- This investment accounts for nearly 15% of the total U.S. investment of $156 billion during the same period.
- Hyundai Motor plans to invest $5.5 billion by 2025 in Georgia’s first dedicated electric vehicle manufacturing facility.
- SK On will invest 6.5 trillion won in a joint battery manufacturing plant with Hyundai Motor.
- LG Energy Solution will invest 2.65 trillion won in a joint battery manufacturing plant with Hyundai Motor.
- The relationship between Georgia and Korean companies began in 1999 when SKC built a videotape factory (now a PET film factory) in Georgia.
- SKC is currently constructing a semiconductor glass substrate factory in Georgia, scheduled to start operations within the year.
- SKC and its subsidiary Absolics have invested $240 million (370 billion won) in Georgia.
- Korean companies began entering Georgia in earnest after Kia Motors established a plant in 2009.
- Kumho Tire established a production facility in Georgia in 2016.
- Hyundai Mobis established a production facility in Georgia in 2022.
- About 140 Korean companies have currently entered the Georgia market.
- SK On has a battery plant in Georgia.
- Battery material company Enchem has electrolyte facilities in Georgia.
- Deokyang Industry has battery module and energy storage system facilities in Georgia.
- Sungil Hightech is investing in used battery recycling facilities in Georgia.
- Korean companies are building a supply chain covering electric vehicles and batteries in Georgia.
- Georgia is expected to become a hub for the U.S. electric vehicle industry once the supply chain is completed.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development received the Van Fleet Award jointly with the Korea International Trade Association at the Korea Society Annual Dinner in New York on September 20.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Korean companies have created more jobs in Georgia than any other foreign direct investment over the past three years.
- GDEcD announced on the 3rd that Georgia has ranked first overall for 10 consecutive years since 2014 in Area Development's "Best State for Business" rankings.
- Georgia received the highest scores compared to other regions in seven categories, including business costs, competitive labor environment, workforce development programs, available real estate, and state government cooperation.
- Georgia has become the third-largest region in the U.S. for Fortune 500 company headquarters, with 18 of these companies headquartered in Atlanta.
- The Georgia government offers investment attraction packages that include site selection, business permits, connections with infrastructure companies, and investment incentives.
- The Georgia government offers the "Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD)" program to help with rapid site selection.
- The GRAD program provides information on industrial sites for factories or offices based on a company's desired schedule.
- The GRAD program conducts essential preliminary investigations (environmental assessments, cultural asset and endangered species surveys) in advance and approves sites via an expert committee to eliminate additional investigations.
- Georgia is divided into 12 grid-like zones, each with a regional project manager assigned to closely support incoming companies.
- Tax incentives for attracting investment include job creation tax benefits such as employment tax credits and superior employment tax credits.
- Companies using Georgia ports or developing new products and services in Georgia receive separate tax credits related to regional economic development.
- Over the past decade (2010-2020), Georgia’s average population growth rate was 10.6%, surpassing the U.S. average of 7.4%.
- As of 2021, 460,000 people moved to Georgia in the past five years.
- Georgia's average population age is 36.4 years, which is 4% younger than the U.S. average.
