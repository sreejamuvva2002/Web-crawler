# Daimler Trucks North America LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2015-08-28

**Source:** [Daimler Trucks North America Demonstrates Continued Commitment to Oregon with $18M Upgrade to Madras Testing Facility - Thomas Built Buses](https://thomasbuiltbuses.com/resources/news/daimler-trucks-north-america-demonstrates-continued-2015-08-28/)  ·  `page_053587`

## Facts

- Daimler Trucks North America (DTNA) announced an $18M investment to upgrade its current research and development facility in Madras, Oregon.
- The upgraded Madras facility will create more than 30 new jobs in an area located approximately 120 miles southeast of Portland.
- DTNA produces class 6-8 vehicles under the Freightliner, Western Star, Thomas Built Buses, and Freightliner Custom Chassis nameplates.
- The Madras facility supports ongoing testing, research, and development to provide value to DTNA's customers.
- The facility's test track is designed to simulate a typical vehicle’s full service life over a variety of road surfaces in less than six months.
- The proximity of the Madras facility to DTNA’s Portland headquarters enables office and field engineers to collaborate more easily on projects.
- Matthew Markstaller, Property Manager at Daimler Trucks North America, stated that locating their own durability test facility in Madras will further centralize and strengthen the company's engineering presence in Oregon.
- Mayor Royce Embanks welcomed DTNA's commitment to Madras and the local economy, noting it as a valuable partnership.
- Local and state officials, including the City Council, Business Oregon, EDCO in Jefferson County, ODOT, and Jefferson County, collaborated to secure state grants and utilize an enterprise zone, which influenced DTNA's decision to build and expand in Oregon.
- Construction of the upgraded facility, which will include a test track and several buildings, was scheduled to begin in winter 2015 and complete by the end of 2016.
- The Madras facility upgrade was enabled by DTNA's significant capital investment alongside support from local and state officials.
- Two years prior to this announcement, DTNA had announced plans to construct a new $150M headquarters building on its campus on Swan Island in North Portland.
- Daimler Trucks North America LLC is headquartered in Portland, Oregon, and operates as the leading heavy-duty truck manufacturer in North America.
- DTNA produces and markets commercial vehicles under the Freightliner, Western Star, and Thomas Built Buses nameplates.
- DTNA is a subsidiary of Daimler, the world’s leading commercial vehicle manufacturer.
