# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-05-20

**Source:** [Hyundai To Build First Dedicated EV Plant and Battery Manufacturing Facility in the U.S. | ⚡ Ford Lightning Forum ⚡](https://www.lightningowners.com/threads/hyundai-to-build-first-dedicated-ev-plant-and-battery-manufacturing-facility-in-the-u-s.1108/)  ·  `page_009298`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group is spending approximately USD 5.54 billion to build an EV plant and a battery manufacturing facility in Bryan County, Georgia.
- Hyundai Motor Group entered an agreement with the State of Georgia to build full electric vehicle and battery manufacturing facilities in Bryan County.
- Commercial production at the new facilities is scheduled to begin in the first half of 2025.
- The new facilities will have an annual capacity of 300,000 units.
- Hyundai Motor Group aims to produce a wide range of innovative EV models at the new facility.
- The Group aims to become a leading provider of electrified mobility solutions with this U.S. investment.
- An official signing ceremony was held in Bryan County, attended by Georgia Governor Brian P. Kemp, other Georgia officials, Hyundai Motor Company President and CEO Jaehoon Chang, and Hyundai Motor President and Chief Operating Officer José Muñoz.
- Hyundai Motor Group Executive Chair Euisun Chung joined the signing ceremony virtually.
- Georgia Governor Brian P. Kemp stated that the project is the largest in the state's history and will deliver high-quality jobs on the leading edge of mobility to Georgians.
- Hyundai is locating a world-class project in Bryan County within a year of Georgia’s investment in the Bryan County Megasite.
- Hyundai Motor Group Executive Chair Euisun Chung stated that the new facilities will be the first dedicated full EV and battery manufacturing facilities in the U.S. for the Group.
- The new facility is expected to break ground in early 2023.
- The battery manufacturing facility will be established through a strategic partnership, with details to be disclosed at a later stage.
- Local EV production will increase U.S. consumer accessibility to the Group’s innovative EVs.
- Through the battery manufacturing facility, the Group aims to establish a stable supply chain and build a healthy EV ecosystem in the U.S.
- The EV and battery manufacturing plant will be located on a dedicated 2,923-acre site in Bryan County, Georgia.
- The site has immediate access to I-95 and I-16 highways, creating easy access to 250 major metro areas.
- The site is located less than 50 kilometers from the Port of Savannah.
- The Port of Savannah is the single-largest and fastest-growing container terminal in the U.S., with two Class I rail facilities on-site provided by Georgia Central Railway.
- West Point, Georgia is already home to the Group’s Kia manufacturing hub in the U.S.
- Hyundai Motor Group selected Georgia due to favorable business conditions, including speed-to-market, a talented workforce, and an existing network of Group affiliates and suppliers.
- Georgia is the Southeastern leader for EV registration per 1,000 registered automobiles.
- Georgia already offers more publicly available EV charging outlets per capita than anywhere else in the Southeast.
- The project is expected to create about 8,100 new jobs.
- The EV plant investment is part of the Group’s 2021 announcement to invest USD 7.4 billion by 2025 to foster future mobility in the U.S., including EV production and smart mobility solutions.
- Hyundai Motor Group has a global target to sell 3.23 million full electric vehicles annually by 2030.
- To realize this goal, the Group plans to establish a global EV production network to ensure a stable supply of EVs around the globe.
- Hyundai Motor President and CEO Jaehoon Chang stated that the new EV plant is the future of their business and will help meet growing U.S. customer demands for leading edge design and safe, zero-emissions vehicles.
- With additional U.S. EV and battery production capabilities, the Group aims to become one of the top three EV providers in the U.S. by 2026.
- The investment aligns with the U.S. government’s roadmap to accelerate electrification.
- Last year, the U.S. government announced an initiative requiring sales of zero-emission vehicles to account for at least half of OEM new vehicle sales by 2030.
- The new plant will feature a highly connected, automated, and flexible manufacturing system that organically connects all elements of the EV ecosystem.
- The Group plans to implement advanced intelligent manufacturing technologies currently under test at the Hyundai Motor Group Innovation Center in Singapore (HMGICS).
- All production processes, including order collection, procurement, logistics, and production, will be optimized utilizing AI and data.
- The innovative manufacturing system will create a human-centered work environment with robots assisting human workers.
- As part of its sustainability commitment, the plant will mainly rely on renewable energy sources and use emission-reduction technologies to meet RE100 requirements.
