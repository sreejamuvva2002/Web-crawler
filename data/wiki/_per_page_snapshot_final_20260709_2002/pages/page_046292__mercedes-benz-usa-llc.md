# Mercedes-Benz USA, LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-12-23

**Source:** [AG Griffin announces $150 million settlement with Mercedes-Benz USA over emissions fraud](https://katv.com/news/local/ag-griffin-announces-150-million-settlement-with-mercedes-benz-usa-over-emissions-fraud)  ·  `page_046292`

## Facts

- Attorney General Tim Griffin announced a settlement of nearly $150 million with Mercedes-Benz USA, LLC, and Mercedes-Benz Group AG for violating state law prohibiting unfair or deceptive trade practices.
- Griffin joined a coalition of 50 attorneys general to announce the settlement regarding vehicles equipped with illegal and undisclosed emissions defeat devices designed to circumvent emissions standards.
- From 2008 to 2016, the states allege Mercedes manufactured, marketed, advertised, and distributed nationwide more than 211,000 diesel passenger cars and vans equipped with software defeat devices that optimized emission controls during emissions tests.
- The states allege that the defeat devices enable vehicles to exceed many legal limits of nitrogen oxide (NOx) emissions.
- Mercedes allegedly engaged in the practice to reach new design and performance goals, such as increased fuel efficiency and reduced maintenance.
- Mercedes allegedly concealed the existence of the defeat devices from state and federal regulators and the public, falsely marketing the vehicles to consumers as 'environmentally friendly.'
- The settlement requires Mercedes-Benz USA, LLC and Mercedes-Benz Group AG to pay $120 million to the states immediately upon the date of the settlement.
- Additionally, $29,673,750 will be suspended and potentially waived pending the completion of a comprehensive consumer relief program.
- Arkansas will receive $250,000 from the settlement.
- Approximately 704 of the impacted vehicles were sold in Arkansas.
- The attorneys general of Alabama, Connecticut, Delaware, Georgia, Maryland, New Jersey, New York, South Carolina, and Texas led today’s settlement, joined by Alaska, Arkansas, Colorado, the District of Columbia, Florida, Hawaii, Idaho, Illinois, Indiana, Iowa, Kansas, Kentucky, Louisiana, Maine, Massachusetts, Michigan, Minnesota, Mississippi, Missouri, Montana, Nebraska, Nevada, New Hampshire, New Mexico, North Carolina, North Dakota, Ohio, Oklahoma, Oregon, Pennsylvania, Rhode Island, South Dakota, Tennessee, Utah, Vermont, Virginia, Washington, West Virginia, Wisconsin, Wyoming, and Puerto Rico.
- Attorney General Tim Griffin stated that protecting consumers from deceptive trade practices is a top priority for his office and that the settlement ensures Mercedes-Benz takes responsibility by compensating consumers and implementing critical reform.
