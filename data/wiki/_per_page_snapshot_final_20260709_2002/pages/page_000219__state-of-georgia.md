# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Powering the Next Generation of Mobility | Select Georgia](https://www.selectgeorgia.com/blog/powering-the-next-generation-of-mobility/)  ·  `page_000219`

## Facts

- Georgia has attracted record investment over the last several years to build an ecosystem across the electric vehicle supply chain, battery production, eVTOL aircraft, and other advanced solutions.
- Georgia has aligned its workforce programs with industry to secure long-term talent for the electric mobility sector.
- Georgia began its transition toward attracting electric mobility and battery technologies well before the Inflation Reduction Act was signed in August of 2022.
- Hanwha Qcells opened its first Georgia facility in 2019.
- Hyundai selected Georgia for its operations before the Inflation Reduction Act became law.
- In 2018, SK Battery America announced a historic investment in Jackson County.
- Groundbreaking for the SK Battery America project followed in 2019, bringing 2,000 jobs and $1.67 billion in capital investment.
- The SK Battery America announcement attracted investment from global e-mobility suppliers including Duckyang, Enchem Ltd, TEKLAS, and GEDIA.
- In 2021, Rivian announced what was then the largest economic development project in Georgia’s history, comprising 7,500 jobs and $5 billion in capital investment.
- Vertical construction on the Rivian site is set to begin this year, with vehicles expected to roll off the line as early as 2028.
- Rivian has established its east coast headquarters in Atlanta.
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America selected Georgia in October of 2021 and formally signed its agreement in May of 2022.
- The initial Hyundai announcement included 8,100 jobs and $5.5 billion in investment.
- With the addition of the LG Energy Solution joint venture on site, the combined total for the Hyundai project rose to 8,500 jobs and more than $7.5 billion in capital investment.
- Twenty suppliers have announced plans to locate in Georgia, bringing more than 7,000 additional jobs and $4.5 billion more in investment.
- By the time the Inflation Reduction Act was signed, at least 34 electric mobility projects were already active or in discussion in Georgia.
- Between 2018 and 2023, e-mobility and battery projects represented more than $27 billion in planned investment.
- In 2015, Georgia attempted to recruit a major automotive OEM to Bryan County but ultimately lost the project.
- Following that loss, state and local leaders formed a joint development authority to ensure regional alignment.
- Leaders preserved land for a future mega-auto project and planned infrastructure years in advance, including power, water, gas, sewer, and rail.
- Georgia studied wetlands early to reduce risk during its seven-year commitment that laid the groundwork to win Hyundai.
- Electric mobility in Georgia includes air mobility via eVTOL solutions like those from Archer Aviation, which are being manufactured in the state.
- Georgia Power and Southern Company are investing in reliable, scalable power to support large-scale manufacturing.
- Georgia Power and Southern Company are expanding EV charging infrastructure across urban and rural communities to alleviate range anxiety.
- Georgia Power and Southern Company are piloting managed charging programs that reduce peak demand and enhance grid stability.
- Georgia Power has extended both its EV infrastructure and charger rebate programs through 2028.
- Georgia Power began a vehicle-to-grid (V2G) pilot using electric school buses this year, marking the first of its kind across the Southern Company system.
- Hyundai and Kia can scale internal combustion or electric production as market demand dictates.
- Rivian is adjusting its product mix in response to consumer preferences.
- Georgia’s approach allows organic adoption by bringing public and private partners together to build infrastructure that lets consumers drive the pace of change rather than mandating market manipulation.
- The current electric mobility wave mirrors the economic impact Georgia experienced following Kia’s decision to locate in West Georgia nearly two decades ago.
- Competition for automotive OEMs is currently intense in Georgia.
- Georgia’s success in electric mobility stems from starting early, staying disciplined, and aligning policy, infrastructure, workforce, and energy leadership around a shared vision.
