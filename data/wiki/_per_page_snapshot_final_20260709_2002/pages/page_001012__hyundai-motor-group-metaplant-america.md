# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-06

**Source:** [Hyundai Opens Georgia EV Plant, Gearing Up for Ioniq 9 Production](https://evmagz.com/hyundai-opens-georgia-ev-plant-gearing-up-for-ioniq-9-production/)  ·  `page_001012`

## Facts

- Hyundai officially opened its new electric vehicle plant in Georgia on Wednesday.
- The opening marks a major milestone in Hyundai's $7.6 billion investment in the state.
- The facility is named Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA).
- HMGMA will produce EVs, including the upcoming Ioniq 9, which is the company’s first three-row electric SUV.
- Construction on the plant began less than two years ago.
- The plant is expected to create 8,500 direct jobs by 2030.
- Hyundai’s broader investment in Georgia, which includes a battery plant with SK On and 18 supplier facilities, has attracted more than $12.6 billion.
- This broader Georgia investment is projected to generate over 58,200 jobs, according to the Center for Automotive Research.
- Hyundai Motor Group Executive Chair Euisun Chung stated during the grand opening: “Together with the talented workforce at HMGMA, we are building the future of mobility with America, in America.”
- The grand opening ceremony was attended by Georgia Governor Brian Kemp, Hyundai CEO Jose Muñoz, and Kia CEO Ho Sung Song.
- The facility will manufacture EVs and hybrids for Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis brands.
- Production at the plant had already started in October when the first 2025 Hyundai Ioniq 5 rolled off the assembly line.
- The updated 2025 Hyundai Ioniq 5 features an extended range of up to 318 miles, refreshed styling, and a NACS charging port for Tesla Supercharger compatibility.
- The next model to launch is the Ioniq 9, a larger three-row electric SUV expected to launch this spring.
- The Ioniq 9 will feature a 110.3 kWh battery and deliver an estimated range of up to 335 miles.
- The plant’s opening coincides with Hyundai’s broader $21 billion investment in the U.S., which was announced earlier this week.
- Of the $21 billion, Hyundai plans to use $9 billion to increase production across Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis to 1.2 million vehicles.
- Hyundai will allocate $6 billion of the broader investment to build a new steel plant in Louisiana.
- During a press conference on Wednesday, Georgia Senator Jon Ossoff warned that potential policy changes could impact the EV industry.
- Ossoff stated: “The Trump Administration’s war on electric vehicles poses a threat to EV sales, to the EV market, and to the automobile industry in Georgia and across the country.”
- Ossoff cautioned that eliminating federal tax credits would put the U.S. at a disadvantage compared to China and other global competitors in the auto and technology sectors.
