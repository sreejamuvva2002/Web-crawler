# DENSO Manufacturing Tennessee Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2022-08-09

## Facts

- DENSO Manufacturing Tennessee Inc. announced a four-year plan and $1 billion investment in 2017 to transform into a hub for electric vehicle parts and safety systems.
- Within the last few months prior to publication, DENSO has invested $11 million in plant renovations.
- DENSO spokesperson David Lazar stated that $10.7 million worth of construction was completed during June as part of the transformation.
- In July, DENSO permitted another $319,375 worth of renovations.
- Development activities span the Maryville campus, including Building 204, manufacturing areas, offices, and communal spaces.
- The company has installed EV chargers in its parking lot.
- Building 204 was opened in December 2018 with a ribbon cutting ceremony.
- The renovation expanded the manufacturing campus with 335,900 square feet of indoor industrial space established for new technology.
- Spokesperson David Lazar stated that despite pandemic and industry challenges, the company is making steady progress in developments and hiring.
- The original $1 billion investment announcement included a goal of creating 1,000 new jobs in Maryville.
- An October 2017 news release from DENSO stated that the new jobs would be in production and support, including roles for technicians and engineers.
- The investments are intended to make the Maryville plant a primary manufacturing center in North America for electrification and safety systems.
- New vehicle systems made in Maryville will play a crucial role in meeting increasing electric vehicle demand.
- A July AAA survey found that 25% of people plan to purchase a fully electric vehicle (not a hybrid) the next time they shop for a car.
- Of those planning to buy an EV, 77% want to save money by not having to visit the gas pump.
- AAA spokesperson Megan Cooper stated that record-high gas prices have brought the cost of owning an EV more in line with a standard gasoline-powered vehicle.
- Cooper noted that although EVs may cost more upfront, they cost less to charge, maintain, and are more efficient.
- Industry predictions state that by 2050, 50% of vehicles on the road will be electric.
- Ryan Stanton, senior project manager for EV Evolution at the Tennessee Valley Authority (TVA), spoke during a statewide Tennessee Advanced Energy Business Council roundtable in July.
- Stanton stated that growth in EVs will contribute to an increase in power grid demand over the next two decades.
- TVA is confident it can meet increased EV demand through current and additional capacity.
- Currently, TVA services about 27,000 EVs and has a goal to increase that service to 200,000 by 2028.
- Stanton noted that servicing 200,000 EVs would still only make up about a half a percent of TVA’s load today.
