# Georgia Emerging Automotive Recognition (GEAR) Awards

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2020-10

**Source:** [GDEcD Announces 2020 Georgia ‘GEAR Award’ Winners - Columbus CEO](https://thecolumbusceo.com/features/2020/10/gdecd-announces-2020-georgia-gear-award-winners/)  ·  `page_020520`

## Facts

- On Monday, October 19th, 2020, the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) announced the five companies that earned the 2020 Georgia Emerging Automotive Recognition (GEAR) Awards.
- Award presentations were held in the winners’ communities throughout the week in accordance with state executive orders and public health guidelines.
- The GEAR Awards were previously known as the Georgia Automotive Awards and are updated for 2020 and beyond to recognize forward movement in the state’s automotive and mobility industries through innovation and emerging technologies.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that the state’s automotive and mobility industries are major drivers of jobs and innovation, and congratulated the winners on their resilience and commitment to creating jobs and opportunities for Georgians.
- Nominations for companies from across the state were reviewed and decided by a diverse committee of experts with deep understanding of the automotive and manufacturing industries.
- Nifco KTW America won Supplier of the Year (employing 200+ people) and is located in Toccoa.
- Sangsin Technology America won Supplier of the Year (employing 199 or fewer people) and is located in McDonough.
- Kubota Manufacturing of America Corporation won Transportation Equipment Manufacturer of the Year and is located in Gainesville.
- Panasonic Automotive Systems won the Automotive Innovation Award and is located in Peachtree City.
- Freudenberg NOK Sealing Technologies won the Citizenship Award and is located in Cleveland.
- Socially distanced award presentations were conducted by leaders from the GDEcD’s Global Commerce division and community economic development partners, including one completely virtual ceremony.
- GDEcD Deputy Commissioner of Global Commerce Scott McMurray noted that the winners’ dedication to employees and customers has been critical to strengthening the state’s diverse automotive and mobility ecosystem.
- Georgia is home to more than 200 automotive-related facilities, and the automotive industry contributes more than $3 billion to the state’s economy.
- The state’s skilled workforce, strong logistics network, and central location among Southeastern Original Equipment Manufacturers continues to attract automotive and mobility suppliers from across the U.S. and internationally.
- Nifco’s Georgia location in Toccoa currently employs more than 350 Georgians.
- Nifco is a globally known Tier 1 supplier of automotive parts whose customers include BMW, Mercedes, Volkswagen, and others.
- Sangsin Technology America supplies to companies like Vanguard, Great Dane, and others, and its McDonough manufacturing facility currently employs approximately 30 people in Georgia.
- Kubota’s North American headquarters are located in Gainesville, Georgia, where approximately 3,000 Georgians help make top-of-the-line Georgia Made™ RTVs, Tractors, Lawn Tractors, Mowers, and more.
- Kubota recently broke ground on its North American Research and Development Center, marking its fourth major expansion within the state.
- Kubota Manufacturing of America President Hirohiko Arai stated that the company is honored to be recognized by the State of Georgia, noting that their strength comes from their products, processes, and people.
- Panasonic’s Georgia facility is located in Peachtree City and employs roughly 1,500 people.
- Panasonic Automotive President Scott Kirchner stated that developing innovative products for safer roadways and a transformed mobility experience is the company's core mission, relying on remarkable talent and culture as their most sustainable competitive advantage.
- Freudenberg NOK Sealing Technologies has had operations in Georgia for more than 30 years, with its Cleveland facility employing roughly 1,200 Georgians.
- Freudenberg’s dedicated ‘Culture Team’ serves the community through initiatives including engineers assisting with ‘Rocket Day’ at White County Middle School, donating funds to the school’s engineering program, and planting trees with the 4-H.
