# Development Authority of Lumpkin County (DALC)

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** workforce_training  ·  **Published:** 2024-01-04

**Source:** [DALC hosts Leadership Lumpkin County Economic Development Day – Development Authority of Lumpkin County](https://picklumpkincounty.org/dalc-hosts-leadership-lumpkin-county-economic-development-day-2/)  ·  `page_045404`

## Facts

- On January 4, 2024, the Development Authority of Lumpkin County (DALC) and the Dahlonega Downtown Development Authority (DDA) hosted the Leadership Lumpkin County Economic Development Day at the JTEKT North America plant in Lumpkin County.
- The event brought together 28 participants to cover economic development fundamentals and community profiles for Lumpkin County and the City of Dahlonega.
- LLC Chair Deb Thompson stated that Lumpkin Leadership is an amazing program that offers even ‘nuggets’ a new perspective and a deeper understanding of our community, and called Economic Development Day key to that increased understanding.
- A MythBusters Panel featured Lumpkin County Chairman Chris Dockery, Community and Economic Development Director Rebecca Mincey, Main Street Manager Ariel Alexander, and City of Dahlonega Councilman Ryan Reagin.
- A Workforce Development Panel included Bobbi Larson (Director of Economic Development and Community Engagement at the University of North Georgia), Kerri Whitmire (Director of Student Services at the Lumpkin County Board of Education), and Troy Lindsey (Dawson Campus Dean at Lanier Technical College).
- These workforce partners collaborate with DALC, local businesses, and community partners to prepare students for careers in Lumpkin County and the region.
- Participants toured two local manufacturers, JTEKT North America and Big Creek Distilling Company, which contribute to Lumpkin County through capital investment, corporate responsibility, and workforce development.
- The event highlighted the diversity of industries and collaborative efforts involved in bringing new businesses to the community to support the local economic engine.
