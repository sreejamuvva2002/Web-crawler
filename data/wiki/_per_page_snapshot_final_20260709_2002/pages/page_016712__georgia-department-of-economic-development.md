# Georgia Department of Economic Development

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2022-03-31

**Source:** [New Georgia Economic Development Records Set for Jobs, Investment in Nine Months - AllOnGeorgia](https://allongeorgia.com/georgia-state-news/new-georgia-economic-development-records-set-for-jobs-investment-in-nine-months/)  ·  `page_016712`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp and the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) announced that through the third quarter of fiscal year 2022 (July 1, 2021 – March 31, 2022), job creation and investments resulting from economic development projects have already surpassed fiscal year 2021 year-end totals.
- The 251 project locations supported by GDEcD’s Global Commerce team will result in the creation of more than 35,400 new jobs and $12.9 billion in investment for the state.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated that Georgia continues to be the preferred place to do business and expressed gratitude to job creators choosing to locate or expand operations in the Peach State.
- The reported totals account for a 48% increase in jobs compared to three-quarters through the previous fiscal year and a 6% increase in jobs compared to the prior year’s overall total.
- There was a recorded increase of 54% in total investment compared to the same time period last year.
- Nearly 78% of both projects and investments were located outside the 10-county metro Atlanta area, and over half of the jobs created were also outside the metro Atlanta area.
- Key industry sectors supported include automotive, advanced manufacturing, logistics and distribution, software and technology, and food processing.
- Over the past year, Georgia’s electric vehicle (EV) and sustainability ecosystems have grown tremendously.
- Georgia is continuing to recruit the entire EV supply chain to ensure an effective, efficient, and affordable environment for EV production.
- Rivian has ongoing plans to develop a $5 billion campus at Stanton Springs North.
- Rivian also plans an electric vehicle service center in Atlanta that will provide training on EVs.
- SK Battery America has partnered with American lithium-ion battery recycling and engineered materials startup Ascend Elements to recycle both cell and module lithium-ion battery manufacturing scrap from SK’s Jackson County facility.
- Ascend Elements announced its first commercial-scale battery recycling plant in Covington in January.
- Aurubis, one of the leading recyclers of copper, precious metals, and non-ferrous materials worldwide, will invest $340 million in a ‘first-of-its-kind in the U.S.’ state-of-the-art recycling and secondary smelting facility for multi-metal recycling in Augusta.
- Aspen Aerogels recently announced plans to locate their new 500,000-square-foot advanced manufacturing facility in Statesboro.
- The Aspen Aerogels facility will produce PyroThin materials that provide thermal management, mechanical stability, and fire protection properties to EV battery systems.
- Georgia Quick Start, the No. 1-ranking workforce training program, helped secure numerous projects and has provided specialized training tailored specifically to business needs for more than 40 years.
- The Georgia Quick Start program is administered through the Technical College System of Georgia.
- The Georgia Ports Authority’s deepwater and regional ports projects have played a key role in securing diverse projects across the state.
- For the past 19 consecutive months, the Georgia Ports Authority has been reporting record-breaking numbers as it expedites plans for long-term growth.
- The Savannah Harbor Expansion Project (SHEP) was recently completed, deepening a 40-mile stretch between the port and the Atlantic Ocean to accommodate super-sized freighters traveling from the expanded Panama Canal.
- Inland ports are attracting investments, such as the Appalachian Regional Port driving Roper Corporation’s decision to invest $118 million in expanding its facilities in Walker County.
- Other assets driving Georgia’s growth include Hartsfield-Jackson International Airport and the state’s extensive rail network.
- The Georgia Department of Transportation maintains more than 1,200 miles of highway as part of Georgia’s highway and interstate system, which connects companies to 80% of the country in two days or less of driving time.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that a drive to create hope and opportunities for Georgia communities is at the heart of the Department’s mission, targeting new strategic industries for growth and supporting existing businesses’ expansions.
