# Hyundai Mobility Training Center of Georgia

**Type:** workforce_training  ·  **Role:** workforce_training  ·  **Published:** 2024-02-09

**Source:** [Training Thousands of Georgians for the Hyundai Metaplant | Georgia](https://georgia.org/blogs/training-thousands-georgians-hyundai-metaplant)  ·  `page_000015`

## Facts

- Georgia Quick Start is ranked as the No. 1 workforce training program in the nation.
- Georgia Quick Start has helped prepare more than 1.8 million Georgians for new jobs since its creation in 1967.
- Quick Start provides free-of-charge job training for qualified companies.
- Quick Start's training services have been utilized by a diverse group of manufacturers, including Kia Georgia in West Point.
- Kia Georgia’s training facility opened in 2008.
- Kia Georgia continues to work with Quick Start on updated training curricula with each expansion, including the addition of the EV9 line.
- The success of the Kia Georgia training facility became a critical advantage for Georgia in the competition to land Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA).
- Strategizing on how to train thousands of Georgians for Hyundai Motor Group’s first fully-dedicated electric vehicle (EV) facility in the U.S. began soon after HMGMA was announced in May 2022.
- The plan is for an over 8,500-employee manufacturing project.
- A multipronged approach is required to ensure future HMGMA and suppliers’ team members will be prepared for success.
- The Hyundai Mobility Training Center of Georgia is the first training center in Georgia dedicated exclusively to the EV market.
- The Hyundai Mobility Training Center of Georgia will be located on state property adjacent to HMGMA.
- The Technical College System of Georgia, Quick Start, and HMGMA broke ground on the center in February 2024.
- The facility is slated to open in late 2025.
- While the main facility is under construction, Quick Start is providing training through its existing Quick Start Advanced Manufacturing Training Center in Pooler.
- Quick Start has established temporary training sites at three locations to further support HMGMA and its affiliates.
