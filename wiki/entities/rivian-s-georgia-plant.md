# Rivian's Georgia Plant

**Type:** Facility  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[vehicle-manufacturing|Vehicle Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** Georgia, United States

## Overview

A $5 billion vehicle manufacturing facility planned by Rivian in Georgia, slated to begin production in 2028 with an initial capacity of 200,000 vehicles per year, aimed at scaling the company’s EV lineup and reducing costs.

## EV supply-chain relevance

The plant will produce Rivian's R2 SUV and R3 models, expanding affordable EV options and increasing domestic vehicle manufacturing capacity in Georgia.

## Key facts

- The Georgia facility will span 1,800 acres and initially produce 200,000 vehicles annually, with a potential second phase to double capacity.
- Rivian aims to use the plant to produce the lower-priced R2 SUV (starting at $45,000) and the smaller R3 model, expanding beyond its premium niche.
- Georgia offered a $1.5 billion incentive package tied to creating 7,500 jobs, underscoring political and economic stakes.
- Rivian's strategy relies on regional suppliers in Georgia to improve logistics and reduce dependence on long-distance sourcing.
- Analyst Alex Oyler of SBD Automotive highlighted scale as the linchpin of EV profitability for Rivian.
- Despite a $1.66 billion loss and removal of federal tax credits, Rivian remains committed to the Georgia plant to achieve cost competitiveness.
