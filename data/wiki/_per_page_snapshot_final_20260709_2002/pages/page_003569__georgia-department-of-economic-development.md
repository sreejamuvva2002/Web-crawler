# Georgia Department of Economic Development

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2023-08-08

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp: Georgia Breaks Economic Development Records for Third Year in a Row | Governor Brian P. Kemp Office of the Governor](https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases/2023-08-08/gov-kemp-georgia-breaks-economic-development-records-third-year-row)  ·  `page_003569`

## Facts

- For Fiscal Year 2023 (July 1, 2022, to June 30, 2023), total investments in facility expansions and new locations totaled more than $24 billion.
- FY23 resulted in 38,400 new jobs through 426 projects supported by GDEcD’s Global Commerce team.
- When excluding the two largest projects in state history – Rivian and Hyundai Motor Group – from the previous year’s job creation figures, total jobs as a result of FY23 projects exceeded the previous record by more than 2,800 new positions.
- Annual investment totals have increased by 131 percent over the last three years.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that the FY23 numbers confirm the state and local partnership approach with community and economic development leaders is working, bringing unprecedented opportunity to communities across Georgia.
- Eighty-two percent of new jobs created and more than $20 billion in investments are going to communities outside the 10-county Atlanta region.
- Metro Atlanta attracted $3.6 billion in investment last fiscal year.
- Expansions account for 71 percent of total projects, and new locations account for 65 percent of new jobs announced across the state.
- Lt. Governor Burt Jones stated that economic development is critical to remain the No. 1 state to do business and best to live, work, and raise a family, noting Georgia has secured its role as an economic and business hub of the Southeast.
- Speaker of the House Jon Burns stated that Georgia's success results from ongoing public-private partnerships and commitment to expanding opportunity, highlighting workforce development and tax reform to maintain a pro-business environment.
- Between July 2018 and July 2023, at least 40 e-mobility projects have been announced, committing more than 30,000 jobs and over $25 billion in investment to the state.
- Job creation in the automotive industry increased by 324 percent in FY23 when compared to FY21.
- Suppliers attracted by Georgia’s mobility original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) resulted in over $2 billion in investment across the state in FY23.
- Mobility and cleantech projects alone delivered three investments of $1 billion or more and six of the largest projects by job creation.
- This mobility success is attributed to fostering innovation with support from organizations like the Georgia Center of Innovation, which connects public and private partners in strategic ecosystems.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that innovation drives a sustainable economy for existing industries like aerospace and agriculture while building an emerging ecosystem to support cleantech and mobility of the future.
- Investments in aerospace-related projects increased by more than 1,000 percent since FY21.
- The aerospace industry ecosystem overlaps with e-mobility through companies like Archer.
- Top regional aerospace employers include Gulfstream.
- Georgia is home to the fifth-largest population of active and retired service members in the country, offering opportunities for defense contractors.
- Partnerships on speed-to-market and workforce solutions like the “Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development” (GRAD) certification and Georgia Quick Start programs spurred a 112 percent growth in advanced manufacturing investments since FY21.
- GDEcD Deputy Commissioner for Global Commerce Kristi Brigman stated that working alongside local communities fulfills their long-term strategies and highlighted the state's superior logistics, infrastructure, education opportunities, and high quality of life.
- Hartsfield-Jackson International Airport is described as the busiest and most efficient airport in the world.
- The Port of Savannah is the single largest and fastest-growing container terminal in America.
- The Port of Brunswick is one of the most utilized port terminals in the U.S. for roll-on, roll-off cargo.
- Georgia welcomed household name brands including Walmart-subsidiary Sam’s Club, work clothing retailer Duluth Trading Company, and an expansion by professional food service supplier WebstaurantStore.
- Georgia benefits from a reliable logistics industry extending throughout the state along a robust network of top-ranked highways and accessible railways.
- Ample cold storage space allows key sectors like agriculture – the state’s number one industry – and food processing to safely transport products to local groceries and markets across the U.S. and abroad.
- Agribusiness-related job creation grew by 29 percent compared to FY21.
- The top international sources of job creation in FY23 were South Korea, Finland, Norway, Japan, Switzerland, and the Netherlands.
- FY23 marked the return of important international economic development missions led by Governor and First Lady Kemp following a pause due to the COVID-19 pandemic.
- Governor and First Lady Kemp traveled to Switzerland, Germany, Israel, the country of Georgia, and France for meetings with major companies, official diplomatic visits, and participation in the Paris Air Show.
- International investments were responsible for 47 percent of expected jobs and 65 percent of the total investment in FY23.
- Additional state missions focused on strengthening relationships were led by GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson, Deputy Commissioner for International Trade Mary Waters, and Deputy Commissioner Kristi Brigman.
