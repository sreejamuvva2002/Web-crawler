# Volvo Trucks North America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Volvo to Sell Electric Class 8 Truck in the U.S. by End of 2020 | Heavy Duty Trucking](https://www.truckinginfo.com/news/volvo-to-start-selling-electric-class-8-truck-here-at-end-of-2020)  ·  `page_014216`

## Facts

- The battery-electric version of Volvo Trucks North America’s VNR Class 8 regional hauler, dubbed the EVNR, is slated to go on sale at the end of 2020.
- Peter Voorhoeve, VTNA President, announced the EVNR sales timeline at an Oct. 6 press conference at the American Trucking Associations’ annual Management Conference and Exhibition in San Diego.
- The EVNR launch will follow ongoing testing of battery-electric trucks with two California-based fleets as part of the Volvo Low Impact Green Heavy Transport Solutions (Volvo Lights) development program.
- The Volvo Lights development program is being conducted with California’s South Coast Air Quality Management District and over a dozen industry partners.
- Most funding for the $90 million Volvo Lights project is coming from the California Air Resources Board, with about $4 million also coming from the SCAQMD.
- Once on the market, the EVNR will initially target local and regional distribution fleets and short-haul drayage operations.
- The EVNR features a conventional Volvo I-Shift automated transmission optimized for electric powertrains and uses large battery packs mounted on the frame rail ahead of the drive tires.
- Sweden-based Volvo Group currently has a million connected assets operating globally, including 560,000 Volvo trucks, with 200,000 operating in the U.S., Canada, and Mexico.
- Peter Voorhoeve stated that connectivity secures more uptime and drives sustainability alongside better vehicle maintenance.
- North America is Volvo’s second-home market and its largest truck-selling market.
- Voorhoeve estimated that 2019 will finish with 325,000 new Class 8 truck orders in North America.
- A strong labor market and consumer spending are still driving U.S. GDP growth, though overall economic growth is slowing down and affecting confidence.
- Volvo’s August year-to-date market share increased slightly from 13.5 to 13.9 in Canada.
- Volvo’s August year-to-date market share increased significantly from 1.9 to 3.2 in Mexico.
- Volvo’s August year-to-date market share decreased in the U.S. from 10.7 to 9.7, attributed to starting slow at the beginning of the year.
- VTNA has made a decade of substantial investments in its dealer network, which now numbers 435 locations.
- Dealers have also made significant investments, with one dealership recently opening a 100-bay facility compared to the typical high number of 30 bays.
- Volvo is investing $400 million over six years to expand and upgrade its New River Valley, Virginia, assembly plant.
- The Virginia plant expansion is partly intended to create capacity for building electric trucks and will lead to over 700 new jobs at the facility.
- Volvo’s trucks are proudly assembled in the U.S.A., with their entire manufacturing process considered "made in the U.S.A."
- VTNA continues to invest in its centralized Uptime Center, allowing staff to remotely monitor customer trucks and provide over-the-air reprogramming of connected components.
- Over-the-air updates can change shift patterns of I-Shift automated manual transmissions to match new duty cycles.
- VTNA partnered with telematics provider Geotab to offer an integrated electronic logging device (ELD) solution for all Volvo-powered trucks of model year 2015 and newer.
- The ELD solution couples Volvo’s factory-fit telematics device with Geotab’s ELD application using a cloud-based system to ensure reliable data capture, FMCSA compliance, and full Android/iOS compatibility.
- Fleet managers can access ELD data through the MyGeotab portal to track compliance, view driver logs, violation alerts, and running fleet status reports.
- Geotab Drive for Volvo Trucks provides reliable ELD data-capture capabilities that keep HOS data safe and secure while avoiding pitfalls of hardwired systems, pairing requirements, or USB downloads.
- Volvo Trucks began partnering with Geotab in 2016 to improve telematics capabilities.
- VTNA’s sister company Mack Trucks separately announced it is rolling out Geotab Drive for Mack Trucks.
- David Pardue, Mack Trucks Vice President of Connected Vehicle and Contract Services, stated the partnership uses existing cloud-based technology to report ELD information and help fleets maintain driver hours of service compliance.
- Cloud-based data reporting simplifies the process for customers by eliminating the need for new ELD devices or reliance on hardware/wireless connections, making data collection more reliable.
- The factory-installed telematics device in every Mack vehicle sends engine and positional data to the cloud to generate automatic duty status logs.
- Drivers can manually enter duty status logs through the Geotab Drive app on a mobile device, which consolidates them into an accurate record of duty status.
