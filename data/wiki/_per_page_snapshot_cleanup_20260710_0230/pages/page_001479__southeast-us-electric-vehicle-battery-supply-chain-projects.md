# Southeast US Electric Vehicle & Battery Supply Chain Projects

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-07-11

**Source:** [Billion Dollar Projects - Welcome to US Public Works -](https://uspublicworks.com/billion-dollar-projects/)  ·  `page_001479`

## Facts

- July 11, 2024 Oglethorpe Power announced its Board of Directors and 38 member cooperatives decided to invest about $2 billion constructing a two unit, roughly 1,200 mw total, highly efficient combined cycle natural gas fired generating plants in Forsyth Georgia.
- July 11, 2024 Oglethorpe Power plans to add a simple-cycle combustion unit to their Box Springs facility in Talbot County Georgia.
- November 11, 2022 Georgia Governor Kemp announced Freyr Battery is planning to invest about $1.6 billion in a new electric vehicle (EV) battery manufacturing plant in Newnan, Georgia (Coweta County).
- November 11, 2022 Freyr Battery is calling the project “Giga America” and the first phase will be $1.7 billion.
- November 11, 2022 The company plans to start engineering work on this project in a few months.
- November 11, 2022 Georgia provided financial incentives to attract the development of this project.
- November 24, 2022 Hyundai announced plans to invest nearly $1 billion ($926 million) in Bryan County Georgia to build a new manufacturing plant producing EV’s and EV powertrains.
- November 24, 2022 Construction should start early in 2023 with production at the facility scheduled to begin in 2025.
- November 24, 2022 This plant is expected to produce 900,000 EV Power systems and 450,000 integrated charging control units.
- November 24, 2022 The Hyundai Mobis plant is located in Belfast Commerce Park in Richmond Hill Georgia.
- December 14, 2022 Redwood Materials announced their plan to spend about $3.5 billion on a new battery materials facility, located at Camp Hall in Berkley County near the Port of Charleston, South Carolina.
- December 14, 2022 They will call the site their next Battery Materials Campus.
- December 14, 2022 The facility is expected to supply a network of electric vehicle (EV) battery plants stretching from Michigan to Georgia.
- December 14, 2022 They hope to break ground on the facility early in 2023.
- December 14, 2022 The facility will recycle, refine and manufacture anode and cathode components; that are currently only sourced outside of North America.
- December 5, 2022 Ford Motor Company in a Joint Venture with South Korean company ‘SK On’ announced they were investing $5.8 billion and building 2 battery manufacturing plants in Glendale (Hardin County) Kentucky.
- December 5, 2022 The Joint Venture (JV) is named ‘BlueOval SK’ and the production of electric vehicle (EV) batteries should begin by the end of 2025.
- December 5, 2022 ‘BlueOval SK’ Battery Park will train new workers in necessary skills at a new Elizabethtown Community and Technical College (ECTC) Training Center, that will also be located at the BlueOval SK Battery Park.
- December 5, 2022 As of December 5th 2022 Ford reports that 4.3 million cubic yards of soil have been moved, 283,000 tons of stone have been placed, 66,000 cubic yards of concrete have been poured, 3,300 tons of rebar have been tied, foundations have been laid and 7,900 tons of structural steel have been erected.
- December 5, 2022 Barton Malow is the General Contractor, along with Gray Construction (Lexington KY).
- December 5, 2022 Walbridge Construction is the lead construction contractor on the Blue Oval project in Stanton Kentucky.
- December 2, 2022 GM and LG Energy solutions announced they were investing an additional $275 million to expand their battery manufacturing capacity in Spring Hill (Maury County) TN.
- April 16, 2021 GM and LG Energy Solutions announced they were investing $2.3 billion to build an Electric Vehicle battery manufacturing facility in Spring Hill (Maury County) Tennessee.
- April 16, 2021 This plant will provide batteries for Cadillac’s Lyriq vehicles and other GM Ultium platform electric vehicle models.
- April 16, 2021 Battery production is expected to begin late in 2023.
- May 31, 2023 Toyota announced it is investing $2.1 billion in its North Carolina battery manufacturing plant.
- May 31, 2023 These batteries will power an all-new, large (three row) SUV to be assembled at its Georgetown Kentucky facilities, starting in 2025.
- May 31, 2023 The new battery plant situated near Greensboro North Carolina, is already under construction, and will receive this additional $2.1 billion investment.
