## Facts

- Hitachi's Green Energy and Mobility segment includes the ZeroCarbon suite, designed to enable fleet operators to transition to electric vehicles.
- In February 2017, Hitachi and Honda announced a partnership to develop, produce and sell motors for electric vehicles.
- In October 2019, talks between Honda and Hitachi to consolidate their four automotive parts businesses began, resulting in the creation of a "mega supplier" named Hitachi Astemo incorporated in January 2021.
- Hitachi Astemo is a 40-40-20 joint venture between Hitachi, Honda, and JIC Capital.
- Hitachi Astemo's business areas include Car Information Systems, Drive Control, Electric Powertrain Systems, and Engine Management Systems.
- The consolidation forming Hitachi Astemo integrated Honda's Showa Corporation, Keihin Corporation, and Nissin Kogyo with Hitachi's wholly owned Hitachi Automotive Systems.
- In September 2025, Hitachi announced a $457 million investment to expand its South Boston transformer manufacturing facility in order to build more power grid components.
- Following the acquisition of ABB's power grid business, Hitachi Energy has become a major supplier of high-voltage direct current transmission systems.
