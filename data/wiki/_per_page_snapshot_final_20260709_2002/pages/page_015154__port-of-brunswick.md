# Port of Brunswick

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [A Georgia seaport is closing the gap with Baltimore, the top US auto port | AP News](https://apnews.com/article/georgia-ports-automobiles-brunswick-47bd7e5cffa13c62a1606ee7385b583b)  ·  `page_015154`

## Facts

- A record 830,000 automobiles moved through the Port of Brunswick south of Savannah in the 2024 fiscal year.
- The combined number of auto and heavy machinery units handled by Brunswick and the Port of Savannah topped 876,000 in the fiscal year ending June 30.
- That volume represents an increase of 21% over the same period a year ago.
- Georgia Ports Authority CEO Griff Lynch called it “a great year for us.”
- The number of cars and light trucks being shipped through the Port of Brunswick has increased significantly in the wake of the COVID-19 pandemic.
- U.S. auto sales saw their biggest increase in a decade in 2023.
- Georgia was investing $262 million in upgrades and expansions in Brunswick to make room for growth.
- The Brunswick upgrade projects are almost complete and should be finished by fall.
- Griff Lynch predicted last October that automobile volumes at Brunswick would surpass the Port of Baltimore by 2026.
- Port officials in Maryland reported that Baltimore handled 847,000 auto imports and exports in the 2023 calendar year.
- Baltimore’s shipping channel shut down completely for weeks following the deadly collapse of the Francis Scott Key Bridge on March 26.
- The waterway reopened in phases before being fully cleared in June.
- Maryland Port Administration spokesperson Richard Sher stated total automobile volumes for the Port of Baltimore over the past 12 months are not yet available because some auto terminals at the port are privately operated and do not report volumes until the end of the calendar year.
- When the bridge collapse forced auto shipments to be diverted from Baltimore, the Port of Brunswick received about 14,000 of those cars and trucks in April and May.
- Griff Lynch noted Georgia’s big gains in the past year largely resulted from automakers shifting their business to Brunswick from other neighboring ports such as Charleston, South Carolina, and Jacksonville, Florida.
- Georgia’s push to become a Southern hub for electric vehicle production could send more autos across Brunswick’s docks.
- Hyundai plans to open its first U.S. plant dedicated to EVs west of Savannah before the year ends.
- Griff Lynch expects the Hyundai factory to focus initially on vehicles for the U.S. market and noted that in the early years they would not be exporting a lot of cars.
- The Port of Savannah handled 5.25 million container units in the latest fiscal year, down 2.3% from fiscal 2023.
- The Port of Savannah is the fourth-busiest U.S. port for cargo shipped in containers.
- Container volumes lagged in the last six months of 2023 as retailers with overstuffed inventories scaled back new orders, but started to rebound in recent months.
