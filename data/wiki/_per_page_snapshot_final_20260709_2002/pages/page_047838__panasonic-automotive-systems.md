# Panasonic Automotive Systems

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2024-04-10

**Source:** [US private equity firm buys majority stake in Panasonic Automotive | WardsAuto](https://www.wardsauto.com/news/archive-auto-panasonic-automotive-apollo-funds-acquistion/711767/)  ·  `page_047838`

## Facts

- Apollo Funds, a private equity firm, has agreed to purchase a majority stake in supplier Panasonic Automotive Systems for 311 billion yen ($2 billion).
- Under the deal, Panasonic Holdings will maintain a minority stake in Panasonic Automotive.
- Panasonic Automotive will continue its strategic relationship with the Panasonic Group.
- The Panasonic Group includes Panasonic Holdings, the holding company, eight operating companies and affiliates within Japan and overseas.
- The companies expect the transaction to close by the end of the first quarter of 2025.
- Panasonic Group CEO Yuki Kusumi stated that through this partnership, Panasonic Automotive could significantly grow as a global leading player.
- Panasonic Automotive will continue its role as a member of the Panasonic Group and collaborate with the Group in procurement and fundamental research.
- Apollo aims to help support the next phase of growth for Panasonic Automotive, a key link in the global auto supply chain.
- Michael Reiss, a partner at Apollo, stated that supporting growth will still involve maintaining the company’s relationships with customers and partners.
- Other suppliers, including Continental and Goodyear, are retooling their strategies and operations as the auto industry transitions to electric and software-defined vehicles.
- Panasonic Automotive offers vision and sensing technology, integrated cockpit systems and other in-vehicle electronics for automotive applications.
- These products support autonomous vehicles, SDVs (software-defined vehicles) and connected services.
- Masashi Nagayasu, representative director and CEO of Panasonic Automotive, said the company has the potential to win through this once-in-a-century transformational period.
- Nagayasu stated that by taking advantage of the partnership with Apollo and realizing PAS’ full potential, they aim to become a top global player in the automotive electronics industry.
- The company aims to provide added value to cars and the mobility experience with customers.
