# Sewon America, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [JIMMY RUIZ HEREDIA EDGAR EMMANUEL SERRANO MONDRAGON v. SEWON AMERICA INC TOTAL EMPLOYEE SOLUTION SUPPORT LLC (2025) | FindLaw](https://caselaw.findlaw.com/court/us-dis-crt-n-d-geo-new-div/116939310.html)  ·  `page_049527`

## Facts

- Sewon America operates an automotive production plant in LaGrange, Georgia.
- Plaintiff Edgar Emmanuel Serrano Mondragon applied for a maintenance engineer position at the Sewon plant in January 2021 after attending a job fair in Mexico.
- Serrano received a job offer on August 19, 2021, for a maintenance engineer role with an annual wage of $36,000.
- Plaintiff Jimmy Ruiz Heredia applied for a mechanical engineer position via labor recruitment company TESS in February 2021.
- Ruiz received an offer letter dated June 14, 2021, for a technical engineer position with an hourly wage of $10.70 for the first three months.
- The June 14 offer letter stipulated that Ruiz's hourly rate would increase by $0.50 every six months until reaching a maximum of $16.80 per hour.
- Sewon's chief financial officer and general manager, Nathan Jung, signed both the initial and subsequent offer letters on company letterhead.
- Ruiz received a second offer letter dated June 21, 2021, which changed his title to Industrial Engineer and offered an annual salary instead of an hourly wage.
- Sewon and TESS provided support letters on Sewon's letterhead to the U.S. Embassy in Mexico City stating the engineering positions were professional and specialized under NAFTA's permitted TN occupations.
- Plaintiffs allege that upon starting work at the Sewon plant, they were assigned manual assembly line labor instead of the skilled engineering roles promised to secure their TN visas.
- Plaintiffs filed a first amended complaint on July 21, 2024, alleging violations of the Georgia RICO Act, breach of contract, and the Fair Labor Standards Act against Sewon and TESS.
- Ruiz alleges he was earning an annualized salary of $31,200 at the time of his termination, while Serrano earns $31,720 annually, both falling short of the $36,000 promised in their offer letters.
- A U.S. District Court order dated February 12, 2025, denied motions to dismiss by Sewon and TESS regarding the plaintiffs' breach of contract and RICO claims.
