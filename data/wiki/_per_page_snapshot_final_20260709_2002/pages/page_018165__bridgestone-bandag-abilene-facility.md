# Bridgestone Bandag Abilene Facility

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-05-19

**Source:** [Bridgestone breaks ground on US$60 mn expansion of tyre plant in Texas – Rubber Journal Asia -News on  Rubber machinery ,  Manufacturers ,  Rubber chemical producers and  Rubber processors](https://rubberjournalasia.com/bridgestone-breaks-ground-on-us60-mn-expansion-of-tyre-plant-in-texas/)  ·  `page_018165`

## Facts

- Bridgestone is making a US$60 million investment in Abilene, Texas.
- The investment will support the rapid growth of the retread tyre business.
- The expansion provides room for additional equipment.
- The expansion increases production capacity.
- The expansion increases days of operation up to seven days a week.
- The plant is adding 50,000 sq ft to an existing 200,000 sq-ft facility.
- Bridgestone recently celebrated the expansion of its Bridgestone Bandag facility with an official groundbreaking ceremony.
- The expansion includes an immediate increase in operational activity by running on six and seven-day production schedules.
- The expansion includes the construction of new mixing operations at the facility.
- The mixing operations are expected to be completed and in operation by January 2025.
- The investment in the facility and the additional days of operations increase the plant’s output by 16%.
- Bandag is an industry-leading supplier of retread tyres for fleets.
- Retread tyres provide fleets with economic benefits.
- Retread tyres are an environmentally responsible way to extend the life of tyres in any fleet.
- Since 1957, Bandag has kept approximately 300 million tyres out of the waste system.
- Keeping these tyres out of the waste system helps reduce material emissions, water, and energy use associated with new tire production.
- The Bandag retreading process has saved four billion gallons of oil since it began 50 years ago.
- The Bandag plant in Abilene celebrated 50 years of operations in 2021.
- The plant continues to produce pre-cured truck tread, rubber cement, and solvent packaging.
- Bridgestone says its investment will build upon the plant’s existing 200,000 sq-ft blueprint.
- The investment will provide capacity needed for future production.
