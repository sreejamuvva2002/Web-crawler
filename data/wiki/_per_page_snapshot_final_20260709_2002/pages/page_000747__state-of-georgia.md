# State of Georgia

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2023-03-04

**Source:** [Georgia’s at the epicenter of nation’s EV revolution | Office of the President](https://president.gatech.edu/blog/georgias-epicenter-nations-ev-revolution)  ·  `page_000747`

## Facts

- Katie Kirkpatrick, president and CEO of the Metro Atlanta Chamber, co-authored an opinion piece in the Atlanta Journal-Constitution about cleantech manufacturing with Ángel Cabrera.
- Georgia has positioned itself to become the nation’s preeminent hub for electric vehicles.
- Sales of EVs nearly doubled between 2020 and 2021 and jumped another 65% in 2022.
- If current trends hold, more than half of the cars sold in the U.S. will be electric by 2030.
- Korea-based SK On started churning out EV batteries in Commerce last year.
- SK On is partnering with Hyundai to build another factory in Bartow County.
- Hyundai broke ground on its own $5.5 billion EV plant near Savannah last fall.
- Rivian plans to build a $5 billion EV facility near Social Circle.
- Rivian’s announcement in December 2021 kicked off a flurry of EV investments in Georgia.
- Cleantech companies flocking to Georgia have announced more than $21 billion of new investments since Rivian's announcement.
- The emerging ecosystem includes charging infrastructure manufacturers like Heliox and EnviroSpark.
- The emerging ecosystem includes clean battery manufacturers like FREYR.
- The emerging ecosystem includes battery recyclers like Ascend Elements and Aurubis.
- Rivian’s Social Circle plant site benefits from extensive interstate and rail systems with direct connections to the world’s busiest airport and the country’s third busiest seaport.
- More than 550,000 students are enrolled in Georgia higher education institutions.
- Nearly 100,000 students are enrolled in the Technical College System of Georgia, where they learn targeted skills for next-generation companies.
- Georgia has the seventh largest higher education enrollment in the nation.
- Most Georgia students are served by the University System of Georgia.
- Georgia offers the second-most generous state financial aid in the nation through programs like the HOPE and Zell Miller scholarships.
- Net tuition at Georgia public universities is the seventh lowest in the nation due to robust state appropriations and generous financial aid.
- University research expenditures in Georgia are now the eighth highest in the nation.
- Emory University, Georgia State University, and the University of Georgia each spend more than $200 million annually on research.
- Georgia Tech has exceeded $1 billion in university research expenditures each of the past two years.
- Research breakthroughs in Georgia are occurring in batteries, motors, electronics, and artificial intelligence, which are critical to the EV industry.
- Qcells recently made Georgia the recipient of the country’s largest-ever investment in clean energy manufacturing.
- The announced projects will provide good jobs that carry over for decades, generating billions of dollars in salaries and tax revenue.
- Governor Brian Kemp spent years bringing together public and private stakeholders to accelerate Georgia’s advancement in electric mobility.
- The Georgia Department of Education launched an Electric Vehicle Career Pathway to prepare high school students for jobs in the field.
- The Technical College System of Georgia is building curricula for a long-term talent pipeline specifically for mobility manufacturing.
- The University System of Georgia is creating new programs to better align with the growing needs of the EV industry.
- Georgia’s congressional delegation in Washington is advocating for the state’s national status as a leader in the new cleantech economy.
