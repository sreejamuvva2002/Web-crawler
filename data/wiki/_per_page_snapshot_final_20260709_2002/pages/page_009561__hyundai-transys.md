# Hyundai Transys

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- Efficiency is especially important in electric vehicles because single-charge distance impacts product selection due to battery capacity and charging problems.
- Mileage per charge is ranked 1st place with 45% of considerations when purchasing an electric vehicle.
- HYUNDAI TRANSYS has developed the world's first AWD disconnector technology for electric vehicles that can improve driving efficiency.
- The disconnector system reduces unnecessary power loss and improves power efficiency (electric fuel efficiency) by about 6% to 8%.
- Vehicle mileage increases by the same percentage due to the system.
- Mounting the disconnection system on a vehicle that can travel 400 kilometers with a single charge allows it to travel about 430 kilometers with the same battery.
- Reducing battery capacity frees up battery space, which can be used to enlarge the interior space of the vehicle.
- AWD provides excellent grip force between the road surface and wheels in difficult driving conditions such as snow, rain, and icy roads.
- 30% of vehicles sold are AWD in North America.
- The number of buyers choosing the AWD system is expected to continue increasing in the future due to EV safety.
- The AWD system in an electric vehicle consists of main drive wheels and auxiliary drive wheels.
- HYUNDAI TRANSYS' disconnector system was installed in Hyundai's IONIQ for the first time.
- In the rear wheel-based IONIQ, the rear wheels serve as the main drive wheels and the front wheels serve as the auxiliary drive wheels.
- The disconnector system is attached to the front auxiliary wheels to cut off power between the motor and the wheels.
- The technology initially failed repeatedly during development because it was the first of its kind applied in electric vehicles.
- During the first endurance test, only 20% of the target level was achieved due to excessive gear tooth wear or poor meshing.
- Engineers questioned whether the design concept could match existing methods since EV power sources use motors rather than engines.
- The product was redesigned by analyzing the power source to control meshing synchronization speed according to motor speed and change mesh length.
- These adjustments increased the meshing success rate and solved "differential noise" and "squeaking (switching shock)" issues not found in internal combustion engines.
- HYUNDAI TRANSYS is the only company with a full lineup of transmissions in the world.
- The company specializes in decelerators that have continuously produced special decelerators for electric vehicles, trains, and subways.
- Decelerators are often mistakenly considered less complicated than conventional transmissions requiring no special skills, but this is not actually the case.
- Decelerators are considered to have many more possibilities and opportunities in the future as a key component for improving fuel efficiency.
- HYUNDAI TRANSYS will continue to develop and introduce reducers suitable for the electric vehicle era, including integrated decelerators.
