# TCF Open Voucher Call

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-07-18

**Source:** [$2.1 Million Available in National Laboratory Assistance Through Open Voucher Call | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/cmei/fuels/articles/21-million-available-national-laboratory-assistance-through-open-voucher-call)  ·  `page_002672`

## Facts

- The U.S. Department of Energy's Office of Technology Transitions and the Technology Commercialization Fund (TCF) unveiled a new program—the TCF Open Voucher Call—that connects the public with researchers at DOE national laboratories.
- This single-phase call will award up to 21 teams with a technical assistance voucher of up to $100,000.
- Vouchers are redeemable at eight national labs: National Renewable Energy Laboratory, Sandia National Laboratories, Pacific Northwest National Laboratory, Los Alamos National Laboratory, Ames National Laboratory, Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory, Argonne National Laboratory, and Oak Ridge National Laboratory.
- Vouchers must be used within a one-year period.
- Awardees will be selected based on innovative ideas and concepts for advancing energy or other lab-supported initiatives.
- Participating national labs will provide awardees with expertise and lab validation capabilities to help them advance their concept closer to commercialization.
- Eligible hydrogen and fuel cell-related topics include hydrogen production, electrolysis and fuel cell components, safety, storage, systems analysis, and techno-economic analysis.
- An informational webinar for the program was scheduled for July 18, 2024, at 2 p.m. ET.
- Applications are due October 3, 2024.
- The TCF Open Voucher Call is open to private entities (for-profits and nonprofits), non-federal government entities such as states, counties, tribes, and municipalities, academic and research institutions, and individuals.
- The goal of this call is to foster a culture of curiosity and collaboration while bridging the gap between scientific expertise and public engagement.
