# Fox Factory Holding Corp.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Fox Factory — Grokipedia](https://grokipedia.com/page/Fox_Factory)  ·  `page_020289`

## Facts

- Fox Factory Holding Corp. is headquartered in Duluth, Georgia.
- The company employs approximately 4,100 people globally as of January 2025.
- Its largest facility is located in Gainesville, Georgia, spanning 336,000 square feet and serving as a key hub for the Powered Vehicles Group.
- The Gainesville facility was completed in late 2021 and encompasses manufacturing, warehousing, distribution, and office functions.
- Powered vehicle operations were relocated from Watsonville, California, to the Gainesville, Georgia site in early 2022.
- Fox Factory operates 27 plants worldwide—15 in the U.S. for the Aftermarket Applications Group, two in the U.S. and one in Italy for the Powered Vehicles Group, and ten across multiple countries for the Specialty Sports Group—totaling approximately 5.4 million square feet as of January 3, 2025.
- The Powered Vehicles Group (PVG) and Aftermarket Applications Group (AAG) accounted for approximately 63% of net sales in fiscal 2024, totaling $882.9 million out of $1.394 billion.
- The Specialty Sports Group (SSG) segment represented about 37% of fiscal 2024 net sales at $511.1 million.
- Approximately 79% of fiscal 2024 sales ($1.097 billion) were derived from North America, while international markets in Europe (12%) and Asia (8%) show growth potential.
- Leadership is headed by Chief Executive Officer Michael C. Dennison, who assumed the role in June 2019 after joining the company in 2018 as President of the Powered Vehicles Group.
- The executive team includes Chief Financial Officer Dennis C. Schemm.
- Core operations are managed through subsidiaries such as Fox Factory, Inc., and recent additions like Marucci Sports, LLC.
- The supply chain prioritizes premium materials such as aluminum, magnesium, steel, and carbon fiber, sourced from a concentrated group of vendors, with 26% of components procured from the top ten suppliers in fiscal 2024.
- Fox Factory demonstrates vertical integration through in-house manufacturing of critical technologies, including shock valving and damping systems.
- The company's global distribution network reaches over 16,000 retail dealers and distributors worldwide, facilitating sales through original equipment manufacturers (44% of 2024 net sales), aftermarket channels (49%), and direct-to-consumer (7%).
- Fox Factory serves as an original equipment manufacturer (OEM) supplier to major brands, including Polaris for UTVs like the RZR series, Can-Am for models such as the Maverick, and Ford for the F-150 Raptor since its 2010 debut.
- Aftermarket offerings include complete kits for vehicles like Jeep Wranglers and Toyota Tacomas.
- The company's Live Valve electronic damping technology uses sensors to adjust compression and rebound in real-time by sampling terrain and driver inputs up to 400 times per second.
- In 2020, Fox Factory introduced Intelligent Quick Switch (iQS), an electronic system for UTVs that enables on-the-fly preload and damping adjustments via a dashboard switch.
- In 2023, Fox Factory acquired Custom Wheel House for $131.6 million, incorporating brands such as Method Race Wheels and Tensor Tires.
- In November 2023, Fox Factory acquired Marucci Sports for an enterprise value of $572 million, with the brand generating $192 million in 2024 sales, representing approximately 14% of the company's total $1.39 billion revenue for the fiscal year.
- The Gainesville, Georgia facility achieved ISO 14001 certification for environmental management in 2022 and maintained it through 2023 audits, alongside ISO 45001 for occupational health and safety.
- These measures align with broader compliance to regulations from the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) and California Air Resources Board (CARB).
- Fox Factory was founded in 1974 by Bob Fox in Scotts Valley, California.
- The company went public via an IPO on NASDAQ under ticker FOXF in August 2013, selling 9,857,143 shares at $15 each for gross proceeds of approximately $147.8 million.
- Revenue expanded from $272.7 million in fiscal 2013 to $366.8 million in fiscal 2015.
- Corporate headquarters were relocated to Duluth, Georgia, in 2018 to optimize logistics and proximity to key markets.
- A Taiwan branch was established in 2011 focused on manufacturing suspension components to shorten supply chains and support Asian production needs.
- In 2015, the company formed RFE Holding (Canada) Corp. through acquisitions to expand distribution and manufacturing capabilities in North America.
- In 2014, Fox Factory acquired assets of Race Face Performance Products and Easton Cycling for approximately $30.2 million.
- Later in 2014, the company acquired assets of Sport Truck USA for approximately $43 million.
- In 2015, Fox purchased assets of Marzocchi's mountain bike suspension product line from SR Suntour for an undisclosed amount.
- In 2017, Fox acquired assets of Flagship Inc., doing business as Tuscany Motor Company, for $53.4 million in cash.
- In 2019, Fox Factory acquired substantially all assets of Ridetech for approximately $14 million, financed via cash and newly issued stock.
- In February 2020, Fox Factory announced the acquisition of SCA Performance Holdings, Inc., completed later that quarter for $328 million plus $13 million in contingent performance-based payments.
- Fox Factory positions its bicycle suspension as a premium OEM and aftermarket solution, supplying components to leading brands such as Specialized, Trek, and Santa Cruz.
- In December 2024, Fox Factory acquired Marzocchi, reviving the brand to offer additional mountain bike suspension products including forks and shocks that complement the FOX lineup.
- Core operations prioritize segment-specific autonomy while maintaining centralized oversight from headquarters in Duluth, Georgia.
