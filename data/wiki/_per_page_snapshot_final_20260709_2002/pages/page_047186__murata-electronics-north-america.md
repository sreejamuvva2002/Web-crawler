# Murata Electronics North America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Murata Electronics trims SMT thermistor real estate - EE Times](https://www.eetimes.com/murata-electronics-trims-smt-thermistor-real-estate/)  ·  `page_047186`

## Facts

- Murata Electronics North America is located in Smyrna, GA.
- The company introduced three 0603 package thermistors to meet the downsizing demands of the computer and automotive markets.
- The PTH9C is used for temperature sensing in power transistors, power diodes and power ICs.
- The PTH8C is designed for overload protection in rechargeable battery packs and their chargers.
- The PTH8L is a leaded package thermistor also designed for overload protection.
- Each product is suited to reflow soldering requirements.
- They are said to feature strong resistance to electric static discharge, mechanical vibration and shock.
- Thermistors are primarily designed for temperature sensing and circuit protection.
- They are used for temperature controls in products such as CD-ROMs, CD changers, cassettes and tuners, battery chargers, power supplies and printers.
- They are also found in automotive components like power seats and sensors.
- One part in the series, the PTH816TBB471M, offers a maximum voltage of 16 volts dc.
- Non-operating current for the part is 7 milliamperes at 60 degrees C.
- Operating current is 25 mA.
- Pricing for the thermistors is approximately 15 cents to 20 cents per unit.
