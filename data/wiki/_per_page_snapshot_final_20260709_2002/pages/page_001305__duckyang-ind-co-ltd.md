# Duckyang Ind. Co. Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2021-05

**Source:** [Korean Automotive Manufacturer Duckyang to Open Company's First U.S. Facility in Jackson County - Middle Georgia CEO](https://middlegeorgiaceo.com/news/2021/05/gov-kemp-announces-korean-automotive-manufacturer-duckyang-open-companys-first--us-facility-jackson-county/)  ·  `page_001305`

## Facts

- Duckyang is an electric mobility parts supplier of automotive battery modules and energy storage systems to SK Battery America (SKBA).
- Duckyang will invest $10 million in opening its first U.S. manufacturing facility in Jackson County.
- The new facility will create 285 jobs in Braselton, Georgia.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced the investment and stated that Duckyang is creating high-paying, advanced automotive manufacturing jobs for Georgians in Jackson County.
- Governor Kemp noted that Georgia has the best workforce training programs in the nation and has been the number one state for business for eight years.
- Duckyang is headquartered in Ulsan, Korea.
- Duckyang supplies cockpit modules to global customers including Hyundai, Kia, Renault Samsung Motors, Genesis, and Mobis.
- Cockpit modules supplied by Duckyang are integrated auto parts comprised of more than 130 subcomponents located near the driver’s seat.
- Opening the Georgia facility will allow Duckyang to better serve its regional automotive customers.
- Dong-in Son, CEO of Duckyang Ind. Co. Ltd., stated that entering the U.S. electric vehicle battery market with SKBA will allow cooperation with other automotive companies like Hyundai and Kia Motors that have U.S. manufacturing bases.
- Dong-in Son expected business expansion opportunities to supply electric vehicle parts and interior parts, including cockpit module, door trim panel module, and other flagship parts to global automakers based in the United States.
- Duckyang’s new facility will be 230,000 square feet and located at 984 Broadway Avenue in Braselton.
- The company will hire for positions in production, maintenance, logistics, and warehouse management.
- Individuals interested in careers with Duckyang are encouraged to contact the company for additional information.
- Town Manager and Clerk of Braselton Jennifer Scott stated that the town looks forward to welcoming Duckyang and is delighted to see a new manufacturing facility in their town.
- John Scott, Director of Economic Development for the Jackson County Area Chamber of Commerce, stated that companies like Duckyang are helping Jackson County and Georgia secure a place in the automotive industry's future as it moves toward electric vehicles.
- Director of Korean Investment Yoonie Kim represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce division on the project.
- The project was facilitated in partnership with the Jackson County Industrial Development Authority, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia EMC.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Georgia is focused on growing the entire electric mobility supply chain.
- Commissioner Wilson noted that strong relationships with SK Battery America and other Korean companies like Duckyang are helping secure local supply of needed parts for companies in the state.
- The State of Georgia facilitated more than $1.3 billion in investment and more than 2,700 jobs from South Korean companies during 2020 alone.
- Georgia remains a national leader in advanced manufacturing and continues to attract business globally.
