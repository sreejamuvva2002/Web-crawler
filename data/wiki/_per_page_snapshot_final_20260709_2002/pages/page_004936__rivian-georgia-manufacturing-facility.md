# Rivian Georgia Manufacturing Facility

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Rivian Announces Optimized Capacity Plan for Georgia Plant](https://secure.businesswire.com/news/home/20260430750054/en/Rivian-Announces-Optimized-Capacity-Plan-for-Georgia-Plant)  ·  `page_004936`

## Facts

- Rivian announced it is increasing its initial production capacity to 300,000 vehicles annually for its future manufacturing facility in Stanton Springs North, Georgia.
- The updated capacity represents a 50 percent increase over the initially planned capacity of 200,000 units.
- The capacity increase aims to facilitate a lower cost per unit while providing significant room for future expansion of capacity in later phases.
- Rivian has worked with the U.S. Department of Energy’s Office of Energy Dominance Financing (EDF) to update its DOE loan.
- Vertical construction at the Georgia facility is expected to commence in 2026.
- Rivian now expects to first draw on the loan by early 2027.
- The company remains on track for vehicle production to begin in late 2028.
- The total DOE loan is aligned with the updated facility design and roadmap at up to $4.5 billion ($4,006 million principal and $494 million capitalized interest) for the initial phase.
- Rivian is transitioning to vertical construction, which is expected to begin this spring.
- Preparations are underway for the development of the stamping press area at the facility.
- The stamping press area is one of the most capital-intensive and technically demanding projects within the plant from a construction perspective.
- Progress on the facility’s primary buildings will accelerate this summer as they begin to take shape.
- Rivian recently announced a partnership with Uber for its Georgia operations.
- Rivian is expected to build up to 50,000 robotaxis at its Georgia plant beginning in late 2028.
- Rivian Founder and CEO RJ Scaringe stated the company is very excited to partner with the U.S. Department of Energy to grow its manufacturing footprint in Georgia.
- The R2 vehicle model dramatically expands Rivian's market opportunity according to leadership.
- Thousands of dedicated people will soon work in the Georgia plant, instrumental to Rivian’s growth as it scales American manufacturing and works to ensure the U.S. retains its leadership in innovation and technology.
- Rivian is an American automotive technology company that develops and manufactures category-defining electric vehicles as well as vertically integrated technologies and services.
- Rivian vehicles are manufactured in the United States and are sold directly to consumer and commercial customers.
