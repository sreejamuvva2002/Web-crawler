# Freudenberg-NOK Sealing Technologies

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Freudenberg-NOK Sealing Technologies Success Story - GaMEP at Georgia Tech](https://gamep.org/success-story/freudenberg-nok-success-story/)  ·  `page_020352`

## Facts

- Freudenberg-NOK Sealing Technologies serves multiple industries such as automotive, aerospace, medical devices, and bottling equipment.
- The company opened a facility in Cleveland, Georgia, thirty years ago.
- The Cleveland, Georgia location employs more than 500 people from the community and surrounding towns.
- The Georgia Manufacturing Extension Partnership (GaMEP) at Georgia Tech reached out to Wyman Hare, production partner manager of Freudenberg-NOK Sealing Technologies, to set up a benchmarking tour for its Advanced Manufacturing Consortium.
- Freudenberg-NOK Sealing Technologies is advanced in lean thinking and has many practices that consortium member companies can learn from.
- Wyman Hare agreed to invite the consortium members into their plant but also requested to join the group himself.
- Wyman Hare stated, “We may have won the Shingo Prize, but we, as a team, feel like we can always continue to learn from other companies in how to improve our operations.”
- During an initial plant tour, Hare made a connection with an organization that was far along in its lean journey.
- Hare arranged for twenty team members from the Cleveland facility and three other Freudenberg-NOK plants to tour a facility where they witnessed a communication method that made them rethink how they share information.
- Wyman Hare said, “the moment we saw the hospital’s Lean Production Management System in place, we knew the changes we needed to make in our facility to have our information flow faster from the bottom to the top of the organization, so that we, as a management team, could be more responsive and communicative with our direct reports and then downward from there.”
- Each morning, every department from the plant floor to human resources gathers for 20 minutes within their respective teams.
- Three additional tiers of meetings occur after the initial gathering, each layer sharing information from the prior meeting until a final meeting takes place among Hare and his direct reports.
- Within two hours, the entire organization understands daily priorities and helps resolve issues in a more timely and organized manner.
- Over time, this communication method proved influential in breaking down silos, earning trust, and increasing plant-wide transparency.
- Since the success at the Cleveland plant, Hare and Jennifer Watson, GROWTH manager, implemented this system division-wide, getting all 15,000 employees on the same communication flow.
- Hare and his team are looking forward to connecting with nine other GaMEP consortium groups statewide outside of Northeast Georgia.
- Wyman Hare stated that their experience with GaMEP and the Advanced Manufacturing Consortium has been extremely positive, serving as a great sounding board to make connections and learn from others.
