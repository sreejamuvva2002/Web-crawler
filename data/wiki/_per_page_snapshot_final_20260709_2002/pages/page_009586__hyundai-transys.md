# Hyundai TRANSYS

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2020-12-01

**Source:** [Hyundai TRANSYS to create 678 new jobs in West Point expansion | Local News | times-herald.com](https://www.times-herald.com/news/local/hyundai-transys-to-create-678-new-jobs-in-west-point-expansion/article_105dd83e-032f-5238-a2df-e20a03cd41dc.html)  ·  `page_009586`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Hyundai TRANSYS will invest more than $240 million in expanding its operations in Georgia.
- The investment will create 678 full-time long-term positions at a new manufacturing facility in the West Point area.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated that Georgia's No. 1 business climate, robust logistics network, and top-ranked workforce training programs are attracting global companies to the state while retaining existing industries.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp thanked Hyundai TRANSYS for its long-term commitment to Georgia and expressed a desire to continue working together to ensure the investment benefits hardworking people of West Georgia.
- Hyundai TRANSYS is the only automobile manufacturing company in the world that specializes in transmissions.
- The new plant will be 620,000 square feet.
- The facility will be located near the company’s current location on the Kia campus in West Point.
- The plant will produce eight-speed transmissions for a global automotive leader.
- Hyundai TRANSYS President of Georgia Powertrain Sangkil Jung stated that years of capital investment, employment, and training support in Georgia made it a natural choice for expansion.
- Sangkil Jung expressed excitement for new opportunities as the company expands its presence in the West Point community.
- Hyundai TRANSYS will bring hundreds of advanced manufacturing jobs to the local community.
- The company will also create management and office staff positions.
- The company plans to create 165 supplementary construction handling and inspection jobs.
- This expansion will increase Hyundai TRANSYS’ total employment in Georgia to 1,741 jobs.
- The company directs individuals interested in career opportunities to its website for updated information.
- West Point Mayor Steve Tramell expressed thrill over Hyundai TRANSYS's long-term investment in West Point and the creation of new jobs.
- Mayor Steve Tramell thanked the West Point Development Authority, the state of Georgia, and Troup County for making the project a reality.
- Hyundai TRANSYS has been a community partner with West Point and Troup County for over a decade.
- Troup County Chairman Patrick Crews stated that Troup County was honored to be chosen as the location for the new manufacturing facility.
- Chairman Patrick Crews noted that the significant capital investment will strengthen the local economy and create economic opportunity for residents through good jobs.
- Chairman Patrick Crews described Georgia as having the best state in the country for business and a community with a history of great leadership.
- Chairman Patrick Crews called the project a "team win for all involved."
- Senior Project Manager Amanda Fields represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s Global Commerce division on the project.
- The project was completed in partnership with the West Point Development Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that companies investing in Georgia become Georgia companies and emphasized commitment to supporting their long-term growth.
- Commissioner Pat Wilson celebrated Hyundai TRANSYS choosing to invest in Georgia again and praised their commitment to the state.
- Commissioner Pat Wilson thanked Governor Kemp and the General Assembly for their continued support of recruiting business and opening new markets for Georgia companies globally.
- Commissioner Pat Wilson noted that communities across Georgia, large and small, urban and rural, continue to benefit from the state's international trade and investment.
- In 2020 alone, Georgia’s international relationships facilitated more than $1 billion in investment and more than 2,000 jobs from South Korean companies.
- Georgia is home to more than 200 automotive-related facilities.
- Georgia remains a national leader in advanced manufacturing, outpacing the United States in 10-year GDP growth in the manufacture of products including machinery, electrical equipment and components, and fabricated metals.
