# Daimler Truck North America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-04-14

**Source:** [Daimler Truck Workers Are Strike-Ready in the Anti-Union South - ZNetwork](https://znetwork.org/znetarticle/daimler-truck-workers-are-strike-ready-in-the-anti-union-south/)  ·  `page_053656`

## Facts

- DTNA has a labor contract with approximately 7,000 United Auto Workers (UAW) members who build heavy trucks and buses at plants in North Carolina, Georgia, and Tennessee.
- The UAW master contract with DTNA is set to expire at midnight on April 26.
- DTNA workers are demanding wage increases, improved health insurance coverage, and an end to tiered employment structures.
- Thomas Built Buses operates plants in High Point and Archdale, North Carolina, which together constitute the largest school bus manufacturing site in North America with approximately 1,700 workers represented by UAW Local 5287.
- A mass rally for DTNA workers was held on April 2 at Local 3520 in Statesville, North Carolina, attended by UAW president Shawn Fain and rank-and-file members from six union locals.
- The DTNA labor struggle is occurring amid a rise in electric vehicle (EV) manufacturing in North Carolina, described as an industrial boom encompassing lithium mining to battery production, with one EV auto plant predicted to employ 7,000 workers.
- The UAW announced that 10,000 autoworkers signed union cards at 13 worksites across the South.
- Workers at the Volkswagen plant in Chattanooga are set to vote on joining the UAW from April 17 to 19.
- Workers at the Mercedes-Benz plant outside Tuscaloosa, Alabama, petitioned for a union election expected in early May.
- The Georgia General Assembly passed an anti-union bill that denies state incentives to businesses that voluntarily recognize labor unions instead of holding secret ballot elections; it is awaiting Governor Brian Kemp's signature.
- Alabama Governor Kay Ivey characterized the UAW's organizing efforts at the Mercedes-Benz plant as a looming threat to the state's historically anti-union, business-friendly economic model.
- Tennessee Governor Bill Lee used scaremongering tactics to dissuade Volkswagen workers in Chattanooga from joining the UAW ahead of their election.
- South Carolina Governor Henry McMaster vowed to fight unionization efforts in the state all the way to the gates of hell.
- DTNA posted record-level revenues of nearly $6 billion in 2023, an increase of 10 percent compared to the previous year.
- On March 8, 96 percent of UAW workers across DTNA voted to authorize their leadership to call a strike if contract demands are not met upon expiration.
- A worker-led coalition including the Southern Workers Assembly, Truckers Movement for Justice, and Tamexun was formed to support DTNA workers and engage unorganized truck drivers and facility workers.
- Percy Payne Jr, a 24-year veteran at Thomas Built Buses in High Point, highlighted that workers in the company's truck plants make $4 more per hour than bus plant workers for the same position due to tiered employment structures.
- Adam Foley, who performs quality inspections at the DTNA plant in Cleveland, North Carolina, cited cost-of-living adjustments and stagnant wages despite record corporate revenues as a primary demand.
- North Carolina ranks almost dead last in union membership according to the US Department of Labor, following early adoption of right-to-work legislation and laws banning public-sector collective bargaining.
- The DTNA contract battle is being closely watched by nonunion workers and bosses across the South as a potential catalyst for labor organizing in the region.
