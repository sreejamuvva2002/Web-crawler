# Suzuki Manufacturing of America Corporation

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-03-05

**Source:** [Suzuki Cycles](https://suzukicycles.com/news/2023/08/suzuki-manufacturing-of-america-corp-to-become-subsidiary-of-suzuki-motor-usa-llc)  ·  `page_010796`

## Facts

- Suzuki Manufacturing of America Corporation (SMAC) became a 100% subsidiary of Suzuki Motor USA LLC (SMO) effective March 1, 2024.
- The organizational change was announced on March 5, 2024, in Brea, California.
- SMAC was founded in 2001 and is based in Rome, Georgia.
- SMAC serves as Suzuki’s All-Terrain Vehicle (ATV) manufacturing facility.
- For over two decades, all Suzuki adult KingQuad ATVs have been manufactured at the Rome, Georgia location.
- Suzuki ATVs are sold in the United States, Canada, and nearly 25 countries worldwide.
- SMO officials cited consolidating ATV sales and production arms to strengthen the overall ATV business in the United States and globally as a primary reason for the change.
- Suzuki introduced the first four-wheel ATV in 1982 with the LT125 model, creating an all-new category of off-road recreational and utility vehicles.
- Suzuki’s current flagship ATV is the KingQuad 750AXi.
- Masami Haga, President of SMO, commented on the new organizational structure.
- Haga stated that a primary goal of the change is to expand commitment to the growth of the entire powersports business.
- The organizational evolution will enable building on relationships with Suzuki dealers and SMAC vendor partners.
- Strengthening the supply chain and purchasing policies for locally sourced parts are objectives of the move.
- By integrating production and sales functions, SMO and SMAC leaders expect to improve responsiveness to the ATV market by more accurately linking production to sales demand.
- SMO aims to improve inventory management for both the company and its dealer body to protect sales margins, flooring costs, and profitability.
- The move will result in better, more direct communication between SMO and SMAC, improving staff interaction between the two companies.
- Leaders expect to create a more collaborative environment between the production and sales groups of Suzuki ATVs, fostering better connection between Rome- and Brea-based teams.
- Suzuki’s United States distribution headquarters are located in Brea, California, where the company relocated in 1981 after first entering the U.S. market in 1963.
- SMAC becoming a subsidiary of SMO sets the stage for new Suzuki ATV offerings and enhancements to existing products in the future.
