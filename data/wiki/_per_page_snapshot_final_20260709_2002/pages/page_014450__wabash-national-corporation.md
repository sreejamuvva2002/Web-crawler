# Wabash National Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-25

**Source:** [Wabash Expands Southeast Footprint to Strengthen Customer Access and Service](https://www.sahmcapital.com/news/content/wabash-expands-southeast-footprint-to-strengthen-customer-access-and-service-2025-09-25)  ·  `page_014450`

## Facts

- Wabash National Corporation (NYSE: WNC) announced a significant expansion of its network in the Southeast.
- The company is opening a new Parts & Service center in the greater Atlanta region to strengthen customer access and service.
- The new Atlanta-area Parts and Services center doors opened on September 18, 2025.
- Strategically located at the heart of the Southeast’s trucking corridor, the Atlanta site provides faster access to parts, service, and truck body upfitting.
- The facility will offer Wabash’s Ready-to-Mount (RTM) truck bodies for dry freight and platform applications with rapid turnaround times for in-stock chassis.
- The Atlanta site joins existing Wabash service centers in California, Florida, Ohio, Pennsylvania, and Texas, with additional locations planned.
- Fleetco, a long-standing Wabash dealer, will expand its relationship to represent Wabash in the greater Atlanta market with trailer sales.
- This dealer expansion broadens Wabash’s dealer footprint in the Southeast and gives customers access to new and used equipment through Fleetco.
- Bill Tirrill, president at Fleetco Inc., noted over 14 years of experience working closely with Wabash and plans to expand the sales force across the region.
- Wabash added three CS Truck and Trailer locations to its Preferred Partner Network in Georgia.
- The Preferred Partner Network addition brings expert service, maintenance, and Wabash Genuine Parts to more customers through authorized third-party dealerships.
- Dave Hill, vice president of Parts & Services at Wabash, stated the expansions reflect building an extensive partner ecosystem that puts parts, service, and support closer to customers.
- Hill added that the Atlanta expansions mean faster turnaround, more coverage, and better reliability for fleets operating across Georgia, Alabama, South Carolina, and Tennessee.
- Wabash combines physical and digital technologies to deliver end-to-end solutions optimizing supply chains across transportation, logistics, and infrastructure markets.
- Headquartered in Lafayette, Indiana, Wabash designs, manufactures, and services dry and refrigerated trailers and truck bodies, platform trailers, tank trailers, and structural composites.
- Customers gain access to a nationwide parts and service network, Trailers as a Service (TaaS)℠, and advanced tools through the Wabash Marketplace and Wabash Parts.
