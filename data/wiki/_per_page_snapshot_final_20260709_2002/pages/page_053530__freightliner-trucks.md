# Freightliner Trucks

## Facts

- Freightliner Trucks is an American semi truck manufacturer headquartered in Portland, Oregon.
- The company is owned by Daimler Truck subsidiary Daimler Truck North America (DTNA).
- Vehicles are currently manufactured in Cleveland, North Carolina, and Mount Holly, North Carolina.
- In 1979, a new plant was constructed in Mount Holly, North Carolina, and a parts manufacturing plant in Gastonia, North Carolina, both located in the Charlotte metropolitan area.
- In 1989, Freightliner acquired a standing plant in Cleveland, North Carolina, near Statesville, that had previously produced transit buses for German manufacturer MAN.
- Under its Freightliner Custom Chassis subsidiary, the company produces bare chassis and cutaway chassis for multiple types of vehicles.
- In 2012, parent company DTNA announced plans to expand its workforce at its Cleveland, NC facility due to increased demand for the Cascadia model.
- As of August 2014, Freightliner employed 2600 workers at its Cleveland facility and 8000 employees across North Carolina.
- In September 2019, parent company Daimler announced it would halt internal combustion engine development initiatives as part of its efforts to embrace electric vehicles.
- As of May 2022, the MT-series step van (called MT50e) is available as an all-electric vehicle.
- The eCascadia, an all-electric semi-truck by Freightliner, was handed to customers for field tests in 2019 and officially premiered in May 2022.
- An all-electric variant of the M2 box truck has undergone field tests.
- Freightliner introduced natural gas versions of the Business Class M2 at its Mount Holly facility in 2009.
- In 2013, Freightliner expanded its alternative-fuel lineup by adding a natural-gas version of the Cascadia.
