# Top 19 Automotive Electronics Companies in the U.S.

## Facts

- The automotive electronics industry in the U.S. focuses on integrating electronic systems within vehicles, including infotainment solutions, safety features, and advanced driver-assistance systems.
- Companies in this field range from giant manufacturers like HARMAN International to smaller firms focusing on niche products.
- The listed companies represent a cross-section of the U.S. automotive electronics market, with headquarters primarily in Michigan and global operations.
- Most companies were founded in the 20th century and have adapted to technological shifts toward electric and autonomous vehicles.
- HARMAN International is an audio electronics company based in Stamford, Connecticut, founded in 1980.
- HARMAN designs connected products for automotive, consumer, and enterprise markets, focusing on advanced audio products and infotainment systems.
- HARMAN is a wholly-owned subsidiary of Samsung.
- HARMAN employs over 27,000 people globally, with substantial presence in India, the United States, and other countries.
- Yazaki North America is based in Canton, Michigan, and has been operational since 1966.
- Yazaki specializes in producing wire harnesses, connectors, and power distribution systems for modern vehicles.
- Yazaki serves major automotive manufacturers and is actively developing technologies for hybrid and electric vehicles.
- Dura Automotive Systems LLC (Dura-Shiloh) is based in Auburn Hills, Michigan, founded in 1914.
- Dura-Shiloh specializes in mobility systems including electrification solutions, lightweight structural systems, and advanced safety technologies.
- Dura-Shiloh's product portfolio includes battery enclosures for electric vehicles, park-lock actuators, and shift-by-wire systems.
- Wells Vehicle Electronics is based in Fond Du Lac, Wisconsin, operating since 1903.
- Wells specializes in vehicle electronic components like ignition systems and engine management sensors.
- Inteva Products was founded in 2008 and headquartered in Troy, Michigan.
- Inteva offers Closure Systems, Interior Systems, and Motors & Electronics to over 100 global automakers.
- Inteva received $755 million in funding in May 2019.
- The Intec Group is a private manufacturing company based in the United States with significant presence in Mexico, founded in 1953.
- Intec specializes in fuel systems, engine electronics, and door systems for Tier One automotive manufacturers.
- GHSP is based in Holland, Michigan, founded in 1924, specializing in mechanical and electromechanical systems.
- GHSP provides advanced driver controls and smart powertrain technologies that integrate electronic controls and motors.
- Sumitomo Electric Wiring Systems, Inc. (SEWS) is based in Bowling Green, Kentucky, founded in 1986.
- SEWS manufactures automotive wiring harnesses, electrified vehicle components, and advanced product development services for EVs and hybrids.
- Pacific Insight Electronics Corp. is a subsidiary of Methode Electronics, based in Durham, California, founded in 1989.
- Pacific Insight provides user interfaces, LED lighting, sensors, and powertrain components for automotive OEMs.
- Nartron was founded in 1967 and is based in Reed City, Michigan.
- Nartron specializes in advanced electronic systems including iQ Power®, Clearfast®, and Smart Close® for automotive and military sectors.
- AISIN World Corp. of America is based in Northville, Michigan, serving as the North American sales headquarters for AISIN Corporation.
- AISIN manufactures parts for hybrid and electric vehicles, including brakes, transmissions, and navigation systems.
- Rostra Accessories was founded in 1987 and is based in Aberdeen, North Carolina.
- Rostra produces electronic cruise control systems, vehicle camera systems, and parking assistance technologies.
- Pi Innovo was founded in 2011 and is based in Plymouth, Michigan.
- Pi Innovo specializes in embedded control units and software solutions for electric and hybrid vehicles.
- AAMP Global was founded in 1987 and is based in Clearwater, Florida.
- AAMP operates through its Stinger brand, offering high-quality audio systems, infotainment radios, cameras, and lighting solutions for the automotive aftermarket.
- DeAmertek Corporation was founded in 1977 and is based in Lombard, Illinois.
- DeAmertek specializes in Electric Power Assisted Steering (EPS), Adaptive Front Lighting Systems (AFS), and Fuel Delivery Modules (FDM).
- Weastec, Inc. was founded in 1988 and is based in Hillsboro, Ohio.
- Weastec produces HMI switches, sensors, and control modules for OEMs and operates a technical center in Dublin, Ohio.
- Ehps (Dare Auto Inc.) was founded in 2013 and is based in Plymouth, Michigan.
- Ehps specializes in brushless DC motors, electronic control systems, Electro Hydraulic Power Steering, keyless entry, and tire pressure monitoring systems.
- Nidec Elesys Americas Corporation was founded in 2002 and is based in Suwanee, Georgia.
- Nidec Elesys develops automotive safety systems and electronic control units, including occupant detection systems, radar technology, and electronic stability control (ESC) systems.
