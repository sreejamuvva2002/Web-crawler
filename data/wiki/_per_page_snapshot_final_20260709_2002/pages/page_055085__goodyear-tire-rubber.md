# Goodyear Tire & Rubber

## Facts

- Goodyear Tire & Rubber (NASDAQ: GT) has shed 71% of its value since January 2022.
- An investor more than doubled their position in Goodyear between $6.28 and $6.50 per share in mid-March.
- Skyrocketing crude oil prices due to Iran war uncertainties are likely to drive up the cost of petroleum-derived rubber, challenging Goodyear over the next couple of quarters.
- Goodyear's 2025 full-year operating results showed declining tire units sold across the Americas, EMEA, and Asia Pacific regions, alongside a shrinking operating margin.
- Goodyear attributed sales declines to a sharp contraction in demand from its Commercial segment in the Americas.
- President Donald Trump's tariffs have negatively impacted Goodyear's business performance.
- High rubber prices, Iran war uncertainty, and tariffs are expected to weigh on Goodyear's results throughout 2026.
- Goodyear initiated an operational turnaround plan called "Goodyear Forward" in November 2023, focusing on divestitures of non-core assets, cost-savings, and higher-margin products.
- Goodyear sold its chemical business to an affiliate of Gemspring Capital Management for $650 million.
- Goodyear sold the Dunlop brand to Sumitomo Rubber Industries for $735 million.
- Goodyear sold its off-the-road tire operations to Yokohama Rubber Company for $905 million.
- Collectively, Goodyear banked around $2.3 billion from these divestitures, primarily using the funds to lower outstanding debt.
- Goodyear surpassed initial cost-cutting expectations, achieving a $1.5 billion run rate cost-saving benefit by the end of 2025 through supply chain and purchasing adjustments plus plant optimizations.
- Consumers are hanging onto their vehicles longer than ever before, increasing demand for replacement tires.
- Electric vehicles wear out tires faster than similar gas vehicles, putting Goodyear in a favorable position in the higher-margin replacement tire market.
- The company's forward P/E was 6 when shares were purchased on March 18-19.
- The company is currently trading at a 37% discount to its book value.
