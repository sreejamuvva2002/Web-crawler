# Rivian Automotive Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-05

**Source:** [Rivian Receiving $1.5 Billion Incentive Package from Georgia | The Truth About Cars](https://www.thetruthaboutcars.com/2022/05/rivian-receiving-1-5-billion-incentive-package-from-georgia/)  ·  `page_059042`

## Facts

- Rivian Automotive Inc., purveyor of the all-electric R1T and R1S, will receive $1.5 billion in incentives from state and local governments to build a new manufacturing facility in Georgia.
- The state is offering a comprehensive incentive package that includes tax breaks to become home to the company’s planned $5 billion assembly plant.
- Under the new agreement, Rivian’s factory would be required to produce 7,500 jobs and its existing investment target by 2028 to receive the full $1.5 billion.
- The facility will include a sizable battery production site.
- The incentive package represents the largest corporate incentivization package in Georgia's history.
- Georgia’s economic development commissioner, Pat Wilson, stated that the upcoming facility represents a major victory placing the region “at the forefront of the electric vehicle revolution.”
- Rivian failed to meet its production goals in 2021.
- Rivian lost a bid to replace the USPs aged fleet.
- Rivian has seen its share price decline since its November IPO.
- Rivian has managed to deliver functional vehicles while some of its peers have not.
- The Georgia plant, located east of Atlanta, would be Rivian’s second U.S. assembly plant, after its plant in Normal, Illinois.
- The company is 20 percent owned by Amazon.com Inc (AMZN.O).
- The company has launched three vehicle models in the U.S.: its R1T pickup, R1S SUV, and EDV delivery van for Amazon.
- Rivian has struggled ramping up production amid supply chain constraints, including a shortage of semiconductors.
- Wall Street investors have been disappointed with the company’s progress and vehicle order numbers.
- Rivian shares have dropped more than 75 percent since the company went public in mid-November.
- Local residents living east of Atlanta have voiced concerns that the new facility would upset their quality of life.
- The State of Georgia seems eager to see new jobs arrive and has stated that the project will follow “locally required standards pertaining to water quality, groundwater recharge and runoff, and local ordinances.”
- Rivian’s Georgia plant will be quite a bit larger than the 5,000-person facility in Illinois.
- The company believes it can be completed by 2024 if everything goes according to plan.
- An executive summary of the project is available for interested parties.
- The Economic Development Agreement (EDA) between the Joint Development Authority (JDA) of Jasper, Morgan, Newton, and Walton counties, the State of Georgia and Rivian was approved and signed by all parties on Monday.
- Rivian stated that the long-term economic partnership promises to deliver value to Rivian, the people of Georgia and their kids’ kids’ kids.
