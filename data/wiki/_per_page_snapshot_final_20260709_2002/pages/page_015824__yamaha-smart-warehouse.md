# Yamaha SMART Warehouse

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2022-06-10

**Source:** [Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation Breaks Ground on Innovative SMART Warehouse in Newnan, Georgia](https://www.theautochannel.com/news/2022/06/10/1154331-yamaha-motor-manufacturing-corporation-breaks-ground-innovative-smart-warehouse-newnan.html)  ·  `page_015824`

## Facts

- Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation (YMMC), based in Newnan, Georgia, broke ground on a new advanced and innovative “SMART” Warehouse.
- The new facility spans 200,000 square feet and is the first-of-its-kind SMART Warehouse for Yamaha Motor Company worldwide.
- The warehouse utilizes state-of-the-art automated storage and retrieval systems.
- SMART stands for Sequenced Material and Reduced Transportation.
- The facility will reduce response time for fulfilling dealer orders for Yamaha WaveRunners, Golf Cars, ATVs, and Side-by-Sides by more than 50 percent.
- The medium parts automated storage system will retrieve and put away 240 containers per hour, compared to the current rate of 170 containers per hour.
- The small parts automated system will handle 2400 containers per hour, compared to the current capability of 550 containers per day.
- Automatic conveyors and tuggers moving parts through the SMART center will enable Yamaha to reduce forklift usage by 50 percent.
- YMMC President Taka Imanishi, YMMC VP Supply Chain Wayne Pierce, YMUS President and CEO Mike Chrzanowski, Newnan Mayor Keith Brady, and Georgia State Senator Matt Brass attended the groundbreaking.
- Mike Chrzanowski stated the warehouse will improve operational efficiency and material flow velocity to factory assembly lines to get products to dealers and customers faster.
- Demand for outdoor recreational products produced at the YMMC factory continues to exceed supply.
- The significant investment in the new facility underscores Yamaha’s long-term commitment to YMMC employees, the City of Newnan, and Coweta County.
- The new system will bring higher paid and more skilled jobs into Coweta County, including robotics experts, software developers, and systems engineers.
- The warehouse was designed by SSI SCHAEFER.
- The facility will include an observation platform for groups such as local school students to learn about manufacturing and automated processes.
- Yamaha expects to complete the SMART Warehouse in 2024.
- Parent company Yamaha Motor Corporation, USA (YMUS) maintains corporate offices in California and two in Georgia, facilities in Wisconsin and Alabama, and factory operations in Tennessee and Georgia.
