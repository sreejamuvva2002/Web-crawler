# Form Energy

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2021-07-26

**Source:** [Form Energy's $20/kWh, 100-hour iron-air battery could be a 'substantial breakthrough' | Utility Dive](https://www.utilitydive.com/news/form-energys-20kwh-100-hour-iron-air-battery-could-be-a-substantial-br/603877/)  ·  `page_050040`

## Facts

- Somerville, Massachusetts-based startup Form Energy announced the chemistry for an iron-air-exchange battery.
- The battery could offer long-duration storage at a price of less than $20/kWh.
- The technology relies on thousands of small iron pellets which rust when exposed to oxygen, then revert back to iron when oxygen is removed.
- Form claims the battery can deliver electricity for 100 hours.
- Form announced a $200 million Series D funding round led by an investment from the innovation fund of steelmaker ArcelorMittal.
- ArcelorMittal is one of the world’s leading iron ore producers.
- ArcelorMittal will non-exclusively supply iron materials developed jointly with Form for use in the batteries.
- Mateo Jaramillo is Form CEO and co-founder.
- Jaramillo does not consider the technology long-duration storage, instead preferring the term 'multi-day storage'.
- The capacity to dispatch energy for 100 hours puts it in a different category than long-duration storage, generally defined as systems with at least 10 hours of duration.
- Jaramillo previously led Tesla’s energy storage arm.
- Jaramillo considers the Form Energy technology complementary, not in competition, with shorter-duration lithium-ion batteries.
- Experts say this balance will be essential to transition the grid to renewable energy.
- Lithium-ion batteries can store energy for hours and distribute it throughout the day.
- A 100% renewable grid will need larger storage systems to tackle the day-to-day or seasonal variability in renewable production.
- High cost and infrastructure difficulties have limited widespread penetration of long-duration technologies on the market.
- Mark Jacobson is director of the Atmosphere/Energy program at Stanford University.
- Jacobson said a $20/kWh cost, if commercial costs end up that low, would be a 'substantial breakthrough'.
- Jacobson stated it 'would enable the rapid transition to 100% clean, renewable electricity on a worldwide scale, while avoiding blackouts, at lower cost than previously thought'.
- Jacobson added in an email that it 'would eliminate the need for any more fossil fuels, nuclear power and bioenergy for electricity faster than we thought'.
- BloombergNEF found that lithium-ion battery pack prices fell to $137/kWh in 2020.
- Projected costs were close to $100/kWh by 2023.
- Manufacturers like Tesla and CATL have dropped prices as low as $80/kWh.
- A March study published in Nature Energy found that the energy capacity cost of long-duration storage technology must fall below $20/kWh to reduce total carbon-free electricity system costs by at least 10%.
- The study found capacity costs would have to drop even lower to displace nuclear and natural gas plants.
- Form will partner with Minnesota electric cooperative Great River Energy on a test project for the iron air exchange battery.
- Construction for the test project is expected in 2023.
- Jaramillo said other test projects are in the works but have not been announced.
- Jaramillo said early on they had an indication that this type of technology had great potential, and now they have to prove the bankability of the asset itself, proving that it is durable and meets the needs of the utilities.
- Jaramillo stated the only way to compare one type of storage to another is in real-world operating conditions.
- Besides the investment from ArcelorMittal, Form Energy has been backed by Breakthrough Energy Ventures, the Bill Gates-led climate investment fund.
- A November 2020 Series C funding round raised $76 million.
- The Series C included investments from Energy Impact Partners, Temasek and NGP Energy Technology Partners III.
