# Alternative Fuel Corridors on U.S. 441 and U.S. 82, Georgia

**Type:** Government Program  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[charging-infrastructure|Charging Infrastructure]]  ·  **Location:** Georgia, United States

## Overview

The Federal Highway Administration designated two Georgia highways (U.S. 441 from Cornelia to Dublin and U.S. 82 from Brunswick to Albany) as alternative fuel corridors to accelerate the deployment of electric vehicle charging stations across approximately 330 miles of roadway.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Designations create a dedicated corridor for EV charging infrastructure, adding roughly 25% (≈330 mi) to the state’s existing network and positioning Georgia to receive federal grant funding for additional stations.

## Investment

- **$5 billion** — Grants to support EV charging stations under the bipartisan infrastructure spending bill

## Key facts

- Two Georgia highways (U.S. 441 from Cornelia to Dublin and U.S. 82 from Brunswick to Albany) will add approximately 330 miles, or 25%, to the state’s EV charging network.
- Designations were selected based on proximity to major job clusters, tourism sites, high EV sales in nearby counties, and hurricane evacuation routes.
- The corridors are expected to improve Georgia’s eligibility for federal funding from the $1.2 trillion bipartisan infrastructure bill, which earmarks $5 billion in grants for EV charging stations.
- GDOT is working with Chicago-based commercial real estate firm JLL to plan a comprehensive network of EV charging stations across the state.
- Georgia currently has about 30,000 electric vehicles on its roads, served by more than 1,300 publicly available charging stations and roughly 3,400 individual outlets.
- A legislative study committee created by the General Assembly during this year’s session will soon begin work on a plan to expand the supply of EV charging stations.
