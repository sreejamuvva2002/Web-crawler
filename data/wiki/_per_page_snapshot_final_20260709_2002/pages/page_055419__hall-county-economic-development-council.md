# Hall County Economic Development Council

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2016-08-02

**Source:** [Gainesville-Hall County Builds on Longstanding Relationships With Japan Trip - Global Atlanta](https://www.globalatlanta.com/gainesville-hall-county-builds-longstanding-relationships-japan-trip/)  ·  `page_055419`

## Facts

- Hall County, home to Gainesville in North Georgia, has attracted the Japanese company that employs the most workers in the state.
- Kubota Manufacturing America Corp. recently expanded its presence in Hall County.
- Kubota’s headquarters in Osaka was visited by a Greater Hall Chamber of Commerce delegation to Japan in late July.
- The chamber used the Japan trip to visit existing Japanese investors and meet new prospects, assisted by Yumiko Nakazono, the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s representative in Tokyo.
- Tim Evans, vice president of economic development for the Chamber, stated the business mission aimed to renew relationships and establish new ones to bring business, jobs, and investment to the community.
- Kubota has spent nearly 50 years building its presence in the county, with operations starting in 1968.
- Kubota constructed a new 500,000-square-foot plant located just three miles from its longest-operating facility.
- Following the expansion and hiring of nearly 600 additional workers, Kubota will employ nearly 3,000 people in Hall County.
- Suppliers such as Shintone USA create additional jobs supporting Kubota’s operations.
- Other Japanese investors and partners operating in the county include Etori, Tatsumi, and American Yazaki.
- Economic development leaders emphasize that expanding existing facilities is crucial for generating new jobs in the state.
- Metro Atlanta Chamber officials stated that “after-care” will be a central tenet of a new plan to attract investment to the entire region.
- Brian Rochester of Rochester & Associates, who chairs the chamber’s economic development council, noted that Gainesville-Hall County supports both the operational needs of Japanese-owned businesses and the quality of life desired by their managers' families.
- Gainesville will host Japanese visitors as one of 15 Georgia cities participating in the Japan-America Grassroots Summit in October.
- The Japan-America Grassroots Summit averages 200 attendees traveling across the Pacific every two years to discuss people-to-people ties.
- Georgia is home to nearly 500 Japanese companies by some counts and has maintained an office in Japan since 1973.
- Hall County leaders reported that workforce development efforts at Lanier Technical College, including manufacturer-focused apprenticeship programs, were progressing positively according to Japanese counterparts.
- The University of North Georgia’s Gainesville campus received a $400,000 grant from the Japan Foundation in May to add Japanese language studies to an East Asian studies program concentration.
- Participants on the Gainesville-Hall County business mission included Perry Barnett (CPA – Rushton), Brian Daniel (Carroll Daniel Construction), Tim Evans (Greater Hall Chamber of Commerce), Jimbo Floyd (Turner, Wood & Smith Insurance), David Lee (Jackson EMC), and Brian Rochester (Rochester & Associates).
