# Daesol Ausys

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [South Korean auto supplier plans $72 million plant in Georgia to build electric vehicle parts - Yahoo News UK](https://uk.news.yahoo.com/south-korean-auto-supplier-plans-221903671.html)  ·  `page_053422`

## Facts

- Daesol Ausys plans to build a $72 million factory in West Point, Georgia.
- The new plant will make luggage boards and covers for compartments in electric vehicles.
- The company will hire more than 140 workers for the new facility.
- Daesol Ausys supplies parts to Hyundai, Kia, and General Motors.
- This will be the third factory in the same West Point industrial park for members of the company's corporate family.
- Daehan Solution opened a million-dollar factory there in 2008 to make headliners and other sound insulation parts for the nearby Kia plant.
- Daesol Material opened a $35 million factory there in 2017 to make automotive fiber padding.
- Daehan Solution currently employs 350 full-time workers.
- Daesol Material currently employs 50 workers.
- Meghan Richardson, an economic developer for the city of West Point, provided employment figures for the existing factories.
- Daehan Solution also has a plant near Montgomery, Alabama, which supplies the Hyundai assembly plant there.
- The announcement follows a wave of electric vehicle suppliers announcing plants in Georgia after Hyundai said in 2022 that it would build a $5.5 billion plant to assemble electric vehicles and batteries in Ellabell, Georgia, near Savannah.
- The Hyundai site in Ellabell could grow to 8,100 employees and is slated to begin producing vehicles in 2025.
- Suppliers for the Hyundai project have pledged to invest nearly $2.2 billion and to hire 5,000 people.
- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp stated that the state's automotive industry employs over 50,000 hardworking Georgians and will continue to grow as e-mobility suppliers choose all corners of the state for the jobs of the future.
- Daesol Ausys is scheduled to begin production in December 2024.
- The company will pay workers $15 to $20 an hour.
- The state will pay to train workers for the new facility.
- The state gave $500,000 last year to help Harris County clear and grade the site and build a road.
- Harris County matched the $500,000 state grant using sales tax collections.
- The city of West Point and Harris County are granting eight years of property tax breaks worth a projected $5.7 million.
- Daesol Ausys could qualify for $875,000 in state income tax credits, at $1,250 per job over five years.
- To qualify for the $875,000 state income tax credits, workers must make at least $31,300 a year.
