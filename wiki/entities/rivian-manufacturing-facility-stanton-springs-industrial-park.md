# Rivian Manufacturing Facility - Stanton Springs Industrial Park

**Type:** Facility  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[vehicle-manufacturing|Vehicle Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** Stanton Springs Industrial Park, Social Circle, Georgia, Walton County, Georgia, United States

## Overview

A planned multi-billion-dollar electric vehicle manufacturing plant by Rivian in the Stanton Springs Industrial Park near Social Circle, Georgia. The project has drawn community opposition and is subject to rezoning and public-meeting processes.

## EV supply-chain relevance

The facility will produce Rivian electric vehicles for customers such as Amazon, expanding EV manufacturing capacity in Georgia.

## Timeline

- **mid-2021** — Joint Development Authority began holding monthly public meetings about the plant site.
- **end of 2023** — Governor Brian Kemp announced the multi-billion-dollar Rivian plant project.
- **Summer 2024 (planned)** — Construction expected to begin on the 2,000-acre facility.
- **2024** — First vehicles slated to be built at the Rivian plant.

## Related organizations & people

- Tommy Wasendorf — Resident protester. Long-time Social Circle resident who voiced opposition at the town hall.
- Joint Development Authority (JDA) — Economic development authority overseeing project. Organized monthly public meetings since mid-2021.
- Walton County Economic Development — Local economic development agency. Director Shane Short communicated protocol and rezoning possibilities.
- Shane Short — Director, Walton County Economic Development. Spokesperson for the county on the project; discussed nondisclosure agreements and rezoning.
- Brian Kemp — Governor of Georgia. Announced the Rivian project at the end of the year.
- Amazon — Customer of Rivian vehicles. Rivian produces electric delivery vans for Amazon.

## Key facts

- More than a hundred residents gathered at Social Circle High School to oppose the EV plant.
- Residents claim planning was done privately and decisions were made at the state capitol.
- The JDA says they recognize public outrage that began after the governor’s announcement.
- About 26 property owners voluntarily sold land for the development; no eminent domain was used.
- Walton County Economic Development director said stopping the project is possible during rezoning but would be difficult.
