# Gainesville-Hall County Metro Area

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2025-01-15

**Source:** [Gainesville-Hall Metro Area #1 in Best Performing Cities - Greater Hall Chamber of Commerce](https://www.ghcc.com/gainesville-hall-metro-area-1-in-best-performing-cities/)  ·  `page_055416`

## Facts

- Gainesville-Hall County Metro Area, Georgia is ranked #1 in the Milken Institute’s 2025 Annual Ranking of the Nation’s Best-Performing Cities among small metropolitan areas.
- The metro area tops the index after being featured among the BPC’s top 10 small metropolitan areas every year since 2016.
- Raleigh, NC tops the index in large metropolitans.
- Gainesville-Hall County and Raleigh’s rankings were fostered by strong job and wage growth and a robust high-tech presence.
- Greater Hall Chamber of Commerce Chairman Glennis Barnes of Georgia Power stated gratitude for the #1 ranking, affirming long-term efforts to invest in infrastructure, support workforce development, and maintain a business-friendly environment.
- Barnes noted that collaborative efforts have achieved consistent economic development success that has made the Gainesville-Hall County Metro Area a top performer for quality jobs, technology investment, and small business growth.
- The Milken Institute Best-Performing Cities index is published annually since 1999 and reflects cities’ effectiveness at leveraging resources to promote economic growth and provide residents with access to essential services and infrastructure.
- The index is based on job creation, wage growth, and the high-tech sector’s output growth, in addition to measures of access to economic opportunities such as housing affordability and broadband coverage.
- Milken Institute senior director of research Maggie Switek, PhD, noted that Raleigh and Gainesville-Hall County have experienced remarkable employment and wage growth coupled with robust performance in their access to economic opportunities indicators.
- The 2025 version of the index emphasizes jobs, wages, high-tech growth, housing affordability, and broadband coverage, in addition to metrics of community resilience and income inequality.
- Gainesville Mayor Sam Couvillon called the Milken Institute selection an incredible honor, reaffirming the city’s meticulous actions and helping identify areas for improvement.
- Mayor Couvillon stated the distinction will further motivate the Gainesville City Council, city management, and staff to keep economic trends and the community moving forward.
- Gainesville-Hall County, Georgia is home to 47 Fortune 500 firms, more than 330 manufacturing and processing concerns, and more than sixty international companies representing twenty-one foreign countries.
- The Gainesville-Hall County Economic Development Council is a public-private partnership of the Greater Hall Chamber of Commerce, Hall County, and the Cities of Gainesville, Flowery Branch, Lula, and Oakwood.
- The Economic Development Council provides one-stop marketing and economic development services to support existing industry retention, workforce development, small business development, and business recruitment and retail development.
