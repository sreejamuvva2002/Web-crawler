## Facts

- Valeo is a leading French automotive technology provider.
- Valeo expects ADAS adoption to accelerate in India’s passenger vehicle sector.
- Valeo anticipates that by 2030, up to 50% of global vehicles will feature Level-2 ADAS.
- Government regulations are expected to support widespread ADAS adoption in India.
- Jayakumar G is Group President and Managing Director of Valeo India.
- Jayakumar G noted that ADAS is likely to grow faster than expected in India, with every car requiring some form of ADAS functionality in the near future.
- Jayakumar G highlighted the government’s role in promoting ADAS technology.
- The front camera, a key safety component, is expected to become a regulatory requirement.
- Valeo has developed an innovative approach to leverage front camera computing power for additional ADAS features.
- This approach could pave the way for more affordable, software-defined vehicles.
- Valeo plans to localize ADAS technology in India.
- Jayakumar G emphasized that the company will ramp up its business plan once it sees higher volumes and customer orders.
- Valeo successfully localized EV components such as the three-in-one unit and ultrasonic parking sensors.
- Volume growth will lead to greater localization and lower costs, which is how Valeo will achieve cost parity for ADAS technology in India.
- Valeo is a major supplier of ADAS components globally, including sensors, high-performance computing units, and software.
- Valeo has worked with several car manufacturers, including Honda, to develop advanced ADAS functionalities.
- The Honda Legend uses Valeo’s Scala 3D LiDARs and front cameras to achieve Level-3 ADAS functionality.
- In India, Valeo supplies front cameras for the ‘Honda Sensing’ Level-2 ADAS suite available in models like the Honda Elevate, City, and Amaze.
- Valeo is aware that India’s road infrastructure and driving behaviors present unique challenges for ADAS deployment.
- Companies like Tata Motors and Mahindra & Mahindra have successfully implemented ADAS in models like the Curvv SUV-coupe and XUV700.
- Valeo is currently fine-tuning its ADAS technology for the Indian market by collecting data through a demo vehicle tested across various cities.
- This data collection exercise aims to tailor ADAS to local conditions and is expected to be completed in the coming months.
- Valeo’s Indian technology centers are located in Chennai, Bengaluru, and Hyderabad.
- These centers play a key role in global R&D activities, with over 50% of their work focused on ADAS development.
- India contributes significantly to Valeo’s global software development efforts, with over 1,700 engineers dedicated to software, electronics, and cybersecurity.
- This expertise is crucial as Valeo anticipates a shift toward Software-Defined Vehicles (SDVs) in India.
- Valeo believes that as vehicle architectures evolve globally, the adoption of centralized computing platforms will reduce costs, making advanced features like ADAS more accessible in India.
- Jayakumar G concluded that as India moves toward SDVs, the same advancements will be applied there, bringing benefits to both automakers and consumers.
- Valeo is a global leader in the automotive industry, specializing in designing, manufacturing, and supplying cutting-edge technology solutions to enhance vehicle performance, comfort, and safety.
- Valeo is headquartered in France.
- Valeo operates across four key business areas: powertrain systems, thermal systems, visibility systems, and comfort and driving assistance systems.
- The company is a pioneer in developing innovative solutions for electric vehicles, autonomous driving, and energy efficiency.
- Valeo has a robust presence in over 30 countries.
