# Kentucky EV & Battery Industry

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2022-12-22

**Source:** [Supplying success: Why Kentucky's EV & battery industry momentum is here to stay - Building Kentucky](https://buildingkentucky.com/news/supplying-success-why-kentuckys-ev-battery-industry-momentum-is-here-to-stay/)  ·  `page_050028`

## Facts

- The future of the automotive industry is electric, with rapid growth in companies directly supporting the EV market.
- Kentucky has positioned itself as a national leader within the EV market in the United States.
- Gov. Andy Beshear stated that the EV and battery infrastructure being created will make Kentucky an industry leader and attract quality companies and jobs for decades to come.
- Beshear claimed the Commonwealth has resources making it the perfect location for growth within the EV and energy storage industries.
- Beshear stated Kentucky will be the nation’s top producer of electric-vehicle batteries.
- Within the past 14 months, Kentucky announced its two largest economic development projects in its history.
- These announcements established Kentucky as the new EV battery capital in the United States.
- In August, Gov. Beshear joined state and local officials and representatives from Japan-based Envision AESC to break ground on a $2 billion gigafactory in Bowling Green’s Kentucky Transpark.
- The 30 GWh plant will create 2,000 skilled jobs in South Central Kentucky.
- The plant will produce battery cells and modules that will power up to 300,000 next-generation EVs by 2027.
- The batteries will be manufactured for multiple global automotive manufacturers.
- Jeff Deaton, managing director of Envision AESC U.S., said the decision to invest in Kentucky was an obvious choice due to the readiness of the skilled workforce required for a project of this scale.
- Deaton noted that batteries are fast becoming a strategically important industry powering the United States’ move to electric vehicles and Kentucky’s economy and jobs.
- In September 2021, Gov. Beshear and executives from Ford Motor Co. and SK On announced the BlueOvalSK Battery Park in Hardin County.
- The $5.8 billion project is set to create 5,000 jobs in the heart of the state.
- The facility will be home to two battery plants to power the next generation of Ford and Lincoln electric vehicles.
- In August 2022, Ascend Elements announced an investment of $310 million to create 250 full-time jobs in Christian County.
- Ascend Elements is a producer of advanced, sustainable battery materials made from recycled lithium-ion batteries.
- Thanks to more than $480 million in federal grants awarded by the U.S. Department of Energy, the Ascend Elements project now includes a nearly $1 billion investment and will create more than 400 full-time jobs.
- The expanded project is known as Apex 1 and will establish the company’s largest U.S. operation.
- CEO Mike O’Kronley stated that clean energy and climate technology industries are bringing good jobs to communities across the country.
- O’Kronley noted they are building a domestic source of sustainable lithium-ion cathode material for EV batteries that does not exist anywhere in the United States.
- The location will house the Hydro-to-Cathode process, a closed-loop direct precursor synthesis manufacturing system that generates no toxic waste and minimal carbon emissions.
- The operation will produce enough recycled battery precursor and sustainable cathode active materials to equip up to 250,000 electric vehicles annually.
- Ascend Elements’ Kentucky operation will serve a range of EV-related customers and build on partnerships with American Honda Motor Co., EcoPro Group, and SK Battery America.
- In January 2022, Piston Automotive LLC increased the scope of a Jefferson County expansion to include 117 full-time jobs with a $26.3 million-plus investment.
- Piston Automotive LLC is a provider of automotive assemblies, including advanced electric- and hybrid-vehicle battery systems.
- The expansion includes a new production line and increased capabilities to support growing market demand for the EV industry in Kentucky and throughout the region.
- Kentucky leverages its central geographic location, logistics strength, and skilled workforce as a premier destination for the EV industry.
