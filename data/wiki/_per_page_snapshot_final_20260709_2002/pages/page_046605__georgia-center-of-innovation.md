# Georgia Center of Innovation

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2025-04-24

**Source:** [Georgia Center of Innovation Hosts 2025 Logistics Summit | Physical Internet Center](https://www.picenter.gatech.edu/news/georgia-center-innovation-hosts-2025-logistics-summit)  ·  `page_046605`

## Facts

- The Georgia Center of Innovation, a strategic arm of the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD), hosted hundreds of attendees at the 2025 Georgia Logistics Summit.
- Established in 2009 as the first state-led event of its kind, the Georgia Logistics Summit is one of the Southeast’s key logistics and supply chain events connecting industry professionals for networking and knowledge-sharing.
- The economic impact of Georgia’s transportation and logistics industry was $107 billion in 2023, according to an economic impact study by the University of Georgia’s Selig Center for Economic Growth.
- Georgia’s transportation and logistics industries supported more than 578,000 jobs, representing one in nine jobs in the state.
- From 2010 to 2023, transportation and logistics jobs in Georgia grew by 68%, outpacing the national growth rate of 52%.
- In 2023 and 2024 alone, new logistics and distribution sector investments, including cold storage and ecommerce fulfillment centers, totaled $3.8 billion and created over 9,000 new jobs.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that decades of strategic investment in ports, rail lines, highways, and air cargo capabilities have led to record-breaking economic investments and trade.
- The Logistics Summit brings together private sector, government, and education leaders to learn from experts, exchange best practices, and explore opportunities in the rapidly evolving logistics landscape.
- Georgia Department of Transportation Commissioner Russell R. McMurry highlighted strategic investments and how GDOT is leveraging technology to improve freight flow.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp and the General Assembly allocated $1 billion to the newly created Georgia Freight Program over the past two years.
- Additional transportation infrastructure investments are advancing the timing for key planned projects to maintain and improve Georgia’s interstate highway system, roads, and bridges.
- Georgia’s multimodal transportation network carried nearly half a billion tons of freight in 2019, valued at $673 trillion.
- Projections show that freight volume is expected to nearly double to 900 million tons by 2050, with freight value more than doubling today’s value by the same year.
- Working with partners including the Center of Innovation and the Georgia Institute of Technology, GDOT is focusing on enhancing safety and efficiency.
- GDOT projects include adding 50% more truck parking and installing fiber internet on Georgia interstates.
- Georgia Ports Authority Vice President of Operations Susan Gardner provided updates on strategic investments to expand port capacity and leverage live data to improve safety, track vessel productivity and containers, and eliminate congestion.
- The Georgia Ports Authority is investing $4.5 billion in improvements over the next decade as part of its port master plan to expand cargo handling capabilities and support future supply chain requirements.
- A feature panel titled “AI and Beyond: Embracing Digital Transformation in Logistics” included leadership from The Home Depot, Havertys Furniture, TOTO USA, and research perspectives from the Georgia Institute of Technology.
- Panelists highlighted a three- to five-year outlook for the industry and provided insights on how AI and automation are transforming supply chains.
- Center of Innovation Executive Director David Nuckolls stated that digital technologies are reshaping supply chains and the Center’s specialized logistics team works alongside the industry to position businesses for growth.
- University System of Georgia Chancellor Sonny Perdue and Technical College System of Georgia Commissioner Greg Dozier provided updates on how state post-secondary institutions are developing a globally competitive workforce.
- Discussion focused on ensuring taught skills match logistics company needs, including creative problem-solving and effective use of new AI and automation tools.
- The breadth of Georgia’s technical college programs was discussed, including the High Demand Career Initiatives program.
- A pilot program called “Dual Achievement” enrolls students who withdrew from high school in a technical college program, enabling them to earn a high school diploma alongside a technical college certificate, diploma, or degree.
- Panelists emphasized helping students progress faster, upskilling the existing workforce, and fostering connections between industry leaders and educators.
- The Center of Innovation’s Logistics Industry Advisory Board recognized winners of the inaugural Future Innovators in Supply Chain competition, which invited college students to create videos about supply chain careers.
- Led by professor Parisa Pooyan, student team “The Masters of Logistinomics” from Kennesaw State University won first prize and a $3,000 grant for the university.
- The winning video was created by Eli Hampton, Angeline Harris, Joe Johnson, and Dana Pazhouhesh.
- Participants in the 2025 Georgia Logistics Summit also included leaders from S&P Global Market Intelligence, Boost Phase Ventures, and M.D. Livingstone Consulting.
- The Center of Innovation helps Georgia businesses find solutions by connecting them with experts, external partners, and independent mentors across six key industries: Aerospace, AgTech, Energy Technology, Information Technology, Logistics, and Manufacturing.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) serves as the state’s sales and marketing arm and lead agency for attracting new business investment, encouraging industry expansion, and locating new markets for Georgia products.
