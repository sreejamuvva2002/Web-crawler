# JAC Products

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [JAC Products Uses Flex Shaft Drills for Increased Roof Rail Production - Bernard and Company](https://bernardandcompany.com/jac-products-uses-flex-shaft-drills-for-increased-roof-rail-production/)  ·  `page_056330`

## Facts

- JAC Products is a Tier One automotive supplier located in Franklin, Georgia.
- The facility puts 30-40 million holes into approximately 6 million pieces of extruded and formed aluminum each year.
- The products made at this facility are used as roof rack rails on nearly every major automobile, mini-van, and truck brand.
- JAC holds a majority share of the North American vehicle market for these roof rail components.
- Mike Traylor serves as the tool room & die shop manager at JAC Products.
- JAC operates hundreds of drilling stations in its Franklin factory, located on the banks of the Chattahoochee River in southeastern Georgia.
- The company’s extrusion department processes millions of pounds of aluminum annually from 6061, 6063, and 6463 alloys.
- Each dedicated production station produces an average of 100,000-200,000 left- and right-side rail sets per year for each specific automobile model.
- Suhner Automation Division, based in Rome, Georgia, supplies flex shaft and direct motor-driven drilling units to JAC Products.
- JAC has maintained a partnership with Suhner for nearly 20 years.
- The flex shaft design removes drive motors from the cutting area, improving accessibility for operators and maintenance personnel.
- Mike Traylor states that JAC achieves upwards of 60-70% greater output from its equipment since beginning to use Suhner drilling solutions.
- Previous drilling equipment was prone to breakdowns and service problems, causing unacceptable delays in production during the industry's transition to just-in-time manufacturing.
- Charles Stitcher is the regional marketing manager at Suhner who presented the company’s flex shaft drilling solutions to JAC.
- The flex shaft design allows machine designers to position drilling mechanisms in various configurations and tighter proximities to meet required accuracies and secondary counterbore operations.
- Mike Traylor has overseen nearly 500 machine builds at JAC and notes the adaptability of the flex shaft drill across a wide variety of applications.
- For a Ford vehicle rail set, up to 11 drills and a cutoff operation are now performed simultaneously instead of sequentially by a single operator.
- Output for one specific job increased from 1200 sets per day to 1200 sets per hour.
- Various Suhner motor-mounted drills are utilized on dedicated machining operations, including a specially designed system for sawing.
- Alberto Blanco is the Senior Launch Manager at JAC Products.
- JAC must hold +/- 0.1-0.2mm tolerances on drilling and +/- 0.5mm on cutoff lengths to meet customer requirements.
- Mike Traylor notes that Suhner rebuild kits containing o-rings and seals simplify maintenance for the virtually non-stop used drills.
- Jeff Cavalier is the engineering & facilities manager at JAC Products.
- The compact work area concept enabled by the equipment saves operator steps, representing a huge annual savings for the company without sacrificing safety.
- SUHNER INDUSTRIAL PRODUCTS, CORP. is located at Hwy 411 S./Suhner Drive, P.O. Box 1234, Rome, GA 30162.
- Lee Coleman works in the Automation Division at Suhner Industrial Products Corp.
