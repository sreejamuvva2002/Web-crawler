# U.S. Department of Energy

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2022-10-20

**Source:** [DOE taps 20 companies to receive $2.8B for battery manufacturing, minerals processing build-out | Utility Dive](https://www.utilitydive.com/news/doe-battery-manufacturing-minerals-processing-award/634562/)  ·  `page_060519`

## Facts

- The U.S. Department of Energy selected 20 companies to receive a combined $2.8 billion for projects that expand domestic battery manufacturing and minerals processing necessary to support the transition to electric vehicles.
- DOE said 21 total projects span a dozen states and will focus on the extraction and processing of lithium, graphite and other battery materials.
- The projects include component manufacturing and demonstration projects, including manufacturing components from recycled materials.
- EV sales are growing quickly.
- The Biden administration has taken steps to develop a domestic battery supply chain.
- Secretary of Energy Jennifer Granholm stated that producing advanced batteries and components domestically will accelerate the transition to electric vehicles.
- According to DOE, almost all lithium, graphite, battery-grade nickel and other materials are produced abroad, with China controlling the supply chains.
- Electrification advocates said the funding represents a real commitment from the federal government to securing domestic supply chains.
- Zero Emission Transportation Association Executive Director Joe Britton said the initiative demonstrates getting serious about competing with China, developing a robust domestic electric vehicle supply chain, and leading the world in clean transportation technology.
- American Battery Technology will receive $57.7 million for development of a commercial-scale facility to demonstrate a new process for manufacturing battery cathode grade lithium hydroxide near Tonopah, Nevada.
- Piedmont Lithium will receive $141.7 million to develop a facility in McMinn County, Tennessee.
- The Piedmont Lithium facility will be capable of producing 30,000 metric tons per year of lithium hydroxide for the domestic battery and EV market.
- This production capacity will double the lithium hydroxide production capacity currently available in the United States.
- 6k Inc. will receive $50 million to develop domestic production of multiple battery chemistries while demonstrating the ability to recycle end-of-life batteries.
- The goal of 6k Inc.'s project is to free the U.S. from its current dependence on conflict nations.
- A final decision on site locations for 6k Inc. is expected in December.
- The announced funding will support development of enough battery-grade lithium for 2 million EVs annually.
- DOE stated the funding is the “first phase” of $7 billion provided by the bipartisan infrastructure law to build out the domestic battery supply chain.
- DOE announced the launch of the American Battery Material Initiative, a dedicated effort to align dozens of federal programs in growing the battery supply chain.
- The initiative will coordinate domestic and international efforts to accelerate permitting for critical minerals projects.
- DOE noted its commitment to the environment and outreach to impacted communities as part of the initiative.
