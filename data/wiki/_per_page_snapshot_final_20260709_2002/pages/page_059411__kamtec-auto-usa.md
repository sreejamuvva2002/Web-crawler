# Kamtec Auto USA

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-06

**Source:** [Kamtec Auto USA to bring $9.2 million investment, 50 jobs to Auburn - Made in Alabama](https://www.madeinalabama.com/2026/06/kamtec-auto-usa-to-bring-9-2-million-investment-50-jobs-to-auburn/)  ·  `page_059411`

## Facts

- Kamtec Auto USA announced it will open its first U.S. manufacturing facility in Auburn, Alabama.
- The new operation represents a $9.2 million investment.
- The facility will create about 50 jobs over the next three years.
- The facility will provide advanced technical components for engine control and electric management in hybrid vehicles.
- Kamtec was established in 2001 and is an affiliate of the Seohan Group headquartered in Seoul.
- Seohan Group has operated in Auburn since 2007.
- Kamtec will initially operate from a leased facility in Auburn.
- Kamtec plans to relocate to a newly constructed dedicated manufacturing facility to be built by Seohan Auto USA.
- Alabama Secretary of Commerce Ellen McNair welcomed Kamtec to Auburn, expressing confidence that the company will find the skilled workforce and ready community partners needed to be successful.
- Ellen McNair stated this is a win for Alabama and an example of how automotive manufacturers in the state create opportunities by capturing follow-up investments from suppliers.
- Auburn Mayor Ron Anders thanked Kamtec leadership for selecting Auburn, noting that private investment and quality jobs are essential to the community's long-term economic health.
- Mayor Ron Anders noted Kamtec’s commitment to innovation and eco-friendly solutions aligns with Auburn residents’ sustainability values and the engineering expertise at Auburn University.
- Kamtec Auto USA President Chang-Yeong Seo stated Auburn’s proximity to Hyundai Motor’s plant in Montgomery and Kia’s plant in West Point, Georgia, will allow the company to manufacture and supply its products locally for the U.S. market.
- Kamtec plans to partner with Auburn University to identify talent for technical positions.
- Kamtec expressed gratitude for the support of the City of Auburn and State of Alabama, aiming to contribute to balanced, sustainable growth for customers, partners, employees, and communities.
