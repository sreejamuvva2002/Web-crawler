# Honda

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-03-12

**Source:** [Facing heavy losses, Honda cancels its three US-made electric vehicles - Ars Technica](https://arstechnica.com/cars/2026/03/facing-heavy-losses-honda-cancels-its-three-us-made-electric-vehicles/)  ·  `page_008926`

## Facts

- Last year, Honda gave Ars a tour of its manufacturing facilities in Ohio.
- The Anna Engine Plant underwent a transformation adding a massive die-cast operation to make electric vehicle battery packs alongside the lines that make engines at Anna.
- The Marysville Auto Plant received a new section filled with robots ready to incorporate three new Honda and Acura EVs into the production mix alongside Accords and Integras.
- Earlier today, Honda announced it is facing heavy losses for the financial year between $5.1 billion and $7 billion (820 billion–1.12 trillion yen).
- To help stanch these losses, Honda canceled the Honda 0 SUV, Honda 0 sedan, and the electric Acura RSX.
- The canceled EVs were revealed at CES last year in a “nearly production” state.
- Honda cited ongoing trade war chaos and tariffs as a primary reason for killing off its new EVs before they reach the market, noting they have eaten into the profitability of the cars it imports into the US.
- A second reason is the US government’s revanchist decision to cease enforcing emissions and fuel economy standards on the auto industry.
- Honda stated that “striving for carbon neutrality” is a “responsibility Honda… must fulfill for the future,” but implied this responsibility only applies when being forced by a government.
