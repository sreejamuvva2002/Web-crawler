# Continental

## Facts

- German auto supplier Continental announced a €4-billion (RM18.7-billion) deal to sell its ContiTech division to US private equity firm Lone Star Funds.
- The sale is part of Continental's major restructuring to slim down operations and focus on its traditional tyre business.
- ContiTech manufactures plastic and rubber products for industrial clients.
- In addition to the €4-billion sale price, Continental could receive up to €250 million more in coming years depending on the business’s performance.
- The deal is expected to be finalised by the end of 2026.
- Funds from the sale will be used to pay down debt and distribute around €2.5 billion to shareholders.
- Continental stated that with the planned sale of ContiTech, it is completing its strategic realignment.
- Last year, Continental spun off a division focused on car components including braking systems and sensors as a separate entity called Aumovio.
- The group has announced thousands of job cuts in recent years alongside other auto suppliers and manufacturers in Germany.
- The wider European auto sector is facing fierce Chinese competition, weak demand in key markets, and a troubled shift to electric vehicles.
