# Wabash National

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2026-01-08

**Source:** [Semi-Trailer Manufacturer Wabash National Closing Facility in Little Falls](https://lptv.org/semi-trailer-manufacturer-wabash-national-closing-facility-in-little-falls/)  ·  `page_014757`

## Facts

- Wabash National, a semi-trailer manufacturer, is closing its facility in Little Falls.
- More than 50 jobs will be eliminated following the announcement of the Little Falls facility closure.
- Wabash National first opened its Little Falls facility in 2017.
- At opening, the company promised an $11 million investment to renovate the former Larson Boat Works plant and to employ over 150 people by 2022.
- The Little Falls facility is shutting down less than 10 years after opening.
- Wabash National announced the closure of the plant and subsequent layoffs due to "idling manufacturing operations" on Jan. 5.
- Workers will be let go starting in early March, with final layoffs being finalized by April 17.
- Little Falls mayor Greg Zylka stated that city officials had worked together with Wabash National for many years to bring the company to the area and celebrated its grand opening.
- City officials are beginning meetings to help workers and the community affected by the plant's closure.
- Mayor Greg Zylka noted that losing a large business hurts the community, especially with rising levy rates and cost of goods, which could be devastating to families.
- The city has been in contact with various manufacturers interested in expanding existing businesses or welcoming new businesses and workers to the region.
- City officials are working with Barrett Petfood to try to expand their facility.
- Other companies have contacted the city looking for land to build.
- Wabash National also announced it would be closing a plant in Goshen, Indiana.
- The closure of the Goshen, Indiana plant will lay off an additional 144 workers.
