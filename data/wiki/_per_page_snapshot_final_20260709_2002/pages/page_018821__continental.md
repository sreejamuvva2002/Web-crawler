# Continental

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Continental to Discontinue Operations at Georgia Textile Plant - TRAC](https://tracanada.ca/industry-news/continental-to-discontinue-operations-at-georgia-textile-plant/)  ·  `page_018821`

## Facts

- Continental announced its decision to discontinue operations at its Aldora Mills textile manufacturing plant in Barnesville, Georgia by the end of 2026.
- The Aldora Mills facility employs approximately 230 people.
- The plant produces textile reinforcement materials, including tire cord fabric, hose yarn, and knitted fabric.
- These materials are used exclusively within Continental’s Tires and ContiTech group sectors.
- Continental acquired the Aldora Mills facility in 1988.
- The decision to discontinue operations follows a comprehensive business review aimed at safeguarding the company’s profitability, competitiveness, and business performance in the Americas region.
- The plant has not been cost competitive for many years despite continuous turnaround efforts.
- Ongoing changes in global market dynamics and supply chains necessitated the discontinuation decision.
- Continental reaffirms its commitment to its customers and partners in the United States.
- Continental operates tire manufacturing plants in Plymouth, Indiana; Clinton, Mississippi; Sumter, South Carolina; and Mount Vernon, Illinois.
- Continental’s U.S. tire plants have an annual production capacity of more than 16 million tires.
- Continental employs more than 8,800 people in the U.S. across its Tires and ContiTech group sectors.
- Over the last decade alone, Continental has invested around 1.5 billion USD in its tire manufacturing operations in the United States.
- In October, Continental announced a more than 85 million USD investment to expand manufacturing operations at its ContiTech plant in Mount Pleasant, Iowa.
- In September 2024, Continental broke ground on its first fully owned tire distribution center in the Dallas–Fort Worth metroplex.
- The new Dallas–Fort Worth distribution center is scheduled to open in early 2026.
- The new distribution center will enhance service levels and customer support across the region.
- The new facility complements seven partner-owned distribution centers that currently serve the U.S. market.
- Continental’s main priority regarding the Aldora Mills closure is to support all affected employees.
- In collaboration with local partners, Continental will offer career counselling and access to employment resources for affected workers.
