# Freyr Battery

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-02-11

**Source:** [Norwegian Battery Firm Pulls Plug on $2.6B Georgia Plant  - Global Atlanta](https://www.globalatlanta.com/norwegian-battery-firm-pulls-plug-on-2-6b-georgia-plant/)  ·  `page_000814`

## Facts

- A Norwegian firm has pulled the plug on a $2.6 billion factory in Newnan that was set to make utility-scale battery energy storage systems.
- Freyr Battery informed the Coweta County Industrial Development Authority of its pullback in a letter last month.
- Executives officially announced the plan during the authority’s board meeting on Feb. 6.
- The company said it would offload the 368-acre site to an undisclosed buyer for $50 million.
- The proceeds from the site sale will be used to repay the state and county for grants totaling $27 million toward site acquisition and preparation.
- Coweta County contributed $20 million to win the investment.
- A state deal-closing fund contributed another $7 million.
- According to the Atlanta Journal-Constitution, Freyr’s state and local incentive package was valued at $358 million.
- Property tax abatements and statutory incentives made up the majority of the total incentive package.
- Statutory incentives are doled out over time and are tied to jobs created and ongoing expenditures on manufacturing equipment, salaries, and research and development.
- The withdrawal wipes out 724 promised jobs.
- Freyr’s departure deals a blow to Georgia’s booming new-energy sector, which has landed more than $20 billion in projects focused on electric vehicles, batteries, and solar power.
- The $7.6 billion Hyundai Meta Plant near Savannah has already kicked into high gear.
- SK Battery’s $2.6 billion complex is located in Commerce.
- Hanwha Q Cells is located in Dalton and has helped the firm become the U.S.’s largest solar panel producer.
- Other major projects in Georgia remain either under construction or stuck in neutral.
- Rivian’s $5 billion automotive plant east of Atlanta was delayed last March.
- A $6.6 billion Department of Energy loan approved in the final days of the Biden administration could revive the Rivian project.
- Joint-venture battery investments by Hyundai in partnership with SK in Cartersville are valued at $5 billion.
- Joint-venture battery investments by Hyundai in partnership with LG Energy Solution, co-located with the Meta Plant, are valued at $4.3 billion.
- These joint-venture projects have yet to come on line.
- The longer such projects remain in development, the greater the chance supportive underlying policies could shift with trade patterns and political winds.
- Even before taking office in January, President Trump called to kill the $7,500 EV tax incentive included in the Biden administration’s Inflation Reduction Act.
- Freyr executives reportedly said in August that a glut of Chinese batteries was reducing prices.
- The glut of Chinese batteries is impeding efforts to raise capital for battery production.
- In announcing the Georgia factory in 2022, Freyr framed domestically produced energy storage in national security terms.
- This framing was given China’s dominant hold on the global battery supply chain.
- Freyr’s Newnan project was initially meant to address the mismatch between when renewable energy is produced and when it is eventually used.
- Battery-energy storage systems (BESS) allow the grid to store renewable energy when produced and release it when demand spikes.
- Freyr’s new leadership decided to move upstream by producing solar panels.
- Executives said producing solar panels would generate immediate sales and bolster the startup’s financial position.
- Producing solar panels could also increase overall demand for the energy storage systems Freyr hopes to eventually bring to the United States.
- In December, Freyr acquired the U.S. manufacturing assets of Trina Solar.
- Trina Solar is a Chinese firm that had set up a factory in Wilmer, Texas, almost 20 miles south of Dallas.
- The $340 million deal gave Freyr immediate access to 5GW worth of solar module production.
- The acquisition made Freyr the third-largest domestic module producer right off the bat.
- Freyr is searching for a separate U.S. site to make solar cells in a push for vertical integration in that supply chain.
- American-made modules, cells, and raw materials qualify for tax rebates under the IRA.
- These IRA tax rebates were a key driver of Freyr’s move to Newnan.
- The pending sale of the Newnan property was set to close on Feb. 15.
- Georgia does not seem to be in consideration for Freyr’s new solar manufacturing facility.
- Freyr said it is moving its corporate headquarters to Austin, Texas.
- Austin is a tech hub just a few hours’ drive from its newly acquired Dallas factory.
- The Dallas factory employs more than 1,000 people.
- Q Cells is the No. 1 panel producer in the U.S.
- Q Cells is spending $2.5 billion across two sites in Cartersville and Dalton to build out more module capacity.
- The project includes domestic sourcing for solar components like wafers, cells, and ingots in Cartersville.
- Freyr went public on the New York Stock Exchange in 2021 via a merger with Alussa Energy.
- The merger raised $704 million, including $115 million from Koch Strategic Platforms.
- A few months after going public, Freyr and KSP launched a $70 million joint venture.
- The joint venture aimed to create a U.S. counterpart to Freyr’s European planned giga-factory above the Arctic Circle.
- They licensed battery technology from 24M.
- Freyr has since ended its commercial arrangement with 24M.
- Freyr has retained its cathode active material factory in Finland.
- The Finnish factory is backed by a €122 million grant.
- The Newnan project was announced in November 2022.
- The project aimed to produce 34 GW worth of battery cells in its first phase.
