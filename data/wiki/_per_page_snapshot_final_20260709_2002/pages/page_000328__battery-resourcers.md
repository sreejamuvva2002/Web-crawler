# Battery Resourcers

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2022-01-05

**Source:** [$43M battery recycling plant set for Georgia, hiring 150 | AP News](https://apnews.com/article/technology-business-georgia-atlanta-massachusetts-3575e1bee53a6e1858006f96f044446e)  ·  `page_000328`

## Facts

- Aims to recycle lithium-ion batteries from vehicles and other uses.
- Will build its first large plant in suburban Atlanta, investing $43 million.
- Hiring 150 people for the new facility.
- Battery Resourcers is based in Worcester, Massachusetts.
- The company will take over an existing building in Covington, Georgia.
- Plans to ramp up to full operation by August.
- Betting that the battery supply chain, currently concentrated in Asia, is shifting to other regions.
- Claims its recycling process will be cleaner and cheaper than mining new materials.
- CEO Michael O’Kronley stated that automotive manufacturers have many discarded batteries and scrap with few responsible and cost-effective disposal options.
- The recycling process involves taking apart, discharging, and shredding batteries before processing the material.
- Produces recovered metals including lithium, cobalt, nickel, and manganese, as well as graphite and other engineered ingredients for new batteries.
- Uses methods claimed to recover more than 97% of metals with zero toxic waste.
- Recovered material outperforms newly mined ingredients.
- The Covington plant will have an annual recycling capacity of 30,000 metric tons (33,000 tons) of batteries.
- This capacity equals approximately 70,000 vehicle batteries per year.
- The project is part of Georgia officials' efforts to build an electric vehicle supply chain.
- SK Innovation is building a $2.6 billion battery plant in Commerce, northeast of Atlanta, which will employ 2,600 workers.
- Rivian Automotive announced plans for a $5 billion battery and assembly plant just east of Covington projected to employ 7,500 workers.
- Vice President of Marketing & Government Affairs Roger Lin stated the Georgia site was chosen for its proximity to EV and battery makers and its strong workforce.
- Georgia Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that battery recycling is a vital part of the EV supply chain and reducing reliance on unstable global regions is crucial.
- The company operates pilot plants in Westborough, Massachusetts, and Novi, Michigan, in addition to Worcester.
- Plans to build two additional plants in Europe by the end of the year.
- Aims to eventually expand operations to Asia with a goal of processing 150,000 metric tons (165,000 tons) of material per year.
- Has raised more than $90 million from investors.
- Signed a contract to process spent batteries from Honda and Acura vehicles.
- Investors include the venture capital arms of Jaguar Land Rover and Hitachi.
- The company will receive a competitive incentive package from Georgia.
- Can claim an income tax credit allowing an annual deduction of $3,000 per job from state income taxes, up to $2.25 million over five years.
- Workers must earn at least $28,000 a year to qualify for the income tax credit.
- May also receive local property tax breaks.
