# Club Car

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [State and local leaders join Club Car in celebrating its new Appling facility - The Augusta Press](https://theaugustapress.com/state-and-local-leaders-join-club-car-in-celebrating-its-new-appling-facility/)  ·  `page_018431`

## Facts

- Club Car hosted a ribbon-cutting ceremony for its new Appling facility on Thursday morning.
- Governor Brian Kemp attended the ribbon-cutting ceremony.
- Columbia County Commissioners Connie Melear, Alison Couch, and Chairman Doug Duncan attended the ceremony and made remarks.
- City Councilmember Richard Bowman attended on behalf of Grovetown.
- Mayor Roxanne Whitaker attended on behalf of Harlem.
- Georgia Senators Lee Anderson, Harold Jones, and Chuck Payne represented the state at the event.
- State Representatives Mark Newton, Lynn Gladney, Trenton Howard, Gloria Fraser, Jodi Lott, and Gary Richardson represented the state at the event.
- Club Car announced plans last October to launch a new manufacturing plant in a 105,391 square-foot building it is leasing in White Oak Industrial Park.
- The Development Authority of Columbia County manages White Oak Industrial Park.
- The new facility is located at 780 Innovation Pkwy.
- The project represents an $8 million investment.
- The new facility sits across the street east to Club Car’s existing half-million square foot distribution center at 750 Innovation Pkwy.
- Both Club Car buildings are located across from the Amazon Fulfillment Center.
- The plant will establish U.S. manufacturing of the Club Car Urban, a new street-legal, lightweight utility vehicle.
- The Club Car Urban's design comes from Polish electric vehicle company Melex.
- Club Car acquired Melex via its purchase of Danish EV manufacturer Garia in 2022.
- Governor Kemp emphasized the manufacturer’s contribution to Georgia’s place in the growing regional electric vehicle sector during his address.
- Governor Kemp stated: “We often tout these incredible investments that we’re seeing… in the auto manufacturer area, but we know that true e-mobility supply chains continue to include great products like those offered by Club Car.”
- Club Car CEO and President Mark Wagner recognized Kemp’s pro-EV initiatives during his remarks.
- Wagner highlighted the company’s reliance on lithium as a key element in the EV industry's supply chain that enabled Club Car’s growth.
- Wagner stated: “It’s wild for us, because we’ve always been in this [electric vehicle] space.”
- Wagner added: “This product [Club Car Urban] wouldn’t be possible without lithium. These electric vehicles are very capable, high range high torque. So it’s allowing us to build on that kind of EV purpose in the corridor.”
