# Jackson Alliance for Economic Development

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2021-12

**Source:** [Jackson Alliance helping to establish Georgia as advanced manufacturing hub - AI Online -](https://ai-online.com/2021/12/jackson-alliance-helping-to-establish-georgia-as-advanced-manufacturing-hub/)  ·  `page_001684`

## Facts

- Georgia is emerging as a national leader in advanced manufacturing thanks to investment attracted through strong international relationships.
- The Jackson Alliance for Economic Development serves as the central economic development organization for Jackson County, Georgia.
- The Jackson Alliance is organized under the Jackson County Area Chamber of Commerce and has been successful in attracting investment.
- Duckyang, a supplier of automotive battery modules and energy storage systems to SK Battery America (SKBA), announced it would invest US$10 million in its first US manufacturing facility in Jackson County.
- South Korean companies are major investors in Georgia, with total investments from South Korean firms in 2020 reaching US$1.3 billion.
- South Korean firms created over 2,700 jobs in Georgia in 2020.
- In June 2020, SK Innovation increased its investment in its Georgia manufacturing plant by US$940 million.
- In 2019, SK Innovation invested US$1.67 billion in a manufacturing plant in Georgia.
- Total job creation by SK Innovation stands at 2,600.
- Jun Kim is the CEO of SK Innovation.
- South Enchem Ltd is spending US$61 million to build two new manufacturing plants in Jackson County.
- Enchem specializes in the development and manufacturing of electrolytes for rechargeable batteries and electrostatic double-layer capacitors (ELDCs).
- Enchem was the first supplier to follow SK Innovation, which broke ground in Jackson County in March 2019 on a US$1.67 billion battery manufacturing plant.
- Since its founding in 2012, Enchem has developed multiple new electrolytes and functional additives to improve and extend battery life.
- Governor Brian Kemp welcomed Enchem to Georgia, noting the investment follows their first international trip in June 2020.
- Toyota Industries Compressor Parts America (TICA) announced in October 2020 that it would expand its Pendergrass, Georgia production facility to meet growing demand for onboard power electronics.
- Toyota is investing US$5.5 million in a project that will create at least 27 new jobs in Jackson County over the next 18 months.
- Toyota set up the TICA facility in 2012 with an initial investment of US$350 million to supply automotive compressors for Toyota Motor Corporation, Chrysler, Ford, General Motors, and others.
- TICA works in concert with the adjacent TD Automotive Compressor Georgia facility, which assembles parts manufactured at the TICA plant.
- The combined facilities encompass over one million square feet of production space and a total investment of more than US$1 billion.
- Kota Otoshi is the executive officer for the electronics division of TICA’s parent company, Toyota Industries Corporation.
- In 2018, GE Appliances invested US$55 million in a new Southeast Region Appliance Distribution Center in Commerce.
- GE maintained a strong presence in Georgia with 1,960 employees at Roper Corporation, a wholly owned subsidiary in LaFayette, and 50 employees supporting Factory Service Operations based out of Atlanta.
- John Scott is the Vice President and Director of Economic Development for the Jackson County Area Chamber of Commerce.
- When complete, SK Innovation's Georgia location will give it a projected annual global capacity of 71 gigawatt hours together with facilities in Europe and Asia.
- Jackson County is centrally located in the Southeast United States’ growing automotive industry.
- The SK Group was among the first Korean investors in Georgia and has a longstanding history in the state.
- Duckyang's investment helps Jackson County and Georgia secure a place in the automotive industry's future by leveraging strong relationships with SK Battery America and other Korean companies.
- Jackson County's strategic location, strong workforce, quality of life, business-ready transportation infrastructure, and investments in workforce and logistics attract significant investment from recognized companies.
