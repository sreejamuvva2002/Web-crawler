## Facts

- Aspen Aerogels, Inc. closed on a $125 million term loan facility and a $100 million capacity asset-based revolving credit facility with MidCap Financial.
- The combined $225 million debt facilities will support supplemental Plant II debt financing and general corporate purposes.
- The collateral structure of the agreement excludes the assets of Aspen Aerogels Georgia LLC, including the planned second aerogel manufacturing facility in Statesboro, Georgia.
- The collateral structure is specifically optimized to fit into the anticipated structure for accelerating the longer-term construction of the second aerogel manufacturing facility in Statesboro, Georgia.
- Aspen Aerogels' PyroThin® products enable solutions to thermal runaway challenges within the electric vehicle market.
- Aspen Battery Materials, the company's carbon aerogel initiative, seeks to increase lithium-ion battery cell performance to help EV manufacturers extend driving range and reduce EV costs.
- Donald R. Young serves as Aspen Aerogels' President and CEO.
- Ricardo C. Rodriguez serves as Aspen Aerogels' Chief Financial Officer and Treasurer.
- The legacy convertible note has a conversion share price of approximately $29.94.
- MidCap Financial provides administrative or other services for over $51 billion of commitments as of June 30, 2024.
- Apollo Capital Management, L.P. manages MidCap Financial and had approximately $696 billion in assets under management as of June 30, 2024.
- The company's strategy involves partnering with world-class industry leaders to leverage its Aerogel Technology Platform into high-value markets.
- Forward-looking statements indicate risks regarding the inability to continue construction of Plant II or do so at costs consistent with estimates and aligned with demand from EV customers.
- EV thermal barrier customers retain the right to cancel contracts with Aspen Aerogels at any time and without penalty.
