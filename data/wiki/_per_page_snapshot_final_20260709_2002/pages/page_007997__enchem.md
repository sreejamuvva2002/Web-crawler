# Enchem

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Enchem Expands North American Shipments Amid Battery Market Recovery... Q2 Growth Trend - The Asia Business Daily](https://www.asiae.co.kr/en/article/IT/2026063008150641813)  ·  `page_007997`

## Facts

- Enchem is a global electrolyte specialist.
- On June 30, Enchem announced a clear trend of sales growth in the second quarter of this year.
- Q2 sales growth was driven by expanded electrolyte shipments to major customers in North America.
- Expectations for improved performance are rising in the global battery industry due to gradual recovery in demand for electric vehicle batteries.
- Demand is also increasing for batteries used in energy storage systems (ESS) and artificial intelligence (AI) data centers.
- Enchem is increasing its electrolyte supply volume in line with production expansion of major customers in North America.
- Shipments to North American customers in the second quarter fueled sales growth, resulting in a year-on-year increase in overall revenue.
- Enchem completed production of the electrolyte volumes scheduled for supply in June and fulfilled most shipments according to customer delivery schedules.
- The company expects its second-quarter sales to increase significantly compared to the same period last year.
- Specific figures for sales and earnings will be confirmed after final shipments, customer inspections, accounting settlements, and external auditor reviews are completed.
- Sales expansion is driven by both market price increases from higher lithium salt prices and actual increases in electrolyte supply volume.
- Production capacity utilization at Enchem’s North American facilities is rising in tandem with production expansion by North American customers.
- Growing electrolyte shipments are leading to a gradual easing of fixed cost burdens.
- Profitability is expected to improve due to fixed cost dilution from increased sales.
- The company anticipates efficiency gains in production, procurement, and logistics costs will contribute to a reduction in operating losses compared to the previous quarter.
- Strengthened inventory and working capital management is expected to help reduce operating losses.
- Tariff refunds related to its North American business are expected to contribute to reduced operating losses.
- Enchem continues to monitor cost structure and management resource operations, focusing on plant utilization rates and customer-specific profitability.
- In the second half of the year, normalization of production at major customers’ plants in North America is expected to serve as an additional growth driver.
- Expanded supply to leading Chinese battery companies is also expected to be a growth driver in the second half.
- If plant operations at certain customers—whose production was temporarily halted or adjusted in the first half—return to normal, electrolyte shipments to those customers are expected to resume gradually.
- In China, Enchem is working to transition new customers who have undergone sample supply, technology verification, quality assessment, supplier registration, and plant audits to mass production supply.
- Enchem plans to sustain sales growth and earnings improvement in the second half by recovering shipments to existing North American customers and expanding supply to new customers in China.
- If Q2 revenue growth and reduced operating loss trends continue into the second half, the company’s foundation for reaching a quarterly break-even point (BEP) will be further strengthened.
- The actual timing for achieving BEP may vary depending on customers’ production plans, order volumes, delivery and inspection schedules, raw material prices, exchange rates, and tariff refund timing.
- An Enchem representative stated that in Q2, actual supply volume increased to major customers in North America and China, resulting in substantial year-on-year sales expansion.
- The representative added that with higher capacity utilization and cost efficiency gains, they expect notable improvement in their earnings structure compared to the previous quarter.
- A spokesperson stated that in H2, normalization of major customers’ production and expanded supply to new customers will be reflected sequentially.
- The company will focus on managing utilization rates and customer-specific profitability to ensure sales growth leads to tangible profitability improvements.
