# Coweta County

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-04-30

**Source:** [Newnan | Coweta County: Town and Country - Georgia Trend Magazine](https://www.georgiatrend.com/2024/04/30/newnan-coweta-county-town-and-country/)  ·  `page_001615`

## Facts

- Coweta County is located about 40 miles southwest of Atlanta and is home to approximately 153,000 people.
- The county's economy is driven by 23 companies from 15 different countries, 10 of which are headquartered there.
- One-third of Coweta’s large manufacturing workforce is employed by these companies.
- Manufacturers are drawn to the county for convenient interstate access, proximity to Hartsfield-Jackson International Airport, a trained workforce, and affordable real estate.
- Hitachi Construction Machinery Americas expanded its current location into its regional headquarters, adding 92 jobs and $33 million in capital investment.
- The Hitachi expansion space spans more than 88,000 square feet.
- County Administrator Michael Fouts states the average annual wage for Hitachi workers is $111,000.
- Korean-based auto-parts supplier Hyundai Industrial announced in November a $24 million investment to retrofit an existing building in Coweta County.
- The Hyundai Industrial investment will create 100 jobs.
- Hyundai Industrial specializes in manufacturing car seats, arm rests and head rests for OEMs, including the Hyundai Metaplant in Bryan County.
- Norwegian battery developer FREYR Battery is a recent investor to the area.
- Coweta County Economic Development Authority President Sarah Jacobs says FREYR is helping put Georgia on the map as part of the “Battery Belt.”
- Jacobs notes Coweta’s proximity to Atlanta, labor market, and local education partnerships have positioned the community well for attracting advanced manufacturing investments like FREYR.
- FREYR will create more than 720 new jobs over the next six years.
- FREYR plans to spend $1.7 billion on the first phase of its “Giga America” factory.
- A later FREYR expansion could include solar parks with storage modules and materials processing, bringing the total investment to nearly $2.6 billion.
- Jacobs notes FREYR jobs are “lifetime career” jobs that do not require four years of education.
- FREYR jobs will pay an average salary of $60,000, which is 127% of the average Coweta County wage.
- Cargill Newnan protein processing plant is adding 120 employees to its facility.
- Winpak Films is investing $890,000 in 44 acres of property adjacent to its existing facility in Senoia.
- University of West Georgia (UWG) President Brendan Kelly says various companies rely on UWG graduates to supply needed talent.
- UWG built computing, cybersecurity and applied business analytics programs with input from 20 tech companies throughout the region.
- Students who graduate from UWG's industry-built academic programs often earn six-figure salaries.
- West Georgia Technical College (WGTC) covers seven counties and has five full-service campuses and several instructional sites.
- WGTC President Julie Post says the school offers over 100 programs at $100 a credit hour, with most careers earning far more than $50,000 a year in two years or less.
- WGTC is looking to expand its precision manufacturing and maintenance program because Coweta is largely a manufacturing area needing that employee base.
- WGTC puts 98% of its graduates to work in businesses that need them, with about 87% placed in the field they studied.
- Coweta’s Central Educational Center (CEC) is a publicly funded charter college and career academy combining business engagement with technical college system and local school district expertise.
- The CEC program was certified by the General Assembly in 2011, which provided an additional investment of more than $160 million.
- CEC CEO Mark Whitlock says students graduate from high school with technical college certificates of credit, showing high-skill, higher-wage career paths.
- Some CEC graduates in their early 20s are making close to six figures with no college debt while working in local jobs.
