# Nisshinbo Automotive Manufacturing, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2018-09-05

**Source:** [Nisshinbo Automotive Manufacturing, Inc. to create 100 jobs in Newton County - Expansion Solutions](https://www.expansionsolutionsmagazine.com/nisshinbo-automotive-manufacturing-inc-to-create-100-jobs-in-newton-county/)  ·  `page_058266`

## Facts

- Nisshinbo Automotive Manufacturing, Inc. (NAMI) will invest $72 million in an expansion of its Covington facility.
- The facility expansion will create 100 new jobs in production and administration.
- Governor Nathan Deal announced the investment and job creation.
- NAMI has maintained a presence in Newton County for more than two decades.
- Along with its business development center in Sterling Heights, Michigan, the Newton County facility produces high-quality friction materials for automotive manufacturers.
- The Newton County facility develops environmentally friendly, copper-free brake pads.
- NAMI CEO Shigeru Matsuki made the announcement together with the Governor’s Office and the Newton County Industrial Development Authority.
- Select Newton Vice President of Economic Development David Bernd noted the project represents more than $70 million in Foreign Direct Investment for 100 well-paying advanced manufacturing careers.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) Project Manager for International Investment Joseph Huntemann represented the Global Commerce Division in partnership with Select Newton.
- Japanese-affiliated companies currently operate more than 500 facilities in Georgia that employ approximately 30,000 Georgians.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that NAMI’s success in Georgia is a combination of strong international relationships and a pro-business environment.
- Governor Nathan Deal highlighted that the expansion allows NAMI to benefit from close proximity to top vehicle manufacturers while reaching customers globally via Georgia’s logistics infrastructure.
- Governor Nathan Deal emphasized that Georgia provides the business climate, skilled workforce, and thriving automotive sector necessary for manufacturers like NAMI to enjoy long-term growth.
- NAMI CEO Shigeru Matsuki expressed hope to grow with the people of Covington for the next 20 years and beyond.
