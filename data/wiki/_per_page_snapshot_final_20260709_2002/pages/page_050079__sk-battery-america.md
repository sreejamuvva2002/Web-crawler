# SK Battery America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [SK On Signals Q2 Turnaround... North American Battery Shipments Double - The Asia Business Daily](https://www.asiae.co.kr/en/article/2025062708345942562)  ·  `page_050079`

## Facts

- SK On's North American battery shipments in the second quarter are expected to more than double compared to the same period last year, significantly narrowing its operating loss.
- The amount of Advanced Manufacturing Production Credit (AMPC) received under the U.S. Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) is projected to reach a quarterly record high.
- SK Battery America (SKBA) in Georgia, USA, shipped approximately 4.3 gigawatt-hours (GWh) in the second quarter of this year.
- The second quarter shipment volume represents an increase of more than 115% compared to the estimated 2.0GWh in the same period last year.
- Under the IRA, batteries produced and supplied within the United States are eligible for a tax credit of $35 (about 47,526 KRW) per kWh for cells and $45 per kWh for modules.
- SKBA's AMPC received in the second quarter is estimated at approximately 241.3 billion KRW, marking the largest quarterly amount since the program was implemented.
- KB Securities forecast that SK On’s operating loss in the second quarter, including AMPC, would be around 35.6 billion KRW, representing an 88.1% improvement from the previous quarter’s 299.3 billion KRW.
- Hana Securities projected the second quarter operating loss at 117.8 billion KRW, expecting a significant reduction compared to the previous quarter.
- SKBA has a total production capacity of 22GWh and serves as the exclusive battery supplier for Hyundai Motor’s electric vehicles produced in the United States.
- Analysts attribute the increase in battery cell production to the start of full-scale electric vehicle production and wholesale sales at Hyundai’s U.S. plant.
- KB Securities researcher Jeon Wooje estimated that SK On’s U.S. plant reached 100% full operation between March and April.
- From May, inventory buildup for high-performance electric vehicles such as the Ioniq 9 and EV9 began, with battery demand exceeding 1.5GWh based solely on Hyundai’s April production.
- SKBA’s supply to Hyundai currently accounts for as much as 75% of its total output.
- SK Innovation recently withdrew the planned IPO of its lubricant subsidiary SK Enmove, citing market conditions and corporate value enhancement.
- Industry insiders view SK Innovation's withdrawal of the SK Enmove IPO as part of a rebalancing strategy to focus capital and resources on SK On.
- SK Innovation CEO Jang Yongho declared since the beginning of this year that portfolio rebalancing is an essential task for survival.
- Jang Yongho added that in the short term, the company will concentrate on areas and markets where it can secure competitiveness to grow into a global top-tier battery company.
- An industry official stated that demand for SK On’s U.S. plant is expected to continue growing due to expanded U.S. electric vehicle production, IRA tax credit effects, and automakers’ local battery procurement strategies.
- The same industry official explained that SKBA’s utilization rate is expected to remain high under these conditions.
