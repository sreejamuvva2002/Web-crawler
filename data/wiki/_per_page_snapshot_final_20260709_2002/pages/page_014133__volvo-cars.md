## Facts

- Volvo Cars is primarily owned by China's Geely Holding.
- Volvo Cars received U.S. government approval to continue vehicle sales despite a new regulatory landscape restricting Chinese vehicular products.
- The new regulatory measures aim to limit Chinese software and hardware influence in the automotive industry and are effective from early 2026.
- Due to its Chinese ownership, Volvo Car USA underwent a specific authorization process with the Department of Commerce, granting it the ability to maintain and grow its U.S. market share.
- U.S. lawmakers are considering further tightening these policies.
- Sales figures for 2025 showed a slight decline, but the company remains optimistic.
- Volvo maintains its U.S. market presence despite new rules from the Biden administration.
- Volvo plans to expand production in South Carolina to boost domestic manufacturing capacity.
- Volvo plans to initiate U.S. production of a new hybrid model by the decade's end, ensuring compliance and market adaptation.
- Future local production of the XC60 SUV in South Carolina is part of Volvo's broader strategy aligning with its hybrid and electric vehicle goals.
