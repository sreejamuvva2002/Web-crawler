# Archer Aviation Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-07-31

**Source:** [Archer](https://news.archer.com/archer-aviation-showcases-midnight-aircraft-in-atlanta-as-near-completion-high-volume-manufacturing-facility-covington-georgia)  ·  `page_017006`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation Inc. is constructing a high-volume manufacturing facility in Covington, GA, adjacent to the Covington Municipal Airport.
- The facility will be nearly 400,000 square feet.
- Construction is on track to be completed by the end of 2024.
- The first build phase, inclusive of grading and foundation work, was completed earlier this year.
- Phase two, involving the erection of exterior walls and roof, is nearing completion.
- Phase three (interior fit out) and phase four (equipment load in and power on) are on track to be completed in the coming months.
- The facility will support Archer’s planned production of up to 650 Midnight aircraft annually in partnership with Stellantis.
- This production volume would make it one of the largest manufacturing facilities by volume in the aviation industry.
- Archer showcased its Midnight aircraft at Atlantic Aviation’s new terminal at DeKalb–Peachtree Municipal Airport.
- Guests at the showcase included government officials from Georgia’s Office of the Governor, Georgia’s Congressional Offices, the City of Atlanta, Georgia’s State Legislature, the Federal Aviation Administration, and other local governments.
- CEO and founder Adam Goldstein stated that Archer is tracking towards completing the manufacturing facility by the end of the year.
- Archer's goal is to be the first in the eVTOL industry to achieve scaled manufacturing once production ramps up to 650 aircraft a year.
- The Midnight aircraft is an electric vertical take-off and landing (eVTOL) aircraft built around Archer’s proprietary electric powertrain.
- The Midnight is designed for back-to-back flights of 20-50 miles with minimal charge time in between.
- The Midnight travels at speeds up to 150 mph, turning hour-long ground commutes into minutes in the air.
- The Midnight is 100x quieter than a helicopter at cruising altitudes and nearly inaudible from the streets below.
- The Midnight features redundant systems targeting safety levels similar to commercial airliners.
- The Midnight is designed to carry a pilot, up to four passengers, and carry-on luggage.
- Archer is designing and developing key enabling technologies and aircraft to power the electrification of aviation for congested cities.
