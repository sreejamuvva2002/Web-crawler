# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-03-25

**Source:** [Hyundai Announces $21 Billion U.S. Investment Amid Growing Pressure on Foreign Automakers - AllAmerican.org](https://allamerican.org/news/hyundai-announcement/)  ·  `page_010882`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group announced plans to spend $21 billion in the United States between 2025 and 2028.
- The announcement signals increasing pressure on foreign automakers to localize operations as trade policy continues to shift under mounting scrutiny from lawmakers and domestic industry advocates.
- Hyundai is a South Korean automotive giant seeking to expand its U.S. footprint across manufacturing, electric vehicles, robotics, and autonomous driving technologies.
- Hyundai confirmed the plan during a press briefing, stating the investment aims to “fortify [its] presence” in American industry.
- The investment will include expansions to Hyundai's EV Metaplant in Georgia.
- The investment will include a new steel manufacturing facility in Louisiana.
- Hyundai’s push to localize appears closely timed with intensifying rhetoric in Washington around enforcing the Inflation Reduction Act and scrutiny of Chinese-affiliated supply chains.
- The Biden administration has recently weighed heightened tariffs on Chinese EVs, citing national security concerns tied to data surveillance risks from connected car technologies.
- Hyundai has already committed $7.6 billion in previous U.S. investments.
- The new announcement brings Hyundai's total U.S. investment commitment to above $28 billion by 2028.
- Around $9 billion of the new commitment is earmarked for expanded vehicle production and local infrastructure.
- The remainder of the new commitment will go toward future-facing technologies such as AI, robotics, and autonomous mobility.
- In Bryan County, Georgia, Hyundai is ramping up operations at its Metaplant America.
- The Metaplant America began partial production in 2024.
- The Metaplant America is slated to become fully operational this year (2025).
- The Metaplant America is expected to produce 300,000 vehicles annually.
- The Metaplant America has already begun hiring thousands of American workers.
- Hyundai is partnering with its steel-making subsidiary to build a $5.8 billion steel factory in Louisiana.
- The Louisiana steel factory will supply Hyundai's own U.S. production facilities and help reduce reliance on foreign inputs.
- The Louisiana steel factory is projected to create more than 1,400 permanent jobs.
- In 2023, Hyundai and Kia sold more than 1.5 million vehicles in the United States.
- Less than half of the vehicles sold by Hyundai and Kia in the U.S. market were assembled domestically.
- Lawmakers are exploring reforms to the de minimis loophole and increasing scrutiny of non-American supply chains, which may put automakers with limited U.S. content at a competitive disadvantage.
