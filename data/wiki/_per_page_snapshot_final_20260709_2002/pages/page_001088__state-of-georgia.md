# State of Georgia

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-06-05

**Source:** [Analyzing a Decade of Economic Success in Georgia - Expansion Solutions](https://www.expansionsolutionsmagazine.com/analyzing-a-decade-of-economic-success-in-georgia/)  ·  `page_001088`

## Facts

- Georgia recorded $24 billion in investments during fiscal year 2023.
- The state received more than $27 billion in e-mobility and battery investments between 2018 and 2023.
- Record-breaking exports surpassed $49.7 billion for the third consecutive year.
- Georgia boasts a workforce of 5.3 million people.
- The state has invested significantly in training programs, apprenticeships, and education initiatives to cultivate talent for evolving industry needs.
- Georgia Quick Start is recognized as the nation’s top workforce training program and has trained over one million employees.
- Lisa Nash, head of HR for Qcells in Dalton, stated that without Georgia Quick Start they would never be able to train the required volume of people, which would have significantly delayed their ramp up.
- Brandon Chadwick serves as a Quick Start training coordinator preparing future Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) employees for Day One and beyond.
- Quick Start training converts workers from industries like McDonald’s or retail into automotive manufacturing professionals.
- Leading universities and colleges, including Georgia Tech and the University of Georgia, ensure a steady stream of qualified professionals for Georgia’s growth.
- The GEORGIA MATCH Direct College Admissions Initiative sends personalized letters to rising high school seniors listing eligible public technical institutions, colleges, and universities to create a pipeline of qualified Georgians.
- Hartsfield-Jackson International Airport is the busiest and most efficient airport worldwide.
- The Port of Savannah is America’s largest container terminal.
- Georgia’s logistics network includes strategic investments in air, sea, rail, and road transportation.
- The Georgia Ports Authority operates an inland port system to move heavy products from inland sites to ports.
- Georgia has more than 5,000 miles of rail.
- Griff Lynch, President and CEO of the Georgia Ports Authority, stated that GPA, the Georgia Department of Economic Development, and the Georgia Department of Transportation are working to foster responsible growth that delivers good jobs while preserving quality of life.
- Anovion Technologies is building a synthetic graphite manufacturing facility in Southwest Georgia.
- Chip Dunn, Executive Chairman of Anovion Technologies, cited access to waterway and land transport as a key reason for locating the company in rural Georgia.
- The Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) Certification Program offers pre-qualified industrial sites with completed due diligence to streamline facility establishment.
- About half of Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) suppliers were attracted through the GRAD Certification initiative.
- Commissioner Pat Wilson of the Georgia Department of Economic Development stated that companies in e-mobility prioritize speed-to-market solutions and that investing in site development and GRAD certification helps communities prepare for new opportunities.
- The Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority secured a 331-acre site at the intersection of two major highways.
- The site became GRAD Select certified after completing one mass grading project, obtaining water access permits, and conducting traffic studies with the Georgia Department of Transportation.
- HMGMA supplier Hwashin became the first tenant at the Dublin-Laurens County site.
- Hwashin broke ground at the site in less than six months after announcing the selected location, joined by Governor Brian Kemp and local leadership.
- The Georgia Center of Innovation drives innovation through collaborative public-private partnerships facilitated by state government investments.
- CJ Foodville USA is establishing its first U.S. plant in Georgia.
- Tony Hunsoo Ahn, President of CJ Foodville USA, cited Georgia’s well-established track record for attracting international investors and job-creators, particularly helping South Korean companies locate, as the reason for choosing the state.
- Mr. Ahn noted that Georgia has cultivated a business-friendly environment, reduced regulatory constraints, and shown a constant willingness to engage with business leaders on a personal level.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development’s existing industries and regional project team serve as go-to resources for businesses operating in their region.
- Stuart Countess, President & CEO of Kia Georgia, stated that partnerships with the Governor’s Office, the Department of Economic Development, Georgia Quick Start, the City of West Point, and Troup County have been tremendous.
- The success of Kia Georgia ultimately helped Georgia secure the largest project in state history: the Hyundai Metaplant.
- All factors that helped Kia Georgia thrive—workforce, connectivity, and partnerships—led Hyundai Motor Group to select Georgia for this generational project.
- Hyundai expects HMGMA to come online a little over two years after announcing the project.
- Hyundai is leveraging Georgia Quick Start at temporary training facilities and Quick Start’s Advanced Manufacturing Training Center for HMGMA.
- Construction is underway on the Hyundai Mobility Training Center of Georgia, which will be the first training center in Georgia dedicated exclusively to the EV market.
