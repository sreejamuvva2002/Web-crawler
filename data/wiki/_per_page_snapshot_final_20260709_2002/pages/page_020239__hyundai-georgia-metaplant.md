# Hyundai Georgia Metaplant

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hyundai Responds To Massive ICE Raid On Georgia EV Metaplant - Autojunction](https://autojunction.org/hyundai-responds-to-massive-ice-raid-on-georgia-ev-metaplant/)  ·  `page_020239`

## Facts

- Hyundai's Georgia Metaplant, where it makes the Ioniq 5 and Ioniq 9 electric vehicles, was raided by law enforcement officers including the U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement.
- About 475 people were detained during the raid, most of them South Korean nationals.
- Hyundai stated that the detainments will not impact car production at the factory.
- The Savannah, Georgia Metaplant is a 16 million square-foot factory.
- Hyundai Motor America officials confirmed that none of the detained workers were directly employed by Hyundai Motor Company.
- The detained workers appear to have been working for LG Energy Solution, which co-owns the plant with Hyundai and operates its nearby battery facility.
- Hyundai officials stated they are closely monitoring the situation while working to understand the specific circumstances.
- Hyundai emphasized its commitment to full compliance with all laws and regulations in every market where it operates, including employment verification requirements and immigration laws.
- Hyundai expects the same commitment to legal compliance from all partners, suppliers, contractors, and subcontractors.
- A Hyundai spokesperson confirmed that vehicle assembly at the plant will not be affected by today's arrests.
- Hyundai is reviewing its processes to ensure all parties working on its projects maintain high standards of legal compliance, including thorough vetting of employment practices by contractors and subcontractors.
- It was not immediately clear why the EV and battery manufacturing site was targeted by the Trump administration.
- The raid comes as the Trump administration continues to dramatically step up immigration-related detentions, arrests, and deportations across the country.
- According to a New York Times report, an attorney for at least two detained suspects said they were in the United States on a visa waiver program that allows stays of 90 days or less for business.
- A New York Times report indicated that Korean companies often bring their own technicians on-site to help set up new operations because they have the training to do such work.
- One immigration lawyer said U.S. visa laws are not properly set up to sponsor trained workers to run those factories.
- ICE officials stated the raid was part of an active, ongoing criminal investigation.
- ICE officials said the arrested individuals were found working illegally, in violation of the terms of their visas and/or statuses.
- People on short-term or recreational visas are not authorized to work in the U.S., according to ICE.
- A green card holder from Mexico was arrested after being determined removable from the U.S. based on multiple criminal convictions.
- Hyundai's Metaplant is perhaps the automaker's most important effort to bolster manufacturing in the U.S., its largest and most important market.
- The plant is also the largest economic development project in Georgia's history.
- The project has a planned capacity of 14,000 manufacturing jobs.
- Hyundai announced this year that the plant can make hybrid cars, amid potential concerns about electric demand following the end of the federal EV tax credits later in the month.
- Hyundai officials stated that the safety and well-being of everyone working at their sites is a top priority.
- Hyundai takes its responsibility as a corporate citizen seriously, noting incidents like this remind them of the importance of robust oversight throughout the entire supply chain and contractor network.
