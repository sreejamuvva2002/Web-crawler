# Stryten Energy

## Facts

- Stryten Energy is based in Alpharetta, Georgia.
- The company generates over $757.4 million in revenue.
- Stryten operates as a national energy partner focused on more than just hardware.
- Its mission is to deliver sustainable, efficient, and resilient energy storage solutions that support America’s power transformation.
- The company offers a portfolio of energy storage technologies critical for transportation systems, military equipment, telecommunications, and factories.
- Stryten specializes in lead batteries, which are long-lasting and recyclable, ideal for backup systems and transportation.
- Stryten specializes in lithium batteries, known for excellent energy density, perfect for fast-paced and mobile applications.
- Stryten specializes in flow batteries, an emerging technology for long-duration energy storage and grid energy storage capacities.
- The company deploys energy storage systems to stabilize network loads and balance renewable inputs for the manufacturing industry.
- Stryten provides high-capacity battery rooms for auto factories and efficient mobile power for robotic systems.
- The company engineers advanced battery designs for EV start-stop batteries, charging station energy support, and mobile backup systems for service vehicles.
- Stryten supports vehicle electrification and charging infrastructure to enable widespread EV adoption while enhancing grid reliability.
- The company is investing in utility-scale and distributed grid storage to collect excess energy during peak generation times and release it when demand surges or supply drops off.
- Stryten provides energy banks for traffic signals, communications towers, and subway systems to ensure essential services stay active during storms, surges, or brownouts.
- The company operates closed-loop recycling programs that recover metals and materials from old lead batteries to reuse them in new ones.
- Stryten participates heavily in the lead-acid battery recycling ecosystem, which has a recycling rate over 99% according to the EPA.
- Stryten has headquarters in Alpharetta, Georgia, and several plants across the U.S.
- The company employs thousands of Americans and supports skilled labor and high-tech manufacturing jobs.
- Stryten is creating job pathways in welding, engineering, robotics, and recycling.
- Projected revenue for Stryten Energy is $950 million (Est.) for 2025 and $1.2 billion (Est.) for 2030.
- The global battery market is expected to grow to $310 billion by 2030.
- Stryten supplies strategic battery systems to defense agencies, including keeping military bases running and arming mobile defense vehicles.
- Reliable domestic battery suppliers like Stryten reduce dependency on foreign technologies for national security and strategic resilience.
