# Kia Georgia, Inc.

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-02-01

**Source:** [GEORGIA LAWMAKERS COMMEND KIA FOR MANUFACTURING AND ITS IMPACT TO THE STATE](https://www.kiamedia.com/us/en/media/pressreleases/19829/georgia-lawmakers-commend-kia-for-manufacturing-and-its-impact-to-the-state)  ·  `page_010179`

## Facts

- Georgia lawmakers led by Governor Brian P. Kemp recognized January 31 as “Kia Day,” commending Kia’s vehicle manufacturing and its impact to the state.
- Kia made one of the biggest single foreign investments in Georgia’s history in 2006 by establishing a state-of-the-art vehicle assembly facility in West Point.
- To date, Kia has invested more than $1.9 billion in the state.
- Kia Georgia is responsible for more than 14,000 plant and supplier jobs in the region.
- Kia Georgia was Kia Corporation’s first manufacturing plant in North America and assembles nearly 40-percent of all Kia vehicles sold in the U.S.
- Assembly of Kia electric vehicles (EVs) is expected to begin in the first half of 2025 at Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA).
- HMGMA officially broke ground near Savannah, Georgia, and will create more than 8,100 jobs over the next few years.
- Kia Georgia has an annual capacity of 340,000 units and began mass vehicle assembly on Nov. 16, 2009.
- The West Point facility is located on 2,200 acres in West Point, GA.
- Kia Georgia’s total production is anticipated to surpass 4 million vehicles in April of 2023.
- Kia Georgia is home to the K5 midsize sedan and various models of the Telluride, Sorento and Sportage SUVs (excluding HEV and PHEV models).
- More than 90-percent of the vehicles assembled at Kia Georgia are destined for dealerships across America.
- The West Point facility has provided over $20 million in support of Georgia communities since 2009, including over $6 million to support educational programs such as the THINC College & Career Academy in LaGrange and SAE’s “A World in Motion.”
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated that Kia chose Georgia because of its great workforce, quality of life, and pro-business approach, and that both sides saw a shared vision for transformational growth and a commitment to the hardworking people of the state.
- Sean Yoon, president and CEO of Kia North America and Kia America, said Kia was the first to open an auto manufacturing plant in Georgia because of the talented workforce, sense of community, respected pro-growth business climate, and commitment to innovation prevalent there.
- Stuart Countess, president and CEO of Kia Georgia, noted that Georgia’s array of resources to promote economic growth and workforce development enabled Kia to focus on building a state-of-the-art facility and the necessary skill sets for hundreds of team members new to automotive manufacturing.
- Kia America is headquartered in Irvine, California, and serves as the "Official Automotive Partner" of the NBA.
- Kia America sells gasoline, hybrid, plug-in hybrid and electrified vehicles through a network of over 750 dealers in the U.S.
- Kia Corporation is based in Seoul, Korea.
