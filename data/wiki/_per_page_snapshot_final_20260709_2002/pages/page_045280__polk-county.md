# Polk County

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2012-07-31

**Source:** [Georgia Trend](https://www.georgiatrend.com/2012/07/31/polk-county-in-a-good-place/)  ·  `page_045280`

## Facts

- Polk County has lost only one employer of significant size since the recession began.
- Rockmart-based Meggitt Polymers & Composites is the county's largest employer, manufacturing aircraft fuel tanks and doubling in size from about 600 employees to more than 1,200 over five years.
- Meggitt Polymers & Composites announced plans to add another 56 new jobs in Rockmart.
- Meggitt Polymers & Composites was recognized as the Large Manufacturer of the Year in 2011 by the Technical College System of Georgia and the Georgia Department of Economic Development.
- Polk County is located 50 miles from Atlanta, 85 miles from Birmingham, Ala., and 83 miles from Chattanooga, Tenn.
- Interstates 20 and 75 are accessible from Polk County via four-lane highways U.S. 27 and U.S. 278.
- Manufacturing is Polk County's largest industry, with The HON Company (furniture), Jefferson Southern Corporation (auto parts), Can Am Yarns (yarn), and Advance Storage Products (storage systems) among the top employers.
- Jefferson Southern Corporation occupies 250,000 square feet in a dual-rail industrial park.
- Nordic Logistics & Warehousing occupies 300,000 square feet in the same dual-rail park served by Norfolk Southern and CSX.
- The $55-million Paulding Northwest Atlanta Airport opened in late 2010 just across the county line.
- The Paulding Northwest Atlanta Airport is expected to have a major impact on economic development in Polk County over the next five to 10 years.
- The Paulding Northwest Atlanta Airport has available land around it to attract large aerospace companies.
- Polk Medical Center, a new $25-million, 25-bed critical access hospital, will break ground between Rockmart and Cedartown by the end of this year (2012).
- The Polk Medical Center project will bring about 30 new healthcare jobs to the county, mostly in surgical nursing.
- A medical office building and medical mall are planned for the Polk Medical Center campus, projected to be complete by January 2016.
- Two publicly owned industrial parks in Rockmart and Cedartown encompass 298 acres of prime industrial land.
- The Cedartown North Business Park has 115 acres of light industrial land just off the U.S. 27 bypass, one mile from U.S. 278.
- The Development Authority of Polk County purchased an additional 220 acres adjacent to the Cedartown North Business Park's eastern boundary.
- A 105,000-square-foot spec industrial building will break ground on a 15-acre pad in the park by the end of this year (2012).
- The Rockmart 101 Business Park contains an additional 183 acres of industrial land.
- Jefferson Southern Corporation occupies 250,000 square feet in the Rockmart 101 Business Park.
- Nordic Logistics & Warehousing occupies 300,000 square feet in the Rockmart 101 Business Park.
- Cedartown is a Department of Community Affairs-designated Main Street City following a redevelopment plan that includes $1.6 million invested in new downtown streetscaping.
- The city of Cedartown offers an incentive package including county and city enterprise zones that double property tax relief for restaurants and opportunity zone tax credits worth up to $3,500 per new employee.
- A group of 16 community leaders from throughout Polk County has been working with the Development Authority of Polk County and the Fanning Institute at the University of Georgia since last September on a countywide strategic economic development plan.
- The strategic economic development plan aims to address workforce challenges, attract new industries, and create a world-class community.
- Georgia Northwestern Technical College in Rockmart offers the Quick Start program, providing customized training to students at no cost to businesses.
- Coosa Valley Technical Institute partnered with The HON Company and the Polk County School District to create Project Success, launching in the fall to give 30 at-risk high school students a job to prepare them for a career after graduation.
- Southern Company and Georgia Power partnered with Georgia Northwestern Technical College to create the Instrumentation and Controls Academy in Polk County, which trains 32 people every year for high-tech jobs in the power generation industry.
- A student with Instrumentation and Controls Academy training can earn up to $60,000 a year two years after high school graduation.
- Polk County passed a Special Purpose Local Option Sales Tax (SPLOST) in 2008 and renewed it through 2018.
- $165,000 was recently approved by the Polk County Commission for infrastructure improvements to help existing industries expand.
- Polk County's population was 41,475 according to the 2010 Census, with Cedartown at 9,750 and Rockmart at 4,199.
- Polk County's per capita income in 2009 was $25,185 compared to Georgia's state average of $35,215.
- The unemployment rate in Polk County was 9.0 percent in April 2012, compared to Georgia's 8.9 percent.
- Top employers in Polk County include Meggitt Polymers & Composites, The HON Company, Tip Top Poultry, AT&T Communications, and Angelica Textile Services.
