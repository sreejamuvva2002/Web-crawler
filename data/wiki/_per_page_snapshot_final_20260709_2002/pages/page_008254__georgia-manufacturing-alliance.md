# Georgia Manufacturing Alliance

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2025-11-30

**Source:** [Catching up with... Jason Moss - Georgia Trend Magazine](https://www.georgiatrend.com/2025/11/30/catching-up-with-jason-moss/)  ·  `page_008254`

## Facts

- Jason Moss is the founder and CEO of the Georgia Manufacturing Alliance (GMA).
- GMA is a membership-based organization that supports Georgia’s manufacturing community.
- Moss founded GMA in 2008 to help grow his market while selling software to manufacturers, after realizing there was no existing place for manufacturers to network.
- Before the COVID pandemic, GMA hosted monthly meetings and approximately 120 live events annually, culminating in the annual Georgia Manufacturing Summit.
- Post-COVID, GMA has adopted a more selective approach and expects to host 50 or 55 events in the coming year.
- Unlike most non-profit associations focused on lobbying or education, GMA operates as a for-profit industry association.
- The organization's sole agenda is supporting initiatives that benefit manufacturing and Georgia, focusing on factory tours, networking events, and educational sessions rather than lobbying.
- AI is identified as a major workforce challenge and trend within Georgia manufacturing.
- GMA has partnered with Georgia AIM to integrate AI into advanced manufacturing, utilizing a massive grant to host events that provide AI access and education, particularly in rural Georgia.
- Automation and robotics are noted as the primary direction for the industry's future.
- GMA acts as a resource for new manufacturing entering the state by helping manufacturers identify vetted backups for their supply chains or service providers.
- The alliance connects manufacturers with suppliers ranging from construction firms to component providers, facilitating mutual business opportunities.
- Solidifying professional networks and supply chains, ensuring redundancies, and maintaining open communication are highlighted as the biggest current trends in manufacturing.
- Confidence in suppliers is described as critical for manufacturers, alongside the necessity of support and redundancy.
