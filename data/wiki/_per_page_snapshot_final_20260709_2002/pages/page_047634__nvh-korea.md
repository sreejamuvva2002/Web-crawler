# NVH Korea

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-06-23

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp: EV Parts Supplier NVH Korea to Create Over 160 Jobs, Invest $72M in Henry County - Peach Tree Times](https://peachtreetimes.com/stories/644720586-gov-kemp-ev-parts-supplier-nvh-korea-to-create-over-160-jobs-invest-72m-in-henry-county)  ·  `page_047634`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that automotive parts manufacturer NVH Korea will establish a new facility in Locust Grove during an economic development trip to the Paris Air Show.
- The project will create over 160 new jobs and bring $72 million in investment to Henry County.
- This announcement marks the second economic development project Governor Kemp announced that week while promoting Georgia internationally.
- NVH Korea is an automotive parts manufacturer based in the City of Ulsan, established in 1984.
- The company specializes in automotive noise, vibration, and heat-control parts currently produced in Korea, Europe, and India.
- NVH Korea already operates a manufacturing facility in Georgia that produces floor mats, cargo mats, and cargo trays.
- The new facility will supply battery parts for electric vehicles in the United States to manufacturers including Hyundai Mobis, Kia Georgia, and Hyundai Motor Group.
- Ja Kyum Koo, Chairman and CEO of NVH Korea, stated that Georgia was chosen as the company's first EV battery component manufacturing site.
- The new facility will be located at Gardner Logistics Park on Colvin Drive in Locust Grove.
- The facility will produce EV battery system components that protect, connect, and sense the performance of battery cells.
- Operations at the new facility are expected to begin in the second quarter of 2024.
- Mayor Robert Price of Locust Grove stated that NVH's investment will increase the local manufacturing jobs base and integrate into Georgia's electric vehicle ecosystem.
- Carlotta Harrell, Chair of the Henry County Board of Commissioners, noted the project will add quality manufacturing jobs and diversify the county's economy.
- Project Manager Jacob Lee represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s Global Commerce team on the project in partnership with the Henry County Development Authority, Georgia EMC, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson highlighted that NVH Korea represents an innovator establishing its first U.S. or e-mobility manufacturing presence, enriching the local industry mix and providing well-paying jobs.
- From July 1 through December 31, 2022, Georgia's automotive industry was the second-largest source of new jobs and investment in the state.
- Since 2020, Georgia's pursuit of job creation along the EV supply chain has resulted in more than $22.7 billion in investments and over 28,400 jobs created.
