# Cellcycle

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [We Visits St Ambrose Preparatory School to Talk Battery Recycling](https://www.cellcycle.co.uk/cellcycle-visits-st-ambrose-preparatory-school-to-talk-battery-recycling/)  ·  `page_060465`

## Facts

- Max Nagle, Head of Marketing and Communications at the SER Group, visited St Ambrose Preparatory School in Hale Barns, Altrincham on Friday 20th June to speak to pupils about battery recycling.
- The visit aimed to explain why batteries cannot simply be thrown away and how materials inside them can be recovered and given a new life.
- Cellcycle's visit formed part of its ongoing commitment to building broader public understanding of battery recycling and its place in the UK’s transition to net zero.
- The UK disposes of 600 million batteries every year.
- Most disposed batteries end up in landfill, where chemicals can leach into soil and water.
- Batteries range from household AA cells to lithium-ion packs inside phones, laptops, and electric vehicles.
- Materials inside batteries include zinc, manganese, lithium, cobalt, nickel, copper, and graphite, which are finite resources that are recoverable.
- Recovered materials have a second use in new batteries, paint, or pharmaceutical applications.
- Building a domestic recycling capability to handle energy transition volumes is a skills and awareness challenge as well as a technical one.
- The session covered the full lifecycle of a battery from raw materials to end-of-life disposal.
- Risks of incorrect disposal include leaking toxic chemicals into soil/water and degraded batteries posing a fire hazard in refuse vehicles.
- Pupils were introduced to the Cellcycle process: collection, safe transport, shredding, material recovery, and re-entry into new products.
- Responsible disposal is straightforward via collection points at supermarkets, libraries, and some schools.
- A quiz/challenge highlighted that if 129 pupils each recycled one battery in a week, 129 batteries would be diverted from landfill before the following Friday.
- Mrs. L. Ellis stated Cell Cycle delivered an informative assembly that was fun, interactive, and helped pupils learn about battery contents, recycling processes, and importance.
- Max Nagle noted the engagement showed young people are seriously thinking about sustainability connected to daily technology, which is what the industry needs from the next generation.
- Cellcycle holds Environment Agency-approved treatment facilities with a combined permitted capacity of 80,000 tonnes per year.
- Cellcycle's LithiumCycle™ process was developed through a Knowledge Transfer Partnership (KTP) with Coventry University.
- The LithiumCycle™ process applies a bio-based refining route to recover cathode active material at a purity and yield viable for direct re-entry into battery manufacturing.
- A patent for Cellcycle's novel direct recycling process has been filed.
- Cellcycle is the battery recycling division of the SER Group.
