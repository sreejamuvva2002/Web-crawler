# Bridgestone Americas, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2019-03-19

**Source:** [Bridgestone Launches 'Fleets We Depend On' Campaign in Push for Retreading](https://www.tirereview.com/bridgestone-bandag-fleets-we-depend-on-retreading/)  ·  `page_018188`

## Facts

- Bridgestone Americas, Inc. launched a new industry-wide campaign focused on educating small-to-medium sized fleets on the advantages of retreads.
- The campaign was launched at the 2019 Technology & Maintenance Council (TMC) Annual Meeting & Transportation Technology Exhibition in Atlanta.
- The initiative is called the “Fleets We Depend On” campaign and spotlights fleets such as fire departments, first responders, and school districts that rely on retreads.
- Eric Higgs, president of truck, bus and retread tires for U.S. & Canada at Bridgestone Americas Tire Operations (BATO), stated Bridgestone is committed to making mobility more efficient and aims to inform small- and mid-sized fleets on the benefits of choosing Bandag.
- LaTres Jarrett, director of marketing for Bandag in the U.S. and Canada, noted that while retreading is common in larger fleets, small to medium-size fleets retread less but could benefit from lower total cost of ownership.
- Bridgestone research showed that retreads cost 30-50% less than buying a new tire.
- Dealers who own a retread plant can gain a second stream of revenue by engaging fleet customers in a program.
- The commercial tire industry has been affected by ultra-low-cost imported tires that are generally used only once and are less likely to be retreaded.
- Bridgestone data estimates the surge in ultra-low-cost tires contributed to the closure of more than 450 independently-owned small and medium retread facilities between 2000 and 2016.
- The decline of retreading may have environmental implications, including reducing CO2 emissions, air pollution, and natural resources.
- The campaign includes a 27-stop roadshow at Bridgestone locations and dealer shops across North America, with cities including Atlanta; Nashville; Phoenix; Indianapolis; Akron, Ohio; Orlando, Florida; and Elizabeth, New Jersey.
- The roadshow will run through July 2019.
- The campaign includes a Fleets We Depend On Award presented by Bandag to recognize reliable individuals and fleets.
- Nominees can be submitted through the Bandag website or at roadshow stops in North America.
- The award winner will be announced in September and will receive a cash prize of $5,000 and a matching donation to a 501(c)(3) organization of their choice.
