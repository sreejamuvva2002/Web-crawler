# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America — Industrial in Ellabell - Exchange](https://exchange.construction/projects/industrial/hyundai-metaplant-america-georgia-ev/)  ·  `page_000996`

## Facts

- Hyundai opened its Georgia “Metaplant” in 2025.
- The company almost immediately started expanding the plant after opening it.
- The $7.6 billion vehicle factory is located near Ellabell, Georgia.
- The factory was originally designed to produce 300,000 electric cars per year.
- The annual production target has been increased to 500,000 vehicles, which will be a mix of EVs and hybrids.
- Expansion work to reach the new target is underway alongside live production.
- Including the plant and two battery joint ventures with LG Energy Solution and SK On, Hyundai’s total investment in Georgia reaches $12.6 billion.
- This represents the largest investment in the state’s history.
- The plant is planned to create 8,500 jobs by 2031.
- The IONIQ 9 three-row SUV is currently being produced at the plant.
- Kia and some Genesis models are scheduled to join production in 2026.
- A $4.3 billion battery cell joint venture plant with LG Energy Solution is scheduled to open in the first half of 2026.
- The opening of the LG battery plant was delayed due to a federal immigration raid at the site.
- The facility is designed as an integrated campus with stamping, body, paint, assembly, and battery operations located on or beside one site.
- Federal immigration enforcement actions have stalled the battery plant’s schedule, placing the project at the center of current labor and immigration debates.
- The developer and owner of the project is Hyundai Motor Group.
- The current status of the plant is under construction.
- The project is funded privately.
