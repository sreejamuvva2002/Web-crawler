# Anovion Technologies

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Press - Development Authority of Bainbridge and Decatur County](https://www.bainbridgedecaturga.com/press/)  ·  `page_000844`

## Facts

- Anovion Technologies is an electric vehicle (EV) battery materials manufacturer.
- Anovion announced it will invest $800 million in Decatur County, Georgia.
- The investment will create 400 jobs.
- A new 1.5 million-square-foot manufacturing facility is being built for the project.
- The facility is located at the county-owned Downrange Industrial Park.
- The facility will produce high-quality synthetic graphite anode, a material critical in the EV battery supply chain.
- Rick McCaskill serves as head of the Development Authority of Bainbridge and Decatur County.
- The Development Authority invested $50 million into the park’s infrastructure for the project.
- Anovion selected the Southwest Georgia location due to its proximity to existing and planned low- and carbon-free energy sources, short supply chains, access to existing rail infrastructure, a skilled workforce, and a business-friendly environment.
- Decatur County is a member of Locate South Georgia, a regional economic development entity comprised of 22 counties in Southwest Georgia.
- Locate South Georgia conducts an annual bus tour covering more than 10,000 square miles to showcase sites and buildings to project managers.
- In November 2022, the Locate South Georgia tour included Decatur County.
- A Georgia Department of Economic Development project manager identified the site during the November 2022 tour as a potential fit for Anovion.
- The request for proposal (RFP) was quickly sent and accepted following the site visit.
- Prior to the Anovion announcement, Locate South Georgia reported $324.5 million in new investment and 575 jobs created in 2022 across its member counties.
- Grant Buckley serves as executive director of the Cordele-Crisp County Industrial Development Council and chair of Locate South Georgia.
- Grant Buckley reported hearing inquiries from Tier 2 or Tier 3 EV suppliers potentially relocating to the region due to the Anovion project.
- Rick McCaskill stated that regional marketing efforts by Locate South Georgia have been a significant benefit for attracting large economic development projects.
- U.S. Sen. Jon Ossoff and Gov. Brian Kemp attended Anovion Technologies’ groundbreaking ceremony.
