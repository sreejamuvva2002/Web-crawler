# Qcells

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2023-01-11

**Source:** [Qcells Invests $2.5 Billion in U.S. Solar Supply Chain - Qcells North America](https://us.qcells.com/blog/qcells-invests-2-5-billion-in-building-complete-solar-supply-chain-in-u-s/)  ·  `page_001591`

## Facts

- Qcells aims to have 8.4-gigawatt of solar panel production capacity by 2024 and hire 2,500 people in the clean-energy sector.
- The investment is the largest in U.S. solar history to build the nation’s only complete and sustainable solar supply chain.
- Qcells announced it will invest more than $2.5 billion to build a complete solar supply chain in the United States.
- The commitment will create nearly 2,500 direct jobs in Georgia.
- The announcement marks the largest investment in the history of American solar and sets Qcells up as the only company in the U.S. to establish a fully-integrated, silicon-based solar supply chain from raw material to finished panel.
- In the first quarter of 2023, Qcells will break ground on a new facility in Bartow County, Georgia.
- The new facility will manufacture 3.3 gigawatts (GW) of solar ingots, wafers, cells, and finished panels.
- The company will assemble an additional 2 GW of solar panels at its Dalton, Georgia facility.
- The Dalton facility is the largest solar panel manufacturing plant in the Western Hemisphere.
- The investment is expected to bring Qcells’ total solar panel production capacity in Georgia to 8.4-gigawatt by 2024.
- The announcement was made by Qcells’ parent company Hanwha Solutions, headquartered in Seoul.
- It represents the parent company’s largest clean energy investment to date in the U.S. and beyond.
- The move aims to accelerate the United States’ renewable energy transition and domestic manufacturing expansion.
- Justin Lee serves as CEO of Qcells.
- Qcells opened its first factory in Georgia in 2019 and hired 750 people to manufacture 1.7 GW of solar.
- Last year, Qcells announced a plan to add 1.4 GW to its Dalton manufacturing output and hire 535 more people.
- Today’s announcement means Qcells will hire an additional 510 people in Dalton.
- This brings Qcells’ total employment in Georgia to 4,000 and accumulated investment in the state to $2.8 billion.
- Governor Brian Kemp commented on the growth of Qcells in Georgia for a second time in less than a year.
- Qcells investments across the full solar value chain follow the passage of the Solar Energy Manufacturing for America Act within the Inflation Reduction Act.
- U.S. Secretary of Energy Jennifer M. Granholm stated that building a strong solar supply chain will tackle the climate crisis while creating good-paying manufacturing jobs.
- Senator Jon Ossoff wrote and passed major legislation to bring more solar manufacturing jobs to Georgia and called this the largest clean energy manufacturing project in American history.
- Senator Reverend Warnock helped lead the fight to make Georgia a top state for clean energy jobs and collaborated with Qcells and Senate leadership on the Solar Energy Manufacturing for America Act.
- According to the Q4 2022 U.S. PV Leaderboard report from Wood Mackenzie as of the third quarter 2022, Qcells topped the U.S. residential solar module market for five years running from 2018 to 2022.
- Qcells has been the leading module provider for the U.S. commercial sector since 2019.
- The investment will pave the way for Qcells to continue its leadership as a major supplier and developer of utility solar projects.
- Investments will occur at two sites with a greenfield expansion encompassing ingots, wafers, cells, and more modules.
- The new site will have 3.3 GW annual manufacturing capacity per value chain and be the largest fully integrated solar manufacturing site in the U.S.
- Qcells aims to begin commercial production at the new site in late 2024.
- The new site will be located in Cartersville, Georgia, about 55 miles outside of Atlanta.
- The build-out follows an expansion of annual solar panel production to 5.1 GW in Dalton, Georgia by 2023.
- The Dalton expansion comes from building a third module manufacturing facility producing an additional 2-GW of solar panels per year.
- Qcells is currently operating a 1.7 GW module factory at Dalton and began construction earlier in 2022 to increase the facility to 3.1 GW.
- Hanwha recently became the leading shareholder in polysilicon producer REC Silicon.
- Efforts are underway to manufacture low-carbon polysilicon at REC Silicon’s factory in Moses Lake, Washington, which is powered by emission-free hydroelectricity and will restart production in the second half of 2023.
- Qcells is headquartered in Seoul, South Korea (Global Executive HQ) and Thalheim, Germany (Technology & Innovation HQ), with manufacturing facilities in the U.S., Malaysia, China, and South Korea.
