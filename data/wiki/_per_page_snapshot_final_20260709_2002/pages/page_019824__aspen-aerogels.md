# Aspen Aerogels

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2022-02-17

**Source:** [Aspen Aerogels plant taking shape one year after big announcement - Statesboro Herald](https://www.statesboroherald.com/local/aspen-aerogels-plant-taking-shape-one-year-after-big-announcement/)  ·  `page_019824`

## Facts

- Aspen Aerogels’ new Bulloch County factory was announced for construction one year prior to the article's publication date of February 17, 2022.
- The plant is located at what is now called Bruce Yawn Commerce Park.
- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp announced on February 17, 2022, that Aspen Aerogels would build a $325 million plant.
- At the time of the announcement, the site was referred to as Southern Gateway Commerce Park, located off U.S. Highway 301 at the I-16 interchange between Statesboro and Claxton.
- Company officials projected the plant would be in operation by late 2023.
- The project was projected to create 250 advanced manufacturing jobs.
- Development Authority of Bulloch County CEO Benjy Thompson reported that drone photographs taken about two weeks prior show construction progress on the site.
- The construction site covers 90 acres.
- Construction progress includes one building with walls and a roof, as well as the steel framework of a larger building with some roofing or sub-roof materials installed on portions of its frame.
- Several possibly temporary buildings and construction trailers are visible on the site.
- Aspen Aerogels officials were preparing for the release of their fourth-quarter and full-year 2022 financial report as of mid-February 2022, with an update to the project timeline expected in the near future.
- The Bulloch County plant will produce aerogel insulation material, described as somewhat like wispy, very light but strong fiberglass.
- Aspen Aerogels particularly promotes its trademarked PyroThin product line for providing thermal runaway barriers for fire prevention between the cells of electric vehicle batteries.
- Aspen Aerogels’ Bulloch County plant announcement preceded Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp’s May announcement of Hyundai Motor Group’s plans for an electric vehicle “Meta Plant” at the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Mega Site in northern Bryan County.
- Aspen Aerogels’ plans were not connected to Hyundai’s EV plant project.
- Aspen Aerogels was the first of four industries to commit to building plants in Bulloch County within a 12-month span.
- As of February 2022, Aspen Aerogels’ project was the only one among the four committed projects actually under construction.
- The four committed plants will eventually create more than 1,400 jobs if all announced commitments are realized.
- The combined announced capital investments for the four Bulloch County plants total $897 million.
- Ecoplastic America Corporation, a direct supplier of injection-molded plastic automotive body parts to Hyundai Motor Group, intends to build a $205 million plant on a site owned by the Development Authority of Bulloch County south of Statesboro.
- Ecoplastic America’s project is promised to create 456 jobs, with investment and job creation phased in over eight years.
- Ajin USA, a supplier of metal auto body parts to Hyundai, has committed to invest $317 million and eventually employ 630 people at its Joon Georgia plant.
- The Ajin USA site is 83 acres, located beside the Aspen Aerogels site in Bruce Yawn Commerce Park.
- revalyu Resources, headquartered in Germany, intends to build a PET plastic-bottle recycling plant on a 43-acre site in Gateway Regional Industrial Park across U.S. Highway 301 South and closer to Statesboro.
- The revalyu Resources project is expected to create 71 jobs and require a $50 million investment in Phase 1.
