## Facts

- Workers at Blue Bird Corp.’s two factories and warehouse in Fort Valley will begin voting Thursday on whether they want to be represented by a labor union.
- More than 1,400 employees at the company’s facilities in Fort Valley will vote through Friday on whether to unionize under the United Steelworkers.
- The United Steelworkers union represents more than 850,000 workers nationwide in a variety of industries.
- Blue Bird workers are seeking higher pay, a more regular schedule, and better vacation and sick time benefits from the publicly held Macon-based company.
- Blue Bird has long been the largest employer in nearby Peach County.
- The unionization vote represents an opportunity for organized labor to make gains on the stony soil of the Deep South.
