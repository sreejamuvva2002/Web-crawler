# Solvay Specialty Polymers

**Type:** Company  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[battery-materials|Battery Materials]]  ·  **Location:** Georgia, United States

## Overview

Belgium-based Solvay Specialty Polymers announced a joint venture with Orbia to build an $850 million plant in Augusta, Georgia that will produce EV battery-grade binders and separator coatings, creating the largest production capacity of this lithium-ion chemical ingredient in North America.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Manufacturing EV battery binders and separator coatings in Georgia to support U.S. electric-vehicle supply chain.

## Key facts

- Solvay will have the largest production capacity of the lithium-ion chemical ingredient in North America.
- The joint venture between Solvay and Orbia will supply raw materials for EV battery parts.
- Georgia is becoming a burgeoning hub for EV industry investments, with Rivian's $5 billion plant and Hyundai's $5.5 billion mega campus.
- "We are proud to receive the federal government’s support for manufacturing technologies that will provide the U.S. with critical raw materials for building an independent, sustainable EV value chain," said Mike Finelli.
- The project aims to fill a significant supply gap for EV battery parts and builds on favorable conditions promoting regional production and material security.
