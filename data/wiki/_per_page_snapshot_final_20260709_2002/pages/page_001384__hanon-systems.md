# Hanon Systems

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** thermal_management  ·  **Published:** 2023-05-24

**Source:** [Hanon brings Bulloch’s developing industries to 5; jobs total tops 1,500 - Coastal Courier](https://coastalcourier.com/news/hanon-brings-bullochs-developing-industries-to-5-jobs-total-tops-1500/)  ·  `page_001384`

## Facts

- Hanon Systems announced a commitment to build a factory in Gateway Regional Industrial Park.
- The announcement brings the number of tier-one Hyundai Motor Group suppliers locating in Bulloch County to three.
- It also brings the number of new manufacturers of all types building in the county to five.
- The five projects' job creation commitments add up to a projected 1,566 employees.
- Their promised capital investments within Bulloch total $937 million.
- All five projects have been announced within 16 months.
- All but one of the projects were announced in the 12 months since Hyundai's electric vehicle Meta Plant America was revealed for the four-county Joint Development Authority site in northern Bryan County.
- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp’s office issued a news release around 1:30 p.m. Tuesday announcing Hanon's commitment.
- Hanon is a maker of “automotive thermal and energy management” equipment, which includes air-conditioning components.
- Hanon will invest more than $40 million in the factory.
- Hanon will create 160 jobs.
- The governor's news release was datelined “Israel” because Kemp is on a weeklong visit there.
- Hanon Systems, like Hyundai Motor Group, is based in South Korea.
- The Development Authority of Bulloch County (DABC) met at its headquarters upstairs at the Queensborough National Bank, Statesboro branch to discuss the project.
- DABC members went into closed session to discuss a memorandum of understanding with Hanon and unanimously approved it upon reopening.
- Billy Allen, chair of the DABC, stated that Statesboro-Bulloch County is honored by Hanon's decision and excited to welcome another automotive supplier working closely with the Hyundai Metaplant and electric vehicle industry.
- Steve Rushing, attorney for the Development Authority, summarized the contents of the memorandum before the board voted.
- Hanon’s new facility will be built next to the Cardinal Glass plant at the Gateway site on 34 acres provided by the DABC in a lease-to-own arrangement.
- The DABC is selling the land at a discount rather than giving it away, similar to other Hyundai suppliers locating in the area.
- After internally valuing the land at $50,000 an acre, the DABC offered it to Hanon for $25,000 an acre.
- The company will pay over time $850,000 for a site the DABC considered to be worth $1.7 million.
- By maintaining legal ownership of the land and facility for 10 years, the tax-exempt DABC will provide the site free of county government property tax to Hanon for that period.
- The company is expected to make payments to the county equal to the Bulloch County Board of Education share of property tax and the fire protection district tax.
- The DABC agrees to provide temporary facilities, such as office space, free to the company up to a rental value of $10,000.
- The DABC will provide $20,000 worth of additional workforce training through Ogeechee Technical College, separate from state Quick Start program training.
- The Development Authority already funded an initial environmental study of the site at a cost of $17,625.
- The DABC is to apply for an EDGE Grant from the OneGeorgia Authority for up to $525,000 to assist Hanon.
- The DABC will make its bond-issuance power available to help finance the company’s investment in Bulloch.
- In exchange for incentives, the company is expected to meet the announced capital investment and job creation goals.
- Average wages are expected to be at least equal to Bulloch County’s average wage of $21.65 an hour, as stated by the DABC.
- Steve Rushing noted that if there is a shortfall in meeting goals, there is a clawback calculation where the company would pay back a portion of the incentives.
- The authority and company expect to close on the deal by July 31.
- The governor’s announcement states that the plant is expected to begin operations in May 2024.
- Gateway Regional Industrial Park is located on the west side of U.S. Highway 301 south of Statesboro and is the authority’s oldest current industrial park.
- A surge of new industries has filled the newer Bruce Yawn Commerce Park on the other side of U.S. 301 at the I-16 interchange, prompting the DABC to buy additional sites.
- DABC CEO Benjy Thompson acknowledged that total promised jobs have increased in a county with a low unemployment rate.
- Thompson stated they are constantly looking for real estate opportunities matching community growth and working with partners to ensure the workforce is trained and skilled to fill incoming jobs.
- Timelines for construction of the factories are short.
- The Hyundai Motor Group Meta Plant itself is projected to start production in January 2025 and eventually employ 8,100 people.
- Thompson noted that job creation numbers are for plants operating at full capacity, which will take time to reach.
- Thompson emphasized that companies won't open next year and immediately be at full capacity, requiring time to settle and ramp up over time.
- Gov. Brian Kemp stated that Georgia’s automotive and e-mobility industries continue to grow and that the state furthers its position as the EV capital of America with new jobs coming to Georgians.
- Aspen Aerogels was announced on Feb. 17, 2022, with construction underway on a 90-acre site in Bruce Yawn Commerce Park.
- Aspen Aerogels has committed to a $325 million investment and at least 250 manufacturing jobs, and will make aerogel insulation used in and around electric vehicle batteries, though it is not linked to the Hyundai factory.
- Ecoplastic America Corporation, a supplier of injection-molded plastic automotive body parts to Hyundai Motor Group, intends to build a $205 million plant that will create 456 jobs phased in over eight years on a DABC-owned site south of Statesboro where site preparation is underway.
- Ajin USA, a supplier of metal auto body parts to Hyundai, has committed to invest $317 million and eventually employ 630 people at its Joon Georgia plant on an 83-acre site beside the Aspen Aerogels location in Bruce Yawn Commerce Park.
- revalyu Resources, headquartered in Germany and not connected to Hyundai, plans to build a PET plastic-bottle recycling plant on a 43-acre site in Gateway Regional Industrial Park that is expected to create 71 jobs and require a $50 million investment in Phase 1.
- Hanon, Ecoplastic, and Ajin are all tier-one suppliers because they deal directly with Hyundai Motor Group, whereas a tier-two supplier would supply materials to a tier-one supplier.
