# Ascend Elements

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2023-03-31

**Source:** [US: Ascend Elements opens battery recycling plant in Georgia - electrive.com](https://www.electrive.com/2023/03/31/us-ascend-elements-opens-battery-recycling-plant-in-georgia/)  ·  `page_001472`

## Facts

- Ascend Elements, a US American company, has opened its commercial-scale lithium-ion battery recycling facility in Georgia.
- The company invested $50 million in the site.
- Ascend Elements claims the site is North America’s largest electric vehicle battery recycling facility to date.
- The facility can recover up to 98 percent of critical metals used in lithium-ion batteries.
- The facility uses Ascend Elements' proprietary 'Hydro-to-Cathode' process for recycling.
- Through its Hydro-to-Cathode process, Ascend Elements engineers the composition and microstructure of lithium-ion cathode particles during direct precursor synthesis to deliver custom-made battery materials to precise customer specifications.
- The Hydro-to-Cathode process is described as more efficient and environmentally friendly than other recycling methods.
- The process recovers 98 percent of critical battery metals from spent lithium-ion batteries and manufacturing scrap.
- The process produces new cathode material at half the cost of similar cathodes made with newly mined and refined metals.
- The process generates 90 percent lower greenhouse gas emissions compared to similar cathodes made with newly mined and refined metals.
- The facility began partial operations in August 2022.
- The facility has now reached its full operational capacity.
- The site can process up to 30,000 tonnes of batteries and manufacturing scrap annually.
- Ascend Elements plans to produce sustainable battery materials for electric vehicles at a future facility in Kentucky.
- The Kentucky facility is expected to be completed towards the end of the year.
- The Kentucky facility will manufacture enough sustainable cathode material and precursors for up to 250,000 electric vehicle batteries per year.
