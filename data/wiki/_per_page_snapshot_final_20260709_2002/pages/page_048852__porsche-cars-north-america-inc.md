# Porsche Cars North America, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2022-12-13

**Source:** [ANB8 Loose Retaining Ring May Cause Dislodged Air Strut - 2021 Porsche Taycan](https://porsche.oemdtc.com/616/anb8-loose-retaining-ring-may-cause-dislodged-air-strut-2021-porsche-taycan/)  ·  `page_048852`

## Facts

- Porsche Cars North America, Inc. is located at One Porsche Drive Atlanta GA 30354.
- The page details recall campaign ANB8 for certain 2021 Taycan, Taycan 4S, Taycan Turbo S, Taycan 4 Cross Turismo, and Taycan 4S Cross Turismo vehicles.
- A retaining ring on top of the suspension strut may come loose due to an incorrectly machined groove, causing air loss from the pneumatic spring.
- The defect can cause a drop in ride height, loss of ride comfort, and adversely affect vehicle handling capabilities, increasing the risk of a crash.
- There are 28 potentially affected vehicles in the U.S., manufactured between July 27, 2021 and February 3, 2022.
- Porsche became aware of the issue in November 2022 through investigation of field cases with dislodged suspension struts.
- The sub-supplier for the front pneumatic spring strut is Vibracoustic GmbH & Co KG, located at Hörstener Str. 45-47 Hamburg, Germany.
- Porsche decided to recall the subject vehicles on December 7, 2022, to prevent negative effects on handling capabilities.
- Dealers will inspect the suspension strut and replace it if necessary, free of charge to customers.
- Owner notification letters are expected to be mailed February 10, 2023.
- There were 4 warranty claims reported from April 2022 to October 2022.
- Vehicles built after September 17, 2021, were produced with parts outside the identified incorrectly machined batches and are within specification.
- The supplier has implemented several process improvements to avoid similar manufacturing deviations in the future.
