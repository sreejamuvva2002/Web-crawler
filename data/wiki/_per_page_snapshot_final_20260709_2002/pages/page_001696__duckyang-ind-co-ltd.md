# Duckyang Ind. Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2022-01-19

**Source:** [Duckyang Ind. – Korean-Machinery.com](https://korean-machinery.com/tag/duckyang-ind/)  ·  `page_001696`

## Facts

- Duckyang Ind. Co., Ltd. was established in 1977 with its headquarters and main plant in Ulsan, Korea, and additional plants in Gyeongju and Yesan.
- The company reports annual sales of approximately $11 billion.
- Duckyang initially supplied crash pads for Hyundai Motor Company's Pony car, which later evolved into the IONIQ 5 electric vehicle.
- The company currently manufactures cockpit modules, door trims, and crash pads as automotive interior components.
- Cockpit modules are the company's best-selling product, integrating approximately 130 items such as audio, air conditioning, and airbag systems around the driver's seat in a process called 'Modulization.'
- Duckyang supplies these interior components to most Hyundai Motor models produced at its Ulsan Plant in Korea.
- Responding to the industry shift from internal combustion engine vehicles to electric vehicles, Duckyang developed Battery Module Assembly (BMA) and Energy Storage System (ESS) components.
- The company was awarded as an R&D national organization hosted by the Korea Institute for Advancement of Technology (KIAT) for its BMA development efforts.
- Duckyang operates a separate R&D department with facilities in Suwon and Gyeongju, focusing on reducing the weight of cockpit modules while maintaining quality.
- The company received the 2021 National Quality Competitiveness Excellent Corporation award from the Ministry of Trade, Industry and Energy for eight consecutive years.
- Duckyang is establishing Duckyang America in Georgia, USA, in partnership with SK Innovation to pursue Battery Module Assembly (BMA) and Energy Storage System (ESS) business.
