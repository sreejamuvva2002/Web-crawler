# Form Energy

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-10-22

**Source:** [Iron-air battery developer Form Energy raises $405M, announces collaboration with GE Vernova | Utility Dive](https://www.utilitydive.com/news/iron-air-battery-developer-long-duration-storage-form-energy-collaboration-ge-vernova/730633/)  ·  `page_060503`

## Facts

- Form Energy has begun construction on a factory expansion that will add nearly 300,000 square feet to the Weirton, West Virginia, facility where it manufactures long-duration energy storage systems.
- The expansion announcement came less than a week after Form announced the closing of a $405 million Series F fundraising round.
- Form signed a memorandum of understanding with GE Vernova involving strategic collaboration on manufacturing operations, supply chain operations, financing, and sourcing.
- The collaboration will also involve leveraging GE Vernova’s Advanced Research Center capabilities.
- The moves bring the company closer to its goal of a 1 million square-foot manufacturing footprint with 500 MW / 50 GWh in annual production capacity by 2028.
- Form Energy aims to commercialize an iron-air battery system that can store electricity for 100 hours at system costs competitive with legacy power plants.
- The technology uses atmospheric oxygen to oxidize (discharge) and deoxidize (charge) iron powder housed in a water-based electrolyte.
- CEO and co-founder Mateo Jaramillo told Utility Dive in 2021 that the company’s technology is "in a different category" than other long-duration storage technologies, which typically discharge over periods of eight to 24 hours.
- Form began trial production earlier this year at its 550,000 square-foot manufacturing facility in Weirton.
- The company plans to begin commercial production by the end of 2024.
- Form Factory 1 sits on 55 acres formerly occupied by a steel mill.
- Form expects to complete the expansion project by the end of 2025.
- The expanded facility will be approximately 850,000 square feet, within reach of Form’s longer-term goal of 1 million square feet by 2028.
- The company has announced 14 GWh of long-duration storage projects to date, representing approximately 18 months of production at Form Factory 1.
- The $405 million fundraise included investments from GE Vernova, Breakthrough Energy Ventures, and Energy Impact Partners.
- CEO Mateo Jaramillo stated the fundraise will enable the company to boost manufacturing capacity and grow operations to deliver even more iron-air batteries to customers nationwide.
- Form broke ground in August on its first commercial pilot, a 1.5 MW/150 MWh project developed in partnership with Minnesota utility Great River Energy.
- The Great River Energy pilot facility is expected to begin operation by the end of 2025.
- Form signed a memorandum of understanding with Puget Sound Energy to explore a potential 10-MW/1-GWh deployment in its service territory as soon as the end of 2026.
- Form is pursuing a 5-MW/500-MWh project funded in part by a $30 million grant from the California Energy Commission at a Pacific Gas & Electric site in northern California.
- Form is pursuing a 10 MW/1-GWh demonstration project in New York funded in part by a $12 million grant from the New York State Energy Research and Development Authority.
- Form is developing a self-funded deployment of 85 MW/8.5 GWh in rural Maine, funded in part by a $147 million grant from the U.S. Department of Energy.
- Form has formed a partnership with Xcel Energy’s Minnesota subsidiary for a 10 MW/1-GWh deployment paired with up to 710 MW of solar.
