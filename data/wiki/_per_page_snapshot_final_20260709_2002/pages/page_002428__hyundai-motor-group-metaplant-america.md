# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-10-25

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp, Hyundai Motor Group Execs Break Ground in Bryan County | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-releases/2022/gov-kemp-hyundai-motor-group-execs-break-ground-georgias-bryan-county-megasite)  ·  `page_002428`

## Facts

- Groundbreaking for Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America's new manufacturing plant occurred on October 25, 2022, at the Bryan County Megasite in Georgia.
- The facility represents a $5.54 billion investment announced by Hyundai Motor Group and its affiliate suppliers in May 2022.
- HMGMA will create more than 8,100 jobs over the next few years to establish a stable supply chain for EV batteries and associated components in the U.S. market.
- The new smart factory is expected to begin commercial production in the first half of 2025.
- The facility will produce a diverse range of innovative, fully electric vehicles (EV) and include a new battery manufacturing facility.
- A morning groundbreaking ceremony was held on-site, followed by an expo and program at Enmarket Arena in Savannah for hundreds of attendees.
- Hyundai Motor Group announced $1.54 million total in corporate social responsibility contributions to support local communities in and around Bryan County.
- Through Hyundai Hope and Genesis Gives, the company committed $120,000 to roughly ten organizations supporting local communities in and around Bryan County.
- Hyundai donated $120,000 to Georgia Tech for STEM education scholarships.
- Hyundai Hope On Wheels presented $500,000 in Scholar Hope and Young Investigator Grants to Children’s Healthcare of Atlanta.
- The Genesis Inspiration Foundation presented $200,000 in donations to Georgia-based nonprofits Drawchange and Paint Love.
- Kia’s "Accelerate the Good" program donated $600,000 to Family Promise of the Coastal Empire, America’s Second Harvest of Coastal Georgia, St. Jude Children’s Research Hospital, and Inspiredu.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated: "Our partnership with Hyundai Motor Group and the groundbreaking of this innovative facility exemplifies that unprecedented success."
- Hyundai Motor Group Executive Chair Euisun Chung stated: "With the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America, we will continue to evolve beyond an automaker to the world’s leading mobility solutions provider."
- Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority Chairman Carter Infinger highlighted that the project signifies a long-term commitment to the Savannah region beyond its investment and job creation.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson noted the Bryan County facility will directly create jobs for the Savannah area and support opportunities in the EV revolution statewide.
- The Bryan County Megasite was selected partly due to its close proximity to the Port of Savannah.
- Hyundai will access global markets quickly via rail and highway connections from the site to the Ports of Savannah and Brunswick.
- Non-affiliated HMGMA suppliers connected to the project will invest approximately $1 billion in other locations around Georgia.
