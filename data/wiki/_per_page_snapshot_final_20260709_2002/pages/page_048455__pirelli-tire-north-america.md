# Pirelli Tire North America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2003-04-01

**Source:** [When in Rome - Tire Review Magazine](https://www.tirereview.com/when-in-rome/)  ·  `page_048455`

## Facts

- PTNA rolled out four new tires in a two-week worldwide event attended by dozens of enthusiast, tire and financial journalists and well over 100 North American dealers.
- The event included a ride-and-drive introduction to the new products at Road Atlanta raceway.
- The event included a tour of PTNA’s new headquarters and tire plant in Rome, Ga.
- Introduced was the Scorpion STR, a T-, H- and V-rated light truck/SUV radial.
- Introduced was the Scorpion Zero Asimmetrico, a W-rated high performance light truck/SUV line.
- Introduced was the P Zero Nero M+S, an all-weather extension of the W-rated ultra-high performance line.
- Introduced was the P Zero Corsa, a Y-rated DOT tire designed for road and racetrack use.
- The Georgia plant features Pirelli’s Modular Integrated Robotized System (MIRS), its fully automated production process.
- The Georgia plant has three complete MIRS modules running, producing some 350 tires per module daily but not at full speed as yet.
- The plant plans to add two more MIRS modules by early 2004.
- The Rome facility has some 460,000 square feet of production space under roof.
- The facility's space is enough for 20 MIRS modules.
- Officials say there is enough land to build two more like-sized buildings at the Rome facility.
- Each MIRS module uses only 3,767 square feet of floor space.
- Each module produces and assembles all tire components, including beads, steel belts, casing plies, and cap plies.
- Each module can simultaneously produce six different sizes of the same tire.
- Gaetano Mannino is PTNA’s president of CEO.
- MIRS is being used to produce motorcycle tires in Europe.
- MIRS is being tested for production of medium truck tires.
- There are no short- or mid-term plans to produce motorcycle or medium truck tires for North America according to Mannino.
- Pirelli will open a new tire plant in Bahia, Brazil, in early 2004.
- The Brazil plant is a traditional manufacturing facility that will exclusively service the North American market.
- PTNA’s strategy is to build replacement business through its dealers and by winning key OE fitments.
- To date, PTNA has OE contracts with Mercedes-Benz, BMW, Volvo, Jaguar, Saab, Audi and Porsche.
- PTNA has earned new business on top end Lincoln and Ford models.
- The new Scorpion Zero Asimmetrico is OE on Dodge’s 500 hp Ram SRT 10.
- Pirelli has a position on an undetermined Ford Focus model.
- The company is working with Ford, GM and others on new fitments for 2004.
- PTNA has hired Value Partners to conduct customer satisfaction surveys of Pirelli dealers to determine where it needs to improve.
- PTNA launched a Web site component that allows dealers to order tires and track tires online through delivery.
- PTNA’s new incentive program will reward dealers with their choice of new vehicles based on order levels from $2 million on down.
- Dealers can choose a new Maserati Coupe, Porsche Cayenne, Ford Excursion, Dodge Ram SRT10, Volvo XC70 or XC90, Ford Focus 2X3 or BMW Mini Cooper based on order levels.
- Orders of $150,000 earn Panoz racing school packages.
- Scorpion STR is M+S rated and offers sizing covering nearly 85% of light duty vehicles in North America.
- PTNA has 29 sizes of the T-, H- and V-rated tire, covering 15- to 18-inch wheels.
- Three extra load sizes are also available for the Scorpion STR.
- The Scorpion Zero Asimmetrico was created for the growing LT performance tuner segment.
- The Scorpion Zero Asimmetrico line features 10 sizes, all extra load, covering 21- to 24-inch wheels.
- The P Zero Nero M+S was developed for the sport tuner market and provides excellent wet and dry handling and grip.
- The P Zero Nero line is available in 14 sizes covering 16- to 19-inch wheels.
- The P Zero Corsa has 13-sizes, is Y-rated, created for "extreme driving," and is available for 17 and 18-inch wheel diameters.
