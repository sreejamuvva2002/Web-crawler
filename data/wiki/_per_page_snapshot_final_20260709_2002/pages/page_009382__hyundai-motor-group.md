# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-05-20

**Source:** [Georgia Officials: Hyundai’s $5.54 Billion EV Plant a Testament to Enduring Focus on South Korea  - Global Atlanta](https://www.globalatlanta.com/georgia-officials-hyundais-5-54-billion-ev-plant-a-testament-to-enduring-focus-on-south-korea/)  ·  `page_009382`

## Facts

- Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson visited South Korea in March before the announcement of Hyundai Motor Group's $5.54 billion investment in the state.
- Hyundai and its sister company Kia have operated plants along Interstate 85 straddling the Georgia-Alabama border for more than a decade.
- Georgia has maintained an investment and trade office in South Korea since the 1980s.
- The relationship between Georgia and Hyundai Motor Group spans more than 15 years.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp took his first economic development mission to South Korea in 2019.
- The state declined to renew its investment recruiter contract in China prior to the 2019 trip.
- Commissioner Pat Wilson stated it was time to dedicate more resources to the Korean market after declining the China contract renewal.
- Georgia previously secured an SK Innovation EV battery plant valued at $1.7 billion, which later expanded to a planned $5.2 billion investment.
- Hyundai Motor Group will build its first dedicated EV and battery plant outside Korea on a 2,284-acre mega-site in Bryan County, approximately 30 miles from Savannah.
- In addition to the $5.5 billion plant investment, an additional $1 billion is expected from suppliers.
- The project is expected to create a total of 8,100 jobs for Georgians.
- Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that shifting focus from China to South Korea was a strategic decision based on advanced activity in battery tech, electric vehicles, and renewable energy.
- A U.S. policy consensus emerged favoring collaboration with allies over adversaries in sensitive economic sectors as Georgia deepened its focus on South Korea.
- The Biden administration signaled a strong commitment to electric vehicles to curb climate change upon entering office in 2021.
- Korean firms in semiconductors, medical items, and EVs announced plans to localize U.S. production during President Moon Jae-in's summit with President Biden.
- Former South Korean President Moon Jae-in visited Georgia’s SK Battery plant after his summit with President Biden.
- Hyundai previously announced a $7.4 billion U.S. investment for EV manufacturing without specifying the location before this announcement.
- President Biden traveled to South Korea in May to shore up alliances through commercial diplomacy.
- President Biden met with newly elected South Korean President Yoon Suk-yeol on May 20.
- Governor Brian Kemp and President Biden share support for EV initiatives despite differing political backgrounds and motivations.
- Governor Kemp marked his second largest-ever state investment in an EV project this fiscal year.
- Rivian Automotive previously announced a $5 billion investment and 7,500 jobs in Covington, Georgia, before facing local opposition.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated at the announcement that the automotive industry will undergo more change in the next 10 years than in the prior 100.
- The Georgia state government purchased the site for Hyundai's plant one year prior to the announcement.
- The Savannah-Interstate 16 Joint Development Authority, comprising Bryan, Bulloch, Chatham, and Effingham counties, provided input on preparing the site.
- Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis captured over 10% U.S. market share last year, surpassing Honda.
- Kia is targeting 14 battery-electric models by 2027 as part of a transition away from internal combustion engines.
- Hyundai and Genesis are set to offer a combined 17 electric models by 2030.
- The group plans to produce 3.23 million EVs in the United States by the end of the decade.
- Governor Kemp cited visiting major companies in South Korea as the reason for choosing it as his first economic development trip.
- Governor Kemp met with Hyundai Motor Group Executive Chair Euisun Chung during his 2019 mission to South Korea.
- Executive Chair Euisun Chung stated the plant's smart factory will incorporate artificial intelligence, connected manufacturing, and renewable energy sources to accelerate carbon neutrality goals.
- The investment will contribute to a stable supply chain for U.S. EV manufacturing and create a wide range of EVs for American customers.
- Hyundai Motor Group considers the U.S. an important part of its global strategy and aims to achieve electrified mobility and sustainability goals with Georgia.
- Hyundai Motor has not specified which exact models will be produced at the new Georgia plant.
- Construction on the Georgia plant is set to begin in January.
- Production at the new facility will start in the first half of 2025.
- The plant will have an annual capacity of 300,000 vehicles.
- A separate battery plant will be built later through a strategic partnership, though details were not provided.
- SK Group, which manufactures batteries in Commerce, Georgia, has supplied batteries for Hyundai and Kia electric models globally.
- Hyundai committed $300 million in April to upgrade its Montgomery, Alabama plant to produce hybrid Santa Fe SUVs and an electrified Genesis G70.
- The first electrified Genesis G70 will roll off the production line in December.
- The Genesis G60 crossover will be introduced to the U.S. market this year.
- Genesis plans to produce only electric models after 2025.
- Hyundai Motor CEO Jaehoon "Jay" Chang stated the U.S. was chosen for its embrace of change and innovation.
- Hyundai Group has invested over $13.5 billion and created over 100,000 jobs in the U.S. since 1985.
- Hyundai's U.S. business grew by 20% last year despite global chip shortages limiting vehicle supplies.
- Kia is one of the Georgia Ports Authority's largest customers, using the Garden City Terminal in Savannah for containerized parts.
- Both Kia and Hyundai ship parts in and out of the automotive port in Brunswick, Georgia.
- Atlanta supports Hyundai's operations through its large Korean diaspora and strong nonstop air links to Seoul.
- Commissioner Pat Wilson noted that Hyundai is very comfortable with Georgia's offerings.
- CEO Jaehoon "Jay" Chang expressed pride in calling Bryan County home and emphasized Hyundai's global enterprise approach.
- Hyundai Motor America President and COO José Muñoz welcomed dealer advisory board members from Hyundai and Genesis at the event.
- Commissioner Pat Wilson praised the Kia EV6 all-electric crossover for its advanced ride comfort and consumer-friendly interior technology.
- Commissioner Pat Wilson used a Hyundai Ioniq 5 electric crossover during a recent economic development mission to London.
- Georgia previously lost out on attracting foreign automaker Volvo, which ultimately built in South Carolina after considering a Georgia megasite.
- Commissioner Pat Wilson praised the four-county joint development authority for preparing the Bryan County site just a year after its formation.
- Savannah Economic Development Authority President Trip Tollison expressed strong support for the Hyundai project at the event's conclusion.
