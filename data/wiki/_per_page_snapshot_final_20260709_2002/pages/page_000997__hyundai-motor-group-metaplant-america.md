# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-05-31

**Source:** [I Visited Hyundai's New $7.6 Billion EV Factory in the US — See Inside - Business Insider](https://www.businessinsider.com/hyundai-metaplant-georgia-us-ev-factory-tour-photos-see-inside-2025-5)  ·  `page_000997`

## Facts

- The $7.6 billion Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) is one of the newest and most technologically advanced car factories in the world.
- The plant is located near Savannah, Georgia, and opened its doors in March.
- It will serve as a key production facility for Hyundai's EVs and PHEVs, as well as vehicles for its Genesis luxury brand and sister company Kia.
- Genesis North America COO Tedros Mengiste cited the investment as an example of Hyundai's track record for 'visionary and strategic, and long-term thinking.'
- The Hyundai Metaplant is situated on a 3,000-acre campus in the south Georgia town of Ellabell.
- It is located just 20 miles from the Port of Savannah, one of the busiest ports in the US.
- The location gives Hyundai much-needed US manufacturing capacity to avoid import tariffs and provides flexibility to export vehicles abroad.
- It gives Hyundai the production footprint to compete against rivals like Tesla, GM, and Rivian, which is also building a new factory in Georgia.
- It is Hyundai Group's second car factory in Georgia, alongside a $3.2 billion, 2,200-acre facility in West Point, Georgia, that builds Kia EV and ICE SUVs.
- The 2026 Hyundai Ioniq 9 EV SUV is one of the vehicles assembled at the Metaplant.
- The Hyundai Ioniq 5 EV is the only other model currently assembled at the plant.
- Hyundai broke ground on the facility in the fall of 2022 and took just two years to complete construction on the main production buildings.
- The Metaplant site consists of 11 buildings totalling 7.5 million square feet of space.
- The facility aims for vertical integration by manufacturing as many key components, ranging from battery packs to seats, on-site.
- Hyundai XCIENT hydrogen fuel cell semi trucks are used to transport parts and supplies, with 21 emission-free XCIENT trucks deployed around the site.
- Production begins in a stamping shop where an on-site Hyundai Steel facility supplies sheet metal cut and stamped into frame parts.
- Almost 300 automated guided vehicles (AGVs) are employed to shuttle spare parts and partially assembled cars.
- Originally expected to produce up to 300,000 electrified vehicles annually, Hyundai announced at the March grand opening that capacity will expand to 500,000 units in the coming years as part of a new $21 billion US manufacturing investment.
- The plant is expected to start production of its first Kia model next year.
- Stamped metal pieces are welded into vehicle bodies by robots and inspected by human employees known as Meta Pros.
- The plant employs more than 1,300 Meta Pros, nearly 90% of whom were hired locally.
- An employee cafeteria with remote ordering capability is located in the main assembly building.
- Vehicles are also inspected by a pair of Boston Dynamics robot dogs called Spot, following Hyundai's 2021 acquisition of an 80% stake in the company for $1.1 billion.
- Outside visitors were denied access to the paint shop due to concerns that they may compromise paint application quality.
- Painted vehicle bodies travel via a bridge to the assembly building, where they are combined with battery packs and skateboard chassis.
- Hyundai Mobis produces the skateboard chassis in a building adjacent to the general assembly facility.
- An on-site battery factory operated in a joint venture with LG is expected to come online next year, while the plant currently sources batteries from other Hyundai facilities, including a North Georgia joint venture with SK.
- Vehicle interiors are assembled by hand.
- Assembled vehicles are loaded onto AGVs that navigate via floor-embedded QR codes to shuttle them through quality control tests.
- Completed EVs undergo testing at an on-site test track before moving to a vehicle preparation center (VPC); regional dealership vehicles use trucks, while shipments over 500 miles use the plant's on-site train terminal.
