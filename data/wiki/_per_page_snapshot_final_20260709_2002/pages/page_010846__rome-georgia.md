# Rome, Georgia

## Facts

- Pirelli inaugurated its North American manufacturing headquarters in Rome, Georgia, in 2002.
- European luxury automobile manufacturers including BMW, Mercedes-Benz, Audi, and Porsche aggressively established and expanded assembly plants across the American Southeast during the late 1990s and early 2000s.
- Pirelli positioned its Rome facility within a geographic triangle defined by Atlanta, Georgia; Birmingham, Alabama; and Chattanooga, Tennessee to enable efficient logistics to regional automotive assembly centers.
- The Rome plant was designed as a highly robotized production center focusing on premium, large-diameter specialty tires for the North American luxury SUV market.
- Pirelli aims to secure Original Equipment (OE) tire supplier contracts for these prestigious European marques through its localized manufacturing presence.
- Pirelli's chemical engineers develop synthetic rubber compounds specifically designed to reduce rolling resistance for new electric SUV models.
- The Rome facility employs a diverse workforce of hundreds, including expatriate engineering specialists from Italy, Brazil, Germany, the United Kingdom, and Venezuela.
- Pirelli's Rome plant is the first tire manufacturing facility globally to achieve Forest Stewardship Council (FSC) certification for sustainable natural rubber sourcing.
- Suzuki Manufacturing of America Corporation (SMAC) officially opened its Rome, Georgia plant in 2001 and began production of Eiger and Vinson ATV models in early 2002.
- SMAC transitioned into a 100% owned subsidiary of Suzuki Motor USA, LLC (SMO) in March 2024 to integrate Rome engineering teams with the national corporate structure.
- The SMAC facility spans 35 acres and represents a $30 million investment, employing hundreds of associates who assemble over one hundred KingQuad 750AXi and 500AXi models daily.
- Suzuki selected Rome for its proximity to the largest consumer ATV market in North America and access to a highly skilled regional labor pool and metallurgical suppliers.
- Marglen Industries operates a polymer recycling facility in Rome that produces ultra-clean recycled PET (rPET) resin pellets for food-grade packaging and high-tensile polyester fiber for flooring and apparel.
- Marglen's Rome facility installed a 95.2-kilowatt solar energy photovoltaic system on its rooftop in 2011 to offset polymer extrusion energy demands.
- Summit Hill Foods was acquired by New York-based private equity firm EagleTree Capital with co-investments from Misland Capital Limited in November 2023.
- Atrium Health Floyd, originally opened as Floyd County Hospital on July 4, 1942, combined with the Atrium Health network in 2019 and currently manages over 1,100 active clinical trials.
- Rome's historical industrial base earned it the title of metallurgical "stove center of the South" before shifting to textiles, synthetic fibers, and heavy paper/containerboard manufacturing.
- The International Paper Rome Mill remains a cornerstone of the local economy alongside historic facilities like the Massachusetts Cotton Mill and American Chatillon Corporation (later Celanese).
- Georgia's state R&D tax credit under O.C.G.A. § 48-7-40.12 offers a 10% credit for research conducted within the state, featuring payroll withholding tax offsets for startups and expanding manufacturers.
- Federal R&D tax credits under IRC § 41 subsidize Qualified Research Expenses (QREs) including W-2 wages for engineers, CAD designers, testing technicians, and raw materials consumed during prototype development.
- Rome's strategic location at the confluence of the Etowah and Oostanaula rivers historically supported riverine transportation networks that facilitated regional industrial supply chains.
