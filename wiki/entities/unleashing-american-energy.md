# Unleashing American Energy

**Type:** Government Program  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[government-support|Government Support]]  ·  **Location:** Georgia, United States

## Overview

President Donald Trump's executive order titled “Unleashing American Energy” aims to roll back federal support for electric vehicles by halting funding for new EV charging stations, considering elimination of the EV tax credit, and directing a 90-day review of Inflation Reduction Act spending.

## EV supply-chain relevance

The order seeks to halt federal funding for new EV charging stations and may eliminate the federal EV tax credit, directly affecting Georgia EV manufacturing projects such as Rivian’s planned plant.

## Timeline

- **2024** — President Donald Trump issued the executive order “Unleashing American Energy” targeting EV incentives.
- **November 2023** — Ford executive warned that a glut of EVs could create price pressure in 2025.
- **2024** — U.S. EV sales reached 1.3 million, up 7.3% from the prior year.
- **2026** — Construction expected to begin on Rivian’s EV manufacturing facility in Stanton Springs North, Georgia.

## Key facts

- The order seeks to ensure a “level” playing field for gasoline-powered motors and halt federal funding for new EV charging stations.
- It references possible elimination of the federal tax credit for EV purchases and renounces a waiver allowing California stricter car requirements.
- Officials implementing the Inflation Reduction Act are directed to undertake a 90-day review to ensure spending does not unfairly favor EVs.
- Nearly half of the $5 billion set aside for new EV chargers has been allocated to states under the infrastructure law, according to an NRDC memo.
- Rivian received a $6.6 billion federal loan to build an EV manufacturing facility in Stanton Springs North, Georgia; construction is slated for 2026.
- StarPlus Energy secured a $7.5 billion loan for lithium-ion battery cell manufacturing plants in Kokomo, Indiana, under a Stellantis-Samsung joint venture.
- Kathy Harris of NRDC called the policy a “sop for ‘fat-cat oil executives.’”
- The Alliance for Automotive Innovation’s president John Bozzella said the country should have a single national standard to reduce carbon in transportation.
