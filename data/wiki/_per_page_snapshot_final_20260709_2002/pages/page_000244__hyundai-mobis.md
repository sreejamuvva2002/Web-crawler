# Hyundai Mobis

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** power_electronics  ·  **Published:** 2022-11-23

**Source:** [EV supplier to make Richmond Hill home and employ 1,500 - Bryan County News](https://www.bryancountynews.com/news/ev-supplier-make-richmond-hill-home-and-employ-1500/)  ·  `page_000244`

## Facts

- Governor Brian Kemp announced that Hyundai Mobis plans to invest $926 million in an Electric Vehicle (EV) Power Electric system plant in Richmond Hill.
- The new facility will eventually employ at least 1,500 people.
- Construction on the facility is expected to begin as early as January 2023.
- Production at the new facility is expected to begin in 2024.
- The plant is located at the Belfast Commerce Park in Richmond Hill.
- Governor Kemp noted that the groundbreaking of Hyundai’s new electric vehicle and battery manufacturing facility in northern Bryan County would unleash transformational job creation and investment in the region.
- This announcement marks the second supplier to locate in the area in just two weeks.
- Richmond Hill Mayor Russ Carpenter called the project by far the single largest investment into the economy of his city.
- Mayor Carpenter stated that Richmond Hill residents know how special their small town is and that the world is catching on, expressing excitement to work with corporate partners from South Korea.
- The EV Power Electric system plant will expand Hyundai Mobis’ current footprint in Georgia.
- Hyundai Mobis has operated a manufacturing facility with almost 1,200 employees in West Point since 2009.
- The West Point facility is the largest Tier 1 supplier to Kia Georgia.
- The West Point plant supplies automotive manufacturers with complete cockpit modules, chassis modules, and bumper assemblies.
- H.S. Oh, Vice President of Hyundai Mobis Electric Powertrain Business Unit, stated that the investment reflects an acceleration in the development of the EV supply chain in Georgia’s auto industry.
- Oh noted that Hyundai Mobis will be a major production player in the EV market, which will trigger more growth within the sector.
- Oh added that Mobis is looking forward to providing high-quality work opportunities to the growing local workforce.
- Once it reaches full production, the 1,200,000-square-foot facility will be able to supply over 900,000 EV Power Electric systems and 450,000 Integrated Charging Control Units annually.
- The plant will supply the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) factory in Bryan County, the Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Alabama (HMMA) factory in Montgomery, and the Kia Georgia plant.
- Development Authority of Bryan County Chairman Jon Seagraves welcomed Mobis to the community, citing close proximity of Belfast Commerce Park to available housing in nearby Heartwood, a top-performing public school system, interstate access, and rail.
- Seagraves noted that the site was purchased about two years ago and had the potential to attract a special project.
- Seagraves highlighted nearly $1 billion of investment and the creation of over 1,500 good-paying jobs.
- Seagraves thanked Bryan County, the City of Richmond Hill, and Raydient Places for their support and partnership on the project.
- Bryan County Commission Chairman Carter Infinger hailed the announcement and stated the county is working with Richmond Hill on infrastructure.
- Infinger said Bryan County is working in unison with Richmond Hill to improve the Belfast Keller corridor in the coming years to ease traffic backups and improve safety.
- The first improvement expected to begin next year aims at improving and widening intersections with Cranston Bluff Road and the new high school entrances.
- Later projects will improve Belfast Keller Road from Belfast River Road to Belfast Commerce Center Drive.
- Alyce Thornhill, Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy, represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The project was developed in partnership with the Development Authority of Bryan County, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia Power.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that suppliers like Hyundai Mobis are transitioning to the jobs of the future and becoming part of Georgia’s growing EV ecosystem.
- In May, GDEcD predicted that non-affiliated HMGMA suppliers in connection with the project would invest an additional $1 billion into Georgia.
- Wilson noted that this prediction was blown past in about two weeks.
- Wilson expressed excitement to prepare Georgia’s workforce of tomorrow for the jobs being created now with support from partners like Georgia Quick Start and sister agencies.
- Georgia is positioned as a hub for the electric mobility industry, building on assets that make the automotive industry successful.
- Since 2020, more than 30 EV-related projects have contributed over $13.3 billion in investment and announced more than 18,100 jobs in Georgia.
- On October 25, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America officially broke ground in Bryan County on its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility.
- Offsite suppliers connected to the HMGMA project are predicted to invest approximately $1 billion in other locations around the state.
