# Global Electric Vehicle Charging Infrastructure Market

**Type:** charging_infrastructure  ·  **Role:** charging_infrastructure  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [EV Charging Infrastructure Market Growth Outlook 2026-32](https://www.marknteladvisors.com/research-library/ev-charging-infrastructure-market.html)  ·  `page_000730`

## Facts

- Global electric vehicle charging infrastructure market size was valued at USD 28.21 billion in 2025 and is projected to grow from USD 33.32 billion in 2026 to USD 90.5 billion by 2032.
- The industry is projected to reflect sustained growth at a steady CAGR of 18.12% during 2026-2032.
- Rivian broke ground on its USD 5 billion plant at Stanton Springs North, Georgia, on September 16, 2025, targeting 400,000 vehicles annually and 7,500 jobs by 2030.
- General Motors committed USD 4 billion across plants in Michigan, Kansas, and Tennessee, designating Factory ZERO as its dedicated EV assembly hub targeting over 2 million U.S.-produced vehicles per year.
- In 2025, global EV sales reached approximately 20.7 million units, with the U.S. accounting for 1.6 million combined BEV and PHEV sales.
- The U.S. public charging stock grew 20% in 2024 to just under 200,000 public charging points.
- The United States has allocated USD 5 billion under the NEVI Formula Program via the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law to expand nationwide charging infrastructure and improve highway connectivity.
- According to the U.S. Department of Energy, DC fast charging stations cost between USD 10,000–USD 40,000 per unit in hardware, with installation adding another USD 4,000–USD 51,000 per port.
- ChargePoint introduced a next-generation AC Level 2 charging architecture featuring bidirectional charging, Vehicle-to-Everything (V2X) capability, and charging speeds up to 19.2 kW in North America and 22 kW in Europe in April 2025.
- SOLUM launched 50 kW modular EV power units designed for scalable, flexible, and cost-efficient charging station deployment at CES 2025 in Las Vegas in January 2025.
- The global electric vehicle charging infrastructure industry is moderately fragmented, with the top five players Tesla, Charge Point, Shell Recharge, ABB, and Electrify America collectively holding around 45% of the global market share.
- Key players in the electric vehicle charging infrastructure industry include Tesla, ChargePoint, ABB, Shell Recharge, Siemens, BP Pulse, EVgo, Blink Charging, Electrify America, EVBox, Schneider Electric, Tritium, IONITY, Wallbox, and Enel X.
- Commercial sector alone contributes approximately 56% share of the overall revenue in the global electric vehicle charging infrastructure industry.
- Wall Mounted chargers account for approximately 57% share of the electric vehicle charging infrastructure industry.
