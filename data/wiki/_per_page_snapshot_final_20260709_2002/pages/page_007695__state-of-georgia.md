# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Foreign Investment Travels Nonstop to Georgia – Site Selection Magazine](https://siteselection.com/foreign-investment-travels-nonstop-to-georgia/)  ·  `page_007695`

## Facts

- Nearly half of the projects announced in 2019 were from internationally owned companies.
- Governor Brian Kemp and the Georgia Department of Economic Development announced in August that international partnerships accounted for nearly $2 billion in foreign direct investment (FDI) in fiscal year 2021.
- Foreign direct investments created twenty percent more jobs than during the previous fiscal year.
- Hartsfield-Jackson International Airport provides nonstop service to more than 70 international destinations in 42 countries across Asia, Europe, Africa, and Latin America.
- Georgia Ports Authority reported record volumes of 3.9 million 20-foot equivalent container units (TEUs) for the fiscal year-to-date (July–March), a 15% increase, with plans to add more than 1.4 million additional TEUs of annual capacity.
- The state hosts 70 international consulates, 42 bi-national chambers of commerce, and 25 international trade offices.
- Georgia provides access to a labor pool of more than 120,000 graduates annually from over 80 colleges, technical schools, and universities.
- South Korea represents the highest job creation from a single country with nineteen projects, bringing more than $1.3 billion in investment and more than 2,700 jobs during 2020 alone.
- Swiss-based projects led in total foreign direct investment.
- German businesses created the second-highest number of jobs during fiscal year 2021.
- SK Innovation, South Korea’s largest energy company, is building two EV battery plants approximately 70 miles northeast of Atlanta.
- The project is part of a $2.6 billion investment in SK Innovation’s U.S. battery business, directly creating more than 2,600 permanent jobs in Jackson County by 2024.
- The first SK Battery America plant is scheduled to begin initial operations in 2021 with mass production in 2022.
- A second plant at the same site is expected to begin mass production in 2023.
- In May, Duckyang, an electric mobility parts supplier of automotive battery modules and energy storage systems to SK Battery America, announced a $10 million investment to open its first U.S. manufacturing facility in Jackson County.
- The new Duckyang facility will create 285 jobs in Braselton, Georgia.
- Duckyang is headquartered in Ulsan, Korea, and supplies cockpit modules to customers including Hyundai, Kia, Renault Samsung Motors, Genesis, and Mobis.
- Dong-in Son, CEO of Duckyang Ind. Co. Ltd., stated that entering the U.S. EV battery market with SKBA allows cooperation with automotive companies like Hyundai and Kia Motors, with expected business expansion to supply EV parts and interior parts (cockpit module, door trim panel module) to global automakers based in the U.S.
- Netherlands-based Vanderlande Industries will invest $59 million to expand its North American headquarters in Cobb County.
- The Vanderlande expansion creates 500 new jobs in Marietta, bringing the company’s total Georgia employment to more than 1,250 people.
- Vanderlande is the global market leader for value-added logistics automation in airport, parcel, and warehouse markets, with systems active in 600 airports including 14 of the world’s top 20.
- Nick Porter, President of Vanderlande’s Airports & Parcel Solutions, North America, stated the company is moving into a new North American head office in Marietta to expand local teams and support North American customers.
- Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport serves 150 U.S. destinations and more than 75 international destinations in 50 countries.
- Georgia ranked among the Top 10 exporting states in 2020 for the first time, with merchandise exports reaching 215 unique countries and territories.
- The state exported $38.8 billion in goods last year, with total trade reaching $137.7 billion, ranking 7th in the U.S.
- Georgia’s top five export markets were Canada, Germany, China, Mexico, and Singapore.
- Exports to Germany increased nearly 50%, followed by China at 45% and Hong Kong at 41%.
- Increased exports to China reflect new purchases under a January 2020 U.S.-China trade agreement and the return of market access for U.S. poultry.
- Approximately two-thirds of Georgia trade involves 12 strategic markets: Brazil, Canada, Chile, China, Colombia, Europe, Israel, Japan, Korea, Mexico, Peru, and the United Kingdom.
- Approximately 90% of Georgia merchandise exports are manufactured goods, with manufacturing exports growing by more than 30% over the last 10 years.
- Aerospace products remain Georgia’s No. 1 export industry, totaling $9.98 billion in 2020 and ranking 5th in the U.S. for aerospace exports.
- Sales to Hong Kong surged more than 140%, while Germany remains the top customer for Georgia aviation products.
- A 13% surge in automotive exports over 2019 was driven by deep water ports and an ideal Southeast location for original equipment manufacturers.
- The state’s automotive industry increased exports nearly 600% to Germany and more than 800% to China.
- Georgia’s agriculture industry delivered $4.25 billion in global exports in 2020, maintaining 2019 totals, with the state remaining a leader in poultry, cotton, peanuts, and forest products.
