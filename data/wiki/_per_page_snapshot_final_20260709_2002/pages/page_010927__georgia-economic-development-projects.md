# Georgia Economic Development Projects

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2025-07-29

**Source:** [Economic Development Around the State - Georgia Trend Magazine](https://www.georgiatrend.com/2025/07/29/economic-development-around-the-state-95/)  ·  `page_010927`

## Facts

- Kubota Manufacturing of America celebrated the grand opening of its 700,000-square-foot facility in Gainesville.
- The Kubota facility began manufacturing front-end loaders for its tractors this summer.
- The $190 million Kubota project will allow the company to produce 170,000 units a year.
- The Kubota project brings 500 jobs to the Gainesville area.
- OXERRA is expanding in Augusta with a new regional distribution center.
- OXERRA, a global manufacturer of iron oxide pigment, recently broke ground on its 120,000-square-foot facility.
- The OXERRA distribution center will incorporate eco-friendly materials and energy-efficient lighting targeting LEED certification.
- The OXERRA project is expected to be completed in January 2026.
- Amazon will open a new package sorting center in Hogansville, Troup County.
- The Amazon facility will span 1.6 million square feet.
- The Amazon sorting center will create 1,000 new jobs when it opens in 2027.
- The Gathering at South Forsyth is a $3 billion mixed-use development that reached an important milestone in June.
- The Forsyth County Commission approved the final agreements for the 100-acre site in June.
- The site will include an 18,000-seat arena, residential, retail and dining components.
- PureCycle Technologies is expanding its proposed Generation 2 recycling facility in Augusta.
- The PureCycle facility uses chemical and thermal processes to extract recyclable materials from finished products.
- The PureCycle facility will recycle polypropylene, aiming for 1 billion pounds of capacity by 2030.
- Construction on the PureCycle Technologies facility should begin later this year.
- Atlanta-based MMM Acquisitions is building a data center campus in Marietta on 31 acres.
- The MMM Acquisitions data center campus will feature two buildings totaling 347,200 square feet and a 4-acre substation.
- Georgia Tech is launching a third innovation district in Midtown Atlanta called the Creative Quarter.
- Georgia Tech is transforming 8 acres of space along North Avenue and Marietta Street into the hub.
- The Creative Quarter will serve as a center for developing, sharing, and experimenting with new technologies for producing art.
- Plans include renovating a historic building to create a vibrant hub for food, arts, academics, and innovation.
