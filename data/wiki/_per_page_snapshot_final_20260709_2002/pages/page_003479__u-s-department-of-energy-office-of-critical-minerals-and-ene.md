## Facts

- The U.S. Department of Energy’s Office of Critical Minerals and Energy Innovation (CMEI) is advancing America's critical minerals supply chains and accelerating next-generation energy technologies to strengthen national energy security and power the future.
- New funding opportunities for the EV and battery supply chain are announced regularly by CMEI.
- DE-FOA-0003514 is a Notice of Funding Opportunity titled "Fiscal Year 2025 Transportation Technologies Office (TTO) Program Wide Funding Opportunity Announcement" with a concept paper deadline of 4/1/2025 and a full application deadline of 6/18/2025.
- DE-FOA-0003383 is a Funding Opportunity Announcement titled "Fiscal Year 2024 Vehicle Technologies Office Batteries Funding Opportunity Announcement" with a concept paper deadline of 9/9/2024 and a full application deadline of 10/30/2024.
- DOE-FOA-0003434 is a Notice of Intent to Issue Funding Opportunity Announcement DE-FOA-3434 titled "Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act Electric Drive Vehicle Battery Improving Economics, Recovery and Reuse, and State of Health" with no concept paper or full application deadlines listed.
- Official notices of solicitations can be found at the CMEI Program Information Center or Grants.gov.
- The published list of funding opportunities is for information only, may not be inclusive of all solicitations, and has funding amounts and schedule dates subject to change.
- The Small Business Innovations Research (SBIR) and Small Business Technology Transfer (STTR) programs are federal research and development initiatives specifically targeting small businesses, allowing awardees to retain technology rights and encouraging commercialization.
- TTO supports SBIR/STTR through broader DOE efforts to increase commercialization of DOE-supported R&D, stimulate private sector innovation, and improve the return on investment from federally funded research.
- State, local, and federal incentives, including tax credits for alternative fuel vehicles and infrastructure, are listed on the Alternative Fuels Data Center.
- The DOE Transportation Technologies Office (TTO) provides a recorded Information Session Series explaining the NOFO process and application components to help potential applicants understand requirements and submission procedures.
- As of October 2024, the funding opportunity process changed from Funding Opportunity Announcement (FOA) to Notice of Funding Opportunity (NOFO) format, making some older references in the session series out of date but still relevant for understanding VTO funding requirements.
- Session 1 of the TTO Information Session Series covers basics of a VTO NOFO, including concept paper and full application phases, key content to review, specific language to pay attention to, and required components.
- Session 2 covers preparing a NOFO budget justification and other application components, including SF 424, allowable project costs, budget justification, cost share, project team information, letters of commitment, and Statement of Project Objectives (SOPO).
- Session 3 provides tips for strong NOFO applications from a reviewer’s perspective, identifying strengths and weaknesses based on TTO’s Technology Integration Program’s technical review criteria, and includes examples for developing an application budget justification.
