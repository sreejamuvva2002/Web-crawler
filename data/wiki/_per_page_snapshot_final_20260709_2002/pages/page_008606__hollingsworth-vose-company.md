# Hollingsworth & Vose Company

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Governor McDonnell Announces $6.1 Million Investment in Floyd County | Virginia Economic Development Partnership](https://www.vedp.org/press-release/2013-11/floyd-county-6.1-million-investment)  ·  `page_008606`

## Facts

- Hollingsworth & Vose Company will invest $6.1 million in its operation in Floyd County, Virginia.
- The company will expand its existing facility to add manufacturing capacity for a product currently only manufactured in the U.K.
- The expansion is expected to create 17 new jobs.
- Virginia successfully competed against Georgia and Massachusetts to secure this project.
- Governor Bob McDonnell announced the investment on November 14, 2013.
- Hollingsworth & Vose has been a major employer in Floyd County for nearly 40 years, operating there since 1974.
- Jim Cheng, Virginia Secretary of Commerce and Trade, stated that Hollingsworth & Vose is a global leader selling products in more than 80 countries.
- Val Hollingsworth, President and CEO, said the company is pleased to continue growing and investing in Floyd, Virginia.
- Mike Clark, H&V Division President for High Efficiency and Specialty Filtration, noted the expansion will provide capacity to support increasing North American demand for high performance synthetic filter media.
- The new state-of-the-art production line will produce H&V's patented Technostat electret filter media, which provides high efficiency filtration with very low pressure drop.
- Hollingsworth & Vose is a global leader in the supply of technically advanced engine, high efficiency and liquid filtration media, battery separator materials, and industrial nonwovens.
- The company operates manufacturing sites and research centers across the Americas, Europe, and Asia.
- The Virginia Economic Development Partnership worked with Floyd County and the New River Valley Economic Development Alliance to secure the project for Virginia.
- Governor McDonnell approved a $100,000 grant from the Governor’s Opportunity Fund to assist with the project.
- The Virginia Tobacco Indemnification and Community Revitalization Commission approved $65,000 in Tobacco Region Opportunity Funds for the project.
- Through the Virginia Jobs Investment Program, the Virginia Department of Business Assistance will provide funding and services to support recruitment and training activities.
- Floyd County Board of Supervisors Chairman Case C. Clinger expressed delight at the company's continued investment and job creation in the region.
- Delegate Terry Kilgore, Chairman of the Virginia Tobacco Indemnification and Community Revitalization Commission, highlighted the additional job creation and investment as a testament to the county's efforts to compete for new business.
