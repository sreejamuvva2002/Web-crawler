# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA)

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-11-11

**Source:** [Metaplant in Motion: Hyundai’s Ultra-Modern EV Factory in Georgia - ENGtechnica](https://engtechnica.com/metaplant-in-motion-hyundais-ultra-modern-ev-factory-in-georgia/)  ·  `page_021172`

## Facts

- Officially named Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA).
- Represents an approximate $7.6 billion investment for the initial factory.
- Total planned investments reach $12.6 billion in Georgia.
- Located near the port of Savannah.
- Facility covers about 697,000 m² (more than 7.5 million ft²).
- Aims for an annual production capacity of up to 500,000 electric vehicles (per IEEE Spectrum).
- Production will serve Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis brands.
- Features a high level of automation and digital integration.
- Hundreds of industrial robots on-site handle welding, stamping, and body assembly.
- Automated guided vehicles (AGVs) navigate the factory floor to move parts and finished vehicles autonomously.
- AGVs are integrated with AI-based logistics and just-in-time supply-chain management.
- Human workers are referred to as “Meta Pros” and occupy roles focused on craftsmanship and quality.
- Repetitive heavy work is handled by machines rather than human labor.
- Sets sustainability targets including solar roofs covering part of the parking lots.
- Uses hydrogen-fuel-cell trucks for parts delivery.
- Aims to source 100% of the plant’s energy from renewables.
- Supported by a large battery-production joint venture with LG Energy Solution.
- The battery production joint venture ensures the EV supply chain is logically co-located with the factory.
- Building the EV factory from scratch allows for optimized line layout, digital twins, scalable production, and lean logistics.
- Underscores a manufacturing shift from human labor to smart systems and from internal-combustion legacy to an electric-vehicle future.
- The facility’s success depends on workforce training, resilient supply chains, and policy support in a competitive global market.
- Serves as a blueprint for next-generation mobility manufacturing in the United States.
