# State of Georgia

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-04-30

**Source:** [Inside Georgia’s $30 Billion EV Bet: The State Rewriting America’s Auto Map - North Atlanta Star](https://northatlantastar.com/georgia-ev-industry-growth-investment-analysis/)  ·  `page_000739`

## Facts

- Georgia has secured more than $27 billion in private investment tied to electric vehicles and battery manufacturing over the past several years.
- Hyundai Motor Group is building a “metaplant” in Bryan County, near Savannah.
- The $7.59 billion Hyundai metaplant facility is designed to produce up to 300,000 electric vehicles a year.
- Battery cells for the Hyundai metaplant will be made through a joint venture with LG Energy Solution.
- The first vehicles at the Hyundai metaplant are expected to include models like the IONIQ 5.
- The Hyundai metaplant represents a fully integrated EV ecosystem on a single site.
- Rivian Automotive is preparing a $5 billion manufacturing campus near Social Circle.
- The Rivian manufacturing campus is expected to produce up to 400,000 vehicles annually when complete.
- The Rivian facility workforce is projected to reach 7,500 employees.
- Rivian has established an East Coast headquarters along the Atlanta BeltLine, adding hundreds of white-collar roles to the region.
- Kia has invested $200 million to retrofit its West Point facility for electric production.
- The EV9 SUV is now rolling off the line at Kia's West Point facility.
- SK Battery America has built out a $2.6 billion battery complex in Commerce.
- The SK Battery America complex supplies lithium-ion cells for vehicles such as the Ford F-150 Lightning and Volkswagen ID.4.
- More than $2.5 billion in additional supplier investments have been announced around the Hyundai metaplant across multiple counties.
- Hyundai Mobis is building power systems facilities around the Hyundai metaplant.
- Firms such as Hwashin and Daechang Seat Corp are investing in chassis components and seating systems for the region.
- The supplier ecosystem operates on a tightly synchronized “just-in-time” manufacturing model.
- Third-party operators DHL, UPS, and GXO Logistics are expanding warehouse and distribution capacity to support parts flow between suppliers and assembly lines.
- State programs like the Quality Jobs Tax Credit and Investment Tax Credit helped offset capital costs for multibillion-dollar facilities.
- The state’s Quick Start workforce program allows companies to train employees before plants even open.
- The Inflation Reduction Act of 2022 includes a $7,500 consumer tax credit tied to North American assembly.
- Since the passage of the Inflation Reduction Act, more than $28 billion in clean energy projects have been announced across Georgia in less than three years.
- Georgia Institute of Technology plays a central role in battery research and manufacturing innovation for the EV sector.
- Technical colleges have introduced accelerated programs tied directly to employers like Rivian.
- The K-12 system is evolving with early-stage career pathways aimed at preparing students for EV-related roles before graduation.
- In early 2026, SK Battery America cut nearly 1,000 jobs at its Georgia facility.
- SK Battery America cited softer-than-expected EV demand and shifting production plans by automakers for the layoffs.
- High vehicle prices and interest rates have slowed EV adoption in parts of North America.
- Market uncertainty has raised questions about whether EV production capacity will outpace demand.
- Gaps in charging networks remain, particularly in rural areas.
- Large-scale plants place significant strain on local resources, including water and energy supply.
- Future changes in federal incentives or state policy could alter the economics that underpin current investments.
- Georgia now leads the nation in announced EV and battery investments.
- Georgia is on pace to rival Michigan in battery production capacity by the end of the decade.
- By 2030, Georgia could be producing hundreds of thousands of electric vehicles annually.
- Georgia is building a modern auto industry from the ground up that is integrated, electrified, and closely tied to global supply chains.
