# Mercedes-Benz

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** research_and_development  ·  **Published:** 2025-05

**Source:** [Mercedes Invests In Georgia For Its Next Big Thing | Carscoops](https://www.carscoops.com/2025/05/mercedes-bringing-jobs-and-new-rd-hub-to-georgia/)  ·  `page_046265`

## Facts

- Mercedes is establishing a new Research & Development hub outside Atlanta.
- The automaker is consolidating its U.S. workforce, with 500 jobs moving to Georgia.
- The consolidation and hub establishment are expected to enable the company to be faster and more agile.
- The new R&D hub represents a multi-million dollar investment in Georgia.
- Georgia is home to Mercedes' U.S. headquarters and the Mercedes-Benz Stadium.
- Mercedes opened its 1MB facility in Sandy Springs seven years ago.
- The 1MB facility currently houses around 800 employees.
- Up to 500 additional employees are coming to Georgia, with arrivals expected by August 2026.
- Mercedes is co-locating its U.S. R&D and customer activities to “enhance its focus on both product excellence and customer experience.”
- Financial services teams and corporate functions currently based in Farmington Hills, Michigan, will move to Sandy Springs.
- Technical teams from across the United States will also relocate to the new R&D hub near Sandy Springs.
- Mercedes stated the location will allow it to “leverage the rich engineering talent pool around institutions like Georgia Institute of Technology.”
- Mercedes stated the location will allow it to “create new opportunities to collaborate with the region’s thriving startup ecosystem and established tech sectors.”
- Mercedes-Benz North America CEO Jason Hoff said the move “strengthens our position for continued growth and reinforces our established commitment to the U.S. market.”
- Jason Hoff added that bringing teams closer together will enable them to be more agile, increase speed to market, and ensure the best customer experience.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp praised the move, stating Georgia continues to lead in mobility and attract world-class companies like Mercedes-Benz.
- Governor Brian Kemp noted they are excited that a job creator with close ties to Georgia is doubling down on its presence in the state.
- Hyundai Motor Group's Metaplant America recently began production outside Savannah.
- Rivian’s factory is advancing with a loan of up to $6.57 billion from the Biden Administration.
- Mercedes called the United States a “vital market” that generated over 374,000 sales last year.
- Mercedes employs around 11,100 people in America.
- A majority of Mercedes' U.S. employees build vehicles at its plant in Tuscaloosa, Alabama.
- Mercedes recently announced plans to add the GLC model to its Tuscaloosa, Alabama plant.
- The Tuscaloosa plant currently produces the GLE, GLE Coupe, GLS, EQE SUV, and EQS SUV.
