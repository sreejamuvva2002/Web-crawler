# SHIROKI Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2013-03-31

**Source:** [SHIROKI Corporation  Business Report FY ended 2013 - MarkLines Automotive Industry Portal](https://www.marklines.com/en/top500/cf/s500_134_hl2012)  ·  `page_049848`

## Facts

- SHIROKI Corporation reported FY ended March 31, 2013 sales of 109,850 million JPY, a 0.6% decrease year-on-year.
- Operating income for FY ended March 31, 2013 was 2,506 million JPY, down 53.0% from the prior year.
- Ordinary income for FY ended March 31, 2013 was 3,063 million JPY, down 42.8% year-on-year.
- Net income for FY ended March 31, 2013 was 1,586 million JPY, down 34.1% year-on-year.
- The company's smaller recliner, spring-lock seat rail, and new-type wire regulators are equipped on the Suzuki "Wagon R" and "Spacia".
- Luggage closures developed by the company are equipped on the Toyota Lexus series and "Crown".
- In March 2013, SHIROKI Corporation was awarded the Quality Management Award from Hino Motors.
- Subsidiary PT. Shiroki Indonesia completed its plant building in July 2012 and launched commercial production in February 2013.
- The company announced in July 2012 that it will establish a subsidiary in Thailand named Shiroki Asia Corporation Limited to support growth in Asia.
- Shiroki Asia Corporation Limited will be capitalized at 10 million baht and 25 million yen.
- Sales for Shiroki Asia Corporation Limited are expected to reach about 80 million baht (approximately 200 million yen) in fiscal 2015.
- Shiroki North America, Inc. supplied door sashes to Nissan North America, marking the fourth vehicle in a row the company helped launch for them.
- The company worked on preparing for the August 2013 production launch of aluminum sashes for GM, which will be the first time supplying GM in North America.
- Shiroki North America, Inc. was awarded the American Region Excellence in Quality Award for the second consecutive year from Nissan North America.
- SHIROKI Corporation is introducing a "satellite supply system" capable of cutting logistics costs by up to 50 percent compared with the current system.
- The satellite supply system carries products such as seat adjusters and door frames as modules or semi-finished products to satellite locations near customer assembly plants for final assembly before delivery.
- The new satellite supply system will be deployed primarily in North America and China where supply areas are widely spread.
- The company envisions establishing up to four satellite locations through its mid-term business plan ending March 2016.
- SHIROKI Corporation announced a new mid-term management plan covering the period ending with the 2016 fiscal year (March 2017).
- The mid-term "Rolling Plan" aims to increase consolidated operating income by 21.7% to 140 billion yen and raise its profit margin from 3.4% to 5.0%, using the 2012 fiscal year as a benchmark.
- Alongside the Rolling Plan, the company announced Shiroki Vision 2021, which plans a business roadmap for the 2021 fiscal year ending in March 2022.
- Performance objectives for Shiroki Vision 2021 include 200 billion yen operating revenue, a 5% profit margin, and a 50% ratio of sales outside Japan.
- The company plans to speed up global operations by building new plants at a pace of one every two years and increase business outside Japan.
- R&D expenditure for the automotive components business was 2,729 million JPY for FY ended March 31, 2013.
- Recent R&D achievements include next-generation power seats, next-generation manual seats, new wire-type regulators, new arm-type regulators, and new door frames.
- Overall capital expenditure for FY ended March 31, 2013 was 5,900 million JPY, with most devoted to the automotive component business to improve productivity, reduce costs, and prepare facilities for new vehicle models.
- Shiroki North America, Inc. in Tennessee, USA plans to install manufacturing equipment with an estimated investment of 1,209 million JPY from January to December 2013.
- Shiroki-GA, LLC in Georgia, USA plans to install manufacturing equipment with an estimated investment of 413 million JPY from January to December 2013.
- Shiroki-GT, LLC in Tennessee, USA plans to install manufacturing equipment with an estimated investment of 43 million JPY from January to December 2013.
- Subsidiary Shiroki (Thailand), Co., Ltd. in Chonburi, Thailand plans to install manufacturing equipment with an estimated investment of 1,078 million JPY from January to December 2013.
- Guangzhou Shiroki Corp. in Guangdong, China plans to install manufacturing equipment with an estimated investment of 684 million JPY from January to December 2013.
- The company holds technology licensing agreements for seat recliners with Keiper GmbH & Co. (Germany) until March 31, 2014 and NHK Spring (Thailand) until December 9, 2013.
- The company licenses door frames to Summit Laemchabang Auto Body Co. (Thailand) until December 31, 2014 and Asan Co., Ltd. (Korea) until project completion.
- The company licenses window regulators to RG Brose (South Africa) and Brose do Brasil (Brazil) until March 31, 2015.
- The company licenses seat recliners, seat adjusters, and window regulators to Technico Industries Ltd. (India) until mass production ceases.
