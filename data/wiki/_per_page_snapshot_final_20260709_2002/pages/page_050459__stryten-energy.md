# Stryten Energy

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- Stryten Energy is helping to create a domestic supply chain that will contribute to job growth and economic development.
- Tax credits, grants and investments in the workforce are creating high-quality manufacturing jobs through the clean energy supply chain.
- The state of Georgia is second in the nation in clean energy investments, with projects worth $23.12 billion and creating almost 29,500 jobs.
- Since the Inflation Reduction Act and Bipartisan Infrastructure Law were passed, clean energy companies have announced more than 271,713 new jobs in 47 states, representing over $352 billion in new investments.
- This law includes vital Investment Tax Credits for domestically sourced and manufactured standalone clean energy storage intended to lower costs, increase energy efficiency and strengthen energy security for America.
- Stryten Energy is headquartered in Georgia.
- Stryten Energy offers advanced lead, lithium, vanadium redox flow or a hybrid of battery chemistries.
- Customers receive the optimal solution for their application and energy storage duration requirements based on this range of technologies.
- Stryten’s battery technology strategy and investments continue to innovate, build and strengthen U.S. manufacturing and material supply chains for lead, lithium and vanadium batteries to ensure they are sustainable for years to come.
- Scott Childers, Vice President, Essential Power, stated that the path to energy independence in the United States begins in Georgia and Stryten Energy is helping to create a domestic supply chain that will contribute to job growth and economic development.
- Domestic manufacturing of battery energy storage systems is a crucial part of creating a future of cleaner, abundant energy while ensuring energy security and resiliency for businesses, consumers and the country, according to Childers.
- At the 2024 Georgia Technology Summit, Stryten Energy was recognized as a Top 10 Innovative Company in Georgia by the Technology Association of Georgia (TAG).
- This annual program recognizes Georgia-based companies of all sizes for their contributions to innovation and economic growth within the state’s technology sector.
- Governor Brian Kemp declared March 28 as Georgia Technology and Innovation Day during his opening remarks at the summit.
- Kemp stated that Georgia’s technology industry and innovative culture are essential parts of its continued economic success.
- Melissa Floyd, Vice President, Communications and Digital Marketing, delivered an overview of Stryten Energy to attendees at the summit.
- Floyd highlighted Stryten's work contributing to the transition to clean energy.
- Melissa Floyd stated that Georgia has long been known as a leader in software technology but is now seeing a shift toward investments in standing-up manufacturing plants that produce batteries, solar panels, EVs and other next-generation technologies.
- Stryten Energy is proud to be part of the growing energy ecosystem centered in the southeast and concentrated in Georgia, according to Floyd.
- Georgia ranks among the top 10 states in the nation in total energy consumption.
- U.S. power consumption is projected to triple by 2050.
- To meet increasing demand for energy, production from renewable energy sources will need to scale up 9-13 times in the next 30 years.
- Stryten Energy is advancing energy storage in Georgia with a wide range of projects.
- In August 2023, Stryten Energy installed a VRFB system with Snapping Shoals EMC, a non-profit electric cooperative.
- The installation provides an opportunity to test flow battery technology in a utility setting for future R&D and scaling up to megawatt-hour systems.
- This project serves as a proof point for emerging LDES technology.
- This project is the first VRFB energy storage system manufactured and installed in Georgia.
- Stryten plans for this installation to be the first of many as it helps the state prepare for future energy demands.
- A lead battery energy storage (BESS) project is already underway for VICARS Community Center, connecting the BESS to a new microgrid, solar array and the legacy power grid.
- This resilience hub will provide neighbors with a location where they can safely gather in the aftermath of a power outage or severe weather event.
- Groundbreaking for this project is scheduled for April 30, 2024.
- Stryten Energy is collaborating with the Georgia Tech Carbon Neutral Energy Solutions Laboratory (CNES) to fine-tune its advanced battery technology.
- A lead BESS and a VRFB BESS are being placed at CNES to serve as a “living laboratory” to further develop the products and applications.
- Another project will target solar arrays plus BESS microgrids for EV Charging Stations, providing support for sustainability and resiliency on the Georgia Tech campus.
- Through these innovative projects, Stryten Energy is helping shape the future of energy storage technology in Georgia that will be important on a national scale for U.S. energy independence and security.
