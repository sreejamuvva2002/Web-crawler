# Georgia Power

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- Georgia Power owns a network of electric generation plants, hydro dams, and multiple solar energy facilities spread across the state.
- The network provides safe, clean, reliable, and affordable energy to 2.7 million customers.
- Georgia Power owns two nuclear facilities named Hatch and Vogtle.
- These nuclear facilities provide about 29 percent of the electricity used in Georgia.
- The plants are managed by Southern Nuclear, a sister company owned by Southern Company that specializes in nuclear operations.
- The Hatch facility is located in Waynesboro, Georgia.
- The Hatch facility has a current total generating capacity of approximately 3,547 MW.
- Hatch Unit 1 began commercial operation on June 1, 1987.
- Hatch Unit 2 began commercial operation on May 20, 1989.
- Hatch Unit 3 began commercial operation on July 31, 2023.
- Hatch Unit 4 began commercial operation on April 29, 2024.
- The Vogtle facility is located in Baxley, Georgia.
- The Vogtle facility has a current total generating capacity of approximately 1,848 MW.
- Vogtle Unit 1 began commercial operation in December 1975.
- Vogtle Unit 2 began commercial operation in September 1979.
- Georgia Power has constructed and is operating 16 solar farms and additional demo facilities across the state of Georgia.
- Many of these solar facilities are built in partnership with military installations.
- The largest solar facility, with a capacity of 128 MW, is located at Robins Air Force Base in Warner Robins, Georgia.
- Other solar facilities add carbon-free energy to the grid and supply renewable energy in support of the Community Solar program.

- Georgia Power's natural gas facilities have a combined generating capacity of over 5,000 MW.
- The McDonough-Atkinson facility in Smyrna, GA has approximately 2,520 MW capacity using 3x Combined Cycle technology.
- The McIntosh facility in Rincon, GA has approximately 1,376 MW capacity using 2x Combined Cycle and 8x Combustion Turbine / Simple Cycle technology.
