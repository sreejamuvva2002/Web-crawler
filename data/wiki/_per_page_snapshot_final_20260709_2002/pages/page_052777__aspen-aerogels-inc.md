## Facts

- Aspen Aerogels' PyroThin Thermal Barrier business addresses the U.S. EV market, which remains in reset mode with market share settling at approximately 5%-6%.
- GM’s monthly EV market share averaged 14.1% this year, suggesting a sales rate over 100,000 EVs in 2026.
- GM produced EVs in Q1 and April at levels below current sales volume, resulting in lower finished vehicle inventory levels.
- GM has maintained its full line of EV nameplates and remains dedicated to long-term EV success, including in its Cadillac division where EV sales represented 28% of total sales in 2025 and over 30% in Q1 2026.
- In Europe, battery electric vehicles now account for more than 20% of new vehicle registrations, with stronger structural drivers supporting early production ramp-up among OEMs with whom Aspen has design awards.
- EU Thermal Barrier revenue in Q1 increased more than threefold versus the prior year quarter, and 2026 revenue is expected in the range of $10 million-$15 million.
- European thermal barrier programs incorporate battery cells from a diversified global supply base including European, Korean, Japanese, and leading Chinese manufacturers.
- Aspen Aerogels is advancing new growth opportunities in battery energy storage systems for grid infrastructure, data centers, and other high reliability applications.
- Q2 2026 guidance assumes GM production at an annualized rate of approximately 55,000-65,000 vehicles in the quarter.
- IHS Markit forecast projects GM will produce nearly 100,000 vehicles for 2026, pointing to more production weighted to the second half of the year.
- Aspen Aerogels received $37.6 million in claim proceeds from GM in Q1, which is recognized as revenue ratably through the end of 2027 with approximately $4.9 million per quarter thereafter.
- An operational disruption occurred on April 8 at Aspen Aerogels' Aerogel manufacturing facility in East Providence involving an explosion in a high-temperature oven, causing plant damage and temporary cessation of operations.
- Staged restart of East Providence operations is expected to begin in May, subject to continued progress in mechanical, operational, and safety reviews.
