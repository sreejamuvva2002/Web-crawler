# Blue Bird Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2017-05-02

**Source:** [Blue Bird Corporation has announced a new product offering](https://peachcountydevelopment.com/news/article/blue-bird-first-to-offer-school-buses-with-allison-transmissions-fuelsense)  ·  `page_052958`

## Facts

- Blue Bird Corporation announced a new product offering for buses ordered on or after May 1, 2017 with Allison transmissions: FuelSense 2.0 featuring DynActive Shifting.
- FuelSense 2.0 is a software update to the previous FuelSense offering that allows fleets to optimize fuel economy and performance to their specific needs.
- Mark Terry, chief commercial officer at Blue Bird Corporation, stated excitement over Allison's response to customer needs to help increase fuel economy and anticipated savings for districts.
- FuelSense 2.0 uses DynActive Shifting to provide an infinitely variable combination of shift points and a learning algorithm to continuously find the ideal balance of fuel economy and performance.
- The technology provides maximum value for applications with heavy start-stop needs, such as school buses.
- Randy Kirk, senior vice president of product engineering at Allison, stated they tested FuelSense 2.0 with customers across various duty cycles, accumulating more than 3 million miles.
- Testing showed fuel economy gains of up to 6 percent.
- This improvement resulted in a potential vehicle savings of $300 to $900 annually based on the application, annual mileage and fuel price.
- Three packages are available: FuelSense 2.0, FuelSense 2.0 Plus, and FuelSense 2.0 Max, all including DynActive Shifting.
- FuelSense 2.0 Plus and Max include Neutral at Stop, while FuelSense 2.0 Max includes enhanced Acceleration Rate Management.
- The Improved ‘Neutral at Stop’ feature lowers fuel consumption and emissions by reducing or eliminating the load on the engine when stopped.
- Two versions of Neutral at Stop are available: Standard (partial/first-level) and Premium (full Neutral at Stop with low-speed coasting capability), both featuring a locked output at stop to prevent rollback.
- Enhanced ‘Acceleration Rate Management’ mitigates aggressive driving by automatically controlling engine torque, providing more precision by limiting vehicle acceleration to a customized rate across five levels of control.
- Heidi Schutte, vice president of marketing and sales for the Americas with Allison Transmission, stated Allison is constantly working towards improved fuel economy and that FuelSense 2.0 offers increased specification flexibility and delivers the best economic value to customers.
- New Blue Bird buses with Allison Transmissions are now available with FuelSense 2.0 options.
- Blue Bird Corporation is celebrating its 90th year in business as of the announcement date.
- Blue Bird (Nasdaq: BLBD) is the leading independent designer and manufacturer of school buses, with more than 550,000 buses sold since its formation in 1927 and approximately 180,000 buses in operation today.
- Blue Bird distinguishes itself by a singular focus on the design, engineering, manufacture and sale of school buses and related parts.
- Blue Bird is the only manufacturer of chassis and body production specifically designed for school bus applications.
- Blue Bird is recognized as an industry leader for school bus innovation, safety, product quality/reliability/durability, operating costs and drivability.
- Blue Bird is the market leader in alternative fuel applications with its propane-powered and compressed natural gas-powered school buses.
- Blue Bird manufactures school buses at two facilities in Fort Valley, Georgia.
- Blue Bird's Micro Bird joint venture operates a manufacturing facility in Drummondville, Quebec, Canada.
- Service and after-market parts are distributed from Blue Bird’s parts distribution center located in Delaware, Ohio.
- Allison Transmission (NYSE: ALSN) is the world's largest manufacturer of fully automatic transmissions for medium- and heavy-duty commercial vehicles and a leader in hybrid-propulsion systems for city buses.
- Allison transmissions are used in applications including refuse, construction, fire, distribution, bus, motorhomes, defense and energy.
- Allison Transmission was founded in 1915, is headquartered in Indianapolis, Indiana, USA, and employs approximately 2,600 people worldwide.
- Allison has a market presence in more than 80 countries with regional headquarters in the Netherlands, China and Brazil.
- Allison has manufacturing facilities in the U.S., Hungary and India.
- Allison has approximately 1,400 independent distributor and dealer locations worldwide.
