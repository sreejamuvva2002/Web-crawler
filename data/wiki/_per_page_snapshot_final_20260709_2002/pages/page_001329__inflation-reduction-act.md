# Inflation Reduction Act

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-08-13

**Source:** [Georgia GOP rep. wants to save Biden’s energy tax credits. Trump says he’ll repeal them - AOL](https://www.aol.com/georgia-gop-rep-wants-save-141641552.html)  ·  `page_001329`

## Facts

- A Georgia Republican who endorsed Donald Trump is concerned about the potential repeal of the Inflation Reduction Act, which has brought billions of dollars of clean energy investment to Georgia.
- Last Tuesday, 18 House Republicans sent a letter to House Speaker Mike Johnson emphasizing the importance of maintaining energy tax credits in the Inflation Reduction Act if Trump were elected in November.
- Donald Trump has stated he would repeal the Inflation Reduction Act on his first day in office.
- Donald Trump has called electric vehicle mandates the "death of the U.S. auto industry."
- The Inflation Reduction Act has put more than $352 billion into clean energy across 47 states.
- Georgia has received $24 billion in clean energy investment according to Climate Power, a climate communication organization.
- Georgia has created 30,661 jobs from clean energy investment according to Climate Power.
- Climate Power states that Georgia leads the United States in clean energy job creation.
- Rep. Buddy Carter of Georgia's 1st District, which encompasses Georgia's coast and inland to Waycross, was one of 17 co-signers of the letter.
- Georgia's 1st Congressional District has received $7.6 billion, which is more than a third of the state's clean energy manufacturing investment.
- Rep. Buddy Carter stated that companies must find a balance between rolling back the worst portions of the Inflation Reduction Act and ensuring stability for continued U.S. investment.
- The Hyundai EV Metaplant and LG Metafactory projects in Southeast Georgia are worth $7.5 billion and bring 8,500 jobs.
- The Seoyon E-HWA EV Parts project invested $76 million and brought 740 jobs.
- These current and ongoing project numbers are according to a new clean energy map by Thirdway, a nonprofit think tank dedicated to climate policy.
- The letter warns that a full repeal of the Inflation Reduction Act would create a worst-case scenario where billions in taxpayer dollars are spent with next to nothing in return.
- The letter states that energy tax credits have spurred innovation, incentivized investment, and created good jobs across many parts of the country.
- The letter was spearheaded by Rep. Andrew Garbarino of New York, who is the co-chair of the bipartisan House Climate Solutions Caucus.
- Hyundai chose Georgia for its access to ports and rails in Savannah.
- Hyundai specifically broke ground in Georgia because of the tax credits laid out in the Inflation Reduction Act, according to an Associated Press report.
- Rep. Buddy Carter stated that Hyundai decided to come to his district before the passage of the Inflation Reduction Act.
- Rep. Buddy Carter expressed excitement over Hyundai as a strong partner in Georgia's First Congressional District, noting the decision predates the act and emphasizing pushing pro-growth policies to beat China.
- Georgia has seen tax benefits from the act beyond business investment according to government studies.
- The IRS recently published new data highlighting tax credit benefits at the household level.
- Climate Power's analysis states that 87,000 Georgia families benefited from more than $148 million in tax credits to lower costs of clean energy and energy efficiency upgrades to their homes during 2023.
- Home improvements covered by the credits include heat pumps, insulation, windows, and efficient air conditioning.
