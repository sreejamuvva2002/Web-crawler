# Rivian

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-10-24

**Source:** [Rivian to cut around 600 jobs - electrive.com](https://www.electrive.com/2025/10/24/rivian-to-cut-around-600-jobs/)  ·  `page_059072`

## Facts

- Rivian plans to cut around 600 jobs, representing approximately 4.5 percent of its workforce.
- The layoff figure comes from an internal memo sent to employees that was seen by various US media outlets.
- The internal memo did not give a specific number, but the Wall Street Journal reported the layoffs would affect more than 600 employees.
- At the end of 2024, Rivian employed just under 15,000 people, meaning 4.5 percent equals 675 jobs.
- The job cuts will mainly be achieved through restructuring in the marketing, vehicle operations, sales/delivery, and mobile operations teams.
- It is not yet known how the job cuts will be distributed among those teams.
- Development and production are not expected to be affected by the layoffs.
- CEO RJ Scaringe stated the cuts were made due to a changing operating backdrop and to rethink how the company is scaling its go-to-market functions.
- Scaringe noted the changes aim to ensure the company can deliver on its potential by scaling efficiently toward building a healthy and profitable business.
- The new and slightly smaller R2 model is expected to go into production in 2026.
- Until the R2 contributes to rising sales and potentially profits, the start of production will initially cost more money.
- In the second quarter, Rivian had a deficit of $1.1 billion on its balance sheet.
- In the third quarter, Rivian recorded rising sales of 13,201 vehicles, which is a 32 percent increase.
- The Q3 sales increase is likely due to a one-off effect surrounding the end of the US tax credit.
- In Q3 2025, many car manufacturers set new electric vehicle records due to subsidy expiry and resulting uncertainty in the US market for electric cars.
- Scaringe cited the subsidy expiry and resulting uncertainty as the likely 'changing conditions' prompting the layoffs.
- Despite strong Q3 sales, Rivian now expects 2025 sales of only 41,500 to 43,500 vehicles.
- The revised 2025 sales forecast is down from Rivian's previous forecast of 46,000 vehicles.
