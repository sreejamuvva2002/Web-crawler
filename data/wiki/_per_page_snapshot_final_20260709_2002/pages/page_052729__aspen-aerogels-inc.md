# Aspen Aerogels, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_material  ·  **Published:** 2026-05-07

**Source:** [Aspen Aerogels (ASPN) Q1 2026 Earnings Transcript | The Motley Fool](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/05/07/aspen-aerogels-aspn-q1-2026-earnings-transcript/)  ·  `page_052729`

## Facts

- Aspen Aerogels reported $37.9 million in revenue for fiscal Q1 2026 (period ended March 31, 2026), down 8% sequentially from fiscal Q4 2025.
- Revenue breakdown included $21.6 million from Energy Industrial and $16.3 million from thermal barrier.
- GAAP net loss was negative $23.7 million compared to negative $72.9 million in fiscal Q4 2025.
- Adjusted EBITDA was negative $12.7 million, improving 29% sequentially from negative $18 million in fiscal Q4 2025 despite lower revenue.
- Company-wide gross margin was 11%, with segment gross margins of 15% for Energy Industrial and 6% for thermal barrier.
- Temporary operational disruption at the East Providence facility due to an explosion caused plant shutdown and near-term cost pressures from repair, expedited freight, and elevated inventory across facilities.
- Aspen Aerogels received $37.6 million in claim proceeds from General Motors (GM).
- $3.5 million of the GM claim was recognized as revenue in fiscal Q1 2026, with $4.9 million per quarter to be recognized through 2027 under ASC 606.
- Cash position ended fiscal Q1 2026 at $175.6 million, an increase from $158.6 million at year-end 2025, supported by GM proceeds and an $8 million working capital benefit.
- Term loan balance was $86 million, with a sole covenant requiring cash equal to at least 100% of the term loan balance after fiscal Q1 2026.
- Fiscal Q1 2026 capital expenditures totaled $1 million, with full-year guidance remaining under $10 million for CapEx and $26 million in scheduled debt payments.
- Fiscal Q2 2026 revenue guidance is between $40 million and $48 million, representing a 5%-28% sequential increase.
- Adjusted EBITDA guidance for Q2 2026 is negative $10 million to negative $4 million, pending production and supply outcomes.
- Management expects approximately 20% segment growth in Energy Industrial in 2026, driven by subsea, LNG, and maintenance turnaround opportunities.
- European thermal barrier revenue tripled year over year for the quarter, with management forecasting $10 million to $15 million in European thermal barrier revenue for 2026.
- General Motors EV market share averaged 14.1%, with Cadillac EV sales representing over 30% of segment sales in fiscal Q1 2026.
- Management cited lower GM production relative to sales, resulting in destocking of finished vehicle inventory.
- PyroThin is Aspen Aerogels' proprietary thermal barrier designed for lithium-ion battery fire protection in electric vehicles and energy storage systems.
- Battery electric vehicles now account for more than 20% of new vehicle registrations in Europe, supporting early stages of production ramp-up among OEMs with whom Aspen has design awards.
- Across European awards, Aspen supports programs incorporating battery cells from a diversified global supply base including European, Korean, Japanese, and leading Chinese manufacturers.
- Aspen is advancing growth opportunities in battery energy storage systems (BESS), actively engaged in qualifications and commercial discussions with developers for grid infrastructure and data centers.
- Management expects sequential revenue growth each quarter through 2026, targeting adjusted EBITDA breakeven at $50 million in quarterly revenue during the second half.
- Guidance assumes the East Providence staged restart proceeds as planned, with delays likely to increase near-term cash outflows and pressure gross profit.
- Sale of Plant 2 assets is expected in fiscal Q4 2026 and will be applied directly for debt reduction.
- Financial framework enables management to reduce breakeven revenue levels from $330 million in 2024 to $175 million by end of 2027.
- An operational disruption occurred at the East Providence facility on April 8, involving an explosion in a high temperature oven that resulted in plant damage confined to a specific area and temporary cessation of operations.
- Staged restart of the East Providence facility is planned for May, conditional on safety reviews and agency coordination.
- Aspen is working closely with its external manufacturing facility (EMF) to enhance capabilities for Energy, Industrial, and Thermal Barrier segments to support short- and long-term supply flexibility.
- Energy Industrial revenues declined 15% sequentially, impacted by delivery delays in the Middle East and conflict in Iran creating logistics and inventory challenges.
- Q2 guidance assumes GM production at an annualized rate of approximately 55,000 to 65,000 vehicles in the quarter, up from Q1 where GM sourced the equivalent of 43,000 vehicles annualized.
- Current IHS forecast has GM producing nearly 100,000 vehicles for 2026, pointing to more production weighted to the second half of the year.
- Elevated costs are expected in Q2 and potentially Q3 due to expedited freight, repair costs, and inventory build across EP and EMF.
- Restructuring actions were designed to achieve EBITDA breakeven at $50 million of quarterly revenue, with management expecting to reach it in the second half of 2026.
- Aspen aims to scale its Energy Industrial business to a $200 million high-margin business without the need for incremental capital investment.
- GM has maintained its full line of EV nameplates and remains dedicated to long-term EV success, including the Cadillac division where EV sales represented 28% of total sales in 2025 and over 30% in Q1 2026.
- Aspen's EU thermal barrier revenue in Q1 increased more than threefold versus the prior year quarter, with momentum expected to translate into 2026 revenue in the $10 million to $15 million range.
- Management initiated a strategic review in Q4 last year to evaluate strategic options and align growth strategy and capital allocation priorities with maximizing long-term shareholder value.
- Aspen expects to reach EBITDA breakeven in the second half of 2026, assuming success of ongoing production and supply mitigation efforts.
- CapEx and scheduled debt payments are expected to total less than $12 million in Q2, with working capital variability depending on inventory production and sales locations.
- Total cash outflows for Q2 could be $20 million to $30 million, including $12 million of CapEx and scheduled debt payments, highly dependent on production and supply mitigation efforts.
- Proceeds from the potential sale of Plant 2 assets are most likely a Q4 event rather than Q3 and would be applied directly to reduce term debt dollar-for-dollar.
- Aspen's financial framework supports flexibility to further delever the business and opportunistically invest in strategic growth initiatives while maintaining balance sheet strength.
