# Continental Tire Mount Vernon Facility

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-19

**Source:** [Continental's $76 Million Warehouse Investment Signals Continued Push Toward Automated Distribution - Distribution Strategy Group](https://distributionstrategy.com/2026/06/continentals-76-million-warehouse-investment-signals-continued-push-toward-automated-distribution/)  ·  `page_018646`

## Facts

- Continental is investing $76 million in a highly automated warehouse at its Mount Vernon, Illinois tire plant.
- The investment highlights a broader shift where manufacturers are allocating more capital to distribution infrastructure to achieve faster delivery times, greater inventory visibility, and improved customer service.
- The new facility is scheduled to begin operations in 2027.
- The warehouse will store approximately 500,000 passenger vehicle tires.
- It will serve as a key distribution hub for Continental’s North American tire business.
- The project is being constructed adjacent to Continental’s largest U.S. tire manufacturing plant.
- The primary goal of the investment is to strengthen logistics and fulfillment capabilities rather than increase production capacity.
- The announcement underscores the growing importance of warehouse automation and network efficiency as customers increasingly expect rapid product availability and reliable delivery performance.
- The project occurs amid a multi-year wave of distribution center investments across manufacturing and wholesale sectors.
- Companies ranging from industrial suppliers and building products distributors to electrical, HVAC, and automotive firms are expanding warehouse networks and deploying automation technologies to improve inventory accuracy, reduce labor requirements, and accelerate order fulfillment.
- Continental stated the Mount Vernon warehouse will be highly automated and designed to support growing North American demand while improving service levels.
- The facility will provide additional storage capacity for finished goods and help move products more efficiently through the company’s distribution network.
- Tansu Işık, chief executive officer of Continental Tires Americas, stated: “Our new highly automated finished-goods warehouse underscores our growth ambitions in North America.”
- Manufacturers increasingly view logistics operations as a strategic asset rather than a back-office function due to elevated transportation costs and rising customer expectations for speed.
- The project signals continued confidence in North American demand despite ongoing economic uncertainty.
- Continental’s Mount Vernon plant produces approximately 11.4 million tires annually.
- The plant employs more than 3,500 workers, making it the company’s largest tire manufacturing operation in the United States.
- The warehouse project is expected to further integrate manufacturing and distribution activities at the site, reducing handling requirements and improving the flow of finished products from production lines to customers.
- Advanced warehouse management systems, automated storage technologies, real-time inventory visibility, and faster fulfillment cycles are becoming standard expectations across industrial supply chains.
- Investments in warehouse productivity, automation, and network efficiency are no longer optional initiatives but core components of long-term growth strategies for distributors.
