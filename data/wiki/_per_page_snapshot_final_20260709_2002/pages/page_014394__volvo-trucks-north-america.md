# Volvo Trucks North America

## Facts

- Volvo Trucks North America will showcase its newest truck lineup, expanded connected services, and award‑winning safety technologies at the Technology & Maintenance Council’s Annual Meeting & Transportation Technology Exhibition, taking place March 16–19 at the Music City Center in Nashville.
- Volvo Trucks will host a press conference on Monday, March 16, 6:00–6:30 p.m. CT in booth #1301, where the company will preview new connected services designed to further improve fleet uptime and reveal early details of a new vocational truck engineered in North America to expand its presence in construction, waste and refuse, concrete, logging, agriculture, heavy-haul highway, and municipal.
- Peter Voorhoeve serves as president of Volvo Trucks North America.
- The new Volvo VNR 300 began production last month at Volvo’s New River Valley plant in Dublin, Virginia.
- The new VNR is designed for high-density regional and urban operations, delivering enhanced maneuverability, advanced connectivity, and improved fuel efficiency to support demanding fleet applications.
- The Volvo VNL 860 is Volvo’s flagship long-haul model that redefined the North American trucking standard when introduced in 2024 with a ground-up redesign that advanced aerodynamics, integrated new safety technologies, and elevated driver comfort to deliver measurable gains in fuel efficiency and uptime.
- Both the VNL and VNR are built at Volvo’s New River Valley plant in Dublin, Virginia.
- By engineering and manufacturing its trucks in the United States, Volvo Trucks continues to invest in domestic production while advancing vehicle and connectivity technologies tailored to North American fleets.
- Load Finder aggregates data from more than 40 load boards into a single platform to reduce empty miles, improve route optimization, and increase revenue opportunities.
- The Volvo Connect kiosk features live demonstrations of self-serve remote programming capabilities and proactive software updates that give fleets greater operational control and streamlined maintenance management.
- Volvo Trucks’ integrated side curtain airbag will be recognized as a Heavy Duty Trucking 2026 Top 20 Product, an award honoring the most innovative new or significantly improved products introduced to the trucking industry in 2025.
- The integrated side curtain airbag technology is now standard on every new Volvo VNL and Volvo VNR built for the North American market.
- Volvo Trucks North America is headquartered in Greensboro, North Carolina.
- Its Uptime Services commitment is delivered by a network of nearly 400 authorized dealers across North America and the 24/7 Volvo Trucks Uptime Center.
- Every Volvo truck is assembled in the Volvo Trucks New River Valley manufacturing facility in Dublin, Virginia.
- The Dublin, Virginia plant meets the internationally recognized ISO 9001 standard for quality, 14001 standard for environmental care and holds a dual ISO 50001/Superior Energy Performance certification at the platinum level.
- Volvo Trucks North America provides complete transport solutions for its customers, offering a full range of diesel, alternative-fuel and all-electric vehicles.
- Volvo Trucks supplies complete transport solutions with customer support provided via a global network of dealers with 2,200 service points in about 130 countries.
- Volvo trucks are assembled in 12 countries across the globe.
- In 2025 approximately 120,000 Volvo trucks were delivered worldwide.
- Volvo Trucks is part of the Volvo Group, one of the world’s leading manufacturers of trucks, buses, construction equipment and marine and industrial engines.
