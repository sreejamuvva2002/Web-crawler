# Denso Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 1949-12-16

**Source:** [Denso - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Denso)  ·  `page_053794`

## Facts

- Denso Corporation is a global automotive components manufacturer headquartered in Kariya, Aichi Prefecture, Japan.
- The company was founded on December 16, 1949, after becoming independent from Toyota Motor Corporation as Nippon Denso Co. Ltd.
- About 25% of the company is owned by Toyota Motor Corporation, which holds a 21.25% stake, while Toyota Industries holds a 5.59% stake.
- The company's parent organization is the Toyota Group.
- As of the year ending March 2016, sales to the Toyota Group accounted for less than 50% of total revenue, with 44% originating from other car manufacturers in Japan, Germany, the U.S., and China.
- In 2023, Denso was ranked as the second largest auto parts supplier in the world.
- In 2022, Denso was listed at #278 on the Fortune Global 500 list with total revenue of $49.0 billion and 167,950 employees.
- As of 2021, the company operates subsidiaries including 64 in Japan, 23 in North America, 32 in Europe, 74 in Asia, and seven in Oceania and other regions.
- The company develops and manufactures various auto parts including gasoline and diesel engine components, hybrid vehicle components, climate control systems, instrument clusters, airbag systems, pre-crash radar systems, and spark plugs.
- Denso also develops non-automotive components such as household heating equipment and industrial robots.
- In June 2020, Denso announced the opening of its Electrification Innovation Center at its plant in Anjō to support development of products and technologies for electric and hybrid vehicles.
- As of 2014, global sales breakdown included thermal systems (30.4%), powertrain control systems (35.0%), electronic systems (15.3%), electric systems (9.4%), electric motors (7.0%), other automotive products (1.4%), industrial and consumer products (1.1%), and other non-automotive products (0.4%).
- Denso Wave is a subsidiary that produces automatic identification products, industrial robots, and programmable logic controllers.
- Engineer Masahiro Hara invented the two-dimensional QR code in 1994 while working at Denso Wave to streamline automotive component tracking.
- Denso International America is the American subsidiary of Denso, which employs over 17,000 people at 38 locations across North, Central, and South America.
- DENSO Sales California, Inc., was founded in Hawthorne, California, in March 1971 to promote air conditioner systems for Japanese-made vehicles.
- In May 1975, Denso Corporation opened a sales division in Southfield, Michigan.
- In September 1975, Denso International America opened a service center in Cedar Falls, Iowa, due to an agricultural parts contract with John Deere that included starter motors and meters.
- At year end on March 31, 2008, combined sales for all American locations totaled $8.3 billion.
- Denso manufactures customized electronics and auto-parts specifically for Toyota-Lexus motorsports development divisions, including spark plugs, starter motors, fuel pumps, alternators, Engine Control Module computer systems, and engine and transmission sensors.
- The Toyota TS030 Hybrid used a Denso kinetic energy recovery system to finish second in the 2013 24 Hours of Le Mans.
- On January 30, 2012, the US Justice Department announced that Denso agreed to pay a $78 million fine for its role in a massive price-fixing scheme with Yazaki that fixed prices and allocated components to car manufacturers like Toyota and Honda.
- In August 2020, a class-action lawsuit was filed in Quebec over alleged defective fuel pumps in Acura, Honda, Lexus, Subaru, and Toyota vehicle models, with a separate lawsuit filed for the rest of Canada.
