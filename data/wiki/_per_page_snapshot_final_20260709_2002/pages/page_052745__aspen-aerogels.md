# Aspen Aerogels

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2022-02-19

**Source:** [Aspen Aerogels to open manufacturing facility in Bulloch County](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/aspen-aerogels-open-manufacturing-facility-230500324.html)  ·  `page_052745`

## Facts

- Gov. Brian Kemp announced that Aspen Aerogels plans to construct an advanced manufacturing facility at Bulloch County's Southern Gateway Commerce Park.
- Aspen intends to invest a minimum of $325 million in the project.
- The company plans to create more than 250 advanced manufacturing jobs at its new site near Statesboro, Georgia.
- Production at the new facility is expected to begin in late 2023.
- Combined with Aspen's original East Providence, R.I., plant, the planned Georgia facility is designed to triple Aspen's nameplate aerogel capacity.
- The expansion supports Aspen's thermal barrier expansion plans in the fast-growing electric vehicle market.
- Aspen aims to capitalize on increased demand for its Aerogel Technology Platform as EV OEMs continue to adopt its PyroThin aerogel thermal barrier solutions.
- Don Young, president and CEO of Aspen, stated that Statesboro and the Bulloch County region offer access to efficient ports, a strong available workforce, and secure low-cost utilities and critical raw materials.
- Aspen's PyroThin materials provide thermal management, mechanical stability, and fire protection properties to EV battery systems.
- Aspen plans to build a 500,000-square-foot advanced manufacturing facility situated on a 90-acre parcel in southeast Georgia.
- The company plans to begin hiring personnel in the second half of 2022 across skilled manufacturing operators, advanced process engineers, quality and maintenance technicians, and professional support roles.
- Billy Allen, vice chair of the Development Authority of Bulloch County, welcomed Aspen Aerogels to the Statesboro-Bulloch County community and highlighted the region's commitment to innovation in the electric mobility ecosystem.
- Georgia Southern University and Ogeechee Technical College are expected to provide critical workforce support to help Aspen flourish.
- Assistant Director Alyce Thornhill represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development's Global Commerce division on this project.
- The project is being pursued in partnership with the Development Authority of Bulloch County, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia EMC.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson welcomed Aspen Aerogels' innovative solutions for the electric vehicle and energy storage markets to Georgia and expressed confidence in Georgia employees collaborating with Aspen's scientists and engineers.
- Southern Gateway Commerce Park is a Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) program-certified park, requiring due diligence and specific certification requirements prior to prospective business visits.
