## Facts

- Ascend Elements was founded in 2015 under the initial name Battery Resources, Inc.
- The company was founded by Professor Yan Wang, a materials science expert.
- The core technology developed is known as the Hydro-to-Cathode process.
- The company's mission focuses on creating a sustainable, closed-loop supply chain for battery materials.
- Early financial backing likely came from academic grants, small angel investments, and support from friends and family.
- In September 2021, Ascend Elements closed a $90 million Series C funding round led by Just Climate, an investment platform created by Generation Investment Management.
- Existing investors Fifth Wall, Hitachi Ventures, and Orbia Ventures also participated in the September 2021 Series C round.
- The September 2021 investments played a crucial role in scaling operations, including the construction of the Apex 1 manufacturing facility in Hopkinsville, Kentucky.
- The Apex 1 facility is designed to produce sustainable cathode active materials and precursor cathode active materials (pCAM).
- The Apex 1 facility is projected to be North America's first commercial-scale plant for producing sustainable cathode active materials and pCAM from recycled lithium-ion batteries.
- In September 2022, Ascend Elements secured a $300 million Series D funding round spearheaded by Decarbonization Partners, a collaboration between Temasek and BlackRock.
- The September 2022 Series D round also saw participation from Apeiron Investment Group, Fifth Wall, LG Technology Ventures, and Orbia Ventures.
- The $300 million Series D funding brought the total funds raised by Ascend Elements to over $542 million.
- Major stakeholders in Ascend Elements include Decarbonization Partners (BlackRock and Temasek), Apeiron Investment Group, Just Climate (Generation Investment Management), Fifth Wall, LG Technology Ventures, and Orbia Ventures.
- The Board of Directors includes investor representatives from major funding rounds, founder Professor Yan Wang, and independent directors with industry expertise.
- Voting power within Ascend Elements aligns with equity ownership, meaning entities holding larger equity stakes have greater voting power.
- Major strategic decisions, such as future funding rounds or potential public offerings, require board approval.
- The company's competitors in the battery recycling and materials sector include Li-Cycle, Redwood Materials, American Battery Technology Company, and Cirba Solutions.
