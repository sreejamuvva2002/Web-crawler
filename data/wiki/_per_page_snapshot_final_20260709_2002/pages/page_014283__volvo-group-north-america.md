# Volvo Group North America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Volvo Group Invests in Expanded U.S. Uptime Center, Co-Location of Volvo Financial Services Headquarters | Rental Equipment Register](https://www.rermag.com/news-analysis/supply-side/article/21235324/volvo-construction-equipment-volvo-group-invests-in-expanded-us-uptime-center-co-location-of-volvo-financial-services-headquarters)  ·  `page_014283`

## Facts

- Volvo Group announced a $41 million investment in North Carolina’s Piedmont Triad.
- The investment is designated to purchase and expand the building housing its U.S. Uptime Center.
- The expanded facility will now also serve as the new global headquarters for Volvo Financial Services (VFS).
- The expansion is located about two miles from VFS’ currently leased location.
- The project will bring about 360 VFS colleagues onto Volvo Group North America’s campus.
- Announcements were made during a groundbreaking ceremony by Martin Weissburg, Volvo Group North America chairman and Mack Trucks president.
- Marcio Pedroso, Volvo Financial Services president at VGNA’s Uptime Center, also participated in the groundbreaking ceremony.
- Martin Weissburg stated the Uptime Center is a strategic site for keeping customers and their vehicles on the road, delivering goods, transporting people, and servicing communities.
- Weissburg noted the purchase and expansion reinforces commitment to the Piedmont Triad communities and welcomes VFS to the campus.
- Marcio Pedroso stated moving VFS allows all Volvo Group entities in the Piedmont to be located on one campus.
- Pedroso added co-location will enable deeper colleague relationships and help retain highly skilled, world-class jobs supporting the local economy.
- The investment includes acquisition of the Uptime Center building, which opened in 2014 and was previously leased by the company.
- The investment includes a 62,000 square-foot (5,800 square meters) expansion to house VFS’ global headquarters.
- At project completion, the facility’s new three-story footprint will be about 185,000 square feet (17,150 square meters).
- The expanded building will be connected by a glass atrium and two upper-level walkways.
- VFS is the captive finance arm of the Volvo Group, providing financial services for customers of Mack Trucks, Volvo Trucks, Volvo Construction Equipment, Nova buses, Prevost coaches, and Volvo buses in North America and globally.
- VFS operates in 50 countries worldwide with approximately 1,700 colleagues.
- The Uptime Center site straddles the cities of Greensboro and High Point.
- The site houses resources to ensure customers’ commercial trucks keep moving, with people and systems dedicated to real-time vehicle issue monitoring and response.
- The site helps dealers troubleshoot difficult cases and find necessary repair parts.
- The site includes connectivity services for over-the-air monitoring and software updates for optimized vehicle performance.
- The site also houses service market logistics, IT, training facilities, and finance and accounting operations.
- The Volvo Group currently employs around 600 people at the Uptime Center.
- At project completion expected in the first quarter of 2023, Volvo Financial Services will relocate approximately 360 jobs to its new headquarters.
