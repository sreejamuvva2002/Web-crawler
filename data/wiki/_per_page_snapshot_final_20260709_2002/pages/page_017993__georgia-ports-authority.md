# Georgia Ports Authority

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2024-03

**Source:** [Georgia Ports Highlights Site Selection Opportunities - Rome CEO](https://romeceo.com/features/2024/03/georgia-ports-highlights-site-selection-opportunities/)  ·  `page_017993`

## Facts

- Georgia Ports Authority links importers and exporters to global markets via 37 weekly container ship calls.
- Georgia’s ports are a key factor in the decision for automakers, their suppliers, other manufacturers, and logistics operations to bring jobs to the state.
- Griff Lynch is Georgia Ports Authority President and CEO.
- Griff Lynch stated that the authority's mission is to drive the success of the entire state, not just Savannah or Atlanta.
- GPA and the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) announced 350 projects from potential port users in Fiscal Year 2023.
- These announcements will bring more than 33,000 manufacturing and logistics jobs and $21 billion in investment to 96 counties across the state.
- Griff Lynch said GPA, GDEcD, and the Georgia Department of Transportation are working to foster responsible growth that delivers good jobs while preserving quality of life.
- Georgia Ports is investing $29 million in an overpass that will route trucks leaving its Ocean Terminal in Savannah directly onto U.S. 17.
- Georgia’s growing electric vehicle ecosystem has touched every corner of the state, led by announcements associated with the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America located in more than 10 counties across the state.
- Pat Wilson is a Commissioner at the Georgia Department of Economic Development.
- Pat Wilson stated that companies in e-mobility are prioritizing speed-to-market solutions.
- Pat Wilson noted that investing in site development and obtaining “Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development” certification are ways communities can prepare for new opportunities.
- Anovion Technologies is a synthetic graphite manufacturer creating 400 jobs for Southwest Georgia in Decatur County.
- Chip Dunn is Anovion Executive Chairman.
- Chip Dunn described the state’s recruitment effort that swayed Anovion's decision to locate in rural Georgia, including meetings with Gov. Brian Kemp, GPA’s inland port system, and access to waterway and land transport.
- The inland port system includes the Appalachian Regional Port in Crandall, Ga., and the Blue Ridge Connector rail yard now under construction near Gainesville, Ga.
- In the Savannah area alone, the inventory of industrial property totals more than 124 million square feet, with more than 14 million square feet under construction at the close of Calendar Year 2023.
- Stephen Ezelle is a broker for industrial real estate firm Cushman & Wakefield.
- Savannah regional economy continues to experience expansion and strong absorption in the industrial sector despite a slowing economy across most U.S. markets.
- Savannah is expected to eclipse 8 million TEUs of throughput by 2030.
- The operational efficiency offered by the GPA and shift in import cargo from the West Coast and NY/NJ are key drivers in the growth of Savannah’s industrial market.
- Savannah’s industrial sector ended 2023 with an overall vacancy rate of 7 percent.
- Augusta is a short drive from Savannah, and a majority of its manufacturers are port users.
- Cal Wray is president of the Development Authority of Augusta, Ga.
- Augusta-Richmond County provides an ideal location for port users due to available, affordable industrial land, over 350 manufacturers already calling it home, and easy rail and highway connections to the port.
- Aurubis Richmond, a metal smelting operation, is bringing 250 jobs and $600 million in investment to Augusta.
- Denkai America, a copper film producer, is providing 250 jobs and $440 million in investment to Augusta.
- Purecycle Technologies, which recycles plastic into like-new pellets for reuse, is creating 100 jobs and $400 million in investment in Augusta.
- A stand-out industry ecosystem in Augusta is electric vehicle manufacturing, with EZGo Textron and Club Car and their related suppliers located in Richmond and Columbia counties.
- Metals and chemical manufacturing are two other important industries in Augusta.
- Stacy Watson is director of economic and industrial development at GPA.
- Stacy Watson stated that being within 30 miles is great, but companies do not have to be in order to be competitive in drayage, lease rates, and overall cost of doing business.
- Stacy Watson noted that locations 40 or 50 miles away from Savannah are an easy drive, attributed to the port’s location upriver of the Savannah population center and Georgia Department of Transportation efforts making truck routes less congested.
- Direct truck routes from the Port of Savannah to Interstates 95 and 16, as well as on-terminal service from two Class I railroads, expedite cargo moving to and from the marine terminal.
- Available properties in Bryan, Liberty and McIntosh counties – as well as points west – offer interstate and rail links to the port and to thriving inland markets.
- GPA supports the efficient operation of existing industries, local economic development efforts, and serves as a key partner in ongoing recruitment of new industry.
