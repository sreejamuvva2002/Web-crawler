# NVH Korea

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-06

**Source:** [EV parts supplier NVH Korea to create over 160 jobs, invest $72M in Henry County – Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority](https://savannahjda.com/2023/06/ev-parts-supplier-nvh-korea-to-create-over-160-jobs-invest-72m-in-henry-county/)  ·  `page_028966`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that automotive parts manufacturer NVH Korea will establish a new facility in Locust Grove.
- The project will create over 160 new jobs.
- The project will bring $72 million in investment to Henry County.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated that Georgia has a reliable infrastructure network connecting partners globally and a strong workforce that enables job creator success.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp highlighted Georgia's incredible growth in the e-mobility and battery industries, calling it a testament to supporting large suppliers like NVH Korea.
- NVH Korea is an automotive parts manufacturer based in Ulsan, South Korea, established in 1984.
- The company specializes in automotive noise, vibration, and heat-control parts currently produced in Korea, Europe, and India.
- NVH Korea already operates a manufacturing facility in Georgia that produces floor mats, cargo mats, and cargo trays.
- The new Locust Grove facility will supply battery parts for electric vehicles (EVs) in the United States.
- Key customers for the new facility include Hyundai Mobis, Kia Georgia, and Hyundai Motor Group.
- Ja Kyum Koo, Chairman and CEO of NVH Korea, stated that Georgia is the company's first EV battery component manufacturing site globally.
- Ja Kyum Koo noted that cooperation between state agencies and company staff guided the project selection process.
- The new facility will be located at Gardner Logistics Park on Colvin Drive in Locust Grove.
- The facility will produce EV battery system components that protect, connect, and sense the performance of battery cells.
- Operations at the new facility are expected to begin in the second quarter of 2024.
- Mayor Robert Price stated that NVH’s investment will increase Locust Grove's manufacturing jobs base and integrate it into Georgia’s electric vehicle ecosystem.
- Carlotta Harrell, Chair of the Henry County Board of Commissioners, stated the project adds quality manufacturing jobs for citizens and diversifies the county's economy.
- Project Manager Jacob Lee represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The project was supported in partnership with the Henry County Development Authority, Georgia EMC, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson noted that manufacturers are creating future jobs aligned with cutting-edge technologies and industry trends.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson welcomed NVH Korea as an innovator establishing its first U.S. or e-mobility manufacturing presence in Georgia.
- For over a century, Georgia has fostered healthy industry practices and positioned itself as a leader in automotive and mobility technology development.
- From July 1 through December 31, 2022, the automotive industry was the second-largest source of new jobs and investment in Georgia.
- Since 2020, Georgia's pursuit of EV supply chain job creation has resulted in more than $22.7 billion in investments and over 28,400 jobs created.
- The announcement date is June 22, 2023.
