# Hapag-Lloyd

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2021-12

**Source:** [Multinational Liner Shipping Company Hapag-Lloyd Selects Georgia for North American Headquarters - Albany CEO](https://albanyceo.com/news/2021/12/multinational-liner-shipping-company-hapag-lloyd-selects-georgia-north-american-headquarters/)  ·  `page_053755`

## Facts

- German multinational liner shipping company Hapag-Lloyd will invest $18 million in its new North American headquarters.
- The new North American headquarters will be located in Dunwoody, Georgia.
- The project will create an additional 250 jobs in the metro Atlanta region.
- Hapag-Lloyd has been moving people and cargo around the globe for over 150 years.
- Logistics is considered an innate part of Georgia’s economic prowess, with the state’s economy connected to the movement of goods via sea, rail, air, and road.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson expressed excitement about bringing one of the most important companies in the logistics industry to Georgia.
- Hapag-Lloyd is based in Hamburg, Germany, and operates a fleet of over 250 modern container ships with a total transport capacity of 1.8 million TEUs.
- Hapag-Lloyd currently employs more than 500 Georgians across the state.
- Once the North American headquarters project is complete, Hapag-Lloyd will employ more than 750 people in Georgia.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson met with company leadership in Hamburg during a November economic development mission to Germany.
- Uffe Ostergaard, President of Hapag-Lloyd’s North American region, stated the new headquarters will bring the team together under one roof to better serve customers and expand career pathways.
- The company will open its new 125,000-square-foot headquarters at Three Ravinia in Dunwoody by summer 2022.
- Hapag-Lloyd will be hiring for careers in finance, human resources, IT services, and more.
- Dunwoody Mayor Lynn Deutsch welcomed the company to Dunwoody and highlighted the longstanding partnership with the State of Georgia and DeKalb County for growing the Dunwoody Perimeter Market.
- DeKalb County President Dorian DeBarr noted that DeKalb has a skilled workforce, is easily accessible to major highways and transportation hubs, and offers a business-friendly community.
- GDEcD Director of Corporate Solutions and Cybersecurity in Global Commerce Kristi Brigman represented the Department on this project in partnership with GDEcD’s Europe office, Decide DeKalb, the Dunwoody Development Authority, and Georgia Power.
