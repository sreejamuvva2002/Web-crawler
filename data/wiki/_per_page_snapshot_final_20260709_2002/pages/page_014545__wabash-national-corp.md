# Wabash National Corp.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2008-03-27

**Source:** [Labor costs drove Wabash National to Kentucky – Indianapolis Business Journal](https://www.ibj.com/articles/9543-labor-costs-drove-wabash-national-to-kentucky)  ·  `page_014545`

## Facts

- Wabash National Corp. is building a $25 million semi-trailer manufacturing plant in Franklin, Ky., rather than in its headquarters city of Lafayette because a persistently low unemployment rate drove up wages and complicated efforts to hire workers.
- CEO Dick Giromini told analysts that the lower costs in Kentucky would help the company be more competitive with its trailers.
- Senior Vice President and Chief Operating Officer Joe Zachman stated that the Lafayette plant hit a bottleneck during peak times, causing trouble hiring people.
- The new plant will employ 460 workers when it opens in two years.
- Wabash National has 2,230 workers at its Lafayette plant following a recent temporary layoff of 280 employees.
- The company had a 21-percent market share last year.
