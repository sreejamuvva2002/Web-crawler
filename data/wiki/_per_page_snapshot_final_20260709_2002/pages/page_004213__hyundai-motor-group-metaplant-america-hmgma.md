# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA)

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-03-26

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America Celebrates Grand Opening, Powering US Economic Growth | Press Releases & Insights | Worldwide | HTWO](https://org-htwo.hyundai.com/en/worldwide/press-releases/article/Hyundai_Motor_Group_Metaplant_America_Celebrates_Grand_Opening,_Powering_US_Economic_Growth)  ·  `page_004213`

## Facts

- HMGMA hosted its Grand Opening celebration on March 26, 2025.
- The ceremony marks the completion of the largest economic development project in Georgia’s history.
- Groundbreaking occurred two and a half years prior to the grand opening.
- Euisun Chung serves as Executive Chair of Hyundai Motor Group.
- Euisun Chung stated HMGMA represents advanced manufacturing capabilities, commitment to innovation, and investment in relationships with partners and communities in Georgia.
- Euisun Chung noted the community's rich history of craftsmanship and manufacturing, and that the talented workforce at HMGMA is building the future of mobility with America, in America.
- Attendees at the grand opening included Hyundai Motor Group Executive Chair Euisun Chung, Governor Brian P. Kemp, U.S. Representative Buddy Carter, Hyundai Motor Group Vice Chair Jaehoon Chang, Hyundai Motor Company President and CEO José Muñoz, Kia Corporation President and CEO Ho Sung Song, company officials, state and local leaders, HMGMA Meta Pro employees, and other distinguished guests.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp called HMGMA a once-in-a-generation opportunity made possible by commitment to working with job creators for decades.
- Governor Kemp highlighted collaborations between HMGMA and Georgia’s universities and technical colleges, including Georgia Quick Start’s on-site training facility, preparing Georgians for high-quality jobs.
- Governor Kemp noted award-winning infrastructure, such as ports connecting companies to markets around the nation and globe.
- Governor Kemp highlighted the plant's positive impact on the state’s economy, role in advancing innovative manufacturing, and cultivating a skilled labor pool in Georgia.
- HMG entered a landmark agreement with the State of Georgia in May 2022 to establish an electric and hybrid vehicle production and battery manufacturing facility in Ellabell, Georgia.
- The facility is located 20 miles outside of historic Savannah.
- HMGMA was officially formed by June 2022.
- Groundbreaking occurred within four months of the company's formation.
- On October 3, 2024, less than two years after breaking ground, the first vehicle rolled off the assembly line.
- The first vehicle produced was a Hyundai IONIQ 5.
- Hyundai’s IONIQ 9 three-row electric SUV is now in production at HMGMA.
- The first Kia model to be built at the facility will join production in 2026.
- The plant manufactures electric vehicles for Hyundai, Genesis, and Kia.
- The plant is equipped to produce hybrid vehicles.
- HMGMA initially has a capacity to produce 300,000 vehicles annually.
- The Group's investment in Georgia related to HMGMA and joint battery ventures with LG Energy Solution and SK On is $12.6 billion.
- This represents the largest single investment in the state's history.
- The investment includes a total of 8,500 jobs at HMGMA by 2031.
- The Group’s total investments in Georgia are expected to create nearly 40,000 direct and indirect jobs.
- These investments are expected to generate $4.6 billion in individual earnings every year, according to the Center for Automotive Research.
- As of July 2024, more than $2.5 billion in capital investment has been announced by HMGMA suppliers in 12 counties across the state.
- Approximately 6,900 jobs have been announced by HMGMA suppliers as of July 2024.
- This supplier investment more than doubles the originally anticipated $1 billion investment.
- To date, Hyundai Motor Group has invested more than $20.5 billion in the U.S.
- These investments have created or supported over 570,000 jobs nationwide.
- Earlier this week relative to March 26, 2025, the Group announced a significant investment of $21 billion in the U.S. from 2025 to 2028.
- $9 billion will be invested to establish an annual production capacity in the U.S. of 1.2 million vehicles.
- The Group plans to expand HMGMA's annual production capacity to a total of 500,000 units.
- This expansion is an increase of 200,000 units from the original plan.
- Existing U.S. production sites – Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Alabama and Kia Autoland Georgia – will also see improvements in production facilities.
- A total of $6 billion will be allocated to enhance the Group’s parts and logistics business.
- Another $6 billion will fund establishing an Electric Arc Furnace-based integrated steel mill in the U.S. through its affiliate, Hyundai Steel.
- The steel mill will have an annual steel production capacity of 2.7 million tons.
- The mill will supply high-quality automotive steel plates to HMGMA when commercial steel production begins in 2029.
- The Group will invest an additional $6 billion to drive innovation and expand strategic partnerships with U.S. companies.
- Partnerships will focus on autonomous driving, robotics, artificial intelligence (AI), and advanced air mobility (AAM).
- Through the $21 billion commitment, the Group anticipates creating 14,000 new direct full-time jobs in the U.S. by 2028.
- The overall economic impact is expected to generate more than 100,000 direct and indirect job opportunities across related industries.
- HMGMA’s architecture blends advanced technology with design elements reflecting Hyundai’s brand identity and the natural beauty of the Savannah area.
- HMGMA integrated proof-of-concept manufacturing technologies and systems from Hyundai Motor Group Innovation Center Singapore (HMGICS).
- These systems were scaled to accommodate larger production volume at the Georgia facility.
- All HMGMA’s processes – order collection, procurement, logistics and production – are optimized using AI and real-time data.
- Cutting-edge robotics and vision systems ensure high-quality vehicle production.
- These innovative manufacturing systems help create a human-centered work environment with robots assisting human workers, improving efficiency.
- HMGMA expands its mixed-model production capability and integrates various robot processes to enhance automation.
- Automation includes automated guided vehicles for an optimized logistics system.
- Boston Dynamic’s Spot is used for exterior quality inspection in the weld shop.
- Parking robots are used in the assembly shop.
- Automation in HMGMA's assembly shop is nearly double that of the Group’s existing facilities.
- Oscar Kwon serves as CEO of HMGMA.
- Oscar Kwon stated it's a historic moment that their state-of-the-art smart plant is officially open.
- Oscar Kwon emphasized creating a comfortable and welcoming work environment for Meta Pros (HMGMA employees) alongside an energy-efficient and technologically advanced plant.
- Oscar Kwon noted it is essential for Meta Pros to be happy, comfortable and safe for long-lasting careers at HMGMA.
- All work zones, from office to production areas, have been designed to encourage communication and build team collaboration.
- Designs feature open layouts with ample daylight and indoor landscaping.
- Production areas, such as those in the assembly building, have team centers with skylights delivering natural light at the center of the building.
- These team centers can be used for employee breaks and meetings.
- The 3,050-foot-long front of the HMGMA building’s exterior is lit in two parallel lines inspired by a stream.
- This lighting outlines an area where interstate travelers can view vehicles as they travel by conveyor from paint to general assembly.
- A solar parking lot located at the front of the site will generate 5.2 megawatts of energy.
- This accounts for roughly five percent of the Metaplant’s total power consumption.
- The solar panels are installed above 1,878 parking spaces.
- The panels provide shade for vehicles while simultaneously producing electricity.
- A 41-acre ecological park will feature prominently in front of the Metaplant.
- The park includes trails, running tracks, sports fields, and picnic areas.
- The park is designed for Meta Pros to enjoy before or after work.
- Construction of the park is ongoing.
- HMGMA has deployed Hyundai Motor XCIENT heavy-duty hydrogen fuel-cell electric trucks for clean logistics operations.
- A total of 21 XCIENT trucks are currently in operation.
- These trucks represent more than a third of the Glovis America truck fleet at HMGMA.
- The trucks transport vehicle parts from suppliers across the region to the 2,906-acre Megasite where HMGMA is located on a daily basis.
- This initiative represents a significant step forward in HMGMA’s leadership efforts to reduce its carbon footprint and promote sustainable practices in daily logistics operations.
- Hyundai Motor has supported several non-profit organizations impacting education, safety, and sustainability under its global vision of smart mobility solutions and sustainable innovation.
- Since 2022, Hyundai and Genesis, along with various social responsibility initiatives and nonprofit organizations, have donated nearly $4.5 million to local organizations in Georgia.
- Kia's Autoland Georgia factory in West Point has provided over $22 million in support of Georgia communities since 2009.
- This includes more than $6 million to support educational programs such as the THINC College & Career Academy, and SAE’s “A World in Motion.”
