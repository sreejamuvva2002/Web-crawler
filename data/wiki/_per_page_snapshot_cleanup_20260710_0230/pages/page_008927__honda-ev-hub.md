# Honda EV Hub

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-02-03

**Source:** [Honda EV Hub Prepares for New Level of Flexible Production in Reimagined Manufacturing Environment](https://www.theautochannel.com/news/2025/02/03/1488154-honda-ev-hub-prepares-for-new-level-flexible-production-reimagined.html)  ·  `page_008927`

## Facts

- Honda has provided a first look at its innovative approach to manufacture electric vehicles (EV) by late 2025 at the Honda EV Hub in Ohio.
- The Honda EV Hub uniquely positions Honda to build internal combustion engine (ICE), hybrid-electric, and battery electric vehicles (EVs) on the same production lines.
- An investment reaching over $1 billion is being used to retool the Marysville Auto Plant (MAP), East Liberty Auto Plant (ELP), and Anna Engine Plant (AEP).
- The all-new Acura RSX SUV will be the first battery electric model produced at the Honda EV Hub in late 2025.
- Production models based on the Honda 0 Saloon and Honda 0 SUV prototypes introduced at CES 2025 will follow the Acura RSX EV.
- The retooled production facilities provide an enhanced work environment for associates and more sustainable production processes and equipment.
- Bob Schwyn, senior vice president of Honda Development & Manufacturing of America, LLC, stated that the Honda EV Hub provides flexibility to quickly respond to shifting customer needs and market conditions.
- The Honda EV Hub will begin EV production in late 2025 with the all-new Acura RSX EV.
- Honda debuted its original vehicle operating system (OS), the ASIMO OS, for use in Honda 0 Series models at CES 2025 in Las Vegas on January 7.
- The use of the ASIMO name pays homage to the Honda ASIMO robot and the "Wise" value of the Honda 0 Series.
- Honda announced plans to establish the Honda EV Hub in Ohio in October 2022, based on a vision to make zero emissions vehicles represent 100% of its vehicle sales by 2040.
- Honda and LG Energy Solution committed to invest $3.5 billion in a new joint venture battery facility named L-H Battery Company.
- The overall investment for the L-H Battery Company JV is projected to reach $4.4 billion.
- The L-H Battery Company facility will have an annual capacity of approximately 40GWh, with production scheduled to start in late 2025.
- The Honda EV Hub serves as the starting point for the global production of the new Honda 0 Series EVs.
- The Honda EV Hub is developing knowledge and expertise for EV technology and production to be shared with other Honda plants in North America and globally.
- Honda engineers implemented energy-saving and environmentally responsible initiatives aligned with the company's Triple Action to Zero approach, aimed at achieving zero environmental impact by 2050.
- The Triple Action to Zero strategy focuses on three areas: carbon neutrality, clean energy, and resource circulation.
- Honda is targeting net-zero CO2 emissions from its products and operations by 2050.
- High efficiency melt furnaces installed at the Anna Engine Plant reduce natural gas consumption and can be modified in the future to Plasma Arc Melting to further reduce CO2 emissions.
- New air HVAC handlers, more efficient lighting, and energy-saving equipment were introduced at all three plants to reduce the carbon footprint.
- Honda's goal is to use 100% sustainable and/or carbon-free energy sources, already offsetting more than 60% of electricity used in North American manufacturing operations through long-term virtual power purchase agreements for renewable wind and solar power.
- Honda aims to utilize 100% sustainable materials by reprocessing products back to raw materials and reusing them in new products.
- Honda recycles scrap material at all plants, including aluminum at the Anna Engine Plant that is melted onsite and reused to make battery cases and engines.
- Honda is transforming associate uniforms into sound-absorbing insulation for use in Honda and Acura automobiles.
- Honda has produced automobiles in Ohio for over 40 years, beginning with automobile production at the Marysville Auto Plant in November 1982.
- Honda established U.S. manufacturing operations in Marysville in 1979 with the start of motorcycle production.
- Over 12,000 associates currently support Honda manufacturing in Ohio across five plants.
- The five Ohio plants represent a total capital investment of $13 billion and have the capacity to produce 460,000 Honda and Acura automobiles, 1.18 million automobile engines, and over 1 million automobile transmissions and two-motor hybrid systems.
- The company's five Ohio manufacturing facilities include the Marysville Auto Plant, East Liberty Auto Plant, Performance Manufacturing Center, Anna Engine Plant, and a transmission plant in Russells Point.
- Honda has invested more than $1.4 billion in these Ohio facilities over the past six years to modernize equipment and adopt new technologies.
- The Marysville Auto Plant consolidated its two production lines to create capability for producing EVs on the same line with ICE and hybrid vehicles.
- MAP is the first Honda plant globally to assemble EV battery packs.
- Retooling at MAP involved installing new weld robots and technology to enable welding EV bodies on the same line with ICE models, including changes to secure the EV battery case in the vehicle floor.
- MAP is adding CDC welding for the first time at Honda globally to expand the use of lightweight and high-strength materials.
- Engineers renovated an area at MAP to create a workspace for assembling battery packs that combine modules from L-H Battery Company with battery case megacasts from the Anna Engine Plant.
- The EV battery pack assembly line at MAP will be the longest automated guided vehicle (AGV) line at Honda.
- A new dedicated area on the MAP production line was established for installing battery packs into EVs, allowing ICE and hybrid vehicles to pass through without stopping.
- A new hybrid-electric battery assembly area was established closer to the installation area and expanded to meet record customer demand for hybrid-electric models.
- The Vehicle Quality Department at MAP evolved with new optical aiming station improvements to check alignment and functioning of safety and connectivity technologies in EVs and future software-defined vehicles.
- The updated VQD accommodates radar and optical aiming verification required for L3 automated driving features on Honda 0 Series vehicles.
- MAP associates have begun new training programs to prepare for skills required for EV production, EV battery pack assembly, and quality checks for software-defined vehicles.
- The Anna Engine Plant is adding megacasting of EV battery cases, which will serve as the main frame structure for the floor of Honda and Acura EVs.
- AEP installed six 6,000-ton high-pressure die cast machines to megacast EV battery case parts in a mass production first for global Honda.
- The battery cases are made in two halves welded together later, enabling production of two different sizes for midsize and large EVs with one-half common to each size.
- Installation of the die-cast machines required modifications including placement of pilons 80 feet below the concrete surface to support the 31-feet tall machines.
- AEP uses high-efficiency natural gas melt furnaces that reduced compressed air usage compared to current furnaces and can be modified to Plasma Arc Melting in the future.
- AEP recycles 100% of scrap aluminum from the die-cast process to be reused in-house for case and engine production.
- AEP is using Friction Stir Welding (FSW) as a mass production first globally to join the two halves of the aluminum battery case and attach an aluminum water jacket stamped at MAP.
- The aluminum water jacket maintains optimal battery module temperature to maximize battery efficiency.
- AEP will employ Photogrammetry using four machines with 90 cameras to visually analyze part shape post-die-cast, and two machines with 120 cameras prior to shipping to ensure mountability.
- The photogrammetry quality checks adopt a human-friendly approach that does not require associates to lift or manually check heavy battery cases.
- AEP adopted Minimum Quantity Lubrication (MQL) machining for the aluminum battery case, reducing energy use and lubricant requirements.
- ELP built a new paint department and strengthened carriers for heavier EV bodies, including a phosphate and E-coat expansion nearing completion to achieve higher paint quality.
- ELP constructed a new Vehicle Quality Department building north of the plant with a new optical aiming station to check alignment and functioning of safety and connectivity technologies.
- Assembly department infrastructure at ELP, including drop lifts, conveyors, and support beams, is being strengthened to handle heavier EV models and offer flexibility for hybrid-electric and ICE models.
- A new approach with AGVs on the ELP final assembly line provides a stronger, larger body carrier for EVs.
- The Battery Pack Mount Mezzanine at ELP enables EV models to disengage from the main assembly line for battery pack installation before rejoining the existing line.
- Weld changepoints at ELP include reinforcement and modifications to existing drop lifts, transfers, and conveyor systems to accommodate EVs.
