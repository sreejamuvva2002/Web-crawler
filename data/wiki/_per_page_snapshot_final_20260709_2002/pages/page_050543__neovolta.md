# NeoVolta

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-04-30

**Source:** [NeoVolta Funding for Georgia Battery Plant: $23M Raised, Production Starts Mid-2026 - News and Statistics - IndexBox](https://www.indexbox.io/blog/neovolta-secures-23m-funding-for-georgia-battery-plant-launch-in-2026/)  ·  `page_050543`

## Facts

- NeoVolta has raised about 23 million dollars in gross proceeds through financing deals in December 2025 and January 2026.
- The capital is intended to support working capital and fund the company's commitments to its joint venture, NeoVolta Power.
- The joint venture involves NeoVolta, system integrator PotisEdge, and solar company Longi.
- Longi increased its stake in PotisEdge to approximately 62% in November 2025, gaining full control and marking its entry into the energy storage sector.
- Both companies described this as a partnership, with Longi launching an Energy Storage One-Stop Solution that includes solar, hydrogen, and storage, initially for markets like the UK, Germany, Italy, and Spain.
- Also in November 2025, NeoVolta secured a 13 million dollar private placement led by Infinite Grid Capital, partly to fund the battery energy storage system manufacturing facility in Georgia.
- The company has finalized its initial 7 million dollar investment in the joint venture, which is the first of three planned funding milestones.
- NeoVolta stated this funding reduces its immediate capital risks and provides sufficient working capital to support its core residential and commercial energy storage business expansion.
- The total planned capital contribution to the joint venture occurs in three phases: an initial 7 million dollars completed in January 2026, an 8 million dollar second milestone by April 30, 2026, and a 10 million dollar amount at the plant's commissioning.
- The operating agreement also allows for up to 15 million dollars in extra contributions until June 30, 2027, if needed for capacity, growth, or working capital.
- The Georgia plant is designed for an initial annual production capacity of 2 gigawatt-hours, with potential expansion to 8 gigawatt-hours.
- Mass production is expected to begin by mid-2026.
- NeoVolta intends to manufacture utility-scale and commercial and industrial energy storage products there, broadening its focus from residential and smaller commercial segments.
- The company noted the facility is structured to qualify for Section 45X Advanced Manufacturing Production Tax Credits and meets domestic content requirements.
- It highlighted the plant's revenue potential, illustrating that 2 gigawatt-hours of annual production could represent approximately 400 million dollars of annual revenue at an illustrative price of 200 dollars per kilowatt-hour at full utilization, clarifying this example is not a forecast or guarantee of future results.
