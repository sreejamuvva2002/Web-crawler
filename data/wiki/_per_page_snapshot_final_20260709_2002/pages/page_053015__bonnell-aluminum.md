# Bonnell Aluminum

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2016-06-29

**Source:** [Bonnell Aluminum shuts production at Georgia plant after blast](https://www.yahoo.com/news/five-people-injured-blast-bonnell-aluminum-plant-georgia-161556107--finance.html)  ·  `page_053015`

## Facts

- Bonnell Aluminum temporarily shut down operations at its Newnan, Georgia plant following an explosion on Wednesday morning that injured five employees.
- The company stated it was too early to speculate on the cause of the accident but is working closely with local fire officials in an investigation.
- The explosion occurred in the casting area of the plant.
- Bonnell produces aluminum extrusions for the building and construction markets, the automotive market, and specialty applications like refrigerators and air conditioners.
- A prolonged outage at the plant could reduce the amount of primary and scrap aluminum consumed by the company, potentially contributing further to a global glut that has sent prices tumbling about 30 percent from their 2014 peak.
- Three-month aluminum on the London Metal Exchange was up 0.7 percent at $1,635.50 per tonne as of 12:36 p.m. EDT (1636 GMT).
- One injured employee was airlifted to a hospital in Atlanta, two were transported to a local hospital, and two were treated at the scene.
- The explosion occurred at approximately 9:15 a.m. EDT (1315 GMT).
- A spokeswoman for the Newnan police stated it was not a situation involving hazardous materials.
- Bonnell Aluminum is a subsidiary of Tredegar Corp.
