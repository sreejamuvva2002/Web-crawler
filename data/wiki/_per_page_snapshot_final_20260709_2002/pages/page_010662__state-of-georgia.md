# State of Georgia

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2023-09-22

**Source:** [Georgia Honored for Korean Investment Wave Credited With Bolstering Bilateral Ties - Global Atlanta](https://www.globalatlanta.com/georgia-honored-for-korean-investment-wave-credited-with-bolstering-bilateral-ties/)  ·  `page_010662`

## Facts

- Gov. Brian Kemp accepted the Korea Society’s top bilateral award on behalf of Georgia.
- U.S.-based executives from Kia and Hyundai highlighted an explosion of Korean investment in Georgia, totaling some $23.6 billion over a decade, recently driven by auto makers responding to the industry’s electric revolution.
- Stuart Countess serves as president of Kia Georgia, which operates a manufacturing plant in West Point that triggered an influx of Korean auto suppliers more than 15 years ago.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) was selected for the Gen. James A. Van Fleet Award for shepherding an ever-growing parade of Korean investors.
- The award was also presented to the Korea International Trade Association (KITA) and accepted by its chairman, Christopher Koo.
- The event took place at The Plaza Hotel in New York City on Sept. 20.
- Gov. Kemp has supported the Kia plant since early in his first term, blessing the launches of the Telluride and Sportage models, with the latter becoming the brand’s top-selling vehicle in the U.S.
- Kia is investing $200 million to upgrade its West Point assembly line for the EV9, which will be Kia’s first U.S.-made electric model.
- The state’s Electric Mobility Innovation Alliance initiative, championed by Gov. Kemp, brings government, business, and academia together to make Georgia the e-mobility capital of the U.S.
- SK On’s catalytic investment in Commerce in 2018 helped spur the Electric Mobility Innovation Alliance initiative.
- Hyundai Motor America President and Group COO Jose Muñoz discussed how Georgia rolled out the red carpet for its Metaplant project.
- The Hyundai Metaplant, including battery investment, will bring $7.59 billion and more than 8,500 jobs to Ellabell.
- The Metaplant is Hyundai’s first dedicated electric-vehicle plant in the U.S.
- Earlier in the week, a partnership was inaugurated between Hyundai and Georgia Tech to work on hydrogen fuel commercial vehicles and skill up engineers in the state.
- North America now accounts for a quarter of Hyundai Motor’s global sales.
- Gov. Kemp highlighted that Georgia hosts the third-largest Korean population in the United States, contributing work ethic and commitment to free enterprise.
- A Korean consulate general was established in Atlanta in 1976, and Georgia opened a trade and investment office in Seoul in 1985.
- SK Group established the first major Korean factory in Georgia in 1996, producing photo films in Covington.
- SK recently inaugurated a new chip substrate plant under the Absolics brand at the former Covington property.
- Korea was the first country Gov. Kemp visited on a trade mission as governor, shortly after SK Battery signaled its potential as an investment source.
- Multi-billion dollar Korean investors mentioned include Hanwha Q Cells and LG.
- LG is partnering with Hyundai in a 50-50 joint venture on the Metaplant battery operation.
- Korea has been the top investor in Georgia by dollar amount and job creation for three consecutive years.
- A total of 144 Korean facilities have established operations in Georgia.
- The Van Fleet Award typically honors individuals, but this year focused on organizations driving trade and investment as trade is increasingly framed as a national security issue.
- Thomas Byrne, president and CEO of The Korea Society, stated the awardees are enhancing trade and investment, creating high-paying jobs, and securing supply chains in critical technologies.
- Gov. Kemp noted that Korean companies view Georgia as a “good bet” amid global economic challenges, citing quality workers, low cost of doing business, high quality of life, and speed-to-market solutions.
- Georgia’s growing ports have direct links with Korea, and Delta and Korean Air fly nonstop to Seoul.
- The first new nuclear reactor installed in the U.S. in 30 years came online in March at Plant Vogtle, providing reliable clean energy for companies seeking to lower carbon emissions.
- Gov. Kemp publicly acknowledged Yoonie Kim, director of Korean investment for GDEcD, for crafting the strategy to welcome and settle new Korean firms in the state.
- A major Georgia contingent attended the event, including GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson, Chief Marketing Officer Mark Jaronski, Yoonie Kim, Georgia Power CEO Kim Greene, Delta Air Lines Executive Vice President Peter Carter, Savannah Economic Development Authority President and CEO Trip Tollison, Savannah Area Chamber President and CEO Bert Brantley, and former U.S. Sen. Saxby Chambliss with his wife Julianne.
- New Korean Consul General Suh Sangpyo was unable to attend but had recently met with the governor.
- Korean companies with a presence in Georgia scheduled to attend included Kumho Tire, LG, and Superior Essex.
- Former U.S. Ambassador to Korea Kathleen Stephens introduced KITA chairman Christopher Koo, noting that KITA now has 73,000 member companies.
