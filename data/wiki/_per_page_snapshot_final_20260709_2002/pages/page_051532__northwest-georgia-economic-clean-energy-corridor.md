# Northwest Georgia Economic & Clean Energy Corridor

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2022-03-30

**Source:** [Northwest: A Greener Path - Georgia Trend Magazine](https://www.georgiatrend.com/2022/03/30/northwest-a-greener-path/)  ·  `page_051532`

## Facts

- TEKLAS, a Turkish supplier of electric vehicle parts, invested $6.5 million in its first North American facility and headquarters.
- The TEKLAS facility will be housed in a 200,000-square-foot building in Calhoun, Gordon County.
- The TEKLAS facility will create more than 120 jobs.
- TEKLAS produces rubber hoses and plastic tubes for automotive fluid systems for companies including General Motors, Volkswagen, and Mercedes-Benz.
- GEDIA Automotive Group is building an $85 million advanced manufacturing facility in Dalton to make parts for electric vehicles.
- The GEDIA facility will eventually add 200 jobs when operating at full capacity.
- GEDIA is expected to start shipping products to Mercedes-Benz for a new electric crossover vehicle later in the year (relative to March 2022).
- Carl Campbell, executive director of the Dalton-Whitfield County Joint Development Authority, notes significant activity and high demand from component makers and battery manufacturers in the region.
- GE Appliances announced plans to invest $118 million to expand manufacturing operations at its subsidiary Roper Corp. in LaFayette, Walker County.
- The GE Appliances/Roper expansion will result in the creation of more than 600 new jobs.
- The investment will improve material flow and increase assembly-line efficiency by adding cutting-edge advanced manufacturing technology.
- Trenton, a small industrial city at the far northwest corner of the state, saw about $15 million in expansions over the last 18 months, with anticipated wage increases of about 60% over four years.
- The Development Authority of Polk County received Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) certification for phase three of the Cedartown North Business Park to enable fast-track construction.
- Oldcastle APG purchased a 100,000-square-foot building and 17.35 adjacent acres in the Cedartown North Business Park last year.
- Oldcastle APG will initially create 20 jobs in Polk County, growing to 45 at full capacity.
- Murray County’s Appalachian Regional Port serves as a rail-connected pathway to and from the Port of Savannah, reducing truck traffic and bypassing congested seaports and Metro Atlanta.
- Companies including GE Appliances, Huali Floors, and United Surface Solutions have invested millions in facilities in Murray County due to the port's location.
- In calendar year 2020, the Appalachian Regional Port handled 32,816 containers, an increase of nearly 14,000 boxes (73%) compared to 2019.
- Logistics and distribution facilities are growing rapidly along the I-75 corridor in Bartow County due to its location north of Metro Atlanta.
- Amazon developed a Last Mile Delivery Station on Acworth’s Third Army Road in southern Bartow County.
- Rome and Floyd County saw more than $810 million in capital investments adding 581 jobs in the last three years.
- Ball Corp.’s aluminum cup plant opened in Rome last year, producing up to 700,000 recyclable beverage cups a day with 115 employees, and is the sole Ball cup plant in the U.S.
- A second production line at the Ball Corp. facility is set to be added in the spring of 2022.
- Pirelli’s Rome plant is producing the world’s first FSC-certified tire using sustainably produced rubber verified by the Forest Stewardship Council.
