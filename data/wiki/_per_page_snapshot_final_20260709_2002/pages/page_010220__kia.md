# Kia

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-04-29

**Source:** [Kia commits $1M to UGA’s initiative on e-mobility](https://www.classiccitynews.com/post/kia-commits-1m-to-uga-s-initiative-on-e-mobility)  ·  `page_010220`

## Facts

- Kia provided a $1 million gift to the UGA College of Engineering and the Georgia Network for Electric Mobility (GNEM).
- UGA President Jere W. Morehead stated that the university is thrilled to partner with Kia on its electric mobility initiative.
- Morehead expressed gratitude for Kia's support of UGA's efforts to advance electric mobility technology, help students become leaders in the field, and create opportunities to strengthen Georgia's economy.
- The $1 million commitment will provide endowed support for undergraduate scholarships, graduate research fellowships, community engagement programs, and experiential learning opportunities related to e-mobility.
- These experiential learning opportunities include co-ops, internships, and capstone projects specifically for students in the UGA College of Engineering.
- Kia's gift also supports the Georgia Network for Electric Mobility (GNEM), which was established by the University of Georgia in early 2023.
- GNEM focuses on enhancing research, education, and outreach opportunities in e-mobility across the state.
- GNEM brings together industry, academia, government, and communities to aid workforce development, economic development, and research.
- GNEM takes special care to involve rural and underserved communities and small businesses in its initiatives.
- Kia North America President and CEO Sean Yoon stated that Kia is committed to future e-mobility initiatives that promote sustainable transportation solutions in Georgia and across the nation.
- Yoon noted that Kia is proud to work with the UGA College of Engineering and the GNEM program to support students and other GNEM projects.
- Kia established a state-of-the-art production facility in West Point, Georgia, in 2009.
- Since opening its West Point plant, Kia Georgia has helped create more than 14,000 jobs.
- The West Point facility has produced more than 4.2 million vehicles to date.
- Kia's all-electric vehicle, the EV9, will soon be produced at the West Point facility.
- Kia Georgia President and CEO Stuart Countess stated that programs like GNEM are timely and critical as Kia Georgia moves into a new era of electric vehicles and future mobility solutions.
- Countess expressed confidence in UGA's forward-thinking vision and noted Kia is honored to be recognized as a founding partner of GNEM.
- As a founding partner of GNEM, Kia is providing perpetual support for educational pursuits, research productivity, and experiential learning efforts for students interested in electric mobility careers.
- This perpetual support expands GNEM's impact on rural and underserved communities, aligning with UGA's land-grant mission to serve all populations within Georgia.
- UGA College of Engineering Dean Donald Leo called Kia's support a testament to the power of collaboration between industry and academia.
- Leo offered sincere thanks to Kia for its commitment to electric mobility in Georgia.
- Kia served as a premier sponsor of UGA's third annual Electric Mobility Summit.
- The summit took place April 23-4.
- The summit focused on the adoption of electric mobility in Georgia, educational programs to support industry growth demands, and the future of electric mobility in Georgia and beyond.
