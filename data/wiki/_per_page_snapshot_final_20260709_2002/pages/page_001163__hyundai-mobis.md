# Hyundai Mobis

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-10-27

**Source:** [Supplier Hyundai Mobis will build a $205M EV battery plant in Montgomery](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/supplier-hyundai-mobis-build-205m-162115858.html)  ·  `page_001163`

## Facts

- Hyundai Mobis is one of the world's largest auto suppliers.
- Hyundai Mobis is building a new $205 million EV battery plant in Montgomery, Alabama.
- The new plant is intended to feed the electric vehicle production process.
- Hyundai Mobis has long been the largest tier 1 supplier to Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Alabama and Kia Georgia.
- Hyundai Mobis currently supplies full vehicle modules and assemblies from its existing 83-acre site in Montgomery.
- The new facility will be a second site measuring 450,000 square feet located near HMMA.
- The new facility will serve as a vehicle battery module plant for Hyundai and Kia.
- Company officials expect the new plant to employ 400 people with "competitive" salaries.
- H.S. Oh, vice president of the electric powertrain business for Hyundai Mobis, stated that strong production capacity is key as the EV market continues to grow and allows Hyundai Mobis to see continued growth in the market.
- H.S. Oh said the new Montgomery facility will be an important step in that process and expressed excitement for the project to be under way.
- Construction at a site just off Interstate 65 in Montgomery is expected to start as early as December.
- EV battery assembly production is expected to start in 2024.
- HMMA will provide the land for the new Hyundai Mobis plant.
- Hyundai announced earlier this year it planned to pour $300 million into its Montgomery plant to start EV production there.
- The $300 million investment includes production of an all-electric Genesis GV70 and a hybrid electric Santa Fe.
- HMMA President and CEO Ernie Kim said the new Hyundai Mobis plant would help Hyundai accelerate its transition to electric vehicles.
- Montgomery Mayor Steven Reed thanked county, state, and HMMA leaders for helping secure the new plant.
- Mayor Reed said HMMA President Kim "didn't want to lose (the Hyundai Mobis project) to Georgia" and sounded like an Alabama fan.
- Mayor Reed stated, "Well, we didn't lose to Georgia."
- Alabama Gov. Kay Ivey said the electric battery plant announcement "speaks volumes as to what we as a city and as a state are all about."
- Gov. Ivey told company leaders that the expansion will help production at Hyundai and provide some 400 more Alabama families a good living, guaranteeing those workers will not let them down.
- Hyundai's Alabama supply chain has been under scrutiny in recent months.
- Hyundai Global President and COO Jose Munoz told Reuters the company plans to "sever relations" with two Alabama suppliers accused of employing underage workers: SL Alabama and SMART Alabama LLC.
- Jose Munoz also told Reuters he ordered a more comprehensive investigation into potential labor violations across Hyundai's supply chain.
- Hyundai spokesman Ira Gabriel told the Montgomery Advertiser that the investigation is ongoing.
