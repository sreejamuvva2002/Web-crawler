## Facts

- voestalpine Automotive Components showcases the phs-door ring, a groundbreaking structural component combining advanced production methods, diverse materials, and engineering expertise.
- The phs-door ring is a large, complex component measuring nearly two meters in length and over one meter in height.
- It brings together multiple elements—such as the sill, A-pillar, B-pillar, and roof frame—into a single, fully functional module.
- The design uses tailor-welded blanks with varying sheet thicknesses and localized reinforcements precisely adapted to different load zones.
- voestalpine now offers a phs-scalefree coating variant for its press-hardened steels.
- Customers can choose from a full range of coatings—galvanized, AlSi-coated, and scalefree—allowing maximum flexibility to balance corrosion protection, formability, and thermal resistance depending on specific design and performance requirements.
- The phs-door ring's optimized material distribution significantly reduces overall vehicle weight.
- Weight reduction helps automakers improve fuel efficiency and vehicle performance without compromising safety.
- This lightweight design contributes to sustainability goals by minimizing material usage and lowering the product’s overall CO₂ footprint.
- The component enhances crash safety through the strategic use of materials with varying strengths and ductility.
- Each section of the door ring is tailored to the specific loads it must withstand, ensuring that energy is absorbed efficiently during a collision.
- Integrating multiple parts into a single functional unit reduces the number of joining steps and simplifies logistics throughout the assembly process.
- The A-pillar area utilizes 1000 MPa steel with high ductility for improved energy absorption.
- A 2000 MPa patch plate in the roof frame enhances rigidity where it’s needed most.
- Press-hardened door rings are gaining significant traction globally—especially in China and the United States—and are now entering European series production.
- voestalpine's Metal Forming Division serves as its center of excellence for high-precision sections, tubes, and strip steel products, as well as for ready-to-install system components made from pressed, stamped, and roll-formed parts.
- Within the voestalpine Automotive Components Group, close collaboration across all sites ensures that each location contributes its specialized skills and experience.
