# Continental AG

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Continental's Tire Sales Slipped in 2023 | Modern Tire Dealer](https://www.moderntiredealer.com/suppliers/article/33038365/continentals-tire-sales-slipped-in-2023)  ·  `page_018853`

## Facts

- In 2023, Continental AG’s overall global sales grew 5.1% and its net income grew by more than 1,600%.
- Tire sales for 2023 totaled $15.40 billion, down 0.3% from $15.45 billion in 2022.
- Continental’s tires sector accounted for about 33% of the company’s overall sales and is its second largest of four sectors.
- The company recorded global sales of $45.7 billion in 2023, up from $43.4 billion in 2022.
- Continental’s net income was $1.2 billion in 2023, up from $73.5 million the prior year, representing a 1,635% increase.
- The company noted its tires business delivered stable profitability despite weak tire replacement markets in Europe and North America.
- CEO Nikolai Setzer stated that 2023 was a challenging year due to geopolitical conflicts, war, weak tire replacement markets, and inflation impacting economic development.
- Setzer noted that four out of five new cars are equipped with hardware and software solutions from Continental.
- Setzer stated that the world’s 10 highest-volume manufacturers of electric vehicles all trust in Continental tires for their original equipment.
- Continental is referring to 2024 as “the beginning of our era of execution,” focusing on electrification, digital services, and ultra-high-performance tire growth.
- The company plans continued investment to strengthen its setup, particularly in APAC (Asia Pacific) and the Americas.
- Continental expects the passenger and light truck replacement tire market in North America to be flat to up 3% in 2024.
- Continental expects the commercial tire market in North America to return to positive in 2024, with replacement market gains of 2% to 4%.
- Continental estimates a slip in commercial vehicle production in North America between 4% and 6% for 2024.
- Continental’s sales outlook for 2024 includes global sales of between $45.2 billion and $48.5 billion.
- The company expects its tire sales to range between $15.4 billion and $16.5 billion in 2024.
- In Q4 2023, Continental’s global tire volumes were up 1.7%, supported by short-term demand in the replacement market.
- In Q4 2023, Continental recorded a negative effect from price/mix, down 1.1%, driven by low demand in truck tire replacement.
- For Q4 2023, Continental’s tire sales totaled $3.9 billion, down 2.5% over the final quarter of 2022.
