# Stryten Energy

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Stryten Energy cuts ribbon on 20 kW/120 kWh demonstration flow battery in Georgia - Best Magazine](https://www.bestmag.co.uk/stryten-energy-cuts-ribbon-on-20-kw-120-kwh-demonstration-flow-battery-in-georgia/)  ·  `page_050535`

## Facts

- Stryten Energy cut the ribbon on a 20 kW/120 kWh demonstration vanadium redox flow battery (VRFB) at Snapping Shoals EMC, a utility provider in Georgia, US.
- The system is the first VRFB system made and installed in the state of Georgia.
- The electrolyte for the system was manufactured in Maryland.
- Stryten Energy is evaluating other suppliers located in Utah and Nevada.
- Stryten Energy designed and sized the smaller system to fit the needs of Snapping Shoals EMC so that testing can last more than six hours.
- The system allows simulation of almost any operating schedule to gauge battery compatibility as defined by DOE national labs for applicability with renewable energy sources and other FTM/BTM applications.
- Testing is expected to take six months.
- Testing covers applications including energy cost control, peak shaving, avoiding curtailment, and renewable integration.
- Future batteries of this scale will be mostly behind the meter.
- Department of Energy data cited by Stryten shows Georgia has some 3.6 GW of solar, wind, and storage capacity.
- Almost 1.1 GW of additional planned clean energy capacity is in the works in Georgia, which will power more than 155,000 homes.
- US Representative Mike Collins attended the ribbon-cutting event.
- Stryten Energy CEO Mike Judd attended the ribbon-cutting event.
- Snapping Shoals EMC CEO Shaun Mock attended the ribbon-cutting event.
- Commissioner Tim Echols attended the ribbon-cutting event.
