# L.K. Technology Holdings Limited

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [L.K. Technology Announces 2025/2026 Annual Results — UzDaily.uz](https://www.uzdaily.uz/en/lk-technology-announces-20252026-annual-results/)  ·  `page_008878`

## Facts

- During the Year, revenue amounted to HK$5,609 million; gross profit amounted to HK$1,375 million with a gross margin of 24.5%; net profit was adjusted to HK$49.5 million with a net profit margin of 0.9%.
- Total assets reached HK$13.3 billion (a year-on-year increase of 11.2%), with net cash of HK$1.32 billion.
- The Group operates three core business segments: die-casting, injection molding, and CNC machining centre.
- Die-casting machinery revenue was HK$3,663 million (65.3% of total), while injection molding generated HK$1,798 million (32.1%) and CNC machining centre generated HK$148 million (2.6%).
- R&D expenses reached HK$318 million during the Year, representing a year-on-year increase of 24.6%.
- The Group commercialised the world's first 16,000T ultra-large intelligent die-casting unit and continues to secure and deliver multiple 9000T-class ultra-large die casting machines.
- Its 6,000T–16,000T ultra-large intelligent die-casting cells integrate digital twin technology with the LK-NET cloud management system and were selected as one of the 'Outstanding Cases of New Quality Productive Forces in the Automotive Industry 2025'.
- These solutions are fully compatible with mass production requirements for full vehicle body structural components.
- As at the end of the Year, the Group ranked first globally in cumulative number of ultra-large die-casting machines delivered.
- The Group's TPI semi-solid magnesium alloy forming technology supports modular transformation of traditional equipment, enabling a 50% reduction in energy consumption and a 20% increase in product toughness.
- A self-developed 5,000-tonne TPI semi-solid forming module secured an order from a leading OEM for inner tailgate panels for new energy vehicles, integrating functions previously requiring nine parts to achieve a 45% weight reduction and 70% material utilisation.
- Orders for TPI magnesium alloy equipment exceeded RMB100 million in shipment value, with related business revenue increasing by 291.1% in the second half of the financial year compared to the first half.
- Self-developed zirconium-based amorphous alloy die casting equipment integrates vacuum melting and full-process automation for producing foldable screen hinges and medical implants.
- Low-pressure die-casting machines feature inert gas pressurisation and waste heat recovery technologies, reducing energy consumption by 30% and are widely used in automotive wheels and motor end covers.
- Horizontal extrusion casting machines with dual pressure and speed control systems are used for high-performance components like automotive steering knuckles, subframes, and battery end plates.
- The Group developed an integrated intelligent machining system for post-processing large die-cast components with over 90% local data processing rate and over 95% accuracy in predicting machining abnormalities.
- Digital twin technology was leveraged to develop intelligent die-casting cells capable of process parameter prediction, remote diagnostics, and digitalised management.
- The Group proactively laid out new productivity tracks including energy storage, AI computing power, humanoid robots, and specialised casting to diversify its customer structure.
- Revenue from Europe surged by 64.4% year-on-year, and the Group secured a high-end equipment order from a well-known European vehicle enterprise.
- Revenue from other emerging markets and the Asia-Pacific region increased by 76.9% year-on-year, with a localised sales and service network covering Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia.
- Revenue from the North American market declined year-on-year.
- CEO Liu Zhuo Ming stated the Group will push into energy storage, photovoltaics, robotics, and computing power cooling while building a smart service system centred on 'AI + Equipment'.
- L.K. Technology Holdings Limited is one of the world's largest die-casting machine manufacturers, operating 15 manufacturing bases and 60 sales and service centers worldwide.
- Products are widely used in automotive bodies and accessories, household appliances, and electronic products.
- The Group owns Idra, which developed the world's first Giga Press ultra-large die-casting equipment adopted by leading global new energy vehicle manufacturers.
- Sales and service companies were established in the United States and India, and a casting factory operates in Fuxin, China for cast iron/steel components.
