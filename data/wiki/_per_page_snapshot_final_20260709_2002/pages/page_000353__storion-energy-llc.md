# Storion Energy, LLC

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2025-02-04

**Source:** [Storion Energy Launched to Establish a Domestic Supply of Flow Battery Components for Long-Duration Energy Storage](https://www.stryten.com/storion-energy-launched/)  ·  `page_000353`

## Facts

- Stryten Energy LLC and Largo Clean Energy Corp. formed the Storion Energy, LLC joint venture on February 4, 2025.
- The joint venture will deliver price-competitive vanadium electrolyte via a unique leasing model to drive rapid commercialization and adoption of vanadium redox flow batteries.
- Stryten Critical E-Storage LLC signed agreements with Largo Clean Energy Corp., a subsidiary of Largo Inc., to establish the joint venture.
- Storion Energy’s mission is to remove barriers to entry for battery manufacturers by enabling domestic sourcing of price-competitive components for flow batteries, including vanadium electrolyte.
- The company combines access to high-quality vanadium supply from the only operating Western Hemisphere vanadium mine with domestic electrolyte production.
- This establishes a fully integrated vertical supply chain for utility-scale vanadium redox flow batteries used in long-duration energy storage applications.
- Storion will leverage a proprietary continuous manufacturing process and patented purification process to offer high-quality, domestically produced electrolyte.
- The initiative supports the DOE’s goal to deliver reliable energy at $0.05/kWh by 2030.
- Through exclusive access to Largo Physical Vanadium Corp., Storion offers a vanadium electrolyte leasing model to help VRFB compete with up-front capital costs of lithium-ion battery energy storage systems.
- Mike Judd, CEO and President of Stryten Energy, announced the formation aims to bring a cost-effective domestic supply chain for vanadium electrolyte and component parts.
- Judd noted VRFBs are safe and reliable long-duration energy storage solutions that provide energy resilience for commercial and industrial applications requiring continuous power.
- Vanadium electrolyte typically constitutes a large portion of the overall VRFB system cost depending on market prices.
- Largo Physical Vanadium’s leasing model allows investors to invest in physical vanadium assets while they are stored in VRFBs.
- Storion holds exclusive rights to offer LPV-owned vanadium to the market as leased vanadium electrolyte.
- Francesco D’Alessio, President of LCE, stated the joint venture accelerates LPV’s vanadium electrolyte leasing offering to the North American long-duration energy storage market.
- D’Alessio noted Storion’s competitive VRFB pricing model is expected to challenge lithium dominance for utility-scale deployments and secure U.S. leadership in this energy infrastructure application.
- VRFB technology provides safe and reliable long-duration energy storage for greater than four hours to ensure grid stability and facilitate renewable utilization across the U.S.
- With proper maintenance, VRFBs can last more than 20 years without electrolyte losing storage capacity.
- The battery's longevity complements wind and solar installation lifespans.
- The infinitely recyclable electrolyte offers a near-limitless cycle life.
- Systems are independently scalable in power and capacity.
- There is potential for substantial cost reduction as discharge time increases.
- Storion Energy has locations in Alpharetta, Georgia and Wilmington, Massachusetts.
- Stryten Energy is headquartered in Alpharetta, Georgia, and provides energy storage solutions across Essential Power, Motive Power, Transportation, Military, and Government sectors.
- Stryten’s technologies include advanced lead, lithium, and vanadium redox flow batteries, intelligent chargers, and energy performance management software.
- Largo Physical Vanadium Corp. aims to provide exchange-traded investment exposure to physical vanadium for steel, aerospace, and energy storage industries.
- LPV’s strategy includes actively supplying vanadium to VRFB end users to advance renewable energy integration in long duration storage.
- Largo Inc. is a globally recognized supplier of high-quality vanadium and ilmenite products sourced from its Maracás Menchen Mine in Brazil.
- Largo holds 50% ownership of Storion Energy and trades on the Nasdaq Stock Market and Toronto Stock Exchange under the symbol “LGO”.
