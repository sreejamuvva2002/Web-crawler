# Uber

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** charging_infrastructure  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-04

**Source:** [Uber set to invest millions in EV charging points in London | The Optimist Daily: Making Solutions the News](https://www.optimistdaily.com/2020/10/uber-set-to-invest-millions-in-ev-charging-points-in-london/)  ·  `page_000759`

## Facts

- Uber pledged to invest more than £5 million ($6.5 million) in public electric vehicle charging infrastructure in London to persuade drivers to switch to electric cars.
- Jamie Heywood, Uber’s regional general manager for northern and eastern Europe, stated that reliable, accessible charging near where drivers live is a key factor in deciding whether to switch to electric vehicles.
- Addressing charging challenges for professional drivers now will help create a mass market for electric vehicles in the years to come, which is critical for the UK to achieve its net-zero goal.
- The investment funds will be directed towards some of the poorest boroughs in London, which currently have only a fraction of the electric charge points found in wealthier parts of the city.
- Uber acknowledges it will need to invest additional money to create sufficient charging infrastructure to persuade more drivers to go fully electric.
- Uber has a fund of more than £100m raised through its self-imposed clean air fee in London, which has charged passengers 15p (20c) per mile since 2018.
- The company aims to have all 45,000 cars on its app in London be electric by 2025.
- Currently, only about 1,000 Uber vehicles in London are fully electric.
