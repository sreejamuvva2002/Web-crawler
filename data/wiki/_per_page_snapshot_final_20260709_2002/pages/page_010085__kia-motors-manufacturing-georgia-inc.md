# Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia, Inc.

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2019-11-18

**Source:** [Kia Motors celebrates decade of manufacturing in Georgia](https://www.kiamedia.com/us/en/media/pressreleases/15730/kia-motors-celebrates-decade-of-manufacturing-in-georgia)  ·  `page_010085`

## Facts

- Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia (KMMG) is Kia Motors’ first North American manufacturing plant.
- KMMG has created more than 15,000 local jobs.
- KMMG has produced more than three million vehicles.
- Governor Brian Kemp and Hyundai Motor Group Executive Vice Chairman Euisun Chung attended a ceremony to mark the facility's 10th anniversary of vehicle production in West Point, Georgia on November 18, 2019.
- KMMG became Kia’s third overseas manufacturing plant when it held its ground-breaking ceremony in October 2006.
- The site underwent over three years of construction before producing its first vehicle, a Sorento, in November 2009.
- The facility is located on a 2,200-acre site in West Point, Georgia.
- KMMG currently has a production capacity of 340,000 vehicles per year.
- Most output at KMMG is for the brand’s popular SUV models, the Telluride and Sorento, as well as the Optima mid-size sedan.
- Kia Motors plans to increase KMMG’s annual production target for the Telluride to 80,000 units from the current 60,000.
- Euisun Chung was president of Kia Motors Corporation when it made the decision to locate in Georgia.
- Kia Motors expressed appreciation for support and cooperation from Georgia’s Department of Economic Development, the Department of Community Affairs, and the Georgia Ports Authority.
- Georgia’s Technical College System and Georgia Quick Start played a role in training and developing KMMG’s workforce during its first decade.
- Georgia Quick Start trained over 3,000 employees at KMMG.
- EVC Chung stated that support from state and local governments, civic organizations, and various partners has been critical to the success of Kia’s first American plant.
- EVC Chung emphasized that Georgia and the larger U.S. market will be the crown jewel of Hyundai Motor Group's global operations.
- EVC Chung said Hyundai Motor Group and Kia Motors would become paradigm-shifters and trailblazers in the rapidly changing automotive industry.
- EVC Chung stated that KMMG will continue to play an important part in the company’s future strategy.
- EVC Chung added that Kia will offer the world’s best smart mobility services, expanding beyond vehicle production.
- EVC Chung stated that in the future Kia will manufacture personal air vehicles and robots, as well as offer related services.
- Georgia Governor Brian P. Kemp lauded a successful first decade of production and a promising future for Kia in West Point.
- Kia Motors recently announced with affiliate Hyundai Motor Company that it will establish an autonomous driving joint venture with Aptiv in the U.S. to lead the future of autonomous driving.
- KMMG contract signing occurred in March 2006.
- KMMG ground-breaking ceremony occurred in October 2006.
- Production of the Sorento began at KMMG in November 2009.
- KMMG grand opening occurred in February 2010.
- Production of the Optima began at KMMG in September 2011.
- Production of the Telluride began at KMMG in January 2019.
- KMMG produced its three-millionth vehicle in September 2019.
- KMMG began mass production on Nov. 16, 2009.
