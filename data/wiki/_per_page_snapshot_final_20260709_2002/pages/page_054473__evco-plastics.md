# EVCO Plastics

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2020-07-01

**Source:** [EVCO Plastics Streamlines Quality Inspection | IndMacDig | Industrial Machinery Digest](https://industrialmachinerydigest.com/quality/evco-plastics-streamlines-quality-inspection/)  ·  `page_054473`

## Facts

- EVCO Plastics is a global injection molder based in DeForest, Wisconsin, with plants throughout the USA as well as Mexico and China.
- Since 1948, the company has produced parts for myriad industries, including powered sports equipment, lawn & garden devices, agricultural and construction machinery, appliances and plumbing fixtures, plus medical and packaging machinery, general industrial products, and more.
- The company operates over 150 presses in the 28-3500 ton range.
- EVCO maintains its own mold production facility.
- The company operates a world-class metrology lab for quality evaluation.
- EVCO performs ISO Class 8 Cleanroom operations.
- The company conducts large part, thin-walled, metal-to-plastic insert, gas-assist, multi-shot and overmolding operations.
- EVCO performs in-mold decorating and labeling in highly automated and robotic facilities.
- The company produces parts in various thermoplastic materials, ranging from basic polypropylene to engineering-grade materials.
- EVCO currently employs over 1000 people at its nine locations worldwide.
- Rich Duval serves as the head of the metrology lab at EVCO.
- Duval's strategy includes taking apart, scanning, and comparing products to CAD/CAM files as early as possible in the process to allow customers to observe how plastic products compare to solid models.
- The EVCO metrology team uses scanning technology and reverse engineering to recreate tooling surfaces where solid models do not exist and provides this data to a tool shop for steel revisions.
- Owing to work volume and challenges with point cloud data collection, the EVCO team partnered with supplier Exact Metrology in Brookfield, Wisconsin.
- EVCO researched earlier generation scanning but selected more advanced Romer and other brands from Exact's product offering.
- Exact Metrology is a dual-purpose vendor that sells metrology equipment and performs complex scanning services for customers.
- Exact operates the first industrial-grade CT scanner in America, which enables geometric views inside molded, cast, and forged parts to check for porosity, hot spots, and material flow issues.
- Dean Solberg serves as VP at Exact Metrology.
- Solberg's team reviewed EVCO's scenario and provided several new Romer arms to the customer.
- One application involved staff taking a damaged tool component, reconstructing the cogs on a machine gear, rechecking through design, and producing a suitable alternative via 3D printing.
- Duval notes the metrology solution reduced inspection time in the lab and provided reverse engineering as an additional option to customers.
- EVCO evaluates older molds using scanning techniques to determine the position of runners, pins, cores, and other components.
- A solid model is produced from scans to show auto-surfacing, solid model processing, and in-use characteristics.
- Reverse engineering from existing parts is performed in the EVCO lab for production issue resolution or part validation.
- EVCO continues to use conventional CMM technology in its metrology department.
- Duval notes his team has been able to "marry" the Romer arm and CMM technologies.
- Solberg adds that combining these technologies was possible only because of the talent on the team at EVCO.
- The combination of 3D scans with bridge CMM plates gives EVCO technicians and design engineers necessary comparative data to resolve issues.
- Duval notes Exact's support aided EVCO during the ramp-up of new scanning technologies, as Solberg proactively amended the training protocol to suit specific requirements after initial questions arose.
- As a supplier to major OEMs in many industries, EVCO is routinely expected to supply considerable part and production data to its customers.
- Duval states plans to utilize Exact equipment to support tooling for seamless capture and transmission of data from part design through production.
- EVCO investigated further use of Exact CT scanning services, but notes many large parts like farm machinery housings exceed the scan envelope of the CT equipment.
- One of EVCO's strongest abilities is the production of near-finish prototypes, often produced with conventional CNC machining or rapidly expanding 3D printing technologies.
- At EVCO, fused deposition modeling (FDM), with and without carbon fiber, is performed.
- 3D scanning techniques from Exact produce necessary data files such as STL that reside in the "point cloud" for use by advanced 3D prototype production technologies.
- Millions of data points provide 3D printing equipment all necessary information to produce highly accurate prototypes and anticipate challenges in the transition from design to tool to production.
- Duval notes the Exact team has continued its high level of service and pro-active suggestions to keep pace with EVCO's expectations.
- The new metrology equipment is helping EVCO attain its stated goal of supplying world-class plastic products and assemblies.
- Exact Metrology has facilities in Cincinnati, OH, Moline, IL, and Milwaukee, WI, plus affiliated offices throughout the country.
- Exact Metrology offers 3D and CT scanning, reverse engineering, quality inspection, product development, and 2D drawings.
- The company provides turnkey metrology solutions, including equipment sales and lease/rental arrangements.
- Exact offers a complete line of portable scanning and measurement technologies as well as contract measurement for 3D laser scanning services, reverse engineering services, non-contact inspection, metrology services, and 3D digitizing.
- Exact's newest equipment includes a CT Scanner, the first in America being used for metrology rather than medical testing.
- Exact sells and rents metrology equipment solutions, provides testing as a service, and offers application software training.
