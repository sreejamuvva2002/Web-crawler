# Athens/Clarke County

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2008-10-01

**Source:** [Athens/Clarke County: More Than A College Town - Georgia Trend Magazine](https://www.georgiatrend.com/2008/10/01/athens-clarke-county-more-than-a-college-town/)  ·  `page_019616`

## Facts

- Athens has a legacy of “art” music spanning three decades and multiple generations, with bands like the B-52s, R.E.M., and Widespread Panic still active and their musicians residing there.
- Most University of Georgia (UGA) graduates love staying in Athens due to a deep fondness for the community.
- Former UGA painting students, the B-52s, recently moved back from New York to record a new album.
- Quality of life is a strategy in Georgia’s effort to address a projected physician shortfall, with Athens overflowing with research opportunities.
- Athens is the only city in Georgia with a multimodal transit station and features extensive citywide bike and walking paths.
- UGA was the only public university in Georgia this year to produce not one but two Rhodes Scholars.
- Recent census figures show Athens experienced a 12.5 percent population increase since 2000, capped by enrollment limits at UGA.
- The government is preparing for massive job growth with the new Medical College of Georgia (MCG) Athens campus, which opened in a temporary facility and will move into the former Navy Supply Corps School in 2011.
- The National Bio and Agro-Defense Facility (NBAF) could bring 400 jobs to Athens if leaders can outsell current favorites Mississippi and Texas.
- Athens Area Chamber of Commerce President and former Mayor Doc Eldridge stated the MCG is as big a development that has ever happened in Athens in his memory and dovetails into becoming a biosciences center for the state.
- Athens Mayor Heidi Davison highlighted infrastructure investments for bike/walking paths and bus service, noting a 45 percent increase in ridership during a promotional free bus day.
- Local government subsidizes bus transportation through taxpayers, as the state provides no funding for transit and the city relies on general funds and federal funds.
- The Athens-Clarke County Economic Development Foundation (EDF) plans to move into the Athens Area Chamber of Commerce building to coordinate employer recruitment, marking the first time the government and chamber have been aligned in a long time.
- Efforts are under way to link Athens and Atlanta with a “Brain Train” light rail commuter line, emphasizing connections to Atlanta’s 10 colleges and universities and the Centers for Disease Control.
- Highway 316, connecting Athens with I-85, has been marketed as a research corridor alongside Georgia Gwinnett College.
- The EDF industrial park will soon include a new 120,000-square-foot speculative building under development by Rooker Development Company.
- Athens’ tourism economy is growing due to gas-wary road-trippers, with restaurants at capacity and hotels like the new Indigo Hotel opening.
- AthFest annual local music festival usually takes place in late June, with 2009 dates set for June 26-28.
- The Athens PopFest takes place in August and spotlights local bands, featuring underground legend Roky Erikson this year.
- The new UGA Art Festival was held Sept. 4-7 to dedicate the Lamar Dodd Art Building and is expected to grow into an annual event.
- The Athens Conference Center is growing ahead of its expansions as several conferences outgrow current facilities.
- Athens is addressing a poverty level of 31 percent, the highest in Georgia and fifth highest in the nation, through a task force called One Athens.
- One Athens is a collaboration between the Athens-Clarke County government, Clarke County School District, Athens Area Chamber of Commerce, Family Connection/Communities in Schools, and UGA.
- One Athens efforts include an affordable housing development fund, expanded transit lines, financial support for two teen pregnancy clinics, changes to sex education curriculum, a K-12 mentor program linking students with UGA faculty, and a submitted charter school application.
- UGA has been threatened with heavy budget cuts that may result in layoffs, though officials hope state funding levels will not be impacted.
- Athens Regional Medical Center recently added a new Mother-Baby Unit and expanded its emergency department, doubling its ability to take patients.
- St. Mary’s Health Care System has seen a 26 percent increase in inpatient admissions over the last three years.
- St. Mary’s is building a five-story medical office building with 100,000 square feet of space and a six-story, 700-space parking deck adjacent to the hospital.
- Landmark Hospital of Athens, an acute care facility adding 42 beds and creating 100 to 150 jobs, opened on July 14 with 15 referrals and nine patients within its first week.
- Hospitals are coordinating efforts to accommodate residencies from the new MCG, with Athens Regional considering changing its mission from a community hospital to a teaching hospital.
- Georgia will be last in the nation for the number of physicians by 2020 unless the state provides resources to address the shortage.
- Dr. David Lee, UGA vice president for Research, stated the MCG campus won’t open until 2010 and will begin with only 30 students.
- The Navy Supply Corps School is closing due to the 2006 Base Realignment and Closure announcements, reducing Athens’ population by around 300.
- UGA’s pharmacy school will expand its labs and teaching space into $42.9 million worth of new space called Pharmacy South.
- The Student Health Center is expanding by 30,000 square feet to meet increased student demands.
- Athens Technical College is planning an 80,000 square foot Life Sciences building that will eventually accommodate some 1,500 students.
- In the last year, Athens Technical College filled 250 slots for programs like nursing, surgical technician, and diagnostic medical stenography.
- Eighty-eight percent of Athens Technical College graduates work within a 50-mile radius of the campus.
- The NBAF decision is expected in December and may put Athens on the map with more than 1,000 high-paying jobs.
- Clarke County unemployment rate was 6.1 percent in July 2008, compared to Georgia’s 6.5 percent.
- Per capita income in 2006 was $23,921.
- Top five private employers include Athens Regional Medical Center (2,771), Pilgrim’s Pride (1,559), St. Mary’s Hospital (1,336), Gold Kist, Inc. (615), and Merial Limited, Inc. (549).
