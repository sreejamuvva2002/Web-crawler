# SK Battery America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-03-09

**Source:** [SK On trims Georgia workforce by 37% as US EV demand cools | Automotive World](https://www.automotiveworld.com/news/sk-on-trims-georgia-workforce-by-37-as-us-ev-demand-cools/)  ·  `page_060598`

## Facts

- SK Battery America announced the lay-off of 958 workers at its plant in Commerce, Georgia on 6 March.
- The layoffs reduce the company's workforce by 37% to roughly 1,600 employees.
- The Commerce, Georgia plant opened in January 2022 at a cost of US$2.6bn.
- The plant had supplied cells for the Ford F-150 Lightning before Ford cancelled the model in December 2025.
- The layoffs follow the December dissolution of SK’s US$11.4bn battery joint venture with Ford.
- The JV dissolution left SK with a KRW3.7tr (US$2.6bn) asset impairment and a Tennessee plant not expected to begin production until 2028.
- Cognisant that regulations have sent EV demand backwards in the US, SK Battery America is expanding into the battery energy storage system (ESS) market.
- There are no signs of a meaningful reversal in EV demand until at least 2028.
- Congress eliminated the US$7,500 consumer EV tax credit in September 2025.
- The Trump administration has moved to weaken fuel economy and emissions standards, removing regulatory incentives for automakers to commit to electrification timelines.
- US EV penetration held flat at around 8% of new vehicle sales in 2025.
- EV demand was stronger in the first nine months of 2025 but was effectively cancelled out by a crash from October onwards.
- The layoffs hold political significance in Georgia, a state that has fluctuated between voting for republicans and democrats since 2018.
- Other steps to roll back EV policy have specifically affected only those interested in buying one, or the automakers and suppliers involved in their production.
- The SK layoffs are a direct consequence of federal policy and an inconvenient one for Georgia, which attracted more Inflation Reduction Act (IRA)-related green investment projects than any other state.
- A Rivian plant in Georgia, granted IRA loans in the final days of the Biden administration, also appears unsteady.
- Despite President Donald Trump’s vocal support for US manufacturing, the SK layoffs serve as a direct counterexample that could lead to blowback in the upcoming midterms.
- A second Georgia plant, built jointly with Hyundai, is on track to begin production in the first half of 2026.
- The Tennessee facility retains the option to supply both automotive and ESS cells.
- The pivot toward energy storage is also being pursued by LG Energy Solution and Samsung SDI.
- This pivot is emerging as the Korean battery industry’s primary hedge against the collapse of US EV demand.
- Whether the ESS market is large enough to accommodate a pivot from all three players remains to be seen.
- SK On faces steep challenges around profitability, posting an operating loss of KRW 441.4bn in the fourth quarter of 2025.
- The Ford JV dissolution removed KRW 5.4tr in debt from the balance sheet and freed SK to pursue new customers without JV governance constraints.
- The company secured at least one major ESS supply deal and a Nissan battery agreement in 2025.
- The Georgia headcount reduction is the operational consequence of the pivot toward ESS being underway in earnest.
