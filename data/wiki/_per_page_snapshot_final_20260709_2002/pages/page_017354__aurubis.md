# Aurubis

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2024-10-02

**Source:** [Copper producer Aurubis to shun takeovers, rebuild trust, says new CEO, ETAuto](https://auto.economictimes.indiatimes.com/news/auto-components/copper-producer-aurubis-to-shun-takeovers-rebuild-trust-says-new-ceo/113871158)  ·  `page_017354`

## Facts

- Aurubis launched a new recycling plant in the U.S. state of Georgia last month.
- The Georgia recycling plant will take until the 2026/27 fiscal year to reach full production.
- Full production at the Georgia facility will yield 70,000 metric tons a year of blister copper.
- Aurubis is executing a 1.7 billion euro (USD 1.9 billion) capital expenditure programme that includes the new recycling plant in the United States.
- The company plans to focus on internal projects and its major capex programme for the next two years before considering further expansion through M&A or building additional recycling plants.
- Aurubis CEO Toralf Haag stated the company will shun takeovers and prioritize rebuilding stakeholder trust following a scandal involving stolen copper and warnings of weaker profits next fiscal year.
- Major shareholder Salzgitter holds a 29.9% stake in Aurubis, but there are no new discussions or developments regarding acquiring more shares or taking over the company.
- Aurubis expects an improvement in spot treatment charges during the course of next year, though it anticipates being impacted by low treatment charges for calendar year 2025.
- Despite expected low treatment charges, Aurubis will not face difficulty sourcing raw materials due to existing long-term supply deals.
