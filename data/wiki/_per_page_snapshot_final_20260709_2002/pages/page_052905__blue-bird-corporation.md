## Facts

- Blue Bird Corporation emerges as a frontrunner in the electric school bus market.
- The company leverages its strong position in traditional buses to drive innovation and growth.
- Q4 2024 financial results exceeded expectations.
- The company reaffirmed FY 2025 EBITDA guidance.
- Blue Bird operates under a one-shift, one-plant labor agreement.
- This labor agreement contributes to an improved cost base and enhanced profitability.
- Analysts project price targets for Blue Bird ranging from $50 to $54.
- These projections reflect the company's potential for sustained growth and market expansion.
- Blue Bird Corporation designs, engineers, manufactures, and sells school buses in the United States, Canada, and internationally.
- The company operates through two segments: Bus and Parts.
- It offers Type C, Type D, and specialty buses.
- It provides alternative power options including propane powered, gasoline powered, compressed natural gas powered, and electric powered school buses.
- It also sells diesel engines.
- The company sells replacement bus parts and provides extended warranties related to its products.
- Blue Bird sells its products through drop ship and a network of dealers.
- It sells directly to fleet operators, the United States government, and state governments.
- Products are sold to independent service centers.
- The company maintains a parts distribution center.
- Blue Bird Corporation was founded in 1927.
- Blue Bird Corporation is headquartered in Macon, Georgia.
