# JAC Products Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [JAC Products Inc. | Business View Magazine](https://businessviewmagazine.com/jac-products-inc/)  ·  `page_056318`

## Facts

- JAC Products Inc. was founded in 1967 and is a leading designer and manufacturer of automotive roof racks and cargo management systems.
- JAC Products supplies its products to Ford, Toyota, Honda, General Motors, Subaru, Renault, Hyundai, Chrysler, Kia Motors, Nissan, and Peugeot.
- The company has offices around the world and manufacturing facilities in Michigan, Georgia, and Portugal.
- JAC's engineering and manufacturing capabilities include plastic injection molding and steel rollform for base components.
- JAC's Franklin, Georgia facility was originally set up as an aluminum extrusion plant in the mid-1960s and was taken over by JAC 20 years ago.
- The extrusion lines at the Franklin, Georgia facility produce millions of pounds per year of 6XXX series aluminum.
- JAC Products has about 1,500 employees in North America.
- JAC's competitors include Germany-based WKW Automotive Group, DURA Automotive (with more than 21,000 employees in 15 countries), and China-based MINTH Group.
- Engineering Manager Jeff Cavalier admits that JAC is relatively small compared to its competitors.
- Cavalier states that JAC's competitive advantage lies in faster engineering turnaround and superior product quality due to its specific technologies.
- JAC's manufacturing process involves stretching and forming metal to fit specific vehicle roof line profiles, rather than just CNC machining or cutting.
- The tolerances for JAC's rail arrangements are defined by tenths and hundredths of a millimeter.
- Cavalier emphasizes that manufacturing practices require repeatable processes, material management support, and timely delivery of quality parts to ensure customer satisfaction.
- JAC's cargo management systems for the trucking industry include side rails, header bars, bed dividers, load bars, and lockable storage bins.
- Cavalier describes JAC's innovations in racking systems for work trucks as top-notch and world-class.
- JAC is broadening its expertise in manufacturing complex step rails.
- JAC Products supports the automotive industry's shift from steel to aluminum substructures to reduce vehicle weight.
- The industry's move toward robotic welding instead of bolting requires JAC to adjust the aluminum alloys it supplies.
- JAC's product development group in Michigan stays closely involved with OEM engineers to support these alloy and manufacturing changes.
- Cavalier credits JAC's Product Development team for forecasting market trends two, five, and ten years out to maintain OEM relationships.
- JAC aims to be a key player in the OEM process by expanding its capabilities beyond roof racks.
- JAC is integrating cameras and LED lighting systems directly into its roof racks.
- Cavalier notes that JAC's roof racks can incorporate cameras for a 360-degree vehicle view and security systems.
- JAC extensively tests its product coatings and sealings to withstand harsh car wash detergents and rough weather.
- Cavalier notes that proximity to manufacturers in Michigan and the Southeast helps minimize freight costs and meet delivery needs.
- The Franklin, Georgia facility's location helps JAC minimize freight costs for its customers.
- JAC's future strategy focuses on diversifying its product line for OEMs and expanding global relationships beyond North America.
- Cavalier identifies China, Central America, and South America as untapped markets with significant potential for JAC's growth.
- JAC Products is currently on top in North America for roof racks and cargo management systems despite not being the largest globally.
- JAC Products Inc. is headquartered in Saline, Michigan.
- Thumb Tool & Engineering is an aluminum extrusion die company acquired in 1998 by the metals division of the Gemini Group.
- In 2013, Thumb Tool & Engineering joined forces with Mid-South Extrusion Die and Central Extrusion Die to position the Gemini Group as the second largest supplier of aluminum extrusion dies and tooling in North America.
