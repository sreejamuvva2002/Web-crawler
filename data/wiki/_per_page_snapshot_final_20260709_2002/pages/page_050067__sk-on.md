# SK On

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-03-07

**Source:** [SK On Cuts One-Third of U.S. Battery Workforce Amid EV Slowdown](https://www.chosun.com/english/industry-en/2026/03/07/ZGEV25KFP5EQXHG54TMJ3X2S5Q/)  ·  `page_050067`

## Facts

- SK On, the battery subsidiary of SK Innovation, has laid off more than one-third of its employees at a U.S. battery factory.
- The large-scale workforce reduction is interpreted as a response to the slowing growth of the U.S. electric vehicle (EV) market.
- The EV market slowdown was exacerbated by the Trump administration’s abolition of EV subsidies.
- SK Battery America, the U.S. subsidiary of SK On, announced via email on the 6th (local time) that it laid off 958 workers (37%) out of 2,566 employees at its Georgia State factory.
- The layoffs account for roughly one-third of the total workforce at the facility.
- The last working day for the affected employees was on the 6th.
- Affected employees are expected to receive salaries until May 6th, when their employment contracts expire.
- The factory was built in January 2022 in Georgia State with an investment of $2.6 billion (approximately 3.85 trillion Korean won).
- The facility has supplied batteries for the F-150 Lightning, Ford’s flagship electric pickup truck.
- Ford decided to halt production of the F-150 Lightning model at the end of last year.
- Ford also suspended development of electric commercial vans and trucks at the end of last year.
- The Trump administration fully abolished $7,500-per-vehicle EV subsidies that had been implemented since October of last year.
- A source from SK Battery America told AP and FOX, “We made the difficult decision to reduce our workforce to align operations with market conditions.”
- The same source added, “We will continue to strive to build a robust domestic supply chain for advanced battery manufacturing in Georgia State.”
