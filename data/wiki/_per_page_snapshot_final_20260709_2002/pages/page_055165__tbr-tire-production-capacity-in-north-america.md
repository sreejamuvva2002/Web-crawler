# TBR Tire Production Capacity in North America

**Type:** supplier_relationship  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [MTD Looks at TBR Tire Production Capacity  | Modern Tire Dealer](https://www.moderntiredealer.com/suppliers/article/55260029/mtd-looks-at-tbr-tire-production-capacity)  ·  `page_055165`

## Facts

- Goodyear Tire & Rubber Co. plans to move truck tire production out of its Danville, Va., plant.
- Goodyear currently has the most TBR tire production capacity in North America.
- The Danville factory dates back to 1966 and will continue to manufacture tires for aviation applications.
- The Danville factory can build up to 11,000 TBR tire units a day according to the latest MTD research.
- Goodyear’s other TBR tire plant is located in Topeka, Kan., with a daily production capacity of 5,500 medium truck tire units.
- The Topeka, Kan. plant also builds OTR tires.
- Bridgestone Americas Inc. has the second-largest TBR tire production capacity among all North American tire companies, with a total estimated ceiling of 15,200 units per day.
- Bridgestone's TBR production is split between two plants in LaVergne, Tenn., and Warren County, Tenn.
- The LaVergne, Tenn. plant can make up to 5,800 tires daily.
- MTD estimates the Warren County, Tenn. plant has a total daily capacity of 9,400 units.
- A $550 million investment at the Warren County plant is still on track with its original schedule to facilitate new technology development capabilities according to Bridgestone officials.
- The capital infusion is expected to expand the Warren County facility by 850,000 square feet.
- The expansion will give Bridgestone the ability to manufacture TBR tires equipped with radio frequency identification tags.
- Michelin North America Inc. has a total TBR tire production capacity of 13,400 units per day according to MTD research.
- Michelin’s plant in Spartanburg, S.C., can build 6,400 units per day at full throttle.
- The company’s Waterville, Nova Scotia, plant enjoys a capacity of 7,000 tires a day.
- Continental Tire the America LLC’s plant in Clinton, Miss., began producing TBR tires in 2020 and has a 2,800-unit-a-day capacity.
- Continental’s Mount Vernon, Ill., plant can build up to 10,300 TBR tires on a daily basis when running at full bore according to MTD data.
- Yokohama Tire Corp.’s TBR tire plant in West Point, Miss., can produce up to 3,000 units per day according to MTD research.
- Grupo Carso/Euzkadi (Continental AG) operates a plant in San Luis Potosi, Mexico, with a daily capacity of 3,800 medium truck tires.
- Corporacion de Occidente SA de CV’s Guadalajara plant can build around 2,800 TBR tire units daily.
- JK Tyre & Industries, formerly CIA Hulera Tornel, enjoys a daily TBR production capacity of 1,500 units at its Mexico City plant according to MTD data.
- Hankook Tire America Corp. is currently about half-way through the construction process at its plant in Clarksville, Tenn., with the project remaining on track.
- In August 2021, Hankook announced it would invest $1.6 billion at the Clarksville plant to double its passenger and light truck tire production to 11 million units while adding TBR tire production capabilities.
- Rob Williams, Hankook Tire America Corp. president, stated that the addition of TBR tire production at the facility is a vital step to their growth in the medium truck tire market.
- Two months ago, Sumitomo Rubber North America Inc. (SRNA) shuttered its consumer, TBR and motorcycle tire production facility in Buffalo, N.Y.
- The Buffalo plant was one of the oldest still-operating tire factories in the U.S., dating back to 1923, after SRNA took it over from Goodyear in 2015.
- The shuttered Buffalo plant had the capacity to build around 2,300 TBR tires per day according to MTD research.
