# Archer Aviation Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Aerospace Company To Bring 1,000 Jobs to Newton County | Aviation Pros](https://www.aviationpros.com/education-training/news/21287862/aerospace-company-to-bring-1000-jobs-to-newton-county)  ·  `page_016814`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation Inc. will create 1,000 jobs.
- Archer Aviation Inc. will invest $118 million over 10 years in a new manufacturing facility.
- The facility will be a state-of-the-art electric vertical takeoff and landing (eVTOL) aircraft manufacturing plant located alongside the Covington Municipal Airport in Newton County, Georgia.
- Archer Aviation is based in Santa Clara, California.
- Archer is currently developing and manufacturing eVTOL aircraft for use in Urban Air Mobility networks.
- The company plans to construct the facility on a large adjacent parcel set aside for future development near the Covington Municipal Airport, which was established in 1963 as part of a long-term airport master plan.
- The site is strategically accessible by Georgia's interstate system via I-20.
- Gov. Brian Kemp stated that Georgia has made fostering innovation a priority and leveraged its established leadership in the aerospace industry to land Archer Aviation in Newton County.
- Adam Goldstein, founder and CEO of Archer, announced the decision to locate the company's first manufacturing facility in Covington to ramp production and progress toward commercialization.
- Archer aims to create Urban Air Mobility networks across major cities nationwide.
- Mayor Steve Horton expressed excitement for Archer joining Covington's business community and noted the city's focus on quality jobs and new technology.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development's Global Commerce Industrial Projects team represented the state in partnership with the Newton County Industrial Development Authority, Metro Atlanta Chamber of Commerce, the Georgia Center of Innovation's Aerospace Industry team, Georgia EMC, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia Tech.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson highlighted Archer's innovative eVTOL aircraft design aimed at sustainably tackling urban mobility issues and praised the specialized Aerospace team's efforts to foster Georgia's aerospace and defense industries.
