# Daechang Seat Corp.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-10-24

**Source:** [South Korean auto parts maker plans USD 72.5 mn plant near new Hyundai facility in Georgia, hiring 500, ETAuto](https://auto.economictimes.indiatimes.com/news/auto-components/south-korean-auto-parts-maker-plans-usd-72-5-mn-plant-near-new-hyundai-facility-in-georgia-hiring-500/104663906)  ·  `page_018924`

## Facts

- Daechang Seat Corp., based in Gyeongju, South Korea, plans to invest USD 72.5 million in a new plant in Savannah.
- The company will hire more than 500 people for the new Savannah facility.
- It is the latest company to announce a plant to supply Hyundai's USD 7.6 billion plant to assemble electric vehicles and batteries in Ellabell, near Savannah.
- The Hyundai plant was announced last year and could grow to 8,500 employees.
- The Hyundai plant could begin producing vehicles as early as 2024.
- Suppliers to the Hyundai plant have pledged to invest nearly USD 2.3 billion.
- Suppliers to the Hyundai plant have pledged to hire nearly 5,800 people.
- Daechang Seat opened a factory in Phenix City, Alabama, in 2019.
- The company announced a USD 23.4 million expansion in Phenix City last year.
- The Phenix City expansion will build a new building and hire an additional 300 people.
- The company employs more than 5,000 people in seven countries.
- Jinsuk Lee, CEO of Daechang Seat Savannah Corp., stated: "Our primary objective is to drive the growth of DSC through facility expansions, ultimately transforming DSC into a global entity."
- Workers will make an average wage of more than USD 18 an hour, not including bonuses and benefits.
- The state will pay to train workers for the new plant.
- Daechang Seat could qualify for USD 10 million in state income tax credits, at USD 4,000 per job over five years.
- Qualification for the tax credits requires workers to make at least USD 31,300 a year.
- Local officials have granted a 15-year property tax reduction that's projected to save the company USD 5.8 million.
- Local officials are also selling land for the plant at a reduced rate.
