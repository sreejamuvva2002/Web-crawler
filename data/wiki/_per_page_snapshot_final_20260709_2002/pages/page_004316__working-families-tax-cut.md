# Working Families Tax Cut

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-04

**Source:** [Secretary Wright Applauds End of New Federal Wind and Solar Subsidies | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/articles/secretary-wright-applauds-end-new-federal-wind-and-solar-subsidies)  ·  `page_004316`

## Facts

- U.S. Secretary of Energy Chris Wright released a statement regarding the Working Families Tax Cut July 4, 2026 deadline ending federal tax credit subsidies for new wind and solar projects not currently under construction.
- The federal government has subsidized wind and solar energy generation for more than three decades.
- In 2025, wind and solar accounted for approximately three percent of total U.S. primary energy consumption.
- Secretary Wright stated that on July 4th, subsidies for wind and solar will end after about 35 years.
- Secretary Wright criticized wind and solar for taking 100 times more land than other sources for a similar amount of energy.
- Secretary Wright noted wind and solar require an enormous amount of materials, including energy-intensive steel, cement, and polysilicon.
- Secretary Wright stated wind and solar require additional transmission lines to connect far-flung production to demand centers.
- Secretary Wright characterized the output as a relatively small amount of low-value energy due to intermittency (wind doesn't always blow and sun doesn't always shine).
- Secretary Wright claimed wind and solar drive up system costs and increase Americans' electricity prices.
- Secretary Wright pledged to drive down electricity prices instead of raising them.
