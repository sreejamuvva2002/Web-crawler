# Guardian Industries Corp.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Timeline](https://www.guardian.com/us/en/about-us/timeline)  ·  `page_060074`

## Facts

- Guardian Glass Company was founded in Detroit, Michigan in 1932.
- At founding, the company had 20 employees producing roughly 100 laminated windshields a day at a plant on Detroit’s Lower East Side.
- Initial equipment consisted of only a press used for laminating glass to the interlayer, with all other work done manually.
- Sales during the company’s first year totaled $130,000.
- Guardian adopted a logo incorporating a big G surrounding a knight on a horse to represent security and protection against wind, weather, and debris for vehicle drivers and passengers.
- During World War II, Guardian produced millions of square feet of laminated glass for windshields in trucks, jeeps, and other military vehicles.
- In the early 1950s, Guardian began production of curved, laminated windshields.
- In 1970, Guardian became the first company to enter the U.S. primary glass industry in nearly 50 years when production started at a new plant in Carleton, Michigan.
- The company now operates plants on five continents with products used in vehicles, homes, and construction projects worldwide.
- Guardian’s first European float glass plant was located in Bascharage, Luxembourg, establishing its worldwide expansion.
- A small number of U.S.-trained managers and technicians moved to Luxembourg to assume leadership roles within the new plant.
- The Financial Times later dubbed Guardian the "raging bull" in Europe for shaking up the glass establishment.
- In 2016, Guardian celebrated 35 years in Luxembourg with the official opening of its new offices in Bertrange.
- Low-Emissivity (low-E) glass features microscopically thin, transparent metal coatings that reflect long-wave radiation to improve energy efficiency in buildings.
- Low-E glass was first manufactured in Europe and introduced to North America in the early 1980s.
- In 1983, Guardian became one of the first companies in the United States to manufacture low-E glass by adding sputter coating technology at its Corsicana, Texas float glass plant.
- Modern low-E coatings are multilayered designs engineered for high visible light transmission, low visible light reflection, and reduced heat transfer.
- Guardian Glass manufactures advanced low-E coatings for residential and commercial applications globally.
- In 1985, Guardian introduced HealthGuard, a global health program aimed at elevating employee well-being and controlling healthcare costs.
- The HealthGuard program features educational initiatives, physical activity, health screenings, and community outreach to help employees and family members improve their lives.
- Company health programs became more common later, with Guardian recognized as a leader in the arena, and its approach featured in a CNN News special.
- In 2000, Guardian opened its Science & Technology Center (STC), one of the most sophisticated and comprehensive glass laboratories in the world.
- Since opening, the STC has launched scores of new products and received hundreds of new patents.
- President and CEO William Davidson directed the creation of the STC to shift focus from capital expenditure on float plants to making existing plants more profitable through innovation.
- Davidson challenged employees to remain low-cost producers and pursue downstream operations and new products to exploit competitive advantages and develop new capabilities.
- The STC location was placed across the street from a float glass plant to keep it connected to day-to-day operations.
- The STC's objectives were to provide innovative products to customers and develop efficient manufacturing processes that increase quality while reducing customer costs.
- In 2015, Guardian completed an expansion of its Science and Technology Center in Carleton, Michigan.
- The expansion added 27,000 square feet to accelerate the rate of product design and development.
- Expansion highlights include a full-size vacuum coater, a glass product showcase wall, an electrically wired full-scale demonstration wall for advanced glazing technologies, and upgraded laboratory space.
- A cutting-edge curtain wall was added to achieve net zero energy results using Guardian’s SunGuard SNX 51/23, SunGuard Spandrel HT, and building-integrated photovoltaic panels.
- Vice President of Research and Development Sheldon Davis stated the high-performance building demonstrates Guardian’s commitment to advancing glass technology.
- In 2009, SRG Global Inc. was formed by combining the automotive trim operations of Guardian Automotive and Siegel-Robert, becoming one of the world's largest manufacturers of chrome-plated plastics for the automotive, commercial truck, and consumer goods industries.
- The evolution began in 2008 when Guardian Automotive acquired Siegel-Robert Automotive.
- SRG Global is headquartered near Detroit, Michigan, with manufacturing presence in North America, Europe, and Asia.
- Through Advanced Development Centers, SRG Global enhances manufacturing processes and accelerates delivery of next-generation materials, coating technologies, and sub-systems globally.
- SRG Global engineers solutions for greater surface durability, structural integrity, enhanced functionality, vehicle efficiency, and optimal design flexibility.
- Koch Industries announced the completion of its acquisition of Guardian Industries Corp. after receiving all regulatory approvals.
- Koch initially invested in Guardian in 2012 by acquiring a 44.5 percent minority interest in the company.
- Guardian brings expertise in float, value-added, and fabricated glass products, high-value coatings on plastics for the automotive industry, and distribution of specialty building products to Koch.
- Ron Vaupel, president and CEO of Guardian, noted the company began as a small windshield company in Detroit in 1932 and now serves a global customer base across multiple industries.
- Charles Koch, chairman and CEO of Koch Industries, stated their philosophy is to invest where there is mutual benefit, with Koch's capabilities helping Guardian serve customers while Guardian's expertise bolsters Koch's operations.
- Following the acquisition, Koch companies now employ more than 120,000 people worldwide, with about 70,000 of those in the United States.
- Guardian companies have nearly 150 locations in more than 25 countries.
- Terms of the acquisition deal were not disclosed.
- Guardian Glass started full production on a technologically advanced new glass coater on July 6 at its second facility in Czestochowa, Poland.
- The state-of-the-art coater uses advanced technology to turn standard float glass into high-performance, value-added glass.
- The new coater will enable expanded production of low-emissivity (low-E) and solar control glass products for residential (Guardian ClimaGuard®) and commercial (Guardian SunGuard®) applications.
- These high-performance products improve building energy efficiency, aesthetics, and occupant comfort.
