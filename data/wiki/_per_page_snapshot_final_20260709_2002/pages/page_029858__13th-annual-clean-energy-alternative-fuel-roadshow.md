# 13th Annual Clean Energy Alternative Fuel Roadshow

**Type:** event  ·  **Role:** workforce_training  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [13th Annual Energy Roadshow Recap | Select Georgia](https://www.selectgeorgia.com/blog/energy-roadshow/)  ·  `page_029858`

## Facts

- Since 2010, the Georgia Public Service Commission has hosted discussions on alternative fuel and the automotive industry in Georgia.
- The Clean Energy Roadshow was founded by Commissioner Tim Echols.
- The original intent of the event was a roundtable of industry experts to discuss challenges of introducing new technologies into fleets and other modes of transportation.
- Over thirteen years, the initiative grew from a roundtable discussion into what is now called the Clean Energy Roadshow.
- Today, the Roadshow operates as a statewide series of energy seminars and ride-and-drive opportunities open to all professionals and government leaders.
- Georgia Power Community and Economic Development was invited to present on supporting workforce and talent pipeline efforts around electric transportation.
- Jamal Jessie and Nikia Johnson from Georgia Power participated in the event and shared highlights from their recent interview.
- Since 2018, there have been 33 EV announcements in Georgia.
- These EV-related developments have created over 29,000 jobs.
- Jamal Jessie noted that the industry has evolved significantly over thirteen years, with many electric and alternative vehicles now available for communities to learn about.
- The event included assessments of workforce availability for emerging transportation industries.
- Georgia is becoming a huge player and an epicenter in the Southeast for Electric Vehicles.
- The Roadshow demonstrates how upgrading fleets to alternative or electric vehicles can impact communities and help businesses save money.
- Nikia Johnson attended her first Clean Energy Roadshow, noting it serves as a space bringing together stakeholders related to electric transportation.
- Georgia Power shared resources and updates on workforce support efforts for manufacturers, suppliers, and funding partners.
- A presentation slide showing the geography of all electric manufacturers in the Southeast was highly impactful and drew significant audience attention.
- Many attendees were unaware of the large concentration of EV and battery manufacturers operating in Georgia and the broader Southeast region.
- Occupational graphics attracted attention by highlighting IT space roles alongside traditional manufacturing and battery occupations, particularly in Savannah.
- For each visited region, specific graphics detailed the number of projects coming to the area as well as associated capital investment figures.
- Jamal Jessie stated that growth from incoming automotive manufacturers, suppliers, and battery manufacturers requires new education pathways into those career fields.
- Nikia Johnson emphasized that businesses and stakeholders must be strategic in their partnerships with educational partners to build necessary workforce infrastructure.
- Georgia Power is actively creating awareness of economic opportunities coming to local communities.
- The company is working closely with technical school partners, specifically noting the Technical College System of Georgia (TCSG) starting EV pathways.
- Workforce development efforts are being extended to K-12 education and career pathways.
- Partnerships with nonprofits are supporting short-term training programs for roles that require verification training rather than an associate's degree.
- Georgia Power acts as a convener and supporter, connecting education partners to available resources.
- Training initiatives are utilizing existing automotive pathways in CTAE (Career, Technical and Agricultural Education) as well as advanced manufacturing pathways.
- The team is actively upskilling students interested in electric transportation careers.
- Upskilling and reskilling current automotive workers into electric vehicles will be a critical factor in workforce availability for incoming industry.
- Georgia is being highlighted nationally as the EV hub.
- New investments and jobs coming to communities are expected to drive economic growth for all Georgians.
- The team focuses on bridging the gap between business and industry by providing alternative career paths beyond four-year colleges.
- A section titled "Economic Development Education" is dated Jan 24, 2022.
