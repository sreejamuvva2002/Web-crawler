# Great Dane Limited Partnership

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2009-01-09

**Source:** [Great Dane Trailers to stop manufacturing in Savannah | Trailer Body Builders](https://www.trailer-bodybuilders.com/trailers/article/21733862/great-dane-trailers-to-stop-manufacturing-in-savannah)  ·  `page_055353`

## Facts

- Great Dane Trailers announced plans to close its original manufacturing facility in Savannah, Georgia.
- The plant closure is effective January 9, 2009.
- The company has begun searching for a new, more efficient plant site to replace the closed facility's capacity.
- Key factors behind the closure decision include the extended economic downturn affecting the transportation industry and the need for more efficient production.
- Phillip Pines, chief operating officer of Great Dane Limited Partnership, stated that the closing affects many family and friends and noted the plant's pivotal role in company history.
- Pines explained that the plant exhausted its ability to produce refrigerated trailers at a competitive price and emphasized that the closure does not reflect on employee caliber or work quality.
- The Savannah facility was originally built in 1919 for the expanding Savannah Blowpipe Company before becoming the starting ground for the first Great Dane trailers.
- For nearly a century, the Savannah plant supplied the transportation industry with complex, high-quality refrigerated (reefer) products and produced the first-ever factory finished refrigerated trailer.
- The company stated that advancements in technology for trailer design and production have placed increased demands on manufacturing capabilities.
- A modernized facility is planned to help maintain Great Dane's position as the leader in the refrigerated trailer market while meeting customer expectations at a competitive price.
- Approximately 270 plant hourly and office employees will be affected by the Savannah plant closing.
- The corporate office, which includes sales and marketing, design engineering, manufacturing engineering, research and development, retail accounting, MIS, corporate quality, and customer service, will not be affected by the closure.
- Until a new plant is established, production from the Savannah facility will be transferred to Great Dane's two other refrigerated trailer plants located in Wayne, Nebraska, and Brazil, Indiana.
