# Rivian Georgia Manufacturing Facility

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09

**Source:** [Rivian Breaks Ground On Its EV Plant Again Without Actually Breaking Ground | Carscoops](https://www.carscoops.com/2025/09/rivian-resumes-work-on-georgia-plant/)  ·  `page_001049`

## Facts

- Rivian broke ground on its Georgia plant, which had been paused in 2024.
- The company held a groundbreaking ceremony for the facility in Social Circle.
- Actual construction is not expected to begin until 2026.
- Vehicle production at the facility is scheduled to start in 2028.
- The plant will manufacture the R2 and R3 vehicle models.
- The facility aims for a combined annual production capacity of 400,000 units.
- Production will be split across two phases, with each phase providing 200,000 units of annual capacity.
- Vehicles produced at the Georgia plant will be sold in both domestic and international markets.
- The first vehicles from the Georgia facility are expected to appear in 2028, approximately two years after R2 manufacturing begins at Rivian's Normal, Illinois plant.
- The facility is expected to span approximately nine million square feet on nearly 2,000 acres of land.
- Rivian envisions the site will include recreational trails for employees and customers, as well as a dedicated "Rivian experience trail."
- Rivian was granted a $6.6 billion loan from the Department of Energy during the waning days of the Biden Administration.
- Stakeholders, including Georgia Republicans, have promised thousands of new jobs for the project.
- The project is expected to create 2,000 construction jobs and 7,500 plant jobs by 2030.
- Nearly 8,000 indirect jobs are also projected to be generated by the facility.
- Rivian stated that the new job openings could generate over $1 billion in annual labor income.
- This economic impact is expected to support suppliers, vendors, and small businesses in Jasper, Morgan, Newton and Walton Counties and the surrounding region.
- Georgia's electric vehicle landscape also includes Hyundai’s Metaplant, which builds the Ioniq 5 and Ioniq 9.
- Rivian CEO RJ Scaringe stated that the Georgia plant will cement the company's future there and help ensure America maintains its technology leadership and excellence in automobile manufacturing.
- Scaringe added that the facility will support Rivian's global expansion and provide the scale necessary to get millions of future drivers in their all-electric vehicles, both in the United States and overseas.
