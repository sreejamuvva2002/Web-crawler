# DAS Corp.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-09-06

**Source:** [DAS Corp. to Bring 300 Automotive Jobs to Metter • The Georgia Virtue](https://www.thegeorgiavirtue.com/local-news-south-georgia/das-corp-to-bring-300-automotive-jobs-to-metter/)  ·  `page_019147`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that DAS Corp. (DAS), an automotive parts company, will establish a new manufacturing facility in Metter.
- The new facility will create 300 new jobs.
- The new facility will generate more than $35 million in investment in Candler County.
- The initiative will involve collaborative indirect investments.
- Suppliers for the Hyundai Metaplant resulted in over $2 billion in investment last fiscal year alone, helping Georgia achieve a third straight year of record-breaking economic growth (Governor Brian Kemp).
- Georgia's logistics assets, including a reliable network of rail lines and highways, connect companies like DAS to key business partners across the state and to markets globally (Governor Brian Kemp).
- DAS was established in Korea in 1987.
- DAS specializes in designing and manufacturing seating systems, safety seating components, and seating structures.
- DAS is a key supplier for Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia (KMMG) and Hyundai Motor Group.
- The strategic location near prominent cities like Savannah and Macon, facilitating convenient access, played a pivotal role in the decision-making process (Sean Kim, Chief Operating Officer at DAS).
- Metter was deemed an ideal location in anticipation of its role as a global electric vehicle production hub due to its close proximity to Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (Sean Kim, Chief Operating Officer at DAS).
- The new DAS manufacturing facility is located in the Candler County Industrial Park in Metter.
- The facility will primarily produce automotive seat structures.
- Operations are projected to begin in the latter half of 2024.
- Over the course of the next five years, DAS will be recruiting for assembly technicians.
- DAS intends to establish collaborations with local employment services as part of its hiring process.
- The City of Metter noted the impact on the community will be transformative, stimulating investment in housing, creating other business opportunities, and improving the social economic wellbeing of citizenry (Metter Mayor Ed Boyd).
- Glyn Thrift, Chairman of the Candler County Board of Commissioners, thanked DAS for choosing Candler County as their new home to spur more economic development.
- Griff Lynch, President and CEO of the Georgia Ports Authority, stated the authority is proud to support the growing automotive manufacturing presence in the state to drive economic activity and jobs.
- Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy Alyce Thornhill represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The project was executed in partnership with the Candler County Industrial Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, Electric Cities of Georgia, and Georgia Quick Start.
- Georgia is well-positioned for the arrival of Hyundai’s supplier network due to years of consistent investment in site preparation, workforce solutions, and connectivity (GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson).
- DAS is another exciting addition to Georgia’s automotive community (GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson).
- For over a century, Georgia has fostered healthy industry practices, encouraged collaboration and innovation, and positioned the state as a leader in developing and harnessing emerging technologies for the evolving automotive and mobility industry.
- As the market transitions to electric vehicles, Georgia has pursued the entire supply chain, creating more than $25 billion in investments and 30,000 jobs since 2018.
- In fiscal year 2023, job creation in the automotive industry increased by 324 percent when compared to FY21.
- Suppliers attracted by Georgia’s mobility original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) resulted in over $2 billion in investment across the state in FY23.
- DAS Corp. maintains a strong focus on innovation and quality to consistently provide premier automotive seating solutions tailored to evolving customer needs.
- Strategic collaborations, including partnerships with Hyundai Motor Group and Kia Motors, continue to drive progress within the constantly evolving automotive industry.
- DAS Corp. garnered a total revenue of approximately $900 million USD during the fiscal year of 2022.
- The article is a press release from the Office of Governor Brian Kemp.
