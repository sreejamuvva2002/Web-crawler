# Panasonic Automotive Systems Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Creating "Joy in Motion" that connects with people— The challenge of becoming the world’s leading "Joy in Motion" design company — | Panasonic Automotive Systems Co., Ltd.](https://automotive.panasonic.com/en/studio/story/01)  ·  `page_047813`

## Facts

- In 2024, Panasonic Automotive Systems introduced its vision to become the world’s leading “Joy in Motion” design company.
- CEO Masashi Nagayasu will share the inspiration and outlook behind this new vision, expressing a commitment to creating comfort not only in vehicles but throughout society as a whole.
- The company launched as a business entity in 2022 with the mission of “creating a sustainable mobility society to improve lives for everyone.”
- The automotive industry is undergoing a once-in-a-century transformation addressing emissions-related environmental concerns, urban congestion, eliminating traffic accidents, and ensuring safe mobility spaces.
- The vision aims to make every form of movement an enjoyable experience by creatively designing solutions that seamlessly integrate into people’s daily lives.
- A major technological shift underway is toward Software-Defined Vehicles (SDV).
- Japan's Ministry of Economy, Trade, and Industry has identified SDVs as a key focus area in its 2024 mobility DX strategy to drive industry transformation.
- Panasonic anticipates that software-based vehicle development will accelerate, intensifying international competition.
- SDVs primarily enable faster development cycles by decoupling hardware from software, allowing vehicles to continuously update features like smartphones.
- When integrated with autonomous driving technology, SDVs will introduce new vehicle value propositions.
- Data generated by these vehicles will unlock new business opportunities and further transform the mobility landscape.
- Hardware ensures driving performance and safety, while software offers various values on top of that.
- The company plans to shift its mindset to propose that mobility exists even when not in motion, focusing on providing value inside parked cars through experiences like sharing instead of ownership.
- In 2024, Panasonic established a strategic partnership with Apollo Global Management, Inc., a leading investment fund based in New York.
- The collaboration aims to expand financial resources needed for software development and gain insights from Apollo’s investments in top-tier companies worldwide.
- The partnership is also intended to drive internal transformation of employees by encouraging self-reflection, challenging comfort zones, and maintaining an outward-focused mindset with agile management.
- Panasonic will develop “Joy in Motion” from multiple perspectives including safety and security, comfort, expanding vehicle possibilities, digitalization, and community engagement.
- The company operates as a B2B entity but embraces a B2B2C mindset by keeping real end-users in mind.
- A key focus is on how personalized their technology can become, incorporating varying perceptions of comfort and concern from passenger to passenger.
- In 2024, Panasonic introduced the “WELL Cabin” to create a movement experience that touches the heart, offering a truly comfortable cabin space.
- The WELL Cabin features state-of-the-art hardware including a 55-inch transparent OLED display, 3D high-resolution speakers, built-in seat sound exciters, and an aroma diffuser.
- Panasonic leverages extensive technical expertise in audio-visual devices to deliver in-car experiences that feel like a living room in motion.
- Advanced image processing and sensor technologies are deeply integrated into various onboard devices such as cameras, electronic mirrors, and head-up displays.
- The company collaborates with the city of Muroran on MaaS (Mobility as a Service) to enhance services for residents.
- Through mobility solutions, they are tackling regional challenges such as optimizing taxi operations and improving waste collection efficiency.
- Masashi Nagayasu serves as the Representative Director, President, and Chief Executive Officer (CEO) of Panasonic Automotive Systems Co., Ltd.
