## Facts

- The Inaugural Battery Belt Charging Forward Forum was held on Tuesday, August 26 in Atlanta, GA.
- The forum was hosted by the Georgia Network for Electric Mobility (GNEM) at the University of Georgia with guest experts from the American EV Jobs Alliance, Kia Georgia, MAGNET, and the Salata Institute for Climate and Sustainability.
- This closed-door session was one of the first regional convenings in the Southeast dedicated specifically to the link between charging policy, infrastructure deployment, and consumer adoption challenges.
- The expert roundtable brought together representatives from utilities, academia, OEMs, industry, and policymakers from Georgia, Alabama, Mississippi, and South Carolina.
- Michael E. Murphy, Executive Director of the American EV Jobs Alliance, shared voter survey data showing that charging reliability is the top concern for EV adoption.
- Michael E. Murphy emphasized that framing electric vehicles around jobs and competitiveness resonates most strongly with the public.
- Mike Oatridge, CEO of MAGNET, outlined MAGNET’s tri-state tech hub and research partnership.
- Southern Company and the University of Georgia/GNEM are founding members of MAGNET.
- Mike Oatridge highlighted how charging access and supply chain resilience directly support U.S. competitiveness and national security.
- Stuart Countess, President & CEO of Kia Georgia, Inc., joined a panel to discuss OEM priorities for scaling charging access.
- Kevin Neal, Director of External Affairs for EV Sales at Ford Motor Company, joined the same panel.
- The panel was moderated by Gabrielle Pierre, GNEM Director of Strategy and Partnerships.
- The panel discussed ensuring that infrastructure keeps pace with manufacturing investment.
- Dr. Elaine Buckberg, Senior Fellow at the Salata Institute for Climate and Sustainability at Harvard University and former Chief Economist at General Motors, presented data-driven insights on charging reliability and maintenance.
- Dr. Omar Isaac Asensio, Associate Professor at the Georgia Institute of Technology's School of Public Policy and Director of the Data Science & Policy Lab, presented alongside Dr. Buckberg.
- The speakers discussed opportunities for model legislation to accelerate infrastructure deployment through mandating data availability on reliability, maintenance, and uptime.
- Stephanie Valdez Streaty, Director of Industry Insights at Cox Automotive Inc., delivered an EV market update.
- Stephanie Valdez Streaty underscored how charging infrastructure impacts both new and used EV adoption and long-term consumer confidence.
- Roundtable themes included cross-state collaboration to reinforce policy alignment across state lines for sustained EV adoption, advanced automotive manufacturing, and regional economic development.
- Roundtable themes recognized charging as a key linchpin for consumer uptake and continued manufacturing competitiveness.
- Roundtable themes focused on leveraging academic, voter, and industry insights to guide policy and improve reliability.
- Roundtable themes emphasized ensuring OEMs, utilities, and policymakers are aligned in building public confidence and scaling infrastructure.
- Gabrielle Pierre stated that charging infrastructure will likely form the backbone of the Southeast’s role as the nation’s leading hub for electric and future mobility manufacturing and innovation.
- Gabrielle Pierre expressed excitement about leading in seeding regional partnerships and collaboration opportunities focused on key unlocks for the industry.
- Georgia public service commissioner Tim Echols attended the meeting.
- Tim Echols stated that batteries have become the new generator providing back-up in case of storm outage, and with more vehicles able to provide power to the home, it is important.
- Tim Echols noted he has had eight EVs over the last 13 years and the products continue to get better.
- The forum marked a critical step in aligning public and private sector leaders to meet electric vehicle drivers’ needs while reinforcing the Southeast’s leadership in electric and broader advanced mobility innovation and manufacturing.
- Elaine Buckberg stated that making public charging seamless for EV drivers is a crucial enabler for EV adoption, which means jobs in the Southeast.
- Elaine Buckberg noted that conversations at the forum position the Southeast Battery Belt region to lead in charging, as well as EV and battery production.
- GNEM will continue convening regional and national expert roundtables in the months and years ahead to strengthen collaboration across the region and beyond.
- The Georgia Network for Electric Mobility (GNEM) is a statewide strategy engine, public-private partnership network, industry consortium, and think tank embedded at the University of Georgia.
- GNEM's founding partners include Georgia Power, Cox Automotive, Kia Georgia, and the Georgia Department of Transportation.
- GNEM works across research, workforce development, community engagement, and industry partnerships to advance electric and future mobility in Georgia and across the Battery Belt.
