# LG Energy Solution and Hyundai Motor Group Joint Venture Battery Plant

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-05-26

**Source:** [Hyundai and LG announce $4.3 billion plant in Georgia to build batteries for electric vehicles | AP News](https://apnews.com/article/hyundai-lg-electric-vehicles-batteries-georgia-db44d911b3dae0fbf454cb53503ce84c)  ·  `page_000253`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution announced they will build a $4.3 billion electric battery plant as part of Hyundai’s new electric vehicle assembly plant in southeast Georgia.
- The companies will split the investment for the project.
- Production at the plant is slated to start as soon as late 2025.
- Hyundai Motor Co. CEO Jaehoon Chang said the battery plant would “create a strong foundation to lead the global EV transition.”
- Chang explained the company wants to speed up efforts to produce electrified Hyundai and Kia vehicles in North America.
- Chang stated, “Hyundai Motor Group is focusing on its electrification efforts to secure a leadership position in the global auto industry.”
- In 2022, Hyundai said it would invest $5.5 billion to assemble electric vehicles and batteries in Ellabell, just west of Savannah.
- The Ellabell site is supposed to have 8,100 employees.
- The Ellabell site is slated to begin producing vehicles in 2025.
- Garrison Douglas, a spokesperson for Republican Gov. Brian Kemp, said the 3,000-job battery plant would be part of the 8,100 overall jobs.
- Douglas said the $4.3 billion investment would be part of the previously announced $5.5 billion total.
- The Hyundai/LG plant is supposed to be able to supply batteries for 300,000 electric vehicles per year.
- This capacity matches the initial projected production of the adjoining vehicle assembly plant.
- Hyundai has said the Georgia plant could later expand to build 500,000 vehicles annually.
- Gov. Brian Kemp said, “This is exactly what we envisioned when Georgia landed the Hyundai Metaplant in May of last year, and this project is the latest milestone in Georgia’s path to becoming the EV capital of the nation.”
- Auto parts suppliers have pledged to invest more than $2 billion in the region around the Hyundai site.
- Those auto parts suppliers have pledged to hire 4,800 people in the region around the Hyundai site.
- The announcements are part of an electric vehicle and battery land rush across the United States.
- Under the U.S. Inflation Reduction Act, EVs must be assembled in North America to qualify for a full $7,500 EV tax credit.
- A certain percentage of battery parts and minerals must come from North America or a U.S. free trade partner to qualify for the full $7,500 EV tax credit under the IRA.
- Currently, no Hyundai or Kia vehicles are eligible for the tax credit unless they are leased.
- Hyundai opposed having foreign-made vehicles excluded from the tax credit, in part because it is building American factories.
- Gov. Brian Kemp has supported that position on the tax credit exclusion.
- Democratic Sen. Jon Ossoff said Hyundai should wait until it is producing vehicles domestically, using American-made batteries.
- Sen. Jon Ossoff told reporters in an online news conference Friday morning that as soon as those vehicles are produced in Georgia, they can be eligible for the credits through the incentives in the IRA.
- Ossoff stated, “These manufacturing incentives are attracting and accelerating billions of dollars of investments in jobs and advanced energy and electric vehicle production capacity here in the state of Georgia.”
- LG said this would be its seventh battery plant in operation or under construction in the U.S.
- LG is concentrating efforts to expand production in the country, citing federal incentives as a lure for manufacturers.
- This is the second huge electric battery plant that Hyundai is partnering to build in Georgia.
- Hyundai and SK On, a unit of South Korea’s SK Group, announced in December they would jointly invest $4 billion to $5 billion to build a new plant northwest of Atlanta.
- The Hyundai/SK On plant will supply electric batteries for Hyundai and Kia electric vehicles assembled in the U.S.
- That plant, in Cartersville, is planned to begin production in 2025.
- The Cartersville plant is projected to employ a projected 3,500 people.
- Hyundai will need batteries for more than just vehicles made in Ellabell.
- The company is already assembling electric vehicles at its plant in Montgomery, Alabama.
- In April, Hyundai announced it would start assembling its electric Kia EV9 large SUV at the Kia plant in West Point, Georgia.
- Partnering with LG and SK also will diversify Hyundai’s supplier base.
- The partnerships give the automaker more than one battery manufacturer from which to buy.
- Because the battery plant is part of the overall Hyundai complex, no additional incentives would be offered for it.
- The state of Georgia and local governments already have pledged $1.8 billion in tax breaks and other incentives.
- Greg LeRoy, executive director of Good Jobs First, said the $1.8 billion is the largest subsidy package a U.S. state has ever promised an automotive plant.
- Good Jobs First is described as a group skeptical of subsidies to private companies.
