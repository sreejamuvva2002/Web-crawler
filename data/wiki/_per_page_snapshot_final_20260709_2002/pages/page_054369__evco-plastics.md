# EVCO Plastics

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [New EVCO Plastics Facility to Advance Medical Molding Opportunities | MoldMaking Technology](https://www.moldmakingtechnology.com/news/new-evco-plastics-facility-to-advance-medical-molding-opportunities)  ·  `page_054369`

## Facts

- Custom plastics manufacturing company EVCO Plastics (based in DeForest, Wisconsin) recently purchased a new facility near CAL 1, located outside of Calhoun, Georgia.
- The new CAL 2 facility is forty thousand square feet and will feature white room and liquid silicone rubber (LSR) molding capabilities.
- CAL 2 adds 40,000 square feet to EVCO’s facility footprint, bringing the company to over one million square feet of space across ten plants globally.
- The new facility enables the company to introduce white room and LSR molding opportunities to meet demand from medical and commercial lighting customers.
- Kate Bashir, director of strategy, stated that after assessing customer needs and industry developments, it was the right time to expand capabilities.
- CAL 2 will help meet customers’ high expectations and long-term production goals.
- The new facility has room for 20 injection molding machines up to 500 tons, along with a separate room for new LSR machines.
- CAL 2 features two bridge cranes for safely and efficiently setting molds in presses.
- CAL 2 includes four material silos.
- CAL 2 contains a 22,000-square-foot white room.
- CAL 2 contains a 2,100-square-foot LSR production room.
- CAL 2 contains a 725-square-foot metrology room.
- EVCO states that CAL 2 will be a food-grade-compliant facility featuring highly automated lights-out production.
- The inclusion of a white room allows for additional medical molding opportunities, representing a new capability for the company.
- The facility will be fully operational in fall 2021.
- In addition to CAL 2, EVCO is currently adding 13,000 square feet to its AMP facility in DeForest, Wisconsin.
- The Wisconsin expansion enables EVCO to adjust the facility’s footprint to add more machines and increases the amount of raw materials and finished goods that can be stored onsite.
- Gary Fortier, director of U.S. operations, stated that reaching over one million square feet is a huge milestone for the company.
- Fortier noted that as customers’ needs grow, so does the company, and the new space allows them to create more opportunities to meet ongoing customer and industry requirements.
