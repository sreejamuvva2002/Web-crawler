## Facts

- VPS (Vehicle Protection Structures) partnered with Kia Georgia to complete a large-scale hail protection and solar canopy system at Kia’s West Point manufacturing facility.
- The West Point manufacturing plant includes 3.2 million square feet of automotive hail protection.
- The West Point manufacturing plant includes 10 MW of solar capacity.
- On Feb. 24, 2026, Kia Georgia team members marked the start of production of the all-new 2027 Kia Telluride at its West Point facility.
- Kia Georgia commemorated the production of the state of Georgia’s first-ever HEV on Feb. 24, 2026.
- Kia Georgia marked the plant’s 5 millionth vehicle milestone on Feb. 24, 2026.
- Eligible Kia owners received $500 in assistance for the purchase or lease of select new Kia vehicles between October 4, 2024, and January 2, 2025.
- On October 4, 2024, Kia America and Kia Georgia, Inc., donated $250,000 to the American Red Cross to aid in relief efforts.
- The 2025 Kia EV9 is the first electric vehicle assembled in the state of Georgia.
- The 2025 Kia EV9 joins the award-winning Telluride, Sorento, and Sportage SUVs currently assembled at Kia’s West Point facility.
- On May 30, 2024, Kia Georgia celebrated a historic milestone at its assembly plant in West Point.
- The Sportage is the fourth model produced by Kia Georgia, joining the Telluride, Sorento, and K5.
- On February 1, 2022, Kia Georgia officially began production of the all-new 2023 Sportage subcompact SUV at its facility in West Point, Georgia.
- On Dec. 17, 2021, Kia Georgia, Inc. announced the retirement of President & CEO Jangsoo (Jason) Shin, effective December 31, 2021.
- Jangsoo (Jason) Shin led the Georgia facility since 2017 and oversaw the inaugural launches of the Telluride and K5 models.
- On August 20, 2021, Kia Georgia, Inc. reopened its online application process for hourly production positions for direct hires for the first time since 2011.
- The plant completed a third wave of hiring prior to reopening applications in August 2021.
- On March 30, 2021, Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia, Inc. (KMMG) donated $50,000 to the American Red Cross to assist with tornado relief efforts across the region.
- KMMG team members raised additional funds for the American Red Cross through an internal channel.
- Chairman Mong-Koo Chung was inaugurated as Honorary Chairman of the Group, and Euisun Chung was recognized for successful leadership as Executive Vice Chairman over the past two years.
- On September 24, 2020, Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia (KMMG) partnered with the Technical College System of Georgia (TCSG).
- KMMG donated 26 quality assessment vehicles to TCSG for their automotive programs as an education tool.
