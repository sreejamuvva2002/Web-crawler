# Blue Bird Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2019-07-09

**Source:** [Blue Bird Unveils New State-of-the-Art Paint Facility - School Transportation News](https://stnonline.com/industry-releases/blue-bird-unveils-new-state-of-the-art-paint-facility/)  ·  `page_052882`

## Facts

- Blue Bird Corporation unveiled a new state-of-the-art paint facility at its Fort Valley, Georgia, assembly plant during a ribbon-cutting ceremony.
- The new paint facility is 60,000-square-foot and utilizes the latest robotic technology.
- Buses roll into the paint facility on a conveyor, meeting programmed robots that spray each bus per engineering specifications.
- Painted buses pass through ovens to set the paint before proceeding through multiple inspection bays and mounting to a chassis.
- The facility operates as an automotive-grade paint facility with a full clean-room environment that blocks out debris.
- The facility features a zero-to-landfill design where all paint overspray is captured, dried, and sent to a power generation plant to be used as fuel.
- Blue Bird has made a significant investment to build and operate the new facility.
- Trey Jenkins, senior vice president of operations at Blue Bird Corporation, stated the new paint facility can paint a bus three times faster than manual methods with a higher paint transfer rate and consistent coverage.
- Jenkins noted that the technology will dramatically reduce the time it takes to paint a bus, allowing the company to meet customer needs at a faster rate and increase efficiencies within production lines.
- Phil Horlock, president and CEO of Blue Bird Corporation, stated the facility illustrates Blue Bird’s commitment to technological innovation that drives quality, value and efficiency.
- Horlock added excitement over the positive impact this investment will have for customers and the specialized jobs it will bring to the local community.
- Buses have recently started to use the painting process at the new facility, which will be fully operational later in 2019.
- Blue Bird (NASDAQ: BLBD) is the leading independent designer and manufacturer of school buses with more than 550,000 buses sold since its formation in 1927.
- Approximately 180,000 Blue Bird buses are in operation today.
- Blue Bird distinguishes itself by a singular focus on the design, engineering, manufacture, and sale of school buses and related parts.
- It is the only manufacturer of chassis and body production specifically designed for school bus applications.
- Blue Bird is the market leader in alternative fuel applications with its propane-powered, electric and compressed natural gas-powered school buses.
- Blue Bird manufactures school buses at two facilities in Fort Valley, Georgia.
- The Micro Bird joint venture operates a manufacturing facility in Drummondville, Quebec, Canada.
- Service and after-market parts are distributed from Blue Bird’s parts distribution center located in Delaware, Ohio.
