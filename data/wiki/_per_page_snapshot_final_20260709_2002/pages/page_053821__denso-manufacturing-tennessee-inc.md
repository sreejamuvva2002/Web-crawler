# DENSO Manufacturing Tennessee, Inc.

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2025-03-25

**Source:** [DENSO Mfg. Tennessee Recognized for Workplace Safety Record - AftermarketNews](https://www.aftermarketnews.com/denso-tennessee-workplace-safety-record/)  ·  `page_053821`

## Facts

- DENSO Manufacturing Tennessee, Inc. (DMTN), located in Maryville, was recognized by the Tennessee Occupational Safety and Health Administration (TOSHA) division of the Department of Labor and Workforce Development (TDLWD) with its Volunteer Safety Through Accountability and Recognition (STAR) Award on March 25, 2025.
- The Volunteer STAR Award is Tennessee’s highest honor for workplace safety and health and is a nationally recognized program patterned after the OSHA Voluntary Protection Program (VPP).
- By receiving the award, DMTN is considered a Volunteer STAR site.
- TDLWD Deputy Commissioner Dewayne Scott stated that DENSO rose to the challenge and not only met but exceeded TOSHA’s high standards for workplace safety in Tennessee, requiring tremendous effort and determination from everyone at the facility.
- Chuntao Ye, president of DMTN and leader of DENSO’s Peace of Mind Business Group, said safety is their top priority whether in the workplace or products, thanked Deputy Commissioner Scott and TOSHA for the recognition, and appreciated their continued partnership in enhancing employee safety.
- Ye expressed gratitude to dedicated team members for earning the honor, noting it was a difficult achievement given the location’s size and complex operations, and stated they will continue to lead into a new era of smarter, safer mobility.
- Team members at the DENSO site manufacture a range of products that are shaping the future of mobility in society, including advanced electrification and safety solutions.
- This is the first time TOSHA has honored DMTN with the Volunteer STAR award.
- Previously, TOSHA awarded the distinction to DENSO Manufacturing Athens Tennessee, Inc. (DMAT).
