# SK Battery America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-02

**Source:** [SK Battery Exceeds Hiring Goal In Georgia Two Years Ahead Of Schedule | Doc and the Block](https://docandtheblock.com/sk-battery-exceeds-hiring-goal-in-georgia-two-years-ahead-of-schedule/)  ·  `page_049973`

## Facts

- SK Battery America (SKBA) has exceeded its hiring goal of 2,600 employees two years ahead of schedule for its two new manufacturing facilities in Commerce, Georgia.
- The hiring milestone was reported according to a press release from Georgia Governor Brian Kemp’s office.
- SK Battery America met its initial hiring goal at the end of 2022.
- The company is further expanding its workforce to approximately 3,000 employees over the next year to meet growing demand for electric vehicles.
- SK Battery America announced its first project in Georgia in 2018 with an initial planned investment of $1.6 billion.
- Since 2019, SK Battery America has invested $2.6 billion to build two battery manufacturing facilities in Jackson County.
- The Jackson County facilities are built to supply electric vehicles, including the Ford F-150 Lightning and the Volkswagen ID.4.
- SK Battery America started mass production at its Georgia facilities in early 2022.
- Governor Brian Kemp described SK Battery America as one of the first companies to put Georgia on the e-mobility map.
- A week prior to this announcement, SK Battery America announced plans to open a new regional IT hub in Roswell, Georgia.
- The new regional IT hub in Roswell, Georgia, will create 200 tech jobs.
- The new regional IT hub in Roswell, Georgia, will invest about $19 million over the next few years.
- Governor Brian Kemp referred to Georgia as the No. 1 state for business while praising SK Battery America’s continued growth.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that SK Battery America brings jobs of the future benefiting Georgians in manufacturing and will tap into tech talent from top-ranking universities.
