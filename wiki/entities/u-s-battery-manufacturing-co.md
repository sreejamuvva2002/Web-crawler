# U.S. Battery Manufacturing Co.

**Type:** Company  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[battery-cell-manufacturing|Battery Cell Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** Augusta, Georgia, Georgia, United States

## Overview

The U.S. Department of Labor's Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA) cited U.S. Battery Manufacturing Co., a Georgia battery manufacturer, for lead exposure, arsenic hazards, unguarded machinery, and other safety violations at its Augusta facility, proposing $115,594 in penalties.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Battery manufacturer operating in Georgia, part of the electric vehicle supply chain.

## Key facts

- The company faces $115,594 in proposed penalties for safety violations.
- Employees were exposed to lead levels exceeding the permissible exposure limit and arsenic above the action level.
- U.S. Battery Manufacturing Co. failed to record medical removals on OSHA's 300 log.
- The firm did not conduct required training, implement a medical surveillance program, or properly guard equipment.
- OSHA cited the manufacturer for unguarded machinery and other safety hazards.
- The investigation was launched under OSHA's Regional Emphasis Program on Lead.
- The company has 15 business days to comply with the citations, request an informal conference, or contest the findings before the Occupational Safety and Health Review Commission.
- "Elevated lead levels can cause debilitating and permanent health issues," said OSHA Atlanta-East Area Director William Fulcher.
