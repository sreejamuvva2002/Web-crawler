# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-11-07

**Source:** [Hyundai’s EV manufacturing campus draws 3 more suppliers to Georgia | Manufacturing Dive](https://www.manufacturingdive.com/news/hyundai-metaplant-draws-3-more-suppliers-hwashin-hyundai-industrial-daechang-seat-corp-usa-georgia/698692/)  ·  `page_018910`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group’s Georgia supplier list expanded over the past few weeks with three more auto part makers announcing investments to establish manufacturing facilities in the state.
- Hyundai Industrial Co., a subsidiary of Hyundai, invested over $24 million to acquire a building in Newnan.
- The Newnan facility investment creates 100 jobs.
- Hyundai Industrial Co. plans to retrofit the Newnan facility to produce armrests and headrests for Hyundai’s upcoming $5.5 billion EV metaplant in Ellabell as well as other OEMs.
- South Korea-based supplier Hwashin plans to establish a $176 million plant in Dublin.
- The Hwashin plant investment creates over 460 jobs.
- Hwashin is a tier-1 supplier that specializes in crafting chassis and body parts for Hyundai, its subsidiary Kia, and other OEMs.
- Production at the Hwashin plant is expected to begin in late 2025.
- Daechang Seat Corp. USA announced an investment of $72.5 million to build a manufacturing facility in Savannah.
- The Daechang Seat Corp. USA facility creates over 500 jobs.
- Daechang Seat Corp. USA specializes in automotive seat frames.
- Daechang Seat Corp. USA will join previously announced suppliers Seoyon E-HWA and PHA at the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center.
- The Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center is located near Hyundai’s metaplant, which remains under construction.
- More than 10 Hyundai suppliers have set up shop in Georgia.
- The latest investments bring an estimated $2.5 billion from a total of 14 suppliers to the Peach State.
- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp stated in a Nov. 1 news release that the growing EV network is creating over 6,000 jobs.
- Hyundai Motor Group invested additional funds to expand its supplier network amid ongoing construction of its EV manufacturing campus.
- In September, Hyundai and LG Energy Solution invested an additional $2 billion toward their joint battery cell manufacturing plant.
- The total investment for the Hyundai-LG Energy Solution joint battery cell plant is now $4.3 billion.
- Production at the joint battery cell factory is slated to begin at the end of 2025.
- The joint battery cell factory will have enough capacity to produce 300,000 EVs annually.
- The joint battery cell factory will be located next to Hyundai’s manufacturing campus.
- Hyundai broke ground on its manufacturing campus over a year ago.
- Construction has recently begun on Hyundai’s production-related buildings at the campus.
- Foundation work for the production-related buildings is nearly complete, according to the company last month.
- The Hyundai manufacturing campus covers nearly 3,000 acres.
- The megasite will use renewable resources to power its mass production operations.
- Using renewable resources helps Hyundai reach its goal of becoming carbon neutral by 2045.
