# LG Energy Solution Vertech and Qcells

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Press Release | Media | LG](https://www.lgcorp.com/media/release/29835)  ·  `page_001253`

## Facts

- LG Energy Solution Vertech and Qcells announced a multi-year commitment to install reliable, affordable, American-made storage products for Qcells’ development projects across the country.
- The agreement includes 5GWh of lithium-ion energy storage projects for utility-scale sites, along with lifecycle services from LG Energy Solution Vertech.
- The joint projects are scheduled for installation between 2028 and 2030.
- All projects under the agreement will meet U.S. domestic content requirements.
- In May 2024, Qcells previously entered into battery purchase agreements with LG Energy Solution Vertech totaling 4.8 GWh of BESS projects developed across the U.S.
- The partnership secures energy storage systems meeting domestic content requirements because LG Energy Solution’s battery energy storage products are manufactured in Michigan and Qcells’ solar panels are made in Georgia.
- Leveraging made-in-America products supports U.S. manufacturing jobs, strengthens the nation’s clean-energy supply chains, enhances grid reliability, and reduces exposure to trade risks and tariffs.
- Chris Hodrick serves as the CEO of Qcells EPC.
- Qcells operates the largest solar panel manufacturing facility of its kind in the United States, located in Dalton, Georgia.
- The company is constructing a second factory in Cartersville, Georgia, dedicated to manufacturing ingots, wafers, cells, and finished panels.
- The Cartersville facility represents the largest investment in U.S. solar history aimed at creating an integrated, sustainable domestic supply chain.
- Once fully operational, Qcells’ annual solar panel production capacity in the U.S. will reach 8.4 gigawatts.
- This production capacity equates to nearly 46,000 panels manufactured every day.
- The daily output is sufficient to power more than 1.3 million American homes each year.
- Jaehong Park serves as the CEO and President at LG Energy Solution Vertech.
- The energy storage systems in this commitment utilize LG Energy Solution’s containerized lithium-ion battery solutions with LFP cells manufactured at U.S. facilities.
- LG Energy Solution is currently manufacturing up to 16.5 GWh of battery energy storage cells in Holland, Michigan.
- LG Energy Solution’s production across sites in North America is set to ramp up to approximately 50GWh by the end of 2026.
