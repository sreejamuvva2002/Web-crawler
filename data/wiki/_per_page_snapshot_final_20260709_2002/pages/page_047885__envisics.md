# Envisics

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2021-01-08

**Source:** [Holographic startup Envisics partners with Panasonic to fast-track in-car AR tech | TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2021/01/08/holographic-startup-envisics-partners-with-panasonic-to-fast-track-in-car-ar-tech/)  ·  `page_047885`

## Facts

- U.K.-based holographic technology startup Envisics reached an agreement with Panasonic Automotive Systems to jointly develop and commercialize a new generation of head-up displays for cars, trucks and SUVs.
- Panasonic Automotive Systems is a Tier 1 automotive supplier and a division of Panasonic Corporation of North America.
- The head-up displays are units integrated in the dash of a vehicle that project images onto the windshield to aid drivers with navigation and provide other alerts.
- The Panasonic HUDs will use Envisics holographic technology.
- The deal was announced ahead of the virtual 2021 CES tech trade show.
- The announcement follows Envisics’ $50 million Series B funding round and news that its tech will be integrated in the upcoming Cadillac Lyriq electric vehicle.
- The funding round brought Envisics a valuation of more than $250 million.
- Investors in the funding round included Hyundai Mobis, GM Ventures, SAIC Ventures and Van Tuyl Companies.
- Envisics’ technology foundation originated from founder and CEO Dr. Jamieson Christmas’ PhD studies at Cambridge University more than 15 years ago.
- The technology electronically manipulates the speed of light to enable images to appear three-dimensional.
- The company has secured more than 250 patents and has another 160 pending certification.
- Envisics is solely focused upon the automotive application of holography.
- The company's first generation technology is already integrated in more than 150,000 Jaguar Land Rover vehicles.
- The new agreement aims to combine Panasonic’s expertise in optical design and its global reach as a Tier 1 supplier with Envisics’ technology to bring holography into the mainstream.
- Mass production of vehicles using the technology is slated for 2023.
- Founder and CEO Dr. Jamieson Christmas stated: 'This is very much about part of our business plan, you know the Series B funding round we undertook was about scaling the business and enabling us to move forward as we enter the market.'
- Christmas added that engaging in partnerships with Tier 1 suppliers is part of the plan to deliver products to market, noting this agreement is the first of its kind.
- The head-up displays will feature high resolution, wide color gamut and large images that can be overlaid upon reality.
- The technology can project information at multiple distances simultaneously.
- Short-term applications include augmented reality navigation, highlighting the lane the driver is supposed to be in, and safety applications.
- Future applications for autonomous driving could include entertainment and video conferencing.
- The technology could also be used for night vision applications by overlaying enhanced information upon a dark road to clarify the road path and obstacles.
