# Aurubis Augusta Smelter

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2023-05-03

**Source:** [Aurubis decides to double Georgia smelter's capacity - Resource Recycling](https://resource-recycling.com/e-scrap/2023/05/03/aurubis-decides-to-double-georgia-smelters-capacity/)  ·  `page_017303`

## Facts

- Aurubis is spending an additional $276 million to double the metals recovery capacity of its secondary smelter in Georgia.
- The Germany-headquartered metals company now plans to invest a total of 640 million euros ($707 million) to build the smelter in Augusta, Ga.
- After adding a second recycling module, the plant will have a throughput capacity of 180,000 tons per year.
- The project was previously planned to cost 300 million euros ($331 million) and have a throughput of 90,000 tons per year.
- Aurubis subsidiary Aurubis Richmond broke ground on the Augusta plant in June 2022.
- The Aurubis board decided in December to boost capital investment in the plant, which will recycle printed circuit boards, cables, and other scrap materials.
- On March 27, the company announced it signed a contract with German equipment manufacturer SMS Group for the second recycling module.
- The increased spending is attributed to the additional recycling system, inflation, and adjustments for infrastructure costs.
- Specifically, the company is allocating an additional 250 million euros ($276 million) for the second processing system and 90 million euros ($99 million) for infrastructure cost increases and inflation.
- Production at the Augusta plant is now scheduled to begin in 2026.
- The company forecasts the facility will generate about 170 million euros ($188 million) in EBITDA starting in the 2026-27 fiscal year.
- Aurubis subsidiary Aurubis Richmond received air quality permits from Georgia authorities last year.
- Environmental group Savannah Riverkeeper opposed issuing the permits for the facility.
- Technology details are outlined in a Georgia Department of Natural Resources air permit narrative document.
- SMS Group will supply a second processing module featuring a top-blown rotary converter as its core technology.
- The top-blown rotary converter processes complex metals-bearing fractions to recover copper, nickel, tin, and precious metals in downstream steps.
- The plant will produce blister copper that will be processed into industrial and precious metals at the company’s European smelter sites.
- Some of the final products from the Augusta facility will be sold directly back into the U.S. market.
- The Augusta equipment is modular, allowing for future addition of components.
- Aurubis CEO Roland Harings stated that the modular system allows the company to plan a prudent market strategy and respond flexibly to needs in the US.
- Aurubis CEO Roland Harings noted that Aurubis will benefit from constant market growth in the US.
- The first stage of the plant is scheduled to come on-line in the first half of 2024.
- The second module is scheduled to come on-line in 2026.
