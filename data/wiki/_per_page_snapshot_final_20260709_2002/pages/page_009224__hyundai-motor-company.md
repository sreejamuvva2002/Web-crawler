# Hyundai Motor Company

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-18

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Company unveiled its 2030 vision and product roadmap at the 2025 CEO Investor Day in New York.
- The company aims for 5.55 million global vehicle sales by 2030.
- Hyundai plans to achieve 3.3 million electrified vehicle sales by 2030.
- The plan includes a comprehensive lineup of hybrid models and region-specific EVs, such as the IONIQ 3 for Europe and India’s first locally designed EV.
- José Muñoz, President and CEO of Hyundai Motor Company, stated that Hyundai is positioned to win in the industry's transformation through compelling products, manufacturing flexibility, technology leadership, outstanding dealer partners, and global scale.
- Hyundai's strategy includes launching Extended Range EVs (EREVs) by 2027.
- These EREVs will offer more than 600 miles of driving range to bridge the transition to full electrification.
- The company is investing $2.7 billion to expand production capacity.
- This investment will create 3,000 new jobs in Georgia.
- The job creation and investment are part of the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America expansion.
- Hyundai plans to produce more than 80 percent of vehicles sold in the United States domestically by 2030.
- The company aims to increase its domestic supply chain content from 60 percent to 80 percent.
- The Genesis luxury brand will introduce EREV, hybrid, and BEV powertrains across its lineup.
- Genesis targets annual sales of 350,000 units by 2030.
- Hyundai Motor’s CFO, Seung Jo (Scott) Lee, announced a KRW 77.3 trillion investment plan over five years from 2026 to 2030.
- The investment aims to strengthen global competitiveness through the development of software talent and strategic investments.
- Hyundai's comprehensive strategy positions it as a leader in the automotive industry’s transformation towards electrification and technological innovation.
