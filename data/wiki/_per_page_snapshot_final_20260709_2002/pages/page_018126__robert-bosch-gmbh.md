# Robert Bosch GmbH

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [The 2024 business year: Bosch held back by market developments - Bosch Media Service US](https://us.bosch-press.com/pressportal/us/en/press-release-26496.html)  ·  `page_018126`

## Facts

- Bosch generated sales revenue of 90.5 billion euros in 2024, with an EBIT margin from operations of 3.5 percent.
- Stefan Hartung, chairman of the board of management of Robert Bosch GmbH, stated that growth markets such as electromobility developed much more slowly than expected, negatively impacting business development due to lack of sales and underutilized capacity.
- By 2030, Bosch aims to be among the leading suppliers in its areas of business in key markets, striving for average annual growth of between 6 and 8 percent with a margin of at least 7 percent.
- Bosch is acquiring the HVAC business of Johnson Controls and Hitachi for around 8 billion U.S. dollars to expand its presence in growth markets such as the U.S. and Asia.
- Bosch sold large parts of its Building Technologies division’s product business for security and communications technology to focus on systems integration.
- In North America, Bosch ended its 2024 fiscal year with $17.4 billion in consolidated sales, representing growth of around six percent compared to the previous year.
- Paul Thomas, president of Bosch in North America, stated that North America and the United States specifically is an important growth market for Bosch.
- The Mobility business sector achieved $10.7 billion USD in North America in 2024, a five percent increase from 2023.
- Bosch continues to invest in and book customer wins related to technologies supporting regional needs such as trucks/SUVs and hybrid technology.
- Bosch is progressing on plans to manufacture the second generation silicon carbide chips on 200mm wafers in Roseville, California in 2026.
- In Energy and Building Technology, preliminary results in North America showed $1.9 billion USD, a 46 percent increase from 2023, including plans to acquire HVAC solutions business for residential and light commercial buildings from Johnson Controls expected to close during 2025.
- For the Industrial Technology business sector, Bosch posted $1.4 billion USD in North America in 2024, a 12 percent decrease year-over-year.
- Electromobility, hydrogen, and sustainable technologies remain a growth business and focus of innovation for Bosch.
- Bosch is testing a cryogenic pump in the U.S. that compresses up to 600 kilograms of liquid hydrogen per hour, allowing trucks to fill up with enough hydrogen for the next 1,000 kilometers in just 10 minutes.
- Around 5,000 AI experts at Bosch are working on making AI suitable for everyday use, including an AI-assisted emergency call service for elevator users.
- Bosch expects to generate sales of more than 6 billion euros with software and services by the beginning of the next decade, with two-thirds coming from the Mobility business sector.
- Bosch is advancing software-defined mobility through systems like Vehicle Motion Management, which enables brake-by-wire braking systems where an electronic brake pedal operates without a mechanical connection.
- As of December 31, 2024, the Bosch Group employed approximately 417,900 associates worldwide, nearly 3 percent lower than the previous year (-11,500).
- In Germany, headcount fell by around 3 percent (-4,400) to just over 129,800 as of December 31, 2024.
- Markus Forschner, member of the board of management and chief financial officer of Robert Bosch GmbH, stated that worldwide growth will remain moderate and the global economy is not expected to pick up again before 2026.
- Bosch assumes the economy will grow by just 2½ percent in 2025 and aims to achieve its target margin of 7 percent by 2026.
