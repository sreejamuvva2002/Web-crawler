# Savannah Economic Development Authority

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-11-20

**Source:** [Savannah GA Business News | Savannah Business News | SEDA](https://seda.org/resources-and-data/news-center/page/2/)  ·  `page_018921`

## Facts

- World Trade Center Savannah hosted its sixth annual Prosperity Through Trade Luncheon on Thursday, May 15, in conjunction with World Trade Month.
- Attendees included World Trade Center Savannah Board of Directors Chairperson John Coleman, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America Chief Administrative Officer Brent Stubbs, and a Georgia Southern University Vice President.
- The 2025 SEDA and World Trade Center Savannah meeting dates include an SEDA Board of Directors meeting at 11 a.m. on Jan. 15, an SEDA Annual Meeting at 8:30 a.m. on Feb. 11 at the Savannah Convention Center, and meetings on April 17 and May 13.
- All SEDA and World Trade Center Savannah meetings are held at 906 Drayton St. unless otherwise noted.
- On November 20, 2024, the Regional Industry Support Enterprise, Inc. (RISE) held its first annual meeting where its board of directors was officially formed and officers appointed.
- RISE's board represents eight counties: Bryan, Bulloch, Candler, Chatham, Effingham, Evans, Liberty, and Screven.
- HydroFleet announced an investment of nearly $33 million in Pooler, Georgia, to construct a hydrogen production and fueling station.
- The HydroFleet facility will service zero-emission heavy-duty trucks powered by hydrogen fuel cells.
- At its November 2024 meeting, SEDA unanimously approved an increase in the Savannah Entertainment Production Incentive rebate.
- The incentive rebate will increase from $100,000 to $175,000 for feature films and from $250,000 to $300,000 for television or internet-distributed episodic productions.
- At its April 16, 2024 meeting, SEDA staff provided an update on the naming and activities of a regional workforce organization announced in December 2023.
- The regional workforce organization was recommended in a Regional Workforce Study released in November 2023.
- At the Jan. 4 SEDA Annual Meeting, President and CEO Trip Tollison announced that SEDA facilitated 11 company announcements in 2023.
- These 2023 announcements resulted in 3,731 new job opportunities and $2,631,994,199 in capital investment.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Plastic Express will build a second shipping facility in Savannah, Georgia.
- The new Plastic Express facility is expected to create 200 new jobs and represent an $80 million investment in Chatham County.
- At a SEDA Board of Directors meeting, Vend Tech International announced it is relocating its headquarters, training center, assembly manufacturing, and global distribution/technology development facility to the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center.
- Vend Tech International produces Naturals2Go vending machines, self-checkout micromarkets, and gourmet coffee equipment.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Daechang Seat Savannah Corp. will invest $72.5 million in the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center.
- The Daechang Seat Savannah Corp. facility is located in close proximity to Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA).
- The Daechang Seat Savannah Corp. investment will create over 500 jobs and supports its specialization in automotive seat frames.
