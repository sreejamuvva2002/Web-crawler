# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2022-07-01

**Source:** [Georgia: Pro-Business Environment, Development-Ready Property, Record-Breaking Investment | Trade and Industry Development](https://www.tradeandindustrydev.com/region/georgia/georgia-pro-business-environment-development-ready-32930)  ·  `page_016887`

## Facts

- Georgia Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that the state broke economic development records for a third year in a row.
- From July 1, 2022, through June 30, 2023, total investments in facility expansions and new locations totaled more than $24 billion.
- These investments resulted in 38,400 new jobs statewide.
- 82 percent of the new jobs were created outside metro Atlanta.
- Statewide annual investment totals increased by 131 percent over the last three years.
- Investment growth is driven in part by international investments from markets such as South Korea, Japan and Europe.
- Corporations choose Georgia for relocations and expansions due to development-ready sites, a skilled and diverse workforce, and logistics infrastructure connecting to a global market.
- Companies locating in Georgia have access to workforce development programs and benefit from a low corporate tax rate and business incentives.
- The state’s corporate tax rate sits at 5.75 percent.
- New and expanding companies can earn Job Tax Credits for creating new jobs in Georgia.
- Morgan Stanley continued to grow its workforce across several locations in the metro Atlanta area in 2023.
- In fiscal year 2023, expansions accounted for 71 percent of total projects announced across the state.
- In fiscal year 2023, new locations accounted for 65 percent of new jobs announced across the state.
- Aurubis and Porsche have made Georgia their North American headquarters.
- 19 FORTUNE 500s including Aflac, Global Payments and UPS use Georgia for their headquarters.
- The U.S. Soccer Federation announced it would move its headquarters and construct a first-of-its kind national training center in Georgia.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development runs the “Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development” (GRAD) Certification Program to streamline economic development.
- GRAD offers businesses and site selectors a portfolio of pre-qualified industrial sites with completed due diligence prior to prospect visits.
- GRAD Certified sites were critical in winning key projects, including about half of the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) suppliers and numerous battery supply chain projects.
- HMGMA broke ground in October 2022.
- Project suppliers for HMGMA have announced investments totaling more than $2.5 billion.
- Automotive parts company Hwashin announced a multibillion-dollar investment in Middle Georgia in October 2023 on a GRAD-Certified site.
- The GRAD program helped spur a 112 percent increase in statewide advanced manufacturing investments in fiscal year 2023 compared to fiscal year 2021.
- Two new sites joined the state’s roster of more than 60 GRAD Certified properties in 2023.
- Georgia’s workforce consists of 5.3 million people, strengthened by investments in technical college training, apprenticeship programs, and K-12 education.
- Georgia Quick Start is the nation’s number one workforce training program and has trained over one million employees for companies expanding or locating in the state.
- Companies like Qcells, Hyundai Motor Group and SK Battery America are benefiting from Georgia Quick Start’s ability to customize training and replicate actual processes for Day One production.
- Part of the Technical College System of Georgia (TCSG), Georgia Quick Start currently operates five training centers.
- Georgia Quick Start is expanding its advanced manufacturing center and designing new facilities for clean energy manufacturers and suppliers.
- In February, TCSG, Quick Start and HMGMA broke ground on the Hyundai Mobility Training Center of Georgia on the same site as the Metaplant.
- The training center is inspired by the facility built for Kia Georgia, which opened in 2008 and is still in use today.
- Georgia’s top-ranked colleges include two of the nation’s top 20 public universities: the Georgia Institute of Technology and the University of Georgia.
- Two of the nation’s top five HBCUs are located in Georgia: Spelman College and Morehouse College.
- Georgia offers specialized programs including Cyber and FinTech Academies and the Georgia Film Academy, which prepares students for the state’s $4.1 billion film industry.
- Savannah College of Art and Design (SCAD) has campuses in Savannah and Atlanta preparing a diverse, creative workforce.
- Georgia’s HOPE Scholarship provides tuition assistance to recent high school graduates and adults seeking further education.
- The state made a million-dollar investment in new apprenticeships to train students for careers in healthcare, manufacturing and construction.
- The state launched one of the largest state-run direct admissions initiatives in the nation.
- Georgia leads in moving people and goods globally through its logistics network supporting cleantech and corporate headquarters.
- Hartsfield-Jackson International Airport is the busiest and most efficient airport in the world.
- The Port of Savannah is the single largest and fastest-growing container terminal in America.
- The Port of Brunswick is one of the most utilized port terminals in the U.S. for roll-on, roll-off cargo.
- Georgia has three inland ports that provide regions across the state with direct paths to the Port of Savannah and Brunswick.
- The state has 5,000 miles of rail, creating the most extensive rail system and largest intermodal hub in the Southeast.
- In fiscal year 2023, suppliers attracted by Georgia’s mobility OEMs resulted in over $2 billion in investment across the state.
- Mobility and cleantech projects alone delivered three investments of over $1 billion and six of the largest projects by job creation in fiscal year 2023.
- The Georgia Center of Innovation connects public and private partners in strategic ecosystems to foster innovation in mobility and cleantech industries.
- Aerospace is growing significantly in Georgia, overlapping with e-mobility through companies like Archer Aviation Inc.
- Gulfstream has maintained its headquarters in Georgia since 1967 and continues to expand.
- Georgia has the fifth-largest population of active and retired service members in the country, offering opportunities for defense contractors.
