# Kennesaw State University Econometric Center

**Type:** research_center  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2016-02-01

**Source:** [Georgia manufacturing stumbles into new year, report says - Coles College of Business](https://www.kennesaw.edu/coles/news/stories/02-01-2016-georgia-manufacturing-stumbles-into-new-year.php)  ·  `page_012084`

## Facts

- Manufacturing in Georgia started the year still moving forward but in a lower gear, according to a report from Kennesaw State University.
- The manufacturing sector employs more than 377,200 people across the state.
- Market conditions for the sector are less encouraging according to the KSU Econometric Center.
- The center calculates indices based on survey responses from manufacturing companies across Georgia.
- Survey answers are factored into the Purchasing Managers Index (PMI) to summarize manufacturing outlook.
- The overall PMI index dipped from 52.5 to 52.0 in January.
- Five of the six measured categories showed a decline in the monthly report.
- Economist Don Sabbarese, director emeritus of the center, provided the analysis.
- A PMI reading above 50 indicates manufacturing activity is expanding, while below 50 indicates contraction.
