## Facts

- Daimler Truck Defence is a new international brand designed to bring disparate military vehicle operations together under one roof.
- The company made a mid-three-digit million-euro investment spread across engineering, manufacturing, sales, and service for the new brand.
- Daimler Truck hopes to reach €1 billion in defense revenue by 2028.
- The company's profits fell 34 percent last year, in part due to U.S. tariffs and slow North American demand.
- Daimler Truck group revenue was €49.5 billion in the previous year.
- According to Dennis Kinzelmann, military vehicles currently account for a low single-digit percentage of sales.
- Dennis Kinzelmann is the recently appointed head of Daimler Truck Defence.
- The Daimler Truck Defence division currently employs about 1,000 people.
- The Wörth am Rhein plant location plans to add over 100 more jobs.
- Over the course of the decade, European defense budgets are expected to surpass €500 billion.
- Daimler Truck relies on tried-and-true civilian platforms such as the Unimog, Zetros, and Arocs for military logistics vehicles.
- The company received a three-figure order from Germany’s military.
- Daimler Truck signed a framework agreement with the French military through Arquus for 7,000 trucks.
- Daimler Truck formed a partnership with General Dynamics Land Systems for at least 1,500 logistics trucks for Canada.
- VW, Stellantis, and Renault are also circling the opportunity of excess manufacturing capacity meeting an unexpectedly wealthy defense market.
- Daimler Truck already has 5,000 service locations in more than 160 countries to support its products long after manufacture.
