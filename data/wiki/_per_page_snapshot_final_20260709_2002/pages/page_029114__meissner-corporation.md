# Meissner Corporation

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2023-04-05

**Source:** [Meissner to Create 1,700 Jobs in Athens-Clarke County | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-releases/2023/life-sciences-manufacturer-meissner-create-1700-jobs-athens-clarke-county)  ·  `page_029114`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Meissner Corporation will invest nearly $250 million in a new facility in Athens-Clarke County.
- The project will create more than 1,700 jobs over the next eight years.
- Georgia is described as the No. 1 state for business and a leader in life sciences.
- Meissner Corporation is one of the world's largest privately held companies in the bioprocessing sector.
- The company develops, manufactures, supplies, and services products and systems for the pharmaceutical and biopharmaceutical industries.
- Meissner was founded in 1984 and is headquartered in Camarillo, California.
- The company's product portfolio enables the development and manufacture of critical medicines in therapeutic areas such as oncology, cardiology, and immunology.
- Meissner recently played a leading role in the industry's response to the COVID-19 pandemic by providing critical products for therapeutics and vaccines.
- Christopher Meissner serves as President of Meissner.
- The new facility will be located at the Christian Industrial Tract, 1310 Spring Valley Road, in Winterville.
- When complete, the new campus will more than double the company's manufacturing footprint in the U.S.
- The site will consist of multiple structures including cleanroom facilities, laboratories, research and development spaces, and office spaces.
- Operations are expected to begin in early 2026.
- The company will hire for roles including technicians, scientists, engineers, information technology, and administrative positions.
- Meissner plans to partner with the University System of Georgia and the Technical College System of Georgia, including the University of Georgia, Georgia Institute of Technology, and Athens Technical College, for internships and collaboration opportunities.
- Mayor Kelly Girtz represents the Athens-Clarke County Unified Government.
- Barton Lowrey is the Director of Industrial Projects at the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) Global Commerce team.
- Project partners include Athens-Clarke County Unified Government Economic Development (Invest Athens), Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Meissner provides critical equipment benefiting other key industries in Georgia, such as life sciences and food processing.
- Meissner's manufacturing campuses include a headquarters in Camarillo, California, and a European location in Castlebar, Ireland.
