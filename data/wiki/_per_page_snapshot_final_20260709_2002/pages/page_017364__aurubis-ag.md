# Aurubis AG

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Expansion of the first multi-metal recycling plant in the USA driven forward by Aurubis and SMS group --  Trade Fair](https://www.gifa.com/en/Media_News/News/Expansion_of_the_first_multi-metal_recycling_plant_in_the_USA_driven_forward_by_Aurubis_and_SMS_group)  ·  `page_017364`

## Facts

- Aurubis AG and SMS group GmbH signed a supply contract for a second plant module for the new Aurubis recycling plant in Richmond, Georgia.
- The additional facilities will increase the capacity of the metal recycling plant currently under construction in Richmond from 90,000 tons of complex scrap metal materials per year to 180,000 tons per year.
- Roland Harings, CEO of Aurubis, stated that the expansion implements the company's "Driving Sustainable Growth" strategy and keeps strategically important mineral raw materials in circulation.
- The plant's core utilizes Top Blown Rotary Converter (TBRC) technology to process complex recycling materials and recover metallic raw materials such as copper, nickel, tin, zinc, precious metals, and platinum group metals.
- The recovered metallic raw materials are indispensable for future technologies including energy transition, climate-friendly mobility, and digitization.
- Michael Rzepczyk, member of the SMS Executive Board, stated that combining SMS group's plant know-how with Aurubis' process expertise will enable highly efficient metal recovery.
- With the planned doubling of production volume at the new U.S. site, Aurubis will take recycling in the U.S. to the next level, according to Rzepczyk.
- The acquired recycling technology features plant modularity, allowing the facility to be supplemented with further components depending on future needs.
- Harings stated that the modular system enables a well-planned market strategy to react flexibly to constant market growth in the U.S.
- Aurubis is building the first secondary smelter specializing in multi-metal recycling in the U.S.
- The project involves a targeted investment of about €640 million across both stages.
- Construction of the Richmond plant project started in mid-2022.
- The plant is scheduled to start operations with its first stage in the first half of 2024.
- The second stage of the plant will start operation in 2026, for which the delivery of the TBRCs was signed on Friday.
