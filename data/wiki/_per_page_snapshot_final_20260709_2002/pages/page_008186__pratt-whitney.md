# Pratt & Whitney

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2026-02-26

**Source:** [Pratt & Whitney Invests $200M to Expand Georgia Manufacturing & Boost Engine Production - News Usa Today](https://news-usa.today/pratt-whitney-invests-200m-to-expand-georgia-manufacturing-boost-engine-production/)  ·  `page_008186`

## Facts

- Pratt & Whitney is investing $200 million to expand its manufacturing presence in Columbus, Georgia.
- The investment aims to bolster production capacity for commercial and military engine programs.
- The expansion centers on adding a seventh isothermal forging press at the Columbus Forge facility.
- Operations for the new forging press are slated to begin in 2028.
- The new press is projected to increase the output of critical engine components, specifically rotating compressor and turbine disks, by 30 percent.
- Increased capacity will directly support production of the geared turbofan engine and the F135 engine powering the Lockheed Martin F-35 Lightning II fighter.
- Shane Eddy, president of Pratt & Whitney, stated that the company has invested more than $1 billion in its Columbus facility since 2008.
- Eddy noted the latest investment will increase output of critical parts for growing military and commercial engine programs and underscores a commitment to ramp industrial capacity.
- Pratt & Whitney recently completed a $70 million, 81,000-square-foot expansion of its Columbus Engine Center.
- The Columbus Engine Center expansion focused on enhancing maintenance, repair, and overhaul (MRO) operations for geared turbofan (GTF) engines using advanced equipment aligned with Industry 4.0 strategy.
- The Columbus Engine Center expansion increased the facility’s annual capacity by over 25 percent and added critical overhaul volume to the GTF MRO network.
- A previously announced $206 million initiative in 2023 also focused on expanding GTF engine manufacturing and overhaul operations.
- The 2023 investment included facility expansions, new equipment, and an anticipated creation of 400 new jobs by 2028.
- Pratt & Whitney’s Columbus site is located approximately 90 miles south of Atlanta.
- The Columbus site comprises both the Columbus Engine Center and Columbus Forge operating on the same campus.
- The Columbus facility supports GTF engines for the Airbus A320neo, Airbus A220, and Embraer E-Jets E2.
- The Columbus facility also supports engines for the Boeing C-17 and McDonnell Douglas F-15/Lockheed Martin F-16.
