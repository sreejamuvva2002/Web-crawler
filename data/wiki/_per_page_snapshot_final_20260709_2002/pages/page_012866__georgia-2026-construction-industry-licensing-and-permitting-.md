# Georgia 2026 Construction Industry Licensing and Permitting Legislation

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-01

**Source:** [Contractor Licensing, Permit Review, and Public Roofing in Georgi](https://natlawreview.com/article/2026-georgia-construction-legislation-contractor-licensing-permit-review-and-public)  ·  `page_012866`

## Facts

- During the 2026 legislative session, the Georgia General Assembly passed several measures affecting the construction industry.
- Governor Brian Kemp signed the construction industry measures into law.
- Principal changes to specialty contractor licensing and permit procedures take effect July 1, 2026.
- A rewritten Chapter 14 of Title 43 will govern specialty contractor licensing starting July 1, 2026.
- New procedures for private-provider inspections and land-disturbance permit review begin July 1, 2026.
- A separate measure applies to certain public roofing contracts entered into since April 22, 2026.
- Senate Bill 553 was signed into law as Act 472.
- Act 472 extends revised licensing framework provisions to electrical, plumbing, conditioned air, low voltage, and utility contractors by repealing and reenacting Chapter 14 of Title 43.
- Under the new rules, one year of “full-time experience” for contractor licensure requires 2,000 hours of documented qualifying work.
- Contractor applicants must submit sworn affidavits verifying experience and consent to applicant-funded criminal background checks, including fingerprinting.
- The revised statute updates experience, examination, and training requirements for electrical, plumbing, and low voltage contractor licenses, as well as utility manager and utility foreman certifications.
- The rewritten chapter explicitly lists license lending, leasing, renting, assigning, or allowing an unlicensed person or entity to use a license as grounds for discipline.
- Sanctions for license violations include reprimand, suspension, revocation, cancellation, or refusal to issue, renew, or restore a license or certificate.
- Contractor licenses and certificates remain subject to biennial renewal with a 31-day late-renewal period.
- Continuing-education hours established by each division cannot be carried forward if unused.
- Senate Bill 447 was signed into law as Act 513.
- Act 513 revises local permit-review procedures and mandates a new permit-transparency requirement.
- Beginning January 1, 2028, counties, municipalities, or consolidated governments that issued more than 250 building permits in the preceding calendar year must provide real-time permit-status information via a publicly accessible website or application.
- The transparency rule defines “building permit” to include land-disturbance permits.
- The public permit system must display permit type, current status, date of most recent update, reviewing department, any missed approval or denial deadlines, and the statutory basis for any denial.
- Permit information must be searchable by permit number, property address, or parcel identification number and available without charge, registration, or login.
- Permit status updates generally must be posted when the status changes or within one business day.
- For private-provider inspections, local governing authorities have two business days to identify deficiencies in an approved inspection report.
- If a local authority fails to provide timely written notice of deficiencies for a private-provider inspection, the inspection is deemed approved as a matter of law.
- Correcting a deficient inspection report may not prevent completion of work already approved by the private professional provider or delay the related project.
- The existing 45-day initial review period for land-disturbance permits remains in effect.
- Local issuing authorities have five days to determine if a land-disturbance application is complete and whether external review is required; without timely notice, the application is deemed complete.
- Local authorities must act on the first resubmission of a land-disturbance permit within 20 days and on later resubmissions within 14 days.
- New comments on resubmitted applications generally must relate to the original comments or the applicant’s revisions.
- A denial or nonacceptance of a land-disturbance permit must state the reasons in writing.
- If a local authority misses the applicable issuance or denial deadline for a land-disturbance permit, it must refund application fees and the applicant may seek mandamus relief with priority on the superior court’s docket.
- Senate Bill 284 was signed into law as Act 377.
- Act 377 changes the treatment of contracts entered into on or after April 22, 2026, for improvements to roofs of existing public structures or buildings.
- Roof improvements under this measure are excluded from the definition of “public works construction,” though ancillary items placed on the roof are not excluded.
- Notice and competitive-award requirements are deemed satisfied when a qualifying roofing contract is awarded to a supplier approved by a cooperative purchasing organization and designated by the Department of Administrative Services as an authorized source of supply.
- Certain local school systems may receive related capital-outlay grants under this measure.
