# U.S. Department of Energy

## Facts

- The U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) and the White House Office of Science and Technology Policy (OSTP) released “The National Blueprint for a Clean & Competitive Industrial Sector” on November 18, 2024.
- The Blueprint outlines five whole-of-government strategies within a private-sector led and government-enabled framework to fuel continued growth of American manufacturing.
- The framework aligns with the Biden-Harris Administration’s agenda to transform U.S. manufacturing to boost competitiveness, reduce pollution, and create good-paying jobs for American workers.
- U.S. Deputy Secretary of Energy David M. Turk stated that the manufacturing sector is expanding rapidly under President Biden’s leadership, employing millions of Americans while providing essential materials and products that people use every day.
- Implementing the strategies outlined in the Blueprint will improve public health, accelerate innovation to support U.S. international competitiveness, and create even more good-paying U.S. jobs.
- The American manufacturing sector contributes 12% to the U.S. Gross Domestic Product annually.
- Many manufacturing jobs pay better than average while being accessible to workers without a four-year degree.
- Robust domestic manufacturing reduces dependence on foreign suppliers and lowers the risk of supply chain shocks, which is important for national security.
- The manufacturing sector is energy intensive and accounts for approximately 20 percent of total annual domestic greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions.
- The Blueprint outlines a suite of new levers and five strategies to guide near-term Federal government coordination.
- One strategy is to accelerate deployment of commercially available, cost-effective lower carbon solutions in the near term.
- Another strategy is to demonstrate emerging solutions at commercial scale to de-risk deployment.
- A third strategy is to increase data use to drive emissions reductions and efficiency gains that can significantly improve performance and track progress.
- A fourth strategy is to innovate and advance research to develop transformative processes and products for deep GHG emissions reductions.
- The fifth strategy is to integrate across the product life cycle to reduce embodied GHG emissions in industrial products and minimize waste.
- Investments in low-carbon industrial technology are investments in the future of American manufacturing competitiveness, as corporations and governments across the globe race to dominate emerging energy technology solutions.
- The Blueprint lays out a holistic vision of coordinated federal actions that will support pollution reduction across U.S. industry while strengthening broadly shared economic prosperity, public health and national security.
