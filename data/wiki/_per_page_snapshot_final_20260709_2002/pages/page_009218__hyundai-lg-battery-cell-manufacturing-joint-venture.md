# Hyundai-LG Battery Cell Manufacturing Joint Venture

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-08-31

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Group announced plant expansion plans](https://www.savannahnow.com/story/business/2023/08/31/hyundai-motor-group-announced-plant-expansion-plans/70726463007/)  ·  `page_009218`

## Facts

- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp announced Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution will invest an additional $2 billion in their battery cell manufacturing joint venture.
- The additional investment brings the total project investment to more than $4.3 billion.
- The expansion will add another 400 jobs, bringing the total regional headcount to 8,500.
- José Muñoz, president and global COO of Hyundai Motor Company and president and CEO of Hyundai and Genesis Motor North America, stated that vehicles produced at the mega plant will be 100% electric vehicles.
- Muñoz explained the need for onsite battery production to support volume requirements and provide next-generation batteries with more range, lower weight, and higher technology.
- In May, Hyundai and LG Energy Solution announced a partnership for the $4.3 billion plant designed to produce 300,000 batteries per year.
- The facility is expected to open in late 2025, shortly after Hyundai's EV assembly factory comes online.
- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp stated that the project broke ground on the largest project in state history and welcomed LG Energy Solution to Bryan County.
- Kemp emphasized efforts to make Georgia the e-mobility capital of the nation, noting investments will support paychecks, improved schools, and infrastructure.
- Battery materials produced at the new facility will be assembled into battery packs by Hyundai Mobis, which is constructing its own facility in Richmond Hill.
- Hyundai Mobis will distribute the finished battery packs to Hyundai Motor Group manufacturing facilities throughout the U.S. for Genesis EV models, Hyundai, and Kia vehicles.
- Carter Infinger, chairman of the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority, stated the announcement will bring well-paying jobs to the Savannah region and described the continued investment as transformative.
- Since 2018, Georgia's EV market job creation has driven more than $25 billion in investments and created more than 30,000 jobs statewide.
