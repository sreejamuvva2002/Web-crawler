## Facts

- Chairman Lee Kang-myung stated that rebounding metal prices and customer demand for non-Chinese materials will allow Sungeel Hitech to return to profitability in the fourth quarter of this year.
- The company recorded 152.9 billion won in revenue last year.
- Sungeel Hitech reported an operating loss of 54.9 billion won last year due to a slowdown in electric vehicle and battery markets.
- Depreciation of its new recycling and hydro facility, completed in June, contributed to the poor earnings.
- The Gunsan facility crushes scrubs and dead batteries into black mass to produce nickel, cobalt, lithium, and other raw materials.
- Nickel and lithium prices increased 10% in the first quarter of this year compared to the fourth quarter of 2024.
- Chinese companies are struggling to maintain low prices for these raw materials despite being the largest producer.
- In October last year, the US House of Representatives passed a law forbidding the use of Chinese batteries, which will take effect in October 2028.
- The US has already imposed 45% tariffs on Chinese batteries.
- Sungeel Hitech plans to start operations of its first North American recycling factory soon at a Recycling Park in Indiana that will collect scrubs and dead batteries.
- A second North American factory will extract usable materials from the collected batteries.
- Another Recycling Park is planned in Georgia, which will process scrubs from partners including Samsung SDI, SK On, and Hyundai Motor.
