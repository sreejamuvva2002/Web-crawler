# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-03-26

**Source:** [Hyundai Metaplant’s AI and Human Processes – OM in the News](https://ominthenews.com/hyundai-metaplants-ai-and-human-processes/)  ·  `page_007761`

## Facts

- The newly-built Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America has an investment cost of $7.6 billion.
- The plant is located in Georgia and boasts the largest automobile manufacturing space in the United States.
- Two EVs, the Ioniq 5 and Ioniq 9 SUVs, are currently produced at the Metaplant.
- Future production plans include adding other brands like Kia and Genesis to the facility.
- Each vehicle goes through 23 AI-based or robotic processes using a blend of human labor and high-tech systems.
- Approximately 850 robots plus AI are used for quality control inspection and vehicle movement at the plant.
- Robots such as “Spot” are used to inspect vehicles at the facility.
- Drones are used to scan and track inventory at the plant.
- AI is used to troubleshoot and identify root causes for early corrective action, which lessens defective units, scrap, and warranty costs.
- Vehicle assembly is handled by robotics and AI, resulting in more consistent quality and fewer errors per vehicle.
- Humans write the programs for the robotics and AI to help identify defective vehicles long before they leave the manufacturing plant.
- By 2031, Hyundai plans to employ 8,500 workers to handle robotics maintenance, oversee data analysis, and install parts on vehicles.
- So far, about 1,000 full-time workers have been hired at the plant.
- Human workers are freed from mundane, repetitive tasks to focus on higher-order activities like programming robots or interpreting production data.
- Humans perform tasks that robots cannot efficiently do, such as handling thin and flexible materials.
