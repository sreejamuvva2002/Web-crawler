## Facts

- JAC initiated an R&D process tailored for the Australian market three years prior to the article's publication.
- The development plan included calibration of the J9’s suspension and driver assistance systems, plus work on local accessories.
- An off-road trip with six JAC J9 pickups to Mount Disappointment north of Melbourne in the Victorian high country was one of the final hurdles before the brand’s arrival in Australian showrooms.
- Danny Lenartic serves as the deputy managing director of JAC Motors.
- One J9 test vehicle has covered more than 20,000 kilometres in various conditions.
- Six additional J9s are equipped in top-level Oasis specification.
- The development effort includes durability testing to ensure vehicle changes align with local preferences and withstand Australian conditions.
- Balancing innovation with timelines and resources is cited as the biggest challenge in the development process.
- The development team consists of engineers from JAC in China, engineering staff from local importer LTS Auto, and contractors.
- JAC and LTS Auto are working in a continued partnership to commit resources toward improving products for Australian conditions and standards.
- The test program began in the final quarter of last year and has been ramping up since then.
- JAC has put special emphasis on driver assistance systems calibration to meet Australian road safety standards.
- Despite the extensive J9 test program, there is no update from JAC about other models for Australia, including SUVs and passenger cars from its Chinese lineup.
- The company’s current focus remains solely on the T9 ute.
- Future models are scheduled to arrive later this year.
- Heavy-duty accessories are being developed and built locally in Australia.
