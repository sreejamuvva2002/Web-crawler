# Freudenberg-NOK Sealing Technologies

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Freudenberg-NOK Expands Material Science Capabilities With Addition Of New Laboratory In Findlay](https://www.plasticsnet.com/doc/freudenberg-nok-expands-material-science-capabilities-0001)  ·  `page_020430`

## Facts

- Freudenberg-NOK Sealing Technologies will increase its testing capabilities with the addition of a new materials laboratory at its Findlay, Ohio manufacturing plant.
- The new laboratory is part of an overall plant expansion taking place in 2015.
- The laboratory will provide the company's Fluid Power Division with new material characterization, failure analysis and mechanical property test capabilities.
- The laboratory will consolidate and expand an existing engineering test laboratory at the facility.
- Findlay's expansion was announced last month and will add 43,000 square feet to the existing factory.
- The new lab will account for approximately 2,000 square feet of the total expansion area.
- The new laboratory represents an investment of approximately $450,000 in equipment and building expenses.
- The entire expansion project represents an investment of approximately $8.6 million in construction and equipment upgrades.
- The expansion will create 25 new jobs at the Findlay plant.
- The project is expected to be complete by winter, 2015.
- The company celebrated the expansion with a groundbreaking ceremony at the plant attended by executives, elected officials, employees and media.
- The Findlay facility produces approximately 700 high performance plastic parts for customers including vehicle manufacturers and tier one and two suppliers using state-of-the art manufacturing processes.
- The new laboratory will perform analysis and testing of parts made from high performance plastics, including thrust washers, seals and other components used in the automotive industry.
- The company is installing a new engineering test rig in the lab that will measure torque loss.
- Data from the new test rig will help improve custom engineered sealing solutions that reduce CO2 emissions and improve fuel economy.
- The 25 new jobs include chemist, chemical engineer and quality technician positions.
- The company worked with the state of Ohio, including the Findlay-Hancock County Economic Development authority, Jobs Ohio and the Regional Growth Partnership on workforce incentives.
- Freudenberg-NOK and its Findlay operations are considered a top employer in Hancock County.
- The plant was a finalist in the IndustryWeek 2014 Best Plants competition, which annually recognizes the best manufacturing plants in North America.
- Findlay maintains nearly perfect statistics in quality, delivery and customer retention.
- Findlay has become the company's lead facility in adopting Single Cavity Net Shape Molding, a breakthrough manufacturing approach.
- The Single Cavity Net Shape Molding process uses compact, custom-engineered one cavity injection and compression molding machines rather than multi-cavity machines to produce crucial thermoplastic parts.
- This new molding process improves part quality and reduces scrap and waste.
- The expansion will help the facility increase its compression and injection molding product production, which includes seal rings, thrust washers, pistons and assemblies.
- The plant currently manufactures 185 million seals and related components annually.
- The company anticipates the new addition will increase production by approximately 25 percent a year.
- "Research and development are core values at Freudenberg-NOK and this laboratory investment supports the company's mission, customers and markets," said Dr. Theodore G. Duclos, president, Freudenberg-NOK.
- "We continue to invest in our scientific material competencies in order to solve ever more complex global system challenges and to remain an essential leader in material developments and the sealing industry," said Dr. Theodore G. Duclos.
- "The addition of the new laboratory and testing capabilities at the Findlay facility will help us meet customer demands for components that function flawlessly in even the most rigorous and even unanticipated environmental conditions," said Roy Schroeder, Lead Center Manager, Fluid Power Automotive, Findlay.
- "We are delighted that Freudenberg-NOK is investing in our facility, our people and our community," said Roy Schroeder.
- Freudenberg-NOK Sealing Technologies is the Americas joint venture between Freudenberg and Co. in Germany and NOK Corp. in Japan.
- Freudenberg-NOK is headquartered in Plymouth, Michigan and operates more than 20 facilities.
