# Toyota Battery Manufacturing North Carolina (TBMNC)

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Toyota Announces $8B Expansion at its North Carolina-Based EV Battery Plant - EDPNC](https://edpnc.com/news/toyota-october-2023-battery-expansion/)  ·  `page_000509`

## Facts

- Toyota will invest an additional $8 billion in its first North American electric vehicle battery manufacturing facility.
- The Economic Development Partnership of North Carolina announced the investment, which adds another 3,000 jobs.
- The project is dubbed “Toyota Battery Manufacturing North Carolina” (TBMNC).
- TBMNC was first announced as a $1.29 billion investment in 2021.
- The project saw expansions in 2022 and 2023.
- The total project value is now $13.9 billion.
- The project will create more than 5,000 jobs.
- The project is expected to be completed in 2025.
- North Carolina Governor Roy Cooper stated that his recent trip to Tokyo and meeting with President Sato strengthened the partnership with Toyota, culminating in this historic announcement.
- Governor Roy Cooper noted that North Carolina’s transition to a clean energy economy is bringing better-paying jobs to support families and communities for decades.
- The facility is located on an 1,825-acre megasite in Liberty, North Carolina.
- Toyota will manufacture batteries for hybrid electric vehicles (HEVs) and battery electric vehicles (BEVs) at the facility.
- The facility will use 100% renewable energy for manufacturing.
- The facility supports Toyota’s global electrification efforts, which include rolling out 30 BEV models globally by 2030.
- Christopher Chung, CEO at the Economic Development Partnership of North Carolina (EDPNC), called the announcement a validation of North Carolina’s national recognition as a hub for innovation and a top location for EV manufacturers and suppliers.
- More than 28,000 North Carolinians work for Japanese companies every day.
- Policies like the Inflation Reduction Act have helped propel North Carolina to be one of the top four states in electric vehicle investment over the past year.
- North Carolina has brought in more than $10 billion in EV investment over the past year.
- The state has seen more than $11 billion invested in the electric vehicle industry in the last three years.
- VinFast, a Vietnamese auto manufacturer, announced a $2 billion investment and selected Chatham County in 2022 for its first North American automotive assembly and battery manufacturing plant.
- The state has become a hub for electric vehicle component manufacturing with investments totaling $760 million from Eplison Advanced Materials, Deere & Company, Kresiel Electric GMBH, and Kempower, Inc. announced in the last eight months.
- Wolfspeed, one of the world’s largest producers of silicon carbide wafers used in electric vehicles to increase battery range, announced a $5 billion investment in its newest semiconductor manufacturing plant in 2022.
- CNBC named North Carolina its Top State for Business in 2022 and 2023.
- Last year saw a record-breaking $19.3 billion invested in the state and nearly 29,000 new jobs announced.
- Nearly 29,000 new jobs were announced last year, including 10,275 from 30 foreign direct investment projects worth a combined $5.21 billion.
