# Hyundai TRANSYS Georgia Seating System, LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2021-04

**Source:** [Hyundai TRANSYS spends $9 Million on Georgia expansion](https://valdostatoday.com/business-2/2021/04/hyundai-transys-spends-9-million-on-georgia-expansion/)  ·  `page_008861`

## Facts

- Hyundai TRANSYS Georgia Seating System, LLC is expanding its manufacturing operations in Georgia.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Hyundai TRANSYS Georgia Seating System, LLC will invest $9 million to expand its seating manufacturing operations in West Point, Georgia.
- Hyundai TRANSYS Georgia Seating System, LLC is a world-renowned producer of automotive parts.
- The $9 million expansion will create more than 150 jobs in Troup County.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated that Georgia’s highly skilled workforce continues to attract manufacturing jobs and praised Hyundai TRANSYS’ continued investment in West Point for creating opportunities in Troup County.
- Hyundai TRANSYS Georgia Seating System manufactures seats and seat foam pads for the Hyundai Santa Fe, KIA Sorento, and other vehicle models.
- In addition to supplying seating structures to automobile manufacturers, the company supplies seats to leading electric vehicle makers.
- Hyundai TRANSYS Georgia Seating System President and CEO Sangkil Jung expressed excitement for the new opportunity and pride in the partnership with the State of Georgia.
- The expansion will bring Hyundai TRANSYS’ total employment count in Georgia to more than 1,890 jobs.
- Hyundai TRANSYS Georgia Seating System is actively hiring for roles in production seat assembly, foam, quality, and logistics.
- West Point Development Authority Chairman Kevin Patrick thanked Hyundai TRANSYS Georgia Seating System for its continued investment and noted that automotive and existing industries are the backbone of the local economic success.
- Senior Project Manager Amanda Fields from the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce division represented the state on this competitive project.
- The project was executed in partnership with the West Point Development Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that the automotive sector contributes more than $3 billion to Georgia’s economy and thanked Hyundai TRANSYS and local/state/federal partners for fostering a pro-business environment.
- Georgia is home to more than 200 automotive-related facilities and remains a national leader in advanced manufacturing.
- Georgia has outpaced the United States in 10-year GDP growth in the manufacture of machinery, electrical equipment and components, and fabricated metals.
- Hyundai TRANSYS is an automotive parts company that produces critical automobile parts including transmissions, axles, and seats.
- Hyundai TRANSYS is the only company in the world specializing in transmissions.
- The company produces all types of automatic transmissions, manual transmissions, dual clutch transmissions (DCT), and continuously variable transmissions.
- Hyundai TRANSYS develops and produces seats for Genesis, Santa Fe, Sorento, and other models.
