# Archer Aviation Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Archer Closes Incentive Transaction; Begins Construction on First of its Kind High-Volume eVTOL Manufacturing Facility in Covington, GA](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20230307005532/en/Archer-Closes-Incentive-Transaction-Begins-Construction-on-First-of-its-Kind-High-Volume-eVTOL-Manufacturing-Facility-in-Covington-GA)  ·  `page_004821`

## Facts

- On February 28th, Archer closed on the local incentive transaction for the project that, along with a Georgia REBA grant, totals ~33% of the planned capital investment.
- On March 2nd, Archer began construction on Phase 1 of the project, which is the build-out of a ~350,000 square-foot facility capable of producing up to 650 aircraft per year.
- The Covington facility will be capable of being expanded by an additional 800,000 square feet, which is estimated to support long-term production targets of up to 2,300 aircraft per year.
- Archer and Stellantis are set to join forces to begin manufacturing Archer’s Midnight eVTOL aircraft in 2024.
- In January, Archer announced an expanded relationship with Stellantis, one of the world’s leading automakers and owner of brands like Jeep®, Ram and Maserati.
- Under the partnership, Stellantis will contribute advanced manufacturing technology and expertise, experienced personnel, and capital.
- Archer will bring its team of eVTOL, electric powertrain, and certification experts to the partnership.
- The collaboration is intended to enable the rapid scaling of aircraft production to meet Archer’s commercialization plans.
- The partnership allows Archer to avoid hundreds of millions of dollars in spending during the manufacturing ramp-up phase.
- The goal is for Stellantis to mass produce Archer’s eVTOL aircraft as its exclusive contract manufacturer.
- Adam Goldstein, Archer’s Founder and CEO, stated that scale is key to unlocking the promise of the eVTOL industry and expressed thrill over support from the State of Georgia and Newton County for the first-of-its-kind facility.
- Archer plans to hold a ceremonial event in April in Covington, GA to mark the construction milestone.
- The facility is strategically located adjacent to the Covington Municipal Airport.
- Archer is designing and developing electric vertical takeoff and landing aircraft for use in urban air mobility networks.
- Archer's team is based in Santa Clara, CA.
