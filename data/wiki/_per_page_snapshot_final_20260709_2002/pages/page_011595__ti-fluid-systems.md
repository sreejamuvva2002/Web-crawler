# TI Fluid Systems

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- TI Fluid Systems is an automotive components manufacturer that designs and manufactures thermal management and fluid handling systems.
- The company is listed on the FTSE-250.
- Revenue declined by 1.4 percent at constant currency in the six months ended June 30 to reach €1,719m (£1,490m).
- The company's share price rose more than 11 percent in early trades.
- The share price remains nearly 18 percent below its starting price for the year.
- The share price rise is attributed to improvements in adjusted earnings before interest and tax (EBIT) margin and outperforming a weak electric vehicle (EV) market.
- The EV market has been soft in 2024 after a frenzy of activity and hype in previous years.
- Manufacturers have blamed high inflation and energy costs for the soft EV market.
- Adjusted earnings before interest and tax (EBIT) at TI Fluid rose by 4.8 percent on a constant currency basis, exceeding analysts' estimates.
- The adjusted EBIT margin rose by 0.4 percent to 7.9 percent due to continued productivity and efficiency improvements, as well as commercial activities.
- TI Fluid raised its full-year EBIT margin guidance to 7.6 percent.
- Bookings rose by 11 percent to €1.5bn (£1.29).
- Only around a third of the bookings are EV awards.
- CEO Hans Dieltjens stated that the bookings highlight the advantages of a product portfolio catering to all propulsions.
- Peel Hunt analysts reiterated a 'buy' recommendation for the stock, noting confidence in TI Fluid's ability to outperform in a slower market.
- CEO Dieltjens added that the first half performance demonstrates strong operational execution, successful strategic delivery, and the resilience of their propulsion agnostic portfolio.
- Through productivity and efficiency measures, the company improved its adjusted EBIT margin, making progress towards a mid-term double-digit target.
- The company has a strong pipeline of productivity initiatives and is agile to respond to changing market conditions.
- CEO Dieltjens expressed confidence that the company's market-leading positions and ability to adapt will continue.
