# Suzuki Manufacturing of America Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Suzuki Recalls KingQuad ATVs over Crash Hazard | Thomas J. Henry](https://thomasjhenrylaw.com/blog/suzuki-recalls-kingquad-atvs-due-crash-hazard/)  ·  `page_010952`

## Facts

- Suzuki Manufacturing of America Corporation manufactures the KingQuad ATVs at a facility in Rome, Georgia.
- The recalled ATVs are manufactured in the United States.
- The recall covers approximately 3,400 units of model year 2016 and 2017 LT-A750XP King Quad ATVs.
- The recalled vehicles were sold nationwide at Suzuki ATV dealers from September 2015 through November 2016.
- The recalled ATVs retailed for $8,600 to $9,600.
- A battery failure in the KingQuad 750 can cause the engine to stop running while in use, potentially causing a crash.
- Suzuki has received six reports of battery discharge or failure to restart related to the recalled ATVs.
- No injuries have been reported from the battery failures.
- The XP model of the KingQuad ATV includes power steering.
- The recalled ATVs are available in green, red, camouflage, and black.
- The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission (CPSC) issued the recall notice.
- Suzuki Motor of America, Inc., located in Brea, California, distributes the recalled ATVs.
- Consumers with recalled ATVs are receiving direct notices from Suzuki and should contact local dealers for free repairs.
