## Facts

- Georgia Quick Start is recognized as one of Georgia’s most effective discretionary incentives for attracting jobs to the state.
- Georgia Quick Start holds the ranking of the No. 1 program of its type in the U.S.
- Hyundai Mobis is the largest tier-one supplier to the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America, supplying three major modules.
- Georgia Quick Start maintains a long-time training relationship with Hyundai Mobis.
- Hanwha Qcells is the largest maker of solar modules in North America.
- Hanwha Qcells has made an investment in Dalton and Cartersville, Georgia.
- Hanwha Qcells' investment helps secure Georgia’s reputation as a clean-tech leader.
- Georgia Quick Start assists Hanwha Qcells in developing a workforce of the future.
- Georgia Quick Start pioneers customized training solutions drawing from fields such as electrical engineering, software design, robotics, and control systems.
- Mechatronics originated in the 1970s when mechanical engineering blended with electronics.
- To meet industry needs, Georgia Quick Start recently built a first-of-its-kind mechatronics trainer.
- Georgia Quick Start designs, equips, and operates training centers to help Georgians learn skills needed for success.
- The Georgia BioScience Training Center is the first facility featured in a new series showcasing Quick Start's training centers.
- The Georgia BioScience Training Center building was named the best “Built Project” by the Georgia chapter of the American Institute of Architecture.
