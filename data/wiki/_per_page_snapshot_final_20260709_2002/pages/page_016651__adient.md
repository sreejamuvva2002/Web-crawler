# Adient

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2025-10-13

**Source:** [Adient and Autoliv Unveil Breakthrough in Dynamic Seat Safety Solutions](https://www.barchart.com/story/news/35405724/adient-and-autoliv-unveil-breakthrough-in-dynamic-seat-safety-solutions)  ·  `page_016651`

## Facts

- Adient and Autoliv co-developed innovative safety solutions to significantly enhance occupant protection in deeply reclined seating positions, commonly referred to as zero-gravity.
- The combined safety solutions are now ready for mass production.
- The solution combines Adient's new seating concept, Z-Guard, with Autoliv's advanced safety technologies.
- Zero-gravity seating is gaining popularity in mid- to high-end vehicle models due to its ergonomic benefits.
- Deeply reclined postures can compromise the effectiveness of conventional safety systems designed for standard upright seating, potentially leading to safety risks.
- Adient's Z-Guard integrates advanced constraint and energy-absorbing technologies into a dynamic safety system built around Multi-Dimensional Collaborative Protection.
- Z-Guard features an Active Cushion Collapse Mechanism that rapidly collapses the seat frame in a crash to absorb energy, adjust posture, reduce spinal impact, and protect vital body regions.
- Z-Guard features an Adjustable Seat Belt Outlet that adapts to various passenger postures and body types to enhance comfort while maximizing precise restraint and minimizing shoulder slippage risks.
- Autoliv technologies complement the system with an Integrated Seatbelt System for improved stability, a Dynamic Lumbar Retractor for controlled restraint force, a Pelvic Cushion Airbag to reduce pelvic impact, and a Head Side Airbag for enhanced side collision protection in reclined positions.
- The combined systems safeguard critical areas including the head, neck, chest, and pelvis to deliver dynamic, full-process occupant protection.
- Z-Guard enables deep integration with vehicle driver assistance systems tailored to OEM requirements and real-world scenarios.
- Leveraging predictive signals, Z-Guard utilizes high-speed, motor-driven seat repositioning to adjust the occupant's posture before a collision to reduce injury risk.
- If a vehicle cannot detect a crash in time or lacks active repositioning capabilities, Z-Guard provides robust protection through dual pretensioners, cushion buffering, and a collapse mechanism to prevent forward movement and submarining.
- The seating concept is designed to meet the evolving demands of cockpit electronic architecture in the era of software-defined vehicles by seamlessly integrating safety features into the smart cockpit environment.
- Adient is leading the commercialization of Z-Guard.
- Z-Guard is scheduled for production in a high-volume model from a major global OEM.
- Adient (NYSE: ADNT) is a global leader in automotive seating with around 70,000 employees in 29 countries.
- Adient operates more than 200 manufacturing/assembly plants worldwide and produces and delivers automotive seating for all major OEMs.
- Adient's expertise spans every step of the automotive seat-making process, from complete seating systems to individual components, taking products from research and design to engineering and manufacturing into millions of vehicles every year.
