# JCB

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2024-06-04

**Source:** [JCB BREAKS GROUND AT NEW $500 MILLION NORTH AMERICAN FACTORY](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/jcb-breaks-ground-at-new-500-million-north-american-factory-302164040.html)  ·  `page_028377`

## Facts

- JCB began construction on a new $500 million factory in San Antonio, Texas, marking the biggest investment in the company's history.
- An official groundbreaking ceremony was held on June 4, 2024, where The Hon. Alice Bamford turned the first shovelful of dirt.
- The new facility will occupy a 720,000 sq. ft. (67,000m²) building on a 400-acre site in Bexar County.
- Construction is underway to create 1,500 new jobs over five years.
- The factory will produce Loadall telescopic handlers and aerial access equipment, with production scheduled to start in 2026.
- The facility will have the capacity to expand and manufacture other products in the future.
- It will be JCB's second largest plant globally, rivaled only by its world headquarters in Rocester, Staffordshire, England.
- Chairman Lord Bamford stated that construction equipment manufacturers sell more than 300,000 machines annually in North America, making it the single largest market worldwide.
- Governor Greg Abbott noted that Texas was named The Best State for Business for a record-breaking 20th year and welcomed JCB as a new business to the state.
- JCB CEO Graeme Macdonald highlighted the vast opportunity for growth in North America and the significance of this record investment.
- Richard Fox-Marrs, President & CEO of JCB North America, explained that Texas is the largest consumer of construction equipment in the USA and San Antonio was chosen for its central location, supply chain proximity, and labor force.
- The Loadall telescopic handler is JCB's biggest selling product in North America and represents a single largest market for aerial access equipment worldwide.
- JCB sold its first machine in North America in 1964 and opened its first US manufacturing plant in Savannah, Georgia, in 2001.
- The existing Savannah, Georgia plant currently employs 1,000 people.
- JCB North America currently employs over 1,000 people and operates out of its headquarters in Savannah, Georgia.
- The company employs 19,000 people globally and operates 22 factories worldwide, including 11 in the UK, seven in India, and others in Brazil and China.
- JCB North America has regional offices in Dallas, Texas; Santa Monica, California; and Miami, Florida.
- JCB manufactures a range of more than 300 products for customers in 150 countries, including backhoe loaders, tracked and wheeled excavators, wheel loaders, compact excavators, skid steer loaders, compact track loaders, Access aerial work platforms, rough terrain forklifts, and Fastrac tractors.
- The new San Antonio facility will manufacture machines specifically for customers in North America.
- JCB will mark its 80th anniversary in 2025.
