# SK On and Hyundai Motor Group Joint Venture

**Type:** Joint Venture  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[battery-cell-manufacturing|Battery Cell Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** Bartow County, Georgia, Bartow County, Georgia, United States

## Overview

A 50/50 joint venture between SK On and Hyundai Motor Group to build a battery cell plant in Bartow County, Georgia, with an investment of $5 billion and an annual capacity of 35 GWh.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Battery cell manufacturing in Georgia to supply up to 300,000 electric vehicles per year.

- **Georgia Battery Cell Plant (Joint Venture)** — Expected to start manufacturing battery cells in the second half of 2025.
- **SK Battery America** — Started production of EV batteries in 2022; supports more than 200,000 EVs a year.

## Investment

- **$5 billion** — Construction of joint-venture battery cell plant
  - Investment by SK On and Hyundai Motor Group for the Bartow County facility.
- **$2.6 billion** (2022) — SK Battery America facility development
  - Investment to build and equip the Commerce, Georgia battery plant.

## Timeline

- **2022** — SK Battery America began production of EV batteries at its Commerce, Georgia site.
- **Second half 2025** — Joint-venture plant expected to start manufacturing battery cells.

## Key facts

- The joint venture will have an annual production capacity of 35 GWh, enough to support the production of 300,000 EVs a year.
- SK Battery America has an annual capacity of about 22 GWh, supporting more than 200,000 EVs a year.
- U.S. EV sales increased by 70% in 2022 to reach nearly 800,000 units (IEA).
- SK has pledged to invest $85 billion in green businesses and cut carbon emissions by 200 million tons by 2030.
- The Bartow County plant is strategically located near Hyundai Motor Group’s existing and planned U.S. facilities.
