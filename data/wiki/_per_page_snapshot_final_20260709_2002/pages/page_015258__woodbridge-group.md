# Woodbridge Group

## Facts

- Woodbridge has corporate headquarters in Mississauga, Ontario, Canada, and automotive headquarters in Troy, Michigan, USA.
- The company employs more than 7,000 teammates across over 40 locations in eight countries.
- Woodbridge offers material technologies for automotive, commercial, recreational, packaging, healthcare, and building product applications.
- The company provides prototyping, consulting, technical support, chemical R&D, and accredited laboratory testing services.
- Woodbridge supports OEMs with performance data, equipment monitoring, virtual capabilities, and computer modeling to analyze and optimize production for Manufacturing 4.0 and digitization integration.
- For driverless vehicles, Woodbridge provides mass reduction solutions including thinner seating profiles, condensed packaging, and enhanced configurability for adjustable or stowable parts.
- The company helps OEMs create quieter cabins through acoustic solutions that reduce noise, vibration, and harshness.
- To address the growing electrified vehicle sector, Woodbridge offers lightweighting solutions with structural integrity to offset electric battery weight.
- Woodbridge's seating solutions for electrified vehicles insulate and dissipate heat to minimize power usage and extend battery charge.
- For ride-hailing and public transit applications, Woodbridge delivers seating solutions designed for increased passenger volume and frequency, offering short-term comfort and long-term durability.
- The company utilizes hypoallergenic and antimicrobial materials in its seating solutions to improve cabin air quality by lowering volatile organic compound (VOC) emissions.
- Woodbridge has developed high-performance polyurethane spray foam thermal-insulating solutions that deliver industry-leading temperature transfer resistance for construction insulation.
- The company provides complete seating systems and assemblies engineered for commercial, industrial, powersports, and personal-use transportation.
- PolyLink Solutions, a subsidiary of Woodbridge, thermoforms and manages molded particle foam projects primarily supplying the automotive, pharmaceutical, and construction markets.
- PolyLink's engineered products include one-way protective packaging, insulated containers, returnable dunnage and trays, handheld totes, and plastic pallets.
- Woodbridge leverages extensive research and development in physical property testing, lab testing, and technical services.
- The company offers healthcare products designed to promote proper alignment, healing, circulation, and rehabilitation.
- Woodbridge partners with bedding and furniture science organizations, including universities and government agencies, to co-develop breathable and hypoallergenic polyurethane foam.
- Woodbridge delivers custom machinery, tooling, and chemical formulations to optimize assembly and product performance.
- The company utilizes lean processes to develop, iterate, and deliver solutions with speed and flexibility.
- Woodbridge maintains renowned R&D labs and extensive chemistry knowledge demonstrated through industry expertise in comfort.
