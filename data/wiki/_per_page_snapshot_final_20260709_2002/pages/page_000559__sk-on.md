# SK On

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-03-07

**Source:** [SK On Layoffs: SK On lays off nearly 1,000 workers at US battery plant amid EV slowdown, ETHRWorld](https://hr.economictimes.indiatimes.com/news/workplace-4-0/talent-management/sk-on-lays-off-nearly-1000-workers-at-us-battery-plant-amid-ev-slowdown/129202614)  ·  `page_000559`

## Facts

- SK On, a major South Korean battery manufacturer, has laid off nearly 1,000 workers at its US battery plant in Georgia amid slowing electric vehicle (EV) demand, according to a company filing on Saturday.
- SK Battery America, SK On's US unit, dismissed 968 employees at its battery manufacturing facility in Commerce, Georgia, per a Worker Adjustment and Retraining Notification (WARN) notice posted on a Georgia state website.
- The layoffs account for about 37 per cent of the plant's total workforce of around 2,500 employees, according to Yonhap news agency.
- SK On stated the workforce reduction is part of restructuring efforts prompted by slowing EV sales and changing market conditions.
- The company made the decision to adjust operations while maintaining its commitment to the state of Georgia and efforts to build a strong U.S. supply chain for advanced battery manufacturing.
- The Georgia plant has been supplying EV batteries to automakers including Germany's Volkswagen and South Korea's Hyundai Motor.
- The plant also supplied batteries for Ford Motor's F-150 Lightning electric pickup truck.
- Profitability was affected after Ford canceled production plans for the F-150 Lightning model.
- SK On is currently building a second battery plant in Georgia to supply batteries to Hyundai Motor, with production scheduled to begin in the first half of this year.
- Another SK On plant in Tennessee is expected to begin production in 2028.
- In October last year, Governor Brian Kemp of Georgia met with the head of South Korean battery maker SK On to discuss ways to enhance cooperation and strengthen business ties.
- Governor Kemp and his delegation visited SK On's headquarters in central Seoul earlier that day.
- Governor Kemp held talks with Lee Seok-hee, chief executive officer (CEO) of SK On.
- Governor Kemp and Lee Seok-hee previously met in June 2024 during Kemp's visit to Seoul.
- SK On operates a 22 gigawatt-hour battery plant in Commerce, Georgia, through its U.S. unit, SK Battery America.
- SK On is building another facility jointly with Hyundai Motor Group in Bartow County.
