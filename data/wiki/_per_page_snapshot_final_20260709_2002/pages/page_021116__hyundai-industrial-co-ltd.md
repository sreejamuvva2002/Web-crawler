# Hyundai Industrial Co. Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-11-10

**Source:** [Korean Auto Supplier Set to Invest $24M in Coweta County, Hiring 100 - Global Atlanta](https://www.globalatlanta.com/korean-auto-supplier-set-to-invest-24m-in-coweta-county-hiring-100/)  ·  `page_021116`

## Facts

- Plans to invest $24 million in Coweta County.
- Plans to hire 100 people in Coweta County.
- Is a maker of seats, arm rests, and head rests for Kia and Hyundai.
- The investment and hiring plans were announced by Gov. Brian Kemp on Nov. 1.
- Is the latest supplier coming to Georgia to serve both the expanding Kia plant in West Point and Hyundai’s Metaplant under construction near Savannah.
- Will enter the U.S. through a subsidiary named HDI America Inc.
- Its subsidiary has purchased an existing building at 83 Amlajack Way near Interstate 85.
- Is based in Ulsan, South Korea.
- Was established in 1978.
- Operates two plants in Korea and two plants in China.
- Has 174 total employees globally.
- Has annual revenues of 264 billion Korean won, or about $200 million.
- Team Leader Woosuck Kim stated the company is “excited to be part of the e-mobility transition happening in Georgia.”
- Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson noted that with Kia in West Georgia and Hyundai on the eastern coast, suppliers like Hyundai Industrial can locate almost anywhere in the state and still be center of at least two major manufacturers.
