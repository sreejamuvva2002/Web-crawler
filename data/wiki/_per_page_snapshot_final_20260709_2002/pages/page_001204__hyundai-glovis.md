# Hyundai Glovis

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2026-04-15

**Source:** [Hyundai Glovis opens LA logistics center, integrated warehouse in Georgia](https://koreajoongangdaily.joins.com/news/2026-04-15/business/industry/Hyundai-Glovis-opens-LA-logistics-center-integrated-warehouse-in-Georgia/2569676)  ·  `page_001204`

## Facts

- Hyundai Glovis has opened a logistics center and an integrated warehouse in California and Georgia to sharpen its ability to navigate supply chain risks across North America.
- The Los Angeles logistics center spans roughly 12,000 square meters (129,000 square feet) and sits about 25 minutes by car from both the Port of Long Beach and the airport.
- Glovis plans to integrate transloading, airfreight, and warehousing and distribution services at the Los Angeles hub while strengthening its own inland transport capabilities.
- Hyundai Glovis stated that the Los Angeles center will serve as a key hub for integrated logistics across the western United States to enhance responsiveness to urgent shipments and enable more flexible cost management amid market volatility.
- Hyundai Glovis has set up a large-scale integrated warehouse in Savannah, Georgia, covering about 69,000 square meters.
- The Savannah warehouse is aimed at handling rising volumes tied to Hyundai Motor Group’s Metaplant America, a massive EV plant, as well as demand from third-party clients in the region.
- Savannah has emerged as a logistics nexus in the U.S. Southeastern areas thanks to its port and rapidly expanding manufacturing plants nearby.
- Demand for logistics services continues to grow in Georgia as supply chains link raw material imports to finished goods exports.
- Glovis stated that the close integration of port and inland transport networks positions Savannah as a strategic springboard for broader expansion across North America.
