# American Honda Motor Co., Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2020-11-07

**Source:** [Honda Unifies Manufacturing Operations and R&D in North America;
Sales Organization Streamlines Auto Sales and Parts & Service Field 
Operations | Honda Global Corporate Website](https://global.honda/en/newsroom/news/2020/c201107eng.html)  ·  `page_008910`

## Facts

- Honda announced plans to combine all of its automobile manufacturing facilities in the U.S. related to frame, engine, transmission, and related engineering and purchasing operations into one new company, to be named Honda Development & Manufacturing of America, LLC (HDMA).
- The new HDMA company will integrate the automobile product development operations of Honda R&D Americas, LLC (HRA).
- The unification of Honda’s manufacturing operations and merger with auto product development will be effective April 1, 2021.
- Shinji Aoyama serves as chief officer of North American Regional Operations of Honda Motor Co., Ltd., and president & CEO of American Honda.
- Automobile product design and certain market research functions currently part of the HRA Los Angeles Center will be integrated into the Regional Operations of American Honda Motor Co., Inc. based in Torrance, Calif.
- HDMA will centralize key business functions to become more efficient and eliminate redundancies.
- HDMA will execute new product development with increased speed and accuracy.
- HDMA will improve manufacturing characteristics with a focus on quality, cost competitiveness and the ability to deliver vehicles that appeal to customers in a timely manner.
- This restructuring mirrors an announcement in February 2020 by Honda Motor Co., Ltd. in Japan integrating the automobile development function of Honda R&D Co., Ltd. and key elements of Honda Engineering Co., Ltd. into parent company Honda Motor’s Automobile Operations.
- Auto-related Honda companies to be wholly or partially consolidated into HDMA include Honda of America Mfg., Honda Manufacturing of Alabama, Honda Manufacturing of Indiana, Honda Transmission Manufacturing of America, Honda Precision Parts of Georgia, Honda Engineering North America, Honda R&D Americas, and Honda Accessory America.
- American Honda announced plans to streamline its Auto Sales and Parts & Service Field operations to enable a more agile response to local market needs.
- In January 2021, Honda and Acura zone offices will be restructured to improve communication between dealers, zone management and National Sales and Parts & Service management.
- The sales restructuring includes four new Regional Manager positions created to provide oversight of Honda Sales, Acura Sales and Parts, Service & Technical field operations.
- The new Regional Managers will become the key link between the company’s National and Field operations, ensuring better alignment among all staff who call on Honda and Acura dealers.
- Decision making authority will be pushed down to local staff to more quickly address dealer and customer needs.
- Honda companies in the U.S. responsible for Powersports business, including motorcycle, ATV and side-by-side products, began restructuring earlier in 2020 to unite the functions of sales, manufacturing, product development and purchasing.
- Honda of South Carolina Mfg., responsible for powersports manufacturing in the U.S., will formally become part of the Powersports business unit within American Honda.
- This follows a similar reorganization in Japan in 2019 when the Motorcycle Operations of Honda Motor and the Motorcycle center of Honda R&D were merged into one organization.
- Honda established operations in North America in 1959.
- The company employs more than 40,000 associates in the region engaged in the development, manufacturing, sales and service support of Honda and Acura automobiles, Honda power equipment, Honda powersports products, the HondaJet advanced light jet and GE Honda HF120 turbofan engines.
- Honda operates 19 major manufacturing plants in North America, working with more than 740 suppliers in the region to produce a diverse range of Honda products.
- In 2019, more than 90 percent of the Honda and Acura automobiles sold in the U.S. were produced in North America, using domestic and globally sourced parts.
- Honda operates 14 major research and development centers in North America which jointly fully design, develop and engineer many of the products the company makes in North America.
