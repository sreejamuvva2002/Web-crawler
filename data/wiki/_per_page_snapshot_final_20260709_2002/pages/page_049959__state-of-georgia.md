# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-05-22

**Source:** [Georgia Takes The Lead In Attracting Clean Energy Projects](https://thebusinessdownload.com/georgia-takes-the-lead-in-attracting-clean-energy-projects/)  ·  `page_049959`

## Facts

- Georgia ranks first in the nation for clean energy projects announced since the Inflation Reduction Act passed in August 2022, with 27 announcements.
- Michigan ranks second with 26 clean energy project announcements.
- The announced deals translate to $15,086,500,000 in investment and 15,481 jobs.
- EV manufacturers have set up shop in Georgia.
- Hyundai has several ongoing operations in the state.
- In Cartersville, Bartow County (northwest of Atlanta), Hyundai is teaming up with SK On on a $4 to $5 billion battery manufacturing plant.
- The Hyundai and SK On joint venture will create more than 3,500 jobs.
- Hyundai is investing more than $4.3 billion in a joint venture with LG Energy Solutions to build a battery cell manufacturing facility in Savannah.
- The Savannah facility is expected to be up and running at the end of 2025.
- Once operational, the Savannah facility will supply batteries for 300,000 EVs.
- The Savannah facility will support more than 3,000 jobs annually.
- Supplier Hyundai Mobis is investing $926 billion in a factory in Richmond Hill.
- The Hyundai Mobis factory is expected to come online this year.
- The Hyundai Mobis factory will create 1,500 jobs.
- The Hyundai Mobis factory will produce 900,000 power systems annually.
- The Hyundai Mobis factory will produce 450,000 integrated charging control units annually.
- The Hyundai/SK On/LG/Mobis projects act as feeders for a $7.6 billion EV and battery production “meta plant” in Ellabell, Bryan County.
- The Ellabell meta plant is expected to begin producing 300,000 EVs per year starting in 2025.
- The Ellabell meta plant will eventually employ 8,500 workers.
- The Ellabell plant was originally announced in May 2022 as Hyundai's first factory in the U.S. dedicated specifically to building EVs.
- Hyundai later added more financing to the Ellabell project.
- The state government offered $2.1 billion in tax breaks for the Ellabell project.
- The Ellabell project is the biggest economic development deal with the biggest incentive package in Georgia’s history, taking the crown from Rivian.
- Rivian was among the first automakers to announce a presence in Georgia.
- In 2021, Rivian announced an investment of $5 billion in Morgan and Walton counties east of Atlanta.
- Rivian received an incentive package of $1.5 billion for its Georgia plant.
- The Rivian plant will eventually employ 7,500 people.
- The Rivian plant will build 400,000 cars annually.
- Rivian temporarily paused construction on the Georgia plant to advance production of its R2 vehicle at its Illinois plant.
- Construction work on the Rivian Georgia plant will eventually continue.
- Kia is expanding its presence in Georgia.
- In July 2023, Kia announced an additional investment of $200 million for its plant in West Point.
- The Kia West Point expansion will create 200 more jobs.
- The West Point factory is Kia's only manufacturing plant in the U.S.
- The West Point factory produces more than 40% of Kia's cars sold in the U.S.
- Kia's additional West Point investment is mainly directed toward building its first electric models in North America.
- Kia's first electric model for the continent includes the 2024 EV9 three-row SUV.
- At the time of the July 2023 announcement, Kia had already invested more than $1.9 billion in Georgia.
- Governor Brian Kemp designated Jan. 31 as “Kia Day” in recognition of Kia's investments.
- In 2019, South Korea-based SK Innovation announced a $1.67 billion investment in two battery plants in Commerce.
- The SK Innovation Commerce plants were expected to create 2,000 jobs.
- In 2020, the SK Innovation Commerce project was expanded by an additional $940 million and 600 jobs.
- In 2022, FREYR Battery announced it would spend $2.57 billion on a battery cell manufacturing plant called Giga America in Coweta County.
- The FREYR Giga America plant will create 723 jobs.
- FREYR considered 170 locations in 34 states before selecting Georgia for its plant.
- In 2022, Ascend Elements (then called Battery Resourcers) selected Covington for a 154,000-square-foot battery recycling facility.
- Ascend Elements invested $43 million in its Covington facility and created 150 jobs.
- In 2023, Anovion declared it would spend $800 million on a facility in Bainbridge.
- The Anovion facility will supply synthetic graphite anode materials for batteries.
- The Anovion facility will create 400 jobs.
- Georgia is focusing on its solar industry.
- In 2019, Korea-based QCells opened a solar panel manufacturing plant in Dalton.
- The QCells Dalton plant employs more than 750 people.
- The QCells Dalton plant produces 12,000 panels daily.
- In 2022, QCells expanded its Dalton plant, creating 510 new jobs.
- In 2022, QCells announced a $171 million investment for a new solar module manufacturing plant in Dalton.
- The new QCells Dalton solar module plant will add 470 more jobs to the state.
- In January 2023, QCells announced an additional $2.5 billion in financing.
- The January 2023 QCells investment is the biggest solar investment in U.S. history.
- The $2.5 billion funding went toward expanding the Dalton plant and building a new solar manufacturing facility in Cartersville.
- The new Cartersville facility will produce solar panels and parts like ingots, wafers, cells, and modules.
- The QCells expansion marked the first solar plant expansion since the passage of the IRA.
- QCells’ operations in Georgia represent the country’s first complete solar supply chain.
- Later in 2023, supplier Hanwha Advanced Materials Georgia, Inc. announced it would spend $147 million on a manufacturing facility for encapsulant film in Cartersville.
- The Hanwha facility will supply the QCells plant and create 160 jobs.
- In 2021, NanoPV invested $36 million in a manufacturing and distribution plant in Americus.
- The NanoPV plant created over 500 jobs.
- Suniva closed down a factory in Norcross in 2017 amidst financial problems.
- In 2023, Suniva announced it was reopening the Norcross plant and creating 240 jobs following the passage of the IRA.
- In February 2024, SOLARCYCLE announced it will invest $344 million in a solar glass manufacturing plant in Cedartown.
- The SOLARCYCLE plant will create 600 jobs.
- Governor Brian Kemp told the Associated Press in January that he sold “the state from an economic development standpoint” at the World Economic Forum in Davos, Switzerland.
- On a panel about EVs at Davos, Kemp explained that companies recruiting to Georgia want to produce with clean energy.
- At last year's Davos meeting, Kemp jokingly called himself a “Georgia redneck going to Davos” to Politico.
- Ahead of the previous Davos meeting, Kemp touted the economic and workforce benefits of inviting the new wave of clean energy manufacturing to Georgia.
- Kemp told Politico he is fulfilling his promise of creating good-paying jobs for the state.
- Kemp noted to Politico that many conservatives are driving electric vehicles.
- Kemp advised conservatives to test drive an EV because “it’ll snap your head back.”
- At this year's Davos meeting, Kemp discussed the benefits of the first new nuclear reactors built in the U.S. in over 30 years.
- The Georgia Power project consists of two reactors at Plant Vogtle.
- Vogtle Unit 3 went live in July 2023.
- Vogtle Unit 3 has the capacity to power about 500,000 businesses and residences for the next 60 to 80 years.
- Vogtle Unit 4 delivered its first electricity to the grid on March 1.
- Commercial operations for Vogtle Unit 4 are expected to follow shortly.
- When all four reactors are running, Plant Vogtle will be the country’s biggest clean energy generator.
- The IRA was a huge boon to Georgia's clean energy economy, but the state has long pursued this agenda.
- Kemp told the Atlanta Journal-Constitution that companies would get federal incentives in Texas, Michigan, or Georgia.
- Kemp stated that companies are coming to Georgia because the state created the necessary ecosystem well before the IRA passed.
- The state is progressing toward Governor Kemp's vision outlined in his 2023 inaugural address.
- Kemp stated in his 2023 inaugural address that he intends for Georgia to be recognized as the electric mobility capital of America by the end of his second term.
