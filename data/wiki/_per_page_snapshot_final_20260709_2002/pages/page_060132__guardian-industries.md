# Guardian Industries

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2014-11-20

**Source:** [Gloucester VA Links and News: Governor McAuliffe Announces 30 New Jobs in the City of Galax](https://www.gloucestercounty-va.com/2014/11/governor-mcauliffe-announces-30-new.html)  ·  `page_060132`

## Facts

- Guardian Industries will invest $1.57 million to expand operations at its Consolidated Glass & Mirror facility in the City of Galax.
- The expansion project will create 30 new jobs.
- Guardian Industries is the world's largest manufacturer of float glass and fabricated glass products.
- Governor Terry McAuliffe announced the investment, noting the company has been an important corporate citizen in Galax since 1979.
- Maurice Jones, Virginia Secretary of Commerce and Trade, stated the expansion solidifies Guardian's commitment to the region and praised Galax's business environment and infrastructure.
- Consolidated Glass & Mirror Corporation was founded in Galax in 1979 and became a subsidiary of Guardian Industries in 1992.
- Guardian is a diversified global manufacturing company headquartered in Auburn Hills, Michigan.
- Guardian holds leading positions in float glass, fabricated glass products, and building products for commercial, residential, and automotive applications.
- Guardian's automotive trim group, SRG Global Inc., is one of the world’s largest suppliers of advanced, high-value coatings on plastics.
- Through its Science & Technology Center, Guardian develops high-performance glass coatings and other advanced products.
- Guardian, its subsidiaries, and affiliates employ 17,000 people and operate facilities throughout North America, Europe, South America, Asia, Africa, and the Middle East.
- Plant Manager Dennis Carroll stated the company appreciates assistance from the Commonwealth, the City of Galax, and Virginia’s aCorridor in making the project a reality.
- The Virginia Economic Development Partnership worked with the City of Galax and Virginia’s aCorridor to secure the project for Virginia.
- The Virginia Tobacco Indemnification and Community Revitalization Commission approved $125,000 in Tobacco Region Opportunity Funds to assist Galax with the project.
- Funding and services to support employee training activities will be provided through the Virginia Jobs Investment Program.
- Galax Mayor C.M. Mitchell said helping local industry succeed is the surest way of revitalizing the economy and praised the 30 new jobs for recognizing citizens' production capabilities.
- Senator Bill Carrico, a member of the Virginia Tobacco Indemnification and Community Revitalization Commission, congratulated Consolidated Glass and Mirror on bringing 30 new jobs to Galax.
- The announcement highlights Consolidated's capital investment of $1.5 million, which will ultimately benefit the entire Twin County region.
