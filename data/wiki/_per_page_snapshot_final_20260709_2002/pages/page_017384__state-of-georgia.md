# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-02

**Source:** [Unlocking Innovation in Georgia’s $27 Billion Mobility Industry - Albany CEO](https://albanyceo.com/news/2024/02/unlocking-innovation-georgias-27-billion-mobility-industry/)  ·  `page_017384`

## Facts

- Total planned investments in Georgia’s e-mobility and battery ecosystem topped $27.3 billion in the five years between 2018-2023.
- The first Georgia-made electric car, the EV9, prepares to roll off the line at Kia Georgia in West Point this year (2024).
- Georgia is historically a top destination for job seekers looking to reduce living expenses.
- Georgia works with employers to identify opportunities to attract and train the local labor pool.
- Georgia Quick Start is helping the state meet the demands of the clean energy boom.
- Georgia Quick Start recently unveiled plans to support the training of more than 8,500 Georgians for Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA).
- CNBC ranked Georgia as the No. 1 state for infrastructure in the U.S. in 2023.
- Georgia has two deepwater ports, one of which is ranked among the fastest growing and the other a top port for automotive Ro/Ro in the country.
- Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport is located near the center of the state and is the busiest airport in the world.
- Cities across Georgia are connected by a seamless network of interstates, highways, and rail lines.
- Reliable energy grids are diversifying Georgia’s energy mix through renewable options, including solar energy and Plant Vogtle.
- The state is supporting the availability of low-cost energy, including solar and hydrogen.
- Georgia is bringing together energy technology thought-leaders to foster innovative ecosystems.
- Georgia’s cutting-edge mobility ecosystem consists of original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) Kia, HMGMA, Rivian, and Archer Aviation.
- Battery manufacturers in the ecosystem include SK Battery America and LG Energy Solution.
- Metal and battery-material recyclers in the ecosystem include Aurubis AG and Ascend Elements.
- Hydrogen fuel cell manufacturer Plug Power is part of Georgia’s mobility ecosystem.
- Synthetic graphite manufacturer Anovion Technologies is part of Georgia’s mobility ecosystem.
- At CES 2024 in Las Vegas, Hyundai Motor Group (HMG) unveiled plans for the Clean Logistics Project.
- The Clean Logistics Project involves HMGMA deploying XCIENT Fuel Cell tractors to manage logistics at the new Metaplant in coastal Georgia.
- GDEcD can be connected with at trade shows including NAATBatt, InterBattery, and SelectUSA Investment Summit.
