# Kia

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [2026 Kia EV4: Wild-Styled EV Sedan (Hopefully) Won't Break the Bank - CNET](https://www.cnet.com/home/electric-vehicles/2026-kia-ev4-first-look-new-york-auto-show/)  ·  `page_010078`

## Facts

- Kia debuted the 2026 Kia EV4 electric sedan at the 2025 New York Auto Show.
- The EV4 is positioned at the entry point of Kia's lineup as a more affordable option for North American shoppers.
- The EV4 utilizes Hyundai Motor Group's Electric Global Modular Platform (E-GMP), which underpins nearly all HMG's dedicated EV models including the Ioniq 5 and EV9.
- The EV4 is scheduled to arrive in early 2026.
- The EV4 is powered by a single 150-kilowatt (201-horsepower) electric motor mounted on the front axle.
- The EV4 will be offered with two 400-volt battery options.
- The base EV4 Light trim includes a 58.3 kilowatt-hour battery pack expected to deliver up to 235 miles of range per charge.
- The EV4 Wind and top-spec EV4 GT-Line trims feature an 81.4 kWh battery unit promising up to 330 miles of Kia-estimated range for the lighter Wind trim.
- The EV4 can charge from 10% to 80% in 29 to 31 minutes depending on the battery via a built-in North American Charging System (NACS) port.
- The EV4's charging port is relocated to the front passenger side to facilitate convenient nose-in parking at Tesla Supercharger stations.
- The EV4 features version 3.0 of Kia's i-Pedal variable regenerative braking system, which automatically adjusts regeneration based on speed and distance to leading vehicles.
- The i-Pedal v3.0 system now operates in reverse and retains the driver's preferred regeneration level when the vehicle is restarted.
- Like other E-GMP vehicles, the EV4 is compatible with Kia's vehicle-to-load (V2L) adapter, converting the external charging port into a 120-volt/15-amp power outlet for running appliances and tools.
- The interior features a dual 12.3-inch display digital dashboard paired with a dedicated 5-inch climate display.
- The cabin tech suite includes the new Kia AI Assistant for natural language voice commands to minimize driver distraction.
- Available technology includes Digital Key 2.0, wireless Android Auto and Apple CarPlay connectivity, and standard Highway Driving Assistant safety tech.
- The EV4's software will be maintained via over-the-air updates and feature-on-demand purchases through the Kia Connect data connection, requiring a subscription after a three-year included trial.
- Final EPA range and pricing for the South Korean-manufactured EV are still pending announcement.
- More details on the EV4 are expected to be announced in the coming months.
- Kia also debuted the 2026 K4 Hatchback variant of its combustion-powered 2026 K4 sedan at the same event.
- The K4 Hatchback offers 22.2 cubic feet of cargo capacity behind the rear seats, expanding to 59.3 cubic feet when folded flat.
- The K4 Hatchback is approximately 11 inches shorter bumper-to-bumper than the sedan while maintaining the same rear legroom and gaining about an inch of rear headroom.
- The K4 Hatchback will be offered with a standard 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine producing 147 hp and 132 lb-ft of torque, or a turbocharged 1.6-liter engine producing 190 hp and 195 lb-ft of torque.
- First examples of the K4 Hatchback are expected to reach dealerships in late 2025.
