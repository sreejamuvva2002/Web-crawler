# Yamaha Motor Co., Ltd.

## Facts

- Yamaha Motor Co., Ltd. is a $17 billion global Japanese brand and the world’s second largest motorcycle manufacturer.
- The company announced February 26 that it is moving its U.S. headquarters from Cypress, California to Kennesaw, Georgia.
- The relocation will occur by function over the period from the end of 2026 until the end of 2028.
- Yamaha plans to sell all fixed assets in Cypress, including land, offices, and warehouses covering 279,000 sq. ft. across three buildings on a 25-acre campus on Katella Avenue.
- The company will utilize a sale-leaseback arrangement to ensure business continuity during the transition.
- Cypress served as Yamaha’s U.S. headquarters for 47 years after the company acquired the land in 1978 and established an office there in 1979.
- Georgia first became involved two decades later when Yamaha relocated its Marine Business Unit to Kennesaw in Cobb County to better serve customers.
- The Motorsports Business was also relocated to Kennesaw in 2019.
- Yamaha already owns and occupies a 458,760-sq.-ft. office building on 37 acres just off Chastain Road in Kennesaw.
- In 2023, Yamaha opened a 75,000-sq.-ft. Marine Innovation Center at the same Kennesaw site.
- Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corp. of America has operated a 1.3 million-sq.-ft. assembly plant in Newnan, Coweta County since 1986.
- The company leased an additional 25,000 sq. ft. at TownPark Commons in Kennesaw last November.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp announced the corporate relocation on March 10, confirming the state did not offer any incentives to convince Yamaha to move.
- Tariff pressures were cited as a driving factor for structural reforms aimed at improving profitability of U.S. operations due to cost increases from U.S. tariffs and market changes.
- The relocation decision was influenced by Georgia’s pro-business environment, infrastructure, workforce, and a face-to-face visit from Governor Kemp during economic development travel in Japan.
- Cobb County, where Kennesaw is located, has over 800,000 people and is the third largest county in Georgia.
- Since 2019, Cobb County has welcomed 40 state-worked projects representing 5,400 new jobs and $550 million of investments.
- Since 2021, a total of 103 California-headquartered firms have announced corporate facility investment projects in Georgia.
- Georgia’s workforce development strategy includes the MATCH program, which sends letters to high school seniors about university and technical college spots based on GPA.
- The state enacted tort reform to stabilize insurance costs and is pursuing legislation to modernize corporate governance litigation to make Georgia more attractive for incorporation.
- The Yamaha headquarters win adds to a series of recent automotive headquarters wins in Georgia, including Porsche, Mercedes-Benz, Asbury, and Cox.
- Governor Kemp attributes success in attracting automotive companies to a cooperative approach, low cost-of-living, reliable transportation and infrastructure, and a pro-business environment.
