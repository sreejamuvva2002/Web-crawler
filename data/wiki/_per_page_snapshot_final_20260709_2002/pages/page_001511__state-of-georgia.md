# State of Georgia

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-05-20

**Source:** [Georgia Earns Top Spot In Site Selection Ranking](https://flavip.com/georgia-earns-top-spot-in-site-selection-ranking/)  ·  `page_001511`

## Facts

- Georgia scored the top spot in Site Selection’s annual Prosperity Cup ranking of state-level successful investment recruiting in the previous year.
- Georgia unseated previous #1 North Carolina, which had claimed the top spot in the Site Selection ranking for the past three years.
- The news was celebrated this week in press releases from both the Governor’s Office and the Georgia Department of Economic Development.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that the award adds to a decade of evidence that the way business is done in Georgia is working, noting job creators get access to global markets through logistics assets like Hartsfield-Jackson International Airport and some of the fastest-growing ports in the nation, plus state and local partners.
- Georgia attracted 318 new and expanded facilities in 2023, leading all states.
- One of the top criteria for the Prosperity Cup is a state’s ability to attract new and expanded facilities in the previous year.
- Georgia led all states with 318 new and expanded projects including the expanded scope of the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant, the largest project in the history of Georgia.
- Originally announced in 2022, the South Korean automaker plans to produce a diverse range of innovative Hyundai, Genesis and Kia electric vehicles (EVs), as well as a new battery manufacturing facility northwest of Atlanta in Bartow County.
- Hyundai Motor Group is investing $7.59 billion to develop HMGMA, which will create 8,500 direct jobs for Georgians.
- Georgia got a boost in this year’s Prosperity Cup ranking when Hyundai expanded its commitment in 2023 by an additional $2 billion and 400 new jobs.
- Anovion Technologies announced an $800-million, 400-job synthetic graphite manufacturing facility in Decatur County.
- Meissner Corporation plans to double the company’s Georgia manufacturing footprint with a new $250 million facility in Athens scheduled to open in 2026.
- GDEcD Commission Pat Wilson credited the state’s pro-business environment as a solid contributing factor in putting Georgia over the top in this year’s Site Selection ranking.
- Pat Wilson stated that taking the top spot in the 2024 Prosperity Cup reaffirms that the Georgia Way works, citing decades of partnerships and laying the groundwork for intentional economic development from the community level to the state level, combined with consistent pro-business policies.
- Florida, North Carolina, and South Carolina earned spots behind Georgia in Site Selection’s ranking of “South Atlantic” states due to their pro-business environments.
- North Carolina earned the #2 spot in Site Selection’s ranking of “South Atlantic” states attracting new and expanded facilities.
- South Carolina earned the #3 spot, followed by #4 Florida.
- Other states earning the #1 spot for attracting new investment in different regions included Ohio (East North Central), Texas (South Central), Pennsylvania (Northeast), Kansas (West North Central), and Arizona (Mountain).
