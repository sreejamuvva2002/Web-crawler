# LG Energy Solution and Hyundai Motor Group joint battery cell plant

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-05-26

**Source:** [LG Energy Solution, Hyundai to build $4.3B joint battery cell plant in Georgia | Manufacturing Dive](https://www.manufacturingdive.com/news/lg-energy-solution-hyundai-4-3b-joint-battery-cell-plant-georgia/651422/)  ·  `page_001250`

## Facts

- LG Energy Solution and Hyundai Motor Group struck a joint venture deal to build a $4.3 billion battery cell plant.
- The companies announced the joint venture on Friday.
- The factory will be located in Savannah, Georgia.
- Production at the new facility is slated to begin at the end of 2025.
- The plant will have enough capacity to produce 300,000 EVs a year.
- LG Energy Solution and Hyundai Motor Group are both South Korea-based companies.
- Each company will take a 50% stake in the venture and facility.
- Hyundai will assemble battery packs using cells from the plant to be sent to its U.S. manufacturing sites.
- Hyundai and LG Energy Solution have worked together in the EV battery sector since 2009.
- In 2009, Hyundai introduced its Elantra Hybrid EV, for which LG Energy Solution provided batteries.
- LG Energy Solution also provided batteries for Hyundai’s Kona Electric and IONIQ 6 EVs.
- This joint venture is not the companies’ first partnership in this space.
- In September 2021, the companies broke ground on a battery cell manufacturing plant in Karawang, Indonesia.
- The $1.1 billion Indonesian plant is set to start production in the first half of 2024.
- A new memorandum of agreement will strengthen Hyundai’s and LG Energy Solution’s ongoing EV battery partnership.
- LG Energy Solution CEO Youngsoo Kwon stated: “Two strong leaders in the auto and battery industries have joined hands, and together we are ready to drive the EV transition in America.”
- Youngsoo Kwon added: “By further advancing our product competitiveness and global operational expertise, LG Energy Solution will commit our best efforts to offering the ultimate sustainable energy solutions to our customers.”
- Hyundai’s upcoming Georgia EV campus has been attracting other South Korea-based auto suppliers to the state.
- Georgia announced its eighth Hyundai supplier to set up shop near its EV campus on Tuesday.
