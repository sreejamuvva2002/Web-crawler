# Georgia’s First Solar-Powered Electric Vehicle Charging Station at West Point Visitor Information Center

**Type:** charging_infrastructure  ·  **Role:** charging_infrastructure  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- A partnership including The Ray C. Anderson Foundation and Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia took the first step in imagining “The Ray,” the Highway of the Future.
- Georgia’s first solar-powered PV4EV (photovoltaic for electric vehicle) charging station was installed at the Visitor Information Center (VIC) in West Point.
- The innovative charging station is powered by the SpotlightTM Solar LIFT.
- The EV charger is a level 3 “quick charge” station, capable of providing 80 percent + battery charge in less than 45 minutes for most EVs.
- It is the first in what is imagined to be a series of infrastructure upgrades that will make The Ray, a 16-mile section of I-85 in West Georgia, a model for the highway of the future.
- Students from the Georgia Tech School of Architecture studied ways to create a sustainable highway.
- They researched technologies including solar panels built into highways, asphalt that generates electricity as cars travel over, and air filtering plant walls to reduce highway emissions.
- Georgia ranks 2nd in the nation for registered electric vehicles.
- John Lanier, executive director and Ray Anderson’s grandson, stated the installation of the PV4EV is a progressive move to help promote more broad adoption of EV’s in Georgia.
- The charging station’s placement at the West Point VIC makes travel more convenient for electric vehicle drivers between West Georgia and Alabama.
- The Ray is a 16-mile stretch of road between exits 2 and 18 in Troup County.
- The Ray is named for Ray C. Anderson, founder of the global carpet tile manufacturer Interface, Inc.
- Anderson had an environmental epiphany in 1994 at the age of 60 and challenged Interface to become the “first name in industrial ecology.”
- Anderson led his company on a journey coined as “Mission ZeroⓇ,” influencing other business and industry leaders that it was both the right and the smart thing to do.
- The Ray is envisioned as a way to change the way we think about our infrastructure in the future.
- A dedication ceremony for the charging station took place on October 14th at the West Point Visitor Information Center.
- A Kia Soul was plugged into and charged by the PV4EV charging station during the ceremony.
- Attendees included representatives from the Ray C. Anderson Foundation, Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia, Hannah Solar, LLC, Board of the Georgia Department of Transportation, Mayors of LaGrange and West Point.
- Randy Jackson, KMMG’s senior vice president of human resources and administration, stated Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia is proud to be a leader when it comes to taking care of their environment and is at the forefront of the Mission Zero Corridor project to usher in regenerative, restorative and sustainable highways of tomorrow.
- Environment is one of the pillars that KMMG’s community relations program is built on, and this project fits into their goals with the potential to improve the region for decades to come.
- Partners include The Ray C. Anderson Foundation, Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia (KMMG), Hannah Solar, LLC, Spotlight Solar, City of West Point, GA, LaGrange-Troup County Chamber of Commerce, Georgia Department of Transportation (GDOT), Georgia Department of Economic Development, Georgia Tech School of Architecture and the Georgia Conservancy.
