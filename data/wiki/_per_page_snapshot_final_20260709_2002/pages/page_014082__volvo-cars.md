# Volvo Cars

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-05

**Source:** [Biden Tried To Ban Chinese Cars And Tech, Trump Just Gave Volvo The Green Light | Carscoops](https://www.carscoops.com/2026/05/volvo-connected-car-waiver/)  ·  `page_014082`

## Facts

- Geely-owned Volvo received a waiver for America’s connected car rule.
- The US Department of Commerce finalized a rule under the waning days of the Biden Administration to prohibit the sale and import of connected vehicle hardware, software systems, and completed connected vehicles from China and Russia.
- The United States justified the rule under national security concerns regarding China’s cyber espionage and intrusion operations threatening US critical infrastructure and public safety.
- The White House stated the rule would prohibit the import or sale of certain connected vehicle systems designed, developed, manufactured, or supplied by entities with ties to the PRC or Russia.
- Restricted connectivity systems and components include Bluetooth, cellular, satellite, and Wi-Fi modules as well as automated driving systems.
- Software restrictions under the rule were set to go into effect for the 2027 model year.
- Hardware restrictions under the rule were set to follow for the 2030 model year.
- Volvo was granted a "specific authorization" from the Office of Information and Communications Technology and Services that acts as a waiver preventing their vehicles and technology from being banned under the Securing the Information and Communications Technology and Services Supply Chain: Connected Vehicles rule.
- The waiver decision followed constructive discussions with the US Department of Commerce and other US officials regarding Volvo Cars’ governance, technology, and data security.
- The automaker is owned by Geely and has faced testy relations between Beijing and Washington.
- Volvo stated the authorization enables them to continue their growth plans in the United States.
- America is one of Volvo's largest markets.
- The brand's massive US plant employs over 2,000 people.
- The brand's massive US plant has received over $1.3 billion of investment.
- The Charleston, South Carolina plant started production in 2017.
- The Charleston, South Carolina plant currently builds the Volvo EX90 and Polestar 3.
- Two more vehicles will be built at the Charleston plant by 2030.
- The XC60 is scheduled to roll off the assembly line starting later this year (2026).
