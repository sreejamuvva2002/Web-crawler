# Kia Georgia, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Kia Georgia Donates $100,000 to Technical College System of Georgia Foundation – TCSG | Technical College System of Georgia](https://www.tcsg.edu/kia-georgia-donates-100000-to-technical-college-system-of-georgia-foundation/)  ·  `page_001074`

## Facts

- Kia Georgia presented a $100,000 donation to the Technical College System of Georgia (TCSG) Foundation at an event in Atlanta celebrating TCSG’s top students and instructors.
- Kia Georgia President and CEO Stuart Countess presented the check at TCSG’s annual GOAL and Rick Perkins Awards banquet in Buckhead.
- TCSG Commissioner Greg Dozier stated that Kia is a valued partner of TCSG and a remarkable corporate citizen in Georgia, and that the funding will help technical college students remain on track to graduate and join Georgia’s workforce.
- Since 2016, Kia Georgia has provided funding to award the state GOAL winner with a brand-new Kia as the grand prize.
- This year’s GOAL winner will receive a 2023 Kia K5, which is built at the Kia facility in West Point.
- The remaining donation funding will support The Last Mile Fund to provide gap funding for more than 131,000 technical education students served within TCSG.
- Stuart Countess, President & CEO of Kia Georgia, stated that TCSG undergirds workforce development by adapting to the evolving technical needs of state industries and businesses.
- Stuart Countess noted that TCSG's impact is seen through economic growth in Georgia and the continued application of latest technologies in manufacturing, logistics, and health care.
- In 2006, Kia made one of the largest single foreign investments in Georgia's history by establishing a state-of-the-art production facility in West Point.
- Kia Georgia has an annual capacity of more than 340,000 vehicles.
- Kia Georgia is home to the Telluride SUV, Sorento SUV, Sportage SUV, and K5 midsize sedan.
- The company recently announced plans to produce the Kia EV9 at the West Point facility, launching its first battery-electric vehicle produced in the U.S.
- The advanced manufacturing facility represents a $1.9 billion investment on a 2,200-acre site and operates 24 hours a day.
- Kia Georgia’s partnership with TCSG produced a Maintenance Certification Program through collaboration with Quick Start to upskill production team members for maintenance tasks on highly automated production equipment.
- The Maintenance Certification Program was expanded into West Georgia Technical College and integrated with the campus's standard offerings.
- This program provides a steady stream of qualified candidates to fill the need for maintenance team members at Kia Georgia and other local manufacturers.
- Kia Georgia, Inc. is the first manufacturing site in North America for Kia Corporation based in Seoul, Korea.
- Kia Georgia began mass production on Nov. 16, 2009.
- Kia Georgia will soon produce the all-electric 2024 EV9 SUV.
