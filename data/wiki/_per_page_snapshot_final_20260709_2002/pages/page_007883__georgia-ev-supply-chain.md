# Georgia EV Supply Chain

**Type:** unknown  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [A QUIET REVOLUTION – Site Selection Magazine](https://siteselection.com/a-quiet-revolution/)  ·  `page_007883`

## Facts

- The University of Georgia operates the largest fleet of electric buses on any college campus in the country.
- UGA’s fleet consists of 33 Proterra E2 buses managed by Transportation & Parking Services.
- The electric buses are constructed from corrosion-resistant fiber and have a potential life expectancy of 30 years, which is more than twice that of diesel buses.
- Don Walter serves as the Director of Campus Transportation at UGA.
- Federal and state grants totaling nearly $18 million aided the acquisition of the Proterra E2 buses.
- The electric buses now make up one-third of the University’s student bus portfolio.
- The EV buses consume approximately $10 worth of electricity per day, costing about one-tenth as much as diesel.
- Yearly maintenance costs for the buses dropped from $22,000 to roughly $7,500 per bus.
- Proterra reports that over a 12-year span, its electric buses will displace more than 2.5 million gallons of diesel.
- The buses eliminate over 4.5 million pounds of carbon emissions annually.
- The buses charge overnight at twelve charging stations installed at the campus transit facility.
- A team of UGA engineering students is redesigning the transit facility to improve efficiency and eventually integrate solar panels.
- Students are exploring additional uses for bus batteries to potentially provide power to other parts of campus.
- Jason Perry works as a specialist with UGA’s Office of Sustainability.
- One of the world’s largest EV battery installations is expected to launch production in 2022, located just 20 miles north of Athens.
- SK Battery America is constructing a $1.67 billion facility near Commerce in Jackson County.
- SK Battery America plans to invest an additional $940 million to accommodate exploding demand.
- SK Battery America is contracted to produce lithium-ion battery cells for the Ford F-150 Lightning and Volkswagen.
- Volkswagen is expanding its production site in nearby Chattanooga to assemble all-electric SUVs beginning in 2022.
- Since 2017, at least ten companies aside from SK Innovation have made investments in EV-related production in Georgia.
- In November 2020, Georgia announced that TEKLAS would invest in opening its first North American facility and headquarters in Dalton.
- TEKLAS is a Turkish advanced research and development, manufacturer, and supplier of EV parts.
- GEDIA Automotive Group announced plans to build an advanced manufacturing facility in Calhoun, Georgia.
- GEDIA specializes in making state-of-the-art automotive parts and will focus on lightweight structural automotive parts for electric vehicles.
- In May 2021, Gov. Brian Kemp announced that Duckyang would invest $10 million to open its first U.S. manufacturing facility in Jackson County.
- Duckyang is an electric mobility parts supplier of automotive battery modules and energy storage systems.
- The new Duckyang facility will create 285 jobs in Braselton, located not far from SK Battery.
- Dong-in-Son is the CEO of Duckyang.
- Enchem, a Korean manufacturer specializing in electrolytes for rechargeable batteries and electrostatic double-layer capacitors, chose Jackson County to supply SK Battery.
- Enchem will invest $61.35 million in two plants expected to create more than 300 jobs.
- John Scott serves as Jackson County vice president and director of economic development.
- Within about a 45-minute drive from the center of Jackson County, there is a population of at least 1.8 million people available to support manufacturing workers.
- Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia invested more than $1 billion on its first North American assembly plant in West Point, West Georgia.
- The Kia plant hired 3,000 Georgians and created work for countless suppliers in a network that followed it.
- Access to Atlanta’s Hartsfield-Jackson International Airport and the Port of Savannah were major factors in Kia’s decision to locate in West Point.
- The Georgia Quickstart training program offers free, customized training to manufacturers through the state’s extensive network of community and technical colleges.
- Stewart Countess is senior vice president and chief operations officer at Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia.
- In August, Governor Brian P. Kemp announced a statewide initiative to strengthen Georgia’s position as a national leader in the electric mobility industry.
- The Electric Mobility and Innovation Alliance (EMIA) is driven by the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD).
- EMIA includes partners from government, industry, electric utilities, education, nonprofits, and other stakeholders focused on advancing Georgia’s role in the electric mobility industry and its supply chain.
- Governor Kemp stated that Georgia has a proven record of investing early in resources and infrastructure needed to connect to the world and develop jobs of the future.
- The five EMIA committees will develop objectives for Supply Chain, Infrastructure, Workforce, Innovation, and Policy/Initiatives.
- The Carl Vinson Institute at the University of Georgia will host EMIA’s committee meetings and integrated research forums.
- According to a report by the Alliance for Automotive Innovation, automakers are planning to invest $250 billion in electrification by 2023.
- IHS Markit predicts there will be 130 EV models available in the U.S. by 2026.
- Georgia is positioned as the center of the southeast automotive corridor with close proximity to the region’s automotive original equipment manufacturers.
