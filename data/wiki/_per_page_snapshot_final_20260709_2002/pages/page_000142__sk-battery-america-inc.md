# SK Battery America Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [US Department of Labor finds electric vehicle battery maker again exposes workers to serious health hazards after investigation of Commerce fire | U.S. Department of Labor](https://www.dol.gov/newsroom/releases/osha/osha20240408)  ·  `page_000142`

## Facts

- Federal workplace safety inspectors found a global electric vehicle battery manufacturer exposing employees to serious safety and health hazards at its Commerce plant for the second time in less than a year.
- Workers suffered potentially permanent respiratory damage in an October 2023 lithium battery fire at the facility.
- OSHA determined SK Battery America Inc. failed to train its employees on how to protect themselves adequately in the toxic atmosphere that left multiple workers injured.
- The agency cited SK Battery for five serious violations.
- The company exposed workers to inhalation hazards, including hydrofluoric acid vapors produced in lithium battery fires, by failing to establish a complete emergency response plan.
- The company did not ensure its staffing agency, Moveret Inc., who supplied workers at the site, made their employees aware of the hazards associated with lithium battery fires.
- The company failed to train on-site emergency responders and members of the fire brigade on hazards associated with lithium battery fires and equip them to treat employees exposed to such hazards.
- The company did not train workers on hazardous chemicals in their work areas and a means to protect themselves from lithium battery fires.
- The company failed to annually fit-test workers required to utilize tight-fitting facepiece respirators.
- OSHA has assessed the company $77,200 in proposed penalties, an amount set by federal statute.
- In December 2023, OSHA cited SK Battery for exposing employees working with cobalt, nickel, and total dust to levels above the Permissible Exposure Limit.
- The December 2023 inspection found the employer failed to institute feasible administrative or engineering controls for chemical hazards.
- The December 2023 inspection also found the employer exposed workers to high levels of occupational noise and failed to implement a monitoring program and audiometric testing.
- Another inspection resulted in a citation that was issued in January 2024 for a hazard associated with the company’s energy control program.
- The company is contesting these findings before the independent Occupational Safety and Health Review Commission.
- OSHA Area Office Director Joshua Turner stated that on multiple occasions in less than a year, SK Battery America has failed to meet required federal standards designed to help every worker end their shift safely.
- Joshua Turner noted that while emerging industries bring innovation and employment opportunities to communities, employers must ensure the safety of their employees is their priority.
- A subsidiary of SK On, SK Battery America Inc. employs about 3,100 people at two battery manufacturing plants in Commerce.
- SK On is part of the Seoul-based SK Group, South Korea’s second-largest conglomerate with companies operating in the energy, advanced materials, biopharmaceuticals and digital industries.
