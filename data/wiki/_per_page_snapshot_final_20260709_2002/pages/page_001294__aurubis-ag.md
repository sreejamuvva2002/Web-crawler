# Aurubis AG

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2025-10-08

**Source:** [Aurubis sets course for multimetal growth and stronger returns](https://www.aurubis.com/en/media/press-releases/press-releases-2025/aurubis-sets-course-for-multimetal-growth-and-stronger-returns)  ·  `page_001294`

## Facts

- Aurubis AG is a leading global provider of non-ferrous metals and one of the world’s largest copper recyclers.
- The company hosted its Capital Market Day in London on Wednesday, October 8, 2025.
- CEO Dr. Toralf Haag outlined a revised strategy titled “Performance 2030: Forging resilience. Leading in multimetal” to guide the company’s future growth and market position.
- Aurubis is positioned to capitalize on structural demand surges for copper and multimetals driven by electrification, AI, advanced technology, energy infrastructure modernization, and geopolitical shifts.
- The company can recover 20 different metals and elements from a wide range of primary and secondary raw materials.
- Aurubis operates an integrated smelter network across Europe and the US to provide scale, flexibility, and operational resilience.
- The company is shifting its focus from strong investment-driven growth to delivering impact from investments and pursuing smart, targeted growth aligned with core strengths.
- Initiatives include expanding commercial reach and service level to access new material streams, strengthening existing ones, and doubling down on securing product competitiveness to maximize profitability.
- Aurubis will boost its position as a leading global multimetal provider by maximizing technical expertise and optimizing its smelter portfolio.
- A focus on operational excellence will allow Aurubis to create more efficient material flows, alleviate system bottlenecks, and draw on internal expertise in processing low-grade material to maximize multimetal yields and process more complex intermediates in-house.
- The company expects to consistently grow its earnings and cash flow generation with an emphasis on strengthening resilience and profitability.
- Aurubis presented an update on its US expansion, highlighting the start of production at a new, first-of-its-kind multimetal recycling facility in Richmond, Georgia.
- The Richmond facility represents a major milestone and is Aurubis’s largest investment outside Europe at approximately €740 million.
- The facility significantly increases recycling capacity and secures Aurubis’s strong position in one of the most attractive global metals markets.
- The site is designed to meet surging North American demand for sustainable metal supply driven by infrastructure investment, supportive industrial policy, and resource security needs.
- CEO Dr. Toralf Haag stated that Aurubis built the first greenfield smelter in the US in over a century and considers their timely entry into the US market a strong foundation for further growth.
- Financial guidance for fiscal year 2025/26 includes operating EBITDA between €580 million and €680 million.
- Guidance for 2025/26 includes operating EBT between €300 million and €400 million, aligned with market expectations.
- Operating ROCE guidance for 2025/26 is set between 7% and 9%.
- Net cash flow guidance for 2025/26 ranges from €640 million to €740 million.
- The company targets a free cash flow break-even before dividend for fiscal year 2025/26.
- Mid-term financial outlook commits to a maximum net leverage of 3.0 and an equity ratio above 40%.
- Aurubis has set a long-term ROCE target of 15%.
- The company introduced a mid-term dividend payout ratio of up to 30% of operating consolidated net income after taxes.
- For fiscal year 2024/25, the company aims for a dividend payout ratio of 25% of operating consolidated net income after taxes.
- CFO Steffen Hoffmann commented that Aurubis combines strong cash generation with a very solid balance sheet and multiple earnings drivers underpinning its business model strength.
- Corporate Communications contacts listed are Christoph Tesch (Head of Corporate Communications) and Meino Hauschildt (Manager Corporate Communications, Spokesperson).
