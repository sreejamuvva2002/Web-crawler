# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Questions arise about how many Georgians work at Hyundai megasite - WJCL](https://www.wjcl.com/article/georgia-hyundai-immigration-employment/66038776)  ·  `page_009248`

## Facts

- Hyundai previously announced that once the plant becomes fully operational, it will employ more than 8,000 workers.
- A South Korean charter plane departed for the United States on Wednesday to repatriate Korean workers detained last week during an immigration raid at the Hyundai Megasite in Bryan County.
- Homeland Security Investigations said a total of 475 people were detained in the operation at the HL-GA Battery Company construction site.
- Most of the detained individuals are Korean nationals.
- According to the Savannah Economic Development Authority, the detained individuals were not full-time, permanent employees but were instead working temporary construction and installation jobs at the site.
- The raid has raised broader questions about local employment versus foreign employment at the Hyundai Metaplant and its affiliates.
- SEDA told WJCL 22 News that Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) and its current onsite affiliates have created 2,824 jobs to date.
- Of the 1,364 positions specifically at Hyundai, just over 73% are held by workers from the greater Savannah area.
- Hyundai said 99% of the future workforce will be Americans.
- HMGMA told WJCL 22 News it currently has 49 expatriates.
- At one point, Hyundai had 56 expatriates.
- The number of expats at the site will continue to drop until they hit a maintenance number.
- HMGMA said it will always maintain an expat presence to assist in communication with Hyundai’s main office in Korea, which is the same as any other foreign-based company.
