## Facts

- Rivian Automotive announced Thursday that it is pausing construction of its $5 billion manufacturing plant in Georgia to speed production and save money.
- The author claims the Georgia plant will never be built.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp addressed Rivian's announcement at a press conference on Tuesday, calling the decision 'disappointing' but stating the state remains optimistic.
- Gov. Kemp stated that Rivian and Georgia remain 'committed' to the $5 billion manufacturing plant and that they are under contract with the automaker.
- Kemp said the state is in a 'really good place' regarding the deal and that there is 'no doubt' about it.
- Gov. Kemp noted that Rivian can still meet its contractual metrics by 2030.
- The state has completed most of the $50 million in roadwork it pledged for the project.
- Signs for 'Rivian Parkway' at a new traffic signal on U.S. 278 had been removed as of last week.
- Electric vehicle startup Fisker warned that it risked running out of cash in 2024 and reported $273 million in sales last year alongside more than $1 billion in debt.
- Fisker hired financial adviser FTI Consulting and law firm Davis Polk to work on a potential bankruptcy filing.
- Fisker issued a going-concern warning citing 'substantial doubt' about its ability to stay in business.
- Henrik Fisker is facing his second bankruptcy court visit with an EV startup, following the 2013 collapse of Fisker Automotive.
- In 2021, Fisker’s investors bought him a $21 million mansion in the Hollywood Hills, and he sold $20 million worth of stock at $9.50 per share around the same time.

- Stephen Scherr's total compensation at Hertz was $182 million, making him the third highest-paid CEO in the U.S. according to C-Suite Comp and the Wall Street Journal.
