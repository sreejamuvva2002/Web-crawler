# Murata Electronics North America Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** power_electronics  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Murata Electronics Launches NTC Thermistors For Cellular Phones - New Products](https://eepower.com/new-industry-products/murata-electronics-launches-ntc-thermistors-for-cellular-phones/)  ·  `page_047044`

## Facts

- Murata Electronics North America Inc. is located in Smyrna, GA.
- The company launched a new line of 0201-packaged negative-temperature-coefficient (NTC) thermistors.
- The company maintains that the new components are among the smallest chip thermistors available.
- Kozo Kagawa serves as Murata's group product manager.
- The NTC thermistor features an unusual inner construction intended to make the lead-free device suitable for high-density mounting in general circuits.
- Termination of the components is accomplished with a nickel barrier.
- The 0201-size chip provides good solderability, supports reflow soldering, and offers high long-time aging stability.
- The new components measure 0.6mm x 0.3mm.
- The components provide temperature compensation for transistors, ICs, and crystal oscillators in mobile communications equipment.
- Temperature compensation and sensing are also provided for LCDs, car audio equipment, and rechargeable batteries.
- Currently available resistance values are 10,000 ohms, with additional values to be expanded in the future.
- The parts are already in mass production.
- Murata will increase the manufacturing capability of all SMT-package NTC thermistors by as much as 50 percent by March.
- Component availability lead time is eight to ten weeks.
- Pricing ranges from $0.19 to $0.21 cents each in 100,000-piece quantities.
