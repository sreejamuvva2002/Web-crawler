# Hanon Systems

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** thermal_management  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hanon Systems Launches Innovative 4th Gen EV Heat Pump | EV Magazine](https://evmagazine.com/technology/hanon-systems-launches-innovative-4th-gen-ev-heat-pump)  ·  `page_055717`

## Facts

- Hanon Systems has announced the development of the world's first 4th generation heat pump system for EVs.
- The innovative thermal management component has been commercially available since July.
- The system extends EV driving range and enhances energy efficiency.
- Hanon Systems' portfolio includes heating, ventilation, air conditioning (HVAC) products, powertrain cooling, compressors, fluid transport, electronics, and fluid pressure.
- The company operates 50 manufacturing sites and three regional innovation centres across 21 countries.
- Hanon Systems employs more than 20,000 people worldwide.
- The fourth-generation heat pump system is the first to use a parallel heat source recovery method.
- The technology recovers waste heat from the motor, battery, and external air for heating, cooling, and battery temperature management.
- By improving energy efficiency, the system significantly enhances the driving range of EVs.
- The modular heat pump system incorporates patented and patent-pending technologies.
- It integrates the Refrigerant Control Module (RCM) and the Coolant Control Module (CCM) into a single control unit for comprehensive thermal system management.
- Subu Nagasubramony serves as co-CEO of Hanon Systems.
- Co-CEO Subu Nagasubramony stated that continually refining heat pump system design improves efficiency and reduces power consumption requirements, contributing to extended EV driving range.
- Nagasubramony noted that as the world's second-largest thermal management company, Hanon Systems will continue striving for lightweight and high-efficiency components through continuous innovation.
- Hanon Systems pioneered heat pump systems with waste heat recovery, introducing the first mass-produced system in 2015.
- The second generation heat pump was launched in 2018, adding battery thermal management functions and becoming the first integrated system to enhance EV driving range.
- In 2021, the third generation was applied to a client's EV-dedicated platform and became the first to incorporate an 800V electric compressor, significantly reducing EV charging times.
- The 4th generation system was first implemented in the Kia EV3 in South Korea.
- Hanon Systems plans to expand production of the 4th generation heat pump system to the US and Europe.
- Yoon Ho Wang serves as Vice President of the AP Innovation Center at Hanon Systems Research Institute.
- VP Yoon Ho Wang stated that close collaboration with their client enabled the proactive development of the world's first new technology, contributing to improved EV driving experiences.
- Hanon Systems has introduced Thin HVAC technology, a world-first innovation.
- The reduced HVAC system decreases overall size by approximately 30%.
- The smaller HVAC system provides more legroom for passengers by minimizing exposure under the cockpit.
