# Hyundai-LG Energy Solution Joint Venture

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hyundai, LG Energy Solution to Open US Battery Plant - Tech Insights](https://eepower.com/tech-insights/hyundai-lg-energy-solution-to-open-us-battery-plant/)  ·  `page_010111`

## Facts

- A joint venture between Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution will result in a new lithium-ion battery plant in Georgia to supply EVs in North America.
- The $4.3 billion facility will be located in Bryan County, near Savannah, Georgia.
- The site is situated near where Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America is currently being built.
- Battery production at the joint venture plant is expected to begin at the end of 2025.
- The facility will have an annual production capacity of 30 gigawatt-hours (GWh).
- The plant's output will support the production of 300,000 electric vehicles.
- LG Energy Solution is the second largest supplier of lithium-ion batteries to the auto industry.
- LGES has partnerships and joint ventures with customers Ford, General Motors (GM), and Stellantis to supply their EV battery needs.
- LGES is building three battery plants in partnership with GM: one in Warren, Ohio (already opened); one in Spring Hill, Tennessee (will open in 2024); and a third in Lansing, Michigan (scheduled to open in early 2025).
- Including the new Hyundai joint venture, LGES will have seven battery plants currently operating or being constructed in the U.S.
- In the Georgia joint venture, each company will hold a 50 percent stake in the cell production facility.
- Hyundai will assemble the lithium-ion cells produced by the new plant into battery packs.
- Hyundai will provide these battery packs to its manufacturing facilities to produce Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis EV models.
- LGES supplies EV batteries for Hyundai Group models including the Elantra Hybrid, Kona Electric, and IONIQ 6.
- Hyundai is building a new $5.5 billion EV manufacturing plant in Georgia to build new Hyundai and Genesis models.
- The 2022 Inflation Reduction Act has led to plans for more than 20 new EV battery production facilities being announced.
- The Act ties incentives to buy EVs in the U.S. to a manufacturer’s North American production of vehicles and their battery packs.
- To be eligible for a $7,500 tax incentive, vehicles must be made in the U.S., Canada, or Mexico.
- SUV EVs must be under $80,000 to qualify for incentives, while the price cap is $55,000 for sedans, coupes, hatchbacks, and wagons.
- In 2020, the total market for battery EVs in the U.S. was under 2 percent.
- In 2022, the U.S. battery EV market rose to around 6 percent.
- By 2030, U.S. battery electric sales are projected to be 25 to 30 percent.
- Sales are projected to reach 45-50 percent by 2035 when companies like GM will no longer sell gasoline models.
- Car makers like Ford, GM, and Tesla are investing in domestic sources of lithium through established mining companies and new startups for valuable battery materials.
