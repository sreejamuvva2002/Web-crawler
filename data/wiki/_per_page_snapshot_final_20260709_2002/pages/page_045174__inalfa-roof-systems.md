# Inalfa Roof Systems

## Facts

- Gov. Nathan Deal announced that Inalfa Roof Systems will open a manufacturing plant in Cherokee County.
- The new plant represents a $17.1 million investment.
- The new plant will create 300 jobs for Georgians.
- Gov. Nathan Deal stated that Inalfa joins the dynamic auto industry Georgia has cultivated for more than 100 years.
- Gov. Nathan Deal noted that Georgia's central location, deep workforce resources, and logistics infrastructure have attracted well over 300 companies and give the state a competitive advantage in this manufacturing sector.
- Inalfa Roof Systems is a leading global supplier of sunroofs and open roofing systems for automotive manufacturers including Hyundai Kia, Ford, General Motors, Volvo, Chrysler Group, and BMW Group.
- The facility is strategically located off Interstate 75 at Cherokee 75 Corporate Park.
- The facility is in close proximity to automotive manufacturers located throughout the Southeast.
- Danny Colaris serves as vice president of North American Operations for Inalfa Roof Systems.
- Danny Colaris stated that after a four-year search for a location in the South, the company found its ideal site in Cherokee County.
- Danny Colaris noted that state and local economic development teams, as well as Georgia Power, were extremely helpful in introducing them to the strategic location and advantages of doing business in Georgia.
- The Cherokee County facility will be Inalfa's fourth plant in the U.S.
- The Cherokee County facility will be Inalfa's first in the Southeast.
- Inalfa has its global headquarters based in Venray, The Netherlands.
- Inalfa employs a global workforce of 3,000 employees.
- Inalfa’s North American headquarters is located in Michigan.
- Inalfa operates a total of four plants in Michigan and Mexico.
- Worldwide, Inalfa Roof Systems has facilities in Slovakia, South Korea, China, Brazil, and Japan.
- Inalfa is currently expanding its footprint in Poland.
- Building plans and preparation for construction for the Cherokee County facility have begun.
- The plant is projected to begin operating in January 2014.
- The new facility will provide job opportunities in engineering, operations and supply chain, purchasing, and manufacturing associates.
- Hiring for these positions will begin in the fourth quarter of this year.
- Hiring will continue through the second quarter of 2015 until all lines are installed and the plant is fully operational.
- Marshall Day serves as chairman of the Cherokee Office of Economic Development.
- Marshall Day stated that Inalfa Roof Systems is the first business to locate in the Cherokee 75 Corporate Park.
- Marshall Day thanked partners at CBRE, Rooker, GDEcD, and Georgia Power for their work on the project.
- Marshall Day noted that the new investment and jobs created will have a significant impact on Cherokee and the northwest Atlanta region.
- Wylly Harrison is the project manager for the Georgia Department of Economic Development.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development partnered with the Cherokee Office of Economic Development in the project location.
- Chris Cummiskey serves as Commissioner of the Georgia Department of Economic Development.
- Chris Cummiskey stated that investment by international companies creates jobs and contributes to the state’s economy.
- Chris Cummiskey noted that commitments from top-notch businesses like Inalfa help other investors understand how Georgia can help them become successful in the U.S. market.
- Inalfa Roof Systems is one of the world’s largest providers of vehicle roof systems.
- Inalfa designs, develops, and manufactures sunroofs and open-roof systems for automotive customers including BMW Group, Daimler, Chrysler Group, Ford, General Motors, Volkswagen, Audi, Volvo, Citroen, Chery, Geely, Hyundai Kia, Land Rover, Renault, and Nissan.
- Inalfa Roof Systems serves as a reliable engineering and assembly partner.
- Inalfa’s global footprint helps leverage its relationships with both customers and suppliers.
- Inalfa stands for innovation, technological competence, and quality.
- Due to its global presence, Inalfa is always in close proximity to its customers’ production lines.
