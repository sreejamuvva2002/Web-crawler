# Seohan Auto Georgia

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-04-27

**Source:** [Seventh auto supplier follows Hyundai to Georgia | WardsAuto](https://www.wardsauto.com/news/archive-auto-seohan-auto-georgia-7th-hyundai-supplier/651369/)  ·  `page_059428`

## Facts

- Seohan Auto Georgia plans to invest $72 million in a new manufacturing plant in Midway, Georgia.
- The South Korea-based auto parts manufacturer will produce H/shafts, axles and brake systems for OEMs.
- Components will be produced for Hyundai’s nearby EV and battery manufacturing hub.
- Seohan Auto Georgia’s upcoming facility is expected to create 180 jobs.
- Production at the new facility is slated to start in late 2024.
- Seohan Auto Georgia is the seventh Hyundai supplier to move to Georgia since Hyundai broke ground on its $5.5 billion EV campus in October.
- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp called Hyundai’s plant the “largest economic development project in state history.”
- Gov. Kemp estimated Hyundai’s site would bring at least 8,100 jobs to Georgia’s coastal region.
- Georgia officials had predicted Hyundai’s Metaplant would attract offsite suppliers to invest an estimated $1 billion.
- Hyundai’s suppliers have outpaced that estimate, with plans to invest nearly $2 billion in the state, according to an April 11 announcement.
- Gov. Kemp stated: “The Hyundai Metaplant is and will continue to be an economic driver for the entire region, creating more jobs for hardworking Georgians in communities like Liberty County.”
- Joon Georgia became the first supplier to announce plans for the state in November, with a $317 million production facility in Statesboro.
- Hyundai Mobis became the second Hyundai supplier later that month, announcing plans for a $926 million EV power electric systems plant.
- Since January, Ecoplastic Corporation, Seoyon E-Hwa, Sewon America and PHA have each announced plans for manufacturing plants to supply Hyundai’s manufacturing hub.
