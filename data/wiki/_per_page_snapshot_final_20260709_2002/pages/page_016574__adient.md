# Adient

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2019-09-30

**Source:** [Adient's Profit Dropped 7%, but It Could Have Been Worse | The Motley Fool](https://www.fool.com/investing/2019/11/07/adients-profit-dropped-7-but-could-have-been-worse.aspx)  ·  `page_016574`

## Facts

- Adient is an auto-seat supplier.
- On Nov. 7, Adient reported its adjusted operating income for the quarter ended Sept. 30 fell 7% from a year ago to $138 million due to weaker sales in the U.S. and China.
- Adient's adjusted earnings per share of $0.63 beat Wall Street's average estimate of $0.26 per share as reported by Thomson Reuters.
- Adient's revenue of $3.92 billion was roughly in line with analysts' average estimate of $4.0 billion.
- The financial result was better than investors had expected, largely due to cost improvements.
- Adient uses a fiscal year that begins on Oct. 1, making the quarter ended Sept. 30, 2019, its fourth quarter of fiscal 2019.
- Q4 2019 revenue was $3.92 billion, representing a 5% year-over-year decline.
- Q4 2019 EBIT was $102 million, which was $1.146 billion higher than the prior year period.
- Q4 2019 adjusted EBITDA was $215 million, a 14% year-over-year decline.
- Q4 2019 net income was a loss of $4 million, marking a decline of $2.645 million.
- Q4 2019 adjusted net income was $59 million, down 52%.
- Beginning in the second quarter of fiscal 2019, Adient changed its business segment reporting from global results for seating, seat structures and mechanisms, and interiors to regional results for Americas; Europe, Middle East, and Africa (EMEA); and Asia.
- Americas adjusted EBITDA was $64 million in Q4 2019, down from $70 million a year ago, due to lower sales volumes partly driven by a 40-day strike that idled General Motors' U.S. factories, partially offset by cost reductions.
- Americas adjusted EBITDA margin was 3.3%, down 0.2 percentage points from a year ago.
- EMEA adjusted EBITDA was $47 million in Q4 2019, down from $55 million a year ago, due to higher manufacturing costs somewhat offset by more favorable commodity prices.
- EMEA adjusted EBITDA margin was 3.1%, down 0.4 percentage points from the same period last year.
- Asia adjusted EBITDA was $126 million in Q4 2019, down from $146 million a year ago, as China's auto market slumped and Adient's direct sales fell alongside equity income from its Chinese joint ventures.
- Excluding equity income, Asia's adjusted EBITDA margin rose to 10.2% in Q4 2019 from 9.2% in the year-ago quarter, helped by lower operating costs.
- As of Sept. 30, Adient had $924 million in cash and cash equivalents, down from $1.03 billion as of June 30.
- Adient had $3.738 billion in total debt as of Sept. 30, compared to $3.777 billion as of June 30, resulting in net debt of $2.814 billion.
- For the fiscal year ending Sept. 30, 2020, Adient expects revenue between $15.6 billion and $15.8 billion (fiscal 2019 result was $16.53 billion).
- For the fiscal year ending Sept. 30, 2020, Adient expects capital expenditures between $465 million and $485 million (fiscal 2019 was $468 million).
- For the fiscal year ending Sept. 30, 2020, Adient expects adjusted EBITDA between $820 million and $860 million (fiscal 2019 was $787 million).
