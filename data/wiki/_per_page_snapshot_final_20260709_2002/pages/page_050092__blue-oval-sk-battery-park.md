# Blue Oval SK Battery Park

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-06

**Source:** [Ford breaks ground on $5.8B Blue Oval SK Battery Park in Kentucky](https://www.teslarati.com/ford-blue-oval-sk-battery-park-kentucky-groundbreaking/)  ·  `page_050092`

## Facts

- Ford has officially broken ground on its $5.8 billion Blue Oval SK Battery Park in Glendale, Kentucky.
- The project is a joint venture between Ford and SK (SK On).
- The facility consists of two massive battery manufacturing plants capable of more than 80-gigawatt hours of production annually.
- Production at the park is scheduled to begin in 2025.
- The project will create 5,000 new jobs in the region upon commencement of production.
- New workers will receive training at a new institution at Elizabethtown Community and Technical College before starting operations.
- The plant is expected to help increase Ford’s electric vehicle production output to two million units per year.
- SK On President and CEO Jee Dong-seob stated that the park will be at the core of the electrification of the North American auto market.
- SK On President and CEO Jee Dong-seob added that the company expects its leadership in the global electric vehicle market to be solidified through BlueOval SK alongside Ford.
- Construction is currently on schedule with significant progress being made at the site.
- Site preparation has involved moving 4.3 million cubic yards of soil.
- Workers have laid 283,000 tons of stone at the construction site.
- Construction crews have poured 66,000 cubic yards of concrete for the facility.
- The project has installed 3,300 tons of rebar ties to reinforce the concrete structure.
- Workers have installed 1,300 deep foundations at the site.
- Construction crews have erected 7,900 tons of structural steel for the buildings.
- Ford is also developing a separate $5.6 billion battery manufacturing plant in West Tennessee known as BlueOval City.
- The BlueOval City project in West Tennessee will also produce a future electric pickup truck model.
- Ford broke ground on the BlueOval City project in late September.
