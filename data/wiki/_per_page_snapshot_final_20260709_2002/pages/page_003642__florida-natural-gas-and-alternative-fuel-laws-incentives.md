# Florida Natural Gas and Alternative Fuel Laws & Incentives

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2030-01-01

**Source:** [Alternative Fuels Data Center: Natural Gas Laws and Incentives in Florida](https://afdc.energy.gov/fuels/laws/NG?state=FL)  ·  `page_003642`

## Facts

- Local governments in Florida may use income from an infrastructure surtax to provide loans, grants, or rebates to residential or commercial property owners to install electric vehicle chargers, propane fueling stations, and natural gas fueling stations, provided a local government ordinance authorizing this use is approved by referendum.
- Natural gas and propane retailers in Florida must obtain a license from the Florida Department of Revenue.
- Effective January 1, 2030, a retailer that does not hold a valid natural gas or propane license is subject to a penalty of 25% of the tax assessed on total purchases.
- Before January 1, 2030, sales of natural gas fuel (including propane, compressed natural gas, and liquefied natural gas) are subject to two taxes per motor fuel equivalent gallon adjusted annually based on CPI change from a 2013 baseline: a State Comprehensive Enhanced Transportation System tax with a 2.9¢/gallon base rate, and an additional “privilege of selling natural gas fuel” tax with a 4.6¢/gal base rate rounded to the nearest tenth of a cent.
- Beginning on January 1, 2030, propane, compressed natural gas, and liquefied natural gas used to fuel motor vehicles are subject to state motor fuel taxes consisting of an excise tax per gasoline gallon equivalent (GGE), a ninth-cent fuel tax, and a local option fuel tax, plus an additional variable component determined annually by the Florida Department of Revenue.
- Starting January 1, 2030, the applicable state motor fuel taxes for alternative fuels are set at $0.02 GGE excise tax, $0.05 ninth-cent fuel tax, and $0.05 local option fuel tax.
- Starting January 1, 2031, the applicable state motor fuel taxes for alternative fuels change to $0.04 GGE excise tax, $0.01 ninth-cent fuel tax, and $0.01 local option fuel tax.
- To determine the variable tax component, the Florida Department of Revenue will require each propane and natural gas retailer to file monthly electronic reports beginning in February 2030.
- For taxation purposes, one GGE is equal to 5.66 pounds or 126.67 standard cubic feet of compressed natural gas; 6.06 pounds of liquefied natural gas; and 1.35 gallons of propane.
- Natural gas utilities in Florida may recover costs associated with renewable natural gas projects, including infrastructure and supply investments, through rates approved by the Florida Public Service Commission.
- Any motor vehicle equipped with idle reduction technology may exceed gross vehicle or internal bridge weight limits by up to 550 pounds, provided the operator presents written verification of the certified weight and demonstrates the technology is fully functional at all times.
- Natural gas vehicles (NGVs) may exceed gross vehicle or internal bridge weight limits by up to 2,000 pounds under the idle reduction exemption.
