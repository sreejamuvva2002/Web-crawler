# Georgia Department of Economic Development

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2016-04-05

**Source:** [areid29's Blog | Built Environment Description: www.georgia.org](https://sites.gsu.edu/areid29/2016/04/05/built-environment-description-www-georgia-org/)  ·  `page_049150`

## Facts

- The Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) plans, manages, and mobilizes state resources to attract business to the state of Georgia.
- GDEcD promotes the state as a tourist destination and a site for on-location filming.
- GDEcD's website navigation menus highlight Competitive Advantages (Incentives, Workforce & Lifestyle), Business Resources (Growth, Relocation & Innovation), Industries in Georgia (Key Sectors & Strengths), and About Us (Contact & News).
- A scrolling billboard on the homepage displays news and important items on a timer of about 30 seconds.
- One poster displayed on the scrolling billboard states Mercedes-Benz’s intention to open facilities in Georgia.
- Another poster displayed on the scrolling billboard states that Georgia is home to 20 Fortune 500 companies.
- The website features a list of dates for important events, including the Brazil Trade Mission, Greater Women’s Business Council, and Leadercast Live – Architects of Tomorrow.
- GDEcD's Newsroom posts recent stories in chronological order and allows users to filter them by sector: Commerce, Tourism, Centers of Innovation, Entertainment, Workforce, Arts and Music.
- The website includes a language flag system that redirects visitors to pages regarding international trade with specific countries.
