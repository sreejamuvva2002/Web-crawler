## Facts

- Archer Aviation is an aerospace company with a market cap of $4.75 billion developing electric vertical takeoff and landing (eVTOL) aircraft to transform urban air mobility.
- The company ended 2024 with its strongest ever cash position and significant progress in manufacturing, certification, and commercial readiness.
- Cathie Wood's flagship ARK Innovation ETF (ARKK) accounts for 2.7% of Archer Aviation stock.
- Archer is set to report its first-quarter fiscal 2025 earnings on May 12.
- Archer stock is down 9.4% year to date, while the broader market has fallen 3.1%.
- Wall Street forecasts the stock to soar around 50% from current levels.
- Archer’s flagship model is the Midnight, designed for dense urban environments using a combination of electric propulsion and advanced AI-powered flight software specifically for short-distance flights.
- The company envisions air taxis alleviating urban congestion while lowering carbon emissions.
- As of now, Archer has not generated revenue from commercial operations.
- Adjusted EBITDA losses totaled $368.9 million in 2024.
- With the launch of Midnight, Archer is actively preparing for revenue-generating operations in late 2025.
- The company remains committed to obtaining certification from the Federal Aviation Administration (FAA).
- Archer is pursuing a dual-path commercialization strategy by deploying Midnight aircraft outside the U.S., primarily in countries that do not require FAA type certification such as the UAE.
- This international track is supported by a new partnership with Abu Dhabi Aviation.
- Partnerships with Etihad for pilot training and Falcon Aviation for infrastructure development help solidify the foundation for scalable operations in the UAE and beyond.
- Domestically, Archer is collaborating with United Airlines (UAL) and Southwest Airlines (LUV) to establish urban air mobility services.
- Archer has collaborated with Palantir Technologies (PLTR) to leverage its Foundry and Artificial Intelligence Platform (AIP) to accelerate the scaling of its aircraft manufacturing capabilities.
- Archer expanded into defense via a partnership with Anduril to collaboratively develop a next-generation hybrid propulsion vertical lift aircraft for both military and civilian applications.
- The Midnight is fully assembled and currently undergoing final ground tests at Archer’s Salinas, California test facility.
- Archer's new Georgia manufacturing facility is now complete and will produce up to 10 Midnight aircraft this year.
- The company expects an adjusted EBITDA loss of $95 million to $110 million in the first quarter of 2025 due to ongoing investment in engineering, production, and commercialization.
- Archer ended 2024 with $835 million in cash and cash equivalents, resulting in a liquidity position of more than $1 billion.
- This liquidity was largely enabled by successful equity raises in 2024 that raised $820.4 million.
- Management stated that the company’s liquidity does not yet include Stellantis’ potential $400 million capital commitment to support scaling up production of the Midnight aircraft, which Archer expects to finalize in the first quarter.
- Analysts predict revenue will be around $28.1 million in 2025 before rising by 404.9% to $142 million in 2026.
- Canaccord Genuity analyst Austin Moeller reiterated a “Buy” rating, citing strategic momentum, innovative edge, the Midnight unveiling, plans for a New York Air Taxi Network, collaborations with aviation industry leaders, and the aircraft’s low noise profile.
- Overall, Archer Aviation stock is rated a “Moderate Buy” on Wall Street, with five analysts rating it “Strong Buy,” two saying “Moderate Buy,” and two rating it “Hold.”
- The average target price of $11.39 is 32% above current levels.
