# Hanon Systems

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** thermal_management  ·  **Published:** 2024-08-09

**Source:** [New fourth-generation heat pump system for EV thermal management - EV Engineering & Infrastructure](https://www.evengineeringonline.com/new-fourth-generation-heat-pump-system-for-ev-thermal-managemen/)  ·  `page_001394`

## Facts

- Hanon Systems announced the development of the world’s first fourth-generation heat pump system for electric vehicle thermal management.
- The new heat pump system is the first to employ a parallel heat source recovery method that uses waste heat from the motor and battery along with heat from external air.
- Recovered heat from the system is used for heating, cooling, and battery temperature management to significantly enhance EV driving range.
- The modular heat pump system incorporates multiple patented or patent-pending technologies.
- The system integrates the Refrigerant Control Module (RCM) and Coolant Control Module (CCM) into a unified control unit for comprehensive thermal system management.
- Hanon Systems pioneered the mass production of heat pump systems with waste heat recovery in 2015.
- The company’s second-generation heat pump system was launched in 2018 and added battery thermal management functions.
- The third-generation system was developed in 2021, applied to a client’s EV-dedicated platform, and was the first to incorporate an 800-V electric compressor to reduce EV charging times.
- The fourth-generation heat pump system was first implemented in the Kia EV3 in South Korea.
- Hanon Systems plans to expand production of the fourth-generation heat pump system to the US and Europe.
- Subu Nagasubramony, co-CEO of Hanon Systems, stated that continually refining their heat pump design improves efficiency and reduces power consumption requirements, contributing to extended EV driving range.
- Hanon Systems is the world’s second-largest thermal management company.
- The company introduced Thin HVAC technology, a world first that reduces the overall HVAC system size by approximately 30%.
- The reduced HVAC system size minimizes exposure under the dashboard, providing more passenger legroom while ensuring a comfortable driving experience.
