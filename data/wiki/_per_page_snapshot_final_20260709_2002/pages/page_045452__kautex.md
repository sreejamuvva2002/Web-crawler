# Kautex

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2014-01-13

**Source:** [Lavonia Industry Announces Expansion - WNEG](https://wnegradio.com/lavonia-industry-announces-expansion/)  ·  `page_045452`

## Facts

- Kautex officials announced the company will add another 95,000 square feet of warehouse space to its existing plant on Highway 17 South.
- The expansion amounts to a $3.3 million investment.
- Kautex is a subsidiary of Textron, Inc., which is based in Bonn, Germany.
- Kautex is considered one of the top 100 automotive suppliers worldwide.
- The company is a leading global supplier of plastic fuel tank systems, selective catalytic reduction systems, and clear vision systems.
- Kautex operates 15 plants worldwide.
- In Lavonia, the facility manufactures fuel systems for major automakers including GM, BMW, Daimler-Chrysler, Nissan, Honda, and Mercedes-Benz.
- Kautex Director of Operations Tyler Miles stated the expansion will accommodate a new product line called SCR Systems for diesel trucks and vehicles.
- According to Miles, the SCR System tank holds a urea fluid that ejects into the catalytic converter, mixes with nitrous oxide, and converts emissions from carbon into water.
- Miles stated this process dramatically reduces emissions for diesel-fueled cars and trucks.
- The SCR System will be installed in both diesel trucks and diesel-powered vehicles.
- Kautex is beginning production of SCR Systems for Volkswagen small products, including the Golf, Jetta, and Passat.
- The company notes significant growth in the smaller vehicle segment.
- The Lavonia plant is the only facility in North America that provides this technology to customers.
- Georgia Commissioner of Economic Development Chris Carr attended the expansion announcement.
- Chris Carr noted that Site Selection Magazine named Georgia the number one state in the nation to do business last year.
- Chris Carr said, “Today we’re here to congratulate Kautex for their exciting announcement and for the contribution of Lavonia and Franklin County to the State of Georgia.”
- Kautex first opened its Lavonia facility in 1993 with 20 employees.
- The original product manufactured at the facility was a window fluid system for the automotive industry.
- In 2003, Kautex expanded into the fuel system segment of the industry.
- The 2003 expansion added a 20,000 square foot facility to the existing building.
- The company plans to open the new addition this spring.
