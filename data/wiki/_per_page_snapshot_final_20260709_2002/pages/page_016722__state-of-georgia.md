# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2026-03-06

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp: Georgia Breaks Trade Records, Becomes Top 10 Exporting State | The Citizen](https://thecitizen.com/2026/03/06/gov-kemp-georgia-breaks-trade-records-becomes-top-10-exporting-state/)  ·  `page_016722`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp joined the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) in announcing the state has again made history by surpassing $60.2 billion in exports in calendar year 2025.
- Georgia's export value saw a year-over-year increase of 12.7%, outperforming the national average of 5.6%.
- Georgia exported goods to 213 unique countries and territories in 2025.
- Georgia ranked No. 7 for dollar value of total trade among U.S. states.
- Georgia ranked No. 9 in the country for dollar value of exports.
- Georgia was last in the top ten exporting states in 2020.
- Georgia companies increased exports over the past decade by 68.9% despite pandemic disruptions.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated Georgia has broken export records for five consecutive years.
- Georgia's coastal and inland ports are continuing to grow capacity.
- The state is making key investments in other logistics infrastructure.
- Civilian aircraft and ancillary parts remained the state's No. 1 export category.
- Computers, hardware, and storage units were a top export category for Georgia.
- Motor vehicles were a top export category for Georgia.
- Network communications equipment was a top export category for Georgia.
- Medical devices were a top export category for Georgia.
- Canada, Mexico, Singapore, Germany, and the Netherlands were the top destinations for Georgia exports.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated Georgia is outpacing the nation in year-over-year growth for trade and export values.
- Georgia's move into the Top 10 exporting states marks a shift in U.S. and global supply chains.
- Georgia serves as a global gateway for aerospace, automotive, agriculture, and advanced manufacturing.
- The state facilitated more than $210.7 billion in total trade in 2025.
- Facilitated trade increased by 5.8% year-over-year, outperforming the national average of 4.9%.
- Facilitated trade reached 219 unique countries and territories.
- The top five products by total trade were civilian aircraft and ancillary parts; computers, hardware, and storage units; motor vehicles; network communications equipment; and parts and accessories for motor vehicles.
- GDEcD Deputy Commissioner of Trade Lizann Grupalo stated Georgia's exports outpaced imports in year-over-year growth combined with record-breaking totals.
- The GDEcD Trade team provides hands-on support, customized consultations, and data-driven insights to large and small exporters.
- Georgia maintains more than a dozen international representatives covering over 60 global markets.
- Markets with full-time Georgia representation accounted for 79% of exports in 2025.
- Markets with full-time Georgia representation accounted for 88% of bilateral trade in 2025.
- Georgia is home to Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport, described as the busiest and most efficient airport in the world.
- The Port of Savannah is the largest single-terminal container facility and third-busiest container gateway in the U.S.
- The Port of Brunswick is one of the nation's top gateways for Roll-on/Roll-off cargo.
- Georgia offers a robust rail and highway infrastructure with more rail miles than any other state in the Southeast.
- Expanded connectivity through Georgia's inland ports and intermodal yards connects directly to deepwater ports by rail.
- GDEcD's annual International Trade Report is based on data from the U.S. Census Bureau and Trade Data Monitor.
- Trade Data Monitor tracks merchandise trade using four-digit Harmonized System (HS) codes and origin of movement.
- GDEcD's Trade team includes more than a dozen International Representatives located in strategic global markets to assist Georgia companies with expanding sales worldwide.
