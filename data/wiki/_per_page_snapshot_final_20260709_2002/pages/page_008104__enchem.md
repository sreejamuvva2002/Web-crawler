# Enchem

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-06

**Source:** [On the 4th, secondary battery electrolyte company Enchem said it expects to receive 10 million U.S. .. - MK](https://www.mk.co.kr/en/business/11484276)  ·  `page_008104`

## Facts

- Enchem expects to receive 10 million U.S. dollars this year through the U.S. Advanced Manufacturing Tax Credit (AMPC) system.
- AMPC is a system that provides tax credits for parts manufactured using advanced manufacturing technologies such as batteries, solar, and wind power in the United States.
- Electrolytes produced at Enchem's Georgia plant are recognized by the U.S. Internal Revenue Service as "electrode active substances," an item subject to AMPC.
- Enchem established a corporation in Commerce, Georgia, USA in 2019.
- Enchem began supplying electrolytes to global customers in 2023.
- Enchem received about 9.8 billion won in 2023 through AMPC.
- Enchem received 16.2 billion won in 2024 through AMPC.
- Enchem expects to supply about 10,000 tons of electrolytes to a multinational battery manufacturer this year.
- The multinational battery manufacturer's supply includes 7,500 tons of electrolytes supplied to China and 2,500 tons of electrolytes supplied to North America.
- An Enchem official stated, "We have local response capabilities based on key production bases in China, North America, and Europe, so we are in an advantageous position to absorb the demand of customers seeking to diversify our supply chains."
