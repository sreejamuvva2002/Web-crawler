# PHA

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-03-07

**Source:** [Hyundai supplier PHA to hire 400 workers in new $67 million Georgia plant | Fortune](https://fortune.com/2023/03/07/hyundai-supplier-plans-to-hire-400-workers-in-new-67-million-georgia-plant/)  ·  `page_021330`

## Facts

- PHA is a South Korean company that makes latches, hinges and other parts for vehicle doors and hoods.
- PHA will become the sixth major supplier to locate near a Hyundai Motor Group plant being built in coastal Georgia.
- PHA announced it would invest $67 million at a site about 15 miles (24 kilometers) west of Savannah.
- PHA plans to hire 400 workers for the new facility.
- PHA already has a plant in Montgomery, Alabama, near the Hyundai facility in that city.
- Hyundai said in May it would build a $5.5 billion plant to assemble electric vehicles and batteries in Ellabell, Georgia.
- The Hyundai plant site could grow to 8,100 employees and is slated to begin producing vehicles in 2025.
- PHA said operations would begin in 2024.
- Hyundai suppliers have now announced $1.8 billion of investments in the region.
- The state will pay to train workers, but the total incentive package from state and local governments wasn’t immediately clear Tuesday.
- The local community will donate land for the plant, said Angela Hendrix, a spokesperson for the Savannah Economic Development Authority.
- Chatham County would abate property taxes for 15 years, saving the company a projected 60% on taxes.
- Hendrix did not release a dollar value for the abatements.
- PHA also could qualify for nearly $8 million in state income tax credits, at $4,000 per job over five years, as long as workers make at least $31,300 a year.
- Sewon Precision Industry Co. plans a $300 million metal stamping plant near Savannah, with 740 workers.
- Seoyon E-HWA plans a $76 million plant near the city to make interior and exterior trim parts, hiring 730.
- Hyundai Mobis announced a $926 million powertrain plant in Richmond Hill, employing 1,500.
- Joon Georgia plans a $317 million plant near Statesboro, employing 630.
- Ecoplastic plans a $205 million plant with 456 workers in Register.
