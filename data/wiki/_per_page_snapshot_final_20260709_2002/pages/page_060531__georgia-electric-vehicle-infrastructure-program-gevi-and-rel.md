# Georgia Electric Vehicle Infrastructure Program (GEVI) and Related Incentives

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** charging_infrastructure  ·  **Published:** 2025-03-25

**Source:** [Georgia's EV charging incentives - AMPECO](https://www.ampeco.com/ev-charging-grants-incentives/a-closer-look-at-georgias-ev-charging-incentives/)  ·  `page_060531`

## Facts

- All NEVI (National Electric Vehicle Infrastructure) funding has been frozen by the new administration until further notice.
- Contracts for approved projects that have already been signed will still receive funding.
- Projects that were approved but not yet signed are highly likely to not receive funding.
- The NEVI funding freeze has created significant uncertainty for stakeholders in the electric vehicle infrastructure sector.
- Many stakeholders had been anticipating the funds to move forward with their projects.
- The future of these approvals remains unclear, depending on the administration’s review and potential changes to its priorities.
- Georgia National Electric Vehicle Infrastructure (NEVI) Deployment Program is at the forefront of Georgia’s efforts to revolutionize its electric mobility landscape.
- The GEVI Deployment Program was born out of the 2021 Bipartisan Infrastructure Law.
- Nearly $5 billion has been allocated nationally over a five-year span for NEVI, commencing in the fall of 2022.
- Georgia’s dedicated share of the national NEVI allocation amounts to approximately $135 million.
- The $135 million investment is earmarked to push forward the development of the state’s portion of the national EV charging network.
- The Federal Highway Administration approved the Georgia NEVI Deployment Plan on September 14, 2022.
- The Georgia Department of Transportation (DOT) is crafting a blueprint to actualize the approved NEVI plan.
- In Round 1 of the GA NEVI Deployment Program, GDOT is actively seeking experienced developers to undertake EV charging infrastructure projects.
- The NEVI program aims to design, install, finance, operate, and maintain EV charging infrastructure that aligns with the National Electric Vehicle Infrastructure Program.
- The due date for responses in the first round of the GA NEVI Deployment Program was November 17th, 2023.
- Cobb EMC’s EV Grant Program provides funding opportunities to businesses, property owners, multifamily unit owners, and government agencies interested in installing EV charging stations at their place of business.
- Cobb EMC grant recipients can receive anywhere from $500 to $5,000.
- Cobb EMC grants offset costs associated with purchasing and installing EV supply equipment (EVSE).
- Cobb EMC grants are allocated on a first-come, first-served basis until the funds are exhausted.
- Georgia Power supports the electrification of transportation through a series of programs including the Make Ready Electric Transportation Program.
- The Make Ready Electric Transportation Program provides financial assistance for businesses and organizations to install infrastructure required to support electric transportation charging systems at various facilities.
- Georgia Power's Level Up with Rebates program offers EV charger rebates for commercial customers who purchase or lease and install Level 2 EV chargers.
- The Level Up rebate can be as high as $500 per charger.
- The maximum rebate under the Level Up program is $2,500 for installing up to five chargers.
- Georgia Power offers Commercial, Workplace, and Residential incentives for business owners, property owners, and government agencies.
- Georgia’s comprehensive approach to EV adoption ensures that everyone can participate in the transition to cleaner transportation.
