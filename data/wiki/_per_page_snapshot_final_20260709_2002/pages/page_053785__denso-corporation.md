# DENSO Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2017-10-06

**Source:** [Governor Haslam, Commissioner Rolfe and DENSO Announce $1 Billion Investment, Expansion in Maryville - TNECD](https://tnecd.com/news/governor-haslam-commissioner-rolfe-and-denso-announce-1-billion-investment-expansion-in-maryville/)  ·  `page_053785`

## Facts

- DENSO announced it will expand its operations in Maryville, Tennessee.
- DENSO will invest $1 billion to make its Blount County facility a primary manufacturing center in North America for electrification and safety systems.
- The expansion will create approximately 1,000 new jobs in Maryville.
- Governor Bill Haslam stated the investment creates 1,000 new jobs and invests $1 billion in Blount County.
- DENSO has been a part of Tennessee for over 30 years.
- Governor Bill Haslam aims to make Tennessee the No. 1 location in the Southeast for high quality jobs.
- Commissioner Bob Rolfe stated Tennessee is No. 1 for growth in automotive employment in the Southeast since 2011, adding over 22,000 jobs.
- DENSO has a presence in Tennessee for nearly three decades with two locations: Maryville and Athens.
- DENSO currently employs roughly 4,500 people in Tennessee.
- The Maryville campus is DENSO’s largest U.S. manufacturing facility.
- The Maryville campus consists of four manufacturing plants on 194 acres.
- The Maryville campus is Blount County’s largest employer.
- Kenichiro Ito, chairman of DENSO’s North America Board of Directors and CEO of DENSO International America, stated the investment positions the company to meet dramatic shifts in the role of transportation in society.
- DENSO produces starters, alternators, instrument clusters, safety products, and a wide range of electronic components and systems for the automotive industry at its Maryville operations.
- Globally, DENSO is a leading developer of electrification systems for environmentally-friendly automobiles.
- DENSO develops sophisticated functions involved in vehicle safety and security.
- DENSO develops products that connect vehicles and society.
- DENSO develops thermal systems to cool the cabin and under-the-hood components.
- Jack Helmboldt, president of DENSO Manufacturing Tennessee, stated the investment enables new opportunities for top talent to advance the next generation of vehicles.
- The expansion will expand multiple production lines to produce advanced safety, connectivity, and electrification products for hybrid and electric vehicles.
- These new products will radically improve fuel efficiency and preserve electric power by recovering and recycling energy.
- The new products will connect all systems and products inside the vehicles.
- Prior to this expansion, DENSO had invested approximately $2 billion in Tennessee.
- Japan is the leading source of foreign direct investment in Tennessee over the last 30 years.
- Japanese businesses have invested more than $17 billion in Tennessee and employ more than 50,000 people.
- Blount County Mayor Ed Mitchell thanked DENSO for its commitment to the community and noted the economic impact across all business sectors throughout Blount County cannot be underestimated.
- Maryville Mayor Tom Taylor stated nearly 30 years ago the city made a commitment to DENSO and it has steadily grown into one of the major economic influencers for the economy.
- Alcoa City Mayor Don Mull thanked DENSO for its diligence in being an active member of the community and appreciated the impact on expanding for the future workforce.
- Blount County Industrial Development Board Chair Fred Lawson stated attracting and retaining good companies with above average wages is one of their tenets.
- Fred Lawson noted the advancement shows the commitment of the Blount Partnership, the State of Tennessee economic development office, and Governor Bill Haslam in making the area a leader in economic development.
- TVA Senior Vice President of Economic Development John Bradley congratulated DENSO on its plans to expand and create over a thousand new job opportunities in Blount County.
- Maryville and Blount County are represented in the Tennessee General Assembly by Sen. Doug Overbey (R – Maryville), Rep. Bob Ramsey (R – Maryville), and Rep. Art Swann (R – Maryville).
- DENSO’s North American headquarters is located in Southfield, Michigan.
- DENSO employs more than 23,000 people at 28 consolidated subsidiaries and 4 affiliates across the North American region.
- Of DENSO's North American facilities, 25 are manufacturing facilities located in the United States, Canada, and Mexico.
- In the United States alone, DENSO employs more than 17,000 people in Alabama, Arkansas, California, Iowa, Georgia, Kentucky, Michigan, North Carolina, Ohio, Pennsylvania, South Carolina, Tennessee, and Texas.
- DENSO’s North American consolidated sales totaled US$9.6 billion for the fiscal year ending March 31, 2017.
- DENSO Corp. is headquartered in Kariya, Aichi prefecture, Japan.
- DENSO has more than 200 subsidiaries and affiliates in 38 countries and regions worldwide.
- DENSO employs more than 150,000 people worldwide.
- DENSO’s consolidated global sales totaled US$40.4 billion for the fiscal year ending March 31, 2017.
- Last fiscal year, DENSO spent 8.8 percent of its global consolidated sales on research and development.
- DENSO common stock is traded on the Tokyo and Nagoya stock exchanges.
