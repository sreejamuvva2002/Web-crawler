# Manufacture of Advanced Key Energy Infrastructure Technologies (MAKE IT) Prize

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [DOE Awards $15 Million to Prize Winners for Domestic Clean Energy Manufacturing Facilities and Will Launch a New $30 Million MAKE IT Prize | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/technologycommercialization/articles/doe-awards-15-million-prize-winners-domestic-clean-energy)  ·  `page_004452`

## Facts

- The U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) announced three winners of the Manufacture of Advanced Key Energy Infrastructure Technologies (MAKE IT) Prize Facilities Track on September 30, 2024.
- Each of the three MAKE IT Prize Facilities Track winners received $5 million throughout the prize for demonstrating readiness to begin building a manufacturing facility for critical clean energy components.
- The DOE is announcing another iteration of the MAKE IT Prize, making an additional $30 million available in the next few months.
- The MAKE IT Prize Facilities Track is made possible by the Office of Technology Transitions (OTT) and the Office of Clean Energy Demonstrations (OCED).
- The prize required competitors to prove they have shovel-ready manufacturing facilities, including showcasing site control, permits, financing, and community engagement.
- DOE Chief Commercialization Officer and Director of the Office of Technology Transitions, Dr. Vanessa Chan, stated the prize will allow winners to fast-track construction of new domestic manufacturing facilities.
- Dr. Vanessa Chan noted that critical clean energy components made at these sites will help power the Investing In America agenda to ensure investments are supported by a U.S. supply chain.
- OCED Director of Portfolio Strategy, Melissa Klembara, emphasized the prize's importance for building out critical supply chains needed for the next wave of infrastructure projects and future clean energy projects.
- The three winners will leverage prize dollars to create more than $100 million of economic activity directly related to the construction of their facilities.
- More than 250 jobs will be created to build and operate these three facilities.
- The winning companies will build more than 250 hybrid inverters per year to upgrade the electric grid and deploy more than 300 MWh per year of long duration energy storage.
- These DOE investments support the Biden-Harris Administration’s efforts to boost domestic manufacturing and competitiveness.
- MAKE IT Prize Round 2 will be open to additional critical components in areas including upgrades to the electric grid, long duration energy storage, industrial decarbonization, carbon capture and storage, direct air capture, clean hydrogen, and advanced nuclear power.
- DOE intends to publish the new prize rules and open the competition in the fall of 2024.
- Stryten Energy of Augusta, GA won a Phase 2 award and plans to repurpose a shuttered facility in Augusta, GA as a new high-volume continuous reactor vanadium electrolyte production facility.
- The Stryten Energy project aims to create a cost-effective domestic supply chain for vanadium electrolyte and establish the U.S. as a leader in this critical infrastructure area.
- Switched Source, LLC of Chicago, IL won a Phase 2 award and aims to expand production capacity for a power-electronics-based hybrid transformer called "Phase-EQ".
- MAKE IT Prize funds will help Switched Source transition from a 5,000 square foot low-volume facility to a ~20,000 square foot manufacturing, high power testing, and warehouse facility.
- The expanded Switched Source facility will be capable of producing over 250 Phase-EQ units per year by the end of 2027.
- Urban Electric Power of Kingston, NY won a Phase 2 award and plans to build and operate a rechargeable zinc-manganese dioxide battery plant for long duration energy storage applications.
- The Urban Electric Power manufacturing technology is designed to meet market demand for safe, reliable, and American-made turnkey battery energy storage systems (BESS).
- Winning teams will locate facilities in currently underutilized spaces in disadvantaged communities and work with local partners on workforce development initiatives.
- Remaining Phase 1 winners in the MAKE IT Facilities Track still have opportunities to submit shovel-ready plans, with one more Phase 2 award available.
- The next submission deadline for Facilities Track Phase 2 is Nov. 5th, with additional deadlines expected in February and May 2025 if funding is available.
- DOE may consider making additional prizes available at the November deadline but does not commit to increasing the prize pool at this time.
- The three-phase MAKE IT Strategies Track, focusing on community-driven strategies for attracting clean energy manufacturing, is still underway through next spring.
- Round 1 competitors in the Strategies Track are currently in Phase 3: Activate, while Round 2 teams are in Phase 2: Create.
- Teams in both Strategies Track rounds will submit materials for review by Nov. 1st, with winner announcements anticipated in December.
- The MAKE IT Prize was developed by DOE’s OTT, OCED, and Office of Energy Efficiency and Renewable Energy (EERE) and is funded by the Technology Commercialization Fund through the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law.
- The prize is administered through the American-Made program, which accelerates the transition from ideas into real-world clean energy solutions.
- The DOE Office of Technology Transitions (OTT) was founded in 2015 and bolsters the technology industry's market skills to enable clean energy technologies to progress through research, development, demonstration, and deployment.
- The Office of Clean Energy Demonstrations (OCED) was established in December 2021 to help scale emerging technologies needed to tackle climate challenges and achieve net zero emissions by 2050.
- OCED is managing more than $25 billion in funding to deliver clean energy demonstration projects at scale in partnership with the private sector.
