# Clean Vehicle Credit

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2022-08-17

**Source:** [Alternative Fuels Data Center: Electric Vehicles with Final Assembly in North America](https://afdc.energy.gov/laws/electric-vehicles-for-tax-credit)  ·  `page_002394`

## Facts

- The Inflation Reduction Act of 2022 (Public Law 117-169) amended the Qualified Plug-in Electric Drive Motor Vehicle Credit (IRC 30D), now known as the Clean Vehicle Credit.
- A new requirement for final assembly in North America took effect on August 17, 2022.
- The final assembly requirement applies to new electric, fuel cell electric, and plug-in hybrid electric vehicles acquired, delivered, and placed in service after August 16, 2022.
- For vehicles placed in service on or after January 1, 2023, Clean Vehicle Credit provisions are subject to updated guidance from the IRS and the U.S. Department of the Treasury.
- Beginning January 1, 2024, buyers can reduce the vehicle's upfront purchase price by the amount of their Clean Vehicle Credit by transferring the credit to the dealer.
- Before 2024, eligible vehicle buyers could only receive the credit amount after filing their tax return.
- Starting January 1, 2024, dealers must submit information to the IRS through IRS Energy Credits Online to determine vehicle eligibility and credit amount at the point of sale.
- Without dealer submission to the IRS, buyers cannot claim a tax credit on their return nor transfer it to a dealer.
- A dealer must provide the buyer a copy of the IRS's approval of the dealer's submission.
- The list of EVs with final assembly in North America applies only to vehicles purchased and delivered between August 17 and December 31, 2022.
- Model Year 2022 and 2023 vehicle eligibility is based on Fuel Economy labeling information and Part 583 data submitted by manufacturers to the EPA and NHTSA as of December 31, 2022.
- Verification of final assembly for vehicles purchased beginning January 1, 2023, should use the VIN decoder.
- Manufacturer sales caps were met for the 2022 Chevrolet Bolt EUV, Chevrolet Bolt EV, GMC Hummer EV Pickup, GMC Hummer EV SUV, Tesla Model 3, Tesla Model S, Tesla Model X, and Tesla Model Y.
- Eligible 2022 models include the Audi Q5, BMW 330e, BMW X5 xDrive45e (PHEV), Chrysler Pacifica PHEV, Ford E-Transit, Ford Escape PHEV, Ford F-150 Lightning, Ford Mustang MACH E, Jeep Grand Cherokee 4xe, Jeep Wrangler 4xe, Lincoln Aviator PHEV, Lincoln Corsair PHEV, Lucid Air, Nissan Leaf, Rivian EDV, Rivian R1S, Rivian R1T, and Volvo S60 Recharge.
- Eligible 2023 models include the BMW 330e, BMW X5 xDrive45e (PHEV), Cadillac Lyriq, Chevrolet Bolt EV, Ford E-Transit, Jeep Grand Cherokee 4xe, Jeep Wrangler 4xe, Lincoln Aviator PHEV, Lucid Air, Mercedes EQS SUV, Nissan Leaf, Rivian R1S, Rivian R1T, Tesla Model 3, Tesla Model S, Tesla Model X, Tesla Model Y, and Volkswagen ID.4.
- The list will be updated as vehicle manufacturers submit Fuel Economy labeling information and Part 583 vehicle identification information to relevant government agencies.
- Manufacturers can use the website contact form to request changes to this vehicle list.
- Final assembly in North America is not required for tax credit eligibility for vehicles purchased before August 17, 2022.
- The build location of a particular vehicle should be confirmed by the VIN or an information label affixed to the vehicle.
- The U.S. Department of Transportation's NHTSA provides a VIN decoder to identify a vehicle's build plant and country of manufacture.
- Verification of North American final assembly is an initial step toward identifying vehicles eligible for the Clean Vehicle Credit, but further eligibility conditions may apply depending on the purchase date.
- For some manufacturers, the build location may vary based on the specific vehicle, trim, or production date because some models are produced in multiple locations.
- To be eligible for the Clean Vehicle Credit, a vehicle must have undergone final assembly in North America.
- In general, North America includes the United States and Puerto Rico, Canada, and Mexico for determining the location of final assembly.
