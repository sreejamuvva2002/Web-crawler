# U.S. Department of Energy

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [$900 Million Available to Unlock Commercial Deployment of American-Made Small Modular Reactors | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/ne/articles/900-million-available-unlock-commercial-deployment-american-made-small-modular-reactors)  ·  `page_004538`

## Facts

- The U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) re-issued a $900 million solicitation to support the deployment of small modular reactors.
- The solicitation aims to better align with President Trump's agenda to unleash American energy and AI dominance.
- U.S. Secretary of Energy Chris Wright stated that America’s nuclear energy renaissance starts now.
- Secretary Wright emphasized that abundant and affordable energy is key to the nation’s economic prosperity and security.
- Secretary Wright described the solicitation as a call to action for early movers seeking to put more energy on the grid through advanced light-water small modular reactors.
- U.S. electricity demand is forecast to soar in the coming years driven by consumer needs, data center growth, increased AI use, and the industrial sector’s need for constant power.
- Small modular reactors could provide reliable power for energy-intensive sectors, with flexible deployment thanks to their compact size and modular design.
- Light-water small modular reactors could leverage the existing service and supply chain supporting the country’s current fleet of light-water reactors, helping speed up near-term deployment of new nuclear reactors.
- DOE is offering funding to de-risk the deployment of Generation III+ light-water small modular reactors (Gen III+ SMR) through two tiers.
- Tier 1: First Mover Team Support will provide up to $800M to support up to two first mover teams of utility, reactor vendor, constructor, and end-users/off-takers committed to deploying a first plant.
- Tier 1 facilitates a multi-reactor, Gen III+ SMR orderbook and the opportunity to work with the National Nuclear Security Administration to incorporate safeguards and security by design into the projects.
- Tier 2: Fast Follower Deployment Support will provide approximately $100M to spur additional Gen III+ SMR deployments by addressing key gaps in design, licensing, supply chain, and site preparation.
- The selection of awardees will be solely based on technical merit.
- Applications are due on April 23, 2025, at 5:00 p.m. ET.
- Previous applicants who applied to the 2024 solicitation must resubmit their proposals following the new guidance to receive consideration.
- New applications are also welcome.
