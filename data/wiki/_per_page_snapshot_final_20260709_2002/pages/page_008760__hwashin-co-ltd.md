# Hwashin Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-11

**Source:** [Hawashin to create 460 jobs with new GA facility](https://valdostatoday.com/news-2/region/2023/11/hawashin-to-create-460-jobs-with-new-ga-facility/)  ·  `page_008760`

## Facts

- Automotive parts company Hwashin will establish a new manufacturing facility in Georgia.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Hwashin will establish its new manufacturing facility in Dublin, Laurens County.
- The new facility will create over 460 new jobs.
- The project involves more than $176 million in investment in Laurens County.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Georgia's goal is to become the e-mobility capital of the nation.
- Hwashin operates as a Tier-1 supplier specializing in crafting chassis and body parts.
- Hwashin's customers include Hyundai Motor Group, Kia, and international original equipment manufacturers (OEMs).
- Hwashin is headquartered in Korea and has established production facilities across the United States, Asia, and South America.
- Seo Jin Jung serves as the CEO of Hwashin.
- Hwashin draws from 50 years of expertise in its collaboration with Georgia and Laurens County.
- Hwashin aims to position the Dublin-Laurens County region at the forefront of the evolving automobile industry.
- Hwashin's new Georgia facility is scheduled to begin production in late 2025.
- The facility will be located at the Georgia Highway 257 Rail Served Site.
- The site is the first Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) Select site in the state.
- A “GRAD Certified Select” status indicates a site has met or exceeded more rigorous certification requirements.
- The facility will primarily manufacture chassis components for OEMs, including Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America and Kia Georgia.
- Dublin Mayor Joshua Kight welcomed Hwashin to the community.
- The Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority staff and board assisted in presenting Dublin-Laurens County as a business location to Hwashin.
- Jeff Davis serves as the Chairman of the Laurens County Board of Commissioners.
- Hwashin will be the seventh international company to call Dublin and Laurens County home.
- The project demonstrates a partnership and "can-do attitude" between Laurens County, the City of Dublin, and the Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority.
- Griff Lynch is the President and CEO of the Georgia Ports Authority.
- The Georgia Ports Authority welcomes Hwashin as the newest member of its manufacturing community.
- Alyce Thornhill is the Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy at the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team.
- The project was executed in partnership with the Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia EMC, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Hwashin will have direct interstate access to at least two long-term automotive partners from its chosen site.
- Locating on a GRAD Select site allows Hwashin to pursue an accelerated construction timeline.
- In fiscal year 2023, job creation in Georgia's automotive industry increased by 324 percent compared to FY21.
- Suppliers attracted by Georgia’s mobility OEMs resulted in over $2 billion in investment across the state in FY23.
- Since 2020, Georgia has created more than $25.1 billion in investments and 29,000 jobs pursuing its entire EV supply chain.
- Hwashin Co., Ltd. was founded in 1975.
- The company actively engages in research and development for chassis and body parts.
- Hwashin has expanded its product offerings to include lightweight aluminum components and eco-friendly solutions.
- Hwashin Co., Ltd. employs more than 4,200 personnel worldwide.
