# Evco Plastics Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Owler Reports - EVCO: Evco Plastics buys Georgia plant to add LSR, white room molding](https://www.owler.com/reports/evco/evco--evco-plastics-buys-georgia-plant-to-add-lsr-/1617846487629)  ·  `page_054421`

## Facts

- Evco Plastics Inc., a family-owned injection molder based in DeForest, Wis., has purchased a building near Calhoun, Ga.
- The company will add white room and liquid silicone rubber (LSR) molding capabilities at the new Georgia site.
- Evco had been leasing the 40,000-square-foot building for warehouse space before purchasing it.
- Kate Bashir, director of strategy, stated the purchase coincided with the need to expand their footprint in Georgia to accommodate new work.
- With the new plant, Evco now has 1 million square feet of manufacturing space across 10 facilities.
- The new site will introduce white room and LSR molding opportunities to meet demand from medical and commercial lighting customers.
- Bashir noted the current need is for an existing customer in the lighting industry while actively quoting new customer work in this area.
- The company is starting to discuss LSR with medical customers, aiming to 'walk' into the technology before 'running.'
- The new facility has room for 20 injection molding machines up to 500 tons, including a separate room for new LSR machines.
- Evco is moving smaller machines from Wisconsin facilities, purchasing new machines, and leaving spaces open to optimize barrel/screws for specific projects.
- LSR molding will be a new capability for Evco, utilizing a task-force team of engineers and processing specialists with previous LSR market experience.
- The company is working with machine manufacturers to help train its team on the tricky thermoset process.
- The plant, named CAL 2, features two bridge cranes, four material silos, a 22,000-square-foot white room, and a 725-square-foot metrology room.
- Evco is enhancing its highly automated lights-out molding capabilities with high-speed automation as a critical component of the manufacturing plan.
- The facility will require trained automation team members to successfully operate lights-out processes and troubleshoot as needed.
- The new Georgia facility will be fully operational in the fall.
- Evco is adding 13,000 square feet to its automated molding facility in DeForest, dubbed AMP, to add more machines and increase raw material/finished goods storage.
- Gary Fortier, director of Evco's U.S. operations, stated that reaching over 1 million square feet is a huge milestone driven by growing customer needs.
- Bashir reported strong business conditions despite continued material shortages challenging increased worldwide demand.
- The company is setting up to run 24/7 in plants needing extra time, optimizing schedules, and reducing turnover time to meet customer needs.
- Evco kicked off 2021 with three months of all-time record sales with no signs of slowing down.
- Founded in 1964, Evco employs more than 1,700 people and operates 235 injection molding machines ranging from 28 to 3,500 tons.
- In addition to Georgia, Evco operates four molding plants in Wisconsin, three in Mexico, and one in China.
- The company was named Plastics News' 2015 Processor of the Year and ranked No. 51 in the most recent PN survey of North American injection molders.
- Evco's molding sales in the region are in the range of $188 million.
