# Georgia Department of Economic Development

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2023-08-08

**Source:** [Georgia Breaks Economic Development Records for Third Year in a Row | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-release/georgia-breaks-economic-development-records-third-year-row)  ·  `page_003718`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp and GDEcD announced that Georgia broke economic development records for the third consecutive year during Fiscal Year 2023 (FY23).
- Between July 1, 2022, and June 30, 2023, total investments in facility expansions and new locations totaled more than $24 billion.
- FY23 projects resulted in 38,400 new jobs through 426 initiatives supported by GDEcD’s Global Commerce team.
- Excluding the Rivian and Hyundai Motor Group projects from the previous year’s job creation figures, FY23 projects exceeded the previous record by more than 2,800 new positions.
- Annual investment totals have increased by 131 percent over the last three years.
- Eighty-two percent of new jobs created and more than $20 billion in investments are directed to communities outside the 10-county Atlanta region.
- Metro Atlanta attracted $3.6 billion in investment during the last fiscal year.
- Expansions account for 71 percent of total projects, while new locations account for 65 percent of new jobs announced across the state.
- Between July 2018 and July 2023, at least 40 e-mobility projects have been announced in Georgia.
- These e-mobility projects commit more than 30,000 jobs and over $25 billion in investment to the state.
- Suppliers attracted by Georgia’s mobility original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) resulted in over $2 billion in investment across the state in FY23.
- Mobility and cleantech projects alone delivered three investments of $1 billion or more during FY23.
- Mobility and cleantech projects accounted for six of the largest projects by job creation in FY23.
- The Georgia Center of Innovation connects public and private partners in strategic ecosystems to foster innovation in mobility and cleantech.
- Aerospace industry investments increased by more than 1,000 percent since FY22, with an ecosystem that overlaps with e-mobility through companies like Archer.
- Gulfstream is a top regional employer within the overlapping aerospace and e-mobility ecosystem.
- Georgia offers opportunities for defense contractors and has a workforce that includes the fifth-largest population of active and retired service members in the country.
- The “Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development” (GRAD) certification spurred a 112 percent growth in advanced manufacturing investments since FY22.
- Georgia Quick Start programs also contributed to the growth in advanced manufacturing investments.
- Hartsfield-Jackson International Airport is cited as the busiest and most efficient airport in the world, supporting logistics for supply chains.
- The Port of Savannah is the single largest and fastest-growing container terminal in America.
- The Port of Brunswick is one of the most utilized port terminals in the U.S. for roll-on, roll-off cargo.
- Georgia maintains a reliable logistics industry extending throughout the state along a robust network of top-ranked highways and accessible railways.
- Top international sources of job creation in FY23 included South Korea, Finland, Norway, Japan, Switzerland, and the Netherlands.
- International investments were responsible for 47 percent of expected jobs and 65 percent of the total investment in FY23.
- Governor Brian Kemp, Lt. Governor Burt Jones, Speaker Jon Burns, GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson, Deputy Commissioner Kristi Brigman, and Deputy Commissioner Mary Waters led state missions to Switzerland, Germany, Israel, France, and the country of Georgia to secure international investments and partnerships.
