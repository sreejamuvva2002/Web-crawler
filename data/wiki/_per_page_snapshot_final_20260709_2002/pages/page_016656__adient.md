# Adient

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-02-04

**Source:** [Adient reports solid first quarter financial results; raises guidance for FY2026 | lifestyle.ribbon.co](https://lifestyle.ribbon.co/story/471581/adient-reports-solid-first-quarter-financial-results-raises-guidance-for-fy2026/)  ·  `page_016656`

## Facts

- Adient is a global leader in automotive seating.
- Q1 GAAP net loss and EPS diluted were $(22)M and $(0.28), respectively.
- Q1 Adj.-EPS diluted was $0.35.
- Q1 Adj.-EBITDA was $207M, a $11M y-o-y improvement.
- Adj.-EBITDA margins increased from 5.6% to 5.7% y-o-y.
- Gross debt and net debt totaled ~$2.4B and ~$1.5B, respectively, at December 31, 2025.
- Cash and cash equivalents were $855M at December 31, 2025.
- The company returned $25M to its shareholders in Q1 FY2026, repurchasing ~1.2M shares.
- Adient raised its FY26 guidance for revenue, Adj.-EBITDA and FCF to $14.6B, $880M, and $125M, respectively.
- The raised guidance is underpinned by an improved vehicle production outlook and the company’s expectation of continued positive business performance.
- Adient has more than 65,000 employees in 29 countries.
- Adient operates ~200 manufacturing/assembly plants worldwide.
- Adient produces and delivers automotive seating for all major OEMs, ranging from complete seating systems to individual components.
- Jerome Dorlack is Adient’s president and chief executive officer.
- Mark Oswald is Adient’s executive vice president and chief financial officer.
