## Facts

- The U.S. government is investing heavily to enable far more resilient and sustainable infrastructures.
- With the passage of the American Rescue Plan Act (ARPA), Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act (IIJA) and Inflation Reduction Act (IRA), there are unprecedented opportunities—through direct funds, grants, tax credits, tax incentives and rebates—for organizations to maximize the return on investment (ROI) of critical infrastructure and clean energy projects.
- Navigating the intricacies of federal funding and tax incentive mechanisms can be a formidable challenge for those unfamiliar with the procedures and processes.
- Qualified clean energy projects that utilize U.S. equipment and materials have the potential to earn bonus tax credits of up to 10%.
- Eaton was awarded more than $26.6 million in investment tax credits from the U.S. Internal Revenue Service as part of the Qualifying Advanced Energy Project Tax Credit (48C) funded by the Inflation Reduction Act.
- These tax credits were awarded for Eaton’s manufacturing investments totaling more than $200 million to support clean energy projects and workforce training in Nacogdoches and El Paso, Texas, and Waukesha, Wisconsin.
- As an experienced Government contractor, Eaton prides itself on ethical conduct and doing the right thing for its customers and suppliers.
- Eaton has implemented a robust government compliance program focused on ensuring compliance with all applicable Government procurement laws and regulations.
