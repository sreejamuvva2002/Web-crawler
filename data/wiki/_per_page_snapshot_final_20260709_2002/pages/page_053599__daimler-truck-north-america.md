# Daimler Truck North America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-03-14

**Source:** [Workers at Daimler Truck North America vote overwhelmingly to authorize strike  - World Socialist Web Site](https://www.wsws.org/en/articles/2024/03/14/dtna-m14.html)  ·  `page_053599`

## Facts

- Workers at Daimler Truck North America facilities in six locations across North Carolina, Georgia and Tennessee voted by 96 percent on March 8 to authorize a strike when the current contract expires on April 26 at 12:01 a.m.
- The company employs nearly 7,000 United Auto Workers members at facilities throughout the South.
- Daimler Truck North America manufactures Freightliner trucks, heavy-duty Western Star trucks and Thomas Built Buses.
- Daimler Truck is the largest manufacturer of commercial vehicles in the world, with over 100,000 employees across 35 countries.
- The firm was spun off from German auto giant Daimler AG (subsequently renamed the Mercedes-Benz Group) in 2021, with Mercedes remaining the largest shareholder.
- In 2023, Daimler Truck made a record €5.5 billion ($6 billion) in earnings before interest and taxes (EBIT), with the figure jumping 38 percent compared to a year earlier.
- The company announced that it expected to exceed earnings forecasts for 2024 and raised its dividend by nearly 50 percent, from €1.30 to €1.90 a share, translating to approximately $1.5 billion for the company’s biggest investors.
- Truck assemblers start at just $19.25 an hour at plants in North Carolina.
- Workers cite insurance, benefits, cost of living, raises, and health and safety issues as motivations for the strike authorization vote.
- Company executives at Ford, GM and Stellantis planned to make up for any pay increases by laying off tens of thousands of workers as part of the transition to electric vehicles.
- In 2022, the UAW made an unsuccessful effort to convince Daimler workers to reopen their contract, implement changes demanded by the company, and extend the agreement by three years.
- The UAW released a video highlighting low wages while pointing to plants Daimler has built in Mexico to foment anti-Mexican sentiment and American chauvinism.
- Rank-and-file workers at Daimler are encouraged to appeal to fellow workers at Volvo and Mack Trucks to fight for their common interests.
