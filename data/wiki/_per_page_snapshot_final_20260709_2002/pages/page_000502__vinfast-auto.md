# VinFast Auto

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-07-20

**Source:** [VinFast holds groundbreaking on $4 billion Chatham County EV plant](https://www.carolinajournal.com/vinfast-holds-groundbreaking-on-4-billion-chatham-county-ev-plant/)  ·  `page_000502`

## Facts

- Groundbreaking for a $4 billion EV manufacturing plant occurred Friday morning at Triangle Innovation Point in Moncure, Chatham County.
- The site is being called the “crown jewel of VinFast’s global expansion”.
- Attendees included Madam Le Thi Thu Thuy (CEO of VinFast Auto), Vietnam ambassador to the U.S. Nguyen Quoc Dzung, NC Gov. Roy Cooper, Rep. Robert Reives (D-Chatham), Sen. Natalie Murdock (D-Durham/Chatham County), Sen. Jim Burgin (R-Harnett), NC Commerce Secretary Machelle Baker Sanders, NCDOT Secretary J. Eric Boyette, Chatham County officials, and members of Merry Oaks Baptist Church.
- The Chatham County site is the first facility VinFast is building outside Vietnam.
- The plant is slated to create 7,500 jobs.
- CEO Thuy stated they are proud to create thousands of jobs in North Carolina and that the recognition reflects mutual ambition for the new facility.
- Thuy noted thanks to support from their parent company, VinFast is well positioned to continue global expansion with a stronger presence in North America.
- The U.S. secretary of the Securities and Exchange Commission cleared the way for VinFast’s “business combination” on the day of the groundbreaking.
- This brings the EV maker one step closer to listing VinFast in the U.S., with an official announcement expected very soon.
- Black Spade Acquisition Company and VinFast issued a joint press release later in the day about the merger.
- On July 13, shareholders of Black Spade Acquisition Company voted to extend the company’s lifespan by another year.
- The vote gave the SPAC until July 20, 2024, to merge with VinFast.
- The merger would allow VinFast to go public through a stock offering in the U.S., valuing the company at approximately $27 billion with an equity value of $23 billion.
- After the transaction, existing shareholders of VinFast would hold about 99% shares of the combined company.
- In connection with the extension, shareholders redeemed approximately $147 million, or over 80% of their shares, leaving $28.56 million in the trust account.
- The company has not updated any information on the change.
- Vietnam ambassador Dzung stated the event coincides with the 10th anniversary of the U.S.-Vietnam comprehensive partnership launched by former Presidents Obama and Sang in 2013.
- Dzung said VinFast’s success is encouraging other Vietnamese businesses to explore investment opportunities in the U.S. and North Carolina.
- NC Gov. Cooper stated the state has been working for decades to bring an automobile assembly plant to North Carolina.
- Cooper credited VinFast for leading an army of clean-energy companies coming to the state.
- Cooper said this influx has helped set records for job growth and economic expansion in the state over the last few years.
- Cooper noted that automakers and truck makers are moving away from gas and diesel into the EV market at breakneck speed.
- Cooper emphasized it is important to pull together to harness the economic windfall for North Carolinians due to fierce competition.
- Cooper said they are working to ensure infrastructure will be ready for EVs by making sure there will be charging stations all over the state.
- Rep. Reives stated Chatham County has been made the example for every other county and southern state about how to get it done.
- The company donated three acres of land on their site to Merry Oaks Baptist Church.
- The company recognized two members of the church in attendance.
- The company is using eminent domain to complete the project.
- The project will displace the 135-year-old church to make way for a new highway needed for site access.
- VinFast first announced plans to build the 1,800-acre site over a year ago.
- The plant was originally scheduled to open in 2024 but has been pushed back to a 2025 opening.
- Plans include a 2.85 million square foot complex comprising eight buildings.
- The complex includes a press shop, which is the tallest building at 75.2 feet.
- The complex includes a general assembly building, which is the largest at 995,900 square feet.
- Other planned buildings include a body shop, central energy plant, paint shop, guard house, pump house, and waste building.
- VinFast’s factory is designed to reach a capacity of 150,000 vehicles per year in Phase 1.
- The factory will consist of two main areas: electric vehicle production and assembly.
- The complex will also house supplementary supplier businesses.
- To attract the company, the state and Chatham County committed to spending nearly $1.2 billion in incentives over the next 32 years.
- Using the state’s Transformative Job Development and Investment Grant program (JDIG), VinFast could get up to $316.1 million in reimbursement from the state over three decades if hiring goals are met.
- The total state appropriation is estimated at $766 million.
- Chatham County is giving VinFast another $400 million incentive package.
- The company reported last month that it lost $598 million, or 49%, in the first quarter of this year.
- According to Reuters, the company attributed a quarterly revenue decline of almost $84 million to lower selling prices and its phase-out of internal combustion vehicles as it pivoted to become a dedicated EV maker.
- The company has been losing money for some time, having lost $2.1 billion in 2022 and $1.3 billion the year prior.
- A recall of all 999 VF8 2023 vehicles sent to the U.S. was issued in May due to safety concerns.
- Reviews of the vehicle used words like abysmal, “very, very bad,” “yikes,” “simply unacceptable,” and “return to sender.”
- MotorTrend said VinFast had the right idea, but the VF8 is nowhere near ready for customer deliveries that are already taking place.
