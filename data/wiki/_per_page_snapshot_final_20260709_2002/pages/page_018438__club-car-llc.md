# Club Car LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-04-19

**Source:** [Club Car Opens UTV Factory in US | For Construction Pros](https://www.forconstructionpros.com/trucks/trucks-accessories/utility-vehicles/article/22893611/club-car-llc-club-car-opens-utv-factory-in-us)  ·  `page_018438`

## Facts

- Club Car has opened a new factory in the state of Georgia for producing electrically powered utility task vehicles (UTVs).
- The facility is named the Club Car Appling Manufacturing Facility.
- The opening was celebrated with tours and a ribbon cutting ceremony attended by Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp, First Lady Marty Kemp, state and local elected officials, and area business leaders.
- The UTVs belong to Club Car's Urban line and can be equipped with different electric lithium options to provide extended range in multiple climates.
- The UTVs feature a truck-like payload capacity.
- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp stated that Club Car is bringing jobs into Georgia in the growing e-mobility sector and leading the way in compact utility EV manufacturing.
- Gov. Kemp noted that Club Car's growth and investment are a testament to efforts being made to continue making Georgia the number one state to do business.
- The Appling Manufacturing Facility comprises 105,000 sq. ft. of flexible production and warehouse storage space.
- Club Car opened the facility to offer customers a better experience as demand in the United States for the Club Car Urban vehicle increases.
- Club Car has committed nearly $8 million in capital investments into the facility over two years.
- The new facility currently operates a state-of-the-art production line and can produce up to 20 vehicles per day.
- Club Car President and CEO Mark Wagner stated that their innovative vehicles are impacting commercial fleet electrification, mini-mobility, and last mile delivery.
- Club Car has been a Georgia-based company for more than sixty years.
- UTVs have become popular in construction, landscaping, and other industries because they are easy to operate and drivers do not need a commercial driver's license (CDL).
- The ease of operation and lack of CDL requirement is particularly helpful as construction, landscaping, and other industries continue to grapple with a worker shortage.
