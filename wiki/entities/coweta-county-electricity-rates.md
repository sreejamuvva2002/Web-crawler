## Key facts

- Coweta County residents who request quotes on the EnergySage Marketplace spend an average of $214 monthly on electricity, or $2,568 per year.
- Typical Coweta County household uses 1,684 kWh per month (20,208 kWh annually).
- As of June 2026, the county's electricity rate is 13 ¢/kWh, roughly 35% lower than the national average.
- Georgia Power is the primary electricity supplier for most of Coweta County and is regulated by the Georgia Public Service Commission.
- Georgia’s electricity market is regulated; consumers cannot choose their utility provider.
- Municipally-owned utilities in some Georgia towns may offer lower rates but often lack net billing benefits.
- Time-of-use (TOU) plans charge more during high-demand periods and less during off-peak hours, encouraging load shifting.
- Georgia Power offers a FlatBill plan that provides a fixed monthly payment for a year.
- Net metering is no longer available in Georgia; instead, net billing allows surplus solar generation to be credited at an avoided cost rate.
- Installing residential solar can reduce or eliminate electric bills and lower carbon footprints.
- Approximately 55% of Georgia’s electricity generation comes from fossil fuels (mainly natural gas) as of August 2025.
- Only about 12% of the state's electricity is sourced from renewable energy.
- A typical $36,400 solar system (pre-incentives) could avoid $2,100 in electric bills in the first year and about $65,700 over 25 years at current rates.
