# Hyundai Metaplant Georgia

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-18

**Source:** [Why Hyundai Is Pouring Another $2.7 Billion Into Georgia](https://insideevs.com/news/772603/hyundai-metaplant-georgia-3-billion/)  ·  `page_009267`

## Facts

- Hyundai is injecting $2.7 billion to kick-start the second phase of its Metaplant expansion in Georgia.
- The $2.7 billion investment will create another 3,000 local jobs to support made-in-America electric vehicles and hybrids.
- Hyundai announced the investment at its annual CEO Investor Day event in New York on September 18, 2025.
- The cash infusion will add an estimated production capacity of 200,000 units.
- Total annual output from Georgia will reach half-million vehicles by 2028.
- The facility will produce between 10 hybrid and full battery-electric models.
- Hyundai's labor pledge represents a 11.5% increase in jobs.
- The investment represents an additional 10% of the $27.3 billion pledged in Georgia's local economy since 2018 from EV manufacturers and their downstream suppliers.
- Hyundai aims to manufacture 80% of the cars it sells in the State domestically.
- Investments in local supply chains will increase locally-sourced parts from 60% to 80%.
- The Metaplant serves as Hyundai's next-gen manufacturing hub in the U.S.
- The company describes the Metaplant as its new "software-defined factory" featuring automation systems and robotics tech derived from investments in Boston Dynamics.
- Hyundai plans to crank out an additional 1.2 million cars by the top of the decade, bringing its total electrified output to 3.3 million units per year.
- The Metaplant will build 15% of those cars.
- Georgia has become the unofficial EV capital of the Southeast.
- Hyundai's cash promise is seen as a vote of confidence in the community and a corporate promise to build where it sells on U.S. soil.
- Continued investment between the U.S. and South Korea may help patch up relations following a massive immigration raid.
- Between Hyundai, Rivian, SK, and numerous other e-mobility companies operating out of the region, the EV industry makes up 26,000 jobs in the region.
