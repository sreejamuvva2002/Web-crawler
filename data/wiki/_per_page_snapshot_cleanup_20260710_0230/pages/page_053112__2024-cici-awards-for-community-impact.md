# 2024 CiCi Awards for Community Impact

**Type:** event  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [2024 CiCi Awards: Community Impact | Trade and Industry Development](https://www.tradeandindustrydev.com/industry/all-industries/2024-cici-awards-community-impact-32928)  ·  `page_053112`

## Facts

- The 2024 CiCi Awards for Community Impact recognized 15 projects announced in 2023 that demonstrate how site selection can make a large difference in a community by providing jobs and opportunities.
- The collective capital investment of the 15 winning projects totals about $3.5 billion, creating and/or retaining more than 6,100 direct jobs.
- Epsilon Advanced Materials announced plans to build its first manufacturing facility in the U.S. in Brunswick County, North Carolina, creating 500 jobs.
- The India-based company will invest $649.9 million at the Mid-Atlantic Rail Industrial Park for its newest facility.
- Epsilon Advanced Materials provides synthetic graphite used in electric vehicle batteries and develops high-performance graphite for anode components in lithium-ion batteries.
- The EAM project was awarded funds from North Carolina’s Job Development Investment Grant (JDIG), a performance-based, discretionary incentive program that provides cash grants directly to a company when it creates jobs and invests in the state.
- Over the course of the 12-year term of the JDIG grant, the project is estimated to grow the state’s economy by $1.3 billion.
- The North Carolina Department of Commerce led the state’s support for the company during its site evaluation and decision-making process.
- Although wages will vary depending on the position, the average salary for the new positions at Epsilon will be $52,264, compared to Brunswick County's current average wage of $46,464.
- Because Epsilon chose a site in Brunswick County, classified as Tier 3, the JDIG agreement calls for moving $1.14 million into the state’s Industrial Development Fund – Utility Account to help rural communities finance infrastructure upgrades.
- Christopher Chung, CEO of the Economic Development Partnership of North Carolina (EDPNC), stated that Epsilon's investment shows North Carolina is continually a top location for the electric vehicle and battery sector and a leader in the clean energy economy.
- Samkee Corp., a major South Korean automotive supplier, announced plans in early 2023 to invest $128 million to open its first U.S. factory in Alabama through a project that will create 170 jobs in Tuskegee.
- The new manufacturing facility will be located in the Tuskegee Commerce Park and serve as a Tier 1 supplier to Hyundai Motor Co.
- The new jobs will pay an average wage of just over $20 an hour, according to the Alabama Department of Commerce.
- Samkee specializes in high-pressure die-cast aluminum components, including parts for engines, transmissions and electric vehicles, along with alloys.
- An analysis by the Center for Business and Economic Research (CBER) at the University of Alabama predicted the Samkee factory will generate $140.2 million in annual economic output in Macon County, contributing over $37 million to the county’s GDP while also generating $1.3 million per year in taxes.
- Local support for the project includes site work in Tuskegee Commerce Park, upgrades to the water and sewer system, and the installation of a new power substation.
- Brenda Tuck, Rural Development Manager for the Alabama Department of Commerce, noted that Samkee’s investment signals that the state’s rural counties remain a magnet for foreign direct investment, particularly within the auto industry.
- Nel Hydrogen announced plans to build a new gigafactory in Plymouth Township, Michigan, representing a total investment of more than $400 million into the local community and creating more than 500 jobs.
- The automated gigafactory will support the development of electrolyzer technology for green hydrogen production.
- The project will be supported in part by a $10 million Michigan Business Development Program grant and a 15-year, 100-percent State Essential Services Assessment Exemption Request valued at up to $6.25 million.
- The new 507,000-square-foot manufacturing facility will be used to manufacture Proton Exchange Membrane and Alkaline hydrogen electrolyzers.
- Nel Hydrogen CEO Håkon Volldal cited the state's engagement from Governor Whitmer, highly skilled workforce, financial incentives, collaboration among universities and research institutions, and proximity to collaborating partner General Motors as reasons for choosing Michigan.
