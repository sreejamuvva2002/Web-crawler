# Hyundai Mobis

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2025-08-27

**Source:** [Hyundai Mobis Unveils Future Growth Strategy at CEO Investor Day](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/hyundai-mobis-unveils-future-growth-strategy-at-ceo-investor-day-302539923.html)  ·  `page_006121`

## Facts

- Held its 2025 CEO Investor Day on August 27, presenting its future growth strategy based on a "New Vision" of leading the mobility paradigm.
- President and CEO Lee Gyu-suk outlined strategic directions focused on three pillars: strengthening technology competitiveness, improving profitability, and expanding its global customer base.
- Is currently building a lineup of leading-edge technologies including windshield displays and SDV solutions.
- Concentrating on advanced technologies in key future mobility areas: electrification, electronics, semiconductors, and robotics.
- Developing a next-generation windshield display using holographic optical film to transform the windshield into a transparent screen, which was first unveiled at CES 2025 and is expected to launch around 2029.
- Co-developing the next-generation windshield display technology with German optics firm ZEISS.
- Developing a standardized software-defined vehicle (SDV) platform based on its advanced E/E architecture to enable software-based control across various vehicle types, targeting full-scale commercialization after 2028.
- Enhancing battery safety in electrification with systems that block heat transfer between cells using fire-resistant materials and an automatic fire suppression system that activates upon ignition.
- Announced plans to strengthen competitiveness in next-generation automotive semiconductors and robotics, stating "We will seize future growth opportunities."
- Pursuing a two-track strategy for next-generation automotive semiconductors focusing on system semiconductors and power semiconductors.
- Developing Communication SoCs and Battery Monitoring ICs while accelerating in-house mass production of power semiconductors, which are critical components for EV drive systems.
- Has developed 16 types of semiconductors to date and plans to produce 20 million units this year.
- Its R&D process has earned the highest ISO 26262 safety rating and is currently developing 11 next-generation chips.
- Working to build a domestic semiconductor ecosystem through partnerships with automakers, fabless firms, and foundries, and will host a dedicated forum next month.
- Announced its entry into the robotics actuator market, leveraging its expertise in vehicle steering systems.
- Actuators control robot movement, share core components with electronic steering systems, and account for over 60% of humanoid robot production costs.
- Plans to expand from actuators into sensors and controllers within the robotics field.
- Restructuring its business portfolio to focus on profitability, aiming to achieve over 8% annual sales growth and a 5–6% operating profit margin by 2027 through product specialization and resource reallocation toward future core technologies.
- Currently evaluating the competitiveness of around 60 products and enhancing its profit management system across all stages from development to mass production.
- Plans to strengthen its global presence by increasing the share of global customers in core parts sales to 40% by 2033.
- Will deepen partnerships in North America and Europe and expand orders in high-growth markets like China and India through localized product strategies and supply chain reinforcement.
- Repurchased and canceled treasury shares worth 620 billion won this year, nearly four times last year's amount, demonstrating dedication to transparent and predictable shareholder return policies including dividends and buybacks.
- Is the global no. 6 automotive supplier headquartered in Korea.
- Has outstanding expertise in sensors, sensor fusion in ECUs, and software development for safety control.
- Products include various components for electrification, brakes, chassis and suspension, steering, airbags, lighting, and automotive electronics.
- Operates its R&D headquarters in Korea with four technology centers in the United States, Germany, China, and India.
