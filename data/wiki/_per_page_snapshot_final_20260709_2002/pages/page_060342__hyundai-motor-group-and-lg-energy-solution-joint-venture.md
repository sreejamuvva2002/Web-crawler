# Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution Joint Venture

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-06

**Source:** [Hyundai and LG to Invest Additional $2 Billion in Georgia Battery Plant, Create 400 Jobs](https://evmagz.com/hyundai-and-lg-to-invest-additional-2-billion-in-georgia-battery-plant-create-400-jobs/)  ·  `page_060342`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution announced plans to increase their joint investment in a Georgia battery manufacturing plant by an additional $2 billion.
- The total investment for the Georgia battery manufacturing plant will reach $4.3 billion.
- The plant is expected to produce approximately 300,000 electric vehicle batteries annually.
- Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution intend to allocate a cumulative investment of $7.59 billion over eight years in Bryan County, Georgia.
- The broader strategy encompasses both the battery manufacturing plant and a distinct electric vehicle manufacturing facility.
- Vehicle production at the electric vehicle manufacturing plant is scheduled to commence in January 2025.
- The EV manufacturing facility has a projected annual output of 300,000 vehicles.
- Hyundai Motor Group is the world’s third-largest automaker in terms of vehicle sales.
- The combined manufacturing facilities are collectively referred to as the “Metaplant.”
- The Metaplant has been strategically incentivized by the 2022 U.S. Inflation Reduction Act.
- The Inflation Reduction Act offers a consumer tax credit of $7,500 contingent on electric vehicles being produced within the United States.
- The Act introduces stringent sourcing criteria for critical minerals and battery components, emphasizing domestic production.
- The law includes provisions for substantial U.S. battery production tax credits to foster domestic battery manufacturing growth.
- Hyundai Mobis will assemble battery packs utilizing cells from the Georgia plant.
- These assembled battery packs will be supplied to Hyundai Motor manufacturing facilities across the United States.
- The supplied battery packs will support the production of electric vehicles under the Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis brands.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp expressed enthusiasm for the investment, highlighting the state’s ability to attract suppliers in support of the manufacturing initiative.
- Hyundai’s Chief Operating Officer Jose Munoz previously indicated that the Georgia plant has the potential to eventually produce up to 500,000 vehicles annually, contingent on market demand.
- Hyundai Motor announced a distinct $5 billion electric vehicle battery joint venture in the United States in collaboration with SK On, the battery unit of SK Innovation Co.
