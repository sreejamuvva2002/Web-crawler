# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

## Facts

- Hyundai has begun producing electric SUVs at its $7.6 billion plant in Georgia.
- The plant is located west of Savannah, Georgia.
- Production began less than two years after breaking ground on the sprawling manufacturing plant.
- Groundbreaking for the plant occurred in late October 2022.
- Hyundai’s factory held an “employee-focused celebration” on a Thursday when its first EV for commercial sale rolled off the assembly line.
- Bianca Johnson is the spokesperson for Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- Production processes were validated to ensure vehicles meet Hyundai Motor Group’s high quality standards.
- Initial production of customer vehicles started ahead of schedule.
- A grand opening celebration at the Georgia plant is expected in the first quarter of 2025.
- South Korean automaker Hyundai and battery partner LG Energy Solution plan to employ 8,500 total workers at the Bryan County site once fully operational.
- The Bryan County site is located about 50 miles (80 kilometers) west of Savannah.
- Hyundai plans to produce up to 300,000 EVs per year in Georgia.
- Hyundai will also produce the batteries that power these vehicles at the Georgia site.
- The plant’s vehicle production areas have been completed and are being staffed by more than 1,000 workers.
- Battery-making facilities at the site remain under construction.
- The first vehicles being produced at the site are 2025 models of Hyundai’s Ioniq 5 electric SUVs.
- These American-made EVs will arrive at U.S. dealerships before the end of this year.
- During the first half of 2024, the Ioniq 5 was America’s second-best-selling electric vehicle not made by Tesla.
- The Georgia plant is the largest economic development project the state has ever seen.
- The project came with $2.1 billion in tax breaks and other incentives from state and local governments.
- Hyundai rushed to start making EVs in Georgia within two years of groundbreaking, spurred by federal electric vehicle incentives that reward domestic production.
- The Inflation Reduction Act was passed in 2022 with provisions intended to combat climate change.
- The Inflation Reduction Act includes a tax credit that saves EV buyers up to $7,500, but only on cars made in North America with domestic batteries.
- Hyundai executives complained the law was unfair.
- Hyundai President and Global Chief Operating Officer Jose Munoz said the law caused the automaker to push to open sooner in Georgia.
