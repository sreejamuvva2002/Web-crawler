# Constellium SE

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-12-31

**Source:** [Constellium Reports Fourth Quarter and Full Year 2023 Results; Announces $300 Million Share Repurchase Program | Constellium](https://www.constellium.com/news/constellium-reports-fourth-quarter-and-full-year-2023-results)  ·  `page_018535`

## Facts

- Constellium SE reported financial results for the fourth quarter and full year ended December 31, 2023.
- Fourth quarter 2023 shipments were 336 thousand metric tons, down 9% compared to Q4 2022.
- Fourth quarter 2023 revenue was €1.6 billion, down 13% compared to Q4 2022.
- Fourth quarter 2023 Value-Added Revenue (VAR) was €681 million, down 2% compared to Q4 2022.
- Fourth quarter 2023 net income was €11 million compared to net income of €30 million in Q4 2022.
- Fourth quarter 2023 adjusted EBITDA was €171 million, up 15% compared to Q4 2022.
- Fourth quarter 2023 cash from operations was €185 million and free cash flow was €58 million.
- Full year 2023 shipments were 1.5 million metric tons, down 6% compared to 2022.
- Full year 2023 revenue was €7.2 billion, down 11% compared to 2022.
- Full year 2023 VAR was €2.9 billion, up 7% compared to 2022.
- Full year 2023 net income was €129 million compared to net income of €308 million in 2022.
- Full year 2023 adjusted EBITDA was €713 million, up 6% compared to 2022.
- Full year 2023 cash from operations was €506 million and free cash flow was €170 million.
- Full year 2023 adjusted return on invested capital (Adjusted ROIC) was 11.3%, up 30 bps compared to 2022.
- Net debt to LTM Adjusted EBITDA ratio was 2.3x at December 31, 2023.
- Constellium announced a three-year share repurchase program of up to $300 million expiring in December 2026.
- Jean-Marc Germain serves as Constellium’s Chief Executive Officer.
- Constellium employs 12,000 people globally.
- CEO Jean-Marc Germain stated that 2023 presented challenges including significant inflationary pressures and demand headwinds in several end markets.
- Constellium achieved record Adjusted EBITDA of €713 million in 2023, which included record results in its A&T (Aerospace & Transportation) segment.
- Looking ahead to 2024, Constellium expects aerospace demand to remain strong as the post-pandemic recovery continues.
- In the packaging sector, canstock demand has stabilized and modest growth is expected versus last year.
- In the automotive sector, despite some demand deceleration in the second half of 2023, Constellium expects demand to remain healthy in 2024.
- Constellium continues to experience weakness in most industrial markets at the start of 2024.
- Inflationary pressures are expected to continue in 2024 at a lower rate than the previous two years.
- Extreme cold weather and snow in January impacted operations at Constellium's Muscle Shoals facility, which is expected to lead to a weaker first quarter in 2024.
- Constellium expects to achieve Adjusted EBITDA (excluding the non-cash impact of metal price lag) of €740 million to €770 million and free cash flow in excess of €130 million in 2024.
- Constellium remains confident in delivering on its long-term target of Adjusted EBITDA (excluding the non-cash impact of metal price lag) of over €800 million in 2025.
- The Board authorized the $300 million share repurchase program as the company is now within its target leverage range.
