# Enchem Co.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Korea’s battery materials maker Enchem scraps $143 mn US plant plan - KED Global](https://www.kedglobal.com/batteries/newsView/ked202510020005)  ·  `page_007957`

## Facts

- South Korean battery materials maker Enchem has scrapped its plan to invest $143 million to build a plant in the US.
- The cancellation is due to a protracted downturn in the world’s third-largest electric vehicle market.
- Enchem, the world’s No. 4 electrolyte manufacturer, canceled its plan to spend 200 billion won ($142.7 million) to construct a production base in Tennessee.
- The planned Tennessee facility was intended to supply electrolytes to BlueOval SK LLC.
- BlueOval SK LLC is a joint venture between South Korea’s battery maker SK On Co. and US automaker Ford Motor Co.
- Supply deals for the Tennessee plant, which were expected to be signed last year, were repeatedly delayed.
- Enchem’s decision coincides with Ford's EV business remaining sluggish, having lost $12 billion in total, including $2.2 billion in the first half of 2025.
- The US administration eliminated the tax credit benefit of up to $7,500 for EV buyers starting this month.
- US President Donald Trump’s policies are boosting internal combustion engine vehicles while hurting the EV industry.
- Ford Chief Executive Officer Jim Farley stated that the elimination and softening of emissions rules will sharply curtail EV demand in the US.
- Farley predicted the share of zero-emission vehicles, currently around 10% of the domestic market, could fall by half.
- These policy changes are expected to affect not only the EV industry but also the battery sector.
- BlueOval SK's total capacity is likely to rise to 127 gigawatt-hours (GWh) by next year.
- BlueOval SK is predicted to underutilize its facilities due to the market conditions.
- An industry source in Seoul stated that all companies involved in the local EV value chain with Ford are now revising their strategies.
- Instead of proceeding with the Tennessee project, Enchem plans to focus its investments on its Georgia facility.
- Enchem's Georgia facility supplies electrolytes to LG Energy Solution Ltd.
- Enchem also operates an Indiana plant that serves Samsung SDI Co.
