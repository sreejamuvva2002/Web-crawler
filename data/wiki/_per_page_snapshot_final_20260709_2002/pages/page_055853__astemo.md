## Facts

- Japanese automotive firm Astemo is investing £100m in its Horwich factory.
- The investment could create more than 200 skilled jobs.
- The Tokyo-based firm makes electronic and powertrain systems for vehicles.
- Astemo will create a new production line in Horwich to manufacture inverters for electric vehicles.
- The expansion is backed by the UK Government and follows a “significant” contract win.
- Astemo states it will be the only company in the UK with that specific EV inverter production capability.
- The move puts Greater Manchester at the heart of the country’s push towards electric vehicles.
- The expansion is expected to create around 220 new high-skilled manufacturing jobs.
- The announcement follows a trade mission to Japan by Greater Manchester leaders.
- Metro mayor Andy Burnham and city council leader Bev Craig met with Astemo executive officer Mathieu Devillard at the company’s Tokyo HQ.
- Andy Burnham called the investment a huge vote of confidence in Bolton and Greater Manchester.
- Burnham stated the project puts the city-region at the forefront of the electric vehicle revolution, creating high-quality, long-term jobs and opportunities in advanced manufacturing.
- Burnham noted that during the trade mission to Japan, Astemo's long-term commitment to Greater Manchester was clear and they will have full backing as they look to grow over the coming years.
- Burnham stated international engagement has made Greater Manchester the number one UK destination for foreign direct investment outside London.
- Burnham added that Japanese companies like Astemo have found a genuine partner in the city-region and its growing reputation for innovation is increasingly making it the natural choice for international businesses who want to grow.
- Bev Craig said the new investment demonstrates the confidence international businesses have in Greater Manchester's economy and the real economic value created by long-term engagement with Japan.
- Craig noted that Greater Manchester's diverse, resilient economy is worth approximately £80 billion annually, providing a foundation for Japanese companies to establish and expand UK operations.
- Craig mentioned looking forward to celebrating the depth of cultural and trading partnership with Japan during Japan Week in Manchester in September this year.
- Bolton Council’s leader, Coun Nick Peel, called the news fantastic for Bolton, which will create hundreds of jobs in Horwich and ensure a new production line for EV inverters.
