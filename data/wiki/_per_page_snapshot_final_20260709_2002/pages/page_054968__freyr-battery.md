# FREYR Battery

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-11-14

**Source:** [FREYR Battery to Open $2.6B Manufacturing Plant in Metro Atlanta, Create 700 Jobs - REBusinessOnline](https://rebusinessonline.com/freyr-battery-to-open-2-6b-manufacturing-plant-in-metro-atlanta-create-700-jobs/)  ·  `page_054968`

## Facts

- FREYR Battery (NYSE: FREY) is a Luxembourg-based battery manufacturer focused on sustainability.
- FREYR Battery has announced plans to open a new manufacturing plant in Coweta County, Georgia.
- The proposed plant site is located approximately 40 miles south of Atlanta.
- The project represents a total capital investment of approximately $2.6 billion.
- The manufacturing facility will be known as Giga America.
- The exact location of the Giga America facility was not disclosed at the time of the announcement.
- The site is reported to span 368 acres.
- The site will be situated near distribution centers for Amazon and Shopify.
- The site offers proximity to Interstate 85, which connects Atlanta with Coweta County.
- Community and company leaders expect to create more than 700 new jobs over the next seven years.
- A specific timeline for project completion was not disclosed.
- FREYR Battery considered more than 150 sites across 25 different states before selecting the Coweta County location.
- The company’s products are designed to decarbonize transportation and energy systems, including as components for electric vehicles.
- Tom Einar Jensen, co-founder and CEO of FREYR Battery, stated that the company expects to make meaningful investments to spur job creation and develop localized, decarbonized supply chains in the U.S. to enhance energy security and economic activity.
- Georgia has recently established itself as a hub for electric vehicle production.
- In December of the previous year, California-based Rivian Inc. (NASDAQ: RIVN) announced plans to build a $5 billion facility in Rutledge, Georgia.
- The Rivian facility is located about 50 miles east of Atlanta.
- The Rivian project has since been met with environmental concerns and backlash from local residents.
- In May of the current year, Hyundai Motor Group revealed plans to develop a $5.5 billion manufacturing hub for its electric vehicle division in Bryan County, Georgia.
- The Hyundai facility is located near Savannah in coastal Georgia.
- At the time of their announcements, local leaders estimated the Rivian project would add about 7,000 jobs.
- At the time of its announcement, local leaders estimated the Hyundai project would add about 8,000 jobs.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp's office cited the state’s prime location, extensive infrastructure, skilled workforce, and business-friendly climate as primary factors attracting companies like FREYR Battery.
- Katie Kirkpatrick, CEO and president of the Metro Atlanta Chamber, welcomed FREYR Battery to metro Atlanta, noting it has quickly become a hub for battery cell research and production.
- Kirkpatrick stated that FREYR Battery will benefit from the region's vibrant research ecosystem and the diversity of talent that stands ready to support the company’s next-generation efforts.
- FREYR Battery’s stock price opened at $14.40 per share on Monday, Nov. 14, marking the first full day of trading since the announcement.
- The stock price represented an increase from $10.92 per share a year ago.
