# SHIROKI Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2012-03-31

**Source:** [SHIROKI Corporation  Business Report FY2012 - MarkLines Automotive Industry Portal](https://www.marklines.com/en/top500/cf/s500_134_hl2011)  ·  `page_049813`

## Facts

- The Company won new program business from Toyota for its energy-saving and environmentally friendly vehicle models, delivering door-sashes for "Prius α", and window regulators for both "Aqua" and "Mira e:S".
- The Company was awarded the Weight-saving Technology Award from Daihatsu for the window regulators delivered to "Mira e:S".
- Shiroki North America, Inc. was awarded the North American Excellence in Quality Award from Nissan North America for vehicle-body parts and high standards in quality of doors.
- As a result of the quality award, the Company was awarded new program business for five more of Nissan NA's vehicle models.
- Financial performance at China operations significantly increased year-on-year due to streamlining initiatives by Guangzhou Shiroki to improve production operations and procure dies and facilities locally, alongside aggressive sales activities with OEMs in China.
- Shiroki Corp. (Thailand) Ltd.'s revenue temporarily dropped due to lower customer production volumes from flooding, but business performance is forecasted to recover due to rising domestic demand and greater export demand to neighboring countries.
- The Company established P.T. Shiroki Indonesia in August 2011 and began constructing a plant to mass-produce window regulators and seat adjusters for local OEMs in Bekasi, West Java.
- P.T. Shiroki Indonesia will launch production in the second half of 2012, aiming for sales of approximately Rp. 170 billion (about 1.5 billion yen) by 2015.
- The new Indonesian subsidiary will be formed in September with an initial capital of 2.5 million dollars and led by Naoki Hiragushi, currently a factory manager of Shiroki Corp. (Thailand) Ltd.
- The 19,000-square-meter Indonesian facility is going to employ 70 people by 2015.
- The Company provided greater technical support to its subsidiary Technico Industries Ltd., which launched commercial production of window regulators and seat adjusters for Maruti Suzuki India "Swift", ensuring profits through effective investment.
- R&D Expenditure for the automotive components business was 2,714 million JPY for the fiscal year ended March 31, 2012.
- Recent R&D achievements in the automotive components business include next-generation power seats, next-generation manual seats, new wire-type regulators, new arm-type regulators, and new door frames.
- The Company holds a technology licensing-in agreement with Keiper GmbH & Co. (Germany) for seat recliners from April 1, 1988 to March 31, 2013.
- The Company holds technology licensing-out agreements with NHK Spring (Thailand) for seat recliners and seat adjusters, Summit Laemchabang Auto Body (Thailand) for door frames, Asan Co., Ltd. (Korea) for door frames, RG Brose (South Africa) and Brose do Brasil (Brazil) for window regulators, and Technico Industries Ltd. (India) for seat recliners, seat adjusters, and window regulators.
- Overall capital expenditure for the fiscal year ended March 31, 2012 was 3,924 million JPY, with most devoted to the automotive component business to improve facilities for productivity, cost reduction, and production for new vehicle models.
- Shiroki-GA, LLC in Georgia, USA has a planned capital investment of 598 million JPY for manufacturing equipment from January 2012 to December 2012.
- Shiroki North America, Inc. in Tennessee, USA has a planned capital investment of 204 million JPY for manufacturing equipment from January 2012 to December 2012.
- Shiroki-GT, LLC in Tennessee, USA has a planned capital investment of 60 million JPY for manufacturing equipment from January 2012 to December 2012.
