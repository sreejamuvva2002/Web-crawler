# GoTrenton! collaborative e-mobility pilot

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** charging_infrastructure  ·  **Published:** 2024-02-14

**Source:** [Alternative Fuels Data Center: Electric Mobility Increases Access to Transportation in New Jersey](https://afdc.energy.gov/case/3130)  ·  `page_003781`

## Facts

- The GoTrenton! collaborative e-mobility pilot launched in September 2023 in Trenton, New Jersey.
- The pilot was conceived and led by Isles, Inc., a community developmental and environmental organization.
- It is one of the first subsidized rideshare efforts in the country to serve low-income communities.
- Partners include the City of Trenton, ChargEVC, Environment NJ, and New Jersey Clean Cities Coalition (NJCCC), which serves as technical advisor.
- NJCCC director Chuck Feinberg noted that 30% of Trenton residents are car-free households, limiting access to jobs, medical appointments, food services, and after-school programs.
- Securing grants reduced ride costs to $2 per ride for the program.
- Trenton has a poverty rate of 27%, making ride accessibility a key program consideration.
- Isles Inc. led citizen engagement initiatives including surveys, listening sessions, community partnership agreements with local businesses, local ambassadors, and a stakeholder advisory committee.
- Circuit Electric Mobility Solutions was awarded the GoTrenton! pilot contract for four electric shuttles and a mobile app following competitive solicitation.
- The project team plans to expand operations to include additional electric shuttles, carshare vehicles, and vans in future phases dependent on funding and ridership.
- During its first few months of limited operations, the program provided more than 1,100 rides serving over 1,600 passengers.
- The project increased publicly available EV charging ports in Trenton by 20, with four owned by Isles and the remainder by the City of Trenton.
- There are now 50 charging ports at nearly 20 charging stations within five miles of the city center.
- Anticipated revenue from the charging stations will be used to subsidize program operations.
- Feinberg and NJCCC staff secured over $1 million in funding through proposal requests and grants, drawing on decades of experience.
- Coordination benefited from relationships with the New Jersey Department of Environmental Protection, New Jersey Board of Public Utilities, New Jersey Economic Development Authority, Delaware Valley Regional Planning Commission (DVRPC), and the local utility for charging installation.
- NJCCC accessed technical assistance from the National Renewable Energy Laboratory as part of a U.S. Department of Energy partnership to advance alternative fuels nationwide.
- Chuck Feinberg stated, “Being responsible for the entire state, I usually focus on the big picture, like reducing emissions through our work with large fleets. This project helped us drill down to demonstrate how Clean Cities coalitions can also help underserved communities with day-to-day needs.”
- Feinberg and NJCCC co-director Caroline McCallum continue advising on scaling GoTrenton!, including route expansion and connecting residents to good-paying jobs at nearby warehouse and fulfillment centers.
- NJCCC is targeting alternative fuel programs in the underserved communities of Newark next.
