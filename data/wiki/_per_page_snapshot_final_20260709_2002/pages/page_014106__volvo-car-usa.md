# Volvo Car USA

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-09-20

**Source:** [Spartanburg County: Volvo planning $19.5M investment](https://www.goupstate.com/story/news/local/2022/09/20/volvo-planning-19-5m-investment-spartanburg-county/69504626007/)  ·  `page_014106`

## Facts

- Swedish carmaker Volvo is planning to invest $19.5 million to purchase tooling, machinery and equipment for use at three suppliers in Spartanburg County.
- Spartanburg County Council on Monday approved the second of three readings that will provide tax breaks for Volvo Car USA.
- The fee-in-lieu-of-tax agreement does not list how many jobs will be created.
- The carmaker is also planning to invest $42 million for equipment to be used by suppliers handling tooling and mold preparation at two locations in Greenville County, according to Greenville Area Development Corporation.
- Volvo currently operates its first U.S. plant, built in 2015, in Berkeley County near Charleston.
- Spartanburg County Councilman David Britt, who chairs the county's economic development committee, stated that Volvo's impact in South Carolina is felt across all 46 counties.
- The approved tax breaks provide a reduced property tax rate of 6% for 30 years, compared to the standard corporate rate of 10.5%.
- Volvo will receive a total of $330,000 in special source revenue credits over three years under the agreement.
- The agreement includes a clawback clause that will retroactively terminate the tax breaks if Volvo does not meet a minimum investment threshold of $15.6 million within a five-year period.
- The agreement identifies three Spartanburg County supplier locations for the investment: Auria Solutions at 1 Austrian Way in Spartanburg, Plasman (formerly Minghua) at 1000 Robinson Road in Greer, and Lear Corporation at 1200 Woods Chapel Road in Duncan.
- The council also approved the first of three readings for tax breaks for 'Project Double Down,' which plans a $33.5 million investment, though the company name and location remain undisclosed until final approval.
- A second reading was approved for a FILOT agreement with TTI Consumer Power Tools Inc. and Fort Prince Building A LLC for a planned $200 million investment that will create 470 new full-time jobs, though the location is unidentified.
- A second reading was approved for a FILOT agreement with Spartanburg SF RRL, LLC and tenant Visual Comfort & Co. for a $68.2 million investment creating 105 new full-time jobs at S.C. 195 and Reidville Road.
- The council approved the final reading for a FILOT agreement with Crusoe Acquisitions LLC and Jupiter Two Properties LLC for a $12.5 million planned investment with no specified jobs at 991 Robinson Road.
- A second reading was approved for a FILOT agreement with Tyger River Industrial Owner, LLC for a $137.7 million investment with no specified jobs at 2000 Victor Hill Road.
- A second reading was approved for a FILOT agreement with The Cubes at Fort Prince for a $65.5 million investment with no specified jobs.
- A second reading was approved for a FILOT agreement with Dev. Property SC LLC for a $34.9 million investment with no specified jobs at 6629 Pottery Road.
