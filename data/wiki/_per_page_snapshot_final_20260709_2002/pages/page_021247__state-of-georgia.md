# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2023-07-01

**Source:** [Georgia Job Creation & Momentum Remains Strong - Athens CEO](https://athensceo.com/features/2024/08/georgia-job-creation-momentum-remains-strong/)  ·  `page_021247`

## Facts

- Governor Brian Kemp and GDEcD announced sustained momentum for business recruitment and expansions in Fiscal Year 2024.
- Between July 1, 2023, and June 30, 2024, GDEcD’s Global Commerce team supported 429 facility expansions and new locations statewide.
- These efforts resulted in more than $20.3 billion in investment and the commitment of 26,900 new private sector jobs statewide.
- 83% of FY2024 expansions and new locations are directed to communities outside the 10-county Atlanta region, representing over 18,300 new jobs and $12.5 billion in investment.
- Manufacturing, including the aerospace, automotive, and e-mobility sectors, accounted for over 50% of expansions or new locations in FY2024.
- Kia Georgia added the EV9 to its lineup at its facility in Troup County.
- Toyota Industries Corporation established a campus in Jackson County.
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America suppliers announced new locations across Bryan, Candler, Chatham, Coweta, Effingham, Harris, and Laurens Counties.
- SOLARCYCLE, an innovator in solar panel recycling, announced plans for a new location in Polk County to support Qcells’ operations.
- Governor and First Lady Kemp led economic development missions to Korea and Switzerland focusing on strengthening relationships with Hyundai Motor Group and its supplier network.
- Georgia landed more than 8,100 new jobs and $5.9 billion in investments from international companies expanding or locating in the U.S. market during FY2024.
- The State of Georgia celebrated over 50 years of continuous representation in Europe and Japan, which remain among the top sources of foreign direct investment.
- Georgia will mark 40 years of continuous representation in Korea in the coming fiscal year, earning the James A. Van Fleet Award from the Korea Society.
- Markets where Georgia maintains business and trade representation accounted for 29% of total new jobs and investments in FY2024.
- Georgia earned the rank of No. 1 state for business from Area Development magazine for 10 consecutive years.
- Georgia earned the No. 1 spot in Site Selection magazine’s annual Prosperity Cup rankings for successful investment recruitment in 2023.
- Georgia was awarded a Gold Shovel by Area Development for achieving significant success in attracting new business investment and job creation in 2023.
- Business Facilities magazine ranked Georgia as the No. 1 state for EV investments.
- Business Facilities magazine ranked Georgia as a top 10 state for food processing, cybersecurity, aerospace and defense, and AI growth hubs.
- Lt. Governor Burt Jones emphasized that workforce development through the Technical College System of Georgia, University System of Georgia, College and Career Academies, and private-public partnerships is critical to supporting incoming businesses.
- Speaker of the House Jon Burns highlighted historic investments in education, workforce development, and infrastructure as key drivers of economic success.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson noted that Georgia's strong partnerships and diverse industry landscape provide major advantages for businesses.
- GDEcD Commissioner Kristi Brigman stated that Georgia is exploring new heights in mobility and technology following a decade of excellence in project milestones.
