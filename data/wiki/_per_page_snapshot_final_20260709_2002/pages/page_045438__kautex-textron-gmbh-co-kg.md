# Kautex Textron GmbH & Co. KG

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2014-01-10

**Source:** [Kautex Expands Lavonia Plant - 92.1 WLHR](https://921wlhr.com/kautex-expands-lavonia-plant/)  ·  `page_045438`

## Facts

- Local Lavonia industry Kautex has announced it will expand its current facility this summer.
- The company will add another 95,000 square feet of warehouse space to its existing plant on SR17S.
- The expansion represents a $3.3 million dollar investment.
- Kautex Textron GmbH & Co. KG is a subsidiary of Textron, Inc., which is based in Bonn, Germany.
- Textron, Inc. is considered one of the top 100 automotive suppliers worldwide.
- The company is a leading global supplier of plastic fuel tank systems, selective catalytic reduction systems, and clear vision systems.
- In Lavonia, Kautex manufactures fuel systems for major automakers including GM, BMW, Daimler-Chrysler, Nissan, Honda, and Mercedes Benz.
- Tyler Miles, Director of Operations for Kautex, announced the expansion is to accommodate a new product line called SCR Systems for diesel trucks and vehicles.
- The SCR System holds a urea fluid that ejects into the catalytic converter for diesel systems, mixing with nitrous oxide to reduce carbon emissions by producing water instead.
- The SCR System will be installed in both diesel trucks and diesel-powered vehicles.
- Kautex is starting production of VW small products, including the Golf, Jetta, and Passat, marking significant growth in the smaller vehicle segment.
- Kautex is the only plant in North America that provides SCR technology to its customers.
- Kautex first opened in Lavonia in 1993 with 20 employees, initially manufacturing a window fluid system for the automotive industry.
- In 2003, the company expanded into the fuel system segment and added a 20,000 square foot facility to its existing building.
- The company plans to open the new addition this summer.
- Georgia Commissioner of Economic Development Chris Carr attended the press conference announcing the expansion.
- Chris Carr noted that last year Georgia was named the number one state in the nation to do business according to Site Selection Magazine.
- Carr stated that last year, 66% of economic development announcements were expansions of existing Georgia companies.
- The expansion aligns with Governor Nathan Deal’s effort to grow the state’s economy by bringing in new industry and offering incentives for existing businesses to expand.
- Georgia Commissioner of Economic Development Chris Carr congratulated Kautex for their announcement and contribution to Lavonia, Franklin County, and the State of Georgia.
- Kautex operates in 15 countries with a total workforce of 5,500 employees.
