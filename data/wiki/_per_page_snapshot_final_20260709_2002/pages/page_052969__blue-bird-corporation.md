# Blue Bird Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2021-12-15

**Source:** [Blue Bird (BLBD) Stock News, Page 14 | StockTitan](https://www.stocktitan.net/news/BLBD/page-14.html)  ·  `page_052969`

## Facts

- Blue Bird Corporation designs, engineers, manufactures and sells school buses, including electric, propane, natural gas and conventional models, and also sells replacement bus parts and extended warranties.
- Blue Bird successfully completed a $75 million securities purchase agreement with Coliseum Capital Management, issuing shares at $16.00 each.
- The $75 million capital will be directed towards enhancing the company's electric vehicle strategy, particularly following new legislation allocating $5 billion for clean school buses.
- Coliseum Capital Management now owns 15% of Blue Bird and has placed a representative on the board.
- The funds will help with working capital, pay down debt, and invest in technology to bolster Blue Bird's leadership in the alternative-power bus market.
- Blue Bird announced its fiscal 2021 fourth quarter and full year financial results on December 15, 2021, at 4:30PM ET via a public audio webcast featuring CEO Matthew Stevenson and CFO Razvan Radulescu.
- Blue Bird is recognized as a leader in the design and manufacturing of school buses, with over 550,000 buses sold.
- Blue Bird announced a fourth amendment to its credit facility, effective through the fiscal second quarter of 2023.
- The credit amendment provides covenant relief by replacing the net leverage ratio with a minimum EBITDA covenant and increases the leverage ratio from 3.50:1 to 4.00:1 for the first two quarters of fiscal 2023.
- The amendment introduces a new pricing grid based on the net debt to EBITDA ratio and allows for an additional $10 million of revolver availability.
- CEO Matthew Stevenson emphasized the importance of the credit amendment for operational flexibility during the pandemic.
- Blue Bird filed a universal shelf registration statement on Form S-3 with the SEC, allowing it to raise up to $200 million through various securities.
- The shelf registration is described as a prudent approach to capital management to capitalize on growth opportunities from the Bipartisan Infrastructure Deal and the evolving electric vehicle market.
- The shelf registration will remain in effect for three years once declared effective, providing flexibility for future offerings.
- Blue Bird is optimistic about the inclusion of student transportation in the recently approved Bipartisan Infrastructure Deal.
- The company has over 20,000 clean fuel buses and aims to enhance emissions-reducing school transportation across the U.S.
- The $5 billion investment in electric bus production is expected to modernize school fleets and decrease maintenance costs, potentially saving districts over $6,000 annually.
- Blue Bird has pioneered electric buses for over 25 years and views increased demand for alternative-powered vehicles as a positive outcome of the legislation.
- Nuvve Holding Corp. announced plans to install 200 V2G DC fast charging stations at Blue Bird's facility in Fort Valley, Georgia.
- The charging hub will optimize electric bus utilization and enable them to function as energy storage assets.
- Utilizing Nuvve's V2G technology, the buses will charge and discharge energy to enhance battery health and reduce utility costs.
- The hub aims to aggregate power from multiple buses, creating a virtual power plant with a capacity of 25 MW that could potentially supply power to over 10,000 homes during peak demand.
- Blue Bird appointed Razvan Radulescu as its new Chief Financial Officer effective October 1, 2021.
- Razvan Radulescu brings over 20 years of experience from Daimler Trucks, having held significant roles in Finance, Procurement, and Compliance.
- He succeeds Phil Tighe, the interim CFO, who will assist in the transition until year-end.
- Blue Bird reported third quarter fiscal 2021 results showing net income of $4.3 million, an increase of $3 million year-over-year.
- Revenue reached $196.7 million, marking a 4% increase from the previous year.
- Adjusted EBITDA improved to $13.2 million, representing a $0.7 million rise.
- Supply chain disruptions led to 550 fewer bookings and caused year-to-date net sales to decline by $106 million, down 17.7%.
- The order backlog remains robust at over 4,000 buses, indicating strong market demand for traditional and electric school buses.
- Micro Bird Holdings, a joint venture of Blue Bird and Girardin, acquired a controlling interest in GranTuned Automobiles (Ecotuned), a supplier of EV drivetrains.
- This acquisition enhances Micro Bird's position in the light and medium-duty EV market by leveraging Ecotuned’s advanced technologies and expertise.
- The partnership holds over 80% market share in North America's Type A school bus segment and is expected to bolster revenue and profitability for both Micro Bird and Blue Bird Corporation.
- Nuvve Holding Corp. and Blue Bird are expanding their partnership to integrate Nuvve's vehicle-to-grid technology into Blue Bird's electric school buses.
- The collaboration aims to offer financing through Levo Mobility LLC, enabling schools to lease V2G electric buses and related infrastructure with no upfront costs.
- Levo has committed up to $750 million, potentially electrifying 3,500 buses.
- This initiative allows schools access to revenue-generating capabilities by utilizing buses to stabilize the grid, enhancing the appeal and affordability of sustainable school transportation.
