# LG Energy Solution

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-03-23

**Source:** [LG Energy Solution and Michigan: Leading the Battery EVolution | Michigan Business](https://www.michiganbusiness.org/news/2022/03/lg-energy-solution-and-michigan-leading-the-battery-evolution/)  ·  `page_056190`

## Facts

- In March 2022, LG Energy Solution announced a $1.7 billion investment to quintuple plant capacity in Holland, Mich.
- The investment provides a pipeline to produce battery components in Michigan today and into the future as the EV industry grows.
- LG Energy Solution promotes sustainability, electrification and innovation.
- Bonchul Koo is President of LG Energy Solution Michigan.
- Michigan was chosen due to its rich pool of talent, proximity to the geographic epicenter of the automotive industry, and strong support.
- LGES aims to achieve carbon neutral by 2050.
- LGES was founded in 1947.
- LGES was formerly known as LG Chem Michigan and is a division of LG.
- The company designs and produces innovative energy solutions for the automotive industry.
- LGES manufactures large lithium-ion polymer battery cells and packs for EVs and other energy storage applications.
- LGES has had a presence in Holland since 2010.
- Michigan was chosen for LGES's first EV battery plant in the United States.
- LGES now has 1,495 employees in Michigan.
- The company grew its Holland battery factory in 2018, adding 100,000 square feet to its facilities.
- GM announced a historic $7 billion investment in the state mere months before this announcement.
- GM's investment includes up to $2.5 billion to build Ultium’s third U.S. battery cell plant in Lansing and Delta Township.
- GM acted as a key partner in bringing LG to Michigan.
- GM's latest Ultium joint venture with LGES is expected to grow the state’s global leadership in EV and advanced battery production.
- The facility will include a clean room environment and machinery/equipment for high-volume cell output.
- The project is expected to generate a total capital investment of $2.5 billion and create up to 1,700 jobs.
- The Ultium battery cell plant project was among the first approved utilizing the new Critical Industry Program and Strategic Site Readiness Program.
- These programs were signed into law by Governor Whitmer in December 2021.
- The programs aim to ensure Michigan can compete for billions in investment and attract tens of thousands of jobs.
- LG has investments statewide in Troy, Holland, and Hazen Park that began under former Michigan Governor Jennifer Granholm (now U.S. Secretary of Energy).
- A new Ultium battery cell plant is in Lansing.
- Michigan is home to about one-third of U.S. battery production.
- Michigan ranks 5th nationally for employment related to electric vehicle battery manufacturing.
- There are 2,000 workers employed at various Michigan battery manufacturing and OEM locations including LGES.
- West Michigan – dubbed the “Smartcoast” – has become a hub for lithium-ion battery technology found in electric cars, RVs and specialty vehicles.
- Holland-based companies Jolt Energy Storage Technologies, Volta Power Systems, and Global Battery Solutions are at the forefront of this entrepreneurial revolution.
- Lakeshore Advantage is a nonprofit economic development organization founded in 2003 with key support from MEDC.
- Lakeshore Advantage aids companies from startups to grownups and helps nurture entrepreneurship in Ottawa and Allegan counties.
- Through its SURGE program located in the Holland SmartZone, Lakeshore Advantage supported Jolt, Volta, and Global Battery Solutions by providing mentorship, resources, and networking opportunities that helped them secure a collective $90,000 to achieve their next milestones.
- Lakeshore Advantage was a vital partner in securing the recent LGES investment in Michigan, providing hands-on support and guidance.
- Jennifer Owens is Lakeshore Advantage President.
- LG choosing West Michigan for expansion cements it as a national competitor for future-focused economic prosperity.
- LGES was drawn to Holland for its quality of life, community-centered focus, and proximity to Lake Michigan.
- LGES's Holland production facilities are landfill-free, meaning less than 1% of its total waste stream goes to a landfill.
- Of the waste produced, 97.4% is repurposed and 2.5% goes to a waste energy facility.
- LGES is developing a Battery Academy for onboarding employees from entry level to experienced.
- The training module lays out advancement pathways.
- Training programs include career path training, apprenticeships, engineering development programs, leadership development, and hydraulic and pneumatic trainings.
- Modules will be developed in cross-collaboration with higher ed institutions: Grand Rapids Community College, Muskegon Community College, and Grand Valley State University.
- LGES provides tuition reimbursement for job-related degrees and certifications.
- LGES debuted on the South Korean stock market in January 2022.
- From its initial 2009 investment in Holland to the latest expansion, LGES has poised Michigan to become a battery capital of the world.
- South Korea is the third highest global investor in Michigan, investing $1.2 billion over the last five years.
- South Korean FDI projects were the largest amongst all countries, with projects averaging $152.7 million in capex.
- Projects originating in South Korea were approximately four times larger than the average across all other source countries.
- Michigan is home to more than 1,400 foreign companies with more than 5,000 locations throughout the state.
