# Trenton Pressing

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Metal forming company jumps into the thick of metal fabricating | Nanning Metal Stamping Group Co.,Ltd](https://www.nbtmi.com/news/metal-forming-company-jumps-into-the-thick-of-metal-fabricating)  ·  `page_012452`

## Facts

- Trenton Pressing is located in Trenton, Ga., and operates as a longtime Tier 1 and Tier 2 supplier of stamped parts and complex assemblies for automotive companies and golf cart manufacturers.
- During the pandemic, new owners purchased the business to diversify its customer base by adding metal fabricating capabilities to its existing stamping, robotic welding, and assembly services.
- The company's ultimate goal is to build full turnkey fabrications for customers rather than just supplying individual parts.
- Brad Coffman works at Trenton Pressing and brings 30 years of experience in the metal fabricating industry, focusing on laser cutting and press brake forming.
- The company built a new 100,000-sq.-ft. building on its campus specifically for warehousing and assembly activities.
- The new building freed up space in Plant 1 to accommodate a laser cutting machine and a press brake.
- Plant 2 houses the company’s 15 robotic welding cells, as well as manual welding and assembly operations.
- In late 2023, Trenton Pressing purchased a BesCutter 20-kW fiber laser with a 5- by 10-ft. bed and automatic loading/unloading capability from Automated Solutions.
- The new laser cutting machine became operational in early 2024 and is designed to process up to 2-in. carbon steel plate.
- The laser can cut using compressed high-pressure air for 3/8- to ½-in. material, which the company finds more economical than using oxygen or nitrogen.
- An automated load and unload gantry system was installed alongside the laser, using vacuum suction cups to load raw sheet metal and fork-style lifters to remove cut parts.
- The automated system features two tables—one for sheet metal and one for finished parts—and can run unattended for eight hours without reloading material.
- To prevent machine downtime, the company purchased two cutting heads that can be switched out when needed, while operators can change the protective lens in seconds.
- In early 2025, Trenton Pressing installed a Haco EuroMaster 275-ton CNC press brake equipped with 6-axis backgauging, CNC crowning, automatic angle measurement during bending, hydraulic upper tool clamping, and offline programming capability.
- The press brake features a 10-ft. bed and a 21.25-in. daylight opening to accommodate parts coming off the laser cutting machine.
- Brad Coffman noted that finding press brake operators is difficult and that it takes longer to train a press brake operator than a laser operator due to a steeper learning curve.
- The company plans to train one of its engineers to run the press brake, creating two forming experts who can help train other staff members.
- Fabricated parts are moved via kanban trucks that travel back and forth among the buildings, with the box truck making at least three stops per day at each facility.
- After leaving fabricating, materials go to the new building where they are distributed as needed to welding, coaters, or directly to customers.
- Management used the opportunity of moving assembly to the new building to refinish Plant 1 floors using large gas-powered floor grinders and polyurethane paint.
- Approximately 60% of all factory floors now feature these refinished concrete surfaces, which management considers a selling tool due to the owners' high standards.
- Randall Hamilton serves as Trenton Pressing’s plant manager and operates the Haco EuroMaster 275-ton press brake.
