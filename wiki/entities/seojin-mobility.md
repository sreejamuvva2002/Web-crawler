# Seojin Mobility

**Type:** Company  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[automotive-components|Automotive Components]]  ·  **Location:** Escobedo, Nuevo León, Mexico, Mexico

## Overview

Korean electric motor manufacturer Seojin Mobility is investing US $300 million to build its first plant in Escobedo, Nuevo León, Mexico. The facility will produce EV motors for Hyundai’s Savannah, Georgia plant and aims to collaborate with Kia, while creating 300 jobs.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Will build electric vehicle motors for the Hyundai plant in Savannah, Georgia, linking Korean motor supply to the Georgia EV manufacturing ecosystem.

## Key facts

- Seojin Mobility will invest US $300 million in its first Mexico plant.
- The first stage of construction is expected to be completed by February 2025 at a cost of $200 million; a second $100 million phase will add hybrid motor production.
- The new facility is projected to create 300 jobs.
- Seojin Mobility will build motors for the Hyundai plant in Savannah, Georgia and plans to collaborate with Kia Motors’ operations in Pesquería.
- Seojin Mobility is the primary supplier for Hyundai and Kia and also holds contracts with GM, Renault, Ferrari, and Volvo.
- Proximity to Tesla’s Santa Catarina plant may provide opportunities for new product development.
- The site was chosen for its location near Monterrey’s industrial center and being 150 miles from Laredo, Texas, as well as the availability of a qualified workforce.
- Escobedo municipal president highlighted the city’s nearshoring strategy, including an upcoming $1 billion industrial district with two hotels.
