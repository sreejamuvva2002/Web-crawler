# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-03-31

**Source:** [Charged EVs | Hyundai to expand production capacity at its Georgia EV plant to 500,000 vehicles per year - Charged EVs](https://chargedevs.com/newswire/hyundai-to-expand-production-capacity-at-its-georgia-ev-plant-to-500000-vehicles-per-year/)  ·  `page_018701`

## Facts

- Hyundai announced plans to expand production capacity at its new $7.6-billion EV factory in Georgia by two-thirds to a total of 500,000 vehicles per year.
- Hyundai broke ground at the Metaplant America in Bryan County, Georgia in the fall of 2022.
- Hyundai began producing EVs at the plant less than two years after breaking ground.
- The company received some $2.1 billion in tax breaks and other incentives from state and local governments.
- Around 1,200 people are currently working at the plant.
- The plant produces Hyundai’s Ioniq 5.
- The plant produces the larger Ioniq 9.
- The Ioniq 9 is to be released this spring.
- Hyundai plans to build a $5.8-billion steel mill in Louisiana.
- The Louisiana steel mill will produce auto parts for Hyundai's assembly plants in Georgia and Alabama.
- Hyundai Motor Group Executive Chairman Euisun Chung said the company has come to Georgia “to stay, to invest and to grow.”
- Hyundai Motor Group Executive Chairman Euisun Chung stated, “I have never been more confident about building the future of mobility in America.”
- Hyundai Motor Company CEO Jose Munoz said, “This plant couldn’t come at a better time than now.”
- Hyundai Motor Company CEO Jose Munoz stated that all cars produced at the plant will be exempted from any tariffs.
