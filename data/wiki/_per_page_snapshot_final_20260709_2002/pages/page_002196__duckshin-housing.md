# Duckshin Housing

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2023-08-03

**Source:** [Duckshin Housing Plans First US Facility in Athens-Clarke County | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-releases/2023/duckshin-housing-plans-first-us-facility-athens-clarke-county)  ·  `page_002196`

## Facts

- Duckshin Housing, a construction materials manufacturer headquartered in Seoul, Korea, plans its first U.S. facility in Athens-Clarke County.
- The company will invest more than $15 million in the new facility.
- The project will create 100 new jobs.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced the investment and job creation on August 3, 2023.
- Duckshin Housing produces architectural deck plates and frames made of galvanized steel and rebars.
- The company's deck plates are designed for efficient production, cost-effectiveness, and enhanced safety.
- The new facility will be located on Olympic Drive at the Athena Industrial Park in Athens.
- Construction is expected to begin later in 2023.
- Upon opening, Duckshin will hire for welder, machine operator, forklift driver, and other operations positions.
- Job postings will be published on the company's website when available.
- Duckshin plans to work with local employment services for hiring.
- Chairman Myung Hwan Kim stated that locating in Athens allows the company to be closer to growing construction projects in Georgia and across the U.S.
- Mayor Kelly Girtz of the Athens-Clarke County Unified Government welcomed Duckshin Housing, noting its products enhance construction safety and efficiency for Southeast communities.
- Yoonie Kim, Director of Korean Investment, represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s Global Commerce team on the project.
- The project was developed in partnership with the Athens-Clarke County Unified Government and Georgia EMC.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson noted that locating construction material companies in the state can reduce construction costs for future facilities.
- Advanced manufacturing is one of Georgia’s leading industries, having announced more than 7,700 new jobs through economic development projects in the last six months of 2022.
- Since 2019, Athens-Clarke County has announced more than 3,400 new jobs and $903 million in investments.
