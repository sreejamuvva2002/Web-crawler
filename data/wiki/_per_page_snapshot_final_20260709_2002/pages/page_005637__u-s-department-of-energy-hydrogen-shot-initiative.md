# U.S. Department of Energy Hydrogen Shot Initiative

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [DOE Releases First in Series of Reports Highlighting Pathways Toward Clean Hydrogen EarthShot | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/articles/doe-releases-first-series-reports-highlighting-pathways-toward-clean-hydrogen-earthshot)  ·  `page_005637`

## Facts

- The U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) announced the findings of a report highlighting ways to achieve affordable, abundant clean hydrogen through thermal conversion pathways.
- The report is the first of three assessments for the Department’s Hydrogen Shot™ initiative.
- The Hydrogen Shot was unveiled in June 2021 as the first goal of the Energy Earthshots Initiative™, a set of eight moonshots to accelerate breakthroughs of more abundant, affordable, and reliable clean energy solutions within the decade.
- The Hydrogen Shot seeks to reduce the cost of clean hydrogen by 80% to $1 per kilogram by 2031.
- Clean hydrogen is produced with zero or near-zero carbon emissions and supports President Biden’s climate and energy security goals.
- U.S. Deputy Secretary of Energy David M. Turk announced the report’s findings at COP28 in Dubai, United Arab Emirates on December 5, 2023.
- Turk stated that early in the Biden-Harris Administration, the administration invested in Energy Earthshots to bridge gaps on key clean energy technologies and make clean hydrogen a viable, affordable source of energy.
- The Hydrogen Shot Technology Assessment: Thermal Conversion Approaches report was led by experts at DOE’s National Energy Technology Laboratory and Office of Fossil Energy and Carbon Management, coordinated with the Hydrogen and Fuel Cell Technologies Office.
- The report presents a snapshot of thermal conversion pathways for clean hydrogen production, including technology status and envisioned approaches for achieving goals through research, development, and deployment advances.
- DOE experts will release two additional reports covering hydrogen production from electrolysis pathways and advanced pathways like photoelectrochemical, solar-thermochemical, and biological hydrogen production.
- The report found that hydrogen costs could be reduced to between $1.30 and $1.40 per kilogram through technology advancement.
- Beyond RD&D improvements, the report identified plant scale, market scenarios, plant site location, optimization of carbon dioxide transport and storage, byproduct sales, CO2 valuation, and integration with other energy systems as factors for further cost reduction.
- The report focuses on producing clean hydrogen through thermal conversion of fossil and/or waste feedstocks with carbon capture and sequestration.
- Thermal conversion is defined as a process that uses heat to drive chemical reactions converting carbon-based feedstocks into other fuels and chemical energy carriers.
- In October, DOE announced $7 billion to launch seven Regional Clean Hydrogen Hubs across the nation to accelerate commercial-scale deployment of low-cost, clean hydrogen.
- These efforts align with the U.S. National Clean Hydrogen Strategy and Roadmap as part of an integrated all-of-government strategy for decarbonization, energy security, resilience, and economic growth.
- Achieving the 80% cost reduction goal can unlock new markets for hydrogen in steel manufacturing, clean ammonia, energy storage, and heavy-duty trucks, creating jobs and reducing greenhouse gas emissions.
