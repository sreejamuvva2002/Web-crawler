# Honda Motor Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Honda Motor Company: Our History | Honda](https://www.honda.com/history)  ·  `page_008929`

## Facts

- By 2050, the company aims to cut in half CO2 emissions from its products and business operations, including its production plants, in order to address greenhouse gas emissions that contribute to global climate change.
- Built upon a state-of-the-art electric vehicle platform, the 2024 Acura ZDX debuts as an all-electric performance SUV that delivers our commitment to precision crafted performance.
- The 2018 Honda Accord, the 10th generation of America's most popular car, earned the prestigious 2018 North American Car of the Year award and was the first model from a Japanese automaker to be made in America, beginning in November 1982 in Marysville, Ohio, with cumulative U.S. production of Accord exceeding 11 million vehicles over more than 35 years.
- In April 2017, Honda showed for the first time all three vehicles in its Clarity series – the Clarity Fuel Cell Vehicle, Clarity Electric and Clarity Plug-In Hybrid – pointing the way toward a cleaner and more eco-conscious automotive future as the world’s first vehicle with three different electrified powertrains.
- The 2016 Honda Civic is selected as the North American Car of the Year by a panel of automotive journalists, marking the second time a Civic has earned the honor.
- As part of its vision to create a more sustainable mobility future, Honda introduced the Clarity Fuel Cell to customers in California in late 2016, representing the leading edge of zero-emissions vehicle technology with a hydrogen-powered range of up to 366 miles on a single tank, refueling in three to five minutes, and emitting only water vapor.
- Honda set up shop in Silicon Valley over 15 years ago and expanded its facility in Mountain View, CA, partnering with entrepreneurial technologists to advance connectivity, telematics, safety, and autonomous driving.
- The unveiling of the powerful, strikingly beautiful Acura NSX supercar causes a sensation in the automotive world.
- Honda Transmission Mfg. of America fires up its brand-new wind turbines in Russells Point, Ohio, becoming the first major automotive manufacturing facility in the U.S. to get a substantial amount of its electricity from onsite wind turbines.
- On March 20, the 10-millionth Honda Accord built in North America rolls off the assembly line at the Marysville Auto Plant as Honda celebrates a total U.S. production of 20 million Honda and Acura automobiles.
- American Honda Motor Co., Inc. marks its 50th year of U.S. operations on June 11, growing from a first store in Los Angeles with six employees to astounding heights.
- Honda breaks new ground introducing the 2009 FCX Clarity fuel cell vehicle, featuring a new, more compact fuel cell stack and becoming the world’s first fuel cell sedan capable of comfortably carrying four occupants.
- Honda Manufacturing of Indiana begins producing the Civic Sedan, while Honda R&D Americas opens its Marine Engine Research Facility in Florida and Honda Aero begins construction on its North Carolina headquarters.
- Honda R&D Americas opens its third design studio in California for the research and styling of future Acura production models.
- The 2006 Honda Civic and 2006 Honda Ridgeline Truck sweep the North American Car and Truck of the Year awards as selected by a panel of automotive journalists in North America.
- In December, the Honda Insight drives through uncharted territory to become the first mass produced hybrid vehicle available in North America, remaining the most fuel-efficient, non-plugin vehicle in its class for the next 17 years.
- Honda launches Acura, the first luxury nameplate from a Japanese automaker, with sales of “precision crafted performance,” initially opening 60 U.S. dealerships and earning four consecutive JD Power and Associates Customer Satisfaction Index awards.
- The initial Accord model is followed by an Accord Sedan in 1979, becoming the best-selling passenger car in America with American car buyers purchasing over 12.5 million from its launch until today.
- Driven by the rallying cry of “Blue skies for our children” Honda engineers create a groundbreaking CVCC engine that makes the Honda Civic the first vehicle to meet the strict emissions standards of the new U.S. Clean Air Act without the use of a catalytic converter.
- The Honda N600 is the first automobile we ever sold in America.
- On June 11, American Honda Motor Co., Inc. is established in Los Angeles, California, as the first overseas subsidiary of Honda Motor Co., Ltd.
