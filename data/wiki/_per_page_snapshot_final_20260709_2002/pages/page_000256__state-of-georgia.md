# State of Georgia

**Type:** unknown  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-04-30

**Source:** [Georgia’s Battery-Powered Future - Georgia Trend Magazine](https://www.georgiatrend.com/2024/04/30/georgias-battery-powered-future/)  ·  `page_000256`

## Facts

- Kia’s Georgia plant was the Korean automaker’s first manufacturing operation in the U.S. and, at the time, the largest economic project in Georgia.
- Kia’s $1.2 billion facility brought thousands of jobs to Troup County, a once-struggling textile area that had one of the highest unemployment rates in the state.
- Kia’s presence made Georgia a player in auto manufacturing again, a legacy dating back to 1915 when Ford established a manufacturing plant and its Southeastern headquarters in Atlanta.
- Kia will celebrate a first this spring with a $200 million investment.
- Kia’s new three-row electric SUV, the Kia EV9, will be the first EV manufactured in Georgia.
- Georgia is emerging as an EV manufacturing hub thanks to Kia’s ongoing commitment and Hyundai’s multibillion-dollar investment.
- Imola, an Italian manufacturer of customized EVs, announced plans to open an EV manufacturing plant in Fort Valley within the next two years.
- Rivian, a California-based truck manufacturer, has put its planned $5 billion Georgia investment on hold.
- Chris Smith, chief project implementation officer and general counsel of Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America, stated that once up to full capacity, the metaplant will have the capacity to produce 300,000 vehicles a year.
- Kristi Brigman, deputy commissioner of global commerce at the Georgia Department of Economic Development, said the five largest projects in the state’s history have all been related to e-mobility.
- Since 2018, projects related to the e-mobility supply chain have created about 32,200 new jobs in Georgia and $27 billion in investment.
- The Hyundai Metaplant in Ellabell in Bryan County is the largest of these projects and is ahead of schedule.
- The project was announced in 2022 and now represents an investment of more than $7.5 billion in Georgia, counting investments by suppliers and partners.
- The investment includes a venture with Korean conglomerate LG Energy Systems to make battery packs at the site.
- The plant broke ground in October 2022.
- In February, Hyundai made it official that the first EVs will be produced in the fourth quarter of 2024, ahead of the previous announced 2025 target.
- Chris Smith noted that the start of production will not mean the facility will be complete by 2024.
- The battery joint venture will come online sometime in 2025.
- At full capacity, the metaplant has room to expand to produce 500,000 vehicles if demand dictates.
- At full employment, there will be 8,500 Hyundai Motor Group employees onsite at the plant.
- The majority of supplier jobs created by these projects are outside the Atlanta-Fulton County area.
- Hwashin, a Tier-1 supplier that makes chassis and body parts for entities such as Hyundai Motor Group, Kia and international OEMs, will open a new manufacturing facility bringing over 460 new jobs and more than $176 million in investment to Dublin and Laurens County.
- The Hwashin facility is scheduled to begin production in late 2025.
- Seoyon E-Hwa’s $96 million investment will create 740 new direct and indirect jobs in Chatham County.
- Seoyon E-Hwa’s new manufacturing facility, located at the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center, will produce automotive interior parts such as door trims, headlining and tailgate trims.
- Production at the Seoyon E-Hwa facility is expected to begin in October 2024.
- DAS Corp., which designs and makes seating systems, safety seating components and seating structures, is building a new manufacturing facility that will create 300 new jobs and generate more than $35 million in investment in Candler County.
- DAS is a key supplier for Kia Georgia and Hyundai.
- Doowon Climate Control America, Inc. will build a new $30 million manufacturing plant creating 200 new jobs in Candler County.
- Doowon Group designs and makes automotive climate control systems for EVs and traditional vehicles and will be a key supplier for Kia Georgia and Hyundai.
- The Doowon manufacturing facility will be located in the Metter-Candler County I-16 Industrial Park.
- Anovion Technologies announced a new manufacturing facility that will create more than 400 jobs and over $800 million in investment in Decatur County.
- Anovion is a supplier of synthetic graphic anode materials for lithium-ion batteries, with production expected to start in 2025.
- Woory Industrial Co., Ltd. will open a new manufacturing facility creating more than 130 new jobs and an estimated $18 million in investment in Dublin-Laurens County.
- The Woory facility is the company’s first in the U.S. and will produce electrical heaters, control units, and actuators for both EV and traditional carmakers in North America.
- Kristi Brigman stated that Georgia has a reputation as a very business-friendly environment and its logistics network (rail, airport, and port) is absolutely critical to growth.
- While Georgia offers tax incentives competitive with other states, Chris Smith said tax incentives were not as much of a deciding factor for Hyundai as geographic position, port access, and a pre-earmarked site.
- Hyundai was drawn to Georgia’s skilled workforce, available largely due to Georgia Quick Start, an internationally acclaimed workforce training program that provides customized training free-of-charge to qualified new, expanding and existing businesses.
- Georgia Quick Start is a division of the Technical College System of Georgia and has more than 50 years of experience in automotive-related advanced manufacturing.
- Quick Start works with companies including Kia, Toya Tire, Kumho Tire, Hyundai Powertech and Hyundai Motor Group, specializing in automotive transmissions.
- In February, Georgia Quick Start broke ground on the first training center in Georgia dedicated exclusively to the EV market.
- The training center is located on state property adjacent to the metaplant near Ellabell and will train employees for Hyundai as well as affiliate companies such as Mobis, Glovis and Transys.
- Georgia Quick Start has facilitated the establishment of new companies and industries in Georgia and played an important role when those companies planned expansions.
