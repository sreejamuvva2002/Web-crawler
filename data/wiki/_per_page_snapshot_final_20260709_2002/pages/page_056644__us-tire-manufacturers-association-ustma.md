# US Tire Manufacturers Association (USTMA)

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [USTMA adds board members, asks chair to remain in place | European Rubber Journal](https://www.european-rubber-journal.com/article/2070093/ustma-adds-board-members-asks-chair-to-remain-in-place)  ·  `page_056644`

## Facts

- The US Tire Manufacturers Association (USTMA) represents 13 tire manufacturers in the US with annual sales of more than $27 million.
- The USTMA operates 57 locations across 17 states.
- Yong-Sik Shin of Kumho Tire USA Inc. was elected to the USTMA board of directors.
- Pietro Berardi of Pirelli Tire North America Inc. was elected to the USTMA board of directors.
- The USTMA extended Board Chair Steve McClellan's term through October 2021.
- Steve McClellan serves as president of Americas for Goodyear.
- USTMA CEO Anne Forristall Luke stated the board's decision to ask McClellan to continue as chair speaks to confidence in his strong leadership during unpredictable times.
- Anne Forristall Luke stated that the elections of Berardi and Shin augment an exceptionally talented and strategic board to help steer the industry through a robust economic recovery and resilient future.
- Shin has been CEO of the American Business Division for South Korea's Kumho Tire Co. Inc. since September 2019.
- Shin previously served as the company's head of global sales.
- Shin leads four Kumho subsidiaries: Kumho Tire USA Inc., Kumho Tire Canada, Kumho Tire Latin America, and the Kumho Tire plant in Georgia from a sales and overall operations perspective.
- Berardi joined Pirelli in March following his work as vice president at the Alliance of Renault, Nissan and Mitsubishi.
- At the Alliance of Renault, Nissan and Mitsubishi, Berardi managed global commercial operations and strategy for the parts and services business.
- McClellan stated he is honored to continue his role as chair of USTMA's board, particularly now.
- The tire industry is a $148 billion (€125 billion) industry that helps power the US manufacturing economy.
- It is critical for the industry to maintain a strong and unified voice on reopening the American economy and on policy issues affecting nearly 100,000 women and men who work for member companies.
