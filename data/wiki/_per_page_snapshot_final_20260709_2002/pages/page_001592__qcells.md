# Qcells

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-05

**Source:** [Qcells Becomes First Polysilicon-Based Solar Manufacturer in the U.S. to Earn Low Carbon Solar Distinction - Qcells North America](https://us.qcells.com/blog/qcells-becomes-first-polysilicon-based-solar-manufacturer-in-the-u-s-to-earn-low-carbon-solar-distinction/)  ·  `page_001592`

## Facts

- Qcells is the first and only polysilicon-based solar manufacturer to achieve Electronic Product Environmental Assessment Tool (EPEAT)’s Low Carbon Solar standard.
- Qcells obtained initial EPEAT status for its solar panels last year, after years of work hitting the stringent low-carbon threshold.
- EPEAT is a globally recognized ecolabel for electronics and technologies that validates sustainable production practices to help customers select low-carbon solutions.
- Qcells achieved Low Carbon status for two products: a residential solar panel and a community, commercial and industrial (CCI) panel.
- Both the residential and CCI panels are assembled at Qcells’ Dalton, Georgia manufacturing facility.
- Initial EPEAT registration for these two panels was achieved in June 2024.
- Qcells’ EPEAT registered modules have a ~73% lower carbon footprint or lower carbon intensity than imported solar products from China.
- Compared to an average utility-scale 100 MW solar PV project, a same-size PV project using Qcells’ EPEAT registered panels would have 37% lower emissions for the same power output.
- Qcells’ EPEAT registered solar panels are 37% more sustainable than a global standard solar panel.
- Qcells outperforms standard modules on sustainability by nearly 40%.
- Kelly Weger, Senior Director of Sustainability at Qcells, stated that the company wants its solar products to be the most sustainably built products on the market and remains fully committed to manufacturing complete clean energy solutions.
- It took more than two years of collecting extensive data for Qcells’ products to meet EPEAT’s rigorous standards.
- Only 30% of solar products are expected to be able to meet the minimum criteria to achieve the EPEAT ecolabel.
- Qcells leads as the first domestic manufacturer to provide EPEAT-registered residential solar panels.
- Qcells plans to continue expanding its number of products on the EPEAT registry manufactured at its Dalton and Cartersville, Georgia manufacturing facilities.
- EPEAT registration measures social and environmental impacts of products from extraction to end of life using lifecycle-based criteria that adjust as sustainability standards evolve.
- EPEAT registered products are listed on the Global Electronic Council’s EPEAT registry for customers with ambitious sustainability goals.
- EPEAT’s online registry helps drive supply chain improvements by simplifying the procurement of sustainably manufactured low-carbon solar panels.
- Qcells is one of the world’s leading clean energy companies, recognized as a manufacturer of high-performance solar cells and modules, intelligent storage systems, and large-scale renewable energy projects.
- Qcells provides renewable electricity retail services and packages to end customers worldwide.
- The company is headquartered in Seoul, South Korea (Global Executive HQ) with international manufacturing facilities in the U.S., Malaysia, and South Korea.
- Qcells serves utility, commercial, governmental, and residential markets through a global business network spanning Europe, North America, Asia, South America, Africa, and the Middle East.
