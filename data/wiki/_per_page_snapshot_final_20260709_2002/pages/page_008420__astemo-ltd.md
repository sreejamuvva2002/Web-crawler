# Astemo, Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2025-04-01

**Source:** [Astemo - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Astemo)  ·  `page_008420`

## Facts

- Astemo is a global automotive components manufacturer focused on technologies for connected, autonomous, shared, and electric mobility.
- The company name was changed from Hitachi Astemo, Ltd. to Astemo, Ltd. on April 1, 2025.
- Astemo develops automotive technologies aligned with connectivity, autonomous systems, and electrification trends for passenger and motorcycle applications.
- Astemo's ownership structure includes major stakes from Hitachi, Ltd. (40%) and Honda Motor Co., Ltd. (40%), with JIC Capital holding the remaining 20%.
- In December 2025, Honda announced it would acquire an additional 21% of Astemo shares from Hitachi, changing the shareholding structure to 61% for Honda, 20% for JIC, and 19% for Hitachi.
- Astemo has expanded its research and development activities in electric powertrains, advanced driver-assistance systems, and in-vehicle software to support its strategic focus on electrification and autonomous mobility.
- Astemo develops components for electric and autonomous vehicles, with a focus on integration of key systems such as power control units, motors, inverters, and software-defined vehicle platforms.
- In 2023, Astemo announced a ¥15.3 billion (~US $100 million) expansion of its Berea, Kentucky plant to support its electrification business.
- As of 2025, Astemo is investing $153 million at its Berea, Kentucky manufacturing plant to expand production of electric vehicle components and create over 200 jobs.
- In July 2025, Astemo announced a £100 million investment in its Horwich manufacturing facility in Bolton, Greater Manchester.
- The project will establish a new production line dedicated to electric vehicle (EV) inverters, making the site the only facility in the United Kingdom with this production capability.
- In September 2025, CBS News and The Detroit News reported that Astemo planned to establish a regional headquarters in Wixom, Michigan, as part of a $95 million investment expected to create about 200 jobs.
- Astemo maintains business relationships with Nissan Motor Co., reflecting Hitachi's historical ties to the Nissan Group.
- Astemo was established in January 2021 through the business merger of Hitachi Automotive Systems, Keihin Corporation, Showa Corporation, and Nissin Kogyo.
- In December 2024, the Government of Cantabria awarded the Hitachi Astemo Buelna plant a €1 million grant through SODERCAN’s competitive programme for major industrial competitiveness projects.
- The funding supports a €5 million experimental development project focused on advanced braking systems, to be completed over 18 months.
