# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor in Talks with Georgia to Build EV Plant in the U.S. - World-Energy](https://www.world-energy.org/article/24762.html)  ·  `page_009297`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group is in talks with Georgia officials to build a new electric vehicle (EV) plant in the U.S. state of Georgia.
- A Kia Corp. plant is currently operating in Georgia, and Hyundai Motor Co. operates a separate facility in neighboring Alabama.
- Both the existing Kia plant in Georgia and the Hyundai plant in Alabama currently produce internal combustion engine vehicles.
- EV models such as the Ioniq 5 and EV6 are currently manufactured in Korea and exported to the United States.
- The United States is the third-largest EV market in the world, following China and Europe.
- A total of 500,000 EVs were sold in the United States in 2021.
- Hyundai stated it is excited to announce a new U.S. EV plant plan soon but has no details to share at this stage.
- Projected investment costs and expected job creation numbers for the proposed Georgia facility are not immediately known.
- Once completed, the new Georgia plant is expected to produce fully electric SUVs (IONIQ 7 and EV9) aimed at the U.S. market.
- Following a mid- to long-term U.S. investment plan announced in May 2021, Hyundai Motor Group chairman Chung Eui-sun formed a task force to select a site for a U.S. EV plant.
- During a U.S. business trip last month, experts predicted that Hyundai chairman Chung Eui-sun would finalize the plant site selection.
- Hyundai Motor Group previously announced it would invest US$300 million to build an EV production line at its Alabama plant for the EV Genesis GV70 and Santa Fe hybrid.
- Hyundai was working to announce its U.S. EV manufacturing investment later that month to coincide with President Biden's planned visit to Seoul.
