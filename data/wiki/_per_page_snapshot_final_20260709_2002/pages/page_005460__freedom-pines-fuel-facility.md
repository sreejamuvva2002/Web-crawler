# Freedom Pines Fuel Facility

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [World’s First Ethanol-To-Jet Fuel Plant Paves the Way for Commercial Production of SAF | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/cmei/fuels/articles/worlds-first-ethanol-jet-fuel-plant-paves-way-commercial-production-saf)  ·  `page_005460`

## Facts

- In January 2024, the first commercial production facility for converting ethanol into sustainable aviation fuel (SAF) opened in Soperton, Georgia.
- The facility is supported by DOE’s Bioenergy Technology Office (BETO).
- LanzaJet’s Freedom Pines Fuel Facility will produce nine million gallons of SAF and one million gallons of renewable diesel in its first year of operations.
- Dr. Mark Shmorhun, BETO Technology Manager for Systems Development and Integration, is the lead overseeing the project.
- The opening of the Freedom Pines Fuel facility is a major milestone in meeting the SAF Grand Challenge.
- The SAF Grand Challenge was launched in 2021 by DOE, the U.S. Department of Transportation, the U.S. Department of Agriculture, and industry partners.
- The SAF Grand Challenge aims to produce three billion gallons of SAFs by 2030.
- The SAF Grand Challenge aims for SAFs to meet 100 percent of U.S. aviation fuel demand by 2050.
- Aviation accounts for roughly 2% of global emissions and 11% of all transportation emissions.
- The facility uses a modular design that allows scaling if engineering assumptions are validated or challenges are resolved.
- Scaling the project dramatically reduces barriers and risks to production.
- The Freedom Pines Fuel Facility serves as a blueprint for innovative ways to produce SAF and reduce emissions.
- The plant validates engineering assumptions, cost of production, operating cost, supply chains for raw materials, and economic assumptions.
- The facility will define with improved certainty what the cost of these plants will be at much larger scales.
- The LanzaJet facility is expected to provide dozens of new quality jobs to Soperton's local economy.
- The facility is expected to provide an estimated $5 million in new annual wages and benefits.
- The facility is expected to have a total economic impact of $70 million annually.
- At peak construction, there were 300 people on-site working every day.
- More than 30 full-time employees have been added on the site just to run the plant.
- Both LanzaJet and LanzaTech, the subsidiary of LanzaTech, will be in Soperton contributing to SAF production.
- LanzaJet is training the local fire department and helping add equipment for them.
- Local businesses, including a BBQ place and gas stations, have benefited from the facility's presence.
- LanzaJet uses ethanol-based technology to scale production of SAF to reduce emissions through feedstocks.
- Globally, there are roughly 30 billion gallons of ethanol production around the world.
- In the United States, ethanol comes from corn; in Brazil, it comes from sugar cane; and in India, it comes from a variety of sources.
- Ethanol is a plentiful and good feedstock and a good basic chemical for downstream products.
- Aviation fuel is a good candidate as an ethanol feedstock.
- Dr. Shmorhun has been with BETO since 2016.
- Dr. Shmorhun has a background in specialty chemicals and biochemical industries in R&D, engineering, and business development roles.
- Dr. Shmorhun received a degree in chemical engineering.
- The LanzaJet process took more than a decade from basic bench top experimental work at Pacific Northwest National Laboratory to commercial production.
- The process was driven by chemical engineers, chemists, synthetic organic chemists, and physical chemists at national labs.
- Detailed engineering requires civil, mechanical, and chemical engineers, plus knowledge of electrical engineering, instrumentation, and controls.
- All design is done on computers using computer-aided design and 3D modeling for material and energy balance.
- Construction requires specialists in concrete, steel, piping, and cabling.
- Manufacturing facilities build vessels, valves, and instrumentation.
- Scheduling tracks every hour to sequence activities and minimize overall project cost.
- Project controls and budget management require individuals with accounting and finance backgrounds.
- Dr. Shmorhun has three decades of experience in the renewable energy field.
- Dr. Shmorhun considers being part of the SAF Grand Challenge from the start, through the MOU and crafting language, a career highlight.
