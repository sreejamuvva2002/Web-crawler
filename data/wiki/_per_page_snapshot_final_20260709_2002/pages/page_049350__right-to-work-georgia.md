# Right to Work Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Right to Work Georgia Welcomes New Business Locations - NRTWC](https://nrtwc.org/right-to-work-georgia-welcomes-new-business-locations/)  ·  `page_049350`

## Facts

- Businesses choose to invest in Right to Work Georgia due to the great economic environment that its Right to Work laws allow to shine through.
- Companies currently investing in Right to Work Georgia include Sam’s Club, Idea Nuova, and Sewon America.
- Each company is adding a new location somewhere within the state and creating new job opportunities.
- The three companies will create a total of 1,420 new jobs altogether.
- Sam’s Club is investing $142 million to add a new fulfillment center in Douglas County, Georgia.
- Sam’s Club will create 600 new jobs at its new location.
- Sam’s Club stated it is on a journey to transform its entire supply chain through strategic expansion and cutting-edge automation, with this location being a great step in that direction.
- Sam’s Club noted the investment is critical to deliver to members with speed and provide an overall great experience.
- Local officials expressed excitement that Sam’s Club has chosen Douglas County to expand its supply chain capabilities and thanked economic development partners for supporting the state’s continued growth.
- Idea Nuova is investing $19 million to add a new facility in Sylvania, Georgia.
- Idea Nuova will create 80 new jobs at its new facility.
- Officials noted that Idea Nuova and its partners in Screven County and the City of Sylvania are creating jobs and opportunities to support a healthy community.
- Advanced manufacturing and logistics companies are thriving in Georgia due to affordable costs and easy access to the logistics hub of Atlanta and port cities of Savannah and Brunswick.
- Idea Nuova will join a strong and growing industry base and workforce in Screven County.
- The company plans to create 80 jobs, invest heavily in facilities and equipment, and pay lucrative wages to provide opportunities for residents of the City of Sylvania, Screven County, and the region.
- Sewon America is investing $300 million to add a new location in Rincon, Georgia.
- Sewon America will create 740 new jobs at its new location.
- Sewon America was founded 15 years ago in west Georgia and is building another facility in east Georgia, viewing the two locations as one family.
- The company's growth is supported by the Sewon team, Governor Kemp, the Georgia Department of Economic Development, and Effingham County.
- Sewon America will continue as an excellent corporate and community partner with continued success expected in Effingham County.
- Sewon America supports Hyundai Motor Group’s operations from Effingham County, which offers a skilled workforce, infrastructure, and collaborative spirit to support the company’s growth.
- Sewon America selected Grande View industrial park as the best location for its significant investment and future growth.
- Sewon America is recognized as one of the largest automobile parts manufacturers in North America.
