# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Federal authorities detain 475 people during immigration raid in Georgia, official says | PBS News](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/nation/federal-authorities-detain-475-people-during-immigration-raid-in-georgia-official-says)  ·  `page_009935`

## Facts

- Approximately 475 people were detained during an immigration raid at a sprawling Georgia site where Hyundai manufactures electric vehicles.
- Steven Schrank is the Special Agent in Charge for Homeland Security Investigations.
- Schrank stated that the majority of the detained individuals were from South Korea.
- Schrank said the operation underscores a commitment to jobs for Georgians and Americans.
- Schrank described it as the largest single site enforcement operation in the history of Homeland Security Investigations.
- The investigation has been ongoing for several months, with authorities receiving leads from community members and former workers.
- South Korean Foreign Ministry spokesperson Lee Jaewoong described the number of detained South Koreans as large but did not provide an exact figure.
- Lee stated the detained workers were part of a network of subcontractors working for various companies on the site.
- The raid targeted one of Georgia's largest and most high-profile manufacturing sites, touted by Gov. Brian Kemp and other officials as the largest economic development project in the state's history.
- Hyundai Motor Group began manufacturing EVs at its $7.6 billion plant a year ago.
- The Hyundai plant employs about 1,200 people.
- Hyundai has partnered with LG Energy Solution to build an adjacent battery plant slated to open next year.
- LG Energy Solution stated it was closely monitoring the situation and gathering all relevant details.
- LG could not immediately confirm how many of its employees or Hyundai workers had been detained.
- LG emphasized ensuring the safety and well-being of employees and partners, pledging full cooperation with authorities.
- Hyundai's South Korean office did not immediately respond to requests for comment.
- ICE spokesman Lindsay Williams confirmed federal authorities conducted an enforcement operation at the 3,000-acre (1,214-hectare) site west of Savannah, Georgia.
- Agents were focused on the construction site for the battery plant.
- Lee announced the South Korean ministry is dispatching diplomats from its embassy in Washington and consulate in Atlanta to the site.
- The South Korean ministry plans to form an on-site response team centered on the local mission.
- Lee stated that business activities of investors and rights of nationals must not be unjustly infringed during U.S. law enforcement.
- The Department of Homeland Security said agents executed a search warrant as part of an ongoing criminal investigation into allegations of unlawful employment practices and other serious federal crimes.
- President Donald Trump's administration has undertaken sweeping ICE operations as part of a mass deportation agenda, raiding farms, construction sites, restaurants, and auto repair shops.
- The Pew Research Center cited preliminary Census Bureau data regarding U.S. labor force changes.
- The U.S. labor force lost more than 1.2 million immigrants from January through July, including both undocumented individuals and legal residents.
- Hyundai and LG's battery joint venture, HL-GA Battery Company, stated it is cooperating fully with authorities and paused construction of the battery site to assist.
- Plant spokesperson Bianca Johnson confirmed operations at Hyundai's EV manufacturing plant were not interrupted.
- The Georgia plant has served as a test ground for robots.
- Hyundai-owned Boston Dynamics announced earlier this year it would start deploying its dog-like Spot robot to inspect exterior quality at the facility's weld shop.
- Boston Dynamics also planned to deploy its humanoid robot, Atlas, at the facility in the future.
