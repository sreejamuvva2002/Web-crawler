# Daimler Truck North America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** workforce_training  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Daimler Truck North America Commits $1.3M to Support PSU | Portland State University](https://www.pdx.edu/news/daimler-truck-north-america-commits-13m-support-psu)  ·  `page_053539`

## Facts

- Daimler Truck North America made a $1.3 million philanthropic investment to support faculty and students in Portland State University’s School of Business and its Maseeh College of Engineering and Computer Science.
- The commitment will provide scholarships and assistantships for undergraduates and graduate students in business and engineering.
- The commitment will also provide support to establish two professorships, one in the PSU School of Business and one in the PSU Maseeh College of Engineering and Computer Science.
- PSU President Ann Cudd described Daimler Truck North America as a key regional employer whose industry leadership and community investments have long had a profound impact on Oregon.
- Portland State University educates the largest percentage of diverse, first-generation students among the state’s urban-serving public universities.
- More than 80% of PSU's diverse, first-generation students remain in the region after graduation to contribute to Oregon’s long-term growth and stability.
- Daimler Truck North America has a long partnership with PSU, with more than 600 alumni working at the company over the years.
- Angela Lentz, chief people officer for Daimler Truck North America, stated the philanthropic commitment is intended to help build a talent pipeline, enhance research collaborations around industry-applicable topics, and drive regional business and industry growth.
- Joseph Bull, dean of the PSU Maseeh College of Engineering and Computer Science, noted that Daimler Truck and PSU share fundamental areas of overlapping interest and expertise in engineering and business.
- The partnership will leverage these capabilities to jointly pursue research collaborations in areas such as autonomous vehicles and advanced manufacturing.
- The partnership will also pursue innovative solutions to emerging challenges.
- Cliff Allen, dean of the PSU School of Business, stated that Daimler Truck leaders have shared industry knowledge, reviewed curriculum and research findings, and mentored high-potential students for decades alongside the university to inspire tomorrow’s diverse talent.
