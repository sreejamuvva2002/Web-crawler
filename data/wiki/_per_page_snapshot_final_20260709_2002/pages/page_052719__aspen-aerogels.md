# Aspen Aerogels

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_material  ·  **Published:** 2021-10-28

**Source:** [Aspen Aerogels (ASPN) Q3 2021 Earnings Call Transcript | The Motley Fool](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2021/10/29/aspen-aerogels-aspn-q3-2021-earnings-call-transcri/)  ·  `page_052719`

## Facts

- Aspen Aerogels selected a 90-acre parcel in the Southeastern United States as the site for its second aerogel manufacturing plant (Plant 2).
- The company is working closely with state and local officials in the Southeast to finalize zoning approvals and incentive arrangements for Plant 2.
- Aspen anticipates making a joint public announcement of the Plant 2 site selection with the governor's office in the near future, subject to board approval.
- Plant 2 will be designed to provide more than twice the capacity of Aspen's existing East Providence plant.
- Phase 1 of Plant 2 is planned to become operational during the second half of 2023.
- Aspen is expediting work on Plant 2 to capitalize on accelerating demand for its PyroThin thermal barriers used in EV batteries.
- Aspen is developing carbon aerogel technology for low-cost, high-performance, silicon-rich anodes in lithium-ion batteries under its Aspen Battery Materials (ABM) division.
- Nearly $10 million was invested in 2021 in scientists, engineers, and facilities to advance ABM's technical milestones for energy density and cycle life.
- Aspen is delivering production parts for PyroThin thermal barriers to a major Asia-based automotive OEM for its first all-electric vehicle (sold in Asian, European, and North American markets) starting in Q4 2021.
- The Asian EV OEM has partnered with another large Asian EV OEM that will use the same battery modules powered by Aspen's materials.
- Aspen is also delivering production parts for PyroThin thermal barriers to a major U.S. automotive OEM during Q4 2021.
- The transition from prototype to production parts signifies thermal barrier components destined for Q4 and Q1 commercial EV launches.
- Aspen's expanded role with EV OEMs now includes broader battery performance and mechanical management solutions beyond just mitigating thermal runaway, resulting in increased content per vehicle.
- Koch Strategic Platforms made a $75 million investment in Aspen to strengthen its balance sheet and leverage the Koch network for scaling operations.
- Aspen engaged Koch Project Solutions to support Plant 2 engineering and construction, including providing onsite project leadership during the build phase.
- Aspen is considering establishing fence-line production of critical raw materials near Plant 2 with principal suppliers to shorten the supply chain and reduce costs.
- Aspen plans to establish a fabrication facility for PyroThin thermal barriers likely in Mexico, targeting mid-2022 establishment and early 2023 fully automated operations.
- Aspen's revised full-year 2021 outlook includes $6 million to $7 million in PyroThin thermal barrier revenue, up by $5 million from prior estimates due to expanded scope for EV battery thermal management and safety.
- Aspen targets doubling revenue every 24 months through the decade, driven by accelerating EV megatrend demand for its aerogel technology platform.
- The company is strategically increasing investment in technical, operational, commercial, and R&D teams to support its thermal barrier business and carbon aerogel battery materials opportunity.
- Aspen's multi-year capital expenditure projections will include funding for Plant 2, Mexican fabrication operations, and other assets required to support EV business opportunities.
