# Georgia Manufacturing Sector

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2023-11-22

**Source:** [Georgia manufacturing - Page 1 | ASSEMBLY](https://www.assemblymag.com/keywords/3345-georgia-manufacturing)  ·  `page_001662`

## Facts

- Unified Legacy plans to invest $125 million in a new manufacturing facility in Macon, Ga.
- The facility will expand domestic production capacity for the aerospace, defense and industrial sectors.
- The project will create 500 jobs over the next several years.
- GE Appliances has added a new production line at its Roper Corporation facility in LaFayette, Georgia.
- The addition is part of a multimillion-dollar investment to expand and modernize manufacturing operations at the company’s flagship cooking products plant.
- A German drug packaging and medical device manufacturer is investing $88 million to build a new assembly plant in Peachtree City, Ga.
- The manufacturer will hire more than 200 new employees.
- Investments from Hwashin, Hyundai Industrial Co. and Daechang Seat Corp. USA bring the Peach State an estimated $2.5 billion.
- Hyundai Motor Group’s Georgia supplier list has grown longer this month.
- Three additional auto parts suppliers have announced plans to establish assembly plants in the state.
- Seoyon E-HWA, a global automotive supplier, will invest $76 million to build a new assembly plant in Savannah, Ga.
- The plant will make interior and exterior auto parts.
- Roper Corp., a Haier company and subsidiary of GE Appliances, will invest $118 million to expand its assembly plant in LaFayette, Ga.
- The project is expected to create 600 jobs in Walker County.
