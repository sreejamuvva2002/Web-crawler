# TI Fluid Systems

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** thermal_management  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- TI Fluid Systems (LSE:TIFS) introduced an innovative approach for coolant fluid management on electrified vehicles.
- The new technology is an Integrated Thermal Manifold assembly (ITMa).
- ITMa provides a one-piece, light-weight, blow-molded plastic manifold to optimize complex line design.
- The ITMa replaces multiple line bundle assemblies of thermal loops for heating and cooling on next generation electric vehicles.
- Current EVs typically use multiple complex rubber, aluminum or multi-layer plastic lines that are packaged tightly together under the hood or chassis mounted.
- TI Fluid Systems evolved their Fuel Tank and Delivery Systems (FTDS) division fuel tank blow molding technology to produce a one piece integrated thermal manifold approach.
- The single piece blow molded plastic manifold provides improved flow performance for efficiency gains.
- The design offers a lower part count for sub and final vehicle assembly.
- The component features lower weight and reduced investment tooling costs.
- TI Fluid Systems' FTDS division demonstrated further integration potential for fluid storage, specifically coolant reservoir expansion tanks.
- The division also demonstrated integration potential for fluid temperature control using heat exchangers and sensors.
- Integration potential extends to fluid delivery components such as pumps.
- These integrated thermal modular assemblies are very bespoke to each OEM vehicle or application.
- The modules can be engineered for various system types of multiple thermal loops.
- Hans Dieltjens, COO, stated that the company's innovations have captured the attention of OEMs as they move from 'build-to-print' components to 'innovative collaboration' of EV sub-systems.
- TI Fluid Systems positions itself as a differentiated supplier to leverage fluid know-how on new thermal EV challenges.
- The company is successfully demonstrating the ability to pivot its fluid competencies, capabilities, and assets to provide a family of new break-through EV products with various levels of integration.
- TI Fluid Systems received their first ITMa global EV platform application release for model year 2023 introduction.
- The application release is with a leading high-volume European OEM.
- TI Fluid Systems is a leading global manufacturer of fluid storage, carrying and delivery systems primarily for the light duty automotive market.
- The company has nearly 100 years of automotive fluid systems experience.
- TI Fluid Systems operates manufacturing facilities in 107 locations across 28 countries serving all major global OEMs.
