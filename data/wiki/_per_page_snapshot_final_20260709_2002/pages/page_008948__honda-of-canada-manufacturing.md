# Honda of Canada Manufacturing

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-05-30

**Source:** [Major local investment at Honda will jolt area's EV manufacturing - Barrie News](https://www.barrietoday.com/jobs-of-the-future/major-local-investment-at-honda-will-jolt-areas-ev-manufacturing-8739945)  ·  `page_008948`

## Facts

- Honda is preparing to create four new plants over the next four years to establish a complete electric vehicle (EV) supply chain at its Alliston location.
- This expansion builds upon manufacturing first established at the site in 1986, which has developed into one of the area’s largest private-sector employers.
- Honda intends to have the capacity to build 240,000 electric vehicles every year in Alliston by 2028.
- Currently, Honda of Canada Manufacturing’s capacity is about 400,000 vehicles per year at its two existing manufacturing facilities combined.
- The multibillion-dollar investment is being described as the largest in Canada’s manufacturing history.
- Honda anticipates boosting its workforce by about 1,000 people to a total of 5,200.
- Parts manufacturers are expected to seek supply agreements with Honda, creating a ripple effect reliant upon goods, technology, and automation services and equipment from other sources.
- Simcoe County has a thriving manufacturing sector, which is buoyed by the automotive sector.
- According to the 2021 census, 29,610 people in Simcoe County (including Barrie and Orillia) worked in manufacturing, representing 10.7 percent of the area’s total workforce of 275,620 in 2021.
- About one-third (or 10,000) of those manufacturing jobs are related to the automotive sector, according to the County of Simcoe.
- Honda will build four plants in Ontario including a new Honda EV plant and a new stand-alone EV battery plant in Alliston.
- Honda’s “EV value chain” is to also include the creation of a cathode active material and precursor (CAM/pCAM) processing plant and a separator plant through two joint venture partnerships.
- Current production of combustion vehicles is expected to continue in Alliston with intentions to train the current workforce to meet the needs of EV manufacturing.
- Honda Canada spokesperson Ken Chiu explained that the company plans to up-skill associates for new technologies and processes related to roles such as IPU (Intelligent Power Unit) sub-assembly and handling the installation of the IPU in the vehicle.
- Chiu added that the transition to producing EVs will create demand for new skills, and Honda is planning training needed for BEV development and manufacturing roles to ensure associates are supported and prepared.
- Adam Kallio, manager of economic development for the County of Simcoe, expects a long-term impact on the area’s economy and job creation.
- Kallio stated that the announcement is the biggest and most impactful automotive announcement in Canadian history and will have strong positive economic and job impacts across Simcoe County for decades to come.
- Kallio noted that many of the new jobs will still be in motor vehicle and parts manufacturing, with future opportunities and trends progressing in electric vehicles across Canada and globally.
- New in-demand positions will include engineering disciplines such as electrical, chemical, industrial, and software development related to battery management, braking systems, acceleration, and entertainment systems.
- Ongoing work will involve robotics, artificial intelligence (A.I.), self-driving, and other automated functions.
- Honda Canada may expand upon its long-standing relationship with Georgian College for education and training support during electrification and future EV development.
- Lakehead University, which has a campus in Orillia, is also anticipated to be involved in potential collaborations.
- Georgian College electrical engineering professor John McCluskey described the college’s relationship with Honda as “complex,” involving many of the local school’s programs.
- McCluskey stated that the investment reflects confidence in the area’s skilled labor and access to infrastructure and education facilities.
- McCluskey said Georgian College’s immediate benefit will be being the No. 1 supplier of their workforce and educating their workforce, citing already set-up relationships, technology, and new buildings and infrastructure to accommodate growth.
- McCluskey pointed out that much of the required knowledge still needs to be developed for lithium-ion batteries, related assembly processes, battery management systems involving hardware and software, and testing.
- Georgian College regularly reviews its programs and courses to ensure they reflect community needs, with anticipation of Honda’s EV transition being part of program advisory committee discussions.
- The college will also take into consideration the needs of Honda’s suppliers when updating programs.
- McCluskey emphasized that Georgian will benefit across the board not just with Honda but also with tier one and suppliers, covering safety, compliance, logistics, operations, project management, business, and environmental aspects as Honda strives to become zero emissions.
