# Freyr Battery

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Battery firm abandons plan for a $2.6 billion plant in Georgia](https://www.bignewsnetwork.com/news/278045875/battery-firm-abandons-plan-usd-billion-plant-georgia)  ·  `page_054966`

## Facts

- Scrapped plans to build a US$2.6 billion battery factory in Newnan, Georgia.
- Opted instead to focus on solar panel manufacturing in Texas.
- Initially announced the Georgia project in 2023.
- Informed local officials last week that it would not proceed with construction.
- The facility was expected to hire over 700 workers.
- The facility was expected to manufacture batteries for storing renewable energy.
- Never broke ground on the 368-acre (149-hectare) site.
- Is a Norwegian-founded startup.
- Moved its headquarters to the U.S. to maximize tax benefits from President Joe Biden's climate law.
- Purchased a $340 million solar panel factory in Texas from China's Trina Solar last year.
- Spokesperson Amy Jaick expressed gratitude for the support and partnership found in Coweta County and throughout Georgia.
- Stated that the company is focusing at the moment on the solar module manufacturing facility in Texas.
- Decision to pivot was influenced by rising interest rates, declining battery prices, leadership changes, and a shift in company goals.
- Coweta County Development Authority President Sarah Jacobs provided the reasons for the pivot.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development previously granted $7 million to assist Freyr in securing land for the project.
- Department spokesperson Jessica Atwell stated that Georgia and Freyr are working together to ensure the grant funds are repaid quickly.
- The company may also owe additional funds to Coweta County.
- Stated that Georgia's incentives process protects the Georgia taxpayer, and when a company's plans change, that process ensures discretionary incentives are repaid.
- Initially planned to build battery factories in Norway and Finland.
- In November, announced plans to sell its European business.
- Terminated its battery technology license, paying $3 million in settlement fees.
- Former CEO Tom Einar Jensen previously warned investors that Chinese manufacturers were producing batteries at lower costs, making it difficult for startups to secure funding.
- Company's cash reserves fell from $253 million at the end of 2023 to $182 million by September 30.
- Georgia remains a significant hub for EV and battery manufacturing despite Freyr's withdrawal.
- SK Innovation invested $2.6 billion in a battery plant in Commerce, though it later furloughed some workers.
- Hyundai Motor Group has begun production at its $7.6 billion electric vehicle and battery facility near Savannah and expects to hire 8,500 workers.
- Electric truck maker Rivian revived its $5 billion factory project near Atlanta after securing a $6.6 billion federal loan.
- State officials remain confident in Georgia's position as a leader in clean energy manufacturing despite Freyr's exit.
