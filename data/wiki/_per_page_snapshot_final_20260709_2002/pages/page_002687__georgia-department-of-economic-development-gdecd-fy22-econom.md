# Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) FY22 Economic Development Record

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2022-08-10

**Source:** [Georgia Shatters Investment and Job Records in FY22 | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-releases/2022/georgia-shatters-investment-and-job-records-fy22)  ·  `page_002687`

## Facts

- During fiscal year 2022 (July 1, 2021, to June 30, 2022), Georgia achieved record economic development investments and job creation supported by GDEcD’s Global Commerce team through 358 projects.
- Investments in expansions and new locations totaled more than $21.2 billion.
- 51,132 jobs were created statewide during fiscal year 2022.
- Excluding the two largest projects in state history—Rivian and Hyundai Motor Group—Georgia jobs and investments for the year totaled 35,532 and $10.7 billion, respectively.
- There were 12 automotive projects that created a total of nearly 16,000 new jobs across the state.
- Eighty-five percent of the investments and more than 30,000 new jobs came from outside the 10-county metro Atlanta region.
- Companies choosing to expand in Georgia accounted for 74 percent of total projects.
- New locations created 33,846 jobs across the state.
- The advanced manufacturing sector added more than 10,000 new jobs in the state.
- Both the automotive and advanced manufacturing industries include manufacturers and suppliers for the electric vehicle (EV) industry.
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America’s (HMGMA) development includes EV battery manufacturing along with affiliate suppliers on-site.
- Component recyclers Aurubis and Ascend Elements have joined HMGMA as part of Georgia’s efforts to build a sustainable, reliable supply chain for its electric mobility ecosystem.
- Foreign direct investment accounted for over $8 billion in investments created in fiscal year 2022.
- The top five sources of foreign direct investment were South Korea, Germany, Japan, France, and the Netherlands.
- Korea-based projects represented the highest job creation and investment from a single country.
- Germany produced the second most jobs and investments after Korea.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson led economic development missions to the United Kingdom, South Korea, and Germany during FY22.
- Missions included meetings with economic development drivers like Hyundai Motor Group and SK On.
- The state discussed new opportunities for development with the German state of North Rhine-Westphalia and the Liverpool City Region Freeport.
- GDEcD offers 'Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development' (GRAD) certification to recognize pre-qualified industrial sites for which due diligence has been completed prior to a prospect visit.
- Through GRAD, Georgia has certified more than 60 industrial sites as ready for fast-track industrial projects.
- Georgia’s Quick Start program provides specialized training tailored specifically to business needs and has operated for 55 years.
- Access to Hartsfield-Jackson International Airport, the Port of Savannah, and a robust network of highways and rail lines supports the logistics and distribution industry.
