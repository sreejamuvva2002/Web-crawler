# Eaton Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** power_electronics  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Eaton to showcase innovative electrified commercial vehicle technologies at IAA Transportation show - Korea Newswire](https://www.koreanewswire.co.kr/newsRead.php?no=951111)  ·  `page_019736`

## Facts

- Eaton will display technologies to support the zero emissions journey in the commercial vehicle market Sept. 20-25 at the IAA Transportation show in Hannover, Germany.
- Scott Adams, president of Eaton’s eMobility business, states that Eaton provides a vast portfolio of EV technologies for full battery electric, fuel cell, and hybrid commercial vehicles, as well as EV charging infrastructure solutions.
- Eaton’s eMobility business offers a new Flex Power Distribution Unit (PDU) for high-voltage electrified commercial vehicles designed to handle multiple load requirements.
- The Flex PDU integrates Eaton’s Breaktor® circuit protection technology, which improves vehicle safety, protects components from overcurrent conditions, is resettable like a circuit breaker, and reduces cost for both OEMs and consumers.
- The Flex PDU leverages sealed and corrosion-resistant terminals and connectors from Royal Power Solutions, a global leader in power distribution and transmission solutions that Eaton acquired earlier this year.
- Eaton’s Bussmann® series fuses are integrated into the Flex PDU to meet requirements for high-powered electrified vehicles with ratings up to 1,000 volts of direct current (VDC) and 600 amps.
- The Flex PDU provides OEM diagnostic information on power consumption to maximize performance and efficiency, while its integrated design reduces component count and complexity.
- Eaton’s new 4-speed transmissions for medium- and heavy-duty electrified commercial vehicles provide superior performance on grades and acceleration, offer flexible gear ratios, improve system efficiency, and enable longer vehicle range and battery life.
- Adams notes that Eaton supplies transmissions for multiple global commercial vehicle manufacturers and continues to build on its heritage as a top supplier of commercial vehicle transmissions.
- Eaton recently introduced a 48-volt DC-DC converter to power accessories such as antilock brakes and lighting for commercial vehicles with 48-volt electrical systems.
- Eaton’s DC-DC converters are operational in ambient temperatures up to 85 degrees Celsius and boast 97% design efficiency.
- The DC-DC converter portfolio covers low- to high-voltage systems and can be tuned for particular duty cycles via advanced digital control architecture and firmware adaptation.
- In March 2021, Eaton acquired Switzerland-based Green Motion SA, a leading designer and manufacturer of electric vehicle charging hardware and related software.
- Eaton’s EV charging infrastructure expertise includes the production and operation of charging stations, designing management software for charging networks, and serving as an electric mobility service provider.
- Eaton’s “Buildings as a Grid” strategy helps fleet managers devise fast-to-implement, cybersecure, and scalable EV charging strategies that can add more capacity when needed.
- The strategy may include on-site EV charging infrastructure, on-site renewables like solar panels, and options for fleet drivers to charge at home or while traveling.
- Eaton transforms workplaces or depots into energy hubs through digital and physical infrastructure, serving as a gateway to electrifying site functionality and investing in the energy transition.
- Eaton was founded in 1911, has been listed on the NYSE for nearly a century, reported revenues of $19.6 billion in 2021, and serves customers in more than 170 countries.
