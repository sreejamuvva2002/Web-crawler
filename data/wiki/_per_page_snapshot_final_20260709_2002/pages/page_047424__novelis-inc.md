# Novelis Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2022-05-11

**Source:** [Novelis Inc to Build USD 2.5 Billion Low Carbon Aluminum Recycling and Rolling Plant](https://www.foodinfotech.com/novelis-inc-to-build-usd-2-5-billion-low-carbon-aluminum-recycling-and-rolling-plant/)  ·  `page_047424`

## Facts

- Novelis Inc. announced it will invest USD 2.5 billion to build a new low-carbon recycling and rolling plant in Bay Minette, Alabama, USA.
- The highly advanced facility will have an initial capacity of 600 kilotonnes of finished aluminum goods per year.
- The facility's capacity will support strong demand for aluminum beverage packaging and automotive solutions.
- Mr. Kumar Mangalam Birla, Chairman of the Aditya Birla Group and the Novelis Board of Directors, stated the investment marks the start of another transformational growth phase for Novelis.
- Novelis continues to invest in each of the markets it serves, including beverage can, automotive, aerospace, and specialties, across all geographies.
- More than half of the capacity of the new facility will be used to serve growing demand for aluminum beverage can sheet in North America, driven by consumer preference for more sustainable packaging.
- Steve Fisher, President and CEO of Novelis Inc., stated the investment demonstrates a commitment to grow alongside customers and meet their needs for low-carbon, highly sustainable aluminum solutions.
- Novelis is well-positioned to efficiently expand capacity at this facility in the future above the 600kt announced to capture ongoing strong demand.
- The decision to build a fully integrated, greenfield recycling and rolling plant is backed by strong North American demand for flat-rolled, low-carbon aluminum from can makers and beverage companies.
- Aluminum beverage cans, bottles, and cups are models of sustainable packaging and the circular economy.
- An average "can-to-can" lifecycle is just a couple of months, with a recycled can able to be back on store shelves in as little as 60 days.
- Ron Lewis, Ball Corporation’s chief operating officer for global beverage packaging, stated Novelis’ new plant will add much-needed domestic production of sustainable aluminum in North America while decreasing the carbon footprint of products created.
- The facility will be the first fully integrated aluminum mill built in the U.S. in 40 years.
- The plant is expected to create up to 1,000 high-paying, advanced careers in modern manufacturing.
- The facility aims to be net carbon neutral for Scope 1 and 2 emissions.
- The plant will be powered primarily by renewable energy, use recycled water, and operate as a zero-waste facility.
- The plant will rely on railroad transportation, which can reduce logistics-related carbon emissions by up to 70% compared to road transport.
- The plant will make significant use of advanced automation and digital technologies, including artificial intelligence, augmented reality, and robotics.
- With the addition of a new recycling center for beverage cans, Novelis will soon be able to recycle 90 billion cans globally, up from the 74 billion used beverage cans the company currently recycles.
- Novelis has been working to develop circular economies for aluminum through state and federal public policies, as well as partnerships with customers and other stakeholders on new approaches that encourage U.S. consumers to recycle more often.
- John Murphy, Chief Financial Officer of The Coca-Cola Company, stated the announcement of this new low-carbon recycling and rolling facility will benefit the Coca-Cola system, its customers, and consumers while reducing environmental impact.
- Site work is under way now as of the publication date.
- The company expects to begin commissioning in mid-2025.
- In addition to the beverage can market, the facility will serve the automotive market, where aluminum is the fastest growing material as automakers make plans to achieve sustainability goals.
