# Absolics

**Type:** Company  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[power-electronics|Power Electronics]]  ·  **Location:** Covington, Georgia, Georgia, United States

## Overview

Absolics, a subsidiary of SKC Co. Ltd., broke ground on a planned $600 million manufacturing site in Covington, GA to produce an advanced glass substrate material for semiconductor packaging, creating over 400 high-skill jobs and strengthening the U.S. semiconductor supply chain.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Provides advanced semiconductor substrate material that underpins power electronics for electric vehicles, enhancing U.S. EV supply-chain resilience.

- The facility will create more than 400 high-skilled jobs in Georgia.
- The glass substrate reduces the space required for multi-chip packages, allowing up to four times more chips per device.
- The material significantly increases performance and energy efficiency of chipsets.
- Energy efficiency gains can reduce power consumption by up to 50%, lowering costs and environmental impact.
- Phase 1 annual production capacity is planned at 12,000 m²; Phase 2 will ramp up to 72,000 m² per year.
