# IONNA

**Type:** Company  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[charging-infrastructure|Charging Infrastructure]]  ·  **Location:** Georgia, United States

## Overview

IONNA operates a nationwide network of electric vehicle charging stations (Rechargeries) that support multiple connector standards and a wide range of automaker apps for remote start, payment, and Plug & Charge functionality.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Provides EV charging infrastructure across Georgia and the United States, supporting all major vehicle brands and connector types.

## Timeline

- **September 30, 2026** — 20% discount for BMW and MINI drivers ends.
- **2025** — Plug & Charge becomes available for Volvo EX60 and EX90 (EX90 may require a free hardware upgrade).
- **2025** — Plug & Charge becomes available for Kia EV6.
- **2026** — Plug & Charge becomes available for Kia EV9.

## Key facts

- Every IONNA Rechargery offers both CCS and NACS connectors.
- IONNA charges all EVs, including vehicles that do not come from its founding automaker brands.
- Customers can start remote charging sessions using a wide range of apps such as ChargeHub, ChargePoint, EV Connect, Ford, Kia Access, Lexus, Mercedes Benz, MINI, My BMW, myCadillac, myChevrolet, myGMC, MyHyundai with Bluelink, My Porsche, Presto, Rivian, Stellantis Free2Move Charge, Toyota and Volvo Cars.
- Payments at IONNA stations can be made via credit card, app, Plug & Charge, Google Pay, or Apple Pay.
- Tesla models before 2021 may require an upgrade to charge at IONNA stations.
- BMW and MINI drivers who use Plug & Charge or the My BMW App receive a 20% discount on charging sessions through September 30, 2026.
- Plug & Charge is available for Volvo EX60 and EX90 (2025 models) nationwide; EX90 may need a free hardware upgrade.
- Plug & Charge is available for Kia EV6 (2025 models) and EV9 (2026 models) nationwide.
