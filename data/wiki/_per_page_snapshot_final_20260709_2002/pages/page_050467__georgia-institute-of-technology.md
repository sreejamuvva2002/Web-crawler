# Georgia Institute of Technology

**Type:** research_center  ·  **Role:** research_and_development  ·  **Published:** 2025-04-28

**Source:** [US' Georgia Tech, Stryten Energy tap lead battery innovation - Energy Storage](https://www.ess-news.com/2025/04/28/us-georgia-tech-stryten-energy-tap-lead-battery-innovation/)  ·  `page_050467`

## Facts

- Georgia Institute of Technology (Georgia Tech) and energy storage manufacturer Stryten Energy are collaborating to give new life to lead batteries, a technology with more than 160 years of history.
- A new lead battery energy storage system (BESS) was unveiled last week and is now located on Georgia Tech’s Atlanta campus.
- The BESS will serve as an experimentation site for advanced research on medium-duration energy storage solutions and applications.
- The lead BESS is designed to round out Georgia Tech’s clean energy offering alongside a previously-installed solar array and a new electric vehicle (EV) charging testbed.
- The system will enable bi-directional EV charging and load shifting of peak solar power generation.
- The system will provide backup power and support Georgia Tech’s microgrid and grid-interactive efficient buildings program.
- The project creates a real-time, real-world testbed for understanding how lead batteries perform in advanced energy applications, particularly in institutional environments.
- A renewed focus on lead batteries for stationary storage reflects the rapidly diversifying energy storage landscape, which is growing more saturated with novel and legacy chemistries alongside dominant lithium-ion batteries.
- Lead batteries offer advantages over lithium-ion devices, including a proven safety record, recyclability, and domestic availability of materials.
- At comparable deployment costs, lead is ideal for smaller, behind-the-meter applications, according to Scott Childers, vice president of essential power at Stryten Energy.
- The growing US policy focus on building secure supply chains is making lead batteries increasingly appealing.
- Stryten’s lead systems are 100% domestically built.
- Stryten has institutionalized a 98%-plus closed-loop recycling behavior for its lead systems.
- Researchers from Georgia Tech’s Carbon Neutral Energy Solutions Laboratory and the Strategic Energy Institute (SEI) will collect and analyze data on system performance, lifecycle impact, and grid interaction.
- Richard Simmons, director of research and studies at the SEI, stated the BESS will complement in-house microgrid and distributed-energy research aimed at identifying and quantifying benefits, and help optimize control strategies.
- Georgia Tech plans to integrate BESS modeling and experiments into undergraduate and graduate-level courses in energy systems and sustainability.
- Researchers have already developed a digital twin model of the battery system for students to simulate the operational and economic performance of various battery profiles.
- The digital twin will demonstrate how battery profiles interact with locally generated rooftop solar energy and real-time electricity prices.
- For Stryten Energy, the project provides an opportunity to validate its technology in a live operational setting while contributing to long-term research for next-generation batteries.
- Scott Childers stated that driving commercial adoption is a core goal, with data from the collaboration fueling further innovation in the space.
- Childers added that Georgia Tech’s third-party validation of real-world equipment use will drive confidence for businesses and investors to adopt distributed energy resources.
