# Rivian

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-03-08

**Source:** [Rivian Pauses Construction at Factory That Costs Georgia Taxpayers $1.5 Billion](https://reason.com/2024/03/08/rivian-pauses-construction-at-factory-that-costs-georgia-taxpayers-1-5-billion/)  ·  `page_060556`

## Facts

- Rivian paused construction on its Georgia factory until "later" and will now build everything in its existing Illinois factory.
- The paused Georgia factory received some of the most generous taxpayer-funded incentives in state history.
- On Thursday, Rivian unveiled three new vehicles that will be available in the coming years.
- Rivian currently offers the R1T luxury truck and R1S luxury SUV, which start at $70,000–$75,000 and can cost $100,000 or more.
- CEO R.J. Scaringe announced the R2, a smaller and more modest SUV expected to be available in 2026 with prices starting at $45,000.
- Rivian also announced the R3 and R3X crossovers, which are expected to be less expensive than the R1 series.
- Rivian went public in November 2021, promising stylish and rugged luxury electric vehicles.
- At the time of its IPO, Rivian operated only a single factory in Illinois.
- In December 2021, Rivian struck a deal to build its second factory in Georgia.
- Under the Georgia deal, Rivian committed to spending $5 billion on the factory.
- In exchange for the factory investment, Georgia state and local governments authorized up to $1.5 billion in tax credits and incentives.
- In May 2023, Bloomberg reported that Rivian had lost 93 percent of its share value.
- At that time, Rivian's market cap reflected "almost no value beyond the company's cash hoard."
- In the fourth quarter of 2023, Rivian lost $43,372 on each vehicle sold.
- This per-vehicle loss increased from $30,648 in the third quarter of 2023.
- Developing the more affordable R2 and R3 models is key to Rivian's long-term survival and aims to appeal to buyers who cannot pay over $75,000 for a luxury vehicle.
- To enable an earlier R2 launch with reduced capital requirements, Rivian plans to start production of the R2 at its existing Normal, Illinois manufacturing facility.
- Rivian stated that its Georgia plant remains an extremely important part of its strategy to scale production of the R2 and R3.
- The timing for resuming construction in Georgia is expected to be later to focus teams on the capital-efficient launch of the R2 in Normal, Illinois.
- The pivot to Illinois is expected to save Rivian $2.25 billion compared to the original forecast of launching the first line of R2 production at the Georgia site.
- In October 2023, Rivian announced that the Georgia site was "95 percent graded" and "nearly ready for construction to begin."
- Under the incentive agreement, Georgia officials paid over $32 million for "clearing and grading" the site.
- One year ago, CEO R.J. Scaringe reaffirmed the company's dedication to the Georgia project, telling The Atlanta Journal-Constitution, "We're committed to this state and this project."
- Scaringe added that "the future of our company in terms of scaling and growing really relies on the future of this project. There's not another option. We're not planning an alternative. This must work."
- The electric vehicle market is currently experiencing flux due to softening consumer demand and persistently high interest rates.
- Last month, Apple canceled its decade-long quest to develop an electric car.
- General Motors and Ford have also rolled back pledged investments in electric vehicles.
- Georgia officials committed significant taxpayer funds based on a plan that foresaw Rivian continuing on a financial path that is no longer feasible.
