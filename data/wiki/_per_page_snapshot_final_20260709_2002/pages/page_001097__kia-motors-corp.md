# Kia Motors Corp.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2006-03-13

**Source:** [Kia Picks Georgia For U.s. Plant | Kia Forum](https://www.kia-forums.com/threads/kia-picks-georgia-for-u-s-plant.32269/)  ·  `page_001097`

## Facts

- Kia Motors Corp., South Korea's second-largest automaker, announced it will set up its first U.S. plant in Georgia.
- Production at the new facility will begin in 2009.
- The $1.2 billion factory is located in West Point, near the Alabama border.
- The Georgia factory will employ 2,500 workers.
- Georgia Gov. Sonny Perdue and Kia President and CEO Euisun Chung made the formal announcement at a ceremony in Seoul.
- Locations in Mississippi and Tennessee had also contended for the plant.
- Georgia Gov. Sonny Perdue stated that Kia's decision to locate in the state "is a testament to the tools, experience and know-how Georgia will deliver to one of the automotive industry's leading innovators."
- Kia manufactures the Optima midsize sedan, the small-size Picanto, and the Sorento SUV.
- Kia is an affiliate of South Korea's largest carmaker, Hyundai Motor Co., which operates a factory in Montgomery, Alabama, 75 miles away.
- Proximity to the Hyundai site combined with a $258 million incentive package from the state helped sway Kia's decision, according to Georgia state officials.
- Georgia has been negatively impacted by Ford Motor Co. and General Motors Corp. closing their plants in the state.
- The new facility is expected to produce 300,000 vehicles per year at maximum capacity.
- Five to six auto suppliers are expected to set up nearby the Georgia plant, resulting in the creation of an additional 2,000 jobs.
- Kia exported three-fourths of its South Korean production last year and is pursuing an aggressive overseas expansion.
- Besides the U.S. plant, Kia is building a second plant in China.
- Kia expects its first European factory to start up in Slovakia at the end of the year-end.
- The company supplies materials to partners for vehicle assembly in Malaysia, Indonesia, Vietnam, Russia, and Iran.
- Kia President and CEO Euisun Chung stated: "Kia Motors has entered an aggressive growth phase in the U.S."
- Kia expects its sales in the United States and Canada to increase 15 percent to 350,000 vehicles in 2006.
- Kia expects its U.S. and Canadian sales to grow further to 800,000 by 2010.
- Kia Motors America posted record sales for February and was 3.2 percent ahead of its record-setting 2005 sales pace at the time of the report.
- In January, Kia reported that its 2005 net profit rose 2.9 percent to 680.9 billion won ($697 million) as revenue gained 4.6 percent to 16 trillion won ($16.4 billion).
- Kia sold 1.27 million vehicles worldwide in 2005, which was 13.9 percent more than the previous year.
- The company has invested more than $300 million in the United States over the last four years prior to this announcement.
- Kia's U.S. facilities include a research and development center in Ann Arbor, Michigan.
- Kia's U.S. facilities include vehicle proving grounds in Mojave, California.
- Kia operates what it calls the largest automotive design studio in California, which it shares with Hyundai Motor America.
- The company was constructing a $87 million U.S. corporate campus in Irvine, California, expected to open in December 2006, which will include a design center.
