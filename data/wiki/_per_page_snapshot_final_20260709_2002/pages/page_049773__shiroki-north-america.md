# Shiroki North America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Shiroki North America Saves Costs with Standard Part Configurator](https://partsolutions.com/automotive-manufacturer-slashes-costs-with-standard-cad-parts-configurator/)  ·  `page_049773`

## Facts

- Shiroki North America is a top-tier automotive supplier and manufacturer.
- Shiroki produces seat tracks, door frames, window regulators, and other parts for Toyota, Nissan, Subaru, Mitsubishi, as well as others.
- In addition to manufactured parts, Shiroki creates the actual fixtures and jigs required to create the components they manufacture for their clients.
- Shiroki worked with MISUMI to standardize its components and designs to streamline its design process.
- Standardizing parts and processes generated both cost and time savings for the shop floor.
- Brandon Johnson serves as the Assistant General Manager of Engineering and Maintenance for Shiroki North America.
- Once engineers design a part to precise dimensions and specifications, that part is standardized to help save time and control costs.
- MISUMI assigns a unique product number to standardized parts, which greatly facilitates re-ordering.
- Shiroki took advantage of MISUMI’s CAD configurator to generate standard CAD parts for its designs.
- Using the CAD configurator reduced the need for time- and labor-intensive fabrication of custom parts, whether in-house or by an outside supplier.
- MISUMI’s CAD support offers automatic download of native CAD files, meaning engineers do not have to spend time drawing components or pay an outside supplier to draw them.
- As a result of standardizing by using MISUMI’s products, standard parts configurator, and downloadable files, Shiroki saw direct results to its bottom line.
- MISUMI enabled Shiroki to build a sizable machine development group and actually manufacture the machines within its own company rather than undertaking the time, hassle, and expense of outsourcing.
- This initiative led Shiroki North America to achieve the largest cost savings in its history.
