# Georgia Economic Development Department

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2023-06-30

**Source:** [Racing Ahead - Atlanta Magazine](https://www.atlantamagazine.com/article/racing-ahead/)  ·  `page_010163`

## Facts

- The Georgia Economic Development Department facilitated 426 new facilities and expansions announced in the fiscal year that ended June 30, 2023.
- The facilitated projects represent more than $24 billion in investments.
- The facilitated projects are expected to create 38,400 jobs.
- The number of projects in fiscal 2023 is up 19 percent from the record 358 facilities announced in fiscal 2022.
- Total investments in fiscal 2023 are up 13 percent from the $21.2 billion announced in fiscal 2022.
- The fiscal 2023 job total is down from more than 51,100 jobs in fiscal 2022.
- In fiscal 2022, Rivian and Honda announced electric vehicle plants costing more than $10.5 billion.
- The Rivian and Honda EV plant announcements promised 15,600 jobs.
- Counties outside the Atlanta metro area received more than 80 percent of the investments and new jobs in fiscal 2023.
- Qcells announced a $2.5 billion expansion in solar module manufacturing in northwest Georgia.
- Approximately $2 billion in new facilities were announced for automotive suppliers drawn to the Savannah area.
- The automotive supplier investments near Savannah are being driven by the construction of Hyundai’s Metaplant America.
- South Korea, Finland, Norway, Japan, Switzerland, and the Netherlands were Georgia’s top foreign job creators in fiscal 2023.
- Kristi Brigman serves as the state’s deputy economic development commissioner for global commerce.
