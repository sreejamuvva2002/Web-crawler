# Enchem America Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2023-07-15

**Source:** [Enchem America locating $152 million plant, 190 new jobs in Brownsville | Business | therogersvillereview.com](https://www.therogersvillereview.com/business/article_c216176e-1aa1-11ee-8b82-1f6dc2fb9b51.html)  ·  `page_007972`

## Facts

- Enchem America Inc. officials announced earlier this month the company will invest $152.5 million to establish electrolyte manufacturing operations in Brownsville.
- The new facility is located at the I-40 Advantage Industrial Park, a Select Tennessee Certified Site.
- The project will be Enchem’s second U.S. plant.
- The project will create 190 new jobs in Tier 4, At-Risk Haywood County.
- The Brownsville facility will operate similarly to Enchem’s U.S. headquarters in Commerce, Georgia.
- The facility will manufacture electrolytes for electric vehicle (EV) batteries as well as other EV battery-related services.
- Enchem America Inc. is a subsidiary of Enchem Co., Ltd., which is based in South Korea.
- Enchem Co., Ltd. specializes in the development and manufacturing of electrolytes and other additives for rechargeable batteries.
- Jung Kang Oh, CEO and founder of Enchem Co. Ltd., stated that the growth of the EV market in recent years has been truly remarkable, and all indications suggest this trend will continue at an accelerated pace over the coming decade.
- Jung Kang Oh expressed confidence that Enchem is well positioned to further capitalize on the growth of the EV market and look forward to exploring new avenues of innovation and partnership opportunities with Haywood County and the City of Brownsville.
- With the additional Tennessee operations, Enchem will be able to further support its EV partners across the Southeast.
- TNECD Commissioner Stuart McWhorter stated that South Korea is one of Tennessee’s top 10 foreign direct investment partners, and they are proud that another global brand like Enchem has chosen to place its trust in their state.
- Stuart McWhorter noted that Enchem is not only investing in one of Tennessee’s At-Risk counties, but the company is also boosting the EV supply chain across Tennessee.
