# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-01-26

**Source:** [Boston Dynamics Atlas Enters Production: Hyundai's Georgia Factory Marks the First Real-World Deployment of Humanoid Robots in Manufacturing | Programming Helper Tech](https://www.programming-helper.com/tech/boston-dynamics-atlas-hyundai-factory-deployment-2026-humanoid-robots-manufacturing)  ·  `page_008323`

## Facts

- In January 2026, Boston Dynamics unveiled the production-ready Atlas humanoid robot at CES and deployed them at Hyundai's new manufacturing facility near Savannah, Georgia.
- This deployment marks the first time humanoid robots have moved from research laboratories into real-world manufacturing environments working alongside human employees on actual production tasks.
- The production Atlas is an electric humanoid robot standing 1.9 meters (6.2 feet) tall and weighing 90 kilograms (198 pounds), with 56 degrees of freedom enabling human-like dexterity.
- It can lift up to 50 kilograms (110 pounds) instantaneously or 30 kilograms (66 pounds) sustained, has a 2.3-meter reach, and operates autonomously in temperatures ranging from -20°C to 40°C.
- The robot features autonomous battery swapping and can navigate to charging stations independently, enabling continuous operation without human intervention.
- Hyundai plans to begin deploying Atlas robots at its U.S. manufacturing facility in 2028, starting with parts sequencing tasks and expanding to component assembly by 2030.
- Hyundai aims to build manufacturing capacity for 30,000 Atlas units annually by 2028, representing one of the largest humanoid robot production commitments in history.
- Boston Dynamics first unveiled Atlas in 2013 as part of a DARPA robotics challenge.
- The production Atlas unveiled in January 2026 replaces the previous hydraulic system with an electric design to improve efficiency and reduce maintenance requirements.
- The robot's AI capabilities have been enhanced to enable learned skills to be deployed across entire Atlas fleets.
- According to technical specifications, the production Atlas features tactile sensors in its three-fingered grippers for sensing pressure and texture.
- The robot has a 360° camera view providing comprehensive situational awareness for navigating factory environments.
- The robot can operate in three modes: fully autonomous, via teleoperator control, or with tablet steering interface.
- Hyundai Motor Group acquired an 80% stake in Boston Dynamics in 2021, making the robotics company a subsidiary.
- The deployment at Hyundai's Georgia facility is officially called the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA).
- The facility is Hyundai's newest and most advanced manufacturing plant, designed from the ground up to incorporate advanced automation and robotics.
- According to Reuters reporting, Hyundai plans to expand Atlas deployment across its entire global manufacturing network as the technology proves successful.
- Field testing at the Georgia facility began in January 2026, with robots performing actual manufacturing tasks.
- According to CBS 60 Minutes coverage, the robots were demonstrated sorting roof racks for the assembly line autonomously.
- Initial tasks include parts sequencing and sorting, which are repetitive and well-defined.
- Hyundai's commitment to manufacturing 30,000 Atlas units annually by 2028 represents an unprecedented scale for humanoid robot production.
- Tesla's Optimus robot has deployed over 1,000 units in Tesla's own factories as a direct competitor.
- Industry analysis indicates Atlas demonstrates superior adaptability for complex tasks with Large Behavior Models (LBM), while Optimus leads in cost optimization targeting a price below $20,000.
- The production Atlas incorporates a 4-hour battery life with autonomous swapping to enable continuous operation.
- Hyundai's deployment strategy follows a phased approach beginning in 2028 focusing on parts sequencing tasks.
- By 2030, Hyundai expects Atlas to move into component assembly tasks requiring coordination between multiple parts.
- Hyundai positions the deployment as "human-centered automation," emphasizing robots as tools that reduce physical strain rather than replace human workers.
- The company acknowledges personnel will still be needed to maintain, train, and work alongside the robots.
- Boston Dynamics is partnering with Google DeepMind to enhance Atlas's AI capabilities using machine learning and robotics expertise.
- Hyundai's phased deployment strategy suggests a careful economic validation process to validate ROI at each phase before committing to broader deployment.
- The company plans to expand Atlas deployment across its entire global manufacturing network following a similar phased approach.
