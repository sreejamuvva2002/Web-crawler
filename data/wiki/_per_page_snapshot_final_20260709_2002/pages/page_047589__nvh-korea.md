# NVH Korea

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [GA: EV Parts Supplier NVH Korea to Create Over 160 Jobs, Invest $72 Million in Henry County | Trade and Industry Development](https://www.tradeandindustrydev.com/region/georgia/news/ga-ev-parts-supplier-nvh-korea-create-over-160-31924)  ·  `page_047589`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that automotive parts manufacturer NVH Korea will establish a new facility in Locust Grove during an economic development trip to the Paris Air Show this week.
- The project will create over 160 new jobs.
- The project will bring $72 million in investment to Henry County.
- This is the second economic development project Governor Kemp has announced this week alone.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated that Georgia's reliable infrastructure network and great workforce allow job creators to find success in every community in the state.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp highlighted the incredible growth of Georgia's e-mobility and battery industries as a testament to supporting large suppliers like NVH Korea, calling them the latest partner in making Georgia the epicenter of the industrial revolution.
- NVH Korea is an automotive parts manufacturer based in the City of Ulsan and was established in 1984.
- The company specializes in automotive noise, vibration, and heat-control parts, which it currently produces in Korea, Europe, and India.
- The company operates a manufacturing facility that produces floor mats, cargo mats, and cargo trays in Georgia.
- The new facility will supply battery parts for electric vehicles (EVs) in the United States for manufacturers such as Hyundai Mobis, Kia Georgia, and Hyundai Motor Group.
- Ja Kyum Koo, Chairman and CEO of NVH Korea, stated that Georgia was chosen as the company’s first EV battery component manufacturing site and that cooperation between state agencies and company staff helped guide the project.
- Ja Kyum Koo noted this is the first step in contributing to the electrification of the mobility industry and expressed excitement about joining the Locust Grove and Henry County community.
- NVH Korea’s new facility will be located at Gardner Logistics Park on Colvin Drive in Locust Grove.
- The facility will produce EV battery system components that protect, connect, and sense the performance of battery cells.
- Operations at the new facility are expected to begin in the second quarter of 2024.
- Mayor Robert Price of the City of Locust Grove stated that NVH’s investment will increase the manufacturing jobs base and integrate the company into Georgia's electric vehicle ecosystem.
- Carlotta Harrell, Chair of the Henry County Board of Commissioners, stated the announcement will add quality manufacturing jobs for citizens and continue to diversify the local economy.
- Project Manager Jacob Lee represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project in partnership with the Henry County Development Authority, Georgia EMC, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that an economic transformation is happening across Georgia as manufacturers create future jobs aligned with cutting-edge technologies and industry trends.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson welcomed innovators establishing their first U.S. or e-mobility manufacturing presence in Georgia, noting they enrich the local industry mix, provide well-paying jobs, and build on the statewide mobility ecosystem.
- For over a century, Georgia has fostered healthy industry practices, encouraged collaboration and innovation, and positioned itself as a leader in developing emerging technologies for the automotive and mobility industry.
- From July 1 through December 31, 2022, the automotive industry was the second-largest source of new jobs and investment in Georgia.
- Since 2020, Georgia's pursuit of job creation along the entire electric vehicle supply chain has resulted in more than $22.7 billion in investments and the creation of over 28,400 jobs.
