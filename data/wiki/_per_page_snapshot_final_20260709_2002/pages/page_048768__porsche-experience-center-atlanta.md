# Porsche Experience Center Atlanta

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2023-03-24

**Source:** [Porsche Enthusiasts Reving Up For A Second Track Coming To The Porsche Experience Center In Atlanta, Georgia - Car Collectors Club](https://carcollectorsclub.com/porsche-enthusiasts-look-forward-to-the-new-second-track-coming-to-the-porsche-experience-center-in-atlanta-georgia/)  ·  `page_048768`

## Facts

- The Porsche Experience Center in Atlanta, Georgia, has been hosting customers and fans since June 2015.
- The facility is undergoing significant expansion, including the addition of a second track.
- Opening April 1st, 2023, the center includes a professional 1.3-mile driving track, a Porsche museum, restaurants, retail shops, a heritage gallery, and professional driving courses.
- Construction has begun on a second track that effectively doubles the length of the existing driver development course.
- The new section can be driven on its own or in combination with the existing track.
- The project is the cornerstone of a multi-million dollar investment in further developing the headquarters campus of Porsche Cars North America, Inc. (PCNA).
- The expansion is part of a 33-acre site development next to the current headquarters’ 27-acre site, which was first inaugurated in 2015.
- Construction will be ongoing through 2023 and include a new Porsche Classic Factory Restoration facility, a parking deck, and the already-opened Porsche Service Center South Atlanta.
- Kjell Gruner, President and CEO of PCNA, stated that the physical connection a driver enjoys with sports cars is core to the Porsche brand.
- Gruner noted that Experience Centers in Atlanta and LA serve as destinations for anyone to experience Porsche sports cars and learn about the brand.
- The Atlanta center has hosted more than 6,000 visitors a month in normal times.
- The new track will feature a 1.3-mile handling circuit with elements inspired by the Corkscrew at Laguna Seca, with a rise of 30 feet and drop of 25 feet through approaching and descending turns.
- The track will include the Carousel from Germany’s Nurburgring-Nordschleife and the twisty Tail of the Dragon in the Smoky Mountains.
- Three new driving modules will be added: a low-friction, wetted 60-meter asphalt circle; an ice-hill with a wet and slick surface along with an incline and decline to demonstrate traction control; and a dynamic area comprising 135 feet by 555 feet of asphalt to demonstrate handling capabilities.
- The expansion will operate independently from the existing South track, but the two layouts can be combined for an overall main handling circuit of 2.3 miles.
- Up to 2.9 miles of track surface can be utilized when all layouts are combined.
- The track expansion is expected to be ready for operation in the first quarter of 2023.
- Porsche Experience Center Atlanta is located at One Porsche Drive Atlanta, GA 30354.
