# Seoyon E-Hwa

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-05-04

**Source:** [Tesla Partners with Seoyon E-Hwa for Cybertruck Parts Supply: Report | TeslaNorth.com](https://teslanorth.com/2023/05/04/tesla-seoyon-ehwa-cybertruck/)  ·  `page_059601`

## Facts

- Seoyon E-Hwa is listed on the Korea Composite Stock Price Index (KOSPI).
- Secured a lucrative contract from Tesla for Cybertruck parts supply.
- Order value expected to be around 300 billion South Korean won ($227 million USD).
- Contract involves the supply of A, B, and C pillar parts for Tesla’s upcoming Cybertruck.
- Report attributed to Maekyung (via @Tslachan).
- Seoyon E-Hwa inked a three-year deal to supply these integral structural components.
- Pillars connect the body and roof of a vehicle and are named A, B, and C depending on their positioning from front to back.
- Company secured orders for all three types of pillars, with the parts contract extending until 2028.
- Rented a factory in Monterrey, Mexico with a 13 billion won budget ($9.8 million USD) to manufacture Cybertruck pillar components.
- Set to send workers to Mexico next month to start production of the parts.
- Primarily a vendor for Hyundai Motor Company and specializes in interior and exterior automobile materials.
- Generates over 90% of its sales from Hyundai Motor and Kia.
- Recorded consolidated sales of KRW 2,845.3 billion in the first quarter.
- Recorded an operating profit of KRW 150.6 billion in the first quarter.
- First quarter consolidated sales marked a year-on-year increase of 30.5%.
- First quarter operating profit marked a year-on-year increase of 128.4%.
- Contract follows Seoyon E-Hwa’s earlier expansion into the American market.
- Established a corporation in Alabama in 2005.
- Established a corporation in Georgia in 2010.
- Invested 37.9 billion Won to expand a new plant in Savannah, Georgia.
- Savannah plant expansion aims to supply parts to Hyundai Motor Group’s exclusive EV factories and broaden its North American business.
- Anticipated to invest up to 100 million dollars in the future to further penetrate the US market.
- Seeking additional orders following the Tesla Cybertruck pillar parts contract.
- Inclusion of floor parts is expected to significantly boost sales despite the relatively low unit price of the filler supply.
- A company official stated, “It is still in progress internally,” and added that a confidentiality agreement prevents sharing further details about the order.
