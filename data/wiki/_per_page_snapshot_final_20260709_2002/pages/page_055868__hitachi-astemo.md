# Hitachi Astemo

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-08-21

**Source:** [Hitachi Astemo Formed to Be Stronger Supplier of Both ICE and BEV Businesses | WardsAuto](https://www.wardsauto.com/news/archive-wards-hitachi-astemo-formed-to-be-stronger-supplier-of-both-ice-and-bev-businesses/797307/)  ·  `page_055868`

## Facts

- Hitachi Astemo was created in 2021 from the merger of Hitachi Automotive Systems with three other Japan-based suppliers: Nissin Kogyo, Keihin, and Showa.
- The newly formed company focuses on safety, electrification, comfort, and sustainability.
- The strategy combines the manufacturing and technical expertise of Nissin and Chassis Brakes in brake systems, Keihin’s powertrain business, and Showa’s suspension and steering business with the core businesses of Hitachi Automotive Systems.
- Tim Clark serves as CEO and president of Hitachi Astemo Americas.
- The "Astemo" portion of the company name stands for Advanced Sustainable Technologies for Mobility.
- Plans include expanding capabilities in powertrain technology, chassis systems, and advanced driver-assistance features.
- The merger reshapes traditional Japanese supplier family groupings, as founding companies come from the Honda family while Hitachi was previously part of the Nissan family.
- 40% of Hitachi Astemo’s business is with Honda.
- The remaining business comes from Nissan, General Motors, and Ford.
- As part of Hitachi Ltd., the company possesses technical depth, software capabilities, advanced manufacturing skills, and global scale.
- The goal is to expand business with other carmakers, with Honda's percentage expected to decline as new business is acquired.
- Annual sales are expected to increase to $14.8 billion in fiscal 2025 with an EBITDA margin of 15%.
- Revenue from the electrification business is expected to reach $3 billion in fiscal 2025 and more than double current levels by 2030.
- The company produces inverters, which convert electricity derived from batteries to alternating current that drives electric motors, as well as electric motors themselves.
- Hitachi Astemo has considerable experience building components necessary for electric powertrains amid an industry debate over whether automakers should make or buy these parts.
- Growth plans will draw on the R&D of Hitachi Ltd. in electrification, digital systems, and software.
- Hitachi Ltd.’s digital expertise aligns with auto industry efforts to deliver over-the-air updates to vehicles.
- John Nunneley serves as senior vice president of Hitachi Astemo Americas.
- Nunneley states the company will not give up on internal combustion engines, anticipating a long life for them but less technical development.
- Current targets include the U.S. reaching a 67% BEV penetration of new light-vehicle sales by 2032 and zeroing out new ICE vehicle sales by 2040.
- A significant number of ICE vehicles will remain on the road after 2040, requiring replacement parts.
- Over the next decade-plus, hybrid ICE and EV systems are expected to increase, providing another source of business.
- The company can supply finished modules and complete systems, which are in demand from many large and small OEMs.
- Hitachi Astemo is a significant supplier to the motorcycle sector due to its long association with Honda.
- The company has substantial business in hydraulic suspensions, foundation brakes, calipers, and throttle bodies.
- The company recently expanded its U.S. manufacturing capacity with a $100 million, 752,000-ft. (69,863-sq.-m) expansion of its Berea, KY plant.
- This expansion is designed to specifically support the growth of BEV-related business.
- To increase brand exposure, the company bought into a sponsorship in the IndyCar series with Team Penske and in motorcycle racing.
