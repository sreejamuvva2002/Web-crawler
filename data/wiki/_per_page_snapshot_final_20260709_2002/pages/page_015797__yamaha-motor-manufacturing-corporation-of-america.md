# Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation of America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation (YMMC) is based in Newnan, Georgia.
- The company sponsored a recent Environmental, Health and Safety Day to highlight the importance of workplace safety and environmental impact for its team.
- Employees and contractors at the facility received T-shirts with the slogan “Stop, Think, Act Revs Our Safety Culture” to remind them of the focus on preventing workplace injury and identifying hazards ahead of time.
- Approximately 2,500 people work at the Newnan location.
- Jason Broshear serves as the division manager of corporate planning and sustainability.
- The company empowers frontline workers to identify safety hazards and develops solutions collectively with management to prevent injuries.
- The “Stop, Think, Act” process instructs workers to stop if they see a hazard, think about how to fix it, and act by taking steps to correct it.
- Daily operational focuses include increasing safety, health and environmental awareness; proactively preventing injuries; employee engagement and management support; prevention of illness; reduction of air emissions; reducing energy and water use; and lowering landfill waste.
- The Newnan facility’s recycling program has a sizable ongoing impact by funding local environmental education, community programs, local charities, and college scholarships.
- For an EHS Day appreciation event, the company used SCRIP from nearby Huddleston Elementary School in Peachtree City to purchase 2,700 gift cards to local restaurants for its workforce.
- This initiative provided Huddleston Elementary School with a fundraising windfall of $5,000.
- Gift cards and T-shirts were funded by proceeds from employee recycling efforts involving cardboard, metal, plastics, and other materials.
- Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation of America designs and assembles WaveRunners, ATVs, Side by Sides, and Golf Cars at the Newnan plant.
- The facility is one of Coweta County’s largest private employers.
- The company is currently expanding its team by hiring another 100 contract employees.
- Most jobs with Yamaha start at $18 per hour and are eligible to become full-time hires in 30 days.
