# Kia Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** workforce_training  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Kia commits $1M to UGA’s initiative on e-mobility - College of Engineering](https://engineering.uga.edu/kia-commits-1m-to-ugas-initiative-on-e-mobility/)  ·  `page_010016`

## Facts

- Kia donated $1 million to the University of Georgia College of Engineering and the Georgia Network for Electric Mobility.
- The donation will provide endowed support for undergraduate scholarships, graduate research fellowships, community engagement programs, and experiential learning opportunities such as co-ops, internships, and capstone projects related to e-mobility.
- Kia's gift also supports the Georgia Network for Electric Mobility (GNEM), which was established by UGA in early 2023.
- GNEM brings together industry, academia, government, and communities to aid workforce development, economic development, and research across the state.
- The network takes special care to involve rural and underserved communities and small businesses in its initiatives.
- As a founding partner of GNEM, Kia is providing support for educational pursuits, research productivity, and experiential learning efforts in perpetuity.
- In 2009, Kia established a state-of-the-art production facility in West Point, Georgia.
- Since opening in 2009, Kia Georgia has helped create more than 14,000 jobs at the West Point facility.
- Since opening in 2009, Kia Georgia has produced more than 4.2 million vehicles at its West Point facility.
- One of the vehicles soon to be produced at the West Point facility will be Kia’s all-electric vehicle, the EV9.
- UGA President Jere W. Morehead stated that the university is thrilled to partner with Kia on the electric mobility initiative and grateful for support advancing the technology and strengthening Georgia's economy.
- Kia North America President and CEO Sean Yoon said Kia is committed to future e-mobility initiatives promoting sustainable transportation solutions in Georgia and across the nation.
- Kia Georgia President and CEO Stuart Countess noted that programs like GNEM are timely and critical for establishing a cohesive strategy for development and implementation of new initiatives within Georgia as Kia moves into a new era of electric vehicles.
- College of Engineering Dean Donald Leo described Kia's support as a testament to the power of collaboration between industry and academia.
- Kia was a premier sponsor of UGA’s third annual Electric Mobility Summit, which took place April 23-24.
- The summit focused on the adoption of electric mobility in the state, educational programs to support industry growth, and the future of electric mobility in Georgia and beyond.
