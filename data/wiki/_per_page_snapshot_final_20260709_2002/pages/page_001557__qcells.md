# Qcells

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2023-10-18

**Source:** [Solar panel company opens massive $171M plant in Georgia | FOX 5 Atlanta](https://www.fox5atlanta.com/news/qcells-solar-panel-company-plant-dalton-georgia-opening)  ·  `page_001557`

## Facts

- Qcells, a unit of South Korea’s Hanwha Group, has begun production at a new solar panel factory in Dalton, Georgia.
- The facility is a two-factory complex located in northwest Georgia.
- The plant can produce enough solar panels to generate 5.1 gigawatts of power yearly.
- This output represents almost 40% of U.S. solar panel capacity, according to the Solar Energy Industries Association.
- Qcells opened its first factory in 2019.
- The company has been building an even larger plant in phases since opening its first facility.
- The company describes this project as the largest solar investment in American history.
- Scott Moskowitz serves as Qcells head of market strategy and public affairs.
- The new Dalton plant is the first solar module factory in the U.S. to begin production since President Joe Biden’s signature climate legislation passed.
- Qcells made a $208 million investment in the Dalton facility.
- Federal incentives are spurring a nationwide boom in renewable energy and electric vehicles.
- Industry leaders warn that surging Asian imports could dampen efforts to manufacture more solar components in the United States.
- The company’s new plant showcases more efficient equipment and processes as part of a larger investment to bring key steps in solar manufacturing to the U.S.
- A solar panel or module is assembled from solar cells, which are most commonly made from wafers cut from ingots of polysilicon.
- Qcells currently imports its solar cells from Asia.
- Thirty miles south of Dalton in Cartersville, Qcells is building a $2.3 billion complex.
- The Cartersville complex will take polysilicon refined in Washington state to make ingots, wafers, and solar cells.
- The Cartersville plant will also produce 3.3 gigawatts of solar modules.
- The Cartersville plant is scheduled to open in phases starting next year.
- Currently, no silicon ingots or wafers are made in the U.S.
- Biden’s Inflation Reduction Act offers an extra tax credit on American-made solar equipment.
- The IRA allows manufacturers to earn incentives for every unit of polysilicon refined and every wafer, cell, and module produced.
- Qcells earns a tax credit of 7 cents per watt for every panel made in Dalton.
- This equals $34.30 for every 490-watt residential panel made.
- Solar industry leaders warn that factories will struggle to compete with a new spike in cheap Asian imports.
- Industry leaders are urging federal officials to investigate whether solar panels are being dumped at unfairly low prices.
- Previous investigations led to anti-dumping tariffs on panels made in China and Taiwan.
- Mike Carr is executive director of the Solar Energy Manufacturers for America Coalition, a group that includes Qcells.
- Carr argues Chinese component makers are pushing out cheap modules from southeast Asian factories to tank panel prices and ensure Chinese dominance while smothering U.S. manufacturing.
- Former President Donald Trump is hostile to renewable energy, adding political risk to the industry's dependence on federal policy.
- Vice President Kamala Harris visited the Dalton plant earlier in 2023.
- Republican Gov. Brian Kemp has wrestled with Democratic U.S. Sen. Jon Ossoff over political credit for electric vehicle and renewable energy investments flowing to Georgia.
- Kemp stated that Qcells chose to operate and expand in Georgia because of the state's unrivaled assets and a competitive package put together by the state.
- Ossoff and fellow Georgia Democratic Sen. Raphael Warnock note they authored the component incentives that benefit Qcells.
- Ossoff stated that Georgia is emerging as the advanced energy capital of the nation thanks to federal infrastructure and manufacturing policies benefiting the state more than any other.
- Qcells praises both state and federal assistance for building these markets and making them work through a whole government approach.
