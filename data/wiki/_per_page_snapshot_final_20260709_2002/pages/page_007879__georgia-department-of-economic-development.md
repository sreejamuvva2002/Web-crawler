# Georgia Department of Economic Development

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Georgia Department of Economic Development - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georgia_Department_of_Economic_Development)  ·  `page_007879`

## Facts

- The Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) is a department of the state of Georgia responsible for managing resources to attract new business investments, expand existing industries and businesses, locate new markets for Georgian products, and promote and fund entertainment projects produced in the state.
- The agency was originally named the Georgia Department of Commerce and was established by law in 1949.
- Governor Herman Talmadge appointed the first five-member board under the Official Code of Georgia Annotated sections 50-7-1 through 50-7-41.
- George C. Gaines served as the first commissioner.
- Pat Wilson was appointed Commissioner of GDEcD by Governor Nathan Deal in November 2016.
- As Commissioner, Pat Wilson leads the state agency responsible for creating jobs and investment in Georgia through business recruitment and expansion, international trade and tourism, as well as the arts, film and music industries.
- Christopher M. Carr served as Commissioner from November 2013 until his appointment as state Attorney General in November 2016.
- GDEcD's key resources include encouraging the expansion of existing industry and small business, developing new domestic and international markets, recruiting new business to locate in the state, identifying key markets for companies' specific needs, attracting tourists, promoting entertainment projects, and planning/mobilizing state resources for economic development.
- The department is divided into seven distinct divisions: Global Commerce, International Trade, Workforce, Georgia Centers of Innovation, Tourism, Film/Music & Digital Entertainment, and the Georgia Council for the Arts.
- These seven divisions are supported by an Administration division and a Marketing and Communications division.
- The Global Commerce Division assists businesses with growing or locating in the state by coordinating site location, employee training, market research, and export assistance and consultation.
- In FY2012, assistance from the Global Commerce division led to 403 announced projects and nearly $6B in investment.
- GDEcD supports small businesses, which make up 97% of all Georgia businesses, through direct company relationships, resource awareness, B-2-B opportunities, and local-level business development.
- The International Trade Division works with Georgia companies seeking global markets and international firms looking to locate in the U.S. or secure American business partners.
- GDEcD has international representation in 11 markets including Brazil, Canada, Chile, China, Colombia, Germany, Japan, Korea, Mexico, Israel, and the United Kingdom.
- The Centers of Innovation (COI) is a program designed to accelerate the growth of technology-based companies.
- The COI program is composed of six centers: agribusiness, aerospace, energy, life sciences, logistics, and manufacturing.
- The COI centers are located in Atlanta, Augusta, Gainesville, Savannah, Tifton, and Eastman.
- Tangible services for the COI program include access to university-level research and development, product commercialization, industry-specific business counsel, matching research grants for qualified companies, networking opportunities, and connecting clients to potential investor networks.
- Georgia Allies was formed in 1997 as a partnership between state government and private corporations.
- On May 12, 2008, Governor Sonny Perdue signed into law the Georgia Entertainment Industry Investment Act, increasing the state tax credit for qualified production and post-production expenditures by as much as 30%.
- The tax credit under the Georgia Entertainment Industry Investment Act is available to both traditional motion picture projects and new industries such as game development and animation.
