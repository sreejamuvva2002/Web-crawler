# Aurubis

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2023-05-09

**Source:** [Aurubis Doubles its Smelter Capacity in Georgia - ITAD Daily](https://itaddaily.com/2023/05/09/aurubis-doubles-its-smelter-capacity-in-georgia/)  ·  `page_017305`

## Facts

- Aurubis has allocated an extra $276 million to double the capacities of its secondary smelting facility in Georgia.
- The German-based metals firm announced its decision to invest 640 million euros ($707 million) to construct the smelter in Augusta, Ga.
- With a planned upgrade resulting in an additional recycling module, this site will have an estimated capacity of 180,000 tons per annum.
- When construction began in mid-2022, it was projected that 300 million euros ($331 million) would be required to complete the project.
- The project was originally designed to process up to 90,000 tons per year.
- After deliberation in December, Aurubis concluded that a major capital investment was necessary for its new plant.
- The new plant will recycle printed circuit boards, cables, and other scrap materials.
- On March 27th, Aurubis formally announced it had established an agreement with German equipment manufacturer SMS Group to install a second recycling module.
- Aurubis CEO Roland Harings stated that the expansion delivers on the company's "Driving Sustainable Growth" strategy.
- The strategy aims to keep strategically important and high-demand mineral raw materials in the resource cycle.
- SMS Group’s technology is being installed as part of Aurubis’ new plant in America.
- The equipment utilizes a top-blown rotary converter (TBRC) to extract raw materials such as copper, nickel, tin, and platinum group metals from complex recycling materials.
- These extracted resources are recovered in downstream processes.
- The recovered resources are essential for future technologies, renewable energy transition, sustainable transportation options, and digitalization.
- Aurubis CEO Roland Harings stated that bundling SMS Group’s plant know-how with Aurubis’ expertise creates an environment for highly efficient metal recovery.
- Harings noted the partnership allows them to take advantage of the enormous potential of the US recycling market.
- He added that backing from a strong partner like SMS Group puts them in an excellent position to realize the project professionally and on time.
- SMS Executive Board member Michael Rzepczyk stated that their plants are helping Aurubis assume a leading role in multimetal recycling in the US.
- Rzepczyk noted that doubling production volume at the new US site takes recycling to the next level in the US.
- He added that together they are building a more sustainable future by closing value chains and maximizing recovery of valuable metals from recycling materials.
- Aurubis released a quarterly report revealing its decision to increase spending to fund the added recycling system, update existing infrastructure, and cover inflation costs.
- The company is injecting an additional 250 million euros ($276 million) into the secondary processing system.
- The company is also injecting an extra 90 million euros ($99 million) for inflationary and cost adjustments.
- Operations at the Augusta plant will commence in 2026.
- Company projections suggest that starting in the 2026-27 fiscal year, a total of 170 million euros ($188 million) will be earned before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization (EBITDA).
- In June of 2022, Aurubis’ subsidiary, Aurubis Richmond, received necessary air quality permits from Georgia authorities.
- Shortly after receiving permits, construction on the project began.
- A press release from Aurubis stated that construction is currently running at full steam.
- The project faced heavy objections from Savannah Riverkeeper and other environmental groups despite securing permits and contracts.
- Savannah Riverkeeper executive director Tonya Bonitatibus stated in a press release that the air cannot stand any more contamination.
- Bonitatibus called it absurd to continue bringing highly polluting industries into the community, arguing people of Augusta do not want to suffer bad air for a few jobs.
- The environmental group's statement continued that it is impossible to comment meaningfully about the project without understanding the interconnectivity of permits and their allowable pollutant loads.
- The Georgia Department of Natural Resources released an air permit narrative document outlining specifics of the company’s technology.
- Using its European facilities, Aurubis will be able to turn blister copper into industrial and precious metals.
- A portion of these items will be sold directly back to the American market.
- Aurubis Richmond has flexibility in its recycling technology due to its modularity.
- This capability allows extra components to be added, enabling expansion to cater to further requirements.
- These features secure the firm's place as a leader in the US recycling market and provide freedom to increase capacity whenever necessary.
- Aurubis CEO Roland Harings stated that the modular system allows planning a prudent market strategy to respond flexibly to US needs.
- Harings declared that Aurubis will benefit from constant market growth in the US.
- Harings declared that economic growth and comprehensive corporate responsibility go hand in hand for the group’s investment in Georgia.
- The facility will resolve intended growth targets and adhere to America’s strictest environmental standards.
- The project will provide nearly 200 new job opportunities.
- Beginning in 2024, phase one of the plant’s construction will be completed and ready for operations.
- Two years afterwards, by 2026, module two is set to come online.
