# Adient plc

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- Adient plc is based in Plymouth, MI and is the world’s largest manufacturer of automotive seating, accounting for one-third of the global market and providing components for 25 million vehicles.
- Adient was founded as a spin-off from Johnson Controls in 2016.
- The company works with all major automakers and vehicle classes and operates some 200 assembly plants in 30 countries.
- One of Adient's factories is a 125,000-square-foot plant located in West Point, GA.
- The West Point facility assembles seats, door panels, and other interior parts for the Kia Sorento.
- Construction of the West Point plant began in 2009 when Adient was still part of Johnson Controls.
- Subassemblies for some 500 cars a day move through the Georgia facility.
- The plant completes 55 jobs an hour with a goal to increase production to 60 jobs an hour.
- Todd Emmons is an engineer in the automotive business unit for Adient and oversees operations at the West Point facility.
- The Georgia plant operates on a just-in-time basis to deliver products exactly when the nearby Kia facility is ready.
- Each workstation has only 84 inches of pick space.
- The facility uses UNEX Manufacturing Flow Cell workstations and carton flow racks for line-side assembly.
- Adient ordered 41 Flow Cells, which are assembled using UNEX instructions in less than a half an hour each.
- Reconfiguring the Flow Cell racks is three times faster than reconfiguring other types of products.
- The plant plans to add new car parts to the line for other types of vehicles soon.
- John Maicki is president of DR Storage Systems and worked with Adient engineers to identify line-side assembly technology for the Georgia facility.
- John Maicki first recommended Flow Cell racks to a customer in 2003 and has recommended them ever since.
