# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-11-09

**Source:** [Hyundai’s EV manufacturing campus draws 3 more suppliers to Georgia | Supply Chain Dive](https://www.supplychaindive.com/news/hyundai-metaplant-draws-3-more-suppliers-hwashin-hyundai-industrial-daechang-seat-corp-usa-georgia/699091/)  ·  `page_001339`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group’s Georgia supplier list expanded with three more auto part makers announcing investments to establish manufacturing facilities in the state.
- Hyundai Industrial Co., a subsidiary of Hyundai Motor Group, invested over $24 million to acquire a building in Newnan, creating 100 jobs.
- Hyundai Industrial Co. plans to retrofit its Newnan facility to produce armrests and headrests for Hyundai’s upcoming $5.5 billion EV metaplant in Ellabell as well as other OEMs.
- South Korea-based tier-1 supplier Hwashin plans to establish a $176 million plant in Dublin, creating over 460 jobs.
- Hwashin specializes in crafting chassis and body parts for Hyundai, its subsidiary Kia, and other OEMs.
- Production at Hwashin’s Dublin plant is expected to begin in late 2025.
- Daechang Seat Corp. USA announced it is investing $72.5 million to build a manufacturing facility in Savannah, creating over 500 jobs.
- Daechang Seat Corp. USA specializes in automotive seat frames and will join previously announced suppliers Seoyon E-HWA and PHA at the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center.
- The Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center is located near Hyundai’s EV metaplant, which is still under construction.
- The latest investments bring an estimated $2.5 billion from a total of 14 suppliers to Georgia.
- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp stated in a Nov. 1 news release that the growing EV network is creating over 6,000 jobs.
- In September, Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution invested an additional $2 billion toward their joint battery cell manufacturing plant.
- The total investment for the Hyundai-LG Energy Solution joint battery cell manufacturing plant is $4.3 billion.
- Production at the joint battery cell plant is slated to begin at the end of 2025.
- The joint battery cell plant will be located next to Hyundai’s manufacturing campus.
- The joint battery cell plant will have enough capacity to produce 300,000 EVs annually.
- Hyundai broke ground on its manufacturing campus over a year ago.
- Construction has recently begun on Hyundai’s production-related buildings, with foundation work nearly complete, according to the company last month.
- The nearly 3,000-acre Hyundai manufacturing megasite will use renewable resources to power its mass production.
- Hyundai aims to reach carbon neutrality by 2045, supported by the campus's use of renewable resources.
