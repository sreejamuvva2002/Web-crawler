# Aurubis AG

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Aurubis and SMS group: First multimetal recycling plant in the US](https://www.wire-tradefair.com/en/Media_News/News/Archive_Industry-News/Aurubis_and_SMS_group_First_multimetal_recycling_plant_in_the_US)  ·  `page_017368`

## Facts

- Multimetal producer and copper recycler Aurubis AG and Düsseldorf-based SMS group GmbH are partnering to accelerate the development of the recycling market for non-ferrous metals in the US.
- The two companies signed a supply contract for a second plant module for the new Aurubis Richmond recycling plant in Georgia.
- The additional equipment will increase the capacity of the metal recycling plant in Richmond from an annual 90,000 tons to 180,000 tons per year.
- The metal recycling plant in Richmond is currently under construction.
- Aurubis CEO Roland Harings stated the expansion delivers on the company’s “Driving Sustainable Growth” strategy while keeping strategically important mineral raw materials in the resource cycle.
- The SMS group recycling equipment will be installed in the second module of the new Aurubis plant in the US.
- The core technology is the top-blown rotary converter (TBRC), which processes complex recycling materials to recover copper, nickel, tin, precious metals, and platinum group metals in downstream stages.
- Recovered metals are crucial raw materials for technologies related to the energy transition, sustainable mobility, or digitalization.
- Aurubis CEO Roland Harings said bundling SMS group’s plant know-how with Aurubis’ expertise creates an environment for highly efficient metal recovery and leverages the enormous potential of the US recycling market.
- Michael Rzepczyk, a member of the SMS Executive Board, stated that doubling production volume at the new US site takes recycling to the next level in the US.
- The recycling technologies at Aurubis Richmond feature modularity, allowing additional components to be added to expand the plant for future requirements.
- Aurubis CEO Harings explained the modular system allows a prudent market strategy to respond flexibly to US market needs and benefit from constant market growth.
- For about €640 million covering both phases, Aurubis is building the first secondary smelter specializing in multimetal recycling in the US.
- Groundbreaking for the Aurubis Richmond plant took place in mid-2022.
- Construction at the site is currently running at full steam.
- Stage one of the plant is scheduled to go online in the first half of 2024.
- The TBRC from the signed contract is destined for the second stage, which is anticipated to start production in 2026.
- Aurubis processes roughly 1 million tons of recycling material annually on a global basis.
- The Aurubis Richmond recycling plant will add another 180,000 tons of annual processing capacity.
- The new plant will comply with the highest environmental standards in the US.
- The investment will create around 200 new jobs.
