# Panasonic Automotive Systems Co. of America

## Facts

- Atlanta has 13,000 technology companies employing 200,000 people.
- Tom Gebhardt, president of Panasonic Automotive Systems Co. of America, announced the company's decision to establish the Panasonic Automotive Innovation Center in Technology Square in Atlanta on the campus of the Georgia Institute of Technology.
- The center began operations at the end of May.
- The center is expected to become an incubator for next-generation automotive infotainment technologies.
- Panasonic Automotive Systems is Panasonic's second largest division.
- The company is expected to add many new jobs in metro Atlanta.
- The company aims to develop a strong partnership with the Georgia Tech Enterprise Innovation Institute and the University's Division of Professional Practice for employing co-ops.
- The company worked closely with Georgia Tech to identify research and education resources within the university to support its objectives.
- Stephen E. Cross, executive vice president for research at the Georgia Institute of Technology, stated a key goal is to involve Georgia Tech students in Panasonic's research and development program to provide real-world industrial experience and connect the company to innovative young minds.
- The Atlanta region has 120,000 students within city limits and 270,000 within the broader region.
- The area is home to 57 colleges and universities, plus seven technical colleges each year.
- Invest Atlanta's CEO Brian McGowan describes the local talent pool as a "technology feeder system of talent."
- Atlanta holds the No. 4 spot for recent college grads according to Bloomberg BusinessWeek (July 2010).
- This readily available talent source contributes to Atlanta's #4 ranking in terms of Fortune 500 headquarters, behind New York, Houston, and Dallas.
- There is a trend of manufacturing companies returning to the city.
- Advanced manufacturing is growing significantly in Atlanta's urban core, according to McGowan.
- A Brookings Institution report states that 80% of the nation's manufacturing jobs are located in and around cities.
- Metro Atlanta holds 150,000 manufacturing jobs, representing a gain of 9,000 since early 2010.
- A decade prior to the article, the region had 186,300 manufacturing jobs.
- The Atlanta region posted a 4.3% increase in manufacturing jobs over the past two years.
- U.S. cities overall saw only a 2.7% increase in manufacturing jobs during the same period, according to The Atlanta Journal Constitution.
- The Technology Association of Georgia works on the economic advancement of the state's technology industry through policy, capital, education, and giving.
- Atlanta has attracted technology giants including AT&T, IBM Corp., General Electric, Verizon Wireless, Cisco, Google, Kimberly Clark, Equifax, and UCB.
