# Stryten Energy LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-04-01

**Source:** [Stryten Energy To Scale Up U.S. Energy Storage With Massive 10 GW Manufacturing Capacity - SolarQuarter](https://solarquarter.com/2025/04/01/stryten-energy-to-scale-up-u-s-energy-storage-with-massive-10-gw-manufacturing-capacity/)  ·  `page_050479`

## Facts

- Stryten Energy LLC has announced a major plan to expand its domestic manufacturing capacity to 24 gigawatts.
- The expansion aims to boost American energy security by increasing battery production across its 11 U.S. plants.
- These facilities currently employ over 2,500 workers.
- The company's facilities manufacture batteries for applications including military, government, data centers, grid storage, automotive, and material handling.
- The expansion investment is backed by advanced manufacturing tax credits to help accelerate growth and strengthen the domestic industrial sector.
- Stryten utilizes advanced lead, lithium, and vanadium redox flow battery technologies.
- Manufacturing plants in Kansas, Georgia, Iowa, Virginia, and Arkansas will expand production.
- The plant expansions are expected to create hundreds of high-paying jobs and boost local economies.
- Stryten operates a vertically integrated supply chain to ensure a steady U.S. supply of battery components.
- Plants in Pennsylvania, New York, Indiana, Missouri, and Wisconsin produce critical materials for battery manufacturing.
- The company is investing in recycling plastic from spent batteries to support sustainability and the circular economy.
- Recycled materials are used for new batteries in automobiles, forklifts, and locomotives.
- Stryten has opened a lithium battery assembly plant in Georgia to meet military and industrial needs using its proprietary lithium module technology.
- Stryten is collaborating with Nevada-based Dragonfly Energy to develop advanced lithium batteries for the automotive aftermarket and battery distributors.
- Mike Judd, President and Chief Executive Officer of Stryten Energy, stated that the company is committed to growing domestic energy storage capacity to serve U.S. customers and partners, emphasizing that American energy security relies on domestic companies manufacturing batteries for supply chains and critical infrastructure backup power.
- Mike Judd noted that increasing gigawatt capacity across the company's footprint will support growing energy demand and achieve national energy resilience goals.
- Stryten recently launched Storion Energy LLC through its affiliate Stryten Critical E-Storage LLC in partnership with Largo Inc. and Largo Clean Energy Corp.
- Storion Energy aims to help U.S. battery manufacturers access cost-effective electrolytes for vanadium redox flow batteries (VRFB) to support long-duration energy storage (LDES) solutions.
- The expansion strengthens U.S. energy security, creates jobs, and supports sustainable battery production for the future.
