# Carcoustics International GmbH

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2019-10-09

**Source:** [Chief Operating Officer of the Georgia Department of Economic Development Bert Brantley leads High-Level Delegation to Carcoustics’ Headquarters - Carcoustics International GmbH](https://www.carcoustics.com/press/news/news-detail/chief-operating-officer-of-the-georgia-department-of-economic-development-bert-brantley-leads-high-level-delegation-to-carcoustics-headquarters)  ·  `page_018327`

## Facts

- On October 9, 2019, Bert Brantley, Chief Operating Officer of the State of Georgia Department of Economic Development, led a high-ranking delegation to Carcoustics’ headquarters in Leverkusen, Germany.
- The visit sought to recognize Carcoustics’ earlier announcement that it would make an investment in the single-digit million range and create 200 new jobs in Buford.
- The visit also aimed to engage in a productive exchange with company executives to establish a close-knit network on the ground.
- Today, 200 employees staff the facility in Buford in Gwinnett County, Georgia, with more positions expected to be filled in the near future.
- Carcoustics provides acoustic and thermodynamic solutions for vehicles.
- Experts from Carcoustics TechConsult, an in-house development center, demonstrated core competencies using various test setups during the visit.
- The delegation toured Carcoustics’ production lines where advanced manufacturing processes were showcased.
- Carcoustics is a medium-sized automotive supplier headquartered in Leverkusen, Germany, on an international course for growth.
- Since 2017, the company has opened five new locations: Nováky (Slovakia), Wroclaw (Poland), Shenyang (China), Querétaro (Mexico), and Buford, Georgia (USA).
- Carcoustics is already present in Howell, Michigan, and maintains a sales location in Troy, Michigan.
- The Buford location allows the company to better serve the Greater Southeast region.
- Lars Johannsen, Business Development Manager at Carcoustics, stated that proximity to customers in the south of the USA ensures optimum networking and avoids long transport routes.
- Production for customers such as BMW and Daimler has already started successfully.
- At the moment, the company faces challenges in the search for a suitable logistics partner and in human resources.
- Dorie Phillips, Human Resources Manager for Carcoustics Buford, stated that in the wake of the general shortage of skilled workers, they are faced with the challenge of finding qualified employees.
- Phillips noted the need to promote trade schools with the same passion as colleges to secure more talent for manufacturing positions.
