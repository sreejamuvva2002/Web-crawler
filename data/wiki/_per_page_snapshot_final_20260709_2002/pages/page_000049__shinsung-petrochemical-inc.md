# Shinsung Petrochemical, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2024-09-25

**Source:** [Automotive Supplier Invests $11.2 Million in Toombs County | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-release/automotive-supplier-invests-112-million-toombs-county)  ·  `page_000049`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced on September 25, 2024 that Shinsung Petrochemical (Shinsung) will invest $11.2 million in a new manufacturing facility in Toombs County.
- The investment will create more than 30 new jobs.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated: “Eighty-three percent of expansions and new locations from the past fiscal year are on their way to rural communities, delivering more than 18,300 new jobs and $12.5 billion in investment to hardworking Georgians.”
- Established in 1974, Shinsung specializes in products like automotive sealant.
- The company will be a key supplier for Hyundai Motor Group and the Metaplant in Bryan County.
- Chan Woo Park, COO of Shinsung, visited the location of the future facility in Lyons to celebrate the next step with new community partners.
- Construction on Shinsung’s new manufacturing facility is expected to finish in 2025.
- The facility will be located at 1407 Highway 1 North in Lyons within the Toombs Corporate Center at U.S. Highway 1.
- Tim Truxel, Chair of the Toombs County Development Authority, highlighted the rewarding transformation of the Toombs Corporate Center over the last few years and expressed appreciation for corporate partners.
- Lyons Mayor Willis NeSmith stated the community worked diligently to make the industrial park premier and that the location is ideal to service the growing automotive industry in Southeast Georgia.
- David Sikes, Chair of the Board of Commissioners of Toombs County, welcomed Shinsung Petrochemical, Inc., to the county as a great addition to its diverse industrial base.
- Alyce Thornhill, Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy for the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s Global Commerce team, represented the department on this project in partnership with the Toombs County Development Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, and Georgia Power.
- Commissioner Pat Wilson highlighted that generational investments in automotive and other key industries have created hope and opportunities across the state through partnerships at every level.
- Since 2018, Georgia has attracted more than 36,000 new jobs and over $28 billion in investments in the e-mobility, clean energy, and battery-related supply chains.
