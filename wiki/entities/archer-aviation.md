# Archer Aviation

**Type:** Company  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[vehicle-manufacturing|Vehicle Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** Covington Municipal Airport, Covington, Newton County, Georgia, United States

## Overview

Archer Aviation Inc., an aerospace company developing electric vertical takeoff and landing (eVTOL) aircraft for urban air mobility, announced a $118 million investment to build a new manufacturing facility at Covington Municipal Airport in Newton County, creating 1,000 jobs.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Manufactures electric vertical takeoff and landing (eVTOL) aircraft for urban air mobility, contributing to the electric vehicle ecosystem.

## Key facts

- Archer aims to obtain a production certificate and begin building aircraft on an assembly line at the Covington plant in mid-2025.
- State and local officials have offered millions of dollars in tax incentives, including a $3 million grant contingent on job creation.
- The company plans to hire hundreds of hourly workers initially, scaling to 650 by 2027 and up to 1,500 by 2030.
- Workforce training will be delivered through a three-week curriculum developed with Georgia Piedmont Technical College and the Georgia Quick Start program.
- Covington was selected for its airport location, affordability, and growth aspirations.
- Archer’s most important goal is certification of its Midnight aircraft in the eVTOL category, expected to stretch into 2025.
- Employees from Archer’s San Jose facility are being trained and will relocate to Covington when the plant opens.
- Georgia officials view e-mobility as a critical industry for the state’s future, citing a tech ecosystem that includes Meta, Rivian, and Takeda.
- Archer plans to invest $118 million over 10 years in a state-of-the-art eVTOL manufacturing facility.
- The Covington facility will have the capacity to produce up to 2,300 electric vertical takeoff and landing aircraft per year.
- The site is adjacent to Covington Municipal Airport, which was established in 1963 as part of a long-term airport master plan.
- Georgia’s interstate system via I-20 provides strategic accessibility to the facility.
- The project will create 1,000 jobs, with hiring expected to begin in late 2023.
- Georgia is home to more than 800 aerospace companies and maintains a robust education pipeline including high schools, technical colleges, and universities offering aerospace programs.
- The Georgia Center of Innovation for Aerospace has been cultivating an ecosystem for advanced air mobility and UAS systems for over 10 years.
- "Georgia has made fostering innovation a priority as we work with companies to bring the jobs of the future to the No. 1 state for business," said Governor Brian Kemp, highlighting the state's commitment to aerospace leadership.
- The nearly 400,000-sq-ft facility will support production of up to 650 Midnight eVTOL aircraft annually.
- Midnight aircraft travels at speeds up to 150 mph, reducing hour-long ground commutes to minutes in the air.
- Designed for back-to-back flights of 20-50 miles with minimal charge time between trips.
- Operates 100× quieter than a helicopter at cruising altitude, making it nearly inaudible on the streets below.
- Redundant systems aim to provide safety levels comparable to commercial airliners.
- Carries a pilot, up to four passengers and carry-on luggage.
- Archer’s buildout is in close partnership with Stellantis to mass-produce the Midnight aircraft.
- CEO Adam Goldstein stated the company is on track to complete the facility by year-end and ramp production to 650 aircraft per year.
- The showcase event included guests from Georgia’s Office of the Governor, Congressional Offices, City of Atlanta, State Legislature, FAA and other local governments.
- Archer aims to advance electrified aviation with its proprietary electric powertrain technology.
- Georgia’s electric vehicle business is taking off-literally.
- Archer Aviation plans to invest $118 million over ten years, build up to 650 four-passenger aircraft per year and eventually employ 1,000 people at its Covington facility.
- The Archer facility could expand to manufacture 2,300 airplanes annually.
- CEO Adam Goldstein said the new plant represents a huge step toward meeting urban air mobility challenges.
- Joon Georgia will invest $317 million and create 630 jobs at a new plant outside Statesboro.
- Hyundai Mobilis will spend $926 million to build EV power systems and integrated charging control units in Richmond Hill, creating 1,500 jobs.
- FREYR Battery’s Giga America plant will initially receive $1.7 billion, potentially growing to $2.6 billion and supporting more than 720 jobs by 2029.
- Athena Studios’ $60-million soundstage complex adds a 14,600-sq-ft learning center for University of Georgia and Georgia Film Academy students, bolstering the state’s workforce development.
- Pat Wilson called Joon’s announcement “a landmark moment as we drive Georgia’s automotive industry into the future.”
