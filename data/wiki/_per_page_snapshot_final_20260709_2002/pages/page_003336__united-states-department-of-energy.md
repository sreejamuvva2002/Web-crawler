# United States Department of Energy

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [DOE Unveils New Interactive Map Showcasing Clean Energy Investment Announcements Nationwide | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/articles/doe-unveils-new-interactive-map-showcasing-clean-energy-investment-announcements)  ·  `page_003336`

## Facts

- The U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) released a new interactive map series showcasing localized details of clean energy investments across the United States.
- The interactive map serves as a resource for tracking industrial revitalization fostered by a clean energy transition built by American workers.
- Since President Biden took office, companies have announced more than 500 planned investments in at least 450 new or expanded clean energy manufacturing facilities nationwide.
- These national announcements total over $160 billion in combined private and public sector investments into solar, electric vehicle assembly, components, and chargers, battery manufacturing, and offshore wind manufacturing.
- Georgia has announced 31 clean energy investment manufacturing announcements totaling over $32 billion since President Biden took office.
- Many announced investments across the nation do not yet include specific dollar amounts, meaning total investment dollars are expected to significantly grow over time.
- U.S. Secretary of Energy Jennifer M. Granholm stated that President Biden’s Investing in America agenda is delivering a manufacturing renaissance across the U.S. and that the DOE’s new interactive map shows Bidenomics in action, translating to jobs.
- John Podesta, Senior Advisor to the President for Clean Energy Innovation and Implementation, stated that the agenda has sparked a clean energy boom bringing good-paying, union jobs and new economic opportunity, and described the DOE map as a resource for understanding its impact on communities nationwide.
