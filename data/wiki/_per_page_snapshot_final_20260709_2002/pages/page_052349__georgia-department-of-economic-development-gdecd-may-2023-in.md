# Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) May 2023 Investment Announcements

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2023-05-15

**Source:** [Three Investment Projects Coming to Georgia Totaling $1.59 Billion Announced Last Month - Tennessee Star](https://tennesseestar.com/the-south/georgia/three-investment-projects-coming-to-georgia-totaling-1-59-billion-announced-last-month/khousler/2023/06/06/)  ·  `page_052349`

## Facts

- During the month of May, the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) announced that three companies are investing a combined total of $1.59 billion to establish new business operations in Georgia.
- On May 15, GDEcD announced that Anovion Technologies will build a new manufacturing facility in Bainbridge.
- Anovion Technologies supplies premium synthetic graphite anode materials for lithium-ion batteries.
- The Anovion project is expected to create more than 400 jobs and over $800 million in investment in Decatur County.
- Governor Kemp announced the Anovion facility, noting it boosts Georgia's future as an e-mobility capital.
- Eric Stopka, CEO of Anovion, stated the company is thrilled to be opening its first large-scale expansion manufacturing facility in Georgia.
- Stopka cited the state’s strong technical workforce and top-tier schools to help advance research partnerships and contribute to a highly skilled talent pipeline for climate technology jobs.
- The week after the Anovion announcement, GDEcD revealed that Hanon Systems will create 160 new jobs and invest more than $40 million in a new manufacturing facility in Bulloch County.
- Hanon Systems is a global provider of automotive thermal and energy management solutions.
- The Hanon facility will focus on assembling automotive thermal solutions.
- Hanon Systems is a supplier to Hyundai Motor Group.
- Hanon's newly-announced operations are expected to begin in May 2024, according to GDEcD.
- Min Sung, President and CEO of Hanon Systems, stated the company is pleased to contribute to the local economy of Bulloch County through job creation and thanked the State of Georgia and agency cooperation.
- The third May investment project announced is a $750 million investment from ADMARES to construct its first U.S. manufacturing facility in Ware County.
- ADMARES specializes in industrialized manufacturing of buildings and homes.
- ADMARES plans to construct a 2.5-million-square-foot build-to-suit manufacturing facility in Waycross to specialize in producing buildings for the housing sector.
- Mikael Hedberg, Founder and CEO of ADMARES, stated Waycross is an ideal location due to easy access to major highways and extensive rail connections.
- Hedberg noted that proximity to the Port of Brunswick offers a competitive advantage for global trade.
