# Hyundai Mobis

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [[News] Hyundai Mobis Unveils Holographic HUD ? ‘Magic Etched on the Windshield’: Transparent Display for All Kinds of Information - LEDinside](https://www.ledinside.com/news/2025/1/2025_01_09_07)  ·  `page_001193`

## Facts

- Hyundai Mobis unveiled its ‘holographic windshield display’ technology at CES 2025, transforming the vehicle’s front windshield into a transparent display without any additional device.
- Kia’s electric vehicle EV9 was used to offer a live demonstration of the holographic windshield display technology to CES 2025 visitors.
- The in-vehicle display market is shifting toward user experience innovation, including large screens, high-definition graphics, 3D and AR technologies, privacy modes, and unique interior designs.
- Global automakers are increasingly adopting advanced displays with various features to establish distinctive brand images.
- The technology projects images and videos anywhere on the front windshield using a special film with an optical component called HOE (Holographic Optical Element) that uses light diffraction to deliver content to driver and passenger eye positions.
- The display offers a privacy mode that prevents the driver from seeing the screen before the passenger does.
- The holographic film can be placed on the top, bottom, or side of the windshield, with side placement allowing it to function like a smartphone’s curved edge screen.
- Hyundai Mobis is teaming up with German optical company ZEISS to develop this advanced, not-yet-mass-produced display technology.
- Both companies intend to complete pre-development by the first half of next year and aim to launch the product for mass production as early as 2027.
- Lee Gyu Suk, CEO of Hyundai Mobis, and Karl Lamprecht, CEO of ZEISS, met at Hyundai Mobis’s CES 2025 booth to discuss further business cooperation and underscore their partnership.
- The companies aim to deepen collaboration by focusing on advanced solutions for driver and cabin monitoring systems as well as innovative augmented reality display technologies.
- Hyundai Mobis’s premium in-vehicle display portfolio includes the big and slim high-definition QL Display, the 34-inch curved Swivel Display, and the Rollable Display.
- Lee Gyu Suk stated that Hyundai Mobis will target the global market with market-leading technology and high-value-added products while actively introducing various new technologies.
- Hyundai Mobis invited global clients to its CES 2025 booth, held over 30 meetings with worldwide clients, and set up a special private zone to showcase 16 strategic products including electrification components, vehicle electronics, chassis, and lamps for global business opportunities.
- Hyundai Mobis invited approximately 40 Korean and international students from MIT, Stanford, and the Georgia Institute of Technology to experience its key exhibits, including the holographic windshield display and Human-centric Interior Lighting, as part of a global talent recruiting program aimed at expanding its student network and attracting top future talent.
