# Anovion Technologies

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2023-06-26

**Source:** [Charged EVs | Anovion to invest $800 million in new US graphite anode materials manufacturing facility - Charged EVs](https://chargedevs.com/newswire/anovion-to-invest-800-million-in-new-us-graphite-anode-materials-manufacturing-facility/)  ·  `page_001496`

## Facts

- Anovion Technologies has selected Decatur County in Southwest Georgia as the location of its new manufacturing facility for the production of synthetic graphite anode materials.
- The Decatur County facility is expected to produce 40,000 metric tonnes of synthetic graphite anode material for lithium-ion batteries per year.
- The new facility will cost $800 million.
- The project will create more than 400 new jobs.
- In 2022, Anovion was awarded a grant totaling $117 million from the US Department of Energy (DOE) to scale production capacity to meet growing demand.
- Anovion is commencing a multi-year expansion program of 150,000 metric tonnes of annual capacity for anode-grade synthetic graphite.
- In addition to the new Georgia facility, the company is expanding its Anovion Center of Excellence.
- The company is also expanding its hydro-powered, sister manufacturing facility near Niagara Falls, New York.
- Eric Stopka, CEO of Anovion, stated that Anovion conducted a thorough search to identify a location that addressed their energy, transportation, logistics and human capital needs that would ensure they have the ecosystem in place to produce synthetic graphite anode material.
