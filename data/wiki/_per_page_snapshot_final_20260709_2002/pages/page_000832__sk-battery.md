# SK Battery

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2021-02-10

**Source:** [Jackson County battery plant punished in trade secret controversy | FOX 5 Atlanta](https://www.fox5atlanta.com/news/jackson-county-battery-plant-punished-for-trade-secret-controversy)  ·  `page_000832`

## Facts

- A multi-billion dollar battery factory is under construction in Commerce, Georgia (North Georgia).
- The US International Trade Commission (USITC) ruled that SK Battery must be punished for using trade secrets from a rival battery manufacturer.
- LG Chem (now called LG Energy Solutions) accused SK Battery of hiring away its employees to steal lithium-ion battery technology.
- SK Battery is building a $2.6 billion factory in Jackson County, Georgia.
- The Jackson County development is expected to eventually generate 2600 jobs.
- The state of Georgia agreed to provide $300 million in tax incentives for the project.
- The plant was scheduled to open and become fully operational in 2022.
- The facility covers 2.4M square feet along Interstate 85 in Jackson County.
- The USITC ordered a 10-year importation ban on SK Battery for all lithium-ion batteries, battery cells, battery modules, and battery packs.
- Many of the banned components are required to build electric vehicle batteries at the Jackson County facility.
- John Hyun Jim, CEO of LG Energy Solutions, stated that SKI's total disregard of warnings and intellectual property rights gave them no choice but to file the case.
- LG Energy Solutions expressed gratitude to the International Trade Commission for protecting its innovations and significant economic investments in the United States.
- LG Energy Solutions' North American headquarters is located in Atlanta.
- SK Battery had already signed deals to provide batteries for the electric Ford F-150 and a Volkswagen SUV being built in Chattanooga, Tennessee.
- The ruling allows SK Battery a four-year window to manufacture batteries for Ford and a two-year window for Volkswagen, giving both automakers time to transition to new domestic suppliers of batteries.
- The USITC originally ruled against SK Battery in February 2020 after an administrative law judge reviewed deleted emails discovered on SK's computers.
- Deleted emails instructed SK employees to remove all materials related to the rival company and delete specific directives after completion.
- State and local leaders were placed in an awkward position lobbying the full USITC for leniency toward SK Battery despite evidence of intentional non-compliance.
- Parent company SK Innovation stated it would immediately enter into discussions with customers Ford Motor Company and Volkswagen regarding the ITC's decision.
- SK Innovation expressed serious concerns about the commercial and operational implications for its EV-battery facility in Commerce, Georgia, which is expected to employ 2600 people upon completion.
- SK Innovation believed the ITC ruling could seriously adversely impact President Biden's policies to combat climate change and expand US auto fleet electrification.
- SK Innovation expressed a desire to have detailed discussions with Biden administration officials reviewing the ITC ruling and implementing EV and environmental policies.
