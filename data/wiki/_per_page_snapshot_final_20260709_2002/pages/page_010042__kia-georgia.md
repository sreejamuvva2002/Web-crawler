# Kia Georgia

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** workforce_training  ·  **Published:** 2026-04

**Source:** [Kia Georgia Invests $100,000 in TCSG Foundation to Support Georgia’s Workforce Pipeline - Metro Atlanta CEO](https://metroatlantaceo.com/news/2026/04/kia-georgia-invests-100000-tcsg-foundation-support-georgias-workforce-pipeline/)  ·  `page_010042`

## Facts

- Kia Georgia announced a $100,000 donation to the Technical College System of Georgia (TCSG) Foundation to support workforce development and technical education across the state.
- The contribution was presented by Kia Georgia President and CEO Stuart Countess at TCSG’s annual GOAL and Rick Perkins Awards banquet.
- Kia Georgia President and CEO Stuart Countess stated that technical education plays a critical role in driving innovation and economic growth, and the investment ensures students have necessary resources.
- The donation will expand access to critical support services for TCSG students, with a portion directed to the TCSG Foundation’s Last Mile Fund.
- The Last Mile Fund provides financial assistance to students nearing graduation to help them overcome unexpected barriers and complete their credentials.
- Since 2016, Kia Georgia has awarded a brand-new Kia vehicle annually to the state’s GOAL winner.
- This year's GOAL winner will receive a new Kia Sportage, which is built at Kia’s advanced manufacturing facility in West Point, Georgia.
- TCSG Commissioner Greg Dozier stated that Kia Georgia continues to be an outstanding partner in building a highly skilled, job-ready workforce.
- Dozier noted that the investment directly supports students and strengthens the talent pipeline that drives Georgia’s economy.
- Kia Georgia's impact in the state dates back to its initial $1 billion investment in 2006 to establish its West Point manufacturing facility.
- Kia Georgia has invested more than $3.2 billion in its West Point operation, which spans 2,200 acres and has the capacity to produce more than 350,000 vehicles annually.
- The West Point plant manufactures the Telluride, Sorento, Sportage, all-electric EV9, and EV6 models, serving as a major driver of job creation and innovation in Georgia.
- More than 159,000 students are currently enrolled across TCSG’s 22 colleges, preparing for high-demand careers that support Georgia’s position as the No. 1 state for business and Top State for Talent.
