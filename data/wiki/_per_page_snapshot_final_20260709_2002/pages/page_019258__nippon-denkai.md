# Nippon Denkai

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2022-07-21

**Source:** [Japanese firm shifts copper foil plans from SC to Georgia | Taiwan News | Jul. 21, 2022 02:17](https://www.taiwannews.com.tw/en/news/4601831)  ·  `page_019258`

## Facts

- A Japanese company announced plans for a $150 million plant to make copper foil for electric vehicle batteries in Augusta, Georgia.
- The plant location was shifted from a previously announced site near an existing Camden, South Carolina, facility.
- Nippon Denkai's American subsidiary said it would hire more than 100 people initially.
- The company could eventually triple production at the Georgia site from an initial 9,500 metric tons (10,500 tons) to 28,500 metric tons (31,500 tons).
- Tripling production would raise investment to $430 million and create 250 jobs over the next five years.
- Denkai America spokesperson Michael Coll stated: “Our goal is to triple the capacity in Augusta; market conditions will determine timeline.”
- The company raised its initial investment by $10 million from what it had announced in December.
- The 115-acre (47-hectare) Augusta site was chosen over South Carolina because there is room for expansion, it can host an “advanced” equipment layout, and it offers a “key cost competitive edge in electricity price.”
- State and local officials will give the company what could be more than $76 million in tax breaks and incentives.
- Nippon Denkai said it will start construction in September and hopes to be shipping samples to manufacturers by the summer of 2024.
- The company sees a big opportunity to make copper foil for electric vehicle battery makers, noting many battery plants are being built in the United States but few copper foil plants are.
- Nippon Denkai said companies will want domestic sources to avoid supply chain problems including risks of politically driven supply disruptions.
- The company projects an annual 21.4% growth in copper foil demand through 2035.
- The ability to triple production in Augusta will allow the company to pursue that projected growth.
- Combined with the 30-year-old Camden plant, the company would have an American production capacity of more than 35,000 metric tons.
- In June, Denkai announced a smaller $14 million expansion at the 80-worker Camden plant, also related to expansion in the electric battery and vehicle market.
- Augusta-Richmond County will give property tax breaks valued at a projected $68.7 million over 25 years.
- The company will pay $30 million in taxes under the deal, according to the Augusta Economic Development Authority.
- The company gets free land valued at $2.25 million, said Cal Wray, the authority's president.
- Georgia officials said they will spend an undisclosed amount of money to train workers.
- Nippon Denkai could claim various state tax breaks, including an income tax credit allowing it to annually deduct $4,000 per job from state income taxes, up to $5 million over five years, as long as workers make at least $28,000 a year.
- Georgia Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson pointed to the announcement as more fruit of the state's focus on what he calls a “generational opportunity” in electric vehicles.
- Wilson cited the $5 billion Rivian Automotive plant east of Atlanta and the $5.5 billion Hyundai Motor Group plant near Savannah as part of this focus.
- Wray said a $347 million, 125-job copper smelter announced by Aurubis AG in Augusta is another development driven by the growth in electric vehicles.
