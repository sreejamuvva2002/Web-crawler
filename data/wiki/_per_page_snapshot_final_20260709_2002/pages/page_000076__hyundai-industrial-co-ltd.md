# Hyundai Industrial Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-11-01

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp: Hyundai, Kia Supplier to Invest in Manufacturing Facility in Coweta | Governor Brian P. Kemp Office of the Governor](https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases/2023-11-01/gov-kemp-hyundai-kia-supplier-invest-manufacturing-facility-coweta)  ·  `page_000076`

## Facts

- On November 01, 2023, Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Hyundai Industrial Co., Ltd. will invest more than $24 million in a new manufacturing facility near Newnan in Coweta County.
- The project will create 100 new jobs, with a primary focus on filling manufacturing production roles.
- Hyundai Industrial purchased an existing building at 83 Amlajack Way in Coweta County to be retrofitted for production.
- The facility will produce arm and head rests for original equipment manufacturers (OEMs), including Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- Hyundai Industrial is a global auto parts supplier established in 1978 and headquartered in Ulsan, Korea.
- The company specializes in manufacturing car seats, arm rests, and head rests, with more than four decades of experience supplying some of the world’s biggest automotive brands.
- Mr. Woosuck Kim, Team Leader at HDI America Inc., stated the company is excited to be part of Georgia's e-mobility transition and work with Coweta County for community and employee growth.
- Coweta County Board of Commissioners Chairman John Reidelbach noted the facility will have close proximity to I-85, making transport to nearby automobile plants effortless.
- Director of Korean Investment Yoonie Kim represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on the project in partnership with the Coweta County Development Authority and Georgia EMC.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that with Kia in West Georgia and Hyundai on the eastern coast, suppliers like Hyundai Industrial can locate almost anywhere in the state while remaining central to at least two major manufacturers.
- Since last year's Hyundai Metaplant announcement, partners like Kia and Hyundai have attracted a network of suppliers committing to bring nearly 6,000 jobs to Georgia, mostly to communities outside large urban areas.
- Since 2020, Georgia has created more than $25.1 billion in investments and 29,000 jobs related to the electric vehicle market growth.
- In fiscal year 2023, job creation in Georgia's automotive industry increased by 324 percent compared to fiscal year 2021.
- Suppliers attracted by Georgia’s mobility original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) resulted in over $2 billion in investment across the state in FY23.
