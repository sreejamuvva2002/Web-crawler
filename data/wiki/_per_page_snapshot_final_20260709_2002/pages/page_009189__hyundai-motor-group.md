# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-09-20

**Source:** [Georgia Tech and Hyundai Motor Group Sign MoU for Future Mobility Collaboration | MOTOR](https://www.motor.com/2023/09/georgia-tech-and-hyundai-motor-group-sign-mou-for-future-mobility-collaboration/)  ·  `page_009189`

## Facts

- Georgia Tech and Hyundai Motor Group signed a memorandum of understanding to collaborate on research and applications to support the future of sustainable mobility, including developing a hydrogen economy, workforce development, and making cities smarter and more sustainable.
- Hyundai Motor Group is investing $12.6 billion in new dedicated electric vehicle and battery manufacturing facilities in Georgia.
- The investment includes the $7.59 billion Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) site in Bryan County, Ga., that will create more than 8,500 direct jobs.
- The investment includes the $5 billion battery manufacturing facility joint venture with SK On in Bartow County, Ga., that will employ approximately 3,500 people.
- Proximity to Georgia Tech was one of the important considerations in deciding the locations for these new facilities.
- Kia Georgia’s West Point assembly plant began vehicle production in 2009 under the leadership of Honorary Chairman Mong-Koo Chung and Executive Chair Euisun Chung.
- University System of Georgia Chancellor Sonny Perdue participated in the HMG-Georgia Tech MOU ceremony and was the Governor of Georgia at the time of the Kia Georgia plant’s opening.
- A recent study by the Center for Automotive Research reveals that Hyundai Motor’s automotive operations contributed $20.1 billion and 190,000 jobs to the U.S. economy in 2021 alone.
- Hyundai’s investments in Georgia are expected to create nearly 40,000 jobs and $4.6 billion in individual earnings every year.
- Jay Chang, President and CEO of Hyundai Motor Company, stated that the partnership with Georgia Tech includes research and applications to support sustainable mobility, hydrogen economy, workforce development, and smart cities, and noted it was one of the reasons for choosing Georgia for EV investments.
- José Muñoz, President and Global COO of Hyundai Motor Company and President and CEO of Hyundai and Genesis Motor North America, said Hyundai’s investments in Georgia will create tens of thousands of jobs and bring tens of billions of dollars in economic impact for decades to come.
- José Muñoz stated that proximity to institutions like Georgia Tech was one of the many reasons Hyundai selected Georgia for its new EV manufacturing facility.
