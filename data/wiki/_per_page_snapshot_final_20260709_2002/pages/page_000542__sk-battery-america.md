# SK Battery America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-03-10

**Source:** [SK Battery America lays off nearly 1,000 workers at Georgia plant | Utility Dive](https://www.utilitydive.com/news/sk-battery-america-lays-off-nearly-1000-workers-at-georgia-plant/814288/)  ·  `page_000542`

## Facts

- SK Battery America is a subsidiary of South Korea-based SK On.
- SK Battery America is laying off hundreds of workers from its production facility in Commerce, Georgia.
- The layoffs are occurring as electric vehicle battery demand slows and federal incentives dry up.
- In December, parent company SK On announced it would expand its focus from EV batteries to stationary energy storage.
- This strategic shift followed the collapse of a joint venture between SK On and Ford.
- The company filed a Worker Adjustment and Retraining Notification (WARN) on Friday, notifying the state of job cuts affecting 958 people.
- Affected workers were notified they will receive pay and benefits for the next 60 days.
- Friday, March 6, was the last day of work for the affected employees.
- The Commerce facility previously employed 2,566 workers.
- The layoffs reduced the site's headcount to approximately 1,600 employees.
- Joe Guy Collier, SK Battery America’s spokesperson, stated the company will assist affected workers during the transition.
- The workforce reductions coincide with broader demand challenges in the electric vehicle battery industry.
- Spokesperson Joe Guy Collier indicated the company is transitioning to diversify its business beyond EV batteries.
- The company stated it remains committed to Georgia and building a robust U.S. supply chain for advanced battery manufacturing.
- SK Battery America is actively pursuing future customers in the Battery Energy Storage System (BESS) market.
- According to the Federal Reserve Bank of Dallas, many automakers and battery companies are cutting back due to slower sales and reduced federal incentives.
- Industry players are shifting focus to meet growing data center demand for backup power and energy storage systems.
- Customer Ford announced in December that it is shifting its electrification plans to include hybrid vehicles and smaller, more affordable EVs.
- Ford also announced plans to grow its energy storage business.
- Ford expects to record a $19.5 billion write-down resulting from its strategic shift.
- Ford dissolved its BlueOval SK battery joint venture with SK On.
- Ford canceled production of the electric F-150 Lightning due to sluggish sales.
- In 2023, SK Battery America announced plans to expand its Commerce site workforce by several hundred employees.
- The company had previously set a hiring goal of 2,600 employees at the Commerce site to meet rising EV demand.
- The Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA) fined SK Battery America twice for exposing workers to respiratory hazards.
- Law firm Strauss Borrelli announced an investigation into SK Battery America for allegedly failing to properly notify the 958 affected employees under the WARN Act.
- The WARN Act mandates that employers provide written notice to workers at least 60 days prior to plant closures or mass job cuts.
- Spokesperson Joe Guy Collier stated that the workforce reduction complies with WARN Act guidelines.
