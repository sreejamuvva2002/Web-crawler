# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2021-06-22

**Source:** [Boston Dynamics is officially part of Hyundai Motor Group - Ars Technica](https://arstechnica.com/gadgets/2021/06/boston-dynamics-is-officially-part-of-hyundai-motor-group/)  ·  `page_009281`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group officially completed its acquisition of robotics company Boston Dynamics on Tuesday.
- The deal was announced in December and valued Boston Dynamics at $1.1 billion.
- The purchase gives Hyundai an 80 percent controlling stake in the company.
- Previous owner Softbank retains a 20 percent ownership stake.
- Boston Dynamics was spun off from MIT in 1992.
- The company survived for most of its history on DARPA research grants.
- Google acquired Boston Dynamics in 2013 as part of an Android co-founder Andy Rubin-led robotics initiative.
- Google freed the company from surviving on military contracts.
- Executive interest in robotics left Google when Andy Rubin departed a year after the acquisition.
- Google sold Boston Dynamics to Softbank in 2017 for a reported $165 million.
- By December 2020, Softbank faced financial difficulties after investments like Uber, WeWork, and OneWeb failed.
- Softbank began selling off major assets like Arm during its financial struggles.
- Hyundai wanted to invest in more "mobility solutions."
- Softbank was able to sell Boston Dynamics at a profit.
- Prior to the acquisition, Boston Dynamics had conducted only pure research.
- Boston Dynamics began selling its first product, the $74,500 robotic dog "Spot," one year prior to the article's publication.
- Reducing costs is essential for Boston Dynamics to achieve profitability through robot sales.
- Few companies specialize in the mass production of robotics parts and components.
- A car manufacturer like Hyundai is considered a good fit for scaling robotics manufacturing.
