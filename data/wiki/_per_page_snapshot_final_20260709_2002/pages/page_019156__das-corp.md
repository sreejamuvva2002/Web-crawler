# DAS Corp.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-06

**Source:** [Georgia lands another auto parts supplier - The Christian Index](https://christianindex.org/stories/georgia-lands-another-auto-parts-supplier,61012)  ·  `page_019156`

## Facts

- A Korean automotive parts company will build a manufacturing plant in Metter to supply Georgia’s Kia facility and the new electric vehicles plant under construction west of Savannah.
- DAS Corp. will invest more than $35 million in the project.
- The project will create 300 jobs.
- Gov. Brian Kemp announced the project on Wednesday.
- Gov. Brian Kemp stated that suppliers for the Hyundai Metaplant resulted in over $2 billion in investment last fiscal year alone, helping Georgia achieve a third straight year of record-breaking economic growth.
- Gov. Brian Kemp noted that logistics assets, including a reliable network of rail lines and highways, connect companies like DAS to key business partners across the state and global markets, providing direct benefits to Georgia communities.
- DAS was established in 1987.
- DAS specializes in designing and manufacturing seating systems, safety seating components, and seating structures.
- Sean Kim, DAS chief operating officer, said the strategic location near prominent cities like Savannah and Macon, facilitating convenient access, played a pivotal role in the company's decision-making process.
- Sean Kim stated that looking ahead to future expansion prospects, the company deemed Metter an ideal location in anticipation of its role as a global electric vehicle production hub thanks to its close proximity to Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- The new DAS facility will primarily produce automotive seat structures.
- Operations are expected to begin in the last half of next year.
- During the next five years, the company will be recruiting assembly technicians.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development’s Global Commerce team worked on the project.
- The project was developed in partnership with the Candler County Industrial Authority.
- The project was developed in partnership with the Georgia Ports Authority.
- The project was developed in partnership with Electric Cities of Georgia.
- The project was developed in partnership with the Technical College System of Georgia’s Quick Start program.
