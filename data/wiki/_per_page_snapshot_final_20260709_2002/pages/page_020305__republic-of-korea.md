## Facts

- Gov. Brian Kemp is taking his first overseas trade mission to South Korea, a trip that starts Saturday and runs through Friday.
- Kemp told the Atlanta Journal-Constitution in an interview that the trip made a “natural fit” for his first overseas job safari.
- South Korea has fast become a major source of investment for Georgia, with $2 billion in projects announced in Georgia over the past year.
- Several more major initiatives from South Korean companies are in the pipeline.
- Months after taking office in 2003, former Gov. Sonny Perdue trekked to South Korea to talk jobs with overseas executives.
- Former Gov. Nathan Deal touched down in Seoul to drum up business for Georgia in his first year as governor.
- $1.7 billion in new investments from South Korean companies have poured into Georgia.
- Kemp told the AJC: "We want to see how we can bring up their investments."
