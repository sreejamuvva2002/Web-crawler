# Paul M. Dabbar

**Type:** person  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [Paul M. Dabbar | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/person/paul-m-dabbar)  ·  `page_003290`

## Facts

- Served as the Department’s fourth Under Secretary for Science.
- Served as the Department’s principal advisor on fundamental energy research, energy technologies, and science.
- Drove mission through programs including nuclear and high energy particle physics, basic energy, advanced computing, fusion, and biological and environmental research.
- Had direct management over a majority of the Department’s national labs and their world-leading user facilities.
- Managed the environmental and legacy management missions of the Department, addressing the U.S. legacy of nuclear weapons production and government-sponsored nuclear energy research.
- Is the lead for technology commercialization activities for the Department and its 17 national labs.
- Prior to confirmation as Under Secretary for Science, worked in operations, finance, and strategy roles in the energy sector.
- Served as a Managing Director at J.P. Morgan, leading various energy business areas.
- Has over $400 billion in investment experience across all energy sectors including solar, wind, geothermal, distributed-generation, utility, LNG, pipeline, oil & gas, trading, and energy technologies.
- Led the majority of all nuclear transactions at J.P. Morgan.
- Had a senior leadership role for the company’s commodity trading business, including power, oil and gas.
- Served on the Department of Energy Environmental Management Advisory Board.
- Has been a lecturer at the U.S. Naval Academy.
- Conducted research at the Johns Hopkins University Applied Physics Laboratory.
- Is a member of the Council on Foreign Relations.
- Received a B.S. degree from the U.S. Naval Academy, and a masters degree from Columbia University.
