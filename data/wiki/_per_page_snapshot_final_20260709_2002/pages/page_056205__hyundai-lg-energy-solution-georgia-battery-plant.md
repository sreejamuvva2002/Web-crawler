# Hyundai-LG Energy Solution Georgia Battery Plant

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-05

**Source:** [Work paused at Hyundai's U.S. site after hundreds of workers detained in raid | CBC News](https://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/windsor/hyundai-us-site-lg-energy-raid-ice-1.7626125)  ·  `page_056205`

## Facts

- Work at Hyundai's U.S. facility in Georgia was paused after hundreds of workers were detained in a major raid by U.S. authorities.
- The raid forced a pause in the construction of a car battery factory that is part of a major investment by the Korean automaker in the state.
- The setback affects a project due to start operating at the end of the year.
- The arrests threaten to roil the relationship between Washington and Seoul, a key ally and investor in the United States.
- South Korea and the U.S. have been at odds over the details of a trade deal that includes $350 billion US of investments.
- Just last month, South Korea pledged $150 billion in U.S. investments, including $26 billion US from Hyundai Motor, at a summit between the two countries.
- South Korea's Foreign Ministry expressed concern about the impact on its nationals detained during the operation by U.S. immigration authorities.
- A DHS agent stated that multiple U.S. agencies conducted a judicially authorized enforcement operation as they actively investigate unlawful employment practices.
- Steven Schrank, special agent in charge of Homeland Security Investigations for Georgia, confirmed that arrests are being made.
- The Atlanta office of the U.S. Justice Department agency ATF reported on X that up to 450 people had been detained.
- A South Korean government official said detainees were being held at a United States Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) detention facility.
- The Korea Economic Daily reported around 560 workers at the Hyundai Motor facility and LG Energy Solution had been detained, citing unidentified industry sources.
- Some 300 of the detained individuals are South Korean nationals, according to local media reports.
- A spokesperson for the Hyundai-GA battery company stated the company is cooperating fully and has paused construction work.
- The facility is a joint venture between South Korean battery maker LGES and Hyundai Motor.
- LG Energy Solution said the facility was due to start operations at the end of this year.
- Hyundai Motor stated that its production of electric vehicles at the sprawling site was not affected by the raid.
- LG Energy Solution issued a statement saying it is closely cooperating with the South Korean government and relevant authorities to ensure employee safety and secure swift release from detention.
- LGES shares dropped 2.3 percent following the news.
- In 2023, Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution announced a $4.3 billion venture to produce EV battery cells, with each company holding a 50 percent stake.
- The plant will supply batteries for Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis EV models.
- The battery factory is part of Hyundai's $12.6 billion investments in Georgia, which also includes the automaker's just-opened car factory.
- The combined investment would be "the largest economic development project" in the state's history.
- Social media video footage showed an HSI agent telling workers in yellow safety vests that they had a search warrant for the whole site and needed construction to cease immediately.
- Under President Donald Trump, ICE has become the driving force of a sweeping crackdown on migrants, bolstered by record funding and new latitude to conduct raids.
- Trump has stated he wants to deport "the worst of the worst" criminals, but ICE figures have shown a rise in non-criminals being picked up.
