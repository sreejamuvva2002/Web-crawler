# ALBAform Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2017-04-10

**Source:** [Czech Firm Positive About Move to Hall County, Looking for More Space and Workers - Global Atlanta](https://www.globalatlanta.com/czech-firm-positive-move-hall-county-looking-space-workers/)  ·  `page_051998`

## Facts

- ALBAform Inc. held a ribbon cutting ceremony attended by the Czech Republic’s foreign minister and ambassador in Flowery Branch, Hall County in September 2013.
- The company develops and manufactures custom metal components for the automotive industry.
- ALBAform represented the first investment by a Czech firm in Georgia.
- At its Hall County outset, the company started with one Czech engineer and one CNC bending machine to bend wires for car seats and other components.
- The company initially opened in Dallas due to personal relations between founder's daughter Monika Vintrlikova and a fellow foreign exchange student from Dallas.
- The company relocated to Flowery Branch after taking over a facility there because Dallas was too distant from potential clients in Alabama or Georgia.
- When moving to Flowery Branch, the company left behind three contract workers in Dallas.
- Upon arriving in Flowery Branch, president Jan Vintrlik hired four employees immediately, all of whom had metal working backgrounds.
- President Jan Vintrlik considers the move to Hall County positive due to its international environment.
- Vintrlik noted that the high concentration of foreign-owned companies in the area makes it easier for non-locals to settle and focus on production.
- The firm’s client base has grown to include Daimler, BMW, VW, and Tesla.
- In Europe, the company's main customers include high-volume carmakers like VW, Skoda, BMW, Mercedes, Hyundai, and Kia, as well as low-volume brands like Rolls-Royce and Bentley.
- The number of employees in Flowery Branch has grown to 55 and the company is looking to employ 10 more.
- The company needs to expand its 30,000 square feet facility by 20,000 square feet to 50,000 square feet to meet a 20 percent anticipated growth in production of metal components for seats and other auto parts.
- The extra space will be used to house more machines and stock products.
- President Jan Vintrlik stated that if he has to move, he won't leave Flowery Branch because the company wants to ensure the same location for its employees.
- The firm is hiring for the quality department, technicians who can program CNC machines, maintenance people, and operators for new machinery such as CNC bending machines and welding robots.
- The company provides special training for employees because the machinery is specialized, though a metal-working background is an advantage.
- The company promotes itself at job fairs in surrounding counties and encourages employees to recommend friends.
- ALBAform is in touch with Lanier Technical College and posts job placements on the Internet.
- President Jan Vintrlik noted that U.S. employees are happy for company success but leave if they find work closer to home or motivated by better wages, requiring more communication about company plans.
- Vintrlik contrasted U.S. workers with Czech counterparts, stating Czechs are stable because they do not move for work, are harder to motivate, get suspicious if talked to too much, and usually do not speak up directly but talk among each other.
- Products made both in the Czech Republic and in Georgia are exactly the same, utilizing copy-and-paste technology, quality standards, and research/development to supply European seat makers in the U.S. and Europe.
- The company continues to conduct research often at the request of customers who try to find savings within production, consolidate products, or find better surface treatments.
- ALBAform asks suppliers for more reliable machines with faster production times to allow more complexity for its products.
- The company owns its own tool shops and advanced quality checking tools.
- The company asks employees to think globally and speak different languages to gain a competitive advantage, with staff attending development meetings in Germany, assembling welding tools in the U.S., and reporting economics to headquarters in the Czech Republic.
- ALBAform tries to add value to its products by asking suppliers for faster production times allowing more complexity.
- Monika Vintrlikova's father Jan Maderic visits the company annually and participates in telecommunication conferences.
