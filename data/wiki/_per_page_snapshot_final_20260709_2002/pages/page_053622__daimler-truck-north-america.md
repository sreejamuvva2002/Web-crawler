# Daimler Truck North America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Daimler Truck Cuts Workforce by 2,000 Amidst Market Slowdown | Transportation News and Trends](https://blog.gettransport.com/news/daimler-truck-temporary-layoffs/)  ·  `page_053622`

## Facts

- Daimler Truck North America is laying off 2,000 workers across five facilities in the U.S. and Mexico due to diminished market demand.
- The layoffs are effective immediately and impact various operational roles, including those in manufacturing and logistics.
- Medium-duty, on-highway, and electric vehicle segments are experiencing a notable slowdown in orders.
- The layoffs are labeled as temporary, yet no definitive date for employee return has been established.
- The announced layoffs start Friday and primarily impact two facilities near Charlotte along with production plants located in Detroit, Portland, and Saltillo, Mexico.
- Each site will face unique challenges depending on its respective business needs, as outlined by the company’s officials.
- A spokesperson for Daimler Truck North America stated that there has been a significant decrease in new truck orders, particularly within the medium-duty, on-highway, and electric vehicle segments.
- Andrew Johnson emphasized that the decision to reduce the workforce across several facilities is essential to adapt to current market conditions.
- Nearly 9,000 jobs have been slashed nationwide in the freight sector recently, underscoring collective industry difficulties.
- In North Carolina, the layoffs will let go of 546 employees from a manufacturing facility in Mount Holly and an additional 27 from a components and logistics site in Gastonia.
- The NC layoffs were notified through a Worker Adjustment and Retraining Notification Act notice.
- The impacted roles range widely from truck assemblers and painters to supervisory, material handling, and logistics positions.
- Daimler Truck North America has approximately 29,200 employees listed on its website.
- The company has a substantial presence across various manufacturing plants in Cleveland, Gastonia, High Point, Mount Holly, Gaffney, Redford, Saltillo, and Santiago in Mexico.
- Daimler Truck North America is a subsidiary of Germany-based Daimler Truck AG.
- The company boasts a portfolio of brands including Freightliner, Western Star, Mercedes-Benz, Fuso, BharatBenz, and Rizon.
- Workforce reductions could lead to delays in shipments and services, making it difficult for businesses to maintain timely deliveries and efficient operations.
