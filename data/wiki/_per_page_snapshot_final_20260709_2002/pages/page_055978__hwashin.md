# Hwashin

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-10-31

**Source:** [South Korean auto supplier Hwashin to launch new facility in Dublin, bringing over 460 jobs](https://wgxa.tv/news/local/south-korean-auto-supplier-hwashin-to-launch-new-facility-in-dublin-bringing-over-460-jobs)  ·  `page_055978`

## Facts

- Governor Brian Kemp announced that automotive supplier Hwashin will open a new manufacturing facility in Dublin.
- The new facility will create over 460 jobs in Laurens County.
- Hwashin is investing more than $176 million in the project.
- Hwashin is a South Korea-based company that produces chassis and body parts for major automakers including Hyundai and Kia.
- The Dublin facility will focus on manufacturing chassis components.
- Production at the new facility is scheduled to begin in late 2025.
- The plant will be located at the Georgia Highway 257 Rail Served Site.
- The site is the first Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) Select certified site in the state.
- GRAD Select certification indicates the site meets rigorous requirements for accelerated project timelines.
- Hwashin CEO Seo Jin Jung stated the company draws from 50 years of expertise and collaboration with Georgia and Laurens County.
- Hwashin aspires to shape a future global city focused on electric vehicles, according to CEO Seo Jin Jung.
- The alliance seeks to position the region at the forefront of the evolving automobile industry.
- Once operational, the facility will supply parts locally to Hyundai Metaplant America and Kia Georgia.
- Georgia officials welcomed Hwashin's investment in the state's growing electric vehicle and mobility ecosystem.
- Dublin Mayor Joshua Kight stated the manufacturing facility will become an economic engine for the region.
- Mayor Joshua Kight noted the project will lead to more employment opportunities and quality growth for Dublin.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development assisted with the project in partnership with local officials.
- The new facility aligns with Georgia's goal of attracting jobs and investments related to electric vehicle manufacturing.
