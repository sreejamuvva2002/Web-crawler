# SK Battery America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [GA: SK Battery America to Open U.S. IT Center, Create 200 High-Tech Jobs in Roswell | Trade and Industry Development](https://www.tradeandindustrydev.com/region/georgia/news/ga-sk-battery-america-open-us-it-center-create-200-31349)  ·  `page_050129`

## Facts

- Georgia Gov. Brian P. Kemp announced that SK Battery America (SKBA) will open a regional IT hub facility in Roswell.
- The new facility will create 200 high-tech jobs.
- The investment for the IT center is approximately $19 million over the next few years.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated: “We're proud of SK Battery America’s continued growth here in the No. 1 state for business.”
- Governor Brian Kemp stated: “This innovative company continues to bring the jobs of the future that will benefit hardworking Georgians in manufacturing, and now it will also tap into the tech talent our top-ranking universities provide.”
- SK Battery America is investing nearly $2.6 billion in two lithium-ion battery manufacturing facilities in Jackson County.
- The Jackson County facilities will supply electric vehicles such as the Ford F-150 Lightning and Volkswagen ID.4.
- The SKBA facility is one of SK Group’s three major investments in the State of Georgia.
- SK Group was founded in 1976.
- SK Group is the second-largest conglomerate in South Korea.
- Jason Choi, Head of Information Technology at SK Battery America, stated: “We decided to open our IT regional center in Roswell to strengthen SK’s position as a leader in the battery industry, and it will help us build and operate an advanced manufacturing system in accordance with further expansion of battery production bases in the United States.”
- Jason Choi stated: “We are excited to expand our presence and engage with the community and surrounding region.”
- SK Battery America’s new IT center will be an integrated IT management center serving the company’s battery manufacturing facilities across the United States, including its Georgia facilities.
- The IT center is located at 1110 Sanctuary Parkway in Roswell.
- Operations are expected to begin in January.
- The company will be hiring IT experts for battery manufacturing execution system development and operations as well as data analysts.
- City of Roswell Mayor Kurt Wilson stated: “We are looking forward to SK Battery America becoming part of our community and the opportunities it will bring to Roswell.”
- Mayor Kurt Wilson stated: “Since taking office a year ago, our priority has been to recruit businesses such as SK Battery America — businesses that can provide high-quality job opportunities for Roswell’s talented, experienced workforce and enhance the vibrancy and economic strength of our city.”
- Mayor Kurt Wilson stated: “Roswell is the perfect location for the IT Regional Center. We are proud SK Battery chose our city for the center and look forward to working closely with them.”
- Robb Pitts, Chairman of the Fulton County Board of Commissioners, stated: “We welcome SK Battery America to Fulton County and the many new job opportunities they will create for our residents.”
- Robb Pitts stated: “Fulton County is a destination for the best and brightest, and today’s announcement reflects that.”
- Director of Korean Investment Yoonie Kim represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The project was in partnership with City of Roswell, Fulton County, and Georgia Power.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated: “In 2019, SK On established SK Battery America as its U.S. production base, and the new IT regional center is a natural result of the company’s commitment to Georgia.”
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated: “In economic development, we often talk about the ripple effects of a company’s initial investment. Suppliers, support centers, and expansions create new opportunities that go beyond the company’s original scope, location, and even industry.”
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated: “We look forward to future opportunities to celebrate SK Battery America and SK On’s success in Georgia, and congratulations to the City of Roswell and Fulton County for this exciting addition to the region’s tech industry!”
- Electric mobility related projects have contributed more than $21 billion in investments to Georgia since 2020.
- Electric mobility related projects have contributed more than 26,700 new jobs to Georgia since 2020.
- SK Battery America’s new IT regional center will support the company’s battery production operations.
- The center will build on the state’s existing automotive assets and developing workforce.
- The center will help to ensure a domestic supply chain for the electric mobility industry.
