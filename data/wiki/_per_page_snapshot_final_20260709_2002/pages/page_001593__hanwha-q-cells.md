# Hanwha Q Cells

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2023-01-11

**Source:** [Qcells to invest $2.5B to build complete US solar supply chain, expand annual capacity to 8.4 GW | Utility Dive](https://www.utilitydive.com/news/qcells-billions-domestic-solar-manufacturing-georgia/640125/)  ·  `page_001593`

## Facts

- Hanwha Q Cells announced an investment of more than $2.5 billion to build up the U.S. solar supply chain and create 2,500 jobs in Georgia.
- The company and federal and state officials describe this as the largest such investment in U.S. history.
- Qcells is a subsidiary of the South Korean industrial group Hanwha Corp.
- Qcells unveiled plans to build a second plant in Georgia that will manufacture 3.3 GW of solar components and panels annually.
- The company plans to assemble an additional 2 GW per year of solar panels at its original plant in Dalton, Georgia.
- Qcells aims to reach 5.1 GW of annual production by 2023.
- The company is working toward a goal of 8.4 GW of total annual solar panel production capacity by 2024.
- Qcells plans to break ground on the second plant in Bartow County in the first quarter of 2023.
- Commercial production at the second plant is scheduled to begin by late 2024.
- The Dalton factory was previously producing 1.7 GW of solar modules each year.
- The Dalton factory is being overhauled to increase output to 3.1 GW per year.
- Qcells is raising its total U.S. output by a combined 6.7 GW over the next two years.
- U.S. Sen. Jon Ossoff (D-Georgia) called the expansion “the largest clean energy manufacturing project in American history.”
- Sen. Ossoff celebrated the company’s plan to create almost 2,500 jobs in Georgia.
- U.S. Sen. Raphael Warnock (D-Georgia) said the announcement follows months of close collaboration with Qcells, Senate leadership, and the administration.
- The collaboration aimed to make the Solar Energy Manufacturing for America Act a reality.
- The Solar Energy Manufacturing for America Act was sponsored by Sen. Ossoff.
- The act was included in the Inflation Reduction Act.
- The legislation adds a new tax credit for domestic production of solar components, including photovoltaic cells and wafers.
- The solar industry is experiencing global supply constraints from an ongoing tariff investigation.
- Global supply constraints are also driven by the enactment of the Uyghur Forced Labor Prevention Act.
- Lingering pandemic impacts continue to affect the solar supply chain.
- Demand for solar panels is rising as supply has become less certain.
- The Inflation Reduction Act extended and increased a number of tax credits for solar projects and manufacturing, contributing to rising demand.
- The company stated that demand for American-made solar panels is increasing rapidly driven by efforts to ensure energy independence.
- Qcells said its domestic manufacturing expansion will fulfill the growing need for clean energy solutions.
