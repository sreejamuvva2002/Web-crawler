# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-08

**Source:** [Massive Immigration Raid at Hyundai-LG Plant Sparks Diplomatic Fallout](https://www.tocsinmag.com/post/massive-operation-low-voltage-immigration-raid-at-hyundai-lg-battery-plant-in-georgia-sparks-diplo)  ·  `page_049961`

## Facts

- The construction site of the Hyundai–LG battery plant (part of Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America) in Ellabell, Bryan County, Georgia was the location of a massive immigration raid dubbed "Operation Low Voltage."
- On September 4, 2025, nearly 500 federal, state, and local law enforcement officers stormed the site and arrested 475 construction workers.
- The operation marked the largest raid at a single location in Homeland Security Investigations (HSI) history.
- The raid was led by ICE and HSI, supported by the FBI, DEA, ATF, and Georgia State Patrol, as part of a months-long criminal investigation into alleged illegal hiring practices and other serious federal violations.
- Roughly 300 or more of the detained individuals were South Korean citizens.
- Detainees also included individuals who entered the U.S. illegally, overstayed their visas, held non-work visas (e.g., tourist visas, visa waiver entries), or otherwise violated visa terms.
- Agents lined up workers to inspect documents, including dates of birth and Social Security numbers, to determine detention status.
- Detainees were transported by bus to the ICE processing center in Folkston, Georgia, over 160 km away from the plant site.
- Some workers attempted to flee by hiding in ventilation ducts or jumping into a sewage pond on-site, with one attempting to flip an agency boat before being arrested.
- The South Korean government voiced "concern and regret," reiterating that the rights and business interests of its citizens and companies should not be unjustly harmed during U.S. law enforcement actions.
- South Korean Foreign Minister Cho Hyun traveled to Washington for urgent negotiations regarding the detainees.
- A charter flight was arranged to fly more than 300 detained Korean workers home once administrative procedures were complete.
- Hyundai stated that none of those arrested were direct employees but rather hired through subcontractors.
- Hyundai pledged full cooperation and announced an internal probe into compliance across its entire supply chain.
- LG Energy Solution affirmed its cooperation with authorities and stated it was gathering relevant information about the incident.
- The Hyundai–LG joint venture represents a $7.6 billion investment including a battery facility modeled to serve 300,000 EVs annually.
- The project was previously hailed as Georgia’s largest economic development project.
- The raid halted construction at the site and introduced delays and cost concerns for the facility.
- The incident shook investor confidence and strained diplomatic relations between the U.S. and South Korea.
- The raid occurred shortly after South Korea agreed to invest $350 billion in U.S. energy and manufacturing projects.
- President Donald Trump posted on Truth Social urging companies to respect U.S. immigration laws and emphasizing the importance of hiring and training American workers.
- The operation highlighted an uncompromising U.S. immigration enforcement stance toward allies, raising questions about America's credibility as a stable investment destination under "America First" policies.
