# Shinsung Petrochemical, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2024-09-25

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp: Automotive Supplier Invests $11.2 Million in Toombs County | Governor Brian P. Kemp Office of the Governor](https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases/2024-09-25/gov-kemp-automotive-supplier-invests-112-million-toombs-county)  ·  `page_000087`

## Facts

- Shinsung Petrochemical will invest $11.2 million in a new manufacturing facility in Toombs County.
- The investment will create more than 30 new jobs.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced the investment on September 25, 2024.
- Eighty-three percent of expansions and new locations from the past fiscal year are located in rural communities.
- These rural expansions delivered more than 18,300 new jobs and $12.5 billion in investment to Georgians.
- Shinsung was established in 1974.
- The company specializes in products like automotive sealant.
- Shinsung will be a key supplier for Hyundai Motor Group and the Metaplant in Bryan County.
- Construction on Shinsung’s new manufacturing facility is expected to finish in 2025.
- The facility is located at 1407 Highway 1 North in Lyons, within the Toombs Corporate Center at U.S. Highway 1.
- Tim Truxel, Chair of the Toombs County Development Authority, commented on the transformation of the industrial park over the last few years.
- Lyons Mayor Willis NeSmith expressed excitement about the investment to the Toombs Corporate Center at U.S. Highway 1 in Lyons.
- David Sikes, Chair of the Board of Commissioners of Toombs County, welcomed Shinsung Petrochemical to the county.
- Alyce Thornhill, Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy, represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s Global Commerce team on this project.
- The project was developed in partnership with the Toombs County Development Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, and Georgia Power.
- Commissioner Pat Wilson highlighted historic generational investments in automotive and other key industries in Georgia over the last few years.
- Since 2018, Georgia has attracted more than 36,000 new jobs and over $28 billion in investments in e-mobility, clean energy, and battery-related supply chains.
- For over a century, Georgia has fostered healthy industry practices and positioned itself as a leader in developing emerging technologies for the mobility and energy industries.
