# Hyundai Motor Group & LG Energy Solution Battery Cell Joint Venture

**Type:** Joint Venture  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[battery-cell-manufacturing|Battery Cell Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** Savannah, Georgia, Bryan County, Georgia, United States

## Overview

A 50/50 joint venture between Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution to build a battery cell manufacturing plant in Bryan County, Savannah, Georgia with an annual capacity of 30 GWh, supporting up to 300,000 EVs per year.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Provides a domestic source of EV battery cells in Georgia, creating a stable supply for U.S. Hyundai, Kia and Genesis electric vehicles.

## Key facts

- The joint venture will have an annual production capacity of 30 GWh, sufficient for about 300,000 electric vehicles per year.
- Investment exceeds USD 4.3 billion (KRW 5.7 trillion) from both partners.
- The plant will be located in Bryan County, Savannah, Georgia, adjacent to Hyundai Metaplant America.
- Construction is slated to begin in the second half of 2023 with production expected by the end of 2025 at the earliest.
- Hyundai Mobis will assemble battery packs from the cells and supply them to U.S. manufacturing facilities for Hyundai, Kia and Genesis EV models.
- The JV aims to create a stable regional supply of batteries and enable rapid response to soaring U.S. EV demand.
- LG Energy Solution now operates or is constructing seven battery plants in the United States, focusing resources on expanding capacity.
- The partnership builds on long-standing collaboration supplying batteries for Hyundai Elantra Hybrid, Kona Electric, and IONIQ 6 models.
- "Two strong leaders in the auto and battery industries have joined hands, and together we are ready to drive the EV transition in America," said Youngsoo Kwon.
