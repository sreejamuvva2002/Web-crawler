# Enchem Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2020-01-30

**Source:** [Korea’s Enchem planning two sites in Georgia](https://evertiq.com/news/47645)  ·  `page_007974`

## Facts

- South Korean battery materials manufacturer Enchem Ltd. has confirmed plans to make an investment of more than USD 60 million in Jackson County, Georgia.
- The project will include two new manufacturing plants.
- The project will create more than 300 jobs.
- Governor Kemp welcomed Enchem to Georgia, citing the project as exactly the type of quality project the state hoped to attract during its first international trip to Korea last June.
- Governor Kemp noted that the industry is building a hub in Georgia as the market for electric vehicles and batteries continues its rapid growth.
- Governor Kemp stated that strong international partnerships between South Korea and Georgia will continue to benefit Georgians and create opportunities throughout the state.
- Enchem was founded in 2012.
- Enchem specializes in the development and manufacturing of electrolytes for rechargeable batteries and electrostatic double-layer capacitors (ELDCs).
- Enchem is the first announced supplier for SK Innovation.
- SK Innovation broke ground on its USD 1.67 billion battery manufacturing plant in Jackson County in March 2019.
- Enchem America CEO Oh, Jung Kang stated that the company is excited to build its factory in Georgia and will benefit from its infrastructure, transportation, and excellent weather.
- GDEcD Director of Korean Investment Yoonie Kim represented the Global Commerce Division on this project in partnership with the Jackson County Development Authority, Georgia EMC, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson congratulated Enchem on its decision to invest in Georgia.
- GDEcD has maintained strategic representation in Korea since 1985 to demonstrate ongoing commitment to the Korean market and community.
- GDEcD relies on partnerships and programs like Georgia Quick Start to ensure workforce and company success.
- In fiscal year 2019, South Korea was the top player in both foreign direct investment and jobs in Georgia, investing USD 1.87 billion and creating 2,507 jobs.
