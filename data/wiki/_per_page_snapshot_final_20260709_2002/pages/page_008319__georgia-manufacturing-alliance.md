# Georgia Manufacturing Alliance

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2022-05-26

**Source:** [The Georgia Manufacturing Alliance partners with The Greener Manufacturing Show in Atlanta June 8-9 2022 - Manufacturing News Today - EIN Presswire](https://manufacturing.einnews.com/pr_news/574161879/the-georgia-manufacturing-alliance-partners-with-the-greener-manufacturing-show-in-atlanta-june-8-9-2022)  ·  `page_008319`

## Facts

- The Georgia Manufacturing Alliance (GMA) partnered with UK-based Trans-Global Events to host the Greener Manufacturing Show on June 8-9, 2022.
- The event took place at the Cobb Galleria in Atlanta, GA, and is described as North America's leading event for environmental and sustainable manufacturing solutions.
- The Greener Manufacturing Show brought together more than 400 sustainability leaders from various industrial sectors to explore industry innovations, market trends, regulatory updates, and optimizing manufacturing processes.
- The event showcased the latest R&D in green materials, green chemicals, and greener manufacturing facilities of the future.
- Three dedicated conference tracks targeted sustainability priority areas for manufacturing companies, including innovations in sustainable materials, new greener chemicals, targeting net-zero operations, and tackling waste plastic and other valuable resources.
- Delegates had access to all three conferences as well as post-conference proceedings.
- Several Georgia Manufacturing Alliance members exhibited at the event.
- Stuart Countess, President & Chief Executive Officer of Kia Georgia, Inc., spoke on June 8 at 9:25 am.
- The event targeted sustainability-focused companies looking to design and manufacture products from more sustainable materials, limit and eliminate toxic chemicals, and reduce the environmental impact of industrial processes.
- Jason Moss, CEO of the Georgia Manufacturing Alliance, stated they were excited to co-host the event to showcase sustainability success stories in the Georgia manufacturing community and allow industry leaders to network and learn best practices.
- The exhibit hall was free but required registration, while conference tickets were required for educational sessions.
- Conference attendees gained access to more than 80 speakers and all three conference sessions alongside the co-located Plastic Waste Free World conference.
- GMA members were eligible for special conference pricing.
- The Georgia Manufacturing Alliance is a membership-based industry organization founded in 2008 to support Georgia's manufacturing community through monthly plant tours, educational sessions, tradeshows, and networking opportunities.
