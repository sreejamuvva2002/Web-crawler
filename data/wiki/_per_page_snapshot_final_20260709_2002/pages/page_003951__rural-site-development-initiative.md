# Rural Site Development Initiative

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-01-30

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp Announces Third Round of Rural Site Development Grants | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-releases/2026/gov-kemp-announces-third-round-rural-site-development-grants)  ·  `page_003951`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced the third round of grants from the Rural Site Development Initiative (RSDI) within the OneGeorgia Authority on January 30, 2026.
- The latest RSDI allotment includes $4.3 million for rural site development projects.
- The total investment committed to rural site development projects since the program’s launch in FY 2025 is up to $21 million.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Georgia is committed to bringing good-paying jobs to every part of the state and views the RSDI as a tool to bring opportunity to every zip code.
- Governor Kemp credited the partnership of the General Assembly and state agency leadership for the initiative's generational impact on rural communities.
- RSDI grants are awarded for either site improvement or to assist with costs of seeking Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) certification on approved projects critical to economic development.
- State funding for these grants is matched by participating communities at various levels based on program criteria.
- Lt. Governor Burt Jones emphasized that workforce development and economic growth are essential for rural communities where new opportunities are emerging daily.
- Speaker Jon Burns stated that OneGeorgia’s Rural Site Development grants represent the state’s commitment to uplifting rural communities, strengthening economic competitiveness, and creating pathways to prosperity in every corner of Georgia.
- Department of Community Affairs Commissioner Christopher Nunn said helping keep Georgia’s communities ready for investment is essential for their long-term success and that DCA works alongside communities to ensure their vision of the future.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that development-ready sites are a must-have to be competitive in key industries such as logistics and manufacturing, and that partnering through GRAD certification and RSDI programs allows marketing every corner of the state for opportunities.
- The OneGeorgia Authority scores and votes on eligible grant awards for the Rural Site Development Initiative.
- Camden County Joint Development Authority in coastal Georgia was awarded $2 million for site readiness improvements, including construction of an entry road, at the Coastal Georgia Commerce Park.
- The total project costs for the Camden County site readiness improvements are $7.3 million.
- Toombs County Development Authority in south Georgia was awarded $2 million for site readiness improvements at the 599-acre, GRAD-certified One Toombs Industrial Park.
- The total project costs for the Toombs County improvements are $2.7 million.
- Brantley County Development Authority in southeast Georgia was awarded $45,000 to certify the 103-acre Satilla Industrial Park.
- The total project costs for the Brantley County certification are $49,500.
- Candler County Industrial Authority in south Georgia was awarded $103,500 to renew certification for and expand the Metter-Candler I-16 Industrial Park.
- The total project costs for the Candler County expansion and renewal are $115,000.
- Rome-Floyd Development Authority in northwest Georgia was awarded $20,625 to certify the 200-acre Enterprise Corner Industrial Park site.
- The total project costs for the Rome-Floyd certification are $27,500.
- Waycross-Ware County Development Authority in southeast Georgia was awarded $161,500 to certify the 391-acre Waycross and Ware County Airport Industrial Park.
- The total project costs for the Waycross-Ware County certification are $188,900.
- Eligible activities for grant funds include site studies, land grading, and other essential community infrastructure efforts supporting site development.
- Grantees demonstrated substantive plans for industrial site development while meeting OneGeorgia Authority eligibility requirements.
- Use of funds is subject to all applicable state laws and regulations, as well as policies and requirements of the OneGeorgia Authority and Department of Community Affairs.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) serves as the state’s sales and marketing arm and lead agency for attracting new business investment and encouraging industry expansion.
- GDEcD is responsible for locating new markets for Georgia products, promoting tourism, and planning and mobilizing state resources for economic development.
- The Georgia Department of Community Affairs (DCA) partners with communities to build strong neighborhoods, commercial and industrial areas through community and economic development, local government assistance, and housing initiatives.
- DCA uses state and federal resources to spur private job creation, implement planning, develop downtowns, generate affordable housing solutions, and promote volunteerism.
