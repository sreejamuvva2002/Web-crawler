# Georgia Data Center Infrastructure & Providers

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Data Centers in Georgia for Colocation (Updated 2025)](https://brightlio.com/data-centers-in-georgia/)  ·  `page_060241`

## Facts

- QTS Atlanta 1 Campus features electric vehicle (EV) charging stations available on-site.
- QTS Suwanee 1 Campus offers EV charging spots for visitors and employees.
- Georgia Power’s 2025 Integrated Resource Plan includes planned battery storage installations as part of its renewable energy expansion.
- Georgia Power plans to expand total renewable resources to about 11,000 megawatts by 2035, including utility-scale solar and rooftop solar programs.
- QTS signed a 20-year agreement with Georgia Power to procure nearly 350 megawatts of renewable energy for its Atlanta-Metro and Suwanee campuses.
- Switch partnered with Georgia Power to use 100% renewable energy from local sources for its Keep Campus in Atlanta.
- QTS Fayetteville campus ("Project Excalibur") aims to operate on 100% carbon-free electricity once completed.
- Georgia’s primary utility, Georgia Power, offers access to a mix of natural gas, nuclear, coal, and renewable energy.
- Surging demand from sectors like data centers is expected to add 8,200 megawatts of new load over six years.
- Georgia Power delayed previously scheduled retirements of Plant Bowen and Plant Scherer coal plants into the 2030s to maintain grid reliability amid surging power demand.
- Georgia offers sales and use tax exemptions for high-tech data centers meeting investment and job creation thresholds, available through December 31, 2031.
- To qualify for tax exemptions in counties over 50,000 people, facilities must meet a $250 million investment and 25 new jobs threshold.
- For counties with 30,000 to 50,000 people, the tax exemption requirement is $150 million and 10 jobs.
- For counties under 30,000, the tax exemption requirement is $100 million and 5 jobs.
- Governor Brian Kemp vetoed a two-year pause on new data center tax exemptions in 2024, warning it would hurt investment.
- House Bill 696, known as the "Switch Bill," provides a 0% sales and use tax rate for Switch’s Keep Campus in Atlanta.
