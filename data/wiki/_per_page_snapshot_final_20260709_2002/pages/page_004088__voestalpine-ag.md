# voestalpine AG

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2014-04-22

**Source:** [voestalpine is Producing Ultra-High Strength Automotive Components in the USA](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/voestalpine-is-producing-ultra-high-strength-automotive-components-in-the-usa-256163991.html)  ·  `page_004088`

## Facts

- voestalpine Group's 17th production site in the United States began operation on April 22, 2014.
- voestalpine Automotive Body Parts Inc., a subsidiary of the Metal Forming Division, manufactures ultra-high strength body-in-white components for premium car manufacturers in Cartersville, Georgia.
- The Cartersville facility required an investment of EUR 50 million (about USD 70 million).
- The new plant will create 220 jobs and employ 220 persons when fully operational.
- This site completes the first phase of voestalpine's extensive internationalization program in the automotive sector.
- Since 2012, voestalpine has invested over EUR 100 million (USD 140 million) in new plants across China, Germany, Romania, South Africa, and the USA.
- Additional plants and expanded capacity are currently in the planning stage for the group.
- New facilities and expanded production capacity have recently gone into operation near well-known premium car manufacturers on four continents.
- CEO Wolfgang Eder stated the company is following particularly European automotive manufacturers into future markets to massively expand its presence there.
- Wolfgang Eder noted that new materials allow the production of components with unique global selling points and that development in processing is only beginning, prompting an accelerated internationalization program.
- In the 2012/13 business year, voestalpine generated 44 percent of its EUR 11.5 billion (USD 15.8 billion) revenue in the mobility area.
- Approximately two-thirds of the mobility area revenue came from the automotive industry.
- President Obama lauded voestalpine's U.S. activities as one of the top foreign investments in the USA during a speech in October 2013.
- Cartersville is centrally located in the southern automotive cluster close to customers, about 60 kilometers (37 miles) northwest of Atlanta, Georgia.
- Herbert Eibensteiner, member of the voestalpine AG Management Board and head of the Metal Forming Division, confirmed the location's strategic proximity to customers and the 220-person employment goal.
- The facility's initial customer base will be European, primarily German automotive manufacturers.
- voestalpine has secured several long-term contracts for sophisticated automotive components with well-known manufacturers.
- Herbert Eibensteiner stated that their components are in high demand by all automotive manufacturers worldwide.
- The U.S. market is strategically important to achieve a goal of increasing voestalpine's North American Group revenue to EUR 3 billion (USD 3.9 billion) by 2020, according to CEO Wolfgang Eder.
- The Metal Forming Division contributes about one-fifth of the Group's total revenue, with complex automotive components making up about half of that division's output.
- Several European and Asian automotive manufacturers have selected southern U.S. locations for their production sites.
- Cartersville will serve as the center of voestalpine's North American automotive activities regarding maximum site efficiency.
- The new plant will assemble phs-ultraform components and stamped press parts, with future production of laser-welded blanks and other spare parts for OEMs (Original Equipment Manufacturers).
- The Metal Forming Division generated EUR 2.3 billion in revenue and EUR 258 million EBITDA in 2012/13, employs around 11,000 people, and produces on behalf of almost all European automotive manufacturers.
- The division is a leading global provider of high-quality metal processing solutions including special tubes/sections, precision strip steel, and complex components.
- The Metal Forming Division operates five business units: Tubes & Sections, Automotive Body Parts, Precision Strip, Material Handling, and Flamco.
- Tubes & Sections is a leading global manufacturer of highest quality tubes, profiles, and precision steel tube products.
- Automotive Body Parts is a direct supplier of innovative, high quality pressed parts in the body-in-white segment to the automotive industry.
- Precision Strip manufactures cold-rolled precision strip steel with a high degree of dimensional stability and excellent surface qualities.
- Material Handling offers sophisticated product solutions in the high-bay warehouses and system racks segments.
- Flamco produces high quality components for heating and portable water installations with a focus on the Benelux countries market.
- voestalpine AG is a steel-based technology and capital goods group with around 500 companies in over 50 countries, listed on the Vienna Stock Exchange since 1995.
- The Group reported EUR 11.5 billion revenue and EUR 1.45 billion EBITDA in 2012/13, with approximately 46,400 employees worldwide.
- voestalpine AG is the world market leader in turnout technology, special rails, tool steel, and special sections.
