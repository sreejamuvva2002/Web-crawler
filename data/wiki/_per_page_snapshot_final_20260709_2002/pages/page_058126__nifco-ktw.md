# NIFCO KTW

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2015-06-10

**Source:** [Hummingbird A Go; German Company Locating in Toccoa - WNEG](https://wnegradio.com/hummingbird-a-go-german-company-locating-in-toccoa/)  ·  `page_058126`

## Facts

- Governor Nathan Deal’s office announced that NIFCO KTW, a German-based automotive supplier of high-quality injection molded components, will locate its U.S. operations in Stephens County.
- The company approved a project agreement for “Project Hummingbird” to establish its first North American headquarters.
- The facility will be a 130,000 square foot building on 32 acres in the Hayestone-Brady Business Park, located on Highway 17 between Toccoa and Martin.
- Over the next five years, the company plans to invest $27 million and create 200 jobs.
- Officials stated that more than half of the 200 jobs will be created in the first year of operations.
- NIFCO KTW supplies automotive manufacturers around the world, and the Toccoa plant will supply parts to BMW and Mercedes Benz USA.
- Stephens County Commission Chair Dennis Bell welcomed the company, stating it is great news for the community and exactly what citizens needed.
- Toccoa Mayor Terry Carter stated the addition of the company will invigorate the economy and put Stephens County on the map for future manufacturing prospects.
- Mayor Carter credited the state’s strong support and incentives for ensuring the area remained a leading competitor during site selection, and noted local leadership stepped outside customary parameters to secure the project.
- The city committed to spending about $300,000 to run water, wastewater, and gas to the company’s building at no cost to NIFCO KTW.
- The county provided 25 years of full property tax relief, though the county is not currently collecting tax on the property because it is owned by the Development Authority.
- The company is qualifying for state job creation tax credits.
- Stephens County Development Authority Chairman Brian Akin called the project a huge win for the local job market and praised local leadership for working through the recruitment process.
- Toccoa-Stephens County Chamber Board Chair Greg Roach pledged to work with the company in any capacity possible to help them acclimate to the local area.
- Governor Deal expressed that he looks forward to the high-quality jobs NIFCO KTW will bring to the region.
- The project agreement required the company to begin constructing its building later in 2015 with completion expected in 2016.
- Stephens County Development Authority Executive Director Tim Martin stated the company could come online in the second half of 2016.
- The company has plans to expand its Toccoa site in the future, and land is available at Hayestone-Brady for that expansion.
