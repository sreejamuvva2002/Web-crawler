# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hyundai_Motor_Group_Metaplant_America)  ·  `page_000410`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) is an electric vehicle production site operated by Hyundai located in Ellabell, Bryan County, Georgia, United States.
- The facility was built between 2022 and 2024 and began operations in October 2024.
- The $7.6billion campus is located along Interstate16 about 25 miles west of downtown Savannah.
- The site contains over 16million square feet of factory floor space across 2,284 acres.
- The address is 1500 Genesis Drive Ellabell, Georgia, U.S.
- The owner is Hyundai Motor Group.
- The company will produce batteries in a joint venture with LG Energy Solution at a separate site now under construction.
- The land was previously designated by Bryan County as the Bryan County Mega Site.
- Volvo considered the area for a new manufacturing plant in 2015 but ultimately built a factory in South Carolina instead.
- In 2021, the state of Georgia, Bryan and Chatham counties, and the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority purchased the area for $61million, combining three separately-owned parcels.
- The site lies directly off Interstate16, with existing ClassI rail links and close access to Interstate 95 and the Port of Savannah.
- Discussions between Hyundai and government officials began in January 2022.
- Hyundai announced the factory in April 2022.
- Construction on the site began in October 2022.
- A groundbreaking ceremony was held on October25, attended by Hyundai CEO and chairman Chung Eui-sun, Georgia governor Brian Kemp, U.S. senators Jon Ossoff and Raphael Warnock, U.S. representative Buddy Carter, and deputy Secretary of Commerce Don Graves.
- Hyundai announced it would establish both the automotive and battery manufacturing facilities prior to the introduction of the Inflation Reduction Act and stated that the law had not influenced the company's decisions.
- Full production at HMGMA began in October 2024, with the first model produced being the 2025 Ioniq5.
- Hyundai plans to offer tours of the factory to the public.
- Immigration authorities raided the site on September4, 2025, detaining about 450 workers, most of them South Korean nationals.
- The United States Department of Homeland Security described the raid as the largest immigration enforcement operation carried out at a single location.
- The detained workers had been involved with the construction of a battery plant to be operated in partnership with LG Energy Solution.
- The raid prompted diplomatic concern from the South Korean government and delayed production by two to three months.
- LG stated that its employees and those from its partnering companies had been taken into custody.
- Hyundai said that none of those detained were its employees.
- HMGMA and its supporting factories have been called the largest economic development project in Georgia history.
- The automobile assembly plant represents a $7.6billion investment by Hyundai.
- The plant employed over 1,400 people as of October 2024, with plans to hire 8,100 by 2031.
- Around 3,200 people worked at the factory as of October 2025.
- The site includes a $4.3billion battery manufacturing facility called the HL-GA Battery Company, operated as a joint venture between Hyundai and LG Energy Solution.
- In the surrounding area, 17 suppliers have announced new factories to support HMGMA, which combined will invest an additional estimated total of $2.7billion and employ another 7,000 workers.
- Supporting manufacturers include DAS Corporation (metal seat frames), Doowon Climate Control America, Hanon Systems, PHA, Seoyon E-HWA, and Woory Industrial.
- The Georgia Department of Transportation has planned infrastructure upgrades including widening US280, constructing frontage roads along I-16, and expanding the interchange at Old Cuyler Road.
- Hyundai calls the plant a Metaplant and its employees Meta Pros.
- According to Hyundai, the factory uses autonomous vehicles instead of conveyor belts to move vehicle bodies.
- Hyundai expects around 70% of completed vehicles to be shipped by rail via the Georgia Central Railway, and constructed an additional rail siding to handle the volume of autorack cars.
- Logistical solutions for the factory are handled by Hyundai GLOVIS EV Logistics America, a subsidiary of Hyundai Glovis.
- The water tower at the factory site along I-16 features artwork by students from Savannah College of Art and Design.
- Hyundai plans to reach full production of over 500,000 vehicles per year at HMGMA.
- Vehicles produced include the Hyundai Ioniq5 (2024–present), Hyundai Ioniq9 (2024–present), and Kia Sportage Hybrid (2026–present).
- A 2024 investigation by Savannah television station WTOC11 found that during construction from April 2023 to July 2024, 53 injuries were reported with 14 described as traumatic and at least one worker died.
- The Occupational Safety and Health Administration opened more than a dozen investigations, and multiple subcontractors were sued or fined for safety violations.
- In March 2025, a man was killed by a forklift driver, and in May 2025, another worker was crushed to death.
- In October 2024, the Georgia Environmental Protection Division approved withdrawals of 6.625million U.S. gallons of water per day from the Floridan aquifer and allowed for the drilling of four new wells in Bulloch County.
- Bryan County is legally required to implement an alternative water source for HMGMA within 25 years as a temporary solution.
