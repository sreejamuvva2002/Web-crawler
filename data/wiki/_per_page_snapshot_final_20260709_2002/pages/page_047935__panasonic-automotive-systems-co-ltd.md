# Panasonic Automotive Systems Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** supplier_relationship  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Panasonic Automotive Systems Earns EcoVadis Bronze Medal](https://industrialsustainabilitymonthly.com/panasonic-automotive-systems-earns-ecovadis-bronze-medal/)  ·  `page_047935`

## Facts

- Panasonic Automotive Systems is a global Tier 1 automotive supplier.
- The company achieved a Bronze Medal in the sustainability assessment conducted by EcoVadis, an international organization headquartered in France that evaluates corporate sustainability.
- The Bronze Medal recognition is granted to companies ranked within the top 35 percent of all organizations assessed.
- EcoVadis evaluates the sustainability performance of more than 150,000 companies across 185 countries.
- Assessments cover four categories: Environment, Labor & Human Rights, Ethics, and Sustainable Procurement.
- Scores are on a 100-point scale based on 21 indicators across these themes.
- In this assessment cycle, Panasonic Automotive Systems received the Bronze Medal, placing the company within the top 35 percent overall.
- The automotive industry faces rising environmental, social, and governance (ESG) expectations due to its ecological footprint and complex global supply chains.
- Companies are expected to address social responsibility issues including human rights, working conditions, ethical sourcing of minerals, coexistence with local communities, and supply chain safety.
- Governance structures must support implementation of these initiatives across group operations while strengthening risk management.
- ESG initiatives have become a key driver of long-term sustainability and competitiveness in the automotive sector.
- Panasonic Automotive Systems’ efforts in the Sustainable Procurement category were highly recognized.
- The evaluation cited the company’s promotion of responsible procurement across its supply chain, including conflict mineral management and due diligence using the Conflict Minerals Reporting Template (CMRT).
- The evaluation also cited systematic management of environmental and social considerations.
- Overall, the company’s policies, initiatives, and support for international standards were assessed as being extremely strong.
- Efforts in Environment, Labor & Human Rights, and Sustainable Procurement were acknowledged as demonstrating a high level of execution and due diligence.
- Recognition against international standards marks a significant step toward achieving the company’s mission to “create a sustainable mobility society to promote a better life for everyone.”
- The company stated it will continue to strengthen sustainability initiatives toward realizing a sustainable mobility society.
- Hisayoshi Nakao serves as Director and Executive Officer in charge of ESG Promotion at Panasonic Automotive Systems Co., Ltd.
- Hisayoshi Nakao said the assessment provided a valuable opportunity to reassess sustainability efforts from an international perspective and that the company will use it as a foundation to further improve initiatives and contribute to industry sustainable development.
- The company remains committed to accountability and transparency in business practices through dialogue with stakeholders, starting with shareholders and customers.
- By acting swiftly, fairly, and with integrity, the company aims to boost its corporate value further.
- Panasonic Automotive Systems Co., Ltd. is headquartered in Yokohama, Kanagawa, Japan.
- The company operates globally with subsidiaries in eight other countries.
- As a Tier 1 automotive supplier, it provides advanced proprietary technologies, including infotainment systems, to automakers in Japan and overseas.
- Panasonic Automotive Systems has announced it will change its company name and brand to Mobitera Inc., effective April 1, 2027.
