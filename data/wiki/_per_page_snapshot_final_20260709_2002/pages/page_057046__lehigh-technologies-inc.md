# Lehigh Technologies Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2012-08-21

**Source:** [Lehigh Technologies Raises $16M](https://vcnewsdaily.com/lehigh-technologies/venture-capital-funding/zcmyxrrqck)  ·  `page_057046`

## Facts

- Lehigh Technologies Inc. is based in Tucker, Georgia.
- The company announced the completion of $16 million in financing on August 21, 2012.
- The financing was led by Leaf Clean Energy and included investments from Kleiner Perkins Caufield Byers, Index Ventures, and NGP Energy Technology Partners.
- The funding will be used to expand the company's geographic reach and research and development initiatives for its micronized rubber powder (MRP) technology.
- Lehigh's MRP technology transforms end-of-life tire and post-industrial rubber into new materials.
- These materials are incorporated into high-performance tires, consumer and industrial plastics goods, asphalt, coatings, and construction materials.
- The MRPs serve as a lower cost, sustainable replacement for higher priced synthetic polymers and associated rubber compounds.
- Lehigh's customer base includes the largest tire companies in the world.
- Over 140 million tires have been manufactured using Lehigh's MRP.
- CEO Dr. Alan Barton stated that support from financing partners enables continued growth and major advancements in MRP technology.
- The company is working with customers to support higher levels of MRP use and developing new end uses for the sustainable material.
- Ellen Pao of Kleiner Perkins Caufield Byers, who serves as a board member of Lehigh Technologies, noted that the investment highlights the company's ability to deliver technology-based innovation and create new market opportunities.
- Leaf Clean Energy Vice President Matthew Fedors stated that industrial customers are motivated by sustainable materials that do not sacrifice quality, safety, cost, or performance.
- Lehigh uses energy efficient cryogenic turbo mill technology to break down raw materials into micron scale powders.
- The company is privately held.
- Leaf Clean Energy is a renewable energy and sustainable technology investment firm listed on the London Stock Exchange's Alternative Investment Market under the symbol LEAF.
- Kleiner Perkins Caufield Byers was founded in 1972 and focuses investments in digital, greentech, and life sciences from offices in Silicon Valley, Beijing, and Shanghai.
