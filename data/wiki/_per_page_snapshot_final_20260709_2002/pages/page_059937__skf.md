# SKF

## Facts

- SKF is a manufacturer of bearings, seals, and lubrication systems.
- The global bearing market is valued at nearly SEK 500 billion (well over $52 billion).
- SKF holds about 20% market share in the bearing industry.
- SKF competes with Schaeffler, The Timken Company, NSK, NTN, and JTEKT.
- Growth in the bearing sector is driven by demand from automotive, aerospace, industrial sectors, renewable energy, electric vehicles (EVs), and smart infrastructure.
- More than 90% of SKF’s innovation portfolio is directed towards high-growth segments where sustainability and electrification megatrends drive demand.
- SKF announced the separation of its automotive business from its industrial business to focus on distinct competencies and resource allocation in each market.
- SKF President and CEO Rickard Gustafson confirmed that the separation provides strategic flexibility for independent business decisions and investments.
- SKF engineers use algorithms to predict machinery performance over a bearing's lifespan by modeling physics and converting operational details into predictive maintenance models.
- SKF prioritizes digitalization, AI, quantum-inspired methods, and sustainability while integrating sophisticated predictive technologies.
- Integrating sensors and condition-monitoring technology into digital products differentiates SKF from competitors and creates opportunities for new markets.
- SKF has developed customer tools over the past 15 years including configurators for bearing selection, calculation software for engineering, and digital twins for gearbox or powertrain models.
- SKF introduced AI tools that answer technical questions instantly via a generative AI interface based on published technical information.
- SKF uses AI internally for smart lead generation and customer data handling in sales, design automation and virtual testing in product development, and optimizing manufacturing processes and logistics in operations and supply chain planning.
- SKF's digital product assistant guides users through product specifications, detailed engineering data, and comparisons based on years of engineering application knowledge.
- Virtual validation supported by computational power is expected to reduce testing capacity by 50% over the next five years.
- Bearings can now last 5 million kilometers between maintenance stops instead of 1 million kilometers.
- SKF collaborated with high-precision machine tools manufacturer DMG Mori to develop the Insight super-precision bearing system for monitoring load, temperature, and vibration in a high-speed spindle during machining.
- Through the collaboration with DMG Mori, lubrication control was improved, exact load measurements were delivered, closed-loop control maximized performance, and failures were predicted well in advance.
- SKF's expertise spans materials selection from steel to ceramics for electric vehicle capabilities.
- SKF ensures its bearings can run electric vehicles that require electric insulation without breaking down.
