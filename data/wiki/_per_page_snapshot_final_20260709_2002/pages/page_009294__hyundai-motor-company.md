## Facts

- United States immigration authorities arrested 475 people in a raid on a Hyundai manufacturing site in Georgia.
- Steve Schrank, special agent in charge of Homeland Security Investigations Atlanta, announced the arrests at a press conference on Friday.
- The Hyundai facility is located in Ellabell, Georgia, approximately 30 miles west of Savannah.
- The raid was conducted as part of an ongoing criminal investigation into allegations of unlawful employment practices and other serious federal crimes.
- The raid was authorized by a judge-issued search warrant following a monthslong investigation of the facility.
- The investigation of the worksite remains active, but no charges of wrongdoing have been filed.
- The arrested individuals worked for several contractors as well as the parent company, Hyundai.
- Steve Schrank stated that this was the largest single-site enforcement operation in the history of Homeland Security Investigations.
- Hyundai released a statement saying it was aware of the incident and closely monitoring the situation while working to understand the specific circumstances.
- Hyundai stated that none of those detained were directly employed by Hyundai Motor Company.
- South Korea's foreign ministry conveyed concerns and regret to the United States over the raid on a Hyundai-LG battery plant.
- A spokesperson for South Korea's foreign ministry urged special attention to ensure that the legitimate rights and interests of Korean citizens are not violated.
- The spokesperson emphasized that the economic activities of Korean companies investing in the U.S. and the rights and interests of their nationals must not be unfairly violated.
- Consular officials were sent to the plant, and local diplomatic missions were instructed to set up a task force to address issues related to the raid.
- A DHS spokesperson stated that the investigation is focused on ensuring accountability for those who violate the law and upholding the rule of law.
