# Mercedes-Benz USA

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2018-03-15

**Source:** [Mercedes-Benz USA Celebrates Grand Opening of New Headquarters](https://media.mbusa.com/releases/release-e38709a1cc517079cb36cfdf371550de-mercedes-benz-usa-celebrates-grand-opening-of-new-headquarters)  ·  `page_046320`

## Facts

- Mercedes-Benz USA (MBUSA) held the official grand opening of its new headquarters in Sandy Springs, 14 miles outside of the heart of Atlanta.
- The new headquarters building is 200,000 square feet and sits on a 73-acre site, described as a 12-acre campus near Georgia 400 at the intersection of Mercedes-Benz Drive (formerly Barfield Road) and Abernathy Road.
- The facility has a capacity for more than 1,000 employees.
- MBUSA president and CEO Dietmar Exler stated that the new Atlanta headquarters marks a highpoint for Mercedes-Benz in the U.S. market as the leading luxury brand for the past two years.
- The building was designed from the inside out with a 1:1 ratio of collaborative seating to individual workstations to encourage interaction and creativity.
- The headquarters is divided into North and South Work Bars joined by an indoor Atrium, with corridors called Bridges on the second and third floors.
- On-site amenities include a conference center, a coffee bar with a barista, a restaurant-quality café emphasizing locally sourced ingredients, a 5,000 square foot fitness center with trainers, an on-site child care facility with four classrooms and an outdoor playground, and a furnished roof deck for outdoor collaboration.
- The building incorporates environmentally-friendly features targeting LEED Silver Certification, including water reduction using drip irrigation and energy-efficient mechanical systems.
- MBUSA is partnering with Second Helpings Atlanta Inc. to eliminate food waste by rescuing surplus food from Metro Atlanta organizations.
- To reduce traffic impact and align with sustainability commitments, MBUSA implemented alternative transportation initiatives including flex scheduling, a dedicated MARTA shuttle for employees, bike racks, and electric vehicle charging stations.
- Construction of the new headquarters required 425,000 man hours and more than 1,000 workers.
- The building consists of 14,000 cubic yards of concrete, 1,500 tons of steel, and 1.6 acres of glass.
- More than 780 trees were planted around the site, with a nature trail encircling the building to provide an outdoor campus feel.
- Georgia Governor Nathan Deal praised Mercedes-Benz for choosing Georgia as its headquarters and acknowledged the company's community service projects.
- During the ceremony, Daimler vice president of sales Axel Harries announced plans to establish a global innovation hub called Lab1886 in Atlanta.
- Lab1886 will operate as an incubator-type entity combining corporate expertise with startup spirit and is scheduled to open in the summer of 2018.
- The Atlanta Lab1886 location will be the fourth globally, joining existing locations in Beijing, Berlin, and Stuttgart.
- Axel Harries cited Atlanta's excellent colleges and universities offering highly skilled graduates, along with a growing number of innovative technology and digital companies, as reasons for the selection.
- Atlanta Mayor Keisha Lance Bottoms expressed excitement about the headquarters and the Lab1886 announcement, highlighting partnerships between corporations, nonprofits, and government in the city.
- MBUSA is involved with over 50 organizations to address local community needs, including efforts in Atlanta's Westside area surrounding the Mercedes-Benz Stadium.
- Sandy Springs Mayor Rusty Paul noted that Mercedes-Benz has been a great partner to the community already.
- Mercedes-Benz USA (MBUSA) is headquartered in Atlanta and is responsible for the distribution, marketing, and customer service for all Mercedes-Benz products in the United States.
- MBUSA offers 15 model lines ranging from the CLA-Class four-door coupe to the flagship S-Class and the Mercedes-AMG GT R.
- MBUSA is also responsible for Mercedes-Benz Vans and smart products in the U.S. market.
