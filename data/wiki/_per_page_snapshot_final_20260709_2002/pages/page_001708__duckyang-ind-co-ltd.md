# Duckyang Ind. Co. Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2021-05-25

**Source:** [Korean Supplier Duckyang to Invest $10M in Jackson County, Hiring 285 - Global Atlanta](https://www.globalatlanta.com/korean-supplier-duckyang-to-invest-10m-in-jackson-county-hiring-285/)  ·  `page_001708`

## Facts

- Korean auto supplier Duckyang is set to invest $10 million to put a facility in Jackson County near the massive SK Battery factory.
- Korean President Moon Jae-in visited the SK Battery factory last weekend relative to the article's publication date.
- Duckyang plans to hire 285 at the site in Braselton.
- The Braselton facility will supply battery modules and storage systems to SK.
- State officials say Duckyang's move demonstrates how the $2.6 billion SK investment will compound on itself through a parade of suppliers.
- SK Battery's plant already promises 2,600 jobs in its own right.
- Duckyang already serves Korean automotive manufacturers like Kia, Hyundai, Renault Samsung and Genesis.
- Duckyang supplies cockpit modules composed of 130 subcomponents around the drivers’ seat of the vehicle.
- The SK deal is bringing Duckyang physically into North America.
- Duckyang will pursue additional opportunities with current partners in North America.
- CEO Dong-in Son stated that it is expected they will have business expansion opportunities to supply not only electric vehicle parts but also interior parts to global automakers based in the United States.
- The interior parts expected to be supplied include the cockpit module, door trim panel module, and other flagship parts.
- The Duckyang announcement came just days before the presidential visit.
- Hyundai Motor Group announced a $7.4 billion investment in the U.S. to electrify more of its vehicle fleet and add other alternative-energy offerings.
- Hyundai Motor Group's investment could include massive investments in Hyundai and Kia factories in Alabama and Georgia, respectively.
- The carefully worded release from Hyundai Motor Group did not specify where exactly the funds would flow.
- Duckyang Industrial was established in 1977 in Korea.
- Duckyang has grown globally through a partnership with Visteon, an automotive supplier spun off from Ford in 1999.
- Duckyang focuses heavily on research and development as it aims to expand market share worldwide.
- As of 2017 Duckyang's revenues eclipsed $800 million.
- Duckyang had two plants in China along with its head office and factory in Ulsan, Korea.
