# Bosch

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-01-16

**Source:** [Bosch to invest over $1 billion in e-mobility capacities in China - electrive.com](https://www.electrive.com/2023/01/16/bosch-to-invest-over-1-billion-in-e-mobility-capacities-in-china/)  ·  `page_018045`

## Facts

- Bosch announced an investment of around one billion US dollars over several years in the development and production of components for electromobility and automated driving in China.
- The investment will fund a new centre for research, development and production in Suzhou.
- The subsidiary Bosch Automotive Products will be responsible for building the facility at the existing Bosch site in Suzhou, west of Shanghai.
- The new centre is titled “Bosch R&D and Manufacturing Base for New Energy Vehicles Core Components and Automated Driving”.
- The facility will cover 300,000 square metres.
- The centre will focus primarily on electrified drive products with the latest SiC power modules, integrated braking systems and solutions for automated driving.
- The first phase of construction is scheduled to be completed by mid-2024.
- The investment sum is also equivalent to around seven billion yuan or 950 million euros.
- According to the German publication Handelsblatt, this is the second-largest single investment in Bosch's history after the chip factory in Dresden.
- Stefan Hartung, Chairman of the Board of Bosch who also oversees Bosch’s China business on the board of management, stated that as a multinational enterprise, Bosch needs to make full use of China’s local R&D capability and production capacity.
- Hartung added that through continuous development in China, Bosch will further expand its global competitiveness and create a solid foundation for the future of mobility.
- Since Bosch Suzhou was founded in 1999, Bosch has continuously expanded its investments in the city and deepened cooperation with local partners.
- According to Handelsblatt, 10,000 people are currently employed at four plants in Suzhou.
- Bosch emphasizes that it is leading the transformation of the entire car industry in Suzhou into a modern sector.
- Yudong Chen, president of Bosch China, stated the investment will enable the company to better respond to the fast-changing and demanding demand of the local market.
- Bosch maintains a “local for local” development strategy to deepen roots in the local market and support China’s path to future electrified and intelligent mobility.
- The Handelsblatt reported concerns about a threatening one-sided dependence on China, noting that silicon carbide power modules are particularly delicate.
- Bosch is the only automotive supplier to build silicon carbide chips itself.
- With Bosch’s power electronics, Chinese electric car manufacturers will become even more powerful and dominant in their home market.
- German carmakers have been losing significant market share in the Chinese market, especially in electric cars.
- Bosch recorded strong annual increases in revenues in electromobility, registering an order volume of more than ten billion euros in the e-mobility sector for the first time in 2021.
- Figures for 2022 are not yet available.
- In addition to drive solutions, Bosch sees a particularly lucrative market in hydrogen components.
- In May 2022, Bosch announced its intention to enter the development of components for electrolysers.
- Bosch has never entered the battery cell market.
