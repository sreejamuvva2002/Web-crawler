# Freyr Battery

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-03-31

**Source:** [Freyr Battery cancels $2.6 billion Georgia battery factory plans](https://www.greencarreports.com/news/1145697_freyr-battery-cancels-2-6-billion-georgia-battery-factory-plans)  ·  `page_001604`

## Facts

- Norwegian battery firm Freyr has canceled plans for a Georgia factory that would have supplied batteries for energy storage.
- The cancellation was first reported by the Newman Times-Herald of Newnan, Georgia.
- Freyr confirmed plans to cancel the factory in a letter to the local Coweta County Development Authority dated Jan. 21.
- Freyr also confirmed the cancellation during a Thursday meeting with the authority.
- Freyr told the Georgia Department of Community Affairs that it would repay grants and incentives tied to the factory project.
- The factory was announced in November 2022.
- The proposed facility was branded "Giga America".
- The factory would have sat on a 368-acre site in Coweta County.
- Coweta County is located on the southwestern edge of the Atlanta metropolitan area.
- Production at the facility was due to be stepped up in phases.
- Phase one involved a $1.7 billion investment to build out 34 gigawatt-hours of annual production capacity.
- A second phase was planned to add more cell production lines.
- The second phase would bring the total project investment to $2.6 billion by 2029.
- Rising interest rates contributed to the decision to cancel the factory.
- Falling battery prices contributed to the decision to cancel the factory.
- A change in leadership at Freyr contributed to the decision to cancel the factory.
- Jason Peace, the company's senior vice president of business development, cited these factors in an interview with The Newman-Times Herald.
- Freyr reported a net loss of $27.5 million in its third-quarter 2024 financial results.
- Freyr reported a net loss of $9.8 million for the same period in 2023.
- Peace told authority members that Freyr had been burning cash to get the battery plant built.
- Freyr is now looking at a nearly-finished solar-panel plant in Texas as an alternative, quicker way to generate revenue.
- Rivian continues to move ahead with plans for an EV assembly plant about an hour from downtown Atlanta.
- Rivian's plant was announced in 2021 and originally scheduled to open in 2024.
- Rivian paused construction on its Georgia plant, pushing the opening first to 2027 and then to 2028.
- A $6.6 billion conditional loan from the Department of Energy may help complete Rivian's plant.
