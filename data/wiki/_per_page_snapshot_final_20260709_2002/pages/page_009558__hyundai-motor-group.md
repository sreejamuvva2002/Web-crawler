# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Group’s Georgia Plant To Use Solar Energy For EV Production | EV.com](https://ev.com/news/hyundai-motor-groups-georgia-plant-to-use-solar-energy-for-ev-production&blogId=1991)  ·  `page_009558`

## Facts

- Hyundai will purchase up to 147 MW of solar energy from Matrix Renewables' Stillhouse Solar Project.
- Hyundai Motor Group announced plans to power its upcoming Georgia Metaplant with solar energy for EV production.
- Hyundai signed a 15-year power purchase agreement (PPA) with Matrix Renewables to provide up to 147 MW of energy from the Stillhouse Solar Project.
- The agreement highlights Hyundai's dedication to reducing its carbon footprint and adopting renewable energy solutions for EV production.
- The Stillhouse Solar Project is expected to start commercial operations by October 2025.
- Hyundai anticipates receiving around 378 GWh of solar electricity annually, which will reduce carbon emissions by 140,000 tons per year.
- The solar energy will also power Hyundai's operations at three affiliated plants: Hyundai Mobis North America Electrified Powertrain, Hyundai Steel Georgia, and Hyundai Transys Georgia Powertrain.
- By incorporating solar energy into its manufacturing process, Hyundai aims to influence more sustainable solutions across its supply chain.
- The move sets Hyundai apart from competitors like Honda, which is developing an Ohio EV Hub, and Toyota, which announced a $1.4 billion investment for electric SUV production at its Indiana plant.
- The Georgia Metaplant is Hyundai's first EV mass-production plant and will also produce EVs for its sister company, Kia.
- Hyundai's success in U.S. EV sales with the IONIQ 5 is driving innovation in EV manufacturing processes and influencing the broader EV industry to adopt solar energy in production.
