# Yamaha Motor Corp., U.S.A.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-03-11

**Source:** [Grand Homecoming: Yamaha Consolidates U.S. Headquarters in Georgia - A.T.L. News](https://georgia.atl.news/2026/03/11/grand-homecoming-yamaha-consolidates-u-s-headquarters-in-georgia/)  ·  `page_015679`

## Facts

- Governor Brian Kemp announced that Yamaha Motor Corp., U.S.A. is officially making Georgia its permanent home.
- Yamaha Motor Corp., U.S.A. is moving its national headquarters and financial services arm from California to Kennesaw, Cobb County, Georgia.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated, “This is a long-awaited homecoming,” noting that Yamaha’s presence in Georgia dates back to 1986.
- The company has long manufactured ATVs and watercraft at a facility in Newnan, Georgia.
- The corporate headquarters relocation solidifies Georgia’s status as the “No. 1 State for Business.”
- The move is expected to bring between 500 and 1,000 new corporate roles to the Kennesaw area.
- The new corporate positions will be high-level roles in finance, legal, and executive management rather than assembly jobs.
- Corporate leadership will now be located a short drive from Yamaha's massive Newnan factory and its state-of-the-art Marine Innovation Center.
- The headquarters relocation positions Yamaha to tap into the talent pool provided by Georgia Tech and Kennesaw State University.
- Consolidating its U.S. headquarters in Georgia ensures Yamaha's future growth is anchored in the Southeast, providing long-term tax revenue and community investment.
- Yamaha is preparing to break ground on its new expanded headquarters facility.
