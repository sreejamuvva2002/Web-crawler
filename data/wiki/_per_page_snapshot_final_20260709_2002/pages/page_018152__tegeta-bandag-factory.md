# Tegeta Bandag Factory

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2025-12-09

**Source:** [Bandag to Build State-of-the-Art Retreading Factory in Georgia in Collaboration with Tegeta Motors and Bridgestone – Executive Bulletin](https://executive-bulletin.com/automotive/bandag-to-build-state-of-the-art-retreading-factory-in-georgia-in-collaboration-with-tegeta-motors-and-bridgestone)  ·  `page_018152`

## Facts

- Bandag signed an agreement with Tegeta Motors to build a state-of-the-art Tegeta Bandag Factory in Rustavi, Georgia.
- The project marks an important step in expanding sustainable retreading solutions across the Caucasus region.
- The facility is supported by an initial investment of USD 2 million.
- The plant aims to produce up to 5,000 retreaded tyres annually.
- Production capacity is expected to double within five years.
- The facility will be the first Bandag retreading factory in the Caucasus.
- Construction is scheduled to begin this year.
- Completion of the facility is targeted for late 2026.
- The project strengthens Tegeta Motors’ position as a leading provider of advanced automotive services in the region.
- Jacques Fourie, President of Bridgestone Africa, Middle East Region, stated that the collaboration combines technical expertise and a deep commitment to sustainable mobility to advance high-quality retreading solutions in the Caucasus.
- The facility will initially retread tyres for buses and trucks to meet international safety and performance standards.
- This approach is expected to significantly reduce environmental impact and lower operational costs for corporate and commercial customers.
- Representatives from Bridgestone and Bandag will provide construction oversight to ensure adherence to global quality standards.
- The plant will be equipped with state-of-the-art Bandag machinery sourced from Europe and the United States.
- The retreading process reinforces the casing of a used tyre with premium materials used in new tyre manufacturing.
- Retreaded tyres cost 30-40% less than new tyres while delivering comparable performance.
- Retreaded tyres provide a service life of up to five years.
- Bandag’s retreading technology requires less raw material and energy, extends tyre life through multiple cycles, reduces waste generation, minimizes carbon emissions, and supports efficient use of natural resources.
- Ekaterine Kavtaradze, CEO of Tegeta Motors, stated that the factory launch advances fleet solutions, promotes sustainable mobility, and strengthens their presence in the Caucasus region.
- Tegeta Motors will integrate the retreading operations into its existing ecosystem, which includes tyre services, oil and battery operations, and recycling activities.
- Bridgestone MEA continues to provide technical expertise and innovation support throughout the factory’s development.
