# Stuart Countess

## Facts

- Stuart Countess serves as President & CEO of KIA Georgia.
- Stuart Countess transitioned to 2026 Chair of the Georgia Chamber of Commerce on January 14, 2026.
- Countess previously served as 2025 Vice Chair of the Georgia Chamber and succeeds Ed Elkins as Chair.
- The transition occurred at the Georgia Chamber’s annual Eggs & Issues Breakfast, held at Mercedes-Benz Stadium with over 3,000 business and civic leaders in attendance.
- Kim Greene, Chairman, President & CEO of Georgia Power, serves as 2026 Vice Chair of the Georgia Chamber and will become Chair in 2027.
- Beth Lowry, President & CEO of Holder Construction, is the 2026 Chair of the Georgia Chamber Foundation and will assume the role of 2027 Vice Chair of the Georgia Chamber.
- Stuart Countess stated his primary focus for the year is continuing workforce development efforts and ensuring Georgians understand the value of economic development.
- Countess emphasized that Georgia manufacturing demonstrates innovation, precision, and world-class jobs daily.
- Ed Elkins, Executive Vice President & Chief Commercial Officer at Norfolk Southern, noted Countess is the first manufacturer to chair the organization in over 30 years and praised his passion for corporate citizenship, workforce development, and job creation.
- Chris Clark, President & CEO of the Georgia Chamber, stated the organization will continue providing data, insight, and policy leadership during the upcoming legislative session.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced his 2026 legislative agenda at the event, focusing on infrastructure, workforce, and affordability.
- Governor Kemp stated that making everyday life more affordable for hardworking Georgians remains his top priority.
- The 2026 Georgia Chamber Executive Committee includes Boyd Pettit (GeorgiaLink Public Affairs Group) as Government Affairs Council Chair, Shane Jackson (Jackson Healthcare) as PAC Chair, Emmie Howard (Onward Reserve) as Operations Council Chair, Samuel L. Gude III (Gude Management Group) and Matt Reed (Georgia CEO) as At-Large Members, and Jim Chasteen (ASW Distillery) as MEWA Chair.
