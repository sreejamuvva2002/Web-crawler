## Facts

- Georgia offers a flat corporate tax rate of 15%.
- Georgian LLCs can reinvest profits without incurring immediate taxes.
- Qualified IT companies operating within Virtual Zones may qualify for a 0% Corporate Income Tax rate.
- Businesses operating in Free Industrial Zones typically pay zero corporate income tax.
- Companies with International Company Status enjoy a reduced 5% CIT rate upon profit distribution.
- Georgia ranks #7 globally for ease of doing business.
- Georgian companies can be registered online in a single day.
- Georgia maintains an anti-corruption environment to foster fairness for businesses of all scales.
- Investor rights protection is a central focus of Georgian business legislation.
- Establishing an LLC in Georgia requires no mandatory initial capital investment.
- LLC directors and owners are not required to be Georgian residents or visit the country.
- The standard VAT rate is 18%, and mandatory VAT registration applies if VATable turnover exceeds 100,000 GEL within 12 months.
- Employers must withhold 20% income tax from employee salaries, plus additional pension contributions for Georgian citizens or permanent residents.
- Individuals typically face a 5% Personal Dividend Tax on distributed profits.
- Dividend payments between Georgian companies are typically tax-free.
- The 15% CIT may also be triggered by exorbitant representative expenses or gratuitous product distribution.
- Sectors including healthcare, finance, construction, and energy require specific business licenses or permits.
- Georgian business licenses are categorized into Activity Licenses for specific operations and Resource Use Licenses for nationally-owned resources.
- Special statuses like Virtual Zone Entity and Free Industrial Zone Enterprise require formal application and qualification processes.
- Public Service Halls provide in-person guidance and services for company registration.
- Popular banking options for international clients include the Bank of Georgia and TBC Bank.
- Remote bank account opening is possible using a proxy with a notarized and apostilled Power of Attorney.
- Monthly tax filings for personal income tax, corporate income tax, and VAT are due on the 15th of each month.
- Tax payments must originate from a Georgian bank account via the government tax portal.
