# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-18

**Source:** [Hyundai says it will spend $2.7 billion expanding part of the Georgia complex raided by ICE](https://www.ksat.com/business/2025/09/18/hyundai-says-it-will-spend-27-billion-expanding-the-georgia-plant-raided-by-ice/)  ·  `page_021161`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group confirmed it is going forward with previously announced plans to expand its Georgia plant.
- The expansion announcement comes just weeks after an immigration raid delayed the startup of an electric vehicle battery plant at the site.
- Hyundai will spend $2.7 billion to increase production capacity at the Ellabell site.
- The capacity increase will add 200,000 vehicles over the next three years.
- Total production capacity at the Georgia site will reach 500,000 vehicles a year.
- Hyundai first announced the expansion in March at the grand opening of the plant west of Savannah.
- In August, Hyundai said it would invest an additional $5 billion in the United States overall.
- The immigration raid included arrests of more than 300 South Korean citizens.
- The raid led to questions about the wisdom of South Korea investing in the U.S.
- Hyundai plans to produce 10 models of electric and hybrid gas-electric vehicles in Georgia.
- This production lineup is up from the current two models the plant has been assembling as it ramps up production.
- Hyundai says it remains on track to expand worldwide production to 5.6 million vehicles a year by 2030.
- The automaker pledged that 60% of those worldwide vehicles will be electric or hybrid powered.
- Target markets for these vehicles include South Korea, North America, and Europe.
- Hyundai plans to make more than 80% of vehicles sold in the United States domestically by 2030.
- Total domestic content for U.S.-sold vehicles will increase from 60% to 80%.
- For the first time, the Georgia plant's vehicle lineup would include a mid-sized pickup truck.
- Hyundai already manufactures the Santa Cruz model, a four-door compact pickup that it started selling in 2021.
- Hyundai CEO José Muñoz stated the immigration raid will delay opening the battery plant by at least two to three months.
- Spokesperson Michael Stewart said Thursday that the battery facility will open in the first half of 2026.
- Both Hyundai executives and Georgia officials have been working to calm the situation since the raid.
- The raid mushroomed into a diplomatic dispute between South Korea and the United States.
- Republican Gov. Brian Kemp told reporters Tuesday at a ceremony marking the groundbreaking of a Rivian Automotive electric vehicle plant that he remains confident Georgia's business advantages would “win out” in terms of foreign investment.
- Kemp said the raid may even wind up smoothing the way for South Korean employees to more easily obtain legal permission to help build and operate facilities in the United States.
- Kemp stated he has had good conversations with companies currently doing business in Georgia and those looking to do business there.
- Kemp stated he has had good conversations with people in the White House about the visa issue.
- Brent Stubbs, chief administrative officer of the Ellabell site, wrote in an opinion piece published Wednesday in The Atlanta Journal-Constitution that the company is still committed to Georgia.
- Stubbs wrote: “This situation doesn’t change our plans to continue expanding and localizing in the United States.
- Stubbs wrote: “Our investments in America are part of a long-term strategic plan.”
- The $2.7 billion investment confirmed Thursday will go to expanding capacity at the plant and group affiliates.
- This investment brings total investment in the auto plant to $10.3 billion.
- The $10.3 billion total does not count the $4.3 billion that Hyundai and its joint-venture partner, LG Energy Solution, are putting into the battery plant.
- Hyundai and its onsite affiliates currently have 3,129 employees in Ellabell.
- Hyundai pledges to hire at least 8,500 workers by the end of 2031.
- State and local governments have promised $2.1 billion in tax breaks and other incentives.
- The boost at the Ellabell site is the biggest part of Hyundai's plan to raise worldwide production by 1.2 million vehicles a year.
- The worldwide expansion includes another 250,000 vehicles out of Pune, India.
- The worldwide expansion also includes 200,000 more vehicles at Hyundai's electric vehicle plant in Ulsan, South Korea.
- Hyundai said it would deliver parts for assembly into an additional 250,000 vehicles at plants in Saudi Arabia, Vietnam, and North Africa.
- Hyundai underlined a previous announcement to deepen its investment in robotics.
- By 2027, Hyundai will launch extended-range electric vehicles with gasoline motors to extend the range of its electric batteries to more than 600 miles (960 kilometers).
