# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2023-10-03

**Source:** [Georgia No. 1 State for Business for 10th Consecutive Year | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-releases/2023/georgia-no-1-state-business-10th-consecutive-year)  ·  `page_003293`

## Facts

- Georgia achieved an unprecedented milestone: a full decade of business excellence, earning the rank of No. 1 state for business from Area Development magazine for 10 consecutive years.
- No other state in the nation has achieved this benchmark.
- The announcement was made by Governor Brian P. Kemp in conjunction with the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD).
- Area Development’s 2023 Top States for Doing Business rankings are based on scores from approximately 50 leading site consulting firms across the U.S. in 14 categories.
- Georgia placed in the top 10 for all 14 categories, earning the No. 1 spot in seven classifications and claiming the overall No. 1 ranking.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that people who help businesses choose where to make long-term investments have repeatedly said Georgia is the best state in the nation for opportunity.
- The administration has brought record-breaking jobs and investments to all four corners of the state, especially to rural Georgia.
- Dennis Shea, Publisher and President of Area Development, congratulated Governor Kemp, the Georgia Legislature, Commissioner Pat Wilson, and the GDEcD team for earning the No. 1 ranking for an impressive and unprecedented 10th consecutive year.
- Area Development Staff Editor Steve Kaelble noted that Georgia is uniformly at or near the top of most lists making a state a great place to do business, having carefully assembled building blocks that location decision-makers look for.
- Georgia earned a No. 1 ranking for the 14th year in a row for its workforce development program, Georgia Quick Start.
- Georgia also earned a No. 1 ranking for competitive labor market.
- Area Development noted that Georgia sits atop the list of states with the best workforce training opportunities, centered on the state’s Quick Start program.
- The Quick Start program is offered through the Technical College System of Georgia and delivers customized training targeted at new and expanding businesses.
- Companies in manufacturing, biotechnology, information technology, and other sectors need tech-savvy workers with specific skills, and this program has helped attract those businesses to Georgia.
- Lt. Governor Burt Jones stated that the recognition is a testament of the administration, the Georgia General Assembly, and business communities working together.
- Jones highlighted Georgia’s affordable cost of doing business, lower rates through a versatile energy mix, and support from hardworking Georgians as factors keeping the state moving in the right direction.
- Speaker of the House Jon Burns stated that Georgia leads in logistics due to the nation’s fastest growing container port, the world’s busiest airport, and a robust road and rail network.
- Burns noted that Georgia will continue to invest strategically in infrastructure to support the economy and keep the state the envy of the nation.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that success requires planning and implementation over years, supported by successive Governors, local leadership, and the Georgia General Assembly.
- Wilson credited the partnership approach to economic development for allowing state and local teams to be responsive to business.
- Wilson noted that the University System and Technical College System of Georgia have been ahead of the curve in preparing Georgians for workforce needs of tomorrow, giving Georgia a competitive advantage.
- Area Development is considered the leading executive magazine covering corporate site selection and relocation, founded in 1965.
- GDEcD is described as the state’s sales and marketing arm and lead agency for attracting new business investment, encouraging expansion of existing industry, locating new markets, and promoting economic development.
