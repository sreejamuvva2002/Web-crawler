# Solvay Specialty Polymers USA, LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [State of New Jersey](https://www.nj.gov/oag/newsreleases20/pr20201110a.html)  ·  `page_029397`

## Facts

- Solvay Specialty Polymers is headquartered in Alpharetta, Georgia.
- Solvay purchased the Gloucester County facility from prior owner Arkema, Inc. in 1990.
- Solvay began producing industrial plastics, coatings, and other chemicals at the site using PFAS compounds.
- Solvay continues to utilize and release replacement PFAS known as chloroperfluoropolyether carboxylates (CIPFPECAs) into the environment.
- The State's complaint alleges Solvay discharged tens of thousands of pounds of PFAS compounds into the environment.
- High concentrations of PFAS compounds, such as perfluorononanoic acid (PFNA) and perfluorooctanoic acid (PFOA), were found in the air, surface water, and groundwater on and near the Solvay property.
- A 2007-2009 survey found PFNA in the Delaware River water near the Solvay site at the highest reported concentration in surface water in the world at that time.
- Levels of PFNA found in the nearby Borough of Paulsboro’s water supply in 2014 were higher than had ever been reported in drinking water elsewhere in the United States or the world.
- Solvay has refused to take full responsibility for necessary investigation and remediation and failed to disclose impacts to DEP and surrounding communities.
- Solvay repeatedly sought to blame other actors instead of investigating all contamination pathways and paying for treatment of contaminated drinking water.
- Excessive concentrations of VOCs, such as carbon tetrachloride and vinyl chloride, exist at the Solvay site.
- Groundwater is contaminated with toxic metals including aluminum, antimony, arsenic, beryllium, chromium, cobalt, iron, lead, manganese, nickel, and sodium at levels exceeding state groundwater quality standards.
- Nine counts of a 10-count complaint name both Solvay and former owner Arkema as defendants, alleging violations of NJ Spill Act, Water Pollution Control Act, Air Pollution Control Act, Solid Waste Management Act, Site Remediation Reform Act, public nuisance, trespass, negligence, and abnormally dangerous activity.
- A single count filed against Solvay only alleges violation of New Jersey’s Brownfield and Contaminated Site Remediation Act.
