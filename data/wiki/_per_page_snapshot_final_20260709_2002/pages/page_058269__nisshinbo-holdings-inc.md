# Nisshinbo Holdings Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** research_and_development  ·  **Published:** 2010-04-01

**Source:** [Nisshinbo Holdings Inc. Business Report FY2009 - MarkLines Automotive Industry Portal](https://www.marklines.com/en/top500/cf/s500_200_hl2009)  ·  `page_058269`

## Facts

- The company announced a new medium-term business plan covering the three-year period through Mar. 2013 called "Challenge 2012".
- Under the "Challenge 2012" program, the company will focus its management resources on environmental and energy businesses.
- The company will work on establishing and nurturing new businesses such as solar cells, fuel cells, electric double-layer capacitors, Carbodilite high-performance chemical products, and carbon catalysts.
- The company announced confirmation of the world's top-level electric power generating attributes of its carbon alloy catalysts when used as alternatives to platinum for fuel cells.
- Compared with platinum catalysts, carbon alloy catalysts offer a 90 percent reduction in costs.
- Carbon alloy products provide extended battery lifecycles because they do not have corrosion behavior to other materials seen in platinum-based catalysts.
- Current voltage level is 0.67V at open-circuit voltage of 0.98V and 0.2 amperes per centimeter.
- Output density is 525 milliwatt per square centimeter.
- The company is conducting research and development activities to enhance performance of a fuel cell separator by taking advantage of features of carbon materials.
- The company is improving durability performance of a dual electric layer capacitor.
- The company made an investment in construction of facilities at the Chiba Plant for manufacturing fuel cell separators.
- The company integrated its automobile brakes business in the U.S. into Nisshinbo Automotive Manufacturing Inc. (NAMI), completing its reorganization into one U.S. corporation after liquidating Nisshinbo Automotive Corp. (NAC).
- The company announced an agreement with Continental to expand operations of their joint venture company Continental Automotive Corporation (CAC) in Japan.
- Three divisions of the Continental Automotive Group, including airbag control units and automotive sensors, as well as plants in China and Korea will be integrated into CAC by Mar. 2010.
- Integration of operations is expected to increase sales of CAC by 50 percent.
