# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-19

**Source:** [Hyundai confirms $2.7 billion Georgia expansion after immigration raid raised investment concerns - Deftechtimes](https://deftechtimes.com/hyundai-confirms-2-7-billion-georgia-expansion-after-immigration-raid-raised-investment-concerns/)  ·  `page_008891`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group confirmed it will proceed with a $2.7 billion expansion plan at its automotive complex in Georgia.
- The expansion announcement follows weeks after a large immigration raid disrupted operations at the same site.
- The Georgia complex is located west of Savannah in Ellabell and serves as one of Hyundai’s key U.S. production centers.
- The new investment will increase the plant’s annual production capacity by 200,000 vehicles over the next three years.
- Once complete, total output at the site will reach approximately 500,000 vehicles per year.
- Hyundai initially announced plans to enlarge the Georgia facility earlier this year during the plant’s grand opening.
- The expansion is part of a broader investment program aimed at strengthening Hyundai’s role in the U.S. market.
- Despite delays from the immigration raid, Hyundai reiterated that the expansion will proceed as planned.
- The expanded plant will manufacture ten models of electric and hybrid vehicles in Georgia.
- The facility currently assembles only two models but will eventually produce a mid-sized pickup truck for the U.S. market.
- The $2.7 billion expansion brings Hyundai’s total investment at the Georgia site to $10.3 billion.
- This figure excludes an additional $4.3 billion being spent with a joint-venture partner to build a battery plant at the same location.
- Hyundai and its affiliates currently employ more than 3,100 people at the Ellabell site.
- The company aims to increase its workforce to at least 8,500 employees by the end of 2031.
- State and local governments have provided $2.1 billion in tax breaks and other incentives to support Hyundai’s expansion in Georgia.
- This investment is considered one of the most significant single boosts to the U.S. automotive industry by a foreign automaker in recent years.
- The Ellabell site will be the center of Hyundai’s domestic production increase, aligning with a goal to raise worldwide production capacity by over one million vehicles annually.
- Hyundai has also announced plans to increase capacity at facilities in India and South Korea, plus additional production arrangements in Saudi Arabia, Vietnam, and North Africa.
- U.S. authorities detained more than 300 workers during the immigration raid, most of whom were South Korean nationals.
- The raid caused tension between the United States and South Korea, resulting in a brief diplomatic dispute.
- The disruption raised concerns that such events could discourage future investment commitments in the U.S.
- Hyundai emphasized that the raid will not alter its long-term strategic plans for the American market.
- The immigration raid has delayed the opening of the new battery plant connected to the Georgia complex.
- Company officials stated the battery facility, built with LG Energy Solution, will now open in the first half of 2026, later than originally expected.
- Hyundai continues to stress its commitment to the U.S. market and Georgia despite the operational disruption.
- The company describes its investments as a crucial part of its long-term strategy to localize production.
- Hyundai aims to produce 5.6 million vehicles annually worldwide by 2030, with 60% of those models being electric or hybrid.
- A portion of the Georgia plant’s expanded output will contribute to this global target, focusing on sales in North America, South Korea, and Europe.
