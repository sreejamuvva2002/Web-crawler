# Toyota Industries Electric Systems North America, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-11-14

**Source:** [Toyota Industries Corporation Expands Georgia Presence, Creating 250 New Jobs - Charge Forward Georgia](https://www.chargeforwardga.com/news-events/news/toyota-industries-corporation-expands-georgia-presence-creating-250-new-jobs/)  ·  `page_012393`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Toyota Industries Electric Systems North America, Inc. (TIESNA) will create more than 250 new jobs.
- TIESNA is expanding the Toyota Industries Corporation campus in Pendergrass with a new manufacturing facility valued at $69 million in investment.
- TIESNA broke ground on the new site on November 14, 2023.
- Japanese Consul General Mio Maeda and local representatives attended the groundbreaking ceremony.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated that Georgia is a leader in the automotive industry and the "best state for business."
- Governor Brian P. Kemp pledged continued collaboration with state and local partners to create opportunities across Georgia.
- TIESNA is a subsidiary of Toyota Industries Corporation, which is headquartered in Japan.
- Toyota Industries develops and manufactures automobile-related products, including car air-conditioning compressors and car electronics components for worldwide carmakers.
- The Toyota Industries group has operated in Pendergrass since 2004.
- The group's initial Pendergrass operation was TD Automotive Compressor Georgia, LLC (TACG).
- Toyota Industries Compressor Parts America, Co. (TICA) was established in Pendergrass in 2012.
- TACG and TICA currently support more than 400 jobs in Jackson County.
- TIESNA’s new facility will be located at 1700 Valentine Industrial Parkway in Pendergrass.
- The new facility will produce DC-to-DC converters that convert high voltage to low voltage to supply power to auxiliary equipment.
- Operations at the new facility are expected to begin in 2025.
- Jackson County Commission Chair Tom Crow welcomed TIESNA as the third Toyota Industries-related company in Jackson County.
- Tom Crow praised TACG and TICA as outstanding corporate citizens and pledged county support for TIESNA's success.
- Griff Lynch, President and CEO of the Georgia Ports Authority, welcomed TIESNA's expansion as part of Georgia's growing automaking sector.
- Griff Lynch noted that significant investments from Toyota Industries and other automotive suppliers are creating local career opportunities.
- GDEcD Senior Regional Project Manager Kristen Miller represented the agency on this competitive project.
- The project was supported in partnership with the Jackson County Area Chamber of Commerce, Georgia Power, the Georgia Ports Authority, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson noted that Toyota Industries has been part of the Jackson County community since 2004.
- Pat Wilson highlighted that building long-term relationships gives Georgia a competitive advantage for company expansions.
- GDEcD has maintained 50 years of representation in Japan to keep direct communication lines open with corporate HQs like Toyota Industries.
- Georgia has positioned itself as a leader in developing and harnessing emerging technologies for the automotive and mobility industry for over a century.
- In fiscal year 2023, job creation in Georgia's automotive industry increased by 324 percent compared to fiscal year 2021.
- Suppliers attracted by Georgia’s OEMs resulted in over $2 billion in investment across the state in FY23.
- Toyota Industries Corporation was established in 1926 in Japan for the manufacturing and sales of automatic looms.
- TICO has more than 280 consolidated subsidiaries worldwide.
- TICO holds the largest world market share in air-jet looms, car air conditioning compressors, and lift trucks.
- TIESNA was established in 2013 in Novi, Michigan.
- TIESNA operates as a sales company handling automotive electronics components such as AC inverters, voltage stabilizers, and motor controllers.
- TIESNA's customers include major OEMs in North America.
