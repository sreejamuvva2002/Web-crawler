# Georgia Electric Vehicle Market

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2022-06-22

**Source:** [As gas prices rise, Georgia expands its Electric Vehicle market - Georgia Conservation Voters](https://gcvoters.org/blog/2022/06/22/as-gas-prices-rise-georgia-expands-its-electric-vehicle-market/)  ·  `page_000268`

## Facts

- As gas prices rise, Georgia is emerging as a leader in electric vehicle manufacturing, which will help fight climate change and provide millions of jobs.
- Between Bluebird and SK Innovations, to the newer Rivian and Hyundai plants, Georgia is certainly becoming a hub for the construction of electric vehicles.
- Governor Brian Kemp helped strike a deal between the State of Georgia and Rivian earlier this year to build a massive electric vehicle factory.
- The Rivian plant will produce electric trucks when it begins operating in 2024.
- The Rivian plant will provide an estimated 7,500 jobs.
- The Rivian plant will cost $5 billion to construct.
- The state of Georgia has offered Rivian $1.5 billion in tax incentives for the project.
- The Rivian deal has created controversy in the surrounding community regarding concerns about how the plant will impact quality of life, especially air and water quality.
- GCV stands with residents in their environmental concerns and expects Rivian to work closely with the community to build the new plant in an ethical and environmentally sound manner.
- President Biden announced that Hyundai will spend $5 billion to open a new electric vehicle factory in Savannah, Georgia.
- The Hyundai plant in Savannah will add 8,000 new jobs to the area.
- A nationwide network of EV chargers supported by the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law will enable families to travel gas-free.
- Georgians are predicted to save $1.3 billion on gasoline per year by 2035 with the increase in usage of electric vehicles.
- The National Electric Vehicle Infrastructure (NEVI) Formula Program was established by President Biden’s Bipartisan Infrastructure Law to build out a national electric vehicle charging network.
- Earlier this year, the U.S. Departments of Transportation and Energy announced nearly $5 billion that will be made available to states for EV infrastructure.
- Georgia will receive an estimated $134,975,283 from the NEVI program for the first five years.
- The Georgia legislature and the state Department of Transportation are working on an EV Infrastructure Deployment Plan that must be submitted no later than August 1, 2022 before the state can access NEVI funds.
- Senator Warnock and Senator Ossoff are fighting to make Georgia a top contender for electric vehicle manufacturing jobs and clean energy investments.
