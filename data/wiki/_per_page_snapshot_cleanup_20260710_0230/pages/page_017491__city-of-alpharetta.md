# City of Alpharetta

## Facts

- Alpharetta invested in optical fiber since the 1980s and hosts multiple data centers.
- In 2019, the fintech industry in the Atlanta area was a driver of data center expansion in Alpharetta.
- Data center expansion in Alpharetta is supported by tax breaks from Fulton County authorities.
- Data center expansion ran into limitations from state regulations and local zoning authorities in 2024.
- Capacity for data centers in Atlanta tripled from 200 MW to over 700 MW in 2023.
- The Georgia Senate passed a bill to suspend data center tax exemptions on March 18, 2024.
- On September 4, 2024, the Atlanta City Council passed rules limiting data center locations, prohibiting construction near the Beltline or within a half mile of MARTA rail stations.
- From the first to the second half of 2023, data center projects in the area skyrocketed from over 235 MW to over 730 MW, equivalent to the output of a typical natural gas plant.
- Tech North Atlanta (formerly known as Tech Alpharetta and the Alpharetta Technology Commission) is an advisory organization established by the City of Alpharetta in 2012.
- Tech North Atlanta is an independent, 501(c)(6) nonprofit organization that aims to help Alpharetta lead in technology innovation.
- The organization runs an advisory board of technology companies based in the city and holds monthly technology events for technology executives.
- Tech North Atlanta operates the Tech Alpharetta Innovation Center, a technology startup incubator.
- As of early 2020, about ten companies had graduated from the Tech Alpharetta incubator and were hiring employees in the North Fulton County region.
- UPS Supply Chain Solutions is one of Alpharetta's top private sector employers, with 1,200 employees as of February 2026.
- Alpharetta is not directly served by MARTA trains but is connected by multiple bus routes, and connecting the city to the rest of Metro Atlanta via heavy rail has been studied.
