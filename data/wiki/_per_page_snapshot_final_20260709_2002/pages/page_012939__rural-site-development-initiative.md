# Rural Site Development Initiative

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [GA: Gov. Kemp Announces Fourth Round of Rural Site Development Grants | Trade and Industry Development](https://www.tradeandindustrydev.com/region/georgia/news/ga-gov-kemp-announces-fourth-round-rural-site-36034)  ·  `page_012939`

## Facts

- Georgia Gov. Brian P. Kemp announced the fourth round of grants from the Rural Site Development Initiative (RSDI) within the OneGeorgia Authority.
- The fourth round of RSDI grants totals $7.8 million for ten local projects across the state.
- With this latest round, the State of Georgia has invested $28 million into rural site development projects since the program's launch in FY 2025.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that over 64 percent of new jobs created during his tenure have gone to parts of the state outside the metro Atlanta region.
- The Rural Site Development Initiative is an important part of a broader effort to bring opportunity to all parts of Georgia.
- Lt. Governor Burt Jones commended Gov. Kemp for leading the charge to fund and promote the program, which has awarded $28 million for critical projects in rural communities across Georgia.
- House Speaker Jon Burns stated that rural communities are the lifeblood of Georgia’s economy and expressed pride in supporting grants that bring good-paying jobs to hardworking Georgians.
- Speaker Jon Burns noted he is especially proud of investments being made in Southeast Georgia as the region navigates unprecedented changes in the forestry industry.
- RSDI grants are awarded for either site improvement or to assist with the costs of seeking Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) certification on approved projects critical to economic development.
- Communities match state funding at various levels based on the program's criteria.
- Georgia Department of Community Affairs Commissioner Christopher Nunn stated that GRAD sites are important to local strategies for attracting employers and investment in Georgia’s communities.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that over the last several years, GRAD sites have helped attract an estimated 10,000 jobs to communities across the state.
- Proactive economic development planning, including through shovel-ready sites, strengthens the state's portfolio and competitiveness as a state.
- The Effingham County Development Authority in coastal Georgia was awarded $1.8 million for site readiness improvements, including the construction of a sewer pump station, at the GRAD-certified Savannah Portside International Park.
- Total project costs for the Effingham County site readiness improvements are $2.5 million.
- The Development Authority of Columbus in west Georgia was awarded $2 million for site improvement work, including clearing and grading, at the GRAD-certified Muscogee Technology Park.
- Total project costs for the Columbus site improvement work are $10 million.
- The McIntosh County Industrial Development Authority near coastal Georgia was awarded $1.5 million for road, water, and sewer infrastructure improvements at the GRAD-certified Tidewaters Industrial Park.
- Total project costs for the McIntosh County infrastructure improvements are $2.8 million.
- This is the second site development award for McIntosh County, which previously received a GRAD-Certification Scholarship for this grant.
- The Thomaston-Upson County Industrial Development Authority in west-central Georgia was awarded $2 million to construct water, sewer, and transportation infrastructure at the GRAD-certified Central Georgia Business and Technology Park.
- Total project costs for the Thomaston-Upson County infrastructure construction are $2.3 million.
