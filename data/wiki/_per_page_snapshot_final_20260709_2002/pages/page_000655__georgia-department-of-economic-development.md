# Georgia Department of Economic Development

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2023-08-14

**Source:** [Georgia’s Record Year Driven by International Investment  - Global Atlanta](https://www.globalatlanta.com/georgias-record-year-driven-by-international-investment/)  ·  `page_000655`

## Facts

- International investment accounted for two-thirds of the $24 billion in capital pledged toward 426 projects in Georgia during the fiscal year ended June 30.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development reported that 47 percent of the 38,400 announced jobs came from foreign subsidiaries.
- Approximately $15.6 billion of the foreign capital investments came from the automotive sector, driven by the transition to electric vehicles and South Korean OEMs and parts suppliers.
- Late in 2022, Hyundai broke ground on a $5.54 billion plant near Savannah.
- In May, Hyundai announced a partnership with LG Energy Solution for an on-site battery plant.
- Hyundai's Bryan County site, called the “Metaplant,” has attracted nearly $2 billion in additional supplier investments.
- These supplier investments are located either co-located on the 2,900-acre property or in nearby communities.
- Hyundai revealed that total investment planned for a separate Cartersville EVbattery plant built in partnership SK with would top $5 billion.
- Hyundai-affiliated projects, including suppliers, accounted for about half of the investment total in fiscal year 2023.
- Hyundai announced a multi-decade partnership with Georgia Tech that ESPN reported will include a $55 million deal for naming rights at what will now be called Bobby Dodd Stadium at Hyundai Field.
- The deal includes a hydrogen research initiative.
- The deal also includes a previously announced $120,000 scholarship fund.
- California-based Rivian accounted for another $5 billion commitment with plans to put a factory in Covington.
- Job creation in the auto sector has grown four-fold in two years.
- The state pointed to an electric-mobility initiative launched under Gov. Brian Kemp.
- Even removing Hyundai and Rivian's generational investments, the state still eclipsed last year’s record job-creation figure by 2,800 positions.
- Norway’s Freyr Battery is set to put a $2.4 billion energy storage plant in Coweta County.
- Finland’s ADMARES said it would bulid a $750 million modular housing plant in Waycross.
- Norway and Finland are among the top-five investors in the state this year, behind Korea and in front of perennial powerhouses Switzerland, Japan and the Netherlands.
- The $2.5 billion Q CELLS expansion in Cartersville from Korea’s Hanhwa was announced in January.
- Executives credited the solar production incentives in the Inflation Reduction Act for providing an attractive regulatory framework.
- Most announced expansion projects are multi-year projects that don’t commence production until years into the future.
- As market conditions and company plans change, the actual number of jobs created and capital deployed may not necessarily match up with initial pledges.
- States can use “clawback” provisions to recoup some tax incentives calculated on the baseline level of jobs and capital investment.
