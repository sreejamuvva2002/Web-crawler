# The Bosch Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2020-12-31

**Source:** [Bosch: the mobility of the future needs fuel cells - Bosch Media Service US](https://www.bosch-presse.de/pressportal/us/en/press-release-11840.html)  ·  `page_049259`

## Facts

- Bosch is developing a fuel-cell powertrain primarily with a focus on heavy-duty trucks with 40-ton payloads.
- When powered with hydrogen produced using renewable energy, this powertrain enables climate-neutral transportation of goods and commodities.
- Bosch plans to start production of its fuel-cell powertrains in 2022–2023.
- Once established in trucks, Bosch fuel-cell powertrains will increasingly find their way into passenger cars as an integral part of tomorrow's powertrain portfolio.
- The Bosch Group employs roughly 394,500 associates worldwide as of December 31, 2020.
- Preliminary figures indicate the company generated $87.1 billion in consolidated sales globally in 2020.
- At 126 locations across the globe, Bosch employs some 73,000 associates in research and development, including roughly 30,000 software engineers.
- The Bosch Group comprises Robert Bosch GmbH and its roughly 440 subsidiary and regional companies in 60 countries.
- In North America, the Bosch Group has established a regional presence since 1906 and employs 34,700 associates in more than 100 locations as of December 31, 2020.
- Preliminary figures show Bosch generated $13.1 billion in sales across the U.S., Canada, and Mexico.
