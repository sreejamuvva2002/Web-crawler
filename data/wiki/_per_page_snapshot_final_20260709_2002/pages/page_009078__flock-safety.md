# Flock Safety

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2025-04-03

**Source:** [Georgia Governor announces new Flock facility in Atlanta area - TheTrucker.com](https://www.thetrucker.com/trucking-news/business/georgia-governor-announces-new-flock-facility-in-atlanta-area)  ·  `page_009078`

## Facts

- Georgia-headquartered Flock Safety is opening a new manufacturing facility in Smyrna.
- The new facility will be 97,000 square feet.
- The project represents an investment of approximately $10 million.
- The facility will ultimately create an estimated 210 new jobs over the next three years.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp announced the new Flock Safety manufacturing facility.
- Over 300 law enforcement agencies and 100 businesses in Georgia have deployed Flock Safety’s technology to solve and reduce crime.
- Flock Safety launched its Drone as First Responder system last year upon acquiring Aerodome, a leader in rapid response drones for public safety.
- The new Smyrna facility is dedicated to the production of advanced public safety tools, including Drone as First Responder technology.
- The facility will also refurbish damaged devices and assemble solar panels.
- Flock Safety’s products can read license plates at up to 100 miles per hour and in heavy traffic.
- Flock Safety’s products can be paired with gunshot detection technology.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp stated, “In Georgia, we foster a business environment where innovative companies can get off the ground and grow into leaders in their industry while operating in safe communities.”
- Flock Safety Founder and CEO Garrett Langley stated, “We feel fortunate to have our roots in Georgia, the state that is undisputedly the leader in the American aerospace industry, and are proud to invest further by creating hundreds of advanced manufacturing and aviation jobs locally.”
- The drones produced will be made-in-America and NDAA-compliant.
