# Georgia Economic Development Projects

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2023-10-31

**Source:** [Economic Development Around the State - Georgia Trend Magazine](https://www.georgiatrend.com/2023/10/31/economic-development-around-the-state-78/)  ·  `page_019056`

## Facts

- Pratt Industries Inc. is building a new production facility in Warner Robins.
- The Warner Robins facility will be the 13th site in Georgia for the Atlanta-based recycled paper and packaging company.
- Pratt Industries is the fifth-largest corrugated packaging company in the U.S.
- The 496,000-square-foot facility will open in 2024.
- The Warner Robins facility will bring Pratt Industries' total investment in Georgia to more than $800 million.
- DAS Corp., a key supplier for both Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia and Hyundai Motor Group, is opening a new manufacturing facility in Metter.
- The DAS Corp. facility represents a $35 million investment.
- The Metter facility will mainly produce auto seat structures.
- The Metter facility will create 300 new jobs.
- Production at the Metter facility is scheduled to begin in late 2024.
- CJ Foodville Corp., a subsidiary of Korean company CJ Group, is opening a $47 million bakery and food processing facility in Hall County.
- The Hall County facility supports the expansion of the TOUS les JOURS bakery café brand.
- At full capacity in 2025, the Hall County facility will produce more than 19,000 tons of products a year.
- Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solutions (LGES) are investing an additional $2 billion into their battery cell manufacturing joint venture at the Metaplant in Bryan County.
- This expansion raises the total investment value of the Hyundai-LGES joint venture to more than $4.3 billion.
- The Metaplant expansion will create another 400 jobs.
- Korean auto interior manufacturer Daesol Ausys Georgia is opening a new facility in Harris County.
- The Daesol Ausys Georgia facility represents a $72 million investment.
- The Harris County facility will create more than 140 jobs.
- Daesol Ausys Georgia is a key supplier for Kia Georgia, Hyundai Motor Group, and General Motors.
- Operations at the Daesol Ausys Georgia facility are scheduled to begin in late 2024.
- Fouts Bros. Inc. is expanding its operations in Milledgeville.
- The Fouts Bros. expansion represents a $4 million investment.
- As part of the expansion, the fire/rescue truck manufacturer is adding nearly 100 jobs this year.
- An additional 70 jobs are planned to follow at the Fouts Bros. facility in 2024.
