# Inalfa Roof Systems

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2013-06-24

**Source:** [Inalfa expected to create 300 jobs in Georgia](https://www.yahoo.com/news/inalfa-expected-create-300-jobs-155327518.html)  ·  `page_045144`

## Facts

- Inalfa Roof Systems will open a manufacturing plant in Cherokee County, Georgia.
- The new facility is expected to create 300 jobs.
- The project represents a $17.1 million investment.
- The plant will be located in the Cherokee 75 Corporate Park off Interstate 75.
- The site is approximately 30 miles northwest of Atlanta.
- Inalfa manufactures vehicle roofing systems, including sunroofs and open roofing systems for automotive manufacturers.
- Key customers include Hyundai Kia, Ford, General Motors, Volvo, Chrysler Group, and BMW Group.
- Governor Nathan Deal stated that Georgia's central location in the Southeast, deep workforce resources, and logistics infrastructure have attracted well over 300 companies to the state's auto manufacturing sector.
- The Cherokee County plant will be Inalfa's fourth facility in the United States.
- The Cherokee County plant will be Inalfa's first facility in the Southeast region.
- Inalfa's global headquarters are located in Venray, The Netherlands.
- Inalfa has a worldwide workforce of about 3,000 employees.
- Inalfa's North American headquarters is located in Michigan.
- Inalfa operates a total of four plants in Michigan and Mexico.
- Danny Colaris serves as vice president of North American Operations for Inalfa Roof Systems.
- Colaris stated the company spent four years searching for a location in the South before selecting Cherokee County.
- Colaris credited state and local economic development teams, as well as Georgia Power, for helping introduce the company to the site's strategic advantages.
- The plant is expected to begin operating in January 2014.
- Job opportunities at the new facility will include roles in engineering, operations, supply chain, purchasing, and manufacturing.
- The industrial park along Interstate 75 is situated in close proximity to automotive manufacturers throughout the Southeast.
