# L.K. Technology Holdings Limited

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-01

**Source:** [L.K. Technology Announces 2025/2026 Annual Results - Asia Business Newswire](https://www.siamnews.net/pr-news/l-k-technology-announces-2025-2026-annual-results/)  ·  `page_011687`

## Facts

- Announced annual results for the twelve months ended 31 March, 2026.
- Revenue amounted to HK$5,609 million.
- Gross profit amounted to HK$1,375 million with a gross margin of 24.5%.
- Net profit was adjusted to HK$49.5 million with a net profit margin of 0.9%.
- Total assets reached HK$13.3 billion, representing a year-on-year increase of 11.2%.
- Maintained a net cash position of HK$1.32 billion.
- Operates three core business segments: die-casting for metal blank forming, injection molding for plastic part forming, and CNC machining centre for high-precision finishing.
- Die-casting machinery business revenue was HK$3,663 million, accounting for 65.3% of total revenue, with automotive vehicles industries as the primary revenue source.
- Injection molding segment generated revenue of HK$1,798 million (32.1% of total) and achieved a three-year compound annual growth rate of 38.7%.
- Revenue from the toy industry within injection molding grew over 55% for a second consecutive year, contributing 16.7% to revenue.
- CNC machining centre business revenue was HK$148 million (2.6% of total).
- R&D expenses reached HK$318 million during the year, a year-on-year increase of 24.6%.
- Commercialised the world’s first 16,000T ultra-large intelligent die-casting unit.
- Continues to secure and deliver multiple 9000T-class ultra-large die casting machines.
- Developed 6,000T–16,000T ultra-large intelligent die-casting cells integrating digital twin technology with the LK-NET cloud die-casting management system.
- Solutions are fully compatible with mass production requirements for full vehicle body structural components of automobile manufacturers.
- Ranked first globally in cumulative number of ultra-large die-casting machines delivered as at the end of the year.
- TPI semi-solid magnesium alloy forming technology covers small precision parts to ultra-large structural parts and supports modular transformation of traditional die-casting equipment.
- Modular transformation enables a 50% reduction in energy consumption and a 20% increase in product toughness.
- Secured an order from a leading OEM for its self-developed 5,000-tonne TPI semi-solid forming module to produce inner tailgate panels for new energy vehicles.
- Integrating nine separate sheet metal parts into one component achieves a 45% reduction in weight and increases material utilisation to 70%.
- Orders for TPI magnesium alloy equipment exceeded RMB100 million in shipment value.
- Revenue from the related magnesium alloy business increased by 291.1% in the second half of the financial year compared with the first half.
- Developed zirconium-based amorphous alloy die casting equipment integrating vacuum melting, automatic solid material feeding, and full-process automation.
- Equipment can be used for large-scale production of foldable screen hinges and medical implants.
- Low-pressure die-casting machines feature innovative inert gas pressurisation and waste heat recovery technologies, reducing overall energy consumption by 30%.
- Low-pressure machines are widely used in manufacturing automotive wheels and motor end covers.
- Horizontal extrusion casting machine meets production requirements for high-performance components such as automotive steering knuckles, subframes, and battery end plates.
- Developed an integrated intelligent machining system with a local data processing rate exceeding 90% and over 95% accuracy in predicting machining abnormalities.
- Completed engineering validation on five-axis gantry machining centres and post-processing equipment for large die-cast components.
- Overcame technical challenges in the high-precision forming process for hydraulic valves, enhancing in-house capabilities in core component supply.
- Developed intelligent die-casting cells using digital twin technology for intelligent prediction of process parameters, remote equipment diagnostics, and digitalised management/control.
- Laid out new productivity tracks including energy storage, AI computing power, humanoid robots, and specialised casting to diversify customer structure.
- Revenue from Europe surged by 64.4% year-on-year due to robust demand for new energy equipment and high-end manufacturing.
- Secured a high-end equipment order from a well-known European vehicle enterprise.
- Revenue from other emerging markets and the Asia-Pacific region increased by 76.9% year-on-year.
- Localised sales and service network covers Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia.
- Revenue from the North American market declined year-on-year.
- Mr. Liu Zhuo Ming serves as Chief Executive Officer of L.K. Technology Holdings Limited.
- Owns Idra, a world-class precision machinery brand that developed the world’s first Giga Press ultra-large die-casting equipment.
- Established sales and service companies in the United States and India to capture overseas markets.
- Operates a casting factory in Fuxin, China, for producing cast iron/steel components.
- Maintains 15 manufacturing bases and 60 sales and service centers worldwide.
