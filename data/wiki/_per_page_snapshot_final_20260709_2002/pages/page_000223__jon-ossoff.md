# Jon Ossoff

**Type:** person  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- Georgia has received more clean energy manufacturing investment since passage of the Inflation Reduction Act than any other state in the nation.
- In January of 2023, Senator Ossoff helped announce a historic $2.5 billion investment by Qcells to significantly expand solar manufacturing.
- The Qcells investment was made possible by Senator Ossoff’s Solar Energy Manufacturing for America Act.
- The Qcells investment marks the largest clean energy manufacturing investment in American history.
- In May, following his third economic delegation to the Republic of Korea, Senator Ossoff announced a partnership between LG and Hyundai to build an electric vehicle battery plant as part of the new Hyundai MetaPlant in Bryan County.
- This summer, Senator Ossoff joined local leaders to celebrate the groundbreaking of a new manufacturing facility in Bainbridge that will manufacture key components for electric vehicles.
- The Bainbridge EV component facility is made possible by a $117 million bipartisan infrastructure law grant.
- Grants were delivered to help communities eliminate railway-highway crossings, improving safety and strengthening supply chains.
- Senator Ossoff secured a historic $8 million investment to begin planning a passenger rail line between Atlanta and Savannah.
- Three new bipartisan infrastructure law grants were secured to accelerate planning of passenger rail between Atlanta-Savannah, Atlanta-Charlotte, and Atlanta-Nashville.
- Senator Ossoff set a goal to establish Georgia as the leading State in advanced energy innovation and manufacturing.
- Legislation passed under Senator Ossoff has made Georgia the top state in the nation for advanced energy manufacturing and investment.
- Senator Ossoff is championing the bipartisan JOBS Act to help students access and afford job training programs for short-term skills and workforce development.
- The JOBS Act would ensure students in Georgia enrolled in high-quality short-term skills and job training programs have access to Pell Grants, which currently may only be used for two- and four-year colleges.
