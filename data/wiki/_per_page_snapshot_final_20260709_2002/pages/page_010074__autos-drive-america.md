# Autos Drive America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- Autos Drive America hosted its first Board meeting of 2023 in Washington, D.C.
- The association’s leadership met with key Congressional leaders and Biden administration officials to discuss policy opportunities and challenges facing international automakers operating in the U.S.
- Autos Drive America announced Pablo Di Si of Volkswagen Group of America, Inc. as its new Board Chair.
- Jennifer Safavian serves as President & CEO of Autos Drive America.
- Autos Drive America members are committed to boosting the economy and creating high-skilled career opportunities through continued investments in communities across the U.S.
- The association will advocate for policies that promote free and fair trade, strengthen global supply chains, and foster investment in careers and communities across the U.S. this year.
- Members discussed international automakers’ sprawling footprint in communities nationwide.
- International automakers account for 2.3 million direct and indirect jobs in the U.S.
- International automakers operate 500 facilities in the U.S.
- International automakers have made investments totaling more than $100 billion in the U.S.
- Pablo Di Si stated that international automakers are critical to the U.S. economy and are investing billions of dollars to strengthen domestic assembly and supply chains for electric vehicles, creating thousands of new jobs for American people.
- Autos Drive America announced its 2023 Board of Directors to guide the association’s policy agenda and advocacy over the year.
- Scott Brand, Executive Vice President of Subaru of Indiana Automotive, Inc., was named Vice Chair.
- David Johnson, Senior Vice President of Manufacturing and Supply Chain Management at Nissan North America, Inc., was named Secretary.
- Stuart Countess, President & CEO of Kia Georgia, Inc., was named Treasurer.
