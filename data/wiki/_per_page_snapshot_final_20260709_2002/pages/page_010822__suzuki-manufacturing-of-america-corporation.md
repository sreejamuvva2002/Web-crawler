# Suzuki Manufacturing of America Corporation

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-06-15

**Source:** [Suzuki Has Made Half-a-Million ATVs at Its US Facility](https://www.rideapart.com/news/723273/suzuki-500000-atv-production-milestone/)  ·  `page_010822`

## Facts

- Headquartered in Rome, Georgia, Suzuki Manufacturing of America Corporation (SMAC) has been producing ATVs in the United States since 2002.
- For 22 years, the SMAC facility has served the US and Canadian markets, as well as 25 other countries around the world.
- Prior to setting up shop in the US, ATVs for the North American market were sourced all the way from Japan.
- Masami Haga, President of SMO and SMAC, explained that North America represents the largest ATV market in the world and noted it is important that these products are built here by skilled American workers.
- SMO works with multiple US-based suppliers in providing parts and components for Suzuki’s ATVs.
- The Suzuki KingQuad 750 had the honor of being the 500,000th ATV to be produced in SMAC's facility.
- Suzuki’s 500,000th ATV is the KingQuad 750 AXi, the brand’s flagship model and one of the best-selling ATVs in the world.
- Suzuki credits its KingQuad range as the “inventor of the four-wheel ATV.”
- The KingQuad line has been under continuous production for 20 years.
- Suzuki pioneered its first four-wheeled ATV all the way back in 1982 with the LT125.
- Suzuki’s current ATV lineup consists of a wide selection of four-wheelers, ranging from kid-specific models to 400cc to 750cc KingQuads.
- Suzuki has a network of over 700 dealers, making its ATVs and powersports vehicles accessible to a wide audience.
- The 500,000th ATV produced will not be sold but will be put up on display at the company's headquarters as a testament to the team's dedication to engineering excellence.
