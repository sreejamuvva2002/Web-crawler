# Hyundai Motor Georgia Plant

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-03-28

**Source:** [Kia to produce hybrid cars at Hyundai's Georgia factory from next year](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/kia-produce-hybrid-cars-hyundais-093822528.html?fr=sycsrp_catchall)  ·  `page_010216`

## Facts

- Kia Corp plans to produce hybrid vehicles at Hyundai Motor's new U.S. factory starting in mid-2026 to meet growing demand.
- Kia CEO Song Ho-sung announced the plan without disclosing specific vehicle models.
- Hyundai is the world's No. 3 automaker when including Kia Motors, in which it owns a 35% stake.
- Hyundai broke ground for an all-electric car factory in Georgia in 2022 under the Biden administration, which promoted EV-friendly policies.
- Hyundai shifted strategy to add hybrid cars to the factory's production lines due to cooling demand for pure electric cars and the Trump administration threatening to end EV subsidies.
- Kia vehicles will represent 40% of the Georgia facility's total production.
- Kia CEO Song Ho-sung made these remarks on the sidelines of an opening ceremony for the factory on Wednesday.
- Hyundai plans to expand the factory's annual production capacity to 500,000 vehicles from an originally planned 300,000.
- The expansion will bring Hyundai and Kia's combined total U.S. production capacity to 1.2 million vehicles per year.
- The upgraded plan is part of $21 billion in investments announced by President Trump and Hyundai chairman Euisun Chung at the White House earlier this week.
- President Trump announced 25% import tariffs on cars and auto parts on Wednesday, a move that may hurt companies, especially Asian automakers which are among the largest vehicle exporters to the U.S.
- South Korea is the world's largest exporter of vehicles to the United States after Mexico, followed by Japan, according to S&P data.
- Kia Motors currently produces most of its hybrid cars, including the Sportage hybrid crossover, in South Korea.
- The new U.S. factory will help achieve Kia's target of boosting U.S. sales to 1.25 million vehicles a year, up from 850,000 currently.
