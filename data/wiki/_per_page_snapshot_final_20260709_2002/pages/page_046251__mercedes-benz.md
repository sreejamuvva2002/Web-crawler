# Mercedes-Benz

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-05-31

**Source:** [Mercedes-Benz Teases New Atlanta Tech Hub to Follow Sandy Springs HQ Expansion - Global Atlanta](https://www.globalatlanta.com/mercedes-benz-teases-atlanta-new-tech-hub-to-follow-sandy-springs-hq-expansion/)  ·  `page_046251`

## Facts

- German auto maker Mercedes-Benz is deepening its investment in metro Atlanta at a time of turmoil in the global automotive sector.
- Mercedes-Benz moved its U.S. headquarters to a new building known as 1MB in the Sandy Springs suburb in 2018.
- The 1MB outpost originally promised 1,000 workers and now employs about 800.
- On May 22, Mercedes-Benz announced up to 500 more jobs would join them by next August.
- The company is making the Sandy Springs office its mothership for the United States and the whole North American market.
- The sleek office includes an EV charging hub.
- New Atlanta jobs will be brought to Atlanta in large part from Farmington Hills, Mich., where corporate and financial services are currently located.
- Those teams will join sales and marketing at 1MB.
- Mercedes-Benz announced it would locate a new research and development hub “near Sandy Springs.”
- The R&D hub will bring technical talent to the metro area from around its U.S. system.
- The move is designed to situate the company better within Atlanta’s growing tech talent pool, including new engineering grads coming out of universities like Georgia Tech.
- The move also aims to deepen collaborations with emerging startups.
- Jason Hoff, who took up the newly created Mercedes-Benz North America CEO position on May 1, said: “Bringing our teams closer together will enable us to be more agile, increase speed to market and ensure the best customer experience.”
- Details on the R&D hub were scant, other than saying it would be a “multimillion-dollar investment.”
- The R&D facility will not be co-located at the Sandy Springs office.
- A spokesperson told Global Atlanta: “We will share additional details on the R&D facility and functions as soon as we can. We look forward to our future R&D facility and bringing more technical roles to the state.”
- At its original opening seven years ago, Mercedes-Benz unveiled a mobility incubator it was planning for Buckhead.
- The Lab1886 office was tasked with dreaming up big ideas for portfolio companies within the Daimler family.
- Lab1886 was staffed with leaders from China, Germany and California.
- When Lab1886 opened, it parked a Volocopter, an early drone taxi that later went bankrupt, in the courtyard of the Terminus building where it had rented a WeWork space.
- Then-Gov. Nathan Deal and Atlanta Mayor Keisha Lance Bottoms attended the Lab1886 opening.
- In December 2020, Daimler spun off 1886Ventures from Lab1886, retaining 10 percent of the fund.
- The Atlanta office closed less than two years after its star-studded opening featuring marketing expert and technology evangelist Guy Kawasaki and other luminaries.
- Mercedes-Benz has continued to grow in the U.S., where the German luxury brand points out that it has been present for more than 120 years.
- In April, amid President Trump’s automotive tariffs, Mercedes-Benz announced it would make its GLC midsize SUV at the Tuscaloosa, Ala., factory.
- The majority of production at the Tuscaloosa factory is exported.
- Mercedes-Benz accounts for 8,000 direct manufacturing jobs in the United States and 11,100 overall.
- Mercedes-Benz is heavily concentrated in the Southeast U.S.
- Beyond the Alabama factory, Mercedes-Benz makes its Sprinter cargo vans in South Carolina.
- Mercedes-Benz sold 374,100 passenger cars and vans in the U.S. in 2024.
