# Porsche Cars North America, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Porsche commences new multi-million dollar development in Georgia adjacent to its North American Headquarters to expand its U.S. footprint - Porsche Newsroom USA](https://newsroom.porsche.com/en_US/company/porsche-cars-north-america-one-porsche-drive-development-22421.html)  ·  `page_048764`

## Facts

- Porsche Cars North America, Inc. (PCNA) announced a new multi-million dollar investment to further develop its North American headquarters and U.S. footprint in Georgia.
- The development is located at the northeast corner of Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport.
- The new 33-acre development adds to PCNA's existing 27-acre headquarters.
- The first stage of development, which starts construction this month, will be a standalone Porsche Service Center independently developed, owned, and operated jointly by Hennessy Porsche North Atlanta and Porsche Atlanta Perimeter.
- In later stages over the next three years, PCNA will invest in a new Porsche Classic Factory Restoration facility as well as a track extension for the Porsche Experience Center Atlanta.
- Kjell Gruner, newly appointed President and CEO of PCNA, stated that Porsche is committed to the United States and its American hometown of Atlanta.
- Gruner noted the expansion enhances customer experience by providing faster service at a unique location next to the world’s busiest airport.
- The expansion starts a longer-term expansion of the Porsche Experience Center Atlanta, which has welcomed over 270,000 visitors since opening in 2015.
- Governor Brian Kemp praised Porsche's growth in Georgia in partnership with two Atlanta area dealers.
- Kemp stated that Georgia’s automotive industry is a $3 billion economic driver in the state and called Porsche an important part of their success story.
- Mayor Alan Hallman of the City of Hapeville expressed excitement about PCNA launching the expansion of its North American headquarters within the Hapeville community.
- Atlanta Mayor Keisha Lance Bottoms thanked Porsche for investing in Atlanta and stated her administration looks forward to working with Porsche’s leadership to strengthen the local economy through highly skilled and middle-wage opportunities.
- The Porsche Service Center South Atlanta will be a 20,815 square foot facility on 2.5 acres of land.
- The new service center facility will include 13 lift bays to service customer vehicles, a Porsche Driver’s Selection boutique, and a reception area and waiting lounge for customers.
- It is one of two standalone service centers for Porsche in the U.S., offering greater service capacity and additional convenience for customers in Atlanta and surrounding cities.
- The expected completion date for the service center is the third quarter of 2021.
- Phase two of development is expected to begin in 2021 with construction of a parking structure.
- Phase two will be followed by construction of the new Porsche Classic Factory Restoration facility and Porsche Experience Center Atlanta track extension, with an expected completion date of mid-2023.
