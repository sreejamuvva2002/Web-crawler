## Facts

- Honda associates celebrated the long legacy of transmission production in Tallapoosa, Georgia today, as the Honda Transmission Manufacturing Plant in Georgia (TMP-G) marked its 20th anniversary.
- The Honda Transmission Manufacturing Plant in Georgia (TMP-G) is the exclusive source of the 10-speed automatic transmission system.
- Honda returned to the Advanced Clean Transportation (ACT) Expo with a display of zero-emissions power technology and clean energy experts to explore U.S. business opportunities.
- Seven Honda U.S. manufacturing plant locations earned the 2025 U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) ENERGY STAR certification for Outstanding Energy Efficiency.
- Honda is celebrating 50 years of research and development (R&D) in the United States, honoring associates and engineers who continue to research, design and develop innovative products and technologies that have shaped mobility.
- Honda announced a planned $9.658 million investment to modernize its North Carolina Manufacturing (NCM) plant, enabling associates at the facility to build critical all-terrain vehicle (ATV) parts in-house, including rear fenders, front fenders, and side components.
- Honda hosted nearly 2,000 students and teachers from more than 60 schools across ten of its U.S. production facilities in conjunction with National Manufacturing Day (MFG Day).
- Associates at the Honda Alabama Auto Plant (AAP) celebrated the start of production of the all-new 2026 Honda Passport and Passport TrailSport, the most off-road capable Honda SUV ever, built exclusively in Lincoln, Alabama.
- Honda provided a first look at its innovative approach to manufacture electric vehicles (EV) by late 2025 at the Honda EV Hub in Ohio, detailing a flexible approach that uniquely positions Honda to build internal combustion engine (ICE), hybrid, and battery electric vehicles on the same manufacturing line.
- Honda updated its progress in establishing the Honda EV Hub in Ohio, which will create flexibility to produce petrol, hybrid-electric, and battery electric vehicles on the same manufacturing line.
- Honda is making critical progress in the establishment of the Honda EV Hub in Ohio, which will serve as a roadmap for production of electric vehicles (EVs) in North America.
- All Honda U.S. and Canada auto plant locations earned the 2023 U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) ENERGY STAR Certificate for Outstanding Energy Efficiency, working toward the company's Triple Action to Zero approach.
- Associates at the Honda Indiana Auto Plant (IAP) marked the 15th anniversary of automobile production by celebrating the past and investing in students who represent the future of the local community in Greensburg.
- The environmental team at Honda’s East Liberty Auto Plant (ELP) was honored with the Ohio Environmental Protection Agency’s (EPA) prestigious Encouraging Environmental Excellence (E3) Platinum Achievement Award.
- Honda celebrated 25 years of building world-class powersports products at Honda South Carolina Manufacturing (SCM) in Timmonsville by expanding production of popular Honda side-by-side models to meet growing customer demand.
- Associates at the company’s Anna Engine Plant in Ohio celebrated production of the 30 millionth engine with the production of the newly refined 2.0-liter Atkinson-cycle four-cylinder engine, which is part of a two-motor hybrid-electric system.
- American Honda Motor Co., Inc. (AHM) celebrated the opening of its new $16 million, 66,000-square-foot Post-Production Option (PPO) facility located on the campus of Honda’s Alabama Auto Plant (AAP).
- Associates at Honda’s East Liberty Auto Plant (ELP) celebrated the beginning of mass production of the all-new 2023 Honda CR-V hybrid, following up on its launch of the gasoline-powered CR-V.
- Honda associates at the Marysville Auto Plant (MAP) in Ohio celebrated the start of mass production of the all-new 2023 Honda Accord and Accord hybrid, America's best-selling car over the last five decades.
- Associates at the Honda Alabama Auto Plant (AAP) celebrated the start of mass production of the all-new 2023 Honda Pilot, which includes the Pilot TrailSport, the most off-road capable Honda SUV ever.
- Honda announced that it will produce an all-new hydrogen fuel cell electric vehicle (FCEV) based on the recently launched, all-new Honda CR-V starting in 2024 at its Performance Manufacturing Center (PMC) in Marysville, Ohio.
