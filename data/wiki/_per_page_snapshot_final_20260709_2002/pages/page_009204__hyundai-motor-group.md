# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-19

**Source:** [Hyundai confirms $2.7-billion expansion of Georgia EV plant despite ICE raid - Los Angeles Times](https://www.latimes.com/business/story/2025-09-19/hyundai-says-it-will-spend-2-7-billion-expanding-part-of-the-georgia-complex-raided-by-ice)  ·  `page_009204`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group confirmed it is going forward with previously announced plans to expand its Georgia plant.
- The expansion confirmation came just weeks after an immigration raid delayed the startup of an electric vehicle battery plant at the site.
- Hyundai will spend $2.7 billion to increase production capacity at the Ellabell site by 200,000 over the next three years.
- The expansion will bring total production capacity at the site to 500,000 vehicles a year.
- The company first announced the expansion in March at the grand opening of the plant west of Savannah.
- Hyundai had said in August that it would invest an additional $5 billion in the United States overall.
- An immigration raid occurred at the site, which included arrests of more than 300 South Korean citizens.
- The raid led to questions about the wisdom of South Korea investing in the U.S.
- Hyundai plans to produce 10 models of electric and hybrid gas-electric vehicles in Georgia.
- This is up from the current two vehicle models the plant has been assembling as it ramps up production.
- Hyundai says it’s still on track to expand production worldwide to 5.6 million vehicles a year by 2030.
- The automaker pledged that 60% of those vehicles will be electric or hybrid-powered.
- The targeted sales markets include South Korea, North America, and Europe.
- Hyundai plans to make more than 80% of vehicles sold in the United States domestically by 2030.
- Total domestic content will increase from 60% to 80%.
- For the first time, the Georgia plant's vehicle lineup will include a mid-sized pickup truck.
- Hyundai already makes the Santa Cruz model, a four-door compact pickup, that it started selling in 2021.
- Hyundai CEO José Muñoz has said the immigration raid will delay opening the battery plant by at least two to three months.
- Spokesperson Michael Stewart said Thursday that the facility will open in the first half of 2026.
- Hyundai executives and Georgia officials have been trying to calm the situation since the raid.
- The raid mushroomed into a diplomatic dispute between South Korea and the United States.
- Republican Gov. Brian Kemp told reporters Tuesday at a ceremony marking the groundbreaking of a Rivian Automotive electric vehicle plant that he remains confident Georgia’s business advantages would “win out” in terms of foreign investment.
- Gov. Kemp said the raid may even wind up smoothing the way for South Korean employees to more easily obtain legal permission to help build and operate facilities in the United States.
- Kemp said he has had good conversations with companies that are here doing business in Georgia, and companies that are looking to do business here.
- Kemp said he has had good conversations with people in the White House about the visa issue.
- Brent Stubbs, the chief administrative officer of the Ellabell site, wrote in an opinion piece published Wednesday in the Atlanta Journal-Constitution that the company is still committed to Georgia.
- Stubbs wrote that the situation doesn’t change their plans to continue expanding and localizing in the United States.
- Stubbs wrote that their investments in America are part of a long-term strategic plan.
- The $2.7-billion investment confirmed Thursday will go to expanding capacity at the plant and group affiliates.
- That would bring total investment in the auto plant to $10.3 billion.
- This total does not count the $4.3 billion that Hyundai and its joint-venture partner, LG Energy Solution, are putting into the battery plant.
- Hyundai and its onsite affiliates currently have 3,129 employees in Ellabell.
- Hyundai pledges to hire at least 8,500 workers by the end of 2031.
- State and local governments have promised $2.1 billion in tax breaks and other incentives.
- The boost at the Ellabell site is the biggest part of Hyundai’s plan to raise production by 1.2 million vehicles a year worldwide.
- This includes an additional 250,000 vehicles out of Pune, India.
- It also includes 200,000 more at Hyundai’s electric vehicle plant in Ulsan, South Korea.
- The automaker said it would also deliver parts for assembly into an additional 250,000 vehicles at plants in Saudi Arabia, Vietnam, and North Africa.
- Hyundai underlined a previous announcement to deepen its investment in robotics.
- By 2027, Hyundai will launch extended-range electric vehicles with gasoline motors to extend the range of its electric batteries to more than 600 miles.
