# HL-GA Battery Company at the Hyundai megasite

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-05

**Source:** [‘The largest single-site operation in history’: HSI, ICE reveals number of Hyundai megasite arrests](https://www.wistv.com/2025/09/05/largest-single-site-operation-history-hsi-ice-reveals-number-hyundai-megasite-arrests/)  ·  `page_001155`

## Facts

- A news conference to announce the results of an ICE raid at the HL-GA Battery Company at the Hyundai megasite was held in Savannah, Ga., around 10:30 a.m. on September 5, 2025.
- The investigation resulted in the arrest of 475 individuals who worked for a variety of contractors and subcontractors on the site.
- The majority of those arrested were Korean nationals who entered the U.S. illegally through various means, including illegal border crossings, visa waiver violations, and visa overstays.
- HSI Special Agent in Charge Steven Schrank stated that the operation underscores a commitment to protecting jobs for Georgians and Americans.
- Most of the arrested individuals were transported to the Folkston ICE Processing Center.
- Charges have not yet been filed, and officials described the situation as an ongoing investigation with no wrongdoing accused at this time.
- There were no substantial uses of force, resulting in only one person overheating and one agent suffering a minor laceration.
- HSI Special Agent in Charge Steven Schrank confirmed this was the largest single-site operation in the history of Homeland Security Investigations in the country.
- The press conference featured participants including US Attorney Margaret E. Heap, ICE Supervisory Detention and Deportation Officer R. Brandon Eccles, FBI Assistant Special Agent in Charge Brian Ozden, ATF Resident Agent in Charge Joseph L. Waller, DEA Assistant Special Agent in Charge Brian A Villella, and U.S. Marshal Brett Hall.
- According to a press release, several individuals attempted to flee the location by running into a sewage pond on the premises, with agents using a boat to retrieve them and one individual attempting to flip the boat over before being captured as illegal workers.
- The investigation was led by Homeland Security Investigations (HSI), with assistance from U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) Enforcement and Removal Operations, FBI, ATF, DEA, IRS Criminal Investigations, DOL Office of Inspector General, U.S. Marshal Service, and Georgia State Patrol.
- Assistant United States Attorneys Tania Groover, Ryan Bondura, Kelsey Scanlon, and E. Greg Gilluly, Jr., are investigating the case.
