# Mack Trucks

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-02-09

**Source:** [Mack Invests $14.5 Million to Expand Roanoke Valley Operations](https://www.macktrucks.com/mack-news/2024/mack-invests-14-5-million-in-roanoke-valley-operations-expansion)  ·  `page_045775`

## Facts

- Mack Trucks is investing $14.5 million to expand its Roanoke Valley Operations (RVO) manufacturing facility in Virginia.
- The expansion prepares the plant for higher demand for the Mack MD Series and Mack MD Electric vehicles.
- Stephen Roy, global president of Mack Trucks, stated that Mack is committed to making industrial and product investments to be a North American market leader.
- Roy said the RVO plant expansion will help grow in a strategic market segment and support sustainability goals.
- Virginia Gov. Glenn Youngkin announced the $14.5 million investment during an event with state and local officials at RVO.
- Gov. Youngkin approved a $255,000 grant from the Commonwealth’s Opportunity Fund for the project.
- The project will result in 51 jobs.
- Roanoke County offered a package of incentives totaling $842,420.
- Gov. Youngkin said Mack Trucks’ expansion strengthens Virginia’s manufacturing industry ecosystem, which is a core focus of the Commonwealth’s economic development strategy.
- Youngkin noted that Mack's initial investment in a new Roanoke County operation four years ago has yielded a second major investment, demonstrating confidence in Virginia.
- Mack’s investment will fund equipment, tooling, and a 72,000 square-foot building expansion.
- The facility expansion increases the total size of RVO to 352,000 square feet.
- Construction on the expansion is scheduled to begin in April 2024.
- The expansion project is expected to be completed in Q4 2025.
- The Virginia Economic Development Partnership worked with Roanoke County Economic Development and the Roanoke Regional Partnership to secure the project.
- The Virginia Jobs Investment Program will support employee training activities for the project.
- Mack is eligible to receive state benefits from the Major Business Facility Job Tax Credit for new full-time jobs created as a result of the project.
- All Mack medium-duty models are produced at RVO.
- Mack launched the diesel-powered Mack MD Series in 2020.
- Mack launched the Mack MD Electric in March 2023.
- Adding the new model to the product lineup increased the need for more space at the facility.
- Roy stated that customer demand for the Mack MD and MD Electric continues to grow beyond expectations.
- The investment and expansion will position Mack Trucks for the future amid growing demand.
- The diesel-powered Mack MD and MD Electric are available in Class 6 and Class 7 ratings.
- The Class 6 model has a Gross Vehicle Weight Rating (GVWR) of 25,995 pounds.
- The Class 7 model has a GVWR of 33,000 pounds.
- Both models are exempt from the 12 percent Federal Excise Tax (FET).
- The MD and MD Electric were developed specifically for medium-duty applications with a focus on reliability, durability, and productivity.
- The trucks will serve trucking applications requiring dry van/refrigerated, stake/flatbed, and dump vocations.
- The MD6 model does not require a Commercial Driver’s License (CDL) to operate for non-hazardous payloads.
- Mack Trucks has provided purpose-built transportation solutions for 125 years.
- Mack is one of North America’s largest producers of heavy-duty trucks.
- Mack trucks are sold and serviced through an extensive distribution network in more than 45 countries.
- Mack trucks, diesel engines, and transmissions sold in North America are assembled in the United States.
- Mack manufacturing locations are certified to ISO 9001 (quality), ISO 14001 (environmental management), ISO 45001 (health and safety), and ISO 50001 (energy management).
- Mack is a sponsor of Share the Road, an American Trucking Associations public information campaign aimed at enhancing roadway safety.
- Mack Trucks is part of the Volvo Group, which offers trucks, buses, construction equipment, power solutions, financing, and services.
- The Volvo Group was founded in 1927, is headquartered in Gothenburg, Sweden, employs about 100,000 people, and serves customers in almost 180 markets.
- In 2025, the Volvo Group's net sales amounted to about $49 billion.
