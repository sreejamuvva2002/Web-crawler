# In Jobs for Georgia Tour

**Type:** event  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2021-06-07

**Source:** [ICYMI: PHOTOS: In Jobs for Georgia Tour, Senator Reverend Warnock Highlights Efforts to Support Georgia Jobs, Strengthen Federal Investments in Infrastructure and Industry » Reverend Raphael Warnock](https://www.warnock.senate.gov/newsroom/press-releases/in-jobs-for-georgia-tour-senator-reverend-warnock-highlights-efforts-to-support-georgia-jobs/)  ·  `page_003122`

## Facts

- U.S. Senator Raphael Warnock (D-GA) traveled across Georgia last week to speak with manufacturing leaders, servicemembers, and port and manufacturing workers about job growth in the state.
- The tour included stops in West Point, Warner Robins, Garden City, Savannah, and Pooler.
- During the tour, Senator Warnock highlighted how the American Jobs Plan and the U.S. Innovation and Competition Act of 2021 will catalyze local economic growth, manufacturing, technological innovation, and job creation across Georgia.
- Immediately following a two-day shutdown at Kia Georgia due to a global semiconductor shortage, Senator Warnock visited Kia's West Point factory to meet with local leadership about preventing future production halts.
- Senator Warnock secured provisions in the bipartisan U.S. Innovation and Competition Act of 2021 that would provide funding to the federal Creating Helpful Incentives to Produce Semiconductors (CHIPS) for America program to increase domestic semiconductor production.
- At Robins Air Force Base on Wednesday, June 2, Senator Warnock met with base and Georgia National Guard leadership to discuss current missions, technological advances, and resources for servicemembers and their families.
- The base supports Middle Georgia through good-paying jobs and STEM field training.
- The visit followed an announcement by Senator Warnock and United States Air Force Acting Secretary John P. Roth regarding four new operational missions coming to the base.
- On Thursday, June 3, Senator Warnock visited the Port of Savannah, which is the fastest growing port in the country and the largest single container terminal in the country.
- Georgia Ports officials stated that additional federal funding could strengthen import and export capacity by widening and deepening shipping ports.
- Senator Warnock emphasized that passing the American Jobs Plan is vital for maintaining top-level port operations, strengthening Georgia's infrastructure network, and creating good-paying jobs.
- He toured the Garden City Terminal while standing on a crane 152 feet above the ground.
- Senator Warnock visited the SeaPoint Industrial Terminal Complex located off the Savannah River, which provides deep-water access to the Port of Savannah's main channel.
- The SeaPoint complex supports sustainable innovation and high-tech job creation, featuring green technologies such as solar power panels and pollinator gardens housing over 240,000 honeybees.
- Senator Warnock stressed the urgency of passing the U.S. Innovation and Competition Act to encourage sustainable solutions that create jobs in coastal Georgia.
- At JCB Manufacturing's facility in Pooler, over 75 Georgians help manufacture and assemble vehicles that serve more than 115 dealers across the United States and Canada.
- As a member of the Commerce and Agriculture Committee, Senator Warnock expressed continued support for JCB's manufacturing operations and their contribution to Georgia's economic health and agricultural industry.
- Senator Reverend Warnock stated that the ripple impact of the semiconductor shortage negatively affects Georgia's economy and emphasized that investing in American products and domestic semiconductor production is necessary to lead.
