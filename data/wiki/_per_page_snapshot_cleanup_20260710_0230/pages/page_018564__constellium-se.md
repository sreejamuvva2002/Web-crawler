# Constellium SE

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2011-01-04

**Source:** [Constellium — Grokipedia](https://grokipedia.com/page/Constellium)  ·  `page_018564`

## Facts

- Constellium was established as an independent entity on January 4, 2011.
- The company was formed through the acquisition of Rio Tinto's Alcan Engineered Products (AEP) business, which encompassed aluminum rolled products, extrusions, and value-added services.
- The transaction involved Omega Holdco B.V., a Dutch private limited liability company incorporated on May 14, 2010, later renamed Constellium Holdco B.V.
- Apollo Global Management secured a 51% ownership stake through its funds.
- Rio Tinto retained a 39% minority interest and the French sovereign wealth fund Fonds Stratégique d'Investissement (FSI) held 10%.
- Post-acquisition, the company achieved €49 million in annual savings in 2011.
- The AEP business was rebranded as Constellium in May 2011.
- Constellium inherited legacy operations from aluminum pioneers including Pechiney and Alusuisse.
- The company generated €3.55 billion in sales for the year of its formation.
- Constellium N.V. completed its initial public offering on May 23, 2013, pricing 22.2 million Class A ordinary shares at $15 each.
- The IPO raised approximately $333 million and listed shares on the New York Stock Exchange under ticker "CSTM" and Euronext Paris.
- In August 2013, Constellium completed a €23 million modernization of its Neuf-Brisach facility in France for aluminum recycling and production.
- By April 2014, the company announced €43 million in investments for two new casthouses at its Issoire plant in France for aerospace applications.
- In May 2014, Constellium committed €15 million to expand casting and extrusion lines at its Decin facility in the Czech Republic for automotive hard alloys.
- An additional €22.5 million investment was announced in February 2015 to boost fabrication capacity.
- In April 2015, Constellium unveiled a $40 million investment at its Van Buren Township facility near Detroit, Michigan.
- The Michigan expansion added 210,000 square feet of manufacturing space and created over 200 jobs for aluminum-intensive body structures.
- In January 2015, Constellium acquired Wise Alloys, gaining control of aluminum rolling operations.
- The company committed up to $750 million through 2022 for upgrades at the Muscle Shoals plant in Alabama.
- Revenue reached approximately $7.3 billion in 2024, supported by expanded production and demand for lightweight aluminum solutions.
- Adjusted EBITDA for 2024 totaled $550 million.
- In 2018, Constellium invested in new homogenizing furnaces and casthouse equipment to boost aluminum processing capabilities.
- By 2024, the Muscle Shoals, Alabama plant received a $23 million U.S. Department of Defense grant to rebuild its casting center.
- The grant adds up to 136,000 metric tons of annual capacity focused on recycled aluminum for defense and commercial applications.
- In January 2019, Constellium acquired full ownership of its U.S. auto body sheet joint venture in Bowling Green, Kentucky, purchasing the remaining 49% stake from UACJ for $100 million plus proportional debt.
- The company signed a multi-year contract with Airbus for aerospace aluminum products supporting all programs.
- In September 2025, Constellium extended a long-term supply agreement with Embraer for advanced alloys like Airware®.
- Constellium leads the £15 million ALIVE project for aluminum battery enclosures in electric vehicles.
- The company committed to reduce Scope 1 and 2 greenhouse gas emissions by 25% by 2025 from a 2018 baseline.
- Constellium SE is a publicly traded company listed on the NYSE under ticker CSTM, with over 96% institutional ownership as of mid-2025.
- Major shareholders include T. Rowe Price Investment Management (9.75%), Fidelity Management & Research Co. LLC (9.41%), BlackRock, FMR LLC, and Bpifrance SA.
- Chief Executive Officer Jean-Marc Germain oversees overall strategy and operations.
- Senior Vice President and Group General Counsel Stephen Walters serves as Secretary to the Board.
- The Board of Directors is chaired by Jean-Christophe Deslarzes and comprises ten directors as of 2025.
- Bradley Soultz was appointed as a new non-executive director at the May 15, 2025 Annual General Meeting.
- Constellium operates through three primary business units: Aerospace & Transportation (A&T), Packaging & Automotive Rolled Products (P&ARP), and Automotive Structures & Industry (AS&I).
- The company leverages rolling, extrusion, and casting processes across 25 production facilities in Europe, North America, and China.
- Constellium employs approximately 12,000 employees globally.
- The Ravenswood plant in West Virginia employs over 1,100 workers and features advanced equipment for high-strength aluminum sheets.
- The Muscle Shoals facility in Alabama has about 1,250 employees and over 100 acres of covered production space with world-class recycling capabilities.
- The Singen, Germany facility employs more than 1,500 people and handles hot/cold rolling alongside a large extrusion press for automotive products.
- Neuf-Brisach, France has a 450,000 metric ton annual capacity for canstock, food stock, and automotive sheet.
- The Issoire, France facility employs 1,600 workers and focuses on aerospace plates using proprietary Airware® technology.
- Děčín, Czech Republic operates with 100,000 metric tons per year capacity in casting and extrusion for hard alloys.
- The Bowling Green, Kentucky facility produces flat-rolled automotive body sheet.
- The White, Georgia facility is dedicated to advanced automotive structural components and crash management systems.
- The San Luis Potosí, Mexico facility produces crash systems with integrated quality labs.
- A joint venture in Changchun, China produces automotive crash management and structural parts.
- Constellium sources aluminum inputs including primary molten metal, slabs, billets, and scrap without engaging in bauxite mining or primary smelting.
- The company uses Laser-Induced Breakdown Spectroscopy (LIBS) technology to sort high-purity automotive scrap for recycling.
- Constellium maintains three dedicated R&D centers with annual investments reaching approximately €52 million in 2023.
- The company fosters over 250 active patent families and around 50 scientific partnerships with universities and labs.
- C-TEC (Constellium Technology Center) in Voreppe, France employs about 250 specialists across 20 nationalities focusing on alloy development and decarbonization.
- The Plymouth, Michigan facility operates as a research and technology hub in the Detroit automotive corridor for tailored aluminum solutions.
- The University Technology Center at Brunel University London hosts around 50 Constellium scientists alongside 20 PhD students and postdoctoral fellows.
- Constellium supplies fuselage skins, wing panels, and structural elements for commercial and military aircraft using Airware® aluminum-lithium alloys.
- The company holds a leading position in large extrusions for railway rolling stock to enable lighter rail cars.
- In June 2025, Constellium demonstrated closed-loop recycling by remelting aluminum from decommissioned aircraft via partnership with TARMAC Aerosave.
- Constellium's extruded automotive components are up to 40% lighter than equivalent steel alternatives while maintaining comparable strength.
- These materials contribute to overall vehicle mass reductions of 10-20% in body-in-white structures.
- The company supplies aluminum structural components for BMW's X Model Sports Activity Vehicles and the 2024 BMW 5 Series.
- Lightweight alloys helped achieve a 137-pound (62 kg) weight savings for the 2024 BMW 5 Series compared to the prior model.
- The ALIVE project demonstrated 12-35% weight reductions in battery enclosures through optimized aluminum designs for electric vehicles.
- The ISA3 R&D initiative with Renault Group launched in 2021 and concluded in 2024, developing next-generation lighter aluminum components.
- Constellium deployed LIBS technology to sort high-purity automotive scrap, enabling higher recycled content without quality loss.
- The CirConAl project develops low-carbon extrusion alloys from post-consumer scrap for circular supply chains.
- Project M-LightEn, announced in February 2025, aims for unibody chassis that are 25% lighter and reduce carbon intensity by 50%.
- Constellium earned the 2018 Innovator of the Year award from the Georgia Automotive Awards for innovations in lighter, safer, and more efficient vehicles.
- Packaging operations manufacture can body stock, end stock, and tab stock using high-quality aluminum coils for beverage, food, cosmetics, and aerosol applications.
- Key packaging clients include Ball Corporation and AB InBev.
- The company uses alloys from the 1000, 3000, and 8000 series formed into coils, sheets, and foil stock.
- Aeral™ alloy reduces aerosol container weight by up to 30% relative to impact extrusion methods.
- HALO™ low-carbon solutions are used for bottle closures in wine, spirits, carbonated drinks, and olive oil.
- 42% of aluminum input is sourced from recycled materials through a closed-loop system that returns used beverage cans to shelves in approximately 60 days.
- Constellium maintains around 700,000 metric tons annually capacity for cansheet.
- The Muscle Shoals facility has 340,000 metric tons of recycling capacity.
- Packaging accounted for 39% of the company's total revenue in 2024.
- Constellium supplies aluminum products for building and construction applications including facades, solar panel frames, and thermal breaks.
- Full-year 2024 revenue totaled $7.335 billion, declining from $7.826 billion in 2023 and $8.555 billion in 2022 due to lower aluminum prices and reduced shipments.
- First half of 2025 revenue reached $4.1 billion, reflecting a 7% increase from the prior year's H1 period.
- Q1 2025 revenue was $2.0 billion (up 5% year-over-year) and Q2 2025 revenue was $2.1 billion (up 9% year-over-year).
- Shipments in Q1 2025 totaled 372,000 tons, down 2%.
- Net income for 2024 was $60 million with an adjusted EBITDA of $623 million.
- H1 2025 net income totaled $74 million ($38 million in Q1 and $36 million in Q2).
- The operating margin stood at 4.04% trailing twelve months as of June 30, 2025.
- Constellium guided full-year 2025 adjusted EBITDA at $620-650 million.
