# Freyr Battery US LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-02-06

**Source:** [Freyr Battery abandons proposed $2.6 billion Georgia factory | News | times-herald.com](https://www.times-herald.com/news/freyr-battery-abandons-proposed-2-6-billion-georgia-factory/article_bd1d4dd0-e49f-11ef-94be-1f58670d5df4.html)  ·  `page_001596`

## Facts

- Freyr Battery US LLC officially announced on Thursday that it will not build its proposed $2.6 billion electric battery factory in Coweta County.
- Climbing interest rates, falling battery prices and a change in leadership at the company all contributed to the decision, according to Jason Peace, Freyr’s senior vice president of business development.
- Jason Peace told Coweta County Development Authority members at their meeting that morning he was super ambitious about building a battery plant there two years ago but now cannot support it.
- The move was confirmed in a letter to the authority dated Jan. 21, citing a realignment of near-term strategic goals.
- Peace stated Freyr has been holding the site for two years and suggested releasing it to someone else.
- Freyr’s decision to abandon the project marks a major shift from its initial plans to establish the Giga America facility.
- The Giga America facility was expected to create 723 jobs over seven years.
- Initially announced in November 2022, the facility was to produce energy storage systems using lithium-ion batteries to store renewable energy from wind and solar power.
- Freyr’s CEO, Tom Einar Jensen, had described the project as transformative for the energy grid, enabling renewable energy to replace coal- and gas-fired power without disruptions.
- In its communication, Freyr praised the support it received from Georgia and Coweta County.
- The company described assistance from state and local officials as "stellar."
- To formally exit the project, Freyr submitted several documents, including an executed notice of prepayment and purchase option for the property incentive bond, a draft redemption notice for the county jobs grant bond, and a draft quitclaim deed release related to the project site.
- The company requested assistance from the authority to determine payoff amounts for the bonds, terminate related leases and debt documents, and finalize the transfer of the project site.
- The authority members approved the documents at their meeting.
- According to the letter, Freyr will also work with the Georgia Department of Community Affairs to confirm the repayment of grants and incentives tied to the project.
- Sarah Jacobs, executive director of the authority, said the failed project illustrates the effectiveness of performance-based incentives.
- Jacobs stated that the approach ensures taxpayers were protected through built-in clawbacks and safeguards.
- In its third-quarter 2024 financial report, Freyr disclosed a net loss of $27.5 million, or $0.20 per diluted share.
- This compared to a $9.8 million loss in the same period in 2023.
- Year-to-date losses totaled $83 million, driven by restructuring charges and declining warrant liability fair value adjustments.
- Freyr reported $184.1 million in cash reserves and no debt as of Sept. 30, 2024.
- This marked a significant drop from $275.7 million in cash reserves at the start of the year.
- Peace told authority members that the company is still committed to green energy and has acquired a nearly finished facility in Texas that will build solar panels.
- The fact that the Texas plant is almost ready to go represented “a fast path to cash” for the company, according to Peace.
- Peace stated the company was burning cash for two years trying to build a battery plant without success.
- Freyr’s 2022 announcement initially positioned Georgia as a growing hub for clean energy and electrification projects.
- The planned Coweta facility joined a wave of electrification initiatives in Georgia, including SK Battery in Jackson County and the future Rivian and Hyundai electric vehicle plants.
- The planned Coweta facility was to be near Amazon and Shopify distribution centers off Interstate 85 near Newnan.
- The site was expected to offer average salaries exceeding $60,000.
- Peace said after the meeting that the property will be sold and there have been interested parties.
- A local Georgia-area developer is looking to buy the site.
- If everything goes well, the sale could close in a couple of weeks.
- Peace was unsure of the developer’s plans for the property.
