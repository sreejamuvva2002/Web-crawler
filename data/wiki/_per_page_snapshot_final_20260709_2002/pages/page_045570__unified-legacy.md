# Unified Legacy

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-05-27

**Source:** [New Manufacturing Plant Promises $125 Million Investment and 500 Jobs in Macon – 360News.com](https://360news.com/west-central-georgia-community-news/new-manufacturing-plant-promises-125-million-investment-and-500-jobs-in-macon/)  ·  `page_045570`

## Facts

- Macon-Bibb County is preparing for a major manufacturing expansion that state and local leaders say will bring a $125 million investment and at least 500 new jobs to Middle Georgia.
- Georgia-based Unified Legacy plans to build a new manufacturing facility off Barnes Ferry Road, near Hawkinsville Road, in Macon.
- Construction is expected to begin in 2026, following an announcement from Gov. Brian Kemp’s office.
- Unified Legacy manufactures products and components for the defense, aerospace, data center, and industrial markets.
- The company already operates United Defense, a manufacturing facility in Byron that opened in 2022.
- Gov. Brian Kemp said Georgia’s manufacturing sector continues to grow because companies benefit from the state’s workforce, infrastructure, and partnership approach.
- Unified Legacy CEO Eric Williams said the Macon expansion reflects confidence in the region and will allow the company to handle larger and more complex projects as demand grows.
- The company expects to hire workers in manufacturing, skilled trades, engineering, logistics, quality control, and administrative positions.
- CEO Eric Williams stated that the goal is to create careers people can be proud of while building a workforce capable of meeting higher standards.
- Macon-Bibb Mayor Lester Miller welcomed the announcement, saying economic development and workforce development remain top priorities for the community.
