# Seoyon E-HWA

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-02-01

**Source:** [Hyundai supplier plans $76M Georgia plant, hiring 500 – WSB-TV Channel 2 - Atlanta](https://www.wsbtv.com/news/local/hyundai-supplier-plans-76m-georgia-plant-hiring-500/FMU7Q6WI6BHDVDJLA2O54ZRZ6Q/)  ·  `page_001344`

## Facts

- Seoyon E-HWA announced plans to invest $76 million in a new manufacturing plant near Savannah, Georgia.
- The facility will manufacture interior and exterior parts including door trim, headlining, and tailgate trim for Hyundai’s upcoming assembly plant in Ellabell.
- Production at the new plant is scheduled to begin in October 2024.
- The company plans to hire more than 500 workers for the new facility.
- Seoyon E-HWA currently operates a plant with 630 employees in LaGrange, Georgia, located near Hyundai’s Montgomery, Alabama plant and Kia’s West Point plant.
- The company operates 32 manufacturing units worldwide.
- Hyundai announced plans in May to build a $5.5 billion plant to assemble electric vehicles and batteries.
- Hyundai’s Ellabell plant could eventually employ up to 8,100 workers and is scheduled to begin vehicle production in 2025.
- Seoyon E-HWA is the fourth Hyundai supplier to announce plans for a southeast Georgia facility since Hyundai's May announcement.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp proclaimed his second-term goal is to make Georgia the leading state for electric-powered vehicles.
- Trip Tollison, president and CEO of the Savannah Economic Development Authority, described the new jobs as “well paying” but did not disclose specific wage details.
- Seoyon E-HWA already imports parts through Savannah’s port to supply its LaGrange facility.
- The state will cover worker training costs, though the total incentive package from state and local governments was not immediately clear at the time of the announcement.
- Seoyon E-HWA could qualify for more than $3.1 million in state income tax credits valued at $1,250 per job over five years, contingent on workers earning at least $31,300 annually.
- Hyundai Mobis previously announced plans for a $926 million powertrain plant in Richmond Hill that will employ 1,500 workers.
- Joon Georgia announced plans for a $317 million plant near Statesboro that will employ 630 workers.
- Ecoplastic announced plans for a $205 million plant in Register that will employ 456 workers.
