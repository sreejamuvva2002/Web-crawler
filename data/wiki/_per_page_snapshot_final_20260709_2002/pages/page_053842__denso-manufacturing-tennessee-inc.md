# DENSO Manufacturing Tennessee, Inc.

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2025-05-28

**Source:** [DENSO Manufacturing Tennessee, Inc., Team Members Earn CADIA Impact Award for Initiative Supporting New Mothers |  News |  News |  DENSO US/Canada Website](https://www.denso.com/us-ca/en/news/newsroom/2025/20250528-01/)  ·  `page_053842`

## Facts

- Christine Gray and Haley Penney led a project to establish and standardize lactation rooms across DENSO Manufacturing Tennessee, Inc. (DMTN) campus facilities in Maryville, Tennessee.
- The initiative increased access to comfortable and secure spaces for working mothers at the DMTN location.
- Gray serves as an electrical controls engineer and Penney works as a quality engineer at DMTN.
- Gray and Penney earned the 2025 CADIA Impact Award in the Systemic Change category from CADIA, an organization dedicated to driving opportunities for all in the automotive industry.
- The award was accepted by Gray on behalf of herself and Penney during a May 21, 2025 award ceremony in Troy, Michigan.
- In December 2022, Gray and Penney began collaborating with DENSO Women’s Associate Resource Group members and site leaders to evaluate how to better support new mothers.
- They identified existing spaces to convert into lactation rooms and developed common standards to provide private, comfortable, and safe experiences for team members.
- A key feature of the initiative was a two-in-one design concept that separated milk storage and retrieval spaces to maintain privacy and avoid disruptions during pumping.
- DMTN currently operates four lactation rooms across its campus, with two additional rooms expected to open in the fall following the May 2025 announcement.
- Once complete, there will be at least one lactation room in each building housing manufacturing or office spaces on the DMTN campus.
- Gray and Penney are also active in Science, Technology, Engineering and Math (STEM) activities aimed at encouraging local students to explore technical fields through their DENSO Women’s Associate Resource Group roles.
- CADIA presents the Systematic Change Award annually to individuals, teams, or organizations that break down systemic bias and address organizational barriers to inclusion and equity in the workplace.
- Chuntao Ye, president of DMTN and leader of the North America Peace of Mind Business Group at DENSO, stated that DENSO strives to be a place where all can succeed and that Christine and Haley embody these values.
- Victoria White of DENSO Manufacturing Athens Tennessee, Inc. won a CADIA Impact award for Creating Inclusive Cultures in 2022.
- Former DENSO Manufacturing Michigan, Inc. team member Christina Khim earned runner-up honors in the same category in 2023.
- DENSO is globally headquartered in Kariya, Japan, and operates as a $47.9 billion leading mobility supplier developing advanced technology and components for nearly every vehicle make and model.
- The company invests in around 180 facilities worldwide to produce cutting-edge electrification, powertrain, thermal, and mobility electronics products.
- DENSO employs 158,000 global employees and spent around 8.6 percent of its global consolidated sales on research and development in the fiscal year ending March 31, 2025.
- In North America, DENSO is headquartered in Southfield, Michigan, employs 27,000+ team members across nearly 50 sites in the U.S., Canada, and Mexico, and generated $12.5 billion in consolidated sales in the fiscal year ending March 31, 2025.
