# Robert Bosch Semiconductor LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** power_electronics  ·  **Published:** 2023-09-01

**Source:** [Bosch completes acquisition of assets of TSI Semiconductors](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/bosch-completes-acquisition-assets-tsi-150000427.html?fr=sycsrp_catchall)  ·  `page_018032`

## Facts

- Bosch completed the acquisition of assets of TSI Semiconductors Corporation on August 31, 2023.
- The acquired assets form a new company called Robert Bosch Semiconductor LLC.
- Thorsten Scheer is heading the new organization as plant manager of the Roseville site and regional president of the Bosch Automotive Electronics division in North America.
- California Competes Tax Credit incentive of $25 million was approved to support a planned $1.5 billion investment into the Roseville site by Bosch.
- In April 2023, Bosch announced its intention to acquire certain assets of TSI Semiconductors.
- Starting in 2026, the first silicon carbide (SiC) chips will be produced on 200-millimeter wafers at the Roseville site.
- Bosch plans to invest some $1.5 billion to transform the Roseville site into a facility that produces and tests silicon carbide semiconductors, which are key building blocks for electric vehicles.
- Michael Budde, President of Bosch Automotive Electronics, stated the company is systematically reinforcing its global portfolio of SiC chips to advance electromobility.
- SiC chips enable greater range and more efficient recharging in electric vehicles by using up to 50 percent less energy.
- Paul Thomas will assume the role of president, Americas for Bosch Mobility beginning January 2024.
- Approximately 250 local associates at the Roseville site are now part of the global Bosch family.
- The Roseville location has nearly 40 years of extensive experience in the design and production of semiconductors for automotive and industrial applications.
- Production at the Roseville site will be built up gradually, and the location is expected to grow.
- Thorsten Scheer is a physicist with a doctorate in economics and brings 10 years of professional experience in the sensor business, along with expertise in post-merger integration.
- Over the next few years, Bosch intends to invest more than $1.5 billion USD in the Roseville site to upgrade manufacturing facilities to state-of-the-art processes.
- The full scope of the planned investment will be heavily dependent on federal funding opportunities.
- Bosch intends to apply for federal funding under the CHIPS and Science Act.
- The Governor's Office of Business & Economic Development (GO-Biz) approved the $25 million California Competes Tax Credit incentive to support redevelopment and investment in Roseville.
- Thorsten Scheer stated that Bosch looks forward to a strong, long-term partnership with the City of Roseville, Placer County, and the State of California.
- The semiconductor experts in Roseville and existing clean-room facilities will allow Bosch to manufacture SiC chips for electromobility on an even larger scale.
- As of December 31, 2022, the Bosch Group employs roughly 421,000 associates worldwide across nearly every country.
- Bosch generated $93.1 billion in sales in 2022, with $15.1 billion from the U.S., Canada, and Mexico combined.
- Bosch employs approximately 85,500 associates in research and development at 136 locations globally, including nearly 44,000 software engineers.
- Ninety-four percent of the share capital of Robert Bosch GmbH is held by Robert Bosch Stiftung GmbH, a charitable foundation.
- The majority of voting rights for Robert Bosch GmbH are held by Robert Bosch Industrietreuhand KG, an industrial trust.
