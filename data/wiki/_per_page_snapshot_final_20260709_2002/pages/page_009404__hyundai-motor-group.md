# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-06-19

**Source:** [Georgia Governor Promises Fast-Track Support for Hyundai's New U.S. Plant](https://fnnewsglobal.com/economy/article/443595/)  ·  `page_009404`

## Facts

- Top executives of Hyundai Motor Group’s key affiliates met with Georgia Governor Brian Kemp during his visit to South Korea to explore strengthening cooperation.
- The State of Georgia expressed willingness to expand investment support for Hyundai Motor Group, including facilitating early operation of its new EV factory, Metaplant America (HMGMA).
- Hyundai Motor Group executives attended the "Night of Georgia" event at the Conrad Hotel in Yeouido, Seoul on June 17 to exchange views with Governor Kemp on activating the company's U.S. mobility business.
- The "Night of Georgia" event was organized by the State of Georgia to strengthen economic partnerships with Korean companies that have or are interested in establishing bases in the state.
- Attending Hyundai Motor Group representatives included Chang Jae Hoon (President & CEO of Hyundai Motor Company), Song Ho Sung (President of Kia), Jose Munoz (Global COO of Hyundai Motor Company), Lee Gyu Suk (President of Hyundai Mobis), Seo Kang Hyun (President of Hyundai Steel), and Yeo Su Dong (President of Hyundai Transys).
- Local partners of Hyundai Motor Group, including Daehan Solution and Dongwon Metal, also attended the meeting.
- Other attendees included Philip Goldberg (U.S. Ambassador to Korea), Park Won Cheol (President of SKC), Kim Dong Myung (President of LG Energy Solution), and Lee Koo Young (CEO of Hanwha Solution Q CELLS division), all of whom have established operations in Georgia.
- Trade between Georgia and South Korea totals approximately $15.8 billion.
- This trade volume accounts for about 10% of Georgia's total trade.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Korean companies have become integral to Georgia's local community and expressed gratitude for the ongoing partnership expected to bring prosperity to both regions.
- Governor Kemp promised Hyundai Motor Group that the state would help accelerate their product launch timelines in the U.S. market.
- Governor Kemp offered full state support for any potential Hyundai Motor Group investments in hydrogen technology.
- Georgia Minister of Economic Development Pat Wilson expressed strong interest in future hydrogen cooperation and praised Hyundai Motor's hydrogen-electric Xcient truck as highly innovative.
- Hyundai Motor Company President & CEO Chang Jae Hoon stated the company is pleased to partner with Georgia and other major Korean companies on a sustainable future.
- Chang noted that Hyundai's new Georgia factory, which will soon produce the latest EVs, marks a significant milestone for eco-friendly mobility.
- Chang emphasized that meetings in Seoul have strengthened the partnership between Korean companies and the State of Georgia.
- Georgia serves as the primary operational base for Hyundai Motor Group's U.S. business.
- Since the 2010 completion of the Kia Georgia plant, Hyundai Motor Group has produced over 300,000 strategic vehicles annually for the U.S. market.
- Hyundai Motor Group is constructing Metaplant America (HMGMA), scheduled to begin operations as early as October, alongside a joint battery cell factory and a battery system factory.
- The State of Georgia provides extensive financial and infrastructure support for factory construction and operation, including property tax reductions, income deductions, tax breaks on construction machinery and materials, and assistance with land purchases and road construction.
