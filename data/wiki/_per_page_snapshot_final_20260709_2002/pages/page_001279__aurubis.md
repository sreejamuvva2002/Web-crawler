# Aurubis

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Aurubis presents corporate strategy: focus on efficiency, stronger returns and US growth](https://www.euwid-recycling.com/news/business/aurubis-presents-corporate-strategy-focus-on-efficiency-stronger-returns-and-us-growth-091025/)  ·  `page_001279`

## Facts

- Aurubis plans to capitalize on megatrends such as electrification, artificial intelligence, and the modernisation of energy infrastructure.
- The group unveiled a revised corporate strategy entitled "Performance 2030: Forging resilience. Leading in multi-metal" at its annual capital market day held in London on Wednesday.
- Aurubis aims to strengthen its position as a leading recycler and supplier of copper and other metals with greater emphasis on operational excellence, profitability, and sustainable value creation.
- CEO Toralf Haag stated the company is focusing on expanding its strong market presence while continuing to set industry standards in processing complex raw materials, metal recovery, technological leadership, sustainability, and operational excellence.
- After a phase of investment-driven expansion, Aurubis is shifting its focus to generating targeted returns from new assets and strengthening cash flow.
- Aurubis is one of only a handful of companies able to recover 20 different metals from a wide range of primary and secondary raw materials.
- The company's integrated smelter network in Europe and the US provides scale, flexibility, and operational resilience.
- Aurubis intends to further optimise its multi-metal production by improving material flows, alleviating bottlenecks, and maximising metal yields.
- Particular emphasis is being placed on expanding recycling operations in the US.
- The company recently commissioned the "Aurubis Richmond" site in Georgia.
- CEO Toralf Haag described the Richmond site as "the first greenfield smelter in the US in more than a century".
- The Georgia plant represents the group's largest investment outside Europe at around €740m.
- The facility is designed to process about 180,000 tonnes of complex recycling materials such as printed circuit boards, copper cables, and other metal-bearing products.
- Aurubis expects its US subsidiary to contribute around €170m to annual Ebitda from the 2027/28 financial year.
- Toralf Haag noted that their timely entry into the US market, unique capabilities, and a supportive funding environment form a strong foundation for further growth in one of the most attractive metal markets in the world.
- Aurubis sees little potential for growth in battery recycling at the moment and announced it will not make any further investments in this segment for the time being.
- The company stated that while technical feasibility of recovering key valuable materials from black mass has been demonstrated, the European black mass market is still in an early stage of development.
- Aurubis intends to pursue an "asset-light" approach in cooperation with partners to minimise risk in this slow-growing market.
- Completed and ongoing investment projects are expected to help raise the group's return on capital employed (ROCE) in the operating business to 15 per cent in the longer term.
- For the current 2025/26 financial year, which began on 1 October, management is targeting an operating ROCE of 7-9 per cent.
- Aurubis is forecasting operating Ebitda of €580-680m and operating earnings before tax (EBT) of €300-400m for the current financial year.
- The company announced a change in its dividend policy, with the payout ratio to rise to up to 30 per cent of operating post-tax group earnings from the 2025/26 financial year on.
- Salzgitter remains Aurubis's largest shareholder with a holding of nearly 30 per cent, while drugstore chain founder Dirk Rossmann held around 11 per cent as of 6 October.
