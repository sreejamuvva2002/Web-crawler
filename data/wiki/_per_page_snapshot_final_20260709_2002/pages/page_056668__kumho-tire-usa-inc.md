# Kumho Tire USA Inc.

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2016-05-04

**Source:** [Eight Years Later, Kumho Officially Opens Macon Plant  - Global Atlanta](https://www.globalatlanta.com/eight-years-later-kumho-officially-opens-macon-plant/)  ·  `page_056668`

## Facts

- Kumho Tire USA Inc. officially opened its Macon plant on May 2, 2016, eight years after initially announcing plans for the facility.
- The company invested $450 million in the new facility.
- The plant has a production capacity of 4 million tires per year.
- The grand opening ceremony was attended by Georgia Governor Nathan Deal and local leaders.
- The facility is described as the largest industrial building to grace Macon since the 1970s.
- Governor Nathan Deal stated he would express deep appreciation for Kumho’s continued investment in Georgia and praised the company's leadership vision that flourished over the last 10 years.
- Gov. Deal highlighted the strong relationships between Kumho, the community, and the state as a model for the culture of collaboration they work to create.
- Kumho Tire USA Inc. will supply tires to Kia Motors, Hyundai, and Chrysler for the original-equipment market (tires sold to auto makers for brand new vehicles).
- The U.S. accounts for 22 percent of tires consumed globally.
- Park Sam-gu, chairman of Kumho Asiana Group, stated that North America is home to one of the world’s largest auto and tire markets and that a production base there is crucial for expanding their presence to become a top tire manufacturer.
- The project faced uncertainty after its initial 2008 announcement was derailed by the global recession of 2009.
- Millions of dollars in state grants were used to prepare the site at Sofkee Industrial Park.
- In December 2014, the Macon-Bibb County Industrial Development Authority approved a new bond issue of up to $475 million for the project, enabling it to move forward again.
- The project was initially slated at $225 million, but delays allowed for new equipment that substantially increased the investment figure.
- Automotive investment ignited by the Kia plant has made Korea one of Georgia’s largest source nations for foreign direct investment.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development maintains a trade and investment office in Seoul.
- Governor Deal has made multiple visits to South Korea during his two terms and touted the “marriage of diverse and talented cultures” in Georgia and Korea at the event.
- Kia Motors recently celebrated the completion of its 2 millionth vehicle in Georgia since opening in 2009.
- Chris Carr, commissioner of the Georgia Department of Economic Development, stated that Kumho’s investment exemplifies how Georgia’s thriving automotive sector, including a robust network and equipped workforce, helps leading companies flourish.
- The state-of-the-art plant sits on 130 acres with buildings occupying 861,000 square feet.
- The facility is capable of producing one tire every 36 seconds.
