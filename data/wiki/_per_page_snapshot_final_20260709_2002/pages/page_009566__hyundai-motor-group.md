# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-04-22

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor, Matrix Renewables sign solar energy deal for EV plant in US | Aju Press](https://www.ajupress.com/view/20240422172350249)  ·  `page_009566`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group will use solar energy to power its electric vehicle plant currently under construction in Georgia.
- Hyundai has signed a 147-megawatt power purchase agreement (PPA) with Matrix Renewables.
- The carmaker will receive electricity generated from Matrix Renewables' 210-megawatt solar power project located in Texas.
- The Texas solar power project is slated to begin operations in October 2025.
- This agreement represents the largest renewable deal a Korean company has ever signed in the United States.
- Hyundai plans to procure 378 gigawatt hours of solar energy annually under the deal.
- The procured solar energy is enough to power about 100,000 households each year.
- The solar electricity will be used for Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA).
- HMGMA is scheduled to start commercial operations in October 2025.
- The solar electricity will also be used for three affiliate local plants: Hyundai Mobis North America Electrified Powertrain, Hyundai Steel Georgia, and Hyundai Transys Georgia Powertrain.
- The deal is expected to enable the automotive group to reduce 140,000 tons of carbon emissions.
- This emission reduction is equivalent to the annual emissions from around 84,000 mid-sized sedans.
- Hyundai stated in a press release that the agreement is significant as it represents the group's substantial move towards securing renewable energy sources.
