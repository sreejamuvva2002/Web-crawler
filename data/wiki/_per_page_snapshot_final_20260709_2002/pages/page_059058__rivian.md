# Rivian

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2021-12-17

**Source:** [Rivian reveals location of second US plant - electrive.com](https://www.electrive.com/2021/12/17/rivian-reveals-location-of-second-us-plant/)  ·  `page_059058`

## Facts

- Rivian has decided to build its planned second US plant to the east of Atlanta, in the state of Georgia.
- Once completed, the plant should be able to produce up to 400,000 electric vehicles per year.
- Construction is scheduled to begin in the summer of 2022 and production is to start in 2024.
- The plant is to be located in Morgan and Walton counties.
- The company says there are plans for a “carbon-conscious campus.”
- In the summer of 2021, there were still rumours that Rivian was planning combined vehicle and battery cell production.
- Rumours regarding the location being Mesa, Arizona or Fort Worth, Texas proved to be wrong.
- The company wants to invest up to five billion dollars (around 4.4 billion euros) in the project in Georgia.
- Investments will flow into buildings, production facilities, and site development.
- Environmental compatibility, the company’s own renewable energy generation, and green spaces are also to play a role.
- The 2,000-acre site will also include “abundant natural space.”
- The site was chosen because of its advantageous logistic position, the availability of clean energy, and the number of available potential employees.
- Rivian plans to employ up to 7,500 people on site.
- Rivian will develop community engagement and workforce training programs in the new location area, similar to its plant in Normal, Illinois.
- Rivian Chief People Officer Helen Russell stated: “We’re happy to partner with Georgia on our new manufacturing site, which will build our next generation of products that are important for scaling our business.”
- In summer 2021, the company secured a number of vehicle names without giving details on what vehicle types these will be.
- Helen Russell stated: “We’re confident that, alongside Illinois, Georgia is the place for Rivian to continue to scale and thrive.”
- Rivian announced an expansion for its current sole plant in Normal, Illinois.
- The electric automaker received approval for a 623,000 square-foot expansion, bringing its total space to more than four million square feet.
- That would be an expansion of nearly 58,000 square feet to just over 370,000 square feet.
- The additional space at the Illinois plant would be used for warehouse and manufacturing capacity.
- Rivian went public in November 2021.
- Rivian confirmed the Illinois expansion in its first-ever post-IPO shareholder report.
- Production capacity at the first plant would be increased from 150,000 to 200,000 vehicles annually.
- The first R1T vehicles have already been delivered.
- In total, there are around 71,000 pre-orders for the R1 alone.
- Rivian has not yet revealed more on its planned European plant.
- A site in the UK south of Bristol and the Netherlands is in running for the European plant location.
- Rivian might be taking over VDL Nedcar’s existing site in the Netherlands.
- These plans appear to have pushed out US startup Canoo.
- Canoo had already placed a contract manufacturing facility at VDL Nedcar, but announced it would not have its Lifestyle Vehicle built there after all.
- This is because Rivian might not only use the Dutch production plant VDL NedCar for its own production but take it over completely.
- As of an update on 03 May 2022, Rivian can count on subsidies and tax breaks totalling $1.5 billion (€1.43 billion) for its second US plant in Georgia.
- The plant is expected to employ around 7,500 people and is scheduled to open at the end of 2024.
- Under the agreement with the state of Georgia, Rivian has until the end of 2028 to reach the 7,500 jobs target and the $5 billion investment target to receive the full amount of incentives and tax savings.
