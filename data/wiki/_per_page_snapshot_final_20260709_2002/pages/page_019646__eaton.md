# Eaton

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** power_electronics  ·  **Published:** 2023-08-31

**Source:** [Eaton invests $500M in North American manufacturing | Manufacturing Dive](https://www.manufacturingdive.com/news/eaton-electrical-utilities-power-manufacturing-500-million-expansion-north-america/692200/)  ·  `page_019646`

## Facts

- Eaton is investing $500 million to grow its North American manufacturing capacity.
- The investment will expand production for the company’s utility, commercial, healthcare, industrial and residential markets.
- Most investments will be completed in 2024 and 2025.
- Eaton’s plans include expanding multiple manufacturing facilities, including in Texas, Wisconsin and South Carolina.
- The company is also expanding distribution centers in Chicago and Dallas.
- Mike Yelton, president of the Americas region electrical sector at Eaton, stated that electrical infrastructure has to work harder and smarter to accelerate decarbonization and electrification.
- Yelton added that steady investments are being made for customers, distributors and employees to meet growing demand in the Americas, with more to come.
- Eaton is doubling the size of its Nacogdoches, Texas manufacturing facility with a 200,000 square foot addition.
- The Texas expansion gives the company twice the production capacity for voltage regulators, which are power unit components used by utility customers to modernize grids.
- Eaton will continue producing multiple types of transformers at the Texas site to address an ongoing shortage of electrical grid products.
- In Wisconsin, the company is expanding its largest facility in Waukesha.
- The Wisconsin expansion increases production capacity for three-phase transformers for utility, data center, commercial and industrial use.
- In South Carolina, Eaton will increase production of components for cable buses, called busways, to meet growing demand.
- Eaton has a well-defined presence in South Carolina, operating in multiple locations including a site in Greenwood that was expanded in 2020.
- The company is investing in distribution capacity to support its increased manufacturing power.
- Eaton recently opened its largest regional distribution center in Chicago.
