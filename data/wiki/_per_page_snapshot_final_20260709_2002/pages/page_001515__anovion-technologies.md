# Anovion Technologies

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Anovion Technologies Announces Plans For $800 Million Initial Investment in New Manufacturing Facility in Southwest Georgia - 40k Metric Tpa of Synthetic Graphite Anode Material for Lithium-ion Batteries - Batteries News](https://batteriesnews.com/anovion-technologies-announces-plans-800-million-initial-investment-in-new-manufacturing-facility-in-southwest-georgia-40k-metric-tpa-of-synthetic-graphite-anode-material-for-lithium-ion-batter/)  ·  `page_001515`

## Facts

- Anovion Technologies announces plans for an $800 million initial investment in a new manufacturing facility in Southwest Georgia.
- The facility will produce 40k metric tpa of synthetic graphite anode material for lithium-ion batteries.
- Anovion selected Decatur County in Southwest Georgia as the location for its first large-scale expansion of manufacturing capacity for premium synthetic graphite anode materials.
- The initial facility will be 1,500,000 square feet.
- The location was chosen based on proximity to existing and planned low- and carbon-free energy sources, short supply chains, access to existing rail infrastructure, a highly valuable skilled workforce, and a business-friendly environment.
- Eric Stopka is the Chief Executive Officer of Anovion.
- Anovion conducted a thorough search to identify a location addressing energy, transportation, logistics, and human capital needs for producing high-quality sustainable synthetic graphite anode material.
- Existing infrastructure makes Southwest Georgia attractive, and proximity to other battery and EV manufacturing plants will help reduce carbon footprint.
- Governor Brian Kemp welcomed Anovion to Georgia, calling it the No. 1 state for business and fast becoming the e-mobility capital of the nation.
- Governor Kemp noted that job creators recognize every corner of the state has resources and workforce needed for success.
- Governor Kemp expressed looking forward to celebrating their partnership with Georgians of Decatur County after meeting their team personally.
- In October 2022, Anovion was awarded a grant totaling $117 million from the U.S. Department of Energy under the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law.
- The grant aims to help secure the domestic battery manufacturing supply chain for the country's electrified future.
- The new expansion facility will be the first of this scale for Anovion and in North America.
- It is a crucial first step to reshoring synthetic graphite anode production.
- Once fully operational, the facility is expected to initially produce 40,000 metric tonnes per annum.
- The facility will create hundreds of high-quality local clean energy jobs for the surrounding region.
- Edward Reynolds is the Bainbridge Mayor.
- He welcomed Anovion to Bainbridge.
- The investment involves $800 million and the creation of over 400 high-paying, quality jobs.
- Mayor Reynolds stated the workforce is ready to embrace the opportunity and that the community will grow and prosper together.
- Anovion will use an innovative, sustainable, and technologically advanced manufacturing process at the new facility.
- The process relies on the region's rapidly decarbonizing energy mix to power manufacturing.
- It introduces proprietary furnace technologies that substantially reduce greenhouse gas emissions.
- This lays out a clear roadmap for Anovion to achieve a carbon-neutral footprint over time.
- Chip Dunn is the Executive Chairman of Anovion.
- The company's long-term strategic vision focuses on growth and innovation, positioning Anovion as a leader in the U.S. energy transition.
- A key goal includes securing a domestic source for critical materials required for U.S. lithium-ion battery production.
- Focus areas include continued R&D in electrochemistry, technology improvements in production processes, and ambitious climate tech initiatives.
- The ultimate goal is to become carbon-neutral over time, mitigate greenhouse gas emissions, and position Anovion as a climate tech leader.
- Anovion is excited to bring its full arsenal of innovation to the Georgia facility to execute its long-term business strategy and combat the climate crisis.
- In addition to efforts in Georgia, Anovion is expanding its Anovion Center of Excellence.
- The company is also expanding a hydro-powered sister manufacturing facility near Niagara Falls, NY.
- The Niagara Falls facility is notably one of the only qualified U.S. sources of battery-grade synthetic graphite that is commercially shipping product today.
- Anovion Technologies is headquartered in Chicago, Illinois.
- It is a U.S.-owned and U.S.-based advanced materials business.
- It is currently one of America's first commercial-scale manufacturers of premium, cleaner anode-grade synthetic graphite.
- The company's existing footprint includes facilities near Niagara Falls, NY and Clarksburg, WV.
- Anovion began commercial production in early 2021.
- It is among a limited number of graphite anode producers in America to have successfully gained qualification for EV applications.
- In 2022, Anovion was awarded the $117 million DOE grant to rapidly scale production capacity to meet growing demand.
- Plans include construction of a large-scale factory in the southeastern U.S. and investment in its relocated Anovion Center of Excellence (ACE) in Sanborn, NY.
- Anovion is commencing a multi-year expansion program of 150,000 metric tonnes of annual capacity for anode-grade synthetic graphite.
- The announcement was made on May 15, 2023.
