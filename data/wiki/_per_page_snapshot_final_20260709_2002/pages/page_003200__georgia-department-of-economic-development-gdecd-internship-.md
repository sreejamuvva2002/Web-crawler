# Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) Internship Program

**Type:** workforce_training  ·  **Role:** workforce_training  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [GDEcD Internship Program | Georgia](https://georgia.org/about-us/career-job-search/gdecd-intern-program)  ·  `page_003200`

## Facts

- The Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) Internship Program gives students and recent graduates hands-on experience working with professionals who promote Georgia’s economy, tourism, and creative industries.
- Interns gain real-world exposure to research, marketing, communications, and event planning while building valuable professional connections.
- Internships are unpaid, with academic credit available when required, and are designed for learning and career exploration.
- Eligible candidates must be undergraduate or graduate-level students, or recent graduates within one quarter or semester following graduation.
- Candidates must complete a minimum of one year of higher education (two semesters or three quarters) before starting the internship.
- The internship must begin no later than the quarter or semester immediately following completion of one year of higher education.
- Candidates must have a GPA of 3.0 or higher.
- Candidates must be residents of Georgia or attend a Georgia institution of higher learning.
- Interns must agree to the terms and conditions specified by GDEcD.
- Interns seeking academic credit must provide program requirements no later than at the time an internship is accepted.
- Interns must commit to a regular schedule of 20 to 40 hours per week for a period of 2 to 8 months.
- Specific terms will be discussed and agreed upon in advance by the intern and their assigned supervisor.
- Parking expenses will be reimbursed, but no additional personal expenses such as housing, food, or mass transit are reimbursed.
- Interested applicants must submit an application and cover letter to apply.
- The cover letter must include dates of availability for the internship, with a minimum duration of two months and a maximum of eight months.
- The cover letter must include academic credit requirements for the applicant's institution, if any.
- Only candidates selected for an interview will be contacted due to the large volume of applications received.
- The Arts Intern gains hands-on experience in grant administration, communications, and project coordination while learning how the arts support Georgia’s economic development.
- Arts Intern responsibilities include assisting with grant applications and reviews, coordinating communications with artists and arts organizations, supporting research, and helping with special initiatives such as Capitol exhibits.
- Strong writing and organizational skills are preferred for the Arts Intern role, and an arts-related major is a plus.
- The Southeast Asia Initiatives Intern supports outreach to Southeast Asian investors and government delegations.
- Southeast Asia Initiatives Intern duties include assisting with trade missions and visits, developing promotional materials, and supporting special events and trade shows focused on the Southeast Asia region.
- The International Trade Intern supports efforts to help Georgia companies expand exports.
- International Trade Intern responsibilities include market and country research, analyzing trade data, responding to client requests, and assisting with event recruitment.
- An interest in global trade, current affairs, and strong research and communication skills are preferred for the International Trade Intern role.
- The Legal Intern provides support to the General Counsel’s office, gaining experience in public-sector legal work.
- Legal Intern responsibilities include legal research, contract review, open records requests, and assisting with rules, regulations, and incentive agreements.
- Candidates for the Legal Intern role must be current or recently graduated law students.
