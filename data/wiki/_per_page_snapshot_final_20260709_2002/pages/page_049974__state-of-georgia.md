# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Georgia’s Republican Politicians Said No, But Took The Dough | Climate Power](https://climatepower.us/research-polling/georgias-republican-politicians-said-no-but-took-the-dough/)  ·  `page_049974`

## Facts

- Since the passage of the clean energy plan, 40 new clean energy projects have created 29,472 jobs and invested $23.12 billion in Georgia.
- 32 of Georgia’s 40 clean energy projects are located in Republican-held districts.
- These 32 projects represent 82% of all clean energy projects in the state.
- The projects in Republican-held districts account for $20.4 billion in investment and over 19,105 new jobs.
- Despite these benefits going to communities across Georgia, state Republican politicians have voted against, voted to repeal, and consistently attacked the clean energy plan.
- Governor Brian Kemp has welcomed 40 new clean energy projects to Georgia since the plan's passage, generating 29,472 new jobs and $23.12 billion in investments.
- Georgia ranks first in the nation for clean energy jobs and is fast becoming a hub for EV manufacturing.
- Governor Kemp has touted Georgia as the “electric mobility capital of America” and sought to make it the “EV capital of the nation.”
- Kemp has repeatedly criticized the Inflation Reduction Act, stating it “hurt us in a lot of different ways” and “hurt Georgia more than it’s helping.”
- Kemp has been “honored” and “excited” to welcome clean energy manufacturing operations, electric vehicle production, and manufacturing investments in the state.
- Rep. Earl “Buddy” Carter represents Georgia’s 1st Congressional District, which has attracted eight new clean energy projects generating 7,222 new jobs and $7.81 billion in investments.
- Carter voted against the Inflation Reduction Act and claimed it was “a bad deal for Georgia and threatens key economic investments that employ thousands of Georgians.”
- Carter co-wrote an op-ed with seven other Republican representatives claiming the law is “full of empty promises that harm some of Georgia’s flagship companies.”
- Carter celebrated Hyundai’s EV and battery plant in his district, saying it would “diversify and expand our economy.”
- Rep. Drew Ferguson represents Georgia’s 3rd Congressional District, which has attracted four new clean energy projects generating 1,163 new jobs and $2.9 billion in investments.
- Ferguson voted against the Inflation Reduction Act, falsely claiming it will raise taxes on hardworking Americans, result in fewer jobs, reduce lifesaving health care innovation, squeeze out American energy producers, spend over $350 billion on radical “Green New Deal” initiatives, and further the economic recession.
- Ferguson co-wrote an op-ed with seven other Republican representatives claiming the law is “full of empty promises that harm some of Georgia’s flagship companies.”
- Ferguson celebrated Daesol Ausys’ investment in a manufacturing facility for EV components and accessories.
- Ferguson celebrated Kia’s investments to expand its electric SUV plant in his district, touting both projects’ job creation.
- Rep. Richard McCormick represents Georgia’s 6th Congressional District, which has attracted one new clean energy project generating 200 new jobs and $19 million in investments.
- While campaigning for his seat, McCormick criticized the clean energy plan, wrongly claiming it will not reduce inflation, re-ignite the economy, or make the country safer, stronger, or more prosperous, and that it raises taxes and boosts energy prices.
- Rep. Andrew Clyde represents Georgia’s 9th Congressional District, which has attracted three new clean energy projects generating 304 new jobs and $67 million in investments.
- Clyde voted against the clean energy plan and argued Democrats are doubling down on failed policies, wreaking havoc on Biden’s broken economy, burning through billions on liberal projects, contributing to national debt, and burdening Americans with tax hikes amidst economic crises.
- Clyde co-wrote an op-ed with seven other Republican representatives claiming the law is “full of empty promises that harm some of Georgia’s flagship companies.”
- Clyde said he was “proud” of SK Battery America’s investments in EV battery production in his district, touting the project’s job creation.
- Rep. Mike Collins represents Georgia’s 10th Congressional District, which has attracted four new clean energy projects generating 410 new jobs and $141 million in investments.
- Despite these benefits to his district, Rep. Collins voted to repeal the clean energy plan.
- Rep. Barry Loudermilk represents Georgia’s 11th Congressional District, which has attracted three new clean energy projects generating 5,560 new jobs and $7.48 billion in investments.
- Loudermilk voted against the clean energy plan and co-wrote an op-ed with seven other Republican representatives claiming the law is “full of empty promises that harm some of Georgia’s flagship companies.”
- Rep. Rick Allen represents Georgia’s 12th Congressional District, which has attracted seven new clean energy projects generating 2,736 new jobs and $1.46 billion in investments.
- Allen voted against the clean energy plan and argued it is a bloated Democrat spending bill that will raise taxes during a recession, while Biden and congressional Democrats focus on socialist Green New Deal initiatives instead of addressing the economic crisis.
- Allen co-wrote an op-ed with seven other Republican representatives claiming the law is “full of empty promises that harm some of Georgia’s flagship companies.”
- Rep. Marjorie Taylor Greene represents Georgia’s 14th Congressional District, which has attracted two new clean energy projects generating 1,110 new jobs and $515 million in investment.
- Despite voting against the clean energy plan and continuing to deny climate change, Greene told POLITICO that “we’re excited to have jobs” in response to QCells’ announcement to invest $2.5 billion in Georgia to expand its solar panel manufacturing plant in Dalton and build a new facility in Bartow County.
- Greene co-wrote an op-ed with seven other Republican representatives claiming the law is “full of empty promises that harm some of Georgia’s flagship companies.”
- Greene celebrated Qcell’s investment in a solar module factory in her district, saying “we’re excited to have jobs.”
