# YKK (USA) Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- Bernard was laid off from an auto factory in Detroit before relocating to Macon to seek employment.
- Bernard secured a job at a zipper plant owned by the US subsidiary of Japanese zipper manufacturer YKK.
- The Macon facility is recognized as the most productive YKK plant globally.
- Foreign real estate holdings in Georgia are largely driven by British, Dutch, Belgian, and West German investors seeking direct air service to Atlanta.
- Former Governor George Busbee aggressively courted Japanese business investment.
- Japanese investment in Georgia increased almost sevenfold within the past decade, reaching $184 million.
- Georgia ranks fourth among US states in attracting Japanese business people, trailing California, New York, and Tennessee (which hosts a large Nissan Motors plant).
- In total investment value, Georgia ranks fifth, behind South Carolina, where Japanese interests hold a major aluminum-processing facility.
- Atlanta's status as a hub with a sizable consular corps and 19 foreign banks channels international investment into the rest of the state.
- Investors are attracted to Georgia by relatively low labor costs, mild winters, and a growing population.
- Yoshinori Tsujimoto, Japanese consul in Atlanta, notes that Southerners' company-loyalty ethic and strong family ties align well with Japanese business culture.
- Over 100 Japanese firms operate in Georgia, including 20 manufacturing companies, with only one having withdrawn while others expand.
- The YKK complex in Macon represents the largest Japanese investment in the state.
- Yoshinori ("Kenny") Kitano serves as executive vice-president of YKK (USA) Inc. and acts as the unofficial dean of Georgia's Japanese business community.
- Worldwide, YKK generates annual sales of some $2 billion to $3 billion.
- YKK competes with entrenched market leader Talon through strict cost control and tailored customer service.
- YKK adapts its service model to specific customers, such as delivering small orders like 17 yellow zippers to a single NYC client via bicycle.
- Founder and president Tadao Yoshida established a "cycle of goodness" philosophy emphasizing profit, societal enrichment, and worker happiness.
- The Macon complex utilizes decentralized management and vertical integration of production lines to eliminate inter-plant shipping of unfinished goods.
- A filament extrusion plant for zippers operates with only three workers per shift, maintained at that level for safety reasons.
- Approximately 400 YKK employees from Japan have visited the Macon facility over the past decade to study its operations.
- YKK replaces all machinery every five years as a standard operational practice.
- Some 96 percent of YKK sales in the US are for domestically manufactured zippers.
- US zipper demand declined from some 2.5 billion ten years prior to about 1.5 billion today due to the garment industry's migration overseas.
- Garmentmakers in South Korea and Taiwan purchase YKK zippers through YKK's Korean and Taiwanese subsidiaries, which compete with YKK (USA).
- Mr. Kitano attributes Japanese business competitiveness to fierce daily domestic rivalry.
- Mr. Kitano expressed distress over US manufacturers abandoning competition, stating the younger generation is forgetting to fight.
