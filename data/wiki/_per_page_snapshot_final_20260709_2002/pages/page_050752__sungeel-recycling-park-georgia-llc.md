# SungEel Recycling Park Georgia, LLC

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2023-09-21

**Source:** [Final installment of SungEel Q&A series - WNEG](https://wnegradio.com/final-installment-of-sungeel-qa-series/)  ·  `page_050752`

## Facts

- Proposed SungEel battery recycling facility is scheduled to be located at Hayestone Brady Industrial Park in Stephens County, Georgia.
- Groundbreaking on the Hayestone Brady facility was originally announced for March 2023 but has been delayed due to equipment upgrades and building drawings, with a new timeline pending general contractor selection.
- In July 2022, an explosion at SungEel HiTech’s Hungary plant seriously injured one worker and left three others with minor injuries.
- The Hungarian explosion was caused by a gas leak due to faulty equipment installation by an outside vendor prior to the facility opening; the site was not yet in operation for battery recycling at the time.
- The Georgia facility will use vacuum-type vessels instead of positive pressure type vessels, which will not be used in US facilities.
- SungEel Hungary operations were fined by Hungarian authorities because battery scrap was stored beyond permitted volume, prompting the company to implement its own management process for compliance.
- A spokesperson addressed reports of inspectors suffering rashes from touring the Hungary plant, stating it was an exaggerated local article and SungEel never received hospital diagnosis notifications or bills for such incidents.
- The company withdrew plans for a battery recycling plant in Endicott, New York, due to public outcry in 2020, and similarly withdrew plans for a location two hours north of Nuremburg, Germany, earlier in the year.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) stated they communicate with community partners during site selection and expect all companies to comply with federal, state, and local laws.
- SungEel will have fire extinguishing systems such as sprinklers and sensors for initial detection in accordance with FM Global standards, which are US fire regulations/insurance regulations.
- SungEel met with local fire personnel and E911 last year and plans a workshop with the Fire Marshall and E911 before operation to ensure confidence in safety commitments.
- Feedstock/scrap will be stored in a safe condition and discharged by water or electric discharge before coming to the warehouse, with oxygen input eliminated in the process to eliminate fire risk during workflow.
- Governor Brian Kemp announced in August 2022 that SungEel Recycling Park Georgia, LLC would invest more than $37 million in the new recycling facility.
- The project will bring 104 new jobs to Stephens County, including positions for technicians, operators, administrative and sales staff, with no management positions mentioned.
- The average pay range for the 104 jobs is above the county’s current average pay wage of $22.85/hour.
- SungEel plans to utilize North Georgia Technical College’s services for employee training and hiring, with full expectation to hire locally qualified individuals.
- Stephens County Development Authority (SCDA) Chairman Brian Akin noted the SCDA worked with SungEel for eight months learning about the company and its processes before the August 2022 announcement.
- GDEcD joined Kemp in welcoming the facility, citing Georgia’s official interest in the electric vehicle industry and believing SungEel’s proximity to other members of Georgia’s electric mobility ecosystem will lead to success.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated, “SungEel is a company at the cutting edge of sustainable technology, and we believe SungEel’s proximity to other members of Georgia’s electric mobility ecosystem will lead to amazing success.”
- The facility will not use an incinerator or natural gas combustion, utilizing an electric-powered dryer instead.
- The recycling process has been used since 2008, but equipment has been upgraded for the Georgia site.
- The process is fully compliant with Federal and State permitting regulations, and the air permit will be obtained prior to starting operation and made available to the public upon submission.
- All materials are recycled for beneficial reuse, with nothing going to a landfill or city wastewater system that cannot be processed without issue.
- SCDA President and CEO Brittany Ivey noted negotiations were ongoing for an MOU including incentives, job creation commitments, investment timeline, and land sale terms, noting the SCDA is asking full price for the land in Hayestone Brady.
- If the MOU stays the same, the project will put SungEel on the tax digest within the first two years of operation.
- Ivey noted a shift from 2017 when they gave land away to now asking full price for it with SungEel.
- In September 2022, SungEel stated an expected production start date of December 2023.
- The Georgia proposal uses an electric-powered dryer instead of the natural gas-fired dryer included in the New York proposal, and will recover more electrolyte material.
- SungEel confirmed there are no PFAs released in the process and is actively working with EPD to ensure nothing exceeds state or federal regulations.
- The facility is not a storage facility; it will have separate areas for raw materials and short-term finished goods storage.
- SungEel committed to six-month employee training in fire precaution procedures required at all other facilities, after meeting with local fire personnel, the EMA director, and the county’s commercial building inspector.
