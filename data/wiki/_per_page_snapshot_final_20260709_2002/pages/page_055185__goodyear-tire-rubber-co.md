# Goodyear Tire & Rubber Co.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2025-02-20

**Source:** [Tariffs threaten Goodyear’s continued sales rebound | Manufacturing Dive](https://www.manufacturingdive.com/news/goodyear-forward-q4-2024-sales-rebound-looming-tariffs-canada-mexico/740370/)  ·  `page_055185`

## Facts

- Goodyear Tire & Rubber Co. exceeded its Q4 2024 expectations with a net income of $76 million, a huge jump compared with last year’s net loss of $291 million, due to actions under its corporate transformation plan.
- The tire manufacturer’s Q4 net sales were down 3.3% year over year at $4.9 billion and the full year’s net sales were down nearly 6% at $18.9 billion due to decreased tire units sold, unfavorable pricing and reduced inventory in its Americas and Asia Pacific segments.
- Net income for the full year skyrocketed to $70 million compared to 2023’s $689 million net loss, driven by a slight bump in segment operating income amid the company’s corporate strategy overhaul.
- The rubber tire maker’s corporate transformation plan was initially announced in 2023 and includes streamlining its portfolio and production to reduce costs.
- Other goals under the plan include generating $2 billion from divesting and seeking alternatives for its businesses, such as its chemical division.
- So far, the company has closed on the $905 million sale of its off-the-road tire business to The Yokohama Rubber Co. on Feb. 3.
- Last month, Goodyear announced an agreement to sell the Dunlop brand to Sumitomo Rubber Industries for approximately $701 million, set to close in mid-2025.
- The company also set aside $1 billion as part of the restructuring plan, according to EVP and CFO Christina Zamarro on a Feb. 14 earnings call.
- Other priorities under the plan include cutting $1.5 billion in costs by the end of the year through optimizing manufacturing plants, supply chain and research and development efforts.
- Last month, the company announced plans to refocus its Danville, Virginia, facility on producing rubber mixing and aviation tires.
- The shift at the Danville facility will include reductions in production capacity and the layoff of approximately 850 workers.
- Goodyear expects to complete the Danville facility transformation plan by year’s end and spend between $130 million to $140 million implementing the job cuts and facility transformation.
- While scaling down one plant, the company plans on increasing capabilities at its Lawton, Oklahoma, facility through a modernization project that will add about 10 million units of new capacity for premium tires in 2025 and 2026.
- President and CEO Mark Stewart stated the company will accelerate the introduction of new products to more effectively compete in the premium tiers and capitalize on blank space opportunities in the U.S.
- Goodyear will introduce five new product lines in the U.S. this year, each with significant improvements in the large rim SKU coverage than the predecessor lines.
- Last month, Goodyear named Don Metzelaar SVP of global manufacturing and supply chain, and Alain Kohnen VP of manufacturing Americas and global plant optimization to help with its revamp goals.
- The rubber tire maker seeks to generate $750 million in 2025, but could lose momentum as a result of the Trump administration’s tariffs and ensuing higher raw material costs.
- Looming tariffs on Mexico and Canada could reduce the company’s flexibility to use its global manufacturing facilities to meet tire demand, as Goodyear and other Tier 1 tire makers source their volumes from factories located in those countries.
- Goodyear is in talks with government officials about the tariff issue and looks forward to continuing collaborations with leaders in Washington to address critical issues impacting its business.
- The company is working across its operations to mitigate any potential near-term impacts of tariffs related to its Canadian and Mexican supply, remaining agile as the situation may be fluid.
- The company is also anticipating the European Commission’s decision on whether or not to place reciprocal tariffs on the U.S.
- Goodyear expects raw material costs to increase by about $350 million in the first half of 2025, according to CFO Christina Zamarro.
