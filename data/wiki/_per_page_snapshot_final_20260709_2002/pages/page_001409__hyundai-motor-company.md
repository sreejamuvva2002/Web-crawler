## Facts

- South Korean automaker Hyundai has announced a second large supplier for its new electric car plant on the Georgia coast.
- Hyundai Mobis plans to invest nearly $1 billion in a new facility in Bryan County.
- The new Hyundai Mobis facility could employ around 1,500 workers.
- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp announced the Hyundai Mobis investment and job details in a news release.
- The Hyundai Mobis plant will be located in Richmond Hill.
- The facility will make powertrains for Hyundai’s electric vehicles manufactured at its new plant scheduled to open in 2025.
- Hyundai’s new EV plant is located about 30 miles (48 kilometers) west of Savannah.
- The Hyundai Mobis plant will supply components for a Kia plant in West Point.
- The Hyundai Mobis plant will also supply components for another Hyundai plant in Montgomery, Alabama.
- Earlier this month, Gov. Brian Kemp announced that Joon Georgia would invest $317 million.
- Joon Georgia will hire 630 workers for a Hyundai parts plant in Statesboro.
- Gov. Brian Kemp stated that Hyundai’s new electric vehicle and battery manufacturing facility is located in Bryan County.
- Kemp described the project as unleashing transformational job creation and investment in that region of Georgia.
- Gov. Brian Kemp attended a groundbreaking ceremony for the new Hyundai plant in Bryan County in October.
- The new Hyundai plant is projected to make up to 300,000 vehicles a year.
- Georgia officials describe the Hyundai project as the largest economic development project the state has ever seen.
