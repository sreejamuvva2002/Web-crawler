# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA)

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hyundai breaks ground at new EV and battery plant in the US | Autocar Professional](https://www.autocarpro.in/news-international/hyundai-breaks-ground-at-new-ev-and-battery-plant-in-the-us-113137)  ·  `page_060473`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America in Georgia will manufacture EVs for Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis brands.
- The plant has a production capacity of 300,000 units with a start of production targeted for the first half of 2025.
- Hyundai Motor Group announced a US-only $5.54 billion (Rs 45,943 crore) investment to establish itself as a global top-tier electric vehicle OEM.
- On October 25, Hyundai held a groundbreaking ceremony for the EV-only plant in Bryan County, Georgia.
- Approximately $1 billion (Rs 8,293 crore) is anticipated to be invested by non-affiliated HMGMA suppliers in other locations.
- Attendees at the groundbreaking included Georgia Governor Brian Kemp, U.S. Senators Raphael Warnock and Jon Ossoff, U.S. Representative Buddy Carter, U.S. Department of Commerce’s Don Graves, Chairman Chung Eui-sun, Hyundai Motor President Jae-Hoon Jang, and Global Chief Operating Officer Jose Munoz.
- Hyundai Motor Group Executive Chair Euisun Chung stated that Hyundai and Georgia share qualities of respect for history, ingenuity, creativity, and determination to improve the next generation's world.
- Chung noted that Hyundai's EVs are recognized as best-in-class and the company aims to become a global leader in electrification, safety, quality, and sustainability.
- Chung added that HMGMA will help the company evolve beyond an automaker into a leading global mobility solutions provider.
- HMGMA will be built on a 11.83 million square metre site capable of mass-producing 300,000 EVs annually.
- Construction is scheduled to begin in the first half of next year.
- The plant will use a flexible manufacturing system to produce multiple EV types for Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis brands to meet local demand.
- The Electric-Global Modular Platform (E-GMP) will underpin EVs ranging from hatchbacks to full-size SUVs across the three brands.
- The three Hyundai Motor Group US production bases are adjacent, enabling economies of scale in parts procurement and supply chain management.
- HMGMA is located approximately 420km from Kia Georgia and about 510km from Hyundai Motor America's manufacturing subsidiary in Alabama.
- HMGMA will utilize a manufacturing innovation platform developed at Hyundai's Singapore Global Innovation Center (HMGICS).
- The HMGICS platform features a demand-oriented AI-based intelligent control system, eco-friendly low-carbon methods for carbon neutrality and 100% renewable energy use, and human-friendly facilities.
- Hyundai Motor Group plans to sell 3.23 million EVs globally by 2030, targeting a 12% market share.
- The company aims to sell 840,000 EVs in the US by 2030.
- In the first three quarters of this year, Hyundai Motor and Kia sold 47,095 EVs in the US, a 212.0% year-over-year increase.
- Hyundai sold 22,418 Ioniq 5 units (up 142.3%), while Kia sold 24,677 EVs including 17,564 EV6 units, resulting in a 322.2% sales increase.
- The proportion of EVs in Hyundai Motor Group's total US sales rose from 1.3% in January-September last year to 4.3% in the first three quarters of this year.
- Hyundai expects US EV sales to grow explosively once HMGMA begins full-scale operations.
- The company plans to establish a stable local procurement system for both finished EVs and batteries.
- Hyundai plans to form a strategic alliance with a global battery company to build a battery cell factory near HMGMA.
- The joint battery plant will develop and mass-produce batteries optimized for Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis EVs.
- Hyundai is accelerating its electrification transition by building EV production bases in both Korea and the US.
- Hyundai Motor will build a new EV plant at its Ulsan driving test site, while Kia will build a PBV EV plant in Autoland Hwaseong.
- Both Korean plants will begin mass production in 2025, aligning with HMGMA's timeline.
- By 2030, Hyundai (including Genesis) will have an 18-EV lineup and Kia will have 13 EVs, producing 1.44 million EVs annually in Korea.
- Last year, Hyundai Motor and Kia's domestic production increased 12% compared to 2004 levels, with finished car exports increasing by 79% over the same period.
