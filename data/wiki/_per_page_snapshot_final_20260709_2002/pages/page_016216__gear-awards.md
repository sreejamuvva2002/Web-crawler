# GEAR Awards

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2022-01-20

**Source:** [Two Foreign Firms Take Home Georgia Automotive Awards - Global Atlanta](https://www.globalatlanta.com/two-foreign-firms-take-home-georgia-automotive-awards/)  ·  `page_016216`

## Facts

- Two foreign-owned suppliers were selected to receive 2021 GEAR Awards, a recognition bestowed by the Georgia Department of Economic Development for contributions to the state’s automotive industry.
- YKK (U.S.A.) Inc., a Japanese-owned company with a longstanding factory in Macon, was named supplier of the year in the large companies category (those with more than 200 employees).
- YKK produces zippers for seats, hook-and-loop fasteners, plastic hardware, and snaps and buttons for seats and truck tonneau cover hold-downs.
- YKK counts 100 customers in the auto sector.
- YKK executives have long been advocates for smooth, predictable rules in the North American trade in cars.
- YKK set up shop in Macon in the 1970s, becoming Georgia’s first Japanese investor.
- The GEAR award for YKK was announced in December and presented at a Jan. 19 ceremony in Macon.
- Andrew Capezzuto, chief administrative officer and general counsel of GDEcD, stated that “The 2021 GEAR award for supplier of the year recognizes companies that are good corporate stewards,” while presenting the award in Macon.
- Solvay, a Belgian chemical company with an Alpharetta-based specialty polymers division, received recognition for its growing impact on Georgia.
- Solvay’s division provides plastics that can withstand extreme temperatures, high pressures and corrosive chemicals for automotive applications.
- Solvay received the GEAR Innovation and Emerging Technology Award for introducing Amodel Supreme polymer, designed for the high-voltage environments of electric vehicles, a segment where Georgia is making a strong global investment push.
- Solvay also produces the similar Amodel BIOS thermoplastic, which is made partially from biological materials and produced using renewable energy.
- Solvay was slated to be a key stop on Belgian Princess Astrid’s tour of Georgia during a trade mission last October.
- The Solvay global CEO was scheduled to fly over for the event.
- The trade mission has been postponed until June due to COVID-19, with a new itinerary yet to be released.
- Comer-based SMI Composites won an award in the 199 employees or fewer category.
- Douglas-based Rock Solid Cargo was named transportation equipment manufacturer of the year.
- Nivel Manufacturing in Cairo received the corporate citizenship award.
- The GEAR awards began in 2016, with foreign companies perennially posting positive showings and sometimes dominating most of the field.
