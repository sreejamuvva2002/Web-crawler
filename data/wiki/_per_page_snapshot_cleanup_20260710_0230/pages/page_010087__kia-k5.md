## Facts

- The third generation Kia Optima is built and manufactured in West Point, Georgia, which began in 2011 with the 2012 model.
- US specification models will be assembled at Kia's West Point, Georgia assembly plant (KMMG).
- Production of the K5 in the United States ended in 2024 with the KMMG plant shifting its focus to the Telluride and Kia's EV lineup.
- American models are now sourced from Korea.
- In September 2011, the Optima Hybrid set a Guinness World Record for 'Lowest Fuel Consumption in a Hybrid Gasoline Vehicle' while driving across the continental United States for 14 days, starting from the Kia factory in West Point, Georgia.
- The Optima Hybrid uses the Hyundai Sonata Hybrid powertrain, combining a 2.4-liter MPI engine with a six-speed automatic transmission.
- The Optima Hybrid powertrain includes a 30kW electric motor and lightweight lithium polymer batteries to produce a full gasoline-electric hybrid.
- The Optima Hybrid has an estimated fuel consumption of 37mpg‑US (6.4L/100km; 44mpg‑imp) city and 39mpg‑US (6.0L/100km; 47mpg‑imp) highway.
- The plug-in hybrid variant features a 50kW electric motor and 2.0-liter 'Nu' four-cylinder GDI engine, mated to a six-speed automatic transmission.
- Retail deliveries of the Kia Optima PHEV began in the United States in January 2017 with 40 units sold.
