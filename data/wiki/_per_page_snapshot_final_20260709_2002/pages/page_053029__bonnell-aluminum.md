# Bonnell Aluminum

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2016-03-03

**Source:** [Bonnell Aluminum to Invest $18 Million, Expand Automotive and Specialty Markets - Heat Treat Today](https://www.heattreattoday.com/industries/automotive-heat-treat/bonnell-aluminum-to-invest-18-million-expand-its-automotive-and-specialty-markets/)  ·  `page_053029`

## Facts

- Bonnell Aluminum announced it will invest approximately $18 million over the next 12 months to fund an expansion project.
- The investment will include the purchase of a new state-of-the-art aluminum extrusion line at its AACOA facility in Niles, Michigan.
- Planned startup for the new line is the second quarter 2017.
- Brook Hamilton, President and General Manager for Bonnell Aluminum, stated that growing customer demand prompted the investment to satisfy opportunities with high-quality products and services.
- Ira Endres, Vice President Sales and Marketing, noted that AACOA serves the automotive market due to its regional location alongside consumer durables and machinery/equipment markets.
- The new line will be comprised of a state-of-the-art 3600-ton extrusion press housing a 9-inch container, handling systems, and ancillary equipment.
- Capacity for the new line is estimated at approximately 16 million pounds.
- The project includes additional floor space to accommodate future value-added fabrication capacity.
- Bonnell Aluminum is a subsidiary of Richmond, Virginia-based Tredegar Corporation (NYSE:TG).
- The company produces soft and medium-strength alloy aluminum extrusions for building and construction, automotive, machinery and equipment, consumer durables, transportation, electrical and distribution markets.
- On October 1, 2012, Bonnell Aluminum acquired AACOA, a manufacturer, fabricator, and anodizer of aluminum extrusions with operations in Niles, MI, and Elkhart, IN.
