# FREYR Battery

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-02-27

**Source:** [FREYR Battery (NYSE:FREY) Q4 2022 Earnings Call Transcript - Insider Monkey](https://www.insidermonkey.com/blog/freyr-battery-nysefrey-q4-2022-earnings-call-transcript-1126788/)  ·  `page_054990`

## Facts

- FREYR Battery announced a target start of production for its U.S. project in 2025.
- The acceleration of the U.S. project is driven by the Inflation Reduction Act, which aims to reduce energy inflation and capitalize regional energy security for the United States.
- IRA incentives can create as much as 50% direct incentives relative to a battery producer's cost structure.
- For every gigawatt hour of batteries produced in the U.S., FREYR sees as much as $37 million in incentives per gigawatt hour of capacity installed yearly until 2030, tapering off until 2032.
- The site selection process for the U.S. project culminated in an attractive site in Coweta County, Georgia.
- Land acquisition for the Georgia site occurred in November 2022 as a key milestone for the development of the U.S. project.
- The U.S. project was initially expected to lag the Giga Arctic project by 10 to 12 months but is being accelerated due to market opportunity.
- FREYR is evaluating multiple options to move startup production from an estimated 2026 into 2025.
- Engineering and production line equipment for the U.S. project will be replicable from the Giga Arctic project in Norway.
- Multiple customers have expressed a preference for FREYR to move downstream into module and DC block production.
- Coweta County approved a $20 million land grant for the project, which was closing in the coming days at the time of the call.
- The State of Georgia has an application pending for a $7 million land grant that should be approved and closed in the next two to three months.
- A $230 million tax abatement incentive program with Coweta County has closed.
- FREYR is in the process with the State of Georgia to close a $140 million tax abatement package.
- The total incentives provide approximately $410 million over multiple phases of the project.
- Meeting minimum capital investment and job creation requirements across two growth phases will be necessary to secure the incentives.
- FREYR is building up a team in the U.S. to initiate the first re-engineering required for an accelerated delivery of the plant.
- The company approached close to 20 suppliers, including some U.S.-based ones, to secure raw material volumes for the Giga America acceleration.
- FREYR's SemiSolid technology platform is already in commercial production in Japan at triple-digit megawatt-hour scale.
- The gigawatt-hour scale SemiSolid platform is positioned to be up to 50% more cost effective than fourth quartile producers.
- The cell design features a much larger electrode that will structurally fit better with ESS and EV configurations than current battery solutions.
- The process dramatically simplifies manufacturing, significantly reducing space, CapEx, energy, and labor consumption.
- FREYR has already set its mark on the E-Mobility sector with an off-take agreement for E-Mobility Solutions earlier in the year.
- The company has ongoing RFQs and partnership discussions with EV-focused OEMs targeting double to triple digit gigawatt hours scale annually.
- FREYR's LFP-based focus is unlocking finance and value accretive opportunities upstream for large-scale plant facilities over time.
- Jeremy Bezdek serves as Executive Vice President of Corporate Development and President of FREYR Battery U.S., leading the U.S. developments.
- The company ended 2022 with $563 million in cash, cash equivalents, and restricted cash with no debt, supporting its balance sheet strength.
