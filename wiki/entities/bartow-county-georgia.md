# Bartow County, Georgia

**Type:** Government Program  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[government-support|Government Support]]  ·  **Location:** Northern Atlanta exurb, about 25 miles northwest of I-285, Georgia, Bartow County, Georgia, United States

## Overview

Bartow County’s rapid economic-development boom is driven by the Highland 75 master-planned industrial park and a series of large EV-related manufacturing investments, including an Hyundai Motor Group/SK On battery plant, a Qcells solar-module facility, and a Hanwha Advanced Materials Georgia component plant.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Host to multiple electric-vehicle battery and materials manufacturing projects; the county’s incentives and infrastructure are key to building a regional EV supply chain.

- **Highland 75 Industrial Park** — 782-acre corporate/industrial park certified under the Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) program.
- **Hyundai Motor Group / SK On EV Battery Manufacturing Facility** — Joint venture to produce electric-vehicle battery cells.
- **Qcells Manufacturing Facility** — Renewable-energy solutions provider building a large manufacturing plant.
- **Hanwha Advanced Materials Georgia (HAGA) Facility** — Manufacturer of lightweight advanced materials supplying the Qcells facility.

## Timeline

- **2004** — Bartow-Cartersville Joint Development Authority formed to split expenses and revenues between city, county, and school systems.
- **2019** — The Foxfield Co. purchased 800+ acres next to the Anheuser-Busch plant for $10.6 million.
- **December 2023** — Hyundai Motor Group/SK On announced joint venture for EV battery manufacturing facility (600 acres, ~$5 billion).
- **January 2024** — Qcells announced $2.5 billion manufacturing facility on 381 acres in Highland 75.
- **March 2024** — Hanwha Advanced Materials Georgia (HAGA) announced $147 million plant to supply Qcells.
- **Spring 2024** — Site work began on both the Hyundai/SK On battery plant and the Qcells facility.
- **2025 (expected)** — Qcells manufacturing facility slated to open.
- **2020** — Anheuser-Busch plant expansion valued at $150 million completed.
- **August 2003** — Booth Western Art Museum opened.
- **December 2021** — Savoy Automobile Museum opened.
- **2022** — Booth named America’s best art museum by USA Today (2020-2022).
- **2021** — LakePoint Sports named 2021 Sports Facility of the Year finalist.

## Key facts

- Bartow County’s population was 112,816 according to the July 2022 census.
- Highland 75 is certified under Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD), allowing fast-track construction.
- Bartow is one of only seven sole-commissioner counties in Georgia, enabling rapid decision-making for economic development.
- Approximately 20 million square feet of Class A industrial space has been built or planned in the county over the past 7-8 years.
- The county added roughly 10,000 jobs in the last ten years, largely driven by logistics and manufacturing growth.
- Proximity to Metro Atlanta and the Appalachian Regional Port in Murray County makes Bartow a logical corridor for institutional investors.
- The Chamber of Commerce hired a workforce development director and maintains the ForBartow.com job portal.
- Two new parking decks are under planning: a three-level structure on Main Street and another adjacent to a future 70,000-sq-ft county administration building.
- Cartersville Farmers Market sales exceeded $703,000 in 2022 and were $55,000 ahead of the previous year.
- Attendance at the Savoy Automobile Museum has exceeded expectations since its December 2021 opening.
- LakePoint Sports drives an estimated $100 million annual economic impact for the region.
- The county’s master plan for 16,500 acres of undeveloped land includes a wildlife management area and limited development along Cassville-White Road.
