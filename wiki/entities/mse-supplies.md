# MSE Supplies

**Type:** Company  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[battery-materials|Battery Materials]]  ·  **Location:** Georgia, United States

## Overview

MSE Supplies provides battery materials-including cathode material NMC111 and Ampcera solid electrolyte products-that are used in Georgia Tech research on low-cost, fast production of solid-state batteries for electric vehicles.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Supplier of cathode and solid electrolyte materials used in Georgia Tech solid-state battery research aimed at EV applications.

## Timeline

- **2021** — Research on electrolyte melt infiltration for solid-state batteries published in Nature Materials (Xiao et al.).
- **March 8** — The new melt-infiltration technique reported in Nature Materials.

## Key facts

- MSE Supplies supplied the cathode material NMC111 used in Georgia Tech solid-state battery research.
- MSE Supplies offers Ampcera solid electrolyte materials (sulfide, LLZO) for order.
- The melt-infiltration technology enables low-temperature (200-300 °C) fabrication of solid-state batteries using existing Li-ion production lines.
- This process could allow automotive lithium-ion batteries to use nonflammable ceramic electrolytes while maintaining current manufacturing processes.
- Solid-state batteries produced by this method may require only heaters instead of expensive cooling systems, reducing vehicle cost.
- The technique is compatible with a broad range of electrode chemistries and could increase energy density by over 20 % now and more than 100 % in the future.
- Researchers believe the approach could enable lighter, safer, more energy-dense EV batteries and help lower battery pack costs below $70/kWh needed for mass-market adoption.
- The research team is further developing electrolytes with even lower melting points and higher conductivities using the same melt-infiltration method.
