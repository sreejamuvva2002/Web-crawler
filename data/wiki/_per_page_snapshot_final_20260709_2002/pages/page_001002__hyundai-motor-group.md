# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-05

**Source:** [ICE Raid Detains Hundreds Of Workers At Hyundai's Georgia Battery Factory](https://carbuzz.com/hyundai-motor-group-metaplant-america-georgia-ice-raid-details/)  ·  `page_001002`

## Facts

- Federal government conducted a raid on a construction site at the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America compound near Savannah, Georgia.
- More than 400 individuals were reportedly detained during the immigration enforcement action.
- A vast majority of the detained individuals are said to be South Korean nationals.
- The US Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms and Explosives claimed the federal government apprehended around 450 people in the raid on Thursday.
- Around 300 of those apprehended individuals are from South Korea.
- ICE spokesperson Lindsay Williams stated the raid focused on the construction site for a new large EV battery plant, which had been previously touted as a major manufacturing win for the state and for America.
- The raid is part of an ongoing criminal investigation into allegations of unlawful employment practices and other serious federal crimes.
- The raid involved officers from the Georgia State Patrol, FBI, DEA, ATF, Homeland Security, ICE, and possibly more in one of the largest immigration enforcement actions taken by any agency in years.
- A Hyundai spokesperson stated the company is cooperating fully but doesn't believe any of the detained individuals were Hyundai employees.
- Hyundai confirmed awareness of the incident at the HL-GA Battery Company construction site in Bryan County, Georgia.
- Hyundai stated it is closely monitoring the situation and working to understand specific circumstances.
- Hyundai understands that none of those detained are directly employed by Hyundai Motor Company.
- Hyundai prioritizes the safety and well-being of everyone working at the site and complies with all laws and regulations wherever it operates.
- Hyundai is committed to full compliance with employment verification requirements and immigration laws in every market where it operates.
- Hyundai expects the same commitment from all partners, suppliers, contractors, and subcontractors.
- The safety and well-being of everyone working at Hyundai sites, whether directly employed or for suppliers, is a top priority.
- Incidents like this highlight the importance of robust oversight throughout Hyundai's entire supply chain and contractor network.
- Hyundai is reviewing its processes to ensure all parties maintain high standards of legal compliance.
- This includes thorough vetting of employment practices by contractors and subcontractors.
- North America Chief Manufacturing Officer Chris Susock will assume governance of the entire megasite in Georgia.
- Hyundai will conduct an investigation to ensure all suppliers and their subcontractors comply with laws and regulations.
- Hyundai has zero tolerance for those who don't follow the law.
- Hyundai continues to invest in American manufacturing and create thousands of jobs in full accordance with U.S. law and in a manner that reflects its values of treating all people with dignity and respect.
- CNN reports that video showed US agents having construction workers in hard hats line up to show documentation.
- The nationalities and identities of hundreds of apprehended people have not been released.
- South Korean Foreign Ministry spokesperson Lee Jaewoong confirmed a 'large' number of South Korean nationals were detained by the US government.
- Lee Jaewoong stated that business activities of investors and rights of nationals must not be unjustly infringed during US law enforcement.
- Diplomats from South Korea's Washington D.C. embassy were dispatched to Atlanta to form an on-site response team for the situation.
- Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Alabama has reportedly stopped producing the Electrified GV70, possibly for good.
- Construction activity at the new battery plant has ceased.
- The battery plant is being built through a joint venture between Hyundai and LG, dubbed HL-GA Battery Company.
- The joint venture stated it was cooperating fully with appropriate authorities.
- The new battery plant was slated to go online next year.
- The plant is said to be the largest single economic development in the history of the State of Georgia when completed and lumped into the rest of the 3,000-acre facility.
- The attached Georgia EV manufacturing plant did not have operations interrupted by the raid.
- The EV plant went online in March with production of Ioniq 5 and Ioniq 9 EVs.
- The EV plant is expanding to more capacity for producing Kia-branded models next year.
- The EV plant will add Genesis-branded vehicle production further in the future.
- Hyundai's current investment total for its Georgia manufacturing footprint is around $7.6 billion and growing.
