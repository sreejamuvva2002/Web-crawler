# Rivian Manufacturing Facility - Stanton Springs North

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-04-30

**Source:** [Rivian DOE Loan Amendment Boosts Georgia Plant Capacity | InsiderFinance](https://www.insiderfinance.io/news/rivian-doe-loan-amendment-boosts-georgia-plant-capacity)  ·  `page_049075`

## Facts

- Rivian amended its Department of Energy (DOE) construction loan on April 30, 2026.
- The amendment reduces total borrowing to $4.5 billion and reconfigures the Stanton Springs North plant plan to raise initial annual output.
- Rivian stated that the capacity increase will lower per-unit manufacturing costs.
- Production of the R2 midsize SUV remains on schedule for late 2028.
- The original DOE loan finalized in early 2025 was $6.6 billion.
- Rivian cut its DOE construction loan by $2.0 billion from the original amount.
- The amended $4.5 billion package includes $4.006 billion of principal and $494 million of capitalized interest.
- The loan will be structured in tranches tied to the updated facility design and capacity.
- The first draw from the loan is expected in early 2027.
- Despite the reduced loan size, the overall construction timeline remains unchanged.
- The Stanton Springs North site spans about 2,000 acres in southern Morgan and Walton counties, roughly an hour east of Atlanta.
- The plant will open with an initial annual capacity of 300,000 vehicles, a 50% increase from the prior 200,000 plan.
- The higher opening capacity aims to improve utilization of capital-intensive parts of the plant early on.
- This change aims to lower per-vehicle manufacturing costs and accelerate the production ramp.
- The plant will primarily produce the R2 midsize SUV.
- The facility is designed to support up to 50,000 fully autonomous Uber robotaxis annually under a commercial partnership starting in late 2028.
- Rivian has committed $5 billion of company capital to the facility.
- Rivian expects to employ about 7,500 workers at the site, including roughly 500 dedicated to robotaxi production.
- Preparatory work is underway on the stamping-press area.
- The stamping-press area is described as one of the most capital-intensive and technically demanding parts of the project.
- Construction activity at the main buildings will accelerate this summer (2026) as vertical construction begins.
- Vertical construction is slated to start in summer 2026.
- Phase-one production and robotaxi manufacturing are targeted for late 2028.
- Rivian stated in a press release on April 30, 2026: “The amended schedule and site design allows Rivian to ramp up its Georgia production efforts more quickly.”
