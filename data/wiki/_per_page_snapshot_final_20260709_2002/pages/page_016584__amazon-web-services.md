## Facts

- AWS announced an $11 billion investment to develop data centers in Georgia.
- The initiative aims to support growing demand for artificial intelligence and cloud computing technologies while boosting the local economy and advancing sustainability goals.
- The plan involves construction of state-of-the-art data centers in Butts and Douglas counties.
- These facilities will leverage high-performance GPUs to handle complex workloads such as machine learning and generative AI models.
- The project will create 550 high-paying technical jobs directly, including roles for data center engineers, network specialists, and operations managers.
- Thousands of indirect jobs will be generated in construction and supply chains during the development phase.
- Community leaders in Georgia praised the investment as a transformative development.
- The Butts County Board of Commissioners and local entities collaborated for more than a year on this initiative.
- The investment is described as the most significant in Butts County history.
- AWS plans to power the new data centers with renewable energy, aligning with its global carbon-neutral objectives.
- Solar farms will support the data centers, including the Bulldog Solar farm.
- The solar farms will collectively generate enough clean energy to power approximately 123,000 U.S. homes annually.
