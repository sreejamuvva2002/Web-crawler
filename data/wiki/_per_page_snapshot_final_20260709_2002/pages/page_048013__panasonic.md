# Panasonic

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-03-29

**Source:** [Panasonic leads by example with its Green Impact vision | Trellis](https://trellis.net/article/panasonic-leads-example-its-green-impact-vision/)  ·  `page_048013`

## Facts

- Panasonic aims to reduce emissions across its value chain and customers by more than 300 megatons (Mt) by 2050.
- In January, Panasonic achieved net zero CO2 emissions at all 14 of its global automotive facilities.
- Panasonic plans to decarbonize all its global manufacturing plants by FY2030.
- In September 2021, Panasonic created a global taskforce to accelerate the transition to zero CO2 factories by standardizing common practices and measurement across the enterprise.
- The taskforce's areas of focus include energy-saving measures, expanding renewable energy use at each site and procuring renewable energy.
- Panasonic aims to impact avoided emissions through its existing businesses by over 38.3 Mt globally by FY2025.
- To achieve this goal, Panasonic is developing new clean energy technologies including pure hydrogen fuel cells, air-to-water heat pumps, vacuum-insulated glass, and EV batteries.
- Panasonic is working closely with the Japanese government to track energy use and encourage customers to reduce consumption and decarbonize their grids.
- Panasonic plans to have all factories recycling 99 percent or more of waste by FY2025.
- Panasonic plans to invest in 13 circular economy-focused businesses and other sustainable ventures.
- Last year, Panasonic joined the World Business Council for Sustainable Development along with 200 of the world's leading sustainable businesses to work towards a net zero future.
- Panasonic supports the International Electrotechnical Commission's work to standardize global definitions and calculation methods for avoided emissions.
- Panasonic signed on to the Low Carbon Patent Pledge to grant free licenses to patents related to low-carbon technologies.
- Panasonic signed on to Climate Action 100+, a commitment through the United Nations by high-emission companies to reduce their impact.
- In 2022, Panasonic was named to the CDP “A List” for its leadership in climate change-related transparency and performance.
