# DENSO Manufacturing Tennessee, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2015-08-25

**Source:** [Governor Haslam, Commissioner Boyd Announce Denso Manufacturing Tennessee, Inc. To Expand Maryville Operations - TNECD](https://tnecd.com/news/governor-haslam-commissioner-boyd-announce-denso-manufacturing-tennessee-inc-to-expand-maryville-operations/)  ·  `page_053812`

## Facts

- DENSO Manufacturing Tennessee will expand its automotive components and systems manufacturing plant in Maryville, making it one of the largest operating campuses in DENSO Corporation’s worldwide operations.
- The company currently has a workforce of more than 3,100 employees and is Blount County’s largest employer.
- DENSO will create 500 new jobs as part of this expansion.
- The company will invest $400 million to expand manufacturing capabilities and construct a new warehouse.
- Due to expanded production needs at two manufacturing plants, existing warehouses will be converted to manufacturing space.
- A new consolidated warehouse will be built on the company’s current campus.
- Tennessee Governor Bill Haslam congratulated DENSO on the expansion and the creation of 500 new jobs in Maryville and Blount County.
- Economic and Community Development Commissioner Randy Boyd noted this is the second DENSO expansion at a Tennessee facility in less than a year.
- DENSO Manufacturing Tennessee President Jack Helmboldt, who also serves as a DENSO Corporation global executive director, thanked skilled associates, state and local leaders, the Blount Chamber Partnership, and TVA for their support.
- DENSO Manufacturing Tennessee produces starters, alternators, instrument clusters, and a wide range of electronic components and systems for the automotive industry.
- The Maryville campus consists of four manufacturing plants on 194 acres and has undergone eight major expansions.
- U.S. Sen. Lamar Alexander stated that the auto industry has spread into 80 of Tennessee's 95 counties, becoming a main contributor to raising family incomes.
- Blount County Mayor Ed Mitchell highlighted collaborative efforts with the state, the cities of Alcoa and Maryville, and local partners in supporting the expansion.
- Maryville City Mayor Tom Taylor praised DENSO as a community partner for creating jobs and giving back to the region.
- To celebrate its 25 years in Maryville, DENSO Manufacturing Tennessee donated $50,000 to Blount Memorial Hospital and $50,000 to East Tennessee Children’s Hospital.
- Alcoa City Mayor Don Mull welcomed the opportunity to further the relationship with DENSO for the next 25 years.
- TVA Senior Vice President of Economic Development John Bradley congratulated DENSO on adding 500 new jobs and noted partnerships with TNECD and the Blount Partnership.
- Blount County Economic Development Board Chairman Fred Lawson stated the investment nearly doubles the capital investment Blount County has enjoyed since 2010.
- Maryville and Blount County are represented in the Tennessee General Assembly by Sen. Doug Overbey, Rep. Art Swann, and Rep. Bob Ramsey.
- DENSO Corp., headquartered in Kariya, Aichi prefecture, Japan, is a leading global automotive supplier of advanced technology, systems, and components in thermal, powertrain control, electronics, information, and safety.
- DENSO's customers include all the world's major carmakers.
- Worldwide, DENSO has more than 200 subsidiaries and affiliates in 38 countries and regions and employs more than 140,000 people.
- Consolidated global sales for DENSO for the fiscal year ending March 31, 2015, totaled US$35.9 billion.
- Last fiscal year, DENSO spent 9.2 percent of its global consolidated sales on research and development.
- DENSO common stock is traded on the Tokyo and Nagoya stock exchanges.
- In North America, DENSO employs more than 22,000 people at 30 consolidated companies and affiliates.
- Of DENSO's North American facilities, 25 are manufacturing plants located in the United States, Canada, and Mexico.
- In the United States alone, DENSO employs more than 14,000 people across California, Michigan, North Carolina, South Carolina, Tennessee, Kentucky, Georgia, Iowa, Ohio, Pennsylvania, Texas, and Arkansas.
- DENSO’s North American consolidated sales totaled US$8.0 billion for the fiscal year ending March 31, 2015.
- Tennessee was named 2014 and 2013 “State of the Year” for economic development by Business Facilities magazine.
- The Tennessee Department of Economic and Community Development seeks to attract new corporate investment and facilitate expansion to make Tennessee the No. 1 location in the Southeast for high quality jobs.
