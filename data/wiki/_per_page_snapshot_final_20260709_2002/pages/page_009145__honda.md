# Honda

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-03-05

**Source:** [Trump mentions new Indiana Honda plant, but company denies announcement](https://www.wrtv.com/news/local-news/trump-mentions-new-indiana-honda-plant-but-company-denies-announcement)  ·  `page_009145`

## Facts

- President Donald Trump expressed optimism regarding growth in the U.S. auto industry during his address to Congress on Tuesday night.
- Trump cited Honda's decision to build a new plant in Indiana as a key example of auto industry growth.
- Trump stated that plants are opening up all over the place and deals are being made never seen, attributing this to an election win and tariffs.
- Trump claimed numerous car companies have announced they will be building massive automobile plants in America.
- Trump specifically mentioned Honda just announcing a new plant in Indiana, describing it as one of the largest anywhere in the world.
- Trump stated he spoke to the top people at the three major auto companies that day and found them excited.
- Trump's comments reference a recent Reuters report stating Honda will produce its next Civic model in Indiana rather than Mexico due to U.S. tariffs.
- WRTV reached out to Honda for clarification regarding the new plant announcement.
- Honda denied that any new plans had been announced.
- Honda currently operates a production plant in Greensburg, Indiana.
- A Honda spokesperson stated the company has made no such announcement and will not comment on the report.
- The Honda Civic has been made at Honda's Indiana Auto Plant since the facility opened in 2008.
- Honda follows a longstanding approach to build products close to the customer, with flexibility to produce products in each region based on customer needs and market conditions.
- A Honda spokesperson acknowledged President Trump's recognition of Honda's commitment to American manufacturing.
- The spokesperson emphasized that the company did not announce any plans for a new plant in Indiana or elsewhere in the U.S. at this time.
- Honda thanked President Trump for recognizing its commitment to manufacturing vehicles in America and expressed a desire to work with the administration and Congress on pro-growth policies supporting a robust U.S. auto industry.
- While not announcing new plant plans, Honda has invested over $3 billion in advanced vehicle manufacturing in America in just the past three years.
- Honda's cumulative investment in American advanced vehicle manufacturing totals more than $24.7 billion.
