## Facts

- Aspen Aerogels, Inc. announced a conditional commitment from the U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) to fund up to $670.6 million for its second aerogel manufacturing facility in Register, Georgia.
- The DOE funding is provided under the Advanced Technology Vehicles Manufacturing program.
- The loan aims to complete the remaining capital expenditure for the plant.
- The Register Plant is expected to generate between $1.2 billion and $1.6 billion in incremental revenue from its PyroThin® thermal barrier products.
- The facility is designed to produce PyroThin® aerogel blankets that provide thermal management and fire protection for batteries.
- The product aims to enhance the safety and performance of electric vehicle (EV) battery systems.
- The project is anticipated to create up to 550 construction jobs.
- The project is anticipated to create 255 permanent full-time positions.
- The initiative aligns with the DOE's mission to foster U.S. manufacturing growth for zero-emissions vehicles.
- Aspen Aerogels reported Q3 2024 preliminary revenues of approximately $117 million.
- The company reported an Adjusted EBITDA of $25 million for Q3 2024.
- The quarter included a net loss of around $13 million, which contained a one-time charge of $27.5 million from the extinguishment of a convertible note on August 19, 2024.
- Aspen Aerogels held approximately $113 million in cash and cash equivalents as of September 30, 2024.
- President and CEO Donald R. Young expressed confidence in the PyroThin® product's capabilities to address critical safety challenges in the EV market.
- The company recently secured contracts with six major OEMs in the U.S. and Europe.
