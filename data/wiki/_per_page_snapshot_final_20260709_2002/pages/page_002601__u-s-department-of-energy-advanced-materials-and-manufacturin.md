# U.S. Department of Energy Advanced Materials and Manufacturing Technologies Office (AMMTO) Funding Opportunity DE-FOA-0003329

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-11-18

**Source:** [$33 Million in Funding Available To Advance Smart Manufacturing Technologies To Help Accelerate a Clean Energy Economy | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/cmei/ammto/articles/33-million-funding-available-advance-smart-manufacturing-technologies-help)  ·  `page_002601`

## Facts

- $33 million funding opportunity aims to accelerate the advancement of smart manufacturing technologies and processes necessary to develop and deploy innovative technologies and materials needed for the nation’s clean energy transition.
- FOA Number is DE-FOA-0003329.
- Deadline for full applications is Nov. 18, 2024, at 5 p.m. ET.
- Topics include smart manufacturing for a circular economy, sustainable transportation, high-performance materials, and domestic mining.
- Smart manufacturing is defined as the use of advanced technologies and processes, including digitalization and artificial intelligence, to improve technical performance, productivity, quality assurance, and security of the manufacturing sector.
- AMMTO Director Christopher Saldaña stated smart manufacturing solutions are critical to domestically manufacture next generation clean energy technologies more economically, efficiently, and at higher quality and scale needed for global competitiveness.
- Funding recipients will support research to develop smart manufacturing technologies that improve efficiency and economics of circular supply chains.
- Recipients will advance sustainable transportation manufacturing.
- Recipients will accelerate development of high-performance materials for the clean energy transition.
- Recipients will create more sustainable, safe, and competitive mining in the United States.
- Topic 1 focuses on smart manufacturing solutions increasing viability of circular supply chains (Re-X pathways) such as recycling, repairing, remanufacturing, and reuse by improving efficiency and economics.
- Area of Interest 1 under Topic 1 covers smart manufacturing technologies for improved sorting and characterization to identify, sort, and characterize materials to facilitate recovery and recycling into new products.
- Area of Interest 2 under Topic 1 covers interoperable and open supply chains for expanded Re-X to help repair or remanufacture products not initially designed for reuse, repair, refurbish, remanufacture, or repurpose.
- Area of Interest 3 under Topic 1 covers improved data transparency for accurate lifecycle assessments to enhance availability and transparency of data critical to bolstering market acceptance of secondary materials.
- Topic 2 focuses on smart manufacturing technologies making tooling and equipment for sustainable vehicles faster and more economical while reducing component defects and enabling new functionalities.
- Sustainable transportation in this FOA refers to electric vehicles based on either batteries or fuel cells across all vehicle types from automobiles to fleets to all truck classes.
- Area of Interest 1 under Topic 2 covers automation by integrating hardware and software systems into manufacturing tooling and equipment to improve component productivity.
- Area of Interest 2 under Topic 2 covers manufacturing asset management for improving system performance to improve cost, quality, and throughput of tooling and equipment, industrial controls, and automation network.
- Topic 3 focuses on developing, validating, and prototyping new high-performance materials and their manufacturing processes.
- Particular interest is in manufacturing processes and fabrication techniques for high-performance materials for the clean energy transition, such as high-conductivity materials and materials for harsh service conditions.
- Area of Interest 1 under Topic 3 covers high-performance materials and process discovery by developing high throughput strategies for conductivity-enhanced and harsh environment materials synthesis/testing used with computational tools.
- Area of Interest 2 under Topic 3 covers high-performance materials manufacturing, assembly and scaleup by creating competitive manufacturing and assembly approaches utilizing smart manufacturing tools in materials manufacturing.
- Topic 4 focuses on developing novel smart manufacturing technologies for sustainable, safe, and competitive mining in the United States.
- Fostering advancements in mining will enable a more resilient, diverse, and secure domestic supply of critical minerals and materials.
- Area of Interest 1 under Topic 4 covers sustainable and competitive domestic mining by establishing smart manufacturing technologies such as sensors, controls, and automation for health, safety, environmental, and economic benefits to mining operations.
- Area of Interest 2 under Topic 4 covers sensing, analytics, and data-driven decision making in mining by advancing new and transformative mining technologies offering significant benefits over current commercially available analytical instruments and sensors that are more accessible and affordable.
- Applicants must submit a concept paper by 5 p.m. ET on Aug. 22, 2024, to be eligible to submit a full application.
- Funding Opportunity Announcement issue date was July 18, 2024.
- Expected submission deadline for replies to reviewer comments is Jan. 14, 2025, at 5 p.m. ET.
- Expected date for selection notifications is April 2025.
- Expected timeframe for award negotiations is April–June 2025.
