# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2022-07

**Source:** [Georgia Emerges as Hub for Sustainability - Savannah CEO](https://savannahceo.com/features/2022/07/georgia-emerges-hub-sustainability/)  ·  `page_050124`

## Facts

- Companies looking to lower emissions and reduce carbon footprint for themselves and their customers have invested over $11.5 billion in Georgia since 2018.
- The state’s competitive advantages, combined with its responsiveness to rapidly evolving business needs, have attracted a range of manufacturers, recyclers, and innovators who are leading the way to a greener, cleaner, more sustainable future.
- Ascend Elements, formerly known as Battery Resourcers, invested $43 million to open a commercial-scale lithium-ion battery recycling plant in Georgia.
- The new facility will extract critically scarce materials like lithium, cobalt, and nickel from batteries.
- The facility will become the largest of its kind in North America when fully operational later this year (2022).
- Roger Lin, Ascend Elements’ Vice President of Marketing and Government Relations, selected Georgia primarily because of the growing electric vehicle and battery ecosystem in the state.
- Proximity to partners and customers is essential in the sustainable battery materials industry, according to Lin.
- The Southeast is a mecca for the electric vehicle and battery industry, and Georgia provides proximity to factories like SK Battery America.
- Locating in Georgia minimizes transportation costs, offers proximity to top talent via nearby universities, and provides access to renewable energy.
- Korea’s SK Battery America is investing nearly $2.6 billion in two facilities near Atlanta.
- SK Battery America will supply batteries to Ford and Volkswagen as well as other electric vehicle makers.
- SK Battery America made its first announcement regarding the Georgia investment in 2018.
- Plug Power recently announced a new $84 million, zero-emission hydrogen fuel production facility near I-95 and Georgia’s deepwater ports.
- Plug Power provides fuel cell systems for e-mobility that deliver efficiency, fast fueling, and low cost to customers.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp launched the Electric Mobility and Innovation Alliance.
- The Electric Mobility and Innovation Alliance is a statewide initiative between government, industries, electric utilities, nonprofits, and other stakeholders focused on growing the electric mobility ecosystem in the state.
- The initiative aims to strengthen Georgia’s position in electrification-related manufacturing and innovation.
- In 2021, EV manufacturer Rivian announced an investment of $5 billion to build a carbon-conscious facility.
- Rivian's facility will make vertically connected electric platforms for trucks, sport/utility vehicles, and delivery vans.
- Hyundai Motor Group will open its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility in the State of Georgia.
- Hyundai announced a $5.54 billion investment with operations slated to begin in the first half of 2025.
- Many other Georgia companies are already making parts for EVs.
- Companies from Korea, Germany, Turkey, and the Netherlands have announced plans to build EV-related facilities in the state.
- Site Selection Magazine ranked Georgia No. 3 for sustainability in the South Atlantic region.
- The Atlanta metro area ranked in the Top 10 nationally for sustainability.
- Since 2009, Georgia has increased net electricity generation from renewable sources by more than 93%.
- Georgia’s electricity mix currently consists of nearly 12% renewables.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that renewable energy capacity installation in Georgia has been driven organically by companies and consumers, accelerated through economic attractiveness and removing barriers rather than mandate.
- Smart policies have allowed Georgia to promote renewable power development and offer a comprehensive portfolio of clean energy options, giving the state a competitive edge in attracting leading businesses.
- Solar PV is Georgia’s fastest-growing source of renewable energy.
- NanoPV will manufacture solar panels and equipment in south Georgia’s Sumter County.
- Qcells is investing more than $320 million in its solar panel assembly plant in northern Georgia.
- Qcells' plant is the largest solar panel assembly plant in the Western hemisphere.
- Recent sustainability-focused announcements in Georgia include German copper recycler Aurubis.
- Electronic waste recycler Igneo has announced plans for a facility in Georgia.
- Danimer Scientific, a manufacturer and developer of biodegradable materials, announced a presence in Georgia.
- Recycled furniture maker SoPoly announced plans for a facility in Georgia.
- In Atlanta, EF Block turns everyday recycled Styrofoam into earth-friendly, stronger, sustainable walls that replace traditional building materials.
- EF Block launched a city-wide Styrofoam recycling initiative with support from GDEcD’s Center of Innovation and the City of Atlanta.
- NANTRenewables is opening a plant on Georgia’s coast to manufacture biodegradable products like plates, straws, cutlery, trays, and cups.
- NANTRenewables provides sustainable products to companies within the food, agriculture, and pharmaceutical industries.
- Harold Meherg, a NANT Renewables senior executive, selected SeaPoint Industrial Terminal Complex in Savannah for shared sustainability values and development-ready infrastructure including roads, rail access, sea access, and nearby utility connections.
- Companies leading mobility research & development, battery manufacturing and recycling, and electric vehicle production all call Georgia home.
