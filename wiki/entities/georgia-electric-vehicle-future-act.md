# Georgia Electric Vehicle Future Act

**Type:** Government Program  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[government-support|Government Support]]  ·  **Location:** Georgia, United States

## Overview

Legislation (Senate Bill 26) that authorizes the Georgia Department of Economic Development to create a statewide electric vehicle manufacturing program and establishes an oversight commission.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Creates a state-wide framework to attract EV manufacturers, develop charging infrastructure, and coordinate workforce training for the electric vehicle supply chain in Georgia.

## Key facts

- The Georgia House passed a measure to advance the state's electric vehicle industry.
- $1.5 billion in taxpayer-funded incentives allocated for a Rivian Automotive assembly plant in Morgan and Newton counties.
- Senate Bill 26, the Georgia Electric Vehicle Future Act, permits the Georgia Department of Economic Development to launch a statewide electric vehicle manufacturing program.
- The legislation would establish the Georgia Electric Vehicle Manufacturing Commission as an oversight body for state-led EV initiatives.
- Georgia Department of Transportation plans to spend roughly $130 million on electric vehicle charging infrastructure over the next five years.
- Federal Highway Administration signed off on Georgia’s National Electric Vehicle Infrastructure Plan, opening the door to millions in federal taxpayer dollars.
- Senate Bill 26 permits the Georgia Department of Economic Development to launch a statewide electric vehicle manufacturing program.
- The act would establish the Georgia Electric Vehicle Manufacturing Commission, comprised of private sector and public officials, serving as an oversight body for state-led EV initiatives.
- The commission will develop policy recommendations for the General Assembly and coordinate with higher education systems to determine workforce needs and train future workers.
- State Rep. Chuck Martin said the agency would work with state agencies, including the Georgia Department of Transportation, to deploy appropriate infrastructure, including EV charging stations.
- The legislation promotes public-private partnerships to develop forward-looking EV solutions, support this job-producing industry, and strengthen Georgia’s position in EV manufacturing and innovation.
- Last year, the Federal Highway Administration signed off on Georgia’s National Electric Vehicle Infrastructure Plan, opening the door to millions in federal taxpayer dollars for the state.
- With FHWA approval, the Georgia Department of Transportation began planning how the state will spend roughly $130 million over the next five years on electric vehicle charging infrastructure.
