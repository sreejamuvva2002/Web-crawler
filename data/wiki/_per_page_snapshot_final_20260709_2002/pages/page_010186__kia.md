# Kia

## Facts

- Two of Kia's most popular EV models now qualify for federal tax credits that can save American consumers thousands of dollars.
- A new U.S. presidential administration is creating an uncertain future for incentives under the Inflation Reduction Act.
- Kia has begun manufacturing its EV6 and EV9 models at a facility in Georgia.
- Kia is using qualifying components for its EV6 and EV9 models.
- Both the EV6 and EV9 are eligible for the IRA's full $7,500 tax credit for new EVs in 2025.
- The U.S. government determines IRA rebate eligibility based on manufacturers' suggested retail prices, battery components and mineral sourcing, assembly location, and individual modified adjusted gross income.
- Kia sold more than 70,000 vehicles in November for the first time ever.
- Kia's EV division grew by 45% year-over-year.
- Demand for the all-electric EV6 soared by as much as 46%, setting another sales record.
- This marks the first time Kia's EV6 and EV9 have been added to the IRA eligibility list.
- According to the Environmental Protection Agency, a typical passenger car releases more than 10,000 pounds of carbon pollution each year.
- The bulk of Kia's sales still come from its gas-powered and hybrid vehicle lineup.
- Kia aims to have 15 EV models in circulation by 2027.
- Kia aims to sell 1.6 million EVs internationally by 2030.
- Kia intends to expand its charging infrastructure.
- Kia has a seven-year partnership with The Ocean Cleanup to tackle plastic pollution.
- Kia aims to operate its business sites entirely on renewable energy by 2040.
- Last year, Kia introduced 6 megawatts of solar generation to plants across South Korea and India.
