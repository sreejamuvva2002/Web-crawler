# Aspen Aerogels Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2023-05-01

**Source:** [City ‘claws back’ $741,000 spent to extend water and sewer to unfinished factory - Statesboro Herald](https://www.statesboroherald.com/local/city-claws-back-741000-spent-to-extend-water-and-sewer-to-unfinished-factory/)  ·  `page_052712`

## Facts

- Statesboro City Council terminated the city’s role as guarantor of a $750,000 May 2023 state grant used to extend water and sewer lines to Aspen Aerogels’ unfinished Bulloch County plant.
- Aspen Aerogels was expected to repay the city government $741,000 it spent from the grant.
- The city was set to forward the $741,000 back to the Georgia Department of Community Affairs (DCA) per a “termination of guaranty” agreement.
- City Manager Charles Penny stated Aspen Aerogels would also repay about $11,000 used to prepare the grant application.
- The DCA awarded the $750,000 to the city and Bulloch County on May 1, 2023 as an Employment Incentive Program (EIP) grant, a type of Community Development Block Grant from federal money allocated to the state.
- The EIP grant came with job creation expectations and was specifically for water and sewer improvements for Aspen Aerogels Georgia LLC.
- The project was handled through the city government as an extension from its water and sewer mains that had been built out years earlier to Bruce Yawn Commerce Park.
- Bruce Yawn Commerce Park is owned by the Development Authority of Bulloch County, where the city also had already placed a water tower.
- The overall cost to install infrastructure specifically for the Aspen plant was nearly $1.7 million, with the company and the city each paying a portion.
- Aspen Aerogels Inc. makes and markets aerogel insulating materials, including thermal barriers for electric vehicle batteries and materials used to protect petroleum pipelines.
- Aspen Aerogels first announced the Statesboro project in February 2022 to build a $325 million plant and create 250 manufacturing jobs at Bruce Yawn Commerce Park off U.S. Highway 301 at the I-16 interchange.
- Following a 2023 “reset in EV demand expectations,” Aspen Aerogels slowed development of the Statesboro plant and ramped up external manufacturing capacity by outsourcing.
- In a February 12, 2025 investor and public relations statement, Aspen Aerogels Inc. acknowledged that it had halted construction of the plant altogether.
- The council’s vote to terminate the guaranty was 5-0, on a motion from District 5 Councilmember Shari Barr, seconded by District 1 Councilmember Tangie Reese Johnson.
- Mayor Jonathan McCollar was authorized to sign the termination agreement.
- Councilmember Shari Barr noted that while the grant money would be returned, the infrastructure would remain in place as an advantage for future tenants.
- City Manager Charles Penny stated during a 9 a.m. April 1 meeting that the funds would be electronically transferred back to the DCA immediately to avoid jeopardizing the city’s eligibility for certain grants.
