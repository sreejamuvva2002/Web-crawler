# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-05-26

**Source:** [(LEAD) Hyundai Motor, LG to invest 5.7 tln won in EV battery plant in U.S.](https://korean-vibe.com/news/newsview.php?ncode=179562803366425)  ·  `page_009286`

## Facts

- South Korea's Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution Ltd. will invest 5.7 trillion won (US$4.3 billion) in a battery cell plant in the United States.
- The investment is a move to better deal with the Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) amid growing demand for electric vehicles.
- The two companies will set up a 50:50 joint venture within this year (2023) to build the plant.
- The plant will have an annual capacity of 30 gigawatt-hours, enough to power 300,000 EVs.
- The plant will be located near Hyundai Motor Group's dedicated electric vehicle and battery plant under construction in Georgia.
- Construction of the plant is planned to begin later this year (2023).
- The companies aim to start production at the plant in late 2025.
- Hyundai Motor President and Chief Executive Officer Chang Jae-hoon stated that Hyundai Motor Group is shifting its focus to electrification and aims to become a leader in the global EV market based on this battery cell plant project.
- LG Energy Solution CEO Kwon Young-soo stated that the two industry leaders have joined hands to take the lead in the North American EV market.
- The joint company will supply battery cells for EVs manufactured at Hyundai Motor Co.'s Alabama plant.
- The joint company will also supply battery cells for EVs manufactured at Kia Corp.'s Georgia plant.
- The joint company will supply battery cells for the upcoming dedicated EV and battery plant named the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA).
- The move responds to IRA tax credits of up to $7,500 to buyers of EVs assembled only in North America.
- There are concerns that Hyundai Motor and Kia could lose ground in the U.S. market because they currently make most of their EVs at domestic plants for export to the U.S.
- In October, Hyundai Motor Group began construction of a separate 300,000-unit-a-year EV and battery plant in Georgia.
- The separate Georgia plant aims to begin commercial production in the first half of 2025.
- Early this year (2023), Hyundai Motor began producing all-electric GV70 SUVs under its independent Genesis brand at the Alabama plant.
- Last month (April 2023), Hyundai Motor Group and SK Group's battery unit SK On announced plans to build a 6.5 trillion-won EV battery plant in Bartow County, Georgia.
- The Hyundai-SK On Georgia plant aims to start production in the second half of 2025.
