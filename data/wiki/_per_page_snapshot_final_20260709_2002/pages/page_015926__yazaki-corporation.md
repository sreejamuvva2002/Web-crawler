# Yazaki Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-03-31

**Source:** [News - Yazaki - MarkLines Automotive Industry Portal](https://www.marklines.com/en/news/tag/600/yazaki)  ·  `page_015926`

## Facts

- Yazaki plans to invest an additional USD 66 million in Nuevo León, Mexico.
- The new capital investment will strengthen the company’s operations in engineering and manufacturing capabilities linked to electrification technologies.
- On March 31, 2026, the Secretary General of the Investment Committee of Cambodia met with a delegation from YAZAKI (Cambodia) Products Co., Ltd. to discuss operations in Koh Kong province and production of vehicle wires and components.
- Yazaki plans to expand its factory in Cambodia for vehicle wires and components by 2027.
- On March 23, Yazaki North America, Inc. announced an agreement to proceed with the construction of a second manufacturing plant in Guatemala.
- The new Guatemalan facility will be located in Pajapita, around seven kilometers from its existing plant in Ayutla.
- The Guatemala site will produce wiring harnesses for vehicles.
- On November 19, the United States activated the Rapid Response Labor Mechanism of the USMCA against automotive supplier Grupo Yazaki in León, Guanajuato.
- The U.S. RRM activation was over alleged violations of workers’ rights to freedom of association and collective bargaining.
- On October 10, Japanese automotive supplier Yazaki temporarily suspended operations at its León plant after detecting a geological fault beneath the facility.
- The suspension aims to protect the safety of over two thousand employees working at the León site.
- On April 3, 2025, auto component manufacturer Yazaki reported plans to set up a new manufacturing plant in Chennai, India, in collaboration with Horizon Industrial Parks.
- The new Indian facility will be located in Horizon’s Chengalpet Industrial Park and span across 316 thousand square feet.
- On January 30, Japanese automotive supplier Yazaki announced the closure of its two manufacturing plants in Colonia and Las Piedras, Uruguay.
- The plant closures mark the end of nearly two decades of operations in Uruguay.
- Production from the closed Uruguayan plants will be moved to Paraguay and Argentina.
- Yazaki produces electrical components for major automakers including Toyota, Renault, Honda, and Volkswagen.
- Yazaki Europe Middle East Africa N.V. (Yazaki EMEA), a subsidiary of Yazaki Corporation, completed the acquisition of the Lüdenscheid-based Junker & Halverscheid Group (JuHa) on July 2, 2024.
- The integration of JuHa involves around 200 employees being integrated into the Yazaki EMEA operations.
- Yazaki EMEA inked an agreement to acquire the Junker & Halverscheid Group, which includes Junker & Halverscheid Formenbau GmbH & Co. KG and JuHa Kunststoffverarbeitung GmbH & Co. KG.
- The acquisition of the Junker & Halverscheid Group is subject to approval by competition authorities.
