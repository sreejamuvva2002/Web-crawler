# Stryten Energy LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-07-31

**Source:** [Stryten Energy To Expand Domestic Capacity for Manufacturing Battery Components | Design 2 Part Magazine](https://www.d2pmagazine.com/2025/07/31/stryten-energy-to-expand-domestic-capacity-for-manufacturing-battery-components/)  ·  `page_050466`

## Facts

- Stryten Energy LLC is a U.S.-based provider of energy storage products.
- The company plans to expand its domestic manufacturing capacity to 24 gigawatts.
- The expansion will increase the domestic supply of batteries for military, long-duration energy storage, transportation, and material-handling applications.
- The plan focuses on increasing production across Stryten's 11 U.S. manufacturing and battery component plants.
- More than 2,500 people currently produce batteries at these facilities.
- Batteries are produced for applications including military and government, data centers, grid storage, automotive, and material handling.
- Mike Judd serves as president and chief executive officer of Stryten Energy.
- The new investment is supported by existing advanced manufacturing production tax credits.
- These tax credits contribute to the company's ability to accelerate capacity growth while supporting the domestic industrial base.
- Stryten stated that energy security means continuous access to reliable sources of power.
- Meeting increasing power demands will require scaling up production of all reliable battery chemistries.
- The company utilizes advanced lead, lithium, and vanadium redox flow battery technology.
- Stryten operates plants in Kansas, Georgia, Iowa, Virginia, and Arkansas.
- The gigawatt capacity expansion plans are expected to bring hundreds of high-paying manufacturing jobs.
- The expansion will deliver a positive economic impact to surrounding communities.
- Stryten operates a domestic, vertically integrated supply chain that provides battery components to its factories and the broader U.S. battery manufacturing industry.
- Additional plants in Pennsylvania, New York, Indiana, Missouri, and Wisconsin ensure a reliable U.S. source for critical materials needed to manufacture batteries.
- The investment plan includes increasing domestic capacity for processing recycled plastic from spent batteries.
- This processed recycled plastic is used to produce new battery components for automobiles, forklifts, and locomotives.
- The recycled material contributes to the sustainable circular economy of the U.S. battery industry.
- Stryten recently opened a lithium battery assembly plant in Georgia.
- The Georgia plant supports military and industrial battery needs using proprietary lithium module technology.
- The company is partnering with Dragonfly Energy, headquartered in Reno, Nevada, to bring advanced lithium batteries to automotive aftermarket and battery distributor partners.
- Stryten is headquartered in Alpharetta, Georgia.
- Stryten powers applications ranging from submarines and subcompacts to microgrids, warehouses, distribution centers, cars, trains, and trucks.
- The company's energy storage technologies include advanced lead, lithium and vanadium redox flow batteries, intelligent chargers, and energy performance management software.
