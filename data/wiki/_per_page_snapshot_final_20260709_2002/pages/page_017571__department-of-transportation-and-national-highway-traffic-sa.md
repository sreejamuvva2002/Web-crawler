# Department of Transportation and National Highway Traffic Safety Administration Automated Vehicle Framework

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2025-04-24

**Source:** [DOT and NHTSA Announce Autonomous Vehicle Framework | Insights | Mayer Brown](https://www.mayerbrown.com/en/insights/publications/2025/04/dot-and-nhtsa-announce-autonomous-vehicle-framework)  ·  `page_017571`

## Facts

- On April 24, 2025, US Transportation Secretary Sean P. Duffy announced a new NHTSA Automated Vehicle (AV) Framework as part of the DOT’s agenda to promote domestic innovation.
- The AV Framework includes three key principles: prioritize safety of ongoing AV operations on public roads, unleash innovation by removing unnecessary regulatory barriers, and enable commercial deployment of AVs to enhance safety and mobility for the American public.
- NHTSA is poised to explore further avenues to accelerate the domestic development of AVs.
- Two initial actions under the Framework include the issuance of the Third Amended Standing General Order 2021-01 and the expansion of the Automated Vehicle Exemption Program to include domestically produced vehicles.
- NHTSA issued a Third Amended Standing General Order 2021-01 (SGO) governing incident reporting for vehicles with Automated Driving Systems (ADS) and Level 2 or higher Advanced Driver Assistance Systems (ADAS).
- The Third Amended SGO takes effect June 16, 2025.
- The amended SGO eliminates the one-day timeline for incident reporting and now requires submission of an incident report no later than five calendar days after receiving notice of the incident.
- The amended SGO eliminates the separate ten-day reporting requirement previously required under the SGO.
- Under the amended SGO, incidents involving property damage only must be reported if the resulting property damage is reasonably expected to exceed $1,000.
- Reporting is also required for property damage not expected to exceed $1,000 if the subject vehicle was the only vehicle in the crash or struck another vehicle or object.
- If a property damage incident falls in either of these two scenarios, it must be reported by the 15th day of the calendar month following the month in which notice of the incident was received.
- Monthly reports are now required only after the receipt of materially new or materially different information for particular fields in the incident report.
- Fields requiring updated monthly reporting for new or materially different information include VIN, engagement status, source, highest severity alleged, subject vehicle damage, subject vehicle pre-crash movement, airbag deployment status for any vehicle involved, data availability, and narrative.
- Reporting entities will no longer need to report when there is no new information to provide.
- NHTSA Chief Counsel Peter Simshauser announced in an open letter to AV developers that the Agency is expanding the Automated Vehicle Exemption Program (AVEP) to include domestically produced vehicles.
- Under 49 C.F.R. Part 591, NHTSA grants exemptions to allow research and demonstrations on imported non-FMVSS-compliant vehicles.
- As of the announcement, NHTSA will now accept requests for vehicles built in the United States to receive these same exemptions for research and demonstration purposes.
- NHTSA indicated that it seeks to streamline the process for receiving the broader 49 C.F.R. Part 555 exemptions, which can include commercialization purposes.
- The announcements indicate a strong focus on accelerating the advancement and innovation of domestically produced AVs through the AV Framework.
- Future initiatives are expected to include modifications to the Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standards (FMVSS) to encourage safe commercial deployment of AVs and promote innovation under this framework.
