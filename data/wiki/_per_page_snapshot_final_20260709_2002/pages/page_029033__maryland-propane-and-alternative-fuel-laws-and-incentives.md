# Maryland Propane and Alternative Fuel Laws and Incentives

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Alternative Fuels Data Center: Propane Laws and Incentives in Maryland](https://afdc.energy.gov/fuels/laws/LPG?state=md)  ·  `page_029033`

## Facts

- At least 50% of state vehicles using petroleum diesel fuel must use a minimum blend of 5% biodiesel (B5) or other biofuel approved by the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency as a fuel or fuel additive.
- This requirement does not apply to any state vehicles for which the use of biodiesel or other biofuel will void the manufacturer’s warranty for that vehicle.
- Biodiesel fuel is defined as a fuel composed of mono-alkyl esters of long chain fatty acids derived from vegetable oils or animal fats that is designated B100 or a blend of biodiesel that meets the requirements of ASTM Standard D6751.
- Bi-fuel and flexible fuel vehicles capable of operating on either alternative fuel or conventional fuel must use alternative fuel when it is available.
- An AFV powered by propane or natural gas may only use the Baltimore Harbor Tunnel and the Fort McHenry Tunnel if the vehicle has a dedicated alternate fuel system installed by the manufacturer or a properly converted fuel system, conforms to applicable federal regulations and industry standards, has a fuel capacity that does not exceed 150 pounds, and displays all proper markings and symbols.
- A vehicle manufacturer or distributor may only sell vehicles directly to consumers in Maryland if it only sells AFVs, does not have a franchised dealership in Maryland, and meets specified cross-ownership limits.
- AFVs eligible for direct sales authorization include all-electric and non-fossil-fuel-powered vehicles.
- The Maryland Smart Energy Communities (MSEC) program, administered by the Maryland Energy Administration (MEA), offers local governments grants for transportation-related projects, including the purchase of new electric vehicles (EVs) or alternative fuel vehicles and the installation of EV chargers.
- MSEC grants provide up to $3,750 per vehicle for the purchase of a new EV with an all-electric range of up to 199 miles.
- MSEC grants provide up to $7,500 per vehicle for the purchase of a new EV with an all-electric range of over 200 miles.
- MSEC grants provide up to $6,000 per EV charger for EV charging equipment and installation.
- Communities may apply for funding for multiple projects under MSEC for a maximum grant award of $55,000 per project.
- Communities may alternatively apply for up to $150,000 for a single project under the MSEC program.
- The Maryland Department of the Environment (MDE) administers a Zero Emission School Bus Transition Grant Program to purchase zero emission school buses, install charging infrastructure, and transition to zero emission school bus fleets.
- MDE and the Maryland Department of Transportation provide technical assistance to county boards of education transitioning school buses to zero emission vehicles throughout the state.
