# Kia Motors America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-07-12

**Source:** [Kia to invest $200 million and bring jobs at West Point plant | Columbus Ledger-Enquirer](https://www.ledger-enquirer.com/news/business/article277245118.html)  ·  `page_010060`

## Facts

- Kia will invest more than $200 million at its West Point plant.
- The investment will create nearly 200 jobs at the West Point plant.
- The project is to produce the Korean automaker’s new electric vehicle sports-utility model, the EV9.
- Gov. Brian Kemp announced the project on Wednesday.
- The EV9 will become the fifth model assembled at the Georgia plant since it opened in 2009.
- Other models assembled at the plant include the Telluride, Sorento and Sportage SUVs, and the K5 mid-size sedan.
- More than 40% of Kia vehicles sold in the U.S. are manufactured in West Point.
- Gov. Brian Kemp stated that Georgia’s longstanding partnership with Kia has led to generational job creation and growth for the West Point area.
- The project aims to bring more opportunity to Georgians and help propel the state toward its goal of becoming the e-mobility capital.
- Sean Yoon, president & CEO of Kia Motors North America and Kia Motors America, stated that the EV9 has the potential to be another change catalyst for Kia.
- Sean Yoon described the EV9 as the most innovative vehicle Kia has ever built and a standout in the EV market.
- To date, Kia has invested more than $1.9 billion in Georgia.
- Kia Georgia is responsible for more than 14,000 plant and supplier jobs in the region.
- Hyundai is building a $5.5 billion EV plant west of Savannah that will generate 8,100 jobs.
- Rivian has under construction a $5 billion EV plant in the Covington area expected to create 7,500 jobs.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development’s Global Commerce team worked on the Kia EV project with the West Point Development Authority, the city of West Point, Troup County, the Georgia Center of Innovation’s manufacturing and energy teams, and the Technical College System of Georgia’s Quick Start program.
- The EV9 plant is expected to begin operations in the second quarter of next year.
