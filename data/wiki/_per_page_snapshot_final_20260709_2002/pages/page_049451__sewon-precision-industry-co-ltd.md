## Facts

- Sewon Company operates primarily on a Business-to-Business (B2B) model targeting major automotive Original Equipment Manufacturers (OEMs).
- The company supplies high-quality components for vehicle assembly, including car body parts, chassis parts, interior parts, and molds.
- Sewon is making strategic investments in an EV body parts plant in Georgia to align with the growing demand for EV components.
- Sewon Precision Industry Co., Ltd. reported trailing 12-month revenue of $116 million as of March 31, 2025.
- Sewon Corporation Company reported trailing 12-month revenue of $168 million as of March 31, 2025.
- A new $300 million manufacturing plant is being built in Rincon, Georgia, dedicated to producing EV body parts.
- The new Rincon facility is expected to begin operations in 2025.
- The Rincon facility will supply major OEMs, including Hyundai Motor Group's Metaplant America.
- The Georgia expansion is projected to create 740 new jobs.
- The project is expected to support a total of 1,600 jobs in Georgia after completion.
- Sewon America, a subsidiary of Sewon Precision Industry Co. Ltd., operates an existing manufacturing facility in LaGrange, Georgia that has expanded over time.
- Sewon's strategic shift toward electric vehicle manufacturers is reflected in its investment in the Georgia EV body parts plant.
- Automotive OEMs prioritize suppliers who consistently deliver high-quality components to ensure vehicle safety and performance.
- Purchasing decisions by automotive manufacturers are often based on long-term contracts and supply chain stability.
- Competitive pricing and the ability to integrate seamlessly into production processes are key factors in supplier selection.
- The shift toward EVs demands suppliers capable of producing specialized components, including lightweight body parts and other EV-specific components.
- Sewon addresses customer needs through continuous R&D and automated manufacturing sites to secure manufacturing competitiveness.
- Sewon Co., Ltd. is headquartered in Daegu, South Korea, with international expansion into China and the United States.
- Direct sales teams actively engage with potential automotive OEM clients to showcase capabilities and generate leads.
- The company participates in industry-specific trade shows and conferences to network and expand its customer base.
- Sewon prioritizes consistent quality and on-time delivery, providing dedicated customer service and technical support to address client needs.
- The company offers customized solutions and product adaptations to meet specific OEM requirements.
- Advanced manufacturing capabilities include automated manufacturing sites and real-time monitoring for quality control.
- Investment in the Georgia EV plant serves as a retention strategy to meet future demands of key customers like Hyundai Motor Group.
