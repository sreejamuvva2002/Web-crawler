# Seoyon E-Hwa

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-02-03

**Source:** [Seoyon E-HWA invests $76 million in Chatham County facility  - Grice Connect](https://www.griceconnect.com/business/seoyon-e-hwa-invests-76-million-in-chatham-county-facility-6506718)  ·  `page_000515`

## Facts

- Seoyon E-HWA, a global auto parts supplier, is investing almost $76 million in a new manufacturing facility in Chatham County.
- Seoyon E-Hwa chairman Ryu Yang-seok stated hope that the facility will provide new jobs in the region and contribute to making Savannah a central city for automobile development and production.
- Governor Brian Kemp announced on Feb. 1 that Seoyon’s new facility will be located at the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center.
- The new facility will create 740 new jobs.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated he promised last year when announcing the largest economic development project in Georgia history that it was just the start in bringing more jobs and opportunity to Georgians.
- Established in 1972, Seoyon E-Hwa operates 32 corporations in North America, South America, Europe, and Asia.
- The company supplies door trim, seats, bumpers, and other parts to Hyundai Motor Group, Kia, Mercedes-Benz, Ford, and Volkswagen.
- Savannah Economic Development Authority president Trip Tollison stated Seoyon E-HWA has a proven track record of supplying the automotive industry and will provide well-paying jobs for the region.
- Approximately 500 of the announced jobs will be permanent roles in human resources, development, quality, and production personnel.
- In October, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America officially broke ground in neighboring Bryan County for its first EV and battery manufacturing facility.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that projects like this will continue to help Georgia become the e-mobility capital of the nation and benefit communities in every zip code.
