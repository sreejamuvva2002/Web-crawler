# Hyundai Motor Group / SK On Georgia EV Battery Joint Venture

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-10-09

**Source:** [Hyundai, SK ON For Georgia EV Manufacturing Facility – Energy Transition Insider](https://energytransitioninsider.com/2024/10/09/hyundai-sk-on-for-georgia-ev-manufacturing-facility/)  ·  `page_008270`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group (HMG) and South Korean battery maker SK On have selected a site in Bartow County, Georgia, for a new electric vehicle (EV) battery manufacturing facility that will supply HMG’s plants in the United States.
- The project is considered one of the largest economic development projects in Georgia state history.
- Stakeholders estimate the project will create more than 3500 new jobs through approximately US$4 billion to US$5 billion of investment in Bartow County.
- HMG and SK On recently signed a memorandum of understanding (MOU) regarding the partnership for a new EV battery facility in the US, with details of the partnership still in development.
- SK On was established in 2021 as the lithium-ion battery subsidiary branch of SK Innovation.
- SK On currently employs more than 2000 people at its SK Battery America facility in Commerce, Georgia.
- Bartow County Sole Commissioner Steve Taylor stated that Bartow County is very pleased with the decision of Hyundai Motor Group and SK On.
- Commissioner Steve Taylor thanked the owners of the former Bartow Centre Industrial property and noted they pursued this extraordinary economic development project together.
- Commissioner Steve Taylor stated the project will have regional benefit for job seekers in the area and establishes Bartow in Georgia’s EV ecosystem in a significant way.
- Commissioner Steve Taylor highlighted that with Bartow’s strong history and talent-base built upon advanced manufacturing, the automotive industry, and innovation, the battery partnership should thrive in this environment.
- Commissioner Steve Taylor expressed looking forward to welcoming HMG and SK in meaningful ways in the community and enjoying a long-term partnership together.
- Director of Korean Investment Yoonie Kim represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The GDEcD partnered with Georgia EMC, Development Authority of Bartow County, the Cartersville-Bartow County Department of Economic Development, and Georgia Quick Start on the project.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that HMG and SK have been pioneering partners for Georgia for decades as one of the major drivers for Georgia’s automotive renaissance and as the first Korean manufacturer to locate in the state, respectively.
- Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that by supporting cooperation and partnerships across Georgia's growing EV ecosystem, they are creating a fully integrated supply chain for automotive OEMs while connecting battery manufacturers with recyclers to close the loop on battery manufacturing.
- Commissioner Pat Wilson expressed excitement for the jobs of the future this will create for Bartow County and northwest Georgia, and gratitude for support from community and utility partners.
- Georgia’s prime location, major ports system, extensive infrastructure, skilled workforce, and pro-business climate have made it an attractive location for a diverse array of rapidly developing industries focused on creating a sustainable future.
- Building on the assets that make the automotive industry successful, Georgia is positioned as a hub for the electric transportation industry.
- Excluding this announcement, EV-related projects announced in Georgia since 2020 total approximately US$17 billion in investment and more than 22,800 new jobs in Georgia.
- HMG separately announced in May its plans to invest US$5.54 billion in Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America, an EV manufacturing facility in Bryan County.
- HMG broke ground on the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America facility in October.
