# Aspen Aerogels Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** thermal_management  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [EV Battery Component Maker Cancels $325M Georgia Plant | Engineering News-Record](https://www.enr.com/articles/60311-ev-battery-component-maker-cancels-325m-georgia-plant)  ·  `page_001598`

## Facts

- Manufacturer Aspen Aerogels Inc. canceled plans to construct a $325-million plant in Statesboro, Ga., as told to investors on Feb. 12.
- Donald Young is the CEO of the Northborough, Mass.-based company Aspen Aerogels Inc.
- Aspen Aerogels will instead focus on maximizing capacity at its existing manufacturing plant in Rhode Island.
- Aspen had planned to produce thermal barriers for electric vehicle batteries at the canceled facility.
- The canceled plant was designed to be 500,000 square feet on a 90-acre site in southeast Georgia.
- New York-based Turner Construction Co. was selected as the contractor for the project.
- CEO Donald Young stated during an investor call that the company is taking decisive actions to navigate an evolving environment.
- Aspen will relocate some equipment from the canceled project to upgrade and expand its Rhode Island plant.
- The announcement follows automakers reporting slower-than-expected EV demand.
- The cancellation coincides with the administration of President Donald Trump pulling back Biden-era policies that supported EVs.
- President Donald Trump issued an executive order on the first day of his new term revoking a 2021 Biden order that set a non-binding goal of having EVs account for half of new cars sold in the U.S. by 2030.
- A Feb. 6 Federal Highway Administration memo informed state transportation officials that it was suspending a $5-billion program providing funding for EV chargers.
- Aspen had previously planned to open the Statesboro plant in 2024.
- Executives previously told investors that contracting out for some materials allowed for a later start of plant operations.
- Aspen now states it can meet long-term demand by expanding capacity at its East Providence, R.I., plant and relying on external manufacturing as needed.
- CFO and treasurer Ricardo Rodriguez stated that in early 2023, the company decided to right-time construction of its planned second aerogel manufacturing facility in Statesboro, Georgia, due to pre-empting a reset in EV demand expectations.
- Aspen subsequently ramped up its external manufacturing capacity for its energy industrial business following the early 2023 decision.
- Solar and battery storage manufacturer Freyr Battery also canceled its $2.6-billion plant project in Coweta County, Ga., in late January.
