# voestalpine Metal Forming GmbH

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Voestalpine Metal Forming Sets GA Plant in Barstow County - Daily News - Business Facilities Magazine](https://businessfacilities.com/voestalpine-metal-forming-sets-ga-plant-in-barstow-county/)  ·  `page_013839`

## Facts

- Gov. Nathan Deal announced that automotive industry supplier voestalpine Metal Forming will build a manufacturing facility near Cartersville in Bartow County.
- The new facility will create 220 jobs.
- The company is investing $62 million in the new facility.
- This facility will be voestalpine's first in the Southeast.
- Gov. Nathan Deal stated that Georgia offers automotive suppliers a great combination of resources, a well-trained workforce, growing logistics infrastructure, and a supportive business environment.
- Voestalpine’s Metal Forming Division, specifically its automotive body parts unit, provides a complete range of pressed parts in the body-in-white segment and highly innovative structural components to the automotive industry and suppliers.
- The company's customers represent several leading manufacturers in the automotive and automotive supply industries.
- CEO Herbert Eibensteiner stated that Cartersville was selected for its strategic location and business-friendly environment, which supports future plans for the North American automotive market.
- Bartow County’s geographic advantages influenced the decision, with convenient access to I-75 allowing efficient reach to key U.S. customers in the Southeast.
- The facility will maintain global connectivity through easy access to the Port of Savannah.
- Voestalpine will be the anchor tenant in the Highland 75 development in Bartow County.
- Highland 75 is a GRAD Certified 707-acre master planned industrial/corporate park.
- The park is located between Atlanta and Chattanooga, one mile east of I-75.
- Highland 75 is a collaboration of Bartow County, the city of Cartersville, and the Bartow-Cartersville Joint Development Authority.
- Bartow County Sole Commissioner Clarence Brown stated that voestalpine will be the first company in Highland 75, following years of development efforts by the Joint Development Authority to attract jobs.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) partnered with the Cartersville-Bartow County Department of Economic Development to secure the business opportunity.
- GDEcD project manager Wylly Harrison managed the project on behalf of the state.
- The project team collaborated with Antje Abshoff, managing director of Georgia’s European Office in Germany.
- The project team received assistance from Parker Poe Consulting, LLC and TPA Realty Services.
- GDEcD Commissioner Chris Cummiskey stated that the project is a significant win for Georgia's logistics network, workforce, and economic development partners.
- The company will receive training assistance from Georgia Quick Start.
- Georgia Quick Start is the nation’s top-ranked workforce training program that offers tailored, operations-specific training to qualified companies free of charge that expand or locate in Georgia.
