## Facts

- Haering Precision USA LP hosted its inaugural Skills Invitational in collaboration with North Georgia Technical College.
- The event aimed to ignite interest in machining and metals jobs among high school students while providing them with a platform to explore diverse career opportunities within the manufacturing industry.
- Participants from local high schools teamed up with senior precision machining students from North Georgia Tech in a proam style contest.
- The contest engaged participants in challenges designed to test their technical skills, creativity, and teamwork.
- Students faced a series of hands-on challenges carefully crafted to simulate real-world scenarios encountered in precision machining and manufacturing throughout the day-long event.
- Challenges included interpreting technical drawings and programming CNC machines.
- Judges evaluated teams based on criteria such as accuracy, efficiency, innovation, and collaborative problem-solving.
- Winners received various prizes, including tools and paid internship opportunities at Haering Precision USA LP's US facility.
- Franklin County High School senior Logan Turner took home first place in the contest.
- The event recognized contributions from participating high schools, students, faculty, and industry partners from Franklin, Hart, Stephens, and Union counties.
- Haering Precision USA LP plans to expand opportunities for students to explore and pursue careers in precision machining and manufacturing following the success of the inaugural event.
