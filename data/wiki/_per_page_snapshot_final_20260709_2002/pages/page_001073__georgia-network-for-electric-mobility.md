## Facts

- The fifth annual Electric Mobility Summit was held on April 14 and 15 in Athens at the University of Georgia Center for Continuing Education & Hotel.
- More than 250 representatives from industry, government, and academia attended the fifth annual Electric Mobility Summit.
- Fort Valley State University hosted a statewide electric vehicle awareness conference in partnership with the Georgia Network for Electric Mobility as a key workforce development initiative.
- The Fort Valley State University electric vehicle awareness conference took place on September 25, 2025, in Fort Valley, GA.
- More than 200 participants attended the Fort Valley State University electric vehicle awareness conference, including students, local officials, educators, industry leaders, and middle school students.
- The inaugural Battery Belt Charging Forward Forum was held on Tuesday, August 26, in Atlanta, GA.
- The Georgia Network for Electric Mobility (GNEM) hosted the inaugural Battery Belt Charging Forward Forum at the University of Georgia.
- Guest experts from the American EV Jobs Alliance, Kia Georgia, MAGNET, and the Salata Institute for Climate and Sustainability attended the Battery Belt Charging Forward Forum.
- Area governors, power company executives, and university presidents established the Mississippi-Alabama-Georgia Network for Evolving Transportation (MAGNET).
- MAGNET is a regional economic development initiative aimed at leveraging the Southeastern U.S. automotive vehicle and battery industry to stimulate economic development and job growth.
- The University of Georgia and Kia Georgia formed a partnership to advance Georgia's workforce development initiatives and overall economic growth.
- The Georgia Automotive Manufacturers Association (GAMA) joined the Georgia Network for Electric Mobility (GNEM) as its first non-profit/start-up partner and member.
- UGA has a corporate partnership with Georgia Power, which was highlighted in a spotlight video.
- Kia committed a $1 million gift to the UGA College of Engineering and the Georgia Network for Electric Mobility to advance electric mobility in Georgia.
- Cox Automotive provided a significant gift to the University of Georgia to help spearhead efforts to make electric mobility accessible to all Georgians and establish the state as a global leader in electric mobility innovation.
- Georgia Power Company committed a $5 million gift to the UGA College of Engineering to help the university aim to be a leader in e-mobility.
