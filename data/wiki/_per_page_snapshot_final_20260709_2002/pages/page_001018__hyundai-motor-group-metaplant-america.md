# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-03-27

**Source:** [Hyundai IONIQ 9 enters production as Georgia EV plant opens](https://electrek.co/2025/03/27/hyundai-ioniq-9-enters-production-georgia-ev-plant-opens/)  ·  `page_001018`

## Facts

- Hyundai’s first three-row electric SUV, the IONIQ 9, has officially entered production at its massive new EV plant in Georgia.
- The company held the grand opening on Wednesday at its massive new Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- The facility will build upcoming Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis EV and hybrid vehicles.
- Construction began less than two years prior to the opening ceremony.
- The new EV plant is part of a $7.6 billion investment in Georgia.
- This investment represents the state’s largest economic development project to date.
- The facility is expected to create about 8,500 direct jobs by 2030.
- Including battery joint ventures with SK On and LG Energy Solutions plus 18 suppliers, Hyundai’s new facility has attracted over $12.6 billion in investments.
- According to the Center for Automotive Research, Hyundai’s investments will create nearly 40,000 jobs in Georgia.
- During the grand opening, Hyundai Motor Group executive chair Euisun Chung said, “Together with the talented workforce at HMGMA, we are building the future of mobility with America, in America.”
- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp, Hyundai CEO Jose Munoz, and Kia CEO Ho Sung Song attended the opening ceremony.
- The new plant will eventually produce up to 500,000 EVs and hybrids under the Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis brands each year.
- Initially, the facility will produce 300,000 vehicles annually.
- Despite the recent opening ceremony, the first model, the 2025 Hyundai IONIQ 5, rolled off the assembly line in October.
- The IONIQ 9 features an upgraded electric SUV with up to 318 miles of range, a new interior and exterior style, and an NACS port for charging at Tesla Superchargers.
- Hyundai confirmed the IONIQ 9 is now officially in production and expects it to launch any day or this spring.
- Like the 2025 IONIQ 5, the IONIQ 9 will include an NACS port.
- The IONIQ 9 packs a massive 110.3 kWh battery, providing an EPA-estimated range of up to 335 miles.
- The vehicle’s “lounge-like” interior boasts up to 1,322 liters of cargo space, which is more than Kia’s three-row EV9 (up to 1,233 liters).
- The IONIQ 9 measures 5,060 mm (199.2″) long, 1,980 mm (78″) wide, and 1,790 mm (70.5″) tall, making it slightly bigger than the EV9.
- Hyundai will reveal prices closer to launch, but the IONIQ 9 is expected to start at about $60,000.
- The IONIQ 5 and IONIQ 9 will be joined by the first Kia vehicle built at the facility in 2026, possibly Kia’s EV4 electric sedan.
- The opening ceremony follows Hyundai’s landmark $21 billion investment in the US announced earlier this week.
- Hyundai will use $9 billion of that investment to boost Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis brand output to 1.2 million vehicles.
- Another $6 billion from the US investment will be used to build a new steel plant in Louisiana.
- GA Senator Jon Ossoff warned during a press conference on Wednesday that the Trump Administration’s war on electric vehicles poses a threat to EV sales, the EV market, and the automobile industry in Georgia and across the country.
- Ossoff stated that killing off federal tax credits will only put the US further behind China and others in the global auto and tech industry.
