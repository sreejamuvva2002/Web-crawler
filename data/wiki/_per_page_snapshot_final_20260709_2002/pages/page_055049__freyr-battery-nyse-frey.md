# FREYR Battery (NYSE: FREY)

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [North American Clean Energy - FREYR Battery Announces Plans for U.S. Gigafactory in Georgia](https://www.nacleanenergy.com/energy-storage/freyr-battery-announces-plans-for-u-s-gigafactory-in-georgia)  ·  `page_055049`

## Facts

- FREYR Battery has announced the selection and purchase of a site in Coweta County, Georgia for its planned Giga America battery plant.
- The company selected the Bridgeport Industrial Park site through a rigorous evaluation process based on key operational, logistical, and financial criteria.
- The site was chosen for its suitability to co-locate modular upstream and downstream projects with the Giga America cell manufacturing facility.
- FREYR is planning to initiate detailed project engineering of Giga America in the coming months.
- The initial phase of Giga America is planned to be a cell production module of approximately 34 GWh.
- The initial phase will utilize the next-generation of 24M’s U.S.-based SemiSolid™ production platform.
- The initial phase has an initial projected capital cost of $1.7 billion.
- The company is evaluating value accretive upstream and downstream modules as well as additional cell production lines.
- These additional modules and lines are expected to bring total capital investments to more than $2.6 billion through 2029.
- Upon completion of all contemplated construction phases, the Giga America complex is expected to be one of the largest battery cell manufacturing developments globally.
- FREYR’s Co-Founder and Chief Executive Officer Tom Einar Jensen stated the announcement underscores FREYR’s ambition to develop a very strong and near-term operational footprint in the United States.
- Jensen noted expanding into the U.S. has been a foundational aspect of FREYR’s long-term strategy from its inception.
- Jensen commented that with the recent passage of the Inflation Reduction Act, they expect U.S. demand for ESS, passenger EV and other electric mobility applications to grow rapidly over the next decade.
- Governor Brian Kemp said job creators and innovators from all over the world bring their operations to Georgia because they know they will have success here.
- Kemp stated Georgia is proud to welcome FREYR as the latest company to bring transformational investments and opportunity to its communities.
- Kemp noted FREYR will be a valued addition to Georgia's No. 1 state for business ranking.
- Kemp expressed looking forward to their growing impact on Coweta and the surrounding region.
- Coweta County Board of Commissioners Chairman Bob Blackburn said they are thrilled to welcome FREYR Battery to the Coweta County community.
- Blackburn stated with the addition of FREYR, Coweta County is quickly becoming a premier destination for top-tier manufacturing industries that support the U.S. economy and beyond.
- Blackburn expressed looking forward to the innovation, opportunities, and success that FREYR will generate.
- In accordance with ambitions to accelerate development of Giga America in parallel with the ongoing construction of Giga Arctic in Mo i Rana, Norway, the company is intensifying and broadening its financing efforts.
- The total package of financial incentives for Giga America includes robust assistance collectively provided by The State of Georgia and Coweta County.
- The incentive package also includes expected production tax credits associated with the recently passed Inflation Reduction Act.
- FREYR intends to apply with the U.S. Department of Energy for packages that could include a grant and/or direct loans to assist with the development of Giga America.
- The U.S. energy storage market is already the largest in the world.
- According to Rystad Energy, to satisfy a 1.6-degree scenario, cumulative collective storage demand of 4.5 TWh from the U.S. and Europe will be required through 2030.
- Under the same scenario, Rystad expects annual collective storage demand in the U.S. and Europe to reach 1.4 TWh by 2030.
- This represents a 34-fold increase from projected corresponding annual 2022 demand.
- Jensen added that FREYR is deeply committed to decarbonizing the transportation and energy sectors.
- Jensen stated they expect to make meaningful investments to spur job creation and the eventual development of localized, decarbonized supply chains in the U.S.
- Jensen noted these efforts aim to enhance energy security and economic activity and meet the growing demand for energy storage solutions in the U.S. market.
- The Giga America project will be based on the SemiSolid™ technology of FREYR’s U.S.-based licensing partner, 24M.
- The SemiSolid™ platform enables capital and energy efficient production of lithium-ion batteries at scale.
- FREYR is currently evaluating clean power supply solutions for the Giga America project with U.S. Independent Power Producers (IPPs).
- The company’s long-term ambition is to replicate the strategy employed at Giga Arctic to produce next-generation batteries that are among the cleanest anywhere in the world.
