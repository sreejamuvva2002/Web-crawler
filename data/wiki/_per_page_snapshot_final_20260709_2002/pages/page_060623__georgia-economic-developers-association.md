# Georgia Economic Developers Association

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2019-08-12

**Source:** [GEDA August Newsletter - Georgia Economic Developers Association](https://www.geda.org/news/geda-august-newsletter)  ·  `page_060623`

## Facts

- August GEDA Monthly Meeting will be in downtown Columbus at the Convention and Trade Center, with the program focus on transformation of downtown redevelopment.
- Panelists for the Columbus meeting include Richard Bishop (Chief of Staff, Office of the Mayor), Becca Zajac (Vice President of Marketing & Community Relations, Uptown Columbus), Peter Bowden (President of VisitColumbusGA), Pace Halter (President, W.C. Bradley Real Estate), and Betsy Covington (President, Community Foundation of the Chattahoochee Valley).
- Coastal Georgia Good News Gathering will be held on Wednesday, August 28, hosted by Jason Coley (Community Development Manager, Georgia Power).
- GEDA Annual Conference will be held at the Westin Savannah Harbor Golf Resort and Spa on September 18-20, 2019.
- The Georgia Small Business ROCK STAR Awards Application is now open, with nominations teaming up the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) and GEDA to recognize outstanding small businesses.
- Criteria for the ROCK STAR Awards include being a Georgia-based business with under 100 employees, with bonus points for those assisted by local or state resources, and peer-to-peer judging added for 2019.
- Andrew Carnes has been promoted from Senior Director of Economic Development to Vice President, Economic Development at Partnership Gwinnett.
- Deven Cason has been promoted from Project Manager to Director of Economic Development at Partnership Gwinnett.
- Andrea Taylor (CEO, Hazlehurst-Jeff Davis Chamber & JDA) was recognized as a new Georgia Certified Chamber Executive (GCCE).
- On August 1, 2019, Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Rivacold will create 140 jobs and invest $5 million in a new manufacturing facility in Buford to manufacture refrigeration systems.
- Partners involved in the Rivacold project include Andrew Carnes and Deven Cason (Partnership Gwinnett), Nico Wijnberg (GDEcD Global Commerce Division), MaryBeth Flournoy (Georgia Power), and Rodger Brown (Quick Start).
- On July 17, 2019, GDEcD announced that Variotech will create 20 new jobs at a new North American headquarters in Fulton County as a world leader in tray solutions.
- Partners for the Variotech project include Fernanda Kirchner (GDEcD Global Commerce Division) and Bob Kosek (Invest Atlanta).
- On July 15, 2019, GDEcD announced that Edifecs plans to hire up to 200 associates over the next three to five years in a new office location in Fulton County.
- Partners for the Edifecs project include Randall Toussaint (GDEcD Global Commerce Division), Rebecca Jordan (Metro Atlanta Chamber), and MaryBeth Flournoy (Georgia Power).
- On July 12, 2019, Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Stitch Fix will open a new distribution center in Lithia Springs, creating over 900 new jobs in the next few years.
- Partners for the Stitch Fix project include Chris Pumphrey (Douglas County Economic Development Authority), Hank Evans (GDEcD Global Commerce Division), MaryBeth Flournoy (Georgia Power), and Josh Stephens (Metro Atlanta Chamber).
- On July 11, 2019, Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Kroger and Ocado Solutions will join forces to build a new distribution facility in Forest Park, creating 410 jobs and investing $121 million.
- Partners for the Kroger/Ocado project include Wylly Harrison (GDEcD Global Commerce Division), Charlie Moseley (Georgia Power), Rodger Brown (Quick Start), and local officials from the City of Forest Park.
- On July 10, 2019, Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Amazon will open a new fulfillment center in Gwinnett and DeKalb counties, creating 1,000 new jobs.
- Partners for the Amazon project include Andrew Carnes (Partnership Gwinnett), Hank Evans (GDEcD Global Commerce Division), and Bradley Harris (Georgia EMC).
- On July 10, 2019, Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Swiss manufacturer Preci-Dip will open its first U.S. production facility in Bryan County to manufacture electronic connector pins, investing $13.6 million and creating 100 new jobs.
- Partners for the Preci-Dip project include Anna Chafin (Development Authority of Bryan County), Joseph Huntemann (GDEcD Global Commerce Division), David Denison (Georgia EMC), and David Sink (Colliers International).
- On August 1, 2019, GDEcD announced it is accepting nominations for the 2019 Georgia Automotive Awards through September 13, with winners to be announced in the fall.
- The Vibrant Communities grant is accepting applications for arts projects in counties without FY20 grants, with a deadline of August 30, 2019.
- GEDA is offering up to $600 in scholarship money as reimbursement for expenses associated with the AC19 conference, with three scholarships available.
