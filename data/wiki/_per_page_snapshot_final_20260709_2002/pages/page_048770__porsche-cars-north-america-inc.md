## Facts

- Porsche Cars North America’s Atlanta campus is expanding with the opening of a second driver experience track to the public and a new classic car restoration facility.
- Kjell Gruner, President and CEO of Porsche Cars North America, Inc., stated that the company has been committed to Atlanta for 25 years and is thrilled to expand its U.S. headquarters campus.
- Both expansion projects were intended to enhance the overall brand experience and were developed with the customer, local community, and environment in mind.
- The new track features a Handling Circuit, which is a 1.3 mile lap around the outer ring designed as a continuous loop with turns and elevation changes.
- The track includes a Low-Friction Circle measuring more than 196 feet in diameter, featuring various levels of grip and polish sprayed with water to test driver control and induce understeer and oversteer.
- The track features an Ice Hill with a highly polished concrete surface, an 8% slope, and computer-controlled water jets.
- The track includes an Autocross area of expansive asphalt that can be configured for slaloms, acceleration and braking zones, or other driving options.
- Porsche’s Journey Restoration facility officially opened in 2024.
- The restoration facility has the capacity to restore more than 400 cars per year.
- As of March 2024, there was a waiting list of 849 vehicles for the restoration service.
- The Porsche Cars North America Experience Center is located at 6100 Interstate 85 Business Loop, College Park, Atlanta.
