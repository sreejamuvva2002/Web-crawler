# Volvo Cars

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-12-22

## Facts

- Volvo Cars will start production of a new next-gen hybrid model before 2030.
- The new model details have yet to be released but will be designed for the U.S. market.
- Producing the new model will help Volvo boost capacity utilization at its South Carolina plant.
- Volvo Cars has plans to produce its XC60 mid-size SUV at the South Carolina plant by late 2026.
- The South Carolina factory currently builds only Volvo's fully electric EX90 SUV and the Polestar 3.
- Production data shows that the EX90 and Polestar 3 models take up only a small fraction of the site's annual capacity of 150,000 vehicles.
- Volvo Cars CEO Hakan Samuelsson stated, "It will take time until America is electric everywhere."
- Samuelsson described the larger hybrid model as "more an electric car, but with a Plan B," explaining that it normally drives electrically but kicks in a combustion engine when the battery is flat.
- Auto makers worldwide are scrambling to rethink production strategies to contend with President Donald Trump's duties on foreign cars as part of his wider policy to boost U.S. manufacturing.
- Volvo Cars announced in July that it plans to start producing its popular XC60 mid-size SUV at the South Carolina plant from late 2026.
- The news coincided with Volvo's 70th anniversary in the United States.
- Volvo, long positioned as an EV trailblazer with the aim of phasing out all non-electric models by 2030, reversed course last year and said hybrids would remain part of its lineup.
- A trade deal struck in July with the European Union cut U.S. tariffs on European cars to 15%, but the agreement has yet to take effect, with vehicles continuing to face a 27.5% levy.
- Volvo Cars Americas president Luis Rezende said adding another model into production there will be "a big step toward realising the full potential of our local manufacturing investments and workforce."
- Samuelsson noted that the 10% duties eliminated on auto imports from the U.S. into Europe will make selling its U.S.-produced EX90 vehicle abroad more attractive.
- Samuelsson stated regarding the tariff reduction, "With this 10% reduction, we have the possibility to grow," referring to Volvo's operations in Europe.
