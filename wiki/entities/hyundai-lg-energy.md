# Hyundai-LG Energy

**Type:** Joint Venture  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[battery-cell-manufacturing|Battery Cell Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** Ellabell, Georgia, Georgia, United States

## Overview

A joint venture between Hyundai and LG Energy to build a large battery manufacturing plant in Ellabell, Georgia. The $7.6 billion project will supply batteries to Hyundai’s nearby electric-vehicle factory and is projected to create 8,500 jobs by 2031.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Battery cell manufacturing for electric vehicles

## Key facts

- Federal agents arrested about 450 workers, including more than 30 South Korean nationals, at the construction site.
- The raid was tied to an investigation into unlawful employment practices and other serious federal crimes.
- South Korea’s foreign ministry expressed concern and regret over the detention of its nationals.
- LG Energy confirmed some employees were detained and said the company has paused work at the site while cooperating with investigators.
- The Ellabell complex represents a $7.6 billion investment by Hyundai and LG Energy and is projected to create 8,500 jobs by 2031.
- Hyundai recently announced boosting its U.S. investment to $26 billion through 2028.
- Analysts warned that large-scale enforcement operations could damage trust and industrial cooperation between the United States and South Korea.
