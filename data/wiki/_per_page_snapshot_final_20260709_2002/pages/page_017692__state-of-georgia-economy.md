# State of Georgia Economy

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2025-04-01

**Source:** [White Paper: The State of Georgia’s Economy in 2025 – Haruka Scott](https://hshbrands.com/2025/04/01/white-paper-the-state-of-georgias-economy-in-2025/)  ·  `page_017692`

## Facts

- Manufacturing GDP rose to $86.6 billion in 2024, driven by durable goods such as machinery, aerospace, and fabricated metals.
- Manufacturing employment showed mild contraction, down roughly 0.7% year-over-year.
- Transportation and Warehousing contributed $37.6 billion in economic output in 2024, highlighting Georgia’s critical logistics position in the southeastern U.S.
- Advanced manufacturing sectors such as electric vehicles, aerospace, and automation present strong medium-term growth prospects for Georgia.
- Georgia’s existing base in transportation equipment and fabricated metal products can serve as a springboard for advanced manufacturing growth.
- There are ongoing opportunities to invest in port infrastructure (Savannah), air freight (Atlanta), and highway expansion to support Georgia’s role as a national logistics center.
- Federal and state funds directed toward these logistics assets will be pivotal for regional economic development.
- Skills gaps persist, particularly in technology and healthcare. While GDP in these areas is rising, matching labor supply with demand will require concerted investment in workforce development.
