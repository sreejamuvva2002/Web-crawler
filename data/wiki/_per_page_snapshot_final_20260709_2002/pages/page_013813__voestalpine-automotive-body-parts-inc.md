# voestalpine Automotive Body Parts Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2016-06

**Source:** [Auto supplier voestalpine to invest $11 million in Alabama facility - Made in Alabama](https://www.madeinalabama.com/2016/06/voestalpine/)  ·  `page_013813`

## Facts

- Austrian automotive supplier voestalpine Automotive Body Parts Inc. announced plans to open an $11.1 million Birmingham facility expected to create 42 jobs.
- The company will produce a variety of automotive components, including stamped and hot formed parts, at an existing facility located at 260-A Lyon Lane in Birmingham’s Oxmoor Valley.
- Governor Robert Bentley stated that automotive manufacturing is a significant industry in Alabama and praised the state's workforce as a strong partner for voestalpine's project.
- The expansion into Alabama stems from the overall growth of the automotive segment and increased demand from voestalpine's customers.
- The Birmingham facility spans 52,000 square feet and provides capacity for future growth.
- Local managing director Philipp Schulz stated the investment is an important step in the company's U.S. growth strategy and expressed excitement about joining Alabama's automotive supplier network.
- The company thanked state and local officials along with the Birmingham Industrial Development Board for their assistance in the project.
- Voestalpine is a key supplier to European automakers and is expanding into Alabama as the state's auto supply chain undergoes significant growth.
- Last year, more than $3 billion in new investment was announced in Alabama's auto sector, with most of that coming from suppliers.
- Employment by automotive suppliers in Alabama has more than doubled since 2010.
- Greg Canfield, secretary of the Alabama Department of Commerce, described voestalpine as a leading partner to the European auto industry and a strong addition to Alabama's expanding automotive supply chain.
- Greg Canfield stated that the state's economic development team will continue working hard to build out the supply chain with new jobs and new investment.
- Voestalpine worked closely with the Alabama Department of Commerce, City of Birmingham, Alabama Power Co., and Birmingham Business Alliance on the expansion.
- As part of "Project Alpine," the City of Birmingham agreed to abate the non-educational allocated portion of sales and property taxes related to the project.
- The tax abatement incentive is valued at an estimated $960,237 over 10 years.
- Birmingham Mayor William A. Bell Sr. noted that this project marks the second company within the voestalpine group to locate within the city, the first being voestalpine Nortrack.
- Jefferson County Commissioner David Carrington welcomed voestalpine to the area and expressed anticipation for its positive economic impact and future expansion opportunities.
- The Linz, Austria-based parent company voestalpine Group AG comprises 500 companies with locations in more than 50 countries across five continents.
- Beyond automotive and railway systems, the parent company operates in segments including energy, construction, mechanical engineering, and aerospace.
- BBA senior project manager Jeff Traywick stated the Birmingham location will provide voestalpine ready access to existing and potential new automotive customers while offering overall efficiencies.
