# Dongwon Autopart Technology

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-02-06

**Source:** [Automotive company to hire 200 people in the CSRA with a record investment - Augusta Business Daily](https://augustabusinessdaily.com/automotive-company-to-hire-200-people-in-the-csra-with-a-record-investment/)  ·  `page_053965`

## Facts

- Dongwon Autopart Technology plans a $30 million investment for a new automotive project in the CSRA, Georgia.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp’s office announced that the company will build a new production facility in Emanuel County.
- Hiring for the new facility will draw workers from Burke, Jefferson, and Jenkins counties to fill management, system leads, and production positions.
- Hiring for approximately 200 positions will begin this year.
- Dongwon Autopart Technology is a 55-year-old company that supplies components such as doorframes, seat frames, and battery frames to automotive original equipment manufacturers (OEMs).
- The company currently supports more than 500 direct jobs in the Southeast.
- Existing Dongwon Autopart Technology facilities in Hogansville, Georgia, will remain operational.
- Heather Hollstein, Human Resources Manager at Dongwon Autopart Technology, stated the company has been operating in the United States since 2003 and remains committed to excellence and supplying customers with high-quality products and service.
- The new manufacturing facility will be located at the Hwy 297 Industrial Park near Swainsboro.
- Ken Warnock, CEO of the Emanuel County Development Authority, stated his organization assisted Dongwon Autopart Technology with site selection and that the project will be the single largest new economic development project for Emanuel County in over 30 years.
