# Hanon Systems

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** thermal_management  ·  **Published:** 2023-05-23

**Source:** [Vehicle AC manufacturer to build Bulloch plant - Statesboro Herald](https://www.statesboroherald.com/local/vehicle-ac-manufacturer-build-bulloch-plant/)  ·  `page_001397`

## Facts

- A manufacturer of vehicle air conditioning systems will build a plant in Statesboro’s Gateway Regional Industrial Park.
- Gov. Brian Kemp announced that Hanon Systems will create 160 new jobs.
- Hanon Systems will invest more than $40 million in the new facility in Bulloch County.
- Hanon Systems is a global provider of automotive thermal and energy management solutions, specifically air conditioning.
- Gov. Brian Kemp stated that Georgia’s automotive and e-mobility industries continue to grow.
- Gov. Brian Kemp stated that Georgia is furthering its position as the EV capital of America.
- South Korean-based Hanon Systems’ new facility will be built next to the Cardinal Glass plant in Gateway.
- The new facility will manufacture AC systems for electric vehicles that will be manufactured at the Hyundai Metaplant in Bryan County.
- Operations at the new Hanon Systems facility are expected to begin in May 2024.
- Billy Allen, chair of the Development Authority of Bulloch County, stated that Statesboro-Bulloch County is honored by Hanon Systems’ decision to locate its new facility there.
- Billy Allen noted that the facility will work closely with the Hyundai Metaplant and the electric vehicle industry.
- Billy Allen stated that the decision highlights the resources offered by the community and region to help companies succeed.
