# Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Georgia (Meta Plant) & Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia (West Point Plant)

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-08

**Source:** [Insights & Observations: Inside Hyundai’s Meta & Kia’s West Point Plants - Autoline](https://www.autoline.tv/featured/insights-observations-inside-hyundais-meta-kias-wet-point-plants/)  ·  `page_008371`

## Facts

- Georgia is quickly turning into an automotive powerhouse.
- Hyundai operates a brand-new manufacturing facility near Savannah called the Meta plant.
- Kia operates an established assembly plant in West Point near Atlanta.
- Both plants operate under the same corporate umbrella (Hyundai Group).
- The Hyundai Meta plant buildings are painted very light sage green to blend with rural surroundings, featuring rounded corners on every building exterior.
- The original plan for the Hyundai Meta plant was to only make electric cars, starting with the Hyundai Ioniq 5 and Ioniq 9.
- EV sales did not materialize as expected, prompting the addition of the Kia Sportage to increase manufacturing volume.
- The Hyundai Meta plant is highly automated but operates at very low volume with lines moving slowly.
- Production at the Hyundai Meta plant started in October 2024.
- The Hyundai Meta plant does not expect to reach full line speed until 2028.
- The plant requires more models to fill assembly lines and its advanced automation needs ongoing tweaking and adjustment.
- Metal stamping at the Meta plant uses one massive transfer press line made by Rotem, a division of Hyundai.
- There is only one transfer press line at the Meta plant, meaning it produces all panels for all three models with no backup if it fails.
- The Meta plant makes all major A-class panels including body sides, roofs, floors, and fenders, while smaller stampings are shipped in from suppliers.
- Racks of stampings are stacked to the ceiling at the Meta plant due to the single press line constraint.
- No forklifts are used at the Meta plant; all material movement is handled by Automated Guided Vehicles (AGVs) and heavy-duty electric tuggers.
- The welding shop at the Meta plant uses huge Fanuc robots to pick up entire body-in-white structures.
- The brand-new Meta plant is still ironing out quality issues, including line workers hammering door sills and bodies being pulled off the line for repair loops.
- Cars spend six to eight hours in the Meta plant paint shop using one gallon of paint per vehicle.
- The paint shop at the Meta plant is not accessible on tours to prevent dust and dirt contamination.
- At the Meta plant final assembly, doors are removed for interior assembly and reinstalled later entirely by robots.
- Every car at the Meta plant rides on an AGV equipped with an automatic up-and-down lift to adjust ergonomic height at each workstation.
- Hyundai leverages its subsidiaries at the Meta plant: Hyundai Steel supplies steel, Hyundai Rotem makes presses, Hyundai Glovis handles logistics, and Hyundai Mobis handles battery pack assembly.
- The Hyundai Meta plant currently employs about 1,800 people.
- The Meta plant is aggressively hiring to add a second shift, which will increase employment to 2,600.
- Production at the Meta plant remains very low, with only about 6,000 cars shipped in the month prior to the visit.
- When the Meta plant reaches capacity, Kia will receive about 40% of the production.
- The Hyundai Meta plant has a current capacity of 300,000 units but plans to boost it to 500,000.
- Hyundai is planning to add a second plant at the Meta site by knocking out an exterior wall to add another 250,000 units of capacity.
- The additional space at the Meta plant will likely be tooled for upcoming midsize body-on-frame trucks and SUVs from both Hyundai and Kia.
- Kia’s West Point assembly plant began operations 16 years ago.
- The Kia West Point plant spans roughly 1.7 million square feet under roof across stamping, paint, body shop, and final assembly.
- The Kia West Point plant is tooled to produce 350,000 vehicles a year.
- A single line at the West Point plant sequentially builds the Telluride, Sorento, Sportage, EV6, and EV9.
- The West Point plant builds gas, hybrid, and pure EV vehicles sequentially on one line.
- The Kia West Point plant employs 3,200 workers across three eight-hour shifts, five days a week.
- The West Point plant performs on-the-fly shift handoffs so the assembly line never stops.
- Daily maintenance at the West Point plant is scheduled during 30-minute lunch breaks and weekends.
- The Kia West Point stamping shop runs two Rotem transfer presses.
- Die sets and storage racks for stamped panels at the West Point plant are color-coded to specific vehicle models to prevent mistakes.
- Robots at the West Point plant pick stamped panels off the press line and keep a “last hit” panel on each rack for quality verification.
- The West Point plant maintains about 36 hours of inventory in stamped panels.
- The West Point body shop uses zero batch building, constructing different bodies sequentially to match sales orders.
- A modular body buck at the West Point plant adapts on the fly to accommodate different models by indexing parts in or out.
- The West Point general assembly area features open sightlines with parts racks no higher than chest-high.
- Unlike the Meta plant, the West Point plant uses two separate trim lines where human operators assisted by mechanical lift equipment handle door installation.
- Workers on the final line at the West Point plant rotate jobs every two hours to prevent fatigue and boredom.
- The West Point plant paints 14 different colors, primarily white, black, and silver.
- The final assembly line at the West Point plant is tightly packed with only about four feet of clearance between cars.
- The Kia West Point plant produces 1,500 cars per day.
- Every vehicle at the West Point plant undergoes testing on a 2.2-mile on-site track vital for calibrating lane-keeping systems.
- The Kia West Point facility features an on-site transmission assembly plant.
- Kia sells transmissions produced at the West Point plant to outside competitors including Nissan and Volkswagen.
- The Kia West Point plant has a 13,000-spot parking lot with protective roofs built over part of the vehicle staging areas.
- A severe hail storm caused massive damage to inventory at the West Point plant last year, resulting in significant repair costs.
- Management added protective roofs and solar panels over the staging areas at the West Point plant but is still assessing cost-effectiveness for full coverage.
- The Hyundai Meta plant relies heavily on vast open space to allow AGVs to maneuver.
- The Kia West Point plant uses about 17 labor-hours to make each vehicle, including stamping.
- Hyundai and Kia are on track to produce more than 1.4 million vehicles a year in the U.S., surpassing current Toyota and Honda output.
