# Fox Factory

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2022-02-24

**Source:** [Fox Factory’s annual revenues up 46%; execs see no letup | Bicycle Retailer and Industry News](https://www.bicycleretailer.com/industry-news/2022/02/24/fox-factory’s-annual-revenues-46-execs-see-no-letup)  ·  `page_020247`

## Facts

- Fox Factory executives are located in Duluth, Ga.
- All orders for Fox Factory's OE bike products are pre-booked for 2022.
- Executives expect no letup in demand until next year or beyond.
- Fox Factory announced that its revenues last year hit $1.3 billion, a company record and a 45.9% increase over its 2020 sales.
- CEO Mike Dennison said bicycle factories will continue to demand Fox, Race Face, Marzocchi and Easton components well into the future.
- Dennison noted that while bikes are starting to show up in dealer showrooms more frequently with better selection, the distribution system from centers to hubs regionally and internationally remains fundamentally empty.
- Dennison estimated there are multiple months of demand just to deliver pre-orders, followed by 12 or 18 months of rebuilding inventory.
- Fox's customers are confident that there will be "new, fresh demand" for bikes well after the inventory is rebuilt.
- Dennison stated Fox has not been a choke point in the supply chain but must carefully balance its production with factories' ability to find other parts needed to complete bikes.
- Fox manufactures in Taiwan, the U.S., and Canada, but relies on parts from China.
- Fox's bicycle product category sales increased 57.8% last year to $579 million, up from $367 million in 2020.
- Fox's Powered Vehicles division saw a sales increase of 37.5% last year to $720 million.
- Dennison described the raw material and component supply chain as "a daily battle" for Fox.
- Dennison expressed concern about brownouts in China manufacturing cutting energy back, which impacts magnesium and magnesium supply monitoring.
- Fox has set a goal of reaching $2 billion in annual sales by 2025.
- Fox is forecasting full-year 2022 sales of $1,435 million to $1,465 million.
- Fox forecasts non-GAAP adjusted earnings per diluted share for 2022 in the range of $4.90 to $5.20.
- Fox's shares are traded on NASDAQ under the FOXF symbol.
