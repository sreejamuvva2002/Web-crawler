## Facts

- Project location is Atlanta, GA.
- Project status is completed.
- Structure type is an office building.
- Target LEED certification is Gold.
- Client is Porsche Cars North America.
- The project involved developing a 27-acre site into a 200,000 SF state-of-the-art facility.
- The headquarters consists of a building, experience center, business center, and an adjacent structure.
- The experience center showcases Porsche vehicle capabilities.
- The experience center includes a 1.6-mile drive development test track running through the courtyard.
- The test track includes six driving modules to demonstrate experiences of different Porsche vehicles.
- A simulator lab allows guests to race on 15 tracks in a virtual setting.
- The building provides modern facilities for 400 employees.
- Facilities include a technical service training center.
- Facilities include a Heritage Center with galleries of vintage automobiles.
- Facilities include a garage to work on concept vehicles.
- Facilities include an employee cafeteria.
- The training center uses advanced laboratory testing equipment to assess individuals' physiological reactions to exercise.
- Assessments are used to formulate unique training plans that maximize efficiency.
- The facility includes Restaurant 356, named after the first automobile to carry the Porsche family name.
- The project was built on a remediated brownfield site previously occupied by a Ford Motor Company automobile plant.
- The facility was constructed at the end of an active runway at Hartsfield Jackson Atlanta International Airport.
- Construction required extensive coordination efforts due to the airport location.
- The project achieved LEED Gold certification.
- The project received the 2015 Project of the Year award from the Construction Management Association of America – South Atlantic Chapter.
- The project received the 2015 Project of the Year award from ENR Southeast.
- The project received the 2015 Best Projects Award from ENR Southeast.
