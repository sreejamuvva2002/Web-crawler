# Georgia Ports Authority

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2024-03-06

**Source:** [Georgia Ports highlights site selection opportunities - DredgeWire : DredgeWire](https://dredgewire.com/georgia-ports-highlights-site-selection-opportunities/)  ·  `page_001264`

## Facts

- Georgia Ports Authority (GPA) is a key factor for automakers, their suppliers, other manufacturers, and logistics operations deciding to bring jobs to Georgia.
- GPA President and CEO Griff Lynch stated that GPA's mission is to drive success across the entire state, not just Savannah or Atlanta.
- In partnership with the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD), the state announced 350 projects from potential port users in Fiscal Year 2023.
- The FY2023 project announcements will bring more than 33,000 manufacturing and logistics jobs and $21 billion in investment to 96 counties across Georgia.
- GPA, GDEcD, and the Georgia Department of Transportation are working together to foster responsible growth that delivers good jobs while preserving quality of life.
- Georgia Ports is investing $29 million in an overpass that will route trucks leaving its Ocean Terminal in Savannah directly onto U.S. 17, avoiding community streets.
- According to GDEcD's Global Commerce division, Georgia's growing electric vehicle ecosystem has touched every corner of the state.
- The growth of Georgia's EV ecosystem is led by announcements associated with Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America, which have located in more than 10 counties across the state.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson noted that companies in e-mobility are prioritizing speed-to-market solutions.
- Communities can prepare for new opportunities by investing in site development and obtaining "Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development" certification.
- Anovion Technologies, a synthetic graphite manufacturer creating 400 jobs in Decatur County, Southwest Georgia, was swayed to locate there by the state's recruitment effort.
- Anovion Executive Chairman Chip Dunn cited meetings with Gov. Brian Kemp, GPA's inland port system, and access to waterway and land transport as factors in the company's decision.
- GPA's inland port system includes the Appalachian Regional Port in Crandall, Ga., and the Blue Ridge Connector rail yard now under construction near Gainesville, Ga.
- The inland port system supports the movement of large, heavy products from centrally located communities to the state's deepwater ports.
- In the Savannah area alone, the inventory of industrial property totals more than 124 million square feet.
- More than 14 million square feet of industrial property in the Savannah area was under construction at the close of Calendar Year 2023.
- Cushman & Wakefield broker Stephen Ezelle stated in a fourth-quarter report that the Savannah regional economy continues to experience expansion and strong absorption in the industrial sector despite a slowing U.S. economy.
- Ezelle projected that Savannah's industrial market will eclipse 8 million TEUs of throughput by 2030 due to continued growth at Georgia Ports.
- The operational efficiency offered by GPA and the shift in import cargo from the West Coast and NY/NJ are key drivers in the growth of Savannah's industrial market.
- Despite national moderation of container trade, Savannah is expected to continue balanced expansion thanks to the Hyundai announcement and GPA's growing national market share.
- Ezelle predicted continued absorption of well-located light industrial warehouse space in Savannah.
- Savannah's industrial sector ended 2023 with an overall vacancy rate of 7 percent.
- GPA Director of economic and industrial development Stacy Watson stated that being within 30 miles of the port is great but not required to be competitive in drayage, lease rates, and cost of doing business.
- Watson noted that locations 40 or 50 miles away from Savannah are an easy drive due to the port's location upriver of the population center and GDOT's efforts to reduce truck route congestion.
- Direct truck routes from the Port of Savannah connect to Interstates 95 and 16, and on-terminal service is provided by two Class I railroads.
- Watson highlighted that available properties in Bryan, Liberty, and McIntosh counties, as well as points west, offer interstate and rail links to the port and inland markets.
- Augusta is a short drive from Savannah, and a majority of its manufacturers are port users.
- Development Authority of Augusta President Cal Wray stated that Augusta-Richmond County provides an ideal location for port users due to available affordable industrial land, a key base for manufacturers with over 350 already calling it home, and easy rail/highway connections to the port.
- Recent announcements in Augusta include Aurubis Richmond, a metal smelting operation bringing 250 jobs and $600 million in investment.
- Denkai America, a copper film producer, is providing 250 jobs and $440 million in investment in Augusta.
- Purecycle Technologies, which recycles plastic into like-new pellets for reuse, announced 100 jobs and $400 million in investment in Augusta.
- Augusta has a stand-out electric vehicle manufacturing ecosystem including EZGo Textron and Club Car with related suppliers in Richmond and Columbia counties.
- Metals and chemical manufacturing are two other important industries in the Augusta area.
- GPA supports efficient operation of existing industries and local economic development efforts, serving as a key partner in recruiting new industry.
- GPA is a resource for existing manufacturers who have invested billions in the region and announced significant further investments in the last five years.
