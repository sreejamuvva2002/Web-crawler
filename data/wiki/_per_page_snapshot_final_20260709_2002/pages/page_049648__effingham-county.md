# Effingham County

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-04-30

**Source:** [Effingham County: Disciplined Growth - Georgia Trend Magazine](https://www.georgiatrend.com/2023/04/30/effingham-county-disciplined-growth/)  ·  `page_049648`

## Facts

- Effingham County is preparing for an influx of new residents, businesses, and industry due to multiple massive economic development announcements over the past year.
- Infrastructure improvements, quality-of-life amenities, and expanded healthcare services are underway across Effingham County and its cities of Springfield, Rincon, and Guyton.
- Last summer (relative to April 2023), Hyundai Motor Group announced its first fully dedicated electric vehicle (EV) and battery manufacturing facility in Georgia, located in Bryan County.
- Effingham County is part of the four-county Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority.
- Brandt Herndon serves as CEO of the Effingham County Industrial Development Authority (IDA).
- The Hyundai project represents a $5.54 billion investment, making it the largest economic development project in Georgia state history.
- The Hyundai project will deliver approximately 8,100 new jobs to Georgia’s coastal region.
- Sewon America, a subsidiary of Korean-based Sewon Precision Industry Co., announced 740 new jobs and a $300 million facility for auto body parts manufacturing in Rincon.
- The Sewon investment is the largest known private investment in the City of Rincon.
- Sewon is one of many suppliers for the Hyundai Metaplant that have collectively committed over $1.8 billion in investment.
- The Effingham County IDA is developing Grande View, a 450-acre park located off Old Augusta Road, where the new Hyundai supplier will be situated.
- The Georgia International Trade Center offers up to 7.2 million square feet of light industrial and manufacturing warehouse space as part of a $500 million development.
- The trade center park spans 1,150 acres and is almost fully built out, leaving only 100 acres of marketable property.
- Tenants at the Georgia International Trade Center include Shaw Industries, Pon.Bike (Pacific Cycle), Serena & Lily, Lowe’s, DeWell Group, Dukal, and Sunland Logistics, all newcomers to Effingham.
- County Manager Tim Callanan arrived approximately four years ago as growth in the area began to explode.
- The county set aside specific areas for industrial development a decade ago, which allowed logistics companies to locate there a few years later.
- Effingham now contains about a half-dozen million-square-foot warehouses due to logistics company locations near the port.
- Logistics operations diversified Effingham's property tax digest and provide revenue for road capacity, parks, and water/sewer expansion.
- The county benefits from Transportation Special Purpose Local Sales Tax (TSPLOST) dollars and has completed about 80% of its planned projects.
- TSPLOST funding covered repaving efforts, including $5 million of the county’s $20 million share of the $52 million Effingham Parkway.
- The Effingham Parkway is a north-south corridor extending from central Effingham into Chatham County, scheduled for completion in 2025 to provide traffic relief.
- Plans include constructing nine roundabouts to improve traffic flow.
- Unlike neighboring counties announcing mega-developments of 10,000 or 13,000 housing units, Effingham maintains steady housing growth.
- The county issues about 600 new home construction permits annually, a sustainable level expected to increase due to proximity to the Hyundai plant.
- To prepare for population growth, Effingham County is expanding its wastewater treatment plant to triple its current capacity.
- Effingham is extending water lines westward toward the Ogeechee River and south of Guyton to the Chatham County border to meet demand from developers near the Hyundai plant.
- SPLOST funds will finance approximately $25 million in parks projects over the next two years, including renovations for all existing parks and acquisition of land for new ones.
- Rincon, which has the county’s largest population, is doubling the capacity of its wastewater treatment plant as part of major infrastructure preparations.
- City Manager Jonathan Lynn states that the Sewon announcement brings jobs and residents needed to attract major retailers like Target, Chick-fil-A, Hobby Lobby, or TJ Maxx.
- Guyton City Manager Meketa Hendricks Brown is surveying her town of 2,300 residents on recreation, housing, and transportation needs to prepare for the Hyundai plant's impact.
- Guyton is educating staff and residents on Korean culture to welcome new residents and will launch a website with city services translated into Korean.
- Guyton is working to accommodate additional sewer capacity in anticipation of residential housing growth within the next 18-24 months.
- Springfield, the county seat, is experiencing increased residential and commercial growth compared to previous years.
- Springfield officials are considering additional downtown parking and converting an old ballpark into greenspace with a playground, walking trails, and new bathrooms.
- Effingham Health System (EHS) has undergone major transformation over the past five years under President and CEO Fran Witt, who holds a doctorate of nursing practice and serves on the American Hospital Association’s Rural Health Services Committee.
- EHS established new centers for cardiology, orthopedics, and cancer care within the past five years to align with growth and expansion.
- EHS implemented Oracle Cerner, an integrated electronic health record and financial ecosystem across its patient care locations.
- EHS plans to expand its emergency room and open a new Effingham Pediatric Center to serve families expected to migrate to the county over the next few years.
- EHS received 2022 Small Hospital of the Year honors from the Georgia Alliance of Community Hospitals and employs over 470 people.
- Andrew Cripps arrived four years ago as head of the Effingham Chamber of Commerce, noting that strong growth was already underway before the Hyundai plant announcement accelerated it.
- The chamber is stimulating commercial and industrial growth in Effingham to provide local job opportunities and reduce commutes to Savannah for residents.
- The chamber urges a balanced approach to commercial and residential growth to prevent the tax burden from falling fully on residential taxpayers.
- Effingham Forward is a chamber initiative educating the community on how balanced growth provides tax benefits, jobs, opportunity, and reduces road infrastructure burdens for commuters.
- The Georgia Salzburger Society was established in 1925 as a genealogical and archaeological organization to recognize the history of Salzburgers who arrived in Georgia in March 1734 after fleeing religious persecution in Austria led by Baron von Firmian.
- Early Salzburgers built brush arbors, pavilions, and shacks on property called Ebenezer before constructing the Jerusalem Lutheran Church using clay and straw bricks, with work widely believed to have been done by women and children whose fingerprints remain visible.
- The brick church survived Revolutionary War and Civil War military occupations and has hosted continuous worship services since its completion.
- The society celebrates Salzburgers' history on Landing Day every March and maintains the Loest Research Library containing colonial-era primary sources like birth/marriage records, diaries, journals, and maps.
- The Georgia Salzburger Society reports several thousand members globally, with approximately 500 active participants.
