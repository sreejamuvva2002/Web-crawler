# Bosch

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [An industry under pressure: Bosch announces massive restructuring](https://www.newstarget.com/2025-09-26-an-industry-under-pressure-bosch-announces-massive-restructuring.html)  ·  `page_018092`

## Facts

- Bosch announced plans to cut a "five-digit number" of jobs, with reports specifying up to 13,000 positions.
- The job cuts are primarily within Bosch's automotive division.
- The restructuring stems from a "major cost-cutting exercise" aimed at addressing a €2.5 billion annual shortfall in its mobility unit.
- The planned layoffs affect about 10% of Bosch's German workforce.
- This follows the elimination of 4,500 jobs last year.
- Stefan Grosch serves as Bosch's HR director and head of industrial relations.
- Bosch's mobility division produces essential components like fuel injectors and driver-assistance software.
- Stefan Grosch stated: "Demand for our products is shifting significantly to regions outside Europe."
- Stefan Grosch added: "We need to orient ourselves to where our markets and customers are."
- Bosch stated it would be "cutting costs across the board – from materials and logistics to capital spending and jobs."
- The escalation of the Ukraine conflict in 2022 led Germany and other EU nations to pivot away from inexpensive Russian energy imports to costlier alternatives.
- This pivot dramatically increased production costs for energy-intensive manufacturing.
- A fierce price war in China is severely cutting into profit margins for both car manufacturers and their suppliers.
- Marco Zehe serves as head of electrified motion at Bosch.
- Marco Zehe acknowledged industry pressure, stating: "There is great price and competitive pressure on the entire automotive industry on both carmakers as well as their suppliers."
- A central challenge has been the slower-than-anticipated adoption of electric vehicles in Europe.
- German automakers and their suppliers invested billions in anticipation of a rapid EV shift, but consumer demand has not kept pace, leaving significant overcapacity.
- Marco Zehe said: "Electromobility has not taken off as quickly as forecast."
- Marco Zehe added: "That means we have lots of overcapacity, particularly in Europe and particularly in Germany."
- Carmakers are increasingly sourcing components locally rather than importing them from Germany as they expand production in key markets like North America and Asia.
- Markus Heyn serves as head of Bosch Mobility.
- Markus Heyn summarized the localization trend to the Stuttgarter Zeitung: "The trend towards localisation is unstoppable. The days when Germany could produce a great deal for the rest of the world are over."
- Volkswagen, Europe's largest automaker, saw its after-tax earnings slump by 36% in the second quarter.
- Volkswagen is planning its own extensive restructuring, potentially involving tens of thousands of job cuts.
- BMW reported a 29% year-on-year drop in first-half profits, citing U.S. import duties and intense "competitive pressure."
- According to the German Press Agency (dpa), Germany's industrial sector has lost more than 100,000 jobs over the past year.
- German Chancellor Friedrich Merz acknowledged that the country was “not just in a period of economic weakness, we are in a structural crisis of our economy,” caused by a loss of competitiveness.
- Frank Sell serves as head of the Bosch Mobility works council.
- Frank Sell vowed to fight the job cut plans, labeling them "historically unprecedented."
- Frank Sell criticized the company for offering no guarantees against site closures in Germany.
- Frank Sell warned: "Bosch is not only breaching the trust of those who have made this company big and successful but leaving behind social devastation in many regions."
- Stefan Grosch told reporters that Germany remained "central" to Bosch's future.
- Stefan Grosch stated: "We stand by it as a location and stand by Europe and are doing all we can to continually improve our competitiveness by our own efforts."
