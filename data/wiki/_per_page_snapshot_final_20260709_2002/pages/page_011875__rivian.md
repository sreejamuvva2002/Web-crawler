# Rivian

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Rivian trims DOE loan as Georgia plant gets bigger - forgeeks](https://forgeeks.dev/rivian-doe-loan-georgia-plant/)  ·  `page_011875`

## Facts

- Rivian now expects to borrow $4.5 billion from the Department of Energy for its Georgia plant, down from the $6.6 billion originally set aside.
- Rivian is raising the Georgia plant's initial capacity to 300,000 vehicles from 200,000.
- The Georgia plant will support production of the R2 SUV.
- The R2 SUV is also moving toward production at Rivian's Illinois factory in Normal.
- Rivian broke ground on the Georgia site outside Atlanta late last year.
- The Georgia plant is currently in early vertical construction.
- Vehicle production at the Georgia plant is slated to begin by the end of 2028.
- Some of the Georgia plant's capacity will be reserved for Uber-branded R2 robotaxis.
- Uber has agreed to make an initial $300 million investment in Rivian.
- Uber expects to buy 10,000 fully autonomous R2 robotaxis ahead of a planned 2028 rollout in San Francisco and Miami.
- The first Uber payment is expected to close in the second quarter.
- A second $250 million Uber investment is due later this year.
- Uber has an option to buy up to 40,000 more autonomous R2 SUVs starting in 2030.
- Uber could invest as much as $1.25 billion through 2031 if Rivian clears a set of milestones.
- Rivian recently started R2 production at its Illinois factory in Normal despite tornado damage at the plant.
- Rivian has made initial R2 deliveries to employees.
- Customer deliveries for the R2 SUV are expected to begin in the coming weeks.
- Rivian reported first-quarter 2026 revenue of $1.38 billion, including $908 million from vehicle sales and $473 million from software and services.
- Automotive revenue fell about 2% from a year earlier, partly due to a drop in regulatory credits.
- Rivian's net loss narrowed to $416 million in the first quarter of 2026, down from $541 million in the same period last year.
