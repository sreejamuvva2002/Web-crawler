# Aurubis AG

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2023-03-30

**Source:** [Press release: Enabling tomorrow - Aurubis releases new Sustainability Report | Presseportal](https://www.presseportal.de/en/pm/109932/5476092)  ·  `page_017349`

## Facts

- Aurubis is building a multimetal recycling plant in the US state of Georgia.
- The Georgia facility will be the country’s first plant for complex recycling materials.
- The processing capacity of the new Georgia plant will be 180,000 tons of input material per year.
- Aurubis is investing in new recycling technology at its two Belgian plants to keep strategically crucial, high demand raw materials in the cycle.
- A pilot plant for recycling lithium-ion batteries from electric cars went into operation at Aurubis's Hamburg site last year.
- Findings from the Hamburg pilot project showed that more than 95 percent of the materials contained in used batteries were recovered.
- Aurubis currently processes more than one million tons of recycling material annually.
- The recycled content in each copper cathode produced by Aurubis reaches almost 45 percent.
- The company is aiming for carbon-neutral production well before 2050.
- Aurubis has invested more than €780 million in environmental and climate protection since 2000, including €47 million in 2021 alone.
- The CO2 footprint of Aurubis's copper cathodes has fallen by 36 percent since 2013.
- All business partners undergo comprehensive Business Partner Screening based on OECD principles for responsible and sustainable supply chain due diligence.
- Aurubis plants in Hamburg, Lünen, and Pirdop were certified under 'The Copper Mark' initiative to verify the sustainability of copper production sites.
- The company aligned its 2023 Sustainability Report with the Global Reporting Initiative Standard (GRI) and the Task Force on Climate-related Financial Disclosure (TCFD).
- At its site in Pirdop, Bulgaria, Aurubis constructed an in-house solar park that powers the plant with 13,500 megawatt hours of electricity and avoids 22,400 tons of CO2 annually.
- The expansion target for the Pirdop solar park is a total of 24,000 MWh from solar power by 2024.
- Since 2023, Aurubis's plant in Olen, Belgium has drawn 90 percent of its energy needs from a Belgian offshore wind park.
- The Belgian offshore wind integration avoids 42,000 tons of CO2 annually.
- Preparations are underway for the second stage of the Industrial Heat project in Hamburg, which has been in operation since 2018.
- In cooperation with public utility Hamburger Energiewerke, up to 20,000 additional households will receive heat from the plant starting in the 2024/25 heating period.
- The expanded Industrial Heat project is the largest of its kind in Germany and will prevent an additional 100,000 tons of CO2 per year.
- A pilot project at Aurubis's Hamburg headquarters used blue ammonia as an energy source to advance the decarbonization of copper production.
