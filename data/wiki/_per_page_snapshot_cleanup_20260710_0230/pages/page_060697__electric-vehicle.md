# Electric vehicle

## Facts

- EVs encompass road vehicles, rail, boats, submersibles, aircraft, and spacecraft.
- Technological advances in lithium batteries since the late 20th century revived public interest as zero-emission vehicle options.
- Plug-in hybrid electric vehicles did not see mass production until the late 2000s, and battery electric cars became practical for consumers in the 2010s.
- As of 2026, China's EV industry produces more than the rest of the world combined.
- Government incentives are available in many areas to promote EV adoption to reduce tailpipe emissions and fossil fuel dependency.
- Over 20% of new cars sold in 2024 were electric only, while only 2% of trucks were.
- China accounted for more than 70% of global EV production and 67% of global sales in 2024.
- Most battery-electric vehicles use lithium-ion batteries due to higher energy density, longer lifespan, and higher power density compared to other practical batteries.
- Nickel-metal hydride batteries were used historically but are outdated due to self-discharge in heat and a Chevron patent that hindered development.
- Lithium-ion battery prices declined over the past decade, but critical mineral prices like lithium increased from 2021 to the end of 2022.
- Most battery-electric vehicles include a separate low-voltage auxiliary battery (typically 12V) that powers accessories and control electronics, recharged via a DC/DC converter.
- In 2024, Hyundai and Kia recalls cited failures of the Integrated Charging Control Unit (ICCU) that could stop charging the 12-volt battery and cause loss of drive power.
- Electric motors achieve approximately 90% energy conversion efficiency over their full range of speeds and power output.
- Tesla vehicles implement induction motors, while Nissan Leaf and Chevrolet Bolt use permanent magnet machines.
- AC charging stations supply grid power to the vehicle's onboard charger (OBC), which converts it to DC for battery recharging.
- DC chargers integrate the converter into the station to provide higher-power charging, bypassing the vehicle's onboard converter.
- GM is adding V2H (bidirectional charging) capability to 2024 Silverado and Blazer EV models, allowing vehicles to send power to homes during grid outages, with promises to continue through model year 2026.
- Battery swapping involves mechanically replacing batteries at special stations in minutes instead of recharging from sockets.
- One source estimates that over a fifth of the lithium and about 65% of the cobalt needed for electric cars will come from recycled sources by 2035.
- EVs release no tailpipe air pollutants and reduce respiratory illnesses like asthma, though their overall environmental impact depends on electricity generation sources and battery manufacturing/recycling impacts.
