# Novelis Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-07-04

**Source:** [Novelis to Start Hydrogen Burning Trials in UK Recycling Plant - H2 IQ](https://h2iq.org/novelis-to-start-hydrogen-burning-trials-in-uk-recycling-plant/)  ·  `page_047430`

## Facts

- Novelis Inc. is a leading sustainable aluminum solutions provider and a world leader in aluminum rolling and recycling.
- Novelis' Latchford plant in the UK was awarded £4.6 million to establish hydrogen burning trials.
- The award is part of the UK Government’s £55m Industrial Fuel Switching Competition.
- This competition is part of the £1bn Net Zero Innovation Portfolio (NZIP).
- The project is also part of the wider regional HyNet project.
- Novelis joined HyNet in 2017 and has been supporting the development of the regional infrastructure project.
- Novelis has been conducting its own technical feasibility studies on using hydrogen as a direct replacement for natural gas.
- Emilio Braghi, Executive Vice President of Novelis Inc. and President of Novelis Europe, stated that switching to renewable energy sources is a key initiative to advance toward carbon-neutral production.
- Braghi noted the collaboration drives industrial decarbonization of the whole North-West region in the UK.
- With the grant from the Department for Energy Security & Net Zero, Novelis’ Latchford plant will test hydrogen on one recycling furnace in a demonstration phase in 2024.
- Allan Sweeney, Plant Manager of Novelis Latchford, said they are pioneers using hydrogen within the aluminum industry and the trials will advance research on integrating hydrogen power in recycling operations globally.
- The trial is set up in collaboration with Progressive Energy, an independent UK energy company.
- The project requires installing new burners and regenerators capable of operating with hydrogen or a blended hydrogen/gas input.
- The furnace lining material will be replaced with one suitable for hydrogen.
- Depending on the final configuration, replacing natural gas with hydrogen to feed the remelting furnace could reduce CO2eq emissions by up to 90% compared to using the same amount of natural gas.
- Suzanne Lindsay-Walker, Vice President of Sustainability at Novelis Inc., stated decarbonizing melting processes is critical to achieving sustainability goals of reducing carbon footprint by 30% by 2026 and being carbon neutral by 2050 or sooner.
- Novelis’ research & development teams worldwide are investigating the ability to use plasma, electricity, and biomass to power manufacturing operations.
- Novelis Inc. is a global leader in the production of innovative aluminum products and solutions and the world’s largest recycler of aluminum.
- Novelis aims to be the leading provider of low-carbon, sustainable aluminum solutions and achieve a fully circular economy.
- Novelis partners with suppliers and customers in aerospace, automotive, beverage can, and specialties industries across North America, Europe, Asia, and South America.
- Novelis had net sales of $18.5 billion in fiscal year 2023.
- Novelis is a subsidiary of Hindalco Industries Limited, an industry leader in aluminum and copper.
- Hindalco Industries Limited is the metals flagship company of the Aditya Birla Group, a multinational conglomerate based in Mumbai.
- Novelis Latchford in Warrington, UK, is one of Europe’s largest aluminium-used beverage cans recycling plants.
- It is also Europe’s largest closed-loop recycling operation for automotive aluminum rolled products with an annual recycling capacity of up to 195,000 tonnes.
- The plant is an essential part of Novelis’ European production, which has enough capacity to recycle every aluminum beverage can sold in the UK.
- Novelis collaborated with Jaguar Land Rover (JLR) to create the first closed-loop system in Europe with a dedicated railway service to efficiently deliver aluminum between Novelis and JLR’s production site in the UK.
- Due to high economic integration and geographical proximity between the EU, UK, and Switzerland, Novelis operates across borders in well-established and efficient supply chains.
- This interconnectedness creates important economies of scale that benefit value chains across Europe.
- It enables Novelis to maximize the use of recycled inputs in line with its ambition to provide low-carbon, sustainable aluminum solutions to customers.
