## Facts

- Kentucky Gov. Andy Beshear announced further growth of the commonwealth’s rapidly growing manufacturing and electric vehicle (EV) support industries.
- Hitachi Astemo Americas Inc. is one of Kentucky’s largest Tier 1 automotive suppliers.
- Hitachi Astemo Americas Inc. announced an expansion of its operation in Madison County.
- The expansion involves a $153 million investment.
- The investment will create 167 new, quality Kentucky jobs.
- Gov. Andy Beshear stated that the unprecedented economic momentum in Kentucky is driven by growth from its largest industries.
- Gov. Beshear noted that manufacturing and the automotive sector have long been staples of Kentucky’s economy.
- The project will see company leaders invest in the current manufacturing operation in Berea to support site improvements and renovations.
- Improvements include increased production lines and additional equipment.
- The facility expansion will include an additional 752,000 square feet on 62 acres.
- The expansion comes in response to increased market demand and growth within the EV industry.
- Hitachi’s investment will create 167 full-time jobs for Kentuckians.
- The investment brings the company’s total Kentucky employment across the Harrodsburg and Berea facilities to over 2,100 workers.
- Tim Clark, president of Hitachi Astemo Americas, stated the company is approaching 40 years of manufacturing in Kentucky.
- The announcement represents a bold step in the direction of vehicle electrification.
- The new company name Hitachi Astemo stands for Advanced Sustainable Technology for Mobility.
- In January 2021, Hitachi Automotive Systems, Keihin, Showa and Nissin Kogyo completed a business merger to form the global mega-supplier Hitachi Astemo.
- The company provides advanced mobility solutions including electric powertrains for automobiles, advanced chassis, autonomous driving/advanced driver assistance systems, and systems for motorcycles.
- Hitachi Astemo is headquartered in Tokyo, Japan.
- The company’s entry into the Americas began in 1972 with a Hitachi subsidiary established in Brazil.
- In the mid-1980s, Hitachi expanded into the U.S. market with its first factory in Kentucky.
- The company serves as a global mega-supplier to some of the largest automobile manufacturers in the world.
- Hitachi Astemo has more than 20 manufacturing sites in the United States, Canada, Mexico and Brazil.
- The company operates two locations in Kentucky.
- Administration-to-date, Kentucky has seen nearly 600 private-sector new-location or expansion projects commit to invest over $22.9 billion.
- These Kentucky projects are expected to create more than 30,000 jobs.
- Madison County Judge/Executive Reagan Taylor expressed pleasure at seeing an existing company continue to grow and invest in the region.
- Berea Mayor Bruce Fraley noted the decision by Hitachi to continue investing in Madison County speaks to the talent of the local workforce and economic climate.
- Hitachi Astemo Americas is the largest employer in the city of Berea.
- Donna Angel, business development director for the Business and Economic Development Office of the city of Berea, highlighted the investment's impact on bringing quality jobs and economic sustainability to the community.
- Hitachi Astemo is at the forefront of developing the newest technology in parts for automotive and other EV engines.
- The new line of services will enhance the automotive industry in the region while highlighting Berea and its various community strengths.
