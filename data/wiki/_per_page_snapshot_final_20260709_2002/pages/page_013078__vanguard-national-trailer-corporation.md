# Vanguard National Trailer Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-06

**Source:** [Chinese investment gives wheelpower to small US town - Chinadaily.com.cn](http://global.chinadaily.com.cn/a/201711/08/WS5a0d2081a31061a738408e6e.html)  ·  `page_013078`

## Facts

- Vanguard National Trailer Corporation is a wholly owned subsidiary of Shenzhen-based China International Marine Containers (CIMC), a leading transportation equipment company.
- The Vanguard factory in Monon, Indiana, employs over 400 people and contributes approximately 80 percent to the local gross domestic product.
- Vanguard entered Monon in 2003 with new equipment, investment, and management after HPA Monon closed in May 2002.
- The Vanguard factory occupies an area half the size of the town and contains lines of diverse semitrailers.
- The two semitrailer companies in White County, Vanguard and Wabash, together produce most of the trailers in the United States.
- Vanguard CEO Charlie Mudd states that hundreds of jobs were created in Monon thanks to the CIMC investment.
- Mudd believes the Chinese investment brings wider development space and market access, enabling global sharing of ideas, information, resources, suppliers, and strategy across CIMC vehicle group companies.
- Vanguard is expanding by opening another factory in Trenton, Georgia, creating about 200 jobs.
- Charlie Mudd describes the expansion as a combination of Chinese knowledge and capabilities paired with American knowledge and opportunities, calling it "true collaboration."
- Manufacturing director James Wilkerson notes that despite being Chinese-owned, the company is American-run and workers have felt little difference since the acquisition.
- Vanguard produces semitrailers from iron sheets and frames designed to haul freight.
