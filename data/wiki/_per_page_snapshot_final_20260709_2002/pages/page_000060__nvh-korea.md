# NVH Korea

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-06-22

**Source:** [NVH Korea to Create Over 160 Jobs, Invest $72M in Henry County | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-releases/2023/ev-parts-supplier-nvh-korea-create-over-160-jobs-invest-72m-henry-county)  ·  `page_000060`

## Facts

- June 22, 2023 – Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that automotive parts manufacturer NVH Korea will establish a new facility in Locust Grove.
- The project will create over 160 new jobs and bring $72 million in investment to Henry County.
- The announcement was made while Governor Kemp was on an economic development trip to the Paris Air Show in France.
- This is the second economic development project Governor Kemp has announced that week.
- NVH Korea is an automotive parts manufacturer based in the City of Ulsan, established in 1984.
- The company specializes in automotive noise, vibration, and heat-control parts, currently produced in Korea, Europe, and India.
- The company already operates a manufacturing facility in Georgia that produces floor mats, cargo mats, and cargo trays.
- The new facility will supply battery parts for electric vehicles (EVs) in the United States for manufacturers such as Hyundai Mobis, Kia Georgia, and Hyundai Motor Group.
- Ja Kyum Koo, Chairman and CEO of NVH Korea, stated that Georgia was chosen as the company’s first EV battery component manufacturing site.
- The new facility will be located at Gardner Logistics Park on Colvin Drive in Locust Grove.
- The facility will produce EV battery system components that protect, connect, and sense the performance of battery cells.
- Operations are expected to begin in the second quarter of 2024.
- Mayor Robert Price of the City of Locust Grove stated that NVH’s investment will increase manufacturing jobs and make the city part of Georgia's electric vehicle ecosystem.
- Carlotta Harrell, Chair of the Henry County Board of Commissioners, stated that the announcement will add quality manufacturing jobs for citizens and diversify the local economy.
- Project Manager Jacob Lee represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project in partnership with the Henry County Development Authority, Georgia EMC, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that NVH’s investment will increase manufacturing jobs, welcome innovators establishing their first U.S. or e-mobility manufacturing presence in Georgia, and enrich the local industry mix.
- From July 1 through December 31, 2022, the automotive industry was the second-largest source of new jobs and investment in Georgia.
- Since 2020, Georgia has pursued job creation along the entire EV supply chain, resulting in more than $22.7 billion in investments and the creation of over 28,400 jobs.
