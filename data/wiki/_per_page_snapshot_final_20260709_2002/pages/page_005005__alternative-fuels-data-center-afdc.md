# Alternative Fuels Data Center (AFDC)

## Facts

- The page provides approximate light-duty vehicle registration counts derived by the National Laboratory of the Rockies with data from Experian Information Solutions.
- Counts are rounded to the closest 100 vehicles and reflect the total number of light-duty registered vehicles through the selected year (2023).
- Fuel types are based on vehicle identification numbers (VINs), which do not reflect aftermarket conversions to use different fuels or power sources.
- Georgia had approximately 92,400 registered electric vehicles (EVs) in 2023.
- Georgia had approximately 22,700 registered plug-in hybrid electric vehicles (PHEVs) in 2023.
- Georgia had approximately 175,300 registered hybrid electric vehicles (HEVs) in 2023.
- Georgia had approximately 92,500 registered biodiesel vehicles in 2023.
- Georgia had approximately 719,400 registered ethanol/flex (E85) vehicles in 2023.
- Georgia had approximately 300 registered compressed natural gas (CNG) vehicles in 2023.
- Georgia had approximately 200 registered propane vehicles in 2023.
- Georgia had zero registered hydrogen and methanol vehicles in 2023.
- Georgia had approximately 8,256,400 registered gasoline vehicles in 2023.
- Georgia had approximately 206,300 registered diesel vehicles in 2023.
- Georgia had approximately 76,900 registered vehicles with unknown fuel types in 2023.
- For a map comparing the density of light-duty vehicles per state and charts comparing registration counts over time, users can refer to TransAtlas.
