# Georgia Power Company

**Type:** Company  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[unknown|Uncategorized]]  ·  **Location:** Georgia, United States

## Overview

Georgia Power announced a series of new generation and battery energy storage projects in Floyd and Bartow counties, including the Hammond BESS (57.5 MW), Bowen BESS Phases 1 & 2 (total 500 MW), two combined-cycle natural gas units (1,482 MW) and additional statewide BESS certifications, to improve grid reliability and transition toward cleaner energy.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Battery storage projects support grid reliability for electric vehicles and the broader EV ecosystem in Georgia.

- **Hammond BESS** — Standalone Battery Energy Storage System built on the site of the retired Plant Hammond, repurposing transmission capacity for grid support.
- **Bowen BESS Phase 1** — Phase 1 of the Bowen battery storage project; together with Phase 2 will total 500 MW.
- **Bowen BESS Phase 2** — Phase 2 of the Bowen battery storage project; together with Phase 1 will total 500 MW.
- **Combined-cycle Natural Gas Plant Units** — Two new combined-cycle natural gas units proposed in PSC filings to diversify the state’s energy mix.
- **Plant Hammond (retired)** — Former coal-fired plant shut down in 2019; site now used for the Hammond BESS.
- **Plant Bowen** — One of the dirtiest coal plants in the nation; regulators will revisit its future by 2025.

## Timeline

- **2019** — Plant Hammond retired (800 MW coal plant shut down).
- **November 2026** — Expected commercial operation of the Hammond BESS.
- **2025** — Regulators will revisit Plant Bowen’s future retirement timeline.

## Related organizations & people

- Georgia Public Service Commission — Regulatory agency. Received Georgia Power’s filings requesting certification for additional BESS facilities.

## Key facts

- Georgia Power is investing in battery energy storage systems to transition toward cleaner, more reliable energy sources.
- The company plans a 57.5 MW BESS at the former Plant Hammond site in Floyd County and 500 MW of battery storage (Bowen Phases 1 & 2) in Bartow County.
- Two combined-cycle natural gas units totaling 1,482 MW are proposed in Bartow County to diversify the energy mix.
- Georgia Power requested certification for ten additional BESS facilities statewide, representing about 3,022.5 MW of capacity.
- The utility projects approximately 8,200 MW of electrical load growth by 2030, with peak demand expected to rise by 2,200 MW versus its 2023 forecast.
- These investments aim to retire outdated coal plants, ensure grid reliability, support Georgia’s economic growth, and transition the state’s energy mix toward cleaner technologies.
