# Ascend Elements

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Ascend Elements bags $480 million US DoE grant to make sustainable battery CAM -](https://etn.news/buzz/ascend-elements-battery-cathode-active-materials-grant-us)  ·  `page_060511`

## Facts

- American battery materials firm Ascend Elements has been awarded grants from the U.S. Department of Energy's Bipartisan Infrastructure Law (BIL) Battery Materials Processing and Battery Manufacturing Initiative.
- The awards total $480 million in matching funds.
- The funding will support construction of the 'Apex' manufacturing facility in southwest Kentucky.
- The project establishes lithium-ion battery supply chain infrastructure that previously did not exist in the United States.
- The planned facility will utilize the company's patented 'Hydro-to-Cathode' technology.
- The technology produces sustainable cathode precursors (pCAM) and cathode active materials (CAM) from spent lithium-ion batteries.
- CEO Mike O'Kronley stated the awards are transformational for the U.S. lithium-ion battery industry.
- The Apex facility will be the first and largest battery material production facility of its kind in the U.S.
- The facility aims to improve security of supply of critical materials for the domestic electric vehicle industry.
- The project aims to improve the United States' competitiveness in the lithium-ion battery industry on a global scale.
- CEO Mike O'Kronley emphasized that the importance of a closed loop lithium-ion battery supply chain cannot be understated.
- The grant accelerates achievement of that closed-loop supply chain.
- CEO Mike O'Kronley noted the $480 million DOE grant was over 2x the next largest grant.
- Construction at the 140-acre site in Hopkinsville is expected to begin this month.
- Once completed, Apex will produce enough material annually to power over 250,000 electric vehicles.
- The project will generate approximately $4.4 billion in total economic impact during its construction phase and the first ten years of operation.
- The project will create over 400 jobs in the Hopkinsville area.
- The Apex project will seek to establish community benefits programs including job training and workforce development, affordable childcare, and affordable transportation initiatives.
