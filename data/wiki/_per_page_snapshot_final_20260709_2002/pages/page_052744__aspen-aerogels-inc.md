# Aspen Aerogels Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2023-09-30

**Source:** [Aspen Aerogels Inc. Reports Strong Q3 Performance with Significant Revenue Growth — TradingView News](https://www.tradingview.com/news/tradingview:7b9b68bd38cc5:0-aspen-aerogels-inc-reports-strong-q3-performance-with-significant-revenue-growth/)  ·  `page_052744`

## Facts

- Aspen Aerogels Inc. is a leading provider of high-performance aerogel insulation for energy, industrial, and electric vehicle (EV) markets.
- The company released its Form 10-Q report for the third quarter of 2023.
- Total revenue was $329.6 million, representing an increase of 113% from $154.5 million for the nine months ended September 30, 2023.
- Revenue growth was driven by accelerating demand in the EV market and continued market share gains in the sustainable insulation materials market.
- Gross profit was $135.8 million, a significant increase from $27.3 million for the nine months ended September 30, 2023.
- Gross profit improvement reflected improved manufacturing and fabrication yields and a favorable mix of products sold.
- Income from operations was $39.8 million, compared to a loss of $50.6 million for the nine months ended September 30, 2023.
- Net income was $2.0 million, compared to a net loss of $45.3 million for the nine months ended September 30, 2023.
- Net income per share was $0.03, compared to a net loss per share of $0.65 for the nine months ended September 30, 2023.
- The company operates in two main segments: Energy Industrial and Thermal Barrier.
- The Energy Industrial segment focuses on high-performance aerogel insulation for energy and industrial markets.
- The Thermal Barrier segment targets the electric vehicle market with products like PyroThin® aerogel thermal barriers designed for battery packs in EVs.
- Revenue from the Thermal Barrier segment has shown significant growth driven by demand in the EV market.
- Major contracts for Thermal Barrier products are held with OEMs like General Motors, Toyota, and Audi.
- The Energy Industrial segment serves oil producers and operators of refineries and petrochemical plants with products like Pyrogel® and Cryogel® aerogel blankets.
- In the U.S., revenue was strong driven by demand for thermal barriers in the EV market.
- Internationally, significant revenue contributions came from Latin America, Europe, Canada, and Asia.
- Latin America showed a notable increase in revenue due to higher sales of thermal barriers.
- The company is actively developing new aerogel products for the EV market, including PyroThin® thermal barriers designed to enhance battery safety in electric vehicles.
- These products are expected to drive future growth as they gain traction with major automotive OEMs.
- The company is expanding its manufacturing capacity with a new plant in Bulloch County, Georgia, to meet growing demand for its aerogel products.
- This Georgia facility will support increased production volumes for both the Energy Industrial and Thermal Barrier segments.
- The company anticipates continued strong revenue growth driven by demand in the EV market.
- It plans to enhance manufacturing capabilities, improve product formulations, and leverage external manufacturing facilities to meet this demand.
- The company expects to benefit from a conditional commitment from the U.S. Department of Energy for a loan to finance its new manufacturing facility.
