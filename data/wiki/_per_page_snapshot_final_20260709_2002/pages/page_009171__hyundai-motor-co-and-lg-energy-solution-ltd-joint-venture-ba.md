# Hyundai Motor Co. and LG Energy Solution Ltd. Joint Venture Battery Facility

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [S.Korea rattled by detention of hundreds of workers at Hyundai-LG battery JV in sweeping US raid - KED Global](https://www.kedglobal.com/automobiles/newsView/ked202509050006)  ·  `page_009171`

## Facts

- Several hundred workers at the electric vehicle battery joint venture between Hyundai Motor Co. and LG Energy Solution Ltd., currently under construction in Georgia, were detained in a major US authority raid.
- The Thursday raid dealt a setback to the project, which is part of what would be the biggest investments in Georgia.
- The incident illustrates the increasing crackdown by the Trump administration on immigrants and its impact on businesses.
- Analysts and corporate executives in Seoul fear the US crackdown could disrupt other multibillion-dollar semiconductor and battery manufacturing projects, potentially hurting US ambitions to tighten its grip on global supply chains.
- Nearly 500 employees, including LG Energy staff, subcontractors, and engineers from Hyundai Engineering, were taken into custody on Thursday morning by ICE and HSI agents.
- The raid also involved the DEA and Georgia state troopers, with armed officers sealing off the site in Ellabell, Bryan County, west of Savannah, using helicopters and armored vehicles.
- One eyewitness described the scene as "like a military operation."
- The 50-50 joint venture between Hyundai and LG is considered one of Georgia's largest and most high-profile manufacturing sites.
- The detained employees are alleged to have worked on the site without proper work permits, having entered the US under ESTA or short-term B1 business visas.
- The $4.3 billion joint venture battery facility is among the largest projects in Georgia and is expected to create 8,500 jobs.
- The facility is scheduled for completion later this year to supply battery cells to Hyundai’s nearby EV factory, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America LLC (HMGMA).
- To prepare the site for commercial operation, LG and its partners dispatched hundreds of engineers on short rotations, many using 90-day ESTA allowances.
- Industry executives warned that the custody of the workers could disrupt the plant's commissioning and affect other Korean projects in the US.
- Construction work on the battery joint venture facility was suspended following the raid.
- Korean conglomerates including Samsung Electronics Co., SK Hynix Inc., Samsung SDI Co., and SK On Co. are heavily investing in US chip and battery facilities with partners like GM and Ford.
- Several of these Korean-invested US manufacturing sites rely on rotating teams of Korean engineers.
- An industry executive stated, "This puts Korean workers in a precarious situation."
- Washington wants rapid onshoring of advanced manufacturing facilities but refuses to provide necessary visa allocations that would allow Korean specialists to transfer their know-how.
- Industry officials said Seoul needs to secure a dedicated visa quota with Washington, similar to arrangements the US has with Australia and Singapore.
- A corporate executive in Seoul stated, "We are investing trillions of won in the US, but getting no visa support."
- The Korean government raised concerns over the detentions, calling the incident "regrettable."
- Seoul's Ministry of Foreign Affairs stated that the economic activities of Korean companies investing in the US and the rights and interests of their nationals must not be unfairly violated.
- A Hyundai official said the company is cooperating with US law enforcement and is committed to abiding by all labor and immigration regulations.
- An executive at the Hyundai-LG battery joint venture said the company is cooperating fully with authorities regarding activity at the construction site and has paused construction to assist them.
- Sources said the Korean consulate in Atlanta is forming a legal team to handle the matter.
