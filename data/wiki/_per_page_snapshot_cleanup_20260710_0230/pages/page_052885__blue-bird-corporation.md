# Blue Bird Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Investment analysis of BLUE BIRD CORP | Freedom24](https://freedom24.com/ideas/details/16209)  ·  `page_052885`

## Facts

- Blue Bird Corporation is headquartered in Macon, Georgia, USA.
- The company operates a manufacturing and assembly facility in Fort Valley, Georgia.
- Blue Bird plans to build a new 600,000 square-foot Type D and EV production facility in Fort Valley, Georgia.
- The company was awarded an $80 million MESC grant by the Department of Energy (DOE) to develop the new Fort Valley facility.
- The total investment for the new Fort Valley plant is $160 million, with Blue Bird funding 50% of the costs.
- Construction of the new Georgia facility is expected to take about two years, with a launch targeted for the end of 2026 or early 2027.
- Final negotiations with the DOE regarding the grant are expected to conclude by December 2024.
- Once operational, the new plant will increase Blue Bird's total production capacity to approximately 14,000 buses per year.
- The new facility is expected to deliver an internal rate of return (IRR) of 28% with a payback period of two years post-launch.
- Blue Bird aims to increase electric school bus sales to 1,150 units by FY2025.
- Blue Bird targets annual electric school bus sales of 5,000 units in the period after FY2027.
- Currently, more than 60% of Blue Bird's unit sales are non-diesel.
- The company formed Clean Bus Solutions, a 50/50 joint venture with Generate Capital, to accelerate electric school bus adoption.
- The Clean Bus Solutions JV offers customers a monthly service fee covering the Blue Bird electric bus fleet and charging infrastructure.
- As of June 2024, commitments for electric school buses in the US reached 12,164 units.
- The EPA's Clean School Bus Program provided over 67% of all committed electric school buses in the US.
- In 2022, the EPA program allocated $900 million to fund nearly 2,300 electric buses across 365 school districts.
- A second funding round in January 2024 added funding for 2,700 electric buses in 270 districts.
- A third funding round awarded $900 million to support the replacement of diesel buses with 3,177 electric buses in nearly 500 districts.
- The Bipartisan Infrastructure Law of 2021 authorizes the EPA to provide rebates and grants for replacing school buses with clean or zero-emission models, securing $5 billion over five years.
- The 2023 EPA CSB Round 3 Rebate Program allocated $940 million for about 3,600 buses.
- The 2024 Clean Heavy-Duty Vehicles Program, funded through the Inflation Reduction Act, will provide around $650 million for school buses.
- Blue Bird expects to secure around 30% of orders for Type A, C, and D electric buses from federal funding initiatives.
- Electric school bus commitments now span 49 states.
- Approximately 200,000 students nationwide currently benefit from electric school bus transportation.
- In Q3 FY2024, Blue Bird delivered over 200 electric buses, marking a nearly 40% year-over-year increase.
- Strong demand from the EPA’s Clean School Bus Program drove a backlog of over 560 EV orders as of Q3 FY2024.
- Buses funded by Phase 1 of the $5 billion program need to be delivered by the end of 2026.
- Blue Bird manufactures Type C, Type D, and specialty school buses with passenger capacities ranging from 10 to 90.
- The company's bus models offer various body styles and engine options, including alternative power and electric configurations.
- Blue Bird generates approximately 93% of its sales in the US, ~7% in Canada, and less than 1% in the rest of the world.
- Three major manufacturers dominate the US school bus industry: Blue Bird Corporation, IC Bus LLC, and Thomas Built Buses Inc., each holding roughly a 30% market share.
- Lion Electric Co. operates as a niche manufacturer of zero-emission vehicles with approximately a 5% market share.
- The company relies on specialist suppliers, some single-source, for critical components, creating supply chain vulnerability.
- Blue Bird imports some components from China and other foreign countries, which may be subject to US trade policies and tariffs.
