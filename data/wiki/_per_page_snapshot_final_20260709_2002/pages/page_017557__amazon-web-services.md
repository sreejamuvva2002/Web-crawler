# Amazon Web Services

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2025-01-08

**Source:** [AWS reveals $11bn data center investment plan for Georgia - DCD](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/aws-reveals-11bn-data-center-investment-plan-for-georgia/)  ·  `page_017557`

## Facts

- AWS plans to invest around $11 billion in data centers in Georgia.
- The infrastructure investment will be used to support growing demand for cloud computing and artificial intelligence (AI) technologies.
- The investment is expected to create at least 550 new jobs.
- Precise details of how the money will be used were not shared.
- Roger Wehner, vice president of economic development at AWS, stated the company is delighted to expand its infrastructure into Georgia with this planned multibillion-dollar investment, expecting to create more than 550 well-paying jobs and drive significant economic growth for the state.
- Wehner noted that state and local leaders have cultivated an environment enabling companies like AWS to make bold, forward-looking investments.
- Amazon, AWS' parent company, has invested $18.5 billion in Georgia since 2010.
- The newly announced investment will be concentrated in Butts and Douglas Counties.
- Dr. Romona Jackson Jones, Douglas County Commission chairwoman, called the news a historic day for the county and welcomed AWS bringing secure, resilient, advanced cloud computing technology to bring hundreds of high-paying, high-tech jobs benefiting the community long-term.
- Russ Crumbley, chairman of the Butts County Board of Commissioners, stated the investment will be the most significant in Butts County history and represents a collaborative effort by the county board, Development Authority of Butts County, Butts County Water and Sewer Authority, and Butts County School System to ensure infrastructure improvements benefit citizens for decades.
- In October 2024, AWS purchased 118 acres in Douglas County, just outside Atlanta, for $37 million.
- This was the second land acquisition in Georgia that year; Amazon also bought around 430 acres in Covington, Newton County, about 40 miles east of Atlanta, at the turn of the year.
- Covington is located north of Butts County; Amazon declined to comment if the Covington site would be used for a data center at the time of purchase.
- In February 2024, AWS launched a Local Zone in Atlanta with upgraded compute infrastructure options.
- Local Zones act as edge locations to host applications requiring low latency to end-users or on-premises installations, often in locations where AWS does not have a cloud region.
- Microsoft is building several data centers in the Atlanta area, including East Point and Palmetto in Fulton County, and Douglasville in Douglas County, having announced plans for a Georgia cloud region in 2021.
- Google has existing data centers in Georgia but does not have a cloud region in the state.
- Several major data center developments are underway in the Atlanta region by companies including Stream, Stack, DC Blox, and TA Realty.
- A September report from JLL named Atlanta as the top market for colocation capacity under construction.
