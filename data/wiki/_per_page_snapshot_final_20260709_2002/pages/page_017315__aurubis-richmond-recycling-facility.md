# Aurubis Richmond Recycling Facility

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- Aurubis AG launched a groundbreaking metal recycling facility in Richmond, Georgia.
- The facility represents an $800 million investment.
- It is Europe's largest copper producer's first major US copper investment on American soil.
- The facility is designed to process up to 180,000 metric tons of complex recycling materials annually.
- It will transform electronic waste, copper cables, and other metal-bearing products into valuable materials.
- The plant will produce 70,000 tons of high-grade blister copper with 98-99% purity.
- The facility employs sophisticated processing technology to recover nickel, tin, and precious metals from waste streams.
- Production at the facility began in September 2025.
- Full operational capacity is expected by the first half of 2026.
- When fully operational, the plant will be one of the most advanced metal recycling facilities in North America.
- US domestic copper demand reaches approximately 1.8 million tons annually.
- Roughly 50% of US copper demand is imported from international sources.
- The facility addresses vulnerabilities by establishing a reliable domestic source of recycled copper for industries ranging from electronics manufacturing to renewable energy development.
- Copper's strategic importance continues to grow across defense and military applications, renewable energy infrastructure, electric vehicle manufacturing, and critical telecommunications and grid infrastructure.
- Recycling complex waste materials domestically keeps valuable resources within the US economy while reducing reliance on international supply chains subject to geopolitical tensions or disruptions.
- The Richmond facility's 70,000-ton annual production capacity will offset approximately 7-8% of current US copper imports when operating at full capacity.
- Electric vehicle production consumes 2-4 times more copper than conventional vehicles.
- Aurubis has explicitly stated intentions to prioritize US customers for the copper produced at Richmond.
- The facility's operational flexibility allows for direct sales to US customers at competitive market rates, further processing through Aurubis' international smelting network, and integration into value-added product manufacturing.
- The facility recovers nickel, which is critical for batteries, stainless steel, and aerospace applications.
- The facility recovers tin, which is essential for electronics manufacturing and soldering.
- The facility recovers precious metals including gold, silver, and platinum group metals with high value-to-weight ratios.
- Electronic waste is processed via thermal processing and separation to yield high-grade blister copper.
- Copper cables are processed via mechanical processing and smelting to yield nickel, tin, and precious metals.
- Metal-bearing products are processed via advanced sorting and metallurgical recovery to yield refined metals for industrial applications.
- The facility creates immediate construction jobs and long-term industrial positions.
- The facility creates high-skill technical positions in metallurgical processing.
- The facility creates supporting logistics and administrative roles.
- The facility creates indirect employment through supplier relationships.
- Copper recycling typically requires up to 85% less energy than primary production from mined ores.
- Copper recycling significantly reduces greenhouse gas emissions per ton of metal produced, water usage, land disruption, and waste entering landfills.
- Aurubis CEO Toralf Haag stated the company sees significant potential for additional projects in the United States.
- Industry analysts note the Richmond facility may represent just the first phase in a broader North American strategy for the European metals giant.
- Aurubis CEO Toralf Haag said: "With the high availability of recycling material and good local conditions, the American market offers Aurubis appealing prospects that we will continue to consider closely in the future as well."
- Factors making the US market attractive for continued metal recycling expansion include growing waste streams, policy support for domestic manufacturing and supply chain security, a strong manufacturing base requiring reliable metal inputs, and well-developed transportation networks and utility systems.
- The facility incorporates highly automated sorting and processing systems, sophisticated metallurgical recovery techniques, advanced material handling capabilities, and efficient energy management and recovery systems.
- The Richmond facility demonstrates scale efficiency, multi-metal focus, integration capabilities connected to international processing networks, and strategic positioning to serve growing North American demand.
- The facility establishes new standards for metal recycling in North America that will likely influence industry practices for complex material processing, regulatory approaches to waste management and recycling, investment patterns in circular economy infrastructure, and manufacturer engagement with recycled material streams.
- The facility helps address critical material availability concerns for clean energy technologies, advanced electronics manufacturing, defense applications, and transportation electrification.
- The facility will process up to 180,000 metric tons of complex recycling materials annually, including printed circuit boards, copper cables, and various metal-bearing products containing valuable recoverable metals from consumer and industrial sources.
