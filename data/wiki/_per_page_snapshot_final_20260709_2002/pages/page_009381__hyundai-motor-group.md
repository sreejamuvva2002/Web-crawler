# Hyundai Motor Group

## Facts

- Autos Drive America, the leading trade association representing international automakers with operations in the U.S., celebrated Hyundai Day in Georgia.
- The Georgia Assembly recently signed a resolution commending Hyundai Motor Group’s dedication to the state.
- Governor Kemp recognized Monday, February 26th as Hyundai Day.
- Jennifer Safavian is President and CEO of Autos Drive America.
- Hyundai Motor Group announced a more than $7.5 billion investment in 2022 to develop its Metaplant America.
- The Metaplant America will create 8,500 direct jobs.
- The Metaplant America will assemble six models across Kia, Hyundai, and Genesis.
- Hyundai and SK On are building a $5 billion EV battery cell plant in Bartow County, Georgia.
- The Hyundai and SK On EV battery cell plant will employ 3,500 workers.
- Hyundai Motor Group announced that its Metaplant will open three months ahead of schedule.
- The Metaplant is slated to begin production later this year.
- According to the Autos Drive America Economic Impact Report, ten international automakers have a presence in Georgia: Hyundai, Kia, BMW, Honda, Mazda, Mercedes-Benz, Mitsubishi, Nissan, Subaru, and Volkswagen.
- In 2022, international automakers, dealers, and suppliers generated 67,906 jobs in Georgia.
- In 2022, these entities paid $4.9 billion in total employee compensation.
- International automakers invested $2.8 billion in Georgia’s auto industry.
- Investments by international automakers in Georgia are growing to produce electric vehicles in the state.
- In 2022, international automakers in Georgia produced 340,000 vehicles.
- The 340,000 vehicles produced accounted for 100% of production in the state.
- International automakers contributed $850 million in state and local revenues.
- International automakers contributed $8.6 billion to Georgia's Gross State Product.
- Governor Kemp and Georgia lawmakers share a commitment to growing the U.S. automotive industry, manufacturing more vehicles on U.S. soil, and creating career opportunities for the U.S. workforce.
