# U.S. Department of Energy Hydrogen Program

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2025-05-22

**Source:** [Program Records | Hydrogen Program](https://www.hydrogen.energy.gov/home/program-records)  ·  `page_006052`

## Facts

- The U.S. Department of Energy Hydrogen Program is led by the Hydrogen and Fuel Cell Technologies Office (HFTO) within the Office of Energy Efficiency and Renewable Energy (EERE).
- The program conducts research and development in hydrogen production, delivery, infrastructure, storage, fuel cells, and multiple end uses across transportation, industrial, and stationary power applications.
- The program includes activities in technology validation, manufacturing, analysis, systems development and integration, safety, codes and standards, education, and workforce development.
- On May 22, 2025, the Energy Department announced updates to remove barriers for American energy producers and unleash investment in domestic hydrogen.
- On April 16, 2025, an H2IQ Hour webinar was held on geologic hydrogen, covering its energy potential and current engineering efforts to bring it from underground to the surface.
- On December 20, 2024, the U.S. Department of Energy unveiled an updated Hydrogen Program Plan that incorporates new input from multiple DOE offices as a strategic framework for hydrogen innovation.
