# Humiscope

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- Humiscope is an Australian owned and operated company that specialises in controlling indoor environments through energy-efficient solutions.
- According to the latest statistics from the Australian Bureau of Statistics, only 14% of engineers in Australia are women.
- In 2020, 22% of bachelor’s degree students in engineering were women, which suggests a significant drop-off in the number of women pursuing engineering careers after graduation.
- Humiscope champions the importance of recognising women in the field of engineering and wants to showcase the three female engineers on its team.
- Ladan Bage is a mechanical engineer with a Masters of Science in Mechanical Engineering who works on several large projects for Humiscope.
- Ladan Bage offers practical mechanical designs while always considering a client’s requirements and specialises in drafting, documentation, mechanical design, reverse engineering, manufacturing, and its supervision.
- Paula Andrea Gil is a drafts(wo)man with a Bachelor’s Degree in Architectural Drafting and Design and over six years’ hands-on experience.
- Paula Andrea Gil works closely with Ladan Bage and is highly skilled in BIM modelling to create digital representations of the physical and functional characteristics of spaces Humiscope designs for clients.
- Melissa Le Roux is the Lead Designer at Humiscope and has been with the company since it opened its doors in 2015.
- Melissa Le Roux has led projects throughout Australia and its neighbouring islands with clients from a diverse range of industries.
- Melissa Le Roux advocates for sharing knowledge to better inform clients, stating that educating clients on solutions and design rationale is key to delivering successful projects.
- The Humiscope team is currently working on a significant project in lithium-ion battery production, a field relatively new within the Australian industrial sector.
- Humiscope was contracted to provide a turnkey solution for a large lithium-ion manufacturing facility.
- The contract covers conceptual designs and complete build of low dew point, ultra-low humidity, ultra-dry production rooms.
- Ladan Bage has engineering experience in the R&D sector, manufacturing, HVAC and construction sites and is excited to learn within the emerging lithium-ion market.
- Ladan Bage noted that designing dry rooms for lithium-ion battery production is a relatively new field where design can be challenging, requiring more research and optimisation to get the best results.
- Paula Andrea Gil found projects involving low-dew point solutions enriching and enjoys seeing designs become reality through client consultation and 3D modelling.
- Humiscope actively recruits and promotes women in engineering, with two thirds of its workforce being women across operational management, marketing, and administration.
- Diverse teams are known to be more innovative and creative, which can lead to better outcomes for businesses and society as a whole.
