# Jackson Alliance for Economic Development

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2021-12

**Source:** [Jackson Alliance helping to establish Georgia as advanced manufacturing hub - AI Online -](https://www.ai-online.com/2021/12/jackson-alliance-helping-to-establish-georgia-as-advanced-manufacturing-hub/)  ·  `page_001691`

## Facts

- Georgia is emerging as a national leader in advanced manufacturing thanks to investment attracted through strong international relationships.
- The Jackson Alliance for Economic Development is the central economic development organization for Jackson County, Georgia.
- Organized under the Jackson County Area Chamber of Commerce, the Alliance has been successful in attracting investment.
- Duckyang, a supplier of automotive battery modules and energy storage systems to SK Battery America (SKBA), announced it would invest US$10 million in its first US manufacturing facility in Jackson County.
- South Korean companies are major investors in the state of Georgia.
- Government figures put total investments from South Korean firms in 2020 at US$1.3 billion.
- South Korean firms created over 2,700 jobs in 2020.
- In June 2020, SK Innovation, a developer and manufacturer of lithium-ion batteries for electric vehicles (EVs), increased its investment in its Georgia manufacturing plant by US$940 million.
- In 2019, SK Innovation invested US$1.67 billion in a manufacturing plant in the state.
- With the new investment, total job creation by SK stands at 2,600.
- Jun Kim, CEO of SK Innovation, stated: “SK innovation’s solid partnership with Georgia will allow SK to continue investing in Georgia, the U.S. economy, and the U.S. electric vehicle industry.”
- South Enchem Ltd is spending US$61 million to build two new manufacturing plants in Jackson County.
- Enchem specializes in the development and manufacturing of electrolytes for rechargeable batteries and electrostatic double-layer capacitors (ELDCs).
- Enchem was the first supplier to follow SK Innovation, which broke ground in Jackson County in March 2019 on a US$1.67 billion battery manufacturing plant.
- Since its founding in 2012, Enchem has developed multiple new electrolytes and functional additives to improve and extend battery life.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated: “I am excited to welcome Enchem to Georgia.”
- Governor Brian Kemp stated: “Building on the success of SK Innovation in Jackson County, this is exactly the type of quality project that we hoped to attract on our first international trip in June 2020. As the market for electric vehicles and batteries continues its rapid growth, it’s great to see the industry building a hub here in Georgia. Our strong international partnerships – like the one we have established between South Korea and Georgia – will continue to benefit hardworking Georgians and create opportunities throughout the state in the years ahead.”
- Toyota Industries Compressor Parts America (TICA) announced in October 2020 that it would expand its Pendergrass, Georgia, production facility to meet growing demand for onboard power electronics.
- Toyota is investing US$5.5 million in a project that will create at least 27 new jobs in Jackson County over the next 18 months.
- Toyota set up the TICA facility in 2012 with an initial investment of US$350 million to supply automotive compressors for Toyota Motor Corporation, Chrysler, Ford, General Motors, and others.
- Toyota Industries Compressor Parts works in concert with the adjacent TD Automotive Compressor Georgia facility, which assembles parts manufactured at the TICA plant.
- The combined facilities encompass over one million square feet of production space and a total investment of more than US$1 billion.
- Kota Otoshi, executive officer for the electronics division of TICA’s parent company Toyota Industries Corporation, stated: “We are very pleased to join TICA’s operations in Georgia... We would like to expand our electrical products from this plant and our onboard power electronics to our market in North America. We look forward to being a corporate citizen who can contribute to the economic and social development of Georgia through the production and sale of our onboard power electronics.”
- In 2018, GE Appliances invested US$55 million in a new Southeast Region Appliance Distribution Center in Commerce.
- GE Appliances had a strong presence in Georgia with 1,960 employees at Roper Corporation, a wholly owned subsidiary in LaFayette, and 50 employees supporting its Factory Service Operations based out of Atlanta.
- John Scott is Vice President and Director of Economic Development for the Jackson County Area Chamber of Commerce.
- When complete, SK Innovation's Georgia location will give it a projected annual global capacity of 71 gigawatt hours (together with facilities in Europe and Asia), making it one of the leading makers of EV batteries in the world.
- Jackson County is centrally located in the Southeast United States’ growing automotive industry.
- The SK Group was among the first Korean investors in Georgia and has a longstanding history in the state.
- Georgia works aggressively to ensure companies like SK Innovation have the best resources necessary for success.
- Enchem's investment validates Jackson County’s thriving business climate attracting top global companies.
- Duckyang's investment helps Jackson County and Georgia secure a place in the automotive industry's future as it moves toward electric vehicles.
- Strong relationships with SK Battery America and other Korean companies like Duckyang help bring suppliers to the state to secure local supply of needed parts.
- Toyota's expansion adds new jobs for Georgians and proves the business climate, workforce investments, logistics infrastructure, and quality of life make a compelling case for growth.
- Jackson County’s strategic location, strong workforce, and quality of life continue to attract significant investment from companies like GE Appliances.
