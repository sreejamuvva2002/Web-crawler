# Georgia Power

**Type:** Company  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[charging-infrastructure|Charging Infrastructure]]  ·  **Location:** Georgia, United States

## Overview

Georgia Power offers electric vehicle (EV) charging infrastructure and rebates for residential Level 2 chargers, aiming to expand EV adoption across the state of Georgia.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Provides EV charging rebates and promotes installation of Level 2 home chargers in Georgia.

## Related organizations & people

- FuelEconomy.gov — Reference site for federal EV tax credit information
- Georgia Power Marketplace — Online marketplace used to claim the rebate

## Workforce & partners

- Georgia Power Marketplace — Rebate platform offering instant $150 rebate for Level 2 chargers

## Key facts

- Georgia is among the top ten largest markets in the nation for Electric Vehicle (EV) charging.
- By 2030, it is predicted that 50% of all new car sales will be electric vehicles.
- Georgia Power is committed to connecting communities with charging infrastructure to provide range confidence statewide.
- Customers may receive up to a $150 rebate for installing a Level 2 charger at home; the rebate is available while funds last and amount is subject to change.
- All electric and plug-in hybrid cars purchased in or after 2010 may be eligible for a federal tax credit, referenced via FuelEconomy.gov.
- Standard 120-volt household circuits can charge EVs, but Georgia Power recommends a dedicated outlet to avoid overloading the circuit.
- Higher-powered chargers may require a permit for home installation; customers are advised to contact an electrician for upgrades.
