# Rivian

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-08-06

**Source:** [Rivian boosts revenue and improves margins in Q2 - electrive.com](https://www.electrive.com/2025/08/06/rivian-boosts-revenue-and-improves-margins-in-q2/)  ·  `page_059096`

## Facts

- Reported a consolidated gross loss of $206 million for Q2 2025, an improvement from minus $451 million in Q2 2024.
- Automotive gross profit remained negative at minus $335 million, attributed to lower production volumes and underutilised capacity.
- Gross profit from software and services was $129 million.
- Reported total revenue of $1.30 billion for the quarter, up from $1.16 billion in Q2 2024.
- Automotive revenue accounted for $927 million.
- Software and services contributed $376 million to revenue, compared to $84 million in the same period last year.
- Reported $182 million in revenue related to its joint venture with Volkswagen Group.
- Received a $1 billion investment from Volkswagen Group in June.
- Ended the quarter with $7.5 billion in cash, cash equivalents, and short-term investments.
- Total liquidity was reported at $8.5 billion, including a credit facility from Volkswagen paid in June.
- Produced 5,979 vehicles during the second quarter of 2025.
- Delivered 10,661 vehicles during the second quarter of 2025.
- Built over 14,600 EVs in Q1 2025 but delivered only 8,640, creating a surplus handed to customers in Q2.
- Expects Q3 2025 to be its highest delivery quarter for the year across both consumer and commercial vehicles.
- The US federal tax credit of $7,500 will expire after 30 September, potentially driving customer purchases before the cut-off date.
- Recently presented quad-motor variants of the R1S and R1T models.
- Deliveries of the new quad-motor R1S and R1T models kicked off in July.
- Reported an adjusted EBITDA of -$667 million for Q2 2025, an improvement over -$857 million last year.
- Reported a net loss for the quarter of $1.12 billion, compared to $1.46 billion in 2024.
- Reaffirmed its 2025 expected delivery target of 40,000 to 46,000 vehicles.
- Reaffirmed 2025 capital expenditure guidance of $1.8 to $1.9 billion.
- Revised full-year adjusted EBITDA loss guidance to $2 to $2.25 billion, citing updated assumptions around regulatory credits and Q2 results.
