# State of Georgia

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2022-07

**Source:** [Georgia Emerges as Hub for Sustainability - Middle Georgia CEO](https://middlegeorgiaceo.com/features/2022/07/georgia-emerges-hub-sustainability/)  ·  `page_017469`

## Facts

- Companies looking to lower emissions and reduce carbon footprint have invested over $11.5 billion in Georgia since 2018.
- Ascend Elements, formerly known as Battery Resourcers, is investing $43 million to open a commercial-scale lithium-ion battery recycling plant in Georgia.
- The Ascend Elements facility will extract critically scarce materials like lithium, cobalt, and nickel from batteries.
- The Ascend Elements plant will become the largest of its kind in North America when fully operational later this year.
- Roger Lin, Ascend Elements’ Vice President of Marketing and Government Relations, stated that Georgia was selected primarily because of the growing electric vehicle and battery ecosystem in the state.
- Proximity to partners and customers is essential in the sustainable battery materials industry, according to Lin.
- The Southeast is a mecca for the electric vehicle and battery industry, and in Georgia, Ascend Elements is close to factories like SK Battery America and others like it.
- Operating in Georgia minimizes transportation costs, provides proximity to top talent from nearby universities, and offers access to renewable energy, notes Lin.
- Korea’s SK Battery America is investing nearly $2.6 billion in two facilities near Atlanta to supply batteries to Ford and Volkswagen as well as other electric vehicle makers.
- SK Battery America made its first announcement regarding the Georgia facilities in 2018.
- Plug Power recently announced a new $84 million, zero-emission hydrogen fuel production facility near I-95 and Georgia’s deepwater ports.
- Plug Power provides fuel cell systems for e-mobility that deliver efficiency, fast fueling, and low cost to customers.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp launched the Electric Mobility and Innovation Alliance, a statewide initiative between government, industries, electric utilities, nonprofits, and other stakeholders.
- The alliance focuses on growing the electric mobility ecosystem in the state and strengthening Georgia’s position in electrification-related manufacturing and innovation.
- In 2021, EV manufacturer Rivian announced an investment of $5 billion to build a carbon-conscious facility to make vertically connected electric platforms for trucks, sport/utility vehicles, and delivery vans.
- Hyundai Motor Group will open its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility in the State of Georgia, announcing a $5.54 billion investment.
- Operations at the Hyundai facility are slated to begin in the first half of 2025.
- The Hyundai facility will have an annual capacity of 300,000 units.
- Many other Georgia companies are already making parts for EVs.
- Companies from Korea, Germany, Turkey, and the Netherlands have announced plans to build EV-related facilities in the state.
- Site Selection Magazine recently ranked Georgia No. 3 for sustainability in the South Atlantic region.
- The Atlanta metro ranked in the Top 10 nationally for sustainability.
- Since 2009, Georgia has increased net electricity generation from renewable sources by more than 93%.
- Georgia’s electricity mix currently consists of nearly 12% renewables.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that the installation of renewable energy capacity in Georgia has been driven organically by companies and consumers, accelerated through economic attractiveness and removing barriers rather than mandate.
- Smart policies have allowed Georgia to promote renewable power development and offer a comprehensive portfolio of clean energy options that give the state a competitive edge in attracting leading businesses, according to Wilson.
- Solar PV is the state’s fastest-growing source of renewable energy.
- NanoPV will manufacture solar panels and equipment in south Georgia’s Sumter County.
- Qcells is investing more than $320 million in its solar panel assembly plant in northern Georgia.
- The Qcells facility is the largest solar panel assembly plant in the Western hemisphere.
- Recent sustainability-related announcements in Georgia include German copper recycler Aurubis, electronic waste recycler Igneo, Danimer Scientific (manufacturer and developer of biodegradable materials), and recycled furniture maker SoPoly.
- EF Block turns everyday recycled Styrofoam into earth-friendly, stronger, sustainable walls that replace traditional building materials.
- With support from GDEcD’s Center of Innovation and the City of Atlanta, EF Block has launched a city-wide Styrofoam recycling initiative.
- NANTRenewables is opening a plant on Georgia’s coast to manufacture biodegradable products like plates, straws, cutlery, trays, and cups.
- NANTRenewables provides sustainable products to companies within the food, agriculture, and pharmaceutical industries.
- Harold Meherg, a NANT Renewables senior executive, stated that SeaPoint Industrial Terminal Complex in Savannah was selected for their new biogenic fillers and bioplastics manufacturing facility due to shared sustainability values and development-ready infrastructure including roads, rail access, sea access, and nearby utility connections.
- Companies leading the charge in mobility research & development, battery manufacturing and recycling, and electric vehicle production all call Georgia home.
