# Robert Bosch GmbH

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Bosch focuses on growth in United States - Bosch Media Service US](https://us.bosch-press.com/pressportal/us/en/press-release-27520.html)  ·  `page_018132`

## Facts

- Over the past five years, Bosch has invested more than $2 billion in capital expenditures in the United States, representing more than 75 percent of its North American capital expenditures.
- Since 2023, Bosch has announced plans to invest around $6 billion in U.S.-based acquisitions.
- The company currently employs more than 20,000 associates and operates 15+ manufacturing sites in the United States, with both numbers expected to grow in 2025 following a major planned acquisition.
- Bosch's Mobility business sector is its largest, expanding its product portfolio to match U.S. mobility trends including software-defined vehicles (SDV), advanced-driver assistance systems (ADAS), hybrid technology, and vehicle motion control.
- The company continues to innovate on internal combustion engine (ICE) technology to support hybrids while also providing battery electric and hydrogen solutions, including fuel cell and hydrogen combustion technology.
- Bosch maintains an experienced U.S. mobility manufacturing workforce across nine locations in California, Indiana, Kentucky, Michigan, Minnesota, and South Carolina.
- Plans are underway to manufacture second-generation silicon carbide (SiC) chips on 200mm wafers at a facility in Roseville, California beginning in 2026.
- The transformation of the acquired Roseville facility represents an investment of up to $1.9 billion.
- Nearly all 250 associates at the Roseville site have been retained during the transformation process to prepare for the 2026 SiC production launch, with advanced training provided from other Bosch global manufacturing sites.
- The Cross-Domain Computing Solutions division is a major growth driver for Bosch Mobility in North America, delivering ADAS and vehicle computers to support U.S. automakers' shift toward software-defined vehicles.
- Bosch has expanded its software and hardware capabilities for assisted and automated driving, particularly Level 2 and Level 2++ systems.
- The company developed a new radar sensor with all core elements manufactured in-house, including a computing chip (SoC) using RF CMOS technology with a 22-nanometer transistor structure size, making Bosch the first Tier 1 supplier to introduce this technology in serial production.
- Bosch signed a major award with the Southeastern Pennsylvania Transportation Authority (SEPTA) to install tram forward collision warning systems across its fleet via retrofit, following a successful 2022 pilot program on four vehicles.
- In 2024, Bosch announced plans to acquire Johnson Controls' HVAC solutions business for residential and light commercial buildings, including the York and Coleman brands, with closure expected in 2025.
- Through its subsidiary Bosch Ventures, the company is providing around $270 million USD for a new venture capital fund focused on stimulating growth in electromobility, hydrogen, and software-defined vehicles.
- The Industrial Technology division's factory automation segment is focusing on growth areas including battery, semiconductor, and consumer goods production.
- Chairman Stefan Hartung stated the company's ambition for the North American region to represent 20 percent of global sales by 2030, with the U.S. accounting for the majority.
- Paul Thomas, president of Bosch in North America and Bosch Mobility Americas, confirmed the company is investing to grow in the U.S. market and has developed regionally relevant products and solutions to increase market share and achieve profitable growth.
