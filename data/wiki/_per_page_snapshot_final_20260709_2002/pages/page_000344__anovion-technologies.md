# Anovion Technologies

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2023-05-16

**Source:** [Anovion, maker of graphite for electric batteries, plans $800 million factory in southwest Georgia | AP News](https://apnews.com/article/anovion-georgia-graphite-batteries-electric-vehicles-factory-087ced276d8409a9b23336b72d3e482c)  ·  `page_000344`

## Facts

- Anovion announced plans to invest $800 million to build a factory in southwest Georgia.
- The company is trying to become a major American-based supplier of graphite for electric batteries.
- The factory will hire 400 workers.
- Anovion Technologies is based in Chicago.
- The company will produce synthetic graphite anodes in Bainbridge, Georgia.
- Production of synthetic graphite anodes for lithium-ion batteries is scheduled to start in 2025.
- The plant supports Georgia's initiative to recruit electric vehicle manufacturers and their suppliers.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp stated the announcement furthers his goal of making Georgia the nation's electric mobility capital.
- Most battery graphite is currently manufactured outside the United States.
- Georgia economic development officials report over 40 EV-related projects announced in the state since 2020.
- These projects pledge $22 billion in investment and 28,000 jobs.
- Anovion was formed last year by combining the graphite businesses of Pyrotek and Amsted Graphite Materials.
- The company received new investment from private equity firm Monomyth Group.
- Anovion operates existing facilities in Sanborn, New York, and Clarksburg, West Virginia.
- The company previously announced plans to build a graphite factory near Muscle Shoals, northwest Alabama.
- The Georgia factory will have a production capacity of 44,000 tons (40,000 metric tonnes).
- Anovion received a $117 million grant under the federal infrastructure law last year.
- The grant will help finance the Georgia plant and improvements at existing facilities.
- The company described planned pay levels only as "competitive."
- Anovion CEO Eric Stopka stated the company was attracted to Georgia by its workforce, research universities, and growing solar energy production.
- CEO Eric Stopka stated the company looks forward to securing the country's electrified future with support from Georgia and federal partners.
- Anovion executives aim to reach 150,000 tons of production in the coming years.
- Synthetic graphite is manufactured from carbon products like petroleum coke (an oil refining byproduct) heated to super-high temperatures.
- The manufacturing process requires huge amounts of electricity, making power costs a key location factor.
- Anovion aims to become carbon neutral by using renewable energy and more efficient furnaces.
- CEO Eric Stopka cited Georgia's proximity to low- and carbon-free energy sources as a key reason for the location choice.
- Anovion may qualify for a state income tax credit allowing an annual deduction of $3,500 per job for five years.
- The tax credit requires workers to earn at least $31,300 annually.
- The potential tax credit value could reach $7 million.
- Anovion may qualify for property tax breaks from Bainbridge and Decatur County.
- The state of Georgia will pay an undisclosed amount to train workers.
- The announcement marks the second major industrial project in two years for Bainbridge, located in the far southwest corner of Georgia.
- Danimer Scientific, a biodegradable plastic maker, announced a $700 million factory and 400 jobs in Bainbridge in 2021.
- Decatur County Commission Chairman Pete Stephens stated diversifying the industry mix is necessary for competitiveness and growth.
