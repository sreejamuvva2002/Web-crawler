# Hella GmbH & Co. KGaA

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hella (company) - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hella_(company))  ·  `page_055766`

## Facts

- Hella GmbH & Co. KGaA is an internationally operating German automotive parts supplier headquartered in Lippstadt, North Rhine-Westphalia.
- The company develops and manufactures lighting, electronic components, and systems for the automotive industry.
- Hella operates one of the largest trade organizations for automotive parts, accessories, diagnostics, and services within Europe.
- It is one of the top 50 global automotive suppliers and one of the 100 largest industrial companies in Germany.
- The company has approximately 37,000 employees at over 125 locations worldwide.
- Hella generated consolidated sales of €8.1 billion in fiscal year 2024.
- In 2022, the company merged with French global automotive supplier Faurecia, forming the merged business named Forvia.
- The two divisions of the merger are formally called Forvia Hella and Forvia Faurecia.
- Products of Hella GmbH & Co. KGaA continue to be sold under its own brand following the merger.
- Sally Windmüller founded the company in 1899 under the name Westfälische Metall-Industrie Aktien-Gesellschaft (WMI) to produce ball horns, candles, and kerosene lamps for carriages.
- Hella's name first appeared in 1908 as a trademark for acetylene headlights.
- In 1923, the manufacturing family Lüdenscheider Hueck took over the majority of the shares.
- The name "Hella" was included in the company name in 1986.
- The Hella brand is attributed to founder Sally Windmüller honoring his wife Helen and using the German word heller (brighter).
- After World War II, expansion led to the first subsidiary company being founded in Wembach in 1951.
- Hella has six production manufacturers in Germany located in Lippstadt, Bremen, Recklinghausen, Hamm (Bockum-Hövel), Nellingen, and Wembach.
- Early internationalization in the 1960s resulted in one of the largest distribution networks in the industry.
- The first foreign production plant was founded in Mentone, Australia in 1961.
- Hella operates more than 100 locations in over 35 countries across Europe, NAFTA/South America, Africa, and Asia/Pacific.
- Since the early 1990s, Hella has been part of several Joint Ventures with other automotive part suppliers to develop new competencies.
- The company cooperates with partners including Mahle Behr, Plastic Omnium, Samlip, Leoni, Mando, TMD Friction, and innocent.
- Joint Ventures include HBPO and BHTC located in Lippstadt.
- A holding named "Hella Stanley Ltd Pty" with Stanley Electric was founded in 2002 and is located in Melbourne.
- In 2008, Hella started a joint venture with Gutmann Messtechnik to focus on diagnostic specialists.
- Also in 2008, the company announced it would pay more attention to items outside the traditional automotive domain.
- In 2008, Hella sold its Danish subsidiary Holger Christiansen A/S to the automotive supplier Bosch.
- Due to strong economic fluctuations, Hella recorded a loss in fiscal year 2005/06.
- Management implemented a program to lower costs and increase efficiency, recording an operational gain in fiscal year 2006/07.
- The operational gain increased again in fiscal year 2007/08.
- Hella recorded its second highest consolidated sale of €4.4 billion in the following fiscal years.
- Constant growth continued, reaching €5.3 billion in 2013/14.
- Since 2017, Hella has spun off Brighter AI Technologies and Yptokey from its incubator.
- In 2021, French automotive supplier Faurecia agreed to acquire the Hueck family's 60% majority stake in Hella for €3.4 billion.
- Faurecia announced an offer for remaining shares at €60 per share, valuing the company at a total of €6.7 billion.
- The takeover agreement for the 60% stake was concluded on 15 August 2021.
- Faurecia purchased half of the remaining shares in August 2022, bringing its stake to 80%.
- Hella's main businesses are divided into three segments: Automotive, Aftermarket, and Special Applications.
- The Automotive Segment develops, produces, and markets lighting and electronic components and systems for vehicle manufacturers and other suppliers.
- The Aftermarket Segment develops, produces, and markets products for the independent aftermarket and garages.
- The Special Applications Segment serves construction machinery producers, boat builders, municipalities, and energy suppliers with innovative lighting and electronic products.
- The lighting division develops and manufactures headlamps, rear lamps, and interior lighting.
- Recent innovations include headlights that adapt to prevailing driving and weather situations.
- Headlamps with LEDs as light sources for low beam and high beam are already produced in series.
- An example of LED headlamp application is the beams in the Cadillac Escalade Platinum.
- As daytime running lights, these are included as standard in many middle- and upper-class vehicles.
- The electronic product portfolio includes data-enabled electronic control units, roof modules, and vehicle access and driving authorization modules.
- Modules for power management optimize the energy balance of the electrical system and improve battery charge balance.
- Innovation and growth areas include electronic driver assistance systems using infrared (lidar), 24-GHz radar, camera, and ultrasonic sensors.
- Other important business segments include position sensors, actuators, vacuum pumps, and wash water systems.
- Hella sells automotive parts and accessories with its own sales companies and partners in more than 100 countries.
- The company supplies the automotive aftermarket and garages with parts covering lighting, electrical, electronics, and thermal management.
- In addition to civilian car accessories, Hella manufactures special signaling for official emergency vehicles including flash beacons, Visual Warning Systems, and sound combinations.
- These emergency vehicle products include compact roof structures combining blue lights, sirens, and additional issues for police, fire departments, and technical aid agencies.
- The Industries segment transfers automotive technologies to new target groups, including a pilot project providing new street lights to cities in China.
- Another project involves LED-airport beacons found at airports in Luxembourg, Paderborn/Lippstadt, and Stavanger.
- In 2012, Hella started a cooperation with Chinese automobile manufacturer BAIC to develop and produce light systems designed for the Chinese market.
- In 2017, Hella partnered with Air-Quality Data Company BreezoMeter.
- As of 31 December 2023, shareholder structure includes Forvia SE (80.59%), Elliott Investment Management LP (Paul Singer) (9.91%), Artisan Partners LP (2.76%), and Public (6.74%).
- The company's revenue was €7,954,140,000 in 2023, with total assets of €7,061,640,000 in 2023.
- Forvia is listed as the owner with an 81.6% stake.
