# Georgia Department of Economic Development

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2022-03

**Source:** [GDEcD State of Small Business: A Roundtable Opportunity - Savannah CEO](https://savannahceo.com/news/2022/03/state-small-business-roundtable-opportunity/)  ·  `page_048259`

## Facts

- Governor Kemp announced a new position to support and empower Georgia’s small business community less than a year prior to March 2022.
- GDEcD established a Director of Small Business Outreach based on a recommendation from Governor Kemp’s Georgians First Commission.
- Companies qualify as small businesses in Georgia if they are independently owned and operated with fewer than 300 employees or less than $30 million in yearly gross receipts.
- More than 1.6 million Georgians are employed by small businesses across sectors including manufacturing, retail, arts, hospitality, and film production.
- In November 2021, Director of Small Business Outreach Allen Fox launched the “State of Small Business” program with a series of regional roundtables.
- The inaugural roundtable event was held in Macon to allow state and local organizations to hear directly from small business owners about their efforts, needs, and concerns.
- Additional roundtables have been presented to partners in Augusta, Butts County, Columbus, the Georgia Black Chamber, Rome, and other locations.
- GDEcD conducts 80% of its work with small businesses through teams covering Global Commerce, International Trade, Tourism, Center of Innovation, Entertainment, Georgia Council for the Arts, and International Relations.
- Communities or organizations interested in hosting a “State of Small Business” presentation should contact Allen Fox via email.
