# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA)

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-02-26

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp, State Leaders Celebrate Hyundai Motor Group’s Historic Impact on Georgia | Governor Brian P. Kemp Office of the Governor](https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases/2024-02-26/gov-kemp-state-leaders-celebrate-hyundai-motor-groups-historic-impact)  ·  `page_000062`

## Facts

- Governor Brian Kemp and First Lady Marty Kemp joined President and Global COO of Hyundai Motor Company and President and CEO of Hyundai and Genesis Motor North America José Muñoz to celebrate Hyundai Motor Group’s historic impact on Georgia.
- The Metaplant America facility will officially start vehicle production in the fourth quarter of this year.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated Hyundai has been an incredible partner to Georgia for decades and highlighted the historic benefits brought by the project.
- Governor Kemp noted Hyundai helps recruit businesses, gives back to communities, and partners with colleges and universities to connect Georgians with opportunities.
- Hyundai and Genesis electric vehicles on display included the IONIQ 5, IONIQ 6, GV60, Electrified GV70, and the hydrogen-powered XCIENT Fuel Cell Truck for logistics between manufacturing facilities and suppliers.
- Governor Kemp officially declared February 26 as Hyundai Day throughout the state.
- The Georgia General Assembly passed a Resolution commending Hyundai Motor Group’s dedication to Georgia and recognizing February 26 as Hyundai Day at the State Capitol.
- José Muñoz stated Hyundai views Georgia as a second home and expressed pride in investments creating tens of thousands of great-paying American jobs and bringing tens of billions of dollars in economic impact for decades.
- Since the HMGMA project was announced in May 2022, LG Energy Solution was named as the joint venture partner for battery component manufacturing.
- An additional expansion of the LG Energy Solution battery facility was announced.
- Hyundai and SK On are building an EV battery manufacturing facility in Bartow County to supply Hyundai Motor Group’s U.S. plants.
- Since the initial announcement, Hyundai Motor Group’s planned investment in Georgia has reached more than $12.6 billion.
- The project will create 12,000 direct, long-term jobs.
- Attendees at the celebration included Lieutenant Governor Burt Jones, House Speaker Jon Burns, legislative leaders from both chambers, Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson, local elected and economic officials from the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority, and company representatives.
- Lt. Governor Burt Jones applauded Governor Kemp, state agencies, and the University System of Georgia (especially Georgia Tech) for their partnership with Hyundai.
- Lt. Governor Jones stated stakeholder commitment enabled Hyundai's positive impact and creation of thousands of jobs.
- Lt. Governor Jones noted these initiatives support Georgia's position as the number one state to do business.
- Up to 300,000 Hyundai, Genesis, and Kia EVs are expected to be produced annually at the Metaplant.
- 17 HMGMA suppliers have announced projects across 10 Georgia counties beyond southeast Georgia.
- Further supplier announcements are expected.
- Supplier facilities are expected to create more than 6,700 new jobs and more than $2.5 billion in investment.
- Supplier facilities will be operational prior to the opening of HMGMA.
- Speaker Jon Burns praised Governor Kemp and the General Assembly for sound policy direction supporting Hyundai's Southeast Georgia investment.
- Speaker Burns stated the Metaplant will have a generational impact as a force multiplier for investments in education, healthcare, housing, and transportation.
- The nearly 3,000-acre Metaplant site has four building exteriors complete with internal outfitting underway.
- The Metaplant’s architecture incorporates technologically advanced elements and design features representing the Savannah area, Hyundai’s brand identity, nature, and clean energy.
- HMGMA is hiring full-time "Meta Pros" at a rapid pace for training and skills on machinery, office and clerical work, and numerous other employment roles.
- HMGMA currently has 566 full-time employees.
- Approximately 75 percent of the current workforce is from the Greater Savannah area.
- An additional 37 employees are from Georgia but outside the local area.
- Georgia Quick Start broke ground on its customized training center at the Metaplant site on February 8, 2024.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated Georgia fulfilled a commitment with the groundbreaking and noted Georgians will be trained in high-skill careers as Meta Pros.
- HMGMA partnered with local technical colleges to prepare students for entry-level employment at the Metaplant.
- Electrical Vehicle Professional Technical Certificate of Credit (EVP TCC) training is available at Coastal Pines Technical College’s Baxley and Waycross campuses, Southeastern Technical College’s Vidalia campus, Savannah Technical College, and Ogeechee Technical College.
- TCSG Commissioner Greg Dozier expressed pride in the partnership with Hyundai for innovation, excellence, and the future of the automotive industry.
- In September 2023, Georgia Institute of Technology (Georgia Tech) and Hyundai Motor Company signed a memorandum of understanding to collaborate on research and applications for sustainable mobility.
- The MOU covers developing a hydrogen economy, workforce development, and making cities smarter and more sustainable.
- U.S. News & World Report ranked Georgia Tech as the top public university and No. 3 nationally in energy and fuel research.
- Georgia Tech President Ángel Cabrera stated the partnership represents a significant corporate relationship to advance science, emerging technologies, and produce the next-generation workforce.
- Since 1998, Hyundai has directed over $6 million towards local Georgia communities through philanthropic programs and partnerships.
- The company is promoting STEM programs in local K-12 schools and hosting educational programs on alternative energy sources.
- Hyundai supports local organizations dedicated to affordable homeownership and focuses on environmental stewardship championing the regional community.
- Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority’s Trip Tollison stated Hyundai has given more than $1 million to regional nonprofits and formed key partnerships with local educational institutions.
- Trip Tollison noted Hyundai committed to $7.59 billion in capital investment in HMGMA and will create 8,500 jobs.
- More than 550 Meta Pros have already been hired, with 75 percent coming from the Savannah region.
- Beginning with mass production start, electricity used to power the Metaplant will come from 100 percent clean power sources.
- This aligns with Hyundai’s commitment to carbon neutrality by 2045.
- A 41-acre park will be featured in front of the plant including trails, running tracks, environmental art displays, sports fields, and amenities for employee use.
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America is Hyundai Motor Group’s first dedicated Electric Vehicle mass-production plant located in Bryan County, Georgia.
- The facility will utilize a highly connected, automated, and flexible manufacturing system that organically connects all elements of the EV ecosystem to realize customer value.
- The Georgia facility will become an intelligent manufacturing plant optimizing order collection, procurement, logistics, and production utilizing AI and data.
- The innovative manufacturing system will create a human-centered work environment with robots assisting human workers.
- Hyundai Motor Group is a global enterprise creating a value chain based on mobility, steel, construction, logistics, finance, IT, and service with about 250,000 employees worldwide.
