# stow Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-01-27

**Source:** [stow Agreement Signing - Georgia Quick Start](https://www.georgiaquickstart.org/stow-agreement-signing/)  ·  `page_000121`

## Facts

- Georgia Quick Start hosted a training plan signing on January 27, 2026 at 11 a.m. to commemorate its partnership with stow Group and Georgia Northwestern Technical College.
- The Belgium-based company is investing $36 million to build a new industrial storage and automation solution facility outside of Adairsville, Georgia.
- stow plans to hire 200 employees for production roles as well as logistics, administrative and management roles.
- Georgia Quick Start, the state’s top-ranked workforce training program, will be providing customized training for stow.
- Training will include several hands-on labs such as a Safety Evaluation Room and Torquing Room.
- Scott McMurray, Deputy Commissioner at Georgia Quick Start, stated that stow’s decision to come to Georgia is a strong vote of confidence in the state's workforce.
- The training scope includes courses on the stow manufacturing process, upright operations, beam operations, bracing operations, logistics and warehousing operations, and assembly.
- Four customized courses will be complete eLearning courses delivered via a computer lab.
- Michael Morgenroth, General Plant Manager for stow Manufacturing in Adairsville, noted that Quick Start will be an invaluable partner as they prepare to bring the facility online.
- The training program involves three distinct partners: stow, Georgia Northwestern Technical College, and Georgia Quick Start, each responsible for successful program completion.
- Quick Start will deliver training during the expansion, while Georgia Northwestern Technical College will serve as stow’s long-term partner for ongoing training and providing a pipeline of qualified graduates.
- Heidi Popham, President of Georgia Northwestern Technical College, expressed pride in partnering to support stow Group’s decision to locate in Gordon County and help ensure a skilled workforce across northwest Georgia.
- The Development Authority of Gordon County, Gordon County Chamber, Georgia Power, and the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) worked with Georgia Northwestern Technical College and Georgia Quick Start to qualify stow Group for training support as operations are upgraded.
- Training is scheduled to begin in March at the Quick Start Cartersville Training Complex.
