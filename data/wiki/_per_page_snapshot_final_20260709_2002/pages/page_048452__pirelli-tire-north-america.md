# Pirelli Tire North America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [How Pirelli Tire North America is Driving Lower Emissions](https://www.industrialdecarbonizationnetwork.com/sustainable-operations/interviews/driving-towards-lower-emissions-at-pirelli-tires)  ·  `page_048452`

## Facts

- Pirelli Tire was founded in the 1800s in Milan by Italian Giovanni Battista Pirelli to produce elastic rubber items.
- The company launched its first tires for velocipedes in 1894.
- Pirelli operates 18 production facilities across 12 countries globally.
- The company provides premium, high-performance tires for cars, motorcycles, and bicycles.
- Pirelli boasts 5.3 billion euros in annual revenue.
- Maureen Kline serves as Pirelli’s Vice President of Public Affairs and Sustainability North America.
- Decarbonization in the automobile industry is driving demand for sustainable tire products within the automotive supply chain.
- Pirelli aims to power all electricity used at its operations with renewable sources by 2025.
- The company's factory in Rome, Georgia was the first tire factory anywhere in the world to be Forest Stewardship Council® - certified for sustainable natural rubber and rayon.
- The Rome, Georgia facility is a highly automated small factory that produces very high-end tires.
- FSC certification requires a complex chain of custody where rubber, mostly sourced from Southeast Asia, is certified at the farm and tracked through the supply chain to production facilities.
- Pirelli is exploring innovative materials to increase product sustainability, including rice husk silica as a substitute for traditional silica.
- The company is investigating lignin sourced from pulp and paper mills to use as a biopolymer antioxidant instead of fossil-derived products.
- In 2021, Pirelli produced a concept tire containing 94% renewable and recycled materials, including silica from rice husks, recycled carbon black, and bio-resins.
- Pirelli targets having 70% of its tires in European efficiency categories A and B by 2025 to indicate low rolling resistance.
- The company is focusing heavily on developing specialized tires for the electric vehicle market to improve vehicle range through low rolling resistance.
- More than half of Pirelli’s global electricity currently comes from renewable sources.
- At its Rome, Georgia manufacturing site, Pirelli has partnered with a solar energy company to produce renewable energy on the property.
- Industrial decarbonization is expensive, and Pirelli states that achieving it requires partnerships with government, regulations, and incentives.
- Pirelli’s R&D and procurement departments are highly specialized in evaluating technological innovations for sustainable materials without compromising tire performance or safety.
