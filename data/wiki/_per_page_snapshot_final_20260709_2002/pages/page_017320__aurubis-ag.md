# Aurubis AG

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2023-02-16

**Source:** [Growth course confirmed at Aurubis Annual General Meeting --  Trade Fair](https://www.gifa.com/en/Media_News/News/Growth_course_confirmed_at_Aurubis_Annual_General_Meeting)  ·  `page_017320`

## Facts

- Aurubis held its Annual General Meeting in Hamburg on February 16, 2023.
- The company is focusing on securing and strengthening its core business in accordance with its strategy "Metals for progress: Driving sustainable growth".
- Over the next three to four years, Aurubis will invest about €1.1 billion to implement its strategic agenda.
- Decided growth projects are expected to contribute around €260 million to the Group's operating result (EBITDA) starting in fiscal year 2026/27.
- The new Aurubis Richmond US site is located in the state of Georgia and serves as a central building block for future growth.
- Total investment for the Aurubis Richmond US site is €640 million.
- The Georgia facility intends to capitalize on a recycling market growth of more than 5% and increased availability of complex recycling materials.
- Roland Harings, Chairman of the Executive Board, emphasized the company's extremely solid financial position and commitment to investing in sustainable growth.
- Aurubis is expanding tankhouse capacity at its Bulgarian site in Pirdop by 50% with an investment of about €120 million.
- Approximately €190 million will be invested in the "CRH - Complex Recycling Hamburg" project to process about 30,000 tons of additional recycling material and complex smelter intermediates starting in 2025.
- The company aims to achieve climate-neutral production well before 2050 through continuous process optimization and decarbonization initiatives.
- Aurubis is expanding its solar park at the Pirdop site by an additional 17 MWp with an investment of about €20 million.
- Roland Harings stated that the growth strategy focuses on consolidating Aurubis's pioneering role in sustainability and its position as the most efficient and sustainable metallurgical network globally.
- Prof. Dr. Fritz Vahrenholt, Kathrin Dahnke, Gunnar Groebler, Prof. Dr. Markus Kramer, Dr. Stephan Krümmer, and Dr. Sandra Reich were elected to Aurubis AG's Supervisory Board during the meeting.
- Prof. Dr. Fritz Vahrenholt was appointed Chairman of the Supervisory Board.
