# Superior Essex Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2008-03-31

**Source:** [page_003253](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1271193/000110465908031755/a08-11429_110q.htm)  ·  `page_003253`

## Facts

- Superior Essex Inc. maintains its principal executive offices at 150 Interstate North Parkway, Atlanta, Georgia 30339.
- The company manufactures and supplies wire and cable products for the automotive end-market, alongside communications, energy, industrial, and commercial/residential sectors.
- Product lines include magnet wire, fabricated insulation products, and copper and fiber optic communications wire and cable products.
- Superior Essex distributes magnet wire, insulation, and related products to smaller original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) and motor repair facilities.
- The company converts copper cathode to copper rod for internal consumption and for sale to other wire and cable manufacturers and OEMs.
- Manufacturing operations are located in the United States, Canada, the United Kingdom, France, Germany, Portugal, Italy, Mexico, and China.
- On January 23, 2008, the company announced a consolidation and restructuring of its North American magnet wire and distribution and copper rod segments’ manufacturing facilities to align production with industry demand and customer requirements.
- The restructuring plan involves a phased closure of the magnet wire manufacturing and copper rod continuous casting facilities in Vincennes, Indiana, and the relocation of existing production to other North American facilities.
- The Board of Directors authorized the restructuring on January 16, 2008, communicated it to employees on January 23, 2008, and expects closures to be completed by the first quarter of 2009.
- Total estimated restructuring charges are $22 million, consisting of approximately $15 million in non-cash accelerated depreciation, $3 million in cash for employee severance and retention, and $4 million for equipment relocation and facility closure costs.
- For the three months ended March 31, 2008, restructuring charges totaled $9.4 million, with $8.0 million attributed to the North American magnet wire and distribution segment and $1.4 million to the copper rod segment.
- Charges for the period include $7.429 million in accelerated depreciation, $1.335 million in employee severance, $237,000 in employee retention costs, and $430,000 in equipment relocation costs.
- Employee retention incentives are structured to reward Vincennes employees who remain employed until specified termination dates while meeting defined safety and attendance goals.
- In March 2008, Essex Europe began discussions with French employee representative bodies regarding the potential closure of its magnet wire manufacturing facility in Chauny, France, which is leased from Nexans through October 2009 and currently employs approximately 130 people.
