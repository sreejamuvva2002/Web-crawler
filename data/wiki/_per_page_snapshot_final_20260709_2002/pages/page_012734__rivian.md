# Rivian

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Rivian boosts Georgia EV plant capacity to 300,000 vehicles with optimised phase-one plan](https://www.automotivemanufacturingsolutions.com/news/rivian-raises-georgia-plant-capacity-to-300000-vehicles-in-optimised-phaseone-plan/2657135)  ·  `page_012734`

## Facts

- Rivian announced a major revision to its manufacturing plans for its future facility in Stanton Springs North, Georgia.
- Initial annual production capacity increased by 50%, from 200,000 to 300,000 vehicles.
- The move compresses what had previously been a two-phase build-out into a single, larger first phase.
- The restructured approach will lower cost per unit while offering meaningful flexibility for further expansion in subsequent phases.
- Announcement comes alongside a renegotiated loan with the US Department of Energy's Office (DOE) of Energy Dominance Financing.
- Updated agreement is valued at up to $4.5 billion, comprising $4,006m in principal and $494m in capitalised interest.
- Loan amount reduced from the $6.6 billion conditional commitment finalised in January 2025.
- Revised loan is aligned with the updated single-phase facility design.
- Company expects to make its first draw on the loan by early 2027, a full year ahead of the prior schedule.
- Vertical construction at the Stanton Springs North site set to begin this spring.
- Preparations already underway for development of the stamping press area.
- Stamping press area described as one of the most capital-intensive and technically demanding elements of the plant from a construction perspective.
- Pace of activity expected to accelerate through summer as facility's primary buildings take shape.
- Vehicle production remains on track to commence in late 2028.
- Georgia plant will anchor production of vehicles on Rivian's all-new midsize platform, with the R2 SUV leading output.
- Platform offers three powertrain configurations: single-motor rear-wheel drive, dual-motor all-wheel drive, and tri-motor.
- New 4695 cell will underpin both battery size options.
- Larger pack estimated to deliver over 300 miles of range.
- Vehicle features an 11-camera, five-radar autonomous perception stack.
- R2 and its production line were designed in concert, not in sequence.
- Rivian built a digital twin of the plant to develop and test the production system virtually while the vehicle was still being finalised.
- Parallel development process allowed team to optimise entire vehicle lifecycle from initial assembly through to long-term serviceability.
- Assembly process is leaner at every stage due to designing vehicle and factory together.
- Rivian eliminated 2.3 miles of wiring from the R2’s electronics architecture.
- Reduced part complexity through extensive use of large, high-pressure die castings.
- Castings removed thousands of welds and fasteners from assembly process.
- Cut build time and reduced overall vehicle mass by around 2,000 lbs compared with the R1.
- Structural battery unit included, where top of pack serves as vehicle floor, further consolidating components and simplifying build.
- Fully automated hang-on system in body shop ensures precise gap and flush alignment across all exterior panels.
- AI-powered robot handles part scanning and placement without manual input.
- Lineside wireless diagnostics identify and correct faults in real time.
- Advanced vision robots carry out precision quality checks at each stage of assembly.
- AI algorithms continuously optimise floor plan utilisation and deploy team members where needed most.
- Production line design allows for 155,000 R2 units annually at existing Normal, Illinois facility.
- Georgia plant will not be limited to consumer R2 production.
- Rivian's recently announced partnership with Uber will see up to 50,000 R2-based robotaxis built at the facility.
- Robotaxi production also beginning in late 2028.
- Initial autonomous deployments planned for San Francisco and Miami.
- Expansion to 25 cities expected by 2031.
- Robotaxi programme layers high-volume autonomous vehicle manufacturing stream on top of consumer production line.
- Any future expansion beyond restructured first phase will be funded by Rivian rather than through DOE loan facility.
- Company secured larger plant for less financing than original deal required, with earlier access to capital.
- Quote from company: "R2 dramatically expands our market opportunity. The thousands of dedicated people who will soon work in our Georgia plant will be instrumental to Rivian’s growth as we scale American manufacturing."
