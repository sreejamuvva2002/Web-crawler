# SK Battery America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-11-14

**Source:** [SK On to furlough workers, cut production at US plant - The Korea Herald](https://www.koreaherald.com/article/3258536)  ·  `page_050071`

## Facts

- SK Battery America, SK On’s US subsidiary, will furlough workers at its battery plant in Jackson County, Georgia.
- The company is cutting down production amid a sluggish electric vehicle market.
- The announcement regarding the furloughs and production cuts was made on Tuesday.
- SK Battery America is temporarily adjusting its line operations and furloughing a portion of its production employees.
- The decision is part of efforts to optimize line operations and workforce management with flexibility as the EV industry adjusts its growth pace.
- The company did not disclose how many employees will be furloughed or the duration of their absence.
- SK Battery America operates two battery factories in Jackson County, Georgia.
- The combined annual production capacity of the two Jackson County facilities is 21.5 gigawatt-hours.
- This capacity is sufficient to power approximately 180,000 EVs.
- An SK On official stated that adjusting production up or down based on market status is standard industry practice.
- SK Battery America remains committed to its Georgia site and optimistic about the long-term growth of the US EV market.
- SK On believes its Georgia site will play a leading role for years to come in manufacturing batteries for American-built EVs.
- The company previously announced layoffs in September.
- The facility employs approximately 3,000 workers.
- The exact number of employees laid off in September was not disclosed.
- The factory currently has more employees than its originally targeted hiring goals.
- The September layoffs were implemented to align production with current market demands.
- SK On has invested a total of $2.6 billion to build the two Georgia battery manufacturing facilities since 2019.
- The Georgia facilities supply batteries for EVs including the Ford F-150 Lightning and the Volkswagen ID.4.
- SK On is pursuing a $5 billion joint venture with Hyundai Motor Group to build a battery cell plant in Bartow County, Georgia.
- SK On stated that the Jackson County production adjustments are unconnected to the Bartow County joint venture.
- The Hyundai-SK On joint venture plant is expected to begin mass production in the second half of 2025.
- The joint venture plant will have an annual production capacity of 35 gigawatt-hours.
- SK On is adjusting production lines amid concerns over short-term weakening EV demand driven by economic recessions and rising costs.
- LG Energy Solution announced on Saturday that it is forgoing plans to build a battery plant in Turkey.
- The abandoned Turkey project was a joint effort with Ford and Koc Holding.
- Koc Holding withdrew from the Turkey battery plant project due to reviews regarding EV adoption rates and investment timing.
