# Storion Energy LLC

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2025-01-31

**Source:** [Largo and Stryten Energy Finalize Formation of Storion Energy to Strengthen U.S. Energy Resilience and Address Long Duration Energy Storage Needs | Nasdaq](https://www.nasdaq.com/press-release/largo-and-stryten-energy-finalize-formation-storion-energy-strengthen-us-energy)  ·  `page_050593`

## Facts

- Largo Clean Energy Corp. and Stryten Critical E-Storage, LLC finalized the formation of Storion Energy LLC as a joint venture.
- The transaction to establish Storion Energy closed effectively on Friday, January 31, 2025.
- Storion focuses on removing barriers to entry for battery manufacturers by providing domestically sourced, price-competitive components for the flow battery industry, including vanadium electrolyte.
- Each of Largo Clean Energy Corp. and Stryten holds a 50% equity interest in Storion Energy.
- Stryten has paid US$1 million in cash directly to Largo and will contribute US$6 million over time to fund Storion’s operations.
- Storion’s board composition will be generally proportional to ownership, with Stryten holding one additional seat.
- Largo amended its Safekeeping and Supply Agreements with LPV and assigned its Safekeeping Agreement to Storion.
- Largo and Storion entered into a separate supply agreement granting Storion a right of first offer for vanadium products.
- Storion intends to establish a fully integrated, domestic vertical supply chain for utility-scale vanadium redox flow battery (VRFB) long-duration energy storage (LDES) solutions in the U.S.
- VRFB technology is described as a safe and reliable option for Battery Energy Storage Systems (BESS) that need to provide energy storage of four or more hours.
- Storion has locations in Alpharetta, Georgia and Wilmington, Massachusetts.
- Stryten Energy is headquartered in Alpharetta, Georgia, and provides energy storage solutions across Essential Power, Motive Power, Transportation, Military, and Government sectors.
- Stryten’s stored energy technologies include advanced lead, lithium and vanadium redox flow batteries, intelligent chargers and energy performance management software.
- Francesco D’Alessio, President of Largo Clean Energy Corp., stated that the transaction closing represents a transformational step for Largo to integrate into the fast-growing vanadium flow battery market.
- Through Storion, Largo is establishing a fully integrated vanadium electrolyte supply chain in North America to address key challenges for vanadium flow battery adoption.
- Largo Inc. is one of the world’s largest primary vanadium producers, sourcing high-quality vanadium and ilmenite from its Maracás Menchen Mine in Brazil.
- Largo maintains a 50% ownership stake in Storion Energy to strategically invest in the clean energy storage sector.
- LPV aims to actively supply vanadium to end users of vanadium flow batteries to advance renewable energy integration in long duration storage.
- The partnership leverages Largo’s high-quality vanadium supply, LPV’s innovative electrolyte leasing model, and Stryten’s U.S.-based manufacturing expertise to enhance grid stability and energy security.
