# Korea Trade-Investment Promotion Agency (KOTRA)

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2022-03-31

**Source:** [Korea Trade-Investment Promotion Agency Opens First Atlanta Office | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-releases/2022/korea-trade-investment-promotion-agency-kotra-opens-first-atlanta-office)  ·  `page_002160`

## Facts

- KOTRA opened its first Atlanta office on March 31, 2022.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) Commissioner Pat Wilson joined Mr. Soo Deuk Sohn, Senior Executive Vice President of KOTRA, to announce the new office.
- KOTRA is the Government of Korea’s trade and investment organization.
- GDEcD and KOTRA signed a Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) to support each other’s services and networks and promote cooperation in economic relations, trade, and investment between Georgia and Korea.
- Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Korea is an established strategic market for investment, trade, and tourism for the State of Georgia.
- Existing connectivity includes daily direct flights between Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport and Incheon International Airport.
- Long-standing agreements exist between the Georgia ports of Savannah and Brunswick and five Korean ports.
- These connections facilitate joint efforts to create an innovative electric mobility ecosystem in Georgia.
- In early March 2022, Commissioner Wilson met with KOTRA President and CEO Mr. Jeoung Yeol Yu during a mission to Korea.
- The mission included meetings with key business representatives from Kia and SK Group.
- The mission re-confirmed the State of Georgia’s commitment to its Korean companies and international partners.
- KOTRA Atlanta will serve as an outpost to support Korean businesses in the Southeastern United States.
- Mr. Soo Deuk Sohn stated that KOTRA will expand cooperation in many industrial fields between Korea and the U.S. and support Korean companies’ local investments.
- KOTRA acts as the first point of contact for Korean companies settling in new markets like Georgia, providing investment and support resources including information on state or local agency programs.
- The new KOTRA office is located at 950 East Paces Ferry Road NE, Suite 999 in Atlanta.
- The office will host teams dedicated to facilitating trade and investment opportunities between Georgia and Korea.
- Atlanta will be the eighth overseas KOTRA office in the United States and the 10th in North America.
- The Republic of Korea has maintained a consular presence in Georgia since 1971.
- Mr. Yoonjoo Park has served as Consul General since June 2021.
- The State of Georgia has maintained continuous representation in Korea since 1985 with an office in Seoul headed by Managing Director Peter Underwood.
- The state also employs a dedicated Georgia-based director of Korean Investment, Yoonie Kim.
- Several Georgia companies including American Megatrends, First Advantage Corporation, InComm, Novelis, Unicoi Systems, and UPS have operations in Korea.
- Georgia is home to at least 100 Korean facilities.
- These Korean-affiliated companies currently employ more than 9,400 Georgians.
- Notable Korean-affiliated companies in Georgia include Kia, SK Battery America, Sangsin Technology America, and Q CELLS Korea.
- Since 2020, numerous Korean companies have announced projects in Georgia.
- SK Group subsidiary company SKC will manufacture glass-based substrates for semiconductor chips in Covington along with several business partners.
- SUNGDO ENG USA announced its corporate U.S. headquarters in the state and provides highly technical construction services to many Georgia-based clients.
- Duckyang, an electric mobility parts and storage systems supplier for SK Group, is opening a new facility in Jackson County.
- Brake pad supplier KB Autosys will open its first U.S. advanced manufacturing facility in west Georgia to serve regional customers such as GM, Hyundai, and Kia.
- The Georgia Ports Authority (GPA) in Savannah offers seven regular shipping services to the Port of Busan.
- GPA’s Brunswick Port offers shipping services to Incheon, Masan, Pyongtaek, and Kunsan.
- In 2021, Georgia exports to Korea totaled $860.59 million.
- Korea is the 10th largest export market for Georgia.
- Top exports from Georgia to Korea include gas turbines, aircraft, medical instruments, chemical woodpulp, and chemical analysis instruments.
- In 2021, Georgia imports from Korea totaled $8.83 billion.
- Korea ranks third among Georgia’s importer nations.
- Top imports from Korea to Georgia include motor vehicles, tractor parts, bulldozers, engines, and fork-lift trucks.
- KOTRA was established in 1962 to contribute to the national economy by promoting trade, facilitating investment between domestic and foreign companies, and supporting industrial technology cooperation.
- KOTRA is headquartered in Seoul but operates over 127 Overseas Trade Centers in 84 countries.
