# DSP I-16 Logistics Center

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [News | Logistics building deal near Georgia Hyundai Metaplant sets new standard](https://www.costar.com/article/553358601/logistics-building-deal-near-georgia-hyundai-metaplant-sets-new-standard)  ·  `page_001445`

## Facts

- MetLife purchased an industrial building one mile from Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America near Savannah, Georgia, on behalf of a Samsung affiliate.
- The purchase set a new benchmark for sales of its property type.
- In September, MetLife Investment Management paid $72 million for the DSP I-16 Logistics Center.
- The purchase price equated to $131.19 per square foot.
- The property is located adjacent to the Interstate 16-U.S. 280 interchange in Ellabell, Georgia.
- Hyundai Metaplant plans to build more than 500,000 electric and hybrid Hyundai, Genesis and Kia vehicles.
- Samsung wanted to invest in properties with connections to the Hyundai automaker's facility because both are large South Korean conglomerates.
- While the building was under contract, Ecoplastic America struck a deal for the remaining 117,692 square feet at the DSP I-16 Logistics Center.
- The building is now 100% leased.
- Ecoplastic America is a subsidiary of South Korea-based Seco Ecoplastic.
- Ecoplastic America manufactures plastic automobile parts including bumpers, consoles and trims for Hyundai and other automakers.
- Ecoplastic America has a 400,000-square-foot plant in Bulloch County, Georgia, near the Metaplant.
- The sale reset per-square-foot pricing for prime-location properties of more than 500,000 square feet along the I-16 growth corridor.
- The deal won a CoStar Impact Award as judged by a panel of local industry professionals.
- MetLife executed the acquisition on behalf of its Samsung separate account.
- The price paid serves as the record for 36'-clear speculative product in the market.
- The transaction validates underwriting centered on rent growth, renewal and expansion probabilities.
- Newmark Southeast Research Manager Neil Matthee stated that setting new price-per-square-foot metrics draws attention and can be a catalyst for more institutional capital to the market.
- Colliers analyst Andrew Harry said the acquisition enhances the emerging EV supply chain ecosystem, anchored by Hyundai's 17 MSF Metaplant and supported by suppliers such as Ecoplastic America.
- Ecoplastic America expanded to 253,807 SF just prior to closing.
- The property was initially marketed at 79% occupancy.
- Ecoplastic America leased the remaining 117,692 SF after award but before closing, representing an uncommon mid-transaction stabilization that strengthened pricing.
- Georgia Southern University professor Michael Toma said the submarket is newly emerging and the transaction highlights the importance of the Hyundai Metaplant on the region's logistics industry.
- The JLL team serving as listing brokers included Britton Burdette (senior managing director), Dennis Mitchell (senior managing director), Jim Freeman (director of industrial capital markets), Bobby Norwood (managing director), and Maggie Holmes Dominguez (managing director).
