# Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution Battery Cell Manufacturing Joint Venture

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-05-26

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution to Establish Battery Cell Manufacturing Joint Venture in the U.S. | Hyundai Motor Group](https://www.hyundaimotorgroup.com/en/news/CONT0000000000093783)  ·  `page_000199`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution announced an EV battery cell manufacturing joint venture in the U.S. on May 26, 2023.
- Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution signed a memorandum of understanding to produce EV batteries in the U.S. to accelerate electrification efforts in North America.
- The signing ceremony took place at LG Energy Solution’s headquarters in Seoul on May 26, 2023.
- Jaehoon Chang, President and CEO of Hyundai Motor Company, and Youngsoo Kwon, CEO of LG Energy Solution, attended the signing ceremony.
- Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution will each hold a 50 percent stake in the joint venture.
- The joint venture involves an investment of over USD 4.3 billion (KRW 5.7 trillion).
- Jaehoon Chang, President and CEO of Hyundai Motor Company, stated that the company is focusing on electrification efforts to secure a global auto industry leadership position and will create a strong foundation for the global EV transition through the new battery cell plant with LG Energy Solution.
- Youngsoo Kwon, CEO of LG Energy Solution, stated that both companies are ready to drive the EV transition in America and will commit to offering ultimate sustainable energy solutions by advancing product competitiveness and global operational expertise.
- The joint venture will have an annual production capacity of 30 GWh.
- The facility's capacity will be able to support the production of 300,000 units of EVs annually.
- The facility will be located in Bryan County, Savannah, Georgia.
- The new facility will be adjacent to Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America, which is currently under construction.
- Construction on the joint venture facility is scheduled to start in the second half of 2023.
- The joint venture plans to start battery production at the end of 2025 at the earliest.
- Hyundai Mobis will assemble battery packs using cells from the new plant.
- The assembled battery packs will be supplied to Hyundai Motor Group’s U.S. manufacturing facilities for the production of Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis EV models.
- The new facility aims to create a stable regional supply of batteries and allow the group to respond quickly to soaring EV demand in the U.S. market.
- With this joint venture, LG Energy Solution now has seven battery plants currently operating or under construction in the U.S.
- LG Energy Solution is concentrating most of its resources on expanding production capacity in the U.S.
- LG Energy Solution aims to provide innovative products both in scale and with speed to expedite the clean energy transition in the U.S.
- Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution have long been partners in electrification, having previously supplied EV batteries for vehicles including the Elantra Hybrid, Kona Electric, and IONIQ 6 dedicated EV.
- The Hyundai Elantra Hybrid, an LPi hybrid vehicle introduced in 2009, was Hyundai Motor Group’s first electrified model.
- In 2021, Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution started construction of a battery cell joint venture in Indonesia, which is set to start production in the first half of 2024.
- The companies aim to further strengthen their ties going forward with continued EV battery cooperation.
