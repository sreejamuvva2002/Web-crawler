## Facts

- The global active smart glass market is driven by the increasing use of smart glass in luxury and electric vehicles.
- On July 14, 2025, Gauzy Ltd. in collaboration with Research Frontiers announced the first customer delivery of General Motors’ Cadillac CELESTIQ, its first fully electric vehicle sedan featuring patented Suspended Particle Device (SPD) smart glass technology supplied by Gauzy.
- Car manufacturers are increasingly turning to active smart glass for sunroofs, side windows, rear windows and panoramic sunroofs.
- Active smart glass technology helps make passengers more comfortable, reduces heat build-up in the cabin and makes the car more energy efficient, which is especially crucial for electric vehicles.
- Automakers are seeking innovative features to differentiate premium and next-generation mobility platforms.
- On January 9, 2025, Gauzy Ltd. unveiled its latest suspended particle device (SPD) Smart Glass technology at CES 2025, created to accommodate demand from the automotive, aeronautics, and architecture industries.
- On January 7, 2025, Gentex Corporation announced that it is set to debut a host of “next-gen” products, including display-integrated dimmable sun visors, digital rearview mirrors with OLED displays, mirror-integrated driver and in-cabin monitoring systems, and enhancements to HomeLink.
- Demand for active smart glass in Germany is being driven by the automotive industry as premium vehicle manufacturers integrate switchable glass technology into panoramic roofs and glazing systems for luxury vehicles to improve comfort and energy management for passengers.
- The proliferation of intelligent transportation systems and premium automotive applications is likely to generate strong growth possibilities for active smart glass.
