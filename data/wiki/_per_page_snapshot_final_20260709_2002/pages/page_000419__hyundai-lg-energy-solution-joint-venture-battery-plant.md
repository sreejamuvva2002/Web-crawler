# Hyundai-LG Energy Solution Joint Venture Battery Plant

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-05

**Source:** [475 people detained in ICE raid on Hyundai, LGES  EV battery factory in Georgia on Thursday – KonnieMoments](https://konniemoments.com/2025/09/05/475-people-detained-in-ice-raid-on-hyundai-lges-ev-battery-factory-in-georgia-on-thursday/)  ·  `page_000419`

## Facts

- 475 people were detained in an ICE raid on Thursday, September 4 at a factory manufacturing batteries for Hyundai and Kia vehicles.
- The facility is located in Ellabell, Georgia.
- Multiple federal agencies, including ICE, FBI, and DEA, conducted the judicially authorized enforcement operation.
- Steven Schrank, DHS Special Agent in charge of Investigations for Georgia, stated that authorities are actively conducting an investigation into unlawful employment practices.
- The $4.3 billion plant is a joint venture between South Korean auto giant Hyundai Motor and battery manufacturer LG Energy Solution (LGES).
- Each company holds a 50% stake in the plant, which will supply batteries for Hyundai and Kia vehicles.
- The facility is part of Hyundai's broader $12.6 billion investment in Georgia.
- South Korean media identified 300 of the detainees as South Korean nationals, with some reports citing a higher figure of 560 total detainees.
- South Korea’s foreign ministry expressed concern about the impact of the raid on its citizens and companies investing in the United States.
- Lee Jae-woong, a spokesperson for South Korea’s foreign ministry, stated that the economic activities of Korean companies in the US must not be unduly violated during US law enforcement operations.
- The raid immediately stopped construction at the facility.
- Governor Brian Kemp had previously hailed the project as a huge boost for the state's economy and the biggest industrial investment in Georgia's history.
- Social media video footage showed officers informing workers they had a search warrant for the whole site and needed all work to cease immediately.
- The Atlanta office of the ATF confirmed at least 475 people were arrested.
- An ICE official at a Friday press conference stated that a majority of the 475 arrested were Korean nationals.
- Detainees are being held at a facility in Folkston, Georgia, until the agency decides their next destination.
- The raid is seen as a setback to Hyundai's substantial investment in the US and a dramatic iteration of the Trump administration’s crackdown on immigration.
- Observers note latent impact on the inflow of foreign investment due to the administration's immigration stance.
- The battery production facility was originally scheduled to start operations at the end of this year according to LGES.
- In 2023, Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution announced the $4.3 billion venture.
- Hyundai-GA battery company announced full cooperation with authorities and confirmed construction work is paused.
- Hyundai Motor stated that its production of electric vehicles at other sprawling sites was not affected by the raid.
- The project was announced in March amidst a ramping up of tariffs by the Trump administration.
