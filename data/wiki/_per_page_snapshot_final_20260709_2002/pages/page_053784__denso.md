# DENSO

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2013-01-15

**Source:** [DENSO To Invest Nearly $1 Billion In North America Over Next Four Years - AftermarketNews](https://www.aftermarketnews.com/denso-to-invest-nearly-1-billion-in-north-america-over-next-four-years/)  ·  `page_053784`

## Facts

- DENSO is investing nearly $1 billion in North America over the next four years.
- The investment will result in more than 2,000 jobs across the region.
- The investment will allow DENSO to better support its North American customers, as well as expand new business areas and localize products, many of which will help automakers meet upcoming fuel requirements.
- More than $750 million will be invested in the United States alone.
- More than 1,200 jobs will be created in the United States.
- Terry Helgesen serves as senior vice president of industry relations at DENSO International America Inc.
- DENSO announced plans to drastically localize products and critical tooling in the regions where it operates.
- In the U.S., DENSO is looking at investing more than $750 million in Michigan, Tennessee, Iowa, California, and North Carolina.
- The U.S. investment represents an increase in research and development, an expansion in existing production lines, the creation of new production lines, and the opening of a new assembly and warehouse facility to support heavy-duty customers.
- DENSO is investing in making more of its own manufacturing equipment and dies within the states.
- A building has been dedicated at DENSO Manufacturing Tennessee in Maryville, Tennessee, for proprietary machinery, serving as the regional headquarters for manufacturing machinery and dies.
- DENSO plans to make all of its critical manufacturing equipment in North America in the future.
- The company has sourced several million dollars of business to American machine shops, which will positively impact communities and create additional jobs.
- Currently, DENSO employs more than 14,000 people at 28 consolidated companies and affiliates in North America.
- Of these North American entities, 26 are manufacturing facilities located in the U.S., Canada, and Mexico.
- In the U.S. alone, DENSO employs more than 10,000 people across California, Michigan, North Carolina, South Carolina, Tennessee, Kentucky, Georgia, Iowa, Ohio, and Arkansas.
