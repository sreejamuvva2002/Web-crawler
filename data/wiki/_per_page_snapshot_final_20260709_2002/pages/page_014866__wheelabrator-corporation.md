# Wheelabrator Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [page_014866](https://webhome.auburn.edu/~uzumemv/research/wheelabrator/whlhist.html)  ·  `page_014866`

## Facts

- Wheelabrator started in the foundry industry, where cast parts are often cleaned by shot or sand (abrasive) blasting.
- In 1932, The American Foundry Equipment Company located in Cleveland, Ohio developed and marketed an airless abrasive blasting machine to foundries, which became known as the Wheelabrator.
- The machines use a rotating wheel to sling shot into parts.
- The entire process is contained in a cabinet, greatly improving factory working conditions and enabling automatic handling of parts.
- Shortly after the airless centrifugal blast wheel machine was developed, the company moved from Cleveland, Ohio to Mishiwaka, Indiana.
- By the 1970s, the company had grown to 1500 employees and operated a 600,000 square foot facility.
- In 1983, the shot-making capability was moved to Bedford, Virginia.
- In 1984, the foundry was relocated to Walterboro, South Carolina.
- The Indiana plant was closed in the mid-1980s because it was too old and large for the volume of business, and the equipment business moved to Georgia.
- The company was divided into equipment assembly, which moved to Shenandoah (Newnan) Georgia, and manufacturing, which moved to LaGrange, Georgia.
- By 1991, the company consolidated its Georgia plants in LaGrange, except for Blastrac machines which are still built in Newnan.
- The company builds many different kinds of airless blast machines for foundries, aircraft and automotive industries, and equipment for the environmental industry.
- The firm's primary US location is in LaGrange, Georgia.
- Wheelabrator builds its wheelabrator machines in LaGrange and a portable blasting machine called the Blastrac in Newnan, Georgia.
- They are affiliated with a foundry in Walterboro, South Carolina (formerly a sister company) that produces cast parts, white iron and steel parts for the wheelabrator machines.
- They also produce equipment and parts, including dust collector systems for the wheelabrator machines, in a small plant in Burlington, Ontario, Canada.
- The company sells about 70 to 100 machines per year, of which about 30% are standard and sold by distributors.
- A Tumblast machine with fancy load and unload system sells for $80 to 90,000 while a small machine might be $25,000.
- Wheelabrator is developing a new machine called the Ultrablast which uses a steel flighted mill to transport parts through the blast chamber for continuous operation rather than batch processing.
