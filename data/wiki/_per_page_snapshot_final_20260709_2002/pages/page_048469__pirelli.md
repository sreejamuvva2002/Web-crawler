# Pirelli

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-05

**Source:** [Pirelli Will Start Cyber Tyre Production in Georgia - Rome CEO](https://romeceo.com/news/2026/05/pirelli-will-start-cyber-tyre-production-georgia-underlining-strategic-importance-us-market/)  ·  `page_048469`

## Facts

- On Thursday, May 7th, 2026, Pirelli announced it will start production of connected tires featuring Cyber™ Tyre technology at its Rome, Georgia plant.
- The Rome, Georgia plant is already dedicated to the most technologically advanced products for the U.S. market in the High-Value segment and Motorsport, and will be further enhanced by the new production line.
- Cyber™ Tyre is described as the world's first hardware-and-software system capable of collecting data and information from sensors embedded in tires and processing them through Pirelli's proprietary software and algorithms.
- The technology communicates in real time with the vehicle's electronics to enable new functionalities integrated with driving and control systems, enhancing the driving experience, increasing safety levels, and supporting connected infrastructure.
- The announcement coincided with Pirelli's participation in the SelectUSA Investment Summit, a flagship event promoted by the U.S. Department of Commerce, where Cyber™ Tyre was showcased as a core innovation shaping smart mobility.
- At the summit, Cyber™ Tyre was showcased at the Georgia State booth, highlighting Pirelli's industrial footprint in the state, where it has been established for more than 20 years.
- Claudio Zanardo, CEO of Pirelli North America, stated that the start of Cyber™ Tyre production in the Rome, Georgia plant is a significant milestone for Pirelli in the country and reflects its commitment to bringing advanced technologies closer to the market while strengthening its industrial footprint and innovation capabilities in the United States.
- Pirelli is finalizing the introduction of the latest version of the MIRS (Modular Integrated Robotized System) production process at the Rome, Georgia facility.
- The MIRS process will be exclusive to the Georgia factory and represents the most advanced manufacturing process for high-end, premium production within the entire Pirelli Group.
- This highly digitalized system enables a direct link between product design and industrial application while increasing productivity, flexibility, and quality.
- The development lays the groundwork for growth in Pirelli's production capacity in Rome, which is an integral part of the Group's industrial development plans and strengthens its presence in the United States, one of its most important strategic markets.
- The Rome, Georgia plant specializes in high-value tire production for the North American market and hosts a dedicated R&D center to strengthen technological development and product innovation.
- The facility is recognized for responsible sourcing practices, including the use of FSC® (Forest Stewardship Council®)-certified natural rubber, underscoring the company's broader environmental commitment across its supply chain.
