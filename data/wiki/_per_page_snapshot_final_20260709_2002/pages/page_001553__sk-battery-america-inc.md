# SK Battery America Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-03-06

**Source:** [SK lays off nearly 1,000 workers at Georgia plant amid cooling automaker EV plans - Newsday](https://www.newsday.com/business/georgia-electric-vehicle-battery-manufacturing-layoffs-workers-i51948)  ·  `page_001553`

## Facts

- SK Battery America Inc. laid off nearly 1,000 workers at its manufacturing plant northeast of Atlanta on Friday.
- The last working day for 958 plant employees was Friday, representing about 37% of the company's workforce.
- Impacted workers will be paid through May 6.
- The Commerce, Georgia plant will continue to employ about 1,600 workers.
- Human resources chief Chuck Moore filed a Worker Adjustment and Retraining Notification (WARN) notice regarding the layoffs.
- SK opened the $2.6 billion battery plant in Commerce, Georgia, in January 2022.
- The plant notably supplied batteries for the Ford F-150 Lightning electric pickup truck before Ford announced plans to cancel its fully electric version in December.
- SK Americas spokesperson Joe Guy Collier stated the workforce reduction was made to align operations with market conditions.
- Collier said: “SK Battery America remains committed to Georgia and to building a robust U.S. supply chain for advanced battery manufacturing.”
- Collier added that the company is pursuing future customers, including in the Battery Energy Storage System arena.
- The City of Commerce and Jackson County commission chair did not immediately respond to requests for comment.
- Georgia U.S. Sen. Jon Ossoff stated: “Let’s be clear: these were battery manufacturing jobs and now they’re gone.”
- Ossoff added that Trump’s policies are hurting Georgia’s economy and that Georgians are losing jobs after a period of booming plant construction.
- SK had announced in June 2020 plans to invest $940 million to expand its battery manufacturing presence in Atlanta.
- At the time, Gov. Brian Kemp’s office stated the expansion would create 600 jobs.
- SK and Hyundai are still jointly building a $5 billion battery factory near Cartersville, northwest of Atlanta.
- Georgia has attracted other massive EV manufacturing investments, including Rivian’s $5 billion factory and Hyundai’s own $7.6 billion factory complex.
- Georgia benefited significantly from Biden’s Inflation Reduction Act, with 33 additional green energy projects announced by the end of 2024, the most nationwide according to E2.
- Projects in Georgia topped $20 billion in value, pledging more than 25,000 jobs.
- Qcells, a unit of South Korea’s Hanwha Solutions, resumed normal production on Friday after temporarily reducing hours and pay due to U.S. customs detaining imported solar panel components.
- A spokesperson for Kemp's office stated that Georgia’s job market remains robust, with over 219,700 new jobs announced since 2019.
- EVs accounted for about 8% of new vehicle sales in the U.S. in 2025, remaining similar to a year earlier.
- Automakers including Ford, General Motors, and Stellantis have reneged on factory, investment, and product plans, laid off workers, and pivoted some efforts to hybrid and plug-in hybrid electric vehicles.
- Under President Donald Trump, Congress eliminated tax credits of up to $7,500 for consumers' purchases of new or used EVs.
- The Trump administration announced plans to weaken fuel economy and greenhouse gas emissions rules for automakers, eliminating federal incentives for cleaner vehicle fleets.
