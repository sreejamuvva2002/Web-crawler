## Facts

- Volvo may turn more of its assembly line in the New River Valley over to robots in the coming years.
- Nearly 3,000 humans still do most of the complex jobs at the Volvo plant in Dublin.
- Magnus Koeck is vice president of strategy and marketing at Volvo trucks North America.
- Koeck stated that maybe in 15 years they can do everything automated, but they still have lots of manual labor for the final assembly.
- A few years ago, Volvo built a second plant in Dublin.
- Inside the second Dublin plant, 170 robots do simpler tasks like painting truck components and attaching doors to cabs.
- Volvo spent $500 million building the second plant.
- Engineers sit inside part of the facility monitoring the robots’ progress.
- Mehdi Ahmadian is an expert in automotive engineering at Virginia Tech.
- Ahmadian stated that the industry is replacing physical labor with intellectual labor.
- Ahmadian expects to see an acceleration in automation in the auto manufacturing industry in the coming decades due to advancing artificial intelligence.
- Ahmadian advises young people to get real-world experiences and training in AI technology before graduating college.
- Companies like Volvo can help current workers learn new skills needed inside a more automated workplace.
