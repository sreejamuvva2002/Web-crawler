# Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution Joint Venture

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-05-26

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Group, LG Energy to build US$4.3 billion EV battery plant in US - The Business Times](https://www.businesstimes.com.sg/companies-markets/hyundai-motor-group-lg-energy-build-us43-billion-ev-battery-plant-us)  ·  `page_056183`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution (LGES) will build an electric vehicle (EV) battery plant in the United States with a combined investment of US$4.3 billion.
- Construction for the Hyundai-LGES plant in Georgia is scheduled to start in the second half of 2023.
- The plant plans to start battery production at the end of 2025 at the earliest.
- The facility will have an annual production capacity of 30 gigawatt-hours (GWh).
- The plant's capacity is enough to support the production of 300,000 EVs each year.
- Hyundai Motor Group and LGES will hold a 50 per cent stake each in the joint venture.
- The investment follows new US sourcing requirements for EV battery components and critical minerals under the Biden administration’s Inflation Reduction Act (IRA).
- Car buyers can qualify for up to US$7,500 in credits under the IRA if they meet the new sourcing requirements.
- Cars made by Hyundai and Kia are currently not eligible for the tax credits.
