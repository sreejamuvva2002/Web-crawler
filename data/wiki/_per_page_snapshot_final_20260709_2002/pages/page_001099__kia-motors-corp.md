# Kia Motors Corp.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2006-03-13

**Source:** [Kia Motors to set up first U.S. plant](https://www.nbcnews.com/id/wbna11807197)  ·  `page_001099`

## Facts

- Kia Motors Corp., South Korea's second-largest automaker, announced it will set up its first U.S. plant in the southern state of Georgia.
- Production at the new facility will begin in 2009.
- The factory represents a $1.2 billion investment and will employ 2,500 workers, according to Kia's press release.
- Georgia Gov. Sonny Perdue and Kia President and CEO Euisun Chung made the formal announcement at a ceremony in Seoul.
- Locations in Mississippi and Tennessee had also contended for the plant.
- The facility will be located in West Point, Georgia, near the Alabama border.
- The West Point plant is expected to produce 300,000 vehicles per year at maximum capacity.
- Five to six auto suppliers are expected to set up nearby the new plant.
- The nearby supplier presence is expected to create an additional 2,000 jobs.
- Kia manufactures the Optima and Amanti midsize sedans, the small-size Picanto, and the Sorento midsize SUV.
- Kia is an affiliate of South Korea's largest carmaker, Hyundai Motor Co., which operates a factory in Montgomery, Alabama, 120 kilometers (75 miles) away.
- Proximity to the Hyundai site combined with a $258 million incentive package from the state helped sway Kia's decision, according to Georgia state officials.
- Georgia has previously lost plants to Ford Motor Co. and General Motors Corp., largely due to increasing competition from Asian automakers.
- Gov. Sonny Perdue stated that Kia's decision "is a testament to the tools, experience and know-how Georgia will deliver to one of the automotive industry's leading innovators."
- Kia exported three-fourths of its South Korean production last year and is pursuing an aggressive overseas expansion.
- Besides the U.S. plant, Kia is building a second plant in China.
- Kia expects its first European factory to start up in Slovakia at the end of the year-end.
- The company supplies materials to partners for vehicle assembly in Malaysia, Indonesia, Vietnam, Russia, and Iran.
- Kia President and CEO Euisun Chung stated, "Kia Motors has entered an aggressive growth phase in the U.S."
- Kia expects its sales in the United States and Canada to increase 15 percent to 350,000 vehicles in 2006.
- Kia projects its U.S. and Canadian sales will grow further to 800,000 by 2010.
- Kia Motors America posted record sales of 20,719 vehicles for February.
- Kia's February sales pace was 3.2 percent ahead of its record-setting 2005 sales pace.
- In January, Kia reported its 2005 net profit rose 2.9 percent to 680.9 billion won ($696.5 million).
- Kia's 2005 revenue gained 4.6 percent to 16 trillion won ($16.4 billion).
- Kia sold 1.27 million vehicles worldwide in 2005, which was 13.9 percent more than the previous year.
- Kia has invested more than $300 million in the United States over the last four years.
- Kia's U.S. facilities include a research and development center in Ann Arbor, Michigan.
- Kia operates vehicle proving grounds in Mojave, California.
- Kia shares what it calls the largest automotive design studio in California with Hyundai Motor America.
- The company is currently constructing a $87 million U.S. corporate campus in Irvine, California.
- The Irvine corporate campus is expected to open in December 2006 and will include a design center.
