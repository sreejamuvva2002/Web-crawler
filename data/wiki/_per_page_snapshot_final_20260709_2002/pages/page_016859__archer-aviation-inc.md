# Archer Aviation Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-12-31

**Source:** [Archer Announces Fourth Quarter & Full Year 2024 Results, Sets Plans For Midnight Commercial Deployments Ahead Of FAA Certification | Nasdaq](https://www.nasdaq.com/press-release/archer-announces-fourth-quarter-full-year-2024-results-sets-plans-midnight-commercial)  ·  `page_016859`

## Facts

- Archer is starting production of its first Midnight aircraft at its ARC facility in Covington, GA.
- Archer plans to build up to 10 Midnight aircraft this year to support ongoing certification testing programs and deployments with key partners.
- Abu Dhabi Aviation (ADA) will be Archer’s first Launch Edition customer for the Midnight aircraft.
- ADA plans to deploy an initial fleet of Midnight aircraft beginning later this year.
- Archer will provide ADA with a team of pilots, technicians, and engineers to support the initial operational ramp.
- Archer announced an exclusive partnership with Anduril Industries Inc. to jointly develop a hybrid vertical take off and landing (VTOL) aircraft for critical defense applications.
- Archer believes the potential demand for the hybrid VTOL aircraft is stronger than expected.
- Archer expects the partnership with Anduril to result in sizable programs of record without needing FAA certification for these specific aircraft.
- Archer’s total liquidity is over $1 billion, which includes a Q4 2024 ending cash balance of $835 million and $302 million raised through an equity offering in February.
- Archer’s spending in Q4 2024 remained within its guided range and stayed nearly flat for the second straight quarter.
- Archer reported total operating expenses of $124.2 million in Q4 2024 (GAAP) and $509.7 million for FY 2024 (GAAP).
- Archer reported a net loss of $198.1 million in Q4 2024 and $536.8 million for FY 2024.
- Archer held cash and cash equivalents of $834.5 million as of the end of Q4 2024.
- Archer reported an adjusted EBITDA loss of $94.8 million in Q4 2024 (non-GAAP) and $368.9 million for FY 2024 (non-GAAP).
- Archer expects its adjusted EBITDA to be a loss of $95 million to $110 million for the first quarter of 2025.
- Archer’s founder and CEO Adam Goldstein stated the company is on track to deliver its first revenue-generating Midnight aircraft later this year.
- The “Launch Edition” commercialization program aims to establish a pragmatic and repeatable playbook to deploy Midnight commercially in dozens of early adopter markets ahead of FAA type certification.
- Non-cash warrant costs for warrants issued to Stellantis in connection with services they provided are classified as research and development expenses.
- Archer reached technology and dispute resolution agreements on August 10, 2023, with Wisk Aero LLC and The Boeing Company.
