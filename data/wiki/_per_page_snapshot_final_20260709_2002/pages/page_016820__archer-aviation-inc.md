# Archer Aviation Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-11-14

**Source:** [Archer](https://archer.com/news/archer-aviation-selects-site-in-georgia-adjacent-to-the-covington-municipal-airport-for-its-manufacturing-facility)  ·  `page_016820`

## Facts

- On November 14, 2022, Archer Aviation Inc. announced plans to locate its manufacturing facility in Covington, Georgia, adjacent to the Covington Municipal Airport.
- The selection of the site represents a key next step as Archer works towards its goal of being first in the industry to bring up a high-capacity eVTOL production facility.
- The facility will be built on a 96-acre site.
- Archer plans to initially build out a 350,000 square-foot facility capable of producing up to 650 aircraft per year.
- The planned eVTOL aircraft manufacturing facility is expected to create over 1,000 jobs over the long term.
- Construction is expected to be complete by the end of 2023, with initial production expected to begin in 2024.
- Construction is expected to be completed in the first half of 2024 with initial production expected to begin in the second half of 2024.
- The facility will be capable of being expanded by an additional 550,000 square feet, which is estimated to support production of up to 2,300 aircraft per year.
- Archer intends to invest $118M over the next 10 years.
- Archer will receive an incentive package totaling 33% of its capital investment commitment.
- The incentive package includes land conveyance, tax incentives, and a Georgia REBA grant.
- Archer completed a comprehensive survey of potential manufacturing sites across the United States before selecting Georgia.
- Key site selection considerations included local labor market talent availability, utility availability, ability to conduct seamless flight test operations, construction costs, and logistics.
- Archer has entered into an LOI with Synovus Bank to obtain financing for the project.
- Synovus Bank is the largest bank headquartered in Georgia.
- Archer anticipates that Synovus will provide financing for the project.
- Archer CEO Adam Goldstein stated that Georgia was selected after many months of discussion and evaluation.
- Goldstein noted that the U.S. has long led the world in aerospace technology and Georgia has played a vital role in that industry.
- Goldstein stated that Archer's eVTOL technology can transform how urban and rural communities live and commute, and the factory can create pathways to highly skilled manufacturing jobs and other ladders of social and economic opportunity.
- Goldstein expressed gratitude to Governor Kemp along with Bob Kosek, Sara Gershon, Serra Hall, Frank Turner, their teams, and Archer colleagues for their collaboration.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated that Georgia has made nurturing innovation a priority as it works with businesses to define the future.
- Governor Kemp noted that Georgia's established leadership in the aerospace industry was essential to landing Archer Aviation in Newton County.
- Governor Kemp highlighted that Archer can take advantage of Georgia's signature workforce training program Quick Start to help fill over 1,000 long-term jobs in Covington.
- Georgia is home to more than 800 aerospace companies.
- Georgia maintains a robust aerospace education pipeline with a dozen high schools offering field training, five technical colleges with aviation programs, and several universities offering degrees in aerospace engineering.
- The Georgia Center of Innovation for Aerospace, a strategic arm of the Georgia Department of Economic Development, has cultivated an ecosystem of opportunities for innovative companies like Archer for more than 10 years.
- Archer designs and develops electric vertical takeoff and landing (eVTOL) aircraft for use in urban air mobility networks.
- Archer's team is based in Santa Clara, California.
