# Kia Georgia

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-11-21

**Source:** [Kia Georgia adds 2nd electric vehicle to production lineup](https://www.wtvm.com/2024/11/21/kia-georgia-adds-2nd-electric-vehicle-production-lineup/)  ·  `page_000293`

## Facts

- Kia Georgia is adding another electric vehicle to its production lineup.
- The announcement was made Thursday at the Los Angeles Auto Show.
- Company officials said the 2025 EV6 SUV will be assembled by team members at the West Point facility beginning early next year.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated that Kia's decades-long partnership with Georgia has been highly beneficial as a job creator for the state and its people.
- Governor Kemp noted that the partnership helped make Georgia the No. 1 state for business and accelerated its development as the electric mobility capital of America.
- Governor Kemp thanked President Sean Yoon and Stuart Countess, as well as their teams, for the decision and the opportunities it will bring to hardworking Georgians.
- The facility recently completed a $217 million expansion, adding about 200 jobs in Troup County, according to the company.
- The EV6 will join the EV9 SUV and the Telluride, Sorento and Sportage SUVs on the plant’s assembly line.
- Kia North America President and CEO SeungKyu (Sean) Yoon stated that the award-winning EV6 has been an integral part of Kia’s ‘Plan S’ strategy since day one and this enhancement will further the brand’s commitment to electrification.
- Yoon noted that building the EV6 in Georgia is a testament to Kia’s ongoing commitment to the U.S. market and the state of Georgia.
- Yoon highlighted that the facility is the home to previous World Car of the Year winners Telluride and EV9, and expressed confidence that the team will continue its legacy of successful launches with the EV6.
- Kia Georgia employs more than 3,200 team members.
- The past Saturday marked 15 years since the company began manufacturing in Georgia.
