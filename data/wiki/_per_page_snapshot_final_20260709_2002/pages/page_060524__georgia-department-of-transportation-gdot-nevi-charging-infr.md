# Georgia Department of Transportation (GDOT) NEVI Charging Infrastructure Program

**Type:** charging_infrastructure  ·  **Role:** charging_infrastructure  ·  **Published:** 2025-11-24

**Source:** [Georgia gets 26 new DC fast-charging stations with $24.4M of NEVI funds | Electrek](https://electrek.co/2025/11/24/georgia-gets-26-new-dc-fast-charging-stations-with-24-4m-of-nevi-funds/)  ·  `page_060524`

## Facts

- Georgia allocated $24.4 million for 26 new DC fast-charging stations across the state.
- The Georgia Department of Transportation (GDOT) selected private-sector partners to build and operate the new stations along federally designated Alternative Fuel Corridors.
- Each site will feature four DC fast chargers available 24/7 with a minimum output of 150 kW per port.
- The chargers are capable of delivering a full recharge in as little as 20 minutes, depending on the EV.
- This award represents the second round of Georgia’s NEVI funding, following the first round in 2024.
- GDOT mapped out 33 priority sites near highway exits and interchanges in mostly rural areas to close charging gaps left after the first round.
- The EV charging industry submitted 41 proposals to cover 26 of those mapped locations.
- Six private-sector winners were selected for the project: Pilot Travel Centers, Silver Comet Energy, Universal EV, PowerUp America, Love’s Travel Stops, and EnviroSpark Energy Solutions.
- Governor Brian Kemp (R-GA) stated that Georgia appreciates GDOT for fulfilling the state’s commitment to a robust, reliable fast-charging network that meets federal standards and serves communities across Georgia.
- Georgia was allocated approximately $135 million through the NEVI program, which is part of President Joe Biden’s Bipartisan Infrastructure Law.
- The federal NEVI program covers up to 80% of project costs, with private partners responsible for covering the remaining balance.
- Round 2 funding follows a legal battle earlier this year when a lawsuit filed by several states (excluding Georgia) compelled the Trump Administration to release funds owed from the NEVI Formula Program.
- A federal judge blocked the Trump administration’s attempt to obstruct the NEVI program in June, clearing the way for planned NEVI EV charging projects to continue.
