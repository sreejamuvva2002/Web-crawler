# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-09-19

**Source:** [Georgia Tech and Hyundai Motor Group Sign MoU for Future Mobility Collaboration](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/georgia-tech-and-hyundai-motor-group-sign-mou-for-future-mobility-collaboration-301932444.html)  ·  `page_002899`

## Facts

- Georgia Tech and Hyundai Motor Group signed a memorandum of understanding on Sept. 19, 2023.
- The partnership will focus on sustainable mobility, hydrogen economy, workforce development, and making cities smarter and more sustainable.
- Hyundai Motor Group is investing more than $12 billion in EV manufacturing and battery production in Georgia.
- Hyundai Motor Group is investing $12.6 billion in new dedicated electric vehicle and battery manufacturing facilities in Georgia.
- The investment includes the $7.59 billion Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) site in Bryan County, Georgia.
- The HMGMA site will create more than 8,500 direct jobs.
- The investment also includes a $5 billion battery manufacturing facility joint venture with SK On in Bartow County, Georgia.
- The SK On joint venture facility will employ approximately 3,500 people.
- The close proximity of these facilities to Georgia Tech was an important consideration in choosing the locations.
- Hyundai's economic impact in Georgia will be tens of billions of dollars in the years to come.
- Hyundai's investments in Georgia are expected to help create nearly 40,000 jobs.
- A recent study by the Center for Automotive Research reveals that Hyundai Motor's automotive operations contributed $20.1 billion and 190,000 jobs to the U.S. economy in 2021 alone.
- According to the study, Hyundai's investments in Georgia are expected to create nearly 40,000 jobs and $4.6 billion in individual earnings every year.
- The Kia Georgia assembly plant began vehicle production in 2009.
- The Kia plant was established under the leadership of Honorary Chairman Mong-Koo Chung and Executive Chair Euisun Chung.
- University System of Georgia Chancellor Sonny Perdue was Governor of Georgia at the time of the Kia plant opening and attended today's ceremony.
- Georgia Tech President Ángel Cabrera stated that Hyundai is a global brand synonymous with quality, innovation, and a commitment to advancing technology, and that the alignment of values between the organizations has become obvious.
- Jay Chang, President and CEO of Hyundai Motor Company, stated that Georgia is like a second home to Hyundai and that the partnership includes research, hydrogen economy, workforce development, and smart cities cooperation.
- Jay Chang noted that this partnership is one of the reasons Hyundai chose Georgia for its EV investments.
- José Muñoz, President and Global COO of Hyundai Motor Company and President and CEO of Hyundai and Genesis Motor North America, stated that Hyundai's investments will create tens of thousands of jobs and bring tens of billions of dollars in economic impact for decades to come.
- José Muñoz noted that proximity to institutions like Georgia Tech was one of the many reasons Hyundai selected Georgia for its new EV manufacturing facility.
- The partnership envisions zero emissions hydrogen-powered vehicles on campus, advanced air mobility shuttling to Hartsfield International Airport, and hands-free autonomous vehicles on I-75 and I-85.
- Under the research and innovation element, Hyundai will collaborate with Georgia Tech on joint R&D programs focused on sustainable mobility, including battery technology and expanding the hydrogen economy.
- Under workforce development, Georgia Tech and Hyundai will create technical training and leadership development programming for Hyundai employees.
- The partnership will initiate engagement activities to stimulate interest in STEM degrees among students.
- Under student engagement, Hyundai will actively participate in career recruitment events at Georgia Tech and create learning programs for undergrad and graduate students.
- Hyundai's partnership includes field-naming recognition at Bobby Dodd Stadium, which is now known as Bobby Dodd Stadium at Hyundai Field.
- The partnership provides a vital pathway for Georgia Tech to expand its stature as a leading research entity in automotive electrification through world-class hydrogen research.
- The partnership offers an opportunity for Georgia Tech to contribute to Georgia's commitment as a hub for EV production.
- The partnership will generate new revenue for Georgia Tech Athletics, providing resources for student-athletes and teams.
- Georgia Tech conducts more than $1 billion in research annually for government, industry, and society.
- Hyundai Motor Group has about 250,000 employees worldwide and its mobility brands include Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis.
