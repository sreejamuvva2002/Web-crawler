# Aldora Mills Textile Manufacturing Plant

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2025-11-14

**Source:** [ALDORA MILLS SHUTTING DOWN - Barnesville.com](https://barnesville.com/aldora-mills-shutting-down/)  ·  `page_018630`

## Facts

- Barnesville-Lamar County will lose a major employer in late 2026 when Continental Tires shuts down its Aldora Mills operation.
- Aldora celebrated 136 years of local operations in May prior to the shutdown announcement.
- A meeting between company officials and local leaders was scheduled for November 14, 2025, to provide more information on the shutdown.
- Continental announced its decision to discontinue operations at the Aldora Mills textile manufacturing plant in Barnesville, Georgia, by the end of 2026.
- The Aldora Mills facility employs approximately 230 people.
- The facility produces textile reinforcement materials, including tire cord fabric, hose yarn, and knitted fabric.
- The produced materials are used exclusively within Continental’s Tires and ContiTech group sectors.
- Continental will support affected employees during the transition by offering career counseling and access to employment resources in collaboration with local partners.
- Continental acquired the Aldora Mills facility in 1988.
- The decision to shut down the plant follows a comprehensive business review aimed at safeguarding profitability, competitiveness, and business performance in the Americas region.
- The plant has not been cost-competitive for many years despite continuous turnaround efforts.
- Ongoing changes in global market dynamics and supply chains necessitated the shutdown decision.
- Continental reaffirms its commitment to customers and partners in the United States.
- Continental operates tire manufacturing facilities in Plymouth (Indiana), Clinton (Mississippi), Sumter (South Carolina), and Mount Vernon (Illinois).
- Continental’s U.S. tire plants have an annual production capacity of more than 16 million tires.
- Continental employs more than 8,800 people in the U.S. across its Tires and ContiTech group sectors.
- In the last decade alone, Continental has invested around 1.5 billion USD in its tire manufacturing operations in the United States.
- In October (2025), Continental announced a more than 85 million USD investment to expand manufacturing operations at its ContiTech plant in Mount Pleasant, Iowa.
- In September 2024, Continental broke ground on its first fully owned tire distribution center in the Dallas–Fort Worth metroplex, scheduled to open in early 2026.
- The new Dallas–Fort Worth distribution center will complement seven partner-owned distribution centers currently serving the U.S. market.
