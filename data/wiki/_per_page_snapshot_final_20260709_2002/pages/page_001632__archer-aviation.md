# Archer Aviation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Innovative Archer Aviation Has Landed in Georgia – Site Selection Magazine](https://siteselection.com/innovative-archer-aviation-has-landed-in-georgia/)  ·  `page_001632`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation is designing and building the Midnight, an electric takeoff and landing aircraft (eVTOL) with 12 propellers that takes off and lands like a helicopter and flies like a plane.
- The company envisions using the Midnight as a rideshare or air taxi.
- Tosha Perkins, Archer’s chief people and partnerships officer, stated that one of the design goals was to create something exciting and fun to get into.
- Georgia offered an aggressive incentives package that heavily influenced Archer’s decision to build the Midnight east of Atlanta.
- The incentive package is worth about one-third of the company’s pledged $118 capital investment.
- The capital investment is intended to create 1,000 incentivized jobs.
- Georgia’s location was selected over a final contender site in Dallas-Fort Worth.
- Georgia already hosts approximately 800 aerospace companies and has a growing electric vehicle sector.
- Tosha Perkins cited the talent pool as the number one reason for choosing Georgia, specifically noting the availability of general talent within aerospace, automotive, electric vehicle, and general manufacturing.
- The selected site is adjacent to a municipal airport in Covington.
- Georgia’s generally benign weather was cited as advantageous for testing the aircraft.
- Perkins highlighted that relationships in Georgia and their willingness to assist with training and workforce development were critical factors beyond the financial incentives.
- Technical colleges and other organizations in Georgia came together to provide Archer with necessary support outside of the incentive package.
- The Archer facility will consist of three buildings spread across approximately 100 acres.
- The company’s target move-in date for the facility is the end of the year.
- Commercialization of the Midnight aircraft is targeted for 2025.
- Archer has partnered with a local chapter of the Future Farmers of America to repurpose trees cleared from the site into furniture for the new facility.
- Perkins noted that Georgia went beyond the incentive package by offering connections and support aligned with Archer’s identity as a sustainable company.
- Archer Aviation has raised more than $1 billion but has not yet turned a profit.
- Tosha Perkins spoke with Site Selection from Santa Clara, which is Archer’s current headquarters.
