# Georgia Electric Mobility and Innovation Alliance

**Type:** Government Program  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[unknown|Uncategorized]]  ·  **Location:** Georgia, United States

## Overview

The Georgia Electric Mobility and Innovation Alliance (EMIA) is a state-led partnership of private and public sector stakeholders, government agencies, electric utilities, education institutions, nonprofits and other entities created to expand Georgia’s electric mobility ecosystem and strengthen its role in electrification-related manufacturing and innovation.

## EV supply-chain relevance

EMIA coordinates efforts across the state to grow EV charging infrastructure, battery production, autonomous vehicle testing, and workforce development, directly supporting Georgia’s electric-vehicle supply chain.

## Key facts

- Georgia has 1,200 miles of federally designated EV-ready Alternative Fuel Corridors and over 2,400 charging stations according to the Department of Energy.
- Georgia Tech ranks No. 1 for Energy among public universities and in the top 5 for materials science and engineering (U.S. News & World Report).
- Port of Brunswick is the leader in roll-on/roll-off cargo in the United States, per Georgia Ports Authority.
- EnviroSpark works across public and private sectors to make charging accessible to EV drivers nationwide.
- The Electric Highway Coalition, a partnership of six major energy companies including Southern Company, aims to connect drivers to a seamless network of charging stations along major highways.
- The Ray Intelligent Highway provides an 18-mile real-world testing facility on Interstate 85 for safe and renewable transportation technology.
- City of Chamblee partnered with Georgia Tech to identify how Shared Autonomous Vehicles could affect public transit usage.
- Gwinnett County and the City of Woodstock are planning for autonomous-vehicle impacts on streets and highways.
- Curiosity Labs in Peachtree Corners operates a 1.5-mile intelligent highway, all-electric autonomous shuttles, e-scooters, and a solar roadway to gather real-time data on emerging mobility technologies.
- MAGNET is a cooperative linking Georgia with Alabama and Mississippi universities and power utilities to advance a regional advanced automotive manufacturing ecosystem.
