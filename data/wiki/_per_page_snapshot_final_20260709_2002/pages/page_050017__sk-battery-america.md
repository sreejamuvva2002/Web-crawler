# SK Battery America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [SK On's Georgia Plant to Supply EV Batteries to Hyundai Motor Starting October - Businesskorea](https://www.businesskorea.co.kr/news/articleView.html?idxno=225488)  ·  `page_050017`

## Facts

- SK Battery America (SKBA), a U.S. subsidiary of SK On, is set to commence mass production of electric vehicle batteries for Hyundai Motor at its Georgia plant starting from October.
- This strategic move marks a significant shift in SK On's production lines, which were initially dedicated to supplying batteries to U.S. automaker Ford.
- During a second-quarter conference call, SK On highlighted the growing importance of U.S. production for original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) due to the scale of subsidies and other factors.
- SK On stated that "some line conversion work at the SKBA plant is underway" because the scale of subsidies makes U.S. production increasingly important for OEMs.
- According to industry sources on September 22, the conversion of production lines at the SKBA plant is already in progress.
- The line conversion is interpreted as a result of aligned interests between Hyundai Motor and SK On.
- Hyundai Motor seeks to benefit from the Inflation Reduction Act (IRA).
- SK On aims to improve the operational rate of its Georgia plant through this shift.
- The IRA is a significant U.S. legislation designed to reduce inflation by investing in domestic energy production and manufacturing, including provisions that incentivize the production of EVs and their components within the U.S.
- The operational rate of the Georgia plant is expected to increase significantly with the line conversion.
- Hyundai Motor Group plans to operate a large-scale electric vehicle-only production plant named 'Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America' (HMGMA) in Savannah, Georgia.
- This new facility underscores Hyundai's strategic focus on expanding its electric vehicle lineup and establishing a reliable battery supply chain.
- The broader context of U.S. policies promoting domestic manufacturing, job creation, and technological innovation in the automotive sector further supports this development.
- The conversion of production lines at the SKBA plant aligns with these U.S. policies and addresses complexities of the global supply chain for EV components, including challenges posed by geopolitical factors and trade policies.
