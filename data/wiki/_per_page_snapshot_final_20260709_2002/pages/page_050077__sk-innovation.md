# SK Innovation

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2020-08-21

**Source:** [SK's plan to expand EV battery business in US hits snag - The Korea Times](https://www.koreatimes.co.kr/business/companies/20200821/sks-plan-to-expand-ev-battery-business-in-us-hits-snag)  ·  `page_050077`

## Facts

- SK Innovation's plan to expand its EV battery business in the US hit a snag after a U.S. lawmaker called for an investigation into its affiliate SK Battery America for allegedly illegally employing Korean workers at its Georgia factory construction site.
- On Aug. 20, Georgia Congressman Doug Collins sent a letter to U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) and U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) requesting an investigation into whether foreign nationals were illegally employed to build the battery factory.
- Collins cited information that CBP officers caught 33 Korean nationals at Hartsfield-Jackson International Airport attempting to enter the U.S. without proper work authorizations in May.
- Collins stated that CBP determined the May incident was not isolated and was part of a larger scheme to illegally bring foreign workers into the United States.
- Collins said he was contacted by a constituent who observed additional Korean nationals illegally working at the Georgia facility on multiple occasions.
- According to the constituent, the 33 Korean nationals intercepted in May represent only a small fraction of the workforce systemically and illegally brought to the U.S. to displace American workers.
- Collins stated that if true, these actions hurt eager American workers, are illegal, and must be stopped.
- SK Innovation, Korea's third-largest EV battery maker, broke ground for the battery factory in Commerce, Jackson County, Georgia, in March 2019 after securing 1.2 square kilometers of land.
- The plant is scheduled to begin mass production of EV battery cells in 2022.
- SK Battery America promised that the new factory would create about 2,600 jobs once completed.
- Georgia Local Union 72, whose members include plumbers, pipefitters, and HVACR technicians, said the company is taking away about 500 jobs from them by providing construction positions to Korean workers.
- David Cagle, a member of Georgia Local Union 72, stated that Georgians have gotten the shaft over the SKI battery plant.
- Cagle reported that when his union members applied for construction jobs to build the plant, they were told nothing was available.
- Cagle said about 500 people are currently out of work who could take these jobs and make a living, but instead Koreans are making a living.
- SK Innovation stated it hired Korean workers only to build the factory because they are specialized in constructing battery factories.
- SK Innovation confirmed that once construction is completed, the Georgia plant will be operated by American employees.
- SK Innovation reiterated that the new Georgia factory will provide about 2,600 new jobs in the Jackson County area.
