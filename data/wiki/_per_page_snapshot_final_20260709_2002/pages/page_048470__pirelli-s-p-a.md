# Pirelli S.p.A.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-05

**Source:** [Pirelli’s Connected Tires Will Be Made In Georgia, The US Almost Blocked Them Over China | Carscoops](https://www.carscoops.com/2026/05/pirelli-cyber-tire-georgia/)  ·  `page_048470`

## Facts

- Pirelli has been given the go-ahead to begin manufacturing its latest series of tires in the United States with help from the Italian government.
- The company’s plant in Rome, Georgia will soon start manufacturing connected tires using Pirelli’s Cyber Tyre technology.
- These new tires include sensors that can collect data and work in conjunction with a vehicle’s on-board electronics, including the ABS and stability control systems, to enhance driving dynamics and safety.
- The Italian government stepped in to limit China’s control over Pirelli by using its ‘golden powers’ rule established to protect companies of national strategic interest.
- Last month, Italy imposed several curbs on Sinochem, noting that none of the eight board members that Sinochem has previously appointed will be allowed to hold top corporate positions like chairman or chief executive.
- Sinochem has been instructed that it cannot exert any influence over Pirelli, and Pirelli doesn’t need to share sensitive information with the Chinese company.
- For more than a year, there had been concerns about Pirelli in the US because the Chinese company Sinochem holds a 34.1 percent stake in the tire giant.
- The US government clamping down on Chinese technology made it seem likely Pirelli would have to build these tires elsewhere.
- Pirelli’s plant in Rome, Georgia can currently produce up to 400,000 tires annually.
- The company did not say how many tires using its Cyber Tyre tech will be added to its production.
- Claudio Zanardo, Pirelli North America chief executive, stated that the start of Cyber Tyre production in the Rome, Georgia plant is a significant milestone for Pirelli in this country.
- Zanardo noted the move reflects Pirelli’s commitment to bringing advanced technologies like Cyber Tyre closer to the market and further strengthening its industrial footprint and innovation capabilities in the United States.
- The development will help offset some of the impacts of US tariffs on the tire manufacturer.
