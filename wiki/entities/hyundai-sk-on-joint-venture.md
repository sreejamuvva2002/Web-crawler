# Hyundai-SK On Joint Venture

**Type:** Joint Venture  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[battery-cell-manufacturing|Battery Cell Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** Bartow County, Georgia, Bartow County, Georgia, United States

## Overview

A joint venture between Hyundai Motor Group and SK On to build a battery cell manufacturing facility in Bartow County, Georgia, supplying cells for Hyundai's U.S. electric-vehicle production.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Provides battery cells for Hyundai Motor Group's U.S. EV factories, enabling IRA tax-credit eligibility.

## Key facts

- Hyundai Motor Group intends to expand US production of electric vehicles under Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis brands and chose SK On as the battery cell supplier.
- The two companies have established a joint venture to supply the battery cells for local US production and plan to invest $5 billion in a local production facility.
- Battery cells manufactured by the joint venture will be assembled into battery packs by Hyundai Mobis and supplied to the Group's production facilities.
- Bartow County, Georgia was selected as the location for the US battery manufacturing facility because it is close to Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Alabama, Kia Georgia, and Metaplant America.
- The plant’s initial annual production capacity is targeted at 35 GWh, enough to support roughly 300,000 electric vehicles.
- Battery-cell production at the Bartow County plant is scheduled for the second half of 2025.
- Hyundai plans a new $5.5 billion EV manufacturing plant in Bryan County, Georgia that will begin production in 2025 and use batteries from the Bartow County facility.
- The partnership with SK On is not exclusive; Hyundai is also in talks with LG Energy to establish two additional battery plants in the US.
- SK On’s pouch-type cells are already used in Hyundai Ioniq 5, Ioniq 6, and Kia EV6, and will be used in upcoming models such as Ioniq 7 SUV, Genesis GV70, and Kia EV9.
- With battery and vehicle production in the US, Hyundai Motor Group’s EVs will qualify for the Inflation Reduction Act tax credit, a significant sales incentive.
