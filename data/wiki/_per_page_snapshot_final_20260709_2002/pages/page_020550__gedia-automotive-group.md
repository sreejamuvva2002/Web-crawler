# GEDIA Automotive Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [German Auto Parts Maker Building Assembly Plant in Georgia | 2020-08-12  | ASSEMBLY](https://www.assemblymag.com/articles/95830-german-auto-parts-maker-building-assembly-plant-in-georgia)  ·  `page_020550`

## Facts

- German auto parts manufacturer GEDIA Automotive Group will invest $85 million to build a new assembly plant in Dalton, GA.
- The facility will create 200 jobs.
- The plant will produce parts for electric vehicles.
- GEDIA supplies lightweight parts to Volkswagen, BMW, Mercedes-Benz, Volvo and other automakers around the world.
- The Georgia plant will be the company’s second in the United States, joining a plant in Michigan.
- Markus Schaumburg, co-CEO of GEDIA Automotive, stated that the project is an essential step into the future for GEDIA and that moving closer to OEMs allows the company to serve the American market even better.
- The new plant is expected to begin operations during the third quarter of 2021.
