# Battery Workforce Initiative

**Type:** workforce_training  ·  **Role:** workforce_training  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [DOE and DOL Announce New Effort to Support and Expand America’s Battery Workforce | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/articles/doe-and-dol-announce-new-effort-support-and-expand-americas-battery-workforce)  ·  `page_005161`

## Facts

- The U.S. Department of Energy (DOE), in coordination with the U.S. Department of Labor (DOL), announced the release of the Battery Workforce Initiative’s National Guideline Standards for registered apprenticeships for battery machine operators on March 26, 2024.
- The DOL-certified guidelines were created in partnership with battery manufacturers, community colleges, and unions to lay out rigorous training requirements for the skilled workforce needed as electric vehicle sales have quadrupled.
- Nearly 300 new or expanded battery facilities have been announced since President Biden took office.
- Energy Secretary Jennifer M. Granholm, Acting Labor Secretary Julie Su, Michigan Governor Gretchen Whitmer, U.S. Representative Elissa Slotkin, and UAW President Shawn Fain attended the announcement at UAW Local 652 Union Hall in Lansing, MI.
- The announcement location was near the Ultium factory being constructed with support from DOE’s Loan Program Office.
- U.S. Secretary of Energy Jennifer M. Granholm stated that over 400 EV and battery manufacturing facilities have been announced in the U.S. in just three years under President Biden’s Investing in America agenda.
- Acting Secretary of Labor Julie A. Su noted that in just under a year, the Battery Workforce Initiative has built a strong partnership between government, industry, technology, and labor to expand pathways into good jobs through Registered Apprenticeships.
- U.S. Secretary of Education Miguel Cardona described the initiative as an example of how agencies and community college partners can create new career pathways into the clean energy workforce.
- The new Standards will inform Battery Workforce Initiative training materials for companies and training providers, with curricula currently under development to support DOE’s broader workforce education activities for battery manufacturing.
- The initiative includes the BWI Pilot Training Project and the Battery Workforce Challenge Program Regional Workforce Training Hubs Project.
- DOE's National Energy Technology Lab will launch the BWI Pilot Training in April.
- Argonne National Laboratory will launch the Battery Workforce Challenge’s Regional Workforce Training Hubs in Summer 2024.
- The new Standards facilitate upfront industry engagement and alignment, speeding up the DOL approval process for new battery training programs and providing a foundation for national credentials.
- BWI materials create a clear pathway for training sponsors to leverage public funds for workforce development, meet battery manufacturers' needs, and provide career-track pathways for workers.
- Bob Galyen, Chief Technology Officer for NAATBatt and SAE International Fellow, stated that after 48 years in the battery industry working in the world’s largest lithium battery manufacturing operations, training is key to a successful battery industry and BWI will accelerate the return to global technology leadership while creating more middle-class jobs.
- Since 2022, the Battery Workforce Initiative has convened meetings and engaged with hundreds of stakeholders representing established and emerging manufacturers, academia, and labor groups.
- The initiative's next agenda item is to examine other jobs in the battery supply chain.
- BWI has already started convening stakeholders working on battery-grade materials processing and recycling to map out skills requirements for that work.
- BWI’s strategic partnerships across agencies, including with the Department of Education, leverage historic investments in clean energy industries as a critical pillar of President Biden’s whole-of-government decarbonization strategy.
