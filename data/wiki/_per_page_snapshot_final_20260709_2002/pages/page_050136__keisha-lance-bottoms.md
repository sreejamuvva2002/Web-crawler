# Keisha Lance Bottoms

## Facts

- Former Atlanta Mayor Keisha Lance Bottoms is running for Governor of Georgia and laid out her platform at a Democratic gubernatorial forum in Savannah on January 8, 2026.
- Bottoms proposed creating the Center for Workforce Innovation at Atlanta Area Tech to train workers for corporate roles.
- The proposed workforce training program would train individuals as coders for Truist, plumbers for Home Depot, mechanics for Delta Airlines, and linesmen for Georgia Power.
- Bottoms stated that the Center for Workforce Innovation program should be scaled statewide across Georgia.
- She proposed finding a pathway to free technical and community college in Georgia to support workforce development.
- Bottoms emphasized scaling workforce training programs throughout Coastal Georgia and the state as a significant game changer for career advancement.
- On January 8, 2026, Bottoms toured the Port of Savannah alongside ILA 1414 to discuss economic development and logistics infrastructure.
- During the port tour, Bottoms observed approximately 14,000 trucks that go in and out of the Port of Savannah, many owned by small businesses.
- Bottoms stated that economic development and workforce development must go hand in hand to ensure people are trained for incoming jobs in Georgia.
- She emphasized holding corporate partners accountable when they receive state incentives to ensure they give back to local communities.
- A study by Georgia Tech identified a shortage of 41,000 homes in Coastal Georgia, which Bottoms cited as an immediate infrastructure challenge affecting economic development and affordability.
- Bottoms proposed investing in Coastal Georgia infrastructure to make it easier for residents to build homes and access grants.
- As mayor of Atlanta, Bottoms created an affordable housing trust fund to help homeowners avoid rising property taxes.
