# Ascend Elements

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2024-09-23

**Source:** [Another $125 million in funding has been awarded by the U.S. Department of Energy to Ascend Elements which is part of a federal effort to boost battery production and recycling for electric vehicles.](https://www.waste360.com/waste-recycling/ascend-elements-receives-125m-in-federal-funds-for-planned-battery-recycling-plants)  ·  `page_052581`

## Facts

- Ascend Elements received $125 million in federal funding awarded by the U.S. Department of Energy.
- The award is part of a federal effort to boost battery production and recycling for electric vehicles.
- Ascend Elements will put the federal award toward a new battery recycling process at facilities in Hopkinsville, Ky.
- The project is being executed in partnership with Orbia.
- The companies will extract graphite from the recycled batteries.
- The extracted graphite will be processed and enhanced at an Orbia facility in Louisiana.
- The companies plan to sell the new battery-grade graphite.
- White House National Climate Advisor Ali Zaidi stated the funding is helping support the technologies that we need in the market today, the components that we will need in the near future, and the innovative technologies we need to advance our vision for a circular domestic battery supply chain.
- The initiative positions the United States to continue leading the global effort on clean energy.
