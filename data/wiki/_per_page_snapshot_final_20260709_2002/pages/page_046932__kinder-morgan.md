# Kinder Morgan

## Facts

- Kinder Morgan introduced plans for a multibillion-dollar expansion of its existing natural gas pipeline system in the southeastern U.S. in February.
- The proposal calls for the addition of 14 new pipeline segments totaling approximately 290 miles across Georgia, South Carolina, and Alabama.
- The plan includes upgrades to 13 compressor stations and the installation of four new metering facilities to manage and measure gas flow.
- Allen Fore, vice president of Kinder Morgan, stated the project is an expansion of an existing natural gas pipeline system operated in Georgia for over 50 years.
- The expansion is branded as the “South System 4 Expansion” and is positioned to meet long-term regional energy demand.
- Natural gas is highlighted as a key source for generating electricity, meaning even residents without direct gas use benefit from the expanded supply.
- Property owners in Jefferson County have expressed concerns about how the project might interfere with future land use.
- Kinder Morgan has emphasized its willingness to work with landowners to accommodate specific needs during recent public forums.
- If regulatory approvals are granted, construction could begin in the coming years, with operations expected to start between 2028 and 2029.
