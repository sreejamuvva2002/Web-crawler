# Freyr Battery / T1 Energy

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Energy Storage Insights: Batteries, Solar, Lithium Film and More - News](https://eepower.com/news/energy-storage-insights-batteries-solar-lithium-film-and-more/)  ·  `page_001624`

## Facts

- Freyr Battery abandoned its $2.6 billion factory project in Georgia.
- The company cited high interest rates, declining battery prices, and a leadership shakeup as contributing factors in discontinuing the Georgia plant.
- Lithium-ion battery pack prices plummeted 20% last year to a record-low $115 per kWh, marking the sharpest annual drop since 2017 per BloombergNEF data.
- Freyr Battery pivoted from battery manufacturing to solar and energy storage, rebranding to T1 Energy.
- Solar and battery storage resources accounted for about 81% of U.S. electric-generating capacity additions in 2024.
- T1 Energy aims to develop solar and storage infrastructure to meet rising electricity demand from domestic manufacturing, artificial intelligence, and data centers.
- Lyten secured agreements with California Sulphur Company at the Port of Los Angeles and another supplier at the Port of Stockton to provide industrial-grade sulfur for its U.S. sites.
- Lyten's U.S. production facilities are located in San Leandro (acquired from Northvolt subsidiary Cuberg), San Jose, and a planned lithium-sulfur gigafactory in Nevada.
- Lyten’s lithium-sulfur batteries offer a high-performance alternative to conventional lithium-ion batteries that rely on nickel, cobalt, and manganese, materials China currently controls.
- Unlike lithium-ion battery materials requiring 30,000 to 50,000 miles of transport during sourcing and production, Lyten’s sulfur can be processed within 100 miles of its U.S. plants.
- Lyten’s sulfur-based cathodes are 40% lighter than those in lithium-ion batteries and 70% lighter than lithium-iron phosphate components, boosting energy density and efficiency.
- Lyten recently received a $650 million letter of interest from the Export-Import Bank of the United States to support its lithium-sulfur battery production.
- In 2024, Lyten delivered pouch batteries to Stellantis and other manufacturers for testing and evaluation, with additional customers like Chrysler joining the lineup.
- Phase 1 of Lyten’s Reno-area gigafactory will launch in 2027, focusing on producing cathode active material and lithium-metal anodes and assembling lithium-sulfur cells in pouch and cylindrical formats.
- Once fully completed, the plant will have up to 10 GWh of annual capacity.
- Elevated Materials, backed by Applied Materials and TPG Rise Climate, launched to develop ultra-thin lithium metal films to enhance battery performance.
- The company uses a roll-to-roll vapor deposition process to manufacture lithium films with improved cycle life and up to 70% higher energy density for electric vehicles, eVTOL aircraft, and grid storage.
- Elevated Materials produces ultra-thin films (<1 to 20 µm) on substrates like copper, graphite, and silicon using this deposition method.
- Elevated Materials enters a competitive field where companies like Sion Power and Li-Metal Corp are also developing lithium thin films and lithium-metal anodes.
- Arrow Electronics partnered with Scotland-based Dukosi to distribute contactless cell monitoring technology for high-performance batteries used in EVs and energy storage systems.
- Dukosi’s decentralized battery management system replaces traditional wired architectures with a secure deterministic communication protocol, enhancing system reliability, thermal management, and cell-level analytics.
- The product is scalable for hundreds of cell monitors in single or multiple networks.
