# Eli Lilly and Company

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Lilly is boosting manufacturing in IN with $2B investment](https://www.fiercepharma.com/manufacturing/eli-lilly-plots-21b-manufacturing-expansion-americas-crossroads-where-it-plans-summon)  ·  `page_008862`

## Facts

- Lilly invested $1 billion into a new injectables factory in North Carolina in January.
- Lilly is investing $2.1 billion to build two new manufacturing sites at LEAP Lebanon Innovation and Research District in Boone County, Indiana.
- The company announced the Indiana expansion on Wednesday.
- Lilly is headquartered in Indianapolis, Indiana.
- The new facilities will broaden Lilly’s manufacturing network for active ingredients plus new modalities like genetic medicines.
- The sites are intended to help Lilly respond to heightened demand for its current and future drugs.
- Lilly highlighted 17 new drug approvals in the past eight years, including this month's approval for tirzepatide (christened Mounjaro) for Type 2 diabetes.
- Lilly expects to hire 500 new employees to staff the Indiana plants.
- Lilly currently employs more than 3,700 manufacturing workers in Indiana.
- CEO David Ricks stated: “More than 146 years after our founding, Lilly remains committed to investing and innovating in Indiana.”
- CEO David Ricks stated: "These new sites will add capacity in support of our growing pipeline of innovative medicines, while also creating more high-tech jobs for Hoosiers."
- Lilly’s most recent expansion in Indiana was unveiled in 2019.
- Prior to the 2019 expansion, Lilly made cash outlays in research and manufacturing around Indianapolis totaling more than $2.5 billion over five years, which generated additional manufacturing jobs.
- For the Boone County project, Lilly plans to partner with several organizations, including the Indiana Economic Development Corporation.
- Lilly expects each new company role to generate “an additional four indirect jobs.”
- The project is estimated to require about 1,500 construction jobs during plant construction.
- In late January, Lilly announced it was investing around 400 million euros ($445.3 million) into a new biologics ingredients plant in Limerick, Ireland.
- Shortly after the Ireland announcement, Lilly telegraphed a $1 billion build-out of an injectables facility in Concord, North Carolina.
- The North Carolina facility plans to recruit approximately 600 new hires.
- Construction on the North Carolina plant is poised to begin in 2022 and will take several years to complete.
- Lilly anticipates “additional future investments in manufacturing to address growth expected from potential new medicines to treat diabetes, Alzheimer’s disease, cancer and autoimmune conditions.”
- In April, CEO David Ricks criticized Indiana's education attainment during a speech before The Economic Club of Indiana, stating: “Our education attainment in the state is not good.”
- Regarding workforce reskilling, Ricks stated: “The ability to reskill the workforce, I think, could improve. Health, life and inclusion, overall, I think conditions rank poorly nationally in our state. And also workforce preparedness, also related to reskilling, is a liability for us.”
- Ricks noted Indiana’s healthcare costs outstrip those of neighboring states, making it unattractive to potential employers.
- The article was updated with additional comments from Lilly CEO David Ricks.
