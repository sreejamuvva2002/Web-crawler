## Facts

- Nisshinbo Automotive Manufacturing, Inc. (NAMI) and Business Development Center (MI) are part of the Nisshinbo Group.
- The Nisshinbo Group consists of 59 subsidiaries and 40 affiliates worldwide.
- Nisshinbo was established in 1907, when it was producing yarns and threads.
- In the 1940s, Nisshinbo was called upon to manufacture automotive friction materials and developed a process using woven fabric embedded with friction material.
- Nisshinbo brakes and friction materials have evolved to serve diverse applications in the global transportation market.
- Nisshinbo maintains a library of more than one thousand different recipes, each specific to customer applications.
- North American operations are dedicated facilities serving OEM transportation manufacturers in the NAFTA market.
- NAC is located in Sterling Heights, Michigan, and provides team management, engineering, prototyping, and manufacturing capabilities.
- NAMI is Nisshinbo's state-of-the-art facility located in Covington, Georgia.
- These North American facilities produce high-quality friction materials and logistical support to serve transportation manufacturers.
