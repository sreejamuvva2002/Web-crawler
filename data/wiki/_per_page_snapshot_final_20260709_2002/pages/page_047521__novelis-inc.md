# Novelis Inc.

## Facts

- Novelis Inc., an aluminum products maker owned by Hindalco Industries Ltd., is seeking to complete a planned US initial public offering as soon as early June.
- The company could begin formally marketing the deal in about two weeks, according to unidentified people familiar with the matter.
- Novelis filed publicly with the US Securities and Exchange Commission for the listing on Monday.
- Hindalco is seeking to raise about $1.2 billion in the IPO for Atlanta-based Novelis.
- The company may target a valuation of about $18 billion.
- Details of the offering, including timing, could change.
- A representative for Novelis declined to comment on the plans.
- Novelis is working with Morgan Stanley, Bank of America Corp., and Citigroup Inc. on the potential New York Stock Exchange listing.
- The stock will be offered by a Hindalco subsidiary.
- Novelis will not receive any proceeds from the sale.
- Novelis is the world’s biggest maker of flat-rolled aluminum products, which are used in an array of goods including cars and soda cans.
- The company stated in February that it had filed confidentially for the listing with the US Securities and Exchange Commission.
- Hindalco purchased Novelis in 2007 at approximately a $6 billion valuation including debt.
- About 42% of Novelis’ global net sales come from North America.
