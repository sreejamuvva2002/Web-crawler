# Volvo Cars

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-02-28

**Source:** [Volvo Cars unveils big plans for SC plant with new car, jobs](https://www.postandcourier.com/business/industry/volvo-cars-charleston-sc-new-hybrid-jobs-vehicles/article_9bf693e0-db28-4552-9610-b6265b28a072.html)  ·  `page_014196`

## Facts

- A decade after beginning manufacturing in South Carolina, Volvo Cars is on an aggressive timeline to add a fourth car to production and double its workforce.
- The Sweden-based company rolled its first car off the Lowcountry assembly line in September 2018.
- Volvo aims to add a new, yet-to-be-named hybrid vehicle to its lineup by 2030.
- The new hybrid will be the fourth product manufactured in South Carolina, joining the fully electric EX90, the Polestar 3, and the midsize hybrid XC60 (announced in July).
- Production of the S60, the first car assembled in Ridgeville, ended last summer.
- With the hybrid's addition, Volvo aims to supply 50 percent of the U.S. volume of its sales from this plant.
- Currently, the North America plant supplies less than 5 percent of Volvo's U.S. volume.
- Francesca Gamboni, chief industrial operations officer of Volvo Cars, stated that aligning local manufacturing to customer needs benefits everyone by getting cars into customers' hands faster and more efficiently.
- Gamboni noted that local manufacturing prioritizes American customers and increases flexibility to adjust volumes based on local needs.
- Gamboni added that the strategy supports more local jobs and brings greater stability and consistency to the local workforce.
- Volvo’s 10-year celebration and media event took place in Ridgeville on Sept. 23.
- Event attendees included Gamboni, Volvo CEO Håkan Samuelsson, Luis Rezende (president of Americas for Volvo), David Stenstrom (vice president of manufacturing for the U.S.), Gov. Henry McMaster, and U.S. Rep. Nancy Mace (R-Charleston).
- The S.C. plant has a capacity to manufacture 150,000 vehicles a year but has yet to hit that milestone.
- Today, vehicles take about 1.5 days to build across 240 stations.
- Total current output is 100 vehicles a day, which equals about 26,000 vehicles a year.
- Gamboni said the new XC60 should bring the plant to about 50-60 percent capacity.
- The addition of the new hybrid will take plant capacity utilization to 100 percent.
- Production expansion would double the workforce from 2,000 to 4,000 employees.
- With Volvo's goal to be fully electric by 2040, the plan includes localizing more partners and suppliers to increase production and investing in technology.
- CEO Håkan Samuelsson stated that investment plans reinforce Volvo's long-term commitment to the U.S. market and manufacturing operations in South Carolina.
- The company is celebrating 70 years of Volvo Cars presence in the United States.
- Volvo has sold over 5 million cars in the U.S. and plans to sell many more in the future.
- David Stenstrom announced he will step down at the end of the week, succeeded by Miguel Tavera, the current chief supply chain officer.
- To-date, Volvo has invested $1.3 billion into operations in South Carolina over the past 10 years.
- Vehicles manufactured stateside are sold in the U.S. and overseas, mainly Europe.
- The Charleston plant currently provides about 20 percent of vehicles for Volvo’s global sales revenue.
