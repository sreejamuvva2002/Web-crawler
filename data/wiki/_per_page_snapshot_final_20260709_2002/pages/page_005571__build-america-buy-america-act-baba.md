# Build America, Buy America Act (BABA)

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2021-11-15

**Source:** [Build America, Buy America | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/management/build-america-buy-america)  ·  `page_005571`

## Facts

- The Build America, Buy America Act was enacted as part of the Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act on November 15, 2021.
- It established a domestic content procurement preference for all federal financial assistance obligated for infrastructure projects after May 14, 2022.
- The Act was enacted under Division G, Title IX of the Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act (IIJA), Public Law 117–58.
- BABA established a domestic content procurement preference requirement to increase a resilient domestic supply chain and manufacturing supply for critical materials for emerging and existing industries in the United States.
- The requirement applies to three product categories purchased under financial assistance awards: iron or steel products, manufactured products, and construction materials.
- The IIJA supports maximizing use of domestically produced goods consistent with Executive Order 14005.
- Per Section 70914 of the IIJA, an agency may not obligate funds for an infrastructure project unless all iron, steel, manufactured products, and construction materials are produced in the United States or a waiver is applied.
- On April 18, 2022, OMB issued Memorandum M-22-11 providing initial implementation guidance on applying the BABA Requirement to federal financial assistance programs.
- OMB amended 2 CFR by adding a new part 184 to assist with BABA implementation.
- On August 23, 2023, OMB finalized 2 CFR 184 as the primary guidance for agencies implementing BABA for federally funded financial assistance.
- The guidance in part 184 aims to improve consistency in implementing BABA requirements across the federal government.
- OMB also amended 2 CFR 200.322 to clarify existing domestic preference provisions within part 200.
- On October 25, 2023, OMB rescinded Memorandum M-22-11 and issued updated guidance via OMB Memorandum M-24-02.
- Memo M-24-02 replaces Memo M-22-11 to remove direct conflicts between it and 2 CFR part 184.
- Memo M-24-02 summarizes aspects of 2 CFR part 184 and provides supplemental guidance for infrastructure projects subject to BABA requirements.
- DOE may issue further guidance to recipients and stakeholders on DOE financial assistance programs.
- OMB may issue additional guidance in the future based on stakeholder feedback.
- The Department of Energy will continue to work with OMB’s Made in America Office to coordinate implementation and compliance of Buy America Requirements.
- All DOE FOAs issued on or after May 14, 2022, must contain the Buy America Requirement for Infrastructure Projects provision.
- The requirement applies to all new and renewal DOE Financial Assistance awards and any funding modifications made on or after May 14, 2022, to existing awards containing public infrastructure projects.
- Any funding modification (continuation award, supplemental award, or other incremental/additional funding) made on or after May 14, 2022, to an existing award must incorporate the Buy America Requirement term and condition.
- When included in an award, the Buy America Requirement flows down to all sub-awardees regardless of entity type, including For-Profit Entities.
- The requirement applies to Non-Federal Entity prime recipients that are States, Local Governments, Indian Tribes, Territories, Institutions of Higher Education (IHEs), and Non-Profit Organizations.
- For-Profit Entities are not included in the definition of Non-Federal Entity when applying the statutory Buy America Requirement to prime recipients.
- Tribal Consultation is required prior to applying the requirement to Indian Tribe prime recipients.
- The requirement applies to a For-Profit Entity if it is a sub-recipient or sub-awardee under an award containing the requirement, or if it voluntarily chooses to use domestically sourced materials by stating so in its application.
- If a For-Profit Entity states compliance in its application and is selected, its award will contain the Buy America Requirement term and condition.
- The prime recipient is responsible for flowing the requirement down to all sub-awards, contracts, subcontracts, and purchase orders for work under the project, including to For-Profit Entities when they are sub-recipients or sub-awardees.
- DOE prime recipients must ensure that absent a waiver, none of the funds (federal share or recipient cost-share) may be used for infrastructure unless specific domestic content criteria are met.
- All iron and steel used must be produced in the United States, meaning all manufacturing processes from initial melting through coating application occurred in the U.S.
- All manufactured products must be produced in the United States, with the cost of components mined/produced/manufactured in the U.S. greater than 55 percent of the total component cost (unless another standard applies).
- Component costs for manufactured products are determined in accordance with 2 CFR 184.5.
- All construction materials must be manufactured in the United States, meaning all manufacturing processes occurred in the U.S.
- The requirement only applies to articles, materials, and supplies consumed in, incorporated into, or permanently affixed to an infrastructure project.
- It does not apply to tools, equipment, and supplies like temporary scaffolding brought to and removed from the construction site.
- It does not apply to equipment and furnishings like movable chairs, desks, and portable computer equipment used at the finished project but not integral to the structure.
- The requirement must flow down to all sub-awards, contracts, subcontracts, and purchase orders for work under the infrastructure project.
- Prime recipients must maintain certifications or equivalent documentation proving compliance that covered articles, materials, and supplies are produced in the United States.
- Certifications must be provided by suppliers or manufacturers and flow up from sub-awardees, contractors, and vendors to the prime recipient.
- Prime recipients must keep these certifications with award/project files and produce them upon request from DOE, auditors, or the Office of Inspector General.
- Recipients should contact the cognizant DOE Contracting or Grants Officer for questions about applying the Buy America Requirement in an award.
- Prime recipients may apply for and DOE may grant project-specific waivers from the Buy America Requirement when necessary.
- Waiver requests must be submitted in writing by the prime recipient to DOE per award terms and conditions.
- DOE may request additional information, which the recipient must provide.
- Waiver requests are subject to review by DOE and OMB, plus a public comment period of no less than 15 calendar days.
- Recipient waiver requests will be made publicly available on DOE’s and OMB’s websites.
- DOE may reject or grant waivers in whole or part based on review, analysis, and feedback.
- DOE’s final determination on approval or rejection cannot be appealed.
- Waiver requests may take up to 90 calendar days to process.
- Recipients should contact the cognizant DOE Contracting or Grants Officer for questions about requesting waivers.
- The Act requires all proposed waivers to be publicly available on DOE’s and OMB’s websites for comment for at least 15 calendar days before issuance.
- Requests to modify or renew a DOE General Applicability waiver require a 30-calendar day public comment period.
- Only comments received prior to the closing date will be reviewed.
- DOE posts all approved waivers on its Buy America Requirement Waiver page.
