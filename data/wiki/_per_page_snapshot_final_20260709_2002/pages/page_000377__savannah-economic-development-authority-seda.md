# Savannah Economic Development Authority (SEDA)

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** workforce_training  ·  **Published:** 2024-01-04

**Source:** [Savannah economic leaders discuss planning for workforce demand](https://www.wtoc.com/2024/01/04/savannah-economic-leaders-discuss-planning-workforce-demand/)  ·  `page_000377`

## Facts

- SEDA held its annual meeting discussing how to secure enough industrial workers to meet employer demand in the Savannah area.
- Boosting the area’s workforce is a top priority for SEDA officials in 2024.
- SEDA President and CEO Trip Tollison stated that recruiting and working with schools and other institutions is necessary to increase the workforce because there are a lot of jobs to hire in a short period of time.
- Tollison spoke about the Authority’s accomplishments and future goals to a crowd at the Savannah Convention Center.
- The Savannah Harbor - Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority released a study projecting a deficit of more than 1,400 area industrial workers in 2025.
- Manufacturers like Hyundai Motor Group and supplier companies plan to hire thousands of employees locally in the coming years.
- SEDA announced the creation of a new organization tasked with boosting regional workforce numbers through six key areas.
- Tollison touted the work of economic officials in 2023 despite the projected deficit.
- Approximately 3,700 jobs were created with $2.4 billion in regional investment in 2023.
- SEDA's focus will shift to securing more manufacturing businesses in the area through new incentive programs in the coming year.
- Tollison stated that 2023 was a remarkable year for not just Savannah, but the whole region.
- Several manufacturing facilities are currently under construction, including Hyundai’s Bryan County electric vehicle and battery processing plant which is expected to start production in early 2025.
