# SK Innovation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-03-31

**Source:** [Battery supplier SK Innovation might not be banned in the US after all](https://www.greencarreports.com/news/1131801_battery-supplier-sk-innovation-won-t-be-banned-in-the-us-after-all)  ·  `page_000549`

## Facts

- SK Innovation will likely be able to move ahead with plans for large-scale U.S. battery production following a favorable decision by the U.S. International Trade Commission (ITC).
- The ITC ruled that SK Innovation does not infringe on LG Chem patents.
- The favorable decision came after pressure from Ford and other companies.
- SK Innovation is engaged in a legal battle with rival LG Chem over allegations of misappropriating trade secrets during the hiring of former employees for factory ramp-ups in the U.S. and Hungary.
- In February, the ITC initially voted to effectively ban SK Innovation from the U.S. market for 10 years.
- The initial ban would have allowed SK Innovation to supply batteries for the Ford F-150 Electric pickup truck for four years and Volkswagen's Tennessee-built electric cars for two years.
- SK Innovation currently supplies battery cells for several Kia models, including the Niro EV, which are sourced from South Korea.
- SK Innovation is constructing a battery factory in Commerce, Georgia.
- The Biden administration's EV plans emphasize both large production volumes and domestic production of components.
- SK Innovation stated that its Georgia factory will be capable of making batteries for 300,000 EVs annually after the second phase is completed.
- SK Innovation stated it will create 2,600 jobs in Georgia by 2024.
- Georgia state lawmakers expressed concern over potentially losing those jobs if the ban were upheld.
- LG Chem is investing $4.5 billion in U.S. battery production over the next four years.
- LG Chem stated it could fulfill demand if SK Innovation's Georgia factory is scrapped.
- The legal dispute between suppliers has complicated battery supply for the North American market.
- Ford and Volkswagen had planned to use cells from SK Innovation's Georgia factory in upcoming EVs.
- Volkswagen ID.4 crossovers were planned to be built at its Chattanooga, Tennessee, factory beginning in 2022 using those cells.
