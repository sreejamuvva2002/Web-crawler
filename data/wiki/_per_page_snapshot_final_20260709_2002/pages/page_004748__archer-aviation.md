# Archer Aviation

## Facts

- Archer is partnering with cities across the country to submit multiple applications to launch initial air taxi operations under the White House’s eVTOL Integration Pilot Program (eIPP), a first-of-its-kind public-private initiative led by the U.S. Department of Transportation (DOT) and Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) to support the safe integration of air taxis in the U.S. beginning as soon as next year.
- The applications coincide with the DOT’s unveiling of its Advanced Air Mobility (AAM) National Strategy today, which lays the framework for integrating eVTOL aircraft into the national airspace.
- Archer’s applications to the eIPP are proposed public-private partnerships with cities across California, Texas, Florida, Georgia and New York.
- Archer’s application with the City of Huntington Beach is exclusive, meaning it is the only air taxi OEM that is part of that application.
- This bid supports Archer's plans for early air taxi operations in and around Los Angeles as part of its role as the Official Air Taxi Provider of the 2028 Olympic Games.
- The eIPP was established via President Trump’s “Unleashing Drone Dominance” Executive Order.
- The program is designed to accelerate adoption of electric air taxis in major cities across the country by creating operational pathways for the top American eVTOL companies.
- Archer’s applications focus on establishing the foundation required for long-term electric air taxi services in select U.S. cities, including building local operations teams, developing or upgrading infrastructure to support eVTOL flights and coordinating closely with public safety and emergency response agencies.
- Led by the DOT and FAA, the eIPP is intended to bring together industry, regulators and local governments to establish early eVTOL operations and help U.S. cities prepare for the widespread adoption of electric air taxis that is expected in the coming years.
- Archer’s founder and CEO, Adam Goldstein, joined the U.S. Transportation Secretary Sean Duffy and other industry leaders in Washington, D.C. as the DOT unveiled the nation’s Advanced Air Mobility (AAM) National Strategy.
- The National AAM Strategy outlines a coordinated roadmap to safely integrate electric vertical takeoff and landing (eVTOL) aircraft into the national airspace, aligning certification, operations, infrastructure and early deployment to enable American companies to move from flight testing into early commercial operations.
- The National AAM Strategy and its accompanying action plan outline 40 recommendations to support early operations and advance President Trump’s America First agenda, including strengthening domestic manufacturing, creating high-skilled jobs and reinforcing U.S. leadership in next-generation aviation.
- U.S. Transportation Secretary Sean P. Duffy stated that the strategy aims to unlock the future of skies, transform how the flying public travels, assist first responders, improve community healthcare access, enable business goods delivery, and support national defense.
- Archer founder and CEO Adam Goldstein stated that the industry has moved past the question of “if” into “when and how,” citing the clearest path to market through close work with the Administration, DOT, FAA and other federal agencies, with a current focus on execution—building, deploying and flying these aircraft in the United States.
- Huntington Beach Mayor Casey McKeon commented that partnering with Archer to explore a safe eVTOL demonstration will bring new opportunities to the community and keep the city at the forefront of innovation.
- For several years, Archer has worked closely with the DOT, the FAA and other federal, state and local agencies to help shape the path to market for air taxis.
- That collaboration has included participation in the Advanced Air Mobility Interagency Working Group, contributions to the development of the Special Federal Aviation Regulation (SFAR) covering air taxis and engagement through the crafting of the eVTOL Integration Pilot Program.
- The FAA is expected to review applications submitted during this initial phase of the eIPP and announce selections in early to mid-2026, with early operational activity anticipated to begin later in the year.
- Archer is designing and developing the key enabling technologies and aircraft necessary to power the future of aviation.
