# Ascend Elements

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Another $1B EV Supplier Factory Under Construction In The 'Battery Belt'](https://www.bisnow.com/national/news/economic-development/battery-belt-grabs-another-ev-supplier-with-inflation-reduction-act-incentives-119306)  ·  `page_000694`

## Facts

- Ascend Elements is based in Massachusetts.
- Ascend Elements extracts cathode material from older batteries to be used in newer ones.
- Ascend Elements broke ground on a new factory in Hopkinsville, Kentucky.
- The company expects to deliver its first cathode supplies to an unnamed buyer in the fourth quarter of 2024.
- The new Kentucky plant is expected to reach full production by 2025.
- Ascend Elements has a contract to deliver a minimum of $1 billion worth of cathodes over the next five years.
- Ascend Elements is the latest EV-related manufacturer to move to the region known as the Battery Belt, which covers areas from Michigan to Georgia.
- The EV battery industry received a boost from tax incentives provided in the 2022 Inflation Reduction Act.
- Since the passage of the Inflation Reduction Act, companies have announced $242 billion in new clean power capital investments.
- These investments include battery plants, wind farms, and EV factories across 41 states.
- Center for American Progress Senior Vice President Trevor Higgins reported these investment figures to a subcommittee of the House Committee on Energy and Commerce in April.
- Georgia has experienced a boom in EV activity.
- Hyundai Motor Group announced a new $4.3 billion EV battery plant located outside of Savannah.
- The Hyundai plant is being developed in a partnership with LG Energy Solutions.
- The Savannah-area plant will be Hyundai Motor Group's second EV battery plant in Georgia.
- The plant will produce power cells for batteries compatible with Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis electric vehicles.
- Ascend Elements CEO Mike O’Kronley stated the Kentucky plant is receiving $480 million in matching grants from the Inflation Reduction Act.
- Mike O’Kronley noted that the non-diluted funding was extremely helpful and is beginning to translate into commercial contracts.
- Ascend Elements was founded in 2015 in Worcester, Massachusetts.
- Ascend Elements already operates an existing plant in Covington, Georgia.
- The Covington, Georgia facility recycles battery material from discarded consumer electronics.
- The Covington, Georgia facility also recycles manufacturing scraps from Honda Motor Co. and Hitachi.
- The new Kentucky plant is expected to produce cathodes capable of supplying 40 gigawatt-hours of batteries annually, or enough for 750,000 electric vehicles.
