# Advanced Technology Vehicles Manufacturing Loan Program (ATVM)

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [Advanced Technology Vehicles Manufacturing Loan Program | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/edf/advanced-technology-vehicles-manufacturing-loan-program)  ·  `page_002757`

## Facts

- The Advanced Technology Vehicles Manufacturing Loan Program (ATVM) provides loans to support the manufacture of eligible advanced technology vehicles and qualifying components, including newly authorized modes from the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law.
- Expanded uses beyond light-duty vehicles include medium- and heavy-duty vehicles, trains or locomotives, maritime vessels including offshore wind support vessels, aircrafts, and hyperloop.
- IRA removed the $25 billion cap on ATVM loan authority.
- IRA appropriated $3 billion in credit subsidy to support these loans.
- The program is administered by the Loan Programs Office.
- Funding amount consists of $3,000,000,000 of credit subsidy appropriations under IRA, resulting in an estimated $40,000,000,000 of direct loans.
- Funding mechanism is direct loans.
- Recipients include a manufacturer of eligible advanced technology vehicles or of qualifying components.
- Period of availability remains open for commitment through 9/30/2028.
- Tribal eligibility is Yes.
- Formula funding is No.
- Cost share requirement is N/A, but the loan amount cannot exceed 80% of eligible project costs.
- Loans fund projects that manufacture a range of advanced technology vehicles and their components, including light-duty vehicles, medium- and heavy-duty vehicles, locomotives, maritime vessels including offshore wind vessels, aircrafts, and hyperloop.
- Funds may be used for the costs of providing direct loans for reequipping, expanding, or establishing a manufacturing facility in the United States to produce, or for engineering integration performed in the United States of, newly authorized types of advanced technology vehicles only if those vehicles emit, under any possible operational mode or condition, low or zero exhaust emissions of greenhouse gases.
