# SK Innovation

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2019-01-10

**Source:** [SK Innovation mulling another $5B for America’s first EV battery plant – Ahead of the Herd](https://aheadoftheherd.com/sk-innovation-mulling-another-5b-for-americas-first-ev-battery-plant/)  ·  `page_000579`

## Facts

- Korean company SK Innovation announced an investment of US$1.67 billion to build the first electric vehicle battery plant in the United States.
- SK Innovation is considering plowing an additional $5 billion into the project planned for Jackson County, Georgia.
- The yet-to-be-built plant would have an initial annual capacity of 9.8 gigawatt-hours.
- The plant plans to eventually ramp up to 105 GWh capacity.
- The eventual 105 GWh capacity will be five times the 20GWh capacity of Tesla’s Gigafactory 1 in Nevada.
- Production at the SK Innovation facility is targeted for 2022.
- The Georgia facility would focus solely on batteries, unlike Tesla's factory which also makes drivetrains.
- It would become the largest EV battery plant in the United States.
- SK Innovation currently supplies batteries to several global automakers, including Daimler and Hyundai Motor.
- Two of SK Innovation's customers, Mercedes-Benz and Hyundai-Kia Motors, will need battery cells in the US, prompting the local investment.
- Locating the EV battery plant in Georgia is strategic because Hyundai, Mercedes, and Honda operate assembly plants in nearby Alabama.
- Mazda and Toyota have broken ground on a factory in Alabama.
- Volkswagen has an assembly plant in Chattanooga, Tennessee.
- SK Innovation aims to be the first mover in establishing a mine-to-battery supply chain in the United States.
- There is currently no mine-to-battery supply chain in North America, including the United States and Canada.
- At Albemarle’s Silver Peak Mine in Nevada, lithium is shipped to Kings Mountain in North Carolina to produce lithium hydroxide for lithium-ion batteries.
- Lithium hydroxide from North Carolina is loaded on ships and sent to Asian battery manufacturers that sell to auto-makers.
- Tesla has shown little interest in making deals with current (Albemarle) and future US lithium miners.
- Tesla inked a deal last May with Kidman Resources, an Australian company developing a lithium processing plant in Western Australia with SQM.
- Tesla plans to buy lithium hydroxide from Kidman and manufacture batteries in China for its Gigafactory.
- Canadian lithium miner Nemaska Lithium will send its lithium hydroxide to Swedish battery maker Northvolt AV, which will take between 3,500 and 5,000 tonnes per year.
- Nemaska accepted an investment of $78 million from Japan-based Softbank in return for a 9.9% stake.
- Nemaska agreed last May to supply spodumene concentrate to Hanwa Co., Ltd. of Japan and General Lithium Corp from China.
- Battery companies are securing long-term prices and supply through offtake agreements due to climbing prices of EV battery ingredients (copper, lithium, cobalt, graphite) and falling prices of finished batteries.
- SK Innovation's Georgia plant requires a US lithium supplier to complete the mine-to-battery supply chain.
- Cypress Development Corp has a lithium claystone deposit in very close proximity to Silver Peak.
- Cypress released a preliminary economic assessment (PEA) last fall and plans to release a prefeasibility study (PFS) soon.
- Cypress’ mine has a 40-year minelife in the initial pit.
- Cypress holds an Indicated Resource of 3.835 million tonnes LCE and an Inferred Resource of 5.126 million tonnes LCE.
- Cypress’ claystone lithium operation is pegged at around 24,000 LCE tonnes a year according to the PEA.
- Cypress could provide battery-grade lithium carbonate and hydroxide to Silver Peak at low cost.
- Cypress’ mine has no water issues, is lower cost than brine or hard-rock lithium operations, and includes rare earths and other by-product credits.
