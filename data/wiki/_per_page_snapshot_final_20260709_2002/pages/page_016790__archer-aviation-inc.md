# Archer Aviation Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2021-02-17

**Source:** [Invest in Urban Air Mobility with Archer Aviation Stock - Nanalyze](https://www.nanalyze.com/2021/02/archer-aviation-stock/)  ·  `page_016790`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation is an electric vehicle company planning to go public via a SPAC merger with Atlas Crest Investment Corp (ACIC).
- The company came out of stealth mode less than a year ago relative to the article's publication date.
- Archer differentiates its offering as the first eVTOL backed by a major airline.
- United Airlines will contribute expertise in airspace management to assist Archer with developing battery-powered, short-haul aircraft.
- Once the aircraft meet United's operating and business requirements, United together with Mesa Airlines would acquire a fleet of up to 200 electric aircraft.
- United Airlines is an investor in Archer Aviation.
- Archer expects to open its first manufacturing facility in 2022.
- First sales are expected in 2024, bringing in $42 million in revenues.
- Revenues are projected to jump +2,375% to over a billion dollars in 2025.
- The SPAC transaction is expected to close in Q2-2021, after which the ticker will change from ACIC to ACHR.
- Archer unveiled its all-new eVTOL production aircraft named the Midnight at an open house event in Palo Alto, California.
- The Midnight is a pilot-plus-four-passenger electric vertical takeoff and landing aircraft.
- The Midnight represents the evolution of Archer's demonstrator eVTOL aircraft, the Maker, which validated proprietary twelve-tilt-six configuration and key enabling technologies.
- The new Midnight model is expected to have a payload of over 1,000 pounds and carry four passengers plus a pilot.
- It will be optimized for back-to-back short distance trips of 20-miles, with a charging time of approximately 10 minutes in-between.
- Archer is working to certify its new Midnight model with the FAA by late-2024 and launch in 2025.
- The company aims to make about 250 battery-electric air taxis in 2025 and scale up production in the following years, after setting a goal of getting its aircraft certified by the end of 2024.
- Stellantis announced a partnership with Archer Aviation to build the all-electric Midnight eVTOL aircraft.
- Stellantis plans to provide $150 million of equity capital for Archer to use at its discretion in 2023 and 2024.
- Stellantis intends to increase its shareholding in Archer by purchasing stock in the open market.
- Stellantis will aid in the manufacture of Archer’s Midnight eVTOL aircraft by contributing personnel, technology, and engineering know-how to the venture.
