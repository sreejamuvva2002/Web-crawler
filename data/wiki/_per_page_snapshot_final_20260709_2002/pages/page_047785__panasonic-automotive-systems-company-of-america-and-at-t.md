# Panasonic Automotive Systems Company of America and AT&T

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2011-06

**Source:** [Panasonic Automotive Systems Company of America and AT&T to Explore Connected Car Concepts - AI Online -](https://ai-online.com/2011/06/panasonic-automotive-systems-company-of-america-and-att-to-explore-connected-car-concepts/)  ·  `page_047785`

## Facts

- AT&T and Panasonic Automotive Systems Company of America formed a working agreement to explore and test connectivity solutions for consumers and commuters.
- The project provides a framework for creating customized products for global automotive manufacturers in North America.
- Initial concept testing for interfaces between in-car infotainment systems and emerging mobile devices will begin in late 2011.
- Testing will take place in the Peachtree City testing community.
- AT&T will provide network services for the project.
- Panasonic Automotive Systems Company will supply hardware and integration services.
- Financial terms of the agreement are not being disclosed.
- Steve Deguchi, President of Panasonic Automotive Systems Company of America, stated that drivers spend increasing amounts of time on the road seeking vehicles that complement their hyper-mobile lifestyles.
- Deguchi noted automakers successfully delivering smart, personalized services to drivers have a tremendous opportunity to stand out from competition.
- Deguchi added the most appealing vehicles will serve as another node in a driver’s existing network of devices.
- The project aims to create Peachtree City as a connected model city fully open to automotive manufacturers, technology providers, and others in the ecosystem.
- Both companies will continue pursuing independent initiatives but plan to work together on select programs combining their strengths for automakers and consumers.
- Glenn Lurie, President of emerging devices, resale and partnerships at AT&T, stated there are incredible wireless opportunities in the automotive space that have only been scratched.
- Lurie noted AT&T continues exploring ways to develop a safer connected ecosystem for emerging devices.
- Panasonic Automotive Systems Company of America is based in Peachtree City, Georgia.
- Panasonic Automotive Systems Company of America maintains Detroit area operations located in Southfield, MI.
- Panasonic Automotive Systems Company of America is a division company of Secaucus, New Jersey-based Panasonic Corporation of North America.
- Panasonic Corporation of North America is the principal North American subsidiary of Panasonic Corporation (NYSE: PC) of Osaka, Japan.
- Panasonic Corporation is one of the world’s leading developers and producers of innovative digital and other electronic products for consumer, business, and industrial use.
- Panasonic Automotive Systems Company of America acts as the North American operating company of Panasonic’s Automotive Systems Company, which coordinates global automotive industry systems and components operations.
