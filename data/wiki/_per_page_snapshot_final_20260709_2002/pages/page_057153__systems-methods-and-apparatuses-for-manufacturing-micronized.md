# Systems, Methods, and Apparatuses for Manufacturing Micronized Powder

## Facts

- The systems relate to producing a predetermined particle size range of micronized powder from materials of heterogeneous size and composition.
- Micronized powders may be used in the food industry, pharmaceutical industry, plastics industry, and rubber industry.
- Recycling vulcanized rubber (e.g., used tires) into micronized rubber powder (MRP) addresses environmental disposal problems.
- MRP can serve as a filler in new rubber mixes, such as tire tread compounds for vehicle tires.
- MRP can be used as a filler for polyolefins in plastic formulations and in asphalt manufacturing.
- In these applications, MRP replaces virgin compound materials and comprises vulcanized elastomer particles of 2 mm or less in diameter conforming to ASTM D-5603-01 standards.
- Traditional MRP production methods include cryogenic grinding, chemical processes, and other methods, which are often expensive and inefficient.
- The disclosed systems allow any vulcanized rubber/elastomer as infeed material regardless of supplementary materials like steel plates and fibrous materials from tires.
- A vibrating screener is used to remove fibrous materials from MRP by retaining the fibers while allowing the powder to pass through.
- A warming apparatus regulates operating efficiency using recirculation of MRP as part of a proportional integral derivative (PID) loop.
- Reintroducing MRP to the warming apparatus increases surface area contact, promoting rapid heat transfer between particles and the apparatus.
- An accumulator, such as a storage silo, decouples the grinding process from the screening process to allow both to operate at optimal rates.
- Operating efficiency of the warming apparatus can be determined by measuring motor amperage, temperature at a predetermined point, current capacity, volumetric flow rate, or using a theoretical model.
- If operating efficiency falls below a predefined threshold, MRP is recirculated through the warming apparatus until efficiency improves.
- A vibrating screener embodiment uses mesh sizes of 20 or 25 and operates for at least 1 minute to retain ≥99% of fibrous material while passing ≥99% of MRP.
- The overall process includes pre-grinding processing, cryogenic freezing, grinding, warming, ferrous metal and fiber removal, accumulation, screening, and storage.
- Pre-grinding processing modifies infeed material by roughly grinding large irregular rubber pieces and may remove extraneous materials like steel plates from tires.
- Cryogenic freezing significantly reduces infeed temperature, often using liquid nitrogen introduced via a rotating auger, to make rubber/elastomer brittle for uniform grinding.
- A conical-shaped impact mill grinds the cryogenically-frozen infeed by pulling it toward a high-speed rotor where particles bounce until broken into MRP.
- MRP exiting the grinder has a broad particle size distribution (e.g., ≥50 mesh and ≤140 mesh, or diameter ≤2 mm, or ≥40 mesh and ≤200 mesh) and remains at cryogenic temperatures of −80° F to −100° F.
- Warming prevents condensation that could turn MRP into a spongy solid, potentially recirculating it until reaching >80° F, above the dew point, a specific flow rate like 6,000 pounds per hour, or 90% capacity.
- Accumulation occurs in silos, storage bins, drums, flexible intermediate bulk containers, or intermediate bulk containers to decouple grinding and screening flow rates.
- A screening apparatus sorts MRP by particle size into predefined narrow ranges using multiple screens of varying mesh sizes.
- Screened MRP is stored or packaged in bags, silos, trucks, etc., for later use in applications like asphalt or tire manufacturing.
- One warming apparatus embodiment uses an 18″×20″ Thomas Conveyor piped with 2″ insulated pipe attached to a HEAT thermal fluid heat transfer system.
