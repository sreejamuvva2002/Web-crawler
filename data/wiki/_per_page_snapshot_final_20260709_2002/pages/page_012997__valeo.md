# Valeo

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2022-02-25

**Source:** [Valeo gives cautious 2022 outlook amid global uncertainties – Wall Street Observer](https://wallstreetobserver.com/valeo-gives-cautious-2022/)  ·  `page_012997`

## Facts

- French car parts maker Valeo expected to report lower core profit margin in 2022 amid geopolitical tensions and inflationary pressures, after its 2021 margin missed the group’s target.
- RBC analyst Tom Narayan noted that Valeo’s H2/21 results were consistent with the Jan 26 pre-announce, but 2022 guidance was well below consensus expectations.
- Analyst Tom Narayan added that this caution aligns with earlier statements from Renault, Michelin, Faurecia, and Stellantis regarding the global chip shortage.
- Valeo shares dropped 10% in midday trade, heading for its worst day since March 2020.
- Semiconductor suppliers, car parts producers, and carmakers expect some improvement in component shortages this year as chipmakers boost production capacity to meet demand.
- Valeo specializes in the design, production, and sale of components and services for the automotive sector.
- Valeo forecast its 2022 annual core profit (EBITDA) margin in a range of 11.8% to 12.3%, compared with 13.4% last year.
- The company sees 2022 sales between 19.2 billion and 20.0 billion euros, representing a growth of 11-16% from a year earlier.
- Valeo said the outlook excluded possible effects from the Ukraine-Russia crisis, which could drag production volumes and hike energy or raw material prices, but included a negative impact from the integration of Valeo Siemens eAutomotive.
- CEO Christophe Perillat told a press conference that the direct impact of the Ukraine-Russia crisis should be minimal, stating the company has no sales in Ukraine while those in Russia account for less than 1% of its total sales.
- Valeo plans to increase its original equipment sales by around 13% in the medium term to reach a revenue of 27.5 billion euros ($30.73 billion) in 2025, betting on recovery in car production volumes.
- The company expects further revenue growth to 40 billion euros by 2030.
