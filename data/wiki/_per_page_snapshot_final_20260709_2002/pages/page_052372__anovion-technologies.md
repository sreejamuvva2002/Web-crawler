# Anovion Technologies

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Anovion Technologies holds groundbreaking ceremony - Development Authority of Bainbridge and Decatur County](https://www.bainbridgedecaturga.com/anovion-technologies-holds-groundbreaking-ceremony/)  ·  `page_052372`

## Facts

- Anovion Technologies held a groundbreaking ceremony for its new Bainbridge facility off of Newton Road on a Tuesday.
- The Chicago-headquartered company specializes in the production of synthetic graphite, a key component in lithium-ion batteries.
- According to Anovion CEO Eric Stopka at an initial announcement, this facility will be the first large-scale factory of its kind in North America.
- Development Authority Vice Chairman Andy Bell spoke at the podium, recognizing all state and local officials present.
- County Commission Chair Pete Stephens delivered the invocation at the event.
- Mayor Edward Reynolds spoke at the event and introduced Governor Kemp.
- Mayor Edward Reynolds stated that throughout Governor Kemp’s first term, Georgia has consistently broken economic records with jobs and investment coming to every corner of the state, including Bainbridge and Decatur County.
- Mayor Edward Reynolds touted the mayor’s record during his introduction, including tax cuts, teacher pay raises, and a crackdown on human trafficking.
- Governor Kemp thanked officials present and stated the project represents many great victories for the state as a culmination of hard work by local communities and sustained talks at the state level through the Department of Economic Development and support from the general assembly.
- Governor Kemp praised Anovion’s business and its stated commitment to build up the American supply chain for lithium-ion batteries, noting much of it is currently based in Asia.
- Governor Kemp reiterated that the facility will create 400 new jobs and represents an $800 million investment.
- Governor Kemp stated that with this project’s groundbreaking, all four corners of Georgia have seen major investment and significant job creation as a result of leading the next Industrial Revolution in the state.
- Anovion Executive Chairman Chip Dunn spoke at the event, recounting how he met Governor Kemp and how Anovion was persuaded to come to Georgia.
- According to Executive Chairman Chip Dunn, factors contributing to choosing Bainbridge include its inland port and access to waterway and land transport.
- Executive Chairman Chip Dunn stated that Anovion is the only commercial sale battery-grade synthetic graphite company in North America.
- Executive Chairman Chip Dunn discussed which components in lithium-ion batteries use synthetic graphite and emphasized the company’s commitment to being climate-focused.
- Executive Chairman Chip Dunn focused on the company’s commitment to reduce emissions associated with producing synthetic graphite, aiming to shift the narrative of the environmentally unfriendly process in China.
- According to Executive Chairman Chip Dunn, Anovion hopes to reduce its production’s carbon footprint to zero over time and aims to be the first major manufacturing business in the world to be carbon-free.
- Congressman Sanford Bishop spoke at the podium discussing how state and national government were involved in bringing Anovion to Georgia and how the state has benefited from federal manufacturing incentives.
- Congressman Sanford Bishop stated that federal resources combined with resources from the governor and general assembly are making historic improvements in high-speed internet connecting families, schools, and businesses.
- Congressman Sanford Bishop noted the Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act is improving roads, ports, railways, bridges, and airports as arteries for commerce.
- SRTC President Jim Glass delivered closing remarks at the ceremony.
- SRTC President Jim Glass revealed that Anovion will be working to offer scholarships in the future, with updates to come later.
- The facility is projected to be operational by 2025.
- The ceremony concluded with officials present breaking ground.
- Story provided by Ethan Reddish of The Post-Searchlight.
