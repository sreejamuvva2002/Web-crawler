# SAFT America, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 1992-09-14

**Source:** [SAFT America, Inc. - 03/19/1993 | Occupational Safety and Health Administration](https://www.osha.gov/enforcement/cwsa/saft-america-inc-03191993)  ·  `page_029775`

## Facts

- SAFT America, Inc. operates a nickel-cadmium battery manufacturing facility at 711 Industrial Boulevard, Valdosta, Georgia.
- The company entered into a settlement agreement with OSHA on March 19, 1993, to resolve litigation regarding the Occupational Safety and Health Standard for Exposure to Cadmium (29 CFR 1910.1027).
- SAFT aims to reduce employee exposure to airborne cadmium below the action level for 70% of exposed employees and to or below the PEL of 5 ug/m(3) for all employees by December 31, 1995.
- Employees currently work 12-hour shifts on three consecutive days and are off work for the next three consecutive days.
- The parties agreed that SAFT will be in compliance with 29 C.F.R. 1910.1027(c) if an employee's time-weighted-average exposure over every consecutive eight-hour period during a work shift is 5 ug/m(3) or less.
- SAFT must install dust-free despiraling by December 31, 1993.
- SAFT must install dust-free pay-off at ECC 1 & 2 by December 31, 1993.
- SAFT must install shrouds and particulate catch pans at six post "ready" stations on impregnation lines by December 31, 1993.
- SAFT must install drying vessel particulate capture modifications for five impregnation vessels by December 31, 1994.
- SAFT must install a central HEPA Vacuum System with drops at Impregnation Lines, ECC machines, and Despiral area by December 31, 1994.
- SAFT must install a pneumatic seal on the mixing bowl in the Plastic Bonded Electrode (PBE) Mix Facility by December 31, 1994.
- SAFT must install HEPA vacuum ventilation or an equally effective dust-removal method at the PBE decortication station by December 31, 1994.
- SAFT must enclose and ventilate AA and Sub-C coilers (winders) by December 31, 1995.
- SAFT must install dust control ventilation on all PSD Plate Preparation department equipment by December 31, 1995.
- SAFT must install enclosures and ventilation on all PBD Electrode Assembly Department equipment by December 31, 1995.
- SAFT must enclose and ventilate the PBD Electrode Assembly Department to the extent necessary by December 31, 1995.
- SAFT must develop and implement a comprehensive preventive maintenance program for cadmium exposure control equipment as it is installed.
- SAFT must discontinue the use of fans that may cause dispersion of cadmium dusts by March 14, 1993.
- SAFT must clean the Chemical Plant to remove accumulated cadmium dust by December 31, 1994.
- SAFT must clean the Assembly Plant to remove accumulated cadmium dust by December 31, 1995.
- SAFT must establish a work practice team by March 22, 1993, to monitor work practice improvements and determine if additional modifications can further reduce employee cadmium exposure.
- SAFT must install an air tunnel in the aisleway leading to the lunchroom by December 14, 1993, to remove cadmium dust from employees' clothing without dispersing it.
- Employees are required to pass through the air shower prior to entering vending and cafeteria areas for break and lunch.
- SAFT must require all employees exposed above the PEL to pass through the air tunnel at the end of their work shift.
- If engineering and work practice controls do not reduce all employee exposures to or below the PEL by December 31, 1995, SAFT must comply with wet shower requirements by April 30, 1996.
- OSHA will grant a permanent variance from the wet shower requirement if SAFT presents sufficient objective evidence that its air shower is as effective as a wet shower in removing cadmium from employees' hair and skin.
- SAFT must provide exposed employees with a list of possible solutions within 15 days of receiving monitoring results showing exposure over the PEL.
- Air monitoring results must be referred to the Work Practice Team, Supervisor, and Area Manager for assessment within 15 days.
- SAFT must post a report in the affected employee area within 30 working days of receiving monitoring results, detailing findings and corrective actions or reasons for no action.
- Lunchroom tables must be wet wiped before each use to maintain them free of cadmium.
- SAFT may monitor each job category by sampling a representative fraction of employees expected to have the highest cadmium exposure.
- Exposure monitoring is limited to between 7:00 a.m. and 7:00 p.m., unless variations in worksite conditions suggest higher exposures for night shift workers, in which case sampling must occur between 7:00 p.m. and 7:00 a.m.
- All air monitoring samples must be sent to an outside accredited analytical laboratory for analysis.
- After January 1, 1999, SAFT must fully comply with medical removal requirements for exposed employees.
- Between March 22, 1993, and January 1, 1999, SAFT must either fully comply with medical removal requirements or follow a specific medical removal protocol based on biological monitoring test results.
- Under the protocol, employees are removed to jobs with airborne cadmium exposure below the action level if B(2)-M levels exceed 300 ug/g Cr coupled with CdU above 3 ug/g Cr or CdB above 5 ug/lwb.
- Employees are removed if CdB levels exceed 10 ug/lwb.
- Employees are removed if B(2)-M exceeds 750 u/g Cr.
- Employees are removed if CdU levels exceed 10 ug/g Cr between July 1, 1993, and July 1, 1995; 9 ug/g Cr between July 2, 1995, and July 1, 1996; 8 ug/g Cr between July 2, 1996, and July 1, 1997; and 7 ug/g Cr thereafter.
- SAFT must notify OSHA of its choice between full compliance or the alternative medical removal protocol by July 1, 1993.
- By April 14, 1993, SAFT must provide initial medical examinations covering specified subjects to each employee covered by the medical surveillance program who has not had a qualifying examination after April 14, 1992.
- Any employee whose most recent examination showed a CdU level above 3 ug/g creatinine must receive a complete initial medical examination by April 14, 1993.
- SAFT must provide all employees covered by the medical surveillance program with periodic medical examinations by December 31, 1993.
- For employees who received a company-provided medical examination after April 14, 1992, SAFT must provide a complete initial or periodic medical examination within one year of their most recent exam.
- If some employees remain exposed above the PEL after December 31, 1995, SAFT must provide a change room in full compliance with the standard by April 30, 1996.
- Effective March 22, 1993, while not required to provide a change room immediately, SAFT must ensure employees remove and store protective clothing and equipment to minimize cadmium dispersion and prevent contamination of street clothes.
