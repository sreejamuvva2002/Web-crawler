# Clean Economy Works (CEW) Analysis

**Type:** research_center  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Clean Economy Works | October 2025 Analysis | E2](https://e2.org/reports/clean-economy-works-october-2025/)  ·  `page_008042`

## Facts

- This Clean Economy Works (CEW) analysis is part of E2’s ongoing monthly tracking of large-scale clean energy project announcements, cancellations, closures, and downsizes across the United States.
- The analysis monitors private-sector investment in clean energy manufacturing, generation, and grid infrastructure projects since federal energy tax credits were passed in August 2022.
- CEW methodology excludes projects that began, were proposed, sited, or in any way began development prior to the Inflation Reduction Act (IRA).
- CEW excludes projects funded entirely by federal sources or lacking specific geographic data.
- CEW measures key indicators including investment value, job creation or losses, project types (manufacturing, generation, research and development), and distribution by sector, state, and congressional district.
- Since 2025, the analysis began to include all project cancellations, closures, and downsizings going back to August 2022 due to rising business uncertainty about the future of U.S. clean energy policy.
- The tracking culminated in the rollback and restriction of energy tax credits included in the federal tax and spending bill passed in July 2025.
- E2’s methodology excludes temporary delays or ownership transfers that do not impact production capacity.
- In October, companies canceled, closed, or downsized nine major clean energy projects that would have otherwise resulted in more than $4.4 billion in economic investments and an estimated 8,700 new jobs.
- During the same month, only one new project was announced: a battery storage operation in Pennsylvania.
- Year-to-date through October 2025, companies canceled, closed, or scaled back 51 large-scale clean energy projects according to E2 analysis of projects tracked by E2 and the Clean Economy Tracker.
- The cancellation of these 51 projects represents the loss of nearly $28.8 billion in private investment in new factories and projects nationwide.
- The cancellations represent the loss of nearly 30,000 new jobs that were previously announced.
- Nine recent project abandonments and downsizes in October include the loss of more than $4.4 billion in investments and at least 8,700 previously announced jobs at major EV, battery, and other clean energy related factories in Mississippi, Tennessee, Michigan, Ohio, and elsewhere.
- Nissan (MS) lost 2,000 jobs and abandoned $500M on 9/30.
- Enchem (TN) lost 190 jobs and abandoned $152.5M on 10/1.
- General Motors (OH) lost 1,520 jobs and abandoned $760M on 10/2.
- GAF Energy (CA) lost 138 jobs and closed its R&D facility on 10/15.
- Gotion (MI) lost 2,350 jobs and abandoned $2.36B on 10/24.
- GM (MI) downsizing impacted 1,200 jobs with an undisclosed investment reduction on 10/29.
- GM/LG Energy Solution (OH) downsizing impacted 550 jobs with an undisclosed investment reduction on 10/29.
- Xalt Energy (MI) lost 550 jobs and abandoned $600M on 10/31.
- Dana (MI) lost 200 jobs and abandoned $54.2M on 10/21.
- Fifty-one large-scale clean energy projects have been canceled, closed, or downsized in 2025, which is more than any previous year since the IRA passed.
- These 51 projects were expected to create 29,944 new jobs and included $28.77 billion in planned investments that are now lost.
- Manufacturing facilities account for nearly all losses, accounting for 45 of the 51 projects abandoned in 2025 and over 29,000 jobs and $24.2 billion in investment lost.
- EV and battery/storage projects continue to drive the majority of cancellations: 24 EV projects and 25 battery/storage projects have been abandoned since 2025.
- Republican congressional districts experienced the greatest economic fallout with 37 canceled projects, 21,916 lost jobs, and $16.9 billion in abandoned investments compared with Democratic-held districts.
- Democratic-held districts saw 24 projects, 13,248 jobs, and $9.9 billion in lost investment.
- 13 projects, 4,378 jobs, and $4.6 billion in lost investment occurred where the specific congressional district is yet to be determined.
- Just a single new clean energy project announcement was made in October, marking the lowest number since E2 began tracking announcements in August 2022.
- Eos Energy Storage (PA) announced a battery/storage manufacturing plant expected to create 1,000 jobs and result in $352.9 million in investments.
- Since E2 began tracking projects in August 2022, companies have announced a total of 417 major clean energy manufacturing, generation, and grid projects across 42 states and Puerto Rico.
- These announced projects represent 124,241 planned jobs and about $132.33 billion in total investment.
- The U.S. clean energy economy is entering a period of instability driven largely by shifting federal policies, new federal roadblocks to clean energy development, and the rollback of clean energy tax incentives.
- Announcements tracking excludes projects that began development, were proposed, or applied for local and state approval before the passage of the Inflation Reduction Act (IRA).
- Tracking does not include investments in which the federal government has provided financial resources for the complete project, lease sales, or projects lacking specific geographic information.
- Cancellations/closures tracking includes all projects canceled or closed since the IRA passage in August 2022.
- The list excludes announced layoffs that are not associated with a project downsizing unless there is a stated decrease in production output.
- The list excludes transfer of project ownership if production will continue under new ownership, power purchasing agreements, or similar announcements.
- Project delays or idling of facilities are not included unless there is an announced decrease in production or investment or the project needs to be restarted to proceed in the future.
