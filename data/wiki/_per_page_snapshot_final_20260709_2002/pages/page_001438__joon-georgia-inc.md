# Joon Georgia, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Read more about Gov. Kemp: First Hyundai Motor Group Supplier to Build Facility in Bulloch County - EIN Presswire](https://www.einnews.com/pr_news/600010225/read-more-about-gov-kemp-first-hyundai-motor-group-supplier-to-build-facility-in-bulloch-county)  ·  `page_001438`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Joon Georgia, Inc., an automotive parts manufacturer, will create 630 new jobs and invest $317 million in Bulloch County.
- Plans for the company’s new manufacturing facility mark the first confirmed supplier for Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America in Bryan County.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that less than two weeks after the groundbreaking of Hyundai Motor Group’s innovative facility, they are already seeing the regional impact it will have.
- Governor Brian Kemp said Joon Georgia will be the first of many companies drawn to the Peach State to support the Metaplant, creating jobs and opportunity for generations of hardworking Georgians.
- Joon Georgia is part of Ajin USA, a current supplier for multiple Hyundai Motor Group facilities.
- The company produces a variety of automotive body parts and electronics for original equipment manufacturers.
- Jung Ho Sea, CEO of Ajin USA, stated they are excited to be one of the first Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America automotive suppliers to build an automotive parts manufacturing facility in Bulloch County and for their first plant in Georgia.
- Jung Ho Sea said Joon Georgia, Inc. will create approximately 630 new jobs with competitive benefits in Bulloch County and the State of Georgia.
- Jung Ho Sea stated their commitment is to be the best parts supplier for automotive and EV companies like Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America, and to be the best employer and corporate citizen giving back to the community of Bulloch County and the City of Statesboro.
- Joon Georgia’s new facility will be located at Bruce Yawn Commerce Park, previously known as the Southern Gateway Commerce Park, near Statesboro.
- The site is in close proximity to the Port of Savannah.
- With connections to the port, both Hyundai and Joon Georgia will be able to access global markets quickly and efficiently.
- Operations at the Bruce Yawn Commerce Park are expected to begin in mid-2024.
- The company will begin hiring mid-2023.
- Billy Allen, Chair of the Development Authority of Bulloch County, stated they are honored and proud to welcome Joon Georgia to the Statesboro-Bulloch County community and region.
- Billy Allen believes their great site in the Bruce Yawn Commerce Park and proximity to the new Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America will serve the company well.
- Billy Allen noted that education resources, including Georgia Southern University and Ogeechee Technical College, will provide critical workforce support to help Joon Georgia succeed.
- Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy Alyce Thornhill represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The project was in partnership with the Development Authority of Bulloch County, Georgia Quick Start, Georgia Ports Authority, and Georgia EMC.
- Workforce development for the new facility will be supported by Quick Start, the top-ranked workforce training program in the nation.
- By partnering with the Technical College System of Georgia, the program helps assess workers, train new employees on unique processes, and develop customized job-specific training using the most current techniques and media.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated Joon Georgia’s announcement is a landmark moment as they drive Georgia’s automotive industry into the future.
- Pat Wilson said Hyundai Motor Group, its suppliers, and the Savannah area JDA are creating generational opportunities for communities in Georgia.
- Pat Wilson noted that supporting the jobs of tomorrow is a team effort and regional collaboration ensures benefits are shared across county lines.
- Georgia is positioned as a hub for the electric mobility industry.
- Since 2020, more than 30 EV-related projects have contributed over $13.3 billion in investment and announced more than 18,100 jobs in Georgia.
- On October 25, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America officially broke ground in neighboring Bryan County on its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility.
- Non-affiliated suppliers connected to the Hyundai project are predicted to invest approximately $1 billion in other locations around the state.
