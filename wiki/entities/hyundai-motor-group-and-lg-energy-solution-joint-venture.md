# Hyundai Motor Group & LG Energy Solution Joint Venture

**Type:** Joint Venture  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[battery-cell-manufacturing|Battery Cell Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** Georgia, Bryan County, United States

## Overview

A 50/50 joint venture between Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution to build a USD 4.3 billion electric-vehicle battery manufacturing facility in Bryan County, Georgia.

## EV supply-chain relevance

The plant will produce up to 30 GWh of EV batteries per year - enough for roughly 300,000 electric vehicles - securing a stable domestic supply for the U.S. market.

## Key facts

- The joint venture will have an annual production capacity of 30 GWh, sufficient for about 300,000 electric vehicles.
- Each partner - Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution - will own 50% of the joint venture.
- Hyundai Motor Group aims to achieve EV sales of 2 million units per year by 2030.
- The plant is intended to provide a stable domestic supply of batteries for the growing U.S. electric-vehicle market.
- CEO Youngsoo Kwon highlighted the partnership as a driver for the EV transition in America.
