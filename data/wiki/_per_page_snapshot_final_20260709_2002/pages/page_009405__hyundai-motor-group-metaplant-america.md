# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-11-11

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor plays down missed IRA incentives for Georgia plant - The Korea Herald](https://www.koreaherald.com/view.php?ud=20241111050591)  ·  `page_009405`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor has missed out on the United States tax incentives for its new $7.6-billion manufacturing plant in Bryan County, Georgia.
- The facility is named Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA).
- According to industry sources, Hyundai Motor did not make the list of companies that will receive tax incentives in the second round allocations of the Qualifying Advanced Energy Project Credit (48C) program.
- The 48C program is an allocated tax credit funded by President Joe Biden’s Investing in America agenda through the Inflation Reduction Act.
- The Biden administration has assigned a total of $10 billion to the 48C program.
- Selected companies under the 48C program could receive up to a 30 percent tax credit for their investments on American soil.
- A local report estimated that Hyundai Motor could have received up to 480 billion won ($344 million) in tax incentives.
- Construction of the Georgia plant has been smooth sailing despite missing the additional tax incentives.
- Hyundai Motor already began early-stage production of electric vehicles at HMGMA in October.
- Hyundai Motor is expected to start full operation of the new plant in the first quarter of next year.
- A Hyundai Motor official stated that while they applied for the 48C program, they did not expect benefits because those tax incentives were made for parts makers not automakers.
- The same official noted that the 48C program was not in consideration when investing in HMGMA.
- The US Department of Energy announced a list of 35 projects across 20 states selected for a total of $1.93 billion in the first round allocations of the 48C program in April this year.
- The first-round projects were categorized into four areas: clean energy and clean vehicle manufacturing, critical minerals and materials, grid components and modernization, and industrial decarbonization.
- Mobis North America electrified Powertrain, a US subsidiary of Hyundai Motor Group’s auto parts maker Hyundai Mobis, was awarded $57.6 million in tax credits.
- The awarded funds are for constructing three different EV component plants that will provide parts for the production of Hyundai and Kia electric vehicles.
