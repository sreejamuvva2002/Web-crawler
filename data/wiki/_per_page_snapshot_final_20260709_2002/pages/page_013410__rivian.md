# Rivian

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2021-12-16

**Source:** [Georgia Power welcomes Rivian to Georgia PR Newswire](https://smb.panews.com/article/Georgia-Power-welcomes-Rivian-to-Georgia/61bbb772dc3d1e0ccb934707)  ·  `page_013410`

## Facts

- Rivian is a world-class electric sport utility vehicle and pickup company.
- Rivian was named the 2022 MotorTrend Truck of the Year.
- Rivian selected Georgia for a nearly 13 million square foot plant for vehicle and battery operations along Interstate 20 near Social Circle.
- The project brings an estimated 7,500 jobs and $5 billion investment to Georgia.
- Georgia Power joined with Governor Brian Kemp and the Georgia Department of Economic Development to welcome Rivian to the state.
- The Electric Mobility Innovation Alliance (EMIA) was launched in July by Governor Kemp to unite public/private leaders, researchers, and utilities for EV industry recruitment and workforce development.
- Chris Womack serves as president, chairman and CEO of Georgia Power.
- Georgia currently has a network of nearly 1,000 public charging stations from multiple partners available across the state.
- Georgia Power supports the EV needs of several OEMs in the Southeast region including Kia, Mercedes-Benz, BMW, and Volkswagen.
- Other EV industry leaders that have moved to or expanded in Georgia include battery producer SK Innovation and EnChem; modules and storage systems producer Duckyang; charging station leader Heliox; and all-electric bus manufacturer Blue Bird.
- Georgia is home to more than 200 automotive-related facilities that contribute more than $3 billion annually to the state's economy.
- In 2021, Georgia had 977,000 workers supporting its automotive operations and facilities.
- Before Rivian's announcement, Georgia's EV and battery production industry created 5,700 new jobs and $3 billion in new capital investment.
- Georgia ranks sixth in the nation for EV charging infrastructure and is currently the top state in the Southeast for EV customer adoption.
- Georgia Power partnered with the University of Georgia's College of Engineering to create an e-Mobility Certificate program covering engineering, public policy, public health, and business acumen to prepare the workforce for electrified transportation.
- Georgia Power is working closely with design students at Georgia Tech to evaluate the environmental benefits and future workforce opportunities of transportation electrification.
- Georgia Power joined Southern Company and utilities across the South, Midwest, Gulf Coast, Central Plains, and mid-Atlantic to enable seamless EV travel through a coordinated charging network.
- The Electric Highway Coalition will provide increased charging options along major highway routes from Texas to Washington, DC.
- Georgia Power is the largest electric subsidiary of Southern Company (NYSE: SO) and serves 2.6 million customers in all but four of Georgia's 159 counties.
- Georgia Power maintains a generation mix that includes nuclear, coal, natural gas, solar, hydroelectric, and wind energy.
