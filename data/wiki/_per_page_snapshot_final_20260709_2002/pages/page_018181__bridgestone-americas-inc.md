# Bridgestone Americas, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2025-07-31

**Source:** [Bridgestone Americas, Inc. - MarkLines Automotive Industry Portal](https://www.marklines.com/en/top500/bridgestone-americas)  ·  `page_018181`

## Facts

- Bridgestone Americas, Inc. is a subsidiary of Bridgestone Corporation (Japan), which holds 100% share capital.
- The company primarily develops, manufactures, and markets tires under brands including Bridgestone, Firestone, Bandag, SureDrive, and Fuzion.
- It produces tires for passenger vehicles, light trucks, commercial vehicles, agricultural vehicles, motorcycles, and off-road vehicles.
- In January 2025, the company announced plans to initiate strategic initiatives to optimize its business footprint, improve competitiveness, and enhance U.S. operations.
- As part of these January 2025 initiatives, the company announced the decision to close its Truck and Bus Radial Tire Plant in LaVergne, Tennessee, effective July 31, 2025.
- Other January 2025 cost optimization initiatives include reducing plant capacity and workforce at its agriculture tire plant in Des Moines, Iowa, and implementing workforce reductions across corporate and sales units.
- The company's tire product portfolio includes all-season tires, touring tires, run-flat tires, all-terrain tires, maximum traction tires, performance tires, summer tires, winter tires, sedan/minivan tires, utility vehicle tires, truck tires, bus tires, agriculture tires, and off-road heavy-duty tires.
- The company also provides other solutions such as end-to-end fleet solutions and Airide air springs.
- In 1988, Bridgestone Corporation purchased The Firestone Tire & Rubber Company.
- In 1990, corporate headquarters for the merged companies moved from Chicago to Akron, Ohio, and Americas operations were consolidated as Bridgestone/Firestone, Inc.
- In 1990, the company finished building a production facility in Warren County, Tennessee.
- In 1992, corporate headquarters moved from Akron, Ohio to Nashville, Tennessee.
- In 1994, the Warren County plant in Tennessee became the first U.S. tire plant to meet the ISO9000 quality standard.
- In 1999, the company opened a passenger and light truck tire manufacturing facility in Aiken County, South Carolina.
- In 2001, Bridgestone Corporation's Americas operations were reorganized under a holding company structure with Bridgestone Americas Holding, Inc. (BSAH).
- In 2004, the Joliette plant in Quebec, Canada produced its 100-millionth tire.
- In 2006, the Aiken County plant in South Carolina produced its 50-millionth tire.
- In 2007, Bridgestone Firestone do Brasil Industria e Comercio Ltda inaugurated its second Brazilian facility in the state of Bahia.
- In May 2007, Bridgestone Americas Holding, Inc. acquired Bridgestone Bandag, LLC.
- In 2009, Bridgestone Americas Holding, Inc. was renamed to Bridgestone Americas, Inc. (BSAM).
- In September 2021, the company completed the acquisition of Azuga Holdings Inc. (Azuga).
- In June 2022, the company opened a new Advanced Tire Production Center (ATPC) in Akron, Ohio.
