# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Inside Hyundai’s Metaplant: What It Takes to Power the Future - Korea](https://www.europesays.com/korea/3378/)  ·  `page_047662`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) is the company’s third U.S. production hub.
- HMGMA began full production in Ellabell, Georgia, in October 2024.
- The factory spans 2,900 acres.
- The site currently produces Hyundai’s Ioniq 5 and Ioniq 9 electric SUVs.
- Plans are in place to expand production to Kia and Genesis models in the future.
- HMGMA plans to add 8,500 on-site jobs.
- This makes it Georgia’s largest economic development project to date.
- At present, the site employs 1,623 workers referred to as "Meta Pros".
- 85% of the current workforce is from Georgia.
- 65% of the current workforce is from the greater Savannah area.
- HMGMA has a production capacity of up to 300,000 vehicles each year.
- The plant can scale up to produce 500,000 vehicles annually when it reaches its goal of 8,500 workers.
- Hyundai invited journalists to tour the production facilities to observe various stages of the car-building process.
- The facility utilizes a streamlined operation where humans and robots work side by side.
- Every vehicle begins as thousands of individual parts that pass through countless steps before arriving at dealerships.
- Trained specialists examine every nook and cranny of each car to ensure it meets the highest quality standards.
- A drive test is one of the final steps in the car-building process, and every car must pass through the facility's test track before continuing in production.
- Hyundai allowed media personnel to drive through the test track for the first time during this tour.
- The test track includes driving over various bumps to listen for any unusual noises.
- The test track includes "figure 8" maneuvers to assess steering wheel responsiveness.
- The test track tests all braking functions on both uphill and downhill inclines.
- A full-speed brake check is a required part of the testing process.
- The track session also tests active safety and driver assistance functions, including lane-keeping assist features.
- Before reaching the production line, employees must complete on-site training at a Quick Start facility known as the Hyundai Mobility Training Center.
- Trainees receive field-specific hands-on training that previews the actual production line.
- The training center features classrooms with instructors and a daily class schedule similar to college courses.
- Training stations include general assembly, exterior quality testing, and a disassembly area.
- Trainees use an S.T.E.P. simulator featuring a large touchscreen while walking on a slowly moving conveyor belt.
