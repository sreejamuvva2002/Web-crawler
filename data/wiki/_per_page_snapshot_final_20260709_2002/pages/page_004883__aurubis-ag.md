# Aurubis AG

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2021-11-10

**Source:** [Aurubis to Open Recycling and Copper Smelting Facility in Augusta | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-release/aurubis-open-state-art-recycling-and-copper-smelting-facility-augusta)  ·  `page_004883`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Aurubis will invest $340 million (€300 million) in a state-of-the-art recycling and secondary smelting facility for multi-metal recycling in Augusta.
- The new facility will be the very first of its kind in the U.S.
- The facility will create 125 jobs in the Augusta area.
- Governor Kemp stated this marks the single-largest investment made by a German company in the State of Georgia.
- Governor Kemp cited Georgia's ports system, highly-skilled workforce, and pro-business environment as factors that attracted the opportunity.
- Aurubis will join more than 500 German companies with operations in Georgia.
- Aurubis’ commitment to sustainable development and raw material security will play a role in building Georgia’s electric mobility value chain.
- From Augusta, the company plans to produce materials found in lithium-ion batteries and other electronics.
- CEO Roland Harings stated the investment sets an example of sustainable growth and positions Aurubis as a forerunner for multi-metal recycling in the U.S., matching its role in Europe.
- Aurubis will build its facility on 150 acres at Augusta Corporate Park.
- The site is a “Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) Certified” location.
- Operations are expected to begin by the first half of 2024.
- The company plans to begin hiring for positions as soon as summer 2022.
- Steven Kendrick, Chairman of the Development Authority of Augusta, welcomed Aurubis as a new corporate tenant and industry leader building a world-class advanced manufacturing facility.
- Kendrick noted the Augusta Corporate Park is proud to host the company and that the Augusta Economic Development Authority stands ready to support its success over the next several decades.
- Griff Lynch, Executive Director of the Georgia Ports Authority (GPA), stated the GPA will help bring jobs and investment to Georgia.
- Lynch noted exports from the Aurubis recycling operation will strengthen the state’s global position via Savannah, which is already one of the nation’s most balanced ports in terms of import-export trade.
- Fernanda Kirchner, Senior Project Manager for GDEcD’s Global Commerce division, represented the department on this project.
- The project involved partnership with the Augusta Economic Development Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, and Georgia Power.
- Sérgio Domingues, Managing Director for the State of Georgia’s Europe Office, played a supporting role in attracting Aurubis to Georgia.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson celebrated the largest single investment by a German company in Georgia.
- Wilson highlighted that Georgia is home to more than 500 German companies and that the longstanding relationship with Germany helps create jobs for green manufacturing.
- Augusta Corporate Park completed due diligence and specific certification requirements prior to prospective business visits as a GRAD Certified park.
- Receiving GRAD certification is a proactive way for community planners and landowners to catalyze economic growth and industrial development.
- Thousands of Georgia jobs have been created on GRAD sites since the program’s inception in 2008.
- Aurubis AG is a leading global provider of non-ferrous metals and one of the largest copper recyclers worldwide.
- The company processes complex metal concentrates, scrap metals, organic and inorganic metal-bearing recycling materials, and industrial residues into high-quality metals.
- Aurubis produces more than 1 million tons of copper cathodes annually.
- From copper cathodes, it makes wire rod, continuous cast shapes, profiles, and flat rolled products made of copper and copper alloys.
- The company also produces precious metals, selenium, lead, nickel, tin, and zinc.
- The portfolio includes additional products such as sulfuric acid and iron silicate.
- Carter Chapman serves as Press Secretary for the Office of Governor Brian P. Kemp.
- Annalise Morning serves as Deputy Press Secretary for the Office of Governor Brian P. Kemp.
- Jessica Atwell serves as Communications Manager for the Georgia Department of Economic Development.
