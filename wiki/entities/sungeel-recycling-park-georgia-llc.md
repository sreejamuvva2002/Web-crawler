# SungEel Recycling Park Georgia, LLC

**Type:** Company  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[battery-recycling|Battery Recycling]]  ·  **Location:** Hayestone Brady Business Park, Toccoa, Stephens County, Georgia, United States

## Overview

Georgia Governor Brian Kemp announced that SungEel Recycling Park Georgia, LLC, a subsidiary of Korea-based SungEel HiTech Co., Ltd., will invest more than $37 million to build the first U.S. lithium-ion battery recycling facility in Stephens County, creating 104 jobs.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Recycles end-of-life electric vehicle batteries and manufacturing scrap, recovering nickel, cobalt and lithium for reuse in the EV supply chain.

## Key facts

- The recycling system uses a 100 % full circulatory process achieving metal recovery rates above 95 %.
- Recovered metals include nickel, cobalt and lithium, essential for EV battery production.
- SungEel has more than 10 years of patented technology in battery recycling.
- Georgia’s electric-mobility ecosystem has attracted over $13 billion in investments and created more than 18,100 jobs since 2020.
- The project is located on a GRAD-certified site ready for accelerated development.
