# EMAAR Group

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [UAE's EMAAR Group to Invest $6 Billion in Georgia | EurAsia Gulf Business Platform](https://eurasiagulf.org/news/uaes-emaar-group-invest-6-billion-georgia)  ·  `page_009646`

## Facts

- UAE-based real estate giant EMAAR Group signed a memorandum of understanding to invest approximately $6 billion in Georgia.
- The agreement was signed in the UAE by Levan Davitashvili, Georgia's Minister of Economy; Tornike Rijvadze, Chairman of the Government of Adjara; and Mohammed Ali Rashed Alabbar, Founder and Managing Director of EMAAR Group.
- The investment will fund a multifunctional development project creating infrastructure in Tbilisi, Batumi, and Gonio.
- Georgian Prime Minister Irakli Kobakhidze led the delegation for the signing.
- Prime Minister Irakli Kobakhidze stated the project will significantly contribute to economic development, accelerate growth rates, create new jobs, and attract more investors.
- Mohammed Ali Rashed Alabbar stated the investment will motivate many other investors and businessmen in Georgia, mirroring their track record in other countries.
- Minister of Economy Levan Davitashvili stated EMAAR's entry will completely change the Georgian real estate market and elevate it to a qualitatively different level.
- The $6 billion investment is expected to drive economic growth of approximately 2-3% for Tbilisi and Batumi within a year.
- The historic investment represents about 20% of Georgia's economy.
- The project is expected to attract additional investments and significantly boost the country's overall economic growth.
