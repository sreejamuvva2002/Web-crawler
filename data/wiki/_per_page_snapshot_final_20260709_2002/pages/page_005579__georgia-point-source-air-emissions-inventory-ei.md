# Georgia Point Source Air Emissions Inventory (EI)

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-05-31

**Source:** [Point Source Emissions Inventory | Environmental Protection Division](https://epd.georgia.gov/forms-permits/air-protection-branch-forms-permits/point-source-emissions-inventory)  ·  `page_005579`

## Facts

- Facilities whose potential to emit (PTE) emissions equal or exceed specific thresholds for pollutants including SO2, VOC, NOx, CO, Pb, PM10, PM2.5, and NH3 are required to submit data for the 2025 calendar year.
- The 2025 EI is an annual reporting year with higher thresholds than a triennial year.
- Facilities identified by the Air Protection Branch (APB) as potentially needing to participate will receive process information by email by the beginning of January.
- Facilities must opt out or submit their 2025 EI data by the deadline of May 31, 2026.
- The Emission Inventory is required by federal regulation 40 CFR Part 51 Subpart A and Georgia Rule 391-3-1-.02(6)(b)1.
- The Point Source Air Emissions Inventory consists of two parts: the Facility Inventory (FI), which contains relatively static emission units and release points, and the Emission Inventory (EI), which contains changing process details, operating parameters, and emissions.
- Georgia collects air emission inventory data using the Combined Air Emissions Reporting System (CAERS), an EPA-developed application, replacing the former "Georgia Environmental Connections Online" (GECO) system since 2019.
- The submission process requires facilities to update contact information via GECO, determine participation status in CAERS via the EPA Central Data Exchange (CDX), and submit/certify an opt-out form or the 2025 EI in CAERS.
- Facilities that did not operate from January 1 to December 31, 2025, do not need to submit an opt-out form but must still update contact information and answer prompts in CAERS.
- The "preparer" role creates and prepares reports on the EPA CDX platform but cannot certify the submission, while the "certifier" is the authorized facility representative who submits the report.
- Potential to emit (PTE) is based on the latest applicable permit, with sources including permits, permit narratives, and Title V applications.
- Important dates include: GECO application posting on Dec. 1, 2025; GA Kickoff Training on Dec. 10, 2025; CAERS opening on Jan. 20, 2026; and the May 31, 2026 deadline for report submission.
- Virtual help sessions are provided on Tuesdays and Thursdays at 10:30 AM starting in March 2026.
- As of June 19, 2023, Georgia Rule 391-3-1-.02(6)(a)4. was removed, eliminating the requirement for facilities with NOx and/or VOC actual emissions greater than 25 tons per year in specific counties to submit annual Emissions Statement data.
