# Aurubis AG

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2021-11-26

**Source:** [DGAP-News: Aurubis AG: Aurubis and SMS to cooperate in construction of multimetal recycling plant in Georgia (US)](https://www.centralcharts.com/en/549-aurubis/news/3456521-dgap-news-aurubis-ag-aurubis-and-sms-to-cooperate-in-construction-of-multimetal-recycling-plant-in-georgia-us)  ·  `page_017420`

## Facts

- Aurubis AG and SMS group signed a contract for the planning, delivery, and assembly of the main facilities for a new multimetal recycling plant in the United States.
- Aurubis announced on November 10 that construction of the new recycling plant will start in mid-2022 in Augusta (Richmond County), Georgia.
- Commissioning is scheduled for the first half of 2024.
- Upon commissioning, the plant will process about 90,000 t of complex recycling materials annually.
- Aurubis is creating over 100 jobs in the region.
- SMS group will plan the facility concept, deliver it, and implement it on site for the greenfield project Aurubis Richmond.
- SMS group is delivering technology for a top-blown rotary converter (TBRC) to process complex recycling materials and recover copper, nickel, tin, zinc, precious metals, and platinum group metals.
- The scope of delivery includes sampling and off-gas cleaning facilities.
- Aurubis and SMS signed a long-term cooperation agreement to enable quick implementation of additional modular recycling facilities in Europe and North America upon a positive investment decision from Aurubis.
- The construction of Aurubis Richmond serves as the starting point for this partnership in the multimetal recycling sector.
- Hans Rosenstock is the Managing Director of Aurubis Richmond.
- This will be the first-ever secondary smelter for multimetal recycling in the United States.
- Circuit boards, copper cable, and other metal-bearing recycling materials will be processed into 35,000 t of blister copper annually.
- Aurubis will further process intermediate products into various industrial and precious metals at its European smelter sites to a great extent, while also selling them directly in the US market.
