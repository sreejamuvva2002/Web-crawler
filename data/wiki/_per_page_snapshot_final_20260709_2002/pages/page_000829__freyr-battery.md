# FREYR Battery

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-11-13

**Source:** [Battery manufacturer to invest $2.57 billion in Coweta County](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/battery-manufacturer-invest-2-57-221600831.html?fr=sycsrp_catchall)  ·  `page_000829`

## Facts

- FREYR Battery, a developer of clean, next-generation battery cell production capacity, will invest $2.57 billion into Georgia's sustainable technology ecosystem.
- FREYR Battery will create 723 new jobs over the next seven years at a manufacturing facility in Coweta County.
- Gov. Brian Kemp stated that job creators and innovators from all over the world bring their operations to Georgia because they know they will have success here.
- Gov. Brian Kemp expressed pride in welcoming FREYR to Georgia as the latest company to bring transformational investments and opportunity to communities.
- Gov. Brian Kemp referred to Georgia as the No. 1 state for business and looked forward to FREYR's growing impact on Coweta and the surrounding region.
- FREYR is dedicated to supporting a domestic supply chain for renewable power sources.
- FREYR's battery cells can be used for stationary energy storage, electric mobility, and additional applications.
- FREYR co-founder and CEO Tom Einar Jensen stated the company is deeply committed to decarbonizing the transportation and energy sectors with U.S. partners.
- Tom Einar Jensen noted that as they advance their U.S. expansion plan with key stakeholders, they expect to make meaningful investments to spur job creation and develop localized, decarbonized supply chains in the U.S. to enhance energy security and economic activity.
- FREYR's new facility will be located at the Bridgeport Industrial Park site.
- The Bridgeport Industrial Park site has been zoned for a mix of heavy and light industrial use since 2014.
- The company will hire technicians, operators, and engineers as well as executive and administrative roles.
- The facility will be called Giga America.
- Giga America will have a planned first phase production of approximately 34 gigawatt hours.
- The facility will use the U.S.-based 24M Technologies SemiSolidTM platform to simplify the production process and increase manufacturing efficiency.
- FREYR is currently evaluating clean power supply solutions for the new facility, including the potential use of renewable energy from solar installations.
- Coweta County Development Authority Chair John Daviston expressed pleasure at welcoming FREYR Battery to the community.
- John Daviston highlighted the opportunities the venture brings to the area and the addition of quality, highly-skilled jobs that will significantly stimulate the local economy.
- Metro Atlanta Chamber President and CEO Katie Kirkpatrick expressed excitement about welcoming FREYR Battery to metro Atlanta, which has quickly become a hub for battery cell research and production.
- Katie Kirkpatrick noted that FREYR will benefit from the region's vibrant research ecosystem and diversity of talent.
- Project Manager John Soper represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The project was developed in partnership with Coweta County Development Authority, the Metro Atlanta Chamber, Georgia EMC, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that companies like FREYR are driving a global revolution based on sustainable technology and solutions.
- Pat Wilson noted that electrification is changing how we power entire cities beyond EVs and mobility.
- Pat Wilson mentioned that to support electrification in home and commercial storage, they are developing a battery ecosystem with manufacturers, recyclers, and customers.
- Pat Wilson stated that Georgia and its communities are ready to support a sustainable future for generations to come.
