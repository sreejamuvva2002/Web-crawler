# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-05-20

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Group to Establish First Dedicated EV Plant and Battery Manufacturing Facility in the U.S. - HMGMA](https://www.hmgma.com/2022/05/20/hyundai-motor-group-to-establish-first-dedicated-ev-plant-and-battery-manufacturing-facility-in-the-u-s/)  ·  `page_009287`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group entered an agreement with the State of Georgia to build its first dedicated full electric vehicle and battery manufacturing facilities in the U.S.
- The new facilities represent an investment of approximately USD 5.54 billion.
- Commercial production is expected to begin in the first half of 2025.
- The facility will have an annual capacity of 300,000 units.
- Hyundai Motor Group plans to produce a wide range of innovative full electric vehicle models for U.S. customers at the new Georgia plant.
- Details regarding specific production models will be shared at later dates.
- The local EV production aims to increase U.S. consumer accessibility to the Group’s innovative EVs.
- The battery manufacturing facility will be established through a strategic partnership, with details to be disclosed at a later stage.
- Through the battery facility, the Group aims to establish a stable supply chain and build a healthy EV ecosystem in the U.S.
- The EV and battery manufacturing plant will be located on a dedicated 2,923-acre site in Bryan County, Georgia.
- The site has immediate access to I-95 and I-16 highways, providing easy access to 250 major metro areas.
- The site is located less than 50 kilometers from the Port of Savannah.
- The Port of Savannah is the single-largest and fastest-growing container terminal in the U.S., featuring two Class I rail facilities on-site provided by Georgia Central Railway.
- West Point, Georgia is already home to the Group’s Kia manufacturing hub in the U.S.
- Hyundai Motor Group selected Georgia due to favorable business conditions including speed-to-market, a talented workforce, and an existing network of affiliates and suppliers.
- Georgia is the Southeastern leader for EV registration per 1,000 registered automobiles.
- Georgia constantly develops access to publicly available EV charging stations and already offers more outlets per capita than anywhere else in the Southeast.
- The Group’s new investment will help accelerate the state’s sustainable shift to electrification.
- The project is expected to create about 8,100 new jobs.
- The EV plant investment is part of the Group’s 2021 announcement to invest USD 7.4 billion by 2025 to foster future mobility in the U.S., including EV production and smart mobility solutions.
- Hyundai Motor Group aims to become a leader in the U.S. auto market’s EV transition with the new facility.
- The Group has a global target to sell 3.23 million full electric vehicles annually by 2030.
- To realize this goal, the Group plans to establish a global EV production network to ensure a stable supply of EVs around the globe.
- With additional U.S. EV and battery production capabilities, the Group aims to become one of the top three EV providers in the U.S. by 2026.
- The Group plans to lead the EV market in sales, design, technologies, and mobility solutions optimized for the electric mobility era.
- The investment aligns with the U.S. government’s roadmap to accelerate electrification as global OEMs expand local EV production in the U.S.
- Last year, the U.S. government announced an initiative requiring zero-emission vehicle sales to account for at least half of OEM new vehicle sales by 2030.
- The new plant will feature a highly connected, automated, and flexible manufacturing system that organically connects all elements of the EV ecosystem.
- The Group plans to implement advanced intelligent manufacturing technologies currently under test at the Hyundai Motor Group Innovation Center in Singapore (HMGICS).
- The Georgia facility will operate as an intelligent manufacturing plant where order collection, procurement, logistics, and production processes are optimized using AI and data.
- The innovative manufacturing system will create a human-centered work environment with robots assisting human workers.
- As part of its sustainability commitment, the plant will mainly rely on renewable energy sources and use emission-reduction technologies to meet RE100 requirements.
- Official signing ceremony was held in Bryan County, attended by Georgia Governor Brian P. Kemp and other Georgia officials.
- Hyundai Motor Company President and CEO Jaehoon Chang and Hyundai Motor President and Chief Operating Officer José Muñoz attended the signing ceremony.
- Hyundai Motor Group Executive Chair Euisun Chung joined the signing ceremony virtually.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated the project is the largest in the state’s history, delivering high-quality jobs on the leading edge of mobility to Georgians.
- Governor Brian Kemp noted it is incredible that within a year of Georgia’s investment in the Bryan County Megasite, they are locating a world-class project with a company with great Georgia history.
- Euisun Chung stated the Group is proud to open its first dedicated full EV and battery manufacturing facilities in the U.S. and is excited to partner with Georgia for electrified mobility and sustainability.
- Jaehoon Chang stated they decided to build their first dedicated EV plant in the U.S. because America embraces change and drives innovation, and it will help meet growing demands for leading edge design, safe, zero-emissions vehicles.
