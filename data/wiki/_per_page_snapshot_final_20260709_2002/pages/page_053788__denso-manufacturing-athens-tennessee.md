# DENSO Manufacturing Athens Tennessee

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2015-01-28

**Source:** [Governor Haslam, Commissioner Boyd Announce DENSO Manufacturing Athens Tennessee to Expand Operations - TNECD](https://tnecd.com/news/governor-haslam-commissioner-boyd-announce-denso-manufacturing-athens-tennessee-to-expand-operations/)  ·  `page_053788`

## Facts

- DENSO Manufacturing Athens Tennessee (DMAT) officials announced the company will expand production of its gasoline direct injection (GDI) capability in Athens by building a new 224,000 square foot facility at its existing location.
- DENSO will invest more than $85 million and create 400 new jobs over the next three years in McMinn County.
- The announcement marks DMAT’s third major investment in an 18-month period.
- The overall expansion effort will represent a total investment of approximately $190 million and more than 700 new jobs.
- Production at the new facility will begin in July 2016.
- Tennessee Gov. Bill Haslam stated that the announcement brings Athens another step closer to the goal of making Tennessee the No. 1 location in the Southeast for high quality jobs.
- Economic and Community Development Commissioner Randy Boyd noted that Tennessee’s automotive strength is reinforced by companies like DENSO that make quality products for suppliers throughout the United States.
- Brian Nolen, General Manager of DMAT’s Gasoline Products Manufacturing Division, stated the project is critical to support the growing need of gasoline direct injection in North America as automakers turn to this technology to meet fuel-efficiency and emissions regulations.
- The localization of gasoline direct injection technology is resulting in new jobs, new production lines, new approaches to training, and new growth among supply partners in North America.
- DMAT currently employs a workforce of more than 1,450 and has been producing fuel injectors since 1998 for the North American region.
- DENSO’s latest generation of GDI technology is geared to deliver more power and lower emissions while being more compact.
- Athens Mayor Ann Davis expressed delight at the expansion, noting it has meant many new jobs for citizens and encouraged continued efforts to ensure a skilled and trained workforce.
- McMinn County Mayor John Gentry highlighted that DENSO Manufacturing’s latest expansion reinforces the positive relationship McMinn County has enjoyed with the company for the past 17 years.
- TVA Senior Vice President of Economic Development John Bradley stated that TVA and Athens Utilities Board are pleased to partner with the state of Tennessee, city of Athens, and McMinn County Economic Development Authority to support DENSO’s expansion and major investment.
- Frank Snyder, chairman of the McMinn County Industrial Development Board, described DENSO as a wonderful corporate citizen whose expansion is a big boost for McMinn County and surrounding communities.
- DENSO Corporation is headquartered in Kariya, Aichi prefecture, Japan, and is a leading global automotive supplier of advanced technology, systems, and components in thermal, powertrain control, electronics, information, and safety.
- DENSO’s customers include all the world’s major carmakers.
- Worldwide, DENSO has more than 200 subsidiaries and affiliates in 38 countries and regions and employs nearly 140,000 people.
- Consolidated global sales for DENSO for the fiscal year ending March 31, 2014, totaled US$39.8 billion.
- Last fiscal year, DENSO spent 9 percent of its global consolidated sales on research and development.
- DENSO common stock is traded on the Tokyo and Nagoya stock exchanges.
- In North America, DENSO employs more than 17,000 people at 32 consolidated companies and affiliates.
- Of DENSO’s North American facilities, 28 are manufacturing locations in the U.S., Canada, and Mexico.
- In the U.S. alone, DENSO employs more than 14,000 people across California, Michigan, North Carolina, South Carolina, Tennessee, Kentucky, Georgia, Iowa, Ohio, Alabama, and Arkansas.
- DENSO’s North American consolidated sales totaled US$7.9 billion for the fiscal year ending March 31, 2014.
- Tennessee was named 2014 and 2013 “State of the Year” for economic development by Business Facilities magazine.
- The Tennessee Department of Economic and Community Development’s mission is to develop strategies to make Tennessee the No. 1 location in the Southeast for high quality jobs, attract new corporate investment, and facilitate expansion and economic growth.
