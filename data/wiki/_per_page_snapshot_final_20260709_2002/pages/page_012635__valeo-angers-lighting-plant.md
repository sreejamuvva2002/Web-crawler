# Valeo Angers Lighting Plant

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Valeo completes 45 million euro maintenance of Angers lighting plant - Automobile Magazine](https://automobilemagazine.com.tr/valeo-completes-45-million-euro-maintenance-of-angers-lighting-plant)  ·  `page_012635`

## Facts

- Valeo is completing a €45 million (US$51.75 million) modernization of its Angers lighting plant in France.
- The modernization program was carried out between 2023 and 2026.
- The investment establishes Angers as Valeo’s European competence center for heavy goods vehicle (HGV) headlights.
- The project supports the Valeo Elevate 2028 strategic plan.
- The plant employs more than 1,200 people across Angers and Blois.
- The facility produces 9,000 headlights and 22,000 modules per day for leading global car manufacturers.
- The modernized facility operates 25 injection presses, five varnishing lines, 11 assembly lines, and 181 robots.
- Artificial intelligence is utilized in the plant for predictive simulation and real-time verification.
- The heavy-duty segment is identified as the main driver of growth for the plant.
- Production share for the heavy-duty segment is expected to increase from 20% to 40%.
- Two major customers are scheduled to enter service at the plant in 2027 and 2028.
- Six of the seven European road transport leaders are projected to use headlights developed by Angers by 2030.
- Jean-Luc Terrasse, Chief Executive Officer of Valeo’s Light Division, stated that lighting is at the heart of tomorrow’s mobility challenges regarding safety, design, and intelligent interaction.
- Terrasse noted that the investment allows the factory to consolidate its industrial excellence and demonstrates that a French factory can design and produce systems of unprecedented optical complexity at the forefront of European Industry 4.0.
- The Angers investment serves as a test case for European industrial policy, demonstrating that complex optical manufacturing is feasible in France.
