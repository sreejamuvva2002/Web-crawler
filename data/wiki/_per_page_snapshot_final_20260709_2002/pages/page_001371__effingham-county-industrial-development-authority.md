# Effingham County Industrial Development Authority

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Southeast Georgia’s Next Generation Is Ready for Growth – Site Selection Magazine](https://siteselection.com/southeast-georgias-next-generation-is-ready-for-growth/)  ·  `page_001371`

## Facts

- Effingham College and Career Academy (ECCA) initiated the Workforce PhD program in partnership with ECIDA and area employers in 2021, and is now launching its fourth round.
- The Workforce PhD program teaches communications, financial literacy, and other soft skills that employers say are often lacking.
- Kaydence Stearns, an 18-year-old graduate of South Effingham High School, participated in the Workforce PhD program.
- Stearns was placed into a two-year logistics pathway class as a junior at South Effingham High School.
- Stearns works at AeroDynamic Aviation, Inc., one of the initial partners of the Workforce PhD program.
- AeroDynamic Aviation expanded into a larger facility a few years ago and currently has 68 employees.
- Stearns started in shipping at AeroDynamic Aviation before moving to an expediter position due to her fast learning ability.
- AeroDynamic Aviation plans for Stearns to eventually move into its planning department to help plan work orders.
- AeroDynamic Aviation is building a new paint shop at its new site located just north of Springfield.
- CEO Chris Burnsed states that the Workforce PhD and other ECCA programs are a huge help because they show local students there is local employment available.
- Burnsed notes his company has found many good young employees through these programs and can refer unmatched talent to Georgia Pacific, automotive companies, or logistics players connected to the Port of Savannah.
- ECIDA Vice President Jessica Hood reports that more than 18,000 residents leave Effingham County for work every day, creating a retention opportunity for local employers.
- The region is building from a solid base of logistics prowess toward higher salaries and skills in high-tech manufacturing.
- South Effingham High School recently offered space to Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) to train future automotive industry professionals for thousands of positions at its multi-billion-dollar EV Metaplant.
- HMGMA's multi-billion-dollar EV Metaplant is located next to I-16 in nearby Bryan County.
- HMGMA is using the SEHS space to conduct critical welding training in preparation for the plant's opening.
- Dr. Brent Stubbs, HMGMA Head of Learning & Development, expressed gratitude to ECSD and SEHS for providing the training space.
- The collaboration aims to allow Hyundai leaders to engage with students interested in high-tech manufacturing.
- ECSD Superintendent Dr. Yancy Ford stated the district is pleased to collaborate with Hyundai for a lasting partnership that benefits Hyundai, students, and the community.
- Broe Group’s Savannah Gateway Industrial Hub (SGIH) is a 2,600-acre multimodal logistics park in Effingham County.
- SGIH added global automotive parts supplier Kyungshin America Corporation (KAC) to its facilities.
- In February 2023, Sewon America announced a $300 million investment creating 740 jobs at Grande View Industrial Park in Rincon.
- The Sewon America project is the largest economic development project in Rincon's history.
- Sewon America President and CEO Lucas Jeung Gi Lee cited existing infrastructure and proximity to the new Hyundai Metaplant as key reasons for choosing Effingham County.
- Lee also identified the ability to hire high-quality workers within the county as one of the biggest selling points for the investment.
- Sewon America signed an agreement with Savannah Technical College for workforce training.
- The first cohort of students completed their training at Savannah Technical College in January.
- Thirty high-achieving students will be primed for leadership roles at the new Rincon facility by pursuing further training at Sewon’s facility in LaGrange, Georgia.
- Sewon America donated $40,000 to various entities to support Effingham County's infrastructure and educational outcomes.
- Savannah Technical College partnered with the Effingham K-12 school system through a dual enrollment program.
- Savannah Technical College Vice President of Economic Development Tal Loos noted that ECSD is a great partner and they are working to expand programs across their four-county service area.
- Savannah Technical College is starting to offer an Electrical Vehicle Professional TCC certificate to support new industry in the area like Hyundai.
- The investment profile was prepared under the auspices of Effingham County Industrial Development Authority.
