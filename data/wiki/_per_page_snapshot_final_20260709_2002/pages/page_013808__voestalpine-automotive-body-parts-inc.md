# voestalpine Automotive Body Parts Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Parker Poe Assists Austrian Company on Launch of First North American Automotive Parts Facility - Who's On The Move](https://whosonthemove.com/parker-poe-assists-austrian-company-on-launch-of-first-north-american-automotive-parts-facility-158297/)  ·  `page_013808`

## Facts

- Economic development professionals with Parker Poe Adams & Bernstein LLP helped voestalpine Group develop its 17th production site in the United States.
- The voestalpine Automotive Body Parts Inc. facility is located in Cartersville, Georgia.
- The new North American production operation recently held a grand opening.
- The project represents an investment of about $70 million that will create 220 new jobs.
- The plant will manufacture ultra-high strength body-in-white components for premium car manufacturers in the region.
- This opening represents the completion of the first phase of an extensive internationalization program in the automotive sector by the Austria-based steel-based technology and capital goods company.
- Site selection was first announced by Georgia Gov. Nathan Deal in August 2012.
- The facility is part of voestalpine’s Metal Forming Division.
- The site is centrally located to the company’s automaking customers across the South.
- Several long-term contracts are in hand, and the company expects more as demand continues for its high-quality components.
- Site selection was driven by logistical advantages to meet critical delivery schedules for automotive OEM customers.
- Key decision factors included a well-trained workforce and commitment by pro-business state and local partners willing to support short- and long-term strategic growth plans.
- The company found these advantages in Bartow County.
- Cartersville in Bartow County was chosen as a central location amidst customers in the South.
- Out of more than 30 candidates extensively evaluated and negotiated with, Cartersville was selected due to its willingness to work with the company, listen to its needs, and make whatever was needed possible.
- Parker Poe served as a valued partner in the site location evaluation process and assisted with the startup of the new North American facility.
- European automakers are making their mark in the region, having a remarkable economic impact in the communities where they locate.
- The voestalpine Group generated overall revenues of EUR 11.5 billion in the 2012/13 business year.
- Eight percent of this revenue (around EUR 1 billion) was generated by the Group’s 2,500 employees at its 68 sites in North America.
- The largest Group company in the USA is voestalpine Nortrak, which has around 1,000 employees and seven production and sales sites, making it the market and technology leader in North America’s turnout technology segment.
- The Group is strongly represented in the automotive, special steel, energy, and aviation sectors.
- Parker Poe Adams & Bernstein LLP has 190 lawyers in five offices located in major markets across North Carolina and South Carolina.
- Parker Poe Consulting LLC is a South Carolina limited liability company and wholly-owned subsidiary of Parker Poe Adams & Bernstein LLP that provides economic development, site selection, and incentive negotiation consultation services.
