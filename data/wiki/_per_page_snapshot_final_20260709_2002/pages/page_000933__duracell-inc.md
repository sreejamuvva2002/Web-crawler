# Duracell Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** research_and_development  ·  **Published:** 2025-02-21

**Source:** [Duracell's $56 Million Investment in Atlanta: A Boost for Georgia Tech and Innovation | News Center](https://news.gatech.edu/news/2025/02/21/duracells-56-million-investment-atlanta-boost-georgia-tech-and-innovation)  ·  `page_000933`

## Facts

- Duracell Inc., a leading U.S. battery manufacturer, has announced a $56 million investment to establish its new R&D global headquarters at Georgia Tech’s Science Square in Atlanta, Georgia.
- The relocation of Duracell's R&D facility from Bethel, Connecticut, to Atlanta is scheduled to open in Summer 2026.
- The investment will create 110 high-paying jobs with average projected salaries of $120,000.
- Georgia Tech president Ángel Cabrera stated the collaboration bridges academia and industry to foster innovative ideas and groundbreaking advancements.
- The new hub is located at Science Square, a research innovation district adjacent to Georgia Tech.
- Science Square is developed in partnership with commercial real estate firm Trammell Crow Co.
- The Science Square development spans 18 acres and features more than 1.8 million square feet of lab and office space, along with apartments and retail space.
- The first phase of Science Square opened last year, and Duracell's new hub will occupy a substantial portion of the building.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp noted that Georgia has set itself apart as a leader in attracting innovative companies due to its research institutions, world-class logistics network, and pro-business environment.
- Duracell's relationship with Georgia dates back to 1980, when the company began running a manufacturing facility in LaGrange.
- Duracell operates a distribution plant in Fairburn that began operation in 2000.
- Duracell chief technology officer Liben Hailu described the move to Atlanta as a significant milestone for driving innovation in battery technology for years to come.
- Duracell's presence is expected to enhance battery research, development, and manufacturing activities already thriving in Georgia.
