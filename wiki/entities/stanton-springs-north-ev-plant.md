# Stanton Springs North EV Plant

**Type:** Facility  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[vehicle-manufacturing|Vehicle Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** Stanton Springs North, Georgia, Georgia, United States

## Overview

Rivian is constructing a $5 billion electric-vehicle manufacturing campus at Stanton Springs North, east of Atlanta. The 9-million-square-foot facility will span roughly 1,800 acres and is designed to produce up to 400,000 vehicles per year.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Manufacturing facility for electric vehicles with a planned annual capacity of over 400,000 EVs.

## Investment

- **$5 billion** — Construction of manufacturing campus
  - Investment required to capture the full state incentive package.

## Jobs

- **7,500 workers** — by the end of 2028
  - Average salary $56,000 per worker.

## Timeline

- **2026** — Construction mobilization scheduled through 2026.
- **2028** — Vehicle production targeted to begin in 2028.

## Related organizations & people

- [[rivian|Rivian]] — Parent company / Developer / Owner
- [[clayco|Clayco]] — General Contractor (Design-Build)

## Key facts

- The plant will cover roughly 9 million square feet on 1,800 acres of greenfield land.
- Designed for a production capacity that aims to top 400,000 vehicles per year.
- Rivian must employ 7,500 workers at an average salary of $56,000 by the end of 2028 to qualify for the state incentive package.
- One of the largest economic-development projects in Georgia’s history.
- The project reshapes the local labor market, pulling thousands of skilled trades into a corridor between Atlanta and Athens.
- Only a handful of contractors are capable of building at this scale.
- Delivery method is Design-Build; status is Under Construction; funding source is Private.
