## Facts

- The Mercedes-Benz GLE-Class Sport Utility, the full-sized GL-Class Luxury Sport Utility and the Mercedes-Benz C-Class vehicles are all built at the Mercedes-Benz U.S. International production facility near Tuscaloosa, Alabama.
- Trucks (6,000 per year in the early eighties) were once assembled in Hampton, Virginia.
- Mercedes opened its sixth battery factory in 2018.
- The six factories are established across three continents.
- In 2022 will be the year in which Daimler has said that the company will have invested $11 billion to ensure that every Mercedes-Benz has a fully electric or hybrid version available on the market.
- Markus Schäfer stated that electric vehicles will be built in six plants on three continents and address every market segment from the smart fortwo seater to the large SUV.
- Markus Schäfer stated that the battery is the key component of e-mobility and as batteries are the heart of their electric vehicles they put a great emphasis on building them in their own factories.
- The brand has also launched its electric EQ brand with the EQC SUV which was set for production in the year 2019.
- In September 2018, Mercedes unveiled the EQC, its first fully electric car, at an event in Stockholm.
- Mercedes unveiled the VISION AVTR at the 2020 Consumer Electronics Show in Las Vegas, which is said to hold clues on features bound for next-generation Mercedes-Benz cars.
- In May 2017, Mercedes partnered with Vivint Solar to develop a solar-energy home storage battery.
- Mercedes-Benz and Smart are preparing for the widespread uptake of electric vehicles (EVs) by beginning the installation of recharging points across its dealer networks.
- So far 20 Elektrobay recharging units, produced in the UK by Brighton-based Elektromotive, have been installed at seven locations as part of a pilot project, and further expansion of the initiative is planned later in 2010.
