# Blue Bird Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-05-12

**Source:** [Georgia Chron - Blue Bird Workers Vote to Join USW - 10226854](https://georgiachron.com/ganews/blue-bird-workers-vote-to-join-usw-georgiachron-10226854)  ·  `page_052936`

## Facts

- Workers at Blue Bird Corporation's Fort Valley, Ga., facility voted on May 12, 2023, to join the United Steelworkers union (USW).
- Approximately 1,400 workers at the facility are seeking a voice on workplace health and safety, work-life balance, and fair pay.
- United Steelworkers International President Tom Conway welcomed the workers to the USW and congratulated them on the successful organizing effort.
- Conway said, 'We're proud that Blue Bird workers chose to join our union, and we're ready to help them bargain a fair contract that accounts for their contributions to the company's success.'
- USW District 9 Director Dan Flippo noted the vote represents one of the most significant recent organizing efforts by manufacturing workers in the South.
- Flippo said, 'Workers at places like Blue Bird in many ways embody the future. They're the ones making the investments in our infrastructure a reality, the ones building the safer, cleaner communities for generations to come.'
- Blue Bird has been approved to receive significant funding through the EPA's Clean School Bus program.
- Flippo cautioned that federal investments like the EPA program require ensuring workers have a seat at the table.
- Flippo added that corporations have historically viewed the South as a place to suppress wages and working conditions by preventing unionization, but USW members now have the opportunity for positive workplace changes.
- Patrick Watkins, a Blue Bird worker on the volunteer organizing committee, said workers deserve fair pay, safe working conditions, and respect, and voted to take their future into their own hands.
- The USW represents 850,000 workers across metals, mining, pulp and paper, rubber, chemicals, glass auto supply, energy-producing industries, healthcare, public sector, higher education, tech, and service occupations.
- The successful vote at Blue Bird Corporation's Fort Valley facility is cited as an example of how unions empower employees on workplace health, safety, work-life balance, and fair pay.
