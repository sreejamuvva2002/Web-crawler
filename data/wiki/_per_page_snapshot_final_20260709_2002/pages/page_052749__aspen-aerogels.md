# Aspen Aerogels

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** thermal_management  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [ICYMI: Latitude Media: Why Aspen Aerogels canceled its LPO loan — and its Georgia factory | Climate Power](https://climatepower.us/news/icymi-latitude-media-why-aspen-aerogels-canceled-its-lpo-loan-and-its-georgia-factory/)  ·  `page_052749`

## Facts

- Aspen Aerogels conditionally received a $670 million Department of Energy Loan Program Office (LPO) loan in October dedicated specifically to its planned manufacturing facility in Statesboro, Georgia.
- In a February earnings call, Aspen Aerogels told investors it had withdrawn from the negotiation process for the DOE loan.
- The company will instead focus on expanding production at an existing factory in Rhode Island, as well as in China and Mexico.
- Aspen plans to increase annual production capacity by $200 million in China by 2026.
- CFO Ricardo Rodriguez stated that Aspen's manufacturing partnerships in China are "capable of delivering increased aerogel production capacity" to replace the Georgia plant, adding there are "literally no limits" to how much capacity can be increased there.
- The earnings call occurred after the Trump administration's first 10% tariff increase on China but before a second tariff that took effect recently.
- Rodriguez stated that OEM customers are not worried about the strategic shift, saying, "They don't care where it's made."
- To qualify for various Inflation Reduction Act tax credits, EV batteries must meet a threshold of components manufactured in the United States, though the fate of those tax credits remains in flux.
- Aspen officially opened two assembly plants outside Monterrey, Mexico in February: one to create aerogel rolls and a second to complete thermal barrier assembly.
- The Mexican factories are currently owned and managed by a consulting company, but Aspen has notified them of its intent to purchase the "automated fabrication facility."
- Due to the Georgia plant cancellation and a "reduction of various positions" in the last quarter, Aspen aims to reduce fixed costs by over $35 million per year.
- Rodriguez noted that working with external manufacturing, including in China, has allowed Aspen to "pick up a couple of points of margin" in other business areas, calling it a "no-brainer" for the EV thermal barrier business.
- As of the end of 2024, Aspen had paid approximately $323.6 million for engineering, design, and construction costs for the Georgia facility.
- Some of the Georgia factory's assets will be moved to the Rhode Island facility to increase its throughput.
- The company is also evaluating sending some equipment to an external manufacturing facility.
- The canceled Georgia project was expected to come online in 2027 and was originally designed to supply thermal barriers for more than 200 million EV batteries each year.
- The project would have created more than 500 construction jobs and 255 full-time operations jobs in a swing state that clinched Trump's election victory.
- Aspen Aerogels makes thermal barriers for electric vehicle batteries, including for General Motors.
- In late January, the president addressed the World Economic Forum, telling delegates that under his second administration there would be "no better place on Earth to create jobs, build factories, or grow a company" than the United States.
- The Trump administration has paused funding disbursements related to the Inflation Reduction Act, and GOP lawmakers have stated plans to cut the law, though 21 House Republicans recently issued a letter supporting clean energy tax credits within it.
- The Trump administration has discouraged EV adoption, including by halting a $5-billion federal EV charger program.
- Rodriguez told investors that Aspen is concerned about the relatively slow rate of EV adoption in the U.S., noting China is at 50% penetration while North America and Europe remain at 10% to 15%.
- Most of Aspen's thermal barrier customers are global OEMs who are mostly "staying the course" but remaining cautious about capital deployment during Trump's second term.
- Rodriguez remarked that "Four years within a 20-year transition of electrification is not a ton."
- Aspen Aerogels is at least the second loan recipient to walk back domestic manufacturing plans, following Kore Power which scrapped an $850 million LPO-backed battery plant in Buckeye, Arizona.
- Kore Power stated it still plans to make batteries domestically but will do so faster and more cheaply via a retrofit rather than a greenfield plant.
- Aspen Aerogels did not cite political factors among its reasons for the Georgia project cancellation and declined to provide additional comment on the decision.
