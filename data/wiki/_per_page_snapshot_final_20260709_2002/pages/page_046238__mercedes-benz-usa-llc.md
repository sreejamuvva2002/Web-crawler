# Mercedes-Benz USA LLC

## Facts

- Mercedes-Benz USA LLC confirmed the company will relocate its headquarters from Montvale, New Jersey, to Atlanta.
- The relocation is designed to better serve its growing customer base and strengthen the company's position for long-term sustainable growth.
- The company will begin moving employees to a temporary location while constructing a new headquarters expected to be completed in 2017.
- Some operations will remain in Montvale and nearby Robbinsville, New Jersey.
- MBUSA President and CEO Stephen Cannon stated New Jersey has been a wonderful home for their U.S. operations for the first 50 years and still is today.
- Cannon noted that sustained profitable growth and efficiencies required locating the headquarters elsewhere, ultimately choosing Atlanta.
- Georgia reportedly offered Mercedes-Benz USA LLC an incentive package worth an estimated $50 million to lure it to Atlanta from New Jersey.
- The North American division of the luxury German automaker initially refused comment early Tuesday regarding the move.
- WSB-TV in Atlanta confirmed the deal through sources and reported Mercedes would make an announcement sometime Tuesday.
- The deal is expected to bring 1,000 jobs to Georgia.
- The company's move comes at the expense of Bergen County, New Jersey, which has been home to Mercedes-Benz USA headquarters since 1972.
- Atlanta offers benefits including easier access to already used ports in Georgia and direct commercial flights into Alabama where Mercedes-Benz operates a large assembly plant.
- Mercedes-Benz USA officials were scheduled to meet with employees Tuesday regarding the relocation.
- Some employees told the Bergen Record anonymously they expected to hear the announcement during the session.
- State Sen. Gerald Cardinale said New Jersey offered a “substantial” tax incentive package to keep the company in the state, but declined to offer details.
- Cardinale, a Republican, stated his state is having trouble competing to keep and attract businesses because of high property and income taxes.
- Mercedes is being offered an incentive package worth as much as $50 million to relocate to Atlanta, according to the newspaper.
- New Jersey and Georgia state officials declined comment on the deal Tuesday.
- Foreign automakers including Toyota, Mercedes-Benz, Nissan, Honda, Hyundai, and BMW have been attracted to the South by low labor costs, large incentive packages, and a lack of strong union culture over the past 25 years.
- Southern Republicans like Sens. Mitch McConnell of Kentucky, Richard Shelby of Alabama, and Bob Corker of Tennessee opposed the 2009 auto industry bailout and called for lowering wages at U.S. automakers to make them more competitive with nonunion workers at Southern factories run by foreign automakers.
- Five years after the multibillion-dollar bailout of General Motors and Chrysler, auto manufacturing has become a less lucrative career choice.
- Even in union shops, employees doing the same job can be paid differently due to a two-tiered system based on seniority.
- Some auto factory workers make as little as $15 an hour and have had to take second jobs.
- Automakers say the wage system is necessary to create and keep jobs.
- Last month, the National Labor Relations Board upheld a ruling that Mercedes-Benz violated labor laws by stopping United Auto Workers supporters at its Tuscaloosa, Alabama, plant from passing out literature to recruit union membership.
