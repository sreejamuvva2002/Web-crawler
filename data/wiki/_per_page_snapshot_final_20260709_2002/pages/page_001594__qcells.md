# Qcells

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2024-05-22

**Source:** [Qcells Investments Boost Georgia’s Clean Energy Economy - Qcells North America](https://us.qcells.com/blog/qcells-investments-boost-georgias-clean-energy-economy/)  ·  `page_001594`

## Facts

- Qcells is a trusted provider of complete clean energy solutions and a leader in the residential, commercial and utility solar market segments.
- On May 22, 2024, Qcells released data showing its historic investments in solar manufacturing are driving massive economic benefits to Georgia.
- A Chmura JobsEQ Q4 2023 report provided by the Cartersville-Bartow County Department of Economic Development estimates 3,800 direct jobs in Bartow and Whitfield Counties from Qcells’ operations.
- The same report estimates total employment (direct, indirect and induced) in Bartow and Whitfield Counties at 6,755 jobs.
- Total potential sales/output created in Bartow and Whitfield Counties is estimated at $2,091,896,919 (i.e., more than $2 billion).
- The Chmura JobsEQ sales/output estimate is based on the annual direct, indirect and induced sales generated when new employees become active in the local economy.
- ‘Indirect’ refers to the jobs created by regional companies supplying goods and services for Qcells’ expansion and new facility.
- ‘Induced’ refers to the jobs created, and sales generated when new employees increase business at local establishments.
- Qcells announced an ambitious $2.5 billion investment in Georgia manufacturing in January 2023 to expand operations in Dalton and build a fully integrated solar supply chain including Cartersville.
- Qcells completed its Dalton factory expansion in October 2023 creating 510 additional clean energy jobs.
- In April 2024, Qcells kicked off solar panel manufacturing at its facility under construction in Cartersville.
- Once the Cartersville facility is completed at the end of 2024, Qcells will have launched a full silicon-based solar supply chain with ingot, wafer, cell and finished panel production.
- In Georgia alone, Qcells’ full annual solar production capacity is anticipated to reach 8.4 GW annually.
- This capacity equates to nearly 46,000 panels per day, which is enough solar to power nearly 1.3 million homes a year.
- Hanwha Solutions, Qcells’ parent company, invested in REC Silicon to help reopen its Moses Lake, Washington, factory and restart polysilicon production.
- Qcells’ Cartersville facility will take 100% of the polysilicon produced in Moses Lake to produce American-made solar panels.
- Resuming production at Moses Lake will bring around 270 people back to work in solar manufacturing.
- Qcells will receive encapsulant film ethyl vinyl acetate (EVA) from Hanwha Advanced Materials Georgia (HAGA), co-located in Cartersville and the only company manufacturing EVA at scale in the U.S.
- The jobs created from this HAGA investment will result in around 160 people working in solar manufacturing.
