# Rivian

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-05-01

**Source:** [Rivian DOE loan for Georgia plant shrinks but available sooner; reports smaller Q1 loss than expected and reaffirms outlook](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/stocks/article/rivian-doe-loan-for-georgia-plant-shrinks-but-available-sooner-reports-smaller-q1-loss-than-expected-and-reaffirms-outlook-163009055.html?fr=sycsrp_catchall)  ·  `page_016615`

## Facts

- Rivian announced an increase in the initial capacity of its upcoming Georgia assembly plant by 50% to 300,000 units.
- Construction of the Georgia assembly plant is scheduled to start in 2026.
- The Department of Energy (DOE) loan commitment for the Georgia facility was reduced from $6.6 billion to around $4.5 billion.
- Rivian will begin drawing on the DOE loan in 2027, with a production start goal of late 2028.
- The previous $6.6 billion loan commitment had Rivian tapping the funding in 2028, making the new deal allow earlier access to government funds.
- CEO RJ Scaringe stated that accessing the DOE dollars sooner and faster will help secure more capacity and volume sooner.
- Renegotiating the Department of Energy loan was done specifically to facilitate increasing the first phase capacity from 200,000 units to 300,000 units.
- After the first phase is operational, Rivian's total capacity will exceed 500,000 units, which would support positive free cash flow.
- Rivian reported Q1 revenue of $1.38 billion, up 11% year-over-year, against Bloomberg consensus estimates of $1.39 billion.
- Rivian posted a Q1 loss per share of $0.33 versus the expected $0.72.
- Adjusted EBITDA for the quarter was a loss of $472 million, wider than last year, but gross profit reached $119 million for the third consecutive quarter.
- The automotive segment posted a gross profit loss, while the software and services segment posted a $180 million gross profit, nearly a 60% gain from a year ago.
- Rivian maintained its full-year 2026 adjusted EBITDA loss projection in a range of $1.80 billion to $2.10 billion.
- Full-year 2026 capital expenditures are projected between $1.95 billion and $2.05 billion.
- Rivian reaffirmed its 2026 vehicle delivery guidance of 62,000 to 67,000 vehicles.
- Last month, Rivian unlocked an additional $1 billion in investment from joint venture partner Volkswagen.
- The JV with Volkswagen successfully tested the first vehicle from their partnership, the VW ID.EVERY1, which utilizes Rivian software and platform technology.
- In March, Rivian announced a robotaxi deal with Uber involving $1.25 billion in investment for up to 50,000 autonomous R2 EVs.
- Rivian ended Q1 with $4.83 billion in cash, cash equivalents, and short-term investments, and $5.39 billion in total liquidity.
- CEO RJ Scaringe stated that Volkswagen and Uber investments, plus the DOE loan and existing cash, provide $13.6 billion of accessible liquidity to fund ramp-up activities over the next several years.
