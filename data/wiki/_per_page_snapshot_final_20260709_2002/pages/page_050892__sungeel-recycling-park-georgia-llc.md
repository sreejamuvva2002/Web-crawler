# SungEel Recycling Park Georgia, LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2022-09-26

**Source:** [SungEel Leaders answer questions about new facility coming to Stephens County - WNEG](https://wnegradio.com/sungeel-leaders-answer-questions-about-new-facility-coming-to-stephens-county/)  ·  `page_050892`

## Facts

- SungEel Recycling Park Georgia, LLC, a lithium-ion battery recycler and raw materials provider, will locate its first U.S. recycling facility in Stephens County.
- The new facility will be located at the Hayestone Brady Business Park, a Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) Certified site, in Toccoa.
- The recycling process will use an electric-powered dryer instead of an incinerator or natural gas combustion.
- The facility will operate entirely without combustion.
- The process involves receiving batteries, dismantling modules, heating cells to dry them, and shredding the dried material to produce battery powder.
- The produced battery powder will be shipped to Korea.
- All materials generated during the recycling process will be recycled for beneficial reuse, with none sent to a landfill.
- No waste from the facility will enter the city's wastewater system in a way that causes processing issues.
- The recycling process has been in use since 2008, though the facility's equipment will be upgraded.
- Additional details regarding local or state incentives for the project will be made public at a later date.
- SungEel plans to invest $37 million in the new facility.
- The company plans to utilize North Georgia Technical College's services for employee training and hiring.
- SungEel intends to act as a community partner through potential corporate sponsorships and donations.
- The recycling process will be fully compliant with federal and state permitting regulations regarding air pollutants.
- An air permit will be secured before operations begin, and the permit application will be publicly available once submitted.
- SungEel is actively working with Georgia's Environmental Protection Division (EPD) and Department of Natural Resources (DNR) throughout the permitting process.
- The company expects to start production in December 2023.
- Unlike the previous proposal in New York which included a natural gas-fired dryer, the Georgia facility will exclusively use an electric-powered dryer.
- The Georgia facility will recover more electrolyte material compared to the previous New York proposal.
- The recycling process will not release any PFAs (per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances).
- Emissions from the facility will not exceed state or federal air quality regulations.
- The facility will not serve as a battery storage site, but will include separate designated areas for raw materials and short-term finished goods storage.
- SungEel met with local fire personnel, the EMA director, and the county's commercial building inspector to address safety questions.
- The facility will not require special equipment from local fire departments to extinguish fires.
- SungEel will install state-of-the-art fire suppressant systems that satisfy all building codes and insurance requirements.
- Local fire personnel will conduct six-monthly training sessions with employees on fire precaution procedures, a requirement at all SungEel facilities.
- The facility will create 104 jobs.
- The average pay range for these positions will be above the county's current average hourly wage of $22.85.
- SungEel expects to hire locally qualified individuals from the Toccoa-Stephens County area to fill the new positions.
