# Porsche Cars North America, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [New Porsche Experience in Atlanta - Branding & Marketing Strategy](http://tpgbrandstrategy.com/uncategorized/new-porsche-experience-in-atlanta/)  ·  `page_048800`

## Facts

- Porsche Cars North America officially opened its new $100 million Porsche Experience Center (PEC) and headquarters in Atlanta, GA.
- The 27-acre complex is located at the Northeast corner of Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport.
- This facility represents the largest investment ever outside Germany for the sports car manufacturer.
- The industry-first facility includes a driver development track, classic car gallery, restoration center, human performance center, driving simulator lab, and a fine dining restaurant.
- A state-of-the-art business center features 13,000 square feet of conference and event space.
- The experience center is specifically designed as a destination for the public, automotive enthusiasts, and Porsche customers.
- An estimated 30,000 guests are expected to visit the Porsche Experience Center each year.
- Visitors and corporate groups can hone driving skills, reserve meeting space, view classic Porsche automobiles, or dine at Restaurant 356.
- The 1.6-mile Driver Development Track runs through the facility's courtyard and includes six instructor-led driving modules.
- The track features a Handling Circuit, Off-Road Course, Dynamics Area, Low Friction Handling Circuit, Low Friction Circle, and the only Kick Plate in North America to demonstrate Porsche engineering and technological capabilities.
- Georgia Governor Nathan Deal stated that Porsche's investment represents confidence in the future success of the state, economy, and workforce.
- The facility is located at One Porsche Drive, with windows overlooking I-75.
- More than 70 alternative locations across multiple states were considered during site selection.
- Atlanta was chosen due to its transportation infrastructure, skilled workforce, and strong support from the city and state.
- Proximity to the world's busiest airport allows approximately 80 percent of the U.S. population to reach the center within two and a half hours of flying time.
- Atlanta Mayor Kasim Reed cited Atlanta's third-highest concentration of Fortune 500 companies, low cost of doing business, high quality of life, and access to efficient passenger airport and fastest-growing container port as key factors.
- Porsche Cars North America (PCNA) has been based in the Atlanta area since 1998.
- PCNA is now joined by Porsche Financial Services, Porsche Business Services, Porsche Consulting, and Mieschke Hofmann & Partners, with all five entities housed under the same roof.
- Ultimately, 450 employees will be based at the new headquarters, over 100 of whom fill positions that are new to Georgia.
- PCNA employs approximately 300 people who provide parts, service, marketing, and training for 189 dealers.
- PCNA is a wholly-owned subsidiary of Porsche AG, which is headquartered in Stuttgart, Germany.
- Established in 1984, PCNA is the exclusive U.S. importer of Porsche 918 Spyder, 911, Boxster and Cayman sports cars, the Macan and Cayenne SUVs, and Panamera sports sedans.
