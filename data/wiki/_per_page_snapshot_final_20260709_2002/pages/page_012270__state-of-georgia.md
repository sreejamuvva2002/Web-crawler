# State of Georgia

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2023-10-03

**Source:** [Georgia Ranked Number 1 State for Business for 10th Consecutive Year - AllOnGeorgia](https://allongeorgia.com/georgia-state-news/georgia-ranked-number-1-state-for-business-for-10th-consecutive-year/)  ·  `page_012270`

## Facts

- Georgia achieved an unprecedented milestone of being ranked the No. 1 state for business by Area Development magazine for 10 consecutive years.
- The ranking is based on scores from approximately 50 leading site consulting firms across the U.S. in 14 categories.
- Georgia placed in the top 10 for all 14 categories, earning the No. 1 spot in seven classifications and claiming the overall No. 1 ranking.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced the milestone alongside constitutional officers, former Governor Nathan Deal, GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson, and other state and local leaders.
- Governor Kemp stated that Georgia has brought record-breaking jobs and investments to all four corners of the state, especially to rural Georgia.
- Dennis Shea, Publisher and President of Area Development, congratulated Governor Kemp, the Georgia Legislature, Commissioner Pat Wilson, and the GDEcD team for earning the No. 1 ranking for a 10th consecutive year.
- Area Development Staff Editor Steve Kaelble noted that Georgia is uniformly at or near the top of most business location lists due to assembling building blocks that decision-makers look for.
- Georgia earned a No. 1 ranking for the 14th year in a row for its workforce development program, Georgia Quick Start.
- Georgia also earned a No. 1 ranking for competitive labor market.
- The Georgia Quick Start program is offered through the Technical College System of Georgia and delivers customized training targeted at new and expanding businesses.
- The Quick Start program helps attract companies in manufacturing, biotechnology, information technology, and other sectors by providing tech-savvy workers with specific skills.
- Lt. Governor Burt Jones highlighted partnerships between the administration, Georgia General Assembly, and business communities as key to maintaining the top ranking.
- Georgia's competitive advantages include an affordable cost of doing business and lower rates through a versatile energy mix.
- Speaker of the House Jon Burns cited Georgia's logistics advantage, including the nation's fastest growing container port, the world's busiest airport, and a robust road and rail network.
- The state continues to invest strategically in infrastructure to support its economy and maintain its business ranking.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson credited successive governors, local leadership, and the Georgia General Assembly for supporting a partnership approach to economic development.
- The University System and Technical College System of Georgia have been working with economic developers to prepare Georgians for future workforce needs, giving the state a competitive advantage.
