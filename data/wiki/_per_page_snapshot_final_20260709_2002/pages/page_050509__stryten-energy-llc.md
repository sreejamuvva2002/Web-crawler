# Stryten Energy LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-01-19

**Source:** [Stryten Energy Enters The Long-Duration Energy Storage Market With Acquisition of Storion Energy’s Vanadium Redox Flow Battery Technology - CleanTechnica](https://cleantechnica.com/2022/01/26/stryten-energy-enters-the-long-duration-energy-storage-market-with-acquisition-of-storion-energys-vanadium-redox-flow-battery-technology/)  ·  `page_050509`

## Facts

- Stryten Energy LLC, headquartered in Alpharetta, Georgia, announced on January 19, 2022 that it purchased the assets of Storion Energy Inc.
- The acquisition brings advanced vanadium redox flow battery (VRFB) technology to Stryten's Essential Power portfolio for applications requiring more than four hours of storage capacity.
- VRFB technology is ideal for utility-scale storage, microgrids, military bases, telecommunications, uninterruptible power supply (UPS), railway, and renewable markets.
- Storion’s proprietary VRFB design solves the energy-to-power tradeoff by allowing batteries to operate for many hours without sacrificing power or release speed through the addition of more electrolytes.
- Key features of the technology include domestically sourced vanadium that is easily recycled, increased power density, reduced battery downtime, potential limitless cycle life with proper maintenance, high-temperature ratings, thermal safety, and scalability.
- The acquisition adds a flow battery technology research and development facility to complement Stryten’s four North American R&D centers focused on advanced lead and lithium energy storage solutions.
- Stryten leverages its team of 2,500 employees and more than two million square feet of manufacturing space to establish reliable U.S.-engineered and produced long-duration batteries.
- Tim Vargo, Chief Executive Officer of Stryten Energy, stated the acquisition positions the company for success in the rapidly growing long-duration energy storage market while expanding capabilities to solve current and future energy challenges.
- Mike Judd, President and Chief Operating Officer of Stryten Energy, noted the combination of Stryten’s industrial resources with Storion’s technology will support essential power, motive power, transportation, military, and government customers.
- Mohan Misra, Founder, Chairman and CEO of Storion Energy, highlighted that leveraging Stryten’s U.S.-based manufacturing footprint and distribution network will accelerate commercial deployment of VRFB technology.
- The new Essential Power division provides advanced lead and lithium battery technologies, services, chargers, and racking systems for backup power in military, government, telecommunications, UPS, railway, utility, microgrid, and renewable sectors.
- Storion Energy Inc. originated from a decade of fuel cell research and an ARPA-E-funded redox flow battery development program that began in 2010, with the company formally formed in 2015 as a commercial spinoff from ITN Energy Systems.
- The technology aims to stabilize inconsistent power from renewable sources, manage peak load demand and frequency regulation, and support the decarbonization of the electric grid toward President Biden’s goal of net-zero carbon emissions by 2035.
