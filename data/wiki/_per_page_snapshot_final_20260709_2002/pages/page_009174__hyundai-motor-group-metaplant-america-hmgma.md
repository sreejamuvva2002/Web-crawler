# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA)

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-04-04

**Source:** [Hyundai opens the smartest factory in America – pumped full of AI and 5G](https://www.rcrwireless.com/20250404/internet-of-things/hyundai-smarte-factory-ai-5g)  ·  `page_009174`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group opened its new $7.59 billion electric and hybrid vehicle manufacturing plant, Metaplant America, in Georgia.
- The facility is Hyundai’s first state-of-the-art smart factory in the US and is described as North America’s largest and most advanced smart factory.
- HMG Metaplant America (HMGMA) sits on 2,900 acres (11.7 square kilometres) of land in Ellabell, Bryan County.
- Hyundai signed a $12.6 billion investment deal with the State of Georgia in May 2022.
- Construction work at the site began in October 2022.
- The first vehicle, a Hyundai IONIQ 5, rolled off the production line in late 2024.
- HMGMA officially opened in early April 2025.
- The facility currently has a headcount of 1,200 employees.
- Current production capacity is 300,000 vehicles per annum.
- HMGMA will produce both electric and hybrid vehicles for the US market.
- The IONIQ 9 electric SUV is now in production at the facility.
- Production of Kia models is scheduled to begin in 2026.
- Production of Genesis models will follow thereafter.
- Hyundai claims to have invested a total of $20.5 billion in US manufacturing since entering the country, creating 570,000 jobs.
- Hyundai has committed to invest an additional $21 billion following the opening of HMGMA.
- The plant aims to expand capacity by about 60 percent, from 300,000 to 500,000 vehicles per year.
- The facility will create 8,500 full-time jobs at the site by 2031.
- Hyundai targets producing 1.2 million electric and hybrid vehicles annually in the US by 2028 with its new $21 billion investment.
- The facility incorporates cutting-edge automation, including autonomous robots, AI vision systems, and automated processes for all parts of vehicle assembly.
- Automation rates in HMGMA’s assembly shop are nearly double that of Hyundai’s other facilities.
- Private 5G elements in the factory were supplied by Georgia-based Industry 4.0 system integrator Future Technologies.
- Future Technologies used Nokia’s private cellular core and radio (RAN) network systems for the 5G deployment.
- Peter Cappiello, chief executive at Future Technologies, noted the company has been headquartered in Georgia for over 25 years and served as the lead system integrator for the facility’s private 5G solution.
- The factory utilizes autonomous mobile robots (AMRs), automated guided vehicles (AGVs), and unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs/drones).
- AI-based management and control systems oversee sensors, robots, machines, and processes, with human oversight and intervention in specialist functions.
- Discriminative AI is used for camera vision and software analysis, though generative or agentic AI are not mentioned.
- The facility features an AI automation and control system for vehicle parts management.
- An AI drone system is used for inventory management planning and storage.
- A ‘One-Kit’ AI vision system verifies component assemblies.
- A Digital Command Centre (DCC) digitizes and integrates production data.
- AI vision systems precisely and automatically align metal sheets.
- A smart press controls power and speed for optimal metal forming.
- An AI vision system detects holes or cracks in panels, marking a world first.
- AI vision guides the precise positioning of the automatic loading system.
- Unmanned transport vehicles carry pallets.
- Automated warehouses efficiently store and manage pallets.
- A ‘Robot Main Buck’ system integrates robots and vision technology.
- An autonomous adjustment system adjusts vehicle door gaps, marking a world first.
- A ‘Metal finish line flow-type AGV’ serves as a collaborative workspace, marking a world first.
- An automated overhead robot grabs and moves heavy body-in-white parts.
- An automated electrocoating scarf sanding system sands vehicle part defects.
- An automated sealer system fills vehicle gaps for waterproofing and soundproofing.
- An automated final inspection system inspects paint jobs and performs markings.
- An automated ‘trim-IN’ robot lifts and lowers vehicles.
- An automated body/chassis marriage system fits the vehicle body and chassis.
- An automated door installation system assembles doors, marking a world first.
- An automated headlamp adjustment system optimizes lamp brightness.
- Hyundai’s $12.6 billion Georgia investment also covers a joint battery venture at HMGMA with LG Energy Solution and SK On.
- The company states the $12.6 billion deal is the largest investment in Georgia’s history.
- Hyundai’s other US sites in Alabama (Hyundai) and Georgia (Kia Autoland) will be made smarter.
- A new $5.8 billion electric arc furnace steel mill in Louisiana will supply automotive steel plates to HMGMA when commercial production begins in 2029.
- Euisun Chung, executive chair at Hyundai, stated that HMGMA represents the group’s advanced manufacturing capabilities and commitment to innovation, and highlighted building the future of mobility with America in America.
