# Hapag-Lloyd

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2021-12

**Source:** [Multinational Liner Shipping Company Hapag-Lloyd Selects Georgia for North American Headquarters - Savannah CEO](https://savannahceo.com/news/2021/12/multinational-liner-shipping-company-hapag-lloyd-selects-georgia-north-american-headquarters/)  ·  `page_049938`

## Facts

- The Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) announced that German multinational liner shipping company Hapag-Lloyd will invest $18 million in its new North American headquarters.
- The new North American headquarters will be located in Dunwoody, Georgia.
- The investment will create an additional 250 jobs in the metro Atlanta region.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Hapag-Lloyd has been moving people and cargo around the globe for over 150 years and is one of the most recognizable names in the shipping industry.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson noted that logistics is an innate part of Georgia’s economic prowess, with the state's economy connected to the movement of goods and people via sea, rail, air, and road.
- Hapag-Lloyd is based in Hamburg, Germany.
- Hapag-Lloyd operates a fleet of over 250 modern container ships with a total transport capacity of 1.8 million TEUs.
- Hapag-Lloyd currently employs more than 500 people across the state of Georgia.
- Once the North American headquarters project is complete, Hapag-Lloyd will employ more than 750 people in Georgia.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson met with company leadership in Hamburg during a November economic development mission to Germany.
- Uffe Ostergaard, President of Hapag-Lloyd’s North American region, stated that the new headquarters will bring more team members together under one roof to better serve customers and expand career pathways for staff.
- The company will open its new 125,000-square-foot headquarters at Three Ravinia in Dunwoody by summer 2022.
- Hapag-Lloyd will be hiring for careers in finance, human resources, IT services, and more.
- Dunwoody Mayor Lynn Deutsch stated that the city is pleased to welcome Hapag-Lloyd and its team members to Dunwoody.
- Decide DeKalb President Dorian DeBarr stated that Hapag-Lloyd’s announcement reinforces DeKalb County’s assets, including a skilled workforce, accessibility to major highways and transportation hubs, and a business-friendly community.
- GDEcD Director of Corporate Solutions and Cybersecurity in Global Commerce Kristi Brigman represented the department on this project.
- The project was executed in partnership with GDEcD’s Europe office, Decide DeKalb, the Dunwoody Development Authority, and Georgia Power.
