# Woory Industrial Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-06-08

**Source:** [Woory to Open EV Parts Manufacturing, R&D Facility in Laurens County - Location Georgia](https://www.locationgeorgia.com/woory-to-open-ev-parts-manufacturing-rd-facility-in-laurens-county-june-2023/)  ·  `page_015378`

## Facts

- The facility will create more than 130 new jobs in Dublin-Laurens County.
- The facility represents an estimated $18 million in investment in Dublin-Laurens County.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Woory Industrial Co., Ltd. will establish a new manufacturing facility in Dublin, Georgia.
- Woory Industrial was established in 1989 and specializes in heating, ventilation, and air conditioning (HVAC) components and systems for the global automotive industry.
- Woory concentrates on developing new products for electric vehicles (EVs) and hydrogen-fueled cars.
- The new facility will be Woory's first U.S. manufacturing location.
- The facility is located at 404 Kellam Road in Dublin.
- The facility will produce electrical heaters, control units, and actuators for EV and internal combustion carmakers in North America.
- Operations are expected to begin in November 2023.
- Woory has been nominated as a supplier for Tesla, Rivian, Lucid, and Canoo.
- Woory is currently supplying the HV heater system used to heat the cabins of EVs.
- Woory has grown into an international enterprise with production plants around the world employing over 1,300 people.
- Dublin Mayor Joshua Kight praised Woory's commitment to placing an existing manufacturing building back into service to promote infill development and create new career paths.
- Dr. Fred Williams, Chair of the Board of Directors of the Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority, noted Dublin and Laurens County's rapidly growing automotive sector due to its location on Interstate 16 and access to a skilled workforce.
- The partnership with area education systems and training providers will ensure training programs are in place to fill Woory’s talent pipeline for years to come.
- Project Manager Jacob Lee represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The project was developed in partnership with the Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson highlighted that projects like Woory create jobs and opportunities in rural communities.
- From July 1 through December 31, 2022, the automotive industry was the second-largest source of new jobs and investment in Georgia.
- Since 2020, Georgia has pursued job creation opportunities along the entire EV supply chain, resulting in over $22.7 billion in investment and the creation of more than 28,400 jobs.
