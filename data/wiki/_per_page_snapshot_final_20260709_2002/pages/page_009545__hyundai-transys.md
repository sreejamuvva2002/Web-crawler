# Hyundai TRANSYS

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2020-12-02

**Source:** [Hyundai to expand operations, add jobs near Columbus, GA | Columbus Ledger-Enquirer](https://www.ledger-enquirer.com/news/business/article247541180.html)  ·  `page_009545`

## Facts

- A Hyundai affiliate is investing more than $240 million and bringing hundreds of jobs to the West Point area as part of a new manufacturing facility.
- Hyundai TRANSYS is an automobile manufacturing company that specializes in transmissions.
- The new factory is located in West Point, near an existing Hyundai TRANSYS factory and a Kia Motors Corp. factory.
- Both facilities are part of Hyundai Motor Group, the Korean auto conglomerate that sells cars under the Kia and Hyundai brands.
- The facility will create 678 full-time long-term positions, according to a release from Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp’s office.
- It is the largest industrial announcement by value of the investment made so far this year in Georgia, according to the Associated Press.
- Sangkil Jung, president of Hyundai TRANSYS Georgia Powertrain, stated that trust built through years of capital investment, employment and training support – along with new opportunities unmatched by any other states – made Georgia a natural choice.
- Sangkil Jung added that the company is excited for the opportunities ahead as it expands its presence in the West Point community.
- The company plans to create 165 additional construction handling and inspection jobs.
- Following the expansion, Hyundai TRANSYS will employ a total of 1,741 people in Georgia.
- The new plant will build eight-speed transmissions.
- The facility will be a 620,000-square foot plant.
- The plant will be part of the Kia campus just off Interstate 85 in West Point near the state line with Alabama.
- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp stated that the state's No. 1 business climate, alongside its robust logistics network and top-ranked workforce training programs, are attracting global companies to the Peach State while retaining existing industries and helping them grow.
