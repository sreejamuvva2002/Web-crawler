# SK Battery America Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [US Department of Labor finds electric vehicle battery manufacturer exposed employees to unsafe nickel levels, other mixed metals at Commerce plant | U.S. Department of Labor](https://www.dol.gov/newsroom/releases/osha/osha20240117-0)  ·  `page_000173`

## Facts

- SK Battery America Inc. exposed employees at its Commerce plant to serious and potentially disabling safety and health hazards, according to a U.S. Department of Labor workplace safety inspection.
- OSHA cited SK Battery America Inc. with six serious violations and one other-than-serious violation.
- The company subjected employees to continuous noise levels exceeding the 8-hour time-weighted average of 85 A-weighted decibels by not developing a monitoring program, nor establishing an audiometric testing program, including a valid baseline audiogram.
- SK Battery America Inc. exposed employees working with cobalt, nickel and manganese to respiratory hazards by failing to complete a workplace hazard assessment.
- The company failed to ensure employees were given clean, disinfected and sanitary respirators for work involving cobalt, nickel and manganese.
- SK Battery America Inc. failed to store respirators properly to protect them from material contamination.
- The company failed to make certain that people working with corrosive materials had access to eyewash stations and emergency showers.
- SK Battery America Inc. left workers handling bags of nickel powder vulnerable to respiratory hazards by not providing feasible administrative or engineering controls to reduce exposure levels.
- OSHA Area Office Director Joshua Turner stated that the lithium battery industry has experienced unprecedented growth, bringing heightened responsibility for safety at the forefront of innovation, and emphasized that materials used to produce lithium batteries can cause debilitating and permanent health issues.
- Turner noted that OSHA’s continued efforts to identify emerging hazards are key in helping the industry eliminate employee exposures, warning that employers must prioritize worker safety alongside technological advancement.
- OSHA has proposed $75,449 in penalties against SK Battery America Inc., an amount set by federal statute.
- SK Battery America Inc. employs about 3,100 people at its two battery manufacturing plants in Commerce.
- SK Battery America Inc. is a subsidiary of SK On, a global electric vehicle battery manufacturer.
- SK On is part of the Seoul-based SK Group, South Korea’s second-largest conglomerate, which includes companies in energy, advanced materials, biopharmaceuticals and digital business.
- SK Battery America Inc. has contested the OSHA findings before the independent Occupational Safety and Health Review Commission.
