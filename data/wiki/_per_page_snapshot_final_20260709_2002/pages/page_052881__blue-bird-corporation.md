# Blue Bird Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-05-16

**Source:** [Blue Bird workers in Fort Valley unionize](https://thegeorgiasun.com/news/blue-bird-workers-in-fort-valley-unionize/)  ·  `page_052881`

## Facts

- About 1,400 workers at Blue Bird Corp.’s Fort Valley school bus manufacturing plant voted to join the United Steelworkers Union (USW).
- The unionization vote contradicts the South’s non-union history.
- United Steelworkers International President Tom Conway stated pride in the vote and readiness to help bargain a fair contract that accounts for workers' contributions to the company's success.
- The Blue Bird union organizing effort is one of the most significant in the South, a region marked by state right-to-work laws that neither require workers to join a union nor pay union dues as a condition of employment.
- President Joe Biden issued a statement congratulating the Blue Bird workers for voting to unionize.
- Biden stated that unions built the middle class and that union workers will be a big part of America's future.
- Blue Bird is an active participant in America’s move toward clean energy, manufacturing both low-emission and zero-emission school buses.
- Blue Bird has been approved to receive significant funding through the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency’s Clean School Bus program.
- USW District 9 Director Dan Flippo stated that the vote gives workers a seat at the bargaining table.
- Flippo noted that corporations historically viewed the South as a place to suppress wages and working conditions by preventing unionization, but the USW has a history of fighting for Southern workers.
- Political and business leaders in Georgia and other Southern states, along with overseas corporate executives, have long cited low union membership in the region as a key factor in corporate decisions to locate job-creating operations there.
