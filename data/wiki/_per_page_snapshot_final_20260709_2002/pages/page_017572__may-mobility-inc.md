# May Mobility, Inc.

## Facts

- Lyft, Inc. and May Mobility, Inc. announced the launch of their autonomous vehicle (AV) pilot program in Atlanta on September 10, 2025.
- This marks the first public autonomous vehicle deployment for the Lyft and May Mobility partnership.
- The service initially launches as a pilot program available to Lyft riders in the area, with plans to expand service hours and vehicle availability in the months ahead.
- Riders can match with May Mobility’s autonomous vehicles through the Lyft app when requesting a standard or Wait & Save ride within the service area.
- The autonomous vehicles will operate around the Midtown Atlanta area, including Virginia Highlands, Old Fourth Ward, and Inman Park.
- The fleet consists of hybrid-electric Toyota Sienna Autono-MaaS vehicles equipped with May Mobility’s patented Multi-Policy Decision Making (MPDM) technology.
- Jeremy Bird, Executive Vice President at Lyft, stated the partnership brings commercial autonomous vehicle rides to Midtown Atlanta and surrounding areas, strengthening the region's position in transportation innovation.
- Initial deployments include a standby operator present in each vehicle to assist the AV and help acclimate riders to autonomous driving technology.
- Standby operators are fully trained to take the wheel and drive manually during initial trips as needed, and will intervene less over time as service is optimized.
- Standby operators will also answer questions and monitor vehicle operations to prioritize a safe and comfortable ride before eventually transitioning the service to driverless operations.
- Edwin Olson, CEO and founder of May Mobility, stated that the launch marks a pivotal moment in the AV industry as they work to bring driverless operations to Atlanta as part of their goal to deploy more AVs onto the platform over the coming years.
- This launch in Atlanta is May Mobility’s second active operation in Georgia.
- The company operates a driverless service in Peachtree Corners, with public, on-demand rides along Technology Parkway.
- The Peachtree Corners microtransit service is in partnership with Curiosity Lads and T-Mobile.
- At the core of the partnership is May Mobility's patented Multi-Policy Decision Making (MPDM) platform, which provides active monitoring, vehicle guidance, and predictive capabilities.
- The MPDM platform applies human reasoning to every situation with AI-powered speed and precision, helping each vehicle navigate complex urban environments found in Atlanta.
- MPDM achieves high safety performance by analyzing thousands of potential scenarios every second and then choosing the optimal maneuver in any situation.
- The technology has proven itself in deployments across the U.S. and Japan, including driverless operations in three cities.
- This technology foundation is combined with May Mobility's millions of commercial autonomous miles.
- More than $90 billion is expected to be invested in transportation improvement and mobility projects across the region by 2040.
- Atlanta's selection as the inaugural market for this partnership reflects the city's and state’s growing reputation as a center for transportation technology advancement.
- Rep. Matt Reeves (R-Duluth), Chairman of the House Insurance Rate Study Committee, stated that Georgia represents an ideal market for autonomous vehicle technology and demonstrated how innovative companies can work together to enhance transportation options while maintaining community trust and safety.
- The collaboration creates opportunities for local workforce development and positions Atlanta as an attractive destination for technology companies seeking to deploy innovative transportation solutions.
- Both companies plan to expand the partnership based on community feedback and operational performance, with potential for additional markets and enhanced service offerings.
- May Mobility appointed Balajee Kannan as Senior Vice President of Autonomy Core Engineering.
- Lyft was founded in 2012 and has grown into a global mobility platform offering rideshare, taxis, private hire vehicles, car sharing, bikes, and scooters across 4 continents and nearly 1,000 cities.
- May Mobility leverages its patented MPDM system to build an industry-leading in-situ AI solution that integrates real-time data every 200 milliseconds.
- May Mobility delivers safer, more efficient transportation across diverse environments, including rural cities, dense urban areas and diverse weather conditions.
- May Mobility is backed by strategic partnerships with industry leaders, including Toyota Motor Corporation and NTT.
- The company has completed nearly half a million rides across 19 deployments worldwide.
