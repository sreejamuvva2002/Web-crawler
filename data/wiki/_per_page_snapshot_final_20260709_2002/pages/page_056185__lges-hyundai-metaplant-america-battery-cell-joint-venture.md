# LGES-Hyundai Metaplant America Battery Cell Joint Venture

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-09-01

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution to Invest Additional $2B in Bryan County - AllOnGeorgia](https://allongeorgia.com/georgia-business/hyundai-motor-group-and-lg-energy-solution-to-invest-additional-2b-in-bryan-county/)  ·  `page_056185`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution (LGES) will invest an additional $2 billion in their battery cell manufacturing joint venture at the Metaplant in Bryan County.
- This expansion raises the joint venture's total investment value to more than $4.3 billion.
- The expansion will create another 400 new jobs.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that in a single year, they broke ground on the largest project in state history.
- Governor Brian Kemp noted that they landed multiple suppliers across the state for Hyundai’s Metaplant.
- Governor Brian Kemp said they welcomed LGES to Bryan County.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated Georgia is being positioned as the e-mobility capital of the nation through these major investments.
- Governor Brian Kemp noted that major investments ultimately go to hardworking Georgians in the form of paychecks, improved schools and infrastructure.
- José Muñoz, president and global COO of Hyundai Motor Company and president and CEO of Hyundai and Genesis Motor North America, stated the incremental investment reflects a continued commitment to create a more sustainable future powered by American workers.
- In May 2023, Hyundai Motor Group and LGES signed a memorandum of understanding establishing LGES as the partner for Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America’s (HMGMA) onsite battery cell manufacturing joint venture.
- With this announcement, the EV manufacturing facility and the battery joint venture represent an estimated $7.59 billion in investment.
- The combined facilities will create 8,500 new jobs for the region over the next eight years.
- Dong-Myung Kim, president and head of the Advanced Automotive Battery Division of LG Energy Solution, stated the investment underscores dedication to driving America’s EV transition while bolstering the local economy through quality jobs.
- The facility will have a capacity of 30 GWh.
- At full operations, the facility will be able to support the production of 300,000 units of EVs annually.
- Hyundai Mobis will assemble battery packs using cells from the plant.
- Hyundai Mobis will supply the assembled battery packs to Hyundai Motor Group’s U.S. manufacturing facilities for production of Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis EV models.
- Carter Infinger, Chairman of the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority (JDA), stated the announcement will bring 400 additional well-paying jobs for those in the Savannah region.
- Alyce Thornhill, Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy, represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The GDEcD partnered with the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority (JDA), Georgia Power, Georgia Ports Authority, and Georgia Quick Start on the project.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated Governor Kemp set a goal of Georgia becoming the top location for EV manufacturing in the United States.
- Since 2018, Georgia has pursued job creation along the entire EV supply chain, resulting in more than $25 billion in investments and the creation of over 30,000 jobs.
- Battery-related projects have accounted for at least $11 billion of that investment.
- In fiscal year 2023, job creation in the automotive industry increased by 324 percent when compared to FY21.
