# Vehicle Technologies Office Annual Merit Review Plenary and Awards

**Type:** event  ·  **Role:** research_and_development  ·  **Published:** 2022-06-21

**Source:** [2022 VTO AMR Plenary and Awards | Department of Energy](https://stage.energy.gov/cmei/vehicles/articles/2022-vto-amr-plenary-and-awards)  ·  `page_002742`

## Facts

- The 2022 VTO Annual Merit Review plenary session was held on Tuesday, June 21, 2022.
- Each year, VTO presents awards to individuals from partner institutions for contributions to overall program efforts and to recognize research, development, demonstration, and deployment achievements in specific areas.
- Amgad Elgowainy and Jarod Kelly from Argonne National Laboratory received a Distinguished Achievement Award for leading a Cradle-to-Grave (“C2G”) analysis in support of the U.S. DRIVE partnership between DOE and industry partners.
- Jiguang (Jason) Zhang from Pacific Northwest National Laboratory received a Distinguished Achievement Award for outstanding technical contributions to VTO’s battery R&D activities.
- Yan (Joann) Zhou and Jim Kuiper from Argonne National Laboratory received a Distinguished Achievement Award in recognition of distinguished achievements supporting electric vehicle charging.
- Matthew Kramer from Ames Laboratory received a Distinguished Achievement Award for years of outstanding skill and leadership in magnetic materials research, planning, and collaboration for electric drive technologies.
- Cummins, Inc. received a Team Award for demonstrating 55% brake thermal efficiency in a heavy-duty diesel engine, which will contribute to the SuperTruck 2 goal of more than doubling freight efficiency compared to trucks produced a decade earlier.
- Navistar, Inc. received a Team Award for demonstrating 55% brake thermal efficiency in a heavy-duty diesel engine in a test cell, which will contribute to the SuperTruck 2 goal of more than doubling freight efficiency compared to trucks produced a decade earlier.
- Clemson University, Honda Development & Manufacturing of America, LLC, University of Delaware, Lanxess, and Proper Group International received a Team Award in recognition of Dr. Srikanth Pilla’s extraordinary expertise in leading carbon fiber thermoplastic composites door R&D to achieve weight savings, performance, and sustainability.
- Brett Aristegui, Trev Hall, Darren Stevenson, David Kirschner, Neil Kirschner, Dan Nardozzi, and Erin Russell-Story from the National Energy Technology Laboratory received a Team Award in recognition of loyal dedication to VTO and commitment to the national network of Clean Cities coalitions as the Clean Cities Regional Managers.
- Dennis Dees from Argonne National Laboratory received a Lifetime Distinguished Achievement Award for decades of world-class research in continuum modeling of high-energy, high power, and long-lasting batteries to support and shape the DOE advanced battery portfolio.
- David Anderson from the U.S. Department of Energy received a Lifetime Distinguished Achievement Award for valuable contributions to VTO by serving as inaugural Program Manager for Energy Efficient Mobility Systems and creating a meaningful and enduring program.
- Ira Bloom from Argonne National Laboratory received a Lifetime Distinguished Achievement Award for two decades of tireless support of multiple DOE programs on advanced batteries in areas of accelerated testing, life estimation, and in elucidating the physical and chemical changes that limit battery life.
- Steve Zimmer from the United States Council for Automotive Research (USCAR) received a Lifetime Distinguished Achievement Award for spirited leadership as executive director of USCAR and contributions to U.S. DRIVE and its predecessor partnerships.
