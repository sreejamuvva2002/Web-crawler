# Dongwon Tech Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2020-11-18

**Source:** [Dongwon Tech to invest $700K in Gainesville plant](https://valdostatoday.com/business-2/2020/11/dongwon-tech-to-invest-700k-in-gainesville-plant/)  ·  `page_016315`

## Facts

- Dongwon Tech Corporation announced on November 18, 2020, that it will invest $700,000 to open an advanced manufacturing facility and sales office in Hall County.
- The project is expected to deliver 40 jobs to the Gainesville area.
- The new facility will be located at 2175 Centennial Drive in Gainesville and will span 20,000 square feet.
- Job positions at the Hall County facility will include roles in production, management, sales, and administration.
- Dongwon Tech Corporation is a U.S. subsidiary of Yuchang Air Con Co., Ltd., which is headquartered in Anyang, Gyeonggi-do, Korea.
- The company produces heating, ventilation and air conditioning (HVAC) ducts, CR/DR systems, and HVAC items that help save energy and meet customer-specific needs.
- The Gainesville facility will primarily produce rectangular air ducts for SK Innovation and another local HVAC company located less than an hour away in Commerce, Georgia.
- Dongwon Tech is planning to invest in the production of spiral ducts to capture a broader market in the coming years.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced the project, citing Georgia as the No. 1 State for Business eight years in a row.
- CheolWoong Heo, CEO of Dongwon Tech Corporation, stated that Georgia's large Korean-American population and existing Korean conglomerates made it easier for the company to enter during early investment stages.
- Tim Evans, Vice President of the Greater Hall Chamber of Commerce, noted that Dongwon Tech chose Gainesville-Hall County for its new North American headquarters and manufacturing center.
- Yoonie Kim, Director of Korean Investment at the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s Global Commerce division, represented GDEcD on this project in partnership with the Greater Hall Chamber of Commerce and Georgia EMC.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Dongwon Tech's investment moves Georgia another step closer to building the battery supply chain necessary to support the electrification of the automotive industry.
- Wilson added that Dongwon Tech will supply SK Innovation in Commerce, Georgia, as well as HVAC customers throughout the Southeastern U.S., highlighting the state's competitive logistics network and business climate.
- The company's main products are all kinds of ducts for HVAC, and it also supplies and installs various HVAC equipment with its Energy Saving Technology.
