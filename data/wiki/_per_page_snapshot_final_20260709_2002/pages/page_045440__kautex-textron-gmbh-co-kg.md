# Kautex Textron GmbH & Co. KG

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Kautex Textron secures order for EV battery housing from major automaker By Investing.com](https://in.investing.com/news/company-news/kautex-textron-secures-order-for-ev-battery-housing-from-major-automaker-93CH-4930162)  ·  `page_045440`

## Facts

- Kautex Textron GmbH & Co. KG, a subsidiary of Textron Inc., received an order from a leading automotive manufacturer for a thermoplastic composite lower battery housing unit for fully electric vehicles.
- The housing is part of Kautex’s Pentatonic battery enclosure portfolio and is designed to seal and protect vehicle batteries while meeting structural and thermal requirements, including crush and impact testing as well as fire tests.
- The component will be produced for a major battery electric vehicle platform that will span multiple vehicle brands.
- Kautex’s strong financial position, with liquid assets exceeding short-term obligations and a healthy current ratio of 1.84, supports its ability to execute on such significant contracts.
- The thermoplastic composite material offers advantages over conventional metal counterparts, including lower costs through component reduction and a smaller carbon footprint.
- The lightweight design contributes to reducing carbon emissions during vehicle operation.
- Joerg Rautenstrauch, President & CEO of Kautex, stated that the innovative BEV lower housing is a testament to their dedication to providing cutting-edge solutions that meet the highest standards of safety, performance, and sustainability.
- This marks Kautex’s second Pentatonic business win following a January 2023 order for a full-BEV underbody protection skid plate that entered production in 2024.
- The company has validated its Pentatonic enclosures through road testing across three test vehicles in the US, Japan, and Europe, accumulating over 100,000 miles collectively.
- Kautex Textron operates 30 plants in 13 countries and manufactures traditional and hybrid fuel systems, engine camshafts, and plastic industrial packaging solutions.
- The company has been expanding its portfolio to include battery systems for electric vehicles.
- Parent company Textron reports annual revenue of $14.1 billion and maintained profitable operations with a net income of $816 million in the last twelve months.
- Textron reported strong financial results for the second quarter of 2025, exceeding analysts’ expectations.
- Textron posted earnings per share of $1.55, surpassing the forecasted $1.45.
- Textron achieved revenues of $3.7 billion in the second quarter of 2025, which was above the projected $3.64 billion.
- UBS upgraded Textron’s stock rating from Sell to Neutral, citing healthy demand for business jets and the company’s decision to divest its challenged Powersports business.
- UBS raised its price target for Textron to $88.00.
- Bernstein SocGen Group maintained its Market Perform rating with a price target of $90.00 following the earnings report.
