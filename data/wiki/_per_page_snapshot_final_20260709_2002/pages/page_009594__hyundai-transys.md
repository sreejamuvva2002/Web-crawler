# Hyundai Transys

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2020-12-02

**Source:** [Hyundai affiliate plans $240M transmission plant in Georgia, ETAuto](https://auto.economictimes.indiatimes.com/news/auto-components/hyundai-affiliate-plans-240m-transmission-plant-in-georgia/79528413)  ·  `page_009594`

## Facts

- Hyundai Transys announced plans for a new transmission factory in West Point, Georgia.
- The company will invest more than $240 million to build the plant.
- The new plant will have a capacity of 620,000 square feet (58,000 square meters).
- The plant will be located on the Kia campus just off Interstate 85 in West Point, near the Alabama state line.
- Hyundai Transys plans to hire 678 workers for the new facility.
- The new plant will build eight-speed transmissions.
- The new factory will adjoin an existing Hyundai Transys factory and a Kia Motors Corp factory.
- Both plants are part of Hyundai Motor Group, a Korean auto conglomerate that sells cars under the Kia and Hyundai brands.
- Hyundai Transys was created by a merger of two Hyundai affiliates last year.
- Hyundai Transys also manufactures axles and vehicle seats.
- The company has operations in South Korea, the United States, and seven other countries.
- The investment will create 165 additional jobs for Hyundai Transys.
- Once the new plant is complete, Hyundai Transys's total employees in Georgia will rise above 1,700.
- West Point Mayor Steve Tramell expressed thrill over Hyundai Transys's long-term investment and the new jobs being created.
- Many supplier plants are clustered near the Hyundai plant in Montgomery, Alabama, and the Kia plant in West Point.
- Kia, its affiliates, and suppliers had more than 4,300 employees in Georgia's Troup County in 2018, according to county financial documents.
- This is the largest industrial announcement by investment value made so far this year in Georgia.
- Hyundai TRANSYS Georgia Powertrain President Sangkil Jung stated that years of capital investment, employment, training support, and unmatched opportunities made Georgia a natural choice for the company.
- Final incentives for Hyundai Transys have not been agreed on yet, according to Marie Hodge Gordon, spokesperson for the Georgia Economic Development Department.
- Hyundai Transys will qualify for a state income tax credit allowing it to deduct $3,500 per job from its Georgia income taxes for five years.
- If Hyundai Transys does not incur $2.37 million in state income taxes each year, it can use the credit to receive cash payments redirected from money withheld from employee paychecks for state income taxes.
- This tax credit applies because the plant is located in an area classified by the state as less-developed.
- Workers must earn at least $28,000 a year for Hyundai Transys to qualify for the tax credits.
- The state will pay to train Hyundai Transys workers.
- The state could contribute money to pay for infrastructure.
- Hyundai will automatically qualify for tax breaks saving it millions in sales and use taxes on machinery.
- Local officials could also abate property taxes.
- West Point's financial statements show that in 2018, Kia, its affiliates, and suppliers were receiving $2.85 million in property tax breaks from the city alone.
- Kia received subsidies and tax breaks worth more than $450 million to locate its plant in West Point, which opened in 2009.
- The state aimed to revitalize a region hit hard by the closure of textile and apparel plants.
- Because the plant is only miles from the Alabama state line, a significant number of its workers live in Alabama.
