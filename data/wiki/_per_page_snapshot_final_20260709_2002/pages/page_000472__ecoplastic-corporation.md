# Ecoplastic Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-01-12

**Source:** [Third Hyundai supplier sets up factory near upcoming EV plant | Manufacturing Dive](https://www.manufacturingdive.com/news/third-hyundai-supplier-sets-up-factory-near-incoming-ev-plant/640074/)  ·  `page_000472`

## Facts

- Ecoplastic Corporation will spend approximately $205 million on a new manufacturing facility in southeast Georgia to supply components to Hyundai Motor Group’s electric vehicle campus.
- Gov. Brian Kemp announced the investment on Thursday.
- Ecoplastic is a Tier-1 supplier for Hyundai and Kia.
- The South Korea-based manufacturer produces automotive interior and exterior plastic parts.
- The company selected Bulloch County, Georgia, to house its facility.
- The facility will be located neighboring Hyundai’s incoming $5.5 billion EV campus.
- The new plant will have injection molding and printing capabilities.
- The plant will produce parts including bumpers, garnishes, consoles, and trims for EVs.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Ecoplastic has decades of experience as a supplier for Kia and Hyundai.
- Wilson noted that officials are building the supplier network for the Hyundai metaplant in Bryan County and are excited to see suppliers spread out across the region.
- Hyundai’s facility broke ground in October.
- Hyundai’s facility has already attracted more than $1 billion in investments from offsite suppliers flocking to Georgia.
- In November, Joon Georgia became the first confirmed supplier for the Hyundai facility.
- Joon Georgia plans to invest $317 million in a production facility in Bulloch County.
- In late November, Gov. Kemp announced Hyundai Mobis as the second confirmed supplier for the Hyundai facility.
- Rivian announced in 2021 that it would spend $5 billion to construct its largest EV production facility in East Augusta, Georgia.
- Suppliers including Solvay Specialty Polymers and Denkai America have announced plans to set up shop near Rivian’s East Augusta site to supply key battery parts.
