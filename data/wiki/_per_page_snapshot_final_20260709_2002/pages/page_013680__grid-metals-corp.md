# Grid Metals Corp.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2023-07-18

**Source:** [Grid Metals Announces Maiden Mineral Resource at Donner Lake Lithium Property; Lease Agreement Signed for True North Mill Provides Additional Flexibility for Future Lithium Production |

## Facts

- Grid Metals announced a maiden National Instrument 43-101 compliant Mineral Resource Estimate on its 75% owned Donner Lake Lithium Project in Manitoba, Canada.
- The resource consists of 6.81 million tonnes (Inferred) grading 1.39% Li2O.
- Grid Metals signed a binding lease agreement with 1911 Gold Corporation to use its nearby True North mill for the production of lithium spodumene concentrate.
- The True North mill is an existing processing facility that will be reconfigured to enable lithium spodumene production.
- The True North mill is a permitted gold mill utilizing a flotation circuit with a nominal annual capacity of 475,000 tonnes.
- The mill lease term is up to five years plus a two-year cancellation period.
- The True North tailings facility is also permitted, though an additional permit is required to reconfigure the mill and tailings for lithium processing.
- Grid Metals signed a contract with Primero Group to complete a scoping-level mill reconfiguration study within 90 days.
- Grid Metals has ongoing discussions with Tantalum Mining Company of Canada Limited for toll milling available resources at the currently operating Tanco Mine, located 35 km south of Donner Lake.
- The Donner Lake resource remains open for expansion, with ongoing property exploration focused on generating new exploration targets.
- Robin Dunbar is the CEO of Grid Metals.
- The global maiden resource estimate consists of 2.10 MMt grading 1.42% Li2O from Open Pit resources (cut-off grade of 0.3% Li2O) and 4.71 MMt grading 1.37% Li2O from below pit underground resources (cut-off grade of 0.5% Li2O).
- The mineral resources are hosted by steeply dipping pegmatite dykes that are exposed at surface and display consistent widths, spodumene content, and lithium grade.
- Lithium mineralization at the Main and NW dykes remains open to depth with strong Li2O values in many of the deepest holes.
- The Company has identified a number of other exploration drill targets at Donner Lake and has an extensive land position it is actively exploring.
- The True North mill is part of an integrated, fully permitted gold mine, mill, and tailings complex last operational in November 2022.
- The True North mill is located 85 km by road from the Donner Lake Property.
- An application to permit the processing of lithium material at the True North mill will be required.
- Primero Group Ltd. has been engaged to complete a plant reconfiguration scoping-level study to assess the viability and cost of reconfiguring the True North mill to process spodumene-bearing material.
- The scoping study is expected to be substantially complete before the end of the 90-day due diligence period stipulated in the Agreement with 1911 Gold.
- Grid Metals has a pre-production MOU with Tanco to process lithium material at its operational lithium circuit.
- The maiden lithium resource estimate was prepared by SGS Geological Services and reported in accordance with National Instrument 43-101 standards.
- The NI 43-101 technical report for the resource estimate will be accessible on SEDAR within 45 days of the news release.
- The current mineral resource estimate comprises spodumene-enriched pegmatite from three northwest striking pegmatite dykes: the Main Dyke, a smaller sub-parallel dyke located ~50 metres north of the Main Dyke (the Hangingwall Dyke), and the NW Dyke.
- The Main Dyke interpretation used 57 drillholes totalling 13,478 metres of core.
- The NW Dyke interpretation used 34 drillholes totalling 7,815 metres of core.
- The resources cover an approximate strike length of 1,100 metres for the Main Dyke and 700 metres for the NW Dyke.
- Vertical extents are approximately 300 metres for the Main Dyke and ~250 metres for the NW Dyke.
- Open pit resources cover the uppermost ~100 metres in both the Main and NW dykes and are constrained by optimized pit models.
- Initial metallurgical testwork confirms that spodumene accounts for over 90% of the lithium-bearing minerals in the Main and NW dykes.
- The open-pit resources total 2.10 MMt grading 1.42% Li2O, including 1.14 MMt grading 1.48% Li2O at the Main and Hangingwall dykes, and 0.96 MMt grading 1.36% Li2O at the NW Dyke.
- The underground resources total 4.71 MMt grading 1.37% Li2O, including 3.67 MMt grading 1.39% Li2O at the Main Dyke and 1.04 MMt grading 1.11% Li2O at the NW Dyke.
- The mineral resources remain open at depth, particularly at the Main Dyke.
- The Company anticipates a new phase of exploration drilling later in the year.
- The mineral resource estimate was created using SGS's proprietary modelling software, Genesis, and open pit shells were created in Whittle software.
- The mineralised wireframe was restricted to drill core logged as lithium-bearing pegmatite.
- Block models were created using a 5m x 3m x 5m block size in the x-, y-, and z-planes.
- Grade was estimated using inverse distance squared (ID2) methodology and three passes of estimation.
- All resources are classified as Inferred Resources, consistent with the 2014 CIM Definitions for Mineral Resources and Mineral Reserves.
- The Mineral Resource Estimate has an effective date of June 27, 2023.
- The Qualified Person for the MRE is Mr. Rohan Millar, P.Geo., an employee of SGS.
- All resources are presented undiluted and in situ, constrained by continuous 3D wireframe models, and are considered to have reasonable prospects for eventual economic extraction.
- Mineral resources which are not mineral reserves do not have demonstrated economic viability.
- It is envisioned that parts of the Donner Lake deposit may be mined using open pit mining methods.
- In-pit mineral resources are reported at a cut-off grade of 0.3% Li2O within a conceptual pit shell.
- Underground Mineral Resources are estimated from the bottom of the pit and reported at a base case cut-off grade of 0.5% Li2O.
- Bulk density values (specific gravity 2.7 grams per cubic centimetre) were determined based on physical test work from each deposit.
- The in-pit base case cut-off grade of 0.3% Li2O considers a lithium concentrate 6% (LC6) Li2O price of US$1800/tonne, a mining cost of US$3.50/t rock and processing, treatment and refining, transportation and G&A cost of US$45.00/t mineralised material, and an overall pit slope of 55 degrees.
- The below-pit base case cut-off grade of 0.5% Li2O considers a lithium concentrate 6% (LC6) Li2O price of US$1800/tonne, a mining cost of US$60.00/t rock and processing, treatment and refining, transportation, and G&A cost of US$45.00/t mineralised material.
- The True North mill is located in Bissett, Manitoba, approximately 85 km by existing all-weather road from the Donner Lake Lithium Project.
- The original processing facility at the True North site was constructed in 1931 but was destroyed by a fire in 1980.
- The facility was reconstructed in 1996 with an operating capacity of 900 tonnes per day, and mill capacity was increased to 2,300 tpd in 2011.
- Since 2011, the mill capacity has been scaled back to 1,300 tpd.
- The mill was last in operation in November 2022 and was reprocessing gold tailings.
- The current processing facility consists of crushing, milling, centrifugal gravity concentration, and flotation circuits.
- Initial metallurgical test results concluded that standard flotation was the best method for recovering lithium from Donner Lake samples.
- Heavy Liquid Separation was conducted as an initial evaluation of the Dense Media Separation process but was determined to not be of material value for enhancing lithium recovery.
- Grid Metals engaged Primero to complete a plant reconfiguration study including preliminary capital costing analysis.
- Primero has worked with lithium companies including Sigma Lithium, Core Lithium, and Sayona Mining.
- The initial lease term for the True North mill is five years, followed by a two-year notice period for cancellation, or it may be extended by mutual agreement for an additional five years.
- The lease agreement conveys rights to Grid Metals including use of the mill to process lithium material and the right to reconfigure the existing mill circuit provided it is returned to original working condition at lease end.
- Grid Metals has the right to use the tailings facility and apply for required permits and government approvals for processing lithium-bearing material.
- Grid Metals has the right to add infrastructure to the mill assets, subject to an obligation to remove any such infrastructure upon termination of the Definitive Agreement.
- Under certain conditions, Grid would provide cost plus toll milling of gold material for 1911 Gold during the lease term.
- Payments by Grid to 1911 Gold include an upfront non-refundable payment of C$300,000.
- Grid must make an equity participation payment in a 1911 Gold private placement of C$450,000 within 90 days of signing the Agreement.
- Within 90 days of signing, Grid must make a total payment of C$1,000,000 consisting of a C$250,000 lease payment and C$750,000 of financial assistance.
- Commencing January 1, 2024, monthly payments will cover ongoing site expenses for site security, environmental monitoring, and maintenance.
- Financial assistance payments include up to C$1,000,000 due on December 31, 2023; C$1,000,000 on December 31, 2024; and C$900,000 on December 31, 2025.
- A payment of C$1,000,000 is due upon commencement of commercial production by Grid at the mill, defined as processing at least 200,000 tonnes of lithium material.
- A payment of C$1,000,000 is due on the fifth anniversary of the lease.
- A payment of C$2,000,000 is due if the parties agree to extend the lease for an additional five-year period at the end of the first five-year term.
- Grid must pay a 1% net smelter returns royalty in favour of 1911 Gold, subject to Grid receiving a right of first refusal on any disposition by 1911 Gold.
- A fee payment of $7.50 per tonne of lithium material processed through the mill facility is required during the lease term.
- Grid must assume C$10,000,000 of reclamation obligations prorated equally over years 6-10 of the lease if extended.
- Grid may terminate the agreement up to 90 days from signing after making initial cash and equity payments, or thereafter once cumulative total payments reach C$2,500,000.
- 1911 Gold shall permit Grid to register a floating charge security interest on the mill assets equal to Grid's total financial assistance payments and capital expenditures after 90 days from signing.
- Grid is pursuing a two-stage permitting process at Donner Lake, starting with an Advanced Exploration Permit application to enable a bulk sample of up to 50,000 tonnes.
- The second permitting step is an application for an Environmental Act Licence (mining permit).
- The True North Mill will require an amendment to its existing permits to enable processing of lithium material.
- Dr. Dave Peck, P.Geo., has reviewed and approved the technical content of the release.
- Grid Metals is focused on both lithium and Ni-Cu-PGM projects in the Bird River greenstone belt in southeastern Manitoba, approximately 150 km northeast of Winnipeg.
- The company controls two major lithium properties with known high-grade lithium pegmatites: Donner Lake and Falcon West.
- The 75% owned Donner Lake Property is situated 35km north of the producing Tanco mine, one of only two lithium-producing mines in Canada.
- In addition to lithium assets, the Company controls a PEA stage Ni-Cu-PGM-Co project (Makwa-Mayville) undergoing exploration and development activity.
- The Donner Lake and Falcon West properties are located in the traditional territory of the Sagkeeng First Nation.
