# TEKLAS

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2021-01-15

**Source:** [Electric Vehicle Manufacturer TEKLAS to Open North American Headquarters in Georgia - Expansion Solutions](https://expansionsolutionsmagazine.com/electric-vehicle-manufacturer-georgia/)  ·  `page_051559`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that TEKLAS will invest $6.5 million in opening its first North American facility and headquarters in Gordon County.
- The project will deliver 120 jobs to Calhoun.
- TEKLAS is a Turkish advanced research and development, manufacturing company and supplier of electric vehicle (EV) parts.
- Founded in Turkey in 1971, TEKLAS designs, develops, and manufactures fluid circulation systems for cooling and heating applications.
- The company produces hoses and pipes from rubber, plastic, and metal for the global automotive industry.
- TEKLAS uses highly innovative and automated manufacturing processes using advanced water molding technology.
- TEKLAS USA will include assembly lines to serve prominent original equipment manufacturers (OEMs).
- Operations at the new facility are scheduled to start in the spring of 2021.
- The 200,000-square-foot facility and headquarters will be located at 320 South Industrial Boulevard in Calhoun, Georgia.
- Hiring will cover a variety of skill levels, including positions in advanced manufacturing, management, and supervision.
- Regional Director Arcan Ergur stated that the Calhoun location will help the company be closer to its main customers.
- Larry Roye, chairman of the Development Authority of Gordon County, applauded state and local partners for helping encourage the company's decision.
- Kathy Johnson, Gordon County Chamber of Commerce President and CEO, commended TEKLAS for its research and advancements in electric vehicle manufacturing.
- GDEcD Project Manager Fernanda Kirchner represented the Global Commerce division on this competitive project.
- The project was executed in partnership with the Economic Development Authority of Gordon County and Georgia Power.
- The Center of Innovation for Logistics, a division of GDEcD, provided freight analysis to support the project and company planning.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Georgia is focused on attracting and growing the jobs of the future.
- Wilson noted the automotive industry's transformation toward electric vehicles and e-mobility and pledged to foster an ecosystem of manufacturers and suppliers on the leading edge of the transition.
- TEKLAS serves major car original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) with more than 45 years of experience in design, development, and production capabilities.
- The company provides innovative solutions for thermoplastics, rubber, and metal products to its customers.
