# Hyundai Motor Co.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09

**Source:** [Hyundai announces Georgia Metaplant to soon undergo $2.7B expansion](https://www.ajc.com/business/2025/09/10-hybrid-and-ev-models-3k-jobs-part-of-hyundais-next-phase-in-georgia/)  ·  `page_009200`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Co. unveiled an ambitious growth plan to accelerate its electrified vehicle offerings in North America.
- The company's strategy hinges on rapid expansion in Georgia.
- Hyundai detailed a $2.7 billion expansion to its electric vehicle factory near Savannah.
- The factory only completed its first construction phase 11 months ago.
- Hyundai plans to hire 3,000 more workers in Georgia.
- Hyundai plans to increase production to 500,000 units annually at the Metaplant by 2028.
- The Metaplant is a 16 million-square-foot factory.
- The 3,000 new jobs are part of the 8,500 total jobs Hyundai previously said it will eventually employ at the campus.
- The campus is located along I-16 in Bryan County.
- By the end of the decade, Hyundai aims for 60% of all vehicle sales to have electrified powertrains, which includes fully electric vehicles and hybrids.
- The Metaplant is slated to eventually assemble 10 different EV and hybrid models.
- Thursday’s announcement comes on the heels of a Sept. 4 immigration raid at the campus’ battery factory, currently under construction.
- The immigration raid triggered an international firestorm.
- Hyundai has said it remains committed to the project despite the raid.
- The raid has caused anger in Korea.
- Construction of the site will temporarily pause amid discussions about visa issues.
