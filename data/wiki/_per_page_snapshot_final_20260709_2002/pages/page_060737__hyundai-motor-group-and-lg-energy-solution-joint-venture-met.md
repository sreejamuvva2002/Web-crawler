# Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution Joint Venture (Metaplant America)

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-08-31

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution to Invest Additional $2B in Bryan County - HMGMA](https://www.hmgma.com/2023/08/31/hyundai-motor-group-and-lg-energy-solution-to-invest-additional-2b-in-bryan-county/)  ·  `page_060737`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution will invest an additional $2 billion in their battery cell manufacturing joint venture at the Metaplant in Bryan County.
- This expansion raises the joint venture’s total investment value to more than $4.3 billion.
- The expansion will create another 400 new jobs.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that in a single year, they broke ground on the largest project in state history, landed multiple suppliers across the state for Hyundai’s Metaplant, and welcomed LGES to Bryan County.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated Georgia is continuing to make itself the e-mobility capital of the nation.
- Governor Brian Kemp noted that these major investments ultimately go to hardworking Georgians in the form of paychecks, improved schools and infrastructure, and more.
- Hyundai Motor Group thanked LGES for recognizing Georgia as the No. 1 state for business.
- The incremental investment reflects a continued commitment to create a more sustainable future powered by American workers.
- In May 2023, Hyundai Motor Group and LGES signed a memorandum of understanding establishing LGES as the partner for Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America’s (HMGMA) onsite battery cell manufacturing joint venture.
- With this announcement, the EV manufacturing facility and the battery joint venture represents an estimated $7.59 billion in investment.
- The facility will create 8,500 new jobs for the region over the next eight years.
- Dong-Myung Kim, president and head of the Advanced Automotive Battery Division of LG Energy Solution, stated the investment underscores dedication to driving America’s EV transition while bolstering the local economy through the creation of quality jobs.
- Dong-Myung Kim thanked the State of Georgia for support to bring top-quality products and operational experience to grow with its communities.
- The facility will have a capacity of 30 GWh.
- The facility will be able to support the production of 300,000 units of EVs annually at full operations.
- Hyundai Mobis will assemble battery packs using cells from the plant.
- Hyundai Mobis will supply the assembled battery packs to Hyundai Motor Group’s U.S. manufacturing facilities for production of Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis EV models.
- The announcement brings 400 additional well-paying jobs for those in the Savannah region.
- Carter Infinger, Chairman of the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority, stated the continued investment by Hyundai Motor Group and announced suppliers like LGES is truly remarkable and will be transformative for the region.
- Alyce Thornhill, Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy, represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The GDEcD partnered with the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority (JDA), Georgia Power, Georgia Ports Authority, and Georgia Quick Start on this project.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated Governor Kemp set a goal of Georgia becoming the top location for EV manufacturing in the United States.
- Pat Wilson noted Georgia continues to add to industry-leading success by building an ecosystem that supports the entire EV supply chain, bringing jobs home to Georgians in every corner of the state.
- Pat Wilson credited state and community partners like the Savannah-area JDA, utility providers, Georgia Ports Authority, and Georgia Quick Start for creating a collaborative, business-friendly environment.
- Since 2018, Georgia has pursued job creation along the entire EV supply chain, resulting in more than $25 billion in investments and the creation of over 30,000 jobs.
- Battery related projects have accounted for at least $11 billion in investment.
- In fiscal year 2023, job creation in the automotive industry increased by 324 percent when compared to FY21.
