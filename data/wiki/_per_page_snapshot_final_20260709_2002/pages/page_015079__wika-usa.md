## Facts

- WIKA USA is a worldwide leader in industrial measurement technology.
- The company was founded in 1946, at which time its core values were established as standards for measuring itself and its employees.
- WIKA USA began the Core Value Award program two decades ago to acknowledge employee dedication and hard work.
- The award program is peer-nominated and recognizes employees who exemplify one of seven core values: integrity, customer satisfaction, partnership, safety, continuous improvement, social responsibility, and excellence.
- A committee composed of senior managers selects the award winners.
- Winners receive a plaque, a monetary award, and a designated parking spot at the main WIKA USA campus in Lawrenceville, Georgia.
- Five employees were recognized for their work in January 2021: Megan Brown (Customer Care), Dwayne Gary (Parts & Receiving), Lisa Thomas (Manufacturing), Khoa Nguyen (Manufacturing), and Abriel “Abby” Vega (Inventory Management).
- John Pugh praised Megan Brown, an order entry specialist, for her diligence and ability to handle all company orders alone after staff reductions.
- Jeff Wood nominated Dwayne Gary from Parts & Receiving, noting he managed high-pressure dock operations during staffing shortages with a determined attitude.
- Pat Campbell commended Lisa Thomas from Manufacturing for implementing Production Logistics and teaching others about brass cell process improvements.
- Justin Durr commended Khoa Nguyen from Manufacturing for helping the CNC (Computerized Numerical Control) team learn new machines and programming, which greatly increased productivity.
- Andrew Campbell thanked Abriel “Abby” Vega from Inventory Management for working tirelessly through the holiday break to resolve inventory data issues from site consolidations and moves.
- The company manages inventory across multiple locations, including a site in Pasadena, Texas.
