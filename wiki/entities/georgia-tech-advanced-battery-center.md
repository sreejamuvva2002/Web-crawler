# Georgia Tech Advanced Battery Center

**Type:** Research Center  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[research-and-development|Research & Development]]  ·  **Location:** Georgia, United States

## Overview

The Georgia Tech Advanced Battery Center (GTABC) unites the expertise of Georgia Tech’s faculty and students to create the next battery technologies for electric vehicles, grid energy storage, electric aviation, and other applications.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Develops batteries for electric mobility, grid storage, and renewable energy applications supporting Georgia's EV ecosystem.

## Key facts

- Georgia Tech has over 20 faculty and more than 150 researchers working to power the future with next generation energy storage technologies.
- Focus on batteries for electric mobility, grid, and renewable energy storage.
- Working to establish a grid storage “living lab” to enable R&D on realistic energy storage use scenarios.
- GTABC unites Georgia Tech faculty and students to create battery technologies for EVs, grid storage, and electric aviation.
- Co-Directors Dr. Matthew McDowell and Dr. Gleb Yushin bring decades of experience in battery research and innovative development.
