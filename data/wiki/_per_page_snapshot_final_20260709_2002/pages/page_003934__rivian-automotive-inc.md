# Rivian Automotive, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-04-30

**Source:** [Rivian Releases First Quarter 2026 Financial Results](https://rivian.com/newsroom/article/rivian-releases-first-quarter-2026-financial-results)  ·  `page_003934`

## Facts

- Started production of saleable R2 vehicles and made first deliveries to employees with external customer deliveries expected in the coming weeks.
- Consolidated revenue grew 11 percent year-over-year, generating $119 million of consolidated gross profit for the quarter.
- Increased Georgia plant initial production capacity to 300,000 vehicles annually.
- Expects first advance on $4.5 billion DOE loan in early 2027.
- Entered a robotaxi partnership with Uber to deploy up to 50,000 fully autonomous robotaxis.
- Received $1 billion of funding from Volkswagen Group through successful completion of winter testing.
- RJ Scaringe, Rivian Founder and CEO, stated that the Department of Energy's support for the $4.5 billion loan to build the Georgia facility enables Rivian to grow American jobs and establish stronger U.S. technology and manufacturing leadership while further scaling its customer base.
- Announced the start of production of saleable R2 vehicles in Normal, Illinois.
- Increased the initial production capacity of its Georgia plant by 50 percent, bringing the first phase to 300,000 units of annual production capacity for its mid-sized vehicle platform.
- The capacity increase is expected to boost cost efficiency while providing significant room for future expansion in later phases.
- Remains on track for vehicle production in Georgia to begin in late 2028.
- Worked with the Department of Energy to make strategic changes to its DOE loan as part of the new vision for the Georgia facility.
- The up to $4.5 billion loan consists of $4,006 million principal and $494 million capitalized interest, aligned with the updated facility design.
- Expects to draw on the DOE loan by early 2027, subject to meeting certain conditions.
- Announced a partnership with Uber in March to accelerate autonomous vehicle plans.
- The Uber deal enables Uber or its fleet partners to purchase 10,000 fully autonomous R2 robotaxis with an option to purchase up to 40,000 more in 2030.
- Uber will invest up to $1.25 billion in Rivian through 2031, subject to milestones and conditions, building toward a scaled, fully-autonomous fleet of Rivian R2 robotaxis available through the Uber platform.
- An initial $300 million equity investment from Uber is expected to close in Q2 2026, subject to conditions.
- An additional $250 million equity investment from Uber is expected later in 2026, subject to conditions.
- Rivian’s joint venture with Volkswagen Group, RV Tech, successfully completed winter weather testing of its production-intent zonal architecture for first-generation software-defined vehicles.
- Produced 10,236 vehicles at Rivian’s manufacturing facility in Normal, Illinois during Q1 2026.
- Delivered 10,365 vehicles to customers during Q1 2026.
- Consolidated revenue was $1,381 million, an 11 percent increase over the same quarter of the previous year.
- Automotive segment revenue was $908 million, a 2 percent decrease over the same quarter of the previous year, primarily due to a $100 million decrease in sales of automotive regulatory credits and a decline in automotive revenue per unit delivered due to a higher mix of commercial vans, partially offset by a 20 percent increase in vehicle deliveries.
- Software and services segment revenue was $473 million, a 49 percent increase over the same quarter of the previous year, due to increased vehicle electrical architecture and software development services, as well as vehicle repair and maintenance service and remarketing.
- Consolidated gross profit was $119 million, an $87 million decrease over the same quarter in the previous year.
- Automotive segment gross profit loss was $(62) million compared to $92 million in gross profit for the same quarter in 2025, due primarily to the $100 million decrease in sales of automotive regulatory credits and lower production volumes, resulting in a $42 million and $3 million increase in depreciation and stock based compensation respectively.
- Software and services segment gross profit was $181 million, a $67 million increase over the same quarter in the previous year, primarily due to higher vehicle electrical architecture and software development services and vehicle repair and maintenance services.
- Total operating expenses increased to $1,000 million compared to $861 million in the same quarter of the previous year, including $180 million in non-cash stock-based compensation and $80 million in depreciation and amortization expense.
- R&D expense was $458 million compared to $381 million in the same quarter of the previous year, primarily related to increased software and cloud spend on autonomy and R2 pre-production costs, including payroll and related expenses.
- SG&A expense was $542 million compared to $480 million in the previous year, primarily related to expanding go-to-market operations and footprint, including higher payroll and stock-based compensation expenses and facilities, software, and other operating expenses.
- Experienced a loss from operations of $881 million compared to $655 million in the same quarter of the previous year due to lower gross profit and higher operating expenses.
- Total adjusted operating expenses for Q1 2026 were $740 million compared to $630 million for the same quarter of the previous year.
- Adjusted R&D expenses for Q1 2026 were $348 million compared to $285 million for the same quarter the previous year.
- Adjusted SG&A expenses for Q1 2026 were $392 million compared to $345 million the previous year.
- Net loss for Q1 2026 was $(416) million compared to $(541) million in the same quarter of the previous year, benefiting from a $506 million gain in other income related to the Series A capital raise and deconsolidation of Mind Robotics.
- Adjusted EBITDA for Q1 2026 was $(472) million compared to $(329) million in the same quarter of the previous year.
- Net cash used in operating activities for Q1 2026 was $(703) million compared to $(188) million in the same quarter of the previous year, driven by cash consumed by working capital, increased operating expenses, and a reduction in regulatory credit sales.
- Capital expenditures for Q1 2026 were $372 million compared to $338 million in the same quarter of the previous year.
- Ended Q1 2026 with $4,830 million in cash, cash equivalents, and short-term investments.
- Held $5,394 million of total liquidity including capacity under its asset-based revolving-credit facility at the end of Q1 2026.
- Reported negative free cash flow of $(1,075) million for Q1 2026 compared to negative free cash flow of $(526) million for the same quarter of the previous year.
- Hosted an audio webcast on April 30, 2026, at 2:00pm PT / 5:00pm ET to discuss results and provide a business update.
