# DENSO Manufacturing Tennessee Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2017-10-07

**Source:** [The DENSO Manufacturing Tennessee Journey: 1987-2017 | News | thedailytimes.com](https://www.thedailytimes.com/news/the-denso-manufacturing-tennessee-journey-1987-2017/article_9a199cb2-5646-592b-8bce-c81caee52f8d.html)  ·  `page_053791`

## Facts

- In December 1987, Nippondenso Co. Ltd. announced a $200 million plant would be built in Maryville with initial plans to hire 550 employees.
- In July 1988, Mike Taira was named the first president of Nippondenso Tennessee.
- In August 1989, the company shipped its first pass-through parts to Subaru from an Ellis Avenue office.
- In March 1990, production began on instrument clusters at Plant 201.
- In May 1990, production began on starters at Plant 101.
- In June 1990, production began on alternators at Plant 101.
- On October 16, 1990, Maryville operations officially grand opened, encompassing the Starter/Alternator and Instrument Clusters divisions.
- In March 1991, production began on MAPS Sensors in the Electronic Products Division at Plant 202.
- In May 1991, production began on A/C Amplifiers in the Electronic Products Division at Plant 202.
- On May 9, 1991, the Electronic Products Division at Plant 202 grand opened.
- In July 1991, Andy Kataoka became the second president of DMTN, serving until 1995.
- In August 1991, production began on Engine ECUs in the Electronic Products Division at Plant 202.
- In July 1992, Phase II expansion added 52,000 square feet to Plant 201 for instrument clusters.
- In February 1993, Phase II expansion added 100,000 square feet to Plant 202 for electronic products.
- In September 1994, the S/A Division at Plant 101 expanded by 116,000 square feet.
- In November 1994, Plants 202 and 203 began a project to connect buildings, adding 126,000 square feet.
- In July 1995, the company expanded into Athens with groundbreaking for the Ceramic Products Division.
- Kurt Shimotsu became the third DMTN president in July 1995, serving until 1999.
- In March 1996, Plant 201 reached a milestone of 5 million instrument clusters produced.
- In May 1996, Plant 202 reached a milestone of 3 million Engine ECU and A/C products produced.
- In June 1996, the company purchased Building 103 for future expansion, measuring 115,000 square feet.
- In July 1996, Nippondenso officially changed its name to DENSO.
- In August 1996, the Starter/Alternator Division expanded with a Cold Forging Project in Building 102.
- In August 1996, Plant 101 reached a milestone of 10 million alternators produced.
- In October 1996, Plant 101 reached a milestone of 10 million starters produced.
- In January 1997, the Electronic Products Division at Plant 202 expanded to produce Micro ISO Relays.
- In June 1997, the Electronic Products Division at Plant 202 expanded to produce Remote Keyless Entry systems, MAPS sensors, TAPS sensors, and Air-bag ECUs.
- In September 1997, Plant 101 expanded alternator production with A-Pro technology.
- In November 1997, the Athens Ceramic Products Division grand opened, producing oxygen sensors.
- In December 1997, Maryville operations received ISO 14001 environmental certification.
- In June 1998, Plant 201 reached a milestone of 10 million instrument clusters produced.
- In June 1998, Plant 202 reached a milestone of 5 million Engine ECU and A/C ECUs produced.
- In June 1998, production began on the Cold Forming Project in Building 102.
- In October 1998, the Athens Fuel Injection Division grand opened.
- In March 1999, Neal Ozeki became the fourth DMTN president, serving until 2004.
- In August 2000, an Associate Center was dedicated as a 10th-anniversary gift.
- In September 2000, a Logistics Center was established in Building 301.
- In February 2001, Plant 202 reached a milestone of 30 million Micro-ISO Relays produced.
- In April 2001, North American Integrated Logistics (NAIL) was established.
- In April 2002, Plant 202 reached a milestone of 10 million Engine ECUs produced.
- In April 2003, the Athens operations became independent as DENSO Manufacturing Athens Tennessee Inc.
- In April 2004, Mack Hattori became the fifth DMTN president, serving until 2008.
- In August 2005, a 15th-anniversary celebration included remodeling of company cafes.
- In July 2006, North American Production Support (NAPS) started operations, including NAPC, NALC, and NATIE.
- In October 2007, mass production began at the Body Division Plant 203 (Body 1 & 2).
- In April 2007, Art Shimmura became the sixth DMTN president, serving until 2009.
- In April 2008, the Body Division Plant 203 grand opened.
- In March 2009, Plant 201 reached a milestone of 30 million instrument clusters produced.
- In April 2009, Van Saka became the seventh DMTN president, serving until 2013.
- In April 2009, Plant 101 reached a milestone of 30 million starters produced.
- In August 2009, Plant 202 reached a milestone of 10 million Airbag ECUs produced.
- In February 2010, Plant 202 reached a milestone of 15 million TAPS sensors produced.
- In May 2010, the company announced plans for a future EcoPark wetland learning environment during its 20th anniversary.
- In June 2010, Plant 202 reached a milestone of 20 million Engine ECUs produced.
- In December 2010, Plant 101 reached a milestone of 50 million alternators produced.
- In October 2011, the Machinery & Tools Division consolidated into the North American Production Promotion Center (NA-PPC) in Building 103.
- In December 2011, EcoPark and Keep Blount Beautiful’s EcoCenter outdoor pavilion were dedicated as a partnership providing environmental lessons to elementary school students.
- In July 2012, DNJP named DMTN’s Jack Helmboldt as the first American on its corporate board.
- In April 2012, Plant 101 expanded production with the new Stop-start Starter product.
- In April 2013, DMTN purchased Building 105, formerly the Key Safety Building.
- In June 2013, Jack Helmboldt became the first American president and CEO of DMTN, serving from 2013 onward.
- In March 2014, Plant 202 expanded production with the new EHV Inverter product and sent its first shipment.
- In June 2014, DENSO Corp. named DMTN President & CEO Jack Helmboldt as a corporate executive director.
- In September 2014, the company announced the DENSO Family Health Center as a 25th-anniversary gift.
- In May 2015, the DENSO Family Health Center held its grand opening.
- On August 25, 2015, DENSO Tennessee announced a $400 million investment at DMTN to create 500 new jobs in Blount County.
- In July 2016, DENSO broke ground on an 186,528-square-foot warehouse as part of the $400 million expansion expected to create 500 new jobs.
- In August 2017, DENSO Manufacturing Tennessee unveiled a new “Crafting the Core” brand.
- On October 6, 2017, DENSO announced a $1 billion investment in a plant to build parts for hybrid and electric cars, creating 1,000 jobs.
- In October 2017, the company cut the ribbon on the 186,528-square-foot consolidated warehouse.
