# Seoyon E-Hwa

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-02

**Source:** [Seoyon E-HWA Announces New Chatham Manufacturing Facility, Nearly Doubles Georgia Employment - Savannah CEO](https://savannahceo.com/features/2023/02/seoyon-e-hwa-announces-new-chatham-manufacturing-facility-nearly-doubles-georgia-employment/)  ·  `page_059529`

## Facts

- Governor Brian Kemp announced that Seoyon E-HWA will create 740 new direct and indirect jobs.
- Seoyon E-HWA will invest almost $76 million in a new manufacturing facility in Chatham County.
- Seoyon E-HWA is a global auto parts supplier that manufactures both interior and exterior components.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated he previously announced the largest economic development project in Georgia history last year.
- Governor Brian Kemp promised to bring more jobs and opportunity to hardworking Georgians.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated Georgia is working to ensure its state is the e-mobility capital of the nation.
- Governor Brian Kemp noted projects like this will continue to choose Georgia as the No. 1 state for business.
- Seoyon E-Hwa was established in 1972.
- Seoyon E-Hwa operates 32 corporations in North America, South America, Europe, and Asia.
- Seoyon E-Hwa supplies door trim, headlining, seats, C/PAD, bumpers, and other parts to Hyundai Motor Group, Kia, Mercedes-Benz, Ford, Volkswagen, and more.
- Seoyon’s LaGrange location will continue to support 630 full time jobs.
- Seoyon E-Hwa Chairman Mr. Ryu Yang-seok stated Seoyon E-Hwa is an automobile manufacturer in various countries such as Korea, Europe, and India.
- Seoyon E-Hwa Chairman Mr. Ryu Yang-seok expressed honor to supply electric vehicle parts in Chatham County, Georgia.
- Seoyon E-Hwa Chairman Mr. Ryu Yang-seok stated the establishment of Seoyon E-Hwa Savannah will provide new jobs in the region and contribute to making Savannah a central city for automobile development and production.
- Seoyon E-Hwa Chairman Mr. Ryu Yang-seok committed to cooperating with the local government continuously.
- Seoyon’s new facility will be located at the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center.
- The site is a Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) Certified site.
- The new manufacturing facility will produce automotive interior parts such as door trims, headlining, and tailgate trims.
- Production is expected to begin in October 2024.
- More than two-thirds, or approximately 500, of the announced jobs will be permanent roles in human resources, development, quality, and production personnel.
- Savannah Economic Development Authority President and CEO Trip Tollison stated the announcement positions Seoyon E-HWA in close proximity to the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- Trip Tollison noted proximity to the Port of Savannah, Interstates I-16 and I-95, and more.
- Trip Tollison highlighted Seoyon E-HWA has a proven track record of supplying the automotive industry.
- Trip Tollison stated the facility will provide well paying jobs for the region.
- Georgia Ports Authority Executive Director Griff Lynch stated Seoyon is a valued customer using the Port of Savannah to supply its manufacturing facility in West Georgia.
- Griff Lynch expressed excitement to help support Seoyon’s growth in this new location as the Hyundai announcement continues to have positive ripple effects throughout the regional economy.
- Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy Alyce Thornhill represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The project was in partnership with the Savannah Economic Development Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia Power.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated Seoyon is committing to nearly doubling the number of jobs it supports in the state with its second Georgia facility.
- Pat Wilson noted Hyundai’s new EV facility is drawing an experienced and dedicated supplier network to the region.
- Pat Wilson stated this adds to the state’s industry expertise and shapes a larger skilled workforce.
- Building on automotive industry assets, Georgia is positioned as a hub for the electric mobility industry.
- Since 2020, more than 35 EV-related projects have announced over $21 billion in investment and 26,700 jobs in Georgia.
- On October 25, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America officially broke ground in neighboring Bryan County on its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility.
- Offsite suppliers for Hyundai's project have already exceeded the $1 billion in investment predicted from other locations connected to the project around the state by more than $600 million.
