## Facts

- Fox Factory Holding Corp. is headquartered in Duluth, Georgia, with zip code 30097 in Congressional District GA-4 ATLANTA.
- The company employs 5600 people.
- Fox Factory manufactures performance-defining suspension products, including shocks, forks, and parts for vehicles.
- The company's core business falls under NAICS code 336330 for Motor Vehicle Steering and Suspension Components Manufacturing.
- Fox Factory has long-term OEM contracts with Ford, Toyota, and Polaris that drive stable revenue.
- Fox Factory's suspension systems are factory-installed on vehicles such as the Ford Raptor and Toyota TRD models.
- The company is aggressively investing in the long-term structural growth drivers of vehicle electrification and intelligent suspension systems.
- A key strategic priority for Fox Factory is to secure next-generation EV platforms with top OEM partners.
- Fox Factory develops electronically-controlled suspension systems, including its proprietary Live Valve e-suspension technology which creates a significant technology moat.
- The company's global manufacturing operations are located in the US, Taiwan, and Canada with a diverse supplier base.
- Past supply chain issues at Fox Factory have occasionally delayed deliveries to OEM partners.
- Geopolitical tensions are currently impacting Fox Factory's manufacturing hub in Taiwan.
- Fox Factory faces high buyer power from large OEM customers like Ford and Trek due to their volume demands and tight integration requirements.
- The company maintains deep OEM integration at the design stage, creating high switching costs for automotive partners.
- The company's strategic priorities include doubling down on electronic suspension technology to widen its technology gap with competitors.
- The company is expanding direct-to-consumer sales in the aftermarket segment to generate higher-margin revenue.
- Fox Factory was founded in 1974 by Bob Fox.
- The company holds a leading market share in high-performance bike and powered vehicle suspension.
- Fox Factory's business model includes revenue streams from Specialty Sports Group (Bike), Powered Vehicle Group (Trucks, UTVs), and aftermarket parts and upgrades.
- The company leverages race-proven technology and engineering to deliver high-performance products for cycling, powersports, and off-road vehicles.
- Fox Factory's premium pricing strategy limits its total addressable customer base but supports historically strong profit margins.
- The company is diversifying into adjacent markets like sports equipment to hedge against the cyclicality of its core automotive and bike business.
- Competition in the suspension market is intense, particularly from SRAM (RockShox) in bikes and players like Öhlins and King in powersports.
- Fox Factory's strategic pillars include dominating core markets, driving tech differentiation in ride dynamics, achieving world-class manufacturing & supply chain, and deepening OEM partnerships from design to delivery.
