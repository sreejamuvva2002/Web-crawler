# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-02

**Source:** [Georgia plant begins Kia Sportage Hybrid production](https://www.savannahnow.com/story/news/state/2026/06/02/georgia-plant-begins-kia-sportage-hybrid-production/90352213007/)  ·  `page_001036`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group will begin manufacturing the Kia Sportage Hybrid in the United States for the first time.
- The vehicle will be produced at the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America in Georgia.
- This marks the first hybrid vehicle and first Kia model to be produced at the Georgia EV plant.
- Hyundai Motor Company announced Tuesday that it plans to manufacture the Kia Sportage Hybrid for the first time in the U.S. at Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- The Kia Sportage Hybrid is currently produced in South Korea.
- Tony Heo, President and CEO of HMGMA, stated that the facility's flexible manufacturing system enabled the production of its first hybrid vehicle and first Kia model.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp, First Lady Marty Kemp, and Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson attended the production celebration.
- Governor Kemp stated that HMGMA was announced as the largest economic plant in Georgia’s history, leveraging the state's two deep water ports.
- HMGMA is Hyundai Motor Company's only EV plant in the U.S. and currently manufactures the Hyundai IONIQ 5 and IONIQ 9.
- The Kia Sportage Hybrid features a futuristic, sleek design reminiscent of Hyundai's EV products and will come in five trims.
- The Kia Sportage Hybrid achieves 42 miles per gallon, with a base MSRP of $30,490.
- George Kennedy, product and technology communications manager at Kia America, emphasized competing on price and offering standard amenities typically found as add-ons on other brands.
- Governor Kemp stated the announcement puts Georgia in a position to continue its success in ensuring quality jobs.
- Governor Kemp highlighted the partnership between Kia America, HMGMA, and the local community as key to supporting long-term jobs in Georgia.
- Approximately 200 facility employees ("Meta Pros") attended the celebration to mark preparation for the new powertrain.
- The Kia West Point facility employs more than 3,000 team members, while HMGMA has nearly 2,000 metapros.
- Kia is not revealing production numbers at this time, however manufacturing of the vehicle has begun.
- HMGMA continues to ramp up production towards its goal of manufacturing 500,000 vehicles annually.
- HMC President and COO José Muñoz reported on LinkedIn that Hyundai's hybrid sales are up 90% and EV sales are up 10%.
- Hyundai sold 87,468 vehicles, up 3% year-over-year, while Genesis achieved its best May ever, marking 20 consecutive months of growth.
- Sales of the Georgia-built IONIQ 5 are up 28%, and sales of the three-row IONIQ 9 are up 279%.
- Kia North America President and CEO Sean Yoon highlighted the significance of introducing the vehicle at the metaplant.
- Sean Yoon noted that Kia broke ground in West Point 20 years ago, praised Georgia's government for training support, and emphasized the need for production flexibility.
- Kia Georgia President and COO Stuart Countess explained that the vehicle addresses a market gap for larger SUVs with hybrid technology.
- Stuart Countess stated that consumer demand for technology and lifestyle fit drives interest in hybrids, noting the battery recharges while running, extended fuel range, and the plant's ability to build three powertrains to adapt to market shifts.
