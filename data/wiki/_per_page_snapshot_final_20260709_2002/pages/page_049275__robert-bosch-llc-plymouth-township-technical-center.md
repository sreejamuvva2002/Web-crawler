# Robert Bosch LLC Plymouth Township Technical Center

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** research_and_development  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Bosch Breaks Ground on Technical Center Expansion in Plymouth Township, Michigan - Bosch Media Service US](https://www.bosch-presse.de/pressportal/us/en/press-release-608.html)  ·  `page_049275`

## Facts

- Michigan Gov. Rick Snyder joined Robert Bosch LLC executives to break ground on the 220,500-square-foot expansion to the site that will nearly double the size of the existing site.
- Total investment of $40 million to design, build, equip and furnish site includes estimated $25 million for construction.
- Added capacity will allow Bosch to increase efficiencies through co-location and collaboration of associates in Southeast Michigan with up to 200 new jobs expected in the next three years.
- The expansion is to be completed in November 2015 and will include several energy-saving technologies, including the installation of a DC Microgrid.
- The groundbreaking ceremony took place in Plymouth Township, Michigan, at the existing Technical Center located at 15000 Haggerty Road.
- Michigan Gov. Rick Snyder stated that Bosch's commitment to expand its presence in Michigan while enhancing research and development work shows that Michigan is a great place to do business.
- Gov. Snyder noted that Michigan's business climate has improved dramatically, making the state a prime location for global companies like Bosch to expand and invest.
- Gov. Snyder highlighted that Bosch is an advocate for preparing tomorrow's workforce through hands-on tech training.
- The expansion will nearly double the size of the Technical Center, which originally opened in 2007.
- When complete, the total site located on 75 acres will have more than 445,000 square feet and capacity for 1,400 people.
- The expansion will further enable the growth of key technologies including automotive electronics, start/stop motors, electrical drives, and a variety of safety and driver assistance systems that will advance automated driving.
- The new site will provide laboratory and office space to co-locate certain associates in southeast Michigan who currently work in different leased facilities, fostering more innovation and collaboration.
- Robert Bosch LLC President Mike Mansuetti stated that breaking ground to begin the expansion at a time when the market offers potential to grow is truly exciting for an innovation-based company.
- Mansuetti referenced founder Robert Bosch's belief that education and sustainability are essential for business and society success, aligning with the company imperative of "Invented for life."
- The Plymouth Technical Center currently houses 850 people.
- In Michigan, Bosch has approximately 3,200 associates across 15 cities including Farmington Hills (headquarters for Robert Bosch LLC), Plymouth Township, Ann Arbor, Bridgeport, Buchanan, Canton, Flat Rock, Kalamazoo, Kentwood, Orion, Novi, Rochester Hills, St. Joseph, and Warren.
- With this expansion and co-location of certain groups, Bosch expects to add up to 200 new jobs in Michigan in the next three years.
- Mansuetti thanked community and government leaders, noting that Plymouth Township offers a thriving environment for high-tech jobs.
- The township allowed Bosch to construct a temporary building on site for about 160 associates to keep jobs in Plymouth Township while the business case for further expansion was built.
- The Bosch Community Fund pledged a contribution of $10,000 to the Friends of the Rouge (FOTR) to assist with projects in the Plymouth community.
- Since the Bosch Community Fund was established in 2011, it has contributed $360,000 to various community organizations in the Township and more than $439,000 nationwide to FIRST Robotics, including $70,000 to the FIRST Plymouth Canton Schools Team 862 Lightning Robotics.
- FIRST (For Inspiration and Recognition of Science and Technology) designs programs that motivate young people to pursue education and career opportunities in science, technology, engineering and math.
- Schoolcraft College will work with two Bosch-sponsored FIRST Robotics teams in its labs during the 2015 season as part of a recently established partnership.
- This collaboration will benefit students who will work with and be mentored by professionals from both Bosch and Schoolcraft College.
- STEM is a key priority for Bosch due to statistics showing the supply of technical talent in STEM-related fields will not meet demand in the years ahead.
- The expanded site will showcase energy-efficient technologies including high-efficiency lighting (LED and induction lighting), a photovoltaic solar array, a battery backup system, high-volume low-speed fans, and a Direct Current (DC) Microgrid.
- Site architect for the expansion is Harley Ellis Devereaux.
