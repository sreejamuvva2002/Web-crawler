# Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** workforce_training  ·  **Published:** 2019-07-19

**Source:** [Doing the Work – Golf Car News](https://www.golfcarnews.com/doing-the-work/)  ·  `page_015646`

## Facts

- Yamaha Golf-Car is a major golf car brand committed to workforce development.
- Yamaha Golf-Car President Tom McDonald met with Secretary of the Interior David Bernhardt at the Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation (YMMC) facility in Newnan, Ga.
- Mike Chrzanowski, President of Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation (YMCC), and Ben Speciale, President of Yamaha U.S. Marine Business Unit, signed the President’s Pledge to America’s Workers.
- The signing reinforces Yamaha’s commitment to workforce development within its U.S. manufacturing facilities.
- Tom McDonald stated that jobs created through Yamaha facilities in Georgia make a positive impact on the state’s economy and contribute to the growth of the outdoor industry overall.
- The golf industry supported $191.9 billion in total annual activity in 2016.
- Golf industry support included 1.9 million jobs and 58.7 billion in wages and benefits.
- The U.S. experienced record golf participation with rounds played up 14% in June.
- Golf participation increased by 19.7% in July.
- Industry experts expect August results to show similar increases when posted next week.
- Yamaha will continue to invest in training and best practices necessary to create successful manufacturing career paths within each of its divisions.
- Yamaha operates a technician training facility in Kennesaw, Ga. that ensures success for Dealers and Distributors across the country.
- The Pledge to America’s Workers is an initiative that stemmed from the National Council for the American Worker, which was established on July 19, 2019.
- The council leads a national strategy for training and re-training workers in high demand industries, such as recreational manufacturing.
- More than 430 companies have signed the pledge to date.
- Signatories are contributing to more than 16 million new training opportunities for American students and workers over the next five years.
