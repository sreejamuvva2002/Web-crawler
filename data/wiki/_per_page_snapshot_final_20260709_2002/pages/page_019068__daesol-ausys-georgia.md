# Daesol Ausys Georgia

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Daesol Ausys entering Georgia EV market with $72M plant](https://theconstructionbroadsheet.com/daesol-ausys-entering-georgia-ev-market-with-m-plant-p1487-174.htm)  ·  `page_019068`

## Facts

- South Korean auto parts company Daesol Ausys Georgia is investing $72 million in a new manufacturing facility in West Point, Georgia.
- Governor Brian Kemp announced the investment on Tuesday.
- Daesol Ausys specializes in designing and manufacturing motor and electric vehicle (EV) interior components and accessories.
- Daesol Ausys is a supplier to Kia Georgia, Hyundai Motor Group, and General Motors.
- The new plant will manufacture luggage boards and luggage covers.
- The facility will be located at the Harris County industrial park factory.
- The new plant is expected to become operational in late 2024.
- The new plant is expected to create 140 new jobs.
- Min Ho Kwon serves as the CEO of Daesol Ausys.
- The project is located in the Northwest Harris Business Park.
- Min Ho Kwon stated that the company is committed to delivering world-class services that exceed client requirements and expectations, and expressed excitement about continuing its partnership with the State of Georgia and Harris County regarding this project.
- Georgia has seen significant investments in automaker and auto parts supplier plants in recent years, particularly in the EV market.
- Hyundai Motor Group is involved in two Georgia EV-related projects with an expected combined investment of approximately $11 billion.
- One Hyundai Motor Group project involves an estimated $5.5 billion investment for an EV plant and EV battery manufacturing facility in Bryan County, Georgia.
- The second Hyundai Motor Group project is valued at another $5.5 billion.
- The second project is a joint venture between Hyundai and SK On.
- Hyundai and SK On are building an EV plant in Bartow County, Georgia.
- Hyundai Mobis currently operates a plant in West Point, Georgia.
- Hyundai Mobis supplies Kia and other automakers with cockpit modules, chassis modules, and bumper assemblies from its West Point location.
- Hyundai Mobis plans to spend $926 million on an EV power electric system plant in Richmond Hill, Georgia.
- The Richmond Hill plant will provide the Bryan County Hyundai plant with an anticipated 900,000 power electric systems annually.
- The Richmond Hill plant will also provide the Bryan County Hyundai plant with an anticipated 450,000 integrated charging control units annually.
- In May, Chicago-based Anovion Technologies announced an $800 million investment.
- Anovion Technologies plans to build a 1.5-million-square-foot synthetic graphite anode materials manufacturing plant in Decatur County, Georgia.
- The synthetic graphite produced at the Anovion facility will be used in the production of lithium-ion batteries.
