## Facts

- Georgia combines a diverse energy mix, world-class research, and a culture of collaboration to drive innovation across the state’s clean energy ecosystem.
- The state features top solar production.
- The state has expanded nuclear capacity at Plant Vogtle.
- The state utilizes waste-to-energy conversion technology.
- The state conducts advanced research in battery, hydrogen, and solar technologies.
- Georgia Tech ranks No. 1 for Energy among public universities and in the top 5 for materials science and engineering, according to U.S. News and World Report.
- Plant Vogtle is the largest generator of clean energy in the U.S.
- Unit 4 at Plant Vogtle has been online since 2024.
- Plant Vogtle is operated by Georgia Power.
- The University System of Georgia and the Technical College System offer specialized programs across the state.
- Georgia advances next-gen energy and transportation technology through public-private partnerships and cutting-edge research.
- Living laboratories like Curiosity Lab and The Ray test real-world solutions.
- Research institutions including Georgia Tech and industry partners drive breakthroughs in batteries, semiconductors, and solar.
- Energy storage remains a cornerstone of Georgia’s clean energy future to ensure renewable power is reliable, scalable, and available when needed.
- Hyundai’s Clean Logistics Project supports a fully integrated, carbon-conscious energy ecosystem.
- Zero-emission fuel production facilities support a fully integrated, carbon-conscious energy ecosystem.
- Twelve percent of the state's current energy mix comes from renewables.
- Thirty-four percent of the state's current energy mix comes from carbon-neutral sources, including Plant Vogtle and Plant Hatch.
- The state connects businesses to resources that turn post-consumer materials into valuable assets.
- Post-consumer waste plastics are converted into high-grade fuels in the state.
- Large-scale recycling of glass and aluminum occurs in the state.
- Next-generation vehicles are rolling off the line at Kia Georgia.
- Next-generation vehicles are rolling off the line at Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA).
- Georgia is emerging as a national leader in electric and hybrid vehicle manufacturing.
- Georgia offers a growing, end-to-end advanced mobility supply chain spanning the state, from vehicle assembly to advanced battery and materials production.
