# Qualifying Advanced Energy Project Credit (48C)

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-05-29

**Source:** [Applicant Self-Disclosed 48C Projects | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/cmei/manufacturing/applicant-self-disclosed-48c-projects)  ·  `page_003179`

## Facts

- The Qualifying Advanced Energy Project Credit (48C) was established by the American Recovery and Reinvestment Act of 2009 and expanded with a $10 billion investment under the Inflation Reduction Act of 2022.
- At least $4 billion of the total $10 billion will be allocated to projects in designated 48C energy communities, which includes communities with closed coal mines or coal plants.
- 48C provides an investment tax credit of up to 30% of qualified investments for certified qualifying advanced energy projects that meet prevailing wage and apprenticeship requirements.
- On May 29, 2024, the IRS released allocations for the first round of the 48C program, with approximately $1.5 billion of the $4 billion in tax credit allocations supporting projects in designated 48C energy communities.
- On January 10, 2025, the IRS released allocations for the second round of the 48C program, with roughly $2.5 billion of the $6 billion in tax credit allocations supporting projects in designated 48C energy communities.
- ArcelorMittal Calvert LLC received a $280,500,000 credit allocation to build an advanced manufacturing facility in Calvert, Alabama, to produce high-quality non-grain oriented electrical steel (NOES) essential for electric vehicle motors and other clean energy technologies.
- The ArcelorMittal Calvert project aims to fill a critical gap in the domestic supply chain, reduce dependency on imports, and support decarbonization of the automotive sector.
- Epsilon Advanced Materials Inc. received a $115,500,000 credit allocation to establish a 30,000 MTPA (Phase 1) manufacturing facility in Leland, North Carolina, to produce sustainable, high performance synthetic graphite anode material for lithium-ion batteries.
- e-VAC Magnetics LLC received a $111,990,000 credit allocation to advance its first U.S. manufacturing facility in Sumter, South Carolina, which is projected to create 300 new jobs with operations beginning in the fall of 2025.
- e-VAC Magnetics produces rare earth neodymium-iron-boron (NdFeB) magnets that are critical to automotive, defense, industrial, and renewable energy applications.
- LANXESS Corporation received a $1,124,095 credit allocation to expand its Charleston, South Carolina facility's phosphorus trichloride (PCl3) production capabilities to manufacture battery-grade PCl3, essential for producing a critical battery component in electric vehicle batteries and energy storage systems.
- The LANXESS expansion will enhance existing workforce roles and support local workforce development through partnerships prioritizing prevailing wages, apprenticeship programs, and community engagement.
- Moment Energy Inc. received a $6,000,000 credit allocation to build the first large-scale U.S. manufacturing facility in Vance, Alabama, to process Giga-Watt hours of electric vehicle (EV) batteries for stationary storage applications.
- The Moment Energy facility will repurpose retired EV batteries with 80% life remaining after their initial use in EVs, ensuring battery materials are kept in the U.S. supply chain before being recycled domestically.
- Moment Energy's initiative creates hundreds of well-paying jobs in the renewable industry and builds upon the success of its 15,000-square-foot concept facility certified by UL Solutions to UL 1974.
- Mobis North America electrified Powertrain, LLC received a $57,666,890 credit allocation to construct three different electric vehicle component part plants in Richmond Hill, Georgia, which will provide parts for the production of Hyundai and Kia electric vehicles.
- The awarded Mobis plant in Richmond Hill will specifically produce Power Electric Systems (PE System), integral to EV production, with a positive environmental and community impact to the United States economy.
- NOVONIX Anode Materials LLC received a $103,063,889 credit allocation to fully equip its recently purchased and retrofitted industrial facility in Chattanooga, Tennessee, to produce high-performance synthetic graphite anode material at a capacity of 20,000 tonnes per annum.
- The NOVONIX project marks the first commercially active large scale synthetic graphite facility dedicated to battery anode material in North America, significantly expanding U.S. production and reducing reliance on China for this key battery material.
- Eaton Corporation received three credit allocations ($16,278,300 for Nacogdoches, TX; $9,000,000 for El Paso, TX; and $1,326,060 for Waukesha, WI) to expand production capacity of voltage regulators, low-voltage switchgear, circuit breakers, panelboards, and three-phase transformers.
- Eaton's expanded equipment provides vital functionality to support electric grid resilience, renewable energy projects, and electric vehicle (EV) charging stations across the nation.
