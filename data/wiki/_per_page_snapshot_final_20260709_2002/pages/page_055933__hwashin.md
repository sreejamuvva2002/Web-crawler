# Hwashin

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-10-31

**Source:** [More than 450 jobs are coming to Laurens County as auto parts company opens facility - AOL](https://www.aol.com/more-450-jobs-coming-laurens-202759848.html)  ·  `page_055933`

## Facts

- Laurens County will see nearly 500 new jobs come to the area after an international automotive parts company opens their new manufacturing facility.
- Hwashin plans to build a new manufacturing facility in Dublin, which will create around 460 jobs and bring more than $176 million investment to Laurens County.
- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp stated that the goal to become the e-mobility capital of the nation is bringing opportunity to communities all across the state.
- Gov. Brian Kemp noted that Hwashin is the latest to join Georgia's growing ecosystem of companies bringing good jobs to hardworking Georgians in this industry.
- The new facility will be located at the Georgia Highway 257 Rail Served Site.
- The facility is scheduled to start production in late 2025.
- The site is the first Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) Select site in the state, which took years of planning and months of work.
- Fred Williams, Chair of the Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority, stated that Hwashin becomes the seventh international company to call Dublin and Laurens County home.
- The new facility will mainly focus on manufacturing chassis components for original equipment manufacturers.
- Original equipment manufacturer customers include Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America and Kia Georgia.
- Hwashin is based in South Korea and is a Tier-1 supplier that specializes in producing chassis and body parts for automobile makers.
- Hwashin has manufacturing facilities in the U.S., Asia, and South America.
- Dublin Mayor Joshua Kight stated that the staff and board of the Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority did an excellent job showing Hwashin why Dublin-Laurens County is a great place to do business.
- Dublin Mayor Joshua Kight noted excitement about beginning work with their new Korean partners.
- Dublin Mayor Joshua Kight stated that the company's manufacturing facility in Dublin will become an economic engine for the region, leading to more employment opportunities and quality growth for the town.
