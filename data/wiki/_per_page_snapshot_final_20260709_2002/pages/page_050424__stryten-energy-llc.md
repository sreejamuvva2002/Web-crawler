# Stryten Energy LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- Stryten Energy is an innovative energy storage solutions provider serving the transportation, motive and essential power industries.
- The company manufactures high-quality advanced lead, lithium, and vanadium batteries and intelligent chargers.
- Stryten offers cloud-based software for smart fleet design decisions.
- It is the only energy storage solutions provider in North America who can offer advanced lead, lithium and vanadium solutions to solve complex energy storage challenges.
- The company maintains a manufacturing, service and distribution network throughout North America.
- Stryten Energy has a growing team of over 2,500 employees.
- Sister-company Motrex LLC provides personnel for professional services roles including information technology, finance and accounting, logistics, EH&S, project management, quality and human resources.
- The company maintains locations in Alpharetta, Georgia.
- Additional Stryten Energy locations include Queretaro, Mexico; Mississauga, Ontario; Bridgeville, Pennsylvania; Charlotte, North Carolina; Ruckersville, Virginia; Columbus, Indiana; Fort Smith, Arkansas; Kansas City, Kansas; Kansas City, Missouri; Lakeland, Florida; Lampeter, Pennsylvania; Littleton, Colorado; Lombard, Illinois; Manchester, Iowa; Minneapolis, Minnesota; Milwaukee, Wisconsin; Niagara Falls, New York; Attleboro, Massachusetts; Salina, Kansas; Edmonton, Alberta; Moncton, New Brunswick; Ottawa, Ontario; and Corona, California.
- In September 2023, Stryten Energy was awarded a five-year contract to provide backup battery systems for U.S. Navy Submarines, supplying high-quality, top-performing battery cells and components.
- Stryten Energy entered the long-duration energy storage market by purchasing the assets of Storion Energy Inc., a technology innovator of advanced vanadium redox flow battery technology.
- Vanadium redox flow batteries are emerging as a solution for long-duration power applications and are a preferred option for large-scale energy storage.
- The company's leadership team includes Tim Vargo, Mike Judd, Petar Oklobdzija, Dan Autey, Scott Childers, Lacey Chitwood, Melissa Floyd, Jeremy Furr, Matt Gould, Wendy Henderson, Bob Holmes, Rodger Meyer, Bill Nonnamaker, Bob Penman, Alvin Shao, Jully Sieglaff, and Brian Woodworth.
