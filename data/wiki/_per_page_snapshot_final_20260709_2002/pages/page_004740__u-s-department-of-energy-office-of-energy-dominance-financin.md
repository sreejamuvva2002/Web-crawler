# U.S. Department of Energy Office of Energy Dominance Financing

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [Letter from Leadership: EDF 2025 Year-in-Review and Looking Forward to 2026 | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/edf/articles/letter-leadership-edf-2025-year-review-and-looking-forward-2026)  ·  `page_004740`

## Facts

- Gregory A. Beard is the director of the U.S. DOE Office of Energy Dominance Financing (EDF), appointed in January 2026.
- Director Beard was previously appointed by the President in April 2025 to serve as a Senior Advisor to EDF.
- Director Beard has extensive experience in natural resources investment, private equity, and capital markets, having sourced and led more than 25 energy investments over more than 30 years.
- Before government service, Director Beard was a senior partner on the management committee at Apollo Global Management, leading natural resources investing for 10 years.
- Director Beard held a senior energy investment role at Riverstone Holdings LLC.
- Director Beard co-founded, chaired, and served as CEO of Stronghold Digital Mining, Inc., launching the first company to integrate waste-to-power generation within co-located data centers to mine Bitcoin.
- Director Beard founded and served as Chairman and CEO of Beard Energy Acquisition Corp., raising a $200 million SPAC and evaluating more than 200 potential transactions in the energy transition space.
- President Trump and Secretary Wright have made commitments to unleash American energy dominance, with EDF on the front lines of delivering affordable, reliable, secure energy.
- Upon taking office in January 2025, DOE senior leadership worked to reverse Biden-era energy policies that led to energy price inflation and grid destabilization.
- Under the Biden Administration, electricity prices increased by 30%, which was 13 times faster than the previous seven years.
- More than 100 gigawatts (GW) of firm reliable power were projected to retire by 2030 under the Biden Administration.
- President Trump took decisive action to redirect DOE's mission through energy-related Executive Orders, including Unleashing American Energy, Reinvigorating the Nuclear Industrial Base, and Strengthening the Reliability and Security of the United States Electric Grid.
- DOE has recentered around data-driven, common-sense policies that return the country to energy abundance and prioritize baseload power.
- Between 2021 and January 2025, the Biden Administration committed over $104 billion in EDF financing.
- Of the Biden-era commitments, $85 billion was closed or committed between Election Day 2024 and Inauguration Day 2025.
- DOE and EDF leadership are restructuring, substantially revising, or eliminating over $83.6 billion in loans and conditional commitments, representing over 80% of the Biden-era loan portfolio.
- EDF has completed or is in the process of de-obligating over $29.9 billion (approximately 29%) of the $104 billion in Biden-era principal loan obligations.
- EDF has completed or is in the process of revising another $53.6 billion (approximately 51%) of the total Biden-era principal loan obligations.
- EDF has eliminated approximately $9.5 billion in government-subsidized, intermittent wind and solar projects.
- EDF is replacing eliminated wind and solar projects with natural gas and nuclear uprates where possible to provide more affordable and reliable energy.
- EDF currently has more than $289 billion in available loan authority, including in the Energy Dominance Financing Program with expanded eligibility criteria thanks to President Trump’s Working Families Tax Cut.
- This funding makes EDF the largest energy lender in the world.
- EDF is prioritizing six foundational sectors: Nuclear, Coal/oil/gas/hydrocarbons, Critical materials and minerals, Geothermal, Grid and transmission, and Manufacturing and transportation.
- EDF financing for manufacturing and transportation is serving as an investment accelerator for U.S. manufacturing and transportation, supporting job creation and supply chains.
- In the final months of 2025, EDF closed three new loans totaling $4.1 billion.
- One of the new loans was to Constellation for the Crane Restart project (formerly known as Three Mile Island) to reinvigorate the nuclear industry.
- Another loan was to a subsidiary of AEP to improve America’s electric grid through reconductoring and rebuilding transmission lines.
- The third loan was to Wabash Valley Resources to utilize a coal plant to produce fertilizer in America’s Corn Belt.
- In 2026, EDF will prioritize financing energy and manufacturing projects that meaningfully contribute to U.S. energy security, grid reliability, and lowering costs for all Americans.
- EDF is partnering with the private sector to strengthen America’s energy foundation and support U.S. leadership in emerging AI technologies.
