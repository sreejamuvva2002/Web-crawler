# TEKLAS

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2020-11

**Source:** [Electric Vehicle Manufacturer TEKLAS to Open North American Headquarters in Georgia, Create 120 Jobs in Calhoun - Albany CEO](https://albanyceo.com/news/2020/11/electric-vehicle-manufacturer-teklas-open-north-american-headquarters-georgia-create-120-jobs-calhoun/)  ·  `page_051290`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that TEKLAS will invest $6.5 million to open its first North American facility and headquarters in Gordon County.
- The project will deliver 120 jobs to Calhoun.
- TEKLAS is a Turkish advanced research and development, manufacturing company and supplier of electric vehicle (EV) parts.
- Founded in Turkey in 1971, the company designs, develops, and manufactures fluid circulation systems for cooling and heating applications, including hoses and pipes from rubber, plastic, and metal for the global automotive industry.
- TEKLAS utilizes highly innovative and automated manufacturing processes using advanced water molding technology.
- The new TEKLAS USA facility will include assembly lines to serve prominent original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) in the industry.
- Operations at the Calhoun location are scheduled to begin in the spring of 2021.
- TEKLAS is headquartered in Turkey and maintains locations in Bulgaria, Serbia, Mexico, and China, along with sales offices in Germany and France.
- Regional Director Arcan Ergur stated that the new Calhoun plant will help the company be closer to its main customers.
- The facility and headquarters will occupy a 200,000-square-foot building at 320 South Industrial Boulevard in Calhoun, Georgia.
- TEKLAS will hire for positions across a variety of skill levels, including advanced manufacturing, management, and supervision.
- Larry Roye, chairman of the Development Authority of Gordon County, applauded state and local partners for helping encourage the company's decision to locate in Calhoun.
- Gordon County Chamber of Commerce President and CEO Kathy Johnson commended TEKLAS for its research and advancements in electric vehicle manufacturing and its contribution to expanding the automotive sector of the local manufacturing base.
- GDEcD Project Manager Fernanda Kirchner represented the Global Commerce division on the competitive project.
- The project was developed in partnership with the Economic Development Authority of Gordon County and Georgia Power.
- The Center of Innovation for Logistics, a division of GDEcD, provided freight analysis to support the project and company planning.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that TEKLAS's establishment of its North American headquarters and first U.S. advanced manufacturing facility in Calhoun supports Georgia's focus on attracting and growing future jobs amid the automotive industry's transition to electric vehicles and e-mobility.
