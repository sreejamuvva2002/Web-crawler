# Wabash National Corp.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Wabash Aligns With Terion, Plans For Smarter Trailers | Heavy Duty Trucking](https://www.truckinginfo.com/88516/wabash-aligns-with-terion-plans-for-smarter-trailers)  ·  `page_014550`

## Facts

- Wabash National Corp. announced an alliance with trailer tracking system provider Terion.
- The alliance is expected to play a key role in the development of smarter trailers.
- Wabash National will factory install Terion’s FleetView Trailer Monitoring System.
- Wabash will provide sales, installation and service through its nationwide distribution network.
- The companies agreed to co-develop and market advanced trailer management systems that improve trailer utilization and fleet productivity by integrating FleetView with Wabash’s Smart Trailer technology.
- Wabash chairman Jerry Ehrlich said the company is expected to begin production of its MBS2 Smart Trailer system next month.
- The MBS2 system will have added "brainpower" to handle a number of other trailer sensing and recording technologies.
- The future system co-developed with Terion will be branded as RealView.
- RealView will store an electronic trailer ID number.
- RealView will track trailer mileage.
- RealView will sense wheel rotation direction to trigger backup lights and alarms when the trailer is backing.
- RealView will sense distance to a dock as the trailer is being backed.
- RealView will automatically lift steerable axles when the trailer is being backed up.
- RealView will monitor heat build-up at wheel spindles and warn of imminent bearing failure and/or wheel loss.
- RealView will monitor brake stroke and warn when it is out of adjustment.
- RealView will monitor brake lining wear.
- Terion's system will allow fleets to monitor critical trailer systems remotely.
- A fleet could potentially manage its trailers entirely from a computer.
- Fleets could find the trailer, determine if it's dispatchable (is it empty, are the brakes OK?), handle maintenance on demand, and prevent catastrophic failures such as wheel loss.
- Terion and Wabash will offer the RealView product and services as an OEM installation, as well as on a retrofit basis.
- The two companies first announced their intent to form an alliance in March.
