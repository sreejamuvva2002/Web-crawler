# Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD)

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2023-01-19

**Source:** [Georgia Economic Development Update - Georgia Independent College Association](https://georgiacolleges.org/news/628722/Georgia-Economic-Development-Update.htm)  ·  `page_019323`

## Facts

- GDEcD’s Global Commerce division supported 218 locations or expansions during the first half of Fiscal Year 2023 (July 1 through December 31, 2022).
- These 218 projects are expected to create approximately 17,500 new jobs and more than $13 billion in investment for the state.
- Both the number of supported locations and total investment amount increased over the same period last year.
- Areas outside the 10-county metro Atlanta region accounted for 85 percent of jobs created and 92 percent of investments from these economic development projects.
- Four of Georgia’s largest industries – advanced manufacturing, automotive, aerospace, and food processing – accounted for nearly 75 percent of new jobs and 84 percent of investments.
- Planned investments resulting from these projects increased by more than 38 percent compared to the same time last year.
- The automotive industry was the second-largest source of new jobs and investment, after advanced manufacturing.
- In October, state and local officials joined company representatives for the groundbreaking of the new Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) EV facility.
- Korean Ambassador to the U.S. Tae-yong Cho attended the HMGMA groundbreaking event.
- The HMGMA EV facility will support more than 8,100 direct jobs in Bryan, Bulloch, Chatham and Effingham Counties.
- Three off-site HMGMA suppliers announced locations following the HMGMA groundbreaking.
- These three supplier additions swiftly surpassed the initial $1 billion in investment predicted to come from them.
- International companies accounted for 23 percent of state projects since July 1, 2022.
- International companies are valued partners in creating a closed-loop electric mobility supply chain in Georgia.
- The partnership between SK On and Hyundai Motor Group in Bartow County is among the top five largest projects by investment in the state’s history.
- Next-generation developer FREYR Battery announced a Coweta County facility, which is also among the top five largest projects by investment in the state’s history.
- Battery recyclers Sung-Il Hitech and Denkai America joined Georgia’s growing electric mobility ecosystem.
- GDEcD Deputy Commissioner for Global Commerce Kristi Brigman highlighted the Georgia Quick Start program as a key resource for companies to build talent and workforce from the ground up.
- The state utilizes GRAD certified sites to support accelerated project timelines.
- Georgia’s economic development strategy combines resources and talent of local, state, and international partners to drive innovation and create meaningful economic impact.
