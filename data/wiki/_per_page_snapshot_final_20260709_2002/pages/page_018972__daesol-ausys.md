# Daesol Ausys

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-08-30

**Source:** [South Korean firm to construct $72M EV parts factory in Georgia | Fox News](https://www.foxnews.com/us/south-korean-firm-construct-72m-ev-parts-factory-georgia)  ·  `page_018972`

## Facts

- A South Korean company plans to build a $72 million factory in Georgia to make parts for electric vehicles, hiring more than 140 workers.
- Daesol Ausys announced its plans to build a factory in West Point, Georgia, to manufacture luggage boards and covers for compartments in electric vehicles.
- The company supplies parts to Hyundai, Kia, and General Motors.
- This facility will be the third factory in the same West Point industrial park for members of the company's corporate family.
- Daehan Solution Co. opened a million factory there in 2008 to make headliners and other sound insulation parts for the nearby Kia plant.
- Daesol Material opened a $35 million factory there in 2017 to make automotive fiber padding.
- Daehan Solution currently employs 350 full-time workers, while Daesol Material employs 50 workers.
- Daehan Solution also operates a plant near Montgomery, Alabama, that supplies the Hyundai assembly plant there.
- The project follows a wave of electric vehicle suppliers announcing plants in Georgia after Hyundai said in 2022 that it would build a $5.5 billion plant to assemble electric vehicles and batteries in Ellabell, Georgia, near Savannah.
- The Hyundai Ellabell site could grow to 8,100 employees and is slated to begin producing vehicles in 2025.
- EV suppliers have pledged to invest nearly $2.2 billion and hire 5,000 people in Georgia.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp stated that the state’s automotive industry employs over 50,000 hardworking Georgians and will continue to grow as e-mobility suppliers choose all corners of the state for the jobs of the future.
- Daesol Ausys is scheduled to begin production in December 2024.
- The company will pay workers $15 to $20 an hour.
- The state of Georgia will pay to train the factory's workers.
- The state provided $500,000 last year to help Harris County clear and grade the site and build a road, with matching funds coming from county sales tax collections.
- The city of West Point and Harris County are granting eight years of property tax breaks worth a projected $5.7 million.
- Daesol Ausys could qualify for $875,000 in state income tax credits, at $1,250 per job over five years, as long as workers make at least $31,300 a year.
