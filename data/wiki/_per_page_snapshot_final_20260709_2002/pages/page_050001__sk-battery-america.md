## Facts

- On February 14, SK Battery America announced it was laying off 958 workers at its plant in Commerce, Georgia, representing 37% of the facility's total workforce.
- The Commerce, Georgia plant manufactured lithium-ion battery cells for the Ford F-150 Lightning.
- Ford cancelled the electric F-150 program after Congress eliminated the $7,500 EV tax credit in a reconciliation bill last fall.
- The SK Battery plant is located in Commerce, Jackson County, Georgia, a rural community of about 72,000 people, and was the largest private employer in the county.
- Laid-off workers were drawing $22-$28/hour wages, a significant step up from previous employment.
- Many laid-off workers had bought homes or cars in anticipation of stable long-term employment.
- The layoff was announced with only 60 days notice, leaving little time to adjust.
- Georgia's state gas tax is $0.29/gallon, which is below the national average.
- Georgia has pipeline access through the Colonial Pipeline, which runs from Houston to New Jersey and passes directly through the state.
- Georgia's current average gas price is $3.41/gallon, up $0.23 since February 28.
- At current crude price trends, Georgia's average gas price is projected to cross $3.50 within the next 7-10 days.
- Workers in Jackson County commute approximately 25 miles each way on rural roads with no public transit alternative, making $3.50 gas a direct cost burden.
- The Democratic incumbent in Georgia's Senate race had a narrow path dependent on running up margins in Atlanta's suburbs while keeping losses in rural areas manageable.
- In suburban areas, educated voters tend to care about climate policy and supported the EV tax credit, but the political framing that Democrats killed good Georgia jobs by protecting EV credits is factually backwards since the credits were eliminated by Republican-aligned legislation.
- The combination of EV job losses and high gas prices creates a narrative for opposing campaigns that Washington made a bet on electric vehicles, killed affordable gas cars, and now residents cannot afford to fill up their current vehicles.
- Georgia exemplifies a broader pattern across competitive Senate races where rural economies are heavily impacted by energy costs compared to insulated suburban or biotech hubs.
- In North Carolina, the Research Triangle's biotech economy is insulated from energy costs in ways that rural western North Carolina is not.
- Maine has the highest percentage of homes heated by oil of any state in the country, making its energy cost story particularly acute.
- Suburban Phoenix voters in Arizona are among the most car-dependent in the country and have swung competitive for Democrats in recent cycles.
- The historical threshold above which the incumbent party's losses in midterm elections become severe is a $3.75/gallon national average gas price.
- At the time of writing, the national average gas price was $3.31, leaving it 44 cents away from the $3.75 threshold.
- If a Hormuz disruption lasts five weeks and Goldman's $100/barrel scenario plays out, national gas prices could cross $3.75 before Memorial Day, giving the energy price story five months of impact before the election.
- If a ceasefire materializes within two weeks and prices retreat to $3.00-$3.10 by summer, the energy price story would fade and elections would revert to previous dynamics.
- Georgia is identified as the state where job destruction from the energy transition and price pain from energy disruption are most visibly converging.
