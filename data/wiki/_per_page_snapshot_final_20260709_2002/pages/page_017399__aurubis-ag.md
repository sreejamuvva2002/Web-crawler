# Aurubis AG

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2023-03-28

**Source:** [Aurubis and SMS group forge ahead with the first multimetal recycling plant in the US](https://www.aurubis.com/en/media/press-releases/press-releases-2023/aurubis-and-sms-group-forge-ahead-with-the-first-multimetal-recycling-plant-in-the-us)  ·  `page_017399`

## Facts

- Aurubis and SMS group signed a supply contract for a second plant module at the new Aurubis Richmond recycling plant in Georgia as part of a strategic partnership.
- The additional equipment increases the capacity of the metal recycling plant in Richmond from 90,000 tons to 180,000 tons per year of complex metal scrap materials.
- Aurubis CEO Roland Harings stated the expansion delivers on the company’s “Driving Sustainable Growth” strategy while keeping strategically important mineral raw materials in the resource cycle.
- The SMS group recycling equipment uses top-blown rotary converter (TBRC) technology to recover copper, nickel, tin, precious metals, and platinum group metals from complex recycling materials.
- Copper, nickel, tin, precious metals, and platinum group metals recovered at the plant are crucial raw materials for future technologies including the energy transition, sustainable mobility, and digitalization.
- Michael Rzepczyk, a member of the SMS Executive Board, stated that doubling the production volume at the new US site takes recycling to the next level in the US.
- SMS Executive Board member Michael Rzepczyk stated that the partnership aims to build a more sustainable future by closing value chains and maximizing the recovery of valuable metals from recycling materials.
- The plant's modular design allows additional components to be added later to flexibly expand capacity to meet future market requirements.
- Aurubis is investing approximately €640 million for both phases of the project to build the first secondary smelter specializing in multimetal recycling in the US.
- Groundbreaking for the Aurubis Richmond plant occurred in mid-2022, with construction running at full steam as of March 2023.
- Stage one of the plant is scheduled to go online in the first half of 2024.
- The second stage, equipped with the TBRC, is anticipated to start production in 2026.
- Aurubis processes roughly 1 million tons of recycling material annually globally, and the Richmond plant will add another 180,000 tons to that volume.
- The new Georgia facility will comply with the highest environmental standards in the US and create around 200 new jobs.
- Aurubis CEO Roland Harings stated that economic growth and comprehensive corporate responsibility go hand in hand for the company.
