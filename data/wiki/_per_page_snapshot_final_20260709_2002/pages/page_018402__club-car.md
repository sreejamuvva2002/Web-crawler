# Club Car

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-04-25

**Source:** [New Manufacturing Facility Unveiled for Club Car](https://www.platinumequity.com/news/facility-designed-to-manufacture-electric-street-legal-club-car-urban-unveiled-in-georgia/)  ·  `page_018402`

## Facts

- Club Car officially opened a new manufacturing facility in Appling, Ga., with a ribbon-cutting ceremony attended by elected officials and area business leaders.
- Club Car CEO Mark Wagner and Georgia Governor Brian Kemp cut the ceremonial ribbon at the facility opening.
- The new facility contains 105,000 square feet of flexible production and warehouse space.
- The facility was opened to produce the fully electric, street-legal Club Car Urban for the United States market.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp stated that Club Car is bringing jobs into Georgia in the growing e-mobility sector and leading the way in compact utility EV manufacturing.
- The production line can produce up to 20 vehicles per day.
- There is currently one production line with 13 stations and room to add two more lines if needed.
- The facility was opened to address expected increased demand in the U.S. for the Club Car Urban.
- Club Car has committed nearly $8 million in capital investments into the facility over two years.
- CEO Mark Wagner stated that their innovative vehicles are impacting commercial fleet electrification, mini-mobility, and last-mile delivery.
- Club Car has been a Georgia-based company for more than sixty years.
- The Club Car Urban is an electric, street-legal vehicle designed to navigate large campuses, last-mile delivery routes, municipalities, and hospitality spaces.
- Prior to this facility's opening, customers had to order the Club Car Urban and wait for it to be built and shipped overseas from Poland.
- Club Car was acquired by Platinum Equity from Ingersoll Rand in 2021.
