# SRG Global Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2011-02-22

**Source:** [SRG to open new Mexican molding site](https://www.plasticstoday.com/srg-open-new-mexican-molding-site)  ·  `page_060071`

## Facts

- SRG Global Inc. has broken ground on an injection molding, top coat, chrome-plating, and assembly plant in Irapuato, Mexico.
- The initial phase of the Mexico project is estimated to cost between $25 and $35 million.
- The facility is expected to be completed by the first quarter of 2012.
- The final size and machine count for the new site are to be determined, with a build-out period of one year.
- SRG expects to install injection molding machines ranging from 250 to 3000 tons at the facility, subject to change.
- Tom Schneider, SRG's director of marketing, stated that SRG currently supplies many OEMs in Mexico.
- SRG ships products to Mexico from its existing sites located in Kentucky, Indiana, Georgia, Tennessee, and Missouri.
- The Irapuato site will serve a variety of automotive and non-automotive OEMs and customers.
- Initial staffing plans for the Mexico facility call for 220 direct employees, with potential for an additional 220 indirect employees.
- Construction of the Mexico facility is part of SRG Global's business strategy to maintain local and regional production capabilities.
- SRG Global's other recent investments include its Advanced Development Center in Taylor, MI (opened 2010) and a production facility in Boleslawiec, Poland (opened 2009).
- In the automotive sector, SRG supplies front and rear end components including grilles, emblems/nameplates, liftgates, deck lids, and spoilers.
- SRG supplies body side parts such as rocker panels, body side moldings, fuel filler caps, wheel flares, door handles, and wheel covers/caps.
- SRG supplies interior components including pillars, window surround moldings, cowls, door belt moldings, door panel parts, center consoles, instrument panels, and steering wheels.
- The current company was formed by Guardian's acquisition of Siegel-Robert Automotive in 2008.
- SRG Global was created in 2009 by combining the trim operations of both companies, with headquarters in Warren, MI.
- Kevin Baird serves as president and CEO of SRG Global.
- Juan Manuel Oliva is the governor of Guanajuato.
