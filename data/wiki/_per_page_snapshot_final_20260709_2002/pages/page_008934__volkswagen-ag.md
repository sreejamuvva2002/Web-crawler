# Volkswagen AG

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-04-23

**Source:** [VW hones in on manufacturing EV 'battery of the future' in Canada](https://ca.finance.yahoo.com/news/vw-hones-manufacturing-battery-future-155459253.html)  ·  `page_008934`

## Facts

- Volkswagen Group Canada Inc. plans to eventually manufacture solid-state batteries at its planned gigafactory in St. Thomas, Ont., says chief executive Vito Paladino.
- Volkswagen AG owns many brands including Audi, where Paladino is also CEO for Canada.
- VW has long signaled interest in solid-state battery technology, but lithium-ion and lithium phosphate batteries are more prevalent today.
- Many, if not all, of VW's EVs currently use lithium-ion batteries, and one industry source said the company will produce those batteries in the short term.
- Paladino stated: "As far as the technology, it is evolving fast. It is incredible."
- Paladino noted it is tough to talk specifics but reported that the solid-state battery will allow them to provide electrification in a more affordable way.
- Paladino made comments regarding consumer sentiment around EVs, noting climate change makes it necessary to move away from internal combustion engine vehicles.
- Paladino said affordability is still an issue for many people when it comes to EVs.
- Paladino stated: "The battery plant that we have in Canada will be solid-state batteries."
- Paladino added these batteries will have a cost benefit, but also way more range and performance.
- Powerco Canada Inc., VW's battery manufacturing subsidiary, said in December that the site was ready for groundbreaking.
- Construction of the $7-billion St. Thomas gigafactory would begin this year with a goal of producing batteries by 2027.
- A spokesperson for Powerco did not return calls seeking comment by the time of publication.
- In December, Powerco described the solid-state cell as "the battery of the future," which would cut charging time, extend driving range and maintain safety standards.
- Whereas lithium-ion batteries use liquid electrolytes, solid-state batteries use solid electrolytes to carry charges, which may improve charging times and make them less prone to catching fire.
- VW has invested hundreds of millions of dollars into California-based Quantumscape Corp., a tech startup working on solid-state batteries.
- In January, Reuters reported that VW was in talks with Blue Solutions SAS, a solid-state battery company with operations in Boucherville, Que., that is now owned by the French conglomerate Bolloré SE.
- A spokesperson for Blue Solutions was not immediately available for comment.
- Regardless of whether solid-state batteries are currently ready for mass commercial production, VW's planned battery plant in St. Thomas remains a year away from initial production.
- VW made headlines in Canada when it announced the plant in 2023.
- The announcement marked the first time in years that Canada attracted a new automaker to build out its supply chain here.
- The past few decades have been characterized by automakers closing operations in Canada and moving to cheaper jurisdictions, such as the U.S. south and Mexico.
- The town of St. Thomas has historically been an auto industry town.
- VW's decision to build a battery factory approximately the size of 210 football fields marks a revival of sorts for the area.
- It would be the largest EV supply chain investment in Canada, although there are reports that Honda Motor Co. Ltd. may soon announce plans to manufacture battery cells and EVs on an even larger scale.
- Such projects have been beset by controversy over whether government incentives that entice automakers to build here end up cancelling out any economic benefits from the jobs and tax base created by the operations.
- The Parliamentary Budget Office described the economic impact of VW's proposed plant as "marginal," though that report has also been critiqued by industry advocates as too simplistic.
- In 2023, sales of passenger EVs in Canada declined by 12.6 per cent from a year earlier.
- Sales of commercial EVs, a larger category that includes SUVs, minivans and other vehicles, increased by 85 per cent.
- Overall, total sales increased 49 per cent to 185,300 vehicles sold in 2023 from about 123,500 vehicles sold in 2022.
- Paladino said Canada's EV mandate may be creating pushback from consumers and there is still more work to be done to make EVs more affordable, including creating more charging stations, especially in condominiums.
- Paladino said EVs offer "a more polished driving experience" and better performance than their internal combustion engine counterparts.
- Paladino stated: "We're living it every day and we see the path forward," adding it is more about ensuring consumers come along excited rather than being forced, which is why they are growing the portfolio.
- Paladino said Audi Canada Inc. is set to introduce a slate of new EVs in every segment during the next six to eight months.
- Audi will also continue to produce internal combustion engine vehicles because some customers are not all the way there yet.
- Research is underway to redesign vehicles so that the interior is more like "a moving living room," aiming for extreme comfort for resting, working, and commuting.
- A clarification noted that Vito Paladino originally said the plant would make solid-state batteries, but a spokesperson later clarified he meant to say the plant would eventually make solid-state batteries.
