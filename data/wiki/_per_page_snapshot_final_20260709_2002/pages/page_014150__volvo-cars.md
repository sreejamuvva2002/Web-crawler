# Volvo Cars

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-23

**Source:** [Volvo Cars to produce new US model amid tariff pressure](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/volvo-cars-produce-us-model-100709630.html?fr=sycsrp_catchall)  ·  `page_014150`

## Facts

- Volvo Cars announced plans to begin producing a new hybrid model in the United States by the end of this decade.
- The new hybrid model will be designed specifically for the U.S. market to help boost capacity utilization at Volvo's South Carolina plant.
- Volvo Cars CEO Hakan Samuelsson stated that it will take time until America is fully electric.
- Volvo's CEO described the new larger hybrid model as primarily an electric car with a backup combustion engine that kicks in when the battery is flat.
- Automakers worldwide are adjusting production strategies to contend with President Trump's duties on foreign cars, part of a wider policy to boost U.S. manufacturing.
- Volvo previously announced in April that it would produce more vehicles in the U.S.
- In July, Volvo stated plans to start producing its XC60 mid-size SUV at the South Carolina plant from late 2026.
- The South Carolina factory currently builds only Volvo's fully electric EX90 SUV and the Polestar 3.
- Production data shows these current models take up only a small fraction of the site's annual capacity of 150,000 vehicles.
- The announcement coincided with Volvo's 70th anniversary in the United States.
- Volvo previously aimed to phase out all non-electric models by 2030 but reversed course last year to keep hybrids in its lineup.
- Luis Rezende, president of Volvo Cars' Americas operation, stated that adding another model to production will take a big step toward realizing the full potential of local manufacturing investments and workforce.
- A trade deal struck in July with the European Union cut U.S. tariffs on European cars to 15%.
- The EU trade agreement has yet to take effect, leaving vehicles subject to a 27.5% levy.
- CEO Hakan Samuelsson said the 10% duties eliminated on auto imports from the U.S. into Europe will make selling its U.S.-produced EX90 vehicle abroad more attractive.
- Samuelsson noted that with the 10% tariff reduction, Volvo has the possibility to grow its operations in Europe.
