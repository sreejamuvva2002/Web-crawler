# Ascend Elements

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2022-09-21

**Source:** [SSOE Group Providing Architecture and Engineering Services for Ascend Elements’ Georgia-Based Brownfield Lithium-Ion Battery Recycling Facility - SSOE Group](https://www.ssoe.com/press-releases/ssoe-group-providing-architecture-and-engineering-services-for-ascend-elements-georgia-based-brownfield-lithium-ion-battery-recycling-facility/)  ·  `page_001462`

## Facts

- The Covington facility will become North America’s largest battery recycling operation.
- Ascend Elements is investing $43 million into its new Covington, Georgia facility.
- The facility will be a commercial-scale, lithium-ion battery recycling operation spanning 154,000 SF.
- Once complete, the facility will have the capacity to process 30,000 metric tons of used lithium-ion batteries and scrap per year.
- The recycling process will minimize the need for mining while returning valuable, battery-grade lithium, nickel, and cobalt back into the battery supply chain.
- The Covington site is strategically located near several electric vehicle (EV) manufacturing hubs and lithium-ion battery producers.
- Ascend Elements aims to be fully operational later this year.
- SSOE Group is providing architecture, engineering, procurement, construction, and commissioning services for the project.
- Dan Russell, Chief Operating Officer of Ascend Elements, stated that SSOE's early engagement for technical evaluation and expertise in battery and automotive markets helped facilitate a seamless transition into facility design.
- Over the past six years, SSOE has been engaged in more than 20 battery / EV manufacturing projects across 18 facilities in the U.S. and Mexico.
- Rick Fox, PMP, serves as the Project Manager for SSOE on the Ascend project.
- Rick Fox noted that despite growth, the EV market is still in its infancy globally.
- SSOE has gained experience in all phases of the battery lifecycle, from materials production to cell production, through recycling.
- The project involves tight schedules, requiring diligence in working with code officials and environmental permitting agencies to ensure safe delivery.
- SSOE is developing alternative execution and procurement strategies to quickly meet Ascend Elements' business objectives.
- Scott Hazen, PE, PMP, serves as the Project Director for the project.
- The project team has remained agile to adjust plans for issues such as delays in materials and equipment due to global supply chain issues.
- SSOE is assisting Ascend Elements with preliminary engineering for future expansions, including greenfield work in Southwestern Kentucky.
- Ascend Elements is based in Westborough, Massachusetts, and operates as a vertically integrated battery recycling and engineered materials company.
- The company utilizes its patented Hydro-to-Cathode™ technology to directly synthesize new cathode active materials from spent lithium-ion cells.
- This technology results in reduced cost, improved performance, lowered GHG emissions, better performance batteries with fewer discarded in landfill, a cleaner manufacturing process, and a sustainable closed-loop circular battery economy.
