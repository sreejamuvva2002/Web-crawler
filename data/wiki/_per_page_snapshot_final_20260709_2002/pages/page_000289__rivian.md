# Rivian

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Rivian Breaks Ground on $5 Billion Manufacturing Plant in Georgia | ASSEMBLY](https://www.assemblymag.com/articles/99555-rivian-breaks-ground-on-5-billion-manufacturing-plant-in-georgia)  ·  `page_000289`

## Facts

- Rivian will hold a groundbreaking ceremony for its $5 billion manufacturing facility in Georgia.
- Company executives, Governor Brian Kemp, state and local officials, and community leaders will attend the ceremony at the 2,000-acre site in southern Morgan and Walton counties.
- The facility was first announced in late 2021.
- The project represents the single-largest economic development investment in Georgia’s history.
- The plant is expected to create 7,500 jobs once fully operational.
- The project was originally slated to open in 2024 but will now be developed in two phases.
- Vehicles are expected to begin rolling off assembly lines in 2028.
- Once complete, the plant will be capable of producing up to 400,000 vehicles annually.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Georgia will be home to Rivian's largest manufacturing facility, calling it the largest investment in state history and a key factor establishing the Peach State's leading role in the automotive industry for generations.
- Rivian leaders describe the Georgia plant as a critical step in broadening the company’s consumer base by introducing EVs at lower price points.
- The project underscores Georgia’s rise as a national leader in EV and battery sector growth, fueled by Governor Kemp’s focus on innovation and electric mobility.
