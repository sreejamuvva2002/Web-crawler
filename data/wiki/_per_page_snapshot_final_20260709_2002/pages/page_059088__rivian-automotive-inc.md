# Rivian Automotive Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-07

**Source:** [A Joint Venture with Volkswagen Provides Rivian with a $5 Billion Lifeline - California Times](https://www.californiatimes.us/a-joint-venture-with-volkswagen-provides-rivian-with-a-5-billion-lifeline/)  ·  `page_059088`

## Facts

- Rivian Automotive Inc. will receive a cash infusion through a new cooperation with Volkswagen AG amid a stalling electric-vehicle industry.
- Plans for a joint venture between Rivian and Volkswagen AG were unveiled.
- Volkswagen will contribute an initial $1 billion to Rivian.
- Volkswagen may contribute up to $4 billion more in the future.
- As compensation, Volkswagen will receive access to Rivian's technology for use in its own electric vehicles.
- Volkswagen will work with a partner to create software and "next-generation" battery-powered cars.
- After the news was announced on Tuesday, Rivian's shares surged more than 50% in after-hours trading.
- The share surge recovered nearly half of Rivian's stock losses for the year.
- The partnership provides a financial lifeline to Rivian, which has struggled to increase output and delivery of its electric SUV and truck models.
- Rivian halted plans in March to construct a new manufacturing facility in Georgia.
- The halt was intended to save money while dealing with significant losses totaling almost $39,000 per car manufactured in the previous quarter.
- General Motors Co. stated it will take "decades" for the EV industry to flourish.
- Ford Motor Co. is decreasing EV investment by $12 billion and postponing the opening of new battery-powered facilities and models.
- Rivian and other pure-play EV manufacturers are struggling due to mainstream consumers' reluctance to embrace electric vehicles.
- Tesla Inc. is experiencing declining profit margins and underwhelming sales.
- Clinical professor Erik Gordon of the University of Michigan’s Ross School of Business stated that the cost of going it alone is too high and investors are less keen on EV companies than when Rivian started.
- A joint statement confirmed that Volkswagen and Rivian will equally control and own the new enterprise.
- Volkswagen intends to acquire the initial $1 billion equity interest in Rivian through an unsecured convertible note.
- The convertible note will exchange into Rivian shares on or after December 1st.
- Amazon.com Inc. is the largest shareholder in Rivian, holding a 16% stake valued at around $2 billion as of Tuesday's close.
- The agreement mandates Volkswagen to spend an extra $2 billion on Rivian shares in two equal installments in 2025 and 2026.
- Volkswagen plans to contribute an additional $2 billion to the joint venture via a loan accessible in 2026 and a payment upon the business's founding.
- Rivian considers the agreement's structure advantageous because if its shares rise above their current price, there would be less equity dilution for investors and Volkswagen would ultimately hold a lower stake.
- During a post-announcement conference call, Rivian CEO RJ Scaringe stated the company can proceed with plans to construct the new facility in Georgia with Volkswagen's backing.
- By the end of the decade, Rivian remains contractually required to invest $5 billion in the Georgia project.
- Volkswagen will gain access to Rivian's EV architecture and software after years of struggling to produce plug-in cars as functional and efficient as Tesla's.
- Rivian abandoned plans to jointly develop EVs with Ford (an early investor) in November 2021.
- Rivian also abandoned a contract with Mercedes-Benz to produce electric vans in December 2022.
