# Volvo Car USA

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-03

**Source:** [Volvo Car USA reports second quarter 2026 sales | Volvo Cars Media US](https://www.volvocars.com/us/media/press-releases/0A9C5A70243A16B2/)  ·  `page_014192`

## Facts

- Reported sales of 34,228 vehicles for the second quarter of 2026, up 9 percent year over year.
- Sales were driven by the recovery of XC60 and XC90 models.
- Electrified vehicles, defined as vehicles with a fully electric or plug-in hybrid powertrain, accounted for 24.1 percent of total U.S. sales in the quarter.
- Sales of electrified vehicles declined 17.1 percent compared to the same period last year as the company continues to balance customer demand for the full range of powertrains across the U.S. market.
- The overall U.S. automotive market remains impacted by low customer sentiment, increased competition in the SUV segment, and a slower than expected recovery in sales of electric and plug-in hybrid cars post subsidy removal.
- Sales increased for two straight months in the second quarter, reflecting the positive impact of actions taken to support retailers in driving business to their stores.
- The award-winning XC90 and best-selling XC60 both delivered a sharp double-digit increase for the second quarter compared to the same period last year.
- Certified pre-owned sales continued their positive momentum, increasing 38 percent year over year in the second quarter, with fully electric certified pre-owned sales surging 183 percent compared with the second quarter of last year.
- Volvo Cars unveiled the all-new EX60 in North America for the first time in May, debuting the highly-anticipated model in New York City and officially opened orders for U.S. customers.
- Luis Rezende, President of Volvo Car Americas, stated that the focus remains on meeting the needs of U.S. customers across their electrification journey through a strong portfolio of premium SUVs, positioning Volvo Cars with the right products, offers, and experience to meet the needs of U.S. drivers.
- Q2 2026 total sales were 34,228 vehicles compared to 31,395 in Q2 2025.
- Electrified vehicle sales in Q2 2026 were 8,262 units, down from 9,970 in Q2 2025.
- Fully electric vehicle sales in Q2 2026 were 1,588 units, down from 2,871 in Q2 2025.
- Plug-in hybrid vehicle sales in Q2 2026 were 6,674 units, down from 7,099 in Q2 2025.
- Mild hybrid vehicle sales in Q2 2026 were 25,966 units, up from 21,425 in Q2 2025.
