# Hanon Systems

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** thermal_management  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp: Automotive Supplier Hanon Systems to Create 160 New Jobs in Bulloch County - EIN Presswire](https://www.einnews.com/pr_news/635476446/gov-kemp-automotive-supplier-hanon-systems-to-create-160-new-jobs-in-bulloch-county)  ·  `page_001400`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Hanon Systems will create 160 new jobs.
- Hanon Systems will invest more than $40 million in a new manufacturing facility in Bulloch County.
- The announcement was made by Governor Kemp while on an economic development trip to Israel on May 23.
- Georgia’s automotive and e-mobility industries continue to grow as the state welcomes Hanon Systems.
- Georgia furthers its position as the EV capital of America with new jobs and opportunities coming to Georgians across the state.
- Min Sung, President and Chief Executive Officer of Hanon Systems, stated the company is pleased to contribute to the local economy of Bulloch County through job creation.
- Hanon Systems thanked the State of Georgia and the cooperation between its agencies for guiding the project.
- The announcement aligns with Hanon Systems' strategy to support the industry shift toward electrification while growing its North America footprint.
- Hanon Systems will build a new facility at Gateway Regional Industrial Park in Bulloch County.
- The new facility will assemble automotive thermal solutions.
- Operations at the new facility are expected to begin in May 2024.
- Billy Allen, Chair of the Development Authority of Bulloch County, stated Statesboro-Bulloch County is honored by Hanon Systems’ decision to locate its new facility there.
- The community is excited to welcome another automotive supplier that will work closely with the Hyundai Metaplant and the electric vehicle industry.
- This decision exemplifies the resources offered by the community and region to help companies succeed.
- Griff Lynch, Executive Director of the Georgia Ports Authority (GPA), stated the announcement further solidifies Georgia’s position as a growing hub for auto manufacturing.
- The GPA supports the operations of Hyundai Motor Group and its suppliers to deliver jobs and opportunity for Georgia residents.
- Alyce Thornhill, Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy, represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The project was executed in partnership with the Development Authority of Bulloch County, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated suppliers like Hanon Systems successfully support larger industry ecosystems in Georgia through partnership-driven solutions ranging from workforce training to logistics systems efficiencies.
- Bulloch County is part of the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority.
- Georgia is positioned as a hub for the electric mobility industry, building on assets that make the automotive industry successful.
- Since 2020, over 40 EV-related projects have announced more than $22.7 billion in investment and 28,400 jobs in Georgia.
- On October 25, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America officially broke ground in neighboring Bryan County on its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility.
- Hanon Systems was formed in 1986 and has grown into a leading supplier of automotive thermal and energy management solutions for electrified and conventional vehicles.
- Hanon Systems' offerings include heating ventilation and air conditioning, powertrain cooling, compressors, fluid transport, and electronics and fluid pressure.
