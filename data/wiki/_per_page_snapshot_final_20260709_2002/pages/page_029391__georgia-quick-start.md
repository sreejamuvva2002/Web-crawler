# Georgia Quick Start

**Type:** workforce_training  ·  **Role:** workforce_training  ·  **Published:** 2023-08-20

**Source:** [Quick Start: Meeting the Demands of Georgia’s Clean Energy Boom | Georgia](https://georgia.org/blogs/quick-start-meeting-demands-georgias-clean-energy-boom)  ·  `page_029391`

## Facts

- Global corporations throughout the clean-energy sector are spending billions to build facilities to manufacture a wide array of solar, e-mobility, and innovative battery products.
- The State of Georgia ranks as a top-choice destination for these projects.
- These projects are literally creating tens of thousands of jobs in the state.
- Georgia Quick Start is a 56-year-old program that provides company-specific workforce training.
- The program has been routinely named the top state workforce development program in the country.
- Quick Start has helped prepare more than 1.8 million Georgians for new jobs since its creation in 1967.
- Companies benefiting from Quick Start include Rivian, Hyundai Motor Group, and SK Battery America.
- Quick Start customizes training to replicate the actual processes that will be used on-site.
- The program creates a workforce ready to start production on Day One.
- Quick Start offers free-of-charge job training for qualified companies.
- While utilized by a diverse group of manufacturing sectors, the majority of its recent clients are clean energy companies and their suppliers.
- In June (2023), the U.S. Secretary of Education spoke at Agnes Scott College.
- The U.S. Secretary of Education praised Quick Start as one of Georgia’s unique workforce-training assets.
- An article by Alison F. Takemura for Canary Media detailed Quick Start's impact in the clean energy space.
- Robert Howey was hired as a 'tabber operator' on the production line at the Hanwha Qcells solar manufacturing plant in Dalton.
- His role involved watching over machines that solder silicon wafers together, an early stage in making a solar module.
- He initially did not understand how his task fit into the overall process and felt nervous about doing his job well.
- Georgia Quick Start provided him guidance during a week of on-ramping.
- Trainers walked him through all the steps of making a solar panel, giving him confidence to start his new career.
- Howey described Quick Start as 'a lifesaver.'
- Quick Start currently operates five training centers.
- The program is expanding its advanced manufacturing center.
- Quick Start is designing three new facilities for clean energy manufacturers and suppliers.
- One of the new facilities will be located in Bryan County.
- Hyundai Motor Group is investing $7.59 billion in an electric vehicle (EV) and battery manufacturing 'metaplant' with aggressive production timelines.
- The state-of-the-art Quick Start facility for Hyundai will be able to train 300 to 400 new Hyundai hires a week.
