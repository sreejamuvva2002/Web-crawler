# Freyr Battery

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Battery Maker Cancels $2.6B Plant Near Atlanta | Engineering News-Record](https://www.enr.com/articles/60309-battery-maker-cancels-26b-plant-near-atlanta)  ·  `page_054987`

## Facts

- Freyr Battery has canceled plans to construct a $2.6-billion battery manufacturing facility in Coweta County, Ga.
- The company is shifting its focus to solar production in Texas.
- Freyr announced plans in November 2022 to construct the Giga America project plant in the Atlanta suburb of Newnan along Interstate 85.
- The project was set to invest funds by 2029.
- The project was expected to create 720 jobs.
- In a Jan. 21 letter to the county development authority, Freyr stated the cancellation decision was made reluctantly and claimed it resulted from a realignment of its near-term strategic goals.
- The plant cancellation follows Freyr's December-announced acquisition of Trina Solar's U.S.-based solar manufacturing assets.
- The acquisition included a 5-GW solar module plant in Wilmer, Texas.
- The Wilmer, Texas plant began production in November.
- The Wilmer, Texas plant is expected to ramp up to full production in 2025.
- Amy Jaick, Freyr senior vice president of communications, said the firm is focusing at the moment on the solar module manufacturing facility in Texas.
- Freyr announced it has selected Austin as its global headquarters to be close to its Wilmer operations.
- In a Feb. 10 release, Freyr stated the move to Texas was done to geographically align the company’s workforce with its operations and strategy to provide solar modules made in the U.S.
- In the Feb. 10 announcement, Freyr said it has an agreement to sell its 368-acre site in Coweta County to an undisclosed party for $50 million.
- The sale of the Coweta County site is expected to close on Feb. 15.
- Freyr expects to net $22.5 million from the sale after repaying state and local grants it has received for the project.
- The Atlanta Journal-Constitution reported in early 2023 that the firm was set to receive up to $358 million in total incentives for the project.
- The incentives included $228 million in local tax breaks over 20 years.
- The county offered a $20 million grant for the project.
- The state offered $90 million in sales tax exemptions.
- The state offered almost $11 million in tax savings via credits.
- A $7-million grant was designated to offset land acquisition costs.
- It was not clear if the project received federal tax credits or other incentives, nor their status.
- The 368-acre parcel in an industrial park has room for future expansion.
- Freyr previously stated its plans followed strong tailwinds in U.S. renewable energy development, an intensifying focus on grid stability initiatives, and tax incentives associated with the Inflation Reduction Act.
- Plans called for an initial investment of $1.7-billion.
- The cell production module was planned to have a capacity of roughly 34 GWh.
- At the time of the 2022 announcement, the project was expected to be one of the largest battery cell manufacturing developments globally.
