# ArcelorMittal

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-02-26

**Source:** [mt-20251231](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1243429/000124342926000020/mt-20251231.htm)  ·  `page_003420`

## Facts

- ArcelorMittal operates a mini-mill and downstream facility in Calvert, AL with a production capacity of 0.1 million tonnes per year producing flat steel.
- ArcelorMittal is constructing an advanced manufacturing facility for non-grain oriented electrical steels (NOES) in Calvert, AL with a capacity of up to 150 thousand tonnes per year, which is essential for EV production and other commercial/industrial applications.
- The Calvert NOES advanced manufacturing facility project consists of an annealing and pickling line (APL), reversing cold mill (RCM), and annealing and varnishing (ACL) lines.
- Forecast completion for the Calvert NOES advanced manufacturing facility is the second half of 2027.
- ArcelorMittal previously completed a new 1.5 million tonnes electric arc furnace (EAF) and caster project at its Calvert site in 2025.
- ArcelorMittal's North America segment produced 7.8 million tonnes of crude steel in 2025, with an achievable capacity of 12.5 million tonnes.
- The North America segment operates 3 blast furnaces and 9 electric arc furnaces (EAF).
- ArcelorMittal Texas HBI operates a facility in Corpus Christi, TX producing hot briquetted iron.
- ArcelorMittal Dofasco operates an integrated and mini-mill facility in Hamilton, Canada with 3.0 million tonnes per year production capacity for flat steel.
- ArcelorMittal Long Products Canada operates a mini-mill in Contrecoeur East and West, Canada with 1.8 million tonnes per year capacity producing long/wire rod, bars, and slabs.
- ArcelorMittal Tubular Products operates downstream pipes and tubes facilities across Brampton, London, Woodstock, Hamilton (Canada), Shelby, Marion (USA), and Monterrey (Mexico).
- ArcelorMittal Tailored Blanks Americas operates downstream laser welded blanks facilities in Woodstock, Detroit (MI), and Silao.
- VAMA is a joint venture located in Loudi, Hunan, China with 2.0 million tonnes per year capacity for steel processing and automotive steel finishing.
- ArcelorMittal's Mardyck facility in France is building facilities to produce 155 thousand tonnes per year of non-grain oriented electrical steels (NOES), including 125 thousand tonnes per year for auto applications, along with a reversing mill and annealing/pickling line.
- Forecast completion for the Mardyck reversing mill is the second half of 2026 and the annealing/pickling line is the first half of 2027.
- ArcelorMittal's crude steel production in 2025 totaled 55.6 million tonnes, comprising 41.2 million tonnes via basic oxygen furnace (BOF) and 14.4 million tonnes via electric arc furnace (EAF).
- ArcelorMittal maintains a joint venture named AMNS India in Hazira, Gujarat with 8.8 million tonnes per year capacity for integrated flat steel production.
- Medium-term Phase 1 plans for the AMNS India joint venture aim to expand production in Hazira to approximately 15 million tonnes per year.
- ArcelorMittal's Las Truchas mine in Mexico is undergoing a revamping project to increase pellet feed capacity by 1 million tonnes per year to 2.3 million tonnes per year, with DRI concentrate grade capability, forecast for completion in the first half of 2027.
- The Serra Azul mine in Brazil will produce facilities capable of producing 4.5 million tonnes per year of DRI quality pellet feed.
- ArcelorMittal's Barra Mansa facility is increasing capacity of HAV bars and sections by 0.4 million tonnes per year.
- AM Green Energy, a subsidiary, is developing renewable energy projects in India including solar, wind power, and Battery Energy Storage Systems (BESS).
- ArcelorMittal's adjusted absolute CO2e footprint was 98.3 million tonnes in 2025, representing a 27.0% reduction from the 2018 baseline of 135.5 million tonnes.
- ArcelorMittal's steel only CO2e intensity was 1.86 tCO2e/tcs in 2025.
- ArcelorMittal's raw material self-sufficiency rates are 72% for iron ore, 91% for coke, and 55% for scrap & DRI.
