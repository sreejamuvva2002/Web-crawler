# Valeo

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2025-04-29

**Source:** [Press release – Q1 2025 Sales | Valeo](https://www.valeo.com/en/press-release-q1-2025-sales/)  ·  `page_013052`

## Facts

- Valeo recorded first-quarter sales of 5.3 billion euros.
- Original equipment sales remained stable on a like-for-like basis (LFL).
- Aftermarket sales increased by 3% on a like-for-like basis (LFL).
- The company confirmed its 2025 objectives based on S&P Global Mobility assumptions, US tariffs, and related trade restrictions effective as of April 29, 2025.
- Valeo maintained original equipment sales in Q1 despite delayed production start-ups in North America and a rapid change in the customer mix in China.
- CEO Christophe Périllat stated that the company is accelerating restructuring plans to reduce administrative and selling costs by around 5% and investments in property, plant, and equipment as well as intangible assets by around 15% in the first half of 2025 compared to the same period in 2024.
- The company is conducting an exhaustive review of its supply chain to neutralize the direct impact of tariffs and obtain compensation for 100% of remaining costs.
- Valeo secured recent order intake from Volkswagen, demonstrating strong business momentum in ADAS (Advanced Driver Assistance Systems).
- The company aims to increase content per vehicle through expertise in hardware and software for driving assistance technologies.
