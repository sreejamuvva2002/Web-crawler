# Milliken & Company

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2022-07-14

**Source:** [Textile factory on Ogeechee River linked to 2011 fish kill to close - The Current](https://thecurrentga.org/2022/07/14/textile-factory-linked-to-2011-fish-kill-to-close/)  ·  `page_046652`

## Facts

- Spartanburg, S.C.-based Milliken & Company announced the closure of its Longleaf plant in Sylvania, Georgia.
- The company will transition all production lines to the expanded and modernized Magnolia Plant in Blacksburg, South Carolina.
- Milliken did not provide a specific closure date but indicated its 260 employees would be offered positions at other Milliken sites.
- The facility was formerly known as King America Finishing before Milliken purchased it in 2014.
- The plant struggled to comply with environmental permits, being out of compliance with the Clean Water Act 11 of the last 12 quarters.
- The U.S. EPA reported the facility had permission to discharge up to 2.77 million gallons per day into the Ogeechee River.
- Ogeechee Riverkeeper Damon Mullis stated Milliken Longleaf was the only large-scale permitted industrial polluter on the river and that its closure removes the primary source of industrial effluent.
- The closure comes as Hyundai Motors announced plans to build a Kia EV plant at a Bryan County megasite, located less than 3 miles from the Ogeechee River.
- Damon Mullis noted that while the textile mill closure is positive for river health, Kia's new plant will likely be 'less impactful as far as toxicity.'
- The riverkeeper plans to step up monitoring efforts between now and the actual closure due to potential permit issues during shutdown.
