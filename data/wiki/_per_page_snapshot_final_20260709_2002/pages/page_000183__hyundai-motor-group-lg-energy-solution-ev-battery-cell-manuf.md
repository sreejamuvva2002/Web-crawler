# Hyundai Motor Group-LG Energy Solution EV Battery Cell Manufacturing Joint Venture

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-05-26

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution to Establish Battery Cell Manufacturing Joint Venture in the U.S. | Hyundai Motor Group](https://www.hyundaimotorgroup.com/news/CONT0000000000093783)  ·  `page_000183`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution announced an EV battery cell manufacturing joint venture in the U.S. on May 26, 2023.
- The two companies signed a memorandum of understanding to produce EV batteries in the U.S. and accelerate Hyundai Motor Group’s electrification efforts in North America.
- The signing ceremony took place at LG Energy Solution’s headquarters in Seoul on May 26, 2023.
- Jaehoon Chang, President and CEO of Hyundai Motor Company, attended the signing ceremony.
- Youngsoo Kwon, CEO of LG Energy Solution, attended the signing ceremony.
- Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution will each hold a 50 percent stake in the joint venture.
- The joint venture will involve an investment of over USD 4.3 billion (KRW 5.7 trillion).
- Jaehoon Chang stated that Hyundai Motor Group is focusing on electrification efforts to secure a leadership position in the global auto industry.
- Jaehoon Chang stated the group will create a strong foundation to lead the global EV transition through establishing a new EV battery cell plant with LG Energy Solution.
- Youngsoo Kwon stated that two strong leaders in the auto and battery industries have joined hands to drive the EV transition in America.
- Youngsoo Kwon stated LG Energy Solution will commit its best efforts to offering ultimate sustainable energy solutions by advancing product competitiveness and global operational expertise.
- The annual production capacity of the new joint venture will be 30 GWh.
- The facility will be able to support the production of 300,000 units of EVs annually.
- The new battery cell manufacturing facility will be located in Bryan County, Savannah, Georgia.
- The facility site is adjacent to Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America, which was under construction at the time of the announcement.
- Construction on the facility is scheduled to begin in the second half of 2023.
- The joint venture plans to start battery production at the end of 2025 at the earliest.
- Hyundai Mobis will assemble battery packs using cells from the new plant.
- The assembled battery packs will be supplied to Hyundai Motor Group’s U.S. manufacturing facilities for the production of Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis EV models.
- The new facility aims to create a stable supply of batteries in the region and enable the group to respond quickly to soaring EV demand in the U.S. market.
- With this joint venture, LG Energy Solution will have seven battery plants operating or under construction in the U.S.
- LG Energy Solution is concentrating most of its resources on expanding production capacity in the U.S.
- LG Energy Solution aims to expedite the clean energy transition in the U.S. by ramping up local production to provide innovative products at scale and with speed.
- Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution have a long-standing partnership in electrification, having previously supplied EV batteries for vehicles including the Elantra Hybrid, Kona Electric, and IONIQ 6.
- The Hyundai Elantra Hybrid, an LPi hybrid vehicle introduced in 2009, was Hyundai Motor Group’s first electrified model.
- In 2021, Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution began construction of a battery cell joint venture in Indonesia, which is set to start production in the first half of 2024.
- Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution aim to further strengthen their ties through ongoing EV battery cooperation.
