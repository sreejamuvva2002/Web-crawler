# Peterson Spring

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- Peterson Spring is a privately held manufacturer of engineered metal products that designs, manufactures and supplies springs, snap-rings, wire forms, clips, clamps, stampings and a variety of sub-assemblies.
- The company traces its corporate history back 100 years to 1914, when Norwegian blacksmith August Peterson arrived in Michigan to produce goods for the burgeoning railroad and automotive industries.
- Three generations of Petersons ran the company through 2008, when a six-member management team was brought in.
- All but the chief financial officer on the new management team had backgrounds in engineering.
- The new management team centralized operations, created standardization in quality systems, cost-estimating systems, and communications to share efficiencies across divisions.
- Don Lowe serves as Senior Vice President for sales and marketing at Peterson Spring.
- Ron Check is the vice president for engineering and technology at Peterson Spring.
- The company currently manufactures engine valve springs for General Motors and has expanded its customer base to other OEMs, marine engines, snowmobiles, and Jet Skis.
- Peterson Spring has expanded with plants in the United Kingdom, Canada, and Mexico.
- Agricultural and automotive opportunities in South America and/or Asia may be next on the company's expansion agenda.
- Lowe stated that current market dynamics have shifted back onshore after years of off-shoring, as rising wages and transportation costs in Asia eroded previous cost advantages and quality declined.
- Some of the company’s pre-recession competition is no longer around, validating Peterson Spring’s mantra of innovation, technology investment and in-house manufacturing discipline.
- The company's corporate headquarters is located in Southfield, Michigan.
