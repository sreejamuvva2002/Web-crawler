# Century Aluminum

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2026-01-26

**Source:** [Energy Department Awardee to Build First American Aluminum Smelter Since 1980 | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/articles/energy-department-awardee-build-first-american-aluminum-smelter-1980)  ·  `page_029041`

## Facts

- U.S. Secretary of Energy Chris Wright joined executives of Century Aluminum to celebrate progress toward constructing a primary aluminum smelter in Inola, Oklahoma.
- The project is supported through a grant from the U.S. Department of Energy (DOE).
- February 10, 2026 marks the date of the announcement and celebration.
- The project demonstrates the economic viability of domestic smelting and reduces U.S. reliance on imports.
- Secretary Wright stated the project will deliver America’s first new aluminum smelter of the 21st century.
- Secretary Wright attributed the project's realization to President Trump’s commitment to revitalizing the manufacturing base and reducing reliance on foreign suppliers.
- Century Aluminum’s joint venture demonstrates how President Trump’s economic policies encourage global companies to partner with U.S. firms, build in America, and create good-paying American jobs.
- Once complete, the plant will be the largest-ever primary aluminum production plant in the U.S.
- The plant has the potential to produce over 500,000 tons of high-quality, primary aluminum per year.
- Approximately 20,000 tons of the annual output will be high-purity aluminum suitable for national defense applications.
- On January 26, 2026, Century Aluminum announced plans to jointly develop the plant with Emirates Global Aluminum (EGA).
- EGA’s investment to help develop the multi-billion-dollar smelter project in Oklahoma was secured by President Trump.
- The investment is part of a strategic partnership announced between the United Arab Emirates and the United States in Abu Dhabi on May 15, 2025.
- In 2025, DOE’s Office of Clean Energy Demonstrations (OCED) awarded $500 million to advance construction of the first new primary aluminum smelter in the United States since 1980.
- The project reduces dependence on foreign imports.
- In 2024, the United States imported 5.46 million metric tons and exported 3 million metric tons of downstream aluminum products and scrap.
- This resulted in net imports of 2.46 million metric tons in 2024.
- There are currently four primary aluminum smelters operating in the United States that produce 683,500 metric tons.
- This production is down from America’s historical ranking as the world’s leading producer when 33 sites were in operation producing up to 5 million tons per year.
- President Trump is ending this trend and re-shoring manufacturing by safeguarding and expanding domestic production of critical minerals and materials through tariffs and other trade policies.
- These trade policies have made investments like this in American projects possible.
