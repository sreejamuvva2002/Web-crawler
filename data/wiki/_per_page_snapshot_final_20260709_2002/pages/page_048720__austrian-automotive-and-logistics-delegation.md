# Austrian Automotive and Logistics Delegation

**Type:** event  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2024-05-03

**Source:** [Six Austrian Automotive, Logistics Firms Wrap Up Southeast U.S. Visit in Atlanta - Global Atlanta](https://www.globalatlanta.com/six-austrian-automotive-suppliers-wrap-up-southeast-u-s-visit-in-atlanta/)  ·  `page_048720`

## Facts

- A delegation of six Austrian suppliers wrapped up a mid-April visit to the Southeast U.S. with a focus on opportunities in Georgia’s automotive sector.
- The visit was hosted by Advantage Austria, represented by Angelika Heitzmann.
- The group consisted of six Austrian firms considering expansion into the U.S. market.
- The delegation began its itinerary in Greer, S.C., touring a BMW factory described as the automaker’s top export plant globally.
- The group also toured an inland port facility in Greer, S.C.
- Upon arrival in Atlanta, the delegation was welcomed by Honorary Consul and industrial real estate executive Paul Seefried.
- On April 17, the group attended the German-American Chamber of Commerce South’s annual SME Summit in Duluth.
- The trip’s theme focused on uncovering the “Best of Automotive & Logistics in the Southeast USA.”
- Key discussion topics for the Austrian companies included workforce development, manufacturing innovation, and logistics solutions for market entry or expansion.
- The delegation met with Porsche Cars North America CEO Timo Resch at the Porsche Experience Center near the Atlanta airport.
- Timo Resch shared insights on Porsche’s growth and its strategic positioning of Atlanta as a nationwide logistics hub.
- Rick Walker of the Georgia Automotive Manufacturers Association briefed the group on recent investment announcements by Hyundai, Kia, and Rivian in the region.
- The delegation met with Knapp North America, an existing Austrian investor in Georgia that provides warehouse automation technologies.
- Knapp North America recently expanded its Kennesaw facility to accommodate a growing workforce.
- Knapp invested $7 million in its Kennesaw facility in 2020 to create space for 400 employees, including 150 on the warehouse floor and 250 in the office.
- Stefan Harrigan of the Metro Atlanta Chamber pitched Atlanta to the delegation after visiting Austria in the months prior to the mission.
- A reception at Arnall Golden Gregory LLP closed out the April 15-18 trip, where attorney Tycho Stahl shared insights on market entry.
- The participating Austrian companies were MSG Mechatronic Systems, DEWETRON, Hödlmayr International, CNT Management Consulting, AMAG Austria Metall, and VEGU Precision Turning Parts.
- Advantage Austria serves as the Austrian government’s trade promotion arm, with its Atlanta office part of a network spanning New York, Washington D.C., Los Angeles, San Francisco, and Chicago.
