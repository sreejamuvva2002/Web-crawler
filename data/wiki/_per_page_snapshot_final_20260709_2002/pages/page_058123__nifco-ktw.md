# NIFCO KTW

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2015-06-10

**Source:** [German Company NIFCO KTW to Locate Plant in Stephens County - 92.1 WLHR](https://921wlhr.com/german-company-nifco-ktw-to-locate-plant-in-stephens-county/)  ·  `page_058123`

## Facts

- NIFCO KTW, a German-based automotive supplier of high-quality injection molded components, will locate its U.S. operations in Stephens County under the name "Project Hummingbird".
- The company will establish a 130,000 square foot facility on 32 acres in the Hayestone-Brady Business Park, located on Highway 17 between Toccoa and Martin, as its first North American headquarters.
- Over the next five years, NIFCO KTW plans to invest $27 million and create 200 jobs, with officials stating that more than half of those jobs will be created in the first year of operations.
- NIFCO KTW Managing Director Roland Furtmayr stated the company is proud to find Toccoa as the home for its new U.S. headquarters and production facility, citing local connectivity and aiming to become a reliable employer contributing to positive economic development in Stephens County.
- The Toccoa plant will supply injection molded parts to BMW and Mercedes Benz USA among other automotive manufacturers worldwide.
- Stephens County Commission Chair Dennis Bell welcomed the project, noting the community needed an investment of this magnitude.
- Toccoa Mayor Terry Carter stated the addition of the company will invigorate the local economy and put Stephens County on the map for future manufacturing prospects, appreciating the state's strong support and incentives during site selection.
- The City of Toccoa committed to spending approximately $300,000 to run water, wastewater, and gas utilities to the company's building at no cost to NIFCO KTW.
- Stephens County will provide 25 years of full property tax relief, though officials noted the county is not currently collecting tax on the property because it is owned by the Development Authority.
- The company is qualifying for state job creation tax credits as part of the project agreement.
- Stephens County Development Authority Chairman Brian Akin described the announcement as a huge win for the local job market and praised local leadership for their work through the recruitment process.
- Toccoa-Stephens County Chamber Board Chair Greg Roach pledged to help the company acclimate to the local area and thanked the community for their support in bringing the project to fruition.
- Governor Nathan Deal stated that automotive suppliers choose Georgia due to trust in its pro-business resources and ready-to-work community, highlighting over 40 years of business ties with Germany in the region and looking forward to the high-quality jobs the company will bring.
- The project agreement requires construction to begin later in 2015, with building completion expected in 2016.
- Stephens County Development Authority Executive Director Tim Martin indicated the facility could come online in the second half of 2016.
- NIFCO KTW has plans to expand its Toccoa site in the future, with additional land available at Hayestone-Brady for that expansion.
