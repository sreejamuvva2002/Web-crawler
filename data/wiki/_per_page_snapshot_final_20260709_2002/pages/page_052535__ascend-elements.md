# Ascend Elements

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Ascend Elements to invest $1 billion in Southwest Kentucky EV battery materials mfg. facility -](https://etn.news/energy-storage/ascend-elements-to-invest-1-billion-in-southwest-kentucky-ev-battery-materials-mfg-facility)  ·  `page_052535`

## Facts

- Ascend Elements announced plans to invest $310 million in Phase 1 — and up to $1 billion over several possible phases — to build a sustainable lithium-ion battery materials facility in Hopkinsville, Ky.
- Ground-breaking for the facility is expected in Q4 2022.
- The first-of-its-kind manufacturing facility, known as "Apex 1", will utilize the company's Hydro-to-Cathode™ direct precursor synthesis process technology.
- The facility will take in black mass from recycled electric vehicle batteries and produce enough lithium-ion battery precursor and sustainable cathode active material to equip up to 250,000 electric vehicles per year.
- This closed-loop manufacturing system generates minimal waste and carbon emissions compared to traditional cathode manufacturing.
- Apex 1 will feature onsite chemical recycling capabilities and a wastewater treatment plant.
- Kentucky Gov. Andy Beshear stated that Kentucky has taken the initiative to ensure the state will be a leader in the automotive industry of the future, calling Ascend Elements' addition to Hopkinsville a tremendous step toward building that foundation.
- The facility is located on over 140 acres in Commerce Industrial Park II in Hopkinsville.
- The 500,000-square-foot Apex 1 facility will begin operations in late 2023.
- With potential expansion phases, the facility would employ up to 400 people in roles ranging from engineers and chemists to warehouse associates and manufacturing operators.
- The project is expected to potentially generate $4.4 billion in economic impact to Kentucky over the construction period and first 10 years of operation.
- Ascend Elements CEO Michael O'Kronley thanked Gov. Beshear, his staff, Christian County, and the City of Hopkinsville for their support, noting that clean energy and climate technology industries are bringing good jobs to communities across the country.
- O'Kronley stated that Ascend Elements is building a domestic source of sustainable lithium-ion cathode material for EV batteries in Kentucky that does not exist anywhere else in the United States.
- The company evaluated more than 50 locations for this facility, with Hopkinsville being the only site that met all requirements.
- Key site selection factors included easy access to transportation, clean renewable power, a diverse workforce, and proximity to electric vehicle manufacturers and lithium-ion battery factories in the southeast United States.
- Ascend Elements' Base 1 battery recycling facility in Covington, Ga. is a key source of recycled battery feedstock (black mass) for the Apex 1 facility.
