# Magna International

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2017-10-18

**Source:** [This Just In: Magna International Stock Upgraded | The Motley Fool](https://www.fool.com/investing/2017/10/18/this-just-in-magna-international-stock-upgraded.aspx)  ·  `page_057522`

## Facts

- Bank of America-Merrill Lynch upgraded shares of Magna International stock to a "buy" rating and assigned a $63 price target.
- Merrill Lynch analysts noted that Magna possesses expertise in numerous automotive component systems made for original equipment manufacturers (OEMs).
- Magna's knowledge of how automotive parts fit together could afford the company more opportunities in contract manufacturing with traditional OEMs along with new industry entrants.
- Magna has a unique competitive advantage over other car parts makers, permitting it to build entire production-ready models of cars, including electric cars, for other manufacturers using its proprietary technology, expertise, and trusted reputation.
- Motley Fool automotive specialist John Rosevear described Magna as "the world's only high-quality contract auto manufacturer."
- Magna holds contracts to build BMW's new 530e plug-in hybrid vehicle.
- Magna holds contracts to build Jaguar's new I-PACE all-electric SUV.
- Merrill Lynch views Magna's ability to build production-ready models as setting the company ahead of competition and positioning it as one of the best-positioned suppliers over the long-term to continue winning business and growing revenues ahead of market growth.
- Magna International generated $1.7 billion in positive free cash flow over the past 12 months.
- Magna reported $2.1 billion in net income under GAAP standards.
- Magna is valued at $19.3 billion, giving it a price-to-free-cash-flow ratio of 11.4.
- Analysts expect Magna to grow profits at about 10% annually over the next five years, with Merrill Lynch believing growth may exceed this expectation.
- Magna stock pays a market average 2% dividend yield with a 19% payout ratio that could be expanded.
- The author estimates the stock offers about a 12% total return, potentially more.
- Magna carries approximately $2.8 billion in net debt after accounting for cash on hand.
- Magna's enterprise value-to-free-cash-flow ratio stands at 13.
