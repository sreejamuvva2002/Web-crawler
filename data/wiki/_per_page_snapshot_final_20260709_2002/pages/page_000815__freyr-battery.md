# FREYR Battery

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-02-12

**Source:** [An interview with the CEO of FREYR battery on their $3B US plant](https://www.freeingenergy.com/podcast-tom-jensen-freyr-battery/)  ·  `page_000815`

## Facts

- FREYR Battery is a publicly traded Luxembourg and Norway-based company emerging as a leader in clean, high-density battery cell manufacturing.
- Tom Jensen serves as the CEO and Cofounder of FREYR Battery.
- FREYR is committing some $2.6 Billion to a facility envisioned for Coweta County in Georgia.
- FREYR's Giga Arctic facility will be powered by 100% renewable energy.
- FREYR's core operational mantras are speed, scale, and sustainability.
- Tom Jensen focuses on scalable industrial systems that can be replicated rapidly in many jurisdictions to reduce CO2 emissions.
- The Inflation Reduction Act launched by the US administration is cited as a poster child example for accelerating the energy transition.
- The IRA incentive program accelerated FREYR's deployment plans in the US, shifting focus from Norway to the US market.
- FREYR originally planned to expand further in Norway before replicating its ambitions elsewhere but changed course due to the IRA and strong interest from customers, technology providers, supply chain partners, regulators, and local/state officials.
- FREYR plans to develop over 40 gigawatt hours of battery cell capacity by 2025.
- FREYR aims to position itself as one of Europe’s largest cell suppliers.
- Norway is the leading adopter of electric vehicles on the planet, with close to 90% of all cars sold there being electric.
- The Norwegian government removed all taxes on electric vehicles to drive adoption.
- Norway launched a national battery strategy earlier this year (relative to February 2023).
- FREYR is establishing the full battery value chain in Norway, including its Giga Arctic site located on the Arctic Circle.
- FREYR's production technology was selected from 24M Technologies, a MIT spinoff founded by material science professor Yet-Ming Chiang.
- FREYR's new production process reduces facility footprint per gigawatt hour by more than 80% compared to conventional solutions.
- The new process consumes more than 60% less energy in the production process itself by eliminating solvent recovery steps.
- The simplified process requires less than half as many people per installed capacity compared to conventional plants.
- FREYR can use existing raw materials including nickel, manganese, cobalt oxide cathodes, lithium-ion phosphate cathodes, graphite-based anodes, and silicone in anodes.
- The first gigawatt-hour scale production line, labeled the customer qualification plant, is expected to be operational in Q1 2024.
- Giga Arctic will feature eight of these gigawatt-scale production lines.
- FREYR's technology offers a 50% lower estimated capital expenditure per gigawatt hour compared to plants currently in operation or under construction.
- The technology is expected to generate anticipated 200% higher production per plant employed compared to industry averages.
- Q CELLS announced a $2.5 billion expansion of its solar panel manufacturing facilities in Georgia, creating 2,500 new jobs.
- Georgia's attractiveness for clean tech includes relatively low energy costs, a huge logistics hub with rail and air, an influential network of technical colleges, favorable right-to-work laws, and state/local government support.
- FREYR is counting on developing and expanding a local supply chain in Georgia as part of its Giga America project.
- Tom Jensen previously worked at Norsk Hydro in the oil/gas and aluminum sectors before focusing on sustainability and batteries.
- FREYR Battery is publicly traded on the New York Stock Exchange.
