# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-04-05

**Source:** [Georgia International – Georgia Political Review](https://georgiapoliticalreview.com/georgia-international/)  ·  `page_057511`

## Facts

- GDEcD International Offices are located worldwide in Argentina, Brazil, Canada, Chile, Colombia, Europe, Israel, Japan, Korea, Mexico, Peru, Southeast Asia, the United Kingdom, and Ireland.
- The GDEcD facilitates international trades, attracts foreign investment, and strengthens state-level international relations.
- Gov. Brian Kemp and Commissioner Pat Wilson frequently go on trade missions, intertwining business and diplomacy by meeting with companies and representatives abroad.
- Gov. Brian Kemp and Commissioner Pat Wilson most recently visited Belgium and the United Kingdom in February 2026.
- The governor and commissioner host delegates and company executives for trade talks, such as the Belgian delegation with Princess Astrid of Belgium visiting Georgia in June 2022.
- The top five exports from Georgia in 2025 were civilian aircraft parts, computers and hardware, motor vehicles, network communications equipment, and medical devices.
- Many large international companies are located in Georgia, including Hapag-Lloyd, Hyundai Motor Group, KIA Motors, La Regina Atlantica, Mercedes-Benz USA, Porsche USA, Q CELLS, SK On, TEKLAS, and more.
- Atlanta alone partners with 17 sister cities, including Brussels, Nuremberg, Tbilisi, Lagos, Rio de Janeiro, Toulouse, and more.
- In 1967, former Mayor Ivan Allen Jr. forged Atlanta’s first sister city relationship with Salzburg, Austria.
- The University of Georgia, Georgia Institute of Technology, and Emory University coordinate study abroad programs across the world, notably with the UGA Campus in Cortona, Italy, and the GT Europe campus in Metz, France.
- Athens, GA, and Cortona are sister cities.
- More than 8,000 UGA students have attended the Cortona program.
- More than 13,000 students have attended the GT Europe campus since its founding in 1990.
- Atlanta has over 70 consular or trade offices and over 30 chambers of commerce.
- The International School of Atlanta opened in 1984 to offer diplomats and other international families International Baccalaureate programs in multiple languages.
- Defense companies are booming industries in Georgia.
- Data centers and Artificial Intelligence are becoming increasingly important topics in Georgia, especially considering the state’s attractive energy prices.
- The automotive industry has been one of the major sectors in Georgia.
- There have been several changes within the automotive industry, with the push for and then the pullback of electric vehicles while balancing the continued demand for combustion vehicles.
- Georgia has invested in renewable energy and sustainability research, but its companies still mainly produce traditional cars and vehicles.
- It is challenging for US and international companies to keep up with the prices and technology in China, which are some of the most competitive in the field.
- Initiatives to increase robotics and automation in Georgia include RoboGeorgia and the Institute for Robotics and Intelligent Machines (IRIM) at GT.
- These programs aim to increase automation by helping companies connect with suppliers, technology, and information to advance competitiveness.
- Changes in US tariff policy have led international companies to express concern that trade relation uncertainty will negatively impact their earnings reports.
- Hyundai has invested billions into Georgia and opened many job opportunities across the state.
- In September 2025, Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) officers arrested approximately 475 people from the Hyundai plant in Georgia.
- There was significant backlash from South Korean officials advocating for the rights of their citizens following the ICE arrests.
- The event fractured the relationship between Korea and the United States, causing tension over the safety of international workers in the US and the relationship between Seoul and Washington, D.C.
- The situation threatened Georgia’s over fifty years of close relations with Korea.
- Despite changing political and trade situations, many companies remain interested in expanding to Georgia because of right-to-work policies that make it easier for manufacturers to manage their local workforce.
- Maintaining strong trade, investment, and diplomatic ties between Georgia and international partners is highlighted as important for continuing long-standing partnerships and benefiting from the US market.
