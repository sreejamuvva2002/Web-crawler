# Joon Georgia, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2022-11-07

**Source:** [First Hyundai Motor Group Supplier to Build Facility in Bulloch County | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-releases/2022/first-hyundai-motor-group-supplier-build-facility-bulloch-county)  ·  `page_000209`

## Facts

- Joon Georgia, Inc., an automotive parts manufacturer, will create 630 new jobs and invest $317 million in Bulloch County.
- Plans for the company’s new manufacturing facility mark the first confirmed supplier for Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America in Bryan County.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced the project on November 7, 2022.
- Joon Georgia is part of Ajin USA, a current supplier for multiple Hyundai Motor Group facilities.
- The company produces a variety of automotive body parts and electronics for original equipment manufacturers.
- Jung Ho Sea serves as the CEO of Ajin USA.
- Joon Georgia will create approximately 630 new jobs with competitive benefits in Bulloch County and the State of Georgia.
- Joon Georgia’s new facility will be located at Bruce Yawn Commerce Park, previously known as the Southern Gateway Commerce Park, near Statesboro.
- The site is situated in close proximity to the Port of Savannah.
- Operations at the Bruce Yawn Commerce Park are expected to begin in mid-2024.
- The company will begin hiring for the facility in mid-2023.
- Billy Allen serves as the Chair of the Development Authority of Bulloch County.
- Georgia Southern University and Ogeechee Technical College will provide critical workforce support to help Joon Georgia succeed.
- Alyce Thornhill, Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy, represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this project.
- The project was executed in partnership with the Development Authority of Bulloch County, Georgia Quick Start, Georgia Ports Authority, and Georgia Power.
- Workforce development for the new facility will be supported by Quick Start, which is ranked as the top workforce training program in the nation.
- Quick Start partners with the Technical College System of Georgia to assess workers, train new employees on unique processes, and develop customized job-specific training using current techniques and media.
- Pat Wilson serves as the GDEcD Commissioner.
- Since 2020, more than 30 EV-related projects have contributed over $13.3 billion in investment and announced more than 18,100 jobs in Georgia.
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America officially broke ground on October 25 in neighboring Bryan County on its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility.
- Non-affiliated suppliers connected to the Hyundai project are predicted to invest approximately $1 billion in other locations around the state.
