# DENSO Manufacturing Michigan Iowa Plant (DMIA)

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** thermal_management  ·  **Published:** 2013-05-13

**Source:** [DENSO Manufacturing Michigan Iowa Plant Celebrates Opening Of Urbandale Facility – UnderhoodService](https://www.underhoodservice.com/denso-manufacturing-michigan-iowa-plant-celebrates-opening-of-urbandale-facility/)  ·  `page_053819`

## Facts

- DENSO celebrated the opening of its new thermal assembly plant and warehouse facility in Urbandale, Iowa this week.
- The new facility is named DENSO Manufacturing Michigan Iowa Plant (DMIA).
- The new facility represents a $500,000 investment.
- The facility creates seven new jobs.
- The company expects to expand to 20 employees within two years.
- Production is expected to nearly double within two years.
- The new Urbandale facility assembles cooling modules for the agricultural and construction industries.
- The plant handles pass-through products, such as radiator assemblies and cooling modules for the construction, agriculture and electric power industries.
- The plant is localizing products that were previously assembled in the United Kingdom for U.S. customers.
- This latest announcement is part of DENSO’s recent expansion initiative to invest nearly $2 billion in North America.
- More than $750 million dollars of the investment is allocated to the United States.
- The initiative aims to strengthen and expand product development and manufacturing capabilities across the region.
- DENSO executives and local city officials celebrated DMIA’s grand opening yesterday in a ribbon cutting ceremony.
- Urbandale Mayor Bob Andeweg attended the celebration as a guest.
- DENSO International America President and CEO Howard Sugi attended the celebration as a guest.
- DENSO Manufacturing Michigan President John Kumagai attended the celebration as a guest.
- DENSO Marston President David Hanson from the U.K. attended the celebration as a guest.
- DMIA Plant Manager Paolo Vercelli attended the celebration as a guest.
- Howard Sugi stated that while this is an extension of existing thermal operations in Michigan and the U.K., it is very much an Iowa facility.
- DENSO has been in Iowa since 1974, when it opened its first facility in Cedar Falls to support heavy-duty, off-highway engine management and others.
- Howard Sugi added that they are excited about opportunities for the new site and DENSO’s heavy-duty business in Iowa in the years ahead.
- Paolo Vercelli stated that the opportunity to put down roots and grow could not be possible without the strong support of the Greater Des Moines Partnership, the city of Urbandale and the Urbandale Development association.
- Vercelli expressed gratitude for the warm welcome and assistance received from the Urbandale Chamber of Commerce.
- DENSO’s motto is “Protecting lives, preserving the planet and preparing a bright future for generations to come.”
- Vercelli stated that DENSO looks forward to sharing this bright future in Urbandale.
