# Porsche Cars North America Inc. Experience Center

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2015-05-07

**Source:** [Porsche's $100 Million Headquarters and Test Track Takes Off - Global Atlanta](https://www.globalatlanta.com/porsches-100-million-headquarters-and-test-track-takes-off/)  ·  `page_048675`

## Facts

- Porsche Cars North America Inc. hosted the grand opening of its new headquarters near the Atlanta airport, kicking off an initiative to rev up the area.
- The $100 million “Experience Center” sits on 27 acres near the international terminal at Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport and serves to reinforce the sports car’s brand.
- The facility is complete with a 1.6-mile test track, a classic car gallery, a simulator lab, and amenities for business conferences.
- About 30,000 people are expected to visit each year, with many trying out driving experiences that start at $300.
- The test track runs through the building's courtyard and is centered on helping drivers understand their cars to enhance performance rather than just speed.
- Instructors lead drivers through six stations on the test track, including a handling circuit with hairpin turns, an off-road course for Porsche SUVs, a low-friction area, and a kick plate that simulates slippage due to ice or wet leaves.
- Georgia’s leaders have been jubilant over Porsche’s economic impact and the visible seal of approval it provides for other international companies.
- The headquarters is Porsche AG’s largest investment in a facility outside of Germany.
- Porsche elected to stay in Atlanta after scouting more than 70 other destinations around the U.S., despite already having its North American office based in Sandy Springs.
- Porsche AG CEO Matthias Müller, Georgia Gov. Nathan Deal, Atlanta Mayor Kasim Reed, and North America President and CEO Detlev von Platen attended the press conference to praise the company and its home.
- Georgia Gov. Nathan Deal stated that Porsche’s investment represents confidence in the future success of the state, economy, and workforce.
- Gov. Deal noted that the facility at One Porsche Drive is innovative, forward-thinking, and performance-driven, and that travelers on I-75 have witnessed the area's progress.
- The opening coincided with Porsche eclipsing sales of 5,000 vehicles for the first time ever.
- Porsche's recent sales growth was mostly driven by the new Macan small SUV.
- All other Porsche models sold in the U.S., including the Boxster/Cayman, 911/918, Cayenne, and Panamera, saw declines in April compared to the same month last year.
- Year-to-date sales for Porsche were up 17 percent at the time of the opening.
- The opening occurred on the same day Mercedes-Benz USA CEO Stephen Cannon was slated to speak to the Atlanta Press Club about relocating that German auto maker’s headquarters to Atlanta.
