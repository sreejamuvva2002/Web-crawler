## Facts

- Kia announced on Thursday that it will assemble the latest model of its electric EV6 crossover at its Georgia factory.
- This marks the second battery-powered model slated for Peach State production after the plant underwent a $217 million upgrade to incorporate EV manufacturing.
- The EV6 is a two-row crossover.
- The EV6 will roll down assembly lines next to the Korean automaker’s three-row electric EV9 SUV at its factory in West Point, roughly 80 miles southwest of downtown Atlanta.
- Gasoline-powered models, including the brand’s Telluride and Sorento fleets, are also built at the factory.
- The factory opened in 2009.
- Gov. Brian Kemp said in a news release: “Today’s announcement by Kia is just the latest testament to how beneficial our decades-long partnership has been, both for this great job creator and the people of Georgia.”
