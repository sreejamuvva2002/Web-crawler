# Haering Precision USA LP

**Type:** workforce_training  ·  **Role:** workforce_training  ·  **Published:** 2025-05-12

**Source:** [Haering Precision and Hart County College and Career Academy Partner to Expand Hands-On Training for High School Students - WNEG](https://wnegradio.com/haering-precision-and-hart-county-college-and-career-academy-partner-to-expand-hands-on-training-for-high-school-students/)  ·  `page_020845`

## Facts

- Haering Precision USA LP partnered with Hart County College and Career Academy to expand hands-on training for high school students.
- Precision machining equipment from Hart County High School was placed directly within Haering’s Lavonia facility.
- The strategic collaboration enhances local workforce development by giving high school students access to real-world training in a high-tech manufacturing environment.
- The machines are located in the Haering Academy for Apprenticeship and Further Training, a dedicated on-site training space at Haering Precision.
- The academy serves as a hub for technical education that combines classroom instruction with practical, industry-relevant experience.
- The equipment installation is part of a broader initiative to enhance Work-Based Learning (WBL) opportunities for students in Hart County.
- Students can train on familiar school-owned machines in an industry setting at Haering while learning directly from experienced professionals.
- Nathan Lindler, Technical Plant Manager at Haering Precision, stated that this is often the students’ very first exposure to a real manufacturing facility.
- The program aims to help students explore their interests, build confidence, and understand career possibilities in manufacturing.
- The integration supports growing demand for skilled machinists and industrial technicians by accelerating career readiness and reinforcing ties between technical education and real-world applications.
- Students gain hands-on experience, mentorship, and the opportunity to be part of a global company known for high-precision components in the automotive industry.
- Steve Burton, CEO of Hart County College and Career Academy, expressed pride in partnering with Haering Precision to provide student access and support community growth.
- The Work-Based Learning program is currently active and welcoming students.
