# Bandag

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Bandag to build state-of-the-art retreading factory in Georgia with Tegeta Motors and Bridgestone](https://dayofdubai.com/news/bandag-to-build-state-of-the-art-retreading-factory-in-georgia-with-tegeta-motors-and-bridgestone)  ·  `page_018140`

## Facts

- Bandag signed an agreement with Tegeta Motors to establish a state-of-the-art tyre retreading facility in Rustavi.
- The facility marks the first Bandag factory in the Caucasus region.
- The project is supported by Bridgestone Middle East and Africa (MEA).
- The initial investment for the project is USD 2 million.
- The initiative aims to expand sustainable mobility and circular economy practices across the region.
- The Tegeta Bandag Factory is designed to produce up to 5,000 retreaded tyres annually in its initial phase.
- Plans are in place to double production capacity within five years.
- Construction of the facility is scheduled to begin in 2025.
- Completion of the facility is targeted for late 2026.
- The plant will introduce internationally recognised retreading standards to the Caucasus market.
- The facility will initially focus on retreading tyres for buses and trucks.
- The operation ensures compliance with global safety and performance benchmarks.
- The project helps fleet operators reduce costs and environmental impact.
- Jacques Fourie, President of Bridgestone Africa, Middle East Region, stated the project represents a major step in expanding high-quality retreading solutions in the Caucasus, combining global expertise, shared vision, and a strong commitment to sustainable mobility.
- The factory will be equipped with advanced Bandag machinery sourced from Europe and the United States.
- The retreading process reinforces used tyre casings with premium materials similar to those used in new tyre manufacturing.
- The process delivers cost savings of 30–40 per cent compared to new tyres.
- The retreaded tyres offer performance and service life of up to five years.
- Ekaterine Kavtaradze, CEO of Tegeta Motors, stated the project will help advance fleet solutions, strengthen the company’s regional presence, and deliver long-term value to customers and communities.
- Tegeta Motors will integrate the new retreading operations into its broader ecosystem of tyre, oil, battery, and recycling services.
- The initiative underscores Bandag’s regional growth strategy and commitment to cleaner, more efficient mobility solutions through strategic partnerships and advanced technology.
