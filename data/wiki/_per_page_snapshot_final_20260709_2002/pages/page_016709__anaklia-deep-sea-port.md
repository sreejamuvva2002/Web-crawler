## Facts

- Discussions with the Uzbek delegation placed particular emphasis on the strategic significance of the Anaklia Deep Sea Port for Georgia, Uzbekistan, and the Middle Corridor.
- Both sides engaged in substantive discussions on opportunities to further deepen bilateral trade and economic cooperation.
- Special attention was paid to strengthening transport and logistics opportunities between the two countries.
- The ongoing construction of the Anaklia Deep Sea Port project was highlighted as highly important for both Georgia and Uzbekistan.
- The Georgian government plans to modernize the country's railway network to boost cargo-handling capacity and enhance freight transportation efficiency.
- Modernization of Georgian Railways is expected to improve and increase the capacity of cargo flows.
- Discussions centered on increasing bilateral trade and broadening export opportunities for Georgian products to diversify markets.
- Members of the Uzbek delegation will be shown the ongoing construction of the Anaklia Deep Sea Port, Poti Free Industrial Zone, and other port infrastructure during the visit.
- The visits aim to identify future cooperation opportunities and develop a practical action plan to accelerate trade and economic relations expansion.
- The Anaklia Deep Sea Port is one of Georgia's most important strategic projects and will play a pivotal role in strengthening the country's economy and reinforcing its strategic position.
- Active construction is currently underway at the Anaklia Deep Sea Port, with the port expected to receive its first vessel in 2029.
- The Anaklia Deep Sea Port will remain under state ownership, while international companies will be involved only in managing terminal infrastructure.
- Development of the Anaklia Deep Sea Port will follow a model ensuring the state's leading role in strategic infrastructure while creating a competitive environment for multiple partner countries and international companies.
- The Georgian government decided to develop the Anaklia Deep-Sea Port under a landlord model, where the state retains ownership of core maritime and port infrastructure and oversees its development and management.
- Georgia and Uzbekistan reached key agreements during the Uzbek presidential visit to boost trade, investments, and the Middle Corridor's role.
- Uzbek Investment Minister Laziz Kudratov praised Georgia's expanding port infrastructure after visiting the Anaklia Deep Sea Port construction site and Poti's port facilities.
- Georgia and Uzbekistan are discussing the establishment of an Uzbek logistics and production hub in Georgia.
