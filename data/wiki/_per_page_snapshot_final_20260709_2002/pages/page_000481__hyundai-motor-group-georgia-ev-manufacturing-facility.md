# Hyundai Motor Group Georgia EV Manufacturing Facility

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-05-20

**Source:** [Hyundai to invest $5.54B in Georgia at first fully dedicated EV manufacturing facility  - Grice Connect](https://www.griceconnect.com/business/hyundai-to-invest-5-54b-in-georgia-at-first-fully-dedicated-ev-manufacturing-facility-6504551)  ·  `page_000481`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp and Hyundai Motor Group Executive Chair Euisun Chung announced that Hyundai Motor Group will open its first fully dedicated electric vehicle (EV) and battery manufacturing facility in the State of Georgia.
- Hyundai Motor Group will invest $5.54 billion in opening a state-of-the-art U.S. smart factory at the Bryan County Megasite.
- Non-affiliated Hyundai Motor Group suppliers will invest approximately another $1 billion in the project.
- The project will deliver approximately 8,100 new jobs to Georgia’s coastal region.
- Governor Kemp was joined by Jay Chang, President and CEO of Hyundai Motor Company, and José Muñoz, President and COO of Hyundai Motor Company, along with Hyundai Motor Group executives and state and local economic development leaders at the premier 2,923-acre site.
- The megasite was purchased jointly by the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority (JDA) and the State less than a year ago.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that the announcement marks the largest economic development project in Georgia's history and aims to make the state a premier destination for quality companies creating jobs.
- Hyundai Motor Group Executive Chair Euisun Chung shared plans to open their first dedicated full EV and battery manufacturing facilities in the U.S., partnering with Georgia to achieve shared goals of electrified mobility and sustainability.
- Hyundai Motor Group expects to begin construction on the new facility in January 2023.
- Full production is expected in the first half of 2025, with an annual capacity of 300,000 units.
- Wages throughout the project will be well-paying advanced manufacturing positions competitive in the local market.
- Jaehoon (Jay) Chang stated that America embraces change and drives innovation, and the new EV plant will help meet growing demands of U.S. customers for leading edge design and zero-emissions vehicles.
- Hyundai Motor Group cited speed-to-market, workforce, and Georgia’s ability to meet the company’s carbon neutrality standards as reasons for choosing the state.
- Georgia is home to an existing network of Hyundai subsidiaries and suppliers.
- The Bryan County Megasite was developed by the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor JDA over several years in partnership with the state to offer speed-to-market requirements that reduce barriers to speedy operations.
- Industrial utilities are adjacent to the site, and extensive due diligence reports have been completed.
- President and COO of Hyundai Motor Company José Muñoz expressed thrill at partnering with Governor Kemp and the Savannah Joint Economic Development Authority to bring a flexible EV smart factory to Bryan County.
- Hyundai Motor Group is headquartered in Seoul, Korea, employs 250,000 people worldwide, and sells vehicles in 193 countries through 5,000 dealerships and showrooms.
- The new Georgia facility will be highly connected, automated, and flexible, with plans to produce a diverse range of innovative full electric vehicles.
- Details of production models will be shared at later dates.
- Through the battery manufacturing facility, Hyundai Motor Group aims to establish a stable supply chain for EV battery and other EV components in the U.S. market.
- When operations near the planned 2025 start, Hyundai Motor Group will seek local vendors and suppliers.
- Carter Infinger, Chairman of the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority Board of Directors, called the announcement transformative, noting the jobs and investment will reverberate for years across Bryan, Bulloch, Chatham, and Effingham counties.
- Bryan County is the fastest-growing county in Georgia, and the strategic purchase of the Bryan County Megasite in 2021 was the largest in state history.
- The site is adjacent to Interstate 16 with immediate access from I-95 and I-16 to 250 major metro areas.
- The site is less than 30 miles from the Port of Savannah, the single-largest and fastest-growing container terminal in the U.S., with two Class I rail facilities on-site provided by Georgia Central Railway.
- Georgia Department of Transportation Commissioner Russell McMurry stated the news exemplifies Georgia’s collective focus on supporting the electric mobility future and the power of partnership and preparation.
- Georgia Ports Authority Executive Director Griff Lynch noted the new location provides stellar access to global and domestic markets for both assembly components and finished vehicle marketing, endorsing Georgia as a dynamic center for cutting-edge manufacturing.
- Georgia is the Southeastern leader for EV registrations per 1,000 registered automobiles and offers more EV charging outlets per capita than anywhere else in the Southeast.
- Georgia’s Electric Mobility and Innovation Alliance is a public-private-nonprofit initiative focused on growing the electric mobility ecosystem in the state and strengthening Georgia’s position in electrification-related manufacturing and innovation.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) was represented by Project Manager Taylor Walden and Director of Korean Investment Yoonie Kim, alongside the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor JDA, Georgia Department of Transportation, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia Power.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that under Governor Kemp’s leadership, Georgia is at the forefront of electric mobility, and the announcement solidifies the state's spot at the vanguard of the EV transition.
- Hyundai Motor Group plans to implement advanced intelligent manufacturing technologies currently under test at its innovation hub, including intelligent A.I.-equipped manufacturing and data-driven processes.
- The plant will support Hyundai Motor Group’s efforts to achieve its carbon neutrality target through the active use of renewable energy for the facility.
- Since 2020 alone, Georgia has announced more than 20 EV-related projects that will invest more than $13.54 billion and create nearly 18,000 jobs in the state.
