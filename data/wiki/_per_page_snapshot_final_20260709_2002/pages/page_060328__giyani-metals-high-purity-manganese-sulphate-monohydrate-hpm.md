## Facts

- South Africa invested R29.9 million in high-purity manganese sulphate monohydrate (HPMSM) production through the Industrial Development Corporation (IDC).
- Funding supports Giyani Metals' demonstration facility in Johannesburg, which has successfully validated reagent consumption optimisation and crystalliser operation protocols.
- Validation achievements enable progression toward definitive feasibility study completion by Q2 2026.
- The total expanded IDC loan facility is R329.9 million, representing an 11x increase from initial funding levels.
- Commercial application targets the K.Hill project in Botswana.
- Battery-grade HPMSM typically demands minimum purity levels of 99.7-99.95% with iron contamination limits below 100 ppm.
- Raw manganese ore trades at approximately $3.50-4.50 per tonne, while high-purity manganese sulphate monohydrate commands $2,500-3,500 per tonne.
- Combined cobalt and nickel contamination must be below 50 ppm, with optimised particle size distribution of 5-15 micrometers.
- Controlled crystalline structure is required for electrochemical stability, alongside consistent chemical composition across production batches.
- The Johannesburg demonstration plant validated reagent consumption efficiency, minimising chemical inputs per unit of finished HPMSM whilst reducing production costs and environmental footprint.
- Crystalliser operation parameters require precise temperature, pH, and residence time control for target specifications.
- South Africa's government implemented a 150% tax deduction on qualifying EV production capital investments between 2026-2036.
