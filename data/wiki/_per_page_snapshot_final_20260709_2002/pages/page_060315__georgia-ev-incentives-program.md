# Georgia EV Incentives Program

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-30

**Source:** [2026 Georgia EV Incentives Guide: Rebates, Tax Credits, Grants & More | Green Cars Compare](https://greencarscompare.com/incentives/georgia/)  ·  `page_060315`

## Facts

- Georgia offers HOV and HOT lane exemptions for EVs displaying proper alternative fuel license plates, regardless of passenger count; qualified applicants must prove full registration fee payment, and this exemption expires September 30, 2025.
- Georgia Power offers a Time-Of-Use (TOU) rate for residential customers who own an EV and have a smart meter capable of separately measuring EV charger usage.
- Georgia Power offers a $200 rebate for residential customers purchasing and installing a Level 2 EV charger; this rebate expires December 31, 2024.
- Carroll Electric Membership Cooperative offers a "Drive Free for a Year" program providing a $30 monthly energy credit during the first year of new EV ownership, totaling up to $360 in annual energy savings.
- Carroll Electric Membership Cooperative offers a $250 rebate for purchasing and installing a qualified EV charging station.
- Snapping Shoals EMC offers a $30 bill credit for 12 months upon purchasing or leasing an electric vehicle, totaling up to $360 in annual energy savings.
- Snapping Shoals EMC offers a $250 rebate for purchasing and installing a qualified EV charging station; the charger must be UL-rated, and NEMA 14-50 adapters do not qualify.
- Tri-County EMC offers a $30 bill credit for 12 months if an electric vehicle is purchased after January 1, 2020, totaling up to $360 in annual energy savings.
- Tri-County EMC offers a $250 rebate for purchasing and installing a NRTL certified Level 2 (240V) EV charging station.
- Cobb EMC offers a $250 incentive for purchasing and installing an ENERGY STAR certified level 2 EV charging station.
- Cobb EMC offers a Time-of-Use (TOU) rate for residential customers; charging during super off-peak hours with monthly usage below 400 kWh incurs no charges.
- Coweta-Fayette EMC offers a $100 rebate for installing a Level 2 (240 volt) NRTL/UL approved electric vehicle charging station.
- Coweta-Fayette EMC offers a Time-of-Use (TOU) rate for residential customers.
- Diverse Power offers a $50 rebate for each NEMA 14-30 or 14-50 outlet installed to help customers create an EV-ready home.
- GreyStone Power Corporation offers a $250 rebate for residential members purchasing and hardwiring a Level 2, UL rated charger at their home; limit is one rebate per household, and NEMA 14-50 adapters & portable chargers do not qualify.
- GreyStone Power Corporation offers a Time-of-Use (TOU) rate for residential customers.
- Jackson EMC offers a $250 rebate for purchasing a residential Level 2 EV charger.
- Jackson EMC offers a Time-of-Use (TOU) rate for residential customers.
- Sawnee EMC offers a $200 rebate for installing a residential Level 2 EV charger; qualification requires participation in one of Sawnee EMC's TOU, PEV, or CPPR rate plans for at least one year on the account where the charger is installed, and the charger must be UL or ETL listed.
- Sumter EMC offers a $250 rebate for installing a residential Level 2 EV charging station.
- City of LaGrange offers a $250 rebate for installing a residential Level 2 EV charging station.
- Coastal Electric Cooperative offers a Time-of-Use (TOU) rate for residential customers.
- Flint Energies offers a Time-of-Use (TOU) rate for residential customers.
- The U.S. federal government offers new EV buyers a tax credit of up to $7,500 depending on the car’s specs and buyer’s income.
- Pre-owned EV buyers may qualify for a federal tax credit of up to $4,000.
- Homeowners in low-income or rural areas can receive up to $1,000 for installing an EV charger.
- All-electric vehicles purchased new in 2023 or after may be eligible for a nonrefundable federal income tax credit of up to $7,500; excess credit cannot be applied to future tax years.
- Federal tax credit availability depends on the vehicle's MSRP, final assembly location, battery component/critical minerals sourcing, and taxpayer’s modified adjusted gross income (AGI).
- Pre-owned all-electric vehicles purchased in 2023 or after may be eligible for a federal income tax credit equal to 30% of the sale price up to a maximum of $4,000; the credit is nonrefundable and excess cannot be applied to future years.
- To qualify for the pre-owned EV credit, the vehicle must be purchased from a dealer, have a sale price of $25,000 or less, and have a model year two years prior to the current calendar year.
- Individuals in low-income communities and non-urban areas who purchase qualified residential EV chargers after January 1, 2023, may receive a federal tax credit of up to $1,000.
- Qualification for the federal EV charger tax credit requires individuals to be located in low-income community census tracts or non-urban census tracts as defined in IRS Notice 2024-20.
