# Aurubis SE

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2023-01-30

**Source:** [Investment in Recycling Facility Strengthens Georgia's Position in EV | Georgia](https://georgia.org/blogs/accelerated-investment-aurubis-augusta-multi-metal-recycling-facility-strengthens-georgias)  ·  `page_004100`

## Facts

- German multi-metal provider and recycling company Aurubis is among an influx of ancillary companies forming to support Georgia's booming closed-loop electric mobility supply chain.
- Aurubis is one of the world’s leading recyclers of copper, precious metals, and non-ferrous materials.
- In November 2021, Aurubis announced it would build a recycling and secondary smelting facility for multi-metal recycling in Augusta, Georgia.
- Leaders from the Georgia Department of Economic Development were at Aurubis's headquarters in Hamburg, Germany, when the investment was announced.
- The announcement followed a series of meetings between the company and local and state officials that occurred on the home soil of each partner.
- In December 2022, Aurubis increased its investment total for the Augusta facility to €640 million.
- Aurubis projects it will double its throughput volume from 90,000 tons to 180,000 tons of complex recycling materials per year.
- The facility is expected to create 200 jobs.
- The announcement marked the single-largest investment of a German company in Georgia.
- The Augusta site is the first facility of its kind in the U.S. and Aurubis's first greenfield project in the country.
- Aurubis plans to produce materials that can be used in lithium-ion batteries and other electronics valuable in the EV industry.
- Shortly after the December 2022 announcement, Georgia Governor Brian Kemp and state leaders traveled to Germany to celebrate the company’s commitment to Georgia's e-mobility and renewable energy ecosystems.
- Georgia Governor Brian P. Kemp stated that the state's ports system, highly skilled workforce, and pro-business environment attracted Aurubis to Augusta.
- Governor Kemp noted that Aurubis will join more than 500 German companies with operations in Georgia.
- Aurubis CEO Roland Harings said today’s high recycling demand cannot be met by existing facilities.
- Harings stated that metals such as copper can be recycled without loss of quality or performance for use in cars, mobile phones, batteries, cables, and computers.
- Harings described the company's increased commitment to the U.S. as a game-changer for the environment, manufacturers, and the transition to a renewable economy.
- Aurubis broke ground on the recycling plant in June 2022.
- The first phase is expected to come online in mid-2024, with the expansion to be completed in 2026.
- The company was able to quickly start work on the site due to its status as a 'Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) Certified' site.
- GRAD certification is a proactive way for landowners and local developers to catalyze economic growth and increase speed to market for companies.
- Cal Wray, president of the Augusta Economic Development Authority, praised the speed at which Aurubis is building the facility and executing further expansions.
- Wray stated that Aurubis will provide great jobs and be a wonderful addition to the Augusta community.
- Aurubis's presence in Georgia highlights the state's status as a national leader in electric mobility and sustainable battery production.
- Germany is one of Georgia’s top three investment partners and was Georgia’s second-largest job-creating country for Foreign Direct investment in FY22.
- At least 513 German facilities operate in the state, employing more than 25,000 Georgians.
- Georgia is celebrating its 50th year of continuous representation in Germany.
