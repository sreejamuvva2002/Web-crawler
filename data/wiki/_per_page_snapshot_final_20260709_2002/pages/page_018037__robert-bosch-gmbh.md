# Robert Bosch GmbH

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2025-12-31

**Source:** [Manufacturing hub Mexico: Bosch plans smart plant for electronic components - Bosch Media Service](https://www.bosch-presse.de/pressportal/de/en/manufacturing-hub-mexico-bosch-plans-smart-plant-for-electronic-components-153856.html)  ·  `page_018037`

## Facts

- Bosch is investing 100 million euros (120 million U.S. dollars) in a new plant in Celaya, Mexico.
- The company plans to build a new smart factory for electronic components in the central Mexican city of Celaya by 2020.
- Stefan Hartung, member of the Bosch board of management, stated ahead of HANNOVER MESSE: “Bosch is committed to Mexico. The country is and will remain an important market and a hub for our global manufacturing and development network.”
- The goal is to create more than 1,200 additional jobs at the new location in Celaya in the coming years.
- In total, the Bosch Group employs around 16,000 associates in Mexico.
- Bosch is active with all four of its business divisions in Mexico.
- In 2016, the company generated sales of 1.1 billion euros in Mexico.
- The Bosch Group employs roughly 413,000 associates worldwide as of December 31, 2025.
- The company generated sales of 91 billion euros in 2025.
- Bosch's operations are divided into four business sectors: Mobility, Industrial Technology, Consumer Goods, and Energy and Building Technology.
- Bosch aims to use technology to help shape universal trends such as automation, digitalization, electrification, and artificial intelligence.
- Bosch employs some 82,000 associates in research and development.
- The company was set up in Stuttgart in 1886 by Robert Bosch (1861-1942) as “Workshop for Precision Mechanics and Electrical Engineering.”
- Ninety-four percent of the share capital of Robert Bosch GmbH is held by Robert Bosch Stiftung GmbH, a limited liability company with a charitable purpose.
- The remaining shares are held by Robert Bosch GmbH and by a company owned by the Bosch family.
- The majority of voting rights are held by Robert Bosch Industrietreuhand KG, which safeguards the company's long-term existence and financial independence.
