# PHA and Sewon America

**Type:** supplier_relationship  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-03-08

**Source:** [Two Hyundai Suppliers From Atlanta’s Korean Sister City Adding 1,140 More Jobs in Georgia - Global Atlanta](https://www.globalatlanta.com/two-hyundai-suppliers-from-atlantas-korean-sister-city-adding-1140-more-jobs-in-georgia/)  ·  `page_001092`

## Facts

- Gov. Brian Kemp announced two more Korean suppliers for the Hyundai electric-vehicle plant in Bryan County, bringing the tally of supplier-announced jobs to about 4,000.
- The two most recent arrivals represent the prospect of 1,140 new positions for the state.
- On March 6, PHA said it would invest $67 million to put a new factory in Chatham County, creating more than 400 jobs.
- PHA is a tier-one supplier whose door modules, latches, locks and hinges go directly from its factories into the original-equipment manufacturer’s assembly line.
- The OEM assembly line is the Hyundai “Metaplant” that has committed $5.54 billion to coastal Georgia.
- PHA operates a U.S. headquarters in Auburn Hills, Mich., and serves Kia as well as other brands beyond Hyundai.
- Founded in 1985, PHA is based in Daegu, the Atlanta sister city located at the center of a South Korean auto parts manufacturing belt in southern South Korea.
- PHA also has facilities in Vietnam, China, Slovakia, the Czech Republic, India and elsewhere.
- PHA's new location will be one of multiple sites in the United States, located at the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center.
- The Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center site is less than two miles off Interstate 16 and has been vetted through the Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) process.
- GRAD certification includes due diligence on zoning, environmental issues and endangered species investigation, utility services, wetlands delineation.
- More than 8,000 jobs have been created on GRAD sites throughout the state.
- A few weeks before the PHA announcement, longtime Kia Georgia supplier Sewon America announced it would invest $300 million for a stamped body components plant in Rincon.
- The Sewon America investment will create 740 jobs and bring its total employment in the state to 1,600.
- Sewon America is a subsidiary of South Korea-based Sewon Precision Industry Co.
- Sewon America opened its first Georgia plant in LaGrange in 2008 with a $170 million investment and expanded twice to meet growing demand from the nearby Kia plant.
- The most recent investment in the LaGrange facility was a $160 million infusion in 2018, less than a year before Gov. Brian Kemp headed to Korea on his first economic development mission abroad.
- Gov. Brian Kemp said in a news release that on his very first international trip as governor to Korea, he congratulated Chairman Moon-ki Kim on the company’s then-recent expansion and discussed how they could continue to work together.
- Gov. Brian Kemp expressed pride that those discussions led to the announcement as Sewon America continues to grow its Georgia operations.
- With Sewon’s Effingham County factory, all four counties that banded together to create the 2,900-acre site that wooed Hyundai have now received supplier investments.
- The $1.8 billion in commitments announced is already $800 million more than the $1 billion Hyundai initially said its suppliers would bring.
- Rincon Mayor Ken Lee said in the governor’s release that the Sewon project will be the largest in the city’s history.
- The Sewon project will be located in the Grande View Industrial Park.
- Sewon is also based in Daegu and was founded in 1989.
