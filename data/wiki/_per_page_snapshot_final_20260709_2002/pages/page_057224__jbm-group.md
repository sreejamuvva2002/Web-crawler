# JBM Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [JBM acquires major shareholding in Linde-Wiemann - The Machinist](https://www.themachinist.in/news/5193/jbm-acquires-major-shareholding-linde-wiemann)  ·  `page_057224`

## Facts

- JBM Group is a $1.5bn conglomerate with business interests in auto systems & assemblies.
- JBM Group has announced that it has acquired a major shareholding in Germany-based Linde-Wiemann GmbH KG.
- Linde-Wiemann is a leading manufacturer of complex structural components & assemblies to automotive OEMs worldwide.
- The acquisition brings synergies for JBM Group in the form of new products, global customers, and new German technologies to expand its global footprint and add strategic value to its Indian operations.
- Linde-Wiemann is headquartered in Dillenburg, Germany.
- Linde-Wiemann specializes in proprietary technologies in hot forming, tubular assemblies, and the usage of alternate materials such as aluminium for lightweighting in auto systems.
- Linde-Wiemann operates 17 manufacturing plants across 8 countries: Germany, US, China, Spain, Turkey, Czech Republic, Hungary, and South Africa.
- Linde-Wiemann has an employee strength of over 2300 plus.
- Linde-Wiemann brings over 70 years of experience serving global auto OEMs including BMW, Volkswagen, Audi, Skoda, Porsche, Daimler, Ford, and PSA.
- JBM will gain access to Linde-Wiemann's strengths in Electric Vehicle components & systems and battery structure & systems.
- Nishant Arya, Executive Director of JBM Group, stated the acquisition is a strategic fit perfectly in sync with their existing scope of operations & solutions.
- Nishant Arya noted that both entities will bring forward an impeccable combination of frugal engineering & world class technology.
- Nishant Arya indicated they will leverage Linde-Wiemann's experience in Europe coupled with JBM's expertise in the auto component domain.
- JBM and Linde-Wiemann will together focus on developing new products that contribute towards improving vehicle safety and performance through lightweighting, increasing product quality and minimizing time to market for OEMs.
- Nishant Arya takes over additional charges as Chairman of Linde-Wiemann.
- Ulrich Schoof, Chief Executive Officer of Linde-Wiemann, described JBM as a highly complementary partner regarding customers and technologies in the global automotive market.
- Ulrich Schoof stated that the use of joint potentials is an essential motivation for the new partnership.
- Through the new business model, Linde-Wiemann will be able to unlock substantial growth by the adaptation of product competencies in the area of chassis and suspension.
- Linde-Wiemann will extend its holistic system understanding in the field of e-mobility through exchange with JBM's electrical bus division.
- Ravi Sardana, EVP of ICICI Securities Ltd, the financial advisor to JBM Group, said the investment brings together two leading players with strong technology and product portfolio, complementary footprint, and matching ambitions for growth.
- The financing of the entire transaction has been done by ICICI Bank Limited.
- Linde-Wiemann's stamping capabilities are reflected by machines with pressing capacities up to 3000 tonnes.
- Linde-Wiemann brings complex progressive dies and transfer presses along with hydraulic press lines being used for forming.
- The core competence of Linde-Wiemann's in-house tool shop includes extensive knowledge in ultra-high-strength steels and the integration of complex feeding units.
- Linde-Wiemann's current product portfolio includes critical auto systems such as cross car beams, bumpers, rocker panels, and tubular assemblies.
