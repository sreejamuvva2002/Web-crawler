# BlueOval SK LLC

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [BlueOval SK | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/edf/blueoval-sk)  ·  `page_005167`

## Facts

- In December 2024, the Department of Energy announced the closing of a $9.63 billion direct loan to BlueOval SK LLC (BOSK).
- The loan funds the construction of three manufacturing plants.
- The plants will produce batteries for Ford Motor Company’s future Ford and Lincoln electric vehicles (EVs).
- The three facilities created more than 5,000 construction jobs as the plants were being built.
- The facilities will create up to 7,500 BOSK operations jobs.
- Together, the plants will manufacture batteries to enable more than 120 gigawatt hours of U.S. battery production annually.
- The batteries will displace over 455 million gallons of gasoline per year for the lifetime of the vehicles.
- Through Ford’s workforce development team, BOSK is partnering with the Tennessee and Kentucky state governments to develop curricula that supports new technical schools in Kentucky and Tennessee.
- The training programs will prepare community members for quality jobs at those facilities.
- To ensure the availability of skilled union labor for construction, BOSK is managing the projects under addendums to the National Maintenance Agreement.
- BOSK is partnering with the Tennessee and Kentucky state governments to create new community college campuses that will serve technical schools and train community members for operations jobs at the facilities.
- BOSK is committed to building a diverse contractor and supplier network through outreach events, partnerships with relevant government offices, and connections with certification organizations.
- BlueOval SK, LLC (BOSK) is a joint venture between Ford Motor Company (Ford) and SK On.
- The manufacturing facilities are located in Glendale, KY and Stanton, TN.
- The loan program is ATVM.
- The loan type is a Direct Loan.
- The guaranteed loan amount is $9.63 billion, last updated in December 2024.
- Permanent U.S. jobs supported by the project are estimated at 7,500 at the time of closing.
