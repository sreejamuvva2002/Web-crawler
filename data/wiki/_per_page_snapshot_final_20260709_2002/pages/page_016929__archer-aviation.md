## Facts

- Archer Aviation published a press release after market close on Thursday announcing that it had completed construction and received the certificate of occupancy for its manufacturing plant in Georgia.
- The company expects to begin producing its Midnight flying electric vehicles at the beginning of 2025.
- By the end of next year, management expects that the plant will be producing two aircrafts a month.
- Archer Aviation remains in a pre-revenue state with a highly growth-dependent and speculative valuation.
- The company still needs to get regulatory approvals to begin commercial operations for its Midnight vehicles.
- Archer says that it already has $6 billion in orders for its Midnight flying taxis.
- CEO Adam Goldstein recently stated that he expects to begin commercial flights next year, indicating there seems to be a visible path to receiving the necessary regulatory approvals.
- Archer Aviation announced it is entering the defense industry by partnering with defense-tech innovator Anduril to develop aircraft for military applications.
- Archer Aviation stock is up 54.5% across 2024's trading and has a market capitalization of roughly $4.1 billion.
