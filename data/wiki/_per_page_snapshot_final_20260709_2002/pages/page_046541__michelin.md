# Michelin

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-02-04

**Source:** [Michelin’s New Tire Designed For  Better Performance On-Road—And Snow](https://www.forbes.com/sites/scottyreiss/2026/02/04/michelins-new-tire-design-means-better-on-road-and-snow-performance/)  ·  `page_046541`

## Facts

- Michelin is a 130-year-old company known for premium tires with longer wear and solid performance.
- The company has focused on innovating tire compounds, renewable and sustainable materials, tire design, and wearability.
- Michelin aims to produce tires that are completely recyclable and 3D printable.
- Some estimates attribute more than 70% of microplastics found in water to tire dust.
- A few years ago, the company introduced the CrossClimate2 tire designed to go 60,000 miles.
- The CrossClimate2 features a novel chevron-style tire tread and new compounds that are softer and more pliable yet longer lasting even on hot summer roads.
- CrossClimate2 tires are designed to perform as well on wet pavement as dry roads.
- Longer wearability from CrossClimate2 is intended to reduce pollution by shedding less tire dust and microplastics into water and reducing worn tires sent to landfills.
- Following the CrossClimate2, Michelin introduced the X-Ice Snow+ tire.
- The X-Ice Snow+ breaks apart longer tread rows into an interlocking lattice-design tread pattern to create more sipes that absorb snow and slush or disperse water.
- The new tread pattern and compounds for the X-Ice Snow+ are designed to deliver better fuel economy and better range for EV drivers.
- Winter tires like the X-Ice Snow+ can add capability to rear wheel drive cars, allowing small sports cars like a Porsche 911 to drive on snowy, slushy roads.
- Michelin used "piano acoustic tuning" to reduce noise from the X-Ice Snow+ tires, utilizing the directional tread of the lattice design to allow stickiness while reducing noise.
- Drivers typically swap all-season tires for winter tires in the Fall and change them back in the Spring, but putting them on too early or leaving them on too long can destroy a set of tires or reduce the tread life.
- Michelin says the tread life on the X-Ice tires should last longer, giving owners more time to make the switch in warm weather and potentially more winters to drive on them.
- The X-Ice tires feature a chevron-angled lattice pattern with an distinct and elegant look.
- The X-Ice tires do not go on sale until the Spring.
- Michelin expects competitors and copy-cats to follow its lead in tire tread design and performance specs.
