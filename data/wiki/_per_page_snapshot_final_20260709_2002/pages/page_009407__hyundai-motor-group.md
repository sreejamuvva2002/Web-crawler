## Facts

- South Korea’s top automaker Hyundai Motor Group has been granted $1.8 billion in tax breaks and other incentives from the state of Georgia and local governments for building its first U.S plant fully dedicated to electric vehicles in the southeastern state, the largest subsidy package for a vehicle plant in Georgia.
- The state of Georgia announced Friday, local time, that it has decided to offer $1.8 billion in incentives for Hyundai Motor’s EV project.
- The amount is larger than the $1.5 billion incentive package promised to electric carmaker Rivian Automotive Inc. last year.
- Under the signed agreement, Georgia will give Hyundai Motor $472 million in property tax breaks over a 26-year period starting in 2023.
- Georgia will reward $212 million in state income tax credits over five years for creating jobs.
- Hyundai Motor unveiled plans in May to build an EV and battery manufacturing facility at a cost of around $5.54 billion near Savannah in Georgia by 2025 with annual capacity of 300,000 units and create 8,100 new jobs.
- Construction for the new facility will begin next year with mass production expected to start in 2025.
- Georgia kept corporate tax rate at 6 percent for more than 30 years and further cut it to 5.75 percent in 2019 to draw investment.
- Companies are provided with prior information of sites for industrial locations to help decision-making.
- The state of Georgia offers information related to environment, cultural properties, and endangered species linked to the sites.
- The benefits went to companies like Adidas, Kumho Tire, and Starbucks.
- The state of Georgia also offers a “Quick Start” program that provides workforce training for companies creating jobs.
- About 3,000 U.S. and foreign companies have nestled in the region, including Korea’s Kia Corp., SK on.
- More than 100 Korean automotive-related companies are in business in Georgia.
