# HELLA Automotive

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Emergency Vehicle Safety Enhanced with 3D Printing Tech & HELLA](https://www.3dprintingtech.com/case-studies/hella-automotive)  ·  `page_055779`

## Facts

- HELLA was founded in 1899 in Lippstadt, Germany.
- The company has grown to a global presence with over 125 locations in 35 different countries.
- HELLA develops and manufactures lighting and electronic components as well as systems for automobiles.
- The company reported sales of $7.3 billion in the fiscal year 2020/2021.
- HELLA employs 36,000 people globally.
- HELLA is recognized as one of the leading automotive suppliers.
- HELLA Automotive developed an LED headlamp replacement specifically for police and emergency vehicles.
- Existing market replacements failed to allow warning lights (turn signals, brake lights, etc.) to function when flashing lights were enabled.
- Installation procedures for alternative headlamps often caused costly mistakes like drilling errors and poor silicone seals that could damage vehicles.
- Long curing times for epoxy used to lock bezels in place slowed the overall installation process.
- HELLA needed a product easily installed into existing mounts across various vehicle makes, models, and years.
- The solution involved modifying the cylindrical body to fit a collar with two semi-circle pieces that snap together to match each vehicle's headlamp shape.
- Traditional manufacturing methods like injection molding were deemed unviable for producing large varieties of adaptors in small batches.
- The product required a material with higher temperature resistance due to operating conditions.
- HELLA partnered with 3D Printing Tech to evaluate if 3D printing could solve these challenges.
- 3D Printing Tech provided reliable final production run cost estimates early on, allowing HELLA to validate ROI during prototyping.
- Initial material testing used FDM/FFF printing with filaments including ABS, PETG, PolyMide from PolyMaker, and Alfa+ from FiloAlfa.
- Alfa+ filament was selected for providing the best finish and performance in high-temperature environments.
- Filament-based parts did not meet installer expectations regarding appearance compared to traditionally manufactured parts.
- Producing FDM/FFF parts in exotic materials posed quality control challenges for 3D Printing Tech.
- Production shifted to SLS using Nylon12 powder sintered with the MfgPro230 xS from XYZPrinting.
- Parts were printed in natural white Nylon12 powder and dyed black to match HELLA's color specifications.
- The final parts matched the look of traditionally manufactured items, making them well-received by installation teams.
- The semi-circle pieces are easily installed to match headlamp shapes for each vehicle.
- First responders can now use their warning lights when flashing lights are enabled.
- SLS production allows over 1000 parts per build, ensuring HELLA's volume needs are met quickly.
- The product became a profitable, stand-alone addition to HELLA's portfolio that increased market share.
- The improved installation method reduced costs and increased revenue for installers.
- The design created safer driving conditions by ensuring warning lights function properly, leading to reduced accidents and costs.
