# Archer Aviation Inc.

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [GA: Archer Completes Construction of High-Volume Manufacturing Facility in Covington | Trade and Industry Development](https://www.tradeandindustrydev.com/region/georgia/news/ga-archer-completes-construction-high-volume-34089)  ·  `page_017084`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation Inc. recently received the certificate of occupancy for its high-volume manufacturing facility, ARC, which is connected to the Covington Municipal Airport in Georgia.
- Archer is now working to complete the tooling load-in for the initial manufacturing line at the ARC facility.
- Production at ARC is planned to begin in early 2025.
- The goal is to ramp production to a rate of two aircraft per month by the end of 2025.
- Archer built ARC in close partnership with Stellantis, and the two companies are continuing to progress their plans to mass-produce Archer’s Midnight aircraft.
- Stellantis is contributing capital, advanced manufacturing technology and expertise, and experienced personnel with the goal of scaling the facility to 650 aircraft annually by 2030.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp commented that announcing Archer’s choice to build the new manufacturing facility in Newton County marked the start of an incredible relationship and reaffirmed Georgia’s commitment to creating quality jobs and opportunities for years to come.
- Congressman Mike Collins of Georgia, who sits on the House Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure and Committee on Science, Space, and Technology, stated that the completion of Archer’s manufacturing facility in Covington highlights Georgia’s leadership in innovation, advanced manufacturing, and job creation.
- Adam Goldstein, Archer’s founder and CEO, noted that with construction complete, the team is focused on the start of production planned for early next year and expressed pride in the team and partners for completing the project fast and on budget.
- Stellantis Chief Manufacturing and Supply Chain Officer Arnaud Deboeuf commented that supporting Archer’s electric aircraft growth with deep manufacturing expertise is a great opportunity and a significant step in taking clean mobility to the sky.
- ARC is where Archer plans to produce its all-electric vertical take-off and landing aircraft, Midnight.
- The Midnight aircraft is designed to offer passengers a sustainable, low-noise, and safe alternative to ground transportation in congested cities around the world.
