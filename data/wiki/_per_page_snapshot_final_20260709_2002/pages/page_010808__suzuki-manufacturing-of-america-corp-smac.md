# Suzuki Manufacturing of America Corp. (SMAC)

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2008-03-31

**Source:** [U.S. Factory Produces 250,000th Suzuki ATV - Roadracing World Magazine | Motorcycle Riding, Racing & Tech News](https://www.roadracingworld.com/news/us-factory-produces-250000th-suzuki-atv/)  ·  `page_010808`

## Facts

- Suzuki’s Georgia-based ATV manufacturing facility celebrated the production of its 250,000th ATV on March 31st, 2008.
- The facility will celebrate 2008 as the 25th anniversary of the industry’s first 4-wheeled ATV, the 1983 Suzuki LT-125, with anniversary events scheduled throughout the summer.
- Suzuki Manufacturing of America Corp. (SMAC) opened in Rome, Ga., in May 2002 as Suzuki’s only U.S.-based manufacturing facility.
- SMAC initially began producing the Eiger series of ATVs upon opening.
- The facility currently produces KingQuad 400s, 450s, and 750s.
- SMAC employs 300 workers who build ATV frames, mold plastic, and assemble units.
- Production capacity exceeds 200 units per eight-hour shift.
- In the previous year (2007), almost 60,000 ATVs were produced at the facility.
- A commemorative ceremony was held at the Rome factory on March 31, 2008, attended by employees, local dignitaries, media, invited guests, and the Team Rockstar / MSR / Suzuki / Yoshimura / Pro Taper race team.
- SMAC’s ATV Sales Manager Alan Horne highlighted the successful cooperation between Suzuki and its host community of Rome during the ceremony.
- Rome Mayor Wright Bagby attended the ceremony and thanked Suzuki for helping expand the city's manufacturing base over the last 10 years.
- Mayor Bagby stated that Suzuki helped save manufacturing jobs and maintain a higher percentage of manufacturing jobs in Rome than most places in the South or country.
- Kory Ellis, Manager for Team Rockstar / MSR / Suzuki / Yoshimura / Pro Taper, introduced the factory's ATV race team at the event.
- AMA ATV Motocross Series current points leader Dustin Wimmer rode the historic 250,000th ATV off the production line.
- The red KingQuad 750 AXi 4×4 was signed by all factory personnel and local government attendees.
- Mel Harris, Vice President of American Suzuki’s Motorcycle/ATV division, praised Rome, Ga., and SMAC employees for their commitment to the project and Suzuki's dedication to the ATV market.
