# Georgia Department of Economic Development

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2020-10-23

**Source:** [GA is first state to receive four Export Services Awards](https://valdostatoday.com/business-2/2020/10/ga-is-first-state-to-receive-four-export-services-awards/)  ·  `page_054797`

## Facts

- The Georgia Department of Economic Development’s International Trade division earned a President’s “E Star” Award for Export Service from the U.S. Department of Commerce on October 23, 2020.
- The award recognizes continuing significant contributions to an increase in U.S. exports and is the highest honor given to providers of services to U.S. exporters.
- GDEcD is the first entity in the nation to earn a fourth “E” Award for export services.
- The division first earned the President’s “E” Award for Exports in 1975, with subsequent awards received in 2007, 2014, and 2020.
- U.S. Secretary of Commerce Wilbur Ross called earning a fourth award “a feat not achieved by any other entity in the 59-year history of the program.”
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated that Georgia’s International Trade team provides personalized support to small and medium-sized businesses to help them grow in global markets.
- Georgia was ranked the Top State for Business for seven years running as of October 2020.
- GDEcD achieved more than 1,170 client trade successes since receiving the President’s “E Star” Award in 2014.
- These export achievements have contributed to national export expansion efforts that support the U.S. economy and create American jobs.
- GDEcD supports export development through representation in 12 strategic international markets: Brazil, Canada, Chile, China, Colombia, Europe, Israel, Japan, Korea, Mexico, Peru, and the United Kingdom.
- Approximately two-thirds of Georgia trade involves markets where the state has representation.
- Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that the recognition testifies to Georgia’s role in the global economy, the quality of its goods and services, and the commitment of its in-state and abroad teams.
- In September 2020, GDEcD’s International Trade division was awarded a grant from the U.S. Small Business Administration to bolster export assistance for small businesses statewide.
- The Go Global Georgia initiative will help small business exporters stretch international marketing budgets and create new global opportunities.
- Georgia’s logistics network is anchored by two deepwater ports, inland ports, road, and rail, providing exporters with a direct connection to the world.
- On October 22, 2020, the Georgia Ports Authority reported that the Port of Savannah handled an increase of 1.6% in twenty-foot equivalent container units (TEUs) during the first quarter of fiscal year 2021, which began on July 1.
- The Port of Savannah is the top export gateway and fastest-growing container port in the U.S., providing cost-effective connections for Georgia exporters.
- GDEcD serves as the state’s sales and marketing arm and lead agency for attracting new business investment, encouraging industry expansion, locating markets for Georgia products, promoting tourism, and planning economic development resources.
