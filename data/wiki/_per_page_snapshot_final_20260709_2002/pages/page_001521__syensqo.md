# Syensqo

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [North America's largest EV battery material facility coming to Augusta - Augusta Good News](https://augustagoodnews.com/north-americas-largest-ev-battery-material-facility-coming-to-augusta/)  ·  `page_001521`

## Facts

- Officials broke ground on April 25 in south Augusta on the largest electric vehicle battery material facility in North America.
- Syensqo’s PVDF production facility will create 500 construction jobs, 100 highly skilled jobs, and the potential for more than 500 indirect jobs according to a news release from the office of Mayor Garnett Johnson.
- The plant represents a more than $178 million investment by the Department of Energy and a supply chain investment of over $850 million.
- Mayor Garnett Johnson stated that Syensqo chose Augusta-Richmond County to build North America’s biggest factory producing electric vehicle materials.
- The project is part of the Biden-Harris Administration Workforce Hub City Initiative.
- Syensqo was previously part of the Solvay Group and has been located in Augusta for more than two decades.
- PVDF is a thermoplastic fluoropolymer that is used as a lithium-ion binder and separator coating in batteries, according to the Syensqo website.
- The facility will produce Solef, Syensqo’s signature PVDF product, which enables EVs to go farther on each charge, extends battery life, and improves battery safety.
- At full capacity, the new facility is forecast to produce enough PVDF for more than five million EV batteries per year.
- The production volume represents 45% of expected PVDF demand required by 2030.
- The facility will create a new and reliable supply for the rapidly growing U.S. EV battery market, as well as hundreds of jobs throughout the value chain.
- Tom Perez, Senior Advisor and Assistant to the President and Director of the White House Office of Intergovernmental Affairs, joined Mayor Johnson and Syensqo officials at the groundbreaking.
- The City of Augusta and the Augusta Economic Development Authority have worked to invest millions of dollars in tax savings on this project.
