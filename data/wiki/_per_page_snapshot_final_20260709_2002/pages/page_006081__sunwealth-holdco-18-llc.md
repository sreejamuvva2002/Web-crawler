# Sunwealth Holdco 18 LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** charging_infrastructure  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [Project Polo | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/edf/project-polo)  ·  `page_006081`

## Facts

- In January 2025, the U.S. Department of Energy’s Loan Programs Office announced the closing of a $289.7 million loan guarantee to Sunwealth Holdco 18 LLC for Project Polo.
- The loan guarantee will finance the deployment of up to 1,000 solar photovoltaic (PV) systems and battery energy storage systems (BESS).
- The PV and BESS systems are located primarily at commercial and industrial facilities and integrated across up to 27 states.
- Sunwealth has partnered with SYSO Technologies of Boston, Massachusetts, to provide its distributed energy management software platform.
- The software platform will allow the project to function as a virtual power plant (VPP).
- VPPs help facilitate the clean energy transition by optimizing resource utilization and coordinating demand response.
- VPPs aggregate electrified, grid-connected devices such as solar-plus-storage systems, grid-interactive efficient buildings, air conditioners, and electric vehicles and their associated charging infrastructure.
- Sunwealth’s distributed energy resources in concert with SYSO’s software platform will store and generate significant amounts of energy.
- VPPs often provide energy at a lower levelized cost than traditional options, doing so more cleanly while offering consumers greater resilience during grid disruptions.
- The Sunwealth VPP offers advanced management of PV and BESS, enhanced forecasting of PV production and coincident peaks, and aggregation and dispatch of DERs.
- This project is expected to create approximately 3,700 jobs.
- The job creation includes over 1,900 solar and storage installation jobs and over 1,700 operations and maintenance jobs.
- Sunwealth estimates the project will lead to the avoidance of up to 4.07 million metric tons of carbon over the project’s lifetime.
- Sunwealth provides employees salary and benefits that target 120% of the living wage in each office location.
- Sunwealth has created “Sunwealth University,” a four-tier internal training program for employees to promote advancement through the company.
- Sunwealth University provides job-specific training, courses on project finance and development, and more.
- Sunwealth is committed to building relationships with the International Brotherhood of Electrical Workers (IBEW) and the National Electrical Contractors Association.
- Sunwealth is committed to integrating local solar nonprofits and trade groups into its developer workforce.
- The company hosts events with local trades and provides grants to local nonprofit training organizations, such as Green City Force, Emerald Cities, and Browning the Green Space.
- Project Polo is owned by Sunwealth Holdco 18 LLC.
- The owner location is Cambridge, Massachusetts.
- The loan guarantee falls under the Title 17 Loan Program.
- The guaranteed loan amount is $289.7 million, which includes principal and any capitalized interest.
- Permanent U.S. jobs supported by the project are estimated at 1,700 at the time of closing.
- The project is expected to avoid up to 4.07 million metric tonnes of CO2 over its lifetime.
