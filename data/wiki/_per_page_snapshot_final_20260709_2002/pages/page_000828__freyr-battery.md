# FREYR Battery

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-11-11

**Source:** [Battery manufacturer to build plant in Coweta County |](https://capitol-beat.org/2022/11/battery-manufacturer-to-build-plant-in-coweta-county/)  ·  `page_000828`

## Facts

- FREYR Battery is a Europe-based producer of next-generation battery cells.
- FREYR Battery will build a manufacturing plant in Coweta County, Georgia.
- The project represents a $2.57 billion investment.
- The project will create 723 jobs during the next seven years.
- Gov. Brian Kemp announced the FREYR plant project on Friday.
- Gov. Brian Kemp stated that job creators and innovators from around the world choose Georgia for success.
- Gov. Brian Kemp welcomed FREYR to Georgia as a company bringing transformational investments and opportunity to local communities.
- FREYR is dedicated to supporting a domestic supply chain for renewable power sources.
- FREYR's battery cells can be used for stationary energy storage, electric mobility, and additional applications.
- FREYR co-founder and CEO Tom Einar Jensen stated the company is committed to decarbonizing the transportation and energy sectors alongside U.S. partners.
- FREYR expects its U.S. expansion plan to spur job creation and develop localized, decarbonized supply chains to enhance energy security and economic activity.
- Georgia is ramping up to become a leading producer of electric vehicles and EV batteries.
- During the past year, Rivian announced plans for a large EV manufacturing plant near Covington.
- During the past year, Hyundai announced plans for a large EV manufacturing plant near Savannah.
- The Rivian and Hyundai projects are the two largest economic development projects in Georgia's history.
- SK Innovation has built two EV battery manufacturing facilities in Northeast Georgia.
- FREYR's new facility will be named Giga America.
- Giga America will have a planned first-phase production capacity of about 34 gigawatt hours.
- The company is evaluating clean power supply solutions for the new plant, including potential renewable energy from solar installations.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development’s Global Commerce team partnered with the Coweta County Development Authority, the Metro Atlanta Chamber, Georgia EMC, and the state Department of Labor’s Quick Start program to land the project.
