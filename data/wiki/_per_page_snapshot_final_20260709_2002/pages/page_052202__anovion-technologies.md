# Anovion Technologies

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2024-01-31

**Source:** [Bainbridge | Decatur County: Working in Synergy - Georgia Trend Magazine](https://www.georgiatrend.com/2024/01/31/bainbridge-decatur-county-working-in-synergy/)  ·  `page_052202`

## Facts

- Anovion Technologies is one of the first U.S. suppliers of synthetic graphite anode materials for lithium-ion batteries used in electric vehicles.
- Gov. Brian Kemp announced the Anovion project last May (2023).
- The facility will be a 1.5 million-square-foot greenfield building located in the Downrange Industrial Park.
- Production at the Anovion site is set to begin in the first quarter of 2025.
- The project is expected to create 400 jobs.
- The investment was initially announced as $800 million but has since grown to a record $1.2 billion.
- Rick McCaskill, executive director of the Development Authority of Bainbridge/Decatur County, notes that Anovion's supply role ties into the Rivian electric vehicle manufacturing plant near Social Circle, Georgia, which is slated to begin production in 2026.
- The city added infrastructure to an undeveloped section of the Downrange Industrial Park specifically for Anovion, including a water tower, sewer lines, and natural gas services.
- Mayor Edward Reynolds states that the state helped fund these industrial park infrastructure projects, while federal money and support from U.S. Rep. Sanford Bishop assisted with the sewer treatment plant and water tower.
- Bainbridge-Decatur County Chamber of Commerce President Karen Tobin focuses on workforce development to provide a qualified workforce for incoming manufacturing companies like Anovion.
- The chamber partners with Southern Regional Technical College and Abraham Baldwin Agricultural College on youth programs in Decatur County high schools to support local talent pipelines.
- Local companies are willing to foot the bill to certify students through dual enrollment programs if they are not pursuing a four-year university degree.
- Rural Zone Tax Incentives for rehabilitation and job creation were implemented between 2018 and 2022, acting as a "game changer" for attracting industry and downtown growth.
- Between October 2022 and September 2023, $6.2 million in private investments went toward rehabilitating nine buildings downtown, strengthening the local business ecosystem.
- The city completed a $28 million wastewater treatment plant renovation last fall and another $30 million in water and sewer upgrades to support industrial and population growth.
- A second Transportation Special Purpose Local Option Sales Tax (TSPLOST) allotment will fund complete street resurfacing and sidewalk repairs to improve infrastructure for workers and businesses.
- Decatur County experienced a 14% population increase per the last census, making it one of the fastest-growing communities in Southwest Georgia, which impacts local workforce availability.
- Many skilled workers currently live in Decatur County but commute to Tallahassee, Thomasville, Dothan, or Albany for work; retaining them locally is a key focus for economic development leaders.
