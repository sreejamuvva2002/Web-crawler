# Novelis Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2019-10-30

**Source:** [Novelis Invests $36 Million to Expand, Upgrade Aluminum Recycling Capabilities in Greensboro, Ga.](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/novelis-invests-36-million-to-expand-upgrade-aluminum-recycling-capabilities-in-greensboro-ga-300948282.html)  ·  `page_028898`

## Facts

- Novelis Inc. broke ground on a $36 million investment to expand and upgrade recycling capacity at its Greensboro, Ga. facility.
- The investment includes adding state-of-the-art equipment for aluminum scrap recycling.
- The project features a new baghouse for improved dust mitigation.
- The upgrades include enhanced designs for safer and more efficient traffic flow within the facility.
- Novelis expects to complete the expansion project by fall of 2021.
- The investment expands Novelis' capabilities in automotive closed-loop recycling, a process that takes aluminum scrap created during stamping and recycles it for new vehicle production.
- Closing the loop preserves the value of the alloy, reduces cost, minimizes environmental impact, and establishes a secure supply chain.
- The Greensboro facility will continue to recycle used beverage cans for the beverage packaging industry.
- Marco Palmieri, SVP and President of Novelis North America, stated that automakers are choosing aluminum as a sustainable material of choice, prompting investments to increase recycled content in new vehicles and reduce carbon emissions.
- Palmieri noted the investment aligns with Novelis' purpose of "Shaping a Sustainable World Together" and demonstrates a commitment to helping customers meet their sustainability targets.
- The facility upgrades will enhance safety systems by more effectively separating pedestrian walkways from mobile equipment traffic.
- Beatriz Landa, Novelis Greensboro Plant Manager, stated the team consists of 145 employees in Greensboro with a proud tradition of offering innovative solutions in aluminum recycling.
- Landa added that the investment enables the facility to continue modernizing its capabilities to serve an even broader customer base for years to come.
- Georgia Governor Brian P. Kemp congratulated Novelis on the expansion, noting that any home-based company expansion is good news for business in Georgia.
- Governor Kemp highlighted Novelis' 40-year history in Greensboro has created countless opportunities for hardworking Georgians in the region.
- Congressman Jody Hice stated manufacturing is an important ingredient to Georgia's thriving economy and expressed thrill at Novelis growing its footprint in the 10th District.
- Hice described Novelis as a global leader in the aluminum industry and the largest recycler of aluminum in the world.
- The Greensboro facility opened in 1980 and is responsible for pioneering the majority of Novelis' recycling technology.
- Novelis Inc. is headquartered in Atlanta, Georgia, operates 23 facilities in 9 countries, has approximately 11,000 employees, and recorded $12.3 billion in revenue for its 2019 fiscal year.
- Novelis is a subsidiary of Hindalco Industries Limited, an industry leader in aluminum and copper, and the metals flagship company of the Aditya Birla Group, a multinational conglomerate based in Mumbai, India.
- Novelis partners with customers in the automotive, beverage can, and specialties industries to deliver sustainable lightweight aluminum solutions throughout North America, Europe, Asia, and South America.
