# South Korea-U.S. Trade & Investment Relationship

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-25

**Source:** [Tennessee-South Korea trade ties strained by auto plant deportations](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/tennessee-south-korea-trade-ties-100318727.html)  ·  `page_008019`

## Facts

- Michael Kofoed, an assistant professor of economics at The University of Tennessee Knoxville, stated that the relationship between South Korea and the U.S. will never be the same following recent events.
- South Korea has opened an investigation into whether human rights violations were committed during a Sept. 4 raid at LG Energy Solution's joint battery plant with Hyundai Motor in Georgia that detained 475 workers, more than 300 of whom were South Korean nationals.
- Following the raid, Korean employees at LG Energy Solution's Spring Hill, Tenn., operation left the U.S., citing growing concerns over visa status and legal uncertainty.
- LG Energy Solution told the Tennessean that its plant is still operating as usual despite reports of departing employees.
- An LG Energy Solutions spokesperson stated the company remains committed to its U.S. projects, will continue necessary investments and business, and noted the U.S. is an important market.
- LG Energy Solution plans to locate half of its total global battery cell-making capacity in North America in the mid- to long-term.
- South Korean President Lee Jae Myung stated at a Sept. 11 news conference that as things stand, Korean businesses will hesitate to make direct investments in the United States.
- Many Georgia detainees held Electronic System for Travel Authorization visa waivers or B-1 temporary business traveler visas, which prohibit foreign nationals from working in construction or equipment installation in the U.S.
- More than 20 South Korean companies have collectively invested nearly $6 billion in Tennessee as of Oct. 2024, according to the Tennessee Department of Economic and Community Development.
- Michael Kofoed noted that injecting fear and uncertainty into the business environment effectively raises the cost of doing business in the United States, similar to raising taxes on companies like LG.
- U.S. Homeland Security Secretary Kristi Noem acknowledged the trade policy impact but stated the deportations signal policy consistency that could ultimately bolster investor confidence.
- Much of the South Korean investment in Tennessee revolves around auto industry second-tier suppliers supporting electric vehicle production for local OEMs like Ford and General Motors.
- Wisarut Suwanprasert, an Associate Professor of Economics at Middle Tennessee State University, stated that while Korean investment might not be noticed by household consumers, it has created thousands of jobs.
- Wisarut Suwanprasert believes the current diplomatic issue is minor and suggested the Trump administration should provide a formal apology to make things smoother.
- Michael Kofoed does not anticipate a U.S. apology nor suggest South Korea would try to restore the bond between partners given the current trajectory.
- Kofoed expects less willingness from foreign partners to do foreign direct investment in the U.S. due to the current trajectory.
- Amid shifting global dynamics and U.S. trade and immigration policies under President Donald Trump, countries like South Korea and Japan have increasingly explored economic ties with other major powers including China.
- Kofoed stated that because the U.S. is receding as a business partner, countries like South Korea are being drawn into the Chinese sphere of influence.
- Kristi Noem argued that tough actions mean there is no uncertainty about the Trump administration's policies and claimed investors prefer a stable, rule-bound environment where immigration laws are enforced evenly to protect American jobs long-term.
- Kofoed expects long-term trust problems, describing the situation as a conflict between trusting a partner and having that trust betrayed regarding investment plans.
- Ultium Cells is a joint venture between Korean-owned LG Energy Solutions and General Motors that manufactures low-cost electric vehicle battery cells known as lithium iron phosphate (LFP).
- Ultium Cells announced a $2.3 billion investment in July, located onsite at the GM manufacturing plant in Spring Hill.
- LG Chem is a global leading chemical company specializing in petrochemicals, advanced materials and life sciences, and is the parent company of LG Energy Solutions following a diversification in December 2020.
- In Nov. 2022, LG Chem announced it would invest roughly $3.2 billion to build a cathode materials plant for electric vehicle batteries in Clarksville.
- Dongwha Electrolyte USA is a subsidiary of Dongwha Enterprise Company that specializes in the production of electrolytes used in lithium batteries.
- In 2023, Dongwha Electrolyte USA announced a $70 million investment into creating a Clarksville production facility.
- Enchem America invested $152.2 million in June 2023 to create an electrolyte manufacturing site for electric batteries in Brownsville.
- Enchem America is a subsidiary of Enchem Company, and its Tennessee operations support its U.S. headquarters located in Commerce, Georgia.
- Hanon Systems specializes in ventilation and air conditioning and announced a $170 million expansion in August 2023 to create a production facility in Huntington Park.
- Hanon Systems' Tennessee operations supply thermal solutions for automakers across North America.
- In January 2024, Unitech announced it would invest roughly $30 million to debut its first U.S. facility in Tennessee.
- Unitech specializes in thermal adhesive and battery compression pad production for electric vehicle manufacturers across the Southeast, and also manufactures industrial adhesives and sealers.
- To support operations at Ford's BlueOval City, the Aluko Group announced a $36.3 million facility to produce aluminum battery casings for the automaker in December 2024.
- The Aluko Group consists of Aluko, Hyundai Aluminum, Gogang Aluminum, Alutec, KPTU, Hyundai Aluminum Vina and Alutec Vina.
