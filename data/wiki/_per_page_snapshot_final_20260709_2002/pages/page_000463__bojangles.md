# Bojangles

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** charging_infrastructure  ·  **Published:** 2026-05-20

**Source:** [Bojangles Opens First EV Charging Station in Savannah - The EV Report](https://theevreport.com/bojangles-opens-first-ev-charging-station-in-savannah)  ·  `page_000463`

## Facts

- Bojangles has opened its first EV charging station at 4401 Ogeechee Road in Savannah, Georgia.
- The Savannah site serves as the opening location in a planned national rollout developed with XLR8 America and Energy and Environmental Design Services.
- Bojangles operates more than 870 restaurants across 23 states.
- The company is positioning the Savannah site as the first step in extending its footprint into mobility infrastructure.
- The Savannah hub combines Level 2 and Level 3 charging with dine-in service, framing the stop as a “charge-and-dine” occasion.
- The site mixes Level 2 (medium-speed) and Level 3 (high-speed) chargers to give drivers a choice between longer meal sessions and shorter stops.
- Bojangles reports targeted uptime of more than 97 percent across the installed equipment.
- The company calls the format “charge-and-dine,” designed to give drivers a reason to stay during a charging session rather than treating it as transactional.
- Bojangles is approaching the rollout as a category move rather than a one-off pilot, with additional sites planned in key markets.
- The partnership brings XLR8 America as a turnkey infrastructure provider, alongside Energy and Environmental Design Services handling installation and engineering work.
- XLR8 America operates on a host-side model in which the charging developer funds, owns, and operates the equipment.
- Richard Del Valle, Chief Information Officer at Bojangles, stated that driving trends and expectations are changing, and EV charging turns waiting time into an asset for relaxation and dining.
- Frank O’Connor, CEO of XLR8 America, added that their philosophy is to charge where you park, and together they are building a national network that makes EV charging effortless by combining it with dining stops.
- Bojangles has not disclosed a specific site count, deployment timeline, or the next markets for the rollout.
