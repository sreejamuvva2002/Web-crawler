# Enchem

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Enchem to Receive $8 Million in U.S. Tariff Refunds... Cumulative Tax Benefits Expected to Reach $68 Million - The Asia Business Daily](https://www.asiae.co.kr/en/article/2026061508045304202)  ·  `page_008107`

## Facts

- On June 15, Enchem announced that its U.S. subsidiary is currently undergoing the tariff refund process under the International Emergency Economic Powers Act (IEEPA).
- The total IEEPA tariff refund amount filed by the U.S. subsidiary is $5.45 million (approximately KRW 800 million).
- Approximately $3.44 million (about KRW 500 million), or around 63% of the filed amount, has already been refunded.
- The remaining tariff refund amount is expected to be refunded in sequence according to U.S. government administrative procedures.
- This refund measure follows a ruling by the U.S. Court of International Trade (CIT).
- The alleviated tariff burden from local distribution and import processes is expected to contribute to improved financial soundness and cash flow for the U.S. subsidiary.
- Enchem's U.S. subsidiary secured accumulated Advanced Manufacturing Production Credit (AMPC) benefits from 2023 to 2025 amounting to approximately $29.78 million (about KRW 4.5 billion).
- An additional $15 million (about KRW 2.3 billion) in AMPC benefits is expected to be received this year.
- The cumulative four-year AMPC benefit is projected to reach a total of $45 million (about KRW 6.8 billion).
- The North American battery market is expanding beyond electric vehicles (EVs) to include the energy storage system (ESS) sector.
- Explosive expansion of artificial intelligence (AI) data centers and increased investment in power infrastructure are driving a surge in ESS demand across North America.
- The surge in ESS demand is fueling the need for long-life and high-safety electrolytes, which are key factors determining battery lifespan and safety.
- Enchem plans to accelerate its entry into the ESS market by leveraging the competitiveness it has already established in the electrolyte market for EVs.
- An Enchem representative stated that the tariff refunds and AMPC benefits resulted from a global supply chain strategy involving calculated risks and proactively establishing a local electrolyte production system in Georgia, United States.
- The U.S. battery industry increasingly values transparency in raw material sourcing and supply chain stability centered on allied nations.
- According to the Enchem representative, the value of its local production base in Georgia will continue to rise as these industry priorities grow.
