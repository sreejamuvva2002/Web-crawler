# HL-GA Battery Co.

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-09

**Source:** [Georgia Governor's Electric Mobility Dream for Georgia Tested After South Koreans Detained in Raid](https://www.usnews.com/news/us/articles/2025-09-09/georgia-governors-electric-mobility-dream-for-georgia-tested-after-south-koreans-detained-in-raid)  ·  `page_018785`

## Facts

- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp's pro-investment stance collided with his tough-on-immigration rhetoric after immigration authorities raided a battery plant construction site and detained foreign workers.
- Approximately 475 people were held in the raid, including more than 300 South Korean nationals.
- The raid targeted the construction site for a battery plant to be operated by HL-GA Battery Co., a joint venture between Hyundai and LG Energy Solution.
- The battery plant is slated to open next year (2026).
- The facility is part of a $7.6 billion complex planned to eventually employ 8,500 people.
- The complex was initially announced and later expanded following Kemp's trips to South Korea to court Hyundai officials.
- The detentions sparked questions in South Korea regarding U.S.-South Korea relations, particularly concerning Hyundai and its partners.
- Hyundai stated it is investing $26 billion in the United States from 2025 to 2028.
- This $26 billion investment includes the Ellabell plant, located about 50 miles (80 kilometers) west of Savannah, where Hyundai Motor Group manufactures electric vehicles.
- The investment also covers a separate $5 billion battery joint venture in Cartersville, Georgia.
- Additional investments include a steel mill in Donaldsonville, Louisiana, a robotics hub, an expansion of the Hyundai plant in Montgomery, Alabama, and the Kia Corp. plant in West Point, Georgia.
- Kemp's office issued a written statement on Friday asserting: "In Georgia, we will always enforce the law, including all state and federal immigration laws. All companies operating within the state must follow the laws of Georgia and our nation."
- Kemp cannot run for governor again in 2026 and passed up a U.S. Senate race, but is entertaining discussions about a potential 2028 presidential run.
- Kemp's office did not immediately respond to questions regarding whether the raid would have a long-term impact on Hyundai's investments.
- The state Department of Public Safety coordinated with ICE to support the raid.
- This is Kemp's second term as governor, during which he has declared a goal of making Georgia the "electric mobility capital of America."
- Kemp has a history of tough-on-immigration rhetoric, including a 2018 ad where he said he would use his pickup truck to round up "criminal illegals," and he backed a state law requiring local jails to check prisoners' immigration status.
- Kemp is aligning more closely with President Donald Trump's administration on immigration enforcement, having last month announced the mobilization of 75 National Guard soldiers to support ICE operations in Georgia.
- Kemp also announced sending more than 300 National Guard members to participate in Trump's law enforcement takeover in Washington, D.C.
- Kemp has traveled twice to South Korea to meet with Hyundai and LG Group executives.
- Kemp disputes claims that tax dollars subsidized the jobs of detained workers; his administration states none of the $2.1 billion in projected state and local incentives for the Hyundai complex directly benefits subcontractors employing detainees.
- Georgia's Department of Economic Development stated: "Without exception, the Department expects anyone doing business in Georgia to follow federal and state laws."
- Kemp has clashed with Democratic U.S. Sen. Jon Ossoff over who deserves credit for Georgia's green energy boom.
- Kemp disputes that incentives signed by President Joe Biden spurred investment, claiming industries were already moving to Georgia before the 2022 Inflation Reduction Act, though he acknowledges the rush accelerated after Biden signed the climate law.
- According to E2, an environmental business group, 33 additional green energy projects announced by the end of 2024 were the most nationwide.
- Green energy projects in Georgia top $20 billion in value, pledging more than 25,000 jobs.
- Hyundai has continued its investment commitments even as President Trump and congressional Republicans have rolled back electric vehicle incentives.
