# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [How to profit off Hyundai's $12 Billion Metaplant in GA](https://the-deal-yard.beehiiv.com/p/how-to-profit-off-hyundai-s-12-billion-metaplant-in-ga)  ·  `page_000452`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group has committed $12.6 billion to Georgia, making it the largest publicly backed economic development project in state history.
- The Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) is located in Bryan County near Ellabell, approximately 25 minutes west of Savannah.
- The facility is a next-generation EV manufacturing campus rapidly reshaping the industrial real estate landscape along the Savannah–Interstate 16 corridor.
- The Ellabell facility is often described as a “museum of modern manufacturing.”
- The plant has a target capacity of 500,000 EVs annually.
- The facility projects 8,500 direct employees.
- Approximately 7,000 additional satellite workers are expected.
- The plant will utilize over 850 robots.
- The facility will deploy 300 automated guided vehicles (AGVs).
- The plant uses AI-driven logistics and procurement systems.
- The plant produces the Ioniq 5 and Ioniq 9 models.
- The vehicles are built on an advanced 800-volt EV architecture designed for ultrafast charging.
- The facility operates as an automation-first, robotics-driven, just-in-time precision production site rather than a labor-intensive legacy factory.
- A $4.3 billion joint venture battery facility with LG Energy Solution is located next to the vehicle assembly plant.
- The joint venture battery plant will begin manufacturing lithium-ion cells for Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis models in 2026.
- Battery manufacturing's energy intensity, material heaviness, logistical complexity, and capital density force supplier proximity and create industrial clusters.
- As of early 2026, over 20 Hyundai suppliers have announced facilities in Georgia.
- Billions in committed supplier capital are being deployed.
- Thousands of supplier jobs have been announced.
- Supplier expansion is occurring across multiple counties along the I-16 corridor.
- The Savannah Harbor–I-16 Corridor serves as an EV manufacturing spine linking Bryan County, Chatham County, Effingham County, and Bulloch County.
- The ecosystem is expanding through logistics optimization, battery integration, port access, and energy infrastructure rather than traditional labor intensity.
- The Port of Savannah is one of the fastest-growing container ports in the United States.
- The port adjacency enables inbound shipments of battery materials, electronics, and components for the Metaplant.
- The port adjacency facilitates outbound exports of finished EVs from the Metaplant.
- The combination of port access, battery manufacturing, and vehicle assembly creates a logistics multiplier effect for the corridor.
- Strongest land demand for the 0–20 mile radius around Bryan County/Ellabell focuses on 10–40 acre tracts with I-16 access for sequencing and just-in-time suppliers.
- Land demand in the 20–60 mile radius (Pooler, Effingham, Statesboro) targets tier 1 and tier 2 suppliers needing power-capable sites and 200K–500K SF pad-ready parcels.
- There is a 3–5 year value-creation window for entitled, infrastructure-ready land in the corridor.
- The Metaplant's hyper-automation drives demand for Industrial Outdoor Storage (IOS) outside the facility fence.
- High-probability IOS applications include trailer drop yards, rack and dunnage storage, battery material staging, contractor laydown yards, and logistics overflow.
- Key IOS nodes are located at I-16 interchanges near Ellabell, the I-95/Pooler logistics belt, and parcels adjacent to the battery plant.
- Successful brokers in the corridor will track Hyundai supplier announcements, map power capacity and transmission upgrades, identify battery-adjacent zoning, and position themselves as EV corridor specialists.
- The supply chain is developing in four waves: the Hyundai anchor, battery integration, tier 1/tier 2 clustering, and logistics/overflow services.
- Key risks to monitor include EV demand volatility, federal tax credit shifts, trade policy changes, labor and immigration enforcement disruptions, and power capacity constraints.
- The facility's automation-heavy design reduces downside risk compared to traditional labor-driven auto factories.
- The emerging ecosystem will feature high capital per square foot, lower labor density, heavy robotics integration, battery-first supply chains, and port-optimized logistics.
- The Savannah–Bryan County–Interstate 16 corridor is becoming one of the most advanced EV manufacturing ecosystems in North America.
