# Suzuki Motor Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Suzuki Announces Technology Strategy for 10 Years Ahead | GLOBAL NEWS | Global Suzuki](https://www.globalsuzuki.com/globalnews/2024/0717.html)  ·  `page_010889`

## Facts

- Suzuki Motor Corporation announced a technology strategy for the next 10 years aimed at realizing "technology that minimizes energy consumption" from manufacturing to recycling.
- The company aims for a carbon-neutral world while providing transportation globally.
- Suzuki will further evolve its light-weight and safety body "HEARTECT" to reduce CO2 emissions during use, resources, and production emissions.
- Suzuki will work to minimize energy consumption through weight reduction technology.
- Suzuki will develop electric vehicles that minimize energy consumption by combining components embodying "Sho-Sho-Kei-Tan-Bi" such as a small and efficient electric unit, and a small and light battery.
- The goal is to provide customers with the most energy-efficient electric vehicles based on the renewal energy ratio and usage conditions in each country and region.
- In 2023, Suzuki developed a high-efficiency engine (the Z12E Engine) that achieved a maximum thermal efficiency of 40%.
- Suzuki plans to extend this high-efficiency engine worldwide and minimize energy consumption by carbon-neutral fuel and next-generation hybrids.
- Suzuki will develop and provide "SDV right," an affordable software-defined vehicle system that minimizes energy consumption with "Sho-Sho-Kei-Tan-Bi."
- The SDV system will use the best mix of wired and wireless (OTA) updates to make software easier to update.
- Suzuki will share hardware to reduce component costs and reuse software to reduce development costs in its SDV development.
- Suzuki aims to achieve minimal energy consumption with a circular economy by designing products for easy disassembly, recycling, and reuse, moving away from the linear economy model.
