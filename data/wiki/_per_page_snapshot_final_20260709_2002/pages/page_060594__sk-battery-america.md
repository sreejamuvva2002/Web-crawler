# SK Battery America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-30

**Source:** [SK On to Lay Off Nearly 1,000 Workers at Georgia Battery Plant Amid EV Market Slowdown](https://evmagz.com/sk-on-to-lay-off-nearly-1000-workers-at-georgia-battery-plant-amid-ev-market-slowdown/)  ·  `page_060594`

## Facts

- SK On will lay off 958 employees at its battery cell production facility in Commerce, Georgia.
- The layoffs reduce about 37% of the plant’s workforce.
- The job cuts affect workers at the SK Battery America facility.
- The plant currently employs 2,566 people.
- The plant produces battery cells used in several electric vehicle models as well as energy storage systems.
- The factory has supplied batteries for vehicles including the now-discontinued Ford F-150 Lightning pickup.
- The factory has supplied batteries for the U.S. version of Volkswagen’s ID.4.
- The factory has supplied batteries for models from Hyundai and Kia.
- The site also manufactures battery cells used in stationary battery energy storage systems.
- SK Battery America stated that the layoffs were part of an operational adjustment.
- SK Battery America said it is aligning operations to market conditions.
- SK Battery America remains committed to maintaining its presence in Georgia.
- SK Battery America remains committed to continuing to develop a domestic battery supply chain in the United States.
- The layoffs come amid broader uncertainty in the U.S. electric vehicle market.
- Policy changes affecting EV incentives are contributing to the market uncertainty.
- The $7,500 federal tax credit for electric vehicle purchases expired on Sept. 30, 2025.
- Emissions regulations for internal combustion vehicles were relaxed.
- Georgia U.S. Senator Jon Ossoff criticized the policy changes.
- Ossoff said the policy changes had affected the state’s emerging battery manufacturing sector.
- Ossoff stated: “Let’s be clear: these were battery manufacturing jobs and now they’re gone.”
- Ossoff stated: “As predicted, Trump’s war on electric vehicles is hurting Georgia’s economy.”
- Ossoff stated: “We were booming and building new plants. Now Georgians are losing their jobs.”
- SK Innovation began constructing the battery plant in Commerce in 2019.
- SK Innovation later added a second facility to the site.
- The total investment brought to the site is about $2.6 billion.
- The site was designed to produce up to 22 gigawatt-hours of battery capacity annually.
- The annual capacity is enough to power roughly 300,000 electric vehicles.
- Mass production at the first plant began in January 2022.
- The first plant initially supplied batteries for Volkswagen’s ID.4.
- Production at the second plant started in early 2023.
- The second plant produces cells for the Ford F-150 Lightning.
- SK Battery America became part of SK On after SK Innovation reorganized its battery business in 2021.
