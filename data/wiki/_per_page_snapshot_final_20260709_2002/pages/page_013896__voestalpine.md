## Facts

- Voestalpine’s first fiscal quarter through June turned out to be satisfactory for the group.
- Order intake from the construction, mechanical engineering, and consumer goods industry market segments weakened.
- Chief executive Herbert Eibensteiner stated that central banks’ measures to fight inflation seem to have reached the manufacturing industry.
- Demand from the construction, mechanical engineering, and consumer goods segments is expected to remain weak throughout the rest of the 2023/24 business year.
- The automotive segment in Voestalpine’s customer portfolio is performing stably.
- Voestalpine expects this stable development in its automotive segment to continue for the rest of the business year.
- Supply bottlenecks faced by the automotive industry for over two years are increasingly dissipating.
- Voestalpine expects the strongest economic slowdown in Europe.
- Voestalpine is more optimistic for the economy in North America.
- Compared with the June-2022 quarter, revenue decreased by 4.3% to €4.4 billion ($4.8 billion).
- EBITDA declined by 42.6% to €505 million.
- CEO Herbert Eibensteiner cautioned that the financial comparison is against the best quarter in the group’s history.
