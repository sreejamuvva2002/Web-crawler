# Archer Aviation Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-01-02

**Source:** [Archer completes construction of high-volume manufacturing facility - Aerospace Manufacturing and Design](https://www.aerospacemanufacturinganddesign.com/news/archer-completes-construction-high-volume-manufacturing-facility/)  ·  `page_017075`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation Inc. received the certificate of occupancy for its high-volume manufacturing facility, ARC.
- The ARC facility is connected to the Covington Municipal Airport in Georgia.
- Archer is working to complete the tooling load-in for the initial manufacturing line at this facility.
- Production of its all-electric vertical take-off and landing (eVTOL) aircraft, Midnight, is planned to begin in early 2025.
- The goal is to ramp production to a rate of two aircraft per month by the end of the year.
- Archer built ARC in close partnership with Stellantis.
- The two companies are continuing to progress their plans to mass-produce Archer’s Midnight aircraft.
- Stellantis is contributing capital to the project.
- Stellantis is contributing advanced manufacturing technology and expertise to the project.
- Stellantis is providing experienced personnel to help scale the facility.
- The goal is to scale the ARC facility to produce 650 aircraft annually by 2030.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp commented on Archer's choice to build the new manufacturing facility in Newton County, highlighting the creation of quality jobs and opportunities for Georgians.
- Congressman Mike Collins of Georgia, who sits on the House Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure and the Committee on Science, Space, and Technology, highlighted Georgia’s leadership in innovation, advanced manufacturing, and job creation driven by the facility.
- Adam Goldstein, Archer’s founder and CEO, stated that the completion of the facility marks a shift from R&D into commercialization, with production starting in early 2025.
- Adam Goldstein noted that construction was completed fast and on budget.
- Arnaud Deboeuf, Stellantis Chief Manufacturing and Supply Chain Officer, commented that supporting Archer’s electric aircraft growth leverages Stellantis's deep manufacturing expertise as a significant step toward taking clean mobility to the sky.
