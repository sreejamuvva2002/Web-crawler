## Facts

- voestalpine in Cartersville, Georgia in the US chose AP&T’s extensive service agreement to secure production capacity and minimize unplanned downtime of press-hardening lines.
- The facility makes structural parts for some of the world’s chief OEMs whose production relies on having a constant flow of parts and components.
- Unplanned downtime in any part of the delivery chain can have dire consequences for voestalpine and its customers.
- Fredrik Stålberg is the Managing Director and CTO of voestalpine in Cartersville.
- About a year ago, voestalpine was experiencing operational pressure with production running around the clock manufacturing enormous volumes.
- To minimize risk from potential operational issues, voestalpine decided to enter a service agreement with AP&T.
- The agreement covers two press-hardening lines from AP&T that have been in operation since 2017 and are critical to the facility’s production capacity.
- voestalpine originally entered a basic service agreement for the two lines but soon discovered the value of a more comprehensive setup.
- Magnus Svenningsson is the Director of Aftermarket Sales at AP&T.
- The agreement signed in the spring of 2022 includes regular preventative maintenance, 24/7 online support and on-site assistance with priority response time at a fixed monthly cost.
- voestalpine now has access to quick, qualified support in the evenings and on weekends, benefiting greatly from remote resolutions and short-notice technician visits.
- Tobias Kronstedt is the Aftermarket Service Manager for AP&T in the US.
- The new agreement allows AP&T to provide voestalpine with exactly the support needed to secure production.
- voestalpine is considering taking advantage of more aspects of AP&T's service offering in the future.
- voestalpine is currently in discussions with AP&T about designing an agreement to guarantee uptime, potentially by handing over stocking spare machine parts to AP&T for guaranteed delivery times.
- This potential future arrangement could free up warehouse space and reduce tied-up capital for voestalpine.
- Andrea Clark is the President of AP&T North America.
