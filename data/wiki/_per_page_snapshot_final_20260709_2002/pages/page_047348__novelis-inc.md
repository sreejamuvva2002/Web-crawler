# Novelis Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2019-10-30

**Source:** [Updated: Novelis invests in aluminum recycling capacity - Recycling Today](https://www.recyclingtoday.com/news/novelis-expands-automotive-scrap-consumption-capacity/)  ·  `page_047348`

## Facts

- Novelis Inc. is an Atlanta-based aluminum company.
- Novelis broke ground on a $36 million investment at its Greensboro, Georgia facility.
- The investment is designed to expand and upgrade the facility's recycling capacity.
- Novelis is adding equipment for aluminum scrap recycling at the Greensboro facility.
- A new baghouse is being added for improved dust mitigation.
- The facility's design is being enhanced to ensure safer and more efficient traffic flow.
- Novelis expects the expansion to be completed by the fall of 2021.
- The investment will expand Novelis' capabilities to recycle automotive stamping scrap in a closed loop.
- The Greensboro facility continues to recycle used beverage cans (UBCs) for the beverage packaging industry.
- Novelis currently has closed-loop recycling programs in place with Ford and other automotive customers.
- Novelis is exploring new opportunities to establish closed-loop recycling partnerships.
- Daniel Groce, lead of Communications and Government Affairs at Novelis, stated that closing the loop preserves the value of the alloy, reduces costs, minimizes environmental impact, and establishes a more secure supply chain.
- Daniel Groce noted that the initial startup cost of establishing a closed-loop recycling program with an automaker can be challenging, but these investments are worthwhile.
- Daniel Groce stated there will be a nominal increase in capacity, but the primary focus is enabling the Greensboro facility to recycle automotive scrap and modernizing the plant.
- Daniel Groce noted that the availability of automotive aluminum scrap is increasing as more automakers turn to aluminum to lightweight vehicles.
- Marco Palmieri, senior vice president and president of Novelis North America, stated that Novelis is investing in recycling capabilities to increase the amount of recycled content in new vehicles and reduce carbon emissions as automakers choose aluminum.
- Marco Palmieri stated the investment aligns with Novelis' purpose of Shaping a Sustainable World Together and demonstrates a commitment to helping customers meet sustainability targets.
- The facility upgrades will enhance safety systems, including a new design that more effectively separates pedestrian walkways from mobile equipment traffic.
- Beatriz Landa is the Novelis Greensboro plant manager.
- The Greensboro facility has a team of 145 employees.
- Georgia Gov. Brian P. Kemp stated that any time a home-based company expands, it is good news for business in Georgia.
- Gov. Kemp noted Novelis' 40-year history in Greensboro has created countless opportunities for hardworking Georgians in the region.
- Congressman Jody Hice stated the expansion of the Greensboro plant is indicative of its sustained success and expressed a look forward to a flourishing partnership.
