# Carcoustics Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2017-06-14

**Source:** [Auto industry supplier bringing 200 jobs to Buford - Gainesville Times](https://www.gainesvilletimes.com/news/auto-industry-supplier-bringing-200-jobs-to-buford/)  ·  `page_018331`

## Facts

- Carcoustics, a supplier to the automotive industry, will create 200 jobs and invest $6 million in a new Buford facility over the next five years.
- The newly created jobs will include positions in manufacturing.
- Georgia Governor Nathan Deal announced the investment and job creation on Tuesday, June 14, 2017.
- Carcoustics is a German company establishing its new plant in Georgia.
- Stefan Vallentin serves as the project manager at Carcoustics for this facility.
- The company selected Georgia due to great international transport connections and logistical proximity between BMW in South Carolina and Mercedes in Alabama.
- Carcoustics Group utilizes a business model that combines a central research center with a manufacturing structure to maintain proximity to major markets.
- Governor Deal stated that Georgia provides automotive suppliers like Carcoustics with resources necessary to compete worldwide, citing a well-trained workforce, a thriving automotive industry, and the nation’s top business environment.
- Governor Deal expressed pride in another German company locating in Georgia as part of ongoing efforts to build international relationships.
