# Sewon America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp: Sewon America Announces 740 New Jobs in Effingham County](https://www.einpresswire.com/article/618267821/gov-kemp-sewon-america-announces-740-new-jobs-in-effingham-county)  ·  `page_001379`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Sewon America will create 740 new jobs in Rincon.
- Sewon America will invest more than $300 million in a new Effingham County manufacturing facility.
- This project is the largest known private investment in the City of Rincon to-date.
- The announcement fulfills the commitment to create jobs and investment in all four Savannah Harbor Interstate-16 Joint Development Authority (JDA) counties.
- This fulfillment results from Hyundai's Metaplant location in Bryan County, which is the largest economic development project in state history.
- Suppliers for the Metaplant have now committed over $1.8 billion in investment.
- The supplier investment surpasses initial estimates by at least $800 million.
- Sewon America is a subsidiary of Korean-based Sewon Precision Industry Co.
- Governor Kemp congratulated Chairman Kim on his first international trip as governor to Korea and discussed continuing their work together.
- Speaker of the House Jon Burns represents part of Effingham County.
- Speaker of the House Jon Burns played a role in securing the project and engaging Sewon America executives.
- Lt. Governor Burt Jones commended Governor Kemp, Sewon America, and stakeholders on the historic economic development announcement for Rincon.
- Sewon America operates one of the largest automobile body parts manufacturing plants in North America.
- The company opened a manufacturing facility in LaGrange in 2008.
- The LaGrange facility has since doubled in size.
- Following completion of the Rincon facility, Sewon is expected to support 1,600 jobs in Georgia.
- Vice Chairman Kim stated Sewon America was founded 15 years ago in west Georgia and is building another facility in east Georgia.
- The growth is supported by the Sewon team, Governor Kemp, the Georgia Department of Economic Development, and Effingham County.
- Sewon's new facility will be located at the Grande View industrial park in Rincon.
- The new manufacturing location will produce electric vehicle (EV) body parts for original equipment manufacturers (OEMs).
- One OEM customer is Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- Operations at the new facility are expected to begin in 2025.
- Rincon Mayor Ken Lee welcomed Sewon America to the community and commended the Effingham County Industrial Development Authority for bringing the project.
- The project will be the largest employment and private investment project in Rincon's history.
- Chairman of the Effingham County Industrial Development Authority (IDA) Troy Smith stated Grande View industrial park was selected as the best location for Sewon's future growth.
- The IDA and Sewon America investment will allow current and future generations to have fulfilling, well-paying careers without leaving Effingham County.
- The project required help from partners at the City of Rincon and the Effingham County Board of Commissioners.
- Executive Director of the Georgia Ports Authority Griff Lynch called the announcement terrific news for Effingham County and Georgia.
- The Georgia Ports Authority will support Sewon's growth in this new location as Hyundai's announcement brings new economic opportunities to the state.
- Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy Alyce Thornhill represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on the project.
- GDEcD partnered with the Effingham County Industrial Development Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia EMC on this competitive project.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated Sewon has integrated into Georgia's automotive community through working with local colleges and Georgia Quick Start.
- Sewon participated in statewide initiatives like Georgia Made.
- Sewon supports Hyundai Motor Group’s operations from Effingham County, which has a skilled workforce, infrastructure, and collaborative spirit.
- On October 25, 2022, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America officially broke ground in neighboring Bryan County on its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility.
- Georgia is positioned as a hub for the electric mobility industry by building on automotive industry assets.
- Since 2020, more than 35 EV-related projects have announced more than $21 billion in investment and 26,700 jobs in Georgia.
- Sewon America was established in 2008 in LaGrange as a Georgia company.
- Sewon primarily operates in the Motor Vehicles and Car Bodies industry within the Transportation Equipment sector.
- Sewon produces stamped body components.
- Sewon Group is celebrated for high-quality automotive parts and will continue its tradition of excellence in the new Rincon facility.
