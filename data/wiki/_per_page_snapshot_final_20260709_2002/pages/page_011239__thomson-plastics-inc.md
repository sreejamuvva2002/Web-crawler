## Facts

- Thomson Plastics, Inc. (TPI) has an extensive press capability ranging from 200 to 3,700 tons of clamp force.
- Approximately 30 percent of TPI's machine fleet exceeds 1,000 tons.
- Matthew Key, director of program management, states the company's goal is to become the largest tonnage molder in the southeast United States.
- TPI guides clients' products from conception to production using a five-chapter Advanced Product Quality Planning (APQP) launch process.
- Chapter zero of APQP involves the quoting phase where program managers and engineering evaluate project requirements.
- Chapter one covers the product award phase, chapter two entails close customer collaboration for product design, chapter three focuses on process design and development, and chapter four is dedicated to product and process verification.
- The final chapter of APQP marks the handover to production, transitioning from new development to everyday operations.
- TPI's program management strictly adheres to AIAG guidelines as a cornerstone of operations.
- Internally, TPI employs a customer satisfaction gauge rated from zero to five to strategically allocate resources and identify improvement areas.
- TPI provides immediate support to clients facing issues during new application launches or new product design development.
- Clients frequently seek TPI's recommendations before finalizing designs due to established trust in its engineering team.
- TPI is currently developing a new design for a large-scale product encompassing multiple tools and components for an automotive company.
- Collaboration with the automotive customer includes shaping vehicle appearance, functionality, material selection, and grain selection.
- Following a recent facility upgrade, TPI made substantial investments in 16 new injection molding machines.
- TPI has ramped up machine output using advanced equipment to meet clients' increasing demands.
