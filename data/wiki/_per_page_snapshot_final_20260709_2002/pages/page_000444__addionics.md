## Facts

- Hyundai announced a $5.5 billion investment in facilities dedicated to manufacturing EVs and batteries in Savannah, Georgia.
- Hyundai will receive an additional $1 billion investment from its suppliers towards the Savannah project.
- The Savannah facility will be Hyundai’s first EV-only plant in the US.
- Construction at the 2,923-acre Savannah site is expected to begin in early 2023.
- Production at the Savannah site is expected to start in the first half of 2025.
- The Savannah facilities aim to produce 300,000 vehicles per year, equivalent to 25,000 per month.
- The Savannah project will create over 8,000 new jobs.
- Stellantis plans to invest over $2.5 billion in partnership with Samsung SDI to build its first US EV battery manufacturing facility in Kokomo, Indiana.
- Tesla signed an agreement with Panasonic in 2014 for a US$1.5–2 billion investment in Gigafactory 1, Tesla’s first factory dedicated to producing lithium-ion batteries for EVs.
- Ford announced a $11.4 billion investment to bring EVs at scale to American customers.
- Ford signed a non-binding MOU with SK On Co., Ltd. and Koç Holding to create one of the largest commercial vehicle battery production sites in Europe.
- Toyota announced plans to build a $1.3 billion US battery plant for EVs.
- Toyota will spend $3.4 billion on battery investments in the US up until 2030.
- GM is investing $6.6 billion on EV plants.
- GM created Ultium, its next-generation batteries and EV platform and technologies.
- GM estimates that cells from its Ultium platform will be capable of a range of up to 724km or more on a full charge with 0-96kmh acceleration in three seconds.
- Mercedes-Benz invested in Sila Nanotechnologies in 2019.
- Mercedes-Benz announced a breakthrough with high silicon automotive batteries.
- Mercedes-Benz plans to provide the option of equipping the upcoming electric Mercedes-Benz G-Class with next-generation battery cells with silicon anodes starting mid-decade.
- The average transaction price for an EV stands at $56,437.
- Batteries currently account for around 30% of the final cost for the customer.
- Essential battery materials including lithium, nickel and cobalt face supply shortages due to supply chain disruptions and ongoing geopolitical conflicts.
- The Tesla Model S has an 85 kWh battery pack that weighs 540 kg and contains 7,104 lithium-ion battery cells divided into 16 modules wired in series.
- Addionics’ technology loads more active material into the battery, allowing a longer driving range and reduction of charging time thanks to lower internal resistance.
- Addionics’ process is low-cost, drop-in ready, and can seamlessly be added to any manufacturing process.
- Addionics could enable the adoption of emerging battery chemistries such as silicon and solid state, and even the next generation of LFP battery chemistry.
