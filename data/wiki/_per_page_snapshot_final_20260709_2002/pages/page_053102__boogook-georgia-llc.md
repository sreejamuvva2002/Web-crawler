# Boogook Georgia, LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-06-28

**Source:** [Korean Automotive Supplier Boogook Industries to Redevelop Existing Building in Macon, Georgia - MBCIA](https://mbcia.com/mbcia-news/korean-automotive-supplier-boogook-industries-to-redevelop-existing-building-in-macon-georgia/)  ·  `page_053102`

## Facts

- Boogook Georgia, LLC is a Korean automotive supplier specializing in fluid-carrying systems for vehicles.
- The company will launch its first location in the United States in Macon, Ga.
- Boogook Georgia will redevelop an existing building within the Macon-Bibb County Industrial Authority (MBCIA) Industrial Park located at 2600 Weaver Road.
- The company plans $300,000 in leasehold improvements at the former B&W property.
- Boogook Georgia will make a total capital investment of $6.8 million to support its operation.
- Joong Suk Oh serves as the Chief Executive Officer of Boogook Georgia, LLC.
- Company leadership recognized the unique opportunity to support Korean automotive manufacturing partners more locally.
- Expanding operations to Macon will serve as a driving force for serving the Southeast U.S. market.
- The decision to locate in Macon was driven, in part, by proximity to Hyundai Motor’s $5.5 billion Metaplant America in Savannah.
- The site is also located near Kia’s West Point manufacturing facility.
- The location at 2600 Weaver Road offers quick highway access via I-16 and is conveniently located less than two miles from the company’s new site.
- Strategic positioning ensures seamless connectivity to key transportation routes for effective customer service and parts access.
- Macon-Bibb County Mayor Lester M. Miller stated that easy access to interstates and highways makes Georgia a prime location for industries looking to move products quickly and efficiently.
- Mayor Miller thanked the Industrial Authority for ongoing efforts and welcomed Boogook Georgia as the newest member of Team Macon-Bibb.
- Boogook Georgia will create 80 full-time jobs when it opens in 2024.
- Additional jobs are expected on the horizon.
- Employment opportunities will provide local professional roles across management, manufacturing, assembly, quality and assurance, and delivery functions.
- Stephen Adams serves as the Executive Director of the MBCIA.
- Adams stated that Boogook's decision reinforces the importance and value of growing automotive industry partners in the region.
- Middle Georgia is described as an extremely attractive and competitive area for these companies.
- Boogook Georgia, LLC is a subsidiary of Boogook Industries CO., LTD, headquartered in Ulsan, South Korea.
- The company is a global leader in the automotive tube industry, specializing in automotive parts and materials that carry fluids within vehicle systems.
- The parent company supplies products and services to major global automobile manufacturers, including Hyundai Motor Corp., Kia Motor Corp., and others.
- Boogook Industries has supplied products and services as a TIER-1 supplier since its foundation in 1987.
- Major products include brake, fuel, steering, intake and exhaust lines.
- Product offerings include brake tubes, fuel tubes, power steering tubes, vacuum tubes, and battery coolant lines for electric vehicles.
- Boogook Industries holds IATF 16949 : 2016 / ISO 45001 : 2018 / ISO 22301 : 2013 / ISO 22301 : 2012 certifications.
- Global production facilities include 2 plants in Korea, 2 plants in China, 1 plant in India, 1 plant in Vietnam, and 1 plant in Georgia, United States.
- The company achieved a global annual sales revenue of 106 million USD in 2018.
- All Boogook Industries employees are dedicated to technology development and quality management to expand business.
- The Macon-Bibb County Industrial Authority works with local, state, and national stakeholders to attract new business investment to Macon-Bibb County.
- The Authority creates a business-friendly climate that results in an increased tax base for the county.
- Catalyzing job growth and strengthening corporate infrastructure at various industrial parks are vital elements of the Authority's economic development record.
