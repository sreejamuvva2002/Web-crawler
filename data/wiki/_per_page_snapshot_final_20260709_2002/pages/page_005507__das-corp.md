# DAS Corp.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-09

**Source:** [DAS Corp. to Bring 300 Automotive Jobs to Metter – Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority](https://savannahjda.com/2023/09/das-corp-to-bring-300-automotive-jobs-to-metter/)  ·  `page_005507`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that DAS Corp. (DAS), an automotive parts company, will establish a new manufacturing facility in Metter.
- The new facility will create 300 new jobs.
- The project will generate more than $35 million in investment in Candler County.
- The initiative will involve collaborative indirect investments.
- DAS was established in Korea in 1987 and specializes in designing and manufacturing seating systems, safety seating components, and seating structures.
- DAS is a key supplier for Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia (KMMG) and Hyundai Motor Group.
- Sean Kim, Chief Operating Officer at DAS, stated that the strategic location near prominent cities like Savannah and Macon facilitated convenient access and played a pivotal role in the decision-making process.
- Metter was deemed an ideal location in anticipation of its role as a global electric vehicle production hub due to its close proximity to Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- The new DAS manufacturing facility will be located in the Candler County Industrial Park in Metter.
- The facility will primarily produce automotive seat structures.
- Operations at the new facility are projected to begin in the latter half of 2024.
- Over the course of the next five years, DAS will recruit for assembly technicians.
- DAS intends to establish collaborations with local employment services as part of its hiring process.
- Metter Mayor Ed Boyd stated that the City of Metter is proud to welcome DAS and noted the impact on the community will be transformative, stimulating investment in housing, creating other business opportunities, and improving socioeconomic wellbeing.
- Glyn Thrift, Chairman of the Candler County Board of Commissioners, thanked DAS for choosing Candler County as its new home to spur more economic development.
- Griff Lynch, President and CEO of the Georgia Ports Authority, stated that the authority is proud to support the growing automotive manufacturing presence in the state and work together to drive economic activity and jobs.
- Alyce Thornhill, Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy, represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The project was executed in partnership with the Candler County Industrial Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, Electric Cities of Georgia, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Georgia is well-positioned for the arrival of Hyundai’s supplier network as a result of years of consistent investment in site preparation, workforce solutions, and connectivity.
- Over the past century, Georgia has fostered healthy industry practices, encouraged collaboration and innovation, and positioned itself as a leader in developing emerging technologies for the automotive and mobility industry.
- As the market transitions to electric vehicles, Georgia has pursued the entire supply chain, creating more than $25 billion in investments and 30,000 jobs since 2018.
- In fiscal year 2023, job creation in the automotive industry increased by 324 percent compared to FY21.
- Suppliers attracted by Georgia’s mobility original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) resulted in over $2 billion in investment across the state in FY23.
- The announcement was made on Sept. 6, 2023.
