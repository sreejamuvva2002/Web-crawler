# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-05-20

**Source:** [Georgia state to provide $1.6 bn in incentives to Hyundai - KED Global](https://www.kedglobal.com/electric-vehicles/newsView/ked202205230020)  ·  `page_009410`

## Facts

- Georgia state government and the Hyundai group agreed on incentives of some 2 trillion won ($1.6 billion), which is the authority’s largest benefit for a company.
- The incentive package includes free land, property tax cuts, additional tax reduction when meeting job creation targets, and financial support for job training.
- On May 20, Hyundai announced a plan to invest about $5.5 billion to build a new EV plant with an annual capacity of 300,000 units as well as a battery production complex in Bryan County, Georgia.
- Earlier, Georgia committed to providing Rivian Automotive Inc., a US EV manufacturer, with a total $1.5 billion worth of incentives for pledging to invest $5 billion in the state.
- Rivian Automotive Inc. manufactures R1T pickup trucks and R1S sport utility vehicles.
- Hyundai is likely to receive better incentives than those offered to Rivian because the South Korean company’s investment and job numbers are higher than those of the US EV maker.
- On May 22, US President Joe Biden pledged not to disappoint Hyundai after a meeting with group Chairman Chung Euisun, indicating large-scale incentives.
- The size of Hyundai's incentives could shrink due to possible opposition by US taxpayer groups and the midterm elections in November.
- Georgia is one of the most business-friendly states in the US, named No. 1 for business for eight straight years since 2014 by Area Development.
- Global information technology giants including Microsoft Corp. and Google have operations in Georgia.
- South Korean company SK On Co., the world’s fifth-largest EV battery maker, has also set up units in Georgia.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development offers a one-stop service for site selection, business permissions, links with infrastructure and incentive information.
- The state provides additional tax incentives to companies that use ports in Georgia and develop new products or services there.
- Higher population growth and a lower minimum wage compared to the US average are also attractive factors for Hyundai's site selection.
- The US Democratic Party is seeking legislation for a $4,500 tax credit for EVs made in a union plant proposed by the Biden administration.
- The existing US plants of Hyundai Motor Co. and Kia Corp. do not have unionized workers.
- Hyundai is likely hoping workers at the planned Georgia EV plant will not set up a labor union.
- Georgia adopts a right-to-work law that gives workers the freedom to choose whether or not to join a union in the workplace.
- An industry source stated that Hyundai will inevitably lose price competitiveness if the Democratic Party's bill is passed, which would remove an additional tax break.
- If the legislation goes through, Hyundai may expect additional incentives because Biden is aware they won't benefit from the tax credit.
- Some sources predicted that the group may allow workers to establish a union in the new factory.
