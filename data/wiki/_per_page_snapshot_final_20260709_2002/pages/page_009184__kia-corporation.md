# Kia Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-04-10

## Facts

- Kia used its April 9 investor day to reset EV targets and announce that Boston Dynamics Atlas humanoid robots will reach Kia AutoLand Georgia in 2029.
- Hyundai Motor Group's first deployment of Atlas at HMGMA in Savannah is scheduled for 2028.
- The timeline marks a shift for Atlas from a single showcase program into a broader rollout across the company's manufacturing footprint.
- Kia raised its five-year investment plan during the investor day, identifying robotics, autonomous driving, and software-defined vehicles as core growth engines.
- HMGMA will function as the first operating proving ground for Atlas deployment.
- Kia AutoLand Georgia will serve as the second site for Atlas deployment.
- Both plants are located in Georgia, which lowers friction for support, service, training, and cross-site learning between the facilities.
- Hyundai plans to field-test Atlas in Savannah, refine task libraries, integration layers, and operator playbooks before moving proven capabilities to Kia's existing Georgia operation.
- Kia's investor-day materials treated robotics as a core strategy rather than a side experiment.
- Boston Dynamics positions Atlas as an enterprise-grade humanoid robot designed for material handling, machine tending, order building, and sequencing work.
- The current Atlas product specifications include 56 degrees of freedom, a 4-hour battery life, a 50-kilogram instant lift capacity, and a 30-kilogram sustained lift capacity.
- Atlas supports autonomous battery swapping, barcode scanning, and integration with enterprise systems through Boston Dynamics' Orbit software platform.
- Reuters reported that Kia plans to invest more than $500 million in physical AI capabilities and vision-language-action models.
- Boston Dynamics and Google DeepMind announced a January partnership focused on giving Atlas stronger perception, reasoning, and task-learning capability.
- NVIDIA is involved in supporting the broader physical AI and autonomy stack for Kia's future manufacturing strategy.
- Hyundai Motor Group has set an annual robot factory goal of 30,000 robots by 2028.
- Reuters reported Kia's investment plan totals $28 billion for the period from 2026 to 2029.
- The company's key investment statistic is listed as KRW 49 trillion for the period from 2026 to 2030.
- Atlas deployment focuses on narrow industrial use cases rather than broad human-like ambition, targeting tasks that are structured enough to automate but varied enough that fixed automation struggles.
- Plant-level deployment plans raise practical questions regarding initial task selection, robot-to-supervisor ratios per shift, safety zones around forklifts and personnel, and cost recovery through reduced cycle-time loss and fewer ergonomic incidents.
