# Hyundai-LG Metaplant

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-09-01

**Source:** [Hyundai, LG Energy investing another $2B in Metaplant |](https://capitol-beat.org/2023/09/hyundai-lg-energy-investing-another-2b-in-metaplant/)  ·  `page_056203`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution are investing another $2 billion in the planned “Metaplant” west of Savannah.
- The Metaplant is already the largest economic development project in Georgia history.
- The two companies announced an expansion of the original $5.5 billion electric vehicles and battery cell manufacturing joint venture on Thursday.
- The expansion brings the total investment to nearly $7.6 billion.
- The expansion adds 400 jobs on top of the 8,100 originally planned for the next eight years.
- Gov. Brian Kemp said the massive project will go a long way toward making Georgia the e-mobility capital of the U.S.
- Gov. Brian Kemp stated: “These types of major investments ultimately go to hardworking Georgians in the form of paychecks, improved schools and infrastructure, and more.”
- José Muñoz, president and CEO of Hyundai and Genesis Motor North America, stated: “This incremental investment in Bryan County reflects our continued commitment to create a more sustainable future powered by American workers.”
- When fully built out, the Metaplant will turn out 300,000 EVs per year.
- Hyundai Mobis will assemble battery packs using cells from the plant.
- Hyundai Mobis will supply the assembled battery packs to Hyundai Motor Group’s U.S. manufacturing facilities for the production of Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis EV models.
- The state Department of Economic Development’s Global Commerce team worked on the project in partnership with the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority.
- The Georgia Ports Authority worked on the project in partnership with the state Department of Economic Development’s Global Commerce team.
- Georgia Power worked on the project in partnership with the state Department of Economic Development’s Global Commerce team.
- The Technical College System of Georgia’s Quick Start program worked on the project in partnership with the state Department of Economic Development’s Global Commerce team.
