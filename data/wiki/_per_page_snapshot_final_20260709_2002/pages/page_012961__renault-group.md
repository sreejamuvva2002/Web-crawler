# Renault Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** power_electronics  ·  **Published:** 2022-02-11

**Source:** [Renault Group, Valeo and Valeo Siemens eAutomotive partner to produce next-gen electric motor](https://electriccarsreport.com/2022/02/renault-group-valeo-and-valeo-siemens-eautomotive-partner-to-produce-next-gen-electric-motor/)  ·  `page_012961`

## Facts

- Renault Group, Valeo and Valeo Siemens eAutomotive signed a memorandum of understanding to form a strategic partnership for the design, co-development and manufacture in France of a new-generation automotive electric motor.
- The partnership aims to eliminate the use of rare earths in the electric motor.
- The three partners will combine their know-how to design a unique electric powertrain system that offers more power on less energy without using rare earths.
- Each of the three partners will contribute to the development and production of two key parts of the electric motor: the rotor and the stator.
- Renault will develop and produce the EESM (electrically excited synchronous motor) rotor technology, designed without the use of rare earths, offering enhanced energy performance.
- The overall architecture of the all-in-one motor for Renault Group will be designed by Renault.
- Valeo and Valeo Siemens eAutomotive will develop and produce the stator based on Valeo’s technological expertise in copper wire assembly.
- Valeo's expertise allows it to use a higher density of copper in the stator to generate more power without having to use more electrical energy.
- Renault Group, Valeo and Valeo Siemens eAutomotive will be the first to mass produce a 200kW electric motor without using rare earths, starting in 2027.
- Production of the motor for Renault Group’s own needs will be based at Renault Group’s Cléon plant in Normandy, France.
- Luca de Meo, Renault Group’s Chief Executive Officer, commented that they are delighted to partner with Valeo and will design and develop a new generation of high-tech electric motors produced at the Cléon plant.
- Christophe Périllat, Valeo’s Chief Executive Officer, stated that the strategic partnership will result in a major step forward for electric mobility and create a new-generation automotive motor that eliminates rare earths.
- The new motor will meet stringent environmental requirements and the highest performance standards.
