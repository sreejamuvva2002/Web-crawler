# Novelis Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2022-05-11

**Source:** [Novelis to Build $2.5 Billion Low-Carbon Aluminum Recycling and Rolling Plant](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/novelis-to-build-2-5-billion-low-carbon-aluminum-recycling-and-rolling-plant-301544691.html)  ·  `page_028895`

## Facts

- Novelis Inc. announced it will invest $2.5 billion to build a new low-carbon recycling and rolling plant in Bay Minette, Alabama, USA.
- The facility will have an initial capacity of 600 kilotonnes of finished aluminum goods per year.
- Mr. Kumar Mangalam Birla serves as Chairman of the Aditya Birla Group and the Novelis Board of Directors.
- Novelis serves markets including beverage can, automotive, aerospace, and specialties across all geographies.
- More than half of the new facility's capacity will serve growing demand for aluminum beverage can sheet in North America.
- Steve Fisher is President and CEO of Novelis Inc.
- Novelis plans to efficiently expand capacity at this facility above the 600kt announced to capture ongoing strong demand.
- The decision to build a fully integrated, greenfield recycling and rolling plant is backed by strong North American demand for flat-rolled, low-carbon aluminum from can makers and beverage companies.
- Aluminum beverage cans have an average "can-to-can" lifecycle of just a couple of months.
- A recycled can can be back on store shelves in as little as 60 days.
- Ron Lewis is Ball Corporation's chief operating officer, global beverage packaging.
- Novelis' new recycling and rolling plant will add domestic production of sustainable aluminum in North America while decreasing the carbon footprint of products.
- The facility will be the first fully integrated aluminum mill built in the U.S. in 40 years.
- It is expected to create up to 1,000 high-paying, advanced careers in modern manufacturing.
- The plant aims to be net carbon neutral for Scope 1 and 2 emissions.
- The facility will be powered primarily by renewable energy.
- The facility will use recycled water and operate as a zero-waste facility.
- The plant will rely on railroad transportation, reducing logistics-related carbon emissions by up to 70% compared to road transport.
- The plant will make significant use of advanced automation and digital technologies, including artificial intelligence, augmented reality, and robotics.
- With the new recycling center for beverage cans, Novelis will soon be able to recycle 90 billion cans globally, up from its current 74 billion used beverage cans.
- Novelis has been working to develop circular economies for aluminum through state and federal public policies.
- Novelis is partnering with customers and other stakeholders on new approaches that encourage and incentivize U.S. consumers to recycle more often.
- John Murphy is Chief Financial Officer of The Coca-Cola Company.
- The new low-carbon recycling and rolling facility will benefit the Coca-Cola system, its customers, and consumers while reducing environmental impact.
- Site work is under way as of May 2022.
- The company expects to begin commissioning in mid-2025.
- In addition to the beverage can market, the facility will serve the automotive market, where aluminum is the fastest growing material as automakers pursue sustainability goals.
- Novelis had net sales of $17.1 billion in fiscal year 2022.
- Novelis is a subsidiary of Hindalco Industries Limited.
- Hindalco Industries Limited is the metals flagship company of the Aditya Birla Group, a multinational conglomerate based in Mumbai.
- Novelis operates throughout North America, Europe, Asia, and South America.
