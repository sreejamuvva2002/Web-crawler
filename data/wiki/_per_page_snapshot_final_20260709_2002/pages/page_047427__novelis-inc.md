# Novelis Inc.

## Facts

- Novelis announced it will invest $2.5 billion to build a new low-carbon recycling and rolling plant in Bay Minette, Alabama, USA.
- The highly advanced facility will have an initial capacity of 600 kilotonnes of finished aluminium goods per year.
- Kumar Mangalam Birla, Chairman of the Aditya Birla Group and the Novelis Board of Directors, stated the investment marks the start of another transformational growth phase for Novelis.
- This project is the largest global greenfield expansion project of the Aditya Birla Group.
- The investment will take the Aditya Birla Group's total investment in the US across businesses to over $14 billion.
- More than half of the capacity of the new facility will be used to serve the growing demand for aluminium beverage can sheets in North America, driven by consumer preference for sustainable packaging.
- Steve Fisher, President and CEO of Novelis Inc., stated the investment demonstrates a commitment to grow alongside customers and meet their needs for low-carbon, highly sustainable aluminum solutions.
- The facility's construction is backed by strong North American demand for flat-rolled, low-carbon aluminium from can makers and beverage companies.
- Aluminium beverage cans, bottles, and cups are described as models of sustainable packaging and the circular economy.
- An average "can-to-can" lifecycle lasts just a couple of months, with a recycled can able to return to store shelves in as little as 60 days.
- The facility will be the first fully integrated aluminium mill built in the U.S. in 40 years.
- The plant is expected to create up to 1,000 high-paying, advanced careers in modern manufacturing.
- The facility aims to be net carbon neutral for Scope 1 and 2 emissions.
- It will be powered primarily by renewable energy, use recycled water, and operate as a zero-waste facility.
- It will rely on railroad transportation, which can reduce logistics-related carbon emissions by up to 70 percent compared to road transport.
- The plant will make significant use of advanced automation and digital technologies, including artificial intelligence, augmented reality, and robotics.
- With the addition of a new recycling centre for beverage cans, Novelis will soon be able to recycle 90 billion cans globally, up from its current 74 billion used beverage cans.
- Novelis has been working to develop circular economies for aluminium through state and federal public policies, as well as partnerships with customers and stakeholders to encourage U.S. consumers to recycle more often.

- In addition to the beverage can market, the facility will serve the automotive market, where aluminium is the fastest-growing material as automakers pursue sustainability goals.
