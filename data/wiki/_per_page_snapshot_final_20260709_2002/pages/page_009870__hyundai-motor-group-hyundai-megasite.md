# Hyundai Motor Group / Hyundai Megasite

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [ICE raid in Savannah, Georgia: 475 people detained](https://www.wyff4.com/article/immigration-raid-hyundai-georgia/65994081)  ·  `page_009870`

## Facts

- Approximately 475 people were detained during an immigration raid at the Hyundai Megasite in Georgia.
- The enforcement operation occurred at the Helga Battery Plant construction site within a sprawling 3,000-acre facility located west of Savannah, Georgia.
- Steven Schrank, Special Agent in Charge of Homeland Security Investigations for Georgia and Alabama, stated the raid was part of an ongoing criminal investigation into allegations of unlawful employment practices and serious federal crimes.
- The investigation had been ongoing for several months, with authorities receiving leads from community members and former workers.
- A majority of the detained individuals were Korean nationals, with Korean media reporting as many as 300, though officials did not provide an exact nationality breakdown.
- The detained workers were employed by a network of subcontractors and subcontractors for subcontractors on the site, rather than directly by the parent company.
- Of the hundreds encountered on the premises, 475 were found to be illegally present or in violation of their U.S. presence, including those who crossed the border illegally, entered on visa waivers prohibited from working, or overstayed visas.
- All detained individuals had their status, documents, and backgrounds checked before being turned over to ICE custody, with most initially transported to the Folkston facility.
- Hyundai Motor Group, South Korea's largest automaker, began manufacturing electric vehicles at the $7.6 billion plant approximately one year ago.
- The EV manufacturing plant currently employs about 1,200 people.
- Hyundai partnered with LG Energy Solution to build an adjacent battery plant through their joint venture, HL-GA Battery Company.
- The adjacent battery plant was slated to open the following year.
- Construction at the battery site was paused to assist authorities following the raid, while operations at Hyundai's EV manufacturing plant continued without interruption.
- Plant spokesperson Bianca Johnson confirmed that EV manufacturing operations were not interrupted by the enforcement action.
- Gov. Brian Kemp and other officials had previously touted the Hyundai Megasite as the largest economic development project in Georgia's history.
- South Korean Foreign Ministry spokesperson Lee Jaewoong stated that diplomats from the U.S. embassy in Washington and consulate in Atlanta were dispatched to the site, with plans to form an on-site response team.
- Lee Jaewoong emphasized that "the business activities of our investors and the rights of our nationals must not be unjustly infringed in the process of U.S. law enforcement."
- Hyundai issued a statement saying, "Our top priority is always ensuring the safety and well-being of our employees and partners. We will fully cooperate with the relevant authorities."
- HL-GA Battery Company released a statement confirming it was cooperating fully with authorities and had paused construction to assist.
- ICE spokesman Lindsay Williams confirmed that federal agents focused their enforcement operation specifically on the construction site for the battery plant.
- No criminal charges were announced at the time of the press conference, and officials noted the matter remains ongoing.
- There were no substantial uses of force during the operation, with only one detainee experiencing overheating treated on scene and one agent sustaining a minor laceration.
