# HELLA Automotive Mexico Irapuato

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2018-09-06

**Source:** [Made in Mexico HELLA lighting up the Mexican and from Abroad Automotive Industry](https://www.promexicoindustry.com/en/article/made-in-mexico-hella-lighting-up-the-mexican-and-from-abroad-automotive-industry)  ·  `page_055796`

## Facts

- HELLA Automotive Mexico Irapuato has been producing for four years as of September 2018 and is positioned as one of the most advanced plants in the manufacturing of lighting components worldwide.
- The German company settled in Irapuato, Guanajuato, with an investment of high double digit in million dollars.
- This facility became the fifth productive plant launched by HELLA in Mexico.
- It is one of the largest plants from Grupo Hella and aims to contribute to the global vision of being a number one company at the forefront for manufacturing lighting, headlights, tail lights, and interior lighting.
- Harpak Mozaffari Gilani, director of Planta Hella Irapuato, chose the area due to proximity with assemblers and sector supply, and detected potential in human capital.
- The plant has important lighting development projects for vehicles, and customers are satisfied by its location and layout, considering it a benchmark in Grupo Hella.
- Developing a new product requested by a customer takes up to four years before market launch.
- Car manufacturers present new vehicle concepts or lighting ideas, or ask Hella to present development proposals.
- The development process involves detailed quoting, proposals, a development phase, tooling manufacturing, and an assembly line setup.
- Each light undergoes a validation and quality process where changes are made or manufacturing starts depending on development complexity.
- The plant currently operates 20 production lines.
- Manufacturing capability reaches 13,000 daily units.
- The facility generates about 6.5 million tail lights and headlights per year.
- Little more than half of the annual output is exported to the United States.
- Automotive supply in Mexico has improved over recent years, with companies becoming increasingly independent of raw material imports.
- Improved local supply reduces order waiting time compared to importing components from other countries, which takes a month.
- Local suppliers deliver components in a couple of hours, providing a logistical advantage.
- Raw material granulated plastic continues to be imported, creating an opportunity for a local company to provide it.
- The Irapuato plant employs more than 2,200 collaborators.
- A 30 percent increase in personnel was projected for May 2019.
- The automotive industry experiences continuous personnel rotation of 6.5 percent.
- Hella seeks to retain talent by generating a better labor environment and including families in recreational activities.
- The plant operates a Training Center with an approximate capacity of 100 people for theoretical and practical training on assembly processes.
- Management aims to foster the idea of Familia Hella, recognizing personnel effort and results while involving families to transfer company values.
- Total property land covers 120,000 m2.
- Total construction area is 55,000 m2.
- Start of operations was in 2014.
