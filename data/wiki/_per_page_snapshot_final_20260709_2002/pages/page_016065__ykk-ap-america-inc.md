# YKK AP America Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2025-08-07

**Source:** [YKK AP America Introduces Autonomous GTP System at Georgia Window Facility](https://www.dwmmag.com/2025/08/07/ykk-ap-america-introduces-autonomous-gtp-system-at-georgia-window-facility/)  ·  `page_016065`

## Facts

- YKK AP America Inc. installed a new goods-to-person (GTP) automation system at its residential window plant in Macon, Georgia.
- The GTP system was installed at the 400,000-square-foot purpose-built facility’s shipping department to further reduce the manual movement of heavy windows.
- Dennis Howard serves as senior manager of transportation and logistics at YKK AP.
- Daymon Loyd serves as director of residential manufacturing at YKK AP.
- The GTP system improves operations and quality and reduces time to load by 39%.
- The GTP system uses autonomous robots to move finished products from the end of the line to material storage units and into a fenced GTP field that houses final products.
- YKK AP is making strategic investments in automation to reduce risk and improve safety.
- YKK AP's goal for residential manufacturing was to reduce material handling to improve quality, starting with the shipping department where most material handling occurs.
- The Macon facility opened in early 2024.
- Upon opening, YKK AP aimed for zero injuries, zero backorders, and 100% on-time delivery.
- The facility currently achieves a 97% on-time delivery rate.
- YKK AP invested in the GTP system to close the gap from 97% to 100% on-time delivery.
- The facility handles 80% of its products with the GTP system.
- The remaining 20% of products are too large for the material storage units.
- Integrating the new autonomous system with YKK AP’s IT infrastructure was the most significant deployment challenge.
- Training for the system interface (not end users) served as a challenge, requiring an aggressive training schedule.
- The bulk of the training involved troubleshooting technical issues as they arose.
- YKK AP did not have to retrain employees but still had to train them to use the specific system.
- The system's user-friendly interface made the training process seamless.
- The GTP system is vital for companies seeking to improve safety via automation, requiring openness to change.
- This was YKK AP’s residential manufacturing team’s first deployment of GTP technology.
- The team had to remain open and flexible to changing original plans to accommodate the technology correctly.
- Much of the capital equipment was on the manufacturing floor before it was in action, requiring time and trial and error to deploy.
- The team has witnessed the project from start to finish and now sees it operating at its full potential.
