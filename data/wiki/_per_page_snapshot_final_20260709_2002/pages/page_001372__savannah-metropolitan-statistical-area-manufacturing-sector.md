# Savannah Metropolitan Statistical Area Manufacturing Sector

**Type:** unknown  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Manufacturers - Savannah Chamber](https://www.savannahchamber.com/economic-development/manufacturers/)  ·  `page_001372`

## Facts

- Savannah MSA manufacturing firms account for 18.5% of the area’s economic output as measured by GDP.
- Manufacturing accounted for $6 billion of output in 2023.
- As of early 2025, there were 527 manufacturing establishments in the Savannah CSA.
- These establishments employed 30,316 workers (+3.2%).
- Average weekly wage was $1,905 (+9.7%).
- Covered employment in manufacturing in the Savannah MSA increased to 24,261 as of early 2025.
- Since the May 2022 Hyundai Metaplant announcement, regional manufacturing base increased 29%.
- This represents a gain of 5,442 workers.
- An additional $900 million in worker compensation is flowing through the regional economy.
- Over 88% of manufacturing growth has been at employers in the transportation equipment sector.
- Growth occurred at Gulfstream, Hyundai Metaplant, and supply chain firms for the Metaplant.
- Recent activity and announcements point toward another strong year for manufacturing in 2026.
- Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority (Savannah JDA) represents Bryan, Bulloch, Chatham, and Effingham counties.
- Savannah JDA continued its partnership with Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA).
- In March 2025, HMGMA held its grand opening.
- The project is the largest economic development project in Georgia’s history.
- Grand opening occurred just two and a half years after breaking ground.
- In September 2025, Hyundai Motor Group announced Phase 2 expansion.
- Phase 2 will bring capacity to 500,000 vehicles.
- Phase 2 will include 3,000 jobs and $2.7 billion in investment.
- HMGMA is currently producing the IONIQ 5 and IONIQ 9.
- HMGMA is expected to begin production of additional EVs and hybrids in 2026.
- Supplier activity surrounding the project expanded with more than $2.5 billion in new capital investment.
- Supplier activity includes approximately 7,216 announced jobs across Georgia.
- Savannah JDA supports site development, infrastructure coordination, and project impact across the four-county region.
- Gulfstream Aerospace Corporation is the largest private employer in Savannah.
- Gulfstream has a global workforce of 20,000+ workers in production, service, maintenance, engineering, and R&D.
- Gulfstream announced the all-new, super-midsize Gulfstream G300 as the latest aircraft to join its next-generation fleet.
- The G300 made its first flight in December 2025.
- First flight demonstrated significant program maturity and launched its rigorous flight test program.
- Gulfstream aircraft surpassed 3 million nautical miles flown on sustainable aviation fuel (SAF) blends in 2025.
- First customer delivery of the all-new Gulfstream G800 was completed in 2025.
- The G800 is the world’s longest range business aircraft.
- G800 entry into service follows FAA and EASA certifications earlier in 2025.
- Gulfstream announced its 50th customer delivery of the G700.
- The G700 was outfitted at the Savannah completions center.
- Since entering service in April 2024, the G700 fleet has accumulated more than 11,700 flight hours.
- The G380 fleet completed its 300th customer delivery.
- The in-service G280 fleet has accrued more than 575,000 flight hours.
- The G280 fleet has completed nearly 358,000 landings.
- JCB’s North American headquarters is centered in Savannah.
- It is a 500,000-square-foot facility supporting over 115 dealers in the U.S. and Canada.
- In August 2025, JCB was awarded a $45 million contract to supply 4CX Backhoe Loaders to the United States armed forces.
- In November 2025, JCB won the “Tractor of the Year” award for its all-new high-power Fastrac 6300.
- Savannah Economic Development Authority (SEDA) worked with partners to attract new job opportunities and investment to Chatham County.
- SEDA facilitated eight new companies or expansions in 2025.
- SEDA finalized and adopted a new strategic plan for priorities over the next five to seven years.
- SEDA collaborated on industry expansion, workforce needs, and opportunities from major regional investments including Hyundai Metaplant America growth.
- World Trade Center Savannah (WTCSav) advanced international engagement through high-impact programs and global outreach.
- In partnership with Georgia Power, WTCSav hosted the Southeast Georgia Good News and International Protocol Training for representatives from more than 23 counties.
- WTCSav convened the inaugural Global Business Summit, bringing together nearly 130 leaders from Quebec, Washington D.C., Georgia state agencies, and Southeast Georgia.
- WTCSav supported the Greater Savannah Ghana Alliance’s first tri-city mission.
- This mission facilitated more than 40 business connections and meetings with government, educational, and industry partners.
- WTCSav led an outbound mission to Marseille, France for the World Trade Center’s Association’s 55th Global Business Forum.
- WTCSav’s TradeBridge program expanded in 2025 with the addition of County Limerick, the first Irish west coast county to join.
- WTCSav completed 118 trade service projects in 2025.
- WTCSav hosted 20 delegations from eight countries in 2025.
- WTCSav delivered cross-continental programs and continued support of 16 Southeast Georgia counties and the City of Savannah as its founding investor.
- Regional Industry Support Enterprise (RISE) team supported workforce development in an eight-county region including Bryan, Bulloch, Candler, Chatham, Effingham, Evans, Liberty, and Screven Counties.
- RISE focus areas include Education (K-12 and Higher Ed), Military, Childcare, Underrepresented populations, Housing, Marketing, and Transportation.
- Key RISE initiatives included a Military/Employer Forum.
- Key initiative: Higher Education Alignment Summit.
- Key initiative: Support for Georgia Department of Transportation Commuter Feasibility Study.
- Key initiative: Partnering with EOA on an underserved populations hiring event.
- Key initiative: Development of a housing toolkit for workforce housing searchers.
- Key initiative: Completion of a Regional Childcare Study in partnership with Georgia Southern University.
- Key initiative: Continuation of the Succeed in Savannah talent attraction campaign, including launch of an online job board.
- Key initiative: Industry Insiders program allowing young professionals to share career journeys with students.
- RISE grew K-12 engagement with 8th Grade Experience, Regional Educator Externship Program, and Building Your Future: Career and College Showcase.
- 21 employer forums with 608 total attendees were held throughout the region in 2025.
- RISE supported six hiring events across the region, reaching more than 3,400 job seekers.
- RISE led a best practices trip to West Georgia with 35 stakeholders from economic development, education, industry, and non-profit partners.
- Bryan County manufacturing employment at 40 establishments increased 18%.
- Employment rose to 1,900 workers as of early 2025.
- As of year-end 2025, employment at Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) EV facility increased to 1,444 workers.
- HMGMA employment represents a 31% gain in one year.
- By year-end, associated automotive manufacturers of componentry, systems, electronics, plastics, and parts employed an additional 2,633 workers in Bryan County.
- Continued employment growth is anticipated in 2026 as production ramps up at the Metaplant and allied firms.
- Effingham County’s manufacturing sector saw strong gains in 2025.
- Two new manufacturers commenced operation in Effingham County in 2025.
- Total employment in Effingham County's manufacturing sector reached 2,600 workers.
- Sewon America announced a $50 million expansion.
- Expansion includes new electroplating capabilities.
- Expansion includes roughly 100,000 square feet of additional space.
- Expansion includes 100 new jobs.
- Sewon is the first expansion announced by a Hyundai Metaplant supplier in the Savannah region.
- Georgia Transformer committed to a major expansion at its Rincon facility.
- Investment is an additional $40 million.
- Expansion adds 400 new jobs.
- Expansion supports increased demand for electrical equipment and grid-modernization components.
- Effingham County welcomed Underwood Ammo in 2025.
- Underwood Ammo announced a $41 million manufacturing facility.
- Facility will bring 120 high-skill jobs.
- Facility introduces defense-related manufacturing to the county’s diversified economy.
- 2025 brought more than $130 million in new capital investment to Effingham County.
- Hundreds of new jobs were added to Effingham County in 2025.
- Liberty County’s 29 manufacturers employed about 2,200 workers in 2025.
- Atlantic Building Components, a truss manufacturing company headquartered in South Carolina, announced an expansion.
- Company is establishing operations in Hinesville with an initial workforce of 25 employees.
- Company is positioned to serve the growing regional housing market.
- Georgia Department of Community Affairs awarded a $2 million grant to support construction of Tradeport West Way.
- Road will open 170 acres for development.
- Project includes 1.5 miles of roadway, drainage, and dirt work.
- LCDA is funding the remaining costs.
- Extension of Technology Drive is underway in Hinesville Technology Park.
- Improvement will enhance access and unlock an additional 15 acres for future projects.
- Bulloch County’s 58 manufacturing establishments employed 2,400 workers as of early 2025.
- Five industrial projects announced for Bulloch County since 2022 include three in support of the Hyundai Metaplant.
- Recent success infuses $1.1 billion investment in Bulloch County.
- There is limited availability of publicly supported development sites in Bulloch County as a result.
