# Archer Aviation Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-01-06

**Source:** [Archer Aviation Completes High-Volume Manufacturing Facility Construction in Covington, GA - UASweekly.com](https://uasweekly.com/2025/01/06/archer-aviation-completes-high-volume-manufacturing-facility-construction-in-covington-ga/)  ·  `page_017085`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation Inc. announced that it received the certificate of occupancy for its high-volume manufacturing facility, ARC, earlier this week.
- The ARC facility is connected to the Covington Municipal Airport in Georgia.
- Archer is working to complete the tooling load-in for the initial manufacturing line at the facility.
- Production is planned to begin in early 2025.
- Archer aims to ramp production to a rate of two aircraft per month by the end of 2025.
- ARC was built in close partnership with Stellantis.
- The two companies are continuing to progress plans to mass-produce Archer’s Midnight aircraft.
- Stellantis is contributing capital, advanced manufacturing technology and expertise, and experienced personnel to scale the facility to 650 aircraft annually by 2030.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp commented that the announcement marks the next step in a relationship aimed at creating quality jobs and opportunities for years to come.
- Congressman Mike Collins of Georgia, who sits on the House Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure and the Committee on Science, Space, and Technology, stated that the facility completion highlights Georgia’s leadership in innovation, advanced manufacturing, and job creation.
- Collins added that he is excited to support this transformative chapter in aviation, which will drive progress and deliver economic benefits for Georgia.
- Archer founder and CEO Adam Goldstein noted that the completion marks a shift from research and development into commercialization.
- Goldstein stated that with construction complete, the team is focused on execution and scaling after production starts early next year.
- Goldstein praised the Archer team and partners for getting the facility done quickly and on budget.
- Stellantis Chief Manufacturing and Supply Chain Officer Arnaud Deboeuf commented that supporting Archer’s electric aircraft growth with deep manufacturing expertise is a great opportunity for both companies.
- Deboeuf called the milestone a significant step in taking clean mobility to the sky.
- ARC will produce Archer’s all-electric vertical take-off and landing aircraft, Midnight.
- The Midnight aircraft is designed to offer passengers a sustainable, low-noise, and safe alternative to ground transportation in congested cities around the world.
