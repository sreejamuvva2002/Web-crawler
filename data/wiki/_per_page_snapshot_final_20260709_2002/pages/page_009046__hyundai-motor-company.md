# Hyundai Motor Company

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-06-24

**Source:** [SEDA Outlines Hyundai Project at Chamber's CEO and Owner's Council Luncheon, - Savannah Chamber](https://www.savannahchamber.com/news-and-events/news/chamber-news/seda-outlines-hyundai-project-at-chambers-ceo-and-owners-council-luncheon/)  ·  `page_009046`

## Facts

- Trip Tollison, CEO and President of the Savannah Economic Development Authority (SEDA), shared details at a Chamber luncheon about how Hyundai chose to locate in Bryan County.
- The new facility is reported to be the largest economic development project in the State of Georgia’s history.
- The project will bring more than $5.5+ billion in investment from the Hyundai Motor Company.
- The project will create 8,000+ jobs.
- Thousands of additional jobs will be created by various parts suppliers that will become public over the next few months.
- Governor Kemp led the State and the Georgia Economic Development by offering necessary incentives to Hyundai, thereby separating Georgia from many other states considered for the same investment.
- In 2014 The Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority (JDA) was created and that began the process of searching for an original equipment manufacturer and developing the necessary site for such a project.
- The JDA is a joint venture of Chatham, Effingham, Bryan, and Bulloch Counties.
- The JDA provided more than $15 million towards the purchase of the property.
- Under the Governor’s leadership, the JDA purchased the mega-site in 2021 by leveraging the proceeds of selling properties in Chatham County.
- During the luncheon, Tollison and SEDA recognized many who contributed to making this announcement possible including the county governments of the JDA, The City of Savannah, Thomas and Hutton, Georgia Power, and Bouhan Falligant.
