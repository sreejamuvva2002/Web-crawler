# Sewon America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2025-08-25

**Source:** [Sewon America Launches $50 Million Expansion in Rincon, Bringing 100 New Jobs and Advanced Manufacturing Capabilities | Effingham County Development Authority](https://effinghamindustry.com/news/article/sewon-america-launches-50-million-expansion-in-rincon-bringing-100-new-jobs-and-advanced-manufacturing-capabilities)  ·  `page_001350`

## Facts

- The $50 million project will add 98,250 square feet to the facility in Rincon, GA.
- The expansion will create 100 new jobs.
- This marks the first expansion by a Hyundai supplier since the launch of Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America in late 2024.
- Sewon America is a subsidiary of Korean-based Sewon Precision Industry Co.
- Misti Martin, Deputy Commissioner of Global Commerce at the Georgia Department of Economic Development, called the expansion a "full-circle moment and incredible milestone for the Metaplant’s supplier network."
- Matt Saxon, Chairman of the Effingham County Industrial Development Authority (ECIDA), stated that Sewon America chose Effingham County for its second U.S. facility.
- Rincon Mayor Kevin Exley expressed excitement about more quality jobs in the community and looked forward to partnering with Sewon to ensure local citizens can take advantage of the positions.
- Robert Vazquez, Director of Human Resources at Sewon America, said the expansion will allow the company to better serve customers and add new capabilities to operations.
- Sewon America operates one of the largest automobile body parts manufacturing plants in North America.
- The company opened its first Georgia facility in LaGrange in 2008, which has since doubled in size.
- Sewon America began operations in Rincon in 2025.
- With the expansion, Sewon will continue to supply electric vehicle (EV) body parts for original equipment manufacturers (OEMs), including Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- The project was supported by the Georgia Department of Economic Development, the City of Rincon, and the Effingham County Board of Commissioners.
