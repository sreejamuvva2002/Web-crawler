# Storion Energy LLC

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Largo and Stryten Energy to Form Storion Energy for the Manufacture of Vanadium Electrolyte and Battery Components for Long Duration Energy Storage](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20241219952915/en/Largo-and-Stryten-Energy-to-Form-Storion-Energy-for-the-Manufacture-of-Vanadium-Electrolyte-and-Battery-Components-for-Long-Duration-Energy-Storage)  ·  `page_029418`

## Facts

- Storion Energy LLC intends to become a leading U.S.-based manufacturer of vanadium electrolyte.
- Storion plans to develop additional vanadium flow battery solutions to satisfy projected demand from the long duration energy storage (LDES) sector.
- The joint venture was established through definitive agreements between Largo Clean Energy Corp., a subsidiary of Largo Inc., and Stryten Critical E-Storage, LLC, an affiliate of Stryten Energy LLC.
- Storion aims to remove barriers to entry in the flow battery manufacturing industry and become a U.S. domestic leader for vanadium electrolyte production and supply.
- The partnership will share product and industry knowledge with Stryten Energy LLC and leverage complementary assets by integrating top-tier talent from both companies.
- Upon closing, Largo Clean Energy Corp. and Stryten will each contribute certain vanadium flow battery-related assets and liabilities to Storion.
- Upon closing, Stryten will pay US$1 million in cash directly to Largo and contribute over time a total of US$6 million in cash, for the purpose of funding Storion’s operations.
- Upon closing, Largo Clean Energy Corp. and Stryten will each hold a 50% equity interest in Storion, with customary pre-emption rights and certain other anti-dilution protections.
- Board representation of Storion will be generally proportional to ownership, with Stryten holding one additional seat so long as LCE and Stryten hold similar ownership interests.
- At closing, Largo will amend its Safekeeping and Supply Agreements with Largo Physical Vanadium Corp., assign the Safekeeping Agreement to Storion, and enter a separate supply agreement providing Storion a right of first offer to purchase vanadium products.
- Gallatin Capital LLC acted as financial advisor to Largo in connection with the transaction, and Linklaters LLP acted as legal counsel.
- Storion will have locations in Wilmington, Massachusetts and Alpharetta, Georgia.
- Storion intends to support scalable domestic electrolyte production to establish a fully integrated vertical supply chain for utility-scale vanadium redox flow battery (VRFB) long-duration energy storage solutions.
- VRFB technology is a safe and reliable option to provide long-duration energy storage greater than four hours to help ensure grid stability and facilitate increased utilization of renewables.
- Stryten Energy LLC is headquartered in Alpharetta, Georgia, and provides energy storage solutions across the Essential Power, Motive Power, Transportation, Military and Government sectors.
- Stryten Energy’s stored energy technologies include advanced lead, lithium and vanadium redox flow batteries, intelligent chargers and energy performance management software.
- Largo Inc. is one of the world’s largest primary vanadium producers, sourcing high-quality vanadium and ilmenite from its Maracás Menchen Mine in Brazil.
- Largo holds a 50% ownership stake in Storion Energy as part of its strategic investment in the clean energy storage sector.
- Largo Physical Vanadium Corp. aims to provide direct exposure to physical vanadium, a metal essential for steel, aerospace and energy storage industries, and plans to actively supply vanadium to end users of vanadium flow batteries.
- The transaction is subject to customary closing conditions, with management noting risks including failure to satisfy conditions, inability to realize anticipated benefits, and challenges in raising additional funds.
