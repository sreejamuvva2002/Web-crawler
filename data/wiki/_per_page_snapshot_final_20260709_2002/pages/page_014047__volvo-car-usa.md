# Volvo Car USA

**Type:** workforce_training  ·  **Role:** workforce_training  ·  **Published:** 2021-08-18

**Source:** [Volvo Car USA provides XC40 Recharge to the New York City Fire Department to advance post-collision safety training on battery electric vehicles - The EV Report](https://theevreport.com/volvo-car-usa-provides-xc40-recharge-to-the-new-york-city-fire-department-to-advance-post-collision-safety-training-on-battery-electric-vehicles)  ·  `page_014047`

## Facts

- Volvo Car USA donated a fully electric XC40 Recharge vehicle to the FDNY to advance post-accident safety training involving battery electric vehicles (BEVs).
- The donation will help FDNY update training procedures and real-life protocols for safely extricating passengers from battery-powered vehicles.
- Anders Gustafsson, Senior Vice President of Volvo Car Corporation and President and CEO of Volvo Car USA, and Eric Miller, Vice President of the Northeast Region for Volvo Car USA, handed over the keys to FDNY representatives and members of Manhattan’s Rescue Company 1 at Volvo Cars Manhattan.
- Eric Miller stated that as the automotive industry speeds toward an electric future, first responders will experience changes in how they interact with these vehicles, and the donated XC40 Recharge will help ready the FDNY for this future.
- Fire Commissioner Daniel A. Nigro noted that with the growing number of electric vehicles on the road, it is critical for FDNY members to train on extrications, fires, and other potential incidents involving them, thanking the FDNY Foundation and Volvo Cars for the support.
- The donated XC40 Recharge includes first responder safety features such as a dedicated battery safety cage, independently sealed and cooled battery modules to prevent damage and leaks, brightly colored high voltage wiring to prevent unintentional contact, and an easily accessible deactivation switch to shut down all high voltage power.
