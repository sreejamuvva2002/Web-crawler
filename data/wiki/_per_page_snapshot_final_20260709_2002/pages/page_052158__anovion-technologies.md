# Anovion Technologies

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2023-05-15

**Source:** [Anovion, maker of graphite for electric batteries, plans $800 million factory in southwest Georgia | Georgia Public Broadcasting](https://www.gpb.org/news/2023/05/15/anovion-maker-of-graphite-for-electric-batteries-plans-800-million-factory-in)  ·  `page_052158`

## Facts

- Anovion announced plans to invest $800 million to build a factory in southwest Georgia.
- The new Georgia facility will hire 400 workers.
- Anovion Technologies is headquartered in Chicago.
- The company will manufacture synthetic graphite anodes at the Bainbridge plant.
- Production of synthetic graphite anodes at the Georgia plant is scheduled to start in 2025.
- The facility supports Georgia's broader initiative to attract EV manufacturers and their supply chains.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp stated the project advances his goal of establishing Georgia as the nation's electric mobility capital.
- The majority of battery-grade graphite is currently manufactured outside the United States.
- Since 2020, Georgia has announced over 40 EV-related projects.
- These 40+ projects pledge a combined $22 billion in investment.
- These 40+ projects pledge the creation of 28,000 jobs.
- Anovion was formed last year by merging the graphite operations of Pyrotek and Amsted Graphite Materials.
- The formation of Anovion included new investment from private equity firm Monomyth Group.
- Anovion currently operates existing facilities in Sanborn, New York.
- Anovion currently operates an existing facility in Clarksburg, West Virginia.
- The company previously announced plans to build a graphite factory with a 35,000-ton annual capacity near Muscle Shoals, Alabama.
- Anovion received a $117 million grant under the federal infrastructure law last year.
- The $117 million grant will help finance the planned Alabama plant and upgrades to the existing New York facility.
- Anovion CEO Eric Stopka cited Georgia's workforce, research universities, and growing solar energy production as key attraction factors.
- CEO Eric Stopka stated the company aims to secure the nation's electrified future with support from Georgia partners and the federal government.
- Anovion executives aim to scale production to 150,000 tons annually in the coming years.
- Synthetic graphite is manufactured by heating carbon-based products like petroleum coke (an oil refining byproduct) at super-high temperatures for extended periods.
- The high-temperature furnaces used to produce synthetic graphite require massive amounts of electricity, making power costs a primary location factor.
- Anovion may qualify for a Georgia state income tax credit allowing an annual deduction of $3,000 per job for five years.
- The state income tax credit requires employees to earn at least $31,300 annually.
- The potential state income tax credits could total up to $6 million.
- Anovion may also qualify for property tax breaks from the city of Bainbridge and Decatur County.
- The announcement marks the second major industrial project secured by Bainbridge in two years.
- Bainbridge is located in the far southwest corner of Georgia.
- Danimer Scientific, a biodegradable plastic manufacturer, announced plans in 2021 to build a $700 million factory in Bainbridge.
- The Danimer Scientific project will hire 400 employees.
- Decatur County Commission Chairman Pete Stephens stated that diversifying the local industry mix is necessary for regional competitiveness and growth.
