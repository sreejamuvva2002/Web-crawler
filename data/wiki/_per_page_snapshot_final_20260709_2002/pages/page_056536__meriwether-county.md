# Meriwether County

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2007-12-01

**Source:** [Meriwether County: Embracing Past And Future - Georgia Trend Magazine](https://www.georgiatrend.com/2007/12/01/meriwether-county-embracing-past-and-future/)  ·  `page_056536`

## Facts

- Northern Meriwether County is anticipating industrial growth sparked by a Kia plant set to open in neighboring Troup County.
- The new Kia plant is expected to open in 2009.
- Greenville, the county seat, is using the state’s Better Hometown program to spruce up its business district and encourage economic development.
- A streetscape project in Greenville has taken precedence to be ready for Kia growth.
- Meriwether County is in Phase I of the Entrepreneur Friendly Community program to create an entrepreneur environment by building small business strategies into economic development.
- The county hopes to develop a youth entrepreneur program to provide local career options for young people.
- Meriwether County has been designated a Work Ready Community.
- Gap training for individuals seeking to qualify for certain jobs will be available at no cost at West Georgia Technical College’s Meriwether County Workforce Development Center.
- The Work Ready Region program aligns Meriwether with neighboring counties and education partners West Georgia Technical College and Flint River Technical College to prepare residents for advanced manufacturing employment.
- The Work Ready Region program is in the grant request stage and targets at-risk and out-of-school youth while integrating science, technology, engineering and mathematics into public school curricula.
- The region is concentrating on the advanced manufacturing industry to provide trained employees for the anticipated influx of suppliers that will follow Kia.
- Finishing touches are being put on the new Meriwether Industrial Park located at I-85 and Highway 54.
- The Meriwether Industrial Park spans 900 acres and is a public-private partnership designed to attract Kia suppliers.
- Supplier industry executives find the park's location between the Kia plant and Atlanta’s Hartsfield-Jackson International Airport very attractive.
- A 240-acre Greenville Industrial Park, located next to the Workforce Development Center, is also nearing completion.
- G&S Metals chose the Manchester Industrial Park in southern Meriwether County for its first Southeastern facility.
- G&S Metals opened in 2006 and expects to have 75 employees and invest $7 million by 2009.
- G&S Metals, headquartered in Indiana, supplies the automotive industry with primary-grade recycled aluminum.
- A Special Purpose Local Option Sales Tax (SPLOST) passed in September will fund water work, roads, bridges, public safety, and recreation to entice new industry.
- Luthersville, located seven miles from the new industrial park, is experiencing growth with new business construction after a 25-year gap.
- Meriwether County's population was 22,881 in 2006.
- Unemployment in Meriwether County was 5.8 percent as of September 2007, compared to Georgia's 4.6 percent.
- Largest employers in the county include the Meriwether County School System (850 employees), Roosevelt Warm Springs Institute for Rehabilitation (500), Georgia-Pacific Corp. (335), Angio Dynamics (140), and Warm Springs Medical Center (130).
