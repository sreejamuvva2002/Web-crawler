# Yamaha Motor Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-03-17

**Source:** [Yamaha leaves California after 50 years and moves HQ to Georgia, Gov. Kemp invites other Cal. companies to follow](https://alpharettalifestylepubs.com/yamaha-leaves-california-after-50-years-and-moves-hq-to-georgia-gov-kemp-invites-other-cal-companies-to-follow/)  ·  `page_015822`

## Facts

- Announced plans to relocate its United States headquarters to Kennesaw, Georgia.
- The relocation marks a significant shift after operating its American headquarters in California for roughly five decades.
- State officials state the relocation will begin later this year and unfold gradually through 2028 as corporate operations transition to Georgia.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp welcomed the decision, calling it a major milestone for the state’s growing reputation as a hub for international business and manufacturing.
- Governor Kemp said the decision reflects years of partnership between the state and the company.
- Governor Kemp stated, “After many years of great partnership, we are honored and proud to welcome Yamaha’s American headquarters to the No. 1 state for business.”
- Governor Kemp added, “This is another loud and clear testament to what we offer job creators from around the world. To any other California-based companies looking for a better home, we’ll give you plenty of reasons to keep Georgia on your mind.”
- The governor praised the announcement as further evidence of Georgia’s appeal to major employers.
- Governor Kemp described the state as a welcoming environment for global companies seeking stability, opportunity, and room to grow.
- Kemp suggested the move sends a broader message to businesses considering relocation, emphasizing that Georgia continues to position itself as a leading destination for investment and job creation.
- Yamaha already maintains a substantial footprint across several Georgia locations and employs more than 2,300 people statewide.
- One of Yamaha's largest operations is the Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation of America facility in Newnan, a 1.3-million-square-foot factory that opened in 1986 and now supports roughly 2,000 jobs.
- Over the years, Yamaha has steadily expanded its Georgia presence.
- Its marine division relocated to Kennesaw in 1999, strengthening the company’s foothold in the region.
- More recently, the firm added a 75,000-square-foot Marine Innovation Center in 2023.
- Earlier, in 2019, Yamaha also moved its motorsports operations to Georgia, allowing the company to bring key functions closer together.
- Founded in 1955 and headquartered in Iwata, Japan, Yamaha Motor Co., Ltd. has grown into a global leader in motorized products ranging from motorcycles and ATVs to boat engines and robotics technologies.
- The company operates under a philosophy centered on creating “Kando,” a concept focused on delivering a sense of excitement and fulfillment through its products.
- With the relocation of its U.S. headquarters now underway, Yamaha’s long-standing relationship with Georgia is set to deepen even further in the years ahead.
