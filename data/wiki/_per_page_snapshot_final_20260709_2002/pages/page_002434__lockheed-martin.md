# Lockheed Martin

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-17

**Source:** [Georgia Applauds Lockheed Martin’s 75 Years of Production, Impact | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-releases/2026/georgia-applauds-lockheed-martins-75-years-production-impact)  ·  `page_002434`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp applauded Lockheed Martin’s positive economic impact over its 75 years of continuous operations in Marietta, Georgia.
- The celebration event took place on June 17, 2026, at Lockheed Martin’s Cobb County manufacturing facility in Marietta.
- Attendees included Governor Brian P. Kemp, First Lady Marty Kemp, representatives from the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD), company officials, and state, local, and federal leaders.
- The Marietta facility supports more than 5,600 Georgia jobs.
- Lockheed Martin announced 1,200 new Marietta-based jobs as a milestone in its partnership with Georgia.
- These new jobs are creating meaningful careers while supporting production of the C-130J Super Hercules and F-35 Lightning II aircraft.
- Since 2019, Lockheed Martin has increased its Georgia workforce by more than 25%.
- The company added over 1,200 jobs at its 8-million-square-foot Marietta facility alone since 2019.
- Statewide, Lockheed Martin employs more than 6,300 people.
- In 2025, Lockheed Martin contributed $4.5 billion to Georgia’s economy.
- An additional 550 employees are based at Naval Submarine Base Kings Bay.
- More than 100 employees are based at Robins Air Force Base.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Lockheed Martin has been a cornerstone employer and leader in delivering advanced capabilities for decades.
- Trish Pagan, Vice President of Air Mobility & Maritime Missions and Marietta site general manager, cited Georgia’s favorable business environment, talent pipeline, university system, robust supplier network, and community support as critical strategic foundations.
- The C-130J Super Hercules is Georgia’s top export and anchors the state’s second-largest manufacturing industry.
- The aerospace and aviation industries generated more than $23.9 billion in GDP in 2024.
- At its Marietta center of excellence, Lockheed Martin produces the C-130J Super Hercules and the F-35 Lightning II Center Wing Assembly.
- The facility supports the ongoing sustainment of the F-22 Raptor, C-5 Super Galaxy, and P-3 Orion.
- The Marietta facility is home to the Skunk Works® division, known for cutting-edge innovation.
- The C-130J and F-35 programs alone support more than 20,000 direct and indirect Georgia jobs through robust supplier networks.
- Ember Bishop Bentley, Executive Director of the Georgia Aerospace & Defense Alliance, highlighted the company's role in building a world-class ecosystem of manufacturers, suppliers, research institutions, and skilled workers.
- Marietta has been the global home of C-130 Hercules production since 1955, hosting America’s longest-running military aircraft production line.
- More than 2,700 C-130s have been built at the Marietta site and delivered to over 70 operators worldwide.
- This manufacturing line is America’s last remaining production capacity for building aircraft for the airlift mission.
- Careers at the Lockheed Martin Marietta facility span advanced manufacturing, engineering, AI, data analytics, IT, finance, operations, and more.
- Sharon Mason, President and CEO of the Cobb Chamber of Commerce, noted that Lockheed Martin is one of Cobb County's largest employers and a cornerstone of the community's identity and economy.
- Marietta employees give back 21,000 volunteer hours to schools and organizations each year.
- Lockheed Martin invests in Georgia’s future workforce through partnerships with the University System of Georgia, Technical College System institutions, and K-12 STEM education.
- These workforce initiatives align with Governor Kemp’s Top State for Talent initiative.
- Katie Kirkpatrick, President and CEO of the Metro Atlanta Chamber, emphasized that Lockheed Martin's focus on high-tech jobs creates high-paying careers in the region.
- Georgia’s nine military bases provide competitive advantages to Lockheed Martin and other defense and advanced manufacturing companies.
- Nearly 2,000 veterans, reservists, and military spouses are employed directly by Lockheed Martin.
- The Marietta facility stands poised to lead the next era of aerospace advancement for Georgia and the world.
