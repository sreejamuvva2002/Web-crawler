# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-05

**Source:** [Officials: 475 people detained during Thursday immigration raid at megasite battery plant construction site - Statesboro Herald](https://www.statesboroherald.com/local/officials-475-people-detained-during-thursday-immigration-raid-at-megasite-battery-plant-construction-site/)  ·  `page_001153`

## Facts

- A Homeland Security official said 475 individuals were detained Thursday during an immigration raid at the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America megasite in Ellabell.
- Steven Schrank is the special agent in charge for Homeland Security Investigations, a branch of Immigration and Customs Enforcement, or ICE.
- Schrank described the operation as “the largest single-site enforcement operation in the history of Homeland Security Investigations.”
- The operation was a multi-month criminal investigation dubbed “Operation Low Voltage” according to a press release from the U.S. Attorney’s Office, Southern District of Georgia.
- The investigation concerned collecting evidence related to the unlawful employment of illegal aliens working at both Hyundai and its affiliated contractors.
- Authorities received leads from community members and former workers over the past year in support of the investigation.
- Schrank stated, “This operation underscores our commitment to protecting jobs for Georgians and Americans, ensuring a level playing field for businesses that comply with the law.”
- Hundreds of ICE and Homeland Security agents executed the federal search warrant at HL-GA Battery Company, LLC in Ellabell.
- HL-GA Battery Company sits next to Hyundai’s electric-vehicle producing Metaplant.
- LG Energy Solutions is the parent company of HL-GA Battery.
- LG Energy Solutions is currently partnering with Hyundai in building the adjacent battery plant, which is scheduled to open in 2026.
- Mary Beth Kennedy is a senior public relations specialist for HL-GA Battery Company.
- HL-GA Battery Company released a statement saying it is cooperating fully with the appropriate authorities regarding activity at its construction site and has paused construction to assist their work.
- The majority of the 475 people detained are South Korean nationals.
- Most of the detained individuals were taken to the D. Ray James Correctional Facility in Folkston in South Georgia for further processing.
- Schrank noted that while some of the detained workers illegally crossed the U.S. border, others entered the country legally but had expired visas or had entered on a visa waiver that prohibited them from working.
- Some of those detained worked for the battery manufacturer, while others were employed by contractors and subcontractors at the construction site.
- No “substantial” uses of force were used in the process of detainment.
- Several people attempted to flee the location, running into a sewage pond located on the premises.
- One agent suffered a minor laceration welt during the operation.
- The Georgia State Patrol and the Georgia Department of Public Safety each previously confirmed they assisted federal agencies in serving the warrant.
- The investigation is ongoing, and no criminal charges have been announced as of Friday.
- Operations at Hyundai’s EV manufacturing plant weren’t interrupted by the raid.
- Hyundai Motor Company stated that none of those detained is directly employed by Hyundai Motor Company and emphasized its commitment to prioritizing safety and complying with all laws and regulations.
