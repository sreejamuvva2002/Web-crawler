# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-03-26

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America Celebrates  Grand Opening, Powering U.S. Economic Growth](https://www.kiamedia.com/us/en/media/pressreleases/23059/hyundai-motor-group-metaplant-america-celebrates-grand-opening-powering-us-economic-growth)  ·  `page_001006`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) is now open and represents the key pillar of the Group’s $12.6 billion investment in Georgia, marking the largest economic development project in the state’s history.
- The vehicle assembly and battery plant will produce up to 500,000 electric and hybrid vehicles annually for Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis brands.
- Hyundai Motor Group commits to an additional $21 billion investment from 2025 to 2028 to drive U.S. manufacturing growth.
- Since entering the U.S., Hyundai Motor Group has invested $20.5 billion in the U.S., creating directly and indirectly more than 570,000 American jobs.
- The Grand Opening celebration was hosted on March 26, 2025, in Ellabell, Ga., marking the completion of the project just two and a half years after breaking ground.
- Euisun Chung, Executive Chair of Hyundai Motor Group, stated that HMGMA represents the Group’s advanced manufacturing capabilities, commitment to innovation, and investment in relationships with partners and communities in Georgia.
- The event was attended by Governor Brian P. Kemp, U.S. Representative Buddy Carter, Vice Chair Jaehoon Chang, Hyundai Motor Company President and CEO José Muñoz, Kia Corporation President and CEO Ho Sung Song, company officials, state and local leaders, HMGMA Meta Pro employees, and other distinguished guests.
- Governor Kemp highlighted collaborations between HMGMA and Georgia’s universities and technical colleges, including Georgia Quick Start’s on-site training facility, as well as award-winning infrastructure like ports connecting companies to markets.
- HMG entered a landmark agreement with the State of Georgia in May 2022 to establish an electric and hybrid vehicle production and battery manufacturing facility in Ellabell, Georgia, located 20 miles outside historic Savannah.
- By June 2022, HMGMA was officially formed, and within four months, the company broke ground on the project.
- On October 3, 2024, less than two years after breaking ground, the first vehicle, a Hyundai IONIQ 5, rolled off the assembly line.
- The Hyundai IONIQ 9 three-row electric SUV is now in production at HMGMA and will be joined in 2026 by the first Kia model built at the facility.
- The plant is equipped to produce hybrid vehicles and initially has a capacity of 300,000 vehicles annually.
- The Group's investment in Georgia related to HMGMA and joint battery ventures with LG Energy Solution and SK On totals $12.6 billion, including 8,500 jobs at HMGMA by 2031.
- Total investments in Georgia are expected to create nearly 40,000 direct and indirect jobs and $4.6 billion in individual earnings every year, according to the Center for Automotive Research.
- As of July 2024, more than $2.5 billion in capital investment and approximately 6,900 jobs have been announced by HMGMA suppliers across 12 counties in Georgia, doubling the originally anticipated $1 billion supplier investment.
- The Group plans to expand HMGMA's annual production capacity to a total of 500,000 units, an increase of 200,000 units from the original plan.
- Existing U.S. production sites, Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Alabama and Kia Autoland Georgia, will also see improvements in production facilities.
- A total of $6 billion will be allocated to enhance the Group’s parts and logistics business and establish an Electric Arc Furnace-based integrated steel mill in the U.S. through affiliate Hyundai Steel, with an annual capacity of 2.7 million tons.
- Commercial steel production at the new mill is scheduled to begin in 2029, supplying high-quality automotive steel plates to HMGMA.
- The Group will invest an additional $6 billion to drive innovation and expand strategic partnerships with U.S. companies in autonomous driving, robotics, artificial intelligence, and advanced air mobility.
- Through the $21 billion commitment, the Group anticipates creating 14,000 new direct full-time jobs in the U.S. by 2028, generating more than 100,000 direct and indirect job opportunities across related industries.
- HMGMA has integrated proof-of-concept manufacturing technologies and systems from Hyundai Motor Group Innovation Center Singapore (HMGICS), scaled to accommodate larger production volumes.
- All HMGMA processes, including order collection, procurement, logistics, and production, are optimized using AI and real-time data.
- Cutting-edge robotics and vision systems ensure high-quality vehicle production, creating a human-centered work environment where robots assist human workers to improve efficiency.
- Automation in HMGMA’s assembly shop is nearly double that of the Group’s existing facilities, featuring automated guided vehicles for logistics, Boston Dynamic’s Spot for exterior quality inspection in the weld shop, and parking robots in the assembly shop.
- Oscar Kwon, CEO of HMGMA, emphasized creating a comfortable and welcoming work environment for employees (Meta Pros) alongside an energy-efficient and technologically advanced plant.
- Work zones from office to production areas feature open layouts with ample daylight, indoor landscaping, and team centers with skylights in the assembly building for employee breaks and meetings.
- The 3,050-foot-long front of the HMGMA building’s exterior is lit in two parallel lines inspired by a stream, outlining an area where interstate travelers can view vehicles moving from paint to general assembly.
- A solar parking lot at the front of the site will generate 5.2 megawatts of energy, accounting for roughly five percent of the Metaplant’s total power consumption, installed above 1,878 parking spaces.
- Construction is ongoing on a 41-acre ecological park featuring trails, running tracks, sports fields, and picnic areas in front of the Metaplant for employees.
- HMGMA has deployed 21 Hyundai Motor XCIENT heavy-duty hydrogen fuel-cell electric trucks for clean logistics operations, representing more than a third of the Glovis America truck fleet at the facility.
- These hydrogen trucks transport vehicle parts from suppliers across the region to the 2,906-acre Megasite where HMGMA is located on a daily basis.
- Since 2022, Hyundai and Genesis have donated nearly $4.5 million to local organizations in Georgia through social responsibility initiatives and nonprofits.
- Kia's Autoland Georgia factory in West Point has provided over $22 million in support of Georgia communities since 2009, including more than $6 million for educational programs like the THINC College & Career Academy and SAE’s “A World in Motion.”
- HMGMA is Hyundai Motor Group’s first dedicated electrified vehicle mass-production plant located in Bryan County, GA, featuring a highly connected, automated, and flexible manufacturing system.
- Hyundai Motor Group operates with about 250,000 employees worldwide and includes mobility brands Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis, alongside value chains in steel, construction, logistics, finance, IT, and service.
