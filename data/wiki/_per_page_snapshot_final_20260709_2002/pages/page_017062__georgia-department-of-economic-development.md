# Georgia Department of Economic Development

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2023-01-19

**Source:** [Key Industries Sustain Momentum for Georgia's Economic Development - AllOnGeorgia](https://allongeorgia.com/georgia-business/key-industries-sustain-momentum-for-georgias-economic-development/)  ·  `page_017062`

## Facts

- The automotive industry was the second-largest source of new jobs and investment, after advanced manufacturing.
- In October, state and local officials joined company representatives, including Korean Ambassador to the U.S. Tae-yong Cho, for the groundbreaking of the new Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) EV facility.
- The HMGMA EV facility will support more than 8,100 direct jobs in Bryan, Bulloch, Chatham and Effingham Counties.
- Three off-site HMGMA suppliers announced locations following HMGMA’s groundbreaking, swiftly surpassing the initial $1 billion in investment predicted to come from such additions.
- International companies have been valued partners in creating a closed-loop electric mobility supply chain and accounted for 23 percent of state projects since July 1, 2022.
- Historic project examples include the partnership between SK On and Hyundai Motor Group in Bartow County.
- The SK On and Hyundai Motor Group partnership is one of the top five largest projects by investment in the state’s history.
- Next-generation developer FREYR Battery announced a Coweta County facility, which is also among the top five largest projects by investment in the state’s history.
- Battery recyclers Sung-Il Hitech and Denkai America joined Georgia’s growing electric mobility ecosystem.
- Four of Georgia’s largest industries – advanced manufacturing, automotive, aerospace, and food processing – accounted for nearly 75 percent of new jobs and 84 percent of investments.
- Planned investments resulting from these projects increased by more than 38 percent, compared to the same time last year.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson highlighted proven solutions such as the Georgia Quick Start job training program and GRAD certified sites that support accelerated project timelines for companies.
- GDEcD Deputy Commissioner for Global Commerce Kristi Brigman noted that companies use programs like Georgia Quick Start to solve workforce challenges and build talent from the ground up.
