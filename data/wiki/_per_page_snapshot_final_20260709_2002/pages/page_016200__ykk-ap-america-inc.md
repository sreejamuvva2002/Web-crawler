# YKK AP America Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Read more about Governor Kemp: Building Materials Manufacturer YKK AP America Expands Residential Window Production Capabilities with New Facility in Bibb County - EIN Presswire](https://www.einnews.com/pr_news/586350420/read-more-about-governor-kemp-building-materials-manufacturer-ykk-ap-america-expands-residential-window-production-capabilities-with-new-facility-in)  ·  `page_016200`

## Facts

- YKK AP America Inc. will invest up to $125 million in an expansion that includes a new manufacturing facility and additional machinery and equipment in Bibb County.
- The expansion will create 100 new jobs in addition to the 250 jobs YKK AP currently supports in Macon, Georgia.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced the investment and stated that being the No. 1 state for business means ensuring companies already operating in Georgia can grow and thrive.
- Governor Kemp noted that YKK AP recognizes the advantages of doing business in middle Georgia and has chosen to expand its relationship with the Macon community through 100 new jobs.
- YKK AP specializes in architectural products for commercial and residential buildings and entered the U.S. market in 1991 with a vertically integrated commercial products factory in Dublin, Georgia.
- With headquarters in Atlanta, YKK AP and its Ontario-based subsidiary Erie Architectural Products employ over 1,200 employees in North America, including nearly 900 within the State of Georgia.
- President Oliver Stepe stated the new facility will expand the workforce, introduce further vertical integration of vinyl extrusion, glass, and window production, and allow enhanced efficiencies to manufacture high-quality windows and doors for homebuilders and homeowners.
- The investment builds toward YKK AP's long-term vision to be the leader in the residential window and door market and positions the company to expand its business in and beyond the Southeast region.
- The new state-of-the-art facility will be located at the Macon-Bibb I-75 Industrial Park, a Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) certified site.
- After completion, YKK AP will relocate its operations and employees from its current site on Ocmulgee Boulevard to the new facility.
- The Macon facility will become home to the company's residential division, which manufactures vinyl windows and doors for builders and homeowners.
- The facility will serve as a model plant for future North American manufacturing facilities, featuring new technologies, advanced sustainable manufacturing processes, and enhanced work environments for employees.
- Operations at the new facility are expected to begin in early 2024.
- YKK AP is currently hiring for numerous positions related to the expansion.
- Macon-Bibb County Mayor Lester M. Miller highlighted that one of their longest-running business partners is making another investment in the community and bringing an additional 100 jobs to the area.
- Mayor Miller noted that using the new state-of-the-art facility as a blueprint for future facilities illustrates YKK AP's trust in Macon-Bibb County and Georgia as the best place to do business.
- Senior Project Manager Candice Scott represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s Global Commerce team on this competitive project in partnership with the Macon-Bibb County Industrial Authority and Georgia Power.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that YKK AP opened its first branch in Atlanta in 1986 and chose middle Georgia to establish its U.S. manufacturing presence five years later.
- Commissioner Wilson noted that companies in the advanced manufacturing industry, like YKK AP, have played a key role in bringing jobs and opportunities to Georgia, creating the second most new jobs in the state last year.
- To earn GRAD certification, a property must undergo due diligence and complete specific certification requirements prior to a visit by a prospective business.
- Completing GRAD certification with GDEcD is a proactive way for community planners and landowners to help catalyze economic growth and industrial development for their regions.
- Through the GRAD program, Georgia has more than 60 industrial certified sites that are ready for fast-track industrial projects.
