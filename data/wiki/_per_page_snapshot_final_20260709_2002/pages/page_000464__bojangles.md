# Bojangles

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** charging_infrastructure  ·  **Published:** 2026-05-20

**Source:** [Iconic Southern Restaurant Chain Bojangles Installs First EV Charging Station - EVinfo.net](https://evinfo.net/2026/05/iconic-southern-restaurant-chain-bojangles-installs-first-ev-charging-station/)  ·  `page_000464`

## Facts

- On May 20, 2026, Bojangles announced the launch of its first EV charging station at its location on Ogeechee Road in Savannah, Georgia.
- The installation introduces a new “charge-and-dine” experience for customers.
- The move positions the fast-growing chain as one of the latest quick-service restaurant brands investing in EV infrastructure to accommodate drivers seeking convenient recharge during daily travel.
- Richard Del Valle, Chief Information Officer at Bojangles, stated that driving trends and expectations are changing, and EV charging turns a stop into an opportunity to relax and refuel.
- The Savannah site was developed in partnership with XLR8 America and Energy and Environmental Design Services.
- This installation marks the beginning of a broader national rollout planned for Bojangles locations across key U.S. markets.
- The charging stations will include both Level 2 and Level 3 fast chargers featuring more than 97% uptime.
- The hardware installed is XCHARGE North America’s C7 Ultra Fast Charger, which offers output up to 400kW in a standalone, compact design.
- Frank O’Connor, CEO of XLR8 America, stated that the partnership aligns with their philosophy to “charge where you park, not park where you charge,” aiming to build a national network making EV charging effortless.
- Bojangles was founded in Charlotte, North Carolina in 1977 and has grown into a chain with more than 870 company-owned and franchised locations across 23 states.
- XLR8 America’s business model allows restaurants, retailers, hospitality brands, and commercial property operators to deploy turnkey EV charging solutions with no upfront capital cost to the host site.
- XLR8 America promotes integrating charging infrastructure into locations consumers already visit regularly rather than dedicated charging destinations.
- XLR8 America reports its active project portfolio now exceeds $250 million in value as demand for EV charging infrastructure continues expanding nationwide.
- EVinfo.net commentary notes that restaurant owners who have not installed EV charging are losing money by missing the opportunity to attract EV drivers spending at nearby businesses.
- Bojangles is attracting an ever-growing number of EV drivers who spend money at or close to where they charge their cost-saving, clean electric vehicles.
- Competitors of Bojangles that have not yet installed charging are missing this opportunity and losing revenue as a result.
- EVinfo.net spoke with Devin and Ryan Grandis of Energy and Environmental Design Services regarding the project.
