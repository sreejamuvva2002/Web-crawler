# Milledgeville-Baldwin County Industrial Park

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2025-04-26

**Source:** [News & Announcements | Baldwin County Development Authority](https://developmilledgeville.com/news-announcements/)  ·  `page_020088`

## Facts

- Commemorative ceremonies to name streets at the Milledgeville-Baldwin County Industrial Park will be held on Saturday, April 26, and Monday, April 28, 2025.
- Earl Seagle Court and Henry Pope Way are the newly named streets honoring two individuals instrumental in shaping the area’s industrial landscape.
- The newly named streets intersect at the park, symbolizing the lasting impact of Seagle and Pope’s shared commitment to economic development and community growth.
- Mr. Earl Seagle is in his 90s and is being recognized for decades of dedicated public service following a distinguished career in the aerospace industry.
- Seagle relocated to Baldwin County in the 1970s to work with Grumman after the company established a manufacturing facility in the area.
- In retirement, Seagle served on both the Milledgeville-Baldwin County Development Authority Board of Directors and the Fall Line Regional Development Authority Board of Directors.
- The dedication of Earl Seagle Court will take place at 11:30 a.m. on Saturday, April 26, 2025, at the Milledgeville-Baldwin County Industrial Park.
- Henry Pope Jr. served for many years as a member and chairman of the Development Authority.
- Under Pope’s leadership, the Authority completed successful spec building projects and helped recruit numerous new industries to Milledgeville and Baldwin County.
- Pope was president of Exchange Bank in Milledgeville and built enduring relationships throughout the community.
- One of the park’s previously unnamed streets, home to Fireline, Inc., will officially be named Henry Pope Way.
- The dedication of Henry Pope Way will be held on Monday, April 28, 2025, at 2 p.m. at the Milledgeville-Baldwin County Industrial Park off GA Hwy 22.
- In March 2024, the Milledgeville City Council unanimously passed resolutions to accept the conveyance of the two streets from the Development Authority.
- Earl Seagle Court and Henry Pope Way will serve as lasting reminders of efforts to attract, retain, and grow business in the region.
- The families of Henry Pope and Earl Seagle attended the Milledgeville City Council meeting in March 2024 when the council accepted the street conveyance from the Development Authority of the City of Milledgeville – Baldwin County.
