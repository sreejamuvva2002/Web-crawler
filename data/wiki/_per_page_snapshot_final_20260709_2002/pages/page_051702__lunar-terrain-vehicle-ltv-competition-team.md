# Lunar Terrain Vehicle (LTV) Competition Team

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Nissan, Sierra Space, Teledyne Brown, Textron Team For Moon Buggy | Aviation Week](https://aviationweek.com/shows-events/space-symposium/nissan-sierra-space-teledyne-brown-textron-team-moon-buggy)  ·  `page_051702`

## Facts

- Teledyne Brown Engineering announced April 6 that it is leading a team with Sierra Space, Nissan North America and Textron Arctic Cat to design the crewed Lunar Terrain Vehicle (LTV).
- Teledyne Brown Engineering built the first lunar rover prototype for Dr. Wernher von Braun in the 1960s and has subsequently supported almost every major U.S. human-rated space mission.
- Sierra Space will provide flight software, space qualified mechanisms, communications, pointing, navigation, and timing for the LTV.
- Former astronaut Steve Lindsey serves as Sierra Space’s chief strategy officer.
- Nissan North America brings long-running experience in mass-market automotive design and production to the team.
- Nissan's U.S.-based arm operates four manufacturing facilities capable of producing 1 million vehicles, 1.4 million engines, 1.4 million forgings and 456,000 castings annually.
- For the LTV project, Nissan will focus on autonomy and teleoperations, power management systems, vehicle connectivity, and human-machine interface.
- Maarten Sierhuis is director of Nissan’s Alliance Innovation Laboratory in Silicon Valley.
- Textron Specialized Vehicles will contribute Arctic Cat off-road capabilities for chassis design, vehicle dynamics and suspension handling.
- Textron Specialized Vehicles operates separately from Textron Aviation and Textron Systems.
- Venturi Astrolab unveiled its Flex rover family last month; the startup comprises NASA and SpaceX alumni based in Hawthorne, California.
- Northrop Grumman announced in November that it is leading an industry team pitching an LTV, partnering with AVL, Intuitive Machines, Lunar Outpost and Michelin.
- Lockheed Martin announced in May 2021 that it was partnering with General Motors to develop an LTV.
- In 2020, the Japan Aerospace Exploration Agency (JAXA) said it was working with Toyota on a pressurized rover utilizing fuel-cell electric vehicle technology.
