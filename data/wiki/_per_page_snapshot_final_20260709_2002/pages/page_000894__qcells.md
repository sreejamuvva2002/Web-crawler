# QCells

## Facts

- Solar manufacturer QCells has started making the key components that go into solar panels at its new factory in Cartersville, Georgia.
- This production start marks a major milestone in moving solar production to the US.
- QCells has been assembling solar modules in Dalton, Georgia, since 2019.
- Until now, the ingots, wafers and cells that make up those panels have been manufactured overseas.
- Scott Moskowitz, vice president of industry affairs for QCells, explained that ingots, wafers, and cells are where the critical technology of a solar panel comes from and what actually powers and creates electricity from it.
- Making those parts abroad can create problems with customs, tariffs, and other supply chain issues.
- Last year, QCells slowed down production and implemented furloughs due to such delays.
- Making the cells locally should prevent those kinds of problems in the future, Moskowitz said.
- Moskowitz called the milestone a really meaningful one for QCells and the US solar industry.
- Moskowitz stated that this development more than doubles the available capacity of US-made cells in the United States.
- The solar, battery, and electric vehicle industries are all trying to move more manufacturing to the US.
- These industries aim to address supply chain issues and take full advantage of tax credits that favor domestic production.
- Georgia has been a leader in that transition, Moskowitz said.
- Moskowitz noted that Georgia has recognized manufacturing is important to its citizens and economy.
- QCells plans to fully ramp up production at the new factory later this year.
- The factory is the first of its kind in the US, where all the major parts of a solar module will be made under one roof.
- Once both facilities are at full capacity, the two QCells facilities in Georgia are expected to produce 47,000 panels a day.
- This output is enough to power more than one million homes for a year, according to the company.
