# Aurubis

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2021-11-11

**Source:** [Aurubis says it's going to build a US e-scrap smelter - Resource Recycling](https://resource-recycling.com/e-scrap/2021/11/11/aurubis-says-its-going-to-build-a-us-e-scrap-smelter/)  ·  `page_001268`

## Facts

- Germany-headquartered Aurubis announced on Nov. 10 its project to build a secondary smelter in Augusta, Ga.
- Aurubis is one of the largest copper recyclers in the world and stated it will be the first secondary smelter for multi-metal recycling in the U.S.
- This announcement marks the second major downstream e-scrap project in Georgia in recent weeks, following Igneo Technologies' plan to build a large metals recovery plant in the same area.
- Igneo Technologies is connected to French e-scrap metals recycling company WEEE Metallica.
- The Augusta facility will process 90,000 metric tons of recovered materials annually.
- At full capacity, the facility will produce up to 35,000 metric tons of blister copper per year.
- Blister copper is an intermediate copper product that is partly purified and has a blistered surface from the smelting process.
- The smelter will use metallurgical technologies described as proven in the market yet still innovative.
- The Georgia facility is located a two-hour-and-15-minute drive from the Port of Savannah.
- The plant will ship some intermediate metal products to Aurubis's European smelters for further refining into pure industrial and precious metals.
- The Georgia plant also expects to sell some of its output to U.S. companies for further processing.
- The new site is located in Richmond County, Ga., and will be named Aurubis Richmond, USA.
- Aurubis plans to invest about 300 million euros (worth about $345 million at Nov. 10 exchange rates) in the project.
- The facility will create over 100 jobs.
- The site will be built on 150 acres at the Augusta Corporate Park, according to an announcement from the governor’s office.
- Aurubis CEO Roland Harings stated that the investment sets a clear example of sustainable growth and positions the company as a forerunner for multi-metal recycling in the U.S., mirroring its existing role in Europe.
- Construction is scheduled to begin in summer 2022.
- Commissioning is set for early 2024.
- Full production capacity could be reached in the 2025-26 fiscal year, according to Aurubis.
