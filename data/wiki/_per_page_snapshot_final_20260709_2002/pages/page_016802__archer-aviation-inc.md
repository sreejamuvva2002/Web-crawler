# Archer Aviation Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Energy, Automobile, EV, Renewable News | Stellantis Invests Additional $55 Million In Archer Following Recent Flight Test Milestone](https://oilandgaspress.com/stellantis-invests-additional-55-million-in-archer-following-recent-flight-test-milestone/)  ·  `page_016802`

## Facts

- Stellantis invests an additional $55 million in Archer following the achievement of its transition flight test milestone.
- This latest investment builds on Stellantis’ series of open market purchases of 8.3 million shares of Archer’s stock in March of this year.
- During 2023, Stellantis invested $110 million in Archer through a combination of open market stock purchases and investments under the companies’ strategic funding agreement.
- Construction nears completion on Archer’s high-volume manufacturing facility in Covington, GA, which is on track to be completed later this year.
- The first phase of the build out is a ~350,000 square foot facility on an ~100 acre site designed to support production of up to 650 aircraft annually.
- This facility would make it one of the largest manufacturing facilities by volume in the aircraft industry.
- Archer’s goal with this facility is to establish a factory that can support its planned commercial ramp up by leveraging Stellantis as its contract manufacturer.
- Stellantis has been a strategic partner to Archer since 2020 through various collaboration initiatives, and as an investor since 2021.
- During this time, Archer has leveraged Stellantis’ deep manufacturing, supply chain, and design expertise in connection with efforts to design, develop, and commercialize its eVTOL aircraft.
- Archer’s Midnight is a piloted, four-passenger aircraft designed to perform rapid back-to-back flights with minimal charge time between flights.
- Archer aims to transform urban travel by replacing 60–90 minute commutes by car with estimated 10–20 minute electric air taxi flights that are safe, sustainable, low noise, and cost-competitive with ground transportation.
- Stellantis CEO Carlos Tavares stated that the additional investment keeps them on course for a future where freedom of mobility extends beyond today’s roads.
- Archer CEO Adam Goldstein highlighted the commitment by Stellantis to provide manufacturing expertise and capital needed to accelerate Archer’s business objectives.
