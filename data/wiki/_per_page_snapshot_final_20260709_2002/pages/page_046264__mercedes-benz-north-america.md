# Mercedes-Benz North America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Mercedes-Benz Establishing North American Headquarters, new Research & Development Hub in Metro Atlanta - Metro Atlanta Chamber](https://metroatlantachamber.com/mercedes-benz-establishing-north-american-headquarters-new-research-development-hub-in-metro-atlanta/)  ·  `page_046264`

## Facts

- Georgia announced Mercedes-Benz's expansion and new Research & Development hub.
- Mercedes-Benz will establish Atlanta as its headquarters in North America by centralizing and uniting key corporate functions.
- The company will move up to 500 jobs to the existing Mercedes-Benz facility, known as “1MB,” in Fulton County.
- The company will make a multi-million dollar investment in a future state-of-the-art Research & Development (R&D) facility to also be located nearby.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Mercedes-Benz will establish Atlanta as its North American headquarters by centralizing and uniting key corporate functions.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Georgia continues to lead the way in the future of mobility and technical innovation, attracting world-class companies like Mercedes-Benz that are driving the automotive industry forward.
- Governor Brian Kemp expressed excitement that a job creator with close ties to Georgia is doubling down on its choice and growing its presence in the best state for business and opportunity.
- Mercedes-Benz opened its “1MB” facility in 2018 in Sandy Springs.
- The “1MB” facility currently supports approximately 800 jobs in Georgia.
- Jason Hoff, CEO of Mercedes-Benz North America, thanked the State of Georgia for its support in deepening Mercedes-Benz’s roots in the Atlanta area as they bring more talented team members to the city.
- Jason Hoff stated that the expansion strengthens their position for continued growth and reinforces their established commitment to the U.S. market.
- Jason Hoff noted that bringing teams closer together will enable agility, increase speed to market, and ensure the best customer experience.
- The “1MB” facility located in Sandy Springs will house existing sales teams, financial services teams, and corporate functions.
- The new state-of-the-art Research & Development hub will be located near Sandy Springs.
- Mercedes-Benz anticipates that the move to metro Atlanta will be completed by August 2026.
- Mayor Rusty Paul of the City of Sandy Springs expressed excitement about Mercedes-Benz expanding in Sandy Springs.
- Mayor Rusty Paul stated that since establishing their headquarters in 2018, Mercedes-Benz has been an outstanding corporate partner.
- Mayor Rusty Paul noted that the decision to grow in Sandy Springs highlights the success of the city’s recent infrastructure and capital investments which are now clearly paying dividends.
- Mayor Rusty Paul described the expansion as a wonderful opportunity and a significant milestone for continued development.
- Robb Pitts, Chairman of the Fulton County Board of Commissioners, stated that having a globally recognized brand like Mercedes-Benz reaffirm its commitment is a testament to the strength and vitality of the community.
- Robb Pitts noted that this proves Fulton County continues to be a destination for corporate solutions, providing major companies an accessible, vibrant, and growing community for their business to thrive in.
- Katie Kirkpatrick, President & CEO of the Metro Atlanta Chamber, stated that the expansion is a testament to both Mercedes-Benz’s commitment to excellence and metro Atlanta’s strength as a hub for innovation and talent.
- Katie Kirkpatrick noted that when the 1MB facility opened in 2018, it quickly became an integral part of the business landscape, driving economic growth and elevating the region’s global presence.
- Katie Kirkpatrick expressed pride in seeing the partnership deepen as Mercedes-Benz continues to invest in future shared success.
- Elizabeth McLean, Assistant Director of Statewide Projects, represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The project was executed in partnership with the City of Sandy Springs, Select Fulton, Metro Atlanta Chamber, and Georgia Power.
- Pat Wilson, GDEcD Commissioner, stated that since the strategic decision to relocate Mercedes-Benz USA to Sandy Springs in 2018, they have become an integral part of the business community.
- Pat Wilson noted that their continued growth and community involvement are a prime example of why Georgia recruits industry leaders such as Mercedes-Benz.
- Pat Wilson stated that this expansion and commitment to R&D in the metro Atlanta area will further strengthen the company’s long-term success and highlights the talent and collaborative partnerships fostered by the University System of Georgia.
