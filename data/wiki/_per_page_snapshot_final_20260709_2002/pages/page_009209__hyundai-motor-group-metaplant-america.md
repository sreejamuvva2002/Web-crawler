# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-06

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor plays down missed IRA incentives for Georgia plant - THE INVESTOR](https://m.theinvestor.co.kr/article/3851672)  ·  `page_009209`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor missed out on United States tax incentives for its new $7.6-billion manufacturing plant, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America, in Bryan County, Georgia.
- The missed credit did not affect Hyundai Motor's investment in the plant.
- Industry sources reported Monday that Hyundai Motor was not included in the list of companies receiving tax incentives in the second round allocations of the Qualifying Advanced Energy Project Credit (48C).
- The 48C program is an allocated tax credit funded by President Joe Biden’s Investing in America agenda through the Inflation Reduction Act.
- The Biden administration has assigned a total of $10 billion to the 48C program.
- Selected companies under the 48C program could receive up to a 30 percent tax credit for investments on American soil.
- A local report citing unnamed industry sources estimated Hyundai Motor could have received up to 480 billion won ($344 million) in tax incentives.
- Construction at the Georgia plant has proceeded smoothly despite missing the tax incentives.
- Hyundai Motor began early-stage production of electric vehicles at HMGMA in October.
- The new plant is expected to start full operation in the first quarter of next year.
- A Hyundai Motor official stated that while the company applied for the 48C program, it did not expect benefits because those tax incentives were made for parts makers, not automakers.
- The same official noted that the 48C program was not in consideration when Hyundai Motor invested in HMGMA.
- The US Department of Energy announced a list of 35 projects across 20 states selected for $1.93 billion in the first round allocations of the 48C program in April this year.
- The 48C projects were categorized into four areas: clean energy and clean vehicle manufacturing, critical minerals and materials, grid components and modernization, and industrial decarbonization.
- Mobis North America electrified Powertrain, a US subsidiary of Hyundai Motor Group’s auto parts maker Hyundai Mobis, was awarded $57.6 million in tax credits.
- Mobis North America electrified Powertrain is set to construct three different EV component plants that will provide parts for the production of Hyundai and Kia electric vehicles.
