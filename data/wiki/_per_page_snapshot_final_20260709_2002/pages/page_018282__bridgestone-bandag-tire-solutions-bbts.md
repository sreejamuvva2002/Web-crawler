# Bridgestone Bandag Tire Solutions (BBTS)

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Bridgestone Bandag launches retread campaign | FleetOwner](https://www.fleetowner.com/operations/article/21680951/bridgestone-bandag-launches-retread-campaign)  ·  `page_018282`

## Facts

- Rising oil prices are contributing to a rapid escalation in new tire costs.
- Bridgestone Bandag Tire Solutions (BBTS), a division of Bridgestone Americas Tire Operations, is launching a campaign to promote the wider use of retreads by truck fleets.
- Tightness in the market supply for new replacement tires in 2011 has made retreading more attractive for all types of fleets.
- Chris Ripani, BBTS director of marketing for strategic brands and channels, explained that fleets are extending tire casing age limits to realize maximum value from owned casings.
- Fleets are returning to or evaluating retreads for the first time as a strategy to maximize their tire programs and keep their trucks rolling.
- BBTS launched a new website designed to provide fleet managers with tools and information on how to get the most out of retreading.
- The website includes a Wheel Position Analyzer tool, a cost-analysis example for considering extending current casing age limits, and an informational video on Shearography inspection technology.
- Mike Bice, tire program manager for Crete Carrier Corp., extended his company's tire casing life from five to seven years.
- Crete Carrier spent about five years tweaking its tire program and diagnosing scrap pile data to determine that a seven-year casing age limit provided the best return on investment.
- By adding two years to the casing age limit, Crete gained one to two additional retreads per casing.
- This strategy significantly lowered overall operating costs for maintaining over 172,000 wheel positions within Crete's fleet.
- BBTS conducted studies of more than 13 million tires rejected for retreading and determined that 30% of those commercial truck-tire casings could have been retreaded at least one more time if the repair specs had been different.
- The Retread Tire Association notes that every typical-size truck tire retread saves approximately 15 gallons of oil.
- Retreading also results in one less tire going to overcrowded landfills.
