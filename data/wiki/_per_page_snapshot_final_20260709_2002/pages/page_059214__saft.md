# Saft

## Facts

- Saft is a French company involved in the design, development, and manufacturing of batteries used in transport, industry, and defense.
- The company was established in 1918 by founder Victor Hérold.
- In 1953, the US Naval Air Command requested 2000 batteries of 24 V, with a contract valued at US$12,033,582 in 2025 dollars.
- In 1980, together with PSA, Saft conducted a study to evaluate the potential of using batteries to power electric cars.
- Following the 1980 study, investment was focused on building a sophisticated robotic assembly line.
- It became the leading manufacturer of nickel-cadmium batteries both in the aviation sector and in the railway sector.
- In the 1970s, Saft opened a subsidiary in the United States.
- In 2001, Saft acquired from Invensys plc the American company Hawker Eternacell, a leading provider of lithium batteries to the American and British armed forces.
- In January 2006, Saft and Johnson Controls Inc announced the launch of a joint venture named Johnson Controls-Saft Advanced Power Solutions LLC to develop, produce, and sell advanced technology batteries for hybrid electric and electric vehicles.
- In January 2008, Johnson Controls-Saft opened the first production facility for lithium-ion hybrid vehicle batteries in Nersac, France.
- In 2009, Johnson Controls-Saft started the construction of a lithium-ion production plant in Michigan.
- In 2009, Saft built another lithium-ion production plant for itself in Florida.
- In May 2011, Johnson Controls requested the dissolution of the joint venture to the Delaware Court of Chancery due to increasing dissatisfaction with agreement restrictions.
- The two companies agreed to the separation, and Johnson Controls paid Saft $145 million for its shares in the joint venture as well as for the right to use certain technology developed by it.
- Johnson Controls retained the Michigan facility built by the partnership after the dissolution.
- The French joint facility was transferred to Saft following the separation.
- In February 2013, Airbus dropped the use of lithium-ion batteries for its A350 due to Boeing's difficulties with the 787 Dreamliner, which hit Saft because it had a supply contract with the aircraft manufacturer.
- The company publicly denied a major impact from the Airbus battery decision.
- In July 2013, Saft was awarded a $6.5 million contract to supply high power lithium-ion batteries for the Lockheed Martin F-35 Lightning II.
- Saft supplies the battery for the Exagon Furtive-eGT, a four-seat electric sports car produced by Exagon Motors.
- The battery supplied to the Exagon Furtive-eGT is capable of withstanding 3,000 charge cycles while conserving 80% of its capacity.
- In 2016, Total (later renamed TotalEnergies) acquired all Saft shares and delisted it from the stock exchange in August of that year.
- In 2020, the Saft part of TotalEnergies signed a joint venture with the Stellantis Group to develop and produce lithium cells for electric vehicles.
- The Automotive Cell Company (AAC) joint venture shall set up a pilot plant at Saft in Nersac.
- A gigafactory is planned to follow in Douvrin, Hauts-de-France, and later in Kaiserslauten, Germany.
- Saft's Aerospace, Defense, and Performance division produces nickel, lithium-ion, and silver-based batteries for aerospace, defense, and performance applications.
- Saft's Industry, Mobility, and Infrastructure division produces nickel technology and lithium-ion batteries for applications including the electrification of industrial vehicles and railway on-board traction systems.
- As of 2023, Saft reported revenue of €1.2 billion.
- Saft has 4,300 employees.
- Cedric Duclos serves as the CEO of Saft.
