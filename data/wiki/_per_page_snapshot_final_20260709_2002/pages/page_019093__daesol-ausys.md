# Daesol Ausys

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2025-01-02

**Source:** [Daesol Ausys Invests USD 72 mn in Georgia Plant for EV Parts](https://www.wiondrive.com/news/daesol-ausys-invests-usd-72-mn-in-georgia-plant-for-ev-parts-4874/)  ·  `page_019093`

## Facts

- South Korean company Daesol Ausys unveiled plans to establish a $72 million factory in Georgia.
- The facility will be focused on producing electric vehicle (EV) components, specifically luggage boards and compartment covers.
- The company intends to employ over 140 workers at the new plant.
- The factory will be constructed in West Point, Georgia.
- This will mark the third factory within the same West Point industrial park connected to the company.
- Daehan Solution Co. started a factory in the park in 2008 for sound insulation parts.
- Daesol Material established a factory in the park in 2017 to create automotive fiber padding.
- Daesol Ausys is a key supplier to Hyundai, Kia, and General Motors.
- The initiative reflects a growing trend of EV suppliers choosing Georgia for their facilities.
- Hyundai previously announced a $5.5 billion plant in the state to assemble EVs and batteries near Savannah.
- Hyundai plans to commence vehicle production at its Georgia plant in 2025.
- The wave of investment is anticipated to create nearly 5,000 jobs in the region.
- Georgia’s automotive industry already employs more than 50,000 individuals.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp emphasized that the state's automotive industry employs over 50,000 hardworking Georgians and will continue to grow as e-mobility suppliers choose all corners of the state for future jobs.
- Daesol Ausys aims to begin production in December 2024.
- The state of Georgia is actively supporting the venture by providing training for workers.
- The company has received financial incentives including property tax breaks worth around $5.7 million.
- The company has also received potential state income tax credits totaling $875,000.
- The investment adds to Georgia's increasing prominence in the electric vehicle supply chain and reinforces the region's position as a vital player in the automotive industry's electrification efforts.
