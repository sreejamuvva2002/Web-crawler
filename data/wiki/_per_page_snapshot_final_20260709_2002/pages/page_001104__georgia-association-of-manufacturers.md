# Georgia Association of Manufacturers

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** workforce_training  ·  **Published:** 2025-02-27

**Source:** [Georgia Association of Manufacturers Partners with Unmudl to Launch Innovative Digital Platform: Manufacturing Georgia](https://unmudl.com/news/georgia-association-of-manufacturers-partners-with-unmudl-to-launch-innovative-digital-platform-manufacturing-georgia)  ·  `page_001104`

## Facts

- GAM announced the development of “Manufacturing Georgia,” the first-ever digital platform designed to help Georgia manufacturers engage directly with the public.
- Manufacturing Georgia is being developed through a multi-year strategic partnership between GAM and Unmudl, a leading Skills-to-Jobs® Marketplace.
- The platform will feature dynamic, social media-like capabilities to connect manufacturers with future workers, especially younger audiences.
- Key goals include improving public awareness of modern manufacturing and increasing the availability of skilled manufacturing workers within the next five years.
- Manufacturing Georgia will be available to all manufacturers in the state.
- Lloyd Avram serves as GAM’s President and CEO.
- GAM formed a focus group in late 2023 to brainstorm public engagement strategies targeting younger generations.
- Unmudl was selected as the development partner following a competitive review process.
- Stephanie Scearce is GAM’s Vice President of Workforce Innovation.
- Jim Lechleitner serves as Head of Strategy at Unmudl.
- The platform will feature real-time job postings, youth apprenticeships, work-based learning opportunities, training programs, and insights into modern manufacturing.
- Patrick Lenz, President and COO of Toyo Tires NA Manufacturing, expressed support for the initiative to engage local high schools and sustain future workforce.
- Stuart Countess, President and CEO of Kia Georgia in West Point, emphasized combating the stigma that manufacturing is dull, dirty, dangerous, or a dead end.
- Brian Cooksey, Director of Workforce Development at Shaw Industries in Dalton, highlighted the platform's role in providing accurate career information to students and families.
- Smaller manufacturers comprise approximately 67 percent of Georgia’s manufacturing sector and will benefit from the platform.
- John Fluker, President and CEO at Grenzebach, stated that connecting with the next generation is key for manufacturers of all sizes in Georgia.
- The platform will launch in phases: a pre-screening event in March, a soft rollout to select manufacturers and education institutions in April, and a public launch in July.
- GAM's Manufacturers Education Foundation and contributions from Toyo Tires, Kia Georgia, Shaw Industries, and Grenzebach are funding the initiative.
- Manufacturers across Georgia will have free access to the platform in 2025, with an affordable subscription fee starting in 2026.
- GAM represents approximately 10,000 manufacturing entities and almost 427,000 workers in Georgia.
- GAM has advocated for manufacturing growth and excellence in Georgia for 125 years.
- Unmudl is a public benefit company that connects talent and employers through a nationwide network of community and technical colleges.
- Unmudl focuses on mid-level ‘hands-on’ technician skills to provide access to high-quality, affordable training for in-demand jobs in key industries including manufacturing.
