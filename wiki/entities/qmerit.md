# Qmerit

**Type:** Company  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[charging-infrastructure|Charging Infrastructure]]  ·  **Location:** Georgia, United States

## Overview

Qmerit is an EV charger installation company that provides a comprehensive guide to Georgia electric-vehicle rebates, incentives, tax credits, charging rates and utility programs, supporting the growth of EV adoption in the state.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Qmerit aggregates and delivers Georgia EV incentive information and installs residential and commercial chargers, directly enabling EV adoption across the state.

## Timeline

- **2016** — Georgia legislature ended the state EV purchase/lease tax credit.
- **October 2025** — Georgia reported more than 2,200 public EV charging stations (1,800 Level 2, 484 DC fast, 76 Tesla Supercharger stations).
- **May 15, 2026** — Page "Georgia EV Rebates, Incentives & Tax Credits" last updated.
- **June 30, 2026** — Federal 30C commercial charging tax credit expires.

## Key facts

- Georgia no longer offers a state EV tax credit; the program was ended by the legislature in 2016.
- Three utilities (Carroll Electric Membership Cooperative, Snapping Shoals EMC, Tri-County EMC) provide a $30 monthly energy credit for the first year of EV ownership through "Drive Free for a Year" or similar programs.
- Georgia Power residential customers can receive up to $150 toward a Level 2 charger; commercial customers can claim rebates up to $30,000 per project and $60,000 per year.
- Carroll EMC offers a $250 rebate for Level 2 chargers when enrolled in its Drive Free for a Year program, saving a total of $610.
- Cobb EMC residential customers qualify for a $250 rebate on Energy Star-certified Level 2 chargers; non-residential grants range from $500 to $5,000.
- Coweta-Fayetteville EMC provides a $100 rebate toward a Level 2 charger for at-home use.
- Diverse Power offers a $50 rebate per NEMA 14-30 or 14-50 outlet to help build EV-ready homes.
- GreyStone Power Corporation supplies a $250 residential charger rebate and a special super-off-peak rate of $0.04/kWh for EV charging.
- Jackson EMC provides a $250 residential charger rebate; commercial rebates range from $500 to $5,000.
- Sawnee EMC offers a $200 rebate for Level 2 chargers when customers enroll in the plug-in vehicle rate.
- Sumter EMC residential customers can obtain a $250 rebate; commercial customers may receive $500 per charger for Level 2 or DC Fast charging.
- Tri-County EMC offers a $250 rebate on Level 2 chargers for both residential and commercial customers.
- The City of LaGrange (MEAG) is the only MEAG public power community offering a $250 residential Level 2 charger rebate.
- Georgia Power time-of-use rates charge $0.137/kWh on-peak, $0.0146/kWh off-peak, with demand charges up to $11.70/kWh; its Overnight Advantage plan offers $0.286/kWh on-peak, $0.098/kWh off-peak and $0.021/kWh super-off-peak.
- Coastal Electric Cooperative’s TOU program ranges from $0.20/kWh on-peak to $0.05/kWh super off-peak.
- Flint Energies EV rate includes a $35/month basic charge, $0.05/kWh super-off-peak, $0.09/kWh off-peak, a $25 first-year bill credit and an optional $8/month reward for eligible EV models.
- As of October 2025 Georgia has over 2,200 public EV charging stations, including 76 Tesla Supercharger stations with 1,005 ports.
- DC Fast charging at Atlantic Station Parking Deck in Downtown Atlanta costs $0.30 per minute.
- Level 2 charging at the Honda dealership on Morrisson Parkway in Alpharetta costs $0.50 per hour for the first two hours, then $3 per hour thereafter.
- Free Level 2 charging stations are available at municipal libraries and the City of Blakely’s Main Street location.
- Alternative Fuel Corridors along I-85 and I-75 host many public chargers; Tesla’s Supercharger network places stations roughly 150 miles apart along these corridors.
- Home electrification incentives funded by the Inflation Reduction Act provide $109.8 million for efficiency rebates and $109.2 million for home electrification projects in Georgia.
- Qmerit partners with EnergySage to offer competing solar installation quotes for homeowners.
