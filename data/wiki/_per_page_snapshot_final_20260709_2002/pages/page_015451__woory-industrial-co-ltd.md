# Woory Industrial Co. Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-06-10

**Source:** [Dublin Nets Korean Supplier of Heaters for Electric Vehicles  – EV Update Media | Global – Electric Vehicles and Battery Industry News & Updates](https://evupdatemedia.us/dublin-nets-korean-supplier-of-heaters-for-electric-vehicles/)  ·  `page_015451`

## Facts

- Woory Industrial Co. Ltd. is set to invest $18 million in its first U.S. factory.
- The company plans to hire 130 people for the new facility.
- The factory will occupy a 76,000-square-foot existing building located at 404 Kellam Road just off Interstate 16 in Dublin, Georgia.
- The site is located about 100 miles west of Hyundai's massive Meta Plant near Savannah.
- Dublin Mayor Joshua Kight stated that Woory's commitment to place an existing manufacturing building back into service helps promote infill development and creates new career paths for the community.
- The project announcement was issued via a release from Gov. Brian Kemp’s office.
- Woory currently operates facilities across Asia, Mexico, and Slovakia.
- Woory's Slovakian facility is located near Kia's European plant.
- Woory has been nominated as a supplier to EV makers Tesla, Lucid, Canoo, and Rivian.
- It remains unclear if Woory is already selling components to all four nominated EV makers.
- Rivian is simultaneously building a $5 billion plant in Georgia.
- Woory manufactures HVAC components like actuators and coolant heaters, along with control systems, for gas-powered vehicles.
- The company began developing electric heaters for EVs approximately a decade ago because EVs do not naturally produce heat.
- Woory's smart heaters are designed to adjust energy usage based on the car's battery level.
- Experts state that demand efficiency across supply chains is necessary to boost EV range and overcome consumer range anxiety.
- Woory is also focused on developing new products for hydrogen-powered cars.
- Hyundai is placing heavy bets on hydrogen technology.
- Jungwoo Kim, CEO of Woory, stated that the automotive industry is facing a revolution for the first time in 100 years and that the transition to electric vehicles is the future.
- Kim noted that the U.S. EV market is the key market among all automotive markets.
- Kim added that over the past 10 years, Woory has successfully grown into a specialized EV component company through continuous innovation.
- Kim stated that Georgia will be a good opportunity for Woory to take another step forward as part of the vanguard of EV development.
- Woory is headquartered in Yongin, just south of Seoul in Gyeonggi province.
- The company maintains a U.S. office registered in Warren, Michigan.
- Jacob Lee, the Georgia Department of Economic Development project manager who spearheaded the project, originally started with the state in its Korean office and moved to Georgia in October.
