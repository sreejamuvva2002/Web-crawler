# Hyundai TRANSYS

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2020-12-02

**Source:** [Korean Transmission Supplier to Invest $240 Million in West Point  - Global Atlanta](https://www.globalatlanta.com/korean-transmission-supplier-to-invest-240-million-in-west-point/)  ·  `page_009494`

## Facts

- Auto parts supplier Hyundai TRANSYS announced it would spend $240 million to build a new factory.
- The new factory will be located next to the company's current location on the Kia Motors campus in West Point, Georgia.
- Hyundai Motor, a sister company to Kia, operates a factory down Interstate 85 in Montgomery, Alabama.
- A row of sometimes-shared Korean auto suppliers has emerged between the two locations over the past decade.
- Hyundai TRANSYS specializes in transmissions and also manufactures seats.
- The company was formed by a merger between Hyundai Dymos and Hyundai Powertech.
- Hyundai Powertech established the original plant near Kia in 2008, followed by Hyundai Dymos in 2013.
- The combined company's global revenues currently stand at $7 billion.
- The company will employ 678 people at the new facility, which will be 620,000 square feet in size.
- These employment and facility details were confirmed in a news release from Governor Brian Kemp’s office.
- An additional 165 construction and inspection jobs are expected to accompany the factory positions.
- The company's total employment in Georgia will increase to 1,471.
- Hyundai TRANSYS Georgia Powertrain President Sangkil Jung stated that Georgia was chosen due to trust built through years of capital investment, employment, and training support, alongside unmatched new opportunities.
- Hyundai TRANSYS expressed excitement about expanding its presence in the West Point community.
- Georgia is home to more than 200 auto-industry plants.
- The state has received commitments of more than $1 billion and 2,000 jobs from South Korean investments in 2020.
- Many of these 2020 South Korean commitments were promised for the SK Innovation electric-vehicle battery factory in Commerce, Georgia.
