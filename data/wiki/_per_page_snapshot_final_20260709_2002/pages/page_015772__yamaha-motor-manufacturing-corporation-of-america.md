# Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation of America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2015-06-10

**Source:** [Yamaha’s Georgia Manufacturing Facility Featured in “Small Town, Big Deal” Television Program  - ATV On Demand](https://www.atvondemand.com/yamahas-georgia-manufacturing-facility-featured-in-small-town-big-deal-television-program/)  ·  `page_015772`

## Facts

- The Yamaha manufacturing facility in Newnan, Ga., was featured in a new episode of the RFD-TV network program "Small Town, Big Deal."
- The episode featuring Newnan and Yamaha first aired on Thursday, June 11, 2015, at 8:30 p.m. Central Standard Time.
- YMMC serves as Yamaha’s worldwide hub of off-highway vehicle (OHV) manufacturing.
- All of the company’s SxS and the majority of their ATV vehicles are produced at the Newnan facility for national and international distribution.
- The Newnan facility covers 280 acres with more than 1.3 million square feet of manufacturing facilities.
- The facility employs approximately 1,473 people.
- Yamaha has invested more than $250 million into U.S. manufacturing over the past 15 years to create the Newnan facility.
- The facility produces ATV or Side-by-Side vehicles, Golf Cars, and Personal Watercraft.
- Mike Chrzanowski, executive vice president of YMMC, stated that Yamaha strives to maintain the highest production quality and environmental standards while giving back to the community through corporate social responsibility programs.
- Rodney Miller, co-host of "Small Town, Big Deal," noted that Yamaha has had a tremendously positive impact on Newnan from both an economic and philanthropic standpoint.
- Yamaha has facilities in Wisconsin and Georgia, as well as factory operations in Tennessee and Georgia.
- YMUS products are sold through a nationwide network of dealers in the United States.
