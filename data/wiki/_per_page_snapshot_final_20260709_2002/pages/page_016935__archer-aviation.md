# Archer Aviation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Archer unveils Midnight four-seater electric vertical takeoff aircraft: Travel Weekly](https://www.travelweekly.com/Travel-News/Airline-News/Archer-Aviation-unveils-four-seater-electric-aircraft)  ·  `page_016935`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation is a startup air taxi developer that has unveiled its prototype four-seat electric aircraft named Midnight.
- The Midnight eVTOL craft will fly up to 150 mph and as far as 60 miles, but is optimized for missions of around 20 miles.
- Recharges from flights of approximately 20 miles take Archer about 10 minutes.
- The unveiling event took place Wednesday evening in a hangar at Palo Alto airport.
- Archer CEO Adam Goldstein stated the company is prioritizing getting the vehicle to market over building the longest or fastest flying vehicle.
- United Airlines has entered an agreement to purchase as many as 200 Archer Midnights.
- United plans to use the Midnight craft to ferry customers quickly from densely populated urban areas to airports.
- United and Archer announced their first shared route will connect lower Manhattan with Newark, delivering commuters to the airport in less than 10 minutes.
- Archer is targeting late 2024 for certification of the air taxi.
- United hopes to begin the commuter service in 2025.
- The companies plan to announce additional routes soon.
- Archer COO Tom Muniz predicted that air taxis and Archer's technology will change how people move and live.
- Midnight features a lean body with a wingspan of nearly 50 feet for stability.
- The craft has 12 engines, including six in the rear used only to provide lift during takeoff.
- Archer is regarded as a leader in the race toward electric air taxi certification alongside competitors Joby, Vertical Aerospace, Lilium, and Eve Air Mobility.
- Joby has backing from Delta Air Lines, Vertical Aerospace has backing from American and Virgin Atlantic, and Eve Air Mobility has backing from Sky West and Republic.
- Midnight was designed since day one with a focus on finding the straightest line toward commercialization for short missions of approximately 20 miles in urban spaces.
- New York is identified as an obvious target market for the service.
- United Airlines venture capital head Mike Leskinen envisions having up to 50 vertiports in the New York-area alone eventually.
- United foresees charging approximately $100 per seat for aerial taxi rides initially, with prices lowering as the service scales up.
- Los Angeles was called out by Goldstein as a model market where more than 5 million people spend more than one hour per day traveling 20 miles.
- A simple engine design and safety redundancy on every part of the Midnight craft are intended to speed Archer's path to FAA certification.
- Goldstein projects that Archer will produce hundreds of aircraft per year by 2028 and scale into the thousands annually after that.
- Target markets during early commercialization include replacing existing helicopter services and scheduled point-to-point routes in targeted cities.
- The company plans to manufacture the Midnight at a plant in Covington, Ga.
- Midnight test flights are targeted to begin early next year.
