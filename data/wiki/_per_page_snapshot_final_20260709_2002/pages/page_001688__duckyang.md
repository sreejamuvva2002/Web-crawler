# Duckyang

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Recap: What Businesses are Coming to Right to Work Georgia? | NILRR](https://nilrr.org/recap-what-businesses-are-coming-to-right-to-work-georgia/)  ·  `page_001688`

## Facts

- Three new businesses are coming soon to Right to Work Georgia: Brightmark, Duckyang, and IC Biomedical.
- Each business will add a new location somewhere within the state of Georgia.
- Brightmark will be locating in Macon-Bibb with a $680 million investment.
- Brightmark is investing $680 million soon in Right to Work Macon-Bibb County, Georgia.
- Brightmark will build its next plastics renewal facility in Macon-Bibb.
- The advanced recycling facility will be the world’s largest and will utilize proven plastics renewal technology.
- Duckyang is investing $10 million soon toward its first US manufacturing location in Right to Work Braselton, Georgia.
- Duckyang's new location will create 285 new jobs.
- In Georgia, partners are focused on growing the entire electric mobility supply chain.
- Strong relationships with SK Battery America and other Korean companies like Duckyang are helping secure a local supply of needed parts for the automotive industry moving toward electric.
- Partners in Jackson County supported the Duckyang project.
- IC Biomedical is investing millions toward a new manufacturing facility in Right to Work Cartersville, Georgia.
- IC Biomedical's new facility will create over 80 new jobs.
- Georgia was chosen for IC Biomedical due to a highly trained workforce, transport links, and state infrastructure committed to the life science sector.
- IC Biomedical joins metro Atlanta’s growing life sciences community alongside assets like Emory School of Medicine, Morehouse School of Medicine, Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, Georgia Tech, and many businesses engaged in research, development, manufacturing, and distribution of life science related products and supplies.
