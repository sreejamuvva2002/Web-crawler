# Essex Solutions

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- Essex Solutions, the US magnet wire unit of South Korea’s LS Group and wholly owned subsidiary of Superior Essex Inc. (SPSX), is seeking an initial public offering next year with an estimated corporate value of 2 trillion won ($1.4 billion).
- Essex Solutions plans to send requests for proposals to South Korean and foreign brokerage houses by early 2025 to select managers for a listing in either the US or Korea.
- The company's enterprise value is estimated at over 2 trillion won, given expectations of a recovery in the electric vehicle market.
- Essex Solutions supplies magnet wire products for EV transaction motors and other components to major global EV manufacturers.
- Formerly known as Essex Furukawa Magnet Wire, the company was founded in 2020 as a 61:39 joint venture between SPSX and Japan’s Furukawa Electric Co., with SPSX taking over Furukawa’s stake in March and changing its name to Essex Solutions.
- The company aims to increase its EV magnet wire market share to 70% in North America and 50% in Europe in the next three to five years with $300 million in investment by 2028.
- Essex Solutions separately seeks to raise $200 million before the planned listing through a rights offering to a consortium of the private equity unit of South Korea’s Mirae Asset Global Investments Co. and PE firm KCGI Co.
- Parent company SPSX reported a net profit of 100.5 billion won in the first half, up 789.3% from a year earlier, as sales rose 5.3% to 2.1 trillion won.
