# Daimler Trucks North America LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2021-04-23

**Source:** [Daimler Trucks North America Leads Electrification Charge During Earth Week - Thomas Built Buses](https://thomasbuiltbuses.com/resources/news/daimler-trucks-north-america-leads-electrification-2021-04-23/)  ·  `page_053608`

## Facts

- Daimler Trucks North America (DTNA) celebrated several electrification milestones during Earth Week in April 2021.
- Vice President Kamala Harris visited the Thomas Built Buses (TBB) production plant in High Point, North Carolina.
- VP Harris met with John O’Leary, president and CEO of DTNA, and TBB employees to review electric school bus benefits and commercial EV manufacturing technology.
- John O’Leary stated that welcoming VP Harris was an honor and highlighted the recognition of their electric vehicles by the administration.
- The visit coincided with the inauguration of Electric Island in Portland, Oregon.
- Electric Island is the first public electric vehicle charging station specifically designed for commercial vehicles.
- The station aligns with the blueprint of the West Coast Clean Transit Corridor Initiative (WCCTCI).
- TBB’s Saf-T-Liner C2 Jouley operates tail-pipe emissions free and noise-pollution free with a 135-mile operating range.
- Nearly 50 Jouley electric school buses have already been delivered to school districts across the U.S., from Alaska to Virginia.
- The Freightliner Electric Innovation and Customer Experience Fleets currently include nearly 40 trucks in customer hands.
- These fleet vehicles have accumulated more than 750,000 miles of real-world use.
- A Freightliner eM2 and an eCascadia are touring the West Coast from Seattle to Los Angeles for customer demonstrations.
- Freightliner recently opened ordering for the eCascadia and eM2 electric trucks.
- Production for the eCascadia and eM2 is scheduled to begin in late 2022.
- At series production start, the eCascadia will feature a 250-mile range and a maximum gross combined weight (GCW) of 82,000 lbs.
- Daimler Trucks North America LLC is headquartered in Portland, Oregon.
- DTNA manufactures, sells, and services commercial vehicles under the Freightliner, Western Star, Detroit, and Thomas Built Buses nameplates.
- DTNA is a subsidiary of Daimler company.
