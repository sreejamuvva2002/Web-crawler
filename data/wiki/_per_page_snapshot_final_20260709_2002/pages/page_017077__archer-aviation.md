# Archer Aviation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-11-14

**Source:** [Electric air taxi company Archer plans $118M Georgia factory | FOX 5 Atlanta](https://www.fox5atlanta.com/news/electric-air-taxi-company-archer-plans-118m-georgia-factory)  ·  `page_017077`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation plans to invest $118 million to construct a plant near Atlanta.
- The company will eventually hire up to 1,000 people.
- Archer Aviation is based in Santa Clara, California.
- The plant will be built adjoining an airport in Covington, Georgia.
- The aircraft is a battery-powered vertical takeoff and landing craft with six propellers that hold four passengers and a pilot.
- The propellers would pivot to allow the aircraft to take off and land like a helicopter and fly like a plane.
- United Airlines plans to fly the craft from downtown Manhattan to its hub at Newark Liberty International Airport in New Jersey, cutting a congested car trip of one hour to 10 minutes.
- United Airlines put down a $10 million deposit to purchase 100 aircraft from Archer for $1 billion.
- After building a prototype weighing 3,330 pounds (1,510 kilograms), the company plans to unveil a production model dubbed "Midnight" on Wednesday.
- The company aims for the "Midnight" aircraft to enter service in 2025.
- The U.S. Air Force is evaluating the company’s aircraft for possible use.
- Archer is seeking Federal Aviation Administration certification and says it could receive it in 2024.
- The company aims to produce 650 aircraft per year at the Georgia site, possibly scaling up to 2,300 per year.
- Automotive firm Stellantis, owner of Dodge, Chrysler, Jeep and Ram, is providing manufacturing and engineering help.
- Archer expects to receive a roughly $40 million incentive package from Georgia and local governments.
- The incentive package includes the donation of 96 acres (39 hectares) of land at Covington Municipal Airport.
- The package also includes a cash grant from the state and other tax incentives.
- A property tax break and a job tax credit could be worth $15 million to Archer over five years, contingent on employees making at least $31,300.
- State and local officials did not immediately respond to questions about the decision to incentivize a startup in an unproven industry.
- The company chose the Georgia location for locally available workers, the ability to conduct test flights, affordable construction costs, and good highway, rail, and air connections.
- Archer CEO Adam Goldstein stated: "Our eVTOL technology can transform how urban and rural communities live and commute and this factory can create pathways to highly skilled manufacturing hobs and other ladders of social and economic opportunity."
- Archer expects to borrow money from Georgia-based Synovus Financial Corp. for the plant’s construction.
- Plant construction is expected to begin and conclude in 2024.
- The company went public last year, raising about $850 million.
- Archer lost $91 million in the quarter ended Sept. 30, reporting $600 million in cash on hand.
- Georgia economic development officials have been focusing on recruiting electric car companies, landing major plants from Rivian Automotive and Hyundai Motor Group last year.
- Norwegian batter startup Freyr announced a $2.6 billion plant in Georgia on Friday, joining an already-constructed electric battery plant owned by South Korean firm SK Innovation.
- Georgia Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson said economic recruiters focused on aerospace had worked with Archer.
- Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that "technology and innovation continue to drive change across strategic industries."
