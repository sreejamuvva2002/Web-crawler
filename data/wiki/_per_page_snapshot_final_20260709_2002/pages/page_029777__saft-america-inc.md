## Facts

- SAFT America Inc is located at 711 Industrial Blvd., Valdosta, GA 31601.
- Mr. Chip Wildes serves as the Environmental Safety and Recycle Manager at SAFT America Inc.
- PHMSA determined that lithium-ion cells and batteries must be classified in accordance with § 173.185 of the Hazardous Materials Regulations (49 CFR parts 171-180).
- Section 173.185 regulates lithium-ion cells and batteries based on the amount of lithium in the anodes of fully charged cells and batteries.
- If lithium-ion cells and batteries meet the conditions for the exception provided in § 173.185(b), they are not subject to the HMR.
- If not excepted, the other provisions of § 173.185 apply.
- The interpretation response regarding lithium-ion battery classification was published on April 29, 1998.
- This letter revises previous PHMSA opinions dated March ?, 1994 and April 10/1995 regarding the classification of lithium-ion batteries under the Hazardous Materials Regulations.
