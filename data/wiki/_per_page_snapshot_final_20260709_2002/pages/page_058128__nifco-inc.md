# Nifco Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-01-29

**Source:** [What is Brief History of Nifco Company? – PortersFiveForce.com](https://portersfiveforce.com/blogs/brief-history/nifco)  ·  `page_058128`

## Facts

- Nifco Inc. is a global leader in manufacturing plastic fasteners and precision plastic components, significantly impacting various industries, especially automotive.
- The company adopted plastic as a lightweight, cost-effective alternative to metal fasteners during Japan's industrial growth.
- Established on February 13, 1967, as Nippon Industrial Fastener Corporation in Japan.
- Initial aim was to offer ingenious plastic solutions for labor and cost savings in industrial applications.
- Operates in 17 countries, boasting over 35 production plants and 4 R&D centers.
- Projected consolidated net sales of approximately ¥353 billion for the fiscal year ending March 31, 2025.
- Co-founded on February 13, 1967, by Nichiei Bussan Co., Ltd. (60% stake) and Illinois Tool Works (ITW) (40% stake).
- Initial capital for the company was 48 million yen.
- Toshiaki Ogasawara's international education and connections facilitated the partnership with ITW.
- Founders recognized a substantial opportunity in Japan's rapidly expanding automotive and home electronics sectors focused on enhancing manufacturing efficiency and reducing production costs.
- Core problem addressed was the inherent inefficiencies and material limitations associated with traditional metal fasteners.
- Initial business model revolved around the manufacturing and sale of industrial plastic fasteners, with the 'Plasti Rivet' being their first significant product.
- Strategic shift to plastic alternatives promised considerable benefits including labor savings, reduced manufacturing expenses, and lighter end products.
- Name NIFCO is an acronym derived from its original designation, 'Nippon Industrial Fastener Corporation'.
- Rapidly developed product line beyond basic fasteners to include mini-dampers and push latches for the automotive sector.
- Secured technical assistance contracts with major automotive manufacturers General Motors, Chrysler, and Ford Motor Company in 1972.
- Achieved sales of 1 billion yen within its first five years and nearly 5 billion yen by its tenth year.
- Consolidated sales surpassed 10 billion yen by 1980.
- Listed on the Second Section of the Tokyo Stock Exchange in 1979 and moved to the First Section in 1984.
- Global footprint expanded in 1983 with the co-founding of Nifco Taiwan Corporation.
- Ventures into South Korea in 1985 and establishment of Nifco America Corporation in the United States in 1987 to serve North American automotive clients.
- Entered European market in 1990 with acquisition of Elta Plastics Ltd. (now Nifco U.K. Ltd.) and established a subsidiary in Germany in 2006.
- Diversified business in 1996 by acquiring Simmons Co., Ltd., a bedding and furniture manufacturer.
- Received the 10th Plastics Award for product excellence in 1978.
- Experienced first operating loss since founding in 2009 due to the Great Financial Crisis.
- Transferred operations of Nifco Germany GmbH and Nifco KTW America Corporation in fiscal year ending March 31, 2024, to address hardship in German OEM business.
- Maintained highest rating of 'AAA' in MSCI ESG Ratings as of May 2024.
- Holds over 3,600 intellectual property rights.
- First globally to develop the mini-damper for smooth motion in consumer electronics and automotive interiors.
- Pioneered push latches and specialized fuel tank components meeting environmental standards.
- Produces complex engine transmission parts.
- Collaborations with Toyota, Honda, and Nissan ensure integration into global vehicle manufacturing lines.
- Actively adapting to market demands by expanding product line to include components for electric vehicles (EVs).
- R&D expenditures increased by approximately 25% from 2020 to 2023.
- Prioritizing lightweight materials and sustainable manufacturing processes, promoting a circular economy.
- Renewed corporate philosophy, logo, and slogan in 2018.
- Completed Initial Public Offering on the Tokyo Stock Exchange in 2014.
- Invested in LexxPluss in 2024 to develop automation solutions for automotive maintenance.
- Launched 'XtoCar Project' in 2025.
- Plans announced to open self-delivering solar power plants to advance carbon neutrality in 2025.
- Forecasts consolidated net sales of ¥348,000 million and operating profit of ¥49,500 million for fiscal year ending March 31, 2026.
- Plans aggressive business investments considering alliances, M&A, and Corporate Venture Capital.
- Committed to integrating ESG management with business operations, addressing climate change by 2030.
