# EnviroSpark

**Type:** Company  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[charging-infrastructure|Charging Infrastructure]]  ·  **Location:** Atlanta, Georgia, United States

## Overview

EnviroSpark designs, builds, and operates electric vehicle (EV) charging infrastructure for private and public sector customers across the United States and Canada, offering services such as Funding-by-EnviroSpark, Charging-as-a-Service, and full ownership solutions.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Provides end-to-end EV charging infrastructure design, installation, financing and operation for Georgia businesses and municipalities.

## Key facts

- EnviroSpark designs, builds, and operates EV charging infrastructure that can be funded by the company or the customer, often with zero upfront costs.
- Offers Charging-as-a-Service model with a monthly subscription fee, allowing customers to keep all EV charging revenue while controlling pricing and access.
- Provides full ownership options where customers purchase the electrical infrastructure and equipment for complete control over usage and revenue.
- Delivers specialty construction services from conduit to concrete for private and public sector projects of any size.
- Has 11 years of experience with Level 2 and Level 3 EV chargers.
- Leverages a network that includes partners such as Tesla, Georgia Power, and Shell.
- Claims to have installed over 10,000 charging ports nationwide across the United States and Canada.
- Founded in 2014.
- Operates more than 8,200 charging plugs across North America.
- EnviroSpark is expanding its workforce by hiring talent made available after Tesla's recent layoffs.
- Tesla laid off hundreds of employees, creating a pool of experienced EV-related workers.
