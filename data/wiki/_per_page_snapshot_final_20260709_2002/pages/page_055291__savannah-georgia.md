# Savannah, Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2008-04-01

**Source:** [Savannah: Intellectual Capital - Georgia Trend Magazine](https://www.georgiatrend.com/2008/04/01/savannah-intellectual-capital/)  ·  `page_055291`

## Facts

- JCB Inc., a U.K.-based manufacturer of farming and construction equipment, moved its North American headquarters from Baltimore to Savannah in 2000 and added a manufacturing plant.
- Graeme Macdonald is the president of JCB Inc.
- Since arriving in Savannah, JCB grew from 150 employees to more than 500 and expanded its product line from one to four.
- JCB added 100 workers last year to begin filling an initial $140 million order for 365 backhoe loaders capable of reaching speeds of 60 miles per hour.
- Over the next four years, JCB will build more than 800 units under a U.S. Army contract.
- The backhoes will be sent to Iraq and Afghanistan to keep up with military convoys.
- The Army contract is the largest in JCB’s 62-year history.
- JCB is a global company, privately owned, with one shareholder (the chairman), allowing it to make quick, long-term decisions without quarterly stock market pressure.
- JCB collaborates with SCAD (Savannah College of Art and Design) on industrial design projects through The Creative Coast Alliance (TCCa).
- Chuck Stallworth is vice president of the Savannah Economic Development Authority.
- SCAD has a huge industrial design capability and acts as a business incubator that births ideas 24 hours a day.
- The Savannah Economic Development Authority is working with SCAD to connect students with local business talent through a program called Coastal Connect, a program of The Creative Coast Alliance.
- A group of about 60 wealthy individuals formed a private investment group called the Ariel Savannah Angel Partners (ASAP) to fund ideas from SCAD students and others.
- In the last three years, The Creative Coast Alliance generated over 200 jobs and $11 million in salaries for knowledge-based companies.
- SCAD has a formal collaboration with Electronic Arts (EA) where students work like apprentices producing actual work for current games.
- Peter Weishar is dean of Film and Digital Media at SCAD.
- Rick Winger is president of the Savannah Economic Development Authority.
- A startup company called Carbon Motors, founded by former Ford executives, is in capital formation with ASAP investors.
- Carbon Motors is designing a purpose car for law enforcement.
- SCAD designed a seat for Carbon Motors that allows gun-wearing officers to more easily exit the vehicle during emergencies.
- Chatham County Commissioner Pete Liakakis notes Savannah has the fastest growing port in the country, bringing in some $15 billion to all of Georgia.
- The military (Army) at Fort Stewart and Hunter Field brings about $1.5 billion a year to the Savannah-Chatham County area.
- In 2006, 600,000 tourists spent $1.4 billion in Chatham County.
- Industries like JCB and Gulfstream bring in about $8 billion to $9 billion a year to the region.
- Chatham County is funding a massive infrastructure improvement program topping $326 million within the next three years.
- Some $100 million will be spent to build a new jail near I-16 on the city’s western border.
- Valdosta-based Ambling Companies, Inc. announced plans for an $800 million mixed-use development on 54 acres along Savannah’s riverfront earlier this year.
- The project, named Savannah River Landing, will add 280,000 square feet of retail space, the same amount of office space, 365 new hotel rooms, 600 condos, 100 townhouses, and 17 riverfront homes.
- The state put up $8.8 million to extend the Riverwalk by 2,200 linear feet.
- The Riverwalk attracts 6.5 million tourists to the city each year.
- An explosion at the Imperial Sugar Company refinery in Port Wentworth claimed 12 lives and left dozens injured as of press time.
- Imperial Sugar had owned the Port Wentworth facility since 1997.
- Chatham County population was 238,410 in 2005; Savannah population was 128,483.
- Unemployment in Chatham County was 4.6 percent in Jan. 2008; Georgia was 5.2 percent.
- Per capita income in Chatham County was $31,691 in 2004; Georgia was $29,782.
- Top manufacturing employers include Gulfstream Aerospace (5,400), Georgia-Pacific Savannah River Mill (1,400), International Paper (740), Great Dane Trailers (545), Imperial Sugar (450), JCB Americas (500), Savannah Morning News (380), Derst Baking Company (308), Colonial Chemical Solutions (285), and Tronox (280).
