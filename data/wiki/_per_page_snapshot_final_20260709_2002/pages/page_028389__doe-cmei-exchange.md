# DOE CMEI eXCHANGE

**Type:** supplier_relationship  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [DOE: CMEI eXCHANGE: Teaming Partners](https://eere-exchange.energy.gov/TeamingPartners.aspx?foaid=ea4fb689-3439-433b-bdf0-31d1e334bdc0)  ·  `page_028389`

## Facts

- P2H2 is a small business developing hybrid liquid alkaline/AEM electrolysis technology that reduces stack cost by ~65% by eliminating expensive and supply chain-constrained metals like iridium, titanium, platinum, and gold.
- P2H2's technology integrates directly with variable renewable energy, produces hydrogen at 250 bar, and solves durability challenges of AEM electrolyzers through a patented cell design that puts the membrane under less stress.
- P2H2 has a signed commercial relationship with Shell and a pilot with Enel Green Power, targeting industrial high-pressure hydrogen/oxygen users and large renewable energy developers, markets amounting to over $100B by 2025.
- P2H2's business model involves manufacturing/selling equipment or installing it on-site for a hydrogen-as-a-service model.
- P2H2's team has 17 members including 5 PhDs, led by CEO Dr. Paul Matter (18 years hydrogen experience, previously led fuel cell technology development for major automotive OEMs) and COO Chris Holt (20+ years materials engineering experience).
- H2scan is a small business focused on hydrogen pilot projects and sensing technologies; its Gen 5 technology provides high-accuracy, low-cost hydrogen gas safety sensing to reduce distribution transformer explosion risk.
- Denso International America is a large business with expertise in manufacturing fuel cell components and enabling electronic/thermal systems, and is currently demonstrating a SOEC.
- JTEKT is a top 20 automotive supplier with over 50 years of manufacturing precision sheet metal components in US plants, operating a Design & Manufacturing Technical Center in South Carolina equipped with a high speed servo press, bearing quality metrology/metallurgy labs, and a machine shop.
- JTEKT is exploring bipolar plate manufacturing, capable of stamping plates ~80 cm2 active area, and has access to laser welding, coating, and sealing via an external partner.
- GenHydro INC is a minority-owned early-stage renewable energy company developing a novel process for producing clean hydrogen from renewable materials at less than $3/kg using scrap aluminum in a self-sustaining chemical process.
- GenHydro's system produces hydrogen, electricity, and green alumina, and is establishing partnerships with Stanley-Black & Decker and Ford Motor Company to reduce their carbon footprint.
- Quantron US Inc is developing a Data-Driven Analysis tool to optimize MEA and Stacking for PEMs in Heavy Duty Vehicles using Quantron's Data-Driven Controls to address fuel cell technology shortcomings.
- Quantron US Inc, a subsidiary of Germany-based Quantron AG, will bring hydrogen-powered trucks and an industry-first 360° solution platform combining vehicle, service, maintenance, insurance, leasing, financing, infrastructure, green power, and hydrogen to the US market.
- University of Oklahoma researchers have extensive experience developing fundamental to system-level computational models for fuel cells using first-principles calculations, machine learning, and statistical analysis.
- Molecule Works Incorporated developed a 50-micrometer thin porous ceramic membrane separator/anodic electrode assembly for alkaline water electrolysis and plans to scale up to pilot production as a US-based supplier.
- Rochester Institute of Technology investigator Patricia Taboada-Serrano combines modeling with experimental work to guide electrode material synthesis, focusing on synthesizing scalable water electrolysis electrodes from earth-abundant metals using additive manufacturing and electrochemical functionalization.
- Skyhaven Systems LLC has locations in Steamboat Springs, CO and Westford, MA, and holds a patented quality control inspection system for non-destructively assessing chemical activity of membrane and electrode assemblies during reel-to-reel manufacturing.
- Skyhaven Systems is developing a novel water electrolyzer design with substantially lower capital cost than traditional PEM-based electrolyzers while maximizing operational efficiency, seeking partners for mathematical modeling and scaling to active areas larger than 25 cm2.
- Austin Power Engineering LLC provides PEM fuel cell manufacturing cost and system analysis using a proprietary bottom-up activities-based manufacturing cost model validated with commercial clients for new manufacturing lines.
- Austin Power Engineering has developed concept designs for 100 MW, 500 MW, 1 GW, and 5 GW H2 production plants and evaluates commercial alkaline, PEM, and SOEC products alongside new electrolysis technologies.
- Hydrogen Technologies LLC offers 100% hydrogen fueled commercial and industrial steam boilers emitting zero greenhouse gases, providing steam from 1,500 kg/hr up to 8,000 kg/hr, with plans to scale up to 50,000 kg/hr in collaboration with National Labs.
- NC Clean Energy Technology Center, an extension of North Carolina State University founded in December 1987, provides technical assistance, policy analysis, and workforce development training related to clean energy and hosts the DOE's CHP Technical Assistance Partnership in the Southeast.
- TreadStone Technologies Inc is located in Princeton, NJ, holds over 60 patents for metallic plate coatings for fuel cells and electrolyzers, and is expanding coating service capabilities to address up to 1 GW by the end of 2023.
- University of Missouri researcher Prof. Yangchuan Xing studies corrosion resistant catalyst supports and developed a condensed layer deposition process for low-cost liquid metal oxide nanocoatings that can incorporate iridium for high utilization with reduced PGM loading.
- ET Energies, established by Electrolytic Technologies Systems LLC which has over two decades of experience installing skid-mounted modular chlorine and sodium hypochlorite generation plants globally, is developing a scalable high-pressure discharge PEM electrolyzer stack with optimized flow-fields and cooling loops.
- Pajarito Powder LLC is a US-based manufacturer of electrocatalyst materials for fuel cells and electrolyzers in Albuquerque, New Mexico, producing proprietary Engineered Catalyst Support and complete catalyst solutions featuring platinum and platinum alloys, and is located in a Disadvantaged Community.
