# SK On-Hyundai Motor Group Battery Plant

**Type:** Facility  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[battery-cell-manufacturing|Battery Cell Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** East of the Floyd/Bartow county line off the north side of U.S. 411 near Bartow Centre, Bartow County, Georgia, United States

## Overview

$5 billion electric-vehicle battery plant partnership between Hyundai Motor Group and SK On, located east of the Floyd/Bartow county line off U.S. 411 near Bartow Centre, expected to anchor Georgia’s EV supply chain and drive regional economic development.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Anchors electric-vehicle battery manufacturing in Georgia, creating a hub for first- and second-tier suppliers.

## Key facts

- The battery plant is expected to become an economic hub, attracting dozens of first- and second-tier supplier businesses.
- First-tier suppliers must be within a certain distance radius; second-tier suppliers should be within about 50 miles (statement by Missy Kendrick).
- Enterprise Corner Development was chosen for its location on the Bartow County border and will soon have water and sewer access.
- The SPLOST package significantly increased interest in Enterprise Corner development.
- West Cartersville is projected to experience huge growth over the next several years, according to Commissioner Steve Taylor.
- Bartow County is building a 2-million-gallon-per-day wastewater treatment plant on the Etowah River, with plans to expand to 4 million gallons; project cost estimated at $70-$80 million.
- Large plots of land are scarce near I-75 up to Chattanooga, driving intense interest in the 411 corridor.
- Floyd, Bartow, and Polk Counties have formed a joint development authority to pursue large business development opportunities across county lines.
- "The fact that Bartow County is growing toward Floyd and Floyd is growing toward Bartow and 411 is just really hot, I think this is a great thing to do," said Rome Floyd Development Authority Chair Jimmy Byars.
