# SK Battery America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2020-09-14

**Source:** [Construction workers tell feds they don't feel safe at SK Battery site | FOX 5 Atlanta](https://www.fox5atlanta.com/news/construction-workers-tell-feds-they-dont-feel-safe-at-sk-battery-site)  ·  `page_050049`

## Facts

- Construction workers at the SK Battery America plant in Commerce, Ga. (Jackson County) report that government contractors are ignoring safety concerns to finish the job on schedule.
- Kimel Brantley worked as a safety officer for Eastern Corporation, one of the major contractors at the electric car battery plant.
- Brantley stated: “They’re sacrificing people’s safety for production.”
- Brantley previously held the same safety officer position at MiDong, a subcontractor at the plant, before quitting earlier in the month.
- Brantley filed a complaint with the Department of Labor Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA).
- Randy Gregory works as an elevator installer on the multi-billion dollar facility.
- Gregory remarked: “It’s a wonder someone hasn’t been killed already.”
- SK Battery, a Korean-owned company, received $300 million in tax breaks and free land to establish the electric car battery plant in Jackson County.
- SK Battery promised more than 2,000 good jobs when the facility opens in 2022.
- Most workers at the construction site were from overseas performing tasks that union leaders stated unemployed American pipefitters or electricians could easily handle.
- David Cagle of Local Union 72 complained: “All I hear is 2,000 jobs... Does the construction industry not matter? The average common man that works with his hands?”
- U.S. Customs caught 33 Koreans attempting to enter the US with fake papers to work at the battery plant.
- American workers have complained that Korean workers do not understand American safety standards on a construction site.
- In May, Customs reported catching 33 Koreans trying to enter the country with fake papers for the battery plant project.
- SK Battery stated it has hired 1,000 Americans to build the plant so far and instructed contractors from the start to prioritize hiring local American workers.
- Brantley alleged he repeatedly saw unqualified Korean workers hired instead of qualified American workers.
- Brantley noted that Korean workers were willing to work for $2 a day compared to his rate of $6,000 a month.
- Brantley, who is half Korean, attempted to enforce safety on site but could not communicate with Korean workers due to a language barrier and received no support from bosses at Eastern or MiDong.
- In his OSHA complaint filed this month, Brantley wrote he witnessed many workers exposed to fall hazards and general safety hazards, but companies prioritized staying on track for the 2022 completion deadline.
- Brantley noted in his complaint that the majority of employees are Korean Nationals and Latino workers who showed a lack of understanding of American safety standards.
- An Eastern Corporation spokesman stated they cannot imagine Brantley’s comments being true, citing Eastern’s strict safety policy regardless of project schedule, and emphasized that safety is their first priority.
- FOX 5 Atlanta contacted MiDong for comment but received no response.
- Jackson County EMS responded to four emergency calls at the site since April, transporting two workers with no life-threatening injuries.
- Workers were observed using a forklift to remove equipment from a flatbed truck, which safety experts warn against because loads can shift or fall when used as a crane.
- Gregory provided photos showing a worker driving a forklift onto a platform suspended 30 feet in the air by a crane to lift pallets of equipment.
- Gregory explained the danger of this practice: “The slings break and that platform comes down with that guy in the forklift on it.”
- The photos are evidence in a separate OSHA complaint filed by the union on behalf of Gregory and other workers, which does not name a specific contractor.
- Gregory stated he took the pictures to leave proof for his wife in case he was killed on the job.
- The union’s OSHA complaint states: “The consensus of the workers that care about leaving the job at the end of the day in the same condition as they were when they walked in that morning is that there is little regard for proper safety procedures and hazard prevention on the job.”
- Gregory, a 26-year veteran of the construction industry, described the SK Battery job site as one of the worst he has ever seen regarding safety.
- In a written statement, SK Battery said although on-site safety management is under general contractors' responsibility, SK Battery America prioritizes construction personnel safety and is working with contractors to ensure all safety standards and procedures are followed.
- Eastern Corporation insisted it regularly trains employees and subcontractors to provide the safest possible work environment for all workers on site.
