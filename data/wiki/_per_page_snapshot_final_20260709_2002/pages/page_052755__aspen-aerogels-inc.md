# Aspen Aerogels, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2024-06-13

**Source:** [Aspen Aerogels, Inc. - Aspen Aerogels Announces Thermal Barrier Award on Major Luxury Sports Car and Reiterates 2024 Financial Outlook](https://ir.aerogel.com/news/news-details/2024/Aspen-Aerogels-Announces-Thermal-Barrier-Award-from-Valmet-Automotive-for-Electric-Porsche-718-Series-and-Reiterates-2024-Financial-Outlook/default.aspx)  ·  `page_052755`

## Facts

- Aspen Aerogels announced a PyroThin® design award from a large EU battery manufacturer to supply a next generation vehicle platform of a major EU luxury sports car brand.
- The EU battery supplier will be the manufacturing partner for the next generation vehicle platform.
- The vehicle platform is expected to underpin multiple nameplates and has an expected start of production in 2025.
- Award represents Aspen’s sixth overall OEM win.
- Aspen President and CEO Donald R. Young stated the company is excited to add another OEM to its growing list of customers as they prepare to launch their next generation EV lineup.
- Mr. Young added that the award and the depth of the quote and development pipeline with additional OEM customers further validates PyroThin® as the go-to EV thermal barrier solution.
- Aspen believes it will add more awards to its roster during 2024 to drive further diversification in its PyroThin® customer base for 2025 and beyond.
- CFO Ricardo C. Rodriguez commented that the profitable Energy Industrial segment, combined with a broad set of blue-chip PyroThin® customers, positions Aspen to continue supporting the long-term EV demand ramp.
- Aspen Battery Materials is the Company's carbon aerogel initiative that seeks to increase the performance of lithium-ion battery cells to enable EV manufacturers to extend driving range and reduce EV costs.
- Aspen has a planned second manufacturing plant in Georgia ("Plant II").
- Plant II has an extended construction and commissioning timeframe.
- Aspen is managing the construction of Plant II to align with expectations of demand from EV customers.
- Forward-looking statements include risks regarding the cost of capital projects, including Plant II.
- Risks include the potential inability to continue construction of Plant II at a cost consistent with estimates and aligned with expectations of EV customer demand.
- EV thermal barrier customers have the right to cancel contracts with Aspen at any time and without penalty.
- Aspen faces risks related to costs, expenses, or investments incurred in excess of projections used to develop pricing under contracts with EV thermal barrier customers.
