# Kia Georgia

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-03-31

**Source:** [LaGrange | Troup County: Unified Community - Georgia Trend Magazine](https://www.georgiatrend.com/2024/03/31/lagrange-troup-county-unified-community/)  ·  `page_000949`

## Facts

- Troup County is experiencing explosive growth driven by Kia and its suppliers in the southwest part of the county, alongside distribution centers like Walmart and UPS.
- The Troup Strategy Center (formerly the Troup County Center for Strategic Planning) leads workforce development initiatives.
- The Troup Strategy Center was incorporated in 2009.
- The Troup Strategy Center launched strategic plan "The Shift Forward" in May 2023.
- The Shift Forward focuses on expanding infrastructure countywide, overcoming employment barriers including daycare and transportation, supporting public schools, and providing diverse housing opportunities.
- Maryanne Lovejoy serves as executive director of the Troup Strategy Center.
- Scott Malone is president of the Development Authority of LaGrange.
- Kelley Bush is director of existing industry and workforce development for the Development Authority of LaGrange.
- Workforce development challenges in the county include a 21% poverty rate, low educational attainment, lack of training, generational poverty, illiteracy, and high school scheduling conflicts preventing college/career academy enrollment.
- Attainable housing is critical to filling the talent pipeline for growing jobs in the area.
- Troup County has 4,000 housing units entitled, in permitting, and/or under construction, a milestone that took seven years to reach.
- West Point Village is a 72-unit mixed-income family apartment complex (one-, two-, and three-bedroom units) that opened in 2023.
- West Point Village was developed through a public-private effort with developer Penrose and the Housing Authority of West Point.
- Hogansville has more than 1,000 housing units in the pipeline for its roughly 3,300-person population.
- Kia Georgia began production in 2009.
- Kia has invested more than $1.9 billion in the state.
- Kia has delivered more than 14,000 Kia and supplier jobs to the area.
- The West Point facility currently manufactures Telluride, Sportage, Sorento SUVs, along with the K5 sedan.
- In 2023, Kia announced it will invest $200 million and create almost 200 jobs to begin assembly of the EV9 SUV.
- Stuart Countess is Kia Georgia’s president and CEO.
- The EV9 expansion provides flexibility to respond faster to consumer demands as Kia moves its mobility focus into the EV space.
- Kia sees the North American market as part of its long-term market strategy and has had great success growing market share in the U.S.
- Kia credits partnerships with state agencies, including the Georgia Ports Authority, for its success.
- The Georgia Ports Authority is constructing the West Georgia Inland Port to connect the region to Savannah via CSX rail.
- Kia suppliers Daehan Solution and KAIS recently moved into a 192,000-square-foot, $35 million facility at the Troup Logistics Center.
- Crow Holdings completed construction of the first building at Pegasus Industrial Park: a 323,000-square-foot, $34 million building leased by Kia in December as a sequencing warehouse for the EV9 project.
- A second Pegasus Industrial Park building (350,000 square feet and $38 million) is slated to open at the end of this year.
- Farpoint Development and Grandview Partners acquired Lafayette Logistics Park, a 134-acre industrial park being developed in two phases for up to 2 million square feet of industrial space.
- Lafayette Logistics Park sits just six miles from the West Georgia Inland Port.
- The EV production line is set to get up and going in the second quarter of this year (2024).
- Mobis is undertaking a $94 million expansion, adding 260,000 square feet and creating 60 new jobs to build chassis for vehicle assembly.
- West Point aims to install an EV charging station in downtown near Point University in 2024.
