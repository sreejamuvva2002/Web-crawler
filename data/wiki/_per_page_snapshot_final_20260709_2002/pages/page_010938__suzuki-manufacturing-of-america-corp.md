# Suzuki Manufacturing of America Corp.

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2008-04-03

**Source:** [Suzuki Manufacturing of America Corp. Celebrates 250,000 ATV Units: Off-Road.com](https://www.off-road.com/atv/feature/suzuki-manufacturing-of-america-corp-celebrates-250000-atv-units-35418.html)  ·  `page_010938`

## Facts

- Suzuki Manufacturing of America Corp. (SMAC) opened in Rome, Ga., in May 2002 as Suzuki's only U.S.-based manufacturing facility.
- Upon opening in May 2002, the Rome facility began producing the Eiger series of ATVs.
- As of April 2008, SMAC employs 300 workers who build ATV frames, mold plastic, and assemble KingQuad 400s, 450s, and 750s.
- The facility produces more than 200 units per eight-hour shift.
- In the year prior to April 2008, almost 60,000 ATVs came off the Rome production line.
- On March 31, 2008, SMAC celebrated the production of its 250,000th ATV at the Rome facility.
- Suzuki plans to celebrate the 25th anniversary of the industry's first 4-wheeled ATV (the 1983 Suzuki LT-125) with events scheduled throughout summer 2008.
- A commemorative ceremony was held at the factory on March 31, 2008, attended by employees, local dignitaries, media, and the Suzuki factory ATV race team (Team Rockstar/MSR/Suzuki/Yoshimura/Pro Taper).
- SMAC ATV Sales Manager Alan Horne stated that the milestone commemorates both Suzuki's ATV production success over the last six years and the successful cooperation with the Rome community.
- Rome Mayor Wright Bagby attended the ceremony and noted that the city has fought for 10 years to save manufacturing jobs, crediting Suzuki employees with helping turn around the manufacturing situation in Rome and the South.
- Mayor Bagby stated that Rome continues to have a higher percentage of manufacturing jobs in the community than most places in the South or the country.
- Team Rockstar/MSR/Suzuki/Yoshimura/Pro Taper Manager Kory Ellis introduced the race team and thanked factory employees for producing quality products and providing support.
- AMA ATV Motocross Series current points leader Dustin Wimmer rode the historic 250,000th unit off the production line to a cheering crowd.
- The 250,000th vehicle was a red KingQuad 750 AXi 4x4 that had been signed by all factory personnel, with additional signatures added by the race team, Mayor Bagby, and other local government attendees.
- American Suzuki Motorcycle/ATV Vice President Mel Harris congratulated Rome and SMAC employees, stating that producing a quarter of a million ATVs in six years is a huge accomplishment and a clear example of Suzuki's commitment to the ATV market.
