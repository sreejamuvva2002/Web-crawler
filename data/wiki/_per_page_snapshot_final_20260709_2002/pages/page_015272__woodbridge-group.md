# Woodbridge Group

## Facts

- The Woodbridge AutoBond™ family of trim foams can be used in a variety of processes and applications.
- The product line is designed to meet the most rigorous OEM durability specifications while still providing superior value to customers.
- Woodbridge AutoBond flexible foam products are manufactured using a continuous-pour method.
- AutoBond polyurethane flexible foams are available in flat and round blocks, and in polyether and polyester foam grades.
- Applications for the foams include seating systems, interior systems, acoustical, and overhead systems.
- Continuous pour flat-block is produced in widths up to 78” (1.98M).
- The largest diameter round block in North America measures 50” (1.27M).
- Polyether and polyester foam grades are available.
- Products offer superior consistency for cell structure and visual appearance.
- A wide range of firmness capability is available for superior craftsmanship.
- Soft firmness options are designed for luxurious "soft" seat insert applications.
- High firmness options are designed for challenging seat trim cover designs.
- High density options are provided for tough bolster durability requirements.
- Thin gauge capability is 0.065” or 1.65mm for low-cost designs.
- Narrow width options are available.
- Excellent acoustical solutions are offered.
- Typical density ranges from 1.5-4.25 lb./ft3 or 27-68 kg/m3.
- Firmness range is 15-175 (25% IFD lb./50 in2).
- Thicknesses range from 1.65-25.5 mm (.065-1 inch).
- Chemistry consists of polyether and polyester open-cell polyurethane.
- Pour types include round block and flat block.
- Fabrication methods include blocks, looping, peeling, sheeting, die cutting, reticulation, and roll compressing.
- Lamination options include PE film, PU film, adhesive films and webs, textile scrims, and non-woven scrims.
- Other key options include a variety of colors, FR/non-FR, acoustics, airflow, and low emission (VOCs and odor).
- AutoBond flexible foams are designed to meet most OEM automotive interiors specifications and requirements.
- Products meet Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard FMVSS302.
- Products meet General Motors specifications GMW15058, GM6291M, GM6083, and GM2101M.
- Products meet Ford specifications WSS-99P48, WSS-M2D243, WSS-M2D491, and ESB-M4D113.
- Products meet FCA specifications MSAY309 and CS-13398.
- Products meet Toyota Motor Company specification TSM6712G.
- Products meet Nissan specification NESM8047.
- Products meet Tesla specification TM-4001.
- Products meet Honda specification HES C209.
- Products meet Hyundai Kia specification MS-300-34.
- Products meet Daimler specifications DBL5450, DBL5454, and DBL5438.
- Products meet Volkswagen specification VW50180.
- The AutoBond Flexible Foam Family includes grades AB, AF, AFX, ABL, ABA, ABS, AL, GC, WLF, LF, and SF.
- Grade AB is a standard Flat Block Ether, Flame-Bondable (FR), FMVSS302 compliant, and Pour-in-Place.
- Grade AF is a High Flat Block Ester, Flame-Bondable (FR), and FMVSS302 compliant.
- Grade AFX is the Highest Flat Block Ether, Flame-Bondable (FR), and FMVSS302 compliant.
- Grade ABL is the Lowest Flat Block Ether.
- Grade ABA is an Alternative Flat Block Ester.
- Grade ABS is a Biocide Flat Block Ether, Flame-Bondable (FR), and FMVSS302 compliant.
- Grade AL is a Flat Block Ether.
- Grade GC is a Pour-in-Place Flat Block Ether, Flame-Bondable (FR), and FMVSS302 compliant.
- Grade WLF is a Low Emission Flat Block Ether, Flame-Bondable (FR), and FMVSS302 compliant.
- Grade LF is a Low Emission Flat Block Ester.
- Grade SF is a Flat Block Ether and Flame-Bondable (FR).
