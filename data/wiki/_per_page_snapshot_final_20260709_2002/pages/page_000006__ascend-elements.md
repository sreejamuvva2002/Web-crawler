# Ascend Elements

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2023-03-30

**Source:** [Ascend Elements Opens North America's Largest Electric Vehicle Battery Recycling Facility in Georgia](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/ascend-elements-opens-north-americas-largest-electric-vehicle-battery-recycling-facility-in-georgia-301786245.html)  ·  `page_000006`

## Facts

- Ascend Elements celebrated the grand opening of its first commercial-scale lithium-ion battery recycling facility in Covington, Georgia on March 30, 2023.
- The $50 million Base 1 facility is North America's largest electric vehicle battery recycling facility.
- The facility began partial operations in August 2022.
- The facility now has an annual capacity to process 30,000 metric tons of used lithium-ion batteries and manufacturing scrap.
- The processing capacity equals 70,000 electric vehicle batteries per year.
- Ascend Elements recovers up to 98% of the critical battery metals in used electric vehicle batteries and gigafactory manufacturing scrap.
- The company uses its patented Hydro-to-Cathode™ process to recycle used lithium-ion batteries and scrap.
- The recycling process can reduce the carbon footprint of new electric vehicle battery cathode materials by up to 90%.
- The facility is powered by renewable energy.
- The facility features onsite wastewater recycling and treatment systems.
- The facility currently employs about 100 team members.
- Ascend Elements plans to increase headcount to 185 by 2024.
- CEO Mike O'Kronley stated the facility is an important part of America's EV battery infrastructure and that this is just the start.
- CEO Mike O'Kronley emphasized the industry needs to continue building a closed-loop battery materials supply chain to make electric vehicle batteries cleaner and more sustainable.
- Ascend Elements is based in Westborough, Mass.
- Ascend Elements produces lithium-ion battery precursor (pCAM) and cathode active materials (CAM) from spent lithium-ion cells using its Hydro-to-Cathode™ direct precursor synthesis technology.
- The company's direct precursor synthesis technology results in improved economics and lowered GHG emissions compared to traditional methods.
