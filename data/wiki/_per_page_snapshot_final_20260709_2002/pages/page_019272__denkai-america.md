# Denkai America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Japanese to build North American headquarters in Augusta — Breaking U.S News](https://newsus.app/japanese-to-build-north-american-headquarters-in-augusta/)  ·  `page_019272`

## Facts

- The Japanese maker of copper foil used in printed electronics will build its U.S. headquarters in Georgia.
- Denkai America is making an initial investment of $150 million in a 115-acre site in Richmond County.
- Ultimately, the company will spend $430 million on a headquarters and production facility.
- The project will create 250 jobs over the next five years.
- Denkai America’s products are used in printed circuit board applications, primarily for the aerospace and defense industries.
- The company's copper foil products are now also used in lithium-ion batteries that power electric vehicles.
- Governor Brian Kemp announced Wednesday that Georgia was selected as the headquarters location for Denkai America.
- Gov. Kemp stated that the selection reinforces Georgia's position as a leader in emerging electric mobility and existing aerospace, defense, and generation industries.
- Gov. Kemp emphasized the state's focus on creating quality jobs for Georgians and attracting unique corporations to Georgia's pro-business environment.
- Nobu Masuda, President and CEO of Denkai America, stated excitement about expanding production presence to Augusta due to its professional and talented corporate workforce.
- Construction is expected to begin in September as the first component of a three-phase investment.
- The facility will be fully operational by December 2024.
- The company will hire qualified commercial technicians, production managers, and engineers.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development, Augusta Economic Development Authority, Georgia Power, and the Georgia Technical College System Quick Start program assisted in landing the project.
- Pat Wilson, Georgia’s Commissioner of Economic Development, stated that as electric mobility adoption becomes widespread, Georgia is committed to ensuring Georgians hired in the auto industry have long-term jobs at home.
- Wilson noted the investment proves Georgia's commitment to building lasting foreign relations and animating economic partnerships.
