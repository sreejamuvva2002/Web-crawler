# Pratt & Whitney

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2026-02-24

**Source:** [RTX’s Pratt & Whitney Announces New $200M Columbus Expansion | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-releases/2026/rtxs-pratt-whitney-announces-new-200m-columbus-expansion-cuts-ribbon-prior-expansion)  ·  `page_002430`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Pratt & Whitney, an RTX business, is investing $200 million to expand its operations in Columbus, Georgia.
- The new investment project will initially create 15 new jobs, with additional jobs to follow.
- Pratt & Whitney President Shane Eddy, Columbus Mayor Skip Henderson, and other public and company leaders celebrated the announcement alongside the completion of a prior expansion announced in 2023.
- The previously announced 2023 project added nearly 400 new jobs and 81,000 square feet to Pratt & Whitney’s Columbus Engine Center.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Pratt & Whitney's decision to invest immediately after completing another major expansion demonstrates the reliable, ongoing, and long-term success companies find in Georgia.
- Governor Kemp noted that throughout its more than forty years in Columbus, Pratt & Whitney has been a valued partner and driver of impact for the state's aerospace industry.
- The Pratt & Whitney Columbus facility opened in 1984 and includes the Columbus Engine Center and Columbus Forge operating on the same campus.
- The Columbus Engine Center maintains Pratt & Whitney GTF™ engines for the Airbus A320neo aircraft family, Airbus A220, and Embraer E-Jets E2.
- The facility also supports the F117 engine for the Boeing C-17 Globemaster III and the F100 engine for the McDonnell Douglas F-15 Eagle and Lockheed Martin F-16 Fighting Falcon.
- The Columbus Forge produces compressor airfoils, and compressor and turbine disks for Pratt & Whitney’s commercial and military engines.
- These components include parts for the F135 engine, which powers all variants of the Lockheed Martin F-35 Lightning II fighter.
- Pratt & Whitney President Shane Eddy stated that over four decades with community and state support, the Georgia presence grew from a small manufacturing facility to a state-of-the-art manufacturing and overhaul center employing 2,600 dedicated people.
- Since 2008, Pratt & Whitney has invested more than $1 billion to continue expanding the footprint and capabilities of the Columbus facility.
- The latest investment will increase output of critical parts for growing military and commercial engine programs and ramp industrial capacity to support customers.
- The Columbus Engine Center is located at 8987 Macon Road, and the Columbus Forge is located at 8801 Macon Road.
- The expansion project is expected to be completed by 2028.
- Columbus Mayor Skip Henderson thanked Pratt & Whitney for its continued faith in the Columbus community and Valley region, and appreciated Governor Kemp, the Georgia Department of Economic Development, and Choose Columbus's assistance.
- Chairman Selvin Hollingsworth of the Development Authority of Columbus, Georgia, stated that the project underscores the strength of the region’s advanced manufacturing sector and the power of partnership in driving economic growth.
- Regional Project Manager Christy Bozeman represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project in partnership with the Development Authority of Columbus, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Pratt & Whitney’s continued growth proves investing in workforce initiatives is the most important factor for the long-term success of companies in Georgia.
- Pratt & Whitney was an early beneficiary of Georgia Quick Start and has engaged with the program for nearly every expansion or major investment over its decades-long relationship.
- GDEcD collaborates with education partners such as Columbus Technical College and Columbus-Muscogee County to support workforce initiatives and company milestones.
- Pratt & Whitney is a world leader in the design, manufacture, and service of aircraft engines and auxiliary power units for military, commercial, and civil aviation customers.
- Since 1925, Pratt & Whitney engineers have pioneered the development of revolutionary aircraft propulsion technologies.
- Pratt & Whitney currently supports more than 90,000 in-service engines through its global network of maintenance, repair, and overhaul facilities.
