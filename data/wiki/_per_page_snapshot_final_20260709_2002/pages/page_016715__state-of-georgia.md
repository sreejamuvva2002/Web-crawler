# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-23

**Source:** [Georgia economic development drives record investments](https://www.atlantamagazine.com/gabiz/georgia-named-the-no1-state-for-business/)  ·  `page_016715`

## Facts

- For fiscal year 2025, the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s Global Commerce team supported 423 facility expansions and new locations.
- The total investment amount is $26.3 billion.
- 23,200 new private sector jobs are expected in the next few years.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that the state's partnership approach and economic development team are delivering results for Georgians that will pay off for generations.
- Governor Brian Kemp thanked partners in the legislature for their support in promoting Georgia to current and prospective employers.
- Seventy-four percent of the 423 facilities were expansions supporting 50,000 existing jobs.
- Seventy-seven percent of the projects are based outside of metro Atlanta.
- Mercedes-Benz established or expanded corporate functions in metro Atlanta.
- Duracell established or expanded corporate functions in metro Atlanta.
- CRH, the leading global provider of building materials, established or expanded corporate functions in metro Atlanta.
- Foreign investment accounted for $3 billion and created 6,500 jobs.
- Sixty-four percent of foreign investments were expansions of companies that already have a footprint in Georgia, such as GreenBox Systems in Butts County and Kiss USA in Bryan County.
- South Korea, Japan, and Canada were the top three sources of foreign investment.
- The state is investing $18 million in rural site preparation through infrastructure grants to help companies set up shop efficiently and effectively in the state.
- Georgia has been named the No. 1 state in which to do business for the 12th year in a row by Area Development magazine.
