## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced during an economic development trip to Israel that Hanon Systems will create 160 new jobs.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Hanon Systems will invest more than $40 million in a new manufacturing facility in Bulloch County.
- Georgia’s automotive and e-mobility industries continue to grow as the state welcomes Hanon Systems.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that as Georgia furthers its position as the EV capital of America, new jobs and opportunities are coming to hardworking Georgians across the state.
- Min Sung, President and Chief Executive Officer of Hanon Systems, stated the company is pleased to contribute to the local economy of Bulloch County through job creation.
- Min Sung thanked the State of Georgia and the cooperation between its agencies that has guided the project.
- Min Sung noted that today’s announcement aligns with Hanon Systems' strategy to support the industry shift toward electrification while growing its North America footprint.
- Hanon Systems will build a new facility at Gateway Regional Industrial Park in Bulloch County to assemble automotive thermal solutions.
- Operations at the new Hanon Systems facility are expected to begin in May 2024.
- Billy Allen, Chair of the Development Authority of Bulloch County, stated that Statesboro-Bulloch County is honored by Hanon Systems’ decision to locate its new facility there.
- Billy Allen noted that the community is excited to welcome another automotive supplier working closely with the Hyundai Metaplant and the electric vehicle industry.
- Billy Allen stated that this decision exemplifies the resources offered by their community and region to help companies succeed.
- Griff Lynch, Executive Director of the Georgia Ports Authority (GPA), stated that the announcement further solidifies Georgia’s position as a growing hub for auto manufacturing.
- Griff Lynch added that GPA is proud to support the operations of Hyundai Motor Group and its suppliers as they deliver jobs and opportunity for Georgia residents.
- Alyce Thornhill, Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy, represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The GDEcD worked in partnership with the Development Authority of Bulloch County, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start on this project.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that suppliers like Hanon Systems successfully support larger industry ecosystems in Georgia through partnership-driven solutions ranging from workforce training to logistics systems efficiencies.
- Pat Wilson noted that Bulloch County, as part of the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority, has been part of a growing success story from the beginning.
- Pat Wilson stated that Georgia’s history of partnership continues to create opportunity for current and future generations.
- Building on the assets that make the automotive industry successful, Georgia is positioned as a hub for the electric mobility industry.
- Since 2020, over 40 EV-related projects have announced more than $22.7 billion in investment and 28,400 jobs in Georgia.
- On October 25, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America officially broke ground in neighboring Bryan County on its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility.
