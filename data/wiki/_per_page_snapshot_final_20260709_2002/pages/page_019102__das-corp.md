# DAS Corp.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-09

**Source:** [DAS Corp. to Bring 300 Automotive Jobs to Metter - Metro Atlanta CEO](https://metroatlantaceo.com/news/2023/09/gov-kemp-das-corp-bring-300-automotive-jobs-metter/)  ·  `page_019102`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that DAS Corp. will establish a new manufacturing facility in Metter.
- The facility will create 300 new jobs.
- The project will generate more than $35 million in investment in Candler County.
- The initiative will involve collaborative indirect investments.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that suppliers for the Hyundai Metaplant resulted in over $2 billion in investment last fiscal year alone, helping Georgia achieve a third straight year of record-breaking economic growth.
- Governor Kemp noted that Georgia's logistics assets, including a reliable network of rail lines and highways, connect companies like DAS to key business partners statewide and globally.
- DAS was established in Korea in 1987.
- DAS specializes in designing and manufacturing seating systems, safety seating components, and seating structures.
- DAS is a key supplier for Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia (KMMG) and Hyundai Motor Group.
- Sean Kim, Chief Operating Officer at DAS, stated the strategic location near prominent cities like Savannah and Macon facilitated convenient access and played a pivotal role in the decision-making process.
- Sean Kim noted Metter was deemed an ideal location in anticipation of its role as a global electric vehicle production hub due to close proximity to Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- The new DAS manufacturing facility is located in the Candler County Industrial Park in Metter.
- The facility will primarily produce automotive seat structures.
- Operations are projected to begin in the latter half of 2024.
- Over the next five years, DAS will be recruiting for assembly technicians.
- DAS intends to establish collaborations with local employment services as part of its hiring process.
- Information about employment opportunities is available online and via email.
- Metter Mayor Ed Boyd stated the City of Metter expects a transformative impact on the community, stimulating investment in housing, creating other business opportunities, and improving social economic wellbeing.
- Glyn Thrift, Chairman of the Candler County Board of Commissioners, thanked DAS for choosing Candler County as its new home and stated they will provide a boost to spur more economic development.
- Griff Lynch, President and CEO of the Georgia Ports Authority, stated the authority is proud to support the growing automotive manufacturing presence in the state to drive economic activity and jobs.
- Alyce Thornhill, Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy, represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The GDEcD partnered with the Candler County Industrial Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, Electric Cities of Georgia, and Georgia Quick Start for this project.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated Georgia is well-positioned for Hyundai’s supplier network due to years of consistent investment in site preparation, workforce solutions, and connectivity.
- Wilson noted DAS is another exciting addition to Georgia’s automotive community.
- For over a century, Georgia has fostered healthy industry practices, encouraged collaboration and innovation, and positioned the state as a leader in developing emerging technologies for the automotive and mobility industry.
- As the market transitions to electric vehicles, Georgia has pursued the entire supply chain, creating more than $25 billion in investments and 30,000 jobs since 2018.
- In fiscal year 2023, job creation in the automotive industry increased by 324 percent compared to FY21.
- Suppliers attracted by Georgia’s mobility original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) resulted in over $2 billion in investment across the state in FY23.
