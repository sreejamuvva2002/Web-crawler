# Murata Electronics North America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [PIR sensor touts tiny footprint - EDN](https://www.edn.com/pir-sensor-touts-tiny-footprint/)  ·  `page_047158`

## Facts

- Murata Electronics North America is located in Smyrna, Ga.
- The company announced its latest pyroelectric infrared (PIR) sensor as the world's smallest with a 5.0mm x 4.7mm x 2.4mm footprint.
- The new sensor carries the global part number IRS-B210ST01-RI and is intended for use in compact, low profile electronic equipment and energy-saving devices.
- According to Murata, the IRS-B210ST01-RI is the only surface mount PIR sensor available on the market today.
- The sensor provides high sensitivity and signal to noise ratio, high stability against temperature changes and white light, and enhanced radio frequency interference (RFI) immunity.
- The PIR sensor includes dual 0.85mm x 1.2mm electrodes with a field of vision of ±70° (horizontal) and ±50° (vertical).
- Its operating temperature range is -40 to +70°C and the device is driven by supply voltage in the range of 2V to 15V.
- The sample price for the sensor is $1.88.
- The lead-time for availability is approximately 12 weeks.
