# Hyundai Motor Georgia Plant

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Kia to produce hybrid cars at Hyundai's Georgia factory from next year - NewsBreak](https://www.newsbreak.com/reuters-555486/3939064123965-kia-to-produce-hybrid-cars-at-hyundai-s-georgia-factory-from-next-year)  ·  `page_010215`

## Facts

- Kia Corp plans to produce hybrid vehicles at Hyundai Motor's new U.S. factory starting in mid-2026.
- Kia CEO Song Ho-sung announced the plan without disclosing specific models.
- Hyundai owns a 35% stake in Kia Motors.
- Hyundai broke ground for an all-electric car factory in Georgia in 2022 under former President Joe Biden's administration.
- The Biden administration promoted EV-friendly policies at the time of the groundbreaking.
- Hyundai is shifting strategy to add hybrid cars to the factory's production lines due to cooling demand for pure electric cars.
- President Donald Trump's administration is threatening to end EV subsidies.
- Kia vehicles will represent 40% of the Georgia facility's total production, according to CEO Song Ho-sung.
- The announcement was made on the sidelines of an opening ceremony for the factory on Wednesday.
- Hyundai plans to expand the factory's annual production capacity to 500,000 vehicles from an originally planned 300,000.
- The expansion brings Hyundai and Kia's combined total U.S. production capacity to 1.2 million vehicles per year.
- The upgraded plan is part of a $21 billion investment package announced by Trump and Hyundai chairman Euisun Chung at the White House earlier this week.
- Trump announced 25% import tariffs on cars and auto parts on Wednesday.
- The tariff move may hurt companies, especially Asian automakers, which are among the largest vehicle exporters to the U.S.
- After Mexico, South Korea is the world's largest exporter of vehicles to the United States, followed by Japan, according to S&P data.
- Kia Motors currently produces most of its hybrid cars, including its Sportage hybrid crossover, in South Korea.
- The new U.S. factory would help achieve Kia's target of boosting U.S. sales to 1.25 million vehicles a year.
- Kia's current U.S. sales volume is 850,000 vehicles.
