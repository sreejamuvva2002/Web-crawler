# Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation of America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2015-06-10

**Source:** [Yamaha’s Georgia Manufacturing Facility Featured in “Small Town, Big Deal” Television Program - UTV Guide](https://utvguide.net/yamahas-georgia-manufacturing-facility-featured-in-small-town-big-deal-television-program/)  ·  `page_015846`

## Facts

- Yamaha’s manufacturing facility in Newnan, Georgia, was featured in a new episode of the RFD-TV network program “Small Town, Big Deal.”
- The episode featuring Newnan and Yamaha first aired on Thursday, June 11, at 8:30 p.m. Central Standard Time.
- The show is co-hosted by Rodney Miller, a long-time leader in the agricultural machinery industry, and Jann Carl, former correspondent and anchor for “Entertainment Tonight.”
- Co-host Rodney Miller stated that Yamaha has had a tremendously positive impact on Newnan from both an economic and philanthropic standpoint.
- Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation of America (YMMC) serves as Yamaha’s worldwide hub for off-highway vehicle (OHV) manufacturing.
- The Newnan facility produces all of the company's Side-by-Side (SxS) vehicles and the majority of its ATV vehicles for national and international distribution.
- The Newnan manufacturing site covers 280 acres with more than 1.3 million square feet of manufacturing facilities.
- The facility employs approximately 1,473 workers.
- YMMC executive vice president Mike Chrzanowski stated that Yamaha has invested more than $250 million into U.S. manufacturing over the past 15 years to create the Newnan facility.
- Products manufactured at the facility include ATV or Side-by-Side vehicles, Golf Cars, and Personal Watercraft.
- Yamaha strives to maintain the highest production quality and environmental standards while giving back to the community through corporate social responsibility programs.
- Yamaha partnered with “Small Town, Big Deal” for an online sweepstakes offering a brand new 2015 Viking EPS Side-by-Side with an approximate retail value of $12,999.
- ATV/SxS marketing manager Steve Nessl stated the partnership was created to show support for communities across the U.S. showcased by the program's hosts.
- The sweepstakes ran from June 10 through August 27 and required no purchase to enter.
- Winners for the sweepstakes were determined by a random drawing.
- The episode aired on RFD-TV, Direct TV channel 345, Dish TV channel 231, and U-verse channel 56.
- Yamaha Motor Corporation, USA (YMUS) has been headquartered in Cypress, California, since its incorporation in 1976.
- YMUS operates facilities in Wisconsin and Georgia, along with factory operations in Tennessee and Georgia.
