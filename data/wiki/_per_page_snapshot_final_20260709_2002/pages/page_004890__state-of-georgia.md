# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-04-09

**Source:** [Why Corporations Consistently Choose Georgia | Georgia](https://georgia.org/blogs/corporations-consistently-choose-georgia-heres-why)  ·  `page_004890`

## Facts

- Corporations across diverse sectors, including FORTUNE 500s like Aflac and UPS and industry leaders like Aurubis and Porsche, are recognizing the benefits of establishing their presence in Georgia.
- Georgia is consistently ranked the Top State for Business.
- The state offers attractive incentives such as a low corporate tax rate.
- Georgia’s workforce consists of 5.3 million people, providing companies with a diverse talent pool.
- The talent pool is strengthened by investments in technical college training, apprenticeship programs, and K-12 education.
- Georgia Quick Start is ranked the nation’s No. 1 workforce training program and offers customized training to create a workforce ready to start production on Day One.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development operates the “Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development” (GRAD) Certification Program to provide development-ready sites.
- GRAD Certified sites have been critical in winning key projects, and the state continues to increase its supply of development-ready properties.
- Georgia continues to invest in a seamless system of ocean, rail, truck, and air transportation to support burgeoning industries.
- Companies have access to Hartsfield-Jackson International Airport, the Port of Savannah, and the Port of Brunswick.
- The state offers a high quality of life as an incentive for companies and their people.
