# Aurubis AG

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- Aurubis is building the country's first plant for complex recycling materials in the US state of Georgia.
- The Georgia facility will have a processing capacity of 180,000 tons of input material per year.
- Aurubis AG is a leading global provider of non-ferrous metals and one of the largest copper recyclers worldwide.
- The company processes complex metal concentrates, scrap metals, organic and inorganic metal-bearing recycling materials, and industrial residues into high-quality metals.
- Aurubis produces more than 1 million tons of copper cathodes annually.
- From its copper cathodes, the company manufactures wire rod, continuous cast shapes, profiles, and flat rolled products made of copper and copper alloys.
- Aurubis also produces precious metals, selenium, lead, nickel, tin, and zinc.
- The company's product portfolio includes sulfuric acid and iron silicate.
- The recycled content in each of Aurubis's copper cathodes is almost 45 percent.
- The company processes more than one million tons of recycling material annually.
- Aurubis aims to achieve carbon-neutral production well before 2050.
- Since 2000, Aurubis has invested more than €780 million in environmental and climate protection, including €47 million in 2021 alone.
- In 2019, the company pledged to develop science-based CO2 reduction targets to help limit global warming.
- CEO Roland Harings stated that Aurubis views sustainability as an essential part of its conduct and business activities, committing to responsible handling of natural resources.
- The company employs approximately 6,900 people and operates production sites in Europe and the USA.
- Aurubis's sustainability reporting aligns with Global Reporting Initiative (GRI) standards and the voluntary Task Force on Climate-related Financial Disclosure (TCFD).
- An external audit for the report was conducted to the exacting standards of "The Copper Mark," an industry initiative verifying copper production site sustainability.
