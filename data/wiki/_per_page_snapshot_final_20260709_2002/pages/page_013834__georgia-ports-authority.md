# Georgia Ports Authority

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [GEORGIA'S PORTS GEORGIA PORTS AUTHORITY ANCHORAGE](https://www.readkong.com/page/georgia-s-ports-georgia-ports-authority-anchorage-5422020)  ·  `page_013834`

## Facts

- Georgia Ports Authority is doubling the space for auto processing at Colonel’s Island in Brunswick, Ga.
- The Savannah Harbor Expansion Project work to deepen the entrance channel to 49 feet and extend it by seven miles is more than 55 percent complete.
- The deeper water will allow Garden City Terminal to better accommodate vessels carrying up to 13,0020-foot equivalent container units.
- At Garden City Terminal, the design phase for a new mega rail terminal reached its first major milestone in March after receiving overwhelming community support.
- Construction contracts on sections of the Garden City mega rail terminal project should be awarded by the end of 2017, with full completion slated for 2020.
- In Chatsworth, Ga., construction has begun on GPA’s second inland terminal to improve rail service and open new markets in Alabama, Tennessee, and Kentucky.
- Operations at the Appalachian Regional Port in Chatsworth are scheduled to commence next year.
- At Colonel’s Island at the Port of Brunswick, 214 acres are in the design or development stages, bringing auto processing space to 547 acres.
- The Authority plans to add another 510 acres on the island’s south side to support auto processing, which will double the capacity for cars and heavy machinery in Brunswick.
- Georgia has been ranked No. 1 state for business for a fourth consecutive year by Site Selection Magazine.
- Gov. Nathan Deal stated that the collaborative framework on state and local levels has led to the creation of hundreds of thousands of jobs, enhanced community outreach by investing millions in local areas, and improved the overall quality of life for all citizens.
- In the last four years since 2011, more than 575,000 private sector jobs have been created in Georgia.
- The Port of Savannah will commission four new ship-to-shore cranes at Garden City Terminal this spring, with each new crane able to lift 65 long tons to a height of 152 feet above the dock.
- GPA Chief Operating Officer Ed McCarthy stated that expanding capabilities allows the Authority to grow along with customers and adjust to logistics marketplace changes such as increasing vessel sizes.
- The new cranes are designed by Konecranes of Finland and can work the largest ships calling on the U.S. East Coast.
- Garden City Terminal will add another three cranes this spring, bringing the total number to 26 this year, with another four Neo-Panamax cranes on order for commissioning in 2018.
- The growing crane fleet working over nearly 10,000 contiguous feet of dock will be able to handle more than 1,000 container moves per hour when all 30 cranes are in place.
- Ed McCarthy stated that the terminal infrastructure improvements demonstrate GPA’s commitment to expand capacity and provide greater flexibility to meet customer needs.
- Nordic Logistics and Warehousing, a member of AGRO Merchants Group, announced plans to develop a new cold storage and repack facility in Savannah.
- In April 2016, AGRO and Nordic completed a 200,000 square-foot expansion to their existing Savannah facility, representing the second investment in the market.
- The new AGRO facility will initially be built to 175,000 square feet adjacent to the current Nordic site with additional land available for future expansion.
- The facility will have 7 multi-temperature rooms with 7,000 total pallet positions, 25,000 square-feet for repacking and bagging operations, and multi-use space set aside to support onsite fumigation and ripening.
- With 25 doors and 85 foot docks, the facility will support efficient product unloading, receiving, inspection, and grading.
- Construction of the AGRO facility is planned to begin in the first quarter of 2017 with operations to begin in the fourth quarter.
- The new facility will be developed and operated with resource expertise from Lucca Freezer and Cold Storage in Vineland, New Jersey, which was acquired by AGRO Merchants in 2014.
- Sonoco announced it will invest $20 million in the development of a new packaging center to support Duracell’s new North America battery packaging operation.
- Sonoco’s Display and Packaging unit will be located in Duracell’s new leased facility in the Atlanta area.
- Sonoco will install and operate state-of-the-art primary packaging equipment at the new center and provide all packaging materials.
- The company will also produce retail merchandising displays which will be packed out at the same facility.
- Variety Wholesalers, Inc. will establish its second major distribution center in Coweta County, creating 320 jobs and investing approximately $10.5 million in the Newnan area.
- The distribution center will support the company’s stores as it expands its presence in the southeastern, midwestern, and south central United States.
- Variety Wholesalers is based in Henderson, N.C., and operates 360 stores in 16 states.
- The newly created jobs at the distribution center will include positions in management, operations, transportation, and logistics.
- Georgia Quick Start will provide customized training for new distribution center employees.
- Wilson Sawyer, chief operating officer of Variety Wholesalers, stated that the opening is the company’s first distribution center outside North Carolina and that they currently operate 48 of their 360 stores in Georgia with a new store opening in Newnan.
- Alliance Tire Group opened a new warehouse in Savannah, Georgia, building a state-of-the-art, 202,000-square-foot facility to permit high efficiency operations such as cross-docking and enhanced put-away systems.
- John Mancinelli serves as Vice President of Operations for Alliance Tire Americas.
- GPA, Cordele Intermodal Services, Glovis America, Hyundai Glovis, and Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia were selected to receive the 2016 Alliance Award by Logistics Management and SMC³.
- In the alliance recognized by the award, Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia moves auto parts via rail from the Port of Savannah to the Cordele Intermodal Services inland terminal, and then to West Point, Ga., via motor carrier.
- A savings of more than 6 million truck miles are created annually as a result of the agreement between Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia and Cordele Intermodal Services.
- Cordele Intermodal Services will handle up to 30,000 TEUs per year for Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia.
- The Alliance Award recognition was revealed at Jump Start 2017, a three-day supply chain event.
