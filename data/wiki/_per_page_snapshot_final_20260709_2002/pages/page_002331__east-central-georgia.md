## Facts

- East Central Georgia is home to the University of Georgia in Athens, chartered in 1785 as the nation’s first public institution.
- Today, the region blends vibrant communities with active research and talent development, supporting innovation, industry, and opportunity across a growing region.
- Area Counties include Barrow, Clarke, Elbert, Greene, Jackson, Jasper, Madison, Morgan, Newton, Oconee, Oglethorpe, and Walton.
- Across the region, a diverse economic base supports established and growing industries like advanced manufacturing and logistics.
- This mix is reflected in communities like Elberton, Monroe, and Winder, where a range of employers continue to grow.
- Weyman Hunt, Owner & CEO, Godfrey’s Feed stated: “Madison in Morgan County is a great place to be. There’s a lot of support in the local community for our products and our industry. It’s a rural county, so it has been a great place to foster what we do. For many years, Morgan County has been the No. 1 county in the state for beef cattle, and I’d like to think we have helped make it that way.”
