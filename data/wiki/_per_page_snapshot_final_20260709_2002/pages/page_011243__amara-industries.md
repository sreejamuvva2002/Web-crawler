# Amara Industries

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2025-12-10

**Source:** [Amara Industries Buys Thomson Plastics Injection](https://www.plasticstoday.com/injection-molding/amara-industries-acquires-thomson-plastics-to-expand-reach)  ·  `page_011243`

## Facts

- Amara Industries has announced its acquisition of Thomson Plastics, a Georgia-based large-tonnage injection molder.
- The terms of the sale were not disclosed.
- Thomson Plastics has clamping force capacities of up to 3,700 tons.
- Thirty percent of Thomson Plastics' fleet exceeds 1,000 tons in clamping force capacity.
- Advanced robotics and automation support Thomson Plastics' operations to ensure reliability and precision.
- Thomson Plastics' technical portfolio includes injection molding, gas-assisted molding, over-molding, insert molding, in-mold labeling, robotic part picking, pad printing, and heat transfer decorating.
- Thomson Plastics has locations in Thomson, GA, and Lexington, NC.
- Vince Orlando serves as co-president at Amara Industries.
- The acquisition combines large-tonnage capacity with advanced automation to meet rising demand for high-performance molded products in key sectors including automotive, consumer goods, industrial, and commercial markets.
- Expansion into the Southeastern United States strengthens Amara Industries' ability to provide customers with faster delivery, greater flexibility, and improved supply chain efficiency.
- Amara Industries was founded in 2025.
- Amara Industries has more than 500 employees.
- Amara Industries' operations span injection molding, profile extrusion, decorating, material compounding, and assembly.
