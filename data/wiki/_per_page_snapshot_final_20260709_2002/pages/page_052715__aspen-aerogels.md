# Aspen Aerogels

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** thermal_management  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [LPO Announces Conditional Commitment to Aspen Aerogels to Produce Aerogel Blankets and Improve Electric Vehicle Battery Safety](https://www.energycentral.com/energy-biz/post/lpo-announces-conditional-commitment-aspen-aerogels-produce-aerogel-bQ2rQNatMZdC0g9)  ·  `page_052715`

## Facts

- LPO announced a conditional commitment for a direct loan of up to $670.6 million to Aspen Aerogels.
- The loan will help finance the construction of an aerogel blanket manufacturing facility in Register, Georgia.
- Aspen’s PyroThin® aerogel thermal barriers are used as a protective layer within electric vehicle (EV) batteries.
- These thermal barriers enable EV manufacturers to achieve critical battery safety goals.
- The project helps the U.S. meet expected future demand for EV adoption.
- The project reinforces the Biden-Harris administration’s "Investing in America" agenda to onshore and re-shore domestic manufacturing technologies.
- This supports the administration’s goal that half of all new vehicles sold in 2030 are zero-emissions vehicles.
- Operating at full capacity, the Georgia factory is expected to supply thermal barriers for over 2 million vehicle batteries annually.
- The project is expected to create up to 550 construction jobs.
- The project is expected to create up to 255 permanent, full-time operations jobs.
- This supports the administration’s efforts to deliver good-paying, high-quality jobs to communities across the country.
- This project adds to the nearly 16 million jobs created since President Biden and Vice President Harris took office.
- While the conditional commitment indicates the U.S. Department of Energy (DOE)'s intent to finance the project, DOE and the company must satisfy certain technical, legal, environmental, and financial conditions before definitive financing documents are entered into and the loan is funded.
