# PIOLAX, INC.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [PIOLAX, INC. Business Report FY2007 - MarkLines Automotive Industry Portal](https://www.marklines.com/en/top500/cf/s500_489_hl2007)  ·  `page_048336`

## Facts

- Piolax, Inc. reported FY2007 sales of 48,820 million JPY, representing a 15.1% increase from FY2006.
- Operating income for FY2007 was 4,074 million JPY, up 74.1% from FY2006.
- Ordinary income for FY2007 was 4,877 million JPY, up 56.6% from FY2006.
- Current net income for FY2007 was 3,281 million JPY, up 65.6% from FY2006.
- Piolax enhanced its production capacity mainly in Asia to cope with rising demand and launched commercial operations at a new plant in China to strengthen its regional supply network.
- In the precision spring segment, Piolax has alliances with Korean and Japanese carmakers to conduct joint development activities.
- Following joint development activities in Thailand and Mexico, Piolax completed similar activities with a Japanese carmaker in China and launched commercial production.
- Major carmakers in and outside Japan are inquiring about Piolax's side locks for glove boxes, expecting an increase in vehicle models equipped with these locks.
- Piolax's share of new dampers grew due to increased joint development activities with carmakers in Japan, Europe, the U.S.A., and Korea.
- A greater number of carmakers outside Japan are adopting Piolax's in-tank evaporation systems for use in metal tanks equipped on their vehicle models.
- Piolax will begin local production of automotive metal parts in China in fiscal 2008 to meet growing needs from local Japanese car manufacturers, including Nissan and Honda.
- The company will study the possibility of adding a new facility to its existing plant in Guangdong Province, China.
- Producing metal parts in addition to resin parts will allow Piolax to triple its automotive sales in China to 2.2 billion yen by 2010.
- The supplier will add production lines on the premises of the new Chinese plant to make metal parts.
- Piolax plans to expand its business with US automakers and has already acquired a new order from Ford Motor Company for pickup truck components.
- Piolax will send engineers to Ford R&D departments to win more orders by getting directly involved with development activities.
- The company will begin supplying fasteners to clip exterior and interior parts to bodies of new Ford pickup trucks planned for release in 2010.
- Fastener production for Ford will begin in the latter half of 2009 at Piolax's Thai plant.
- Piolax has a plan to expand its capacity to produce glove box side lock components for General Motors Corporation.
- By fiscal 2010, Piolax aims to triple its sales to GM to 600 million yen compared to the current level.
- These efforts will allow Piolax to raise its ratio of sales to US makers to 7 to 8% in ten years, which is about 2.5 times the current level.
- R&D expenses were 544 million yen in FY2007.
- R&D expenditure on automotive products was 427 million yen.
- Piolax developed compressed coil spring materials used in engines and gears.
- The company commercialized products that are more compact and lighter in weight.
- Piolax strengthened its development activities on complex springs, enabling them to be installed easier.
- The company worked on developing surface coatings for spring materials that do not contain hexavalent chromium.
- Piolax started development activities on products that comply with the new REACH Regulation in Europe.
- The company is developing versatile fasteners that can be used on both thin and thick metal plates, fasteners that require less force when being installed, fasteners with very powerful fastening capabilities, and fasteners that are easier to disassemble during car scrapping.
- Piolax is considering migrating to materials that emit less odor in vehicle interiors while working to develop environmentally friendly products that enhance interior comfort.
- The company is currently developing high-strength, user-friendly net hooks used in car interior parts.
- Piolax developed side locks for glove boxes and released them in January 2003, which are now being equipped on an increasing number of vehicle models to improve performance and workability.
- The company worked on developing new dampers.
- Piolax is currently developing hinge parts to open/close the console box lid and the floor board in the rear luggage compartment.
- In the area of valves for plastic tanks, Piolax enhanced the performance and reduced the manufacturing costs of rollover valves and inlet check valves.
- As of March 2008, Piolax has licensing-in agreements with Norma Germany GmbH for automotive fuel delivery components and plastic fuel tank components, with contracts renewing annually or covering terms from September 18, 1995 to December 12, 2006.
- As of March 2008, Piolax has licensing-out agreements for manufacturing technology to Sanjia Industrial Co., Ltd. (Taiwan) for metallic and plastic fasteners, Taiwan Houmu Industrial Co., Ltd. for automotive clutch pressure springs, torsion springs, and engine valve springs, and various Piolax affiliates in the USA, UK, Korea, Thailand, and China for precision plastic slips, precision springs, and related assembly products.
- In FY2007, Piolax's capital investments totaled 3,567 million yen, mainly spent on automotive parts production facilities.
- The company spent 2,469 million yen in Japan and 1,098 million yen outside Japan for capital investments.
