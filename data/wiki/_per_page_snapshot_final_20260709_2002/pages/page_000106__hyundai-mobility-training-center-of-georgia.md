# Hyundai Mobility Training Center of Georgia

**Type:** workforce_training  ·  **Role:** workforce_training  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [HMTCG Grand Opening - Georgia Quick Start](https://www.georgiaquickstart.org/hmtcg-grand-opening/)  ·  `page_000106`

## Facts

- On November 5th, 2025, Governor Brian Kemp and Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America President and CEO Tony Heo joined state and local leaders to celebrate the grand opening of the Hyundai Mobility Training Center of Georgia.
- The center is an 89,000-square-foot state-of-the-art facility designed to train the next generation of electric vehicle (EV) manufacturing professionals.
- The facility is strategically located adjacent to Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) in Bryan County.
- This marks the first training center in Georgia geared toward EVs and sets a new standard for workforce development.
- HMGMA has announced an expansion that will include hybrid vehicles, and the flexible facility is designed to accommodate training for those vehicles in the future.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that with record-breaking economic development statewide, the focus remains on bringing quality jobs to hardworking Georgians and connecting them to the skills needed for success.
- Governor Brian Kemp added that the Hyundai Mobility Training Center of Georgia will give thousands of people over the years the knowledge they need to benefit from this generational project.
- José Muñoz, President and CEO of Hyundai Motor Company, stated that the center represents a commitment to investing in people as the company continues to create high-paying American jobs.
- José Muñoz noted that as production expands at HMGMA, one of the most advanced facilities in the world, a workforce equipped with skills to build next-generation vehicles with next-generation production processes is needed.
- José Muñoz mentioned that the partnership with Georgia Quick Start ensures Meta Pros have access to world-class training that will help them succeed and help Georgia lead in advanced manufacturing.
- José Muñoz thanked Governor Kemp, Commissioner Pat Wilson, and the economic development team in Georgia for their ongoing support.
- Hyundai’s project in Georgia will create approximately 8,500 jobs by 2031.
- At full capacity, the facility can train up to 824 individuals at a time.
- The center provides customized, immersive education tailored to Hyundai’s advanced EV manufacturing processes.
- The center is operated by Georgia Quick Start, the State’s renowned workforce development program and part of the Technical College System of Georgia (TCSG).
- The center will prepare employees for roles at HMGMA and affiliate companies.
- Greg Dozier, Commissioner of TCSG, stated that the center is about giving Georgians the tools to build their own futures right here at home.
- Greg Dozier added that the center reflects Georgia’s commitment to innovation, accessibility, and long-term economic opportunity.
- Quick Start’s training professionals collaborated with HMGMA to design and build world-class infrastructure that closely mirrors the processes trainees will encounter at the Metaplant.
- The facility includes a STEP Line (Safe Transition for Efficient Production), an introductory line where trainees build timing, awareness, and coordination skills in a controlled, digital environment.
- The facility includes an IMPACT Line (Immersive Manufacturing Process and Conveyor Training), a full-length, moving production line with 53 integrated assembly activities and a built-in quality check system replicating real-world EV production.
- The facility includes a High Voltage Battery and Safety Lab for hands-on learning with high-voltage systems critical to EV and hybrid vehicle performance.
- The facility includes a VR Experience Lab, a simulated environment for safety and hazard identification.
- The facility includes an Automation & Mechatronics Lab focused on programmable logic controllers (PLCs), sensors, motion systems, and industrial automation.
- Quick Start Deputy Commissioner Scott McMurray stated that subject matter experts partnered directly with HMGMA to learn the skills its Meta Pros will need when they hit the production floor.
- Scott McMurray added that the team has simulated those processes in a safe, hands-on training environment.
- Additional facilities include a 5,764-square-foot multipurpose “Momentum Room,” six flexible classrooms, a robotics lab, welding lab, paint lab, and a robotic dog that supports advanced automation training.
- The training center stands as a core pillar of Georgia’s broader strategy to prepare and train the state’s workforce for success in advanced manufacturing.
- The launch reaffirms Georgia’s leadership in the global mobility and EV manufacturing sectors while delivering unmatched value to communities across the state.
- Tony Heo stated that the grand opening marks a proud milestone for Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America, the State of Georgia, the Technical College System, and Georgia Quick Start.
- Tony Heo added that the training center gives confidence that trained talent will be available to shape the future of mobility and American manufacturing right here in Southeast Georgia.
