# Manufacture of Advanced Key Energy Infrastructure Technologies (MAKE IT) Prize

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [DOE Awards $5 Million Towards Clean Energy Manufacturing Development Through MAKE IT Prize | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/technologytransitions/articles/doe-awards-5-million-towards-clean-energy-manufacturing-development)  ·  `page_029455`

## Facts

- U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) announced winners in both the Facilities and Strategies Tracks of the Manufacture of Advanced Key Energy Infrastructure Technologies (MAKE IT) Prize.
- DOE awarded a total of $4,500,000 to nine Facilities Track Phase 1 winners.
- DOE awarded a total of $600,000 to 12 Phase 1 Strategies Track winners.
- The MAKE IT Prize was developed by the Office of Technology Transitions (OTT), the Offices of Clean Energy Demonstrations (OCED), and Energy Efficiency and Renewable Energy (EERE).
- The prize aims to catalyze domestic manufacturing of critical clean energy technology components.
- Facilities Track winners demonstrated credible plans for establishing manufacturing facilities with intent to break ground by the end of the competition.
- Strategies Track winners demonstrated expertise, experience, and connections to develop roadmaps for attracting clean energy manufacturing and secure commitments from manufacturers.
- DOE Chief Commercialization Officer and Director of OTT Dr. Vanessa Z. Chan stated the MAKE IT Prize is helping catalyze domestic clean energy manufacturing and development of secure, diverse, and domestic supply chains.
- Dr. Chan noted that thanks to President Biden's Investing in America agenda, domestic manufacturing is being brought back and MAKE IT helps communities take advantage of this momentum.
- Anderson Clean Energy, on behalf of Bosch US, plans to establish an electrolyzer manufacturing facility in South Carolina.
- Electrolyzers from the South Carolina facility will be a critical component for OCED-funded Regional Clean Hydrogen Hubs and future clean hydrogen generation facilities.
- Switched Source plans to establish a manufacturing facility for hybrid inverters, identified as a bottleneck for electric grid upgrades.
- CellCube plans to establish a manufacturing facility for vanadium redox flow batteries, described as one of the most promising long-duration energy storage solutions.
- The Rosebud Sioux Tribe will engage with community stakeholders to develop an integrated approach to workforce development, clean energy development, and manufacturing benefiting Native Americans on their Reservation.
- Ballard Power Systems intends to establish a new hydrogen fuel cell engine assembly facility in Rockwall, Texas, estimated to create over 100 new highly skilled jobs.
- CellCube Lean Energy Assembly Network (CCLEAN) seeks to accelerate vanadium redox flow battery manufacturing in the U.S.
- Ceramic NaSICON Membranes for LDES, led by Adena Power and partnering with Current Chemicals, will revitalize an existing brownfield facility to rapidly scale manufacturing of sodium super ionic conductor ceramic electrolyte membranes for long-duration sodium battery technologies.
- Components for Fuel Cell Trucks: 100% Made in US, led by Boston Materials, aims to manufacture proton-exchange membrane fuel cell components (bipolar plates) that are lighter and lower cost than incumbent technology with a potential life of 30,000 hours.
- Conformable Hydrogen Tanks for Production Capacity, led by Noble Gas Systems, seeks to convert a currently vacant building in Southeast Michigan into a manufacturing facility for conformable hydrogen fuel tanks, envisioning creation of 50 high-quality jobs in a disadvantaged community.
- H2 Electrolyzers for North American Market, a Bosch US-led project, will initiate domestic manufacturing of 1.25MW PEM electrolyzer stacks for the North American market.
- Hybrid Transformer–Power Electronics Equipment Manufacturing by Switched Source will ramp up manufacturing of hybrid transformers for distribution utilities, leveraging power electronics for distribution automation through medium voltage power flow control, expecting to create more than 75 new jobs in disadvantaged communities while collaborating on upskilling and workforce development initiatives.
- Linde Hydrogen Storage Vessel project by Linde Engineering will begin domestic fabrication of vacuum-insulated storage vessels for gaseous and liquid hydrogen, aiming to help secure the U.S. supply chain for liquid hydrogen.
- Securing America’s Vanadium Electrolyte Supply, led by Stryten Energy, will redevelop a shuttered facility in Augusta, Georgia to build a large-scale manufacturing facility for vanadium electrolyte production, hoping to rapidly scale US-based production and commercialization of vanadium redox flow battery components and systems.
- Phase 2 winners must demonstrate they are “shovel-ready” for construction, including proof of location access, permits, financing, and community engagement, while finalizing their intended manufacturing facility location.
- Facilities Track Phase 2 winners will be awarded $4.5 million each in the final phase of this track.
- Strategies Track Round 2 Phase 1 winner Building A Climate Resilient Future for All (Delaware), led by Delaware Sustainable Clean Energy Coalition, will bring together community partners, universities, community colleges, industry professionals, entrepreneurs, and environmental justice organizations to build a comprehensive roadmap enabling Delaware to become a hub for clean energy innovation, production, and workforce development.
- Strategies Track Round 2 Phase 1 winner Clean Energy Manufacturing in Mendenhall, MS (Mississippi), led by Son Solar Mississippi, will develop a roadmap to establish clean energy manufacturing, economic development, and job creation in rural Mendenhall, MS.
- Strategies Track Round 2 Phase 1 winner Creating the Equitable Clean Energy Future (Georgia), led by Advancing & Catalyzing Clean Energy Leadership at the Georgia Institute of Technology, will develop a clean energy technology roadmap for Georgia aiming to position it as a global leader in clean energy technology within the next 10 years.
- Strategies Track Round 2 Phase 1 winner Inland SoCal for Advanced Manufacturing (California), led by Blended Impact with partners including California Governor's Office of Business and Economic Development, aims to spur clean energy manufacturing and economic growth in Riverside and San Bernardino counties.
- Strategies Track Round 2 Phase 1 winner Innovate Jonesboro (Maine), led by Born Global, will develop novel bioproduct manufacturing capacity throughout Maine tailored to disadvantaged communities by introducing novel bioenergy products and creating new manufacturing jobs.
- Strategies Track Round 2 Phase 1 winner Joliet Commercialization Coalition (JCC) (Montana), led by the JCC, will expedite assessment and cleanup of brownfield sites across Montana while advancing clean energy manufacturing and environmental justice.
- Strategies Track Round 2 Phase 1 winner Make It Mohawk Valley (New York) seeks to establish the Mohawk Valley as an innovation hub for clean energy manufacturers through collaboration and outreach, leveraging existing metal fabrication, electrical wiring, and specialty tool manufacturers.
- Strategies Track Round 2 Phase 1 winner MAKE IT on the Rosebud Reservation (South Dakota), led by the Rosebud Sioux Tribe, will work to attract clean energy and related manufacturing partners benefiting both partners and members.
- Strategies Track Round 2 Phase 1 winner Metal Fuels Alliance (Virginia), led by Dominion Energy Innovation Center, brings together partners, utilities, community labor, and tribal organizations to advance green alumina manufacturing and drive industrial decarbonization across Virginia’s I-64 Corridor.
- Strategies Track Round 2 Phase 1 winner Project 81 (Oklahoma) aims to establish advanced manufacturing incubators along US Highway 81 in Oklahoma to modernize and expand the existing manufacturing base, provide access to advanced manufacturing technologies for small manufacturers, and create workforce development programs with the Oklahoma Career Technical System.
- Strategies Track Round 2 Phase 1 winner Quincy’s Strategy to “Make It” Happen! (Florida), led by Big Bend Community Development Corporation, seeks to attract clean tech manufacturers and fuel cell developers to create employment opportunities and economic growth in rural Quincy, FL.
- Strategies Track Round 2 Phase 1 winner South Arkansas Green Energy Project (SAGEP) (Arkansas) seeks to attract clean energy manufacturing to the City of Strong and Union County, AR, with partners including the City of Strong, Southwest Arkansas Planning and Development District, University of Arkansas Pine Bluff, and Black Community Development Chamber of Arkansas.
- Approximately 20 Strategies Track winners will be awarded $100,000 each across two rounds of Phase 2 to create regional roadmaps and demonstrate manufacturer interest before advancing to Phase 3.
- Approximately eight grand prize winners will ultimately be selected and awarded $250,000 each for developing clean energy manufacturing roadmaps and attracting facility plans to their regions.
- Competing teams for both MAKE IT Prize tracks will submit relevant materials in May.
- At the end of the competition, DOE will have awarded a total of approximately $30 million in federal funding to teams from both tracks.
- The MAKE IT Prize is funded by the Technology Commercialization Fund through the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law and administered through the American-Made program.
