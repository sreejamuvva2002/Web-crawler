# City of Canton Economic Development

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [City of Canton Economic Development | Canton, GA](https://www.cantonga.gov/business/business-support/city-of-canton-economic-development)  ·  `page_029639`

## Facts

- Canton's economy has diversified from cotton textile manufacturing to include healthcare, education, technology, advanced manufacturing, corporate and regional headquarters, government services, and retail trade.
- The City of Canton, along with economic development partners, offers exceptional incentives to relocating and expanding businesses.
- Businesses that locate within the Central Business District may qualify for an 80% reduction on all new water and sewer tap fees.
- Businesses that locate within the Central City Opportunity Zone may become eligible to receive $3,500 per created job when two or more jobs are created.
- The Downtown Development Revolving Loan Fund (DDRLF) is available through the Georgia Department of Community Affairs (DCA) and offers below-market rate financing to fund capital projects in core historic downtown areas and adjacent historic neighborhoods where it will spur commercial redevelopment.
- The Canton Main Street Program in partnership with Canton Tourism administers a grant program for downtown property owners seeking to improve the exterior of their buildings.
- Property owners can apply for up to $2,500 in matching grant funds that can be used for a variety of exterior work through the Façade Improvement Program.
- The City offers a 100% Freeport Inventory Tax Exemption covering raw materials and goods in process of manufacture, finished goods produced in Georgia within the last 12 months, and finished goods stored in Georgia within the last 12 months destined for shipment out-of-state.
- Businesses that locate within Riverstone may qualify for a 30% discount on new sewer tap fees.
- Businesses that locate within the River Mill Livable Centers Initiative (LCI) Enterprise Zone may qualify for abatement of occupational tax, inspection fees, and other fees based on the project’s economic impact.
- Property tax abatement is provided for a certain period of time based on the value of investment into existing property improvements in the River Mill LCI Enterprise Zone.
- Eligible businesses in the River Mill LCI Enterprise Zone must create and maintain at least five new full-time jobs.
- Businesses that locate within the Technology Ridge Opportunity Zone may become eligible to receive $3,500 per created job when two or more jobs are created.
- The Canton Campus of Chattahoochee Technical College offers popular business courses to students, including Business Management, Accounting, and Marketing Management.
- These programs prepare students for entry into management or supervisory occupations in a variety of businesses and industries.
- The Cherokee Office of Economic Development (COED) works each day to drive economic development in Cherokee County by promoting the community’s business, academic and lifestyle strengths to continually attract dynamic companies and professionals to the county.
- The Georgia Department of Labor provides services including administration of unemployment insurance, employment service, provision of workforce information to the public and private sectors, and oversight of child labor issues.
- The Georgia Small Business Development Center (SBDC) provides tools, training and resources to help small businesses grow and succeed, with a mission to enhance the economic well-being of Georgians by providing educational services for small business owners and aspiring entrepreneurs.
- The Go Build Georgia Foundation is the private arm of the public/private partnership of the Go Build Georgia program undertaken by the Governor’s Office of Workforce Development.
- Go Build Georgia educates children on the value of learning a trade.
