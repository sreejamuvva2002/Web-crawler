# Georgia Department of Economic Development

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2020-07-01

**Source:** [Georgia economic development in FY 21: 24,000 new jobs, $8.43 billion in new investment](https://www.yahoo.com/news/georgia-economic-development-fy-21-210600181.html)  ·  `page_008249`

## Facts

- Gov. Brian Kemp announced that more than 24,000 jobs were created across all regions of Georgia by economic development projects during the first three quarters of fiscal year 2021.
- These projects total more than $8.43 billion in new investments, representing a 67% increase in new investments compared to the first nine months of the previous fiscal year.
- Job creation is up 49% over the same period during Fiscal Year 2020.
- Gov. Kemp stated that creating high-paying jobs for Georgians regardless of zip code has been a key priority since day one, and that targeted investments in K-12 education, workforce development, and innovative partnerships are delivering opportunities statewide.
- During the third quarter of the fiscal year between January and March 2021, Georgia solidified its position as an internationally recognized tech hub and data center region with commitments from Airbnb, The Adecco Group, and Google.
- Microsoft announced plans to build on its existing Georgia footprint to develop one of the company's largest hubs in metro Atlanta.
- Microsoft will establish new data centers in Fulton and Douglas counties.
- Georgia's fiscal year-to-date investment and job creation totals do not include complete details on expected investments and job creation from Microsoft or Airbnb.
- Business expansions accounted for 68% of all projects and total investments as companies chose Georgia as the best location for growth.
- New locations to the state accounted for 58% of new jobs in economic development projects from July 1, 2020, through March 31.
- Food processing, manufacturing, and logistics and distribution industries continued a robust pace of development, accounting for 63% of jobs created by economic development projects.
- Georgia's automotive and information and technology industries accounted for nearly 5,000 new jobs generated three-quarters into the fiscal year.
- Freshly opened its first Southeastern U.S. facility in Cobb County.
- Sailfish Boats expanded in the southwest Georgia city of Cairo.
- Feit Electric invested in their first East Coast distribution facility in McDonough.
- Brake pad supplier KB Autosys selected Meriwether County in west Georgia for its first U.S. automotive manufacturing facility, locating near regional customers such as Hyundai, Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia, and General Motors.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Georgia's economy is diverse, promising, and continuing to gain momentum after a volatile year, thanking private and public sector partners for helping maintain the state's position as the No. 1 state to live, work, and raise a family.
