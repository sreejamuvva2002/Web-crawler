# Sewon America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Georgia: EV auto parts manufacturer to create 700-plus jobs - WJCL](https://www.wjcl.com/article/sewon-america-rincon-effingham-county-jobs/43008982)  ·  `page_001361`

## Facts

- Sewon America will create 740 new jobs in Rincon.
- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp announced more than 700 new jobs are on their way to Effingham County.
- Sewon America is a subsidiary of Korean-based Sewon Precision Industry Co.
- Sewon America will invest more than $300 million in a new manufacturing facility.
- Georgia House Speaker Jon Burns, who represents part of Effingham County, stated that the announcement marks a proud day for Effingham County and coastal Georgia.
- Jon Burns thanked Sewon America, Governor Kemp, and public/private partners for making the announcement possible.
- Sewon America operates one of the largest automobile body parts manufacturing plants in North America.
- Sewon opened a manufacturing facility in LaGrange in 2008, which has since doubled in size.
- Following the completion of the Rincon facility, Sewon is expected to support 1,600 jobs in Georgia.
- Sewon’s new facility will be located at the Grande View industrial park in Rincon.
- The new manufacturing location will produce electric vehicle body parts for original equipment manufacturers.
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America is listed as an original equipment manufacturer customer for the new facility.
- Operations at the new Rincon facility are expected to begin in 2025.
- On October 25, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America officially broke ground on its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility in neighboring Bryan County.
- Georgia is positioned as a hub for the electric mobility industry.
- Since 2020, more than 35 EV-related projects have announced more than $21 billion in investment and 26,700 jobs in Georgia.
