# Ascend Elements

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-06

**Source:** [Republic of Poland Offers Ascend Elements Up to USD 320mn to Support Construction of Lithium-ion Battery Materials Plant in Poland - Brands Review Magazine](https://brandsreviewmagazine.com/technology/republic-of-poland-offers-ascend-elements-up-to-usd-320mn-to-support-construction-of-lithium-ion-battery-materials-plant-in-poland/)  ·  `page_052600`

## Facts

- The Ministry of Economic Development and Technology in Poland offered Ascend Elements up to USD 320 million (1.22 billion Polish Zloty) to support construction of a sustainable precursor cathode active material (pCAM) manufacturing facility in Poland.
- pCAM is a high-value, precisely engineered material used in the manufacture of lithium-ion batteries for electric vehicles and other advanced industrial applications.
- The cash subsidy is one of the largest grants ever offered by the Republic of Poland.
- The grant operates under the E.U.’s Temporary Crisis and Transition Framework (TCTF) to support Europe’s transition to a net-zero economy.
- Linh Austin, President and CEO at Ascend Elements, stated the offer marks a significant step forward in the company's European growth strategy and commitment to building a sustainable battery materials supply chain on both sides of the Atlantic.
- Ascend Elements has identified a location in Poland for its first advanced battery materials (pCAM) manufacturing facility in Europe.
- CEO Linh Austin met with Poland’s Secretary of State Michał Jaros at the Polish Ministry of Economic Development and Technology in Warsaw to receive the grant offer.
- Pawel Pudlowski, Ph.D., Vice President of the Management Board at the Polish Investment and Trade Agency (PAIH), stated battery sector projects are key to PAIH’s investment portfolio and called the Ascend Elements project one of the largest U.S. investments in recent years, strengthening Poland’s role in the battery supply chain and supporting regional development and cooperation with local educational institutions.
- Ascend Elements plans to commercialize innovative technology for manufacturing sustainable nickel, manganese, and cobalt (NMC) pCAM made from recycled lithium-ion battery materials at the new Polish facility.
- Tomasz Poznar, Ph.D., Senior Vice President of Commercial at Ascend Elements, described the project as a critical step in transforming Europe’s battery materials landscape by bringing Hydro-to-Cathode technology to Poland to bridge the cathode precursor supply gap and contribute to EU net-zero goals.
- The company’s Hydro-to-Cathode direct pCAM synthesis technology produces new NMC pCAM material from spent lithium-ion cells more efficiently than traditional methods, improving economics and lowering carbon emissions.
- A recently published life cycle assessment found the Hydro-to-Cathode process produces 49% lower carbon dioxide equivalent (CO2e) emissions and 26% lower particulate matter 2.5 (PM 2.5) emissions compared to traditional battery recycling methods.
- By 2030, Ascend Elements aims to achieve 86% lower CO2e emissions and 94% lower PM 2.5 emissions through additional decarbonization efforts.
- In 2024, Ascend Elements and Elemental Strategic Metals established a joint venture to own and operate the AE Elemental JV battery recycling facility in Zawiercie, Poland, while the new pCAM facility will be solely owned and operated by Ascend Elements.
- In the United States, Ascend Elements owns and operates one of North America’s largest battery recycling facilities in Covington, Georgia.
- A new lithium recovery line at the Georgia plant is expected to produce up to 3,000 metric tons of Lithium Carbonate (Li₂CO₃) annually, making Ascend Elements the first U.S. company to produce Li₂CO₃ from recycled content.
- In Hopkinsville, Kentucky, the company is building an advanced Hydro-to-Cathode pCAM manufacturing facility to produce engineered battery materials for up to 750,000 electric vehicle batteries annually.
- The Kentucky facility is expected to be operational in late 2026 and will be North America’s largest NMC pCAM manufacturer when complete.
