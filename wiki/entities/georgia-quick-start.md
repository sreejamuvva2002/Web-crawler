# Georgia Quick Start

**Type:** Government Program  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[government-support|Government Support]]  ·  **Location:** Georgia, United States

## Overview

A discretionary state incentive program that provides customized workforce training and support to attract and retain jobs in Georgia’s automotive, battery and clean-tech supply chains.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Provides training for tier-one automotive suppliers and clean-tech manufacturers, supporting the EV supply chain workforce in Georgia.

## Timeline

- **2015** — Georgia Quick Start was asked to build a customized Advanced Manufacturing Training Center on a site just outside Savannah, Georgia.
- **2022** — Announcement of the largest economic development project in state history; expansion of the training center to support Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America and other EV supply-chain companies.
- **1967** — Georgia legislature passed legislation creating Georgia Quick Start, the first discretionary incentive to provide free customized workforce training.
- **2010** — Area Development magazine first survey where Georgia Quick Start won the top spot for workforce training programs
- **2011** — Georgia Quick Start again ranked No.1 in Area Development survey
- **2012** — Georgia Quick Start again ranked No.1 in Area Development survey
- **September 2024** — Latest Area Development survey published, citing Georgia Quick Start as the best customized workforce training program in the U.S.
- **2024** — Georgia named No.1 State for Doing Business overall according to Area Development ranking

## Key facts

- Georgia Quick Start is well-known as one of Georgia’s most effective discretionary incentives for attracting jobs to the state.
- Quick Start pioneers customized training solutions, drawing from electrical engineering, software design, robotics, and control systems.
- Quick Start recently built a first-of-its-kind mechatronics trainer to meet industry needs.
- Quick Start has been training King’s Hawaiian Bakery since they located in Oakwood, GA, supporting their expansion.
- Hyundai Mobis, the largest tier-one supplier to Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America, maintains a long-time training relationship with Quick Start.
- Hanwha Qcells’ investment in Dalton and Cartersville is helping secure Georgia’s reputation as a clean-tech leader, with Quick Start developing a workforce of the future for the company.
- Design, develop and deliver customized, company-specific workforce training for qualified companies investing and creating jobs in Georgia.
- "It’s impressive. I think this facility just continues to give us something that we can offer to really any kind of company that would want to come do advanced manufacturing in Georgia." - Governor Brian Kemp
- The center trains workers for Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America and other companies supplying parts needed to assemble electric vehicles.
- Hyundai Transys is currently training at the Advanced Manufacturing Training Center, focusing on vehicle comfort components.
- Quick Start is building a state-of-the-art 90,000-sq.ft. training center to help prepare the “Meta Pros” of tomorrow and Hyundai affiliates.
- The design of the facility pays homage to historic Savannah while meeting the needs of a 21st-century smart factory.
- Georgia is now a leader in clean technology; the training facility is expanding to accommodate new trainees and emerging technologies.
- Quick Start often conducts project studies with detailed analysis of materials, technology and corporate culture for its clients.
- Georgia Quick Start provides customized, company-specific workforce training at no cost to companies investing in and creating jobs in Georgia.
- In 1967, Georgia’s leadership passed legislation to create a first-of-its-kind organization to provide free customized workforce training to attract new industry to the state.
- Quick Start remains the No. 1-ranked program of its type and serves as a model for other states.
- By continually adapting to industry needs, Quick Start is the state’s most effective discretionary incentive, influencing companies of all sizes to create more and better jobs for Georgians.
- Quick Start provides customized workforce training as an incentive for companies investing and creating jobs in Georgia.
- The Quick Start proven process includes collaboration, development, delivery, and confidentiality phases.
- Collaboration: Quick Start meets with company leadership, subject-matter experts, and operations teams to define the scope of training needs.
- Development: Quick Start maps out unique training plans for each company, emphasizing visual learning and hands-on training.
- Delivery: "Flexible and responsive" have been Quick Start’s watchwords for decades; training is delivered whenever and wherever it makes sense for each company project.
- Confidentiality: Quick Start upholds the highest standards of confidentiality to protect a company’s intellectual property.
- Quick Start has been ranked the #1 customized workforce training program in the United States for 15 consecutive years (2010-2024).
- The program offers customized workforce training to qualified companies as a discretionary incentive to support investment and job creation in Georgia.
- Area Development’s survey is considered the gold standard for assessing state appeal among site location consultants.
- Georgia Quick Start has been pivotal in attracting companies that require tech-savvy workers with specific skills, bolstering the state’s competitive labor market.
- States that excel in workforce training have invested heavily in customized programs meeting specific industry needs, according to Area Development editors.
