# Lithium Americas Corp.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [Department of Energy Restructures Lithium Americas Deal to Protect Taxpayers and Onshore Critical Minerals | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/articles/department-energy-restructures-lithium-americas-deal-protect-taxpayers-and-onshore)  ·  `page_004537`

## Facts

- U.S. Department of Energy Secretary Chris Wright announced that the Loan Programs Office (LPO) has restructured its deal with Lithium Americas Corp. (LAC).
- The deal restructuring includes support from General Motors (GM).
- The announcement was made on September 30, 2025.
- The new terms provide the U.S. Government with 5% equity ownership in the form of LAC warrants.
- The new terms also provide an additional 5% ownership in the form of warrants in the LAC/GM joint venture.
- LPO utilizes warrants as part of the overall collateral package on a loan to help reduce repayment risk for taxpayers.
- DOE worked with both parties to ensure greater loan resilience.
- The revised agreement includes robust loan amendments.
- The revised agreement includes more than $100 million of new equity.
- The U.S. Government’s stake in the project delivers on President Trump’s agenda to better steward taxpayer dollars.
- The government stake ensures the project will go forward, spurring the creation of manufacturing jobs.
- The government stake ensures the project secures the nation's access to the largest confirmed lithium deposit in North America.
- U.S. Energy Secretary Chris Wright stated that despite having some of the largest deposits, the United States produces less than 1% percent of the global supply of lithium.
- U.S. Energy Secretary Chris Wright stated that thanks to President Trump’s bold leadership, American lithium production is going to skyrocket.
- U.S. Energy Secretary Chris Wright stated that today’s announcement helps reduce dependence on foreign adversaries for critical minerals by strengthening domestic supply chains.
- U.S. Energy Secretary Chris Wright stated that the announcement ensures better stewardship of American taxpayer dollars and that President Trump promised to do both and is delivering.
- LPO and LAC entered into a financial loan agreement in October 2024.
- The new terms of the deal will help finance the construction of facilities for manufacturing lithium carbonate at Thacker Pass.
- Once fully operational, the facility is expected to produce approximately 40,000 tonnes per year of battery-grade lithium carbonate.
- The produced lithium carbonate will be used in lithium-ion batteries.
- The project helps ensure the Trump Administration’s priority of onshoring U.S. supply chains will be realized.
- LPO utilizes a variety of measures to protect taxpayers, including warrants which are one of the terms borrowers negotiate as an option to exercise.
- This warrant practice has been used in LPO loans, including to Tesla in 2010.
- DOE is conducting a thorough review of every applicant and borrower to ensure every single taxpayer dollar is being used to advance the best interest of the American people.
