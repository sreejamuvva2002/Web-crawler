# Revalyu Resources

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [German Plastics Recycler Begins Building Facility in Statesboro, Ga. : CEG](https://www.constructionequipmentguide.com/german-plastics-recycler-begins-building-more-than-200m-facility-in-statesboro-ga/62822)  ·  `page_019894`

## Facts

- Revalyu Resources announced in December 2022 that it would locate a plant in Statesboro, Ga.
- Revalyu Resources broke ground on Oct. 4 at the 43-acre site in the city's Gateway Regional Industrial Park.
- The original planned investment of $50 million was increased to more than $200 million for the Statesboro plant.
- The plant will be capable of recycling 200 million pounds of polyethylene terephthalate (PET) plastic each year.
- Revalyu's Statesboro plant will recycle 100 percent post-consumer material at a rate of about 25 million bottles each day.
- Construction of the facility's first phase is expected to be completed in the third quarter of 2025.
- The first phase will employ 71 people, with another 50 employees added in Phase 2.
- Jan van Kisfeld, managing director of Revalyu, stated that this is the company's first plant in the U.S. and a very important step for expansion.
- Revalyu produces 100 percent recycled pellets that are equivalent in quality to conventional oil-based PET pellets.
- The advanced recycling plant will serve existing and future U.S. customers directly from the U.S., enabling quicker transportation time, lower cost, and a smaller carbon footprint.
- Revalyu has been supplying chemically recycled PET chips or pellets to manufacturers in the United States for more than six years.
- The company's choice of the Statesboro/Bulloch County site followed three months of intensive search and serious negotiations with the Development Authority of Bulloch County (DABC).
- Benjy Thompson, CEO of the DABC, noted that Revalyu is the first German-owned company to locate in Bulloch County.
- Revalyu was founded in 2007 by Vivek Tandon and was originally called perPETual.
- Vivek Tandon attended the Statesboro groundbreaking.
- The company's existing processing plant is in India and is being expanded to recycle 240 metric tons of PET a day.
- The new southeast Georgia plant will be able to process 100 metric tons of PET daily.
- Revalyu's revolutionary process allows used PET plastic to be efficiently, profitably, and easily recycled again and again without degradation of quality.
- The proposed Revalyu facility is one of five Bulloch County industrial plant site selections announced by the DABC in the past 20 months.
- Together, the five companies have promised to eventually create more than 1,500 jobs at their respective sites.
- Massachusetts-based Aspen Aerogels announced a $325 million plant to make aerogel insulation used in and around electric vehicle batteries.
- Aspen Aerogels is constructing its 90-acre plant on a site in Bruce Yawn Commerce Park.
- Ecoplastic America Corp., a supplier of injection-molded plastic automotive body parts to Hyundai Motor Group, intends to build a $205 million plant.
- Ecoplastic America promises to create 456 jobs phased in over eight years at its facility south of Statesboro on a site owned by the DABC.
- Site preparation for the Ecoplastic America factory is already under way.
- Ajin USA, a supplier of metal auto body parts to Hyundai, committed to invest $317 million and eventually employ 630 people at its Joon Georgia plant.
- The Ajin USA site encompasses 83 acres next door to the Aspen Aerogels site in Bruce Yawn Commerce Park.
- Hanon Systems will build a $40 million manufacturing plant for producing automotive air control equipment, including air conditioners, heaters, cooling modules, compressors, radiators, fans, evaporators, and condensers.
- Hanon Systems will be a tier-one supplier to the Hyundai Metaplant that is building EV cars northwest of Savannah.
- The Hanon Systems facility will be constructed in Gateway Industrial Park and create 160 jobs.
