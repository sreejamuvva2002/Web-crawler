## Facts

- SK Battery America operates a plant in Jackson County near Commerce, Georgia.
- Many storefronts in downtown Commerce were vacant when SKBA broke ground on its factory.
- Since the factory began commercial operations, a Korean restaurant opened followed by other businesses gradually filling empty spaces.
- A city official stated that SKBA has acted as a catalyst for revitalizing downtown Commerce.
- Misti Martin, deputy commissioner of global commerce at the Georgia Department of Economic Development, said SKBA is one of the largest employers in northeast Georgia.
- Many local residents who used to commute long distances for work can now have good jobs close to home.
- Taylor Young from SKBA’s human resources department noted the company offers relatively high wages compared to other local employers.
- Many high school graduates choose to work at SKBA.
- Engineer Christopher Chastain reported his income rose significantly after switching jobs and built real engineering expertise over four years despite starting with no experience.
- Chastain’s wife joined the quality control department three months ago, making multiple family members work at the same facility.
- Paula Byars has worked in SKBA’s HR department for nearly three years after learning about the job through her cousin’s spouse in the electrode division.
- SKBA has helped improve income levels for many people in the community.
