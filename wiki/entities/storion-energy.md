# Storion Energy

**Type:** Joint Venture  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[battery-materials|Battery Materials]]  ·  **Location:** Alpharetta, Georgia, Georgia, United States

## Overview

Storion Energy is a joint venture created to domestically produce price-competitive vanadium electrolyte for utility-scale vanadium redox flow batteries (VRFB) used in long-duration energy storage, removing barriers for battery manufacturers.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Provides domestically sourced vanadium electrolyte-a key battery material-for flow batteries that enable long-duration storage supporting electric-vehicle grid integration and renewable energy utilization.

## Key facts

- Storion will leverage a proprietary continuous manufacturing process and patented purification to produce high-quality vanadium electrolyte domestically.
- The JV aims to offer a leasing model for vanadium electrolyte, helping VRFBs compete with upfront capital costs of lithium-ion BESS.
- DOE’s goal is to deliver reliable energy at $0.05/kWh by 2030; Storion’s domestic supply supports this target.
- VRFB technology provides safe, reliable long-duration storage (>4 hours) and can last more than 20 years without electrolyte degradation.
- The vanadium electrolyte is infinitely recyclable and offers near-limitless cycle life, complementing wind and solar installations.
- Scalable VRFB systems can reduce costs as discharge time increases, making them a preferred option for large-scale energy storage.
- Storion’s domestic supply chain integrates the only operating Western Hemisphere vanadium mine with U.S. electrolyte production.
