# YKK (U.S.A.) Inc. Field Technology Center

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [YKK USA's Field Technology Center takes YKK's environmental pledge to heart - YKK Americas](https://ykkamericas.com/ykk-usas-field-technology-center-takes-ykks-environmental-pledge-heart/)  ·  `page_016043`

## Facts

- In 1994, YKK’s former chairman and CEO Tadahiro Yoshida established the YKK Group Environmental Pledge, which states that “The YKK Group proclaims that it will address and promote ‘harmony with the environment’ as the highest priority of its business activities.”
- In 2009, YKK established an Environmental Compliance Committee within its North and Central America region to bring together managers to discuss environmental regulatory requirements and best practices.
- The Environmental Compliance Committee is made up of environmental managers from each of YKK’s manufacturing locations in the U.S., Canada, and Latin America.
- The committee meets quarterly to continuously look for ways to ensure employees at different sites are aware of applicable environmental regulatory requirements for compliance and environmental protection.
- Each company within YKK’s North and Central America region has programs in place at their facilities to help ensure they are following applicable environmental laws.
- Many of these regional facility programs include recycling, the elimination of hazardous wastes, and other sustainable manufacturing practices.
- The YKK (U.S.A.) Inc. Field Technology Center (FTC) is located in Marietta, Georgia.
- The FTC recycles its machines, scrap metal, spare parts, paper, and cardboard.
- The FTC continues to replace outdated fluorescent lighting with more energy-efficient lighting.
- The FTC eliminates waste solvents and paints from its machinery repainting process by having the work done outside of the facility rather than inside.
- The FTC tracks its water and power consumption and separates wastewater from irrigation water.
- The FTC continually promotes energy-saving and recycling programs among its employees.
