# Anovion Technologies

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Anovion Technologies Announces Plans for $800 Million Initial Investment in New Manufacturing Facility in Southwest Georgia](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20230515005298/en/Anovion-Technologies-Announces-Plans-for-800-Million-Initial-Investment-in-New-Manufacturing-Facility-in-Southwest-Georgia)  ·  `page_000214`

## Facts

- Anovion Technologies announced plans for an $800 million initial investment in a new manufacturing facility in Southwest Georgia.
- The Decatur County facility will produce 40,000 metric tonnes per annum of synthetic graphite anode material for lithium-ion batteries.
- The facility will create hundreds of clean energy jobs.
- Anovion selected Decatur County in Southwest Georgia as the location for its first large-scale expansion of manufacturing capacity for premium synthetic graphite anode materials.
- The initial facility will be 1,500,000 square feet.
- Anovion chose the location based on proximity to existing and planned low- and carbon-free energy sources, short supply chains, access to existing rail infrastructure, a highly valuable skilled workforce, and a business-friendly environment.
- Eric Stopka is the Chief Executive Officer of Anovion.
- Anovion conducted a thorough search to identify a location addressing energy, transportation, logistics, and human capital needs.
- The proximity to other battery and EV manufacturing plants will allow Anovion to further reduce its carbon footprint.
- Governor Brian Kemp welcomed Anovion to Georgia, calling it the No. 1 state for business and fast becoming the e-mobility capital of the nation.
- In October 2022, Anovion was awarded a grant totaling $117 million from the U.S. Department of Energy under the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law.
- The DOE grant aims to help secure the domestic battery manufacturing supply chain for the country’s electrified future.
- This new expansion facility will be the first of this scale for Anovion and in North America.
- The facility is a crucial first step to reshoring synthetic graphite anode production.
- Once fully operational, the facility is expected to initially produce 40,000 metric tonnes per annum.
- Bainbridge Mayor Edward Reynolds welcomed Anovion to Bainbridge.
- Anovion's decision will create over 400 high-paying, quality jobs.
- Anovion will use a sustainable and technologically advanced manufacturing process at the new facility.
- The facility will rely on the region’s rapidly decarbonizing energy mix to power manufacturing.
- Anovion will introduce proprietary furnace technologies that substantially reduce greenhouse gas emissions.
- The company has laid out a clear roadmap to achieve a carbon-neutral footprint over time.
- Chip Dunn is the Executive Chairman of Anovion.
- Anovion's long-term strategic vision focuses on growth and innovation, positioning itself as a leader in the U.S. energy transition.
- The goal includes securing a domestic source for critical materials required for U.S. lithium-ion battery production.
- Anovion will continue research and development in electrochemistry and technology improvements in its production process.
- In addition to Georgia, Anovion is expanding its Anovion Center of Excellence.
- Anovion is also expanding a hydro-powered sister manufacturing facility near Niagara Falls, NY.
- The Niagara Falls facility is one of the only qualified U.S. sources of battery-grade synthetic graphite that is commercially shipping product today.
- Anovion Technologies is headquartered in Chicago, Illinois.
- Anovion is a U.S.-owned and U.S.-based advanced materials business.
- Anovion is currently one of America’s first commercial-scale manufacturers of premium, cleaner anode-grade synthetic graphite.
- Anovion's existing footprint includes facilities near Niagara Falls, NY and Clarksburg, WV.
- As a leader in lithium-ion battery materials, Anovion began commercial production in early 2021.
- Anovion is among a limited number of graphite anode producers in America to have successfully gained qualification for EV applications.
- In 2022, Anovion was awarded the $117 million DOE grant to rapidly scale production capacity to meet growing demand.
- The grant supports construction of a large-scale factory in the southeastern U.S. and investment in its relocated Anovion Center of Excellence (ACE) in Sanborn, NY.
- Anovion is commencing a multi-year expansion program of 150,000 metric tonnes of annual capacity for anode-grade synthetic graphite.
