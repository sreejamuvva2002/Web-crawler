# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) is a multi-billion-dollar electric vehicle and battery manufacturing complex located in Bryan County.
- The HMGMA facility is approximately 20 miles from downtown Savannah and roughly equidistant from Richmond Hill.
- The project represents a multi-billion-dollar capital investment by Hyundai Motor Group.
- Associated supplier facilities and component manufacturers are locating within the broader Joint Development Authority footprint, which spans Bryan, Bulloch, Chatham, and Effingham Counties.
- The development is generating thousands of jobs, hundreds of supplier and contractor positions, and a sustained relocation wave that is reshaping the rental economy across Richmond Hill and Pooler.
- Direct Hyundai employees and contractors are relocating from Hyundai's existing U.S. and international operations to the area.
- Relocating direct Hyundai employees and contractors typically require 60-to-180-day furnished housing while they buy or build permanent homes in the area.
- Supplier-company personnel, including engineers, plant management, and skilled trades from dozens of supplier and component manufacturers, are making 30-to-90-day project assignments.
- Construction and ramp-up contractors working on the ongoing facility build-out, supplier facility construction, and broader infrastructure projects are typically on 30- to 60-day assignments tied to specific project phases.
- Temporary management and consulting personnel embedded in operations during ramp-up phases are typically staying for 14-to-45 days, often midweek-only with weekend departures home.
- Pre-relocation house-hunters and exploratory visitors are flying in for one-to-two-week stays to scout neighborhoods, schools, and properties before relocating.
- The pre-relocation house-hunter segment is growing as the workforce build-out continues.
- The Metaplant-related demand is structurally less seasonal and less weekend-concentrated than leisure travel.
- Metaplant-related workers are willing to pay rates that compete with or exceed leisure-traveler pricing benchmarks for the same property.
- A two-bedroom Richmond Hill townhome grossing $35,000 per year on leisure-vacation bookings can gross $40,000 to $55,000 per year on a properly-positioned extended-stay mix tied to the Metaplant.
- The HMGMA development is described as the single largest economic event in coastal Georgia in decades.
- The Bryan County Development Authority and the Savannah Joint Development Authority publish updates on Metaplant-related employment, supplier location announcements, and broader economic development.
- Track these sources for shifts in workforce size, supplier ramp-up, and project-phase changes that affect demand patterns.
- The Bryan County Chamber of Commerce and the Richmond Hill Chamber serve as additional sources for local business and relocation context.
