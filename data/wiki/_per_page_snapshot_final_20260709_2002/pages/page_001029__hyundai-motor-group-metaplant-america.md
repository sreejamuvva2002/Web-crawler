# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-05-31

**Source:** [Hyundai just built a $7.6 billion EV factory in Georgia to compete with Tesla and GM — see inside - AOL](https://www.aol.com/hyundai-just-built-7-6-100201587.html)  ·  `page_001029`

## Facts

- The Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America is an all-new $7.6 billion EV factory.
- HMGMA is located near Savannah, Georgia, and opened its doors in March of this year.
- The factory will be able to build 500,000 EVs and hybrids for Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis every year.
- The plant is one of the newest and most technologically advanced car factories in the world.
- It serves as a key production facility for Hyundai's EVs and PHEVs, as well as those belonging to its Genesis luxury brand and sister company Kia.
- In a recent interview with Business Insider, Genesis North America COO Tedros Mengiste cited the investment as an example of Hyundai's track record for "visionary and strategic, and long-term thinking."
- The Hyundai Metaplant is situated on a 3,000-acre campus in the south Georgia town of Ellabell.
- The plant is located just 20 miles from the Port of Savannah, one of the busiest ports in the US.
- The location gives Hyundai much-needed US manufacturing capacity to avoid import tariffs and provides flexibility to export vehicles abroad.
- The facility gives Hyundai the production footprint to compete against rivals like Tesla, GM, and Rivian, which is also building a new factory in Georgia.
- It is Hyundai Group's second car factory in Georgia.
- Hyundai Group also operates a $3.2 billion, 2,200-acre facility in West Point, Georgia, that builds Kia EV and ICE SUVs.
- The 2026 Hyundai Ioniq 9 EV SUV is one of the vehicles assembled at the Metaplant.
- The Hyundai Ioniq 5 EV is the only other model currently assembled at the plant.
- Hyundai broke ground on the facility in the fall of 2022.
- Construction on the main production buildings took just two years to complete.
- The Metaplant site consists of 11 buildings totaling 7.5 million square feet of space.
- The Metaplant aims for vertical integration by manufacturing as many key components as possible on-site, ranging from battery packs to seats.
- There are 21 emission-free Hyundai XCIENT hydrogen fuel cell semi trucks deployed around the Metaplant site to transport parts and supplies.
- The production process begins in a stamping shop where sheet metal is cut and stamped into parts for the car frame.
- Sheet metal is supplied by an on-site Hyundai Steel facility.
- Stamped parts are transported by automated guided vehicles (AGVs).
- The plant employs almost 300 AGVs to shuttle items ranging from spare parts to partially assembled cars.
- The Metaplant was originally expected to produce up to 300,000 electrified vehicles annually.
- At the plant's grand opening in March, Hyundai announced that capacity will be expanded to 500,000 units in the coming years as part of a new $21 billion investment in US manufacturing.
- The plant is expected to start production of its first Kia model next year.
- In the welding shop, stamped metal pieces are welded together by robots to form the vehicle body.
- The work done by welding robots is inspected by human employees known as Meta Pros.
- The Metplant employs more than 1,300 Meta Pros, nearly 90% of whom were hired locally.
- Vehicles are inspected by a pair of Boston Dynamics robot dogs called Spot.
- In 2021, Hyundai acquired an 80% stake in Boston Dynamics in a deal valuing the company at $1.1 billion.
- In the assembly building, painted bodies are combined with battery packs and skateboard chassis.
- Hyundai Mobis produces the skateboard chassis in a building next door to the general assembly facility.
- The Metaplant's on-site battery factory, operated in a joint venture with LG, is expected to come online next year.
- The plant currently sources its batteries from Hyundai's other facilities, including one in North Georgia that is a joint venture with SK.
- Assembled vehicles are loaded onto different AGVs that navigate the facility by reading QR codes embedded into the floor.
- Completed EVs are tested at an on-site test track before being sent to the vehicle preparation center (VPC) to be readied for shipping.
- Vehicles destined for regional dealerships are put on trucks, while those traveling more than 500 miles are shipped by rail at the Metaplant's on-site train terminal.
