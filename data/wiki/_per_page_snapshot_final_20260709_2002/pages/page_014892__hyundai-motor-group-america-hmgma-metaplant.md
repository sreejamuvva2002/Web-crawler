# Hyundai Motor Group America (HMGMA) Metaplant

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-06-19

**Source:** [Hyundai's Metaplant First Production Vehicle to Be 2025 IONIQ 5](https://thekoreancarblog.com/2024/06/19/hyundais-metaplant-first-production-vehicle-to-be-2025-ioniq-5/)  ·  `page_014892`

## Facts

- Hyundai’s Metaplant first production vehicle will be the 2025 IONIQ 5.
- HMGMA is actively training and preparing its workforce to begin production in the fourth quarter of this year (2024).
- The new facility will manufacture all trim lines of the IONIQ 5, with the exception of the IONIQ 5 N.
- The Metaplant will eventually become the sole production site for the IONIQ 5 model in the U.S.
- Oscar Kwon, president and CEO of HMGMA, stated that the company is in the final phase of construction and getting ready for the 2025 Hyundai IONIQ 5 to roll off the line.
- The IONIQ 5 was named Best Electric SUV.
- The IONIQ 5 was named Best EV for the Money.
- The IONIQ 5 was named One of the Best Cars for Families by U.S. News & World Report.
- The IONIQ 5 was named Best Electric Vehicle for the second consecutive year by Cars.com.
- The IONIQ 5 previously won the 2022 World Car of the Year.
- The IONIQ 5 previously won the 2023 MotorTrend SUV of the Year.
- The IONIQ 5 previously won the 2022 Car and Driver EV of the Year.
- The IONIQ 5 earned the highest TOP SAFETY PICK+ award from the Insurance Institute For Highway Safety (IIHS).
- The Hyundai IONIQ 5 remains Hyundai’s highest volume EV.
- The Hyundai IONIQ 5 saw a 43% increase in sales in 2024.
- The Hyundai IONIQ 5 saw an 82% surge in sales in May alone.
- Buyers of the 2024 IONIQ 5 are eligible for a $7,500 cash rebate as part of June incentives.
- Customers who lease the 2024 IONIQ 5 can benefit from a $7,500 federal tax credit.
- Hyundai Motor Group is making a substantial investment of $7.59 billion to develop HMGMA.
- The development of HMGMA will create 8,500 direct jobs.
- In partnership with SK On, Hyundai is constructing a $5 billion EV battery cell plant in Bartow County, Georgia.
- The SK On and Hyundai EV battery cell plant in Bartow County, Georgia, is expected to employ 3,500 workers.
- A study by the Center for Automotive Research estimates that Hyundai’s investments in Georgia will generate nearly 40,000 jobs and $4.6 billion in individual earnings annually.
- Additional vehicles from Hyundai, Genesis, and Kia are planned for future production at the Metaplant.
