# Georgia Ports Authority

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Expanding Georgia’s Gateway - The George-Anne Media Group](https://thegeorgeanne.com/50719/inkwell/expanding-georgias-gateway/)  ·  `page_017918`

## Facts

- The Port of Savannah handled a record 5.4 million container units last year.
- A multibillion-dollar expansion is underway at the Port of Savannah's Ocean Terminal to transform it into a dedicated container facility, improve gate and crane capabilities, and establish larger ship berths.
- The Georgia Ports Authority (GPA) is accommodating this growth through infrastructure upgrades, harbor dredging, and a new container terminal on Hutchinson Island.
- The GPA views its inland ports as a way to extend port services inland to help support industry and economic development in Georgia.
- The $127 million Blue Ridge Connector inland port is 75% complete and expected to open in spring 2026.
- The Blue Ridge Connector is funded entirely through GPA revenues rather than taxpayer dollars.
- The Blue Ridge Connector is located near Interstate 85 about 50 miles northeast of Atlanta.
- The terminal will serve a region of more than two million residents and a growing concentration of forest products industries, food processing, and manufacturing.
- The location was chosen to better serve existing Georgia forest customers and manufacturers who currently have to send drivers all the way to the coast to pick up cargo.
- With the new terminal, companies will be able to move hundreds of boxes by train instead of one container per truck, saving cost, time, and emissions.
- Once operational, daily trains will bridge Gainesville and Savannah, delivering containers to the Blue Ridge site within 48 hours.
- The terminal is anticipated to handle 26,000 containers in its first year.
- The GPA invested $4.8 million in Hall County road improvements, with upgrades completed in summer 2025 to secure safer and more efficient local access.
- No major expansions are planned beyond the initial footprint of the Blue Ridge Connector, but capacity could grow over time as demand increases.
- The terminal will start with seven rubber-tire gantry cranes, with plans to add more as demand increases.
- The Blue Ridge Connector will join the GPA’s inland port network alongside the Appalachian Regional Port and the Carolina Connector.
- The terminal will strengthen Georgia’s position as a manufacturing and export powerhouse, serving companies like Williams Sonoma, Kubota, and SK Battery already operating in the area.
- These industries move key commodities such as furniture, heavy equipment, and poultry.
- The Blue Ridge Connector aims to make Georgia’s businesses more globally competitive by giving them more affordable and faster access to international markets.
- The GPA is aiming to strengthen regional supply chains, lower costs for businesses, and guarantee that the prosperity generated at Georgia’s coast reaches industries across the state.
