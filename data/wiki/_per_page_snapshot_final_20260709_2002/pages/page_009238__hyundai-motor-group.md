# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-05

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Group To Build New EV And Battery Plant In Georgia Creating 8,100 Jobs | Carscoops](https://www.carscoops.com/2022/05/hyundai-motor-group-to-build-new-ev-and-battery-plant-in-georgia/)  ·  `page_009238`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group announced plans to invest approximately $5.54 billion (£4.43 B / €5.25 B) into the state of Georgia.
- The investment will be used to construct dedicated electric vehicle and battery manufacturing facilities on a 2,923 acre site in Bryan County.
- The site is located less than 31 miles (50 km) from the Port of Savannah.
- Hyundai noted the Port of Savannah is the “single-largest and fastest-growing container terminal in the U.S.”
- Construction on the plant is slated to begin early next year.
- The plant should begin producing vehicles in the first half of 2025.
- The plant will have an annual production capacity of 300,000 units.
- The facility will build a “wide range of full electric vehicles for U.S. customers.”
- Hyundai declined to say which specific vehicles will be produced at the new facility.
- Previous reports suggested the facility could be tasked with making the Hyundai Ioniq 7 and Kia EV9.
- Both models are large crossovers that will appeal to American consumers.
- The vehicles are slated to ride on the E-GMP platform.
- The vehicles will offer three rows of seating as well as ranges of at least 300 miles (483 km).
- The battery plant will be established through a strategic partnership with details being released at a later date.
- The project is expected to create approximately 8,100 new jobs.
- The project is designed to support Hyundai Motor Group’s goal of “becoming a leader in electric mobility in the U.S.”
- Hyundai Motor Group wants to become one of the top three EV providers in America by 2026.
- Hyundai Motor Group Executive Chair Euisun Chung stated the company is incredibly proud to share its plan to open its first dedicated full EV and battery manufacturing facilities in the U.S.
- Euisun Chung added that the U.S. has always held an important place in the Group’s global strategy, and expressed excitement to partner with the State of Georgia to achieve shared goals of electrified mobility and sustainability in the U.S.
