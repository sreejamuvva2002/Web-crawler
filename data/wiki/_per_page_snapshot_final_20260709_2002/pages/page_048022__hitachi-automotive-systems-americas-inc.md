# Hitachi Automotive Systems Americas, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2019-05-17

**Source:** [Hitachi Automotive Systems America expanding in Monroe, GA - Location Georgia](https://www.locationgeorgia.com/2019/05/17/hitachi-automotive-systems-america-expanding-in-monroe-ga/)  ·  `page_048022`

## Facts

- On May 17, 2019, Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Hitachi Automotive Systems Americas, Inc. plans to create 100 jobs and invest at least $100 million, with potential up to $330 million, in an expansion at its existing facility in Monroe, Georgia.
- Governor Kemp stated that Hitachi has been a great partner to the Walton County community by investing in employees and creating jobs for more than twenty years.
- Governor Kemp expressed confidence that the investment will positively impact the local community and encourage continued success in Georgia’s strong automotive industry.
- Hitachi Automotive Systems Americas, Inc. is a group company of Hitachi Automotive Systems, Ltd., which is headquartered in Tokyo.
- The company manufactures and markets engine powertrain systems, electric powertrain systems, and integrated vehicle control systems for major automotive original equipment manufacturers (OEM) worldwide.
- Hitachi Automotive Systems Americas, Inc. President and CEO Paul Carroll stated the company has been operating in Georgia for over twenty-two years.
- The Georgia factory supplies advanced technology products for major automotive OEMs including Ford Motor Company, General Motors, Nissan, Subaru, and Honda.
- The company cited its partnership with the State of Georgia, Walton County, and the City of Monroe as a reason for continuing to invest in the state.
- Hitachi Automotive Systems Americas, Inc. opened its first facility in Georgia in 1997.
- The company currently employs 900 Georgians at its existing facility.
- The expansion is intended to increase production capacity in response to the growth of the automotive industry.
- Walton County Commission Chairman Kevin Little noted that Hitachi has been a great corporate partner and has become the county's largest employer.
- Mayor John Howard of Monroe stated that Hitachi continues to invest in high technology and top-quality jobs, with community support described as second to none.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) Sr. Project Manager Tammy Caudell represented the Global Commerce Division alongside the Development Authority of Walton County, Walton EMC, and the Georgia Department of Community Affairs.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that companies like Hitachi, which create jobs and take an active role in the community, elevate the state's economic development efforts.
- Commissioner Wilson noted that companies like Hitachi Automotive Systems have driven significant success and interest from Japanese companies in Georgia.
- Hitachi Automotive Systems, Ltd. is a wholly owned subsidiary of Hitachi, Ltd., headquartered in Tokyo, Japan.
- The parent company is engaged in the development, manufacture, sales, and services of automotive components, transportation related components, industrial machines and systems.
