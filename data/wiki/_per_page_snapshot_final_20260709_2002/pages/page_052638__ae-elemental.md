# AE Elemental™

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2024-04-09

**Source:** [Ascend Elements | Ascend Elements and Elemental Strategic Metals Establish AE Elemental, an Electric Vehicle Battery Recycling JV in Poland](https://ascendelements.com/ascend-elements-and-elemental-strategic-metals-establish-ae-elemental-an-electric-vehicle-battery-recycling-jv-in-poland/)  ·  `page_052638`

## Facts

- Joint venture AE Elemental will own and operate an advanced Li-ion EV battery recycling facility in Zawiercie, Poland.
- Another AE Elemental facility is planned for central Germany.
- Elemental Strategic Metals of Zawiercie, Poland, and Ascend Elements of Westborough, Mass., US, announced the formation of the JV on April 9, 2024.
- The JV partners will jointly own (50/50) and operate the newly constructed facility in Zawiercie, Poland.
- The Zawiercie facility will disassemble, discharge, and shred EV batteries to produce black mass for new engineered EV battery materials, including cathode active material (CAM) and cathode precursor (pCAM).
- The Zawiercie facility has a capacity to recycle up to 12,000 metric tons of batteries per year, or approximately 28,000 EV batteries annually.
- JV partners agreed to jointly invest in large-scale lithium extraction from black mass.
- Lithium extraction capabilities processing up to 20 000 metric tons of black mass per year will begin construction in Fall 2024.
- The lithium extraction facility is scheduled to be operational in 2026.
- Companies plan to begin building a new EV battery recycling facility in central Germany with a capacity to recycle up to 25,000 metric tons of batteries per year, or approximately 58,000 EVs annually.
- Pawel Jarski, CEO of Elemental Holding, stated the partnership is a transformative step towards becoming a key player in the battery materials industry.
- Jarski noted that combining Ascend Elements’ cutting-edge technology with their recycling expertise, large collection network, recycling platform, and global footprint puts AE Elemental in a pole position in this evolving market.
- Mike O’Kronley, CEO of Ascend Elements, stated the JV represents their first commercial-scale battery recycling facility in Europe.
- O’Kronley noted expanding into Europe will allow them to better service customers locally and help the industry comply with new EU rules requiring recycled material in new batteries.
- Elemental Strategic Metals is a member of Elemental Group and operates a large, state-of-the-art multi-metal extraction facility in Poland focused on recovery of battery metals and platinum group metals.
- Elemental Group is a global leader in urban mining, extracting strategic metals from spent automotive catalysts (SACs), waste electrical and electronic equipment, printed circuit boards (PCBs), used Li-Ion batteries, and non-ferrous scrap metal.
- Elemental recovers strategic metals such as platinum group metals, copper, and other base and precious metals in an environmentally friendly way.
- Elemental currently has subsidiaries in 35 countries across Europe, Asia, and North America.
- Ascend Elements is based in Westborough, Mass., and is a leading provider of sustainable, closed-loop battery material solutions.
- Ascend Elements uses Hydro-to-Cathode® direct precursor synthesis technology to produce new pCAM and CAM from spent lithium-ion cells more efficiently than traditional methods.
- This technology results in improved economics and lowered GHG emissions for the lithium-ion battery industry.
