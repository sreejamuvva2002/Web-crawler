# SK Innovation Georgia Battery Plant

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2021-04-10

**Source:** [Senator Reverend Warnock Statement Regarding Georgia Battery Factory » Reverend Raphael Warnock](https://www.warnock.senate.gov/newsroom/press-releases/senator-reverend-warnock-statement-regarding-georgia-battery-factory/)  ·  `page_029374`

## Facts

- U.S. Senator Reverend Raphael Warnock (D-GA), a member of the Senate Banking and Commerce committees, issued a statement after media reports surfaced that a settlement has been reached in a legal dispute threatening the viability of the SK Innovation electric battery plant in Commerce, GA.
- Senator Warnock stated his sharp focus has been on protecting jobs with good wages that were committed to Georgians.
- Warnock spoke repeatedly to senior leaders at the companies involved and numerous officials in the Biden Administration about the value of the Commerce factory to the local economy and the critical effort in Georgia and across the country to reduce pollution and strengthen environmental sustainability.
- The reached settlement ensures that jobs will stay in Georgia, prompting Warnock to express gladness that all parties listened and that the community of Commerce received the decision they hoped for to help keep the local economy moving forward.
- Senator Warnock has been a vocal champion for the Commerce factory and advocated strongly for the companies involved to reach a settlement to prevent additional litigation and continued uncertainty for the plant.
- Since being sworn in, Senator Warnock and his office have maintained frequent contact with the Biden Administration, senior leaders at SK Innovation and LG Chem, and local residents and business community leaders in Commerce and around Georgia.
- These contacts aimed to encourage amicable negotiation to ensure the jobs committed to Commerce would come to fruition.
- Senator Warnock pressed Biden Administration officials on the importance of keeping the Commerce plant online to help advance the nation’s long-term environmental sustainability goals.
- Prior reporting noted that Democratic Sen. Raphael Warnock said an ITC decision threatened seriously the future of the project in Commerce and President Biden’s own goals.
- Warnock’s office was actively working on the SK Battery issue, with his office stating a settlement would be the best outcome.
