# Georgia Department of Economic Development

## Facts

- The Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) serves as the state's lead agency for business recruitment and expansion.
- Georgia's primary cash incentives include the REBA (Regional Economic Business Assistance) grant for closing assistance on major projects.
- OneGeorgia Authority grants fund infrastructure projects supporting business expansion or location in rural Georgia counties.
- The Job Tax Credit and Quality Jobs Tax Credit are statutory rather than discretionary.
- Georgia operates one of the most active state film-and-television production grant/credit ecosystems.
- REBA is a discretionary grant fund used for site preparation, infrastructure, and other closing assistance on major economic-development projects.
- REBA grants are negotiated through GDEcD project managers and are not broadly available as a small-business grant.
- OneGeorgia Authority grant applications must be submitted by local governments, with businesses benefiting through site readiness at qualifying locations.
- The state primarily relies on federal small-business certifications (SBA 8(a), HUBZone, WOSB/EDWOSB, SDVOSB/VOSB) for set-aside eligibility.
- State agency procurement may apply federal certifications where federal funds pass through, but no state-administered formal small-business set-aside program operates beyond the standard MBE/WBE/DBE registries.
- Georgia does not currently operate a statewide SBIR/STTR Phase 0 or Phase I match program.
- Eligible small businesses apply directly to federal SBIR/STTR through participating federal agencies (DoD, NIH, NSF, DOE, NASA, etc.).
- Georgia state-administered grant programs typically use program-specific application portals on individual agency sites rather than a single statewide grants portal.
- Federal pass-through funds (HUD CDBG, EDA, USDA Rural Development) route through Grants.gov for the federal half, then through the state sub-recipient process.
- HUD CDBG-State dollars are administered by GDEcD and sub-granted to localities for economic development.
- EDA public-works and economic-adjustment grants flow through regional EDA offices.
- USDA Rural Development provides Business and Industry loan guarantees and Rural Business Development Grants for rural-county operations.
- SBA programs (7(a), 504, Microloan, CDFI) are accessed via local lenders.
- Business eligibility for federal grant channels depends on entity size, location (rural vs urban), and use-of-funds.
- The Community Development Block Grant (CDBG) program flows from HUD to GDEcD, which sub-grants to localities.
- Businesses do not apply directly to the state for CDBG dollars; they apply to participating cities or counties for economic development sub-awards focused on job creation, blight remediation, and low-to-moderate-income workforce support.
- Most flagship Georgia grant and incentive programs require either an existing operation, a defined hiring commitment, or capital investment milestones.
- Pure pre-revenue startups should usually pair federal SBIR/STTR (where R&D-eligible) with state innovation match programs (if available) and CDFI lending.
- Some Georgia regional or university-based innovation programs may provide application support for federal grants.
- Most state-administered programs require pre-registration with a state vendor identification number before an application can be submitted.
