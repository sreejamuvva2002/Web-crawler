# Hyundai Georgia EV Plant

**Type:** Facility  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[vehicle-manufacturing|Vehicle Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** Bryan County, Georgia, Bryan County, Georgia, United States

## Overview

A $7.6 billion electric-vehicle manufacturing facility that Hyundai is building in Bryan County, Georgia. The plant will produce US-made Hyundai electric vehicles beginning with the updated 2025 IONIQ 5 and later the three-row IONIQ 9.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Primary manufacturing hub for US-made Hyundai electric vehicles, anchoring a growing Georgia EV supply chain and attracting multiple downstream suppliers.

## Investment

- **$11.2 million** (2024) — Construction of Shinsung Petrochemical manufacturing facility in Toombs County
  - Investment announced by Governor Brian Kemp; facility will be the first in Toombs County and supply Hyundai.
- **$7.6 billion** (2024) — Construction of Hyundai Georgia EV Plant
  - Funding for a 8,500-job vehicle assembly plant slated to open as early as October 2024.

## Jobs

- **8,500 direct jobs at Hyundai plant; >30 jobs at Shinsung facility; 3,500 jobs at SK On battery plant** — 2024-2025
  - Areas: vehicle assembly, auto sealant manufacturing, battery production
  - Center for Automotive Research estimates the Hyundai plant will ultimately generate more than 58,200 new jobs and attract $12.6 billion in additional investments.

## Key facts

- Hyundai’s $7.6 billion EV plant will produce US-made electric vehicles, beginning with the updated 2025 IONIQ 5 and later the three-row IONIQ 9.
- The plant is expected to create 8,500 direct jobs and attract over $12.6 billion in additional investments according to a Center for Automotive Research study.
- Shinsung Petrochemical becomes the 18th supplier to Hyundai’s Georgia EV plant, investing $11.2 million in its first Toombs County facility.
- The Shinsung facility will be the first manufacturing operation in Toombs County and create more than 30 jobs.
- SK On is building a $5 billion battery plant in Bartow County that will add roughly 3,500 positions to Georgia’s EV supply chain.
- Since 2018, Georgia has secured over $28 billion in EV-related investments and generated more than 36,000 new jobs.
- Hyundai expects its U.S.-made EVs to qualify for the full $7,500 federal tax credit once the on-site battery unit becomes operational.
- The 2025 IONIQ 5 will feature a Tesla NACS charging port and an XRT off-road variant with exclusive bumpers and side skirts.
- Hyundai and Kia together accounted for 10% of U.S. EV sales in Q2 2024, surpassing Ford (7.4%) and GM (6.3%).
