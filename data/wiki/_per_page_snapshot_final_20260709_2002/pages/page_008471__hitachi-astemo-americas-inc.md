# Hitachi Astemo Americas Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-07-11

**Source:** [Hitachi Astemo Americas to Expand in Berea with $153M Investment - Expansion Solutions](https://www.expansionsolutionsmagazine.com/hitachi-astemo-americas-to-expand-in-berea-with-153m-investment/)  ·  `page_008471`

## Facts

- Hitachi Astemo Americas Inc. is one of Kentucky’s largest Tier 1 automotive suppliers.
- The company announced an expansion of its operation in Madison County with a $153 million investment.
- The investment will create 167 new, quality Kentucky jobs.
- Gov. Beshear stated that unprecedented economic momentum in Kentucky is driven by growth from large industries, particularly manufacturing and the automotive sector.
- The rapidly growing EV sector is bringing quality jobs and investments to Kentucky communities.
- The project involves investing in the current manufacturing operation in Berea to support site improvements and renovations.
- Renovations will include increased production lines and additional equipment.
- The facility expansion will add 752,000 square feet on 62 acres.
- The expansion responds to increased market demand and growth within the EV industry.
- Hitachi’s investment will create 167 full-time jobs for Kentuckians.
- The company’s total Kentucky employment across its Harrodsburg and Berea facilities will exceed 2,100 workers.
- Tim Clark is president of Hitachi Astemo Americas.
- The company is approaching 40 years of manufacturing in Kentucky.
- The announcement represents a step toward vehicle electrification and aligns with the company name standing for Advanced Sustainable Technology for Mobility.
- In January 2021, Hitachi Automotive Systems, Keihin, Showa, and Nissin Kogyo completed a business merger to form the global mega-supplier Hitachi Astemo.
- The company provides advanced mobility solutions including electric powertrains for automobiles, advanced chassis, autonomous driving/advanced driver assistance systems, and motorcycle systems.
- Hitachi Astemo is headquartered in Tokyo, Japan.
- The company’s entry into the Americas began in 1972 with a Hitachi subsidiary established in Brazil.
- In the mid-1980s, Hitachi expanded into the U.S. market with its first factory in Kentucky.
- The company serves as a global mega-supplier to some of the largest automobile manufacturers in the world.
- Hitachi Astemo has more than 20 manufacturing sites in the United States, Canada, Mexico, and Brazil, including two locations in Kentucky.
- Kentucky’s administration-to-date has seen nearly 600 private-sector new-location or expansion projects commit to invest over $22.9 billion and create more than 30,000 jobs.
- Madison County Judge/Executive Reagan Taylor expressed pleasure at the expansion, noting it speaks well for the existing facility and community strength.
- Berea Mayor Bruce Fraley noted the decision highlights the talent of the local workforce and the positive economic climate of Berea and Madison County.
- Hitachi Astemo Americas is the city of Berea’s largest employer.
- Donna Angel, business development director for the Business and Economic Development Office of Berea, highlighted that the investment will bring quality jobs and economic sustainability to the community.
- Hitachi Astemo is at the forefront of developing the newest technology in parts for automotive and other EV engines.
- The new line of services will enhance the regional automotive industry and highlight Berea’s community strengths while offering important, quality jobs to residents.
