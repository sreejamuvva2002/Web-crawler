# SK Innovation EV Battery Plant in Commerce, Georgia

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2021-03-12

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp Advocates for Georgia Jobs, Asks Biden to Overturn Trade Commission Ruling | Governor Brian P. Kemp Office of the Governor](https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases/2021-03-12/gov-kemp-advocates-georgia-jobs-asks-biden-overturn-trade-commission-1)  ·  `page_000097`

## Facts

- On March 12, 2021, Governor Brian Kemp sent a letter to President Biden repeating his request to overturn an International Trade Commission (ITC) ruling against SK Innovation.
- The ITC ruling prevents SK Innovation from importing battery parts and components for ten years.
- The ITC ruling includes a limited exception allowing the company to produce batteries at the Commerce, Georgia plant for Ford for four years and for Volkswagen for two years.
- SK Innovation told the state of Georgia that the ITC ruling will make the Commerce plant economically unviable.
- Absent presidential action to disapprove the ITC ruling, SK Innovation will be forced to shutter the Commerce facility.
- The Commerce, Georgia EV battery plant will employ nearly 2,600 people upon completion.
- The project represents the largest foreign investment in Georgia's history at nearly $2.6 billion.
- When completed, the factory will account for nearly half of the nation's vitally needed non-captive EV batteries, available on the free market for EV manufacturers.
- The plant's initial yearly output will supply 22 GWh of EV battery capacity.
- The initial capacity is enough for 330,000 electric cars.
- SK Innovation has plans to expand the facility to employ more than 6,000 workers and produce 50 GWh annually by 2025.
- The factory is owned by the Korean company SK Innovation ("SKI").
- The Commerce plant will be the only major EV battery plant in the nation built without federal subsidies.
- President Biden's administration is conducting an important review regarding the future of the EV battery plant in Commerce, Georgia.
- Governor Kemp stated the Commerce plant aligns with President Biden's publicly announced goal of electrifying the U.S. auto fleet with good, high-paying jobs for local workers.
- President Biden's recently announced Executive Order on supply chains recognized the critical role of EV batteries to the economy and national security.
- China is currently the leading producer of EV batteries.
- Closing the Commerce, Georgia plant will result in the United States falling further behind China in the global EV battery race.
- Governor Kemp requested that President Biden exercise his legal authority to disapprove the ITC ruling on grounds that it is contrary to the public interest and will seriously jeopardize the administration's environmental and economic goals.
- President Obama took similar action in 2013 when he concluded that an ITC decision threatened the public interest and would harm U.S. consumers.
- Governor Kemp emphasized that the livelihoods of thousands of Georgians are at stake due to the ruling.
