# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-02-26

**Source:** [Hyundai Announces New Production Start Date at Metaplant America During Hyundai Day in Georgia](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/hyundai-announces-new-production-start-date-at-metaplant-america-during-hyundai-day-in-georgia-302071754.html)  ·  `page_000184`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) has accelerated its official start of production to the fourth quarter of 2024.
- The grand opening celebration for HMGMA is targeted for the first quarter of 2025, moving up from a previously announced first quarter of 2025 date.
- Georgia Governor Brian P. Kemp, First Lady Marty A. Kemp, and Hyundai executives celebrated Hyundai Motor Group's positive impact on the state, officially marking February 26, 2024, as Hyundai Day.
- The Georgia General Assembly passed a Resolution commending Hyundai Motor Group's dedication to the state and recognizing February 26, 2024, as Hyundai Day at the State Capitol.
- Hyundai and Genesis electric vehicles on display included the IONIQ 5, IONIQ 6, GV60, and Electrified GV70.
- A hydrogen-powered XCIENT Fuel Cell Truck was displayed for use in logistics between Hyundai's manufacturing facilities and suppliers.
- José Muñoz, president and global COO of Hyundai Motor Company and president and CEO of Hyundai and Genesis Motor North America, stated that Hyundai's investments in Georgia will create tens of thousands of great-paying American jobs and bring tens of billions of dollars in economic impact for decades to come.
- Governor Brian Kemp noted that Hyundai helps recruit businesses, gives back to communities, and works with world-class colleges and universities to connect Georgians with opportunities.
- Oscar Kwon, president and CEO of HMGMA, called the project the largest economic development project in Georgia's history and credited the team's rapid progress to the Meta Pros and support from state and local partners including the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Joint Development Authority.
- Attendees at the celebration included Lieutenant Governor Burt Jones, House Speaker Jon Burns, legislative leaders, Department of Economic Development Director Pat Wilson, and officials from the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority.
- Hyundai Motor Group is investing $7.59 billion to develop HMGMA, which will create 8,500 direct jobs.
- Hyundai and SK On are building a $5 billion EV battery cell plant in Bartow County, Georgia, that will employ 3,500 workers.
- A Center for Automotive Research study projects Hyundai's investments in Georgia will create nearly 40,000 jobs and generate $4.6 billion in individual earnings annually.
- HMGMA continues hiring efforts and has developed educational partnerships with area colleges to create an employment pipeline.
- The Electric Vehicle Professional Technical Certificate (EVP TCC) is being offered at four area technical colleges as part of this collaboration.
- Students who successfully complete the EVP TCC program receive preferred status when applying for positions at HMGMA.
- Hyundai is actively developing a hydrogen mobility value chain at HMGMA, as announced at CES 2024.
- Through the Clean Logistics Project in Georgia, the Metaplant will utilize XCIENT Fuel Cell tractors to manage logistics for the plant and its suppliers.
- The XCIENT is Hyundai's commercialized class 8 6x4 fuel cell electric model with a proven record of real-world application and over 6.25 million accumulated miles.
- Hyundai is developing hydrogen refueling infrastructure needed to support this fuel cell activity, which is anticipated to stimulate economic growth and create job opportunities in the state.
- HMGMA is Hyundai Motor Group's first dedicated electric vehicle mass-production plant and is located in Bryan County, Georgia.
- The facility will feature a highly connected, automated, and flexible manufacturing system that organically connects all elements of the EV ecosystem to realize customer value.
- The Georgia facility will become an intelligent manufacturing plant where order collection, procurement, logistics, and production are optimized using AI and data.
- The innovative manufacturing system is designed to create a human-centered work environment with robots assisting human workers.
- Hyundai Motor America supports Hyundai Motor Company's Progress for Humanity vision and operates significant U.S. facilities including its North American headquarters in California, the Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Alabama assembly plant, HMGMA in development in Georgia, and several R&D facilities.
- Combined with operations from 835 independent dealers, Hyundai's U.S. operations contribute $20.1 billion annually and 190,000 jobs to the U.S. economy according to a recent economic impact report.
