# Superior Essex Communications

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2019-05-06

**Source:** [Superior Essex Communications on Leadership Panel at CRU - Superior Essex – Corporate](https://www.superioressex.it/news/superior-essex-communications-on-leadership-panel-at-cru/)  ·  `page_010541`

## Facts

- Superior Essex Communications President Brad Johnson will participate in a leadership panel on optical cables at the CRU Wire & Cable Conference.
- The conference agenda includes a special focus on the use of wires and cables in the automotive industry, with insights into trends specific to power, communication, construction, and automotive sectors impacting business operations.
- Natalie Noor-Drugan, Senior Analyst and Editor-in-Chief at CRU, noted that wire and cable companies must provide increasingly high-tech products and services to meet government goals for electric vehicles (EVs) and connected vehicles, as well as the clean energy transition, smart grids, smart cities, 5G, IoT, and factory automation.
- Companies in the sector must increasingly demonstrate sustainable supply chains, low environmental impact products, and compliance with recent regulations to succeed.
- Superior Essex is a leading manufacturer and supplier of communications cables and accessory products serving the communications industry and related distribution markets for over 80 years.
- Superior Essex Communications operates as a trading name for Superior Essex International LP and other companies within Superior Essex Inc.
- The company's corporate address is located at 5770 Powers Ferry Rd. NW Suite 400, Atlanta, GA 30327.
- Brad Johnson stated that joining the leadership panel offers a significant opportunity to highlight how Superior Essex Communications leads in next-generation wire/wireless, digital infrastructure, sustainability, and technology innovation to help shape the future wiring landscape.
- The CRU Wire & Cable Conference will be held on June 11-12, 2019, at Steigenberger Wiltcher's in Brussels, Belgium, exploring EU construction, power, and communication projects and wire/cable quality standards.
