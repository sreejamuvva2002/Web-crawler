# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-01-06

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Group plans to deploy humanoid robots at U.S. factory from 2028](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/01/06/hyundai-motor-group-plans-to-deploy-humanoid-robots-at-us-factory-from-2028.html)  ·  `page_021335`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group plans to deploy humanoid robots at its U.S. manufacturing plant in Georgia starting in 2028.
- The deployment marks a step toward automating higher-risk and repetitive manufacturing tasks.
- Hyundai unveiled the production version of the Atlas humanoid robot, developed by its unit Boston Dynamics, at the Consumer Electronics Show in Las Vegas.
- The company did not disclose the volume or cost of the robots.
- Hyundai aims to roll out adoption across all of its manufacturing sites as part of a push into "physical AI".
- The robots will initially carry out parts sequencing tasks from 2028.
- Applications for the robots will expand gradually as safety and quality benefits are validated.
- By 2030, Atlas robots were expected to move into component assembly.
- A longer-term plan exists for Atlas robots to take on tasks involving heavy loads, repetitive motions, and complex operations across production sites.
- The robots are designed to reduce physical strain on workers by taking on higher-risk and repetitive tasks.
- This deployment lays the groundwork for broader commercial use in industrial settings.
- At Hyundai Motor's affiliate Kia Corp, the labor union last year called to establish a body to address potential labour rights issues in preparation for the AI era.
- Workers at Kia raised concerns about expanding automation.
- Hyundai expects humanoid robots to become the largest segment of the physical artificial intelligence market.
- Physical AI refers to AI systems embedded in hardware that collect real-world data and make autonomous decisions, spanning areas such as robotics, smart factories, and autonomous driving.
- Atlas features human-scale hands with tactile sensing.
- Atlas has the ability to lift up to 50 kg (110 pounds).
- The robot can operate autonomously.
- Atlas is designed to function in industrial environments ranging from minus 20 degrees Celsius to 40 degrees Celsius.
- Hyundai is accelerating development through partnerships with global AI leaders, including a collaboration with Nvidia and Google.
- The collaboration with Nvidia and Google aims at improving safety, efficiency, and real-world deployment.
