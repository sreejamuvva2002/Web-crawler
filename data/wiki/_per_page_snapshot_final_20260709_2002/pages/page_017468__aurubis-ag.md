# Aurubis AG

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Aurubis to open state-of-the-art recycling, copper smelting facility in Georgia](https://www.recyclingmonster.com/article/aurubis-to-open-stateoftheart-recycling-copper-smelting-facility-in-georgia/1410)  ·  `page_017468`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Aurubis will invest $340 million (€300 million) in a state-of-the-art recycling and secondary smelting facility for multi-metal recycling in Augusta.
- Aurubis is one of the leading recyclers of copper, precious metals, and non-ferrous materials worldwide.
- This new facility will be the very first of its kind in the U.S.
- The project will create 125 jobs in the Augusta area.
- Governor Kemp stated this marks the single-largest investment made by a German company in the State of Georgia.
- Aurubis will join more than 500 German companies with operations in Georgia.
- Aurubis’ commitment to sustainable development and raw material security will play a role in building Georgia’s electric mobility value chain.
- From Augusta, the company plans to produce materials found in lithium-ion batteries and other electronics.
- Roland Harings is the CEO of Aurubis AG.
- The company aims to become a forerunner for multi-metal recycling in the U.S., mirroring its existing role in Europe.
- Aurubis will build its facility on 150 acres at Augusta Corporate Park.
- Augusta Corporate Park is a “Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) Certified” site.
- Operations are expected to begin by the first half of 2024.
- The company plans to begin hiring for positions as soon as summer 2022.
- Steven Kendrick is Chairman of the Development Authority of Augusta.
- The Augusta Economic Development Authority and City of Augusta welcome Aurubis as their newest corporate tenant.
- Griff Lynch is the Executive Director of the Georgia Ports Authority (GPA).
- Exports produced by the Aurubis recycling operation will strengthen the state’s global position via Savannah, which is already one of the nation’s most balanced ports in terms of import-export trade.
- Fernanda Kirchner is Senior Project Manager representing the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce division on this project.
- The project involves partnerships with the Augusta Economic Development Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, and Georgia Power.
- Sérgio Domingues is Managing Director for the State of Georgia’s Europe Office and played a supporting role in attracting Aurubis to Georgia.
- Pat Wilson is GDEcD Commissioner.
- The facility will be located in Richmond County.
- Augusta Corporate Park has completed due diligence and other specific certification requirements prior to visits by prospective businesses as part of its GRAD certification.
- Thousands of Georgia jobs have been created on GRAD sites since the program’s inception in 2008.
