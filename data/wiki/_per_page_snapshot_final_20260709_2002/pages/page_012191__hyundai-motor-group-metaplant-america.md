# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [The Prospect of an EV Future: An Inside Look at Hyundai’s Metaplant America - AUTOMOTIVE RHYTHMS](https://www.automotiverhythms.com/the-prospect-of-an-ev-future-an-inside-look-at-hyundais-metaplant-america/)  ·  `page_012191`

## Facts

- HMGMA is located in Bryan County, GA, roughly 25 miles west of the Georgian Coastal town of Savannah.
- The facility sits directly off Interstate 16 with fast access to I-95 and the Port of Savannah.
- The campus spans 3,000 acres and encompasses 11 buildings and 7.5 million square feet.
- One of the main parking lots features over 1,800 solar panels generating up to 5% of the plant’s daily energy needs.
- An ecological park for employee recreation is in the works at the facility.
- Everyone at HMGMA, from the CEO to the assembly line, carries a single unified title: Metapro.
- The term "Meta" means transformative and shapes the company's hiring, culture, and community impact.
- Hyundai committed $12.6 billion to build this facility, representing the single-largest economic development investment in Georgia’s history.
- The investment will create 8,500 onsite jobs and 7,000 offsite supplier jobs.
- Full production at HMGMA launched in October 2024.
- The initial 300,000-vehicle annual goal was quickly raised to 500,000 EVs annually.
- At the October 2025 Investor Day, Hyundai announced phase two of expansion.
- Phase two includes an additional $2.7 billion investment and will create 3,000 more direct and indirect jobs.
- The average salary at HMGMA is around $58,000 per year, excluding benefits.
- This average salary is approximately 25% higher than the average wage in Bryan County, translating to a local annual payroll of half a billion dollars.
- HMGMA currently produces two vehicles: the IONIQ 5 and IONIQ 9.
- The 2026 IONIQ 5 delivers up to 318 miles of range.
- The IONIQ 5 can recharge 10–80% at a DC Fast Charging station in roughly 24 minutes via its 800V architecture.
- Power output for the IONIQ 5 ranges from 225-horsepower for a RWD to 320-horsepower for the AWD.
- The XRT variant of the IONIQ 5 features rugged styling and raised ride height.
- Hyundai added the IONIQ 9 to the lineup in March 2025.
- The IONIQ 9 extends range up to 335 miles with ultra-fast 800V charging.
- Power for the IONIQ 9 runs from RWD to a dual-motor AWD delivering up to 422-horsepower.
- The IONIQ 9 cabin features a fully flat floor, a sliding Universal Island 2.0 console, available relaxation seats, Bose audio, and optional premium H-Tex materials.
- Hyundai’s human-centric manufacturing vision was first witnessed at the Hyundai Motor Group Innovation Center in Singapore (HMGICS), a seven-story smart mobility lab where roughly 50% of tasks are handled by robots and AI alongside a skilled human workforce.
- Every element of production at HMGMA, from order collection, procurement, logistics, and assembly, is optimized in real time through AI and data systems.
- Hyundai is developing the IONNA charging network, a joint venture with other major automakers that has 107 live charging sites across North America.
- There are 46 more IONNA charging sites under construction and 500+ sites contracted.
- A partnership with Circle K promises to bring fast charging to one of the most ubiquitous convenience store footprints in the country.
- Hyundai is developing Home energy solutions and in-car payment integration, alongside growing IONNA–NACS compatibility for broad network access.
- Hyundai Motor Group climbed from 5th to 3rd in U.S. EV sales rankings between 2021 and 2025, trailing only Tesla and GM.
- Electrified vehicles accounted for 33.8% of all Hyundai sales as of Q1 2026, up from just 19.4% in 2023.
- Euisun Chung serves as Executive Chair of Hyundai Motor Group.
