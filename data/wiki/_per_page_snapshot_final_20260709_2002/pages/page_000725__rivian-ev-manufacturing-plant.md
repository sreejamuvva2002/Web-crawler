# Rivian EV Manufacturing Plant

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-01-03

**Source:** [Clayco nabs $5B Rivian EV factory project in Georgia | Construction Dive](https://www.constructiondive.com/news/clayco-rivian-ev-factory-project-georgia/703317/)  ·  `page_000725`

## Facts

- American automaker Rivian selected Chicago-based construction firm Clayco to build its $5 billion EV manufacturing plant in Stanton Springs, Georgia.
- The 1,800-acre complex will sit about 40 minutes from Atlanta.
- The plant will produce about 400,000 vehicles per year when fully complete.
- Clayco expects the plant to begin vehicle production in 2026.
- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp announced the $5 billion campus in December 2021.
- The announcement was cited as a sign of success in Georgia's push to recruit the entire electric mobility ecosystem to the state.
- Other EV plant projects in Georgia include LG Energy Solution and Hyundai Motor Group’s $4.3 billion battery cell plant in Savannah, Georgia.
- Auto parts manufacturer Sewon America is building a $300 million facility in Rincon, Georgia.
- The state initiative Georgia Quick Start will establish and operate a manufacturing training center to meet workforce needs.
- The training center will offer Rivian a tailored training program aligned with the company’s startup requirements.
- The Georgia Department of Labor will collaborate with Rivian in identifying and recruiting skilled workers.
- The project marks Clayco’s continued involvement in EV manufacturing construction.
- In August, Clayco kicked off phase one of a $4 billion VinFast EV manufacturing campus in North Carolina.
- The VinFast campus spans 3 million square feet and about 1,800 acres.
- The VinFast plant consists of five main production areas: a body shop, general assembly, press shop, paint shop, and an energy center.
- Clayco expects the VinFast factory to start production in 2025.
- The VinFast factory will have a production capacity of 150,000 vehicles per year.
