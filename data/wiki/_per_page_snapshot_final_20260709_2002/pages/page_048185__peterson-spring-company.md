# Peterson Spring Company

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2015-12-10

**Source:** [PAC Racing Springs, A Division of Peterson Spring Acquires Schroeder Torsion Bar Manufacturing - Peterson Spring](https://www.pspring.com/pac-racing-springs-a-division-of-peterson-spring-acquires-schroeder-torsion-bar-manufacturing/)  ·  `page_048185`

## Facts

- PAC Racing Springs, a division of Peterson Spring based in Detroit, Michigan, announced the acquisition of Schroeder Torsion Bar Manufacturing.
- Schroeder Torsion Bar Manufacturing has over 50 years of heritage in industry-leading manufacturing and technology for torsion bar and sway bar manufacturing.
- Schroeder Torsion Bar Manufacturing was founded by Gordon Schroeder in Burbank, California, with deep roots in circle track racing history.
- The acquisition combines a cumulative 150 years of experience in high-performance spring products.
- PAC Racing Springs utilizes a total system approach coupling expertise in engine valve train component design, materials, and processing to expand into torsion bar product manufacturing.
- Jason Youd, PAC Racing Springs’ Manager of Business Development, stated the company is proud to bring the quality of Schroeder Product lines and Manufacturing into their company.
- Gary Schroeder expressed excitement for PAC Racing Springs to carry on the tradition of Schroeder Torsion Bar Manufacturing and technology.
- PAC Racing Springs is the aftermarket and racing manufacturer division of the Peterson Spring Company, headquartered in Southfield, Michigan.
- Peterson Spring manufactures springs, wire forms, spring stampings, and coiled retaining rings for automotive, industrial, recreational, appliance, agriculture, and lawn/garden markets.
- Peterson Spring has 12 locations across the USA, Canada, Mexico, and the United Kingdom.
- PAC Racing Springs specializes in engine valve springs, suspension springs, and recently expanded into sway bar and chassis suspension systems.
- PAC Racing Springs draws on over 100 years of manufacturing experience from the Peterson Spring Group of companies to improve materials and high-performance processing.
