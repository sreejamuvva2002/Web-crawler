# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-04-22

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor to power US EV plant with renewable energy - The Korea Herald](https://www.koreaherald.com/view.php?ud=20240422050575)  ·  `page_009541`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group inked a 147-megawatt power purchase agreement with Matrix Renewables, a Spanish renewable energy company, to power its electric vehicle plant currently under construction in Georgia.
- The deal marks the largest single PPA signed by a Korean company in the US.
- Four Hyundai Motor Group companies joined hands for the contract: Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America, Hyundai Mobis North America electric, Hyundai Steel Georgia, and Hyundai Transys Georgia Powertrain.
- Through this deal, HMGMA has set up a renewable energy system for not only its auto plant but also its supply chains.
- The deal signifies Hyundai Motor Group’s pan-conglomerate step to secure renewable energy.
- The purchasing cost for the renewable energy was saved as the conglomerate’s overseas offices pushed for a joint purchase agreement instead of pursuing a deal individually.
- The North American offices of Hyundai Mobis, Hyundai Steel and Hyundai Transys will utilize the secured solar power to supply automation parts, EV steel sheets and seats to HMGMA.
- As this PPA delivers power from a new solar power plant instead of signing a contract with existing renewable energy plants, it will have better impacts on reducing global carbon emissions.
- The solar power supply is expected to decrease carbon emissions by 140,000 tons per year.
- This emission reduction is equal to absorbing the carbon emissions produced by about 84,000 mid-sized sedans annually.
- Matrix Renewables is constructing a 210-megawatt solar power plant named Stillhouse Solar in Bell County, Texas with the goal to start operating in October 2025.
- Hyundai Motor secured about 70 percent of the generation capacity for the project.
- Hyundai Motor will obtain about 378 gigawatt-hours per year from the agreement.
- The secured power is enough to power approximately 100,000 households for a year.
- A Hyundai Motor Group official stated they will conduct investments and deals to turn Hyundai Motor Group’s US footholds into using renewable energy.
- The official added that they will continue to push for cooperation between the conglomerate’s companies to secure renewable energy efficiently.
- Hyundai Motor’s EV-dedicated plant in Georgia’s Bryan County is expected to begin operations in October this year.
- The Georgia plant's start date is three months ahead of its initial planned timeline.
