# U.S. International Trade Commission (ITC) Antidumping and Countervailing Duty Petition

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2025-12-04

**Source:** [American manufacturers trigger truck "dumping" investigation - SaintGeorge](https://www.sgibinc.com/en/us-manufacturers-start-truck-dumping-investigation/)  ·  `page_013210`

## Facts

- The U.S. truck market is at the center of a trade dispute over alleged dumping by foreign trailer manufacturers from Canada, China, and Mexico.
- The U.S. International Trade Commission (ITC) opened an investigation to determine whether imported trailers are sold below fair value and receive government subsidies.
- The American Trailer Manufacturers Coalition filed an antidumping and countervailing duty petition regarding the unfair practices.
- The coalition is made up of domestic companies including Great Dane, Stoughton, and Wabash.
- The coalition argues that trailer imports from Canada, China, and Mexico have increased significantly, displacing domestic production and causing declines in activity, employment, and profitability among U.S. manufacturers.
- Hyundai Translead has expanded its presence in Mexico since 2016, importing an average of nearly 57,000 trailers per year between 2017 and 2024, which is double the volume recorded between 2010 and 2015.
- Vanguard National Trailer Corporation's parent company, China International Marine Containers (CIMC), increased imports of subassemblies from China.
- CIMC opened a plant in Canada in 2020 after U.S. tariffs were imposed in 2018.
- Canadian imports from the CIMC plant have risen by 147% between 2021 and 2022 since operations began in 2021.
- The coalition claims that low-priced imports created an oversupply in 2022 and 2023, causing new trailer orders to drop sharply in 2024.
- The coalition warns that persistent oversupply will keep demand weak and allow imports to continue pressuring domestic manufacturers through unfair pricing.
- Estimated dumping margins in the petition are nearly 300% for Canada, 1,400% for China, and more than 430% for Mexico.
- The ITC is expected to issue a preliminary ruling on January 5.
- The Department of Commerce will release its own ruling around mid-2026.
- The final determination is expected by the end of next year, though tariffs could be imposed at any point during the process.
- The American Trailer Manufacturers Coalition secured a victory in May when it requested that truck trailers be included among steel and aluminum derivative products subject to Section 232 tariffs.
- The request was approved in August, applying a 25% tariff on the value of the steel and aluminum contained in the trailers.
- Washington's trade barriers are disrupting land freight flows, raising the cost of heavy equipment, and reshaping traditional route maps for U.S. truckers.
