# Georgia Department of Economic Development

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-15

**Source:** [Economic Development Investments Break New Record | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-releases/2025/economic-development-investments-break-new-record)  ·  `page_002309`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp and the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) announced steady growth for business recruitment and expansions in Fiscal Year 2025.
- Between July 1, 2024, and June 30, 2025, GDEcD’s Global Commerce team supported 423 facility expansions and new locations.
- These commitments represent more than $26.3 billion in investment, a new record for the state.
- The projects will create 23,200 new private-sector jobs over the next few years.
- Expansions at existing Georgia companies accounted for 74% of the FY25 projects.
- These expansions build on an estimated 50,000 jobs already employing Georgians.
- Lt. Governor Burt Jones stated that the state continues to invest in workforce programs to ensure a steady talent pipeline with existing industry partners.
- Workforce training partners include the University System of Georgia, the Technical College System of Georgia, and College Career Academies.
- 77% of all expansions and new locations in FY25 occurred in communities outside the 10-county Atlanta region.
- Metro Atlanta remains one of the Southeast’s top business hubs.
- Companies like TriNet, A.I.G., CRH, Duracell, and Mercedes-Benz established or expanded key corporate functions in the 10-county Atlanta region.
- Georgia attracted more than 6,500 new jobs from international companies expanding or locating in the U.S.
- The Republic of Korea, Japan, and Canada were the top sources of international investments into Georgia.
- International companies expanding or locating in Georgia committed to more than $3 billion in investment.
- 64% of international investments are slated for expansions within the state.
- Notable international investments include Irving Tissue’s expansion in Macon, GreenBox Systems’ new location in Butts County, and KISS USA’s expansion in Bryan County.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson noted that Georgia has achieved sustained, steady growth in job creation over the last decade.
- Georgia remains the No. 1 state for business according to Area Development magazine, a title claimed for over a decade.
- Georgia is investing over $18 million to help rural communities prepare new industrial sites through the Rural Site Development Initiative.
- These grants support site improvements and add new locations to the “Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development” (GRAD) Program.
- The GRAD Program now includes more than 70 certified sites.
- Speaker Jon Burns highlighted Georgia’s ports at Savannah and Brunswick, extensive rail network, vast energy infrastructure, and world-class roads, bridges, and highways as key business enablers.
