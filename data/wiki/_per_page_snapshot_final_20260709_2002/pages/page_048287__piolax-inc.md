# PIOLAX, Inc.

## Facts

- In March 1937, started business with Ford Japan Ltd., delivering prototype parts through Ford Japan to Ford Motor Company in the U.S., passing strict specification tests to be supplied as genuine Ford parts.
- In October 1969, established a technical tie-up with FT Fasteners Inc. (present TRW Inc.) and manufactured plastic fasteners for the first time as a spring manufacturer.
- In June 1972, established the Overseas Department after receiving inquiries from overseas companies.
- In October 1972, concluded an annual export contract with GE Company and Cumins Engine Company in the U.S.
- In October 1973, established K.P. Products Corporation as a joint venture with Peterson American Corporation, a major U.S. spring manufacturer.
- In November 1986, established KHK of America Inc. in Illinois, U.S., later moving to Michigan in August 1988 to launch strategies for yen appreciation.
- In May 1988, established Piolax Corporation in Canton, Georgia, U.S., starting production of metal springs for home electric appliances and automobiles.
- In November 1991, completed construction of a second plant in the U.S. and started manufacturing plastic fasteners for automobiles in Canton, Georgia, shifting from export to local production.
- In October 1998, started business with Toyota at the newly established Fuji Plant production base in Shizuoka.
- In December 2000, reinforced U.S. bases by merging KHK of America Inc. and Piolax Corporation, consolidating operations in Michigan and Georgia.
- In August 2001, obtained QS9000 international quality certification, allowing PIOLAX to meet the requirements of the U.S. Big 3 automakers.
