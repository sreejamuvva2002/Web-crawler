## Facts

- Vista Metals announced it will invest approximately $60 million in a new facility in Bowling Green, Warren County, Kentucky.
- The California-based company plans to focus on commercial aviation products that serve the aerospace extrusion, forging and rolling markets.
- The new 75,000-square-foot plant will also be able to support customers in the defense, automotive and general industries.
- Sitework is already underway at the project location.
- The project location will likely encompass 100 acres.
- Construction will follow a phased timeline.
- Kentucky Gov. Andy Beshear's office confirmed the sitework and construction timeline details.
- The Kentucky Economic Development Finance Authority (KEDFA) preliminarily approved a 15-year incentive agreement with Vista Metals as part of the Kentucky Business Investment program.
- The performance-based agreement means Vista Metals will not receive any money until it hits capital investment and job targets.
- The agreement will pay the company up to $2.5 million in tax incentives.
- KEDFA also approved Vista Metals for up to $500,000 in tax incentives through the Kentucky Enterprise Initiative Act (KEIA).
- KEIA allows qualified businesses to recover Kentucky sales and use taxes on construction costs and building fixtures, as well as on equipment used for research, development and electronic processing.
- Vista Metals will be able to take advantage of no-cost recruitment services via Kentucky's workforce service providers.
- Vista Metals will receive job placement services through Kentucky's workforce service providers.
- Vista Metals will access reduced-cost customized training via Kentucky's workforce service providers.
- Vista Metals will receive job-training incentives via Kentucky's workforce service providers.
- "We are excited to announce our new location in Kentucky, with a plan to bring over 50 new jobs to the area," said Andy Primack, CEO of Vista Metals.
- "Bowling Green is a growing city with a strong industrial business base," Primack said. "Critical to our success is their proven track record of attracting qualified manufacturing talent, the close interaction between business and education and their long-term community development plan."
- Envision AESC, a Japanese company, has a $2 billion electric vehicle battery plant located in Bowling Green.
- The Envision AESC plant is still under construction.
- Envision AESC broke ground on its project in April 2022.
- Envision AESC topped out the structure of its project in September of last year.
- The Envision AESC project spans three million square feet.
- The Envision AESC plant is expected to supply enough battery power for 300,000 vehicles a year by 2027.
