# U.S. Department of Energy

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** charging_infrastructure  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [DOE Invests $68 Million in Innovative Heavy-Duty Electric Vehicle Charging Solutions | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/cmei/vehicles/articles/doe-invests-68-million-innovative-heavy-duty-electric-vehicle-charging)  ·  `page_003918`

## Facts

- DOE announced a $68 million investment to design, develop, and demonstrate innovative electric vehicle (EV) charging sites near key ports, distribution hubs, and major corridors.
- The projects are selected and funded through DOE’s SuperTruck Charge initiative.
- The initiative aims to accelerate deployment of large-scale public EV charging infrastructure for medium-and heavy-duty (MHD) EVs to improve grid resiliency and reliability.
- DOE’s SuperTruck Program launched in 2009 with four industry participants successfully demonstrating new, efficient technologies for Class 8 tractor trucks.
- SuperTruck 2 followed with five industry participants more than doubling Class-8 tractor truck efficiency while significantly improving engine brake thermal efficiency.
- SuperTruck 3 projects, scheduled to be completed in 2027, will reduce MHD truck greenhouse gas and air pollution emissions by 75% (on a lifecycle basis) and reduce the total cost of truck ownership.
- Jeff Marootian, principal deputy assistant secretary for the Office of Energy Efficiency and Renewable Energy, stated that the SuperTruck program has significantly advanced energy-efficient technologies for freight trucks and the new projects will demonstrate replicable models for cost-effective, high-power charging for electric trucks.
- Each SuperTruck Charge project targets large-scale, replicable, high-power charging installations to serve MHD electric fleets with tens to hundreds of vehicles.
- The projects will develop and demonstrate high-capacity charging infrastructure to serve MHD electric trucks for long-haul use cases (more than 500 miles per day) along major corridors and rural regions where grid capacity is limited.
- Pearl Street Property Co. (Terawatt Infrastructure) in San Francisco, California received $20 million for the 'Supercharging the Southwest' project to demonstrate innovative grid and load management strategies at a charging site along the I-10 corridor in either Goodyear or Tucson, Arizona.
- The Supercharging the Southwest project provides 10 pull-through truck charging stalls with megawatt charging systems (MCS) compatible chargers, solar canopies, and 3 megawatts (MW) of battery electric storage systems.
- Greenlane Infrastructure, LLC in Santa Monica, California received $26 million for the 'Mega Charging the I-15 Corridor' project to support multiple generations of MHD EV charging on freight corridors.
- Greenlane will deploy a publicly accessible 10+ MW MHD EV charging station at Greenlane Center in Barstow, California, featuring distributed energy resource systems including solar arrays and energy storage.
- The Mega Charging project site will offer combined charging systems and be futureproofed with scalable MCS direct current fast charging MHD EV chargers.
- Utah State University in Logan, Utah received $22 million for the 'SUPERCHARGE' project to establish a reliable, replicable, and scalable charging infrastructure blueprint implementable nationwide.
- The SUPERCHARGE project will demonstrate a facility with 9 MW of max concurrent charging capability and 12 MW of installed EV charging equipment while drawing less than 4.5 MW from the grid.
- SuperTruck Charge is administered by DOE’s Vehicle Technologies Office.
- Connected Communities 2.0 focuses on advancing grid-edge solutions for resilient communities, including demonstrating urban, suburban, and rural-use cases for smart charge management.
- The Joint Office of Energy and Transportation’s Communities Taking Charge Accelerator invests more than $43 million in projects to fill gaps in public charging infrastructure.
- Selection for award negotiations is not a commitment by DOE to issue an award or provide funding before the negotiation process.
- Full funding for these projects is subject to future congressional appropriations.
