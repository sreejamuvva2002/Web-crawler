# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-20

**Source:** [Hyundai doubles down on Georgia plant expansion plans | Columbus Ledger-Enquirer](https://www.ledger-enquirer.com/news/state/georgia/article312177018.html)  ·  `page_009201`

## Facts

- Hyundai has not canceled plans to complete its electric vehicle battery plant in Georgia.
- Hyundai has recommitted to the EV battery plant project and broadened its agreement significantly.
- Hyundai announced a sweeping production expansion and an overall investment of $26 million over three years at its CEO Investor Day.
- CEO José Muñoz outlined Hyundai Motor’s global growth goals while speaking in New York.
- Hyundai announced a $5 billion battery joint venture in Cartersville, Georgia.
- Hyundai announced expansions to the Kia Corp. plant in West Point, Georgia.
- Hyundai announced plans for a steel mill in Donaldsonville, Louisiana.
- Hyundai announced plans for a robotics hub and an expansion of its plant in Montgomery, Alabama.
- Hyundai announced a reduction to its 2025 budget, citing a current tariff rate of 25%.
- Hyundai's new operating profit margin will be approximately 7%, with a goal to return to approximately 9% by 2030.
- The 25% tariff is directly affecting Hyundai’s profitability by raising costs on non-domestically made cars and components, as only about 40% of its U.S. sales are currently made domestically.
- Hyundai announced a plan to increase U.S. car production and plans for rapid growth at its Ellabell Georgia “Metaplant.”
- Hyundai targets producing 500,000 vehicles a year at the Georgia facility, including hybrids and EVs.
- Hyundai plans to introduce a midsize truck for the first time ever.
- Hyundai plans to offer 18 hybrid models globally.
- Hyundai plans to launch extended-range EVs in 2027.
- Hyundai will introduce several U.S.-specific vehicle offerings.
- Hyundai plans to have a 10-model lineup at its Georgia facility.
- In 2025, Hyundai raised its revenue growth target and adjusted its operating profit.
- Hyundai announced an investment of KRW 77.3 trillion ($55.66 billion in USD) by 2030.
- Hyundai plans to roll out a Software-Defined Vehicle (SDV) platform and a High-Performance Vehicle Computer.
- In 2026, the Genesis brand will introduce hybrid models.
- An all-new Palisade Hybrid will launch in 2026.
- A cloud-based battery management system will launch in 2026.
- Hyundai plans to build a plant in Saudi Arabia in 2026.
- In 2027, Hyundai will debut its first Extended Range electric vehicle (EREV).
- In 2028, Hyundai will expand at Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA).
- The 2028 expansion adds 200,000 units of annual capacity.
- The 2028 expansion will create 3,000 new jobs.
- By 2028, Hyundai will reach production of 500,000 hybrid and EV vehicles per year.
- By 2030, Hyundai aims to reach a target of 55.5 million vehicles sold globally.
- By 2030, Hyundai's hybrid choices will expand to 18 or more models.
- In 2030, Hyundai will announce its first midsize pickup truck.
- Following an ICE raid at the Georgia plant, President Trump stated on Truth Social that foreign workers are “welcome” and that the U.S. should take its cues from other high-tech industries.
