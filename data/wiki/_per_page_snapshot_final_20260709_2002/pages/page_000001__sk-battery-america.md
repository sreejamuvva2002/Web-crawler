# SK Battery America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-01-30

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp: SK Battery America Exceeds Hiring Goal, On Track to Reach 3,000 Workers | Governor Brian P. Kemp Office of the Governor](https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases/2023-01-30/gov-kemp-sk-battery-america-exceeds-hiring-goal-track-reach-3000-workers)  ·  `page_000001`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced on January 30, 2023 that SK Battery America (SKBA) has exceeded its hiring goal of 2,600 employees two years ahead of schedule for its two lithium-ion battery manufacturing facilities in Commerce, Georgia.
- The company met its hiring goal at the end of 2022 and is further expanding its workforce to approximately 3,000 over the next year to meet the growing demand for electric vehicles.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated that SK Battery America is one of the first companies to put Georgia on the e-mobility map and thanked them for going above and beyond in delivering on their commitments.
- SK Battery America announced its first project in Georgia in 2018, with an initial planned investment of $1.6 billion.
- Since 2019, the company has invested $2.6 billion to build two battery manufacturing facilities in Jackson County to supply electric vehicles, including the Ford F-150 Lightning and the Volkswagen ID.4.
- SKBA started mass production in early 2022.
- SK Battery America CEO Timothy Jeong credited the State of Georgia’s Quick Start program for helping attract and train workers with a speed and scale that would be difficult for any company to do on its own.
- More than 300,000 new electric vehicles a year will be on American roads because of the work SK Battery America is doing in Georgia.
- SKBA has worked with Georgia Quick Start, the state’s top-ranked job training program through the Technical College System of Georgia, since announcing its first project in 2018.
- In July 2022, SKBA announced they would partner with the Georgia National Guard’s Work for Warriors Georgia program to focus on hiring veterans, servicemembers, retirees, reservists, and their families in the pursuit of their career goals.
- The company has held numerous career fairs, visited schools, and held community events in recent months.
- To attract the highest-quality workforce, SKBA has consistently improved wages, benefits, and its overall work environment.
- Jackson County Board of Commissioners Chairman Tom Crow expressed appreciation for SK Battery America’s commitment to provide high quality jobs within the county and their substantial financial investment.
- The company is still hiring for jobs in production, quality, utility, logistics, ERT, maintenance, and other areas.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson praised SK Battery America for its out-front role in the development of the electric mobility ecosystem in the state and participation in every opportunity presented to them.
- SK On is the parent company of SK Battery America and is a global leading electric vehicle battery developer, manufacturer, and solutions provider.
- The Georgia facilities will have a combined capacity to make 22 GWh of battery cells a year.
- The produced batteries are enough to power the equivalent of about 300,000 EVs annually.
