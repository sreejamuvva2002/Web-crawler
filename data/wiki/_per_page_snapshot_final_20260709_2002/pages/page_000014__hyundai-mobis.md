# Hyundai Mobis

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** power_electronics  ·  **Published:** 2022-11

**Source:** [Second Global Automotive Supplier for Hyundai Metaplant To Create 1,500 Jobs – Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority](https://savannahjda.com/2022/11/second-global-automotive-supplier-for-hyundai-metaplant-to-create-1500-jobs/)  ·  `page_000014`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Hyundai Mobis plans to invest $926 million in an Electric Vehicle (EV) Power Electric system plant in Bryan County.
- The plant will eventually employ at least 1,500 people.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp celebrated the groundbreaking of Hyundai’s new electric vehicle and battery manufacturing facility in Bryan County.
- This announcement marks the second supplier to locate in that area in just two weeks.
- The EV Power Electric system plant will expand Hyundai Mobis’ current footprint in Georgia.
- Hyundai Mobis has operated a manufacturing facility with almost 1,200 employees in West Point since 2009.
- The existing West Point facility is the largest Tier 1 supplier to Kia Georgia.
- The West Point plant supplies automotive manufacturers with complete cockpit modules, chassis modules, and bumper assemblies.
- H.S. Oh serves as Vice President of Hyundai Mobis Electric Powertrain Business Unit.
- The investment reflects an acceleration in the development of the EV supply chain in Georgia’s auto industry.
- Hyundai Mobis will be a major production player in the EV market, triggering more sector growth.
- Once at full production, the new facility will have 1,200,000 square feet.
- The facility will supply over 900,000 EV Power Electric systems annually to Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) in Bryan County, Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Alabama (HMMA) in Montgomery, and the Kia Georgia plant.
- The facility will also supply 450,000 Integrated Charging Control Units annually to those same locations.
- Construction on the new facility is expected to begin as early as January 2023.
- Production at the new facility is expected to begin in 2024.
- The facility is located at the Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) Certified Belfast Commerce Park in Richmond Hill.
- Jon Seagraves serves as Chairman of the Development Authority of Bryan County.
- The site has close proximity to available housing in nearby Heartwood, a top-performing public school system, interstate access, and rail.
- The Development Authority purchased the site about two years ago.
- The project represents nearly $1 billion of investment and the creation of over 1,500 good-paying jobs.
- Bryan County, the City of Richmond Hill, and Raydient Places provided support and partnership on the project.
- Alyce Thornhill serves as Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy at the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team.
- The GDEcD partnered with the Development Authority of Bryan County, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia Power on this competitive project.
- Hyundai Mobis and other suppliers are transitioning to jobs of the future and becoming part of Georgia’s growing EV ecosystem.
- In May, officials predicted that non-affiliated HMGMA suppliers would invest an additional $1 billion into Georgia.
- That prediction was blown past in about two weeks.
- Partners like Georgia Quick Start and sister agencies are preparing Georgia’s workforce for the jobs being created now.
- Georgia is positioned as a hub for the electric mobility industry.
- Since 2020, more than 30 EV-related projects have contributed over $13.3 billion in investment to Georgia.
- These projects have announced more than 18,100 jobs in Georgia.
- On October 25, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America officially broke ground on its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility in Bryan County.
- Offsite suppliers connected to the HMGMA project are predicted to invest approximately $1 billion in other locations around the state.
