# Daesol Ausys Georgia

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-08-29

**Source:** [Daesol Ausys Georgia to Invest $72 Million in Harris County | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-release/automotive-supplier-daesol-ausys-georgia-invest-72-million-harris-county)  ·  `page_000046`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced on August 29, 2023, that Daesol Ausys Georgia will establish a new manufacturing facility in West Point.
- The facility will create more than 140 new jobs and $72 million in investment in Harris County.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated that West Point has become an automotive capital in its own right, and Daesol Ausys will join other innovators in Harris County.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp noted that Georgia's automotive industry employs over 50,000 hardworking Georgians and will continue to grow as e-mobility suppliers choose all corners of the state for the jobs of the future.
- Daesol Ausys was established in Korea in 2017.
- The company specializes in designing and manufacturing motor and electric vehicle interior components and accessories.
- It is a key supplier for Kia Georgia, Hyundai Motor Group, and General Motors.
- Min Ho Kwon, CEO of Daesol Ausys, stated the company is committed to delivering world-class services that exceed client requirements and expectations.
- Min Ho Kwon expressed excitement about the project in the Northwest Harris Business Park and looking forward to continuing the partnership with the State of Georgia and Harris County.
- The new facility will produce luggage boards and luggage covers.
- Operations are expected to begin in December 2024.
- The company will be hiring for positions in production, purchasing, quality control, maintenance, human resources, and management.
- Robert Grant, Chairman of the Harris County Commission, stated that Daesol Ausys’ decision to locate in the Northwest Harris Business Park represents a commitment to Harris County.
- Robert Grant noted that West Point and Harris County have a wonderful relationship with Daesol Ausys’ sister companies Daehan Solutions and Daesol Materials.
- Craig Greenhaw, Chairman of the Harris County Development Authority, welcomed Daesol Ausys and noted the venture brings jobs and capital investment into the region.
- Alyce Thornhill, Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy, represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The project was developed in partnership with the Harris County Commission, Harris County Development Authority, City of West Point, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that economic development is driven by the local level and congratulated Harris County and community partners for bringing Daesol Ausys to West Georgia.
- Pat Wilson noted that the automotive industry is providing generational change in the region and across the state, and expressed excitement for opportunities Daesol Ausys will provide.
- For over a century, Georgia has fostered healthy industry practices, encouraged collaboration and innovation, and positioned the state as a leader in developing and harnessing emerging technologies for the automotive and mobility industry.
- As the market transitions to electric vehicles, Georgia has pursued the entire supply chain, creating more than $25 billion in investments and 30,000 jobs since 2018.
- In fiscal year 2023, job creation in the automotive industry increased by 324 percent compared to FY21.
- Suppliers attracted by Georgia’s mobility original equipment manufacturers resulted in over $2 billion in investment across the state in FY23.
- Daesol Ausys Georgia is a leading manufacturer of automotive components and a key supplier of luggage boards and luggage covers.
- The company has a long history as one of the top suppliers to Kia and Hyundai worldwide.
