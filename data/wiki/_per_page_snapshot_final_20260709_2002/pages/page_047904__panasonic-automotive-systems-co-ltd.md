# Panasonic Automotive Systems Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-01-11

**Source:** [We’ve entered an era in which cars watch over and accommodate people.In this report, we look at the Tokyo Auto Salon’s “Joy in Motion” design booth. | Panasonic Automotive Systems Co., Ltd.](https://automotive.panasonic.com/en/studio/story/07)  ·  `page_047904`

## Facts

- Panasonic Automotive Systems (PAS) operated a booth at Tokyo Auto Salon 2026 from January 9 to January 11, 2026, at Makuhari Messe in Chiba Prefecture.
- The booth's theme focused on how AI is changing mobility experiences.
- PAS demonstrated signage that uses cameras and AI to read a visitor's hand skeletal structure in real time without X-ray scanning.
- The system reproduced hand gestures as a 3D wireframe model with almost no lag using only camera video images and AI analysis.
- PAS exhibited a steering wheel with embedded sensors that analyzes a driver's state of mind by detecting heart rate and minute perspiration on palms to infer mental state while driving.
- The steering wheel system can detect when drivers are tired or impatient, even if the drivers haven't noticed themselves.
- PAS displayed signage using cameras to watch people and determine actions like yawning, making phone calls, or drinking beverages.
- Sensors and AI were demonstrated to assess people's states with a high degree of accuracy for mobility applications.
- PAS showcased the "WELL Cabin Craie2," a vehicle concept using internal and external sensing functions and AI to provide "Joy in Motion."
- The WELL Cabin Craie2 proposes driving routes based on driver preferences, avoiding congested roads, narrow streets, or areas causing discomfort.
- While driving, the car uses sensors to detect surrounding conditions and can relax drivers by complimenting them.
- The system includes sensors for blind spots like rear left corners to issue alerts.
- It detects if a driver yawns and suggests taking a break.
- Back-seat passengers can connect to the car via smartphone to share route information and suggest stops, changing the route directly without distracting the driver.
- Future possibilities for the WELL Cabin Craie2 include using information from occupants' smartphones to recommend places to eat or music to listen to.
- The system uses AI to analyze what is seen from the car, such as identifying fireworks by gesture and explaining their names and origins.
- If a passenger points at a building, the AI can identify it.
- The car can detect when a passenger looks at the camera and smiles, automatically taking a photo and compositing it with the view in front of the car to send to the passenger's phone.
- PAS coordinated with the MAHA Group, which provides hospitality services to inbound tourists, to provide premium mobility experiences through the "WELL Cabin Luxe" based on the Toyota Alphard.
- An actual floor model of the upcoming next-generation "WELL Cabin GranLuxe" was exhibited at the Tokyo Auto Salon.
- The WELL Cabin GranLuxe focuses on providing a heightened sightseeing experience and features an exterior designed in collaboration with Italian design studio Italdesign, featuring a traditional Japanese motif of blue ocean waves.
- The GranLuxe cabin includes comfortable seats, elegant indirect lighting, acoustic speakers, and a large 55-inch transmissive screen.
- Occupants can watch informational tourism videos or ambient videos like campfire scenes on the display, with lighting linked to the video content.
- Presentation techniques are used to prevent car sickness, including a transparent front-facing view that blends real-world scenery with video content.
- The GranLuxe features "WELL Attendant," an AI concierge that conveys information about sightseeing destinations like history or culture through natural conversation with an AI avatar.
- WELL Attendant answered questions instantly and naturally, taking a slight pause after the user finished speaking to avoid talking over them.
- The AI concierge supports numerous languages including English, Chinese, French, Mandarin, Cantonese, and various dialects from across Japan.
- The goals of the AI concierge are to provide greater "Joy in Motion" and help enterprise operators address issues like a lack of sightseeing guides or difficulty providing multilingual support.
- Future plans for the sightseeing info include optimizing it based on passenger states such as facial expressions.
- PAS's vision is to become a "Joy in Motion" design company, advancing intensely human technologies to turn mobility into a partner that provides wonderful experiences.
