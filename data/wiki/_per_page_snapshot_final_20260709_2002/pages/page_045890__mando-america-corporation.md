# Mando America Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2021-04-30

**Source:** [21E039 - Anti-Lock Brake System Module May Short Circuit - Mando Anti-lock Brake System (ABS) Modules](https://blog.oemdtc.com/6182/21e039-anti-lock-brake-system-module-may-short-circuit-mando-anti-lock-brake-system-abs-modules)  ·  `page_045890`

## Facts

- NHTSA Campaign Number 21E039000 addresses Anti-Lock Brake System (ABS) modules that may short circuit.
- An electrical short circuit increases the risk of an engine compartment fire while parked or driving.
- Mando America Corporation is the manufacturer of the recalled ABS modules.
- The ABS module may leak brake fluid and cause a short circuit.
- Vehicle manufacturers have developed and will perform the remedy procedure for the recall.
- June 2018: Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia (KMMG) notified Mando America Corporation of a Quality Information Report concerning 2014 Hyundai Santa Fe Sport vehicle’s ABS module overheating, issued by Hyundai Motor America.
- Mando requested KMMG to recover the parts for analysis and received three ABS modules from incident vehicles listed in Hyundai’s QIR.
- Mando did not observe any significant failure in the ABS modules that could lead to a fire and informed KMMG that a potential root cause cannot be determined by component inspection alone.
- Mando suggested KMMG conduct a vehicle inspection to identify a root cause of the issue.
- March 2019: Mando conducted a joint inspection with HMA and Hyundai Motor Company on a repurchased 2015 Santa Fe Sport vehicle to identify a possible source of an engine compartment fire, but severe damage prevented determination.
- Hyundai Motor Company recovered the ABS module for further study by Mando.
- From additional inspection, Mando observed a leakage of brake fluid inside the recovered ABS module but found no quality deviation in its manufacturing process or history relative to this issue.
- April 2019—December 2019: Mando received three additional incident ABS modules from HMC and inspected those recovered parts.
- Mando observed brake fluid leakage inside the three additional modules but could not find a correlation between the leakage and a compartment fire, leaving an approximate failure mechanism or root cause undetermined.
- June 2020: Kia Motors Corporation sent Mando Corporation of Korea an ABS module involved in an electrical failure for a 2015 Kia Sorento.
- Mando Korea evaluated the recovered ABS module with KMC and observed brake fluid leakage and internal damages.
- August 27, 2020: Kia Motors America filed a Part 573 defect information report identifying a defect in certain Kia Optima and Kia Sorento vehicles manufactured at KMMG (Recall No. 20V-519).
- HMA filed a Part 573 defect information report identifying a defect in certain Hyundai Santa Fe Sport vehicles manufactured at KMMG (Recall No. 20V-520).
- September 2020—Jan 2021: Mando proactively informed General Motors about the HMA and KMA recalls on Sep 3, 2020.
- GM conducted a safety investigation concerning their vehicles equipped with the same HECU.
- In January, GM informed Mando that a field action is not necessary for GM vehicles equipped with the Mando HECU.
- January 2021—Present: Mando Korea received four ABS modules from KMC recovered through KMA’s recall inspection procedure and found signs of internal leaks.
- Mando Korea and Mando are continuing an investigation with KMC to determine the root cause of the leakage, including verifying component failures, brake system configurations, and user driving conditions.
- April 2021: NHTSA requested Mando to submit a 573 Defect Report to identify purchasers of the recalled HECU equipment.
- On April 30, 2021, Mando filed a 573 Defect Report to NHTSA to identify vehicle manufacturers who purchased the recalled HECU equipment, although Mando has not identified a defect in their HECU.
- To date, Mando has not yet determined the presence of a defect in the HECU/ABS modules.
- The affected equipment is listed as MANDO ANTI-LOCK BREAK SYSTEM with no part number or production dates provided.
