# GEDIA Automotive Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2020-07-24

**Source:** [German Automotive Parts Manufacturer GEDIA to Locate First Southeast U.S. Site in Whitfield County, Create 200 Jobs | Governor Brian P. Kemp Office of the Governor](https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases/2020-07-24/german-automotive-parts-manufacturer-gedia-locate-first-southeast-us-site)  ·  `page_005967`

## Facts

- GEDIA Automotive Group is a family-owned international company headquartered in Germany that specializes in manufacturing state-of-the-art automotive parts.
- The company announced it will build an advanced manufacturing facility in Whitfield County, Georgia, marking its first site in the Southeast U.S. and second facility in the United States.
- GEDIA will invest $85 million in this project.
- The facility will create 200 jobs for the greater Dalton area.
- Production will focus on parts for electric vehicles.
- Operations are expected to begin in the third quarter of 2021.
- The company has more than 100 years of experience and currently supplies lightweight structural automotive parts to Volkswagen, BMW, Mercedes-Benz, Volvo, and numerous other world-renowned companies.
- GEDIA will use state-of-the-art press hardening, tempering, and automated welding processes to produce its parts.
- The plant's core technology will be a hot-forming line featuring the company's patented TemperBox®, which enables the creation of body components with lower weight and significantly higher crash safety performance while conserving energy and material.
- GEDIA will occupy a 180,000-square-foot facility on a GRAD Certified property in Carbondale Business Park.
- The Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) certification program requires properties to undergo due diligence and complete specific requirements before a prospective business visits, with over 8,000 jobs created on GRAD sites since the program's inception.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson traveled to Germany to meet with GEDIA leadership and other companies finalizing U.S. market entry decisions, supported by Georgia’s government partners in the Free State of Bavaria.
- Director of International Relations Nico Wijnberg represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development on the project.
- The project was developed in partnership with the Georgia Electric Membership Corporation, Whitfield County, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GEDIA will offer opportunities for management and staff positions, highly skilled technicians, and production workers.
- Additional information about the project was expected to be available by the beginning of the fourth quarter of 2020.
- Globally, GEDIA employs more than 4,300 people across eight production plants and is involved in joint ventures and research companies.
- The company reported an annual turnover of $750 million in 2019.
- GEDIA develops and produces lightweight body-in-white automotive parts and assemblies, as well as chassis components.
