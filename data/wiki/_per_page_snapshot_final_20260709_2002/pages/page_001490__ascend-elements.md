# Ascend Elements

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2023-04-03

**Source:** [Ascend Elements Launches Largest EV Battery Recycling Facility in North America](https://electrifynews.com/news/ascend-elements-launches-largest-ev-battery-recycling-facility-in-north-america/)  ·  `page_001490`

## Facts

- Ascend Elements has opened the biggest EV battery recycling center in Georgia, making it the largest in North America.
- Ascend Elements celebrated the grand opening of its first commercial-scale lithium-ion battery recycling facility this week.
- The $50 million Base 1 facility is located in Covington, Georgia.
- The facility began partial operations in August 2022.
- The facility now has an annual capacity to process 30,000 metric tons of used lithium-ion batteries and manufacturing scrap.
- The processing capacity is equal to 70,000 electric vehicle batteries per year.
- Ascend Elements CEO Mike O’Kronley stated that the facility is an important part of America’s EV battery infrastructure and just the start.
- Lithium-ion battery recycling is becoming an increasingly important part of the EV battery materials supply chain as demand for electric vehicles increases.
- Ascend Elements recovers up to 98% of the critical battery metals in used electric vehicle batteries and gigafactory manufacturing scrap.
- The company uses its patented Hydro-to-Cathode™ process to recycle used lithium-ion batteries and scrap.
- The Hydro-to-Cathode™ process can reduce the carbon footprint of new electric vehicle battery cathode materials by up to 90%.
- The facility is powered by renewable energy.
- The facility features onsite wastewater recycling and treatment systems.
- The facility currently employs about 100 team members.
- Ascend Elements plans to increase the facility's headcount to 185 by 2024.
- Ascend Elements is based in Westborough, Massachusetts.
- Ascend Elements produces lithium-ion battery precursor (pCAM) and cathode active materials (CAM).
- The company's Hydro-to-Cathode™ direct precursor synthesis technology produces new CAM from spent lithium-ion cells more efficiently than traditional methods.
- The technology results in improved economics and lowered GHG emissions.
