# Aurubis

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2023-11-09

**Source:** [Aurubis Investing Nearly $700M on US Smelter in Georgia](https://www.waste360.com/waste-recycling/aurubis-investing-nearly-700m-on-us-smelter-in-georgia)  ·  `page_017285`

## Facts

- Aurubis, a German metals supplier, is investing almost $700 million in a new secondary smelter for complex recycling materials in Richmond County, Georgia.
- The new facility is considered the first of its kind in North America.
- The smelter will be commissioned in the second half of 2024.
- An expansion to the plant is already in progress.
- The expansion will increase the facility’s total processing capacity to 180,000tpa of complex recycling materials.
- Aurubis aims to establish a reliable and sustainable supply chain to recover more value from recyclable materials.
- The company is committing to investing more than $200,000 in the local community for scholarships and job training programs for young workers.
- The facility will create long-term, secure jobs for more than 200 people.
- The metals recovered from the recycling process are essential to the mobility and energy transition and contribute to the electric vehicle ecosystem.
- The project aligns with Georgia’s Electric Mobility and Innovation Alliance initiative.
- Inge Hofkens, Aurubis multimetal recycling chief operations officer, stated that the plant is strategically important to the US industry.
