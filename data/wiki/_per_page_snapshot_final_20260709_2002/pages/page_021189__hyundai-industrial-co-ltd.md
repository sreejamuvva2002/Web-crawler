# Hyundai Industrial Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-11

**Source:** [Hyundai, Kia Supplier to Invest in Manufacturing Facility in Coweta - Georgia_CEO](https://goldenislesceo.com/news/2023/11/hyundai-kia-supplier-invest-manufacturing-facility-coweta-news/)  ·  `page_021189`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Hyundai Industrial Co., Ltd. will create 100 new jobs and invest more than $24 million in a new manufacturing facility near Newnan in Coweta County.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that partners like Kia and Hyundai are attracting a large network of suppliers, creating jobs and opportunity for Georgians across the state.
- Suppliers have committed to bringing over 5,300 jobs to Georgia, mostly to communities outside of large urban areas, since last year's Hyundai Metaplant announcement.
- Hyundai Industrial was established in 1978 and is headquartered in Ulsan, Korea.
- The supplier specializes in manufacturing car seats, arm rests, and head rests for the Hyundai and Kia automotive brands.
- Mr. Woosuck Kim, Team Leader at HDI America Inc., stated that Hyundai Industrial has more than four decades of experience providing components for some of the world's biggest automotive brands.
- Hyundai Industrial has purchased an existing building at 83 Amlajack Way in Coweta County.
- The purchased building will be retrofitted to produce arm and head rests for OEMs, including Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- Hyundai Industrial will focus on filling manufacturing production roles, and jobs will be posted online when open.
- Coweta County Board of Commissioners Chairman John Reidelbach welcomed Hyundai Industrial Co., Ltd. to the county's thriving manufacturing industries.
- The new facility will have close proximity to I-85, making transport to nearby automobile plants effortless.
- Director of Korean Investment Yoonie Kim represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The project was developed in partnership with the Coweta County Development Authority and Georgia EMC.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson noted that with Kia located in West Georgia and Hyundai on the eastern coast, suppliers like Hyundai Industrial can locate almost anywhere in the state while remaining central to at least two major manufacturers.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson described the relationship between Coweta County and Hyundai Industrial as a classic example of Georgia’s partnership approach to economic development.
- Georgia has positioned itself as a leader in developing and harnessing emerging technologies for the automotive and mobility industry.
- Since 2020, Georgia has created more than $25.1 billion in investments and 29,000 jobs by pursuing the entire electric vehicle supply chain.
- In fiscal year 2023, job creation in Georgia's automotive industry increased by 324 percent compared to fiscal year 2021.
- Suppliers attracted by Georgia’s mobility OEMs resulted in over $2 billion in investment across the state in fiscal year 2023.
