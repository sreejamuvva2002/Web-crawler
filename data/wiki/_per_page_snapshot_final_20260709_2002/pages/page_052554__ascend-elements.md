# Ascend Elements

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Why Investors Gave Battery Recycler Ascend a Fresh $800 Million - Business Insider](https://www.businessinsider.com/ascend-elements-series-c-electric-vehicle-battery-recycling-supply-chain-2022-10)  ·  `page_052554`

## Facts

- Ascend Elements is a battery-recycling startup that has secured $800 million in fresh funding.
- CEO Mike O'Kronley plans to spend the funding on beating out rivals like Redwood Materials.
- The company was founded in 2015 in Westborough, Massachusetts.
- Its technology enables car companies and battery-makers to obtain materials at a lower cost and more sustainably than earth-mined metals.
- Investors from Fifth Wall and car-industry giants contributed $300 million to the company.
- The Series C funding consists of $200 million in equity investments and $100 million in debt financing.
- This funding accompanies two grants from the Department of Energy totaling $480 million.
- The funding includes a $50 million strategic investment from a division of South Korean energy giant SK received in September, which is part of the $200 million equity portion.
- Many recycling methods only scour used batteries for precious metals like lithium and nickel, requiring additional processing before reuse.
- Ascend's "hydro-to-cathode" technology recycles materials and processes them so they are ready to go straight back into battery supply chains.
- CEO Mike O'Kronley stated: "We are ideally suited to work with battery manufacturers to help them close the loop and produce sustainable battery material, taking their manufacturing scrap, processing it, and returning the material that they can use right back to them."
- Recycling is critical as the auto industry faces a brutal battery-supply shortage and materials-sourcing requirements in this summer's climate bill for EV tax credit qualification.
- Automakers and stakeholders are establishing plans to return spent battery materials back into manufacturing.
- Estimates suggest very little lithium has been recovered from batteries to date, but 9% to 11% of lithium demand in 2035 could be met with properly recycled materials.
- Properly recycled materials would eliminate mining-related issues such as time, expense, and environmental damage.
- Competitor Redwood Materials secured a $700 million Series C last year.
- Competitor Li-Cycle brought in $200 million earlier this year from a contract with Glencore.
- Ascend's first commercial-scale plant is located in Covington, Georgia, near the new "battery belt."
- The Covington plant began running a new battery-shredding line and will recycle 30,000 metric tons of batteries and manufacturing scrap annually once fully operational.
- Ascend is investing up to $1 billion into a new factory in Kentucky.
- Partnered business tied to Ford and Volkswagen EVs will reduce Ascend's limitation on battery feedstock volume.
- CEO Mike O'Kronley stated: "It's a very different value proposition that we offer to the market."
- An October 26, 2022 correction clarified that the $50 million strategic investment from SK is part of the $200 million equity funding, not an additional amount.
