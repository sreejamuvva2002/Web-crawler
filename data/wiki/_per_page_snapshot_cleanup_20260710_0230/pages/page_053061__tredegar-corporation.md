## Facts

- Tredegar Corporation is an industrial manufacturer based in Richmond, Virginia, operating through Aluminum Extrusions and Electronic Materials segments.
- Bonnell Aluminum is Tredegar's largest segment and a leading North American manufacturer of custom-finished aluminum extrusions.
- Key markets for Bonnell Aluminum include the automotive sector, specifically for structural components and battery housings.
- The company offers comprehensive services including design, extrusion, painting, anodizing, and fabrication for its aluminum products.
- Tredegar maintains long-term relationships with top-tier automotive OEMs who rely on its precise tolerance levels and finishing quality.
- The push for Electric Vehicles (EVs) is a massive catalyst for aluminum extrusions, as aluminum is preferred for EV frames and battery trays due to its strength-to-weight ratio.
- Tredegar’s 2024-2025 strategy focuses on capital expenditures for Bonnell Aluminum expansion, particularly in the automotive sector.
- The "Near-shoring" trend in North American manufacturing prioritizes domestic supply chains for automotive parts over overseas imports, serving as a long-term tailwind for Tredegar.
- New trade tariff policies, such as the Section 232 tariff increase on imported aluminum, protect Tredegar's domestic market share.
- In 2025 Q4, the aluminum extrusion segment sales grew 26.2% year-over-year, driven by robust demand in U.S. automotive markets.
- The company successfully passes rising metal costs to downstream customers to maintain profit margins in the automotive and construction sectors.
- Analysts note that Tredegar's strategic refocusing aligns with growing demand for electric vehicle components.
- Bonnell Aluminum remains one of the top 5 independent (non-integrated) extruders in North America.
- According to Grand View Research, the North American aluminum extrusion market is projected to grow at a CAGR of ~4.5% through 2030.
- Tredegar reported net income of $1.0 million for full year 2024, rebounding from a $99.2 million loss in 2023.
- Ongoing operations earnings reached $17.2 million ($0.50/share) as of Q3 2024.
- Following the 2024 divestiture of its Terphane business, Tredegar reduced its net leverage ratio from 3.7x to 1.1x by March 2025.
- The company sold Terphane for approximately $116 million (subject to closing adjustments) to pivot toward specialized industrial niches.
- As of 2025 year-end, Tredegar had approximately $87 million in available credit facility (ABL Facility) for potential acquisitions or capital expenditures.
- Major institutional holders include BlackRock Inc., The Vanguard Group, and Dimensional Fund Advisors.
