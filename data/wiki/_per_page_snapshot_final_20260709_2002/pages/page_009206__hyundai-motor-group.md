# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** research_and_development  ·  **Published:** 2026-04-29

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Group and Georgia Tech Sign MOU to Further Collaborate on Hydrogen Mobility Development_FuelCellChina, Your Gateway to China's Hydrogen Ecosystem](https://www.fuelcellchina.com/Industry_information_details/8582.html)  ·  `page_009206`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group and Georgia Institute of Technology announced an expansion of their collaboration on April 29, 2026, to advance hydrogen-powered transportation, deepen applied research and education, and accelerate zero-emissions vehicles in Georgia.
- The multi-year partnership includes Hyundai Motor North America donating four NEXO fuel cell electric SUVs exclusively for campus use.
- Hyundai Motor Group is donating a hydrogen electrolyzer project that will be installed at Georgia Tech’s North Avenue Research Area (NARA).
- The donated vehicles and infrastructure will support campus operations and interdisciplinary research focusing on engineering, sustainability, energy systems, and public policy.
- Faculty and students will study the real-world performance of hydrogen technology, infrastructure requirements for large-scale deployment, and environmental impacts of hydrogen energy systems.
- Insights from the initiative aim to inform and accelerate the widespread use of hydrogen technology in campuses, fleets, cities, and freight corridors.
- The partnership also aims to advance innovation in autonomous driving, electric vehicle batteries, charging infrastructure, materials science, and cybersecurity.
- Ken Ramírez serves as Executive Vice President and Head of Global Energy and Hydrogen Business at Hyundai Motor Group, is a Georgia Tech alumnus (class of 1991), and currently sits on the Georgia Tech Advisory Board.
- Randy Parker is the President and CEO of Hyundai Motor North America.
- Ángel Cabrera is the President of the Georgia Institute of Technology.
- Georgia serves as a hub for zero-emission transportation through HTWO Logistics, a clean logistics partnership operating Hyundai XCIENT fuel cell heavy-duty trucks near Savannah.
- The collaboration builds on Hyundai Motor Group's regional presence, which includes the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) in Georgia.
- The initiative supports Georgia Tech’s strategic plan goal of expanding the use of zero-emissions vehicles powered by sustainable energy sources.
- Hyundai Motor Group aims to achieve carbon neutrality by 2045 through its long-term clean mobility and hydrogen initiatives.
- Additional announcements regarding research projects, educational programs, and vehicle deployment are expected in the coming months.
