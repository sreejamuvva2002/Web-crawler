# Hyundai TRANSYS

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2021-04-08

**Source:** [Hyundai TRANSYS Adds Another $9M and 150 Jobs to Major Expansion   - Global Atlanta](https://www.globalatlanta.com/hyundai-transys-adds-another-9m-and-150-jobs-to-major-expansion/)  ·  `page_021391`

## Facts

- Korean seat and transmission supplier Hyundai TRANSYS is adding another 150 jobs to Troup County.
- The job addition brings its total investment to nearly a quarter-billion dollars within the last four months.
- In December, the company announced it would invest $240 million in its local transmission facility.
- The December announcement included hiring 678 people at a 620,000-square-foot plant.
- This week, the company pledged to inject $9 million into a separate factory making seats and seating pads.
- The new factory will produce components for vehicles made at Hyundai and Kia factories in Alabama and Georgia, respectively.
- Hyundai TRANSYS was formed by a combination of Dymos and Powertech, both prior Hyundai subsidiaries with operations in West Georgia.
- The combined company also makes axles.
- The combined company has global revenues of $7 billion.
- The expansion will bring the company’s total employment in Georgia to more than 1,890.
- Hyundai TRANSYS Georgia Seating System President and CEO Sangkil Jung stated excitement about expanding its presence in the West Point community and praised the partnership with the State of Georgia.
