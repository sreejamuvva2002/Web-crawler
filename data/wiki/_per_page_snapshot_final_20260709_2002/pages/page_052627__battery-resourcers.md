# Battery Resourcers

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2022-01-05

**Source:** [Covington future home to continent’s largest lithium-ion battery recycling facility - The Covington News](https://www.covnews.com/news/business/covington-future-home-to-continents-largest-lithium-ion-battery-recycling-facility/)  ·  `page_052627`

## Facts

- Battery Resourcers plans to invest $43 million to open its first commercial-scale battery recycling plant in Covington.
- Gov. Brian Kemp announced the investment and facility opening plans on Wednesday.
- The Covington facility is expected to generate 150 jobs in Newton County.
- The facility will be the largest lithium-ion battery recycling facility of its kind in North America.
- Battery Resourcers is headquartered in Worcester, Massachusetts.
- The company was founded in 2015 with a mission to return 100% of battery active materials back into new batteries.
- Battery Resourcers manufactures EV-grade, finished cathode active materials that meet or exceed performance requirements set by other industry-leading brands.
- The Covington facility is scheduled to open in spring 2022.
- Upon opening, the facility will be able to process 30,000 metric tons of discarded lithium-ion batteries and scrap annually.
- The annual processing capacity is equivalent to 70,000 vehicle batteries per year.
- The company's recycling process returns battery-grade lithium, cobalt, and nickel back into the battery supply chain.
- Battery recycling is only one component of Battery Resourcers' technology and strategic expansion plans.
- The company has engineered a process to turn recyclables back into critical battery materials, specifically nickel-manganese-cobalt cathodes.
- The recovered nickel-manganese-cobalt cathode materials are sold back to battery manufacturers.
- Major brand investors in Battery Resourcers include Jaguar Land Rover, Orbia, TDK, TRUMPF Group, Doral Energy, and Hitachi.
- CEO and Director Michael O’Kronley stated the company is looking forward to bringing 150 tech jobs to the Covington area.
- Automotive manufacturers currently have very few options for responsible and cost-effective disposal of discarded batteries and scrap.
- The convenient U.S. location and company technology will provide a sustainable solution that minimizes the need for mining and returns valuable, battery-grade materials back into the lithium-ion supply chain.
- Battery Resourcers will occupy an existing 154,000-square-foot facility located on Industrial Drive in Covington.
- The company has already begun hiring for the new facility.
- Newton County Industrial Development Authority Chairman Lanier Sims expressed pride in Battery Resourcers joining the area's mix of businesses.
- Local officials stated the company's presence will help continue making the I-20 corridor a business and technology-focused hub.
- Covington Mayor Steve Horton stated that Battery Resourcers is an outstanding addition to the community's business ecosystem and will complement many regional businesses in Georgia.
- The facility is expected to be fully operational in July.
- Project Manager Virginia Sengewald represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s Global Commerce division on the project.
- The project was developed in partnership with the Newton County Industrial Development Authority, the Metro Atlanta Chamber, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia Power.
- The announcement follows Rivian's recent plan to locate its second EV manufacturing facility at Stanton Springs, a multi-county industrial park near Social Circle.
- Rivian is investing $5 billion and will create 7,500 jobs for its Georgia facility.
- Construction of Rivian's plant is scheduled to start in summer 2022.
- Once fully operational, Rivian expects to produce up to 400,000 vehicles per year at the site.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that battery recycling has become a vital part of the EV supply chain as the industry grows rapidly.
- Cutting reliance on unstable global areas for battery materials is crucial for the future, according to Commissioner Wilson.
- The timing of Battery Resourcers' announcement aligns well with Georgia's recent acquisition of Rivian's second EV manufacturing facility and campus.
