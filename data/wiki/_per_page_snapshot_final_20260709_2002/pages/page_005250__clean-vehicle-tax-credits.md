# Clean Vehicle Tax Credits

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-30

**Source:** [Clean vehicle tax credits | Internal Revenue Service](https://www.irs.gov/clean-vehicle-tax-credits)  ·  `page_005250`

## Facts

- The New Clean Vehicle Credit, Previously-Owned Clean Vehicle Credit, and Qualified Commercial Clean Vehicle Credit are not available for vehicles acquired after Sept. 30, 2025.
- A vehicle must be placed in service to claim the credit.
- If a vehicle is placed in service after Sept. 30, 2025, it must have been acquired on or before Sept. 30, 2025 to remain eligible for the credit.
- Acquisition eligibility can be demonstrated by entering into a binding written contract and making a payment on the vehicle on or before Sept. 30, 2025.
- A vehicle is considered placed in service when the buyer takes possession of it.
- Sellers must provide buyers with information about an eligible new or previously-owned clean vehicle's qualifications upon taking possession.
- Sellers are required to register online and report vehicle qualification information to the IRS, or the vehicle will be ineligible for the credit.
- The page outlines changes to tax credits under the One, Big, Beautiful Bill.
- The New Clean Vehicle Credit under section 30D is available only for vehicles acquired on or before Sept. 30, 2025.
- Details regarding the credit for new vehicles were updated on Jan. 1, 2023.
- The Previously-Owned Clean Vehicle Credit applies exclusively to vehicles acquired on or before Sept. 30, 2025.
- The Previously-Owned Clean Vehicle Credit is available only to individuals.
- Installing an electric vehicle charger at home can simplify charging, increase convenience, and potentially raise property value.
- A tax credit is available to reduce the costs of EV charging equipment and its installation.
- Qualified vehicle refueling and recharging property installed at a home and placed in service before July 1, 2026, may qualify for the Alternative Fuel Vehicle Refueling Property Tax Credit.
- The Treasury Department and IRS issued FAQs addressing the accelerated termination of several energy provisions under the One, Big, Beautiful Bill (OBBB).
- Additional FAQs were published regarding modifications to sections 25C, 25D, 25E, 30C, 30D, 45L, 45W, and 179D under Public Law 119-21, 139 Stat. 72 (July 4, 2025).
