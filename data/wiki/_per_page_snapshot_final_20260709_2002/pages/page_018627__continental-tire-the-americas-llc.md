## Facts

- Continental Tire is closing in 2026 and employees are currently being assisted by numerous local and regional work force resources.
- The status of the plant itself is not yet determined as the company's priority is its employees until year end.
- Based in Fort Mill, S.C., Continental Tire the Americas, LLC manufactures and distributes a complete premium line of passenger, light truck and commercial tires for original equipment and replacement markets.
- Continental’s ultra-high, performance tires include award-winning technologies that focus on safety, help save money, and are #ForWhatYouDo.
- Continental’s premium products are available at leading independent tire dealers, car dealers, and mass retail companies across North America.
- Continental Tire of Barnesville manufactures tire cord fabric for Continental Tire.
- The plant has been in operation since 1888 where it operated as a cotton mill, producing tire cord fabric in the early 1920s.
- In 1930, the plant was sold to General Tire & Rubber Co., which was purchased by Continental A.G. in 1986.
- Continental Tire the Americas, LLC was formerly known as Continental Tire North America, Inc. and changed its name to Continental Tire the Americas, LLC in February 2010.
- The company was founded in 1983 and is based in Fort Mill, South Carolina.
- Continental Tire the Americas, LLC operates as a subsidiary of Continental Aktiengesellschaft.
- The Barnesville facility address is Packages: 160 Aldora St., Mail: PO Box 190, Barnesville, GA 30204, United States.
