# ZF

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2017-10-04

**Source:** [Germany’s ZF Marks 30 Years of Expansion in Gainesville - Global Atlanta](https://www.globalatlanta.com/germanys-zf-marks-30-years-expansion-gainesville/)  ·  `page_016308`

## Facts

- ZF opened a manual transmission plant in the North Georgia city of Gainesville in 1987.
- ZF's initial presence has expanded into four facilities, with two located in Gainesville and two in Oakwood.
- ZF employs more than 1,000 people across its Hall County facilities.
- ZF owns Kelsey-Hayes Co., a braking systems manufacturer with a presence in Oakwood, following its 2015 acquisition of U.S. rival TRW Automotive.
- In 2011, Georgia Gov. Nathan Deal inaugurated a new $100 million factory in Gainesville producing gear boxes for wind turbines.
- The wind turbine gear boxes produced at the 2011 plant weigh 16 tons each and are transported one per semi-truck.
- The 2011 Gainesville plant was ZF’s first in the U.S. for the wind turbine gear box line of business, with initial plans to expand to about 250 workers.
- Thirty years after its initial investment, ZF retains 14 of its original 88 Gainesville employees.
- ZF's manufacturing team now includes employees representing 28 different nationalities.
- At least one employee on ZF's Georgia team relocated from one of the company's six plants in Mexico.
- Economic development leaders emphasize that winning new greenfield investment is only the beginning, as most new jobs come from supporting existing companies and helping them expand.
- Gainesville Mayor Danny Dunagan and Oakwood Mayor Lamar Scroggs led city and county leaders to the ZF facility last month to celebrate the company’s 30th anniversary in Hall County.
- Sept. 23 was designated “ZF Day” through a joint declaration by the governments of Oakwood, Gainesville, and Hall County.
