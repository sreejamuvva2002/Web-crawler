# South Korean Companies in Georgia

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-08

**Source:** [South Korean Companies Halt Georgia Investments After ICE Raid](https://www.jalopnik.com/1961930/south-korean-companies-halt-georgia-investments-ice-raid/)  ·  `page_011817`

## Facts

- U.S. immigration authorities (ICE) raided an under-construction Hyundai plant in Georgia on Sept. 4, detaining 475 workers.
- About 300 of the detained workers were from South Korea and were chartered home by the South Korean government.
- The raid has caused South Korean companies to rethink and pause current U.S. investment plans.
- The Hyundai Motor-LG Energy Solution battery joint venture plant (HL-GA) in Savannah, Georgia, was cited as one of the largest U.S. investment projects by a global company.
- Kim Gap-song, head of the Korean American Service and Education Consortium in New York, dispatched staff to Savannah to support detained HL-GA workers and reported that some Korean companies have preemptively suspended construction due to fears of further incidents.
- Georgia is considered a hub for Korean investment in the U.S., hosting more than 110 Korean companies.
- Hyundai Motor Group operates in Savannah, SK On operates in Commerce, and Hanwha Qcells operates in Dalton.
- A coordinated crackdown by DHS's ICE, FBI, and DEA has left many Koreans feeling betrayed and caused local businesses like Costco and downtown restaurants to lose Korean customers.
- Areas revived by Korean corporate investment could take a direct economic hit from the immigration enforcement actions.
- Volkswagen is still interested in investing in the U.S. and is considering building a plant to manufacture Audi cars there.
- VW was in advanced talks with the U.S. government over substantial investments in the U.S. auto market amid tariffs that have cost the company billions of euros.
- VW Chief Executive Oliver Blume stated the company does not appreciate an asymmetric tariff deal (15% on EU auto imports vs. no tariffs for U.S. industrial goods into Europe) and is counting on its U.S. investment plans to boost local employment and supply chain.
- Blume expressed hope for a quick solution to localize business decisions at the IAA Munich car show.
