# U.S. Department of Energy Bioenergy Technologies Office

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** research_and_development  ·  **Published:** 2024-10-17

**Source:** [Bioenergy Past Meetings and Workshops | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/cmei/fuels/bioenergy-past-meetings-and-workshops)  ·  `page_005117`

## Facts

- The U.S. Department of Energy’s Bioenergy Technologies Office (BETO) hosted the Alternative Aviation Fuel Workshop in Macon, Georgia, on September 14-15, 2016.
- The workshop aimed to advance understanding of current technical barriers for increasing the competitiveness of aviation biofuels.
- The event noted that alternative energy systems such as batteries, fuel cells, and natural gas are options for on-road, off-road, and marine engine applications, but these alternative fuels are not yet practical for aviation.
- The DOE's Sustainable Aviation Fuel (SAF) Grand Challenge involves the DOE, U.S. Department of Transportation, U.S. Department of Agriculture, and other federal agencies working to supply 100% of projected domestic aviation jet fuel by 2050 using purpose-grown energy crops like algae.
- BETO's Co-Optima initiative focuses on the concurrent development of advanced fuels and engines that are economically viable and commercially scalable for every vehicle on the road.
- BETO hosted a workshop in Charleston, South Carolina, on March 26-27, 2014, focused on research and developments needed to achieve renewable algae-based biofuels.
- BETO has organized numerous past meetings and workshops from 2014 to 2024 covering topics including biomanufacturing, circular economy for plastics, soil carbon storage, first-generation ethanol, algae crop protection, waste-to-energy feedstocks, hydrothermal liquefaction, bioeconomy data utilization, predictive modeling for bio-based fuels, waste-to-conversion-ready feedstocks, plastic waste solutions, dedicated energy crops for water quality, advanced process technologies for 1G ethanol, circular carbon economy in agriculture and energy, cell-free synthetic biology, engineered carbon reduction, wet and gaseous waste streams, biobased chemicals, algae cultivation for carbon capture, biorefinery optimization, energy-positive water resource recovery, hydrogen from wastewaters, feedstock supply systems, grass biomass markets, liquid transportation fuels from wet waste, lignocellulosic conversion, bio-oil co-processing with petroleum refining, biomass indirect liquefaction, and drop-in hydrocarbon deployment.
