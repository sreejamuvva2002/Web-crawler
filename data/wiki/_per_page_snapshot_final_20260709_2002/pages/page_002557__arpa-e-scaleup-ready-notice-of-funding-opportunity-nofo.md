# ARPA-E SCALEUP Ready Notice of Funding Opportunity (NOFO)

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-01-28

**Source:** [DOE: ARPA-E eXCHANGE: Funding Opportunities](https://arpa-e-foa.energy.gov/)  ·  `page_002557`

## Facts

- ARPA-E has issued the SCALEUP Ready NOFO to support scaling promising ARPA-E-funded technologies into early commercial products.
- The announcement facilitates collaboration among potential performing teams to respond to the program.
- The NOFO provides specific program goals, technical metrics, and selection criteria, with terms being controlling.
- The program supports innovative energy research and development that complements ARPA-E’s focus on early-stage transformational energy technologies requiring proof of concept.
- The NOFO will remain open until closed or modified, with applications accepted at any time while open.
- ARPA-E’s mission is to develop and deploy transformational energy technologies supporting U.S. national security and economic competitiveness goals.
- ARPA-E funds R&D for energy technologies that reduce foreign energy imports, reduce energy-related emissions including greenhouse gases, and improve energy efficiency across economic sectors.
- The authorizing statute directs ARPA-E to ensure the U.S. maintains a lead in advanced energy technologies by accelerating transformational advances industry would not undertake due to technical and financial uncertainty.
- Technologies achieving substantial advancement under ARPA-E may face technical and commercial challenges post-funding, risking being stranded in their development path.
- Pre-commercial scaling projects aim to translate bench-scale performance to commercially scalable versions, integrate technology with broader systems, provide extended performance data, and validate manufacturability and reliability.
- Successful scaling projects enable industry stakeholders to justify financial, personnel, manufacturing facility, and material commitments for commercial deployment.
- The program seeks to scale the most promising technologies previously funded by ARPA-E.
- Unadopted ARPA-E funded technologies leave intellectual property vulnerable to foreign competitors, harming U.S. national competitiveness in global energy markets.
- ARPA-E encourages organizations with entrepreneurial scientists and engineers along with commercialization and financial entities across diverse sectors to participate.
- Interdisciplinary and cross-sector collaboration is encouraged to accelerate scientific and technological outcomes previously viewed as extremely difficult or impossible.
- ARPA-E is compiling an optional Teaming Partner List for SCALEUP Ready to help form project teams and identify collaborations.
- The Teaming Partner List will be available on ARPA-E eXCHANGE starting in October 2024.
- The list will be updated periodically as long as the NOFO remains open to reflect new partners.
- Organizations can join the Teaming Partner List by providing Organization Name, Contact details, Organization Type, Area of Technical Expertise, and a Brief Description of Capabilities.
- Submitters must specify interest as a project team lead or partner, describe sought partners if leading, and describe roles and capabilities if partnering.
- Submission consents to publication of the provided information.
- ARPA-E does not endorse, evaluate qualifications, pay for information, or compensate respondents for the Teaming Partner List.
- Responses submitted via email or other means will not be considered; participation is voluntary and has no bearing on NOFO application evaluation.
- This notice does not constitute a NOFO; applicants must refer to the SCALEUP Ready NOFO issued on October 2, 2024 for application instructions and funding terms.
- Questions about the NOFO should be directed to the designated program contact.
