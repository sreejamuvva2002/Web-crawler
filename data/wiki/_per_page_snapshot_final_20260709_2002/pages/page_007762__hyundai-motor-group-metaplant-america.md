# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-04-03

**Source:** [More than meets the eye awaits Hyundai Meta Pros at its Savannah region metaplant](https://www.yahoo.com/news/more-meets-eye-awaits-hyundai-090321434.html)  ·  `page_007762`

## Facts

- The Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) held its Grand Opening on March 26.
- At the time of the article, the project was described as the largest economic development project in the history of Georgia.
- Land clearing for the facility began in 2022 along Interstate-16.
- HMGMA refers to its employees as 'Meta Pros.'
- Employee Julisa Donahue, previously a Trane technician for a Georgia HVAC company, has been employed at the plant since September 2023 and commutes daily from Reidsville.
- The facility employs over 1,200 Meta Pros.
- South Korean natives onsite provide training for the robotics and assembly line systems.
- Chief Administrative Officer Brent Stubbs discussed Hyundai's cultural training elements in an interview in January.
- Last year, the company sent over 250 of its Meta Pros to Korea for a culture exchange experience to learn about the history of Hyundai and Korea.
- The workforce includes homegrown Georgia southerners, employees newly arrived from the West Coast, and 56 South Korean expatriates employed by HMGMA.
- HMGMA provides free Korean cultural education and language classes on site for interested employees.
- The company teaches English as a Second Language (ESL) on site to foster global literacy among workers.
- The plant produces Hyundai Ioniq 5 models.
- Employee Charlene Toole, previously a dispatcher at a local HVAC company, joined the plant in October 2023 as employee number 300.
- All Meta Pros know their hire number, which serves as a point of pride for being part of the facility's foundation team.
- The facility utilizes Autonomous Mobile Robots (AMR) and Automated Guided Vehicles (AGV) to transport car parts and assist in manufacturing.
- Boston Dynamics Spots are used by robots to scan vehicles as part of the quality control process.
- The plant has thousands of robots, including over 450 welding robots that will increase to almost 1,000 with the second line.
- There are over 200 AMRs at the facility, compared to under 50 in other assembly plants.
- The plant was designed with humans in mind, featuring skylights and lamps that emulate natural light for worker benefit.
- AMRs and AGVs handle repetitive or intensive tasks that could strain or endanger human workers.
- Break areas called team centers are stationed throughout the plant just off the assembly line.
- Meta Pros can order lunches through an app for delivery to the team centers.
- HMGMA intends to build a second battery plant as part of a joint venture with LG Solutions.
- The company plans to expand its own production by 200,000 units per year.
- The timeline for increased production is unclear and depends on fully staffing the first shift and bringing on a second full shift.
- Hyundai Motor Company President and CEO José Muñoz stated that production expansion would happen 'the sooner the better' but did not commit to a specific timeline.
- The need for a second battery plant and added hybrid vehicle production is expected to lead to 5,500 new jobs on site.
- HMGMA Assistant Manager of Communications and Public Relations Bianca Johnson stated that how additional jobs are distributed among HMGMA and onsite affiliates remains to be seen.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development Executive Director of Communications Marie Gordon stated via email on Tuesday that there has been no request to further amend the economic development agreement.
