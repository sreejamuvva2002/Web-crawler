# Eaton Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** power_electronics  ·  **Published:** 2020-11-12

**Source:** [Eaton Corp Closing Its Toccoa Manufacturing Plant - 128 Workers Losing Their Jobs - 92.1 WLHR](https://921wlhr.com/eaton-corp-closing-its-toccoa-manufacturing-plant-128-workers-losing-their-jobs/)  ·  `page_019698`

## Facts

- Eaton Corporation announced they are closing their Toccoa manufacturing plant in Stephens County.
- The targeted closing date for the Toccoa plant is during the first quarter of 2021.
- According to Stephens County Development Authority Executive Director Tim Martin, the closure will displace 128 employees.
- Displaced workers have options available to them, including finding work in other industries in Toccoa and surrounding counties that are actively hiring.
- The Georgia Department of Labor is in the process of mobilizing its resources to assist those displaced workers.
- According to Eaton Plant Manager Richard Haislip, the company has put together a robust severance package for its employees, which includes out-placement and counseling assistance.
- Haislip stated it is important that they hold on to their local workforce until after the first of the year as the company has contracts to fulfill.
- Once the plant is closed, plans are to market the land and building.
- Eaton is a power management company made up of over 92,000 employees, doing business in more than 175 countries.
- The company manufactures energy-efficient products and services to help customers effectively manage electrical, hydraulic and mechanical power more reliably, efficiently, safely and sustainably.
