# Archer Aviation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Archer Aviation will build major factory in Georgia](https://www.calcuttanews.net/news/273104191/archer-aviation-will-build-major-factory-in-georgia)  ·  `page_017064`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation announced it will invest $118 million to construct a plant near Atlanta, Georgia.
- The aircraft plant will be built adjoining an airport in Covington, Georgia.
- The company plans to eventually hire up to 1,000 people at the facility.
- Archer Aviation is developing a battery-powered vertical takeoff and landing craft with six propellers that holds four passengers and a pilot for short urban flights.
- United Airlines announced it would operate the aircraft on a route from downtown Manhattan to Newark Liberty International Airport in New Jersey, cutting travel time to 10 minutes.
- The U.S. Air Force is evaluating Archer's aircraft for possible use.
- Archer Aviation is seeking Federal Aviation Administration certification and expects it could be certified by 2024.
- The Georgia site aims to produce 650 aircraft annually, with production capacity potentially rising to 2,300 per year.
- Archer expects to receive an incentive package worth some $40 million from Georgia and local governments.
- The incentive package includes the donation of 96 acres of land at Covington Municipal Airport, a state cash grant, and other tax incentives.
- Archer chose Georgia due to the availability of local workers, the ability to conduct test flights, affordable construction costs, and good highway, rail, and air connections.
- Archer CEO Adam Goldstein stated that their eVTOL technology can transform urban and rural commuting and create pathways to highly skilled manufacturing hubs and economic opportunity.
- Georgia Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson noted that economic recruiters specializing in aerospace worked with Archer on the project.
