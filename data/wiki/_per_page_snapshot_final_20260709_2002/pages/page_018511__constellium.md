# Constellium

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-05-19

**Source:** [Constellium workers in Michigan strike for safety and a living wage - World Socialist Web Site](https://www.wsws.org/en/articles/2023/05/19/cons-m19.html)  ·  `page_018511`

## Facts

- 160 workers in the United Auto Workers union launched a strike at the Constellium automotive plant in suburban Detroit on Wednesday.
- The strike demands improved health and safety conditions, wage increases to compensate for the soaring cost of living, and an end to management’s abusive disciplinary measures.
- The factory is located in Van Buren, Michigan.
- Constellium is a key supplier to Ford.
- Constellium produces aluminum structures and crash management systems for the Ford F-150, F-150 Lightning, Explorer and Super Duty at six assembly plants.
- The Paris, France-based Constellium employs more than 12,000 workers internationally.
- Constellium is a global manufacturer of aluminum rolled products, extruded products and structural parts.
- Constellium had revenue of $8.73 billion in 2022, including $1.9 billion in sales to automakers.
- Constellium had a net profit of $332 million last year, up from $282 million in 2021.
- CEO Jean-Marc Germain pocketed nearly $9 million last year.
- The Constellium strike is part of the growing wave of struggles by autoworkers against plans by the global automakers to force workers to pay for the transition to electric vehicles.
- In Holland, Ohio, 525 UAW members have been on strike for over a week against Clarios, the world’s largest auto battery manufacturer.
- A judge granted Clarios an injunction to limit picketing at its Toledo-area factory.
- Clarios began bringing in strikebreakers to continue production of batteries for Ford, GM, Stellantis and other automakers.
- The UAW called the strike after initially extending the previous contract for several days after its May 13 expiration date.
- UAW Region 1D Director Laura Dickerson stated that Constellium has zero interest in taking members’ proposals seriously, calling it a prime example of employer arrogance.
- Union officials are limiting the struggle to an “unfair labor practices” (ULP) strike and seeking to call it off at the earliest opportunity.
- Negotiations resumed Thursday, according to UAW international servicing rep Michael Shumaker.
- A Constellium spokesperson said the company’s goal in negotiations was to reach a “mutually beneficial agreement.”
- The spokesperson added that they do not anticipate any disruption to production and will work closely with customers to ensure continuity of operations.
- Pickets reported that temps from a local agency were inside the plant working to maintain production on Thursday evening.
- Workers are urged to form a rank-and-file strike committee led by shop floor workers to coordinate with striking workers at Clarios and other manufacturers.
- Demands include restoration of COLA, wage increases to compensate for years of below-inflation raises, and rank-and-file control over health and safety measures.
- Constellium workers voiced support for a statement by the International Workers Alliance of Rank-and-File Committees (IWA-RFC) calling for mobilization support for the Clarios strike.
- A rank-and-file picket summed up the sentiment on the line, saying they are glad more people are getting involved and that workers need to unite around the world.
- One worker noted the last contract provided a 3 percent wage increase per year, while inflation is between 6 and 8 percent.
- Another worker stated they left General Motors making $15.68 an hour to come to Constellium for $3 more, but it is still hardly enough to live on.
- A worker compared wages at Mayser across the street, noting they make $14 an hour, which is insufficient given day care costs of $300-$400 a week.
- Workers highlighted that they helped the company earn the Q1 quality flag this year, bringing more business, and deserve compensation for their labor.
- The Van Buren plant opened 10 years ago.
- Workers report chronic failures of basic maintenance and extremely hazardous conditions at the Van Buren plant.
- Receiving docks overflow with water when it rains.
- Rust Lick cutting fluid leaks from machines and mixes on the floor with rainwater that pours through the roof.
- A 24-inch circular saw cuts an aluminum extrusion but lacks a brake, posing a fatal risk to inexperienced operators.
- OSHA was called regarding the saw issue but did not fix it.
- At the former Novi plant, a rotating welding machine part for the Chevy Cruze rubbed against an electrical power cable until insulation wore through and charged the machine's metal.
- The Constellium strike is at least the second walkout by workers at the company in recent years.
- At the end of 2020, 400 aluminum workers (USW members) walked out at a Constellium plant in Muscle Shoals, Alabama over unsafe conditions, threats to end seniority rights, and health care benefit cuts.
- The USW limited that struggle to a “unfair labor practices” strike and pushed through a concessionary contract in January 2021 after isolating workers for nearly a month.
