# SK Innovation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2021-05-20

**Source:** [Korean President Set to Visit SK Plant in Georgia This Weekend - Global Atlanta](https://www.globalatlanta.com/korean-president-set-to-visit-sk-plant-in-georgia-this-weekend/)  ·  `page_001308`

## Facts

- South Korean President Moon Jae-in is scheduled to visit Georgia this weekend to survey progress on the SK Innovation electric-vehicle battery plant.
- The visit follows Mr. Moon’s summit with President Joe Biden in Washington on Friday, where economic cooperation was highlighted as a priority amplified by recent developments in Georgia.
- A legal feud between Korean battery giants LG Energy Solution and SK had threatened to close the largest-ever foreign investment in Georgia.
- The dispute was settled in April when SK agreed to pay $1.8 billion to LG.
- At least 2,600 jobs and $2.6 billion were on the line during the dispute.
- The clarity provided by the settlement and the announcement of Mr. Moon’s visit triggered a flurry of Korean investment announcements in Georgia.
- Hyundai Motor Group said it would invest $7.4 billion into mobility initiatives including upgrading facilities to prepare for electrification.
- Hyundai Motor Group stopped short of committing those funds specifically to its plants in Alabama and Georgia.
- SK and Ford announced a $5.3 billion battery joint venture called BlueOvalSK on Thursday.
- The partners indicated they could build two new U.S. battery plants as part of the BlueOvalSK joint venture.
- SK supplies batteries to the electric Ford F-150 Lightning, which President Biden drove earlier in the week before its official Wednesday unveiling.
- Korean news sources reported that SK Innovation’s chairman left Korea on a flight bound for the U.S. on Wednesday.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson welcomed the collaboration, stating it could connect Georgia’s future-facing EV sector with its traditional auto manufacturing heritage.
- Commissioner Pat Wilson stated the announcement provides an opportunity to renew a long-held partnership with Ford, which manufactured cars in Georgia from 1947-2006, while rapidly expanding SK’s footprint in the state.
- Commissioner Pat Wilson noted that every new commitment by an automotive OEM shifting goals to e-mobility drives the transition for suppliers and accompanying jobs.
- Commissioner Pat Wilson stated Georgia is open for business and ready to work together to seize the moment.
- Georgia announced a $10 million investment on Thursday by Korean automotive firm Duckyang.
- Duckyang supplies SK Battery with modules and energy-storage systems.
- Duckyang sells cockpit modules globally to Kia, Hyundai, Renault, and other companies.
