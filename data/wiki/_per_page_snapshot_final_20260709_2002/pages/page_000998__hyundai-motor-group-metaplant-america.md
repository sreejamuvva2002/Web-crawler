# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-03-27

**Source:** [Hyundai's $8 billion Metaplant opens in Georgia as Trump announces auto tariffs](https://koreajoongangdaily.joins.com/news/2025-03-27/business/industry/Hyundais-8-billion-Metaplant-opens-in-Georgia-as-Trump-slaps-car-tariffs-/2271523)  ·  `page_000998`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group officially unlocked its $7.6 billion metaplant in Georgia Wednesday.
- The facility opening saved the company from tariff threats as U.S. President Donald Trump formalized 25 percent levies on all imported cars.
- The metaplant is located in Bryan County, Georgia.
- The metaplant covers 11.76 million-square-meter (126.58 million-square-foot).
- The plant will help Hyundai boost its U.S. production capacity to roughly 1.2 million units.
- This capacity represents around 70 percent of the company's total U.S. sales as of last year.
- The facility includes an automobile plant focusing on EVs and hybrids.
- The facility includes a joint battery plant with LG Energy Solution.
- The facility includes an auto parts factory from Hyundai Mobis.
- The auto plant's current capacity stands at 300,000 units.
- The goal is to increase the auto plant's capacity up to 500,000 units in the future.
- This beats the Hyundai Alabama plant's 360,000 units.
- This beats Kia's Georgia plant's 340,000 units.
- Hyundai's Ioniq 5 and Ioniq 9 are being manufactured at the plant as of now.
- Euisun Chung, executive chair of Hyundai Motor Group, spoke during the plant's opening ceremony.
- The opening ceremony was attended by Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp, Hyundai Motor Group Vice Chairman Chang Jae-hoon, and CEO José Muñoz.
- Euisun Chung stated: "Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) not only represents the group's advanced manufacturing capabilities and commitment to innovation, but also our investment in relationships with our partners and communities right here in Georgia."
- Euisun Chung also stated: "With the rich history of craftsmanship and manufacturing in this community, together with the talented workforce at HMGMA, we are building the future of mobility with America, in America."
- Trump declared 25 percent tariffs on all imported cars to the U.S. market on Wednesday.
- The tariffs will take effect on April 2.
- A correction was issued on March 27 noting an earlier version misstated the location and size of Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
