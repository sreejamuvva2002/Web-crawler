# Lithium & Battery Supply Chain Ecosystem

**Type:** supplier_relationship  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Four Companies Leading the Rise of Lithium & Battery Technology](https://www.globalxetfs.com/four-companies-leading-the-rise-of-lithium-battery-technology/)  ·  `page_008214`

## Facts

- Electric vehicles (EVs) are upending the status quo of internal combustion vehicles (ICE).
- Fueling the shift to electric power requires next-generation battery technology and an ample supply of lithium.
- Lithium is the key raw material for lithium-ion batteries.
- The industry’s ecosystem starts upstream with lithium miners that extract the metal from the earth.
- Raw materials move into the chemical conversion process to produce lithium carbonate or lithium hydroxide.
- Battery producers combine carbonate or hydroxide with materials to form a cathode and an anode, together forming an individual battery cell.
- Thousands of cells may be combined to create a battery pack for an EV.
- Ganfeng Lithium is a leading Chinese lithium mining company that has evolved into refining and processing lithium, battery manufacturing, and recycling.
- Panasonic is a top-3 global EV battery manufacturer from Japan.
- Livent is a top-5 lithium producer from the US.
- Contemporary Amperex Technology Limited (CATL) is a top-3 EV battery manufacturer from China.
- Ganfeng Lithium along with Albemarle, SQM, Tianqi Lithium, and Livent control more than 70% of the world’s lithium supply.
- Ganfeng was established in 2000.
- Ganfeng is the third-largest lithium compound producer in the world and the leading producer in China.
- Ganfeng covers a wide swath of the lithium-ion battery supply chain, including lithium resource development, refining & processing (75% of total revenue), battery manufacturing (17% of total revenue), and battery recycling & other (8% of total revenue).
- Ganfeng has a broad international footprint with lithium operations in Australia, Argentina, Mexico, and Ireland, in addition to its resources throughout China.
- Mount Marion in Western Australia is Ganfeng’s primary source of lithium.
- In Australia, Ganfeng uses hard rock lithium extraction processes.
- In Argentina, Ganfeng extracts lithium via a brine process as is common in Latin America’s Lithium Triangle (Argentina, Bolivia and Chile).
- In Mexico, Ganfeng is exploring new lithium clay extraction methods with an expected production start in 2023.
- Ganfeng boasts long-term supply contracts with major battery producers and OEMs like Tesla, Panasonic, LG Chem, Volkswagen, Samsung, and BMW.
- Ganfeng expects to double its capacity from 100,000 metric tons in 2020 to 200,000 by 2025.
- Surging EV demand should help companies like Ganfeng secure long-term contracts at higher prices.
- Ganfeng is focused on growing its lithium hydroxide production and sales because it is better suited than lithium carbonate for next-generation EV battery technology.
- Batteries with NMC 811 cathodes and other nickel-rich batteries require lithium hydroxide.
- By 2021, Ganfeng expects to produce and sell 50,000–60,000 metric tons of lithium hydroxide and 20,000–30,000 metric tons of lithium carbonate.
- Panasonic has more than 100 years of history and is the world’s third-largest supplier of EV batteries.
- Panasonic is considered a Tier 1 lithium-ion battery producer according to Benchmark Mineral’s classification standards for automotive uses.
- In 2014, Panasonic and Tesla partnered to build Giga Nevada, the world’s largest lithium-ion battery factory.
- Panasonic started working on Tesla’s featured battery cell called 4680.
- The 4680 format is expected to store more energy and have an easier manufacturing process to further reduce battery costs.
- A battery represents approximately 29% of the total cost of an EV.
- Panasonic is the sole provider of lithium-ion batteries to Tesla for EVs manufactured in the U.S.
- Panasonic recently partnered with Toyota to build a lithium-ion plant in Japan to supply 500,000 EVs starting in 2022.
- Livent was spun out of FMC Corporation in 2018 and is a Philadelphia-based company with a lithium history dating back to the 1940s.
- Compared to other large Western players like Albemarle and SQM, Livent is a pure-play company focused only on lithium.
- Livent operates one of the lowest-cost lithium mineral deposits in the world, the Salar del Hombre Muerto in Argentina.
- Livent’s operations sit at the low end of the global cost curve to produce lithium carbonate.
- Livent’s strategy focuses on lithium hydroxide, requiring a two-step conversion process from brine-based raw materials that adds costs.
- Livent estimates its all-in cost structure for lithium hydroxide to be about $5,800, which remains below today’s depressed lithium prices.
- The growing prevalence of nickel-rich batteries supports Livent’s business strategy.
- Livent estimates nickel-rich batteries should increase their market share from about 25% today to 75% by the end of the decade.
- Livent is known for its relationship with Tesla, being one of its main lithium hydroxide suppliers.
- In November, Livent announced the extension of a multi-year supply agreement with Tesla through 2021, committing to deliver greater volumes than in 2020.
- Livent management indicated they are working to extend their partnership for 2022 and beyond.
- Livent recently announced a 50/50 joint venture with private equity firm Pallinghurst Resources to buy Canada’s lithium projects previously managed by Nemaska Lithium.
- Pallinghurst, through Quebec Lithium Partners, is expected to own 50% of New Nemaska Lithium.
- Livent is expected to see 25% of the potential economic benefits of New Nemaska Lithium.
- The Canadian location can help Livent meet the growing demand of battery grade lithium from North American and Europe.
- CATL is the world’s second-largest battery producer, behind LG Chem.
- CATL is a Tier 1 battery producer, joining the select group of LG Chem, Panasonic, Samsung SDI, Tesla, SK Innovation, and AESC.
- CATL distinguishes itself as the battery maker with the largest number of relationships with OEMs, including Tesla, BMW, Daimler, Geely, Great Wall, Honda, Hyundai, Volkswagen, and NEVS.
- In February, CATL and Tesla agreed to produce batteries for EVs manufactured at Giga Shanghai, Tesla’s second battery megafactory.
- Tesla is currently producing Model 3’s at an annualized rate of 250,000 EVs.
- Helped by CATL’s cobalt-free lithium iron phosphate (LPF) batteries and local procurement, the Model 3 is the lowest priced premium mid-sized sedan in China at $36,800.
- CATL recently announced a project to roll out a “million-mile” battery that costs less than $100 kWh by 2021.
- Such a battery could support decades of heavy use for an EV, well beyond the lifespan of an internal combustion engine vehicle.
- CATL’s relationships with OEMs across multiple regions, especially Chinese and European markets, allow it to remain a key player in the EV supply chain.
- Recent reports from the China Association of Automobile Manufacturers show that new energy vehicles sales increased 105% year-over-year in October 2020.
- New energy vehicle sales totaled approximately 160,000 during October 2020, marking the fourth consecutive month of gains.
- Europe is projected to command the greatest share of EV sales growth.
- Research firm Rho Motion estimates EV sales in Europe could double by the end of 2020 from 2019 due to significant policy support from European regulators.
- Beyond the four highlighted firms, dozens of other lithium miners and battery producers around the world enable the rapid growth of electric vehicles.
- The Global X Lithium & Battery Tech ETF (LIT) invests in the full lithium cycle, from mining and refining the metal through battery production.
