# VinFast

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-07-19

**Source:** [VinFast to break ground on NC plant, first in North America | Raleigh News & Observer](https://www.newsobserver.com/news/business/article277457133.html)  ·  `page_000504`

## Facts

- VinFast announced in March 2022 that it would open an electric vehicle assembly plant in North Carolina.
- The battery manufacturing plant will be built in Chatham County.
- The plant is expected to eventually create 7,500 jobs.
- It is the largest economic development announcement in the state’s history.
- On July 28, VinFast will launch formal construction of its planned $4 billion electric vehicle production and assembly plant.
- The plant is located near the small Chatham County town of Moncure, about 30 miles southwest of Raleigh.
- VinFast anticipates the plant will initially produce 150,000 vehicles a year.
- The target opening for the plant is in 2025.
- VinFast Auto CEO Le Thi Thu Thuy stated that when operations begin, the factory will be VinFast’s primary supplier of electric vehicles to the North American market, allowing them to optimize production and business activities.
- VinFast was founded in 2017.
- VinFast is a subsidiary of the Vietnamese conglomerate Vingroup.
- Vingroup is owned by the wealthiest man in Southeast Asia.
- Since pivoting to a fully electric car lineup in 2021, VinFast has spent billions pursuing global ambitions.
- The plant is expected to cover approximately 1,800 acres.
- A zoning permit application filed last week to the Chatham County Planning Department projects the site’s initial phase will include almost 3 million square feet of space.
- VinFast represents a milestone for North Carolina officials who have repeatedly tried to recruit a major automaker to the state.
- In recent decades, North Carolina attempted to land Volkswagen, BMW, Volvo, Mercedes, and a joint Toyota-Mazda plant, but each selected nearby southeastern states.
- In March 2022, North Carolina and VinFast officials announced their partnership.
- The automaker has experienced setbacks, including poor reviews for its first U.S. car and a delay in its Chatham plant timeline.
- Around 350 VinFast cars have been delivered to U.S. customers, according to a company spokesperson.
- By 2028, VinFast has committed to create 7,500 area jobs.
- Between the state and Chatham County, the company is set up to receive incentives worth $1.25 billion if it meets its hiring and investment goals.
- Construction will be divided into two phases, with Phase 1 focused on car production and Phase 2 on manufacturing electric vehicle batteries.
- The project has received basic permits to begin Phase 1 construction.
- On Feb. 9, the North Carolina Department of Environmental Quality issued VinFast an air quality permit.
- The state has also approved multiple permits for erosion and sediment control plans.
- DEQ is still reviewing VinFast’s applications related to water quality and buffers.
- The company is in the process of applying for a dam safety permit for its stormwater ponds.
