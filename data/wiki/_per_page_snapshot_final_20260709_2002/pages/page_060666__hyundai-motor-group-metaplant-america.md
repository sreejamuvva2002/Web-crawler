# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-08-29

**Source:** [HMGMA Announces Leadership Transition - HMGMA](https://www.hmgma.com/2025/08/29/hmgma-announces-leadership-transition/)  ·  `page_060666`

## Facts

- Oscar (Ohchung) Kwon, president and CEO of Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA), will retire at the end of August after 33 years with Hyundai Motor Company.
- Kwon oversaw the establishment of HMGMA's new manufacturing plant and initiated automobile production in October 2024 with the Hyundai IONIQ 5.
- Production continued in March with the addition of the Hyundai IONIQ 9.
- Hyundai Motor Company appointed Tony Heo as the new president and CEO of HMGMA, effective September 2.
- Heo joined Hyundai Motor Company in 1995 and has demonstrated leadership in manufacturing operations, strategic planning, and production coordination.
- Since 2021, Heo has served as executive coordinator of production at Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Alabama.
- Heo was on the team that selected the Ellabell, GA megasite as the location for HMGMA.
- Heo majored in mechanical engineering at Pusan National University in Busan, South Korea.
- Chris Susock, Chief Manufacturing Officer of Hyundai Motor North America, stated that Kwon's leadership laid the foundation for the future of the Metaplant.
- Susock expressed excitement about welcoming Heo to the "Meta Pro family" at HMGMA.
- HMGMA is Hyundai Motor Group's first dedicated electrified vehicle mass-production plant, located in Bryan County, GA.
- The plant features a highly connected, automated, and flexible manufacturing system that connects all elements of the EV ecosystem to realize customer value.
- The Georgia facility will become an intelligent manufacturing plant.
- All production processes—including order collection, procurement, logistics, and production—are optimized utilizing AI and data.
- The innovative manufacturing system creates a human-centered work environment where robots assist human workers.
