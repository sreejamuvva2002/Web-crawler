# Volvo Group Powertrain Operations

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2022-10-10

**Source:** [President Joe Biden visits Volvo plant in Maryland | Trucks, Parts, Service](https://www.truckpartsandservice.com/business/article/15301316/president-joe-biden-visits-volvo-plant-in-maryland)  ·  `page_014211`

## Facts

- President Joe Biden visited the Volvo Group Powertrain Operations facility in Hagerstown, Md., for National Manufacturing Day.
- During the event, Biden met with Volvo Group executives, toured the facility, and spoke with employees.
- Martin Weissburg is chairman of Volvo Group North America and Mack Trucks president.
- The Hagerstown site provides advanced transportation technologies to meet customer needs today and in the future.
- Over the past 10 years, the Volvo Group has invested nearly $320 million in the Hagerstown site.
- The Hagerstown campus produces engines, transmissions, and axles for Mack Trucks, Volvo Trucks, Prevost motorcoaches, and Volvo Buses sold in North America.
- The facility recently began production of Modular Power Boxes (MPB).
- The MPBs are at the heart of the Group’s new zero-emission battery-electric vehicles.
- The zero-emission battery-electric vehicle models include the Volvo VNR Electric and Mack LR Electric.
- President Biden toured the MPB subassembly, assembly, and testing areas while on campus.
- Marcus Minkkinen is vice president and general manager at the Volvo Group Powertrain Operations in Hagerstown.
- The company states that the visit highlights the importance of manufacturing to the U.S. economy and helps encourage people to pursue a career in manufacturing.
- The site offers great jobs in a modernized facility that is transforming the transportation industry.
