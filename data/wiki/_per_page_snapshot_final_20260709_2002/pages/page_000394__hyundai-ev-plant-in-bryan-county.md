# Hyundai EV Plant in Bryan County

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-02-17

**Source:** [Hyundai EV plant in Bryan County to be 3x size of Montgomery and other updates from SEDA](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/hyundai-ev-plant-bryan-county-193747675.html)  ·  `page_000394`

## Facts

- Construction of the incoming Hyundai EV plant is underway as of Jan. 9, with the clearing portion completed.
- Clearing of the nearly 3,000-acre track of land in Bryan County began last August.
- The $5.5 billion manufacturing facility near Savannah was announced last year.
- Plans include employing 8,100 workers between an electric vehicle assembly plant and an adjacent EV battery factory.
- It is the largest single economic development project in Georgia history.
- Initial plans called for manufacturing around 300,000 vehicles a year once the assembly facility comes online around January 2025.
- The facility will have the capacity to manufacture 500,000 vehicles, nearly doubling the initial estimate.
- Hyundai's plant in Montgomery, Ala., has the capacity to produce nearly 400,000 vehicles per year.
- Hyundai has operated a 3.4-million square-foot manufacturing plant in Montgomery, Alabama, since 2005.
- The Montgomery facility cost $1.8 billion and was Hyundai's first manufacturing plant in the U.S. prior to the Bryan County announcement.
- The Montgomery plant resides on 1,744 acres of land.
- The Bryan County facility will be three times the size of the Montgomery plant, with the estimate only containing the assembly plant and not the battery plant.
- The adjacent EV battery factory will start being constructed in the spring.
- An announcement on a battery partner is expected in the coming months.
- Former state senator and one-time gubernatorial candidate Eric Johnson will join the Savannah JDA as project director.
- Eric Johnson is looking into doing a housing study for South Korean families moving to the area.
- New Hampstead will be ground zero for future housing developments, with more need and opportunities expected.
- Pooler is popular with South Korean families who are already here.
- Around 100 South Korean families have come so far, and another 100 are expected to take up positions at the Hyundai plant.
- A site for the Quick Start Center has been selected on Highway 280.
- The Quick Start Center facility will cost $70 million and will be the home for training the local workforce for the incoming EV plant.
- Suppliers have already begun to move into the region as the megasite is built out.
- Projected supplier jobs total more than 3,400 between the four suppliers currently in the area.
- The Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center played a big role in getting suppliers for Hyundai to come to the area.
- Igneo Tech, which resides at the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center, has 150 new jobs coming to the region.
