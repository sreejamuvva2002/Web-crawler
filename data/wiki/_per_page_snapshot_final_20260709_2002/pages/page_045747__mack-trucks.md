## Facts

- Mack trucks like the Anthem, Granite, LR, and Pinnacle are heavyweights in the trucking industry, serving an array of heavy-duty applications across the construction, transport, and vocational segments.
- With roots dating back to 1890, the Mack Trucks brand has a long history of motor vehicle innovation.
- Mack’s first successful vehicle was the Mack bus, a 40-horsepower (30-kilowatt) vehicle with a 20-passenger capacity introduced in 1900.
- After converting the Mack bus into a truck, the company found a new path as a leading innovator of truck technology, introducing modern truck staples like advanced air cleaners and oil filters, power brakes, and shock absorbers.
- In 2017, Mack introduced the Anthem, which replaced the Pinnacle axle back model.
- The Anthem is a Class 8, on-highway truck designed with aerodynamics in mind, featuring roof fairings, hood, bumper, cover tow loops, air dam, and close-out flange to reduce wind resistance.
- Mack equips the Anthem with features designed to improve fuel efficiency, such as its Predictive Cruise Control with Econo-Roll and the HE+ fuel efficiency package.
- The HE+ fuel efficiency package works with the powerful Mack MP8HE engine and Mack’s Energy Recovery Technology to capture waste energy from the engine’s exhaust and convert it into additional torque.
- The Granite is engineered for construction and vocational truck markets, with buyers able to select day cab trucks pre-fitted with standard mixer, bridge mixer, lift axle dump, conventional dump, rear loader, or hydraulic roll off hoist.
- Mack Granite mixers can include an 11LMP7 or 13LMP8 engine ranging from 325 to 505 HP (242 to 376 kW).
- Mack’s LR is built for the refuse and recycling industries, featuring a large windshield and windows in the door to reduce blind spots.
- The LR Electric is a fully electric Class 8 vehicle designed to help refuse customers meet sustainability goals and provide quieter operation.
- The LR Electric can be fitted with equipment bodies from numerous manufacturers based on customer needs.
- The LR Electric has four lithium-ion batteries and two motors that produce up to 448 HP (334 kW) of continuous power and 4,051 pounds-foot (5,492 newton-meters) of torque.
- Mack introduced the Pinnacle in 2006, replacing the Vision as its leading option for hauling applications.
- The Anthem replaced the axle-back Pinnacle variant in 2017, but the axle-forward Pinnacle continues to be a reliable platform for regional and bulk hauling.
- The Super-Liner was produced in North America from 1977 to 1993, and models can still be found today.
- Mack Trucks Australia continues to manufacture the Super-Liner.
