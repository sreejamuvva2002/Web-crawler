# Vernay Laboratories Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Celebrating 75 Years of Vernay Labs](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20210827005018/en/Celebrating-75-Years-of-Vernay-Labs)  ·  `page_003930`

## Facts

- Atlanta-based Vernay Laboratories Inc. celebrated its 75th anniversary in 2021.
- The company is the world’s leading designer and manufacturer of critical flow-control components for medical, automotive, and other markets.
- Founder Sergius Vernet invented a wax-filled thermostat to control internal combustion engine temperature in the 1930s while working at Antioch College in Yellow Springs, Ohio.
- Vernay Laboratories Inc. was founded in 1946 by Sergius Vernet, adopting a phonetic spelling for easier pronunciation.
- The company's early thermostats were utilized in military airplanes and tanks during World War II.
- In 1944, the U.S. Army awarded Vernay’s plant the Army-Navy “E” Award for excellence in war production, making it one of the smallest companies to receive the honor.
- Sergius Vernet began molding rubber components himself after customers struggled to find suppliers capable of meeting critical thermostat part requirements.
- Vernay Labs currently employs over 800 people globally.
- The privately held firm operates five plants worldwide located in the U.S., the Netherlands, Italy, and China.
- Vernay manufactures more than 1 billion precision molded elastomeric components per year.
- The company utilizes an extensive library of custom formulated elastomeric materials and compounds for its products.
- Founder Sergius Vernet was known in the mid-20th century for hiring African Americans, women, and Japanese Americans into key roles to promote diversity and social justice.
- Sergius Vernet and his wife Suzanne created the philanthropic Vernay Foundation to fund civic institutions in Yellow Springs.
- At the time of his death in 1968 at age 69, Sergius Vernet held more than 100 patents for his inventions.
- Vernay Laboratories has earned 81 patents on product geometries and processes throughout its history.
- Bob Ferguson serves as vice president of global research and development and is a 16-year company veteran.
- Over the past dozen years, Vernay has hired key executives with significant global experience, increased automation, adopted Industry 4.0 protocols, and developed a unified product development approach while continuing to custom formulate its own material compounds.
- Ed Urquhart was named CEO in 2009.
- The company focuses on co-development with customers, assisting in concept generation and fluid/gas flow design for their applications.
- Vernay is currently expanding clean-room space in the U.S. and Italy to serve the medical devices sector.
- The company was designated an “essential supplier” by the U.S. and Italian governments during the COVID-19 pandemic for providing critical ventilator and respirator components.
- Andy Woodward has served as global vice president of sales and marketing since 2010.
- Vernay Laboratories formulates its own compounds to address specific applications, drawing from a library of over 33,000 elastomeric materials and compound formulations.
