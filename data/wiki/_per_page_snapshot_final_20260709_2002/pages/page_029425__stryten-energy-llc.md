# Stryten Energy LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-01-10

**Source:** [Stryten Energy Partners with Snapping Shoals EMC to](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2023/01/10/2586295/0/en/Stryten-Energy-Partners-with-Snapping-Shoals-EMC-to-Demonstrate-VRFB-Applications.html)  ·  `page_029425`

## Facts

- Stryten Energy LLC, a U.S.-based energy storage solutions provider headquartered in Alpharetta, Georgia, is partnering with Snapping Shoals EMC to demonstrate its advanced vanadium redox flow battery (VRFB) technology for energy storage and deployment uses.
- The partnership will evaluate where the VRFB technology will provide maximum benefits.
- Power consumption is projected to triple by 2050 due in part to electrification.
- Renewable energy production will need to scale up nine to 13 times in the next 30 years to meet increasing energy demand.
- A variety of battery technologies will be necessary for long-duration energy storage capacity resulting from increased renewable production.
- Demand for energy storage is expected to accelerate following the passage of the Inflation Reduction Act in August 2022.
- The Inflation Reduction Act includes Investment Tax Credits for domestically sourced and manufactured standalone clean energy storage intended to lower costs, increase energy efficiency, and strengthen energy security.
- Scott Childers, Vice President of Stryten Energy’s Essential Power Division, stated the collaboration will provide a deeper understanding of how VRFB technology operates in real-world conditions.
- Testing the battery energy storage system against a variety of usage needs will help identify where it can make the most impact for energy providers.
- VRFB technology is ideally suited to provide medium and long-duration energy storage to ensure grid stability and facilitate increased utilization of renewables for businesses and consumers across the U.S.
- These systems are independently scalable in power and capacity, with scalability potentially leading to substantial cost reduction as discharge time increases.
- Stryten Energy’s VRFB technology features domestically sourced vanadium electrolyte that is easily recycled.
- The VRFB technology offers increased power density and reduced battery downtime.
- The technology has the potential for limitless cycle life with proper maintenance.
- The systems feature high-temperature ratings and are thermally safe.
- The batteries are scalable to meet varying energy storage capacity needs.
- Snapping Shoals EMC provides electricity to approximately 100,000 homes and businesses across an eight-county area in Georgia including Newton, Henry, Rockdale, DeKalb, Butts, Walton, Jasper, and Morgan counties.
- Stryten Energy’s stored energy technologies include advanced lead, lithium, and vanadium redox flow batteries, intelligent chargers, and energy performance management software.
- Stryten Energy is backed by more than a century of expertise in delivering energy solutions for sectors including Transportation, Military, and Government.
