# Northeast Georgia Inland Port

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Georgia Announces New Inland Terminal Location - Informed Infrastructure](https://informedinfrastructure.com/43962/georgia-announces-new-inland-terminal-location/)  ·  `page_016248`

## Facts

- Gov. Nathan Deal, along with representatives of the Georgia Ports Authority, the Greater Hall Chamber and Norfolk Southern Railroad announced plans to make Hall County the site of the GPA's next inland port.
- The Northeast Georgia Inland Port will be situated in the heart of the manufacturing and logistics corridor along Interstate 85.
- Hall County and the surrounding region are an important area for the production of heavy equipment, food and forest products.
- The inland port will act as an economic development tool, drawing new investment from business and industry to Hall and its surrounding counties.
- Norfolk Southern will provide service on a direct rail route to and from the Port of Savannah's Garden City Terminal for handling both import and export containers at the Gainesville terminal.
- Jeff Heller serves as Vice President Intermodal & Automotive at Norfolk Southern.
- Hall County and the surrounding region in Northeast Georgia are key areas of expansion in the state for manufacturers and distributors.
- The GPA's inland port at Gainesville combined with Norfolk Southern's rail service will provide crucial links in the supply chains of local industries, consumers, and the rest of the world.
- The project will serve as a catalyst for new opportunities for industrial development.
- Improved access to rail at the new terminal will increase logistics options and overall efficiency while reducing congestion on Georgia highways.
- Currently, containers moving by truck travel a 600-mile roundtrip to and from the Port of Savannah.
- When the new rail yard opens, drivers will be able to make shorter trips from area manufacturers and distribution facilities to the Northeast Georgia site.
- Griff Lynch serves as Executive Director of the Georgia Ports Authority.
- The new Gainesville location is part of the Network Georgia initiative, providing new and existing port customers with additional logistics options.
- The new rail hub will allow importers and exporters to move loaded containers to the coast with greater efficiency.
- The facility will provide a ready source of empty containers for Georgia exports.
- Jimmy Allgood serves as GPA Board Chairman.
- The new cargo handling option will transform the local rail market and help port customers optimize their supply chains.
- The inland terminals are bringing ports and producers closer together, providing new economic opportunities.
- Georgians will benefit from increased employment options as more companies expand or locate in the region.
- Georgia Ports currently support more than 440,000 jobs across every corner of the state.
- The new terminal is located in the Gateway Industrial Centre on GA 365 and covers 104 acres.
- The facility's services will draw from a 1.5-million population in Hall, Gwinnett and surrounding counties.
- Tim Evans serves as vice president of economic development for the Greater Hall Chamber of Commerce.
- The new inland port terminal will shorten the supply chain for many manufacturers, processors and distributors in the region.
- Rail deliveries to and from the Port of Savannah can shorten truck delivery times from approximately seven hours to less than 30 minutes.
- The facility provides direct access to the Port of Savannah, which is the fastest growing container port in the U.S.
- Auto Metal Direct, a worldwide distributor of auto body panels and trim for classic cars and trucks, announced its intent to open a new 318,000 square-foot distribution and fulfillment center.
- Construction on Auto Metal Direct's new facility will soon begin in the Gateway Industrial Centre.
- The $15 million Auto Metal Direct development will bring 40 jobs to Hall County upon completion.
- Mark Headrick serves as President of Auto Metal Direct.
- Auto Metal Direct receives hundreds of containers each year, making Georgia Ports Authority services essential for its business.
- The proximity of the new inland port was an influencing factor in Auto Metal Direct's decision to locate its new building there.
- Auto Metal Direct expects quicker service, lower costs, and ease of movement at its new location.
- Pat Wilson serves as Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner.
- The Georgia Ports Authority acts as a critical economic development partner and catalyst for growth across the state.
- The ports have positioned Georgia as a global leader in logistics infrastructure and helped locate new projects and industry expansions across the state.
- The expansion of Auto Metal Direct will further bolster the strength of the workforce in the community.
- The Northeast Georgia Inland Port will create exciting economic opportunities for the community and region.
- The Northeast Georgia Inland Port is the second Network Georgia-related announcement of the year.
- The GPA held a grand opening for the Appalachian Regional Port on Aug. 22 on a 42-acre site just north of Chatsworth, Ga., in Murray County.
- The Hall County inland terminal is scheduled to be complete in 2021.
- At full build-out, the terminal will have the capacity to handle up to 150,000 containers per year.
- Phil Sutton serves as Vice President Administration at Kubota Manufacturing of America Corporation.
- The Georgia Ports Authority has supported Kubota for many years by helping reduce lead times for in- and outbound shipments.
- Using the Port of Savannah provides Kubota a cost-effective alternative to West Coast ports.
- Kubota anticipates potential cost savings from the new inland port, including reduced chassis and container fees and reduced FTZ administrative fees.
- Kubota expects reduced lead times due to greater availability of empty containers and shorter transit times to and from the inland port.
- Kubota sees huge potential to significantly reduce reliance on OTR motor carriers and reduce lead times for inbound parts and outbound finished goods.

- The proximity of Georgia poultry producers to the port gives the industry a competitive advantage for efficient and cost-effective overseas delivery.
- The Georgia Ports Authority's investment in infrastructure improves poultry shipment service, with the new inland rail yard continuing to enhance connectivity for the Georgia poultry industry.

- Tatsumi Intermodal USA's business model involves appointing container equipment and negotiating trucking rates between Hall County, Atlanta, Savannah, and Charleston.

- The terminal's use will simultaneously reduce the number of tractor trailers on interstates.
