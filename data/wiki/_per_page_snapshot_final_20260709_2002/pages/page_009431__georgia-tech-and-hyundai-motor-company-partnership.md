# Georgia Tech and Hyundai Motor Company Partnership

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-08-08

**Source:** [Georgia Tech and Hyundai Announce Multi-Decade Partnership | News Center](https://news.gatech.edu/news/2023/08/08/georgia-tech-and-hyundai-announce-multi-decade-partnership)  ·  `page_009431`

## Facts

- Georgia Tech and Hyundai Motor Company announced a multi-decade partnership as part of Hyundai’s investments in the state of Georgia.
- The partnership vision includes research and applications to support sustainable mobility, hydrogen economy, workforce development, and smart cities.
- More details about the partnership will be announced in the coming months.
- Hyundai is investing $5.54 billion to develop the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- The new plant is located in Bryan County, Georgia.
- The facility will be a dedicated electric vehicle and battery plant.
- The plant will create more than 8,100 direct jobs.
- Hyundai created a $120,000 STEM scholarship at Georgia Tech when it broke ground on the site in October 2022.
- The partnership includes field-naming recognition for Bobby Dodd Stadium, which will now be known as Bobby Dodd Stadium at Hyundai Field.
- Sponsorship elements feature enhancements to boost the gameday experience for fans in and around the stadium.
- Georgia Tech President Ángel Cabrera stated that Hyundai is a global brand synonymous with quality, innovation, and advancing technology, and expressed gratitude for Hyundai's transformative investments in the state.
- Cabrera noted he is proud the Hyundai brand will feature prominently on campus and looks forward to working with Hyundai leaders to develop exceptional leaders and produce ideas shaping the automotive industry and advancing mobility.
- José Muñoz, president and global COO of Hyundai Motor Company and president and CEO of Hyundai and Genesis Motor North America, stated that proximity to institutions like Georgia Tech was one of the reasons Hyundai selected Georgia for its new EV manufacturing facility.
- Muñoz added the partnership will include opportunities for student professional development, cooperative work programs, and athletic engagements.
- J Batt, director of Athletics at Georgia Tech, stated the partnership will be truly transformative for Georgia Tech Athletics and expressed appreciation to Muñoz and his team.
- The partnership provides a vital pathway for Georgia Tech to expand its stature as a leading research entity in automotive electrification through world-class hydrogen research.
- The partnership offers an opportunity for Georgia Tech to contribute to the state of Georgia’s commitment as a hub for electric vehicle production.
- The deal generates new revenue for Georgia Tech Athletics to provide resources for student-athletes and teams and continue delivering a world-class experience for students, alumni, and fans.
- Bobby Dodd Stadium at Hyundai Field is the oldest on-campus stadium in NCAA Division I’s Football Bowl Subdivision and opened in 1913 as Grant Field before being renamed in 1988.
- The historic Grant Field name will continue to be memorialized with a display at the stadium.
- Legends, which has managed corporate partnerships and multimedia rights for Georgia Tech Athletics since 2021, helped facilitate the athletics partnership with Hyundai.
- Georgia Tech Athletics fields 400-plus student-athletes across 17 varsity sports and competes at the highest level of intercollegiate athletics as a member of NCAA Division I and the Atlantic Coast Conference.
- Hyundai Motor Company was established in 1967, is present in over 200 countries, and employs more than 120,000 people.
- Hyundai is accelerating its transformation into a Smart Mobility Solution Provider and invests in advanced technologies such as robotics and Advanced Air Mobility (AAM).
- Hyundai will continue introducing zero emission vehicles equipped with industry-leading hydrogen fuel cell and EV technologies.
- Legends was founded in 2008 and operates six divisions worldwide to provide data-and-analytics-fueled service solutions for sports, entertainment, and leisure clients.
