# American Honda Motor Co., Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-06-20

**Source:** [Honda Honors 54 Suppliers in North America for Outstanding Performance](https://hondanews.com/en-US/releases/honda-honors-54-suppliers-in-north-america-for-outstanding-performance)  ·  `page_015581`

## Facts

- Honda honored 54 North American suppliers for outstanding performance in supplying parts and materials during 2021.
- Honda purchased $27 billion in parts from mass production suppliers in North America in 2021.
- Honda purchased more than $1 billion in parts from Service Parts suppliers in North America.
- The supplier purchases supported the sales of more than 1.3 million Honda and Acura vehicles in North America in 2021.
- Honda recognized its suppliers with awards for over 35 years during its annual Honda Supplier Conference, which was conducted as a virtual event in 2022 for the second consecutive year.
- Honda recognized 35 suppliers for outstanding performance in providing parts and materials for mass production operations in 2021.
- Mass Production Supplier Awards were given in four categories: quality & delivery, value, innovation, and sustainability.
- For the first time, Honda recognized one supplier with an award for Distinguished Performance.
- Mike Lapham, general manager of procurement at Honda Development & Manufacturing of America, stated that working together as one team with suppliers has been critical to Honda in the face of industrywide supply chain disruptions during the past year.
- Mike Lapham noted that supplier teamwork will serve as a springboard to future success as they enter the electrified era.
- DENSO International America, Inc. of Southfield, Michigan received the Distinguished Performance Award for exceptional performance in cost, quality, and delivery.
- Aisin World Corporation of America of Marion, Illinois received the Supplier Sustainability Award for commitment to social responsibility, compliance, ethics, environmental governance, diversity, health and safety, and trade compliance.
- Honda does business with 692 OEM suppliers across North America which provide parts to the company’s 18 plants in the region that produce Honda and Acura cars and light trucks and the engines and transmissions that power them.
- Honda honored 19 suppliers for excellent performance in providing service parts for customers after purchasing Honda or Acura products.
- Service Parts supplier awards reflect a supplier’s combined performance in quality, cost, and delivery.
- Mark Wiles, assistant vice president of the Parts Division at American Honda Motor Co., Inc., stated that nothing is more important to Honda than serving customer needs and appreciates supplier efforts during the challenging business environment.
- Mark Wiles noted they are working more closely with suppliers than ever before to overcome supply issues and meet customer needs.
- Honda does business with nearly 1,200 service parts suppliers in North America to provide service and accessory parts for more than 20 million Honda and Acura automobiles in operation in the United States.
- Honda manages a national network of parts and distribution facilities that deliver high-quality and value service parts to Honda and Acura dealers nationwide as well as supporting global parts operations.
- Honda established operations in North America in 1959.
- Today, the company employs more than 40,000 associates in the region engaged in development, manufacturing, sales and service support of Honda and Acura automobiles, Honda power equipment, Honda powersports products and the HondaJet Elite S advanced lightjet.
- Based on its longstanding commitment to build products close to the customer, Honda operates 18 major manufacturing plants in North America, working with nearly 800 suppliers in the region to produce a diverse range of quality products.
- Honda has built automobiles in North America for 40 years and, in 2021, more than 95% of all Honda and Acura automobiles sold in the U.S. were produced in North America.
- Honda also operates 21 North American research and development facilities that fully design, develop and engineer many of the company’s North American-made products.
