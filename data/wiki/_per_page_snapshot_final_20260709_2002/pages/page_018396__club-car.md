# Club Car

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Club Car - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Club_Car)  ·  `page_018396`

## Facts

- Club Car is an American manufacturer of electric and gas-powered golf carts and small utility vehicles for personal and commercial use.
- Founded in 1958, the company is headquartered in Evans, Georgia.
- It is one of the largest employers in Columbia County.
- The company was acquired by Platinum Equity in 2021.
- Prior to acquisition, it operated as a business unit in Ingersoll Rand corporation's Industrial Technologies division.
- Club Car was founded in 1958 in Houston, Texas, under the name Landreath Machine.
- In 1962, entrepreneur Bill Stevens Jr. purchased the company and relocated it to Augusta, Georgia, renaming it Club Car.
- The company has maintained its global headquarters in Augusta since 1962.
- In 1978, a group of eight former EZGO executives acquired Club Car, positioning it as a local competitor in the golf cart manufacturing industry.
- William Dolan, co-founder of EZGO, was among the new leadership.
- Club Car was acquired by Ingersoll Rand in 1995 and became part of its Infrastructure division.
- Club Car was later merged with Bobcat to form a Compact Vehicles Technology division that expanded offerings into utility and commercial vehicles.
- In 2017, Ingersoll Rand purchased GPS Industries, which provided fleet management systems for Club Car since 2009.
- In 2021, Platinum Equity acquired Club Car from Ingersoll Rand in an all-cash transaction valued at $1.68 billion.
- Nearly a year later, Club Car announced and completed the acquisition of Garia, a Danish low-speed electric vehicle manufacturer, including its subsidiary Melex from Poland.
- The Garia acquisition marked the company's first acquisition under Platinum Equity.
- The deal extended the company's reach into street-legal Neighborhood Electric Vehicles (NEVs) and lightweight utility vehicles.
- In addition to its global corporate headquarters, Club Car operates two other Georgia-based facilities: a distribution center and a production facility.
- The company also operates facilities in Mielec, Poland and JiaXing, China.
- In June 2025, Craig Scanlon was named President and CEO of Club Car.
- Club Car produces vehicles for personal use, business and commercial applications, and golf operations.
- The company's first product was a three-wheeled golf cart introduced in 1958.
- It is regarded as an industry leader involved in many innovations, including producing one of the first street-legal golf carts.
- The DS line of golf carts began in 1980, named after designer Dom Saporito, and remained a hallmark until 2004 when the Precedent line was introduced.
- The company revealed the Precedent i2 to critical acclaim, and again in 2008 with an all-new drive system.
- A partnership with GPSI in 2008 produced the world's first connected golf cart (Precedent i3), featuring a system to connect an entire golf fleet to the clubhouse and options like score tracking.
- The Precedent i2, i3, and 4Fun were used by golf courses, while the i2 was available for personal use.
- The Club Car Tempo vehicle launched in 2018, replacing the Precedent.
- In 2017, the company launched the first of its Onward series of personal transportation vehicles, introducing online customization for accessories and color.
- The initial Onward launch featured 2-passenger, 4-passenger, and 4-passenger lifted vehicles, with 6-passenger and lithium-ion options added later.
- In 2025, Club Car announced its most advanced personal vehicle yet, the Onward LSV (Low-Speed Vehicle).
- The Onward LSV standard equipment includes a seven-inch touchscreen display, four-wheel brakes, an Occupant Protection System with reinforced frame, seatbelts, side mirrors, turn signals, DOT-approved glass windshield, back-up camera, and an Acoustic Vehicle Alerting System (AVAS).
- Advanced testing for the Onward LSV included automotive style brake performance tests and roof crush tests.
- Beginning in 2008 and 2009, Club Car entered the utility vehicles market with its XRT line of personal utility vehicles.
- The XRT line ranged from the XRT800 4x2 UTV to XRT1550 4x4 UTV, with the ability to add work attachments.
- Club Car offers street-legal golf carts with automotive features such as seat belts, turn signals, windshields, and more.
- These vehicles, UTVs, and LSVs were manufactured and branded for other companies, making Club Car the OEM.
- In 1985, the company created vehicles targeted at business applications with its Carryall II, aimed at manufacturing facilities, college campuses, and golf course operations.
- In 2014, the company re-launched its commercial utility vehicle line with improved efficiency and a new line of accessories.
- Two new types of vehicles were developed in 2009 and 2010: street-legal low-speed vehicles (LSV) and vehicles for transporting multiple people at resorts, venues, and campuses.
- In April 2024, Club Car celebrated a new manufacturing facility in Appling, Georgia, with Gov. Brian Kemp in attendance.
- Club Car opened a second factory in Poland to cope with increased demand in April 2024.
