# Stryten Energy LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Energy, Automobile, EV, Renewable News | Support from Stryten Energy Enables Rivers of Recovery to Expand Veteran Rehabilitation Programs](https://oilandgaspress.com/support-from-stryten-energy-enables-rivers-of-recovery-to-expand-veteran-rehabilitation-programs/)  ·  `page_050525`

## Facts

- Stryten Energy LLC is headquartered in Alpharetta, Georgia.
- Stryten Energy provides a broad range of energy storage solutions across the Essential Power, Motive Power, Transportation, Military, and Government sectors.
- Stryten Energy powers applications including submarines, subcompacts, microgrids, warehouses, distribution centers, cars, trains, and trucks.
- Stryten's stored energy technologies include advanced lead, lithium, and vanadium redox flow batteries, intelligent chargers, and energy performance management software.
- Stryten Energy has more than a century of expertise in delivering top-performing energy solutions for today and tomorrow.
- Mike Judd serves as the Chief Executive Officer and President of Stryten Energy.
- At Stryten Energy, our commitment to supporting military veterans remains unwavering, according to CEO Mike Judd.
- Stryten Energy provided a generous financial donation for the fifth year in a row to support wounded U.S. combat veterans participating in Rivers of Recovery programs.
- The donation directly supports combat veterans suffering post-traumatic stress, mild traumatic brain injury, or other psychological wounds of war by allowing them to go through RoR’s outdoor rehabilitation program at no cost.
- As part of the sponsorship, veteran employees from Stryten Energy will have the opportunity to participate in several fly-fishing trips as volunteers alongside RoR guides.
- For the second consecutive year, Stryten Energy facilitated an additional generous donation on top of its annual donation after winning a charity cornhole tournament for the second year in a row.
- Amy Simon is the Executive Director of Rivers of Recovery.
- Thanks to Stryten’s extraordinary contribution for 2025, RoR is poised to dramatically expand its impact this year.
- Stryten's 2024 donation funded a groundbreaking research partnership with Cornell University on outdoor therapy effectiveness and enabled program expansions across California, Arkansas, Connecticut, Wisconsin, New York, and Idaho.
- Stryten's support also helped conduct river trips, launch all-women veterans’ retreats in Wisconsin, and support a landmark Catalina Island experience that served 40 California veterans.
- RoR plans to expand its program in 2025 by adding additional trips to California, Arkansas, Connecticut, Wisconsin, New York, and Idaho.
- RoR is advancing a pioneering collaboration with Cornell University on outdoor therapy research, enhancing the evidence base for nature-based interventions in veteran rehabilitation and suicide prevention.
- RoR is developing innovative technology to measure program outcomes related to resilience and protective factors against suicide.
- RoR is exploring the return to states that previously operated successful programs, including Oregon, Washington, and Florida.
- RoR is evaluating opportunities to establish new programs in states like Arizona.
- RoR is expanding its women veterans initiative by building on the success of women-led Wisconsin retreats and looking at other locations.
- In 2024, RoR hosted its first women’s event in Arkansas.
- RoR's 2024 success included eight hunting trips across Illinois, New York, and California, including a Catalina Island experience, Stockton archery group, and a second New York turkey hunt.
- Stryten is the platinum sponsor for RoR’s Stars and Stripes charity fishing tournament to be held June 5-6, 2025, in Newburg, MD.
- Rivers of Recovery was founded in 2008 by Dan T. Cook to help combat veterans overcome the invisible scars of war.
- Rivers of Recovery utilizes a medically designed curriculum coupled with outdoor recreational activities to treat combat veterans suffering with Post Traumatic Stress (PTS), mild Traumatic Brain Injury (mTBI), stress, anxiety, and depression.
