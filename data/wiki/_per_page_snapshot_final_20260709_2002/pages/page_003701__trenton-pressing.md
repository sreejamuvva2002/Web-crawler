# Trenton Pressing

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Manufacturer Connects to New Technology and Strategic Customers | NIST](https://www.nist.gov/mep/successstories/2024/manufacturer-connects-new-technology-and-strategic-customers)  ·  `page_003701`

## Facts

- In 2020, the current owners purchased the company with the intent to modernize, streamline, and make the company more profitable, while also maintaining jobs for the existing workforce.
- COO Hans Pfunder met John Zegers, Georgia Manufacturing Extension Partnership (GaMEP) at Georgia Tech’s Northwest Georgia region manager, and explained their plans.
- GaMEP at Georgia Tech is part of the MEP National Network™ and helped the company become a better manufacturer to offer its capabilities to others.
- Zegers connected the Trenton Pressing team with Sean Madhavaraman, a GaMEP technology project manager, who saw an opportunity to connect legacy machinery to a Manufacturing Execution System (MES) using sensors and other Industry 4.0 technologies.
- The company had various types of stamping presses, robotic welding cells, and other machinery that needed to be monitored in real-time to understand output, efficiency, and downtime.
- Pfunder worked with Madhavaraman to create a roadmap to connect the machinery and train employees on how to use the new data.
- The engineering and maintenance teams installed new monitoring equipment and utilized software to allow real-time data tracking, helping predict equipment failure before it happened.
- The software was also used to optimize production planning and improve employee experience.
- Improvements allowed the company to ship substantially more units and freed up additional capacity to take on new customers.
- Director of business development Marco Linner told Zegers about an idea to diversify their business by taking on more complex, lower-volume projects that could develop into long-term partnerships.
- Within a week, Zegers learned of an agricultural equipment company in Sandersville, Georgia, looking for a contract manufacturer.
- That company had been working on product design with Bob Wray, a GaMEP technology project manager, and asked for help sourcing a manufacturing partner who could jump in quickly to start production.
- Zegers connected the two companies, as it sounded like a perfect fit for Trenton Pressing.
- Linner met with the company and found that the product was indeed the type of business opportunity they were seeking.
- The product is now in production at Trenton Pressing.
- The first units were delivered just three months after their initial introduction, which was ahead of schedule.
- The partnership is going well and both companies hope to produce more products together in the future.
