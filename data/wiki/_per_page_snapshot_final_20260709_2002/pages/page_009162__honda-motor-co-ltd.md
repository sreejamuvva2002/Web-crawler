# Honda Motor Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-05-14

**Source:** [Summary of 2026 Honda Business Briefing | Honda Global Corporate Website](https://global.honda/en/newsroom/news/2026/c260514beng.html)  ·  `page_009162`

## Facts

- Honda Director, President and Representative Executive Officer (Global CEO) Toshihiro Mibe held a press briefing on May 14, 2026, regarding initiatives toward rebuilding its automobile business.
- Honda will focus on rebuilding its automobile business structure over the next three years to achieve consolidated operating profit of more than 1.4 trillion yen by the fiscal year ending March 31, 2029.
- Honda is strategically reallocating corporate resources and prioritizing North America, Japan, India, and China as priority regions for future growth.
- Starting in 2027, Honda will launch next-generation hybrid models featuring an all-new hybrid system and platform.
- Honda plans to launch 15 next-generation hybrid models globally by the end of the fiscal year ending March 31, 2030, primarily in North America.
- In North America, Honda will launch large-size hybrid models in the D-segment or above in 2029.
- Honda presented prototypes of its next-generation hybrid models: the Honda Hybrid Sedan Prototype and the Acura Hybrid SUV Prototype, scheduled for sale within the next two years.
- Honda aims to reduce the cost of its next-generation hybrid system by more than 30% compared to the hybrid system introduced in 2023.
- Honda will improve the fuel economy of next-generation hybrid models by more than 10% by combining the new hybrid system with a next-generation platform and newly-developed electric AWD unit.
- Honda is developing next-generation ADAS with plans for market launch in 2028, applying it to more than 15 models over five years.
- Honda will reallocate all excess capacity at its auto plants in Ohio to gasoline and hybrid vehicle production.
- Honda will make all of its auto plants in North America capable of producing hybrid models.
- Honda and joint venture partner LG Energy Solution will convert part of the EV battery production lines at L-H Battery Company to hybrid battery production.
- Honda will increase the local content of assemblies (ASSY) and component parts of motors and inverters by more than four times the current level in North America to prepare for hybrid vehicle expansion and mitigate U.S. tariff impacts.
- Honda will pursue a battery procurement strategy with a focus on competitiveness in North America.
- Honda will indefinitely suspend the project to build a comprehensive EV value chain in Canada.
- Honda will not pursue complete in-house battery sourcing at this time, instead fully utilizing L-H Battery facilities and producing batteries for hybrid vehicles and other applications.
- Honda is laying groundwork for introducing a highly competitive EV hardware platform for the future.
- Honda continues research and development of all-solid-state batteries.
- Honda will apply its ASIMO OS to EVs and hybrid models to increase mobility value.
- Honda adopted a domain-based E&E architecture for EVs and hybrids to flexibly address country-specific differences and market conditions.
- Honda expects EV-related losses to be resolved by the fiscal year ending March 31, 2029.
- Over the three years until the fiscal year ending March 31, 2029, Honda will reallocate resources from EVs to hybrid vehicles and control EV-related investments at approximately 0.8 trillion yen.
- Honda will invest 1.0 trillion yen in software technologies and 4.4 trillion yen in gasoline and hybrid vehicles over the three-year period, totaling 6.2 trillion yen in resource investment.
- Honda expects to generate more than 7 trillion yen in operating cash flow after R&D adjustment by the fiscal year ending March 31, 2029, excluding EV-related losses.
- Honda will maintain stable dividend payments with a target DOE of 3%.
- Honda aims to realize its long-standing ROIC (Return on Invested Capital) target of 10% by the fiscal year ending March 31, 2031.
- Honda will reduce development cost, timeframe, and workload by half compared to 2025 levels through a “Triple Half” approach.
- Honda will improve production efficiency by approximately 20% over the next five years by efficiently executing and allocating resource investments in new models and equipment.
