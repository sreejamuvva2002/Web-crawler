# Thailand Electric Vehicle Ecosystem

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-03

**Source:** [Thailand EV investment tops 137bn baht as BOI targets ASEAN hub](https://www.nationthailand.com/business/automobile/40068192)  ·  `page_060637`

## Facts

- Investment promotion approvals in Thailand's EV industry reached 198 projects worth more than 137 billion baht as of May 2026.
- Approved projects cover the full EV ecosystem, including electric vehicle production, batteries, key components, charging stations, and battery-swapping facilities.
- Narit Therdsteerasukdi serves as secretary-general of the Board of Investment (BOI) and secretary of the National Electric Vehicle Policy Committee (EV Board).
- The battery electric vehicle (BEV) segment accounts for 39.5 billion baht across 18 approved projects.
- Hybrid electric vehicles (HEVs) represent 29.9 billion baht across seven approved projects.
- Plug-in hybrid electric vehicles (PHEVs) account for 9.429 billion baht across seven approved projects.
- Other EV categories, including electric buses and electric motorcycles, represent 3.1 billion baht across 18 approved projects.
- Investment in batteries and energy storage systems totals 33.5 billion baht across 57 approved projects.
- Key EV components, including traction motors, battery management systems, and EV power control systems, account for 12.5 billion baht across 49 approved projects.
- Charging stations and battery-swapping projects account for 9.788 billion baht across 42 approved projects.
- Approved charging projects will support the installation of more than 22,900 charging outlets nationwide, including more than 10,000 fast chargers.
- Most BEV manufacturers granted BOI promotion over the past three to four years have already started production in Thailand.
- Mercedes-Benz was among the first BEV manufacturers to start production in Thailand under BOI promotion.
- Other early BEV manufacturers include Great Wall Motor, SAIC Motor-CP, BYD, AION Automobile, Changan, and EV Primus.
- BMW, Hyundai Mobility, and Omoda & Jaecoo were the most recent BEV manufacturers to start production in Thailand in 2026.
- The BEV manufacturers granted BOI promotion collectively employ more than 16,000 Thai workers.
- The BOI has introduced measures to promote joint ventures between Thai operators and foreign companies to help domestic parts makers adapt to technological change and enter global supply chains.
- The BOI has stepped up activities encouraging the use of locally made parts, including Subcon Thailand and Sourcing Day.
- Over the past two years, the BOI organized 18 business-matching events connecting Thai parts manufacturers with EV makers.
- These business-matching activities have generated more than 1,200 business matches involving more than 800 capable Thai suppliers.
- The BOI expects the business matches to create more than 60 billion baht in local parts procurement.
- These matches are expected to open the way for Thai suppliers to enter next-generation production lines and grow alongside a new wave of EV investment.
- The transition to electric vehicles presents Thailand with an important opportunity to become a center for the region’s EV supply chain and ecosystem.
- Thailand aims to serve as a platform for international cooperation, enabling established and new manufacturers from different countries to build stable business bases and expand in the country.
- The strategy is intended to create opportunities for Thai people through employment, skills development, and the upgrading of local enterprises into new supply chains.
- Thailand’s automotive development strategy has progressed through successive phases, from one-tonne pickup trucks and internal combustion engine vehicles to globally competitive eco-cars.
- The latest phase of Thailand’s automotive development strategy focuses on clean-energy and intelligent vehicles, particularly the development of production bases for various types of EVs.
- The BOI has adjusted its investment promotion measures to cover all vehicle technologies, allowing manufacturers across the industry to invest and grow in Thailand.
- The BOI's approach aims to build a fully connected future automotive supply chain covering vehicle makers, parts manufacturers, batteries, intelligent systems, software, research and development centers, and testing facilities.
- The broader goal of Thailand's EV strategy is to increase the role of Thai enterprises in the global supply chain and support sustainable industrial growth.
- The BOI's approach has helped Thailand maintain its position as the region’s strongest automotive production base and one of the leading bases globally.
- In 2025, all types of electric vehicles together accounted for more than 44% of new vehicle registrations in Thailand.
- Five years earlier, electric vehicles accounted for just 3% of new vehicle registrations in Thailand.
- In 2025, HEVs held the largest EV share at 21.8%, followed by BEVs at 19.6% and PHEVs at 2.9%.
- Figures indicate that different EV technologies can advance together, with hybrids expected to play an important role during the transition toward electric mobility.
