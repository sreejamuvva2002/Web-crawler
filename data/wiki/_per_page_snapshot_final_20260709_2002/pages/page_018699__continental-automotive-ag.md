# Continental Automotive AG

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Continental Automotive AG | IBM](https://www.ibm.com/case-studies/continental-automotive)  ·  `page_018699`

## Facts

- Continental is a major supplier of automotive parts to nearly every car producer in the world.
- Continental is a leader in the autonomous driving intelligence space.
- Its ADAS business unit began developing intelligent sensors and data-driven traffic safety solutions more than 20 years ago.
- The ADAS business unit has been working to increase the speed of development using deep learning and training artificial neural networks.
- Continental’s Vision Zero project aims to virtually eliminate deaths by traffic accident through the development of ADAS technologies.
- Continental improved AI training time by 70% using IBM Storage Scale and NVIDIA DGX systems.
- Continental has the ability to run at least 14x more deep learning experiments per month at the same time.
- Continental’s ADAS Vision Zero initiative employs a test fleet equipped with sensors that drive 15,000 kilometers per day.
- The test fleet generates and records over 100 TB of data daily.
- Data is ingested, processed, selected, assessed, annotated, and used for training and validation of the system.
- The team uses NVIDIA DGX systems for training and validation.
- Continental needs high performance AI processing and access to data plus a powerful storage solution to analyze hundreds of thousands of images per second with NVIDIA GPU computing.
- As automation of driver assistance and vehicle safety systems increased, software complexity grew, as did the number of safety requirements across multiple geographies.
- Continental needed a powerful parallel file system to meet high-speed AI demands and protect sensitive data.
- The solution needed to provide fast random access, support protocols such as SMB and Amazon S3, and provide several different access management options.
- Robert Thiel is Head of Artificial Intelligence, Advanced Driver Assistance Systems (ADAS), at Continental.
- David Enenkel is Head of IT Operations, ADAS, at Continental.
- Balazs Lorand, PhD, is Head of AI Competence Centre, ADAS@Budapest, at Continental.
- Continental established comprehensive testing and evaluated top storage solutions with IBM Business Partner SVA System Vertrieb Alexander GmbH.
- The IBM Storage for Data and AI with NVIDIA DGX solution provided required performance and advantages.
- The parallel high performance architecture and simple-to-scale node deployment provided the AI infrastructure required, with resilience and scalability.
- IBM Storage Scale solution ensures infrastructure will support growth in cloud or on premises.
- IBM has extensive experience in the automotive industry.
- With the new solution, Continental optimized for deep learning with multi-node training, increasing model accuracy for higher safety levels without impacting time to production.
- Continental scaled to a cluster of DGX to handle 14 times more experiments per month.
- The system can test millions of permutations in environmental conditions (rain, snow, sunlight, clouds) or transients (cars moving too close during lane change).
- Continental can now run 20, 40, 80 GPUs simultaneously to speed up training.
- Training time was reduced from weeks to days.
- The solution supports growth in containerized hybrid cloud environments, on premises, and in multiple data centers.
- Continental built a completely new infrastructure in the Frankfurt, Germany AI-ready data center at Equinix.
- SVA implemented the overall integration of the storage solution in the cluster, including installation, deployment, configuration, commissioning, and training.
- The new solution includes a multimode GPU cluster, non-blocking InfiniBand network infrastructure, IBM ESS with fast NVMe drives, NVIDIA DGX systems, and NVIDIA V100 Tensor Core GPUs.
- Continental uses IBM Storage Scale software with its Kubernetes environment for modern application development.
- To meet Euro NCAP five-star rating requirements, Continental needs to keep developing more intelligent products and establish data environments.
- These improvements translate to a strong competitive position for Continental, enabling faster development of new, safer autonomous driving solutions.
- Continental develops pioneering technologies and services for sustainable and connected mobility.
- Founded in 1871, Continental offers safe, efficient, intelligent, and affordable solutions for vehicles, machines, traffic, and transportation.
- The Autonomous Mobility and Safety business area develops and produces integrated active and passive driving safety technologies as well as products that support vehicle dynamics.
