# Ascend Elements

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2022-10-27

**Source:** [Ascend Elements Secures $300 Million in Funding](https://www.waste360.com/waste-recycling/ascend-elements-secures-300-million-in-funding)  ·  `page_052557`

## Facts

- Ascend Elements is a U.S.-based engineered materials and lithium-ion battery recycling company based in Westborough, Mass.
- On October 27, 2022, Ascend Elements announced it secured $300 million in equity and debt financing.
- The funding includes $200 million in Series C equity investments from an international group of strategic and financial investors.
- The funding round was led by Fifth Wall Climate.
- SK ecoplant, the environmental unit of South Korean conglomerate SK Group, joined as an investor.
- New investors include Oman Investment Authority, Lithium Americas Corporation, GLy Capital Management's New Mobility Fund, Mirae Asset Capital & LS, and Shinhan GIB.
- Investors from previous funding rounds participated in the latest round, including Hitachi Ventures, Jaguar Land Rover's InMotion Ventures, TDK Ventures, Orbia Ventures, At One Ventures, TRUMPF Venture, and Doral Energy-Tech Ventures.
- The funding is in addition to two recently awarded grants totaling $480 million from the Department of Energy.
- The funding will accelerate commercialization of Ascend Elements' proprietary Hydro-to-Cathode™ direct precursor synthesis process.
- The company's technology establishes a closed-loop, EV battery materials supply chain in North America.
- Ascend Elements disclosed plans to invest up to $1 billion to build a sustainable lithium-ion battery materials facility in Hopkinsville, Ky.
- The first-of-its-kind manufacturing facility is named "Apex 1".
- Apex 1 will produce enough lithium-ion battery pCAM and sustainable CAM to equip up to 250,000 electric vehicles per year.
- CEO Mike O'Kronley stated that the company produces sustainable, high-performance cathode active materials that go directly back into new EV batteries rather than simply recovering metals for global commodity markets.
- The company's "Base 1" facility in Georgia is already generating revenue and processing end-of-life batteries and manufacturing scrap.
- Base 1 will have 30,000 metric tons of EV battery recycling capacity by the end of 2022.
- Base 1 will ultimately feed materials to Apex 1 in Kentucky.
- The company expects to recycle more than 150,000 metric tons of lithium-ion batteries per year globally by 2026.
- Ascend Elements efficiently transforms recycled batteries into high-performance, customized EV battery cathode active material precursor (pCAM) and cathode active materials (CAM).
- The produced pCAM and CAM meet or exceed the performance requirements of top-tier battery manufacturers.
- Peter Gajdoš, Fifth Wall Partner and Co-Lead of the Climate Investment team, stated that investment partners understand the urgent need to produce sustainable, lithium-ion battery materials.
- SK ecoplant CEO Park Kyung-il said the investment continues their strategic partnership with Ascend Elements and lays the foundation for SK ecoplant as the first shareholder to dominate the global battery recycling industry.
- Ibrahim Al Eisri, Director – Private Equity at Oman Investment Authority, stated they are pleased to partner with Ascend Elements to develop and transform the lithium-ion battery recycling industry globally.
- The company's proprietary Hydro-to-Cathode™ technology produces new CAM from spent lithium-ion cells more efficiently than traditional methods.
- This process results in reduced cost, improved performance, and lowered GHG emissions compared to traditional methods.
