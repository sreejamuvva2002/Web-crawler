# Hyundai Motor America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** workforce_training  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-22

**Source:** [Hyundai Expands Education Initiatives in Georgia](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/hyundai-expands-education-initiatives-in-georgia-302562296.html)  ·  `page_002789`

## Facts

- Hyundai expanded its partnership with the H2GP Foundation to deliver the Hyundai Hydrogen RC Program to high school students in Bryan County.
- Hyundai expanded its partnership with the SAE Foundation to deliver SAE International's award-winning A World In Motion (AWIM) PreK-12 STEM education program in the Bryan County School District.
- Hyundai continued partnerships with the Georgia Historical Society and Coastal Empire Habitat for Humanity, donating funds to support education and housing affordability in Coastal Georgia.
- These initiatives are part of Hyundai's corporate social responsibility program, Hyundai Hope, which seeks to uplift families and communities by fostering education, health, safety, and sustainability.
- The Bryan County area is home to Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA), Hyundai Motor Group's first dedicated electric vehicle mass-production plant.
- Brent Stubbs, chief administrative officer of HMGMA, stated that Hyundai sees it as its responsibility to give back to local organizations creating positive change near the operating plant.
- The expanded partnership with the H2GP Foundation will implement programming for a second year in Bryan County for the Hyundai RC Program.
- The upcoming program year will feature 16 teams.
- Two of Bryan County's teams recently competed at the H2GP World Finals.
- Hyundai previously partnered with SAE International to implement the AWIM program in Bryan County fourth-grade classrooms.
- The AWIM program will continue this year for over 800 fourth graders in the Bryan County School District.
- Students will participate in the Skimmer challenge, designing and building fan-propelled paper sailboats while learning about air resistance, propulsion, surface area, teamwork, collaboration, and critical thinking.
- The AWIM curriculum connects to HMGMA's renewable energy resources, emission reduction technologies, and career paths.
- A donation to the Georgia Historical Society will support educational outreach programs and bolster resources for educators.
- Dr. W. Todd Groce, president and CEO of the Georgia Historical Society, noted that Hyundai's generosity allows hundreds of thousands of Georgia students to study state and national history and supports events like the Georgia History Festival.
- Brandon Ramirez, director of corporate social responsibility at Hyundai Motor North America, stated that expanding these partnerships aims to inspire career pathways in clean energy and the automotive industry.
- Hyundai partnered with Coastal Empire Habitat for Humanity in 2023 to support affordable home ownership opportunities in the community.
- The donation supports Habitat's mission of building homes, communities, and hope, aligning with Hyundai Hope's goal to enhance community health and quality of living.
- Since 2022, Hyundai and its nonprofit organization Hyundai Hope on Wheels have donated over $6 million to local organizations and hospitals in Georgia.
- Hyundai Motor America offers U.S. consumers a lineup of cars, SUVs, and electrified vehicles while supporting the Progress for Humanity vision.
- Hyundai's significant U.S. operations include its North American headquarters in California, the Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Alabama assembly plant, the all-new HMGMA, and several cutting-edge R&D facilities.
- These operations, combined with those of Hyundai's 850 independent dealers, contribute $20.1 billion annually and 190,000 jobs to the U.S. economy according to a published economic impact report.
