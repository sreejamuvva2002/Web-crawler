# Aurubis Richmond, LLC

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2024-09-25

**Source:** [Continued economic development in Georgia and metro Augusta - Augusta Business Daily](https://augustabusinessdaily.com/continued-economic-development-in-georgia-and-metro-augusta/)  ·  `page_017460`

## Facts

- A Sept. 20 ribbon-cutting ceremony marked the start of operations at the Aurubis Richmond, LLC plant in south Augusta.
- Gov. Brian Kemp (R) attended the Friday event.
- The event was held just over a week after Area Development Magazine named Georgia the top state to do business for the 11th year in a row.
- Kemp stated that even though the national and world economies are ebbing and flowing, Georgia continues to have record economic development.
- Kemp said Georgia's pipeline is very good and the state is excited and bullish about the future.
- Kemp emphasized working hard on workforce despite the labor market being softened and changed a little bit.
- Cal Wray, President of the Augusta Economic Development Authority (AEDA), echoed the Governor’s comments.
- Aurubis is a multi-billion dollar international company that chose Augusta for its first U.S. facility.
- Wray said Aurubis's presence shows Augusta as a place you can do business and trust.
- Aurubis completed everything they promised in two years, including quick construction, quick hiring, and quick partnership with local institutions.
- Aurubis partnered on workforce development with Augusta Tech and Augusta University.
- Georgia ranked among the best states for access to qualified labor.
- Area Development Magazine pointed to Georgia’s Quick Start program, coordinated by the Technical College System of Georgia (TCSG), which can customize workforce training for a specific company.
- Kemp cited a program started last year to streamline how high school students apply for post-secondary education.
- Kemp said they rolled out the Georgia MATCH program last year, resulting in technical college enrollment up almost 10% and university system enrollment over 6%.
- Each year, the Georgia MATCH program sends a letter to high school students listing the TCSG and University System of Georgia (USG) institutions into which the student is eligible to enroll.
- Kemp and Wray said existing businesses like Aurubis, Gulfstream in Savannah, and Kia in west Georgia serve as ambassadors to help sell the Peach State to other companies.
- Potential businesses can talk to operating companies about workforce efforts, business environment, and local/state partnerships.
- Wray said Aurubis is one of GF Casting's biggest cheerleaders.
- When GF Casting was announced earlier this year, it created a unique challenge for AEDA because the company will use 147 acres in the Augusta Corporate Park off Mike Padgett Highway, which just about fills the 1,800-acre park to capacity.
- Only two real sites remain in the park: a 65-acre and a 115-acre site.
- AEDA has a few small sites up Tobacco Road and Doug Barnard.
- AEDA identified a 1,000-acre site that they are doing due diligence on for one user.
- AEDA is also evaluating other 50-acre to 200-acre sites to have other options.
- GF Casting Solutions AG, a division of Georg Fischer AG in Schaffhausen, develops and produces parts for multiple industries including light vehicles, trucks, and aerospace.
- Site preparation is underway directly across from the Aurubis facility for the $148 million GF Casting plant.
- The GF Casting plant will eventually create 350 jobs.
- Wray said the site has been cleared and graded, with foundations expected to go in after city permitting, and a building ready to accept equipment by the second quarter of next year.
- AEDA maintains a list of developed and undeveloped sites available on its website under the “Site Selection” tab.
