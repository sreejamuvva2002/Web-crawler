# Luminar Technologies, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2021-06-24

**Source:** [Shares of start-up Luminar surge on lidar deal with Volvo](https://www.cnbc.com/2021/06/24/volvo-making-luminars-lidar-technology-standard-on-new-ev.html)  ·  `page_014020`

## Facts

- Volvo is integrating Luminar Technologies' lidar system on its upcoming electric flagship SUV.
- This marks the first time a major carmaker has integrated lidar technology as standard equipment on a vehicle.
- The specific sensor being used is Luminar's new Iris lidar system, which allows vehicles to better see their environment.
- The Volvo SUV will serve as a successor to the current XC90 model.
- The new vehicle is expected to be unveiled next year (2022).
- Luminar went public through a SPAC deal in December 2020.
- On the announcement day, Luminar shares rose by as much as 13% during intraday trading before closing at $23.95 per share, up 6.8%.
- Volvo CEO Hakan Samuelsson stated that Volvo has always been a safety leader and will now define the next level of car safety with this standard hardware.
- The standard hardware will allow Volvo to continuously improve safety features over the air and introduce advanced autonomous drive systems.
- Lidar (light detection and ranging) systems use light to create high-resolution images for more accurate environmental views than cameras or radar alone.
- While lidar has been discussed for self-driving delivery vehicles, consumer adoption was previously limited by cost.
- Luminar states its system will initially enhance vehicle safety and will cost approximately $1,000 per vehicle.
- Depending on the launch timing, the Volvo SUV could be the first vehicle with lidar as standard equipment.
- This partnership represents a major win for Luminar in standardizing lidar technology.
- Luminar founder and CEO Austin Russell confirmed the technology is being integrated into Volvo's next-generation EV platform.
- The integration suggests Volvo may offer lidar as standard or optional on all of its upcoming EVs.
- Volvo has announced plans to transition to a fully electric car company by 2030.
- The new vehicles are expected to eventually offer advanced hands-free highway driving with some self-driving capabilities.
- Austin Russell identified 2023 as the "first major ramp up" of Luminar's lidar technology for the automotive industry.
- Russell projected that in the second half of the decade, millions of vehicles will be on the road equipped with this technology.
