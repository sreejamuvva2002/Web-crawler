# Rivian

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-05

**Source:** [Rivian Announces Optimized Capacity Plan for Georgia Plant - Green Stock News](https://greenstocknews.com/news/nasdaq/rivn/rivian-announces-optimized-capacity-plan-for-georgia-plant)  ·  `page_000273`

## Facts

- Rivian announced it is increasing its initial production capacity to 300,000 vehicles annually for its future manufacturing facility in Stanton Springs North, Georgia.
- The increased capacity represents a 50 percent increase over the initially planned capacity of 200,000 units.
- The capacity increase is intended to facilitate a lower cost per unit and provide significant room for future expansion in later phases.
- Rivian has worked with the U.S. Department of Energy’s Office of Energy Dominance Financing (EDF) to update its DOE loan.
- The total DOE loan is aligned with the updated facility design and roadmap at up to $4.5 billion ($4,006 million principal and $494 million capitalized interest) for the initial phase.
- Vertical construction at the Georgia plant is expected to commence in 2026.
- Rivian expects to first draw on the DOE loan by early 2027.
- Vehicle production at the facility is expected to begin in late 2028.
- Preparations are underway for the development of the stamping press area, one of the most capital-intensive and technically demanding projects within the plant from a construction perspective.
- Progress on the facility’s primary buildings is expected to accelerate this summer as they begin to take shape.
- Rivian recently announced a partnership with Uber.
- The company is expected to build up to 50,000 robotaxis at its Georgia plant beginning in late 2028 under the partnership with Uber.
- Rivian Founder and CEO RJ Scaringe stated that the R2 vehicle model dramatically expands their market opportunity.
- RJ Scaringe noted that thousands of dedicated people will soon work in the Georgia plant to help scale American manufacturing and ensure U.S. leadership in innovation and technology.
- The capacity increase is expected to boost cost efficiency, support thousands of U.S. manufacturing jobs, and bolster American tech leadership on the global stage.
