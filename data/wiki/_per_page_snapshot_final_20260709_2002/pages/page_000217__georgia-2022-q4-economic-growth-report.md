# Georgia 2022 Q4 Economic Growth Report

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Georgia's 2022 4th quarter growth | Select Georgia](https://www.selectgeorgia.com/news-and-updates/locations-and-expansions/georgia-4Q22-growth/)  ·  `page_000217`

## Facts

- 9,460 jobs were created in the 4th quarter of 2022.
- $10.5 billion in capital investment was announced in the 4th quarter of 2022.
- The fourth quarter growth was driven largely by the electric transportation industry.
- Hyundai suppliers announced locations in Georgia to support the previously announced Hyundai Metaplant in Bryan County.
- A battery manufacturing facility located in Coweta County.
- Almost 80% of job creation in the 4th quarter came from the manufacturing sector due to automotive industry locations.
- Over 90% of capital investment in the 4th quarter came from the manufacturing sector due to automotive industry locations.
- The year 2022 overall was record-setting for economic development in Georgia.
- Only 10 locations accounted for 50% of job creation and 74% of capital investment in 2022.
- A record 64% of job creation in 2022 was attributed to the manufacturing sector, an unusual trend considering service and non-manufacturing sectors have historically dominated job creation in Georgia.
- Warehousing and distribution sector activity accounted for almost 6,000 jobs and $1.3 billion in capital investment in 2022.
- Hyundai Motor Group and SK On announced 3,500 jobs in Cartersville.
- Hyundai Mobis announced 1,500 jobs in Richmond Hill.
- Freyr Battery announced 723 jobs in Newnan.
- Ajin announced 630 jobs in Statesboro.
- Solvay Specialty Polymers announced 100 jobs in Augusta.
- Grupo Bimbo announced 295 jobs in Valdosta.
- Orgill announced 355 jobs in Tifton.
- NewCold announced 170 jobs in McDonough.
- Exotec announced 150 jobs in Atlanta.
- Norma Precision Ammunition announced 600 jobs in Ellabell.
- Kiss USA announced 395 jobs in Ellabell.
- Quikrete announced 150 jobs in Johns Creek.
- Stuckey's announced 150 jobs in Wrens.
- Vanderlande announced 150 jobs in White.
- "With the establishment of a logistics center in the Savannah area, one of the United State’s leading container terminals; we are confident that a more smooth product supply to the eastern region, excellent manpower in the region, and active cooperation and help from local and state governments will lead to upgraded business operations."
