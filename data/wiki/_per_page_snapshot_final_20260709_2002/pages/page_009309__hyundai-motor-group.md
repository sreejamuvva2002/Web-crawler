# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-06-01

**Source:** [Electric Development - Atlanta Magazine](https://www.atlantamagazine.com/article/electric-development/)  ·  `page_009309`

## Facts

- The State of Georgia and the Savannah Harbor–Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority created a 2,284-acre megasite for economic development in May 2021.
- Hyundai Motor CEO and President Jaehoon Chang and Governor Brian Kemp signed an agreement on May 20 for Hyundai to invest $5.54 billion into a facility to build electric vehicles and their batteries.
- The Bryan County site on Interstate 16 at U.S. 280 expanded to 2,923 acres to accommodate the project.
- Trip Tollison serves as president and CEO of the Savannah Economic Development Authority and is part of the regional Joint Development Authority.
- Georgia’s largest-ever economic development project promises 8,100 jobs.
- Outside suppliers are expected to invest an additional $1 billion.
- Hyundai could begin construction as early as January 2023.
- Production is scheduled to start in the first half of 2025.
- The facility will have an annual capacity of 300,000 vehicles.
- The Joint Development Authority is taking bids until June 15 for the first grading contract at the site.
- Governor Brian Kemp conducted an economic development mission to Korea in 2019.
- Since 2020, Georgia has announced more than 20 projects related to electric vehicles.
- These EV projects total $13.54 billion in investment and nearly 18,000 jobs.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development has not announced the incentives it is offering Hyundai.
- Rivian is receiving state and local tax breaks and other incentives totaling $1.5 billion.
- Rivian will bring 7,500 jobs to Morgan and Walton counties.
- Rivian will manufacture electric trucks and SUVs.
- Hyundai has not confirmed which specific vehicles it will produce at the new plant.
- The new plant will utilize smart factory technologies.
- The new plant will rely on renewable energy.
- Hyundai Motor North America President and CEO José Muñoz had dealer representatives for Hyundai and its luxury Genesis model attend the signing ceremony.
- Kia is a Hyundai subsidiary that builds vehicles in West Point.
- In March, Hyundai announced a global annual goal of selling 1.87 million units of 11 Hyundai and six Genesis electric models by 2030.
- Including Kia, Hyundai's target is to produce 3.23 million electric vehicles a year.
- The Bryan County site is located 16 miles west of I-95 and 28 miles from the port of Savannah.
- Georgia Ports Authority Executive Director Griff Lynch stated that ongoing capacity-building at the ports of Savannah and Brunswick positions them to facilitate trade for Hyundai and future suppliers.
- Georgia Central Railway runs along the site’s eastern edge.
- The railway will provide last-mile freight service for supplies and first-mile service for finished products.
- The railway connects to CSX in Savannah and Norfolk Southern in Macon.
- Supporting infrastructure includes ports, rails, interstates, and Savannah/Hilton Head International Airport.
- Trip Tollison cited the local workforce and quality of life as key factors that helped win Hyundai over.
- The original land was purchased from three families last summer.
- Purchasing the land allowed proposed projects to bypass initial steps and move directly to construction and prep phases.
