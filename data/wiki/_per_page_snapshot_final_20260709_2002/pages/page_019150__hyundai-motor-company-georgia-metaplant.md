# Hyundai Motor Company Georgia Metaplant

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-04-09

**Source:** [Hyundai Metaplant Georgia Expansion 2025: CEO Vows Increased Production | EpochEdge](https://www.epochedge.com/hyundai-metaplant-georgia-expansion-2025/)  ·  `page_019150`

## Facts

- During yesterday’s quarterly earnings call, Hyundai Motor Company CEO Jaehoon Chang announced a significant production increase at the Bryan County facility.
- Chang cited robust demand for the company’s electric vehicle lineup and improving supply chain conditions.
- CEO Jaehoon Chang told analysts: “We’re doubling down on our Georgia investment because we see tremendous potential in the North American market.”
- The South Korean automaker broke ground on the 2,923-acre site near Savannah in October 2022.
- Initial production at the facility was expected to begin by early 2025.
- The facility was originally designed to produce 300,000 vehicles annually.
- Chang revealed plans to boost capacity to 500,000 units by 2027 through a $1.8 billion expansion.
- According to Cox Automotive data, Hyundai and its affiliate Kia captured 7.8% of the U.S. electric vehicle market in Q3 2023, up from 5.2% the previous year.
- The Georgia plant will produce several models from Hyundai’s IONIQ electric lineup.
- The Georgia plant will also produce Genesis luxury EVs.
- Michael Rodriguez, senior manufacturing economist at Moody’s Analytics, stated: “Hyundai’s increased investment signals strong faith in Georgia’s business climate and workforce.”
- Rodriguez added: “The ripple effects will extend well beyond direct employment at the plant.”
- The expansion is expected to create an additional 1,500 jobs on top of the 8,500 already promised.
- This brings the total employment impact to approximately 10,000 positions.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development estimates the overall economic impact could reach $20 billion over the next decade when including supplier facilities and related developments.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp stated: “This expanded commitment from Hyundai validates our strategy of positioning Georgia as a hub for next-generation mobility.”
- Kemp added: “The Metaplant is already attracting an ecosystem of suppliers and technology partners.”
- Battery manufacturer LG Energy Solution is constructing a $4.3 billion plant in nearby Bartow County.
- Semiconductor producer SK hynix is exploring options for a manufacturing facility that would support Hyundai’s advanced driver assistance systems.
- Federal Reserve data shows consumer interest in electric vehicles temporarily cooled in mid-2023 amid concerns about charging infrastructure and higher interest rates.
- The Atlanta Federal Reserve’s economic outlook released last month projects steady growth in EV adoption, particularly in the mid-price segment where Hyundai has positioned much of its lineup.
- Chang explained during the call: “We’ve weathered the economic uncertainty and see clear signals that demand is stabilizing.”
- Chang noted: “The Inflation Reduction Act’s revised requirements for tax credits have actually strengthened our position, as we’re bringing more of our supply chain to North America.”
- Industry observers note Hyundai’s strategy represents a significant shift from just five years ago when the company primarily imported vehicles to the U.S. market.
- Jessica Caldwell, executive director of insights at Edmunds, stated: “They’re executing one of the most aggressive manufacturing expansions in the automotive industry.”
- Caldwell added: “The Georgia plant is central to their transformation from an import-heavy business to a domestic producer.”
- Local officials in Bryan County report that site preparation is proceeding ahead of schedule.
- Major structural components are already visible from Interstate 16.
- The company recently completed a 2.3-million-square-foot stamping and welding facility that will begin equipment installation next month.
- The tight labor market in coastal Georgia has prompted Hyundai to launch recruitment and training initiatives at technical colleges throughout the state.
- Transportation infrastructure improvements around the massive site have required coordination between state and federal agencies to ensure adequate highway access and rail connections.
- Environmental groups have raised concerns about water usage and habitat disruption.
- Hyundai has pledged that the Metaplant will be its most sustainable production facility globally.
- The company plans to power 30% of operations with on-site solar generation.
- The company plans to implement closed-loop water recycling systems.
- Production lines are scheduled to begin operating in approximately 14 months from the time of the announcement.
- Hyundai aims to establish itself as a dominant player in the U.S. electric vehicle market for decades to come.
