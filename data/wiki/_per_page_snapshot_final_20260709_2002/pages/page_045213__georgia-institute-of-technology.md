# Georgia Institute of Technology

**Type:** research_center  ·  **Role:** research_and_development  ·  **Published:** 2021-12-14

**Source:** [Georgia Tech Named a Finalist in the $1B Build Back Better Regional Challenge](https://whatnow.com/atlanta/real-estate/georgia-tech-named-a-finalist-in-the-1b-build-back-better-regional-challenge/)  ·  `page_045213`

## Facts

- Georgia Tech was named one of sixty regional coalition finalists for the Build Back Better Regional Challenge administered by the U.S. Economic Development Administration.
- The project is called the Georgia AI Manufacturing Pilot Facility and Technology Corridor, abbreviated as “GA-AIM.”
- GA-AIM will be used for technical assistance to create the Georgia Artificial Intelligence Manufacturing Corridor.
- The project is led by Thomas Kurfess and Aaron Stebner in the George W. Woodruff School of Mechanical Engineering at Georgia Tech.
- GA-AIM aims to fill existing technology gaps and build a technological opportunity framework that includes underrepresented communities and rural Georgia counties.
- The initiative seeks to better secure Georgia’s manufacturing infrastructure.
- Georgia Tech’s partners in the effort include the Russell Innovation Center for Entrepreneurs, Spelman College, the Technical College System of Georgia, and the Georgia Department of Economic Development.
- Each of the sixty regional coalition finalists will compete for up to $100 million in American Rescue Plan Funding.
- The finalists have proposed projects that will develop or scale industry sectors, develop and train the workforce of today, and build resilient economies.
- Phase 2 of the Challenge will award 20-30 regional coalitions up to $100 million.
- The GA-AIM project was selected from a pool of 529 applicants.
- GA-AIM will receive an initial grant of approximately $500,000 to develop the proposed project further.
- These initial grants will help finalists take projects to the next level and position non-awardees to find new partners and funding sources.
- The total GA-AIM project is valued at $30 million.
- Phase II matching funds for the $30 million project include a $2M contribution from Georgia state entities GDEcD and TCSG.
- Georgia Tech will contribute investments valued at $5M, including Career Grants, Technology Incubation Grants, leadership and staff salaries and benefits toward the program, facility investments, capital planning, and management for the AMPF renovation.
- An industry cost share of $8M will complete the application matching fund requirement.
- Alejandra Y. Castillo, Assistant Secretary of Commerce for Economic Development, stated that the EDA is helping communities work together in coalitions to craft ambitious plans to rebuild communities and tackle challenges related to climate change, manufacturing, and supply chains.
