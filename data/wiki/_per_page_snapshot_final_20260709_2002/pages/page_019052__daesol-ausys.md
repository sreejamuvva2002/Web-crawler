# Daesol Ausys

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-08-29

**Source:** [$72 million Harris County auto parts plant project to create 140 jobs in Columbus area](https://www.yahoo.com/news/72-million-harris-county-auto-185439636.html)  ·  `page_019052`

## Facts

- More than 140 new jobs will be created in the Columbus area from an automobile parts supplier opening a new manufacturing facility in Harris County.
- The $72 million project is located in West Point, Georgia.
- Daesol Ausys Georgia joins other automotive suppliers in the area, including Kia Motors (which opened its only U.S. manufacturing plant in 2010), Adient, Daehan Solutions, Daesol Materials, Kopla, and Hyundai Glovis.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated in a news release that West Point has become an automotive capital in its own right and expressed excitement about Daesol Ausys joining other innovators in Harris County.
- Georgia's automotive industry currently employs over 50,000 Georgians and will continue to grow as e-mobility suppliers choose locations across the state.
- Daesol Ausys is a South Korean company established in 2017 that designs and manufactures motor and electric vehicle interior components and accessories for carmakers such as Kia, Hyundai, and General Motors.
- CEO Min Ho Kwon stated the company is committed to delivering world-class services and is excited about the project at the Northwest Harris Business Park, noting a partnership with the State of Georgia and Harris County.
- The new facility is expected to open in December 2024.
- At the new facility, Daesol Ausys will produce luggage boards and luggage covers.
- The company plans to hire personnel for roles in production, purchasing, quality control, maintenance, human resources, and management.
- Harris County Commission Chairman Robert Grant stated that Daesol Ausys' decision to locate in the Northwest Harris Business Park represents a commitment to Harris County.
- West Point and Harris County have a strong relationship with Daesol Ausys' sister companies Daehan Solutions and Daesol Materials, and officials have worked closely with company officials to bring this new venture to West Point.
- Harris County Development Authority Chairman Craig Greenhaw welcomed Daesol Ausys as a new corporate citizen and noted the venture brings jobs and capital investment to the region.
