# Denkai America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-06

**Source:** [Japanese manufacturer to build North American headquarters in Augusta - The Christian Index](https://christianindex.org/stories/japanese-manufacturer-to-build-north-american-headquarters-in-augusta,28249)  ·  `page_019240`

## Facts

- The Japanese manufacturer of copper foil used in printed electronics will build its North American headquarters in Georgia, as announced by Gov. Brian Kemp.
- Denkai America is making an initial investment of $150 million on a 115-acre site in Richmond County.
- Ultimately, the company will spend $430 million on a headquarters and manufacturing plant.
- The project will create 250 jobs over the next five years.
- Denkai America’s products have traditionally been used in printed circuit board applications primarily for the aerospace and defense industries.
- The company's copper foil products are now also used in lithium-ion batteries powering electric vehicles.
- Gov. Brian Kemp stated that Georgia is proud to be selected as the home of Denkai America, further strengthening the state’s status as a leader in emerging electric mobility and existing aerospace, defense, and technology industries.
- Gov. Brian Kemp emphasized the state's focus on bringing quality jobs to highly trained Georgians and highlighted Georgia’s top-ranking pro-business environment.
- Nobu Masuda, Denkai America’s president and CEO, stated the company is thrilled to expand its manufacturing presence into Augusta due to the skilled workforce needed for growth.
- Construction is expected to begin in September as part of a three-phase investment.
- The facility will be fully operational by December 2024.
- The company will be hiring skilled industrial technicians, production managers, and engineers.
- The state Department of Economic Development worked with the Augusta Economic Development Authority, Georgia Power, and the Technical College System of Georgia’s Quick Start program to secure the project.
- Georgia Commissioner of Economic Development Pat Wilson stated that as electric mobility adoption becomes widespread, Georgia is committed to ensuring Georgians in the automotive industry have access to future jobs at home.
- Pat Wilson noted the investment demonstrates Georgia’s commitment to building lasting international relationships and driving economic partnerships across the state.
