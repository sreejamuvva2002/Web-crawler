# U.S. Department of Energy

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [Biden-Harris Administration Announces Nearly $71 Million to Continue Strengthening America's Auto Communities, Expanding Battery Recycling, and Modernizing Manufacturing | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/cmei/manufacturing/articles/biden-harris-administration-announces-nearly-71-million-continue)  ·  `page_002761`

## Facts

- The U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) announced $70.8 million to support federal partnerships with state and local governments for small- and medium-sized manufacturers (SMMs) across the automotive, battery recycling, and smart manufacturing sectors.
- The projects were selected by the Office of Manufacturing and Energy Supply Chains (MESC).
- The funding will help SMMs upgrade manufacturing facilities to support advanced vehicles, increase the efficiency of battery recycling programs, and modernize state-level manufacturing bases.
- The initiative aims to ensure benefits of federal investments in climate, clean energy, and other areas flow to disadvantaged communities marginalized by underinvestment and overburdened by pollution.
- U.S. Secretary of Energy Jennifer M. Granholm stated that the projects cement the Administration’s state-based efforts to support the nation’s auto workforce and communities while strengthening domestic manufacturing and bolstering energy security.
- Five states with significant automotive workforces were selected to receive $50 million from the Inflation Reduction Act to invest in upgrading auto facilities for growing vehicle markets, including electric, hybrid, or fuel cell vehicle supply chains.
- This vehicle facility upgrade funding is made possible by the Department’s $2 billion Domestic Automotive Manufacturing Conversion Grant program.
- Michigan was allocated $22,653,072 for automotive facility upgrades.
- Indiana was allocated $10,793,685 for automotive facility upgrades.
- Kentucky was allocated $6,001,534 for automotive facility upgrades.
- Tennessee was allocated $5,555,067 for automotive facility upgrades.
- Illinois was allocated $4,996,639 for automotive facility upgrades.
- Seven states and local governments were selected for award negotiation to receive $11.5 million in Bipartisan Infrastructure Law funds as part of the second round of MESC’s State and Local Battery Recycling program.
- Tennessee's Department of Environment and Conservation was selected for battery recycling funding.
- Michigan's Department of Environment, Great Lakes and Energy was selected for battery recycling funding.
- Nevada's Washoe County was selected for battery recycling funding.
- New York's Erie County was selected for battery recycling funding.
- Wisconsin's Department of Natural Resources was selected for battery recycling funding.
- Iowa's Dubuque Metropolitan Area Solid Waste Agency was selected for battery recycling funding.
- Indiana's Allen County was selected for battery recycling funding.
- Five states were selected for award negotiation to receive $9.3 million in Bipartisan Infrastructure Law funds as part of the second round of the State Manufacturing Leadership Program.
- The State Manufacturing Leadership Program partners with states to establish programs accelerating smart manufacturing and high-performance computing adoption across SMMs, building on twelve prior round 1 awards.
- Illinois was selected under the Smart Manufacturing Lighthouse Program (SMLP).
- Michigan was selected under Project DIAMOnD Statewide Expansion: Distributed, Independent, Agile, Manufacturing On Demand.
- Oregon was selected under the Oregon Smart Manufacturing Initiative (OSMI).
- Tennessee was selected under the Tennessee Partnership for Smart Manufacturing (TPSM).
- Virginia was selected under the Virginia Smart Manufacturing Accelerator 2 (VSMA 2).
- Smart manufacturing uses emerging and advanced technologies to increase traditional manufacturing efficiency, lower costs, increase energy efficiency and performance, reduce material demand, and improve product quality.
- The program expands SMM access to technical assistance, training, facility assessments, apprenticeships, and financial assistance.
- Selection for award negotiations does not constitute a commitment by the DOE to issue awards or provide funding.
- Before funding issuance, applicants must undergo a negotiation process and complete an environmental review.
- The DOE reserves the right to cancel negotiations and rescind selections at any time during the process.
