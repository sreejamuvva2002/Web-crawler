# LGES-Hyundai Metaplant Joint Venture

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-08-03

**Source:** [Governor Kemp Announces Further $2B Investment from Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution in Georgia | Midsouth Mechanical Contractors](https://midsouthmechanical.com/governor-kemp-announces-further-2b-investment-from-hyundai-motor-group-and-lg-energy-solution-in-georgia/)  ·  `page_009217`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced an additional $2 billion investment from Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution for their battery cell manufacturing collaborative project at the Metaplant site in Bryan County, Georgia.
- The additional investment brings the venture's total investment to $4.3 billion.
- The project promises to generate 400 new employment opportunities in the region.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that within just a year, they inaugurated the grandest project the state has ever witnessed.
- Governor Brian Kemp noted that numerous suppliers were attracted statewide for Hyundai’s Metaplant and LGES was ushered into Bryan County.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Georgia is being positioned as the nucleus of e-mobility in the country.
- Governor Brian Kemp remarked that massive investments transform into wages for Georgians, facilitating the improvement of educational institutions and infrastructure.
- José Muñoz, who holds significant positions at Hyundai Motor Company, vocalized commitment to fostering a greener future facilitated by the American workforce.
- José Muñoz acknowledged relentless support from Governor Kemp and Georgia communities, aiming to become pioneers in the electric mobility sector globally.
- Earlier in May 2023, Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution formulated a memorandum of understanding.
- The memorandum of understanding established LGES as Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America’s (HMGMA) integral partner for on-site battery cell manufacturing.
- The present declaration symbolizes a monumental $7.59 billion venture.
- The venture aims to create 8,500 job opportunities in the coming eight years.
- Dong-Myung Kim leads the Advanced Automotive Battery Division at LG Energy Solution.
- Dong-Myung Kim emphasized dedication towards accelerating America’s transition to electric vehicles and fortifying the local economy with substantial employment opportunities.
- Dong-Myung Kim expressed enthusiasm to integrate premium products and established operational expertise within Georgia’s communities.
- The facility will have a production capacity of 30 GWh.
- The facility will substantiate manufacturing of 300,000 electric vehicle units annually at its peak.
- Hyundai Mobis is slated to assemble battery packs utilizing cells manufactured at this plant.
- The assembled battery packs will cater to Hyundai Motor Group’s American factories producing Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis electric vehicle models.
- Carter Infinger, Chairman of the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority, anticipates the additional investment will revolutionize the Savannah region by generating 400 more lucrative job positions.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson commended the strategic efforts and collaboration of multiple state and community partners.
- Pat Wilson emphasized Georgia’s sustained industry leadership and success in fostering job opportunities across the state.
- Georgia remains steadfast in cultivating job prospects encompassing the entire EV supply chain as the market gains momentum.
- Since 2018, Georgia has secured over $25 billion in investments and generated more than 30,000 jobs related to the EV supply chain.
