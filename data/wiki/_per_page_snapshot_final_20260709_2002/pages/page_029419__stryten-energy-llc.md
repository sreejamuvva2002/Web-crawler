# Stryten Energy LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Stryten Energy Unveils Plan to Add 10 Gigawatts of New U.S. Energy Storage Manufacturing Capacity](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20250331252002/en/Stryten-Energy-Unveils-Plan-to-Add-10-Gigawatts-of-New-U.S.-Energy-Storage-Manufacturing-Capacity)  ·  `page_029419`

## Facts

- Stryten Energy LLC is a U.S.-based energy storage solutions provider.
- The company announced a new plan expected to expand its domestic manufacturing capacity to 24 Gigawatts.
- The plan focuses on increasing production across its 11 U.S. manufacturing and battery component plants.
- More than 2,500 people currently produce batteries at these facilities for multiple applications.
- Battery applications include military and government, data centers, grid storage, automotive, and material handling.
- The new investment is supported by existing advanced manufacturing production tax credits.
- These tax credits contribute to the company's ability to accelerate capacity growth while supporting the domestic industrial base.
- Mike Judd serves as President and Chief Executive Officer of Stryten Energy.
- Mike Judd stated that investments will significantly increase gigawatt capacity across the company's footprint to support growing energy demand and achieve national goals of true energy resilience.
- Mike Judd emphasized that American energy security must rely on domestic companies like Stryten manufacturing batteries for supply chains and critical infrastructure backup power.
- Energy security requires continuous access to reliable power, necessitating scaling up production of all reliable battery chemistries.
- Stryten advances energy storage in the U.S. with a unique suite of domestically manufactured battery solutions using advanced lead, lithium, and vanadium redox flow battery technology.
- The company's plants are located in Kansas, Georgia, Iowa, Virginia, and Arkansas.
- Plans for increasing gigawatt production capacity will bring hundreds of high-paying manufacturing jobs to surrounding communities.
- Stryten operates a domestic, vertically integrated supply chain that provides battery components to its factories and the broader U.S. battery manufacturing industry.
- Plants in Pennsylvania, New York, Indiana, Missouri, and Wisconsin ensure a reliable U.S. source for critical materials needed to manufacture batteries.
- The investment plan includes increasing domestic processing capacity of recycled plastic from spent batteries.
- Recycled plastic is used to produce new battery components for automobiles, forklifts, and locomotives.
- This initiative contributes to the sustainable circular economy of the U.S. battery industry.
- Stryten recently opened a lithium battery assembly plant in Georgia that supports military and industrial battery needs with proprietary lithium module technology.
- The company is partnering with Dragonfly Energy, headquartered in Reno, Nevada, to bring advanced lithium batteries to automotive aftermarket and battery distributor partners.
- Stryten announced the launch of Storion Energy LLC through its affiliate Stryten Critical E-Storage LLC.
- Storion Energy was formed with a subsidiary of Largo Inc. (NASDAQ, TSX: LGO) and Largo Clean Energy Corp. (LCE).
- Storion Energy focuses on removing barriers to entry for battery manufacturers to domestically source price-competitive electrolytes used in vanadium redox flow batteries (VRFB) for long-duration energy storage (LDES) applications in the U.S.
- Stryten Energy is headquartered in Alpharetta, Georgia.
- The company's energy storage technologies include advanced lead, lithium, and vanadium redox flow batteries, intelligent chargers, and energy performance management software.
- Stryten powers applications ranging from submarines and subcompacts to microgrids, warehouses, distribution centers, cars, trains, and trucks.
