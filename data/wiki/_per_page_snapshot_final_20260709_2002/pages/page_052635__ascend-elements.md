# Ascend Elements

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2022-10-20

**Source:** [Ascend Elements | Ascend Elements Begins Construction of Apex 1 in Southwestern Kentucky](https://ascendelements.com/ascend-elements-begins-construction-of-apex-1-in-southwestern-kentucky/)  ·  `page_052635`

## Facts

- Ascend Elements began construction of its largest electric vehicle battery recycling and engineered materials manufacturing facility, named Apex 1, with a ceremonial groundbreaking on October 20, 2022.
- The Apex 1 facility is located in Hopkinsville, Kentucky (Christian County).
- The project involves a 140-acre campus and a 500,000-square-foot manufacturing facility.
- Ascend Elements plans to invest close to $1 billion in the Apex 1 campus and facility.
- When finished, the Apex 1 facility will produce enough engineered precursor (pCAM) and battery-ready cathode active materials (CAM) to equip 250,000 electric vehicles per year.
- The facility will employ up to 400 full-time jobs.
- The project is expected to generate up to $4.4 billion in economic impact to Kentucky over the construction period and first 10 years.
- Apex 1 will utilize Ascend Elements’ Hydro-to-Cathode™ direct precursor synthesis process technology, a closed-loop system that produces new cathode active material from spent lithium-ion cells with minimal waste and carbon emissions compared to traditional manufacturing.
- Governor Andy Beshear stated the investment has grown to nearly $1 billion and will create 400 full-time jobs, making it the single largest investment in Western Kentucky.
- Governor Andy Beshear noted that Kentucky has quickly become a national leader in the electric-vehicle space due to a substantial supplier network including innovative companies like Ascend Elements.
- Michael O’Kronley, CEO of Ascend Elements, stated that the facility represents a domestic source of sustainable lithium-ion cathode material for EV batteries critical for U.S. energy independence and national security.
- Ascend Elements is based in Westborough, Massachusetts, and is a leading provider of sustainable, closed-loop battery material solutions from EV battery recycling to commercial-scale production of pCAM and CAM.
