# Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-05

**Source:** [Reports: ICE Detains 300 Korean Migrants at Georgia Construction Site](https://www.breitbart.com/economy/2025/09/05/ice-detains-300-korean-migrants-georgia-construction-site/)  ·  `page_050078`

## Facts

- ICE detained 450 illegal migrants at a construction site in Georgia on September 4.
- The sweep included hundreds of Korean nationals who likely misused B-1/B-2 business tourist visas to take jobs from American construction workers.
- More than 300 Koreans were arrested during the sweep, according to Korean news sites.
- A giant Korean-owned battery factory is being built at the Ellabell, Georgia construction site.
- The battery factory is being constructed for Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution Ltd.
- More than 1,400 people are currently working at the Ellabell construction site.
- South Korea’s government filed a sharp complaint regarding the detention sweep because of the Korean-owned battery factory under construction.
- Lee Jae-woong, a ministry spokesperson for South Korea, stated that the economic activities of their companies investing in the U.S. and the rights of their nationals must not be unfairly violated.
- Lee Jae-woong conveyed concern and regret through the U.S. Embassy in Seoul on the day of the press briefing.
- Immigration expert Jay Palmer labeled Hyundai a "repeat offender" for allegedly using staffing companies to legally shield executives from liability when flying migrants to job sites.
- Palmer noted that other companies like Mercedes, Kia, and Tesla also use staffing companies to avoid responsibility for workers' immigration status via master service agreements.
- Palmer explained the corporate outsourcing strategy is driven by economics: hiring foreign workers for $5 to $8 an hour without insurance versus American workers for $20 to $30 an hour plus benefits.
- This corporate labor strategy clashes with President Donald Trump’s push to hire blue-collar Americans for skilled trades.
- Palmer warned that the Georgia raid will cost Hyundai a significant amount of money due to construction delays.
- In 2020, Fox News reported on the widespread use of B-1/B-2 visas by Korean workers at another battery factory for SK Battery America in Georgia.
- David Cagle of Georgia Local Union 72 complained that Georgians were excluded from applying for construction jobs at the SKI battery plant.
- Cagle stated that about 500 local people were out of work and available to build the plant, but instead Koreans were hired.
- Cagle clarified that much of the construction work was "generic stuff," including electrical work, ductwork, pipework, pipe fitting, and pipe welding.
- The recent ICE detentions were conducted with support from the FBI, ATF, and the Georgia State Patrol.
- In July, South Korea pledged $350 billion in U.S. investment to lower President Donald Trump’s threatened tariffs on its products, which were ultimately set at 15%.
- In March, Hyundai announced it would invest $21 billion in U.S. onshoring from 2025 to 2028.
- Hyundai later increased its stated investment commitment to $26 billion.
- Hyundai's investment initiatives include a new $5.8 billion steel plant in Louisiana, expanded U.S. auto production capacity, and a state-of-the-art robotics facility.
- These Hyundai initiatives are expected to create about 25,000 new direct jobs in the U.S. over the next four years.
- Breitbart News has closely covered the illegal use of B-1 visas to facilitate the inflow of airport migrants from India, Mexico, and Europe into American trucking, technology, and construction industries.
- Much of this labor travel activity is quietly backed by foreign governments that extract U.S. dollars in taxes and remittances via workers who enter legally but work illegally.
- Trump’s deputies have begun efforts to curb the misuse of B-1 visas, which are intended for tourists and visiting business executives.
