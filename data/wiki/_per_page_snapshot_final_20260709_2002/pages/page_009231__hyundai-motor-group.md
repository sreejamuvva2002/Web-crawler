# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2025-03-24

**Source:** [Hyundai plans fresh $20 billion investment in US, including new Louisiana steel plant, as tariff threats loom](https://nypost.com/2025/03/24/us-news/hyundai-plans-fresh-20-billion-investment-in-us-including-new-louisiana-steel-plant-as-tariff-threats-loom/)  ·  `page_009231`

## Facts

- Hyundai announced plans to invest a fresh $20 billion in the US.
- The investment includes a new $5.8 billion steel plant near Donaldsonville, Louisiana, about 40 miles south of Baton Rouge.
- The Louisiana steel facility would be Hyundai's first such plant in North America and will provide metal supplies to neighboring states.
- President Trump claimed the steel facility would create more than 1,400 jobs as part of the investment.
- Hyundai already operates factories in Alabama and Georgia.
- President Trump stated that Hyundai will massively increase its auto manufacturing in Georgia.
- President Trump stated that Hyundai will invest billions of dollars in advanced American technology companies.
- The investment announcement was made to avoid looming 25% tariffs on foreign-made steel and aluminum announced by the administration in February.
- Metals produced in the US will be exempt from the 25% tariff, which applies without exceptions or exemptions to all countries.
- Chung Eui-sun, executive chairman of Hyundai Motor Group, congratulated Trump on his administration and stated the company shares a vision for "American industrial leadership."
- CEO José Muñoz said the president's promised tariff policies incentivized the company to invest big time in America as the most important market to increase localization.
- The announcement coincided with an April 2 deadline for reciprocal tariffs on global goods, which the administration promoted as "Liberation Day."
- South Korea has a massive trade surplus with the US and is likely to be affected by the April 2 tariffs.
- President Trump indicated he may give breaks to many countries regarding the April 2 tariffs but did not specify which nations would be affected.
- Other foreign companies, including Japan's Softbank and Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Co., recently announced major planned US investments to curry favor with the administration before the tariffs take effect.
