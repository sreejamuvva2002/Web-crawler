# Hyundai Mobis

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** power_electronics  ·  **Published:** 2022-11-23

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp: Second Global Automotive Supplier for Hyundai Metaplant To Create 1,500 Jobs | Governor Brian P. Kemp Office of the Governor](https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases/2022-11-23/gov-kemp-second-global-automotive-supplier-hyundai-metaplant-create-1500)  ·  `page_000013`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Hyundai Mobis plans to invest $926 million in an Electric Vehicle (EV) Power Electric system plant in Bryan County.
- The new plant will eventually employ at least 1,500 people.
- This announcement marks the second supplier to locate in the Bryan County area in just two weeks following Hyundai’s EV and battery manufacturing facility groundbreaking.
- The EV Power Electric system plant will expand Hyundai Mobis’ current footprint in Georgia.
- Hyundai Mobis has operated a manufacturing facility with almost 1,200 employees in West Point since 2009.
- The West Point facility serves as the largest Tier 1 supplier to Kia Georgia.
- The West Point plant supplies automotive manufacturers with complete cockpit modules, chassis modules, and bumper assemblies.
- H.S. Oh, Vice President of Hyundai Mobis Electric Powertrain Business Unit, stated the Bryan County investment reflects an acceleration in the development of the EV supply chain in Georgia’s auto industry.
- H.S. Oh stated Hyundai Mobis will be a major production player in the EV market and expects to trigger more growth within the sector.
- Hyundai Mobis plans to provide high-quality work opportunities to the growing local workforce.
- Once at full production, the 1,200,000-square-foot facility will supply over 900,000 EV Power Electric systems and 450,000 Integrated Charging Control Units annually.
- The annual output will be supplied to Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) in Bryan County, Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Alabama (HMMA) in Montgomery, and the Kia Georgia plant.
- Construction on the new facility is expected to begin as early as January 2023.
- Production at the new facility is expected to begin in 2024.
- The new facility is located at the Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) Certified Belfast Commerce Park in Richmond Hill.
- Jon Seagraves, Chairman of the Development Authority of Bryan County, welcomed Hyundai Mobis to the community and noted the site was purchased about two years ago.
- Belfast Commerce Park offers close proximity to available housing in nearby Heartwood, a top-performing public school system, interstate access, and rail.
- Jon Seagraves cited nearly $1 billion of investment and the creation of over 1,500 good-paying jobs as key project benefits.
- Jon Seagraves thanked Bryan County, the City of Richmond Hill, and Raydient Places for their support and partnership on the project.
- Alyce Thornhill, Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy, represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The GDEcD partnered with the Development Authority of Bryan County, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia Power on the project.
- Pat Wilson, GDEcD Commissioner, stated suppliers like Hyundai Mobis are transitioning to the jobs of the future and becoming part of Georgia’s growing EV ecosystem.
- In May, GDEcD predicted that non-affiliated HMGMA suppliers in connection with the project would invest an additional $1 billion into Georgia.
- Pat Wilson noted that the May prediction was exceeded in about two weeks.
- GDEcD plans to prepare Georgia’s workforce of tomorrow for the jobs being created now using support from partners like Georgia Quick Start and sister agencies.
- Georgia is positioned as a hub for the electric mobility industry, building on assets that make the automotive industry successful.
- Since 2020, more than 30 EV-related projects have contributed over $13.3 billion in investment and announced more than 18,100 jobs in Georgia.
- On October 25, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America officially broke ground on its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility in Bryan County.
- Offsite suppliers connected to the HMGMA project are predicted to invest approximately $1 billion in other locations around the state.
