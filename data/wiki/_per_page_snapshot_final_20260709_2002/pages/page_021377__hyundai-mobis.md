# Hyundai Mobis

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** power_electronics  ·  **Published:** 2022-11-23

**Source:** [Second supplier for Hyundai metaplant to create 1,500 jobs - Coastal Courier](https://coastalcourier.com/news/second-supplier-hyundai-metaplant-create-1500-jobs/)  ·  `page_021377`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Hyundai Mobis plans to invest $926 million in an Electric Vehicle (EV) Power Electric system plant in Bryan County.
- The new plant will eventually employ at least 1,500 people.
- Gov. Kemp stated the announcement follows the recent groundbreaking of Hyundai’s new electric vehicle and battery manufacturing facility in Bryan County, which he said would unleash transformational job creation and investment in the region.
- This marks the second supplier announced in just two weeks to locate in the Bryan County area.
- The EV Power Electric system plant will expand Hyundai Mobis’ current footprint in Georgia.
- Hyundai Mobis has operated a manufacturing facility with almost 1,200 employees in West Point since 2009.
- The West Point facility serves as the largest Tier 1 supplier to Kia Georgia.
- The West Point plant supplies automotive manufacturers with complete cockpit modules, chassis modules, and bumper assemblies.
- H.S. Oh, vice president of Hyundai Mobis Electric Powertrain Business Unit, said the investment reflects an acceleration in the development of the EV supply chain in Georgia’s auto industry.
- H.S. Oh stated Hyundai Mobis will be a major production player in the EV market and expects to trigger more growth within the sector.
- H.S. Oh noted the company looks forward to providing high-quality work opportunities to the growing local workforce.
- Once at full production, the 1.2 million-square-foot facility will supply over 900,000 EV Power Electric systems annually to Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) in Bryan County.
- The facility will also supply over 900,000 EV Power Electric systems and 450,000 Integrated Charging Control Units annually to the Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Alabama (HMMA) factory in Montgomery.
- The new plant will supply components to the Kia Georgia plant as well.
- Construction on the new facility is expected to begin as early as January 2023.
- Production at the new facility is expected to begin in 2024.
- The facility is located at the Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) Certified Belfast Commerce Park in Richmond Hill.
- Jon Seagraves, chairman of the Development Authority of Bryan County, welcomed Hyundai Mobis to the community.
- Seagraves cited the site's close proximity to available housing in nearby Heartwood, a top-performing public school system, interstate access, and rail as key factors in attracting the project.
- Seagraves noted the site was purchased about two years ago and highlighted nearly $1 billion of investment and the creation of over 1,500 good-paying jobs.
- Seagraves thanked Bryan County, the City of Richmond Hill, and Raydient Places for their support and partnership on the project.
- Alyce Thornhill, Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy, represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on the competitive project.
- The project was developed in partnership with the Development Authority of Bryan County, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia Power.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that suppliers like Hyundai Mobis are transitioning to jobs of the future and becoming part of Georgia’s growing EV ecosystem.
- In May, GDEcD predicted that non-affiliated HMGMA suppliers in connection with the project would invest an additional $1 billion into Georgia.
- Commissioner Wilson noted that this prediction had already been blown past in about two weeks.
- Since 2020, more than 30 EV-related projects have contributed over $13.3 billion in investment and announced more than 18,100 jobs in Georgia.
- On October 25, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America officially broke ground on its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility in Bryan County.
- Offsite suppliers connected to the HMGMA project are predicted to invest approximately $1 billion in other locations around the state.
