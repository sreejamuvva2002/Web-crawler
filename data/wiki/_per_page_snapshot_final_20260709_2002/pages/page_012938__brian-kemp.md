# Brian Kemp

**Type:** person  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2025-01-14

**Source:** [Here Are Brian Kemp's Top Priorities for Georgia in 2025](https://thegeorgiasun.com/government/here-are-brian-kemps-top-priorities-for-georgia-in-2025/)  ·  `page_012938`

## Facts

- Governor Brian Kemp is calling for sweeping changes in education, workforce development, and infrastructure to ensure Georgia remains a national leader in business and opportunity.
- Kemp laid out his agenda for the 2025 legislative session at the Georgia Chamber’s Eggs and Issues breakfast.
- Kemp’s proposed “Top State for Talent Act” aims to address workforce challenges by linking education and job training to industry demands.
- The initiative expands the Georgia MATCH program, which guarantees college admission for eligible high school students, by integrating it into students’ individual graduation plans starting in 9th grade.
- The expanded program could provide families and students with greater access to high-paying jobs in growing industries.
- Businesses could benefit from a pipeline of skilled workers tailored to their needs, helping Georgia maintain its competitive edge as workforce challenges like declining high school graduate rates loom nationwide.
- Kemp’s Rural Workforce Housing Initiative is already providing grants to build housing for workers near new job hubs, with $42 million allocated to 21 communities.
- The housing program ensures that as industries expand in rural Georgia, workers can live where they work.
- Tort reform is Kemp’s top legislative priority for 2025, designed to address business leaders' concerns about Georgia’s legal climate being a barrier to economic growth and affordable healthcare.
- Details of the tort reform package will be announced in the coming weeks.
- Kemp called on local chambers of commerce to rally their members in support of the tort reform.
- The proposed tort reform is expected to face pushback from trial lawyers and advocacy groups.
- For small businesses and healthcare providers, the proposed changes could lower costs and reduce the risks of frivolous lawsuits.
- Kemp announced a major push for infrastructure improvements, with $530 million earmarked for freight and logistics.
- Kemp also allocated $250 million for water and wastewater projects.
- These investments build on last year’s $1.5 billion infrastructure funding.
- The infrastructure upgrades will likely mean smoother commutes, more efficient shipping for businesses, and better access to clean water and waste management for rural and urban communities alike.
- By 2035, Kemp projects the state will save over $1 billion in reduced delays and crashes thanks to these investments.
