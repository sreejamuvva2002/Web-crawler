# Global Battery Industry

**Type:** research_center  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [The battery industry has entered a new phase – Analysis - IEA](https://www.iea.org/commentaries/the-battery-industry-has-entered-a-new-phase)  ·  `page_010516`

## Facts

- In 2024, electric car sales rose by 25% to 17 million globally.
- Annual battery demand surpassed 1 terawatt-hour (TWh) in 2024.
- The average price of a battery pack for a battery electric car dropped below USD 100 per kilowatt-hour in 2024.
- Lithium prices dropped by more than 85% from their peak in 2022.
- Global battery manufacturing capacity reached 3 TWh in 2024.
- The next five years could see another tripling of production capacity if all announced projects are built.
- Markets have shifted from regionalised and small to global and very large, with technological approaches giving way to standardisation.
- Economies of scale, partnerships along the supply chain, manufacturing efficiency, and speed to market will be crucial for competition.
- Greater consolidation across the sector is likely, reshaped by government-driven efforts to geographically diversify battery supply chains.
- China produces over three-quarters of batteries sold globally.
- In 2024, average battery prices in China dropped by nearly 30%, faster than anywhere else.
- Batteries in China were reported to be cheaper than in Europe and North America by over 30% and 20%, respectively.
- Declining battery prices are a major reason why many EVs in China are now cheaper than conventional counterparts.
- Over 70% of all EV batteries ever manufactured were produced in China, creating extensive manufacturing know-how.
- Giant manufacturers such as CATL and BYD have centralised expertise in the battery sector and driven innovation.
- These companies have scaled up production faster and more efficiently than competitors and achieved higher manufacturing yields.
- Supply chain integration, through acquisitions and close cooperation among leading firms, has supported faster innovation and declined manufacturing costs.
- The Chinese battery ecosystem covers all steps of the supply chain, from mineral mining and refining to production of equipment, precursors, components, final batteries, and EVs.
- Access to below-market prices for critical minerals supports cost declines in China.
- Chinese producers have prioritised lithium-iron phosphate (LFP), a cheaper battery chemistry.
- LFP batteries now cover nearly half the global EV market after more than tripling their share within the past five years.
- LFP batteries are about 30% less expensive than lithium nickel cobalt manganese oxide (NMC) batteries while offering competitive ranges.
- Fierce domestic competition has shaped the Chinese battery market, which is home to almost 100 producers.
- The number of companies producing batteries in China is likely to fall amid tough competition and shrinking margins.
- Certain producers will acquire greater influence and pricing power.
- China is expected to remain the largest battery manufacturer by some distance in the medium-term.
- Many battery producers in Europe are postponing or cancelling expansion plans due to uncertainty about future profitability.
- Production costs in Europe are about 50% higher than in China.
- The European battery supply chain ecosystem is still relatively weak and lacks specialised workers.
- Northvolt, Europe’s largest investment in a homegrown battery maker, went bankrupt.
- Smaller manufacturers in Europe are struggling to scale up production and reach sufficient yields.
- Clear policy signalling continued demand growth and reducing investment risks is essential for building a competitive European battery industry.
- Efforts to produce cheaper LFP batteries in Europe are beginning to expand.
- Korean manufacturers lost almost one quarter of their market share in the European Union over the past two years, dropping from nearly 80% in 2022 to 60% in 2024.
- The drop in Korean market share is partly due to the increased success of LFP batteries made in China.
- Some Korean companies have started investing in making LFP batteries in Europe to compete with Chinese producers.
- Chinese battery makers are likely to keep expanding their European footprint through partnerships.
- The joint venture between Stellantis and CATL could speed up the uptake of LFP batteries in Europe, improve the ecosystem, and reduce the cost gap with China.
- Korea and Japan are major players in the global battery industry with strong expertise in NMC batteries.
- Both countries have limited domestic battery production but host established manufacturers with significant overseas investments.
- Korean companies lead in overseas manufacturing capacity with nearly 400 gigawatt-hours (GWh).
- Japan’s overseas manufacturing capacity is 60 GWh, and China’s is 30 GWh.
- Korean producers supplied over one-fifth of global electric car battery demand in 2024.
- Japanese producers covered nearly 7% of global electric car battery demand in 2024.
- These producers have strong innovation track records and are racing to develop new technologies such as solid-state batteries.
- In the United States, battery manufacturing capacity doubled since 2022 following the implementation of tax credits for producers.
- US battery manufacturing capacity reached over 200 GWh in 2024.
- Nearly 700 GWh of additional manufacturing capacity is under construction in the United States.
- Around 40% of existing US capacity is operated or developed by established battery makers in close collaboration with automakers.
- Developing domestic capacity for manufacturing battery components in the US has progressed more slowly, so most anode and cathode demand is still satisfied by imports.
- Battery demand for stationary applications in the US has increased by over 60% annually for the past two years.
- Southeast Asia and Morocco are emerging as potential production hubs for batteries and their components.
- Southeast Asia has attracted significant Chinese investment, which could speed up technology and innovation transfer.
- In Indonesia, home to half the world’s mined nickel, the first EV battery manufacturing and graphite anode plants began production in 2024.
- Morocco has the largest reserves of phosphate, a mineral essential for LFP batteries.
- Morocco has an established car manufacturing industry and free trade agreements with the European Union and the United States.
- These factors contributed to over USD 15 billion in announced investments in battery and components manufacturing in Morocco in 2022.
- The degree of concentration in battery supply chains has raised security concerns among governments.
- China recently proposed export limitations on battery cathode and lithium processing technologies.
- Diversifying battery production requires time, investment to bolster domestic manufacturing, build expertise, and reduce production cost gaps relative to China.
- Sufficient and sustained battery demand is required, with electric vehicle sales accounting for 85% of the battery market.
- Strategically deploying automation, digitalisation and innovation can help reach sufficient production yields to compete with Chinese production.
- Collaboration with incumbent battery producers through joint ventures or technology licensing agreements can decrease time and investments required to onshore battery production.
- International collaboration is needed as many individual markets may not be sufficiently large to justify necessary investments.
- Cooperation with resource-rich countries in South America, Africa, Australia, and Indonesia may be necessary.
- The IEA will continue to monitor these trends and publish a special report focused on the car industry later this year, including new analysis on battery supply chains.
