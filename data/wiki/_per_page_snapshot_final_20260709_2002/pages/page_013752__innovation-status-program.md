# Innovation Status Program

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2025-04-04

**Source:** [What is Innovation Status in Georgia and How to Get It (Before Everyone Else)](https://www.gegidze.com/post/what-is-innovation-status-in-georgia-and-how-to-get-it-before-everyone-else)  ·  `page_013752`

## Facts

- On March 31, 2025, the Georgian government confirmed that innovative startups in Georgia will be eligible for up to 10 years of tax breaks.
- Innovative startups in Georgia will receive 0% corporate income tax for the first 3 years.
- The incentive package includes extended tax incentives for a total of 10 years.
- Additional support is provided for small and mid-sized companies working on tech-driven products.
- Amendments are being made to two prominent legal frameworks: the Law “On Innovations” and the Tax Code of Georgia.
- A new legal definition will outline precisely what qualifies a company as an “innovative startup.”
- The policy opens eligibility for SMEs working on industry-specific innovations, not just large tech startups.
- Georgia’s GII ranking has been improving from 2020 to 2024.
- Progress in the GII ranking represents improvements in startup infrastructure, better support for R&D, more IP protection laws like the Georgia Uniform Trade Secrets Act, a shift toward attracting foreign investment and tech talent, and stronger alignment with global innovation policy standards.
- Historically, Georgia has had a competitive corporate tax rate of 15%.
- Qualified companies will pay 0% corporate tax in Georgia for the first three years.
- After the initial three years, companies may continue to receive reduced tax rates or exemptions for up to a decade depending on activities and compliance.
- Other likely benefits include VAT exemptions on R&D services, reduced tax on dividend distributions, and simplified reporting for qualified startups.
- The government sees tech and entrepreneurship as long-term economic stabilizers and growth engines amid recovery from regional instability and the pandemic.
- Georgia is recognized for its ease of doing business and aims to add tax efficiency to that reputation.
- Eligibility criteria likely require businesses to be registered and operating in Georgia, develop proprietary technology or software, conduct R&D locally, demonstrate potential for market growth or exports, and maintain transparent accounting and legal records.
- Companies must be recognized as Georgian tax residents to qualify for local benefits including the zero-tax regime.
- Tax residency requires incorporation in Georgia, management and decision-making based in Georgia, a Georgian bank account, a tax ID, and local filings.
- Having a Georgian domain or office lease is not enough to establish tax residency.
- Founders must choose the right business structure to affect eligibility for tax incentives, ability to receive investment, IP assignment, and tax residency compliance.
- Businesses must document their innovation by providing product development timelines, technical architecture or prototypes, evidence of R&D, and customer use cases or testing feedback.
- All IP including code, content, designs, and patents must be transferred to the business entity.
- Common IP protection tools include employment or contractor agreements with IP clauses, NDAs with collaborators or vendors, trademark and patent filings, and internal IP assignment documentation.
- Businesses need a Georgia tax ID number to submit tax returns and prove eligibility for certification.
- Companies must set up accurate and GAAP-aligned bookkeeping, regular Georgia tax return submissions (especially Form 500), clear financial reporting linked to the Georgian entity, and proper categorization of R&D expenses, salaries, and investments.
- The Georgia tax return form 500 is the official document used to verify profit/loss and tax liability for the 0% corporate income tax benefit.
- Consistent and compliant filing protects against audit risks and builds a paper trail for future grants, partnerships, or funding rounds.
- Gegidze is a firm that works with startups, growth-stage companies, and foreign investors to establish a compliant presence in Georgia and navigate the business-friendly tax regime.
- The 0% corporate income tax exemption is expected to begin once the new legal framework is fully implemented in 2025.
- Foreign-owned startups can apply for Innovation Status as long as the company is legally based in Georgia, meets local tax residency requirements, and proves innovative activity through documentation, IP ownership, and local operations.
