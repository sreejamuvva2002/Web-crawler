# Volvo Construction Equipment (Volvo CE)

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [FinancialContent - Volvo Construction Equipment to Expand Production in North America](https://markets.financialcontent.com/stocks/article/bizwire-2025-6-10-volvo-construction-equipment-to-expand-production-in-north-america)  ·  `page_014289`

## Facts

- Volvo Construction Equipment will begin producing crawler excavators and large wheel loaders in Shippensburg, Pennsylvania.
- The Shippensburg site will add capabilities to produce mid- to large-size excavators, thereby increasing its global industrial footprint and improving flexibility.
- The total global investment of $261 million aims to strengthen Volvo CE's crawler excavator position across three main production sites in South Korea, Sweden, and the United States.
- In addition, the Shippensburg site will add four large wheel loader models to its current wheel loader production.
- Production is planned to begin in the first half of 2026.
- Approximately $261 million will be invested across three main sites (Shippensburg, Penn., USA; Changwon, South Korea; and a location in Sweden) to expand crawler excavator production.
- The investment aims to meet growing customer demands, mitigate supply chain risks, and reduce reliance on long-distance logistics.
- Updates will be made to the existing space within the Shippensburg factory to install assembly lines, integrate more automation technologies into the manufacturing process, and train employees.
- Melker Jernberg serves as the Head of Volvo CE.
- Scott Young serves as the Head of Region North America for Volvo Construction Equipment.
- Over 50% of Volvo CE's North American machine supply can be built in Shippensburg as a result of the increased production capacity.
- The increase in production capacity will result in shorter lead times while creating opportunities for supplier growth.
- Supply chain risks will be mitigated by expanding domestic supplier bases, allowing the company to more nimbly manage economic or regulatory challenges.
- Fostering collaboration with local suppliers and customers will better position the company for sustained growth and innovation.
- Volvo CE acquired the Shippensburg site in 2007 from Ingersoll Rand.
- Volvo CE relocated its regional headquarters to Shippensburg in 2012.
- On top of recent investments, Volvo CE will invest approximately $40 million locally over the next five years.
- The announcement is described as a win for central Pennsylvania, showing the company's continued commitment to the local community and its economic vitality.
- Volvo Construction Equipment is a leading international manufacturer of premium construction equipment with over 10,000 employees.
- Volvo CE offers a wide range of products and services in more than 140 countries through its global distribution network.
- Volvo CE is part of the Volvo Group, which employs more than 101,000 people and serves customers in almost 190 markets.
- In 2024, the Volvo Group's net sales amounted to SEK 527 billion (EUR 46 billion).
