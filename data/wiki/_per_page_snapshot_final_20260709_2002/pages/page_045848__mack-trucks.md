## Facts

- Mack Trucks is targeting a 10% Class 8 truck share in North America.
- Mack's leadership team is led by Stephen Roy.
- Mack acquired the Commercial Vehicle Group Inc. (CVG) plant located in Kings Mountain, North Carolina.
- The CVG facility employs 230 people and focuses solely on cab production for commercial vehicles.
- The acquisition of the CVG plant closed on October 1.
- Mack projects it will recapture its historical market share range of about 8.5%.
- Volvo Group executives noted that prior production issues caused "a significant loss of volumes."
- Mack's dealer networks span over 500 locations across North America.
- Half of Mack's current unit sales are tied to the long-haul segment.
- Mack plans to unveil a new flagship semi in 2025.
- Mack is enhancing the Roanoke Valley Operations in Virginia for medium-duty production capacity expansion.
- The CVG plant acquisition represents a $40 million investment accelerating cab production efficiency.
- Volvo Group is injecting capital into existing plants to accommodate newly launched models.
- Mack aims to provide flexible financing, in-depth training for fleet operators, and an expanded network of certified repair centers.
- The forthcoming 2025 model promises enhanced fuel efficiency and innovative connectivity features.
- Stephen Roy highlighted that the strategic move was "really, really important" for reversing capacity problems that have lingered since 2019.
- Dealers have "invested while we've been short," according to Roy, indicating readiness to stock more vehicles as supply catches up.
- Volvo Group is working toward a substantial North American market share by 2030 across its family of commercial vehicle brands.
- The group drives investments in electric and alternative-fuel trucks worldwide.
- Mack's immediate emphasis remains on diesel platforms for the Class 8 and medium-duty segments.
- Mack anticipates increased volume, streamlined workflow with shorter lead times, and elevated quality control from dedicating the CVG plant to its production needs.
