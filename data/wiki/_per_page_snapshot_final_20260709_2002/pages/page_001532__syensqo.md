# Syensqo

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2024-04-25

**Source:** [Syensqo Breaks Ground on New EV Material Facility in Augusta - Augusta Economic Development Authority](https://augustaeda.org/uncategorized/syensqo-breaks-ground-on-new-ev-material-facility-in-augusta/)  ·  `page_001532`

## Facts

- April 25, 2024: The Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) joined executive leadership from Syensqo and local/state representatives to celebrate the start of construction on Syensqo’s new battery-grade PVDF manufacturing facility in Augusta.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Georgia is developing an e-mobility supply chain to position the state as a leader in the industry for years to come, and expressed excitement for Syensqo to become part of the ecosystem and community.
- The new Augusta facility will create approximately 100 new full-time, highly skilled local manufacturing jobs in Richmond County.
- The facility’s products will support Georgia’s growing EV manufacturing ecosystem by providing material for more than 5 million EV batteries per year at full capacity.
- The project will create hundreds of jobs throughout the value chain.
- Syensqo was born from the demerger of Solvay in December 2023 and is now a standalone listed company.
- Syensqo has a portfolio addressing electrification, lightweighting, advanced connectivity, resource efficiency, improving quality of life, and sustainable sourcing.
- The company’s U.S. headquarters in Alpharetta and its existing manufacturing facility in Augusta support more than 800 jobs in Georgia.
- Mike Finelli, Chief Technology & Innovation Officer and Chief North America Officer at Syensqo, thanked GDEcD for their support in expanding the Augusta footprint, noting it enables new jobs, reinforces the local economy, and underscores Augusta's strategic importance in the EV supply chain along the Battery Belt.
- The new facility is located at 3702 Clanton Road in Augusta.
- The facility will supply polyvinylidene fluoride (PVDF), used as a lithium-ion binder and separator coating in electric vehicle batteries.
- The plant will be the largest PVDF production facility in North America.
- The facility will be hiring mechanics, technicians, engineers, and more.
- Augusta Mayor Garnett Johnson noted Syensqo has been part of the community for over two decades as a premier employer, expressed excitement about the expansion and quality jobs, and thanked partners.
- Augusta Economic Development Authority Chairman Wayne A. Gossage, Jr. stated Syensqo fits the goal of creating well-paying, skilled manufacturing jobs, highlighted Augusta-Richmond County as the right place for manufacturing to grow, and thanked local leadership.
- Senior Project Manager Adela Kelley represented GDEcD’s Global Commerce team on this competitive project in partnership with the Augusta Economic Development Authority, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia EMC.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated companies are making once-in-a-generation investments for electrification, expressed excitement that Syensqo selected Georgia for a critical EV supply chain piece, noted Georgia's forefront position in attracting e-mobility and battery investments thanks to state leadership and Governor Kemp, and congratulated all parties.
- The Syensqo Augusta site is a partner of the Giving Your Best Award with WJBF, Piedmont Hospital, and Security Federal Bank for volunteerism recognition.
- Syensqo is supporting higher education institutions in Georgia, including Augusta Tech, with workforce development projects.
- In 2022, Syensqo opened its New Applications Development Lab at its R&I Center in Alpharetta to support customers in the EV, renewable energy, and aerospace industries.
- The inauguration ceremony for the Alpharetta lab was attended by Her Royal Highness Princess Astrid of Belgium, high-ranking Belgian officials, and State of Georgia leadership.
- In the last four years, Syensqo has invested more than $30 million in the Alpharetta site.
