# Charging and Fueling Infrastructure (CFI) Discretionary Grant Program

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-08-28

**Source:** [Applications Open for $1.3 Billion in Funding to Expand National Electric Vehicle Charging Network and Alternative Fueling Infrastructure | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/cmei/fuels/articles/applications-open-13-billion-funding-expand-national-electric-vehicle-charging)  ·  `page_004739`

## Facts

- The Joint Office of Energy and Transportation opened applications for a historic $1.3 billion funding opportunity for electric vehicle charging and alternative fueling infrastructure, including hydrogen fueling infrastructure.
- The funding targets deployment in urban and rural communities as well as along designated highways, interstates, and major roadways.
- The funding opportunity is made possible by the Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act's Charging and Fueling Infrastructure (CFI) Discretionary Grant Program.
- Additional funds are sourced from the National Electric Vehicle Infrastructure (NEVI) Formula Program set aside for strategic grants to states and local governments.
- The CFI program is supported by unifying guidance and technical assistance from the Joint Office of Energy and Transportation.
- The CFI program is administered by the Federal Highway Administration.
- Eligible applicants include state, regional, Tribal, and local government entities.
- The CFI program is divided into two distinct grant funding categories: Community Charging and Fueling Grants and Alternative Fuel Corridor Grants.
- The program requires that 50% of the funding over five years be made available for both communities and corridors.
- Community Charging and Fueling Grants will strategically deploy publicly accessible EV charging infrastructure and hydrogen, propane, and natural gas fueling infrastructure in urban and rural communities.
- Alternative Fuel Corridor Grants will strategically deploy publicly accessible EV charging infrastructure and hydrogen, propane, and natural gas fueling infrastructure along designated alternative fuel corridors.
- Eligible applicants also include metropolitan planning organizations, port authorities, Indian Tribes, and U.S. territories.
- Applications are due by August 28, 2024.
