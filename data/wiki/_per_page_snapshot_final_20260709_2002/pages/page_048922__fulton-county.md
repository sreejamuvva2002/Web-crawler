# Fulton County

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- Coca-Cola Co.’s home base in Atlanta and Fulton County continues to be a Southern corporate and cultural icon.
- John Pemberton was motivated to concoct the Coca-Cola drink in 1886 in part because of Prohibition legislation passed by the city and county.
- Coca-Cola Co. Chairman and CEO Muhtar Kent said, “Simply put, there would be no Coca-Cola without Atlanta … and Atlantans.”
- Coca-Cola is investing more than $250 million in a strategic expansion of its metro-area production and supply system facilities to meet the needs of its Freestyle fountain dispensing systems.
- The Coca-Cola investment will create 100 new jobs by the end of 2012 at Coke and its primary supplier.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development and the Development Authority of Fulton County assisted Coca-Cola in a $100-million plant expansion to manufacture ingredients used in those machines.
- Fulton County comprises Atlanta and 13 other municipalities within its boundaries.
- Ken Dobson serves as the economic development administrator for Fulton County.
- Progress Partners of North Fulton Atlanta is a coalition launched last year to support regional economic development.
- The Development Authority of Fulton County encompasses 534.5 square miles of unincorporated areas and municipalities, including the City of Atlanta.
- The Development Authority of Fulton County has issued more than $16 billion in bonds and helped well over 300 businesses and institutions build, upgrade, renovate, expand or relocate in Fulton County.
- Fulton County is interweaving asset-based, knowledge-based, and technology-based economic development approaches into one integrated process.
- The county's economic development strategy starts with engaging institutional and corporate leaders as champions and forming an economic development advisory group.
- Six topical committees will feed into the advisory group, focusing on real estate development, technology-based entrepreneurship, sustainable development, schools and work-force education, development finance, and international trade and commerce.
- Metro Atlanta hosts more than 60 foreign governments' consulates and trade offices, many located in Fulton County.
- In May, Fulton County formalized an agreement with the State of Bahia, Brazil, to promote cooperation and joint initiatives between the two governments.
- Fulton County Chairman John Eaves and fellow county commissioners formed the Fulton County Economic Development Division in December 2011 with a county-wide focus.
- Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport is the state's largest employer at 58,000 employees.
- The new Maynard H. Jackson Jr. International Terminal at the airport is expected to create another 1,500 direct and indirect jobs.
- The airport's total economic impact was assessed at $32.6 billion in 2009.
- Delta Airlines, the airport's largest tenant, employs 30,000 people.
- The world's busiest airport saw 92 million passengers pass through in 2011 and hosts 950,000 takeoffs and landings a year.
- The airport just opened its new $1.4-billion Maynard Jackson International Terminal and is seeing increased cargo activity.
- Louis Miller serves as the aviation general manager at Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport.
- Porsche Cars North America decided to locate its headquarters at the former Ford Motor Co. plant site at the end of one of the airport runways.
- The Porsche headquarters complex is designed by HOK, with development partner Jacoby pursuing related development around the site.
- Tim Quinn, vice president for after sales at Porsche Cars North America, stated that Atlanta has turned out to be almost the ideal place for their headquarters.
- The decision to stay in Atlanta was the result of a thorough evaluation process that spanned metro Atlanta and several out-of-state sites.
- The new Porsche headquarters will house PCNA, Porsche Financial Services, Inc., and Porsche Business Services, Inc., both currently located in Lisle, Illinois.
- A total workforce of 400 will be deployed at a technical service and training center, as well as a customer experience center that will feature a handling road course.
- Partners in attracting Porsche included the Georgia Dept. of Economic Development, Atlanta Development Authority, the Fulton County Development Authority, the City of Hapeville, Georgia Power, Jacoby Development, and Cushman & Wakefield.
- Porsche plans to move into its new headquarters during the second half of 2013.
- Stan Conway is senior vice president and director of development for Majestic Realty Co., overseeing 11 million sq. ft. in Dallas and Atlanta.
- Majestic Realty has built some 9 million sq. ft. in South Fulton along South Fulton Parkway in the past decade, including a build-to-suit for Kraft and a facility in Union City for biotech manufacturer Dendreon.
- County officials were instrumental in getting Majestic Realty going on South Fulton Parkway, balancing resident needs with industry needs and understanding logistics business drivers.
- County taxes in Fulton County have remained quite low relative to the rest of the country, especially among major logistics centers.
- The Fulton Industrial Boulevard corridor in unincorporated South Fulton comprises more than 4,500 acres of industrial and commercial property.
- The State of Georgia granted the Fulton Industrial Boulevard corridor Opportunity Zone status through 2020, featuring tax incentives for creating as few as two jobs.
- Moses Brown has lived in the Atlanta area since 1971 and recently retired from Reed Elsevier Group.
- Brown was part of the Equifax team that decided in 1987 to move some operations to Alpharetta, building a data center there in 1989 and a second facility in 1991.
- Equifax spun off Choicepoint, which later became part of Reed Elsevier’s LexisNexis Risk Solutions business, still operating in Alpharetta alongside other corporate residents.
- Brown serves as vice chairman of the North Fulton Community Improvement District, a self-taxing district that has invested more than $13.6 million in $75 million worth of new interchange, roadway, and bridge work.
- The infrastructure improvements in the North Fulton Community Improvement District have sped up commutes for many of the 77,000 employees who work in the district.
- Majestic Realty recently acquired developer CRB, which had successful projects in Johns Creek and mid-Fulton.
- Parkway South is a plan by CRB for some 2,500 acres along South Fulton Parkway that could be a potential hit for the industrial sector.
