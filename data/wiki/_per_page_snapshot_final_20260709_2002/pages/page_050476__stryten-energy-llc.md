# Stryten Energy LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-06

**Source:** [Major U.S. Energy Storage Expansion: Stryten Adds 10GW Manufacturing Capacity | LGO Stock News](https://www.stocktitan.net/news/LGO/stryten-energy-unveils-plan-to-add-10-gigawatts-of-new-u-s-energy-m3tb03grrajn.html)  ·  `page_050476`

## Facts

- Stryten Energy LLC announced a new plan expected to expand its domestic manufacturing capacity to 24 Gigawatts.
- The expansion focuses on increasing production across the company's 11 U.S. manufacturing and battery component plants.
- More than 2,500 people currently work at these U.S. facilities producing batteries for military and government, data centers, grid storage, automotive, and material handling applications.
- The new investment is supported by existing advanced manufacturing production tax credits to accelerate capacity growth while supporting the domestic industrial base.
- Mike Judd, President and Chief Executive Officer of Stryten Energy, stated that the company is committed to growing its domestic energy storage capacity to serve U.S. customers and partners.
- The expansion will increase the domestic supply of batteries for military, long-duration energy storage, transportation, and material-handling applications.
- Stryten advances energy storage in the U.S. with a suite of domestically manufactured battery solutions using advanced lead, lithium, and vanadium redox flow battery technology.
- The company operates plants in Kansas, Georgia, Iowa, Virginia, and Arkansas.
- Plans to increase gigawatt production capacity will bring hundreds of high-paying manufacturing jobs and deliver a positive economic impact to surrounding communities.
- Stryten operates a domestic, vertically integrated supply chain that provides battery components to its factories and the broader U.S. battery manufacturing industry.
- The company's plants in Pennsylvania, New York, Indiana, Missouri, and Wisconsin ensure a reliable U.S. source for critical materials needed to manufacture batteries.
- The investment plan includes increasing domestic processing capacity of recycled plastic from spent batteries to produce new battery components for automobiles, forklifts, and locomotives.
- Stryten recently opened a lithium battery assembly plant in Georgia that supports military and industrial battery needs using proprietary lithium module technology.
- The company is partnering with Dragonfly Energy, headquartered in Reno, Nevada, to bring advanced lithium batteries to its automotive aftermarket and battery distributor partners.
- Stryten Critical E-Storage LLC, an affiliate of Stryten Energy, recently announced the launch of Storion Energy LLC jointly with a subsidiary of Largo Inc. and Largo Clean Energy Corp.
- Storion Energy is focused on removing barriers for battery manufacturers to domestically source price-competitive electrolytes used in vanadium redox flow batteries for long-duration energy storage applications in the U.S.
- Stryten Energy is headquartered in Alpharetta, Georgia.
- The company provides energy storage solutions across Essential Power, Motive Power, Transportation, Military, and Government sectors.
- Stryten's energy storage technologies include advanced lead, lithium, and vanadium redox flow batteries, intelligent chargers, and energy performance management software.
