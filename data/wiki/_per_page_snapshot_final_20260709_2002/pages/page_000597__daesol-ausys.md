# Daesol Ausys

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-08-31

**Source:** [South Korean auto supplier plans $72 million plant in Georgia to build electric vehicle parts, ETEnergyworld](https://energy.economictimes.indiatimes.com/news/power/south-korean-auto-supplier-plans-72-million-plant-in-georgia-to-build-electric-vehicle-parts/103235988)  ·  `page_000597`

## Facts

- Daesol Ausys is a South Korean auto supplier planning to build a $72 million plant in Georgia to manufacture electric vehicle parts.
- The company announced plans on a Tuesday to construct a factory in West Point, Georgia, specifically to produce luggage boards and covers for EV compartments.
- Daesol Ausys currently supplies automotive parts to Hyundai, Kia, and General Motors.
- This new facility will be the third factory in the same West Point industrial park built by members of the company's corporate family.
- Daehan Solution Co. opened a $1 million factory in West Point in 2008 to manufacture headliners and other sound insulation parts for the nearby Kia plant.
- Daesol Material opened a $35 million factory in West Point in 2017 to produce automotive fiber padding.
- Daehan Solution currently employs 350 full-time workers, while Daesol Material employs 50 workers.
- Daehan Solution also operates a manufacturing plant near Montgomery, Alabama, that supplies parts to the Hyundai assembly plant there.
- The announcement follows Hyundai's 2022 plan to build a $5.5 billion electric vehicle and battery assembly plant in Ellabell, Georgia, near Savannah.
- The Hyundai Ellabell site is projected to grow to 8,100 employees and is slated to begin vehicle production in 2025.
- EV suppliers have pledged to invest nearly $2.2 billion and hire 5,000 people for the broader Georgia project ecosystem.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp stated that the state's automotive industry employs over 50,000 Georgians and will continue expanding as e-mobility suppliers choose locations across the state.
- Daesol Ausys is scheduled to begin production at its new West Point facility in December 2024.
- The new plant will hire more than 140 workers.
- Daesol Ausys will pay hourly wages ranging from $15 to $20 per hour.
- The state of Georgia will fund worker training for the project.
- The state provided $500,000 last year to help Harris County clear and grade the site and build a road, with the county matching the funds using sales tax collections.
- The city of West Point and Harris County are granting eight years of property tax breaks worth a projected $5.7 million.
- Daesol Ausys could qualify for $875,000 in state income tax credits, calculated at $1,250 per job over five years, provided workers earn at least $31,300 annually.
