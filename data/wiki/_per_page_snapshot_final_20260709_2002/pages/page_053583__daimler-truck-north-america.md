# Daimler Truck North America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-04-13

## Facts

- Autoworkers in the South are currently engaged in a historic, high-stakes labor struggle against the multinational corporation Daimler Truck North America.
- The labor contract between DTNA and seven thousand United Auto Workers (UAW) members is set to expire at midnight on April 26.
- UAW members build heavy trucks and buses at plants in North Carolina, Georgia, and Tennessee.
- Daimler Truck posts multibillion-dollar profits.
- DTNA workers report being beaten down by stagnant wages, the rising cost of living due to inflation, and an increasingly demoralizing work environment.
- UAW members are fighting for wage increases, improved health insurance coverage, and an end to divisive tiered employment structures, among other demands.
- If their demands are not met, autoworkers say they are ready to strike.
