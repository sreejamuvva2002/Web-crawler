# TE Connectivity

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-07

**Source:** [TE Connectivity boosts sales despite tax hit (Update)](https://www.inquirer.com/philly/blogs/inq-phillydeals/te-connectivity-boosts-sales-takes-tax-hit-20180124.html)  ·  `page_060318`

## Facts

- TE Connectivity reported a surge in new orders from clients such as Google, Facebook, and global carmakers.
- Net sales for the three months ended Dec. 31 hit $3.5 billion, up 14 percent from a year earlier.
- More than half the growth came from new sales by established TE Connectivity businesses, with the rest from newly acquired units.
- The company expects sales will total around $3.6 billion in the quarter ending March 31, up 12 percent over last year.
- TE Connectivity reported a quarterly loss totaling $40 million, or 11 cents a share, due to a tax restatement that wiped out fourth-quarter net income.
- The company was forced to declare past losses it had hoped to use for future tax relief as a result of last year's U.S. income tax overhaul.
- TE Connectivity has been reviewing facilities for closure and sale, including its former AMP/Tyco Electronics office building in Harrisburg which is for sale because the company moved offices to Middletown.
- CEO Terrence Curtin told investors that because the company's legal headquarters is in Switzerland, it will not benefit from lower U.S. corporate rates and tax legislation will have little impact on investment decisions.
- A majority of TE Connectivity's customers and factories are located outside the United States.
- The company boosted sales by concentrating on factory automation sensors at a time when manufacturers are rapidly replacing human workers with robots.
- New orders for the quarter totaled around $3.9 billion, including plans for undersea cables linking Europe, the U.S., and South America.
- In the automotive sector, TE Connectivity's transport margins increased strongly and sales grew several times faster than world auto production.
- Demand for TE Connectivity auto electronics connectors, sensors, and assemblies is decoupled from global production and rising even where car and truck sales are not going up, noted Bank of America analyst Wamsi Mohan.
- Electric vehicles are still a small market today compared with internal-combustion cars and trucks, noted Morgan Stanley analyst Craig Hettenbach.
- The decline in diesel-vehicle sales following revelations that Volkswagen lied about engine pollution boosted demand for electric-car parts faster than expected, especially in Europe.
- TE Connectivity now supplies around $62 worth of components for every electrical vehicle made.
- CEO Terrence Curtin said the company has developed programs to sell up to $500 worth of sensors and assemblies per vehicle, whether it's a wireless or a connected solution.
- Chinese vehicle-electronics parts demand has been very strong.
- The move by U.S. companies to expand data centers supporting remote cloud-computing and wireless applications has also juiced sales.
- TE Connectivity's SubCom business recently announced orders to run private cables under the Atlantic to Denmark and under the Pacific to Chile for Google and its partners.
- Manufacturers of medical devices, appliances, vehicles, and data services are rapidly automating their processes regardless of unit sales, driving demand for TE Connectivity's components.
- CEO Terrence Curtin concluded that the company is benefiting from cloud computing and miniaturization trends, with heavier take-up rates as volumes strengthen.
