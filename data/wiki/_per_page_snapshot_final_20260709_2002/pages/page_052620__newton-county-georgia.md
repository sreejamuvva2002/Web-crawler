# Newton County, Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2023-06-27

**Source:** [Newton County: People, Partnership, Progress - Georgia Trend Magazine](https://www.georgiatrend.com/2023/06/27/newton-county-people-partnership-progress/)  ·  `page_052620`

## Facts

- Serra Hall, executive director of the Newton County Industrial Development Authority (NCIDA), states that Newton County has become recognized as a 'boom town' with $6.9 billion announced and over 9,000 quality jobs committed in the last two years.
- According to NCIDA, the median income in Newton County has increased 35% in the last 10 years, and the county’s tax digest has more than doubled in the last eight years, from $2 billion to $4.2 billion.
- Debbie Harper, president of the Newton County Chamber of Commerce, identifies advanced manufacturing, biopharma, film/gaming/music, and commercial as the four main sectors that keep the industrial base virtually recession-resistant.
- Piedmont Healthcare recently opened a new 30,000-square-foot medical office complex anchoring the redeveloped Eastside Crossing Shopping Center with a $10 million investment.
- The Piedmont Healthcare offices are located about a mile from Piedmont Newton Hospital and will eventually house cardiology, neurology, endocrinology, and rheumatology practices.
- Approximately 5,000 new residential units are in the pipeline to account for projected population growth of 8.75% by 2026, according to the Covington mayor’s office.
- Mayor Steve Horton states that due to industrial, commercial, and residential growth, the City of Covington is investing significant resources into rehabilitation and improvements to water and sewer infrastructure.
- The Newton County Water and Sewerage Authority, the Newton County Board of Commissioners, and the City of Covington were awarded a $212.8 million drinking water grant in January.
- Mike Hopkins, executive director of the Newton County Water and Sewerage Authority, notes this is the most funding his organization has received in 53 years, far exceeding the previous record of $900,000 in 2009.
- The $212.8 million grant was funded through the American Rescue Plan Act via the State of Georgia’s $400 million Drinking Water Projects to Support Increased Population (DWPSIP) grant program.
- Tres Thomas, Covington’s city manager, states the joint governmental application was one of only three to be awarded funds and highlights the success as an example of intragovernmental collaboration.
- A majority of the Newton County Water and Sewerage Authority’s $91 million portion of the grant will go to the county water reuse facility.
- The county water reuse facility will return water to users, taking pressure off the county’s potable water supply while allowing for additional growth without additional strain on water resources.
- Marcello Banes, chair of the Newton County Board of Commissioners, states that Takeda, Facebook (Meta), and Rivian found Newton County through a long-standing vision to work collaboratively on its multicounty industrial park.
- Stanton Springs industrial park supports sustainable development and hosts major businesses including Meta data centers, Takeda Pharmaceuticals, and Rivian.
- Covington is investing $52 million to upgrade the Eastside Sewer Basin, improve the Eastside Lift Station, and improve the Water Reclamation Facility using a combination of grant dollars and a loan from the Georgia Environmental Finance Authority.
- Covington uses a Georgia Environmental Protection Division-sponsored Land Application System (LAS) that is jointly owned by the city and the Newton County Water and Sewerage Authority.
- The LAS covers 2,000 acres of vegetated land that naturally filters wastewater while growing trees for timber and soybeans for livestock feed.
- Covington’s Green Fuels Facility is one of the few public compressed natural gas filling stations in Georgia with plans to incorporate electric vehicle chargers in the near future.
- Mayor Horton states Covington has leveraged sustainable resources and partnered with a local nonprofit, Sustainable Newton, to integrate environmentally sustainable initiatives and educate citizens.
- Ascend Elements opened the largest lithium-ion battery recycling facility in North America in Covington in March with a $43 million investment.
- CEO Mike O’Kronley states the Ascend Elements facility is the first commercial-scale EV battery recycling operation and delivers annual capacity to process 30,000 metric tons of lithium-ion batteries or the equivalent of about 70,000 EV batteries.
- Ascend Elements’ facility occupies a converted 154,000-square-foot industrial building.
- As of April, Ascend Elements employed approximately 100 people in Newton County with plans to hire 85 more team members in the next year.
- O’Kronley states proximity to electric vehicle battery manufacturing facilities, accessibility to railroads, highways, and renewable power sources were key locational advantages for choosing Newton County.
- The Newton County Industrial Development Authority and the Georgia Department of Economic Development played important roles in Ascend Elements' site selection.
- Archer Aviation broke ground on a new electric vertical takeoff and landing aircraft (eVTOL) manufacturing facility next to the Covington Municipal Airport in March.
- Glen Burks, vice president of manufacturing at Archer, states the Silicon Valley-based aerospace company will advance $118 million in investment over the next decade.
- Burks projects that as production output increases, the facility will create upwards of 1,000 new jobs long-term for the local community.
- The two-building Archer Aviation facility will be able to produce up to 2,300 eVTOL aircraft per year after completion in the first half of 2024.
- The Archer Aviation site is more than 96 acres located less than 1.5 miles from Interstate 20.
- Burks states Newton County stood out during site selection due to proactive planning, affordable housing solutions, and improved infrastructure including roads, school systems, and utilities built ahead of growth.
- Mayor Horton notes Covington’s involvement in the film industry dates back more than 70 years, with iconic productions including The Dukes of Hazzard, Cannonball Run, In the Heat of the Night, Remember the Titans, The Vampire Diaries, and Sweet Magnolias.
- Three Ring Studios operates just outside city limits as a sign that Covington’s reputation as a regional filming destination will continue to thrive.
- A quarter-million tourists are expected to visit the area each year.
- A record 96,383 people from 57 countries checked in at the Covington Welcome Center in 2022, according to the city’s community development office.
- Mayor Horton reports that surging hotel-motel tax revenue translated into $200 in property tax savings on average per resident in 2022.
- Debbie Harper states the Newton County Chamber of Commerce is partnering with the county on a new comprehensive plan nearing completion to offer strategic vision for rural estate living, multiuse development, and walkable communities.
- Sara Vinson, Sustainable Newton co-founder, states the organization is educating people about the Inflation Reduction Act (2022 law) that will lower energy costs for consumers and reduce U.S. carbon pollution by up to 40% from 2005 levels by 2030.
- Sustainable Newton wants to ensure Newton County households are aware of IRA tax credits and rebates to go solar, electrify appliances and vehicles, and make energy efficiency improvements.
- A grant from Meta is funding the installation of a solar-energy system for a local early childhood education nonprofit.
- David Eady, mayor of Oxford and senior manager at the Ray C. Anderson Center for Sustainable Business at Georgia Tech’s Scheller College of Business, connected Sustainable Newton with students to gather data on converting the City of Covington fleet to electric vehicles and reinstituting glass recycling.
- General Mills, one of Newton County’s largest manufacturers, sponsored the annual 5K Cheerios Challenge race on Earth Day to showcase electric vehicles, bicycles, and yard tools while raising awareness of IRA benefits through an interactive Rewiring America IRA calculator exhibit.
- Maurice Carter, Sustainable Newton co-founder, notes Yale University reports 53% of Americans are alarmed or concerned about global warming, but only 8% of citizens are actively engaged in climate action.
