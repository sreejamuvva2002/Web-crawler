# Hwashin Co.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hyundai Supplier Hwashin to Build USD 176M Georgia Plant: Rediff Moneynews](https://money.rediff.com/news/market/hyundai-supplier-hwashin-to-build-usd-176m-georgia-plant/1204120231101)  ·  `page_056063`

## Facts

- South Korean auto parts maker Hwashin Co. will invest USD 176 million in a new Georgia plant to supply Hyundai's electric vehicle facility.
- The investment will create 460 jobs.
- Hwashin is investing more than USD 176 million and hiring more than 460 employees for the new Georgia facility.
- The company plans to build its plant in the town of Dublin, located in the middle of Georgia.
- Production at the new Georgia plant is planned to start in 2025.
- The facility will produce metal pieces for auto chassis.
- Chassis parts will be supplied to the new Hyundai Motor Group plant being built in Ellabell.
- Parts will also be supplied to sister company Kia's facility in West Point.
- Hwashin is the latest supplier to announce a plant for Hyundai's USD 7.6 billion EV and battery assembly plant in Ellabell, near Savannah.
- Hyundai's Ellabell plant was announced last year.
- The Hyundai plant could grow to 8,500 employees.
- The Hyundai plant could begin producing vehicles as early as 2024.
- Suppliers to the Hyundai Ellabell plant have collectively pledged to invest more than USD 2.4 billion.
- These suppliers have pledged to hire nearly 6,300 people.
- Hwashin is headquartered in Yeongcheon, South Korea.
- Hwashin previously established a plant in Greenville, Alabama, to supply Hyundai in 2003.
- The Alabama plant has expanded multiple times and currently employs 750 people.
- Hwashin supplies parts to other manufacturers, including Volkswagen AG in Chattanooga, Tennessee.
- The state of Georgia will pay for worker training at the new facility.
- Hwashin could qualify for USD 9.2 million in state income tax credits.
- The tax credits are valued at USD 4,000 per job over five years.
- To qualify for the tax credits, workers must earn at least USD 31,300 a year.
- Local officials could grant additional property tax breaks to the company.
- The company is acquiring 60 acres (24 hectares) for the new plant.
- The land is part of a larger site that local officials have already cleared and graded.
