# Nidec Kabushiki gaisha

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2017-03-31

**Source:** [Nidec - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nidec)  ·  `page_058087`

## Facts

- Nidec Corporation is a Japanese manufacturer and distributor of electric motors.
- Its products are found in automobiles, hard-disk drives, electric appliances, commercial equipment, and manufacturing equipment.
- Automotive products account for 22% of the company's sales.
- Hard-disk drive motors account for 16% of sales.
- The company is the global market leader for spindle motors that power hard-disk drives.
- As of 2017, Nidec operates 296 subsidiary companies located across Japan, Asia, Europe, and the Americas.
- Consolidated headcount reached 107,062 employees as of March 31, 2017.
- In 2006, Nidec established Nidec Motors & Actuators by acquiring the motor and actuator business of French company Valeo S.A.
- On January 31, 2017, Nidec acquired Leroy-Somer from Emerson Electric, forming Nidec Leroy-Somer Holding.
- On July 31, 2017, Nidec acquired Secop GmbH, a German hermetic compressor manufacturer, from Aurelius Equity Group.
- In April 2018, Nidec entered an agreement with Whirlpool Corporation to acquire the refrigeration compressor business of Embraco.
- In November 2018, Nidec became a shareholder of Taiwan-based cooling company Nidec Chaun-Choung Technology Corporation, increasing its stake to 67% by December 2020.
- Due to antitrust concerns, Nidec sold its Secop business in 2019.
- The company changed its name from Nippon Densan Corporation to Nidec Kabushiki gaisha in April 2023.
- Shigenobu Nagamori serves as chairman of the board, president, and CEO.
- FY 2017 revenue was US$11.15 billion (JPY 1199.3 billion).
- Nidec is listed on the Tokyo Stock Exchange under ticker TYO: 6594 and is a constituent of the TOPIX 100 index.
