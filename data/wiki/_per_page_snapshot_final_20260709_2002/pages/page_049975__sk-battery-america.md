# SK Battery America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-04-08

**Source:** [US says SK Battery faces $77,200 in fines over employee health hazards](https://bluewaterhealthyliving.com/us-says-sk-battery/)  ·  `page_049975`

## Facts

- The U.S. Labor Department said on Monday that SK Battery America faces $77,200 in fines for five serious safety violations.
- Workers at the company's Georgia plant suffered potentially permanent respiratory damage from an October 2023 lithium battery fire.
- This is the second violation notice issued by the department against SK Battery America in recent months.
- In January, OSHA said SK Battery exposed employees at a U.S. battery plant to unsafe levels of nickel and other metals.
- The company faces $75,000 in fines over six serious violations from the January citation.
- SK Battery supplies Volkswagen and Ford Motor.
- The company employs 3,100 workers at its Commerce, Georgia plant.
- OSHA Area Office Director Joshua Turner stated that on multiple occasions in less than a year, they found SK Battery America failing to meet required federal safety standards for workers.
- OSHA emphasized that when employers fail to provide safe and healthful workplaces, it will hold them accountable.
- SK Battery America stated it is working diligently to assess the issues raised and resolve any questions with the ultimate goal of employee safety.
- The company stated that it has comprehensive safety protocols and training programs in place.
- SK Battery America is a subsidiary of SK On, a global EV battery manufacturer.
- SK On is a unit of SK Innovation, which is part of SK Group, the second-largest conglomerate in South Korea.
- SK Battery America did not immediately comment on Monday regarding the new citation.
- The company has challenged the findings from the January citation before the independent Occupational Safety and Health Review Commission.
- OSHA said following the October 2023 fire that SK Battery failed to train its employees on how to protect themselves adequately in the toxic atmosphere that left multiple workers injured.
- OSHA said SK exposed workers to inhalation hazards, including hydrofluoric acid vapors produced in lithium battery fires, by failing to establish a complete emergency response plan.
- OSHA said SK Battery did not ensure its staffing agency Moveret Inc., which supplied workers at the site, made their employees aware of the hazards associated with lithium battery fires.
- OSHA said in January that SK Battery exposed employees working with cobalt, nickel and manganese to respiratory hazards by failing to complete a workplace hazard assessment.
- The company failed to ensure employees were given clean, disinfected and sanitary respirators.
- The company failed to store respirators properly.
