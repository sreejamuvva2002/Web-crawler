# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-10-25

**Source:** [Hyundai breaks ground on $5.5B electric car plant in Georgia](https://wgxa.tv/news/state-news/hyundai-breaks-ground-on-55b-electric-car-plant-in-georgia)  ·  `page_009170`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group broke ground on its first U.S. plant dedicated to making electric vehicles in Bryan County west of Savannah, Georgia.
- The manufacturing plant represents a $5.5 billion investment.
- Vast areas of the site have already been cleared of trees ahead of the groundbreaking ceremony.
- The factory is planned to open in 2025.
- The facility will employ at least 8,100 workers.
- Production capacity will be up to 300,000 electric vehicles per year.
- Euisun Chung, executive chairman of Hyundai Motor Group, stated the plant will be the envy of the industry.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp called it the largest economic development project the state has ever seen.
- Gov. Kemp described the project as transformational for the region's current and future generations.
- The groundbreaking ceremony took place two weeks before the Nov. 8 election.
- A yellow-and-black robotic dog delivered champagne flutes for Gov. Kemp and top Hyundai executives during a celebratory toast.
- Officials and dignitaries participated in turning ceremonial scoops of dirt at the event.
- Gov. Kemp and Sen. Raphael Warnock shared a brief handshake after the ceremony.
- Sen. Raphael Warnock, a Democratic junior U.S. Senator, stated he wants to see more jobs in Georgia.
- Warnock's Senate race against Republican Herschel Walker will help determine party control of the U.S. Senate.
- Gov. Kemp faces an Election Day rematch with Democrat Stacey Abrams.
- State and local officials worked to lure Hyundai to Georgia with a package of $1.8 billion in tax breaks and incentives.
- President Joe Biden has embraced boosting U.S. production and sales of electric vehicles as part of his strategy for slowing climate change and adding manufacturing jobs.
- The White House recently announced $2.8 billion in grants to ramp up EV battery production in 12 states.
- A sweeping climate and health care law passed by congressional Democrats in August includes incentives for EV manufacturing and purchasing.
- The law provides tax credits for EV buyers worth up to $7,500.
- Changes to the tax credit limit eligibility only to electric vehicles produced in North America.
- Hyundai EVs will not qualify for the tax credit until the company starts production in Georgia.
- South Korea's ambassador to the U.S., Tae-yong Cho, told dignitaries that his country believes the revised EV tax credit unfairly excludes Hyundai.
- Ambassador Cho stated Korean companies are at risk of being disadvantaged by the provision and that limiting consumer choices is not good for Korea’s partnership, Georgia, or climate change efforts.
- Sen. Warnock voted in support of the Inflation Reduction Act containing the revised tax credit.
- Warnock defends the law overall as proof the U.S. is serious about investing in EV manufacturing.
- Warnock introduced a bill to push back the date the tax credit revisions take effect.
- Warnock asked the Treasury Department to use maximum flexibility in adopting regulations for the new criteria.
- Warnock stated he will continue working with Ambassador Cho and other officials to perfect the project.
- Rivian announced plans last year for a $5 billion EV plant east of Atlanta.
- Rivian plans to employ 7,500 workers at its Georgia facility.
- A Morgan County judge rejected local governments' plan to exempt Rivian from an estimated $700 million in property taxes.
- Rivian agreed to pay $300 million in lieu of the exempted property taxes.
- State and local officials are weighing an appeal of the judge’s decision regarding Rivian's tax break.
- The property tax break was a major component of the $1.2 billion in incentives offered to Rivian.
- Rivian, a California automaker, has seen its stock value decline sharply this year.
- Rivian recently announced a recall to tighten a loose fastener that could affect drivers' ability to steer.
- Gov. Kemp expressed confidence that the Rivian Georgia plant will still happen despite the tax ruling.
- Gov. Kemp noted the vast majority of people want the Rivian project to come and added excitement about the whole EV marketplace in Georgia.
