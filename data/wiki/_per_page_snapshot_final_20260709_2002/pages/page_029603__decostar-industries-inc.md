# Decostar Industries, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [EPA Reaches Agreement with Decostar Industries, Inc. in Carrollton, Georgia to Reduce Hazardous Air Emissions | U.S. EPA News Releases | US EPA](https://www.epa.gov/archive/epa/newsreleases/epa-reaches-agreement-decostar-industries-inc-carrollton-georgia-reduce-hazardous-air.html)  ·  `page_029603`

## Facts

- The U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) reached a settlement agreement with Decostar Industries, Inc. (Decostar) to address violations of the Resource Conservation and Recovery Act.
- Decostar will take steps to control levels of hazardous air emissions from their facility in Carrollton, Ga., in order to protect workers and the surrounding community and improve overall air quality.
- As part of the settlement, Decostar will implement a leak detection and repair program.
- Decostar will perform a supplemental environmental project valued at over $2.7 million.
- Decostar will pay a $377,900 civil penalty.
- EPA Region 4 Administrator Trey Glenn stated that the agreement will reduce hazardous air emissions at the Carrollton facility, protect worker health, and improve air quality in the surrounding area.
- The injunctive relief addresses specific violations concerning leak detection and repair by instituting a program to ensure compliance.
- The leak detection and repair program involves specifying the roles and responsibilities of all employees and contractor personnel assigned to monitoring.
- The program includes a tracking system that ensures new pieces of equipment added to the facility are integrated into the equipment program.
- The program requires removing pieces of equipment that are taken out of service from the program.
- Decostar will replace existing robots and paint applicators with devices that increase transfer efficiency as part of the supplemental environmental project.
- The replacement project is expected to reduce hazardous waste generated at the facility by 4,130 pounds per year.
- The project is expected to reduce hazardous air pollution emissions by 1,385 pounds per year.
- The project is expected to reduce emissions of volatile organic compounds by 1,972 pounds per year.
