# Archer Aviation Inc.

## Facts

- Archer Aviation Inc. announced operating and financial results for the first quarter ended March 31, 2026.
- Founder and CEO Adam Goldstein issued a shareholder letter discussing highlights from the quarter.
- US operations are expected to begin this year under the White House’s eVTOL Integration Pilot Program (eIPP) and in preparation for LA28 Olympic Games.
- Archer expects Midnight operations in American cities to begin this year as part of the White House’s eIPP and in preparation to serve as the Official Air Taxi Provider of the LA28 Olympic Games, in coordination with the DOT and FAA.
- Archer was recently selected as an air taxi partner in 3 winning applications encompassing 8 states, including Florida, Texas, and New York.
- In April, Archer became the first eVTOL company to close Phase 3 of the FAA’s 4-phase Type Certification process for its Midnight aircraft.
- Archer has been driving progress in Phase 4 of certification, where Midnight's compliance with FAA airworthiness requirements will be demonstrated through formal testing and analysis.
- The flight test program expanded this quarter, with piloted VTOL and CTOL flights across its expanded fleet occurring nearly every day, often multiple times a day.
- Archer took over operations of Hawthorne Airport in LA to develop the site into the airport of the future.
- Located near LAX and several major LA sports and entertainment venues, Hawthorne Airport is expected to anchor Archer’s planned LA air taxi operations while serving as an innovation hub for next-generation aviation technologies.
- Archer continues work alongside Anduril on a dual-use, hybrid, autonomous aircraft, aiming to deliver one of the most sophisticated vertical lift platforms in this category.
- Archer anticipates beginning to win phased government awards for its dual-use aircraft program later this year.
- NVIDIA is integrating its IGX Thor platform to power safety-capable onboard compute and autonomy-ready flight systems for Archer.
- Starlink will deliver high-speed, low-latency LEO satellite connectivity for Midnight aircraft.
- Partner Palantir was recently downselected as a finalist for the FAA's SMART AI project, which is instrumental to the DOT’s ~$20B air traffic control modernization effort.
- Archer ended Q1 2026 with approximately $1.8 billion in liquidity and limited debt exposure.
- Q1 2026 marked consistent financial execution, with Archer meeting its Adjusted EBITDA loss guidance for the quarter.
- Archer ended Q1 2026 with $1,775.9 million of cash, cash equivalents, and short-term investments on its balance sheet, plus an additional $7.3 million of restricted cash.
- Q1 2026 cash, cash equivalents, and short-term investments decreased by $188.8 million from Q4 2025, primarily due to $149.1 million used in operating activities and $32.6 million used for property and equipment purchases.
- Q1 2026 revenue increased by $1.3 million from Q4 2025 to $1.6 million as operations expanded at Hawthorne Airport in LA.
- Q1 2026 operating expenses increased by $21.5 million from Q4 2025 due to continued investment in flight testing, certification efforts, production activities for Midnight aircraft, and design/development of its hybrid aircraft.
- Q1 2026 net loss increased by $28.8 million from Q4 2025, driven by a $21.5 million increase in operating expenses, a $7.3 million decrease in non-cash gain within other income, and a $1.2 million decrease in interest income, offset by a $1.3 million revenue increase.
- Q1 2026 Adjusted EBITDA was a loss of $172.5 million, within the guidance range of $160 million to $180 million, representing a planned increase of $34.6 million over Q4 2025.
- Archer’s financial estimates for Q2 2026 project an Adjusted EBITDA loss of $170 million to $200 million.
- Archer builds aircraft and core technologies that will define the next era of flight for aerospace and defense.
