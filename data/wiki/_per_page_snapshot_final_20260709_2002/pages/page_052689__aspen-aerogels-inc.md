# Aspen Aerogels, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2024-08-08

**Source:** [Aspen Aerogels, Inc. (ASPN) Q2 2024 Earnings Call Transcript - TradePoint.io](https://tradepoint.io/aspen-aerogels-inc-aspn-q2-2024-earnings-call-transcript/)  ·  `page_052689`

## Facts

- Aspen Aerogels is focused on its second Aerogel manufacturing facility in Georgia, which will add approximately $1.2 billion of revenue capacity by 2027.
- The U.S. Department of Energy Loan Programs Office invited Aspen into the formal due diligence and term sheet negotiation stage for a loan application that is one of the key drivers for restarting construction of Plant 2.
- Aspen has made steady progress with the DOE Loan Programs Office but does not have assurance that the DOE will issue a conditional commitment, though it remains deeply engaged and believes it is a strong candidate to partner with them.
- Aspen believes it is in the final stages of the due diligence process and expects to provide additional details prior to its next quarterly earnings call.
- In Q2 2024, Aspen spent $12.3 million towards slowly advancing progress to fully enclose the main structures at Plant 2 and temperature control in all areas.
- To date, Aspen has incurred $300.2 million in cumulative expenses through the end of the second quarter towards Plant 2 in Georgia to position the project for a potential restart of construction after obtaining conditional approval from the DOE Loan Programs Office.
- The loan application is pursuant to the DOE’s advanced technology vehicle manufacturing (ATVM) loan program to fund the remaining construction cost of Plant 2.
- Aspen only spent $20.5 million in the first half of 2024 towards advancing the construction of Plant 2 in Georgia versus its original expectation of $30 million.
- Aspen is not planning to spend more than $15 million advancing the construction of Plant 2 until it receives a potential conditional approval on the loan pursuant to the DOE’s ATVM program.
- This investment will ensure the site is advanced enough to preserve all investments made to date and enables the potential reacceleration of construction in the fourth quarter of this year.
- Aspen's EV commercial activity remains at peak levels, with expectations to deliver over 100,000 prototype or preproduction parts to over a dozen programs in its development pipeline during Q3 2024.
- Aspen announced its sixth design award from a large EU battery manufacturer to supply the next-generation battery platform for Porsche, with an expected start of production in 2025.
- The EU battery manufacturer has delivered over two million battery systems since 2019.
- Aspen is in final contract negotiations with a major European OEM which it expects to become a formal design award during the third quarter, marking its seventh.
- General Motors reiterated during its Q2 earnings call that it is targeting to produce between 200,000 and 250,000 EVs in 2024.
- IHS cited 108,000 Ultium-based EVs produced in the first half of 2024, with anticipated acceleration in the second half of the year.
- Aspen anticipates that GM will produce 180,000 LTM-based EVs for GM nameplates plus an additional 45,000 Ultium-based EVs for Honda and Acura in 2024.
- The potential upside to Aspen's revenue outlook is largely based on GM maintaining its current ramp-up and achieving its targeted production range.
- Aspen expects to supply over 225,000 vehicles and enable its EV thermal barrier business to deliver over $240 million of revenue in combination with Toyota, some initial Scania and Stellantis volumes, along with a high level of prototype sales.
- The Honda Prologue, produced by General Motors, launched earlier than expected, with almost 3,500 sold in July and an expected annual sales run rate of 42,000 units.
- GM revised its external 2024 Ultium production forecast from a range of 200,000 to 300,000 units down to a range of 200,000 to 250,000 units.
- Aspen revised its baseline production outlook of GM’s Ultium vehicles down by 10% from 200,000 vehicles to 180,000 to be safe.
- The U.S. EV market did not grow year-to-date through the end of July 2024 relative to last year, increasing only around 1%, comparable to overall new vehicle sales growth.
- EVs made up around 7% of the U.S. market, with over 1.3 million EVs expected to be sold in the U.S. this year.
