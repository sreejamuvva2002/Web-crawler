# City of Toccoa / Stephens County Development Authority

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2022-03-30

**Source:** [Toccoa | Stephens County: The Right Toolbox - Georgia Trend Magazine](https://www.georgiatrend.com/2022/03/30/toccoa-stephens-county-the-right-toolbox/)  ·  `page_058122`

## Facts

- Nifco KTW America Corp., which makes injection-molded parts for autos, has set up a hub in Stephens County.
- Copper wire manufacturer Sark Wire has established a manufacturing hub in the county.
- MagnaFlow operates a catalytic converter and exhaust technology parts distribution center in the area.
- ASI Southeast, a manufacturer of plastics and metals specializing in commercial bathroom partitions and fixtures, is the largest private employer in Stephens County with 500 workers across three plants.
- Patterson Pump, Sage Automotive Interiors, and American Woodwork are also major industrial employers in the county.
- Manufacturing remains the largest employment sector in Stephens County, employing approximately 1,800 workers, or 18% of the local labor pool.
- The Currahee Campus of North Georgia Technical College provides customized workforce training for local industries and has worked closely with companies like Sark Wire and Nifco KTW America Corp.
- Stephens County High School operates a work-based learning program where over 300 students completed 92,000 hours in retail and manufacturing roles, earning $800,000 during the 2020-2021 school year.
- Stephens County operates three industrial parks to attract businesses, including the newly developed Hayestone Brady Business Park.
- The county leverages its proximity to Interstate 85, an expanded airport, and both freight and Amtrak rail services to recruit industrial prospects.
- The expansion of State Route 17 into a four-lane highway connecting to Interstate 85 in 2016 improved logistics and accelerated industrial growth.
- Toccoa's location between Atlanta and Greenville, South Carolina, places it near the planned Northeast Georgia Inland Port in Gainesville and the SK Battery plant in Jackson County.
- A local façade grant program has funded the renovation of more than 80 storefronts to stimulate downtown business recruitment.
- Downtown Toccoa was designated under Georgia’s Rural Zone program from 2018 through 2022, allowing businesses to receive special tax credits for property acquisition, rehabilitation, and job creation.
- More than $38 million in private investment and $16 million in public investment have been directed into downtown development since 1990.
- The Toccoa-Stephens County Airport generates an economic impact of more than $3.7 million annually, featuring a 6,000-square-foot terminal and two runways, one exceeding 5,000 feet in length.
- Amtrak invested $2 million to upgrade the Toccoa train depot platform with safety features and decorative lighting.
- The Stephens County Development Authority launched a recruitment campaign targeting over 7,000 county residents who currently commute out of state to fill local industrial jobs.
- Brittany Ivey serves as the president and CEO of the Stephens County Development Authority (SCDA).
- Jeanette Jamieson serves as the mayor of Toccoa.
- Julie Paysen is the president of the Toccoa-Stephens County Chamber of Commerce.
