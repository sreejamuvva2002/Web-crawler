# Porsche Financial Services, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2025-10-27

**Source:** [Porsche Financial Services, Inc. Issues Prime Auto Transaction in the U.S. ABS Market](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/porsche-financial-services-inc-issues-150400054.html?fr=sycsrp_catchall)  ·  `page_048708`

## Facts

- Porsche Financial Services, Inc. (PFS) is headquartered in Atlanta, Georgia.
- PFS issued an auto Asset Backed Securities (ABS) offering in the USA with a principal amount of $870.4 million dollars.
- This transaction marked the second bond issuance for PFS in 2025.
- PFS is an indirect, wholly owned subsidiary of German luxury car maker Dr. Ing. h.c. F. Porsche AG ("Porsche AG").
- The securities issued in the Rule 144A transaction maintained a 'AAA' rating from the rating agencies and achieved competitive pricing.
- The transaction was divided into five tranches, including a floating rate tranche, and was backed by a pool of auto contracts financing Porsche vehicles.
- RBC Capital Markets led the transaction, with BofA Securities, Scotiabank, Société Générale, and Truist Securities serving as book runners.
- The deal achieved an upsize from a growing investor base comprised of investment funds, asset managers of financial institutions, trusts, banks, and corporates.
- Tobias Hausladen serves as Treasurer & Chief Financial Officer for Porsche Financial Services, Inc.
- PFS stated that despite launching during one of the busiest weeks in the 2025 ABS primary market, PFAST 2025-1 received solid investor support and reflected confidence in their credit platform and performance consistency.
- PFS is the dedicated provider of leasing and financing products for Porsche in the United States.
- PFS was founded in 1991 and provides custom financial solutions and products to Porsche customers and dealers in the United States.
- In 2012, PFS expanded its North America operations to become the captive finance provider for the exclusive brands of the Volkswagen Group which include Bentley, Lamborghini, and Bugatti.
