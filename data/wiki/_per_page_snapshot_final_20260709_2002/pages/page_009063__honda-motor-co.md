# Honda Motor Co.

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-04-25

**Source:** [Honda announces four Ontario EV factories in $15-billion deal](https://electricautonomy.ca/ev-supply-chain/2024-04-25/honda-ev-battery-ontario-factory/)  ·  `page_009063`

## Facts

- The investment represents Canada’s largest single EV supply chain announcement.
- EV production at the new Ontario plant is scheduled to start in 2028.
- Battery-making facilities will be developed in tandem with the EV factory.
- Honda is building a series of four factories in Ontario as part of a massive $15-billion investment in its North American EV manufacturing footprint.
- The announcement was held at the automaker’s existing auto assembly plant in Alliston, Ont.
- Honda is exploring setting up two factories with two joint venture partners: South Korea’s Posco Chemicals and Japanese chemicals company Asahi Kasei.
- Honda bills the investment as a plan “to establish [a] comprehensive electric vehicle value chain in Ontario.”
- The move complements ongoing development of Honda’s EV Hub project in Ohio and “will represent a significant aspect of the company’s overall value chain in North America.”
- Honda Canada president and CEO Jean Marc Leclerc described the investment as “historic” and noted it builds on Honda’s multi-decade track record of automotive manufacturing in Canada.
- Honda’s investment surpasses all previous competitor EV and battery supply chain commitments made in Canada since 2020 in both cost and scale.
- It is too early to specify what exact EVs will be made at the new plant, but they will include an “affordable SUV” that “Canadians want.”
- The new battery plant will begin by making lithium-ion batteries, with plans in place to transition later to solid-state technology.
- Honda will be leveraging the latest battery chemistry technology it is developing in Japan.
- The four-part investment carries a $15-billion price tag and will create at least 1,000 new jobs.
- Honda is eligible to receive up to $5 billion in incentives and subsidies, divided equally between the provincial and federal governments.
- The federal portion of the incentives will come chiefly through the new EV Supply Chain Investment Tax Credit introduced in this month’s budget as well as the proposed Clean Technology Manufacturing Investment Tax Credit.
- Ontario’s $2.5-billion contribution to the incentives will come from a mix of direct and indirect incentives.
- Dignitaries at the announcement included Honda Global CEO Toshihiro Mibe, Prime Minister Justin Trudeau, Finance Minister Chrystia Freeland, and Minister of Innovation, Science and Industry François-Philippe Champagne.
- Ontario Premier Doug Ford and Vic Fedeli, Minister of Economic Development, Job Creation and Trade, were also present at the announcement.
- Vic Fedeli confirmed that the deal was two years in the making and was finalized over a family-style meeting at Premier Doug Ford’s home in Etobicoke, Ont.
