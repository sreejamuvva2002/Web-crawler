## Facts

- The $7.6 billion Hyundai electric vehicle project in Georgia is nearing completion.
- A Korean-speaking technician was trapped in a conveyor belt at the construction site for 15 excruciating minutes, bleeding uncontrollably from his left thigh and having skin stripped from his hand.
- Emergency medical responders described the 40-year-old worker's injuries as a crushed chest, deformed hand, and mangled leg.
- The injured technician was airlifted to Savannah’s trauma hospital, located 30 miles away.
- On June 3, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) released a statement confirming a worker was treated for severe internal and external injuries and was in stable condition.
- On June 5, the federal organization that oversees workplace safety finished an onsite inspection of the construction site.
- More than two months after the June 5 inspection, the federal workplace safety organization had yet to assign any blame.
