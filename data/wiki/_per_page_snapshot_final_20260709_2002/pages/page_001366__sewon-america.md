# Sewon America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [GA: Sewon America's New Manufacturing Facility to Create 740 Jobs in Effingham County | Trade and Industry Development](https://www.tradeandindustrydev.com/region/georgia/news/ga-sewon-americas-new-manufacturing-facility-31448)  ·  `page_001366`

## Facts

- Georgia Gov. Brian P. Kemp announced that Sewon America will create 740 new jobs in Rincon.
- Sewon America will invest more than $300 million in a new Effingham County manufacturing facility.
- This project is the largest known private investment in the City of Rincon to date.
- The announcement fulfills a commitment to create jobs and investment in all four Savannah Harbor Interstate-16 Joint Development Authority (JDA) counties.
- This fulfillment results from Hyundai's Metaplant location in Bryan County, which is the largest economic development project in state history.
- Suppliers for the Metaplant have now committed over $1.8 billion in investment.
- Supplier investments surpass initial estimates by at least $800 million.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that the announcement celebrates delivering on the promise to create jobs in all four JDA counties and highlights the growth of a valued partner.
- On his first international trip as governor to Korea, Kemp congratulated Chairman Kim on the company’s then-recent expansion and discussed continuing collaboration.
- Those discussions led to this announcement as Sewon America continues to grow its Georgia operations.
- Speaker of the House Jon Burns, who represents part of Effingham County, played a role in securing the project and engaging Sewon America executives.
- Speaker Jon Burns celebrated the creation of 740 new jobs for Effingham County and coastal Georgia.
- Lt. Governor Burt Jones commended Governor Brian Kemp, Sewon America, and stakeholders on the historic economic development announcement for Rincon.
- Burt Jones noted that investments by companies like Sewon build on Georgia’s commitment to remain the No. 1 state in which to do business while attracting a robust workforce.
- The investment of more than $300 million in Effingham County will have a positive impact on the local community and state.
- Sewon America operates one of the largest automobile body parts manufacturing plants in North America.
- The company opened a manufacturing facility in LaGrange in 2008, which has since doubled in size.
- Following completion of the Rincon facility, Sewon is expected to support 1,600 jobs in Georgia.
- Vice Chairman Kim of Sewon America stated that Sewon America was founded 15 years ago in west Georgia and expressed excitement about building another facility in east Georgia as one family in two locations.
- Kim credited the growth to the Sewon team, Governor Kemp, the Georgia Department of Economic Development, and Effingham County.
- Sewon’s new facility will be located at the Grande View industrial park in Rincon.
- The new manufacturing location will produce electric vehicle (EV) body parts for original equipment manufacturers (OEMs).
- OEM customers include Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- Operations are expected to begin in 2025.
- Rincon Mayor Ken Lee welcomed Sewon America and commended the Effingham County Industrial Development Authority for bringing the largest employment and private investment project in the city’s history.
- Chairman of the Effingham County Industrial Development Authority (IDA) Troy Smith stated that selecting Grande View industrial park allows current and future generations to have fulfilling, well-paying careers without leaving Effingham County.
- Smith thanked partners at the City of Rincon and the Effingham County Board of Commissioners for their help.
- Executive Director of the Georgia Ports Authority Griff Lynch called the announcement terrific news for Effingham County and the State of Georgia.
- Lynch noted that the Hyundai announcement continues to bring new economic opportunities to the Peach State.
- Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy Alyce Thornhill represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- GDEcD partnered with the Effingham County Industrial Development Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia EMC.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Sewon has integrated into and lent its own industry experience to Georgia’s automotive community through local colleges and Georgia Quick Start.
- Wilson mentioned Sewon's participation in statewide initiatives like Georgia Made.
- Wilson noted that Sewon is reinvesting in Georgia as they support Hyundai Motor Group’s operations from Effingham County, which has the skilled workforce, infrastructure, and collaborative spirit to support growth.
- On October 25, 2022, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America officially broke ground in neighboring Bryan County on its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility.
- Georgia is positioned as a hub for the electric mobility industry.
- Since 2020, more than 35 EV-related projects have announced more than $21 billion in investment and 26,700 jobs in Georgia.
