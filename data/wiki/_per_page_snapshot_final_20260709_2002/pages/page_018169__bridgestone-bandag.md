# Bridgestone Bandag

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-05-16

**Source:** [The Development Corporation of Abilene Announces Bridgestone Bandag Groundbreaking on $60 Million Expansion in Abilene, Texas | Development Corporation of Abilene](https://developabilene.com/2023/05/16/the-development-corporation-of-abilene-announces-bridgestone-bandag-groundbreaking-on-60-million-expansion-in-abilene-texas/)  ·  `page_018169`

## Facts

- Bridgestone Bandag broke ground on the expansion of its retread tire plant in Abilene, Texas.
- The project represents a $60 million capital investment into Abilene.
- The expansion will add 50,000 square feet to their existing 200,000 square-foot facility in Abilene, Texas.
- Misty Mayo, President and Chief Executive Officer of the DCOA, stated that Bridgestone Bandag has continued to invest in Abilene for over 50 years.
- The expansion project is expected to retain 179 full-time employees.
- The project will create an additional 25 full-time positions for Abilene.
- Over the next 10 years, the $60 million project is estimated to add over $21 million in taxes for the City of Abilene, Taylor County, and Abilene Independent School District.
- Abilene Mayor Weldon Hurt stated that Bridgestone Bandag continues to grow and invest in the City of Abilene.
- Over the next 10 years, the total estimated indirect and direct impact of the retention and expansion is $1.81 billion.
- Sam Vinson, DCOA Board Chair, noted that Bridgestone Bandag has chosen to stay, grow, and prosper in Abilene, Texas.
- The expansion project will focus on the development of an automated operation for rubber mixing and production.
- Billy Fralick, Plant Manager of the Abilene Plant, stated the expansion will allow them to grow production capabilities to meet customer needs.
- The Abilene plant expansion supports Bridgestone’s E8 Commitment, which consists of 8 values starting with “E” (Energy, Ecology, Efficiency, Extension, Economy, Emotion, Ease, and Empowerment) to realize a sustainable society.
- Chris Daniel, Executive Director of Bandag Manufacturing at Bridgestone Americas, stated the expansion is driven by growing demand for products and the team’s commitment to delivering innovation and superior quality.
- The company aims to build greener retreads while helping maximize fuel efficiency for fleets.
- The project taps into Abilene’s skilled labor force in the manufacturing industry.
- Abilene has four universities, a technical college, a community college, and state-of-the-art training facilities for high school and college students.
- Abilene is located directly on I-20, just two and a half hours from the Dallas/Fort Worth metro.
- The city of Abilene has a population of 126,518 people and a labor shed of over 140,000.
- Bridgestone Americas, Inc. is the U.S.-based subsidiary of Bridgestone Corporation, headquartered in Nashville, Tenn., and employs more than 50,000 people across its worldwide operations.
- Bandag has been responsible for keeping approximately 300 million tires out of the waste system since 1957.
- The Bandag retreading process has saved four billion gallons of oil since it began 50 years ago.
- The Development Corporation of Abilene (DCOA) is the economic development driver of Abilene, leveraging the DevelopAbilene partnership to market the city for business expansion.
