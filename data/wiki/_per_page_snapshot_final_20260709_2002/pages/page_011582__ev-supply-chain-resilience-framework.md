# EV Supply Chain Resilience Framework

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Five decisions that can create resilient EV supply chains](https://www.automotivelogistics.media/ev-and-battery/five-decisions-that-separate-resilient-ev-supply-chains-from-vulnerable-ones/2692203)  ·  `page_011582`

## Facts

- Ganpati Goel is the senior global supply chain manager, power electronics, at Lucid Motors, and the author of peer-reviewed research on AI-driven EV procurement and dual-sourcing risk.
- The Liberation Day tariff announcement of April 2025 added an estimated $3,600 per vehicle in import duty costs for the average US assembled vehicle manufacturer and roughly $6,000 to an imported vehicle, according to Cox Automotive.
- By early 2026, US EV sales had fallen 22.6% year-to-date while total vehicle sales contracted 5.3%, according to Omdia data presented at Automotive Logistics' Finished Vehicle Logistics North America 2026 conference.
- Iranian missile strikes on Qatar's Ras Laffan LNG hub drove helium spot prices above $450 per thousand cubic feet, causing a 40% spike that is now rationing semiconductor production at TSMC and Samsung.
- The author has managed cross-border EV component procurement across eight countries: the US, Mexico, the Philippines, Canada, India, Germany, Japan, and South Korea.
- The 2020–22 semiconductor crisis, the 2024 tariff escalations on Chinese-origin printed circuit board assemblies (PCBAs), and the Iran-Hormuz semiconductor shock have each forced real-time decisions with documented financial consequences.
- The AMS/ABB Automotive Manufacturing Outlook Survey 2025, drawing on 473 industry respondents, found that supply chain disruption has ranked among the top two challenges every year since 2022 and now tops cost concerns at 45%.
- Supply chain disruption ranks ahead of raw materials at 42% and labour at 39% in the AMS/ABB survey.
- Full domestic sourcing of EV components is a 2035 aspiration.
- Rare earth processing, advanced printed circuit board assembly, and precision powertrain machining are geographically concentrated due to decades of capital investment and process know-how that cannot be replicated overnight by executive order.
- Most supplier contracts default to the seller's preferred Incoterm, typically EXW or FOB, because it is the path of least resistance at negotiation.
- When duty liability was reclassified on Chinese-origin components in 2024 and 2025, procurement teams operating under DDP terms were insulated while those under FOB or CFR absorbed retroactive duty exposure that had never been priced into their cost models.
- Free Carrier (FCA) has emerged as a more defensible structure for cross-border EV transactions because it transfers risk at the named point of origin, is compatible with letter-of-credit financing, and allocates export clearance to the supplier.
- Teams should audit their Incoterm mix annually against the current tariff environment rather than only at contract inception.
- In live procurement operations across an eight-country network, FCA renegotiation following the February 2026 Supreme Court tariff rulings delivered immediate cost savings on impacted purchase orders and removed recurring refund-recovery overhead.
- Qatar supplies approximately one-third of global helium, which is used in semiconductor wafer fabrication at 3nm and 2nm nodes.
- South Korea sourced 64.7% of its helium imports from Qatar in 2025 and was operating on chipmaker stockpiles estimated at around six months as of early 2026.
- Taiwan faces comparable exposure to the helium supply loss.
- EV power electronics rely on advanced integrated circuits and semiconductors produced at fabs now under supply pressure.
- Procurement teams buying battery management unit PCBAs, variable frequency drive components, and onboard charger assemblies face extended lead time notifications from tier one suppliers due to the helium crisis.
- The AMS/ABB survey confirms that 28% of respondents identify supply chain visibility as a critical gap, and 64% expect increased adoption of supply chain management software over the next year.
- The traditional tariff management approach became obsolete during 2024 due to material HTSUS classification changes, Section 301 rate increases doubling to 50% on Chinese semiconductors effective January 2025, and IEEPA tariffs layered on top.
- High-performing procurement teams have moved to a continuous tariff signal process involving a designated commodity owner monitoring HTSUS updates and Federal Register notices weekly.
- Tariffs now represent the single largest cost pressure, cited by 45% of AMS/ABB survey respondents ahead of raw materials and labour.
- The Red Sea diversion of 2024, the Strait of Hormuz disruption of 2026, and port congestion events across Los Angeles-Long Beach exposed structural vulnerabilities in single-lane logistics strategies.
- The correct architecture for critical EV components like BMU PCBAs, power conversion modules, and contactor assemblies is a standing secondary routing with a named alternate forwarder, alternate port pair, and trigger protocol typically activated at a 72-hour delay notification.
- Research supports a transit buffer inventory calibration of three to six weeks of consumption for single-source critical components, with an estimated 50–60% reduction in supply disruption risk under adverse routing conditions.
- The AMS/ABB survey found that 27% of respondents are actively shifting from single to dual sourcing.
- Production downtime at a luxury EV OEM can exceed $500,000 a day.
- Standard contract templates create enforcement gaps across civil and common law systems in Asia, North America, and Europe, particularly regarding force majeure definitions, IP ownership for custom tooling/dies, and termination-for-convenience notice periods.
- Jurisdiction-specific review is particularly consequential in South Korea, India, and China where local statute may conflict with boilerplate buyer-owns-tooling language.
- The AMS/ABB survey's medium-term optimism shows 60% of respondents expect production growth in the three-year outlook.
