# Seojin Mobility

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2024-04-03

**Source:** [Korean EV supplier Seojin Mobility starts building plant in Mexico](https://mexiconewsdaily.com/business/korean-ev-supplier-breaks-ground-on-new-plant-in-northern-mexico/)  ·  `page_000449`

## Facts

- Korean electric motor manufacturer Seojin Mobility will invest US $300 million in its first Mexico plant.
- The Mexico plant is now under construction in the northern state of Nuevo León.
- The first stage of construction is expected to be completed by February 2025 at a cost of $200 million.
- The second $100 million phase will involve the construction of hybrid motors.
- Seojin Mobility anticipates the plant, once operational, will create 300 jobs.
- A groundbreaking ceremony was held on March 28 in the city of Escobedo, just north of Monterrey.
- Byung Hyuk Kang, general director of Seojin Mobility México, and José Antonio Quiroga, the local municipal president, presided over the groundbreaking ceremony.
- Seojin Mobility will build motors for electric vehicles for the Hyundai plant in Savannah, Georgia.
- Seojin Mobility has plans to collaborate with Kia Motors’ operations in the city of Pesquería, 36 kilometers west of Escobedo.
- When asked about building motors for the Tesla plant in Santa Catarina (27 kilometers southwest of Escobedo), Byung Hyuk said that is unlikely, though not impossible.
- Seojin Mobility will focus on four Hyundai models in the United States.
- Kia Motors has a plan for a small model electric vehicle in 2025.
- Seojin Mobility is headquartered in Siheung, South Korea.
- Seojin Mobility is the primary supplier for Hyundai and Kia and also has contracts with GM, Renault, Ferrari and Volvo.
- The proximity of the Tesla plant presents several advantages for Seojin Mobility.
- Byung Hyuk stated that with Tesla being nearby, they might find an opportunity to develop new products.
- The new plant will serve to strengthen Seojin’s association with Hyundai and Kia México and develop the local electric vehicle market.
- Seojin Mobility chose Escobedo for its location near the industrial center of Monterrey and just 150 miles from Laredo, Texas.
- Seojin Mobility chose Escobedo due to the presence of a qualified workforce.
- Escobedo has been preparing to take advantage of nearshoring by creating an industrial district.
- The municipality’s development plan includes the construction of two hotels at a cost of $1 billion.
