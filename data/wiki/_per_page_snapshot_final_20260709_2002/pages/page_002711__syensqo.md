# Syensqo

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2024-04-25

**Source:** [Syensqo Breaks Ground on New EV Material Facility in Augusta | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-release/syensqo-breaks-ground-new-ev-material-facility-augusta)  ·  `page_002711`

## Facts

- Groundbreaking and start of construction on Syensqo’s new battery-grade PVDF manufacturing facility in Augusta occurred on April 25, 2024.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD), executive leadership from Syensqo, and local/state representatives attended the groundbreaking ceremony.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Georgia is developing an e-mobility supply chain to position the state as a leader in this industry for years to come.
- The new facility will create approximately 100 new full-time, highly skilled, local manufacturing jobs in Richmond County.
- At full capacity, the facility’s products will provide material for more than 5 million EV batteries per year.
- The project is expected to create hundreds of jobs throughout the value chain.
- Syensqo was formed from the demerger of Solvay in December 2023 and operates as a standalone listed company.
- Syensqo’s portfolio addresses electrification, lightweighting, advanced connectivity, resource efficiency, improving quality of life, and sustainable sourcing.
- The company’s U.S. headquarters in Alpharetta and existing Augusta manufacturing facility currently support more than 800 jobs in Georgia.
- Mike Finelli, Chief Technology & Innovation Officer and Chief North America Officer at Syensqo, thanked GDEcD for supporting the expansion and noted it underscores Augusta’s strategic importance as a key player in the EV supply chain along the Battery Belt.
- The new facility is located at 3702 Clanton Road in Augusta.
- The plant will supply polyvinylidene fluoride (PVDF), which is used as a lithium-ion binder and separator coating in electric vehicle batteries.
- The Augusta plant will be the largest PVDF production facility in North America.
- The facility will hire mechanics, technicians, engineers, and other roles.
- Augusta Mayor Garnett Johnson noted Syensqo has been part of the Augusta community for more than two decades as a premier employer and expressed excitement over the expansion adding quality jobs to serve the community for years to come.
- Augusta Economic Development Authority Chairman Wayne A. Gossage, Jr. stated the project fits the community’s goal of creating well-paying, skilled manufacturing jobs and confirms Augusta-Richmond County as an ideal location for manufacturing growth.
- Senior Project Manager Adela Kelley represented the GDEcD Global Commerce team on the competitive project in partnership with the Augusta Economic Development Authority, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia EMC.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson highlighted that companies are making once-in-a-generation investments to keep up with the electrification of the mobility industry and praised state leadership under Governor Brian Kemp for attracting e-mobility and battery investments.
- The Syensqo Augusta site partners with WJBF, Piedmont Hospital, and Security Federal Bank on the Giving Your Best Award to recognize community volunteerism.
- Syensqo is supporting Georgia higher education institutions, including Augusta Tech, with workforce development projects.
- In 2022, Syensqo opened a New Applications Development Lab at its R&I Center in Alpharetta to support customers in the EV, renewable energy, and aerospace industries.
- The Alpharetta lab inauguration was attended by HRH Princess Astrid of Belgium, high-ranking Belgian officials, and State of Georgia leadership.
- Over the last four years leading up to 2024, Syensqo has invested more than $30 million in its Alpharetta site.
