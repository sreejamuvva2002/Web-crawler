# DOE Funding Opportunity Announcement DE-FOA-0003371 FY24 Scale-up of Integrated Biorefineries

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [DOE: CMEI eXCHANGE: Funding Opportunities](https://eere-exchange.energy.gov/Default.aspx?Search=3371)  ·  `page_003088`

## Facts

- Supports higher risk bioenergy technologies that are ready to scale, ultimately demonstrating feedstock-conversion variations or production pathways to support commercialization.
- Aims to meet the Sustainable Aviation Fuel (SAF) Grand Challenge goal of 35 billion gallons per year of SAF production by 2050.
- Aims to meet the Clean Fuels and Products Shot goal of 50% projected demand for maritime, rail, off-road, and renewable carbon-based chemicals by 2050.
- Funds cost-shared research, development, and demonstration projects with partners in industry, academia, and National Laboratories focused on design, construction, operation, and validation of new technology and feedstock pathways for engineering scale integrated biorefineries.
- Intent is to reduce technology uncertainty and risk through cost-shared pilot- and demonstration-scale biorefinery projects.
- Supports BETO’s multi-year strategic goals to decarbonize the transportation sector through production of cost-effective SAF and other strategic fuels.
- Supports BETO’s multi-year strategic goals to decarbonize the industrial sector through cost-effective and sustainable chemicals, materials, and processes utilizing biomass and waste resources.
- Topic Area 1 identifies, evaluates, and selects applications proposing project definition, development, and execution plans for scaling pre-pilot (TRL 5) biofuel technologies to pilot scale (TRL 6).
- Topic Area 2 identifies, evaluates, and selects applications proposing project definition, development, and execution plans for scaling pilot (TRL 6) biofuel technologies to demonstration scale (TRL 7-8).
- Topic Area 3 supports scale-up activities to produce organic chemicals from renewable biomass and waste feedstocks that can replace those currently derived from petroleum.
- Topic Area 3 identifies, evaluates, and selects applications proposing project definition, development, and execution plans for scaling pre-pilot and pilot (TRL 5-6) biochemical technologies to pilot and demonstration scale (TRL 6-8).
- Original Full Application submission deadline was January 16th, 2025 at 5:00PM ET.
- Amendment 000001 extends the Full Application submission deadline to January 31st, 2025 at 5:00PM ET to allow sufficient time for development during end of calendar year activities.
- All proceeding dates, such as Reply to Reviewer Comments and award notification dates for Research, Technology, and Economic Security review, were adjusted by an equal amount of time.
- Table 3 was updated to align the Allowable Fuel Types with Table 1.
- Required Concept Paper due date is November 7, 2024 at 5PM ET.
- Full Application due date is January 31st, 2025 at 5PM ET.
- The eXCHANGE system enforces hard deadlines for Concept Paper and Full Application submissions.
- APPLY and SUBMIT buttons automatically disable at the defined submission deadlines to consistently enforce a standard deadline for all applicants.
- Late submissions due to technical difficulties are accepted only if the applicant encountered difficulties beyond their control, contacted the helpdesk for assistance, and submitted the application through eXCHANGE within 24 hours of the FOA's posted deadline.
