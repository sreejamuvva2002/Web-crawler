# Georgia Right to Work Economic Development Initiative

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Thousands of Jobs for Right to Work Georgia - NRTWC](https://nrtwc.org/thousands-of-jobs-for-right-to-work-georgia/)  ·  `page_059356`

## Facts

- Meissner Corporation is investing $250 million in order to expand and create 1,700 new jobs in Athens-Clarke County, Georgia.
- According to Area Development Magazine, Meissner provides critical filtration equipment that benefits other key industries in Georgia, including life sciences and food processing.
- The addition of Meissner is expected to complement the state's industry mix while diversifying the local economy in Athens-Clarke County.
- Seohan Auto Georgia is investing $72 million and creating 180 new jobs in order to add a new facility in Liberty County, Georgia.
- According to Area Development Magazine, Seohan Auto Georgia believes Georgia will be the center of the EV industry and a new frontier for its future with limitless opportunities.
- Officials note that the ongoing transition to e-mobility and sustainable technology creates a window of opportunity for companies and states that remain on the forefront of this developing industry.
- State officials stated they are pleased that Seohan Auto Georgia selected Liberty County as their location, describing the company and its affiliated enterprises as responsible, thorough, and progressive organizations striving to advance innovative technology.
- Zoetis is investing $100 million in order to add a new location and create 150 new jobs in Douglasville, Georgia.
- According to Area Development Magazine, the Douglasville facility provides an opportunity to expand production and supply capacity while benefiting the local economy by adding new jobs to serve veterinarians, livestock producers, pet owners, and their animals.
- Local economic development leaders state that Zoetis choosing Douglasville for its new southeast facility is a testament to their commitment to attracting new investment and quality jobs, opening new avenues for recruiting additional life science and health technology companies.
- Altogether, the three announced investments will create 2,030 new jobs in Georgia.
- The page attributes the creation of these jobs to incentives created by Georgia's Right to Work laws.
