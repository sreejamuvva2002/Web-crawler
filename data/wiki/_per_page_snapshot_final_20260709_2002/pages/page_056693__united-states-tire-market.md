# United States Tire Market

**Type:** unknown  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- The United States tire market size reached 363.1 Million Units in 2025.
- IMARC Group expects the US tire market to reach 408.8 Million Units by 2034, exhibiting a growth rate (CAGR) of 1.32% during 2026-2034.
- An expansion in the automotive industry and the rising demand for electric vehicles (EVs) are primarily driving tire market growth in the United States.
- Rising environmental consciousness coupled with extended support by the U.S. Federal Government to promote eco-friendly and sustainable manufacturing techniques in tire production processes is contributing to market growth.
- Replacement tire demand is experiencing tremendous growth, especially in the passenger vehicle segment, due to high car ownership levels and cars being held for longer periods of time.
- In March 2024, Bridgestone created a high-performance lunar rover tire through the use of Air Free technology, with applications for greater durability and sustainability in global and U.S. tire marketplaces.
- In January 2025, Goodyear revealed its SightLine smart tire with integrated sensors to sense rain and ice, allowing for earlier automatic emergency braking to enhance vehicle safety on low-friction roads.
- Smart tires improve vehicle performance, safety, and fuel efficiency, particularly in commercial fleets and electric vehicles.
- Manufacturers are creating tires with reduced rolling resistance, recycled content, and longer life due to increasing pressure towards environmental sustainability and federal/state regulatory objectives for energy efficiency and emissions reduction.
- E-commerce and online retail platforms are revolutionizing tire purchasing through convenience, extensive choice, competitive prices, and access to customer feedback.
- Radial tires are the market leaders in the United States due to their better structure design, performance, longevity, traction, fuel economy, and heat dissipation.
- Original Equipment Manufacturers (OEMs) are a dominant segment of the tire industry, with automakers seeking customized tire solutions compatible with particular model specifications to enhance vehicle performance and fuel efficiency.
- The growth in electric vehicles (EVs) is impacting OEM demand, with manufacturers specifically seeking tires that feature low rolling resistance and quiet running.
- Passenger cars hold the largest proportion in the United States tire market due to high vehicle ownership ratios and continuous urbanization.
- Growing interest in electric and hybrid cars is driving the creation of dedicated passenger car tires designed to ensure quiet running and efficiency.
- Offline distribution maintains dominance through physical dealerships, auto service workshops, and specialty shops that provide expert installation, alignment, and servicing.
- All-season tires are the most popular seasonal option in America due to their versatility, effective performance in summer and winter, and low maintenance requirements.
- The South region has the highest regional percentage of the United States tire market, fueled by high vehicle density, large road networks, and a favorable climate that lessens the need for seasonal tire changes.
- Texas, Florida, and Georgia are among the contributing states to overall volume due to their size, extensive metropolitan areas, and prevalent culture of vehicle ownership.
- Due to relatively stable weather conditions, South customers value durability, heat tolerance, and fuel efficiency more than winter features.
- The expanding logistics and transportation industry in the South underpins demand for commercial and fleet tire usage.
- Key players in the competitive landscape include Michelin North America, Inc., Bridgestone Americas, Inc., The Goodyear Tire & Rubber Company, Continental AG, Sumitomo Rubber Industries, Ltd., Kumho Tire USA, Inc., Hankook Tire Co., Ltd., Toyo Tire U.S.A. Corp., Pirelli Tire S.p.A., and Yokohama Tire Corporation.
- The sudden outbreak of the COVID-19 pandemic led to stringent lockdown regulations across the nation, resulting in the temporary closure of numerous tire manufacturing units.
