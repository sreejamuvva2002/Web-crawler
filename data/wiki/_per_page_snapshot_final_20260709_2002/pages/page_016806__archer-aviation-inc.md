# Archer Aviation Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Archer Completes Aircraft Manufacturing Facility In Georgia](https://businessfacilities.com/archer-completes-aircraft-manufacturing-facility-in-georgia/)  ·  `page_016806`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation Inc. received the certificate of occupancy for its high-volume manufacturing facility, which is connected to the Covington Municipal Airport.
- Archer is now working to complete the tooling load-in for the initial manufacturing line at the facility, referred to as ARC.
- Production is planned to begin early this year, with the goal of ramping to a rate of two aircraft per month by the end of 2025.
- Archer built ARC in close partnership with Stellantis, and the two companies are continuing to progress their plans to mass-produce Archer’s Midnight aircraft.
- Stellantis is contributing capital, advanced manufacturing technology and expertise and experienced personnel with the goal of scaling this facility to 650 aircraft annually by 2030.
- Archer founder and CEO Adam Goldstein stated that construction on ARC was completed fast and on budget, shifting the industry from R&D into commercialization.
- Stellantis Chief Manufacturing and Supply Chain Officer Arnaud Deboeuf highlighted supporting Archer’s electric aircraft growth with deep manufacturing expertise as a great opportunity for both companies.
- ARC is where Archer plans to produce its all-electric vertical take-off and landing aircraft, Midnight, designed to offer passengers a sustainable, low-noise and safe alternative to ground transportation in congested cities around the world.
- Aerospace and defense products are Georgia’s No. 1 export ($11.1 billion in 2023) and the state’s second largest manufacturing industry, generating a $57.5 billion economic impact.
- More than 800 aerospace companies call Georgia home, including Pratt & Whitney, Delta, and Gulfstream Aerospace.
- Other recent investments in Georgia include StandardAero’s $33 million expansion, and Gulfstream Aerospace’s $150 million expansion.
- Governor Brian Kemp commented that Archer’s choice to build the new manufacturing facility in Newton County marks a next step in an incredible relationship to create quality jobs and opportunities for years to come.
- Congressman Mike Collins, who sits on the House Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure and Committee on Science, Space, and Technology, stated the facility completion highlights Georgia’s leadership in innovation, advanced manufacturing and job creation.
