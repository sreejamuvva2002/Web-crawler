# LG Energy Solution

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- LGES is a leader in battery technology.
- LGES secured a $4.3 billion LFP battery contract with European automaker Ampere.
- LGES formed a $4.3 billion joint venture with Hyundai in Georgia.
- The company's strategy leverages low-cost LFP chemistry and IRA-driven North American manufacturing.
- Lithium iron phosphate (LFP) batteries offer lower costs, longer lifespans, and reduced reliance on geopolitically sensitive materials like cobalt and nickel.
- LGES deploys Cell to Pack (CTP) technology, which eliminates intermediate modules to optimize space and energy density.
- The 39 GWh contract with Ampere is set to power 590,000 EVs over five years.
- Manufacturing these batteries in Poland reduces exposure to China's dominance in battery materials.
- The move aligns with EU battery regulations that mandate sustainability and traceability standards.
- The Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) offers a $7,500 tax credit for consumers who purchase eligible vehicles.
- IRA eligibility requires that 60% of a battery's components be manufactured in North America, a threshold rising to 80% by 2026.
- LGES's joint venture with Hyundai is located in Bryan County, Georgia.
- The $4.3 billion Georgia plant has a 30 GWh capacity.
- The plant will supply Hyundai's U.S. assembly lines.
- The plant is designed to source 100% of battery components domestically.
- Vertical integration is supported by on-site battery pack assembly by Hyundai Mobis, which slashes lead times and costs.
- Plant alignment with IRA requirements ensures Hyundai's EVs qualify for tax credits.
- The partnership targets a market projected to see 60% of EV sales driven by IRA-eligible vehicles by 2030.
- The U.S. and EU seek to reduce dependence on Chinese battery supply chains.
- LFP's lower material costs reduce price volatility.
- LGES has an existing partnership involving a $3 billion investment in U.S. production lines for Toyota.
- By 2030, the Georgia plant alone could contribute $1.2 billion in annual revenue, assuming a 30% margin.
- LGES stock is currently trading at a forward P/E of 12.4.
- Potential risks include tightening IRA thresholds and supply chain bottlenecks delaying production.
