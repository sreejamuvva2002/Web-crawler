# Volvo Cars

**Source:** [Celebrating Volvo in the United States](https://history.volvocars.us/)  ·  `page_013941`

## Facts

- Volvo Cars broke ground on its first US production plant in 2015.
- The S60 began rolling off the line in Ridgeville, South Carolina, in 2018.
- The flagship fully electric SUV, the EX90, is assembled in the US by over 2,000 local employees for export around the world.
- Two additional models will be added to production at the Ridgeville plant soon.
- Quinnshona ‘Meka’ Sumpter was one of the first 100 hires when Volvo Cars began recruiting for its first US manufacturing plant.
- Three of Meka Sumpter's children work alongside her across different areas of the business at the plant.
- Volvo retailers employ over 10,000 people in their local communities across the United States, in addition to the over 2,500 employed by Volvo Cars’ corporate and manufacturing presence.
- $1,300,000,000 has been invested in US manufacturing.
- The state-of-the-art plant in Ridgeville, South Carolina, is designed to produce up to 150,000 cars per year for electric, hybrid, or gas vehicles.
- In September 2025, Volvo announced it will build two new models in the US: a next-generation hybrid and the XC60.
- The XC60 is now Volvo Cars’ best-selling model of all time.
- The first electric Volvo came to the US in 2020 as the XC40 Recharge.
- In March 2025, Volvo Cars sold its 5 millionth car in the US, an XC60 delivered in Illinois.
- There are 281 Volvo retailers in 48 states.
- City Safety autonomous emergency braking (AEB) technology became standard in all new Volvo models by 2016.
- From 2029, the NHTSA will require AEB in all new cars sold in the US.
