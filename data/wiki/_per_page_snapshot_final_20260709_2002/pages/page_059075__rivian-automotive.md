# Rivian Automotive

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-11-26

**Source:** [Biden Throws Struggling Rivian $6 Billion Lifeline For EV Factory  – iftttwall](https://ifttt.itbehere.com/2024/11/26/biden-throws-struggling-rivian-6-billion-lifeline-for-ev-factory/)  ·  `page_059075`

## Facts

- The US Department of Energy announced a direct loan of up to $6.57 billion ($5.975 billion principal and $592 million capitalized interest) to finance Rivian’s EV factory in Stanton Springs North, near Social Circle, Georgia.
- The Georgia plant project was shelved in early March due to an urgent need to reduce costs.
- A cash crunch forced Rivian to pause construction of the Georgia plant in March.
- Rivian has burned through $19 billion since going public in 2021 and has been unable to meet production and sales targets.
- The new Georgia facility is intended to help Rivian boost production capacity for more affordable EV models, as its current R1 vehicle costs $70,000 or more.
- Rivian plans to construct the Georgia facility in two phases, each yielding 200,000 units of annual production capacity for a total of 400,000 units annually.
- Phase 1 of the Georgia plant is expected to begin production in 2028.
- Rivian expects to create approximately 7,500 operations jobs at the Georgia facility through 2030.
- The project will also include approximately 2,000 full-time construction jobs utilizing regional talent.
- These Georgia positions complement thousands of existing and planned jobs at Rivian's current manufacturing plant in Normal, Illinois.
- In June, German automaker Volkswagen provided Rivian with a $5 billion investment via a joint venture to help stem cash losses.
- The DOE loan announcement comes ahead of President-elect Trump's expected elimination of the EV tax credit, currently valued at up to $7,500 for new EVs and $4,000 for used ones.
- Tesla CEO Elon Musk has publicly supported rolling back EV tax credits, claiming it would bankrupt Rivian's competitors.
- Following the announcement, Rivian shares rose 8% in premarket trading to approximately $12.56.
- As of the previous Monday's close, Rivian stock was down 50% year-to-date, with roughly 18% of its float shorted (about 135 million shares).
- The incoming Trump administration may claw back the DOE loan funds intended for struggling companies like Rivian.
