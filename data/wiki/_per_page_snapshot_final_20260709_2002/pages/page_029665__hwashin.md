# Hwashin

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-10-31

**Source:** [How Laying a Strong Foundation Helped Dublin Land a Large Project | Georgia](https://georgia.org/blogs/how-laying-strong-foundation-helped-dublin-georgia-land-multimillion-dollar-project)  ·  `page_029665`

## Facts

- Automotive parts company Hwashin announced it will build a new manufacturing facility in Dublin, Georgia.
- The project will create more than 460 new jobs.
- The project involves an investment of more than $176 million.
- The initiative resulted from three years of planning and 15 months of ongoing site preparation by the local community.
- Dr. Fred Williams serves as Chair of the Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority (DLCDA).
- Hwashin will be the seventh international company to call Dublin and Laurens County home.
- The facility is situated on a 331-acre site at the intersection of U.S. Highway 441 and Georgia State Route 257.
- Sixty acres of the parcel have been secured for Hwashin's use.
- The DLCDA prepared the property to undergo development reviews required for state-approved certification through the “Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development” (GRAD) Program.
- The GRAD program markets pre-qualified, “ready for development” industrial sites to business and site selectors as a one-stop shop.
- The Dublin site was the first GRAD-certified site to earn the "Select" status.
- The Authority hired an engineering firm to continue site improvements after receiving detailed engineering questions from prospective companies.
- Ryan Waldrep serves as President of the DLCDA.
- The Authority obtained permits for water access and collaborated with the Georgia Department of Transportation to conduct traffic studies.
- A mass grading project moved enough cubic yards of material to fill a line of dump trucks stretching over 130 miles from the Dublin site to the 17th Street Bridge in Atlanta.
- During a site visit, a company consultant noted that the ongoing clearing and grading made it easy to envision the facility on the property.
- The DLCDA will install water, sewer, a road, and a retention pond on the site to service future developments.
- GRAD Certified sites have been critical in securing key projects, including about half of Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America’s suppliers and numerous battery supply chain projects.
