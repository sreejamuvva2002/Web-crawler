# Qcells

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-09

**Source:** [Largest Solar Cell Factory In USA Starts Production in Georgia - CleanTechnica](https://cleantechnica.com/2026/06/09/largest-solar-cell-factory-in-usa-starts-production-in-georgia/)  ·  `page_001554`

## Facts

- Qcells has just begun manufacturing solar cells at its new factory in Cartersville, Georgia.
- The company already produces solar modules at the Cartersville facility.
- The Cartersville facility will operate as a vertically integrated production facility for solar modules.
- When fully operational, the facility will add 3.3 GW of vertically integrated ingot capacity.
- When fully operational, the facility will add 3.3 GW of wafer capacity.
- When fully operational, the facility will add 3.3 GW of cell capacity.
- When fully operational, the facility will add 3.5 GW of module capacity.
- Qcells' total U.S. output will hit 8.6 GW by the end of Q3 2026.
- The total U.S. output is approximately the energy needed to power roughly 1.3 million American homes for a year.
- This will be the first and only manufacturing facility in the United States to produce ingot, wafer, cell, and module components under one roof.
- Qcells can claim tax credits for each of these parts and the panel as a whole.
- The Biden administration established large subsidies for producing solar panels and solar PV components in the U.S.
- These subsidies have survived various Trump administration attacks on pro-cleantech policies.
- Having all production in one spot makes for very competitive products from Qcells.
- Customers will benefit from an easier path to qualifying for the domestic content tax credit.
- Customers will benefit from greater supply certainty for their projects.
- The domestic content bonus is 10% under the Investment Tax Credit.
- Because the major components of each module are made domestically, customers can pursue qualifying projects with greater confidence on pricing, supply and sourcing.
- Qcells expects strong interest in Cartersville-produced modules due to growing demand for fully domestic solar equipment.
- The company is already at full module production at the factory.
- The factory is able to produce 16,700 solar PV modules a day.
- Full solar cell production will be achieved in the fall of this year.
- Once fully operational, it will be the largest solar cell production facility in the history of the United States.
- It will also become the biggest ingot and wafer production facility in the country.
- By Q3 2026, Cartersville will make 3.3 GW each of ingots, wafers, cells, and 3.5 GW of modules a year.
- The Dalton factory tripled module capacity to 5.1 GW in late 2023.
- Together with the expanded Dalton factory, Qcells' total module capacity in Georgia will reach 8.6 GW a year.
- Combined with Dalton, Qcells will produce 47,000 panels a day.
- The combined output is approximately the energy needed to power roughly 1.3 million U.S. homes for a year.
- The Cartersville investment is bringing thousands of skilled manufacturing jobs to Northwest Georgia.
- Together with the expanded Dalton campus, Qcells' Georgia operations are expected to employ nearly 4,000 people.
- This includes an estimated 3,800 direct jobs across Bartow and Whitfield Counties.
