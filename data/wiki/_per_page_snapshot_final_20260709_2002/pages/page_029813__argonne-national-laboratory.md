# Argonne National Laboratory

**Type:** research_center  ·  **Role:** research_and_development  ·  **Published:** 2023-05-04

**Source:** [A new look at the electric vehicle supply chain as battery-powered cars hit the roads en masse | Argonne National Laboratory](https://www.anl.gov/education/a-new-look-at-the-electric-vehicle-supply-chain-as-batterypowered-cars-hit-the-roads-en-masse)  ·  `page_029813`

## Facts

- EV adoption is beginning to take off, meaning more people will be purchasing EVs now and in the future.
- Scientists and policymakers want to know how the battery supply chain will accommodate and reinforce increasing demand.
- A new wave of investment in battery development and manufacturing of next-generation EV battery packs in the U.S. will help America hit the goal of roughly 10 million new EVs sold per year by 2030.
- Argonne researchers have studied trends in EV manufacturing and adoption, analyzed the supply chain, and determined the impacts of more widespread EVs in terms of displaced petroleum consumption and greenhouse gas emissions.
- Dave Gohlke, an Argonne energy and environmental analyst, stated that EVs are beginning to gain a broader foothold in the market and this trend will only accelerate throughout the remainder of the decade.
- Gohlke noted that increased investment and adoption of EVs will create thousands of new American jobs, help decarbonize the country, combat climate change, and shape the economy and environment.
- Joann Zhou, interim director of Argonne's System Assessment Center and manager of its mobility and deployment group, stated that the 2022 Inflation Reduction Act provides tax incentives for domestically produced EVs to promote an American EV manufacturing pipeline.
- Zhou said the Inflation Reduction Act marks an important milestone for getting more EVs produced domestically.
- Argonne researchers track EV sales and new investment announcements in battery and EV manufacturing, asking who made the battery and where, and who assembled the car and where.
- Gohlke reported that since last year, there has been roughly one new announcement of a battery development or EV manufacturing project every few days, a dramatic increase even since the beginning of the pandemic.
- Xinyi Wu, an Argonne postdoctoral researcher, noted that many of these announcements are substantial in terms of allocated dollars and planned jobs.
- Argonne's chief goal is to supply DOE and others with information about the impacts of accelerated EV manufacture and adoption, focusing on job creation numbers and quantitative analysis of potential gasoline and greenhouse gas reductions.
- Gohlke stated that tracking and analyzing this data is essential for helping DOE and the Biden Administration meet their decarbonization goals.
- To reduce EV production costs, companies are tending to locate battery manufacturing facilities in states that contain vehicle assembly plants.
- Zhou explained that major investments in EV technology have led the automotive community to efficiently build vehicles, resulting in an emerging 'battery belt'.
- Argonne research identified Michigan and Kentucky as key states for EV battery production through 2030, with each supplying between 97 and 136 gigawatt-hours of battery capacity by the end of the decade.
- Georgia, North Carolina, Ohio, Tennessee, and Kansas would also be important contributors to domestic EV battery capacity.
- Domestic EV battery manufacturing capacity will increase by almost 20 times between 2021 and 2030, according to a November 2022 Argonne report.
- A map on the DOE website based on Argonne's work shows the locations of new U.S. battery manufacturing and supply chain investments.
- Zhou pointed to battery recycling as a way to obtain critical materials in the U.S. needed for new batteries, noting Argonne's long history in the field could help secure the supply chain.
- The ReCell Center, a multilab center funded by DOE and led by Argonne, is exploring battery recycling technologies to establish a circular battery ecosystem.
- Argonne National Laboratory is the nation's first national laboratory, conducting leading-edge basic and applied scientific research in virtually every scientific discipline.
- Argonne researchers work closely with hundreds of companies, universities, and federal, state, and municipal agencies to solve specific problems and advance America's scientific leadership.
- Argonne is managed by UChicago Argonne, LLC for the U.S. Department of Energy’s Office of Science.
- The U.S. Department of Energy’s Office of Science is the single largest supporter of basic research in the physical sciences in the United States.
