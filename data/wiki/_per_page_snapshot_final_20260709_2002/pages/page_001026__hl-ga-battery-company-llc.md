# HL-GA Battery Company, LLC

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-05-26

**Source:** [LG battery plant ICE operation: Details emerge after raid of Hyundai megasite in Georgia](https://www.yahoo.com/news/articles/lg-battery-plant-ice-operation-090649489.html)  ·  `page_001026`

## Facts

- Homeland Security Investigations targeting four individuals led to the detainment of about 475 people on Thursday, Sept. 4.
- A U.S. Department of Justice press release referred to the HSI and ICE raid at HL-GA Battery Company, LLC as Operation Low Voltage.
- The battery plant is located at 9730 US Highway 280 in Ellabell, Ga.
- The search warrant named Andreina Fuentes-Tovar, Kevin Zavaleta-Ramirez, David Zavaleta-Ramirez and Julio Gonzalez Alvarado as Target Persons of the Sept. 4 raid.
- Approximately 400 officers across multiple federal and Georgia agencies assisted with the operation.
- The battery plant was first announced on Friday, May 26, 2023 as a joint venture between Hyundai and LG Energy Solution.
- The plant is part of the state's economic agreement with Hyundai to build an electric vehicle manufacturing plant and affiliate facilities in Bryan County.
- Production of EV batteries was slated to begin later in 2023 once construction was completed.
- LG was responsible for the governance of the HL-GA facility, including during its construction, until the raid.
- LG officials announced it is cooperating fully with authorities and has paused construction on the site.
- The warrant identifies the HL-GA plant as a 35-acre site within the larger Hyundai megasite.
- The megasite houses or will house five Hyundai affiliates: Hyundai Mobis, Hyundai Glovis, Hyundai Transys, Hyundai Steel, and Hyundai Material.
- Hyundai's economic agreement with Georgia requires all companies on the megasite to create and sustain a combined 6,800 to 8,500 permanent jobs by Dec. 31, 2031.
- The agreement mandates that the jobs' salaries must average $58,105.
- The battery plant initially committed to fulfilling 1,600 of those required jobs and later committed to 400 more for a total of 2,000.
- HMGMA officials stated it aims to hire around 2,600 workers.
- Hyundai's affiliates will cover the remaining positions, though exact hiring numbers for each have not been made public.
- Roughly 300 of the detainees were Korean.
- LG Communications and Government Affairs Manager Phil Lienert confirmed that 47 of those detained are LG employees, with 46 being Korean and one Indonesian.
- About 250 detainees are affiliated with HL-GA's partner companies, with most being Korean but exact numbers and nationalities needing confirmation.
- Individuals detained in the Sept. 4 operation were immediately taken to Folkston Detention Center in South Georgia.
- In June, Charlton County approved a $50 million contract with ICE to expand Folkston, which would make it the largest detention center in the country.
- Some workers may have relied on the U.S. Visa Waiver Program and ESTA for stays of 90 days or less without obtaining a visa.
- Reuters reported that many Korean detainees had After-Sales Service and Installation Visas, otherwise known as B-1 Visas.
- Atlanta-based immigration lawyer Charles Kuck affirmed many Korean detainees were in the country on B-1 visas for business consulting, contract negotiation, or short-term training.
- No evidence had been shared confirming that any detained person used these authorizations to work or contract at the HL-GA plant.
- The warrant for Operation Low Voltage was filed Tuesday, Sept. 2, commanding execution on or before Sept. 14.
- U.S. Magistrate Judge Christopher Ray of the Southern District of Georgia signed the warrant.
- Assistant United States Attorney Tania Groover filed a request to unseal the warrant on behalf of U.S. Attorney Meg Heap.
- Investigators on the case include Groover, Ryan Bondura, Kelsey Scanlon, and E. Greg Gilluly, Jr.
- The warrant sought to seize documents related to ownership and management of HL-GA and contractors TK LLC, Autorica LLC, SBY America, Beyond Iron Construction LLC, and Steel Brothers Development Inc.
- Sought documentation included time cards, payroll information, photographs of employees, Visas, permanent resident cards, employment authorization cards, passports, and birth certificates.
- Beyond Iron's site address is listed as 1 LG Road, Ellabell, Georgia, 31308 with a mailing address at 3229 Weyhill Trail, Buford, Georgia, 30519.
- Whether the pause on construction will impact EV production at HMGMA remains unknown.
- HMGMA had asserted it will produce 500,000 units annually once fully operational.
- Hyundai Motor Company President and CEO José Muñoz stated in March that those units would include hybrid vehicles at what was initially touted as an all-EV plant.
- The Trump Administration froze $5 billion for EV charger infrastructure for America's highways in February, which lasted about six months until a federal judge ruled in favor of states suing the government.
- Aspen Aerogels Inc. pulled out of its contract to construct a $325-million plant in Statesboro in February.
- The Trump Administration removed federal tax credits for EVs, making $4,000 for used EVs or $7,500 for new ones unavailable after Sept. 30.
