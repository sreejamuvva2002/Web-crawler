# Daechang Seat Corp. USA

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-10-23

**Source:** [Press release, 2023 October 23. - Digital Library of Georgia](https://dlg.usg.edu/record/dlg_ggpd_137338798-2023-10-23)  ·  `page_018891`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Daechang Seat Corp. USA will invest $72.5 million in the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center.
- The facility will be located in close proximity to Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA).
- The project will create over 500 jobs in Chatham County.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that suppliers for the Hyundai Metaplant project alone reach at least nine counties.
- Daechang Seat Corp. (DSC) was established in 1979 in Kyungju, South Korea.
- DSC has become one of the largest seat frame producers in the world.
- Globally, DSC employs more than 5,000 people across seven countries.
- The company's production process includes fully automatic lines with arc welding and spot-welding machines.
- Jinsuk Lee serves as the CEO of Daechang Seat Savannah Corp.
- DSC maintains a prominent partnership with Hyundai Transys and Hyundai Mobis at HMGMA.
- The company's primary objective is to drive growth through facility expansions to transform into a global entity.
- DSC will be hiring for assembly operators, equipment technicians, mechanical engineers, and electrical engineers.
- Hiring will occur through local job fairs and website postings in the future.
- Griff Lynch is the Georgia Ports Authority President and CEO.
- The new facility is located only minutes from the Port of Savannah.
- Trip Tollison is the Savannah Economic Development Authority President and CEO.
- DSC will be the third Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America supplier to locate in the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center.
- Alyce Thornhill represents the Georgia Department of Economic Development's (GDEcD) Global Commerce team as Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy.
- GDEcD partnered with the Savannah Economic Development Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start on this project.
- Pat Wilson is the GDEcD Commissioner.
- Since 2018, Georgia has created more than $25 billion in investments and 30,000 jobs by pursuing the entire EV supply chain.
- In fiscal year 2023, job creation in the automotive industry increased by 324 percent compared to FY21.
- Suppliers attracted by Georgia's mobility original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) resulted in over $2 billion in investment across the state in FY23.
- DSC has recently ventured into the electric vehicle sector and continues to expand its scope.
- DSC anticipates greater opportunities for growth with the completion of new manufacturing facilities, which will provide larger installation spaces.
