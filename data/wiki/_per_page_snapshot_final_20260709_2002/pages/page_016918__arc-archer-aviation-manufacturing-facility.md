# ARC (Archer Aviation Manufacturing Facility)

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Archer Completes 400,000 square foot eVTOL manufacturing facility](https://zagdaily.com/zag-air/archer-completes-400000-square-foot-evtol-manufacturing-facility/)  ·  `page_016918`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation has completed construction of its high-volume manufacturing facility, ARC, in Covington, Georgia.
- The 400,000-square-foot facility is located next to Covington Municipal Airport and serves as a key milestone for scaling production of Archer's Midnight eVTOL aircraft.
- Initial manufacturing at the facility is set to begin in early 2025.
- The decision to establish ARC in Georgia followed an extensive nationwide site selection evaluation.
- Key considerations for selecting Georgia included local labor talent availability, utility availability, ability to conduct seamless flight test operations, construction costs, and logistics, according to Glen Burks, Archer’s Vice President of Operations.
- Archer received strong support from state and local officials during the process, including Governor Brian Kemp and Newton County leaders.
- The ARC facility is expected to create over 1,000 jobs long-term, cementing Georgia’s role as a hub for advanced manufacturing.
- Georgia is home to more than 800 aerospace companies and maintains a robust aerospace education pipeline with a dozen high schools offering field training, five technical colleges with aviation programs, and several universities offering aerospace engineering degrees.
- Archer has been working closely with local educational institutions to attract and prepare local talent by creating opportunities for Georgia residents.
- Archer is leveraging its partnership with Stellantis, which has embedded dozens of personnel in the project to apply automotive manufacturing expertise to eVTOL production.
- Stellantis provided Archer with expertise throughout the site selection process and continues to contribute across several key teams related to manufacturing and beyond.
- With ARC’s completion, Archer is transitioning from research and development into full-scale commercialization, aiming to ramp production to two aircraft per month by the end of 2025.
