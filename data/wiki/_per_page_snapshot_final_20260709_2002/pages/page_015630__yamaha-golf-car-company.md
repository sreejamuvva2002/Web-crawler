# Yamaha Golf-Car Company

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2020-09-24

**Source:** [INTERIOR SECRETARY DAVID BERNHARDT MEETS WITH YAMAHA GOLF-CAR COMPANY PRESIDENT TOM MCDONALD | Golf Cart Resource](https://golfcartresource.com/interior-secretary-david-bernhardt-meets-with-yamaha-golf-car-company-president-tom-mcdonald/)  ·  `page_015630`

## Facts

- On September 24, 2020, Yamaha Golf-Car President Tom McDonald met with Interior Secretary David Bernhardt at the Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation (YMMC) facility in Newnan, Ga.
- Mike Chrzanowski, President of Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation (YMCC), and Ben Speciale, President of Yamaha U.S. Marine Business Unit, signed the President’s Pledge to America’s Workers during the event.
- The signing reinforces Yamaha’s commitment to workforce development within its U.S. manufacturing facilities.
- Tom McDonald stated that jobs created through Yamaha facilities in Georgia make a positive impact on the state’s economy and contribute to the growth of the outdoor industry overall.
- In 2016, the golf industry supported $191.9 billion in total annual activity, including 1.9 million jobs and $58.7 billion in wages and benefits.
- U.S. golf participation saw record increases with rounds played up 14% in June and 19.7% in July, with industry experts expecting similar August results.
- McDonald emphasized that Yamaha will continue to invest in training and best practices necessary to create successful manufacturing career paths within each of its divisions.
- Yamaha operates a technician training facility in Kennesaw, Ga., which supports dealers and distributors across the country.
- The President’s Pledge to America’s Workers originated from President Trump’s National Council for the American Worker, established on July 19, 2019, to lead a national strategy for training and re-training workers in high-demand industries like recreational manufacturing.
- More than 430 companies have signed the pledge, contributing to over 16 million new training opportunities for American students and workers over the next five years.
- Yamaha Motor Corporation, USA (YMUS) offers a wide range of products including Power Assist Bicycles, Golf Cars, Power Assist Wheelchair Systems, and various powersports and marine vehicles.
- YMUS maintains corporate offices in California and two locations in Georgia, along with facilities in Wisconsin and Alabama, and factory operations in Tennessee and Georgia.
- Yamaha Golf-Car Company is headquartered in Kennesaw, Georgia, with assembly and production of all its cars taking place at its Newnan, Georgia production facility.
- The company was the first to develop Electronic Fuel Injection technology in a golf car, producing the best gas mileage and smallest carbon footprint in the industry.
- Yamaha Golf-Car Company was also the first to introduce a golf car with fully independent, four-wheel suspension and the first gas-powered car that is almost as quiet as an electric one.
