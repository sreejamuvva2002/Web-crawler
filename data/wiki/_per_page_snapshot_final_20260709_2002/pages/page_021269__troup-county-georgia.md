# Troup County, Georgia

## Facts

- Troup County comprises 446 square miles in west-central Georgia.
- The county is located about 45 minutes south of Atlanta, about 45 minutes to the east of Auburn, Alabama, and about 45 minutes north of Columbus, Georgia.
- Troup County has a population of about 70,000.
- The local economy shifted from textile manufacturing to the automotive industry, advanced manufacturing, and tourism around 2007-08.
- Kia established operations in La Grange around 2007-08.
- Kia's North American headquarters is located in Troup County.
- The facility produces the Kia Telluride, the upcoming EV9 electric SUV, the Kia Sportage, the K5 (formerly the Optima), and the Kia Sorrento.
- Kia employs about 3,000 people in Troup County.
- Subsidiary suppliers around Kia include Hyundai and Sewon.
- Approximately 13-15,000 employees are directly related to Kia across the county's supplier network.
- Troup County serves as the international headquarters for Duracell.
- County officials state that the majority of batteries utilized throughout the world are manufactured in Troup County.
- The county is expanding its water and sewer infrastructure to support economic development and industrial growth.
- Troup County has invested heavily over the past ten years to maintain high-quality roads and corridors for company access.
- A CSX and Georgia Ports Authority inland port project is being developed to transport goods from Kia, Duracell, and other suppliers to the Port of Savannah and beyond.
- The county hosts three higher education institutions: LaGrange College, Point University, and West Georgia Technical College.
- Hyundai Transys Georgia Seating System LLC operates in West Point, Georgia, supplying premium seating solutions to Hyundai, Kia, Lucid, and Rivian.
- Hyundai Transys focuses on ergonomic design, cutting-edge technology, and environmental responsibility for automotive seating components.
