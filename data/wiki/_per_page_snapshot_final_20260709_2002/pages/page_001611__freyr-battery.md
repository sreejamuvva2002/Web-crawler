# FREYR Battery

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-03-28

**Source:** [The POWER Interview: FREYR Leads Wave of New Battery Manufacturing](https://www.powermag.com/the-power-interview-freyr-leads-wave-of-new-battery-manufacturing/)  ·  `page_001611`

## Facts

- Growth in the energy storage market, including the transportation sector, has spurred a ramp-up in global battery manufacturing and investment in new technologies.
- Passage of the Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) in the U.S. has brought a flurry of announcements from foreign companies eager to build manufacturing plants in America.
- FREYR, a Norway-based company, late last year announced it had purchased a site in Coweta County, Georgia, for its Giga America battery plant.
- The company projected an initial capital spend of $1.7 billion for the Georgia project.
- The Georgia project was projected to create some 720 jobs in the region.
- South Korea-based LG Energy Solution on March 24 said it will quadruple its initial budget for a battery manufacturing plant in Queen Creek, Arizona, spending $5.5 billion for the project.
- LG’s Arizona site will have two main facilities: one for building cylindrical batteries used in electric vehicles (EVs), and another for lithium-iron-phosphate pouch-type batteries for energy storage systems.
- LG recently announced construction is underway for a battery-manufacturing project in Ohio in conjunction with Honda.
- The U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) expects U.S. battery production will increase 20-fold by 2030 compared to 2021 levels.
- The DOE stated a wave of new planned electric vehicle battery plants will increase North America’s battery manufacturing capacity from 55 Gigawatt-hours per year in 2021 to nearly 1,000 GWh/year by 2030.
- The DOE noted many battery plants will be co-located with automotive plants to optimize supply chain logistics.
- Most planned projects in the United States are concentrated along a north-south band from Michigan to Alabama.
- Based on current plans, Kentucky, Tennessee, Georgia, and Michigan will see the highest growth in battery manufacturing capacity.
- Korea’s Samsung SDI announced plans for multiple U.S. projects, including a $2.5 billion facility in Indiana in partnership with Stellantis.
- FREYR’s Georgia factory will likely be a model for that company as well as for other U.S. battery manufacturing facilities.
- Tom Jensen, co-founder and CEO, said the Georgia site “underscores FREYR’s ambition to develop a very strong and near-term operational footprint in the United States.”
- Expanding into the U.S. has been a foundational aspect of FREYR’s long-term strategy from its inception.
- With the recent passage of the Inflation Reduction Act, FREYR expects U.S. demand for ESS, passenger EV and other electric mobility applications to grow rapidly over the next decade.
- FREYR is supporting its expansion plans with the opening of a Customer Qualification Plant (CQP) in Norway.
- The CQP facility in Mo i Rana in northern Norway came online March 28.
- The CQP is energized by hydropower as part of a long-term agreement with Statkraft from that company’s Rana hydropower facility.
- The factory features a demonstration production line built at the actual physical size of a normal production line in commercial operation.
- The demonstration line allows creation of sample sales for customers and provides capability to test different materials in the manufacturing process.
- The building serves as a training facility for FREYR workers.
- The training facility enables workers to know the challenges that could be part of operating the company’s planned commercial gigafactories such as the one in Georgia.
- Jensen said the Chapter One opening ceremony at the CQP inaugurates the global launch of one industrial-scale production line of the 24M Technologies SemiSolid production platform at GWh scale.
- The company planned an investor-focused event at the CQP this summer to demonstrate automated production of clean, next-generation batteries with this ground-breaking technology.
- FREYR announced potential development of industrial-scale battery cell production in Vaasa, Finland, as well as the U.S.
- The company intends to install 50 GWh of battery cell capacity by 2025.
- The company plans for 100 GWh annual capacity by 2028.
- The company plans for 200 GWh of annual capacity by 2030.
- The company announced it has begun discussions on a potential strategic coalition with Glencore Plc, Caterpillar, Siemens AG, and Nidec Corp. to pursue the scale up of sustainable battery solutions across Europe, North America, and beyond.
- Jensen said the announcement represents an important first step for FREYR and its four potential global partners as they launch this commercial initiative.
- FREYR’s vision for the coalition is to accelerate the energy transition across key sectors by scaling up clean battery production from gigafactories under development, and to incubate and develop innovative technology solutions together.
- Tom Jensen answered questions about the facility and the company’s plans to expand its battery manufacturing and provide innovative technology for the energy storage and e-mobility sector.
- FREYR aims to provide industrial-scale clean battery solutions to reduce global emissions and produce green battery cells to accelerate decarbonization of energy and transportation systems globally.
- One reason for choosing Mo i Rana in northern Norway is the availability of abundant and low-cost renewable energy.
- In May 2022, FREYR signed an agreement with Statkraft, Europe’s largest producer of renewable energy, to secure a supply of renewable power under globally competitive terms for operations in Mo i Rana.
- FREYR decided early to build the CQP as a full industrial-scale production line at GWh scale, marking a world first for the SemiSolid technology platform.
- The company’s ambition is to install eight of these lines in Giga Arctic in Mo i Rana and replicate them in Giga America across the Atlantic.
- Having an actual industrial-scale production line in the CQP will shorten the time to reach targeted yield and uptime in subsequent gigafactories.
- FREYR has chosen a U.S. technology licensing partner in the MIT-spinoff 24M Technologies Inc.
- The battery cell design is improved through providing substantially larger and thicker electrodes, which implies fewer repeating layers in the cell and increases the amount of energy-carrying material per battery cell, lowering bill of material costs per KWh.
- The larger physical structure provides for significant system-level cost savings as it requires fewer modules, packs and Battery Management System-related costs at the final product/system level.
- The production process is dramatically simplified and fundamentally improved, projecting substantially lower capex, lower energy consumption, less space, and up to three times as many batteries produced per employee when produced at GWh scale.
- FREYR created real option value with parallel project development in Norway and the U.S. catalyzed by CQP and the IRA.
- The company is evaluating multiple options to fast-track the start of production at Giga America, targeting start-up in 2025.
- Customers value that FREYR can provide products from multiple geographies.
- The U.S. is the fastest-growing ESS market in the world, which is a core focus segment for FREYR.
- The SemiSolid platform significantly simplifies the production process, reducing the number of production process steps in half.
- The technology produces larger and thicker electrodes, making final battery products much more adapted to end products like vehicles and energy storage containers.
- The fundamental change in the technology is that the electrolyte is used as the binder and solvent upfront in the production process, eliminating the need for expensive and space-consuming solvent recovery and drying process steps.
- FREYR projects the simplified production process will reduce capex per installed GWh unit significantly, reduce energy consumption, and triple productivity per employee through further automation and digitalization.
- At the final product level, physically larger products could reduce total costs significantly by reducing the number of repeating structures at the system level.
- FREYR’s general strategy is to develop a network of global suppliers while targeting regional and decarbonized supply chains.
- The company wants to minimize logistics chains to make them as cost-efficient and carbon-efficient as possible while maintaining global flexibility to be resilient.
- A major share of raw materials needed in Europe will be supplied from Europe, and for the U.S., from North America, with some cross-continental supply including Asia.
- FREYR has so far been able to secure all raw materials it needs.
- A major part of the volatility in underlying raw material costs is covered through pass-through mechanisms in offtake contracts.
- To meet growing demand and stay within reasonable climate boundaries, the world will need to install seven times more battery capacity in the next seven years.
- The main challenge facing the battery industry is scaling up fast, which requires huge amounts of capital and competence in record-breaking time to ensure high-quality products with impeccable safety and sustainability standards.
