# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-10-25

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp, Hyundai Motor Group Execs Break Ground at Georgia’s Bryan County Megasite | Governor Brian P. Kemp Office of the Governor](https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases/2022-10-25/gov-kemp-hyundai-motor-group-execs-break-ground-georgias-bryan-county)  ·  `page_000100`

## Facts

- October 25, 2022: Governor Brian P. Kemp, Hyundai Motor Group Executive Chair Euisun Chung, and company officials officially broke ground on Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America's new manufacturing plant at the Bryan County Megasite.
- The $5.54 billion investment for this facility by the company and its affiliate suppliers was announced in May of this year.
- The facility includes plans to produce a diverse range of innovative, fully electric vehicles (EV) and a new battery manufacturing facility.
- HMGMA will create more than 8,100 jobs over the next few years.
- The company is establishing a stable supply chain for EV battery and associated components in the U.S. market.
- HMGMA’s new smart factory is expected to begin commercial production in the first half of 2025.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that record jobs and investment are coming to all parts of Georgia, citing award-winning workforce development programs and infrastructure as drivers for the state's economic success.
- Hyundai Motor Group Executive Chair Euisun Chung said Hyundai and Georgia share qualities like respect for history, ingenuity, creativity, and determination to improve the world for the next generation.
- Chung noted that Hyundai's EVs are recognized as best in class and the company aims to be the global leader in electrification, safety, quality, and sustainability.
- Chung stated that through the Metaplant America, Hyundai will evolve beyond an automaker to become the world’s leading mobility solutions provider.
- The groundbreaking celebration included a morning on-site event followed by an expo and program at Enmarket Arena in Savannah for hundreds of attendees highlighting "advanced technology and extraordinary human achievement."
- HMGMA announced corporate social responsibility programs totaling $1.54 million to enrich local communities.
- Programs through Hyundai Hope and Genesis Gives collectively committed $120,000 to roughly ten organizations supporting local communities in and around Bryan County.
- Hyundai announced a $120,000 donation to Georgia Tech for STEM education scholarships.
- Hyundai Hope On Wheels presented $500,000 in Scholar Hope and Young Investigator Grants to Children’s Healthcare of Atlanta.
- Genesis Inspiration Foundation presented donations totaling $200,000 to two Georgia-based nonprofit organizations, Drawchange and Paint Love.
- Kia’s "Accelerate the Good" program donated $600,000 to help local youth, with gifts to Family Promise of the Coastal Empire, America’s Second Harvest of Coastal Georgia, St. Jude Children's Research Hospital, and Inspiredu.
- Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority Chairman Carter Infinger said the groundbreaking signifies HMGMA's commitment to the community and Savannah region, noting the $5.54 billion investment and 8,100 jobs alongside community contributions.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Hyundai placed a bet on Georgia sixteen years ago and has since become a Georgia company, changing lives in West Point and across the state.
- Wilson said the Bryan County facility will directly create jobs of the future for the Savannah area and support opportunities in the EV revolution for Georgians across the state.
- Central to the selection of the Bryan County Megasite was its close proximity to the Port of Savannah.
- Under the leadership of Griff Lynch and the Georgia Ports Authority, the Ports of Savannah and Brunswick have expanded to meet recent challenges and moved record amounts of cargo.
- With connections to the port by both rail and highway, Hyundai will be able to access global markets quickly and efficiently.
- Non-affiliated HMGMA suppliers in connection with the project will invest approximately $1 billion in other locations around the state.
- Hyundai Motor Group is a global enterprise with about 250,000 employees worldwide.
- The Group's mobility brands include Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis.
