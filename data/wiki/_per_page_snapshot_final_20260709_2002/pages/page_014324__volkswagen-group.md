# Volkswagen Group

**Source:** [VW growing plants with strong routes in North America | Feature | Automotive Logistics](https://www.automotivelogistics.media/vw-growing-plants-with-strong-routes-in-north-america/8151.article)  ·  `page_014324`

## Facts

- Fabio Freccia is the head of logistics for Volkswagen at its operations in the United States.
- Roman Müller is the head of logistics for Volkswagen at its operations in Mexico.
- Logistics and supply chain management are keeping apace with the OEM’s rapid growth in production in North America.
- The recovery in the North American automotive industry has brought record profits to some American OEMs and a resurgence to Japanese carmakers following tsunamis, floods, and recalls.
- Volkswagen Group has experienced impressive pure growth in North America.
- The group's US sales surged by more than 30% in 2012, including Volkswagen, Audi, and Porsche.
- This sales surge came on the back of a significant investment in North American manufacturing and supply chain capacity.
- Volkswagen's new Chattanooga, Tennessee plant produced more than 150,000 vehicles in its first full year of production.
- The group operates a sprawling manufacturing complex in Puebla, Mexico, located some 130km east of Mexico City.
- Production at the Puebla plant rose almost 40% from 2010 levels to surpass 605,000 vehicles in 2012.
