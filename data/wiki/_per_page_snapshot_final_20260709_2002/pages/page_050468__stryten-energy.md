# Stryten Energy

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-04-01

**Source:** [Stryten Energy ramps up its U.S. battery manufacturing capacity to 24 GW - pv magazine USA](https://pv-magazine-usa.com/2025/04/01/stryten-energy-ramps-up-its-u-s-battery-manufacturing-capacity-to-24-gw/)  ·  `page_050468`

## Facts

- Battery manufacturer Stryten Energy announced plans to add 10 GW of domestic energy storage capacity across its 11 U.S. manufacturing and battery component plants.
- The expansion brings Stryten’s total domestic manufacturing capacity to 24 GW.
- The expanded capacity will increase the domestic supply of batteries for long-duration energy storage applications.
- The expanded capacity will increase the domestic supply of batteries for military applications.
- The expanded capacity will increase the domestic supply of batteries for transportation applications.
- The expanded capacity will increase the domestic supply of batteries for material-handling applications.
- Existing advanced manufacturing tax credits supported Stryten's new investment.
- The Georgia-based company’s investment will fund its facilities in Georgia, Arkansas, Kansas, Iowa, Ontario, Indiana, Missouri, Pennsylvania, Wisconsim and New York.
- Mike Judd, president and CEO of Stryten Energy, stated that the path to American energy security must rely upon domestic companies like Stryten that manufacture batteries to keep supply chains running and critical infrastructure supplied with backup power.
- Stryten operates a domestic, vertically integrated supply chain that provides battery components to its factories and the broader U.S. battery manufacturing industry.
- Stryten’s energy solutions are built in the U.S. mostly using domestic components, though it uses some imported components.
- Stryten has a suite of domestically manufactured battery solutions that use advanced lead, lithium and vanadium redox flow battery technology.
- Stryten recently opened a lithium battery assembly plant in Georgia that supports military and industrial battery needs with its lithium module technology.
