# Georgia Department of Economic Development

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2025-09

**Source:** [Why Georgia Stays on Top: GDEcD's Misti Martin on Growth, Talent, and What’s Next - Metro Atlanta CEO](https://metroatlantaceo.com/features/2025/09/why-georgia-stays-top-misti-martin-growth-talent-and-whats-next/)  ·  `page_012268`

## Facts

- Georgia has been ranked the No. 1 state for business for a decade, with success driven by focused, flexible, and community-driven leadership.
- GDEcD Deputy Commissioner of Global Commerce Misti Martin shared in an interview with Site Selection magazine how the state’s partnerships, infrastructure investments, and business-first mindset continue to deliver results.
- Martin stated: “We’ll accomplish our goals by delivering on our consistent and proven methods. Georgia’s collaborative approach and strong, solutions-based partnerships with our industries allow us to support their growth trajectories.”
- Before joining GDEcD, Martin led economic development in Cherokee County, one of metro Atlanta’s fastest-growing areas, bringing local experience to a statewide strategy focused on job creation, smart expansion, and transformative investments.
- The state is leveraging transformative investments from global companies including Hyundai Motor Group, Kia, Q CELLS, and Rivian.
- Martin identifies three pillars giving Georgia its competitive edge: a skilled workforce, well-planned infrastructure, and a consistent business-friendly climate.
- Georgia Quick Start is ranked as the No. 1 workforce training program in the nation.
- The Technical College System of Georgia (TCSG) experienced a 10.6% enrollment boost in 2024.
- Programs like GEORGIA MATCH and Be Pro Be Proud Georgia are helping students connect with in-state higher education and training to strengthen the talent pipeline for Georgia employers.
- Beyond major electric mobility and clean energy headlines, the state is now focused on building out entire supply chains that include logistics providers, advanced manufacturers, recyclers, and R&D firms.
- Billions have been allocated for highway, airport, and freight corridor improvements to help Georgia stay globally competitive for the next decade of growth.
- GDEcD collaborates with local economic developers, utility partners, technical colleges, and employers to ensure each project aligns with regional goals.
- Supporting Georgia-grown businesses to expand and innovate remains a top priority after initial company arrival.
- Georgia’s logistics infrastructure includes two deepwater ports, the world’s busiest airport, and an integrated highway and rail system, making it one of the most connected states in the country.
- Martin emphasizes that despite strong infrastructure, the state’s greatest competitive strength remains its people.
