# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA)

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-10-26

**Source:** [Hyundai launches construction of EV factory in the USA - electrive.com](https://www.electrive.com/2022/10/26/hyundai-launches-construction-of-ev-factory-in-the-usa/)  ·  `page_009202`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group has started construction of its new electric car plant in the US state of Georgia.
- The Bryan County plant is scheduled to begin production in the first half of 2025.
- The plant will have an annual capacity of 300,000 Hyundai, Kia and Genesis electric cars and batteries.
- Hyundai announced the plant in May and originally planned to start construction in early 2023.
- Construction was broken ground in October because of new rules for electric car subsidies in the USA.
- The planned start of operations remains in the first half of 2025.
- Hyundai plans to invest just over $5.5 billion.
- The project will create 8,100 jobs at Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA), often just called Metaplant.
- Georgia governor Brian P. Kemp attended the groundbreaking ceremony in Savannah.
- Hyundai CEO Euisun Chung attended the ceremony.
- Hyundai CEO Euisun Chung stated: “Today, our EVs are recognized as best in class, and with this partnership, we are determined to be the global leader in electrification, safety, quality, and sustainability.”
- Hyundai CEO Euisun Chung added: “With the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America, we will continue to evolve beyond an automaker to the world’s leading mobility solutions provider.”
- The South Korean government and some EU countries disagree with the new US subsidy for electric vehicles, specifically the US value-added requirements for eligibility.
- US President Joe Biden expressed willingness in October to “continue discussions with South Korea on the recent US legislation”.
- Hyundai currently builds its E-GMP models such as the Ioniq 5 or the Ioniq 6 only in South Korea.
- Some of Hyundai’s upcoming electric models are particularly targeted at the US market, such as the Hyundai Ioniq 7 and Kia EV9 large E-GMP SUVs.
- According to earlier reports, the Hyundai Ioniq 7 and Kia EV9 will initially be built at the new Georgia plant.
- If these vehicles were not eligible for the $7,500 tax credit, it would be a major competitive disadvantage.
