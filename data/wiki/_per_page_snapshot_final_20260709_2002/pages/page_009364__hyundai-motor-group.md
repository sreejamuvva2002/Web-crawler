# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [US immigration officers raid Hyundai's electric vehicle site in Georgia | AP News](https://apnews.com/article/immigration-raid-hyundai-georgia-electric-vehicles-191904f42f540898035feb2a6623d98e)  ·  `page_009364`

## Facts

- U.S. immigration authorities raided the sprawling site where Hyundai manufactures electric vehicles in southeast Georgia on Thursday.
- The operation shut down construction on an adjacent factory being built to produce EV batteries.
- The site is touted by the governor and other officials as one of Georgia’s largest and most high-profile manufacturing sites, and the largest economic development project in the state’s history.
- Hyundai Motor Group began manufacturing EVs at the $7.6 billion plant a year ago.
- The plant employs about 1,200 people.
- U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement spokesman Lindsay Williams confirmed federal authorities were conducting an enforcement operation at the 3,000-acre (1,214-hectare) site west of Savannah.
- Agents focused their search on the construction site for the battery plant.
- The Department of Homeland Security stated agents executed a search warrant as part of an ongoing criminal investigation into allegations of unlawful employment practices and other serious federal crimes.
- DHS did not disclose whether anyone was detained or arrested during the operation.
- Georgia State Patrol troopers blocked roads to the Hyundai site to assist federal authorities dispatched by the Georgia Department of Public Safety.
- Video posted to social media showed workers in yellow safety vests lined up while a man wearing a tactical vest with HSI (Homeland Security Investigations) letters ordered construction to cease immediately and all work to end on the site right now.
- President Donald Trump’s administration has undertaken sweeping ICE operations as part of a mass deportation agenda, including raids on farms, construction sites, restaurants, and auto repair shops.
- The Pew Research Center cited preliminary Census Bureau data indicating the U.S. labor force lost more than 1.2 million immigrants from January through July, including both undocumented individuals and legal residents.
- In addition to making electric vehicles at the site facing Interstate 16 in Bryan County, Hyundai partnered with LG Energy Solution to build the adjacent battery plant.
- The joint venture battery plant is slated to open sometime next year.
- HL-GA Battery Company stated it is cooperating fully with appropriate authorities and has paused construction to assist their work.
- Plant spokesperson Bianca Johnson confirmed that operations at Hyundai’s EV manufacturing plant were not interrupted by the raid.
- Production and normal office hours had already begun for the day when authorities shut down access, and the operation did not impact employees getting to work.
