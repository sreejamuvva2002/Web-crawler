# Georgia Department of Economic Development

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2023-01-19

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp: Key Industries Sustain Momentum for State's Economic Development | Governor Brian P. Kemp Office of the Governor](https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases/2023-01-19/gov-kemp-key-industries-sustain-momentum-states-economic-development)  ·  `page_000094`

## Facts

- GDEcD’s Global Commerce division supported 218 locations or expansions during the first half of Fiscal Year 2023 (July 1 through December 31, 2022).
- These projects are expected to create approximately 17,500 new jobs and more than $13 billion in investment for the state.
- The automotive industry was the second-largest source of new jobs and investment after advanced manufacturing during this period.
- Advanced manufacturing, automotive, aerospace, and food processing accounted for nearly 75 percent of new jobs and 84 percent of investments.
- Planned investments from these projects increased by more than 38 percent compared to the same period last year.
- Areas outside the 10-county metro Atlanta region accounted for 85 percent of jobs created and 92 percent of investments from these economic development projects.
- In October, state and local officials joined company representatives for the groundbreaking of the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) EV facility.
- Korean Ambassador to the U.S. Tae-yong Cho attended the HMGMA groundbreaking.
- The HMGMA EV facility will be located in Bryan, Bulloch, Chatham, and Effingham Counties.
- The HMGMA EV facility is expected to support more than 8,100 direct jobs.
- Three off-site HMGMA suppliers announced locations following the groundbreaking.
- These supplier additions swiftly surpassed the initial $1 billion in investment predicted from them.
- International companies accounted for 23 percent of state projects since July 1, 2022, contributing to a closed-loop electric mobility supply chain.
- SK On and Hyundai Motor Group announced a partnership for a facility in Bartow County.
- The SK On and Hyundai Motor Group project is among the top five largest projects by investment in Georgia’s history.
- Next-generation developer FREYR Battery announced a facility in Coweta County.
- The FREYR Battery facility is among the top five largest projects by investment in Georgia’s history.
- Battery recycler Sung-Il Hitech joined Georgia’s electric mobility ecosystem.
- Battery recycler Denkai America joined Georgia’s electric mobility ecosystem.
- GDEcD utilizes a best-in-class job training program called Georgia Quick Start to help companies solve workforce challenges.
- GDEcD offers GRAD certified sites that support accelerated project timelines for companies.
- GDEcD Deputy Commissioner for Global Commerce Kristi Brigman stated that companies use Georgia Quick Start to build talent and workforce from the ground up.
