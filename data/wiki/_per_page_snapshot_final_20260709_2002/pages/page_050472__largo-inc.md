# Largo Inc.

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Largo Announces a Proposed Joint Venture with Stryten Energy to Bring Innovation and Scale to North American Vanadium Flow Battery Market | INN](https://investingnews.com/largo-announces-a-proposed-joint-venture-with-stryten-energy-to-bring-innovation-and-scale-to-north-american-vanadium-flow-battery-market/)  ·  `page_050472`

## Facts

- Largo Inc. signed a non-binding letter of intent with Stryten Energy LLC to establish a 50:50 joint venture combining Largo Clean Energy Corp. with Stryten's vanadium redox flow battery business.
- The joint venture aims to combine decades of VRFB technology expertise, access to raw vanadium supplies from friendly sources, and high-volume electrolyte production capabilities to transform the long-duration energy storage sector in North America.
- The estimated market in North America for VRFB LDES solutions is hundreds of Gigawatts in size, requiring a vertically integrated vanadium supply chain.
- The joint venture would provide access to U.S.-produced vanadium electrolyte needed for VRFB manufacturers to accelerate commercial deployment of vanadium battery solutions.
- Daniel Tellechea, Director and Interim Chief Executive Officer of Largo, stated the agreement aims to unlock and maximize the value of LCE through its patented vanadium flow battery stack technology, electrolyte purification technology, and access to vanadium via Largo Physical Vanadium Corp.
- Stryten's ability to produce electrolyte in large volumes is expected to help reduce the overall cost for VRFB solutions, a critical factor in catalyzing commercial adoption and meeting DOE LCOS targets.
- The proposed transaction remains subject to negotiation of definitive agreements, completion of due diligence by both parties, and receipt of required Board and regulatory approvals.
- Stryten Energy is headquartered in Alpharetta, Georgia, and provides energy storage solutions across Essential Power, Motive Power, Transportation, Military, and Government sectors.
- Stryten's stored energy technologies include advanced lead, lithium, and vanadium redox flow batteries, intelligent chargers, and energy performance management software.
- Largo Physical Vanadium Corp. aims to supply physical vanadium to end users of VRFBs to advance the integration of renewable energy in long duration storage.
- Largo is currently ramping up production of its ilmenite concentrate plant and evaluating its U.S.-based clean energy business, including advanced VCHARGE vanadium battery technology.
- The announcement coincides with Enel Green Power España and LCE going live with a 5.5-megawatt hour VRFB in Spain, deploying one of the largest utility-scale vanadium systems in Europe.
