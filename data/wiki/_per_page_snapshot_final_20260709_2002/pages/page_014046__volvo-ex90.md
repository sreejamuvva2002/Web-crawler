## Facts

- The three-row electric vehicle market is described as "lucrative" and "tremendously important" for EV sales in the U.S.

- Customers who pre-ordered the Volvo EX90 ($80K) will take delivery next month.
- Rivian's second-generation R1S three-row SUV ($75,900) is receiving praise for its handling and updated tech.
- Cadillac unveiled the Vistiq ($78,790), which accelerates from 0 to 60 in 3.7 seconds and makes 615 horsepower.
- Cadillac has the upcoming Escalade IQ ($127,000), which could compete for sales with the Lucid Gravity ($94,900).
- Industry experts note that these new models could finally sway mainstream drivers to buy a battery-powered vehicle.
- Three-row electric SUVs are already stealing sales from gas-powered SUVs, with the R1S ranking as the 11th top-selling EV nameplate (22,000 units) as of September and the Kia EV9 selling 16,000 units after launching in October 2023.
- Industry experts expect sales of three-row electric SUVs to climb higher, with the Volvo EX90 adding to the mix.
- The Volvo EX90 features minimalist Swedish design and advanced tech, appealing to consumers seeking luxury, unique styling, and an eco-friendly lifestyle.
- Gary Kalsaria, head of future cars for Volvo Cars USA, stated that the EX90 is one of their most important vehicles.
- The EX90 is built on an all-new platform in Charleston, South Carolina, with production already started and customer deliveries starting later this year as preorders far exceed demand.
- The EX90 can be configured with seating for six or seven, gets 310 miles of range on one charge, and accelerates from 0 to 60 mph in 4.7 seconds.
