# City of Calhoun, Gordon County, Georgia

## Facts

- Manufacturing is the primary economic sector in Gordon County, employing over 6,700 workers (about 25% of the total labor force) and centered on carpet, flooring, and textile production.
- Major manufacturing employers in Calhoun include Shaw Industries Group and Mohawk Industries, both leveraging regional expertise in tufted carpet manufacturing.
- Shaw Industries operates multiple facilities in Calhoun, including a production plant located at 355 South Industrial Boulevard.
- Shaw Industries has invested in yarn processing capacity and maintains ongoing hiring for dozens of positions as of 2025.
- Mohawk Industries relocated its headquarters to Georgia and maintains a significant manufacturing presence in Calhoun.
- Mannington Mills made a 2020 investment in a 10.5-acre Calhoun site, creating 268 jobs in related flooring production.
- Carpet and flooring operations account for a plurality of manufacturing jobs in Gordon County within the northwest Georgia cluster that produces approximately 85% of U.S. carpet within a 65-mile radius of Dalton.
- Georgia's carpet mills are projected to employ about 16,473 workers statewide as of 2025.
- Calhoun serves as a logistics hub for northwest Georgia's emerging industries, facilitated by its strategic location along Interstate 75.
- Mid-20th-century construction of Interstate 75 enhanced connectivity to Atlanta and Chattanooga, enabling larger-scale manufacturing and attracting investment in heavy equipment and outboard motors alongside textiles.
- The Gordon County Campus of Georgia Northwestern Technical College (GNTC), located at 1151 Highway 53 Spur SW, Calhoun, GA 30701, provides postsecondary education and workforce training tailored to regional industrial demands.
- GNTC's core offerings emphasize practical skills in aviation and industrial technologies, including welding and precision machining, as well as business, cyber-related, and health technologies.
- GNTC partners with local businesses for apprenticeships and customized training to address skill gaps in technical sectors.
- Diversification efforts into logistics and non-textile manufacturing are complementing but not supplanting the traditional carpet and textile base in Calhoun.
- Gordon County's gross domestic product totaled $3.142 billion in 2023, reflecting growth from $3.068 billion in 2022.
- The county supported approximately 26,800 jobs in 2023, with employment expanding by 1.64% from the prior year amid an unemployment rate averaging around 3.3% through 2024.
