# Bartow County

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2023-08-29

**Source:** [Cartersville | Bartow County: Boom Time in Bartow - Georgia Trend Magazine](https://www.georgiatrend.com/2023/08/29/cartersville-bartow-county-boom-time-in-bartow/)  ·  `page_013805`

## Facts

- Bartow County is a northern Atlanta exurb located 25 miles northwest of I-285.
- Bartow County has a population of 112,816 according to the July 2022 census.
- Highland 75 is a 782-acre master-planned corporate/industrial park located a mile east of the I-75 Cassville-White Road exit in Cartersville.
- Highland 75 is certified under the Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) Program, which puts construction projects on a fast track.
- The Bartow-Cartersville Joint Development Authority was formed in 2004 by Cartersville and the county to split expenses and revenues between the city, the county, and the city and county school systems.
- Bartow is one of only seven sole commissioner counties in Georgia, which allows for quick decision-making that helps economic development.
- Steve Taylor has served as a commissioner for 13 years as of the article's publication.
- The Cartersville-Bartow County Chamber of Commerce hired a workforce development director to work with companies and posts information for employers on its website.
- The chamber works with Georgia Highlands College on business internships and Chattahoochee Technical College on a Project Purpose program to train high school seniors in technical skills.
- In December, Hyundai Motor Group/SK On announced a joint venture to build an electric vehicle battery manufacturing facility on 600 acres several miles west of I-75 between Cartersville and Rome.
- The Hyundai Motor Group/SK On joint venture project is valued at nearly $5 billion and will create 3,750 jobs.
- Both Hyundai Motor Group/SK On and Qcells began site work for their facilities in the spring.
- In January, Qcells announced it would build a $2.5 billion manufacturing facility on 381 acres in Highland 75, creating 2,500 jobs.
- The Qcells facility is expected to open in 2025.
- In March, Hanwha Advanced Materials Georgia, Inc. (HAGA) announced it would build a $147 million manufacturing facility creating 160 jobs in the park to supply the Qcells facility.
- The HAGA plant is projected to open next summer.
- In the last seven or eight years, about 20 million square feet of logistics/manufacturing space (Class A Industrial) has been built or planned in Adairsville, Emerson, the Cassville White corridor and Cartersville.
- Approximately 10,000 jobs have been added in Bartow County over the last 10 years.
- Melinda Lemmon is the executive director of the Cartersville-Bartow County Department of Economic Development.
- Drivers of industrial growth include proximity to Metro Atlanta, interstate access, and the nearby Appalachian Regional Port in Murray County.
- In 2019, The Foxfield Co. paid about $10.6 million for 800-plus acres next to the Anheuser-Busch plant at I-75 and Cassville White Road.
- Anheuser-Busch undertook a $150 million expansion in 2020.
- Duluth Trading Company was scheduled to open a facility in Adairsville in the summer of 2023.
- Highland 75 includes Voestalpine Automotive Body Parts Inc., headquartered in Linz, Austria, which supplies parts to Mercedes, BMW and Volkswagen.
- Highland 75 also includes Constellium Automotive Structures N.A.
