# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-25

**Source:** [Georgia Named #1 for Business for 12th Straight Year - The ATL Airport Chamber](https://airportchamber.com/georgia-named-1-for-business-for-12th-straight-year/)  ·  `page_060298`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp, in conjunction with the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD), announced that the state has been recognized as the No. 1 state for business by Area Development magazine for the 12th year in a row.
- The 2025 Top States for Doing Business rankings from Area Development are determined by scores in 15 categories, based on input from approximately 50 leading site consultants nationwide.
- Georgia ranked in the top five in 10 categories and claimed the overall No. 1 ranking.
- The Top States for Doing Business Survey is the 16th in the annual series.
- Building on this recognition, Georgia continues investing in future opportunities through the Rural Site Development Initiative, which helps local communities prepare development-ready industrial sites.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that success in economic development takes years of planning, investment, and strong partnerships to achieve and sustain success.
- Through support from the Georgia General Assembly, local leadership, and collaborations with sister state agencies, Georgia has been able to strengthen communities and prepare sites for future growth.
- GDEcD highlighted combining these efforts with world-class workforce programs and unmatched global connectivity to ensure companies have what they need to thrive.
- Lt. Governor Burt Jones noted that the announcement reflects partnership between Governor Kemp’s Office, the legislature, state agencies, and local leaders and economic developers across the state.
- Lt. Governor Burt Jones praised Georgia's workforce for continuing to excel and serve as an example of why Georgia is the best state to live, work, and raise a family.
- Speaker of the House Jon Burns stated that Georgia's designation as the No. 1 state for business for 12 consecutive years is the direct result of decades of strong leadership, forward-thinking policies, and strategic investments.
- Speaker of the House Jon Burns noted that this accomplishment represents historic job creation and economic growth in every corner of the state.
- The Georgia House remains committed to building on this record of success and ensuring that Georgia’s economy and communities continue to thrive for generations to come.
