# Hyundai Mobis

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-06

**Source:** [Hyundai Mobis Secures Significant Order from Volkswagen for Battery System Assembly](https://evmagz.com/hyundai-mobis-secures-significant-order-from-volkswagen-for-battery-system-assembly/)  ·  `page_001192`

## Facts

- Hyundai Mobis, a South Korean automotive supplier under the Hyundai Motor Group umbrella, has clinched a major contract with Volkswagen.
- The agreement involves the delivery of Battery System Assembly (BSA) for Volkswagen’s forthcoming generation of electric vehicles.
- The exact magnitude of the order has not been disclosed by the partnering companies thus far.
- Hyundai Mobis has revealed its intention to establish a dedicated manufacturing facility close to Volkswagen’s manufacturing plants in Spain later this year.
- This facility will cater to the production of battery systems, following requisite board approvals.
- The battery systems are destined for compact electric cars built upon Volkswagen’s MEB Entry platform.
- These vehicles include VW ID.2, Cupra Raval, VW ID.2 X, and a Skoda derivative, slated for production at Martorell and Pamplona plants commencing in 2025.
- Volkswagen has publicly disclosed its ambition to manufacture three million compact electric vehicles within its Spanish facilities between 2025 and 2030.
- Seat, a subsidiary of Volkswagen, is injecting three billion euros into the Martorell plant’s transformation near Barcelona to facilitate this endeavor.
- Seat has also allocated 300 million euros for battery assembly activities at the Martorell site.
- Hyundai Mobis might exclusively furnish batteries for models originating from the Pamplona plant.
- The supply of battery cells is entrusted to PowerCo’s establishment in Sagunt, near Valencia, which has been under construction since March 2023.
- The comprehensive battery systems to be delivered by Hyundai Mobis encompass the battery pack, battery management system, and thermal management components.
- The press release does not explicitly specify whether this engagement pertains solely to contract production based on Volkswagen’s specifications or if Hyundai Mobis will additionally partake in developmental services.
- Hyundai Mobis underscores the significance of this “significant order for electric vehicle components received from a prominent global automaker.”
- Hyundai Mobis boasts over a decade of experience in large-scale production of electric vehicle parts.
- Hyundai’s supplier division already operates battery system production facilities in South Korea and the Czech Republic.
- The Czech plant focuses on assembling batteries for the Hyundai Kona Electric model.
- Plans for new battery system manufacturing units in Indonesia and the United States (Georgia, catering to the extensive Hyundai Metaplant) have already been confirmed.
- An accompanying map indicates the presence of a Battery System Assembly (BSA) facility in China as well.
