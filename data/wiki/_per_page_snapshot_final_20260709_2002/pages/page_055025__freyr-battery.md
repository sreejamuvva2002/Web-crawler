# FREYR Battery

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-06-06

**Source:** [FREYR Battery Announces Leadership Changes to Support Strategic Focus on Accelerating Path to Profitability - Battery Tech Association](https://batterytechassociation.org/freyr-battery-announces-leadership-changes-to-support-strategic-focus-on-accelerating-path-to-profitability/)  ·  `page_055025`

## Facts

- Co-Founder Tom Jensen will return to the Chief Executive Officer role.
- Current Director Daniel Barcelo has been appointed Chair of the Board.
- Evan Calio, a recognized financial expert in the energy sector, has been appointed as the Company’s new CFO.
- Birger Steen stepped down from his CEO and Board positions at FREYR and is leaving the Board effective immediately.
- Oscar Brown notified the Company of his intention to resign as CFO for personal reasons.
- Both departing executives will remain with the Company temporarily to ensure seamless transitions.
- The leadership changes support FREYR’s accelerated strategic shift to the U.S. market and key initiatives to drive revenues and profitability.
- The Board believes the new management team is aligned with pursuing organic and inorganic opportunities in the U.S. to accelerate timelines to revenues and EBITDA.
- The company plans to advance its next-generation 24M SemiSolidTM technology through operations at the Customer Qualification Plant (CQP).
- FREYR intends to finalize a conventional technology agreement with an established cell producer.
- Tom Jensen stated that the company is in the final stages of finalizing an accretive partnership with a well-established battery producer globally to provide cost-competitive products to its large customer base.
- Todd Kantor, a Director and early investor who joined the Board last month, expressed confidence that the new team will translate FREYR’s technology into value-enhancing partnership transactions in the near term.
- Kantor noted that Encompass Capital Advisors LLC first invested in FREYR in 2020.
- Evan Calio has more than 30 years of energy experience on Wall Street, including working with Freyr management on its IPO and $704 million capital raise.
- Calio has led more than $60 billion in capital raises and executed over $75 billion of M&A transactions.
- Prior to his current role at BTIG, Calio spent 14 years at Morgan Stanley as a Managing Director within Equity Research covering U.S. exploration and production companies, refiners, and integrated oils.
- FREYR Battery is operating its Customer Qualification Plant (CQP) for technology development in Mo i Rana, Norway.
- The company is commencing development of the Giga America battery manufacturing project in Coweta County, Georgia, in the U.S.
- FREYR seeks to serve primary markets of energy storage systems (ESS) and commercial mobility, with an ambition to serve the passenger electric vehicles market.
- The Company’s mission is to accelerate the decarbonization of global energy and transportation systems by producing sustainable, cost-competitive batteries.
