## Facts

- SungEel HiTech is adding its first US recycling facility in Right to Work Toccoa, Georgia.
- The company is investing $37 million toward the project.
- The project will create 104 new jobs.
- According to Area Development Magazine, SungEel Recycling Park Georgia will conduct its full responsibility to build a U.S. eco-friendly industrial ecosystem in line with the expectations of the State of Georgia and Stephens County.
- Area Development Magazine states that SungEel HiTech’s entry into Georgia is the last piece of the puzzle to build a sustainable ecosystem of Georgia’s electric vehicle supply chain.
- Area Development Magazine notes that SungEel is at the cutting edge of sustainable technology and its proximity to other members of Georgia’s electric mobility ecosystem will lead to success.
- Local officials state that creating jobs of the future and protecting opportunities means preparing key industries, such as the auto industry, for the next technological revolution.
- Partners in Stephens County invested in speed-to-market solutions like site preparation to welcome SungEel HiTech.
- Officials worked with SungEel HiTech over the past eight months to learn about the company and its processes before finalizing the expansion.
- Investments in infrastructure, workforce development, and education were significant factors in SungEel HiTech's selection process to expand to Toccoa-Stephens County.
