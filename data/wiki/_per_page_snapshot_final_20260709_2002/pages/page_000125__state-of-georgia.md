# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-07-08

**Source:** [The Emerging Leader in EV Production: Georgia, not Michigan](https://www.governing.com/transportation/the-emerging-leader-in-ev-production-georgia-not-michigan)  ·  `page_000125`

## Facts

- Georgia currently leads the nation in committed investments and permanent jobs in EV and battery manufacturing, beating out Michigan and every other state.
- Since November 2021, a whopping $22 billion and nearly 24,000 new jobs in private-sector EV and battery manufacturing have been announced for Georgia.
- Blue Bird’s factory in Peach County has taken advantage of the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law’s Clean School Bus program to shift production from polluting diesel engines toward more efficient electric buses.
- Blue Bird’s Fort Valley facility currently employs 2,000 workers in a town of only 9,000 residents.
- Blue Bird has announced plans to ramp up EV bus production from two per day to 20 per day.
- Blue Bird is developing a “registered apprentice” program to train workers in partnership with local colleges, high schools and trade schools.
- Hyundai operates several EV and battery-related facilities in Georgia.
- Hyundai entered into a memorandum of understanding with Savannah Technical College to provide prospective EV industry employees with training for shop operations, electrical principles, and servicing of hybrids and EVs.
- Similar EV training programs now exist at Columbus and Augusta Technical Colleges.
- Hyundai’s $7.6 billion assembly plant in Ellabell is projected to create upward of 2,000 construction jobs and 8,500 permanent jobs.
- Hyundai’s Ellabell plant will eventually build 300,000 EVs annually.
- Rivian plans to build its second production facility in Georgia for electric SUVs and pickup trucks.
- Rivian's Georgia facility is anticipated to employ 7,500 workers by 2030.
- The infusion of EV and battery-industry funding driving Georgia's growth is supported directly by the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law and tax credits codified through the 2022 Inflation Reduction Act.
- Norwegian battery company FREYR chose to build a $2.6 billion facility near Atlanta instead of in Norway because of incentives from the IRA.
- Out of 130 options across 25 states, the Atlanta metro area was selected for FREYR due to its strong connections to air, sea and rail ports and a robust engineering workforce trained at schools like Georgia Tech.
- EV policy has become a partisan lightning rod, with Democratic leaders often claiming to be pro-EV and Republicans against.
- Georgia Republican Governor Brian Kemp recently visited the Kia plant in West Point to celebrate hundreds of new jobs accompanying production of the EV9.
- The EV9 won recognition as the North American Utility Vehicle of the Year.
- 97 percent of the nearly 24,000 EV and battery manufacturing jobs announced for Georgia since the passage of the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law are located in congressional districts represented by Republicans.
- These EV facilities are creating significant tax revenues for the state and cementing Georgia’s place as a major technology hub in the growing clean-energy economy.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated after driving the first Georgia-built EV off the production line at West Point’s Kia factory: “We are working to become the e-mobility capital of the nation.”
