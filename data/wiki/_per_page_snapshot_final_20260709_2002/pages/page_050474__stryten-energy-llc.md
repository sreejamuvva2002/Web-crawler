# Stryten Energy LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-04-01

**Source:** [Stryten Energy Expands U.S. Battery Manufacturing to 24GW](https://www.thebatterymagazine.com/stryten-energy-unveils-plan-to-add-10-gigawatts-of-new-u-s-energy-storage-manufacturing-capacity/)  ·  `page_050474`

## Facts

- Stryten Energy LLC has announced a plan to expand its domestic manufacturing capacity to 24 Gigawatts to support American energy security and resilience.
- The expansion plan focuses on increasing production across the company's 11 U.S. manufacturing and battery component plants.
- More than 2,500 people currently work at these facilities producing batteries for military and government, data centers, grid storage, automotive, and material handling applications.
- The new investment is supported by existing advanced manufacturing production tax credits, which help accelerate capacity growth while supporting the domestic industrial base.
- Mike Judd, President and Chief Executive Officer of Stryten Energy, stated that the company is committed to growing its domestic energy storage capacity to serve U.S. customers and partners.
- Judd emphasized that American energy security must rely on domestic companies manufacturing batteries to keep supply chains running and critical infrastructure supplied with backup power.
- The company is advancing energy storage in the U.S. with a suite of domestically manufactured battery solutions using advanced lead, lithium, and vanadium redox flow battery technology.
- Stryten operates production plants in Kansas, Georgia, Iowa, Virginia, and Arkansas.
- The gigawatt capacity expansion plans are expected to bring hundreds of high-paying manufacturing jobs and deliver a positive economic impact to surrounding communities.
- Stryten maintains a domestic, vertically integrated supply chain that provides battery components to its own factories and the broader U.S. battery manufacturing industry.
- Additional plants in Pennsylvania, New York, Indiana, Missouri, and Wisconsin ensure a reliable U.S. source for critical materials needed to manufacture batteries.
- The investment plan includes increasing domestic processing capacity of recycled plastic from spent batteries, which is used to produce new battery components for automobiles, forklifts, and locomotives.
- Stryten recently opened a lithium battery assembly plant in Georgia that supports military and industrial battery needs using its proprietary lithium module technology.
- The company is partnering with Reno, Nevada-headquartered Dragonfly Energy to bring advanced lithium batteries to automotive aftermarket and battery distributor partners.
- Stryten Critical E-Storage LLC, an affiliate of Stryten Energy, recently announced the launch of Storion Energy LLC in partnership with a subsidiary of Largo Inc. (NASDAQ, TSX: LGO) and Largo Clean Energy Corp. (LCE).
- Storion Energy aims to remove barriers for battery manufacturers to domestically source price-competitive electrolytes used in vanadium redox flow batteries (VRFB) for long-duration energy storage (LDES) applications in the U.S.
