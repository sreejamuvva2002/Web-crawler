# Club Car

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-04-10

**Source:** [Georgia-based Club Car to acquire Danish golf cart manufacturer](https://golfweek.usatoday.com/story/sports/golf/2022/04/10/club-car-acquire-danish-golf-car-manufacturer/76458011007/)  ·  `page_018434`

## Facts

- Club Car is a Georgia-based golf cart manufacturer.
- Club Car has signed a definitive agreement to acquire Garia A/S, which has produced luxury golf and leisure vehicles since 2005.
- The acquisition deal is expected to be finalized by the end of June.
- Financial terms of the agreement were not disclosed.
- In 2015, Garia launched its Garia Utility line of vehicles designed for work environments such as facility management and last-mile delivery.
- Last November, Garia acquired Polish lightweight utility vehicle manufacturer Melex.
- Both companies' utility products are mostly models that Club Car doesn't currently manufacture, according to a media release.
- Club Car President Mark Wagner stated that the product lines complement each other nicely with very little overlap.
- Mark Wagner called the Garia deal 'great news for our dealers and customers in Europe and the United States who will have access to a wider range of zero-emission, on and off-road vehicles at a greater variety of price points.'
- Garia is a subsidiary of the Lars Larsen Group, which owns companies in the golf car, furniture, hotel, and restaurant sectors.
- Club Car is a subsidiary of private-equity firm Platinum Equity LLC, which acquired Club Car in 2021 from Ingersoll-Rand Inc.
- The global golf car industry is projected to grow from $1.69 billion in 2021 to $2.55 billion in 2028, according to market data research firm Fortune Business Insights.
