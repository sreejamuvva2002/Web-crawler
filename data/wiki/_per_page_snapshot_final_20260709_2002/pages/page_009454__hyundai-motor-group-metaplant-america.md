## Facts

- A Statesboro man was identified as the construction worker who died after a fall at the new Hyundai Metaplant.
- An obituary by Lovett & Son's Funeral Home identifies the victim as 35-year-old Victor Javier Cajija Gamboa.
- According to the Bryan County Coroner's Office, Gamboa was killed Saturday at the construction site located at I-16 and Highway 280 after a 60-foot fall.
- The coroner's office received the call regarding the incident at 11:24 a.m.
- The fall occurred in the area designated to become the paint shop of the electric vehicle plant.
- Hyundai's new facility will be the largest economic development deal in state history.
- The facility will be the first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility in Georgia.
- Once complete, the facility will employ more than 8,000 workers.
- Hyundai Motor Group stated the death occurred when Gamboa fell from a three-story structure.
- Initial information indicates part of Gamboa's safety harness failed.
- The Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA) is investigating the workplace death.
- Hyundai stated, “Together with the contractor and the relevant authorities, we are fully supporting the investigation to understand the full circumstances of the incident to ensure this type of accident does not reoccur.”
- The Savannah Harbor Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority issued a statement on Tuesday.
- The authority offered condolences to the family, friends, and coworkers of the subcontractor who died on April 29 at the construction site for Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- The deceased employee was employed by a subcontractor on the site.
- Hyundai Motor Group, Hyundai Engineering America, and the subcontractor stated their primary concern is and will continue to be the safety and security of everyone on the construction site.
- All parties are fully cooperating with the investigation.
- Hyundai broke ground in October on its first U.S. plant devoted to manufacturing electric vehicles.
- The automaker is investing $5.5 billion in the Georgia plant.
- Georgia officials say it is the largest economic development project in the state's history.
- Hyundai plans to open the factory in 2025.
- The facility will produce up to 300,000 electric vehicles each year.
- Hyundai expressed deep sadness that a subcontractor died on April 29, 2023, as a result of injuries sustained in a workplace accident at the construction site of its Metaplant America manufacturing facility in Bryan County, Georgia.
- The company reiterated that safety and security is their highest priority and they have full commitment to following Occupational Industrial Safety and Health policies.
- Hyundai confirmed the individual fell from a three-story paint shop structure.
- Initial information indicates the individual’s safety harness lanyard failed.
- Hyundai expressed deepest condolences to the individual’s family, friends, and coworkers.
