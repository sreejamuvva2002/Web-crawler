# Ascend Elements

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2025-05-08

**Source:** [Poland Grants $320 Million to Support Ascend Elements’ Battery Plant - Battery-Tech Network](https://battery-tech.net/poland-grants-320-million-to-support-ascend-elements-battery-plant/)  ·  `page_052623`

## Facts

- The Polish Ministry of Economic Development and Technology extended a grant of up to USD $320 million (1.22 billion PLN) to Ascend Elements for a sustainable pCAM manufacturing facility in Poland.
- The subsidy is one of the largest grants ever provided by the Republic of Poland.
- The grant aligns with the European Union’s Temporary Crisis and Transition Framework (TCTF) to support Europe's shift toward a net-zero economy.
- pCAM is a crucial material in the production of lithium-ion batteries used in electric vehicles and other advanced industrial applications.
- Ascend Elements has pinpointed a location in Poland for its first advanced pCAM manufacturing plant in Europe.
- CEO Linh Austin met with Poland’s Secretary of State Michał Jaros to formalize the grant offer.
- The meeting marks a significant advancement in Ascend Elements' European expansion plans.
- Pawel Pudlowski, Ph.D., Vice President of the Management Board at PAIH, stated the project is among the largest U.S. investments in Poland in recent years.
- The project is expected to enhance Poland’s position within the global battery supply chain and promote regional development through technology advancements and collaborations with local educational institutions.
- Ascend Elements intends to commercialize its Hydro-to-Cathode® technology at the Polish facility to produce sustainable NMC pCAM from recycled lithium-ion battery materials.
- The Hydro-to-Cathode® process is more efficient and environmentally friendly than traditional methods, resulting in reduced carbon emissions and particulate matter.
- A recent life cycle assessment highlighted that the Hydro-to-Cathode® process generates 49% lower CO2e emissions and 26% lower PM 2.5 emissions.
- Ascend Elements aims to achieve further emission reductions by 2030 through additional decarbonization efforts.
- Ascend Elements is expanding its presence in the United States alongside its Polish operations.
- The company operates a major battery recycling facility in Covington, Georgia.
- A new lithium recovery line at the Covington, Georgia facility is expected to produce up to 3,000 metric tons of Lithium Carbonate annually.
- Ascend Elements is developing an advanced Hydro-to-Cathode® pCAM manufacturing facility in Hopkinsville, Kentucky.
- The Hopkinsville, Kentucky facility is projected to become North America’s largest NMC pCAM producer by late 2026.
- The Polish government grant underscores Ascend Elements’ commitment to building a sustainable and circular battery materials supply chain for European and global net-zero targets.
