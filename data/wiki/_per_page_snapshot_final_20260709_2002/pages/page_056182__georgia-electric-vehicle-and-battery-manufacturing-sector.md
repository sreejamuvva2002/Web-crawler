# Georgia Electric Vehicle and Battery Manufacturing Sector

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [In 2023, The United States Started Building Big Again, Thanks To Biden’s Inflation Reduction Act](https://www.energycentral.com/home/post/2023-united-states-started-building-big-again-thanks-biden-s-inflation-irSVy5TXLb76D8l)  ·  `page_056182`

## Facts

- The country’s clean energy economy surged thanks to President Biden’s Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) and growing demand for clean energy technologies, adding nearly $100 billion in new investment and more than 80,000 new jobs.
- The IRA was the largest-ever investment in U.S. climate, clean energy, electrification, and energy efficiency.
- Combined with the CHIPS Act, Bipartisan Infrastructure Act, and other federal actions, the IRA is supercharging the economy and jumpstarting a new clean industrial revolution through onshoring domestic manufacturing of EVs, batteries, solar panels, wind turbines, heat pumps, and other clean energy technologies.
- Since the IRA was signed into law in August 2022, 142 projects have been announced totaling $98 billion in investment dollars and more than 80,000 jobs, including nearly $60 billion and 50,000 jobs in 2023 alone.
- EV and battery manufacturing facilities make up nearly 90% of the total $98 billion invested, with 19 out of the top 20 largest investments going to batteries and EVs.
- Georgia led states in attracting investment, securing $15.4 billion.
- South Carolina secured more than 10,000 new clean energy and electrification manufacturing jobs.
- North Carolina attracted $13.7 billion in invested capital.
- Arizona, Nevada, Indiana, Illinois, Louisiana, New Mexico, and Michigan each attracted $1 billion in investment during 2023.
- Georgia now boasts four multi-billion dollar investments: Hyundai/SK ($5 billion), Freyr Battery ($2.6 billion), Hanwha Qcells ($2.5 billion), and Hyundai/LG Energy Solution ($2 billion more added this year).
- South Carolina welcomed two multi-billion investments in 2023: Scout Motors in Blythewood ($2 billion) and battery manufacturer Albemarle in Chester County ($1.3 billion).
- Toyota announced three additional investments for its North Carolina battery plant after the IRA's passage, including an additional $8 billion in October 2023 that brings the total investment to nearly $14 billion and creates over 5,000 jobs for U.S. autoworkers.
- Among all states, Georgia, South Carolina, and North Carolina rank highest in total new investment dollars at $40.7 billion combined.
- Georgia leads the way with over 13,000 new manufacturing jobs.
- South Carolina ranks third with over 10,000 jobs.
- North Carolina ranks sixth with over 4,000 jobs.
- Toyota, Hyundai, Honda, Tesla, Stellantis, General Motors, Ford, and Scout Motors have all made billion-plus dollar investments into American-made batteries and/or EV assembly since the IRA passed.
- LG Energy Solution nearly quadrupled its initial $1.4 billion investment into an Arizona battery plant and committed $5.5 billion in March 2023, which was at the time the largest investment for a single American battery manufacturing facility before being passed by Toyota’s North Carolina battery plant.
- LGES stated that its decision to increase investment in cylindrical EV battery production in North America comes from rising demand from EV makers for locally manufactured high-quality, high-performance batteries to satisfy the Inflation Reduction Act’s EV tax credits.
- Tesla announced in January 2023 that it was investing $3.6 billion into its growing Gigafactory Nevada, adding 3,000 new jobs.
- Redwood Materials committed $1.1 billion and 701 jobs to an expanded Storey County facility one month prior to Tesla's announcement.
- J.B. Straubel, co-founder and CEO at Redwood Materials, stated that the Inflation Reduction Act has really accelerated even what was already a fast process of companies locating or expanding battery production in the U.S.
- Stellantis resurrected a shuttered assembly plant in Belvidere, Illinois at the beginning of 2023, committing over $3 billion to a new battery plant and rehiring many previously laid off workers with help from the IRA, state and local leaders, and the United Auto Workers.
- Michigan added the second-most jobs since the IRA passed (10,685) and ranks in the top-eight in total investment.
- Ohio added the fourth-most jobs (5,785) and also ranks in the top-eight in total investment.
- Illinois landed the Stellantis investment and $2 billion from battery manufacturer Gotion in Manteno, which will create 2,600 jobs.
- Between the two facilities and a few smaller announced investments, Illinois will see a total of $5.25 billion in new investments and over 4,000 jobs created.
- Indiana attracted two multi-billion investments: a $3 billion joint venture from GM and Samsung SDI in New Carlisle and a $3.2 billion joint venture from Stellantis and Samsung SDI.
- Canadian Solar made an $800 million investment in October to Jeffersonville, creating 1,200 jobs.
- Indiana statewide investments from 2023 totaled up to $7.2 billion and 4,500 jobs.
- Collectively, Michigan, Ohio, Illinois, and Indiana compose 30% of all investment dollars and 25,000 of the jobs created in investments tracked since the IRA passed.
- Governor Brian Kemp (R-GA) stated he intends for Georgia to be recognized as the electric mobility capital of America.
- Governor Kemp and Senator Jon Ossoff (D-GA) have successfully recruited businesses to Georgia through bipartisan collaboration, including multiple international trips that helped land major Hyundai investments.
- These recruitment efforts are bolstered by a workforce program called Georgia Quick Start that helps support workforce training and recruitment efforts for new EV, battery, and solar manufacturing plants.
- Governor Gretchen Whitmer (D-MI) created the Office of Future Mobility and Electrification to make Michigan more attractive to investments and support the future workforce for transportation electrification.
- Governor McMaster (R-SC) signed an executive order supporting South Carolina’s aim to recruit EV business and prepare its workforce for these jobs.
- The U.S. just eclipsed one million EVs sold in 2023, a first in the country’s history, with demand continuing to grow for batteries, solar panels, wind turbines, heat pumps, and other clean technologies.
- States that adopt favorable policies, build strong workforce programs, and capitalize on new federal grant programs can tap billions in IRA-spurred investment to attract economic development opportunities, high-paying jobs, and secure the domestic clean energy economy.
