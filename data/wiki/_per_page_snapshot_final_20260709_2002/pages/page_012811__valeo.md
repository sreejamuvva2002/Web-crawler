# Valeo

## Facts

- The 2030 CO2 reduction targets are unreachable without automotive hybridization.
- Valeo's 48-Volt powertrain system architecture is a hybridization solution suited to all types of vehicles, allowing significant gains compared to a 12 Volts hybridization system and a full electrification solution for light vehicles.
- A 48V mild hybrid system is a hybrid electric vehicle technology that uses a 48-volt electrical system in addition to the conventional 12-volt system found in most cars.
- The 48V system typically includes a 48V lithium-ion battery, a belt-driven integrated starter-generator (BISG), and a DC/DC converter.
- Cars with 48V-volt technology promise weight reduction, small size that does not impact interior space, fuel efficiency reducing CO2 emissions, and affordability being less expensive than a full hybrid system.
- Reaching the voltage level to 48V maximizes the hybrid system benefits in terms of CO2 reduction and fuel economy.
- The powertrain system can be used in either 100% electric or hybrid vehicles and is on average 20% more affordable than high-voltage motors greater than 60V due to a simplified architecture that reduces safety equipment requirements.
- Valeo offers two levels of 48V hybridization: Hybrid4all and a 48V hybrid system with electric propulsion.
- Level 1 of the Hybrid4all system provides 5% fuel savings with increased comfort and power compared to a 12 Volts system.
- Level 1 includes regenerative braking that stores energy in the 48V lithium-ion battery.
- Level 1 uses torque assist where the 48V starter generator uses recovered energy to assist the internal combustion engine when accelerating.
- Level 1 supplies electrical energy stored in the 48V lithium-ion battery through a 48V converter to power vehicle accessories like car radio and windscreen wipers.
- Level 2 is a hybrid with 100% electric propulsion offering around 15% fuel savings with greater comfort and power.
- Level 2 includes Level 1 features plus an added 48V electric propulsion engine.
- Combined with a high-capacity battery, the vehicle can be driven in 100% electric mode and offers similar performance to a high-voltage hybrid system.
- Valeo's range of 48V electric motors meets different needs for existing motor hybridization ranges.
- Valeo’s 48V starter-alternator, from 4 to 6 kW, has already been sold in more than 1 million units and was developed using know-how acquired with the 12V starter-alternator.
- The 48V 15 kW electric motor is oil-cooled, can be fitted to a hybrid dual-clutch gearbox, and will go into series production in 2021.
- The 48V 25kW water-cooled electric motor can be positioned on the rear axle of the vehicle to provide an electric 4WD function and enhance driving experience.
- Valeo is producing the 48V power converter that transfers energy stored in the battery to the vehicle’s 12 Volts on-board power supply.
- Series production of the air-cooled DC/DC converter began in 2017 and meets cost-performance requirements.
- A new generation of the DC/DC converter launched in 2021 is capable of meeting all energy needs thanks to adaptable air or water cooling.
- Valeo's solutions include an additional 48 Volts lithium-ion battery, a 48V converter, a new 48V starter generator, and electric motors incorporating regenerative braking, torque assist, and electrical supply functions.
- Valeo offers the very first 48V low-voltage rechargeable vehicle resulting from its pursuit of excellence in 48V system development.
- The Four-Wheel drive electric architecture includes a 48 Volts starter-alternator and a 48V electric motor on the rear axle to significantly improve handling in harsh weather conditions such as snow.
- Combining 48V hybridization with a larger 48V battery offers an electric range of up to 80 km for plug-in hybrid solutions, guaranteeing a drastic overall reduction in CO2 emissions.
- The Valeo 48V plug-in hybrid solution enables all-electric travel for around 40 kilometers, with the thermal engine taking over for longer journeys.
- Using geolocation, the 100% electric mode is automatically activated when the vehicle enters a low emission zone.
- When a driver enters a route into the GPS, the vehicle can adapt the use of the electric drive system accordingly.
- Compared to conventional high-voltage plug-in hybrid vehicles, the Valeo 48V plug-in hybrid system is more economical with an average cost around 20% lower.
- Valeo proposes applying 48V technology initially developed for hybrid vehicles to new mobility applications such as light 2, 3, or 4 wheels.
- Valeo supplies the engine for the Citroën AMI.
- The Valeo eCityCar is a 100% electric prototype operating at a low voltage of 48V and is powered entirely by Valeo excluding batteries.
- The two-seater eCityCar can reach a maximum speed of 100km/h and has a range of 150km.
- Its small size and weight make it suitable for short journeys and city trips, and it can be recharged at any electric charging point.
- The 48V 100% electric vehicle is more economical being 20% less expensive than a current all-electric solution because its low-voltage system does not require specific protection unlike high-voltage vehicles subject to restrictive regulations and accreditations.
- The cost of a small 48V 100% electric vehicle could be around €7,500 after deduction of purchase subsidies.
- This type of vehicle equipped with Valeo’s solution gave manufacturers the impulse for the new era of low-voltage urban mobility which arrived on the streets of French cities in 2020 with the very first full electric 48V Citroën AMI.
