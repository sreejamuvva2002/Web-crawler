# Hitachi Astemo Americas Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-07

**Source:** [Hitachi Astemo Americas to expand in Berea with $153 million investment - The Lane Report | Kentucky Business & Economic News](https://www.lanereport.com/166333/2023/07/hitachi-astemo-americas-to-expand-in-berea-with-153-million-investment/)  ·  `page_008433`

## Facts

- Hitachi Astemo Americas Inc., one of Kentucky’s largest Tier 1 automotive suppliers, announced plans to expand its operation in Madison County with a $153 million investment creating 167 new, quality Kentucky jobs.
- The project will see company leaders invest in the current manufacturing operation in Berea to support site improvements and renovations, including increased production lines and additional equipment.
- The facility’s expansion will include an additional 752,000 square feet on 62 acres and respond to increased market demand and growth within the EV industry.
- Hitachi’s investment will create 167 full-time jobs for Kentuckians, bringing the company’s total Kentucky employment across the Harrodsburg and Berea facilities to over 2,100 workers.
- Tim Clark, president of Hitachi Astemo Americas, stated that as the company approaches 40 years of manufacturing in Kentucky, it is pleased to continue its commitment to growing there.
- Tim Clark described the announcement as a bold step toward vehicle electrification and noted it embodies the new company name Hitachi Astemo, which stands for Advanced Sustainable Technology for Mobility.
- In January 2021, Hitachi Automotive Systems, Keihin, Showa and Nissin Kogyo completed a business merger to form the global mega-supplier Hitachi Astemo.
- The company provides advanced mobility solutions in electric powertrains for automobiles, advanced chassis, autonomous driving/advanced driver assistance systems, and systems for motorcycles.
- Headquartered in Tokyo, Japan, Hitachi's entry into the Americas began in 1972 with a subsidiary established in Brazil.
- In the mid-1980s, Hitachi expanded into the U.S. market with its first factory in Kentucky.
- The company now serves as a global mega-supplier to some of the largest automobile manufacturers in the world and has more than 20 manufacturing sites in the United States, Canada, Mexico, and Brazil.
- The company currently operates two locations in Kentucky.
- This expansion adds to Kentucky’s thriving manufacturing sector, which has seen nearly 600 private-sector new-location or expansion projects commit to invest over $22.9 billion and create more than 30,000 jobs administration-to-date.
- In March 2023, the Kentucky Economic Development Finance Authority (KEDFA) preliminarily approved a 10-year incentive agreement with the company under the Kentucky Business Investment program.
- The performance-based agreement can provide up to $2.4 million in tax incentives based on the company’s investment of $153 million and annual targets.
- Annual targets for the incentive include the creation and maintenance of 167 Kentucky-resident, full-time jobs across 10 years.
- Annual targets also require paying an average hourly wage of $25.71, including benefits, across those jobs.
- KEDFA approved Hitachi Astemo for up to $200,000 in tax incentives through the Kentucky Enterprise Initiative Act (KEIA).
- KEIA allows approved companies to recoup Kentucky sales and use tax on construction costs, building fixtures, equipment used in research and development, and electronic processing.
- By meeting its annual targets over the agreement term, the company can be eligible to keep a portion of the new tax revenue it generates.
- The company may claim eligible incentives against its income tax liability and/or wage assessments.
- Hitachi Astemo can receive resources from Kentucky’s workforce service providers, including no-cost recruitment and job placement services, reduced-cost customized training, and job-training incentives.
