# Georgia Power

## Facts

- Georgia Power is committed to supplying households with reliable energy and supporting businesses.
- Georgia Power was recognized for a third consecutive year as a top utility in Business Facilities Magazine.
- Business Facilities Magazine is a leading publication highlighting economic development news and site selection factors and rankings from around the world.
- Georgia Power has been featured in Business Facilities Magazine's annual list of top utilities since 2021.
- Energy availability continues to be a leading factor in site selection, and Georgia Power Economic Development provides companies with information for choosing sites to locate or expand.
- Vogtle 4 entered commercial operation on April 29, 2024.
- Plant Vogtle units 3 and 4 will provide reliable, emission-free energy to customers for at least 60 to 80 years.
- With all four units now in operation, Plant Vogtle is the largest generator of clean energy in the nation.
- Plant Vogtle is expected to produce more than 30 million megawatt hours of electricity each year.
- The development supports Southern Company's goal of achieving net-zero carbon emissions by 2050.
- Southern Company has an interim target of a 50% greenhouse gas emissions reduction by 2030.
- Georgia Power has achieved a 60% reduction in carbon emissions.
- Georgia Power's first large-scale battery energy storage system, the Mossy Branch Battery Facility, announced commercial operation in October 2024.
- The Mossy Branch Battery Facility supports grid reliability and optimizes renewable energy sources like solar.
- The Mossy Branch facility can be deployed back to the grid over a four-hour period to add resiliency to the state's power grid.
- In 2023, Georgia Power supported more than 16,000 new jobs and over $9 billion in new commercial investment across Georgia's top industries.
- Walt Farrell, Vice President of Economic Development for Georgia Power, stated that the company worked in partnership with the state and local communities to support the 2023 job and investment figures.
- Many projects supported by Georgia Power align with national trends in automotive supply chain and solar manufacturing.
- GF Casting Solutions AG is investing more than $184 million in a new manufacturing facility in Augusta to produce lightweight components for the mobility and energy industries.
- The GF Casting Solutions project in Augusta will create 350 new jobs for Richmond County.
- SOLARCYCLE is investing $344 million in a solar glass manufacturing facility in Cedartown.
- The SOLARCYCLE facility in Cedartown will create more than 600 new full-time jobs in Polk County.
- The SOLARCYCLE facility will be the first-of-its-kind in the country to use recycled materials from retired solar panels to make new solar glass.
- These supported projects benefited from Georgia Power's site selection searches, engineering and research services, power delivery timeline needs, and collaboration with state and local economic development agencies.
