## Facts

- Pirelli announced its new manufacturing line for connected tires will be established in Georgia, USA.
- The decision is part of the company’s broader strategy to localize high-tech tire production to meet rising demand in North America.
- The facility is expected to produce Pirelli’s latest line of connected tires, including the Pirelli P Zero Elect and Cinturato P7 All Season.
- These tires are tailored for electric vehicles (EVs) and premium sedans.
- The tires feature embedded sensors that communicate with the vehicle’s onboard systems to provide continuous feedback for optimizing handling and energy efficiency.
- Georgia has emerged as a hub for automotive manufacturing due to its strategic location, skilled workforce, and favorable economic incentives.
- Pirelli’s choice underscores the importance of producing high-technology tires domestically to meet North American automakers’ standards.
- Automakers pushing toward electrification and smart vehicle features include Tesla, Ford, and General Motors.
- The move is expected to create dozens of jobs and stimulate local economies.
- The project faced unexpected hurdles from the United States government, particularly concerns raised by the Committee on Foreign Investment in the United States (CFIUS).
- CFIUS almost blocked the project over fears related to Chinese influence and technology transfer.
- Concerns included worries that the embedded sensors and connectivity systems could involve Chinese technology firms or be vulnerable to espionage.
- The U.S. government expressed concerns about reliance on Chinese-made components or software compromising national security.
- Broader geopolitical tensions between the U.S. and China around trade and technology heightened scrutiny on foreign investments in critical infrastructure.
- After negotiations and reassurances from Pirelli regarding intellectual property protections, the project was allowed to proceed.
- The U.S. government imposed certain restrictions and oversight measures on the project.
- The manufacturing facility remains on track to open.
- Major competitors like Michelin, Continental, and Goodyear are also investing heavily in sensor-integrated tires.
- Michelin is developing the Uptis (Unique Puncture-proof Tire System), which focuses on maintenance reduction and sustainability.
- Continental offers ContiConnect, which provides tire monitoring for commercial fleets.
- Goodyear developed the Oxygene concept, an innovative design for airless, sustainable tires with embedded sensors.
- Tesla Model S and Model 3 already support tire pressure monitoring systems (TPMS).
- The industry’s collective move toward connectivity reflects a broader push for safer, more efficient, and sustainable mobility.
- Pirelli supplies tires for Formula 1, World Superbike, and GT racing, which influences its consumer products.
- Widespread adoption of connected tires in mainstream models could take 3-5 years, depending on technological maturity, regulatory environment, and automaker integration.
