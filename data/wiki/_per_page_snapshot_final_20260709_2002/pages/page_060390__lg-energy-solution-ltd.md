# LG Energy Solution Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [LG Energy to secure $1.36 bn to raise US battery output - KED Global](https://www.kedglobal.com/batteries/newsView/ked202111260010)  ·  `page_060390`

## Facts

- LG Energy Solution Ltd. is set to secure $1.36 billion to expand US battery output capacity.
- The company is joining the fundraising race among South Korean battery makers to increase overseas production bases.
- LG Energy’s US unit decided on a rights offering of 788.1 billion won ($681 million).
- Parent company LG Chem Ltd. announced the rights offering in a filing to a South Korean financial regulator.
- LG Energy Solution Michigan Inc. plans to raise $681 million through loans.
- The company is slated to use the proceeds as investment sources to meet the increasing demand for electric vehicle batteries and energy storage systems (ESSs).
- LG Energy operates a battery plant with an annual capacity of 5 gigawatt hours (GWh) in Michigan, US.
- The company is expected to increase battery output in North America to 150 GWh.
- LG Energy plans to set up another 40 GWh-capacity factory.
- The company is building two battery plants of 35 GWh capacity each through a joint venture with General Motors Co.
- One of the GM JV battery plants will be located in Ohio.
- The other of the GM JV battery plants will be located in Tennessee.
- In October, LG Energy and Stellantis N.V. launched a 4 trillion won joint venture to build a 40-GWh capacity battery manufacturing facility in the US.
- LG Energy is set to go public on South Korea’s main bourse around the end of January 2022.
- The IPO aims to raise as much as 10 trillion won in capital.
- Competitor SK On Co. already planned to raise about 3 trillion won in a pre-IPO share sale next year to fund its massive investment plans for overseas factories.
