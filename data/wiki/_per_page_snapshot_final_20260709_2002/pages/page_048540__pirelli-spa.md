# Pirelli SpA

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2012-07-16

**Source:** [$300 Million Mexico Plant is Cornerstone of Pirelli's N.A. Growth Targets - Tire Review Magazine](https://www.tirereview.com/300-million-mexico-plant-is-cornerstone-of-pirelli-s-n-a-growth-targets/)  ·  `page_048540`

## Facts

- Pirelli SpA formally opened its new $300 million passenger and light truck/SUV tire plant in Silao, Mexico.
- The Silao plant is Pirelli’s first facility in Mexico and 22nd globally.
- The facility is part of a planned $400 million investment in tire production in Mexico.
- The plant will produce higher-end performance and light truck/SUV tires for the U.S., Canada, and Mexico at an initial rate of 400,000 annually by the end of 2012.
- Targeted capacity expansions will increase production to 3.5 million tires per year in 2015 after the first phase of development.
- The facility will reach full operational capacity of 5.5 million units annually in 2017.
- Mexico President Felipe Calderón Hinojosa attended the May 31 opening ceremony, arriving via helicopter to meet Pirelli executives and unveil a ceremonial plaque alongside Chairman and CEO Marco Tronchetti Provera.
- The plant supports Pirelli’s premium strategy by providing a shortened supply chain and increased capacity for North American customers.
- Prior to this facility, the NAFTA market was served mostly from Brazil, with partial supply from Europe and China.
- Marco Tronchetti Provera stated the new plant allows Pirelli to deliver tires anywhere in the U.S. within four days, compared to 45 days from Brazil.
- The new facility will enable Pirelli to service customers that were previously unable to be supplied.
- The initial phase consists of a 135,000-square-meter building and represents a $300 million initial investment.
- An additional $100 million will be spent on the project by 2017.
- The facility will employ approximately 1,000 workers through 2013.
- Additional hiring is planned to bring total employment to 1,800 in 2017.
- Pirelli stated the new plant will augment its existing factory in Rome, Ga., which specializes in production using MIRS technology.
- With the new plant, Pirelli’s North American production will reach 3.9 million tires in 2015.
- North American production is projected to rise to 5.9 million pieces in 2017.
- The new facility increases Pirelli’s local NAFTA production share from 6% in 2011 to 11% in 2012, and finally to 53% in 2015.
- Paolo Ferrari, recently appointed chairman and CEO of Pirelli Tire North America (PTNA), outlined near-term market share goals during the ceremony.
- Ferrari stated that premium market share is currently around 6% in the U.S. and 15% in Canada.
- As part of a three-year plan, PTNA aims to grow premium volume at least twice as fast as the overall market, potentially gaining two to three points of market share.
- Ferrari attributed this growth strategy to improved service and supply plans, upcoming launches of significant new products, and an aggressive marketing plan.
- Tom Gravalos, PTNA vice president of marketing, motorsports and original equipment, stated the company wants to set a new standard in product launches and maximize the hundreds of millions invested in the Silao plant.
- Pirelli officials planned to announce new products coinciding with Formula One's return to the U.S. at November’s F1 race in Austin, Texas.
