# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-05-20

**Source:** [Hyundai bringing $5 billion electric vehicle and battery manufacturing plant to Bryan Co.](https://fox28savannah.com/news/local/hyundai-bringing-5-billion-electric-vehicle-and-battery-manufacturing-plant-to-bryan-co)  ·  `page_000379`

## Facts

- Gov. Brian Kemp and Hyundai Motor Group announced plans to build the first electric vehicle and battery manufacturing plant in Georgia, located in Bryan County.
- Gov. Brian Kemp stated that this project represents the largest economic deal in Georgia's history.
- Hyundai Executive Chairman Euisun Chung confirmed the company will invest $5.54 billion to open the facility at the Bryan County Megasite.
- The 2,923-acre Bryan County Megasite was purchased by the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority and the state of Georgia less than a year prior to the announcement.
- The project is expected to create 8,100 new jobs in Coastal Georgia.
- Gov. Brian Kemp noted the economic benefits will extend beyond Bryan County to include Effingham, Bulloch, and Chatham counties, as well as the broader region and state.
- Pat Wilson, commissioner of Georgia’s Department of Economic Development, described Hyundai as the fastest-growing automotive company in the world based on market share.
- Wilson stated that partnering with Hyundai puts Georgia at the forefront of a significant industry transformation.
- Hyundai selected Georgia due to its existing network of subsidiaries and suppliers, as well as site resources designed to increase shipping speeds.
- Hyundai expects construction on the new facility to begin in January 2023.
- The plant is scheduled to become operational in the first half of 2025.
