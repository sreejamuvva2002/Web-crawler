# Denkai America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2022-07-28

**Source:** [Governor Kemp announced that Denkai America will locate its new manufacturing facility and North American Headquarters in Richmond County - MDMH Augusta](https://mdmh-augusta.com/governor-kemp-announced-that-denkai-america-will-locate-its-new-manufacturing-facility-and-north-american-headquarters-in-richmond-county/)  ·  `page_019288`

## Facts

- Denkai America will establish an ED copper foil production facility with an initial investment of $150 million.
- The facility will focus on electric vehicle (EV) battery foil.
- The project will be located on a 115-acre site in Richmond County.
- The company aims to invest a total of $430 million and create 250 new jobs over the next five years.
- Governor Kemp stated Georgia was selected as the home of Denkai America, strengthening the state's status in electric mobility, aerospace, defense, and technology industries.
- Governor Kemp emphasized a focus on bringing quality jobs to highly-trained Georgians in a pro-business environment.
- Denkai America’s materials have traditionally been used in printed circuit board (PCB) applications for the aerospace and defense industries.
- The company's materials are now also used in lithium-ion batteries powering electric vehicles.
- Denkai has operated for more than 60 years with team members worldwide and operations in Japan and North America.
- Heday Nakajima, Ph.D., Denkai Group President and CEO, stated the investment highlights a commitment to leading ED battery foil manufacturing in North America for many years to come.
- Nobu Masuda, Denkai America’s President and CEO, cited Augusta's skilled workforce as key to the company's growth.
- Denkai America will locate its North American Headquarters at the Augusta Corporate Park.
- Steven Kendrick, Chairman of the Augusta Economic Development Authority, welcomed the company and noted expectations for high-skilled jobs and corporate citizenship.
- The Augusta Commission and Augusta Utilities Department provided support to make the project happen.
- Barton Lowrey, Global Commerce Director of Corporate Solutions and Cyber Security at the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD), represented the state on this competitive project.
- The project was developed in partnership with the Augusta Economic Development Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- Georgia Quick Start is the Technical College System of Georgia’s workforce training program, which has provided specialized business-tailored training for more than 40 years.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated the state is committed to ensuring Georgians in the automotive industry have access to future electric mobility jobs at home.
- Wilson noted Denkai America plays a vital role in the EV ecosystem and highlighted Georgia's commitment to building lasting international relationships and recruiting the wider electric mobility value chain.
- Georgia's prime location, extensive infrastructure, skilled workforce, and business-friendly climate attracted diverse industries focused on a sustainable future.
- The state is cultivating a vertically integrated supply chain to help companies increase efficiencies by reducing reliance on imported materials.
- EV-related projects have contributed more than $12.6 billion in investments and more than 17,800 new jobs to Georgia since 2020.
- Augusta Corporate Park holds Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) certification.
- GRAD certification requires property due diligence and completion of specific requirements prior to a prospective business visit, administered by the Georgia Department of Economic Development to catalyze regional economic growth.
