# Club Car

**Type:** Company  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[vehicle-manufacturing|Vehicle Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** Augusta, Georgia, Richmond County, Georgia, United States

## Overview

Club Car is a worldwide leader in golf and utility vehicles headquartered in Augusta, Georgia. It supplied 443 vehicles for the 2025 Ryder Cup at Bethpage Black, including seven custom golf cars for captains and vice-captains.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Manufacturer of electric utility vehicles (brands Garia and Melex) based in Georgia, contributing to the state's EV fleet and event logistics.

## Timeline

- **1990** — Club Car partnered with PGA of America.
- **1991** — Club Car’s Ryder Cup partnership began at Kiawah Island's "War on the Shore."
- **2022** — Club Car became Worldwide Supplier for the Ryder Cup.
- **September 4, 2025** — Delivered 443 vehicles to Bethpage Black for the 45th Ryder Cup.
- **1997** — Club Car began its partnership with the Ryder Cup at Valderrama, Spain.
- **September 25 - October 1, 2023** — 2023 Ryder Cup held at Marco Simone Golf & Country Club in Rome, Italy; Club Car supplied a fleet of 550 vehicles and introduced solar-powered charging stations.
- **26 September 2023** — Club Car issued press release announcing solar energy charging for its Ryder Cup fleet.

## Key facts

- Club Car delivered 443 vehicles to Bethpage Black in New York for the 45th Ryder Cup.
- The fleet includes seven custom golf cars crafted exclusively for U.S. Captain Keegan Bradley, European Captain Luke Donald, and their vice-captains.
- Vehicles support captains, vice-captains, match officials, staging crews, broadcasters, greenkeeping teams, emergency response, spectator mobility, and operational logistics.
- Club Car is headquartered in Augusta, Georgia and has a history spanning over six decades.
- The Club Car product portfolio now includes electric vehicle brand Garia and lightweight electric utility vehicle brand Melex.
- Fred Palmer said: "We take great pride as a trusted partner to the Ryder Cup and PGA of America."
- Club Car introduced solar energy charging for its fleet vehicles on-site for the very first time at the Ryder Cup.
- The majority of Club Car vehicles were charged using solar-powered stations, highlighting a commitment to sustainability and carbon neutrality.
- Club Car assembled and delivered a fleet of 550 vehicles for the 2023 Ryder Cup, including team cars for captains and hundreds of support vehicles.
- Club Car is headquartered in Augusta, Georgia and has a six-decade history of designing and manufacturing electric golf, consumer, and utility vehicles.
- Club Car’s sustainability policy includes a goal to be carbon neutral by 2040.
