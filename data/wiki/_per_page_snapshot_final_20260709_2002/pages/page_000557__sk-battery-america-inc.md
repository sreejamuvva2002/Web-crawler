# SK Battery America Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-03-06

## Facts

- SK Battery America terminated 958 positions at its Commerce facility on Friday.
- The layoffs were cited due to a cooling EV market and changing manufacturer priorities.
- The Commerce plant represents a $2.6 billion investment.
- The plant previously supplied batteries for the Ford F-150 Lightning electric pickup truck.
- Ford announced plans to cancel the fully electric version of the F-150 Lightning in December.
- Impacted workers will receive severance pay through May 6.
- The final day of work for the laid-off employees was Friday.
- A Worker Adjustment and Retraining Notification (WARN) notice detailing the layoffs was filed by human resources chief Chuck Moore.
- SK Battery America opened the Commerce battery facility in January 2022.
- Federal policy shifts include the Trump administration steering support away from electrification earlier this year.
- The Trump administration has opted for more lax automotive emissions policies and a broader agenda supporting oil and gas industries.
- Congress eliminated consumer tax credits of up to $7,500 for the purchase of new or used EVs.
- The administration announced plans to weaken fuel economy and greenhouse gas emissions rules.
- These regulatory changes essentially eliminate federal incentives for auto companies to prioritize cleaner vehicle fleets.
- SK Battery America stated it remains committed to Georgia and its U.S. operations despite the layoffs.
- SK Battery America is pursuing future customers, including in the Battery Energy Storage System arena.
- Sen. Jon Ossoff blamed the current White House administration for the job losses, stating Trump's war on electric vehicles is hurting Georgia's economy.
- Sen. Raphael Warnock criticized the administration for slashing clean energy investments and imposing harmful tariff taxes that make manufacturing in Georgia more expensive.
