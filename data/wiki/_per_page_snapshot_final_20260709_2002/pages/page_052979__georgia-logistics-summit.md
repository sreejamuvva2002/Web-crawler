# Georgia Logistics Summit

**Type:** event  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2022-03-21

**Source:** [Georgia Center of Innovation Hosts 2022 Logistics Summit in Macon - The ATL Airport Chamber](https://airportchamber.com/georgia-center-of-innovation-hosts-2022-logistics-summit-in-macon/)  ·  `page_052979`

## Facts

- The Georgia Center of Innovation hosted hundreds of attendees at the March 16th Georgia Logistics Summit in Macon.
- The total sales impact of Georgia’s logistics industries jumped to $83.4 billion in 2021, equaling approximately 7% of the state’s total sales, an increase from $60.7 billion (or 6%) in 2018.
- Georgia outpaced the nation in warehousing services growth at 92.8% growth since 2018.
- In 2018, one out of every 14 Georgia jobs owed its existence to logistics; today, that number has grown to one in every nine Georgia jobs.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that long-term investments have made Georgia’s logistics networks a gateway to the entire United States, with connectivity and speed-to-market service leading to record-breaking economic investment and trade year after year.
- The Summit featured two panels: “Inside the Mind – Awakening Your Talent Search” and a keynote panel, “A Vision for Supply Chain Digitalization in 2030.”
- Panelists included business leaders from Amazon, RaceTrac, Blue Bird Corporation, and Accenture who shared experiences on how technology is shaping logistics and fulfillment operations.
- Companies are focusing more on vertical integration through leading-edge technology and automation to streamline logistics sequences through the supply chain.
- It was noted that 50% of existing repetitive tasks can be automated through existing technology.
- Amazon highlighted its electric delivery van fleet order with American startup Rivian to accommodate increasing demand for “last mile” deliveries.
- Sean Holloway, General Manager, Sav 3, at Amazon in Macon, stated that companies will become more focused on being good stewards and green technology moving into the future.
- Macon Mayor Lester Miller and Irving Consumer Products highlighted the host city of Macon and factors making it a prime community for logistics success.
- Georgia Department of Transportation Planning Director Jannine Miller provided updates on strategic investments to maintain and improve Georgia’s top-ranked interstate highway system, roads, and bridges.
- GDOT provided a briefing on strategic investments in local projects to help trucks move more efficiently and updates on long-term projects such as the I-75/I-16 Interchange in Macon.
- Center of Innovation Executive Director David Nuckolls stated that digitalization changes how business is done and the Center fills a critical need for Georgia businesses by showcasing industry best practices to make the logistics industry more competitive.
- The event’s first panel focused on educating the needed workforce, with representatives from secondary and higher education, along with the Technical College System of Georgia, discussing aligning education with industry needs.
- New career pathways in warehousing and distribution are preparing students for direct entry into the industry upon graduation, and in some cases while still in school.
- The availability and skill of Georgia’s large population of veterans was discussed, including opportunities through the VECTR Center (Georgia Veterans Education Career Resource).
- The VECTR Center provides unique, accelerated programs in high demand and strategic industries tailored to abbreviate the process of receiving post-secondary certificates and degrees by recognizing military training.
- Panelists focused on developing students’ core skills, creating future leaders, and developing partnerships with local schools and industrial and economic development authorities.
- Georgia is home to 85% of third-party logistics companies and is ranked No. 1 for infrastructure and access to global markets by Area Development.
- The state is home to the busiest and most efficient airport in the world, Hartsfield-Jackson International Airport, and the fastest-growing seaport in the U.S. at the Port of Savannah.
- Georgia offers a robust rail and highway infrastructure, with more rail miles than any other state in the Southeast at 5,000 miles of rail, and 1,200 miles of interstate highways including I-75, I-85, I-95, and I-20, plus 20,000 miles of federal and state highways.
- Many world-leading logistics companies have operations or headquarters in Georgia including Amazon, The Coca-Cola Company, Delta Air Lines, The Home Depot, Norfolk Southern, and UPS.
