# YKK Corporation of America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2022-03-17

**Source:** [YKK named Automotive Supplier of the Year in 2021 Georgia Gear Awards | Georgia](https://georgia.org/blogs/ykk-zips-title-automotive-supplier-year-200-plus-employees-2021-georgia-gear-awards)  ·  `page_004547`

## Facts

- YKK USA manufactures parts for cars, bridging zipper manufacturing with automotive supply chains.
- YKK’s Macon plant does business with almost every automobile maker in North and Central America.
- The Macon plant manufactures a range of applications for more than 100 customers.
- The company works directly with OEMs, Tier 1, and Tier 2 companies from the initial development phase to vehicle launch.
- A typical car in Georgia might include at least 15 YKK-made parts.
- YKK won the 2021 Automotive Supplier of the Year (200 or more employees) award in the GEAR Awards.
- The GEAR Awards are presented by the Georgia Department of Economic Development and voted on by leaders across Georgia’s automotive industry, representing advanced manufacturing companies, universities, and innovation centers.
- YKK products ensure cars are assembled properly and safely, with quality parts lasting the vehicle's lifetime.
- YKK products contribute to sustainability by allowing manufacturers to eliminate heavy plastic parts and thereby increase fuel efficiency.
- Automotive parts made at the Macon facility include zippers, metal, and plastic components for seat trim closure applications.
- The Macon facility produces hardware for strap hold-down applications.
- The Macon facility produces buttons for child safety car seat attachments.
- The Macon facility produces clips, rails, and extruded hook fasteners for seat trim attachments.
- The Macon facility produces truck tonneau cover hold-downs.
- YKK is AITF 16949 certified, a mandatory industry requirement that aligns automotive quality management systems throughout the world.
- Jim Reed, president of YKK Corporation of America, states that clients use YKK products to make trim, foam buns, headrests, and armrests supplied to seat assembly and OEM plants to build complete vehicles.
- YKK supplies customers with closures and fasteners to manufacture tops for Jeeps and SUVs, truck tonneau covers, commercial truck compartment curtains, battery hold-downs, car interior components, and off-road vehicle cabin covers.
- YKK designs, manufactures, leases, and services machinery for the automotive industry.
- Once a vehicle is in production, YKK provides continuous support to automotive factories, design studios, and engineering departments, monitoring product performance throughout the vehicle's life.
- YKK has been located in Georgia for 52 years.
- YKK employs around 820 Georgians in Marietta and Macon.
- YKK’s North and South America regional headquarters is located in Marietta, as is the company’s American headquarters.
- YKK’s architectural products division, YKK AP, is headquartered in Atlanta and employs approximately 800 people in Atlanta, Marietta, Dublin, and Macon.
- YKK USA’s Macon manufacturing facility is one of the company’s largest plants outside Japan.
- Former Governor Jimmy Carter convinced YKK founder Tadao Yoshida that Macon was the ideal location for YKK’s first vertically integrated production plant in the United States.
- Georgia governors have continued to support the Macon plant.
- Company leaders welcomed Governor Brian Kemp to the Macon plant to help launch Georgia Made™, a program through GDEcD that provides support in logistics, manufacturing, and marketing of Georgia products.
- YKK founder Tadao Yoshida identified Georgia’s sound water source as an important resource for manufacturing.
- The interstate systems and deep-water port of Savannah provide a perfect system for importing materials and exporting finished products for YKK.
- By air, YKK can reach any location in the continental U.S. in four hours, and by truck the farthest location is five days, with half the country accessible in two days.
- Jim Reed praises Georgia’s reliable workforce and cutting-edge high school and university systems.
- YKK has an excellent working relationship with many Georgia agencies, appreciating their responsiveness and genuine concern.
- The factors that first drew YKK to Georgia are still the factors that support its success, according to Jim Reed.
- Jim Reed believes the GEAR award will highlight awareness of YKK’s role as a supplier of vital products to the automotive industry.
- The company promoted the GEAR award externally and internally, honoring automotive-related employees at an awards ceremony and launching a social media campaign to showcase the faces behind their products.
- Jim Reed states that Georgia resources have positioned YKK to grow from a small manufacturing company to become the global leader in the fastening industry.
- Georgia is now regarded as the center of Japanese industry in the U.S. Southeast.
- YKK remains grateful to early visionaries who pursued foreign investment in the state and proud of its role in building the robust relationship between Japan and Georgia.
- Jim Reed appreciates Governor Kemp and previous governors for ensuring Georgia is business-friendly, stating Georgia is the ideal place for any company to call home.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) is the state’s sales and marketing arm and lead agency for attracting new business investment, encouraging expansion of existing industry and small businesses, locating new markets, attracting tourists, and promoting the state for arts, film, music, and digital entertainment.
