# Technology Association of Georgia / ATDC / Georgia Cyber Innovation & Training Center

**Type:** research_center  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2023-02-27

**Source:** [Growing Tech in Georgia - Georgia Trend Magazine](https://www.georgiatrend.com/2023/02/27/growing-tech-in-georgia/)  ·  `page_008250`

## Facts

- Carpool Logistics was cofounded by Michael Malakhov and Eric Morris and housed in Atlanta Tech Village (ATV), a tech hub that functions as an incubator, accelerator, and coworking space for entrepreneurs.
- ATV offers month-to-month leases requiring no upfront capital, which was a key consideration for the cofounders who had just quit their day jobs.
- David Cummings sold his marketing automation company Pardot and created ATV in 2012.
- Atlanta Ventures, the venture capital arm of ATV, invested $2 million in Carpool Logistics in 2022.
- The seed round allowed Carpool, which was just a year old at the time, to boost its customer base and staff.
- Carpool Logistics aims to optimize vehicle shipping by enabling multiple customers including consumers, dealers, carmakers, and auction houses to ship cars on one truck.
- Combining smaller shipments saves money for customers and helps lower emissions by reducing the number of partially filled trucks on the road.
- Carpool’s tech platform allows customers to track their shipments.
- Atlanta has a major stake in the supply-chain economy and hosts Cox Automotive, which owns Autotrader and Dealer.com among other brands.
- Georgia serves as a logistics hub especially for cars, with the majority of imported vehicles in the U.S. coming into the Port of Brunswick and Jacksonville.
- Automotive supply chains are moving down to the South, particularly when it comes to electric vehicles.
- Costs to launch a business and for owners and employees to live in Georgia are much lower than on the West Coast.
- In 2021, startups in Georgia raised more than $4 billion, setting a new record that beat the previous $2 billion set in 2020.
- Atlanta is the state’s epicenter for tech startups while other emerging hubs include logistics and supply chain in Savannah, fintech and robotics in Columbus, and ag tech in Macon and Middle Georgia.
- Technology Association of Georgia (TAG) president and CEO Larry Williams notes the state has a balanced portfolio of companies and industry sectors which helps overall during economic slowdowns.
- Some layoffs occurred in 2022 at fast-growing startups including highly touted unicorns valued at $1 billion or more like cybersecurity firm OneTrust and digital experience intelligence company FullStory.
- TAG president Larry Williams says founding and growing a company outside Atlanta is harder because angel and early-stage investors are concentrated there but it makes sense to go where the action is for specific sectors like ag tech.
- ATDC (Advanced Technology Development Center) was founded in 1980 as one of the oldest incubators in the country and has graduated more than 190 companies.
- Headquartered at Georgia Tech but funded by the state, ATDC serves as the state’s official incubator with staff in Athens, Augusta, Savannah, multiple spots in Metro Atlanta, Macon, Columbus, and Albany.
- Ben Andrews serves as ATDC’s statewide program manager and notes the organization wants to make it possible to create startups where people are living rather than forcing everyone into big cities.
- It makes sense for logistics companies to be in Savannah and cybersecurity startups in Augusta, with Atlanta companies often having satellite offices in other Georgia cities.
- Being close to customers can be as important as being close to investors, especially for startups that make sense to be near rural areas where their customers are.
- ATDC offers tiered resources starting with ATDC Educate for basics like customer discovery and financial literacy, followed by ATDC Accelerate for early-stage companies.
- ATDC Accelerate companies must have at least one full-time and one part-time person dedicated to the business, a minimally viable product, and may be pre-revenue seeking initial funding.
- ATDC Signature companies have business models and leadership teams in place, scalable products or services, and industry-changing technology or intellectual property.
- Carpool Logistics is an ATDC Signature company that joined because ATDC built out a new supply-chain vertical.
- Most startups at ATV are probably SaaS-platform related software-as-a-service companies that deliver applications over the web.
- There are usually between 30 and 40 Signature companies in the incubator at a time.
- Startups graduate from ATDC after hitting $1 million in annual recurring revenue from the product side for two years in a row, with a little over 90% still in business or having successful exit events five years later.
- Do Process is an HR tech company growing in South Georgia and Albany that uses video to optimize employee onboarding and training.
- Cofounder Blake Cook describes Do Process as like a learning-management system and TikTok had a baby, mapping out company processes into video-based formats for faster training and access.
- Using written employee manuals is inefficient for employers and employees especially for small businesses according to Cook.
- Do Process opened an Atlanta office at ATDC but remains Albany-based with cofounders Blake Cook and Tracy Goode being Albany natives.
- Cook notes having a tech company in Albany is an advantage because they see where the biggest needs are including people struggling to hire and train.
- Zach Hodges cofounded Cyber Security Solutions in Tampa in 2015 with two other Air Force veteran cofounders before opening a satellite office in Augusta’s Georgia Cyber Innovation & Training Center.
- The startup moved after hearing an announcement at the Augusta airport name-checking the city as the soon-to-be cyber capital of the world.
- Cyber Security Solutions grew by adding employees using a grant program that provides salary reimbursements for three to six months.
- The company partnered with the Georgia Cyber Center on the Georgia AI Manufacturing (GA-AIM) grant project designed to speed integration of AI technologies in industries across the state.
- The GA-AIM grant and project is led by the Georgia Tech Research Corporation with a number of initiatives.
- The Cyber Center’s role is to perform cybersecurity assessments for small, underserved rural manufacturing companies according to Rob Dennis executive director of corporate relations for Augusta University.
- Cyber Security Solutions leverages knowledge gained doing cybersecurity assessments for companies contracting with the U.S. Department of Defense to partner with the center.
- They will work with Augusta University and Augusta Technical College to train students to do streamlined assessments using technology that scans configurations so human questions focus on physical and personnel security.
- The Cyber Center does not house an incubator but Clubhou.se a coworking space aiming to encourage interaction among entrepreneurs has space in the center.
- Michael Shaffer executive vice president strategic partnerships and economic development for the Georgia Cyber Center says conversations are ongoing about creating an entrepreneurship center in downtown Augusta in partnership with Augusta Tech.
- The plan is to take advantage of everything from university IP to the talent pool that comes from Fort Gordon.
- Michael Shaffer states the Georgia Cyber Innovation and Training Center showed what an ecosystem can do and how important it is to build something.
- Coming off the 2021 record year, Georgia saw a decrease in venture capital investment of about 50% in 2022 with companies raising $2.25 billion.
- TAG president Larry Williams says investors are currently in implementation mode focusing on profitability rather than growth but always look for good quality deal flow.
- Providing great companies and deal flow will help Georgia’s venture capital funds grow locally and attract more venture capital from around the country to keep tech growing.
