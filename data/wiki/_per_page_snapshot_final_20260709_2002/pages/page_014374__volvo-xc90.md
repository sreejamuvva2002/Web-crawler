# Volvo XC90

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-30

**Source:** [Does The Volvo XC90 Qualify For A Tax Credit ?](https://volvoinsights.com/does-the-volvo-xc90-qualify-for-a-tax-credit/)  ·  `page_014374`

## Facts

- The federal EV/PHEV tax credit expired for vehicles purchased after September 30, 2025.
- Before expiration, the XC90 Recharge/T8 plug-in hybrid historically qualified for a partial or full federal credit of up to $7,500 in some years.
- The gas/mild-hybrid XC90 (B5/B6) never qualified for the EV credit because it is not a plug-in vehicle.
- Buyers financing a new, U.S.-assembled model may qualify for a deduction on up to $10,000 in annual car loan interest.
- The Volvo XC90 is assembled in South Carolina, which qualifies it for the new car loan interest deduction.
- Only buyers with auto loans can claim the interest deduction; leases or cash purchases do not qualify.
- The purchase must be after December 31, 2024, and the deduction phases out starting at $100,000 modified adjusted gross income for single filers or $200,000 for joint filers.
- A 2026 buying-guide analysis estimated that a buyer financing over $50,000 at 5%-plus interest could see more than $2,500 in tax savings from the interest deduction alone.
- Volvo upgraded the XC90 Recharge’s battery to 18.8 kWh with up to 35 miles of electric-only range starting with the 2023 model year.
- Most XC90s were historically assembled in Torslanda, Sweden, which complicated eligibility under the North American assembly requirement that applied to the EV credit before its expiration.
- Some states still offer their own EV/PHEV rebates independent of federal policy, though amounts and eligibility vary widely.
- States like California, New York, and Colorado have offered rebates for plug-in hybrids independent of federal policy.
- Section 179 business deduction is separate from the EV credit and is based on GVWR, not powertrain.
