# FREYR Battery

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-05-23

**Source:** [FREYR Battery Provides Operations Update - Battery Tech Association](https://batterytechassociation.org/freyr-battery-provides-operations-update/)  ·  `page_055002`

## Facts

- FREYR has completed its first production trial of unit cells using the full capabilities of the Casting and Unit Cell Assembly at the Customer Qualification Plant (“CQP”).
- With this achievement, FREYR is the first company globally to successfully demonstrate the functionality of giga scale production line equipment for the second generation of the 24M Technologies SemiSolidTM platform.
- The CQP team will now move on to producing unit cells using the full automation of the production line equipment in a continuous process.
- Following that milestone, teams at the CQP plan to proceed to manufacturing multi-layer pouch battery cells in Q2 2024 for customers while continuously improving the electrochemical and electromechanical performance of the technology platform.
- FREYR teams at the CQP will continue to fine tune processes before commencing continuous operation to harvest production data and product quality evaluations at scale through powder preparation, slurry mixing, electrode casting, unit cell merge, stacking, pouch singulation, charging, formation and aging.
- The operational update covers progress at the CQP in Mo i Rana, Norway.
- Teams, vendors, and partners at the CQP have reached the company’s most significant technical milestone to date by producing unit cells with the full functionality of the Casting and Unit Cell Assembly machine, the novel component of the SemiSolid™ platform.
- While significant work remains to further streamline automation, improve material composition, and complete the final stages of commissioning, this accomplishment marks the completion of critical path equipment commissioning.
- The milestone is expected to enable FREYR to begin increasing production speeds and manufacturing multi-layer battery sample cells in Q2 2024.
- Mike Brose serves as FREYR’s Head of the Asset Mo team.
- Mike Brose commented that demonstrating the ability to manufacture cells with the next-generation Casting and Unit Cell Assembly positions the team to move into trial production using a continuous process, perform data and performance analysis, surge production speeds, and proceed to manufacturing mechanically complete sample cells in Q2.
- Adam Holland serves as CEO of Mpac Group plc.
- Adam Holland commented on behalf of the Mpac team that Mpac is proud to contribute to FREYR’s mission to create a sustainable future with next-generation battery technology.
- The production trial was enabled by the successful integration of casting webs across the cathode, anode, and merge units of the Casting and Unit Assembly equipment using the highly advanced Multi-Carrier System (“MCS”).
- The MCS is the largest of its kind globally according to FREYR’s automation partner Siemens AG.
- Brian Holliday serves as Managing Director at Siemens Digital Industries.
- Brian Holliday commented that the achievement was an amazing team effort between FREYR, Mpac, Festo and Siemens working together at the leading edge to highly automate battery production, marking a huge step forward for next generation technology designed to enhance industry sustainability.
- Following the trial, FREYR is following a three-step road map to producing sample cells in Q2 2024.
- The first step involves conducting a cell production trial using a continuous process with no interruptions to fine tune the equipment.
- The second step involves surging production speeds for short periods of time to demonstrate GWh scale manufacturing capability for the casting of electrodes.
- The third step involves producing multi-layer, mechanically in-spec pouch battery sample cells in Q2 2024.
- Birger Steen serves as FREYR’s Chief Executive Officer.
- Birger Steen commented that proving the ability to navigate electromechanical challenges of producing batteries on novel manufacturing equipment demonstrates FREYR’s technical and operational qualifications as an emerging industrialization partner, and the team is moving to replicate what was just done in a continuous process before commencing production of multi-layer pouch battery sample cells.
- Conventional lithium-ion battery technology is estimated to account virtually all current global commercial production, with only 6% originating from the U.S., according to a recent International Energy Agency report.
- The development of novel, U.S.-based technologies such as the SemiSolidTM platform has the potential to enhance regional energy security and long-term competitiveness of U.S. battery producers.
- In conjunction with the milestone, FREYR is evaluating additional opportunities to leverage the CQP to incubate additional technological advances in cooperation with key stakeholders in the U.S.
- Tom Einar Jensen serves as Co-founder and Executive Chair of FREYR.
- Tom Einar Jensen commented that FREYR has demonstrated the ability of its teams and global partners to scale a novel U.S. IP centric battery technology platform, noting that the work is just beginning despite representing a proud moment for the company.
- The cost of imported battery solutions to target markets is highly dynamic, so the development of U.S. origin technologies at scale is critical if the U.S. is to establish a viable competitive position in the global battery ecosystem.
- Currently, 95% of battery solutions for the energy storage market are imported from Asia.
- With the energy transition accelerating in the U.S., homegrown supply of critical energy infrastructure is a fundamental pillar to regional energy security as well as job creation and sustained economic growth.
- FREYR’s aspiration is that the CQP, as a first of its kind facility based on U.S. Intellectual Property, could ultimately serve a unique role as a center of innovation and scaling platform for competitive battery solutions and the backbone of a new, increasingly localized energy system.
- FREYR Battery is a developer of clean, next-generation battery cell production capacity with a mission to accelerate the decarbonization of global energy and transportation systems by producing sustainable, cost-competitive batteries.
- FREYR seeks to serve the primary markets of energy storage systems (ESS) and commercial mobility, and maintains an ambition to serve the passenger electric vehicles market (EV).
- FREYR is operating its Customer Qualification Plant (CQP) for technology development in Mo i Rana, Norway.
- FREYR is commencing development of the Giga America battery manufacturing project in Coweta County, Georgia, in the U.S.
