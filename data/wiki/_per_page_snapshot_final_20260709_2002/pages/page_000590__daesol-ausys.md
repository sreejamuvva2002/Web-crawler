# Daesol Ausys

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [South Korean auto supplier plans $72 million plant in Georgia to build electric vehicle parts | AP News](https://apnews.com/article/daesol-ausys-auto-parts-hyundai-south-korea-19bcd1a4e2afefc16c571c777b3d6873)  ·  `page_000590`

## Facts

- A South Korean company plans to build a $72 million factory in Georgia to make parts for electric vehicles, hiring more than 140 workers.
- Daesol Ausys announced its plans on Tuesday to build a factory in West Point, making luggage boards and covers for compartments in electric vehicles.
- The company supplies parts to Hyundai, Kia and General Motors.
- It will be the third factory in the same West Point industrial park for members of the company’s corporate family.
- Daehan Solution Co. opened a million factory there in 2008 to make headliners and other sound insulation parts for the nearby Kia plant.
- Daesol Material opened a $35 million factory there in 2017 to make automotive fiber padding.
- Daehan Solution today employs 350 full-time workers, while Daesol Material employs 50 workers, said Meghan Richardson, an economic developer for the city of West Point.
- Daehan Solution also has a plant near Montgomery, Alabama, supplying the Hyundai assembly plant there.
- The project is another in a wave of electric vehicle suppliers announcing plants in Georgia after Hyundai said in 2022 that it would build a $5.5 billion plant to assemble electric vehicles and batteries in Ellabell, Georgia, near Savannah.
- The Hyundai site could grow to 8,100 employees and is slated to begin producing vehicles in 2025.
- Suppliers have pledged to invest nearly $2.2 billion and to hire 5,000 people.
- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp said in a statement: “Our state’s automotive industry employs over 50,000 hardworking Georgians and will continue to grow as e-mobility suppliers choose all corners of the state for the jobs of the future.”
- Daesol Ausys is scheduled to begin production in December 2024.
- The company would pay $15 to $20 an hour, said Meghan Richardson.
- The state will pay to train workers for the facility.
- The state gave $500,000 last year to help Harris County clear and grade the site and build a road, funds the county matched using sales tax collections.
- The city of West Point and the county are granting eight years of property tax breaks, worth a projected $5.7 million.
- Daesol Ausys could qualify for $875,000 in state income tax credits, at $1,250 per job over five years, as long as workers make at least $31,300 a year.
