# Georgia Institute of Technology

**Type:** research_center  ·  **Role:** research_and_development  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Georgia Tech and Stryten Energy Unveil Installation of Lead Battery Energy Storage System for Advanced Research](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20250423704154/en/Georgia-Tech-and-Stryten-Energy-Unveil-Installation-of-Lead-Battery-Energy-Storage-System-for-Advanced-Research)  ·  `page_029423`

## Facts

- Georgia Tech and Stryten Energy LLC announced the successful installation of Stryten Energy’s Lead Battery Energy Storage System (BESS) at the Carbon Neutral Energy Solutions Laboratory (CNES).
- The CNES building is located in the North Avenue Research Area of the Georgia Tech campus.
- The CNES houses the Strategic Energy Institute (SEI), an interdisciplinary research institute focused on energy research.
- SEI hosts multiple research groups dedicated to renewable energy and energy infrastructure-related topics.
- The BESS installation aims to create a living-learning lab on campus that supports research and real-world applications of medium-duration energy storage solutions.
- Lead BESS was selected for this initial installation due to its cost-effectiveness, high discharge rates, and recyclability.
- Extensive research demonstrates the reliable performance of lead BESS.
- The BESS integrates renewable energy sources into the existing power mix, providing stable backup power and reducing grid dependency during peak hours.
- The system includes additional components and software capable of bi-directional charging, allowing current to flow into the battery for charging and out to power the grid or microgrid.
- Christine Conwell serves as SEI’s interim executive director.
- Georgia Tech's strategic plan envisions its campus as a dynamic laboratory and experimental test bed where sustainable practices are seamlessly integrated into operations.
- Partnerships with organizations like Stryten create mini ecosystems that yield valuable situational data to help chart a path for innovative energy research.
- Scott Childers is vice president of essential power at Stryten Energy.
- The BESS deployment targets commercial and industrial microgrids paired with EV charging stations to help achieve U.S. energy goals.
- The installation demonstrates the advantages of lead BESS from cost savings, technology, environmental, and safety perspectives.
- Richard Simmons serves as SEI’s director of research and studies.
- Simmons called the Stryten lead BESS system an enabling piece of the Distributed Energy Resources (DER) puzzle.
- At the CNES lab, Georgia Tech researchers can control charging and discharging cycles for the battery in coordination with the existing Solar PV array and a new EV charging test bed.
- This research tool allows the time-shifting of peak solar input by several hours to meet late afternoon building loads.
- The system stores renewable energy for the overnight charging of campus vehicles.
- Research focuses on DERs' impact on the grid, their contribution to system reliability, and their effect on energy costs during the transition to clean energy.
- Simmons hopes the lead BESS will be one of several living lab battery pilots at Georgia Tech.
- Along with regional partners, researchers are exploring similar R&D and testing projects involving flow batteries for longer-duration storage.
- Researchers are also exploring lithium-ion BESS that may integrate second-life EV battery modules for grid resilience.
- Georgia Tech is one of the top public research universities in the U.S., developing leaders who advance technology and improve the human condition.
- Georgia Tech researchers work across the energy value chain in basic and applied science in EVs, photovoltaics, hydrogen, carbon capture, industrial decarbonization, grid security and resilience, and related social sciences.
- Georgia Tech is consistently ranked among the top universities in the nation for graduating underrepresented minorities in engineering, physical sciences, and energy-related fields.
- U.S. News & World Report ranked Georgia Tech as the No. 1 public university and No. 3 overall in energy and fuels research.
- Stryten Energy is headquartered in Alpharetta, Georgia.
- Stryten's stored energy technologies include advanced lead, lithium, and vanadium redox flow batteries, intelligent chargers, and energy performance management software.
