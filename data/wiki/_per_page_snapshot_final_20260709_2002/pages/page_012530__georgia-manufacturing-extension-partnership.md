# Georgia Manufacturing Extension Partnership

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2025-07

**Source:** [Georgia Manufacturing Extension Partnership at Georgia Tech Celebrates 65 Years of Service - Tifton CEO](https://tiftonceo.com/features/2025/07/georgia-manufacturing-extension-partnership-georgia-tech-celebrates-65-years-service/)  ·  `page_012530`

## Facts

- The Georgia Manufacturing Extension Partnership (GaMEP), a program of the Enterprise Innovation Institute at Georgia Tech, received recognition from Gov. Brian P. Kemp for 65 years of service to the manufacturing industry at the Georgia State Capitol on Monday, July 14th, 2025.
- The commendation acknowledges GaMEP's commitment to supporting manufacturers across the state through educational opportunities and technical assistance.
- GaMEP supports an industry that adds $82 billion to the economy and employs 425,000 residents, according to the National Associations of Manufacturers.
- By 1960, more than 4,500 manufacturers had planted roots across Georgia due to strategic efforts by state leaders with economic development assistance from Georgia Tech.
- The Georgia General Assembly voted to establish the Georgia Tech Industrial Extension Service (now known as GaMEP) to provide statewide field offices delivering on-site technical expertise to help manufacturers stay competitive.
- Tim Israel, GaMEP director and EI2 associate vice president — corporate engagement/firm-based programs, stated that in 2024, Georgia experienced a significant return on investment with GaMEP generating $294 for every state dollar allocated to manufacturing projects.
- Over the past decade, GaMEP has provided assistance and education to more than 3,900 manufacturers across 144 counties.
- During that same decade, GaMEP helped manufacturers create or retain 14,500 jobs.
- Over the past decade, GaMEP helped manufacturers invest $1 billion in capital improvements.
- Over the past decade, GaMEP helped manufacturers realize $3.5 billion in sales.
- Over the past decade, GaMEP helped manufacturers save nearly $450 million in costs.
- GaMEP primarily serves small- to medium-sized manufacturers, with 75% employing less than 250 workers.
- GaMEP's top-served industries include fabricated metal product, food, machinery, chemical, and transportation equipment manufacturing.
- David Bridges, EI2 vice president, stated that when Georgia manufacturers become more productive and profitable, they hire more people, pay better wages, and stabilize local economies, especially in rural and underserved areas of the state.
- David Bridges also noted that GaMEP's work creates pathways for career advancement for frontline workers who might not have had previous opportunities.
- GaMEP's reach spans 10 regions across the state, each led by a dedicated region manager who lives and works locally to offer manufacturers direct connections to expert teams and resources.
- Lloyd Avram, Georgia Association of Manufacturers CEO and president, called GaMEP a trusted collaborator and valued partner in strengthening manufacturing across Georgia.
- In the Coastal Region (Bryan, Bulloch, Camden, Chatham, Effingham, Glynn, Liberty, Long, McIntosh and Screven counties), GaMEP served 269 manufacturers, created/retained 283 jobs, facilitated $23,171,292 in capital improvements, saved $1,645,061 in costs, and realized $13,965,000 in sales, with Roger Wood Foods as a success story.
- In the East Region (Burke, Columbia, Glascock, Hancock, Jefferson, Jenkins, Lincoln, McDuffie, Richmond, Taliaferro, Warren, Washington and Wilkes counties), GaMEP served 169 manufacturers, created/retained 3,899 jobs, facilitated $127,754,280 in capital improvements, saved $14,771,582 in costs, and realized $975,465,000 in sales, with Nutritional Resources as a success story.
- In the East Central Region (Barrow, Clarke, Elbert, Greene, Jackson, Jasper, Madison, Morgan, Newton, Oconee, Oglethorpe and Walton counties), GaMEP served 209 manufacturers, created/retained 621 jobs, facilitated $19,703,035 in capital improvements, saved $2,535,494 in costs, and realized $29,486,000 in sales, with Creature Comforts Brewing Company and Poly Tech Industries as success stories.
- In the Metro Atlanta Region (Cherokee, Clayton, Cobb, DeKalb, Douglas, Fayette, Fulton, Gwinnett, Henry and Rockdale counties), GaMEP served 1,601 manufacturers, created/retained 2,928 jobs, facilitated $235,763,480 in capital improvements, saved $112,083,262 in costs, and realized $844,679,890 in sales, with Compass Technology Group, Construction Specialties, and Highland Forge as success stories.
- In the Middle Region (Baldwin, Bibb, Crawford, Houston, Jones, Monroe, Peach, Pulaski, Putnam, Twiggs and Wilkinson counties), GaMEP served 170 manufacturers, created/retained 972 jobs, facilitated $121,814,846 in capital improvements, saved $8,810,950 in costs, and realized $300,213,400 in sales, with Unified Defense as a success story.
- In the Northeast Region (Banks, Dawson, Forsyth, Franklin, Habersham, Hall, Hart, Lumpkin, Rabun, Stephens, Towns, Union and White counties), GaMEP served 280 manufacturers, created/retained 1,029 jobs, facilitated $88,443,395 in capital improvements, saved $63,999,228 in costs, and realized $259,453,900 in sales, with Freudenberg Sealing Technologies as a success story.
- In the Northwest Region (Bartow, Catoosa, Chattooga, Dade, Fannin, Floyd, Gilmer, Gordon, Haralson, Murray, Paulding, Pickens, Polk, Walker and Whitfield counties), GaMEP served 387 manufacturers, created/retained 1,090 jobs, facilitated $92,948,931 in capital improvements, saved $141,460,651 in costs, and realized $326,366,408 in sales, with Trenton Pressing as a success story.
- In the South Region (Appling, Bleckley, Candler, Dodge, Emanuel, Evans, Jeff Davis, Johnson, Laurens, Montgomery, Tattnall, Telfair, Toombs, Treutlen, Wayne, Wheeler and Wilcox counties), GaMEP served 176 manufacturers, created/retained 969 jobs, facilitated $219,300,221 in capital improvements, saved $6,596,254 in costs, and realized $39,632,275 in sales, with Healthy Pet as a success story.
- In the Southeast Region (Atkinson, Bacon, Ben Hill, Berrien, Brantley, Brooks, Charlton, Clinch, Coffee, Cook, Echols, Irwin, Lanier, Lowndes, Pierce, Tift, Turner and Ware counties), GaMEP served 166 manufacturers, created/retained 281 jobs, facilitated $20,684,800 in capital improvements, saved $3,098,700 in costs, and realized $77,724,500 in sales, with CJB Industries and South Georgia Pecan as success stories.
- In the Southwest Region (Baker, Calhoun, Colquitt, Decatur, Dougherty, Early, Grady, Lee, Miller, Mitchell, Seminole, Terrell, Thomas and Worth counties), GaMEP served 130 manufacturers, created/retained 130 jobs, facilitated $12,266,730 in capital improvements, saved $1,682,790 in costs, and realized $5,002,020 in sales, with Sweet Grass Dairy as a success story.
- In the West Region (Chattahoochee, Clay, Crisp, Dooly, Harris, Macon, Marion, Muscogee, Quitman, Randolph, Schley, Stewart, Sumter, Talbot, Taylor and Webster counties), GaMEP served 111 manufacturers, created/retained 713 jobs, facilitated $37,948,131 in capital improvements, saved $3,197,600 in costs, and realized $69,588,348 in sales, with Oneda Corporation as a success story.
- In the West Central Region (Butts, Carroll, Coweta, Heard, Lamar, Meriwether, Pike, Spalding, Troup and Upson counties), GaMEP served 234 manufacturers, created/retained 1,658 jobs, facilitated $90,750,763 in capital improvements, saved $89,931,074 in costs, and realized $615,900,002 in sales, with Complete Truck Bodies and Mountville Mills as success stories.
