# Anthro Energy and EnPower Inc.

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-05-14

**Source:** [Anthro Energy partners with EnPower to develop lithium-ion cells in the U.S. - The Robot Report](https://www.therobotreport.com/anthro-energy-partners-enpower-develop-lithium-ion-cells-u-s/)  ·  `page_008942`

## Facts

- Anthro Energy and EnPower Inc. signed a memorandum of understanding to establish a long-term, multi-phase partnership.
- The companies plan to jointly develop, manufacture, and scale high-performance lithium-ion cells for robotics applications.
- EnPower will combine its U.S.-based electrode and pouch cell manufacturing capabilities with Anthro Energy’s Proteus electrolyte platform.
- The partnership aims to support longer mission times, safer system designs, and operation in more demanding environments.
- David Mackanic, co-founder and CEO of Anthro Energy, stated the partnership is about reindustrializing American manufacturing and rebuilding a domestic battery supply chain for the next generation of energy storage.
- The partnership integrates a paradigm-shifting battery platform with U.S. manufacturing capabilities to accelerate commercialization and deliver high-performance energy solutions for defense, robotics, and autonomous systems.
- EnPower and Anthro Energy plan to produce the batteries in the U.S.
- Alameda, Calif.-based Anthro Energy develops advanced polymer electrolytes to enhance lithium-ion battery performance and safety.
- Indianapolis-based EnPower is a developer of lithium-ion battery cell design, integration, and manufacturing.
- The partners plan to create a fully domestic pathway from advanced electrolyte chemistry through finished cell production.
- A vertically integrated U.S. cell stack will address growing concern over foreign dominance of the lithium-ion supply chain.
- The collaboration aims to support the reindustrialization of American battery manufacturing for defense and dual-use applications.
- The companies anticipate scaling production capacity beyond 750 MWh.
- This capacity would make it one of the largest U.S.-based advanced lithium-ion cell platforms outside of automotive supply chains.
- According to a 2026 industry report, the artificial intelligence and robotics market in aerospace and defense is projected to grow from $26.9 billion in 2025 to $29.7 billion in 2026.
- This reflects a 10.5% annual growth rate driven by increased adoption of autonomous drones, robotics, and AI-enabled defense systems.
- Each platform depends on high-performance, domestically manufactured batteries, a category currently underserved by the U.S. supply base.
- Anthro Energy and EnPower will collaborate across multiple phases to deliver high-performance and high-power cells.
- A separate agreement will define product specifications, performance targets, and commercial timelines.
- The partners are developing high-performance pouch cell designs for mission-critical applications.
- Cells will exceed 350 watt-hours per kilogram (Wh/kg) for high-energy applications while maintaining industry-leading safety.
- EnPower and Anthro Energy will achieve this by combining synergistic electrolyte and separator technologies.
- The companies will develop high-rate or ultra-high rate capability for applications needing lift (drones) or ride-through power (AI data centers).
- Target applications include defense, unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs), unmanned underwater vehicles (UUVs), drones, robotics, embodied AI systems, and public safety equipment.
- Adrian Yao, founder and CEO of EnPower, stated EnPower is strategically positioned to serve as an integrator of domestic battery technologies and a vector for rapid commercialization.
- Anthro Energy recently announced plans to establish a large-scale, U.S.-owned and operated advanced electrolyte manufacturing facility in Louisville, Ky.
- The Louisville facility will be just two hours away from EnPower’s 92,000 sq. ft. (8,547 sq. m) Indianapolis facility.
