# Inoac Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Inoac Corporation - MarkLines Automotive Industry Portal](https://www.marklines.com/en/top500/inoac?&sitesearchKey=philippines)  ·  `page_015288`

## Facts

- Inoac Corporation develops urethane, rubber, plastics, and composite materials.
- The company delivers automotive interior and exterior decorative parts, industrial materials for interior products, cosmetic containers, and housing materials.
- The company has established joint ventures with Woodbridge Foam based in Canada, and a Chinese supplier Ningbo Huaxiang Electronic Company.
- Inoac Corporation is an unlisted company.
- Products include interior parts such as instrument panels, energy absorption parts, engine covers, molded ceilings, air-intake ducts, and pillar garnishes.
- Seat parts produced include armrests, seat cushions, and head rests.
- Molding parts include window moldings, roof moldings, aero spats, side mudguards, mudguards, and rear spoilers.
- Functional parts include air intake ducts/resonators, sound deadening parts for resonator, steering column hole covers, hood seals/door weatherstrip, sealing, door cushions, plug holes/packing, and Cellasto (Suspention).
- The company provides material solutions for EV battery.
- In 1926, the company established the Inoue Gomu Seizousyo to produce bicycle tire tubes.
- In 1934, it started overseas operations by exporting the IRC Brand tire tube to Southeast Asia.
- In 1938, it incorporated Inoue Rubber Co., Ltd.
- In 1954, it established MTP Kasei, becoming the first company in Japan to manufacture and sell polyurethane foam.
- In 1955, it adopted ceiling materials and heat insulating materials for engine compartments.
- In 1960, it started to produce vacuum formed polyvinyl chloride sheet products.
- In 1968, it adopted plastic molds for automobiles.
- In 1979, it started to produce plastic bumpers (PP).
- In 1980, it established Inoue MTP Co. Ltd. through a merger of Inoue Rubber and MTP Kasei, launched the "INOAC" brand, and established Inoue Cellotechs as a joint venture with Jim Walter Inc.
- In 1984, it adopted instrument panels using powder slash molds.
- In 1987, it started to produce blow molded spoilers.
- In 1988, it started to produce Beneron sheets domestically by entering into a technical partnership with Benecke-Kaiko Group.
- In June 1990, the company changed its name to Inoac Corp.
- In 1994, it started to produce instrument panels using HPI Technology.
- In 1995, it established Kansai Inoac and Chita Inoac.
- In 1996, it started to produce instrument panels integrated with airbag doors.
- In 1997, Seino Inoac was renamed Inoac Elastomer Co., Ltd.
- In 1998, it established IW foam corporation, a joint venture company with Woodbridge of Canada, to manufacture automobile cushions.
- In 1999, it established BASF Inoac polyurethane.
- In 2000, it established SAI Automotive SAI Japan KK, a joint venture company, with Faurecia of France.
- In 2001, it established a joint venture company with RECTiCEL S.A. of Belgium to manufacture automobile interior parts, and established Inoac Tokai (Thailand) Co., Ltd. to produce medium-pressure and low pressure hoses in Thailand.
- In 2004, it established Tianjin Inoac Polymer Co., Ltd. in China to manufacture and sell automotive components, and established Inoac Automotive (Thailand) Co., Ltd. in Thailand to manufacture automotive resin products.
- In 2005, it established the Toyohashi Plant in Aichi Prefecture as an integrated production base for headrests with a seamless production structure including cutting of headrest cover through finishing.
- In 2006, it established Dongguan Inoac Polymer Co., Ltd. in China to manufacture high-performance urethane foam, and established Inoac (Thailand) Co., Ltd. in Thailand to supervise the Group's affiliated companies in Asia.
- In Nov. 2007, the company and Woodbridge Foam of Canada signed a joint-venture contract to collaborate in the automotive seats urethane foam business, with Woodbridge Foam acquiring 50 percent of the share capital in Kyusyu Inoac Co., Ltd., while the company obtained 50 percent of the share capital in Saltillo Interior Trim S.A. de C.V.
- In Nov. 2007, it established the Kitakami Plant in Iwate Prefecture as an integrated production base for automotive interior and exterior components.
- In 2008, it established Saltillo Interior Trim S.A. de C.V. in Mexico to manufacture foam products.
- In 2009, it established Tianjin Inoac Huaxiang Automobile Produces in Tianjin, China.
- In 2011, it acquired 100% of the shares in Inoac Exterior Products LLC based in North America to enhance its automotive spoiler business.
- In 2012, it established ABC Inoac Exterior Systems in USA and Canada to produce automotive exterior components.
- In 2013, it started series production at Inoac Polymer Lanka (PUT) Ltd. in Sri Lanka, initially producing urethane form for the apparel business in India.
- In 2014, it established the general foundation named the "Polyurethane International Technology Promotion Foundation".
- In 2015, it started volume production on a new foaming line at INOAC (THAILAND) CO., LTD.
- In 2016, it started volume production on foaming lines at INOAC VIETNEM CO., LTD. and PT. INOAC POLYTECHNO INDONESIA, and established INOAC LIVING VIETNAM CO., LTD.
- In 2017, it established Inoac Ryukyu Co., Ltd. as the group's first company in Okinawa prefecture.
- In 2021, it built a new production plant at PT Inoac Polytechno Indonesia.
