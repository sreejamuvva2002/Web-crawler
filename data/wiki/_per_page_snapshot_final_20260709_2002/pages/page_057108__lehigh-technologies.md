# Lehigh Technologies

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2022-12-08

**Source:** [Lehigh Technologies helps turn Georgia’s Lawrenceville Highway into Easy Street :: Michelin North America, Inc.](https://michelinmedia.com/lehigh-helps-turn-georgia-lawrenceville-highway-into-easy-street/)  ·  `page_057108`

## Facts

- Lehigh Technologies is an Atlanta-based subsidiary of Michelin North America, Inc.
- Lehigh’s Rubber Modified Asphalt (RMA), made with recycled tire rubber powder, is being used to resurface 6.3 miles of Lawrenceville Highway between Tucker and Lilburn, Georgia.
- The paving project began in September and was in its final stages as of the article's publication date.
- RMA is produced using Lehigh’s MicroDyneTM rubber powder plus proprietary additive RheopaveTM.
- This product blend has been used to produce more than 1,500 miles of road in recent years.
- Kara Fulcher, Director of Sustainability and Government Affairs for Michelin North America, Inc., states that RMA uses end-of-life tires already available in the North American market.
- The recycled material replaces a polymer derived from the oil-refining process.
- RMA serves as a locally sourced replacement for an ingredient generally imported from Asia, providing a resilient response to a long supply chain.
- Kara Fulcher states that Michelin and Lehigh are committed to taking end-of-life tires and giving them new life in other products through innovation.
- RMA can modify liquid bitumen at an asphalt terminal or be directly added at an asphalt contractor’s site via the “Dry Process,” which was used for this project.
- Jason Stravinski, President and CEO of Lehigh Technologies, states that Rubber Modified Asphalt produced via the Dry Process provides a paving solution equally effective as oil-derived polymer modified asphalt, yet cheaper and more environmentally friendly.
- The repaved route covers 6.3 miles of Lawrenceville Highway (State Road 8) from the DeKalb County line to Ronald Reagan Parkway.
- Lehigh’s headquarters is located in Tucker, Georgia, a suburb of Atlanta.
- In addition to road surfacing, Lehigh’s micronized rubber powders are used in new tires, as well as a variety of rubber goods, roofing, plastics, industrial coatings, and automotive components.
- Lehigh Technologies uses cryogenic turbo mill technology to convert end-of-life tires and other post-industrial rubber into micron-scale powders for consumer and industrial applications.
- Lehigh's customers include more than 60 leading tire, asphalt, and plastics companies around the world.
