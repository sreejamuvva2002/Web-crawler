# Generation III+ SMR Pathway to Deployment Program

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [Energy Department Awards $94 Million to American Companies to Help Expedite the Deployments of Small Modular Reactors in the United States | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/articles/energy-department-awards-94-million-american-companies-help-expedite-deployments-small)  ·  `page_003286`

## Facts

- U.S. Department of Energy announced the selection of eight companies to support the near-term deployment of advanced light-water small modular reactors in the United States on May 14, 2026.
- Awardees will collectively receive more than $94 million in Federal cost-shared funding.
- Funding aims to spur additional Gen III+ SMR deployments by addressing key gaps that have hindered the domestic nuclear industry in licensing, supply chain, and site preparation.
- Projects will bolster the supply chain needed to deliver new nuclear generation in the 2030s.
- Projects aim to strengthen the development of Gen III+ SMR orderbooks.
- Awards advance President Trump’s Executive Orders to usher in a nuclear renaissance and expand America’s Energy Dominance agenda.
- U.S. Secretary of Energy Chris Wright stated that President Trump has made clear America is going to build more energy, not less, and nuclear is central to that mission.
- Wright noted advanced light-water SMRs will provide reliable, round-the-clock power to fuel the President’s manufacturing boom, support data centers and AI growth, and reinforce a stronger, more secure electric grid.
- Awards are administered under the Generation III+ SMR Pathway to Deployment Program.
- Constellation SMR Development, LLC received $17,264,292 to pursue a U.S. Nuclear Regulatory Commission approved Early Site Permit for a location in New York to support future deployment of Gen III+ SMRs.
- Nebraska Public Power District received $27,864,860 to obtain a U.S. Nuclear Regulatory Commission approved Early Site Permit for a location in Nebraska to support future deployment of Gen III+ SMRs.
- BWXT Nuclear Energy, Inc. received $21,423,305 to procure equipment for an existing facility in Mount Vernon, Ind., required for final assembly of reactor pressure vessels and manufacture of other large nuclear reactor components.
- Container Technologies Industries, LLC received $547,900 to expand the company’s nuclear quality assurance certifications for their facility in Helenwood, Tenn., to enable production of steel for Gen III+ SMR deployments.
- Framatome U.S. Government Solutions, LLC received $8,800,000 to expand a fuel fabrication facility in Richland, Wash., by increasing ceramic pellet production lines and adding approximately 200 metric tons of uranium annual capacity.
- Global Nuclear Fuel Americas, LLC received $3,000,000 to establish a second production line for fuel rod fabrication for boiling water reactors, acquire capital equipment to automate pellet inspection, and implement automated storage and handling for pellets at their facility in Wilmington, N.C.
- American Forgemasters Company received $2,900,000 to procure a new furnace for their facility in New Castle, Pa., to facilitate domestic production of large component forgings for Gen III+ SMRs.
- Scot Forge Company received $12,267,000 to procure and install a large vertical turning lathe and gantry style milling machine for a facility in Spring Grove, Ill., to facilitate domestic production and manufacturing of large components for Gen III+ SMRs.
- Light-water SMRs offer flexible deployment, proven technology, and ability to leverage existing supply chains, making them the fastest and most scalable path to new nuclear capacity.
- In March 2025, DOE issued a $900 million solicitation to de-risk the deployment of Gen III+ SMRs.
- DOE announced $800 million in Tier 1 awards in December 2025 to the Tennessee Valley Authority and Holtec Government Services to advance initial projects in Tennessee and Michigan.
- These initial projects aim to expand the country’s nuclear capacity while facilitating additional follow-on projects and associated supply chains.
- Another round of awards under Tier 2 may be issued if additional funds become available.
