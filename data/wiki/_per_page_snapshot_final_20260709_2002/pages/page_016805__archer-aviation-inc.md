# Archer Aviation Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-07-03

**Source:** [Stellantis Invests Additional $55 Million In Archer Following Recent Flight Test Milestone | Corporate | Stellantis Media](https://www.media.stellantis.com/me-en/corporate/press/stellantis-invests-additional-55-million-in-archer-following-recent-flight-test-milestone)  ·  `page_016805`

## Facts

- Stellantis invests an additional $55 million in Archer following the achievement of its transition flight test milestone last month.
- This latest investment builds on $39 million worth of open market purchases of Archer stock that Stellantis completed in March of this year.
- During 2023, Stellantis invested $110 million in Archer through a combination of open market stock purchases and investments under the companies’ strategic funding agreement.
- Construction nears completion on Archer’s high-volume manufacturing facility in Covington, GA, which is on track to be completed later this year.
- The first phase of the build out is a ~350,000 square foot facility on an ~100 acre site.
- The facility is designed to support production of up to 650 aircraft annually.
- Archer aims to establish a factory that can support its planned commercial ramp up by leveraging Stellantis as its contract manufacturer.
- Stellantis has been a strategic partner to Archer since 2020 through various collaboration initiatives, and an investor since 2021.
- Archer leverages Stellantis’ deep manufacturing, supply chain, and design expertise in connection with designing, developing, and commercializing its eVTOL aircraft.
- Archer’s Midnight is a piloted, four-passenger aircraft designed to perform rapid back-to-back flights with minimal charge time between flights.
- Archer aims to transform urban travel by replacing 60–90 minute car commutes with estimated 10–20 minute electric air taxi flights that are safe, sustainable, low noise, and cost-competitive with ground transportation.
- Carlos Tavares serves as Stellantis CEO.
- Adam Goldstein serves as Archer CEO.
