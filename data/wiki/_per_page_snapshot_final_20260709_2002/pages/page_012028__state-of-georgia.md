# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-01-14

**Source:** [Kemp outlines priorities for affordability, infrastructure, workforce](https://www.wrdw.com/2026/01/14/kemp-outlines-priorities-affordability-infrastructure-workforce/)  ·  `page_012028`

## Facts

- Outgoing Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp headlined his final breakfast gathering of the state’s preeminent business and political leaders at the Georgia Chamber of Commerce's annual Eggs & Issues Breakfast at Mercedes-Benz Stadium in Atlanta.
- The breakfast serves as a preview for Kemp's State of the State address, which is set for 11 a.m. Thursday.
- Among other proposals at the breakfast, the governor announced historic investments in affordability, transportation and energy infrastructure, and education and workforce development.
- Georgia has maintained its coveted ranking as the No. 1 state for business and celebrated more Georgians working than ever before.
- Georgia ports have broken new records for trade and movement of goods, making them the fastest growing on the east coast.
- Kemp's administration returned over 9.7 billion dollars to taxpayers through tax cuts, homestead tax exemptions, multiple tax rebates, and suspended state gas taxes.
- Making everyday life more affordable for hardworking Georgians will continue as a top priority for the upcoming legislative session.
- Commissioner John King announced three auto insurers are lowering their rates in 2026 following the passage of tort reform legislation.
- Georgia's Public Service Commission approved a three-year rate freeze for Georgia Power customers to ensure reliable, affordable energy.
- The state is positioning itself as a national leader in renewable, reliable energy through smart, locally driven growth and investment while ensuring large load customers pay more.
- Kemp announced his budget proposal will invest an additional 1.8 billion dollars to increase roadway capacity on I-75 south in Henry County.
- The I-75 south funding will construct dedicated express lanes for both the North and South-bound directions to address bottlenecks for nearly 200,000 vehicles a day, including a quarter that are trucks hauling goods.
- GDOT and SRTA will manage the I-75 south project, with initial estimates indicating that once completed, as much as 70 percent more vehicles will be able to pass through that corridor during rush hours.
- Kemp announced an additional 200-million-dollar investment to continue improvements along Gorgia 316, commonly known as University Parkway, from Gwinnett County to Athens.
- The GA 316 funding will go toward ongoing upgrades of intersections to interchanges, over passes, and other safer connections to create a true expressway.
- The state will provide an additional 250 million dollars for community-level road projects through the Local Maintenance and Improvement Grant program this year.
- In the prior two years, the state put a total of 515 million dollars into LMIG grants, doubling the initial investment.
- There will be no match required from local governments for the new LMIG funding round.
- The state will add another 100 million dollars to strengthen and improve bridges across rural communities in Georgia.
- Kemp's amended budget proposal includes 35 million dollars in one-time money to create a natural gas infrastructure improvement fund within the Georgia Environmental Finance Authority, following Senate Bill 13.
- The natural gas infrastructure fund will particularly benefit rural Georgia communities to improve quality of life and grow their ability to attract economic development projects.
- Since taking office, Kemp's administration will have invested roughly 6 billion dollars in transportation, water, and energy infrastructure projects across the state.
- Kemp announced that in the coming days his administration will propose the Education and Workforce Strategy Act to ensure unprecedented collaboration between the University and Technical College Systems, the Georgia Department of Education, and others continues.
- Three new articulation agreements were announced between higher-ed systems in the fields of teacher preparation, cybersecurity, and accounting, building on a first compact in nursing.
- The state will establish a cutting-edge Career Navigator application this year to connect students, job seekers, and employers across Georgia.
- Labor Commissioner Bárbara Rivera Holmes is leading efforts to connect Georgians in every part of the state, including rural areas, with jobs coming to their communities.
- Kemp announced the creation of a new Homelessness Response Grant program with a one-time state investment of 50 million dollars to tackle homelessness in Atlanta and other urban areas.
- The Homelessness Response Grant will be coupled with funds from public, private, and non-profit partners, with awards going to local governments and nonprofit organizations addressing street-level homelessness.
- Resources for the grant will go to emergency shelter and transitional housing, street outreach and engagement teams, and wraparound services like mental healthcare and drug rehabilitation.
- Georgia will host a major influx of visitors this summer comparable to the 1996 Olympics, coinciding with the FIFA World Cup in Atlanta in the coming months.
