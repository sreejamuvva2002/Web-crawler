# Trenton Pressing

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Metal forming company jumps into the thick of metal fabricating | Guangdong Steel Pipe Co.,Ltd](https://www.smartsitesuk.com/news/metal-forming-company-jumps-into-the-thick-of-metal-fabricating)  ·  `page_012545`

## Facts

- Trenton Pressing is located in Trenton, Ga.
- The company is a longtime Tier 1 and Tier 2 supplier of stamped parts and complex assemblies for automotive companies and golf cart manufacturers.
- During the pandemic, new owners purchased the business with the goal of diversifying its customer base.
- The company expanded its services to include metal fabricating alongside existing stamping, robotic welding, and assembly operations.
- The ultimate goal of the new ownership is to build full turnkey fabrications for customers rather than just supplying individual parts.
- A new 100,000-sq.-ft. building was constructed on the company campus specifically for warehousing and assembly activities.
- Constructing the new building freed up additional space in Plant 1 for manufacturing equipment.
- Plant 2 houses 15 robotic welding cells along with manual welding and assembly areas.
- In late 2023, Trenton Pressing purchased a BesCutter Mach 3015 20-kW fiber laser cutting machine from Automated Solutions.
- The laser cutting machine features a 5- by 10-ft. bed and automatic loading/unloading capability.
- The new laser cutting machine became operational in early 2024.
- The laser is capable of processing up to 2-in. carbon steel plate.
- The machine utilizes compressed high-pressure air instead of oxygen or nitrogen for cutting, which reduces operating costs for 3/8- to ½-in. material.
- An automated load and unload gantry system was installed with the laser, using vacuum suction cups for loading raw sheet metal and fork-style lifters for unloading finished parts.
- The automated gantry system can run unattended for eight hours without requiring material reloading.
- The company purchased two cutting heads to allow quick swaps during maintenance and prevent machine downtime.
- In early 2025, the company installed a Haco EuroMaster 275-ton CNC press brake with a 10-ft. bed and a 21.25-in. daylight opening.
- The press brake includes advanced features such as 6-axis backgauging, CNC crowning, automatic angle measurement during bending, hydraulic upper tool clamping, and offline programming capability.
- Randall Hamilton serves as Trenton Pressing’s plant manager and operates the new press brake.
- Brad Coffman, an employee with 30 years of metal fabricating experience, led the equipment selection process.
- The company plans to train its own engineers to operate the new laser cutting machine and press brake to reduce reliance on specialized operators.
- Work-in-process materials are moved between buildings using kanban trucks that make at least three stops per day at each facility.
- Management refinished approximately 60% of the factory floors by grinding concrete and applying polyurethane paint to meet high operational standards.
