# Qcells

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-05-07

**Source:** [Qcells Announces Michigan-Based Manufacturing Collaboration with Jabil for Domestic Content Eligible Residential Battery Storage - Qcells North America](https://us.qcells.com/blog/qcells-announces-michigan-based-manufacturing-collaboration-with-jabil-for-domestic-content-eligible-residential-battery-storage/)  ·  `page_001566`

## Facts

- Qcells and its parent company Hanwha have invested a total of $17 million to-date to onshore the assembly of solar-plus-storage products used by Qcells customers, including microinverters.
- Qcells announced a collaboration with Jabil to offer its first domestically assembled residential energy storage system (ESS) to the U.S. residential solar and energy storage market.
- The new battery storage system is a domestic content eligible version of Qcells’ third-generation residential ESS, known as Q.HOME CORE G3.
- The product is commercially available and ready to ship to customers nationwide.
- This announcement marks a continuation of Qcells’ existing collaboration with Jabil to assemble Qcells microinverters in Michigan for use in Qcells AC module products.
- A latest investment totaling $11.6 million in advanced manufacturing operations for Q.HOME CORE G3 and future ESS products is expected to contribute to job growth at Jabil’s Auburn Hills, Michigan factory.
- Q.HOME CORE G3 can contribute to unlocking the domestic content adder bonus for eligible projects while helping to drive down household energy costs and support the creation of American manufacturing jobs.
- The collaboration enables a single, complete residential solar-plus-storage solution backed by one brand and one warrantor.
- In Georgia, Qcells has invested nearly $3 billion in building a U.S. solar supply chain.
- Qcells is closing in on completing construction of a one-of-a-kind factory in Cartersville, GA that will build critical solar components including ingots, wafers, cells and modules under one roof.
- Il Hyeong No, head of Qcells North America’s Residential Business, stated that pairing solar panels from Georgia with battery systems from Michigan can help unlock significant value for homeowners, particularly when installed in the “Grid Support” configuration.
- Q.HOME CORE G3 pairs seamlessly with Qcells’ AC module products Q.PEAK DUO AC and Q.TRON AC, which are both assembled in Georgia.
- The integrated microinverters for these systems are manufactured in Michigan.
- Combined, the products make up a cohesive AC-coupled system architecture designed to maximize performance, simplify system installation, and reduce energy bills for homeowners.
- Q.HOME CORE G3’s “Grid Support” configuration helps homeowners avoid costly utility rates by charging their battery with excess daytime solar or off-peak utility power and dispatching stored energy during on-peak evening rates.
- Homeowners impacted by frequent power outages can add battery backup capabilities to the system for extra grid resilience.
- Q.HOME CORE G3 integrates with competitive financing products offered by EnFin by Qcells, allowing homeowners to access flexible third-party ownership (TPO) products including Power Purchase Agreements (PPAs) and leasing options.
- Combining Q.HOME CORE G3 with EnFin financing enables access to affordable solar and storage through a single ecosystem for both installers and homeowners.
- Qcells will formally debut its domestic content Q.HOME CORE G3 product at scheduled roadshow events in May 2026.
- Qcells is headquartered in Seoul, South Korea, with diverse international manufacturing facilities in the U.S., Malaysia, and South Korea.
- Qcells provides renewable electricity retail services and packages to end customers across the world.
