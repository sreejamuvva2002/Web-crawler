# Kumho Tire USA

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2011-12-12

**Source:** [Ring in the New: Kumho Holds Dealer Meeting Early - Tire Review Magazine](https://www.tirereview.com/ring-in-the-new-kumho-holds-dealer-meeting-early/)  ·  `page_056690`

## Facts

- Kumho Tire USA held its 2012 dealer meeting at the close of 2011 rather than waiting for the start of the new year.
- The event featured a "Let's Go" theme to reflect the tiremaker's eagerness to continue momentum from 2011 into 2012 and beyond.
- Kumho used the event to communicate that successful moves made in 2011 would continue into 2012, including progress in dealer communication, product availability, and brand awareness.
- The meeting was held just outside of Liberia, Costa Rica, and included 38 key dealers in attendance.
- Kumho officials addressed challenges faced going into 2011, including a sluggish economy, financial troubles of parent company Kumho Asiana Group, and an industry-wide supply shortage.
- J.H. Kim, Kumho Tire Co. president and CEO, stated that the company continued to progress through its debt workout situation in 2011 with dealer help, noting good progress was made despite remaining work.
- J.H. Kim predicted more change in 2012 that would bring challenges and opportunities, emphasizing the importance of working together for future success.
- J.B. Kim, Kumho Tire USA president and CEO, attended the meeting and reported greatly improved fill rates through consolidating warehouses to three locations: Rancho Cucamonga, Calif., Chicago, and Atlanta.
- J.B. Kim noted that during the year, Kumho's fill rates were among the best of any supplier for passenger tires.
- Armand Allaire, national vice president of sales, stated that supply was a bright spot for Kumho in 2011 and that moving from five to three warehouses allowed concentration of the supply pipeline and replenishment schedules on three locations.
- Armand Allaire reported that as a result of the warehouse consolidation, fill rates were quite good and the company continues to work toward a 100% fill rate goal.
- J.B. Kim announced a new marketing direction focusing on sports marketing to place the Kumho brand among well-known and popular sports teams in the U.S.
- Through sponsorships of the NBA’s Los Angeles Lakers and Miami Heat, NFL’s New York Jets and Buffalo Bills, USC Trojans football, and U.S. men’s and women’s soccer teams, Kumho’s overall brand awareness climbed from 21% to 25%.
- In the New England area, brand awareness increased to 29% due to NFL sponsorships.
- Rick Brennan, vice president of marketing, stated Kumho will continue building on sports marketing momentum in 2012 with more initiatives.
- The Ecsta 4X tire, launched in July, earned impressive marks in testing, with 8.6 out of 10 drivers who purchased it saying they would consider purchasing it again.
- Charles van Heusden, manager of training and product planning, noted Kumho added 12 more sizes to the Ecsta 4X lineup and plans to add sizes to the Ecsta LE Sport and Ecsta LX Platinum lines.
- An improved winter tire program will be offered in 2012, with the entire line of I’zen studless and studdable tires for passenger cars, light trucks, SUVs, and CUVs seeing improved fill rates.
- Charles van Heusden noted Kumho plans to inventory some winter tires in its warehouses to save dealers space in their inventories.
- The consumer rebate on the Ecsta 4X and LX Platinum was extended through Jan. 31 due to its success, with similar rebates paired with sports tie-ins planned between April and October of 2012.
- David Koh, marketing communications manager, stated Kumho’s TV and consumer magazine advertising campaigns will return even larger in 2012, with an estimated 140 million impressions planned during the April to October timeframe.
- The advertising will target the age 18-49 male demographic, which is Kumho’s strongest.
- David Koh added that Kumho will continue focusing on Internet presence and social media campaigns, which brought success in 2011.
- David Koh noted Kumho extended an invitation for dealers to develop exclusive sports tie-ins for markets where Kumho holds sponsorships, citing the Dunn Tire co-branding initiative with the Buffalo Bills as an example.
- Armand Allaire stated these adjustments will offer dealers better tools to improve their businesses and added that Kumho Tire USA is going to be much easier to do business with in 2012.
