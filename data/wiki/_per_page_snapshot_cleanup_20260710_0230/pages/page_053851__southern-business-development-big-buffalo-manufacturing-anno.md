# Southern Business & Development Big Buffalo Manufacturing Announcements

**Type:** event  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Big Buffalo Awards | Southern Business and Development](https://sb-d.com/magazine/article/sidebar-manufacturing-sector)  ·  `page_053851`

## Facts

- The article profiles 25 of the biggest manufacturing announcements made in the South from 1992-2017 by Southern Business & Development.
- BMW’s Spartanburg County, S.C. plant paved the way for many more foreign automakers in the Southern Automotive Corridor, including Mercedes-Benz the next year.
- Nissan Powertrain in Decherd, Tenn. began supplying electric motors to support U.S. production of the all-electric Nissan LEAF assembled in Tennessee in 2013.
- Bridgestone's expansion in Aiken County, S.C., transformed Western South Carolina including Augusta, Ga.
- Kia Motors announced it would build its first U.S. plant near the Alabama-Georgia border in West Point, Ga., a little over 10 years ago.
- The first vehicle at the Kia West Point plant, a Kia Sorento, rolled off the line in November of 2009.
- Kia and Hyundai plants share dozens of suppliers located in Alabama and Georgia due to a symbiotic relationship.
- Kia’s engines are built by Hyundai in Alabama and Hyundai’s transmissions are built by Kia in Georgia.
- During the economic recovery, both the Kia West Point and Hyundai Montgomery plants ran three shifts a day for a while.
- A Kia executive found the 2,200-acre site in West Point, Ga., while driving to the Hyundai plant in Montgomery from Atlanta.
- SB&D publisher Michael Randle identified Troup County, Ga., as the first location in Georgia on an Interstate 85 map between Montgomery and Georgia during Kia’s negotiations with Mississippi.
- Randle contacted Jane Fryer, the economic development director in Troup County at the time, to initiate contact with Kia.
- The Kia West Point plant transformed much of West Georgia and East Alabama, particularly Troup, Meriwether, and Harris counties in Georgia.
- DENSO Manufacturing Athens Tennessee Inc. manufactures ignition and exhaust systems and gasoline delivery systems for global customers including Hyundai, GM, Ford, Honda, and Nissan, operating 18 facilities in the Southern Auto Corridor.
- Volkswagen’s Chattanooga plant produces a sedan and a large SUV with plans to add a small SUV, and some of Northwest Georgia has benefited from its economic impact.
- Alabama captured Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Alabama with a $253 million incentive package, just edging out Kentucky for the automaker's first U.S. assembly facility.
- Toyota site teams initially wanted to place a new plant somewhere in the traditional Southern Auto Corridor, which is spined by Interstate 65, before marketing pushed for Texas.
