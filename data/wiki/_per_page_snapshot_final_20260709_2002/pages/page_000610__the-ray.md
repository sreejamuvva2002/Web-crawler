# The Ray

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** charging_infrastructure  ·  **Published:** 2018-03-01

**Source:** [This 18-mile stretch of Georgia highway is a living laboratory for clean energy](https://www.popsci.com/georgia-highway-ray-clean-energy/)  ·  `page_000610`

## Facts

- Carpet manufacturer Ray Anderson cut net carbon emissions by more than 80 percent after reading The Ecology of Commerce in 1994, inspiring the clean energy highway project.
- Anderson’s daughters have turned an 18-mile stretch of rural Georgia highway named after him into a living laboratory for clean energy innovation.
- The Ray aims to be “a zero carbon, zero deaths, zero waste, zero impact highway.”
- Harriet Langford, president of the Ray, stated that the project is fundamentally changing assumptions and expectations of highway infrastructure to make roads safe, sustainable, and regenerative.
- Super slender, extra durable, skid-resistant solar panels are installed along the roadway to supply power to the Visitor Information Center in West Point, GA.
- A solar-powered charging station at the West Point Visitor Information Center can charge most electric vehicles 80 percent of the way in less than 45 minutes.
- A roll-over tire pressure monitor plate at the West Point Visitor Information Center gauges the pressure and tread depth of each tire and delivers a printout with the measurements.
- Drivers can fill up their tires for free at a nearby pump, which improves fuel efficiency.
- Engineers are looking to build highways with embedded wireless charging lanes that use an electromagnetic field to transmit energy to next-generation electric cars.
- Noise-canceling barriers with embedded solar panels are being considered as solar barriers along the highway.
- Solar-powered smart studs featuring a small solar panel and battery are being considered to light up at night, urge drivers to slow down in rain, or mark shoulders as extra lanes during rush hour.
- The Ray is considering installing roadside wind turbines and additional solar panels.
- Wheat farms along the route will produce straw that can be turned into toilet paper and paper towels.
- Flower gardens are being planted to serve as habitats for pollinators including birds, bees, bats, butterflies, and other creatures.
- The Ray is considering using drones to inspect damage to roads and bridges.
- The highway is looking to be repaved using a mix of asphalt and recycled tires to cut down on noise and improve durability.
- Harriet Langford stated that these innovations demonstrate her father’s philosophy of doing well by doing good.
