# Valeo

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2021-11-23

**Source:** [With its new generation LiDAR, Valeo makes autonomous mobility a reality](https://www.prnewswire.co.uk/news-releases/with-its-new-generation-lidar-valeo-makes-autonomous-mobility-a-reality-867481795.html)  ·  `page_012833`

## Facts

- Valeo presented its third generation scanning LiDAR, set to make its market debut in 2024.
- Geoffrey Bouquot serves as Valeo's Senior Vice-President, R&D and Strategy.
- Bouquot stated that Valeo's third generation LiDAR is a major technological advance toward the autonomous vehicle and strengthens Valeo's technological and industrial leadership as the only supplier currently series-producing an automotive-grade LiDAR scanner.
- Bouquot noted that the number one goal with the device remains to save lives on the road.
- The new LiDAR delivers unrivaled performance in terms of range, resolution, and frame rate.
- It reconstructs a 3D real-time image of the vehicle's surroundings at a rate of 4.5 million pixels and 25 frames per second.
- Compared to the previous generation, the resolution has been increased 12-fold, the range 3-fold, and the viewing angle 2.5-fold.
- The LiDAR can see things that humans, cameras, and radars cannot, enabling driving to be delegated to the vehicle in many situations including level 2 automation and above.
- Vehicles equipped with the third-generation scanning LiDAR can manage emergency situations autonomously, including on highways at speeds of up to 130km/h.
- The scanning LiDAR detects, recognizes, and classifies all objects located around the car, measuring speed and direction if they are moving.
- It adapts to all light conditions and measures the density of raindrops to calculate the right braking distance.
- It tracks nearby vehicles even when they are no longer in the driver's line of sight, using algorithms to anticipate trajectories and trigger necessary safety maneuvers.
- The LiDAR protects people inside the car and those around it, such as pedestrians and cyclists.
- Beyond the vehicles it equips, this LiDAR will alert other vehicles of road hazards via the Cloud.
- Valeo designs and manufactures the entire system, including the hardware, software, and associated artificial intelligence that combines collected data to enable instant decision-making.
- The software automatically adapts to the environment and improves its performance over time through regular updates.
- Valeo's LiDARs are produced in Germany at its Wemding plant in Bavaria, where components are assembled with a micron level of precision.
- The plant's production lines draw on Valeo's state-of-the-art expertise in optics, mechanics, and photonics.
- 300 engineers at Valeo are dedicated solely to this technology, for which over 500 patents have already been filed.
- Valeo is the first and only company to produce a scanning LiDAR on an industrial scale, having already produced over 150,000 units.
- 99% of cars equipped with a scanning LiDAR scanner worldwide are equipped with a Valeo scanning LiDAR.
- Up to 30% of premium new vehicles are set to reach level 3 automation by 2030, requiring them to be equipped with LiDAR technology.
- Autonomous shuttles, robotaxis, delivery droids, autonomous trucks, and sectors including agriculture, mining, and infrastructure will also need to be equipped with one or more LiDARs.
- The LiDAR market is expected to represent more than $50 billion by 2030.
- Valeo's new third generation LiDAR will be a key contributor to this change in scale.
