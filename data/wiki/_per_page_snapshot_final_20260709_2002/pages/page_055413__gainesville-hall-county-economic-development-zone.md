# Gainesville-Hall County Economic Development Zone

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2023-09-28

**Source:** [Gainesville | Hall County: Crowd Favorite - Georgia Trend Magazine](https://www.georgiatrend.com/2023/09/28/gainesville-hall-county-crowd-favorite/)  ·  `page_055413`

## Facts

- Hall County's advanced manufacturing sector accounts for nearly 30% of local employment.
- The Gainesville-Hall County area is home to more than 330 manufacturing and processing facilities.
- The region hosts 60 international companies from 21 foreign countries.
- Tim Evans, senior vice president of the Greater Hall Chamber of Commerce, stated that advanced manufacturing, healthcare, and education services provide diverse economic drivers that help withstand national or global recession headwinds.
- The planned Northeast Georgia Inland Port received National Environmental Policy Act approval in May 2023.
- Port activity currently supports 9,682 jobs in the Gainesville-Hall County area.
- The Georgia Ports Authority anticipates the inland port rail hub will open after its 2026 completion with volumes of 60,000 containers per year.
- The inland port will serve a key area for the production of heavy equipment, food, and forest products.
- Griff Lynch, president and CEO of the Georgia Ports Authority, noted the inland port will deliver more economical supply chain alternatives and extend Georgia’s rail advantage.
- The inland port project is expected to reduce approximately 36 million truck miles in the terminal’s first year of operation.
- The inland port will be located in the heart of the I-85 manufacturing and logistics corridor with rail connections to Norfolk Southern and proximity to the National Highway Freight Network.
- Hall County Commission Chair Richard Higgins stated the inland port will cement Hall County's legacy as a leading manufacturing hub for the state and region while providing jobs and an overall boost to the local economy.
- The 1,300-acre Gainesville 85 Business Park contains 20 lots sized from 14 to 112 acres for industry and office use.
- Car hauler manufacturer Cottrell purchased two lots in the Gainesville 85 Business Park before its April 2021 groundbreaking.
- A sixth lot in the Gainesville 85 Business Park was pending sale in late summer 2023.
- Bryan Lackey, city manager for Gainesville, stated the business park was developed with environmental design features including nature trails and existing youth sports facilities.
- The Gainesville-Hall County Development Authority partnered with the city of Gainesville on the development of the Gainesville 85 Business Park.
- Swiss firm medmix will start production in November 2023 at a new 300,000-square-foot facility in Flowery Branch.
- The medmix facility will use high-tech injection molding and assembly to manufacture devices used during surgery and dental procedures, as well as EpiPen-like drug delivery systems.
- James Kirk, president and site lead for medmix Healthcare U.S., stated the plant will expand through five phases over the next 10 years.
- The medmix facility is expected to employ over 250 workers with competitive wages and climate-controlled environments.
- Medmix cited proximity to Atlanta, Hartsfield-Jackson airport, the Port of Savannah, and the region’s interstate system as key logistics advantages for its U.S. expansion decision.
- Flowery Branch offers cost savings on the cost of doing business compared to major metro areas.
- Medmix has initiated conversations with area colleges including Kennesaw State University, Georgia Tech, and the University of Georgia regarding an injection molding European-style apprenticeship program to encourage trade skills in the region.
- The area was recognized as a rapidly growing trades workforce region by medmix leadership.
