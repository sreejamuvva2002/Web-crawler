# Novelis Greensboro Facility

## Facts

- An industrial blast occurred at the Novelis Greensboro facility in Georgia on March 1, 2026, halting plant operations.
- Greene County Emergency Management Director Joe Bashore identified that the explosion originated from baghouse equipment and caused significant structural damage to the closest building.
- The incident resulted in secondary fires spreading to aluminum scrap stockpiles and conveyor belt systems.
- Baghouse dust collection systems process particulate-laden air through fabric filter bags, concentrating combustible aluminum dust in confined spaces during metal cutting, shredding, casting, ingot handling, and material transfer.
- Aluminum particulate matter has a low minimum ignition energy of 1-5 millijoules and high combustion velocity once ignited.
- The dust creates explosion thresholds between 20-40 g/m³, which is significantly lower than many other industrial dusts.
- The combustion reaction of aluminum dust generates extremely high temperatures exceeding 3,000 Kelvin with exceptional violence.
- Industry data indicates aluminum dust explosions achieve Kst values of 80-150 bar·m/s, classifying them among the most severe industrial explosion hazards.
- All 16 workers at the Novelis facility were successfully evacuated without injuries.
- The Greensboro facility processes used beverage can (UBC) materials and 5182 scrap aluminum specifically for beverage can end manufacturing.
- This specialized material stream feeds directly into Novelis rolling mills for sheet production serving beverage industry customers.
- Approximately 50% of all beverage cans worldwide contain recycled aluminum content, with 44.5 million tons of aluminum beverage cans circulating in North America in 2024 and recycling rates reaching 46.0% in 2023.
- The UBC recycling process involves collection and sorting, shredding into small metal fragments, density separation to remove contaminants, melting in induction furnaces at approximately 660°C, casting into ingot form (typically 500+ kg units), and transport to rolling facilities for sheet production.
- Novelis management indicated the company would leverage three Kentucky facilities (Berea, Guthrie, Russellville) and one New York location (Oswego) to absorb Greensboro production volumes.
- The Oswego facility experienced multiple fires in Q4 2025 that disrupted hot-rolling mill operations and automotive sheet production.
