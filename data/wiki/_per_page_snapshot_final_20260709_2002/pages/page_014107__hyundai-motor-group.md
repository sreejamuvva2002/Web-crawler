# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hyundai Will Build First Fully-Dedicated EV Plant In Georgia - Automotive - Business Facilities Magazine](https://businessfacilities.com/hyundai-will-build-first-fully-dedicated-ev-plant-in-georgia/)  ·  `page_014107`

## Facts

- Seoul, Korea-based Hyundai Motor Group (HMG) will make a $5.54 billion investment to open its first fully dedicated electric vehicle (EV) and battery manufacturing facility in Georgia.
- HMG’s new state-of-the-art U.S. smart factory will be located at the Bryan County Megasite.
- Non-affiliated Hyundai Motor Group suppliers will invest approximately $1 billion in the project.
- The project will create approximately 8,100 new jobs in Georgia’s coastal region.
- Hyundai Motor Group Executive Chair Euisun Chung stated they are incredibly proud to share plans to open their first dedicated full EV and battery manufacturing facilities in the U.S.
- Euisun Chung noted the U.S. has always held an important place in the Group’s global strategy and expressed excitement to partner with the State of Georgia for electrified mobility and sustainability goals.
- Hyundai Motor Group expects to begin construction on the new facility in January 2023.
- Full production is expected in the first half of 2025.
- The facility will have an annual capacity of 300,000 units.
- Hyundai Motor Company President and CEO Jaehoon (Jay) Chang stated they decided to build their first dedicated EV plant in the U.S. because America embraces change and drives innovation.
- Jaehoon (Jay) Chang noted the new EV plant is the future of their business and will help meet growing demands of U.S. customers for leading edge design, safe, zero-emissions vehicles.
- HMG cited speed-to-market, workforce, and the state’s ability to meet the company’s carbon neutrality standards as reasons for choosing Georgia.
- Georgia is home to an existing network of Hyundai subsidiaries and suppliers.
- The 2,923-acre Bryan County Megasite was purchased jointly by the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority (JDA) and the state of Georgia less than a year ago.
- The megasite offers needed speed-to-market requirements that reduce barriers to speedy operations.
- Industrial utilities are adjacent to the site, and extensive due diligence reports have been completed.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp welcomed Hyundai Motor Group to Georgia and mentioned initial conversations on his economic development mission to Korea.
- Governor Kemp noted Georgia’s investment in the Bryan County Megasite prepared them for this opportunity.
- Governor Kemp stated this announcement is now the largest economic development project in the state’s history.
- President and COO of Hyundai Motor Company José Muñoz expressed thrill to partner with Governor Kemp and the Savannah Joint Economic Development Authority.
- José Muñoz described the facility as a flexible EV smart factory that will position Hyundai well to win in a competitive market with consumers demanding smart, sustainable mobility solutions.
- The new highly connected, automated, and flexible EV smart factory will produce a diverse range of innovative full electric vehicles.
- Through the battery manufacturing facility, HMG aims to establish a stable supply chain for EV battery and other EV components in the U.S. market.
- Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor JDA Board of Directors Chairman Carter Infinger called the announcement transformative.
- Carter Infinger stated the jobs and investment will reverberate for years and generations to come.
- Carter Infinger noted this is a great day for Bryan, Bulloch, Chatham and Effingham counties as well as the entire region and the State of Georgia.
- Bryan County is the fastest-growing county in the state.
- The strategic purchase of the Bryan County Megasite in 2021 was the largest in state history.
- The site is adjacent to Interstate 16 with immediate access from I-95 and I-16 to 250 major metro areas.
- The site is less than 30 miles from the Port of Savannah, the single-largest and fastest-growing container terminal in the U.S.
- Two Class I rail facilities on-site are provided by Georgia Central Railway.
- Georgia Department of Transportation Commissioner Russell McMurry stated Hyundai’s facility announcement is evidence that Georgia’s collective focus on supporting the electric mobility future is working.
- Russell McMurry highlighted the power of partnership and preparation, noting Georgia’s planning ensures logistics networks are safe, sustainable, innovative, and ready to support economic growth.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) Commissioner Pat Wilson stated they have been focused on putting Georgia at the forefront of electric mobility under Governor Kemp’s leadership.
- Pat Wilson noted the announcement solidifies Georgia’s spot at the vanguard of the EV transition.
- Pat Wilson welcomed the Hyundai Group to Georgia and celebrated the investment, stating the facility will create new possibilities for all Georgians and transform an entire region.
- Since 2020, Georgia has announced more than 20 EV-related projects that will invest more than $13.54 billion and create nearly 18,000 jobs in the state.
