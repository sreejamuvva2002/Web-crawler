# Georgia Department of Community Affairs

## Facts

- The Georgia Department of Community Affairs (DCA) partners with communities to create a climate of success for Georgia's families and businesses through community and economic development, local government assistance, and safe and affordable housing.
- Using state and federal resources, DCA helps communities spur private job creation, implement planning, develop downtowns, generate affordable housing solutions, and promote volunteerism.
- DCA coordinates a variety of economic incentives and tools, tax credits, and financing plans.
- DCA works with and supports the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) in their mission to promote economic development in Georgia.
- DCA economic development finance programs include OneGeorgia Authority funds and programs, the Regional Economic Business Assistance program, the Georgia Business Expansion and Support Act income tax credit programs and many others.
- In April 2012, the Georgia General Assembly passed and Governor Nathan Deal signed legislation updating the state's Open Records Act to promote economic competitiveness, provide clarity on the responsibilities of state and local governments and increase transparency for residents of the state.
- The revised code keeps the state's sensitive information regarding economic activities confidential while ensuring that a citizen's personal information is protected.
- Pursuant to O.C.G.A. 50-18-72 (a)(46), the Georgia Department of Economic Development will comply with updated public notice requirements for the use of state funds and economic development incentive programs.
- For projects where GDEcD committed OneGeorgia Authority or REBA funds, the department must post a notice of the binding commitment on its website along with records documenting the commitment.
- The department must also publish notice of the project and participating parties in the legal organ of each county where the economic development project is located.
- Notice will be given on the webpage no later than five (5) business days after GDEcD secures a binding commitment with a qualified economic development project.
- Notices will reside on the page for a period of 30 days.
- An economic development project pertains to a plan or proposal to locate or expand a business that includes an expenditure of over $25,000,000 by the company.
- An economic development project pertains to a plan or proposal to locate or expand a business that includes hiring of 50 or more employees by the company.
- An economic development project pertains to a plan or proposal to locate or expand a business that includes commitment of state funds from OneGeorgia Authority or REBA.
- A binding agreement between GDEcD and Dongwon Autopart Technology Georgia, Emanuel LLC was entered into on May 19, 2026.
- The Dongwon Autopart Technology Georgia project is located in Swainsboro, Emanuel County.
