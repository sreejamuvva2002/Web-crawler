# Hyundai Motor Group and SK On EV Battery Manufacturing Facility

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-12-08

**Source:** [Hyundai, SK On Select Bartow County for Build EV Battery Facility in Georgia - The EV Report](https://theevreport.com/hyundai-sk-on-select-bartow-county-for-build-ev-battery-facility-in-georgia)  ·  `page_049966`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Hyundai Motor Group (HMG) and SK On selected a site in Bartow County for a new EV battery manufacturing facility.
- The new facility will supply Hyundai Motor Group’s plants in the U.S.
- Stakeholders estimate the project will create more than 3,500 new jobs through approximately $4-5 billion of investment in Bartow County.
- Hyundai Motor Group and SK On recently signed a memorandum of understanding (MOU) regarding the partnership for the new EV battery facility in the U.S.
- Details of the HMG and SK On partnership are still in development.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Hyundai Motor Group and SK On are valued partners and key players in Georgia’s ever-growing automotive industry.
- SK On was established in 2021 as the lithium-ion battery subsidiary branch of SK Innovation.
- SK On currently employs more than 2,000 Georgians at its SK Battery America facility in Commerce.
- In July, SK Battery America announced it would partner with the Work for Warriors Georgia program to hire veterans, servicemembers, and their families at its Georgia operations.
- The new facility's development is subject to execution of relevant agreements and final board decisions by HMG and SK On.
- The selected site is Bartow Centre, a zoned manufacturing and industrial site located on Highway 411.
- The project aims to begin operations in 2025.
- Bartow County Sole Commissioner Steve Taylor expressed pleasure with the decision and thanked the owners of the former Bartow Centre Industrial property.
- The project will establish Bartow in Georgia’s EV ecosystem and leverage the county's strong history and talent base in advanced manufacturing, the automotive industry, and innovation.
- Director of Korean Investment Yoonie Kim represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on the project.
- The GDEcD partnered with Georgia EMC, Development Authority of Bartow County, the Cartersville-Bartow County Department of Economic Development, and Georgia Quick Start on this competitive project.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that HMG and SK have been pioneering partners for Georgia for decades, with HMG being a major driver for Georgia’s automotive renaissance and SK On being the first Korean manufacturer to locate in the state.
- The partnership aims to create a fully integrated supply chain for automotive OEMs while connecting battery manufacturers with recyclers to close the loop on battery manufacturing.
- Georgia is positioned as a hub for the electric transportation industry due to its prime location, major ports system, extensive infrastructure, skilled workforce, and pro-business climate.
- Excluding this announcement, EV-related projects announced in Georgia since 2020 total approximately $17 billion in investment and more than 22,800 new jobs.
- Hyundai Motor Group separately announced in May plans to invest $5.54 billion in Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America, an EV manufacturing facility in Bryan County.
- Hyundai Motor Group broke ground on the Metaplant America facility in October with Governor Kemp and other state, local, and federal officials.
