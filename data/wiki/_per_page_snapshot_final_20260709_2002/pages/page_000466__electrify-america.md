# Electrify America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** charging_infrastructure  ·  **Published:** 2022-01-30

**Source:** [EV charging company helps reduce air pollution in the Central Valley](https://bakersfieldnow.com/news/local/ev-charging-company-helps-to-reduce-air-pollution-in-the-central-valley)  ·  `page_000466`

## Facts

- EV charging company Electrify America operates solar-powered charging stations all across the United States.
- Senior Director of Sales and Marketing Robert Barrosa states that cheaper cars hitting the market and more charging station installations are making access to electric vehicles easier than ever.
- Robert Barrosa said: "Electric cars are normal now. You can get into an electric vehicle and live the electric lifestyle, you don’t have to be a millionaire."
- Lower income areas and regions with fewer charging stations prevent people from switching to electric vehicles.
- Electric vehicle adoption in California's Central Valley is taking longer compared to other parts of the state.
- Fresno currently has 5,481 electric cars on the road.
- San Francisco has over 16,242 electric vehicles on the road.
- Governor Newsom outlined a historic $10 billion zero-emission vehicle package aimed at accelerating the phasing out of gas-powered cars by 2035.
- The Central Valley continues to experience the worst air pollution in the region.
- In November 2021, a coalition of climate justice organizations sued the Environmental Protection Agency over failing to intervene in the area's climate crisis.
- Robert Barrosa states that pollution and climate change are the driving forces behind Electrify America's push to increase electric vehicle adoption.
- Robert Barrosa said: "Making the transition to electric vehicles is extremely important. One of the things that we wanna do, especially in areas like the Central Valley that continues to be plagued by heavy pollution levels, is to transform the transportation sector."
- Electrify America works with organizations like Valley Clean Air Now and local nonprofits to make electric vehicle adoption more accessible.
- The company has donated electric vehicles to community-based organizations in Bakersfield and throughout the Central Valley for services such as driving people to doctor's appointments or making deliveries.
- Using these donated cars for community services allows others to experience what it is like to use an electric vehicle.
