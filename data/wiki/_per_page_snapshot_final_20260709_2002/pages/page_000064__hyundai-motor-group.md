# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-05-20

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp: Hyundai Motor Group to Invest $5.54 Billion in Georgia at First Fully Dedicated Electric Vehicle and Battery Manufacturing Facility | Governor Brian P. Kemp Office of the Governor](https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases/2022-05-20/gov-kemp-hyundai-motor-group-invest-554-billion-georgia-first-fully)  ·  `page_000064`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group will invest $5.54 billion in opening its first fully dedicated electric vehicle (EV) and battery manufacturing facility in Georgia.
- Non-affiliated Hyundai Motor Group suppliers will invest approximately another $1 billion in the project.
- The project will deliver approximately 8,100 new jobs to Georgia’s coastal region.
- Wages throughout the project will be well-paying advanced manufacturing positions competitive in the local market.
- Construction on the new facility is expected to begin in January 2023.
- Full production at the facility is expected in the first half of 2025.
- The facility will have an annual capacity of 300,000 units.
- Hyundai Motor Group plans to produce a diverse range of innovative full electric vehicles at the highly connected, automated, and flexible EV smart factory.
- Details of production models will be shared at later dates.
- Through the battery manufacturing facility, Hyundai Motor Group aims to establish a stable supply chain for EV battery and other EV components in the U.S. market.
- The 2,923-acre Bryan County Megasite was purchased jointly by the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority (JDA) and the State less than a year ago.
- The site purchase is noted as the largest in state history.
- Bryan County is the fastest-growing county in Georgia.
- The megasite is adjacent to Interstate 16 with immediate access from I-95 and I-16 to 250 major metro areas.
- The site is less than 30 miles from the Port of Savannah, which is the single-largest and fastest-growing container terminal in the U.S.
- Two Class I rail facilities are on-site provided by Georgia Central Railway.
- Hyundai Motor Group cited speed-to-market, workforce, and the state’s ability to meet the company’s carbon neutrality standards as reasons for choosing Georgia.
- Georgia is home to an existing network of Hyundai subsidiaries and suppliers.
- Industrial utilities are adjacent to the Bryan County Megasite site.
- Extensive due diligence reports have been completed for the site.
- Hyundai Motor Group plans to implement advanced intelligent manufacturing technologies currently under test at its innovation hub, including intelligent A.I.-equipped manufacturing and data-driven processes.
- The plant will support Hyundai Motor Group’s efforts to achieve its carbon neutrality target through the active use of renewable energy.
- When operations near the planned 2025 start, Hyundai Motor Group will seek local vendors and suppliers.
- Since 2020, Georgia has announced more than 20 EV-related projects that will invest more than $13.54 billion and create nearly 18,000 jobs in the state.
- Georgia is the Southeastern leader for EV registrations per 1,000 registered automobiles.
- Georgia offers more EV charging outlets per capita than anywhere else in the Southeast.
- The Georgia Electric Mobility and Innovation Alliance is a public-private-nonprofit initiative focused on growing the electric mobility ecosystem in the state.
- Hyundai Motor Group is headquartered in Seoul, Korea, and employs 250,000 people worldwide.
- The Group’s vehicles are sold in 193 countries through 5,000 dealerships and showrooms.
- The Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority (JDA) includes Bryan County, Bulloch County, Chatham County, and Effingham County.
