# Rivian

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Rivian Boosts Initial Georgia Plant Capacity To 300,000 Units](https://insideevs.com/news/794630/rivian-georgia-plant-capacity-300000/)  ·  `page_017939`

## Facts

- Rivian's initial Georgia plant capacity has been increased to 300,000 units annually.
- The new capacity is up from the previously planned 200,000 units.
- Production at the Georgia factory is on track to begin in late 2028.
- Rivian announced the capacity increase as part of its first-quarter earnings release.
- The company now plans to borrow $4.5 billion through the Department of Energy to build the facility, down from a previous plan of $6.6 billion.
- Rivian will begin drawing on the DOE loan in 2027.
- The Georgia factory will produce vehicles based on Rivian's midsized platform, including the R2 model.
- The plant will also produce a robotaxi variant as part of a 50,000-vehicle partnership with Uber.
- Production of Rivian's first mass-market crossover has begun at an expanded facility in Normal, Illinois.
- Rivian's Illinois facility has the capacity to build 155,000 R2 vehicles annually.
- CFO Claire McDonough stated that a combined capacity of 515,000 vehicles is expected to help the company achieve positive free cash flow.
- In the first quarter, Rivian reported $1.38 billion in revenue and a net loss of $416 million.
- Rivian is relying on the R2 model to achieve meaningful economies of scale that the higher-priced R1T and R1S models could not.
- Rivian's initial plan for the Georgia plant called for two construction phases, each yielding 200,000 units of production capacity.
- CFO Claire McDonough stated on the earnings call that the Georgia plant will have room for future expansions.
