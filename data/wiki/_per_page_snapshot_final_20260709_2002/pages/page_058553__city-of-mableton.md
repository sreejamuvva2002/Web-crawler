# City of Mableton

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-07-11

**Source:** [Get To Know The New Mableton - Cobb In Focus](https://www.cobbinfocus.com/2024/07/11/get-to-know-the-new-mableton/)  ·  `page_058553`

## Facts

- The City of Mableton was incorporated for the first time as a city in November following a voter referendum and approval from state legislators and Gov. Brian Kemp.
- Gov. Brian Kemp signed the bill to create the new City of Mableton in May 2022, which passed with a 134-2 vote in the House and a 55-0 vote in the Senate.
- The incorporation process took approximately six years from initial planning to voter approval.
- Mableton now has a population of approximately 80,000 residents, making it the largest city in Cobb County.
- The newly incorporated city covers almost 36 square miles.
- Mableton is building its own departments for planning, zoning, code enforcement, parks, and sanitation.
- Public safety services, including police and fire, remain under the jurisdiction of Cobb County.
- The city charter provided two years to complete service transitions, with a target date of June 1, 2025.
- By June 1, 2025, Mableton aims to have at least three to four key services fully transitioned to city management.
- Initial working space for the new city administration was established at the Riverside Epicenter.
- The $33-million Riverside Epicenter is located adjacent to Six Flags and down the street from the future Veterans Village site.
- Tunnels to Towers is partnering with elected officials and Home Depot to develop a new facility in Mableton.
- The Tunnels to Towers project will offer safe and secure housing to more than 90 Atlanta veterans from across metro Atlanta.
- The future Veterans Village will be located on the former Wingate Hotel site.
- The Veterans Village will feature livable apartments equipped with a bedroom, living room, kitchen, and bathroom.
- Veterans at the new facility will have access to a community gym, business center, cafeteria, commercial kitchen space, and 24/7 support services.
- Mayor Michael Owens is leading a vision of revitalizing major corridors including Veterans Memorial Highway, Mableton Parkway, and Riverside Drive to attract new businesses and bolster existing ones.
- The Mableton 2045 Comprehensive Plan serves as a blueprint for sustainable growth over the next two decades.
- The comprehensive plan covers land use planning, transportation, housing, economic development, parks and recreation, environmental sustainability, and community facilities and services.
- In the next five years, Mableton plans to further develop commercial districts, enhance public transportation options, and invest in sustainable development projects.
- Over 10 years, Mableton's vision includes becoming a regional hub for innovation and culture with thriving business sectors, green spaces, and robust community engagement.
