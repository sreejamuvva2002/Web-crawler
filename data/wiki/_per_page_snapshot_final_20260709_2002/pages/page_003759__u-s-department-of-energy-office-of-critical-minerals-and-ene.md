# U.S. Department of Energy Office of Critical Minerals and Energy Innovation

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [DOE’s Office of Critical Minerals and Energy Innovation Announces $134 Million To Bolster Rare Earth Element Supply Chains | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/cmei/articles/does-office-critical-minerals-and-energy-innovation-announces-134-million-bolster)  ·  `page_003759`

## Facts

- The U.S. Department of Energy’s Office of Critical Minerals and Energy Innovation announced $134 million for two projects to strengthen domestic rare earth element supply chains.
- The announcement was made on June 2, 2026.
- The funding aims to reduce reliance on foreign sources and improve U.S. energy security.
- The selected projects will demonstrate the commercial viability of recovering and refining rare earth elements from unconventional feedstocks, including mine tailings, electronic waste, and other waste materials.
- Assistant Secretary of Energy (EERE) Audrey Robertson stated that achieving energy independence requires finding value in overlooked resources and expanding capabilities to recover rare earth elements from waste products.
- Rare earth elements such as praseodymium, neodymium, terbium, and dysprosium are vital components in advanced manufacturing, defense systems, and high-performance magnets used in power generation and electric motors.
- Colorado School of Mines was selected for award negotiations to design, construct, commission, and operate a rare earth element demonstration facility near the Gramercy alumina refinery in St. John the Baptist Parish, Louisiana.
- The Colorado School of Mines facility will process "red mud," a critical mineral-rich bauxite waste product.
- By separating rare earth oxides and refining them into rare earth metals, the Colorado School of Mines facility will demonstrate the commercial feasibility of an integrated domestic rare earth extraction, separation, and refining process.
- The Colorado School of Mines project partners include ElementUSA, Pacific Northwest National Laboratory, Principal Mineral, and Rare Earth Technologies Inc.
- Phoenix Tailings was selected for award negotiations to design, construct, commission, and operate a demonstration-scale facility to produce high-purity rare earth metals from domestic industrial waste-derived feedstocks.
- The Phoenix Tailings project will establish a new commercial pathway for domestic production of heavy rare earth metals.
- Phoenix Tailings will partner with the Massachusetts Institute of Technology and the University of Minnesota on its project.
- DOE’s Manufacturing Deployment Office manages this funding opportunity through its Rare Earth Elements Demonstration Facility Program.
- The Rare Earth Elements Demonstration Facility Program is designed to demonstrate full-scale integrated rare earth extraction and separation facilities in the United States.
- Selection for award negotiations is not a commitment by DOE to issue an award or provide funding.
- Before funding is issued, DOE and the applicant will undergo a negotiation process, and DOE may cancel negotiations and rescind the selection for any reason during that time.
- DOE award amounts are subject to change pending negotiations.
