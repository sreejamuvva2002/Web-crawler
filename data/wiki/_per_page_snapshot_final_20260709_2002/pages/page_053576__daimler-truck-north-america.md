# Daimler Truck North America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Daimler demonstrates autonomous electric truck potential with Freightliner eCascadia | FleetOwner](https://www.fleetowner.com/technology/article/55038184/daimler-demonstrates-autonomous-electric-truck-potential-with-freightliner-ecascadia)  ·  `page_053576`

## Facts

- Daimler Truck North America has an autonomous eCascadia daycab equipped with Torc Robotics’ self-driving technology and Level 4 autonomous sensors and computing power.
- The OEM showed off the autonomous eCascadia demonstrator during a virtual media presentation Tuesday broadcast from its North American headquarters in Portland, Oregon.
- Torc Robotics is an independent Daimler Truck subsidiary that plans to roll out its diesel-powered AV product to fleets in 2027.
- The new EV-AV demonstrator complements traditional Freightliner Cascadia long-haul sleeper tractors packed with autonomous technology and opens a path for Daimler to adapt Torc technology on future powertrains.
- Joanna Buttler, Daimler Truck’s head of global autonomous technology, stated the company initially focused on diesel configuration for launch but is combining freight efficiency potential of autonomous trucks with zero emissions and decarbonization via series production electric vehicles.
- The ZE-AV combo starts with a battery-electric eCascadia but envisions the same technology fitting with potential hydrogen fuel cell EVs and hydrogen internal combustion engine tractors.
- Daimler is the first legacy North American truck OEM to attach autonomous technology to one of its Class 8 EVs.
- Torc rival Kodiak Robotics pursued an OEM-agnostic approach to self-driving freight, first featuring an all-electric autonomous Class 8 truck a year ago by upfitting a Peterbilt Model 579EV shown at the Advanced Clean Transportation Expo in 2023.
- Autonomous technology provider Aurora has North American partnerships with Volvo Trucks and Paccar’s Kenworth and Peterbilt.
- Daimler Truck plans to show off its demonstration EV-AV at this year’s ACT Expo later this month.
- Since 2022, more than 55 U.S. fleets have integrated eCascadias into their operations, logging more than 6 million miles, according to DTNA.
- The company considers its demonstration autonomous EV a research and advanced engineering project with potential to evolve into a modular, scalable platform that is propulsion-agnostic for flexible use in different trucking applications.
- Autonomous trucking is already proving to be more fuel-efficient than human-controlled trucks, with some AV companies noting fuel savings of up to 17% in testing across Texas and the Southwest.
- John O’Leary, DTNA president and CEO, said combining zero-emission and autonomous technologies tests solutions for future customer challenges and gives them choices to keep the world moving.
- In advance of its 2027 rollout, diesel-powered Freightliner Cascadias with Torc’s AV technology are in tests between Phoenix and Oklahoma City, hauling freight for FleetOwner 500 fleets such as Schneider and C.R. England.
- Daimler expects autonomous trucking to generate more than $3 billion in revenue as early as 2030.
- Current Torc-driven Cascadias work on long-haul, hub-to-hub freight movement, while the autonomous eCascadia would be more applicable for shorter, repeatable routes based around charging infrastructure.
- Daimler hopes to find synergies between ZE and AV infrastructure, studying routes and locations that suit both needs, with future possibilities including combining charging infrastructure with autonomous freight hubs so an electric AV can charge while loaded and unloaded.
- Truck charging venture Greenlane, created by DTNA, NextEra Energy Resources, and Blackrock, is building out infrastructure in California with plans to expand west toward Texas and the Northeast.
- Greenlane aims to develop a nationwide network of commercial charging and hydrogen infrastructure locations across the U.S., serving battery-electric passenger car and light-duty fleet customers while providing hydrogen refueling for commercial vehicles in the coming years.
- Suman Narayanan, DTNA’s chief vehicle engineer for autonomous driving, said the demonstrator will help set up Daimler Truck for any future propulsion technology, noting similarities between fuel cell propulsion and battery-electric vehicles to aid transition to hydrogen fuel cells or internal combustion engines.
