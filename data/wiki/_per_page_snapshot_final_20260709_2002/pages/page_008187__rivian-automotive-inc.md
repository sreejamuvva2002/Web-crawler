# Rivian Automotive Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-04-20

**Source:** [Rivian increases Georgia plant capacity to 300,000 vehicles By Investing.com](https://www.investing.com/news/company-news/rivian-increases-georgia-plant-capacity-to-300000-vehicles-93CH-4651185)  ·  `page_008187`

## Facts

- Rivian Automotive Inc. announced it will increase initial production capacity at its planned Georgia manufacturing facility to 300,000 vehicles annually.
- The increased capacity is up from the previously planned 200,000 units.
- Rivian is valued at a market cap of $19.93 billion as it works to scale production and improve unit economics.
- The expanded capacity at the Stanton Springs North facility represents a 50 percent increase aimed at reducing per-unit costs.
- The design allows for additional capacity expansion in later phases.
- Rivian has updated its loan agreement with the U.S. Department of Energy’s Office of Energy Dominance Financing.
- The total DOE loan stands at up to $4.5 billion, comprising $4,006 million in principal and $494 million in capitalized interest for the initial phase.
- The company expects to begin drawing on the loan in early 2027 as vertical construction commences in 2026.
- Vehicle production at the facility remains scheduled to begin in late 2028.
- Rivian Founder and CEO RJ Scaringe said, "We’re very excited to partner with the U.S. Department of Energy to grow our manufacturing footprint in Georgia."
- RJ Scaringe added, "R2 dramatically expands our market opportunity."
- Vertical construction is expected to start this spring, with preparations underway for the stamping press area.
- Construction progress is expected to accelerate during the summer months as primary buildings take shape.
- Rivian previously announced a partnership with Uber to build up to 50,000 robotaxis at the Georgia plant beginning in late 2028.
