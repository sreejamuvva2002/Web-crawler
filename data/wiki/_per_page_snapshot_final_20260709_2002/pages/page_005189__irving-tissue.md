# Irving Tissue

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2024-11-21

**Source:** [Irving Tissue to Expand, Invest $600 Million | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-releases/2024/irving-tissue-expand-invest-600-million)  ·  `page_005189`

## Facts

- Irving Tissue announced an expansion at its Macon facility involving nearly $600 million in investment.
- The expansion will add at least 100 new jobs.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced the expansion on November 21, 2024.
- The announcement was celebrated during Irving Tissue’s fifth anniversary celebration in Macon.
- Since choosing Macon in 2017, Irving Tissue has invested around $1.5 billion into the community.
- Irving Tissue has created 400 well-paying jobs since 2017, with another 100 jobs on the way.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Georgia is the No. 1 state for business and emphasized ongoing relationships with job creators.
- Irving Tissue’s current facility is located at 1897 Allen Road in the Sofkee Industrial Park.
- The facility employs more than 400 people.
- The facility produces ultra-premium quality household paper products, including soft bath tissue and high-quality paper towel.
- Irving Tissue President Robert K. Irving expressed appreciation for the State of Georgia’s commitment to economic prosperity, jobs, and growth.
- Robert Irving credited the state’s drive to promote advantages and foster a positive business environment for making the investment possible.
- Robert Irving highlighted the highly skilled workforce in the area and continued support from employees, customers, and suppliers as key factors enabling the expansion.
- The expansion includes a third ThruAir Dry paper making machine.
- The expansion includes additional converting lines.
- The expansion includes a new, fully automated warehouse.
- The announcement will increase Irving Tissue’s annual ThruAir Dry capacity by 75,000 tonnes.
- The capacity increase is equivalent to 15 million cases.
- The total annual capacity at the Macon facility will reach 225,000 tonnes.
- The total capacity is equivalent to 45 million cases.
- Irving Tissue will be hiring for management, supervisory, and machinist positions.
- Macon-Bibb County Mayor Lester Miller stated that Irving has been a true member of the community with a deep commitment to its people.
- Lester Miller noted that the expansion speaks volumes about the strong foundation Irving Tissue has built in Macon.
- Chairman Robby Fountain of the Macon-Bibb County Industrial Authority (MBCIA) described working with Irving over the past five years as an incredible journey.
- Robby Fountain stated that Irving Tissue became a valued part of the community and its growth is a testament to hard work, dedication, and strong local connections.
- Regional Project Manager Candice Scott represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project in partnership with MBCIA.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Irving Tissue has demonstrated what it means to become part of the community fabric over the last five years.
- Pat Wilson noted that each expansion builds on partnerships and collaboration supported by the state’s business-friendly environment and world-class logistics infrastructure.
- The logistics infrastructure will connect Irving’s Macon facility to markets across the East Coast.
- Irving Tissue produces premium household store brand paper products for many of North America's top retailers.
- Irving Tissue also produces some of the top-selling tissue brands in the marketplace.
- Irving Tissue is part of Irving Consumer Products, one of North America's leading manufacturers of household paper and baby diaper products.
