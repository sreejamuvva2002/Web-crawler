# Toyota Industries Electric Systems North America, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp: Toyota Industries Corporation Expands Georgia Presence, Creating 250 New Jobs](https://www.einpresswire.com/article/668536703/gov-kemp-toyota-industries-corporation-expands-georgia-presence-creating-250-new-jobs)  ·  `page_012368`

## Facts

- Toyota Industries Electric Systems North America, Inc. (TIESNA) will create more than 250 new jobs.
- TIESNA is expanding its campus in Pendergrass with a new manufacturing facility valued at $69 million in investment.
- Japanese Consul General Mio Maeda and local representatives joined Governor Brian Kemp for the ground-breaking announcement on November 14.
- TIESNA is a subsidiary of Toyota Industries, which is headquartered in Japan.
- Toyota Industries develops and manufactures automobile-related products, including car air-conditioning compressors and car electronics components for worldwide carmakers.
- The Toyota Industries Group has operated in Pendergrass since 2004, starting with TD Automotive Compressor Georgia, LLC (TACG).
- TACG was followed by Toyota Industries Compressor Parts America, Co. (TICA) in 2012.
- TACG and TICA currently support more than 400 jobs in Jackson County.
- TIESNA’s new facility will be located at the 1700 Valentine Industrial Parkway campus in Pendergrass.
- The new facility will produce DC-to-DC converters, which convert high voltage to low voltage to supply power to auxiliary equipment.
- Operations at the new facility are expected to begin in 2025.
- Jackson County Commission Chair Tom Crow welcomed TIESNA as Toyota Industries' third related company to Jackson County.
- Tom Crow noted TACG and TICA have been outstanding corporate citizens and pledged support for TIESNA's success.
- Griff Lynch, President and CEO of the Georgia Ports Authority, highlighted TIESNA’s expansion as part of Georgia’s growing automaking sector delivering career opportunities.
- Senior Regional Project Manager Kristen Miller represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on the project.
- The project was executed in partnership with Jackson County Area Chamber of Commerce, Georgia Power, the Georgia Ports Authority, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson noted that maintaining 50 years of representation in Japan allows direct communication with corporate HQs like Toyota Industries to give Georgia a competitive advantage for expansions.
- In fiscal year 2023, job creation in Georgia’s automotive industry increased by 324 percent compared to fiscal year 2021.
- Suppliers attracted by Georgia’s original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) resulted in over $2 billion in investment across the state in FY23.
- Toyota Industries Corporation was established in 1926 in Japan and currently holds more than 280 consolidated subsidiaries worldwide.
- TICO holds the largest world market share in air-jet looms, car air conditioning compressors, and lift trucks.
- Toyota Industries Electric Systems North America, Inc. was established in 2013 in Novi, Michigan as a sales company for automotive electronics components like AC inverters, voltage stabilizers, and motor controllers.
- TIESNA’s customers include major OEMs in North America.
