# Archer Aviation Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Archer and United Airlines announce first commercial electric air taxi route in the U.S. - Vertical Mag](https://verticalmag.com/press-releases/archer-and-united-airlines-announce-first-commercial-electric-air-taxi-route-in-the-us/)  ·  `page_016967`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation Inc. and United Airlines have announced their first airport to city center route between United’s hub at Newark Liberty International Airport (EWR) and New York City.
- The Downtown Manhattan Heliport, located just above Battery Park on Pier 6 has been confirmed as the first planned takeoff and landing site for Archer’s broader plan to launch an Urban Air Mobility (UAM) network across the New York Metropolitan Area.
- The goal of Archer’s electric air taxi network is to provide passengers with safe, quick, quiet and cost-effective transportation to and from EWR.
- Archer’s proposed Manhattan to EWR route marks the first specific route that has been announced in the eVTOL industry.
- United’s hub at EWR is ideally located to serve Manhattan and its surrounding boroughs, as well as communities in New Jersey.
- Archer estimates the journey from the Downtown Manhattan Heliport to EWR will take less than 10 minutes via its all-electric Midnight aircraft, a low noise, sustainable and safe alternative to helicopters.
- A similar journey by car can take upwards of an hour or more during rush hour traffic.
- Adam Goldstein, Archer’s Founder and CEO, stated they are excited to confirm New York as the first of many routes they will announce alongside United as they work to build out their national UAM network.
- Goldstein expressed looking forward to continuing collaboration with United and working closely with state and local government leaders in the New York and New Jersey area to bring this new form of transportation to life.
- Michael Leskinen, President of United Airlines Ventures, said electric air taxis will fundamentally change how United customers experience comfort, convenience, and efficiency during commutes to the airport.
- Leskinen noted that Archer’s New York to Newark route marks another important benchmark towards providing a carbon-neutral and safe commute that drastically reduces travel time and produces a fraction of the sound emitted by helicopters.
- Archer’s early launch routes will provide airport to city center transportation service, which it refers to as “trunk” routes.
- Once primary trunk routes are established, Archer will begin building out “branch” routes to connect to surrounding communities.
- The first phase of this UAM network is targeted for launch in 2025.
