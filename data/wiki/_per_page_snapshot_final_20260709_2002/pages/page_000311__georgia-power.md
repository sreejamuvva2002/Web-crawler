# Georgia Power

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** charging_infrastructure  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- Georgia is among the top ten largest markets in the nation for Electric Vehicle (EV) charging.
- By 2030, it is predicted that 50% of all new car sales will be electric vehicles.
- Georgia Power is committed to connecting communities with charging infrastructure at home and in public spaces so drivers can explore the state with range confidence.
- As the number of electric vehicles on the road increases, Georgia Power is committed to increasing the availability of electric vehicle charging stations.
- Georgia Power customers may be eligible to receive up to a $150 rebate for installing a Level 2 Charger in their home.
- The rebate is available while funds last and the amount is subject to change.
- Shopping for Level 2 Chargers on the Georgia Power Marketplace provides an instant rebate of $150.
- Electric vehicles provide quiet, clean, and efficient transportation while offering savings on fuel and maintenance costs and reducing environmental emissions.
- All electric and plug-in hybrid cars purchased in or after 2010 may be eligible for a federal tax credit.
- Charging an electric vehicle can cost as little as $0 when using a standard 120-volt circuit available in every home.
- Georgia Power recommends using a dedicated 120-volt outlet to avoid overloading the circuit.
- Higher-powered chargers are available for faster charging but may require a permit for home installation due to higher voltage requirements.
