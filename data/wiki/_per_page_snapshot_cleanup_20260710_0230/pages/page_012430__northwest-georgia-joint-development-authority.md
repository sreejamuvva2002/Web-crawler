# Northwest Georgia Joint Development Authority

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Northwest Georgia JDA - News & Events - Press Releases](https://northwestgeorgia.us/news-events-press-releases.php)  ·  `page_012430`

## Facts

- GE Appliances invests $180M in its Roper Corp. plant in LaFayette, adding 600+ jobs, boosting automation, and launching new products.
- GNTC’s Automation Engineering Technology program prepares students to design, implement and troubleshoot automated industrial processes on robotic equipment and other automated systems; it is the only engineering-based automation program in Georgia and features industry-standard equipment.
- Port activity in Georgia supports approximately 40,200 jobs in Northwest Georgia (Catoosa County: 1,901; Chattooga County: 670; Dade County: 595; Walker County: 2,294) and over 600,000 full- and part-time jobs across the state, per a UGA Terry College of Business study.
- Walker County issued 352 permits for new single-family home construction in 2023 (a nearly 148% increase over 2022), with 91 permits issued so far that year, indicating momentum behind construction.
- Dade Industrial Development Authority was awarded a $31,500 due diligence grant from the OneGeorgia Authority’s Rural Site Development Initiative to assist with costs for seeking GRAD certification for the Dade Industrial Park.
- Walker County Industrial Development Authority applied for two state grants: a Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) grant covering 70% of the cost for Trenton-Dade Business Park certification, and a $250,000 grant for site preparation at an additional county site.
- Shaw Industries Group is investing $90 million in Plant RP in Ringgold, Ga., to expand domestic resilient flooring manufacturing capabilities, more than doubling the facility’s production capacity by 2026.
- Dalton State College, Georgia Highlands College, and GNTC launched the year-long Northwest Georgia Leadership and Development Program (NWGA LEAD) for emerging and mid-level leaders.
- GNTC partnered with Miura America to launch a paid training program for steam solutions and energy provider trade skills.
- Evan Stone, executive director of the Dade County Industrial Development Authority, announced a local industrial summit for Oct. 24 targeting CEOs, CFOs, and HR managers from county industries.
- GNTC was named Georgia’s first NC3 Leadership School and awarded approximately 1,200 certificates through the National Coalition of Certification Centers to train skilled workers.
- Walker County released a draft Digital Connectivity Plan inventorying ISPs, speeds, affordability, and underserved areas to attain Broadband Ready Community status.
- Trenton Pressing opened a new $12 million facility adding over 100,000 square feet for operations making parts for BMW and golf cart producer Club Car.
- Georgia earned the No. 1 spot in Site Selection’s annual Prosperity Cup rankings for successful investment recruitment in 2023.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp signed legislation establishing a high-demand career list, changing REACH scholarship funding, simplifying accelerated career pathways, increasing apprenticeships under the High Demand Apprenticeship Program, and creating programs to assist state agencies with hiring/retention of qualified individuals with disabilities.
- Blake Elsberry, sole commissioner of Chattooga County, highlighted the growing partnership with GNTC in healthcare and other career fields for workforce development.
- Connect Humanity awarded $30,000 to Walker County to participate in the Appalachian Digital Accelerator initiative for custom internet connectivity plans.
- A commission reported $9.82 billion in new investment and expansion with 7,838 new jobs announced in Northwest Georgia over the past decade.
- Apex PVC owner David Rogers is purchasing the old SHOWA glove mill in Menlo to produce polymer products for toys, furniture, and tools, aiming to employ locals familiar with the former plant (which laid off 80 people in July 2022).
- Southeast Lineman Training Center (SLTC) in Trenton plans to expand its campus; established in 1999, it trains over 1,000 electrical and communications linemen annually.
- Governor Kemp announced changes to the College Career Ready Performance Index (CCRPI) to incentivize high schools to adopt high-demand career pathways aligned with DOE and TCSG definitions for 2025.
- The Technical College System of Georgia approved a grant exceeding $3 million for Walker Launch, a collaborative college career academy between Walker County Schools and GNTC benefiting students in Walker and Dade counties.
- TCSG launched an online Labor Exchange Platform connecting job seekers and employers across Georgia as part of WorkSource Georgia.
- GNTC hosted a ribbon-cutting for new Mobile Training Labs on Oct. 16, offering self-contained courses in automation, industrial maintenance, PLCs, robotics, and welding.
- Truist Financial Corporation granted $40,000 to GNTC’s Adult Education program; Lisa Shaw (VP of Adult Education at GNTC) noted it enables "Training on the Go" sessions for industry credentials.
- Georgia ranked No. 1 state for business for the 10th consecutive year per Area Development magazine, announced by Governor Kemp and GDEcD.
- A $10.3 million public-private partnership with broadband provider Kinetic will provide high-speed fiber internet to approximately 3,400 homes and businesses in Chattooga County (Summerville, Trion, Lyerly, Menlo).
- Employees at a northwest Georgia concrete supplier are receiving CDL apprenticeship training at GNTC through a new grant program.
- GNTC received NACEP accreditation for its concurrent enrollment and college-provided faculty models, making it the third institution in Georgia and one of three nationally to hold both endorsements.
- The Northwest Georgia Joint Development Authority and Top of Georgia Economic Development Initiative hosted a regional economic summit attended by over 125 leaders from Catoosa, Chattooga, Dade, and Walker counties to share recruitment strategies and resources.
- Walker County school district is surveying the Northwest Georgia business community to identify professional skills needed by the next generation of workers.
- Trenton Pressing is investing $10 million to build a 105,000-square-foot facility on 12 acres next to its existing factory for making parts for BMW and Club Car; company president and CEO is Mike Pfunder.
- Unemployment rates in February 2023 were reported at 2.9% in Dade County, 3.2% in Walker County, and 2.8% in Catoosa County.
- Approximately 50 local education, business, and industry leaders attended the first Northwest Georgia Apprenticeship Summit at GNTC’s Whitfield Murray Campus to discuss workforce investment.
- GNTC students won five gold medals and one silver medal at the 2023 SkillsUSA Georgia competition in areas including CNC programming, industrial motor controls, and electrical construction wiring.
- A development group is working on two larger industrial sites in Catoosa County off I-75, aiming to attract over $100 million in new industrial development and more than 700 jobs, following the recruitment of Volkswagen supplier Sesé Industrial Services and container company Pods to a nearby Chattanooga park.
- Approximately 100 employees at area manufacturers have participated in an apprenticeship program at GNTC since its inception in 2017, according to John Gentry (Curriculum and Faculty Credential).
