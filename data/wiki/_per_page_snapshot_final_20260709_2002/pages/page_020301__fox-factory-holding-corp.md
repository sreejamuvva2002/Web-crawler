# Fox Factory Holding Corp.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Fox Factory Investing $50M In Georgia - Corporate Headquarters - Business Facilities Magazine](https://businessfacilities.com/fox-factory-investing-50m-hall-county-georgia/)  ·  `page_020301`

## Facts

- Fox Factory Holding Corp. is a designer and manufacturer of performance-defining, ride dynamics products primarily for bicycles and powered vehicles.
- The company expects to create up to 800 jobs and invest up to $50 million in new Georgia facilities, including the relocation of its corporate headquarters.
- The project will utilize a 23-acre site to diversify the company’s manufacturing platform and provide additional long-term capacity to support growth in its Powered Vehicles Group.
- CEO Larry L. Enterline announced the new state-of-the-art campus in Hall County, Georgia, stating it will allow the addition of an additional world-class manufacturing operation for the Powered Vehicles Group that they intend to be ISO9001 and IATF 16949 certified.
- The new facility will increase the company’s ability to better serve its OEM customers and on-road aftermarket business.
- Fox Factory Holding Corp. is expected to relocate its corporate headquarters from Scotts Valley, CA to its existing offices in Georgia by the end of 2018.
- The first phase of the Hall County project is expected to be completed in early 2020.
- When complete, the new facility will be used for manufacturing, warehousing, distribution and office space.
- Gov. Nathan Deal stated that Georgia provides the business environment and skilled workforce for innovative companies like Fox Factory Holding Corp. to enjoy long-term success and compete worldwide.
- Gov. Nathan Deal noted that by relocating its headquarters to Georgia and expanding in Hall County, the company will continue to enjoy the benefits of being the No. 1 state for business, including expansive logistics infrastructure and a low-tax climate conducive to manufacturing.
- Gov. Nathan Deal added that Fox Factory Holding Corp.’s investment will produce meaningful results for Georgia and create even more opportunities in the Hall County community.
- GDEcD Director of Advanced Manufacturing Mike Grundmann represented the Global Commerce Division in partnership with the Greater Hall Chamber of Commerce (GHCC) and Georgia EMC.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that the decision to locate in Hall County speaks volumes about the quality of the workforce and business-friendly environment, noting Georgia’s business assets make it the perfect home for manufacturers and expressing excitement about the many job opportunities being created for citizens.
