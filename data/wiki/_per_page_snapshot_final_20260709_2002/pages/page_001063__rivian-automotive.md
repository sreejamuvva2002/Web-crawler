## Facts

- Rivian operates a plant in Normal, Illinois, formerly owned by Mitsubishi Motors North America.
- Rivian produces the five-passenger pickup R1T EV (2021-), seven-passenger SUV R1S EV (2022-), and five-passenger SUV R2 EV (2026-).
- Amazon is a customer for EDV 700 (2022-), EDV 500 (2023-), and EDV 900 (2023-) electric delivery vehicles.
- Production of Enduro electric drive units began on February 3, 2023 in a new building on the south side of the Normal plant.
- Rivian is planning a new plant in Georgia for EV R2, EV R3 and EV R3X vehicles.
- Production at the Georgia plant is scheduled to begin in 2028.
- New plans revealed on April 30 are for an initial 300,000 unit capacity as production starts in 2028.
- The Georgia plant project is backed by a reduced DOE loan of USD 4.5 billion.
- Construction at the Georgia plant started in September 2025.
- With USD 6.6 billion DOE loan, plans are back on for production beginning in 2028.
- Rivian announced a temporary halt to the Georgia plant project.
- Rivian produced 57,000 units in 2023, up 135% year-over-year.
- Rivian's sales surged but business losses continued in 2023.
- Rivian achieved production and sales of over 20,000 units of three commercial vehicle models in 2022.
- Rivian's U.S. sales increased 1,032.5% to 2,854 units in December 2022.
- Rivian's total U.S. sales for all of 2022 increased 3,381.5% to 20,332 units.
- Rivian's U.S. sales increased 71.3% to 4,890 units in December 2023.
- Rivian's total U.S. sales for all of 2023 increased 146.5% to 50,122 units.
- Rivian Automotive began production and delivery of pickups and SUVs in 2021.
- Rivian began delivering pickups from June 2021, followed by deliveries of SUVs.
- Rivian purchased a U.S. plant formerly owned by Mitsubishi Motors.
- Rivian's total U.S. new car sales for 2024 were 53,451 units with a 6.6% share.
- Volkswagen Group formed a partnership with Rivian and XPeng for SDV Development.
- On June 27, Rivian CEO RJ Scaringe highlighted at an Investor Day that the company’s vertical integration means Rivian owns the electronics in the vehicle and the software stack.
- Rivian's focus on autonomy will see the company control the full stack including camera sets and radar sets.
- The Normal, Illinois plant was purchased from Mitsubishi in 2017 for USD 16 million.
- Pilot-build vehicles were reported to go down the assembly line at Rivian’s Normal plant since the beginning of 2020.
- Component pilot production for its own pickup truck began prior to 2020, as evidenced by images of stamped door surrounds tweeted by Rivian in July 2019.
- On July 21, Rivian started adding Tesla/NACS cables to its Rivian Adventure Network (RAN).
- The updated RAN enables Tesla vehicles and other brands with NACS ports to charge on Rivian’s charging network.
- Rivian has started adopting NACS charge ports on its 2026 R1T and R1S vehicles.
- NACS charge ports will be made standard on the upcoming R2.
- Rivian is in the process of scaling up its charging network.
- On May 12, Rivian introduced Rivian Assistant and Rivian Unified Intelligence to unlock hands-free utility with Connect+.
- The AI-powered Rivian Assistant will roll out to all compatible Rivian Gen 1 and Gen 2 owners who subscribe to Connect+ or are in an active trial.
- Rivian has created an AI system with the ability to think, predict, and grow more capable over time.
- Rivian's total U.S. new car sales for 2025 were 45,489 units.
