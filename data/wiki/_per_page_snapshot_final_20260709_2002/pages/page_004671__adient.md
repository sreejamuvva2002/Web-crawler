# Adient

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2022-08-05

**Source:** [Adient reports third quarter 2022 financial results](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/adient-reports-third-quarter-2022-financial-results-301600587.html)  ·  `page_004671`

## Facts

- Adient is a global leader in automotive seating.
- Adient has approximately 75,000 employees in 33 countries.
- Adient operates 208 manufacturing/assembly plants worldwide.
- Adient produces and delivers automotive seating for all major OEMs.
- Adient's product range spans from complete seating systems to individual components.
- Adient's expertise covers every step of the automotive seat-making process, from research and design to engineering and manufacturing.
- Adient supplies its products into more than 20 million vehicles every year.
- Q3 2022 GAAP net loss was $(30)M with diluted EPS of $(0.32).
- Q3 2022 adjusted diluted EPS was $0.08.
- Q3 2022 adjusted EBITDA was $143M, up $25M year-over-year (up $18M when adjusting for portfolio actions executed in FY21).
- Adient held strong cash and cash equivalents of $892M at June 30, 2022.
- Total liquidity was approximately $1.7B at June 30, 2022.
- Gross debt totaled approximately $2.7B and net debt totaled approximately $1.8B at June 30, 2022.
- Douglas Del Grosso serves as Adient's president and chief executive officer.
- Jeff Stafeil serves as Adient's executive vice president and chief financial officer.
- Adient cites the Ukraine conflict and widespread COVID lockdowns in China as impacting regional/global economies, supply chains, and vehicle production.
- Adient identifies semiconductor availability, energy and commodity prices (particularly steel), and raw material access as key supply chain risks.
