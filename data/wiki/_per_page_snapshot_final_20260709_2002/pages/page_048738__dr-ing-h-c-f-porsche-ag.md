# Dr. Ing. h.c. F. Porsche AG

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2014-05

**Source:** [Porsche continues to create jobs and now employs over 20,000 individuals - FINCHANNEL](https://finchannel.com/porsche-continues-to-create-jobs-and-now-employs-over-20000-individuals/38020/oil-auto/2014/05/)  ·  `page_048738`

## Facts

- Dr. Ing. h.c. F. Porsche AG stepped up its deliveries, revenue, and operating profit in the first three months of 2014.
- Deliveries reached 38,663 vehicles in Q1 2014, beating the previous year by 4.5 percent.
- Revenue rose by one-fifth to 3.93 billion euro in the first quarter of the fiscal year.
- Operating profit grew by 22 percent to 698 million euro.
- The number of jobs exceeded the threshold of 20,000 employees in February 2014, according to Porsche Cars North America, Inc.
- Lutz Meschke, Member of the Executive Board Finance and IT of Porsche AG, underscored a high return on sales of almost 18 percent.
- Porsche will face substantially rising labor costs and write-offs due to the launch of the new Macan model range.
- High expenditures are required for meeting CO2 requirements and substantial investments in the build-out and modernization of the Zuffenhausen, Leipzig, and Weissach locations.
- Lutz Meschke expressed optimism for the 2014 fiscal year, aiming for at least a profit comparable to the previous year, assuming European sales stabilize despite unresolved structural problems.
- Matthias Müller, Chairman of the Executive Board of Porsche AG, stated that substantial investments and development expenditures will each amount to significantly more than one billion euro in the ongoing fiscal year.
- The company's strategy focuses on offering sportiest and technologically cutting-edge vehicles long-term, with improved fuel efficiency and responsible resource use as integral parts.
- Porsche CEO Matthias Müller expects sales to rise again in the 2014 fiscal year, particularly in the United States and China markets.
- The market launch of the Macan will provide an additional boost to overall sales.
- The number of employees reached 20,416 as of March 31, 2014, representing a 15 percent increase compared to a year ago.
- The employee count increased by 960 in the first quarter of 2014 alone.
- Staff at the Leipzig plant showed the greatest growth, where the new Macan is being produced.
- New jobs were created at other Porsche locations as well, according to Porsche Cars North America, Inc.
