# Hanon Systems

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** thermal_management  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hanon Systems Develops the World’s First 4th-Generation Heat Pump System for Electric Vehicles, “Improving Driving Range with Parallel Heat Source Recovery Method” < Tech & Bio < Article - AVING NEWS](https://us.aving.net/news/articleView.html?idxno=51088)  ·  `page_055705`

## Facts

- Hanon Systems announced on the 8th that it had developed the world’s first 4th-generation heat pump system for electric vehicles, which has been commercialized since July.
- Hanon Systems’ 4th-generation heat pump system is the first to use a parallel heat source recovery method that utilizes external air heat and waste heat from motors and batteries.
- The recovered heat is used for heating, cooling, and battery temperature management, contributing to improving electric vehicle driving range.
- This modular heat pump system incorporates several new patents, securing a technological advantage.
- It features an integrated control system that manages the refrigerant control module (RCM) and the coolant control module (CCM), enabling comprehensive management of the entire thermal management system.
- Nagasubramony Ramachandran, co-CEO of Hanon Systems, stated, “Hanon Systems is the first in the world to develop a 4th-generation heat pump system, contributing to improvements in driving range. As the global second-largest company in thermal management, we continue to aim for ‘lightweight’ and ‘high-efficiency’ parts and tirelessly develop new technologies.”
- Hanon Systems first mass-produced a waste heat recovery type heat pump system in 2015.
- Its second generation, mass-produced in 2018, added battery thermal management features and contributed to increased electric vehicle range as the first integrated thermal management system.
- The third generation, developed in 2021, was the first to be applied to an 800V electric vehicle platform.
- The fourth-generation heat pump system was first applied to Kia’s EV3.
- Production will start in Korea, and the company plans to expand its manufacturing bases to the US and Europe.
- Wang Yun-ho, Vice President of the AP Innovation Center at Hanon Systems Research Institute, said, “We were able to develop the world’s first new technology proactively through close collaboration with our clients, and I am pleased that it contributes to enhancing the driving experience of electric vehicle users.”
- Moreover, Hanon Systems has also applied the world’s first Thin HVAC technology.
- Thin HVAC means a ‘reduced’ heating, ventilation, and air conditioning system.
- Reducing the overall size of the HVAC system by about 30% minimizes the exposure of the HVAC system area under the cockpit (dashboard module), providing more legroom for passengers and a comfortable and quiet driving experience with low power consumption and low noise design.
