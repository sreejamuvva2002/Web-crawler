# Kia

## Facts

- The Vehicle Interest Deduction allows eligible buyers to deduct up to $10,000 in interest paid on an auto loan for qualifying new vehicles.
- This federal tax benefit runs from 2025 through 2028.
- It was created to encourage the purchase of vehicles assembled in the United States by offering a substantial tax break on financing interest.
- To qualify, the vehicle must be a new 2025 model year or newer.
- The vehicle must be financed with a loan rather than leased or paid in full with cash.
- The vehicle must be used for personal use rather than business use.
- The vehicle's final assembly must have occurred in the United States.
- Buyers must have a Modified Adjusted Gross Income (MAGI) under $100,000 if filing single or $200,000 if filing jointly to qualify fully.
- The deduction phases out above these MAGI thresholds.
- Only Kia models with final assembly in the U.S. qualify for the Vehicle Interest Deduction.
- The Kia Telluride, Kia Carnival, Kia Seltos, and the 2023+ Kia Sportage are all assembled at Kia’s manufacturing facility in West Point, Georgia.
- These Kia models meet the final assembly rule and are eligible for the interest deduction assuming other personal criteria are met.
- Buyers financing one of these models could benefit from up to $10,000 in deductible interest on their federal tax return.
- When financing a vehicle, buyers must keep all loan paperwork and report the VIN when filing taxes.
- Buyers should track interest payments throughout the year and work with a qualified tax advisor to ensure MAGI eligibility and properly claim the deduction.
- Battleground Kia is located in Greensboro and assists customers in finding U.S.-assembled Kias, confirming eligibility, and guiding them through financing.
