# Archer Aviation Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-01-28

**Source:** [Archer Aviation: Unlocking the Skies of Urban Air Mobility | Investing.com Canada](https://ca.investing.com/analysis/archer-aviation-unlocking-the-skies-of-urban-air-mobility-200613155)  ·  `page_001666`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation Inc. is designing and developing a fully electric vertical takeoff and landing (eVTOL) aircraft for use in direct-to-consumer Urban Air Mobility (UAM) networks.
- The company plans to begin production in 2025.
- The Midnight aircraft is designed to carry one pilot and four passengers.
- Midnight uses 12 electric propellers, including six tilt propellers for vertical takeoff and forward flight transitions, and six fixed propellers dedicated to takeoff and landing.
- The aircraft is powered by six independent battery packs to provide redundancy and reduce single-point failure risk.
- Archer is exploring advanced technologies like solid-state batteries to potentially double energy density and extend the aircraft's range.
- Midnight can reach speeds of up to 150 mph (240 km/h) with a range of 100 miles.
- Archer must obtain three critical FAA certifications: type, production, and operational.
- The company has already secured Part 135 Air Carrier and Operator certificates, as well as the Part 145 Maintenance and Repair certificate.
- Archer leverages components from established suppliers like Honeywell, Safran, and Garmin that are already certified for existing aircraft to reduce regulatory risks.
- Archer has completed a 400,000-square-foot manufacturing facility in Georgia to support certification and production goals.
- The Georgia facility is designed to handle the scale of production needed to meet anticipated demand.
- Archer plans to produce two aircraft per month by the end of 2025.
- Production will eventually scale to 650 units annually by 2030.
- United Airlines placed a $10 million deposit for 100 Midnight aircraft, with options for an additional 200 units.
- Archer and United Airlines plan to launch the first commercial air taxi route in Chicago.
- In Abu Dhabi, Archer was selected as the primary eVTOL supplier for the Emirate's air taxi plans.
- The Abu Dhabi partnership is supported by a multi-hundred-million-dollar investment from the Abu Dhabi Investment Office.
- Commercial services in Abu Dhabi could begin as early as Q4 2025.
- Archer formed a joint venture with Sumitomo Corporation to expand into Japan.
- The Japan agreement could result in up to $500 million in aircraft orders.
- These deals bring Archer's indicative order book to over $6 billion.
- Archer secured a strategic collaboration with Anduril Industries to develop hybrid-propulsion eVTOLs for military applications, including a potential program with the Department of Defense.
- Last year alone, Archer incurred over $450 million in expenses, resulting in a negative free cash flow (FCF) of $320 million.
- For the first nine months of 2024, FCF reached a negative $322 million.
- As of last quarter, Archer had just over $500 million in cash.
- The company recently raised $430 million in equity capital through key partners such as Stellantis, United Airlines, and the Abu Dhabi Investment Office.
