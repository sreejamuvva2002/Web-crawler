# Aspen Aerogels Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** thermal_management  ·  **Published:** 2024-10-16

**Source:** [US to Loan EV Battery Parts Maker $671M for Georgia Plant - TT](https://www.ttnews.com/articles/us-loan-aspen-aerogels)  ·  `page_052648`

## Facts

- The Biden administration is poised to loan nearly $671 million to Aspen Aerogels Inc.
- The funding is intended to make a key component of electric car batteries in the election battleground state of Georgia.
- The conditional financing was announced Oct. 16 by the U.S. Energy Department.
- The funding will finance construction of a new manufacturing plant producing thermal barriers that help prevent battery fires.
- The project is part of President Joe Biden’s push to build a domestic supply chain for electric vehicles and the advanced batteries that power them.
- Jigar Shah, director of the Energy Department Loan Programs Office, stated: “We are making sure that the supply side is made here in this country.”
- Jigar Shah added: “We have taken very seriously the onshoring of the entire supply chain.”
- Lithium-ion battery cells can overheat and cause adjacent cells to do the same, leading to a fire.
- Aspen Aerogels is based in Massachusetts.
- Aspen has contracts to supply its thermal barriers to General Motors Co., Toyota Motor Corp., and other automakers.
- The new plant will be located in Register, near Savannah, Georgia.
- The facility is expected to supply enough thermal barriers for over 2 million electric vehicles annually.
- Aspen has invested $300 million into the approximately $1 billion facility.
- CEO Donald Young stated that Aspen plans to complete construction by late 2026 or early 2027.
- The funding comes from the Energy Department’s Advanced Technology Vehicles Manufacturing Loan Program.
- CEO Donald Young noted that the funding was critical for the relatively small and fast-growing company.
- Donald Young stated: “It’s a little hard to imagine how we’d do this without the support of the LPO program.”
- Donald Young added: “Having access to this level of capital would be challenging for us.”
