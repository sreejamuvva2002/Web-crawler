# Made in Georgia: the EV Energy Supply Chain roundtable

**Type:** event  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2025-02-19

**Source:** [It's electric! EV industry event at Savannah Tech asserts Georgia's role in global market](https://www.yahoo.com/news/electric-ev-industry-event-savannah-101200199.html)  ·  `page_001558`

## Facts

- LG Energy Solution North America Executive Vice President Robert Lee could not find an electric vehicle charging station on the Savannah Technical College campus after visiting twice.
- LG Energy Solution operates a leading-edge EV plant and is the number one global battery maker located near Savannah Tech.
- Savannah Technical College has partnered with Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America on an EV Production certification program.
- Robert Lee served as the keynote speaker for the 'Made in Georgia: the EV Energy Supply Chain' roundtable held Tuesday afternoon.
- The Zero Emission Transportation Association (ZETA) Education Fund organized the event, bringing together representatives from major regional, national, and global partners to support Georgia's goal of becoming the U.S. EV manufacturing epicenter.
- ZETA Executive Director Albert Gore, son of former U.S. Vice President Al Gore, led the roundtable discussion.
- In 2023, 74% of electric vehicles sold in the United States were made in the United States.
- The most recent quarterly data showed 79% of EVs sold in the U.S. were made in North America.
- China still produces nearly 40% of all types of motor vehicles.
- Worldwide EV and plug-in hybrid vehicle sales reached 17 million.
- That represents a 25% year-over-year increase from the previous year.
- Consumers purchased more than one million fully battery electric vehicles for the second consecutive year.
- A ZETA press release stated that across battery manufacturing, charging deployment, and automotive assembly, the EV and battery supply chain supports over $27.3 billion in investment and tens of thousands of jobs across Georgia.
- The average cost of an EV is around $56,000, compared to an average of $48,000 for internal combustion engine cars.
- EV costs declined by 10% between 2023 and 2024.
- Internal combustion engine car costs increased by 3% during the same period.
- Robert Lee foresees EVs costing less on average in roughly two years.
- Lee wants the industry to produce $30,000 EVs with 300-mile ranges in the near future.
- Georgia Public Service Commissioner Tim Echols moderated a panel discussion with industry partners at the event.
- Panelists included Colton Hotary (LG Energy Solution senior director of government and corporate affairs), Corey Ershow (Rivian Automotive director of federal affairs), Mickey Daniel (Georgia Power area manager), Scott Moskowitz (QCells vice president of market strategy and industry affairs), and Job van Campen (Heliox, a Siemens Company, head of sales).
- The roundtable occurred six days after Massachusetts-based Aspen Aerogels, Inc. demobilized its Statesboro, Georgia plant project, exposing the company to incentive agreement clawbacks or penalties.
- McKinsey & Company released a report on December 19, 2024, identifying persistent long-term challenges for the industry into 2030, including a regional raw battery materials supply and demand imbalance.
- On February 6, the Federal Highway Administration suspended $5 billion allocated for states' EV charging infrastructure deployment.
- The suspension followed the Trump Administration's January 20 'Unleashing American Energy' order, which announced a desire to terminate the electric vehicle mandate.
- The order claims unfair subsidies and other government-imposed market distortions favored EVs over other technologies.
- Georgia's District 1 Representative Buddy Carter participated in a fireside chat with Savannah Area Chamber of Commerce President and CEO Bert Brantley at the event.
- Carter stated he was one of 18 congressional members who wrote a letter urging Republican leaders not to dismantle key provisions of the Inflation Reduction Act that bolster domestic manufacturing economic security.
- In a post-event interview, Carter noted that Trump is 'just trying to shake things up' and emphasized communicating the importance of EV production to Georgia's 1st District, U.S. economic security, and national security.
- Lee acknowledged uncertainties regarding shifting government EV subsidy policies but remains cautiously optimistic due to private sector investment interest in EV charging infrastructure at universities, malls, and hotels.
- Gore remarked that 'a bit of shuffling' is common with new presidential administrations and the industry will adjust.
- Gore cited agreements Hyundai and Rivian have with mineral producers and refiners that have chosen to invest in America.
- Gore stated long-term EV growth trends are phenomenal, particularly because three of the five most popular EVs now cost below $45,000.
