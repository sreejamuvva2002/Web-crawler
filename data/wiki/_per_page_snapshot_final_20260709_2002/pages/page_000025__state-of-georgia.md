## Facts

- Georgia’s strategic location plugs positions automotive manufacturers and suppliers into the growing Southeast supply chain and easy access to key consumer markets.
- Georgia has been leading the industry for over 100 years.
- Georgia’s automotive industry thrives thanks to its business-friendly environment, extensive infrastructure, and a highly skilled workforce.
- For more than a century, Georgia has fostered innovative automotive industry practices, encouraged collaboration, and established itself as a leader in emerging technologies for the evolving automotive and mobility sectors.
- Seven global automotive companies are headquartered in Georgia.
- The Port of Brunswick is the top Ro/Ro port in the United States, according to Georgia Ports Authority in 2025.
- Georgia’s robust automotive ecosystem supports every aspect of the manufacturing process (BusinessWise, 2025).
- University System of Georgia and the Technical College System offer specialized programs across the state.
- Located in the center of the southeastern United States, Georgia offers proximity to diverse manufacturers within a 300-mile radius.
- Connected ports, railways, and interstates enable fast delivery to customers.
- Georgia is quickly becoming a national leader in electric and hybrid vehicle manufacturing due to the well-established automotive industry.
- Since 2018, advanced mobility investments have steadily increased, anchored by major projects from Kia, Hyundai Motor Group, and Rivian.
- Georgia offers an end-to-end supply chain for vehicle assembly, advanced battery, and materials, supporting innovation, scalability, and long-term growth in electrification.
- Collaboration and innovation are encouraged in Georgia through partnerships among businesses, nonprofits, universities, and government.
- These partnerships have resulted in living laboratories – such as Curiosity Lab and The Ray – that develop, test, and advance next-generation technologies for autonomous and connected vehicles.
- Strength in engineering, technology, and cybersecurity also make the state an ideal hub for innovation centers for a variety of industry leaders.
- Leading automotive innovators in Georgia include Novelis, GM IT Innovation Center, Panasonic Automotive, Heliox, and Porsche Digital.
- Georgia provides business support and industry development resources for companies already located in the state.
