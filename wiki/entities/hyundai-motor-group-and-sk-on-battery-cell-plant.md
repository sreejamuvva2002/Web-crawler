# Hyundai Motor Group & SK On Battery Cell Plant

**Type:** Facility  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[battery-cell-manufacturing|Battery Cell Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** Bartow County, Georgia, Bartow County, Georgia, United States

## Overview

A joint-venture battery cell production facility in Bartow County, Georgia, owned 50/50 by Hyundai Motor Group and SK On. The $5 billion plant is slated to begin operations in the second half of 2025 with a 35 GWh annual capacity, enough to supply batteries for roughly 300,000 electric vehicles.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Battery cell manufacturing in Georgia that will feed EV assembly plants and support production of 300,000 electric vehicles.

## Key facts

- The plant will have an annual production capacity of 35 GWh, sufficient to support the production of 300,000 EVs.
- Hyundai Motor Group and SK On each hold a 50% stake in the new battery cell plant.
- Estimated project cost is $5 billion.
- Battery cells produced at the plant will be shipped to Hyundai Mobis in Montgomery for assembly into battery modules.
- Modules will then be sent to HMMA, Kia Georgia, and Metaplant Atlanta for vehicle integration.
- "As the EV market continues to grow, having a strong production capacity will be key in allowing Hyundai Mobis to see continued growth in the market," said H.S. Oh.
