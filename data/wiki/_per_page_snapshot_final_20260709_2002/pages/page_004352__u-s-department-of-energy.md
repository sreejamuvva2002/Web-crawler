# U.S. Department of Energy

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [DOE's Top Clean Energy Accomplishments in 2024 | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/articles/does-top-clean-energy-accomplishments-2024)  ·  `page_004352`

## Facts

- In 2024, investments through DOE’s Office of Manufacturing and Energy Supply Chains (MESC) helped companies create or retain nearly 50,000 jobs.
- In September, DOE announced more than $2.8 billion to support affordable clean power in the Midwest through new nuclear energy facilities that will create more than 2,000 good-paying union jobs.
- The Industrial Training and Assessment Center (ITAC) Program invested $100 million in workforce development, funding over 200 projects to help ensure America has the skilled labor force necessary to sustain recent manufacturing momentum.
- In May, DOE announced the first faculty cohort for the Faculty-Applied Clean Energy Sciences (FACES) Program to help teachers inspire STEM students to pursue careers in clean energy.
- In 2024, DOE’s Grid Deployment Office (GDO) announced more than $5.4 billion in grid improvement grants and competitive awards to strengthen and expand the nation’s grid, covering all 50 states, 264 tribes, 5 territories, and the District of Columbia.
- DOE deployed $900 million in funding to revitalize the nuclear sector and add more carbon-free energy to the grid.
- DOE announced $30 million in funding to deploy AI software solutions to accelerate the energy interconnection application process and created a resource hub for datacenter owners and operators to secure clean, reliable energy solutions.
- In August, DOE dedicated its new Grid Storage Launchpad facility to strengthen the resilience of the grid to withstand future tests and challenges.
- In 2024, MESC deployed over $12 billion to transform energy supply chains, enabling the construction of 80+ manufacturing facilities across 31 states.
- In April, DOE announced $75 million for a project to develop a Critical Minerals Supply Chain Research Facility, funded by the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law to strengthen domestic supply chains and reduce reliance on unreliable foreign sources.
- DOE announced more than $850 million for America's rural and remote communities through the Energy Improvements in Rural or Remote Areas Program.
- DOE launched the Arctic Energy Ambassadors program to facilitate energy projects in Alaska.
- DOE directed $190 million through the Renew America’s Schools Program to support school districts across the country in implementing energy upgrades.
- In October, DOE announced $518 million to support 23 selected projects that will strengthen the nation’s infrastructure for permanent, safe storage of carbon pollution.
- DOE announced nearly $85 million to accelerate manufacturing electric heat pumps, helping to reduce energy consumption and harmful greenhouse gas emissions.
- In December, DOE announced $850 million for 40+ projects as part of the joint EPA-DOE Methane Emissions Reduction Program (MERP), providing a total of $1.36 billion to support monitoring and mitigation of methane emissions from the oil and natural gas sector.
- DOE’s Office of Clean Energy and Demonstrations announced up to $6 billion for 30+ projects as part of the Industrial Demonstrations Program (IDP) to help accelerate the commercial-scale demonstration of emerging industrial decarbonization technologies, strengthening domestic manufacturing.
- Thanks to the Inflation Reduction Act, DOE rolled out the $8.8 billion home energy rebates program in 10 states this year, helping American households save up to $14,000 on residential energy efficiency and electrification upgrades.
- DOE launched tools for Americans to save money on home appliances and electric vehicles, including the Tax Credit Product Lookup Tool and the Local Fuel Savings Tool.
- The Weatherization Assistance Program (WAP) served approximately 38,000 homes this year, reducing energy costs for low-income households by increasing the energy efficiency of their homes.
- In 2024, the Assisting Federal Facilities with Energy Conservation Technologies (AFFECT) grant program announced more than $250 million in funding to leverage public-private partnerships to reduce pollution, create jobs, and cut costs for taxpayers.
- For the first time, the Loan Programs Office published full Community Benefit Plans (CBPs) for projects that have reached financial close, ensuring tangible benefits for communities of color and low-income communities.
- Through the $5 million Regional Energy Democracy Initiatives (REDI) program, DOE’s Office of Energy Justice and Equity (EJE) is providing capacity building and technical assistance for communities in Texas and Louisiana to maximize benefits from clean energy investments.
- The Office of Indian Energy announced $25 million to support clean energy technology deployment on tribal lands and delivered $9 million to Tribal Colleges and Universities (TCUs) to advance clean energy projects and bolster food sovereignty initiatives on their campuses.
- Key Federal programs supported by the Joint Office of Energy and Transportation have spurred private sector investment to grow the national electric vehicle (EV) charging network to more than 205,000 public EV chargers, with nearly 1,000 new public chargers turned on every week.
- Thanks to the National Electric Vehicle Infrastructure (NEVI) Program and $2.5 billion Charging and Fueling Infrastructure (CFI) Program, there are 259 federally funded public charging ports operational across 15 states, with projects for 24,800 federally funded charging ports underway across the country.
- DOE announced millions of dollars in investments in innovative clean energy technology, including solar energy, geothermal, and offshore wind.
- DOE commissioned El Capitan, the fastest supercomputer in the world, to support clean energy innovation.
- DOE embedded energy innovators at national labs to develop next-generation technologies.
- In 2024, DOE supported research and development opportunities, surpassing $1 billion in total funding to advance nuclear energy research and training and announcing over $200 million for small business research and development grants.
- Over the last four years, DOE committed more than half a billion dollars in Tribal energy investments for clean energy generation and deployment, electrification and grid resilience.
- In September 2024, the Loan Programs Office closed on their first loan to finance the development of a solar-plus-long-duration-energy-storage microgrid on the lands of the Viejas Band of the Kumeyaay Indians on the Viejas Reservation near Alpine, California for $72.8 million.
- In August 2024, the Biden-Harris Administration announced the government’s first-ever purchase of carbon pollution-free electricity from a Tribal majority-owned business under the Indian Energy Purchase Preference provisions of the Energy Policy Act of 2005, led by the General Services Administration and supported through interagency collaboration with DOE.
- In February 2024, more than 700 Tribal Leaders, staff, and partners attended the DOE Tribal Clean Energy Summit on the homelands of the Pechanga Band of Luiseño Indians in Temecula, California to build networks, exchange ideas, and explore clean energy solutions that support Tribal energy sovereignty.
