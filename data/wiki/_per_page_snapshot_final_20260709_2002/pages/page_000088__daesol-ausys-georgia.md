# Daesol Ausys Georgia

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-08-29

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp: Automotive Supplier Daesol Ausys Georgia to Invest $72 Million in Harris County | Governor Brian P. Kemp Office of the Governor](https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases/2023-08-29/gov-kemp-automotive-supplier-daesol-ausys-georgia-invest-72-million)  ·  `page_000088`

## Facts

- On August 29, 2023, Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Daesol Ausys Georgia will establish a new manufacturing facility in West Point.
- The new facility will create more than 140 new jobs and represent $72 million in investment in Harris County.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that West Point has become an automotive capital in its own right and is excited for Daesol Ausys to join other innovators in Harris County.
- Georgia's automotive industry employs over 50,000 hardworking Georgians, according to Governor Kemp.
- Daesol Ausys was established in Korea in 2017 and specializes in designing and manufacturing motor and electric vehicle interior components and accessories.
- The company is a key supplier for Kia Georgia, Hyundai Motor Group, and General Motors.
- Min Ho Kwon, CEO of Daesol Ausys, expressed excitement about the project at the Northwest Harris Business Park and the partnership with the State of Georgia and Harris County.
- Daesol Ausys’ new facility will produce luggage boards and luggage covers.
- Operations at the new facility are expected to begin in December 2024.
- The company will be hiring for positions in production, purchasing, quality control, maintenance, human resources, and management.
- Robert Grant, Chairman of the Harris County Commission, noted that Daesol Ausys’ decision represents a commitment to Harris County and highlighted the relationship with sister companies Daehan Solutions and Daesol Materials.
- Craig Greenhaw, Chairman of the Harris County Development Authority, welcomed Daesol Ausys as a new corporate citizen bringing jobs and capital investment into the region.
- Alyce Thornhill, Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy at GDEcD’s Global Commerce team, represented the state in partnership with the Harris County Commission, Harris County Development Authority, City of West Point, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that economic development is driven by the local level and praised community partners for bringing Daesol Ausys to West Georgia.
- Since 2018, Georgia's pursuit of the entire EV supply chain has created more than $25 billion in investments and 30,000 jobs.
- In fiscal year 2023, job creation in Georgia's automotive industry increased by 324 percent compared to fiscal year 2021.
- Suppliers attracted by Georgia’s mobility original equipment manufacturers resulted in over $2 billion in investment across the state in fiscal year 2023.
- Daesol Ausys Georgia is a leading manufacturer of automotive components and has a long history as one of the top suppliers to Kia and Hyundai worldwide.
