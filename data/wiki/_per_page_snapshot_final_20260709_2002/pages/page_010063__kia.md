# Kia

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Kia plans $200m expansion for EV assembly at Georgia plant | Automotive Manufacturing Solutions](https://www.automotivemanufacturingsolutions.com/electrification/kia-plans-200m-expansion-at-georgia-plant-for-assembly-of-ev9-suv/534280)  ·  `page_010063`

## Facts

- Kia is investing $200m into an expansion of its Georgia West Point plant to accommodate the assembly of its new EV9 SUV in the second quarter of 2024.
- The EV9 model will be Kia’s first EV to be assembled in North America.
- The expansion to produce the fifth vehicle assembled at the plant will create almost 200 additional jobs in Troup County, according to the OEM.
- Currently, the Georgia West Point plant assembles the Telluride, Sorento and Sportage SUVs as well as the K5 mid-size sedan.
- Sean Yoon, president and CEO of Kia North America and Kia America, stated that like the Telluride, the EV9 has the potential to be another game changer for Kia.
- Sean Yoon also noted that the EV9 will be the most sophisticated vehicle Kia has ever built and a standout in the EV market and on the road.
- Earlier in the year, Kia’s sister plant, the Hyundai plant in Montgomery, Alabama, added EV production to produce the first Genesis model made in the US.
- The South Korean carmaker invested $300m to bring about production of the Genesis Electrified GV70 SUV in the Montgomery plant, resulting in the creation of 200 jobs.
- Hyundai and LG Energy Solution officially announced a plan last month to establish a 50/50 EV battery joint venture in Savannah, Georgia.
- The Hyundai-LG Energy Solution joint venture signed a memorandum of understanding to build a 30GWh battery factory worth more than $4.3 billion.
- In April, the carmaker announced plans to invest $5 billion, along with SK On, to build a new EV battery cell plant in Georgia.
- Production at the new Kia-SK On EV battery cell plant is slated to start in the second half of 2025.
- SK On has been a strategic partner for Kia in its work on the Genesis GV60 and other models.
- The Hyundai-LG Energy Solution joint venture will support the group’s electrification efforts and provide a stable supply of batteries for EV production in the US, according to Hyundai.
- The new battery plant is set to be based in Bartow County, Georgia.
- The Bartow County plant location is close to Hyundai’s US production facilities including the Kia Georgia plant, the Hyundai Motor Manufacturing plant in Alabama, and Hyundai’s Motor Group Metaplant America.
