# Hyundai Mobis

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-06-01

**Source:** [Hyundai Mobis Breaks Ground On New Battery System Plant - Invest Indonesia](https://investindonesia.co.id/2023/06/01/hyundai-mobis-breaks-ground-on-new-battery-system-plant/)  ·  `page_001190`

## Facts

- Hyundai Mobis announced the beginning of construction of a new EV-dedicated battery system plant in Indonesia.
- The company plans to complete the Indonesian plant by the first half of next year.
- Hyundai Mobis intends to use Indonesia as a beachhead for the ASEAN market to become a leader in the electrification ecosystem, which includes cells, battery systems, and OEMs.
- A groundbreaking ceremony for the battery system plant was held in Bekasi, West Java, on the outskirts of Jakarta on May 31.
- The ceremony was attended by about 150 participants.
- Attendees included Mochamad Ridwan Kamil (Governor of West Java Province), Ambassador of the Republic of Korea Lee Sang-deok, Oh Heung-sub (Senior Vice President of Electrification at Hyundai Mobis), Indonesian Government officials, industry leaders, and local journalists.
- The Indonesian battery system plant will serve as a foundation for Hyundai Mobis' global electrification business, particularly targeting the ASEAN market.
- The Indonesian government is accelerating efforts to secure leadership in the EV market by leveraging the country's ample natural resources.
- The demand for EVs in the ASEAN region, centered on Indonesia, is increasing rapidly.
- Hyundai Mobis will use battery cells supplied by the joint venture HLI Green Power for the plant.
- The plant will convert these cells into modules that include a controller and a heat controller.
- The completed large battery systems will be supplied to global automakers.
- Hyundai Mobis will act as an intermediary to complete the EV ecosystem through this plant.
- The Indonesian plant will be located in the Deltamas industrial complex, approximately 60 km southeast of Jakarta.
- The site is located 3 km from Hyundai Motor Company’s Indonesian plant and 10 km from HLI Green Power to maximize delivery efficiency.
- Hyundai Motor Group has invested a total of USD 60 million for the plant.
- The plant will be built on a land area of approximately 33,000 square meters.
- The battery systems will initially be supplied to flagship EV models scheduled for release in Southeast Asia next year.
- The Indonesian government provided high support for administrative matters related to the plant's construction, aligning with its policy to strengthen the local EV market and ecosystem.
- The Indonesian government provided support regarding construction approvals, environmental assessments, logistics costs, and tax benefits.
- Oh Heung-sub, Senior Vice President of Electrification at Hyundai Mobis, thanked the Indonesian government for its support.
- Oh Heung-sub stated that "Hyundai Mobis will become a partner for Indonesia and will play a pivotal role in establishing the EV ecosystem."
- Hyundai Mobis holds half of the stake in the joint battery cell venture HLI Green Power (Hyundai Mobis 25%, Hyundai Motors 15%, Kia 10%).
- HLI Green Power is a joint battery cell venture currently being built near Hyundai Mobis' local plant.
- Hyundai Mobis aims to expand and develop the Indonesian plant into a long-term strategic base for major global customers due to the rising ASEAN market.
- Hyundai Mobis currently produces EV components, including battery systems, in Korea and Europe (Czech Republic, Slovakia).
- In October of the previous year, Hyundai Mobis announced a plan to invest USD 1.3 billion by 2030 to expand its electrification production base in North America.
- Hyundai Mobis is currently constructing five additional electrification component plants in Alabama and Georgia, USA.
- The global expansion will complete an electrification value chain linking Asia (Korea and Indonesia), Europe, and North America.
- The expansion is expected to reinforce Hyundai Mobis' capability to respond to the EV market.
- Hyundai Mobis is the global No. 6 automotive supplier, headquartered in Seoul, Korea.
- The company has expertise in sensors, sensor fusion in ECUs, and software development for safety control.
- Hyundai Mobis' products include components for electrification, brakes, chassis and suspension, steering, airbags, lighting, and automotive electronics.
- Hyundai Mobis operates its R&D headquarters in Korea with four technology centers in the United States, Germany, China, and India.
