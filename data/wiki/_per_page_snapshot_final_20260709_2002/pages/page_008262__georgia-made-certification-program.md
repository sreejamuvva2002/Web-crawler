# Georgia Made Certification Program

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2025-03-31

**Source:** [Made in Georgia: Manufacturing Excellence - Georgia Trend Magazine](https://www.georgiatrend.com/2025/03/31/made-in-georgia-manufacturing-excellence/)  ·  `page_008262`

## Facts

- Manufacturing facilities in Georgia generated more than $79 billion and employed more than 430,000 people in 2023.
- Manufacturers accounted for 8.27% of the state’s workforce as of the second quarter of 2024.
- The Georgia legislature created the Georgia Made branding program to recognize the impact of manufacturing.
- Lindsay Martin oversees the Georgia Made program as director of small business, Georgia Made and GRAD Certification for the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD).
- There are an estimated more than 7,000 manufacturing facilities statewide, making up nearly 10% of the state’s gross domestic product.
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America became Georgia Made certified in February 2025.
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America is dedicated to building electrified vehicles in Georgia.
- Brent Stubbs serves as chief administrative officer at Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- Hurst Boiler & Welding Company in Thomas County was one of the first manufacturers to receive Georgia Made certification in 2020.
- Chad Fletcher is director of purchasing and aftermarket sales at Hurst Boiler & Welding Company.
- Hurst Boiler & Welding Company has been an American manufacturer of industrial boilers since 1967.
- In 2007, Hurst completed a large expansion that doubled its manufacturing space to 350,000 square feet on a campus of more than 48 acres in Coolidge.
- Hurst Boiler & Welding Company employs 250 people in Coolidge, which has a population of just 529.
- Hurst recruits employees from nearby universities like Southern Regional Technical College, which has a campus less than 20 minutes away in Thomasville.
- EssilorLuxottica manufactures lenses and distributes prescription eyeglasses and sunglasses at its 1,700-employee manufacturing plant in McDonough.
- Rob Harris is senior director of optical manufacturing for EssilorLuxottica.
- EssilorLuxottica uses the Georgia Made designation as a selling point to attract potential suppliers and employees.
- Companies like EssilorLuxottica benefit from alignment with the Technical College System of Georgia and workforce training initiatives including Georgia Quick Start.
- Kubota Manufacturing of America Corp. uses multiple programs associated with the Technical College System of Georgia to bolster its workforce.
- Ryan Owen is vice president of human resources, facilities, quality, safety and environmental at Kubota Manufacturing of America Corp.
- Kubota has been building tractors, mowers and utility vehicles on a 180-acre campus in Gainesville since 1988.
- Kubota expanded four times since 2003 and now employs 3,100 workers across three campuses in Hall County.
- Kubota's research and development facility is located in Gainesville.
- Kubota Industrial Equipment subsidiary has operated large manufacturing and distribution facilities in Jackson County since 2005.
- Georgia Quick Start is a company-specific workforce development program that helps train employees for high-tech jobs.
- GDEcD Chief Operating Officer Sydne Smith notes that many manufacturers have benefited from the Georgia Quick Start program.
- GEORGIA MATCH is a program that informs high school seniors which schools would admit them if they applied to encourage upskilling.
- Technical colleges tailor their program offerings to reflect the local industry mix and work with local industry to place graduates.
- In 2018, Kubota's Gainesville manufacturing plant and Jefferson industrial equipment facility donated $1 million to the Lanier Technical College Foundation.
- The Kubota donation funded the purchase of state-of-the-art equipment in Lanier Technical College's technical and industrial building, located about one mile from the Gainesville plant.
- TenCate Protective Fabrics is celebrating its 100-year anniversary as a Georgia manufacturer in 2025.
- TenCate Protective Fabrics produces flame-resistant textiles used in clothing and gear for firefighters, military personnel and industrial safety professionals.
- The company was originally founded in 1925 as Southern Mills in Union City and acquired by TenCate Protective Fabrics in 2004.
- TenCate Protective Fabrics received Georgia Made certification in 2024.
- TenCate Protective Fabrics employs 381 workers at four separate manufacturing facilities in Senoia, Zebulon and Molena and its corporate office and lab in Union City.
- CEO Maria Gallahue-Worl states that the Georgia Made certification is very important for the economic vitality of the areas where TenCate operates.
- Fabrics manufactured by TenCate are used by local fire departments in Macon, Cobb County, Gwinnett County and Coweta County, as well as the Fire Department of New York.
- Roper Corp. has held Georgia Made certification since 2020.
- Roper Corp. is a wholly owned subsidiary of GE Appliances (GEA).
- Roper manufactures cooktops, freestanding ranges and wall ovens at its 2,500-employee facility in LaFayette.
- Since 2016, GE Appliances invested $161 million and created 700 new jobs in Walker County for the Roper plant.
- In 2023, Roper showcased upgrades from a $118 million investment to expand its manufacturing capabilities.
- The Roper expansion unveiled assembly lines featuring advanced manufacturing technology to build new models of freestanding ranges available in 2024.
- The Roper expansion more than tripled the robotics used in the factory, improving efficiency and ergonomics for employees.
- The state of Georgia gave grant money to the Roper plant to fund an existing apprenticeship program with Georgia Northwestern Technical College that trained 45 employees in robotics.
- The Roper-GNTC apprenticeship program started in 2017 with workers training in GNTC’s mechatronics program at its Catoosa County campus.
- Luther Ingram is president and executive director at Roper Corp.
- More than 50% of all homes in the U.S. have at least one GE appliance, many of which are made at the LaFayette manufacturing plant.
