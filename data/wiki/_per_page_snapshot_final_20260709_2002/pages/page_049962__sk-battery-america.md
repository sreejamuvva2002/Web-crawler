# SK Battery America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Why Did SK Battery America Lay Off 37% of Workers at Its Georgia Plant? "Impact of Sluggish EV Sales" - MK](https://www.mk.co.kr/en/business/11981653)  ·  `page_049962`

## Facts

- SK On, the battery subsidiary of SK Innovation, operates a plant in Commerce, Georgia.
- The plant announced layoffs on the 6th (local time).
- SK Battery America dismissed 968 employees.
- The layoffs represent 37% of the plant's total workforce of 2,566 workers.
- The restructuring was carried out in response to sluggish electric vehicle sales and changing market conditions.
- SK Battery America stated it reduced its workforce to adjust operations to current market conditions.
- SK Battery America remains committed to honoring promises to Georgia and building a robust U.S. supply chain for advanced battery manufacturing.
- The plant supplies EV batteries to Germany’s Volkswagen.
- The plant supplies EV batteries to South Korea’s Hyundai Motor Company.
- The plant supplied batteries for Ford Motor Company’s electric pickup truck, the Ford F-150 Lightning.
- Profitability was negatively impacted after Ford canceled production of the F-150 Lightning model.
- Ford shifted focus to profitable hybrid and internal combustion engine vehicles after the administration of Donald John Trump ended tax credit benefits for EV purchases.
- The South Korean battery industry faces difficulties due to a slowing shift from internal combustion engine vehicles to EVs in major markets like the United States.
- SK Innovation is constructing a second battery plant in Georgia.
- The second Georgia facility will supply batteries to Hyundai Motor Company.
- The second Georgia facility is scheduled to begin production in the first half of this year.
- A joint venture plant with Ford in Tennessee is expected to start production in 2028.
- The Tennessee plant may supply both automotive batteries and energy storage batteries.
