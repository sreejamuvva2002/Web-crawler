# Stryten Energy LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-08-02

**Source:** [Stryten Energy and Snapping Shoals EMC Celebrate Installation of Georgia’s First Vanadium Redox Flow Battery System - EIN Presswire](https://www.einnews.com/pr_news/647757443/stryten-energy-and-snapping-shoals-emc-celebrate-installation-of-georgia-s-first-vanadium-redox-flow-battery-system)  ·  `page_050514`

## Facts

- Stryten Energy LLC installed its advanced vanadium redox flow battery (VRFB) system at Snapping Shoals EMC, marking the first VRFB energy storage system manufactured and installed in Georgia.
- The installation was celebrated on August 2, 2023.
- The VRFB system can store and provide clean energy to Snapping Shoals customers for up to 20 years without losing capacity.
- Stryten Energy's partnership with Snapping Shoals EMC will focus on demonstrating the VRFB's energy storage capabilities and evaluating additional use cases for long-duration energy storage technology.
- Shaun Mock, President and Chief Executive Officer of Snapping Shoals EMC, identified Stryten Energy as a natural partner in harnessing VRFB power for long-duration energy storage as the utility shifts to clean energy sources.
- The VRFB system will support Snapping Shoals EMC's 100,000+ residential and business customers by reliably and affordably storing clean energy.
- Mike Judd, Chief Executive Officer and President of Stryten Energy, stated that long-duration energy storage technologies are needed to support widescale renewable power generation and that VRFB is ideally suited for this application.
- The demonstration project aims to prove VRFB capabilities in utility use cases including energy cost control, peak shaving, and avoiding curtailment.
- Stryten Energy's VRFB offers industry-leading power density with a versatile, modular platform that is independently scalable in power and capacity.
- With proper maintenance, the VRFB system has an unlimited cycle life and can operate for more than 20 years without the electrolyte losing energy storage capacity.
- The technology is uniquely suited for medium- to long-duration energy storage applications requiring six or more hours to ensure grid stability and increase renewable utilization across the U.S.
- The Department of Energy reports that Georgia has more than 3.6 gigawatts (GW) of solar, wind, and storage capacity.
- Almost 1.1 GW of additional planned clean energy capacity is in development in Georgia, which will power more than 155,000 homes.
- Snapping Shoals EMC serves approximately 100,000 homes and businesses across an eight-county area including Newton, Henry, Rockdale, DeKalb, Butts, Walton, Jasper, and Morgan counties.
- Stryten Energy is headquartered in Alpharetta, Georgia, and provides energy storage solutions across Essential Power, Motive Power, Transportation, Military, and Government sectors.
- Stryten Energy's stored energy technologies include advanced lead, lithium, and vanadium redox flow batteries, intelligent chargers, and energy performance management software.
- The company is backed by more than a century of expertise in delivering reliable and sustainable energy storage capacity.
