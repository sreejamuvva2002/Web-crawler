# Volvo Cars

## Facts

- Volvo received authorization from the U.S. government to continue importing and selling connected vehicles in the United States.
- The authorization was granted by the U.S. Office of Information and Communications Technology and Services on May 26th, 2026.
- Volvo is majority-owned by Zhejiang Geely Holding Group, a Chinese automotive conglomerate that acquired the Swedish brand in 2010.
- The approval allows Volvo to continue selling vehicles in the U.S. market despite new federal restrictions on connected vehicle technologies originating from China and Russia.
- A Biden-era regulation took effect on March 17th, 2025, prohibiting the import and sale of connected vehicles using Chinese and Russian software starting with the 2027 model year.
- Restrictions on imported hardware for connected vehicles are set to follow, beginning with the 2030 model year.
- Volvo operates vehicle manufacturing facilities in Chengdu, Daqing, and Taizhou, as well as a design center in Shanghai.
- The authorization represents only the first step for compliance, as manufacturers must still determine how to satisfy the broader requirements of the Commerce Department rule.
- Volvo stated the authorization provides a pathway for the company to continue forward with its plans for growth in the U.S. auto market.
- The new federal regulation targets hardware and software considered vulnerable to exploitation by foreign adversaries, potentially providing access to vehicle and driver data or allowing remote control of various vehicle functions.
- The rule restricts manufacturers with certain connections to the Chinese government from selling connected vehicles in the United States, even if components or software are sourced elsewhere.
- The decision arrives amid broader bipartisan efforts in Washington to limit the influence of Chinese automakers and technology providers in the U.S. automotive sector.
- Legislation targeting Chinese vehicle imports has been proposed in both the House and Senate.
- A letter signed by more than 70 lawmakers urges stricter oversight of Chinese vehicle imports.
- GM confirmed it will build the Buick Envision at its Fairfax plant in Kansas City by 2028, after importing the Envision from China for roughly a decade.
