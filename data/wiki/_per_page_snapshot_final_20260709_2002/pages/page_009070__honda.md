# Honda

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-04-22

**Source:** [Honda expected to announce multi-billion dollar deal to assemble EVs in Ontario: sources | CBC News](https://www.cbc.ca/news/politics/honda-ev-ontario-1.7180899)  ·  `page_009070`

## Facts

- Honda is expected to announce a multi-billion dollar commitment to build electric vehicles and their parts in Ontario later this week.
- The deal is expected to involve new facilities to build batteries.
- The deal is expected to involve facilities to process cathode active materials, a component of the batteries used in electric vehicles.
- The news was first reported by Bloomberg.
- Ontario Premier Doug Ford called the deal the 'largest deal in Canadian history' during remarks at the annual First Nations Major Projects Coalition conference on Monday.
- Ford teased that the deal will involve multiple facilities 'right across the region.'
- Ford stated that 'everyone's going to benefit' from the deal.
- The announcement is set to be made Thursday at Honda's plant in Alliston, Ont.
- Details of government subsidies for the project could become public at the Thursday announcement.
- Ontario Economic Development Minister Vic Fedeli suggested the massive auto deal is worth something in the ballpark of $15 billion.
- Fedeli stated that EV investment in the province went from zero to $28 billion in three years.
- Fedeli suggested the new deal would bring total EV investment in the province to around '$43 billion ... or a number in and around there.'
- Last week's budget, which has yet to pass Parliament, pitched an EV supply chain investment tax credit that likely acted as a sweetener for Honda.
- The government is proposing a 10 per cent tax credit on the cost of buildings used in three keys area of the EV supply chain: assembly, battery production and cathode active material production.
- Finance Minister Chrystia Freeland offered no details about the anticipated deal but praised the budget's measures for attracting foreign investment.
- Freeland expressed confidence that the tax credits would bring more significant investment to Canada in the days, weeks, and months to come.
- The anticipated Honda deal is the latest in a series of announcements of projects building up the electric vehicle industry in Canada, mainly in Ontario and Quebec.
- Last year, federal and provincial governments announced a number of deals with EV battery producers Northvolt, Volkswagen and Stellantis-LGES.
- Governments have defended subsidizing EV production as a way to keep pace with measures contained in the Inflation Reduction Act, a $369 billion U.S. program that offers subsidies and incentives to American companies building electric cars.
- Automotive Parts Manufacturers' Association president Flavio Volpe said supplier interest is high reacting to the deal.
- Volpe stated that Honda does not make speculative term bets.
- Volpe noted that in almost 40 years of making cars in Canada without failure or retreat, a Honda supply contract is extremely bankable.
