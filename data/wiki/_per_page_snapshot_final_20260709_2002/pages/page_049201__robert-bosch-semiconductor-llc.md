# Robert Bosch Semiconductor LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** power_electronics  ·  **Published:** 2024-12-16

**Source:** [Bosch nabs $225M in CHIPS funds | Manufacturing Dive](https://www.manufacturingdive.com/news/bosch-commerce-department-225-million-chips-funding-roseville-california-facility/735549/)  ·  `page_049201`

## Facts

- The Department of Commerce is offering up to $225 million in CHIPS and Science Act funding to automotive supplier Bosch.
- The funding includes an additional $350 million in loans from the CHIPS Program Office.
- The funding supports Bosch’s $1.9 billion project to expand its Roseville, California, facility for silicon carbide semiconductor production for the automotive industry.
- The project is expected to create up to 1,000 construction jobs.
- The project is expected to create 700 manufacturing, engineering and research and development roles.
- The Roseville wafer fab was acquired by Bosch in August 2023 from TSI Semiconductors Corp.
- Bosch vowed to significantly grow its global portfolio of SiC semiconductors by the end of 2030.
- The location will be Bosch’s first semiconductor facility in the country.
- The facility extends the auto supplier’s international semiconductor manufacturing network.
- Bosch launched a new spinoff company called Robert Bosch Semiconductor LLC for the transformation.
- Commerce Secretary Gina Raimondo stated that SiC chips are important components for applications in the automotive, telecommunications, and defense industries because they utilize less energy.
- Production at the 200-millimeter wafer facility is expected to start in 2026.
- Production will focus on electric vehicle driving and charging efficiency.
- The facility has the potential to represent 40% of U.S.-based SiC manufacturing capacity.
- The Roseville facility will perform both front-end device manufacturing and backend testing, sorting and dicing processes.
- Bosch plans to claim the advanced manufacturing investment credit to cover 25% of qualified capital expenditures.
- The site received a $25 million tax incentive from the state of California grant.
