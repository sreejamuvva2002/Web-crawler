# Yamaha Motor Co. Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-03-04

**Source:** [Yamaha Motor Set to Move U.S.  Headquarters to Kennesaw From California - Global Atlanta](https://www.globalatlanta.com/yamaha-motor-set-to-move-u-s-headquarters-to-kennesaw-from-california/)  ·  `page_015689`

## Facts

- Yamaha Motor Co. Ltd., which makes ATVs, boat engines, personal watercraft and more, is set to move its U.S. headquarters from California to Kennesaw.
- The relocation of Yamaha Motor Corp. USA will take place gradually starting this year and continuing through 2028.
- The company will mark its half-century anniversary in the United States in 2028.
- Yamaha purchased the land for its former U.S. headquarters in Cypress, Calif., in 1978.
- The company slowly built up its U.S. business from the 25-acre Cypress site.
- The Cypress site will be liquidated under a sale and leaseback transaction once the transition is completed.
- The headquarters move culminates a gradual eastward migration consolidating much of Yamaha Motor’s inventory, executive team, and manufacturing in Georgia.
- Before the headquarters relocation decision, Yamaha already had about 2,400 employees across its divisions in Georgia.
- Yamaha Motor moved its marine division to Kennesaw in 1999.
- The company later consolidated sales and warehousing functions for its outboard engines in Kennesaw.
- In 2023, the Kennesaw operation was expanded with the opening of a 75,000-square-foot Marine Innovation Center.
- Gov. Brian Kemp visited the Marine Innovation Center for its ribbon-cutting ceremony.
- The new center complemented a long-standing parts warehouse on Chastain Road that the company kept after outgrowing it.
- The motorsports business was relocated to Georgia in 2019.
- This relocation provided proximity to the Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corp. of America factory in Newnan.
- The Newnan factory spans 1.3 million square feet.
- The Newnan factory employs 2,000-plus people.
- The Newnan factory has produced close to 5 million units over 30-plus years of manufacturing.
- Products manufactured at the Newnan facility include ATVs, waverunners, and side-by-sides.
- The Newnan unit recently contracted with DHL to manage logistics on its site.
- The company transferred 175 employees from its own operations to DHL as part of the logistics contract.
- Yamaha Motor USA CEO Mike Chrzanowski worked at the Newnan site for about 20 years before becoming top U.S. executive two years ago.
- Mr. Chrzanowski was given the option to relocate to Cypress or stay in Kennesaw.
- Mr. Chrzanowski stated, “I chose Kennesaw because this is where all the action is,” during a 2024 celebration of international investment at the Cobb Chamber of Commerce.
- The consolidation benefits Georgia amid a tough U.S. operating environment for Yamaha Motor.
- According to a news release, Yamaha is “undertaking structural reforms aimed at improving the profitability of its U.S. operations in response to cost increases resulting from U.S. tariffs and changes in the market environment.”
- In addition to cutting costs, the company is working on tying profitability less to relentless top-line revenue growth and more to new innovations.
- The move supports a thesis by Metro Atlanta Chamber economist Jerry Parrish from early 2025 that pain elsewhere in the U.S. could benefit metro Atlanta as companies cut costs and centralize corporate functions in cities with diverse industries, competitive costs, and strong talent bases.
- For its U.S. operations, Yamaha is focused on creating "kando," described as a Japanese word for the simultaneous feelings of deep satisfaction and intense excitement experienced when encountering exceptional value.
