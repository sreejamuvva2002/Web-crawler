## Facts

- A study published by Towards Chemical and Materials projects the metal stamping business will grow from an estimated $254.36 billion in 2025 to $385.66 billion in 2035.
- The projected growth represents an increase of $131.3 billion over the decade.
- The global metal stamping market is growing based on a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 4.25%.
- The Asia Pacific region currently dominates the industry, accounting for 42.2% of global metal stamping revenue in 2024.
- North America is expected to beat the average growth rate with a CAGR of 5.2%.
- Asia Pacific's growth rate is predicted at 4.26% over the same period.
- Steel accounted for 50.6% of the metals-stamping market in 2024.
- Aluminum pressing is expected to grow rapidly at a CAGR of 4.8% over the coming decade.
- Steel is noted as offering high strength, durability, and cost-effectiveness relative to aluminum.
- Aluminum is the second-largest segment in the metals-stamping market.
- Metal stamping companies are increasingly adopting robotics, AI, and IoT sensors.
- The industry is shifting towards lightweight materials like advanced steels and aluminum alloys.
- The shift to lightweight materials is partly driven by the need to improve electric vehicle performance.
- Companies are adopting eco-friendly processes such as implementing servo presses and scrap metal recycling programs.
- AI could enable predictive maintenance to generally improve metal-stamping processes.
- Towards Chemical and Materials used 2023 as a base year for estimation in drafting the report.
- The report utilized historical data from 2018 through 2025.
- The report profiles 14 metal stamping companies.
- Profiled companies include Acro Metal Stamping, Goshen Stamping Company, Nissan Motor Co., Ltd., and Ford Motor Company.
- General Motors is investing $491 million to upgrade its Marion, Indiana metal stamping factory.
- Gestamp is building a new hot stamping plant in Pune, India to supply Ford and Tata Motors.
- Melling Engine Parts acquired Casalandra Metal Stamping.
- GSC Steel Stamping LLC acquired Dixien, LLC.
- Interplex purchased OCP group.
