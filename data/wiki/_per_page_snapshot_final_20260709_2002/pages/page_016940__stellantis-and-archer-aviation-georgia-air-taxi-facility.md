# Stellantis and Archer Aviation Georgia Air Taxi Facility

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-07-05

**Source:** [Stellantis and Archer Aviation on track for launching Georgia air taxi factory | Manufacturing Dive](https://www.manufacturingdive.com/news/stellantis-archer-provide-update-on-georgia-air-taxi-facility/684906/)  ·  `page_016940`

## Facts

- Construction is “well underway” on Stellantis and Archer Aviation’s high-volume electric air taxi manufacturing facility in Covington, Georgia.
- The companies provided an update late last month regarding the facility's progress.
- Stellantis is providing expertise to Archer Aviation on production automation and component sourcing.
- The companies are preparing to launch operations in mid-2024.
- The partnership between Archer and Stellantis was announced in January.
- Archer broke ground on the Covington facility in March.
- Stellantis personnel are now embedded across almost all of Archer Aviation’s operations, including manufacturing, engineering, supply chain, quality and facilities.
- The Covington site is expected to produce 650 aircraft per year.
- The site has room for expansion to make up to 2,300 units annually.
- Stellantis is helping enable Archer to quickly scale production of its flagship air taxi, the Midnight, to meet production targets.
- Stellantis is contributing advanced manufacturing tech, expertise and personnel to the project.
- Stellantis is contributing up to $150 million in capital towards the Covington facility.
- Aircraft manufacturers currently lack the capabilities to build air taxis at the scale needed for mass adoption, including the necessary supply chains and distribution networks.
- Archer Aviation founder and CEO Adam Goldstein stated: “Our goal is not just to get to commercialization, but to achieve it at scale.”
- Adam Goldstein added: “High-volume manufacturing is critical to ensuring we can meet this goal ... I couldn’t be prouder of what we’ve already accomplished with this partnership as we continue to lead the industry in building out manufacturing capabilities.”
- Germany-based electric air taxi company Volocopter opened its production facility in Bruchsal, Germany, in April.
- Volocopter's production facility has capacity to build more than 50 air taxis per year.
