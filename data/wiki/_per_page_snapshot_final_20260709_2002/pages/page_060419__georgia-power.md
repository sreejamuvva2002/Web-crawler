# Georgia Power

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** research_and_development  ·  **Published:** 2024-11-05

**Source:** [Georgia Power is drilling holes more than a mile underground. Here’s why - Southern Business & Development](https://southernbusiness.com/georgia-power-is-drilling-holes-more-than-a-mile-underground-heres-why/)  ·  `page_060419`

## Facts

- Georgia Power is boring holes thousands of feet into the Earth’s crust at a trio of spots in rural Georgia.
- The company is testing whether rocks as much as 2 miles down are suitable for storing carbon dioxide produced by its oil- and gas-fired power plants.
- The testing evaluates sites for geologic carbon sequestration, a technique that captures CO2, compresses it into a liquid-like state, and injects it into porous rock.
- Georgia Power and its parent company Southern Company state that the testing is preliminary.
- The federal government is pumping millions of dollars into advancing carbon storage technology.
- New, more lucrative tax credits are available for companies that pursue carbon storage.
- New emissions limits for power plants could force many utilities to capture and store CO2 to remain in operation.
