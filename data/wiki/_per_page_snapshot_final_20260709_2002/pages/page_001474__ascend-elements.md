# Ascend Elements

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Can You Buy Ascend Elements Stock Pre-IPO? What to Know in 2026 | The Motley Fool](https://www.fool.com/investing/how-to-invest/stocks/how-to-invest-in-ascend-elements-stock/)  ·  `page_001474`

## Facts

- Founded in 2015.
- Developing a closed-loop system for lithium-ion battery production.
- Sources materials from spent lithium-ion batteries and uses them to produce new batteries.
- In 2023, announced working with Honda to explore the potential for supplying battery materials for the automaker's electric vehicles manufactured in North America.
- Started operations at its first commercial-scale lithium-ion battery recycling facility in Georgia.
- The Georgia facility opened in 2023.
- Closed the Georgia facility for several months in 2025 to address fire concerns.
- In September 2025, produced for the first time lithium carbonate that is more than 99% pure with material recovered from used lithium-ion batteries at its Georgia facility.
- Management targets annual lithium carbonate production of 3,000 metric tons in the Georgia facility.
- Targets more than 15,000 metric tons in the U.S. and Europe by 2027.
- Completed a Series D funding round in February 2024.
- Remains a privately held company with no sign of an IPO planned anytime soon.
- Has raised a total of $1.7 billion over 14 funding rounds according to Crunchbase.
- Generating revenue from processing end-of-life batteries and manufacturing scrap at its Georgia facility, according to a 2022 press release.
- Construction on another facility in Kentucky is currently paused.
- Hopes the Kentucky facility will open in 2026.
- In May 2025, Poland offered the company up to $320 million to construct a facility in Poland.
- Has a $1 billion supply agreement with an unnamed customer for battery materials.
- The supply agreement started in the first quarter of 2025 and has the potential to expand to $5 billion over several years.
