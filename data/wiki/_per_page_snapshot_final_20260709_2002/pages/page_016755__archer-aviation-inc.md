## Facts

- Archer Aviation Inc. is developing electric vertical takeoff and landing (eVTOL) aircraft for commercial air taxi services under its Urban Air Mobility (UAM) sector focus.
- Archer Aviation targets a 2026 U.S. commercial launch for its Midnight eVTOL aircraft.
- The company secured a Part 135 Air Carrier and Operator Certificate from the Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) in June 2024, enabling commercial operations for system refinement.
- Archer Aviation obtained a Part 141 certification from the FAA in February 2025 specifically for pilot training.
- As of August 2025, Archer had completed 15% of its FAA certification compliance documents for the Midnight eVTOL, progressing toward Type Inspection Authorization (TIA).
- United Airlines is a strategic investor in Archer Aviation and holds a provisional order for up to 200 Midnight aircraft.
- Archer Aviation and United Airlines have jointly announced planned routes, including a corridor between Manhattan and Newark Liberty International Airport.
- Global automaker Stellantis partners with Archer Aviation, providing equity capital and critical manufacturing expertise.
- Archer Aviation operates a high-volume manufacturing facility located in Covington, Georgia.
- The Covington, Georgia facility aims to produce up to 650 eVTOL aircraft per year.
- Production of eVTOLs at the Georgia facility commenced in early 2025.
- As of October 2025, six Midnight aircraft are in production at the facility, with three units in final assembly.
- Archer Aviation maintains a liquidity position of approximately $1.7 billion as of Q2 2025.
- The anticipated commercial launch of air taxis is expected to accelerate investment and development for companies involved in vertiport construction and charging infrastructure.
- Archer's progress validates the eVTOL market and attracts investment into ancillary industries including battery technology, advanced materials, air traffic management software, and urban planning for vertiport integration.
- The company's immediate operational focus includes accelerating the FAA Type Certification process for the Midnight aircraft to meet its targeted 2026 U.S. commercial launch.
- Ramping up production at the Covington, Georgia facility is a key indicator of Archer's ability to scale operations and meet future demand.
- Archer Aviation was selected as the Official Air Taxi Provider for the LA28 Olympic and Paralympic Games.
- Archer delivered its first piloted Midnight aircraft to the UAE in August 2025 for flight tests.
- In June 2025, Archer formed a partnership with Jetex in the UAE.
- On October 8, 2025, Archer reached an agreement with Cleveland Clinic Abu Dhabi to establish the UAE's first hospital-based vertiport.
- Archer has commercial deals in Asia with Japan Airlines and Sumitomo Corporation's joint venture, Soracle, for services in Osaka.
