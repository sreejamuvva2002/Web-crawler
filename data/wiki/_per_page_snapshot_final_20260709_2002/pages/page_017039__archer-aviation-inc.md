# Archer Aviation Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-11-14

**Source:** [Archer](https://news.archer.com/archer-aviation-selects-site-in-georgia-adjacent-to-the-covington-municipal-airport-for-its-manufacturing-facility)  ·  `page_017039`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation Inc. announced plans on November 14, 2022, to locate its manufacturing facility in Covington, Georgia, at a site adjacent to the Covington Municipal Airport.
- The selected site is located in Newton County.
- Archer plans to initially build a 350,000 square-foot facility on a 96-acre site.
- The initial facility will be capable of producing up to 650 aircraft per year.
- Construction is expected to be completed in the first half of 2024, with initial production expected to begin in the second half of 2024.
- The facility can be expanded by an additional 550,000 square feet.
- The expansion is estimated to support production of up to 2,300 aircraft per year.
- Archer intends to invest $118 million over the next 10 years.
- Archer will receive an incentive package totaling 33% of its capital investment commitment.
- The incentive package includes land conveyance, tax incentives, and a Georgia REBA grant.
- Archer has entered into a letter of intent with Synovus Bank to obtain financing for the project.
- Synovus Bank is the largest bank headquartered in Georgia.
- Archer completed a comprehensive survey of potential manufacturing sites across the United States before selecting Covington.
- Key site selection considerations included local labor market talent availability, utility availability, ability to conduct seamless flight test operations, construction costs, and logistics.
- The planned eVTOL aircraft manufacturing facility is expected to create over 1,000 jobs over the long term.
- Archer CEO Adam Goldstein stated that Georgia was selected after many months of discussion and evaluation.
- Adam Goldstein noted that Georgia has played a vital role in the U.S. aerospace industry.
- Archer's eVTOL technology is intended to transform how urban and rural communities live and commute.
- The factory is expected to create pathways to highly skilled manufacturing jobs and other ladders of social and economic opportunity.
- Adam Goldstein expressed gratitude to Governor Brian Kemp, Bob Kosek, Sara Gershon, Serra Hall, Frank Turner, and their teams for their support.
- Georgia Governor Brian P. Kemp stated that nurturing innovation is a state priority as they work with businesses to define the future.
- Governor Kemp noted that Georgia's established leadership in the aerospace industry was essential to landing Archer Aviation in Newton County.
- Governor Kemp highlighted that Archer will utilize Georgia's Quick Start workforce training program to help fill over 1,000 long-term jobs in Covington.
- Georgia is home to more than 800 aerospace companies.
- The state has a dozen high schools offering aerospace training, five technical colleges with aviation programs, and several universities offering degrees in aerospace engineering.
- The Georgia Center of Innovation for Aerospace, a strategic arm of the Georgia Department of Economic Development, has supported innovative aerospace companies for over 10 years.
- Archer is designing and developing electric vertical takeoff and landing aircraft for use in urban air mobility networks.
- Archer's corporate team is based in Santa Clara, California.
