## Facts

- Stuart Countess serves as president and CEO of Kia Georgia in West Point.
- Drew Ferguson currently serves as senior vice president of governmental affairs for Hyundai Motor Group in Washington, D.C.
- Kia’s West Point manufacturing facility recently produced its five millionth vehicle.
- Kia launched the first hybrid electric vehicle built in the state of Georgia at its West Point facility.
- Ferguson described Kia's West Point milestone as evidence of the generational impact of economic development decisions made nearly two decades ago.
- Ferguson stated, 'Think about where this community was 20 years ago. Those were generational decisions that brought Kia here, and now we’re seeing the generational success that came from them.'
- Ferguson noted that companies face significant uncertainty related to trade policy, immigration, and tax debates, particularly as negotiations continue with major U.S. trading partners such as Canada and Mexico.
- Ferguson identified four priorities central to community success: access to quality jobs, safe housing, strong educational systems, and the freedom for individuals to build better futures for their families.
- Ferguson highlighted artificial intelligence and digital infrastructure as among the most significant issues facing the nation’s future economy.
