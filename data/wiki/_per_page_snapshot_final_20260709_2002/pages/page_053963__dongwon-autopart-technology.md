# Dongwon Autopart Technology

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [GA: Dongwon Autopart to Invest $30 Million, Create 200 Jobs in Emanuel County | Trade and Industry Development](https://www.tradeandindustrydev.com/region/georgia/news/ga-dongwon-autopart-invest-30-million-create-200-35480)  ·  `page_053963`

## Facts

- Georgia Gov. Brian P. Kemp announced that Dongwon Autopart Technology plans to invest $30 million in a new production facility in Emanuel County.
- The automotive project is anticipated to create 200 new jobs over the next several years.
- Established in 1971, Dongwon Autopart Technology supplies components such as doorframes, seat frames, and battery frames to automotive original equipment manufacturers (OEMs).
- The company supports more than 500 direct jobs in the Southeast, including in Hogansville, Georgia, at facilities that will remain operational.
- Dongwon Autopart Technology has been in the United States since 2003.
- Construction on Dongwon Autopart Technology’s new manufacturing facility will be located at the Hwy 297 Industrial Park near Swainsboro.
- The company will be hiring for positions such as management, system leads, and production personnel.
- Hiring is expected to begin in 2026.
- Representative Butch Parrish, Chairman of the House Rules Committee, stated the Georgia House of Representatives is committed to bringing high-quality jobs to rural communities.
- Emanuel County Development Authority Chairman Will Donaldson welcomed Dongwon Autopart Technology to Emanuel County.
- Ken Warnock, CEO of the Emanuel County Development Authority, assisted Dongwon’s leadership team in locating the site and called it the single largest new economic development project for Emanuel County in over 30 years.
- Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy Alyce Thornhill represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The project was developed in partnership with the Emanuel County Development Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson noted the automotive industry is expanding outward along the I-16 corridor as suppliers choose strategic locations with access to OEMs and Georgia’s ports.
- Dongwon Autopart Technology’s new facility will be its second location in Georgia.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated Georgia’s automotive industry has been creating jobs across the state for more than 100 years.
