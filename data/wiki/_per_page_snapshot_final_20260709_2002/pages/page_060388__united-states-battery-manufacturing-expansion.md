# United States Battery Manufacturing Expansion

## Facts

- Battery manufacturing infrastructure in the United States has undergone dramatic transformation since 2024, with production capabilities expanding from minimal grid storage capacity to comprehensive domestic supply sufficiency.
- 2024 baseline shows 70 GWh annual production capacity across limited facility networks.
- 2025 expansion reaches 145 GWh capacity through foreign investment integration.
- 2026 projections indicate 280 GWh capacity representing 300% growth over two years.
- 2027 targets project 421.5 GWh capacity with mature supply chain networks.
- The Centre on Global Energy Policy documented cost reduction mechanisms driving this growth, noting production cost decreases of up to 30 percent through federal incentive structures.
- Current supplier networks include 10 module assembly operations and projected expansion to 11 suppliers by 2027.
- Great Lakes Corridor operations in Michigan and Ohio have $15+ billion investment focused on LFP battery cells.
- Southeast Hub plants in Georgia and Tennessee have $20+ billion investment focused on module assembly.
- Southwest Expansion facilities in Texas and Arizona have $8+ billion investment focused on system integration.
- Lithium iron phosphate (LFP) chemistry dominates stationary storage applications due to enhanced safety profiles and extended operational lifespans compared to traditional lithium-ion formulations used in transportation applications.
- The Inflation Reduction Act created competitive positioning for domestic manufacturing through multiple incentive mechanisms including manufacturing tax credits, local content requirements, supply chain incentives, and system integration benefits.
- Manufacturing tax credits provide direct production cost reduction averaging 30%.
- Investment data from S&P Global indicates the United States imported over $100 billion in batteries and components since 2021, with approximately 50% sourced from Chinese suppliers.
- Korean manufacturing companies committed approximately $20 billion toward U.S. battery manufacturing expansion between 2025 and 2029.
- Benchmark Minerals projects Korean producers will contribute over 40% of domestic capacity growth during this period.
- Chinese suppliers control approximately 85% of graphite processing, 60% of lithium processing, and 75% of cathode material production.
- LG Energy Solution invested $1.4 billion to establish dedicated lithium iron phosphate production lines with 16.5 GWh current capacity at its Holland, Michigan operation.
- LG Energy Solution projects capacity expansion to 50 GWh by end-2026, representing 200% growth within 18 months.
- Samsung SDI and SK Innovation complement LG's expansion through coordinated facility development across multiple states with combined Korean investment exceeding $20 billion.
- Tennessee, Georgia, and Michigan emerged as preferred locations for Korean companies due to existing automotive manufacturing expertise and logistical infrastructure.
- Tesla Energy operates the largest single-site battery manufacturing facility in California with 40 GWh annual Megapack production capacity.
- Stryten Energy pursues multi-chemistry diversification across lead-acid, lithium-ion, and vanadium redox flow battery technologies and operates 11 existing facilities with planned 10 GW capacity addition across military, grid storage, and transportation market segments.
- International Energy Agency analysis identifies production capacity and technical expertise concentration in Asian markets as primary supply security risks.
- Active materials are primarily supplied by China at a 75% dependency level.
- Precursor chemicals are primarily supplied by China at an 80% dependency level.
- Processing equipment is primarily supplied by China and Korea at a 70% dependency level.
- Quality control systems are primarily supplied by Korea and Japan at a 60% dependency level.
- Training programmes require 6-12 months for technician certification in electrochemical processing operations.
- Energy storage demand growth is 21% annually, driven primarily by renewable energy integration requirements and data centre backup power needs.
- Production capacity growth exceeds 50% annually, creating potential oversupply conditions.
- Domestic production capacity is projected at 280 GWh annually in 2026.
- Projected U.S. demand in 2026 is 200 GWh combined grid and transportation.
- Export potential in 2026 is 40 GWh available for international markets.
- Market concentration involves 10-12 major suppliers competing for market share.
- Data centre expansion represents the fastest-growing battery storage market segment as artificial intelligence and cloud computing requirements drive backup power needs.
- Major facility investments include Toyota-Panasonic in Liberty, NC ($14 billion, 75 GWh, 2025-2027), LG Energy Solution in Holland, MI ($1.4 billion, 50 GWh, 2025-2026), AESC in Tennessee ($2.8 billion, 30 GWh, 2026-2027), and Samsung SDI in multiple states ($5 billion, 45 GWh, 2025-2028).
- Financial constraints and market uncertainty resulted in approximately $8 billion in cancelled investments, including KorePower Arizona ($1.2 billion) and Freyr Georgia ($2.6 billion) projects.
- Project cancellations concentrated amongst smaller companies lacking established customer relationships or proven manufacturing expertise.
- Solid-state battery development threatens current lithium-ion manufacturing investments through superior energy density and safety characteristics, with commercial deployment delayed until 2030 or later.
- Alternative chemistry development includes sodium-ion and iron-air storage systems targeting different market segments than traditional lithium-ion applications.
- Potential industry consolidation by 2027-2028 reflects manufacturing capacity exceeding demand absorption rates.
- Market share concentration amongst 3-5 major producers appears likely as competitive pressures eliminate marginal operations.
- North American integration through Canadian and Mexican partnerships creates natural export opportunities for excess production capacity.
- The U.S. battery manufacturing expansion represents a fundamental restructuring of global energy storage supply chains, transforming America from import-dependent to potentially export-capable within five years.
