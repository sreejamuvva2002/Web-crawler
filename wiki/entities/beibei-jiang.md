# Beibei Jiang

**Type:** Person  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[battery-materials|Battery Materials]]  ·  **Location:** Kennesaw, Georgia, Georgia, United States

## Overview

Assistant professor in the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at Kennesaw State University leading a research team developing a sulfur-modified solid electrolyte for safer, faster solid-state batteries.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Research aims to improve solid-state battery performance and safety for electric vehicles and other energy storage applications.

## Key facts

- The sulfur-modified solid electrolyte reduces resistance, allowing lithium ions to move faster and charge the battery more quickly.
- Jiang’s team discovered a strong interaction between sulfur and zirconium in the ceramic portion of the electrolyte-the first documented identification of this effect.
- Replacing flammable liquid electrolytes with solid materials improves safety by reducing risks of overheating, short circuits, and fires.
- The research has strong potential for electric vehicles, renewable energy storage, and consumer electronics.
- Most work is conducted in Jiang’s lab on Kennesaw State’s Marietta Campus where students synthesize materials and test prototype batteries.
- "Our goal is to replace all those flammable components, so the battery becomes much safer," Jiang said.
- The project received a $200,000 grant from the National Science Foundation plus seed funding from Kennesaw State’s Office of Research.
