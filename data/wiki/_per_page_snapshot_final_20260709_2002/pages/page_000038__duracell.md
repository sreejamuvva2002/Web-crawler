# Duracell

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-02-19

**Source:** [Duracell Selects Georgia for New R&D Headquarters | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-releases/2025/duracell-selects-georgia-new-rd-headquarters)  ·  `page_000038`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced on February 19, 2025 that Duracell will establish its new Global Headquarters for Research and Development at Science Square in Atlanta.
- The new R&D headquarters project involves an investment of approximately $56 million and will create 110 jobs.
- Duracell currently operates a manufacturing facility in LaGrange, Georgia that has been in operation since 1980.
- The LaGrange facility currently supports approximately 400 jobs.
- Duracell also operates a logistics and distribution plant in Fairburn, Georgia that began operations in 2020.
- The Fairburn plant supports an additional 275 jobs.
- Duracell is an American manufacturer of alkaline, lithium coin, and hearing aid batteries.
- Duracell is a leader in the primary battery market in North America.
- The new Global Headquarters for Research and Development will be located at 101 Nerem Street NW in Atlanta.
- Science Square is adjacent to Georgia Tech’s Midtown Atlanta campus.
- Science Square is an 18-acre multi-phase development centered on innovation featuring more than 1.8 million square feet of lab and office space.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Georgia has set itself apart as a leader in attracting innovative companies due to its research institutions, world-class logistics network, and pro-business environment.
- Mayor Andre Dickens noted that Atlanta’s transportation infrastructure, diverse talent pool from top-tier universities, and thriving tech ecosystem make the city ideal for corporate innovation and growth.
- Mayor Andre Dickens highlighted that the investment of more than 100 new jobs will provide opportunities for Atlanta residents to build promising careers.
- Katie Kirkpatrick, President & CEO of the Metro Atlanta Chamber, stated that Duracell's decision strengthens the region’s reputation as a hub for innovation and furthers Georgia's growing battery ecosystem.
- Katie Kirkpatrick noted that Duracell is locating literally next door to world-class talent at Georgia Tech and in close proximity to tens of thousands of new graduates in the region.
- Robb Pitts, Chairman of the Fulton County Board of Commissioners, stated that Duracell’s choice solidifies Fulton’s leadership in innovation and talent attraction.
- Assistant Director of Statewide Projects John Soper represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The GDEcD partnered with Invest Atlanta, Select Fulton, Metro Atlanta Chamber, Georgia Power, and the University System of Georgia for this competitive project.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that locating the new Global Headquarters for Research and Development in Atlanta makes it clear that Duracell sees the State of Georgia as a long-term partner in its success strategy.
- The Duracell brand and company was started in the 1920s.
- Berkshire Hathaway Inc. acquired Duracell in 2016.
- Berkshire Hathaway Inc. is a $250 billion holding company owning subsidiaries that engage in diverse business activities.
