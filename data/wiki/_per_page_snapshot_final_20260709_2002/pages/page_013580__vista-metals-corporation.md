# Vista Metals Corporation

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Vista Metals | Projects | Choate Construction](https://choateco.com/project/vista-metals/)  ·  `page_013580`

## Facts

- Choate Construction is returning to the Vista Metals campus in Adairsville, GA, to construct a massive pit for a Lithium Furnace.
- The Lithium Furnace pit is located within a pre-engineered metal framed building and measures 50 feet by 30 feet with a depth of 35 feet.
- Construction of the Lithium Furnace pit requires engineered shoring systems, confined-space entrance safety measures, and extra thick concrete slabs and walls.
- Additional features for the Lithium Furnace pit include casting pit process piping work, air piping, gas piping, casting pit steam exhaust pits, and miscellaneous trenches.
- The project includes multiple ledges, haunches, abutments, embedded steel items, and concrete elevation changes typical with equipment pits.
- This expansion adds a 23,000 SF aluminum billet cast house to the Vista Metals Corporation foundry campus in Adairsville, GA.
- The facility expansion increases capacity at the foundry to supply raw aluminum billets used by aluminum extruders such as Universal Alloy in Canton, Georgia.
- This project marks the third collaboration between Choate Construction and the valued repeat client Vista Metals Corporation.
- The casting pit construction consists of a 45-foot deep casting pit, a lube pit, a pump pit, a casting cart pit, and steam exhaust pits.
- Foundations added include bases for an aluminum pre-heater, a cooling tower, and two tilting melting furnaces.
- Shoring is required for roughly 20 feet by 30 feet to a depth of 45 feet for the casting pit.
- A dewatering system is installed during construction, with the water table at the casting pit located at 21 feet below the finish floor.
- The casting pit is built from the bottom up upon completion of the dewatering system.
