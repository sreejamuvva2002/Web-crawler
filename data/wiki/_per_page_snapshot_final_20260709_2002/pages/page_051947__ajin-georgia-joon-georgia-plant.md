# Ajin Georgia (Joon Georgia plant)

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-12-07

**Source:** [Ajin gives $40K to community, school and public safety agencies here while factory is being built - Statesboro Herald](https://www.statesboroherald.com/local/ajin-gives-40k-community-school-and-public-safety-agencies-here-while-factory-being-built/)  ·  `page_051947`

## Facts

- Ajin Georgia is a stamped-metal auto body parts manufacturer building a factory in Bulloch County’s Bruce Yawn Commerce Park.
- The facility will serve as a supplier to Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- During a luncheon, Ajin Georgia presented a total of $40,000 in donations to 12 local community service, public safety, and educational organizations.
- The project was originally announced in November 2022 under the name Joon Georgia plant.
- The plant under construction operates under the name Ajin Georgia as part of the South Korea-based Ajin Corporation.
- The company is committed to creating more than 600 jobs.
- The project was initially announced last year as a $317 million investment.
- The projected cost of building and equipping the facility has been revised to $278 million.
- The plant will be 853,000-square-foot.
- Ajin executives stated that October is now the target month for the factory to begin production.
- Hiring for production jobs is expected to begin in the early part of 2024.
- Jamie Calloway serves as the Human Resources General Manager for Ajin Georgia and acted as master of ceremonies at the luncheon.
- Senior Human Resources Manager Jaewook “Jake” Jeong was present at the luncheon.
- Five other Ajin Georgia executives and managers originally from Korea were introduced at the event.
- Ajin USA CEO Jung-Ho Sea toured the plant site the week of Thanksgiving.
- Each of the 12 recipient organizations received a check (amounts varied) and a small “Ajin Georgia” sign for display on their premises.
- Public safety recipients included the Bulloch County Fire Department, Bulloch County Sheriff’s Office, and Bulloch County Emergency Medical Service.
- Community service recipients included the Statesboro Kiwanis Club, Safe Haven (Citizens Against Violence shelter), the Boys & Girls Club of Bulloch County, the Bulloch County Recreation and Parks Department, and the city of Statesboro’s Village Builders youth initiative.
- Education recipients included Bulloch County Schools, Evans Region College and Career Academy (based in Claxton), Metter College and Career Academy, and the Ogeechee Technical College Foundation.
- Aerial photos from October showed the factory buildings taking shape behind the TA Travel Center off U.S. Highway 301 at the I-16 interchange.
- A video was shown highlighting operations at the established Ajin USA plant in Cusseta, Alabama, which has been in production since 2009.
- The new Georgia facility will use blanking presses, stamping presses, and die cutters to shape metal into parts that comprise much of the framework of passenger vehicles.
- Parts-handling robots will be used at almost all of the work stations in the new facility.
- Ajin Georgia is one of three plants being built in Bulloch County by companies that will supply different kinds of components to Hyundai Motor Group's Metaplant electric vehicle and battery manufacturing complex under construction in northern Bryan County.
- As of December 7, job listings showed seven managerial and administrative positions available, with no production jobs listed yet.
