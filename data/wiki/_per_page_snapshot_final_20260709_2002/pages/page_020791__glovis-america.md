# Glovis America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-06

**Source:** [Hyundai Glovis to Deploy 21 Hydrogen Fuel-Cell Trucks for Metaplant America Logistics](https://evmagz.com/hyundai-glovis-to-deploy-21-hydrogen-fuel-cell-trucks-for-metaplant-america-logistics/)  ·  `page_020791`

## Facts

- Glovis America has announced plans to deploy 21 units of the Hyundai Xcient hydrogen fuel-cell trucks to support operations at the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) electric vehicle manufacturing facility in Georgia.
- The fuel-cell trucks will transport vehicle parts from HMGMA suppliers to the factory, marking a significant step towards sustainable logistics.
- The Xcient hydrogen fuel-cell trucks are part of the Class 8 heavy-duty category and will make up more than one-third of Glovis America’s truck fleet at HMGMA.
- Each truck is equipped with two 90 kW hydrogen fuel cell systems, providing a total output of 180 kW.
- The trucks are paired with a 350 kW electric motor and offer a range of over 450 miles per charge.
- A mobile hydrogen refueling station will be established at the Georgia Megasite to support the trucks.
- Plans exist for a permanent hydrogen production and refueling station to be announced in the future.
- Oscar Kwon, CEO of HMGMA, stated that HMGMA is committed to creating sustainable transportation solutions.
- The Hyundai Xcient hydrogen fuel-cell trucks release zero emissions and offer a cleaner alternative to traditional freight logistics methods used at other manufacturing facilities.
- This initiative highlights Hyundai’s ongoing commitment to integrating sustainable solutions in its operations, especially in the supply chain of its manufacturing facilities.
- The introduction of hydrogen-powered logistics is expected to play a crucial role in reducing emissions and enhancing the green credentials of Hyundai’s operations in the U.S.
