# SK On

## Facts

- SK On is headquartered in Seoul, South Korea.
- SK On was spun off from SK Innovation in July 2021 and launched as an independent entity in October 2021.
- SK On is a subsidiary of SK Innovation, which is a member of SK Group.
- SK On's batteries are used for electric vehicles and energy storage systems (ESS).
- From 2024 to 2025, SK Trading International, SK Enterm, and SK Enmove were merged into SK On.
- In December 2022, SK On announced a new battery plant joint venture with Hyundai in Cartersville, Georgia.
- In February 2023, SK On withdrew from a joint venture with Ford and Koç Holding in Turkey.
- In August, construction started on a cathode material plant joint venture with EcoPro BM and Ford in Bécancour, Quebec.
- Construction on the Bécancour, Quebec cathode material plant was paused multiple times.
- In November 2024, SK Trading International, a crude oil and petroleum products affiliate, merged into SK On.
- In February 2025, SK Enterm, a commercial tank terminal operator under SK Trading International, merged into SK On.
- In March, SK On secured a battery supply contract with Nissan.
- In April, Slate selected SK On as its battery supplier.
- In July, SK On announced the acquisition of SK Enmove, a lubricant maker under SK Innovation.
- In September, SK On completed construction on an all-solid-state battery plant in Daejeon, South Korea.
- In November, SK On swapped stakes in its two joint ventures with Eve Power in China and took full ownership of the Jiangsu site.
- In December, SK On and Ford announced the dissolution of their joint venture BlueOval SK, with SK On taking ownership of the Tennessee battery facility.
- In March 2026, SK On laid off more than a third of its workforce at its plant in Commerce, Georgia.
- In 2019, SK Innovation began construction of its first U.S. electric vehicle battery manufacturing facility in Commerce, Georgia.
- The Commerce, Georgia plant is a 2.4 million-square-foot facility located on 283 acres along Interstate 85, approximately 65 miles northeast of Atlanta.
- The Commerce, Georgia plant was completed in two phases: Phase 1 in 2020 and Phase 2 in late 2021, at a combined investment of approximately $2.6 billion.
- The Commerce, Georgia plant supplies batteries to Volkswagen and Hyundai.
- Following the October 2021 spin-off, the Commerce, Georgia plant became part of SK On.
- SK On's manufacturing locations include Seosan (South Korea), Commerce (United States), Stanton (United States), Kingston (United States), Yancheng (China), Beijing (China), Changzhou (China), Komárom (Hungary), and Iváncsa (Hungary).
