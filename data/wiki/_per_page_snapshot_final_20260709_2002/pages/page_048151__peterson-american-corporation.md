## Facts

- Established in 1914, Peterson Spring is the largest privately held spring manufacturer in North America.
- The company designs, manufactures, and supplies springs, snap-rings, wire forms, clips, clamps, stampings, and a variety of sub-assemblies.
- Peterson Spring has expanded to 12 worldwide locations.
- The company address is 21200 Telegraph Road, Southfield, Michigan, United States ZIP: 48033.
- The mission of Peterson American Corporation is to work with customers to provide the best products available to the world market.
- The company aims to improve quality and reduce costs through practical, innovative thinking while maintaining equitable relationships with customers, employees, shareholders, and communities.
- Peterson Performance Culture emphasizes discipline, hard work, integrity, operational and business excellence, and innovation by all.
- The company's history begins in the 1880s with the immigration of Norwegian blacksmith August Christian Peterson to America.
- August studied blacksmithing and gradually rose through spring manufacturing ranks in the early 1900s.
- In 1914, August moved his family to Detroit to form a company meeting demand for springs in the railroad industry and the budding automobile industry.
- August worked with his eldest son Alfred, who left school after eighth grade to work in steel mills' spring plants.
- Other sons Sigurd, Conrad, and Harold joined the business later; Sigurd made the greatest contribution as a mechanic who developed innovative machines.
- Precision Spring prospered alongside the automobile industry and post-WWII manufacturing explosion.
- The 1960s and 1970s saw overseas expansion through purchases of foreign firms and joint ventures.
- In 1978, the company split into two halves with one renamed Peterson American Corporation.
- The entire Peterson family of companies was brought back under the PAC umbrella in 1991 when they purchased the assets of the departed half.
- Peterson Spring is a founding member of the Spring Makers Institute (SMI) since its inception in 1933.
- Former employees Bud and Pete Peterson, grandson and great-grandson of founder August Christian Peterson, have both served two-year terms as president of SMI.
- In 1989, Bud received the SMI’s Golden Coil Award for contributions to the spring industry.
- Numerous Peterson Spring employees have served on SMI committees.
- Peterson American Corporation operates 13 facilities worldwide with a geographic footprint allowing customer focus and competitiveness.
- All facilities are TS/ISO/QS certified and hold numerous customer achievement and recognition awards.
- Decades of innovating thinking in the high-performance automotive market have given Peterson Spring knowledge to thrive in fields implementing springs.
- Peterson’s metallurgists, engineers, and designers create springs used daily in products ranging from lawnmowers to military applications.
