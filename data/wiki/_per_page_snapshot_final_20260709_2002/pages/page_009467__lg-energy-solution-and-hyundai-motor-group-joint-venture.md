# LG Energy Solution and Hyundai Motor Group Joint Venture

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-05-26

**Source:** [Hyundai, LG Energy to build $4.3 billion EV battery plant in U.S.](https://www.cnbc.com/2023/05/26/hyundai-lg-energy-to-build-4point3-billion-ev-battery-plant-in-us.html)  ·  `page_009467`

## Facts

- South Korea's Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution announced they will build a $4.3 billion electric vehicle battery plant in the United States.
- The companies are building the plant amid a push to take advantage of tax credits.
- Manufacturers must adhere to new U.S. sourcing requirements for EV battery components and critical minerals so that buyers of their vehicles can qualify for up to $7,500 in tax credits under the Inflation Reduction Act.
- Vehicles from Hyundai Motor and sister automaker Kia are currently not eligible.
- Construction of the factory in the state of Georgia will begin in the second half of 2023.
- Battery production at the facility is scheduled to start at the end of 2025 at the earliest.
- The plant will have an annual production capacity of 30 gigawatt-hours.
- This capacity is enough for 300,000 EVs.
- Hyundai Motor Group, the world's third-largest automaker by vehicle sales, is building EV and battery manufacturing facilities in Bryan County in Georgia.
- The joint factory with LGES will be based in Bryan County.
- LGES and Hyundai Motor Group will each own 50% of the joint venture.
- Hyundai Motor Group houses Hyundai Motor, Kia, and auto parts maker Hyundai Mobis.
- LGES supplies automakers including Tesla and General Motors.
- LGES CEO Youngsoo Kwon said in a statement, 'Two strong leaders in the auto and battery industries have joined hands, and together we are ready to drive the EV transition in America.'
- In April, Hyundai Motor finalized a $5 billion EV battery joint venture in the United States with SK On.
- SK On is a battery unit of SK Innovation.
- The joint venture with SK On is intended to boost electrification efforts in Hyundai's largest market.
