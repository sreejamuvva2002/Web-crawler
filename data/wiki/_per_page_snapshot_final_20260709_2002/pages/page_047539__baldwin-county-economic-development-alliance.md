# Baldwin County Economic Development Alliance

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2023-03-23

**Source:** [How JobsEQ Has Helped Baldwin County EDA Nurture 21% Job Growth in Transportation/Warehousing Since 2015](https://www.chmura.com/blog/baldwin-county-jobseq-21-percent-job-growth)  ·  `page_047539`

## Facts

- Baldwin County Economic Development Alliance subscribed to JobsEQ by Chmura in 2015 to identify target industries that would bring high-wage jobs and diversify the county's economy.
- Baldwin County EDA identified the Transportation/Warehousing sector as a key industry, noting an average wage of $49,000 in the sector compared to $39,000 county-wide.
- Baldwin County EDA attracted an Aldi distribution center and an Imperial Dade logistics hub, bringing a total of 250 new jobs to Baldwin County.
- Imperial Dade is investing $20 million in a 220,000 square foot new logistics hub, expanding on an existing facility.
- The bid for the $100 million Aldi distribution center was competitive, with JobsEQ data helping demonstrate a large base of underemployed people in Baldwin County to attract the project.
- Baldwin County EDA worked closely with the local school system to create a full-time Career Tech high school launching in the 2024-2025 school year.
- The new Career Tech high school will train students in aerospace, maritime, welding, healthcare, information technology, and other programs.
- Baldwin County EDA used JobsEQ data to highlight University of South Alabama, Coastal Alabama Community College, and the new Career Tech High School as future talent pipeline sources for Novelis.
- In May 2022, Novelis Inc. selected the South Alabama Mega Site in Baldwin County for a new fully integrated aluminum mill facility.
- Novelis announced plans to invest more than $2.5 billion into building the zero-waste, renewable energy powered facility.
- Novelis' facility will create approximately 1,000 new jobs and generate indirect roles.
- The average annual salary projected for Novelis' facility is $65,000.
- Novelis plans to expand the facility in the future to capture ongoing demand.
- 44% of the workforce living within Baldwin County commutes out of the county for work each day.
- Baldwin County is the 7th fastest-growing Metro area in the United States, ranking in the Top 3% of 3,143 counties for net incoming domestic migration.
- JobsEQ data projects Baldwin County's population to reach nearly 300,000 by 2034.
- The South Alabama Mega Site is a 3,000-acre county-owned site marketed for large industrial employers.
- Greg Canfield, Secretary of the Alabama Department of Commerce, stated that Novelis' decision to locate its technologically advanced aluminum mill in Baldwin County highlights the state's attractive business climate and talented manufacturing workforce.
- Baldwin County EDA uses JobsEQ to compile data on the local labor force, identify worker surpluses, and answer "What-If" analytic questions regarding economic benefits for potential projects.
