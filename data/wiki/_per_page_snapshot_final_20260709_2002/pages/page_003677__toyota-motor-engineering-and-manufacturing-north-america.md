# Toyota Motor Engineering and Manufacturing North America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Toyota Motor Engineering and Manufacturing North America: Supply Chain-Treasure Hunt Program | Better Buildings & Better Plants Initiative](https://betterbuildingssolutioncenter.energy.gov/implementation-models/toyota-motor-engineering-and-manufacturing-north-america-supply-chain)  ·  `page_003677`

## Facts

- The Treasure Hunt program’s purpose includes promoting awareness and benefits of energy reduction within industrial plants.
- The program aims to find new ways to identify energy efficiency opportunities through facility-wide Treasure Hunts.
- The program shares technical resources with other partners through tools, knowledge, and resources.
- The program builds strong relationships with national suppliers.
- The program’s creation reflects Toyota’s commitment to sustainability.
- Toyota believes sustainability creates three values for society: Safety and Peace of Mind, Environmental Sustainability, and Waku-doki (excitement and exhilaration that wows you).
- Toyota has placed the environment as a management priority in all stages of vehicle production.
- Every five years Toyota establishes the Toyota Environmental Action Plan consisting of three priority themes: contribution to a low carbon society, contribution to a recycling-based society, and environmental conservation and contribution to a society in harmony with nature.
- To go beyond zero environmental impact and achieve a net positive impact, Toyota has set six challenges for itself.
- Challenge 1 is the New Vehicle Zero CO2 Emissions Challenge.
- Challenge 2 is the Life Cycle Zero CO2 Emissions Challenge.
- Challenge 3 is the Plant Zero CO2 Emissions Challenge.
- Challenge 4 is the Challenge of Minimizing and Optimizing Water Usage.
- Challenge 5 is the Challenge of Establishing a Recycling-based Society and Systems.
- Challenge 6 is the Challenge of Establishing a Future Society in Harmony with Nature.
- Toyota’s Supplier Relations team works closely in the Treasure Hunt recruitment process.
- Together with the Purchasing team, Toyota sends out a voluntary application process for suppliers interested in the program.
- The Purchasing team functions as the main point of contact for supplier applications.
- Suppliers are asked if they are willing to open their facility to Toyota and non-competitive suppliers for a complete energy audit.
- Once the Purchasing team identifies four suppliers (one per quarter), Toyota’s facility engineers are selected to facilitate the Treasure Hunt.
- The Supplier Relations team helps promote the program through a network of suppliers interested in water and energy efficiency savings.
- Preliminary steps before the on-site Treasure Hunt include an introductory call to familiarize the host supplier with the program and Toyota’s facility engineers.
- An in-depth Data Collection call is held next, allowing Toyota to do initial research.
- The team collects key data metrics including total electricity use, HVAC tonnage, lighting systems, exhaust fans, and hours of operation.
- Once data is aggregated and potential resources are identified, a Kick-off Meeting is scheduled.
- The Kick-off Meeting is set up over web conference and brings together the host supplier, Toyota’s facility engineers, and other non-competitive Toyota suppliers participating in the Treasure Hunt.
- Program logistics include Toyota delivering a Detail Sheet along with specific energy calculation tools.
- Teams of 5 to 10 are formed, consisting of a mix of members from every party.
- The Treasure Hunt begins on Sunday so teams can monitor the facility while it’s still asleep (production offline yet energy still being consumed).
- Teams arrive early Monday morning to see the facility wake up, and return on Tuesday to observe break times (lunch) throughout the day.
- Total onsite analysis typically takes over 4 hours.
- Each team concludes their findings Tuesday afternoon.
- Dozens of savings opportunities are likely identified, but only the most cost-effective projects (3-5) are selected.
- Once a presentation is assembled (~10 Slides), the host supplier takes ownership of the project and delivers it to senior management.
- Toyota provides a Detail Sheet (calculators) during the first Data Collection call.
- The sheet aggregates provided information and runs a multivariate regression analysis.
- For every Treasure Hunt, there are typically as many as 150 energy efficiency ideas.
- Toyota’s calculation sheet contains a built-in macro which ranks the top energy and water savings opportunities.
- Supplier success is measured by potential dollars saved.
- For Toyota, a more energy-efficient supply chain can cut down on costs, improve production reliability and transparency, and improve competitiveness.
- Toyota bases its success on replicability for other facilities.
- Since FY08, 40 Suppliers have hosted training events.
- Employees from 180 Tier 1 suppliers have participated and been trained on the Treasure Hunt process.
- There has been $4.4 M in total annual energy reduction opportunities identified.
- The identified savings are equivalent to 43.5 million kilowatt-hours or 15,200 metric tons of CO2 per year.
- A detail sheet tool is used to track energy data and progress towards energy savings.
