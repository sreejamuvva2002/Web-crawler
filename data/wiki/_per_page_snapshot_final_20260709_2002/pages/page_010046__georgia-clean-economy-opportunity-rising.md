# Georgia Clean Economy: Opportunity Rising

**Type:** research_center  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-02-16

**Source:** [New analysis shows Georgia clean energy investments are paying off for workforce and economy - SaportaReport](https://saportareport.com/new-analysis-shows-georgia-clean-energy-investments-are-paying-off-for-workforce-and-economy/sections/reports/mark-lannaman/)  ·  `page_010046`

## Facts

- A report titled “Georgia Clean Economy: Opportunity Rising” was released at the Georgia Clean Energy Summit in Atlanta.
- The report was produced for Environmental Entrepreneurs (E2) and features economic analysis and predictive models by BW Research.
- Predictions include 403,000 new jobs nationwide, with 100,000 being permanent positions.
- Predictions include $156 billion added to U.S. GDP from clean energy investments.
- Predictions include $111 billion in new wages for workers across the U.S.
- Predictions include more than $32 billion generated in tax revenue for federal, state, and local governments.
- Researchers tracked 19 clean energy projects in Georgia launched between August 2022 and August 2023.
- The report predicts 38,600 jobs will be created over a five-year construction period for these 19 Georgia projects.
- An additional 6,800 jobs per year are predicted to be created annually after the construction phases for these projects.
- $13.9 billion is predicted to be added to Georgia’s Gross State Product (GSP) during the construction phase of the tracked projects.
- $926.5 million is predicted to be added to Georgia’s GSP annually during the operational life of the projects.
- Economic impact was measured using direct, indirect, and induced effects.
- Billions were poured into Georgia from the Biden administration’s Inflation Reduction Act (IRA), which was signed into law in 2022.
- EVs accounted for 49 percent of capital investments among the major clean energy categories tracked.
- Battery storage accounted for 27 percent of capital investments.
- Solar accounted for 24 percent of capital investments.
- Wind, electric transmission & distribution, and clean fuels were negligible on this scale.
- Stuart Countess, president and CEO of Kia Georgia, served as the keynote speaker at the summit.
- Kia plans to build the Kia EV9 in Georgia.
- Kia hopes to have the EV9 hitting the road by summer of 2024.
- Bob Keefe, executive director of E2, stated that clean energy technology has finally caught up with environmental problems, making deployment economically feasible.
- E2’s motto is “Good for the economy. Good for the environment.”
- New administrations or policy rollbacks could negatively impact Georgia’s clean energy investment direction and opportunities.
- Bob Keefe noted that creating jobs and driving economic growth were historically bipartisan goals in Georgia.
- E2 has operated for 23 years and utilizes conservative models and estimates for its economic projections.
- An estimated $14 billion will be added to Georgia’s GSP during the construction phase of the 19 tracked projects.
- Nearly $1 billion is expected to be added to Georgia’s GSP during the operational phase.
- An estimated $2.6 billion in tax revenue is projected during the construction phase of these projects.
- Georgia’s state budget for the year is $36 billion.
