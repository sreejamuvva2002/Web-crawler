# FREYR Battery

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-11-11

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp: Battery Manufacturer to Invest $2.57B, Create Over 700 Jobs in Coweta County | Governor Brian P. Kemp Office of the Governor](https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases/2022-11-11/gov-kemp-battery-manufacturer-invest-257b-create-over-700-jobs-coweta)  ·  `page_000002`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced on November 11, 2022 that FREYR Battery will invest $2.57 billion into Georgia’s sustainable technology ecosystem.
- The investment will create 723 new jobs over the next seven years at a manufacturing facility in Coweta County.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated that job creators and innovators from around the world bring operations to Georgia because they know they will have success there, welcoming FREYR as the latest company to bring transformational investments and opportunity to communities.
- FREYR is dedicated to supporting a domestic supply chain for renewable power sources.
- The company’s battery cells can be used for stationary energy storage (ESS), electric mobility, and additional applications.
- FREYR’s Co-Founder and Chief Executive Officer Tom Einar Jensen stated the company is deeply committed to decarbonizing the transportation and energy sectors with U.S. partners and expects meaningful investments to spur job creation and eventual development of localized, decarbonized supply chains in the U.S.
- The new facility will be located at the Bridgeport Industrial Park site, which has been zoned for a mix of heavy and light industrial use since 2014.
- The company will be hiring for technicians, operators, engineers, executive, and administrative roles.
- The facility, to be called Giga America, will have a planned first phase production of approximately 34 gigawatt hours.
- The facility will use the U.S.-based 24M Technologies SemiSolidTM platform to simplify the production process and increase manufacturing efficiency.
- FREYR is currently evaluating clean power supply solutions for the new facility, including the potential use of renewable energy from solar installations.
- Coweta County Development Authority Chair John Daviston stated the authority is extremely pleased to welcome FREYR Battery to the community and excited about the opportunities and addition of quality and highly-skilled jobs that will provide a significant stimulus to the local economy.
- Metro Atlanta Chamber President and CEO Katie Kirkpatrick stated metro Atlanta has quickly become a hub for battery cell research and production, and FREYR Battery will benefit from the vibrant research ecosystem and diversity of talent in the region.
- Project Manager John Soper represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project in partnership with Coweta County Development Authority, the Metro Atlanta Chamber, Georgia EMC, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated companies like FREYR are driving a global revolution based on sustainable technology and solutions, and that electrification is changing how entire cities are powered while the state develops a battery ecosystem with manufacturers, recyclers, and customers to support home and commercial storage.
- Georgia’s prime location, extensive infrastructure, skilled workforce, and business-friendly climate has made it an attractive location for a diverse array of rapidly developing industries focused on creating a sustainable future.
- Georgia is cultivating a vertically integrated supply chain that will help companies increase efficiencies by reducing reliance on imported materials.
- FREYR Battery aims to provide industrial scale clean battery solutions to reduce global emissions and is listed on the New York Stock Exchange.
- FREYR’s mission is to produce green battery cells to accelerate the decarbonization of energy and transportation systems globally.
- FREYR has commenced building the first of its planned factories in Mo i Rana, Norway.
- FREYR announced potential development of industrial scale battery cell production in Vaasa, Finland, and the United States.
- FREYR intends to install 50 GWh of battery cell capacity by 2025, 100 GWh annual capacity by 2028, and 200 GWh of annual capacity by 2030.
