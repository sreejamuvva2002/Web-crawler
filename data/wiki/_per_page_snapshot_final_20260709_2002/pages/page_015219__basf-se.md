## Facts

- BASF SE is a German multinational chemical company headquartered in Ludwigshafen am Rhein, Germany.
- It is the largest chemical producer worldwide by revenue.
- As of end 2023, BASF employed around 112,000 people globally.
- Under a strategy called Winning Ways, BASF is reshaping its portfolio and how it manages its businesses distinguishing between core and standalone businesses to optimize focus, capital allocation, and operational efficiency.
- In the 2000s, BASF acquired major chemical businesses including Engelhard Corporation (2006), the construction chemicals business of Degussa AG, and Ciba (2008–2009).
- More recently, completed January 31, 2024, BASF took over one of the two MDI plants from a joint venture in China previously shared with Huntsman Corporation.
- In Asia, BASF invests heavily in regional R&D through its Innovation Campus Shanghai, serving as BASF’s largest R&D site in Asia and collaborating with local institutions for materials, catalysis, specialty chemicals, process and digitalization research.
- At the 2025 industry event PU TECH 2025 (Greater Noida, India), BASF showcased high performance PU solutions designed for lower environmental footprint including synthetic leather for automotive seating with reduced greenhouse gas emissions, and PU composites for building insulation and PV frame applications.
- BASF is enhancing materials for next generation batteries including Debonding on Demand adhesives, battery potting systems, and advanced surface treatments/coatings for battery packs to support performance, safety, and recyclability.
- In 2025, BASF unveiled a new advanced facility in Ludwigshafen dedicated to process catalysts R&D aimed at bringing lab developed catalysts rapidly into production and strengthening its catalyst technology leadership.
- In 2024, BASF spent around €2,061 million on R&D globally.
- Around 10,000 employees worldwide work in R&D for BASF.
- Over the past five years to 2024, innovations newly launched by BASF have generated about €11 billion in sales.
- In 2025, BASF presented progress under its Winning Ways strategy confirming financial targets for 2028 and reaffirming its focus on integrated core businesses plus selective standalone units.
- As part of that strategy, BASF decided to retain the standalone business BASF Environmental Catalyst and Metal Solutions (ECMS), which generated €7.0 billion in sales in 2024.
- The global polyurethane market is experiencing rapid growth motivated by demand across the automotive industry for durable, lightweight, and versatile materials.
- Lightweight polyurethane materials improve fuel efficiency and vehicle performance, with applications including automotive panels and EV battery casings.
- The automotive segment for polyurethane is seeing a notable increase due to increasingly increasing use of interiors, seating, and coatings for durability and comfort.
- The EV trend is driving innovative and higher adoption of PU products in the automotive sector.
- North America expects significant growth in the polyurethane market driven by robust demand in the automotive industry.
- The U.S. dominates the polyurethane market backed by a thriving automobile sector and expanding infrastructure initiatives, with strong demand for flexible foam coatings and adhesives.
- Manufacturers in the U.S. are spending more money on cutting-edge PU technologies to satisfy performance and sustainability standards aligned with stringent environmental laws.
- Growing demand for materials in electric vehicles including batteries, lightweight plastics, and coatings represents a key opportunity for chemical suppliers.
