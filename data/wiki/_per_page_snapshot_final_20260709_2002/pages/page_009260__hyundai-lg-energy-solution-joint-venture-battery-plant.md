# Hyundai–LG Energy Solution Joint Venture Battery Plant

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-05

**Source:** [Immigration raid targets Hyundai EV plant construction site in Georgia | Fox News](https://www.foxnews.com/us/federal-agents-arrest-hundreds-hyundai-plant-construction-site-georgia)  ·  `page_009260`

## Facts

- Homeland Security Investigations (HSI) announced the arrest of 475 illegal migrants during a major immigration enforcement raid on Thursday at a Hyundai electric car battery factory in Georgia.
- HSI Atlanta Special Agent in Charge Steven Schrank noted that while the raid was at a Hyundai facility, not all the migrants worked for the parent company and some worked for subcontractors at the site.
- Steven Schrank stated during a news conference on Friday: 'We are sending a clear and unequivocal message that those who exploit our workforce, undermine our economy and violate federal laws will be held accountable.'
- Several other agencies, including U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE), took part in the raid at the Hyundai–LG Energy Solution battery plant construction site in Ellabell, which is located about 20 miles outside Savannah.
- The raid was the largest single site operation HSI has carried out and follows major raids at other workplaces over the last few months under the Trump administration.
- The facility, a $4.3 billion to $7.6 billion Hyundai–LGES joint venture, is slated to supply batteries to Hyundai’s nearby Savannah EV plant and stands as the largest single industrial investment in Georgia’s history.
- Construction at the site, which stretches over 3,000 acres, has been suspended, but there was no interruption at the adjacent EV manufacturing facility.
- The South Korean foreign ministry expressed 'concern and regret' over the raid and sent a counselor and embassy officials to the location.
- Lee Jae-woong, a spokesperson for South Korea’s foreign ministry, said on Friday: 'Our companies’ economic activities and our people’s rights should not be infringed unfairly in the US legal enforcement process.'
- Georgia State Patrol troopers blocked roads to the Hyundai site, and the Georgia Department of Public Safety confirmed they were dispatched to assist federal authorities.
- The raid comes after South Korea agreed to $350 billion worth of investments in the U.S. as part of a trade deal struck in July.
- LGES told the Financial Times that the battery plant under construction is scheduled to begin operations at the end of this year.
- LGES told Fox News Digital: 'We are closely monitoring the situation and gathering all relevant details. Our top priority is always ensuring the safety and well-being of our employees and partners. We will fully cooperate with the relevant authorities.'
- Hyundai said it is aware of the raid and believes that none of its workers were detained.
- Hyundai told Fox News Digital: 'As of today, it is our understanding that none of those detained is directly employed by Hyundai Motor Company. We prioritize the safety and well-being of everyone working at the site and comply with all laws and regulations wherever we operate.'
- The Department of Homeland Security said ICE HSI and other law enforcement offices 'executed a judicial search warrant as part of an ongoing criminal investigation into allegations of unlawful employment practices and other serious federal crimes.'
- DHS told Fox News Digital: 'This operation underscores our commitment to protecting jobs for Georgians, ensuring a level playing field for businesses that comply with the law, safeguarding the integrity of our economy, and protecting workers from exploitation.'
- DHS stated the investigation is focused on ensuring accountability for those who violate the law and upholding the rule of law, requiring collaboration with the U.S. Department of Labor Office of Inspector General, FBI, DEA, Customs and Border Protection, ATF, IRS, and the Georgia State Patrol.
- The department noted that each agency contributed specialized expertise that was critical to the success of this lengthy investigation.
- Video posted to social media Thursday showed workers in yellow safety vests lined up as a man wearing a face mask and tactical vest with HSI letters announced they have a search warrant for the whole site.
- The man in the video stated: 'We need construction to cease immediately. We need all work to end on the site right now.'
