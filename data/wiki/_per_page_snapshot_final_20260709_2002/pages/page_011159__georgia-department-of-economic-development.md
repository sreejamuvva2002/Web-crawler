# Georgia Department of Economic Development

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2022-03-31

**Source:** [New Georgia Economic Development Records Set for Jobs, Investment in Just Nine Months - Savannah CEO](https://savannahceo.com/features/2022/04/new-georgia-economic-development-records-set-jobs-investment-just-nine-months/)  ·  `page_011159`

## Facts

- Governor Brian Kemp and GDEcD announced that through Q3 of FY2022 (July 1, 2021 - March 31, 2022), job creation and investments from economic development projects surpassed FY2021 year-end totals.
- 251 project locations supported by GDEcD’s Global Commerce team will result in the creation of more than 35,400 new jobs and $12.9 billion in investment for the state.
- Job totals account for a 48% increase compared to three-quarters through the previous fiscal year and a 6% increase compared to the prior year’s overall total.
- Total investment saw a recorded 54% increase compared to the same time period last year.
- Nearly 78% of both projects and investments were located outside the 10-county metro Atlanta area, with over half of the jobs created also outside this region.
- Key industry sectors driving growth include automotive, advanced manufacturing, logistics and distribution, software and technology, and food processing.
- Georgia’s electric vehicle (EV) and sustainability ecosystems have grown tremendously over the past year.
- The state is continuing to recruit the entire EV supply chain to ensure an effective, efficient, and affordable environment for EV production.
- Rivian plans to develop a $5 billion campus at Stanton Springs North.
- Rivian also plans an electric vehicle service center in Atlanta that will provide training on EVs.
- SK Battery America has partnered with American lithium-ion battery recycling and engineered materials startup Ascend Elements to recycle cell and module lithium-ion battery manufacturing scrap from SK’s Jackson County facility.
- Ascend Elements announced its first commercial-scale battery recycling plant in Covington in January.
- Aurubis will invest $340 million in a ‘first-of-its-kind in the U.S.’ state-of-the-art recycling and secondary smelting facility for multi-metal recycling in Augusta.
- Aspen Aerogels announced plans to locate a new 500,000-square-foot advanced manufacturing facility in Statesboro.
- Aspen Aerogels will produce PyroThin materials that provide thermal management, mechanical stability, and fire protection properties to EV battery systems at its new Statesboro facility.
- Georgia Quick Start, the No. 1-ranking workforce training program, helped secure numerous projects across key industries.
- The Georgia Quick Start program has provided specialized training tailored specifically to business needs for more than 40 years and is administered through the Technical College System of Georgia.
- The Georgia Ports Authority (GPA) deepwater and regional ports projects have played a key role in securing diverse projects, supporting logistics and supply chain infrastructure.
- For the past 19 consecutive months, the GPA has been reporting record-breaking numbers as the port expedites plans for long-term growth.
- The Savannah Harbor Expansion Project (SHEP), a joint state and federal project, was recently completed, deepening a 40-mile stretch between the port and the Atlantic Ocean to accommodate super-sized freighters from the expanded Panama Canal.
- Inland ports are attracting investments, such as the Appalachian Regional Port driving Roper Corporation’s decision to invest $118 million in expanding its facilities in Walker County.
- Hartsfield-Jackson International Airport and the state’s extensive rail network serve as key assets driving Georgia’s growth.
- The Georgia Department of Transportation maintains more than 1,200 miles of highway connecting companies to 80% of the country in two days or less of driving time.
- This transportation infrastructure was integral to landing Ryder System Inc.’s new facility in Henry County that will create more than 250 new jobs.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that targeting new strategic industries and supporting existing business expansions are central to the department's mission to prove Georgia is where companies want to be.
