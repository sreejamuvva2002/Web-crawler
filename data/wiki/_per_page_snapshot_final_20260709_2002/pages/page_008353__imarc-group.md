## Facts

- General Motors Co. announced an investment of USD 491 Million in September 2022 to increase the manufacturing of steel and aluminum stamped parts for future products, including electric vehicles, at its Marion metal stamping facility in Indiana.
- Toyota Tsusho America, Inc. and Southern Mobility Products formed a joint venture named Madison Metal Processing (MMP) in Huntsville, Alabama, in February 2022 to focus on blanking and storing steel goods such as hot-rolled steel, cold-rolled steel, and exposed and coated steel.
- GSC Steel Stamping, LLC acquired Dixien LLC's assets in January 2024 to continue its 43-year tradition in automobile parts stamping.
- The United States recorded 1.4 million new electric car registrations in 2023, marking a market disruption with EV adoption rates increasing by more than 40% compared to 2022.
- Automotive manufacturers are shifting towards electric and hybrid vehicles, creating a growing need for specialized metal components for battery enclosures, powertrain components, and charging infrastructure.
- Carmakers are pushing for design innovations that bolster the requirement for advanced stamping technologies like progressive die stamping and deep drawing to meet EV demands for lighter materials such as aluminum.
- The automotive industry is a major driver of metal stamping demand, utilizing stamped parts for body panels, engine parts, transmission systems, chassis components, brackets, and structural reinforcements.
- Steel accounted for the largest market share in 2025 due to demand for stamped components made from steel materials, which are crucial for automotive body panels and chassis components due to high strength and cost-effectiveness.
- Mechanical presses led the market in 2025 and are widely used in automotive manufacturing for producing complex and precision components such as car body parts, engine components, and transmission parts.
- The U.S. metal stamping market is growing due to a high defense budget, industrial demand, civilian manufacturing stability, and significant demand for automobile component production.
- Increasing drives for reshoring manufacturing coupled with reducing reliance on imports are strengthening domestic U.S. markets.
- Federal policies and increasing awareness regarding sustainability are spurring innovation in the U.S. metal stamping industry.
