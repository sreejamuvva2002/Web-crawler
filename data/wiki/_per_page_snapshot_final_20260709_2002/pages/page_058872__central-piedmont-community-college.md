# Central Piedmont Community College

**Type:** workforce_training  ·  **Role:** workforce_training  ·  **Published:** 2024-06-17

**Source:** [Rick Hendrick and Hendrick Automotive Group make gift of land to Central Piedmont for new training center | Central Piedmont](https://www.cpcc.edu/news/rick-hendrick-and-hendrick-automotive-group-make-gift-land-central-piedmont-new-training-center)  ·  `page_058872`

## Facts

- Rick Hendrick and Hendrick Automotive Group donated approximately 23 acres of land to Central Piedmont Community College.
- The donated property is adjacent to the college’s Levine Campus in Matthews.
- Central Piedmont will use the donated land to build a new first responder training facility.
- The gift continues a long-time relationship between Central Piedmont, the Hendrick family, and Charlotte-based Hendrick Automotive Group.
- The college opened the Joe Hendrick Center for Automotive Technology in 2006 at the Levine Campus.
- The Joe Hendrick Center is named for Joseph R. Hendrick Jr., father of Rick Hendrick.
- Each year, the Joe Hendrick Center serves more than 3,000 students aspiring to be automotive technicians as well as employed technicians seeking updated skills.
- A gift from the Hendrick family allowed the college to construct the Joe Hendrick Center.
- Dr. Kandi Deitemeyer is Central Piedmont president.
- The new first responder training center will include facilities for law enforcement, fire and rescue, EMS, forensics, detention, and telecommunicators.
- Outdoor training spaces at the new center will include a scenario training village and emergency driving range.
- Indoor facilities at the new center will provide a firing range and a fire and rescue area with specialized equipment for comprehensive training.
- The first responder training center will be constructed in phases.
- Phase one of the new center is scheduled to open in 2027.
- Phase two of the new center is scheduled to open in 2028.
- Rick Hendrick serves as chairman and CEO of Hendrick Automotive Group.
- Groundbreaking for the Joe Hendrick Center occurred in 2005.
- Central Piedmont offers an associate degree program in Public Safety Administration.
- The two-year Public Safety Administration program provides students with knowledge and skills in technical, managerial, and administrative areas necessary for entrance or advancement within public safety and government organizations.
- Graduates of the Public Safety Administration program can pursue careers in fire and police departments, emergency management organizations, governmental agencies, industrial firms, correctional facilities, private industries, insurance organizations, educational organizations, security and protective organizations, and through self-employment opportunities.
