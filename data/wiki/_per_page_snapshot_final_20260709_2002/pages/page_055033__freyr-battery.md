# Freyr Battery

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-02-10

**Source:** [Battery firm abandons plan for a $2.6 billion plant in Georgia | Georgia Public Broadcasting](https://www.gpb.org/news/2025/02/10/battery-firm-abandons-plan-for-26-billion-plant-in-georgia)  ·  `page_055033`

## Facts

- Freyr Battery told officials in Newnan on Thursday that it would not build a $2.6 billion plant.
- The abandoned plant was supposed to hire more than 700 people.
- Freyr sent a Jan. 21 letter to the Coweta County Development Authority announcing its plans to end the project.
- The planned factory would have built batteries to store electricity produced by renewable sources and release it later.
- When announced in 2023, the project would have been the second-largest battery factory worldwide.
- Freyr is a startup founded in 2018.
- Freyr never began construction on the 368-acre (149-hectare) site.
- Freyr moved its corporate headquarters from Norway to Newnan in part to maximize eligibility for U.S. tax benefits under President Joe Biden's climate law.
- Freyr is shifting its focus to a newly opened solar panel factory it bought last year for $340 million from top Chinese solar panel maker Trina Solar.
- Freyr spokesperson Amy Jaick stated the company is grateful for support in Coweta County and Georgia but is currently focusing on its solar module manufacturing facility in Texas.
- The Newnan Times-Herald first reported the story.
- Freyr senior vice president of business development Jason Peace met with local officials on Thursday.
- Jason Peace told Coweta County Development Authority board members the decision was driven by rising interest rates, falling battery prices, a change in company leadership, and a shift in goals.
- Coweta County Development Authority President Sarah Jacobs confirmed the meeting and reasons in an email on Friday.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development conveyed a $7 million grant to buy a site for Freyr in Newnan.
- Newnan is located about 35 miles (55 kilometers) southwest of Atlanta.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development spokesperson Jessica Atwell said the state and company are working together to ensure the $7 million grant is repaid expeditiously.
- Freyr may also owe money to Coweta County.
- Jessica Atwell stated that Georgia's incentives process protects taxpayers and ensures discretionary incentives are repaid when a company's plans change.
- Sarah Jacobs said planning for the Freyr project made Coweta County a stronger candidate for future projects.
- The company had previously planned to build battery factories in Norway and Finland.
- In November, the company announced it would try to sell its European business.
- The company is terminating its license for battery-making technology and paying $3 million to the licensing company.
- Tom Einar Jensen, then the company's CEO, told investors in August that raising money to make batteries had become difficult due to a surplus of lower-cost Chinese batteries.
- The company switched its strategy to businesses that would allow it to raise cash, including solar panel manufacturing.
- The company's cash on hand fell from $253 million at the end of 2023 to $182 million on Sept. 30.
- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp has targeted the recruitment of the electric vehicle industry.
- Korean firm SK Innovation built a $2.6 billion battery plant in Commerce, northeast of Atlanta, hired 3,000 workers, and later laid off or furloughed some workers.
- Hyundai Motor Group has started production at a $7.6 billion electric vehicle and battery plant near Savannah, with plans to hire 8,500 workers.
- Electric truck maker Rivian revived plans to build a plant east of Atlanta after receiving a $6.6 billion loan from the Biden administration.
