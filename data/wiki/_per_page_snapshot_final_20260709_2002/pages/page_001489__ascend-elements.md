# Ascend Elements

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2025-11-19

**Source:** [Ascend Elements: Strategic Player in Li-ion Battery Recycling and High-Value IP for CAM Technologies](https://www.knowmade.com/technology-news/energy-technology-news/batteries-news/ascend-elements-a-dynamic-player-in-the-competitive-space-of-li-ion-battery-recycling/)  ·  `page_001489`

## Facts

- Ascend Elements is a recycling company focused on Li-ion batteries.
- The company was founded in 2015 as a spin-out of the Worcester Polytechnic Institute to develop commercially its research activities ongoing since 2011 to develop NMC recycling methods.
- Its industrial activities were first implemented in its headquarters state of Massachusetts before expanding to Michigan and Georgia from 2020 onward.
- Recycling of Cathode Active Materials (CAM) is Ascend Elements’ core business, represented by the introduction of its Hydro-to-Cathode® technology for CAM precursors (pCAM) synthesis process.
- The company delivered its first commercial shipment of refined pCAM in the form of Lithium Carbonate (Li2CO3) in early 2025.
- Ascend Elements expanded its view overseas to Europe, partnering with Strategic Elemental Metals to establish a joint venture on Polish ground.
- The commercial expansion led to the creation of the joint venture AE Elements in partnership with Elemental Strategic Metals to further develop industrial capabilities in Europe, starting from Poland and planning to build another facility in Germany.
- Ascend Elements’ published patent portfolio comprises 35 patent families grouping 119 individual patents, with most focusing on protecting CAM-related methods and processes and a clear predominance of lithium ternary oxides.
- Most patents were published over the past 3 years, with a noticeable peak in 2024, and the USA remains the main jurisdiction where patents are filed, followed by Europe, China, and South Korea.
- The years 2023-24 saw the most IP activity, with over half of the total Ascend Elements’ patent publications, while 2025 saw patent filings only in the USA and PCT applications.
- One patent family from Ascend Elements has been cited an impressive 232 times, while a handful of others have received up to 30 citations each.
- A key patent (US10522884) discloses a method and apparatus for LIB recycling that allows recovering elements such as Co, Ni, Mn, and Al at the same time from black mass recovered from Li-ion batteries.
- The patented invention isolates key metals from Cathode Active Materials without a preferred cathode chemistry, though it is particularly apt to recover metals commonly used in CAM such as NMC, NCA, and LCO.
- The method involves preliminary leaching with Sulfuric Acid and Hydrogen Peroxide at mild temperatures, followed by precipitation of metals hydroxides using Sodium Hydroxide, pH adjustment, calcination to obtain pristine CAM, and isolation of Lithium Carbonate.
- Another patent (JP7649364) reports a method to recover Graphite from black mass resulting from crushing Li- or Na-based batteries, involving impurity removal, alkaline treatment, washing, and sintering for high purity.
- A patent from partner Elemental Strategic Metals (PL2146860) describes an electrochemical method to recover cobalt, nickel, and manganese(IV) oxide from electrolytes obtained from leaching black mass, depositing Co and Cu on the cathode and MnO2 on the anode in the first step, followed by Ni deposition on the cathode.
- Dr. Filippo Farina, a Technology Analyst at KnowMade, highlighted that IP competition across the globe for LIB recycling is fierce, fast-paced, and multi-faceted.
