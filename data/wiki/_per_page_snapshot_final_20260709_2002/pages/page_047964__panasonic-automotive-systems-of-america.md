# Panasonic Automotive Systems of America

## Facts

- Panasonic Automotive Systems of America is based in Peachtree City, GA.
- At CES 2021, Panasonic Automotive introduced its new Augmented Reality (AR) HUD.
- The AR HUD utilizes advances in optics, volume optimization, and imaging technology combined with AI from the SPYDR cockpit domain controller.
- The system renders near-field and far-field content for vehicle information like speed, object and pedestrian detection, and mapping/route guidance.
- Scott Kirchner serves as president of Panasonic Automotive and executive director of Panasonic Smart Mobility.
- Panasonic's AR HUD solutions cover more of the roadway than traditional HUDs by displaying traditional cluster content in the near field and 3D overlays in the far field.
- The AR HUD could provide an added level of comfort and assurance for AV passengers in a future with more self-driving vehicles.
- The system projects 3D, AI-driven key information into the driver's line of sight to help reduce driver distraction and potentially increase safety.
- Panasonic's AR HUD development utilizes a PRIZM process addressing precise placement, reflection via AI smart optical graphic road overlays, intuitive focus discrimination, zonal UX optimized field-of-view organization, and mission control dynamic imaging.
- Eye tracking technology projects information at the driver's level of sight based on eye position to eliminate mismatch when the driver moves their head.
- Advanced optics provide an expanded field-of-view beyond 10 by 4 degrees for a virtual image distance of 10m or greater.
- The advanced optics detect pedestrians and objects through enhanced low light and nighttime view, with tilted virtual image planes adjusting visibility.
- An embedded camera system allows discrete monitoring for the driver's eye location.
- AI-driven AR navigation technology detects and provides multi-color 3D navigation graphics that adjust with the moving vehicle's surroundings, displaying lane markers and GPS arrows where turns will occur.
- A proprietary camera image stability algorithm enables AR icons to lock onto the driving environment regardless of road bumpiness.
- Driving environment updates for ADAS, AI, and AR environment information occur in real-time in less than 300 milliseconds.
- The system includes a 3D imaging radar providing sensor-captured full 180-degree forward vision up to 90 meters and across approximately three traffic lanes.
- The AR HUD features efficient compact packaging designed to fit any vehicle configuration.
- The display offers 4K resolution using advanced laser and holography technology for static near-field cluster information and far-field image plane for AR graphic overlay.
- Panasonic collaborates with Envisics for striking, dual plane, high-resolution laser holography via a patent-protected dynamic holographic platform.
- Panasonic collaborates with Phiar for 3D localization technology and AI navigation and situation awareness analytics via a patent-protected spatial-AI, AR navigation platform.
- Panasonic Automotive announced two variants of in-vehicle wireless charging technology: moving coil and static coil.
- The wireless charging technology aims to provide efficient in-vehicle charging that meets or exceeds most current in-vehicle charging systems.
- The moving coil variant uses exclusive detection technology with patent-pending tracking to target a device and move the coil into an optimum position for alignment.
- The moving coil variant has better coupling capability compared to other wireless charging systems.
- The moving coil variant delivers 15W of charging power, providing one of the fastest charging times in the industry that rivals wired/tethered systems.
- Both static and moving coil variants support NFC BT/Wi-Fi connectivity, allowing calls and app use to continue running while the device is charging.
- Both wireless charging variants are set to follow the Qi 1.3 charging standard.
