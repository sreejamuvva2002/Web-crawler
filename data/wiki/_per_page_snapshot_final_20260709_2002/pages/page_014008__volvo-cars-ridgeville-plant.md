# Volvo Cars Ridgeville Plant

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-23

**Source:** [Volvo's Ridgeville plant gears up for expansion with new models and increased production](https://abcnews4.com/news/local/volvos-ridgeville-plant-gears-up-for-expansion-with-new-models-and-increased-production-charleston-county-manufacturing-jobs-trump-tarrifs-wciv-abc-news-4-9-23-2025)  ·  `page_014008`

## Facts

- Volvo Cars announced plans to ramp up production at its Ridgeville plant as part of continued investment in the facility.
- Luis Rezende, president of Americas for Volvo Cars, stated the company is starting an ambitious journey to transform its U.S. business and expand growth in the country.
- Charleston serves as the foundation to build Volvo's future, according to Rezende.
- With $1.3 billion already invested in the plant over the last decade, Volvo plans to expand its output in the U.S. in 2026 by adding another car to the production line.
- CEO Håkan Samuelsson said if the company is going to be serious and present on the American market, it also needs to be present industrially.
- Volvo is ready to take the next step to make sure that the Charleston factory becomes the crucial part of its growth strategy.
- Volvo first broke ground at the Ridgeville location in 2015.
- The Ridgeville plant has a production capacity of 150,000 cars a year.
- Plant officials stated that the facility is not utilizing full capacity.
- In May, Volvo Cars made a "small reduction" to its workforce at the plant amid trade uncertainty.
- The workforce change was part of a shift to "regionalization" in response to what a spokesperson described as "challenging macro conditions."
- Officials claim the high-capacity plant, equipped with advanced technology and capable of producing multiple platforms and models, is the focus of renewed domestic investment.
- CEO Håkan Samuelsson stated that employees are proudly assembling the company's flagship, the EX 90, which is built here for exports to around the world, mainly to Europe.
- The plant currently only produces two cars: the fully electric EX90 SUV and the Polestar 3.
- President of Americas Luis Rezende said the company aims to build where it sells, meaning big plans for Charleston.
- Volvo aims to ensure that what is produced in Charleston over the next five years will reflect what customers buy.
- Starting in late 2026, the plant will include production of Volvo's bestselling XC 60.
- Officials said adding the XC 60 is a smart move since the company has sold over 27,000 units since the beginning of the year, a 20% increase from 2024.
- South Carolina Governor Henry McMaster stated that Volvo's investment sends a message to international investors looking for places to invest hundreds of millions and billions of dollars that this is the place.
- Governor McMaster, a Republican, presented awards to Volvo executives and thanked them for investing in South Carolina.
- Governor McMaster noted that more and more people will be educated and trained to do jobs that become more sophisticated every day.
- The governor emphasized that when people have working jobs, it supports happy families and generates tax money needed for roads and schools.
- U.S. Representative Nancy Mace joined the event and said she is proud that Volvo is continuing to invest in the Lowcountry and will provide more job opportunities.
- Rep. Mace noted there were two employees at the event who have been working with Volvo for 28 years.
- She also met many folks this morning who have been working the line there for years.
- The event marked Volvo's 10-year anniversary at the Ridgeville plant.
- Rep. Mace highlighted the importance of education, not just college education in South Carolina, but also vocational training.
- The Swedish company has plants around the world, with the Lowcountry location being key to its success in the U.S. today.
- Chief Industrial Operations Officer Francesca Gambina stated that Charleston produces less than 5% of U.S. volumes with the rest coming from Europe.
- Volvo aims to produce more than 50% of the U.S. volume right at the Charleston facility in five years.
- The company plans to add a fully hybrid model to the South Carolina production line before 2030.
