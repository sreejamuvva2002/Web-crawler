# JTEKT North America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-12-07

**Source:** [JTEKT North America expands manufacturing operation in Greenville County](https://www.upstatescalliance.com/announcements/jtekt-north-america-expands-manufacturing-operation-in-greenville-county/)  ·  `page_045349`

## Facts

- JTEKT North America is expanding its manufacturing operation in Greenville County, South Carolina.
- The expansion involves a $48.4 million investment.
- The investment will create 87 new jobs.
- JTEKT North America is part of JTEKT Corporation.
- The company manufactures products under the brands JTEKT Automotive Systems, Koyo Bearings, and Toyoda Machine Tools.
- JTEKT North America is a global leader in engineering and manufacturing automotive systems, bearing solutions, and high-performance machine tools.
- In addition to its regional headquarters in Greenville, South Carolina, the company operates 12 manufacturing sites, three technical centers, and five distribution centers throughout North America.
- The expansion adds seven new machining lines to the company's existing facility.
- The expansion also includes two new assembly lines at the existing facility.
- Additional processes will be added to machine components internally at the facility.
- The existing facility is located at 1866 Old Grove Road in Piedmont, South Carolina.
- The expanded facility will gain the capability to produce front drive shafts.
- Operations for the expansion are scheduled to come online in the third quarter of 2025.
- JTEKT Americas President and CEO Gary Bourque stated that the investment reflects absolute confidence in current and future associates, as well as South Carolina's leadership, while delivering next-generation solutions to customers and trusted brands.
- South Carolina Governor Henry McMaster praised the state's exceptional workforce for allowing companies like JTEKT North America to thrive and expressed anticipation for the expansion's impact on Greenville County.
- South Carolina Secretary of Commerce Harry M. Lightsey III noted that JTEKT North America contributes to the state's reputation as an automotive powerhouse and that the investment will advance manufacturing potential by providing automakers with additional products built in-state.
- Greenville County Council Chairman Dan Tripp expressed pride in supporting the expansion, citing the automotive sector as a target industry, and highlighted Greenville's growing talent pool, strong technical college system, and robust business climate.
