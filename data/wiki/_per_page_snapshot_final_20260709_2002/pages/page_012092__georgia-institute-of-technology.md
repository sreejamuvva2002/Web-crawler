# Georgia Institute of Technology

**Type:** research_center  ·  **Role:** workforce_training  ·  **Published:** 2022-09-02

**Source:** [Building Georgia with AI and Manufacturing | George W. Woodruff School of Mechanical Engineering](https://www.me.gatech.edu/news/building-georgia-ai-and-manufacturing)  ·  `page_012092`

## Facts

- Georgia Institute of Technology received a $65 million grant from the U.S. Department of Commerce’s Economic Development Administration for the Georgia AI Manufacturing (GA-AIM) Technology Corridor.
- The grant was announced by President Joe Biden on September 2, 2022.
- The funding is part of the $1 billion Build Back Better Regional Challenge (BBB) competition under the Investing in America’s Communities initiative within the American Rescue Plan Act of 2021.
- Georgia Tech was selected as one of 21 Phase II awardees in the BBB competition.
- The largest of nine projects within the GA-AIM corridor will transform the Advanced Manufacturing Pilot Facility (AMPF) into the Artificial Intelligence Manufacturing Pilot Facility (AI-MPF).
- The AMPF is located on 14th Street, about a mile north of Georgia Tech's campus off Hemphill Avenue.
- The facility currently spans 24,000 square feet and will more than double in size following the grant.
- AMPF serves as a flagship component of the Georgia Tech Manufacturing Institute (GTMI) and functions as a testbed for scaling basic research into implementable technologies coupled with education and workforce training.
- Research fields at AMPF include additive/hybrid manufacturing, composites, digital manufacturing/Industry 4.0, and industrial robotics.
- Aaron Stebner, an associate professor in the George W. Woodruff School of Mechanical Engineering, outlined a plan in his 2019 cover letter to establish Georgia Tech as a world leader in additive manufacturing of solid materials (ceramics & metals) R&D, fusing data sciences and AI.
- Stebner predicted the goal would take 10-15 years but noted it is being realized two years after his arrival on campus.
- Stebner stated that AMPF has been a shell waiting for a vision like Build Back Better to fill it out, and the transformation aims to create one of the nation's first manufacturing labs designed for autonomy to help the state and nation become world AI manufacturing leaders.
- Prior to Georgia Tech, Stebner built an additive manufacturing technology center and a public-private innovation consortium at the Colorado School of Mines, working with Colorado companies to integrate machine learning.
- Stebner identified that bottlenecks in using AI in additive manufacturing stem from inconsistent human processes in acquiring and processing data rather than AI technologies themselves.
- He emphasized that computers and machines excel at consistent repetition without boredom or creativity-driven deviations, contrasting them with human operators.
- Stebner argued the country greatly underutilizes machine learning in manufacturing, comparing current usage to using a supercomputer for handheld calculator tasks.
- He advocated for a new mindset mimicking supercomputers making unfathomable calculations and stated that a facility autonomously creating and curating AI data is necessary for manufacturing innovation leaps.
- Stebner noted it is too early and high-risk for the federal government to establish a national autonomous manufacturing lab, positioning Georgia and Georgia Tech as ideal for a first prototype with AI-MPF.
- Manufacturing is Georgia's second-leading employer behind real estate, with diverse sectors including defense, food, timber, and energy spread throughout the state.
- Georgia Tech produces approximately 4% of the nation's mechanical engineers.
- Devesh Ranjan, Eugene C. Gwaltney, Jr. School Chair at the Woodruff School, stated the school prides itself on being an inclusive, innovative, and thriving educational and research environment, and the grant will enable researchers to collaborate for a better local, national, and global future.
- Tom Kurfess serves as GA-AIM's regional economic competitiveness officer and GTMI's executive director.
- Kurfess recently returned to campus after working on leave as chief manufacturing officer at Oak Ridge National Laboratory (ORNL) from 2019-2021, where he handled strategic planning for advanced manufacturing.
- Kurfess previously led the advanced manufacturing team at the White House Office of Science and Technology Policy for the Obama administration from 2012-2013.
- Kurfess holds the HUSCO/Ramirez Distinguished Chair in Fluid Power and Motion Control and is a professor in the Woodruff School.
- He highlighted that industry is growing rapidly in Atlanta and the southeast, with Georgia Tech researchers studying machines, robots, manufacturing plants, and the entire supply chain to integrate technology, people, and privacy via GA-AIM.
- Raheem Beyah, Dean of the College of Engineering and Southern Company Chair, noted that workforce development sometimes takes a backseat to research but GA-AIM provides funding to tie workforce development with AI research to create a new generation of engineers and technicians for Georgia.
- GA-AIM aims to revolutionize the state's industrial economy by developing and deploying talent and innovation in AI across all manufacturing sectors, with transforming AMPF being just one component.
- The initiative aligns with Georgia Tech's strategic focus on expanding access and empowering people of all backgrounds and life stages.
- Women currently comprise 18% of Georgia's manufacturing workforce, with GA-AIM planning to improve this to 23% over the next five years.
- Black workers currently make up 6% of the state's manufacturing workforce, with a target of 15% over the next five years.
- Additional initiatives will reskill Georgia workers across all demographics, with special focus on rural and distressed counties.
- Kurfess emphasized addressing the national need for a workforce with greater technology and manufacturing capability, stating it is Georgia Tech's responsibility to upskill mid-career workers facing technology disrupters.
- Tim Brown, managing director of Georgia Tech’s Supply Chain and Logistics Institute (SCL), is tasked with developing executive education and youth- and minority-focused programs to upskill workers around Georgia using GA-AIM.
- Brown will divide the Peach State into four regions to capitalize on each area's manufacturing strengths and needs.
- Savannah's regional focus will be on shipping and distribution operations, along with reskilling veterans and military families from nearby bases.
- Albany's priorities will center on training military families and community members to stimulate the local economy, leveraging its proximity to the Marine Corps Logistics Base.
- Dalton's focus will target the flooring industry, while Gainesville area initiatives will cover poultry-related industries and the expanding advanced manufacturing community in northeast Georgia.
- Over the next five years, Brown and his team will hold virtual sessions and travel to each region to help companies and employees test new processes and learn emerging technologies.
- Brown described the initiative as creating change agents and exposing middle managers to technology to improve manufacturing and supply chain resilience, develop business cases for change, and sell executives on technology-enabled investments.
- The team's role in the Build Back Better initiative includes examining logistical flows and planning approaches alongside manufacturing processes to improve overall supply chain resilience.
- Brown will start with 25 students and four certificate courses per region, totaling 100 people per year, and will establish industry councils in each region to develop pilot projects for state manufacturers.
- A second outreach initiative led by Woodruff School senior research engineer Roxanne Moore will focus on inventiveness and entrepreneurship in K-12 schools and expand partnerships with technical colleges and minority-serving institutions.
- This project aims to teach up to 1,000 students and 100 teachers from underserved areas, focusing on rural communities via Georgia Tech programs, nonprofits, the Technical College System of Georgia, Southwest Georgia Regional Commission, local manufacturers, and K-12 school leaders.
- Georgia Tech’s K-12 InVenture Prize education program and competition will expand offerings through partnerships with GoSTEM to better serve Latino populations.
- GA-AIM will support an existing high school entrepreneurship program in Fitzgerald and work with Albany State University to host teacher workshops, support local schools, and host regional competitions focusing on inventiveness and entrepreneurial mindset.
- Moore stated the goal is to illustrate relationships between innovation, entrepreneurship, and manufacturing so students see how ideas come to life and improve communities, hoping regional ecosystems become role models for expanding high-quality STEM access to diverse students.
- The $65 million grant comes 21 years after the groundbreaking of Tech Square, which grew from 3 acres to 13 acres and now houses the highest density of startups, corporate innovators, and research centers in the Southeast.
- Kurfess sees similarities between AMPF's location off 14th Street and Hemphill Avenue and Tech Square's expansion, noting AMPF is already surrounded by local businesses, student housing, and accessible via Georgia Tech’s Stinger bus system.
- He predicted that in five or ten years, AMPF will become a societal juggernaut similar to Tech Square, prompting people to look back at the moment as a crazy time at Georgia Tech.
- GA-AIM has the capability to expand Georgia Tech's impact on the state beyond its research institute role.
- Stebner noted that rural companies often take years to fill machine vision jobs due to an inability to train local workers and reluctance of Atlanta residents to relocate.
- He stated the state needs people in rural areas and rural communities need them, with GA-AIM creating programs that train the trainer where they live.
- On campus, the focus on creating an autonomy-capable facility at AI-MPF gives Georgia Tech a national and world-leading resource to create breakthroughs in AI usage and manufacturing advancement over the next several decades or longer.
