# Novelis Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2010-02-12

**Source:** [Novelis Inc. relocates North American headquarters](https://www.wtvm.com/story/11975969/novelis-inc-relocates-north-american-headquarters/)  ·  `page_047399`

## Facts

- Novelis Inc., a world leader in the production of flat-rolled aluminum products, will relocate its North American headquarters from Cleveland, Ohio to Atlanta, Georgia.
- The relocation will add 80 corporate jobs to Novelis' Atlanta staff.
- Novelis' Atlanta staff is expected to include 220 people by the end of 2010 when combined with other expected staff additions.
- Atlanta was already home to the company's world headquarters, which located to Georgia in 2005.
- Georgia Governor Sonny Perdue announced the relocation and stated that the headquarters' consolidation is a testament to the benefits of Georgia's pro-business environment.
- Novelis produces an estimated 19 percent of the world's flat-rolled aluminum products.
- The company is also a global leader in aluminum can recycling.
- Novelis currently has about 12,000 employees in 11 countries spanning four continents.
- The North American headquarters relocation to Atlanta is part of a series of steps over the past year designed to realign global operations, consolidate corporate functions, enhance organizational effectiveness, increase efficiencies, and reduce costs.
- Philip Martens, president and chief operating officer of Novelis, stated that moving the North American headquarters to Atlanta will streamline processes and better align the North American business group with the global corporate center.
- Customers served by Novelis include major brands such as Coca-Cola, Anheuser-Busch, Ford, General Motors, Lotte Aluminum, Kodak, and ThyssenKrupp.
- The company is the leading producer of aluminum rolled products in Europe and South America.
- Novelis is the second leading producer of aluminum rolled products in both North America and Asia.
- Hans Gant, senior vice of economic development for the Metro Atlanta Chamber, said the consolidation validates that Atlanta continues to be a great location for headquarters, particularly for multinational companies.
- Gregg Simon, manager of business engagement for the Atlanta Development Authority (ADA), expressed delight at helping entice Novelis to expand its Atlanta headquarters and highlighted the positive business climate offered in the city of Atlanta.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) partnered with the Metro Atlanta Chamber of Commerce and the Atlanta Development Authority on this project.
- GDEcD statewide project manager Blair Lewis assisted the company with the relocation.
