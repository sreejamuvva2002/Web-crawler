# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2023-12-14

**Source:** [Georgia: 50 Years in Japan and Europe | Georgia](https://georgia.org/blogs/50-years-japan-and-europe)  ·  `page_004927`

## Facts

- Atlanta and Savannah serve as primary global gateways for people and products through air, sea, and rail.
- State leadership under then-Governor Jimmy Carter began to establish Georgia’s physical presence abroad fifty years ago.
- Coryn Marsik serves as Director of International Investment at the Georgia Department of Economic Development.
- The State of Georgia’s international office in Tokyo was first established in 1973, providing direct access to rapidly developing markets in East Asia.
- Japanese companies continue to be the top international employer in Georgia.
- Georgia maintains active ties with organizations such as the Japan-America Society of Georgia and the Japan External Trade Organization.
- Georgia is a charter member of the Southeast U.S.-Japan Alliance.
- The State of Georgia’s Europe Office was established on the heels of opening its Japan Office in 1973.
- Professionals for the Europe Office serve the European market from Munich, Germany.
- A mission to Europe in 2020 propelled bringing mobility of the future to the forefront of Georgia’s manufacturing recruitment plans.
- Governor Brian Kemp and a State of Georgia delegation met with automotive companies headquartered in Germany, where electrification and other alternative fuels were repeatedly discussed.
- Following the 2020 mission, the team came back with a new goal to identify opportunities at every step of the supply chain.
- Just three years later, the mobility and battery supply chain have announced more than $25.5 billion in investments.
- These announced investments are estimated to create 30,700 new jobs over the next few years.
- GEDIA Automotive, Aurubis AG, and Heliox are European-based companies that are enhancing Georgia’s mobility ecosystem.
- The success of Georgia’s mobility and clean tech industries thrust the state to platforms like the 2023 World Economic Forum in Davos-Klosters, Switzerland.
- Governor Brian Kemp served on a panel at the 2023 World Economic Forum, stating that 'one thing you can count on is stability and a great economy, a great business environment in the State of Georgia.'
- Ellen Kraft serves as Managing Director of the Europe Office.
- The Europe Office highlights expanding ties with European partners, including the new North Rhine-Westphalia (NRW) office in Atlanta.
- Commissioner Pat Wilson explains how Georgia is reaping the seeds sown decades ago by visionary leaders.
