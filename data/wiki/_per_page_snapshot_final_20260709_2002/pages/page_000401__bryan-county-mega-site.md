# Bryan County Mega-site

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2021-06-04

**Source:** [Governor Brian Kemp visits Bryan County mega-site, calls on companies to buy in](https://www.wtoc.com/2021/06/04/governor-brian-kemp-visits-bryan-county-mega-site-calls-companies-buy/)  ·  `page_000401`

## Facts

- Georgia leaders announced the biggest economic development site purchase in state history.
- The development covers more than 2,000 acres located in Bryan County.
- The site is intended to attract companies seeking speed to market for new business operations.
- The state is working in partnership with the Georgia Ports Authority and the Department of Economic Development to attract investors.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that few states can assemble a site ready to go to market as quickly as this property.
- Governor Kemp credited local elected leaders, taxpayers, and economic development teams for advancing the site to its current stage.
- Governor Kemp called on major original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) to set up operations at the site.
- The site is positioned as one of the premier locations in the country for speed to market, proximity to a port, and access to a highly skilled workforce.
- Governor Kemp envisions a company investing billions of dollars at the site, drawing a comparison to SK Battery's investment in northeast Georgia.
- The initial funding required to prepare the site is considered minimal compared to the long-term economic impact it will generate for the region over generations.
- The sale of the property is expected to close within the coming weeks.
