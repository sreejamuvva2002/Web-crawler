# F&P Georgia

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2020-10-26

**Source:** [Japanese Auto Supplier to Make Sixth Expansion in Rome - Global Atlanta](https://www.globalatlanta.com/japanese-auto-supplier-to-make-sixth-expansion-in-rome/)  ·  `page_010831`

## Facts

- Japanese supplier of auto parts to Honda and Nissan in the Southeast U.S. is set to embark on its sixth expansion over two decades in Rome, Ga.
- The latest expansion from F&P Georgia will create 15 jobs on $22.8 million in investment.
- Much of the $22.8 million investment will go toward a massive new stamping press and robotic equipment.
- F&P Georgia is one of five locations under the North American arm of F-Tech Inc.
- The company opened its Rome plant in 2001 with an initial investment employing 70 people making sub frames, suspension parts and pedal assemblies.
- F&P now employs more than 400 people thanks to a gradual expansion.
- The company previously invested another $31 million in 2012 during a prior expansion.
- Rome Floyd Chamber of Commerce President Jeanne Krueger noted that most new local jobs are created when companies grow their existing footprints rather than setting up new plants.
- The chamber has placed a particular focus on the Greater Rome Existing Industries Association (GREIA), a council of companies that meets monthly to trade insights and best practices.
- GREIA addresses workforce and infrastructure challenges, especially amid the COVID-19 pandemic.
- Rome sees itself as having core advantages for manufacturing, including being home to four colleges and sitting close to interstates and a new inland port that has helped lure manufacturing investment.
- F&P prepares to make front and rear subframes for a new original equipment manufacturer, perhaps the joint Toyota-Mazda plant in Huntsville, Ala., that is set to begin production next year.
- F&P Senior Manager Nat Massey credited Rome and Floyd County as a great home for F&P and thanked the support of the people, government and Development Authority of Rome and Floyd County.
- Al Hodge, the Rome-Floyd Chamber CEO at the time of the investment, was approached via phone by the Georgia Department of Economic Development, which had given the project the code name 'Trojan Horse'.
- Hodge was told there was 'something good in the belly of that horse, because this one has the potential to for expansion at least once, if not twice'.
- Al Hodge now serves as chair of the Japan-America Society of Georgia while continuing his economic development consultancy.
- Hodge maintained ties with Yuichi Fukuda, who led the expansion into Rome and eventually became president and CEO of F-Tech, the family-owned parent company.
- F-Tech Inc. is based in Japan's Saitama prefecture and was founded in the 1950s.
- Hodge met Fukuda last May in Japan during a Japan Foundation-backed exchange trip.
- Rome and Floyd County are also home to all-terrain vehicle manufacturer Suzuki Manufacturing of America Corp., which has expanded at least once since its initial investment.
- Rome and Floyd County are also home to Neaton Auto Products Manufacturing Inc., a subsidiary of Nihon Plast Co. Ltd. that makes interior trim products for the automotive sector and has expanded at least once since its initial investment.
