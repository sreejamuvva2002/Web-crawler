# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Paid Program: The Ripple Effect of Investing in American Jobs](https://partners.wsj.com/hyundai/miles-that-define-us/the-ripple-effect-of-investing-in-american-jobs/)  ·  `page_019862`

## Facts

- Hyundai is spearheading its new $7.59 billion “Metaplant” in Bryan County, Georgia, described as the largest economic development initiative in Georgia history.
- The Metaplant site is located about an hour west of Savannah on Interstate 16 and spans nearly 3,000 acres.
- The facility is scheduled to come online in early 2025.
- Upon completion, the Metaplant will produce up to 300,000 electric vehicles each year.
- Upon completion, the Metaplant will also produce the batteries that power the vehicles.
- Chris Smith, chief project implementation officer for Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America, describes the project as a huge undertaking involving vehicle manufacturing, battery facilities, affiliate suppliers, construction teams, utilities, and government entities.
- The project is anticipated to create an estimated 8,500 new jobs for the region.
- During the site vetting process, Hyundai conducted an in-depth analysis of workforce demographics, education levels, and local population trends to confirm Bryan County and the Savannah area could support the facility and its jobs.
- Eight additional suppliers plan to build their own sites in Georgia.
- These supplier sites account for an additional $2 billion investment and another estimated 5,000 jobs.
- South Korea-based supplier Ecoplastic has construction underway on a facility in nearby Statesboro.
- The Ecoplastic facility will manufacture bumpers, consoles, and trim for EVs produced at the Metaplant.
- The Ecoplastic facility will create around 450 jobs when completed next year.
- The Ecoplastic facility could staff hundreds more as it increases capacity over time.
- Charlie Kim, president and CEO of Ecoplastic America, states this is the company's first U.S. facility opening and aims to drive job creation and economic impact in Georgia alongside Hyundai.
- Hyundai recently announced a joint $5 billion venture with SK On to establish an EV battery cell manufacturing facility in northern Georgia’s Bartow County.
- The Hyundai-SK On joint venture plant will produce batteries for the EVs being built at the Metaplant and add an additional 3,500 jobs to the area.
- According to the Georgia Department of Economic Development, the Hyundai Metaplant and supplier sites will have an estimated $4 billion annual economic impact.
- Over the next 27 years, Hyundai is estimated to pay over $523 million in property tax payments.
- A local health care provider will open a 7,700-square-foot on-site medical center for Hyundai employees next year as part of the community benefits.
- Trip Tollison, president and CEO of the Savannah Economic Development Authority, expects the project to elevate nearby counties and communities, driving growth in housing development, schools, restaurant openings, and commercial activity built on quality jobs.
- José Muñoz, president and global COO of Hyundai Motor Company and president and CEO of Hyundai and Genesis Motor North America, notes that Hyundai has supported U.S. economic and environmental goals for nearly four decades through manufacturing investments and is leading the EV transition in the U.S.
