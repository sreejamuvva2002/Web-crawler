# Goodyear Tire & Rubber Co.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2018-03-12

**Source:** [OSHA: Georgia Goodyear plant lapses put employees at serious risk - Reveal](https://revealnews.org/blog/osha-georgia-goodyear-plant-lapses-put-employees-at-serious-risk/)  ·  `page_055057`

## Facts

- Goodyear Tire & Rubber Co. operates a manufacturing plant in Social Circle, Georgia.
- The Georgia plant faces about $70,000 in fines for failing to follow federal workplace safety laws that endangered workers.
- Federal OSHA investigators imposed seven serious citations against the company in January after inspecting its Social Circle plant last year.
- Federal officials found the company failed to provide proper protective gear to workers processing rubber through hot metal presses with temperatures exceeding 350 degrees Fahrenheit.
- Investigators discovered hazards from unguarded machines, rainwater and leaking equipment that put workers at risk of slipping on the production floor.
- William Fulcher, the OSHA area office director in Atlanta, stated that the inspection found multiple safety deficiencies putting employees at risk of serious injury or death.
- Goodyear has contested the proposed fines.
- Laura Duda, a Goodyear spokeswoman, stated the company cooperated with OSHA during its inspection and is following the standard administrative process for responding to citations.
- A Reveal investigation from The Center Investigative Reporting in December showed how Goodyear’s lax approach to safety contributed to the deaths of motorists on the road and workers in its plants.
- Goodyear ranked among the top five manufacturers in the United States for worker deaths since 2009, according to Reveal’s analysis.
- At least four motorists over the last seven years have died in accidents after tires made at Goodyear plants failed.
- Tires were manufactured in plants where intense production demands and leaks in the roof endangered both workers and consumers.
- Court documents and former workers in Goodyear’s plants in Danville, Virginia, and Fayetteville, North Carolina, detailed how water leaked through the roof and onto machines.
- Workers are trained to avoid moisture in tire production because it can prompt treads to separate, causing a blowout.
- Former employees of Goodyear’s Danville and Fayetteville plants said they felt pressure to put production before workplace safety.
- Some former employees recalled a quota-driven motto invoked on the shop floor: “Round and black and out the back.”
- Hours after Reveal’s investigation was published, a Goodyear spokeswoman issued a statement regarding the findings.
- Barbara Hatala, Goodyear’s communications manager for Americas operations, stated that over the past two years the company fell short of its own expectations for safety and mourned the loss of valued coworkers at two U.S. manufacturing plants.
- The story detailed how protections for factory workers are being dismantled under the Trump administration, which rolled back and postponed Obama-era protections in keeping with goals laid out by the National Association of Manufacturers.
- Richard Kramer, Goodyear’s chairman, chief executive officer and president, serves on the National Association of Manufacturers’ board.
- Since October 2008, Goodyear has been fined more than $1.9 million for more than 200 health and workplace safety violations, far more than its four major competitors combined.
- Besides the most recent proposed OSHA fines of $69,058, Goodyear’s plant in Social Circle, Georgia, has been previously cited for three workplace safety violations, two of them serious.
- Jordan Barab, former deputy assistant secretary of OSHA under President Barack Obama, called the most recent fines “deeply discouraging.”
- Barab stated that even with multiple preventable deaths and serious injuries in plants across the country and multiple OSHA citations, Goodyear never seems to learn that they are legally and morally required to provide safe workplaces for their employees.
