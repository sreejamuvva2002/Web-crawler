# Seohan Auto Georgia Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-04-11

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp: Seohan Auto Georgia Announces 180 New Jobs in Liberty County | Governor Brian P. Kemp Office of the Governor](https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases/2023-04-11/gov-kemp-seohan-auto-georgia-announces-180-new-jobs-liberty-county)  ·  `page_000078`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced on April 11, 2023 that Seohan Auto Georgia will create at least 180 new jobs.
- Seohan Auto Georgia will invest more than $72 million in a new manufacturing facility in Liberty County.
- The new manufacturing facility will be located at the Dunwoody Court Site in the Tradeport East Business Center, a Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) Certified site.
- Seohan Auto Georgia Corporation is a Tier-1 automotive parts supplier for Hyundai Motor Group America.
- The company is based in South Korea and operates production facilities around the world, including multiple facilities in the United States.
- CEO Jung Kee Koo stated that sustained EV market growth suggests accelerated changes to the automotive market are inevitable.
- Jung Kee Koo believes Georgia will be the center of the EV industry and a new frontier for Seohan's future with limitless opportunities.
- The new facility is scheduled to begin production in late 2024.
- The facility will produce H/shafts, axles, and brake systems for OEMs, including Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- Liberty County Development Authority Chairman Allen Brown described Seohan and its affiliated enterprises as responsible, thorough, and progressive organizations striving to advance innovative technology.
- GDEcD Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy Alyce Thornhill represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s Global Commerce team on the project.
- The project was developed in partnership with the Liberty County Industrial Development Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia Power.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson noted that the transition to e-mobility creates a window of opportunity for companies and states remaining on the forefront.
- Pat Wilson stated that communities investing in speed-to-market solutions, like Liberty County with the Tradeport East Business Center, give Georgia a competitive advantage.
- On October 25, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America officially broke ground in neighboring Bryan County on its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility.
- Offsite suppliers connected to Hyundai's project were predicted to invest approximately $1 billion in other locations around the state.
- Projects announced to date have already exceeded the $1 billion supplier investment prediction by nearly $1 billion.
- Georgia is positioned as a hub for the electric mobility industry, building on assets that make the automotive industry successful.
- Since 2020, more than 35 EV-related projects have announced more than $21 billion in investment and 27,800 jobs in Georgia.
- Seohan Auto Georgia Corporation is a new subsidiary of Korea Movenex Co., Ltd, which was established in 1974.
- Korea Movenex employs more than 2,800 employees globally and possesses advanced technology and experience in the automotive parts industry.
- Korea Movenex strives to create new value by developing advanced technology and supplying parts to collaborative partners rather than simply transferring value through distribution or sales operations.
