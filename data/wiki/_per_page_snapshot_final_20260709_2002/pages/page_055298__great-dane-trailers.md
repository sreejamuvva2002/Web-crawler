# Great Dane Trailers

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Great Dane closes Savannah plant, takes a look back at rich history of trailer production | Trailer Body Builders](https://www.trailer-bodybuilders.com/trailers/article/21741053/great-dane-closes-savannah-plant-takes-a-look-back-at-rich-history-of-trailer-production)  ·  `page_055298`

## Facts

- Great Dane closed its manufacturing plant in Savannah, Georgia.
- The last trailer rolled off the Savannah facility's assembly line three days before January 12.
- The final trailer produced was a reefer headed for Sysco.
- Great Dane was founded in 1900 in Savannah and built its first trailer there in 1930.
- Great Dane announced the Savannah facility closure in November, citing the extended economic downturn on the transportation industry and the need for more efficient production.
- Corporate offices will be retained at the Savannah location, housing the research and development lab, sales and marketing, accounting, customer service, and engineering.
- On January 8, Great Dane gathered its 270 employees who would lose their jobs, fed them breakfast, and took group pictures.
- Chris Hammond IV is 41 years old and serves as vice president of dealer sales.
- Kit Hammond is 69 years old and is a retired Great Dane president.
- Chris Hammond II is 96 years old and is a retired chairman of the board.
- Chris Hammond II was present when the first refrigerated trailer rolled off the assembly line in the 1940s.
- Bernie Griner is a quality-control supervisor who worked at the plant for 35 years; his father worked there for 45 years.
- Connie Barker is a plant superintendent and a 42-year veteran of the company.
- Walter Ross is a specialist at the plant.
- Dewey Cribbs was a former plant manager who started working at age 15, remained for 53 years, requested his tombstone feature a Great Dane outline, and was buried on May 6, 2002, wearing his 50-year service pin.
- The Savannah plant historically manufactured virtually every conceivable trailer type except dump trailers, including truck bodies, marine and domestic containers, refrigerated trailers, van trailers, flat trailers, lowboy trailers, gas/petroleum tank trailers, asphalt, grain, bulk fruit, and open top trailers.
- In 1900, The Savannah Blowpipe Company was established in Savannah with a total capitalization of $3000.
- By 1912, the company began fabricating light structural steel and steel plate products.
- In 1919, the company moved to a new location on Lathrop Avenue with 10,000 square feet and a 10-ton traveling crane.
- In 1931, The Savannah Blowpipe Company was incorporated as The Steel Products Company, with total assets of $100,000.
- In 1938, The Steel Products Company responded to market pressures with lightweight tank trailers and van trailers made of high-tensile steel.
- In the early 1940s, The Steel Production Company pioneered the refrigerated trailer field by building the first produce van with a wet ice bunker and a gas engine and blower system.
- In 1947, they made the first mechanically refrigerated trailers.
- By 1949, the company produced a van trailer made of aluminum with a tandem axle.
- In 1953, refrigerated aluminum trailers became the standard for the eastern United States due to demand in the Florida perishables market.
- Sales outlets were established in 31 cities and 18 states throughout the eastern half of the United States by 1953.
- In 1958, The Steel Production Company changed its name to Great Dane Trailers, Inc.
- In 1959, Great Dane brought out a redesigned line of van trailers and a new tank trailer line, both largely of lightweight aluminum.
- Great Dane entered the piggyback and container market, completing its first piggyback trailer order in 1961 and its first maritime container order in 1963.
- Sales outlets were established in 24 states blanketing the eastern part of the country by 1963.
- In 1966, Great Dane announced a $2 million expansion program, which increased trailer production by 100%.
- In 1972, Great Dane acquired Arrow Trailers in Memphis, Tennessee.
- Since 1972, production at the Memphis plant has increased by almost 200%.
- In 1974, a decision was made to build another manufacturing facility for van trailers in Brazil, Indiana.
- The Brazil facility was expanded by adding a refrigerated trailer production line, completed during the first quarter of 1978.
- In 1988, Great Dane acquired the SuperSeal reefer product line and the Wayne, Nebraska, plant from Timpte.
- In 1996, Great Dane opened a dry van plant in Terre Haute, Indiana, as well as a parts distribution center adjacent to it.
- In 1997, there was a merger of Great Dane and Pines Trailer, creating Great Dane Limited Partnership, the world's largest trailer company.
- In 1999, Great Dane expanded the Little Rock, Arkansas, sales office to a full-service branch.
- In 2000, Great Dane celebrated its 100th anniversary and completed its plant's 250,000th trailer in Savannah.
- Between 2001 and 2002, Great Dane made two major acquisitions: Strick's Eastern Van Manufacturing facilities in Danville, Pennsylvania, and Abbeville, South Carolina, and two of Trailmobile's manufacturing facilities in Jonesboro, Arkansas, and Charleston, Illinois.
- In 2001, Great Dane introduced the "Next Generation from Great Dane" campaign, including redesigns of all refrigerated and platform trailers, plus two new freight van trailers.
- In 2002, the SSL dry freight van was added to the product line, featuring steel-lined interior walls.
- In July 2003, a new branch opened in Charlotte, North Carolina.
- Between 2004 and 2005, Great Dane announced five new products: three new trailers and two advancements in liner technology.
- The FREEDOM line of flatbeds combined aluminum and steel.
- The Super LT refrigerated trailer was designed for the eastern US and Canadian truckload market.
- The i-Van was introduced as a semi-insulated freight van for temperature-sensitive loads like candy and chemicals, produced in Brazil, Indiana.
- In early 2005, the company announced a $5 million re-tooling of the Brazil plant for special foam presses and modular assembly needed for the i-Van.
- Great Dane introduced PunctureGuard liner for reefers and vans, a thermoplastic innovation.
- The company introduced ThermoGuard, a glass-reinforced thermoplastic with a composite layer to seal reefer insulation.
- In fall 2004, Great Dane Limited Partnership announced the acquisition of a production facility in Huntsville, Tennessee, previously owned by Wabash National Corporation.
- By the third quarter of 2005, two full production lines and two finishing lines were added to the Huntsville plant.
- Kit Hammond officially retired from day-to-day responsibilities at the end of 2005 after more than 40 years.
