# Ascend Elements

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Ascend Elements opens Li-Ion battery Recycling facility in Georgia | Energy Tech](https://www.energytech.com/energy-storage/article/21263166/ascend-elements-opens-li-ion-battery-recycling-facility-in-georgia)  ·  `page_000698`

## Facts

- Ascend Elements has opened its first commercial-scale lithium-ion battery recycling facility in Covington, Georgia.
- The Massachusetts-based company claims its Base 1 facility cost $50 million to build.
- Ascend Elements claims the Base 1 facility is the largest electric vehicle (EV) battery recycling facility in North America.
- Partial operations at the facility began in August 2022.
- The facility now has an annual capacity to process 30,000 metric tons of used lithium-ion batteries and manufacturing scrap.
- This processing capacity is equivalent to 70,000 EV batteries per year.
- The facility is powered by renewable energy.
- The facility features onsite wastewater recycling and treatment systems.
- Ascend Elements claims it can recover up to 98 percent of critical battery metals in used EV batteries and gigafactory manufacturing scrap using its patented Hydro-to-Cathode process.
- The company states that recycling used lithium-ion batteries and scrap can decrease the carbon footprint of new EV battery cathode materials by up to 90 percent.
- Mike O’Kronley, CEO of Ascend Elements, stated that the facility is an important part of America’s EV battery infrastructure and 'this is just the start.'
- O’Kronley emphasized the industry's need to continue building a closed-loop battery materials supply chain to make electric vehicle batteries cleaner and more sustainable.
- The company currently employs about 100 people at the facility.
- Ascend Elements plans to increase the facility's workforce to 185 employees by 2024.
