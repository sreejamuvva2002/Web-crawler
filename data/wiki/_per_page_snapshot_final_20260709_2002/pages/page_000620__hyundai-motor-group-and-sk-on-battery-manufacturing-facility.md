# Hyundai Motor Group and SK On Battery Manufacturing Facility

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-12-08

**Source:** [Hyundai, SK building new battery plant to Bartow County, bringing 3,500 new jobs | FOX 5 Atlanta](https://www.fox5atlanta.com/news/hyundai-sk-battery-plant-bartow-county-3500-new-jobs)  ·  `page_000620`

## Facts

- A new Hyundai electric vehicle battery manufacturing facility will bring billions of dollars of investment and thousands of new jobs to Georgia.
- Governor Brian Kemp announced that Hyundai Motor Group and SK On selected a location in Bartow County, Georgia, to supply automotive manufacturing plants across the country.
- Officials state the new facility will be one of the largest economic development projects in Georgia history, with an investment of approximately $4 to $5 billion into Bartow County.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Hyundai Motor Group and SK On are valued partners and key players in Georgia's growing automotive industry.
- Governor Brian Kemp noted his administration's focus on bringing jobs and opportunity to previously overlooked communities across Georgia.
- Governor Brian Kemp expressed pride that SK On and Hyundai Motor Group are investing further in Georgia, which he called the No. 1 state for business.
- Officials estimate the facility will create more than 3,500 new jobs.
- The facility will be located at Bartow Centre, an industrial site on Highway 411.
- The facility is expected to begin operations in 2025.
- Bartow County Sole Commissioner Steve Taylor expressed the county's pleasure with Hyundai Motor Group and SK On's decision.
- Commissioner Steve Taylor thanked the owners of the former Bartow Centre Industrial property.
- Commissioner Steve Taylor stated that local officials and the companies pursued the project together as an extraordinary economic development initiative.
- Commissioner Steve Taylor noted the project will provide regional benefits for local job seekers and significantly establish Bartow County in Georgia's EV ecosystem.
- SK On, the lithium-ion battery division of SK Innovations, currently employs over 2,000 workers at its existing SK Battery America facility in Commerce, Georgia.
- The construction of the existing Commerce plant has faced controversy due to FOX 5 I-Team investigations alleging worker safety issues at its construction site.
- Earlier this year, Hyundai Motor Group announced plans to invest over $5.5 billion in a separate electric vehicle manufacturing facility in Bryan County, which prompted multiple parts suppliers to announce new facilities to support it.
