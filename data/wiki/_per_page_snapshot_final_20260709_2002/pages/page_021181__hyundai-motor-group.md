# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-03

**Source:** [Hyundai to Invest $21B in US, Build Steel Plant to Supply Georgia Auto Plants - Rome CEO](https://romeceo.com/news/2025/03/hyundai-invest-21-billion-us-build-steel-plant-supply-georgia-auto-plants/)  ·  `page_021181`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group is announcing a significant investment of USD 21 billion in the United States from 2025 to 2028.
- The commitment reflects the Group’s strategic focus on expanding its manufacturing capabilities, advancing future technologies, and enhancing energy infrastructure in America.
- This latest U.S. investment builds on the Group’s existing allocation of approximately USD 20.5 billion since entering the U.S. market in 1986.
- Hyundai Motor Group stated it is deepening its partnership with the United States, reinforcing a shared vision for American industrial leadership, and expanding operations and the American workforce.
- To reinforce production capabilities, the Group will invest a total of USD 9 billion to establish an annual U.S. production capacity of 1.2 million vehicles across Hyundai Motor, Kia, and Genesis brands.
- The Group plans to invest in improving its production facilities, including Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Alabama and Kia Autoland Georgia.
- A total of USD 6 billion will be allocated to increase the localization rate of automotive components, including core EV parts like battery packs, to form an auto cluster following facility expansions.
- The same USD 6 billion allocation covers strengthening Group logistics for robust supply chains and investing in U.S. steel production.
- Hyundai Steel, the Group’s steel affiliate, will construct an Electric Arc Furnace (EAF) steel mill in Louisiana capable of producing 2.7 million tons of steel annually.
- The Louisiana steel facility will produce low-carbon steel sheets using abundant U.S. steel scrap to enhance agility and flexibility against external uncertainties.
- The Group will invest USD 6 billion to drive innovation and expand strategic partnerships with U.S. companies in autonomous driving, robotics, artificial intelligence (AI), and advanced air mobility (AAM).
- Key initiatives include collaborating with Boston Dynamics to expand the U.S. ecosystem for robotics components and establish a mass-production system.
- The Group is partnering with NVIDIA to accelerate AI solutions development for future mobility, including autonomous driving and robotics.
- R&D will be advanced with Supernal, the Group’s U.S. affiliate for AAM business, to commercialize an eVTOL vehicle by 2028.
- Hyundai Motor Company will supply robotaxis to Waymo as part of a strategic partnership and co-develop autonomous driving services with Aptiv.
- The Group plans to invest in potential startups through venture capital and other funding mechanisms to support U.S. startups specializing in mobility, robotics, and AI.
- As part of the USD 6 billion commitment, the Group will invest in energy infrastructure projects to secure new business opportunities and contribute to sustainable energy generation development.
- Energy initiatives include strategic cooperation between Hyundai Engineering & Construction and Holtec International on Small Modular Reactor (SMR) technology.
- The Group plans to establish infrastructure to bolster the use of renewable energy.
- The Group is investing in the IONNA EV charging alliance to expand infrastructure.
- Through these investments, the Group anticipates creating 14,000 new direct full-time jobs in the U.S. by 2028.
- The overall economic impact is expected to generate more than 100,000 direct and indirect job opportunities across related industries.
- The Group plans to hold its Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America Grand Opening celebration in Georgia later this week, marking the completion of the largest economic development project in Georgia’s history, just two and a half years after breaking ground.
- HMGMA exemplifies HMG’s dedication to driving economic growth, furthering technological advancements, and delivering sustainable solutions to the U.S. market, creating or supporting over 570,000 jobs nationwide.
