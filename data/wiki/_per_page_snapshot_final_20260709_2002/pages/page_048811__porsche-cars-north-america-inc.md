# Porsche Cars North America, Inc.

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2012-11-28

**Source:** [Porsche Cars North America Celebrates A Landmark Groundbreaking | Automotive World](https://www.automotiveworld.com/news-releases/porsche-cars-north-america-celebrates-a-landmark-groundbreaking/)  ·  `page_048811`

## Facts

- Porsche Cars North America, Inc. (importer and distributor of Porsche sports cars, Cayenne SUVs, and the Panamera four-door sports sedan) celebrated a landmark groundbreaking for its new headquarters, to be known as One Porsche Drive.
- The new headquarters will be located on a 26.4-acre site adjacent to Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport.
- The site is the first complex to be built in the “Aerotroplis Atlanta” business district.
- Employees from Porsche Cars North America and its affiliates (Porsche Financial Services, Porsche Business Services, and Porsche Consulting) attended the groundbreaking event alongside executives from Porsche Group companies in North America and Germany.
- Most of Porsche’s North American operations will be consolidated under one roof at the new facility upon its completion in the second half of 2014.
- Porsche’s presence has been embraced by Atlanta and the state of Georgia for the last 14 years, according to Matthias Mueller, President and CEO of Porsche AG.
- The new facility will contribute to Georgia’s economic development and add glamour to the local community, according to Matthias Mueller.
- The relationship between Americans and Porsche dates back to the early 1950s, serving as a powerful incentive for the company to safeguard it.
- The investment in Atlanta testifies to Porsche’s belief in the future of the U.S. and confidence in this important market, according to Matthias Mueller.
- Attendees celebrating the groundbreaking included Dr. Wolfgang Porsche (Chairman of the Supervisory Board of Porsche AG), Georgia Governor Nathan Deal, Atlanta Mayor Kasim Reed, Bernhard Maier (Member of the Executive Board Sales and Marketing of Porsche AG), Detlev von Platen (President and CEO of Porsche Cars North America), and Matthias Mueller.
- One Porsche Drive will ultimately house 400 employees, including 100 jobs that will be new to Georgia.
- In addition to corporate offices, the facility will comprise a Technical Service and Training Center, a Conference Center, and a Museum.
- The headquarters campus will feature an industry-first Customer Experience Center with a 1.6-mile test track and handling road course to showcase Porsche vehicles.
- The United States remains Porsche's single largest market, underscoring the company's commitment to U.S. customers and dealers, according to Detlev von Platen.
- The ceremony symbolizes Porsche putting down permanent roots in Atlanta, which has been its home since 1998, according to Detlev von Platen.
- Porsche is the first tenant of the Aerotropolis development site, according to Atlanta Mayor Kasim Reed.
- Porsche's presence underscores Atlanta's continuing commitment to attract world-class companies and iconic brands, according to Mayor Kasim Reed.
- The development sends a clear signal to other companies that Atlanta is open for business, according to Mayor Kasim Reed.
- Neighboring facilities include the Delta Air Lines world headquarters, Wachovia Bank’s principal processing center, and Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport (the world's busiest airport, serving over 92 million travelers in 2011).
- The new headquarters will be a terrific asset to Georgia’s economy and a unique travel destination for arriving travelers, according to Georgia Governor Nathan Deal.
- Governor Nathan Deal expressed confidence that One Porsche Drive will become a renowned address standing for vision, innovation, and success, as well as one of the greatest driving experiences in the world.
