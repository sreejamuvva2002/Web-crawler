# Qcells

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2026-03-23

**Source:** [Qcells launches New Homes solar platform in U.S.](https://www.hanwha.com/newsroom/news/press-releases/qcells-launches-qcells-new-homes-the-residential-building-industrys-only-direct-from-manufacturer-solar-and-storage-partner.do)  ·  `page_001570`

## Facts

- Qcells announced the official launch of Qcells New Homes, a new division designed to work with homebuilders to integrate onsite, end-to-end clean energy solutions for new residential construction across the U.S.
- The new division aims to create manufacturing jobs while addressing homebuyer affordability challenges.
- The Qcells New Homes platform provides a turnkey, vertically integrated package including solar panels manufactured at Qcells’ Georgia factory, domestically produced battery systems, financing, installation support, and long-term system monitoring.
- Qcells New Homes is positioned as the only direct-from-manufacturer solar and storage partner available to homebuilders.
- The division is backed by the A-rated financial strength of parent company Hanwha Group to provide bankability and long-term stability for homebuilders.
- David Shin serves as President of Qcells North America and stated that the launch raises the bar for convenience, compliance, and quality by offering a reliable, single-source partner for clean energy in new communities.
- Qcells is helping builders integrate advanced energy solutions seamlessly into new construction to improve long-term value for homeowners as energy demands evolve.
- The platform addresses household energy affordability, operating costs, and grid reliability through a homebuyer-friendly operating lease with in-house financing.
- Integrated battery storage allows power produced by solar to be stored and sold to the grid to enhance local community reliability in states supporting onsite clean energy programs like NEM 3.0 in California.
- Qcells New Homes offers battery configurations that can provide reserved backup during outages while supporting broader grid stability needs.
- Phil Narodick is President of Qcells New Homes and noted that the company is cutting out intermediaries to deliver solar panels assembled in America directly to new communities, leveraging decades of homebuilding industry experience.
- Qcells is headquartered in Seoul, South Korea, with international manufacturing facilities in the U.S., Malaysia, and South Korea.
- The company provides renewable electricity retail services and packages to end customers globally across utility, commercial, governmental, and residential markets.
- The company operates a global business network spanning Europe, North America, Asia, South America, Africa, and the Middle East.
