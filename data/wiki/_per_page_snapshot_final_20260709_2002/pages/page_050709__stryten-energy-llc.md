# Stryten Energy LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-08-02

**Source:** [Stryten Energy and Snapping Shoals EMC Celebrate Installation of Georgia’s First Vanadium Redox Flow Battery System](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/stryten-energy-snapping-shoals-emc-130000596.html)  ·  `page_050709`

## Facts

- Stryten Energy LLC is a U.S.-based energy storage solutions provider headquartered in Alpharetta, Georgia.
- On August 2, 2023, Stryten Energy commemorated the installation of its advanced vanadium redox flow battery (VRFB) at Snapping Shoals EMC.
- The project represents the first VRFB energy storage system manufactured and installed in the state of Georgia.
- Stryten Energy's partnership with Snapping Shoals EMC focuses on demonstrating the VRFB's energy storage capabilities and evaluating additional use cases for long-duration energy storage technology.
- The VRFB system can store and provide clean energy to Snapping Shoals customers for up to 20 years without losing capacity.
- Shaun Mock, President and Chief Executive Officer of Snapping Shoals EMC, stated that VRFB will be a game-changer in how the utility reliably and affordably stores clean energy for its 100,000+ residential and business customers.
- Mike Judd, Chief Executive Officer and President of Stryten Energy, noted that long-duration energy storage technologies are needed to support the widescale power generation from renewable energy sources.
- Judd added that VRFB is ideally suited for utility use cases such as energy cost control, peak shaving, and avoiding curtailment.
- Stryten Energy's VRFB offers industry-leading power density with a versatile, modular platform that is independently scalable in power and capacity.
- An unlimited cycle life with proper maintenance enables the VRFB system to operate for more than 20 years without the electrolyte losing energy storage capacity.
- The technology is uniquely suited for applications that require medium- to long-duration energy storage of six or more hours to help ensure grid stability and facilitate increased utilization of renewables across the U.S.
- According to the Department of Energy, Georgia has more than 3.6 gigawatts (GW) of solar, wind, and storage capacity.
- There is almost 1.1 GW of additional planned clean energy capacity in Georgia that will power more than 155,000 homes.
- Snapping Shoals EMC provides electric service to approximately 100,000 homes and businesses across an eight-county area including large portions of Newton, Henry, Rockdale, DeKalb, Butts, Walton, Jasper, and Morgan counties.
- Stryten Energy provides energy storage solutions across the Essential Power, Motive Power, Transportation, Military, and Government sectors.
- The company's stored energy technologies power applications ranging from submarines and subcompacts to microgrids, warehouses, distribution centers, cars, trains, and trucks.
- The company's stored energy technologies include advanced lead, lithium, and vanadium redox flow batteries, intelligent chargers, and energy performance management software.
