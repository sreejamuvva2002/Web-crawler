# Panasonic Automotive Systems (later Mobitera)

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-04

**Source:** [PE Case Study: Panasonic AutomotiveApollo Global Management](https://www.apollo.com/insights-news/insights/2026/04/partnership-in-motion-panasonic-automotive)  ·  `page_047837`

## Facts

- Apollo funds acquired an 80% stake in Panasonic Automotive Systems in 2024, with Panasonic Holdings retaining 20%.
- The deal was the largest sponsor-led corporate carve-out in Japan by revenue, spanning 20 subsidiaries across more than 20 countries, with roughly $8 billion in revenue.
- As a standalone company, Panasonic Automotive has set a clear goal to become the global number one in integrated cockpit systems and advanced in-vehicle solutions.
- CEO Masashi Nagayasu noted that the partnership with Apollo has helped change how the company operates by enabling faster decisions, sharper priorities, and greater accountability.
- Following its separation, the company has been streamlining operations, investing in technology, and improving EBITDA minus capex as a key performance indicator.
- Panasonic Automotive is targeting new business to become an integral supplier to all original equipment manufacturers in Japan.
- Early signs of success include improving margins and building new order momentum across a wider group of Japanese manufacturers.
- The company will soon operate under the new name Mobitera, underscoring its transition from a division to a standalone entity with its own identity, strategy, and ambitions.
- Tetsuji Okamoto, Partner at Apollo, stated that focused ownership can unlock the next phase of growth for world-class Japanese businesses and that Apollo's role is to partner with management to translate strategic ambition into operational execution.
