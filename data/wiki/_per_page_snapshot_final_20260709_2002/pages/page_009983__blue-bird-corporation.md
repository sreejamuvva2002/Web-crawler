## Facts

- IMMI and Blue Bird Corporation, which is based in Georgia, opened a new manufacturing plant in Macon.
- The new facility will create 80 new jobs.
- The facility enhances safety features in school buses.
- John Wyskiel, President and CEO of Blue Bird Corporation, stated that Blue Bird has led the way in school bus safety for nearly 100 years.
- The new facility spans 75,000 square feet.
- The facility will produce school bus seats equipped with three-point seat belts.
- Blue Bird is pioneering three-point seat belts as a standard feature on its buses.
- Additional safety features include a steering wheel airbag for driver protection.
- Larry Gray, CEO of IMMI, stated that the project adds 80 good-paying U.S. manufacturing jobs to middle Georgia.
- The introduction of lap-shoulder belts as standard equipment aligns with recommendations from the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration and the National Transportation Safety Board.
- The Macon facility operates under the brand SafeGuard.
- The facility will manufacture state-of-the-art seats contributing to the safety of millions of children who travel on school buses daily.
- Demand for IMMI SafeGuard’s lap-shoulder belt seats is expected to rise, potentially leading to more job opportunities in the future.
- Illinois recently passed legislation requiring lap-shoulder belts on new school buses.
- Illinois joins eight other states with similar seat belt mandates for school buses.
- Blue Bird and IMMI expect the trend of state seat belt mandates to continue as more states recognize the importance of seat belts in enhancing school bus safety.
- The opening of the plant boosts local employment and sets a new standard for school bus safety across the United States.
- As more states adopt seat belt requirements, IMMI and Blue Bird’s innovations are likely to play a crucial role in protecting student passengers nationwide.
