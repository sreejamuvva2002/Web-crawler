# Novelis Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2025-12-29

**Source:** [Novelis Gets Tax Exemption for $255M NY Plant Fire Repairs | 2026 Update - News and Statistics - IndexBox](https://www.indexbox.io/blog/novelis-secures-15m-tax-exemption-for-oswego-plant-repairs-after-2025-fires/)  ·  `page_047579`

## Facts

- According to Recycling Today, Novelis has received financial assistance from the Oswego County Industrial Development Agency to help cover the costs of repairing its Oswego, New York, aluminum facility following multiple fires in 2025.
- The IDA approved the application on January 7.
- The Atlanta-based company filed for assistance on December 29, 2025, detailing $255 million in total repair costs.
- These costs include $187 million for building repairs, $61 million for equipment and machinery, $2 million in engineering fees, and $5 million in other expenses.
- Novelis noted in its application that it had already invested $165 million in the project as of the filing date.
- The IDA granted Novelis an exemption from sales and use taxes associated with the renovations, with the financial assistance through exemptions estimated at $15 million.
- The need for repairs stems from two major fires on September 16, 2025, and November 20, 2025, with a smaller incident occurring between those dates.
- The September fire affected the hot mills structural surroundings, primarily the roof, and other plant areas were impacted by the 5 million gallons of water used to fight the fire.
- Novelis initially anticipated a three-month recovery and a December 2025 restart.
- The November fire was contained to the hot mill and hot mill motor room—the same general area as the first fire—and damaged the hot mills finishing mill.
- Novelis stated in its application that the finishing mill parts are highly specialized, all need to be inspected for reconditioning or rebuilding needs, and due to this, a recovery timeline is still to be determined.
- Novelis says the project will help retain 1,122 full-time and four part-time employees at the site.
- The current annual Oswego County payroll and benefits expenditure is approximately $180 million.
- The company stated the project will also support its continued local spending on services and the local supply chain.
- The Oswego facility produces more than a billion pounds of aluminum sheet annually for the automotive, beverage can, and building and construction markets.
- Novelis supplies several automakers, including Ford.
