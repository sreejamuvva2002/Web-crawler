# voestalpine Automotive Components

## Facts

- voestalpine Automotive Components is a manufacturing partner for door rings in lightweight automotive construction, offering optimized crash performance, weight and cost reduction, and simpler body assembly.
- A door ring is a single part that combines several body components such as the A-pillar, B-pillar, roof frame and sill, and is manufactured from a laser-welded blank using hot forming.
- voestalpine Automotive Components is a leader in this high-end technology.
- The company operates the world's largest plant for tailor-welded blanks where door ring blanks can be manufactured from different steels with different sheet thicknesses and strengths.
- Welded-on patches can further strengthen crash performance at relevant points with pinpoint accuracy.
- voestalpine specialists provide support starting with material selection, design and geometry optimization, through to production design and pre-assembling to enable body-in-white integration.
- Door rings are manufactured from customized blanks using hot forming, with voestalpine Automotive Components locations on four continents guaranteeing market and customer proximity.
- Starting with the automotive industry in China and the USA, door rings are becoming increasingly relevant as a safety-enhancing and advantageous solution for vehicle production.
- The company is a specialist in laser-welded blanks and phs forming, leveraging its global presence to serve the market.
- Sonja Kronawitter serves as a Senior Key Account Manager at voestalpine.
- To ensure hot forming efficiency, voestalpine uses the latest laser technologies, has optimized hot forming technology, and equips its global forming companies with environmentally friendly burners and its own energy supply from renewable sources.
- All door ring blanks made of voestalpine steels are available in the greentec steel edition with a reduced CO2 footprint.
- The CO2 footprint of greentec steel products is documented with a declaration verified by an independent third party (LRQA).
- Customers can use this documentation to prove emission reductions to their customers.
