# HL-GA Battery Plant

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-04

**Source:** [Georgia Hyundai plant: 475 workers arrested in ICE raid](https://www.newsnationnow.com/us-news/immigration/border-coverage/475-arrested-ice-raid-georgia-hyundai-south-korea/)  ·  `page_009936`

## Facts

- Federal agents detained roughly 475 people at a Hyundai plant in Ellabell, Georgia.
- Officials called the operation the “largest single site operation” in Homeland Security Investigations history.
- The ICE-led raid targeted the HL-GA battery plant, which is part of a mega site developed by the South Korean auto company.
- HSI special agent in charge Steven Schrank stated the raid was months in the making.
- Federal agents in tactical gear, supported by Georgia State Patrol, FBI, and DEA, entered the facility under a criminal search warrant tied to alleged unlawful employment practices.
- The raid occurred on Thursday night, according to NewsNation local affiliate WSAV.
- Steven Schrank stated that the detainees were “illegally present in the United States, or in violation of their presence in the United States, working unlawfully.”
- A majority of the detained workers were South Korean nationals.
- No one was injured during the operation.
- ATF Atlanta reported on Twitter that approximately 450 unlawful aliens were apprehended at the Hyundai mega site battery plant in Bryan County, Georgia.
- The ATF Atlanta tweet regarding the apprehension numbers was posted on September 4, 2025.
- South Korean Foreign Ministry spokesperson Lee Jaewoong described the number of detained South Koreans as “large.”
- Lee Jaewoong stated that the detainees belonged to a “network of subcontractors.”
- No criminal charges have been announced yet regarding the operation.
- Steven Schrank confirmed that the investigation remains an ongoing matter.
- The company behind the plant, a joint venture between Hyundai and LG, temporarily paused construction following the raid.
- White House Deputy Press Secretary Abigail Jackson stated that the United States looks forward to continuing historic investments and partnerships secured by President Trump.
- Abigail Jackson emphasized that any foreign workers brought in for specific projects must enter the United States legally with proper work authorizations.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp touted the $7.6 billion site as one of Georgia’s largest economic developments during a grand opening for its electric vehicle factory in March.
- Governor Kemp described the project as a “once-in-a-generation opportunity” for the state.
- U.S. Representative Buddy Carter, R-Ga., stated at the grand opening that the hard-working and dedicated local people are the reason for the project's success.
