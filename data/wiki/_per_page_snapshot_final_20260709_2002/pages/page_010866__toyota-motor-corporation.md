## Facts

- Suzuki Motor’s Indian unit is set to deliver its first EV to Toyota Motor by spring 2025.
- The SUV-style EV will be exclusively manufactured at Maruti Suzuki’s Gujarat facility in India.
- This marks the first time Suzuki and Toyota have collaborated on an electric vehicle.
- The new model has been co-developed by Suzuki, Toyota, and Daihatsu.
- The SUV will carry a 60 kWh battery pack, although specific range details have yet to be disclosed.
- The SUV will join Toyota’s lineup as part of its plan to launch 10 battery-powered models by 2026.
- Suzuki has yet to offer EVs globally but has been selling hybrid models in India manufactured by Toyota’s Indian unit.
- Maruti Suzuki’s Gujarat facility is set to add a new production line capable of producing 250,000 EV units annually.
- The partnership aims to supply the vehicle worldwide, including markets in Japan and Europe.
- The collaboration extends Suzuki and Toyota’s long history of working together on traditional vehicles to battery-electric SUVs.
- Toyota has reported more than 108,000 battery-powered EV sales worldwide this year so far, comprising 1.5% of its global sales, including its premium Lexus brand.
- This partnership aligns with Toyota’s strategic push to boost its presence in the EV sector, particularly the fast-growing SUV segment.
