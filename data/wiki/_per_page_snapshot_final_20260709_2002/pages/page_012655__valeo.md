# Valeo

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-02-18

**Source:** [Valeo to invest Rs 2,147 crore in India over the next three years - BusinessToday](https://www.businesstoday.in/auto/story/french-supplier-valeo-to-invest-rs-2147-crore-in-india-over-the-next-three-years-516776-2026-02-18)  ·  `page_012655`

## Facts

- Valeo announced an investment of €200 million (Rs 2,147 crore) in India over the next three years.
- The company aims to more than treble its sales to €700 million by 2028.
- India currently contributes only 1% to Valeo’s global sales.
- Valeo plans to grow its India business by tapping into the growing demand for feature-rich cars and electric vehicles.
- Christophe Périllat, CEO of Valeo, stated that additional content and technology in cars sold in India is the reason for their determination to grow there.
- Périllat noted that India is the fastest-growing automotive market in the world.
- More than 60% of new orders in India are SUVs.
- Valeo plans to triple its sales from €220 million to around €700 million by 2028.
- The company is preparing for the next step of high growth beyond 2028, targeting 1 billion euros of sales.
- Valeo currently supplies electric powertrains to Mahindra & Mahindra’s BE 6 and XEV 9e electric vehicles.
- The auto parts supplier offers e-axles, battery cooling plates, chargers, inverters, sensors, lighting equipment, and ADAS (advanced driver assistance systems).
- In India, Valeo will invest mainly in its electrification and ADAS divisions.
- Nearly 4% of all new car sales in India are electric.
- Périllat reaffirmed the importance of the Indian market, noting that technology content in Indian cars is growing.
- Valeo is localizing the electric motor and power electronics in India.
- The company is localizing battery cooling components in India.
- For ADAS, Valeo is localizing sensors, cameras, and radars among others in India.
- Valeo anticipates that by 2032, the share of EVs and hybrids will be 25% in India.
- Valeo entered the Indian market in 1997.
- The company operates six production sites across Chennai, Pune, Sanand, and Gurugram.
- Valeo maintains global R&D centres in Chennai and Bengaluru.
- Valeo employs over 7,500 people in India.
- Over 50% of Valeo's Indian employees are engineers contributing to global research, development, and manufacturing activities.
- Valeo has commissioned a new plant and dedicated e-axle production lines at its Pune facility.
- The new Pune plant and e-axle lines were commissioned to support rising demand for Mahindra & Mahindra’s EVs.
