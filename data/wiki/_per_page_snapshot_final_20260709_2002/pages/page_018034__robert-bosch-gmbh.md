# Robert Bosch GmbH

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [The 2023 business year: Bosch increases sales and result despite headwind  - Bosch Media Service US](https://us.bosch-press.com/pressportal/us/en/press-release-23104.html)  ·  `page_018034`

## Facts

- In 2023, Bosch announced a $130 million investment in Lincolnton, North Carolina to support manufacturing of power tools accessories.
- In 2023, Bosch announced and closed an acquisition of a wafer fab in Roseville, California.
- Bosch intends to invest $1.5 billion at the site to produce silicon carbide chips by 2026.
- The silicon carbide (SiC) chips produced by Bosch are used in the power electronics of electric cars and can increase driving range.
- Starting in 2026, Bosch plans to manufacture the first vertically structured SiC chips on cost-effective 200-millimeter wafers.
- Bosch closed its acquisition of U.S.-based specialist hydraulic company Hydraforce in 2023, strengthening its global hydraulics business.
- Bosch plans to grow its integrator business in North America, evidenced by the acquisition of Paladin Technologies in 2023.
- Mike Mansuetti, president of Bosch in North America, stated that Bosch continues to register profitable growth in North America and is investing to further grow and diversify its business as an integral part of the global portfolio.
- The integrated Mobility business sector has been in place since the beginning of 2024, with horizontal responsibility for software, semiconductors, and vehicle computers.
- Bosch is aiming for 10 percent annual growth in demand for new redundant braking systems, which support automated and electrified driving, up through 2030.
- Dr. Stefan Hartung, chairman of the board of management of Robert Bosch GmbH, stated that current climate policies are destabilizing the business environment, citing Germany’s sudden abolition of electric-car subsidies as an example of erratic policy structure impeding long-term purchasing decisions.
- Bosch expects production levels in the automotive industry for 2024 to be similar to those of the previous year.
- Bosch plans to spend around 4 billion euros by 2030 on training and reskilling associates to move them from shrinking areas to growing ones.
- As of December 31, 2023, the Bosch Group employed some 427,600 people worldwide.
