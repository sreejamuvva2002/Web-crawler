# Volvo Trucks

## Facts

- Volvo Trucks aims to reclaim lost ground and capture more market share in North America as the global truck market pulls out of the post-Covid hangover.
- By 2025, commercial trucks in the U.S. have an average age of around 10.2 years.
- The average age for regional haul trucks is 6.8 years, while linehaul units are about 6.4 years old.
- Demand forecasts for 2025 suggest around 212,000 units will be delivered in North America, which is below the five-year order average of 285,000 units.
- The market downturn may stem from tariffs, emissions regulations, and a cautious approach from operators regarding fleet growth.
- Volvo Trucks noted that the North American market might struggle until 2026 due to ongoing decreases in freight activity and new tariffs affecting costs.
- Regulatory uncertainties around emissions standards are making fleet owners hesitant to invest in new trucks.
- Volvo’s focus on innovation includes electrification and self-driving technology to gain market share as these trends become prominent.
- Volvo holds a 19.6% market share in Greater Europe.
- The company has made significant investments totaling US$2 billion in the new VNL model and US$400 million in production facilities.
- In North America, the Class 8 truck market is primarily dominated by Daimler Trucks (Freightliner), Paccar (Kenworth and Peterbilt), Navistar (International), and Volvo Trucks.
- In 2024, Volvo Trucks achieved a 10.5% market share in North America, an increase from 10.2% the previous year.
- The new VNL model promotes fuel efficiency and performance.
- In 2024, Volvo reported a 6% increase in truck orders attributed to strong demand in vocational trucks.
- Volvo experienced a 5% decrease in deliveries in 2024 due to transitioning to new models.
- Volvo’s heavy-duty market share is currently 7.2%.
- In global markets, Volvo Trucks achieved a 17.9% market share in the European heavy-duty sector (16 tonnes and above) in 2024.
- Volvo secured a 23.7% market share in Brazil in 2024.
- Economic factors like tariffs and regulatory compliance related to emissions are creating apprehensive purchasing behaviors among operators.
- The electrification trend is vital, with Volvo leading in electric heavy-duty truck deliveries.
- By October 2025, Volvo reported over 5,700 electric truck deliveries across 50 countries.
- In North America, Volvo holds a 30% market share in the electric truck segment.
- Freightliner held a 38.4% market share in North America in 2025.
- Peterbilt held a 14.8% market share in North America in 2025.
- Kenworth held a 14.3% market share in North America in 2025.
- International (Navistar) held an 11.6% market share in North America in 2025.
- Mack held a 7.7% market share in North America in 2025.
- Western Star held a 5.4% market share in North America in 2025.
- DAF Trucks held a 14.4% market share in Greater Europe in 2025.
- Mercedes held an 18% market share in Greater Europe in 2025.
- MAN held a 14% market share in Greater Europe in 2025.
- Iveco held a 13% market share in Greater Europe in 2025.
- Scania held a 12% market share in Greater Europe in 2025.
- Renault held a 9% market share in Greater Europe in 2025.
- In April 2024, Volvo Group announced plans to build a new heavy-duty truck manufacturing plant in Monterrey, Mexico.
- The Monterrey plant is expected to be operational by 2026 and will focus on producing heavy-duty conventional vehicles for Volvo and Mack brands.
- The Monterrey plant will be approximately 1.7 million square feet in size.
- The facility will include comprehensive assembly capabilities, such as cab body-in-white production and painting.
- Volvo Trucks completed a six-year, $400 million investment to upgrade its NRV plant in Virginia.
- The NRV plant upgrade was undertaken to prepare the facility for the production of the new Volvo VNL model.
- Enhancements at the NRV plant include expanding the industrial footprint and installing state-of-the-art equipment aimed at improving plant efficiency and product quality.
- The Monterrey plant will provide additional capacity to support growth plans for Volvo Trucks and Mack Trucks in the U.S. and Canadian markets.
- The expansion is expected to support Mack truck sales in Mexico and Latin America.
- The strategic location of the Monterrey plant offers logistical efficiencies for supporting sales to the southwestern and western regions of the U.S., Mexico, and Latin America.
- Magnus Koeck serves as Vice President of Strategy, Marketing, and Brand Management at Volvo Trucks North America.
- Magnus Koeck stated, “The tide will turn. The question is, when the tide will turn,” indicating cautious optimism for future growth.
- Magnus Koeck noted, “We aspire to grow a lot here in North America during the next business cycle,” underscoring Volvo’s commitment to enhancing market share with innovative products like the new VNL model.
- Regarding sustainability, Koeck stated, “Achieving a 10% improvement in fuel efficiency across the North American trucking industry would have a significant impact on decarbonization efforts, equivalent to removing 30,000 new battery-electric trucks from the road.”
- Koeck pointed out pre-buy effects expected around upcoming emissions regulations, stating, “Signs of this pre-buy activity are already emerging,” as operators adapt ahead of increased truck costs.
- Volvo anticipates deliveries of 265,000 units in 2025, a downward revision indicative of economic uncertainties and fluctuating freight prices.
- The U.S. and Canada are grappling with a freight market recession characterized by reduced volumes and prices.
- Laid-off workers in various Volvo facilities underscore the consequences of declining market sentiment and high tariffs on imported components.
- New emissions standards set to take effect in 2027 will impact the market and lead to price increases as manufacturers adapt product lines.
- Historic trends suggest customers may engage in pre-buying activities to avoid higher prices, resulting in short-term demand fluctuations.
- Volvo’s focus on electric heavy-duty trucks has enabled it to maintain a 30% market share in this segment despite broader market challenges.
- Projected deliveries for 2024 are 250,000 units with an average freight price of $3,500 and a market growth rate of -4.0%.
