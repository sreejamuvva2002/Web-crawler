# Mercedes-Benz USA LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [PolitiFact | Mercedes-Benz on target with superlative about Atlanta stadium rights](https://www.politifact.com/factchecks/2015/aug/27/steve-cannon/mercedes-benz-target-superlative-about-atlanta-sta/)  ·  `page_046347`

## Facts

- German luxury automaker Mercedes-Benz signed a multimillion-dollar deal aimed at further cementing its relationship with Atlanta, home of its new U.S. headquarters.
- Mercedes-Benz bought the naming rights to the still-under construction $1.4 billion stadium that will be home to the NFL’s Atlanta Falcons and MLS’s Atlanta United.
- Steve Cannon, CEO of Mercedes-Benz USA, stated that buying the naming rights to the new Falcons stadium is the largest marketing deal in the company’s history.
- Officials were mum about the cost to Mercedes-Benz for the near-record 27-year naming rights at Monday’s press conference.
- Donna Boland, manager of corporate communications for Mercedes-Benz USA LLC, confirmed that since Cannon has been with the company for 24 years, he can be trusted that this is the biggest marketing initiative they have undertaken.
- As a matter of policy, Mercedes-Benz does not disclose marketing or sponsorship costs for this deal or others.
- Experts contacted by PolitiFact estimated the annual value of the naming rights ranged from $8 million to $20 million.
- Don Muret of Sports Business Journal put the price of the Atlanta naming rights at $12 million a year, essentially double what Mercedes-Benz paid in 2011 for the naming rights to the Superdome for 10 years.
- Andrew Zimbalist, professor of economics at Smith College, valued the deal at $8 million to $10 million a year, or minimally $216 million for 27 years.
- Joel Maxcy, associate professor at Drexel University, projected the highest value at $20 million a year, citing national and regional television exposure and stadium visibility to commuters and travelers.
- Victor Matheson, professor of economics at College of Holy Cross, expected the naming rights went for about $11 million.
- Mercedes-Benz previously had naming rights in New Orleans costing $60 million.
- The company also has its names on arenas in Shanghai, China, and Stuttgart, Germany.
- Sports Business Journal reported Mercedes-Benz signed the Shanghai deal in 2009 for 10 years at $6 million a year, totaling $60 million.
- The automaker spends about $340 million a year in the U.S. on advertising across all media, according to Kantar Media.
- Parent company Daimler estimated U.S. ad spending of $561.9 million in 2014, primarily for its Mercedes-Benz business, while also marketing trucks and buses under brands including Freightliner, Thomas Built Buses, Western Star, and Fuso.
- Bradley Johnson, director of data analytics for Advertising Age, noted that the Atlanta stadium deal will likely account for just a small part of the automaker’s annual marketing budget.
