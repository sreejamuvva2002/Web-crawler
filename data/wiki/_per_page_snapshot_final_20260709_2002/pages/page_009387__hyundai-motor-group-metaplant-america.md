# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-02

**Source:** [State Leaders Celebrate Hyundai Motor Group’s Historic Impact on Georgia - Middle Georgia CEO](https://middlegeorgiaceo.com/features/2024/02/state-leaders-celebrate-hyundai-motor-groups-historic-impact-georgia/)  ·  `page_009387`

## Facts

- Governor Brian Kemp and First Lady Marty Kemp joined President and Global COO of Hyundai Motor Company and President and CEO of Hyundai and Genesis Motor North America José Muñoz to celebrate Hyundai Motor Group’s impact on Georgia and provide an update on Metaplant America on Tuesday, February 27th, 2024.
- The facility will officially start vehicle production in the fourth quarter of 2024.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated Hyundai has been an incredible partner for decades, helping recruit businesses, give back to communities, and work with colleges and universities to connect Georgians with opportunities.
- Hyundai and Genesis electric vehicles on display included the IONIQ 5, IONIQ 6, GV60, Electrified GV70, and the hydrogen-powered XCIENT Fuel Cell Truck, which will be used for logistics between Hyundai’s manufacturing facilities and suppliers.
- Governor Kemp officially declared February 26 as Hyundai Day throughout the state.
- The General Assembly passed a Resolution commending Hyundai Motor Group’s dedication to Georgia and recognizing February 26 as Hyundai Day at the State Capitol.
- José Muñoz stated Hyundai’s investments in Georgia will create tens of thousands of great-paying American jobs and bring tens of billions of dollars in economic impact for decades to come.
- Since the HMGMA project was announced in May 2022, LG Energy Solution was named as the joint venture partner in the manufacture of battery components, with an additional expansion of the battery facility announced.
- Hyundai and SK On are building an EV battery manufacturing facility in Bartow County that will supply Hyundai Motor Group’s U.S. plants.
- Since the initial announcement of HMGMA’s location, Hyundai Motor Group’s planned investment in Georgia has reached more than $12.6 billion, bringing with it the creation of 12,000 direct, long-term jobs.
- Lieutenant Governor Burt Jones and House Speaker Jon Burns, along with legislative leaders from both chambers, Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson, local elected and economic officials from the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority, and numerous company representatives joined the celebration.
- Lt. Governor Burt Jones applauded Governor Kemp, state agencies, and the University System of Georgia (especially Georgia Tech) for their partnership with Hyundai, noting it enabled positive impact and thousands of jobs, supporting Georgia's position as the number one state to do business.
- Up to 300,000 Hyundai, Genesis, and Kia EVs are expected to be produced annually at the Metaplant.
- 17 HMGMA suppliers have announced projects across 10 Georgia counties spanning beyond southeast Georgia, with further announcements expected.
- Supplier facilities are expected to create more than 6,700 new jobs and more than $2.5 billion in investment, and will be operational prior to the opening of HMGMA.
- Speaker of the House Jon Burns stated Hyundai's Metaplant will have a generational impact as a force multiplier for investments in education, healthcare, housing, and transportation, and praised the commitment to start vehicle production in Q4 2024.
- At the nearly 3,000-acre Metaplant site, four building exteriors are complete and outfitting for internal operations is underway.
- The Metaplant’s architecture incorporates technologically advanced elements plus design features representing the greater Savannah area, Hyundai’s brand identity, nature, and clean energy.
- HMGMA’s full-time employees ("Meta Pros") are being hired at a rapid pace for training and skills on machinery, office and clerical work, and numerous other roles.
- HMGMA currently has 566 full-time employees.
- Of that workforce, approximately 75 percent are from the Greater Savannah area.
- An additional 37 employees are from Georgia but outside the local area.
- Georgia Quick Start broke ground on its customized training center at the Metaplant site on February 8.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that with the groundbreaking for the Georgia Quick Start Training Center, Georgia fulfilled a commitment to Hyundai and Georgians, who will be trained in high-skill careers as Meta Pros.
- HMGMA partnered with local technical colleges to prepare students for entry-level employment at the Metaplant.
- Electrical Vehicle Professional Technical Certificate of Credit (EVP TCC) training is available at Coastal Pines Technical College’s Baxley and Waycross campuses, Southeastern Technical College’s Vidalia campus, Savannah Technical College, and Ogeechee Technical College.
- TCSG Commissioner Greg Dozier stated the Technical College System of Georgia is proud of its partnership with Hyundai, providing comprehensive training programs tailored to the growing EV industry to cultivate a highly skilled workforce.
- In September 2023, Georgia Institute of Technology (Georgia Tech) and Hyundai Motor Company signed a memorandum of understanding to collaborate on research and applications for sustainable mobility, including developing a hydrogen economy, workforce development, and making cities smarter and more sustainable.
- U.S. News & World Report ranked Georgia Tech as the top public university and No. 3 nationally in energy and fuel research.
- Georgia Tech President Ángel Cabrera stated the strategic partnership represents a significant corporate relationship to advance science, emerging technologies, and produce the next-generation workforce.
- Since 1998, Hyundai has directed over $6 million towards local Georgia communities through philanthropic programs and partnerships.
- The company is promoting STEM programs in local K-12 schools, hosting educational programs on alternative energy sources, supporting affordable homeownership organizations, and focusing on environmental stewardship.
- Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority’s Trip Tollison stated Hyundai has given more than $1 million to regional nonprofits, formed key partnerships with local educational institutions, committed to $7.59 billion in capital investment in HMGMA, will create 8,500 jobs, and hired more than 550 Meta Pros (75% from the Savannah region).
- Beginning with mass production start, electricity used to power the Metaplant will come from 100 percent clean power sources, aligning with Hyundai’s commitment to carbon neutrality by 2045.
- A 41-acre park will be featured in front of the plant including trails, running tracks, environmental art displays, sports fields, and other amenities for employee use.
