# Volvo Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-02-09

**Source:** [Volvo Q4 truck orders fall 34% YoY in North America | Trucking Dive](https://www.truckingdive.com/news/volvo-trucks-group-q4-2025-reduce-production-north-america/811486/)  ·  `page_014288`

## Facts

- Volvo Group plans to modestly scale back U.S. truck production after orders in North America declined by 34% year over year to about 11,100 units.
- The OEM pointed to the lingering freight recession resulting in low levels of demand as the cause for reduced orders.
- The company will implement “stop weeks” in Q1 rather than make more permanent structural changes.
- CEO and President Martin Lundstedt announced the Q1 stop weeks during a Jan. 28 earnings call.
- The move follows an undisclosed number of stop days in Q4, which resulted in under-absorption and weighed on the manufacturer’s overall performance for the period.
- Truck deliveries fell 16% to 11,490 trucks during that same period.
- CEO Martin Lundstedt stated the company will muddle through, keep focus, and wait for the North American market to recover to align capacity and inventory levels.
- Volvo executives said recent ordering activity points to a gradual recovery in North America later in the year.
- Carriers could prebuy vehicles due to uncertainty surrounding the Environmental Protection Agency’s 2027 emissions standards.
- The company raised its 2026 guidance for truck registrations in North America to 265,000 units, an increase of 15,000 from the forecast issued in Q3 2025.
- If realized, that figure would represent a slight improvement over the 257,968 registrations recorded in 2025 but would still fall well short of the 304,274 registrations seen in 2024.
- Lundstedt noted that Volvo has observed stabilization in several markets but cautioned that geopolitical developments could still weigh on demand.
- Lundstedt stated the company is starting to see somewhat better activity levels and noted there are a number of parameters supporting a potential recovery, though it may be too early to confirm.
