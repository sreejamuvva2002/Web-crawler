# SK On-Hyundai Motor Group Joint Venture Battery Plant

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Georgia offered $641M in incentives to woo SK-Hyundai EV battery plant](https://www.ajc.com/news/georgia-offered-700m-in-incentives-to-woo-sk-hyundai-ev-battery-plant/EO6SLED67RDEHPMXA7OLRR5QI4/)  ·  `page_050147`

## Facts

- Incentives for SK-Hyundai EV battery plant could total $641M.
- Two Korean conglomerates could receive more than $640 million in grants, tax breaks, free infrastructure and other incentives from state and local governments to build a $5 billion battery factory in Bartow County.
- The incentive package offered to SK On was detailed in documents obtained by The Atlanta Journal-Constitution through the Georgia Open Records Act.
- The company later brought on Hyundai Motor Group as an equal partner.
- Plans for the factory near Cartersville were announced in December.
- It is expected to employ at least 3,750 workers with an average wage of nearly $53,000.
- The factory will be roughly the size of two Lenox Square malls.
- The facility will join other multibillion-dollar endeavors by SK and Hyundai across Georgia, furthering the Peach State’s status as an EV hub.
- The Bartow plant will supply batteries to the Kia factory in West Point and a Hyundai plant in Alabama that are incorporating EVs into their production lines.
- Total incentives for the project amount to $641.4 million.
- State tax breaks total $191.02 million.
- Local job creation grants total $89.3 million.
- Plant property tax abatement totals $64.0 million.
- ABER tax credit amounts to $4.0 million.
- Georgia Quality Jobs grant totals $11.0 million.
- State and local infrastructure funding totals $6.0 million.
- Local economic development incentives total $3.4 million.
- Total value not included in the incentive package is $25.1 million.
- Total value of incremental incentives received by the plant is not disclosed.
- SK On staved off competition from Kia, Hyundai Genesis EV, and others to build a multibillion-dollar battery plant.
- SK On was awarded the project after outcompeting other bidders for state and local incentives.
- The Georgia Open Records Act allowed AJC to obtain the incentive documents.
- The facility will be located in Cartersville, Bartow County.
- SK On CEO Kim Jong-ho said, 'We are thrilled to bring this project to Georgia.'
- Michael Moore, Secretary of Transportation, said that as a state and local government, they are committed to creating jobs and economic development for the future, calling it a win-win situation.
