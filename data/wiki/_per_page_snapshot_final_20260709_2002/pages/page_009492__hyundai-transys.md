# Hyundai TRANSYS

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [New Hyundai TRANSYS Plant Part of $240M Expansion in Georgia : CEG](https://www.constructionequipmentguide.com/new-hyundai-transys-plant-part-of-240m-expansion-in-georgia/50613)  ·  `page_009492`

## Facts

- An automotive parts producer specializing in transmissions will expand its Georgia operations with a $240 million investment that includes adding hundreds of new jobs.
- Hyundai TRANSYS will build a 620,000-square-foot facility in West Point, near the company's current location on the Kia campus.
- The facility will produce eight-speed transmissions for Hyundai Motor Company.
- Hyundai TRANSYS is the only automobile manufacturing company in the world that specializes in transmissions.
- New jobs will include advanced manufacturing and management roles, as well as supplementary construction handling and inspection.
- The expansion will bring Hyundai TRANSYS' total employment in Georgia to 1,741 jobs.
- Sangkil Jung, president of Hyundai TRANSYS Georgia Powertrain, stated that trust built through years of capital investment, employment, and training support, along with new opportunities unmatched by any other states, made Georgia a natural choice.
- Sangkil Jung expressed excitement for future opportunities as the company expands its presence in the West Point community.
- Senior Project Manager Amanda Fields represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development's Global Commerce division on securing the project.
- The project was secured in partnership with the West Point Development Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- Georgia has seen more than $1 billion in investment and created 2,000 jobs from South Korean companies like Hyundai in 2020.
- Earlier in 2020, TEKLAS announced it would invest $6.5 million to open its first North American facility and headquarters in Gordon County.
- TEKLAS is a Turkish manufacturing company and supplier of electric vehicle parts.
- Over the summer, GEDIA Automotive Group announced an $85 million investment to build a new 180,000-square-foot manufacturing facility in Whitfield County.
- SK Innovation announced a $940 million plan to expand its EV lithium-ion battery plant in Commerce.
- Georgia is home to more than 200 automotive-related facilities.
