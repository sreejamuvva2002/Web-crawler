# Georgia Emerging Automotive Recognition (GEAR) Awards

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2020-10

**Source:** [GDEcD Announces 2020 Georgia ‘GEAR Award’ Winners - Albany CEO](https://albanyceo.com/features/2020/10/gdecd-announces-2020-georgia-gear-award-winners/)  ·  `page_010929`

## Facts

- The Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) announced the 2020 Georgia GEAR Award winners on Monday, October 19th, 2020.
- The awards were previously known as the Georgia Automotive Awards and are designed to recognize forward movement in the state’s automotive and mobility industries through innovation and emerging technologies.
- Nominations for companies from across the state were reviewed and decided by a diverse committee of experts who retain a deep understanding of the automotive and manufacturing industries.
- Socially distanced award presentations recognizing the winners, including one completely virtual ceremony, were conducted by leaders from GDEcD’s Global Commerce division and community economic development partners.
- Georgia is home to more than 200 automotive-related facilities.
- The automotive industry contributes more than $3 billion to the state’s economy.
- The state’s skilled workforce, strong logistics network, and central location among Southeastern Original Equipment Manufacturers continues to attract automotive and mobility suppliers from across the U.S. and internationally.
- Nifco KTW America won Supplier of the Year for companies employing 200+ people and is located in Toccoa, Georgia.
- Nifco’s Georgia location in Toccoa currently employs more than 350 Georgians.
- Nifco is a globally known Tier 1 supplier of automotive parts with customers including BMW, Mercedes, Volkswagen, and many more.
- Sangsin Technology America won Supplier of the Year for companies employing 199 or fewer people and is located in McDonough, Georgia.
- Sangsin supplies to companies like Vanguard and Great Dane and its McDonough manufacturing facility currently employs approximately 30 people in Georgia.
- Kubota Manufacturing of America Corporation won Transportation Equipment Manufacturer of the Year and is located in Gainesville, Georgia.
- Kubota’s North American headquarters are located in Gainesville, Georgia, where approximately 3,000 Georgians help make top-of-the-line Georgia Made™ RTVs, Tractors, Lawn Tractors, Mowers, and more.
- Kubota recently broke ground on its North American Research and Development Center, marking its fourth major expansion within the state.
- Panasonic Automotive Systems won the Automotive Innovation Award and is located in Peachtree City, Georgia.
- Panasonic’s Georgia facility employs roughly 1,500 people and helps numerous Original Equipment Manufacturers adapt to modern consumer trends like ride-sharing apps and electric vehicle purchases.
- Freudenberg NOK Sealing Technologies won the Citizenship Award and is located in Cleveland, Georgia.
- Freudenberg has had operations in Georgia for more than 30 years, where its facility employs roughly 1,200 Georgians.
- Freudenberg’s dedicated ‘Culture Team’ serves its community through initiatives including engineers assisting with ‘Rocket Day’ at White County Middle School, donating funds to the school’s engineering program, and planting trees with the 4-H.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that the state’s automotive and mobility industries are major drivers of jobs and innovation.
- GDEcD Deputy Commissioner of Global Commerce Scott McMurray noted that winners’ dedication to employees and customers has been critical to strengthening the state’s diverse automotive and mobility ecosystem.
- Kubota Manufacturing of America President Hirohiko Arai stated, “We are very honored to be recognized by the State of Georgia for this award. As a manufacturer in this field, our strength comes from our products, our processes and most importantly, from our people.”
- Panasonic Automotive President Scott Kirchner stated that developing innovative products for safer roadways is their core mission and praised the innovation and dedication of Panasonic Automotive Employees.
