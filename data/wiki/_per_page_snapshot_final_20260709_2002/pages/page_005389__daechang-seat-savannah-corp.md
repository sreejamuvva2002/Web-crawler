# Daechang Seat Savannah Corp.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-10

**Source:** [Automotive supplier Daechang Seat Savannah Corp. to create 500 jobs in Chatham County – Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority](https://savannahjda.com/2023/10/automotive-supplier-daechang-seat-corp-usa-to-create-500-jobs-in-chatham-county/)  ·  `page_005389`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Daechang Seat Savannah Corp., a supplier specializing in automotive seat frames, will invest $72.5 million in the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center.
- The facility is located in close proximity to Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA).
- The project will create over 500 jobs.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that suppliers for the HMGMA project alone reach at least nine counties.
- Governor Brian Kemp noted the growing manufacturing base is centered around Chatham County and the Savannah region.
- Daechang Seat Corp. (DSC) was established in 1979 in Kyungju, South Korea.
- DSC has become one of the largest seat frame producers in the world.
- Globally, DSC employs more than 5,000 people across seven countries.
- The company’s production process includes fully automatic lines with arc welding and spot-welding machines.
- CEO Jinsuk Lee expressed heartfelt appreciation to the State of Georgia, its local communities, and the City of Savannah.
- DSC maintains a prominent partnership with Hyundai Transys and Hyundai Mobis at HMGMA.
- The company's primary objective is to drive growth through facility expansions to transform into a global entity.
- The new facility will be located at the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center.
- The company will hire assembly operators, equipment technicians, mechanical engineers, and electrical engineers.
- Hiring will occur through local job fairs and website postings.
- Georgia Ports Authority President and CEO Griff Lynch stated DSC is only minutes from the Port of Savannah.
- DSC is ideally located to take advantage of Georgia’s world-class connectivity for U.S. supply chains.
- Savannah Economic Development Authority President and CEO Trip Tollison announced DSC is the third Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America supplier locating in the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center.
- The project brings 500 new jobs and $72.5 million in capital investment to Chatham County.
- Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy Alyce Thornhill represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The project was developed in partnership with the Savannah Economic Development Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that countless partners are helping transform Georgia into a center of the automotive and mobility industry.
- As the market transitions to electric vehicles, Georgia has pursued the entire supply chain.
- Georgia has created more than $25 billion in investments and 30,000 jobs since 2018.
- In fiscal year 2023, job creation in the automotive industry increased by 324 percent when compared to FY21.
- Suppliers attracted by Georgia’s mobility original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) resulted in over $2 billion in investment across the state in FY23.
- Daechang Seat Co. recently ventured into the electric vehicle sector and continues to expand its scope.
- DSC anticipates greater opportunities for growth with the completion of new manufacturing facilities, providing larger installation spaces.
- The announcement was published on Oct. 23, 2023.
