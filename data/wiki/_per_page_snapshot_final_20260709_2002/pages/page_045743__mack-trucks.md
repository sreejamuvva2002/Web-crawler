# Mack Trucks

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-08-15

**Source:** [In Macungie, Sen. Bob Casey tackles inflation, investment in good jobs | WITF](https://www.witf.org/2022/08/15/in-macungie-sen-bob-casey-tackles-inflation-investment-in-good-jobs/)  ·  `page_045743`

## Facts

- U.S. Sen. Bob Casey (D-PA) visited the Mack Trucks assembly plant in Macungie on Friday to discuss recent federal legislation.
- The Inflation Reduction Act was set to pass the U.S. House of Representatives around August 2022, alongside the recently signed CHIPS and Science Act.
- Both the Inflation Reduction Act and the CHIPS and Science Act include billions of dollars in government investment for technology research and development, plus sustainability incentives.
- Sen. Casey stated that manufacturing incentives from these bills will help alleviate inflation, reduce future price shocks by lowering clean energy costs, and relieve supply chain bottlenecks to bring down clean vehicle costs.
- Mack representatives showcased their new LR Electric trash-collection truck during the visit.
- Mack representatives noted that heavy-duty electric trucks are in their infancy and require government assistance for development and deployment.
- The CHIPS and Science Act allocates $53 billion toward domestic semiconductor chip manufacturing to address shortages and foreign import reliance.
- Heavy-duty trucks like the LR Electric can require up to 1,500 semiconductor chips per truck to control key safety and control functions.
- Scott Wolf, president of United Auto Workers Local 677, stated that supply chain issues and limited chip supplies have challenged the trucking industry and its workers.
- Wolf noted that chip shortages are slowing production and delivery times and reducing worker wages due to unscheduled downtime.
- Jonathan Miller, senior vice president of public affairs for Mack’s parent company Volvo, stated that federal investments are critical for supporting Mack's operations.
- Miller emphasized that the availability of semiconductor chips significantly impacts manufacturing ability and called for increased domestic production of critical infrastructure components like chips, citing the CHIPS Act as helpful.
- Miller expressed optimism about sustainable vehicles like the LR Electric and their impact on the region.
- Miller stated Mack believes sustainable trucks are the future of the classic trucking industry and is positive about the job outlook in the Lehigh Valley, noting production at the facility since 1976.
- Miller indicated the LR Electric is just the first step in Mack's zero-emission truck platform efforts.
- Miller highlighted that federal tax credits, increased semiconductor production, and charging infrastructure construction will be crucial for the future of electric trucks like the LR Electric.
- Sen. Casey emphasized that domestic manufacturing investment is vital for national security to ensure U.S. control over production rather than economic rivals like China.
- Casey warned that failing to make these federal investments in research and development would cause the U.S. to lose to China.
- Casey described the Lehigh Valley facility's work as emblematic of the future of the region, America, the economy, and national security.
- Casey stated that federal investments will support both a clean energy future and good-paying union jobs.
