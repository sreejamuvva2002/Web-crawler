# Hyundai-LG Energy Solution Georgia EV Battery Joint Venture

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hyundai and LG plan new EV battery plant – EVs & Beyond](https://evsandbeyond.co.nz/hyundai-and-lg-plan-new-ev-battery-plant/)  ·  `page_056202`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution (LGES) intend to establish a $7.084 billion (US$4.3b) battery cell manufacturing joint venture in Georgia, US.
- The joint venture will be located where Hyundai’s “Metaplant America” is being built.
- Battery production at the new plant is expected to start at the end of 2025.
- The facility will have a 30GWh annual production capacity.
- The capacity will be capable of supporting the production of 300,000 EVs.
- This will be the second battery manufacturing joint venture for Hyundai and LGES.
- Hyundai states the aim is to continue strengthening ties with their long-time partnership in electrification efforts.
- Hyundai Motor Company president and chief executive Jaehoon Chang and LGES chief executive Youngsoo Kwon signed a memorandum of understanding in Seoul on May 26.
- The MoU aims to produce EV batteries in the US and further accelerate Hyundai’s electrification efforts in North America.
- Hyundai Motor Group president Jaehoon Chang stated that Hyundai Motor Group is focusing on its electrification efforts to secure a leadership position in the global auto industry.
- Chang added that they will create a strong foundation to lead the global EV transition through establishing a new EV battery cell plant with LG Energy Solution, a leading global battery producer and long-time partner.
- LGES chief executive Youngsoo Kwon stated that LGES will commit its best efforts to offering the ultimate sustainable energy solutions to customers.
- Construction of the new battery plant is due to start in the second half of this year.
- Hyundai Mobis will assemble battery packs using cells from the plant.
- The assembled packs will be supplied to the group’s US manufacturing facilities for production of Hyundai, Kia and Genesis EV models.
- This supply chain setup allows the group to respond quickly to soaring US EV demand.
- With this joint venture, LGES will have seven battery plants operating or under construction in the US.
- LGES is concentrating most of its resources in the US to expand production capacity.
- Hyundai and LGES have long been partners in electrification, having previously worked on EV battery supplies for vehicles including the Elantra Hybrid, Kona Electric, and Ioniq 6.
- The Hyundai Elantra Hybrid, an LPi hybrid vehicle introduced in 2009, was the group’s first electrified model.
- In 2021, Hyundai and LGES started construction of an Indonesia battery cell joint venture set to begin production in the first half of 2024.
