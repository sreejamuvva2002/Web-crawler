# Pratt & Whitney

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2023-06-20

**Source:** [Jet engine maker announces big Georgia expansion at Paris Air Show | Stars and Stripes](https://www.stripes.com/theaters/us/2023-06-20/pratt-whitney-expansion-columbus-georgia-10495425.html)  ·  `page_016908`

## Facts

- Pratt & Whitney announced a $206 million expansion to its Columbus, Ga., campus at the 2023 Paris Air Show in France on Tuesday.
- The expansion promises an additional 400 jobs.
- Pratt & Whitney has used Columbus, Ga. (Georgia’s second-largest city, which hugs the Alabama border) as its home base for manufacturing critical components for military and commercial engines since the mid-1980s.
- Airbus is a major aerospace customer of Pratt & Whitney.
- Airbus supplies passenger jets for Atlanta-based Delta Air Lines and carriers around the world.
- Gov. Brian Kemp attended the Paris Air Show where multiple Georgia-related announcements were made.
- Kemp touted Pratt & Whitney’s new investment in a news release as a sign that Georgia’s sky-high business ambitions are being rewarded.
- Kemp said: “From the flights that go through the world’s most utilized airport to the engines in the very airplanes, the Peach State is crucial to the movement of goods and products.”
- Kemp added: “Pratt & Whitney’s decision to expand will only further that reputation.”
- Georgia is home to Hartsfield-Jackson International Airport, the world’s busiest airport.
- Georgia has more than 800 aerospace facilities, including several operated by Lockheed Martin and private jet-maker Gulfstream.
- Last year, Georgia factories shipped $9.2 billion of aerospace products across the globe.
- This made Georgia the fifth-largest exporter in the U.S., according to the Georgia Department of Economic Development.
- Pratt & Whitney has an existing employee base of roughly 2,000 in Columbus.
- Workers at the Columbus campus maintain hundreds of engines and produce compressor airfoils (the metal blades seen whirring in a jet turbine that produce thrust).
- The expansion is expected to be completed by 2028.
- The project will include 81,000 square feet of new engine maintenance space and new machinery.
- The new space and machinery will allow the company to annually overhaul 400 Geared Turbofan (GTF) engines.
- Pratt & Whitney President Shane Eddy said: “Our Columbus business has grown from a small manufacturing facility to a large, state-of-the-art manufacturing and overhaul center.”
- Eddy added: “This investment will help ensure that we have the infrastructure, machinery and equipment upgrades in place to provide the best products and services to our customers worldwide.”
- Pratt & Whitney will likely qualify for job creation tax credits and worker training through Georgia Quick Start.
- A Georgia Department of Economic Development spokesperson said negotiations involving discretionary incentives, such as grants, remain active.
- The expansion comes on the heels of a new engine repair shop by Delta TechOps, which services Pratt & Whitney’s GTF engines.
- The Delta TechOps facility is 155,000 square feet and houses more than 100 mechanics.
- These mechanics repair engines used across about 150 airlines.
- Officials for Archer Aviation announced progress on the California-based company’s forthcoming 100-acre campus in Covington, Ga., east of Atlanta.
- Archer plans to build its first mass production facility for electric vertical takeoff and landing aircraft for taxi service at the Covington campus.
- The factory is a partnership with European auto brand Stallantis.
- The Archer Aviation facility is on track to open in mid-2024.
- Archer founder and CEO Adam Goldstein said: “High-volume manufacturing is critical to ensuring we can meet this goal and joining forces with one of the leading mobility companies in the world is helping us realize the once-in-a-generation opportunity we have to redefine urban transportation.”
