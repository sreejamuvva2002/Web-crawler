# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-03-26

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America Celebrates Grand Opening, Powering U.S. Economic Growth AUTOJUNCTION - Autojunction](https://autojunction.org/hyundai-motor-group-metaplant-america-celebrates-grand-opening-powering-u-s-economic-growth-autojunction/)  ·  `page_060485`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America is the key pillar of the Group’s $12.6 billion investment in Georgia.
- It is the largest economic development project in Georgia's history.
- The vehicle assembly and battery plant will produce up to 500,000 electric and hybrid vehicles annually for Hyundai, Kia and Genesis brands.
- Hyundai Motor Group commits to an additional $21 billion investment from 2025 to 2028 to drive U.S. manufacturing growth.
- Since entering the U.S., Hyundai Motor Group has invested $20.5 billion in the U.S., creating directly and indirectly more than 570,000 American jobs.
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America hosted its Grand Opening celebration on March 26, 2025, in Ellabell, Ga.
- The ceremony marks the completion of the largest economic development project in Georgia’s history, just two and a half years after breaking ground.
- Euisun Chung, Executive Chair of Hyundai Motor Group, stated that HMGMA represents advanced manufacturing capabilities, commitment to innovation, and investment in relationships with partners and communities in Georgia.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp attended the event and highlighted collaborations between HMGMA and Georgia’s universities and technical colleges, including Georgia Quick Start’s on-site training facility.
- HMG entered into a landmark agreement with the State of Georgia in May 2022 to establish an electric and hybrid vehicle production and battery manufacturing facility in Ellabell, Georgia, located 20 miles outside of historic Savannah.
- By June 2022, HMGMA was officially formed, and within four months, the company broke ground on the project.
- On October 3, 2024, less than two years after breaking ground, the first vehicle – a Hyundai IONIQ 5 – rolled off the assembly line.
- Hyundai’s IONIQ 9 three-row electric SUV is also now in production at HMGMA.
- The first Kia model to be built at the facility will join production in 2026.
- In addition to manufacturing electric vehicles for Hyundai, Genesis, and Kia, the plant is equipped to produce hybrid vehicles.
- HMGMA has the capacity to initially produce 300,000 vehicles annually.
- The Group's investment in Georgia related to HMGMA and joint battery ventures with LG Energy Solution and SK On is $12.6 billion.
- This includes a total of 8,500 jobs at HMGMA by 2031.
- The Group’s total investments in Georgia are expected to create nearly 40,000 direct and indirect jobs.
- These investments are expected to generate $4.6 billion in individual earnings every year, according to the Center for Automotive Research.
- As of July 2024, more than $2.5 billion in capital investment and approximately 6,900 jobs have been announced by HMGMA suppliers in 12 counties across the state.
- This more than doubles the additional $1 billion investment by suppliers that was originally anticipated.
- The Group announced a significant investment of $21 billion in the U.S. from 2025 to 2028, with $9 billion invested to establish an annual production capacity in the U.S. of 1.2 million vehicles.
- The Group plans to expand HMGMA's annual production capacity to a total of 500,000 units, an increase of 200,000 units from the original plan.
- The Group’s existing U.S. production sites – Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Alabama and Kia Autoland Georgia – will also see improvements in production facilities.
- A total of $6 billion will be allocated to enhance the Group’s parts and logistics business, as well as establishing an Electric Arc Furnace-based integrated steel mill in the U.S. through its affiliate, Hyundai Steel.
- The steel mill will have an annual production capacity of 2.7 million tons and will supply high-quality automotive steel plates to HMGMA when commercial steel production begins in 2029.
- The Group will invest an additional $6 billion to drive innovation and expand strategic partnerships with U.S. companies in areas including autonomous driving, robotics, artificial intelligence (AI), and advanced air mobility (AAM).
- Through this $21 billion commitment, the Group anticipates creating 14,000 new direct full-time jobs in the U.S. by 2028.
- The overall economic impact is expected to generate more than 100,000 direct and indirect job opportunities across related industries.
- HMGMA has integrated proof-of-concept manufacturing technologies and systems from Hyundai Motor Group Innovation Center Singapore (HMGICS), scaling them to accommodate larger production volume at the Georgia facility.
- All HMGMA’s processes – order collection, procurement, logistics and production – are optimized using AI and real-time data.
- Cutting-edge robotics and vision systems ensure high-quality vehicle production and help create a human-centered work environment with robots assisting human workers.
- Oscar Kwon, CEO of HMGMA, stated that the state-of-the-art smart plant is officially open and emphasized creating a comfortable and welcoming work environment for employees alongside an energy-efficient and technologically advanced plant.
- All work zones, from office to production areas, have been designed to encourage communication and build team collaboration with open layouts, ample daylight, and indoor landscaping.
- Production areas in the assembly building feature team centers with skylights delivering natural light at the center of the building for employee breaks and meetings.
- The 3,050-foot-long front of the HMGMA building’s exterior is lit in two parallel lines inspired by a stream, outlining an area where interstate travelers can view vehicles traveling by conveyer from paint to general assembly.
- A solar parking lot at the front of the site will generate 5.2 megawatts of energy, accounting for roughly five percent of the Metaplant’s total power consumption.
- The solar panels are installed above 1,878 parking spaces, providing shade for vehicles while simultaneously producing electricity.
- A 41-acre ecological park featuring trails, running tracks, sports fields, and picnic areas will be located in front of the Metaplant for employees to enjoy before or after work, with construction ongoing.
- HMGMA has deployed Hyundai Motor XCIENT heavy-duty hydrogen fuel-cell electric trucks for clean logistics operations, with a total of 21 XCIENT trucks currently in operation.
- These trucks represent more than a third of the Glovis America truck fleet at HMGMA and transport vehicle parts from suppliers across the region to the 2,906-acre Megasite where HMGMA is located on a daily basis.
- Since 2022, Hyundai and Genesis, along with various social responsibility initiatives and nonprofit organizations, have donated nearly $4.5 million to local organizations in Georgia.
- Kia's Autoland Georgia factory in West Point has provided over $22 million in support of Georgia communities since 2009, including more than $6 million to support educational programs such as the THINC College & Career Academy and SAE’s “A World in Motion.”
