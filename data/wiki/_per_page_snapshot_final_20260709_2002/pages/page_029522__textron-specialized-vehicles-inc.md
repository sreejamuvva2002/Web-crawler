# Textron Specialized Vehicles, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Textron Specialized Vehicles Recalls Bad Boy Off-Road Utility Vehicles Due to Risk of Serious Injury or Death; One Death and One Injury Reported | CPSC.gov](https://www.cpsc.gov/Recalls/2017/Textron-Specialized-Vehicles-Recalls-Bad-Boy-Off-Road-Utility-Vehicles)  ·  `page_029522`

## Facts

- Textron Specialized Vehicles, Inc. is located in Augusta, Georgia.
- The company manufactures Bad Boy XTO and Bone Collector XTO model off-road utility vehicles.
- The recalled four-wheeled vehicles are battery-powered and have a bench seat for a driver and one passenger and a two-passenger rear-facing bench seat on the back.
- Recalled vehicles were sold in camouflage, black, white, forest green, flame red, and patriot blue with the brand and model name printed on the side and front panels.
- The recalled vehicles have serial numbers ranging from 8000020 through 8004934 located on the steering column connected to the wheel.
- The recalled utility vehicles were sold at Bad Boy dealers nationwide from November 2010 through June 2013.
- Recalled vehicles were priced between $13,200 and $14,200.
- The recalled utility vehicles were manufactured in the United States.
- The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission (CPSC) and Textron Specialized Vehicles announced a recall of about 1,100 Bad Boy off-road utility vehicles due to the lack of seat belts posing a risk of serious injury or death from rollovers.
- A 14-year-old male passenger died in Toombs County, Georgia in 2014 as a result of a vehicle rollover.
- An adult male operator suffered a serious leg injury in a separate rollover incident involving the vehicles.
- Consumers are instructed to immediately stop using the recalled vehicles and contact Textron Specialized Vehicles or an authorized dealer for a free installation of seat belts.
- Textron Specialized Vehicles is contacting all known purchasers directly regarding the recall remedy.
