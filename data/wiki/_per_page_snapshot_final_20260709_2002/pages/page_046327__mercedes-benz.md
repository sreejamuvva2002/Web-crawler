# Mercedes-Benz

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-05-27

**Source:** [Mercedes-Benz expands in Sandy Springs, new HQ and R&D | James Magazine Online](https://jamesmagazinega.com/2025/05/27/mercedes-benz-expands-in-sandy-springs-new-hq-and-rd/)  ·  `page_046327`

## Facts

- Governor Brian Kemp announced that Mercedes-Benz is making its Sandy Springs U.S. headquarters its North American headquarters and developing a nearby state-of-the-art research and development facility.
- Approximately 500 new jobs will join the existing facility in Sandy Springs, now known as “1MB”.
- The expansion brings the total number of high-paying jobs at the Fulton County facility to approximately 1,300.
- The Sandy Springs facility originally opened in 2018 housing sales teams and has since been expanded to house financial services and corporate arms.
- The relocation and development are expected to take up to a year, with all jobs and facilities open by August of next year.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Georgia continues to lead in mobility and technical innovation, attracting world-class automotive companies.
- Jason Hoff, CEO of Mercedes-Benz North America, thanked the State of Georgia for supporting the deepening of the company's roots in the Atlanta area.
- Hoff noted that consolidating teams will enable greater agility, increased speed to market, and improved customer experience.
- Mercedes-Benz employs approximately 175,000 people worldwide and is increasingly making inroads in electric mobility and vehicle software.
- The company traces its lineage to Benz & Cie., founded in 1883 in Mannheim, Germany, with current global headquarters established in Stuttgart in 1904.
- Sandy Springs Mayor Rusty Paul stated that Mercedes-Benz has been an outstanding corporate partner since establishing its headquarters in the city in 2018.
- Mayor Paul highlighted that the expansion highlights the success of the city’s recent infrastructure and capital investments, representing a significant milestone for continued development.
