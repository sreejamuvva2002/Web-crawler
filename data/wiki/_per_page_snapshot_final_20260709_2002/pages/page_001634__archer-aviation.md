# Archer Aviation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Georgia Intelligence Report - Aerospace & Aviation: Innovative Archer Aviation Has Landed in Georgia: Relationships are key to another E-mobility project.](https://archive.siteselection.com/issues/2024/mar/innovative-archer-aviation-has-landed-in-georgia.html)  ·  `page_001634`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation has selected a site east of Atlanta, Georgia, for its Midnight electric takeoff and landing aircraft (eVTOL) project.
- The chosen location is a tract adjacent to a municipal airport in Covington spanning about 100 acres.
- The Archer facility will consist of three buildings.
- Georgia's incentive package for the project is worth about one-third of the company's pledged $118 capital investment.
- The project is expected to create 1,000 incentivized jobs.
- Archer chose Georgia over a competing site in Dallas-Fort Worth.
- Georgia's existing ecosystem of some 800 aerospace companies and a growing EV sector was a key factor in the decision.
- Tosha Perkins, Archer’s chief people and partnerships officer, stated that the talent pool for aerospace, automotive, electric vehicle, and general manufacturing was the number one reason for choosing Georgia.
- The airport location was favored for its benign weather, which is good for testing.
- Georgia's technical colleges and other organizations came together to provide training and workforce development support beyond the incentive package.
- Archer has a partnership with a local chapter of the Future Farmers of America (FFA) where trees cleared from the site are being used to make furniture for the facility.
- The company's current target is to move into the facility by the end of the year.
- Commercialization of the Midnight aircraft is targeted for 2025.
- Archer Aviation has raised more than $1 billion but has not yet turned a profit.
- The company's current headquarters is in Santa Clara, California.
- Tosha Perkins described the Midnight as having 12 propellers that take off and land like a helicopter and fly like a plane.
- Archer's vision for the Midnight includes using the aircraft as rideshare or an "air taxi."
