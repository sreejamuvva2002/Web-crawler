# Woory Industrial Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [EV Parts Manufacturing and R&D Facility Coming to Georgia](https://alabamaledger.com/ev-parts-manufacturing-and-rd-facility-coming-to-georgia-p1895-103.htm)  ·  `page_015415`

## Facts

- Georgia Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Woory Industrial Co., Ltd. will establish a new manufacturing facility in Dublin, Georgia.
- The facility will create more than 130 new jobs and an estimated $18 million in investment in Dublin-Laurens County.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated that Woory is helping Georgia on the path to becoming the e-mobility capital of the nation.
- He cited the state's highly-skilled workforce and reliable infrastructure network as key assets for the partnership with Dublin-Laurens County.
- Woory Industrial Company was established in 1989 and specializes in heating, ventilation, and air conditioning components and systems for the global automotive industry.
- The company is concentrating on the development of new products for electric vehicles (EV) and hydrogen-fueled cars.
- CEO Jungwoo Kim stated that the transition to electric vehicles is the future and the U.S. EV market is the key market among all automotive markets.
- He noted that over the past 10 years, Woory has grown into a specialized EV component company through continuous innovation.
- Kim expressed confidence that Georgia would serve as a vanguard for EV development and appreciated the partnership with the State of Georgia and City of Dublin.
- The new facility will be located at 404 Kellam Road in Dublin and will be the company’s first U.S. manufacturing location.
- The facility will produce electrical heaters, control units, and actuators for EV and internal combustion carmakers in North America.
- Operations at the new facility are expected to begin in November 2023.
- Electric vehicles do not have a traditional heating source, so heaters are required for cabin heating and maintaining battery temperature.
- Woory's pioneering development of electric heaters over the last 10 years has made it a leader in this growing market segment.
- Woory will be hiring personnel from all fields necessary for an automotive parts manufacturing plant.
- Dublin Mayor Joshua Kight stated that Woory’s commitment to placing an existing manufacturing building back into service promotes infill development and creates new career paths.
- Dr. Fred Williams, Chair of the Board of Directors of the Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority, noted that Dublin and Laurens County has a rapidly growing automotive sector due to its location on Interstate 16 and access to a skilled workforce.
- The partnership with area education systems and training providers will ensure training programs are in place to fill Woory's talent pipeline.
- Project Manager Jacob Lee represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The project was completed in partnership with the Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Georgia has invested for decades to match competitive projects with communities prepared for development.
- He emphasized that creating jobs and opportunities in rural communities is a priority for the department's manufacturing transformation efforts.
- From July 1 through December 31, 2022, the automotive industry was the second-largest source of new jobs and investment in Georgia.
- Since 2020, Georgia's pursuit of job creation along the entire EV supply chain has resulted in over $22.7 billion in investment and the creation of more than 28,400 jobs.
- Woory started producing fuel senders in Suwon, South Korea approximately 35 years ago before growing into an international enterprise with production plants worldwide.
- The company currently employs over 1,300 people globally.
- Woory's business is focused on the development and production of HVAC auto components for all types of vehicles, including electric, internal combustion engine, eco-friendly, and hydrogen-fueled cars.
- Woory has been nominated as a supplier for Tesla, Rivian, Lucid, and Canoo.
- The company is currently supplying the HV heater system used to heat the cabins of EVs.
