## Facts

- Mativ Holdings, Inc. is headquartered in Alpharetta, Georgia.
- The company operates two segments: Filtration & Advanced Materials (FAM) and Sustainable & Adhesive Solutions (SAS).
- Consolidated sales for the three months ended March 31, 2025 were $484.8 million, a decrease of 3.1% year over year and 0.2% on an organic basis.
- GAAP loss for Q1 2025 was $425.5 million, including a non-cash goodwill impairment charge of $411.9 million and $14.5 million of organizational realignment and asset impairment expenses.
- Adjusted EBITDA for Q1 2025 was $37.2 million, down 19% versus the prior year period.
- FAM segment sales were $187.6 million in Q1 2025, down 7.4% versus the prior year, reflecting lower volume/mix, lower selling prices, and unfavorable currency translation.
- SAS segment sales were $297.2 million in Q1 2025, up 5.7% on an organic basis, driven by higher volume across all product categories excluding closed and divested plants.
- FAM products include filtration media and components, advanced films, coating and converting solutions, and extruded mesh products, serving transportation and construction-related end markets.
- SAS products include tapes, labels, liners, specialty paper, packaging, and healthcare solutions.
- The company manufactures on three continents and generates sales in over 80 countries through its family of business-to-business and consumer product brands.
- Mativ's broad portfolio combines polymers, fibers, and resins to optimize customer products across multiple stages of the value chain.
- President and CEO Shruti Singhal stated the company is pivoting to a higher sense of urgency to drive enhanced commercial execution, de-lever the balance sheet, and conduct a strategic portfolio review.
- Total debt was $1,122.8 million as of March 31, 2025, with cash and cash equivalents of $84.0 million, resulting in net debt of $1,038.8 million.
- Total liquidity was approximately $407 million, consisting of $84 million of cash and cash equivalents and $323 million of revolver availability.
- The company's debt matures on a staggered basis between 2027 and 2029.
- Year-to-date 2025 cash used in operating activities was $15.9 million, with capital spending and software costs totaling $13.9 million.
- Working capital was a $22.1 million use of cash due to an increase in accounts receivable, partially offset by a decrease in inventories and increase in accounts payable.
- The company announced a quarterly cash dividend of $0.10 per share on May 7, 2025, payable on June 27, 2025.
- During Q1 2025, the company did not repurchase any shares.
- Risk factors highlight potential supply chain disruptions, including failures of energy, resin, fiber, and chemical suppliers to supply materials as needed.
- The company faces risks from changes in employment, wage and hour laws, labor shortages, and labor strikes or stoppages.
- Interest expense was $17.8 million for Q1 2025 versus $18.3 million in the prior year period.
- Unallocated organizational realignment costs totaled $9.2 million in Q1 2025.
- The FAM segment's adjusted EBITDA decreased 30% versus the prior year due to lower volume/mix, higher manufacturing and distribution costs, and unfavorable net selling price versus input cost performance.
- The SAS segment's adjusted EBITDA increased $1.0 million (or more than 3%) compared to the prior year period, driven by higher volume across all product categories excluding closed/divested plants, and lower SG&A expenses.
