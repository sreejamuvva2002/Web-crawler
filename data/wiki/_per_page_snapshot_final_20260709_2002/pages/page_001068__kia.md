# Kia

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-02-16

**Source:** [Kia Debuts First Georgia-Made EV at State Capitol - TT](https://www.ttnews.com/articles/kia-debuts-georgia-made-ev)  ·  `page_001068`

## Facts

- Kia executives met with Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp and state leaders on Feb. 13 to showcase the first electric vehicle built in Georgia.
- A fleet of three-row electric EV9 SUVs, fresh off assembly lines at Kia’s West Point factory and recently highlighted in a Super Bowl ad, was parked outside the State Capitol (Gold Dome).
- The display signals the Peach State's appeal as Kia, Hyundai, and Rivian ramp up plans to mass produce EVs across Georgia.
- Steven Center, chief operating officer and executive vice president for Kia America, expressed pride in Kia being first with the EV9 leading the pack.
- “We’re very proud of that, and we’re very excited for Georgia because of its big future in e-mobility,” Center said.
- The EV9 is essentially an electric version of Kia’s popular three-row gas-powered Telluride crossover.
- The EV9 starts at $56,395 and is currently on sale.
- Kia’s West Point plant is undergoing a $200 million expansion to incorporate EV9 production onto its current assembly lines.
- The expansion adds 200 jobs.
- Stuart Countess, head of Kia’s operations in West Point, stated the expansion is on pace to officially open in the spring.
- Production lines at the West Point plant will be able to switch between producing gas-powered vehicles and plug-in SUVs.
- “We have flexibility, so that we can move as the market is moving in either direction,” Countess said.
- The West Point factory has an annual production capacity of about 350,000 vehicles.
- Kia has shifted its focus toward hiring and training workers as part of the expansion.
- Kia recently launched its Veterans Technical Apprenticeship Program, which company officials discussed with state leaders.
- Kia and other automakers are facing renewed unionization campaigns, with the United Auto Workers specifically targeting Southern factories including the West Point plant.
- Steven Center stated that Kia brass does not expect union representation efforts to succeed.
- “We have a good relationship with people in manufacturing, so there’s no upside for them to have (union) representation,” Center said.
