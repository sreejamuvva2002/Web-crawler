# State of Alabama

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Alternative Fuels Data Center: Alabama Laws and Incentives](https://afdc.energy.gov/laws/all?state=AL)  ·  `page_028774`

## Facts

- U.S. Department of Transportation’s NEVI Formula Program required the Alabama Department of Transportation to submit an annually updated EV Infrastructure Deployment Plan for fiscal years 2022 through 2026.
- Plans must describe how the state intends to distribute NEVI funds and their physical and cybersecurity strategies, including a Community Engagement Outcomes Report.
- Submitted plans must address updated NEVI Guidance.
- The Alabama Department of Economic and Community Affairs offers grants for light-duty EV chargers and the replacement of qualified medium- and heavy-duty diesel vehicles with new diesel or alternative fuel vehicles.
- Grants are available for EV chargers; medium- and heavy-duty trucks; school, shuttle, and transit buses; freight switchers; airport ground support equipment; and forklifts and port cargo handling equipment.
- Vehicles must meet model year requirements to qualify for grants.
- Funding amounts are based on vehicle type, fuel type, and applicant type.
- Grants are funded by Alabama’s portion of the Volkswagen Environmental Mitigation Trust.
- The Alabama Department of Economic and Community Affairs administers the Alabama Research and Development Enhancement Fund Program to encourage new and continuing research and development efforts within the state to increase employment opportunities and products/services for citizens.
- The production of biofuel is eligible for ARDEF funding.
- Companies that invest in the development of a biofuel production facility may be eligible for a tax credit of 3% of the previous year’s annual employee wages.
- Companies may claim the 3% wage tax credit against utility gross receipts and utility service use taxes for up to 10 years, with options for refundability during the incentive period or carryforward for earned but unused amounts.
- Companies may also be eligible for a tax credit of 1.5% of qualified capital investment annually for up to 10 years.
- The 1.5% capital investment tax credit may be claimed against income tax, estimated income taxes, financial institution excise tax, or insurance premium tax, with carryforward options for unused amounts.
- For the purposes of the credit, biofuel is defined as a motor vehicle fuel produced from grain, starch, oilseeds, vegetable, algae, animal materials, or other biomass.
- To be eligible for the biofuel tax credits, companies must execute a project agreement with the Governor.
- Any motor vehicle equipped with an auxiliary power unit or other idle reduction technology may exceed gross, axle, tandem, or bridge formula weight limits by up to 400 pounds.
- Eligibility for the idle reduction weight exemption requires written proof or certification of the APU weight and demonstration that the technology is fully functional at all times.
- A vehicle primarily powered by natural gas may exceed the state’s gross vehicle weight limits by a weight equal to the difference between the NGV tank/system weight and a comparable diesel tank/system weight.
- The NGV maximum gross weight may not exceed 82,000 pounds.
- The Alabama Department of Economic and Community Affairs administers the EV Charging Infrastructure Program.
- Government and non-government entities are eligible to receive funding for installing publicly accessible DC fast chargers and Level 2 EV chargers along non-interstate corridors.
- Alabama Power is an investor-owned utility operating in Alabama that provides incentives related to alternative fuels and vehicles.
- Central Alabama Electric Cooperative is a cooperative operating in Alabama that provides incentives related to alternative fuels and vehicles.
- City of Bessemer Utilities is a municipal utility operating in Alabama that provides incentives related to alternative fuels and vehicles.
- City of Tarrant is a municipal utility operating in Alabama that provides incentives related to alternative fuels and vehicles.
- Coosa Valley Electric Cooperative is a cooperative operating in Alabama that provides incentives related to alternative fuels and vehicles.
- Tennessee Valley Authority is a municipal utility operating in multiple states that provides incentives related to alternative fuels and vehicles.
- Alabama Power lists EV Time-of-Use rate, DC fast charging station purchase rebate, and DC fast charging station installation rebate as incentive types, though notes no incentives are currently offered for these categories.
- Central Alabama Electric Cooperative, City of Bessemer Utilities, City of Tarrant, and Coosa Valley Electric Cooperative note no residential or non-residential EV incentives are currently offered.
- Tennessee Valley Authority lists EV TOU rate, DC fast charging station purchase rebate, DC fast charging station installation rebate, and EV charger deployment pilot programs as incentive types, though notes none are currently offered.
- The Alabama Department of Transportation is authorized to administer the Electric Transportation Infrastructure Grant Program to distribute grants for EV charging infrastructure expansion.
- An autonomous vehicle may be operated without a driver physically present if capable of complying with federal and Alabama traffic laws, certified per federal motor vehicle safety standards, can achieve a minimal risk condition if unable to perform a task or exits its operational design domain, and has proof of insurance.
- An entity that owns, operates, leases, or controls electric vehicle charging stations is not defined as a public utility under Alabama law.
- EV owners must pay an annual fee in addition to standard registration fees.
- All-electric vehicle owners must pay an annual fee of $203 and plug-in hybrid electric vehicle owners must pay an annual fee of $103.
- Beginning in 2023, EV fees increase by $3 every four years.
- A portion of EV fees contributes to the Electric Transportation Infrastructure Grant Program for EV charging stations.
- The state road tax for propane vehicles is paid through an annual flat fee sticker based on the vehicle’s gross vehicle weight rating.
- Each person owning or operating a propane vehicle must obtain a decal annually from the Alabama LPG Board as proof of payment.
- Vehicle owners must apply for a propane decal within 10 days of converting a vehicle to operate on propane, or a 20% penalty will be applied.
- Out-of-state alternative fuel vehicle operators purchasing propane in Alabama must pay the current Alabama motor fuel tax or elect the annual flat fee decal.
- Propane dealers or suppliers must remit collected funds to the LPG Board before the 20th of the month following the date of sale.
- The Alabama Department of Revenue administers an excise tax on compressed natural gas and liquefied natural gas used as vehicle fuel.
- CNG taxes are set at $0.13 per GGE from October 1, 2023, until September 30, 2028, and $0.18 per GGE from October 1, 2028 onward.
- LNG taxes are set at $0.13 per DGE from October 1, 2023, until September 30, 2028, and $0.18 per DGE from October 1, 2028 onward.
- A GGE equals 5.66 pounds or 126.67 cubic feet of natural gas for CNG, and a DGE equals 6.06 pounds for LNG.
- The Alabama Legislature established a Green Fleets Review Committee and Green Fleets Policy outlining procurement procedures for state vehicles based on fuel economy and life cycle costing.
- State fleet managers must classify their vehicle inventory for compliance and submit annual plans for procuring fuel-efficient vehicles.
- Plans must reflect a 4% annual increase in average fleet fuel economy for light-duty vehicles, a 3% increase for medium-duty vehicles, and a 2% increase for heavy-duty vehicles per fiscal year.
- Government entities must manage and operate fleets to be energy efficient, minimize emissions, and reduce petroleum dependency using proven technology identified by the Committee.
