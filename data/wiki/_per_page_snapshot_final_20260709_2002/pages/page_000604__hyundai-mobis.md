# Hyundai Mobis

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** power_electronics  ·  **Published:** 2022-11-29

**Source:** [Second Hyundai Supplier Announces $926M for EV Powertrain Plant  - Global Atlanta](https://www.globalatlanta.com/second-hyundai-supplier-announces-926m-for-ev-powertrain-plant/)  ·  `page_000604`

## Facts

- Hyundai Mobis will provide EV powertrains to the new Hyundai Meta Plant in Bryan County.
- Hyundai Mobis is placing a $926 million, 1.2 million-square-foot facility nearby that will hire more than 1,500 people.
- At full production, the facility will churn out more than 900,000 battery power systems and 450,000 per year once it hits its stride.
- These production figures were cited in a news release from Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp’s office.
- Hyundai Mobis is the latest tier-one supplier expected to flock to the area around the new plant and will likely be one of the largest.
- Joon Georgia committed to build a $317 million body components factory in Statesboro.
- Combined with Joon Georgia's commitment, supplier investment has already reached $1.2 billion.
- This $1.2 billion surpasses the $1.1 billion officials projected when Hyundai broke ground on its $5.54 billion plant.
- Hyundai Mobis has worked with Kia Motors across Georgia in West Point for more than a decade.
- Since setting up in West Point, Mobis has supplied cockpit modules, chassis modules and bumper assemblies to Kia and other automakers around the Southeast.
- Hyundai Mobis currently employs 1,200 people in Georgia.
- Its global system of plants includes sites in Alabama, Ohio and Michigan, totaling 44 plants worldwide.
- The new Bryan County plant will be located in the Belfast Commerce Park in Richmond Hill.
- The Bryan County plant is set to provide components destined for Kia’s plant in Georgia and Hyundai’s in Montgomery, Ala.
- Hyundai Mobis won the right to produce the electric powertrain for the e-GMP platform on which Kia and Hyundai are building their electric future.
- Executives announced plans for a new U.S. site in September without disclosing the location at the time.
- The company expects its compound annual growth rate to average 36 percent through 2030.
- Order volumes stood at $660 million in North America in 2020, $1.4 billion in 2021 and $1.7 billion in the first half of 2022 alone.
- H.S. Oh, vice president of Hyundai Mobis’s Electric Powertrain Business Unit, said EV investment is moving faster than expected.
- H.S. Oh stated, “We’re going to be a major production player in the EV market, and that’s going to trigger more growth within the sector.”
- Construction on the new facility could begin as early as January.
- Production is most likely to commence in early 2024.
- In October, about a month before this announcement, Mobis said it would spend $200 million on a new battery module assembly facility in Montgomery, Ala.
- The Montgomery plant operates on an 83-acre site that Mobis has occupied for 20 years.
- That plant will supply Hyundai’s Montgomery facility, which is investing $300 million into a production line to make electrified Genesis G70 SUVs.
