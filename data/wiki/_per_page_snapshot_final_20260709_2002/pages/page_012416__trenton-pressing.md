# Trenton Pressing

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-06

**Source:** [Trenton Pressing Breaks Ground On New $10 Million Manufacturing Facility - Chattanoogan.com](https://www.chattanoogan.com/2023/4/6/466945/Trenton-Pressing-Breaks-Ground-On-New.aspx)  ·  `page_012416`

## Facts

- Trenton Pressing, a tier one manufacturing supplier located in Dade County, broke ground on a new $10 million facility.
- The new facility will be 105,000 square feet.
- The additional space will allow for expanded manufacturing and assembly services and significantly increase the organization’s manufacturing capacity.
- Hans Pfunder, chief operating officer for Trenton Pressing, stated the company is extremely grateful for Dade County’s support throughout the expansion process.
- Over just over two years, Trenton Pressing purchased a struggling manufacturing company and completely turned it around through a reorganization of management, technology and equipment upgrades, and personnel training.
- The expanded facility will allow the company to better service its growth and meet the manufacturing needs of clients which include BMW, Club Car, ZF, and more.
- Trenton Pressing is currently one of the top five employers in Dade County with 220 employees.
- The company plans to add 20 more jobs with the facility expansion.
- Construction on the new facility is expected to be completed by December of 2023.
- Trenton Pressing began operations in Dade County in 2021.
- The company provides metal stamping processes, welding and assembly processes, warehousing, and shipping.
- Dade County Executive Ted Rumley noted that Trenton Pressing has been a great community partner since beginning operations in the county in 2021, providing jobs and supporting schools and the environment.
- Trenton mayor Alex Case stated that Trenton Pressing has been a great asset to the city since arriving just over two years ago and praised its commitment to improving the quality of life for citizens.
- Jeff Mullis, president and CEO of the Northwest Georgia Joint Development Agency and retired Georgia state senator, commended Trenton Pressing on its expansion efforts and for adding jobs in the Northwest Georgia area.
