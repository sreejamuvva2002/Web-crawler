# Michelin North America, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2025-10-16

**Source:** [MICHELIN ANNOUNCES FULL ROLLOUT OF TREADEYE TECHNOLOGY ACROSS U.S. RETREAD NETWORK | FinancialContent](https://www.financialcontent.com/article/gnwcq-2025-10-16-michelin-announces-full-rollout-of-treadeye-technology-across-us-retread-network)  ·  `page_046602`

## Facts

- Michelin announces the full deployment of TreadEye technology across its U.S. retread network following a period of phased implementation.
- TreadEye is a proprietary tread depth assessment tool designed to enhance the retread process and deliver measurable value to fleets.
- The system accurately and consistently collects 1,200 measurement points to provide precise insights into tread wear and casing condition.
- Nate Kirian, Vice President of Sales and Distribution for Commercial Business Lines at Michelin North America, Inc., described TreadEye as a game-changer for retread operations and the fleets served.
- Key benefits of TreadEye include identification of pull points by fleet location to maximize rubber on the road and avoid early pulls.
- The technology helps prevent casing damage, reduces DOT violations, and minimizes downtime from late pulls.
- TreadEye improves efficiency in the tire removal process, directly impacting a fleet’s bottom line.
- Michelin North America, Inc. is headquartered in Greenville, S.C., and employs approximately 23,500 people.
- The company operates 36 production facilities across the United States and Canada.
- Michelin has pioneered engineered materials for more than 130 years and manufactures high-quality tires and components for applications in mobility, construction, aeronautics, low-carbon energies, and healthcare.
