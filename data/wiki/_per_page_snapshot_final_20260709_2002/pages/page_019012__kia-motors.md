# Kia Motors

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Kia Motors says to suspend U.S. plant; shares drop | IBTimes](https://www.ibtimes.com/kia-motors-says-suspend-us-plant-shares-drop-511056)  ·  `page_019012`

## Facts

- Kia Motors said it would suspend production at its U.S. plant on Monday and Tuesday.
- The suspension followed media reports that a fire broke out at a parts supplier.
- News of the suspension sent shares in Kia and parent Hyundai Motor down due to concerns about possible output disruptions.
- Kia's U.S. plant is located in Georgia.
- The Georgia plant has an annual production capacity of 300,000 vehicles.
- The plant produces the Optima sedan, the Sorento SUV, and Hyundai Motor's Santa Fe SUV.
- A South Korean media report said a fire struck a Georgia factory operated by Daehan Solution.
- Daehan Solution is an unlisted South Korean parts supplier for Kia.
- An official at Daehan Solution declined to comment on the fire.
- Kia did not identify the specific reason for the plant suspension.
- A Kia spokesman said the suspension would have a limited impact on sales because the facility had an inventory of 1.9 months.
- Production is scheduled to resume at 6:30 a.m. EDT on Wednesday, March 21.
- Ahn Se-hwan, an analyst at IBK Securities, said the closure could lead to short-term production disruptions for the Santa Fe and Sorento.
- Shares in Kia Motors were down 1.9 percent.
- Shares in Hyundai Motor were down 0.7 percent.
- The wider market was up 0.3 percent as of 0312 GMT.
- Kia, an affiliate of Hyundai Motor, saw its monthly sales jump 37 percent to a new record high in February compared to a year earlier.
