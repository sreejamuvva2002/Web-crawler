# Storion Energy LLC

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2024-12-19

**Source:** [Largo and Stryten Energy to Form Storion Energy for the Manufacture of Vanadium Electrolyte and Battery Components for Long Duration Energy Storage - Junior Mining Network](https://www.juniorminingnetwork.com/junior-miner-news/press-releases/663-tsx/lgo/172534-largo-and-stryten-energy-to-form-storion-energy-for-the-manufacture-of-vanadium-electrolyte-and-battery-components-for-long-duration-energy-storage.html)  ·  `page_050623`

## Facts

- Storion Energy LLC intends to become a leading U.S.-based manufacturer of vanadium electrolyte.
- Storion plans to develop additional vanadium flow battery solutions to satisfy projected demand from the long duration energy storage (LDES) sector.
- Definitive agreements were signed on December 19, 2024, between Largo Clean Energy Corp. and Stryten Critical E-Storage, LLC to establish Storion Energy LLC.
- Francesco D’Alessio serves as Chief Commercial Officer of Largo and President of LCE.
- The transaction aims to maximize the value of Largo’s vanadium products and leverage Storion’s patented purification process to accelerate vanadium electrolyte manufacturing.
- The partnership is anticipated to increase demand for vanadium from Largo Physical Vanadium Corp. (LPV).
- Storion will be positioned as a U.S. domestic leader for vanadium electrolyte production and supply, with future plans to develop competitively positioned vanadium flow battery components for the long-duration energy storage market.
- Storion will share product and industry knowledge with Stryten Energy LLC, a leading U.S.-based battery manufacturer.
- Significant synergies are anticipated by leveraging complementary assets and integrating top-tier talent from both Largo and Stryten.
- Upon closing, LCE and Stryten will each contribute certain vanadium flow battery-related assets and liabilities to Storion.
- Upon closing, Stryten will pay US$1 million in cash directly to Largo.
- Upon closing, Stryten will contribute to Storion over time a total of US$6 million in cash to fund operations.
- LCE and Stryten will each hold a 50% equity interest in Storion upon closing, with customary pre-emption rights and anti-dilution protections.
- Board representation will be proportional to ownership, with Stryten holding one additional seat while ownership interests remain similar.
- At closing, Largo will amend its Safekeeping and Supply Agreements with LPV to adjust commercial terms and assign the Safekeeping Agreement to Storion.
- At closing, Largo and Storion will enter into a separate supply agreement giving Storion a right of first offer to purchase vanadium products.
- Gallatin Capital LLC acted as financial advisor to Largo, and Linklaters LLP acted as legal counsel for the transaction.
- Storion intends to remove barriers to entry for battery manufacturers by providing domestically sourced, price-competitive electrolyte for vanadium redox flow batteries (VRFB) used in LDES.
- Storion will maintain locations in Wilmington, Massachusetts and Alpharetta, Georgia.
- The JV is expected to support scalable domestic electrolyte production to establish a fully integrated vertical supply chain for utility-scale VRFB LDES solutions.
- VRFB technology provides long-duration energy storage greater than four hours to ensure grid stability and facilitate increased utilization of renewables for businesses and consumers.
- Stryten Energy is headquartered in Alpharetta, Georgia, and partners with major companies to meet demand for reliable and sustainable energy storage capacity.
- Stryten’s stored energy technologies include advanced lead, lithium and vanadium redox flow batteries, intelligent chargers, and energy performance management software.
- Largo Physical Vanadium (LPV) aims to provide direct exposure to physical vanadium, a non-degrading and fully recyclable metal essential for steel, aerospace, and energy storage industries.
- LPV’s strategy includes actively supplying vanadium to end users of vanadium flow batteries to advance renewable energy integration in long duration storage.
- Largo produces critical materials from its Maracás Menchen Mine in Brazil and is one of the world’s largest primary vanadium producers.
- Largo holds a 50% ownership stake in Storion Energy as part of its strategic investment in the clean energy storage sector.
- Forward-looking statements assume collaborative party performance and management execution, with risks including transaction completion failure, inability to realize benefits, and capital raising challenges.
