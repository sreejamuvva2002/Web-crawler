# MollerTech

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2016-11-09

**Source:** [MollerTech Breaks Ground Bringing 220 Jobs to Spur Growth | The Bibb Voice](https://www.bibbvoice.com/2016/11/14/mollertech-breaks-ground-bringing-220-jobs-to-spur-growth/)  ·  `page_057936`

## Facts

- Global auto supplier MollerTech officially launched work on a $46.3 million manufacturing facility near Woodstock, Alabama on Tuesday, November 9, 2016.
- The project will create more than 220 jobs and spur growth in the rural region.
- Greg Canfield, secretary of the Alabama Department of Commerce, stated that Alabama’s rural areas are now better positioned to compete for new capital investment and jobs.
- Canfield noted that conventional wisdom previously held large companies would not go to rural settings, but this is no longer true.
- The facility is located on a 20-acre site near Woodstock, which has a population of 900.
- Bulldozers have flattened a hillside location in the Scott G. Davis Industrial Park for the factory.
- The factory will be 150,000 square feet and become MollerTech’s flagship U.S. manufacturing facility.
- The new facility joins MollerTech locations in Georgia and Michigan.
- Steve Jordan, president of MollerTech’s North American operation, stated the company is already eager to hold a ribbon-cutting ceremony for the Bibb County facility.
- Jordan told reporters that the company is a family company that likes to work with local communities.
- The facility was formally announced just three weeks prior to the groundbreaking ceremony.
- The facility is expected to be in full production by 2019.
- It will supply interior parts for next-generation sports utility vehicles produced at Mercedes-Benz’s Alabama assembly plant in neighboring Tuscaloosa County.
- MollerTech is the first automotive supplier to locate in Bibb County.
- The selected Woodstock area is just minutes away from the Mercedes mothership in Vance.
- Bibb County Commissioner Ricky Hubbard stated the project's impact will be felt for many years in Bibb County.
- Secretary Canfield said recent project activity underscores that rural communities across Alabama are receiving increased consideration for economic development projects.
- The Alabama Department of Commerce and its allies have been working strategically to generate more economic development action in the state’s rural areas, which historically faced disadvantages like small labor pools and inferior transportation networks.
- Canfield stated they will continue to focus on growth, job opportunities, and capital investment in rural Alabama.
- Beginning last year, companies locating a qualified project in one of Alabama’s 25 “targeted” counties (fewer than 25,000 residents) became eligible for enhanced job creation and investment credits.
- As part of its project agreement, MollerTech was granted an enhanced job credit valued in total at $2.7 million.
- The company will receive assistance from AIDT, the state’s primary workforce development agency, in assembling its employee base.
- Two Rivers Lumber Co. received enhanced job credits for a $65 million sawmill project in rural Marengo County.
- AIDT is part of the Alabama Department of Commerce and will assist with pre-screening and training the company’s Alabama workforce.
- Canfield stated they have the workforce capability and that AIDT has the talent and expertise to assemble a workforce with the skills employers need.
- An updated version of the Accelerate Alabama strategic economic development growth plan is in the works, focusing on facilitating rural economic development.
- Canfield noted significant economic development activity is taking place in rural areas, and the Accelerate Alabama 2.0 strategy is focused on developing opportunities there.
