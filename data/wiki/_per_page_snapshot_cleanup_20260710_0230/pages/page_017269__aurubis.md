# Aurubis

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Aurubis Plans US Copper Smelter with Government Support](https://discoveryalert.com.au/aurubis-us-copper-operations-expansion-2025/)  ·  `page_017269`

## Facts

- Aurubis commenced production at its Richmond, Georgia facility in late 2023.
- The Richmond facility is Aurubis' first greenfield plant constructed in 115 years and marks its first major manufacturing presence in the United States.
- The Georgia recycling facility has an annual output capacity of 70,000 metric tons of high-grade blister copper.
- The facility uses advanced recycling technology to process complex materials and copper-containing scrap into valuable intermediate products.
- Aurubis is exploring three expansion pathways for its US operations: a new copper smelter, an expansion of the existing Georgia facility, or a second recycling plant on the West Coast.
- Expanding the Georgia facility would involve adding an anode furnace and tank house to produce finished copper cathodes instead of intermediate blister copper.
- The proposed Georgia expansion could include rod production capabilities to directly serve wire and cable manufacturers.
- The Georgia facility expansion offers a reduced implementation timeline of approximately 3-4 years compared to building a new smelter.
- Aurubis CEO Toralf Haag stated that preliminary discussions with U.S. government officials regarding support for a new domestic smelter have yielded "positive signs," though concrete proposals are still in development.
- The United States currently has only three operating copper smelters, including Aurubis' Georgia plant, compared to approximately 60 in China and 15 in Europe.
- Current U.S. refined copper demand is approximately 1.7 million metric tons annually, with domestic production covering only about 50% of the requirement.
- U.S. copper demand is projected to surge to 2.3 million metric tons by 2035, representing a 35% increase from current levels.
- Recent U.S. tariffs on copper products exempt raw materials like ores, concentrates, and cathodes to incentivize domestic processing and retain scrap within domestic markets.
- Copper is essential for electric vehicle production, charging networks, renewable energy infrastructure (solar, wind, transmission), AI data centers, defense manufacturing, and telecommunications.
- The U.S. copper recycling market is projected to expand by 26% over the next decade, increasing annual recycling volumes to approximately 555,000 metric tons.
- Aurubis set its European customer premium at a record $315 per metric ton for 2024, marking a 38% increase from the previous year.
- Benchmark copper prices on the London Metal Exchange recently reached a 16-month peak of $11,000 per ton.
- Aurubis is simultaneously planning a 500 million euro investment in its Hamburg facilities for a new precious metal refinery and complex recycling plant.
- Expansion of copper processing capacity would generate high-skilled industrial jobs including production operators, maintenance technicians, laboratory specialists, engineers, and management roles.
- Recycled copper requires up to 85% less energy to process than copper produced from ore, resulting in substantially lower carbon emissions per ton of output.
- Aurubis' advanced processing technology enables the recovery of precious metals (gold, silver, platinum, palladium) and industrial metals (tin, nickel, antimony) from complex scrap materials.
- Modern copper recycling facilities incorporate advanced air filtration systems, closed-water circuits, and waste heat recovery systems to minimize environmental impact.
- Electric vehicles use 3-4 times more copper than traditional vehicles, driving sustained demand growth in the EV sector.
- Government support mechanisms for copper industry development can include tax incentives, grants or low-interest loans, expedited permitting, infrastructure development, R&D partnerships, tariff protection, and energy cost subsidies.
