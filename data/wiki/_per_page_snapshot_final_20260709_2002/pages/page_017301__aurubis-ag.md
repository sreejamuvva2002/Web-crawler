# Aurubis AG

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2022-12-20

**Source:** [Press release: Aurubis adopts € 530 million investment package to accelerate growth ... | Presseportal](https://www.presseportal.de/en/pm/109932/5399438)  ·  `page_017301`

## Facts

- Aurubis AG adopted a comprehensive investment package of around €530 million approved by its Supervisory Board on December 20, 2022.
- The investment accelerates the expansion of the modular recycling system and doubles processing capacity at the Aurubis Richmond site in Georgia, USA.
- Additional investments target the Complex Recycling Hamburg (CRH) project for processing complex recycling materials and intermediate products.
- A third component involves doubling the output of the largest national and in-house photovoltaic plant at the Pirdop site in Bulgaria.
- The three strategic projects are projected to deliver an annual earnings contribution of around €130 million (EBITDA).
- The Supervisory Board also approved a comprehensive modernization project of the company’s IT landscape, including migration to the S/4HANA SAP platform.
- CEO Roland Harings stated that Aurubis is accelerating growth in recycling with the next expansion stage at its US plant and strengthening its core business by investing in innovative metallurgy in Hamburg.
- A second recycling module with an investment volume of around €250 million will be added to the Aurubis Richmond plant currently under construction in Georgia.
- An additional €90 million was approved for a capital expenditure adjustment covering infrastructure requirements and inflation for the Georgia project.
- Production at the expanded Aurubis Richmond facility is scheduled to begin in 2026.
- Recycling in the USA is growing by 5% every year, resulting in rapid increases in the availability of complex recycling materials.
- The expansion will double the throughput volume from a previously planned 90,000 tons to 180,000 tons of complex recycling materials per year.
- The additional plant will be built in accordance with the highest environmental standards to allow for environmentally friendly recycling of input materials.
- The expansion will create around 80 additional jobs in the region.
- Once both recycling modules are completed, Aurubis anticipates employing around 200 people at the Richmond site.
- Starting in 2025/26, Aurubis anticipates additional EBITDA totaling around €90 million per year from the approved expansion phase.
- The Richmond plant will benefit from attractive local electricity and natural gas prices that will positively impact cost efficiency.
- Construction is already underway on the first plant at the Richmond site.
- Total anticipated investments for the Richmond site, including the planned expansion, amount to €640 million.
- The Richmond plant is expected to provide an additional contribution to EBITDA of around €170 million starting in fiscal year 2026/27.
- Aurubis plans to finance the approved projects primarily out of current cash flow without needing a capital increase.
- The company projects an operating EBT between €400 and €500 million for the current fiscal year 2022/23.
- The Supervisory Board and Executive Board will recommend a dividend of €1.80 per share, the highest in the company’s history.
- Aurubis secured an investment of around €190 million for the CRH project at the Hamburg site.
- The CRH project will add capacity to process around 30,000 more tons of recycling material and internal complex smelter intermediary products.
- COO Heiko Arnold noted that the CRH process retains much more value creation in-house and highlights the company’s research and development expertise.
- The CRH project creates a foundation for additional strategic projects at the plant and represents a clear commitment to the Hamburg site.
- Once the CRH project is ramped up in 2025, the company forecasts an annual earnings contribution of around €40 million.
- An initial investment volume of around €12 million will be used to increase the Pirdop solar park’s output from 14 MW to 24 MW.
- Solar panels for future enlargement of the Pirdop solar park will also be purchased for €8 million.
- The current expansion employs stationary, double-sided modules that produce more than conventional PV modules.
- The expanded Pirdop plant is scheduled to be commissioned as early as 2024.
- Once complete, the expanded solar park will generate electricity equivalent to powering 9,000 households or a small city every year.
- Compared to coal-fired power generation, the expanded solar park will save 34,000 tons of CO2 emissions per year.
- Over the planned operating period of 15 years, the project will save over 500,000 tons of CO2 emissions.
- The in-house solar park supports Aurubis’ sustainability goal of a 50% reduction of Scope 1 + 2 emissions by 2030.
- For the outstanding 2021/22 result, the company reported an operating EBT of €532 million.
- A comprehensive commentary on the fiscal-year result will accompany the publication of the 2021/22 Annual Report on December 21, 2022.
