# Saft America Inc.

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Saft Confirms Location For Lithium-ion battery factory In Florida - News](https://eepower.com/news/saft-confirms-location-for-lithium-ion-battery-factory-in-florida/)  ·  `page_059246`

## Facts

- Saft America Inc. received a $95 million grant from the U.S. Department of Energy under the American Recovery and Reinvestment Act.
- The project followed successful negotiations between Saft America Inc., the state of Florida, and the city of Jacksonville.
- The new facility will be located at Cecil Commerce Center in Jacksonville, Florida.
- The total estimated cost of the project is around $200 million.
- The project will bring roughly 279 jobs to the area during the next six years.
- The facility will operate as a high-volume manufacturing plant building advanced lithium-ion (Li-ion) cells and batteries.
- Battery products will serve applications including military hybrid vehicles, aviation, smart grid support, broadband back-up power, and energy storage for renewable energy.
- Construction is scheduled to commence within the next few months and be completed before the end of 2010.
- The facility will ensure competitive battery solutions are imminently available to U.S. and world markets from Jacksonville.
- Thomas Alcide, President of Saft America, Inc., thanked the Governor’s Office of Tourism, Trade and Economic Development, Jacksonville Economic Development Commission, Cornerstone Regional Development Partnership, and Enterprise Florida for their dedication to bringing the project to fruition.
