# Freyr Battery

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-02-15

**Source:** [Freyr Battery abandons plans for Georgia battery factory - pv magazine USA](https://pv-magazine-usa.com/2025/02/10/freyr-battery-abandons-plans-for-georgia-battery-factory/)  ·  `page_001600`

## Facts

- Freyr Battery announced it abandoned plans to build a battery factory in Coweta County, Georgia.
- The Georgia battery factory was originally announced in 2022.
- The first phase of the Georgia factory was expected to produce approximately 34 GWh of lithium-ion batteries.
- The estimated capital investment for the first phase was $1.7 billion.
- Freyr Battery is moving its global corporate headquarters to Austin, Texas.
- The company plans to sell the 368-acre site of the planned Georgia battery factory to an undisclosed party.
- The sale of the Georgia site is expected for gross sales proceeds of $50 million.
- The Georgia site sale transaction is expected to close on February 15, 2025.
- Freyr expects estimated net proceeds from the site sale to total $22.5 million following repayment of state and local grants.
- The Georgia project was planned as a multi-phase clean battery manufacturing project.
- After the first phase, additional investment in the Georgia project would amount to $2.6 billion by 2029.
- Over the lifetime of the Georgia project, the company had estimated it would bring more than 720 U.S. jobs to Georgia.
- Freyr was motivated to build the Georgia factory based on the strength of the U.S. renewable energy and electric vehicle industries coupled with tax incentives from the Inflation Reduction Act.
- Freyr currently has a gigafactory under construction in Norway called the Giga Arctic project.
- The Giga Arctic project in Norway has a planned nameplate capacity of 29 GWh.
- Late last year, Freyr announced it entered into an agreement to acquire the U.S. solar manufacturing assets of Trina Solar for $340 million.
- The acquired Trina Solar assets include a 5 GW, 1.35 million square foot solar module manufacturing facility in Wilmer, Texas.
- The Wilmer, Texas solar facility started production in November 2024.
- The Wilmer, Texas facility is expected to ramp up to full production this year with 30% of capacity already contracted with U.S. customers.
- This acquisition is the first step in Freyr's plan to build out a vertically integrated domestic manufacturing footprint.
- The next phase of Freyr's domestic footprint will include a 5 GW solar cell facility.
- Freyr is currently selecting a site for the 5 GW solar cell facility and is targeting Q2 2025 for the start of construction.
- Freyr anticipates that the first Freyr cells would roll off the line in the second half of 2026.
- The decision to pull out of the Georgia project is a direct consequence of uncertainty created by Donald Trump’s stance on tariffs and his threats to repeal the Inflation Reduction Act.
- A company statement noted that the stability and growth of the renewable sector depend on clear, consistent policy rather than political volatility.
- The company stated that businesses cannot justify billion-dollar investments when a single individual’s agenda puts the entire industry at risk.
- The company characterized the decision as stemming from reckless policymaking that undermines American jobs, energy independence, and long-term economic growth.
