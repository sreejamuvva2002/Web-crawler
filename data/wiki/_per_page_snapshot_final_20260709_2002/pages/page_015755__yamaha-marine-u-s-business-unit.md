# Yamaha Marine U.S. Business Unit

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [New Marine Innovation Center in Kennesaw - Yamaha Outboards](https://yamahaoutboards.com/newsroom/company-news/yamaha-marine-u-s-business-unit-opens-new-marine-innovation-center-in-kennesaw-deepens-commitment)  ·  `page_015755`

## Facts

- Yamaha Marine U.S. Business Unit opened a new Marine Innovation Center in Kennesaw, Georgia.
- The new facility is the fourth major Yamaha facility in Georgia, joining the company's Marine headquarters in Kennesaw, the Motorsports and Intelligent Machinery Group headquarters in Marietta, and a manufacturing facility in Newnan.
- Yamaha currently employs approximately 2,300 team members in Georgia.
- The company plans to add more employees by partnering with local education institutions to hire engineers for the new Kennesaw facility.
- Products under development at the center include DockPoint™, a feature of Helm Master® EX Full Maneuverability that automatically docks properly equipped boats.
- The facility is also developing Siren Marine® products, which allow customers to monitor and control boat functionality from mobile devices.
- New Yamaha WaterCraft vehicles and accessories are currently under development at the facility.
- Career opportunities within the Marine Connected Division include business planning, user experience, IoT/Cloud Infrastructure, product planning, data analysis, sales, and marketing positions.
- Positions within the U.S. Marine Development, Product Management and Marine Technical Marketing Division include control engineers, perception engineers, product development engineers, project managers, hydrodynamics engineers, and various software engineers.
- The Yamaha Marine Innovation Center is currently accepting applications for employment.
- The Yamaha U.S. Marine Business Unit markets and sells marine outboard motors ranging from 2.5 to 450 horsepower.
- The unit also markets and sells fiberglass, jet-drive sport boats ranging from 19 to 27 feet, as well as personal watercraft.
- The business unit includes manufacturing subsidiaries and divisions of Yamaha Marine Systems Co., Inc.
- Kracor of Milwaukee handles rotational molding for the unit.
- Bennett Marine of Deerfield Beach, Fla. manufactures trim tabs.
- Siren Marine, Inc. of Newport, RI develops Connected Boat technology.
- Yamaha Marine Precision Propellers of Indianapolis produces stainless steel propellers.
- Yamaha Marine Group operates as a division of Yamaha Motor Corporation, U.S.A., based in Cypress, California.
- Governor Kemp stated that Georgia has been the number one state in the country for business for nine consecutive years.
- According to the Outdoor Industry Association, recreational businesses bring over $27 billion to Georgia's economy annually and employ around 238,000 people.
