# Rural Site Development Initiative

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-29

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp Announces Fourth Round of Rural Site Development Grants | Governor Brian P. Kemp Office of the Governor](https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases/2026-06-29/gov-kemp-announces-fourth-round-rural-site-development-grants)  ·  `page_002310`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced the fourth round of grants from the Rural Site Development Initiative (RSDI) within the OneGeorgia Authority.
- The fourth round of RSDI grants totals $7.8 million for ten local projects across Georgia.
- With this latest round, the State of Georgia has invested $28 million into rural site development projects since the program's launch in FY 2025.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that over 64 percent of new jobs created under his administration have gone to parts of the state outside the metro Atlanta region.
- RSDI grants are awarded for either site improvement or to assist with the costs of seeking Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) certification on approved projects critical to economic development.
- Communities match state funding at various levels based on the program's criteria.
- Lt. Governor Burt Jones stated that RSDI has awarded $28 million for critical projects in rural communities across Georgia.
- Speaker Jon Burns highlighted House support for grants bringing good-paying jobs, noting special pride in investments in Southeast Georgia navigating forestry industry changes.
- Georgia Department of Community Affairs Commissioner Christopher Nunn stated that GRAD sites are important to local strategies for attracting employers and investment in Georgia’s communities.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that over the last several years, GRAD sites have helped attract an estimated 10,000 jobs to communities across the state and shovel-ready sites strengthen state competitiveness.
- The Effingham County Development Authority was awarded $1.8 million for site readiness improvements, including a sewer pump station, at the GRAD-certified Savannah Portside International Park, with total project costs of $2.5 million.
- The Development Authority of Columbus was awarded $2 million for site improvement work, including clearing and grading, at the GRAD-certified Muscogee Technology Park, with total project costs of $10 million.
- The McIntosh County Industrial Development Authority was awarded $1.5 million for road, water, and sewer infrastructure improvements at the GRAD-certified Tidewaters Industrial Park, with total project costs of $2.8 million.
- This is the second site development award for McIntosh County, which previously received a GRAD-Certification Scholarship for this grant.
- McIntosh County’s progression from a scholarship to a site development award demonstrates the importance of project-ready sites within Georgia’s communities.
- The Thomaston-Upson County Industrial Development Authority was awarded $2 million to construct water, sewer, and transportation infrastructure at the GRAD-certified Central Georgia Business and Technology Park, with total project costs of $2.3 million.
- The Albany-Dougherty Economic Development Commission was awarded $135,000 to certify the Pecan Grove Industrial Park, with total project costs of $180,000.
- The Glynn County Development Authority was awarded $107,500 for surveys and research to prepare the Mallard Industrial Park for GRAD certification, with total project costs of $119,500.
- The Development Authority of Harris County was awarded $54,400 to certify the largest remaining tract of the Northwest Harris Business Park, with total project costs of $77,500.
- The Development Authority of Jeffersonville & Twiggs County was awarded $22,500 for surveys and studies required to certify the Hillandale Tract, with total project costs of $25,000.
- The Screven County Development Authority was awarded $121,500 for studies and surveys necessary for the Screven Industrial Park to receive GRAD certification, with total project costs of $135,000.
- The Seminole County Development Authority was awarded $47,250 for assessments and surveys to GRAD certify the East Industrial Park, with total project costs of $52,500.
- Eligible activities for grant funds include site studies, land grading, and other essential community infrastructure efforts that support site development.
- Grantees demonstrated substantive plans for industrial site development while meeting OneGeorgia Authority eligibility requirements.
- Use of funds is subject to all applicable state laws and regulations, as well as the policies and requirements of the OneGeorgia Authority and the Department of Community Affairs.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) serves as the state’s sales and marketing arm and the lead agency for attracting new business investment and encouraging industry expansion.
- The Georgia Department of Community Affairs (DCA) partners with communities to build strong commercial and industrial areas through economic development, local government assistance, and housing initiatives.
