# Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation (YMMC)

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-06

**Source:** [Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation Breaks Ground on Innovative SMART Warehouse in Newnan - Middle Georgia CEO](https://middlegeorgiaceo.com/news/2022/06/yamaha-motor-manufacturing-corporation-breaks-ground-innovative-smart-warehouse-newnan1/)  ·  `page_015844`

## Facts

- Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation (YMMC) broke ground on a new SMART Warehouse in Newnan, Georgia, on June 12, 2022.
- The facility is the first-of-its-kind SMART Warehouse for Yamaha Motor Company worldwide.
- The warehouse spans 200,000 square feet and utilizes state-of-the-art automated storage and retrieval systems.
- The new facility will fulfill dealer orders for Yamaha WaveRunners, Golf Cars, ATVs, and Side-by-Sides.
- The warehouse is expected to reduce response time for fulfilling dealer orders by more than 50 percent.
- SMART is an acronym for Sequenced Material and Reduced Transportation.
- The medium parts automated storage system will retrieve and put away 240 containers per hour, compared to the current rate of 170 containers per hour.
- The small parts automated system will handle 2400 containers per hour, compared to the current capability of 550 containers per day.
- New automated systems will enable Yamaha to reduce forklift usage by 50 percent through automatic conveyors and tuggers moving parts to factories.
- Mike Chrzanowski, President and CEO of Yamaha Motor Corporation, stated the warehouse will improve operational efficiency and material flow velocity to factory assembly lines.
- Chrzanowski noted that demand for all outdoor recreational products produced at the Newnan factory continues to exceed supply.
- The significant investment underscores Yamaha's long-term commitment to YMMC employees, the City of Newnan, and Coweta County.
- The facility will bring higher paid and more skilled jobs into Coweta County, including roles for robotics experts, software developers, and systems engineers.
- The warehouse was designed by SSI SCHAEFER.
- The facility will include an observation platform for local school students to learn about manufacturing and automated processes.
- Yamaha expects to complete the SMART Warehouse in 2024.
