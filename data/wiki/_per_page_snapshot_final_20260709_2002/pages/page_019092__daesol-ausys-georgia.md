# Daesol Ausys Georgia

**Source:** [Korean auto parts supplier to open factory near Kia’s West Point plant | Foshan AIR VENT Co.,Ltd](https://www.cnxssy.com/blog/korean-auto-parts-supplier-to-open-factory-near-kias-west-point-plant)  ·  `page_019092`

## Facts

- Daesol Ausys Georgia is a South Korean automobile parts supplier that announced it will build a new manufacturing facility near Kia’s factory in West Point.
- The company is a supplier for Kia, Hyundai Motor Group, and General Motors.
- Daesol Ausys Georgia will build a $72 million factory in Harris County, roughly 80 miles southwest of downtown Atlanta.
- The new plant will employ more than 140 workers.
- The facility will manufacture luggage boards and covers for electric vehicles.
- Kia’s new electric EV9 SUV is expected to roll off the West Point production lines next year.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated in a news release that the project continues the ring of growth around Kia’s auto plant, which the state aims to recreate with two other massive EV factories.
- Governor Brian Kemp said, “West Point has become an automotive capital in its own right... Our state’s automotive industry employs over 50,000 hardworking Georgians and will continue to grow as e-mobility suppliers choose all corners of the state for the jobs of the future.”
- Founded in 2017, Daesol Ausys specializes in designing and manufacturing motor and EV interior components and accessories.
- The company plans to open its new facility at the Northwest Harris Business Park off Ga. 103 in December 2024.
- Kia expects to launch its three-row SUV called the EV9 next year, which requires a $200 million expansion to the Korean automaker’s existing factory.
- When Kia’s West Point plant opened nearly 15 years ago, it became Georgia’s only vehicle manufacturing plant.
- Hyundai Motor Group, Kia’s parent company, is building its $5.54 billion “Metaplant” near Savannah.
- EV startup Rivian expects to begin construction soon on its $5 billion EV factory in southern Morgan and Walton counties, an hour east of Atlanta.
- Georgia has emerged as a leader in EV production.
- Since 2018, the state has attracted $25 billion in investment and 30,000 jobs in the EV industry, according to Kemp’s office.
- This fiscal year, suppliers flocking to locate near the forthcoming Georgia factories represent $2 billion of that investment.
- Daesol Ausys will likely qualify for job creation tax credits and worker training through Georgia Quick Start.
- A Georgia Department of Economic Development spokesperson said negotiations involving discretionary incentives, such as grants, remain active.
