# Kia

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-05-30

**Source:** [U.S. Federal Manufacturing Incentives Working for Georgia: KIA Begins EV9 Production in West Point – U.S. Senator for Georgia Jon Ossoff](https://www.ossoff.senate.gov/press-releases/u-s-federal-manufacturing-incentives-working-for-georgia-kia-begins-ev9-production-in-west-point/)  ·  `page_000194`

## Facts

- U.S. Senator Jon Ossoff celebrated the start of production for Kia’s first American-made electric vehicle in West Point, Georgia.
- The production start occurred less than two years after the passage of manufacturing policies championed by Sen. Ossoff.
- In July 2023, one year after the passage of domestic manufacturing policies, Kia announced it would build its first American EV production line in Georgia.
- A Kia representative confirmed that the company plans to ensure EV9s produced in Georgia qualify for incentives under the Inflation Reduction Act.
- The Inflation Reduction Act incentivizes domestic production of electric vehicles and advanced energy technologies.
- Sen. Ossoff stated: “I congratulate KIA Georgia on this huge milestone reached less than two years after the enactment of made-in-America manufacturing incentives we passed into law. I thank all at the state, local, and Federal levels who have for years supported KIA’s success in our state. Georgians are proud these beautiful cars are produced in Georgia and made in America.”
- Sen. Ossoff cautioned: “I caution those who have called for the repeal of made-in-America manufacturing policies: repealing these incentives will hurt Georgia.”
- Former President Trump and some Georgia politicians have supported the repeal of the made-in-American manufacturing incentives.
- The made-in-American manufacturing incentives are intended to make Georgia-produced electric vehicles more affordable for consumers.
- According to a recent study, Georgia has benefitted more than any other state from the Federal manufacturing incentives.
