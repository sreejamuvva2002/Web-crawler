# Dongwon Autopart Technology

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-03-03

**Source:** [Korean Auto Parts Company to Invest $30M in Emanuel County  - Global Atlanta](https://www.globalatlanta.com/korean-auto-parts-company-to-invest-30m-in-emanuel-county/)  ·  `page_053960`

## Facts

- Dongwon Autopart Technology is investing $30 million in Emanuel County.
- The company is setting up a new facility in Emanuel County to serve Hyundai.
- Its Meriwether County factory has supplied Kia Motors in West Point since that plant’s opening in 2009.
- Dongwon Autopart makes seat, door and battery frames.
- The company was the first tenant in the Meriwether Industrial Park, which has since attracted other Korean investors.
- At one point, its operation employed more than 300 people.
- Combined, Dongwon employs more than 500 across the Southeast U.S.
- The company entered the U.S. market in 2003.
- Heather Hollstein is the human resources manager at Dongwon Autopart Technology.
- Heather Hollstein stated, “We are proud to be part of growing the state’s automotive industry – then and now.”
- The company has set its sights on Swainsboro, just north of Interstate 16 and east of Dublin.
- The company will hire 200 people at the Hwy 297 Industrial Park.
- Local leaders called the facility the biggest economic development project Emanuel County has seen in 30 years.
- The facility is located in a small community adjacent to I-16, the corridor leading from Macon to the Hyundai Metapant and onward to Georgia’s ports.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson praised Toombs County for preparing the Toombs Corporate Center as a top-tier GRAD (Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development) site in anticipation of wooing such projects.
- Pat Wilson wrote on LinkedIn, “Projects like this do not happen by chance. Economic Development is intentional.”
- Toombs attracted an $11.2 million investment plan from Shinsung Petrochemical, a Hyundai supplier also augmenting an existing footprint in the South.
- Shinsung set up in Alabama to serve Hyunda’s initial plant in Montgomery, Ala., back in 2010.
- Emanuel is the sixth largest county in Georgia by land area and has about 22,000 residents.
- According to the county’s website, it has the fourth highest forestry output among the state’s 159 counties and is No. 8 for onion production among counties in Georgia.
