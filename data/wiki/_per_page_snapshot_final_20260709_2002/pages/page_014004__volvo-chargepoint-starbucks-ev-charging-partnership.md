# Volvo-ChargePoint-Starbucks EV Charging Partnership

**Type:** charging_infrastructure  ·  **Role:** charging_infrastructure  ·  **Published:** 2022-03-15

**Source:** [Volvo, Chargepoint, Starbucks to expand Seattle-Denver EV charging - Ars Technica](https://arstechnica.com/cars/2022/03/volvo-chargepoint-starbucks-to-expand-seattle-denver-ev-charging/)  ·  `page_014004`

## Facts

- Starbucks is partnering with Volvo Cars and ChargePoint to install new DC fast chargers along the 1,350-mile (2,173 km) route between Seattle and Denver.
- Driving an electric vehicle between Seattle and Denver should be a bit easier by the end of this year due to the new charger installations.
- Anders Gustafsson serves as president and CEO of Volvo Car USA.
- Anders Gustafsson stated: “Volvo wants to give people the freedom to move and lower their impact on the environment. Working with Starbucks, we can do that by giving them enjoyable places to relax while their cars recharge.”
- Michael Kobori serves as chief sustainability officer at Starbucks.
- Michael Kobori stated: “We are thrilled to partner with Volvo Cars to test how we can charge our customers’ electric vehicles at Starbucks stores. Imagine a future where Starbucks helps our customers to connect—more sustainably.”
- The new DC fast chargers will be installed at Starbucks stores along the Seattle-Denver route.
- Planning an EV drive between Denver and Seattle is currently relatively straightforward due to existing infrastructure.
- Tesla currently operates 25 Superchargers along the Seattle-Denver route, spaced at convenient intervals through Washington, Oregon, Idaho, Utah, Wyoming, and Colorado.
- Tesla’s infrastructure includes several fast chargers in southern Wyoming.
- EVs that require CCS or CHAdeMO fast chargers currently must detour south to reach Denver via a longer route through Utah and Colorado to avoid running out of charge.
- The US Department of Energy’s Alternative Fuels Data Center shows more than 140 DC fast chargers (both CCS and CHAdeMO) along the Seattle-Denver route.
