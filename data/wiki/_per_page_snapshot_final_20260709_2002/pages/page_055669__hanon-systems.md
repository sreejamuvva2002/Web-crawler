# Hanon Systems

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** thermal_management  ·  **Published:** 2023-05-23

**Source:** [Automotive Supplier Hanon Systems to Create 160 New Jobs in Bulloch County  | Nanjing Conveyor Co.,Ltd](https://www.bayermesse.com/news/automotive-supplier-hanon-systems-to-create-160-new-jobs-in-bulloch-county)  ·  `page_055669`

## Facts

- Hanon Systems, a global provider of automotive thermal and energy management solutions, will create 160 new jobs and invest more than $40 million in a new manufacturing facility in Bulloch County.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced the investment while on an economic development trip to Israel on May 23, 2023.
- Hanon Systems will construct its new facility at Gateway Regional Industrial Park in Bulloch County.
- The new facility will be used to assemble automotive thermal solutions.
- Operations at the Hanon Systems facility are expected to begin in May 2024.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated that Georgia’s automotive and e-mobility industries continue to grow as the state welcomes Hanon Systems.
- Governor Kemp noted that Georgia is furthering its position as the EV capital of America, with new jobs and opportunities coming to hardworking Georgians statewide.
- Min Sung, President and Chief Executive Officer of Hanon Systems, said the company is pleased to contribute to Bulloch County’s local economy through job creation.
- Min Sung thanked the State of Georgia and its cooperating agencies for guiding the project, noting it aligns with Hanon Systems’ strategy to support the industry shift toward electrification while growing its North America footprint.
- Billy Allen, Chair of the Development Authority of Bulloch County, said Statesboro-Bulloch County is honored by Hanon Systems’ decision to locate there.
- Billy Allen highlighted that the new facility will work closely with the Hyundai Metaplant and the electric vehicle industry.
- Griff Lynch, Executive Director of the Georgia Ports Authority (GPA), stated the announcement further solidifies Georgia’s position as a growing hub for auto manufacturing.
- Griff Lynch noted that GPA supports the operations of Hyundai Motor Group and its suppliers to deliver jobs and opportunity for Georgia residents.
- Alyce Thornhill, Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy at GDEcD’s Global Commerce team, represented the department on this competitive project.
- The GDEcD project was completed in partnership with the Development Authority of Bulloch County, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that suppliers like Hanon Systems successfully support larger industry ecosystems through partnership-driven solutions ranging from workforce training to logistics systems efficiencies.
- Pat Wilson noted that Bulloch County is part of the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority and has been part of Georgia’s growing success story in automotive manufacturing.
- Georgia is positioned as a hub for the electric mobility industry, building on assets that make its automotive industry successful.
- Since 2020, over 40 EV-related projects have announced more than $22.7 billion in investment and 28,400 jobs in Georgia.
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America officially broke ground on October 25 in neighboring Bryan County for its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility.
- Hanon Systems was formed in 1986 and has grown into a leading supplier of automotive thermal and energy management solutions for electrified and conventional vehicles.
- Hanon Systems’ offerings include heating ventilation and air conditioning, powertrain cooling, compressors, fluid transport, and electronics and fluid pressure solutions.
