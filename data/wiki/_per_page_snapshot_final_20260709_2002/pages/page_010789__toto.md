# TOTO

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [TOTO Opens $224M Georgia Manufacturing Facility, Increasing U.S. Luxury Toilet Production Capacity by 150% | phcppros](https://www.phcppros.com/articles/22027-toto-opens-224m-georgia-manufacturing-facility-increasing-us-luxury-toilet-production-capacity-by-150)  ·  `page_010789`

## Facts

- TOTO is the world’s leading plumbing manufacturer with over $5.34 billion in annual sales.
- Opened a $224 million state-of-the-art manufacturing facility in Morrow, Georgia.
- The new facility marks a major investment in the company’s Americas growth strategy.
- Enables faster delivery of strategic high-end one-piece toilets to meet surging market demand.
- Shifting more production from Asia-based TOTO Group plants to North America to strengthen its regional supply chain and enhance manufacturing resilience.
- Taro Muroi, CEO of TOTO USA, stated the opening is “the next chapter in our U.S. manufacturing story.”
- Production at the original Morrow plant began in 1991, uniting TOTO’s manufacturing expertise, innovative technology, local craftsmanship, and commitment to sustainability.
- Muroi emphasized the facility strengthens bonds of trust, collaboration, and shared vision with employees, partners, customers, and communities across the Americas.
- With the new facility online, TOTO’s production capacity in the Americas rises to approximately one million units annually across its Morrow, Lakewood, and Mexico plants.
- The new building in Morrow alone adds production capacity of approximately 300,000 units per year.
- Luxury one-piece toilets are a strategic product for the Americas market, previously sourced primarily from TOTO Global Group plants in Asia with extended lead times.
- Localized production now ensures timely delivery and supports the company’s growth strategy across the Americas.
- In June 2023, TOTO broke ground on the new facility on the site of its original Morrow plant.
- Construction concluded in November 2024, with production scheduled to begin in the fall of 2025.
- The expansion adds about 161,500 square feet (15,000 m²) of advanced manufacturing space.
- The new three-story facility totals over 363,393 square feet (33,800 m²) and sustains 420 local jobs.
- The facility features a Tank Bonding Robot, a first for any TOTO Group plant, jointly developed with an equipment manufacturer to precisely position and bond tanks to bowls for one-piece toilets.
- An automation fleet of thirty-four advanced robots streamlines processes from molding to finishing while maintaining local artisanal craftsmanship.
- Six Automated Guided Vehicles (AGVs) carry three toilets each between molding, drying, glazing, and firing over approximately 44 yards (40 meters).
- High-Pressure Casting Equipment overcomes previous production limitations by enabling the manufacture of larger, more complex designs.
- Heat-resistant QR codes applied to each product enable real-time tracking, quality control, and big-data analysis to optimize manufacturing processes.
- Two planned convection-heated kilns with advanced insulation and digital temperature control will improve energy efficiency and cut CO₂ emissions by approximately 23% compared to current kilns.
- The new kilns are planned for installation in 2027, will accommodate taller products, improve firing consistency, and reduce energy loss associated with older kiln technology.
- The facility was built with sustainability at its core, aligning with TOTO WILL2030 global strategy centered on Cleanliness, Comfort, Health, the Environment, and Connections with People.
- Sustainability initiatives include energy-efficient manufacturing systems embedded throughout production and localized production that reduces shipping distances and lowers transportation-related carbon emissions.
- These measures support TOTO’s mission to contribute to the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) and maintain its leadership in sustainable innovation.
- TOTO began operations in the Americas in 1989 in California and launched U.S.-based manufacturing in Lakewood, Georgia, in 1991.
- The company opened its first major manufacturing facility in Morrow in 1996.
- For fiscal year 2024, TOTO USA recorded sales of $464 million, underscoring strong demand for its premium bathroom products despite a slowdown in the U.S. housing market.
- The company is a key growth driver for the TOTO Global Group.
- Muroi stated the expansion cements leadership in luxury bathroom innovation while creating a more agile, resilient manufacturing network to serve customers and support employees with safer, more efficient technology.
