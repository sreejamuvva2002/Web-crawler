# Kia Georgia, Inc.

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-02-01

**Source:** [Kia Georgia begins production of the all-new 2023 Sportage](https://www.kiamedia.com/us/en/media/pressreleases/18390/kia-georgia-begins-production-of-the-all-new-2023-sportage)  ·  `page_010117`

## Facts

- Kia Georgia officially began production of the all-new 2023 Sportage subcompact SUV on Tuesday, February 1, 2022.
- The plant facility is located in West Point, Georgia.
- The 2023 Sportage becomes the fourth model produced by Kia Georgia, joining the Telluride, Sorento, and K5 models.
- The celebratory launch vehicle was jungle green in color with the X-Pro Prestige trim package.
- The all-new Sportage is Kia's longest-running nameplate and has undergone its most significant changes to date as part of Kia’s “Movement that inspires” transformation.
- Sean Yoon, president and CEO for Kia North America, introduced the vehicle at the launch event.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp drove the celebratory Sportage off the assembly line alongside James Watson, Kia Georgia’s vice president of production.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated: “It was an honor to be here today for this milestone achievement by the Kia Georgia team. The Sportage is yet another great product built in Georgia, and its launch here in West Point is a proud moment for our state. I congratulate all of the great Kia Georgia team members for their continued commitment to quality and growth, and thank them for bringing more opportunity to the No. 1 state for business.”
- Sean Yoon stated: “The launch of Sportage in Georgia is a further testament to Kia’s longstanding dedication to the U.S. market and the local economy. Joining our tremendous fleet of SUVs, the all-new Sportage is poised to take the industry by storm with its cutting-edge design, adventurous capability and desirable in-car technology. Coming off a record-setting sales year in 2021, today’s launch adds further momentum to the Kia brand’s fast start in 2022.”
- Stuart Countess, president and CEO of Kia Georgia, credited the team's long history of success and reputation for quality as key factors in adding a fourth model to the plant.
- Stuart Countess stated: “The Kia Georgia team as a group is second to none and has been producing award-winning vehicles for more than 12 years. Today’s ceremony is another phenomenal accomplishment by the Kia Georgia team and I hope that each team member will take a lot of pride in being a part of this special day.”
- Kia Georgia, Inc. is the first manufacturing site in North America for Kia Corporation, which is based in Seoul, Korea.
- Kia Georgia has an annual production capacity of 340,000 units.
- The Kia Georgia facility is located on 2,200 acres in West Point, Georgia.
- Kia Georgia began mass production on November 16, 2009.
- Kia Georgia's current production lineup includes the Telluride SUV, Sorento SUV, Sportage SUV, and K5 mid-size sedan.
