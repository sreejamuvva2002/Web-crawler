# Rivian Automotive

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-03-01

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp, Rivian CEO RJ Scaringe and State Leaders Celebrate Rivian Day | Governor Brian P. Kemp Office of the Governor](https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases/2023-03-01/gov-kemp-rivian-ceo-rj-scaringe-and-state-leaders-celebrate-rivian-day)  ·  `page_000093`

## Facts

- On March 01, 2023, Governor Brian P. Kemp, First Lady Marty Kemp, Rivian founder and CEO RJ Scaringe, Lt. Governor Burt Jones, Speaker Jon Burns, Department of Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson, legislative leaders, local elected and economic officials, and company representatives celebrated the first-ever Rivian Day at the State Capitol in Atlanta, GA.
- The event included a legislative luncheon for members of the General Assembly to engage with Rivian executives regarding planned operations in Georgia.
- Governor Brian Kemp officially recognized Wednesday, March 1, as Rivian Day in Georgia.
- Governor Kemp stated that Georgia is fast becoming the EV capital of the nation and thanked RJ Scaringe and his team for their ongoing commitment to making Georgia the No. 1 state for business.
- Governor Kemp noted that Rivian chose Georgia because it has all that is needed for continued success and mentioned preparations for a facility groundbreaking later in 2023.
- In December of 2021, Governor Kemp and Rivian executives announced the company would invest $5 billion in a manufacturing facility at the 2,000-acre East Atlanta Megasite represented by the Joint Development Authority of Jasper, Morgan, Newton, and Walton Counties.
- The plant will create 7,500 jobs and be capable of producing up to 400,000 vehicles per year.
- Rivian CEO and founder RJ Scaringe thanked state legislative leaders and local elected officials, expressing excitement to build the next plant in Georgia and citing the growing EV ecosystem as a perfect environment to scale the business and launch new products grounded in a mission to preserve the world for generations.
- Site development for the facility began in 2022 with production expected to begin in 2026.
- Rivian’s technology centers at the new campus will support the company’s research and development programs.
- The company is actively working to ensure that both the construction process and future facility meet high standards of conservation and sustainability.
- Since 2020, more than 35 EV-related projects have announced more than $21 billion in investment and 27,400 jobs in Georgia.
- Senator Reverend Raphael Warnock called Rivian one of the leaders of Georgia’s electric vehicle revolution and stated that the multi-billion dollar investment will uplift Georgia workers and their families while keeping the state economy moving forward.
- Senator Jon Ossoff expressed appreciation for Rivian’s continued investment in Georgia jobs and prosperity, stating the state will continue working to make Georgia the national leader in advanced energy production and manufacturing.
- Rivian’s strategic location on the I-20 corridor will enable access to resources and speed products to market.
- Georgia offers 1,200 miles of highway and 5,000 miles of rail to move products like Rivian’s vehicles quickly and efficiently.
- The Mason Mega Rail Terminal at the Port of Savannah is the largest on-terminal rail facility in North America, which will allow Rivian to efficiently tap into supply chain needs.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Rivian’s choice of Georgia is a historic win that has put the state at the forefront of the electric mobility ecosystem.
- Commissioner Wilson noted that the generational investment delivering well-paying jobs in Jasper, Morgan, Newton, and Walton Counties will benefit families, schools, and public services beyond the region.
