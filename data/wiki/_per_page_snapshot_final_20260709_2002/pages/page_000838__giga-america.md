# Giga America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-11-30

**Source:** [Charged EVs | FREYR Battery to build Giga America battery plant in Georgia - Charged EVs](https://chargedevs.com/newswire/freyr-battery-to-build-giga-america-battery-plant-in-georgia/)  ·  `page_000838`

## Facts

- FREYR Battery purchased a site in Coweta County, Georgia for its planned Giga America battery plant.
- The company will begin detailed project engineering for the Giga America plant in the coming months.
- FREYR plans to invest some $1.7 billion in the first phase of Giga America.
- The first phase will be a cell production module with an annual capacity of approximately 34 GWh.
- The company is evaluating upstream and downstream modules as well as additional cell production lines for the project.
- These expansions are expected to bring total capital investments to more than $2.6 billion through 2029.
- The Giga America project will be based on SemiSolid battery technology.
- The SemiSolid battery technology was developed by FREYR’s US-based licensing partner, 24M.
- FREYR is currently evaluating clean energy supply solutions with US-based independent power producers for the plant.
- The company’s long-term ambition is to use as much renewable energy as possible to run the plant, as it does at its existing Giga Arctic facility.
- FREYR co-founder and CEO Tom Einar Jensen stated that expanding into the US has been a foundational aspect of FREYR’s long-term strategy.
- Jensen noted that with the recent passage of the Inflation Reduction Act, FREYR expects US demand for ESS, passenger EVs, and other electric mobility applications to grow rapidly over the next decade.
- Jensen added that as FREYR advances its US expansion plan with key stakeholders, it expects to make meaningful investments to spur job creation and develop localized, decarbonized supply chains in the US.
