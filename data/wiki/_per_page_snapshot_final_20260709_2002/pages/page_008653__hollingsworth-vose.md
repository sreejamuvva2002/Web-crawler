# Hollingsworth & Vose

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- Hollingsworth & Vose (H&V) is a global leader in the lead-acid battery industry for decades, manufacturing advanced separator products.
- H&V targets new technologies aligning with clean energy, decarbonization, electrification, and climate technology megatrends.
- H&V develops separator products for emerging battery chemistries serving energy storage systems (ESS), data centers, and advanced lithium-ion and solid-state batteries.
- H&V also produces separators for hydrogen production and CO2 capture applications.
- H&V actively seeks partnerships with battery chemistry developers to optimize new battery chemistry performance early in the development process using material science, manufacturing at scale, and non-woven design architecture.
- H&V is spearheading transformational change in emerging battery technologies including alternative aqueous-based batteries for high-power applications (e.g., data center UPS), flow batteries for long-duration storage, GEM technology (next-generation lead-acid batteries), and lithium-ion batteries.
- These emerging battery technologies require durable, scalable, and robust electrolyte-compatible separators to meet application demands.
- Lithium-ion (Li-ion) has become a dominant and preferred energy storage choice for many automotive and industrial applications.
- Nickel-zinc and other battery technologies are receiving renewed attention due to increasing demand for commercial-scale storage and varied use cases.
- H&V controls factors such as chemical compatibility, porosity, volume, and mechanics to deliver proper separator solutions for various battery chemistries.
- H&V leads the market in battery separator innovation for emerging technologies including alkaline batteries, solid-state batteries, and synthetic and glass separators for other battery chemistries.
- H&V is developing a nonwoven glass electrode material (GEM) that can replace lead plates found in lead-acid batteries.
- GEM technology offers improved cycle life by eliminating failure mechanisms associated with lead plates.
- GEM technology improves manufacturability by allowing engineering firms to replace stamping lead plate equipment with equipment for glass electrode materials.
- Research and development in GEM technology is ongoing, with H&V aiming to bring safe and viable new battery technologies like GEM to global marketplaces.
- Rising electrification and lithium-ion batteries throughout mobility, automotive, and energy storage industries are increasing demand for thermal barrier technologies to protect users from battery failure and thermal runaway.
- Thermal barriers prevent thermal propagation and battery fires, giving passengers enough time to safely evacuate in automotive applications.
- H&V is developing a physical thermal barrier material that goes between battery cells and between the battery pack and the vehicle cabin.
- H&V leverages glass fiber and non-woven fiber production capabilities to deliver thermal management solutions for consumers and original equipment suppliers throughout the value chain.
- Working with global partners, H&V is aiming for a soft launch of its thermal barrier in 2025.
- H&V manufactures alkaline-stable, high-efficiency separators using comprehensive production and additive incorporation capabilities to support low greenhouse gas (GHG) electrolysis methods for hydrogen production.
- H&V produces highly CO2-absorbing non-woven materials that improve run time efficiency and durability for various carbon capture systems.
- H&V's current battery separator offerings surpass competitor products on the market due to unique engineering expertise and manufacturing capabilities.
- H&V has decades of proven experience supplying innovative materials and solutions that shape the future of lithium-ion batteries, lead-acid batteries, and other emerging battery technologies.
