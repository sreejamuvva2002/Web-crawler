# Panasonic Automotive Systems Company of America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2019-05-02

**Source:** [Panasonic Automotive Streamlines Vehicle Bluetooth Pairing with Patent Pending Friction Free Connectivity Solution | Panasonic North America - United States](https://na.panasonic.com/news/panasonic-automotive-streamlines-vehicle-bluetooth-pairing-with-patent-pending-friction-free-connectivity-solution)  ·  `page_047784`

## Facts

- Panasonic Automotive presented its new Friction Free Connectivity solution at the 2019 CES on the Hill event in Washington D.C.
- The company is a leading global supplier of automotive infotainment and connectivity systems solutions.
- According to the 2018 JD Powers Initial Quality Study, Bluetooth Connectivity problems were ranked as the second most recurring issue in new vehicles.
- Panasonic Automotive has found that current connection issues include technical complexity during initial pairing, maintaining connection and overall security concerns.
- Panasonic Automotive’s Friction Free Connectivity is a Bluetooth pairing via Wi-Fi solution that requires a smartphone app for a one-time secure setup.
- New vehicle owners register within the smartphone app via a streamlined log-in process and establish rights to their vehicle using a Vehicle Identification Number (VIN).
- This setup is expected to dramatically improve hands-free adoption rates seen today.
- The solution can be integrated into existing vehicle manufacturer applications, as well as ride-sharing and rental car apps for easy automatic Bluetooth pairing while traveling.
- The Friction Free Connectivity pairing solution offers enhanced security with its ability to cryptographically trust both the user and the vehicle, making it very difficult for “drive-by-pairing” or accidentally connecting to the wrong vehicle.
- Sixteen U.S. states have passed laws requiring the use of hands-free technology while driving, and seven others impose some level of restriction.
- According to the 2019 Zendrive “Distracted Driving Snapshot” study, 60% of motorists use their mobile phone while driving.
- “Bluetooth is nearly standard today with 86% of new vehicles coming equipped with the technology. However, the complexity of the pairing platform is a barrier to adoption, which perpetuates distracted drivers to the roadways” said Jeffrey Zellen, Senior Engineering Manager, Panasonic Automotive.
- Jeffrey Zellen serves as Senior Engineering Manager at Panasonic Automotive.
- Panasonic Automotive Systems Company of America is headquartered in Peachtree City, Georgia.
- The company maintains sales, marketing, and engineering operations in Farmington Hills, Michigan.
- Panasonic Automotive Systems Company of America acts as the North American affiliate of Panasonic Corp.’s Automotive Company, which coordinates global automotive and industrial systems and components operations.
- Panasonic Corporation of North America is headquartered in Newark, New Jersey, and is the principal North American subsidiary of Osaka, Japan-based Panasonic Corporation.
