# Volvo Cars

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-05-26

**Source:** [Volvo Cars wins US approval to keep importing vehicles with ‘connected car’ technology | The Mighty 790 KFGO | KFGO](https://kfgo.com/2026/05/26/volvo-cars-wins-us-approval-to-keep-importing-vehicles-with-connected-car-technology/)  ·  `page_014084`

## Facts

- Volvo Cars, majority owned by China’s Geely Holding, received approval from the U.S. government allowing it to continue selling vehicles.
- In January 2025, President Joe Biden’s administration finalized rules effectively barring nearly all Chinese cars and trucks from the U.S. market as part of a crackdown on vehicle software and hardware from China.
- The rules included a ban on most Chinese-developed and maintained software that took effect in March 2026 for the 2027 model year and covered companies with significant Chinese ownership.
- Lawmakers have proposed making the rules even tougher.
- Volvo Car USA was required to follow a process with the U.S. Department of Commerce to obtain a specific authorization for the continued import and sale of connected cars in the U.S.
- With this specific authorization, Volvo Cars can continue its growth plans in the United States.
- Volvo Cars sold 121,600 vehicles in the United States in 2025, down 2.9% from 2024.
- In September, Volvo Cars said it would begin producing a new hybrid model in the U.S. by the end of the decade.
- The new model will be designed for the U.S. market and help Volvo boost capacity utilization at its South Carolina plant.
- In April 2025, Volvo Cars CEO Hakan Samuelsson said the company would produce more vehicles in the U.S.
- The group announced in July that it plans to start producing its popular XC60 mid-size SUV in South Carolina in late 2026.
- Volvo, long positioned as an EV trailblazer with the aim of phasing out all non-electric models by 2030, last year reversed course and said hybrids would remain part of its lineup.
- Volvo Cars currently imports all of its cars into the U.S. from Europe except its electric SUV EX90, which it assembles in South Carolina.
- The Swedish automaker used to also import cars from China but halted those after tariffs on Chinese-made cars were put into effect.
- Volvo said the Commerce Department gave the company a specific authorization following constructive discussions with government agencies regarding Volvo Cars’ governance, technology and data security.
- Sweden’s Polestar, which is also majority-owned by Geely, separately said it continues to work with U.S. authorities to meet the requirements of the announced regulations.
