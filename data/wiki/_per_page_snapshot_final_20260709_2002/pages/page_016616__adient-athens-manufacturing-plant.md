# Adient Athens Manufacturing Plant

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-04-22

**Source:** [Athens Auto Plant Set To Shutter As 210 Jobs Hang In The Balance](https://hoodline.com/2026/04/athens-auto-plant-set-to-shutter-as-210-jobs-hang-in-the-balance/)  ·  `page_016616`

## Facts

- Adient will shut down its Athens manufacturing plant at the end of June.
- Local officials say the closure will affect roughly 210 employees.
- Adient filed a Worker Adjustment and Retraining Notification (WARN) notice with the Tennessee Department of Labor regarding the shutdown.
- The WARN notice states that the Athens plant will close on June 30.
- About 210 jobs will be cut as a result of the closure.
- The filing is part of a series of WARN notices Adient has made in the region.
- The filing contributes to statewide layoff totals logged so far this year.
- Local officials said the WARN notice activated state and local rapid-response procedures designed to get in front of employees early and connect them with resources before the final shutdown date.
- The McMinn County Economic Development Authority is coordinating with the local workforce development board and state partners to organize reemployment services and training.
- A rapid response team will be on site to provide immediate help to workers navigating the transition.
- The EDA will ensure affected employees are informed about employment services, training options, and reemployment assistance.
- The EDA is also helping Adient manage the transition.
- City of Athens leaders plan to work alongside the EDA throughout the transition so affected families can tap into immediate support.
- Tennessee’s Rapid Response system and the American Job Center network provide reemployment support, job-search workshops, and training opportunities for workers who lose their jobs.
- The Tennessee Department of Labor and Workforce Development’s WARN technical assistance guide outlines how rapid-response teams can hold on-site briefings, assist with unemployment insurance, and connect eligible employees with training or trade-adjustment programs.
- The guide directs workers to local career centers as a key entry point.
- Affected Adient employees can apply for unemployment benefits through the state’s career services network.
- Employees can look into apprenticeship or retraining paths through the state’s career services network.
- Adient is listed among McMinn County’s major employers in the county’s business directory, Make It In McMinn.
- Officials stated that their top priority is getting support to the workers while the EDA and the city coordinate next steps.
- Employees are being encouraged to monitor official county updates and stay in touch with state career centers for the latest information on services and opportunities.
