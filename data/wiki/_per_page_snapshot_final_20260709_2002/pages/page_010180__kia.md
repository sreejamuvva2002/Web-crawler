# Kia

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-02

**Source:** [Kia Investing More Than $200 Million In Georgia Facility, Creating 200 New Jobs | Doc and the Block](https://docandtheblock.com/kia-investing-more-than-200-million-in-georgia-facility-creating-200-new-jobs/)  ·  `page_010180`

## Facts

- Republican Georgia Governor Brian Kemp announced that Kia will invest over $200 million at its West Point facility.
- The investment will create almost 200 new jobs at the West Point facility.
- The investment is intended to accommodate assembly of Kia's new all-electric 2024 EV9 three-row SUV.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Georgia's longstanding partnership with Kia has led to generational job creation and growth for the West Point area.
- Governor Brian Kemp noted that the project will help propel Georgia toward its goal of becoming the "e-mobility capital."
- The EV9 will become the fifth model assembled at Kia Georgia through a production line transformation.
- The EV9 joins the Telluride, Sorento and Sportage SUVs, and the K5 mid-size sedan in Kia Georgia's assembly lineup.
- EV9 assembly at the facility is expected to begin in the second quarter of 2024.
- Currently, more than 40 percent of Kia vehicles sold in the U.S. are manufactured at the West Point facility.
- Adding the EV9 to the lineup will further increase the West Point facility's output.
- The EV9 is Kia's first three-row EV SUV and the first Kia EV to be assembled in North America.
- The EV9 serves as the flagship vehicle for Kia's global Plan S strategy focused on sustainable mobility leadership.
- Sean Yoon, President & CEO of Kia Motors North America and Kia Motors America, stated that the EV9 has the potential to be another change catalyst for Kia, similar to the Telluride.
- Sean Yoon described the EV9 as the most innovative vehicle Kia has ever built and a standout in the EV market.
- The EV9 will be assembled in West Point, Georgia.
- So far, Kia has invested more than $1.9 billion in the state of Georgia.
- Kia Georgia is responsible for more than 14,000 plant and supplier jobs in the region.
- Governor Kemp and state lawmakers recognized January 31 as "Kia Day" earlier this year to commend Kia's vehicle manufacturing and impact.
- Kia's 2006 announcement to establish a state-of-the-art vehicle assembly facility in West Point was one of the biggest single foreign investments in Georgia's history.
