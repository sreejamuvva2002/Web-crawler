# GEDIA Automotive Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2020-07-27

**Source:** [German auto parts maker building plant in Georgia | News | rockdalenewtoncitizen.com](https://www.rockdalenewtoncitizen.com/news/german-auto-parts-maker-building-plant-in-georgia/article_ca00613d-5128-5c8d-a22a-fa9d94d214aa.html)  ·  `page_020585`

## Facts

- GEDIA Automotive Group is bringing its first manufacturing plant in the Southeast to Northwest Georgia.
- Gov. Brian Kemp announced the project late last week.
- GEDIA Automotive Group will invest $85 million in a state-of-the-art plant in Whitfield County.
- The facility will create 200 jobs in the greater Dalton area.
- The facility will produce parts for electric vehicles.
- Gov. Brian Kemp stated that Georgia is a national leader in manufacturing, logistics and workforce training.
- Gov. Brian Kemp expressed confidence that the family-owned business would be pleased with its decision to join the growing electric vehicle ecosystem in Georgia.
- Gov. Brian Kemp noted that the state’s consistent investment in its Germany-based European office is paying off.
- GEDIA supplies lightweight automotive parts to Volkswagen, BMW, Mercedes-Benz, Volvo and other automakers around the world.
- The Georgia plant will be GEDIA Automotive Group’s second manufacturing facility in the United States.
- GEDIA Automotive Group already operates a plant in Michigan.
- State Commissioner of Economic Development Pat Wilson was traveling in Germany to meet with government officials and trade partners when the announcement was made.
- GEDIA’s German headquarters is located in the Bavaria region.
- Markus Schaumburg, one of two GEDIA Automotive Group CEOs, called the project an essential step into the future for the company.
- Markus Schaumburg stated that moving closer to original equipment manufacturers allows the company to serve the American market even better.
- The new plant is expected to begin operations during the third quarter of next year.
