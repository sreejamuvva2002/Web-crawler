# Georgia Power

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2025-07-31

**Source:** [Georgia Power Projects Coming to Bartow & Floyd Counties – What You Need to Know](https://georgiamediagroup.org/2025/07/31/bartow-and-floyd-counties-are-in-the-power-companys-bullseye/)  ·  `page_000642`

## Facts

- Georgia Power unveiled plans to invest significantly in new generation capacity and battery energy storage systems (BESS) across the state, particularly in Floyd and Bartow counties.
- Georgia Power is constructing a standalone Battery Energy Storage System (BESS) in Floyd County known as the Hammond BESS.
- The Hammond BESS facility has a capacity of 57.5 MW and is being developed on the site of the retired Plant Hammond, repurposing its transmission capacity for grid support.
- The Hammond BESS project is expected to go into commercial operation by November 2026.
- Georgia Power plans two new battery storage installations in Bartow County called Bowen Phases 1 & 2, totaling 500 MW.
- The Bowen Phases 1 & 2 BESS projects are designed to enhance grid stability during peak usage by storing energy during off-peak hours.
- Georgia Power proposes adding two combined-cycle natural gas units with a combined capacity of 1,482 MW in Bartow County.
- These new natural gas plants are part of a larger initiative to deliver nearly 3,700 MW of natural gas generation statewide for improved reliability.
- In filings with the Georgia Public Service Commission, Georgia Power requested certification for 10 additional BESS facilities across the state totaling approximately 3,022.5 MW.
- The 10 additional BESS facilities are being sited in counties including Bibb, Lowndes, Floyd, Cherokee, and Laurens.
- Alongside the new BESS facilities, Georgia Power is pursuing additional solar-plus-storage proposals and natural gas generation contracts amounting to 2,821 MW.
- The company projects about 8,200 MW of electrical load growth by 2030.
- Peak demand is expected to rise by 2,200 MW versus the company's 2023 forecast.
- Plant Hammond in Floyd County had an 800 MW capacity and shut down in 2019.
- Plant Bowen, located in Bartow County, remains in operation with around 3,376–3,499 MW of coal generation.
- Plant Bowen is recognized as one of the dirtiest plants in the nation.
- Regulators will revisit Plant Bowen's future and potential retirement timeline by 2025.
- Georgia Power has already built and operates 16 solar farms across Georgia, including numerous projects in North Georgia.
- Georgia Power also operates solar facilities at military installations across the state.
- More utility-scale solar capacity is still being sought through bids and contracts to support the utility's clean energy goals.
