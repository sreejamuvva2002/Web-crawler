# Rivian Automotive

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-10-31

**Source:** [Despite legal battle, $5B Rivian electric vehicle plant still in progress for Georgia | Georgia | thecentersquare.com](https://www.thecentersquare.com/georgia/despite-legal-battle-5b-rivian-electric-vehicle-plant-still-in-progress-for-georgia/article_ea91a028-595e-11ed-9500-1b9da1c7fcf2.html)  ·  `page_060572`

## Facts

- Work on electric vehicle manufacturer Rivian Automotive’s $5 billion plant will progress as planned despite an ongoing legal fight, officials confirmed.
- Site grading began in the early fall.
- The judge’s ruling does not affect progress on development.
- Marie Gordon is a spokeswoman for the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD).
- GDEcD and the Joint Development Authority (JDA) of Jasper, Morgan, Newton, and Walton Counties filed an appeal of Ocmulgee Superior Court Judge Brenda Trammell’s decision to deny bond validation for the Rivian project.
- Economic development officials stated the bonds "are not a debt obligation" of the state or the local community.
- Officials would not explicitly guarantee Georgia taxpayers would not be left holding the bag if the deal goes south.
- Earlier this year, GDEcD and JDA announced a deal to give $1.5 billion in incentives to Rivian.
- The company plans to build a plant along Interstate 20 in Morgan and Newton counties.
- On July 8, Georgia officials filed a petition to validate $15 billion in taxable revenue bonds to finance the Rivian project.
- Seven Morgan County residents subsequently filed a motion to intervene.
- On Sept. 29, Trammell denied the bond validation for the project.
- GDEcD and JDA stated they are confident in the strength of their appeal and have every intention of seeing the project through to completion for the benefit of all Georgians.
- GDEcD and JDA stated the agreement is one of the strongest the State has secured in terms of clawbacks and that due diligence was performed, including a review of Rivian's financial records and outlook.
- Economic officials said the debt obligation is solely Rivian’s responsibility.
- Officials declined to elaborate on what might happen if Rivian declares bankruptcy, pointing to a Friday statement.
- GDEcD and JDA stated the judge’s ruling improperly considered Rivian’s finances and disregarded its significant cash reserves, manufacturing performance at its existing facility in Normal, Illinois, and the rising market trend toward electric vehicles.
- A spokesperson for Gov. Brian Kemp did not respond to a request to confirm Georgia taxpayers wouldn’t be on the hook for the project’s cost.
- Officials previously said the plant would begin production in 2024.
