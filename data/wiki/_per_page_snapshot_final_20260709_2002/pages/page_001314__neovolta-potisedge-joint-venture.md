# NeoVolta PotisEdge Joint Venture

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-05-19

**Source:** [Battery Storage Factory Involving Major Chinese Energy Player Planned for Jackson County  - Global Atlanta](https://www.globalatlanta.com/battery-storage-factory-involving-major-chinese-energy-player-planned-for-jackson-county/)  ·  `page_001314`

## Facts

- A joint venture to manufacture battery energy storage systems (BESS) for industrial facilities is set to begin production in Jackson County this summer.
- NeoVolta, a California-based firm previously focused solely on home BESS systems, is branching into the commercial space with a new entity in Pendergrass, Ga.
- The joint venture will build an initial run of 2 GWh of BESS systems at a logistics center just off Interstate 85 in partnership with PotisEdge, a Canadian company recently acquired by Longi Green Energy Technology Co. Ltd.
- Longi Green Energy Technology Co. Ltd. is headquartered in Xi’an, China, has annual revenues exceeding $10 billion, and acquired a majority interest in PotisEdge as part of a global effort to offer an integrated “one-stop” solution from generation to storage.
- The joint venture was announced in a January news release that teased a mid-2026 Georgia production launch and the creation of 89 jobs in the first phase.
- The plant will initially focus on prismatic-cell battery pack assembly and DC container integration, with plans to quickly double production to 4 GWh after the initial ramp-up, according to NeoVolta CEO Ardes Johnson.
- NeoVolta owns 80 percent of the venture, while PotisEdge takes a 20 percent stake, creating a majority U.S.-controlled entity required to qualify for investment tax credits that can offset 30-40 percent of construction costs.
- Energy storage systems are allowed until 2032 before investment tax credits are phased out, whereas electric vehicle tax credits from the Inflation Reduction Act were eliminated soon after the One Big Beautiful Bill Act of 2025 passed.
- Battery storage projects breaking ground in 2026 require 55 percent of overall expenditures on a qualifying facility to go toward domestically sourced content, with the local-content requirement rising each year.
- PotisEdge manufactures many of its components in Suzhou, China, but does not manufacture battery cells, which constitute the majority of any system's cost.
- NeoVolta plans to import Chinese batteries this year, then move sourcing to other international locations, and ultimately shift to on-shore purchases of cells as domestic capacity grows.
- PotisEdge currently sources from Chinese cell suppliers for assembly of energy storage systems, and the Georgia plant will replicate much of that production with more automation, according to reports citing PotisEdge CEO Minjie Shi.
- NeoVolta is traded on the NASDAQ and collaborates with established players like PotisEdge and Longi on the technology side.
- The arrangement allows NeoVolta’s executives to focus on finance and compliance while leveraging the hardware strengths of their Chinese partners, who have given NeoVolta “more support than we have even asked for.”
- Longi has a track record with FEOC compliance through its partnership with Invenergy in Ohio, giving the Georgia venture “bankability from day one,” according to Johnson.
- PotisEdge signed the initial lease in Pendergrass to get the project moving.
- NeoVolta will see a major revenue step change by tackling the utility-scale storage market first, followed by the commercial and industrial sector, which CEO Ardes Johnson calls the “missing middle” of storage.
- NeoVolta’s revenues are projected to grow to $20 million in the short term from just a few million dollars last year, while still focusing on home-energy systems.
- Full production at the Georgia plant is calculated to represent approximately $400 million in potential BESS sales at current market rates.
- The joint venture needs to raise some $20 million beyond the $23 million it has already netted to power the Georgia plant’s initial phase.
- In late 2025, NeoVolta received a $13 million private placement to fund the joint venture, led by a $10 million infusion from Infinite Grid Capital, a private equity fund focused on energy storage with executives experienced in China.
- Jackson County Chamber of Commerce President and CEO Steve Wittry stated that while he was unaware of the project, the nearby SK plant has drawn attention to the area and put Jackson County “on the map” for companies producing materials SK is interested in.
- Early on, the SK investment attracted South Korean suppliers like Enchem and later Duckyang.
- NeoVolta Power is bringing established Chinese technology to Georgia with partners who understand long-term partnership approaches needed to succeed in the U.S. market.
- CEO Ardes Johnson, a former Tesla sales executive, visited PotisEdge’s processes and assembly lines designed for Georgia during trips to China and believes the partnership will successfully marry both sides’ strengths.
