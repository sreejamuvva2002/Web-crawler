# Rivian EV Plant (Georgia)

**Type:** Facility  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[vehicle-manufacturing|Vehicle Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** Georgia, United States

## Overview

A planned Rivian manufacturing facility in Georgia that will produce electric trucks, creating thousands of jobs and receiving significant state tax incentives.

## EV supply-chain relevance

The plant will add 7,500 EV manufacturing jobs and increase electric vehicle production capacity in Georgia.

## Key facts

- Georgia is emerging as a leader in electric vehicle manufacturing.
- Rivian will build a massive factory that will produce electric trucks and create an estimated 7,500 jobs.
- The Rivian plant construction cost is $5 billion, with the state offering $1.5 billion in tax incentives.
- Hyundai announced a $5 billion investment to open a new EV factory in Savannah, adding 8,000 jobs.
- Predicted savings of $1.3 billion on gasoline per year for Georgians by 2035 due to increased EV adoption.
- The NEVI Formula Program aims to build a national EV charging network; Georgia will receive approximately $134,975,283 over the first five years.
- Georgia’s EV Infrastructure Deployment Plan is required by August 1, 2022 to access federal funds.
