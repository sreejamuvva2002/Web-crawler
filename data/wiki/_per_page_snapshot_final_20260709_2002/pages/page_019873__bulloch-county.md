# Bulloch County

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2024-06-07

**Source:** [Manufacturing impact in Bulloch exceeds $1 billion - Statesboro Herald](https://www.statesboroherald.com/local/manufacturing-impact-bulloch-exceeds-1-billion/)  ·  `page_019873`

## Facts

- Annual economic impact of manufacturing in Bulloch County surpassed $1.17 billion in 2023 according to a Georgia Southern University study.
- The Georgia Southern University study was commissioned by the Development Authority of Bulloch County.
- Manufacturing economic impact in Bulloch County increased by 32 percent from a similar analysis conducted in 2020 prior to the COVID pandemic.
- Manufacturing currently supports more than 4,000 jobs in Bulloch County with an average annual salary of $56,339.
- Benjy Thompson, CEO of the Development Authority of Bulloch County, stated that the county’s manufacturing sector provides significant value to citizens and that manufacturers invest a great amount of capital in facilities while providing good-paying and well-benefitted jobs.
- The Development Authority of Bulloch County partners with Bulloch County, the City of Statesboro, Bulloch County Schools, and education/workforce partners for manufacturer recruitment and support.
- Local manufacturing employers included in the analysis are Briggs & Stratton, Great Dane Trailers, Cardinal Glass, and other small and large industrial businesses.
- Benjy Thompson noted that additional industry partners coming online in the next few years will grow manufacturing's impact on the county, providing more jobs and economic growth.
- Hanon Systems is building a factory in Gateway Regional Industrial Park.
- Hanon Systems is one of three tier-one suppliers to Hyundai Motor Group’s Metaplant, which is under construction in Bryan County, that are building facilities in Bulloch County.
- Two other non-Hyundai related businesses are also building plants in Bulloch County.
- New plants in Bulloch County have job creation commitments totaling a projected 1,600 employees.
- Promised capital investments for the new Bulloch County plants total $1 billion.
- Aspen Aerogels announced its project on Feb. 17, 2022, with construction nearly complete underway on a 90-acre site in Bruce Yawn Commerce Park.
- Aspen Aerogels committed to a $325 million investment and at least 250 manufacturing jobs for its Bulloch County plant.
- Aspen Aerogels’ plant will produce aerogel insulation used in and around electric vehicle batteries, though it is not linked to the Hyundai factory.
- Ecoplastic America Corporation, a supplier of injection-molded plastic automotive body parts to Hyundai Motor Group, intends to build a $205 million plant on a site owned by the DABC south of Statesboro.
- Ecoplastic America promises to create 456 jobs phased in over eight years, with site preparation currently underway.
- Ajin USA, a supplier of metal auto body parts to Hyundai, committed to invest $317 million and eventually employ 630 people at its Joon Georgia plant.
- Ajin USA’s 83-acre site is located beside the Aspen Aerogels site in Bruce Yawn Commerce Park.
- revalyu Resources, a Germany-headquartered company not connected to Hyundai, plans to build a PET plastic-bottle recycling plant on a 43-acre site in Gateway Regional Industrial Park.
- revalyu Resources is expected to create 71 jobs and require a $50 million investment in Phase 1, which is the only phase counted in current totals.
- Hanon Systems, Ecoplastic America Corporation, and Ajin USA are all tier-one suppliers because they deal directly with Hyundai Motor Group.
- A tier-two supplier provides materials to a tier-one supplier, and subsequent tiers supply further down the chain.
- Manufacturers in Bulloch County provide significant revenue to both Bulloch County and Bulloch County schools.
- Gateway Regional Industrial Park, which includes several manufacturing facilities and a Walmart Distribution Center, paid more than $3.5 million in property taxes in 2023.
