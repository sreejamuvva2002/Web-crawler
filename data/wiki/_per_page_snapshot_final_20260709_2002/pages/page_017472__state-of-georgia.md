# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2022-07-20

**Source:** [Georgia Emerges as Hub for Sustainability - The ATL Airport Chamber](https://airportchamber.com/georgia-emerges-as-hub-for-sustainability/)  ·  `page_017472`

## Facts

- Companies looking to lower emissions and reduce carbon footprint have invested over $11.5 billion in Georgia since 2018.
- Ascend Elements, formerly known as Battery Resourcers, is investing $43 million to open a commercial-scale lithium-ion battery recycling plant in Georgia.
- The Ascend Elements facility will extract critically scarce materials like lithium, cobalt, and nickel from batteries.
- The Ascend Elements plant will become the largest of its kind in North America when fully operational later this year (2022).
- Ascend Elements selected Georgia primarily because of the growing electric vehicle and battery ecosystem in the state, according to Roger Lin, Vice President of Marketing and Government Relations.
- Proximity to partners and customers is essential in the sustainable battery materials industry, notes Roger Lin.
- The Southeast is a mecca for the electric vehicle and battery industry, and Georgia offers proximity to factories like SK Battery America, per Roger Lin.
- Proximity minimizes transportation costs, provides access to top talent from nearby universities, and offers access to renewable energy, notes Roger Lin.
- Korea’s SK Battery America is investing nearly $2.6 billion in two facilities near Atlanta to supply batteries to Ford, Volkswagen, and other EV makers.
- Since SK Battery America's first announcement in 2018, more businesses focused on EV sustainability have invested in Georgia.
- Plug Power recently announced a new $84 million, zero-emission hydrogen fuel production facility near I-95 and Georgia’s deepwater ports.
- Plug Power's fuel cell systems for e-mobility deliver efficiency, fast fueling, and low cost to customers.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp launched the Electric Mobility and Innovation Alliance, a statewide initiative between government, industries, electric utilities, nonprofits, and other stakeholders.
- The Electric Mobility and Innovation Alliance focuses on growing the electric mobility ecosystem in the state and strengthening Georgia’s position in electrification-related manufacturing and innovation.
- In 2021, EV manufacturer Rivian announced an investment of $5 billion to build a carbon-conscious facility in Georgia.
- Rivian's facility will make vertically connected electric platforms for trucks, sport/utility vehicles, and delivery vans.
- Hyundai Motor Group will open its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility in Georgia with a $5.54 billion investment.
- Hyundai's Georgia facility operations are slated to begin in the first half of 2025, with an annual capacity of 300,000 units.
- Many other Georgia companies are already making parts for EVs.
- Companies from Korea, Germany, Turkey, and the Netherlands have announced plans to build EV-related facilities in Georgia.
- Site Selection Magazine recently ranked Georgia No. 3 for sustainability in the South Atlantic region.
- The Atlanta metro ranked in the Top 10 nationally for sustainability.
- Since 2009, Georgia has increased net electricity generation from renewable sources by more than 93%.
- Georgia’s electricity mix currently consists of nearly 12% renewables.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that renewable energy capacity installation in Georgia has been driven organically by companies and consumers, accelerated through economic attractiveness rather than mandate.
- Smart policies have allowed Georgia to promote renewable power development and offer a comprehensive portfolio of clean energy options for competitive advantage, according to Pat Wilson.
- Solar PV is the state’s fastest-growing source of renewable energy, accelerating its appeal to solar panel companies such as NanoPV Solar and Qcells.
- NanoPV will manufacture solar panels and equipment in south Georgia’s Sumter County.
- Qcells is investing more than $320 million in its solar panel assembly plant in northern Georgia.
- Qcells' facility is the largest solar panel assembly plant in the Western hemisphere.
- Recent sustainability-related announcements include German copper recycler Aurubis.
- Recent announcements include electronic waste recycler Igneo.
- Recent announcements include Danimer Scientific, a manufacturer and developer of biodegradable materials.
- Recent announcements include recycled furniture maker SoPoly.
- In Atlanta, EF Block turns everyday recycled Styrofoam into earth-friendly, stronger, sustainable walls that replace traditional building materials.
- With support from GDEcD’s Center of Innovation and the City of Atlanta, EF Block has launched a city-wide Styrofoam recycling initiative.
- On Georgia’s coast, NANTRenewables is opening a plant to manufacture biodegradable products like plates, straws, cutlery, trays, and cups.
- NANTRenewables provides sustainable products to companies within the food, agriculture, and pharmaceutical industries.
- Harold Meherg, a NANT Renewables senior executive, selected SeaPoint Industrial Terminal Complex in Savannah for the new biogenic fillers and bioplastics manufacturing facility due to shared sustainability values and development-ready infrastructure including roads, rail access, sea access, and utility connections.
- Companies leading mobility research & development, battery manufacturing and recycling, and electric vehicle production all call Georgia home.
- Ascend Elements’ Roger Lin stated that Georgia is in a strategic location with a critical mass of factories locating or operating in the area.
- Lin noted that Georgia is a very friendly, welcoming place to open and grow a business with large facilities like theirs.
