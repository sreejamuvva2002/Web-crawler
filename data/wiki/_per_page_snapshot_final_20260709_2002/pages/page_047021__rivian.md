# Rivian

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-08

**Source:** [Georgia government agencies seek $540K in legal fees from Rivian critics](https://www.ajc.com/news/2025/08/state-seeks-more-than-540k-in-legal-fees-from-rivian-factory-opponents/)  ·  `page_047021`

## Facts

- Rivian has a planned electric vehicle factory located an hour east of Atlanta.
- The Georgia state government and a local development authority are seeking more than $540,000 in legal fees.
- These fees accumulated from two unsuccessful lawsuits filed by six Morgan County property owners who challenged the factory’s zoning.
- State agencies say they are trying to recoup taxpayer-funded litigation costs for what they called frivolous lawsuits intended to delay construction.
- The residents argue their lawsuits raised legitimate legal concerns and that the fee request is merely government intimidation.
- Governments have a right to pursue litigation costs in Georgia when lawsuits are deemed frivolous, a distinction determined by a judge.
- First Amendment advocates say this sum of money is unprecedented in a lawsuit that pits government agencies against citizens.
- Richard T. Griffiths, a former CNN executive and Georgia First Amendment Foundation board member, said the state has never gone after attorneys fees for citizens’ First Amendment protected right to advocate in courts.
- The agencies pursuing the litigation costs are the Georgia Department of Economic Development and the Joint Development Authority of Jasper, Morgan, Newton and Walton counties.
- The JDA denied trying to stifle free speech and said it is pursuing legal fees from only two of seven lawsuits filed against the Rivian project involving the same pool of litigants.
- The JDA board wrote in a Covington News op-ed that weaponizing the legal system to block a perfectly legal project is not legitimate and that the lawsuits were designed to delay progress, costing taxpayers and the community.
- A Morgan County judge previously ruled that the state and JDA must pay their own legal fees, but the state is appealing.
