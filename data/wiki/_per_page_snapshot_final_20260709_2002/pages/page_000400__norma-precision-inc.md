# Norma Precision Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2022-11-10

**Source:** [Norma Precision Inc to Create 600 Jobs, Invest $60 Million in Bryan County - AllOnGeorgia](https://allongeorgia.com/georgia-business/norma-precision-inc-to-create-600-jobs-invest-60-million-in-bryan-county/)  ·  `page_000400`

## Facts

- Beretta Holding S.A., through its subsidiary Norma Precision Inc., will expand its footprint in Georgia and build a new manufacturing, assembly, and distribution facility in Bryan County.
- The $60 million project will create 600 new jobs for which the company is currently hiring.
- Governor Kemp stated that it is a testament to Georgia’s strong workforce and economy that companies continually choose to further invest and expand their operations in the No. 1 state for business.
- Governor Kemp noted that Norma Precision is already hiring hardworking Georgians on the coast and the new facility will support healthy communities across the Savannah area.
- Norma Precision was formerly a U.S. subsidiary of Ammotec Inc. and was recently acquired by Beretta Holding S.A., one of the world’s oldest industrial groups.
- Norma Precision will continue to develop and produce high-end ammunition to serve key markets in hunting, sporting goods, optics, military, and law enforcement sectors.
- Dr. Pietro Gussalli Beretta, President and CEO of Beretta Holding S.A., stated that Norma Precision manufactured ammunition in the United States for 12 years as RUAG Ammotec USA and experienced steady growth over the past four years.
- Dr. Pietro Gussalli Beretta expressed confidence that the long-term partnership with Georgia will be mutually beneficial while focusing on the Norma ammunition brand to better serve customers and industry partners in the United States and abroad.
- Paul Lemke, CEO of Norma Precision, stated that the new facility offers a significant opportunity to expand small arms ammunition manufacturing and distribution operations to meet customer expectations in the United States and abroad.
- Paul Lemke thanked Bryan County, Chatham County, local communities, and the State of Georgia for their support and expressed excitement about hiring, training, and transferring staff to manufacture, sell, and distribute Norma world-class ammunition and trade goods.
- Norma Precision’s new facility will be 300,000 square feet in size.
- The facility will be located at Interstate Centre III in Bryan County, which is a Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) certified site.
- To earn GRAD certification, a property must undergo due diligence and complete specific certification requirements prior to a visit by a prospective business.
- Following completion of the new facility, operations from the Chatham County location will be moved to Bryan County.
- Current employees at the Chatham County facility will have the option to transfer to the new facility.
- The company is currently hiring for multiple positions and experience levels in manufacturing, distribution, HR, marketing, and sales.
- Jon Seagraves, Development Authority of Bryan County Chairman, expressed excitement about Norma Precision locating a new manufacturing facility in Interstate Centre III.
- Jon Seagraves noted that Norma Precision is backed by the nearly 500 years of experience and tradition that comes with Beretta Holding.
- Griff Lynch, Executive Director of the Georgia Ports Authority, welcomed Norma Precision’s growth in the region and highlighted the state’s broad logistics network and ability to reach major domestic and global markets efficiently.
- Fernanda Kirchner, Senior Project Manager, represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) on this project in partnership with the Development Authority of Bryan County, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Power, the State of Georgia Europe Office, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that projects of this size benefit entire regions of the state through hiring and partnering with local suppliers.
- Pat Wilson noted that Bryan and Chatham counties worked together to create an innovative solution to secure Norma Precision’s new jobs for Georgians in the region.
