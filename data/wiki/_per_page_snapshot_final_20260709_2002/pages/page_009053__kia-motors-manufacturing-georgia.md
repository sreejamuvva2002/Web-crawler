# Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [How Much Do Kia Plant Workers Make - GearShifters](https://gearshifters.org/kia/how-much-do-kia-plant-workers-make/)  ·  `page_009053`

## Facts

- Georgia secured Kia's first US auto manufacturing facility via diligence, prompt action, and close collaboration.
- The KMMG project announcement came six months after Kia requested a special meeting with representatives of the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD).
- Georgia was chosen for its skilled workforce, world-class transportation infrastructure, top-ranked state-sponsored employee training program, pro-growth corporate environment, and dedication to fostering innovation.
- The facility has reached capacity thanks to these resources.
- More than 3 million automobiles have been produced by KMMG since operations started in 2009.
- KMMG produces the Sorento, K5 (formerly Optima), and Telluride at its 2,200-acre, $1.1 billion plant in Troup County, Georgia.
- The plant employs 2,700 workers.
- More than 14,000 jobs have been created in the West Georgia region by Kia and its suppliers.
- More than 40% of all Kia automobiles sold in the US are now produced by KMMG.
- 940 dealerships in the US and Canada receive more than 90% of vehicles built in West Point.
- Vehicles are shipped to Mexico, countries in the Caribbean, and the Pacific.
- GDEcD's Trade Division assisted Kia in shipping Tellurides to the Middle East via the Brunswick port with the Georgia Ports Authority.
- Jangsoo (Jason) Shin serves as President and CEO of KMMG.
- Shin stated that "the total support of the state agencies has been nothing short of spectacular."
- Kia has received assistance from over a dozen local organizations, including Labor, Transportation, and Community Affairs Departments, Georgia Ports Authority, Office of the Attorney General, and the state's technical college system.
- KMMG is a founding partner of Georgia MadeTM, a GDEcD program offering logistics, production, and marketing assistance for goods made in Georgia.
- Governor Brian Kemp visited the facility to support Georgia MadeTM's launch.
- Georgia has been rated the best state for business for seven years straight.
- Leaders commend TCSG's Quick Start program, which built a 70,000 square foot Kia Georgia Training Center and collaborates with Kia on its operation.
- Quick Start uses cutting-edge methods to offer specialized, advanced industrial training to KMMG employees.
- Shin states that Georgia Quick Start serves as a model for workforce development initiatives.
- The state's first online application process handled more than 125,000 applications for Kia positions.
- Georgia Quick Start created an industrial maintenance certification program taught at West Georgia Technical College to help KMMG overcome a labor shortage.
- Kia's workforce development activities led to the creation of hundreds of co-op programs for college and technical school students.
- Along with Troup County's THINC College and Vocational Academy, KMMG sponsors STEM initiatives in the neighborhood school system.
- KMMG's corporate social responsibility program addresses diversity, wellness, environment, and education.
- "The Ray" is a stretch of I-85 in West Georgia serving as a testing ground for new transportation technology.
- At the state-run West Point Visitor Information Center, KMMG provides a free solar panel charging station and a tire inspection station.
- KMMG modified operations to create face shields during the COVID-19 crisis, producing about 550,000 units with assistance from GDEcD and Georgia Tech Manufacturing Institute.
- KMMG gave thousands of life-saving equipment items (face shields, masks, gloves, surgical caps, alcohol pads, BioHazard bags) to the Georgia Emergency Management and Homeland Security Agency.
- Six Kia vehicles were made available by the company to transport medical supplies across the state.
- Pat Wilson is Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner.
- Wilson stated that Kia inquired how they could help during the pandemic, stepped up to offer life-saving equipment, and expressed appreciation for Kia's support as a member of the Georgia MadeTM family.
- Shin cites Wilson's emphasis on interpersonal connections inside Georgia, noting success begins with confidence developed from them and that there is a wonderful network of accessibility between state and local economic groupings.
- KMMG is Kia Motors Corporation's first manufacturing facility in North America, located in West Point, Georgia, on 2,200 acres.
- KMMG started mass production on November 16, 2009, with an annual capacity of 340,000 units.
- The Kia Motors assembly factory in West Point was forced to halt operations due to a semiconductor chip shortage caused by COVID-related work stoppages in Asia, costing the plant two weeks of production that year.
