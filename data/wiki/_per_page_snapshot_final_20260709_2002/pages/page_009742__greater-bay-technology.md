# Greater Bay Technology

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-06-07

**Source:** [New 621-mile range EV battery charges in six minutes and works in any weather](https://interestingengineering.com/innovation/chinese-startup-new-ev-battery-any-weather)  ·  `page_009742`

## Facts

- Chinese startup Greater Bay Technology has claimed that its new electric vehicle (EV) battery can work in any weather.
- The Phoenix cell uses superconducting materials and thermal management to bring freezing temperatures to normal room temperature in just five minutes.
- Extreme temperatures have been the bane for batteries of electric vehicles since they were introduced, reducing charging efficiency when temperatures dip below 32 degrees Fahrenheit (zero degrees Celsius).
- Carmakers have turned to installing heat pumps to keep battery temperatures in a high-efficiency range, but that is considered an inefficient way to manage the battery.
- Greater Bay Technology was founded by Huang Xiangdong and industry veteran Pei Feng in 2020.
- Huang and Pei were colleagues at GAC, the third most popular EV brand in China after BYD and Tesla.
- Huang studied automotive engineering in Italy in the 1980s and worked as a postdoctoral researcher at Fiat’s research center for five years before returning to China in 1991.
- From 2006, Huang led the R&D Center at GAC, where he oversaw the development of the GS4 SUV.
- Huang retired in 2016 but returned in 2020 to bring in battery technology that can make EVs as easy to own and operate as gasoline-powered cars.
- Their first-generation battery can recharge in about 15 minutes and can deliver a range of 310 miles (500 km).
- The first-generation battery technology is already being implemented in GAC’s Aion, an electric SUV.
- The Phoenix cell can deliver a range of 621 miles (1,000 km) on a single charge.
- The use of superconducting materials and thermal management technology ensures the battery can be heated from temperatures as low as -4 Fahrenheit (-20 degrees Celsius) to 77 Fahrenheit (25 degrees Celsius) in just five minutes.
- Once within its normal operating temperature range, the Phoenix cell can be rapidly charged and reach full capacity in just six minutes.
- Huang stated that the Phoenix battery addresses long charging times for EVs and other pain points, ensuring range is unaffected by hot or cold weather.
- Huang is confident that his technology will increase the mass adoption of EVs.
- The Phoenix cell could be seen in action as early as next year on GAC’s Aion EVs as well as EVs of other carmakers.
- In just two years, Greater Bay Technology has become a unicorn company valued at $1 billion.
- Greater Bay Technology is now mentioned alongside battery industry leaders Contemporary Amperex Technology Limited (CATL) and BYD in China.
