# HL-GA Battery Co.

**Type:** Company  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[battery-cell-manufacturing|Battery Cell Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** Bryan County MegaSite, Ellabell, Bryan County, Georgia, United States

## Overview

HL-GA Battery Co. is constructing an electric vehicle battery plant at the Bryan County MegaSite in Ellabell, Georgia, scheduled to begin production of EV batteries for Hyundai, Kia and Genesis vehicles in early 2026.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Battery manufacturer producing EV batteries for major automakers; part of Georgia's emerging EV supply chain.

## Key facts

- The plant is located about 25 miles from downtown Savannah.
- The accident occurred when a load fell off a forklift while being unloaded.
- HL-GA Battery Co. issued a statement expressing sadness and emphasizing safety as its highest priority.
- OSHA and the Department of Labor are investigating the May 20 fatality and a prior March 21 incident.
- Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution have pledged over $4.3 billion to fund the plant.
- The facility will produce EV batteries for Hyundai, Kia and Genesis vehicles beginning in early 2026.
- The battery plant is part of a $7.6 billion complex planned to eventually employ 8,500 people.
- More than 300 South Korean workers were detained during the immigration raid at the construction site.
- Hyundai announced it is investing $26 billion in the United States from 2025 to 2028, including the Ellabell battery plant.
- Governor Kemp stated Georgia will always enforce state and federal immigration laws while supporting companies operating in the state.
- The Department of Economic Development expects anyone doing business in Georgia to follow federal and state laws.
- The raid raised questions about South Korea-U.S. relations regarding Hyundai’s investments.
- $2.1 billion in projected state and local incentives for the Hyundai complex do not directly benefit subcontractors employing detainees.
