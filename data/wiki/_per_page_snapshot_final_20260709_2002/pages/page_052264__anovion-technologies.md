## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Anovion Technologies will build a new manufacturing facility in Bainbridge.
- Anovion is a supplier of premium synthetic graphite anode materials for lithium-ion batteries.
- The project is expected to create more than 400 jobs and over $800 million in investment in Decatur County.
- Governor Kemp stated Georgia is fast becoming the e-mobility capital of the nation.
- Governor Kemp noted that job creators are recognizing every corner of the state has the resources and workforce needed for success.
- Eric Stopka, Chief Executive Officer of Anovion, said the company is opening its first large-scale expansion manufacturing facility in Georgia.
- Stopka stated Georgia's strong technical workforce and top-tier schools will help advance research partnerships and contribute to a highly skilled talent pipeline for Anovion's innovation and climate technology jobs.
- Stopka noted the region's growing availability of clean energy will result in increased sustainability for Anovion's manufacturing process.
- The facility will be 1,500,000-square-foot with proprietary furnace technologies located at Downrange Industrial Park in Bainbridge.
- Operations are expected to begin late 2025.
- The facility will produce synthetic graphite for use in batteries that power EVs, electric storage systems, electronics, military applications, and other products.
- Graphite is the largest battery material used in electric vehicles by mass, more than copper, nickel, manganese, cobalt, and lithium.
- Anovion plans to hire for technical, production, engineering roles as well as administrative and management positions.
- Mayor Edward Reynolds said Anovion's decision to locate in Bainbridge is a huge win due to the $800 million investment and creation of over 400 high-paying quality jobs.
- Chairman Pete Stephens from Decatur County Board Of Commissioners added that the new green industry jobs will provide quality employment opportunities for decades to come.
- Project Manager Taylor Walden represented GDEcD's Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The project was supported by City Of Bainbridge, Development Authority Of Bainbridge And Decatur County, University System Of Georgia, Georgia EMC, And Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated much of the critical supply chain for e-mobility and manufacturing is currently sourced outside of the U.S., and Anovion fills that need by delivering another critical component to Georgia.
- Georgia has announced 40 EV related projects with more than $21 billion investment since 2020, making it a hub for the electric mobility industry.
- The new project represents another step for Decatur County towards diversifying its industry mix while providing quality employment opportunities.
