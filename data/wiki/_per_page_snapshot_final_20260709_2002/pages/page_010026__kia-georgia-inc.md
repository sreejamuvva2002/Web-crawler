# Kia Georgia, Inc.

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-02-24

**Source:** [Kia Georgia celebrates trio of major milestones, including Start of Production for 2027 Kia Telluride - Kia Georgia](https://www.kiageorgia.com/kia-georgia-celebrates-trio-of-major-milestones-including-start-of-production-for-2027-kia-telluride/)  ·  `page_010026`

## Facts

- On Feb. 24, 2026, Kia Georgia team members marked another historic day at its West Point facility with the start of production of the all-new 2027 Kia Telluride.
- The ceremonial vehicle represented two other major milestones: the first hybrid electric vehicle (HEV) assembled in the state of Georgia and the 5 millionth vehicle produced at Kia Georgia.
- A ceremony for the milestone was attended by Georgia Governor Brian P. Kemp, team members and state/community leaders, with the single Telluride symbolizing Kia’s continued growth, innovation and investment in advanced manufacturing in Georgia.
- Sean Yoon, President and CEO of Kia North America and Kia America, stated that as the only plant in the world that builds Telluride, the launch reflects the strength of the West Point team, customer trust, and Kia’s commitment to building the future of mobility in Georgia.
- Yoon added that reaching five million vehicles and introducing, not only the first HEV assembled in Georgia, but Kia’s first US-assembled HEV, is a testament to both Kia’s storied history and direction going forward.
- Since beginning production in 2009, Kia Georgia has grown into one of the region’s largest employers and a cornerstone of automotive manufacturing in the Southeast.
- The Telluride is one of the industry’s most awarded vehicles, has become a flagship model for the brand, and serves as a symbol of quality built in West Point.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp stated that since its introduction in 2019, the Telluride has been exclusively Georgia-made and has become one of the most successful vehicles in Kia’s lineup.
- Governor Kemp attributed this success to a proven partnership approach creating long-lasting relationships between Kia Georgia, the local community, and the entire state.
- Stuart Countess, Kia Georgia’s President and CEO, emphasized that the day’s milestones were made possible by the dedication of the team members at Kia Georgia.
- Countess stated that five million vehicles do not happen by chance but are the result of famous teamwork, versatility, and talent in producing world-class gasoline-powered vehicles, EVs and now HEVs.
- Kia Georgia, Inc. is the first manufacturing site in North America for Kia Corporation based in Seoul, Korea.
- The facility has an annual capacity of 350,000 units and is located on 2,200 acres in West Point, Ga.
- Kia Georgia began mass production on Nov. 16, 2009.
- The plant produces the Telluride, Sorento (excludes HEV and PHEV models), Sportage (excludes HEV and PHEV models) SUVs, and the all-electric EV9 and EV6.
