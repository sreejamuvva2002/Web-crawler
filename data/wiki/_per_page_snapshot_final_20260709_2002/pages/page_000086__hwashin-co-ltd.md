# Hwashin Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-10-31

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp: Auto Parts Supplier Hwashin to Bring 460 Jobs to Middle Georgia | Governor Brian P. Kemp Office of the Governor](https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases/2023-10-31/gov-kemp-auto-parts-supplier-hwashin-bring-460-jobs-middle-georgia)  ·  `page_000086`

## Facts

- On October 31, 2023, Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that auto parts company Hwashin will establish a new manufacturing facility in Dublin.
- The project will create over 460 new jobs and more than $176 million in investment in Laurens County.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated the goal is to become the "e-mobility capital of the nation" and noted Hwashin joins a growing ecosystem of companies bringing good jobs to Georgians.
- Hwashin operates as a Tier-1 supplier specializing in crafting chassis and body parts for Hyundai Motor Group, Kia, and international original equipment manufacturers (OEMs).
- The company is headquartered in Korea and has established production facilities spanning the United States, Asia, and South America.
- Seo Jin Jung, CEO of Hwashin, stated the company aspires to shape a future global city focused on electric vehicles (EVs) drawing from its 50-year expertise.
- Jung noted Hwashin's commitment to being a reliable partner to customers and an engaged contributor to the local community, expressing gratitude to the State of Georgia and Laurens County for their cooperation.
- The new Georgia facility is scheduled to begin production in late 2025.
- The facility will be located at the Georgia Highway 257 Rail Served Site, which is the first Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) Select site in the state.
- A "GRAD Certified Select" status indicates that a site has met or exceeded more rigorous certification requirements.
- The facility will primarily focus on manufacturing chassis components for OEMs, including Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America and Kia Georgia.
- Dublin Mayor Joshua Kight stated the city is pleased to welcome Hwashin and praised the Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority for demonstrating why the area is a great place to do business.
- Mayor Kight noted the manufacturing facility will become an economic engine for the region, leading to more employment opportunities and quality growth.
- Jeff Davis, Chairman of the Laurens County Board of Commissioners, stated Hwashin is the seventh international company to call Dublin and Laurens County home.
- Davis highlighted the partnership between Laurens County, the City of Dublin, and the Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority, noting the project will provide new career opportunities and develop increased opportunities in housing and retail development.
- Georgia Ports Authority President and CEO Griff Lynch stated significant investments from Hwashin and other automotive suppliers are delivering career opportunities for Georgians.
- Lynch noted the Georgia Ports Authority welcomes Hwashin as the newest member of its manufacturing community to efficiently connect companies to international markets.
- Alyce Thornhill, Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy, represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on the project.
- The project was developed in partnership with the Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia EMC, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated Hwashin will have direct interstate access to at least two long-term automotive partners from its chosen site.
- Wilson noted locating on a GRAD Select site allows the company to pursue an accelerated construction timeline.
- Wilson emphasized that decades of elected leadership have supported infrastructure and workforce development, areas where Georgia excels against competition.
- Over a century, Georgia has fostered healthy industry practices, collaboration, innovation, and positioned itself as a leader in the automotive and mobility industry.
- In fiscal year 2023, job creation in the automotive industry increased by 324 percent compared to FY21.
- Suppliers attracted by Georgia’s mobility OEMs resulted in over $2 billion in investment across the state.
- As the electric vehicle market grows, Georgia has pursued the entire supply chain, creating more than $25.1 billion in investments and 29,000 jobs since 2020.
- Since its inception in 1975, Hwashin Co., Ltd. has held a prominent position in the Korean auto parts industry for nearly five decades.
- The company maintains a specialized focus on chassis and body parts while actively engaging in research and development.
- Hwashin has expanded its product offerings to encompass lightweight aluminum components and eco-friendly solutions aligned with global automotive industry trends.
- Hwashin Co., Ltd. employs more than 4,200 personnel worldwide.
