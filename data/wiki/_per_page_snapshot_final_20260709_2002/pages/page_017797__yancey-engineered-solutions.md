# Yancey Engineered Solutions

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-28

**Source:** [Shuttered Cordele Factory Roars Back To Life As Yancey Bets On 300](https://hoodline.com/2026/06/shuttered-cordele-factory-roars-back-to-life-as-yancey-bets-on-300-jobs/)  ·  `page_017797`

## Facts

- Yancey Engineered Solutions, a division of Austell-based Yancey Bros., plans to invest $5.7 million into the shuttered Big Tex plant in Cordele.
- The company will renovate the facility located at 502 South Midway Road to create a hub for power-generation packaging systems.
- Company officials project the revamped factory could ultimately support up to 300 jobs over the next several years.
- The project was formally announced on June 25 via a press release from the governor’s office.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated the expansion will provide direct opportunity for hardworking Georgians in Crisp County and help drive further growth statewide.
- Cordele Mayor Wesley Rainey and Crisp County officials welcomed the announcement, noting it could allow former Big Tex workers to return to the shop floor.
- Yancey plans to hire welders, quality-control technicians, assembly workers, and drivers for the new facility.
- The Cordele-Crisp Industrial Development Authority, Crisp County Power Commission, and Georgia Quick Start partnered to help secure the deal.
- Yancey Engineered Solutions focuses on packaging, containerization, and sound-attenuated enclosures for generators.
- The company's existing testing and service center in Griffin handles similar enclosure work to help the Cordele site ramp up smoothly.
- Georgia Quick Start will provide workforce training as hiring scales up over the next several years.
- Senior Regional Project Manager Christy Bozeman represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development on the competitive project.
- Local leaders noted that reusing an existing industrial building cuts down on lead time and improves chances of bringing experienced manufacturing workers back.
- Site work at the facility is expected to begin soon, according to Cordele-Crisp Industrial Development Authority officials.
- Company and county leaders have not provided a firm opening date but stated hiring will roll out gradually as the building is refurbished.
