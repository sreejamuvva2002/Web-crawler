# Aurubis AG

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2021-11-10

**Source:** [Aurubis to Open State-of-the-Art Recycling and Copper Smelting Facility in Augusta - Augusta Economic Development Authority](https://augustaeda.org/uncategorized/aurubis-to-open-state-of-the-art-recycling-and-copper-smelting-facility-in-augusta/)  ·  `page_017326`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Aurubis will invest $340 million (€300 million) in a state-of-the-art recycling and secondary smelting facility for multi-metal recycling in Augusta.
- The new facility will be the very first of its kind in the U.S.
- The facility will create 125 jobs in the Augusta area.
- Governor Kemp stated this marks the single-largest investment made by a German company in the State of Georgia.
- Aurubis is one of the leading recyclers of copper, precious metals, and non-ferrous materials worldwide.
- From the Augusta facility, the company plans to produce materials found in lithium-ion batteries and other electronics.
- Roland Harings, CEO of Aurubis AG, said the investment sets a clear example of sustainable growth and positions the company as a forerunner for multi-metal recycling in the U.S.
- Aurubis will build its facility on 150 acres at Augusta Corporate Park.
- Augusta Corporate Park is a “Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) Certified” site.
- Operations at the new facility are expected to begin by the first half of 2024.
- The company plans to begin hiring for positions as soon as summer 2022.
- Steven Kendrick, Chairman of the Development Authority of Augusta, welcomed Aurubis as a new corporate tenant that will build a world-class advanced manufacturing facility and bring skilled jobs to Augusta.
- Griff Lynch, Executive Director of the Georgia Ports Authority (GPA), stated that exports produced by the Aurubis recycling operation will strengthen the state’s global position via Savannah.
- Fernanda Kirchner, Senior Project Manager for the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce division, represented GDEcD on this project in partnership with the Augusta Economic Development Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, and Georgia Power.
- Sérgio Domingues, Managing Director for the State of Georgia’s Europe Office, played a supporting role in attracting Aurubis to Georgia.
- Pat Wilson, GDEcD Commissioner, noted that Georgia is home to more than 500 German companies and highlighted the project's role in keeping Georgia in the driver’s seat of the green manufacturing future.
- Augusta Corporate Park has completed due diligence and other specific certification requirements prior to visits by prospective businesses as part of the GRAD program.
- The GRAD program, administered by GDEcD, is a proactive way for community planners and landowners to help catalyze economic growth and industrial development for their regions.
- Thousands of Georgia jobs have been created on GRAD sites since the program’s inception in 2008.
- Aurubis AG is a leading global provider of non-ferrous metals and one of the largest copper recyclers worldwide.
- The company processes complex metal concentrates, scrap metals, organic and inorganic metal-bearing recycling materials, and industrial residues into metals of the highest quality.
- Aurubis produces more than 1 million tons of copper cathodes annually.
- From copper cathodes, Aurubis manufactures wire rod, continuous cast shapes, profiles, and flat rolled products made of copper and copper alloys.
- Aurubis also produces precious metals, selenium, lead, nickel, tin, and zinc, along with sulfuric acid and iron silicate.
