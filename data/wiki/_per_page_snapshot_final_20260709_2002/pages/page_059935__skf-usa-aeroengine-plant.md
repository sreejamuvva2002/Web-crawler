# SKF USA Aeroengine Plant

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2025-05

**Source:** [Local SKF USA Plant Making Investments | News, Sports, Jobs - Post Journal](https://www.post-journal.com/news/top-stories/2025/05/local-skf-usa-plant-making-investments/)  ·  `page_059935`

## Facts

- SKF USA’s Aeroengine Plant in Falconer is receiving a mini-grant from the Jamestown BPU’s Retool WNY program.
- The grant supports efforts to right-size the company and create efficiencies amid seven consecutive quarters of organic sales losses.
- The plant is investing in additional equipment and automation tools to ramp up production of ceramic rolling elements for the electrified aircraft market, part of the emerging clean energy sector.
- Ceramic rollers and balls are 40 percent lighter than their equivalent steel rolling elements.
- The lighter-weight components help reduce fuel usage and enable growth in the electrified aircraft market.
- SKF is adding a robotic arm to two existing loading and unloading machines to improve production line efficiency and increase output to meet rising demand for lighter-weight rollers.
- Reinvesting and right-sizing efforts are ongoing throughout the entire SKF company.
- SKF reported first quarter financial results showing net sales of SEK 23,966,000, a decrease from SEK 24,699,000 in the first quarter of 2024.
- Organic growth was -3.5%, an improvement over the -7% organic growth recorded in the first quarter of 2024.
- Adjusted operating profit was SEK 3,233,000, a decrease from SEk 3,303,000 in the first quarter of 2024.
- Adjusted operating margins improved by .1% to 13.5%.
- Net cash flow from operating activities decreased from SEK 1,781,000 in the first quarter of 2024 to SEK 977,000 in the first quarter of 2025.
- Rickard Gustafson, SKF president and CEO, stated during a conference call that the company operates in continued weak demand with tough market conditions and volatile geopolitics, but sees signs of markets bottoming out, especially in Europe.
- Unlike Cummins officials who withdrew second quarter or full year financial projections, SKF expects continued volatility and plans for another quarter with negative volumes.
- SKF expects organic sales to weaken somewhat in the second quarter of 2025 compared to the same period in 2024.
- SKF is conducting an organizational review to rightsize its organization to better withstand future turbulent markets.
- Staff cuts, including in Europe, will be presented in conjunction with the 2025 second quarter financial statements.
- SKF owns the SKF Aeroengine North America plant in Falconer after acquiring MRC Bearings in 1986.
- The Falconer plant is part of SKF’s aerospace division.
- Gustafson declined to give a specific number for staff reductions due to ongoing discussions by country, but noted roughly 13,000 employees are classified as staff globally as the base for the reduction.
- Sales declines are not currently due to tariffs, though that effect is expected later once more is known.
- Tariffs remain a consideration for SKF officials, with Gustafson noting it is hard to predict impacts given recent pauses and discussions between China and the U.S. about potentially relaxing some tariffs.
