# Stryten Energy LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2022-06-07

**Source:** [Stryten Energy Releases New Research Comparing Today's](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2022/06/07/2457758/0/en/Stryten-Energy-Releases-New-Research-Comparing-Today-s-Automotive-Battery-Technologies.html)  ·  `page_029439`

## Facts

- Stryten Energy LLC is a U.S.-based energy storage solutions provider headquartered in Alpharetta, Georgia.
- The company released new research comparing today's automotive battery technologies on June 07, 2022.
- An independent third-party research company conducted an in-depth analysis evaluating the performance of the Stryten Energy Enhanced Flooded Battery (EFB) compared to the Stryten Energy SLI and a competing premium flooded battery.
- Bill Nonnamaker, Vice President of Sales, Transportation, stated that today’s vehicles require advanced battery technology to power key systems for safety and fuel efficiency, and that Stryten’s EFB offers high performance and battery life to meet these demands.
- The tested batteries were all L3/H6 type.
- Stryten EFB specifications are 750 CCA / 120 RC / 74 AH.
- Stryten SLI specifications are 750 CCA / 133 RC / 70 AH.
- The competing premium flooded battery specifications are 730 CCA / 120 RC.
- Three samples of each battery type were subjected to the SAE j2801 cycle life test and the EN50342-6 17.5% depth of discharge (DoD) cycle life test.
- The SAE j2801 test is the industry standard high temperature (75°C) life cycle test for SLI batteries designed to simulate battery usage in a standard internal combustion vehicle application.
- The EN50342-6 / 17.5% DoD test is an industry-standard partial state of charge and micro cycling life cycle test focused on Start/Stop and vehicles with additional power demands.
- Including the partial state of charge testing was intended to better address the changing power needs of modern vehicles.
- Test results show that Stryten EFB has superior performance over competing premium flooded batteries.
- The Stryten EFB meets the additional depth of discharge requirements for vehicles with Start-Stop functionality.
- The Stryten EFB yields higher heat tolerance with a unique grid design.
- On the 17.5% DoD partial state of charge test, the Stryten EFB yielded 2X+ the cycle life of either the competing premium flooded battery or the Stryten SLI.
- The Stryten EFB was equal to the competing premium flooded battery on the high-temperature j2801 cycle life test.
- Both the Stryten EFB and competing premium battery performed 25% greater than the Stryten SLI on the high-temperature j2801 test.
- Further teardown indicates the competing premium battery utilizes a relatively thick grid compared to their standard product to achieve improved SAE j2801 performance.
- The Stryten grid is also thicker than standard but pairs this with a more robust grid alloy to provide even more heat protection on a grid thickness basis.
- While the competing premium flooded battery addresses high-temperature life compared to standard SLI designs, it does not address cycling failure modes or Start-Stop functionality.
- The Stryten EFB provides a solution for these issues and is designed from the ground up to combat the fastest-growing causes of premature battery failure.
- Stryten Energy helps solve energy challenges across the Essential Power, Motive Power, Transportation, Military, and Government sectors.
- The company partners with recognized global companies to meet growing demand for reliable and sustainable energy storage capacity.
- Stryten powers applications ranging from submarines to subcompacts, microgrids, warehouses, distribution centers, cars, trains, and trucks.
- Their stored energy technologies include advanced lead, lithium, and vanadium redox flow batteries, intelligent chargers, and energy performance management software.
- The company is an industry leader backed by more than a century of expertise.
