# Duckyang

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-06

**Source:** [Hyundai’s Automotive Electronics Supplier to Establish First U.S. Factory in Georgia-Machinery Insights Insider MingYang](https://machinerys.net/automotive-manufacturing-equipment/hyundai-s-automotive-electronics-supplier-to-establish-first-u-s-factory-in-georgia/)  ·  `page_007729`

## Facts

- State officials announced that a maker of automotive electronics will set up its first U.S. factory in northern Georgia.
- Duckyang plans to invest $10 million in the new facility located in Braselton, about 45 miles northwest of Atlanta.
- The company expects to create 285 new jobs at the site.
- The plant will provide battery parts to SK Innovation, a fellow Korean company that will supply electric vehicle batteries to Ford and Volkswagen.
- SK Innovation is building a battery plant in nearby Commerce, Georgia.
- Duckyang currently supplies cockpit modules, which are integrated components located near the driver’s seat, to Hyundai, its affiliated brands Kia and Genesis, its parts subsidiary Mobis, and Renault Samsung Motors.
- Company officials stated that the initial U.S. facility will provide new opportunities to work with North American automakers, including Kia and Hyundai, which operate assembly plants in Georgia and Alabama, respectively.
- Duckyang CEO Dong-in Son said the company expects business expansion opportunities to supply not only electric vehicle parts but also interior parts, including cockpit modules, door trim panel modules, and other flagship parts to global automakers based in the United States.
- The Associated Press reported that the company could save up to $1.8 million per year in state income tax credits.
- Georgia will provide job training but no additional discretionary incentives for the project.
- Local governments could provide property tax breaks for the facility.
