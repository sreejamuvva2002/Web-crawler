# Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia, Inc.

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2012-07-03

**Source:** [Strong demand for vehicles built at Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia leads to additional hiring for on-site supplier - Kia Georgia](https://www.kiageorgia.com/strong-demand-for-vehicles-built-at-kia-motors-manufacturing-georgia-leads-to-additional-hiring-for-on-site-supplier/)  ·  `page_010047`

## Facts

- Together with local suppliers, Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia, Inc. (KMMG) is responsible for the creation of more than 10,000 jobs in West Point and surrounding areas.
- Powertech America, the on-site transmission supplier for KMMG, announced plans to add 143 new positions at its West Point facility.
- Powertech America announced plans to add a third shift at its West Point facility.
- KMMG added a third shift in 2011 as part of a $100 million expansion.
- KMMG's production contributed to Kia's string of 21 consecutive monthly sales records.
- KMMG produces the award-winning and top-selling Kia Sorento CUV and Kia Optima mid-size sedan.
- On-site and local suppliers have created or announced nearly 8,000 jobs.
- KMMG has directly created more than 3,000 jobs.
- Byung Mo Ahn, Group President and CEO for Kia Motors America and KMMG, stated that the success of KMMG has led to tremendous growth at the plant and with suppliers who continue to expand with them.
- Byung Mo Ahn expressed pride in the work of team members at KMMG and its suppliers who contributed to continued regional success.
- Powertech held job fairs beginning the week of June 11.
- The separate third shift at Powertech is taking place by January.
- This latest expansion will increase total employment at Powertech to 454 team members.
- KMMG is the first manufacturing site in North America for Kia Motors Corporation based in Seoul, Korea.
- KMMG is located on 2,200 acres in West Point, Georgia.
- KMMG began mass production on Nov. 16, 2009.
- Beginning in 2012, the plant has the ability to build 360,000 vehicles annually from U.S. and globally-sourced parts.
