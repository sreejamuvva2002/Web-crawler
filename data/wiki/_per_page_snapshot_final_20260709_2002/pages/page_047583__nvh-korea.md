# NVH Korea

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-06-23

**Source:** [EV Parts Supplier NVH Korea To Establish Georgia Facility](https://www.aftermarketnews.com/nvh-korea-georgia-manufacturing-facility/)  ·  `page_047583`

## Facts

- Automotive parts manufacturer NVH Korea will establish a new facility in Locust Grove, Georgia.
- The project will create over 160 new jobs.
- The project will bring $72 million in investment to Henry County.
- Gov. Brian Kemp’s office announced the project.
- This is the second economic development project announced this week as Georgia promotes itself to international job creators while abroad.
- Gov. Brian Kemp stated that reliable infrastructure and a great workforce allow job creators to find success in every community in the state.
- Gov. Brian Kemp highlighted the incredible growth of the e-mobility and battery industries in Georgia as a testament to supporting large suppliers like NVH Korea.
- Gov. Brian Kemp welcomed NVH Korea as the latest partner in making Georgia the epicenter of the industrial revolution of their time.
- NVH Korea is an automotive parts manufacturer based in the City of Ulsan, South Korea.
- NVH Korea was established in 1984.
- The company specializes in automotive noise, vibration and heat-control parts.
- NVH Korea currently produces its parts in Korea, Europe and India.
- The company already operates a manufacturing facility in Georgia that produces floor mats, cargo mats and cargo trays.
- The new facility will supply battery parts for electric vehicles (EVs) in the US.
- NVH Korea's EV battery parts will be supplied to manufacturers such as Hyundai Mobis, Kia Georgia, and Hyundai Motor Group.
- Ja Kyum Koo is the chairman and CEO of NVH Korea.
- Ja Kyum Koo stated that Georgia was chosen as the company’s first EV battery component manufacturing site.
- Cooperation between state agencies and NVH Korea staff helped guide the project.
- Ja Kyum Koo described this as the first step of the company's effort to contribute to the electrification of the mobility industry.
- The new facility will produce EV battery system components that protect, connect and sense the performance of battery cells.
- Operations at the new facility are expected to begin in the second quarter of 2024.
- From July 1 through December 31, 2022, the automotive industry was the second-largest source of new jobs and investment in Georgia.
- Since 2020, Georgia has pursued job creation along the entire EV supply chain, resulting in more than $22.7 billion in investments.
- Since 2020, Georgia's EV supply chain pursuit has resulted in the creation of over 28,400 jobs.
