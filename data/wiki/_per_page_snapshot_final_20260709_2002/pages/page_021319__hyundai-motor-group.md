# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hyundai to continue with $2.7 billion expansion of Georgia plant raided by ICE - CBS News](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/georgia-plant-hyundai-ev-expansion-ice-raid/)  ·  `page_021319`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group confirmed it is proceeding with previously announced plans to expand its Georgia plant.
- An immigration raid delayed the startup of an electric vehicle battery plant at the site.
- Hyundai will spend $2.7 billion to increase production capacity at the Ellabell site.
- The expansion will add 200,000 vehicles to annual capacity over the next three years.
- Total annual production capacity at the Ellabell site will reach 500,000 vehicles.
- Hyundai first announced the expansion in March at the grand opening of the plant west of Savannah.
- In August, Hyundai stated it would invest an additional $5 billion in the United States overall.
- The immigration raid included arrests of more than 300 South Korean citizens.
- Hyundai plans to produce 10 models of electric and hybrid gas-electric vehicles in Georgia, up from the current two.
- Hyundai remains on track to expand worldwide production to 5.6 million vehicles a year by 2030.
- The automaker pledged that 60% of its worldwide vehicles will be electric or hybrid powered by 2030.
- Target markets for the expanded production include South Korea, North America, and Europe.
- Hyundai plans to manufacture more than 80% of vehicles sold in the United States domestically by 2030.
- Total domestic content for U.S.-sold vehicles will increase from 60% to 80%.
- The expanded Georgia production will include a mid-sized pickup truck for the first time.
- Hyundai already manufactures the Santa Cruz, a four-door compact pickup, which it started selling in 2021.
- Hyundai CEO José Muñoz stated the immigration raid will delay the battery plant opening by at least two to three months.
- Spokesperson Michael Stewart confirmed the battery facility will open in the first half of 2026.
- The immigration raid escalated into a diplomatic dispute between South Korea and the United States.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp expressed confidence that Georgia's business advantages will win out regarding foreign investment despite the raid.
- Gov. Kemp suggested the raid may smooth the way for South Korean employees to more easily obtain legal permission to work in the U.S.
- Gov. Kemp reported having good conversations with current and prospective businesses in Georgia regarding the situation.
- Gov. Kemp has held conversations with White House officials about the visa issue.
- Brent Stubbs, chief administrative officer of the Ellabell site, stated in an opinion piece that the company remains committed to Georgia.
- Stubbs stated the situation does not change plans to continue expanding and localizing in the United States.
- Stubbs noted that Hyundai's investments in America are part of a long-term strategic plan.
- The confirmed $2.7 billion investment will fund capacity expansion at the plant and group affiliates.
- Hyundai and its onsite affiliates currently employ 3,129 workers in Ellabell.
- Hyundai and joint-venture partner LG Energy Solution previously announced $12.6 billion in investments at the Georgia site.
- The partnership pledged to hire at least 8,500 workers by the end of 2031.
- State and local governments have promised $2.1 billion in tax breaks and other incentives for the project.
- The Ellabell site expansion is the largest component of Hyundai's plan to increase worldwide production by 1.2 million vehicles a year.
- Worldwide production increases include 250,000 additional vehicles from Pune, India.
- Worldwide production increases include 200,000 additional vehicles from Hyundai's electric vehicle plant in Ulsan, South Korea.
- Hyundai will deliver parts for assembly into an additional 250,000 vehicles at plants in Saudi Arabia, Vietnam, and North Africa.
- Hyundai plans to deepen its investment in robotics.
- Hyundai will launch extended-range electric vehicles with gasoline motors by 2027.
- The new extended-range electric vehicles will extend battery range to more than 600 miles (960 kilometers).
