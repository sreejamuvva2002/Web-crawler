# Rivian Automotive

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Rivian's Strategic Momentum: Supply Chain Resilience and Production Scalability Post-Georgia Factory Groundbreaking](https://www.ainvest.com/news/rivian-strategic-momentum-supply-chain-resilience-production-scalability-post-georgia-factory-groundbreaking-2509/)  ·  `page_001053`

## Facts

- Rivian has broken ground on its $5 billion Georgia factory.
- The project is contingent on creating 7,500 direct jobs and 8,000 indirect jobs by 2030.
- Rivian secured a $6.6 billion federal loan from the U.S. Department of Energy for the project.
- Rivian secured a $1.5 billion state incentive package from Georgia for the project.
- Construction at the Georgia factory is scheduled to begin in 2026.
- The incentives are tied to Rivian's commitment to localize its supply chain amid rising tariffs and geopolitical uncertainty.
- Rivian partnered with LG Energy Solution (LGES) to produce 4695 cylindrical battery cells at a new Arizona facility.
- The Arizona-made cells will power Rivian's R2 and R3 models.
- The new 4695 cells offer six times the capacity of the 2170 cells currently in use, enhancing energy density and safety.
- The LGES collaboration aligns with Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) requirements for domestic battery production.
- The partnership reduces Rivian's reliance on overseas suppliers while qualifying the company for federal tax credits.
- Rivian has proactively stockpiled lithium iron phosphate (LFP) battery cells from Gotion High-Tech in China and Samsung SDI in South Korea.
- The stockpile ensures short-term supply chain stability amid tariff risks.
- Rivian aims to co-locate suppliers near its Georgia plant to reduce logistics costs and lead times.
- This supplier co-location strategy is echoed by traditional automakers like Toyota and Ford in their Southeastern U.S. operations.
- The Georgia factory features a two-phase design targeting 400,000 annual vehicles by 2028.
- Each phase of the Georgia facility will produce 200,000 units annually.
- Rivian currently produces 15,000 units annually at its Illinois plant.
- The company burns through $1 billion annually in operating costs.
- Rivian has partnered with Georgia's technical colleges and universities to create apprenticeship programs for advanced manufacturing labor.
- The Georgia plant is located near I-20 and major ports, providing a logistical edge for distribution to the Eastern U.S. and export markets.
- Competitors like Tesla and Ford are scaling production, with Tesla's Texas and Berlin Gigafactories targeting 500,000 units annually by 2025.
- Rivian differentiates itself through premium utility vehicles (R1T/R1S) and upcoming mass-market R2 and R3 models aimed at lower price points.
- Environmental concerns from local residents regarding the Georgia plant's impact on farmland could delay permits or trigger regulatory scrutiny.
- Rivian is addressing environmental concerns through sustainability initiatives including renewable energy integration and modern construction techniques.
- The project's success depends on continued progress with LGES and domestic battery producers for supply chain localization.
- Meeting the 2028 production targets is essential for Rivian to achieve economies of scale and profitability.
