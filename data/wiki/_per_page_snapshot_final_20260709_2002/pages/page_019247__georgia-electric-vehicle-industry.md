# Georgia Electric Vehicle Industry

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2022-08-02

**Source:** [EV Industry Keeps Rolling Along - Atlanta Magazine](https://www.atlantamagazine.com/article/ev-industry-keeps-rolling-along/)  ·  `page_019247`

## Facts

- Georgia remains on an electric-vehicle-driven roll, with innovation extending beyond its roads to traditional economic pillars like agriculture and defense growing through technology.
- On July 20, Denkai America announced it will manufacture electrodeposited copper foil in Richmond County and relocate its North American headquarters there.
- The Augusta Corporate Park facility will mainly supply foil necessary for lithium-ion batteries used in electric vehicles.
- Denkai America is part of Japan’s Nippon Denkai and is currently North America’s only producer of high-quality electrodeposited copper foil, which it manufactures in Camden, South Carolina.
- The existing Camden facility will continue producing foil, primarily for printed electronics.
- Copper from these electronics will be recycled at a metal recycling facility that German company Aurubis is building in Richmond County, though Aurubis and Denkai have no relationship.
- Denkai originally planned to build the new plant in Camden but lacked sufficient space, according to Denkai COO Michael Coll.
- The Georgia site offers increased initial capacity from 9,000 to 9,500 metric tons a year, room to triple production, and a locked-in low cost for electricity.
- The initial facility will cost $150 million, with construction due to start in September and samples shipping in mid-2024.
- Denkai aims to invest $430 million over five years and create 250 jobs at the Georgia site.
- Michael Coll stated that Denkai America and the Georgia Economic Development team share the same vision regarding the automotive transition from internal combustion engines to EVs.
- Georgia reports more than $12.6 billion in investment from EV-related projects since 2020.
- Rivian announced a $5 billion EV facility in December, followed by Hyundai with a $5.5 billion project in May.
- Georgia ranks number one in the Southeast for new electric vehicle registrations.
- In August, Georgia is submitting a plan to secure its share of $5 billion in federal National Electric Vehicle Infrastructure (NEVI) grants.
- The state Department of Transportation expects to receive $116.2 million over five years to build out “alternative fuel corridors” with public electric charging stations.
- Jannine Miller, GDOT’s director of planning, noted that strengthening the electric charging network requires collaboration among government, industry, utilities, and stakeholders at every stage.
- Georgia could also seek grants from a $2.5 billion discretionary EV infrastructure program, which alongside the NEVI program is part of the 2021 Bipartisan Infrastructure Law.
- Military drone maker Area-I is spending $60 million on a new manufacturing and research facility in Atlanta, expecting to create 180 jobs over three years.
- Area-I, a Georgia-based manufacturer of advanced unmanned aircraft, is a subsidiary of California defense technology company Anduril Industries.
- Matt Grimm, Anduril’s chief operating officer, stated they are proud to be an active part of Atlanta’s growing technology and defense community, citing its highly skilled workforce in aerospace, engineering, and robotics.
- In Marietta, Lockheed Martin is laying groundwork for an expansion costing up to $1.6 billion over 20 years, fueled by possible new defense contracts and Development Authority of Cobb County bonds, envisioning 500 to 3,000 new jobs.
- Ohio-based 80 Acres Farms is building its ninth farm and first outside the Midwest in Covington as a $120 million facility expected to be operational in August and distribute produce early next year.
- The indoor vertical farm operates year-round without pesticides and produces the equivalent of a traditional farm covering 300 times the area.
- Seven rural Georgia counties (Appling, Bacon, Dodge, Elbert, Franklin, Hart, and Union) placed among the top 50 nonmetropolitan communities in the nation per a Site Selection magazine ranking based on economic development projects from the 12 months ending April 30.
- Alma, the seat of Bacon County, led with five qualifying projects including a La Regina di Marzano tomato sauce plant and an InFlex plastics plant.
- Okabashi Brands is investing $20 million into its Buford location over five years to double production and add 340 jobs, announced in June.
- Okabashi CEO Sara Irvani highlighted community support fostering growth for producing quality American-made footwear and expressed thrill at contributing to the local economy.
- Okabashi’s fully recyclable sandals are among the 1 percent of shoes sold in the United States that are made in America.
