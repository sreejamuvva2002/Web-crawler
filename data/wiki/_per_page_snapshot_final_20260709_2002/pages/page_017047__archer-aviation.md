# Archer Aviation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-08-11

**Source:** [Archer Aviation Earns Fresh Funding from Bitter Rival Turned Ally](https://www.flyingmag.com/archer-aviation-earns-fresh-funding-from-bitter-rival-turned-ally/)  ·  `page_017047`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation and rival Wisk Aero jointly announced an agreement to settle a yearslong trade secrets dispute on undisclosed terms.
- Archer will issue warrants to Wisk for up to 13.2 million shares as part of the settlement.
- The settlement includes a collaboration making Wisk the sole provider of autonomy technology for Archer.
- Boeing will fund the integration of Wisk's autonomy technology on a future variant of Archer’s Midnight eVTOL.
- Boeing bought out Kitty Hawk’s remaining shares in Wisk to become its sole owner in June.
- Archer announced a $215 million investment from Stellantis, United Airlines, and ARK Investment Management.
- The funding raised Archer’s valuation to $1.1 billion.
- The funding includes Boeing’s money as well as a $70 million acceleration from Stellantis, part of an exclusive manufacturing partnership signed in January.
- Archer revealed the FAA has greenlit its Midnight aircraft for initial test flights.
- The FAA approval keeps Archer on track for type design flight testing in 2024 and a commercial launch in 2025.
- The first delivery of Midnight aircraft to an Air Force base is on schedule under the firm’s $142 million AFWERX contract.
- The announcements came just over a month before Archer and Wisk’s legal dispute was set to head to trial.
- In May 2021, Wisk sued Archer for the “brazen theft” of over 50 trade secrets, alleging a former employee downloaded sensitive information before departing for Archer.
- Archer escalated the dispute in August with a $1 billion countersuit for defamation, calling Wisk’s claims an “extra-judicial smear campaign.”
- A motion for injunction by Wisk was denied, allowing Archer to continue developing its Maker aircraft.
- Judge William Orrick III blocked Archer's attempt to dismiss the case, ruling Wisk’s allegations were plausible.
- Federal prosecutors declined to charge former Wisk engineer Jing Xue in February 2022.
- Archer and Wisk entered a second round of mediation in March but reached no resolution before settling.
- Archer’s net loss widened significantly in Q2 as it prepares to ramp up manufacturing and flight testing.
- Archer has about $407 million in cash and short-term investments, compared to rival Joby Aviation's reported $1.2 billion.
- Archer has agreements with United Airlines to fly eVTOLs in Chicago and New York City.
- Archer will compete in the emerging advanced air mobility (AAM) market, flying passengers on short trips to and from airfields.
