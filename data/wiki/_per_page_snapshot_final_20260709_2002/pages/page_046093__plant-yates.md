# Plant Yates

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2025-08-15

**Source:** [First new natural gas turbine delivered to Georgia Power's Plant Yates - Aug 15, 2025](https://southerncompany.mediaroom.com/2025-08-15-First-new-natural-gas-turbine-delivered-to-Georgia-Powers-Plant-Yates)  ·  `page_046093`

## Facts

- Georgia Power and Mitsubishi Power announced the delivery of the first of three new state-of-the-art simple cycle gas combustion turbines at Plant Yates in Coweta County, Georgia.
- Plant Yates is located about an hour southwest of Atlanta.
- The turbine was assembled at Mitsubishi Power's Savannah Machinery Works facility.
- The turbine was transported to the site using both rail and truck.
- The turbine weighs nearly 350 tons and measures 50 feet long by 18 feet wide.
- The new natural gas units will feature Mitsubishi Power M501JAC combustion turbines (CTs).
- These are the first new natural gas turbines added to Georgia Power's fleet in a decade.
- When all three units are in service, they will provide 1,300 megawatts (MW) of generation capacity for customers.
- Plant Yates began commercial operation in 1950 and was the first Georgia Power plant built to support the post-World War II economic boom.
- In 2014, five of the seven coal-fired units at Plant Yates were decommissioned, with the remaining two converted to natural gas generation.
- The expansion will provide approximately 600 jobs during the construction process.
- The project will add 15 permanent new jobs once completed, expanding the plant workforce to 75 full-time jobs.
- The new units were approved by the Georgia Public Service Commission (PSC) in the 2023 Integrated Resource Plan (IRP) Update.
- The units are expected to be online by the end of 2027 to meet new energy demand in the state.
- Rick Anderson, senior vice president and senior production officer for Georgia Power, stated that the new natural gas units will use existing property and infrastructure to deliver value for customers and provide investment in Coweta County.
- The three advanced class gas turbines provide higher output and greater efficiency than previous generations of simple cycle CT designs.
- The air-cooled Mitsubishi Power M501JAC Series design eliminates the need for steam cooling, offers a shorter start-up time of approximately 30 minutes, and has a lower turn down rate.
- As a contingency, the units have built-in flexibility to run on oil, with on-site oil storage capability.
- With minor future modifications, the M501JAC is capable of using a hydrogen mix as a fuel.
- Georgia Power recently partnered with Mitsubishi Power for a 50% hydrogen-blending project at Plant McDonough-Atkinson.
- Cheryl Boddiford, senior vice president of North American Manufacturing and Supply Chain Management at Mitsubishi Power, noted the delivery reflects a long-standing partnership and commitment to U.S.-based manufacturing and skilled workforce development.
- Georgia Power has been approved for combined cycle and simple cycle upgrades on all combustion turbines at Plant McIntosh near Savannah in the 2025 IRP.
- The Plant McIntosh enhancements will add an additional 268-megawatts of capacity.
- A recent all-source RFP certification filing with the PSC includes a request to certify five new combined cycle (CC) units totaling 3,692 MW.
- The proposed CC units are planned to be placed at Plants Bowen, McIntosh, and Wansley to help ensure grid stability and reliability.
