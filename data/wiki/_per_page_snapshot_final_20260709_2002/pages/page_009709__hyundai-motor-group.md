# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-19

**Source:** [Hyundai moves ahead with Georgia plant expansion post immigration raid](https://americanbazaaronline.com/2025/09/19/hyundai-moves-ahead-with-georgia-plant-expansion-post-immigration-raid-467823/)  ·  `page_009709`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group announced it will proceed with its planned expansion of its Georgia manufacturing facility despite recent disruptions caused by an immigration enforcement action that temporarily stalled the launch of an electric vehicle battery plant on the site.
- Hyundai said it will invest $2.7 billion to boost production at the Ellabell, Georgia, facility.
- The investment will increase production by 200,000 vehicles over the next three years.
- This raises the plant’s total annual capacity to 500,000 vehicles.
- The $2.7 billion will be used to expand production capacity at the Georgia plant and related Hyundai affiliates.
- Company spokesperson Michael Stewart confirmed this allocation.
- Combined with earlier investments, total spending on the auto facility reaches $10.3 billion.
- This excludes the $4.3 billion Hyundai and joint-venture partner LG Energy Solution are contributing to the battery plant.
- Hyundai initially unveiled the Ellabell expansion in March during the plant’s grand opening west of Savannah.
- In August, Hyundai announced plans to invest an additional $5 billion across the United States.
- A recent immigration raid resulted in the arrest of more than 300 South Korean workers.
- The raid sparked scrutiny over the company’s continued investment in the U.S.
- Hyundai CEO José Muñoz acknowledged that the recent immigration raid will push back the opening of the battery plant by at least two to three months.
- Stewart said on Thursday that the facility is now expected to begin operations in the first half of 2026.
- Ellabell site chief administrative officer Brent Stubbs stated, 'this situation doesn’t change our plans to continue expanding and localizing in the United States.'
- Stubbs wrote in an opinion piece in The Atlanta Journal-Constitution that the company is still committed to Georgia.
- The opinion piece was published Wednesday, as per AP.
- The South Korean automotive company revealed that the Georgia plant will eventually assemble 10 different electric and hybrid models.
- This is up from the two currently in production as the facility scales up.
- Hyundai remains on track to reach a global production target of 5.6 million vehicles annually by 2030.
- Plans call for 60% of those global vehicles to be electric or hybrid.
- These will be aimed at markets in South Korea, North America, and Europe.
- Hyundai plans to produce more than 80% of the vehicles it sells in the United States domestically by 2030.
- This increases domestic content from 60% to 80%.
- For the first time, the lineup will include a mid-sized pickup truck.
- The lineup will also include the Santa Cruz, a four-door compact pickup that the company began selling in 2021.
- Stewart said Hyundai and its on-site affiliates currently employ 3,129 workers at the Ellabell facility.
- The company has pledged to expand its workforce to at least 8,500 by the end of 2031.
- State and local governments have offered $2.1 billion in tax incentives and other benefits in support of the project.
- The expansion at the Ellabell plant represents the largest component of Hyundai’s plan to increase global production by 1.2 million vehicles annually.
- The strategy includes adding 250,000 units from its Pune, India, facility.
- The strategy includes adding 200,000 from its electric vehicle plant in Ulsan, South Korea.
- Hyundai said it will supply parts for another 250,000 vehicles to be assembled at plants in Saudi Arabia, Vietnam, and North Africa.
- Hyundai reaffirmed its commitment to advancing robotics technology.
- The company announced plans to introduce extended-range electric vehicles by 2027.
- These new models will combine electric batteries with gasoline engines.
- They will enable a driving range of more than 600 miles (960 kilometers).
- This reflects the company’s push to innovate in both efficiency and performance.
