# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-05-20

**Source:** [Georgia Power welcomes Hyundai Motor Group to Coastal Georgia](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/georgia-power-welcomes-hyundai-motor-group-to-coastal-georgia-301552302.html)  ·  `page_006089`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group announced its first fully dedicated electric vehicle (EV) and battery manufacturing facility in the State of Georgia.
- The new state-of-the-art U.S. smart factory will be located at the more than 2,900-acre Bryan County Megasite in Coastal Georgia.
- Hyundai Motor Group is making a $5.54 billion investment for the project.
- Non-affiliated Hyundai suppliers are expected to invest approximately another $1 billion in the project.
- The facility will deliver approximately 8,100 new jobs to Georgia's coastal region.
- Georgia Power joined Governor Brian Kemp and the Georgia Department of Economic Development to celebrate the announcement on May 20, 2022.
- Hyundai Motor Group is headquartered in Seoul, Korea, and employs 250,000 people worldwide.
- The company sells vehicles in 193 countries through 5,000 dealerships and showrooms.
- Chris Womack, chairman, president, and CEO of Georgia Power, stated that the announcement is historic for the state and highlights the foresight of state and local leaders in creating a megasite for international manufacturers.
- Chris Womack also serves on the Georgia Ports Authority Board.
- The highly connected, automated, and flexible EV smart factory will produce a diverse range of innovative full electric vehicles.
- Through the battery manufacturing facility, Hyundai Motor Group aims to establish a stable supply chain for EV batteries and other EV components in the U.S. market.
- Georgia is home to more than 200 automotive-related facilities that contribute more than $3 billion annually to the state's economy.
- In 2021, Georgia had 977,000 workers supporting its automotive operations and facilities.
- Before this announcement, Georgia's EV and battery production industry had already created 13,200 new jobs and $8 billion in new capital investment.
- Georgia is recognized as a leader in electric transportation and is near the top in the nation for EV customer adoption and EV charging, with thousands of connections available across the state.
- Georgia Power supports proactive efforts to attract multibillion-dollar companies involved in auto manufacturing production, recycling, and development, as original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) shift product lines to meet increasing EV production demands.
- Chris Womack noted that as more Georgians choose electric vehicles, Georgia offers an ideal market for auto manufacturers, suppliers, and technology leaders to collaborate on the future of the EV marketplace.
- Georgia Power recently partnered with the University of Georgia's College of Engineering to create an e-Mobility Certificate program covering engineering, public policy, public health, and business acumen to prepare the workforce for electrified transportation.
- Georgia Power is working closely with design students at Georgia Tech to evaluate environmental benefits and future workforce opportunities of transportation electrification.
- In 2021, Georgia Power joined Southern Company and utilities across the South, Midwest, Gulf Coast, Central Plains, and mid-Atlantic to form the Electric Highway Coalition.
- The Electric Highway Coalition enables a coordinated network of charging stations along major highway routes from Texas to Washington, DC to provide easier access to EV options for drivers.
- Georgia Power is the largest electric subsidiary of Southern Company (NYSE: SO), America's premier energy company.
- Georgia Power serves 2.7 million customers in all but four of Georgia's 159 counties.
- The company delivers clean, safe, reliable, and affordable energy at rates below the national average.
- Georgia Power maintains a diverse generation mix including nuclear, coal, natural gas, and renewables such as solar, hydroelectric, and wind.
- Georgia Power is recognized by J.D. Power as an industry leader in customer satisfaction.
