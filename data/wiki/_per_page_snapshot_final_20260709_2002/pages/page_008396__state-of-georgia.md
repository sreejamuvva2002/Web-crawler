# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-09-29

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp celebrates Georgia's 'Top State for Business' ranking at Workforce Summit  - Grice Connect](https://www.griceconnect.com/local-news/gov-kemp-celebrates-georgias-top-state-for-business-ranking-at-workforce-summit-9516843)  ·  `page_008396`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp delivered the keynote address at the second annual Governor's Workforce Summit.
- Georgia was named the No. 1 state for business for the 11th consecutive year by Area Development magazine.
- The announcement was made during the second annual Governor's Workforce Summit.
- Governor Kemp provided an update on the state of Georgia MATCH and other state efforts to strengthen workforce development.
- Governor Kemp put forth the goal of making Georgia the Top State for Talent.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated they are honored to accept the title of 'Top State for Business' for an unprecedented 11th year.
- The recognition underscores Georgia's pro-business approach to economic development creating opportunities for Georgians in every corner of the state.
- Governor Kemp thanked the state economic development team and their community partners across the state for securing the ranking.
- Georgia MATCH has already seen a notable increase in enrollment at both USG and TCSG institutions.
- Leaders from across the public and private sector remain engaged in solving the need for more workers.
- No other state has earned the distinction of Top State for Business for so many years.
- Area Development’s annual poll considers approximately 50 leading site consulting firms from across the U.S.
- The poll considers 14 different factors to make the determination for the Top State ranking.
- Georgia earned a No. 1 ranking for the 15th year in a row for its workforce development program, Georgia Quick Start.
- Governor Kemp hosted the second annual Governor's Workforce Summit at the Georgia World Congress Center.
- The summit brought together leaders from business, education, and public policy to align these sectors in preparing Georgians for jobs statewide.
- The highlight of last year's summit was the announcement of Georgia MATCH, the largest direct college admission program in the nation.
- Georgia MATCH sent a letter to every high school senior in the state letting them know there is a spot at USG and TCSG institutions.
- Following last year's rollout, both systems saw a preliminary year-over-year increase in enrollment this fall of 9.3% for TCSG and 6.2% for USG.
- The enrollment increases occurred despite challenges created by the federal government's mishandling of this year's FAFSA rollout.
- Dennis Shea, Publisher of Area Development, stated Georgia has annually maintained top 10 rankings in each survey subcategory for well over a decade.
- Survey subcategories include available workforce, cost of doing business, cooperative and responsive state government, energy availability and costs, logistics and infrastructure, and site readiness programs.
- Site selectors recognize that Georgia provides the whole package for companies choosing to grow or expand in the state.
- Dennis Shea congratulated Governor Kemp, the Georgia Legislature, Commissioner Pat Wilson, and the GDEcD team for maintaining the state’s No. 1 ranking.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated Georgia does economic development differently than anywhere else by prioritizing partnerships and investments in infrastructure, education, and workforce development.
- Pat Wilson noted this approach drives success for businesses and Georgians alike.
- Pat Wilson thanked all partners who worked together to achieve opportunities delivering long-term benefits to Georgia’s communities and industries.
- Governor Kemp announced the state sustained another year of economic development momentum in fiscal year 2024.
- Between July 1, 2023, and June 30, 2024, GDEcD’s Global Commerce team supported 429 facility expansions and new locations.
- These expansions and new locations totaled more than $20.3 billion in investment.
- The investments resulted in plans for 26,900 new jobs statewide.
