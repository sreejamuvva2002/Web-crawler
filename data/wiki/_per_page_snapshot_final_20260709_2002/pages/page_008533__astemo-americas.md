# Astemo Americas

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-02

**Source:** [Gov. Whitmer Welcomes New Regional Headquarters for Astemo Americas | Michigan Business](https://www.michiganbusiness.org/press-releases/2025/09/astemo-americas/)  ·  `page_008533`

## Facts

- Automotive supplier Astemo Ltd. is making a $95 million investment to create a regional headquarters in Wixom.
- The investment will create 200 jobs and retain 175 jobs.
- Governor Gretchen Whitmer celebrated the investment on Tuesday, September 2, 2025.
- Astemo will build a facility in Wixom to accommodate physical space needed to showcase cutting-edge capabilities to attract talent and increase collaboration with business units and customers.
- Astemo chose Michigan for its expansion over competing sites in Ohio.
- Kohei Takeuchi, President & CEO of Astemo Ltd., stated that as a global mega-supplier of automotive components and systems specializing in advanced mobility solutions like electric powertrains, chassis systems, and autonomous driving technologies, the investment cements presence in Michigan and enhances the state's standing as best-in-nation for the automotive industry.
- Oakland County Executive David Coulter stated that choosing Oakland County for the new headquarters and technical center in Wixom will support 350 people who will work at the supplier and boost the county's status as a prime location for advanced mobility technology.
- Astemo anticipates hiring over 150 engineer-type positions as part of the 200 jobs coming with the new regional site in Wixom.
- The company currently employs over 15,000 employees in North America and 80,000 employees globally.
- Astemo offers opportunities for training and/or upskilling through a tuition reimbursement program.
- Quentin L. Messer, Jr., CEO of the Michigan Economic Development Corporation (MEDC), stated that the investment expands Michigan's supply chain and talent capacity for mobility and advanced manufacturing, noting Team Michigan is deepening relationships in a globally competitive environment to execute the state's 'Make it in Michigan' economic development strategy.
- As part of the selection process, the City of Wixom offered a letter of support for the project.
- City Manager Steven Brown stated that Wixom has a long history as home to world-leading automotive suppliers and looks forward to building the state-of-the-art facility with Astemo Americas.
- This is the second time Governor Whitmer has led an investment mission in Japan, following a previous mission in September 2023 which was the first Michigan governor business visit to Japan since 2017.
