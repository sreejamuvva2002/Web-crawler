# Pirelli

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Pirelli to bring ‘cyber tire’ production to US plant | European Rubber Journal](https://www.european-rubber-journal.com/article/2099158/pirelli-to-begin-cyber-tire-production-to-us-plant)  ·  `page_048544`

## Facts

- Pirelli announced plans to start production of ‘cyber tire’ connected tires at its plant in Rome, Georgia.
- The announcement was made in a 6 May statement dated 7 May 2026.
- The Georgia facility already manufactures “the most technologically advanced products for the US market.”
- The plant will be further expanded to include production of tires equipped with cyber tire technology.
- Pirelli stated the move highlights the strategic importance of the US for its global growth roadmap and strengthens its integrated industrial and technological presence in the country.
- No details regarding planned investment levels or additional production capacity were disclosed, with further details to be communicated "in the coming months following the finalisation of the development plans."
- The announcement coincided with Pirelli’s participation in the SelectUSA Investment Summit in Washington, where the company showcased cyber tire as “a core innovation shaping the future of smart mobility.”
- According to Pirelli, the cyber tire is “the world’s first hardware-and-software system capable of collecting data and information from sensors embedded in tires.”
- The system processes data in real time and is claimed to enable “new functionalities integrated with driving and control systems to enhance the driving experience and increase safety levels.”
- Claudio Zanardo, CEO of Pirelli North America, commented on the decision.
- Zanardo described the start of cyber tire production in Georgia as “a significant milestone for Pirelli in this country.”
- He added it reflects their commitment to bringing advanced technologies closer to the market, further strengthening their industrial footprint and innovation capabilities in the US.
- As part of the development, Pirelli is introducing the latest version of its MIRS (modular integrated robotised system) manufacturing process at the Rome plant.
- The company described MIRS as “the most advanced manufacturing process for high-end, premium production within the entire Pirelli Group,” noting the technology will be exclusive to the Georgia facility.
- According to Pirelli, the ‘highly digitalised system’ enhances “robotised production capabilities,” improving productivity, flexibility, and quality.
- The process enables “a direct link between product design and its industrial application.”
- This will lay the groundwork for growth in Pirelli’s production capacity in Rome and further strengthen its position in the US, described as “one of its most important and strategic markets.”
- Operational since 2002, the facility in Georgia has a production capacity of 400,000 units per year.
- The facility manufactures 'high-value tires for the North American market.'
- The operations also include an R&D centre focused on technological development and product innovation.
