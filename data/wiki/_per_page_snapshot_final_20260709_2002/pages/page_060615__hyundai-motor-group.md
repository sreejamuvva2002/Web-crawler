# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-12-16

**Source:** [Hyundai is emerging as a dark horse in the US](https://electrek.co/2025/12/16/hyundai-emerging-dark-horse-us/)  ·  `page_060615`

## Facts

- Hyundai is chasing its fifth straight record sales year in the US and has bigger plans over the next few years.
- New US auto tariffs and an ICE raid on its battery plant in Georgia have not swayed Hyundai from its plans to play a bigger role in the market.
- Hyundai Motor’s CEO, Jose Munoz, stated during an interview with the Atlanta Business Chronicle that the company will increase production capacity in Georgia beyond what has already been announced.
- Hyundai confirmed it remains committed to a previously announced $26 billion investment to boost US sales over the next four years.
- Around $2.7 billion of the total investment will be funneled into Georgia, in addition to the $12.6 billion earmarked for the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America and other facilities.
- Hyundai plans to overcome US tariffs, which were recently lowered to 15% from 25%, by boosting local production, a strategy CEO Jose Munoz described as “very simple” and “localization.”
- The remarks follow an ICE raid in September that detained over 300 South Korean workers at the construction site for a new Hyundai and LG Energy Solutions battery plant.
- Hyundai’s investment in Georgia, the largest in state history, is expected to create around 40,000 jobs across manufacturing and battery plants, as well as all of its suppliers.
- As production ramps up at HMGMA, Hyundai will continue hiring until it reaches full capacity.
- Hyundai is working on multiple projects on different fronts, including robotics and AI.
- Although initial plans called for the plant to exclusively produce EVs, Hyundai is expected to begin building hybrids in 2026.
- Hyundai is taking actions to remain competitive and optimize costs by localizing both its production and its supply chain.
- Through the first 11 months of 2025, Hyundai sold nearly 823,000 vehicles in the US, up 8% from the same period last year, putting it on track for a fifth consecutive record retail sales year.
- Hyundai fully expects to achieve five consecutive record retail sales years (“5 for 5”) in 2025.
- Hyundai is compensating for the loss of the $7,500 federal tax credit with some of the largest discounts among US automakers.
- The IONIQ 5 electric SUV is among the most affordable EVs to lease in the US, starting at just $189 a month.
- Hyundai builds the IONIQ 5 and the three-row IONIQ 9 at the HMGMA facility in Georgia.
