# Fouts Bros. Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-09-26

**Source:** [Fouts Brothers to expand in Milledgeville and Baldwin County | The Union-Recorder](https://www.unionrecorder.com/2023/09/26/fouts-brothers-to-expand-in-milledgeville-and-baldwin-county/)  ·  `page_020047`

## Facts

- Fouts Bros. Inc. recently announced a local expansion in Milledgeville and Baldwin County that will bring new jobs and investment to the community.
- The company is elite when it comes to manufacturing fire and fire rescue trucks.
- When Scott Edens first came to Milledgeville, the company was building 150 trucks annually with a goal to double that number as soon as operations got underway.
- President and CEO Scott Edens moved the company headquarters from Smyrna to Milledgeville a few years ago to continue growing the business founded by his grandfather, C.J. Fouts, in 1952.
- The expansion includes $4 million in investment for this year and next.
- The company is adding 96 jobs in 2023 with a forecast to add 70 more in 2024.
- These employment figures will bring the company's total workforce up to nearly 500 employees, making it one of Baldwin County's top employers.
- The company is currently using only about half of the space at its facility, which was the former home of Rheem.
- Fouts Bros. has plans to build on the back 30 acres of the property due to business expansion.
- Executive Director Jonathan Jackson shared the expansion details during a DAMBC board meeting on Monday morning.
- Sharon Seymour, chairwoman of The Development Authority of Milledgeville and Baldwin County (DAMBC), stated the authority is glad Fouts Bros. chose their community as home and looks forward to working with them to provide good incomes for hundreds of employees.
- Jonathan Jackson noted that the company's hiring has been a challenge, but they found the local community better suited to handle it than their previous location in Smyrna.
- The facility site was previously classified as a Brownfield site requiring well monitoring and sub-surface ventilation due to Rheem's operations.
- Through cooperation between Rheem and Fouts Bros., groundwater monitoring and underground ventilation requirements were eliminated, and the site was removed from the Georgia Hazardous Sites list.
- Jackson described the environmental remediation as a textbook example of how Georgia’s Brownfield program works through the EPD, calling it a huge accomplishment.
- Fouts Bros. is continuing to perform necessary environmental work at the site.
- Employees at the facility are making good wages and doing well, according to Jackson.
- The company was searching for a building and site that could work for its operations and ultimately chose Milledgeville and Baldwin County due to local leadership, economic development efforts, and community members.
