# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-06-22

**Source:** [Money Talks: The Electric Vehicle Revolution Trumps Red State Politics - Infralog](https://www.infralog.in/money-talks-the-electric-vehicle-revolution-trumps-red-state-politics/)  ·  `page_050132`

## Facts

- Hyundai announced that its popular Ioniq 5 SUV will be the first electric vehicle to roll off the production line of its new $7.5 billion manufacturing campus in Georgia.
- The Ioniq series has been a hit in the US and globally, pulling the rug out from under EV market naysayers despite some glitches last year.
- EV sales began to pick up in January 2024 and momentum has continued.
- Hyundai stated in a June 18 press release that “The Hyundai IONIQ 5’s sales numbers prove its popularity.”
- The Ioniq 5 is Hyundai’s highest volume EV, with sales up 43% so far in 2024.
- Ioniq 5 sales increased by 82% in May alone according to the June 18 press release.
- In a June 18 announcement, Hyundai affirmed that its new Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America will host production of the 2025 Ioniq 5.
- The 2025 Ioniq 5 model has not yet had an official unveiling in North America.
- HMGMA is currently training and preparing to begin production in the fourth quarter of this year (2024).
- The new plant will produce all trim lines except IONIQ 5 N.
- The plant will eventually be the sole facility building IONIQ 5 models for the U.S. market.
- The Metaplant came to attention in 2022 as the nexus for Hyundai’s growing EV supply chain web in Georgia.
- Hyundai is investing $7.59 billion to develop the new campus located in Ellabell.
- The investment expects to create 8,500 new direct jobs.
- Hyundai and SK On are building a $5 billion EV battery cell plant in Bartow County, Georgia that will employ 3,500 workers.
- A Center for Automotive Research study calculated Hyundai’s electric vehicle investments in Georgia will create almost 40,000 jobs.
- The same study estimated an annual figure for individual earnings of $4.6 billion.
- The state of Georgia and local governments are on track to give $2.1 billion in tax breaks and other incentives to Hyundai Motor Group.
- This follows an announcement that Hyundai will invest an additional $2 billion at its EV complex in Georgia.
- Projected incentives will rise by more than $290 million from the originally announced $1.8 billion deal last year.
- Only $2.75 million of the incentive increase represents additional cash from the state; the rest comes from increases in tax breaks.
- Georgia has a longstanding school-to-factory worker training and hiring program attractive to manufacturers.
- Georgia is a “right to work” state under regulations passed in 1947, making it difficult for workers to unionize.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) states the state is rapidly developing as the capital of EV manufacturing for the nation.
- GDEcD notes Kia’s facility in West Point will begin making the company’s new EV9 model this year.
- Rivian announced plans for a $5 billion factory in Georgia back in 2021 but switched focus to Illinois after site preparation, though characterized as temporary.
- GDEcD is targeting the whole EV ecosystem, noting expansion across cathode and anode manufacturing, chemical and mineral solutions, battery manufacturing, metals and aluminum manufacturing, and battery recycling.
- Energy storage startup Freyr Battery Inc. announced relocating its home base back to the US from Europe earlier this year.
- Freyr cited global tax developments and U.S. incentive programs for battery manufacturers as a key advantage of the move.
- The redomiciling will help facilitate construction of Freyr’s new Giga America factory in Coweta County, Georgia.
- Freyr announced the site selection for Giga America in 2022.
- The initial phase of Giga America is planned to be a cell production module of approximately 34 GWh based on the next-generation of 24M’s U.S.-based SemiSolidTM production platform.
- Initial projected capital cost for the Giga America phase is $1.7 billion.
- Freyr name-checked the Inflation Reduction Act as a key element supporting the project.
- Freyr expects U.S. demand for ESS, passenger EV and other electric mobility applications to grow rapidly over the next decade due to the IRA.
- The total financial incentive package for Giga America includes assistance from the State of Georgia and Coweta County plus expected production tax credits from the IRA.
- Freyr indicated in a corporate update earlier this year that plans for the Giga America battery factory are moving forward.
