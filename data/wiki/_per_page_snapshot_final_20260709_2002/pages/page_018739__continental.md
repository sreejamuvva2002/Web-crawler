# Continental

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2025-06-03

**Source:** [Continental presents temperature sensor for electric motor rotors - electrive.com](https://www.electrive.com/2025/06/03/continental-presents-temperature-sensor-for-electric-motor-rotors/)  ·  `page_018739`

## Facts

- Continental presents the electric motor rotor temperature sensor (eRTS).
- The eRTS delivers significantly more precise measurement results than current software-based temperature simulation.
- The tolerance range for temperature measurement is reduced from 15 to just three degrees Celsius.
- The eRTS and its data can help vehicle manufacturers reduce the amount of rare earths used in electric motors.
- Rare earths are currently used to make magnets inside PSM rotors more heat-resistant.
- Precisely measured temperatures allow rare earths to be dosed more accurately during development.
- The cooling system can be controlled more precisely and purposefully during operation using the sensor data.
- Utilizing the limits of the previously generous tolerance range can increase performance.
- Rotors can reach temperatures of up to 150 degrees Celsius under full load and require cooling to prevent permanent magnets from demagnetizing.
- Current solutions calculate rotor temperature based on stator temperature sensor data, phase current measurements, and environmental variables rather than measuring directly.
- The two-part eRTS consists of a small sensor called 'Mote' located near the magnets in the rotor.
- The second part is a receiver unit called 'Transducer' located outside the motor.
- The transducer is connected to the inverter controller and transmits temperature measurement data via a communication interface.
- Bin Huo, Head of the Passive Safety and Sensorics (PSS) segment in Continental’s Automotive division, states that less resource consumption and lower costs make eRTS sensor technology advantageous over current solutions.
- Christoph Busch, Head of the E-Mobility Sensors Product Centre, says the center aims to increase efficiency and sustainability in electric vehicles.
- Reducing rare earth use contributes to a more sustainable supply chain as EV numbers are expected to greatly increase in coming years and decades.
- In combination with other sensor technologies like the e-Motor Rotor Position Sensor, the eRTS can act as a system solution to create synergies that save car manufacturers money and effort.
- Continental plans to successively expand its EV sensor portfolio.
