# Erdrich USA Inc.

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2018-10-03

**Source:** [New feeder improves stamper’s forming efficiency](https://www.thefabricator.com/thefabricator/article/bending/new-feeder-improves-stamper-s-forming-efficiency)  ·  `page_019972`

## Facts

- Erdrich Metal Forming Technology is headquartered in Germany and was founded 50 years ago producing stamping tools.
- The company grew to mass-produce stamped and bent parts and developed considerable expertise in deep drawing, flow forming of thick sheets, and fineblanking.
- Erdrich uses a stamping technology that combines fineblanking and forming in a single press.
- The manufacturer expanded from a small craftsman’s shop to a global player with facilities in China, Germany, the Czech Republic, and the U.S.
- Erdrich USA Inc. began production in Dublin, Ga., in 2013.
- The Dublin facility stamps components for the automotive industry, ranging from large chassis parts to housings for electronic devices.
- COO Alexander Kehrer stated that most cars on the streets are made with some of their parts.
- The plant’s portfolio includes very slim aluminum covers for electronic boxes and robust, thick-gauge steel components for wheel axles.
- The plant’s daily output is roughly 30,000 metal parts.
- Most parts go to clients in the U.S. and Mexico, but also overseas.
- Components must be engineered precisely to deliver safe and accurately finished products.
- Coils weigh up to 10 tons each and require precise handling to ensure efficient processes.
- The company relies on levelers to flatten advanced high-strength steel (AHSS) before stamping it.
- Sheet thicknesses range from 0.06 to 0.31 inch (1.5 to 8 millimeters).
- Erdrich revamped its coil feeding lines to boost efficiency due to a highly competitive environment and mounting cost pressures.
- Previously, the company operated with coil lines from an overseas supplier, which caused operational vulnerabilities during increased customer demand due to time zone differences, time lags, language barriers, and additional travel costs for support.
- COO Alexander Kehrer emphasized the need for on-site professional maintenance technicians for normal operational issues rather than just warranty issues.
- In early 2017, Erdrich USA purchased an ARKU coil feeding line that includes a CoilMaster® 7050/17 precision leveler EcoPlan® and a zig-zag servo feeder.
- Key factors for choosing the equipment were the manufacturer's nearby U.S. service office, strong connection to the service partner, user-friendly equipment, and state-of-the-art engineering.
- Kehrer noted that calling the service partner results in a technician arriving the next day due to the stateside service center.
- Good pricing was offered because service could be provided through the U.S. office.
- A special zig-zag feeder stamps metal parts in a zig-zag pattern to keep material waste to a minimum, which Kehrer identified as key to enhancing production efficiency.
- The coil feeder and leveler implementation was completed in May 2017.
- Installation took only four weeks, allowing operations to resume earlier than planned and save valuable production time.
- The line features ARKU’s EcoPlan energy-efficient drive, which delivers up to 30 percent more performance with the same electrical power requirements.
- The line’s material transport system prevents rapid wear of the leveling rollers while also protecting the strip material.
- The machine is equipped with process visualizations on the control panel to simplify control.
- The software menu structure is similar to that used on personal computers, making it intuitive for use, especially during coil changes.
- The user-friendliness of the system reduces training requirements for new employees.
