# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant Georgia

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09

**Source:** [HSI says immigration raid at Georgia site was largest in agency history](https://www.ajc.com/business/2025/09/475-arrested-in-unprecedent-immigration-raid-at-georgias-hyundai-ev-site/)  ·  `page_009358`

## Facts

- Federal officials detained 475 people at a construction site on the Hyundai Metaplant campus near Savannah during a Thursday operation.
- The operation was described as the largest single immigration raid in the history of Homeland Security Investigations (HSI).
- Steven Schrank, special agent in charge of HSI Atlanta, stated the arrests stemmed from a monthslong investigation into alleged undocumented and illegal work at a battery plant under construction.
- The battery factory is part of Hyundai Motor Group’s 3,000-acre Metaplant site, a $12.6 billion project.
- Georgia leaders have touted the project as the largest economic development deal in state history.
- HSI was founded in 2010 as a division within Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE).
