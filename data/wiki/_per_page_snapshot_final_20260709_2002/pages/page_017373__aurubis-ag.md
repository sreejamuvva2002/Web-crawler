# Aurubis AG

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2023-03-27

**Source:** [Press release: Aurubis and SMS group forge ahead with the first multimetal recycling ... | Presseportal](https://www.presseportal.de/en/pm/109932/5472788)  ·  `page_017373`

## Facts

- Aurubis AG and SMS group GmbH signed a supply contract for a second plant module at the new Aurubis Richmond recycling plant in Georgia.
- The signing occurred last Friday relative to March 27, 2023, as part of a strategic partnership in the global multimetal recycling sector.
- A modular system design allows for flexible operations and quick growth in the dynamic US recycling market.
- Aurubis is considerably accelerating its growth course in the future recycling and circular economy market.
- The additional equipment will increase the capacity of the metal recycling plant in Richmond from an annual 90,000 tons to 180,000 tons of complex metal scrap materials.
- The Richmond plant is currently under construction.
- Aurubis CEO Roland Harings stated the expansion delivers on the company's 'Driving Sustainable Growth' strategy while keeping strategically important and high-demand mineral raw materials in the resource cycle.
- SMS group recycling equipment will be installed in the second module of the new US plant.
- The core technology for the second module is the top-blown rotary converter (TBRC).
- TBRC is state-of-the-art equipment used for processing complex recycling materials to recover copper, nickel, tin, precious metals, and platinum group metals in downstream stages.
- Recovered metals are described as crucial raw materials for energy transition, sustainable mobility, and digitalization technologies.
- Aurubis CEO Roland Harings noted that bundling SMS group's plant know-how with Aurubis' expertise creates an environment for highly efficient metal recovery.
- Michael Rzepczyk, a member of the SMS Executive Board, stated the partnership helps Aurubis assume a leading role in multimetal recycling in the US and takes recycling to the next level by doubling production volume.
- The modular technology allows additional components to be added later to expand the plant to meet future requirements flexibly.
- Aurubis is investing about €640 million for both phases of the project to build the first secondary smelter specializing in multimetal recycling in the US.
- Groundbreaking for the Aurubis Richmond plant took place in mid-2022.
- Stage one of the plant is scheduled to go online in the first half of 2024.
- The TBRC for the second stage is anticipated to start production in 2026.
- Aurubis currently processes roughly 1 million tons of recycling material annually globally, and the Richmond plant will add another 180,000 tons.
- The new Georgia plant will comply with the highest environmental standards in the US.
- The project is expected to create around 200 new jobs.
