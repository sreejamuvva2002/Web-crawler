# Rivian Georgia EV Manufacturing Plant

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Rivian and DOE Finalize $6.6 Billion Loan for Georgia EV Manufacturing Plant](https://www.constructconnect.com/construction-economic-news/rivian-and-doe-finalize-6.6-billion-loan-for-georgia-ev-manufacturing-plant)  ·  `page_000741`

## Facts

- Rivian finalized a $6.6 billion loan from the US Department of Energy (DOE) to construct an electric vehicle manufacturing facility in Stanton Springs North, near the City of Social Circle, Georgia.
- The 9-million-square-foot manufacturing plant will be located about an hour from downtown Atlanta.
- The facility will create over 7,500 jobs.
- The plant will produce Rivian’s R2 SUV and R3 crossover.
- Construction of the manufacturing facility is expected to start in 2026.
- Vehicle production at the facility is expected to begin by 2028.
- The facility will feature advanced manufacturing techniques and sustainable design.
- The $6.6 billion loan will be divided into two construction phases: the first phase will amount to $3.4 billion, and the second phase to $2.6 billion.
- The remainder of the $6.6 billion loan is capitalized interest.
- Rivian Founder and CEO RJ Scaringe stated that the loan will help accelerate the launch of the Georgia plant for the R2 and R3 models, providing thousands of jobs in the state.
- RJ Scaringe added that additional capacity for mass market products is key to U.S. leadership in the electric vehicle industry.
- Rivian and the DOE worked together closely for over two years to finalize the loan.
- The Rivian R2 is an all-electric, 5-seat SUV with an estimated range of 300 miles.
- Rivian sells its vehicles directly to consumer and commercial customers.
- Rivian's first manufacturing facility is located in Normal, Illinois.
- Rivian's headquarters in Irvine, California, manages its engineering and design, electronics, software, propulsion, battery systems, supply chain, and logistics.
