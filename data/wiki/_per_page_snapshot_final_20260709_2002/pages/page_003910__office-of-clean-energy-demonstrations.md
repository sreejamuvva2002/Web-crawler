# Office of Clean Energy Demonstrations

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [Apply for Funding | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/cmei/oced/apply-funding)  ·  `page_003910`

## Facts

- Applying to a Notice of Funding Opportunity (NOFO) from the Office of Clean Energy Demonstrations (OCED) can take several weeks.
- Applicants must review the relevant Funding Notice for all submission deadlines and resources.
- Applications must be submitted via the OCED eXCHANGE unless stated otherwise in the NOFO.
- Questions regarding a specific NOFO should be sent to the email listed on the Funding Notice page or within the NOFO itself.
- The OCED website provides comprehensive information on funding opportunities, mission, programs, news, project portfolio, leadership team, and career opportunities.
- Interested applicants may view and download OCED Funding Opportunity Announcements on OCED eXCHANGE.
- Applicants must register with required systems to apply to a NOFO, and high demand may cause longer than expected wait times for registration.
- Applicants should start and complete the registration processes as early as possible due to potential delays.
- Applicants must register with and submit NOFO application materials through OCED eXCHANGE, which is OCED’s online application portal.
- Detailed instructions on how to apply are available at Financial Opportunities: Manuals (energy.gov).
- The “Apply” and “Submit” buttons on the application portal will be automatically disabled after the listed submission deadlines.
- Applicants experiencing technical difficulties with a submission should contact the OCED eXCHANGE helpdesk for assistance.
- Each applicant must register in the System for Award Management (SAM) before submitting an application, unless excepted under 2 CFR § 25.110.
- Designating an Electronic Business Point of Contact is a required step in SAM registration.
- Applicants must provide a valid unique entity identifier (UEI) number in the application.
- Applicants must maintain an active SAM registration with up-to-date information while holding an active federal award, application, or plan under consideration.
- DOE will not make a federal award until the applicant complies with all applicable UEI and SAM requirements.
- If an applicant fails to fully comply with UEI and SAM requirements by the time DOE is ready to make an award, DOE will determine the applicant is not qualified to receive it.
- Entities should start the UEI and SAM registration process as soon as possible due to potential delays.
- Technical difficulties with UEI validation or SAM registration can be addressed using the Help feature on SAM.gov.
- Additional entity validation resources can be found at GSAFSD Tier 0 Knowledge Base - Validating your Entity.
- To receive email updates on NOFO amendments, applicants must register with Grants.gov.
- Applicants should click the “register” link in the top right corner of the grants.gov page and then the “get registered now” button to complete registration.
- Applicants must register in FedConnect to apply.
- Some NOFOs require applicants to submit a letter of intent or concept paper before submitting the full application.
- Application submission deadlines are located on the cover page of each NOFO.
- All letters of intent, concept papers, and applications must be submitted via OCED eXCHANGE unless stated otherwise in the NOFO.
- If there are inconsistencies between the NOFO and non-legally binding DOE communications like presentations or staff statements, the NOFO takes precedence.
- Applicants may submit questions via email to the address identified in the NOFO Questions/Agency Contacts section.
- The Standard Form SF-424 is the government-wide standard form for grant application packages and requires basic applicant information including name, address, telephone number, and type of applicant.
- The SF-424 requires a list of sources of proposed funding and a description of the proposed project.
- Applicants must provide a Budget and a Budget Justification Workbook with their application.
- For any subaward listed in the application, a separate Budget Justification Workbook must be provided for each subaward.
- Applicants must use the Standard Form SF-424-A BUDGET INFORMATION - Non-Construction Programs to submit their budget.
