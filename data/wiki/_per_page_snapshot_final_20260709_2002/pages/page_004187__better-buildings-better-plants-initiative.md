# Better Buildings & Better Plants Initiative

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Highlights from AEE World 2023 | Better Buildings & Better Plants Initiative](https://betterbuildingssolutioncenter.energy.gov/beat-blog/highlights-aee-world-2023)  ·  `page_004187`

## Facts

- More than 1,600 industry professionals convened at AEE World 2023 in Orlando, Florida.
- Better Plants was a gold-level sponsor of the event and had its own track dedicated to highlighting partner success and solutions.
- Former Vice President Al Gore participates in a Q&A session during the opening plenary.
- DOE’s Better Plants team hosted three technical sessions covering energy efficiency and energy management, industrial electrification, and low-carbon fuels, feedstocks, and energy sources.
- Partners who shared strategies and experiences included Eastman Chemical Co., GE Aerospace, Pactiv Evergreen, Volvo Group North America, Whirlpool, RTX, Nestlé, and the City of Fort Wayne, Indiana.
- DOE recognized Cleveland-Cliffs, Oshkosh Corporation, and Volvo Group North America for achieving their energy efficiency and GHG emissions reduction goals at the Better Plants luncheon.
- Maria T. Vargas, director of the Better Buildings & Better Plants Initiative, discussed critical pathways for reducing carbon emissions and advancing electrification.
- Anne Hampson, acting deputy director of DOE’s Industrial Efficiency & Decarbonization Office, joined a panel discussion to share insights on public-private collaboration and how DOE builds partnerships to drive a clean energy future.
- Better Plants is a voluntary partnership initiative that succeeds because partners eagerly step up to share solutions at events like AEE World.
- Upcoming events include Technology Days on February 7-8, 2024 at Oak Ridge National Laboratory and the 2024 Better Buildings & Better Plants Summit on April 2-4 in Washington, D.C.
