# Right to Work Georgia Business Investments

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Businesses Are Investing in Right to Work Georgia - NRTWC](https://nrtwc.org/businesses-are-investing-in-right-to-work-georgia/)  ·  `page_001332`

## Facts

- Seoyon E-HWA is investing $75 million to establish a new facility in Chatham County, Georgia.
- Seoyon E-HWA will create 500 new jobs.
- Seoyon E-HWA is an automobile manufacturer operating in various countries such as Korea, Europe, and India.
- Seoyon E-HWA will supply electric vehicle parts in Chatham County, Georgia.
- Seoyon E-HWA aims to contribute to making Savannah a central city for automobile development and production through continuous cooperation with the local government.
- This investment marks Seoyon's second Georgia facility, committing to nearly doubling the number of jobs it supports in the state and extending its partnership with the business community.
- Seoyon E-HWA will be located in the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center, ideally positioned close to Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America, the Port of Savannah, Interstates I-16 and I-95, and more.
- Green Georgia is investing $59 million to establish its headquarters in Thomaston, Georgia.
- Green Georgia will create 170 new jobs in Upson County.
- Green Georgia is an eco-friendly building solutions company that aims to transform the way buildings are constructed today.
- Green Georgia LLC’s eco-friendly building materials are designed to help businesses grow while reducing their impact on the environment.
- Upson County has successfully grown its manufacturing industry over the last few years.
- SK Battery America is investing $19 million to add a new location in Roswell, Georgia.
- SK Battery America will create 200 new jobs.
- In 2019, SK On established SK Battery America as its U.S. production base.
- The new SK Battery America location will serve as an IT regional center.
- Collectively, these three investments will result in 870 new jobs in Georgia.
