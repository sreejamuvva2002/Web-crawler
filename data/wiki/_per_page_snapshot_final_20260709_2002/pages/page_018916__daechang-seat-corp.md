## Facts

- South Korean auto parts maker Daechang Seat Corp. plans a $72.5 million plant near a new Hyundai facility in Georgia, hiring more than 500 people.
- The company makes automotive seat frames and will open a factory in Savannah to supply a new Hyundai Motor Group plant.
- Daechang Seat Corp. is based in Gyeongju, South Korea.
- The new Savannah plant investment totals $72.5 million.
- The Savannah facility will hire more than 500 people.
- The Savannah plant will supply Hyundai's $7.6 billion plant to assemble electric vehicles and batteries in Ellabell, near Savannah.
- Hyundai's Ellabell plant was announced last year (relative to October 2023).
- Hyundai's Ellabell plant could grow to 8,500 employees.
- Hyundai's Ellabell plant could begin producing vehicles as early as 2024.
- Suppliers to the Hyundai plant have collectively pledged to invest nearly $2.3 billion.
- Suppliers to the Hyundai plant have collectively pledged to hire nearly 5,800 people.
- Daechang Seat opened a factory in Phenix City, Alabama, in 2019.
- The company announced a $23.4 million expansion in Phenix City last year (relative to October 2023).
- The Phenix City expansion will include building a new facility.
- The Phenix City expansion will hire an additional 300 people.
- Daechang Seat employs more than 5,000 people across seven countries.
- Jinsuk Lee serves as the CEO of Daechang Seat Savannah Corp.
- Jinsuk Lee stated: "Our primary objective is to drive the growth of DSC through facility expansions, ultimately transforming DSC into a global entity."
- Workers at the new Georgia plant will make an average wage of more than $18 an hour, excluding bonuses and benefits.
- Angela Hendrix serves as a spokesperson for the Savannah Economic Development Authority.
- Angela Hendrix confirmed the average worker wage details for the facility.
- The state of Georgia will pay to train workers for the new plant.
- Daechang Seat could qualify for $10 million in state income tax credits.
- The state income tax credits are valued at $4,000 per job over five years.
- Workers must earn at least $31,300 a year to qualify for the $10 million tax credits.
- Local officials have granted a 15-year property tax reduction projected to save the company $5.8 million.
- Angela Hendrix confirmed the details of the local property tax reduction.
- Local officials are selling land for the plant at a reduced rate.
