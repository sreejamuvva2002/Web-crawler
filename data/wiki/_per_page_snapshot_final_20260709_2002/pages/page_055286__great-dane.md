# Great Dane

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Great Dane Trailers - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Dane_Trailers)  ·  `page_055286`

## Facts

- Founded in 1900 by J.P. Wheless and T.H. McMillan as the Savannah Blowpipe Company in Savannah, Georgia.
- Headquarters is located in Chicago, Illinois, with an additional corporate office in Savannah, Georgia.
- Manufactures dry van, refrigerated van, flatbed semi-trailers, dry freight, and refrigerated truck bodies.
- Early Great Dane trailers built by the Steel Products Company were 16- to 20-foot-long flatbed trailers with single axles and payload capacities of three to six tons.
- By the mid-1950s, the company had sales outlets throughout the Eastern United States located in 31 cities across 18 states.
- In 1972, purchased an all-platform trailer manufacturing location in Memphis, Tennessee from Arrow Trailer.
- In 1974, built a manufacturing plant in Brazil, Indiana that originally focused on vans and the western/mid-western business spheres.
- Completed a 10,000 square foot Savannah Engineering Research Lab for testing design and construction, which allowed the facility to double production.
- In 1982, installed computer-controlled road simulation equipment in the Research and Development Lab in Savannah, Georgia.
- The Savannah R&D lab's computer-controlled road simulation equipment is the only one of its kind still in operation.
- In 1988, acquired the SuperSeal reefer production line and plant in Wayne, Nebraska.
- In 1996, opened a dry van plant in Terre Haute, Indiana along with an adjacent parts distribution center.
- The merger of Great Dane and Pines Trailer created the Great Dane Limited Partnership.
- In 2002, purchased Strick's Eastern Van manufacturing facilities in Danville, Pennsylvania and Trailmobile manufacturing facilities in Jonesboro, Arkansas.
- In 2004, acquired a production facility in Huntsville, Tennessee from Wabash National Corporation.
- The Memphis, Tennessee plant closed in 2005.
- In 2006, produced 60,000 trailers across nine different plants.
- In January 2009, closed its original manufacturing plant in Savannah, Georgia that had been open since 1919 due to the economic impact of the recession.
- In 2010, opened a new reefer plant in Statesboro, Georgia to expand manufacturing.
- In 2010, acquired Johnson Refrigerated Truck Bodies.
- In 2011, president and COO Phil Pines retired after more than 50 years of service and was replaced by Dean Engelage.
- The company officially changed its name from Great Dane Trailers to simply Great Dane in 2011.
- In 2016, introduced two new truck body designs: The Alpine and The Sahara.
- In 2016, opened a new plant in Elysburg, Pennsylvania for dry van manufacturing.
- Branch locations include Atlanta, Georgia; Birmingham, Alabama; Charlotte, North Carolina; Dallas, Texas; Columbus, Ohio; Houston, Texas; Indianapolis, Indiana; Jacksonville, Florida; Knoxville, Tennessee; Little Rock, Arkansas; Memphis, Tennessee; Miami, Florida; Mount Joy, Pennsylvania; Nashville, Tennessee; Philadelphia, Pennsylvania; Richmond, Virginia; Springdale, Arkansas; and Tampa, Florida.
- Products and parts are sold at certified dealerships across the United States, Canada, Chile, Colombia, Mexico, and Peru.
- The SSL dry freight van features a patented design with steel-lined interior walls.
- In 2006, over 20,000 SSL model trailers were sold, accounting for nearly 15 percent of the industry's total shipments that year.
- Manufacturing processes utilize high-tensile steel, aluminum, FRP panels, robotics for welding operations, and plasma arc cutting.
