# LG Electronics Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** power_electronics  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [MI: Penske Logistics, LG Electronics, Lear Corp. Invest $158M, Hiring 1,130 | Trade and Industry Development](https://www.tradeandindustrydev.com/industry/news/mi-penske-logistics-lg-electronics-lear-corp-13162)  ·  `page_056969`

## Facts

- Penske Logistics, LG Electronics and Lear Corporation will collectively make $158.8-million capital investments and create 1,130 jobs.
- The projects are among a range of projects approved by the Michigan Strategic Fund (MSF) that incentivized total capital investment of $595 million and 1,500 new jobs.
- Jeff Mason, CEO of the Michigan Economic Development Corporation (MEDC), stated that the new investments underscore Michigan’s expanding economy and appeal for logistics capacity, automotive industry, and autonomous vehicle potential.
- Penske Logistics will receive a $2.5-million Michigan Business Development Program performance-based grant as part of its $98.6-million investment into a 606,000-square-foot storage and distribution center.
- The Penske Logistics project will create 403 jobs in Romulus.
- Penske Logistics operates more than 360 locations across North America, South America, Europe, and Asia, offering warehousing, transportation management, lead logistics, and freight brokerage.
- Doug Studley, director of operations at Penske Logistics, credited the MEDC, city of Romulus, InSite Real Estate, and Bonner Advisory Group for making the project possible.
- The MSF grant played a vital role in attracting Penske Logistics to Michigan over Indiana.
- Wayne County Executive Warren C. Evans noted that the project aligns with the Aerotropolis, a seven-community, two-county public-private partnership focused on corporate expansion around Detroit Metropolitan Airport and Willow Run Airport.
- LG Electronics Inc. will establish a 250,000-square-foot factory for advanced electric vehicle (EV) components in Hazel Park.
- The LG Electronics plant will produce power electronics subassemblies for EVs starting in 2018.
- LG Electronics' $25-million private capital investment is supported by a $2.9-million Michigan Business Development Program grant.
- Ken Chang, LG Electronics USA senior vice president and head of the LG Vehicle Components North American Business Center, stated that producing world-class EV subassemblies in the U.S. is a key pillar of their strategy to partner with U.S. automakers.
- The LG project will create at least 292 jobs, including 137 factory workers in Hazel Park and 155 engineers and technicians at the expanded LG R&D Center in Troy.
- LG Electronics USA operates four business units: Home Entertainment, Mobile Communications, Home Appliance & Air Solutions, and Vehicle Components.
- The Vehicle Components unit collaborates with General Motors on the Chevrolet Bolt EV.
- Oakland County Executive L. Brooks Patterson highlighted LG's continued growth in Oakland County and praised the partnership between the county, MEDC, Troy, and Hazel Park for facilitating business expansions.
- Southfield-based Lear Corporation will construct a 160,000-square-foot building at the location of the former administration building at Buick City, north of downtown Flint.
- The MSF board approved a $4.35-million Michigan Business Development Program performance-based grant to support Lear’s $29.3-million capital investment.
- Lear's project includes the subassembly and final assembly of automotive seats and will create 435 new jobs in the state.
- Ray Scott, Lear executive vice president and president of Seating Operations, stated the expansion reflects a positive business outlook and contributes to Flint's revitalization.
- The city of Flint offered a tax abatement to reduce overall project costs for Lear.
- Michigan was selected over competing sites in the United States and Mexico for Lear's investment.
- Lear aims to begin construction at the site in the fall with operations starting next spring, marking the return of auto manufacturing to the location since 1999.
- Flint Mayor Karen Weaver stated the project will provide hundreds of hard-working Flint residents with good-paying jobs within the city limits and boost the local economy.
- Tim Herman, CEO of the Flint & Genesee Chamber of Commerce, noted that Lear's presence continues the region's rich automotive history and pledged chamber support for the company's success.
- The Michigan Economic Development Corporation (MEDC) serves as the state’s marketing arm and lead advocate for business development, job awareness, and community talent development.
