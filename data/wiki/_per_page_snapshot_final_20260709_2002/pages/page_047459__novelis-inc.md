# Novelis Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-24

**Source:** [Novelis Releases 2025 Sustainability Report :: Novelis Inc.](https://investors.novelis.com/news-events/press-releases/detail/1405/novelis-releases-2025-sustainability-report)  ·  `page_047459`

## Facts

- Novelis Inc. released its sustainability report for the 2025 fiscal year, which covers April 1, 2024, to March 31, 2025.
- The company achieved a 10% reduction in greenhouse gas intensity (Scope 1, location-based Scope 2 & Scope 3, categories 1 and 4) during the last fiscal year.
- Novelis implemented new technologies in remelt furnaces to improve efficiency, reduce fuel and energy use, and increase the use of low-carbon primary aluminum inputs.
- The company recycled more than 84 billion used beverage cans.
- Novelis products contain a 63% average recycled content across all product lines.
- The company achieved a 12% reduction in waste to landfill intensity.
- Novelis recorded an 8% reduction in water intensity.
- The company donated $8.8 million to communities where its employees work and live.
- Novelis is pursuing its 'Novelis 3x30' vision, which includes accelerating circularity to achieve 75% average recycled content in products.
- A key 3x30 objective is to become the lowest emissions flat rolled products (FRP) aluminum provider with less than 3 tonnes of CO2e per tonne of FRP shipped.
- Francisco Pires, SVP and Chief Strategy and Sustainability Officer, stated that designing out waste, keeping materials in use, and lowering emissions across the value chain delivers long-term value for customers, communities, and the planet.
- Novelis serves customers in the aerospace, automotive, beverage can, and specialties industries throughout North America, Europe, Asia, and South America.
- The company aims to achieve a fully circular economy by partnering with suppliers and customers globally.
- Novelis reported net sales of $17.1 billion for fiscal year 2025.
- Novelis Inc. is a subsidiary of Hindalco Industries Limited, which is the metals flagship company of the Aditya Birla Group, a multinational conglomerate based in Mumbai.
