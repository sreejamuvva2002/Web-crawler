# Rivian

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-04

**Source:** [Rivian Just Raised Its 2026 Outlook While Tesla Stock Stumbled. Is the Electric Vehicle Underdog Finally a Buy? | The Motley Fool](https://www.fool.com/investing/2026/07/04/rivian-just-raised-its-2026-outlook-while-tesla-st/)  ·  `page_059063`

## Facts

- Rivian delivered 12,194 vehicles in the second quarter, exceeding its own Q2 outlook of 9,000 to 11,000.
- Rivian raised its full-year delivery target to 65,000 to 70,000 vehicles, up from a previous range of 62,000 to 67,000.
- Rivian delivered 10,365 vehicles in the first quarter, bringing total first-half deliveries to 22,559.
- Reaching the low end of the new full-year delivery range requires approximately 42,000 deliveries in the second half, nearly doubling the first-half pace.
- The company is simultaneously scaling an entirely new R2 model alongside this steep second-half delivery ramp.
- Rivian's R2 is a new, lower-priced vehicle designed for volume sales, distinct from its premium-priced R1 trucks and SUVs.
- In the first quarter, Rivian's revenue rose 11% year over year to $1.38 billion.
- Rivian generated $119 million in gross profit in the first quarter, representing a 9% gross margin.
- The software and services segment produced $181 million in gross profit in the first quarter.
- The automotive segment ran a $62 million gross loss in the first quarter, primarily due to a $100 million year-over-year decline in sales of automotive regulatory credits and lower production volumes.
- Rivian's first-quarter operating loss widened to $881 million from $655 million a year earlier.
- Rivian raised the initial production capacity planned for its Georgia plant by 50%, to 300,000 vehicles annually.
- The Georgia plant project is backed by an up to $4.5 billion Department of Energy loan.
- A completed testing milestone in March unlocked a $1 billion investment from Volkswagen Group.
- Rivian currently loses money on every vehicle it sells, with automotive gross profit expected to improve as R2 volumes build.
- The second-quarter financial report is due July 30 and will serve as the first checkpoint for automotive gross profit improvement.
