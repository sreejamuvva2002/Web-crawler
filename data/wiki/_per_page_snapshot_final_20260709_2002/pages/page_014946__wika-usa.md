# WIKA USA

## Facts

- WIKA USA’s relationship with one of the largest energy providers in the country has grown from gas analyzers to a future calibration lab for SF6 testing equipment.
- A major utility company in the South was seeking a reliable vendor for gas analyzers.
- The utility's parent company already had an excellent relationship with WEgrid Solutions, a wholly owned WIKA subsidiary.
- The utility uses WIKA's full-trailer solution: GPU-S-3000 gas cart, adapters, gas analyzers, hoses, and leak detectors.
- Utility executives recommended that the utility reach out to WIKA regarding its issues.
- WEgrid proposed stocking spare parts for the GA11 gas analyzer for as long as the utility uses this model.
- WEgrid proposed giving plenty of advance notice before upgrading the technology.
- WEgrid proposed that existing customers will receive preferential pricing when a new model is introduced.
- The electric utility company switched to WEgrid in November 2019.
- The GA11 and GIR-10 leak detector satisfied all of the customer’s requirements for performance.
- WEgrid Services provided maintenance, repairs, and spare parts as necessary.
- WIKA USA’s Lawrenceville (Georgia) headquarters rearranged its production floor to accommodate a large space for calibrating and repairing SF6 testing equipment.
- The state-of-the-art calibration facility is expected to be operational in the near future.
- The parent company previously had to send its SF6 testing equipment to Europe for calibration, which takes four to six weeks.
- Having a lab nearby would greatly reduce downtime.
- WEgrid’s partnership with this major utility holding company is based on high-quality SF6 gas equipment and superior after-sale support.
- Some of the country’s largest electric utilities rely on WEgrid Solutions for quality SF6 equipment and trusted service.
- The new US-based calibration lab will cut turnaround time in half compared to sending equipment to Europe.
