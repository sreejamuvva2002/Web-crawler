# Yamaha Golf-Car Company

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** workforce_training  ·  **Published:** 2020-09-24

**Source:** [Press Release:Interior Secretary David Bernhardt Meets with Yamaha Golf-Car Company President Tom McDonald](https://whyyamaha.com/pressroom/press-release/president-tom-mcdonald-meets-with-secretary-of-the-interior-david-bernhardt/)  ·  `page_015750`

## Facts

- On September 24, 2020, Interior Secretary David Bernhardt met with Yamaha Golf-Car Company President Tom McDonald at the Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation facility in Newnan, Georgia.
- Mike Chrzanowski, President of Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation, and Ben Speciale, President of Yamaha U.S. Marine Business Unit, signed the President’s Pledge to America’s Workers during the event.
- The signing reinforces Yamaha’s commitment to workforce development within its U.S. manufacturing facilities.
- Tom McDonald stated that jobs created through Yamaha facilities in Georgia make a positive impact on the state’s economy and contribute to the growth of the outdoor industry overall.
- The golf industry supported $191.9 billion in total annual activity in 2016, including 1.9 million jobs and 58.7 billion in wages and benefits.
- Yamaha reported record golf participation with rounds played up 14% in June and 19.7% in July of that year.
- Yamaha committed to continuing investment in training and best practices necessary to create successful manufacturing career paths within each of its divisions.
- The company operates a technician training facility in Kennesaw, Georgia, which supports dealers and distributors across the country.
- The President’s Pledge to America’s Workers stems from President Trump’s National Council for the American Worker, established on July 19, 2019.
- The council leads a national strategy for training and re-training workers in high-demand industries, such as recreational manufacturing.
- More than 430 companies have signed the pledge, contributing to more than 16 million new training opportunities for American students and workers over the next five years.
- Yamaha Motor Corporation, USA (YMUS) manufactures golf cars alongside powersports, marine, and outdoor power equipment products sold through a nationwide network of distributors and dealers.
- YMUS maintains two corporate offices in Georgia, with factory operations located in Tennessee and Georgia.
- The Yamaha Golf-Car Company is headquartered in Kennesaw, Georgia, with assembly and production of all its vehicles taking place at the Newnan, Georgia facility.
- Yamaha was the first to develop Electronic Fuel Injection technology in a golf car, producing the best gas mileage and smallest carbon footprint for the category.
- Yamaha introduced the first golf car with fully independent, four-wheel suspension.
- Yamaha developed the first gas-powered golf car that is almost as quiet as an electric one.
