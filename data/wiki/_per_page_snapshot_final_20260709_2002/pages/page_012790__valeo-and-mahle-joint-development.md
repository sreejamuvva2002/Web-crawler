# Valeo and MAHle Joint Development

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2024-10-14

**Source:** [Valeo and MAHLE expand their product range of magnet free electric motors to upper segment applications - MAHLE Newsroom](https://newsroom.mahle.com/press/en/press-releases/valeo-and-mahle-expand-their-product-range-of-magnet-free-electric-motors-to-upper-segment-applications-106048)  ·  `page_012790`

## Facts

- Valeo and MAHLE signed a Joint Development Agreement to expand their product range of magnet-free electric motors to upper segment applications.
- The partnership will develop an innovative magnet-free electric axle system targeting upper segment electric vehicles with peak power ranging from 220 kW to 350 kW.
- The new technology is named the iBEE system (inner Brushless Electrical Excitation).
- Valeo will contribute expertise in electric motors, highly efficient inverters, and associated motor control laws.
- MAHLE will contribute expertise on magnet-free rotors utilizing its MAHLE Contactless Transmitter (MCT) technology.
- The companies are developing an innovative cooling concept to achieve an outstanding continuous to peak power ratio.
- The carbon footprint of the new system is expected to be reduced by more than 40% versus a permanent magnet electric motor of equivalent power.
- Testing of the first prototypes will be completed by the end of 2024.
- Xavier Dupont, Valeo Power Division CEO, stated that MAHLE develops the EESM rotor and brushless excitation system, while Valeo provides dedicated control embedded in its inverter.
- Martin Wellhoeffer, Member of the MAHLE Group Management Board, noted that the new e-axle will set a new benchmark in packaging and efficiency for EESM based e-axles.
- Valeo's first EESM development was launched in 2022 in cooperation with a European OEM to create a new magnet-free electric motor generation.
- Initial targets for the 2022 project included achieving an all-in-one architecture with a new stator and cooling technology to increase power density by 30% compared to current motors on the road.
- A second initial target was to shrink the carbon footprint by 30% versus an electric motor equipped with magnets (PMSM).
- A validation phase for more than 6 months was successfully completed, with results exceeding initial expectations in power and efficiency.
- The performance matches that of permanent magnet electric motors for B/C segment vehicles while offering benefits like no rare earth materials and a lower carbon footprint.
- MAHLE has been focusing on developing electric drive systems as part of its electrification strategy, leveraging its contactless transmitter technology for magnet-free electric motors.
- Avoiding rare earth materials contributes to e-mobility sustainability and offers advantages in costs and resource security.
- The key feature of the technology is inductive, contactless, and wear-free power transmission, allowing motor operation at benchmark efficiency levels.
