# Piolax

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Piolax Success Story- GaMEP at Georgia Tech](https://gamep.org/success-story/piolax-success-story/)  ·  `page_048307`

## Facts

- Piolax is a leading metal and plastic manufacturer that designs, produces, and tests fasteners, fuel systems, and open/close parts for the automotive industry.
- The company was founded in Japan in 1939 with five employees.
- Piolax has expanded over the years to operate in nine countries.
- The company established a location in Canton, Georgia in 1988.
- Piolax currently employs 2,883 people globally.
- The Canton manufacturing complex employs 343 employees and spans 279,000 square feet.
- Piolax has developed environmental and sustainability policies as part of its commitment to sustainability.
- The company has achieved ISO 14001:2015 certification.
- The company has achieved ITAF 16949:2016 certification.
- Since 2016, Piolax has engaged the Georgia Manufacturing Extension Partnership (GaMEP) at Georgia Tech on various projects.
- GaMEP assisted Piolax with their ISO 14001:2015 certification.
- In December 2024, Betty Wurzel, Piolax compliance coordinator, earned her certification for completing the Sustainability Cohort.
- The Sustainability Cohort was led by Sandra Enciso, GaMEP energy and sustainability project manager.
- The year-long cohort assisted with developing and implementing prevention strategies to achieve cost savings and reduce environmental pollution through interactive education, implementation support, individual coaching, and technical assistance.
- The Sustainability Cohort began with an energy assessment where participants identified the top 10 electricity- and gas-consuming equipment in their facilities.
- Piolax initially assumed their 129 plastic injection machines consumed the most energy but discovered their seven chillers used more after analyzing the data.
- With guidance from GaMEP, Piolax invested in energy consumption monitors that use sensors to measure and record electricity usage.
- Real-time data revealed their chillers were working against each other.
- Piolax started experimenting by raising the initial temperature of 50/55 degrees by five degrees on one set of chillers.
- When they reached the maximum temperature allowed without experiencing issues, GaMEP helped them install a chiller sequencer that optimizes energy efficiency and performance.
- Throughout the cohort, Piolax identified waste reduction opportunities such as improper fan installation and gas burnoff during heat treatment.
- Piolax engaged GaMEP to help find solutions, including reversing airflow and developing a safe filtration system that conserves heat and water.
- The company considered looking into an evaporation system to eliminate shipping wastewater offsite.
- Kelly Grissom, GaMEP energy and sustainability project manager, introduced Piolax to the Industrial Training and Assessment Center (ITAC) Implementation Grant.
- The ITAC Implementation Grant helps manufacturers implement onsite assessment recommendations.
- In June 2023, Piolax initiated a solar project.
- One year later, Piolax installed their first solar array.
- The first solar array produced 306 MWh, equating to offsetting 246.79 k of coal or planting 3.69 trees.
- Piolax is planning to install a second solar array.
- Piolax applied for and received the ITAC Implementation Grant for the second solar array installation.
- The second solar array installation is scheduled to begin in May 2025.
- Without the grant, the solar arrays would have taken eight to 10 years to pay for themselves.
- With the grant, Piolax anticipates a payback period of just four years for the solar arrays.
- Since participating in the Sustainability Cohort two years ago, Piolax has significantly reduced their energy consumption and operational costs.
- Betty Wurzel stated that the cohort fostered a community for sharing concerns and ideas without judgment and was enlightening, motivating, and worth the time investment.
