# Hyundai TRANSYS

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2020-12-06

**Source:** [Hyundai TRANSYS expansion to bring 678 jobs to west Georgia | The Citizen](https://thecitizen.com/2020/12/06/hyundai-transys-expansion-to-bring-678-jobs-to-west-georgia/)  ·  `page_009489`

## Facts

- An expansion by Hyundai TRANSYS near LaGrange will include an investment of more than $240 million and the creation of 678 jobs.
- Gov. Brian Kemp announced on Dec. 1 that Hyundai TRANSYS will invest more than $240 million to expand operations in Georgia.
- The investment will create 678 full-time, long-term positions at a new manufacturing facility in the West Point area.
- Gov. Brian Kemp cited Georgia's No. 1 business climate, robust logistics network, and top-ranked workforce training programs as key factors attracting and retaining global companies.
- Gov. Brian Kemp thanked Hyundai TRANSYS for its long-term commitment to Georgia and expressed hope that the investment will continue to benefit the company and West Georgia residents.
- Hyundai TRANSYS is the only automobile manufacturing company in the world that specializes in transmissions.
- The new 620,000-square-foot West Point plant will be located near the company's current location on the Kia campus.
- The new plant will produce eight-speed transmissions for a global automotive leader.
- Hyundai TRANSYS Georgia Powertrain President Sangkil Jung cited years of capital investment, employment, training support, and unmatched opportunities as reasons Georgia was a natural choice for expansion.
- Sangkil Jung expressed excitement about expanding Hyundai TRANSYS's presence in the West Point community.
- Hyundai TRANSYS will bring hundreds of advanced manufacturing jobs, along with management and office staff positions, to the local community.
- The company plans to create an additional 165 supplementary construction handling and inspection jobs.
- The expansion will bring Hyundai TRANSYS's total employment in Georgia to 1,741 jobs.
- Individuals interested in career opportunities with Hyundai TRANSYS are encouraged to check the company's website for updated information.
- West Point Mayor Steve Tramell expressed thrill over Hyundai TRANSYS's long-term investment in West Point and the creation of new jobs.
- Mayor Tramell thanked the West Point Development Authority, the State of Georgia, and Troup County for their contributions to making the project a reality.
- Hyundai TRANSYS has been a community partner with West Point and Troup County for over a decade.
- Troup County was chosen by Hyundai TRANSYS as the location for its new manufacturing facility.
- Troup County Chairman Patrick Crews stated the significant capital investment will strengthen the local economy and create economic opportunities for residents.
- Chairman Crews described Georgia as having the best state environment for business and a community with a history of great leadership.
- Chairman Crews called the project a team win resulting from combining leadership with resources.
- Senior Project Manager Amanda Fields represented the GDEcD Global Commerce division on the project.
- The project was developed in partnership with the West Point Development Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that companies investing in Georgia become Georgia companies and will be supported for long-term growth.
- Commissioner Wilson celebrated the partnership leading to more Georgians being employed by Hyundai TRANSYS.
- Commissioner Wilson expressed gratitude for Hyundai TRANSYS choosing to invest in Georgia again.
- Commissioner Wilson thanked Gov. Kemp and the General Assembly for supporting business recruitment and market expansion efforts.
- Communities across Georgia, both urban and rural, continue to benefit from the state's international trade and investment.
- In 2020, Georgia's international relationships facilitated more than $1 billion in investment and over 2,000 jobs from South Korean companies.
- Georgia is home to more than 200 automotive-related facilities.
- Georgia remains a national leader in advanced manufacturing, outpacing the U.S. in 10-year GDP growth for machinery, electrical equipment and components, and fabricated metals.
- Hyundai TRANSYS produces critical automobile parts, including transmissions, axles, and seats.
- The company produces all types of automatic transmissions, manual transmissions, dual clutch transmissions (DCT), and continuously variable transmissions.
- Hyundai TRANSYS develops and produces seats for Genesis, Santa Fe, Sorento, and other models.
