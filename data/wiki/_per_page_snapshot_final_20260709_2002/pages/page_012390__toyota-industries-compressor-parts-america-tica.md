# Toyota Industries Compressor Parts America (TICA)

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Consulate-General of Japan in Atlanta](https://www.atlanta.us.emb-japan.go.jp/TICAgrandOpening_E.html)  ·  `page_012390`

## Facts

- The grand opening ceremony for Toyota Industries Compressor Parts America (TICA) was held on October 9 in Pendergrass, Georgia.
- Attendees at the ceremony included Georgia Governor Nathan Deal, Representative Doug Collins, Jackson County Commission Chairman Tom Crow, TICO President Akira Onishi, Denso Corporation Managing Director Katsuhisa Shimokawa, Consul General Kazuo Sunaga, and business associates.
- Governor Nathan Deal congratulated TICA on its manufacturing facility's progress since the groundbreaking ceremony held two years prior.
- Consul General Kazuo Sunaga expressed gratitude for the support provided to TICA by the state, county, and cities.
- During the ceremony, TICA presented a $200,000 check to the North Georgia Community Foundation to establish a High School Scholarship fund.
- TICA is led by President Haruhiko Kimata.
- TICA operates as the third production facility of the North America subsidiary of Toyota Industries Corporation Compressor Division.
- The facility began production of compressor parts for automobile air conditioning in September 2013.
- Total investment for the facility is $350 million.
- The facility currently employs 320 people.
- In 2013, TICA and TD Automotive Compressor GA (TCGA) began a $190 million joint expansion plan.
- The joint expansion plan between TICA and TCGA is projected to create 120 new jobs.
