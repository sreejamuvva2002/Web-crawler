# Anovion Technologies

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2023-05-15

**Source:** [Anovion Technologies to Create Over 400 Jobs in Bainbridge, Invest $800M in Manufacturing Facility - Location Georgia](https://www.locationgeorgia.com/anovion-technologies-to-invest-800m-in-manufacturing-facility-in-bainbridge-february-2023/)  ·  `page_001513`

## Facts

- Anovion Technologies is a climate tech-driven advanced materials company headquartered in Chicago, Illinois.
- Anovion is a U.S.-owned and U.S.-based business and currently America’s first commercial-scale manufacturer of premium, cleaner anode-grade synthetic graphite.
- Anovion operates an existing footprint near Niagara Falls, New York, and Clarksburg, West Virginia.
- Anovion began commercial production in early 2021.
- Anovion is one of the first qualified U.S. suppliers of synthetic graphite anode materials for use in e-mobility applications.
- Anovion is among a limited number of graphite anode producers in America to have successfully gained qualification for EV applications.
- Graphite is the largest battery material used in electric vehicles by mass, exceeding copper, nickel, manganese, cobalt, and lithium.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Anovion will build a new manufacturing facility in Bainbridge, Georgia.
- The new facility will be 1,500,000 square feet and feature Anovion’s proprietary furnace technologies.
- The facility is located at Downrange Industrial Park in Bainbridge.
- This facility will be the first of its size and scale in North America.
- The project represents over $800 million in investment in Decatur County.
- The project will create more than 400 jobs.
- The facility will produce synthetic graphite for use in batteries that power EVs, electric storage systems, electronics, military applications, and other products.
- Operations at the new Bainbridge facility are expected to begin in late 2025.
- Anovion will be hiring for technical, production, engineering, administrative, and management roles.
- CEO Eric Stopka stated that Georgia’s strong technical workforce and top-tier schools will help advance research partnerships and contribute to a highly skilled talent pipeline.
- CEO Eric Stopka noted the region’s growing availability of clean energy will increase sustainability for Anovion’s manufacturing process.
- CEO Eric Stopka expressed encouragement regarding support from partners in Georgia and the Federal government.
- Governor Brian Kemp called Georgia 'fast becoming the e-mobility capital of the nation' and praised job creators like Anovion.
- Bainbridge Mayor Edward Reynolds highlighted the $800 million investment and creation of over 400 high-paying, quality jobs as a huge win for the community.
- Decatur County Board of Commissioners Chairman Pete Stephens noted the county’s long-term focus on attracting quality companies and diversifying its industry mix.
- Project Manager Taylor Walden represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on the project.
- The project was developed in partnership with the City of Bainbridge, Development Authority of Bainbridge, Decatur County, University System of Georgia, Georgia EMC, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that much of the critical e-mobility supply chain is currently sourced outside the U.S., and Anovion fills that need by delivering a critical component to Georgia.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson highlighted public-private partnerships working with utility partners, establishing infrastructure, and attracting top talent through the University System of Georgia.
- Since 2020, 40 EV-related projects have announced more than $21.9 billion in investment and 28,000 jobs in Georgia.
- More than 10 of these Georgia projects are battery manufacturers, recyclers, and suppliers, representing approximately $8.5 billion in investment and 6,000 jobs.
