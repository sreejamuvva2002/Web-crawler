# Georgia Ports Authority

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2026-05-04

**Source:** [Georgia Ports Authority’s Gainesville inland port set for May inauguration - FreshFruitPortal.com](https://www.freshfruitportal.com/news/2026/03/26/georgia-ports-expansion/)  ·  `page_017917`

## Facts

- The Georgia Ports Authority (GPA) is opening its new inland port in Gainesville, Ga., on May 4, 2026.
- The infrastructure was formerly known as the Blue Ridge Connector.
- The project entailed a $134 million investment.
- The facility will have an annual capacity of 200,000 containers.
- The port features a direct connection to Savannah’s global ocean carrier network, which operates 40 ships per week.
- The facility provides a direct rail with five-day-a-week service between Northeast Georgia and Savannah.
- The rail service serves as an alternative to a 600-mile round-trip truck route.
- The project is expected to benefit the entire Atlanta metropolitan area.
- GPA President and CEO Griff Lynch stated the inland rail facility will significantly offset truck traffic congestion in Atlanta and improve air quality.
- The new facility is estimated to replace 26,000 truck round-trips in its first year alone.
- The Georgia Ports Authority is executing a nearly $5 billion infrastructure investment plan over the next decade.
- The $5 billion initiative aims to expand berths, yards, gates, inland ports, and rail capacity.
- GPA Board Chairman Alec Poitevint emphasized that new infrastructure assets require planning and time, advocating for steady investment to deliver port capacity ahead of customer needs.
- The renovation of Ocean Terminal in Savannah is progressing as part of the broader expansion efforts.
- Phase one of the $1.6 billion Ocean Terminal project will open in July 2027.
- Phase two of the Ocean Terminal project will open in December 2028.
- The renovated dock at Ocean Terminal will stretch nearly 2,700 feet, allowing two large ships to dock simultaneously.
- A new gate complex at Ocean Terminal featuring 12 inbound and six outbound lanes will open by November 2026.
- A GPA-funded $29 million overpass is already open, enabling trucks to enter the I-16 corridor directly and keeping traffic off neighborhood streets.
- The Port of Brunswick is undergoing renovations, with dredging operations expected to wrap up by the end of March 2026.
- Additional work at the Port of Brunswick aimed at returning the inner and outer harbor to authorized depth should be completed in summer 2026.
