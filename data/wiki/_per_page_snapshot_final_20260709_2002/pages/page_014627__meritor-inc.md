# Meritor, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2019-01-01

**Source:** [Meritor Announces Standard Position Supply Agreement with Wabash National to Begin in January 2019 - The BRAKE Report](https://thebrakereport.com/meritor-announces-standard-position-supply-agreement-with-wabash-national-to-begin-in-january-2019/)  ·  `page_014627`

## Facts

- Meritor announced that various products have been selected as standard position and preferred options on all Wabash National dry and refrigerated van trailer products.
- The recently signed three-year agreement takes effect Jan. 1, 2019.
- Meritor’s loose axles will be standard equipment designed to reduce weight and offer structural advantages, contributing to improved fleet productivity through increased fuel efficiency and payload capacity.
- The Meritor Tire Inflation System (MTIS) has been selected as the preferred automatic tire inflation system.
- Meritor air disc brakes, Meritor Trailing-Arm (MTA) suspension, hubs, drums and slack adjusters, and Meritor’s new MTec6™ large-diameter axle are optional in all Wabash configurations.
- The MTec6™ large-diameter axle provides a 40-pound weight savings to a standard axle.
- Dave Pritchard serves as general manager, Trailer for Meritor.
- Dave Pritchard stated that Meritor greatly values Wabash as a long-term customer and appreciates Wabash selecting Meritor for this important business.
- For more than 30 years, Meritor has supplied a variety of trailer axle and braking systems to Wabash.
- Wabash is one of the industry’s largest manufacturers of trailers for traditional van and vocational configurations across multiple applications.
- Meritor has engineered and manufactured groundbreaking trailer suspensions, axles and brakes for over 60 years.
- Meritor has built more than 10 million trailer axles that incorporate features to improve quality, extend life and reduce maintenance.
- These axles are delivered within standard lead-times.
- More than 8 million fleet tires are currently protected by MTIS.
- Almost one in every three trailers built today is specified with the tire inflation system.
- According to Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration (FMCSA) and U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) studies, tire inflation systems extend tire life by 10 percent.
- According to FMCSA and EPA studies, tire inflation systems increase fuel economy an average 1.4 percent.
- MTIS decreases maintenance costs due to longer intervals between retreads and less time spent on manual pressure checking and filling.
