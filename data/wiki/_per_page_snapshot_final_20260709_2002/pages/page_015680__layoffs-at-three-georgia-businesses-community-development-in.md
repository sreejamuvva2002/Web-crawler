# Layoffs at Three Georgia Businesses (Community Development Institute Head Start, Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation, Americold Logistics)

**Type:** event  ·  **Role:** workforce_training  ·  **Published:** 2025-08-07

**Source:** [One week, three Georgia businesses, 249 employees laid off. Here's what we know](https://www.yahoo.com/news/articles/one-week-three-georgia-businesses-180659498.html)  ·  `page_015680`

## Facts

- In the span of two days, more than 200 employees across three different Georgia companies are being let go according to WARN Notices received by the USA Today Network.
- Community Development Institute Head Start served as the short-term interim operator of the Savannah River Head Start program while a process by the Office of Head Start identified a replacement recipient.
- 77 layoffs at Community Development Institute Head Start will be effective Sept. 30 or within 14 days beginning on Oct. 1 when the new administration starts.
- The CDI HS layoffs are spread across 11 worksites, with the largest impacts being 15 layoffs at Emanuel Head Start in Swainesboro and 13 at Burke Head Start in Waynesboro.
- The CDI HS layoffs are expected to be permanent, and the program location will no longer be operated by CDI HS.
- Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation is impacting employees at its Newnan operations due to a change in ownership and control of two distribution centers starting around Oct. 4.
- Around 160 employees at Yamaha Motor Manufacturing will be permanently fired during the ownership transition.
- A July 31 WARN Notice for Americold Logistics in Atlanta indicates that 12 employees will be impacted by layoffs.
- The three Georgia businesses are collectively laying off a total of 249 employees.
