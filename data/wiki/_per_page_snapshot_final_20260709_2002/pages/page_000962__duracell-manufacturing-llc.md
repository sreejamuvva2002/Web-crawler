# Duracell Manufacturing, LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2023-04-04

**Source:** [Governor Lee, Commissioner McWhorter Announce Duracell Manufacturing, LLC to Expand Manufacturing Operations in Bradley County - TNECD](https://tnecd.com/news/governor-lee-commissioner-mcwhorter-announce-duracell-manufacturing-llc-to-expand-manufacturing-operations-in-bradley-county/)  ·  `page_000962`

## Facts

- Duracell Manufacturing, LLC announced it will invest $25 million to expand its existing battery component manufacturing operations in Cleveland, Tennessee.
- The expansion project will create 25 new jobs as a result of the project.
- The expansion will support Duracell’s LaGrange, Georgia, site by providing strategic battery components supply and allowing the company to meet and exceed its growing North American battery demand.
- The Cleveland plant opened in 1961 and has been producing batteries under the Duracell brand name since 1964.
- Tennessee Gov. Bill Lee, Department of Economic and Community Development Commissioner Stuart McWhorter and Duracell Manufacturing officials announced the expansion.
- Stefaan Boterberg, president, Duracell Manufacturing, LLC stated the site adds a new important role of supporting the greater North American supply chain.
- Stefaan Boterberg noted the company is excited to invest in and leverage the skill sets of current and new Duracell team members.
- Cleveland Mayor Kevin Brooks confirmed the $25 million expansion is located at the local Duracell plant on Mouse Creek Road.
- Cleveland Mayor Kevin Brooks highlighted Duracell’s community support through the “Power Forward” relief program and supporting local organizations.
- Bradley County Mayor D. Gary Davis stated the $25 million investment and new jobs underscore Duracell’s commitment to improving its production capacity.
- Bradley County Mayor D. Gary Davis emphasized the project solidifies a partnership with the community for decades to come.
- TVA senior vice president of economic development John Bradley confirmed TVA and Cleveland Utilities are partnering with the City of Cleveland, Bradley County, Cleveland-Bradley Chamber of Commerce and TNECD to help support Duracell.
- Tennessee State Sen. Adam Lowe (R-Calhoun) expressed appreciation for the work of state and local officials who helped facilitate the investment.
- Tennessee State Rep. Kevin Raper (R-Cleveland) stated the expansion will further strengthen the economy and bring more quality jobs to the area.
- Since 2019, TNECD has supported more than 40 economic development projects in the Southeast Tennessee region.
- These TNECD-supported projects have resulted in approximately 7,800 job commitments and $3.2 billion in capital investment.
