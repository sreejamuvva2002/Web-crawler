# Aurubis Richmond Multimetal Recycling Plant

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Aurubis and SMS group forge ahead with the first multimetal recycling plant in the US - SMS group GmbH](https://www.sms-group.com/en-mx/press-and-media/press-releases/press-release-detail/aurubis-and-sms-group-forge-ahead-with-the-first-multimetal-recycling-plant-in-the-us)  ·  `page_017302`

## Facts

- Aurubis AG and SMS group GmbH signed a supply contract for a second plant module at the new Aurubis Richmond recycling plant in Georgia as part of their strategic partnership.
- The additional equipment will increase the capacity of the metal recycling plant in Richmond from an annual 90,000 tons to 180,000 tons of complex metal scrap materials.
- The core technology for the second module is the top-blown rotary converter (TBRC), which processes complex recycling materials to recover copper, nickel, tin, precious metals, and platinum group metals.
- These recovered metals are crucial raw materials for technologies related to the energy transition, sustainable mobility, or digitalization.
- Aurubis CEO Roland Harings stated that the expansion delivers on the company's 'Driving Sustainable Growth' strategy while keeping strategically important mineral raw materials in the resource cycle.
- Michael Rzepczyk, a member of the SMS Executive Board, noted that the planned doubling of production volume at the new US site takes recycling to the next level in the country.
- The recycling plant utilizes a modular system that allows additional components to be added later to expand capacity and respond flexibly to market needs.
- Aurubis is investing about €640 million for both phases of the project to build the first secondary smelter specializing in multimetal recycling in the US.
- Groundbreaking for the Aurubis Richmond plant took place in mid-2022, with construction currently running at full steam.
- Stage one of the plant is scheduled to go online in the first half of 2024.
- The second stage, equipped with the newly contracted TBRC, is anticipated to start production in 2026.
- The investment in Georgia will comply with the highest environmental standards in the US and create around 200 new jobs.
- Aurubis AG is a leading global provider of non-ferrous metals and one of the largest copper recyclers worldwide, processing roughly 1 million tons of recycling material annually.
- Aurubis produces more than 1 million tons of copper cathodes annually, along with wire rod, continuous cast shapes, profiles, flat rolled products, precious metals, selenium, lead, nickel, tin, zinc, sulfuric acid, and iron silicate.
- Aurubis has around 6,900 employees, production sites in Europe and the USA, and its shares are listed on the German Stock Exchange (MDAX, GCX, STOXX Europe 600).
- SMS group GmbH is a Düsseldorf-based plant and machine manufacturer with worldwide sales of around 2.6 billion euros and 14,500 employees globally.
