# Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution Joint Venture

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-08-01

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Group, LG Energy Jointly Investing $4.3 Billion in EV Battery Plant in Georgia | The Epoch Times](https://www.theepochtimes.com/business/hyundai-motor-group-lg-energy-jointly-investing-4-3-billion-in-ev-battery-plant-in-georgia-5293511)  ·  `page_001235`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution plan to invest 5.7 trillion won ($4.3 billion) to build an electric vehicle (EV) battery plant in the United States.
- The investment is part of a push to “drive the EV transition in America” and take advantage of tax credits provided by the Inflation Reduction Act (IRA).
- The two companies signed a memorandum of understanding to produce EV batteries in the United States and “further accelerate Hyundai’s electrification efforts in North America.”
- The memorandum of understanding was signed on May 26, according to a statement.
- Hyundai and LGES will each hold a 50 percent stake in the joint venture.
- The new plant will be located in Bryan County, Savannah, Georgia.
- The facility site is where Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America is currently being built.
- The plant will have an annual capacity of 30 GWh.
- The 30 GWh capacity is enough to power 300,000 electric vehicles.
- Construction of the factory will begin in the second half of 2023.
- Battery production at the facility will start at the end of 2025 at the earliest.
- Jaehoon Chang, CEO of Hyundai Motor Co., stated they will create a strong foundation to lead the global EV transition through establishing a new EV battery cell plant with LG Energy Solution.
- Youngsoo Kwon, CEO of LG Energy Solution, said the two auto and battery leaders are “ready to drive the EV transition in America.”
- The joint venture marks Hyundai’s second battery partnership with LG.
- Hyundai previously announced the construction of a $1.1 billion battery cell plant located in Karawang New Industry City, near Jakarta, Indonesia, in 2021.
- Automakers and battery companies are swiftly ramping up EV battery production in North America driven by President Joe Biden’s IRA.
- The IRA provides $369 billion in renewable energy incentives.
- The incentives include a $7,500 federal EV tax credit for cars assembled in North America.
- The incentives are part of President Joe Biden’s efforts to reduce reliance on China.
- To qualify for the tax credits, a certain percentage of the battery’s critical materials and components must be sourced domestically or from countries with which the United States has a free trade agreement.
- Sen. Jon Ossoff (D-Ga.) recently led a trade mission to South Korea to discuss the deal with the two companies.
- Ossoff welcomed the announcement in a statement on Thursday.
- Ossoff stated that Georgia now ranks second nationwide in new investment from IRA manufacturing incentives.
- Securing the LG-Hyundai battery investment to Georgia was a key goal of Ossoff's trade delegation to South Korea last month.
- Ossoff noted that the IRA’s manufacturing incentives continue to bring jobs and investment to Georgia.
- Ossoff stated his goal remains to make Georgia the world leader in advanced energy production.
- Ossoff thanked LG Chairman Koo Kwang-mo, LG Energy Solutions President David Kim, Hyundai Chairman Chung Eui-sun, Senator Reverend Warnock, and all local and state officials for making the announcement possible.
