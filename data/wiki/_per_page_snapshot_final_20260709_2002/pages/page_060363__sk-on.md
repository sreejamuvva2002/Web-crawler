# SK On

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-08-16

**Source:** [SK On to invest $1.1B, quadruple domestic battery capacity](https://koreajoongangdaily.joins.com/news/2023-08-16/business/industry/SK-On-to-invest-11B-quadruple-domestic-battery-capacity-/1847823)  ·  `page_060363`

## Facts

- SK On is investing 1.5 trillion won ($1.1 billion) to build a third battery factory in Seosan, South Chungcheong.
- This investment represents the largest domestic investment ever for the Korean battery maker.
- SK On signed a memorandum of understanding with the South Chungcheong Provincial Office and the Seosan city government to secure investment to construct a factory at a 44,125-square-meter (10.9-acre) site near its No. 1 and 2 facilities.
- The latest investment will increase the company's production capacity in Seosan fourfold.
- The third factory will be constructed by 2025 and will have a capacity of 14 gigawatt-hours (GWh) by the end of 2028.
- This expansion will boost SK On's domestic production capacity to 20 GWh, enough to supply around 280,000 electric vehicles.
- SK On CEO Jee Dong-seob stated that the latest investment is meaningful in strengthening the country's overall battery production capability and that SK On will endeavor to become a leader in the EV industry by contributing to a regional economy based on aggressive investments.
- The third factory in Seosan will be a smart factory, which will raise production speed by 30 percent and increase accuracy.
- Seosan is where SK On first started its battery production back in 2012.
- The company's No. 1 plant initially had a 0.2 GWh capacity, which was expanded to the current 5 GWh through a series of expansions.
- SK On's investment comes as Hyundai Motor prepares to break ground on its first domestic EV factory in Ulsan in September.
- SK On is a major battery supplier to Hyundai Motor and is currently the fifth-largest battery maker globally with 89 GWh of global production capacity.
- SK On is building four more plants in the United States and Europe, which will boost its capacity to 220 GWh by the end of 2025.
- SK On aims to become the third-biggest market player by 2025.
