# Daesol Ausys Georgia

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-08-30

**Source:** [Automotive components manufacturer plans West Point facility - Georgia - The Black Chronicle](https://blackchronicle.com/southeast/georgia/automotive-components-manufacturer-plans-west-point-facility/)  ·  `page_019041`

## Facts

- Daesol Ausys Georgia was formed in November 2022.
- The company plans to spend $72 million on a new manufacturing facility in West Point, Harris County.
- The West Point facility is expected to create more than 140 jobs.
- Daesol Ausys supplies Kia Georgia, Hyundai Motor Group, and General Motors.
- A Georgia Department of Economic Development representative confirmed the project is still active.
- The company will work with Georgia Quick Start for workforce training development.
- State officials are declining to release details about tax incentives offered to attract the company because they classified the project as active.
- Daesol Ausys was established in Korea in 2017 and specializes in designing and manufacturing vehicle accessories and interior components.
- The new facility will produce luggage boards and covers.
- Robert Grant, chairman of the Harris County Commission, noted a wonderful relationship between West Point/Harris County and Daesol Ausys’ sister companies Daehan Solutions and Daesol Materials.
- Operations at the new facility are expected to start in December 2024.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that West Point has become an automotive capital and expressed excitement about Daesol Ausys joining other innovators in Harris County.
- Governor Brian Kemp noted that Georgia’s automotive industry employs over 50,000 Georgians and will continue to grow as e-mobility suppliers choose locations across the state.
