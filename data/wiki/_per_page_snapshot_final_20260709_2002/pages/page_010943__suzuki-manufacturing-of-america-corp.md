# Suzuki Manufacturing of America Corp.

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-05-24

**Source:** [Suzuki Celebrates 20 Years of KingQuad ATV Manufacturing](https://www.motorcyclepowersportsnews.com/suzuki-celebrates-20-years-kingquad-atv-manufacturing-us/)  ·  `page_010943`

## Facts

- Suzuki Motor USA is celebrating 20 years of manufacturing KingQuad ATVs at the Suzuki Manufacturing of America Corp. (SMAC) facility.
- KingQuad ATVs first rolled off the assembly line in Rome, Georgia, in May of 2002.
- KingQuad ATVs have continued to be built exclusively in the U.S. ever since their initial production.
- Suzuki has been building the award-winning KingQuad ATVs at a multimillion dollar, 35-acre manufacturing facility since 2002.
- The SMAC facility is located in the center of the biggest ATV market in the world.
- The facility's location allows access to real-world rider feedback for product refinement and timely delivery of KingQuad ATVs to Suzuki dealers worldwide.
- Suzuki led the industry with the creation of the first ever four-wheel ATV in 1983.
- “This is an extremely important milestone for Suzuki,” said Kerry Graeber, vice president, division manager MC/ATV sales and marketing.
- The performance, toughness and durability of Suzuki ATVs represent the commitment, hard work and dedication of the people who build them in the Rome, Georgia, facility.
- The SMAC facility is a key to the continued success of Suzuki’s global ATV business.
- Suzuki KingQuad ATVs built at the SMAC facility are shipped to U.S. Suzuki dealers and nearly 30 countries worldwide.
