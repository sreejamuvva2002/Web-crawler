# Vernay Laboratories

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2021-07-05

**Source:** [Critical Business: Controlling the Flow - Vernay](https://vernay.com/news-and-events/critical-business-controlling-the-flow/)  ·  `page_013380`

## Facts

- Vernay Labs has its headquarters in Atlanta, GA.
- The company designs and custom manufactures critical flow-control valves for leading OEMs and medical device manufacturers worldwide.
- During the COVID-19 pandemic, Vernay supplied the majority of ventilator manufacturers across the world.
- Vernay is a key supplier to Ventec Life Systems, which partnered with General Motors to urgently increase output of critical-care ventilators.
- Vernay components are used in visible medical devices and in the industrial mixing of chemicals for sanitizers and disinfectants.
- The Griffin, GA plant successfully managed double-digit volume increases during manufacturing facility shutdowns in China and Italy through collaboration between global teams.
- Vernay has engineering, testing, and manufacturing capabilities in the U.S., Italy, the Netherlands, and China, with sales and customer service offices in Germany, Japan, and Singapore.
- Vernay is investing in the United States and Europe to add more than 15,000 square feet of clean room space.
- The company earned ISO Class 8 clean-room certification for the assembly area at its Griffin, GA plant.
- Quality Systems Manager Christina Ward oversees the ISO Class 8 certification effort and is a 21-year veteran in medical manufacturing.
- Ward explained that certifying an adjacent manufacturing space as an ISO class 8 clean room is challenging because the area houses more than 18 molding presses and 30 finishing/assembly stations.
- Ward is leading efforts to educate and train employees working in the medical area on necessary protocols.
- Ward noted that gaining additional clean-room certification will open the door to new customers and allow expansion of offerings to existing customers.
- Vernay is building a new clean room at its Asti, Italy facility, due to be completed by year’s end, as part of an expansion of the entire 43,000-square-foot site.
- The Asti site currently has a soft-wall, ISO 8 clean room used for washing and packaging products, which is being expanded to address increased customer demands.
- Managing Director Vanna Villata explained that the company performs custom elastomeric molding, overmolding, and assemblies of rubber, plastic, and metal parts, designing and manufacturing fully functioning assemblies and connectors for complex flow control needs.
- The 125-person Asti plant produces more than 300 million parts annually for the global fluid-control marketplace and is poised to grow exponentially to serve increased medical demands in Europe and Asia.
- Vernay Italia shut down for more than a month in spring 2020 due to the region’s COVID-19 outbreak, with Technical and Quality Director Mauro Gertosio noting that authorities initially did not recognize the importance of their products to the medical supply chain.
- Gertosio assembled a team focused on the medical sector, and the site gained ISO-13485 certification in just a few weeks during the pandemic after already adhering to the guidelines.
- Gertosio explained that the company worked diligently with the Italian government to be designated as an essential business and reopened the plant in May 2020.
- Reopening allowed the resumption of supplying parts for ventilators, blood pumps, oxygenators, and disposable surgical supplies, while also accelerating plans to expand the facility and gain ISO Class 7 clean room certification.
- Over 85% of all components produced by Vernay are custom parts designed specifically for a customer’s unique need, following a streamlined methodology from concept to launch.
- Devices relying on Vernay components include those used in cancer tissue diagnosis, bio-reactors, microfluidics, minimally invasive surgeries, pediatric respiratory inhalers, anesthesia delivery, one-way valves and septums for bag systems, dialysis, and negative wound pressure therapies.
- Vernay produces engineered solutions for critical medical applications using transfer, compression, injection, flashless, and liquid injection molding techniques, along with overmolding, bonding, welding, inline leak and flow testing, and camera systems.
- The company has developed more than 33,000 elastomeric materials and compound formulations tailored to each end use, utilizing proprietary linear and non-linear molding software simulation to understand elastomer flow behavior.
- Vernay is positioned to take on additional medical business with ISO 8 clean room certification in the U.S. and expansion plus ISO 7 clean room certification in Europe.
- Vernay Laboratories has been a technology leader in customized flow-control components since its founding in 1946 and formulates its own compounds for each specific application.
- The privately held firm produces more than 1 billion components per year using a library of more than 33,000 elastomeric materials and compound formulations, operating six plants globally in the U.S., the Netherlands, Italy, and China.
