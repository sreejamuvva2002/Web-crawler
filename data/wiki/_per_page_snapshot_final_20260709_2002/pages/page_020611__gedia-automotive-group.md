# GEDIA Automotive Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2020-07-24

**Source:** [German automotive parts manufacturer to locate site in Whitfield County | News | griffindailynews.com](https://www.griffindailynews.com/news/business/german-automotive-parts-manufacturer-to-locate-site-in-whitfield-county/article_0cdec682-f540-5328-bc82-a1aa356898d5.html)  ·  `page_020611`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that GEDIA Automotive Group will build an advanced manufacturing facility in Whitfield County.
- GEDIA is a family-owned international company that specializes in making state-of-the-art automotive parts.
- The facility will begin production with a focus on parts for electric vehicles.
- GEDIA will invest $85 million in this project.
- The project will deliver 200 jobs to the greater Dalton area.
- The site is located at a Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) certified property in Whitfield County.
- Governor Kemp stated that Georgia is a national leader in manufacturing, logistics, and workforce training.
- Kemp expressed confidence that GEDIA will be pleased with joining the growing electric vehicle ecosystem in Georgia.
- Kemp noted that the state’s consistent investment in its Germany-based European office is paying off.
- The announcement was supported by Georgia’s government partners in the Free State of Bavaria, one of Georgia’s strategic partner regions.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) Commissioner Pat Wilson traveled to Germany to meet with government officials, tourism and trade partners.
- Commissioner Wilson met at GEDIA headquarters and visited other companies in the final stages of the decision-making process on entering the U.S. market.
- GEDIA has more than 100 years of experience supplying lightweight structural automotive parts to Volkswagen, BMW, Mercedes-Benz, Volvo, and numerous other world-renowned companies.
- GEDIA is headquartered in Germany.
- This facility will mark GEDIA’s first site in the Southeast and its second facility in the United States.
- Markus Schaumburg, one of two GEDIA Automotive Group CEOs, stated that moving closer to original equipment manufacturers allows the company to serve the American market even better.
- The new innovative production facility will have 200 planned positions.
- Helmut Hinkel, GEDIA Automotive Group's additional CEO, stated that the heart of the plant in Whitfield County will be a hot-forming line with patented TemperBox®.
- The patented TemperBox® technology takes tailored tempering technology to the next level, enabling the creation of body components with lower weight and significantly higher crash safety performance while conserving energy and material.
- GEDIA will use state-of-the-art press hardening, tempering, and automated welding processes to produce parts for electric vehicles.
- Operations are expected to begin in the third quarter of 2021.
- Whitfield County Board of Commissioners Chairman Lynn Laughter welcomed GEDIA to Whitfield County’s industrial park.
- Laughter noted that the location is in the heart of the Southeast automotive manufacturing market, providing a significant advantage for the company.
- GEDIA will offer opportunities for management and staff positions, highly skilled technicians, and production workers.
- Additional information about the project will be available by the beginning of the fourth quarter of 2020.
- GEDIA will build and occupy a 180,000-square-foot facility on a GRAD Certified property in Carbondale Business Park.
- To earn a GRAD program certification, a property must undergo due diligence and complete specific certification requirements prior to a visit by a prospective business.
- Completing the GRAD certification program is a proactive way for community planners and landowners to help catalyze economic growth and industrial development.
- Since the inception of GDEcD’s GRAD certification program, more than 8,000 jobs have been created on GRAD sites.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development was represented in the project by Director of International Relations Nico Wijnberg.
- Wijnberg partnered with the Georgia Electric Membership Corporation, Whitfield County, and Georgia Quick Start.
- Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that investing in a GRAD certified property was undoubtedly a main driver in GEDIA’s site selection process.
