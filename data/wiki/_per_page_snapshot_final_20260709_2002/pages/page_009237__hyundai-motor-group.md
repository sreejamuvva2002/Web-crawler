# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-05-20

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Group to Invest $5.54 Billion in Electric Vehicle Manufacturing Facility | Development Authority of Bryan County](https://bryancountyga.com/news/article/hyundai-motor-group-to-invest-5.54-billion-in-georgia-at-first-fully-dedica)  ·  `page_009237`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group will invest $5.54 billion in Georgia at its first fully dedicated electric vehicle and battery manufacturing facility.
- The investment is for opening a state-of-the-art U.S. smart factory at the Bryan County Megasite.
- Non-affiliated Hyundai Motor Group suppliers will invest approximately another $1 billion in the project.
- The project will deliver approximately 8,100 new jobs to Georgia’s coastal region.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp and Hyundai Motor Group Executive Chair Euisun Chung announced the investment at a signing ceremony on May 20, 2022.
- Jay Chang (President and CEO of Hyundai Motor Company) and José Muñoz (President and COO of Hyundai Motor Company) attended the announcement.
- The Bryan County Megasite is 2,923 acres in size.
- The megasite was purchased jointly by the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority (JDA) and the State less than a year ago.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated the project is the largest economic development project in Georgia's history.
- Hyundai Motor Group expects to begin construction on the new facility in January 2023.
- Full production is expected in the first half of 2025.
- The facility will have an annual capacity of 300,000 units.
- Wages throughout the project will be well-paying advanced manufacturing positions competitive in the local market.
- Hyundai Motor Group cited speed-to-market, workforce, and the state’s ability to meet the company’s carbon neutrality standards as reasons for choosing Georgia.
- Georgia is home to an existing network of Hyundai subsidiaries and suppliers.
- The Bryan County Megasite was developed by the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor JDA over several years in partnership with the state.
- Industrial utilities are adjacent to the site, and extensive due diligence reports have been completed.
- Hyundai Motor Group plans to produce a diverse range of innovative full electric vehicles at the new Georgia highly connected, automated, and flexible EV smart factory.
- Details of production models will be shared at later dates.
- Through the battery manufacturing facility, Hyundai Motor Group aims to establish a stable supply chain for EV battery and other EV components in the U.S. market.
- When it nears planned 2025 start of operations, Hyundai Motor Group will seek local vendors and suppliers.
- Carter Infinger is the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority Board of Directors Chairman.
- Bryan County is the fastest-growing county in Georgia.
- The strategic purchase of the Bryan County Megasite in 2021 was the largest in state history.
- The site is adjacent to Interstate 16 with immediate access from I-95 and I-16 to 250 major metro areas.
- The site is less than 30 miles from the Port of Savannah, the single-largest and fastest-growing container terminal in the U.S.
- Two Class I rail facilities on-site are provided by Georgia Central Railway.
- Russell McMurry is the Georgia Department of Transportation Commissioner.
- Griff Lynch is the Georgia Ports Authority Executive Director.
- Georgia is the Southeastern leader for EV registrations per 1,000 registered automobiles.
- Georgia offers more EV charging outlets per capita than anywhere else in the Southeast.
- The Georgia Electric Mobility and Innovation Alliance is a public-private-nonprofit initiative focused on growing the electric mobility ecosystem in the state.
- Taylor Walden is the Project Manager for the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD).
- Yoonie Kim is the Director of Korean Investment at GDEcD.
- The Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor JDA includes Bryan County, Bulloch County, Chatham County, and Effingham County.
- Georgia Quick Start and Georgia Power are involved in the project support.
- Pat Wilson is the Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner.
- Hyundai Motor Group plans to implement advanced intelligent manufacturing technologies currently under test at its innovation hub, including intelligent A.I.-equipped manufacturing and data-driven processes.
- The plant will help support Hyundai Motor Group’s efforts to achieve its carbon neutrality target through the active use of renewable energy for the facility.
- Since 2020, Georgia has announced more than 20 EV-related projects that will invest more than $13.54 billion and create nearly 18,000 jobs in the state.
- Information related to inquiries about project RFIs, RFPs listing, and supply chain outreach will be available online when it becomes available.
