# Buildings Energy Efficiency Frontiers & Innovation Technologies (BENEFIT) Funding Opportunity

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2023-08-07

**Source:** [Meet DOE’s Newest Research Projects from BENEFIT 22-23 | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/cmei/buildings/articles/meet-does-newest-research-projects-benefit-22-23)  ·  `page_005720`

## Facts

- DOE released $46 million in funding for 29 projects across 15 states to develop advanced technologies and retrofit practices for buildings that will benefit occupants and the grid through efficient, affordable, sustainable building operations.
- On August 7, 2023, DOE released $46 million in funding for 29 projects across 15 states under the Buildings Energy Efficiency Frontiers & Innovation Technologies (BENEFIT) funding opportunity to help buildings electrify while improving energy efficiency and demand flexibility.
- Georgia Institute of Technology, Georgia received $2.4 million to design and integrate Thermochemical Energy Storage (TCES) into buildings for load shedding/shifting, developing a high-energy, closed-cell thermochemical energy storage module that connects with a residential heat pump to improve efficiency and store energy during periods of peak demand year-round.
- Southern Company Services, Georgia received $1.5 million to deeply integrate Battery Energy Storage Systems (BESS) into an existing large commercial building automation system alongside HVAC, solar, and EV charging infrastructure to achieve net zero carbon goals.
- Southern Company Services will use a supervisory control layer to coordinate local and grid objectives for the BESS integration, breaking traditional BESS and BAS silos.
- Selection for award negotiations is not a commitment by DOE to issue an award or provide funding; before funding is issued, DOE and applicants will undergo a negotiation process, and DOE may cancel negotiations and rescind the selection for any reason during that time.
