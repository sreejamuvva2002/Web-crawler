# West Point, Georgia

## Facts

- Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia (KMMG) is located in West Point, Georgia, and has been operational since 2010.
- The plant represented an initial $1.2 billion investment that has since expanded to a cumulative economic impact exceeding $4 billion on the surrounding west Georgia region.
- KMMG is the only Kia manufacturing facility in the United States.
- The facility initially created 2,500 direct jobs, with production focused on vehicles like the Sorento and Telluride SUVs.
- This development absorbed displaced textile workers and spurred ancillary employment in logistics, suppliers, and services.
- Infrastructure upgrades such as road expansions and utility enhancements were implemented to support the facility's operations.
- In July 2023, KMMG announced a $200 million investment to initiate assembly of the EV9 electric SUV.
- The initiative added nearly 200 new positions and enhanced the plant's capacity for sustainable manufacturing.
- City-led initiatives between 2015 and 2024 fostered over 1,400 additional jobs and more than $700 million in private capital investments, including expansions in automotive supply chains along Kia Boulevard.
- The Downtown West Point Development Authority introduced 50/50 matching facade grants for storefront improvements in 2025 to revitalize the central business district by leveraging Kia's proximity.
- West Point is positioned as a hub for advanced manufacturing within the Columbus-Auburn metropolitan area, though sustained growth depends on adapting to automotive industry volatility including supply chain disruptions and electrification demands.
- Kia Georgia, Inc. operates one of the largest automotive assembly plants in the United States, employing approximately 3,000 workers in vehicle production with an annual capacity exceeding 350,000 units as of 2024.
- The facility was established in 2009 and has expanded multiple times.
- Hyundai Dymos America supports Kia's operations by producing automotive seats with around 500 employees.
- Johnson Controls manufactures seat systems and door panels with approximately 500 workers.
- Powertech America, Inc. specializes in transmissions with about 500 staff.
- Daehan Solution provides automotive parts to the local cluster with 90 employees.
- The newly established Daesol Ausys Georgia facility supports manufacturing operations with over 140 jobs.
- Industrial activities at the Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia plant necessitated wastewater infrastructure upgrades, with federal funding allocated in 2018 to address sewage capacity strained by economic growth.
- The Georgia Environmental Protection Division issued permits for the facility's discharges to ensure compliance with effluent limits.
