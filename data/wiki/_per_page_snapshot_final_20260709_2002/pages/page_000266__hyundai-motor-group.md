# Hyundai Motor Group

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group promised its electric vehicle manufacturing facility near Savannah would be the “factory of the future.”
- The manufacturing campus is located in Ellabell, near Savannah.
- The South Korean automaker debuted what it calls the Metaplant to the public on Wednesday.
- Robots both big and small stamped, welded and assembled autos at the facility.
- EVs moved about the shop floor on self-propelled dollies that use GPS and motion sensors for navigation.
- Four-legged robots that resembled medium-sized dogs inspected the work.
- Employees, known as Meta Pros, monitored equipment or performed tasks too tactile for the machines to handle.
- The peek inside was part of the ceremonial grand opening for the $7.59 billion manufacturing campus.
- The facility is the largest economic development project in Georgia state history.
- The facility is projected to employ 8,500 at full build out.
- Of the projected employees, 2,600 will be building automobiles.
- The assembly facility spans 16 million square feet.
- The facility is designed to produce up to 500,000 vehicles per year.
- The annual production capacity was revised from 300,000 at Wednesday’s event.
