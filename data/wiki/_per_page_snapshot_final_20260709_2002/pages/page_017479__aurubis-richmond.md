# Aurubis Richmond

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-24

**Source:** [Aurubis Richmond: First US multimetal recycling plant starts production of strategic metals | Corporate - EQS News](https://www.eqs-news.com/news/corporate/aurubis-richmond-first-us-multimetal-recycling-plant-starts-production-of-strategic-metals/204b6e7c-dbba-4a20-850d-682a28be266e_en)  ·  `page_017479`

## Facts

- Aurubis Richmond achieved a strategic milestone with the ramp-up and start of production at its new US site on September 24, 2025.
- The facility will produce key strategic metals including copper, nickel, tin, and precious metals in Georgia.
- These metals are essential for expanding data centers, AI applications, energy infrastructure, high-tech products, and the defense industry.
- American industry currently requires approximately 1.8 million tons of copper per year.
- Experts estimate copper demand will rise nearly 30% over the next five years.
- The United States currently imports about half of the copper it processes.
- Multimetal recycling at Aurubis Richmond is positioned to quickly close this supply gap and reinforce American supply chain independence.
- A "First Melt" celebration was held on September 24, 2025, attended by US Representative Rick W. Allen, German Ambassador Jens Hanefeld, and other political and business figures.
- Dr. Toralf Haag, CEO of Aurubis AG, stated the company is the frontrunner in multimetal recycling and processing in the US and will continue expanding its expertise in producing key strategic raw materials domestically.
- Georgia Governor Brian P. Kemp noted in a video message that Aurubis Richmond is now a significant employer for Georgia and plays a major role in the American multimetal supply chain, standing as a testament to American manufacturing independence and resiliency.
- The project involves an investment of approximately $800 million.
- Aurubis will create more than 240 jobs in Georgia through this facility.
- The plant is designed to process up to 180,000 tons of complex recycling material annually.
- An expansion stage for the facility is scheduled to begin in 2026.
- Recycling materials processed at the site will include printed circuit boards, copper cables, and other metal-bearing products.
- Aurubis Richmond recovers critical metals from these materials on-site to close the value chain locally.
- Because the plant will be supplied locally, it will help keep valuable raw materials within the domestic market.
- US Representative Rick W. Allen emphasized that the facility is a strategic site in Georgia’s 12th District that reinforces America's ability to supply itself with metals critical for national security, infrastructure, and technology leadership.
- David Schultheis, President and Managing Director of Aurubis Richmond, described the facility as an important player in the North American multimetal supply chain and thanked Georgia policymakers and governmental authorities for their strong support and smooth approval processes.
- The new plant utilizes cutting-edge technology that meets the latest environmental standards enacted by Georgia state and federal authorities.
- Operations will maintain high consideration for water, air, and soil quality.
- The facility is designed as a flexible "one-stop shop" capable of receiving a broad range of metal-bearing recycling materials from US suppliers.
- Aurubis AG is a leading global provider of non-ferrous metals and one of the largest copper recyclers worldwide.
- The company processes complex metal concentrates, scrap metals, organic and inorganic metal-bearing recycling materials, and industrial residues into high-quality metals.
- Aurubis produces more than 1 million tons of copper cathodes annually.
- From its copper cathodes, the company manufactures wire rod, continuous cast shapes, profiles, and flat rolled products made of copper and copper alloys.
- Additional metals produced by Aurubis include precious metals, selenium, lead, nickel, tin, and zinc.
- The company's portfolio also includes sulfuric acid and iron silicate.
- Sustainability is a fundamental part of Aurubis's strategy, integrating sustainable conduct into its corporate culture regarding natural resources and ecological conduct.
- Aurubis employs approximately 7,100 people globally and operates production sites in Europe and the US with an extensive worldwide distribution network.
- Aurubis shares are listed on the German Stock Exchange (Prime Standard Segment), MDAX, Global Challenges Index, and STOXX Europe 600.
