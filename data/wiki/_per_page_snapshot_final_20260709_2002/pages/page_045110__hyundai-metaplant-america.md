# Hyundai Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-05-05

**Source:** [Georgia Gov. Kemp signs tax exemption, state budget into law at Hyundai EV plant site](https://www.yahoo.com/news/georgia-gov-kemp-signs-tax-203422055.html?fr=sycsrp_catchall)  ·  `page_045110`

## Facts

- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp signed a sales use tax exemption for projects of regional significance at the future site of the Hyundai Metaplant in Bryan County.
- The signing ceremony took place on Friday at the megasite construction location.
- Hyundai's electric vehicle and battery manufacturing facility fits the definition of a project of regional significance.
- The factory is scheduled to open in late 2024.
- The facility expects to employ 8,100 workers.
- Georgia Speaker of the House Jon Burns (an Effingham County native who owns a farm near the site) and Georgia Sen. Ben Watson (a Savannahian whose district includes Bryan County) joined Kemp for the signing ceremony.
- In addition to signing the tax exemption extension, Kemp made law the state's Fiscal Year 2024 budget during the event.
- House Bill 408 extends a sales use tax break for regional projects meant to spur investment and entice future organizations to do business in Georgia.
- Gov. Kemp called Hyundai Metaplant America and another battery plant coming to Bartow County "transformational projects" and cited them as one of the reasons why House Bill 408 is imperative.
- Most of the communities that had benefited from this tax tool are located outside the metro Atlanta area.
- Suppliers related to the Hyundai project have exceeded financial projections.
- Over 4,500 new jobs have been announced for site suppliers in roughly $1.96 billion dollars of investment.
- The supplier investment number is nearly double the original estimate for all site suppliers.
