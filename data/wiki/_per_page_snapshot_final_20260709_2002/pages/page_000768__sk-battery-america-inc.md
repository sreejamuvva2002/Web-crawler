# SK Battery America Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [OSHA Finds Serious Safety Violations at SK Battery America Plant Following Battery Fire](https://www.batterytechonline.com/battery-manufacturing/osha-finds-serious-safety-violations-at-sk-battery-america-plant-following-battery-fire)  ·  `page_000768`

## Facts

- OSHA found serious safety violations at the SK Battery America plant following a battery fire.
- A federal investigation uncovered repeated safety lapses at the Georgia plant.
- The US Department of Labor's Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA) uncovered alarming safety breaches at the SK Battery America plant in the aftermath of a lithium battery fire.
- The findings have raised urgent concerns about safety standards within the electric vehicle battery manufacturing sector.
- The OSHA investigation was prompted by an October 2023 lithium battery fire at the Commerce, GA, plant.
- OSHA inspectors found SK Battery America culpable of failing to adequately train its workforce to navigate the hazardous aftermath of the incident.
- Multiple workers suffered potentially irreversible respiratory damage due to exposure to toxic fumes.
- The company was cited for exposing workers to inhalation hazards, including hydrofluoric acid vapors, by neglecting to establish a comprehensive emergency response plan.
- SK Battery America failed to ensure that staffing agency Moveret Inc. informed its workers about the dangers associated with lithium battery fires.
- There was inadequate training for on-site emergency responders and fire brigade members regarding lithium battery fire hazards and treatment procedures for exposed employees.
- Employees lacked training on hazardous chemicals in their work areas and the means to protect themselves during lithium battery fires.
- The company neglected to conduct annual fit-testing for workers required to utilize tight-fitting facepiece respirators.
- OSHA imposed fines totaling over $77,000 on SK Battery America for the safety infractions.
- This marks the second instance within a year where the company has been found wanting in adhering to federal safety standards.
- The safety lapses underscore broader concerns within the battery manufacturing sector regarding employee safety and regulatory compliance.
- The regulatory framework governing environmental standards in this sector has yet to catch up with the rapid pace of development, posing significant challenges for EV manufacturers, as described by US law firm Troutman Pepper.
- Joshua Turner, OSHA's Area Office Director in Atlanta-East, underscored the importance of prioritizing worker safety, particularly in emerging industries like battery manufacturing.
- Turner emphasized that innovation must not come at the expense of employee well-being.
- Safety advocates and industry stakeholders are calling for stringent safety measures to be implemented without delay.
- SK Battery America Inc. is a subsidiary of SK On, a key player in South Korea's SK Group.
- The company employs approximately 3,100 workers at its Commerce plants.
- BlueOval SK is a joint venture between Ford and SK On to manufacture batteries in Glendale, KY, and Stanton, TN, for Ford and Lincoln EVs.
- SK On and Hyundai Motor Group planned a joint venture in April in Bartow County, GA, for Hyundai and Kia EVs.
- SK On and Westwater Resources signed an agreement in 2023 to develop anode materials tailored to batteries produced at SK On’s US plants.
- Industry stakeholders emphasize that worker safety must take precedence over all other considerations and that concerted efforts are needed for industry-wide reforms to prevent future tragedies.
