# EnChem America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-01

**Source:** [GrowHub Shares Pop After EnChem Filing, Investors Weigh Dilution](https://ts2.tech/en/growhub-shares-pop-after-enchem-filing-investors-weigh-dilution/)  ·  `page_008066`

## Facts

- GrowHub Ltd signed a binding reverse-merger term sheet with EnChem America on May 28 to hand control of its listed shell to the battery-electrolyte materials company.
- If the reverse merger closes, EnChem America’s holders would own 85% of the fully diluted combined public company, while existing GrowHub holders keep 15%.
- The term sheet assigns EnChem America a minimum $400 million valuation in the merger.
- EnChem America is the U.S. arm of South Korea’s EnChem Co Ltd and develops and makes battery electrolyte materials, which are the chemicals allowing ions to move inside rechargeable batteries.
- Under the deal terms, EnChem America can exit if GrowHub’s closing VWAP falls under $4.00 per share.
- The merger requires final paperwork, GrowHub shareholder sign-off for new shares under Nasdaq rules, Nasdaq approval for listing new shares, regulatory steps, PCAOB-standard audits from EnChem, and SEC declaration of registration statements effective.
- All current GrowHub directors and top executives will step down at closing, with EnChem’s picks taking every board and senior management spot.
- MarketsandMarkets lists EnChem, Capchem, and Guangzhou Tinci Materials Technology as top players in the battery electrolyte space.
- EnChem CEO Oh Jung-Kang told Theworldfolio in a 2024 interview that Chinese players are “cut off from the American supply chain” and described it as a “window of opportunity for Korean companies.”
- GrowHub received a Nasdaq compliance notice on May 26 stating it fell below the minimum stockholders’ equity standard for the Nasdaq Capital Market, with until July 10 to submit a compliance plan.
- GrowHub’s 2025 annual filing reported revenue dropped to S$83,032 ($64,574) from S$237,014 the prior year, with a net loss of S$17.2 million ($13.4 million) and a $2.3 million shareholders’ deficit in U.S. dollars.
- GrowHub shares jumped about 541% to $2.23 on Monday morning trading after hitting $2.95 earlier, following the SEC filing of the term sheet.
