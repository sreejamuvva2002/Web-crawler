## Facts

- Hyundai announced plans to bring 3,000 new jobs to Southeast Georgia with $2.7B expansion.
- On Thursday, the company announced its roadmap to 2030, which includes plans for new electric vehicles, some region-specific, and a major manufacturing expansion.
- Just weeks after an immigration operation at Hyundai's megasite in Bryan County garnered international headlines, Hyundai has announced a major expansion, scheduled to bring thousands more jobs to Southeast Georgia.
- Hyundai is investing $2.7 billion to expand its Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) in Ellabell.
- The expansion will add 200,000 units of production capacity by 2028.
- The expansion will create 3,000 additional jobs.
- Globally, the company says it plans to increase production by 1.2 million units by 2030.
- Significant contributions to the global production increase will come from facilities in India, Saudi Arabia, and Vietnam.
- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp released a statement on X Thursday congratulating @HMGnewsroom on the announcement.
- Gov. Kemp's statement reinforced Hyundai's commitment to this project and the state, expressing anticipation for their continued growth and success for years to come.
