# Pratt & Whitney

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2026-02-24

**Source:** [RTX’s Pratt & Whitney Announces New $200M Columbus Expansion, Cuts Ribbon on Prior Expansion | Governor Brian P. Kemp Office of the Governor](https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases/2026-02-24/rtxs-pratt-whitney-announces-new-200m-columbus-expansion-cuts-ribbon)  ·  `page_002621`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Pratt & Whitney, an RTX business, is investing $200 million to expand its operations in Columbus, Georgia.
- The project will initially create 15 new jobs, with more jobs to follow.
- Along with Pratt & Whitney President Shane Eddy, Columbus Mayor Skip Henderson, and other public and company leaders, Governor Kemp celebrated the announcement of this new investment and the completion of a prior expansion announced in 2023.
- This previously announced project added nearly 400 new jobs and 81,000 square feet to Pratt & Whitney’s Columbus Engine Center.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Pratt & Whitney's decision to invest in Georgia immediately after completing another major expansion is an incredible testament to how companies operating in the state find reliable, ongoing, and long-term success.
- Governor Kemp noted that throughout its more than forty years in Columbus, Pratt & Whitney has been a valued partner and driver of the impact the aerospace industry is delivering statewide.
- Governor Kemp expressed gratitude that the partnership will continue to grow and flourish, benefiting the job creator, the people of Columbus, and all of Georgia.
- The Pratt & Whitney Columbus facility opened in 1984 and includes the Columbus Engine Center and Columbus Forge, both operating on the same campus.
- The Columbus Engine Center maintains Pratt & Whitney GTF™ engines for the Airbus A320neo aircraft family, Airbus A220, and Embraer E-Jets E2.
- The facility also supports the F117 engine for the Boeing C-17 Globemaster III and the F100 engine for the McDonnell Douglas F-15 Eagle and Lockheed Martin F-16 Fighting Falcon.
- The Columbus Forge produces compressor airfoils, and compressor and turbine disks for Pratt & Whitney’s commercial and military engines.
- These components include those for the F135 engine, which powers all variants of the Lockheed Martin F-35 Lightning II fighter.
- Pratt & Whitney President Shane Eddy stated that over the past four decades with community, state, and Governor support, Pratt & Whitney’s presence in Georgia has grown from a small manufacturing facility to a state-of-the-art manufacturing and overhaul center.
- The facility employs 2,600 dedicated people focused on connecting and protecting the world.
- Since 2008, Pratt & Whitney has invested more than $1 billion to continue expanding the footprint and capabilities of the Columbus facility.
- This latest investment will increase output of critical parts for growing military and commercial engine programs and underscores an ongoing commitment to ramp industrial capacity to support customers.
- The Columbus Engine Center is located at 8987 Macon Road.
- The Columbus Forge is located at 8801 Macon Road.
- The expansion is expected to be completed by 2028.
- Columbus Mayor Skip Henderson thanked Pratt & Whitney for its continued faith in the Columbus community and the Valley region.
- Mayor Henderson appreciated Governor Kemp, the Georgia Department of Economic Development, and Choose Columbus’ help in getting the project across the finish line.
- Mayor Henderson described Pratt & Whitney as an outstanding community partner and expressed a desire to continue growing that relationship.
- Chairman Selvin Hollingsworth of the Development Authority of Columbus, Georgia thanked Pratt & Whitney for its additional capital investment and creation of high-quality jobs.
- He stated the project underscores the strength of the region’s advanced manufacturing sector and the power of partnership in driving economic growth.
- Regional Project Manager Christy Bozeman represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The project was completed in partnership with the Development Authority of Columbus, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Pratt & Whitney’s continued growth in Columbus proves that investing in workforce initiatives is the most important factor for the long-term success of companies in Georgia.
- Pratt & Whitney was an early beneficiary of Georgia Quick Start, a partner they have engaged with for nearly every expansion or major investment over their decades-long relationship.
- By working with education partners and local communities such as Columbus Technical College and Columbus-Muscogee County, Georgia offers solutions that support decades of accomplishments and milestones.
- Pratt & Whitney is an RTX business and a world leader in the design, manufacture and service of aircraft engines and auxiliary power units for military, commercial and civil aviation customers.
- Since 1925, its engineers have pioneered the development of revolutionary aircraft propulsion technologies.
- Today it supports more than 90,000 in-service engines through a global network of maintenance, repair and overhaul facilities.
