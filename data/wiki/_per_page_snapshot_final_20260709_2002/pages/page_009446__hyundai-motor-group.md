# Hyundai Motor Group

## Facts

- Plans to deploy humanoid robots at its U.S. manufacturing plant in Georgia starting in 2028.
- Aims to build a factory capable of manufacturing 30,000 robot units annually by 2028.
- Robots will initially carry out parts sequencing tasks from 2028.
- Applications will expand gradually as safety and quality benefits are validated.
- By 2030, Atlas robots expected to move into component assembly.
- Longer-term plan includes taking on tasks involving heavy loads, repetitive motions, and complex operations across production sites.
- Robots designed to reduce physical strain on workers by taking on higher-risk and repetitive tasks.
- At Hyundai Motor's affiliate Kia Corp, the labour union last year called to establish a body to address potential labour rights issues in preparation for the AI era due to worker concerns about expanding automation.
- Hyundai Motor vice chair Jaehoon Chang stated at CES that the company understands concerns about job losses but people will be needed to maintain and train the robots, requiring additional personnel.
- Company expects humanoid robots to become the largest segment of the physical artificial intelligence market, defined as AI systems embedded in hardware that collect real-world data and make autonomous decisions.
- Atlas features human-scale hands with tactile sensing and can lift up to 50 kilograms (110 pounds).
- Robot can operate autonomously and function in industrial environments ranging from minus 20 degrees Celsius to 40 degrees Celsius.
- Hyundai is accelerating development through partnerships with global AI leaders, including collaborations with Nvidia and Google aimed at improving safety, efficiency, and real-world deployment.
- Some automakers see an overlap between autonomous driving and robotics due to similar sensing, perception, and decision-making technologies underpinning embodied AI.
