# Daimler Truck North America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-08-07

**Source:** [Daimler Truck North America orders fall by more than 50% in Q2 | Trucking Dive](https://www.truckingdive.com/news/daimler-trucks-north-america-q2-orders/756972/)  ·  `page_053559`

## Facts

- Daimler Truck North America sales declined by 20% in Q2.
- Daimler Truck North America orders fell by more than 50% in Q2.
- Executives stated customers are grappling with a dynamic market environment during the company’s earnings call held Aug. 1.
- The manufacturer lowered its 2025 sales guidance for North America.
- Daimler Truck now expects to sell between 135,000 to 155,000 units in North America in 2025.
- This revised forecast is down from the 155,000 to 175,000 units previously forecasted as of May.
- The earlier forecast was 180,000 to 200,000 units as of March.
- Daimler Truck CEO Karin Rådström said during the Aug. 1 earnings call: “I think our customers are probably mostly concerned with what happens with the American economy because that drives the need for transport or not.”
- Rådström noted that continued uncertainty makes it difficult for customers to predict freight volume development and gear investments accordingly.
- Daimler is scaling back production in North America due to a lack of demand, projecting a 20% drop in truck volumes this quarter compared to Q2.
- In recent weeks, the truck maker reduced its headcount by 2,000 people in the U.S. and Mexico.
- Executives confirmed the headcount reduction was a mix of white- and blue-collar workers.
- The company builds Western Star and Freightliner trucks.
- Daimler Truck has manufacturing facilities in Oregon, North Carolina, South Carolina, and Mexico.
- CFO Eva Scherer stated that every week counts when it comes to orders.
- Orders picked up in July compared to June.
- The company expects or hopes that the July ordering momentum continues as the base for its full-year guidance.
- Daimler Truck North America saw an improvement in orders in May versus April before another backslide in June.
- The segment reported 13,842 incoming orders in Q2.
- This compares to 29,604 orders during the same period last year, according to Daimler’s earnings presentation.
- Scherer described the environment as very volatile and said it is too early to call the July increase a trend.
- The company will keep monitoring the ordering activity closely.
