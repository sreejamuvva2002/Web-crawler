# WIKA USA

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** charging_infrastructure  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [EV Charging Stations Pave Way for Clean Transport - WIKA blog](https://blog.wika.com/us/wika-insight/wika-usas-e-vehicle-charging-stations-promote-clean-transportation/)  ·  `page_015026`

## Facts

- WIKA USA partnered with Georgia Power to install six Level 2 electric vehicle chargers at its Lawrenceville location.
- The initiative supports energy diversity and environmental sustainability efforts.
- Georgia Power launched the Electric Transportation Make Ready Program to facilitate electric charging infrastructure installation at public and private locations across the state.
- WIKA USA senior managers, including Bobby Herring, Director of Facilities for WIKA Americas, applied to participate in the Make Ready program.
- Under the program agreement, Georgia Power installs and maintains all necessary e-charging infrastructure—including panels, switchgear, wiring, conduits, and disconnects—at no cost to WIKA USA.
- WIKA USA was responsible for purchasing the charging stations, allocating parking lot space, and installing protective wheel stops.
- Bobby Herring described the project as an easy decision and a smart investment that promotes sustainable transportation through low/zero emissions, energy efficiency, and affordable transport modes.
- The charger installation was completed in May 2022.
- The WIKA USA headquarters facility currently operates six Level 2 charging stations with the capacity to expand to a total of 10 stations.
- Charging service is provided completely free of charge to employees and visitors.
- Within the first four months of operation, users consumed almost 3,000 kW of electricity, which is equivalent to approximately 90 gallons of gasoline.
- Employee feedback regarding the on-site charging convenience has been positive.
- Bobby Herring expressed hope that the facility's chargers will encourage others to purchase electric vehicles or plug-in hybrids.
