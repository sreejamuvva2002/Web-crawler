# Joon Georgia, Inc.

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2024-12-06

**Source:** [Chapter 2: The Formula Explained (Pt. 1) - Southeast Regional Center](https://srceb-5.com/chapter-2-the-formula-explained-part-1/)  ·  `page_056509`

## Facts

- SRC and Ajin USA have enjoyed a strong relationship for more than a decade.
- All projects pursued by SRC with Ajin USA have been located in Rural Targeted Employment Areas (TEAs).
- The latest project with Ajin USA is the Joon Georgia, Inc. factory in Bulloch County, Georgia.
- The Joon Georgia, Inc. factory is located in a Rural TEA, qualifying it for the Rural TEA visa set-aside pool under the EB-5 Program.
- Twenty percent of the entire annual EB-5 visa allotment (2,000 visas) may only be issued for projects located in Rural TEAs.
- This Rural TEA visa feature was passed in the Reform and Integrity Act of 2022.
- The Joon Georgia, Inc. factory is a critical supplier to Hyundai’s new electric vehicle plant in next door Bryan County, Georgia.
- Hyundai located its USD 5.5 billion plant in America because the country’s executive and legislative branch has signaled the importance of electric vehicles in the Inflation and Reduction Act of 2022, which provides tax credits for purchases of US-built EVs.
- Hyundai must have Joon Georgia’s output to produce its EVs.
- Ajin USA has been one of Hyundai’s most important US suppliers for more than 40 years.
- Ajin USA's product quality control is described as state-of-the-art.
- SRC and Ajin’s loan model features proprietary elements that have stood the test of time regarding investment safeguards and terms.
- USCIS sometimes gives preference to projects viewed as critical to US national interests, such as large-scale manufacturers of clean-energy technology.
