# Freyr Battery SA

## Facts

- Norway-based Freyr Battery SA stock price charged 33.19% higher the week ended June 30.
- The company assembled and successfully charged its first semi-automated lithium-ion battery unit cells at a Norwegian facility.
- CEO Tom Einar Jensen said the successful tests pave the way for the company to ramp up process automation, battery cell production, and testing of cells it produced.
- The company anticipates supplying customers with samples in the second half of this year.
- Jensen noted an “increasingly robust pipeline of customer opportunities and use cases for our products.”
- Shares gapped up and closed 20.26% higher following that announcement.
- Freyr Battery SA presented its first “capital markets” day at the New York Stock Exchange as a pitch to attract further investment.
- The stock bolted another 3% higher on June 30 after analysts and investors heard the company’s presentation.
- Weekly trading volume was triple the average.
- Jensen stated that the company is moving toward a totally automated process, which will increase production speed and yields.
- Battery applications include transportation, heating, and industrial processes transitioning from fossil fuel-based systems to electric-powered systems.
- The company has yet to show any revenue, as its operations are financed through post-IPO offerings, additional rounds of private equity, and grants from Norwegian governmental entities.
- Analysts don’t expect the company to be profitable either this year or next.
- In his NYSE presentation, Jenner mentioned the company’s planned U.S. gigafactory that will be built in Coweta County, Georgia.
- The company is also exploring a potential gigafactory site in Vaasa, Finland.
- Batteries produced at the U.S. site will qualify for subsidies under the Inflation Reduction Act.
- Investor and miner Glencore plc (OTCMKTS: GLNCY) is a financial backer of Freyr and part of the consortium to produce batteries and bring them to market.
- Nidec Corp. (OTCMKTS: NJDCY), Siemens AG (OTCMKTS: SIEGY), and Caterpillar Inc. (NYSE: CAT) are also partners in the consortium.
- Freyr Battery SA went public in February 2021 via a SPAC merger.
- The company ended its first quarter with $475 million in cash.
- 2023 capital expenditures are focused on three main projects: a customer qualification plant in Norway to verify product suitability, the Georgia Giga America plant, and maintaining progress at its Giga Arctic facility in Norway.
- The Giga Arctic facility will be the company’s first full-scale battery manufacturing facility.
- With no earnings and no revenue, and losses forecast into the foreseeable future, Freyr is a poster child for a speculative stock.
- Freyr Battery SA could easily become an acquisition target of any number of companies needing batteries.
- Potential acquisition suitors could include global automakers, utilities, or big industrial companies.
