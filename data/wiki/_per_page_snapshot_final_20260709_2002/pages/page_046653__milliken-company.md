# Milliken & Company

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Georgia plant announces plans to close, impacting 260 jobs - WJCL](https://www.wjcl.com/article/milliken-plant-georgia-closing/40624591)  ·  `page_046653`

## Facts

- Milliken & Company plans to close a textile plant in Screven County, Georgia.
- The Screven County plant currently employs 260 workers.
- Plant operations will be phased out and transferred to Milliken's facility in Cherokee County, South Carolina.
- Kevin Brown, senior vice president of global operations for Milliken’s textile business, stated the consolidation benefits customers with increased capacity and helps realize key sustainability and operational metrics.
- The company will make every effort to find new jobs for Screven County workers who wish to transfer to another Milliken location.
- The facility manufactures finished goods textiles, including engineered flame-resistant textiles, for workwear use.
- Milliken operates more than 70 facilities worldwide and will maintain 10 facilities in Georgia following the closure.
- The plant is located on the Ogeechee River.
- King America Finishing previously owned the plant before selling it to Milliken in 2014.
- In 2011, more than 30,000 fish died near the facility due to illegally dumped treated wastewater by the previous owner, King America Finishing.
- The Ogeechee Riverkeeper conservation group noted that Milliken made extensive upgrades and improvements to the plant while continuing to discharge treated wastewater under a government permit.
- Damon Mullis, executive director of the riverkeeper group, stated the closure removes the primary source of industrial effluent into the river while acknowledging the social and economic impacts on the community.
