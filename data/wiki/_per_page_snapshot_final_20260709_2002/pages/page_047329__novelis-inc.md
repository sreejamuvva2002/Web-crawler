# Novelis Inc.

## Facts

- Novelis Inc. announced its second quarter results of FY2021-22 on Wednesday, November 3.
- The company revealed net income of $237 million compared to a net loss of $37 million in Q2 FY2021.
- Net income from continuing operations stood at $239 million versus $144 million a year ago.
- Net sales improved 38 per cent year-on-year in 2QFY22 from $3 billion to $4.1 billion.
- Sales improvement was primarily driven by a 5 per cent increase in shipments and higher average aluminum prices.
- Flat rolled products shipments increased from 923 kilotonnes in Q2 FY21 to 968 kilotonnes in Q2 FY22.
- The shipment increase was buoyed by strong demand for sustainable aluminum beverage packaging and specialty products.
- Adjusted EBITDA gained 22 per cent to $553 million in Q2 FY22 from $455 million during the same period last year.
- The EBITDA gain was attributable to higher volume and favourable metal benefits.
- Novelis achieved a record Adjusted EBITDA per tonne shipped of $571 in Q2 FY2022, compared to $493 in the prior year.
- President and CEO Steve Fisher stated that the company's diverse portfolio of high-recycled-content products allowed them to capture strong market demand and deliver another record quarter of EBITDA.
- Steve Fisher noted that while navigating near-term supply chain challenges, the company is actively seeking and implementing new investment opportunities to capture growing customer demand for sustainable, low-carbon aluminum solutions.
- Novelis announced plans to invest $500 million in growth capital projects.
- The investment plans include a $375 million investment to expand rolling and recycling capabilities in Zhenjiang, China.
- Year-to-date free cash flow from continuing operations was $158 million compared to the prior year of $169 million.
- Executive Vice President and Chief Financial Officer Devinder Ahuja stated that since announcing Novelis' long-term capital allocation framework earlier this year, the company reduced gross debt, improved net leverage to below its targeted 2.5x level, and returned $100 million of capital to its common shareholder.
- Devinder Ahuja added that the company maintained adequate liquidity to effectively manage working capital needs while remaining strongly positioned to reinvest capital in new capacity and capabilities.
