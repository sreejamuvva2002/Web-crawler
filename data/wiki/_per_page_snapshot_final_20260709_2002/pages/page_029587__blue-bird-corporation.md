# Blue Bird Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-07-19

**Source:** [Acting Secretary Su visits Georgia for Blue Bird Corp. first contract signing; spotlights Biden-Harris administration’s support for unions in the South | U.S. Department of Labor](https://www.dol.gov/newsroom/releases/osec/osec20240724)  ·  `page_029587`

## Facts

- Blue Bird Corp. signed its historic first contract with the United Steelworkers (USW) on July 19, 2024, in Fort Valley, GA.
- Acting Secretary Su presided over the contract signing and toured the Blue Bird Corp. facility in Fort Valley, speaking with workers.
- Attendees at the signing included Fort Valley Mayor Jeffery Lundy, Blue Bird Corp. CEO Phil Horlock, Georgia State AFL-CIO President Yvonne Brooks, and masking production associate Dee Thomas.
- The Biden-Harris administration supported the contract signing, citing its commitment to supporting union jobs in the South and the growth of clean energy jobs facilitated by the Investing in America agenda.
- Acting Secretary Su stated that President Biden is taking a whole-of-government approach to create good union and clean energy jobs to meet job and climate goals through the Investing in America agenda.
- Dee Thomas, an 11-year Blue Bird employee and USW member, emphasized that workers win a voice through unions and affirmed that Georgia supports unions.
- Acting Secretary Su met with both union and company executives in March to challenge them to reach a deal quickly, which the CEO referred to as "the Julie Su challenge."
- The union contract reached in May includes up to 40 percent raises for some workers, new health and safety standards, and expanded retirement benefits.
- Blue Bird Corp. CEO Phil Horlock described the union relationship as a partnership and expressed gratitude that the company listened to administration guidance to finalize the deal.
