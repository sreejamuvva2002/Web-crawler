# Freyr Battery SA

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Freyr Battery Powers Up After Successful Tests, NYSE Presentation — TradingView News](https://www.tradingview.com/news/marketbeat:eb63e301f094b:0-freyr-battery-powers-up-after-successful-tests-nyse-presentation/)  ·  `page_055017`

## Facts

- Norway-based Freyr Battery SA assembled and successfully charged its first semi-automated lithium-ion battery unit cells at a Norwegian facility.
- CEO Tom Einar Jensen said the successful tests pave the way for the company to ramp up process automation, battery cell production, and testing of cells it produced.
- The company anticipates supplying customers with samples in the second half of this year.
- Jensen noted an “increasingly robust pipeline of customer opportunities and use cases for our products.”
- Freyr presented its first “capital markets” day at the New York Stock Exchange as a pitch to attract further investment.
- Jensen stated the company is moving toward a totally automated process, which will increase production speed and yields.
- Battery applications include transportation, heating, and industrial processes, which are in the process of moving from fossil fuel-based systems to electric-powered systems.
- The company has yet to show any revenue, as its operations are financed through post-IPO offerings, additional rounds of private equity, and grants from Norwegian governmental entities.
- In his NYSE presentation, Jensen mentioned the company’s planned U.S. gigafactory that will be built in Coweta County, Georgia.
- Batteries produced at the U.S. site will qualify for subsidies under the Inflation Reduction Act.
- The company is also exploring a potential gigafactory site in Vaasa, Finland.
- Partner companies revealed include investor and miner Glencore plc (financial backer), Nidec Corp., Siemens AG, and Caterpillar Inc.
- The company ended the first quarter with $475 million in cash.
- 2023 capital expenditures are focused on three main projects: its customer qualification plant in Norway, the Georgia Giga America plant, and maintaining progress at its Giga Arctic facility in Norway.
- The Georgia facility will be the company’s first full-scale battery manufacturing facility.
- Freyr went public in February 2021 via a SPAC merger.
