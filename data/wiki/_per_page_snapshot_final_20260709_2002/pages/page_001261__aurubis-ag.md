# Aurubis AG

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Aurubis US Copper Recycling: Transforming Domestic Supply](https://discoveryalert.com.au/news/aurubis-richmond-us-copper-recycling-2025/)  ·  `page_001261`

## Facts

- Aurubis AG launched a groundbreaking metal recycling facility in Richmond, Georgia.
- The project represents an $800 million investment.
- It marks Europe's largest copper producer's first major US copper investment on American soil.
- The plant is designed to process up to 180,000 metric tons of complex recycling materials annually.
- Production at the facility began in September 2025.
- Full operational capacity is expected by the first half of 2026.
- When fully operational, the plant will produce 70,000 tons of high-grade blister copper with 98-99% purity annually.
- The facility recovers valuable nickel, tin, and precious metals (including gold, silver, and platinum group metals) from waste streams.
- Input materials include electronic waste, copper cables, metal-bearing products, and printed circuit boards from consumer and industrial sources.
- Processing technologies employed include thermal processing, mechanical processing, advanced sorting, metallurgical recovery, and highly automated systems.
- The facility's 70,000-ton annual production capacity will offset approximately 7-8% of current US copper imports when operating at full capacity.
- Domestic copper demand in the United States reaches approximately 1.8 million tons annually, with roughly 50% currently imported.
- Aurubis has explicitly stated intentions to prioritize US customers for the copper produced at Richmond.
- The facility will offer direct sales to US customers at competitive market rates and integrate into Aurubis' international smelting network for specialized applications.
- Copper recycling at the facility typically requires up to 85% less energy than primary production from mined ore.
- The plant creates high-skill technical positions in metallurgical processing.
- The plant supports logistics and administrative roles.
- The plant generates indirect employment through supplier relationships.
- Aurubis CEO Toralf Haag stated that the company sees significant potential for additional projects in the United States due to high availability of recycling material and good local conditions.
- The facility supports strategic industries including defense and military applications, renewable energy infrastructure, electric vehicle manufacturing, and critical telecommunications and grid infrastructure.
- Industry experts anticipate the Richmond facility may represent just the first phase in a broader North American strategy for Aurubis.
- The plant's environmental performance will significantly exceed regulatory requirements while establishing new standards for the industry.
