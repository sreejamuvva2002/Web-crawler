# Vanguard National Trailer Corporation

## Facts

- James Wilkerson supervises workers building semitrailers from iron sheets and frames for hauling freight at a factory in Monon, Indiana.
- Vanguard National Trailer Corporation is a wholly owned subsidiary of Shenzhen-based China International Marine Containers (CIMC).
- HPA Monon previously occupied the site, employing over 400 workers in the late 1980s and early 1990s before slashing its workforce to less than 100 and closing in May 2002.
- Vanguard came into the picture in 2003 with new equipment, investment, and management, gradually recovering the factory to a healthy operation.
- Vanguard now employs over 400 people, making it Monon's largest employer and contributing some 80 percent to the local gross domestic product.
- Randy Mitchell, president of White County Economic Development, stated that Vanguard is so important to the town's health that probably every family has had a member work there now or previously.
- The two semitrailer companies in White County, Vanguard and Wabash, together produce most of the trailers in the United States right now.
- Vanguard CEO Charlie Mudd stated that hundreds of jobs were created in Monon thanks to the CIMC investment.
- Mudd noted that CIMC vehicle group companies across the globe share ideas, information, resources, suppliers, and strategy in ways no other trailer company can.
- Vanguard is expanding by opening another factory in Trenton, Georgia, creating about 200 jobs.
- Mudd commented that the best success comes from combining Chinese knowledge and capabilities with American knowledge and opportunities, describing it as 'True collaboration.'
