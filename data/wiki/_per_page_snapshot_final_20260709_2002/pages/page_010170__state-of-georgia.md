# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2022-09

**Source:** [Georgia named No. 1 state for business](https://valdostatoday.com/news-2/region/2022/09/georgia-named-no-1-state-for-business/)  ·  `page_010170`

## Facts

- Area Development magazine named Georgia the number one state for business for a ninth consecutive year.
- No other state has earned this distinction for so many years.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced the ranking alongside executives from Kia Georgia, Bridgestone Golf, and W.C. Bradley Company (parent of Char-Broil), plus members of the General Assembly, state and local economic developers, and local officials.
- The announcement took place at the Kia Georgia Training Center in West Point.
- Governor Kemp cited partnership approach, conservative governing, pandemic response, world-class infrastructure, award-winning workforce training programs, pro-business environment, and hardworking Georgians as reasons for the ranking.
- Area Development's annual poll considers approximately 50 leading site consulting firms across the U.S. and evaluates 13 different factors.
- Georgia earned a No. 1 ranking for its workforce development program, Georgia Quick Start, for the thirteenth consecutive year.
- Georgia earned top rankings in six additional categories, including cost of doing business, cooperative and responsive state government, competitive labor environment, available real estate, and tied for first place in energy availability and costs.
- Area Development's Editor Geraldine Gambale stated that state and local economic developers in Georgia consistently work together to guide companies through location and retention processes.
- The 13 evaluation categories include overall cost of doing business, cooperative/responsive state government, favorable regulatory environment, corporate tax environment, business incentives, competitive labor environment, workforce development programs, logistics/infrastructure, access to capital/project funding, available real estate, energy availability/costs, speed of permitting, and site-readiness programs.
- Georgia placed in the top 10 for all 13 categories.
- Lt. Governor Geoff Duncan attributed the ranking to business/workforce centric policies, an influx of major investments from small and large businesses, and one of the lowest unemployment rates in state history.
- Speaker David Ralston highlighted pro-business policies like cutting taxes and incentivizing investment to create private sector jobs across the state.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that hundreds of companies chose Georgia in the last year alone to create future jobs.
- During fiscal year 2022 (July 1, 2021 – June 30, 2022), investments in expansions and new locations totaled more than $21.2 billion.
- 51,132 jobs were created in the state through 358 projects supported by GDEcD’s Global Commerce team during that period.
- The two largest projects in state history are Rivian and Hyundai Motor Group, which totaled more than $10.5 billion in investments and 15,600 new jobs combined.
- Area Development was founded in 1965 and is considered the leading executive magazine covering corporate site selection and relocation.
- GDEcD serves as the state’s sales and marketing arm for attracting business investment, encouraging industry expansion, locating markets, promoting tourism/arts/film/music/digital entertainment, and mobilizing state resources for economic development.
