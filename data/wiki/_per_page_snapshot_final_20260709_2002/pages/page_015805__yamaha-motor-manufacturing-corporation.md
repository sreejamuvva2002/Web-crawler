# Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2022-06-20

**Source:** [Yamaha breaks ground on innovative SMART Warehouse in Newnan | The Citizen](https://thecitizen.com/2022/06/20/yamaha-breaks-ground-on-innovative-smart-warehouse-in-newnan/)  ·  `page_015805`

## Facts

- Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation (YMMC), based in Newnan Georgia, broke ground June 9 on a new, advanced, and innovative “SMART” Warehouse.
- The SMART Warehouse is the first of its kind for Yamaha Motor Company worldwide.
- The facility uses state-of-the-art automated storage and retrieval systems.
- The new warehouse will be 200,000-square-foot.
- The facility will reduce response time for fulfilling dealer orders for Yamaha WaveRunners, Golf Cars, ATVs, and Side-by-Sides by more than 50 percent.
- The automated system is described as a seven-story vending machine that uses high-speed automation to send parts immediately to build recreational vehicles.
- Mike Chrzanowski, President and CEO of Yamaha Motor Corporation, stated the new warehouse will improve overall operational efficiency and material flow velocity to factory assembly lines to get product to dealers and customers faster.
- Demand for all outdoor recreational products produced at this factory continues to exceed supply.
- SMART is an acronym for Sequenced Material and Reduced Transportation.
- The medium parts automated storage and retrieval system will be capable of retrieving and putting away 240 containers per hour, compared to the current rate of 170 containers per hour.
- The small part automated system will be capable of handling 2400 containers per hour, in comparison to the current capability of 550 containers per day.
- The new systems will enable Yamaha to reduce forklifts by 50 percent using automatic conveyors and tuggers to move parts through the SMART center to the factories.
- The significant investment underscores the company’s long-term commitment to YMCC employees as well as the City of Newnan and Coweta County.
- The system will bring higher paid and more skilled jobs into Coweta County, including robotics experts, software developers, and systems engineers.
- The warehouse was designed by SSI SCHAEFER.
- The facility will include an observation platform for groups such as students from local schools to learn about manufacturing and automated processes.
- Yamaha expects to complete the SMART Warehouse in 2024.
- Due to strong product demand and continuous growth, Yamaha is currently hiring.
- Most careers at Yamaha start Contract to Perm.
- Yamaha Motor Corporation, USA (YMUS) is a recognized leader in the outdoor recreation industry.
- YMUS product offerings include Motorcycles and Scooters, ATV and Side-by-Side vehicles, Snowmobiles, WaveRunner Personal Watercraft, Boats, Outboard Motors, Outdoor Power Equipment, Power Assist Bicycles, Golf Cars, Power Assist Wheelchair Systems, Surface Mount Technology (SMT) and Robotic Machines, Unmanned Helicopters, Accessories, Apparel, and Yamalube products.
- YMUS products are sold through a nationwide network of distributors and dealers in the United States.
- YMUS has a corporate office in California, two corporate offices in Georgia, facilities in Wisconsin and Alabama, and factory operations in Tennessee and Georgia.
- Additional U.S.-based subsidiaries include Yamaha Marine Systems Company (YMSC) with divisions Bennett Marine (Florida), Kracor Systems (Wisconsin) and Siren Marine, Inc. (Rhode Island), Skeeter Boats (Texas) with division G3 Boats (Missouri), and Yamaha Precision Propeller (Indiana).
