# Coweta County

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2012-01-15

**Source:** [2011 a good year for business in Coweta | The Citizen](https://thecitizen.com/2012/01/15/2011-good-year-business-coweta/)  ·  `page_015626`

## Facts

- Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corp. announced that production of its all-terrain sport vehicle (ATV) line currently located in Japan will be transferred to the company’s Newnan facility.
- Yamaha employs more than 1,000 people at the Newnan facility which consists of three plants.
- The Newnan facility manufactures four recreational product lines including golf cars, ATVs, SxS utility vehicles and is the only location in the world where the Yamaha Waverunner personal watercraft is built.
- Chromalloy expanded at its Shenandoah Industrial Park facility by adding a gas turbine engine service capability, creating 125 jobs.
- West Georgia Technical College began construction on two buildings totaling 83,500 square feet at its new 38.5-acre campus facility near Turkey Creek Road and I-85.
- West Georgia Technical College is expected to begin classes in fall 2012 with degree and certification offerings in Allied Health Care including registered nursing, medical assistant, physical therapy assistant, electronic medical records technician, certified first responder and home health aide.
- West Georgia Technical College anticipates future construction of seven additional buildings, bringing the campus to 200,000 square feet.
- Coweta County Development Authority visited 37 industries in the county to assist companies with expansion projects and help identify resources available to improve operations.
- The Coweta County Development Authority works with economic development partners including the Georgia Dept. of Economic Development, the Georgia Dept. of Labor and Georgia Tech.
