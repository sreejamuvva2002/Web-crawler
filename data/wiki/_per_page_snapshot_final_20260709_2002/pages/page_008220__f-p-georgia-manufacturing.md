# F&P Georgia Manufacturing

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2002-09-07

**Source:** [F&P Georgia announces expansio | Local New |  | northwestgeorgianews.com](https://www.northwestgeorgianews.com/f-p-georgia-announces-expansio-local-new/article_dc911f79-2fad-571e-bcf4-accb0a8ab18c.html)  ·  `page_008220`

## Facts

- During Friday’s open house at F&P Georgia Manufacturing, the company announced it is planning an expansion to meet increasing demand from Honda Motor Co.
- Akihide Fukuda, chairman of parent company F-Tech, stated the open house is an opportunity to prepare for expansion.
- The F&P Georgia plant is located in the Floyd County Industrial Park.
- The plant supplies subframe assemblies and other components to the Honda Odyssey minivan plant in Lincoln, Ala.
- The Alabama plant currently turns out 650 minivans a day.
- In December, the Alabama plant announced it would double production to 1,300 vehicles a day by 2004.
- Yuichi Fukuda serves as president of F&P Georgia.
- F&P Georgia will add a second production line to handle an output of 1,300 units a day.
- The new production line and equipment will be installed in time to meet Honda’s production increase in 2004.
- The second production line can be built within the existing plant footprint.
- F&P Georgia holds a 10-year option to purchase an additional 18 acres in the industrial park for future expansion.
- F&P Georgia had 80 employees when it began production in November 2001.
- F&P Georgia currently employs 133 workers operating across two shifts.
- Several hundred attendees, including company officials, Honda representatives, suppliers, and city, county, and state officials, toured the plant during the open house.
- The primary component manufactured at the Rome plant is the subframe assembly, which connects the engine, front suspension, and steering in the Honda Odyssey.
- Each subframe consists of 45 separate components assembled with 208 spot welds, many performed by robotic welding machines.
- F&P Georgia broke ground on its facility in November 2000.
- F&P Georgia made its first shipment to Honda in November 2001.
- The plant produces 650 subframe assemblies per day, averaging 325 per shift.
- The facility averages 7.2 shipments per day to Honda in Lincoln, Ala.
- Each shipment to Honda averages 90 vehicle sets.
- F&P Georgia typically maintains one day’s worth of finished goods and five days’ worth of raw materials on hand at the plant.
- Parent company F-Tech operates three other North American operations: F&P America, F&P Canada, and Dyna-Mig.
- F-Tech was founded in Japan in 1947.
- In 1949, F-Tech began supplying components to Honda when the automaker produced only motorcycles.
- F-Tech opened its first North American operations in 1994.
- F-Tech maintains subsidiaries in Korea, India, Taiwan, Mexico, Brazil, and the United Kingdom.
- The F&P Georgia facility occupies a 144,800-square-foot building on 51 acres within the Floyd County Industrial Park.
- Employee headcount is projected to potentially reach 300-350 by 2005.
- Total investment in the F&P Georgia plant is $16.8 million.
- The facility manufactures suspension components for the Honda Odyssey minivan assembled in Lincoln, Ala.
- F-Tech’s North America Group also includes F&P Manufacturing Inc. in Tottenham, Ontario, Canada; F&P America Manufacturing Inc. in Troy, Ohio; and Dyna-Mig in Ontario.
