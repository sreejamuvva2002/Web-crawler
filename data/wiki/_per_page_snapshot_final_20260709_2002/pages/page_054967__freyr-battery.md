# FREYR Battery

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-02-06

**Source:** [Plans for nearly $2.6B FREYR Battery plant in Coweta County nixed | FOX 5 Atlanta](https://www.fox5atlanta.com/news/plans-nearly-2-6b-freyr-battery-plant-coweta-county-nixed)  ·  `page_054967`

## Facts

- A planned nearly $2.6 billion lithium-ion battery plant will not be coming to Coweta County after all.
- FREYR Battery plans to exercise its option to pull out of the planned $2.57 billion plant.
- The canceled plant would have brought 723 new jobs to Georgia.
- The company exited a deal that was originally announced in November 2022.
- In a letter to the Coweta County Development Authority dated Jan. 21, FREYR Battery stated the decision was made because it realigned its near-term strategic goals.
- The company acknowledged that the Coweta County Development Authority, Coweta County, and the State of Georgia provided extraordinary assistance and support.
- The company stated the decision does not reflect concerns about Georgia's support, calling it a great state to do business and noting they would remain advocates based on positive engagements.
- FREYR Battery is a Norwegian-based clean energy solutions provider that was seeking to capture more of the U.S. market within the next 10 years, with the Coweta County factory playing a key role.
- The company cited rising interest rates, declining battery prices, and changes in corporate leadership as reasons for canceling the plant.
