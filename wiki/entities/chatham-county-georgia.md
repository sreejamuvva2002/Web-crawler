# Chatham County, Georgia

**Type:** Government Program  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[charging-infrastructure|Charging Infrastructure]]  ·  **Location:** Unincorporated areas outside Savannah, Georgia, Chatham County, Georgia, United States

## Overview

Chatham County is developing a plan to install public electric vehicle (EV) charging stations in unincorporated areas outside Savannah, focusing on equitable access for low-income residents and flood-resilient locations.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Planning and siting of public EV charging infrastructure to expand access across Chatham County.

## Key facts

- The project aims to address the lack of charging infrastructure that can be a barrier to EV adoption for lower-income residents.
- Nine potential locations were identified based on accessibility, flood risk, evacuation routes, and service to low-income areas.
- Public feedback raised safety concerns at some proposed sites, prompting officials to consider lighting and security improvements.
- Funding comes from a $21 million grant awarded by the Southeast Crescent Regional Commission for infrastructure and economic development.
- Consultants surveyed approximately 50 spots throughout the county before recommending nine locations across Georgetown, Garden City, Thunderbolt, and the islands.
- Both Level 2 (slower) and DC Fast (Level 3) chargers will be installed to serve a range of vehicle types.
- The county emphasizes equitable access, stating it is trying to provide charging options for residents who cannot afford home chargers.
- Third-party operators are expected to be needed to build and run the stations due to the challenging business case without existing customers.
- A pilot project is being considered that would allow EV rentals in low-income qualified housing developments.
- Savannah’s earlier participation in Georgia’s “Make Ready” program resulted in four stations with eight chargers each, demonstrating a precedent for public-private collaboration.
- Residents expressed concerns about safety, especially lighting and security at the proposed site near Highways 204 & 17.
