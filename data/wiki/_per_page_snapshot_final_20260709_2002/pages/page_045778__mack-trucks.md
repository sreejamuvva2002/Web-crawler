# Mack Trucks

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-06-25

**Source:** [Mack’s Anthem to live on, with completely new design and purpose - Truck News](https://www.trucknews.com/equipment/macks-anthem-to-live-on-with-completely-new-design-and-purpose/1003198923/)  ·  `page_045778`

## Facts

- The Mack Anthem has been redesigned and repurposed to serve as the company’s new regional haul tractor.
- The Anthem relinquished its title of on-highway flagship to the recently introduced Mack Pioneer.
- The new Anthem was introduced June 25.
- The new Anthem shares design similarities with the all-new Pioneer both internally and externally.
- The Anthem's bumper-to-back-of-cab (BBC) measurement has been shortened from 117 inches to 113 inches to afford greater maneuverability.
- The cab has been widened by nine inches to increase interior space and driver comfort.
- A 45-degree wheel cut improves maneuverability.
- A more sloped hood improves forward visibility by about 12%.
- The original Mack Anthem was available in only two sleeper configurations: a short 48-inch service sleeper and a 70-inch studio.
- The lack of intermediate sleeper options previously cost Mack business in applications like bulk haul.
- The new Anthem offers more configurations including low-roof sleeper, day cab, mid-roof sleeper in 44- and 64-inch lengths, and rigid day cab.
- Jonathan Randall is president of Mack Trucks North America.
- Combined with the Pioneer's 76-inch sleeper, Mack now has every sleeper configuration between the Pioneer and Anthem to address all application needs.
- Regional customers can dress down the truck’s aerodynamics to prioritize maneuverability over fuel economy.
- The mid-roof sleeper is more than seven feet high, providing ample headroom for taller drivers.
- Brian Wright is a Mack Trucks consultant.
- Customers can opt to remove the chassis fairing and air dam to allow for greater ride height to navigate city streets, potholes, curbs, and rugged terrain.
- Canadian customers may forego small fuel economy gains in wintry conditions for greater ground clearance due to snow and ice buildup.
- The Anthem's BBC is about a foot shorter than the Pioneer’s, with four fewer inches between the bumper and front axle for a better turning radius.
- The hood is now made of composite material rather than fiberglass and comes in three sections for easier and more cost-effective repairs.
- The composite hood is lighter, stronger, and has better paint adhesion.
- A larger radiator opening improves cooling capabilities.
- Six different wheelbases are available ranging from 16 to 19 feet in length.
- Blake Routh is senior product manager of on-highway trucks at Mack.
- The Pioneer features a significantly longer 125-inch BBC and availability of a 76-inch sleeper, while the Anthem is limited to a 64-inch sleeper.
- Prioritizing fuel efficiency points toward the Pioneer, which offers up to an 11% improvement over the old Anthem.
- Prioritizing maneuverability points toward the Anthem.
- The new Anthem features the newly named Mack Protect suite of active safety systems combining camera and radar with braking capabilities.
- Mack Protect includes frontal collision and rollover protection, adaptive cruise control, automatic emergency braking, pedestrian detection, and road sign detection.
- Side safety features include blind spot assistance, lane departure assistance, side pedestrian detection, lane centering, and oversteer guidance through Command Steer.
- All lights are LED throughout and heated for colder climates, with additional LED fog lights and cornering lights.
- The Anthem takes new electronic air brakes from the Pioneer, which are easier to control and reduce driver injury risk upon exiting before brake application due to automatic activation.
- Power is supplied by the newly named MP13 (previously MP8) 13-liter engine in two variations.
- The variable geometry turbo (VGT) version produces up to 515 hp and 1,900 lb.-ft. of torque.
- The MP13 HE (high efficiency) uses turbocompounding to convert previously wasted heat to engine torque, available with power ratings up to 505 hp and 1,950 lb.-ft. of torque.
- Engine design improvements include a shorter seven-wave piston, a new injector, and a longer connecting rod for almost total combustion in the cylinder.
- More thorough combustion leaves less soot for the aftertreatment system to deal with.
- Todd Shakespeare is head of leasing and service solution sales at Mack.
- Most software updates can be done over the air to reduce downtime.
- In ideal conditions, oil change intervals are stretched from 75,000 to 90,000 miles (120,000 to 144,000 km).
- Chassis lube intervals are stretched from 15,000 to 30,000 miles (24,000 to 48,000 km).
- These maintenance interval improvements offer a maintenance savings of about US$685 a year.
- The truck typically requires less maintenance than other models even in harsher environments.
- Kent Worthington is sales development manager for powertrain solutions at Mack.
- The Mack powertrain learns routes and optimizes shifting based on terrain and load conditions.
- Field testing proved better performance in stop-and-go driving and less downshifting required on hills due to increased torque available at lower RPMs.
- Increased torque at lower RPMs reduces driver fatigue as well as component wear.
- The new Anthem will be publicly shown for the first time in October at the American Trucking Associations’ annual Management Conference & Exhibition.
- Orders will be taken as early as August when the price book is released.
- Production will begin in January 2026 with customer deliveries commencing soon thereafter.
- The truck is available in Mack’s Truck Builder configurator for customers to play with specs and configurations.
