# Yamaha Motor Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-03-10

**Source:** [Yamaha to move U.S. headquarters to Georgia | News | times-herald.com](https://www.times-herald.com/news/yamaha-to-move-u-s-headquarters-to-georgia/article_caaf3287-445e-4701-a42e-85ae5065afca.html)  ·  `page_015661`

## Facts

- Brian Kemp announced that Yamaha Motor Co., Ltd. will relocate its U.S. headquarters to Kennesaw, moving from California after roughly 50 years of operation there.
- The relocation of the Yamaha Motor Corp. U.S.A. headquarters is expected to begin this year and continue through 2028.
- Yamaha manufactures ATVs, boat engines, personal watercraft, and other motorized products.
- The company already has a substantial presence in Newnan and employs more than 2,300 people across Georgia.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that welcoming Yamaha's American headquarters to the No. 1 state for business is a testament to what Georgia offers job creators from around the world.
- The company operates a major manufacturing facility in Newnan known as the Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation of America plant.
- The 1.3-million-square-foot Newnan plant opened in 1986 and employs about 2,000 workers.
- Yamaha moved its marine division to Kennesaw in 1999.
- The company expanded its Kennesaw location with a 75,000-square-foot Marine Innovation Center in 2023.
- In 2019, the company relocated its motorsports operations to Georgia to be closer to its existing facilities.
- Governor Kemp first discussed the potential relocation with Yamaha executives in October during the Southeast U.S./Japan annual gathering in Tokyo.
- Yamaha Motor Co., Ltd. was founded in 1955 and is headquartered in Iwata, Shizuoka, Japan.
- The company produces motorcycles, marine products, power equipment, and robotics under its corporate mission of being a “Kando Creating Company,” focused on delivering new excitement and enriching lives around the world.
