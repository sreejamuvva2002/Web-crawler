# Wabash National Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [J.B. Hunt orders 6,000 containers from Wabash National | FleetOwner](https://www.fleetowner.com/news/article/21661562/jb-hunt-orders-6000-containers-from-wabash-national)  ·  `page_014457`

## Facts

- Wabash National Corp. received orders exceeding $70 million from J.B. Hunt Transport Services Inc. for 6,000 53-foot DuraPlate domestic containers and a smaller number of container chassis.
- The 53-foot domestic containers feature a new design utilizing Wabash National's proprietary DuraPlate technology.
- The stackable, high-cube design includes an uninterrupted sidewall and interior dimensions that closely resemble conventional trailers.
- These will be the first 53-foot units in the J.B. Hunt fleet with sufficient strength to be positioned on the bottom of a "double-stack" on railcars.
- Richard E. Dessimoz, acting CEO of Wabash, stated pleasure that J.B. Hunt selected the DuraPlate container for intermodal operations alongside the DuraPlate trailer for over-the-road operations.
- Wabash said the containers incorporate features designed to reduce maintenance costs and increase durability.
- These will be the first production domestic containers to use the DuraPlate sidewall material.
- The DuraPlate material is composed of a polyethylene plastic core sandwiched between two sheets of pre-painted high-strength galvanized steel.
- Wabash claims the resulting panel is efficient, lightweight, and incredibly strong, providing advantages over aluminum plate and traditional sheet and post sidewall designs.
- Kirk Thompson, president & CEO of J.B. Hunt Transportation Services, expressed delight with the performance and quality of their DuraPlate van trailers and noted the opportunity to convert their intermodal container fleet to exclusively 53-foot equipment by replacing 48-foot equipment with stackable 53-foot DuraPlate containers.
- Thompson stated that J.B. Hunt’s intermodal customers will have access to North America's largest fleet of exclusively 53-foot containers, while rail partners can stack them on top in traditional configurations or at the bottom of 53-foot rail cars.
- With the addition of DuraPlate domestic containers, Wabash now offers a complete line of intermodal equipment including domestic containers, piggyback trailers, and bi-modal equipment.
