# Industrial Demonstrations Program

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [Industrial Demonstrations Program Selected and Awarded Projects: Cement and Concrete | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/cmei/oced/industrial-demonstrations-program-selected-and-awarded-projects-cement-and-concrete)  ·  `page_006117`

## Facts

- Federal Cost Share up to $61.7 million awarded to Roanoke Cement Company, LLC in Troutville, VA.
- Roanoke Cement Company (RCC) leads the Calcined Clay Production for Limestone Calcined Clay Cement project.
- RCC plans to demonstrate utilizing widely available clay types to minimize clinker use.
- Clinker is identified as the most carbon-intensive component in cement production.
- The project validates the market for calcined clays in a region with high demand.
- Project anticipates reducing carbon intensity by approximately 83% compared to products with clinker.
- Federal Cost Share up to $189 million awarded to Brimstone Commercial, LLC at TBD location.
- Brimstone Commercial, LLC leads the Deeply Decarbonized Cement Project.
- Project plans to construct a first-of-a-kind commercial-scale demonstration plant.
- Plant will annually produce an estimated 103,000 metric tons of decarbonized OPC, supplementary cementitious materials, and smelter grade alumina.
- Smelter grade alumina is identified as a critical mineral.
- Project avoids more than 77,000 metric tons of carbon dioxide emissions per year using calcium silicate rocks and alternative industrial production methods.
- Project builds on previously supported Advanced Research Projects Agency–Energy (ARPA-E) research pathways.
- Project expects to reduce technical risks and demonstrate sufficient demand for future industrial-scale decarbonized facilities.
- Federal Cost Share up to $86.9 million awarded to Sublime Systems in Holyoke, MA.
- Sublime Systems leads the First Commercial Electrochemical Cement Manufacturing project.
- Plans to build a new ultra-low-carbon cement manufacturing facility in Holyoke, Massachusetts.
- Method replaces carbon-intensive limestone with abundant calcium silicate-based feedstock.
- Produces industry-standard cement electrochemically instead of using high heat.
- Builds on previously supported ARPA-E research pathways.
- Aims to strengthen American supply chains for low-carbon products and catalyze industry-wide change.
- Federal Cost Share up to $500 million awarded to National Cement Company of California, Inc. in Lebec, CA.
- National Cement Company of California, Inc. (NCC-CA) leads the Lebec Net Zero Project.
- Project plans to produce carbon-neutral cement at the Lebec, CA cement plant.
- Deploys three strategies: using locally sourced biomass from agricultural byproducts like pistachio shells to replace fossil fuel reliance; producing LC3 using calcined clay; and capturing/sequestering remaining CO2 emissions estimated at 950,000 metric tons each year.
- Aims to demonstrate how decarbonization levers drive emissions of existing U.S. cement production facilities to net-zero.
- Federal Cost Share up to $500 million awarded to Heidelberg Materials US, Inc. in Mitchell, Indiana.
- Heidelberg Materials leads the Mitchell Cement Plant Decarbonization Project.
- Plans to construct and operate an integrated carbon capture, transport, and storage system at a newly modernized plant in Mitchell, Indiana.
- Captures at least 95% of carbon dioxide from one of the largest cement plants in the nation.
- Stores captured CO2 in a geologic formation beneath the plant property.
- Expects to prevent two million tons of carbon dioxide per year from entering the atmosphere.
- Demonstrates a pathway to decarbonize existing U.S. cement plants.
- Builds on ongoing OCED awarded front-end engineering and design study and sequestration site development.
- Represents one of the first carbon capture and storage projects for cement facilities in the nation.
- DOE selection for award negotiations is not a commitment to issue an award or provide funding.
- DOE and each selectee will negotiate a cooperative agreement, with funding provided only after negotiations complete and Contracting Officer executes the agreement.
- DOE may cancel award negotiations and rescind selection before a funding agreement is executed.
- Federal Cost Share up to $215.6 million awarded to Summit Materials, Inc. for the Low-Carbon Calcined Clay Cement Demonstration project.
- Project locations include Port Deposit, Maryland; McIntyre, Georgia; Elmendorf, Texas; and Sulphur Springs, Texas.
- Summit Materials leads the Low-Carbon Calcined Clay Cement Demonstration project.
- Plans to construct four new calcination facilities in Maryland, Georgia, and Texas.
- Demonstrates viability of displacing high-emitting limestone-based cement with a clay-based product in multiple geographies.
- Range of sites builds case for replicability given diverse clay sources and availability of cementitious products across the U.S.
- Scale offers potential for substantial emissions reductions, preventing 1.1 million metric tons of carbon emissions per year.
- Addresses 2% of the U.S. 2030 projected demand for cement.
- Expects to generate more than 4,000 direct, indirect, and induced jobs across the four sites during the project lifetime.
- Summit plans to negotiate a Community Workforce Agreement or Project Labor Agreement for construction phases at each site.
- Community engagement goals and strategies may evolve at each location to respond to community needs.
- Plans to provide tailored workforce training investments, including manufacturing training certifications, engineering programs, and partnerships with local workforce and educational institutions.
