# Kia

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-04-16

**Source:** [Kia EV9 And EV6 Now Eligible For Full $7,500 Tax Credit With One Trim Left Out](https://www.soscip.org/kia-ev9-and-ev6-now-eligible-full-7500-tax-credit/)  ·  `page_010189`

## Facts

- Kia America reported record-breaking first-quarter sales.
- Kia attributes its U.S. sales surge in part to a manufacturing shift and production ramp-up of the EV9 and EV6 at its plant in Georgia.
- Eric Watson serves as VP of Sales for Kia America.
- In 2023, Kia decided to move EV9 production from South Korea to the U.S. to maximize benefits of the Inflation Reduction Act (IRA).
- The IRA provides a $7,500 tax credit for qualifying EV purchases.
- Initially in 2024, the EV9 only qualified for half of that credit ($3,750) because its batteries were sourced from South Korea and China.
- In 2024, Kia changed its battery sourcing to its Georgia facility, making the EV9 fully eligible for the complete $7,500 federal tax credit.
- Starting in 2025, the EV6 will join the EV9 in being produced on American soil for the first time.
- Most trims of the EV6 will be eligible for the full federal tax credit starting in 2025.
- High-performance GT trims of both the EV6 and EV9 remain in production in South Korea.
- Because the GT trims are produced in South Korea, they do not qualify for the federal EV tax credit under current IRA rules.
- The GT models offer more horsepower and sportier styling than standard versions.
- Leasing may serve as a workaround for buyers interested in GT versions because IRA restrictions on tax credits do not apply to leased EVs in the same way.
- A 25% tariff on imported vehicles was announced in early April.
- President Donald Trump paused the 25% tariff for 90 days.
- If the tariffs are reinstated, GT trims made in South Korea could face significantly higher ownership costs due to combined tariff impacts and tax credit ineligibility.
- Kia is doubling down on U.S.-based EV production to align with evolving government policies and buyer incentives.
