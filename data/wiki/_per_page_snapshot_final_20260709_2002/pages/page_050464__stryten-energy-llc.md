# Stryten Energy LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-10-26

**Source:** [Stryten Energy to Emphasize Increasing Market Opportunities for Enhanced Flooded Battery Technology at AAPEX 2023 - EIN Presswire](https://www.einnews.com/pr_news/664330392/stryten-energy-to-emphasize-increasing-market-opportunities-for-enhanced-flooded-battery-technology-at-aapex-2023)  ·  `page_050464`

## Facts

- Stryten Energy LLC is headquartered in Alpharetta, Georgia.
- The company features its Enhanced Flooded Battery (EFB) technology along with a full lineup of energy storage solutions for the automotive aftermarket.
- Nearly 30% of new start-stop vehicles sold in the U.S. now come equipped with an EFB.
- According to the EPA, half of new vehicles produced in 2021 included start-stop technology.
- Independent third-party testing found Stryten Energy’s EFB offers three times the cycle life and twice the charge acceptance compared to standard flooded batteries.
- Testing showed Stryten Energy’s EFB yields more than two times the cycle life of a competing premium flooded battery.
- The EFB contains a more robust alloy for higher heat tolerance at a lower grid thickness.
- The EFB has an average capacity output 50% greater than Absorbed Glass Matt (AGM) for an equally sized battery.
- The EFB outperforms AGM in under-hood applications for vehicles equipped with a flooded battery from the factory, given its greater energy availability, increased battery life and high heat tolerance.
- Stryten Energy will feature these technologies at AAPEX 2023, which takes place October 31 - November 2 at the Venetian Expo in Las Vegas.
- Bill Nonnamaker serves as Vice President of Sales, Transportation for Stryten Energy.
- Bill Nonnamaker stated that batteries need more energy to support start-stop technology alongside conveniences and advanced safety features, and choosing the right battery will become increasingly important as vehicle power demands evolve.
- Nonnamaker noted the Stryten EFB is specifically designed to resist cycling-related failures associated with increased electrical demands of modern vehicles.
- The company will make a $100 donation (up to $10,000) to the University of the Aftermarket Foundation for every selfie taken with the Stryten Jeep at booth 2620.
- The University of the Aftermarket Foundation funds scholarships and educational grants to help talented individuals maximize career potential in the automotive aftermarket industry.
- Bob Egan, MAAP, chairman of the University of the Aftermarket Foundation, stated: 'We appreciate the creative way that Stryten Energy is supporting the University of the Aftermarket Foundation and encourage all AAPEX attendees to visit booth 2620 to take a selfie.'
- Stryten Energy implemented LED lighting and will power down its booth during non-show hours for efficient energy use.
- The company is using QR Codes instead of printed literature to minimize paper waste and replacing branded plastic water bottles with a water cooler and recycled cups.
- Stryten Energy partners with recognized companies to meet demand for reliable and sustainable energy storage capacity across Essential Power, Motive Power, Transportation, Military and Government sectors.
- The company's stored energy technologies include advanced lead, lithium and vanadium redox flow batteries, intelligent chargers and energy performance management software.
