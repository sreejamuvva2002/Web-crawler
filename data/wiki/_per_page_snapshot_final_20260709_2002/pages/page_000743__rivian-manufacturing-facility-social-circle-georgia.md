# Rivian Manufacturing Facility (Social Circle, Georgia)

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-01-11

**Source:** [Social Circle residents upset over upset over planned electric vehicle plant | FOX 5 Atlanta](https://www.fox5atlanta.com/news/social-circle-residents-upset-over-upset-over-planned-electric-vehicle-plant)  ·  `page_000743`

## Facts

- More than a hundred people packed a Social Circle High School to tell economic development leaders they do not want an electric vehicle company to build a manufacturing plant in their community.
- Residents say they feel planning was done in private, while project leaders said they had monthly public meetings about the site as early as last May that few people attended.
- Tommy Wasendorf, who has lived in the area for more than 20 years, stated at a Joint Development Authority (JDA) town hall: "We're not getting any say in this, this was done at the state capitol."
- The planned factory will stand at the Stanton Springs Industrial Park, located an hour east of Atlanta.
- Walton County Economic Development director Shane Short stated that everything was done through protocol nondisclosure agreements to prevent competing companies from interfering with plans.
- Other JDA members confirmed there have been monthly public meetings to outline the process since mid-2021.
- The JDA recognizes public outrage began when Gov. Brian Kemp announced the project at the end of the year.
- Tommy Wasendorf, a manufacturing worker, noted that non-disclosure agreements protect companies but do not protect neighborhoods and communities.
- Gov. Brian Kemp announced plans for a multi-billion-dollar Rivian plant last month (December 2021).
- Rivian produces Amazon electric vehicles.
- The project promises $5 billion in investments and 7,500 jobs with a starting salary of $56,000 a year.
- Construction on the 2,000-acre facility is scheduled to begin this summer (2022), with first cars being built in 2024.
- The recurring question at the town hall was whether the development can be stopped.
- Shane Short stated that stopping the project during the rezoning process is possible but will be difficult.
- About 26 property owners willingly sold their land for the development, and no eminent domain was used to finalize the project.
- Residents at the town hall plan to research ways to prevent the plant's construction.
