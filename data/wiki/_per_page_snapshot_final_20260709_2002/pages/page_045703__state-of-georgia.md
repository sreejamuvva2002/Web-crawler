# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2024-02

**Source:** [Georgia breaks export records for third year](https://valdostatoday.com/news-2/region/2024/02/georgia-breaks-export-records-for-third-year/)  ·  `page_045703`

## Facts

- The State of Georgia surpassed export records with almost $50 billion in 2023 for the third straight year.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp joined the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) to announce that the state surpassed $49.7 billion in exports in 2023.
- The 2023 export figure eclipsed the record-setting $47 billion recorded in 2022.
- Georgia currently ranks seventh in the U.S. for dollar value of trade.
- The state facilitates more than $186 billion in total trade to 222 unique countries and territories.
- Georgia ranks 12th in the country for dollar value of exports.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that for the third year in a row, Georgia has broken every record when it comes to exports, bringing billions of dollars to communities across the state.
- An estimated 87 percent of those exports come from small businesses.
- International trade touches every county in the state, supporting jobs in logistics, manufacturing, agriculture, and more.
- Top exports for 2023 included civilian aircraft products, motor vehicles, turbojets and turbines, poultry, and chemical woodpulp products.
- Canada, Mexico, China, Germany, and Singapore were the top destinations for Georgia exports.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that international representatives in overseas markets have helped find homes for Georgia products and goods for decades.
- These representatives leverage their unique understanding of overseas markets to support small businesses and increase the state’s overall exports.
- Representatives work with Georgia-based export specialists to ensure Georgia businesses have an advantage and the economy remains strong.
- Support from the General Assembly and local leadership helps Georgia identify new opportunities in the global economy.
- In 2023, the state’s top five bilateral trading partners were Mexico, South Korea, Canada, China, and Germany.
- There was notable year-over-year growth in total trade between the State of Georgia and South Korea.
- Markets where Georgia maintains full-time representation accounted for 67 percent of exports and 71 percent of bilateral trade in 2023.
- Lizann Grupalo, GDEcD Deputy Commissioner of International Trade, stated that Georgia punches above its weight in exports due to the hard work and dedication of Georgia-based businesses.
- Trade builds a more resilient economy, allowing businesses to shift strategies as market opportunities evolve.
- There is an estimated one in nine jobs in Georgia tied to the logistics industry.
- Georgia is home to Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport, described as the busiest and most efficient airport in the world.
- Georgia has the fastest growing and third-busiest container gateway in the U.S. at the Port of Savannah.
- Georgia has one of the nation’s busiest gateways for Roll-on/Roll-off cargo at the Port of Brunswick.
- Georgia offers a robust rail and highway infrastructure, with more rail miles than any other state in the Southeast at 5,000 miles of rail.
- Georgia has 20,000 miles of federal and state highways.
- GDEcD’s International Trade team works to bolster Georgia exports and brand the state as a competitive source of quality products and services.
- The team includes International Representatives located in 12 strategic global markets.
- The International Trade office provides Georgia businesses with Global Insight and Global Connections to successfully diversify their international customer base.
