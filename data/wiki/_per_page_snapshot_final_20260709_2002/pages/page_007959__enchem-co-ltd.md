# ENCHEM Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [The Worldfolio: ENCHEM: The Winning Plan to Dominate the Battery Electrolyte Market?](https://www.theworldfolio.com/interviews/enchem-the-winning-plan-to-dominate-the-battery-electrolyte-market/6407/)  ·  `page_007959`

## Facts

- Enchem is a fast-moving Korean electrolyte leader establishing a localized strategy to dominate the battery electrolyte market amid trade policy disruptions and supply chain realignments.
- The success of Korean conglomerates like Samsung, LG, and SK has provided growth momentum and core capabilities to domestic suppliers like Enchem.
- Enchem has built competency to supply global giants like CATL and AESC through joint development with major Korean multinationals.
- The implementation of the Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) is gradually cutting Chinese competitors off from the American supply chain, creating a window of opportunity for Korean companies like Enchem.
- Experts estimate electrolyte demand will reach around 2,864,000 tons by 2030, driven by pent up demand from batteries, electric vehicles (EVs), and energy storage systems (ESS).
- Enchem anticipates electrolyte demand could exceed 10 million tons, potentially equaling a market size of 30 trillion KRW.
- Chinese competitors are generally assumed to take 5-6 years from business plan to commercial production, with the earliest supply to customers appearing to be 2027.
- Enchem stabilized its electrolyte production in North America since 2022 and is currently scaling up and enhancing its global capacity.
- By 2025, Enchem will commence operations of electrolyte plants in Kentucky, Ontario, and Texas.
- Enchem's primary goal is to preempt the U.S. and EU markets by 2027, achieving economies of scale and maximizing market share to gain a head start on Chinese contenders.
- CEO Oh Jung-Kang stated that Enchem is focusing on targeting major battery companies in North America and Europe to achieve more than 50% market share in the global market.
- Enchem's strategy involves opening production hubs within a 500-kilometer radius of clients' bases to maximize localization rates and dominate the market by 2025.
- Enchem is securing price competitiveness and just-in-time delivery by internalizing its supply chain and minimizing supply distances between factories and clients.
- Enchem is developing new technologies and products, including NMP recycling and CNT dispersion slurry, to distribute inventory and logistics costs across product categories.
- Around 70% of electrolyte production costs currently come from raw material procurement.
- Enchem has established joint ventures with globally leading solvent and lithium salt makers to internalize raw materials and reduce price fluctuations.
- Enchem is establishing the Saemangeum plant to produce high-quality lithium salt, which will later be expanded to the U.S. mainland.
- Enchem's operating margin in the electrolyte market is currently at 5%.
- Internalizing lithium salt production can secure an additional 3 to 5% margin, while localizing raw materials and being within 500 kilometers of clients can save another 2% in operational costs.
- Achieving economies of scale with one production site having a capacity of 100,000 tons (equivalent to about 1 trillion KRW) can lead to an additional 3% cost saving.
- These measures aim to elevate Enchem's price competitiveness to around 15% from the current 5%.
- Enchem expects to achieve 2.4 trillion KRW in revenue by 2026.
- By the end of the year, Enchem will have orders from each member of the top 10 battery makers, increasing to the top 20 next year.
- Enchem recently expanded its business portfolio into NMP recycling and CNT conductive slurry dispersion markets due to high entry barriers and growth potential.
- Enchem collaborates with battery manufacturers to develop new additives tailored to specific requirements, conducting technological exchanges with the top 20 global battery makers.
- Enchem's complete vertical integration spans from raw materials to electrolytes and additives, allowing it to provide one-stop solutions.
- Enchem's stock price jumped from 60,000 KRW per share a year ago to a peak of more than 360,000 KRW in April 2024.
- Since Q1 of this year, Enchem has been supplying electrolytes to Ultium Cells, Company “P”, and Company “T”.
- The company operates a North American factory located in Georgia, USA.
- Enchem plans to increase its U.S. production capacity to 650,000 tons, representing about 70% of the total U.S. capacity share.
- Enchem is the only electrolyte company that will integrate raw material production with synthesis technology in the U.S.
- The biggest risk for Enchem's North American business is market uncertainty regarding EV sales and domestic consumption, as EVs manufactured in the U.S. cannot be exported to China.
- High interest rates have reduced the purchasing power of U.S. citizens, increasing market uncertainty.
- U.S. consumers prefer higher-priced EVs ranging from 50 million KRW to over 100 million KRW, making the U.S. market five to ten times larger than the Chinese one in terms of potential size.
- U.S. consumers favor high-quality, costly electrolytes and expect EV ranges of 600 to 1,000 km on a single charge.
- Enchem has operational plants in Poland (established to supply LG) and Hungary (established to supply SK and Samsung SDI).
- Enchem is considering establishing a factory in Spain in the future to strengthen European production capabilities.
- In France, Enchem has local partnerships with ACC and Verkor.
- Enchem considers collaborations with Chinese companies operating in Europe (AESC, CATL, EVE, Gotion) and explores partnerships with German and Swedish firms.
- Enchem is revising its 10-year plan through 2035, aiming to evolve from a global electrolyte specialist to a comprehensive global chemical company.
- The company plans to diversify into chemicals used in vehicles, semiconductors, and medicinal chemistry, as well as develop high molecular electrolytes for robots and solid-state or polymeric electrolytes.
