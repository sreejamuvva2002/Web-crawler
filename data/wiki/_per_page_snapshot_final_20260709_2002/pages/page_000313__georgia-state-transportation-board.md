# Georgia State Transportation Board

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** charging_infrastructure  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Georgia awards US$24.4M for EV charging infrastructure](https://www.evinfrastructurenews.com/ev-networks/georgia-awards-us-24-4-million-for-highway-charging)  ·  `page_000313`

## Facts

- Georgia State Transportation Board awarded US$24.4 million in federal funding to establish 26 new EV fast-charging stations across the state.
- This marks the second round of deployment for the US National Electric Vehicle Infrastructure (NEVI) programme.
- The funding announcement was made at the board's 20 November meeting.
- The funding will support the construction and operation of fast-charging stations through public-private partnerships.
- Six awardees were selected to design, install, finance, operate and maintain the stations for at least five years.
- The second round of the procurement process focuses on closing strategic gaps identified after the initial 2024 awards.
- The Georgia Department of Transportation identified 33 potential locations and received 41 proposals for 26 of these sites.
- Selection criteria included site readiness, charging reliability and community impact.
- The NEVI programme is funded through the Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act and allocated US$5 billion nationwide.
- Georgia received approximately US$135 million from the NEVI programme allocation.
- NEVI funds cover up to 80% of project costs.
- Georgia's multi-year plan focuses on rural and underserved areas where private investment in EV charging infrastructure has been lagging.
- The state's NEVI deployment strategy prioritises corridor charging along major highways while addressing coverage gaps that could limit long-distance EV travel.
- The 26 new stations will complement existing charging networks and support the state's goal of establishing reliable charging access every 50 miles along designated corridors.
- Selected locations span multiple regions, with installations planned to begin in 2025.
- Each station will feature multiple DC fast chargers capable of serving various EV models and charging standards.
- The public-private partnership model ensures long-term operation and maintenance while leveraging private sector expertise in charging technology deployment.
- Georgia Power has been piloting vehicle-to-grid technology and expanding EV charging capabilities as part of broader grid modernisation efforts.
- Georgia Power's pilot programmes have tested bidirectional charging technology and smart grid integration, providing valuable data for scaling the deployment of EV infrastructure across the state.
