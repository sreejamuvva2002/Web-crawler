# Electric Mobility and Innovation Alliance

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2021-07-20

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp Announces Statewide Initiative for Electric Mobility | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-releases/2021/gov-kemp-announces-statewide-initiative-accelerate-georgias-electric-mobility)  ·  `page_000055`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced a statewide initiative to strengthen Georgia's position as a national leader in the electric mobility industry on July 20, 2021.
- The initiative is driven by the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD).
- The Electric Mobility and Innovation Alliance (EMIA) includes partners from private and public sectors ranging from government, industry, electric utilities, education, nonprofits, and other stakeholders.
- GDEcD launched new website assets for the initiative to advance Georgia’s role in developing the electric mobility industry and its supply chain.
- Governor Kemp stated that Georgia has a proven record of investing early in resources and infrastructure needed to connect to the world and develop jobs of the future.
- EMIA aims to ensure Georgia continues leading the nation in the rapidly growing electric mobility industry through innovative solutions and strategies.
- The announcement references nearly $2.6 billion SK Innovation electric vehicle (EV) battery manufacturing facilities located in Jackson County.
- In recent months, Georgia has attracted international investments from Dutch e-mobility charging systems leader Heliox.
- Georgia has also attracted Turkish EV-parts manufacturer TEKLAS as an international investment.
- Georgia has attracted German-owned lightweight automotive-body parts manufacturer GEDIA as an international investment.
- Georgia has attracted SK-supplier EnChem of Korea as an international investment.
- In 2018, Georgia Made school bus manufacturer Blue Bird introduced their all-electric buses.
- Blue Bird recently reported its 500th delivery or sale of electric buses.
- EMIA will offer policy recommendations intended to further enhance the state’s attractiveness to the electric mobility industry and foster innovation in related fields.
- Innovation areas targeted by EMIA include drones, autonomous vehicles, connected vehicles, battery technology, and more.
- EMIA consists of five committees that will develop a multi-step approach setting objectives for Supply Chain, Infrastructure, Workforce, Innovation, and Policy/Initiatives.
- The Carl Vinson Institute at the University of Georgia will host EMIA’s committee meetings and integrated research forums.
- A report by the Alliance for Automotive Innovation states automakers are planning to invest $250 billion in electrification by 2023.
- IHS Markit predicts there will be 130 EV models available in the U.S. by 2026.
- Georgia is positioned as the center of the southeast automotive corridor with close proximity to the region’s automotive original equipment manufacturers to capitalize on electrification trends and support the entire EV supply chain.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that EMIA will give Georgia a greater, more comprehensive strategy for the wave of electrification across industry sectors.
- Wilson noted that bringing together industry leaders and public and private sector partners is proactively developing a roadmap to keep Georgia at the forefront of electric mobility and innovation.
