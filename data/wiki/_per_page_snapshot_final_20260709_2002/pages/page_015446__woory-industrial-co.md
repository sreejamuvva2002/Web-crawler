# Woory Industrial Co.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-06-07

**Source:** [EV parts supplier to build plant in Georgia |](https://capitol-beat.org/2023/06/ev-parts-supplier-to-build-plant-in-georgia/)  ·  `page_015446`

## Facts

- A South Korea-based auto parts maker will build its first U.S. manufacturing plant in Georgia.
- Woory Industrial Co. will invest an estimated $18 million in a facility in Dublin.
- The new facility will create more than 130 jobs.
- The company specializes in heating, ventilation, and air conditioning components for both electric and internal combustion vehicles.
- Gov. Brian Kemp announced the project on Wednesday.
- Gov. Brian Kemp said, “We’re proud to welcome Woory Industrial as the latest job creator to help us on the path to becoming the e-mobility capital of the nation.”
- Gov. Brian Kemp added, “This emerging industry continues to bring historic levels of new jobs and investment to communities all over the state, thanks to our highly skilled workforce and reliable infrastructure network.”
- Unlike internal combustion engines, EVs do not have a heating source, making cabin heaters necessary for maintaining battery temperature.
- Pioneering development by Woory has established the company as a leader in EV heating technology.
- The company is developing additional new products for EVs and hydrogen-fueled vehicles.
- Jungwoo Kim, Woory’s CEO, said, “We are facing a revolution in the automobile industry for the first time in 100 years.”
- Jungwoo Kim stated, “The transition to electric vehicles is the future, and the U.S. EV market is the key market among all automobile markets.”
- Woory will hire for positions across all fields necessary for an automotive parts plant.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development’s Global Commerce team collaborated with the Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority, Georgia Power, and the Technical College System of Georgia’s Quick Start program to secure the project.
- The new plant is expected to begin operations in November.
