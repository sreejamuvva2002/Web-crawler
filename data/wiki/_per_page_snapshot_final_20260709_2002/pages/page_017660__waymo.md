## Facts

- Mayor Eric Adams announced the news on Friday, noting it is the city’s first official rollout of autonomous vehicle testing.
- Waymo will test up to eight vehicles in Manhattan and Downtown Brooklyn through late September, with the option to continue the program afterward.
- New York law still requires a human driver behind the wheel during all test drives.
- The move comes after Waymo filed for permits just two months ago as it looks to expand nationally.
- The company has already launched in cities like Austin and has expanded services in the San Francisco area.
- Waymo plans to enter other major urban markets, such as Atlanta, Miami, Washington D.C., and Philadelphia.
- Waymo’s CEO recently stated that the company had reached over 10 million robotaxi rides.
- The Adams administration has been helping Waymo by setting safety rules and launching a permit system to support responsible testing.
