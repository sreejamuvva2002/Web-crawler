# Volvo Group North America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-01-31

**Source:** [U.S. hits Volvo Group North America with $130 mln penalty over delayed recalls | World Auto Forum](https://worldautoforum.com/u-s-hits-volvo-group-north-america-with-130-mln-penalty-over-delayed-recalls/)  ·  `page_014409`

## Facts

- A U.S. auto safety agency imposed a $130 million civil penalty on Volvo Group North America for failing to recall vehicles in a timely fashion.
- The penalty is one of the largest ever for violations under the recall law.
- Volvo Group North America agreed to a three-year consent order that includes oversight by an independent third-party auditor.
- The company will meet regularly with NHTSA to ensure it addresses any potential safety issues.
- Acting NHTSA Administrator Ann Carlson stated that NHTSA will use the full scope of its authorities to protect the public from safety defects and manufacturers failing to comply with the law.
- Volvo Group North America is the parent company to Mack Trucks, Nova Bus, Prevost Car (US), VNA Holding, and Volvo Group Canada.
- The company stated it appreciated the opportunity to summarily resolve the matter and noted that wide-ranging improvements in its North American safety processes and systems are already underway.
- NHTSA opened an investigation in October 2018 into the company’s compliance with recall requirements, including recall timeliness, manufacturer communications, and early warning reporting requirements.
- Volvo Group failed to submit certain quarterly recall reports, manufacturer communications, report some death and injury incidents, and submit certain field reports.
- Volvo Group must pay $65 million within 60 days, with an additional $45 million due if it fails to comply with the agreement.
- Volvo is spending $20 million to develop a safety data analytics infrastructure to enhance its ability to detect and investigate potential safety defects.
- The company will develop written procedures and training for its employees on compliance.
- NHTSA may extend the consent agreement for up to two additional years if required.
- The third-party auditor must evaluate all of Volvo's recalls since July 2013 and recommend any changes warranted.
- Volvo Group is a separate entity from Volvo Cars.
