# Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation of America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2021-10-13

**Source:** [Yamaha Funds Six All Kids Bike Programs in Georgia and California | The Outdoor Wire](https://www.theoutdoorwire.com/releases/e628188c-04e2-4785-9168-f8006eed3af8)  ·  `page_015649`

## Facts

- Yamaha Motor Corp., USA (YMUS) announced $30,000 in funding to All Kids Bike for six programs in Georgia and California.
- The grant covers costs for kindergarten Physical Education classes teaching children how to ride bikes.
- Funding is provided through the Yamaha Outdoor Access Initiative (OAI).
- Steve Nessl serves as Yamaha’s Motorsports Marketing Manager.
- Ryan McFarland is the founder of All Kids Bike and helped deliver bikes to Morris Elementary in Cypress, California.
- YMUS employees from Marietta, Georgia, and Cypress, California corporate offices, along with YMMC personnel in Newnan, Georgia, volunteered to build and deliver bikes and helmets to local schools.
- Full All Kids Bike programs delivered included 24 balance bikes, pedal conversion kits, helmets, and a teacher’s bike.
- Programs were delivered to Elm Street Elementary in Newnan, Georgia; A.L. Burruss Elementary School in Marietta, Georgia; and Juliet Morris Elementary School in Cypress, California.
- Three additional schools in Newnan, Marietta, and Cypress are guaranteed funding through the Yamaha grant for the year.
- The All Kids Bike program includes an eight-lesson Kindergarten PE Learn-To-Ride Curriculum, teacher training and certification, and a five-year support plan.
- Bob Brown is Vice President, Finance and Operations Support at YMMC.
- Newnan schools are the first in Georgia to receive the All Kids Bike program.
- The All Kids Bike program operates in 350 schools across 45 states, with 50 additional schools in training.
- Ryan McFarland is also founder and CEO of Strider Sports International, Inc., which manufactures the Strider Bikes used in the program.
- The Yamaha Outdoor Access Initiative has issued quarterly grants to non-profit organizations for over 12 years to support riding groups, outdoor enthusiasts, land stewardship organizations, and land managers.
- Yamaha has contributed more than $4.5 million in aid to nearly 400 projects nationwide through the OAI program.
- The YMMC facility was founded in 1986 and produces golf cars, personal watercraft, Side-by-Side vehicles, and all-terrain vehicles (ATVs).
- The YMMC factory holds ISO9001 Quality Management System and ISO14001 Environmental Management System certifications.
- YMUS product offerings include Power Assist Bicycles, Golf Cars, Motorcycles and Scooters, ATVs, Side-by-Side vehicles, Snowmobiles, WaveRunner Personal Watercraft, Boats, Outboard Motors, Outdoor Power Equipment, Power Assist Wheelchair Systems, Surface Mount Technology (SMT), Robotic Machines, Unmanned Helicopters, Accessories, Apparel, Yamalube products, and more.
- YMUS maintains a corporate office in California, two corporate offices in Georgia, facilities in Wisconsin and Alabama, and factory operations in Tennessee and Georgia.
- YMUS U.S.-based subsidiaries include Yamaha Marine Systems Company (with Bennett Marine in Florida and Kracor Systems in Wisconsin), Skeeter Boats (Texas) with division G3 Boats (Missouri), and Yamaha Precision Propeller (Indiana).
- YMUS products are sold through a nationwide network of distributors and dealers in the United States.
