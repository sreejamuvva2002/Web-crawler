# Daimler Truck North America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Daimler Truck Celebrates 850,000th Truck Built in North Carolina | Heavy Duty Trucking](https://www.truckinginfo.com/news/daimler-truck-celebrates-850-000th-truck-built-in-north-carolina)  ·  `page_053531`

## Facts

- Daimler Truck North America (DTNA) marked the production of its 850,000th truck at its Cleveland, North Carolina manufacturing plant.
- The milestone vehicle was a Western Star 47X delivered to Alamo Group for snow and ice operations.
- Alamo Group is a long-standing DTNA customer that provides trucks to its Snow & Ice Team for municipal, highway, and airport winter maintenance operations.
- Alamo Group manufactures plow trucks, snow removal systems, and airport de-icing equipment.
- Alamo Group is based in Seguin, Texas, has more than 40 brands, employs about 3,750 people, and reports $1.69 billion in annual revenue.
- David Carson, senior vice president of Sales and Marketing at DTNA, stated the Western Star 47X is purpose-built for demanding applications like snow and ice management.
- Western Star, a brand of DTNA, produces vocational trucks used in construction and municipal operations.
- The Western Star 47X is powered by a Detroit DD13 engine and features designed for heavy-duty and cold-weather applications.
- Over the past decade, DTNA and Alamo Group expanded their relationship from division-level purchases to a consolidated national account.
- Jean Philippe Bourque, group vice president of Alamo Group’s Snow and Ice Division, stated the Western Star 47X is a critical part of their portfolio valued for reliability, performance, and support.
- DTNA’s Cleveland Truck Manufacturing Plant was established in 1989 and is the company’s largest heavy-duty truck plant in the U.S.
- The Cleveland facility spans 1.2 million square feet on 178 acres.
- The Cleveland plant employs approximately 2,000 people.
- The Cleveland plant currently assembles the Fifth Generation Freightliner Cascadia and Western Star 47X and 49X.
- The Cleveland plant is one of nine DTNA manufacturing facilities in North America.
- The facility uses robotics and lean manufacturing techniques while retaining manual processes for parts of truck assembly.
- Jeff Allen, senior vice president of operations and specialty vehicles at DTNA, stated the plant is a cornerstone of their production network and vital to the Rowan County community.
- The Cleveland plant operates as a zero-waste-to-landfill facility.
- The plant is part of DTNA’s goal to reduce emissions, water use, waste, and energy consumption by 2030.
- The Cleveland plant is one of the largest manufacturing employers in Rowan County and participates in local efforts including disaster relief, the Ride of Pride veteran support program, and school and holiday donation drives.
