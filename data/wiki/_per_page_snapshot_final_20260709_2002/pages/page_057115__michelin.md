# Michelin

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Michelin reveals plan to increase bio-based components and recycling in tyres | Bioenergy International](https://bioenergyinternational.com/michelin-reveals-plan-increase-bio-based-components-recycling-tyres/)  ·  `page_057115`

## Facts

- Michelin revealed during its Movin’On 2018 event an ambitious plan to ensure that by 2048, all of its tyres will be manufactured using 80 percent sustainable materials and 100 percent of all tyres will be recycled.
- According to the World Business Council for Sustainable Development (WBCSD), an estimated 1 billion end-of-life tyres (ELT) will be generated worldwide in 2018, representing around 25 million tonnes.
- Currently, the global recovery rate for tyres is 70 percent with 50 percent of the material recycled and used in products such as rubber used in sports surfaces, and 20 percent is energy recovery.
- Michelin's tyres are currently made using 28 percent sustainable materials – 26 percent bio-sourced materials such as natural rubber, sunflower oil and limonene and 2 percent recycled materials such as steel or recycled powdered tyres.
- Michelin is investing in “high technology recycling” to increase sustainable material content to 80 percent and produce tyres that are 100 percent recycled for future vehicles.
- Michelin proposes to develop partnerships and identify new ways to recycle tyres or find new outlets for recycled tyres.
- The route to this target will be achieved by research programmes into bio-sourced materials like Biobutterfly and working with high-level partners on advanced technologies and materials.
- Michelin acquired Lehigh Technologies Inc., a US-based specialty chemical company and a specialist in high technology micro powders derived from recycled tyres.
- Lehigh is now part of the High Technology Materials Business Unit of Michelin and is the leading producer of Micronized Rubber Powders (MRP), a sustainable raw material that reduces feedstock costs by up to 50 percent while delivering performance across markets.
- MRP replaces oil- and rubber-based feedstocks in industrial and consumer applications including high-performance tyres, plastics, consumer goods, coatings, sealants, construction materials and asphalt.
- Lehigh technical experts collaborate with customers to optimize products for each application.
- Lehigh operates the world’s largest MRP manufacturing plant in Tucker, Georgia, with an annual production capacity of 54 000 tonnes.
- Lehigh’s state-of-the-art Application & Development Center is also located in Tucker and serves as an innovation hub where Michelin conducts research and formulates MRPs in collaboration with its customers.
- Lehigh Spain, a joint venture with Hera Holding, is based in Barcelona.
- The first Lehigh plant outside of the US, located in Murrillo del Fruto, is under construction and will begin operations in the summer of 2018.
- Christophe Rahier, Director of the High Technology Materials Business Line at Michelin, stated the acquisition demonstrates Michelin’s strategic determination to capitalize on expertise in high-tech materials beyond tyres by promoting innovative recycled tyre materials in non-pneumatic industrial sectors.
- Michelin revealed its VISION concept at Movin’On last year: an airless tyre made of bio-sourced and recycled products using advanced materials and 3D printing technologies for tread renewal, making it 100 percent recyclable.
- The VISION concept features a connected ecosystem providing services and advice to the driver, a bio-degradable tread renewable with a 3D printer, and a mobility solution that reduces the environmental footprint of car journeys.
- Achieving the 80 percent sustainable materials and 100 percent recycling targets will save Michelin an estimated 33 million barrels of oil per annum or 54 TWh, equivalent to about one month’s total energy consumption in France.
