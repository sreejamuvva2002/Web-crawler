# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-05-15

**Source:** [Op-Ed: Atlanta Journal-Constitution: Hyundai metaplant investment](https://georgia.org/blogs/op-ed-atlanta-journal-constitution-hyundai-metaplant-investment-shows-importance-georgia)  ·  `page_000041`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group opened its new metaplant just outside Savannah in March.
- Georgia celebrated the 40th anniversary of its investment office in Seoul in 2025, which was one of the first such offices established by any U.S. state in Korea.
- Korea opened a consular presence in Georgia in 1971.
- KOTRA, Korea’s trade and investment organization, maintains an active office in Georgia to support bilateral investment and trade.
- Georgia was honored with the James A. Van Fleet Award from The Korea Society in 2023, which was accepted by Gov. Brian Kemp.
- SK Group subsidiary SKC opened a plant in Georgia in 1996, marking the first major Korean-owned manufacturing facility in the state.
- Since 2019, SK Battery America has invested $2.6 billion in an electric vehicle battery facility in the state.
- Hyundai Motor Group’s investment of $12.6 billion includes the new metaplant and two battery joint ventures with LG Energy System and SK On.
- Hyundai's investment will generate up to 40,000 direct and indirect jobs.
- The metaplant alone will produce 500,000 electric and hybrid vehicles annually at its state-of-the-art facility near Georgia’s coast and deepwater ports.
- Kia, also part of Hyundai Motor Group, has invested $2.8 billion into its West Point plant since 2009.
- Kia's West Point plant now produces 350,000 vehicles per year.
- Kia's facility has expanded to include EV manufacturing lines alongside traditional models.
- In 2018, Hanwha Qcells announced its solar panel manufacturing plant in Dalton, which opened as the largest in the Western Hemisphere.
- Hanwha Qcells has expanded three times since 2019.
- A second Hanwha Qcells plant in Cartersville is now ramping up and is expected to support up to 4,000 jobs in northwest Georgia.
- CJ Foodville will open a $47 million bakery and food processing facility in Hall County this year, supplying Tous les Jours cafés with over 19,000 tons of baked goods each year.
- Over the past decade, Korean companies supported directly by the State of Georgia are slated to contribute more than $24 billion in investment and create more than 33,600 new jobs across automotive, logistics, food production, and advanced technology sectors.
- Around 100 Korean-owned facilities operate in Georgia, employing more than 17,000 people as of 2024.
- Georgia is home to one of the largest Korean communities in the U.S.
- Georgia-based companies such as Coca-Cola, Novelis and UPS operate in Korea.
- Delta Air Lines connects Atlanta to Seoul with direct flights, making business and cultural exchange even more seamless.
- In 2024, Korea ranked as Georgia’s third-largest total trade partner, with more than $17.5 billion in goods traded.
- Pat Wilson is the commissioner of the Georgia Department of Economic Development.
