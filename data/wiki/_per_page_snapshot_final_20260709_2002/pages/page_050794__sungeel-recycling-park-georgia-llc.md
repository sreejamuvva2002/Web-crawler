# SungEel Recycling Park Georgia, LLC.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Korean Lithium-Ion Battery Recycler to Build First U.S. Recycling Facility - 2022-08-22 | ScrapMonster](https://www.scrapmonster.com/news/waste-recycling/korean-lithiumion-battery-recycler-to-build-first-us-recycling-facility/84687)  ·  `page_050794`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that lithium-ion battery recycler and raw materials provider SungEel Recycling Park Georgia, LLC., will locate its first U.S. recycling facility in Georgia.
- SungEel Recycling Park Georgia, LLC. is a subsidiary of the Korea-based global industry leader SungEel HiTech Co., Ltd.
- The company will invest more than $37 million in the new facility.
- The project will create 104 jobs in Stephens County.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated that Georgia recently announced record-breaking numbers for fiscal year 2022 and that companies like SungEel are evidence that the rapidly growing electric mobility ecosystem continues to generate new jobs for hardworking Georgians across the state.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp noted that Korea has been a key partner in Georgia’s growing sustainable technology industry and welcomed SungEel's choice of the Peach State for their first U.S. facility.
- SungEel specializes in complete recycling technology using a 100 percent full circulatory system with more than 10 years of patented technology.
- The company's recycling system processes end-of-life batteries and battery manufacturing scrap.
- The recycling process achieves a metal recovery rate of more than 95 percent.
- Recovered metals from the facility's operations will include nickel, cobalt, and lithium.
- Suk Jae Yim, Representative of SungEel Recycling Park Georgia, stated that SungEel HiTech’s entry into Georgia is the last piece of the puzzle to build a sustainable ecosystem of Georgia’s electric vehicle supply chain.
- SungEel Recycling Park Georgia will conduct its full responsibility to build a U.S. eco-friendly industrial ecosystem in line with the expectations of the State of Georgia and Stephens County.
- The new facility will be located at the Hayestone Brady Business Park, a Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) Certified site, in Toccoa.
- The GRAD Program is a proactive effort by Georgia’s economic development community to develop a portfolio of available sites ready for fast-track development.
- Operations at the new facility are expected to begin in early 2024.
- Over the next few years, the company will be hiring for technicians, operators, administrative, and sales roles.
- Brian Akin, Chairman of the Stephens County Development Authority, stated it has been a pleasure working with SungEel HiTech over the past eight months and learning more about the company and its processes.
- Brian Akin described the project as an exciting time for Toccoa-Stephens County and a solid boost for the local job market.
- Infrastructure, workforce development, and education investments were significant factors in SungEel's selection process to expand to Stephens County.
- Yoonie Kim, Director of Korean Investment, represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) on this competitive project in partnership with the Stephens County Development Authority and Georgia EMC.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that SungEel is a company at the cutting edge of sustainable technology and believes SungEel’s proximity to other members of Georgia’s electric mobility ecosystem will lead to amazing success.
- Pat Wilson emphasized that creating the jobs of the future and protecting the opportunities of today means preparing key industries, such as the auto industry, for the next technological revolution.
- GDEcD expressed gratitude to partners in Stephens County for their investment in speed to market solutions like site preparation.
