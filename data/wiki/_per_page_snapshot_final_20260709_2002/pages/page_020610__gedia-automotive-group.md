# GEDIA Automotive Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2020-08-07

**Source:** [GEDA August 2020 Newsletter - Georgia Economic Developers Association](https://www.geda.org/news/geda-august-2020-newsletter)  ·  `page_020610`

## Facts

- GEDIA Automotive Group is a family-owned international company that specializes in making state-of-the-art automotive parts.
- The company will build an advanced manufacturing facility that will begin production with a focus on parts for electric vehicles.
- GEDIA plans to invest $85 million in the project.
- The investment will deliver 200 jobs to the greater Dalton area.
- The facility will be located at a Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) certified property in Whitfield County.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced the project on July 24, 2020.
- Key team members involved in locating the project include Carl Campbell (Executive Director, Dalton-Whitfield County Joint Development Authority), Nico Wijnberg (Director of International Relations, Marketing, Communications and International Engagement Division, Georgia Department of Economic Development), Lonnie Smallwood (Senior Project Manager, Georgia Electric Membership Corporation), and Rodger Brown (Director of Strategic Communications, Georgia Quick Start).
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) announced it is accepting nominations for the 2020 Georgia Emerging Automotive Recognition (GEAR) Awards.
- The GEAR Awards are designed to recognize forward movement in the automotive and mobility industries through innovation and emerging technologies.
- The deadline for nominations is August 14th.
