# Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia

**Type:** Company  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[vehicle-manufacturing|Vehicle Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** West Point, Georgia, Troup County, Georgia, United States

## Overview

Kia Georgia is a vehicle assembly plant in West Point, GA that began operations in 2009 after a request to the Georgia Department of Economic Development. The plant has attracted numerous Korean and local suppliers, partners with the Technical College System of Georgia for workforce training, sponsors STEM programs and community projects, operates a free solar-panel charging center at the West Point Visitor Center, and is an inaugural partner in the Georgia Made™ program.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Kia Georgia contributes to electric-vehicle manufacturing and sustainability initiatives in Georgia through green vehicle production, renewable-energy charging infrastructure, and workforce training for EV-related jobs.

## Workforce & partners

- Quick Start Program (Technical College System of Georgia) — Training partner. Partnered with Kia to construct and operate the 70,000-square-foot training center and develop a maintenance certification program at West Georgia Technical College.
- Georgia Made™ — Program partnership. Kia is an inaugural partner; the program requires 50% of final product be manufactured in Georgia, providing logistics, manufacturing, and marketing support.

## Key facts

- The automotive industry has been a cornerstone for the economic relationship between Georgia and Korea.
- Kia Georgia sponsors local school system STEM programs and Troup County’s THINC College and Career Academy, providing students with multiple career opportunities in automotive and manufacturing industries.
- Kia Georgia sponsors a free solar panel charging center and tire inspection center at the state-operated West Point Visitor Information Center.
- Kia Georgia is an inaugural partner in Georgia Made™, which requires 50% of the final product be manufactured in Georgia, offering logistics, manufacturing, and marketing support to qualifying companies.
- SK On is investing nearly $2.6 billion in two battery manufacturing facilities in Jackson County to supply electric vehicles, including the Ford F-150 Lightning.
- Qcells’ 300,000-square-foot facility in Dalton employs more than 700 local workers and produces 1.7 GW of solar module capacity annually, making about 12,000 panels per day.
- Meta Platforms announced a $1 billion data center in Stanton Springs, Georgia powered by 100% new renewable energy sources, using solar panels manufactured at Qcells’ Dalton plant.
- Georgia’s utility providers met Meta’s renewable-energy requirement by supplying solar panels from the Qcells facility.
- The State of Georgia has hosted a Korean consular presence since 1971 and maintains continuous representation in Seoul since 1985.
- Governor Brian Kemp’s 2019 mission to Korea was his first as governor, emphasizing the importance of Georgia-Korea economic ties.
