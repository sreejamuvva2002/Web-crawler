## Facts

- Georgia’s GDP exceeded 100 billion GEL in 2025 for the first time in its history.
- Prime Minister Irakli Kobakhidze presented economic results showing a 7.5% growth rate in 2025.
- The average economic growth rate over the past five years reached around 9.3%.
- Economic growth stood at 8.2% in January–April 2026.
- Georgia’s GDP was below 28 billion GEL in 2012 and had not reached 50 billion GEL in 2020.
- Consumer prices in Georgia increased by 6.4% over the past three years.
- Food and non-alcoholic beverage prices rose by 9.7% within the inflation structure.
- Average salary increased by 47.9%, significantly outpacing price dynamics.
- Goods exports increased by 11.2% in 2025, reaching a record $7.3 billion.
- Foreign direct investment rose by 7.6% to around $1.7 billion.
- The Ministry of Finance placed $500 million in Eurobonds with demand exceeding supply by 5.5 times.
- Treasury bonds worth 400 million GEL were issued with demand exceeding supply by 4.5 times.
- The government created an Efficiency Department to review state programs and procurement.
- In 2025, the Efficiency Department reviewed procurement worth 115 million GEL, generating over 67 million GEL in savings.
- An additional 67 million GEL in savings was achieved in 2026 through efficiency reforms.
- Energy sector reforms eliminated the practice of reselling grid connection permits and introduced a 10-year development plan.
- Optimization of state-owned energy companies reduced annual costs by nearly 25 million GEL.
- The railway system cut around 1,100 positions and brought outsourced processes in-house, generating over 40 million GEL in annual savings.
- Railway procurement optimization added 190.4 million GEL in savings.
- Georgian Railway profit increased from 68 million to 145 million GEL, with operating margin rising from 25% to 36%.
- Aviation procurement optimization resulted in 70 million GEL in savings and expanded international agreements.
- Tbilisi Airport capacity increased by 53%, leading to growth in state revenues.
- The IMF forecasts Georgia among the highest-growth economies in Europe and the region for 2026–2031.
- Georgia maintains leading positions in international rankings on budget transparency.
- Analysts note that accelerating GDP, moderate inflation, and declining debt burden create space for long-term planning.
- Government programs set the development pace for transport, energy, construction, and logistics sectors.
- Export growth and rising FDI indicate deeper integration into global value chains.
