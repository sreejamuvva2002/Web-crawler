# Hyundai Mobis

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** power_electronics  ·  **Published:** 2022-11

**Source:** [Hyundai Mobis invests $926M on Georgia EV plant](https://valdostatoday.com/news-2/region/2022/11/hyundai-mobis-invests-926m-on-georgia-ev-plant/)  ·  `page_001166`

## Facts

- Hyundai Mobis plans to invest $926 million in an EV Power Electric system plant in Bryan County, GA.
- The new plant will eventually employ at least 1,500 people.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced the investment and project details.
- Hyundai Mobis is one of the world’s largest automotive suppliers.
- This announcement marks the second supplier to locate in the Bryan County area in just two weeks.
- The EV Power Electric system plant will expand Hyundai Mobis’ current footprint in Georgia.
- Hyundai Mobis has operated a manufacturing facility with almost 1,200 employees in West Point since 2009.
- The West Point plant is the largest Tier 1 supplier to Kia Georgia.
- The West Point plant supplies automotive manufacturers with complete cockpit modules, chassis modules, and bumper assemblies.
- H.S. Oh serves as Vice President of Hyundai Mobis Electric Powertrain Business Unit.
- Once it reaches full production, the new facility will be 1,200,000 square feet.
- The new facility will supply over 900,000 EV Power Electric systems annually to Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) in Bryan County, Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Alabama (HMMA) in Montgomery, and the Kia Georgia plant.
- The new facility will also supply 450,000 Integrated Charging Control Units annually to those same customers.
- Construction on the new facility is expected to begin as early as January 2023.
- Production at the new facility is expected to begin in 2024.
- The new facility is located at the Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) Certified Belfast Commerce Park in Richmond Hill.
- Jon Seagraves serves as Chairman of the Development Authority of Bryan County.
- The site purchased about two years ago is near available housing in nearby Heartwood, a top-performing public school system, interstate access, and rail.
- Raydient Places provided support and partnership on the project alongside Bryan County and the City of Richmond Hill.
- Alyce Thornhill is the Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy for the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team.
- GDEcD partnered with the Development Authority of Bryan County, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia Power on this competitive project.
- Pat Wilson serves as Commissioner of the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD).
- In May, GDEcD predicted that non-affiliated HMGMA suppliers would invest an additional $1 billion into Georgia.
- That prediction was exceeded in about two weeks following the announcement.
- Since 2020, more than 30 EV-related projects have contributed over $13.3 billion in investment and announced more than 18,100 jobs in Georgia.
- On October 25, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America officially broke ground on its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility in Bryan County.
- Offsite suppliers connected to the HMGMA project are predicted to invest approximately $1 billion in other locations around the state.
