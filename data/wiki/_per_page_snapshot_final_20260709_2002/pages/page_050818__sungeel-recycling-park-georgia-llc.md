## Facts

- SungEel Recycling Park Georgia, LLC., a subsidiary of the Korea-based global industry leader SungEel HiTech Co., Ltd., will locate its first U.S. recycling facility in Georgia.
- The company will invest more than $37 million in the new facility and create 104 jobs in Stephens County.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that companies like SungEel are evidence that the rapidly growing electric mobility ecosystem continues to generate new jobs for hardworking Georgians across the state.
- With more than 10 years of patented technology, SungEel specializes in complete recycling technology using a 100 percent full circulatory system.
- The company's system recycles end-of-life batteries and battery manufacturing scrap, achieving a metal recovery rate of more than 95 percent.
- Recovered metals from the process include nickel, cobalt, and lithium.
- Suk Jae Yim, Representative of SungEel Recycling Park Georgia, stated that SungEel HiTech's entry into Georgia is the last piece of the puzzle to build a sustainable ecosystem of Georgia’s electric vehicle supply chain.
- SungEel Recycling Park Georgia will conduct its full responsibility to build a U.S. eco-friendly industrial ecosystem in line with the expectations of the State of Georgia and Stephens County.
- The new facility will be located at the Hayestone Brady Business Park, a Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) Certified site, in Toccoa.
- The GRAD Program is a proactive effort by Georgia’s economic development community to develop a portfolio of available sites ready for fast-track development.
- Operations are expected to begin in early 2024.
- Over the next few years, the company will be hiring for technicians, operators, administrative, and sales roles.
- Brian Akin, Chairman of the Stephens County Development Authority, noted that investments made in infrastructure, workforce development, and education were significant factors in SungEel HiTech's selection process to expand to Toccoa-Stephens County.
- Director of Korean Investment Yoonie Kim represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) on this competitive project in partnership with the Stephens County Development Authority and Georgia EMC.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that SungEel is a company at the cutting edge of sustainable technology and believes its proximity to other members of Georgia’s electric mobility ecosystem will lead to amazing success.
- Creating the jobs of the future and protecting the opportunities of today means preparing key industries, such as the auto industry, for the next technological revolution, according to Commissioner Wilson.
- Georgia is cultivating a vertically integrated supply chain that will help companies increase efficiencies by reducing the reliance on imported materials.
- EV-related projects have contributed more than $13 billion in investments and more than 18,100 new jobs to Georgia since 2020.
