# Prime, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2026-04-14

**Source:** [Prime, Inc. Bringing 120 Jobs To Spalding County | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-releases/2026/prime-inc-bringing-120-jobs-spalding-county)  ·  `page_004839`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced on April 14, 2026 that Prime, Inc. will invest more than $160 million to establish a new Southeastern regional hub in Spalding County.
- The new facility will create more than 120 new, full-time jobs.
- The company will also hire over 50 professional drivers.
- Prime, Inc. specializes in refrigerated, flatbed, tanker, hopper, intermodal, and logistics transportation services.
- Prime, Inc. operates a fleet of over 7,000 units with more than 8,500 drivers.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Prime’s new campus will add to Georgia’s $107 billion transportation and logistics industry that creates and supports jobs statewide.
- Robert Low, CEO and Founder of Prime Inc., stated that Spalding County’s strategic proximity to many top customers enhances service delivery for the company's fleet of professional drivers.
- Prime’s new Southeastern campus will be located at High Falls 16 Business Center near Griffin.
- The terminal will house state-of-the-art facilities including driver development and training, equipment maintenance, and comfort amenities.
- Additional services will include selling well-maintained used truck/trailer equipment and recycling used truck tires to produce aggregate rubber material for usable mulch and pellets.
- The company's tire recycling efforts currently shred more than 1 million truck tires per year.
- Prime will be hiring for mechanics, driving operators, and facility maintenance roles starting immediately.
- Chairman Nat Doughie of the Griffin-Spalding Development Authority stated that the facility promises a significant capital investment and excellent career opportunities for residents of Griffin and Spalding County.
- Katie Kirkpatrick, President & CEO of the Metro Atlanta Chamber, stated the investment will boost the region's powerful logistics industry and strengthen other key sectors.
- Assistant Director of Statewide Projects Elizabeth McLean represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project in partnership with the Griffin-Spalding Development Authority, Metro Atlanta Chamber, and Georgia EMC.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that logistics supports one in nine Georgia jobs and the new campus will strengthen an industry that touches every community in the state.
- Since 1970, Prime Inc. has operated as a premier North American carrier with refrigerated, flatbed, tanker, intermodal, and hopper divisions serving an international clientele.
