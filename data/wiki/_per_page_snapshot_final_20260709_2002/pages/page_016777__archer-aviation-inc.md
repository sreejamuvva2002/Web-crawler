# Archer Aviation Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-03-07

**Source:** [Archer Closes Incentive Transaction; Begins Construction on First of its Kind High-Volume eVTOL Manufacturing Facility in Covington, GA | Nasdaq](https://www.nasdaq.com/press-release/archer-closes-incentive-transaction-begins-construction-on-first-of-its-kind-high)  ·  `page_016777`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation Inc. closed a local incentive transaction for its Covington, GA manufacturing facility project on February 28, which combined with a Georgia REBA grant totals approximately 33% of the planned capital investment.
- On March 2, Archer began construction on Phase 1 of the project, involving the build-out of an approximately 350,000-square-foot facility capable of producing up to 650 aircraft per year.
- The Covington facility is strategically located adjacent to the Covington Municipal Airport and is believed to be the first industry-high-volume manufacturing facility for eVTOL aircraft.
- The facility can be expanded by an additional 800,000 square feet, which is estimated to support long-term production targets of up to 2,300 aircraft per year.
- Archer and Stellantis will join forces at the facility to begin manufacturing Archer's Midnight eVTOL aircraft in 2024.
- In January, Archer announced an expanded partnership with Stellantis under which the two companies will manufacture the Midnight production aircraft.
- Stellantis will contribute advanced manufacturing technology and expertise, experienced personnel, and capital to the partnership.
- The partnership aims to enable rapid scaling of aircraft production and help Archer avoid hundreds of millions of dollars in spending during the manufacturing ramp-up phase.
- The goal of the partnership is for Stellantis to mass-produce Archer's eVTOL aircraft as its exclusive contract manufacturer.
- Adam Goldstein, Archer's Founder and CEO, stated: "The key to unlocking the promise of what the eVTOL aircraft industry can bring is scale, and we are thrilled by the support the State of Georgia and Newton County are providing to this first of its kind facility in such an exciting new industry."
- Archer plans to hold a ceremonial event in April in Covington, GA, to mark the construction milestone.
- Archer is designing and developing electric vertical takeoff and landing (eVTOL) aircraft for use in urban air mobility networks.
- Archer's team is based in Santa Clara, CA.
