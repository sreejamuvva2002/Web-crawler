# Joon Georgia

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2022-11-09

**Source:** [Hyundai supplier to build factory near automaker’s Georgia EV campus | Manufacturing Dive](https://www.manufacturingdive.com/news/hyundai-supplier-to-build-factory-near-its-savannah-ev-campus/636192/)  ·  `page_000358`

## Facts

- Auto parts manufacturer Joon Georgia plans to build a production facility in southeast Georgia to supply components to Hyundai Motor Group’s first U.S. electric vehicle plant.
- Joon Georgia will invest $317 million to manufacture parts in Bullock County.
- Joon Georgia is the first confirmed supplier for Hyundai’s incoming EV campus in Savannah.
- Hyundai’s EV campus in Savannah broke ground in October.
- The new plant will begin hiring for approximately 630 jobs mid-next year.
- Production at the new plant is slated to begin in mid-2024.
- Gov. Brian Kemp stated that Joon Georgia is the “first of many companies” expected to open shop in Georgia to support Hyundai’s project.
- Hyundai’s Savannah campus represents a $5.5 billion investment.
- Hyundai’s Savannah campus is set to start production of up to 300,000 EVs per year in 2025.
- Joon Georgia is a subsidiary of Alabama-headquartered Ajin USA.
- Ajin USA currently supplies other Hyundai facilities with auto body parts and electronics.
- The new facility will be Ajin’s first in Georgia.
- Ajin USA CEO Jung Ho Sea confirmed the new facility's status in a statement.
- The facility will be located in Statesboro.
- The Statesboro location is in close proximity to the Port of Savannah, granting it access to “global markets quickly and efficiently.”
- More parts manufacturers are growing their presence in Georgia to match their OEM customers.
- Combined, Hyundai and Rivian have invested more than $10.5 billion in Georgia over the past year.
- The combined investments mark the two biggest economic development projects in Georgia.
- Polymer manufacturer Solvay Specialty Polymers announced plans to construct an $850 million plant to manufacture key lithium-ion battery parts in Augusta.
- Copper foil manufacturer Denkai America is set to locate its North America headquarters and new facility in Augusta.
- Georgia officials predict non-affiliated suppliers connected to Hyundai’s project will invest an estimated $1 billion in other locations around the state.
