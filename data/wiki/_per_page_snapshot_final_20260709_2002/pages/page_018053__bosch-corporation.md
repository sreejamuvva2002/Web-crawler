# Bosch Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2022-09-26

**Source:** [Background of the VM Division's focus on recruiting SW associates and its future goals | Recruitment Site for Mid-Career | Bosch Corporation](https://saiyo.boschjapan-brandtopics.jp/mid-career/en/pickup/technical-content02.html)  ·  `page_018053`

## Facts

- The Vehicle Motion Division (VM Division) is one of the backbones supporting Bosch.
- The development of braking systems that ensure the safety and security of automobiles is particularly important to Bosch.
- The role of software control has become extremely important in recent years for automotive safety.
- Yuji Tokumasu heads the Engineering Software Department within the VM Division.
- The VM Division is responsible for ESP® (Electronic Stability Program, anti-skid device), ABS (anti-lock braking system, technology to ensure vehicle safety during sudden braking, etc.), iBooster (electro-mechanical brake booster), airbags, and on-board sensors.
- The Software Engineering Department comprehensively conducts project management in software development, function development from specification analysis, software architecture design, network communication, integration, and software testing.
- Yuji Tokumasu's department mainly develops software for braking systems.
- In recent years, the importance of software technology has increased because hardware faces limits on how quickly technology can be updated and improved.
- Customers have a vision for future technical improvements to be provided as software in their central units rather than hardware.
- At Bosch, a software foundation called a platform is basically developed in Germany.
- Various software components are prepared based on a common standard software architecture for automotive electronic control units, such as AUTOSAR.
- These platforms serve as the core part that supports all customers.
- Bosch listens directly to requirements from automobile manufacturers to finalize specifications.
- Actual software development follows a style where Japan is the center, with bases in Germany, India, Vietnam, France, and the United States sharing the work.
- Once specifications are decided, Bosch leverages the platform to design, code, test, integrate components, and conduct system tests including vehicle tests before releasing to customers.
- The mainstream platform contains components required for products, including control and network technologies, and algorithms/interfaces for individual software like ESP.
- Know-how about controlling and moving vehicles is consolidated in the platform, which is then customized by incorporating customer-specific specifications.
- Bosch's Japan team serves Japanese automobile manufacturers as customers.
- Development tailored to customer needs is carried out in Japan, requiring direct communication in Japanese.
- Bosch often makes proposals rather than just responding to detailed requests from customers.
- Bosch adopts a development system close to customers globally: German headquarters handles VW and Daimler; the North American team handles Ford, GM, and Tesla.
- The technology of Japanese automobile manufacturers is highly regarded worldwide, supporting Bosch's world-leading technological capabilities.
- Decision-making power for platforms remains with German headquarters, but Japan's influence has increased as orders from Japanese OEMs have grown.
- Globally, the number of personnel in Japan's software development team is high.
- Demand from Japanese automobile manufacturers for software has been increasing, with demands expanding and becoming more detailed.
- Brake control specifications differ for each car model, requiring responses to individual company requirements.
- Connected automobiles are vulnerable to cyberattacks via networks, making cybersecurity crucial for vehicle safety.
- Cybersecurity requires software-based responses that comply with laws, regulations, and the latest technology.
- The software development team plays an important role in addressing cybersecurity threats.
- Both vehicle manufacturers and suppliers must protect safety as software evolves quickly.
- Yuji Tokumasu has worked at Bosch for a period (third company), citing constant new missions like creating the future of automated driving using AI.
- Development methods are constantly changing, such as adopting agile practices in software development.
- The role of software in automated driving technology will be important in the future.
- Responding to new technological concepts for automated driving is a key step for the Engineering Brake Systems Department.
- Bosch has adopted a strategy presenting various portfolios to respond flexibly to any direction each vehicle manufacturer takes.
- DevOps viewpoint, promoting flexible and speedy development and operations, will become important in creating future automobiles with manufacturers.
- Project teams organize people according to concept regardless of nationality (e.g., Japanese or Indian).
- Communication with engineers at overseas bases is basically in English.
- Software terminology is basically the same worldwide.
- Engineers from each country are Bosch colleagues with high technical capabilities.
- Online communication became the mainstay over the past two years due to the coronavirus pandemic, replacing travel to Bengaluru (India base).
- Japan has a number of excellent suppliers, and the entire Bosch Group often works closely with a single customer to develop software for them.
- Advanced initiatives in development with Tesla have been accumulated globally, allowing each base to propose the essence to local customers.
- Bosch's embedded software standard development language is C, with Python used for automation of business and data processing parts.
- Basic knowledge of C and Python is desirable for software personnel.
- A basic understanding of software structure and architecture is beneficial.
- Experience with embedded software is not required; the door is open to anyone interested in software development (application, web, image processing).
- Vehicle control software requires knowledge of hardware and physics, but the barrier is not high for those who find it enjoyable.
- Bosch products keep people safe, leading to great pride and responsibility in safety standards and software quality.
- The Bosch education system includes company-provided training and self-study opportunities for growth.
- As a European company, Bosch has a rational organizational culture compared to Japan, with a very systematic training system.
- The training curriculum is diverse, allowing free choice of subjects even if not directly related to work.
- Leadership training curriculum was useful for career advancement, teaching how to think as a leader and manager.
- This culture applies to career choices, offering opportunities to take initiative in jobs one wants to challenge.
- Plenty of opportunities for self-improvement exist due to many different jobs.
- Flexibility to absorb and ability to learn can lead to becoming an engineer even without automotive industry experience.
