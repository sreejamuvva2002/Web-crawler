# Rivian Automotive Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-06-26

**Source:** [VW Invests $5 Billion in Rivian to Revitalize Electric Vehicle Ambitions](https://theglobaleconomics.com/2024/06/26/vw-invests-in-rivian/)  ·  `page_059151`

## Facts

- Rivian Automotive Inc. is set to receive funding through a partnership with Volkswagen.
- The partnership involves an initial investment of $1 billion and possibly additional funding of up to $4 billion.
- VW and Rivian will equally control and own the new enterprise.
- In return, VW will receive a partner to create “next-generation” battery-powered vehicles and software, as well as access to the startup’s technology for use in its EVs.
- After the announcement on Tuesday, Rivian shares increased more than 50% in extended trading, regaining over half of the stock’s losses for the year.
- The partnership helps the EV manufacturer overcome difficulties in increasing the output and delivery of its SUV and truck models.
- The American EV manufacturer halted plans in March to construct a new manufacturing facility in Georgia to save money.
- The firm was dealing with significant losses that totaled almost $39,000 for each car manufactured during the previous quarter.
- The broader auto sector is facing layoffs brought on by an unexpected decline in demand for EVs.
- General Motors Co. recently stated that it will take “decades” for the EV industry to flourish.
- Ford Motor Co. is reducing investment in electronic vehicles by $12 billion and delaying the opening of new facilities and models powered by batteries.
- Consumers in general are largely unwilling to change to electronic vehicles and because of this, Rivian and other companies are struggling.
- Even Tesla Inc., the market leader, is seeing declining profit margins and underwhelming sales.
- Clinical professor Erik Gordon of the University of Michigan’s Ross School of Business stated it would be too expensive to go it alone and investors are not as interested in EV companies as they were when Rivian first started.
- Through an unsecured convertible note exchanged into Rivian shares on or after December 1st, VW intends to acquire the initial $1 billion equity interest in Rivian.
- The largest shareholder in Rivian is Amazon.com Inc., with a 16% stake valued at around $2 billion as of Tuesday’s close.
- The agreement mandates that VW spend an extra $2 billion in Rivian shares in two equal installments in 2025 and 2026.
- The German manufacturer plans to contribute $2 billion to the joint venture through a loan accessible in 2026 and a payment upon the business’s founding.
- The EV manufacturer finds the agreement’s structure advantageous because if Rivian shares rise above their current price, there will be less equity dilution and VW will ultimately hold a lower stake.
- Rivian CEO RJ Scaringe stated on a conference call that the company will be able to move forward with plans to build the new facility in Georgia with VW's support.
- By the terms of the contract, Rivian must still contribute $5 billion to the Georgia project by the end of the decade.
- Analyst Garrett Nelson of CFRA Research says the announcement is a “vote of confidence in Rivian,” but it “does little” to solve the company’s cash deficit and operational issues.
- In November 2021, at the height of excitement around the imminent introduction of electric automobiles, Rivian went public, garnering interest as a potential formidable challenger to Tesla.
- For a brief period, Rivian’s market value outdid that of Ford and GM due to an early surge in share prices.
- Since then, a lot of EV firms have failed as mainstream consumers have shifted away from expensive versions.
- VW will now have access to Rivian’s EV architecture and software after years of battling to produce plug-in vehicles as successful and efficient as Tesla’s.
- In November 2021, Rivian abandoned its plans to collaborate on the development of electric vehicles with Ford, an early investor.
- In December 2022, Rivian cancelled an agreement with Mercedes-Benz to manufacture electric vans.
