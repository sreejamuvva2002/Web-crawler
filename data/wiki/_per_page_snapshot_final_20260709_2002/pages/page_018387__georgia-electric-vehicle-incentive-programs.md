# Georgia Electric Vehicle Incentive Programs

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Georgia EV Incentives 2026 — Tax Credits, Rebates & Savings](https://caralpha.org/states/georgia/ev-incentives/)  ·  `page_018387`

## Facts

- Georgia offers incentives designed to encourage the adoption of electric and alternative-fuel vehicles.
- These state incentive programs can save buyers thousands of dollars on the purchase of an EV, in addition to federal incentives.
- The federal Clean Vehicle Credit, established under the Inflation Reduction Act, offers up to $7,500 for qualifying new battery electric vehicles and plug-in hybrids that meet domestic manufacturing requirements and critical mineral sourcing thresholds.
- A used EV tax credit of up to $4,000 (or 30% of the sale price, whichever is less) is available for qualifying used electric vehicles purchased from a licensed dealer, provided the vehicle is at least two model years old and priced at $25,000 or less.
- Starting in 2024, buyers can transfer federal EV tax credits to dealers at the point of sale to reduce the purchase price immediately instead of waiting for a tax return refund.
- Businesses can claim a commercial clean vehicle credit of up to $7,500 for qualifying light-duty EVs and up to $40,000 for heavier commercial EVs used for business purposes.
- Federal new EV tax credit income limits require modified AGI to be below $300,000 for married filing jointly, $225,000 for head of household, or $150,000 for single filers.
- Federal used EV tax credit income limits require modified AGI to be below $150,000 for married filing jointly, $112,500 for head of household, or $75,000 for single filers.
- Federal tax credits for SUVs, vans, and pickups require the vehicle's MSRP to not exceed $80,000.
- Federal tax credits for all other vehicles, such as sedans and hatchbacks, require the vehicle's MSRP to not exceed $55,000.
- Federal EV tax credit eligibility depends on where the vehicle was assembled, where the battery components were manufactured, and where the critical minerals were sourced.
- The IRS maintains an updated list of qualifying vehicles online for buyers to verify before purchasing.
- Popular qualifying vehicle models include select US-assembled Tesla models, Chevrolet Bolt/Equinox EV/Blazer EV, Ford F-150 Lightning/Mustang Mach-E, Rivian R1T/R1S, Hyundai and Kia US-assembled models, BMW select models, and Chrysler/Stellantis Jeep and Ram plug-in hybrids.
- The list of eligible vehicles for federal tax credits is updated quarterly, so buyers must verify qualification status before purchasing.
- To claim the federal EV tax credit, buyers must verify vehicle eligibility by checking IRS and Department of Energy websites for the specific make, model, and trim.
- The new vehicle federal tax credit requires the EV to be purchased or leased from a licensed dealer rather than a private party.
- Dealers are required to submit a time-of-sale report to the IRS confirming the vehicle qualifies and the applicable credit amount.
- Buyers can claim the federal credit by filing IRS Form 8936 with their tax return or by electing an immediate point-of-sale transfer to the dealer.
- Georgia state EV incentive programs may require separate applications through the state's energy office or DMV.
- Many Georgia state incentive programs have limited funding and operate on a first-come, first-served basis.
- Level 1 EV charging uses a standard 120V household outlet, adds approximately 3-5 miles of range per hour, has no installation cost, and is best suited for plug-in hybrids or low-mileage drivers.
- Level 2 EV charging requires a 240V outlet or hardwired installation, adds 20-40 miles of range per hour, and typically costs $500-$2,000 to install depending on home electrical capacity.
- The federal Alternative Fuel Vehicle Refueling Property Credit covers 30% of home charger installation costs, up to $1,000 for residential installations.
- Many electric utility companies in Georgia offer time-of-use rates that make overnight EV charging significantly cheaper.
- Some Georgia utility companies also offer rebates specifically for EV charger installation.
