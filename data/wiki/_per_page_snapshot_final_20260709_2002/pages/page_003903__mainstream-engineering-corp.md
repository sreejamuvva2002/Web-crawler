# Mainstream Engineering Corp.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** thermal_management  ·  **Published:** 2022-03-27

**Source:** [Ice Storage and Other Thermal Storage-Related Systems | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/cmei/buildings/articles/ice-storage-and-other-thermal-storage-related-systems)  ·  `page_003903`

## Facts

- Lead Performer is Mainstream Engineering Corp., located in Rockledge, FL.
- Partner organization is National Laboratory of the Rockies, located in Boulder, CO.
- Document date is October 12, 2021.
- DOE Total Funding for the project is $199,874.45.
- FY20 DOE Funding allocated to the project is $199,874.45.
- Project Term runs from June 28, 2021 to March 27, 2022.
- Funding Type is Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR) Phase 1.
- Growing concerns regarding the impacts of CO2 emissions and climate change have prompted both states and the federal government to promote the adoption of renewable energy sources.
- Combined with rapidly decreasing costs, the share of intermittent renewable energy generation on the grid, such as wind and solar, has increased rapidly.
- Intermittent energy sources do not necessarily align with periods of high electric demand, putting additional stress on the grid.
- Electric utilities are required to rely on high-cost, short-term, fossil-fuel power generation during peak demand.
- Excess renewable generation capacity exists during specific periods, leading to curtailment of these resources.
- No significant grid-level storage technology has yet gained traction.
- Utilities are attempting a market-based approach to shift electric demand away from peak periods with Time-of-Use (TOU) pricing.
- Consumers can easily shift some electric loads to off-peak periods, but heat and cooling (HVAC) systems must run at specific times of day when climate control is required.
- HVAC systems are the single largest electrical load for most residential and commercial customers.
- HVAC systems are not able to effectively shift their electrical usage and take advantage of TOU pricing.
- Mainstream Engineering and partners at the National Laboratory of the Rockies will develop and demonstrate a low-cost thermal energy storage heat exchanger using water as a phase-change material (PCM).
- The PCM heat exchanger (PCM-HX) can be integrated into existing residential and commercial scale HVAC systems.
- The PCM-HX will be produced with advanced materials and manufacturing techniques.
- During periods of low electrical demand where prices are low, the system charges by freezing the water in the PCM-HX.
- During high demand periods the PCM-HX discharges, significantly reducing the electrical power required by the integrated HVAC system.
- The product allows consumers to shift their electrical usage to off-peak periods and take advantage of lower TOU prices.
- Adoption of this HVAC thermal storage technology will have significant benefits to individual consumers, grid stability, and the further adoption of intermittent renewable energy sources.
- Integrating this thermal storage scheme into HVAC systems using either the Thermal Energy Storage Subcooler (TESS) or the Integrated Two-Phase Pump Loop (I2PPL) design will increase costs on the order of $800 to $2,500.
- This cost increase represents a 20 to 60 percent increase in the cost of a new HVAC system.
- Return on investment for the additional cost could be as low as two to four years depending on TOU rates.
- TOU rates are only likely to become more advantageous in the future.
- The technology is significantly cheaper than pure battery storage, which can cost on the order of $10,000 for even the most basic or low-capacity systems.
- This technology could allow low-to-middle income households to participate in the green energy revolution that has thus far been limited to those who can finance solar and battery systems costing tens of thousands of dollars.
- Shifting electrical loads will reduce increasing costs incurred by utilities and customers due to peak electrical demand and the effects of intermittent but low-cost energy sources, benefiting all users.
- Energy storage will be required for adoption of significant percentages of renewable energy generation, particularly approaching the 2030 California 50% RPS.
- This will become increasingly important as the nation and world attempt to limit carbon emission in the coming decades.
- The Phase I effort will develop and demonstrate a novel PCM-HX that can be integrated into either the TESS or I2PPL schemes to achieve load shifting.
- DOE Technology Manager is Fredericka Brown.
- Lead Performer contact is Jeffrey Milkie, PhD, from Mainstream Engineering.
