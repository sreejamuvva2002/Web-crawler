# MollerTech

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2016-10-26

**Source:** [MollerTech plans $46M Alabama auto parts plant with 222 jobs - Yellowhammer News](https://yellowhammernews.com/mollertech-plans-46m-alabama-auto-parts-plant-222-jobs/)  ·  `page_057965`

## Facts

- German automotive supplier MollerTech announced it will invest $46.3 million to build a manufacturing facility in Bibb County, Alabama.
- The project will create 222 jobs in Alabama’s auto sector.
- Governor Robert Bentley stated that attracting investment and new jobs to rural counties is a key priority in the state's economic development strategy.
- Governor Robert Bentley noted that MollerTech’s facility represents the type of project the state has been targeting.
- Governor Robert Bentley mentioned new tools are available to help rural counties overcome economic development obstacles and expressed commitment to using them.
- MollerTech plans to construct a 150,000-square-foot facility in the Scott G. Davis Industrial Park.
- The facility site is located near Interstate 20/59 and close to the Woodstock community in northern Bibb County.
- The facility will manufacture interior parts for Mercedes-Benz’s next-generation SUVs.
- These SUVs will be produced at Mercedes-Benz’s Alabama assembly plant following a $1.3 billion expansion.
- MollerTech North American President and CEO Steve Jordan stated the Bibb County facility will serve as the company’s flagship U.S. manufacturing facility.
- The new facility will incorporate new ideas and best practices from other operations in the company’s global manufacturing footprint.
- Steve Jordan said the company spent many months visiting various states and numerous counties in Alabama before deciding to settle in Bibb County.
- Jordan stated MollerTech's culture is centered on people and local communities, finding a "good fit" with Bibb County.
- Jordan noted that financial and personal help from the state and county helped influence the decision.
- Jordan thanked the State of Alabama and Bibb County for their patience and support during the venture.
- The project is codenamed "Helios".
- The Birmingham region competed for the Helios project against other sites across the Southeast.
- The Bibb County Commission, Alabama Department of Commerce, Economic Development Partnership of Alabama, Alabama Power Co., and Birmingham Business Alliance collaborated to bring MollerTech to the Birmingham region.
- Greg Canfield, secretary of the Alabama Department of Commerce, joined company leaders and local officials at an announcement ceremony at the Bibb County Board of Education.
- Bibb County provided 20 acres of land in the Scott G. Davis Industrial Park for the facility.
- Bibb County provided an abatement of non-educational sales and use taxes and property taxes.
- The state provided jobs and investment credits and AIDT workforce training, all valued at $12.3 million over a 15-year period.
- Bibb County Commissioner Ricky Hubbard said the project will be a "great boost" for the county and its workforce.
- Hubbard stated MollerTech will be the second largest manufacturing employer in the county and the third largest overall.
- Hubbard expressed excitement that the investment will impact the lives of many local people.
- MollerTech originated as a family copper trading and processing business in 1730.
- This project represents the 18th German corporate investment in the seven-county Birmingham metro area, according to the Birmingham Business Alliance (BBA).
- These 18 companies will employ approximately 1,700 people once MollerTech is operational.
- BBA president and CEO Brian Hilson expressed excitement about MollerTech's future workforce and the impact of the 222 job opportunities on employees and their families.
- MollerTech is the automotive arm of Germany-based MollerGroup, which remains a family-owned company with 2,300 employees worldwide.
- Besides Mercedes-Benz, MollerTech develops and produces interior parts for automakers including Audi, BMW, General Motors, Honda, and Toyota.
- MollerTech has existing U.S. facilities in Elberton, Georgia, and Shelby Township, Michigan.
- The company hopes the Alabama facility, located just six miles from the Mercedes plant in Vance, will reach full production by the end of 2019.
