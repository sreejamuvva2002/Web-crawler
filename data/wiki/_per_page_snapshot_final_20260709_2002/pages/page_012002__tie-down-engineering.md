# Tie Down Engineering

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Business Spotlight | Tie Down Engineering | Fulton Industrial CID](https://boulevardcid.org/the-district/business-spotlights/tie-down-engineering/)  ·  `page_012002`

## Facts

- Tie Down Engineering is a manufacturer and supplier of products for the agriculture, safety, marine, manufactured housing, and roofing industries.
- The company has been located in the Fulton Industrial Boulevard area for more than four decades.
- Tie Down Engineering was founded in Miami in 1968 by Chuck MacKarvich.
- The company initially produced tie-down anchors for manufactured homes.
- Founder Chuck MacKarvich remains chairman today.
- The company relocated from South Florida to Atlanta in 1981 due to challenges of operating a rapidly expanding manufacturing business from Miami.
- Chuck MacKarvich selected Atlanta because drawing a circle around the metropolitan area provides access to approximately 70% of the U.S. population within one day’s truck drive.
- Atlanta serves as a crossroads between the East, West, North, and South.
- Quick access to four major interstates intersecting Atlanta influenced the choice of the Fulton Industrial area.
- In the early 1980s, Tie Down Engineering built a 40,000 square-foot facility on Stonehill Drive in the Fulton Industrial area.
- The company purchased adjacent property to allow for future growth.
- Tie Down Engineering currently operates 12 buildings, eight of which are contiguous.
- The company employs over 300 people.
- Tie Down Engineering manufactures more than 8,000 products across six different divisions.
- The company is known for innovative product design, custom manufacturing, and streamlining the assembly process.
- Operations run over approximately 800,000 square feet of ISO-9001 certified facilities in Atlanta.
- All Tie Down Engineering products are manufactured in America at its Atlanta headquarters.
- Chuck MacKarvich was a proponent of creating the Fulton Industrial Community Improvement District (CID).
- He served as a board member and treasurer for the CID for nearly five years.
- Many of the CID's early meetings were held at the Tie Down Engineering office.
- Chuck MacKarvich stated that the CID cleaned up the area, improved public safety, and increased property values compared to the previous lack of government support.
- The company's two sons, son-in-law, and nephew all work in the business.
- Family members agree the Atlanta location is perfect for Tie Down Engineering.
