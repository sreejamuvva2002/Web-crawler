# Aurubis

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2024-09-20

**Source:** [Aurubis recycling plant brings over 200 jobs to the Augusta-Aiken area](https://www.wrdw.com/2024/09/20/kemp-comes-augusta-ribbon-cutting-aurubis-recycling-plant/)  ·  `page_017334`

## Facts

- Gov. Brian Kemp came to Augusta on Friday for the ribbon-cutting of a new metal recycling plant in south Augusta.
- The project took two years in the making since the groundbreaking occurred a little over two years ago at the Aurubis plant site.
- At the time of the June 2022 groundbreaking, the $320 million price tag made it Germany’s all-time largest investment in Georgia.
- The facility will bring more than 200 jobs to the Augusta-Aiken area.
- The plant will create 235 local jobs paying more than a livable wage and above the average county wage.
- Aurubis expects to be fully staffed with every position filled by the middle of 2025.
- The company is working with technical colleges in Augusta and Aiken and Richmond County schools, providing nearly 200,000 in scholarships.
- Aurubis is also collaborating with local organizations like United Way and Golden Harvest.
- The plant will be the first of its kind in the U.S.
- From Augusta, the company plans to produce materials found in lithium-ion batteries and other electronics.
- The facility will process more than 180,000 tons of complex recycling materials a year.
- The recovered metals are used in countless modern-day essentials, from mobile phones and computers to power plants and electric vehicles.
- Aurubis is a global provider of non-ferrous metals and one of the largest copper recyclers worldwide.
- The company processes complex metal concentrates, scrap metals, organic and inorganic metal-bearing recycling materials and industrial residues into metals.
- Aurubis produces more than 1 million tons of copper cathodes annually, which are used to make wire rod, continuous cast shapes, profiles and flat rolled products.
- The company also produces precious metals, selenium, lead, nickel, tin and zinc, along with sulfuric acid and iron silicate.
- Augusta Mayor Garnett Johnson said, “This site is a result of a lot of hard work.”
- Gov. Brian Kemp stated, “We want to bring jobs that are paying above the average county wage. And certainly these jobs are,”
- Tonya Bonitatibus of the Savannah Riverkeeper raised concerns about the environmental impact and potential for air pollution.
- Bonitatibus said, “I think the potential for recycling companies to harm people, not only is it extremely real, we have a history of it here in Augusta Hyde Park.”
- She suggested the company should relocate to Evans or West Augusta instead.
- Aurubis states that its production is carried out with very high environmental protection standards to maintain a clean environment.
- The American Lung Association reported that this year Augusta is one of the worst places for particle pollution.
