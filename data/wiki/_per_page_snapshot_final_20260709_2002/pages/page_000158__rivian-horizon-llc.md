# Rivian Horizon, LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-05-02

**Source:** [EX-10.1](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1874178/000119312522143653/d356209dex101.htm)  ·  `page_000158`

## Facts

- The Economic Development Agreement was signed on May 2, 2022, between Rivian Horizon, LLC, the State of Georgia, the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD), and the Joint Development Authority (JDA) of Jasper, Morgan, Newton, and Walton Counties.
- Rivian Automotive, LLC executed the agreement to agree to provisions applicable to it.
- The project aims to establish an electric vehicle manufacturing plant in Morgan County, Newton County, and the City of Social Circle, Georgia.
- The company committed to a capital investment totaling $5,000,000,000, which may be increased at its option under Section 3.6(b).
- The project will create 7,500 new full-time jobs directly employed by the company or an affiliate.
- Average annual wages for the new jobs are $56,000.
- The investment and job creation commitments must be met by December 31, 2028.
- Performance standards require a minimum of 7,500 jobs and $5,000,000,000 investment to avoid incentive repayment.
- Reaching an eighty percent combined performance level satisfies basic performance standards but must be maintained through the specified performance period.
- Failure to meet performance standards triggers direct repayment of incentives to the applicable public party.
- The project site consists of forty-four parcels totaling approximately 1,978 acres near Interstate Highway I-20 and US Highway 278 in Walton and Morgan Counties.
- The JDA currently owns approximately 665.17 acres across three parcels designated as the JDA Property.
- The JDA holds options to purchase an additional approximately 1,312.83 acres designated as the Option Property.
- A trailing tract of approximately 272.2 acres will be acquired by the JDA no later than July 15, 2022.
- The facility site will be provided to the company on a long-term basis via a rental agreement and is offered as-is with all faults.
- GDEcD oversees state activities for the project and will appoint a dedicated government liaison to monitor performance.
- The JDA acts as an instrumentality of Jasper, Morgan, Newton, and Walton Counties under the Development Authorities Law.
- GDEcD confirmed REBA budget funding is available for the Project Development Grant.
- The Georgia legislature allocated $111,307,760 to the Georgia Department of Community Affairs via the Amended Year 2022 Budget to fund JDA Grant costs.
- A commitment letter from the DOT was provided for funding public roadway improvements.
- The JDA authorized issuance of Project Bonds (Series 2022) in an aggregate principal amount up to $15,000,000,000.
- Public parties will provide site infrastructure work, permit assistance, and workforce training facilities and programs.
- Due diligence materials provided by public parties include Phase I and II environmental assessments, wetlands delineation, geotechnical reports, traffic studies, and title work.
- The JDA is responsible for conducting additional due diligence per Exhibit A-4 and will share final reports with the company within ten days of completion.
- Confidentiality of due diligence reports is subject to the Georgia Open Records Act, Open Meetings Act, and Trade Secrets Act.
- Incentives include a Project Development Grant, state tax incentives, real and personal property tax incentives, PILOT payments, Freeport Tax Exemption, and Mega-Projects Job Tax Credit.
- A Revenue Sharing Agreement for Stanton Springs dated November 16, 2021, governs intergovernmental contracts among the counties, school districts, and taxing authorities.
- The project requires a §404 Wetlands Permit from the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers for stream and wetland impacts.
- Workforce training commitments reference the Quick Start program (Exhibit C) and a manufacturing training center with defined construction funds and option prices.
- Early access to the project site may be granted via an early access agreement prior to or after conveyance to the state, excluding the trailing tract until acquired.
