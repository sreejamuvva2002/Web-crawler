# Volvo Cars

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-04-11

**Source:** [Volvo Cars may take up to two years to expand US production to avoid tariffs, CEO tells daily DN](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/volvo-cars-may-two-years-150618418.html?fr=sycsrp_catchall)  ·  `page_014166`

## Facts

- Sweden's Volvo Cars, which is controlled by Chinese auto maker Geely, will need up to two years to expand its U.S. car production in order to avoid hefty import tariffs.
- CEO Hakan Samuelsson told daily Dagens Nyheter (DN) on Friday that the expansion timeline is necessary.
- Volvo Cars is one of the most exposed automakers to U.S. President Donald Trump's auto tariffs as it imports most of its hybrid and electric models from Europe.
- A Volvo spokesperson declined to comment when contacted by Reuters.
- Samuelsson told DN that it would not be sustainable for the company in the long term to sell European-made cars in the United States at a 27.5% tariff.
- Importing vehicles from the company's Chinese plants is "impossible" given the much higher U.S. tariffs on China.
- In the short term, within one to two years, it will be about selling the cars the company already has.
- The tariff situation will put pressure on profit margins and customers will also have to pay more.
- Samuelsson last week said Volvo Cars was working towards increasing production in the U.S. as a response to tariffs.
