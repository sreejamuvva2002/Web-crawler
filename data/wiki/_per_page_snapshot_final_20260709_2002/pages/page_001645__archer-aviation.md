# Archer Aviation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-06

**Source:** [Archer Aviation on Track to Complete Georgia Manufacturing Facility by Year-End](https://evmagz.com/archer-aviation-on-track-to-complete-georgia-manufacturing-facility-by-year-end/)  ·  `page_001645`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation is set to finalize the construction of its 400,000-square-foot manufacturing plant in Covington, Georgia by the end of 2024.
- The facility will be equipped to produce 650 units of Archer Midnight, the company’s electric aerial vehicle (EAV).
- The Midnight is designed for short trips of 20 to 50 miles and can carry four passengers and a pilot.
- The vehicle boasts a maximum speed of 150 mph and requires only 10 minutes of battery charging between flights.
- The vehicle is intended for low-altitude operations and is designed for rapid, consecutive flights.
- CEO and founder Adam Goldstein stated that thanks to the hard work of the Archer and Stellantis teams, the company is tracking towards completing the manufacturing facility by the end of the year.
- Once complete, Archer will focus on ramping production up to 650 aircraft a year, with the goal of being the first in the eVTOL industry to achieve scaled manufacturing.
- Archer has signed an agreement with Southwest Airlines to develop an electric air taxi network in California utilizing Archer’s Midnight eVTOLs.
- The Southwest network will integrate with the airline’s operations at 14 airports across California, offering a seamless door-to-door travel experience.
- Archer secured a $55 million investment from Stellantis following a successful transition flight test in June.
- Stellantis had previously invested $110 million in Archer in 2023 and purchased $8 million in Archer stock earlier this year.
- The partnership leverages Stellantis’ supply chain and manufacturing resources to advance the development of Archer’s eVTOLs.
- Archer announced plans to establish an air mobility network connecting five locations in the San Francisco Bay area.
- In collaboration with Signature Aviation, Archer will gain access to major United Airlines hubs and private aviation terminals across the U.S.
- Archer has received Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) approval to commence commercial operations.
- Archer is now the second air taxi company, following Joby Aviation, to achieve FAA certification.
- The company aims to start commercial operations with United Airlines after receiving Type Certification next year.
- Archer secured a deal with KakaoMobility in South Korea for up to 50 Midnight EAVs, valued at $250 million, with operations projected to begin in 2026.
- Archer has partnered with Etihad Training to recruit and train pilots in the United Arab Emirates, with support from the Abu Dhabi Investment Office.
- Archer’s total funding has reached $1.1 billion, bolstered by recent investments from Stellantis, Boeing, United Airlines, and ARK Investment Management.
