# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-07

**Source:** [South Korea says it reached a deal with U.S. to release workers detained by ICE in Georgia Hyundai raid - CBS News](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/south-korea-reached-deal-us-release-workers-detained-ice/)  ·  `page_009336`

## Facts

- A deal was reached between South Korea and the United States on Sunday to release more than 300 workers detained in an immigration enforcement raid at a massive Hyundai plant in Georgia.
- During the Thursday raid, Immigration and Customs Enforcement agents arrested 475 immigrants suspected of living and working in the U.S. illegally.
- According to South Korea's Foreign Minister Cho Hyun, more than 300 of the detained workers were South Korean nationals.
- South Korea's presidential chief of staff Kang Hoon-sik said negotiations for the workers' releases had been finalized.
- The detained workers will be returned to South Korea as soon as remaining administrative steps are completed.
- South Korea plans to send a charter plane to transport the released workers back home.
- Hundreds of U.S. federal agents raided Hyundai's sprawling manufacturing site in southern Georgia last week.
- The targeted facility is where the Korean automaker manufactures electric vehicles.
- Steven Schrank, special agent in charge of Homeland Security Investigations in Georgia and Alabama, stated at a Friday news conference that a majority of the detained individuals were Korean nationals.
- Schrank noted he did not know the exact number of Korean nationals detained during the operation.
- The detained workers were employed by different companies, including subcontractors, according to Schrank.
- The raid was conducted as part of the Trump administration's mass deportation agenda.
- State officials have long designated the targeted facility as Georgia's largest economic development project.
- Steven Schrank confirmed the raid was executed as part of a month-long investigation into allegations of unlawful employment practices and other federal crimes.
- Schrank described the operation as the largest enforcement action at a single site in the history of Homeland Security Investigations, an ICE unit.
- Video released by U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement on Saturday showed a caravan of vehicles arriving at the site with federal agents directing workers to line up outside.
- Some detainees were ordered to place their hands against a bus while being frisked and shackled around their hands, ankles, and waist.
- Agents specifically focused their operation on a plant that remains under construction.
- Hyundai has partnered with LG Energy Solution at the facility to produce batteries for electric vehicles.
- Most of the detained individuals were transported to an immigration detention center in Folkston, Georgia, near the Florida state line.
- As of Friday, none of the detainees had been charged with any crimes, according to Schrank.
- Schrank added that the underlying investigation remains ongoing.
- The South Korean government expressed "concern and regret" over the raid targeting its citizens and sent diplomats to the site.
