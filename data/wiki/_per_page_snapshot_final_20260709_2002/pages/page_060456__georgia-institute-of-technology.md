# Georgia Institute of Technology

**Type:** research_center  ·  **Role:** research_and_development  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-06

**Source:** [Fast Charging Strengthens Zinc-Ion Batteries, Georgia Tech Study Finds](https://evmagz.com/fast-charging-strengthens-zinc-ion-batteries-georgia-tech-study-finds/)  ·  `page_060456`

## Facts

- A research team at Georgia Institute of Technology has discovered that fast charging can improve the performance of zinc-ion batteries.
- The discovery challenges conventional assumptions about battery degradation.
- The team was led by Hailong Chen, an associate professor in Georgia Tech’s George W. Woodruff School of Mechanical Engineering.
- Higher charging rates suppress the formation of dendrites—needle-like metal spikes that can short-circuit batteries.
- Faster charging allows zinc to stack into smooth, dense layers instead of accelerating dendrite formation.
- Hailong Chen stated: “We found that using faster charging actually suppressed dendrite formation instead of accelerating it.”
- Zinc-ion batteries are considered a potential alternative to lithium-ion batteries because zinc is more abundant, less expensive, and safer.
- Dendrite formation has historically limited the practical applications of zinc-ion batteries.
- The findings were published in Nature Communications.
- Fast charging may prevent dendrite-related failure modes and extend the battery’s lifespan.
- Chen’s team developed a custom experimental tool to observe zinc’s behavior in real time under different charging speeds.
- Hailong Chen noted: “We weren’t just seeing whether the battery worked or not,” adding, “We were watching the structure of the material evolve as it charged.”
- The team identified patterns in dendrite suppression that had previously gone unnoticed in laboratory tests.
- While the zinc anode showed improved performance, the cathode still requires optimization to match the anode’s durability.
- The team is currently experimenting with zinc blends to create a more robust full battery system.
- Researchers see potential for zinc-ion batteries in large-scale applications such as home solar energy storage and grid stabilization.
- Hailong Chen stated: “You can imagine these zinc-ion batteries being used to store solar energy in homes, or for grid stabilization. Anywhere you need reliable, affordable backup power.”
- The team estimates that zinc-ion batteries could be commercially available within five years.
- Zinc-ion batteries are positioned as an alternative amid fluctuating lithium prices and supply chain pressures.
