# Volvo Trucks

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Volvo Trucks starts production of the new Volvo VNR in the U.S.](https://www.volvotrucks.com/en-en/news-stories/press-releases/2026/feb/volvo-trucks-starts-production-of-the-new-volvo-vnr-in-the-u-s-.html)  ·  `page_014292`

## Facts

- The Volvo VNR was first unveiled in March 2025 with order books opening six months later.
- The first units of the new Volvo VNR have now rolled off the assembly line, marking the start of production.
- The all-new Volvo VNR is assembled at Volvo Trucks’ New River Valley Plant.
- All Volvo trucks for the North American market are built at the Virginia facility.
- The Virginia facility is Volvo’s largest manufacturing site globally and plays a central role in its U.S. operations.
- Volvo has recently invested $400 million in major upgrades at the plant.
- The investment includes the addition of a new 350,000-square-foot facility for cab welding along with upgraded paint and material flow systems.
- Volvo has built trucks for the U.S. and Canadian markets in Virginia for more than 40 years.
- Peter Voorhoeve, president of Volvo Trucks North America, stated that the expansion supports the company’s growth plans in North America and its long-standing commitment to U.S. manufacturing and a skilled workforce.
- The Volvo VNR is built on a completely new platform that is 90 percent new compared with the legacy model.
- The Volvo VNR is purpose-built for navigating congested city streets, distribution centers, and urban routes where visibility, precision, and agility are critical.
- Peter Voorhoeve stated that driving a truck in a busy city environment is challenging and the new VNR is engineered to address real-world operating challenges and improve safety for drivers and road users.
- The new VNR delivers up to a 7.5 percent improvement in fuel economy compared with the legacy model.
- The fuel economy improvement is driven by enhanced aerodynamics, advanced powertrain features, and idle-shutdown systems.
- The truck incorporates a comprehensive Volvo safety package with advanced onboard technologies designed to support operation in dense urban traffic and pedestrian-heavy environments.
- The truck offers better forward visibility than the previous model, enabled by a sloped hood design and a larger panoramic windshield.
- The truck incorporates industry-first safety innovations including integrated side curtain airbags.
- The start of production of the Volvo VNR follows the launch of Volvo Trucks’ all-new long-haul Volvo VNL.
- Together, the VNL and VNR reflect the company’s focus on application-specific trucks designed to improve efficiency, safety, and uptime across a range of fleet operations.
- Volvo Trucks supplies complete transport solutions for professional customers with its full range of medium- and heavy-duty trucks.
- Customer support is provided via a global network of dealers with 2,200 service points in about 130 countries.
- Volvo trucks are assembled in 12 countries across the globe.
- In 2025 approximately 120,000 Volvo trucks were delivered worldwide.
- Volvo Trucks is part of the Volvo Group, one of the world’s leading manufacturers of trucks, buses, construction equipment and marine and industrial engines.
- The group also provides complete solutions for financing and service.
- Volvo Trucks’ work is based on the core values of quality, safety and environmental care.
