# Hyundai Motor Georgia Plant

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Three workers died at Hyundai's Georgia plant since 2022, before US immigration raid, WSJ reports - NewsBreak](https://www.newsbreak.com/reuters-555486/4287909670207-three-workers-died-at-hyundai-s-georgia-plant-since-2022-before-us-immigration-raid-wsj-reports)  ·  `page_009450`

## Facts

- Three workers have died since Hyundai Motor started construction of its $7.6 billion auto plant in Georgia in 2022.
- More than a dozen workers have suffered serious injuries, including from falling without wearing harnesses and getting crushed by forklifts.
- Hyundai Motor North America conducted a comprehensive audit and strengthened safety oversight across the site.
- Hyundai has enhanced contractor vetting, training, and accountability to ensure all partners meet Hyundai’s standards and legal requirements.
- Dozens of current and former workers, many of them safety coordinators who helped oversee construction, reported that the work environment involved many inexperienced immigrant laborers.
- Workers reported that the work environment often had lax safety standards and frequent accidents.
- The plant is operated through a joint venture between Hyundai and South Korea's LG Energy Solution.
- An immigration raid last month detained hundreds of South Korean workers in the largest single-site enforcement operation in the U.S. Department of Homeland Security's history.
- Workers stated that Hyundai did not ensure that people were properly trained.
- Safety regulators did little to prevent worksite violations.
- Hyundai CEO Jose Munoz stated: 'We acted immediately and comprehensively to prevent anything like this from happening again.'
- Hyundai CEO Jose Munoz traveled to Georgia to tell the team directly: 'Their safety comes before production schedules, before costs, before profits, before everything.'
- Construction on the electric vehicle and battery plant is ongoing.
- The plant is located in Bryan County about 30 miles west of Savannah.
