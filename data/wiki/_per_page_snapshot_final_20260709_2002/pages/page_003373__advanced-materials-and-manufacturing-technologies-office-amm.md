# Advanced Materials and Manufacturing Technologies Office (AMMTO)

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2025-12-17

**Source:** [Advanced Materials and Manufacturing Technologies Office | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/cmei/ammto/advanced-materials-and-manufacturing-technologies-office)  ·  `page_003373`

## Facts

- AMMTO supports a globally dominant U.S. manufacturing and industrial base for a resilient energy system and secure supply chain.
- AMMTO's mission is to drive and inspire innovation that transforms materials, manufacturing, and workforce and advances America’s energy economy.
- AMMTO provides analysis, planning, management, and direction necessary for a balanced program of research, development, demonstration, technical assistance, and advanced manufacturing workforce development.
- AMMTO operates as a suboffice within the U.S. Department of Energy's Office of Critical Minerals and Energy Innovation.
- On December 17, 2025, DOE announced strategic investments to advance material supply chains and product lifecycles in support of a robust manufacturing sector and secure supply chain.
- On November 19, 2025, DOE pursued novel manufacturing processes and digital systems that enhance materials and energy efficiency to deliver benefits throughout the economy.
- On April 9, 2025, AMMTO advanced workforce education and entrepreneurial ecosystems for key energy system-enabling technologies, such as semiconductors and batteries.
- The office is located at Mail Stop EE-1, Department of Energy, Washington, DC 20585, under the Office of the Assistant Secretary for Energy Efficiency and Renewable Energy.
