# Unified Legacy

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-05-15

**Source:** [Unified Legacy To Invest $125M In New Macon-Bibb Manufacturing Facility - AirPro News](https://airpronews.com/2026/05/18/unified-legacy-to-invest-125m-in-new-macon-bibb-manufacturing-facility/)  ·  `page_052860`

## Facts

- Unified Legacy will invest $125 million to construct a new manufacturing facility in Macon-Bibb County, Georgia.
- The expansion is projected to create 500 new jobs over the next several years.
- Executing this project will effectively double Unified Legacy's footprint and production output within the state of Georgia.
- The development reinforces Georgia’s position as a critical supplier for the aerospace, defense, and rapidly expanding data center sectors.
- The new facility will be located on Barnes Ferry Road in Macon, Bibb County.
- Construction on the new facility is scheduled to begin in 2026.
- Parrish Construction was selected as the general contractor for the build.
- A Development of Regional Impact (DRI) filing with the Middle Georgia Regional Commission indicates the expansion is expected to generate up to $600,000 in annual tax revenue for the local area.
- The successful bid for the expansion involved a collaborative effort by the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD), the Macon-Bibb County Industrial Authority, and Georgia Power.
- Unified Legacy plans to hire across manufacturing, skilled trades, engineering, logistics, quality control, and administrative disciplines.
- Local leaders view the hiring initiative as a major step in creating fresh pathways into skilled trades for Middle Georgia residents.
- Macon-Bibb County Mayor Lester Miller stated that 500 more families will have the chance at careers and better lives due to the expansion.
- Unified Legacy is headquartered in Macon, Georgia, and serves as the parent company for Unified Defense and Prince Service & Manufacturing.
- The company specializes in advanced machining, welding, and precision metal fabrication.
- Unified Defense has operated a manufacturing facility in nearby Byron, Georgia, since 2022.
- Product lines include custom solutions such as ground support equipment, welded assemblies, generator enclosures, fuel storage tanks, and precision-machined components.
- These products are primarily targeted at the defense, aerospace, industrial, and data center infrastructure markets.
- Eric Williams serves as the CEO of Unified Legacy.
- Georgia Governor Brian P. Kemp announced the substantial economic development project on May 15, 2026.
- The official press release highlights a national push to strengthen domestic manufacturing, particularly within national security and defense ecosystems.
- Pat Wilson, Commissioner of the Georgia Department of Economic Development, noted that Georgia offers a competitive edge with a highly skilled workforce, world-class logistics, and strong local and state partnerships for strengthening domestic manufacturing critical to national security.
- AirPro News analysis observes that the $125 million investment is a strong indicator of shifting dynamics in U.S. supply-chains and the materialization of large-scale capital expenditures for aerospace and defense localization.
- The expansion specifically targets the physical hardware and enclosures required to house advanced computing systems, highlighting a critical bottleneck in the tech industry's data center infrastructure sector.
- By positioning itself at the intersection of aerospace, defense, and data centers, Unified Legacy aims to insulate its growth against sector-specific downturns while capitalizing on Georgia’s robust industrial incentives.
