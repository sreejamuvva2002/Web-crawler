# Hwashin Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-10-31

**Source:** [Auto Parts Supplier Hwashin to Bring 460 Jobs to Middle Georgia - Charge Forward Georgia](https://www.chargeforwardga.com/news-events/news/auto-parts-supplier-hwashin-to-bring-460-jobs-to-middle-georgia/)  ·  `page_056061`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced on October 31, 2023, that Hwashin will establish a new manufacturing facility in Dublin.
- The project will create over 460 new jobs in Laurens County.
- The investment totals more than $176 million.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated the goal is to become the e-mobility capital of the nation and highlighted Hwashin as part of a growing ecosystem bringing good jobs to Georgians.
- Hwashin operates as a Tier-1 supplier specializing in chassis and body parts for Hyundai Motor Group, Kia, and international original equipment manufacturers (OEMs).
- The company is headquartered in Korea and maintains production facilities across the United States, Asia, and South America.
- CEO Seo Jin Jung stated Hwashin aims to shape a future global city focused on electric vehicles using its 50-year expertise and collaboration with Georgia and Laurens County.
- The new Georgia facility is scheduled to begin production in late 2025.
- The site will be located at the Georgia Highway 257 Rail Served Site, which is the first Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) Select site in the state.
- A GRAD Certified Select status indicates a site has met or exceeded more rigorous certification requirements.
- The facility will primarily manufacture chassis components for OEMs including Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America and Kia Georgia.
- Dublin Mayor Joshua Kight welcomed Hwashin to the community and praised the Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority for demonstrating why the area is a great place to do business.
- Jeff Davis, Chairman of the Laurens County Board of Commissioners, noted Hwashin is the seventh international company to call Dublin and Laurens County home.
- The county chairman highlighted the partnership between Laurens County, the City of Dublin, and the development authority, predicting new career opportunities and increased housing and retail development.
- Georgia Ports Authority President and CEO Griff Lynch welcomed Hwashin as a member of the manufacturing community and emphasized the port's mission to connect companies to international markets.
- Alyce Thornhill, Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy at GDEcD's Global Commerce team, represented the department on the project alongside partners including the Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia EMC, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson noted that from the chosen site, Hwashin will have direct interstate access to at least two long-term automotive partners and can pursue an accelerated construction timeline due to the GRAD Select status.
- Georgia's fiscal year 2023 saw a 324 percent increase in automotive industry job creation compared to fiscal year 2021.
- Suppliers attracted by Georgia’s mobility OEMs resulted in over $2 billion in investment across the state.
- Since 2020, Georgia has pursued its entire EV supply chain, resulting in more than $25.1 billion in investments and 29,000 jobs.
- Hwashin Co., Ltd. was founded in 1975 and has held a prominent position in the Korean auto parts industry for nearly five decades.
- The company actively engages in research and development and continuously expands its product offerings to include lightweight aluminum components and eco-friendly solutions.
- Hwashin Co., Ltd. employs more than 4,200 personnel worldwide.
