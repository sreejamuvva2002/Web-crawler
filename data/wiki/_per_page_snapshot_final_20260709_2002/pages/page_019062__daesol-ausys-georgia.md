# Daesol Ausys Georgia

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-08-29

**Source:** [Automotive Supplier Daesol Ausys Georgia to Invest $72 Million in Harris County](https://ourcommunitymedia.org/us-news/automotive-supplier-daesol-ausys-georgia-to-invest-72-million-in-harris-county/)  ·  `page_019062`

## Facts

- Daesol Ausys Georgia is a Korean company specializing in designing and manufacturing motor and electric vehicle interior components.
- The company will establish a new manufacturing facility in West Point, Georgia.
- The facility will create more than 140 new jobs.
- The project involves a $72 million investment in Harris County.
- Daesol Ausys is a key supplier for Kia, Hyundai, and General Motors.
- The new facility will produce luggage boards and luggage covers.
- Operations at the new facility are expected to begin in December 2024.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced the project on August 29, 2023.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that West Point has become an automotive capital in its own right.
- Georgia’s automotive industry employs over 50,000 Georgians and will continue to grow as e-mobility suppliers choose all corners of the state, according to Governor Kemp.
- Daesol Ausys was established in Korea in 2017.
- Min Ho Kwon serves as the CEO of Daesol Ausys.
- The company is excited about locating in the Northwest Harris Business Park and continuing its partnership with the State of Georgia and Harris County, according to CEO Min Ho Kwon.
- Daesol Ausys Georgia will hire for positions in production, purchasing, quality control, maintenance, human resources, and management.
- Robert Grant serves as the Chairman of the Harris County Commission.
- West Point and Harris County have a strong relationship with Daesol Ausys’ sister companies Daehan Solutions and Daesol Materials, according to Robert Grant.
- Craig Greenhaw serves as the Chairman of the Harris County Development Authority.
- The project was developed in partnership with the Harris County Commission, Harris County Development Authority, City of West Point, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- Alyce Thornhill is the Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy at the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team.
- Pat Wilson serves as a GDEcD Commissioner.
- The automotive industry is providing generational change in the region and across the state, according to Commissioner Pat Wilson.
- Georgia has positioned itself as a leader in developing and harnessing emerging technologies for the evolving automotive and mobility industry.
- As the market transitions to electric vehicles, Georgia has pursued the entire supply chain, creating more than $25 billion in investments and 30,000 jobs since 2018.
- In fiscal year 2023, job creation in the automotive industry increased by 324 percent compared to FY21.
- Suppliers attracted by Georgia’s mobility original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) resulted in over $2 billion in investment across the state in FY23.
- Daesol Ausys Georgia is a leading manufacturer of automotive components and has a long history as one of the top suppliers to Kia and Hyundai worldwide.
