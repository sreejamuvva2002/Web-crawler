# Georgia Department of Economic Development

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2023-04-27

**Source:** [Representatives From Georgia’s 12 International Offices Convene | Georgia](https://georgia.org/blog/representatives-georgias-12-international-offices-convene-2023-visit)  ·  `page_002632`

## Facts

- Georgia’s Department of Economic Development welcomed representatives from the state’s 12 international offices abroad for a weeklong visit to Georgia during the last week of April 2023.
- The representatives are placed in 12 strategic markets, including Brazil, Canada, Chile, China, Colombia, Europe, Israel, Japan, Korea, Mexico, Peru, and the United Kingdom and Ireland.
- Georgia’s Japan and Europe offices were opened by then-Governor Jimmy Carter 50 years ago.
- In 2022, 52% of Georgia exports and 57% of Georgia’s total trade involved markets where Georgia maintains international representation, according to the 2022 Trade Report.
- Every county in the state exports goods, and small businesses make up 85% of Georgia’s exporters.
- Over the course of the week, 22 representatives met with local communities and businesses during their journey from Atlanta to Savannah.
- The group connected with GDEcD’s Atlanta office for training and project update sessions.
- The group attended receptions hosted by Georgia EMC, the Metro Atlanta Chamber, Georgia Allies, the Savannah Economic Development Authority (SEDA), and the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority.
- Penelope Kim from the Korea Office stated: “This week is like going to ‘Georgia school!’”
- The state tour featured multiple industry visits, including companies that support a few of Georgia’s key exports such as automotive products.
- JD Irving / Irving Consumer Products industry visit took place in Macon.
- Irving Tissue has successfully expanded in the state multiple times, helping the company reach key markets to drive growth while also setting high standards for community involvement.
- The business currently employs over 300 Georgians and over the last six years has invested nearly $1 billion into a community and state that have acted as a partner to this international business.
- Denmark-based company Dinex hosted the representatives for a tour of their operations in Dublin Willie Paulk Industrial Park.
- Since locating in Dublin, Dinex has invested more than $6.5 million in machinery and equipment and its employment has grown to nearly 100 people.
- Dinex manufactures emission control equipment for OEM and aftermarket truck and heavy equipment brands, representing a staple in Dublin-Laurens County’s automotive sector.
- The Dublin facility represents Dinex’s manufacturing presence in North America.
- Before departing Dublin, the group viewed the Georgia Highway 257 Rail Served Site and ADFORS/Saint-Gobain and Erdrich, both located on Willie Paulk Parkway.
- At the Georgia Advanced Manufacturing Training Center, Georgia Quick Start’s latest training facility in the state, representatives observed how the program provides customized training free-of-charge to qualified new, expanding, and existing businesses in the manufacturing industry.
- Various new electric vehicle related companies announced and under construction around the state are eligible to utilize Quick Start as a partner, including the nearby Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- The group met with partners at the Georgia Ports Authority (GPA) for a tour of the Port of Savannah, the largest single-terminal container facility of its kind in North America.
- More than $174 billion in goods flowed through the Ports of Savannah and Brunswick in 2022.
- GPA continues to invest in port capacity and infrastructure to maintain the port’s position as a global gateway.
- In February, GPA welcomed the largest ship-to-shore cranes on the East Coast at the Port of Savannah.
- GPA is planning $1.8 billion in investments in the ports this year (referring to 2023).
- In Bryan County, the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority (JDA) briefed the representatives about Hyundai’s plans for its first dedicated electric vehicle (EV) and battery manufacturing facility in Georgia.
- As a sign of the state’s growing presence in international business and its position as a leader in the electric mobility industry, seven Tier-1 suppliers have announced investments in the EV ecosystem around the state since Hyundai’s May 2022 announcement.
- In the last three years, at least 38 EV investments have been announced in the state totaling over $21.9 billion in investment and creating more than 28,000 new jobs in Georgia.
