# Industrial Info Resources (IIR)

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Metal Recycling Offers Answer to Rising Copper, Battery Needs](https://www.industrialinfo.com/iirenergy/industry-news/article/metal-recycling-offers-answer-to-rising-copper-battery-needs--358519)  ·  `page_001298`

## Facts

- Global demand is rising for recycled metals, with copper materials and lithium-ion batteries among those most needed.
- China dominates the global metal-recycling investment market, accounting for about 80% of investment at metal-recycling facilities worldwide.
- The U.S. accounts for nearly 11% of global investment in metal-recycling facilities.
- Industrial Info Resources is tracking nearly 500 active and proposed metal-recycling projects worldwide, totaling more than US$45 billion of investment.
- China accounts for more than 94% of the US$14.6 billion worth of projects deemed by Industrial Info Resources as having a high likelihood (81% or more) of beginning construction as scheduled within the next 12 months, with the U.S. making up only 3.1%.
- Strong demand from the data-center and automotive markets is keeping global copper prices at elevated levels.
- Aurubis Richmond LLC, a subsidiary of German firm Aurubis AG, expects to finish construction later this year on an expansion of its copper scrap recycling and processing plant in Augusta, Georgia.
- Under construction since 2022, the additional capacity at the Augusta plant is expected to double the facility's output to 70,000 tons per year, with demand fueled by data centers and EVs.
- Aurubis stated in a September 2025 press release that American industry alone currently needs about 1.8 million tons of copper per year, and experts estimate that demand will rise nearly 30% in the next five years.
- Aurubis noted that as of now, the U.S. imports about half of the copper it processes, and multi-metal recycling can close this gap quickly.
- Cirba Solutions, a subsidiary of environmental-services and heavy-construction firm The Heritage Group, has two projects set to be completed this year: a lithium-ion battery-recycling plant in Columbia, South Carolina, and an expansion of its battery-recycling campus in Lancaster, Ohio.
- The new South Carolina plant is expected to produce up to 500,000 EV batteries per year.
- The Ohio expansion is expected to boost output by more than 250,000 per year.
- Ara Partners is proposing an expansion of its lithium-ion battery-recycling plant in Bartlesville, Oklahoma.
- The first phase of the Ara Partners facility completed construction in December and can process 14,000 tons per year of battery materials.
- The Ara Partners expansion is designed to elevate processing capacity to 50,000 tons per year.
- China's copper-recycling market is experiencing a regional shortage of copper scrap raw materials, fueling a sustained rally in copper prices.
- State-owned and private companies across China are investing in secondary copper smelting; among them is Beiliu Guitong Equity Investment Partnership Enterprise, preparing to begin construction this summer on its secondary copper smelter in Yulin, Guangxi.
- The Beiliu Guitong project is expected to produce anode copper from scrap copper waste.
- Industrial Info Resources tracks over 250,000 current and future projects worth $30.2 trillion (USD) across the world.
