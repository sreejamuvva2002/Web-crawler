# EVCO Plastics

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** workforce_training  ·  **Published:** 2018-01-02

**Source:** [EVCO Plastics takes aim at skilled labor shortage with EVCO Educates](https://www.plasticstoday.com/injection-molding/evco-plastics-takes-aim-at-skilled-labor-shortage-with-evco-educates)  ·  `page_054389`

## Facts

- EVCO Plastics is a plastics processing company with nine manufacturing facilities located throughout the world.
- The company established a holistic education program called EVCO Educates to combat skilled labor shortages in the plastics industry.
- The cornerstone of the program is the EVCO Educates internship targeting college sophomores, juniors, or recent graduates of two-year technical schools.
- The summer internship focuses on automation, project engineering, quality engineering, design and manufacturing engineering, emphasizing practical applications on real customer projects.
- Dale Evans, President of EVCO Plastics, stated that part of the company's mission is to embrace and foster professional growth for both new industry entrants and current employees.
- The EVCO Educates program started about eight to 10 months prior to the January 2, 2018 article date.
- Rich Duval, Director of Quality and Human Resources at EVCO Plastics, explained the program is based on recruiting experiences from staff and aims to change their approach to attract needed demographics.
- EVCO developed a YouTube video for its website to help potential applicants understand the company before applying.
- The company implemented an employee referral program where employees receive rewards ranging from $500 to $2,000 depending on the job if they refer someone who is hired.
- EVCO's first recruiting effort under this program was scheduled for February 2018.
- The program will be implemented at all of EVCO's U.S. sites, with challenges varying by facility location.
- In Deforest, Wisconsin, local unemployment is about 2.2%, while areas up toward Green Bay see 2.6% unemployment.
- In North Georgia, unemployment is higher at 4.8%, but the company faces the same recruiting challenges as in Wisconsin.
- In Georgia, EVCO utilizes a Georgia Work Ready program to ensure employees have access to training and possess core job skills.
- EVCO Educates offers continuous skill enhancement opportunities, including grant-funded training sessions through the technical college system.
- The company contracts for custom classes through Technical College Training.
- A Supply Base Training program was implemented where EVCO invites suppliers to conduct on-site training regarding materials and colorants.
- The company offers tuition reimbursement and pay-for-performance programs, tying higher pay to increased responsibility as employees grow in their roles.
- EVCO benchmarked other local manufacturers to gauge benefits for retirement, vacation, and "new baby" leave.
- The company provides four weeks of paid leave for new moms and two weeks for new dads, which is in addition to a general Paid Time Off policy.
- EVCO is actively seeking automation and maintenance people and moldmakers, noting these are high-paying jobs.
- The company tries to grow positions running molding machines and process/technical work internally.
- EVCO works with local polytechnic schools and offers plant tours to ensure local students know the company's offerings.
