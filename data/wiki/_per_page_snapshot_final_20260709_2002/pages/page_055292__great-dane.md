# Great Dane

## Facts

- Great Dane is an American manufacturer of commercial semi-trailers and truck bodies, founded in 1900 as the Savannah Blow Pipe Company in Savannah, Georgia, and headquartered in Chicago, Illinois.
- The company produces a range of products including dry van trailers for truckload and less-than-truckload freight, refrigerated vans for temperature-controlled transport, flatbed trailers in steel, aluminum, and combo configurations, and custom truck bodies for foodservice and grocery applications.
- With over 125 years of operation, more than 5,000 employees, and approximately 400,000 trailers on the road, Great Dane emphasizes innovations such as FleetPulse telematics for fleet management, Microban-infused liners for antimicrobial protection, and SlipNot safety flooring with a 15-year warranty.
- Originally focused on metal fabrication, Great Dane entered the trailer industry in the 1930s amid the expansion of the U.S. highway system, introducing its first factory-finished refrigerated trailer in the 1940s.
- During World War II in the 1940s, Great Dane shifted significant resources to military logistics, producing approximately 12,000 specialized heavy-duty trailers under government contracts and receiving the Army-Navy E award five times.
- The company adopted the "Great Dane" name in the 1960s, expanded manufacturing plants across the United States in the 1970s, and merged with Pines Trailer in 1997 to form Great Dane Limited Partnership.
- In 2011, the company rebranded from Great Dane Trailers to Great Dane to better encompass its expanding portfolio of transportation solutions beyond just trailers.
- Great Dane operates as a privately held subsidiary of CC Industries, a Chicago-based investment holding company controlled by the Crown family.
- As of 2025, Dean Engelage serves as CEO after assuming the role in January 2023, while Rick Mullininx holds the positions of President and COO with his planned retirement set for December 31, 2026, after 45 years of service.
- Leadership at Great Dane is centered in its Chicago headquarters at 222 N. LaSalle Street, established during post-2010s centralization efforts, with additional legacy corporate offices in Savannah, Georgia.
- The company operates 11 manufacturing facilities across the United States serving as the core of its production infrastructure for trailers and truck bodies.
- The Statesboro, Georgia facility specializes in refrigerated trailer production, features a 450,000-square-foot operation capable of producing over 5,000 units annually, and employs more than 400 workers.
- In Brazil, Indiana, the plant focuses on dry van trailers, reefers, containers, and truck bodies, having produced over 485,000 units since its 1975 opening and currently employing nearly 900 people.
- The Elysburg, Pennsylvania plant opened in 2016 for dry van trailer production and reached its 25,000th trailer production milestone in May 2025.
- Recent expansions in Pennsylvania include a $3.5 million investment at the Elysburg plant in 2021 to add a second production shift and increase output, creating over 200 jobs.
- Great Dane initiated truck body production at its Danville, Pennsylvania facility in 2025, which also earned the 2024 Truck Trailer Manufacturers Association Plant Safety Award.
- Great Dane maintains a network of 19 company-owned branches across the United States, providing direct sales, service, and support to customers in key logistics hubs such as Atlanta, Georgia, and Dallas, Texas.
- The company relies on an extensive dealer network of authorized partners throughout the U.S., Canada, Chile, Colombia, Mexico, and Peru to broaden market reach.
- In April 2025, Trudell Trailer Sales rebranded to Midwest Great Dane, deepening integration with the manufacturer's brand and enhancing dealer services in the Midwest region.
- The Champion series dry van trailer features a Superior Strength Lightweight (SSL) van with galvalume steel side panels, a puncture-resistant liner six times stronger than traditional plywood, aerodynamic side panels, hot-dipped galvanized components, and an EnduroGuard rear frame.
- Great Dane's refrigerated Everest series supports multi-temperature zoning options for simultaneous carriage of fresh, frozen, and chilled items in partitioned compartments.
- Refrigerated trailers incorporate advanced refrigeration units from partners like Thermo King or Carrier, enabling precise temperature maintenance from 34°F to 41°F for refrigerated loads or -10°F to -20°F for frozen cargo.
- Insulation in refrigerated trailers is achieved through composite panels and ClimaCore technology, an HFO-based system with near-zero global warming potential that enhances thermal efficiency.
- Great Dane's flatbed trailers feature decks constructed from high-tensile steel, lightweight aluminum, or combo configurations blending both materials, enabling payload capacities supporting gross vehicle weight ratings up to 80,000 lbs.
- Great Dane's truck bodies utilize the AnchorPoint mounting system that eliminates traditional U-bolts and filler strips, enabling seamless integration and transfer between chassis.
- The Johnson and Alpine refrigerated truck body series incorporate integral cold plate technology with automatic defrost and all-electric refrigeration systems, achieving high thermal performance through high-pressure panel foaming and ArcticTherm™ liners.
- Microban antimicrobial liners eliminate 99.9% of bacteria to meet food safety standards such as the FSMA across Great Dane's temperature-controlled product lines.
- FleetPulse telematics is standard on all new trailers since its introduction, enabling real-time GPS tracking, cargo monitoring, and predictive maintenance alerts to optimize fleet operations.
- Aerodynamic enhancements such as side skirts and integrated fairings on various trailer lines reduce fuel consumption by up to 5% by minimizing drag, verified through EPA SmartWay certification.
- In 2025, Great Dane showcased the Sahara C-Series truck body at NTEA Work Truck Week, featuring an innovative tie-down system that secures mounting without U-bolts.
- Facilities received TTMA Plant Safety Awards in 2020 for Elysburg and Statesboro plants, in 2021 for Jonesboro and Huntsville, in 2022 for Statesboro, and in 2024 for the Danville, Pennsylvania plant.
