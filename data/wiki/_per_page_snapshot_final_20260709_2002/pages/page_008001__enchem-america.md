# Enchem America

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2023-07-24

**Source:** [Enchem America to receive $2 million in incentives from Tennessee - Tennessee - The Black Chronicle](https://blackchronicle.com/southeast/tennessee/enchem-america-to-receive-2-million-in-incentives-from-tennessee/)  ·  `page_008001`

## Facts

- Tennessee acknowledged $2 million in subsidies for Enchem America.
- Enchem America plans to spend $152.5 to create electrolyte manufacturing operations in Brownsville.
- The electrolytes are used in electric vehicle batteries.
- The facility will be located at the I-40 Advantage Industrial Park.
- The I-40 Advantage Industrial Park recently received a separate $1 million grant for access road construction and sewer infrastructure.
- This subsidy joins the $884 million Ford Blue Oval City electric truck subsidy.
- Tennessee has given $78 million in subsidies to the Ultium Cells plant in Spring Hill.
- Ultium plans to employ a workforce of 1,700 in Maury County.
- Magna announced plans to build three manufacturing facilities in Tennessee to supply Blue Oval City.
- Two of Magna's planned facilities will be at the West Tennessee Megasite adjoining Ford.
- The third Magna facility will be located in Lawrenceburg.
- Subsidies for Magna have not yet been released.
- The State Funding Board canceled its July meeting where it would normally approve those subsidies.
- Tennessee gave 6K Energy Tennessee a $1.75 million incentive for its battery material manufacturing plant in Jackson.
- 6K Energy Tennessee received a $50 million federal Department of Energy grant for the $166 million plant.
- Tennessee’s Department of Community and Economic Development continues to tout the subsidies as being necessary to bring the electric vehicle facilities to Tennessee.
- Academic researchers question whether those subsidies actually impact the economy.
- Research has shown that the subsidies benefit politicians in both political donations and votes more than they impact the economy.
- University of Texas professor Nathan Jensen previously told The Center Square the main reason to give incentives to vehicle plants is because they tend to have lots of high-paying jobs and deep supply chains for other local companies that manufacture vehicle components.
- It is not clear there are any benefits to giving subsidies for EV manufacturing.
- States do things such as pay for infrastructure as megasites are built, and then also hand out subsidies when companies agree to occupy the megasite.
- University of Texas professor Nathan Jensen stated that with EVs, these are not established companies sometimes and they have much shorter supply chains because EVs are simpler devices.
