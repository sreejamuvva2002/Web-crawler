# Anovion Technologies

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2023-05

**Source:** [Anovion Technologies to Create Over 400 Jobs in Bainbridge, Invest $800M in Manufacturing Facility - Augusta CEO](https://augustaceo.com/news/2023/05/anovion-technologies-create-over-400-jobs-bainbridge-invest-800m-manufacturing-facility/)  ·  `page_052267`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Anovion Technologies, a supplier of premium synthetic graphite anode materials for lithium-ion batteries, will build a new manufacturing facility in Bainbridge.
- The project will create more than 400 jobs.
- The project will involve over $800 million in investment in Decatur County.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated that Georgia is fast becoming the e-mobility capital of the nation.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp noted that job creators are recognizing that every corner of the state has the resources and workforce needed for success.
- Anovion is a climate tech-driven advanced materials company.
- Anovion is one of the first qualified U.S. suppliers of synthetic graphite anode materials for use in e-mobility applications.
- Graphite is the largest battery material used in electric vehicles by mass, more than copper, nickel, manganese, cobalt, and lithium.
- Eric Stopka, Chief Executive Officer of Anovion, stated the company is opening its first large-scale expansion manufacturing facility in Georgia.
- Eric Stopka noted that Georgia’s strong technical workforce and top-tier schools will help advance research partnerships and contribute to a highly skilled talent pipeline for Anovion’s innovation and climate technology jobs.
- Eric Stopka mentioned encouragement by the region’s growing availability of clean energy, resulting in increased sustainability for Anovion’s manufacturing process.
- Anovion’s new facility will be 1,500,000 square feet.
- The facility will utilize proprietary furnace technologies.
- The facility is located at Downrange Industrial Park in Bainbridge.
- The facility will be the first of its size and scale in North America.
- The facility will produce synthetic graphite for use in batteries that power EVs, electric storage systems, electronics, military applications, and other products.
- Operations are expected to begin in late 2025.
- The company will be hiring for a variety of technical, production, and engineering roles.
- The company will also hire for administrative and management positions.
- Bainbridge Mayor Edward Reynolds welcomed Anovion to Bainbridge.
- Mayor Edward Reynolds noted the $800 million investment and creation of over 400 high-paying, quality jobs as a huge win for the community.
- Pete Stephens, Chairman of the Decatur County Board of Commissioners, stated Decatur County leadership has focused for many years on attracting quality companies and jobs.
- Pete Stephens noted that diversifying the industry mix is necessary to remain competitive and grow.
- Project Manager Taylor Walden represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The project is in partnership with the City of Bainbridge, Development Authority of Bainbridge, Decatur County, University System of Georgia, Georgia EMC, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated much of the critical supply chain for e-mobility and manufacturing is currently sourced outside the U.S.
- Pat Wilson described Anovion as an innovative industry leader that fills that need and delivers another critical component to Georgia.
- Pat Wilson noted a diverse mix of public-private partnerships are coming together to support the next revolution in the automotive industry, from working with utility partners and establishing infrastructure to attracting and retaining top-talent through the University System of Georgia.
- Building on automotive industry assets, Georgia is positioned as a hub for the electric mobility industry.
- Since 2020, 40 EV-related projects have announced more than $21.9 billion in investment and 28,000 jobs in Georgia.
- More than 10 of these projects are battery manufacturers, recyclers, and suppliers.
- These battery-related projects represent approximately $8.5 billion in investment and 6,000 jobs.
