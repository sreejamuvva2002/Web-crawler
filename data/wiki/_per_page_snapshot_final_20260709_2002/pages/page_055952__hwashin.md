# Hwashin

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-10-31

**Source:** [More than 450 jobs are coming to Laurens County as auto parts company opens facility](https://www.yahoo.com/news/more-450-jobs-coming-laurens-202759311.html)  ·  `page_055952`

## Facts

- Hwashin plans to build a new manufacturing facility in Dublin, Laurens County.
- The facility will create around 460 jobs and bring more than $176 million investment to Laurens County.
- Nearly 500 new jobs will come to the Laurens County area following the opening of the facility.
- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp stated that Hwashin is the latest company to join Georgia's growing ecosystem of companies in the e-mobility industry.
- The new facility will be located at the Georgia Highway 257 Rail Served Site.
- Production at the new facility is scheduled to start in late 2025.
- The site is the first Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) Select site in the state, following years of planning and months of work.
- Hwashin will be the seventh international company to establish operations in Dublin and Laurens County.
- The facility will mainly focus on manufacturing chassis components for original equipment manufacturers (OEMs).
- Key OEM customers for the chassis components include Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America and Kia Georgia.
- Hwashin is a South Korean-based Tier-1 supplier that specializes in producing chassis and body parts for automobile makers.
- Hwashin currently operates manufacturing facilities in the U.S., Asia, and South America.
- The Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority successfully pitched the region to Hwashin as a prime location for business operations.
- Dublin Mayor Joshua Kight stated that the new facility will serve as an economic engine for the region, driving employment opportunities and quality growth.
