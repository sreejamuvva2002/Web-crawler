# HL-GA Battery

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09

**Source:** [Opening of Hyundai-LG EV battery plant to be delayed after ICE raid](https://www.ajc.com/news/2025/09/opening-of-georgia-ev-battery-plant-to-be-delayed-after-ice-raid/)  ·  `page_001131`

## Facts

- The HL-GA Battery facility is located next to Hyundai’s EV assembly factory along I-16 west of Savannah, Georgia.
- From the outside, the electric vehicle battery plant appears ready for operations, but construction remains ongoing inside.
- Evidence of ongoing construction includes a dozen or so lifts and hoists sitting outside the doors, plus several yet-to-be paved roads and parking lots.
- Work at the site was interrupted last week by an Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) raid that triggered an international firestorm.
- A top official stated that the plant’s opening has been delayed by two to three months following the raid.
- Immigration and Customs Enforcement arrested approximately 475 laborers accused of working at the site illegally.
- Among those arrested were 316 Koreans and 14 others from various Asian countries.
- The detained Korean workers, along with the other Asian detainees, flew back to Korea on Thursday after spending a week at a federal detention center in Folkston, southeast Georgia near the Florida state line.
- A Korean government official stated that the detained citizens were technicians and specialists tasked with setting up machinery at the Georgia factory site.
