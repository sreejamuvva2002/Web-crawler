# Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution Joint Venture Battery Plant

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-05-26

**Source:** [Hyundai and LG announce $4.3 billion plant in Georgia to build batteries for electric vehicles | Science News | thecanadianpressnews.ca](https://www.thecanadianpressnews.ca/science/hyundai-and-lg-announce-4-3-billion-plant-in-georgia-to-build-batteries-for-electric/article_d61783a5-eb09-5f66-81fe-8f5f45ad3952.html)  ·  `page_046673`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution announced they will build a $4.3 billion electric battery plant as part of Hyundai's new electric vehicle assembly plant in southeast Georgia.
- The companies will split the investment for the project.
- Production is slated to start as soon as late 2025.
- Hyundai Motor Co. CEO Jaehoon Chang said the battery plant would create a strong foundation to lead the global EV transition.
- Chang explained the company wants to speed up efforts to produce electrified Hyundai and Kia vehicles in North America.
- Chang stated Hyundai Motor Group is focusing on its electrification efforts to secure a leadership position in the global auto industry.
- In 2022, Hyundai said it would invest $5.5 billion to assemble electric vehicles and batteries in Ellabell, just west of Savannah.
- The Ellabell site is supposed to have 8,100 employees and is slated to begin producing vehicles in 2025.
- Garrison Douglas, spokesperson for Republican Gov. Brian Kemp, said the 3,000-job battery plant would be part of the 8,100 overall jobs.
- Douglas stated the $4.3 billion investment would be part of the previously announced $5.5 billion total.
- The Hyundai/LG plant is supposed to be able to supply batteries for 300,000 electric vehicles per year.
- This capacity matches the initial projected production of the adjoining vehicle assembly plant.
- Hyundai has said the Georgia plant could later expand to build 500,000 vehicles annually.
- Gov. Brian Kemp said this is exactly what they envisioned when Georgia landed the Hyundai Metaplant in May of last year.
- Kemp stated the project is the latest milestone in Georgia's path to becoming the EV capital of the nation.
- Auto parts suppliers have pledged to invest more than $2 billion and hire 4,800 people in the region around the Hyundai site.
- The announcements are part of an electric vehicle and battery land rush across the United States.
- Under the U.S. Inflation Reduction Act, EVs must be assembled in North America to qualify for a full $7,500 EV tax credit.
- A certain percentage of battery parts and minerals must come from North America or a U.S. free trade partner to qualify for the full tax credit.
- Currently, no Hyundai or Kia vehicles are eligible for the tax credit unless they are leased.
- Hyundai opposed having foreign-made vehicles excluded, in part because it is building American factories.
- Gov. Kemp has supported that position against excluding foreign-made vehicles from the tax credit.
- Democratic Sen. Jon Ossoff said Hyundai should wait until it is producing vehicles domestically using American-made batteries before claiming the credits.
- Ossoff told reporters that as soon as those vehicles are produced in Georgia, they can be eligible for the credits through the incentives in the IRA.
- Ossoff stated these manufacturing incentives are attracting and accelerating billions of dollars of investments in jobs and advanced energy and electric vehicle production capacity in Georgia.
- LG said this would be its seventh battery plant in operation or under construction in the U.S.
- LG is concentrating efforts to expand production in the country, citing federal incentives as a lure for manufacturers.
- This marks the second huge electric battery plant Hyundai is partnering to build in Georgia.
- Hyundai and SK On, a unit of South Korea's SK Group, announced in December they would jointly invest $4 billion to $5 billion to build a new plant northwest of Atlanta.
- The Cartersville plant will supply electric batteries for Hyundai and Kia electric vehicles assembled in the U.S.
- The Cartersville plant is planned to begin production in 2025 and employ a projected 3,500 people.
- Hyundai will need batteries for more than just vehicles made in Ellabell.
- The company is already assembling electric vehicles at its plant in Montgomery, Alabama.
- In April, Hyundai announced it would start assembling its electric Kia EV9 large SUV at the Kia plant in West Point, Georgia.
- Partnering with LG and SK diversifies Hyundai’s supplier base, giving the automaker more than one battery manufacturer to buy from.
- Douglas said no additional incentives would be offered because the battery plant is part of the overall Hyundai complex.
- The state of Georgia and local governments have already pledged $1.8 billion in tax breaks and other incentives.
- Greg LeRoy, executive director of Good Jobs First, stated this is the largest subsidy package a U.S. state has ever promised an automotive plant.
- Good Jobs First is described as a group skeptical of subsidies to private companies.
