# Ascend Elements

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Ascend Elements Opens Largest EV Battery Recycling Facility of North America](https://www.saurenergy.com/solar-energy-news/ascend-elements-opens-largest-ev-battery-recycling-facility-of-north-america)  ·  `page_001468`

## Facts

- Ascend Elements has opened its first commercial-scale lithium-ion battery recycling facility.
- The facility is located in Covington, Georgia.
- The facility cost $50 million to build.
- The facility is named "Base 1".
- Ascend Elements claims the Base 1 facility is North America's largest electric vehicle battery recycling facility.
- The facility began partial operations in August 2022.
- The facility has an annual capacity to process 30,000 metric tons of used lithium-ion batteries and manufacturing scrap.
- This processing capacity is equal to 70,000 electric vehicle batteries per year.
- Mike O'Kronley serves as the CEO of Ascend Elements.
- CEO Mike O'Kronley stated that the facility is an important part of America's EV battery infrastructure and emphasized the industry's need to continue building a closed-loop battery materials supply chain for cleaner and more sustainable EV batteries.
- Ascend Elements claims to recover up to 98% of critical battery metals in used EV batteries and gigafactory manufacturing scrap.
- The facility uses a patented Hydro-to-Cathode process that can reduce the carbon footprint of new EV battery cathode materials by up to 90%.
- The facility is powered by renewable energy.
- The facility features onsite wastewater recycling and treatment systems.
- Ascend Elements is headquartered in Westborough, Massachusetts.
- The company commercially produces lithium-ion battery precursor (pCAM) and cathode active materials (CAM).
- The Hydro-to-Cathode direct precursor synthesis technology produces new CAM from spent lithium-ion cells more efficiently than traditional methods, resulting in improved economics and lowered GHG emissions.
