# Aventure Aviation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2001-09-01

**Source:** [How Aventure Aviation Became a Global Aviation Brand](https://www.atlantamagazine.com/gabiz/up-up-and-away-global-aviation/)  ·  `page_016721`

## Facts

- Zaheer Faruqi launched Axis Aviation, a business providing spare aircraft parts, from his two-car garage in Peachtree City on September 1, 2001.
- The American aviation industry halted following the September 11 terrorist attacks, creating immediate hurdles for the new U.S.-based business without a domestic customer base.
- In late 2002, Faruqi partnered with the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) to guide his international expansion.
- Aventure Aviation now operates locations in China, Pakistan, Turkey, England, Canada, and the U.S.
- Faruqi earned degrees in administration management and aircraft maintenance engineering from Northrop University in Inglewood, California, and holds an FAA airframe and power plant license.
- Before founding his company, Faruqi worked for Avatar Alliance, a now-defunct aviation parts company in Fairburn, Georgia.
- The company's vision was to act as the "AutoZone of aviation" by sourcing and sending spare parts to keep commercial aircraft flying.
- Faruqi signed his first consignment deal in September 2003.
- Following then-President Georgia W. Bush's "Axis of Evil" remark, Faruqi rebranded Axis Aviation to Aventure Aviation.
- GDEcD facilitated an international trade trip to Toronto and Montreal that led to a deal with Air Canada Jazz, which became the company's best international customer.
- Aventure opened its first international office in Toronto in 2005 to service its growing customer base.
- Sixty percent of Aventure Aviation's revenue now comes from international customers.
- A GDEcD representative suggested expanding to Beijing, provided translated Mandarin business cards and brochures, arranged an interpreter, and set up meetings.
- Taube Ponce, GDEcD’s senior international trade manager, described Aventure as a "model client" for smartly using free economic development resources.
- In 2003, the company moved from its garage to a shared space six miles away in Peachtree City, with Georgia company Hipage handling shipping and receiving.
- Approximately five years later, Aventure constructed a new global headquarters featuring a 16,000-square-foot purpose-built facility with office and warehouse space near Atlanta.
- The company established a military division in 2010 to open new markets.
- In 2014, Aventure acquired its first aircraft, a Boeing 767, which it dismantled to harvest and sell parts.
- Due to airline cost-sensitivity, the business model shifted from accepting consignment parts to purchasing retired airplanes directly.
- In June 2025, Aventure Aviation completed its largest aircraft deal by purchasing 12 Boeing 737NGs from Alaska Airlines.
- Six weeks after the June 2025 purchase, the company opened a 70,000-square-foot warehouse as the first phase of its new headquarters in Peachtree City.
- Construction of a second phase, including a 54,000-square-foot office and an additional 36,000 square feet of warehouse space, is slated to begin later this year.
- Aventure Aviation currently employs 70 people worldwide, with 31 based internationally.
- The company received the 2025 Exporter of the Year award for Georgia and the Southeast Region from the Small Business Administration.
- Aventure was honored with a 2022 Exporter of the Year Award from the Export-Import Bank of the United States.
- The company achieved the 2021 U.S. President’s “E” Award for Export Excellence.
- Faruqi completed Aventure's first corporate acquisition of Atlanta Aviation International, an FAA-certified repair station based in Atlanta.
- Faruqi's three sons, Talha, Hamzah, and Osman, are owners of the company.
