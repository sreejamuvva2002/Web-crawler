# Rivian

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-08-19

**Source:** [Rivian moving forward on $5B Georgia EV plant | WardsAuto](https://www.wardsauto.com/news/archive-auto-rivian-kickoff-georgia-plant-gov-brian-kemp/758048/)  ·  `page_001054`

## Facts

- Rivian will host two special events next month to mark the restart of construction of its $5 billion Georgia electric vehicle factory.
- A community event is set for Sept. 14.
- A formal kickoff ceremony is set for Sept. 16 “in celebration of our strong partnership with Georgia and the planned resumption of construction in 2026.”
- The kickoff will be attended by stakeholders and government officials including Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp.
- The plant near Social Circle is expected to start production in 2028.
- The plant is expected to have an annual capacity of about 200,000 units.
- In March 2024, Rivian announced it was stopping construction on the Georgia factory project to save money.
- As part of that March 2024 decision, Rivian decided to move production of its upcoming R2 SUV to its plant in Normal, Illinois, to expedite sales.
- The project got back on track after Rivian won final approval for a $6.6 billion Department of Energy loan in January.
- The DOE loan funds are available through September 2028 but cannot be accessed until Rivian resumes work on the Georgia factory.
- In July, Rivian announced it was opening a new East Coast headquarters in Atlanta later this year.
- The new Atlanta headquarters will initially employ 100 people.
- The Atlanta headquarters will ultimately house 500 workers to support Rivian's operations in Georgia.
