# Bridgestone Corp.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Bridgestone launches new truck-tire business unit | FleetOwner](https://www.fleetowner.com/operations/article/21671559/bridgestone-launches-new-trucktire-business-unit)  ·  `page_018273`

## Facts

- Bridgestone officially merged its North American truck tire and retreading operations earlier this month.
- Bridgestone publicly launched the new Bridgestone Bandag Tire Solutions (BBTS) business unit for 1,000 dealers and distributors gathered in New York for the truck tire group’s annual BizCon meeting.
- Saul Solomon was introduced as the president of the newest Bridgestone Corp. business unit.
- The new business unit has moved its headquarters to the former Bandag offices in Muscatine, Iowa.
- Bridgestone purchased the privately held retread franchising business in mid-2007.
- The goal of the new operating unit is to bring fleets tires, retreads and solutions into one integrated package.
- Solomon stated the new operating unit has been structured “to be easy to do business with,” while acknowledging that “no one size fits all customers.”
- Sales and marketing for the merged Bridgestone truck tire and retread business will remain in Bridgestone’s North American headquarters in Nashville.
- Singh Alhuwalia, a Bridgestone veteran, now serves as president, sales & marketing for BBTS.
- The company's brands – Bridgestone, Firestone, Dayton and Bandag – will continue to go to market under their current mix of channels.
- Distribution channels include fleet sales, independent dealers, truckstops, and franchises for the Bandag retread line.
- Bridgestone will remain the premium truck tire brand, with a support infrastructure for fleets to match that market position.
- Firestone will continue to offer a less costly option.
- The Dayton brand will compete on price with low-cost imported truck tires.
- Bandag will retain its franchise structure.
