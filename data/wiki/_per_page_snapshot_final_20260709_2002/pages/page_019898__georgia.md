# Georgia

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Recap: Major Economic Developments Happening RTW Georgia | NILRR](https://nilrr.org/recap-major-economic-developments-happening-rtw-georgia/)  ·  `page_019898`

## Facts

- Businesses are choosing Georgia as a result of its Right to Work economy.
- The addition of 1,751 new jobs within the state comes from businesses such as Ecoplastic, Renewal by Andersen, and KISS USA.
- Altogether, these three businesses will be investing a total of $746 million into the Georgia economy.
- Ecoplastic Corporation is investing $205 million to establish a new facility in Right to Work Bulloch County, Georgia.
- Ecoplastic Corporation will create 456 new jobs.
- Ecoplastic Corporation is establishing a production facility for electric vehicle parts in Bulloch County, Georgia.
- Ecoplastic Corporation will provide a stable supply of high quality products to its client, HMGMA.
- Ecoplastic Corporation has decades of experience as a supplier for Kia and Hyundai.
- Ecoplastic is building the supplier network for the Hyundai metaplant in Bryan County.
- Renewal by Andersen is investing $420 to establish operations in Right to Work Henry County.
- Renewal by Andersen will create 900 new jobs.
- This is Renewal by Andersen’s first manufacturing facility in Georgia.
- Renewal by Andersen's products include flooring, appliances, and windows.
- Renewal by Andersen operations will be located in Locust Grove and Henry County.
- KISS USA is investing $121 million to establish operations in Right to Work Bryan County, Georgia.
- KISS USA will create 395 new jobs.
- KISS USA was attracted by Georgia's unmatched logistics network and combination of assets.
- Ecoplastic Corporation stated it looks forward to contributing to the economic development of the State of Georgia and Bulloch County.
- A state/county official stated they are pleased that this important Hyundai supplier has chosen their community, confident location, workforce, and quality of life will help them succeed.
- Renewal by Andersen stated that while this is its first manufacturing facility in Georgia, Andersen’s products have been in the state for years, including in stores of a large Georgia-based home improvement company.
- Bryan County officials stated they are excited to welcome KISS and look forward to continuing the relationship as KISS creates 395 new jobs.
