# Amara Industries

## Facts

- Amara Industries formally announced its acquisition of Thomson Plastics, a prominent player in large tonnage injection molding based in the Southeastern United States.
- The strategic acquisition is expected to bolster Amara's manufacturing footprint and expand its capabilities, positioning the company as a key competitor in the North American injection molding landscape.
- Thomson Plastics operates from two state-of-the-art locations in Georgia and North Carolina.
- Thomson Plastics specializes in large tonnage injection molding, with clamping force capacities reaching up to 3,700 tons.
- About 30% of Thomson Plastics' fleet includes machines that exceed 1,000 tons.
- Thomson Plastics is equipped with cutting-edge robotics and automated systems to ensure consistent performance in high-volume production environments.
- Thomson Plastics' technical portfolio covers injection molding, gas-assisted molding, over-molding, insert molding, in-mold labeling, robotic part picking, pad printing, and heat transfer decorating.
- Vince Orlando, Co-President at Amara Industries, stated that the addition of Thomson Plastics represents a strategic milestone for the organization that will enhance their ability to serve customers with scale, speed, and precision across North America.
- The acquisition marks a notable turning point in the North American injection molding industry.
- With the integration of Thomson Plastics, Amara Industries will expand its production capabilities and enhance service offerings to key sectors including automotive, consumer goods, industrial applications, and commercial markets.
- The strategic expansion into the Southeastern United States allows Amara Industries to provide customers with quicker delivery times, increased flexibility, and improved supply chain efficiency.
- Amara Industries is a comprehensive manufacturing solutions provider with a dedicated workforce of over 500 employees.
- Amara Industries spans various facets of manufacturing, covering design, product development, engineering, and materials science.
- Amara Industries employs advanced manufacturing techniques including injection molding, assembly, profile extrusion, decorating, and material compounding.
- Amara Industries holds key certifications such as ISO, IATF, and CTPAT.
