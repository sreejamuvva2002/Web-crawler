# Georgia Power Company

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** charging_infrastructure  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [EV manufacturing supply chain 'powering investments in Georgia'](https://worldbatterynews.com/ev-manufacturing-supply-chain--powering-investments-in-georgia--p1013-95/)  ·  `page_008217`

## Facts

- Georgia is currently home to more than 200 automotive-related facilities contributing $3bn (£2.1bn) annually to the state’s economy.
- These facilities include companies involved in the production, recycling, and development of EV batteries.
- The state has a fleet of around 30,000 EVs and 930 public charging stations.
- More than 116 million EVs on the road are expected by 2030.
- Nearly 60% of all passenger vehicle miles around the world will be in an electric vehicle by 2030.
- Georgia Power is dedicated to partnering with customers, businesses and government entities across the state to ensure they have the latest in charging capabilities and infrastructure.
- OEMs in the state and throughout the south-eastern US are already shifting their product lines to keep up with the increasing production of EVs.
- Georgia Power is working with state authorities to support the EV needs of OEMs including Mercedes-Benz, BMW, Kia and Volkswagen.
- Working with the Georgia Public Service Commission, Georgia Power has installed 51 direct current fast chargers in the state.
- Georgia Power will invest nearly $6m by 2022 in fast-charging infrastructure across Georgia.
- Ford and South Korea’s SK Innovation (SKI) announced they will manufacture next-generation battery cells and arrays in the US as part of a new joint venture – BlueOvalSK.
- SKI subsidiary, SK Battery America, is set to start mass production of lithium-ion EV batteries at its new plant in Georgia in 2022.
- SK Battery America will follow up with a second EV battery facility in 2023.
- Nicole Faulk serves as senior VP of customer strategy and solutions at Georgia Power.
