# Kumho Tire Co., Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-04-08

**Source:** [Who Makes Kumho Tires and Are They Worth Buying in 2026 - Tire Advise](https://tireadvise.com/who-makes-kumho-tires/)  ·  `page_056575`

## Facts

- Kumho Tire Co., Inc. is a major South Korean tire manufacturer founded in 1960 and headquartered in Seoul.
- Kumho operates manufacturing plants in South Korea, China, Vietnam, and the United States (Macon, Georgia).
- The Macon, Georgia facility opened in 2016 after a $200+ million investment.
- The Macon, Georgia plant employs hundreds of American workers.
- The Macon, Georgia plant primarily produces passenger car and light truck tires for the North American market.
- Kumho is an independent, publicly traded company that is NOT a subsidiary of any other tire company.
- In 2018, Chinese tire manufacturer Doublestar acquired a controlling stake (45%) in Kumho Tire.
- Kumho operates dedicated R&D centers in South Korea and maintains its own manufacturing standards and engineering teams.
- Kumho holds ISO 9001, ISO 14001, and IATF 16949 quality certifications.
- Kumho has earned OEM fitments from BMW, Mercedes-Benz, Hyundai/Kia, Ford, and Chrysler/Dodge.
- Kumho's popular tire lines include the Solus TA71 (Grand Touring All-Season), Crugen HP71 (SUV/Crossover Touring), Ecsta PA51 (Ultra-High Performance All-Season), and Road Venture AT52 (All-Terrain for Trucks/SUVs).
- The Solus TA71 comes with a 70,000-mile treadwear warranty.
- The Crugen HP71 carries a 65,000-mile warranty.
- Kumho tires sold in the US market fully comply with Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standards (FMVSS), specifically FMVSS 139 for new pneumatic radial tires.
- Consumers can verify the manufacturing origin of a specific tire by looking at the DOT code on the sidewall.
- Kumho is the second-largest tire maker in South Korea and ranks among the top 15 tire manufacturers globally.
