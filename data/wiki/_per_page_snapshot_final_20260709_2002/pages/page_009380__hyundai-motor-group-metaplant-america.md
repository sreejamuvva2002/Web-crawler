# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-06

**Source:** [Hyundai's $7.6B Georgia Plant Starts Electric SUV Production Amid Safety Concerns](https://www.latintimes.com/hyundais-76b-georgia-plant-starts-electric-suv-production-amid-safety-concerns-561561)  ·  `page_009380`

## Facts

- Hyundai started production of electric small utility vehicles (SUVs) at its $7.6 billion plant in Georgia.
- The automaker broke ground at the site in late October 2022, less than two years before production began.
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) announced that the first EV for commercial sale rolled off the assembly line.
- Spokesperson Bianca Johnson provided details to the Associated Press regarding the launch.
- HMGMA validated its production processes to ensure vehicles meet Hyundai Motor Group's high quality standards before starting initial customer vehicle production ahead of schedule.
- A grand opening celebration at the Georgia plant is expected in the first quarter of 2025.
- The sprawling Georgia facility, located west of Savannah, received $2.1 billion in tax rebates and incentives from state and local governments.
- This project is described as the biggest economic development Georgia has ever seen.
- Once fully functional, Hyundai and battery partner LG Energy Solution plan to employ 8,500 total workers at the Bryan County site.
- The Bryan County site is located about 50 miles west of Savannah.
- Hyundai plans to produce up to 300,000 EVs per year in Georgia.
- The plant will also produce the batteries that power these vehicles.
- Following the recent launch of the IONIQ 5, the Georgia facility is facing safety issues involving 20 injuries and 12 traumatic occurrences.
- The Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA) has issued fines and conducted investigations regarding the facility.
- OSHA's actions highlight a conflict between worker safety and economic growth at the site.
- The Ioniq 5 is the first electric SUV produced at the facility.
- 2025 variants of the Ioniq 5 are expected to hit U.S. showrooms by the end of 2024.
- In the first half of 2024, the Ioniq 5 was the second-best-selling electric car in America, excluding Tesla.
- The plant's vehicle production areas are currently completed and employ more than 1,000 workers.
- The battery-making facilities at the plant remain under construction.
- To be eligible for 2024 tax advantages, the U.S. government mandates that vehicles be fully constructed in North America.
- The federal mandate requires at least 60% of battery components to be supplied from the North American region.
- The goal of this directive is to decrease dependency on foreign suppliers and increase home production.
- Hyundai benefited from the Inflation Reduction Act in the United States.
- Despite early reservations, President Jose Munoz stated that Hyundai accelerated the construction of its Georgia factory due to the law's $7,500 EV tax credit.
