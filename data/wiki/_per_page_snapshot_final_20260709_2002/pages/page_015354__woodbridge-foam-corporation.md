# Woodbridge Foam Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Moving toward Carbon-Negative Automobiles: Incorporating Lignin in Automotive Parts on Commercial Scale : Centre for Research & Innovation in the Bio-economy](https://cribe.ca/projects/woodbridge-foam-lignin/)  ·  `page_015354`

## Facts

- Project Start Date is 01/12/2021.
- Project End Date is 31/03/2023.
- CRIBE Funding amount is $269,000.
- Project Total Value is $538,000.
- Consortium Members include Woodbridge Foam Corporation, Michigan State University (Prof. Mojgan Nejad), West Fraser, Fortum Bio2X, Sweetwater Energy Inc, and Ford Motor Company.
- The project aims to increase the commercial readiness of lignin-containing polymers by scaling up the production of lignin-based polyurethane (PU) flexible foams to move toward carbon-negative cars.
- The consortium targets achieving its goals by the end of May 2023.
- The project involves formulating liquid lignin-polyol using technical lignins produced by Fortum Bio2X (organosolv wheat straw), Sweetwater (hydrolysis hardwood), and West Fraser (kraft softwood).
- The formulated liquid lignin-polyol will be used to partially replace petroleum-based polyol in the formulation of PU flexible foam by Woodbridge Foam Corporation.
- Woodbridge Foam Corporation will scale up production of lignin-containing PU flexible foams and test them in the manufacturing line of Ford Motor Company.
- Project outcomes will be communicated via conferences, technical reports, journal papers, industry meetings, and social media.
