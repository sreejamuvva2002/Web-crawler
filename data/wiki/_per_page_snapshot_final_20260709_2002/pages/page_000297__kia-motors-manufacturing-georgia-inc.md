# Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia, Inc.

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-02-24

**Source:** [News - Kia Georgia](https://www.kiageorgia.com/stories/)  ·  `page_000297`

## Facts

- VPS, in partnership with Kia Georgia, completed a large-scale hail protection and solar canopy system at Kia’s West Point manufacturing facility.
- The hail protection system covers 3.2 million square feet of automotive protection.
- The solar canopy system provides 10 MW of solar capacity to strengthen resilience and sustainability at the plant.
- On Feb. 24, 2026, Kia Georgia team members marked the start of production of the all-new 2027 Kia Telluride at its West Point facility.
- The ceremonial 2027 Telluride vehicle represented the plant’s 5 millionth vehicle produced.
- The 2027 Telluride is a hybrid electric vehicle (HEV), marking Georgia’s first-ever HEV production at the facility.
- Following a $217 million expansion, Kia Georgia began assembling the all-electric 2025 EV6 SUV.
- The EV6 is the plant’s second electric vehicle and fifth overall vehicle currently being assembled in West Point, Georgia.
- The announcement regarding the EV6 assembly was made at the 2024 Los Angeles Auto Show on Nov. 21, 2024.
- Kia Georgia began production on Nov. 16, 2009.
- Since beginning operations, Kia Georgia has assembled more than 4.5 million vehicles.
- Kia Georgia has helped create more than 14,000 jobs in the region.
- On Nov. 20, 2024, Kia Georgia commemorated 15 years of manufacturing in Georgia.
- The 2025 Kia EV9 is the first electric vehicle assembled in the state of Georgia.
- The EV9 joins the Telluride, Sorento, and Sportage SUVs currently assembled at Kia’s West Point facility.
- On May 30, 2024, Kia Georgia celebrated a historic milestone at its West Point assembly plant with the introduction of the EV9.
- The Sportage became the fourth model produced by Kia Georgia, joining the Telluride, Sorento, and K5 to expand the plant’s product lineup.
- On Feb. 1, 2022, Kia Georgia officially began production of the all-new 2023 Sportage subcompact SUV at its West Point facility.
- On Dec. 17, 2021, Kia Georgia announced the retirement of President & CEO Jangsoo (Jason) Shin, effective Dec. 31, 2021.
- Shin led the Georgia facility since 2017 and oversaw the inaugural launches of the Telluride and K5 models.
- On Aug. 20, 2021, Kia Georgia reopened its online application process for hourly production positions.
- This reopening marked the first time direct hire hourly production positions were available since 2011.
