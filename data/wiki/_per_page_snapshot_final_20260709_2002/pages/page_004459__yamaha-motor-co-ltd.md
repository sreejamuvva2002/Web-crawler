# Yamaha Motor Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-03-10

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp: Yamaha to Move HQ to Georgia | Governor Brian P. Kemp Office of the Governor](https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases/2026-03-10/gov-kemp-yamaha-move-hq-georgia)  ·  `page_004459`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced on March 10, 2026, that Yamaha Motor Co., Ltd. has officially decided to move its U.S. headquarters to Kennesaw, Georgia.
- The relocation decision follows a meeting between Governor Kemp and Yamaha executives in October of last year at the Southeast U.S./Japan (SEUS-Japan) annual gathering in Tokyo.
- Yamaha is relocating out of California following roughly 50 years of operation there.
- The relocation of the Yamaha Motor Corp. U.S. headquarters will begin this year and is expected to continue through 2028.
- Yamaha Motor Co., Ltd. manufactures ATVs, boat engines, personal watercraft, and other motorized products.
- The company currently employs over 2,300 Georgians across various facilities in the state.
- The 1.3-million-square-foot Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation of America factory in Newnan opened in 1986 and currently employs roughly 2,000 Georgians.
- Yamaha relocated its marine division to Kennesaw in 1999.
- The company expanded the Kennesaw facility with a 75,000-square-foot Marine Innovation Center in 2023.
- In 2019, the company moved its motorsports operations to Georgia to benefit from close proximity to the existing facilities.
- Governor Brian Kemp called Georgia the "No. 1 state for business" and welcomed Yamaha's American headquarters as a testament to what the state offers job creators.
- Governor Kemp addressed other California-based companies looking for a better home, stating he would give them plenty of reasons to keep Georgia on their mind.
- Yamaha Motor Co., Ltd. is a world-leading producer of motorcycles, marine products, power products, and robotics.
- The company was founded in 1955 and is headquartered in Iwata, Shizuoka, Japan.
- The company operates under the corporate mission of being a "Kando Creating Company," aiming to offer new excitement and a more fulfilling life for people all over the world.
