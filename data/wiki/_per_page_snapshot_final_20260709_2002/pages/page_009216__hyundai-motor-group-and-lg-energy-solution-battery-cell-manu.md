# Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution Battery Cell Manufacturing Joint Venture

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-05-26

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution to Establish Battery Cell Manufacturing Joint Venture in the U.S. - HMGMA](https://www.hmgma.com/2023/05/26/hyundai-motor-group-and-lg-energy-solution-to-establish-battery-cell-manufacturing-joint-venture-in-the-u-s/)  ·  `page_009216`

## Facts

- The new battery cell joint venture will be located in Bryan County, Savannah, Georgia, adjacent to Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America which is currently under construction.
- The joint venture involves a total investment of over USD 4.3 billion (KRW 5.7 trillion).
- Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution will each hold a 50 percent stake in the joint venture.
- Battery production at the new facility is aimed to start at the end of 2025 at the earliest.
- The joint venture has an annual production capacity of 30 GWh, capable of supporting the production of 300,000 EVs annually.
- Construction of the joint venture facility is scheduled to start in the second half of 2023.
- Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution announced the EV battery cell manufacturing joint venture on May 26, 2023.
- The parties signed a memorandum of understanding to produce EV batteries in the U.S. and accelerate electrification efforts in North America.
- The signing ceremony took place at LG Energy Solution’s headquarters in Seoul on May 26.
- Jaehoon Chang, President and CEO of Hyundai Motor Company, attended the signing ceremony.
- Youngsoo Kwon, CEO of LG Energy Solution, attended the signing ceremony.
- Hyundai Motor Group stated it is focusing on electrification efforts to secure a leadership position in the global auto industry.
- Jaehoon Chang stated the group will create a strong foundation to lead the global EV transition through establishing the new battery cell plant with LG Energy Solution.
- Youngsoo Kwon stated that LG Energy Solution will commit its best efforts to offering ultimate sustainable energy solutions by advancing product competitiveness and global operational expertise.
- Hyundai Mobis will assemble battery packs using cells from the plant and supply them to the Group’s U.S. manufacturing facilities for Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis EV models.
- The new facility aims to create a stable regional battery supply and allow the group to respond quickly to soaring U.S. EV demand.
- With this joint venture, LG Energy Solution now has seven battery plants operating or under construction in the U.S.
- LG Energy Solution is concentrating most of its resources on expanding production capacity in the U.S. to expedite the clean energy transition.
- Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution have previously supplied EV batteries for vehicles including the Elantra Hybrid, Kona Electric, and IONIQ 6.
- The Hyundai Elantra Hybrid, an LPi hybrid vehicle introduced in 2009, was the group’s first electrified model.
- In 2021, Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution started construction of a battery cell joint venture in Indonesia set to begin production in the first half of 2024.
- This facility marks the second battery manufacturing joint venture between the group and LG Energy Solution as they aim to strengthen their long-time partnership in electrification.
