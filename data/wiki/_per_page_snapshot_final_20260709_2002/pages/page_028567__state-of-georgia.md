## Facts

- Overall cost of living remains below the national average.
- Housing is more attainable in many communities across the state.
- Georgia’s economy supports a wide range of careers and industries through a strong workforce ecosystem with universities, technical colleges, and training programs helping people build skills and advance their careers statewide.
- Through the Georgia Center of Innovation, public and private partners collaborate to develop key industries including advanced manufacturing, logistics, aerospace, fintech, and AgTech.
- Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport, the world’s busiest airport, places more than 80% of the U.S. within a two-hour flight.
- A strong highway and rail network keeps people and goods moving statewide.
- Through a statewide network of colleges and technical institutions, residents can pursue degrees, credentials, and hands-on training across urban and rural campuses.
- Programs such as the HOPE and Zell Miller Scholarships make higher education more affordable.
- Dual enrollment, apprenticeships, and workforce grants offer flexible pathways for students and adult learners to advance without leaving their communities.
