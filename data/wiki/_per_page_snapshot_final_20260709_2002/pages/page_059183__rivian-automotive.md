# Rivian Automotive

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-06-29

**Source:** [Rivian Automotive delays its goal of having over 600 sites by the end of 2023 | EV](https://eletric-vehicles.com/rivian/rivian-automotive-delays-its-goal-of-having-over-600-sites-by-the-end-of-2023/)  ·  `page_059183`

## Facts

- The US electric vehicle maker Rivian announced it opened three fast charging sites in Colorado and California this week, marking the first deployments of its nationwide Rivian Adventure Network.
- In March 2021, Rivian stated its ambition for the Adventure Network was to have over 3,500 fast chargers at more than 600 sites by the end of 2023.
- With first sites launching in mid-June, Rivian would need to build 597 sites with more than 3,400 fast chargers in 18 months to meet its initial goal.
- Rivian deleted the end of 2023 goal from its official website, giving no exact timeframe for the Adventure Network plan.
- The Level 3 DC fast-charging sites will initially provide over 200 kilowatts of power.
- The chargers allow Rivian drivers to add as much as 140 miles of range in just 20 minutes.
- The first Rivian Adventure Network site in Salida, Colorado opened to the public on Monday, June 27 with four chargers.
- The Salida site includes a pull-through charger to accommodate vehicles towing trailers.
- The Salida site also hosts four Level 2 Rivian Waypoints chargers available for use by any EV through the Rivian smartphone app.
- The second and third Adventure Network sites in Inyokern and Bishop, California will open on June 28 and 29.
- The California sites support areas like Yosemite National Park, Sequoia National Forest, Mammoth Lakes, and Death Valley National Park.
- Trent Warnke, Rivian’s Senior Director of Energy and Charging Solutions, stated that the charging was designed to support electrified adventure and enable drivers to reach natural spaces responsibly.
- Warnke noted the fast charging rollout is designed to ensure travelers have places to charge along major transportation corridors coast to coast.
- In mid-June, Rivian unveiled plans for a large-scale wind turbine at its Normal manufacturing campus to provide clean energy for new R1 vehicles.
- Earlier this year, Rivian installed a 783-kilowatt solar canopy scheduled to begin generating electricity later that summer.
- The wind project aligns with Rivian’s strategy of adding renewable resources to parts of the country that need them most.
- Illinois’ grid is powered by 11% renewables and aims to reach net-zero carbon emissions by 2040, 10 years ahead of the Paris Agreement, according to the company.
- Rivian Automotive Field Specialist Patrick Sarsgard announced the opening of a new service center in Boston.
- Sarsgard stated that the U.S. electric vehicle maker is actively delivering on the East Coast.
- During Q1 2022 Earnings, Rivian reiterated its forecast to produce 25,000 vehicles in 2022.
