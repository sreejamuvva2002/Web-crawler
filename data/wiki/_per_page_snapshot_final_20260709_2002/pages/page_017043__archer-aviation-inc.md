# Archer Aviation Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-11-17

**Source:** [HeliHub.com : Archer Selects Site in Georgia for Manufacturing Facility - HeliHub.com](https://www.helihub.com/2022/11/17/archer-selects-site-in-georgia-for-manufacturing-facility/)  ·  `page_017043`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation Inc. (NYSE: ACHR) announced plans to locate its manufacturing facility in Covington, Georgia at a site adjacent to the Covington Municipal Airport.
- Archer plans to initially build out a 350,000 square-foot facility on a 96-acre site.
- The planned eVTOL aircraft manufacturing facility will be capable of producing up to 650 aircraft per year.
- The facility is expected to create over 1,000 jobs over the long term.
- Construction is expected to be completed in the first half of 2024.
- Initial production is expected to begin in the second half of 2024.
- The facility will be capable of being expanded by an additional 550,000 square feet.
- The expansion is estimated to support production of up to 2,300 aircraft per year.
- Archer completed a comprehensive survey of potential sites across the United States to select the location for its first manufacturing facility.
- Key considerations for site selection included the availability of talent in the local labor market, utility availability, ability to conduct seamless flight test operations, construction costs, and logistics.
- Archer will receive an incentive package that includes land conveyance, tax incentives, and a Georgia REBA grant.
- Archer anticipates that financing for the project will be provided by Synovus, the largest bank headquartered in the state.
- Adam Goldstein serves as Archer’s CEO.
- Adam Goldstein stated, “After many months of discussion and evaluation, we’re excited to select Georgia as the location for our manufacturing facility.”
- Adam Goldstein noted that the U.S. has long led the world in aerospace technology and Georgia has played a vital role in that industry.
- Adam Goldstein said Archer’s eVTOL technology can transform how urban and rural communities live and commute, and the factory can create pathways to highly skilled manufacturing jobs and other ladders of social and economic opportunity.
- Adam Goldstein expressed gratitude to Governor Kemp along with Bob Kosek, Sara Gershon, Serra Hall, Frank Turner, and their teams for their work on the project.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp commented on the decision to locate the facility in Georgia.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated, “Georgia has made nurturing innovation a priority as we work with businesses to define the future.”
- Governor Brian P. Kemp added, “Our established leadership in the aerospace industry was essential to landing Archer Aviation in Newton County.”
- Archer can take advantage of Georgia's signature workforce training program Quick Start to help fill over 1,000 jobs in Covington in the long term.
- Georgia is home to more than 800 aerospace companies and maintains a robust aerospace education pipeline.
- The local education pipeline includes a dozen high schools that offer training in the field, five technical colleges with aviation programs, and several universities offering degrees in aerospace engineering.
- The Georgia Center of Innovation for Aerospace is a strategic arm of the Georgia Department of Economic Development.
- The Georgia Center of Innovation for Aerospace has been cultivating an ecosystem of opportunities for innovative companies like Archer for more than 10 years.
