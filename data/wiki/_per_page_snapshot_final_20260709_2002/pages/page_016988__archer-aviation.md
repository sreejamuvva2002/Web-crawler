# Archer Aviation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-03-26

**Source:** [Should You Buy Archer Aviation While It's Below $12.47? | The Motley Fool](https://www.fool.com/investing/2025/03/26/should-you-buy-archer-aviation-while-its-below-124/)  ·  `page_016988`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation expects to deploy a fleet of its "Midnight" eVTOL to an international customer later this year.
- The company anticipates certification by the Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) in the U.S.
- Shares have soared more than 160% in the past six months but are down 35% from their 52-week high of $12.47 reached on Jan. 7.
- The four-passenger Midnight eVTOL can travel up to 150 miles per hour for short-range commuter trips, flying above traffic congestion.
- Archer envisions a worldwide network supporting thousands of its aircraft operating as an air taxi service.
- Pricing is expected to eventually become competitive with ground-based transportation.
- The company intends to sell the aircraft directly, including through deals with United Airlines and Southwest Airlines.
- Industry experts estimate the urban air mobility market could be a $1 trillion opportunity by 2040.
- Archer recently completed a dedicated manufacturing facility in Georgia.
- The company plans to build 10 Midnight aircraft this year at the Georgia site.
- The Georgia site was developed in partnership with automobile giant Stellantis.
- The facility is intended to scale to an annual production rate of 650 aircraft by 2030.
- Archer expects to begin generating revenue by Q4 from the initial batch of Midnight eVTOLs destined for its first customer, Abu Dhabi Aviation (ADA) in the United Arab Emirates.
- The company has completed more than 400 successful test flights.
- Archer will use "Launch Edition" models as part of its final FAA "Type Certification," demonstrating aircraft safety and performance in real-world conditions.
- Archer announced a partnership with Anduril Industries to jointly develop a hybrid-propulsion aircraft for military applications.
- The partnership leverages Anduril's expertise in advanced technology, including artificial intelligence (AI) and automation, to target U.S. Department of Defense contracts.
- Archer has more than $1.1 billion in total liquidity.
- Key upcoming catalysts include the first Midnight rolling off the assembly line, a piloted test flight, commercial delivery, and possibly an FAA announcement.
- A logistical bottleneck for Archer is the need to train and hire possibly hundreds of pilots on the new platform.
- From a net loss of $570 million in 2024, analysts project only $29 million in revenue this year from initial Midnight sales.
- Revenue is projected to ramp up to $151 million in 2026.
- Archer currently has a market value of $4.6 billion.
- Profitability is expected to remain elusive into the next decade.
- The company faces increasing competition from Joby Aviation, China-based EHang, and several private companies developing alternative eVTOL aircraft.
