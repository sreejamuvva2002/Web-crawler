# Astemo Americas, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2025-06-09

**Source:** [Changes Are Underway At Astemo Americas - A Leading Auto Supplier](https://menafn.com/1109652302/Changes-Are-Underway-At-Astemo-Americas-A-Leading-Auto-Supplier)  ·  `page_008449`

## Facts

- Astemo Americas is introducing new mobility technology to its North American customers, expanding its footprint in the Americas and making name and branding changes.
- The company has 80,000 employees worldwide, including more than 40 offices and manufacturing sites with more than 14,000 employees in the Americas.
- Astemo Americas CEO and President Tim Clark states the company aims to strengthen its position as a leader in electrification and mobility intelligence as the industry moves into the software-defined vehicle age.
- The company is accelerating investment in advanced mobility, including electrification, advanced driver-assistance systems (ADAS), autonomous driving, and advanced chassis technology.
- Astemo is showcasing next-generation autonomous driving, EV, chassis, suspension, steering, and brake technologies to major OEM customers in the Americas this summer.
- Technologies on display include 3D sensing-and-control, semi-active suspension, steer-by-wire, and brake-by-wire for use on future new-model vehicles sold in the Americas.
- Astemo Americas Vice President for Advanced Engineering Harsha Badarinarayan notes that advanced AI-based multi-camera systems are gaining wide acceptance among automakers for future AD/ADAS applications.
- Astemo is developing automated driving solutions using proprietary AI-based stereo vision technology that generates a 360° denser point cloud than conventional LiDAR along with color and brightness information.
- The company is showcasing a full suspension system with AI-powered control logic using deep-learning algorithms that delivers ride-and-handling improvements at a lower cost compared to similar systems today.
- The showcased suspension system includes a "preview" camera to prepare for bumps and potholes in the road ahead.
- Additional suspension technologies include a performance series damper system focused on Off-Road vehicles and a Virtual Development Lab to support customers in eliminating physical prototypes.
- Astemo's latest generation of steer-by-wire technology features a hand-held device designed to replace a vehicle's steering wheel as part of the Smart Steer-by-Wire System.
- The company is showing its electro-mechanical brake-by-wire system, Smart Brake, for the first time in North America, which reduces lag-time between driver input and brake response.
- Smart Brake's e-pedal eliminates the traditional hydraulic connection between the brake pedal and vehicle brakes, simplifying vehicle assembly, extending battery range, and reducing maintenance by eliminating brake fluid use.
- Astemo's Smart Brake project is financially supported by the French Government as part of the France 2030 investment plan.
- The company was formerly known as Hitachi Astemo Americas but has officially changed its name to Astemo Americas following a corporate headquarters name change from Hitachi Astemo, Ltd. to Astemo Ltd.
- The new branding message is "Astemo, Mobility Beyond – From the Core of Mobility, Freedom," featured on the global website and marketing materials.
- The name Astemo is derived from "Advanced Sustainable Technologies for Mobility," underscoring a focus on cutting-edge, sustainable mobility solutions.
- Astemo Americas CEO Tim Clark notes the company has outgrown its technical center and headquarters in Farmington Hills, Michigan, and plans to expand to a new location in the near future.
- Astemo is a joint venture between Hitachi, Ltd., Honda Motor Co., and JIC Capital Ltd., headquartered in Tokyo, Japan.
- The company operates Electric Business Division, Vehicle Business Division, and Motorcycle Business Divisions engaged in development, manufacture, sales, and service of automotive parts and transportation/industrial machinery systems.
- Astemo Americas manufactures and markets engine management, electric powertrain, and integrated vehicle controls for major automotive manufacturers worldwide, adhering to ISO/TS:16949:2009 industry standards.
