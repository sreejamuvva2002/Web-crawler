## Key facts

- Electric vehicle adoption in Georgia is expected to accelerate over the next three years as automakers develop factories in the state.
- The expert emphasizes that it is not a question of if EVs will arrive in Georgia, but how quickly they will be produced and adopted.
- Manufacturing or assembly facilities naturally attract supplier ecosystems, fostering broader supply-chain development.
- Companies such as FedEx and UPS are anticipated to transition to electric fleets, which would further stimulate charging infrastructure growth.
- Georgia already hosts a battery manufacturer, providing an existing network for the automotive industry.
- Increased charging station deployment is expected before a significant rise in electric vehicle traffic on Georgia highways.
- Bill 406 enables kilowatt-hour based billing for EV charging and unlocks $135 million in federal funding for public chargers.
- The state initiative aims to place public charging stations at multiple parks and historic sites, expanding access for travelers.
- Federal policy commits the U.S. government fleet to zero-emission vehicles by 2027 (light-duty) and 2035 (heavy-duty).
- Currently, electric vehicles represent less than 1 % of all vehicles on Georgia roads.
