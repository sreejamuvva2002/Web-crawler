# Ascend Elements

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2026-03-27

**Source:** [Team AE, Author at Ascend Elements](https://ascendelements.com/author/aronkerr/)  ·  `page_052583`

## Facts

- Ascend Elements achieved a milestone producing >99% pure, recycled lithium carbonate (Li2CO3) from black mass for the first time at commercial scale at its Covington, Ga. facility on September 3, 2025.
- The company plans to produce more than 15 Kilotons of Recycled Lithium Carbonate annually in the United States and Europe by 2027.
- On December 10, 2024, Ascend Elements announced it would increase U.S. production of lithium carbonate by 60% with the start of a new lithium recovery line at its Covington, Ga. facility in 2025.
- The new lithium recovery line at the Covington, Ga. facility will produce up to 3,000 metric tons of lithium carbonate (Li2CO3) annually.
- The Covington, Ga. EV battery recycling facility was certified to ISO 9001:2015 Quality Management Standard by DQS Inc.
- Base 1 is Ascend Elements’ first commercial-scale EV battery recycling facility located in Covington, Georgia.
- When fully operational in Q4 2022, Base 1 will recycle 30,000 metric tons of lithium-ion batteries and manufacturing scrap a year, equivalent to about 70,000 electric vehicle batteries per year.
- The Covington, Ga. facility was planned to open by August 2022 as a 154,000-square-foot commercial-scale lithium-ion battery recycling facility with capacity to process 30,000 metric tons annually.
- Ascend Elements will recycle lithium-ion battery manufacturing scrap for SK Battery America at its Covington, Georgia facility, recovering 98% of battery materials and returning critical elements to the supply chain.
- The company plans to invest up to $1 billion to build a sustainable lithium-ion battery materials facility in Hopkinsville, Ky.
- Ascend Elements began construction of its largest EV battery recycling and engineered materials manufacturing facility (Apex 1) in southwestern Kentucky with a ceremonial groundbreaking attended by Gov. Andy Beshear.
- The Apex 1 campus is designed as a 150-acre site for America’s first commercial-scale pCAM manufacturing plant.
- Ascend Elements launched a logistics simulation at its Apex 1 facility currently under construction in Hopkinsville, Kentucky, selecting EY US to develop the complex model.
- A separate $316M U.S. DOE grant to Ascend Elements for pCAM infrastructure remains active to help fund construction of the Apex 1 project in Hopkinsville, KY.
- The company was awarded $480M in grants from the U.S. Department of Energy’s Bipartisan Infrastructure Law Battery Materials Processing and Battery Manufacturing Initiative.
- A separate $164M DOE grant for Cathode Active Material (CAM) was mutually canceled due to changing market conditions.
- Ascend Elements secured an up to $5 billion deal to supply sustainable cathode precursor (pCAM) made from recycled battery materials to a major U.S. customer while building one of North America’s first commercial-scale NMC pCAM manufacturing facilities in southwest Kentucky.
- The company's Hydro-to-Cathode™ technology recovers 98% of critical elements in used lithium-ion batteries and uses microstructure engineering techniques to adjust the elemental crystal structure of new cathode material to precise customer specifications.
- An independently reviewed Life Cycle Assessment found Ascend Elements’ Hydro-to-Cathode® direct pCAM synthesis process produces 49% lower carbon dioxide equivalent (CO2e) emissions than traditional battery recycling methods.
- The company announced a multi-year offtake agreement with Trafigura to pair its Hydro-to-Cathode® technology with Trafigura’s global marketing and logistics platform to accelerate a resilient battery materials supply chain.
- Ascend Elements and Koura (Orbia’s Fluorinated Solutions business) agreed to explore joint commercialization of the patented Hydro-to-Anode® graphite recycling technology, which produces 99.9% pure graphite from used lithium-ion batteries.
- The company plans to supply up to 5,000 metric tons of recycled lithium carbonate per year to Koura Global for new electric vehicle batteries.
- Ascend Elements and EcoPro Group announced an MOU to cooperate in the development, qualification, and integration of recycled battery materials into EcoPro’s cathode products, paving the way for a sustainable, closed-loop supply of domestic lithium-ion battery materials in the United States.
- The company shipped decarbonized cathode materials to Freudenberg e-Power Systems, a leading supplier of emissions-neutral energy solutions for heavy-duty applications.
- Ascend Elements produces premium cathode active materials for Navitas Systems, a U.S. leader in comprehensive energy storage solutions and a U.S. Department of Defense contractor, under a contract signed on January 31, 2022.
- The company formalized an agreement with Call2Recycle to offer customized EV battery management, logistics, and recycling services to automotive dealerships, recyclers, and repair facilities.
- Ascend Elements signed an agreement with Honda to recycle Honda & Acura Electric Vehicles (EV) batteries at its Worcester, Mass. facility.
