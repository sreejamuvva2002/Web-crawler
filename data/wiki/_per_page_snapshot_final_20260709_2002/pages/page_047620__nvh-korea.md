## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that automotive parts manufacturer NVH Korea will establish a new facility in Locust Grove.
- The project will create over 160 new jobs.
- The investment totals $72 million for Henry County.
- This is the second economic development project Governor Kemp has announced during his trip to the Paris Air Show.
- NVH Korea is an automotive parts manufacturer based in Ulsan, South Korea, established in 1984.
- The company specializes in automotive noise, vibration, and heat-control parts.
- NVH Korea currently produces its parts in Korea, Europe, and India.
- The company already operates a manufacturing facility in Georgia that produces floor mats, cargo mats, and cargo trays.
- The new facility will supply battery parts for electric vehicles (EVs) to manufacturers including Hyundai Mobis, Kia Georgia, and Hyundai Motor Group.
- Ja Kyum Koo serves as Chairman and CEO of NVH Korea.
- NVH Korea selected Georgia as its first EV battery component manufacturing site.
- The new facility will be located at Gardner Logistics Park on Colvin Drive in Locust Grove.
- The facility will produce EV battery system components that protect, connect, and sense the performance of battery cells.
- Operations at the new facility are expected to begin in the second quarter of 2024.
- Mayor Robert Price of Locust Grove stated the investment will increase the local manufacturing jobs base and integrate the city into Georgia's electric vehicle ecosystem.
- Chair Carlotta Harrell of the Henry County Board of Commissioners noted the project will add quality manufacturing jobs for citizens and diversify the local economy.
- Jacob Lee, Project Manager for the Georgia Department of Economic Development's (GDEcD) Global Commerce team, represented the agency on this competitive project.
- The project received assistance from the Henry County Development Authority, Georgia EMC, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson commented that the investment enriches the local industry mix and provides well-paying jobs for Georgians.
- Since 2020, over $22 billion has been invested into electric vehicle production in Georgia.
- This investment has resulted in over 28 thousand jobs created across the state.
- GDEcD data from July 1 through December 31, 2022 shows the automotive industry was the second largest source for new jobs and investments during that period.
- Georgia is positioning itself as a leader in developing and harnessing emerging technologies for the evolving automotive and mobility industry.
