# Choose Columbus / Columbus Economic Development Region

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-03-10

**Source:** [Automotive](https://www.choosecolumbusga.com/site-selectors/target-industries/automotive/)  ·  `page_019073`

## Facts

- Columbus, Georgia is positioned at the center of the automotive industry’s growth in the Southeastern U.S., with the regional corridor including Huntsville, Alabama, and Savannah, Georgia.
- Columbus’ geographical proximity to Kia’s only U.S. automotive plant provides a strategic advantage for suppliers like Daesol Ausys.
- The region offers a skilled automotive workforce, strategic location near auto manufacturers, a mild climate, a supportive business environment, and affordable energy.
- Automotive companies located within 30 minutes of Columbus include Kia Motors Manufacturing, Daesol Ausys, Hyundai Mobis Alabama, Hyundai Dymos, Hyundai Transys, and Glovis Georgia.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp stated that the state’s automotive industry employs over 50,000 Georgians and will continue to grow as e-mobility suppliers expand across the state.
- Columbus provides speed-to-market through GRAD-certified, shovel-ready sites and an expedited permitting process.
- Local stakeholders including the Chamber, local and state government, utility providers, and higher education partners collaborate to streamline site selection, secure available financial incentives, accelerate approvals, and connect businesses with a skilled workforce.
- At the Regional Workforce Summit on March 13, 2026, Columbus Technical College President Martha Ann Tood highlighted the college's role in regional workforce development and its delivery of high-demand credentialing programs.
- Columbus Technical College partnered with Pratt & Whitney (an RTX Company) to create an aviation maintenance training program in Columbus.
- Pratt & Whitney employs approximately 25% veterans in its Columbus workforce and leverages the U.S. Department of War's Skillbridge program for talent acquisition.
- Alan Durhan, an economic developer at Georgia Tech University’s Enterprise Innovation Institute, led a multi-year study of the housing market in the Columbus AL-GA Metropolitan Statistical Area to identify gaps and recommend strategies for regional economic success.
- Local efforts are underway to improve the availability of quality, affordable childcare for employees, presented by Jennifer St. John (Senior Vice President, United Way of the Chattahoochee Valley) and Cindy Spann (Executive Director, Child Care Resource and Referral of SW Georgia).
- Columbus State University President Dr. Stuart Rayfield outlined the university's "Better Together: 2030" strategic plan to continuously transform education and deliver the workforce employers need.
- The Regional Workforce Summit featured Lightning Talks with educators and workforce leaders including Dr. Clarence Anthony (Partnership for Innovation), Dr. Victoria Thomas (Muscogee County Schools), Casie Williams (Aflac), and B. Ryan Willougby (Jordan Vocational High School College & Career Academy).
- The Georgia Association of Manufacturers launched the "Manufacturing Georgia - Make Your Way" program, a web-based platform digitally connecting people of all ages to manufacturing employers, presented by Vice President Stephanie Scearce.
- Columbus maintains a pro-business, pro-growth stance with fast speed-to-market, GRAD-certified sites, and strong supply chain support for automotive and other industries.
