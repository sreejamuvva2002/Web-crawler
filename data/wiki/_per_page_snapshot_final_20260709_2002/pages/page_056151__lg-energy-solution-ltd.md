# LG Energy Solution Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2020-12-01

**Source:** [LG Energy Solution - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/LG_Energy_Solution)  ·  `page_056151`

## Facts

- LG Energy Solution Ltd. is a public subsidiary headquartered in Seoul, South Korea, with KRX ticker 373220.
- The company was founded on December 1, 2020, after LG Chem announced the spin-off of its battery business in September 2020 to cope with growing global automotive demand.
- LGES is one of the largest battery makers in the world, producing lithium-ion batteries for electric vehicles and energy storage systems (ESS).
- Its parent company holds an 81% ownership stake.
- Kwon Young-soo serves as chairman.
- LG Chem began its battery business in 1992 after chairman Koo Bon-moo visited the United Kingdom Atomic Energy Authority and brought back rechargeable battery samples for research.
- LG Chem produced Korea's first lithium-ion battery in 1999.
- In the late 2000s, LG began supplying automotive batteries for the Chevrolet Volt produced by General Motors.
- The company later became a battery supplier to global automakers including Ford, Chrysler, Audi, Renault, Volvo, and SAIC Motor.
- LGES undertook an IPO process to secure funds for increased battery production capacity and debuted on the Korea Exchange in January 2022, marking South Korea's biggest IPO at that time.
- In the first half of 2025, LGES won three injunctions against Sunwoda for patent infringement.
- On September 4, 2025, United States Immigration and Customs Enforcement detained LG employees and contractors during a raid of Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- LG Energy Solution established Ultium Cells LLC as a joint venture with General Motors in December 2019, with a 50% ownership share and $2.3 billion initial investment.
- The company formed NextStar Energy as a joint venture with Stellantis in March 2022, holding a 51% share with a $4.1 billion initial investment, scheduled to dissolve in February 2026.
- LGES partnered with Honda to establish L-H Battery Company in January 2023, with a 51% ownership share and $3.5 billion initial investment.
- A joint venture named HL-GA BATTERY COMPANY was established with Hyundai Motor Company in May 2023, featuring a 50% ownership share and $4.3 billion initial investment.
- LG-HY BCM was established as a joint venture with Zhejiang Huayou Recycling Technology and Toyota Tsusho in August 2023.
- A battery recycling joint venture was set up with Derichebourg in April 2025, with LG holding a 50% share.
- Green Metals Battery Innovations was established as a joint venture with Toyota Tsusho in June 2025, with LG holding a 49% share.
- LG Energy Solution signed a long-term battery supply agreement with Toyota in October 2023 valued at approximately $3 billion, with supply starting in 2025 for a 20-year duration.
- A supply agreement was signed with Ampere in July 2024 to deliver 39 GWh over five years, starting in 2025.
- LGES agreed to supply Aptera Motors with W730b cylindrical cells under a January 2025 deal worth $499.2 million, spanning from 2025 to 2031 for 4.4 GWh.
- A supply agreement was signed with O&J Automotive Netherlands in June 2025 for 8 GWh over six years starting in 2026, with an estimated deal value of 1 trillion won ($732 million).
- LGES secured a $4.3 billion storage battery supply deal with Tesla in July 2025, with deliveries scheduled from 2026 to 2030.
- Ford Motor Company and LG Energy Solution had a supply agreement starting in October 2024 for 109 GWh over 4-6 years beginning in 2026, but the deal was terminated in December 2025.
- Mercedes-Benz Group signed a 10-year battery supply deal with LGES in October 2024 starting in 2028 for 50.5 GWh.
- In September 2025, LGES deepened ties with Mercedes-Benz with additional deals starting in 2028 through 2035 for 32 GWh, and another starting in 2029 through 2037 for 75 GWh.
- A December 2025 agreement with Mercedes-Benz is valued at $1.4 billion, covering supply from 2028 to 2035.
- General Motors reported that manufacturing defects in batteries supplied by LGES caused 13 confirmed battery fires in Chevrolet Bolt vehicles.
- The faulty batteries were produced at LGES plants in South Korea and Michigan, prompting GM to pursue reimbursement.
- LGES and LG Electronics agreed to pay GM up to $1.9 billion for recalls on every Chevrolet Bolt car produced since 2016.
- LG Energy Solution plans to buy GM's stake in its Michigan battery plant for $2 billion as of April 2025.
- LG Energy Solution resumed business trips to the US following worker detentions at a Georgia Hyundai plant in October 2025.
