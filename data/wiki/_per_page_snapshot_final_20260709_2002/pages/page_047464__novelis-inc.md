# Novelis Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2022-08-04

**Source:** [Novelis sees growth, constraints in Q1 financial report - Recycling Today](https://www.recyclingtoday.com/news/novelis-releases-first-quarter-financial-report/)  ·  `page_047464`

## Facts

- Reported financial results for the first quarter of its 2023 fiscal year, noting net income and net sales increased while shipments decreased slightly.
- President and CEO Steve Fisher stated the company delivered record financial performance in the first quarter following last fiscal year's record performance.
- Net income attributable to common shareholders increased 28 percent compared with a record $307 million in the previous year.
- Net income from continuing operations increased 1 percent to $307 million.
- Excluding special items, net income from continuing operations increased 18 percent compared with the previous year, largely due to higher underlying adjusted EBITDA, unrealized derivative gains, and a lower tax provision in the current year, partially offset by a tax litigation gain benefiting prior year net income.
- Net sales increased 32 percent to $5.1 billion for the first quarter of fiscal year 2023 compared with $3.9 billion in the prior-year period, primarily driven by higher average aluminum prices and local market premiums.
- Total flat-rolled product shipments were 962,000 metric tons, 1 percent lower than prior-year shipments of 973,000 metric tons, mainly owing to supply chain constraints.
- Adjusted EBITDA increased 1 percent to a record $561 million in the first quarter of fiscal 2023 compared with $555 million in the prior-year period, which included a $47 million gain related to a favorable decision in a Brazilian tax litigation.
- The underlying increase in adjusted EBITDA is primarily from higher product pricing, including some higher cost pass-through to customers, favorable product mix on improved automotive and aerospace shipments, and lower metal costs arising from improved recycling performance, partially offset by high-cost inflation and unfavorable foreign exchange translation.
- Adjusted free cash flow from continuing operations was an outflow of $72 million in the first quarter of fiscal 2023, higher than the $30 million the previous year, primarily because of a less favorable metal price lag.
- The company reported a net leverage ratio of 2.2x at the end of the first quarter of fiscal year 2023 compared with 2.5x in the prior year period.
- Executive vice president and chief financial officer Devinder Ahuja stated that in a strong demand but capacity-constrained environment, the company continues to focus on delivering high-quality, high-recycled-content products to customers while optimizing operations and portfolio.
- The company is vigorously allocating capital to grow alongside its customers, with more than $4.5 billion of investment opportunities on its horizon.
- Earlier this year, Novelis broke ground on a $365 million investment in a highly advanced recycling center in Guthrie, Kentucky.
- The Guthrie, Kentucky recycling center will be able to cast 240,000 tons of sheet ingot for its automotive customers per year.
- Novelis announced a strategic partnership with Sortera Alloys Inc. of Fort Wayne, Indiana, designed to help the company increase its use of prime and obsolete scrap.
- The company maintains a strong total liquidity position of $2.4 billion as of June 30.
