## Facts

- Archer Aviation will invest $118 million to construct a plant near Atlanta, Georgia.
- The aircraft plant will be built adjoining an airport in Covington, Georgia.
- The company plans to eventually hire up to 1,000 people at the facility.
- Archer Aviation is building small electric aircraft, specifically battery-powered vertical takeoff and landing (eVTOL) crafts with six propellers that hold four passengers and a pilot.
- The eVTOL craft could be used for short flights, especially in urban areas.
- United Airlines said it would fly the craft from downtown Manhattan to Newark Liberty International Airport in New Jersey.
- The planned United Airlines route would cut a trip that can take an hour by car in congested traffic to 10 minutes.
- The U.S. Air Force is evaluating Archer's aircraft for possible use.
- Archer is seeking Federal Aviation Administration certification, with the company saying it could be certified by 2024.
- Archer aims to produce 650 aircraft annually at the Georgia site.
- Production capacity at the Georgia site has the potential to rise to 2,300 aircraft per year.
- Archer expects to receive an incentive package worth some $40 million from Georgia and local governments.
- The incentive package includes the donation of 96 acres of land at Covington Municipal Airport.
- The incentive package includes a cash grant from the state.
- The incentive package includes other tax incentives.
- Archer chose Georgia because of the availability of local workers.
- Archer chose Georgia for the ability to conduct test flights.
- Archer chose Georgia for affordable construction costs.
- Archer chose Georgia for good highway, rail, and air connections.
- Archer CEO Adam Goldstein stated that their eVTOL technology can transform how urban and rural communities live and commute.
- Goldstein added that the factory can create pathways to highly skilled manufacturing hubs and other ladders of social and economic opportunity.
- Georgia Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson said economic recruiters specializing in aerospace had worked with Archer.
- Wilson stated that "technology and innovation continue to drive change across strategic industries."
