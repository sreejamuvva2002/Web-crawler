# New Clean Vehicle Tax Credit

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-30

**Source:** [Credits for new clean vehicles purchased in 2023 or after | Internal Revenue Service](https://www.irs.gov/credits-deductions/credits-for-new-clean-vehicles-purchased-in-2023-or-after)  ·  `page_004145`

## Facts

- The New Clean Vehicle Credit is not available for vehicles acquired after Sept. 30, 2025.
- A vehicle must be placed in service to claim the credit.
- If a vehicle is placed in service after Sept. 30, 2025, acquisition must have occurred on or before Sept. 30, 2025 to remain eligible for the credit.
- Acquisition can be demonstrated by entering into a binding written contract and making a payment on the vehicle on or before Sept. 30, 2025.
- A vehicle is considered placed in service when the buyer takes possession of it.
- The page covers changes to the credit under the One, Big, Beautiful Bill.
- Buyers who acquire a new plug-in electric vehicle (EV) or fuel cell vehicle (FCV) on or before Sept. 30, 2025 may qualify for the clean vehicle tax credit upon placing the vehicle in service.
- Sellers must provide buyers with information about the vehicle's qualifications when they take possession of the vehicle.
- Sellers must register online and report vehicle qualification information to the IRS; failure to do so renders the vehicle ineligible for the credit.
- Buyers may qualify for a tax credit of up to $7,500 under Internal Revenue Code Section 30D for purchasing a new, qualified plug-in EV or FCV.
- The credit is available to both individuals and their businesses.
- To qualify, the vehicle must be purchased for the buyer's own use rather than for resale.
- To qualify, the vehicle must be used primarily within the United States.
- Buyers' modified adjusted gross income (AGI) cannot exceed $300,000 for married couples filing jointly or a surviving spouse.
- Buyers' modified AGI cannot exceed $225,000 for heads of households.
- Buyers' modified AGI cannot exceed $150,000 for all other filers.
- Buyers can use their modified AGI from the year they take delivery of the vehicle or the prior year, whichever is lower.
- If a buyer's modified AGI falls below the threshold in either of the two relevant years, they remain eligible to claim the credit.
- The credit is nonrefundable at tax filing if not transferred at the time of sale, meaning taxpayers cannot receive a refund exceeding their tax liability through this credit.
- Any excess credit amount cannot be applied to future tax years.
- The final credit amount depends on when the vehicle was placed in service (took delivery), regardless of the actual purchase date.
- For vehicles placed in service between January 1 and April 17, 2023, the base credit is $2,500.
- An additional $417 is added for vehicles with at least 7 kilowatt hours of battery capacity.
- An additional $417 is added for each kilowatt hour of battery capacity beyond 5 kilowatt hours.
- The maximum credit for the January 1 to April 17, 2023 period is up to $7,500 total.
- In general, the minimum credit will be $3,751 ($2,500 plus three times $417) for a vehicle with the minimum required 7 kilowatt hours of battery capacity.
- For vehicles placed in service on April 18, 2023, and after, buyers must meet new critical mineral and battery component requirements to qualify.
- Vehicles meeting only the critical minerals requirement qualify for a credit up to $3,750.
- Vehicles meeting only the battery components requirement qualify for a credit up to $3,750.
- Vehicles meeting both the critical mineral and battery component requirements qualify for a credit up to $7,500.
- A vehicle that fails to meet either the critical mineral or battery component requirement is ineligible for any credit.
- Qualified vehicles must have a battery capacity of at least 7 kilowatt hours.
- Qualified vehicles must have a gross vehicle weight rating of less than 14,000 pounds.
- Qualified vehicles must be manufactured by a qualified manufacturer.
- Qualified vehicles must undergo final assembly in North America.
- Qualified vehicles must meet critical mineral and battery component requirements as of April 18, 2023.
- The credit only applies to sales where the buyer purchases the vehicle new.
- Sellers are required to report the buyer's name and taxpayer identification number to the IRS for the buyer to be eligible to claim the credit.
- The vehicle's manufacturer suggested retail price (MSRP) cannot exceed $80,000 for vans, sport utility vehicles, and pickup trucks.
- The vehicle's MSRP cannot exceed $55,000 for other vehicle types.
- MSRP is defined as the manufacturer's suggested retail price including installed options, accessories, and trim but excluding destination fees, and may differ from the actual purchase price.
- Buyers can find their vehicle's weight, battery capacity, final assembly location (listed as final assembly point), and VIN on the vehicle's window sticker.
- To claim the credit, taxpayers must file Form 8936, Clean Vehicle Credits, with their tax return.
- Claiming the credit requires providing the vehicle's VIN on the tax form.
- Dealers are required to provide a paper copy of a time-of-sale report to buyers upon taking possession of the vehicle.
- Buyers should keep the time-of-sale report copy for their records as it confirms the dealer reported the purchase date to the IRS.
- Taxpayers must file Form 8936 in the tax year they take delivery of the vehicle, regardless of whether the credit was transferred at sale or deferred to filing.
