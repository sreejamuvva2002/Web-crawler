# City of Lagrange and Troup County Economic Development Zone

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2018-04-02

**Source:** [LaGrange | Troup County : An Open Invitation - Georgia Trend Magazine](https://www.georgiatrend.com/2018/04/02/lagrange-troup-county-an-open-invitation/)  ·  `page_000971`

## Facts

- LaGrange is home to Kia, Interface, Jindal Films, Caterpillar, and more than a dozen other companies, widely recognized as a forerunner in industry and manufacturing.
- LaGrange ranks sixth in Georgia for the number of people employed by international companies.
- The Georgia Ports Authority’s Strategic Plan designates LaGrange as the state’s third inland port, with the process taking 24 to 30 months to complete.
- KIA is one of the Georgia Ports Authority’s largest customers, making the inland port location near the KIA plant a strategic decision for industrial and economic development in the region.
- As of January, Troup County reported more than $1 billion in new capital investment under construction.
- A $530-million facility is being constructed by an unnamed Chinese tire manufacturer, which remained under a media moratorium at press time as owners prepared to take the company public in China.
- Interface is undergoing a $54-million expansion for its LaGrange facility, which has manufactured modular commercial carpet tile since the 1970s; the project includes growing the facility and adding new technology to retain its 1,200 employees.
- Duracell’s LaGrange location has produced every nine-volt battery sold in North America since 1981.
- Duracell is undergoing a $100-million expansion to transition production from nine-volt batteries to AA and AAA batteries.
- The Duracell expansion will consolidate global engineering, purchasing, and customer service on a 58-acre campus and add approximately 100 jobs in the business center.
- Jindal Films is continuing a $205-million project that includes expanding an existing plant and relocating its North American headquarters and research and development facilities from Rochester, N.Y., to LaGrange.
- The Jindal Films relocation will bring 243 very high-end, high-paying jobs to the headquarter facilities.
- Caterpillar’s global headquarters for its forestry division has operated in LaGrange for 20 years, manufacturing every piece of forestry equipment the company sells.
- Caterpillar recently invested $15 million to complete a 200,000-square-foot inbound logistics facility, adding approximately 50 jobs.
- Duracell, Caterpillar, and Jindal have all been in LaGrange for over 20 years and collectively invested $325 million in expansions in the past year alone.
- Scott Malone serves as president of the Development Authority of LaGrange.
