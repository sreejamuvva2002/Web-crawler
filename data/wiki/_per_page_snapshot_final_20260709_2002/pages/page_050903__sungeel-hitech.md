# SungEel HiTech

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2023-09-11

**Source:** [SungEel HiTech & Samsung plan battery recycling in Germany - electrive.com](https://www.electrive.com/2023/09/11/sungeel-hitech-sumsung-plan-battery-recycling-in-germany/)  ·  `page_050903`

## Facts

- Construction of the €45 million battery recycling plant in Gera will start in March 2024.
- The first of two production lines should go into operation in early 2025, the second in early 2027.
- Old batteries will be dismantled, crushed and dried on site.
- Materials are subsequently separated in a mechanical process to leave only raw materials that can be used for new batteries.
- The joint venture between SungEel HiTech and Samsung had initially been planned for a site in the Thuringian municipality of Rudolstadt.
- The initial plan in Rudolstadt failed partly due to massive local objections.
- In Gera, the city council supported the project in a decisive vote only by a narrow majority.
- The plant is to be built in the Gera-Cretzschwitz industrial park on an area of around 30,000 square meters.
- The plant will provide 100 jobs.
- The planned construction by the South Korean partners will be the second large recycling plant for electric vehicle batteries in the German state of Thuringia.
- Finnish company Fortum is also building a battery recycling hub in Thuringia.
- SungEel HiTech previously initiated a battery recycling joint venture within Korea with SK Innovation.
- The SK Innovation JV is expected to combine SK Innovation’s independently developed lithium hydroxide recovery technology and SungEel HiTech’s nickel, cobalt and manganese recovery technology.
- According to SK Innovation’s statement at the time, the JV aims to “secure a differentiated competitive advantage in the fast-growing market.”
- SungEel HiTech made its stock market debut in the summer of 2022.
- SungEel HiTech is the only company in Korea that recovers cobalt, nickel, manganese and copper from lithium-ion batteries in a large-scale hydrometallurgical plant.
- An update dated 25 March 2025 states that the planned battery recycling plant in Gera-Cretzschwitz industrial estate between SungEel HiTech and Samsung has come to nothing.
