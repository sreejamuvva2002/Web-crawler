# Ascend Elements

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2025-05-08

**Source:** [Poland lures Ascend Elements battery recycling with a grant worth millions - electrive.com](https://www.electrive.com/2025/05/08/poland-lures-ascend-elements-battery-recycling-with-a-grant-worth-millions/)  ·  `page_052547`

## Facts

- The location for its new battery recycling plant in Poland has been identified, though a final investment decision has not yet been made.
- Ascend Elements specializes in producing battery materials from black mass.
- The company has been active in Poland for approximately one year through a joint venture with Elemental Strategic Metals.
- The joint venture operates a recycling plant in Zawiercie, Poland.
- Ascend Elements intends to independently build and operate a new planned cathode material (pCAM) plant in Poland.
- The new Polish facility will utilize the company's hydro-to-cathode technology, a direct synthesis process that creates pCAM material from used lithium-ion cells.
- The plant aims to commercialize the production of sustainable nickel, manganese, and cobalt (NMC) pCAM made from recycled lithium-ion battery materials.
- Tomasz Poznar, Senior Vice President of Commercial at Ascend Elements, stated the company is grateful for Polish government support and views the project as a critical step in transforming Europe's battery materials landscape.
- The company aims to bridge the growing gap in cathode precursor supply and contribute to the EU’s strategic net-zero goals by building a sustainable circular battery materials supply chain in the region.
- Co-founder and Chief Technical Officer Eric Gratz noted that while US tariffs would impact manufacturers relying on virgin materials from Canada and China, Ascend Elements is unaffected because it relies on domestic recycled materials.
- Recycled cobalt is expected to become cheaper than imported cobalt due to tariff dynamics.
- In February, Ascend Elements canceled a $164 million grant from the US Department of Energy (DOE) for cathode active material production at its Kentucky plant.
- The cancellation of US federal funding is paving the way for regions like Europe to welcome US battery recycling expertise and technology previously funded by the US government.
- The Polish government is offering a grant of 1.22 billion Polish zloty (approximately 290 million euros) to establish the new plant, which Ascend Elements describes as one of the largest grants ever awarded by the Republic of Poland.
- President and CEO Linh Austin stated the grant is an important step in the company's European growth strategy and expressed appreciation for the significant financial support from the Polish Ministry.
- Pawel Pudlowski, Vice President of the Management Board at the Polish Investment and Trade Agency (PAIH), described the project as one of the largest recent US investments in Poland, strengthening the country's role in the battery supply chain and supporting regional development and cooperation with local educational institutions.
- Ascend Elements already owns and operates a large battery recycling plant in Covington, Georgia.
- Construction on the company's planned hydro-to-cathode production plant in Hopkinsville, Kentucky, has been paused but is scheduled to resume after clearing several hurdles.
- The Kentucky plant is expected to go into operation at the end of 2026.
- Ascend Elements plans to scale up its business selling recycled lithium carbonate to battery manufacturers in both the USA and Europe.
