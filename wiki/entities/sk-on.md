# SK On

**Type:** Company  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[battery-cell-manufacturing|Battery Cell Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** Seoul, South Korea, Georgia, United States

## Overview

South Korean battery manufacturer SK On laid off nearly 1,000 workers at its U.S. plant in Commerce, Georgia amid slowing electric-vehicle demand, while continuing to build a second battery plant in Bartow County and maintaining its commitment to the Georgia EV supply chain.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Operates a 22 GWh battery plant in Commerce, GA that supplies EV batteries to major automakers and is expanding capacity with a second Georgia plant, directly supporting the U.S. electric-vehicle supply chain.

## Key facts

- SK On laid off 968 employees, about 37% of the Commerce plant's workforce of roughly 2,500.
- The layoffs were attributed to slowing EV sales and changing market conditions.
- SK On reaffirmed its commitment to Georgia and to building a strong U.S. supply chain for advanced battery manufacturing.
- The Commerce plant supplies EV batteries to Volkswagen, Hyundai Motor, and previously Ford's F-150 Lightning.
- SK On is constructing a second battery plant in Bartow County, Georgia, with production slated for the first half of this year.
- A third SK On plant in Tennessee is expected to begin production in 2028.
- Governor Brian Kemp met twice (October last year and June 2024) with SK On leadership to discuss cooperation and business ties.
