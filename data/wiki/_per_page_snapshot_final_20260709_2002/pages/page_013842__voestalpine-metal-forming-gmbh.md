# voestalpine Metal Forming GmbH

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [GA: Auto Supplier Creating Manufacturing Facility, 220 Jobs by Cartersville | Trade and Industry Development](https://www.tradeandindustrydev.com/industry/manufacturing/news/ga-auto-supplier-creating-manufacturing-facility-6850)  ·  `page_013842`

## Facts

- Gov. Nathan Deal announced that automotive industry supplier voestalpine Metal Forming, under its automotive body parts unit, will build a manufacturing facility near Cartersville in Bartow County creating 220 jobs.
- The company is investing $62 million in this new facility.
- This facility will be voestalpine's first in the Southeast.
- Gov. Nathan Deal stated that Georgia offers automotive industry suppliers a great combination of resources necessary to compete worldwide.
- Gov. Nathan Deal noted that by choosing Georgia, voestalpine can be assured of a well-trained workforce, a logistics infrastructure growing in global prominence, and a business environment supportive of future growth.
- Voestalpine’s Metal Forming Division provides the automotive industry with a complete range of pressed parts in the body-in-white segment.
- The division also provides highly innovative structural components to well-respected suppliers.
- The company’s customers represent several of the leading manufacturers in the automotive and automotive supply industries.
- CEO of voestalpine Metal Forming GmbH and member of the Board of voestalpine AG Herbert Eibensteiner stated that the company has a very high commitment to quality for innovative products and smooth supply chains.
- Herbert Eibensteiner noted that Cartersville offers a strategically located and business-friendly environment that supports their mindset and will stimulate future plans for the North American automotive market.
- Herbert Eibensteiner confirmed that the state of Georgia, the Georgia Department of Economic Development, and local authorities representing Bartow County and Cartersville beneficially supported their selection process.
- Bartow County’s geographic advantages played a significant role in voestalpine’s decision to choose the county for this new manufacturing operation.
- The site offers convenient access to I-75, allowing efficient reach to key U.S. customers in the Southeast.
- The site allows global connectivity with ease of access to the Port of Savannah.
- Voestalpine will be the anchor tenant in the Highland 75 development in Bartow County.
- Highland 75 is a GRAD Certified 707-acre master planned industrial/corporate park located between Atlanta and Chattanooga, one mile east of I-75.
- Highland 75 is a collaboration of Bartow County, the city of Cartersville, and the Bartow-Cartersville Joint Development Authority.
- Bartow County Sole Commissioner Clarence Brown stated that projects like this require the efforts of many involved and committed people.
- Clarence Brown noted that years of hard work from the Joint Development Authority and others are now beginning to pay off.
- Clarence Brown added that investing in Highland 75 was intended to attract jobs, welcoming voestalpine as the first company in the development for its positive economic impact.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) partnered with the Cartersville-Bartow County Department of Economic Development to secure this business opportunity for Georgia.
- GDEcD project manager Wylly Harrison managed this project on behalf of the state.
- The project team collaborated with managing director of Georgia’s European Office in Germany Antje Abshoff.
- The project team received assistance from Parker Poe Consulting, LLC and TPA Realty Services.
- GDEcD Commissioner Chris Cummiskey stated that the project is significant for Georgia as a great intersection of efforts to attract leading manufacturers and international businesses.
- Chris Cummiskey added that voestalpine’s decision to locate in Georgia is an important win for the state's high-performing logistics network, workforce, and economic development partners.
- The company will receive training assistance from Georgia Quick Start.
- Georgia Quick Start is described as the nation’s top-ranked workforce training program that offers tailored, operations-specific training to qualified companies free of charge for expansion or relocation in Georgia.
- Future employment opportunities information is available through the Georgia Department of Labor’s Cartersville Career Center.
- Voestalpine Metal Forming is part of the voestalpine Group and operates business units including Automotive Body Parts, Tubes & Sections, and Precision Strip.
- The voestalpine Group is a steel, processing, and technology group that operates worldwide manufacturing and processing facilities developing high-quality steel products.
- With top-quality flat steel products, the Group is one of the leading partners in the automotive, white goods, and energy industries in Europe as well as the oil and gas industries worldwide.
- The Group maintains 400 production and sales companies in more than 60 countries on five continents.
- The Group has been listed on the Vienna Stock Exchange since 1995.
- The Group has approximately 46,500 employees worldwide.
