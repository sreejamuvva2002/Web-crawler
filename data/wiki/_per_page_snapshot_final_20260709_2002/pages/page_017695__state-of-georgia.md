# State of Georgia

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- During Fiscal Year 2023, total investments in facility expansions and new locations across Georgia totaled more than $24 billion.
- Fiscal Year 2023 resulted in 38,400 new jobs through 426 projects supported by the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s GlobalCommerce team.
- This marks the third consecutive year that Georgia broke records for economic development during Fiscal Year 2023.
- When excluding the two largest projects in state history, Rivian and Hyundai Motor Group, from previous year's job creation figures, total jobs as a result of FY23 projects exceeded the previous record by more than 2,800 new positions.
- Annual investment totals have increased by 131 percent over the last three years.
- Eighty-two percent of new jobs created and more than $20 billion in investments are directed to communities outside the 10-county Atlanta region.
- Metro Atlanta attracted $3.6 billion in investment during the last fiscal year.
- Expansions account for 71 percent of total projects, while new locations account for 65 percent of new jobs announced across the state.
- Between July 2018 and July 2023, at least 40 e-mobility projects have been announced in Georgia.
- These e-mobility projects commit more than 30,000 jobs and over $25 billion in investment to the state.
- Job creation in the automotive industry increased by 324 percent in FY23 compared to FY21.
- Suppliers attracted by Georgia’s mobility original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) resulted in over $2 billion in investment across the state in FY23.
- Mobility and cleantech projects delivered three investments of $1 billion or more during FY23.
- Mobility and cleantech projects accounted for six of the largest projects by job creation in FY23.
- The Georgia Center of Innovation connects public and private partners to foster innovation in strategic ecosystems.
- Partnerships on workforce solutions like the “Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development” (GRAD) certification and Georgia Quick Start programs spurred a 112 percent growth in advanced manufacturing investments since FY21.
