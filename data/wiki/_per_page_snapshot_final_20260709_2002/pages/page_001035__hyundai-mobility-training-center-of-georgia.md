# Hyundai Mobility Training Center of Georgia

**Type:** workforce_training  ·  **Role:** workforce_training  ·  **Published:** 2025-11-06

**Source:** [Georgia Hosts Grand Opening for Hyundai Mobility Training Center](https://www.prweb.com/releases/georgia-hosts-grand-opening-for-hyundai-mobility-training-center-302607854.html)  ·  `page_001035`

## Facts

- Governor Brian Kemp and Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America President and CEO Tony Heo joined state and local leaders on November 5 to celebrate the grand opening of the Hyundai Mobility Training Center of Georgia.
- The training center is an 89,000-square-foot state-of-the-art facility designed to train the next generation of electric vehicle (EV) manufacturing professionals.
- It is strategically located adjacent to Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) in Bryan County.
- This marks the first training center in Georgia geared toward EVs and sets a new standard for workforce development.
- HMGMA has announced an expansion that will include hybrid vehicles, and the facility is designed to accommodate training for those vehicles in the future.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that the Hyundai Mobility Training Center of Georgia will give thousands of people over the years the knowledge they need to benefit from this generational project.
- José Muñoz, President and CEO of Hyundai Motor Company, said the center represents a commitment to investing in people as they continue to create high-paying American jobs.
- As HMGMA expands production at one of the most advanced facilities in the world, it needs a workforce equipped with skills to build next-generation vehicles with next-generation production processes.
- Hyundai's partnership with Georgia Quick Start ensures Meta Pros have access to world-class training that will help them succeed and help Georgia lead in advanced manufacturing.
- A special thank you was given to Governor Kemp, Commissioner Pat Wilson, and the economic development team in Georgia for their ongoing support.
- Hyundai's project in Georgia will create approximately 8,500 jobs by 2031.
- At full capacity, the new facility can train up to 824 individuals at a time, providing customized, immersive education tailored to Hyundai's advanced EV manufacturing processes.
- The center is operated by Georgia Quick Start, the State's renowned workforce development program and part of the Technical College System of Georgia (TCSG).
- The center will prepare employees for roles at HMGMA and affiliate companies.
- Greg Dozier, Commissioner of TCSG, said the center reflects Georgia's commitment to innovation, accessibility, and long-term economic opportunity.
- Quick Start's training professionals collaborated with HMGMA to design and build world-class infrastructure that closely mirrors the processes trainees will encounter at the Metaplant.
- The facility includes a STEP Line (Safe Transition for Efficient Production) where trainees build timing, awareness, and coordination skills in a controlled, digital environment.
- It includes an IMPACT Line (Immersive Manufacturing Process and Conveyor Training), a full-length, moving production line with 53 integrated assembly activities and a built-in quality check system replicating real-world EV production.
- It features a High Voltage Battery and Safety Lab for hands-on learning with high-voltage systems critical to EV and hybrid vehicle performance.
- It includes a VR Experience Lab, a simulated environment for safety and hazard identification.
- It has an Automation & Mechatronics Lab focused on programmable logic controllers (PLCs), sensors, motion systems, and industrial automation.
- Quick Start Deputy Commissioner Scott McMurray said subject matter experts partnered directly with HMGMA to learn the skills its Meta Pros will need when they hit the production floor.
- Additional facilities include a 5,764-square-foot multipurpose "Momentum Room," six flexible classrooms, a robotics lab, welding lab, paint lab, and a robotic dog that supports advanced automation training.
- The training center stands as a core pillar of Georgia's broader strategy to prepare and train the state's workforce for success in advanced manufacturing.
- This launch reaffirms Georgia's leadership in the global mobility and EV manufacturing sectors while delivering unmatched value to communities across the state.
- Tony Heo said the grand opening marks a proud milestone for Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America, the State of Georgia, the Technical College System, and Georgia Quick Start.
- Heo stated the training center gives confidence that trained talent will be needed to shape the future of mobility and American manufacturing in Southeast Georgia.
- Georgia Quick Start is a program within TCSG that offers customized workforce training to qualified companies as a discretionary incentive to support investment and job creation in the state.
- Georgia Quick Start is the oldest workforce training program in the United States, as well as the most awarded by site selection consultants.
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) is Hyundai Motor Group's first dedicated Electrified Vehicle mass-production plant located in Bryan County, GA.
- The HMGMA plant boasts a highly connected, automated, and flexible manufacturing system that organically connects all elements of the EV ecosystem to realize customer value.
- The Georgia facility will become an intelligent manufacturing plant where all processes of production are optimized utilizing AI and data.
- The innovative manufacturing system helps create a human-centered work environment with robots assisting human workers.
