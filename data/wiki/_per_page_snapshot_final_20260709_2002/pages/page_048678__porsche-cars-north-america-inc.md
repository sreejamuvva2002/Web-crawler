# Porsche Cars North America, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2020-09

**Source:** [Porsche to Build Multimillion-Dollar Project in Atlanta - Savannah CEO](https://savannahceo.com/news/2020/09/porsche-build-multimillion-dollar-project-atlanta-/)  ·  `page_048678`

## Facts

- Porsche Cars North America, Inc. (PCNA) announced a new multi-million dollar investment to further develop its North American headquarters and U.S. footprint in Georgia.
- The new development is located at the northeast corner of Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport.
- The 33-acre development adds to PCNA's existing 27-acre headquarters.
- Construction for the first stage of the development began in September 2020.
- The first stage consists of a standalone Porsche Service Center independently developed, owned, and operated jointly by Hennessy Porsche North Atlanta and Porsche Atlanta Perimeter.
- PCNA will invest in later stages over the next three years, including a new Porsche Classic Factory Restoration facility and a track extension for the Porsche Experience Center Atlanta.
- Kjell Gruner, newly appointed President and CEO of PCNA, stated that Porsche is committed to the United States and sees a prime opportunity to enhance the customer experience by expanding its campus next to the world's busiest airport.
- The Porsche Experience Center Atlanta has welcomed over 270,000 visitors since opening in 2015.
- Governor Brian Kemp noted that Georgia’s automotive industry is a $3 billion economic driver in the state and praised Porsche's continued commitment to Georgia.
- Mayor Alan Hallman of Hapeville expressed excitement for Porsche breaking ground and expanding its footprint within the city, symbolizing tremendous growth and opportunities.
- Atlanta Mayor Keisha Lance Bottoms stated that the administration looks forward to working with Porsche to strengthen the local economy through highly skilled and middle-wage opportunities.
- The Porsche Service Center South Atlanta will be a 20,815 square foot facility situated on 2.5 acres of land.
- The service center will include 13 lift bays to service customer vehicles, a Porsche Driver’s Selection boutique, and a reception area with a waiting lounge for customers.
- The Porsche Service Center South Atlanta is one of two standalone service centers for Porsche in the U.S.
- The expected completion date for the Porsche Service Center South Atlanta is the third quarter of 2021.
- Phase two of the development is expected to begin in 2021 with the construction of a parking structure.
- Phase two will also include construction of the new Porsche Classic Factory Restoration facility and the Porsche Experience Center Atlanta track extension, with an expected completion date of mid-2023.
