# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-07-24

**Source:** [Georgia Grants Hyundai $1.8B Incentive](https://www.thefinancialdistrict.com.ph/post/georgia-grants-hyundai-1-8b-incentive)  ·  `page_009409`

## Facts

- The state of Georgia and local governments are giving $1.8 billion in tax breaks and other incentives to Hyundai Motor Group.
- The incentives are provided in exchange for Hyundai building its first US plant dedicated to electric vehicles near Savannah.
- The signed agreement was disclosed on Friday, July 22, 2022.
- The project site covers 2,900 acres (1,170 hectares) in Bryan County, west of Savannah.
- Hyundai will invest $5.5 billion in its Georgia plant as part of the agreement.
- The agreement includes hiring 8,100 workers for the facility.
- This deal is described as the state's greatest economic development deal in history.
- The announcement comes only months after Georgia concluded another major deal with electric vehicle manufacturer Rivian to build a factory in the state.
- Pat Wilson, the state's economic development commissioner, stated that these generational projects solidify Georgia's spot at the vanguard of the EV transition and ensure thousands of Georgians benefit from future jobs.
- Hyundai executives and Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp celebrated the agreement with a champagne toast in May at the project site.
- Hyundai intends to begin building the facility next year.
- The plant is scheduled to produce up to 300,000 automobiles per year by 2025.
- The new factory will also manufacture automobile batteries.
- The deal is worth approximately $300 million more than the incentives offered to Rivian.
- The incentive package costs nearly $228,000 for every job produced in Georgia and four counties in the Savannah area.
- Hyundai's payroll at the new plant is estimated to exceed $4.7 billion over the next ten years.
- Thousands of new employment opportunities are projected to be created by parts suppliers.
- Greg LeRoy, executive director of Good Jobs First, noted that the $1.8 billion in incentives is the highest subsidy package a US state has ever committed for an automobile plant.
- LeRoy described the subsidy as inherently super-risky because it bets a huge amount on one company and one facility.
- Local governments are granting Hyundai more than $472 million in property tax breaks.
- Hyundai will pay more than $357 million in lieu of taxes over a 26-year period beginning in 2023.
