# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA)

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-03-27

**Source:** [GA Quick Start provides HMGMA employees state-funded training](https://billswire.usatoday.com/story/business/employment/2025/03/27/ga-quick-start-trains-all-hyundai-metaplant-employees-through-state-funded-quick-start/78033320007/)  ·  `page_009415`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) employees, known as Meta Pros, receive specialized training through Georgia's Quick Start program.
- The state of Georgia invested heavily in HMGMA, including a dedicated training center and tax incentives, to secure the project and its economic benefits.
- Quick Start's training program for HMGMA employees includes corporate culture, safety instruction, and specialized skills training based on job roles.
- Georgia's investment in HMGMA is expected to have long-term financial benefits for the region, including a significant increase in Bryan County's tax base.
- Bonnie Williams works as a Quality Control Meta Pro within the Body Division at HMGMA, spot-checking machine welding for questionable welds that might need human assistance.
- Hyundai held an Ioniq 9 reveal in November at AutoNation Hyundai Savannah, located at 7011 Abercorn St., where Bonnie Williams observed finished vehicles after typically only seeing the "bones of the cars."
- The music playing while machines operate was a unique part of Bonnie Williams' experience as a Quality Control Meta Pro.
- Antoine Kelly works as a Patrol Team member conducting quality control throughout the entire assembly process at HMGMA.
- "Meta Pro" is a term HMGMA uses for its employees to indicate trust in colleagues as an integral part of the transformation of mobility and manufacturing, according to its website.
- HMGMA features testimonials from Meta Pros on its YouTube Channel.
- Courtney McSwain, a Savannah native and long-time industrial maintenance professional at HMGMA, stated she loves her job because "they actually train you."
- Georgia's Quick Start program, a division of the Technical College System of Georgia, partners with HMGMA to provide training paid for by Georgia taxpayers.
- Training happens at two separate Quick Start facilities: Port City Logistics at 100 Myrtle Drive, Port Wentworth, and Quick Start Advanced Manufacturing at 1500 Pine Meadow Drive, Pooler.
- The Quick Start Advanced Manufacturing facility in Pooler is slated for a $10.6 million state-funded 30,000-square-foot expansion.
- Construction is well underway on a HMGMA-dedicated Quick Start Hyundai Mobility Training Center, which is a $62.5 million state-funded facility.
- The state of Georgia agreed to cover the new training center's operating costs, with an estimated value of training services at nearly $5 million per year for five years.
- The state's agreement with Hyundai notes an "in-kind estimated cost" of $54,818,400 for customized Quick Start workforce recruitment program services for HMGMA affiliate suppliers and the battery joint venture.
- Hyundai will receive a 27-year property tax abatement as part of Georgia state and local tax incentives and abatements.
- Hyundai is expected to pay leasehold and ad valorem property tax payments starting in 2026 after initial construction is complete.
- Those estimated annual tax payments are more than $7 million, which is more than 180 times the property's previous estimated taxes of roughly $39,000 based on 2021 undeveloped land values.
- The Ellabell, Georgia mega site's physical configuration and visibility from Interstate-16 were key enticement factors for Hyundai, alongside economic incentives.
- An Aug. 23, 2023 Georgia Joint Tax credit subcommittee meeting at the Georgia Southern University Armstrong Campus addressed Hyundai's decision to choose Bryan County.
- Ulrich Schmidt, a Hyundai site selection consultant and managing director of KPMG LLP’s Global Location and Expansion Services, spoke at the meeting about the site selection process.
- Factors in Hyundai's analysis included labor and utility costs as well as recurring taxes.
- Ulrich Schmidt stated that "Incentives are essentially the offset to what that bottom line might look like."
- Christopher Smith, HMGMA chief project implementation officer and general counsel, called incentives "absolutely critical" to Hyundai's choice.
- Christopher Smith noted that Georgia had not offered the most in tax incentives among competing states, but elements like the Quick Start partnership and the Savannah area's promise of workforce availability helped seal the deal.
- Quick Start's Deputy Commissioner Scott McMurray said the institution works with employer-partners across the state to establish training programs that fit employees' needs and align with company missions.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development decides which companies are eligible for the Quick Start incentive, looking at manufacturing job types, number of jobs, and hiring schedules.
- The threshold for eligibility is around 90-100 trainees in a short time period, though the department typically assists employers with large hiring needs over longer timeframes.
- HMGMA and its onsite affiliate suppliers need to hire and train between 6,800 and 8,500 employees, which certainly met the requirements.
- All Meta Pros go through a week-long training as part of the onboarding process.
- Depending on an employee's position, they may require additional weeks of training in their skill areas.
- HMGMA has denied requests for its quarterly and yearly hiring goals.
- Chief Administrative Officer Brent Stubbs stated the hesitancy to share hiring glide paths is because "The auto industry is incredibly competitive and hiring glide paths are signals to production goals."
- Scott McMurray said roughly 20-30 Meta Pros a month go through initial hire training, which typically entails corporate culture and safety instruction.
- Quick Start adjusts resources like instructors or space if there is a surge of around 100 employees.
- The new training center features adaptability with dividing walls that can up- or down-size classrooms and an auditorium that can hold roughly 600 people.
- Quick Start has provided similar state-funded training since 2009 at the Kia Georgia Training Center near its Westpoint plant.
- Both the Kia and HMGMA training centers are purpose-built facilities with agreement options for potential future purchase, differing from typical Quick Start leases that renovate spaces for a few years.
- Kia has opted out of purchasing its training facility when the option was presented.
- HMGMA could opt to buy the facility after the initial five years or continue the agreement with Quick Start.
- At the August 2023 subcommittee meeting, Savannah Chamber President Bert Brantley stressed that the state conducted in-depth analysis ahead of the HMGMA deal and noted recognition of the cost to the state and local government.
- Savannah Economic Development Authority President Trip Tollison said Georgia's investment will have long-lasting financial benefits for the region.
- Trip Tollison noted that when the property re-enters the tax roll, it will increase Bryan County's tax digest by 800% in a single day, calling it "absolutely unheard of."
