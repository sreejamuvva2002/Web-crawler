# YKK (U.S.A.) Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2024-05

**Source:** [YKK USA Celebrates 50 Years of Innovation and Excellence at Macon Factory - Middle Georgia CEO](https://middlegeorgiaceo.com/features/2024/05/ykk-usa-celebrates-50-years-innovation-and-excellence-macon-factory/)  ·  `page_016118`

## Facts

- YKK (U.S.A.) Inc. is celebrating the 50th anniversary of its Macon Factory on May 4, 2024.
- The anniversary event features speeches from Mayor of Macon Lester Miller, Consul General of Japan Mio Maeda, YKK Corporation Chairman Masayuki Sarumaru, President of YKK Corporation of America Jim Reed, and President of YKK (U.S.A.) Inc. John Smith.
- The company will honor employees marking their 45th through 50th anniversaries with the firm during the event.
- The ceremony includes the rededication of the Heart of Friendship sculpture, relocated to Carolyn Crayton Park, originally donated by YKK in 1974.
- President John Smith highlighted the Macon Factory's pivotal role in embodying the company’s philosophy and its commitment to the community over fifty years.
- Japan-based YKK first entered the U.S. market in 1960 as a zipper distributor in New York City near the garment district.
- Founder Tadao Yoshida established a vertical integration model known as the “Cycle of Goodness” philosophy, controlling the entire production process to ensure quality and cost efficiency.
- A site selection team explored Tennessee, North Carolina, South Carolina, Alabama, and Georgia as the U.S. textile industry shifted southward.
- Georgia was selected due to its robust infrastructure, including four interstate highways, the deep-water port of Savannah, and a major international airport.
- A meeting arranged by Cloyd Hall, a Macon native and Special Assistant to then-Governor Jimmy Carter, facilitated a connection between Yoshida and Carter that clinched the decision to settle in Georgia.
- YKK established its manufacturing facility just south of Macon in the newly developed Ocmulgee East Industrial Park.
- The facility's development began with a visit by prominent Macon leaders to YKK’s manufacturing center in Kurobe, Japan.
- Under leadership of Yoshinori Kitano, construction and operation strategies were crafted for the new plant.
- Newly hired engineers, including future YKK Corporation of America CEO Alex Gregory, were trained in Japan to transfer specialized manufacturing techniques to Macon.
- The Macon plant officially opened in 1974.
- Initially, the facility produced zippers tailored for the American market, including varieties for clothing, furniture, and outdoor equipment.
- Over the decades, the factory has expanded dramatically and now employs over 600 local residents.
- The Macon facility has diversified its product line into advanced solutions for the automotive, government, and medical sectors.
- In 1977, YKK played a pivotal role in establishing a sister city relationship between Kurobe, Japan, and Macon, Georgia.
- Over four decades, the sister city partnership facilitated cultural exchange with more than 1,100 citizens from both cities visiting each other's countries.
- YKK is an annual sponsor of the Macon Cherry Blossom Festival.
- In 1990, YKK donated $300,000 toward the construction of a dedicated building for festival organizers.
- The city of Macon donated $60,000 worth of land to complement the 1990 festival building donation.
- Chairman Tadahiro Yoshida personally attended the 1990 groundbreaking ceremony for the festival building.
- YKK remains committed to enhancing the prosperity of Macon and enriching Georgia while advancing the global textile industry.
