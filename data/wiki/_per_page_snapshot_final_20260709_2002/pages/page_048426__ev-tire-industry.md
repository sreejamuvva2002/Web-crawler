# EV Tire Industry

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [How Tiremakers Are Advancing EV Tire Technology | Modern Tire Dealer](https://www.moderntiredealer.com/retail/article/55377405/how-tiremakers-are-advancing-ev-tire-technology)  ·  `page_048426`

## Facts

- Global electric vehicle market projected to reach $6.5 billion by 2030 according to a Grand View Research report.
- Tire manufacturers are transitioning entire passenger/CUV/SUV portfolios to be EV-ready rather than creating EV-only or EV-dedicated SKUs.
- Top tire technology focuses include lowering rolling resistance, increasing load capacity, and enhancing durability to handle heavier EVs with instant torque.
- Dealers should focus on customer education regarding proper tire maintenance and reinforcing trade-offs and efficiencies when selling and servicing EV tires.
- Leading tire manufacturers are shifting their entire passenger/CUV/SUV portfolio to be EV-ready instead of creating dedicated EV-only SKUs, according to Keith Calcagno, chief strategy officer for proprietary brands at American Tire Distributors.
- The U.S. vehicle car parc is getting heavier, creating an exceeding emphasis on tires to handle torque, weight, noise, and rolling resistance.
- Innovations in tire compounding, structural belt package, and pattern/sidewall designs are pushing the frontier to level up every tire produced.
- Apollo Tyres Ltd. is focused on tuning noise and rolling resistance to support range without compromising wear as the market shifts toward EV-compatible products, according to Karl Jin, divisional head of product and pricing.
- Many EV owners expect ICE-like tire life despite added weight and instant torque accelerating wear, making customer education equally important.
- Atturo Tire Corp. positions any tire meeting the correct size, load index, and speed rating as suitable for hybrid or EV use, with matching the tire to driver intent being paramount, according to president Michael Mathis.
- Two key technological advances for EV tires are the heavy-load load index designation addressing increased curb weight and instantaneous torque, and cold-weather suitable tread compound engineering utilizing 3-Peak Mountain Snowflake ratings.
- U.S. EV tire development focuses on extending wear life, lowering rolling resistance, and reducing noise because EVs lack engine vibration to mask road noise, according to Ian McKenney, senior product manager at Bridgestone Americas Inc.
- Continental Tire the Americas LLC has optimized its tires for over 10 years to achieve lower rolling resistance, low rolling noise, and long service lives with zero compromise on safety, benefiting vehicles with electric drive systems, according to Björn Gläser, head of product for PLT.
- EV-specific tires target energy efficiency and range as a main benefit while giving up treadwear mileage, which is the exact same trade-off any OE tire makes, according to David C. Poling, vice president of R&D and technical center at Giti Tire North America Inc.
- Proper tire pressure maintenance is a key factor in driving rolling resistance down, increasing vehicle range, and reducing CO2 emissions, according to Jenny Walker, director of global product strategy at Goodyear Tire & Rubber Co.
- Noise comfort has become an important development factor as drivers become more aware of tire and road interaction noise, which is particularly relevant for EVs generating as little as half the amount of noise as traditional vehicles due to the absence of an ICE.
- U.S. EV tire development is moving in five directions: lower rolling resistance, higher load capacity, lower noise, better wear performance, and a dual-track strategy of EV-specific plus EV-compatible products, according to David Wang, managing director at Gripmax Tires Inc.
- Range remains a major purchase consideration for U.S. consumers, making rolling-resistance optimization a core engineering priority alongside addressing heavier weight, instant torque, and exposed tire noise.
- Hankook Tire America Corp. focuses EV tire development on managing weight, torque, and noise through advanced compounds for lower rolling resistance, reinforced structures for durability, and tread designs extending wear under instant torque, according to Robert Nasca, product training manager.
- Kumho Tire U.S.A. Inc. targets both electric and internal combustion engine vehicles simultaneously with its product portfolio strategy, according to Shawn Denlein, president of sales and marketing.
- The U.S. EV tire market is evolving around efficiency, noise reduction, and durability, with improving rolling resistance directly impacting driving range, which remains a top consumer concern.
- Manufacturers are focusing on advanced noise reduction technologies such as optimized tread patterns and internal sound-absorbing materials because electric vehicles operate much more quietly than internal combustion vehicles.
- Heavier EV weight and instant torque require tires with reinforced structures and enhanced wear resistance to maintain durability and performance, according to Jay Lee, product planning director at Nexen Tire America Inc.
- The industry is increasingly exploring advanced materials, sustainable compounds, and smart tire technologies that can monitor real-time performance.
- Nokian Tyres Inc. has worked on EV innovations for many years as a Nordic company where electric vehicle sales are thriving, with every tire produced intended to suit the needs of EVs, according to Dave Johnston, director of portfolio, pricing and business planning.
- Omni United takes an approach of making all new premium collection tires EV-compatible because tire requirements for EVs overlap with high-performance combustion engine vehicles on durability, stability, high load capacity, low rolling resistance, and low noise.
- Pirelli Tire North America Inc. identifies key innovation trends including low rolling resistance compounds to maximize battery efficiency and extend driving range, enhanced load capacity and reinforced structures for heavier platforms, noise reduction technologies like foam inserts, and instant torque durability requiring new tread compounds and patterns.
- Prinx Chengshan Tire North America is not launching EV-specific tires for the U.S. market but its next-generation touring, H/T, and ultra-high performance lines will feature lower rolling resistance and popular EV sizes, according to Ken Coltrane, vice president of marketing and product development.
- Sailun Tire Americas centers the EV tire conversation on range, durability, and noise, with rapid advancements in low rolling resistance, compounds extending range, reinforced constructions for heavier vehicles and instant torque, and noise-optimized tread patterns enhancing comfort.
- Tire Group International LLC observes a clear shift toward lower rolling resistance to maximize range, higher load capacity to support heavier vehicles, reduced road noise, and more durable compounds to handle instant torque and prevent premature wear.
- Smarter tire monitoring is emerging as efficiency and performance are directly tied to how the tire is maintained, according to Joaquin Gonzalez, president of Tire Group International LLC.
- Toyo Tire U.S.A. Corp. notes that EVs are generally heavier than comparable internal combustion vehicles, making instant torque and added weight tough on tires, while drivers are more concerned about vehicle range due to charging time and infrastructure still being in its infancy.
- The largest advancements in tire technology will come from the necessity to improve compounding, construction, and materials to support EV vehicles while reducing environmental impact, according to Todd Bergeson, senior manager of product planning and technical services at Toyo Tire U.S.A. Corp.
- For the U.S. replacement market, EV tire demand prioritizes tire mileage and overall cost efficiency more than OE tires, which develop with strong emphasis on noise reduction, rolling resistance, and handling performance, according to Jeffrey Zhang, general manager of international business at Zhongce Rubber Group Co. Ltd./ZC Rubber.
