# Fouts Brothers Fire Equipment

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-10-26

**Source:** [Case Study: Compressed Air Dryer Use by Fouts Brothers](https://www.gosuburban.com/tsunami/blog/fouts-brothers-case-study-compressed-air-dryer)  ·  `page_020031`

## Facts

- Founded in 1952, Fouts Brothers Fire Equipment is a family-owned company based in Milledgeville, Georgia.
- The company operates a second location in La Grange, Texas.
- Fouts Brothers produces a wide range of standard line model trucks designed to meet the needs of all fire stations regardless of department size.
- The facility produces 7,500 trucks annually for fire stations across the country.
- In 2019, the company's headquarters and main production facility were moved to Milledgeville, located about 90 miles south of Atlanta.
- The new Milledgeville facility is a 340,000-square-foot building that required significant renovations to support effective product manufacturing.
- Fouts Brothers utilizes a three-compressor system with Ultra Series Rail Mounted dryers from Tsunami Compressed Air Solutions to ensure uninterrupted production lines.
- The interconnection of the compressor system has prevented extended shutdowns due to component issues over three years of operation.
- GSB Industries, a Tsunami distributor, provided assistance during the renovation of the Milledgeville facility.
- Heath Lawrence serves as the Director of Facilities at Fouts Brothers Fire Equipment.
- The company has used the Ultra Series system for three years and completed required maintenance to keep manufacturing lines running while avoiding many repairs.
- The compressed air system has helped reduce tool replacement costs at the facility.
- Pneumatic rivet guns are used extensively in the van line, making clean, dry air critical to prevent water damage and extend tool life.
- Regular maintenance of tools shows no signs of rust and corrosion, which was previously common due to summer condensation.
- Alternatives considered for the new facility included refrigerant and desiccant dryers.
- Lawrence noted that refrigerant dryers fail to remove substantial moisture during high-humidity southern summers and have long-term reliability issues.
- Desiccant systems were also considered but have limited volume capacity and enormous ongoing costs for changing the desiccant.
- The choice to use regenerative desiccant technology was made after consultation with experts.
- Fouts Brothers partnered with GSB Industries, which specializes in installation and service for spray booths, compressors, and related equipment.
- Tsunami’s Ultra Series tank-mounted dryer system has prolonged the life of many air tools used at the facility.
- The system greatly improved the paint process and quality at the facility.
- A Tsunami secondary filter is now used in addition to the primary filter at paint locations.
- The facility handles work on around 500 trucks each month, requiring extensive use of air tools.
- Water through pneumatic tools leads to extensive wear quickly, making dry air essential for achieving high-gloss paint finishes comparable to higher-end sports cars.
- In southern climate zones, the quality of compressed air produced by Tsunami systems is far superior to that of refrigerant units.
- The compressed air system reduces repair budgets and the long-term cost of media replacement.
