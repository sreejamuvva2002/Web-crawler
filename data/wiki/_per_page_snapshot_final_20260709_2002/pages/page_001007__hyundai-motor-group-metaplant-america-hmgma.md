# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA)

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-02

**Source:** [KIA AMERICA AND HYUNDAI MOTOR GROUP METAPLANT AMERICA CELEBRATE PRODUCTION OF THE 2027 KIA SPORTAGE HYBRID](https://www.kiamedia.com/us/en/media/pressreleases/24747/kia-america-and-hyundai-motor-group-metaplant-america-celebrate-production-of-the-2027-kia-sportage)  ·  `page_001007`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) and Kia America celebrated the start of production for the 2027 Kia Sportage Hybrid, marking three major milestones: the first Kia model produced, the first hybrid electric vehicle (HEV), and the third vehicle assembled at the site.
- The Kia Sportage Hybrid joins HMGMA’s lineup alongside award-winning Hyundai IONIQ 5 and IONIQ 9 electric vehicles, demonstrating Hyundai Motor Group’s comprehensive powertrain capabilities.
- Production flexibility was built into HMGMA’s original design, enabling seamless integration of different powertrains with minimal modifications.
- The plant has the ability to produce up to 10 different vehicles.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp stated that mutual success is the direct result of a proven partnership approach creating long-lasting relationships between Kia America, HMG’s Metaplant, the local community, and hardworking Georgians.
- Kia North America and Kia America President and CEO Sean Yoon called the event very important for Kia in Georgia and for customers across the US, noting Metaplant is Kia’s second major investment in the state and a testament to confidence in Georgia’s future as an automotive powerhouse.
- Sean Yoon added that Metaplant will continue the strong momentum the Sportage Hybrid has achieved since being updated last year to quickly become one of Kia’s highest volume models.
- HMGMA President and CEO Tony Heo stated that through the dedication of Meta Pros and a flexible manufacturing system, they successfully prepared to produce their first hybrid vehicle and first Kia model.
- The event expands Kia America’s nearly two-decade commitment to production of award-winning vehicles in Georgia.
- Kia’s West Point factory currently assembles the all-new 2nd generation Telluride, all-electric EV9 and EV6, and internal combustion powered versions of the Sportage and Sorento SUVs.
- The celebration was attended by approximately 200 Meta Pros who shared in the milestone after extensive preparation for the new powertrain.
- Many team members traveled to Kia facilities in West Point, Georgia and South Korea to learn best practices for Sportage Hybrid production.
- Kia’s West Point facility currently employs over 3,200 team members.
- HMGMA adds nearly 2,000 Meta Pros, with a very high percentage coming from the state of Georgia.
- Distinguished guests at the ceremony included Georgia Governor Brian Kemp, Kia North America and Kia America President and CEO Seungkyu (Sean) Yoon, and Kia Georgia President and CEO Stuart Countess.
- During the ceremony, the first Kia Sportage Hybrid assembled at HMGMA debuted by traveling to the stage on an Autonomous Mobile Robot (AMR), highlighting the plant’s advanced automation capabilities.
- Governor Kemp rode in the vehicle as it was presented during the ceremony.
- The addition of the Kia Sportage Hybrid marks a significant step in expanding HMGMA’s production portfolio across Hyundai Motor Group’s three brands: Kia, Hyundai, and Genesis.
- Kia now has capacity for up to 550,000 vehicles across the West Point and HMGMA facilities on an annual basis.
- Future model plans are not being released at this time.
- Kia America is headquartered in Irvine, California, and was recognized as one of the TIME World's Most Sustainable Companies of 2025.
- Kia serves as the 'Official Automotive Partner' of the NBA and WNBA and offers a range of gasoline, hybrid, plug-in hybrid, and electric vehicles sold through a network of nearly 800 dealers in the U.S.
- The 2026 and 2027 all-electric EV6 and EV9, Sportage (excluding PHEV model), Sorento (excludes HEV and PHEV models), and Telluride are assembled in the United States from U.S. and globally sourced parts.
