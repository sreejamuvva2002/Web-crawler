# Tegeta Bandag Plant

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [In the Caucasus, the first Bandag tire retreading plant will appear](https://mosautoshina.com/news/na-kavkaze-poyavitsya-pervyj-zavod-bandag-po-vosstanovleniyu-shin/)  ·  `page_018259`

## Facts

- Bandag (part of Bridgestone) and Tegeta Motors signed an agreement to build a tire retreading plant in Rustavi, Georgia.
- The new facility will be Bandag's first plant in the Caucasus region.
- Initial investments for the project are around $2 million.
- Retreaded tires cost 30-40% less than new tires while maintaining comparable characteristics and a lifespan of up to five years.
- The retreading process requires less raw materials and electricity, extends tire life through multiple cycles, reduces CO2 emissions, and promotes efficient natural resource use.
- Construction is expected to begin before the end of this year and be completed by the end of 2026.
- The plant will initially have a capacity to retread up to 5,000 tires per year, with plans to double capacity within five years.
- Initially, the facility will retread tires for trucks and buses.
- Jacques Fourie, President of Bridgestone MEA, stated that the collaboration combines technical expertise and a shared vision for sustainable mobility to benefit fleets and communities in the region.
- Representatives from Bridgestone and Bandag will ensure the plant meets global standards.
- The facility will be equipped with modern equipment manufactured in Europe and the US.
- Tegeta Motors is involved in tire sales, car maintenance services, and waste recycling.
- Ekaterine Kavtaradze, General Director of Tegeta Motors, noted the launch expands their fleet solutions portfolio, promotes eco-friendly mobility, and strengthens their presence in the Caucasus region.
