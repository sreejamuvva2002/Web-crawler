# Suzuki Manufacturing of America Corp.

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- Suzuki Manufacturing of America Corp. is located in Rome, Ga., about 60 miles northwest of Atlanta.
- The manufacturing site occupies 35 acres.
- The facility contains a 100,000-square-foot manufacturing building.
- The company employs about 150 people at the Georgia location.
- Suzuki remains a mostly Japan-based manufacturer but is beginning to see the effects of other Asian imports to the U.S.
- The company is paying attention to Chinese and Taiwanese manufacturers that have begun distributing in the U.S. over the last five or six years.
- Competitors are offering lower prices, though whether they can offer equivalent performance, service, and durability remains an open question.
- Suzuki's strategy involves providing affordable products with high performance and supporting dealers to stimulate sales and service users.
- Glenn Hansen serves as communications manager for Suzuki.
- Suzuki pioneered innovations such as four-wheel suspension, rack and pinion steering, and front-wheel drive.
- The company was originally founded in 1920 to manufacture textile looms before transitioning to motorized transportation.
- In 1952, Suzuki started making motorized bicycles powered by two-stroke engines.
- Suzuki first entered the U.S. market in 1963 with its motorcycles.
- Twenty years after entering the U.S. market, Suzuki combined two-wheeling and four-wheeling innovations to make the first “quad” all-terrain vehicle (ATV) in the U.S.
