# Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia, Inc. (KMMG)

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2011-04-19

**Source:** [New Equipment Arrives at Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia to Support Production Expansion - Kia Georgia](https://www.kiageorgia.com/new-equipment-arrives-at-kia-motors-manufacturing-georgia-to-support-production-expansion/)  ·  `page_010248`

## Facts

- One of the final and largest pieces of Kia Motors Manufacturing Inc.’s second 5,400-ton transfer stamping press arrived at the West Point, Georgia facility on April 18, 2011.
- The new press will be used to stamp and shape millions of vehicle panels at the $1 billion facility and is a key element of the plant’s ongoing production expansion.
- KMMG will be hiring new team members to support the increased production driven by the new equipment.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development served as the lead agency on the project from the beginning and was critical in all aspects of the site’s development.
- The Georgia Ports Authority facilitated the arrival and discharge of the massive cargo shipment.
- The Georgia Department of Transportation escorted numerous shipments of cargo from Savannah to West Point.
- Georgia Quick Start served as Kia’s workforce development and training partner for the project.
- Governor Nathan Deal stated that the equipment arrival is a fine example of Georgia’s commitment to supporting existing industry for growth, citing resources like Quick Start workforce training and strengthened ports and highways.
- In 2010, Kia Motors recorded its best-ever full-year sales with 356,268 units sold in the U.S., increasing market share for the 16th consecutive year to an all-time high of 3.1 percent.
- Kia set its company’s all-time single month sales record in March (prior to April 2011), topping the previous record by more than 40%.
- The crown assembly and 3,500 tons of additional stamping equipment traveled more than 15,000 miles from Masan, Korea to the Port of Savannah, then across Georgia’s highways and interstates to West Point.
- KMMG is the first manufacturing site in North America for Seoul-based Kia Motors Corporation.
- The plant began mass production of the 2011 Kia Sorento on November 16, 2009.
- At full capacity, the plant will have the ability to build 300,000 vehicles annually using U.S. and globally-sourced parts.
- KMMG Director of Human Resources Randy Jackson thanked local communities in and around Troup County for their cooperation and welcoming environment.
