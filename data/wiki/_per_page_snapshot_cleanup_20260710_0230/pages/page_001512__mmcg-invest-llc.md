## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group is building a $7.6 billion electric vehicle assembly campus in Ellabell, Bryan County.
- The HMGMA megasite spans 2,996 acres with more than 16 million square feet of factory floor.
- HMGMA targets 8,500 direct jobs and a 500,000-vehicle annual production capacity.
- The adjacent HL-GA Battery Company joint venture between Hyundai and LG Energy Solution is a $4.3 billion full-scale plant.
- As of mid-2024, seventeen announced suppliers committed approximately $2.5 billion in capital and roughly 6,900 additional jobs across twelve Georgia counties for the HMGMA cascade.
- HMGMA hired 81 percent of its workforce from within a 60-mile radius per HMGMA Communications.
- The Port of Savannah moved 5.7 million TEUs in fiscal year 2025, an 8.6 percent year-over-year gain.
- Garden City Terminal remains the largest single-terminal container facility in North America.
- Georgia Ports Authority completed $3.2 billion in port infrastructure over the past decade and announced a $4.5 billion ten-year plan including five big-ship berths, the Mason Mega Rail terminal, and inland ports.
- Port-proximate Savannah industrial rents trade at approximately 55 percent above the broader U.S. average.
- Atlanta emerged as the second-largest U.S. data center market with 1,459.2 megawatts operating against just 2 percent vacancy.
- AWS announced an $11 billion Georgia expansion on January 7, 2025, creating at least 550 jobs in Butts and Douglas counties.
- Microsoft, Google, Meta (in Newton County), QTS, Digital Realty, and DC BLOX are expanding in Atlanta's data center market.
- Hyperscale users pre-leased over 300 megawatts in 2025 with another 600 megawatts expected.
- The Georgia Public Service Commission approved Georgia Power's 2025 Integrated Resource Plan on July 15, 2025.
- The PSC ordered a freeze of Georgia Power base rates through 2028 on July 31, 2025.
- On December 19, 2025, the PSC approved an agreement allowing Georgia Power to add 9,885 megawatts of new generation (6,000-megawatt minimum, 8,500-megawatt maximum).
- Georgia Power secured over 8 gigawatts of new generation capacity scheduled to come online after 2030.
- Utility lead times for new substations approach four years in the most constrained corridors.
- The PSC's January 2025 large-load tariff rule requires developers to demonstrate end-user commitments, zoning confirmation, and posted collateral for power procurement.
- Atlanta industrial inventory exceeds 800 million square feet with approximately 18.9 million square feet under construction at year-end 2025.
- Major industrial occupiers include Amazon, FedEx, UPS, The Home Depot (Cobb County HQ), and Lockheed Martin Aeronautics (Cobb County, F-22 and F-35 production).
- Industrial feasibility analysis along the I-285 perimeter and I-75/I-85 outbound corridors must model HMGMA-driven supplier demand spillover from Savannah back through Macon into the metro.
- Anovion Technologies in Bainbridge is building an $800 million synthetic graphite facility for EV batteries.
- Aspen Aerogels in Statesboro announced a $1.1 billion project.
- Hanwha Qcells in Cartersville is building a $2.5 billion solar panel manufacturing facility, the largest North American solar manufacturing facility.
- SK Battery America in Commerce is building a $2.6 billion EV battery cell plant.
- Plant Vogtle in Burke County is the only U.S. nuclear plant with four reactors totaling 4,536 megawatts; Units 3 and 4 entered commercial operation July 31, 2023 and April 29, 2024.
- Combined Vogtle expansion cost reached approximately $34 to 36.8 billion, more than double the original $14 billion estimate.
- Vogtle 3 added approximately $5.42 per month to the average Georgia Power residential bill; Vogtle 4 added approximately $8.95 per month.
- Georgia Power serves approximately 2.7 million customers across 155 of 159 counties.
- Forty-one Electric Membership Corporations serve approximately 5 million Georgia residents across 73 percent of the state's land area on 200,400 miles of distribution lines.
- EMCs jointly own generation share of Plant Vogtle through Oglethorpe Power (30 percent) and Municipal Electric Authority of Georgia (22.7 percent), with Dalton Utilities at 1.6 percent and Georgia Power retaining 45.7 percent.
- Walton EMC supplies Meta's Newton County data center on a 100 percent renewable basis.
- Jackson EMC serves the I-85 NE corridor (Gwinnett through Banks counties) and competes for hyperscale loads.
- Cobb EMC serves the Northwest Georgia region.
- Any large-load deal (5 megawatts and above) requires an early service-territory analysis due to differing power lead times between Georgia Power and EMCs.
- Pooler, Richmond Hill, Guyton, Rincon, and the broader Effingham-Bryan-Bulloch corridor multifamily and rental absorption now move with HMGMA hiring milestones rather than traditional Savannah MSA fundamentals.
- Feasibility studies must phase rent and occupancy ramps to actual production milestones including October 2024 SOP IONIQ 5, March 2025 grand opening, and 2026 to 2027 ramp toward 500,000-unit annual capacity.
- A September 2025 federal immigration enforcement event detained approximately 450 mostly South Korean workers at the HL-GA Battery construction site.
- Georgia is the largest U.S. peanut producer, largest U.S. pecan producer, largest U.S. blueberry producer, one of the top three sweet onion producers (Vidalia), a major poultry processor (largest U.S. broiler-producing state), a leading cotton producer, and a major timber state with 24 million acres of working forestland.
- Georgia's craft brewing and distilling cluster includes more than 130 active breweries plus the Atlanta-headquartered American Distilling Institute member roster.
- Senate Bill 68 and Senate Bill 69 tort reform package was signed by Governor Brian Kemp on April 21, 2025.

- Commercial general liability, commercial auto, and habitational/multifamily premium re-rating typically lags statutory reform by 18 to 30 months.
