## Facts

- Battery company SK Battery America Inc. laid off nearly 1,000 workers at a manufacturing plant northeast of Atlanta on Friday.
- The layoffs occur amid automakers’ changing electrification plans and uncertain consumer demand for EVs.
- Friday marked the last working day for 958 plant employees, about 37% of its workforce.
- A Worker Adjustment and Retraining Notification (WARN) notice detailing the layoffs was filed by human resources chief Chuck Moore.
- Impacted workers will be paid through May 6.
- The plant will continue to employ about 1,600 workers.
- SK opened the $2.6 billion battery plant in Commerce, Georgia, in January 2022.
- The Korean company notably supplied the Ford F-150 Lightning electric pickup truck.
- Ford announced plans to cancel the fully electric version of the truck in December.
