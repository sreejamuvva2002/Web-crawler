# Hyundai-LG Energy Solution Georgia EV Battery Plant Joint Venture

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-06-05

**Source:** [Hyundai, LG Energy to Build $4.3b EV Battery Plant in Georgia](https://www.contractornews.com/677/hyundai-lg-energy-to-build-4-3b-ev-battery-plant-in-georgia)  ·  `page_001233`

## Facts

- Hyundai and LG Energy Solution will collaborate to create a $4.3 billion electric car battery plant in Bryan County, Savannah, Georgia.
- The new plant is expected to create 3,000 jobs.
- The plant site will be adjacent to the currently under-construction Hyundai EV "Meta Plant."
- Both companies signed a joint venture memorandum to produce electric vehicle batteries in the United States to accelerate electrification efforts in North America.
- The agreement was signed at LG Energy Solution's headquarters in Seoul on May 26th.
- Youngsoo Kwon, CEO of LG Energy Solution, and Jaehoon Chang, President and CEO of Hyundai Motor Company, were present at the signing.
- $4.3 billion will be invested in the project, with each company holding a 50% stake in the joint venture.
- The joint venture plans to begin construction on the project in the second half of 2023.
- The plant is scheduled to produce batteries by the end of 2025.
- The yearly production capacity of the plant will be 30GWh and will have the potential to support 300,000 EVs.
- Hyundai believes its annual capacity will increase to 500,000 EV productions later.
- Hyundai Mobis will assemble these cells into battery packs and supply them to Hyundai production units for Kia and Genesis EV models.
- Hyundai and LG Energy Solution have worked together in the EV battery sector since 2009 when Hyundai unveiled its Elantra Hybrid EV.
- LG Energy Solution provided batteries for the Hyundai Elantra and later contributed batteries to the Kona Electric and IONIQ 6 EVs.
- Both companies began constructing a separate $1.1 billion battery cell manufacturing plant in Karawang, Indonesia, in September 2021.
- The Indonesian project is scheduled to begin operating in the first part of 2024.
- Under the US Inflation Reduction Act, EVs must be built in North America with a specific portion of battery components and minerals originating in North America or a US free trade partner to qualify for a $7,500 tax credit.
- Hyundai's current EVs, including Kia and Genesis models, do not qualify for the tax credits as they do not meet domestic manufacturing requirements.
- Democratic US Senator Jon Ossoff of Georgia stated that the IRA's manufacturing incentives continue to bring jobs and investment to Georgia.
- Senator Ossoff added that his goal remains to make Georgia the world leader in advanced energy production.
- Jaehoon Chang stated that the battery plant has the potential to "create a strong foundation to lead the global EV transition."
- Chang further stated that Hyundai Motor Group is focusing on its electrification efforts to secure a leadership position in the global auto industry.
- Youngsoo Kwon stated, "Two strong leaders in the auto and battery Industries have joined hands, and together we are ready to drive the EV transition in America."
- Kwon added that LG Energy Solution will commit its best efforts to offering ultimate sustainable energy solutions by advancing product competitiveness and global operational expertise.
