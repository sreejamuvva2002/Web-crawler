# Technology Integration Program

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [Technology Integration | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/cmei/vehicles/technology-integration)  ·  `page_005006`

## Facts

- The Transportation Technologies Office's (TTO) Technology Integration Program supports a broad technology portfolio that includes alternative fuels, energy efficient mobility systems and technologies, and other efficient advanced technologies to reduce transportation energy costs for businesses and consumers.
- The program provides objective, unbiased data and real-world lessons learned to inform future research needs and support local decision making.
- The program includes projects to disseminate data, information, and insight, as well as online tools and technology assistance to cities and regions working to implement alternative fuels and energy efficient mobility technologies and systems.
- For more than 35 years, the Transportation Technologies Office has sponsored advanced vehicle technology competitions (AVTCs) in partnership with the North American auto industry to educate and develop the next generation of automotive engineers.
- TTO's advanced vehicle technology competitions provide hands-on, real-world experience focusing on science, technology, engineering, and math to support the development of a workforce trained in advanced vehicle technologies.
- The EcoCAR EV Challenge was launched in 2022 as the latest iteration of the advanced vehicle technology competitions.
- The EcoCAR EV Challenge tasked 15 North American universities with engineering a next-generation battery electric vehicle (BEV) that utilizes automation and vehicle-to-everything (V2X) connectivity.
- General Motors provided a 2023 Cadillac LYRIQ for each team to demonstrate the potential of advanced propulsion systems, connected and automated vehicle (CAV) technologies, and other innovative technologies to analyze energy efficiency.
- The Battery Workforce Challenge was launched in 2023 as a three-year engineering competition.
- The Battery Workforce Challenge challenges North American universities and their community college partners to design, build, test, and integrate an advanced EV battery pack into a 2024 Ram ProMaster EV from Stellantis.
- The Alternative Fuels Regulatory activity provides technical and analytical support for the implementation of federal legislation related to the deployment of alternative fuels and fuel-efficient fleet vehicles.
- Relevant federal legislation includes the Energy Policy Act (EPAct) of 1992, EPAct 2005, the Energy Conservation Reauthorization Act of 1998, the Energy Independence and Security Act (EISA) of 2007, and other amendments to EPAct.
- EPAct regulated fleets include State & Alternative Fuel Provider Fleets and Federal Fleets managed by the Federal Energy Management Program.
