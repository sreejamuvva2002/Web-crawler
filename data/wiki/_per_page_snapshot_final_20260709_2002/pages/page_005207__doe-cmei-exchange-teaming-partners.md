## Facts

- UC Davis Professor Francis Assadian serves as Director of the Future Mobility Lab, researching energy management and advanced control systems for alternative vehicle powertrains including fuel cell electric powertrains, with interest in reformer optimization for hydrogen production efficiency (CA).
- Argonne National Laboratory Vehicle Technology Evaluation Lead Ram Vijayagopal leads a team using modeling and simulation to evaluate techno-economic viability of new technologies, utilizing the widely licensed Autonomie vehicle simulation tool for projects like Supertruck and consortiums 21CTP & USDRIVE (IL).
- Northeastern University Distinguished Professor Sanjeev Mukerjee focuses on implementing corrosion-resistant electrocatalysts with core-shell Nb/Ta supports to achieve MEAs capable of 1,000,000-mile life expectancy and low precious group metal loading under 0.2mg/cm2 (MA).
- Great Plains Institute Senior Policy Manager Val Stori facilitates hydrogen market development and community acceptance with state energy offices and disadvantaged communities, supporting clean energy jobs, workforce development, environmental justice, and DEIA initiatives across the Upper Midwest (MN).
- Hago Energetics Benefit Corporation CEO Wilson Hago leads a low carbon hydrogen producer creating carbon negative, fuel cell grade hydrogen from waste materials, expressing interest in motorcoach industry development (CA).
- NCSU NC Clean Energy Technology Center Sr. Project Manager Shelly Parker manages the Piedmont & Coastal NC Clean Communities DOE Clean Cities program extension, focusing on disadvantaged communities in mid-sized/rural NC, managing CMAQ grants, and providing workforce training for clean transportation (NC).
- Award Advisors Principal Dan Durst provides specialized consulting for federal grant processes, specializing in SF 424/DOE Budget Justification Worksheet completion, indirect cost rate calculation/negotiation, and post-award compliance with 2 CFR 200 Uniform Guidance (VA).
- Questek Innovations LLC Director of Federal Solutions Keith C Fritz leads a company founded in 1997 in Evanston, IL, using Integrated Computational Materials Engineering (ICME) and the Materials by Design approach to develop high-performance alloys and hydrogen-related materials for aerospace, automotive, energy, and defense sectors (IL).
- University of Oklahoma Assistant Professor Ahmad Al-Douri specializes in chemical process safety and hydrogen systems quantitative risk assessment, having conducted full QRA studies for fuel cell forklifts and electrolysis systems (OK).
- University of North Dakota Associate Professor Hadi Jabbari advances hydrogen transportation under Topic 2A through FRP materials development, Frontal Polymerization manufacturing, ML/AI monitoring, sensor technology, and mechanical testing, leveraging the Heartland Hydrogen Hub network with UW and LSU (ND).
- CATE PLLC Owner Azize Akcayoglu runs an engineering consulting firm with over 30 years of academic experience, offering expertise in thermal/fluid sciences, CFD/FEA modeling, HVAC&R optimization, renewable energy systems, and extensive mechanical/material testing equipment operation (TX).
- Hawaii Natural Energy Institute Assistant Researcher Tatyana Reshetenko provides PEM fuel cell testing, contaminant tolerance prediction, UAV/UUV fuel cell systems for Navy applications, and catalyst synthesis capabilities, including 6 test stations for cells/stacks up to 2kW and production lab scale membrane/electrode fabrication (HI).
- Delcotek LLC Principal Consultant Caroline Del Core offers government grant funding strategy and proposal services, with over 12 years of experience leading battery and hydrogen fuel cell proposals selected for awards totaling over $85 million from DOE, CA Energy Commission, CARB, and others (CA).
- Physics Inverted Materials Inc. CEO Emil Annevelink develops machine learning accelerated simulation technology to predict material properties, seeking partnerships on Topics 2 or 3 to accelerate new materials development for hydrogen storage/transport or PEM/ionomers (PA).
- AVL Mobility Technologies Inc Electrification Segment Lead Ben Playfoot provides e-mobility and fuel cell system solutions across component engineering, test equipment, and commercial software, with recent US projects including Class 6/8 FCEV integration and H2 Data Center backup energy demonstrations (MI).
- Missouri University of Science and Technology Associate Professor Pourya Shamsi offers capabilities in electric power trains, motor drives, energy management, hydrogen-battery hybridization, and leads a center dedicated to hydrogen and fuel cell research (MO).
- Clemson Composites Center Associate Director Saeed Farahani provides composite materials engineering, large-scale parts design/manufacturing, characterization/testing, and tooling system development for high-pressure/cryogenic hydrogen service applications (SC).
- Strategic Analysis Inc. Principal Engineer Mark Jensen specializes in techno-economic analysis, market studies, and capital/life-cycle cost estimation for hydrogen production/storage, fuel cells, batteries, and carbon capture, utilizing Aspen Plus/HYSYS and DFMA tools to model H2 $/kg and electricity $/kWh costs (VA).
- Pratt & Miller Engineering & Fabrication LLC Sr. Chief Engineer Robert Prohaska leads full-vehicle system integration for electrification and hydrogen technologies, aiming to integrate fuel cell tech into motorcoach platforms with experience spanning Class 8 trucks, autonomous vehicles, and defense platforms (MI).
- American Bureau of Shipping Director Kirk H. Waltz provides verification and certification services including New Technology Qualification, Statement of Fact inspections, and Type Approval, serving as a Recognized Standards Organization for maritime heavy-duty applications and US Coast Guard compliance (TX).
- Pacific Northwest National Laboratory Sr. Advisor Jamie Holladay leads high-performance materials development for hydrogen storage/distribution, featuring lightweight structural materials, solid-phase processing, composite tank optimization, and advanced testing capabilities including servohydraulic frames up to 500,000 lbs and temperature ranges from cryogenic to 1200°C (IL).
- Shell Catalysts and Technologies Senior Principal Scientist Michael A. Reynolds utilizes a unique cryogenic reactor capability to catalytically convert ortho-hydrogen to para-hydrogen for liquefaction down to 20 K (-253 C), with expertise in catalyst development and testing (TX).
- GenCell In Grant Manager Rich Hernandez represents a global leader in hydrogen and ammonia-based fuel cell solutions, offering EVOX and REX systems for long-duration energy storage, off-grid EV charging, and microgrid integration across telecom, healthcare, and disaster recovery sectors (CA).
- RockeTruck Inc President & CEO Michael Simon developed an advanced fuel cell power generation system using a new automotive fuel cell co-developed with a global passenger car OEM to increase motor coach vehicle efficiency and range while reducing costs.
