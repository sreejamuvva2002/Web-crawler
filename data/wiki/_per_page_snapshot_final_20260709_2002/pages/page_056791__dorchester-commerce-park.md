# Dorchester Commerce Park

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2023-12-13

**Source:** [Flint Development Breaks Ground on 2.1 MSF Distribution Project Near Savannah - Uneoth.com](https://uneoth.com/flint-development-breaks-ground-on-2-1-msf-distribution-project-near-savannah/)  ·  `page_056791`

## Facts

- Construction has begun on the 2.1 million-square-foot, three-building Dorchester Commerce Park in Midway, Liberty County, Ga., about 30 miles southwest of the Port of Savannah.
- Flint Development is the owner of the speculative Class A project.
- Caddell Construction is the general contractor.
- The development engages Davidson Architecture + Engineering for architecture and engineering services.
- CBRE serves as the leasing broker.
- Building 3 is scheduled for completion in April 2024.
- The project will fully deliver by spring 2025.
- All three buildings will feature tilt-up construction, high-bay LED lighting, exterior LED lighting, and ESFR sprinkler systems.
- The site location is adjacent to I-95 at its interchange with U.S. 84.
- Building 1 will total 1.2 million square feet and have 566 trailer spaces and 458 car parking spaces.
- Building 1 will be the final structure completed in 2025.
- Building 2 is a cross-dock facility totaling 594,522 square feet of divisible space with 189 trailer spaces and 300 car parking spaces.
- Building 2 is expected to be delivered next July.
- Building 3 will total 276,062 square feet of divisible space with 32 trailer spaces and 132 car parking spaces.
- The Savannah region’s industrial space market remains notably solid despite overall vacancy creeping up to 7.6 percent per a third-quarter report from CBRE.
- Absorption in the Savannah region compares well with recent years and average rents are up.
- Very substantial deliveries exceeded 6 million square feet in the quarter but are expected to moderate soon.
- Growth is driven by tenants attracted to the Port of Savannah, the nation’s third-busiest and fastest-growing port since the completion of a $1.4 billion expansion in March 2022.
- The Georgia Ports Authority is underway on further projects intended to boost container capacity from the current 6.0 million TEUs to 9.5 million by 2026.
- Kyungshin America Corp recently committed to building a 136,000-square-foot facility at The Broe Group’s Savannah Gateway Industrial Hub in Rincon, Ga.
- In late November, Plastic Express leased all of a 982,800-square-foot building under construction at Capital Development Partners’ Central Port Logistics Center in Savannah.
- In October, joint venture partners Stonemont Financial Group and The Davis Cos. delivered two buildings totaling 2.7 million square feet at their Georgia International Trade Center in Rincon.
- In late September, Lincoln Property Co. announced the imminent groundbreaking on its Bloomingdale Trade Center, a 482,350-square-foot distribution facility in Savannah.
