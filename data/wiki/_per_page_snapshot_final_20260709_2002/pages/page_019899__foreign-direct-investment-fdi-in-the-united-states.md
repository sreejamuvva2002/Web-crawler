# Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) in the United States

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Foreign Direct Investment in the US on the Rise](https://camoinassociates.com/resources/foreign-direct-investment-in-the-us/)  ·  `page_019899`

## Facts

- Year-end foreign direct investment (FDI) numbers for 2022 show strong interest in expansion within the United States.
- Top investing countries for FDI in the US include Japan, Germany, Canada, the United Kingdom, Ireland, and France.
- International companies are attracted to invest in the United States due to overall positive business climates, availability of diverse workforce and higher education programs, reasonable regulatory environments and business-friendly tax structures, efficient freight options and solid transportation infrastructures, access to a strong and robust consumer market, and strong concentration of industry clusters.
- Global events including the COVID-19 pandemic, Brexit, US tensions with China, and the Russia-Ukraine war created significant supply chain backlogs across several industries.
- Many decision makers planning to invest in or expand in the United States are doing so to achieve better proximity to customers and overcome current supply chain and logistics issues.
- Continued increases in FDI announcements could help communities struggling with workforce concerns by creating more opportunities in higher education, technical skills, workforce training, and certification programs as expanding companies provide details on labor needs requiring specialized skills.
- Effective tools for attracting foreign direct investment include international trade missions, targeted conferences, and communications that highlight regional advantages and incentives.
- Economic development organizations are advised to incorporate international prospecting strategies into their budgets, such as attending the SelectUSA Investment Summit, to better identify companies looking to invest in the United States.
- Canada-based IKO Industries, a roofing products manufacturer, is establishing its first South Carolina operations in Chester County.
- Finland-based Kempower, a manufacturer of EV charging equipment, announced it would open its first US facility this year in Durham County, NC.
- Finland-based Nokian Tyres, a global manufacturer of vehicle tires, announced it will double the production capacity of its eastern Tennessee plant and established its first US plant in Dayton, TN, in 2019.
- France-based Tageos, a manufacturer of high-tech RFID inlays and tags, announced it will establish its North American headquarters and first manufacturing site in the US in Fletcher, NC.
- Japan-based SkyDrive Inc., an electric vertical take-off and landing aircraft manufacturer, has plans to enter the US market and establish its home base in South Carolina.
- Portugal/Brazil-based MSS Steel Tubes, a manufacturer of steel tubing owned by Portuguese industrial company Metalogalva Group and Brazilian steelmaker Soufer Group, announced plans to establish its first US operations in Memphis, TN.
- South Korea-based Sewon America, a subsidiary of Sewon Precision Industry Co., will establish a production facility in Rincon, GA.
- South Korea-based Samkee Corp., a manufacturer of aluminum automotive components, plans to establish its first US operations in Tuskegee, AL.
- South Korea-based Seoyon E-HWA, a global auto parts supplier that manufactures both interior and exterior components, will establish a new manufacturing facility in Chatham County, GA, which is its second facility in the state.
- South Korea-based Ecoplastic Corporation, a company that manufactures plastic automobile parts for original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) and other suppliers, will invest in a new manufacturing facility in Bulloch County, GA.
