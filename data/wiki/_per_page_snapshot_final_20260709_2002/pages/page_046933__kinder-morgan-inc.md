# Kinder Morgan Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-06

**Source:** [Kinder Morgan Expanding Southeast Natural Gas Pipeline, Citing ‘Jaw Dropping’ Expectations for Power Demand](https://www.naturalgasintel.com/news/kinder-morgan-expanding-southeast-natural-gas-pipeline-citing-jaw-dropping-expectations-for-power-demand/)  ·  `page_046933`

## Facts

- Pipeline operator Kinder Morgan Inc. (KMI) is moving ahead with a major natural gas pipeline expansion to serve growing demand from the electric power sector in the Southeast.
- KMI completed an open season for the proposed $3 billion South System 4 Expansion on the Southern Natural Gas Co. LLC pipeline system during the quarter.
- The open season secured 20-year take or pay contracts with creditworthy shippers.
- The South System 4 expansion project is targeted for in-service in late 2028.
- The South System 4 expansion would increase capacity by 1.2 Bcf/d.
- CEO Kim Dang stated that upon completion, the project will help to meet the growing power demand and local distribution company demand in the southeastern markets.
- The South System 4 expansion makes up the lion’s share of a $1.9 billion increase to KMI’s backlog at the end of the second quarter.
- KMI’s backlog rose to $5.2 billion from $3.3 billion in the first quarter.
- KMI is in commercial discussions for more than 5 Bcf/d of incremental midstream capacity related to power demand.
- Of that incremental capacity, 1.6 Bcf/d is targeted for data centers.
- CEO Kim Dang noted that while certainly not all of these projects will come to fruition, the activity level supports a belief that growth in natural gas between now and 2030 will be well in excess of 20 Bcf/d.
- Executive Chairman Richard Kinder stated that consensus on the expected electric demand growth for artificial intelligence (AI) and data centers from power consumers, utilities, and regulators has been “jaw dropping.”
- Annual electricity demand growth over the last 20 years has averaged around 0.5%.
- Industry experts have predicted annual electricity demand growth from now until 2030 at a range of 2.6%.
- One projection estimates annual electricity demand growth from now until 2030 at an amazing 4.7%.
- KMI transports about 40% of the natural gas consumed in the United States.
- KMI expects natural gas to play an important role along with renewables and dedicated nuclear power to help the industry meet the needed 24/7 reliability.
- Executive Chairman Richard Kinder noted that ERCOT has estimated Texas would need 152 GW of power generation by 2030.
- This Texas estimate is up from last year’s estimate of 111 GW.
- The 152 GW estimate represents a 78% increase from 2023 peak power demand of about 85 GW in Texas.
- The Texas Energy Fund, a program that would extend low-cost loans for new natural gas-fired generating facilities, was massively oversubscribed.
- Requests to the Texas Energy Fund could finance 55,908 MW of proposed dispatchable power generation projects for the state’s electric grid.
- Kinder stated that this oversubscription is clear evidence that generators are projecting increased demand for natural gas-fired facilities.
- KMI believes the additional fuel needed to supply the surge in power generation will be significant and makes the future even more robust for natural gas demand overall and for its midstream industry.
- Natural gas gathering volumes were up 10% year/year in the quarter, according to President Tom Martin.
- The volume increase was driven by Haynesville shale volumes, which were up 21%.
- The volume increase was also driven by Eagle Ford shale volumes, which were up 8%.
- KMI is lowering its guidance for gathering volumes for the remainder of the year as a result of low gas prices.
- Given the current gas price environment, KMI expects gathering volumes to average about 6% below its 2024 plan.
- Despite the pullback, KMI expects gathering volumes to still be 8% over 2023.
- NGI’s Henry Hub bidweek price averaged $1.890/MMBtu in 2Q2024.
- NGI’s Henry Hub bidweek price was $2.096 in 2Q2023.
- KMI views the slight pullback in gathering volumes as temporary.
- Higher production volumes will be necessary to meet demand growth from LNG expected in 2025.
- KMI reported net income of $575 million (26 cents/share) in the second quarter.
- This net income is roughly flat with profits of $586 million (26 cents) in 2Q2023.
- Revenues totaled $3.57 billion in the second quarter.
- Revenues were up $71 million from the second quarter of last year.
