# HL-GA Battery Company

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [U.S. detains over 30 Korean workers tied to Hyundai–LG battery facility in Georgia](https://v.daum.net/v/20250905151347104)  ·  `page_050064`

## Facts

- More than 30 Korean employees on business trips to the United States were detained by immigration authorities at a joint battery factory operated by Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution in Georgia.
- U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) and Homeland Security Investigations (HSI) conducted a raid Thursday morning at the HL-GA Battery Company construction site in Ellabell, near Savannah, Georgia.
- Most of those detained were team leaders or higher-level employees from LG Energy Solution's headquarters in Korea.
- The detained workers reportedly entered the United States under B-1 business visas or through the Electronic System for Travel Authorization (ESTA) and were officially registered as business travelers.
- Authorities suspect they were directly involved in installing factory equipment and preparing for production, which are activities not permitted under those visa categories.
- ICE and HSI were initially investigating the presence of undocumented foreign workers near the construction site before discovering the Korean nationals engaging in work that allegedly violated their visa terms.
- The Korean Embassy in Washington confirmed the detainment of more than 30 Korean nationals.
- LG Energy Solution spokesperson stated: “Construction has been suspended to cooperate with the investigation, and we plan to fully cooperate with authorities.”
- LG Energy Solution added: “We are still working to confirm the exact number of detainees and whether they remain in custody.”
- In September 2020, 13 Korean workers employed at an SK Battery America plant were detained by ICE for illegal employment after entering on ESTA.
- In May 2020, another 33 Koreans were denied entry and deported at the airport.
- Rep. Doug Collins (Republican from Georgia) criticized Korean companies for illegally hiring their own nationals instead of local residents and called for a full investigation by ICE and Customs and Border Protection.
- SK Battery America responded to past incidents by tightening worker identification procedures and increasing local hiring efforts.
- A business community source noted: “Obtaining employment visas such as H-1B can take several months in the United States. Some companies resort to sending staff on short-term business or ESTA visas to fill urgent labor shortages.”
- The same source stated: “Whether the workers’ activities align with their visa status has become a key issue in crackdowns. Coordination between governments and systemic reform is needed.”
- The Trump administration has ramped up immigration enforcement following its return to power.
- ICE reported that in the first 200 days of the new administration, approximately 359,000 undocumented immigrants were arrested, and about 332,000 were deported.
