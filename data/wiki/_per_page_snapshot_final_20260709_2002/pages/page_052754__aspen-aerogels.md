# Aspen Aerogels

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** thermal_management  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Baystreet.ca - Aspen Aerogels stock jumps on DOE loan commitment for new plant](https://www.baystreet.ca/investing/104185/Aspen-Aerogels-stock-jumps-on-DOE-loan-commitment-for-new-plant)  ·  `page_052754`

## Facts

- Aspen Aerogels shares surged more than 13% Wednesday following the announcement of a major funding development and strong preliminary Q3 2024 results.
- The company secured a conditional commitment from the U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) for a loan of up to $670.6 million.
- The funding is part of the Advanced Technology Vehicles Manufacturing loan program.
- The funding will finance the construction of Aspen's second aerogel manufacturing facility in Register, Georgia.
- The new plant will provide a revenue capacity between $1.2 billion and $1.6 billion.
- Revenue will be driven by the production of PyroThin thermal barriers designed to enhance battery safety for electric vehicles (EVs).
- Aspen CEO Donald Young stated the proposed loan would fully fund the required CAPEX for the second aerogel manufacturing facility.
- The facility is expected to play a key role in scaling PyroThin supply for Aspen's rapidly growing Thermal Barrier business.
- Aspen's PyroThin product has attracted six major OEM contracts in the U.S. and Europe.
- PyroThin contributed to a 277% year-over-year growth in thermal barrier revenues.
- Aspen reported preliminary Q3 financial results of $117 million in revenue and $25 million in adjusted EBITDA.
- The company posted a net loss of $13 million, impacted by a one-time $27.5 million charge from the extinguishment of a convertible note.
- Chief Financial Officer Ricardo Rodriguez stated the proposed loan and its terms will provide an opportunity to optimize capital structure if finalized.
- The Register Plant project is expected to create 255 permanent jobs.
- The project aligns with the DOE's goal of supporting domestic manufacturing for zero-emissions vehicles.
