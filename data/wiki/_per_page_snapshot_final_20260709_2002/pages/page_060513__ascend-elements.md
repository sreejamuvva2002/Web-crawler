# Ascend Elements

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2023-06-08

**Source:** [Ascend Elements Transforms Battery Industry with Sustainable, High-Nickel Cathode Manufacturing Technology - The EV Report](https://theevreport.com/ascend-elements-transforms-battery-industry-with-sustainable-high-nickel-cathode-manufacturing-technology)  ·  `page_060513`

## Facts

- Ascend Elements is a Westborough, Mass. based company.
- Ascend Elements is on the brink of becoming the top domestic provider of sustainable, engineered battery materials in North America.
- Ascend Elements utilizes a revolutionary recycling process and patented cathode engineering technology.
- These advancements position Ascend to become the leading supplier of high-nickel, NMC cathode precursor (pCAM) to the rapidly expanding lithium-ion battery industry in North America.
- Ascend Elements recently inked a multi-year deal to provide around $1 billion worth of sustainable pCAM for a leading U.S. company’s battery manufacturing process.
- The multi-year deal kicks off in Q4 2024.
- The agreement terms provide the customer the option to expand the contract up to $5 billion.
- Ascend Elements is constructing one of North America’s first large-scale NMC pCAM production facilities in southwest Kentucky.
- Mike O’Kronley serves as the CEO of Ascend Elements.
- Mike O’Kronley pointed out that almost 100% of the world’s pCAM is manufactured in Asia.
- Mike O’Kronley believes the United States needs to manufacture its own critical battery materials to secure the supply chain in North America, cut down on carbon emissions, and maintain energy independence.
- The U.S. Department of Energy granted Ascend Elements two matching funds totaling $480 million in October 2022 to speed up construction of the southwest Kentucky facility.
- Ascend Elements intends to invest over $1 billion in this project.
- The Hopkinsville, Kentucky-based Ascend Elements facility will serve as a unique, sustainable cathode manufacturing plant.
- The facility will have the ability to produce NMC pCAM for up to 750,000 electric vehicles annually.
- Ascend Elements uses its patented Hydro-to-Cathode® direct precursor synthesis process to manufacture NMC pCAM and cathode active material (CAM) from used lithium-ion batteries and battery gigafactory manufacturing scrap.
- This closed-loop process eliminates several intermediary steps in the traditional cathode manufacturing process.
- Peer-reviewed studies confirm that Ascend Elements’ recycled battery materials perform on par with materials derived from virgin or mined sources while significantly reducing carbon emissions related to mining.
- The company's process aims to reduce the number of batteries ending up in landfills.
