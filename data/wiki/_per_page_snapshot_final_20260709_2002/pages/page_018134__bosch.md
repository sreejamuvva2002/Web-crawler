## Facts

- Bosch announced it will slash 13,000 jobs primarily in its automotive division.
- The layoffs are driven by faltering demand, fierce Chinese competition, and a sluggish transition to electric vehicles (EVs).
- The announcement was made on Thursday, Sept. 25.
- Bosch cited a need to save €2.5 billion ($2.92 billion) annually in its mobility division.
- The company framed the move as a painful but necessary adaptation to shifting demand.
- Stefan Grosch serves as Bosch's labor director.
- Labor director Stefan Grosch stated, "We need to orient ourselves to where our markets and customers are."
- The announcement acknowledges the accelerating exodus of business from Europe.
- All layoffs are located in Germany and represent 10 percent of Bosch's domestic workforce.
- Competitors Schaeffler and Continental have already shed thousands of jobs amid similar industry struggles.
- Automakers Volkswagen and Porsche source parts from Bosch.
- Volkswagen and Porsche are scaling back EV investments due to weak consumer uptake.
- Volkswagen plans to eliminate up to 30,000 positions alongside a massive restructuring.
- The job cuts highlight the deepening crisis facing German manufacturers as global trade barriers rise and key markets pivot toward local suppliers.
- China now favors domestic brands like BYD, squeezing profit margins in a brutal price war.
- The EV transition has stalled in Europe, leaving manufacturers burdened with excess capacity.
