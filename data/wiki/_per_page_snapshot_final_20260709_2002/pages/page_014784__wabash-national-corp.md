# Wabash National Corp.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2012-03-28

**Source:** [Wabash National shares rise on $360M deal – Indianapolis Business Journal](https://www.ibj.com/articles/33490-wabash-national-shares-rise-on-360m-deal)  ·  `page_014784`

## Facts

- Shares of Indiana-based Wabash National Corp. jumped after the maker of truck trailers announced it would diversify its business by purchasing liquid-transportation company Walker Group Holdings LLC for $360 million.
- Privately held Walker Group is based in New Lisbon, Wis., and owned by Dallas-based private equity firm Insight Equity Holdings LLC.
- Wabash, headquartered in Lafayette, obtained financing from Morgan Stanley and Wells Fargo for up to $450 million to fund the deal and provide additional money.
- The deal is subject to regulatory approval and is expected to close during the second quarter.
- Shares of Wabash jumped as much as 12.8 percent in afternoon trading before closing up 9.5 percent, to $10.71 each.
- Wabash's stock was down from its 52-week high of $11.99 and had fallen sharply over the summer due to broader market tumbles and recession fears after trading above $11.50 last spring.
- The stock fell as low as $4.22 per share in early October.
- Wabash stated the deal will help it offer new products to its customers and immediately start adding to its profits, noting that the company makes heavy trailers used by freight haulers and semi-trucks.
- Wabash's stock performance has been negatively impacted in recent years as high fuel prices and weak economic growth have squeezed over-the-road freight haulers.
- Walker Group makes trailers that can carry liquids like dairy products, chemicals, or fuel.
- The deal will give Wabash a new source of revenue and a business with fatter profit margins.
- Last year, Wabash National posted a profit of $15 million on revenue of $1.19 billion, which was better than the prior year when the company reported a net loss of $167.2 million on revenue of $640.4 million.
- During its 2011 fiscal year, Walker Group Holdings generated a profit of $28.2 million on revenue of $344 million.
- Sterne Agee Group Inc. analyst Jeff Kauffman said the deal could boost Wabash National's profits.
- Kauffman noted that Walker Group Holdings is a market leader in the liquid tanker business, with an adjusted profit margin before taxes and other factors of about 15 percent to 16 percent last year.
- By contrast, Wabash National's estimated profit margin before taxes and other factors for 2012 is 7.2 percent, according to Kauffman.
