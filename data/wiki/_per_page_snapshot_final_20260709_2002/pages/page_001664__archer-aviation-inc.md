# Archer Aviation Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-06

**Source:** [Archer Aviation Selects Site in Georgia Adjacent to the Covington Municipal Airport for its Manufacturing Facility - Green Stock News](https://greenstocknews.com/news/nyse/achr/archer-aviation-selects-site-in-georgia-adjacent-to-the-covington-municipal-airport-for-its-manufacturing-facility)  ·  `page_001664`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation Inc. (NYSE: ACHR) announced plans to locate its manufacturing facility in Covington, Georgia at a site adjacent to the Covington Municipal Airport.
- Archer plans to initially build out a 350,000 square-foot facility on a 96-acre site.
- The initial facility will be capable of producing up to 650 aircraft per year.
- The planned eVTOL aircraft manufacturing facility is expected to create over 1,000 jobs over the long term.
- Construction is expected to be completed in the first half of 2024.
- Initial production is expected to begin in the second half of 2024.
- The facility will be capable of being expanded by an additional 550,000 square feet.
- The expansion is estimated to support production of up to 2,300 aircraft per year.
- Archer completed a comprehensive survey of potential sites across the United States to select the location for its first manufacturing facility.
- Key considerations for site selection included the availability of talent in the local labor market, utility availability, ability to conduct seamless flight test operations, construction costs, and logistics.
- Archer will receive an incentive package that includes land conveyance, tax incentives, and a Georgia REBA grant.
- Financing for the project is anticipated to be provided by Synovus, the largest bank headquartered in Georgia.
- Adam Goldstein serves as Archer's CEO.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp, Bob Kosek, Sara Gershon, Serra Hall, and Frank Turner were involved in the site selection process.
- Georgia's established leadership in the aerospace industry was essential to landing Archer Aviation in Newton County.
- Archer can take advantage of Georgia's signature workforce training program Quick Start to help fill over 1,000 long-term jobs in Covington.
- Georgia is home to more than 800 aerospace companies.
- The state maintains a robust aerospace education pipeline that includes a dozen high schools offering training, five technical colleges with aviation programs, and several universities offering degrees in aerospace engineering.
- The Georgia Center of Innovation for Aerospace, a strategic arm of the Georgia Department of Economic Development, has been cultivating an ecosystem of opportunities for innovative companies like Archer for more than 10 years.
- Archer is designing and developing electric vertical takeoff and landing aircraft for use in urban air mobility networks.
- Archer's team is based in Santa Clara, CA.
