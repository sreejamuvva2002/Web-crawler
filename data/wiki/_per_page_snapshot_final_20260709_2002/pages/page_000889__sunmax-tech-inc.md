# Sunmax Tech Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2024-05-01

**Source:** [Energy manufacturer Sunmax Tech chooses Georgia for operation base - Southern Business & Development](https://southernbusiness.com/energy-manufacturer-sunmax-tech-chooses-georgia-for-operation-base/)  ·  `page_000889`

## Facts

- Sunmax Tech Inc., an energy component manufacturer, is opening a 300,000-square-foot manufacturing operation base in Adairsville.
- The company is a supplier for Tesla and Hanwha, the parent company of QCells.
- Hanwha/QCells also has factories in North Georgia.
- Sunmax Tech plans to invest a total of $193 million over a three-phased plan.
- The investment will create at least 242 new jobs.
- Kristi Brigman, Georgia Department of Economic Development deputy commissioner of global commerce, stated that Georgia’s growing solar panel manufacturing industry is creating jobs and opportunities for many communities, especially in Northwest Georgia.
- During the first phase, Sunmax Tech will buy equipment for solar junction boxes and cables.
- In the second phase it will expand its production line to create EVA film, cable line plastic material, aluminum solar frames and copper wire.
- The company plans to do $100 million in annual sales by 2027.
- Sunmax Tech aims to capture 40% of the photovoltaic junction box and connectors market share in North America.
