# Hyundai Mobis

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** power_electronics  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hyundai Mobis building $926M Georgia EV power system factory](https://theconstructionbroadsheet.com/hyundai-mobis-building-m-georgia-ev-power-system-factory-p1137-174.htm)  ·  `page_021277`

## Facts

- South Korean auto supplier Hyundai Mobis will invest $926 million in an electric vehicle (EV) power electric system plant.
- The new plant will be located in Richmond Hill, Bryan County, Georgia.
- At full capacity, the new facility will span 1.2 million square feet.
- At full capacity, the facility will produce more than 900,000 power electric systems each year.
- At full capacity, the facility will produce 450,000 integrated charging control units each year.
- The new facility's production will supply Hyundai Motor Group's $5.5 billion megaplant in Bryan County, Georgia.
- The new facility's production will also supply Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Alabama in Montgomery, Alabama.
- The new facility's production will also supply the Kia Georgia plant in West Point, Georgia.
- Hyundai Mobis is a Tier 1 supplier to Kia Georgia.
- Hyundai Mobis already operates a manufacturing facility in West Point, Georgia.
- The existing West Point facility supplies Kia and other automakers with cockpit modules, chassis modules, and bumper assemblies.
- Construction on the new Hyundai Mobis plant could begin as early as January.
- The new plant will be located at a Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) Certified industrial park.
- Production at the new facility is expected to begin in 2024.
- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp stated that Hyundai Mobis is the second supplier announced in just two weeks to locate in the Bryan County area.
- Automotive parts manufacturer Joon Georgia, a division of Ajin USA, said it would build a factory near Hyundai Motor Group's new operation in Bryan County.
- The state expects the Joon Georgia factory to be the first of $1 billion in third-party supplier investment near Hyundai's new operations.
- Ajin is already a Hyundai Motor Group supplier.
- Joon Georgia expects to be in production in 2024.
- Since 2020, Georgia officials said more than 30 EV-related projects have committed more than $13.3 billion of investments in Georgia.
