# TI Fluid Systems

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** thermal_management  ·  **Published:** 2024-06-26

**Source:** [TI Fluid Systems' New Michigan e-Mobility Innovation Center to Fast-track North American EV Development](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/ti-fluid-systems-new-michigan-e-mobility-innovation-center-to-fast-track-north-american-ev-development-302183298.html)  ·  `page_003452`

## Facts

- TI Fluid Systems officially opened its all-new e-Mobility Innovation Center (eMIC) in Auburn Hills, Michigan on June 26, 2024.
- The Auburn Hills eMIC is designed to nurture customer collaboration for developing thermal management systems for hybrid, plug-in hybrid, and battery electric vehicles.
- The new facility spans 48,000 square feet.
- The facility was constructed around TI Fluid Systems' Core Competency model, combining virtual engineering, designing, processing, prototyping, product testing, and vehicle testing under one roof.
- This integrated approach reduces development time for prototype thermal management components and systems from six months to as little as two weeks.
- The new methods can achieve system-weight reductions of up to 60 percent.
- The new methods can achieve overall cost savings of up to 30 percent.
- The facility features equipment enabling rapid prototyping of fully functioning components and systems using 3D-printing and injection molding.
- The facility includes two vehicle lifts and 42 electric vehicle charging stations.
- Hans Dieltjens serves as the president and CEO of TI Fluid Systems.
- Hans Dieltjens stated that the Auburn Hills eMIC represents a significant step forward in automotive thermal management development in North America, enabling faster development, collaboration, and innovation crucial for hybrid, plug-in hybrid, and battery electric vehicles.
- The Auburn Hills facility is one of five eMICs opened worldwide since 2022.
- The other four eMICs are located in key regions for automotive and e-Mobility development: China, Germany, Japan, South Korea, and the USA.
- Hans Dieltjens noted that constructing five eMICs in key e-Mobility development regions underscores TI Fluid Systems' commitment to supporting customer competitiveness in a fast-changing automotive landscape.
- The construction of the eMICs highlights how an innovative approach to thermal management impacts the efficiency, performance, and cost of electric vehicles.
- TI Fluid Systems is listed on the London Stock Exchange under the ticker TIFS.
- TI Fluid Systems has its corporate headquarters in Auburn Hills, Michigan.
- TI Fluid Systems employs 8,600 people globally.
- TI Fluid Systems operates 21 manufacturing locations in the Americas, including 12 locations in the United States.
- Alongside thermal management solutions for hybrid, plug-in hybrid, and battery electric vehicles, TI Fluid Systems is a leading global provider of fuel systems, lines and connectors, and thermal management solutions for internal combustion engine vehicles.
- TI Fluid Systems serves all major automotive manufacturers with more than 100 years of automotive supply experience.
- TI Fluid Systems operates across 27 countries worldwide.
