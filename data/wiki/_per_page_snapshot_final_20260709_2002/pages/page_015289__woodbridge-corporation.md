# Woodbridge Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2024-11-25

**Source:** [UAW holds first strike ever at Georgia automotive parts plant – The Militant](https://themilitant.com/2024/11/16/uaw-holds-first-strike-ever-at-georgia-automotive-parts-plant/)  ·  `page_015289`

## Facts

- Located in Lithonia, Ga., the Woodbridge Corp. plant is the site of the first strike in UAW Local 472's history.
- Approximately 70 union members walked out on Oct. 30 after voting by 98% to authorize strike action in August.
- Workers at the facility produce seating and dashboard components for Yamaha and Nissan cars and golf carts.
- Strikers report that Woodbridge is hiring temporary workers and attempting to keep one shift operating during the walkout.
- The company implemented a two-tier pay system in 2009, which union members are actively trying to eliminate.
- Under the current pay structure, new hires start at approximately $15 an hour.
- Top pay for a production job at the plant is capped at $20.50 an hour.
- Workers cite forced overtime and understaffing as major grievances, noting that employees are sometimes sent home after only four or five hours when work slows down.
- Union representatives include Myra Williams, Shawn Milligan (second shift representative), Neal Turner (31-year veteran), and Keith Walker (34-year veteran).
- The workforce is predominantly female, with union members originating from Nigeria, Ghana, Ethiopia, and Cuba.
- Strikers are demanding better pay and benefits, an end to unfair treatment by supervisors, and improved work-life balance due to forced overtime.
