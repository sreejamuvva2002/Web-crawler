# OneGeorgia Authority Rural Site Development Initiative

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2025-06-18

**Source:** [Governor Kemp Announces Second Round of Rural Site Development Grants | Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority](https://dlcda.com/news/article/governor-kemp-announces-second-round-of-rural-site-development-grants)  ·  `page_056084`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced the second round of grants in the OneGeorgia Authority's Rural Site Development Initiative on June 18, 2025.
- The program enables Georgia’s rural communities to identify, assess, and develop new, ready-for-development industrial sites to grow the state's economic capacity.
- Eligible grant activities include site studies, land grading, and other essential community infrastructure efforts supporting site development.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Georgia has been named the top state for business for over a decade due to its workforce, connectivity, and business-friendly environment.
- Total funding for this second round of grants is $8 million.
- More than $18 million in grants have been distributed to rural site development since the program’s launch.
- Speaker Jon Burns highlighted that the initiative represents the state's commitment to bringing new economic opportunities to every corner of Georgia.
- Lt. Governor Burt Jones noted the approved funding will help current projects grow and future projects begin while maintaining Georgia’s business environment as a national model.
- Department of Community Affairs Commissioner Christopher Nunn stated the tool enables communities to remain attractive for future prospects and achieve economic prosperity.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson noted that GRAD-certified sites offer speed-to-market advantages for companies and strengthen Georgia’s competitive site portfolio.
- Applicants must leverage other funding sources to demonstrate community commitment, and funds must be expended within the timeframe specified in grant award documentation, generally two years from the award date.
- The Development Authority of the City of Milledgeville and Baldwin County was awarded $2 million for site readiness improvements, including an entry road and other road improvements, at the 1,500-acre GRAD-certified Sibley Place.
- The total project cost for the Milledgeville and Baldwin County site readiness improvements is $2,193,982.
- The Development Authority of Bulloch County received a grant of $1,518,920 for site grading and sewer improvements at the 69-acre GRAD-certified Cannady Site.
- The Development Authority of Bulloch County is contributing to the total infrastructure cost of $2,024,960 for the Cannady Site.
- The Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority was awarded a grant of $2 million for sewage and drainage improvements at the 250-acre “GRAD Select” Georgia Highway 257 Rail Site.
- The Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority is contributing to the total infrastructure cost of $6,039,671 for the rail site.
- The Worth County Economic Development Authority received a grant of $2 million for water, drainage, road, and other infrastructure improvements at the 196-acre “GRAD Select” Worth County Industrial Complex.
- The expected total project cost for the Worth County Industrial Complex improvements is $2,248,723.
- The Development Authority of Burke County was awarded $35,278 to assist with GRAD certification costs for the 150-acre Waynesboro-Burke Industrial Park.
- The total project cost for the Burke County GRAD certification is $39,198.
- The Douglas-Coffee County Economic Development Authority was awarded $75,000 to assist with GRAD certification costs for the 72-acre Douglas Aviation Industrial Park.
- The total project cost for the Douglas-Coffee County GRAD certification is $90,050.
- The Development Authority of Jefferson County was awarded $75,000 to assist with GRAD certification costs for the 230-acre Louisville Airport Industrial Park.
- The total project cost for the Jefferson County GRAD certification is $82,500.
- The McIntosh County Industrial Development Authority was awarded $192,745 to assist with GRAD certification costs for the 247-acre Tidewater East Industrial Park.
- The total project cost for the McIntosh County GRAD certification is $260,345.
- The Pulaski County-Hawkinsville Development Authority was awarded $31,455 to assist with GRAD certification costs for the 170-acre Hawkinsville-Pulaski County Industrial Park.
- The total project cost for the Pulaski County GRAD certification is $34,950.
- The Washington-Wilkes Payroll Development Authority was awarded $142,450 to assist with GRAD certification costs for the 455-acre Washington-Wilkes Industrial Park Expansion.
- The total project cost for the Washington-Wilkes GRAD certification is $167,450.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) serves as the state’s sales and marketing arm and lead agency for attracting new business investment, encouraging industry expansion, locating markets, promoting tourism, and mobilizing state resources for economic development.
- The Georgia Department of Community Affairs (DCA) partners with communities to build neighborhoods and commercial/industrial areas through community development, local government assistance, and housing initiatives, while helping spur private job creation and develop downtowns.
