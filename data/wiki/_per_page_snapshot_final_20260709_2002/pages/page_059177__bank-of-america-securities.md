## Facts

- Alexander Perry has resumed coverage of major North American automakers at BofA Securities as Head of North American Autos Research.
- Perry replaces Federico Merendi, who returned to Wolfe Research after three and a half years with Bank of America’s equity research team.
- The analyst has taken a generally more bullish stance on auto stocks than predecessors Federico Merendi and John Murphy.
- In a new research note published on Wednesday, the analyst set a $17 price target on Ford Motor Co. stock with a Buy rating.
- The target implies an upside potential of 33.9% on Ford’s shares based on Tuesday’s closing price of $12.70.
- Ford’s stock has jumped 31.6% in the last 12 months despite a 12.0% decline recorded in the past seven days.
- BofA’s valuation of Ford is based on 3.8 times its adjusted EBITDA estimates for 2027.
- The analyst stated that Ford is positioned well to capitalize on the significant shift in the regulatory backdrop under the current administration, which should enable it to shift focus to its most margin accretive trucks/SUVs.
- Ford stopped production of the fully electric Ford F-150 Lightning late last year due to low interest for high-end EVs.
- CEO Jim Farley cited low interest for high-end EVs as the reason for stopping F-150 Lightning production.
- The company is shifting focus towards internal combustion engine and hybrid models while preparing to launch a more affordable electric pickup truck under its Universal EV Platform.
- In December, Ford reported a loss of $19.5 billion as it restructured its EV business.
- Ford expects its ‘Model e’ business to become profitable by 2029.
- The company announced that its EV unit lost $4.8 billion in 2025 and was expected to lose $4 billion to $4.5 billion in 2026.
- UBS analyst Joseph Spak called Ford’s $20 billion write-down a “bold action.”
- BofA’s Alexander Perry sees near-term benefits as Model E losses abate and the company’s high-margin commercial business stabilizes.
- The analyst expects Ford to make progress toward its 8% EBIT margin guide, including a large step-up in 2027.
- Alexander Perry assumed coverage of Tesla with a Buy rating and $460 price target on the stock.
- In a research note published on October 29, 2025, Federico Merendi had reiterated a Neutral rating on Tesla’s stock.
- Tesla closed 2.7% down at $392.43 on Tuesday’s trading session, implying a 17.2% upside potential based on BofA’s updated target.
- Tesla’s stock climbed to an all-time high of $498.83 in late December before dropping below $400 in February.
- The analyst emphasized the value BofA assigns to Tesla’s humanoid robotics and energy businesses.
- Perry valued Tesla’s Optimus humanoid segment at over $30 billion, representing 2% of the company’s valuation.
- Optimus will likely first be used in manufacturing and possibly replace a portion of the 13 million US manufacturing jobs, with future adoption in households.
- Elon Musk said it’s “probably true” that people will forget Tesla ever built cars after launching the Optimus V3, scheduled for later this year.
- The humanoid robot will be built at GigaTexas on manufacturing lines formerly dedicated to Model S and Model X production.
- Bank of America values Tesla’s energy business at $90 billion, representing 6% of the company’s valuation.
- BofA expects Tesla to have leading share with its residential Powerwall batteries and Megapacks for utilities and data centers.
- Alexander Perry resumed coverage on US EV makers Lucid Motors and Rivian, both headquartered in California.
- The analyst reiterated an Underperform rating on Lucid Motors with a $10 price target, matching the pre-reverse split basis set in August last year.
- Lucid’s stock has crashed by nearly 99% from its peak five years ago and hit a new record low of $8.90 immediately after earnings release, equivalent to $0.89 pre-reverse split.
- Based on Tuesday’s closing price of $10.11, BofA’s price target implies a 1% decline on Lucid’s stock.
