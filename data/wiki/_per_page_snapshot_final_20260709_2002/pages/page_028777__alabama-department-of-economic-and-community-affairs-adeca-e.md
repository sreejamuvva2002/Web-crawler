# Alabama Department of Economic and Community Affairs (ADECA) EV Charger and Diesel Replacement Rebate Program

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2023-10-01

**Source:** [Alternative Fuels Data Center: Natural Gas Laws and Incentives in Alabama](https://afdc.energy.gov/fuels/laws/NG?state=AL)  ·  `page_028777`

## Facts

- The Alabama Department of Economic and Community Affairs (ADECA) offers grants for light-duty EV chargers and the replacement of qualified medium- and heavy-duty diesel vehicles with new diesel or alternative fuel vehicles.
- Grants are available for EV chargers; medium- and heavy-duty trucks; school, shuttle, and transit buses; freight switchers; airport ground support equipment; and forklifts and port cargo handling equipment.
- Funding amounts for the rebates are based on vehicle type, fuel type, and applicant type.
- The rebate program is funded by Alabama’s portion of the Volkswagen Environmental Mitigation Trust.
- The state administers an excise tax on compressed natural gas (CNG) and liquefied natural gas (LNG) used as vehicle fuel.
- CNG taxes are set at $0.13 per GGE from October 1, 2023, until September 30, 2028, and $0.18 per GGE from October 1, 2028 and beyond.
- LNG taxes are set at $0.13 per DGE from October 1, 2023, until September 30, 2028, and $0.18 per DGE from October 1, 2028 and beyond.
- A GGE is defined as equal to 5.66 pounds or 126.67 cubic feet of natural gas for CNG, and a DGE is equal to 6.06 lbs. for LNG.
- The Alabama Legislature established a Green Fleets Review Committee and Green Fleets Policy outlining procurement procedures for state vehicles based on fuel economy and life cycle costing.
- State fleet managers must submit annual plans reflecting a 4% annual increase in average fleet fuel economy for light-duty vehicles, a 3% annual increase for medium-duty vehicles, and a 2% annual increase for heavy-duty vehicles per fiscal year.
- Government entities must manage fleets to be energy efficient, minimize emissions, and reduce petroleum dependency using proven technology identified by the Committee.
- Any motor vehicle equipped with an auxiliary power unit or idle reduction technology may exceed gross, axle, tandem, or bridge formula weight limits by up to 400 pounds.
- A vehicle primarily powered by natural gas may exceed state gross vehicle weight limits by a weight equal to the difference between its weight and a comparable diesel vehicle's weight, with a maximum gross weight of 82,000 lbs.
