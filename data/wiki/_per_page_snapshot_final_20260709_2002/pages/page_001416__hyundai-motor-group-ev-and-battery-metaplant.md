# Hyundai Motor Group EV and Battery Metaplant

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-07-01

**Source:** [5 things: Hyundai development - The Current](https://thecurrentga.org/2023/07/01/5-things-hyundai-development/)  ·  `page_001416`

## Facts

- The Hyundai metaplant is under construction in Bryan County.
- Official announcements project 8,100 jobs at the site.
- Construction spending is projected to be at least $5.5 billion.
- Suppliers plan to hire thousands more employees for the project.
- Hyundai Motor Group projects 8,100 new jobs at its site located at Interstate 16 and Georgia 280 in Bryan County once the EV and battery plant is fully operational.
- According to 2022 Georgia Department of Labor data, only 11 private companies employ over 1,000 workers across Chatham, Bryan, and Effingham counties.
- Gulfstream is the largest private employer in the region with roughly 11,500 workers around Savannah.
- The Savannah-Chatham Public School System employs approximately 5,500 staff members.
- Approximately 3,500 civilians work at Fort Stewart, and 640 civilians work at Hunter Army Air Field.
- About 27,000 people work in the hospitality industry across Chatham, Bryan, and Effingham counties, distributed among roughly 1,600 small businesses.
- Hyundai and its adjacent battery plant will offer an average salary of at least $58,105 per job, plus benefits.
- The average salary is structured to qualify for state and local grants, tax breaks, and land incentives valued at approximately $228,000 per job.
- Supplier Ecoplastic's plant near Register will provide 456 jobs over eight years upon full functionality, manufacturing plastic car parts.
- Ecoplastic workers must be paid at least the county's average wage of $21.65 per hour, per a February 2023 agreement with Bulloch County.
- A 40-hour workweek at $21.65 per hour equates to approximately $43,300 annually for Ecoplastic workers.
- In exchange for building in Bulloch County, Ecoplastic received a $2.3 million discount on its site purchase price and a 15-year county property tax discount.
- The property tax discount for Ecoplastic does not apply to school and fire district taxes.
- Ecoplastic may also be eligible for a $1.6 million state grant.
- Supplier Joon Georgia has a contract with Bulloch County and the state guaranteeing an hourly wage matching the county average for 630 promised jobs at its new facility.
- Joon Georgia received a $3.1 million land discount near I-16, a $2.7 million state grant, and a 10-year county property tax break.
- The property tax abatement for Joon Georgia does not cover school and fire district rates.
- The Hyundai Metaplant site spans more than a mile along Black Creek, approximately three miles upstream from where it joins the Ogeechee River.
- Hyundai and its suppliers are locating facilities near interstates and within the Ogeechee watershed.
- A new wastewater treatment plant across the interstate from Hyundai will process the facility's wastewater and north Bryan County's sewage before discharging it into the Ogeechee River.
- Environmentalists from Ogeechee Riverkeeper are monitoring water quality in Black Creek and the Ogeechee River due to concerns about state pollution monitoring.
- Eight Hyundai suppliers will be distributed across Bryan, Bulloch, Chatham, Effingham, and Liberty counties.
- None of the supplier factory locations are currently served by public transportation lines.
- Hyundai production at the metaplant is scheduled to begin in the first half of 2025.
- The metaplant site is located on the south side of I-16, approximately 22 miles northwest of Savannah.
- Satellite imagery shows site clearance is underway and reveals industrial development in the area dating back to 2005.
- Daniel Defense and ORAFOL facilities are located directly across the interstate from the Hyundai metaplant site.
- The bid team that secured Hyundai included state and county leaders plus the newly formed Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority.
- The bid team competed against other regional states, anticipating manufacturers would demand billions in subsidies.
- Georgia's Department of Economic Development highlighted its "Best State for Business" ranking in its offer letter to Hyundai, citing outstanding logistics, a well-trained workforce, low business costs, a pro-business climate, and high quality of life.
- The "Best State for Business" ranking notably omits worker quality of life metrics.
- The state is investing tens of millions of dollars into job training programs tailored to the new Hyundai and supplier workforce.
