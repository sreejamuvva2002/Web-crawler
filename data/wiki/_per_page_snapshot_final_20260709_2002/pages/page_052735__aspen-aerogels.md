# Aspen Aerogels

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2026-05-07

**Source:** [Aspen Aerogels (ASPN) Q1 2026 Earnings Transcript - AOL](https://www.aol.com/articles/aspen-aerogels-aspn-q1-2026-192846702.html)  ·  `page_052735`

## Facts

- Aspen Aerogels reported $37.9 million in revenue for fiscal Q1 2026 (period ended March 31, 2026), down 8% sequentially from fiscal Q4 2025.
- Revenue breakdown included $21.6 million from the Energy Industrial segment and $16.3 million from the thermal barrier segment.
- GAAP net loss was negative $23.7 million, compared to a negative $72.9 million in fiscal Q4 2025.
- Adjusted EBITDA was negative $12.7 million, representing a 29% sequential improvement from negative $18 million in fiscal Q4 2025 despite lower revenue.
- Company-wide gross margin was 11%, with segment gross margins of 15% for Energy Industrial and 6% for thermal barrier.
- A temporary operational disruption occurred at the East Providence facility due to an explosion, causing a plant shutdown and near-term cost pressures from repairs, expedited freight, and elevated inventory across facilities.
- Aspen Aerogels received $37.6 million in claim proceeds from General Motors (GM).
- Of the GM claim proceeds, $3.5 million was recognized as revenue in fiscal Q1 2026, with $4.9 million per quarter to be recognized through 2027 under ASC 606.
- The company ended fiscal Q1 2026 with $175.6 million in cash and cash equivalents, up from $158.6 million at year-end 2025, supported by GM proceeds and an $8 million working capital benefit.
- The term loan balance stood at $86 million, with a sole covenant requiring cash to equal at least 100% of the term loan balance after fiscal Q1 2026.
- Fiscal Q1 2026 capital expenditures totaled $1 million, with full-year guidance remaining under $10 million for CapEx and $26 million in scheduled debt payments.
- Fiscal Q2 2026 revenue is expected between $40 million and $48 million, representing a 5% to 28% sequential increase.
- Q2 adjusted EBITDA guidance ranges from negative $10 million to negative $4 million, pending production and supply outcomes.
- Management expects approximately 20% segment growth in Energy Industrial for 2026, driven by subsea, LNG, and maintenance turnaround opportunities.
- European thermal barrier revenue tripled year over year for the quarter, with management forecasting $10 million to $15 million in European thermal barrier revenue for 2026.
- General Motors EV market share averaged 14.1%, with Cadillac EV sales accounting for over 30% of segment sales in fiscal Q1 2026.
- Management cited lower GM production relative to sales, resulting in destocking, and anticipates GM will align production rates more closely with sales volumes consistent with a demand-driven approach.
- PyroThin is Aspen Aerogels' proprietary thermal barrier designed for lithium-ion battery fire protection in electric vehicles and energy storage systems.
- Across European awards, Aspen supports programs incorporating battery cells from a diversified global supply base, including European, Korean, Japanese, and leading Chinese manufacturers.
- The staged restart of the East Providence facility is planned for May, conditional on safety reviews and agency coordination.
- The sale of Plant 2 assets is expected in fiscal Q4 2026 and will be applied directly for debt reduction.
- Financial framework from restructuring enables management to reduce breakeven revenue levels from $330 million in 2024 to $175 million by the end of 2027.
- Q2 guidance assumes GM production at an annualized rate of approximately 55,000 to 65,000 vehicles in the quarter.
- The current IHS forecast has GM producing nearly 100,000 vehicles for 2026, pointing to more production weighted to the second half of the year.
- Q2 cash outflows are projected between $20 million and $30 million, including $12 million of CapEx and scheduled debt payments.
- Management reiterated that sequential improvement for each quarter in 2026 is expected, targeting adjusted EBITDA breakeven at $50 million in quarterly revenue during the second half.
- Expenses were impacted by one-time items, including a $2.2 million property tax charge and approximately $1 million of charges related to nonrecurring professional services in fiscal Q1 2026.
- GM has maintained its full line of EV nameplates and stated it remains dedicated to its long-term EV success, including in the Cadillac division where EV sales represented over 30% in Q1 2026.
- Battery electric vehicles now account for more than 20% of new vehicle registrations in Europe, supporting early stages of production ramp-up among OEMs with whom Aspen has design awards.
- Aspen is advancing growth opportunities in battery energy storage systems, engaging in qualifications and commercial discussions with developers serving grid infrastructure and data centers.
