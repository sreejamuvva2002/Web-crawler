# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Georgia leaders offer $1.8 billion in incentives for Hyundai jobs - WJCL](https://www.wjcl.com/article/bryan-county-hyundai-plant-incentives/40707720)  ·  `page_009416`

## Facts

- Georgia offers $1.8 billion in incentives for an 8,100-job Hyundai plant coming to Bryan County.
- The state of Georgia and local governments are giving $1.8 billion in tax breaks and other incentives to Hyundai in exchange for the automaker building its first U.S. plant dedicated to electric vehicles.
- The deal calls for Hyundai to invest $5.5 billion in its Georgia plant and hire 8,100 workers.
- It is the largest economic development deal in the state’s history.
- The deal comes just months after Georgia closed another major deal with electric vehicle maker Rivian to build a factory in the state.
- Pat Wilson, the state’s economic development commissioner, said the generational projects solidify Georgia's spot at the vanguard of the EV transition and ensure thousands of Georgians will benefit from future jobs.
- Hyundai executives and Gov. Brian Kemp announced the deal in May with a champagne toast at the project’s 2,900 acre (1,170 hectare) site in Bryan County, west of Savannah.
- Hyundai plans to start construction on the plant next year.
- Hyundai plans to begin producing up to 300,000 vehicles per year in 2025.
- The new factory will also produce vehicle batteries.
- Officials declined to reveal what incentives the automaker had been promised until after the agreement was signed.
- The package disclosed Friday is worth roughly $300 million more than incentives promised to Rivian.
- The deal amounts to Georgia and four counties in the Savannah area giving Hyundai about $228,000 per job created.
- Wilson said Hyundai’s payroll at the new plant is expected to reach $4.7 billion over 10 years.
- Parts suppliers are expected to create thousands of additional jobs in the state.
- The $1.8 billion in incentives is easily the largest subsidy package a U.S. state has ever promised for an automotive plant, according to Greg LeRoy, executive director of Good Jobs First.
- Greg LeRoy called the deal "inherently super-risky" because it bets a huge amount on one company and one facility.
- Local governments are giving Hyundai more than $472 million in property tax breaks.
- Hyundai will pay more than $357 million in lieu of taxes over a 26-year period starting in 2023.
- The company will also receive more than $212 million in state income tax credits, at $5,250 per job over five years.
- If Hyundai didn’t owe that much state corporate income tax, Georgia would instead give the company personal income taxes collected from Hyundai workers.
- The state and local governments spent $86 million to purchase the plant site.
- The state will spend $200 million on road construction and improvements.
- The state will spend $50 million more to help fund construction, machinery and equipment.
- Sales tax exemptions on construction materials and machinery expenses are estimated to cost $396 million.
- Georgia officials say the deal requires Hyundai to pay back a portion of the incentives if the company falls below 80% of promised investment or employment.
- Kia, another subsidiary of the Hyundai Motor Group, got more than $450 million in incentives for its plant in West Point, southwest of Atlanta.
- Georgia has promised SK Innovation $300 million in incentives for a $2.6 billion, 2,600-worker battery plant that the Korean company is building northeast of Atlanta.
