# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA)

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-03-27

**Source:** [Hyundai's $8B Georgia plant faces critical test in Trump's U.S.](https://koreajoongangdaily.joins.com/news/2025-03-27/business/industry/Hyundais-8B-Georgia-plant-faces-critical-test-in-Trumps-US/2271889)  ·  `page_009173`

## Facts

- Hyundai officially opened its $7.6 billion Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) in Ellabell, Bryan County on Wednesday.
- The plant spans 11.76 million-square-meter (126.58 million-square-foot).
- The facility opening will likely allow Hyundai EV buyers to qualify for up to $7,500 in U.S. government subsidies.
- The plant aims to propel Hyundai's position in the U.S. EV market, which is currently dominated by Tesla.
- The facility includes an automobile factory and a battery facility.
- The plant is key to raising Hyundai's total U.S. production to 1.2 million units.
- The auto facility's current capacity stands at 300,000 units.
- Hyundai aims to increase the facility's capacity to 500,000 units in the future.
- This planned capacity will exceed Hyundai's Alabama plant capacity of 360,000 units and Kia's Georgia plant capacity of 340,000 units.
- Hyundai's Ioniq 5 and Ioniq 9 SUVs are currently being manufactured at the plant.
- Kia’s hybrid vehicles and Genesis vehicles are slated to roll off the assembly line in the future.
- Euisun Chung, executive chair of Hyundai Motor Group, spoke during the plant's opening ceremony alongside Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp, Hyundai Motor Group Vice Chairman Chang Jae-hoon, and CEO José Muñoz.
- President Trump signed an executive order on his first day directing agencies to immediately suspend disbursement of funds allocated under the Inflation Reduction Act (IRA), including for EV charger implementation and tax credit system re-examination.
- Hyundai and Kia's combined U.S. sales of eco-friendly cars stood at 346,441 units last year, which the plant's 300,000-unit capacity can cover.
- Combined EV sales in the U.S. were 6,962 units in February, representing a 10.4 percent decline from the same month the previous year.
- Jae Kim, a leading accountant for the Korea desk at Atlanta-based Aprio, stated that entirely killing subsidies is unrealistic due to Republican lawmaker opposition, but subsidies will likely be slashed by some margin.
- GlobalData cut its outlook for the U.S. EV market share in 2030 to 28 percent from 33 percent following Trump’s election victory.
- BGR Group reported that potential rollback of tax credits and regulatory support has led to delays or cancellations of projects in hydrogen, electric vehicles, and wind power sectors.
- The Savannah city government stopped treating the Georgia plant's wastewater in September due to violations of government parameters after finding the water could be harmful to the environment and humans.
- Hyundai’s wastewater contained more than six times the acceptable limit of copper and more than twice the allowable zinc concentration.
- The city halted treatment immediately after discovering Hyundai failed to continuously observe its wastewater conductivity.
- Since September, Hyundai has been transporting millions of gallons of wastewater via tanker trucks to external treatment facilities in states like Florida and North Carolina.
- Hyundai stated it will resolve the wastewater issue once Bryan County completes its $129 million North Bryan County Water Reclamation Facility.
- Construction of the water reclamation facility is expected to be completed no earlier than the end of 2025.
- The carmaker's factory began mass production on Wednesday, the same day as the official opening ceremony.
- A Hyundai Motor spokesperson confirmed that after receiving notice of violations, the company immediately stopped discharging wastewater and began a root cause analysis to determine the source.
- Hyundai contracted a specialized company to pump wastewater into tanker trucks and verified that all receiving treatment facilities are properly classified, permitted, and authorized.
