# Honda

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Honda Debuts Two '0 Series' EV Concepts Aimed at 2026 Production - CNET](https://www.cnet.com/home/electric-vehicles/honda-debuts-two-0-series-ev-concepts-aimed-at-2026-production/)  ·  `page_008884`

## Facts

- Honda debuted two "0 Series" EV concepts (Saloon and Space-Hub) following a CES stage appearance with Sony regarding their joint-venture Afeela EV.
- The Honda 0 concept Saloon is positioned as the flagship model with a wide, low and angular silhouette of a sports coupe.
- Behind its gull wing doors is seating for four passengers tucked low for a sporty feel.
- Ahead of the driver is a steering yoke and a full-width dashboard display that stretches from door to door.
- Honda states that the core concept behind the Honda 0 models is "man maximum/machine minimum," indicating its commitment to driver-first, engaging performance and lightweight efficiency.
- The 0 Series will include steer-by-wire and robotics-based automated driving technologies.
- Honda promises that the production car based on the Honda 0 Saloon will arrive in 2026 with "the joy of driving" intact.
- The EVs will feature e-axles, which tuck the electric motors in-line with the wheels rather than in an engine bay.
- The EVs will use compact, high-density battery packs that help maximize interior space.
- Honda promises "stress-free charging" with a production expectation of 10 to 15 minutes for a 15% to 80% charge.
- Honda targets less than 10% capacity loss after 10 years of driving to minimize battery degradation over time.
- The Honda 0 Space-Hub concept is more upright and van-shaped compared to its conceptual sibling.
- The Space-Hub features a generous transparent canopy, an airy living room on wheels design, and center-facing bench seats that encourage passengers to engage with one another on their journey.
- Both concepts feature single-frame front fascia with small, rectangular headlamps.
- Rather than a grille, each EV features an illuminated panel that creates the perception of depth while displaying a blue particle animation.
- The 3D design is also echoed at the rear end of both concepts.
- Both concepts feature a new frameless "H mark" badge designed for Honda's next-generation EVs to distinguish production Honda 0 models from the rest of the family.
