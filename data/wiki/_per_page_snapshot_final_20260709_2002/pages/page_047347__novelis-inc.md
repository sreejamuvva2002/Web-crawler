# Novelis Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2025-05-12

**Source:** [Novelis Reports Fourth Quarter and Full Fiscal Year 2025 Results - Metro Atlanta CEO](https://metroatlantaceo.com/news/2025/05/novelis-reports-fourth-quarter-and-full-fiscal-year-2025-results/)  ·  `page_047347`

## Facts

- Novelis Inc. is a leading sustainable aluminum solutions provider and the world leader in aluminum rolling and recycling.
- Fourth quarter fiscal year 2025 net income attributable to common shareholders was $294 million, up 77% year-over-year.
- Fourth quarter fiscal year 2025 net income excluding special items was $262 million, up 46% year-over-year.
- Adjusted EBITDA for the fourth quarter of fiscal year 2025 was $473 million, down 8% year-over-year but up 29% quarter-over-quarter.
- Rolled product shipments in the fourth quarter of fiscal year 2025 totaled 957 kilotonnes, up 1% year-over-year.
- Adjusted EBITDA per tonne shipped for the fourth quarter was $494, down 9% year-over-year.
- Full fiscal year 2025 net income attributable to common shareholders was $683 million, up 14% year-over-year.
- Full fiscal year 2025 net income excluding special items was $764 million, up 11% year-over-year.
- Adjusted EBITDA for full fiscal year 2025 was $1.8 billion, down 4% year-over-year.
- Rolled product shipments for full fiscal year 2025 totaled 3,757 kilotonnes, up 2% year-over-year.
- Adjusted EBITDA per tonne shipped for full fiscal year 2025 was $480, down 6% year-over-year.
- Novelis maintained the recycled content of its products at 63% in fiscal 2025.
- Net sales for the fourth quarter of fiscal year 2025 increased 13% versus the prior year period to $4.6 billion, driven by higher average aluminum prices and a 1% increase in total rolled product shipments.
- Higher demand for beverage packaging, aerospace, and specialty products in the fourth quarter was partially offset by lower automotive shipments.
- Fourth quarter net income growth was primarily driven by favorable metal price lag, gains in unrealized derivatives, and a lower income tax provision.
- Fourth quarter Adjusted EBITDA decline was primarily driven by higher aluminum scrap prices and operating costs, partially offset by higher product pricing.
- Net sales for full fiscal year 2025 increased 6% versus the prior year to $17.1 billion, driven by higher average aluminum prices and a 2% increase in total flat rolled product shipments.
- The full year shipment increase was mainly due to record high beverage packaging shipments and higher aerospace product shipments, partially offset by lower specialties and automotive products.
- Full year net income growth was primarily driven by a favorable change in metal price lag, unrealized gains on derivatives, and a lower income tax provision, partially offset by impacts from the Sierre flooding and lower Adjusted EBITDA.
- Full year Adjusted EBITDA decline was mainly driven by higher aluminum scrap prices compared to the prior year, unfavorable product mix, and higher operating costs, partially offset by higher total shipments and higher product pricing.
- Net cash flow provided by operating activities was $1.0 billion in fiscal year 2025 compared to $1.3 billion in the prior fiscal year.
- Adjusted Free Cash Flow was an outflow of $737 million in fiscal year 2025 compared to a prior year period outflow of $75 million, due primarily to a 24% year-over-year increase in capital expenditures and lower cash flow from operating activities.
- Fiscal year 2025 capital expenditures totaled $1.7 billion, reflecting planned increases in strategic, sustainability-focused capital investment projects supporting increased long-term customer demand.
- In January 2025, Novelis issued $750 million in senior unsecured notes due January 2030, with proceeds primarily used to repay outstanding borrowings under its ABL revolver.
- In March 2025, the company borrowed $1.25 billion of term loans that mature in March 2032, with proceeds primarily used to repay previously issued term loans due in 2026 and 2028.
- The company had a net leverage ratio of 2.9x at the end of the fourth quarter of fiscal year 2025.
- Total liquidity stood at $2.8 billion as of March 31, 2025, consisting of $1.0 billion in cash and cash equivalents and $1.7 billion in availability under committed credit facilities.
- Steve Fisher, president and CEO of Novelis Inc., stated that strong shipments led by robust demand for beverage packaging continue to underpin the fundamental strength of the business.
- Steve Fisher noted the company is implementing actions and technologies to expand scrap input types to mitigate scrap cost challenges from elevated aluminum scrap prices.
- Steve Fisher emphasized a focus on optimizing cost structure and increasing operational efficiency to drive margin improvements and maintain strong return on invested capital.
- Dev Ahuja, executive vice president and CFO of Novelis Inc., stated that disciplined cash management enables strategic investment for growth.
- Dev Ahuja highlighted the company's focus on cost efficiency while completing investments to increase rolling and recycling capacity.
- Novelis will discuss its fourth quarter and full fiscal year 2025 results via a live webcast and conference call at 7:00 a.m. EDT/4:30 p.m. IST on Monday, May 12, 2025.
- Novelis aims to be the leading provider of low-carbon, sustainable aluminum solutions and achieve a fully circular economy by partnering with suppliers and customers in the aerospace, automotive, beverage packaging, and specialties industries throughout North America, Europe, Asia, and South America.
- Novelis is a subsidiary of Hindalco Industries Limited, an industry leader in aluminum and copper, and the metals flagship company of the Aditya Birla Group, a multinational conglomerate based in Mumbai.
