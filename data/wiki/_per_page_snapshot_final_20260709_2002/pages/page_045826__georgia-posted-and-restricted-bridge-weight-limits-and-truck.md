# Georgia Posted and Restricted Bridge Weight Limits and Truck Routing Policy

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2020-03-14

**Source:** [Georgia Department of Public Safety - CVE Regulations Compliance](https://www.gamccd.net/OSPermit/PostedBridges.aspx)  ·  `page_045826`

## Facts

- Posted and restricted bridges limit the load weight on truck travel in Georgia.
- Road signs are posted indicating the type of truck and corresponding weight limits along the road in the direction of travel approaching a posted bridge.
- The 00 on the signs indicates that the specific vehicle type is not currently posted.
- Numerical values beside each truck on the signs indicate the legal weight limit for that truck.
- Pursuant to Governor Kemp’s Executive Order dated March 14, 2020, vehicles obtaining permits for weight under this order shall not cross posted bridges nor other restricted bridges to facilitate the uninterrupted supply of goods and other emergency related materials.
- For other permit dimensions, drivers must refer to the Georgia Oversize Truck Route Map.
- The Georgia Department of Transportation (GDOT) requires trucks that contain loads over the posted or restricted weight limit to take an alternate route.
- Truck drivers may need to plan routes in advance to avoid posted or restricted bridges due to GDOT requirements.
