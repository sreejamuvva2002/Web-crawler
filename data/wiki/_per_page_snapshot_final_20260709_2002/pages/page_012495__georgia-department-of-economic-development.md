# Georgia Department of Economic Development

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2025-06-30

**Source:** [2026 Economic Yearbook - Georgia Trend Magazine](https://www.georgiatrend.com/2026/03/31/2026-econmic-yearbook-georgia-trend/)  ·  `page_012495`

## Facts

- Georgia’s fiscal year 2026, which began in July, has been one of record-breaking achievement.
- Domestic and international corporations are launching new operations throughout the state’s 159 counties while existing companies expand to support the manufacturing and artificial intelligence sectors.
- Misti Martin, deputy commissioner of global commerce for the Georgia Department of Economic Development, states that Georgia’s success comes from deep partnerships and a focus on diversified economic development across agriculture, manufacturing, logistics, technology, and tourism.
- Gov. Brian Kemp announced that the state surpassed $60.2 billion in exports in calendar year 2025.
- The export increase was 12.7% year-over-year, compared to a national average of 5.6%.
- GDEcD’s figures from January 1 to November 25, 2025 cover the state's economic metrics for that period.
- There were more than $30.3 billion in investments during that period.
- There were an estimated 420 new locations or expansions across the state.
- These investments are expected to create 26,170 new jobs over the next few years.
- The investment figures span all industries, including automotive and agribusiness.
- Much of the economic growth lies in less populated areas throughout the state rather than just Metro Atlanta.
- In the last fiscal year (July 1, 2024-June 30, 2025), 77% of expansions and new locations were outside the 10-county Metro Atlanta region.
- Expansions at existing Georgia companies accounted for around three-quarters of the projects during that fiscal year.
- These existing company expansions built on an estimated 50,000 jobs already held by Georgians.
- Georgia is ranked the No. 1 state for doing business for a record 12 consecutive years.
