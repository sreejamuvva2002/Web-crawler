# Daesol Ausys

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-08-29

**Source:** [Another Korean auto parts manufacturer to open facility in Georgia, governor announces](https://www.yahoo.com/news/another-korean-auto-parts-manufacturer-181109002.html)  ·  `page_019039`

## Facts

- Gov. Brian Kemp announced that Daesol Ausys Georgia will build a new manufacturing operation in West Point.
- The facility will create more than 140 jobs.
- Daesol Ausys Georgia will invest $72 million in Harris County.
- West Point is home to Kia and Hyundai assembly sites.
- Daesol Ausys is a key supplier for Kia, Hyundai, and General Motors.
- Gov. Brian Kemp stated that West Point has become an automotive capital in its own right and expressed excitement about Daesol Ausys joining other innovators in Harris County.
- Georgia's automotive industry currently employs over 50,000 Georgians and will continue to grow as e-mobility suppliers expand across the state.
- The newly announced West Point plant will focus on producing luggage boards and covers.
- Daesol Ausys specializes in designing and manufacturing interior components and accessories for electric vehicles.
- Automotive industry job creation in Georgia rose 324% in the 2023 fiscal year compared to 2021, according to the governor's office.
- The governor's office attributes the job growth to Georgia's business climate and efforts to pursue the entire supply chain for electric vehicles.
- Min Ho Kwon, CEO of Daesol Ausys, stated the company is committed to delivering world-class services that exceed client requirements and expectations.
- Min Ho Kwon expressed excitement about the project in the Northwest Harris Business Park and continuing the partnership with the State of Georgia and Harris County.
- The Daesol facility in West Point is expected to begin operations in December 2024.
- The company will hire for job openings in production, purchasing, quality control, maintenance, human resources, and management.
- Multiple Korean manufacturing companies with automotive ties have recently announced developments in Georgia, including EV parts manufacturer NVH Korea and automotive supplier Hanon Systems.
- Woory Industrial announced in June that it would open an EV parts manufacturing and research facility in Georgia.
