# Critical Metals Corp.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-03

**Source:** [Home - Critical Metals Corp](https://www.criticalmetalscorp.com/)  ·  `page_013551`

## Facts

- Critical Metals Corp. is an emerging leader in rare earth supply and one of the few companies advancing a fully integrated rare earth supply chain outside of China.
- The company is developing world-class capabilities from resource extraction and processing to advanced materials manufacturing.
- Operations in Greenland unlock the Tanbreez Project, which contains one of the richest untapped rare earth deposits in the world.
- Tanbreez utilizes hydro-electric power and is expected to be one of the greenest mining operations in the world.
- The company's essential materials power critical industries including clean energy, electric mobility, defense, aerospace, robotics, and advanced technologies.
- The automotive industry is a major consumer of rare earths, with neodymium, dysprosium, and praseodymium used in electric vehicle motors.
- The global EV market is expected to reach over 40 million vehicles by 2030, significantly increasing demand for these critical metals.
- Tanbreez contains a significant percentage of the global supply of critical minerals restricted by China: Yttrium (45%), terbium (45%), dysprosium (62%), gadolinium (35%), lutetium (80%), and samarium (19%).
- The deposit also has high concentrations of zirconium, niobium, tantalum, hafnium, and gallium.
- Tanbreez contains the majority of the global supply of zirconium metal, which is critical for hypersonic weapons and is relatively easy to extract from this type of ore.
- The mining operation concept involves an open pit mine with onsite dry magnetic separation and a floating dock to load ore directly onto ships alongside a 200-foot-deep fjord.
- The mine will sell feldspar and arfvedsonite for construction materials to ensure profitability, while Heavy Rare Earth Elements (HREE) and other key minerals are contained in eudialyte with an average grade of 0.4% total Rare Earth Oxides that include 27% HREE.
- The fjord is ice-free year-round, with winter temperatures in the low 30s to high 20s Fahrenheit and summer temperatures in the 50s Fahrenheit.
- Ships leave the fjord directly to North America or Europe without transiting a choke point.
- Critical Metals Corp. (Nasdaq: CRML) has successfully acquired a strategic Arctic ship housing asset providing long-term accommodations at the Tanbreez site for up to 300 workers.
- The company commenced a 10,000-meter drilling campaign at the Tanbreez Rare Earth Project in Greenland in June 2026.
- Critical Metals Corp. is proposing an acquisition of European Lithium, with amendments to implementation mechanics not changing the agreed Scheme consideration or principal conditions to completion.
