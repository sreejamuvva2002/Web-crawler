# Sewon America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-02

**Source:** [Sewon America Announces 740 new jobs in Effingham County – Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority](https://savannahjda.com/2023/02/sewon-america-announces-740-new-jobs-in-effingham-county/)  ·  `page_029351`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Sewon America, a subsidiary of Korean-based Sewon Precision Industry Co., will create 740 new jobs in Rincon.
- Sewon America will invest more than $300 million in a new Effingham County manufacturing facility.
- This project is the largest known private investment in the City of Rincon, to-date.
- The announcement fulfills the commitment to create jobs and investment in all four Savannah Harbor Interstate-16 Joint Development Authority (JDA) counties.
- This fulfillment is a result of Hyundai’s Metaplant location in Bryan County.
- Hyundai’s Metaplant is the largest economic development project in state history.
- Suppliers for the Metaplant have now committed over $1.8 billion in investment.
- Supplier investments surpass initial estimates by at least $800 million.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp celebrated delivering on the promise to create jobs in all four JDA counties and the growth of a valued partner.
- On his first international trip as governor to Korea, he congratulated Chairman Kim on the company’s then-recent expansion and discussed continuing to work together.
- Those discussions led to this announcement as Sewon America continues to grow their Georgia operations.
- Speaker of the House Jon Burns represents part of Effingham County and played a role in securing the project and engaging Sewon America executives.
- Lt. Governor Burt Jones commended Governor Brian Kemp, Sewon America, and stakeholders on the historic economic development announcement for Rincon.
- Lt. Governor Burt Jones noted investments by companies like Sewon build on Georgia’s commitment to remain the No. 1 state in which to do business while attracting a robust workforce.
- Sewon America operates one of the largest automobile body parts manufacturing plants in North America.
- The company opened a manufacturing facility in LaGrange in 2008, which has since doubled in size.
- Following completion of the Rincon facility, Sewon is expected to support 1,600 jobs in Georgia.
- Vice Chairman Kim stated Sewon America was founded 15 years ago in west Georgia and expressed excitement about building another facility in east Georgia as one family in two locations.
- Growth is possible thanks to full support from the Sewon team, Governor Kemp, the Georgia Department of Economic Development, and Effingham County.
- Sewon’s new facility will be located at the Grande View industrial park in Rincon.
- The new manufacturing location will produce electric vehicle (EV) body parts for original equipment manufacturers (OEMs), including Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- Operations at the new facility are expected to begin in 2025.
- Rincon Mayor Ken Lee welcomed Sewon America and commended partners at the Effingham County Industrial Development Authority for bringing the largest employment and private investment project in the city’s history.
- Chairman of the Effingham County Industrial Development Authority (IDA) Troy Smith stated Sewon America selected Grande View industrial park for significant investment and future growth.
- The IDA chairman noted the investment will allow current and future generations to have fulfilling, well-paying careers without leaving Effingham County.
- Executive Director of the Georgia Ports Authority Griff Lynch called the announcement terrific news for Effingham County and Georgia and stated the authority is excited to support Sewon’s growth.
- Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy Alyce Thornhill represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- GDEcD partnered with the Effingham County Industrial Development Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia EMC for this project.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson noted Sewon integrated into and lent industry experience to Georgia’s automotive community through work with local colleges, Georgia Quick Start, and statewide initiatives like Georgia Made.
- Sewon is reinvesting in Georgia while supporting Hyundai Motor Group’s operations from Effingham County, which has the skilled workforce, infrastructure, and collaborative spirit to support growth.
- On October 25, 2022, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America officially broke ground in neighboring Bryan County on its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility.
- Georgia is positioned as a hub for the electric mobility industry, building on assets that make the automotive industry successful.
- Since 2020, more than 35 EV-related projects have announced more than $21 billion in investment and 26,700 jobs in Georgia.
- Sewon America was established in 2008 in LaGrange as a Georgia company primarily operating in the Motor Vehicles and Car Bodies industry within the Transportation Equipment sector.
- Sewon produces stamped body components and is celebrated for high-quality automotive parts, continuing this tradition in the new Rincon facility.
