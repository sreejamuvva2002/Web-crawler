# Pratt Industries, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-09-07

**Source:** [Georgia-based Pratt Industries Plans New Manufacturing Facility in Pea | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-releases/2023/georgia-based-pratt-industries-plans-new-manufacturing-facility-peach-county)  ·  `page_029112`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced on September 7, 2023 that Pratt Industries will build a new production facility in Warner Robins (Peach County), Georgia.
- The new location will create more than 125 jobs and over $120 million in investment.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated that expansions like this account for more than 70 percent of last year's economic development projects.
- Pratt Industries is based in Atlanta, is the fifth-largest corrugated packaging company in the United States, and is the largest privately owned producer of 100 percent recycled containerboard in the world.
- Anthony Pratt, Global Executive Chairman for Pratt Industries, stated that the new facility will bring the company's total workforce in Georgia to over 2,100 employees and 11,700 nationwide.
- The new factory will be the company’s 13th site in Georgia and will bring the company’s total investment in Georgia to over $800 million.
- Anthony Pratt stated that Georgia has substantially grown its role as a regional hub for supporting businesses and manufacturing needs across the Southeast.
- The facility will be located at Robins Industrial Park, a Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) certified site in Warner Robins within Peach County.
- The planned facility will be 496,000 square feet and will be one of the most modern of the company’s 72 factories spread across 25 states.
- The facility will produce corrugated boxes using 100 percent recycled containerboard, largely sourced from the company’s mill in Conyers, Georgia.
- Operations are expected to begin in late 2024.
- Warner Robins Mayor LaRhonda W. Patrick stated that Pratt's investment will be the largest single private investment in the history of the city and will provide over 100 new careers.
- Peach County Board of Commissioners Chairman Martin Moseley stated that the project brings opportunities for good jobs to citizens and will be an asset to other industries in Middle Georgia that rely on Pratt's products.
- Senior Project Manager John Soper represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project in partnership with the Development Authority of Peach County and Georgia Power.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that by investing in site preparation and obtaining GRAD certification, communities are better positioned to attract investments that create jobs aligned with their strategic goals.
- Pratt Industries was founded in the U.S. more than 35 years ago and operates manufacturing facilities in more than 25 states.
- The company operates an extensive recycling company to supply six modern 100 percent recycled paper mills located in Conyers, Georgia; Staten Island, New York; Valparaiso, Indiana; Shreveport, Louisiana; Wapakoneta, Ohio; and Henderson, Kentucky.
