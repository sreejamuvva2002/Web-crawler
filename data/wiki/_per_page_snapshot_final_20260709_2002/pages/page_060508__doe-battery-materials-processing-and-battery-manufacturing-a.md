# DOE Battery Materials Processing and Battery Manufacturing and Recycling Programs Funding Round

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2024-09-24

**Source:** [DOE’s $3B funding round includes battery recycling projects | Waste Dive](https://www.wastedive.com/news/battery-recycling-grants-department-of-energy-american-battery-li-industries-ascend-cirba-clarios/727887/)  ·  `page_060508`

## Facts

- The U.S. Department of Energy has announced over $3 billion in grants for 25 projects meant to help boost domestic production of batteries and battery materials.
- Several lithium-ion battery recycling projects are part of the new funding round.
- American Battery Technology Co. received a grant of $150 million as part of the awardees with recycling projects.
- Cirba Solutions received a grant of $200 million as part of the awardees with recycling projects.
- Clarios Circular Solutions received a grant of $150 million as part of the awardees with recycling projects.
- This funding round is the second from the DOE’s Battery Materials Processing and Battery Manufacturing and Recycling Programs.
- The agency expects to invest a total of $16 billion from the two DOE programs to fund battery recycling, processing and manufacturing initiatives.
- The grants are funded through the $1 trillion infrastructure law passed in 2021.
- The most recent round of DOE grants continues a long-term effort to bolster the country’s supply chain for critical materials contained in batteries.
- The Biden administration says such research and investment is important to reduce reliance on other countries.
- A prior round of DOE funding offered $1.82 billion for 14 projects.
- American Battery Technology Co. plans to use its $150 million grant to help construct a new commercial scale lithium-ion battery recycling facility in South Carolina.
- The South Carolina facility is expected to process about 100,000 metric tons of battery materials a year from battery manufacturers and automotive partners.
- The South Carolina facility will produce “battery grade” minerals including nickel, cobalt, manganese, copper, aluminum, and graphite.
- The South Carolina site will be the second commercial-scale lithium-ion battery recycling facility for American Battery Technology Co.
- American Battery Technology Co.'s first facility is a battery recycling facility near Reno, Nevada, which opened in the fall of 2023 and had also received grants from the DOE and from car manufacturers.
- CEO Ryan Melsert stated that the company is greatly appreciative of the confidence and support received from partners and is energized to move forward with feedstock supply, product offtake, and strategic stakeholders in this critical expansion of domestic capabilities.
- Other battery manufacturers and recyclers will use an infusion of DOE funding to advance recycling plans, upgrade plants or start new partnerships for recycling specific materials needed in battery manufacturing.
- Clarios Circular Solutions plans to spend its $150 million grant for retrofitting its existing South Carolina facility to be able to recycle lithium-ion battery production scrap into components needed for new batteries.
- Clarios Circular Solutions expects the retrofitted South Carolina facility to have an annual output of 20,000 metric tons per year.
- Cirba Solutions’ $200 million grant is expected to help it build a facility in South Carolina meant to process over 60,000 tons of batteries annually.
- Li Industries will spend $55.2 million to convert a former manufacturing plant into a recycling plant in partnership with General Motors.
- Ascend Elements received $125 million to advance its recycled graphite production facility in Kentucky, which it says is the first facility of its kind.
- Blue Whale Materials received $55.2 million for a recycling facility in Oklahoma meant to process EVs, consumer lithium-ion batteries and battery scrap for further downstream recovery of cobalt, nickel and lithium at metal refineries.
