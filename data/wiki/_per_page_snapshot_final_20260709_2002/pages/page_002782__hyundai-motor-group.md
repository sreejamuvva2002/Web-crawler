# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** research_and_development  ·  **Published:** 2026-04-30

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Group and Georgia Tech Sign MOU to Further Collaborate on Hydrogen Mobility Development](https://www.hyundai.com/worldwide/en/newsroom/detail/hyundai-motor-group-and-georgia-tech-sign-mou-to-further-collaborate-on-hydrogen-mobility-development-0000001167)  ·  `page_002782`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group and Georgia Tech announced an expansion of their collaboration on April 30, 2026, to advance hydrogen-powered transportation, deepen applied research and education, and accelerate zero-emissions vehicles in Georgia.
- The partnership brings hydrogen fuel cell vehicles and fueling infrastructure to the Georgia Tech campus, positioning it as one of the nation's most prominent campus-based examples for hydrogen mobility.
- Ken Ramírez serves as Executive Vice President and Head of Global Energy and Hydrogen Business at Hyundai Motor Group; he is a 1991 Georgia Tech alumnus and currently sits on the Georgia Tech Advisory Board.
- The President and CEO of Hyundai Motor North America announced that Hyundai donated four NEXO fuel cell electric SUVs to expand clean mobility on campus and provide real-world experience with zero-emissions transportation technology.
- The partnership includes a hydrogen electrolyzer project to be installed at Georgia Tech’s North Avenue Research Area (NARA).
- The donated vehicles and installed infrastructure will support campus operations and interdisciplinary research, using the university as a living laboratory for clean transportation technologies.
- Faculty and students will study insights from this initiative to inform and accelerate the widespread use of hydrogen technology in campuses, fleets, cities, and freight corridors.
- The initiative supports Georgia Tech’s strategic plan goal of expanding the use of zero-emissions vehicles powered by sustainable energy sources.
- Beyond hydrogen energy, the partnership aims to advance innovation in broader mobility areas and create an ecosystem for collaboration, innovation, and education.
- Hyundai's regional presence in Georgia includes the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) and HTWO Logistics, a clean logistics partnership operating Hyundai XCIENT fuel cell heavy-duty trucks near Savannah.
- The collaboration reinforces the link between education, research, and Hyundai’s long-term goal of achieving carbon neutrality by 2045.
- The partnership aims to generate workforce talent and position Georgia as a national leader in hydrogen and zero-emissions transportation.
- Additional announcements regarding this partnership’s research projects, educational programs, and vehicle deployment are expected in the coming months.
