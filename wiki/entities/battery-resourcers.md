# Battery Resourcers

**Type:** Company  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[battery-materials|Battery Materials]]  ·  **Location:** Covington, Georgia, Newton County, Georgia, United States

## Overview

Battery Resourcers is a lithium-ion battery recycling and engineered materials startup that will invest $43 million to open the largest commercial-scale lithium-ion battery recycling plant in North America at Covington, Georgia, creating at least 150 jobs.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Recycles discarded lithium-ion batteries and returns battery-grade lithium, cobalt, and nickel to the electric vehicle supply chain.

## Key facts

- The plant will process 30,000 metric tons of discarded lithium-ion batteries and scrap annually, equivalent to about 70,000 vehicle batteries per year.
- Battery Resourcers' recycling process returns battery-grade lithium, cobalt, and nickel back into the EV supply chain.
- The facility will be the largest lithium-ion battery recycling plant in North America when fully operational.
- $43 million investment announced to fund the Covington recycling plant.
- At least 150 tech jobs will be created in Newton County.
- Battery Resourcers has engineered a process to turn recyclables into nickel-manganese-cobalt cathodes sold back to battery manufacturers.
- The facility occupies an existing 154,000-square-foot building at 9172 Industrial Drive Northeast, Covington, GA.
- "This is just the latest job creator to move to Georgia because of our leadership position in the electric vehicle manufacturing space," said Governor Kemp.
- "We are looking forward to becoming part of the Covington community and bringing 150 tech jobs to the area," said CEO Michael O’Kronley.
- "The Newton County Industrial Development Authority is proud to welcome Battery Resourcers to our business mix," said Lanier Sims.
- "Battery Resourcers is an outstanding addition to our business ecosystem," said Mayor Steve Horton.
- "Metro Atlanta is the ideal location for businesses in the electric vehicle and clean energy industries," said Katie Kirkpatrick.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson highlighted that battery recycling reduces reliance on unstable global sources as EV industry grows.
- Battery Resourcers is building a commercial recycling plant for lithium-ion batteries in Covington, Georgia.
- The plant will have a capacity to process 30,000 tonnes of battery material per year.
- The facility will be set up in an existing hall.
- The project will create 150 jobs in Covington.
- The site is strategically located near several electric-vehicle production centres and lithium-ion gigafactories.
- Current processes will primarily recycle cathode materials from NMC, NCA and LFP batteries.
- Battery Resourcers plans to open additional recycling facilities globally to process up to 150,000 tonnes per year.
- The company is considering opening an additional facility to produce cathode materials in 2023.
- "Automotive OEMs are sitting on mountains of discarded batteries and scrap, and right now they have very few options for responsible and cost-effective disposal," said Michael O’Kronley, CEO and director of Battery Resourcers.
