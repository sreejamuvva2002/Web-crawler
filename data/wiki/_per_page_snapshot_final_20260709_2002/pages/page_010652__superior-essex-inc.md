# Superior Essex Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- Superior Essex Inc. (SPSX) is an Atlanta-based cable supplier and the world’s top magnet wire provider under South Korea’s LS Group.
- The company is mulling an initial public offering on Korea’s main Kospi bourse more than 15 years after leaving the US Nasdaq market.
- In 2008, Superior Essex was acquired by Korea’s LS Group in a deal where LS agreed to pay shareholders $45.00 per share in cash, a 50% premium over the year-to-date-average closing price of its common stock.
- After the takeover, Superior Essex was delisted from the US market but pursued a return to the Nasdaq market until last year.
- IPO market conditions for cable companies in the US have turned negative, with AT&T Inc. inching up only 5.4% over the last year, and US public listing requirements being more complicated than those in Korea.
- The Korean IPO market has been on a recovery track since late last year, unleashing more listing opportunities.
- LS Corp.’s Chairman Koo Ja-eun stated at Korea’s InteBattery 2024 exhibition in March that the group is seeking to list one or two more affiliates between LS E-Link and LS MnM IPOs, making Superior Essex a strong candidate to go public before LS MnM.
- LS Corp. is LS Group's holding company controlling Korea’s top wire and cable producer LS Cable & System Ltd., LS Electric Co., global leading smelter LS MnM Inc., and LS Mtron Co.
- Superior Essex should complete its IPO by 2026 because LS raised funds from Korean private equity firm SKS Credit Co. in August last year in a pre-IPO deal giving the investor drag-along rights to force the sale of shares if the IPO fails until that deadline.
- Brokerage firms are in talks with LS Group to tap Superior Essex’s IPO in Korea.
- Superior Essex is the world’s largest producer of magnet wire and a leader in the North American communications cable market.
- In March, it joined the US government’s 55 trillion won project to build high-speed communication networks.
- The company has entered the EV motor magnet wire market after fully taking over the minority interest held by Furukawa Electric in the Essex Furukawa Magnet Wire joint venture in March.
- Superior Essex reported $1.5 billion in sales in 2023.
