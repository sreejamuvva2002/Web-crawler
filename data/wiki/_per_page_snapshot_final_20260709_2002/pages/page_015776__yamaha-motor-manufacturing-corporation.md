# Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [DHL Supply Chain to Build New Distribution Facility on Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Campus | Yamaha](https://www.yamaha-motor-georgia.com/node/77)  ·  `page_015776`

## Facts

- YMMC and DHL Supply Chain are progressing with studies, permitting, and filings that could lead to the construction of a new distribution facility on YMMC’s Newnan, Georgia campus.
- The proposed facility will be 784,700 square feet.
- The project represents an estimated $60–90 million investment.
- The facility would be solely dedicated to YMMC operations.
- Currently, freight traffic flows between YMMC’s main plant and off-site distribution centers, contributing to congestion along Highway 34 and surrounding intersections.
- Consolidating logistics operations on the YMMC campus could reduce truck traffic on local roads, ease congestion, improve safety, and incur no added cost to taxpayers.
- Bob Brown, President of YMMC, stated that the opportunity is about strengthening their supply chain while also being a good neighbor, and they are working carefully through necessary studies and permitting to ensure the project benefits both Yamaha and the community for years to come.
- The facility will serve as a dedicated, world-class logistics hub to support Yamaha’s manufacturing operations.
- Consolidating operations on campus will reduce truck traffic on local roads.
- The site design will be thoughtful to maintain the character of the surrounding area.
- The project would invest in roadway improvements such as new turn lanes and upgraded intersections to improve traffic flow and safety.
- Sustainable features will include stormwater management systems, tree buffers, and pervious surfaces to reduce environmental impact.
- Pending successful completion of studies and required approvals, construction could begin as early as 2026.
- Operations at the new facility could potentially start in the first quarter of 2028.
- Dave Rumler, President of Automotive Engineering and Manufacturing at DHL Supply Chain North America, stated that DHL is proud to collaborate with Yamaha on a project that enhances supply chain performance and supports infrastructure improvements that benefit the Newnan community.
