# Adient Seating Canada

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2022-11-16

**Source:** [Nearly 200 jobs lost after Adient Tillsonburg, Ont. plant suddenly closes Tuesday - London | Globalnews.ca](https://globalnews.ca/news/9283413/adient-tillsonburg-seat-manufacturing-plant-closure/)  ·  `page_016617`

## Facts

- An auto manufacturing plant in Tillsonburg, Ontario, suddenly closed its doors on Tuesday, leaving nearly 200 workers without a job.
- Adient Seating Canada, formally known in the town as Johnson Controls Ltd., had been making polyurethane seating foam for the auto industry for at least 50 years.
- The 192 workers, including management and salaried employees, were informed at 11 a.m. on Tuesday that Monday’s shift was their last and that the facility would be shutting down.
- Jobs will be moved to Adient facilities in Tennessee and Kentucky, both right-to-work states.
- A spokesperson for Adient stated that after evaluating their foam plant footprint, the company decided to close operations in Tillsonburg.
- Adient continually re-evaluates its facilities footprint to ensure operations are most efficient and meet customer needs.
- The Tillsonburg plant opened in 1969 and manufactured foam for automotive seats, with foam production moving to several different plants in the U.S.
- According to Adient’s website, the company employs more than 150,000 workers in 33 countries across the world.
- In 2019, the Tillsonburg plant hired more than 100 employees after winning a contract to manufacture foam seats for the Ford F-150.
- Many of the Tillsonburg employees had at least 30 years at the plant, with probably half having 20-plus years of service.
- Talks between the union and Adient are ongoing with meetings scheduled with local management on Friday and with corporate representatives on Dec. 5.
- The union aims to bargain for extension of benefits, severances, and pensions if jobs cannot be brought back.
- Unifor national president Lana Payne condemned the closure, stating Adient managers have no decency or respect for workers who made them a wealthy and successful multinational corporation.
- London and area region director for Unifor Luis Domingues stated the plant had extraordinarily good safety records and productivity numbers with no inkling that the closure was going to happen.
