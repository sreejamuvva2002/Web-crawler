# Rivian

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-01-19

**Source:** [Rivian’s factory activities point to imminent R2 launch - electrive.com](https://www.electrive.com/2026/01/19/rivians-factory-activities-point-to-imminent-r2-launch/)  ·  `page_059066`

## Facts

- Validation vehicle production for the R2 model is scheduled for the first half of 2026, marking the final step before series production.
- Pre-production cars for the R2 are already manufactured at Rivian's Illinois facility for final certification and crash testing.
- CEO RJ Scaringe commented on social media platform X that the company is thrilled to begin deliveries to customers soon.
- Hopes indicate the R2 launch could take place early in the first half of 2026.
- The R2 is positioned as Rivian's breakthrough model, comparable to the Tesla Model 3 with higher production volume and lower costs.
- Rivian's premium vehicle models are the R1T and R1S.
- The compact and affordable electric SUV R2 was unveiled in spring 2024.
- The R2 has a starting price of around $45,000 (approximately €38,700), with more expensive variants expected to launch first.
- Initially, the R2 will be produced at Rivian's existing plant in Normal.
- Construction of a new Rivian plant in Georgia has been postponed, with work only beginning in September 2025.
- Postponing the Georgia plant construction is expected to save the company $2.25 billion in capital required for the R2 market launch.
- Expanded capacity at the Normal facility could produce up to 155,000 R2 units per year.
- Once the postponed Georgia production becomes operational, annual capacity could increase by an additional 400,000 units.
- Rivian delivered just over 42,000 vehicles in 2025, representing an 18 percent decline compared to the previous year.
- Vehicle delivery drops were more pronounced in the fourth quarter of 2025 due to the expiration of the US tax credit for electric vehicles at the end of Q3.
- Volkswagen holds a current stake of 12.3 percent in Rivian, making it the company's second-largest shareholder, with plans to increase its involvement further.
- The joint venture between Rivian and Volkswagen for developing vehicle architectures and software systems contributed around $214 million to Rivian's revenue in the third quarter of 2025.
