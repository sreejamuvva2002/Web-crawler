# The Goodyear Tire & Rubber Company

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Full text of "Goodyear Tire & Rubber Co. 2021 Annual Report"](https://archive.org/stream/goodyear-tire-rubber-gt-annual-report-2021/Goodyear+Tire+&+Rubber+(GT)+Annual+Report,+2021_djvu.txt)  ·  `page_055118`

## Facts

- Goodyear is one of the world’s leading tire companies, with one of the most recognizable brand names.
- It develops, manufactures, markets and distributes tires for most applications and manufactures and markets rubber-related chemicals for various uses.
- The company has established itself as a leader in providing services, tools, analytics and products for evolving modes of transportation, including electric vehicles, autonomous vehicles and fleets of shared and connected consumer vehicles.
- Goodyear was the first major tire manufacturer to offer direct-to-consumer tire sales online.
- It offers a proprietary service and maintenance platform for fleets of shared passenger vehicles.
- Through its worldwide network of aligned dealers and wholesale distributors and its own retail outlets and commercial truck centers, Goodyear offers its products for sale to consumer and commercial customers, along with repair and other services.
- It is one of the world’s largest operators of commercial truck service and tire retreading centers.
- Following the acquisition of Cooper Tire in 2021, the company manufactures its products in 57 facilities in 23 countries and has operations in most regions of the world.
- Its two Innovation Centers are located in Akron, Ohio, and Colmar-Berg, Luxembourg.
- In 2021, Goodyear launched the ElectricDrive GT, the first electric vehicle replacement tire in North America.
- The ElectricDrive GT is an ultra-high performance, all-season tire that delivers long-lasting treadwear and a quiet ride for EV drivers and passengers.
- Cooper Tire became part of the Goodyear family when it was acquired in 2021, with its portfolio complementing Goodyear’s product lineup across the value spectrum.
- The acquisition closed in June 2021, making Goodyear an even stronger U.S. leader in the global tire industry.
- The transaction nearly doubled Goodyear's presence in China, a market with tremendous growth prospects.
- The combined company expects to deliver $250 million of synergies, representing a 50% increase from the initial plan.
- A complete integration of Cooper Tire's business is expected to continue into 2023.
- Goodyear tires are designed to handle the added stresses of increased vehicle weight, regenerative braking and higher torque associated with electric vehicles.
- The company's tire technology helps extend vehicle range through reduced rolling resistance.
- Goodyear has secured a presence on nearly half of the electric vehicle platforms produced in Europe.
- In Europe, Goodyear launched the FuelMax Endurance, described as its most versatile and fuel-efficient commercial tire yet.
- Goodyear added DrivePoint to its Total Mobility offering, a tool enabling optimal tire maintenance and improved tire longevity and fuel efficiency.
- The company launched Goodyear SightLine, a suite of technologies using sensors with cloud-based algorithms to identify and address problems in real-time.
- SightLine became the first tire intelligence solution for cargo van fleets, initially available in North America and Europe.
- At the 2020 Consumer Electronics Show, Goodyear announced AndGo by Goodyear, a vehicle servicing marketplace platform connecting mobility companies with qualified service providers.
- Last year, the company experienced five-fold growth in vehicle maintenance jobs through its fleet solutions business.
- Goodyear formed a partnership with Gatik, the first autonomous middle-mile logistics service provider in North America, to advance mobility solutions for the autonomous B2B short-haul logistics industry.
- Gatik’s medium-duty fleet will use tires equipped with tire intelligence technology powered by Goodyear SightLine.
- Goodyear began partnering with AmpUp, an EV-charging technology leader, to bring charging solutions to fleet customers considering electrification.
- An investment was made through Goodyear Ventures, the company's venture capital fund, to participate in AmpUp's journey.
- Goodyear formed a strategic relationship with Plus, a global provider of self-driving truck technology, to provide connected “intelligent” tires and optimized autonomous trucking solutions.
- The company began piloting select tire monitoring solutions with Voyomotive, a connected car technology company.
- In an industry first, Goodyear developed a non-pneumatic (“airless”) tire and wheel assembly to support autonomous vehicle transportation in a city setting with the Jacksonville (Florida) Transportation Authority.
- The airless tire was also adopted by Starship Technologies, a global provider of autonomous delivery services operating self-driving delivery robots.
- In Europe, Goodyear partnered with automotive leader ZF to integrate fleet and tire management solutions through a customizable telematics-based offering.
- Goodyear began collaborating with Dutch research organization TNO on a demonstration vehicle to test the interface of connected tires with a vehicle's anti-lock brake system.
- Goodyear announced a goal to achieve net-zero greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions by 2050, with minimal reliance on offsets.
- The company established new intermediate-term emission-reduction targets aligned with Science-Based Targets initiative (SBTi) standards.
- Goodyear announced a plan to procure 100% renewable energy in most of its facilities across Europe and Turkey by year-end.
- The company is working with third-party experts and developers, including Monolith, to develop carbon black produced from methane and biomethane suitable for tires.
- In January, Goodyear scientists and engineers constructed a demonstration tire with 70 percent sustainable-material content out of thirteen featured ingredients, including technical grade polyester sourced from recycled plastic bottles.
- The company is on track to reach its goal of a 100% sustainable-material and maintenance-free tire by 2030 and complete petroleum oil replacement in its products by 2040.
- Net sales were $17,478 million in 2021, compared to $12,321 million in 2020.
- Tire units sold reached 169.3 million in 2021, reflecting a 34.3% increase compared to 2020.
- Research and development expenditures totaled $496 million in 2021.
- Capital expenditures were $981 million in 2021.
- The company had 72,000 associates as of the end of 2021.
- Goodyear is experiencing shortages of qualified and reliable workers, particularly in the U.S., leading to manufacturing inefficiencies, increased training costs, and higher wages.
- To address labor shortages, Goodyear accelerated hiring, increased training capacity, and started adjusting future investment plans to consider worker availability.
- A severe winter storm in the first quarter of 2021 caused temporary shutdowns of three chemical facilities and limited production at three tire manufacturing facilities in the U.S.
- The company estimates the negative impact of the Q1 2021 winter storm on its earnings was approximately $54 million ($44 million after-tax and minority).
