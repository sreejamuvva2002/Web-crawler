# Aurubis Richmond

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Aurubis Richmond to be the first US multimetal recycling plant producing strategic metals - Engineer Live](https://www.engineerlive.com/aurubis-richmond-be-first-us-multimetal-recycling-plant-producing-strategic-metals/)  ·  `page_017473`

## Facts

- Aurubis, a global provider of non-ferrous metals and a copper recycler, has established a new US site called Aurubis Richmond in the state of Georgia.
- The facility will produce key strategic metals, including copper, nickel, tin, and precious metals.
- These metals are needed to expand data centres and AI applications, as well as for energy infrastructure, high-tech products, and the defence industry.
- The American industry alone requires approximately 1.8 million tonnes of copper annually.
- Experts predict demand for strategic metals like copper will increase by nearly 30% over the next five years.
- Currently, the US imports around half of the copper it processes.
- Aurubis Richmond is expected to reinforce the independence of American supply chains by processing complex recycling materials.
- A 'First Melt' celebration was held with participants including Rick W. Allen (US representative for Georgia's 12th District) and Jens Hanefeld (German ambassador to the US).
- Dr Toralf Haag, CEO of Aurubis AG, stated that Aurubis Richmond makes them the frontrunner in multimetal recycling and processing in the US, a position they already hold in Europe.
- Dr Toralf Haag noted that high availability of recycling material and good local conditions make the American market appealing for future expansion.
- Georgia Governor Brian P. Kemp stated in a video message that Aurubis Richmond is now a significant employer for Georgia and plays a major role in the American multimetal supply chain.
- Governor Kemp added that the facility stands as a testament to American manufacturing, independence, and resiliency.
- Aurubis is creating over 240 jobs in Georgia at the new facility.
- The plant is expected to process up to 180,000 tonnes of complex recycling material annually.
- The recycling materials will include printed circuit boards, copper cable, and other metal-bearing products.
- The plant will recover critical metals from these materials to close the value chain on-site.
- US Representative Rick W. Allen described Aurubis Richmond as a strategic site in Georgia’s 12th District that reinforces America’s ability to supply itself with metals for national security, infrastructure, and technology leadership.
- Rep. Allen noted that Aurubis exemplifies how international partnerships can contribute to solidifying America’s industrial foundation and strengthening economic independence.
- David Schultheis, president and managing director of Aurubis Richmond, stated the facility is now an important player in the North American multimetal supply chain.
- Schultheis expressed pride in the team that developed and built the state-of-the-art recycling plant.
- Schultheis thanked policymakers and governmental authorities in Georgia for strong support, smooth approval processes, and enabling quick settlement.
