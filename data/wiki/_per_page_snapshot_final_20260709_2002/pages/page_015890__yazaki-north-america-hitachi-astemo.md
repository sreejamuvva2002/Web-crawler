# Yazaki North America & Hitachi Astemo

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Embracing The Future: How Yazaki North America And Hitachi Astemo Are Leading The Industry 4.0 Revolution - MITechNews](https://mitechnews.com/featured/embracing-the-future-how-yazaki-north-america-and-hitachi-astemo-are-leading-the-industry-4-0-revolution/)  ·  `page_015890`

## Facts

- Yazaki North America, part of the Japanese-based Yazaki Group, created its Monozukuri Research Laboratory in Canton in 2014 to increase innovation and build customer trust.
- The lab's products include wire harnesses, power distribution and control products, connectors, driver information systems, sensors, and hybrid and electric vehicle products.
- Lab Director Doug Neely stated that reducing cost is the No. 1 reason for implementing smart manufacturing tools, with quality being important as well.
- The lab's mission remains researching technologies and testing them to determine if they reduce costs and improve efficiency in factories.
- Neely noted the goal is to bring more digitization into the factory with more confidence and control during product launches.
- Neely added, “We are just a ways off from modeling everything effectively.”
- Neely said there is no end in sight to the team’s work because new technologies with smart factory applications are constantly being invented.
- Neely stated, “How do we know we have arrived at Industry 4.0 compliance? We will never arrive because when we do, we’ll be in developing I5.0.”
- Neely expressed interest in exploring General Motors CEO Mark Reuss’s suggestion to capture knowledge as it happens using video technologies that could include smartphones.
- David E. Cole, founder of the Center for Automotive Research in Ann Arbor and former University of Michigan engineering professor, captured Reuss’s suggestion that he wanted to capture the innovation of his young engineers who are very tech savvy, even though they may not have a lot of real-world auto manufacturing experience.
- Hitachi Astemo Americas, a Tier 1 auto supplier, created what it calls a Smart Factory Center of Excellence.
- Maurice Henson, Senior Director of Operations – Information Technology at Hitachi Astemo Indiana, said the center is composed of cross-functional teams where experts specializing in essential areas for Hitachi regularly meet for developing products.
- The Smart Factory Center consolidates various hubs for gathering and spreading knowledge primarily for training purposes and is augmented by video training and knowledge capture.
- Henson noted that global and local groups have created many Smart Factory-focused videos posted on SharePoint to highlight how Hitachi Astemo is actively pushing forward with Industry 4.0.
- Hitachi Astemo’s Smart Factory initiative uses Amazon Web Services to aggregate data and make it easily available to all employees throughout the global corporation.
- An additional connected factory improvement is currently in development approval to create the Hitachi Astemo Standard to accelerate making data available to connected workers.
- Sudhanshu Gaur, vice president of R&D at Hitachi America Ltd and chief architect of Smart Manufacturing at Hitachi Astemo, said the company captures knowledge from employees using cloud-based platforms and Artificial Intelligence, with insights shared across the organization to enable informed decision making.
- Generative AI enhances information retrieval within Hitachi Astemo’s initiative.
- Gaur stated the vision is to provide timely and efficient access to designed information, tailoring the retrieval process to the specific needs and contexts of different teams within their global network.
- The company’s online Communities of Practice comprises various manufacturing sites that are actively adopting Digital Transformation initiatives, where practitioners collaborate, exchange ideas, discuss challenges, and share success stories.
- Hitachi Astemo captures knowledge from production operations using video snapshots of newly developed applications to demonstrate new ideas and advancements, encouraging other teams and sites to explore similar innovations.
- The company records interviews with staff members describing their experiences, insights, and best practices, ranging from engineers discussing complex problem-solving to operators sharing daily routines.
- Gaur said the company encourages teams to create short video snippets when facing specific challenges or issues, which are then shared with relevant experts to facilitate efficient troubleshooting.
- The company is using AI-driven insights from captured videos to improve production efficiency, automate quality inspections, and augment operators’ daily work routines.
- A Hitachi Astemo spokeswoman said the company is looking into how to capture knowledge as it happens using video technologies that could also include smartphones.
- Maurice Henson believes most Michigan manufacturers will adopt I4.0 technologies as businesses recognize the benefits of embracing these principles to enhance operations, improve efficiency, and stay competitive globally.
- Henson noted that recognizing this principle may mean the difference in how companies survive for the next 25 years in Michigan, which is largely known as the home of the US auto industry.
- Henson stated it will likely be necessary for automakers like General Motors and Ford Motor Company to demand I4.0 compliance by their respective supply chains.
- Sudhanshu Gaur echoed that a mandate by OEMs, together with MEDC incentives, can certainly drive compliance and create a standardized approach to adopting I4.0 practices.
- Gaur noted that I4.0’s focus on data-driven decision-making means seamless data exchange between manufacturers and OEMs can enhance the entire product lifecycle and potentially unlock new business opportunities.
- Gaur summarized that a healthy business ecosystem is needed to help set Industry 4.0 implementation goals and provide the right mentorship and training incentives to realize those goals.
- The article is the third in a series highlighting Michigan manufacturers addressing Industry 4.0 challenges, following stories on Michigan manufacturers needing to adopt I4.0 and General Motors embracing I4.0 technologies.
