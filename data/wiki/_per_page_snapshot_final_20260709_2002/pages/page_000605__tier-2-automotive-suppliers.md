# Tier-2 Automotive Suppliers

**Type:** supplier_relationship  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-03-16

**Source:** [How to Build a List of Tier-2 Automotive Suppliers by Region](https://www.facilitiesfinder.com/blog/tier-2-automotive-suppliers-by-region)  ·  `page_000605`

## Facts

- The US has roughly 3,000–4,000 active Tier-2 automotive suppliers, the large majority of them privately held companies with 50–500 employees.
- Tier-2 suppliers make components such as stamped metal parts, plastic housings, machined castings, and wire harnesses that get built into Tier-1 systems.
- The US Census assigns NAICS codes at the establishment level for automotive suppliers under NAICS 3361–3363.
- NAICS code 3361 covers Motor Vehicle Manufacturing.
- NAICS code 3362 covers Motor Vehicle Body and Trailer Manufacturing.
- NAICS code 3363 covers Motor Vehicle Parts Manufacturing.
- NAICS code 336310 covers Motor Vehicle Gasoline Engine and Engine Parts.
- NAICS code 336320 covers Motor Vehicle Electrical and Electronic Equipment.
- NAICS code 336330 covers Motor Vehicle Steering, Suspension, and Interrelated Parts.
- NAICS code 336340 covers Motor Vehicle Brake System Parts.
- NAICS code 336350 covers Motor Vehicle Transmission and Power Train Parts.
- NAICS code 336360 covers Motor Vehicle Seating and Interior Trim.
- NAICS code 336390 covers Other Motor Vehicle Parts.
- Some Tier-2 facilities also carry adjacent NAICS codes such as metal stamping (332116), rubber product manufacturing (326291), or precision machined components (332721).
- GM operates assembly plants in Flint (MI), Lansing (MI), Fort Wayne (IN), Bowling Green (KY), Spring Hill (TN), Wentzville (MO), Fairfax (KS), Arlington (TX), and Hamtramck/Factory Zero (MI).
- Ford operates assembly plants in Dearborn (MI), Chicago (IL), Kansas City (MO), Louisville (KY), Flat Rock (MI), Hermosillo (MX adjacent but worth noting for AZ/TX reps), and Wayne (MI).
- Stellantis operates plants in Detroit (Mack/Jefferson, MI), Belvidere (IL — idled/reopening), Toledo (OH), Windsor (ON adjacent), and Brampton (ON adjacent).
- Toyota operates assembly plants in Georgetown (KY), Princeton (IN), San Antonio (TX), and Buffalo (WV).
- Honda operates assembly plants in Marysville (OH), East Liberty (OH), Lincoln (AL).
- Nissan operates assembly plants in Smyrna (TN) and Canton (MS).
- Hyundai/Kia operates assembly plants in Montgomery (AL) and West Point (GA).
- BMW operates an assembly plant in Spartanburg (SC).
- Mercedes-Benz operates an assembly plant in Vance (AL).
- Volkswagen operates an assembly plant in Chattanooga (TN).
- Tesla operates assembly plants in Fremont (CA) and Austin (TX).
- Rivian operates an assembly plant in Normal (IL).
- EV battery plants include BlueOval (Marshall, MI + Stanton, TN), Ultium (Spring Hill, TN + Lordstown, OH + Lansing, MI + Tennessee), Toyota Battery (Liberty, NC), and Honda/LG (Jeffersonville, OH).
- Each of these OEM and battery plants anchors a 100–250 mile supplier radius.
- The Midwest and upper South are by far the densest regions for Tier-2 facilities, with the MI–OH–IN–KY–TN corridor containing the majority of US Tier-2 facilities.
- GM's Spring Hill plant produces the Cadillac XT5, XT6, and Chevy Colorado.
- GM's Spring Hill plant is surrounded by a dense Tier-1 and Tier-2 cluster across middle Tennessee, northern Alabama, and southern Kentucky.
- Querying suppliers around Spring Hill, TN can include state filters for TN, AL, KY, GA.
- Nissan Smyrna overlaps with the Toyota Georgetown radius in terms of supplier states (TN, AL, KY).
- Hyundai Montgomery's key supplier states include AL, GA, TN.
- BMW Spartanburg's key supplier states include SC, NC, GA.
- Mercedes Vance's key supplier states include AL, GA, MS.
- Tesla Austin's key supplier states include TX, OK.
- Rivian Normal's key supplier states include IL, IN, OH, MO.
- The US automotive supply chain is mid-transition from ICE to EV, creating two distinct prospect segments for automation vendors: high automation urgency (ICE-specific Tier-2s) and growth investment mode (EV-adjacent Tier-2s like battery pack components, power electronics, thermal management).
