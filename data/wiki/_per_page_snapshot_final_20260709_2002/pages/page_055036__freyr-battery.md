# FREYR Battery

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-11-14

**Source:** [Battery Manufacturer to Invest $2.57B, Create Over 700 Jobs in Coweta County - AllOnGeorgia](https://allongeorgia.com/georgia-business/battery-manufacturer-to-invest-2-57b-create-over-700-jobs-in-coweta-county/)  ·  `page_055036`

## Facts

- FREYR Battery will invest $2.57 billion into Georgia’s sustainable technology ecosystem.
- FREYR Battery will create 723 new jobs over the next seven years.
- The investment and job creation are for a manufacturing facility in Coweta County.
- Governor Brian Kemp welcomed FREYR to Georgia, citing the state's No. 1 ranking for business.
- FREYR is dedicated to supporting a domestic supply chain for renewable power sources.
- FREYR's battery cells can be used for stationary energy storage (ESS), electric mobility, and additional applications.
- FREYR Co-Founder and CEO Tom Einar Jensen stated the company is committed to decarbonizing transportation and energy sectors with U.S. partners.
- Tom Einar Jensen noted that as FREYR advances its U.S. expansion plan, it expects to make meaningful investments to spur job creation and develop localized, decarbonized supply chains in the U.S.
- FREYR's new facility will be located at the Bridgeport Industrial Park site.
- The Bridgeport Industrial Park site has been zoned for a mix of heavy and light industrial use since 2014.
- FREYR will hire technicians, operators, engineers, executives, and administrative staff.
- The new facility will be named Giga America.
- Giga America's planned first-phase production capacity is approximately 34 gigawatt hours (GWh).
- The facility will utilize the U.S.-based 24M Technologies SemiSolidTM platform to simplify production and increase efficiency.
- FREYR is currently evaluating clean power supply solutions for the new facility, including potential renewable energy from solar installations.
- Coweta County Development Authority Chair John Daviston welcomed FREYR Battery to the community.
- John Daviston stated the venture will bring quality, highly-skilled jobs and provide a significant stimulus to the local economy.
- Metro Atlanta Chamber President and CEO Katie Kirkpatrick welcomed FREYR Battery to metro Atlanta, noting the region's rapid growth as a hub for battery cell research and production.
- Katie Kirkpatrick stated FREYR will benefit from the region's vibrant research ecosystem and diverse talent pool.
- Project Manager John Soper represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on the project.
- The project was developed in partnership with the Coweta County Development Authority, the Metro Atlanta Chamber, Georgia EMC, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that companies like FREYR are driving a global revolution based on sustainable technology and solutions.
- Pat Wilson noted that electrification is changing how cities are powered and that Georgia is developing a battery ecosystem with manufacturers, recyclers, and customers to support home and commercial storage.
- Georgia's prime location, extensive infrastructure, skilled workforce, and business-friendly climate attract diverse industries focused on creating a sustainable future.
- Georgia is cultivating a vertically integrated supply chain to help companies increase efficiencies and reduce reliance on imported materials.
- FREYR Battery aims to provide industrial-scale clean battery solutions to reduce global emissions.
- FREYR Battery is listed on the New York Stock Exchange.
- FREYR's mission is to produce green battery cells to accelerate the decarbonization of energy and transportation systems globally.
- FREYR has commenced construction on its first factory in Mo i Rana, Norway.
- FREYR has announced potential industrial-scale battery cell production developments in Vaasa, Finland, and the United States.
- FREYR intends to install 50 GWh of battery cell capacity by 2025.
- FREYR plans to reach 100 GWh of annual capacity by 2028.
- FREYR targets 200 GWh of annual capacity by 2030.
