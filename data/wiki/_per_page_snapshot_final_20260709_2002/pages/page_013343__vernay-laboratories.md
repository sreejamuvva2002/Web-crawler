# Vernay Laboratories

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2025-05-06

## Facts

- On Thursday, April 24, Vernay’s Milledgeville team celebrated the grand re-opening of its manufacturing facility.
- The facility upgrades support Vernay’s expanding North American operations.
- More than 30 guests attended the celebration, including representatives from the Milledgeville-Baldwin County Chamber of Commerce and students from Baldwin High School.
- Vernay staff from across the region joined the ribbon-cutting ceremony, guided tours, and leadership remarks.
- Kara Lassiter, President and CEO of the Milledgeville-Baldwin County Chamber, spoke at the event.
- Doug Cumpston, Milledgeville Plant Manager, stated the facility is part of the community and recognized employee work.
- Andy Woodward, Global Vice President of Sales and Marketing for Vernay, was among the speakers.
- Guests explored new additions including a high-speed inspection machine and an upgraded electronic precision cutter during guided tours.
- A layout for a new product line planned for Q3 installation was revealed to guests.
- The facility changes represent a significant investment in efficiency and future capability to serve new and existing customers.
- Vernay invited Baldwin High School students to explore the plant to gain real-world insights into engineering and manufacturing careers.
- Roberto Hornung, Aviation Instructor at Baldwin High School, stated the event helped students connect classroom learning to real-world careers.
- Maurizio Trabucco, Director of Human Resources for North America, explained the upgrades aim to create a positive work environment that attracts and retains top talent.
- Vernay Laboratories has been a technology leader in the design, development, and manufacture of customized flow-control components since its founding in 1946.
- The company formulates its own compounds to address specific applications.
- Vernay’s products are used in millions of medical devices, automobiles, and consumer products.
- The privately held firm produces more than 1 billion components per year.
- Production draws from a library of more than 33,000 elastomeric materials and compound formulations.
- Vernay operates six plants around the world in the U.S., the Netherlands, Italy, and China.
