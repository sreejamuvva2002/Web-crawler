# Duckyang Ind. Co. Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2021-05-24

**Source:** [S. Korean-based company to open first-of-its kind manufacturing plant | KRDO](https://krdo.com/news/national-world/2021/05/24/s-korean-based-company-to-open-first-of-its-kind-manufacturing-plant/)  ·  `page_001687`

## Facts

- Duckyang, a South Korean-based company, will open its first U.S. manufacturing facility in Georgia.
- The company will invest $10 million to open a manufacturing plant in Jackson County.
- The project is expected to create an estimated 285 jobs.
- The new facility will be 230,000 square feet and located at 984 Broadway Avenue in Braselton.
- Duckyang is seeking to hire for production, maintenance, logistics, and warehouse management roles.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that the project will create high-paying, advanced automotive manufacturing jobs for hardworking Georgians in Jackson County.
- Georgia has remained the number one state for business for the past eight years according to Governor Kemp.
- Duckyang currently supplies cockpit modules to customers worldwide including Hyundai, Kia, Renault Samsung Motors, Genesis, and Mobis.
- Cockpit modules are auto parts made of more than 130 subcomponents found near the driver’s seat of a vehicle.
- The new Georgia facility will enable Duckyang to better serve automotive customers within the region.
- Dong-in Son, CEO of Duckyang Ind. Co. Ltd., stated that entering the U.S. electric vehicle battery market with SKBA will allow cooperation with other automotive companies like Hyundai and Kia Motors that already have a U.S. manufacturing base.
- The company expects business expansion opportunities to supply electric vehicle parts, interior parts including cockpit modules, door trim panel modules, and other flagship parts to global automakers based in the United States.
