# Georgia Economic Development Incentives & Financing Programs

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Georgia | Economic Development Incentives & Financing Programs](https://www.blsstrategies.com/incentives/georgia)  ·  `page_021097`

## Facts

- BLS & Co. periodically revises state incentive pages to provide current information on legislative and regulatory developments affecting available programs.
- Businesses that create net new full-time jobs may be eligible for an annual non-refundable and non-transferable tax credit of $1,250 to $4,000 per new job for 5 years.
- The Georgia facility must be engaged in a specified operation or the headquarters of a company engaged in a specified industry, including manufacturing, warehousing, distribution, logistics, software development, contact centers, and others.
- The exact value of the as-of-right Job Tax Credits depends on job creation and the development tier of the county or census tract where the project is located.
- Unused Job Tax Credits may be carried forward for up to 10 years.
- A company may claim either the Job Tax Credit or the Investment Tax Credit, but not both.
- The Quality Jobs Tax Credit is an as-of-right program offering tax credits to companies that create at least 50 net new jobs within a 24-month period with wages at or above 110% of the county average wage.
- Quality Jobs Tax Credits range from $2,500 to $5,000 per job, per year for 5 years.
- The credit amount depends on qualifying jobs' average wage compared to the county's average wage, ranging from $2,500 for wages at 110%-120% of the county average up to $5,000 for wages at 200% or greater.
- Quality Jobs Tax Credits can be used to offset up to 100% of state corporate income tax liability.
- If Quality Jobs Tax Credits exceed a company's tax liability in a given year, the excess may be used against state payroll withholding.
- Companies that create at least 1,800 net new jobs over six years and invest a minimum of $450 million or have an annual payroll of at least $150 million may claim the Mega Project Tax Credit of $5,250 per job, per year for 5 years.
- Companies investing at least $600 million are granted an additional two years to meet Mega Project job creation requirements.
- Companies investing at least $800 million are granted an additional four years to meet Mega Project job creation requirements.
- The Mega Project must pay an average wage above specified minimums or show high growth potential.
- Mega Project Tax Credits can offset up to 100% of state corporate income tax liability, with excess credits applied to state payroll withholding.
- Claiming the Mega Project Tax Credit forgives claiming any other credit besides R&D and retraining tax credits.
- Premium Tax Credits are used against annual premium tax applied to insurance companies and require satisfying job creation requirements specific to county tiers.
- The Premium Tax Credit award ranges from $750 to $3,500 per eligible job per year for up to five years.
- Unused Premium Tax Credits can be carried forward for 10 years.
- The Investment Tax Credit provides tax credits to companies engaged in manufacturing or telecommunications support that have operated in Georgia for at least 3 years.
- Businesses with at least $100,000 of qualified investment may be eligible for an Investment Tax Credit ranging from 1% to 8%.
- The actual Investment Tax Credit depends on the development tier of the county and the type of capital expenditures made.
- Investment Tax Credits can offset up to 50% of state corporate income tax liability, and eligible companies in Rural Counties within Tiers 1 to 2 can apply credits to payroll withholding.
- Unused Investment Tax Credits can be carried forward for up to 10 years.
- Port Activity Bonus Tax Credits are available to companies qualifying for the Job or Investment Tax Credit that increase imports or exports through a Georgia deepwater port by a specified amount over an indicated year.
- The base year port traffic minimum for Port Activity Bonus is 75 net tons, 5 containers, or 10 TEUs.
- The Port Activity Bonus provides an additional $1,250 tax credit per job, per year for up to five years when used with Job Tax Credits.
- When used with the Investment Tax Credit, the Port Activity Bonus increases the credit to the equivalent of a Tier 1 location regardless of the actual tier level.
- Businesses may claim a Research and Development Tax Credit equal to 10% of qualified R&D spending in Georgia minus a base amount.
- The R&D Tax Credit can offset up to 50% of net Georgia corporate income tax liability after all other credits have been applied.
- Excess R&D credits can be applied to state payroll withholding and carried forward for up to 10 years.
- The Life Sciences Manufacturing Tax Credit is a bonus added to the Job Tax Credit for manufacturing pharmaceuticals, medicines, and medical equipment/supplies in Georgia.
- Recipients of the Life Sciences Manufacturing Tax Credit must also be eligible for the Job Tax Credit.
- The Life Sciences Manufacturing Tax Credit equals an additional $1,250 for eligible jobs and may offset up to 100% of corporate income tax liability.
- Excess Life Sciences Manufacturing Tax Credits may offset state payroll withholding and can be carried forward for 10 years.
- Georgia exempts the 6%-9% sales tax that manufacturers must make for their operations, covering expenses like manufacturing machinery and equipment, repair to industrial machinery, raw materials and packaging, energy used in manufacturing, primary material handling equipment, pollution control equipment, and clean room equipment.
- Data centers are exempt from the 6%-9% sales tax if they create a specified number of new high-paying jobs and meet a minimum qualified investment threshold that depends on the county.
- The sale of certain computers is exempt when the total qualifying purchases of a high-tech company exceed $15 million.
- Effective January 1, 2024, any entity claiming the data center or high-tech sales tax exemption must pay 10% of all sales and use tax imposed on the first $15 million.
- Distribution centers are eligible for the 6%-9% sales tax exemption when they invest $5 million in the purchase or expansion of a facility in qualifying expenses.
- A distribution center cannot have retail sales above or equal to 15% of the facility's total revenues to qualify for the exemption.
- Businesses that retrain employees to use new equipment or learn new technology skills may qualify for a Retraining Tax Credit equal to 50% of direct training expenses.
- The maximum Retraining Tax Credit is $500 per full-time employee per approved training program per year, with a total cap of $1,250 per employee per year.
- Retraining Tax Credits can offset up to 50% of state corporate income tax liability and unused credits can be carried forward for up to 10 years.
- Retraining programs must be approved by the Technical College System of Georgia.
- The Quick Start Employee Training incentive provides training space, instructors, and all needed training materials for new employees in skill-based jobs at no cost to qualifying companies.
- Quick Start helps companies assess, select, and train new employees for expansion or relocation projects.
- Direct property tax abatements are statutorily banned in Georgia, but local communities can provide relief through bond-leaseback transactions of real and personal property.
- The public ownership structure enables a negotiated Payment-In-Lieu-Of-Taxes (PILOT) to effectuate a temporary partial tax exemption.
- Counties and Municipalities in Georgia have the option of enacting local property tax exemptions for four classes of inventory at 20%, 40%, 60%, 80%, or 100% under Level One Freeport Law.
- Class One inventory covers goods in the process of being manufactured or produced, including raw materials and partly finished goods.
- Class Two inventory covers finished goods manufactured within Georgia held by the manufacturer for a period not exceeding 12 months.
- Class Three inventory covers finished goods stored in a warehouse, dock, or wharf destined to be shipped outside Georgia for a period not exceeding 12 months.
- Class Four stock in trade covers fulfillment center inventory stored in a warehouse for a period not exceeding 12 months.
