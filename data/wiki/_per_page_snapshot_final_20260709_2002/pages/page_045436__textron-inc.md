# Textron Inc.

## Facts

- Textron Inc. announced its intent to separate its Industrial segment from its core aerospace and defense businesses to enhance strategic focus and drive long-term stakeholder value.
- The Industrial segment is composed of Kautex and Textron Specialized Vehicles.
- Textron intends to explore multiple paths for the separation, including a sale of the Industrial businesses or a tax-free separation into a standalone, publicly traded company.
- Following the separation, New Textron will become a pure-play aerospace and defense company aligned with its core franchises: Textron Aviation, Bell, and Textron Systems.
- Lisa M. Atherton serves as Chief Executive Officer of Textron.
- Scott C. Donnelly serves as Textron's Executive Chairman.
- New Textron expects over $12 billion in expected 2026 revenues and $19 billion in backlog post-separation.
- New Textron will be anchored by general aviation under the Cessna and Beechcraft brands, military and commercial rotorcraft under the Bell brand, and Textron Systems' aerospace and defense products and services.
- The Industrial segment expects over $3 billion in expected 2026 revenues post-separation.
- Kautex is a leader in plastic fuel systems, battery enclosures, and clear-vision systems for the automotive industry.
- Textron Specialized Vehicles manufactures specialized vehicles and equipment with globally recognized brands including E-Z-GO, PACE Technologies, Jacobsen, and TUG Technologies.
- The company is targeting completion of the separation within 12 to 18 months, subject to customary conditions including regulatory approvals and final board approval.
- Goldman Sachs & Co. LLC is serving as financial advisor to Textron for the transaction.
- Latham & Watkins LLP is serving as legal advisor to Textron for the transaction.
