# Elan Technology

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Ceramic Materials - Elan Technology](https://www.elantechnology.com/ceramics/ceramic-materials/)  ·  `page_054213`

## Facts

- Elan Technology offers several varieties of steatite, alumina and zirconia advanced ceramic materials.
- Steatite ceramic is a magnesium silicate material used in insulators/enclosures for electrical components, and in appliance, aerospace and automotive applications.
- Alumina ceramics are advanced ceramic components used in a wide range of applications such as electronics, pump components and automotive sensors.
- Zirconia ceramics are commonly used in applications such as valves, bearings and filters.
- 96% Alumina (102-04) has a density of 3.72, flexural strength of 353 MPa (51 psi × 103), and Young’s Modulus of 330 GPa.
- 96% Alumina is the most widely used composition of alumina that Elan Technology offers.
- 94% Alumina High CaO2 (102-03) has a density of 3.64, flexural strength of 362 MPa (52 psi × 103), and Young’s Modulus of 300 GPa.
- 94% Alumina Low CaO (102-02) has a density of 3.63, flexural strength of 364 MPa (53 psi × 103), and Young’s Modulus of 290 GPa.
- 85% Alumina 14% SiO2 (102-01) has a density of 3.35, flexural strength of 185 MPa (27 psi × 103), and Young’s Modulus of 245 GPa.
- The listed alumina grades are commonly used as electric insulators in electronics, pump components & automotive sensors.
- Steatite (101-01) has a density of 2.75, flexural strength of 178 MPa (26 psi × 103), and Young’s Modulus of 111 GPa.
- White Steatite (101-02) has a density of 2.56, flexural strength of 139 MPa (29 psi × 103), and Young’s Modulus of 97 GPa.
- Low Loss Steatite (101-03) has a density of 2.91, flexural strength of 152 MPa (22 psi × 103), and Young’s Modulus of 113 GPa.
- The steatite grades are commonly used for insulators/enclosures for electrical components.
- Cordierite (103-01) has a density of 2.26-2.30, flexural strength of 92 MPa (13 psi × 103), and Young’s Modulus of 220 GPa.
- Cordierite is commonly used in appliances, heating element supports, refractory supports (ovens/kilns) & thermocouples.
- Yttria Partially Stabilized Zirconia (104-01) has a density of 6.02, flexural strength of 976 MPa (142 psi × 103), and Young’s Modulus of 220 GPa.
- Yttria Partially Stabilized Zirconia is commonly used in pump components, cutting instruments, valves, bearings & filters.
- Elan Technology's engineers can assist with application support and material selection.
