# SungEel HiTech

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2022-08-19

**Source:** [Korean battery recycler invests millions in US hub • Recycling International](https://recyclinginternational.com/commodities/electronics-recycling/korean-battery-recycler-invests-millions-in-us-hub/50403/)  ·  `page_050754`

## Facts

- SungEel HiTech has announced the opening of its first US battery recycling plant.
- The South Korean firm will invest more than US$ 37 million in the new site.
- The new site will be operated by its subsidiary SungEel Recycling Park Georgia, LLC.
- The new battery recycling facility will be built in Stephens County, Texas.
- The project will create a total of 104 jobs.
- Operations at the new site are expected to begin in early 2024.
- This expansion follows SungEel’s decision to acquire Australian firm Envirostream earlier this year.
- Suk Jae Yim, representative of SungEel’s US venture, stated that their entry into Georgia is the last piece of the puzzle to help Georgia build a sustainable ecosystem for its electric vehicle supply chain.
- SungEel possesses more than 10 years of patented technology in complete recycling technology.
- The company's technology allows it to recover 95% of battery metals, including nickel, cobalt, and lithium.
- SungEel treats both end-of-life batteries and manufacturing scrap.
- Pat Wilson, Stephens County Development Authority commissioner, stated that creating the jobs of the future and protecting resources means preparing key industries, such as the automotive industry, for the next technological revolution.
- Pat Wilson met with SungEel in Korea during the week of the article's publication.
