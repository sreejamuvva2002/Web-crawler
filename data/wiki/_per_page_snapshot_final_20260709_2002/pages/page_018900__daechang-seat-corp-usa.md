# Daechang Seat Corp. USA

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-02

**Source:** [Daechang Seat Corp Opening New Facility In Georgia, Creating More Than 500 New Jobs | Doc and the Block](https://docandtheblock.com/daechang-seat-corp-opening-new-facility-in-georgia-creating-more-than-500-new-jobs/)  ·  `page_018900`

## Facts

- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp announced that Daechang Seat Corp. USA would be investing $72.5 million in a new facility.
- The investment will create over 500 new jobs.
- The new facility will be located at the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center, close to the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA).
- Daechang Seat Corp. USA is a supplier specializing in automotive seat frames.
- Governor Kemp stated that suppliers for the HMGMA project alone reach at least nine counties and are adding to the growing manufacturing base centered around Chatham County and the Savannah region.
- Daechang Seat Corp. (DSC) was established in 1979 in Kyungju, South Korea.
- DSC has become one of the largest seat frame producers in the world.
- The company currently employs more than 5,000 people across seven countries.
- Jinsuk Lee, CEO of Daechang Seat Savannah Corp., expressed appreciation to the State of Georgia, local communities, and the City of Savannah for creating improved opportunities and employment prospects within Chatham County and the Savannah area.
- DSC's primary objective is to drive growth through facility expansions and transform into a global entity.
- DSC currently holds a prominent partnership with Hyundai Transys and Hyundai Mobis at HMGMA.
- The company will hire for assembly operators, equipment technicians, mechanical engineers, and electrical engineers through local job fairs and website postings in the future.
- Georgia has pursued its entire EV supply chain, creating more than $25 billion in investments and 30,000 jobs since 2018.
- In fiscal year 2023, job creation in Georgia's automotive industry increased by 324 percent compared to fiscal year 2021.
- Suppliers attracted by Georgia's mobility original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) resulted in over $2 billion in investment across the state in FY23.
