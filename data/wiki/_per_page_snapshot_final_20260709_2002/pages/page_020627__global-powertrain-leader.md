# Global Powertrain Leader

## Facts

- The client is a global leader in powertrain technology.
- The company is headquartered in the European Union.
- The company was built on the industrial expertise and legacy of one of the world’s leading automotive manufacturers.
- The company operates manufacturing plants and warehouses across multiple continents.
- The company spans design, production, and distribution of powertrain systems.
- The group maintains a strong culture of safety, health, and continuous improvement embedded across every level of operations.
- Its Health, Safety, and Environment (HSE) policies target zero work-related illnesses and accidents.
- Dedicated teams are focused on injury prevention and ongoing improvement of workplace conditions.
- The client had developed a custom ergonomics standard tailored specifically to their work domain.
- The custom ergonomics standard was a rigorous framework that went beyond generic assessment methods.
- The process for executing ergonomic assessments remained manual prior to the new platform.
- Manual assessments were largely based on Excel spreadsheets and in-person observation by trained experts.
- Slow and resource-intensive execution created operational bottlenecks.
- Each manual evaluation took approximately 120 minutes per task.
- Hundreds of workstations existed across multiple countries.
- The assessment backlog grew faster than experts could clear it.
- Ergonomics specialists needed to travel to each facility for on-site observation.
- Expert travel generated approximately $10,000 per expert per year in travel costs.
- Travel dependency limited site coverage.
- With EHS teams stretched across safety audits, incident investigations, and compliance, ergonomic assessments were often the first activity deferred.
- Deferred assessments left risk unquantified.
- The client selected ErgoEdge to transform their ergonomics program from a manual, Excel-driven process to an AI-powered digital platform.
- A critical requirement was the ability to digitize and run their proprietary ergonomics standard alongside established open methods.
- ErgoEdge digitized the client’s proprietary scoring methodology directly into the platform.
- The custom standard runs alongside RULA, REBA, and NIOSH in a single unified environment.
- No separate tools or parallel spreadsheets are required.
- Frontline supervisors record workers using standard smartphones and tablets.
- No wearable sensors, depth cameras, or additional hardware are required.
- Videos are uploaded to a secure cloud for AI processing.
- Computer vision processes each video frame-by-frame.
- The system estimates 3D body joint positions and calculates risk scores.
- Comprehensive reports include posture simulation and recommendations.
- Experts no longer need to travel for assessments.
- Local teams capture video on-site while specialists review findings remotely from anywhere.
- The solution was deployed across 5 countries.
- Assessments are now 10X faster, taking roughly 10 minutes per task compared to the previous ~120 minutes.
- Ergonomic risk assessment transformed from a bottleneck into a routine activity.
- EHS teams assess three to four times more workstations, including previously unassessed lower-priority stations and remote sites.
- Remote assessment eliminated expert travel, recovering significant travel budget (~$10K saved per expert per year).
- Faster assessments, eliminated travel, and reduced consultant dependency substantially lowered per-assessment cost.
- Ergonomics became economically scalable.
- The digital platform enabled real-time demonstrations and interactive training for new EHS team members.
- New assessors can begin conducting assessments independently after a short onboarding session, replacing weeks of shadowing senior ergonomists.
- Most AI ergonomics platforms only support standard methods like RULA, REBA, and NIOSH.
- This implementation is distinguished by digitizing the client’s proprietary ergonomics standard developed specifically for their work domain and refined over years of internal expertise.
- The custom methodology was built directly into the platform alongside established open standards.
- A quote states: “ErgoEdge enabled our team to cover three to four times more workstations while reducing each assessment from two hours to just ten minutes. We can now run both our custom standard and established methods on a single platform.”
