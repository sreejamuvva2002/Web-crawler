# DPA Nuclear Fuel Cycle Consortium

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [Department of Energy’s Defense Production Act Consortium Unveils New Initiative to Grow Nation’s Nuclear Fuel Cycle | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/ne/articles/department-energys-defense-production-act-consortium-unveils-new-initiative-grow)  ·  `page_005577`

## Facts

- U.S Department of Energy’s Office of Nuclear Energy announced an ambitious new initiative to secure the nation’s nuclear fuel supply chain.
- The initiative operates through the Defense Production Act (DPA) Nuclear Fuel Cycle Consortium, where the federal government will work with the domestic nuclear industry to ensure enough nuclear fuel for current reactors and future advanced reactors.
- The Consortium is comprised of representatives from more than 90 companies spanning the nuclear industrial base.
- The initiative addresses all facets of the nuclear fuel supply chain including milling, conversion, enrichment, deconversion, fabrication, recycling, and reprocessing.
- The announcement took place at the Ronald Reagan Building and International Trade Center with partners and the public present.
- Under the “Nuclear Dominance — 3 by 33” campaign, the Consortium aims to achieve three goals by 2033.
- The first goal is to catalyze a secure and cost-competitive domestic fuel supply chain.
- The second goal is to accelerate advanced reactor deployment and close the fuel cycle.
- The third goal is to explore how the DPA framework can be activated to grow and align workforce, finance, innovation and collaboration in support of nuclear build out.
- The initiative will begin a series of 60-day sprints designed to make rapid progress on the Consortium’s goals.
- Assistant Secretary of Nuclear Energy Ted Garrish stated the work comes at a pivotal time for nuclear energy growth and expressed pleasure with the Committee's dedication, looking forward to rapid progress on near-term goals for a robust American-made supply of nuclear fuel.
- The country’s need for secure and reliable energy is projected to rise in coming years, driven by growth in industrial manufacturing and power needs of data centers supporting artificial intelligence.
- Nuclear energy currently provides the country with nearly 20 percent of its power.
- Long-term success of efforts to put more power on the grid through nuclear plant uprates, restarts, and commercial deployment of advanced reactors depends on nuclear fuel availability.
- In May of 2025, President Trump released four executive orders to catalyze a nuclear energy resurgence.
- Within months, DOE announced the creation of the DPA Consortium to seek voluntary agreements with U.S. companies focused on increasing fuel availability, providing more access to reliable power, and ending reliance on foreign sources of enriched uranium and critical materials.
