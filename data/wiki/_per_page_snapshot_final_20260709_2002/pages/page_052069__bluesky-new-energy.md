## Facts

- A "Solar Roof photovoltaic energy storage charging station" combines photovoltaic power generation (solar panels converting light to electricity), an energy storage system (battery modules storing electricity), and EV charging piles for renewable energy applications.
- Global EV car growth is expected to reach more than 30% in 2030, creating a huge gap in EV charging infrastructure.
- Some regions in China provide 30%~50% investment subsidies for photovoltaic energy storage charging projects.
- In Europe, carbon emission reduction trading (ETS) and green electricity certification (such as RECs) increase revenue for these stations.
- The typical ratio of photovoltaic installed capacity to energy storage capacity is 1:0.2 to 1:0.5.
- A 1:0.2 configuration ratio equates to 1MW photovoltaic plus 200kWh energy storage.
- The ratio of energy storage capacity to charging pile power usually ranges from 1:0.5 to 1:5, depending on the system's charge/discharge rate and charger power.
- A 1:1 storage-to-charger ratio means 200 kWh of energy storage supports a 200 kW EV charging pile for continuous 1-hour charging.
- For an example system with 1MW Solar PV, 200kWh energy storage, and 10 fast charging piles, the total investment is $275,000.
- In the example system, the PV system costs $110,000 (35%~50%), the energy storage system costs $83,000 (30%~40%), EV charging costs $41,000 (10%~15%), and construction/operation/maintenance costs $41,000 (5%~10%).
- The cost share of EV charging in the entire solar EV charging station is only about 15%.
- Split EV charging piles consist of a power cabinet and a charging terminal, suitable for large-scale stations to improve efficiency via intelligent distribution.
- Bluesky offers split liquid-cooled EV charging models BSEC-960K100S (960kW), BSEC-720K100S (720kW), and BSEC-4800K100S (480kW).
- Bluesky's power cabinet dimensions are 2400 mm × 1025 mm × 1830 mm, floor-mounted, with 95% efficiency at full load, air cooling, IP55 protection, and supports 4G/CAN/RS485 communication.
- The power cabinet input voltage is 380Vac±10%/620-800Vdc, frequency 50/60Hz, power factor ≥0.99, harmonic ≤5%, with input current ranging from 770A to 1920A and output voltage 200-1000Vdc.
- The power cabinet supports a maximum of 6 terminals with dual connectors, steady flow accuracy ≤ ±1%, voltage stabilization accuracy ≤ ±0.5%, operating temperature -40℃~+75℃, storage temperature -40℃~+70℃, altitude ≤2000m, and humidity 5%RH～95%RH.
- The power cabinet includes over-current, under-voltage/over-voltage, residual current, lightning, short circuit, and over-temperature protection, plus 220Vac auxiliary power for the terminal controller.
- Bluesky's liquid-cooling super charging terminals output a maximum of 500A per gun, while natural cooling fast charging terminals output 250A per gun.
- Terminal models BSEC-480K1000S and BSEC-1000K1000S have dimensions around 990x345x2140 mm or 962x664x1835 mm, floor-mounted, IP55 protection, operating temp -40℃~+75℃, noise ≤55dB/≤50dB, and storage temp -40℃~+70℃.
- Terminals support output voltage 200~1000Vdc, OCPP1.6J communication (LAN/WiFi/4G), authentication via RFID/APP/QR Code/POS, and connectors CCS2/CCS1/GBT/CHAdeMO.
- Integrated DC EV charging combines the power module, controller, and charging gun into one independent body.
- Integrated models CDZ-YL and CDZ-WY offer output powers of 60kW/120kW/160kW/180kW/240kW, dimensions around 1110x1260x2110 mm or 1110x600x2200 mm, and share similar environmental, communication, authentication, connector, and security specs with the terminals.
- The system uses matrix connection for intelligent power distribution, allowing each gun to call any module group, with power expansion possible by connecting two charging power sources in parallel.
- Tesla Kettleman City Supercharging Station configuration includes Solar Roof (2MW), Megapack energy storage (15MWh), and 50 units V3 supercharging piles.
- The Tesla station achieves a daily average power generation of 8 MWh, with energy storage covering 30% of charging needs and annual carbon reduction exceeding 5,000 tons.
- CATL's Photovoltaic Storage Charging and Inspection Intelligent Supercharging Station configuration includes Photovoltaic (150kW), Energy Storage (200kWh), 12 units 30kW DC piles, and a Rapid Detection System.
- The CATL station innovation integrates battery safety detection, allowing car owners to simultaneously detect battery health status while charging.
- Future trends include declining battery costs increasing the proportion of energy storage, and integrated photovoltaic storage and charging equipment from companies like Huawei and CATL reducing deployment costs for larger scale adoption.
- Innovative business models include virtual power plants (VPPs) participating in the electricity market for ancillary service revenue, and vehicle-to-grid (V2G) interaction using EV batteries as distributed energy storage units.
- Bluesky focuses on the R&D and manufacturing of EV charging station equipment, providing ODM/OEM services worldwide.
