# Denkai America

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2022-07-20

**Source:** [Copper is King in Augusta, Where Japanese Foil Manufacturer Plans to Invest $430 Million - Global Atlanta](https://www.globalatlanta.com/copper-is-king-in-augusta-where-japanese-foil-manufacturer-plans-to-invest-430-million/)  ·  `page_019248`

## Facts

- Augusta is hosting a second copper-focused project bringing hundreds of jobs and hundreds of millions in capital investment from a foreign entity to support Georgia’s electric-vehicle sector.
- Denkai America makes copper foil used in printed circuit boards for electronics in the aerospace and defense industry and is setting up a new facility producing similar materials for lithium-ion batteries powering future cars.
- The initial investment on the 115-acre site in Richmond County will be $150 million.
- The Japanese subsidiary plans to invest another $280 million and hire 250 people as it expands over two additional phases.
- The Augusta site will serve as the headquarters for Denkai Americas.
- Nobu Masuda, Denkai America’s president and CEO, stated in a news release: “We are thrilled to expand our manufacturing presence into Augusta, which has the skilled and talented work force our company needs for growth.”
- Denkai follows Aurubis AG, which broke ground on a Richmond County recycling plant producing blister copper, bringing more than 100 jobs and nearly $350 million in capital investment, constituting the largest single German investment in Georgia to date.
- Both projects reflect Georgia’s ongoing effort to recruit all components of the electric-vehicle value chain, from raw materials to cars produced by $5 billion-plus plants announced by Rivian and Hyundai, to ensure EV supply-chain resiliency.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson called the state's workforce opportunity a “generational opportunity.”
- Denkai America is a subsidiary of Nippon Denkai, based in Chikusei City in Ikabara prefecture, about two hours drive north of Tokyo.
- The company completed an acquisition of Oak Mitsui Inc. in 2020, including its plant in Camden, S.C., which it expanded with $14 million and 10 additional jobs last year.
- The South Carolina facility now employs more than 80 people and will continue operations.
- Denkai America initially planned the expansion in South Carolina, but site limitations forced a reassessment as growth projections became more ambitious.
- Denkai is already the market leader in copper foils for EV batteries in Japan, holding a 60 percent market share there and 40 percent in North America according to Nikkei.
- Denkai sees the market for anode materials growing globally by an average of 21.5 percent per year through 2035, with the U.S. and Japan taking a significant share.
- Company projections indicate that not only will the electric vehicle market grow, but battery sizes in cars will also increase, necessitating more copper foil for anodes.
- The company forecasts electrics in the U.S. and Japan combined will make up about a quarter of the 62 million EVs it believes will be sold globally by 2035, at a rate of more than 14 percent a year.
- When the plant begins shipping samples in the summer of 2024, it will be the only copper foil manufacturer on the East Coast.
- The facility will help battery makers like Georgia’s SK Battery reduce dependence on imports and lower their geopolitical risk, according to an outline on the company’s website.
- In switching from South Carolina to Georgia, Denkai increased its initial phase production capacity from 9,000 metric tons to 9,500 metric tons and raised its planned capital investment by $10 million.
- The plant is expected to triple in size over subsequent phases as dictated by market demand.
- A key differentiator for Georgia was Georgia Power’s willingness to lock in a competitive rate for energy prices well into the future, according to a presentation on the company’s website.
- The company will reportedly receive land valued at $2.25 million, according to the Associated Press, alongside other incentives likely to materialize, including training from Georgia Quick Start.
- Training for new hires in Georgia will be completed at the South Carolina plant, located just two hours away.
- Denkai will be hiring for skilled industrial technicians, production managers, and engineers.
