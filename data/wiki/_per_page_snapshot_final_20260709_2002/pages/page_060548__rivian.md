# Rivian

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2021-12-17

**Source:** [Rivian Expanding to Georgia With 2nd BEV Plant | WardsAuto](https://www.wardsauto.com/electric/rivian-expanding-to-georgia-with-2nd-bev-plant)  ·  `page_060548`

## Facts

- Bolstered by a stock offering last month that raised an estimated $13 billion, Rivian says it is moving ahead with plans to expand by building a new manufacturing plant on a site near Atlanta, GA.
- The new $5 billion plant will be built on a 2,000-acre site spread across Morgan and Walton counties to the east of Atlanta.
- The “carbon conscious” campus, when completed, will expand the company’s capacity to build electric vans, trucks and SUVs.
- Construction on the new Rivian plant is expected to begin in summer 2022.
- The plant will have capacity to build 400,000 vehicles annually.
- The plant will employ 7,500 workers when fully operational.
- Production at the new Georgia plant is slated to begin in 2024.
- Rivian Chief People Officer Helen Russell stated the company is happy to partner with Georgia on its new manufacturing site, which will build next-generation products important for scaling the business.
- Russell noted their work together is driven by a compelling purpose and rooted in building a highly collaborative environment that creates a true sense of belonging.
- Both General Motors and Ford previously had plants in Atlanta.
- The Ford plant in Atlanta closed in 2006.
- The GM plant in Doraville closed in 2008.
- Kia now operates in the Southwest corner of Georgia.
- Helen Russell said they are excited for Georgians to bring their creativity and ideas to Rivian.
- Russell stated that alongside Illinois, Georgia is the place for Rivian to continue to scale and thrive.
- Rivian is scaling up capacity at its existing plant in Normal, IL, with a 623,000-sq.-ft. addition.
- The addition will bring the total footprint of the Normal plant to approximately 4 million sq.-ft.
- The Rivian plant in Normal already employs more than 3,000 people.
- The automaker plans to add up to another 1,000 employees at the Normal plant by the second quarter of 2022.
- Rivian considered settling in Texas before choosing Georgia.
- The company is eager to hire employees in Georgia.
- Town hall meetings are being planned for 2022 near the manufacturing site so area residents can learn more about jobs, the company and its plans.
- It has launched a careers portal to scout for employment prospects with the company.
- After a long ramp up, Rivian is producing and shipping its first product, the R1T electric pickup truck.
- An all-electric SUV, the R1S, is expected to ship from the Normal plant in early 2022.
- Rivian has contracts to build delivery vans for Amazon.
- Amazon now owns about 20% of Rivian's shares.
- Rivian’s market capitalization is now greater than both GM and Ford and second only to Tesla.
- Rivian will deliver its first 1,000 vehicles to customers by the end of 2021.
- According to SEC filings, Rivian remains a money-losing startup.
- Ford is trimming its ties to Rivian.
- The two companies confirmed they were canceling plans to develop an EV together for the Lincoln brand.
- Due to earlier investments, Ford owns a 12% stake in Rivian.
- Rivian was valued at nearly $10 billion after its initial public offering.
- The IPO is financing Rivian’s expansion and continuing product development.
- The IPO is making the company’s founder R.J. Scaringe a billionaire.
- Scaringe began planning for Rivian while he was a graduate student in automotive engineering at MIT.
- MIT became one of the sources of seed money for the EV company.
- Scaringe founded the company in 2009 when GM and Chrysler were filing for bankruptcy.
- In 2018, Scaringe told Wards that at the time of founding, investors had no interest in a car company.
