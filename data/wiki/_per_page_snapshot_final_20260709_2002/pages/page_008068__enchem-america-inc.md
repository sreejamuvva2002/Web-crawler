## Facts

- EnChem America, Inc. is a U.S. corporation engaged in the development, manufacture, and sale of battery electrolyte materials.
- EnChem America, Inc. is a U.S. subsidiary of EnChem Co., Ltd., a corporation organized under the laws of the Republic of Korea.
- On May 28, 2026, The GrowHub Limited (Nasdaq: TGHL) entered into a binding summary term sheet with EnChem America, Inc. for a proposed business combination.
- Under the proposed merger, EnChem America will merge with a newly formed, wholly-owned subsidiary of GrowHub and become the surviving company post-merger.
- Immediately upon closing, EnChem America's pre-closing equity holders will receive 85% of the equity of GrowHub on a fully diluted basis.
- Pre-closing equity holders of GrowHub will retain 15% of the equity of GrowHub on a fully diluted basis immediately following closing.
- The agreed minimum equity value of EnChem America for purposes of the merger is USD 400,000,000.
- Post-closing ownership allocation reflects 142,848,176 shares for EnChem (85.0%) and 25,299,810 shares for TGHL existing holders (15.0%), totaling 168,147,986 total shares.
- If the closing VWAP is less than USD 4.00 per share, EnChem America has a walk-away right to terminate the agreement without liability within five business days of the VWAP determination date.
- The closing VWAP is defined as the volume-weighted average trading price of TGHL Common Stock on Nasdaq for the twenty consecutive trading days ending three trading days prior to the closing date.
- The parties anticipate one or more capital raising transactions in connection with or following the closing.
- Upon becoming F-3 eligible, GrowHub shall file a shelf registration statement registering an amount not less than USD 200,000,000.
- Post-closing, the combined company may conduct capital raises using follow-on offerings, Equity Lines of Credit on form F-1 or S-1, PIPEs, registered direct offerings, confidentially markets public offerings, at-the-market programs, or convertible debt offerings.
- Conditions to closing include execution of merger documents, no governmental orders prohibiting the transaction, regulatory approvals (HSR, CFIUS if applicable), Nasdaq approval for listing of Merger Shares, requisite stockholder approvals, and SEC effectiveness of required registration statements.
- Public company stockholders must approve the issuance of Merger Shares pursuant to Nasdaq Listing Rule 5635(a).
- Private company shareholders must approve the merger under applicable corporate law.
- A joint registration statement on Form S-4 or Form F-4, including a proxy statement, will be filed with the SEC as soon as reasonably practicable following execution of the Merger Agreement.
- EnChem America shall deliver PCAOB audited financial statements prepared in accordance with U.S. GAAP to GrowHub.
- Upon closing, all incumbent directors of GrowHub shall resign, and all post-closing board seats (5 to 7 directors) will be filled by individuals designated by EnChem America, subject to a minimum of three satisfying Nasdaq director independence requirements.
- All senior executive officers of GrowHub (including CEO, CFO, and other C-suite positions) shall tender their resignations effective at the closing.
- The post-closing senior management team will be designated by EnChem America.
- Upon closing, all Class B shares will convert to Class A on a 1:1 basis, constituting a full transfer of economic ownership and operational control to EnChem America.
- An Amended and Restated Certificate of Incorporation will eliminate Class B Common Stock and re-designate all shares as a single class of Common Stock.
- All Merger Shares issued to pre-closing equity holders of EnChem America shall be subject to lock-up restrictions for 180 days following the closing date.
- GrowHub shall execute a Registration Rights Agreement to register the resale of Merger Shares under the shelf or a new registration statement upon expiration of the lock-up period, maintaining such registration for not less than 36 months following lock-up expiration.
- The exclusivity period runs from execution through the earlier of 90 days or mutual written termination, with automatic 30-day renewal periods unless non-renewal notice is given.
- During exclusivity, GrowHub cannot solicit, initiate, or encourage inquiries for alternative merger or business combination transactions, except for bona fide superior proposals considered in good faith by the board.
- The transaction may be terminated by mutual consent, if closing has not occurred within 12 months of the Merger Agreement date, upon a final non-appealable governmental order, failure of stockholder approvals, or material uncured breach with a 30-day cure period.
- Each party shall bear its own transaction costs and expenses, with shared costs allocated equally.
- The definitive Merger Agreement will contain customary representations, warranties, covenants, conditions, and indemnification provisions.
- Ancillary documents may include Lock-Up Agreements, Registration Rights Agreement, Voting Agreements, Officer and Director Indemnification Agreements, and Amended Certificate of Incorporation and Bylaws.
- The term sheet and exchanged information are strictly confidential, with exceptions for advisors, SEC/Nasdaq rules, regulatory approvals, and required KOSDAQ disclosures in Korea.
- The transaction is governed by Delaware law, with disputes subject to the exclusive jurisdiction of the Court of Chancery of the State of Delaware, waiving jury trial rights.
- All terms become legally binding and enforceable simultaneously with formal signing and sealing by both parties.
