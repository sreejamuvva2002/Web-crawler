# Novelis Inc

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2018-01-23

**Source:** [Novelis to build US$300m aluminum plant amid auto optimism - The Business Times](https://www.businesstimes.com.sg/companies-markets/transport-logistics/novelis-build-us300m-aluminum-plant-amid-auto-optimism)  ·  `page_047444`

## Facts

- Novelis Inc plans to build a US$300 million plant in Kentucky targeted at automotive customers looking to minimize vehicle weight amid more stringent efficiency rules.
- Novelis Chief Executive Officer Steve Fisher stated the investment reiterates the industry's view on strong penetration of aluminum as the material of choice on vehicles and does not see it as a leap of faith.
- The facility will be located in Guthrie, Kentucky.
- The plant is expected to create about 125 jobs.
- The facility will have a capacity of 200,000 metric tons.
- Novelis operates as the US unit of Hindalco Industries Ltd.
- Novelis expects to complete the project by 2020.
- Indian billionaire Kumar Mangalam Birla is expanding his overseas operations through Novelis.
- Automotive companies continue to expand the amount of aluminum used in vehicles, particularly in trucks and SUVs.
- An industry report projects that aluminum content in North American passenger vehicles will increase 42 percent by 2028 from 2015 levels.
- Last April, Braidy Industries Inc. announced plans for a US$1.3 billion aluminum mill in eastern Kentucky.
- Braidy Industries CEO Craig Bouchard stated the mill would be the first of its kind in three decades in the US.
- Two years prior to publication, aluminum prices were near their lowest in almost seven years due to a China-led glut.
- Novelis CEO Steve Fisher stated he did not expect any near-term relief from the China-led glut at that time.
- Chinese smelting cutbacks have helped send aluminum prices back to their highest level since 2012.
- The price increase provides incentives for producers in the US and elsewhere up and down the supply chain to restart capacity or undertake new projects.
- Via Novelis, Hindalco is among bidders for US producer Aleris Corp.
