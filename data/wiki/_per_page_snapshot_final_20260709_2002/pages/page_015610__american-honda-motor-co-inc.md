# American Honda Motor Co., Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-04-24

**Source:** [Honda Recognizes 56 North American Suppliers with Awards for Operational Excellence](https://hondanews.com/en-US/releases/release-00b08be100b45a4ab9ae5793dd0da49b-honda-recognizes-56-north-american-suppliers-with-awards-for-operational-excellence)  ·  `page_015610`

## Facts

- Honda recognized 56 North American suppliers during its annual Honda Supplier Conference in Columbus, Ohio, with awards for outstanding performance in supplying parts and materials in 2025.
- Thirty-seven suppliers were honored for supporting Honda with parts and materials for mass production, and 19 suppliers were honored for supplying service parts that serve the needs of customers after they purchase a Honda or Acura product.
- Paul Dentinger, senior vice president of the Purchasing & Supply Chain Center at Honda Development & Manufacturing of America, LLC, stated that Honda is focusing its automobile business on maximizing hybrid and gas-powered models while continuing to invest in its North American supplier network.
- Honda purchased over $35 billion in parts for mass production operations from North American suppliers in 2025.
- The purchased parts were used to produce more than 1.52 million Honda and Acura vehicles sold in North America in 2025.
- Honda also purchased more than $1.78 billion in parts from Service Parts suppliers used by Honda and Acura dealers to serve customers in North America.
- Mass production supplier awards were given in four categories: Excellence in Quality and Delivery; Value; Quality, Delivery & Value; and Sustainability.
- Honda conducts business with approximately 700 OEM suppliers across North America that provide parts to the company's 13 plants nationwide producing Honda and Acura vehicles, engines, and transmissions.
- Service parts awards reflect a supplier's combined performance in quality, cost, and on-time delivery.
- Honda works with nearly 1,600 service parts suppliers in North America to provide service and accessory parts for more than 26 million Honda and Acura automobiles in operation in the United States, as well as for powersports and power equipment products.
- Honda manages a national network of parts and distribution facilities that deliver high-quality service parts to customers through Honda and Acura dealers across the country and support global parts operations.
- Astemo Rock Spring, Inc., located in Rock Spring, Georgia, was recognized as a 2025 Mass Production Supplier Award Winner.
- Maxxis International - USA, located in Suwanee, Georgia, was recognized as a 2025 Mass Production Supplier Award Winner.
- Honda has produced automobiles in North America for over 40 years, beginning in November 1982 at the Marysville Auto Plant in Ohio.
- In 2025, nearly 99% of all Honda and Acura automobiles sold in the U.S. were made in North America.
- Honda established manufacturing operations in North America in 1979 with motorcycle production in Marysville, Ohio.
- Honda currently employs about 33,000 associates in North America who support manufacturing operations at 18 plants.
- These 18 plants have an annual capacity to produce 1.6 million automobiles, nearly 2 million engines, 52,000 power equipment products, and about 250,000 powersports products.
- The facilities also produce the HondaJet advanced light jet and GE Honda HF120 turbofan engines.
- Cumulatively, Honda has invested more than $25 billion in its North American manufacturing capabilities, including more than $3.1 billion over the past five years alone.
- The company works with over 750 original equipment suppliers in North America with cumulative parts purchases of over $631 billion since 1982.
