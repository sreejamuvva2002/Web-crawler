# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-02

**Source:** [Hyundai Metaplant begins producing a third vehicle - Statesboro Herald](https://www.statesboroherald.com/local/hyundai-metaplant-begins-producing-a-third-vehicle/)  ·  `page_001086`

## Facts

- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp drove the new hybrid vehicle during its rollout on Tuesday.
- The Kia Sportage Turbo-Hybrid is the first Kia and first hybrid electric vehicle produced at the Ellabell plant.
- It is the third vehicle manufactured at the site since production began in 2022, joining the Hyundai IONIQ 5 and IONIQ 9.
- This marks the first time the Sportage Hybrid is being manufactured in the U.S., though it has been produced in Korea.
- Gov. Kemp, Kia North America and Kia America President and CEO Seungkyu (Sean) Yoon, and Kia Georgia President and CEO Stuart Countess joined about 200 Metaplant employees to celebrate the vehicle reveal.
- The hybrid Kia Sportage debuted on an Autonomous Mobile Robot, a machine used throughout HMGMA's production processes.
- Two yellow "robot dogs" greeted guests at the event.
- Gov. Kemp called the reveal a historic moment and emphasized the partnership between Kia America, HMG's Metaplant, the local community, and Georgians to support jobs for years to come.
- The new vehicle's production marks the first step in the Metaplant's pivot to producing hybrid vehicles.
- Chris Susock, chief manufacturing officer for Hyundai Motor North America, stated the plant's original intention was to manufacture all-electric vehicles.
- Susock cited a sharp decline in U.S. electric vehicle demand, especially following the expiration of federal tax credits outlined in President Donald Trump's "big beautiful bill."
- Midway through construction, HMGMA officials decided to shift focus to make the plant adaptable to hybrid electric vehicle production to balance market shifts.
- Susock highlighted the flexibility in automation and technology, allowing the plant to switch powertrain mixes quickly based on market demand.
- Last month, HMGMA shipped about 6,000 units outside the facility.
- This shipment volume represents about 50% of the plant's current capacity.
- The plant is ramping up to reach full capacity of about 580,000 units by 2028.
- Product planning will now look at forecasts to determine what models to build, with capability to quickly switch between Kias, different hybrids, Genesises, or other models.
- Kia President and CEO Seungkyu (Sean) Yoon noted the timing aligns with higher demand for hybrids, especially SUVs like the Sportage, Carnival, and Telluride.
- HMGMA President and CEO Tony Heo stated employees received extensive training to produce the new vehicle.
- Many team members traveled to Kia facilities in West Point, Georgia, and South Korea to learn best practices for Sportage Hybrid production.
- Both the Ellabell Metaplant and Kia's plant in West Point emphasize creating high-quality jobs and economic opportunities for their local communities and across Georgia.
- Yoon stated the Savannah Metaplant testifies to confidence in Georgia's future as an automotive powerhouse and expects it to maintain strong momentum with the Sportage Hybrid becoming one of Kia's highest volume models.
