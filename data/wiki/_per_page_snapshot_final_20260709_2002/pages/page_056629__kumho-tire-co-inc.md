# Kumho Tire Co., Inc.

## Facts

- Kumho Tire Co., Inc. was founded in 1960 in Gwangju, South Korea, by entrepreneur Pak In-chon as a workshop producing 20 tires per day.
- The company is currently among the top 20 global tire manufacturers with revenue exceeding $2.27 billion.
- Kumho's country of origin is South Korea, combining Japanese discipline, American technology, and a philosophy of rapid improvement.
- Three plants in South Korea are located in Pyeongtaek, Gokseong, and Gwangju, producing high-tech models for the premium segment.
- Since 2018, 45% of Kumho's shares have been controlled by the Chinese group Doublestar.
- The brand operates eight production facilities across four countries: South Korea, China, Vietnam, and the USA.
- Production locations include Pyeongtaek, Gokseong, and Gwangju in South Korea for premium lines, R&D, and aviation tires; Tianjin, Nanjing, and Changchun in China for the mass segment and Asian export; Binh Duong in Vietnam for eco-lines and cost reduction; and Macon, Georgia, USA for the North American market and localization.
- The Macon, Georgia plant was launched in 2016 to serve the North American market, reducing delivery times for local dealers.
- The country of manufacture depends on the specific batch and is indicated on the tire sidewall.
- There are no assembly facilities for Kumho within the Russian Federation.
- An official representative operates in Moscow at Krasnopresnenskaya Naberezhnaya, 12, coordinating import, EAEU certification, warranty service, dealer relations, marketing support, and staff training.
- After 2022, the brand adapted supplies to the Russian market through parallel imports and official partners.
- Authenticity can be verified by checking DOT or E1 markings on the sidewall.
- The company operates five technological centers in Gwangju, Akron, Frankfurt, Tianjin, and Seoul, investing millions of dollars annually in new compounds and constructions.
- Key innovations include ESCOT technology for contact patch optimization, XRP construction for reinforced sidewalls, and 3D siping for wet road grip.
- Mercedes-Benz, BMW, Volkswagen, Hyundai, and Kia use Kumho tires in the original equipment of certain models.
- Independent tests show Ecsta PS91 and WinterCraft WP52 models consistently rank in the top 10 by ADAC and Autobild.
- Passenger car tire recommendations include Ecowing ES31 for low rolling resistance, Solus HA32+ for all-season urban use, Ecsta PS91 for dry asphalt grip, and Ecsta HS52 for sporty handling.
- SUV/crossover tires include Crugen HP71 (all-season) and Road Venture AT52 (off-road).
- Winter solutions include WinterCraft Ice WI31 (studded) and WinterCraft WP52 (friction/non-stud).
- Optimal tire age is no more than 3 years from production, indicated by four digits in an oval on the sidewall.
- Chinese plants operate under unified corporate standards with quality control centralized in Gwangju.
- Winter lines are tested in Ivalo, Finland above the Arctic Circle, and all-season models are adapted for temperature swings from -35°C to +40°C.
- Doublestar's investments enabled equipment modernization and expanded R&D, confirmed by ESG ratings and EcoVadis Gold awards.
- By 2028, a first European plant is planned in Opole, Poland, with $587 million in investments to reduce logistics costs and speed up deliveries to Eastern Europe, including Russia.
- Green technology development aims for eco-friendly materials to reach 44.5% of production by 2026.
- Ecowing-marked models demonstrate a 3-5% reduction in fuel consumption due to optimized rubber compound.
- Tire lifespan with proper operation and maintenance is 40,000–60,000 km.
- Warranty terms depend on the model and region, coordinated by the Moscow office.
