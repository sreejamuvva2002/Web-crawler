## Facts

- Ford CEO Jim Farley was recently seen discussing the company's plans for its newest EV plant outside Memphis, Tennessee.
- The new Tennessee EV plant is projected to create 6,000 new jobs once operational.
- The new Tennessee EV plant is projected to generate $5.6 billion.
- Ford announced potential losses due to significant investments in boosting EV production.
- By the end of 2023, Ford aims to produce 600,000 electric vehicles annually.
- Ford aims to produce two million electric vehicles annually by 2026.
- Jim Farley advocates for a robust, U.S.-based supply chain from mining to battery production to reduce dependency on overseas sources.
- Jim Farley stated: "We have to have mines and processing to build a digital economy here in the U.S. We cannot continue to import batteries and rare earth from overseas. We have to move it to America."
- Ford's electric vehicle division is currently in a "startup" phase, requiring heavy investment in finances and resources to build foundations for a sustainable electric future.
- Ford's all-new electric truck will be equipped with updatable software akin to smartphones.
