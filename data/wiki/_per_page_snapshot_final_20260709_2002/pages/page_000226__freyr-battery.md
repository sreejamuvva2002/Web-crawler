# Freyr Battery

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-11-11

**Source:** [Georgia Lands New Gigantic $2.6B Electric Battery Plant](https://www.govtech.com/transportation/georgia-lands-new-gigantic-2-6b-electric-battery-plant)  ·  `page_000226`

## Facts

- Freyr Battery is a Norway-based clean-tech company named after a Norse god.
- Freyr Battery will invest $2.57 billion in Georgia by building a plant on a 368-acre site in Coweta County.
- The facility will produce Lithium-Ion battery cells for stationary energy storage, electric mobility, and additional applications.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp touted Freyr as the latest investment in Georgia’s fast-growing electric battery industry.
- Georgia’s electric battery industry is bolstered by forthcoming electric vehicle factories and parts suppliers.
- Tom Einar Jenson serves as Freyr’s co-founder and CEO.
- Jenson stated the Coweta factory represents a large step in enhancing the U.S. domestic supply chain for Lithium-Ion batteries, a technology currently dominated by China.
- Jensen said Freyr is deeply committed to decarbonizing the transportation and energy sectors with U.S. partners.
- Freyr expects to make meaningful investments to spur job creation and develop localized, decarbonized supply chains in the U.S. to enhance energy security and economic activity.
- The factory will be named Giga America.
- Giga America is expected to create 723 jobs over the next seven years.
- Georgia and Coweta County are collectively providing strong financial incentives for the Giga America project, though specific amounts were not disclosed.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development declined to provide further details on the incentives, stating the project is still active.
- The facility will be located at the Bridgeport Industrial Park site.
- Bridgeport Industrial Park is an industrial megasite off I-85 near Newnan.
- Coweta County Development Authority Chair John Daviston welcomed the venture, citing the addition of quality and highly-skilled jobs as a significant stimulus to the local economy.
