# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-08

**Source:** [From Global to Local: Industries that Put Georgia on the Map - Tifton CEO](https://tiftonceo.com/features/2024/08/global-local-industries-put-georgia-map/)  ·  `page_050116`

## Facts

- The State of Georgia maintains full-time representation in a dozen international markets.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development and its economic development partners capitalized on the 2024 SelectUSA Investment Summit to demonstrate why Georgia stands out to international companies seeking to locate or expand in the U.S.
- Georgia has emerged as a leader in the e-mobility sector, with more than $27.3 billion in investments since 2018, creating over 32,200 new jobs.
- Major Original Equipment Manufacturers (OEMs) making significant investments include Kia, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA), Rivian, and Archer Aviation.
- Battery manufacturers at the forefront of battery innovation include SK Battery America and LG Energy Solution.
- Recyclers and fuel cell manufacturers advancing recycling and hydrogen fuel technologies include Aurubis AG, Ascend Elements, and Plug Power.
- Georgia’s traditional automotive sector exports reached $4.1 billion in 2023.
- The Georgia Ports Authority’s Brunswick facility is the second-busiest automotive Roll-on/Roll-off port in the U.S.
- The state offers an affordable cost of doing business, available sites, and a robust workforce pipeline from institutions like the University System of Georgia and the Technical College System of Georgia.
- The Georgia Center of Innovation supports e-mobility, aerospace, and defense sectors with industry-specific expertise and strategic partnerships.
- For over a decade, Georgia has established itself as a prime location for global business growth, offering diverse opportunities across key industries.
- Georgia ensures a steady, skilled workforce pipeline through its 22 technical colleges across the state.
