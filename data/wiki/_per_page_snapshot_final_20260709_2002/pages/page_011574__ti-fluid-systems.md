# TI Fluid Systems

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** thermal_management  ·  **Published:** 2024-07-03

**Source:** [New Michigan e-Mobility Innovation Center to fast-track EV development - EV Engineering & Infrastructure](https://www.evengineeringonline.com/new-michigan-e-mobility-innovation-center-to-fast-track-ev-development/)  ·  `page_011574`

## Facts

- TI Fluid Systems has officially opened its all-new e-Mobility Innovation Center (eMIC) in Auburn Hills.
- The Auburn Hills eMIC is designed to foster customer collaboration and enable North American automakers to develop thermal management systems for hybrid, plug-in hybrid, and battery electric vehicles more quickly and efficiently.
- The new facility spans 48,000 square feet.
- The facility was constructed around TI Fluid Systems’ Core Competency model, bringing together virtual engineering, designing, processing, prototyping, product testing, and vehicle testing under one roof.
- The center reduces the development time for prototype thermal management components and systems from six months to as little as two weeks.
- The facility can achieve system-weight reductions of up to 60 percent.
- The facility can achieve overall cost savings of up to 30 percent.
- The facility features equipment that enables rapid prototyping of fully functioning components and systems using 3D printing and injection moulding.
- The facility includes two vehicle lifts and 42 electric vehicle charging stations.
- Hans Dieltjens, president and CEO of TI Fluid Systems, stated that the Auburn Hills eMIC represents a significant step forward in automotive thermal management development in North America.
- Dieltjens noted that the center enables speeded-up development, nurtures collaboration, and promotes innovation, which is crucial for the continued progress of hybrid, plug-in hybrid, and battery electric vehicles in North America and beyond.
- The Auburn Hills facility is one of five eMICs opened worldwide since 2022.
- Each eMIC is located in a key region for automotive and e-Mobility development, including China, Germany, Japan, South Korea, and the USA.
- Dieltjens stated that constructing five eMICs in key e-Mobility development regions across the world underlines TI Fluid Systems’ commitment to supporting customers to remain competitive in a fast-changing automotive landscape.
- He added that it highlights the difference an innovative approach to thermal management can have on the efficiency, performance, and cost of an electric vehicle.
- TI Fluid Systems is listed on the London Stock Exchange under the ticker TIFS.
- The company's corporate headquarters is located in Auburn Hills, Michigan.
- TI Fluid Systems has 8,600 employees.
- The company operates 21 manufacturing locations in the Americas, including 12 locations in the United States.
- Alongside thermal management solutions for hybrid, plug-in hybrid, and battery electric vehicles, TI Fluid Systems provides fuel systems, lines and connectors, and thermal management solutions for internal combustion engine vehicles.
