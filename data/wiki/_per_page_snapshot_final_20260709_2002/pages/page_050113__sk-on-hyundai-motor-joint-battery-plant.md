# SK On-Hyundai Motor Joint Battery Plant

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [SK On and Hyundai Motor Joint Battery Plant Clinches 'Deal of the Year' Award - Korea IT Times](https://www.koreaittimes.com/news/articleView.html?idxno=126581)  ·  `page_050113`

## Facts

- The Georgia Economic Developers Association announced on November 13 that the joint battery plant project of SK On and Hyundai Motor Group won the "Deal of the Year" award in the Large Community category.
- The Georgia Economic Developers Association initiated its "Deal of the Year" award in 2008.
- The association annually selects award nominees based on their economic impact on Georgia, contract finalization complexity, and required expertise.
- SK On and Hyundai Motor Group disclosed a collaborative investment of USD 5 billion (approximately KRW 6.5 trillion).
- The investment is for constructing a 35 GWh battery production plant in Bartow County, Georgia.
- The joint plant has a targeted operational commencement date in the second half of 2025.
- SK On previously won the "Deal of the Year" award in the Mid-size Community category in 2019.
- The 2019 award was for SK On's battery production plant, "SK Battery America," located in Commerce, Georgia.
- SK On plays a crucial role in establishing the EV industry belt in the southeastern U.S.
- Commissioner Pat Wilson of the Georgia Department of Economic Development highlighted SK On's pivotal role in the EV industry belt.
- Pat Wilson stated that SK Battery America was the first mover in creating an American battery facility.
- Pat Wilson emphasized that SK's facility opening marked the beginning of an evolving ecosystem for battery production in the U.S.
- SK On secured the top position on Fortune magazine's "Change the World" list of innovative companies for its leadership in North American electrification.
- SK On currently operates two battery production plants in Georgia.
- SK On is actively constructing a joint battery plant with Hyundai Motor Group.
- SK On is simultaneously building three additional battery plants: two in Kentucky and one in Tennessee.
- The three additional battery plants are being built in collaboration with Ford Motor Company.
- The completion of the Kentucky and Tennessee plants in 2025 is expected to bring SK On's total U.S. battery production capacity to over 180 GWh.
