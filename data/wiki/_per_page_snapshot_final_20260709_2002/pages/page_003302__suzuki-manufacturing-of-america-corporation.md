## Facts

- Georgia Foreign-Trade Zone, Inc., grantee of FTZ 26, submitted a notification of proposed production activity to the FTZ Board on behalf of Suzuki Manufacturing of America Corporation (SMAC), operator of Subzone 26L.
- SMAC operates facilities located in Rome, Jonesboro, and Cartersville, Georgia.
- The notification conforming to FTZ Board regulations was received on May 21, 2014.
- SMAC already has authority to produce all-terrain vehicles (ATVs) and related components including carriers, footrests, fuel tanks, grips/handle bars, frames, and rear box assemblies.
- The current request would add certain foreign components to the scope of authority for production under FTZ procedures.
- Production under FTZ procedures could exempt SMAC from customs duty payments on foreign status components used in export production.
- For domestic sales, SMAC can choose the duty rate during customs entry procedures that applies to ATVs and related components (2.5%) for the noted foreign status inputs.
- Customs duties could possibly be deferred or reduced on foreign status production equipment.
- Components sourced from abroad include plastic resins, chip plates, rubber hoses with/without fittings, rubber v-belts, tires and tubes, steel tubing, runners/banners, printed books/manuals/brochures, chain cam/oil pump drives, fasteners of steel and aluminum, aluminum gaskets, mountings and brackets, fenders, oil strainers, crankshaft bearings and assemblies, bearing housings, metal gaskets, stainless o-rings, batteries, spark plugs, starter motors, capacitive discharge ignition (CDI) units, magnetos, compact disc sets, copper and paper gaskets, gear shift assemblies, light bulbs, KD parts of ATVs, gear/fork shafts, therma-coupler nozzles, speedometers, and battery plates.
- The duty rate for the sourced components ranges from free to 9.0%.
- Public comment on the proposed activity was invited until July 22, 2014.
- Andrew McGilvray serves as the Executive Secretary of the FTZ Board.
