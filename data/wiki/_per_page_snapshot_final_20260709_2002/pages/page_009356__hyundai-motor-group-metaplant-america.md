# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-05-15

**Source:** [Hyundai avoids millions in fines over wastewater violations - The Current](https://thecurrentga.org/2025/05/12/hyundai-fined-30000-over-wastewater-violations/)  ·  `page_009356`

## Facts

- Georgia regulators fined Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America $30,000 for improper disposal of wastewater from its Bryan County electric vehicle manufacturing site.
- The fine could have potentially surpassed $7 million under Georgia law.
- Officials from the Georgia Environmental Protection Division and Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America reached a settlement.
- On April 25, officials signed a consent agreement outlining the alleged violations and the agreed-upon fine.
- The consent agreement notes that the Director determined an amicable disposition of the alleged violations is in the best interest of the citizens of the State of Georgia.
- The consent agreement alleges two violations: Hyundai discharged industrial wastewater to a publicly owned treatment works without an industrial pretreatment permit for such discharge.
- The second alleged violation is that Hyundai used a sewage holding tank at the facility for purposes other than receiving sanitary waste from construction trailers at a construction site.
- The Current first reported on Hyundai’s wastewater troubles in March.
- Hyundai’s initial wastewater disposal problems date to September when the EV plant began production.
- The plant needed to dispose of industrial wastewater from processes including metal finishing and car washing.
- Construction of the EV plant had outpaced the construction of a new $129 million North Bryan County wastewater treatment facility just across I-16.
- Anticipating a short-term need for wastewater treatment until the Bryan facility came online, Hyundai applied for and received a permit from the city of Savannah to have its wastewater treated at the city’s Travis Field facility.
- The pre-arranged stopgap treatment began in September but lasted only a few weeks before Hyundai’s wastewater exceeded limits on contaminants, including copper and zinc.
- The city of Savannah stopped accepting Hyundai’s wastewater after it exceeded contaminant limits.
- Savannah issues its own permits to companies seeking treatment of industrial wastewater.
- The April 25 consent agreement does not address the violation of the permit Savannah issued to Hyundai.
- Savannah took action regarding the permit violation, with city spokesman Joshua Peacock stating that the penalty for pretreatment permit violation was the cessation of flow on Sept. 26, 2024, and flow has not resumed.
- With Travis Field unwilling to take the wastewater, Hyundai began a “pump and haul” operation that continued to the present.
- The pump and haul operation involves holding wastewater onsite in sewage holding tanks and trucking it out to third-party treatment facilities, two of them publicly owned.
- Treating industrial wastewater at a publicly owned wastewater treatment facility without first getting a pretreatment permit is not allowed.
- Hyundai hauled its waste to the Richmond Hill facility late last year.
- Hyundai also pumped waste to a tank on the Bryan County facility across I-16.
- These alleged violations occurred for about 143 days from about October 1, 2024, until February 21, 2025, confirmed by EPD officials.
- Georgia law provides for fines of up to $50,000 a day for each day that a violation occurs.
- The maximum allowable fine for the alleged violation totals $7.15 million.
- The use of sewage-holding tanks after the construction period ended is not allowed under Georgia law.
- That practice appears to have begun at the Hyundai facility around Oct. 1, 2024.
- The practice continues to the present, though the company is working with regulators to resolve this violation and follow conditions of the consent order to ensure safe operation of pumping and hauling the contents of the holding tanks.
- It is unclear how many days Hyundai would have been subject to a fine under the sewage-holding tank alleged violation.
- Savannah and Bryan County officials declined to comment on the fines placed on Hyundai.
- The Ogeechee Riverkeeper discovered the wastewater issues as a result of Georgia Open Records Act requests.
- The Ogeechee Riverkeeper said the alleged violations are serious.
- The Ogeechee Riverkeeper wrote in a prepared statement that industrial waste being delivered to non-permitted treatment facilities is an imminent threat to water resources and human health.
- The Ogeechee Riverkeeper expressed gladness that the Georgia EPD took enforcement actions against these violations and expects it will serve as a deterrent to industrial wastewater producers against any future violations.
- The Ogeechee Riverkeeper hopes the facility has been able to correct whatever issues have been preventing it from meeting the City of Savannah’s pretreatment standards.
- The Ogeechee Riverkeeper encouraged Bryan County to require the facility to meet pretreatment standards that protect the Ogeechee River before allowing any industrial wastewater to be discharged to the new North Bryan Water Reclamation Facility.
- EPD has not yet signed off on a plan to return Hyundai to compliance with wastewater disposal.
- Wastewater is currently being transported to off-site permitted facilities.
- HMGMA submitted its Corrective Action Plan to Georgia EPD and is waiting for their review and approval, according to HMGMA spokeswoman Bianca Johnson.
- Following EPD approval, HMGMA intends to return to the use of Travis Field until operations begin at the Bryan County Wastewater Reclamation Facility.
- The new facility is still months away from being operational.
- Bryan County spokesman Nicholas Beard wrote that the county is anticipating the Water Reclamation Facility being operational in Q3 of this year (August-October timeframe).
