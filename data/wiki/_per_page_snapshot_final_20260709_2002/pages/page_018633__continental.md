# Continental

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-02-27

**Source:** [Struggling German Auto Supplier Continental to Sell Unit](https://english.aawsat.com/business/5291984-struggling-german-auto-supplier-continental-sell-unit)  ·  `page_018633`

## Facts

- German auto supplier Continental announced a four-billion-euro ($4.6-billion) deal to sell its ContiTech division to a US investment group.
- The sale is the latest move in a major restructuring as Continental struggles amid a crisis in the wider European auto sector.
- Continental has been slimming down its operations to focus on its traditional tire business.
- ContiTech manufactures plastic and rubber products for industrial clients.
- In addition to the four-billion-euro sale price, Continental could receive up to 250 million euros more in coming years depending on the division's performance.
- The planned sale of ContiTech completes Continental's strategic realignment.
- The deal is expected to be finalized by the end of 2026.
- Some funds from the sale will be used to pay down debt, while around 2.5 billion euros will be distributed to shareholders.
- Last year, Continental spun off a division focused on car components including braking systems and sensors as a separate entity called Aumovio.
- The group has announced thousands of job cuts in recent years, alongside other auto suppliers and manufacturers in Germany.
- The broader automotive sector is facing fierce Chinese competition, weak demand in key markets, and a troubled shift to electric vehicles.
