# L.K. Technology Holdings Limited

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-01

**Source:** [L.K. Technology Announces 2025/2026 Annual Results](https://brandspurng.com/2026/07/01/l-k-technology-announces-2025-2026-annual-results/)  ·  `page_011524`

## Facts

- L.K. Technology Holdings Limited (Stock code: 558) announced annual results for the twelve months ended 31 March, 2026.
- The group operates in the intelligent equipment manufacturing industry and faced cyclical adjustments with demand-side pressures from downstream vehicle enterprises' slowing retail sales growth and tightened capital expenditures.
- Revenue for the year amounted to HK$5,609 million; gross profit was HK$1,375 million with a 24.5% gross margin; adjusted net profit was HK$49.5 million with a 0.9% net profit margin.
- Total assets reached HK$13.3 billion (up 11.2% year-on-year), with net cash of HK$1.32 billion.
- The group has three core business segments: die-casting (metal blank forming), injection molding (plastic part forming), and CNC machining centre (high-precision finishing).
- Die-casting machinery is the core business, generating HK$3,663 million in revenue (65.3% of total), primarily serving automotive vehicle industries.
- Injection molding segment generated HK$1,798 million in revenue (32.1% of total), with toy industry revenue growing over 55% for two consecutive years to 16.7% of contribution, achieving a 3-year CAGR of 38.7%.
- CNC machining centre business generated HK$148 million in revenue (2.6% of total).
- R&D expenses reached HK$318 million during the year, representing a 24.6% year-on-year increase.
- The group commercialized the world’s first 16,000T ultra-large intelligent die-casting unit, setting a new industry benchmark.
- The group continues to secure and deliver multiple 9000T-class ultra-large die casting machines.
- LK TECH's 6,000T–16,000T ultra-large intelligent die-casting cells integrate digital twin technology with the LK-NET cloud die-casting management system and were selected as one of the “Outstanding Cases of New Quality Productive Forces in the Automotive Industry 2025”.
- These solutions are fully compatible with mass production requirements for full vehicle body structural components.
- As at the end of the year, LK TECH ranked first globally in cumulative number of ultra-large die-casting machines delivered.
- The group's TPI semi-solid magnesium alloy forming technology covers a full-scale product matrix from small precision parts to ultra-large structural parts and supports modular transformation of traditional die-casting equipment.
- This technology enables a 50% reduction in energy consumption and a 20% increase in product toughness, supporting commercialization of magnesium alloys.
- The group's self-developed 5,000-tonne TPI semi-solid forming module secured an order from a leading OEM for inner tailgate panels for new energy vehicles.
- Integrating functions previously requiring nine separate sheet metal parts into one component achieves a 45% weight reduction and increases material utilization to 70%.
- Orders for TPI magnesium alloy equipment grew strongly, with shipment value exceeding RMB100 million.
- Revenue from the related magnesium alloy business increased by 291.1% in the second half of the financial year compared to the first half.
- The group developed zirconium-based amorphous alloy die casting equipment integrating vacuum melting, automatic solid material feeding, and full-process automation, reaching internationally advanced standards for high-end products like foldable screen hinges and medical implants.
- Low-pressure die-casting machines feature innovative inert gas pressurisation and waste heat recovery technologies, reducing overall energy consumption by 30%, used for automotive wheels and motor end covers.
- Horizontal extrusion casting machines meet production requirements for high-performance components such as automotive steering knuckles, subframes, and battery end plates.
- The group developed an integrated intelligent machining system for post-processing large die-cast components, with local data processing exceeding 90% and abnormality prediction accuracy over 95%.
- Technology completed engineering validation on five-axis gantry machining centres and post-processing equipment for large die-cast components.
- The group overcame technical challenges in high-precision forming for hydraulic valves, enhancing in-house capabilities for core component supply.
- Leveraging digital twin technology, the group developed intelligent die-casting cells capable of intelligent process parameter prediction, remote diagnostics, and digitalized management.
- To respond to cyclical fluctuations, the group diversified into new productivity tracks including energy storage, AI computing power, humanoid robots, and specialised casting.
- Revenue from Europe surged by 64.4% year-on-year due to robust demand for new energy equipment and high-end manufacturing.
- The group secured a high-end equipment order from a well-known European vehicle enterprise, breaking market barriers for Chinese companies entering mainstream European and American vehicle supply chains.
- Revenue from other emerging markets and the Asia-Pacific region increased by 76.9% year-on-year due to industrial relocation and rapid order growth.
- The localized sales and service network covers Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia.
- Revenue from the North American market declined year-on-year, but diversified global presence mitigated regional fluctuations.
- CEO Liu Zhuo Ming stated the group will leverage technological strengths against cyclical headwinds, push into energy storage, photovoltaics, robotics, and computing power cooling, and build a smart service system centred on ‘AI + Equipment’.
- LK TECH is one of the world’s largest die-casting machine manufacturers, engaging in design, manufacture, and sales of die-casting machines, plastic injection moulding machines, and CNC machining centres.
- Products are widely used in automotive bodies and accessories, household appliances, accessories, and electronic products.
- The group has 15 manufacturing bases and 60 sales and service centers worldwide.
- LK TECH owns Idra, a world-class precision machinery brand that developed the world’s first Giga Press ultra-large die-casting equipment adopted by leading global new energy vehicle manufacturers.
- Sales and service companies were established in the United States and India to capture overseas markets.
- The group operates a casting factory in Fuxin, China, for producing cast iron/steel components to strengthen upstream and downstream synergies.
