# Suzuki Manufacturing of America Corporation (SMAC)

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-03-05

**Source:** [Suzuki Manufacturing Of America Becomes Subsidiary Of Suzuki Motor USA - Roadracing World Magazine | Motorcycle Riding, Racing & Tech News](https://www.roadracingworld.com/news/suzuki-manufacturing-of-america-becomes-subsidiary-of-suzuki-motor-usa/)  ·  `page_010824`

## Facts

- Suzuki Manufacturing of America Corporation (SMAC) has become a 100% subsidiary of Suzuki Motor USA, LLC (SMO) effective March 1, 2024.
- SMAC was founded in 2001 and is based in Rome, Georgia.
- SMAC serves as Suzuki’s All-Terrain Vehicle (ATV) manufacturing facility.
- For over two decades, all Suzuki adult KingQuad ATVs have been manufactured at the Rome, Georgia location.
- Prior to the change, SMAC stock was owned by Suzuki Motor Corporation (SMC) (20%) and Suzuki Motor of America, Inc. (SMAI) (80%).
- SMAI is owned by SMC and acts as the holding company for Suzuki companies operating in the United States.
- The organizational change aims to consolidate ATV sales and production arms to strengthen the overall ATV business in the United States and globally.
- Masami Haga, President of SMO, stated a primary goal is to expand commitment to the growth of the entire powersports business.
- The move will enable building on relationships with Suzuki dealers and SMAC vendor partners.
- Strengthening the supply chain and purchasing policies for locally sourced parts are objectives of the organizational change.
- Integrated production and sales functions are expected to improve responsiveness to the ATV market by more accurately linking production to sales demand.
- SMO aims to improve inventory management for both the company and its dealer body.
- Improved inventory management is intended to protect sales margins, flooring costs, and ultimately the profitability of both groups.
- ATV industry sales are generally steady throughout the calendar year with demand typically increasing in the fall months.
- The change will result in better, more direct communication between SMO and SMAC resulting in improved staff interaction between the two companies.
- A more collaborative environment will be created between the production and sales groups of Suzuki ATVs.
- Better connection will be fostered between the Rome- and Brea-based teams.
- Suzuki’s United States distribution headquarters are in Brea, California, where it relocated in 1981 after first coming to the United States in 1963.
- SMAC becoming a subsidiary of SMO sets the stage for new Suzuki ATV offerings and enhancements to existing products in the future.
