# Rivian Automotive

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-05-03

**Source:** [Georgia sets $1.5B in aid for electric vehicle maker Rivian | AP News](https://apnews.com/article/technology-business-georgia-atlanta-electric-vehicles-172bfd6fcd2db913fb11532ab57ba6b7)  ·  `page_001042`

## Facts

- The state of Georgia and local governments will give Rivian Automotive $1.5 billion of incentives to build a 7,500-job, $5 billion electric vehicle plant east of Atlanta.
- The size of the incentive package reflects the largest single industrial announcement in Georgia history.
- Rivian pledges to reach full investment and job targets by the end of 2028.
- Jobs at the new plant will pay an average of $56,000 a year, plus benefits.
- The state hopes Rivian will anchor an entire electric vehicle industry in Georgia.
- Greg LeRoy, executive director of Good Jobs First, stated it is the largest incentive package any American state has ever given to an auto plant.
- Rivian is based in Irvine, California.
- Rivian is a startup manufacturer of electric trucks and commercial delivery vans.
- The company challenges established automakers like Ford and General Motors and electric vehicle leader Tesla.
- Rivian is already producing vehicles at its existing facility in Normal, Illinois.
- Rivian hopes to break ground on the Georgia plant as early as this summer.
- Rivian plans to begin production in Georgia in 2024.
- The company aims to produce 400,000 vehicles a year in Georgia.
- Rivian stated the long-term economic partnership promises to deliver value to the company, Georgians, and future generations.
- The plant site covers 2,000 acres (800 hectares).
- The site is located between Social Circle and Rutledge, about 45 miles (70 kilometers) east of downtown Atlanta.
- The state took over planning and zoning for the project after opponents overwhelmed Morgan County officials.
- Residents have voiced concerns about possible well-water contamination, light pollution, and the disruption of wildlife and farmland.
- A site plan shows Rivian responding to concerns by shifting the plant away from wetlands and agreeing to limit light pollution.
- Former U.S. Sen. David Perdue is challenging Kemp in the May 24 Republican primary and lines up with opponents of the project.
- George Soros bought $2 billion worth of shares around the time Rivian chose Georgia but owns only 2% of Rivian, with no evidence he influenced the plant location.
- Perdue criticized the deal, saying it pads Soros' pockets and leaves hardworking Georgians footing the bill instead of helping small businesses.
- Governor Kemp reiterated his backing for Rivian during a debate, supporting 7,500 jobs going to rural Georgia.
- Local governments have agreed to $700 million in property tax breaks for the project.
- Rivian plans to make more than $300 million in payments in lieu of taxes over 25 years beginning in 2023.
- The state would spend $200 million to buy the site, grade it, build road improvements including a new Interstate 20 interchange, and extend utilities.
- Georgia would spend $62.5 million to build a dedicated training center for the plant.
- Georgia would spend a projected $27 million on providing job training.
- Other benefits include a $200 million income tax credit at $5,250 per job over five years.
- If Rivian did not owe that much state corporate income tax, Georgia would give personal income taxes collected from workers instead.
- Georgia estimates sales tax exemptions will save Rivian $175 million on machinery and $105 million on construction materials.
- Kia received more than $450 million in incentives for its plant in West Point, southwest of Atlanta.
- Georgia promised SK Innovation $300 million in incentives for a $2.6 billion, 2,600-worker battery plant northeast of Atlanta.
- The state has protections to claw back money if Rivian falls below 80% of promised investment or employment.
- The state touts a $420 million annual payroll from the project.
- An analysis shows there will be 8,000 more jobs created elsewhere in the state with a total impact of more than $7 billion.
- LeRoy said incentives will cost $200,000 per job and state and local governments will never collect enough increased taxes to cover that.
- Rivian currently plans two consumer models: the R1T pickup with a base price of $67,500 and the R1S SUV with a base price of $70,000.
- Amazon owns 18% of Rivian and has ordered 100,000 delivery vans, launching the company into commercial vehicles.
- Rivian raised $11.9 billion in a stock offering on Nov. 10 to finance the new plant.
- Rivian's shares have fallen 70% since the stock offering as investors worry about production delays.
