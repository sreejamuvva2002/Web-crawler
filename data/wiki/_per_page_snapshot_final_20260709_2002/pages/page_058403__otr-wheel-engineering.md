# OTR Wheel Engineering

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2017-02-05

**Source:** [Rome’s OTR Wheel Engineering on a roll | AP News](https://apnews.com/article/business-rome-dcfbc7cc50bc4fbda72615f44f81b3a9)  ·  `page_058403`

## Facts

- Fred Taylor relocated from Ellsworth, Michigan to Rome a little more than three decades ago.
- Taylor opened his own business in the tillage tool industry providing items such as plows and disks upon moving to Rome with his wife, Mary Taylor, in 1983.
- OTR Wheel Engineering is located at 6 Riverside Industrial Park in Rome.
- The company expects to make more than $200 million in sales in 2017.
- OTR has offices across the U.S. and the globe, including China, England, Sri Lanka, and France.
- The business employs 125 workers in Floyd County and more than 600 worldwide.
- After four years in Rome, Taylor purchased an Illinois-based company that made wheel and rim parts.
- Taylor started the business with $5,000 in savings alongside Charles Jackson from Cave Spring.
- In its first year, the company did $50,000 in business, made $10,000 in profit, and split $5,000 each.
- During the second year, OTR did $300,000 in sales and hired its first employee, a truck driver.
- In 1990, OTR hit $1 million in sales.
- The company grew as much as 50 percent a year during the first few years.
- Taylor and Jackson's goal was to reach $30 million a year in sales with a 10 percent pretax net profit, which they achieved in 1998.
- In its 30th year (2017), OTR and its affiliate Green Carbon expect to do $220 million in sales.
- The company reported annual double-digit growth until 2016, which was flat, but returned to double-digit growth in 2017.
- Growth is attributed to developing new products and innovating industry-specific wheels, parts, and components for distribution to tire dealers throughout the country.
- In 1994, OTR began working with tires, developing the 'Outrigger' tire for aerial work platforms (construction lifts), which is now on 90 percent of them around the world.
- The number of wheel assembly manufacturers decreased from over a half-dozen to four.
- In 1999, OTR partnered with Kubota Tractor to develop lawn mower tires, supplying the company's North American branch.
- Supplying the all-terrain vehicle industry has been the primary driver of OTR's growth.
- Honda is OTR's second-biggest customer behind Kubota for their side-by-side ATVs.
- OTR holds about 20 percent of the lawn and garden market and only 5 percent of the RTV and ATV industry, leaving room for growth.
- OTR secured a contract with Oshkosh for a series of newly developed tire and wheel assemblies.
- The company also struck a deal with Arctic Cat.
- OTR facilities in China make large forged wheels used on massive mining trucks.
- Rome-based affiliate Green Carbon recycles mammoth tires into their original steel, carbon black, and petroleum products.
- The headquarters plant is located in the old Celanese Industrial Park.
- OTR purchased most of the old Galey & Lord mill in Shannon, with production set to begin later in 2017.
- About a year and a half prior to 2017, OTR bought the old Spears Mattress plant in the Celanese complex to use as a warehouse for after-market sales products.
- After-market sales doubled in the two years leading up to 2017.
- Charles Jackson was elected to the Cave Spring City Council in November and makes sales calls 8-10 times a month across the country and Europe.
