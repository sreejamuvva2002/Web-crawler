# Hyundai Mobis

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hyundai Mobis to supply chassis modules for four Mercedes-Benz e-SUVs | Autocar Professional](https://www.autocarpro.in/news-international/hyundai-mobis-to-supply-chassis-modules-for-four-mercedesbenz-esuvs-103079)  ·  `page_001164`

## Facts

- Hyundai Mobis’ Alabama plant begins production of chassis modules which will be installed in four all-electric Mercedes-Benz SUVs.
- Hyundai Mobis has begun supplies of chassis modules for Mercedes-Benz’s electric vehicle models in the US market, becoming the first non-European supplier for chassis modules for the German luxury carmaker.
- This is the second time that large-scale module orders have been won overseas by Hyundai Mobis, after Chrysler in the United States.
- Hyundai Mobis has built a plant in McAlla, Alabama near Mercedes-Benz's US plant for this chassis module supply and started mass production from the third quarter of this year.
- This facility is its fifth production base in the US, following the Alabama and Georgia plants that supply modules to Hyundai Motors and Kia, and the Michigan and Ohio plants that supply Chrysler modules.
- A chassis module refers to a large-scale component combination that combines steering, braking, and suspension devices around a frame under the vehicle body.
- The chassis module is divided into a front chassis and a rear chassis according to the mounting location and corresponds to a core system that determines the driving stability of a vehicle.
- Hyundai Mobis will supply its chassis modules for pure electric vehicles, specifically four SUV models equipped with an electric vehicle-only platform.
- According to the mass production schedule for these models, Hyundai Mobis plans to supply front chassis and rear chassis modules sequentially.
- In the process of winning the chassis module order, Hyundai Mobis held several technical briefing sessions and directly demonstrated the module factory mass production system.
- Since 1999, Mobis has produced three types of modules, including cockpit-modules (control systems of the driver’s seat), front-end modules (integrating system of HVAC, lighting and bumper system), and chassis modules.
- The company is currently operating a total of 28 module production sites: 8 in Korea, and 20 overseas.
- Hyundai Mobis has been supplying its module systems to Chrysler since 2006 and has been recognised for its module technology and quality excellence in the North American market.
- Following the new business from Mercedes-Benz, Hyundai Mobis expects to win orders from other global automakers.
- Hyundai Mobis currently supplies key parts such as electric equipment, lamps, and sound systems to GM, Stellantis, Volkswagen and Geely.
