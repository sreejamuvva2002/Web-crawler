# Hyundai Mobis

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** power_electronics  ·  **Published:** 2022-11-23

**Source:** [Automotive supplier to bring 1,500 additional jobs to Bryan County](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/automotive-supplier-bring-1-500-045900980.html)  ·  `page_060475`

## Facts

- Hyundai Mobis plans to invest $926 million in an Electric Vehicle Power Electric system plant in Bryan County.
- The plant will eventually employ at least 1,500 people.
- Hyundai Mobis is one of the world's largest automotive suppliers.
- Gov. Brian Kemp celebrated the groundbreaking of Hyundai's new electric vehicle and battery manufacturing facility in Bryan County.
- Kemp stated the project would unleash transformational job creation and investment in that region of Georgia.
- Hyundai Mobis is the second supplier to locate in that area in just two weeks.
- The EV Power Electric system plant will expand Hyundai Mobis' current footprint in Georgia.
- Hyundai Mobis has operated a manufacturing facility with almost 1,200 employees in West Point since 2009.
- The West Point plant is the largest Tier 1 supplier to Kia Georgia.
- The West Point plant supplies automotive manufacturers with complete cockpit modules, chassis modules, and bumper assemblies.
- H.S. Oh is vice president of the Hyundai Mobis Electric Powertrain Business Unit.
- Oh stated the investment reflects an acceleration in the development of the EV supply chain in Georgia's auto industry.
- Oh stated they will be a major production player in the EV market, triggering more growth within the sector.
- Mobis is looking forward to providing high-quality work opportunities to the growing local workforce.
- Once at full production, the 1,200,000-square-foot facility will supply more than 900,000 EV Power Electric systems annually.
- The facility will also supply 450,000 Integrated Charging Control Units annually.
- These products will go to the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America factory in Bryan County.
- These products will also go to the Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Alabama factory in Montgomery.
- These products will also go to the Kia Georgia plant.
- Construction on the new facility is expected to begin as early as January 2023.
- Production is expected to begin in 2024.
- The facility is located at the Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development-certified Belfast Commerce Park in Richmond Hill.
- Jon Seagraves is Chairman of the Development Authority of Bryan County.
- Seagraves welcomed Mobis to the community.
- Belfast Commerce Park has close proximity to available housing in nearby Heartwood.
- The area has a top-performing public school system, interstate access, and rail.
- The Development Authority purchased the site about two years ago.
- Seagraves noted nearly $1 billion of investment and creation of over 1,500 good-paying jobs.
- Seagraves expressed gratitude to Bryan County, the city of Richmond Hill, and Raydient Places for their support and partnership.
- Alyce Thornhill is Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy.
- Thornhill represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development's Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The project was in partnership with the Development Authority of Bryan County, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia Power.
- Pat Wilson is GDEcD Commissioner.
- Wilson stated suppliers like Hyundai Mobis are transitioning to the jobs of the future and becoming part of Georgia's growing EV ecosystem.
- In May, they predicted that non-affiliated HMGMA suppliers in connection with the project would invest an additional $1 billion into Georgia.
- They have already blown past that prediction in about two weeks.
- Wilson stated they are excited to prepare Georgia's workforce for the jobs being created now with support from partners like Georgia Quick Start and sister agencies.
