# Duckyang Industrial Co.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2021-05-20

**Source:** [Korean firm to invest $10M, hire 285, to make battery parts | AP News](https://apnews.com/article/technology-government-and-politics-business-fb17de22764deba996497000d472897d)  ·  `page_007892`

## Facts

- Duckyang Industrial Co. will invest $10 million in a new facility in Braselton, Georgia.
- The company plans to hire 285 people for the Braselton plant.
- The facility is located northeast of Atlanta in an industrial park near an Amazon warehouse.
- The Braselton plant will manufacture battery modules and energy storage systems for SK Innovation's nearby Commerce, Georgia plant.
- SK Innovation's Commerce plant has a total investment of $2.6 billion and will employ 2,600 workers.
- Duckyang did not disclose a production start date for the Braselton facility.
- The new building will occupy 230,000 square feet (21,000 square meters).
- Duckyang currently supplies cockpit modules to automakers including Kia and Hyundai.
- The supplied cockpit modules are dashboard assemblies containing more than 130 parts, including steering wheels, instruments, and electronics.
- Kia operates a manufacturing plant in West Point, Georgia.
- Hyundai operates a manufacturing plant in Montgomery, Alabama.
- Duckyang CEO Dong-in Son stated the company is entering the U.S. electric vehicle battery market through SKBA to cooperate with other automotive companies like Hyundai and Kia Motors that have U.S. manufacturing bases.
- The company hopes to supply interior parts and electrical vehicle parts from the Braselton facility.
- Duckyang may claim an income tax credit allowing it to annually deduct $1,250 per job from state income taxes, capped at 50% of its tax liability.
- The tax breaks could save the company up to $1.8 million over five years.
- To qualify for the tax savings, workers must earn at least $28,000 per year.
- The state of Georgia will provide job training for the facility's workers but will not provide any discretionary incentives.
- The city of Braselton and Jackson County may provide property tax breaks to Duckyang.
- Georgia Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that flagship companies like SK Innovation attract supplier follow-on investment.
- Georgia is focused on growing the entire electric mobility supply chain, according to Commissioner Wilson.
- SK Innovation announced a joint venture with Ford Motor Co. to manufacture batteries and related equipment starting in 2025.
- The joint venture is named BlueOval SK.
- BlueOval SK's production volume will require approximately two jointly owned plants.
- Ford Motor Co. anticipates needing battery capacity equivalent to 10 factories worldwide by 2030.
- SK Innovation will spend approximately $5.3 billion to build the BlueOval SK plants, separate from its $2.6 billion Commerce plant investment.
- The U.S. International Trade Commission ruled in February that SK Innovation stole 22 trade secrets from LG Energy and initially barred it from importing, making, or selling batteries in the U.S. for 10 years.
- The dispute was settled in April with SK Innovation agreeing to pay $1.8 billion and an undisclosed royalty, allowing production at the Commerce plant to proceed.
