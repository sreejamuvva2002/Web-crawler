# Magna International Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2025-08-01

**Source:** [Magna International raises outlook on back of cost-cutting measures - The Globe and Mail](https://www.theglobeandmail.com/business/article-magna-international-earnings-tariffs-autos/)  ·  `page_057498`

## Facts

- Magna International Inc. continues to work closely with its customers to mitigate the impact of tariffs.
- CEO Swamy Kotagiri told analysts during the second-quarter earnings call that the company has settled almost all of its 2025 net tariff exposure with major automakers.
- The company will continue to pass on tariff costs to its customers.
- Magna lowered its estimated annualized tariff-related cost projections for the year to $200 million, from a previous estimate of $250 million.
- The company is continuing to increase its compliance with the Canada-U.S.-Mexico Agreement.
- General Motors Inc. reported a 35 percent profit decline in its second quarter after taking a US$1.1 billion hit from tariffs.
- General Motors expects its net tariff costs to be higher in the next quarter.
- Magna's net income for the three months ending June 30 was US$379 million, or $1.35 per diluted share, up from US$313 million or $1.09 per share in the same quarter last year.
- Adjusted diluted earnings worked out to $1.44 per share, up from $1.35 per share last year, beating the mean analyst estimate of $1.14 per share.
- Magna raised its annual outlook despite lower vehicle production, crediting cost-saving efforts over the past months.
- The revised outlook shows a dip in overall North American vehicle production but increased output in China.
- Magna boosted its sales outlook by US$400 million to upwards of US$42 billion.
- Magna reported quarterly sales totalled US$10.63 billion, down from US$10.96 billion in the same quarter last year.
- Light vehicle production was down six percent in North America and two percent in Europe.
- The company ended production of some programs.
