# Qcells

## Facts

- Solar panel manufacturer Qcells is expected to announce an expansion in Dalton plus a massive new plant northwest of Atlanta.
- The expansion could be the largest clean energy manufacturing investment in U.S. history.
- Qcells plans to build a new solar factory in Bartow County.
- Qcells plans to develop a second facility near its existing Dalton plant.
- The two projects combined are expected to bring roughly 2,600 jobs to the state.
- Gov. Brian Kemp is expected to announce the project during the Georgia Chamber of Commerce’s annual “Eggs and Issues” breakfast on Wednesday.
- Some details of the company’s plans are fluid and could change ahead of Wednesday’s planned unveiling.
- Qcells is owned by South Korea-based Hanwha Solutions.
- Qcells' first Georgia plant opened in 2019 near Dalton and employs roughly 750 workers.
- Last year, the company began building a new, $171 million facility nearby capable of producing 1.4 gigawatts of Qcells’ solar panels each year.
- When that expansion opens, it’s expected to create 470 jobs.
- With the expected Wednesday announcement, the company appears set to more than double its Georgia-based manufacturing workforce.
- The announcement is part of a flurry of green energy projects heading to Georgia.
- In the past 13 months, EV upstart Rivian (Social Circle) and Hyundai Motor Group (Bryan County) announced plans for sprawling factories to build plug-in vehicles in Georgia.
- Rivian and Hyundai have pledged collectively to employ more than 15,000 workers.
- In November, Freyr Battery announced plans for a $2.6 billion battery plant in Coweta County.
- Freyr will build massive utility-scale storage systems that use batteries to hold electricity from renewable sources like solar and wind.
- Last month, Hyundai and SK announced a joint venture that plans a $4 billion to $5 billion battery assembly plant in Bartow County.
- The Hyundai-SK joint venture will employ 3,500 workers.
- The wave of clean energy investments began in late 2021 with Rivian.
- President Biden’s Inflation Reduction Act (IRA), passed last August, has supercharged clean energy investments in the state.
- Georgia’s Democratic U.S. senators Jon Ossoff and Raphael Warnock were critical votes to pass the IRA.
- Ossoff sponsored significant new solar provisions within the IRA.
- The IRA provides $10 billion in tax credits for building new solar manufacturing facilities.
- The IRA provides $30 billion for U.S. manufacturers to make components for solar panels, batteries, wind turbines and to process minerals needed for renewable energy technologies and electric vehicles.
- The law extended a 30% income tax credit for rooftop solar for 10 years.
- The law offers a credit for battery systems that store excess energy when the sun isn’t shining.
- Many electric utilities are turning to solar as they shutter uneconomical coal-fired power plants and attempt to reduce greenhouse gas emissions.
- Georgia Power plans to increase its solar capacity by around 200% by 2035.
- The IRA legislation offers investment and production tax credits for solar to companies like Georgia Power.
- Companies have the potential to unlock even more savings if they use U.S.-made components.
- Supply chain issues and trade disputes with China have slowed solar deployment in the U.S.
- Most raw materials and other components needed to build solar panels are still manufactured abroad, particularly in China.
- China holds more than an 80% share in all of the various materials needed to produce panels.
- Democrats promised the IRA will help end the country’s reliance on China and bring jobs to the U.S.
- A Qcells expansion would further cement economic ties between Georgia and South Korea.
- Georgia opened its Seoul trade office in 1985.
- Multiple Georgia governors have visited Korea, and in 2019, Kemp made South Korea his first overseas visit as governor.
- In 2006, Kia Motors announced it would build its first U.S. factory in West Point near the Alabama border.
- The Kia project mushroomed into thousands of jobs across not only the Kia plant but the campuses of dozens of suppliers.
- The Georgia-Korea partnership kicked into overdrive when SK announced its first Peach State battery factory in Jackson County in 2018.
- The announcement of the $5.54 billion Hyundai EV plant near the coast followed last May.
- Today, Korea is the 10th largest export market for Georgia.
- Georgia’s imports from Korea totaled $8.83 billion in 2021.
