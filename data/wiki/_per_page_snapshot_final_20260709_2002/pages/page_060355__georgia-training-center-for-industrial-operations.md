# Georgia Training Center for Industrial Operations

**Type:** workforce_training  ·  **Role:** workforce_training  ·  **Published:** 2025-12-17

**Source:** [Beam signing marks milestone for OTC robotics training center - Statesboro Herald](https://www.statesboroherald.com/local/beam-signing-marks-milestone-for-otc-robotics-training-center/)  ·  `page_060355`

## Facts

- In November 2024, Ogeechee Technical College President Lori Durden said the Georgia Training Center for Industrial Operations would not be just another addition to the college’s campus when it opens in the fall of 2026.
- The facility's groundbreaking ceremony took place at the corner of A.J. Riggs Road and Highway 301 South.
- Ogeechee Technical College President Lori Durden described the center as a gateway to advancement, a catalyst for community growth, a crucial role in preparing a skilled workforce for advanced industries, and a hub for career development across the region and state.
- Last week, Ogeechee Technical College President Lori Durden was joined by Technical College System of Georgia Commissioner Greg Dozier, OTC faculty and staff, the college’s Foundation Board and members of the community in signing one of the last beams that will be placed in the Center.
- OTC Vice President for Economic Development Jan Moore stated that the beam signing marks a key milestone in the completion of the new Georgia Training Center for Industrial Operations.
- The facility is unique within the Technical College System of Georgia and will deliver state-of-the-art training in industrial systems and advanced automation, including robotics.
- A painted white beam signed by attendees will be installed in the ceiling of the Training Center but remain visible from inside the building.
- The 37,000-square-foot Training Center will provide training to employees of manufacturers within the region, as well as companies that use automation in their supply and warehousing facilities.
- Once operational, the college’s noncredit training capacity will increase to 460,000 hours annually, tripling the college’s current output.
- Most of the demand now and in the future for robotics training stems from when Hyundai broke ground on the 2,541-acre Bryan County Metaplant in October 2022 to manufacture electric and hybrid vehicles.
- The Hyundai Bryan County Metaplant complex is projected to employ, eventually, 8,500 people on-site.
- In 2024, Lori Durden stated that the vision for the Training Center was inspired by the sheer amount of demand for training in industrial systems and robotics to equip the region's workforce with necessary skills.
- More than a dozen manufacturing plants under construction or in operation in southeast Georgia are directly related to the Metaplant, including three in Bulloch County.
- Most of these related manufacturing plants use advanced forms of robotics in their manufacturing process.
- Ajin USA, a supplier of metal auto body parts to Hyundai, opened its $317 million plant in July 2024 in the Bruce Yawn Commerce Park next to I-16.
- Two other suppliers to the Metaplant opened in Bulloch County in 2025.
- Hanon Systems is a maker of "automotive thermal and energy management" equipment, which includes air-conditioning components.
- Ecoplastic America Corporation manufactures injection-molded plastic automotive body parts for Hyundai Motor Group vehicles.
