# Dr. Ing. h.c. F. Porsche AG

## Facts

- Dr. Ing. h.c. F. Porsche AG is a German automobile manufacturer specializing in luxury, high-performance sports cars, SUVs and sedans, headquartered in Stuttgart, Baden-Württemberg, Germany.
- The company is owned by Volkswagen AG, a controlling stake of which is owned by Porsche Automobil Holding SE.
- Porsche's current lineup includes the 911, Panamera, Macan, Cayenne, and Taycan.
- In May 2011, Porsche Cars North America announced plans to spend $80–$100 million to move its North American headquarters from Sandy Springs, a suburb of Atlanta, to Aerotropolis Atlanta on the site of the old Ford Hapeville plant adjacent to Hartsfield–Jackson Atlanta International Airport.
- The headquarters relocation will receive about $15 million in economic incentives.
- Designed by architectural firm HOK, the new North American headquarters facility will include a new office building and test track at One Porsche Drive.
- In October 2017, Porsche Cars North America announced the launch of Porsche Passport, a sports car and SUV subscription program initially available in Atlanta.
- During the COVID-19 pandemic in March 2020, Porsche suspended its manufacturing in Europe for two weeks.
- In August 2022, Bloomberg News reported that Porsche lined up interest in its initial public offering for a valuation between US$60–85 billion, expected to be listed on the Frankfurt Stock Exchange in September.
- The company has a long-standing relationship with Volkswagen, as Ferdinand Porsche designed the first Volkswagen Beetle.
- In 2009, Porsche entered an agreement with Volkswagen to merge car manufacturing operations by 2011 to form an 'Integrated Automotive Group'.
- As of the end of 2015, Porsche SE holds a 52.2% control interest in Volkswagen AG.
- In February 2022, Volkswagen AG announced it would examine the feasibility of a possible IPO of Porsche AG, with share capital divided into 50% non-voting preference shares and 50% ordinary shares.
- Volkswagen AG will retain 75% of ordinary shares while Porsche SE acquires 25%, and Qatar Investment Authority committed to buy 4.99% of preference shares.
- As part of the preliminary offering, 113,875 thousand shares were sold at 82.5 euros, estimating the company's value at 75 billion euros.
- In April 2022, Porsche Australia announced plans to open an e-fuel manufacturing facility in Tasmania named the HIF Tasmania Carbon Neutral eFuel Plant, slated to open in 2026.
- Porsche’s operating profit in 2025 collapsed by roughly 98%, falling from approximately €5.3 billion in 2024 to €90 million.
- The company reversed a high-value investment in electric vehicle research and production, resulting in a heavy write-down of €3.9 billion restructuring charge due to BEV sales stagnation.
- United States tariffs increased to 15% and briefly 25% on importing 'finished vehicles', impacting Porsche's operations.
- Global sales dropped 10% from 310,718 in 2024 to 279,449 in 2025.
- New CEO Michael Leiters is focusing on a leaner, more efficient model lineup while reducing reliance on volume, maintaining a full year 2026 forecast of a 5% to 7% profit margin range.
- Porsche's headquarters and main factory are located in Zuffenhausen, Stuttgart, where it produces flat-6 and V8 piston engines.
- Cayenne and Panamera models are manufactured in Leipzig, Germany, with SUV parts also assembled at the Volkswagen Touareg factory in Bratislava, Slovakia.
- Since 2011, the Zuffenhausen plant area has more than doubled from 284,000m2 to 614,000m2 following purchases of former Layher, Deltona and Daimler sites.
- In May 2025, Porsche North America confirmed that production for all current 718 Boxster and 718 Cayman variants is scheduled to end in October 2025.
- Porsche CEO Oliver Blume confirmed future production of full-electric replacements for the 718 lineup will arrive in the 'medium term.'
