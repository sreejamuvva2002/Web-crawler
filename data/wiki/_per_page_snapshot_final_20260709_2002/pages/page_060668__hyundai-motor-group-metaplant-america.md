# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-07

**Source:** [EV production to begin Q4 2024 - Statesboro Herald](https://www.statesboroherald.com/local/ev-production-begin-q4-2024/)  ·  `page_060668`

## Facts

- The American arm of South Korean automaker Hyundai Motor Co. plans to commence production operations at the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America by the fourth quarter of 2024.
- The official opening of the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America facility is targeted for the first quarter of 2025.
- The Metaplant complex is currently under construction on a 3,000-acre site in Black Creek just south of I-16 East near Blitchton.
- The Metaplant is projected to eventually employ 8,500 people on-site.
- Parts suppliers building factories in the region, including some in Bulloch County, expect to employ almost 6,000 more workers.
- Hyundai Motor Group projects that the Metaplant and off-site suppliers together will create a total of 14,476 jobs at full production.
- HMG Metaplant America CEO and President Oscar Kwon stated during an October 2023 press tour that they were on track to start production in early 2025.
- Hyundai Motor North America president and CEO Josè Muñoz attended the plant's groundbreaking ceremony in 2022 alongside Hyundai executives from South Korea and Gov. Brian Kemp.
- On Monday, Muñoz joined Kemp and other elected state leaders in Atlanta for “Hyundai Day” at the state capitol.
- Gov. Brian Kemp stated that Hyundai Motor Group has been an incredible partner to the state and highlighted the historic benefits of the project for Georgians.
- Up to 300,000 Hyundai, Genesis, and Kia EVs are expected to be produced annually at the Metaplant.
- 17 HMGMA suppliers have announced projects across 10 Georgia counties spanning beyond southeast Georgia, with further announcements expected.
- Supplier facilities are expected to create more than 6,700 new jobs and involve more than $2.5 billion in investment.
- Supplier facilities will be operational prior to the opening of the Metaplant.
- Josè Muñoz stated that Hyundai’s investments in Georgia will create tens of thousands of great paying American jobs and bring tens of billions of dollars in economic impact for decades to come.
- Four building exteriors at the Metaplant site are complete, and outfitting for internal operations is underway.
- The Metaplant’s architecture incorporates technologically advanced elements.
- A 41-acre park will be featured in front of the plant, including trails, running tracks, environmental art displays, sports fields, and other amenities for employee use.
- Full-time Metaplant employees are being hired at a rapid pace for training and skills on machinery, office and clerical work, and numerous other plant jobs.
- The Metaplant currently has 566 full-time employees.
- Approximately 75 percent of the current Metaplant workforce is from the greater Savannah area.
- An additional 37 Metaplant employees are from Georgia but outside the local area.
- Georgia Quick Start broke ground on its customized training center at the Metaplant site on Feb. 8.
- The Metaplant has partnered with Ogeechee Technical College and other area technical colleges to prepare students for entry-level employment.
- Electrical Vehicle Professional Technical Certificate of Credit training is available at Coastal Pines Technical College’s Baxley and Waycross campuses, Southeastern Technical College’s Vidalia campus, and Savannah Technical College.
