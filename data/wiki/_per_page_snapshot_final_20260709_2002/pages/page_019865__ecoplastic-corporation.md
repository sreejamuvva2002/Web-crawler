# Ecoplastic Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Georgia: Auto parts manufacturer to create nearly 500 jobs - WJCL](https://www.wjcl.com/article/ecoplastic-corporation-bulloch-county-jobs/42407885)  ·  `page_019865`

## Facts

- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp announced that automotive parts manufacturer Ecoplastic Corporation will create 456 jobs and invest about $200 million in a new manufacturing facility.
- Ecoplastic Corporation was founded in 1984.
- Ecoplastic Corporation is a Korea-based company.
- The company manufactures plastic automobile parts for original equipment manufacturers and other suppliers.
- The company is a Tier-1 supplier for Hyundai Motor Group and Kia.
- The company specializes in the manufacturing of automotive interior and exterior plastic parts, including bumpers, consoles, trims and plastic molds.
- Gov. Brian Kemp stated that Ecoplastic will be able to serve the Hyundai Metaplant well from Statesboro due to its close proximity to Georgia's ports system.
- The company's new facility will be located at 4822 Highway 301 South Register near Statesboro.
- The plant will be capable of injection molding and painting.
- The plant will produce exterior parts, bumpers and garnishes, interior parts, consoles and trims for electric vehicles.
- Benjy Thompson, CEO of the Development Authority of Bulloch County, noted that Ecoplastic is another large Hyundai supplier located in the region to serve the Hyundai meta plant in Bryan County.
- The Development Authority of Bulloch County is currently finishing negotiations surrounding the Ecoplastic Corporation project.
- This project will be the second Hyundai supplier located in Bulloch County.
- Combined with the other supplier, Bulloch County will have a little over 1,000 jobs tied up in Hyundai suppliers.
- The region will see hundreds of millions of dollars of capital investment from these suppliers.
- Randy Parker, CEO of Hyundai Motor America, stated at a Savannah Economic Development Authority meeting that the Hyundai facility is a $5.5 billion investment in a new EV and battery manufacturer that will create more than 8,100 local jobs.
- Ecoplastic Corporation expects to begin operations by October 2024.
- The company will be hiring for HR, finance, parts development, quality control, facility maintenance, injection and paint production positions.
