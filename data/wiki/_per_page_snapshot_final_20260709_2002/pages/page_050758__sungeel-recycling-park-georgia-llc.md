## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that SungEel Recycling Park Georgia, LLC., will locate its first U.S. recycling facility in Georgia.
- SungEel Recycling Park Georgia is a subsidiary of the Korea-based global industry leader SungEel HiTech Co., Ltd.
- The company will invest more than $37 million in the new facility.
- The project will create 104 jobs in Stephens County.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Georgia recently announced record-breaking numbers for fiscal year 2022.
- Governor Kemp noted companies like SungEel are evidence that the rapidly growing electric mobility ecosystem continues to generate new jobs for hardworking Georgians across the state.
- Governor Kemp highlighted Korea has been a key partner in Georgia’s growing sustainable technology industry.
- Governor Kemp welcomed SungEel to the Peach State for their first U.S. facility.
- SungEel has more than 10 years of patented technology specializing in complete recycling technology using a 100 percent full circulatory system.
- The company's system recycles end-of-life batteries and battery manufacturing scrap with a metal recovery rate of more than 95 percent.
- Recovered metals from the process include nickel, cobalt, and lithium.
- Suk Jae Yim, Representative of SungEel Recycling Park Georgia, stated that SungEel HiTech's entry into Georgia is the last piece of the puzzle to build a sustainable ecosystem of Georgia’s electric vehicle supply chain.
- SungEel Recycling Park Georgia will conduct its full responsibility to build a U.S. eco-friendly industrial ecosystem in line with the expectations of the State of Georgia and Stephens County.
- The new facility will be located at the Hayestone Brady Business Park, a Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) Certified site, in Toccoa.
- The GRAD Program is a proactive effort by Georgia’s economic development community to develop a portfolio of available sites ready for fast-track development.
- Operations are expected to begin in early 2024.
- Over the next few years, the company will be hiring for technicians, operators, administrative, and sales roles.
- Brian Akin, Chairman of the Stephens County Development Authority, stated it has been a pleasure working with SungEel HiTech over the past eight months.
- Akin noted this is an exciting time for Toccoa-Stephens County and a solid boost for the local job market.
- Akin highlighted that investments in infrastructure, workforce development, and education were all significant factors in the selection process.
- Director of Korean Investment Yoonie Kim represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) on this competitive project.
- The GDEcD partnered with the Stephens County Development Authority and Georgia EMC on this project.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated SungEel is a company at the cutting edge of sustainable technology.
- Wilson believes SungEel’s proximity to other members of Georgia’s electric mobility ecosystem will lead to amazing success.
- Wilson noted creating the jobs of the future and protecting the opportunities of today means preparing key industries, such as the auto industry, for the next technological revolution.
- Wilson expressed gratitude to partners in Stephens County for investment in speed to market solutions like site preparation.
- Georgia’s prime location, extensive infrastructure, skilled workforce, and business-friendly climate have made it an attractive location for a diverse array of rapidly developing industries focused on creating a sustainable future.
- Georgia is cultivating a vertically integrated supply chain that will help companies increase efficiencies by reducing the reliance on imported materials.
- EV-related projects have contributed more than $13 billion in investments to Georgia since 2020.
- EV-related projects have contributed more than 18,100 new jobs to Georgia since 2020.
