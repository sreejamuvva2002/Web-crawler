# Solvay Specialty Polymers

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2022-11-04

**Source:** [Georgia Lands EV Plant After Awarding Company $178M Grant](https://www.govtech.com/transportation/georgia-lands-ev-plant-after-awarding-company-178m-grant)  ·  `page_029394`

## Facts

- Solvay Specialty Polymers announced it will build a plant next to its existing Augusta factory to manufacture EV-grade binders and separator coatings.
- The parts produced at the new factory are critical components within lithium-ion batteries that power EVs.
- Sen. Raphael Warnock credited a $178 million federal grant awarded to Solvay for the new factory.
- Sen. Raphael Warnock is a Democrat in the middle of a re-election campaign against Republican Herschel Walker.
- Warnock stated he was proud to fight for and help secure the $178 million needed for this new facility.
- Warnock stated he will continue to work closely with businesses looking to start or expand in Georgia to secure critical investments that will help create local, good-paying jobs.
- Solvay received the grant last month through the Battery Materials Processing and Battery Component Manufacturing and Recycling program.
- The grant program is funded through the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law enacted in 2021.
- The project will create 500 construction jobs.
- The project will create 100 manufacturing jobs.
- To qualify for the grant, Solvay must match the awarded funds.
- To qualify for the grant, Solvay must produce a $356 million facility.
- The U.S. Department of Energy awarded the grant.
- The U.S. Department of Energy expects the project to create an additional 500 indirect jobs.
- Solvay has had a presence in Augusta since 2000.
- In 2000, Solvay purchased a manufacturing facility from BP Amoco.
- The company produces specialty polymers and plastic resins which are used in various products, from airplanes to medical devices.
- By bringing EV-related manufacturing to Georgia, the company joins the state’s fast-growing electric vehicle production industry.
- Hyundai is building a $5.5 billion EV factory near Savannah as one of Georgia's two largest and most high-profile economic development projects.
- Rivian is building a $5 billion EV factory east of Atlanta as one of Georgia's two largest and most high-profile economic development projects.
- The Hyundai and Rivian factories aim to make Georgia a leader in producing plug-in vehicles.
- Augusta has emerged as a desirable spot for EV parts companies.
- German copper smelting company Aurubis announced a $340 million factory last year.
- Japanese copper foil producer Denkai announced a $430 million factory last year.
- The Aurubis and Denkai factories will produce metal materials for EV batteries.
- Mike Finelli is president of Solvay Growth Platforms and Chief North American Officer.
- Finelli stated that large federal grants will help the U.S. bolster its domestic EV parts supply chain.
- Finelli stated they are proud to receive the federal government’s support for manufacturing technologies that will provide the U.S. with critical raw materials for building an independent, sustainable EV value chain.
