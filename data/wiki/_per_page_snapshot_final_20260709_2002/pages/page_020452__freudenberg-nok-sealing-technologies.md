# Freudenberg-NOK Sealing Technologies

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2015-05-05

**Source:** [Freudenberg-NOK Plans To Expand Facility In Findlay, Ohio](https://www.import-car.com/freudenberg-nok-plans-to-expand-facility-in-findlay-ohio/)  ·  `page_020452`

## Facts

- Freudenberg-NOK Sealing Technologies announced plans to expand its automotive sealing facility and hire new employees to meet increasing customer requirements for quality components.
- Freudenberg-NOK’s Findlay, Ohio, plant produces approximately 700 high-performance plastic parts for customers including vehicle manufacturers and Tier One and Two suppliers using state-of-the art manufacturing processes.
- Company executives officially announced plans to pursue a 43,000-foot factory expansion that could result in retaining 148 jobs and creating 25 new jobs at the plant.
- Final plans are contingent upon formal approval of all State of Ohio workforce and incentive programs.
- Freudenberg-NOK leaders have worked with JobsOhio, state and local officials on incentives to support the expansion.
- The company expects to invest approximately $8.6 million in construction and equipment upgrades.
- The expansion and construction will be complete by winter 2015.
- Dr. Theodore Duclos, president of Freudenberg-NOK, stated that the Findlay facility is a benchmark for lean manufacturing and production of quality components, and escalating customer demand offers tremendous new opportunities for business expansion in Ohio.
- Dr. Duclos expressed happiness about investing in the success of the facility and the greater Findlay community, anticipating the expansion will help better serve existing and new customers with quality sealing products.
- Freudenberg-NOK and its Findlay operations are considered a top employer in Hancock County.
- The plant was a finalist in the IndustryWeek 2014 Best Plants competition last year.
- The IndustryWeek competition annually recognizes the best manufacturing plants in North America.
- The Findlay plant maintains nearly perfect statistics in quality, delivery, and customer retention.
- Findlay has become the company’s lead facility in the successful adoption of a new breakthrough manufacturing approach called Single Cavity Net Shape Molding.
- The single cavity approach uses compact, custom-engineered one cavity injection and compression molding machines rather than multi-cavity machines to produce crucial thermoplastic parts.
- This process improves part quality and reduces scrap and waste.
- Roy Schroeder, Lead Center manager for Fluid Power Automotive in Findlay, said the project is a testament to the skills of their people and the success of their processes.
- Schroeder noted the plant expansion will allow them to continue pursuing world-class manufacturing processes and products, expand markets, and look forward to completion and adding new employees.
