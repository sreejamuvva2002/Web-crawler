# Hyundai TRANSYS

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hyundai TRANSYS to bring 678 jobs to Georgia – Albany Herald](https://albanyherald.com/jobs/hyundai-transys-to-bring-678-jobs-to-georgia/)  ·  `page_009512`

## Facts

- Gov. Brian Kemp announced that Hyundai TRANSYS will invest more than $240 million in expanding its operations in Georgia.
- The investment will create 678 full-time long-term positions at a new manufacturing facility in the West Point area.
- Hyundai TRANSYS is the only automobile manufacturing company in the world that specializes in transmissions.
- The brand-new plant will be 620,000 square feet and located near the company’s current location on the Kia campus.
- The facility will be responsible for producing eight-speed transmissions for a global automotive leader.
- Hyundai TRANSYS Georgia Powertrain President Sangkil Jung cited years of capital investment, employment, training support, and unmatched opportunities as reasons Georgia was a natural choice.
- Hyundai TRANSYS will bring hundreds of advanced manufacturing jobs along with management and office staff positions to the local community.
- The company plans to create 165 supplementary construction handling and inspection jobs.
- The expansion will bring Hyundai TRANSYS’ total employment in Georgia to 1,741 jobs.
- West Point Mayor Steve Tramell thanked the West Point Development Authority, the state of Georgia, and Troup County for making the project a reality.
- Hyundai TRANSYS has been a community partner with West Point and Troup County for over a decade.
- Troup County Commission Chairman Patrick Crews stated the significant capital investment will strengthen the local economy and create economic opportunity for residents.
- Senior Project Manager Amanda Fields represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s Global Commerce division on this competitive project.
- The project was completed in partnership with the West Point Development Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson thanked Gov. Kemp and the General Assembly for their continued support of recruiting business and opening new markets for Georgia companies.
- In 2020 alone, the State of Georgia’s international relationships facilitated more than $1 billion in investment and more than 2,000 jobs from South Korean companies.
- Georgia is home to more than 200 automotive-related facilities and remains a national leader in advanced manufacturing.
- Georgia outpaced the United States in 10-year GDP growth in the manufacture of products including machinery, electrical equipment and components, and fabricated metals.
