# Section 247 Maintaining and Enhancing Hydroelectricity Incentive Program

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-09-05

**Source:** [Section 247: Maintaining and Enhancing Hydroelectricity Incentives Selections | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/oe/section-247-maintaining-and-enhancing-hydroelectricity-incentives-selections)  ·  `page_004040`

## Facts

- On September 5, 2024, the Grid Deployment Office announced the selection of 293 capital improvement projects for negotiations across 33 states to receive over $430 million in Maintaining and Enhancing Hydroelectricity Incentive payments.
- DOE resumed negotiations with the selectees on April 27, 2026.
- Per statutory requirements, DOE will fund all qualified capital improvement projects at limits set forth in Section 247, covering 30% of total project costs not to exceed $5,000,000 within a qualified hydroelectric facility.
- The total incentive per facility cannot exceed $5,000,000 even if multiple project applications are submitted.
- A total of 47 projects are located throughout Alabama, Arkansas, Georgia, Kentucky, Louisiana, North Carolina, South Carolina, Tennessee, Virginia, and West Virginia.
- The Southeast Region requested a total of $83 million in incentive payments for its 47 projects.
- Capital improvement categories for the Southeast Region projects include Grid Resiliency (18 projects), Dam Safety (21 projects), and Environmental Improvements (8 projects).
- Projects in the Southeast Region will provide economic benefits by committing to hiring local workers or contractors.
- Projects in the Southeast Region will provide economic benefits by providing apprenticeship, pre-apprenticeship, and youth education programs to showcase opportunities available in the clean energy workforce.
- Nearly 70 contracts are already in place with, or commitments have been made to a contractor preference for, businesses majority owned or controlled by underrepresented persons or groups of underrepresented persons in the Southeast Region.
- The Buzzards Roost Hydroelectric Facility in Greenwood County, Chappells, South Carolina requested $5 million to redesign and construct an emergency spillway channel and structure to prevent spilling of water over the top of the dam and maintain continued water supply to two counties during major flooding.
- The Buzzards Roost Hydroelectric Facility is located at the outfall of Lake Greenwood and began operations in 1939.
- Duke Energy Carolinas, LLC requested $5 million for the Dearborn Dam project in Great Falls, South Carolina to conduct a major habitat and recreational water flow project on the Long Bypass Reach and Short Bypass Reach along the Great Falls Reservoir.
- The Dearborn Dam project resulted in the creation of whitewater river channels, enhancement of natural habitats, construction of a large access area, and restoration of a historic building.
- The Dearborn Dam facility is located on the Catawba River and began operations in 1923.
