# Hanon Systems

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** thermal_management  ·  **Published:** 2023-05-23

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp: Automotive Supplier Hanon Systems to Create 160 New Jobs in Bulloch County | Governor Brian P. Kemp Office of the Governor](https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases/2023-05-23/gov-kemp-automotive-supplier-hanon-systems-create-160-new-jobs-bulloch)  ·  `page_000089`

## Facts

- Hanon Systems will create 160 new jobs and invest more than $40 million in a new manufacturing facility in Bulloch County.
- The new facility will be located at Gateway Regional Industrial Park in Bulloch County.
- The facility will assemble automotive thermal solutions.
- Operations at the new facility are expected to begin in May 2024.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated that Georgia’s automotive and e-mobility industries continue to grow as they welcome Hanon Systems to Georgia.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp noted that as the state furthers its position as the EV capital of America, new jobs and opportunities are coming to hardworking Georgians all over the state.
- Min Sung, President and Chief Executive Officer of Hanon Systems, expressed pleasure in contributing to the local economy of Bulloch County through job creation and thanked the State of Georgia and its agencies for guiding the project.
- Min Sung stated that the announcement aligns with Hanon Systems' strategy to support the industry shift toward electrification while growing its North America footprint.
- The project was supported by the Development Authority of Bulloch County, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy Alyce Thornhill represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- Billy Allen, Chair of the Development Authority of Bulloch County, stated that Statesboro-Bulloch County is honored by Hanon Systems’ decision to locate its new facility there.
- Billy Allen noted excitement over welcoming another automotive supplier to the community, particularly one that will work closely with the Hyundai Metaplant and the electric vehicle industry.
- Billy Allen described the decision as an example of the resources offered by the community and region to help companies succeed.
- Georgia Ports Authority (GPA) Executive Director Griff Lynch stated that the announcement further solidifies Georgia’s position as a growing hub for auto manufacturing.
- Griff Lynch said GPA is proud to support the operations of Hyundai Motor Group and its suppliers as they deliver jobs and opportunity for Georgia residents.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that suppliers like Hanon Systems can successfully support larger industry ecosystems in Georgia thanks to partnership-driven solutions ranging from workforce training to logistics systems efficiencies.
- Pat Wilson noted that Bulloch County, as part of the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority, has been part of a growing success story from the beginning.
- Since 2020, over 40 EV-related projects have announced more than $22.7 billion in investment and 28,400 jobs in Georgia.
- On October 25, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America officially broke ground in neighboring Bryan County on its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility.
- Hanon Systems was formed in 1986 and has grown into a leading supplier of automotive thermal and energy management solutions for electrified and conventional vehicles.
- Hanon Systems' offerings include solutions in heating ventilation and air conditioning, powertrain cooling, compressor, fluid transport, and electronics and fluid pressure.
