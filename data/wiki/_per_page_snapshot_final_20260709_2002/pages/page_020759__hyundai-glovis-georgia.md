# Hyundai Glovis Georgia

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Gov. Nathan Deal: Hyundai Glovis Georgia To Create 190 Jobs In Harris County | GaPundit](https://gapundit.com/gov-nathan-deal-hyundai-glovis-georgia-create-190-jobs-harris-county/)  ·  `page_020759`

## Facts

- Governor Nathan Deal announced that Hyundai Glovis Georgia, a leading logistics services company for the automotive sector, will relocate its parts consolidation center to West Point.
- The relocation project will create up to 190 jobs over four years.
- Governor Nathan Deal stated that Georgia’s booming automotive and logistics industries play critical roles in creating jobs and bringing investment to the state.
- Deal noted that companies like Hyundai Glovis need high-quality resources to serve their global customer base and remain internationally competitive.
- The company will work with a general contractor to develop a new, state-of-the-art 310,080-square-foot sub-assembly and sequencing facility in the Northwest Harris Business Park.
- The facility will be used to assemble and distribute components to meet the strict demands and timetables of its suppliers.
- Hyundai Glovis Georgia Chief Operating Officer Hong Kee Rhee expressed appreciation for the support received from West Point, Harris County, and the state of Georgia.
- Yoonie Kim, senior project manager on the international investment team at the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD), assisted Hyundai Glovis Georgia on behalf of the state.
- The City of West Point Development Authority and Georgia Electric Membership Corp also assisted with the project.
- Transwestern represented the company during the site selection process.
- Harris County Commission Chairman Harry Lange welcomed Hyundai Glovis Georgia as a new corporate citizen, stating its decision to locate in the Northwest Harris Business Park represents a commitment to the county.
- Officials anticipate a grand opening for the facility in the fall of 2015.
- GDEcD Commissioner Chris Carr highlighted that the automotive sector has grown significantly in Georgia over the past few years due to a competitive business climate, solid supplier network, direct access to leading original equipment manufacturers, and interconnected transportation infrastructure.
- Hyundai Glovis designs logistics strategies and processes tailored to customers’ environments using the latest information systems and advanced logistics technologies.
- The company provides total logistics services in domestic and international markets, shipping business operations, and logistics consulting.
