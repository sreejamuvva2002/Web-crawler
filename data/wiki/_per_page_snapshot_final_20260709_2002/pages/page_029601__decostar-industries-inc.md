# Decostar Industries, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2016-05-26

**Source:** [Decostar Settlement Information Sheet | US EPA](https://www.epa.gov/enforcement/decostar-settlement-information-sheet)  ·  `page_029601`

## Facts

- Decostar manufactures parts for automobile manufacturers including Nissan, BMW, Hyundai, and EZ Go.
- The company operates a facility in Carrollton, Georgia.
- Decostar has operated at the Carrollton location since 2003.
- On May 26, 2016, Region 4 EPA and the State of Georgia conducted a RCRA Compliance Evaluation Inspection at the facility as part of the Organic Air Emissions National Enforcement Initiative.
- The inspection identified numerous generator violations including RCRA Organic Air Emission violations under Subparts BB and CC.
- Violations included failure to conduct inspections, failure to maintain and operate the facility to prevent a release, failure to provide required training, and failure to establish emergency preparedness procedures.
- Specific Subpart CC violations included failure to properly determine air emission control levels, failure to design and install fixed roof or closure devices, failure to monitor hazardous waste for leaks, failure to make repairs within 5 days, and failure to maintain records on tank defects and repairs.
- Decostar reached a settlement agreement with the U.S. EPA to address violations of the Resource Conservation and Recovery Act.
- As part of the settlement, Decostar will implement a leak detection and repair program to control hazardous air emissions from the facility.
- The company will perform a supplemental environmental project valued at over $2.7 million.
- The supplemental environmental project includes replacing existing robots and paint applicators with devices that increase transfer efficiency.
- Decostar will pay a $377,900 civil penalty, with the remaining penalty amount mitigated by the equipment upgrade.
- Within sixty days after the Effective Date of the Order, Decostar must develop a “BB Equipment Program” document to catalog regulated equipment, monitor recordkeeping activities, and implement a tracking program.
- The environmental project provides for 4,130-pound reductions in annual hazardous waste generation.
- The project also provides for 1,385-pound reductions in annual hazardous air pollution emissions.
- Additionally, the project provides for 1,972-pound reductions in annual volatile organic emissions.
- Decostar proactively eliminated volatile organic emissions detected during the inspection.
- Andy Crossland serves as the Waste Enforcement Branch Chief for the EPA's Waste and Chemical Enforcement Division regarding this case.
