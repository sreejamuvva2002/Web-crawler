# Magna International

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2021-03-30

**Source:** [Electric vehicle spotlight (EVS): Magna International | Electrek](https://electrek.co/2021/03/30/electric-vehicle-spotlight-evs-magna-international/)  ·  `page_057443`

## Facts

- Magna International has over 60 years of experience manufacturing auto parts.
- The company’s recent focus is on electric mobility solutions and AI.
- Magna is a mobility manufacturer specializing in automotive components.
- Magna International is headquartered in Aurora, Ontario, north of Toronto.
- It is the largest automotive part supplier in North America.
- It is the third-largest automotive part supplier in the world.
- As a contract manufacturer for other OEMs, Magna has designed and produced countless components for nearly every major automaker on the planet.
- They manufacture complete vehicles for other OEMs that currently includes some electric vehicles in low volume.
- This includes New Entrant Fisker, for which Magna recently announced it will soon be producing an EV.
- The company considers itself “a 64-year-old startup.”
- Magna International applies its experience with big innovations to a startup mentality.
- It has remained on the forefront of new technologies, thanks to its research and development departments.
- In the electrification of vehicles especially, Magna is developing unique and scalable solutions that target issues current OEMs face in terms of efficiency and cost.
- Magna International’s Executive Vice President of R&D is Sherif Marakby.
- Sherif Marakby stated that Magna focuses on automotive and transportation trends to identify growth areas like electrification and driver assistance.
- Marakby noted that no one knows exactly what the volumes will be or how big the market is going to be by segment.
- Magna has taken a modular approach to its electric drivetrains to keep components adaptable to suit all customers.
- This modular approach helps keep costs down, no matter whether the volume is for 5,000 vehicles or 50,000.
- The modular approach allows Magna to work with OEM customers to provide the most suitable configuration of electric drive(s) that best fit the vehicle.
- Whether it’s payload, towing capacity, or range, Magna’s components can be added or subtracted purposefully.
- Magna recently announced its eBeam technology.
- eBeam is an electric axle drive system that can be dropped into an existing vehicle.
- Due to this design, eBeam can replace a traditional transmission and rear axle in a ladder frame chassis quite easily.
- The existing brakes, suspension, and chassis itself can stay as is when using eBeam.
- Ladder frame chassis is prevalent in most pickup trucks.
- Magna International developed the eBeam with the goal of electrifying one of the toughest vehicles, the pickup truck.
- Sherif Marakby explained that the pickup truck market is huge in the US but focused on attributes like towing and capabilities.
- He argued that a pickup truck is probably the last thing you want to electrify because of those capabilities and battery electrics can get very expensive for a heavier vehicle.
- By having a modular approach, having the capabilities that this drop-in replacement can provide, is important to the pickup truck customer.
- Boat-towing, horse-hauling pickup truck owners remain some of the toughest customers to sway toward electric.
- If automakers can deliver the same torque, payload, and towing capacity, the argument gets a little easier.
- The eBeam is highly modular and can work in a lightweight truck, or a heavy-duty commercial vehicle, large battery, small battery, or even a hybrid.
- Magna calls this its “building block approach,” allowing OEMs to configure multiple drivetrain pieces together depending on the purpose of the truck.
- Since Magna International is a parts provider to nearly all major automakers, the eBeam can one day be implemented on different-sized chassis with different size batteries.
- According to Marakby, the eBeam is still a few years away.
- So far the interest level from OEMs for the eBeam has been “tremendous.”
- He elaborated that it's a strong indication in the space that they’re a few years out, maybe less.
- He noted it's a promising technology in a tough segment and while consumer change might be slow, providing a component that lends itself to an electric drive may start low volume but he’s convinced it’s going to take off.
- As for electric mobility solutions, Magna recently announced a joint venture agreement with LG to expand its powertrain electrification.
- Next, the biggest focus will be getting the highest efficiency and power density out of its products at the lowest cost.
- In addition to electric powertrains, Magna is also quite fluent in autonomous driving and AI.
- The company is one of the few in the world to bundle radar, LiDAR, cameras, and domain controllers.
- It continues to steadily expand those capabilities.
- Magna-made ADAS products can now be found on more than 250 different vehicle models.
- AI is already part of their business in many ways, from employing it in manufacturing facilities for planned maintenance, to incorporating it in vehicle technologies.
- Their connected eDrive systems leverage AI to predict and improve driving dynamics and efficiency, enabling the vehicle to predict changes in terrain and traffic situations and better manage battery usage for extended range.
- They also use AI as they continue to grow their ADAS L1/2+ business.
- In developing driver assistance and AI technologies, Magna International is providing OEMs all components of a vehicle, inside and out.
- The company is using its vast knowledge of manufacturing and research to help other automakers electrify their future vehicles more quickly and easily.
