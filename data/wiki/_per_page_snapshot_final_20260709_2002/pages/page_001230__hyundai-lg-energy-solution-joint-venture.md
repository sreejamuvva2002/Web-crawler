# Hyundai-LG Energy Solution Joint Venture

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-05-30

**Source:** [Georgia To Lead Electric Vehicle Revolution, ESG Or Not](https://cleantechnica.com/2023/05/30/georgia-to-lead-electric-vehicle-revolution-esg-or-not/)  ·  `page_001230`

## Facts

- Georgia is among US states in which public officials have been on a rant against investing that follows environmental, social, and governance principles, or ESG for short.
- Georgia has become a key state in the Biden administration’s efforts to step up the pace of decarbonization.
- A new $4.3 billion joint venture between Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution is the latest clean tech investment in Georgia.
- The EV battery joint venture will be located next to Hyundai’s forthcoming Metaplant America, which is already under construction in Savannah.
- Under the agreement, the 50-50 venture will produce batteries for electric vehicles at a rate of 300,000 EVs per year.
- This production rate comes close to half the total number of EVs sold in the US last year, which topped 800,000 for the first time.
- Construction for the joint venture is scheduled to start in the second half of 2023.
- The joint venture plans to start battery production at the end of 2025 at the earliest.
- Hyundai Mobis will assemble battery packs using cells from the plant, then supply them to the Group’s U.S. manufacturing facilities for production of Hyundai, Kia and Genesis EV models.
- The new facility aims to help create a stable supply of batteries in the region and allow the Group to respond fast to the soaring EV demand in the U.S. market.
- The new electric vehicle battery factory in Georgia joins six others under Hyundai's wing, including some still in the construction phase.
- An Energy Department report indicates Georgia, Kentucky, and Michigan will dominate electric vehicle battery manufacturing in the United States by 2030.
- Kansas, North Carolina, Ohio, and Tennessee will also be key players in EV battery manufacturing.
- These EV battery-friendly states have been front and center in a Republican-organized movement to protect fossil energy stakeholders from competition by throwing up roadblocks to ESG investing.
- Renewable energy is a prominent feature of ESG guidelines.
- Georgia, Kentucky, Michigan, Kansas, North Carolina, Ohio, and Tennessee will collectively help electric vehicle manufacturers meet the demand for zero emission mobility.
- A Federal Reserve Bank report reported a total of more than $40 billion in planned investments for the North American electric vehicle battery buildout.
- There is an overlap between the electric vehicle battery race and states with anti-union “right to work” laws.
- About 28 states have laws on the books that restrict the ability of unions to organize, with the aim of driving down labor costs and attracting manufacturers.
- Many right-to-work laws go back to the 1940s, long before the mass market for electric vehicles began to take shape.
- Overseas competition was a leading factor in union-friendly northern states sliding into the Rust Belt, while right-to-work states continued to attract manufacturers with low labor costs.
- The low-cost labor factor has helped set the table for EV production, and Georgia is a leading example.
- Fortune reported on Hyundai’s other new electric battery venture in Georgia: an investment of up to $5 billion with the South Korean firm SK On.
- A photo caption noted Hyundai has been investing in Georgia for years, referring to a 2010 ribbon-cutting ceremony with former Georgia Governor Sonny Perdue for Hyundai’s first Kia factory in the US, located in West Point, Georgia.
- The SK project adds more fuel to Georgia’s competition with fossil energy.
- SK already has a $2.6 billion battery plant in Commerce, northeast of Atlanta, with more than 2,000 workers.
- The Commerce plant also makes EV batteries for Ford and Volkswagen.
- LG Energy Solution promotes Georgia as an epicenter of the decarbonization trend.
- LGES aims to ramp up local production to provide innovative products both in scale and with speed, thereby expediting the clean energy transition in the U.S.
- Republican officials in Georgia signed on to an anti-ESG open letter organized earlier this year by Florida Governor Ron DeSantis, joining 18 other states.
- The letter claims freedom-loving states can leverage state pension funds to force change in how major asset managers invest money of hardworking Americans, ensuring corporations focus on maximizing shareholder value rather than woke ideology.
- The letter claims the proliferation of ESG throughout America is a direct threat to the American economy, individual economic freedom, and way of life.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp signed the anti-ESG letter on behalf of his home state.
- Hyundai’s new $5 billion EV factory in Georgia is one of the largest economic development deals in state history.
- The state of Georgia committed an incentive package worth $1.8 billion for the automotive plant.
- The incentive package was described as easily the largest subsidy package a U.S. state has ever promised for an automotive plant.
