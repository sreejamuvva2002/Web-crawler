# Ultium Cells LLC

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [GM, LG to build second US EV battery plant in Tennessee - KED Global](https://www.kedglobal.com/ev-battery-plants/newsView/ked202104190005)  ·  `page_060375`

## Facts

- LG Energy Solution Ltd. and General Motors Co. will spend 2.7 trillion won ($2.4 billion) to build their second US electric vehicle battery plant in Tennessee.
- The new Tennessee plant should double their EV battery output in the country.
- Ultium Cells LLC, the joint venture between LG Energy Solution and General Motors, aims to complete the new Tennessee plant with an annual production capacity of 35-gigawatt hours (GWh) by the first half of 2024.
- LG Energy Solution and General Motors are already working on a $2.3 billion EV battery plant in Lordstown, Ohio.
- The Ohio plant has an annual production capacity of 35 GWh and is set to be completed by the second half of this year.
- The combined 70 GWh capacity of the Ohio and Tennessee plants will be enough to charge around 1 million high-performance, all-electric EVs that run for more than 500 km on a single charge.
- LG Energy Chief Executive Kim Jong-hyun stated at an April 16 event announcing the new plant that they will put efforts into building a stable US supply chain from R&D to raw materials procurement to battery production.
- Kim Jong-hyun added that LG Energy and GM will contribute to the success of the US Green New Deal policies together.
- LG Energy became the latest EV battery maker and global automaker to make new investments in the US, aligning with President Joe Biden’s green transportation policy.
- Early last week, LG Energy reached a settlement with domestic rival SK Innovation Co. to end a two-year US legal dispute over trade secret infringement and battery-related patent violations.
- Under the settlement agreement, SK Innovation will pay 2 trillion won to LG Energy, consisting of one trillion won in cash compensation and one trillion won in royalties.
- SK Innovation is currently constructing two battery plants in Georgia that will supply EV batteries to Ford Motor Co. and Volkswagen AG.
- The first SK Innovation plant in Georgia is set to begin operations in the first quarter of next year, with the second plant scheduled for mass production in 2023.
- Since being named the sole battery supplier for GM's first mass-produced EV, the Chevrolet Volt, in 2009, LG has provided batteries for GM's flagship models, including the Chevrolet Spark and Chevrolet Bolt EV.
- LG Energy's other clients include Tesla, Volkswagen AG, Hyundai Motor Co., and Kia Corp.
- On April 16, SK Innovation announced a joint development of hybrid electric vehicle batteries for Hyundai Motor Co. and Kia Corp.
