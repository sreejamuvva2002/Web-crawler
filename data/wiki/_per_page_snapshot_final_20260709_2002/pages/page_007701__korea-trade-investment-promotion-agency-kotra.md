# Korea Trade-Investment Promotion Agency (KOTRA)

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2022-04

**Source:** [KOTRA opens overseas office in Georgia](https://valdostatoday.com/business-2/2022/04/kotra-opens-overseas-office-in-georgia/)  ·  `page_007701`

## Facts

- A Korean strategic investment agency announced the opening of a new overseas office in Georgia to promote economic relations.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson and KOTRA Senior Executive Vice President Soo Deuk Sohn announced the opening of the new KOTRA overseas office in Atlanta.
- KOTRA is the Government of Korea’s trade and investment organization.
- GDEcD and KOTRA signed an MOU to support each other's services and networks and promote cooperation in economic relations, trade, and investment between Georgia and Korea.
- Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Korea is an established strategic market for investment, trade, and tourism for Georgia.
- Commissioner Wilson noted that KOTRA's decision to open an overseas office in Atlanta will strengthen existing partnerships.
- Existing connectivity includes daily direct flights between Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport and Incheon International Airport.
- Long-standing agreements exist between the Georgia ports of Savannah and Brunswick and five Korean ports.
- These connections facilitate joint efforts to create an innovative electric mobility ecosystem in Georgia.
- In early March, Commissioner Wilson visited Korea and met with KOTRA President and CEO Jeoung Yeol Yu.
- The mission included meetings with key business representatives from Kia and SK Group.
- The mission re-confirmed Georgia's commitment to its Korean companies and international partners.
- KOTRA Atlanta will serve as an outpost to support Korean businesses in the Southeastern United States.
- KOTRA aims to expand cooperation in many industrial fields between Korea and the U.S. and support Korean companies' local investments.
- KOTRA acts as the first point of contact to help Korean companies settle in new markets like Georgia.
- The agency provides investment and support resources, including information on programs from state or local agencies like GDEcD.
- The new KOTRA office is located at 950 East Paces Ferry Road NE, Suite 999 in Atlanta.
- The office will host teams dedicated to facilitating trade and investment opportunities between Georgia and Korea.
- This Atlanta office is the eighth overseas KOTRA office in the U.S. and the 10th in North America.
- The Republic of Korea has maintained a consular presence in Georgia since 1971.
- Yoonjoo Park has served as Consul General since June 2021.
- Georgia has maintained continuous representation in Korea since 1985 with a Seoul office headed by Managing Director Peter Underwood.
- The state has a dedicated Georgia-based director of Korean Investment, Yoonie Kim.
- Several Georgia companies, including American Megatrends, First Advantage Corporation, InComm, Novelis, Unicoi Systems, and UPS, have operations in Korea.
- Georgia is home to at least 100 Korean facilities.
- Korean-affiliated companies in Georgia currently employ more than 9,400 Georgians.
- These Korean-affiliated companies include Kia, SK Battery America, Sangsin Technology America, and Q CELLS Korea.
- Since 2020, numerous Korean companies have announced projects in Georgia.
- SK Group subsidiary SKC will manufacture glass-based substrates for semiconductor chips in Covington.
- SUNGDO ENG USA announced its corporate U.S. headquarters in Georgia.
- SUNGDO ENG USA provides highly technical construction services to many Georgia-based clients.
- Electric mobility parts and storage systems supplier for SK Group, Duckyang, is opening a new facility in Jackson County.
- Brake pad supplier KB Autosys will open its first U.S. advanced manufacturing facility in west Georgia.
- The KB Autosys facility aims to serve regional customers including GM, Hyundai, and Kia.
- The Georgia Ports Authority in Savannah offers seven regular shipping services to the Port of Busan.
- GPA's Brunswick Port offers shipping services to Incheon, Masan, Pyongtaek, and Kunsan.
- In 2021, Georgia exports to Korea totaled $860.59 million.
- Korea is the 10th largest export market for Georgia.
- Top exports from Georgia to Korea include gas turbines, aircraft, medical instruments, chemical woodpulp, and chemical analysis instruments.
- In 2021, Georgia imports from Korea totaled $8.83 billion.
- Korea ranks third among Georgia's importer nations.
- Top imports from Korea to Georgia include motor vehicles, tractor parts, bulldozers, engines, and fork-lift trucks.
- KOTRA was established in 1962 to promote trade, facilitate investment, and support industrial technology cooperation.
- KOTRA is headquartered in Seoul and operates over 127 Overseas Trade Centers in 84 countries.
