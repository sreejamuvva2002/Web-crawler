# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-18

**Source:** [Hyundai to expand Georgia plant hit by immigration raid with $2.7B investment](https://www.atlantanewsfirst.com/2025/09/18/hyundai-expand-georgia-plant-hit-by-immigration-raid-with-27b-investment/)  ·  `page_009422`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America is located in Ellabell, Georgia, about 20 miles northwest of Savannah.
- The plant is scheduled to produce 500,000 units by 2028.
- The expansion involves a $2.7 billion investment.
- The expansion will create 3,000 more jobs.
- Hyundai CEO José Muñoz said the company wants to produce more than 80% of its cars sold in the U.S. domestically by 2030.
- Several automakers are following similar steps after President Donald Trump announced steep auto tariffs to encourage domestic production.
- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp congratulated Hyundai on the project in a statement.
- Gov. Kemp wrote, “We look forward to working with them on their continued growth and success for years to come!”
- On Sept. 4, ICE conducted a raid on the southeast Georgia manufacturing site, detaining about 475 people.
- More than 300 South Korean workers were flown back to their home country in a charter plane last week.
- The incident caused alarm in South Korea, whose citizens saw videos of workers shackled with chains around their hands, ankles and waists.
- South Korean Foreign Ministry spokesperson Lee Jaewoong said in a televised statement from Seoul, “The business activities of our investors and the rights of our nationals must not be unjustly infringed in the process of U.S. law enforcement.”
- Homeland Security Investigations officials said the raid resulted from a months-long investigation into accusations of illegal hiring at the site.
- According to the workers’ lawyers, they were in the country to help install parts of the factory that American workers don’t have the training to do.
- Gov. Kemp has since called for a rehaul of the U.S.’s visa system.
- The automaker announced the plant expansion about two weeks after immigration authorities detained hundreds of South Korean nationals at the site.
