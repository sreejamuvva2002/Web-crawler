# Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-07-29

## Facts

- Kia Georgia is an automotive manufacturer located in West Point, Georgia.
- In 2024, Kia Georgia expanded its operations to include production of two all-electric SUVs.
- The expansion added 200 new jobs to the facility's existing workforce of more than 3,000 employees.
- Stuart Countess serves as Kia Georgia’s president and CEO.
- Countess noted that bringing EV production online required navigating supply chain disruptions and workforce challenges similar to those during the COVID-19 pandemic.
- Kia Georgia maintains communication with partners like the GDEcD and the Georgia Ports Authority to manage logistics and supply chain uncertainties.
- The company attributes its ability to bring EV production online and weather trade crises to a mindset of nimbleness, outward thinking, and strong internal leadership.
- Georgia’s manufacturing companies face uncertainty amid evolving trade policies and tariffs that have disrupted access to foreign markets and increased costs for importing materials or exporting goods.
- Georgia operates three Foreign-Trade Zones anchored to Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport and the Ports of Savannah and Brunswick.
- Manufacturing facilities using imported inputs can utilize Georgia’s Foreign-Trade Zones to defer customs duties and improve cash flow.
