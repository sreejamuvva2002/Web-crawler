# Archer Aviation Inc.

## Facts

- Archer Aviation Inc. (NYSE: ACHR) has updated on the progress of its high-volume manufacturing facility in Covington, GA.
- Archer Aviation confirms its Covington, GA manufacturing facility is on track for completion in 2024.
- Last week, Archer displayed its Midnight aircraft at Atlantic Aviation’s new terminal at DeKalb–Peachtree Municipal Airport.
- The event welcomed officials from Georgia’s Office of the Governor, Congressional Offices, Atlanta City, State Legislature, Federal Aviation Administration, and local governments.
- Construction of the Covington facility is advancing smoothly.
- Archer has finished the first phase of construction, which included grading and foundation work.
- The second phase of construction, involving the erection of exterior walls and roof, is nearly complete.
- The remaining phases of construction, including interior fit-out and equipment installation, are set to finish in the coming months.
- Upon completion, the Covington facility will span nearly 400,000 square feet.
- The facility aims to produce up to 650 Midnight aircraft yearly, potentially making it one of the aviation industry’s largest manufacturing facilities by volume.
- Archer is collaborating closely with Stellantis on this project to develop plans for mass production of the Midnight aircraft.
- Adam Goldstein, Archer’s CEO and founder, stated that their teams are working hard to complete the facility by year-end.
- Once finished, Archer will focus on scaling production to 650 aircraft annually.
- Archer aims to be the first in the eVTOL industry to achieve large-scale manufacturing.
- The Midnight is an electric vertical take-off and landing (eVTOL) aircraft and a strong entrant in the burgeoning urban air mobility market.
- The Midnight is built around Archer’s proprietary electric powertrain.
- The Midnight offers speeds up to 150 mph, turning long ground commutes into quick flights.
- The Midnight has capacity for back-to-back 20-50 mile flights with minimal charging time.
- The Midnight operates 100 times quieter than helicopters at cruising altitudes.
- The Midnight features redundant systems for airline-level safety.
- The Midnight has capacity for a pilot, four passengers, and carry-on luggage.
- The Midnight aims to provide a sustainable, quiet, and safe alternative to ground transportation.
- Urban Air Mobility (UAM) involves using small, highly automated aircraft for passenger or cargo transport within urban and suburban areas.
- eVTOL aircraft offer the potential to significantly reduce traffic congestion, increase accessibility, and cut down on travel times.
- The UAM sector is currently experiencing rapid development.
- Advancements in battery technology, electric propulsion, and autonomous flight systems are paving the way for a new era of urban transportation.
- Major aerospace companies and startups are actively developing various eVTOL prototypes, pushing the boundaries of what’s possible.
- High cost associated with developing and operating these new technologies is a key challenge before widespread commercialization.
- Ensuring the safety of passengers and those on the ground is paramount.
- Redundancy systems and robust regulations are crucial to building public trust in UAM.
- Integrating UAM into existing urban environments and infrastructure presents a challenge.
- Cities will need to consider noise pollution and potential environmental impacts when planning for vertiports, the designated landing and take-off zones for eVTOLs.
- The potential benefits of UAM include creating new business models, generating job opportunities, and positively impacting urban planning and development.
