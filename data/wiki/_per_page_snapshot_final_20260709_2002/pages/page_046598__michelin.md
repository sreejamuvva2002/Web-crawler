# Michelin

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- Presented in 2021, “Michelin in Motion 2030” defines Michelin’s sustainable growth strategy for 2030.
- The Group is targeting premium and specialty tires, relying on its technological leadership to accompany the development of electric mobility.
- Michelin already counts nearly 400 EV homologations with over 60 brands across the globe.
- As a pioneer and leader in sustainable materials, the Group targets 100% renewable and recycled materials by 2050.
- Michelin is pursuing a global approach aiming at reducing the overall environmental impact of its tires at every life-cycle stage.
- The company is developing offers for fleet managers and mobility stakeholders grouped under the “MICHELIN Connected Fleet” brand, utilizing data processing from connected objects.
- Using imaging technologies as a base, Michelin is offering solutions and recommendations to help road infrastructure managers make decisions for safer mobility.
- Michelin aims to enhance its expertise in high-tech materials through Polymer Composite Solutions, targeting fields including mobility, construction, aeronautics, low-carbon energies, and healthcare.
- The strategic plan targets 85 % employee engagement in 2030.
- The strategic plan targets 35 % women in executive positions in 2030.
- The strategic plan targets 20 % - 30 % annual growth between 2023 and 2030.
- The strategic plan targets ~ 5 % sales made beyond the tire in 2030.
- The strategic plan targets 40 % renewable or recycled materials in tires in 2030.
- The strategic plan targets a -50 % reduction in CO2 emissions in 2030 compared to 2010.
