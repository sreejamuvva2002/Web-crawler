# Panel Built, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Panel Built's International Commerce is Recognized with a GLOBE Award! | Panel Built](https://www.panelbuilt.com/blog/panel-builts-international-commerce-is-recognized-with-a-globe-award/)  ·  `page_060225`

## Facts

- Panel Built, Inc. was recognized by the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) for doing business internationally.
- The GDEcD presented the Georgia Launching Opportunities by Exporting (GLOBE) award to Panel Built at the State Capitol building during their sixth annual ceremony on December 6th.
- The GLOBE award ceremony was created by GDEcD to recognize Georgia businesses' work in international markets within the past year.
- This year's ceremony featured 36 individual Georgia businesses ranging from medical devices to software development to craft brewing.
- The awards ceremony placed an additional emphasis on the contributions of Georgia's small businesses to the international climate.
- Three out of four GLOBE award winners were considered small businesses with less than 100 employees, and nearly half have 20 employees or less.
- Panel Built has worked on projects for companies abroad in Canada, Mexico, South Korea, Morocco, Angola, Niger, and more.
- As a modular construction company, Panel Built offers value to overseas companies by ensuring quality structures that may require American-made standards.
- International projects include ballistic rated security booths, multi-story modular offices, bolt together steel mezzanines for scientific labs in Greenland, exterior buildings for oil production in the Democratic Republic of Congo, and rappel training towers in north Africa.
- A good portion of these international projects are for the United States government, involving military bases or US embassies.
- All of Panel Built's buildings are fabricated in one of its specialized modular construction facilities.
- Panel Built has worked in the modular construction industry for nearly 25 years.
- GDEcD offers assets and insights to help companies navigate international markets, with global knowledge and specialized representatives.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson, Deputy Commissioner Mary Waters, and Georgia Governor Brian Kemp were present at the GLOBE Awards to give remarks and take photos with winners.
- GDEcD was established in 1949 and acts as a resource for current and future Georgia businesses.
- The department has seven distinct divisions: Global Commerce, International Trade, Workforce, the Georgia Centers of Innovation, Tourism, Film, Music & Digital Entertainment, and the Georgia Council for the Arts.
- GDEcD is one of the main contributing factors to Georgia's status as a top state to do business.
- Georgia's economic production spans from automotive manufacturing to movies, being ranked near the top states for film in recent years.
