# Ascend Elements

## Facts

- Ascend Elements, a U.S.-based manufacturer of sustainable, engineered battery materials for electric vehicles, has raised $542 million in new equity investments.
- The $542 million equity financing includes $460 million in Series D investments and $82 million in earlier investments this year.
- The investment will help Ascend Elements realize its vision of commercially producing sustainable, engineered battery materials.
- The sustainable lithium-ion battery materials will power electric vehicle batteries and hasten the global transition to zero carbon emissions.
- Series D funding for Ascend Elements was led by Decarbonization Partners, Temasek of Singapore, and Qatar Investment Authority (QIA).
- Other investors include Tenaska, Alliance Resource Partners, PULSE – CMA CGM Energy Fund, BHP Ventures, Fifth Wall, Hitachi Ventures, Mirae Asset, At One Ventures, Agave Partners, Alumni Ventures and other leading global investors.
- The $542 million equity financing is one of the ten largest cleantech equity private placements in the United States in 2023.
- The $542 million equity financing is one of the ten largest equity private placements in the United States this year.
- The funding will facilitate construction of the Apex 1 facility in Hopkinsville, Kentucky.
- The Apex 1 facility will be the first sustainable cathode precursor (pCAM) and cathode active material (CAM) manufacturing facility in North America.
- CAM and pCAM are engineered materials manufactured to exact microstructure specifications for use in batteries for electric vehicles.
- While most of the world’s pCAM and CAM are made in China from primary (mined) metals, U.S.-based Ascend Elements is commercializing an ultra-efficient method to manufacture sustainable pCAM and CAM from black mass.
- Black mass is the traditional output of lithium-ion battery recycling facilities.
- In the past year, Ascend Elements reached several significant milestones, including the signing of its first commercial scale pCAM contract.
- In the past year, Ascend Elements reached the commencement of construction on its Apex 1 facility.
- In the past year, Ascend Elements reached the receipt of two grants from the U.S. Department of Energy (DOE).
- When finished, the one-million-square-foot facility will produce enough sustainable pCAM to power 750,000 electric vehicles (EVs) annually.
- In October 2022, the U.S. Department of Energy awarded Ascend Elements two grants totaling $480 million as part of the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law (BIL).
