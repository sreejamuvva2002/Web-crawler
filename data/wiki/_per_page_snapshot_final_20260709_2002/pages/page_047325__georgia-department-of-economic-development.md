# Georgia Department of Economic Development

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2016-12-09

**Source:** [Georgia Honors Its Foreign Auto Investors and Has High Hopes to Welcome More - Global Atlanta](https://www.globalatlanta.com/georgia-honors-foreign-auto-investors-high-hopes-welcome/)  ·  `page_047325`

## Facts

- Mike Grundmann serves as director of automotive, aerospace and advanced manufacturing at the Georgia Department of Economic Development.
- Foreign companies have invested $2.8 billion in Georgia’s automotive sector.
- Georgia is strategically located as a center for assembly and manufacturing, possesses a quality workforce, and has developed a reputation for innovation in the industry.
- The department along with affiliated organizations announced winners of its inaugural Georgia Automotive Awards in five categories to honor contributions to the state's robust automotive ecosystem.
- Novelis Inc. received the automotive innovation award.
- Toyo Tire U.S.A. Corp. received the automotive supplier of the year award.
- Hitachi Automotive Systems Americas Inc. received the community impact award.
- Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia Inc. received the Georgia automotive company of the year award.
- J. Randy Jackson received the Georgia automotive leader of the year award.
- All award recipient companies had expanded their operations in the state since initially settling there.
- Toyo Tire arrived in Georgia in 2004 and has since expanded its facility several times to 3 million square feet under one roof, representing a local investment of more than $1 billion.
- Toyo Tire grew during the recession.
- The department looks for automotive suppliers that show strong growth and have an impact on the health of the state.
- Novelis developed sophisticated aluminum for Jaguar, Land Rover and Ford vehicles to help them meet evolving fuel efficiency and greenhouse gas emissions standards by reducing vehicle weight without compromising safety or strength.
- Monroe-based Hitachi employs 1,000 people and manufactures valve timing systems, propeller shafts and power steering systems.
- Hitachi was honored for community involvement through the F.I.S.H. (Faith in Serving Humanity) program, which provides food to combat child hunger in Walton County.
- Hitachi is active in the Communities in Schools program.
- The Communities in Schools program works to ensure at-risk children remain in school by providing one-on-one relationships with caring adults, a safe place to grow and learn, proper nutrition and exposure to marketable skills upon graduation.
- Kia Motors Manufacturing Inc. won automotive company of the year, exemplifying relevant factors and demonstrating overall industry leadership within the state.
- Kia celebrated the manufacture of its 2-millionth car earlier this year (relative to December 2016).
- Kia donated $2.1 million toward STEM education in local elementary schools.
- Kia made a $3.5 million contribution to establish THINC, a new college and career academy at West Georgia Technical College’s LaGrange campus.
- J. Randy Jackson was Kia’s chief administrative officer and a key figure behind the company's success in Georgia.
- The award for J. Randy Jackson was conferred posthumously to honor his memory for support of STEM education, workforce development and the auto industry within the state.
- Grundmann anticipates more foreign investment in the state’s auto sector.
- Grundmann cited the success of Georgia’s Quick Start program in preparing the state’s workforce and credited it with attracting Chinese company Sentury Tire.
- Grundmann anticipates more Chinese investment, stating that the department is always recruiting from China and many Chinese companies are interested in establishing operations.
- Grundmann mentioned continued interest from Korean and Japanese companies.
- Volvo Cars of North America LLC announced a $500 million investment for a plant 30 miles northwest of Charleston, S.C., which serves as an incentive for suppliers to move to Georgia with easy access to the plant.
- LINDE + WIEMANN GmbH KG, a German manufacturer of structural steel systems for automotive OEMs in Europe, Asia and Africa, announced a $35 million investment in January.
- LINDE + WIEMANN will benefit from its location in Hart County, which borders South Carolina and is in fairly easy reach of Charleston.
- Georgia’s future role on the cutting edge of automotive innovation is secured by the Panasonic Automotive Innovation Center near the Georgia Institute of Technology’s campus, which will serve as an incubator for next-generation automotive infotainment technologies.
- Georgia will benefit from the AT&T Drive Studio in Midtown, where AT&T will develop and test connected car technologies such as internet connectivity, remote diagnostics and over-the-air software updates.
- Grundmann stated that Georgia is rich in innovation and at the epicenter of talent and research.
- Grundmann added that the state has an autonomous eco center that may now be its most valued asset.
