# Hughes Telematics Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2007-10-31

**Source:** [Telematics Services Agreement - HUGHES Telematics Inc. & Mercedes-Benz USA, LLC](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1410240/000119312509144308/dex1018.htm)  ·  `page_028670`

## Facts

- Hughes Telematics Inc. (HTI) is a Delaware corporation with its principal address at 41 Perimeter Center East, Suite 400, Atlanta, Georgia 30346.
- HTI is in the business of providing telematics equipment and services for the automotive industry.
- The Telematics Services Agreement between HTI and Mercedes-Benz USA, LLC (MBUSA) was executed on October 31, 2007.
- MBUSA will factory-install telematics equipment capable of receiving services in MCG Vehicles and authorize HTI to deliver those services.
- MCG Vehicles are defined as vehicles manufactured by or for MBUSA during calendar year 2010 or thereafter for sale or lease within the Territory.
- The HTI Telematics Communicator (TCU) is a device capable of receiving PASS Services and MBTA Services, provided through an Authorized Manufacturer and/or Authorized Suppliers.
- Hughes Network Systems, LLC is deemed an Authorized Supplier of HTI with respect to the HTI Telematics Communicators.
- LCT Enabled Vehicles are vehicles sold or leased by MBUSA equipped with a Low Cost Tele Aid (LCT) Communicator or successor devices.
- Embedded Cellular Technology refers to transmission and reception via a device installed as original equipment using wireless signals based on communication networks.
- The agreement outlines standards and service levels for PASS Services and MBTA Services in Exhibit B.
- Technical specifications for the HTI Telematics Communicator are detailed in Exhibit G.
- MCG Vehicle models and planned production volumes are listed on Exhibits D-1 and D-2, respectively.
- HTI must meet specific milestone requirements reflected on Exhibit N of the agreement.
- The first year of subscription service to PASS Services and MBTA Services is provided free to subscribers.
- Subscription fees for the second and subsequent years are charged by HTI to subscribers for the telematics services.
- Vehicle and subscriber data collected by both parties is summarized in Exhibit V, which serves as a living document subject to mutual written modification.
- Personal information, data protection, and security requirements are governed by Exhibit AA of the agreement.
- An Executive Steering Committee and an Operating Committee will be established to oversee the telematics services relationship.
- The agreement covers intellectual property ownership for prior developed, contributed, and jointly created technology between HTI and MBUSA.
- Indemnification obligations regarding intellectual property infringement and general liabilities survive the termination or expiration of the agreement.
- The Territory covered by the agreement includes the fifty states of the United States, the District of Columbia, and Puerto Rico.
