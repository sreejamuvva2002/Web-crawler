# SK Battery America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-01-26

**Source:** [SK Battery America to Open U.S. IT Center, Create 200 High-Tech Jobs in Roswell - AllOnGeorgia](https://allongeorgia.com/georgia-business/sk-battery-america-to-open-u-s-it-center-create-200-high-tech-jobs-in-roswell/)  ·  `page_000494`

## Facts

- SK Battery America (SKBA) will open a regional IT hub facility in Roswell.
- The new facility will create 200 high-tech jobs.
- SK Battery America is investing approximately $19 million over the next few years for the Roswell IT center.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated Georgia is the No. 1 state for business.
- Governor Brian Kemp noted the company brings jobs of the future that benefit hardworking Georgians in manufacturing and tap into tech talent from top-ranking universities.
- SK Battery America is investing nearly $2.6 billion in two lithium-ion battery manufacturing facilities in Jackson County.
- The Jackson County facilities will supply electric vehicles such as the Ford F-150 Lightning and Volkswagen ID.4.
- The Roswell IT facility is one of SK Group’s three major investments in the State of Georgia.
- SK Group was founded in 1976 and is the second-largest conglomerate in South Korea.
- Jason Choi, Head of Information Technology at SK Battery America, stated the decision to open the IT regional center in Roswell aims to strengthen SK’s position as a leader in the battery industry.
- The IT center will help build and operate an advanced manufacturing system in accordance with further expansion of battery production bases in the United States.
- Jason Choi expressed excitement to expand SK Battery America's presence and engage with the community and surrounding region.
- The new IT center will serve as an integrated IT management center for SKBA’s battery manufacturing facilities across the United States, including its Georgia facilities.
- The Roswell facility is located at 1110 Sanctuary Parkway in Roswell.
- Operations at the new IT center are expected to begin in January.
- The company will be hiring IT experts for battery manufacturing execution system development and operations as well as data analysts.
- City of Roswell Mayor Kurt Wilson stated he looks forward to SK Battery America becoming part of the community and bringing opportunities to Roswell.
- Mayor Kurt Wilson noted that since taking office a year ago, his priority has been recruiting businesses like SK Battery America that provide high-quality job opportunities for Roswell’s workforce and enhance economic strength.
- Mayor Kurt Wilson called Roswell the perfect location for the IT Regional Center and expressed pride in the choice.
- Fulton County Board of Commissioners Chairman Robb Pitts welcomed SK Battery America to Fulton County and noted the new job opportunities they will create for residents.
- Robb Pitts described Fulton County as a destination for the best and brightest, reflecting the announcement.
- Director of Korean Investment Yoonie Kim represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The project was in partnership with the City of Roswell, Fulton County, and Georgia Power.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated SK On established SK Battery America as its U.S. production base in 2019.
- Pat Wilson noted the new IT regional center is a natural result of the company’s commitment to Georgia.
- Pat Wilson discussed the ripple effects of economic development, noting suppliers, support centers, and expansions create opportunities beyond the original scope, location, and industry.
- Pat Wilson expressed looking forward to future opportunities to celebrate SK Battery America and SK On’s success in Georgia, and congratulated Roswell and Fulton County.
- Electric mobility related projects have contributed more than $21 billion in investments to Georgia since 2020.
- Electric mobility related projects have contributed more than 26,700 new jobs to Georgia since 2020.
- SK Battery America’s new IT regional center will support the company’s battery production operations.
- The center will build on Georgia’s existing automotive assets and developing workforce.
- The center will help ensure a domestic supply chain for the electric mobility industry.
- SK On is the parent company of SK Battery America.
- SK On is a global leading electric vehicle battery developer, manufacturer, and solutions provider.
- As SK On’s U.S. production base, SK Battery America produces safe, fast charging, and long-lasting batteries currently installed in the Ford F-150 Lightning and Volkswagen ID.4.
- The company will have a combined capacity to make 22 GWh of battery cells a year.
- This capacity is enough to power the equivalent of about 300,000 EVs annually.
- Since 2019, SK Battery America has invested a total of $2.6 billion to build EV battery plants in Commerce, Georgia.
- These investments created 2,600 high-skilled jobs.
