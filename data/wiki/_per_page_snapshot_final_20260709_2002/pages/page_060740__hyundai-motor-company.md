# Hyundai Motor Company

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** research_and_development  ·  **Published:** 2025-02-25

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor and Samsung Unveil Breakthrough Private 5G RedCap Technology for Smart Manufacturing - HMGMA](https://www.hmgma.com/2025/02/25/hyundai-motor-and-samsung-unveil-breakthrough-private-5g-redcap-technology-for-smart-manufacturing/)  ·  `page_060740`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Company collaborated with Samsung Electronics Co., Ltd. to verify Private 5G (P-5G) RedCap technology for smart manufacturing.
- The P-5G RedCap technology enables fast, seamless communication with lower power consumption and cost.
- The technology will be showcased at MWC25 Barcelona from March 3rd to 6th.
- Hyundai Ulsan Plant and Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) are pioneering P-5G in mass production.
- Hyundai Motor plans to expand the technology to its new EV-dedicated plant in Ulsan, which is scheduled to open in 2026.
- Hyundai Motor was the first Korean company to implement P-5G in mass production.
- Hyundai Motor is the industry’s first in the mobility manufacturing sector to verify P-5G RedCap technology.
- Jae Min Lee, Vice President and Head of E-FOREST Center of Hyundai Motor and Kia, stated that Hyundai will continue to accelerate the commercialization of smart manufacturing solutions.
- Simon Lee, Vice President and Head of B2B·B2G Business Development at Samsung Electronics Networks Business, said the collaboration represents how industry leaders can drive business innovation by merging expertise.
- Since January 2025, Hyundai Motor has completed testing of Samsung’s P-5G solutions including radio, core, and management systems with in-house vehicle inspection equipment at Samsung’s Suwon campus.
- Hyundai developed a custom vehicle inspection device to verify the network’s operational capabilities.
- P-5G is a dedicated onsite system installed using a specific frequency band that ensures no external interference and seamless data transmission for centralized control of industrial robots and devices.
- The collaboration verified P-5G RedCap technology, which offers simplified device configurations, compact infrastructure, and reduced bandwidth usage.
- RedCap technology results in lower power consumption, cost efficiency, and improved performance over traditional Wi-Fi in speed, data processing, connection stability, and low latency.
- Previously limited to high-performance equipment, RedCap now enables high-speed wireless communication for vehicle inspection equipment, small tools, cameras, and tablets.
- Hyundai Motor integrated RedCap enabled Qualcomm’s Snapdragon® X35 5G Modem-RF System chipsets into its Diagnostic Scan (D Scan) system for efficient vehicle inspection data transmission via Samsung’s 5G network.
- Samsung’s P-5G solution reduces power consumption and more than doubles uplink capacity for real-time IoT data transmission, featuring an integrated management system for automation.
- Hyundai established a P-5G testbed in Korea in late 2022 before applying the technology in mass production at its Ulsan plant in October 2024.
- At the Ulsan plant, Hyundai deployed dozens of automated guided vehicles using the P-5G technology.
- At Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA), Hyundai adapted its P-5G to meet local regulations, enabling the efficient operation of over 200 autonomous mobile robots.
- Implementation at plants in Korea and the US has significantly reduced communication disruptions and downtime.
- Hyundai developed a patented dual wireless communication solution combining 5G networks with Wi-Fi, leading to zero communication-related downtime since deployment.
- Hyundai plans further expansions of P-5G technology to key plants beyond Ulsan and HMGMA.
