# U.S. Battery Manufacturing of Augusta

**Type:** Company  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[battery-cell-manufacturing|Battery Cell Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** Augusta, Georgia, Georgia, United States

## Overview

Federal OSHA investigation found that U.S. Battery Mfg. Augusta Inc.'s Augusta facility exposed employees to unsafe lead levels, resulting in four citations and proposed penalties of $160,727.

## EV supply-chain relevance

The company designs and manufactures deep-cycle batteries used in electric equipment such as golf carts, which are part of the broader EV supply chain.

## Timeline

- **1926** — Company founded.
- **2018** — Federal safety inspections identified numerous lead-exposure violations at the Augusta facility.
- **2019** — OSHA issued citations for one serious, one repeat, and one other-than-serious violation.
- **May 2023** — OSHA inspections opened that found the company lacked engineering controls and proper respirator cleaning.
- **September 27, 2023** — OSHA released findings, citing four violations and proposing $160,727 in penalties.

## Key facts

- U.S. Battery Mfg. Augusta Inc. designs and manufactures deep-cycle batteries for scissor lifts, golf carts, boats, and other uses.
- OSHA cited the company for four violations related to lead exposure and proposed $160,727 in penalties.
- Lead concentrations at the Augusta facility exceeded permissible limits; respirators were not cleaned or disinfected.
- Since 2018, multiple OSHA inspections have identified numerous lead-related safety violations at the site.
- The company operates facilities in Corona, California, and in Evans and Augusta, Georgia.
- "U.S. Battery is well aware that elevated lead levels can cause debilitating and permanent health issues but once again, our inspectors found the employer failing to protect its workers," said OSHA Area Office Director Josh Turner.
