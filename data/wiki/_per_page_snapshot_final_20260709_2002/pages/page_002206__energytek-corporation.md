# EnergyTek Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** charging_infrastructure  ·  **Published:** 2025-08-29

**Source:** [EnergyTek Corporation Selects Colquitt County | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-releases/2025/energytek-corporation-selects-colquitt-county)  ·  `page_002206`

## Facts

- EnergyTek Corporation is planning to invest at least $275 million in establishing new manufacturing operations in Moultrie, Colquitt County.
- Following a multi-year ramp up, the company expects to create a total of 1,300 jobs at full operations.
- EnergyTek plans to manufacture utility scale and commercial solar panels, inverters, EV charging systems, micro grids, and other renewable energy components in Moultrie.
- The new manufacturing operations will be located in Citizen Business Park, a “Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development” (GRAD) site, in Moultrie.
- Construction on the first phase is expected to begin in the fourth quarter of 2025.
- The company will be hiring for management, engineering, production, and administrative positions over the next few years.
- EnergyTek Corporation is focused on bringing low cost of electricity infrastructure to commercial and utility scale customers by vertically integrating the value chain between power generation, management, and distribution.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Georgia’s commitment to innovation continues to attract companies like EnergyTek that are investing and creating good jobs in every corner of the state.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson congratulated partners in Colquitt County on this important milestone for the Citizen Business Park.
- Dr. Cristian Toma, CEO of EnergyTek, said the multi-phase investment will allow the company to manufacture multiple aspects of the energy and electrification supply chain, simplifying vertical integration.
- Dr. Cristian Toma also noted that Georgia’s industrial ecosystem and world-class logistics will support efficient distribution and supply chain integration as EnergyTek scales up production in Moultrie over the next few years.
- Chairman Ross Dekle of the Moultrie-Colquitt County Development Authority stated that EnergyTek has chosen Moultrie and Colquitt County as the site of its new manufacturing facility.
- Chairman Ross Dekle added that the significant investment will create high-quality, well-compensated manufacturing jobs for residents and represents a meaningful contribution to the continued growth and prosperity of the community.
- Assistant Director of Existing Industry and Regional Recruitment Tina Herring represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project in partnership with the Moultrie-Colquitt County Development Authority, City of Moultrie, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
