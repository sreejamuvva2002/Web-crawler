# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-05

**Source:** [Georgia Scores Top Spot in Competitive Economic Rankings - LaGrange CEO](https://lagrangeceo.com/features/2024/05/georgia-scores-top-spot-competitive-economic-rankings/)  ·  `page_052350`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp and the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) announced that Georgia earned the No. 1 spot in Site Selection’s annual Prosperity Cup rankings for successful investment recruitment in 2023.
- The Prosperity Cup criteria include total values of new and expanded facilities, business-climate attractiveness, and workforce readiness.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that he proudly tells companies across the country and the world how great Georgia is for operating and growing a business.
- Governor Brian Kemp noted that the Site Selection award adds to a decade of evidence that the way they do business in the Peach State is working.
- Companies choosing Georgia gain access to global markets through logistics assets like Hartsfield-Jackson International Airport and some of the fastest-growing ports in the nation.
- According to Site Selection, Georgia attracted 318 new and expanded facilities in 2023 to secure the No. 1 ranking.
- The expanded scope for Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) is the largest project in state history.
- HMGMA announced an additional 400 new jobs and $2 billion in investment last year, bringing its total investment to $7.59 billion and netting 8,500 new jobs for hardworking Georgians.
- Anovion Technologies selected southwest Georgia for its $800 million, 400-job synthetic graphite manufacturing facility in May and broke ground at the site in June of last year.
- In northeast Georgia, Meissner Corporation plans to create more than 1,700 new jobs at a new campus that will more than double the company’s manufacturing footprint in the U.S.
- Site Selection’s Mark Arend stated that the Prosperity Cup recognizes state-level economic development competitiveness.
- Georgia’s consistent delivery of location attributes required by capital investors demonstrates to readers that it competes to win, according to Mark Arend.
- Following HMGMA’s groundbreaking in October 2022, Georgia welcomed more than 10 Hyundai and automotive suppliers in 2023 reaching middle Georgia and beyond.
- These Hyundai and automotive suppliers will ultimately create over 4,300 new jobs and bring $1.2 billion in combined investment.
- Kia’s facility in West Point and the Toyota campus in Pendergrass both announced expansions during 2023, creating a combined 440 new jobs and $286 million in investment.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Georgia’s economic development team is truly collaborative, including sister state agencies, utility partners, local economic developers, and many others.
- Taking the top spot in the 2024 Prosperity Cup reaffirms that the Georgia Way works, according to Commissioner Pat Wilson.
- Decades of partnerships, intentional economic development from the community level to the state level, and consistent pro-business policies have led to these positive results.
- Commissioner Pat Wilson thanked Georgia leaders throughout the state for supporting efforts that make a difference for Georgia families.
