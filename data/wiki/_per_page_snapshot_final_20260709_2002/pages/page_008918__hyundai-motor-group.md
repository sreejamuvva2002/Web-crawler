# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-04-03

**Source:** [Hyundai Targets 30,000 Atlas Robots Per Year as Boston Dynamics Shifts From Prototype to Mass Production — The Machine Herald](https://machineherald.io/article/2026-04/03-hyundai-targets-30000-atlas-robots-per-year-as-boston-dynamics-shifts-from-prototype-to-mass-production/)  ·  `page_008918`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group plans to purchase tens of thousands of Boston Dynamics robots.
- Hyundai Motor Group will build a dedicated robotics factory capable of producing 30,000 humanoid Atlas units annually by 2028.
- The initiative is backed by a $26 billion U.S. investment plan that includes the new robotics factory.
- This commitment represents the largest known order for humanoid robots by any single manufacturer.
- Boston Dynamics unveiled the production-ready version of its electric Atlas humanoid at CES 2026 in January.
- The final enterprise Atlas model can reach up to 7.5 feet tall, lift 110 pounds, and operate in temperatures ranging from minus 4 to 104 degrees Fahrenheit.
- Boston Dynamics CEO Robert Playter called the Atlas robot “the best robot we have ever built.”
- All Atlas deployments for 2026 are fully committed, with initial fleets shipping to Hyundai’s Robotics Metaplant Application Center and to Google DeepMind.
- Google DeepMind is integrating its Gemini Robotics AI foundation models into the Atlas platform.
- Hyundai plans to begin using Atlas robots in its automotive plants by 2028, starting with parts sequencing tasks at its $7.6 billion Metaplant America facility outside Savannah, Georgia.
- By 2030, the Atlas robots are expected to handle component assembly, repetitive motions, heavy loads, and other complex operations.
- Hyundai Vice Chair Jaehoon Chang stated that “physical AI and humanoid robots will transform our business landscape to the next level.”
- Robert Playter noted that Hyundai Motor Group will become Boston Dynamics’ biggest customer.
- Boston Dynamics’ Spot quadruped robots are already deployed for industrial inspection and predictive maintenance at Hyundai facilities.
- Spot units are currently performing exterior quality inspections in the Metaplant America’s weld shop.
- Hyundai Mobis announced a strategic collaboration framework with Boston Dynamics to supply actuators for the Atlas robot.
- Actuators represent more than 60 percent of a humanoid robot’s material cost, making them the single most expensive subsystem.
- Hyundai Mobis plans to build a mass-production system for actuators and gradually expand its robotics portfolio to include hand grippers, sensors, controllers, and battery packs.
- Boston Dynamics’ Zack Jackowski said the collaboration “allows us to access the well-established cost structures and scale potential of the automotive industry.”
- Hyundai acquired Boston Dynamics from SoftBank in 2021 for $1.1 billion.
- Competitors such as Figure AI, Apptronik, and Tesla’s Optimus program have not disclosed production capacity targets on a similar scale.
- Boston Dynamics’ existing commercial customers include Gap Inc., InBev, and South Korean steelmaker POSCO.
- Goldman Sachs projects the humanoid robotics market will exceed $38 billion by 2035.
- Atlas pricing has not been disclosed, while Mercedes-Benz’s Apollo humanoid robots from Apptronik cost less than $100,000 each.
- The first large-scale Atlas production lines could begin operating by 2028.
