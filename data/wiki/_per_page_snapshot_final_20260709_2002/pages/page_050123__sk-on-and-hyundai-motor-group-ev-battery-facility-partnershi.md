# SK On and Hyundai Motor Group EV Battery Facility Partnership

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-12-08

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Group and SK On To Build EV Battery Facility in Bartow County - HMGMA](https://www.hmgma.com/2022/12/08/hyundai-motor-group-and-sk-on-to-build-ev-battery-facility-in-bartow-county/)  ·  `page_050123`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Hyundai Motor Group (HMG) and SK On have selected a site in Bartow County for a new electric vehicle (EV) battery manufacturing facility.
- The new facility will supply Hyundai Motor Group’s plants in the U.S.
- Stakeholders estimate the project will create more than 3,500 new jobs through approximately $4-5 billion of investment in Bartow County.
- Hyundai Motor Group and SK On recently signed a memorandum of understanding (MOU) regarding the partnership for a new EV battery facility in the U.S.
- Details of the partnership are still in development.
- SK On was established in 2021 as the lithium-ion battery subsidiary branch of SK Innovation.
- SK On currently employs more than 2,000 Georgians at its SK Battery America facility in Commerce.
- In July, SK Battery America announced it would partner with the Work for Warriors Georgia program to hire veterans, servicemembers, and their families at their Georgia operations.
- The new facility is subject to execution of relevant agreements and HMG and SK On’s final board decisions.
- The facility will be located at Bartow Centre, a zoned manufacturing and industrial site located on Highway 411.
- The project is aiming to begin operations in 2025.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated that SK and HMG share his administration's goal of bringing jobs and opportunity to overlooked communities, and expressed pride in their investment in Georgia, calling it the No. 1 state for business.
- Bartow County Sole Commissioner Steve Taylor stated that the project will have regional benefit for job seekers, establishes Bartow in Georgia’s EV ecosystem significantly, and noted Bartow’s strong history and talent base built upon advanced manufacturing, the automotive industry, and innovation.
- Director of Korean Investment Yoonie Kim represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The GDEcD partnered with Georgia EMC, Development Authority of Bartow County, the Cartersville-Bartow County Department of Economic Development, and Georgia Quick Start on the project.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that HMG and SK have been pioneering partners for Georgia for decades, highlighted Georgia’s prime location, major ports system, extensive infrastructure, skilled workforce, and pro-business climate as attractive factors, and expressed excitement for creating a fully integrated supply chain while connecting battery manufacturers with recyclers to close the loop on battery manufacturing.
- Excluding this announcement, EV-related projects announced in the state since 2020 total approximately $17 billion in investment and more than 22,800 new jobs in Georgia.
- Hyundai Motor Group separately announced in May its plans to invest $5.54 billion in Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America, an EV manufacturing facility in Bryan County.
- Hyundai Motor Group broke ground on the Metaplant America facility in October with Governor Kemp and other state, local, and federal officials.
