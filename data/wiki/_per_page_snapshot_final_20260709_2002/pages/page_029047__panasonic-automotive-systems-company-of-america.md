# Panasonic Automotive Systems Company of America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-11-28

**Source:** [Panasonic Automotive Introduces Virtual SkipGen, Now Available on AWS Marketplace](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/panasonic-automotive-introduces-virtual-skipgen-now-available-on-aws-marketplace-301999285.html)  ·  `page_029047`

## Facts

- Panasonic Automotive Systems Company of America is a tier-one automotive supplier.
- On Nov. 28, 2023, at AWS re:Invent, Panasonic Automotive announced the availability of Virtual SkipGen™ (vSkipGen™) on AWS Marketplace.
- vSkipGen is a virtual replica of the physical, 3rd generation Digital Cockpit solution known as SkipGen.
- The platform enables automakers to 'shift left' the automotive development lifecycle, allowing engineers and developers to begin work earlier without requiring physical hardware.
- vSkipGen decouples software development from the hardware development lifecycle to reduce time to market and help improve software quality.
- The solution leverages industry standard VirtIO-based device virtualization technology.
- It offers fully optimized support for Android Automotive for infotainment and Automotive Grade Linux for cluster vehicle domains.
- The platform supports needed automotive peripherals and sensor simulation for the development of modern cockpit systems.
- The virtual platform is cloud-native and utilizes cloud servers' computing capability to provide hardware acceleration for multimedia, graphic, audio, and display rendering.
- Panasonic and Amazon maintain an ongoing collaboration to evolve the eCockpit.
- Andrew Poliak serves as chief technology officer at Panasonic Automotive Systems Company of America.
- Poliak stated that the partnership with AWS to run cockpit domains as virtual machines in the cloud is critical to ensure development teams can seamlessly work in parallel, enable 3rd party and app development, and conduct more predictive integration and testing earlier in the process.
- Panasonic has invested in advancing the software defined vehicle to support market growth and its GREEN IMPACT mission.
- The solution reflects Panasonic's environmental goals by eliminating the need for a development or prototype hardware platform, thereby saving resources and components.
- Panasonic Automotive Systems Company of America is headquartered in Peachtree City, Georgia.
- The company maintains sales, marketing, and engineering operations in Farmington Hills, Michigan.
- Panasonic Automotive Systems Company of America operates as a division company of Panasonic Corporation of North America.
- Panasonic Corporation of North America is based in Newark, New Jersey.
- Panasonic Corporation of North America serves as the principal North American subsidiary of Osaka, Japan-based Panasonic Corporation.
