# Kia Georgia Plant

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-02-25

**Source:** [Kia Georgia Plant Hits 5 Million Vehicle Milestone, Launches Telluride Hybrid - Seoul Economic Daily](https://en.sedaily.com/finance/2026/02/25/kia-georgia-plant-hits-5-million-vehicle-milestone-launches)  ·  `page_010072`

## Facts

- Kia Corp.'s Georgia subsidiary announced it reached cumulative production of 5 million vehicles as of Dec. 24 local time.
- The 5 millionth vehicle was the all-new Telluride Hybrid, marking the first hybrid vehicle produced in the state of Georgia.
- Kia has begun full-scale hybrid production at its West Point, Georgia plant with the launch of the 2027 model year "All-New Telluride."
- Since starting production in 2009, Kia Georgia has established itself as a key manufacturing hub for the automotive industry in the southeastern United States and grown into a leading regional employer.
- The Telluride is produced exclusively at the West Point plant and was developed based on North American consumer demands and U.S. road conditions as a local strategic model.
- The first-generation Telluride swept major awards in 2020, including North American Car of the Year, World Car of the Year, and MotorTrend SUV of the Year.
- Governor Brian Kemp joined local government officials and company employees at a commemorative ceremony to celebrate Kia Georgia's achievement.
- Sean Yoon, president and CEO of Kia North America and head of the North America region, stated that Kia Georgia is the only plant in the world that produces the Telluride.
- Reaching 5 million units and producing Georgia's first hybrid vehicle demonstrates Kia's technological advancement and future strategic direction, according to Sean Yoon.
- Governor Kemp said the Telluride has become one of the most successful models in Kia's lineup since its 2019 launch due to a close partnership between the local community and state government.
- Stuart Countess, chief administrative officer of Kia Georgia, stated that adding hybrid models to internal combustion and electric vehicle production proves the plant's flexibility and technical capabilities.
