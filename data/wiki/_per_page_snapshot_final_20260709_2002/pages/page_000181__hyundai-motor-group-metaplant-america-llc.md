# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America LLC

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-10-25

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Group Breaks Ground on Metaplant America Dedicated EV and Battery Plant](https://www.hyundai.com/worldwide/en/newsroom/detail/hyundai-motor-group-breaks-ground-on-metaplant-america-dedicated-ev-and-battery-plant-0000000132)  ·  `page_000181`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group Executive Chair Euisun Chung, Governor Brian P. Kemp, and company officials, along with state and local leaders, officially broke ground on the new manufacturing plant at the Bryan County Mega site in Bryan County, Ga., on Oct. 25, 2022.
- The celebration included a morning groundbreaking followed by a future technology expo at Enmarket Arena in Savannah highlighting advanced technology and human achievement for hundreds of community attendees.
- Hyundai Motor Group announced a $5.54 billion investment for the project five months ago.
- The facility plans to produce a diverse range of innovative Hyundai, Genesis, and Kia electric vehicles (EVs) alongside a new battery manufacturing facility.
- HMGMA will create more than 8,100 jobs over the next few years.
- The company aims to establish a stable supply chain for EV batteries and other EV components in the U.S. market through this project.
- HMGMA’s new smart factory is expected to begin commercial production in the first half of 2025.
- Hyundai Motor Group Executive Chair Euisun Chung stated that Hyundai and Genesis EVs are recognized as best in class and expressed determination to become the global leader in electrification, safety, quality, and sustainability through this partnership.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp highlighted record jobs and investment coming to all parts of the state, citing award-winning workforce development programs and infrastructure as drivers of economic growth alongside the Hyundai partnership.
- Approximately $1 billion is anticipated to be invested by non-affiliated HMGMA suppliers in connection with the project in other locations.
- Hyundai Motor Group plans to sell 3.23 million full electric vehicles annually globally by 2030 as part of its accelerated electrification efforts.
- The group intends to establish a global EV production network to ensure a stable supply of EVs worldwide and maintain its position as one of the top three EV providers in the U.S.
- The investment aligns with the U.S. government’s roadmap to accelerate electrification, following a federal initiative requiring zero-emission vehicles to account for at least half of OEM new vehicle sales by 2030.
- The Georgia facility will utilize a highly connected, automated, and flexible manufacturing system that organically connects all elements of the EV ecosystem to realize customer value.
- All production processes, including order collection, procurement, logistics, and production, will be optimized using AI and data.
- The innovative manufacturing system will create a human-centered work environment where robots assist human workers.
- The plant will primarily rely on renewable energy sources to power operations and will use emission-reduction technologies to meet RE100 requirements.
- Hyundai Motor Group has made total donations of $1.54 million as part of its corporate social responsibility initiatives for the project.
- Hyundai and Genesis, along with various social responsibility initiatives and nonprofit organizations, donated $940,000 to local organizations.
- This includes programs through Hyundai Hope and Genesis Gives, which have previously collectively committed $120,000 to nearly ten organizations supporting local communities in and around Bryan County.
- Kia’s Accelerate the Good corporate social responsibility program will donate $600,000 to help local youth in Bryan County realize their hopes and dreams.
- Kia’s initiatives include purchasing homes for homeless families that recently lost their homes.
- Kia’s initiatives establish after-school programs where low-income children can eat and receive tutoring.
- Kia’s initiatives pay travel expenses for pediatric cancer care at St. Jude in Memphis, Tennessee.
- Kia’s initiatives support low-income children with technology and resources to facilitate learning.
