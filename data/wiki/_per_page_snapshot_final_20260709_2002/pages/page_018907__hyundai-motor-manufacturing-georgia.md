# Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Georgia

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-11-07

**Source:** [Hyundai’s EV manufacturing campus draws 3 more suppliers to Georgia | WardsAuto](https://www.automotivedive.com/news/hyundai-metaplant-draws-3-more-suppliers-hwashin-hyundai-industrial-daechang-seat-corp-usa-georgia/699021/)  ·  `page_018907`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group's Georgia supplier list expanded over the past few weeks with three additional auto part makers announcing investments to establish manufacturing facilities in the state.
- Hyundai Motor Group's subsidiary, Hyundai Industrial Co., invested over $24 million to acquire a building in Newnan.
- Hyundai Industrial Co.'s Newnan acquisition will create 100 jobs.
- Hyundai Industrial Co. plans to retrofit its Newnan facility to produce armrests and headrests for Hyundai’s upcoming EV metaplant in Ellabell as well as other OEMs.
- Hyundai’s upcoming EV metaplant in Ellabell has a projected cost of $5.5 billion.
- South Korea-based supplier Hwashin plans to establish a $176 million plant in Dublin.
- Hwashin's Dublin plant will create over 460 jobs.
- Tier-1 supplier Hwashin specializes in crafting chassis and body parts for Hyundai, Kia, and other OEMs.
- Production at Hwashin's Dublin facility is expected to begin in late 2025.
- Daechang Seat Corp. USA announced an investment of $72.5 million to build a manufacturing facility in Savannah.
- Daechang Seat Corp. USA's Savannah facility will create over 500 jobs.
- Daechang Seat Corp. USA specializes in automotive seat frames.
- Daechang Seat Corp. USA will join previously announced suppliers Seoyon E-HWA and PHA at the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center.
- The Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center is located near Hyundai's EV metaplant.
- Hyundai's EV metaplant in Ellabell is still under construction.
- The latest supplier investments bring the total to an estimated $2.5 billion from 14 suppliers in Georgia.
- The growing EV supply chain network is creating over 6,000 jobs, according to Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp in a Nov. 1 news release.
- In September, Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution invested an additional $2 billion toward their joint battery cell manufacturing plant.
- The total investment for the Hyundai-LG Energy Solution joint battery cell manufacturing plant is $4.3 billion.
- Production at the joint battery cell manufacturing plant is slated to begin at the end of 2025.
- The joint battery cell manufacturing plant will have a capacity to produce 300,000 EVs annually.
- Hyundai broke ground on its Georgia manufacturing campus over a year ago.
- Construction has recently begun on Hyundai's production-related buildings at the campus, with foundation work nearly complete as of last month.
- Hyundai's Georgia manufacturing campus is a nearly 3,000-acre megasite.
- The campus will use renewable resources to power its mass production.
- Using renewable resources at the campus will help Hyundai reach its goal of becoming carbon neutral by 2045.
