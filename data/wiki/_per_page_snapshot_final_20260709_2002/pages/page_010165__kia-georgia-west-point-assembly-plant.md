# Kia Georgia West Point Assembly Plant

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Kia Georgia Makes History with 2025 EV9 SUV Assembly | EV.com](https://ev.com/news/kia-georgia-makes-history-with-2025-ev9-suv-assembly)  ·  `page_010165`

## Facts

- Kia Georgia has started production with its West Point assembly plant in Georgia for its 2025 Kia EV9 electric vehicle (EV) SUV.
- This marked a historical milestone as the first EV assembled in the West Point assembly plant.
- Governor of Georgia Brian Kemp, James Watson (Kia Georgia’s Vice President of Production), and SeungKyu (Sean) Yoon (President and CEO for Kia North America and Kia America) attended the celebration.
- Governor Brian Kemp praised the milestone, stating he wants to congratulate the Kia Georgia team members and thank them for choosing the Peach State yet again as they work to become the e-mobility capital of the nation.
- Previously, the Kia Georgia’s West Point assembly plant only had the Telluride, Sorento, and Sportage models.
- The addition of the EV9 to Kia Georgia’s assembly roster came with an investment of over $200 million.
- This investment led to creating around 200 new jobs at the plant.
- The new EV9 production in Georgia was aimed to be eligible for the $7,500 EV tax credit, leveraging the incentive with local production for further appeal.
- The EV9 has won several accolades including 2024 World Car of the Year and 2024 World Electric Car at the New York International Auto Show, as well as ‘Best of the Best at the Red Dot Award: Product Design 2024.
- SeungKyu Yoon stated that having achieved prestigious accolades such as World Car of the Year, World EV of the Year, and North American Utility Vehicle of the Year, the EV9 will be in excellent hands with the team members at Kia Georgia.
- Following production start, the company anticipates increased success for the EV9 in the U.S. market.
- Recent reports track the EV9 to exceed the 50,000-unit milestone within its first year of launch due to high demand in global EV markets.
