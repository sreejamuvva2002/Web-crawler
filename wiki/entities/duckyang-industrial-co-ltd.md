# Duckyang Industrial Co. Ltd

**Type:** Company  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[automotive-components|Automotive Components]]  ·  **Location:** Ulsan, South Korea, Georgia, United States

## Overview

Korean electric mobility parts supplier Duckyang, which provides automotive battery modules and cockpit interior components to global automakers, announced a $10 million investment to open its first U.S. manufacturing facility in Braselton, Jackson County, Georgia, creating 285 jobs.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Duckyang supplies battery modules and interior parts for electric vehicles and is entering the U.S. EV battery market through its Georgia facility.

- **July 1977** — Duckyang Industrial was established, becoming the first company in Korea to develop and produce automobile cockpit modules.

## Key facts

- The new facility will create 285 high-paying, advanced automotive manufacturing jobs for Georgians.
- Duckyang supplies cockpit modules composed of more than 130 subcomponents located near the driver’s seat.
- The Braselton plant is 230,000 sq ft and will produce interior parts such as door trim panel modules in addition to battery modules.
- Governor Kemp emphasized Georgia’s top-ranked workforce training programs and business climate as reasons for Duckyang’s success.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson noted that Georgia’s international relationships helped secure over $1.3 billion in investment and 2,700 jobs from South Korean companies in 2020.
- Duckyang maintains a strategic alliance with Visteon, an American global automotive supplier.
- The company is continuously promoting research and development on new technologies to lead the global automotive market and plans to expand production capabilities for interior automotive parts and electronics.
