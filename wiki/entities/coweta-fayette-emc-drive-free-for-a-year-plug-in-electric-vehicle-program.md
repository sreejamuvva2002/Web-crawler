# Coweta-Fayette EMC Drive Free for A Year Plug-in Electric Vehicle Program

**Type:** Government Program  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[charging-infrastructure|Charging Infrastructure]]  ·  **Location:** Coweta County, Georgia, United States

## Overview

A utility incentive program that provides qualified electric-vehicle owners in Coweta County a monthly credit on their power bill to cover charging costs for one year.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Encourages EV adoption by offsetting charging expenses for residents of Coweta County, directly expanding the state’s charging-infrastructure ecosystem.

## Key facts

- Georgia EV sales grew dramatically, with 6,000 vehicles sold in 2018-a 147% increase from the previous year.
- Most charging stations are concentrated in urban areas like Atlanta; rural regions such as Coweta County have far fewer.
- Newnan (in Coweta County) has 16 EV charging stations, 13 of which offer free charging.
- "The lack of charging stations throughout many rural areas restricts the viability of electric vehicles..." - Center for Rural Affairs.
- Georgia’s state news release emphasized support from universities, a skilled workforce, and economic-development relationships to strengthen the EV market.
- International firms SK Innovations, TEKLAS, and GEDIA Automotive Group have partnered with the Georgia Department of Economic Development to invest in EV supply-chain production.
- Coweta-Fayette EMC’s Drive Free for A Year program provides a monthly power-bill credit for one year to qualifying plug-in EV owners, starting April 22 2019.
- "EV’s technologies have improved… longer driving ranges, extended warranties and can use charging points located along major roadways, places of employment, shopping and entertainment" - Coweta-Fayette EMC.
- General Motors aims to become an all-electric vehicle company by 2035 and plans to release two Chevrolet Bolt models under $34,000 this summer, followed later in the year by an electric Hummer priced at $113,000.
- Volkswagen intends to produce 28 million electric vehicles by 2028 and is investing heavily in its EV program.
- Yale School of Environment predicts that, with strong private-sector and federal commitment, the majority of new U.S. automobiles will be electric within two decades.
- A more extensive nationwide charging network and improved battery technology are needed for broader EV adoption - per Yale analysis.
- Coweta County is being considered by battery manufacturers; LG Batteries evaluated Coweta but may locate in Tennessee due to a GM incentive package.
- "We have good sites, workforce and skill set… when someone says they need 400 acres within driving distance of Atlanta and the airport, we’re really the only game in town right now" - Trae Westmoreland.
