# Enchem America, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2025-10-02

**Source:** [EV battery component company backs out of plans for $152M Haywood County facility](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/ev-battery-component-company-backs-194720075.html)  ·  `page_007966`

## Facts

- Enchem America, Inc., a South Korean battery component maker, planned to build an electrolyte manufacturing facility at the I-40 Advantage Industrial Park in Brownsville, Tenn.
- The proposed facility was valued at $152.5 million.
- The project would have brought 190 jobs to Haywood County.
- Enchem America announced its plan to build on the 'shovel ready' industrial park in 2023.
- The site is located roughly 16 miles from BlueOval City, a massive electric vehicle manufacturing campus.
- BlueOval City is slated to produce Ford Motor Company's new all-electric pickup truck and electric vehicle batteries in partnership with SK On.
- The BlueOval City campus is expected to serve as manufacturing space for BlueOval SK, Ford, and electric vehicle component suppliers.
- The campus is intended to inject jobs and residents into the surrounding rural West Tennessee communities.
- The Enchem facility would have produced electrolytes used for electric vehicle batteries.
- Production at the Enchem facility was expected to magnify the company's ability to supply and support its electric vehicle partners in the United States.
- Enchem America decided to withdraw from the project in mid-September 2025 after careful consideration and an extensive review of its North American business strategy.
- The withdrawal decision was primarily driven by a recent shift in demand from the company's key North American customers.
- Brownsville-Haywood County Economic Development Corporation Executive Director Aaron Stewart informed the corporation's board members and West Tennessee mayors of Enchem America's withdrawal via email on Sept. 26.
- Enchem America did not respond to a request for comment regarding the withdrawal.
- The project cancellation occurs amid continuing shifts in the electric vehicle market.
- Ford Motor Company announced it will delay mass production of its next-generation pickup truck at BlueOval City until 2028.
- Ford still plans to start producing prototypes of the truck at BlueOval City in 2027.
- The Brownsville-Haywood County Economic Development Corporation is working to terminate the state incentives Enchem America was set to receive for the project.
- Enchem America received a $2 million recruitment grant in 2023, according to a state database.
- The Brownsville-Haywood County Economic Development Corporation will work with the state and the Tennessee Valley Authority to put the site back on the market.
- The Tennessee Valley Authority has contributed grant funds to prepare the industrial park for future development.
- Enchem America completed preliminary site grading but had not yet begun construction.
- Enchem America is not abandoning a facility, as all site preparation was completed as part of the state's Select Tennessee Certified Sites program.
- The site always generated interest due to its proximity to Memphis and Jackson.
- With BlueOval City nearby and production beginning in 2027 alongside mass production in 2028, the Brownsville-Haywood County Economic Development Corporation suspects a new tenant will draw interest similar to before.
- More details about the site's future will be discussed at the Brownsville-Haywood Economic Development Corporation board meeting on Oct. 8.
- The Brownsville-Haywood County Economic Development Corporation executive expressed no concern regarding uncertainty in the evolving electric vehicle market.
- Navigating Washington policies, international supply chains for rare earth metals and aluminum production, tariffs, and trade agreements makes it difficult for automakers to chart a course for the next 12 months, let alone five to 10 years.
- The BlueOval City campus features a $5.6 billion investment.
- The BlueOval City campus contains nearly 11 million square feet under a roof, with machinery and equipment still being installed and calibrated at a slower pace.
