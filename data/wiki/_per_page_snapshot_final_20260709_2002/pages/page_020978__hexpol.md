# Hexpol

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- Hexpol has appointed board member Henrik Elmin as its next CEO, effective August 1.
- Henrik Elmin will drive the company's 2030 strategy.
- Elmin previously served as Business Area President for Industrial Technique at Atlas Copco, a large-cap industrial machinery company with global scale.
- Elmin holds an engineering degree from Lund and an MBA from INSEAD.
- Hexpol manufactures compounded polymers including rubber, thermoplastic elastomers, and engineering plastics.
- Hexpol's products are used in car tires, wheel rims, seals, and gaskets.
- Hexpol operates in two business segments: Hexpol Compounding and Hexpol Engineered Products.
- Revenue in the compounding segment fell 6% last year to roughly SEK 17.8 billion.
- Total trailing revenue is down about 9%.
- First quarter 2026 operating profit missed analyst estimates at SEK 701 million versus SEK 719 million expected, and well below the SEK 839 million a year earlier.
- Hexpol's business performance is tied to auto OEM cycles and raw material pricing.
- When carmakers slow production or demand cheaper suppliers, Hexpol feels the impact.
- Hexpol's feedstock costs swing with oil price fluctuations.
- EBIT margins have been falling from the mid-teens toward double digits due to industry-wide margin compression.
- Elmin replaces Klas Dahlberg as CEO, who started in July 2024 and left after less than 20 months without a publicly explained reason.
- Dahlberg previously worked at NIBE Climate Solutions, a Swedish HVAC and heat pump company.
- Hexpol trades at a trailing P/E of about 14, with a market cap around SEK 26 billion.
- An analyst recently trimmed Hexpol's stock price target from SEK 85 to SEK 84 while maintaining a neutral rating.
- Hexpol's 2030 strategy points toward recycled materials and circular economy products to move away from virgin fossil-based feedstock.
- Recycled-content polymers face thin initial margins and heavy certification requirements despite being a real growth trend.
- The board appointed Elmin to provide continuity rather than revolution.
