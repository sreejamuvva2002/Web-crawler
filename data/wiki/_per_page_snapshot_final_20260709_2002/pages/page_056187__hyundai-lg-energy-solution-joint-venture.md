# Hyundai-LG Energy Solution Joint Venture

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hyundai, LG Energy Solution to Open US Battery Plant - Tech Insights](https://eepower.com/market-insights/hyundai-lg-energy-solution-to-open-us-battery-plant/)  ·  `page_056187`

## Facts

- A joint venture between Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution will result in a new lithium-ion battery plant in Georgia to supply EVs in North America.
- Korea-based Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution will build a $4.3 billion facility in Bryan County, near Savannah, Georgia.
- The new facility is located near Hyundai Motor Group's Metaplant America site, which is currently under construction.
- Battery production at the joint venture plant is expected to begin at the end of 2025.
- The facility will have an annual production capacity of 30 gigawatt-hours (GWh).
- The plant's output will support the production of 300,000 electric vehicles annually.
- Korea-based LG Energy Solution is the second largest supplier of lithium-ion batteries to the auto industry.
- LG Energy Solution has partnerships and joint ventures with customers including Ford, General Motors (GM), and Stellantis to supply EV battery needs.
- LG Energy Solution is building three battery plants in partnership with GM: one in Warren, Ohio (already opened), one in Spring Hill, Tennessee (opening in 2024), and a third in Lansing, Michigan (scheduled to open in early 2025).
- Including the new Hyundai joint venture, LG Energy Solution has seven battery plants currently operating or under construction in the U.S.
- In the Georgia joint venture, Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution will each hold a 50 percent stake in the cell production facility.
- Hyundai will assemble the lithium-ion cells from the new plant into battery packs and supply them to its manufacturing facilities for Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis EV models.
- LG Energy Solution currently supplies EV batteries for Hyundai Group models, including the Elantra Hybrid, Kona Electric, and IONIQ 6.
- Hyundai is building a new $5.5 billion EV manufacturing plant in Georgia to produce new Hyundai and Genesis models.
- Following the 2022 Inflation Reduction Act, plans for more than 20 new EV battery production facilities have been announced.
- The Inflation Reduction Act ties EV purchase incentives to a manufacturer's North American production of vehicles and battery packs.
- To be eligible for a $7,500 federal tax incentive, EVs must be manufactured in the U.S., Canada, or Mexico.
- To qualify for the incentive, SUV EVs must be priced under $80,000, while sedans, coupes, hatchbacks, and wagons must be under $55,000.
- In 2020, the total U.S. market share for battery EVs was under 2 percent.
- By 2022, the U.S. battery EV market share rose to around 6 percent.
- U.S. battery electric vehicle sales are projected to reach 25 to 30 percent by 2030, and 45 to 50 percent by 2035.
- Companies like GM have announced plans to phase out gasoline models by 2035.
- The transition to electric transportation presents continuing challenges regarding domestic sourcing of raw materials for battery cells.
- Automakers including Ford, GM, and Tesla are investing in domestic lithium sources through established mining companies and new startups exploring novel extraction methods.
