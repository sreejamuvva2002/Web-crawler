# Astemo, Ltd.

## Facts

- Operates worldwide as a global mega-supplier of automotive parts with approximately 80,000 employees across the United States, Asia, China, Europe, and Japan.
- Develops, manufactures, sells, and services automotive parts, transportation and industrial machinery, equipment, and systems through three business segments: Electrification Business, Vehicle Business, and Motorcycle Business.
- Fiscal 2024 revenues were approximately 2,200 billion yen.
- Fiscal 2024 operating income was approximately 67.0 billion yen.
- As of March 2025, has 98 group companies.
- As of March 2025, operates 134 bases in 26 countries and regions (45 in Japan, 89 overseas).
- Consolidated number of employees is approximately 80,000 as of March 2025.
- Has approximately 70 customers who are automobile and motorcycle manufacturing companies worldwide.
- Holds the No. 1 company share globally in the motorcycle product market for injectors, throttle bodies, suspensions, and brakes according to 2024 Astemo research.
- Ranked 19th (5th in Japan) among the top 30 automotive parts suppliers by revenue in FY2024 according to MarkLines.
- Holds approximately 16,800 patents as of March 2025.
- Research and development expenses are approximately 162.0 billion yen as of March 2025.
- Provides advanced mobility solutions in three business areas: electrification, vehicle, and motorcycle businesses.
- Mission is to contribute to a sustainable society and improved quality of life by providing world-leading advanced mobility solutions.
- Develops advanced technologies focused on Environment, Safety, and Digitalization.
- Environment technology includes rare-earth-free motors that combine rare-earth-free magnets and synchronous reluctance motors to support outputs from main drives to auxiliary drives.
- Environment technology includes in-wheel motors as a next-generation mobility solution that contributes to reduced environmental impact and improved ride comfort.
- Environment technology includes e-Axles for small motorcycles that integrate the motor, inverter, gearbox, and cooling fan for a compact, lightweight, and cost-efficient design.
- Safety technology includes 360-degree 3D sensing technology that detects fallen objects and road surface abnormalities around the vehicle.
- Safety technology includes NOA (Navigation on Autopilot) combined with 360-degree 3D sensing technology to reduce driver burden and realize comfortable autonomous travel.
- Safety technology includes electric brakes, a next-generation brake system that enhances safety through high-precision brake control.
- Safety technology includes the Steer-by-wire system (SBWS®), a linkless steering system providing safety and new value with an extensive product lineup.
- Safety technology includes ADAS for motorcycles that uses road surface detection through a stereo camera to enable improved stability control.
- Digitalization technology includes an IoV platform that links on-board and back-end aspects to enable quick circulation from data collection to software updates and service development.
- Digitalization technology includes value creation DX, contributing to the continuous evolution of vehicles through generative AI and digital environments.
- Digitalization technology includes dynamics integrated control platform technology to optimize vehicle motion according to driver preferences for a more comfortable driving feel.
