# Northeast Georgia Inland Port

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [GA: New Inland Terminal Location to Serve Fast-Growing Gainesville Region | Trade and Industry Development](https://www.tradeandindustrydev.com/region/georgia/news/ga-new-inland-terminal-location-serve-fast-growing-14760)  ·  `page_016283`

## Facts

- Governor Nathan Deal, along with representatives of the Georgia Ports Authority, the Greater Hall Chamber and Norfolk Southern Railroad announced plans to make Hall County the site of the GPA's next inland port.
- The Northeast Georgia Inland Port will be situated in the heart of the manufacturing and logistics corridor along Interstate 85.
- The inland port will act as an economic development tool, drawing new investment from business and industry to Hall and its surrounding counties.
- Norfolk Southern will provide service on a direct rail route for import and export containers between the Gainesville terminal and the Port of Savannah's Garden City Terminal.
- Norfolk Southern Vice President Intermodal & Automotive Jeff Heller stated that Hall County and the surrounding Northeast Georgia region are key areas of expansion for global manufacturers and distributors.
- The combination of the GPA inland port at Gainesville and Norfolk Southern's rail service will provide crucial links in supply chains and serve as a catalyst for industrial development.
- Port officials state that improved access to rail will increase logistics options and overall efficiency while reducing congestion on Georgia highways.
- Presently, containers moving by truck travel a 600-mile roundtrip to and from the Port of Savannah.
- When the new rail yard opens, drivers will be able to make shorter trips from area manufacturers and distribution facilities to the Northeast Georgia site.
- GPA Executive Director Griff Lynch stated that the Gainesville location is part of the "Network Georgia" initiative, which provides additional logistics options for port customers.
- The new rail hub will allow importers and exporters to move loaded containers to the coast with greater efficiency and provide a ready source of empty containers for Georgia exports.
- GPA Board Chairman Jimmy Allgood said the new choice in cargo handling will transform the local rail market and help port customers optimize their supply chains.
- Jimmy Allgood noted that inland terminals are bringing ports and producers closer together, providing increased employment options as companies expand or locate in Georgia.
- Georgia Ports currently supports more than 440,000 jobs across every corner of the state.
- The new 104-acre terminal will be located in the Gateway Industrial Centre on GA 365.
- The facility's services will draw from a 1.5-million population in Hall, Gwinnett and surrounding counties.
- Greater Hall Chamber of Commerce Vice President of Economic Development Tim Evans stated that the new inland port terminal will shorten the supply chain for regional manufacturers, processors and distributors.
- Rail deliveries to and from the Port of Savannah can shorten truck delivery times from approximately seven hours to less than 30 minutes.
- Direct access to the Port of Savannah, the fastest growing container port in the U.S., provides existing businesses a competitive advantage through logistics infrastructure innovation.
- Auto Metal Direct, a worldwide distributor of auto body panels and trim for classic cars and trucks, announced its intent to open a new 318,000 square-foot distribution and fulfillment center.
- Construction will soon begin on the new Auto Metal Direct facility in the Gateway Industrial Centre.
- Upon completion, the $15 million development will bring 40 jobs to Hall County.
- Auto Metal Direct President Mark Headrick stated that hundreds of containers each year are received by AMD, making GPA services essential for business maintenance and growth.
- The proximity of the new inland port was an influencing factor in Auto Metal Direct's eventual location decision, with anticipated benefits including quicker service, lower cost and ease of movement.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that GPA has positioned Georgia as a global leader in logistics infrastructure and been critical for locating new projects and industry expansions.
- The expansion of Auto Metal Direct will further bolster the strength of the workforce in the community, according to Commissioner Wilson.
- The Northeast Georgia Inland Port is the second Network Georgia-related announcement this year.
- GPA held a grand opening for the Appalachian Regional Port Aug. 22 just north of Chatsworth, Ga., on a 42-acre site in Murray County.
- The Hall County inland terminal is scheduled to be complete in 2021.
- At full build-out, the terminal will have the capacity to handle up to 150,000 containers per year.
- Kubota Manufacturing of America Corporation Vice President Administration Phil Sutton stated that GPA has helped reduce lead times for in- and outbound shipments and that the new inland port offers cost savings on chassis/container fees and FTZ administrative fees.
- Kubota anticipates reduced reliance on OTR motor carriers and reduced lead times for inbound parts and outbound finished goods due to greater availability of empty containers and shorter transit times.
- Georgia Poultry Federation President Mike Giles noted that the Port of Savannah currently ranks as the number one export facility for poultry products in the nation.
- The proximity of Georgia poultry producers to the port provides a competitive advantage for delivering chicken products to overseas customers efficiently and cost-effectively.
- GPA's investment in infrastructure continues with the new inland rail yard to improve future connectivity between the Georgia poultry industry and worldwide customers.
- Tatsumi Intermodal USA Business Development Manager Arrik Williams stated that the terminal's proximity to their facility enhances operations, allows for better rate negotiation, adds transloading services, and reduces interstate tractor trailer traffic.
- ZF Gainesville LLC Director of Operations Brandon Loftus stated that continued supply chain development is vital for future growth plans and that port infrastructure improvements will help streamline logistics for assembly and manufacturing processes.
- FOX Factory CEO Larry L. Enterline stated that GPA's plans enhance service for Hall County companies and reflect a favorable business climate that factored into their decision to expand their Powered Vehicles Group operations in Georgia.
- Mugele Group Vice President Benjamin Sippl stated that having a rail facility that can carry material from the coast will make it easier for manufacturers to get materials delivered to assembly lines on time and at a more economical cost due to trucker availability challenges.
- Rail access will allow Mugele to offer companies a consolidation warehouse nearby a port for just-in-time (JIT) delivery, reducing transportation risks and offering flexibility with in-house storage capacity.
