# State of Georgia

## Facts

- Georgia's state gross domestic product (GDP) is over $710 billion in 2025.
- The state maintains a flat corporate income tax rate of 5.19%, which is lower than those of California and New York.
- Georgia consistently ranks as one of the top US states for doing business due to its probusiness policies, such as low corporate taxes and less restrictive regulations.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) serves as the state’s main agency for supporting businesses, providing export assistance, information on state tax credits and incentives, and industry-specific support.
- Georgia Quick Start is a free workforce training program that provides customized training for new or expanding businesses.
- Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport is the busiest airport in the world in 2026.
- Georgia possesses major interstate highways and a well-connected rail system.
- The state has deepwater ports including the Port of Savannah and the Port of Brunswick.
- The combination of its ports, airport, and highway network positions Georgia as a shipping and warehousing hub for freight companies and third-party logistics providers.
- The state offers job tax credits for creating jobs in specific industries or regions.
- Georgia provides research and development (R&D) tax credits for businesses that invest in R&D.
- A Port Tax Credit Bonus is available for companies that increase their use of Georgia’s ports.
- Invest Georgia operates as a state venture capital fund supporting early-stage businesses.
- The state sales and use tax rate in Georgia is 4%.
- Georgia has a large, diverse talent pool supported by strong education systems and workforce training programs.
- The state is home to 2.1 million STEM professionals.
- The Georgia Procurement Registry is an online government tool that helps businesses find state contracting opportunities.
