## Facts

- Nader Hammoud serves as a Senior Application Engineer for Bosch's Two-Wheeler & Powersports team.
- His role focuses on developing active safety systems and applying a 'calibrator mindset' to balance system limits for comfort and control.
- Hammoud studied mechanical engineering at the American University of Beirut and completed graduate mechanical engineering studies at Georgia Tech.
- Bosch associates on his team are based in Michigan and collaborate daily with teams in India, Japan, China, and other distant time zones.
- The team modifies power steering systems originally developed for cars so they can be adapted for off-road vehicles such as Utility Task Vehicles (UTVs).
- Team members undergo regular training at Rawhyde Adventures in California under the guidance of Jim Hyde, a well-known figure in two-wheeled off-road motorsports.
- Bosch offers a two-year junior management program that Hammoud completed before joining the Two-Wheeler team.
- The team tests safety systems on surfaces covered in basalt tiles that mimic oily or icy roads when sprayed with water to evaluate spin recovery and braking performance.
