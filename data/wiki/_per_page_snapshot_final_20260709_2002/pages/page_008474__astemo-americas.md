# Astemo Americas

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2025-06-10

**Source:** [Astemo Americas Drives Automotive Future - The BRAKE Report](https://thebrakereport.com/astemo-americas-drives-automotive-future/)  ·  `page_008474`

## Facts

- Astemo Americas is undergoing significant changes, introducing new mobility technologies, expanding its North American footprint, and implementing brand updates.
- The company is part of the global Astemo Group, a major automotive supplier.
- Astemo is accelerating its investments in advanced mobility to solidify its leadership in an industry increasingly defined by software.
- The company is introducing next-generation technologies in autonomous driving, electrification, chassis, suspension, steering, and braking to its North American customers.
- Astemo plans to expand its headquarters in Farmington Hills, Michigan, to support its growth.
- Key innovations include an AI-based multi-camera system for AD/ADAS, an AI-powered full suspension system, and new “by-wire” steering and braking systems.
- The company was formerly known as Hitachi Astemo Americas and has officially been renamed Astemo Americas to align with its global parent company.
- Astemo is strategically positioning itself as a leader in electrification and mobility intelligence.
- The company is increasing its investment in key areas such as electrification, advanced driver-assistance systems (ADAS), autonomous driving, and advanced chassis technology.
- This summer, Astemo will showcase a wide range of new mobility solutions to its major OEM customers across the Americas through a series of presentations, displays, and vehicle test drives.
- The company has announced plans to move its Farmington Hills, Michigan, technical center and headquarters to a new, larger location in the near future.
- Astemo is previewing several cutting-edge systems for automakers that are designed for future vehicle models.
- The company has developed automated driving solutions that use a multi-camera system with proprietary AI-based stereo vision.
- This technology generates a 360-degree point cloud that is denser than conventional LiDAR and includes color and brightness information.
- A new full suspension system utilizes AI-powered control logic and deep-learning algorithms to improve ride and handling at a lower cost than comparable systems.
- The suspension system is equipped with a “preview” camera that can anticipate and prepare for bumps and potholes in the road.
- Astemo’s latest steer-by-wire technology replaces the traditional steering wheel with a handheld device, representing a key step toward autonomous driving.
- The company is making its North American debut with the “Smart Brake,” an electro-mechanical brake-by-wire system that reduces the lag time between driver input and brake response.
- By eliminating the traditional hydraulic connection, the Smart Brake simplifies vehicle assembly, helps extend battery range, and reduces maintenance by removing the need for brake fluid.
- The name Astemo is derived from “Advanced Sustainable Technologies for Mobility,” highlighting the company’s commitment to developing cutting-edge and sustainable solutions.
- The company's new branding message is “Astemo, Mobility Beyond – From the Core of Mobility, Freedom.”
- Astemo operates as a joint venture between Hitachi, Ltd., Honda Motor Co., and JIC Capital, Ltd.
- The company employs 80,000 people worldwide.
- Over 14,000 of Astemo's employees are located in the Americas.
