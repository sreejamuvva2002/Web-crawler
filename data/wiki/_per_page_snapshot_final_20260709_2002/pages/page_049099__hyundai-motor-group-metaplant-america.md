# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-03-26

**Source:** [Communities prepare for change as Hyundai plant celebrates official opening](https://www.savannahnow.com/story/business/employment/2025/03/26/communities-prepare-for-change-as-hyundai-plant-celebrates-official-opening/82658878007/)  ·  `page_049099`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America is Georgia’s largest economic project-to-date.
- HMGMA is on course to become the single most transformative influence on the Savannah region’s economy, workforce, public resources and way of life for the next half century.
- The plant’s physical address is in what has traditionally been rural Bryan County.
- The seismic impact of its $7.59 billion investment is rippling out across Chatham, Effingham and Bulloch counties and spreading across the state.
- Per the Hyundai agreement, the company is committed to an average annual salary of $58,105.
- In 2023, Hyundai Motor Company announced it is committed to raise salaries by 25% in a few years.
- At 8,500 employees, that would be pushing about a half billion dollars' worth of payroll through the economy, according to Michael Toma, Fuller E. Callaway Professor of Economics at Georgia Southern University.
- The deal that Hyundai signed to earn a 27-year property tax abatement involves capital commitments and the creation of around 8,500 direct jobs.
- Indirect positions supported by LG’s joint venture EV battery facility and the 18 Hyundai suppliers that have committed to moving to the area are expected to reach nearly 38,000 throughout the state over the next 10 years.
- HMGMA moved from announcement to actual production in less than two years.
- HMGMA sits on nearly 3,000 acres that before development was mostly planted pines.
- The plant is roughly the size of Tybee Island.
- It is expected to pay leasehold and ad valorem property tax payments starting in 2026 once the initial construction is complete.
- Bryan, Bulloch, Chatham, Effingham and Liberty counties are each within a 60-mile radius of the plant.
- The north side of Bryan County will become the epicenter for the most transformative change.
- Michael Toma said they are developing an electric vehicle industry from the ground up, including the full supply chain, from nothing.
- Paige Clayton, assistant professor for the School of City & Regional Planning at the Georgia Institute of Technology, noted challenges with basic infrastructure capacity and housing constraints due to attracting people from other areas.
- Clayton said the look and feel of the city will change to an extent but it won't become a very urban area in the near term.
- The city of Pembroke is just eight miles from HMGMA and may see more commercial growth such as nail salons, doctor’s offices and places to eat.
- Pembroke has approved rezoning for the addition of 3,000 homes.
- Heartwood community on the south end of the county expects to see 10,000 homes at full build out.
- Road improvements to support travel to and from and around the metaplant are already underway, including five roundabouts and a new interchange at I-16, totaling more than $180 million.
- The frontage road that runs in between I-16 and HMGMA is complete.
- Georgia Department of Transportation (GDOT) is ramping up road improvements for one of its biggest projects in the state.
- Construction of five roundabouts is underway along a stretch of U.S. 280 between the Interstate Centre entrance to the north and the Hyundai plant entrance to the south.
- A new interchange at the intersection of I-16 and Old Cuyler Road is scheduled to begin in quarter three of this year and will consist of three multi-lane roundabouts.
- The road improvement projects amount to $187.3 million, according to GDOT.
- Bryan County Schools is investing more than $200 million into new schools on the north and south end of the county.
- The new Richmond Hill High School is under construction and slated to open this fall, costing $97 million.
- An additional $35 million will be spent on athletic facilities at Richmond Hill High School.
- Bryan County High School will cost $90 million.
- In 2024, The Savannah Economic Development Authority (SEDA) created the Reginal Industry Support Enterprise (RISE) to address manufacturing hiring needs brought on by the industry's boom and its need for a robust manufacturing workforce.
- Georgia's Quick Start program, a division of the Technical College System of Georgia paid for by Georgia taxpayers, currently partners with HMGMA to provide training for all Hyundai employees.
- Training happens at two separate Quick Start facilities: Port City Logistics in Port Wentworth and Quick Start Advanced Manufacturing in Pooler.
- The Quick Start Advanced Manufacturing facility is slated for a $10.6 million state-funded expansion.
- Construction is also well underway at a HMGMA-dedicated Quick Start Hyundai Mobility Training Center, a $62.5 million state-funded facility.
- Bryan County taxpayers could likely soon foot the bill for the construction of a new jail, which the county estimates could cost $75 to $100 million.
- Sheriff Mark Crowe told the Savannah Morning News that the incoming growth is concerning and they have to keep up with that growth rate by adding more deputies every year, getting more vehicles and making sure deputies are trained accordingly.
- Water supply and quality has remained a contentious issue during the metaplant's development with threatened lawsuits, permit challenges, and election upsets at the local level.
- The metaplant will require up to 4 million gallons of water a day once it reaches full production.
- An interlocal agreement between Bryan County and neighboring Bulloch County allowed for the drilling of four wells in Bulloch's farming community to pump up to 6.65 million gallons of pristine drinking and agricultural water daily from the Floridan Aquifer.
- The Floridan Aquifer is an extensive reservoir covering 100,000 square miles beneath all of Florida and parts of Georgia, Alabama and South Carolina.
- The remaining 2.65 million gallons is being pumped to supply the projected growth and development in North Bryan County.
- The state created a pool funded in equal parts by Bulloch and Bryan counties and the Hyundai metaplant to address any potential significant impacts to private wells within a 5-mile radius of the new withdrawal sites.
- Recently, the state approved $502 million for a comprehensive plan to address water infrastructure needs across the Coastal Empire, which involves creating an alternative surface water supply for Hyundai to replace the Bulloch County wells.
- The plant is expected to hire 8,500 workers by 2030.
- Savannah Morning News education and workforce development reporter Joseph Schwartzburt produced a four-part series, Behind the Wheel, that provides a detailed analysis of the jobs situation, from the promises made and the public and private dollars invested to make sure those promises are kept.
