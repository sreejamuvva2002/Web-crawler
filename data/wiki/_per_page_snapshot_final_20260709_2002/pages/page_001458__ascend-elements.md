# Ascend Elements

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2023-03-30

**Source:** [Ascend Elements opens Georgia battery recycling facility - Recycling Today](https://www.recyclingtoday.com/news/ascend-elements-opens-georgia-battery-recycling-facility/)  ·  `page_001458`

## Facts

- Lithium-ion battery recycler Ascend Elements, Westborough, Massachusetts, recently celebrated the grand opening of its first commercial-scale recycling facility in Covington, Georgia.
- The company says the $50 million Base 1 facility is the largest of its type in North America.
- The 150,000-square-foot facility began partial operations in August 2022.
- The facility has an annual capacity to process 30,000 metric tons of used lithium-ion batteries and manufacturing scrap, equal to 70,000 electric vehicle batteries per year.
- Ascend Elements CEO Mike O’Kronley states that the facility is an important part of America’s EV battery infrastructure and 'just the start.'
- CEO Mike O’Kronley emphasizes the industry's need to continue building a closed-loop battery materials supply chain to make electric vehicle batteries cleaner and more sustainable.
- Ascend Elements recovers 98 percent of the critical battery metals in used electric vehicle batteries and gigafactory manufacturing scrap.
- The company uses its patented Hydro-to-Cathode process to recycle used lithium-ion batteries and scrap.
- This process can reduce the carbon footprint of new electric vehicle battery cathode materials by up to 90 percent.
- The Hydro-to-Cathode direct precursor synthesis process produces sustainably engineered cathode precursor (pCAM) and cathode active materials (CAM).
- The new facility is powered by renewable energy.
- The facility features on-site wastewater recycling and treatment systems.
- It currently employs about 100 team members.
- The company plans to increase its headcount to 185 by 2024.
