# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-05

**Source:** [Gov’t says 475 caught in immigration raid at GA Hyundai electric vehicle plant were here illegally – WSB-TV Channel 2 - Atlanta](https://www.wsbtv.com/news/local/us-immigration-officers-raid-georgia-site-where-hyundai-makes-electric-vehicles/MREDNL2DRNGNDPAG3S5JDN5QHM/)  ·  `page_009939`

## Facts

- A major immigration raid at the Hyundai Electric Vehicle Plant near Pooler led to the arrest of more than 450 individuals, primarily South Korean nationals.
- Hyundai says those who were arrested weren’t directly employed by the South Korean car company.
- The operation, conducted by Immigration and Customs Enforcement and Homeland Security, involved around 400 agents.
- The government says the raid was prompted by allegations of unlawful employment of immigrants here illegally.
- The majority of those arrested are currently detained at an ICE facility in Folkston, Georgia.
- The White House has expressed support for the raid, emphasizing the importance of legal entry for foreign workers involved in U.S. projects.
- Democratic critics have labeled the raid as politically motivated.
- South Korea is responding to the incident by sending diplomats from its embassy in Washington and its consulate to Georgia.
- Hyundai Motor Company released an updated statement Friday night, saying the company believes none of the people arrested works directly for the company.
- Hyundai stated it is committed to full compliance with all laws and regulations in every market where it operates, including employment verification requirements and immigration laws, and expects the same commitment from all partners, suppliers, contractors, and subcontractors.
- At a news conference in Savannah, Homeland Security officials clarified that this was not a routine ICE raid but was specifically targeted due to federal law violations.
- U.S. Attorney Margaret E. Heap stated the goal of the operation is to reduce illegal employment and prevent employers from gaining an unfair advantage by hiring unauthorized workers.
- U.S. Attorney Margaret E. Heap also stated another goal is to protect unauthorized workers from exploitation.
- Steven Schrank, Special Agent in Charge, Homeland Security Investigations, said at a news briefing Friday that the majority of the people detained were from South Korea.
- Steven Schrank stated the operation underscores our commitment to jobs for Georgians and Americans.
- Steven Schrank noted that this was in fact the largest single site enforcement operation in the history of Homeland Security Investigations.
- A spokesperson for Gov. Brian Kemp said Friday that the Department of Public Safety coordinated with federal agencies to provide support for the operation.
- The spokesperson stated that Georgia will always enforce the law, including all state and federal immigration laws, and that all companies operating within the State must follow the laws of Georgia and our nation.
- The investigation has been ongoing for several months, with authorities receiving leads from community members and former workers.
- The operation targeted one of Georgia’s largest and most high-profile manufacturing sites, touted by the governor and other officials as the largest economic development project in the state’s history.
- Hyundai Motor Group began manufacturing EVs a year ago at the plant.
- The plant represents a $7.6 billion investment.
- The plant employs about 1,200 people.
- ATF Atlanta joined HSI, FBI, DEA, ICE, GSP and other agencies in a major immigration enforcement operation at the Hyundai mega site battery plant in Bryan County, GA.
