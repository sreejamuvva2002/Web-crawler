# Woory Industrial Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Woory To Invest $18M In Georgia EV Parts Facility - Advanced Manufacturing - Business Facilities Magazine](https://businessfacilities.com/woory-to-invest-18m-in-georgia-ev-parts-facility/)  ·  `page_015406`

## Facts

- South Korea-based automotive parts manufacturer Woory Industrial Co., Ltd. will invest an estimated $18 million to establish a new manufacturing facility in Dublin, Georgia.
- The facility will create more than 130 new jobs in Laurens County.
- The new facility will be Woory’s first U.S. manufacturing location.
- The facility will produce electrical heaters, control units, and actuators for electric vehicle (EV) and internal combustion carmakers in North America.
- Operations at the new facility are expected to begin in November 2023.
- Jungwoo Kim, CEO of Woory Industrial Company, stated that Georgia will be a good opportunity for Woory to take another step forward as part of the vanguard of EV development.
- Jungwoo Kim expressed confidence that Woory’s experience and technology will contribute to the prosperity of the community.
- Jungwoo Kim noted that the automobile industry is facing a revolution for the first time in 100 years, with the transition to electric vehicles being the future.
- Jungwoo Kim identified the U.S. EV market as the key market among all automotive markets.
- Over the past 10 years, Woory has successfully grown into a specialized EV component company through continuous innovation.
- Jungwoo Kim expressed sincere appreciation for the partnership of the State of Georgia and the City of Dublin.
- Unlike internal combustion engine vehicles, EVs do not have a heating source, making heaters required for cabin heating and maintaining battery temperature.
- Woory’s pioneering development of electric heaters over the last 10 years has led the company to become a leader in this growing market segment.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated that the emerging industry continues to bring historic levels of new jobs and investment to communities all over the state, thanks to a highly-skilled workforce and reliable infrastructure network.
- Governor Kemp said he is proud to welcome Woory Industrial as the latest job creator to help Georgia on the path to becoming the e-mobility capital of the nation.
- Governor Kemp expressed confidence that Woory will have a long and beneficial partnership with Dublin-Laurens County.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team worked with the Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start to land the project.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that projects like Woory are exactly why they have made it a priority to be on the cutting-edge of this transformational opportunity in manufacturing by creating jobs and opportunities in rural communities.
- Pat Wilson noted that Georgia has invested for decades to match competitive projects with communities and sites that are prepared for development.
- Pat Wilson congratulated Dublin-Laurens County on bringing e-mobility jobs of the future to middle Georgia.
- Since 2020, Georgia has pursued job creation opportunities along the entire EV supply chain, resulting in over $22.7 billion in investment and the creation of more than 28,400 jobs.
- Hanon Systems is expanding into Bulloch County, Georgia, bringing 160 new jobs and $40 million in capital investment.
- Dublin Mayor Joshua Kight commented that fostering quality and sustainable growth is a goal of the City of Dublin.
- Mayor Kight noted that Woory’s commitment to place an existing manufacturing building back into service helps promote more infill development while creating new career paths for the community.
- Dr. Fred Williams, Chair of the Board of Directors of the Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority, said Dublin and Laurens County has a rapidly growing automotive sector thanks to its geographic location on Interstate 16 and access to a skilled and trained workforce.
- Dr. Williams stated that Woory’s partnership with area education systems and training providers will ensure training programs are in place to help fill Woory’s talent pipeline for years to come.
