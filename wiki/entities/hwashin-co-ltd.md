# Hwashin Co., Ltd.

**Type:** Company  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[automotive-components|Automotive Components]]  ·  **Location:** Dublin, Georgia, Laurens County, Georgia, United States

## Overview

Hwashin, a Korean Tier-1 automotive parts supplier specializing in chassis and body components for Hyundai Motor Group, Kia and other OEMs, will build a new manufacturing facility in Dublin, Georgia creating over 460 jobs with more than $176 million in investment.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Supplier of chassis components for electric-vehicle manufacturers; part of Georgia’s e-mobility ecosystem.

## Timeline

## Key facts

- Hwashin is a Tier-1 supplier specializing in chassis and body parts for Hyundai Motor Group, Kia, and other international OEMs.
- The new plant will be located at the Georgia Highway 257 Rail Served Site, the first GRAD Certified Select site in the state.
- The project will create over 460 jobs and bring more than $176 million of investment to Laurens County.
- Governor Kemp highlighted Georgia’s goal to become the e-mobility capital of the nation.
- Hwashin aims to shape a future global city focused on electric vehicles, leveraging its 50-year expertise.
- The facility will have direct interstate access to at least two long-term automotive partners, enabling an accelerated construction timeline.
- Multiple state and local agencies-including GDEcD, the Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia EMC, and Georgia Quick Start-are supporting the project.
- Georgia has attracted over $25.1 billion in EV-related investments since 2020, creating 29,000 jobs.
