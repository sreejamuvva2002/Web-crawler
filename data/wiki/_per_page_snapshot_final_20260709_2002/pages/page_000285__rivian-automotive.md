# Rivian Automotive

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Rivian's Georgia Plant: A Strategic Inflection Point for EV Market Share and Scalability](https://www.ainvest.com/news/rivian-georgia-plant-strategic-inflection-point-ev-market-share-scalability-2509/)  ·  `page_000285`

## Facts

- Rivian Automotive's $5 billion Georgia plant represents a pivotal gamble in the high-stakes race to dominate the electric vehicle market.
- The Georgia facility is slated to begin production in 2028.
- The facility spans 1,800 acres.
- The plant will initially produce 200,000 vehicles annually.
- A second phase of the plant could potentially double that capacity.
- Rivian's current per-unit costs currently hinder its ability to compete with Tesla and traditional automakers like Ford and General Motors.
- The Georgia plant will focus on producing the R2 SUV, which starts at $45,000.
- The Georgia plant will also produce the smaller R3 model aimed at broadening appeal beyond its current premium niche.
- The state of Georgia has sweetened the deal with a $1.5 billion incentive package tied to creating 7,500 jobs.
- Rivian's flagship R1T model is priced at $69,900.
- Rivian's flagship R1S model is priced at $75,900.
- The R1T and R1S models cater to a luxury audience but limit mass-market adoption.
- Analyst Alex Oyler of SBD Automotive argues that scale is the linchpin of EV profitability.
- Rivian's projected 200,000-unit annual output from the Georgia plant could allow the company to spread fixed costs and achieve breakeven margins.
- Rivian reported a $1.66 billion loss in the first half of 2025.
- The financial loss was exacerbated by the removal of federal EV tax credits and rising tariffs that add $2,000 per vehicle.
- Rivian's CEO remains confident in the company's ability to compete on product quality and innovation.
- Rivian's strategy targets the adventure-oriented EV segment with rugged pickups and SUVs.
- The R1T and R1S carved out a unique niche but face competition from models like the Ford F-150 Lightning and Chevrolet Silverado EV.
- Rivian held a 3% market share in the first half of 2025.
- Tesla holds 45% dominance in the EV market.
- Ford and GM are accelerating their EV rollouts, with Ford's Blue Oval City in Tennessee and GM's $7 billion investment in Ohio posing direct threats.
- Rivian relies on third-party suppliers for battery cells, unlike Tesla's in-house production.
- Risks to the Georgia plant include delays in construction, environmental concerns from local residents, and the high cost of scaling production.
- Rivian's stock has plummeted over 80% since its 2021 IPO.
- Rivian has a partnership with Volkswagen involving a $5.8 billion investment for technology access.
- Rivian received a $6.6 billion loan from the U.S. Department of Energy.
