# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Georgia Attracts Foreign Direct Investment for Automotive, Life Sciences - Area Development](https://www.areadevelopment.com/stateResources/georgia/Georgia-investment-fdi-automotive.shtml)  ·  `page_054732`

## Facts

- Ken Stewart, commissioner of the Georgia Department of Economic Development, called fiscal year 2007 "a significant year," noting that 281 projects were announced domestically and internationally.
- The Georgia economy was projected to grow at 2.4 percent, which is higher than the national average.
- Kia auto assembly facility in West Point was announced with a commitment to hire 2,500 workers and begin operations next year.
- A number of Tier-One suppliers have chosen to locate in Georgia following the Kia announcement.
- Korea-based Sewon Precision is locating its first U.S. plant in LaGrange, creating 700 jobs to manufacture stamped chassis, body components, and decorative trim pieces for the Kia plant.
- Kia supplier Sejong Georgia LLC anticipates 250 jobs at its new LaGrange muffler and exhaust systems manufacturing facility, planned to begin production in October 2009.
- Canada is Georgia's largest trading partner, with $4.7 billion of exports.
- At the end of 2007, Governor Sonny Perdue signed a declaration to charter the SEUS (South East United States)-Canadian Provinces Alliance to promote trade and tourism between Canada and the southeastern states.
- The SEUS-Japan alliance, started in 1975, has been a factor in attracting more than 300 Japanese companies currently operating in Georgia.
- China is Georgia's second-largest trading partner.
- In April, the governor opened the Beijing Georgia Business Advisory Center in Beijing to encourage foreign direct investment and find new export markets and tourism opportunities.
- Point-of-sale manufacturer Pax Technology Ltd. of China announced an Atlanta venue for its U.S. headquarters, sales, and support center.
- General Protecht announced a Barnesville assembly and distribution facility for electrical devices in 2007.
- Sany Heavy Industry Company, Ltd. established its U.S. headquarters and assembly center in Peachtree City.
- Transformer company Group EFACEC of Portugal is creating 600 jobs in Georgia.
- Compressed air technology firm BEKO of Germany is adding 35 jobs in Georgia.
- Wipro of India is establishing a software development center expected to hire 500 people over the next three years.
