# Hollingsworth & Vose

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2006-09-07

**Source:** [Hollingsworth & Vose - Nonwovens Industry](https://www.nonwovens-industry.com/hollingsworth-amp-vose-157093/)  ·  `page_008561`

## Facts

- Hollingsworth & Vose operates a manufacturing plant in Hawkinsville, GA.
- The Hawkinsville, GA facility holds ISO 9001:2000 and AS 9100 certification status.
- The battery products segment supplies battery chemistries for industrial and automotive markets including alkaline manganese, nickel cadmium, nickel metal hydride, valve regulated lead acid, specialty-lithium thionyl chloride, zinc air, and paste fiber-PA10-6.
- Hollingsworth & Vose produces high quality gas diffusion layer (GDL) substrate for use in PEM fuel cells, offering both coated and uncoated versions.
- The company is leveraging its expertise with electrochemical devices to participate in the broader alternative energy market, which includes both fuel cells and NiMH batteries for hybrid electric vehicles.
- H&V's saturated friction material is used in the automotive aftermarket for use in automatic transmissions and offers quality and durability.
- Total nonwoven rolls goods sales in 2005 were $187 million, up a solid 7% from 2004 revenues of $175 million.
- Continued high raw material prices for fibers, binders and chemicals, and the ongoing high cost of energy resulted in earnings pressure and necessitated further price increases to customers during 2005.
- In October, H&V unveiled plans to install a wetlaid microglass manufacturing line at its Suzhou, China facility to serve filtration and battery separator markets.
- Site construction in Suzhou should be completed by the end of 2006, with the previously announced machine for engine filtration markets to be started in the first quarter of 2007.
- Engineering for the glass machine is underway and the line is expected to be operational in early 2008.
- In January 2005, H&V purchased its joint venture subsidiary in Apizaco, Mexico as an initial step in continued investment to support regional and global engine and industrial filtration customers.
- A new meltblown production line was started up successfully in Hatzfeld, Germany during 2005 to significantly extend capability and capacity for high efficiency filtration market segments.
- Val Hollingsworth serves as president and CEO of Hollingsworth & Vose.
- Angelika Mayman is the director of new markets for H&V.
