# Rivian

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-04-30

**Source:** [Rivian Announces Optimized Capacity Plan for Georgia Plant | Nasdaq](https://www.nasdaq.com/press-release/rivian-announces-optimized-capacity-plan-georgia-plant-2026-04-30)  ·  `page_017594`

## Facts

- Rivian is increasing its initial production capacity to 300,000 vehicles annually for its future manufacturing facility in Stanton Springs North, Georgia.
- The increased capacity represents a 50 percent increase over the initially planned capacity of 200,000 units.
- The capacity boost will facilitate a lower cost per unit and provide significant room for future expansion in later phases.
- Rivian is partnering with the U.S. Department of Energy’s (DOE) Office of Energy Dominance Financing (EDF) to update its DOE loan.
- The updated DOE loan aims to bolster American manufacturing and construction jobs.
- Vertical construction at the Georgia plant is expected to begin in spring 2026.
- Rivian expects to first draw on the DOE loan by early 2027.
- Vehicle production at the facility is expected to begin in late 2028.
- The total DOE loan for the initial phase is aligned with the updated facility design and roadmap at up to $4.5 billion, consisting of $4,006 million principal and $494 million capitalized interest.
- Preparations are underway for the development of the stamping press area, which is one of the most capital-intensive and technically demanding projects within the plant from a construction perspective.
- Progress on the facility's primary buildings is expected to accelerate this summer as they begin to take shape.
- Rivian recently announced a partnership with Uber.
- The company is expected to build up to 50,000 robotaxis at its Georgia plant beginning in late 2028.
- Rivian Founder and CEO RJ Scaringe stated the company is excited to partner with the DOE to grow its manufacturing footprint in Georgia.
- RJ Scaringe noted that the R2 vehicle model dramatically expands Rivian's market opportunity.
- RJ Scaringe emphasized that thousands of dedicated people will soon work in the Georgia plant and will be instrumental to Rivian's growth as it scales American manufacturing.
- The project aims to ensure that the U.S. retains its leadership in innovation and technology on the global stage.
- Rivian is an American automotive manufacturer and technology company that develops and manufactures electric vehicles and vertically integrated technologies and services.
- Rivian vehicles are manufactured in the United States and sold directly to consumer and commercial customers.
