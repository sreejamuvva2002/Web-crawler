## Facts

- The global EV market was projected to generate approximately $457.60 billion in revenue by 2023.
- The EV market is expected to have an annual growth rate (CAGR) of 17.02% between 2023 and 2027.
- By 2027, the global EV market is anticipated to reach a total value of $858.00 billion.
- Estimated global EV sales by 2027 are projected at 16.21 million vehicles.
- The average price per EV is projected at $53,190.
- China is expected to lead in revenue generation, contributing approximately $190.4 billion in 2023.
- Uganda's transportation sector accounts for 19% of the country's total energy use.
- Over 90% of Uganda's total energy consumption comes from biomass, including firewood (78.6%), charcoal (5.6%), and crop residues (4.7%).
- Uganda's electricity generation is predominantly renewable, with over 99% sourced from hydropower, solar, and bioenergy.
- Uganda's emission intensity averages 8.8 g CO₂/kWh.
- EV chargers are classified by power flow direction as unidirectional or bidirectional.
- Bidirectional charging enables vehicle-to-grid (V2G) applications, allowing EVs to supply power back to the grid or serve as backup energy sources during outages.
- On-board chargers face limitations in power capacity due to constraints in weight, space, and cost.
- Off-board chargers are installed at fixed locations like public charging stations and provide higher power output for significantly faster charging.
- Level 1 charging uses a standard household outlet and is mainly intended for overnight home charging.
- Level 2 charging requires a 240-volt outlet and is widely installed in residential and public locations.
- Level 3 (DC fast) charging relies on three-phase power and is primarily found at commercial locations such as gas stations, malls, hotels, parking garages, and rest stops.
- AC charging generally takes between 4 and 8 hours for a full charge due to the need to convert AC power to DC form.
- DC fast chargers can deliver a full charge within 15 minutes to 1 hour by delivering power directly to the vehicle's battery.
- Single-phase home chargers typically provide up to 7 kW of power.
- Three-phase public chargers offer higher power outputs of up to 22 kW.
- Grid-connected charging stations can utilize AC or DC bus designs, with DC bus systems minimizing energy conversion stages and reducing power losses.
- Off-grid EV charging stations operate independently of the electrical grid, relying on renewable energy sources like solar or wind power.
- Solar PV accounted for nearly 165 gigawatts (GW) of global power capacity in 2016, representing 66.7% of net global capacity additions.
- Solar PV was projected to reach 43% of global capacity by 2022.
- The global total includes 212,394 slow chargers and 109,871 fast chargers.
- PV-grid charging systems draw electricity from the grid when solar generation is insufficient and can export excess PV energy to the grid for financial benefits.
- Energy storage units (ESUs) stabilize DC bus voltage but come with high initial, operational, and maintenance costs.
- Low-cost lead-acid batteries can be used with a battery management system (BMS) to reduce investment costs while ensuring safety and longevity.
- PV-standalone charging systems power EVs exclusively using photovoltaic energy without reliance on the utility grid.
- Direct PV-to-EV connection links the solar array directly to the EV battery.
- PV-to-EV with an intermediate ESU stores excess solar energy for later use when solar power is insufficient.
- Hybrid PV-standalone systems can combine lead-acid batteries with hydrogen fuel cells to enable charging during both day and night.
- Vehicle-integrated PV (VIPV) embeds thin-film PV cells on the EV body to enhance efficiency by 10–20% and support auxiliary systems like air conditioning and electronics.
- Future paintable solar coatings using silicon crystals with quantum points currently offer a potential but inefficient charging alternative at ≤2% efficiency.
- Lithium-ion batteries offer higher energy density, good efficiency ratio, and lower cost but have poor cycle life and low safety.
- Flow batteries provide excellent power quality, good cycle life, high safety, and scalable energy storage capacity separately from power output, but carry high capital costs.
- Integrating variable solar power into the grid can cause voltage flicker and instability, requiring battery storage or ultracapacitors for stabilization.
- Solar energy's diffuse nature requires substantial land area for large-scale deployment of solar-powered EV charging stations.
- Countries including Uganda, Nigeria, Morocco, South Africa, and Kenya are implementing policies to support EV adoption and charging infrastructure.
