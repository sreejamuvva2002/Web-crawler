# Anovion Technologies

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [$800 million electric battery factory announced for South Georgia](https://www.wtxl.com/news/local-news/anovion-maker-of-graphite-for-electric-batteries-plans-800-million-factory-in-southwest-georgia)  ·  `page_001494`

## Facts

- Anovion Tecnologies, based in Chicago, announced it will invest $800 million to build a factory in Bainbridge, southwest Georgia.
- The company plans to hire 400 workers for the new facility.
- Production of synthetic graphite anode at the Bainbridge plant is supposed to start in 2025.
- Anovion was created last year by combining the graphite businesses of Pyrotek and Amsted Graphite Materials, along with new investment from private equity firm Monomyth Group.
- The company has existing facilities in Sanborn, New York, and Clarksburg, West Virginia.
- Anovion previously announced plans to build a graphite factory with a 35,000-ton yearly capacity near Muscle Shoals in northwest Alabama.
- The company won a $117 million grant under the new federal infrastructure law last year to help finance the Alabama plant and improvements at its New York facility.
- CEO Eric Stopka stated the company was attracted to Georgia by its workforce, research universities, and growing solar energy production.
- Anovion executives have stated they aim to reach 150,000 tons of synthetic graphite production in coming years, starting from almost nothing.
- Synthetic graphite is manufactured from carbon products like petroleum coke by heating it to super-high temperatures for long periods, requiring huge amounts of electricity and making power costs a key location factor.
- The Bainbridge plant could qualify for a state income tax credit allowing an annual deduction of $3,000 per job from state income taxes for five years.
- To qualify for the tax credit, workers must earn at least $31,300 annually, and the total incentive could be worth up to $6 million.
- Anovion may also qualify for property tax breaks from the city of Bainbridge and Decatur County.
- Republican Governor Brian Kemp said the announcement furthers his goal of making Georgia the nation's electric mobility capital.
- Georgia economic development officials report that more than 40 electric vehicle-related projects have been announced in Georgia since 2020, pledging $22 billion in investment and 28,000 jobs.
- Most battery graphite is currently manufactured outside the United States.
- The Bainbridge announcement marks the second major industrial win in two years for the city, located in the far southwest corner of Georgia.
- In 2021, Danimer Scientific announced plans to build a $700 million factory in Bainbridge, hiring 400 employees.
- Decatur County Commission Chairman Pete Stephens stated that diversifying the industry mix is necessary for the county to remain competitive and grow.
