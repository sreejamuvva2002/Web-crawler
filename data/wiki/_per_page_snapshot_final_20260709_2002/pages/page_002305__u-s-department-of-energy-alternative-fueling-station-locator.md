# U.S. Department of Energy Alternative Fueling Station Locator EV Charging Infrastructure Trends

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** charging_infrastructure  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Alternative Fuels Data Center: Electric Vehicle Charging Infrastructure Trends](https://afdc.energy.gov/fuels/electricity-infrastructure-trends)  ·  `page_002305`

## Facts

- The U.S. Department of Energy's Alternative Fueling Station Locator contains information on public and private non-residential alternative fueling stations in the United States and Canada.
- The locator currently tracks ethanol (E85), biodiesel, renewable diesel, compressed natural gas, electric vehicle (EV) charging, hydrogen, liquefied natural gas, and propane stations.
- EV charging continues to experience rapidly changing technology and growing infrastructure among tracked fuels.
- Quarterly reports on EV charging infrastructure trends from the Alternative Fueling Station Locator provide snapshots of the state of EV charging infrastructure in the United States.
- These reports break down the growth of public and private non-residential charging infrastructure by charging level, network, and location.
- Beginning in the First Quarter 2023, these reports measure progress towards meeting projected public charging needs cited in the 2030 National Charging Network: Estimating U.S. Light-Duty Demand for Electric Vehicle Charging Infrastructure report.
- These reports help transportation planners, policymakers, researchers, infrastructure developers, and other transportation stakeholders understand the rapidly changing landscape for EV charging.
- In Q2 of 2024, there was a 6.3% increase in the number of EV charging ports in the Station Locator.
- The Q2 2024 increase included a 6.5% increase in public ports and a 4.4% increase in private ports.
- DC fast charging ports increased by the greatest percentage (7.4%) in Q2 2024.
- The Northeast region had the largest increase in public charging in Q2 2024 (13.2%).
- California continues to lead the country in the number of available public EV charging ports as of Q2 2024.
- In Q1 of 2024, there was a 4.6% increase in the number of EV charging ports in the Station Locator.
- The Q1 2024 increase included a 4.8% increase in public ports and a 3.2% increase in private ports.
- DC fast charging ports increased by the greatest percentage (8.2%) in Q1 2024.
- The Northeast region had the largest increase in public charging in Q1 2024 (6.9%).
- California continues to lead the country in the number of available public EV charging ports as of Q1 2024.
- In Q4 of 2023, there was a 5.0% increase in the number of EV charging ports in the Station Locator.
- The Q4 2023 increase included a 5.2% increase in public ports and a 3.5% increase in private ports.
- DC fast charging ports increased by the greatest percentage (9.2%) in Q4 2023.
- The Southeast region had the largest increase in public charging in Q4 2023 (7.8%).
- California continues to lead the country in the number of available public EV charging ports as of Q4 2023.
- In Q3 of 2023, there was a 7.7% increase in the number of EV charging ports in the Station Locator.
- The Q3 2023 increase included an 8.4% increase in public ports and a 2.8% increase in private ports.
- Level 1 EV charging ports increased by the greatest percentage (15.5%) in Q3 2023.
- The Northwest region had the largest increase in public charging in Q3 2023 (13.0%).
- California continues to lead the country in the number of available public EV charging ports as of Q3 2023.
