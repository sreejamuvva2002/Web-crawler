# Decostar Industries, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2014-10-27

**Source:** [Decostar pays $38.5K Settlement](https://natlawreview.com/article/decostar-industries-to-pay-38500-to-settle-eeoc-religious-discrimination-suit)  ·  `page_053666`

## Facts

- Decostar Industries, Inc. is a manufacturer and supplier of automotive parts based in Carrollton, Ga.
- The company will pay $38,500 and provide other relief to settle a religious discrimination lawsuit filed by the U.S. Equal Employment Opportunity Commission (EEOC).
- The EEOC charged that employee Dina Lucas Velasquez made numerous requests to be excused from mandatory overtime on Saturdays due to her religious beliefs prohibiting work during the Sabbath.
- Decostar initially approved Velasquez's accommodation request until January 2014, when a new supervisor took over her department and repeatedly denied her ongoing requests.
- Decostar discharged Velasquez on Oct. 27, 2014, after she refused to violate her religious beliefs.
- The alleged conduct violates Title VII of the Civil Rights Act of 1964.
- The EEOC filed suit in U.S. District Court for the Northern District of Georgia, Newnan Division after attempting a pre-litigation settlement.
- The two-year consent decree requires Decostar to adopt and implement a new policy for employees to request religious accommodation for their bone fide religious beliefs.
- The decree also requires the company to provide annual equal employment opportunity training to its managers.
- The decree further requires the company to post a notice to its employees about the lawsuit and to provide periodic reporting to EEOC about disability discrimination complaints.
- Antonette Sewell, regional attorney for the agency's Atlanta District Office, stated that it is unconscionable and unlawful for employers to force members of their workforce to choose between their livelihood and their religion.
