# Georgia-Korea Economic Partnership Mission

**Type:** event  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Economic Partnerships in Korea - The ATL Airport Chamber](https://airportchamber.com/economic-partnerships-in-korea/)  ·  `page_047707`

## Facts

- Governor Brian Kemp and First Lady Marty Kemp announced they are leading a delegation of state officials on an international mission to the Republic of Korea to reinforce strategic economic development partnerships.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that the Republic of Korea was the very first country he visited when he became governor because of the incredible partners located there.
- Governor Kemp noted that friendships with Korean partners are bringing historic investments and jobs to communities all across Georgia.
- Georgia’s connections to Korea go back decades, and this trip will further strengthen relationships having a generational impact on Georgia being the No. 1 state for business.
- The state delegation consists of state elected officials, including President Pro Tem John Kennedy, Representative Soo Hong, and Representative Lehman Franklin.
- The delegation includes representatives from the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD), Georgia Environmental Protection Division, and Georgia Ports Authority.
- Governor and Mrs. Kemp and the delegation will conduct meetings with existing industries operating or under construction in Georgia.
- Industries to be met include Hyundai Motor Group, Kia, CJ Foodville, LG Group, SK Group, and Hanwha Qcells.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that connectivity leads to business, and relationships build the foundations of mutual progress.
- Meeting with the leadership of existing industries at their headquarters provides a unique opportunity to learn more about their perspective on an industry and where it is headed.
- By keeping the lines of communication open, Georgia can stay ahead of the curve in industry shifts, positioning itself at the forefront of new opportunities.
- The State of Georgia has maintained continuous representation in Korea since 1985.
- Korea’s Consular Office with jurisdiction across the Southeast, Puerto Rico, and U.S. Virgin Islands has been located in Georgia since 1976.
- Korea has been one of the top sources of Foreign Direct Investment and international job creation in Georgia for the last three years.
- In fiscal year 2023 alone, Korean companies announced more than $10 billion in investments and the creation of 12,605 jobs across the state.
- Total trade between the State of Georgia and Korea totaled over $15.8 billion in calendar year 2023.
- Trade with Korea accounted for nearly 10 percent of the state’s total trade in calendar year 2023.
