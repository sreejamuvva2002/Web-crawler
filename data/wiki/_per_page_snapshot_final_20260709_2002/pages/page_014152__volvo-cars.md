# Volvo Cars

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-05-18

**Source:** [Volvo reveals EX60 EV to start at $58,400, plans to expand US production](https://finance.yahoo.com/sectors/technology/article/volvo-reveals-ex60-ev-to-start-at-58400-plans-to-expand-us-production-184853317.html?fr=sycsrp_catchall)  ·  `page_014152`

## Facts

- Volvo revealed the all-electric EX60 SUV as part of its revamped product lineup.
- The EX60 will be built in Europe, making it subject to tariffs, while Volvo plans to expand US-based production.
- Volvo will bring production of its high-volume XC60 gas-powered SUV to its South Carolina plant and build another brand-new vehicle for the US market.
- Volvo Cars CEO Håkan Samuelsson stated at a New York City press event that the company is developing a bigger, family-oriented multifuel or hybrid vehicle specifically for the US market.
- Samuelsson noted that manufacturing in the US avoids tariffs and allows 0% export duties to Europe, which benefits the Charleston South Carolina factory.
- Volvo's current US product portfolio consists of large SUVs, midsize and compact SUVs, and the V60 Cross Country wagon, with gas-powered cars as mild or plug-in hybrids and EVs using the EX prefix.
- The new EV launch follows the expiration of the federal tax credit and regulatory emissions credits that previously boosted automaker profits.
- Volvo Cars Americas president Luis Rezende reported a drop in EV demand after the 45W credit expired, but noted rising interest due to increased oil prices.
- Rezende claimed Volvo is seeing improving metrics in May versus April for EVs and plug-in hybrid EVs compared to regular mild-hybrid cars.
- Website traffic for Volvo's EVs and PHEVs is rising, indicating heightened consumer interest in the US.
- The EX60 will start at $58,400 when it goes on sale in the US later this year, with orders available online starting Monday.
- The cheapest model, the EX60 P6, targets a 307-mile range, while the range-topping EX60 P12 AWD hits 400 miles.
- Fast charging on the P6 trim adds 155 miles in just 10 minutes.
- Rezende stated that fast-charging time is nearly equivalent to filling up at a gas station, offering an EV that costs the same as a gas car but provides a more premium ride, look, and feel.
- Samuelsson and Rezende believe Volvo is well-positioned in the US market ahead of the EX60's later this year launch.
