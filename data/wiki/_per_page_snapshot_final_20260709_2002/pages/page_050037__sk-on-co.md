# SK On Co.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [SK On lays off nearly 1,000 workers at Georgia battery plant amid EV slowdown | Yonhap News Agency](https://m-en.yna.co.kr/view/AEN20260307001100320?section=k-biz/index)  ·  `page_050037`

## Facts

- SK On Co., a major South Korean battery manufacturer, has laid off nearly 1,000 workers at its U.S. battery plant in Georgia amid slowing electric vehicle demand.
- SK Battery America Inc., SK On's U.S. unit, dismissed exactly 968 employees at its battery manufacturing facility in Commerce, Georgia.
- The layoffs were reported via a Worker Adjustment and Retraining Notification (WARN) notice posted on a Georgia state website.
- The workforce reduction accounts for approximately 37 percent of the plant's total workforce of around 2,500 employees.
- The company stated the reduction is part of restructuring efforts prompted by slowing EV sales and changing market conditions.
- The decision was made to adjust operations while maintaining the company's commitment to the state of Georgia and efforts to build a strong U.S. supply chain for advanced battery manufacturing.
- The Georgia plant has been supplying EV batteries to automakers including Germany's Volkswagen and South Korea's Hyundai Motor.
- The plant also supplied batteries for Ford Motor's F-150 Lightning electric pickup truck, though profitability was affected after Ford canceled production plans for the model.
- SK On is currently building a second battery plant in Georgia to supply batteries to Hyundai Motor.
- Production at the second Georgia plant is scheduled to begin in the first half of this year (2026).
- Another SK On plant in Tennessee is expected to begin production in 2028.
