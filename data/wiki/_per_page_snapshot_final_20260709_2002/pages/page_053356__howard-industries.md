# Howard Industries

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** power_electronics  ·  **Published:** 2025-06-26

**Source:** [Howard Industries investing $236 million in Clarke, Jones and Simpson counties | Mississippi Development Authority](https://mississippi.org/news/howard-industries-investing-236-million-in-clarke-jones-and-simpson-counties/)  ·  `page_053356`

## Facts

- Howard Industries is an electrical transformer and technology products manufacturer.
- The company is investing $236.95 million in its Mississippi facilities across Clarke, Jones, and Simpson counties.
- The project will create a total of 450 jobs.
- The expansion will enable the company to meet U.S. Department of Energy’s new requirements for electrical transformers.
- The expansion addresses growing demand for power from sectors such as data centers, electric vehicles, transportation, and industry.
- The Mississippi Development Authority is providing assistance through the Mississippi Flexible Tax Incentive (MFLEX) program.
- The Mississippi Development Authority is providing assistance for land acquisition, infrastructure improvements, and construction costs.
- The Clarke County Board of Supervisors is assisting with the project.
- The Simpson County Board of Supervisors is assisting with the project.
- The Jones County Board of Supervisors is assisting with the project.
- Howard Industries is a privately held, family-owned Mississippi business established in 1968.
- The company is a global leader in distribution transformers with more than 14 locations throughout Mississippi.
- Howard Industries employs more than 4,000 workers across its locations.
- The company has produced over 10 million transformers that are in service throughout the U.S. and abroad.
- Howard Industries plans to complete the project within five years.
- Howard Industries plans to fill the new jobs within five years.
- Governor Tate Reeves stated the $236 million investment highlights record-breaking private sector growth across Mississippi and proves the state is open for business.
- Mississippi Development Authority Executive Director Bill Cork stated the investment validates Mississippi as an ideal location for companies seeking long-term growth and generational success.
- Howard Industries CEO Michael Howard stated the company is deeply committed to Mississippi's growth and that the investment will enhance production capabilities and create new job opportunities in Clarke, Jones, and Simpson counties.
- Economic Development Authority of Jones County President Ross Tucker noted Howard Industries has brought new jobs and investment to Jones County for decades, with its first transformer plant in Laurel and corporate headquarters in Ellisville.
- Simpson County Development Foundation Executive Director Pat Brown stated the company's growth and retention plans are beneficial for the community and that elected leadership made a sound investment in the facility.
- Newt Ishee, Executive Director of the Mid-Mississippi Development District, stated the Clarke County Board of Supervisors and the city of Quitman extend gratitude to Howard Industries for choosing Quitman for the significant investment.
