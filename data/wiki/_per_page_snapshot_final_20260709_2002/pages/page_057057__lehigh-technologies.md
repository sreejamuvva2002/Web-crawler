# Lehigh Technologies

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2012-07-20

**Source:** [Leaf Clean Energy invests $5m in green materials group Lehigh Technologies | AIM:LEAF](https://www.proactiveinvestors.co.uk/companies/news/38304/leaf-clean-energy-invests-5m-in-green-materials-group-lehigh-technologies-45794.html)  ·  `page_057057`

## Facts

- Leaf Clean Energy Company (LON:LEAF) has decided to invest US$5 million in Tucker, Georgia-headquartered green materials company Lehigh Technologies.
- Leaf led the investment round, joining other major investors in Lehigh.
- Lehigh has the leading technology for addressing of end-of-life tires, a market worth billions of pounds per year.
- Lehigh turns end-of-life tire materials and other post-industrial rubber into sustainable powders.
- Lehigh’s micronized rubber powders are used in tires, consumer goods, building materials and other consumer and industrial applications.
- Last year, the company’s powder was used to make more than 100 million tires.
- Each pound of micronized rubber powder saves 0.8 gallons of oil and 10 kilowatt hours of electricity.
- Clean Leaf added that the company’s products are becoming increasingly attractive to customers in view of rising rubber, polymer and energy prices.
- Executive of Leaf Clean Energy Keogh stated: “Lehigh is a compelling investment because they address a major environmental problem with a product that has a great value proposition for their customers.”
- Leaf's mission is to support companies that can achieve environmental sustainability with economically viable business models.
