# Daimler Trucks North America LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2018-12-20

**Source:** [Daimler Trucks North America Delivers its First Battery-Electric Commercial Truck to Penske Truck Leasing - Newsroom - Penske](https://www.gopenske.com/newsroom/2018_12_20_ptl_daimler_battery-electric-truck/)  ·  `page_053588`

## Facts

- Daimler Trucks North America delivered the first vehicle in its Freightliner Electric Innovation Fleet, a Freightliner eM2, to Penske Truck Leasing on December 20, 2018.
- DTNA president and CEO Roger Nielsen ceremonially handed over the eM2 key to Penske Truck Leasing president and CEO Brian Hard during an event in Carson, California.
- The Freightliner eM2 is an electrified solution for local distribution, pickup and delivery, food and beverage delivery, and last-mile logistics applications.
- The Freightliner eCascadia is a Class 8 tractor designed for local and regional distribution and drayage.
- Both the Freightliner eM2 and eCascadia enter series production in 2021.
- The eCascadia and eM2 are part of Daimler Trucks' global electrified truck initiative, joining the Thomas Built Buses all-electric Saf-T-Liner eC2 school bus, the FUSO eCanter, and the Mercedes-Benz eActros.
- Earlier in 2018, DTNA formed the Freightliner Electric Vehicle Council composed of 30 customers with strong use-cases for electric trucks, including Penske Truck Leasing.
- The customer council co-develops deployment strategies for battery electric vehicles, covering applicable use cases, current legislation, facility requirements, charging infrastructure, and service support.
- Starting in December 2018, Penske Truck Leasing will install 20 high-power charging stations across five of its California locations as the first step in infrastructure deployment.
- In 2019, Penske will put an additional nine medium-duty electric eM2 trucks and 10 heavy-duty eCascadia electric trucks into targeted service in California and the Pacific Northwest.
- The Freightliner Electric Innovation Fleet is partially funded with a nearly $16 million grant from the South Coast Air Quality Management District (SCAMQD).
- The U.S. Environmental Protection Agency and the ports of Los Angeles and Long Beach also contributed to the grant funding the fleet.
- DTNA is headquartered in Portland, Oregon, and manufactures, sells, and services commercial vehicles under the Freightliner, Western Star, Detroit, and Thomas Built Buses nameplates.
- Penske Truck Leasing operates more than 300,000 vehicles and serves customers from more than 1,000 locations across North America, South America, Europe, Australia, and Asia.
