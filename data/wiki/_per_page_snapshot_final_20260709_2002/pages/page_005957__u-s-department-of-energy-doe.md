# U.S. Department of Energy (DOE)

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [U.S. Department of Energy Announces Intent to Issue $13M in Two New Grid Modernization Funding Opportunities | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/oe/articles/us-department-energy-announces-intent-issue-13m-two-new-grid-modernization-funding)  ·  `page_005957`

## Facts

- The U.S. Department of Energy’s (DOE) Office of Electricity (OE) released two Notices of Intent (NOIs) to issue funding for grid modernization research and development.
- OE intends to issue two Notices of Funding Opportunity (NOFOs) totaling $13 million later this year to promote a more reliable, resilient, secure, and affordable electric grid.
- Upgrading critical systems and controls will extend the life of existing grid components, increase electricity transmission capacity, and improve communications to predict and prevent failures.
- Gene Rodrigues, Assistant Secretary for Electricity, stated the funding opportunities will advance innovative hardware and software solutions to increase reliability and lower costs of America’s electricity transmission system.
- The first opportunity is an $8 million Renewable Integration Management with Innovative High Voltage Direct Current Power Circuit Breakers (REIMAGINE BREAKERS) NOFO.
- REIMAGINE BREAKERS supports R&D for high voltage direct current (HVDC) power circuit breakers to reduce technology costs and support widespread HVDC adoption.
- DOE anticipates announcing the $8 million NOFO ($7 million from OE, $1 million from WETO) by the end of 2024.
- The number of awards for REIMAGINE BREAKERS depends on applications submitted and fund availability.
- The second opportunity is a $5 million Human-Centric Analytics for Resilient & Modernized Power sYstems (HARMONY) NOFO.
- HARMONY aims to improve risk assessment and communication for grid operators amid big data trends, including the integration of renewable energy and EV charging.
- Grid transformation trends involving renewable energy and EV charging are changing long-standing assumptions about power system operations and causing gaps in assessing systematic uncertainties.
- Advanced physics- and human behavior-aware analytics are critical to help grid operators predict, prevent, and mitigate cascading failures in power grids.
- Approximately $5 million is expected to be available for three-year R&D cooperative agreements under HARMONY.
- OE expects to announce the HARMONY NOFO by the end of 2024.
- Both NOFOs will be posted at FedConnect if released.
