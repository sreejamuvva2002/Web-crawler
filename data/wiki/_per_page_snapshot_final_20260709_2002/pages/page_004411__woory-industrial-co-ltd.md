# Woory Industrial Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-06-07

**Source:** [Woory to Open EV Parts Manufacturing, R&D Facility in Laurens County | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-releases/2023/woory-open-ev-parts-manufacturing-rd-facility-laurens-county)  ·  `page_004411`

## Facts

- Woory Industrial Co., Ltd. announced plans to open a new EV parts manufacturing and R&D facility in Dublin, Georgia on June 7, 2023.
- The project represents an estimated $18 million investment in Dublin-Laurens County.
- The facility will create more than 130 new jobs.
- Woory Industrial Company was established in 1989 and specializes in heating, ventilation, and air conditioning (HVAC) components and systems for the global automotive industry.
- The company is currently concentrating on developing new products for electric vehicles (EV) and hydrogen-fueled cars.
- Jungwoo Kim, CEO of Woory Industrial Company, stated that the transition to electric vehicles represents a revolution in the automobile industry and identified the U.S. EV market as key among all automotive markets.
- Over the past 10 years, Woory has grown into a specialized EV component company through continuous innovation.
- The new facility will be located at 404 Kellam Road in Dublin and will serve as the company’s first U.S. manufacturing location.
- Operations at the Dublin facility are expected to begin in November 2023.
- The facility will produce electrical heaters, control units, and actuators for EV and internal combustion carmakers in North America.
- Unlike internal combustion engine vehicles, electric vehicles lack a built-in heating source, requiring heaters to warm the cabin and maintain battery temperature.
- Woory's pioneering development of electric heaters over the last 10 years has positioned it as a leader in this market segment.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced the project and highlighted Georgia's goal to become the e-mobility capital of the nation.
- Governor Kemp credited the state's highly-skilled workforce and reliable infrastructure network for attracting historic levels of new jobs and investment.
- Dublin Mayor Joshua Kight emphasized that Woory’s commitment to repurposing an existing manufacturing building promotes infill development and creates new career paths.
- Dr. Fred Williams, Chair of the Board of Directors of the Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority, noted Dublin and Laurens County's rapidly growing automotive sector driven by its location on Interstate 16 and access to a skilled workforce.
- The project involves partnerships with the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team, the Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- Project Manager Jacob Lee represented the GDEcD Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Georgia has invested for decades to match competitive projects with prepared communities and sites.
- From July 1 through December 31, 2022, the automotive industry was the second-largest source of new jobs and investment in Georgia.
- Since 2020, Georgia's pursuit of EV supply chain opportunities has resulted in over $22.7 billion in investment and the creation of more than 28,400 jobs.
- Woory currently employs over 1,300 people across its international production plants.
- The company has been nominated as a supplier for Tesla, Rivian, Lucid, and Canoo.
- Woory is currently supplying the HV heater system used to heat the cabins of EVs.
- Area education systems and training providers will partner with Woory to ensure training programs are in place to fill its talent pipeline.
