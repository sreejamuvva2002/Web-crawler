# State of Georgia

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-04-18

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp Signs Historic Tax Cut Package Into Law | Governor Brian P. Kemp Office of the Governor](https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases/2024-04-18/gov-kemp-signs-historic-tax-cut-package-law)  ·  `page_005726`

## Facts

- Governor Brian Kemp signed HB 1015 into law on April 18, 2024, during a ceremony in Augusta, GA, accelerating the largest state income tax cut in Georgia history.
- HB 1015 builds upon HB 1437 to provide a step down of 10 basis points in the individual income tax rate starting in 2025.
- The accelerated reduction sets the individual income tax rate for Tax Year 2024 at 5.39 percent, down from the 5.49 percent originally established by HB 1437.
- This change marks a 36-basis-point reduction from the Tax Year 2023 individual income tax rate of 5.75 percent.
- The Office of Planning and Budget estimates approximately $1.1 billion in taxpayer savings for calendar year 2024 due to the tax cut acceleration and HB 1437's 26-basis-point reduction, with total estimated savings of approximately $3 billion over the next 10 years.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that conservative budgeting and a pro-growth, business-friendly environment will keep billions of dollars in Georgians' pockets rather than funding government bureaucracy.
- Governor Kemp simultaneously signed HB 581, HB 1021, HB 1023, and SB 496 into law on the same day.
- HB 1023 lowers the corporate income tax rate from 5.75 percent to 5.39 percent for Tax Year 2024 by aligning it with the individual income tax rate, aiming to maintain a business-friendly environment that creates good-paying jobs statewide.
- HB 581 enables a constitutional amendment allowing counties to implement a statewide homestead valuation freeze that limits property value appreciation to the inflation rate.
- HB 581 also authorizes a special local option sales tax for counties and municipalities to fund property tax relief.
- HB 1021 increases the state income tax dependent exemption by 33 percent, raising the deductible amount per dependent from $3,000 to $4,000.
- SB 496 expands historic home certification criteria and extends tax credits for historic home rehabilitation to December 31, 2029, and for rural zone revitalization to December 31, 2032.
