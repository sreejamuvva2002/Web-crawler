# Freyr Battery

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-11-07

**Source:** [Freyr to acquire Trina Solar U.S. manufacturing assets - pv magazine Global](https://www.pv-magazine.com/2024/11/07/freyr-to-acquire-trina-solar-u-s-manufacturing-assets/)  ·  `page_055046`

## Facts

- Freyr Battery is a U.S.-based battery manufacturer.
- Freyr agreed to acquire Trina Solar's U.S. solar manufacturing assets for $340 million.
- The acquired assets include a 5 GW, 1.35 million square foot solar module manufacturing facility in Wilmer, Texas.
- The Texas facility started production in November 2024.
- The Texas facility is expected to ramp up to full production in 2025.
- Thirty percent of the Texas facility's capacity is already contracted with U.S. customers.
- This acquisition is the first step in Freyr's plan to build out a vertically integrated domestic manufacturing footprint.
- The next phase of the footprint will include a 5 GW solar cell facility.
- Freyr is currently selecting a site for the new solar cell facility.
- Construction on the solar cell facility is targeted for Q2 2025.
- First Freyr cells are anticipated to roll off the line in the second half of 2026.
- The buildout plans include creating up to 1,800 direct jobs.
- The expansion will provide domestic content for the U.S. solar industry.
- Daniel Barcelo is Freyr's newly appointed chief executive officer.
- Tom Einar Jensen, Freyr's co-founder, will assume the role of CEO of Freyr Europe to oversee optimization and monetization of the European portfolio.
- Since going public in July 2021, Freyr set up a giga-factory in Georgia with a $1.7 billion initial capital investment.
- Plans for the Georgia facility include producing 34 GWh of batteries in 2025.
- Production at the Georgia facility is planned to scale up to 100 GWh by 2028.
- The Georgia facility will bring more than 720 jobs.
- Investment in the Georgia battery facility is projected to exceed $2.6 billion through 2029.
- Freyr anticipates closing the transaction at the end of 2024.
- Solar module production at the Texas facility is expected to begin in Q4 2024.
- Freyr initiated 2025 EBITDA guidance of $75 to $125 million.
- Freyr expects to exit 2025 at a full-year run rate EBITDA of $175 to $225 million.
