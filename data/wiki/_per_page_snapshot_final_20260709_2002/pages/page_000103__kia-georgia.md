# Kia Georgia

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-05-30

**Source:** [Kia Georgia Launches EV9 - Georgia Quick Start](https://www.georgiaquickstart.org/projects/kia-georgia-launches-ev9/)  ·  `page_000103`

## Facts

- Kia Georgia celebrated a historic milestone on May 30, 2024, when it rolled an all-electric 2025 EV9 off its assembly line in West Point, Ga.
- The three-row SUV is the first electric vehicle ever produced in Georgia.
- The launch marks the culmination of a $200 million expansion at Kia’s plant.
- The expansion has added about 200 jobs at Kia’s plant.
- SeungKyu (Sean) Yoon, President and CEO for Kia North America and Kia America, introduced the vehicle.
- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp and James Watson, Kia Georgia’s Vice President of Production, drove the EV9 off the assembly line.
- SeungKyu (Sean) Yoon stated that the launch is the latest example of Kia’s commitment to the U.S. market and the state of Georgia.
- SeungKyu (Sean) Yoon noted that the EV9 will be in excellent hands with the team members at Kia Georgia, citing their track record for assembling award-winning, world-renowned products.
- Georgia Quick Start worked with Kia to design and develop an “Introduction to EV Vehicles” course for new and current Kia employees on the EV9 production line.
- Georgia Quick Start also developed an EV safety course for new and current Kia employees on the EV9 production line.
- Stuart Countess, President and CEO of Kia Georgia, stated that Kia aims to change the future for the better and be a company people feel inspired by and want to work for.
- More than 15 years ago, Quick Start helped Kia start up its operation making cars at the West Point location.
- Quick Start is currently helping Kia add electric vehicles to its award-winning lineup.
- Kia's headquarters are located in Seoul, South Korea.
