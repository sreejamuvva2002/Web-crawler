# State of Georgia

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2023-10-25

**Source:** [Georgia- South Korea Investment Ties Celebrated at Korea Society Gala in NYC – Asia Matters for America](https://asiamattersforamerica.org/articles/georgia-south-korea-investment-ties-celebrated-at-korea-society-gala-in-nyc)  ·  `page_019109`

## Facts

- The Georgia Department of Economic Development was honored for its contributions towards strong US-South Korea ties at The Korea Society’s 2023 Annual Dinner at the Plaza Hotel in New York City this September.
- The department received the Van Fleet Award, previously given to heads of state such as former US President George W. Bush and former ROK President Kim Dae Jung for outstanding contributions to US-Korea relations.
- Korea International Trade Association (KITA) was also recognized as an awardee at the event.
- Korea Society President and CEO Thomas Byrne stated that both awardees play an integral part in enhancing trade and investment between the two countries, creating high-paying jobs in American communities and securing supply chains in critical technologies.
- Georgia is one of the top destinations for Korean foreign direct investment, with $19.5 billion in greenfield investment in the state since 2003.
- Many of the largest Korean investments in Georgia are related to automotives and EV batteries.
- A Hyundai Motor Group-LG Energy Solutions joint venture in Bryan County will bring a total of $7.5 billion in investment into a battery facility and a new EV factory known as the ‘Metaplant,’ along with over 8,500 jobs in the next eight years.
- Hyundai is partnering with SK on a joint venture to develop an EV battery plant in Georgia’s Bartow County with a $4-5 billion investment and an expected 3,500 new jobs.
- Automotive supplier Daesol Ausys Georgia will build a new facility for manufacturing motor and EV components in Harris County, bringing a $72 million investment.
- Automotive parts manufacturer NVH Korea will establish a facility in Henry County with a projected $72 million in investment.
- DAS Corp, a supplier for the Hyundai Metaplant, will invest $35 million in Candler County to establish a manufacturing facility.
- These smaller Korean investments were all announced in 2023.
- In September of 2023, Hyundai signed a memorandum of understanding with Georgia Tech to celebrate their collaborative partnership focused on sustainable development.
- CJ Foodville, a division of CJ group, announced a $47 million investment in a baked goods factory in Hall County to support its bakery chain ‘TOUS les JOURs’ growing presence in the US.
- The Korean American population in Georgia has continued to grow to about 73,000 according to US Census data as of 2021.
- In Fall 2023, 1,910 South Korean students are projected to enroll in universities in Georgia.
- There are 44 programs that US students from Georgia universities can choose from to study abroad in South Korea.
- Jae Kim, President of the Southeast US Korean Chamber of Commerce and Senior Manager of Aprio, noted that a 'Pali Pali' (quick, quick) corporate culture from Koreans is making it into the American workforce when working together with the US.
- Jae Kim added that beyond historically large monetary amounts coming into the US from Korea, there is a next level wave of exchanges including joint ventures, joint research and development, joint workforce development, and joint culture impacting both small and large businesses due to recent investments.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated in his keynote speech that the Georgia-Korea partnership foundations were laid nearly 40 years ago when Georgia first established an economic office in Korea.
- Governor Kemp noted the partnership blossomed when Kia chose Georgia for its first US manufacturing plant in 2006 and will continue to grow over the next 10, 20, and 30 years as new opportunities bring further transformation.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development’s receipt of the Van Fleet Award serves as a testament to economic, cultural, and educational US-South Korea ties and shared prosperity to come.
