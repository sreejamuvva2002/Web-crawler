# Eaton Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** power_electronics  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Eaton Corporation — Grokipedia](https://grokipedia.com/page/Eaton_Corporation)  ·  `page_019609`

## Facts

- Eaton Corporation established its eMobility business unit in 2018 to integrate electrical and vehicle segment expertise for vehicle electrification demands.
- The initial commitment for the eMobility initiative was $500 million to develop intelligent power electronics, advanced power systems, and circuit protection technologies.
- Eaton's eMobility offerings target battery electric vehicles (BEVs), plug-in hybrid electric vehicles (PHEVs), and broader EV applications.
- Components produced include onboard chargers, inverters, DC-DC converters, power distribution units, high-voltage fuses, stamped battery terminals, and automated transmissions optimized for electrified powertrains.
- Eaton developed Breaktor circuit protection technology specifically designed for EV architectures to enhance safety and reliability under high-voltage conditions.
- In the early 2020s, Eaton intensified its strategic pivot toward electrification and digital power management.
- A 2023 initiative was launched to enhance European manufacturing and design capabilities for EV components to meet growing demand for electrified commercial and passenger vehicles.
- By 2024, Eaton's ePowertrain offerings encompassed EV transmissions, gearing, and differentials to capitalize on the shift from internal combustion engines to battery-electric systems.
- In February 2025, Eaton announced advanced testing facilities in Europe for ICE and EV powertrains to accelerate development amid varying electrification adoption rates.
- In October 2025, Eaton unveiled a next-generation 800 VDC architecture aimed at supporting higher-power data centers and EV infrastructure through modular, scalable power distribution.
- Eaton offers AC and DC chargers, power management systems, and software for residential, commercial, and fleet applications, incorporating bidirectional capabilities and vehicle-to-grid (V2G) integration.
- A partnership with ChargePoint was established to provide ultrafast DC V2G chargers delivering up to 600 kW.
- In May 2023, Eaton expanded its European eMobility operations to bolster design, technology, and manufacturing capacity amid rising EV adoption.
- The Vehicle and Mobility Sector was established in August 2023 to unify the Vehicle Group and eMobility businesses for internal combustion engine, hybrid, and fully electrified vehicles.
- In 2023, the Vehicle and Mobility group generated sales of $2.3 billion and employed over 14,000 people globally.
- The sector's headquarters is in Southfield, Michigan, with key offices in Turin, Italy; Shanghai, China; and São Paulo, Brazil.
- Eaton acquired Royal Power Solutions in 2022 to enhance high-voltage capabilities for electrified powertrains.
- In March 2026, Eaton completed the acquisition of Boyd Thermal (the thermal management business of Boyd Corporation) for $9.5 billion.
- The Boyd Thermal acquisition adds advanced cooling solutions including liquid cooling systems, cold plates, heat pipes, and thermal interface materials tailored for high-density applications like AI data centers.
- These thermal capabilities integrate with Eaton's existing power management systems and air-based cooling innovations to provide end-to-end grid-to-chip thermal and power management solutions.
- Eaton's Electrical Sector achieved global sales of $17.7 billion in 2024, representing a significant portion of the company's total revenue of $24.9 billion.
- The Electrical Sector is targeting over $31 billion in sales by 2030 through approximately 10% organic compound annual growth rate and margin expansion to around 30%.
- Eaton has invested over $1 billion in capacity expansions across more than 20 sites, adding over 2 million square feet of manufacturing space.
- Eaton collaborates with suppliers, universities, and research institutions to advance emerging areas like next-generation materials and system designs for eMobility.
- Dedicated eMobility labs are used to simulate real-world operational stresses on components before deployment.
- In 2024, the Electrical Sector captured an estimated 27.6% of U.S. industry revenue in power conversion equipment manufacturing.
- Eaton's overall revenue grew to $24.9 billion in 2024, marking a 7.3 percent increase from $23.2 billion in 2023.
- For 2026, the company guided for organic growth of 7 to 9 percent and adjusted earnings per share of $13.00 to $13.50.
