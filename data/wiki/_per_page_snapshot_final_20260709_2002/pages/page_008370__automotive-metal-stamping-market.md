## Facts

- The global automotive metal stamping market size was valued at USD 105.13 billion in 2025 and is projected to grow from USD 110.45 billion in 2026 to USD 163.99 billion by 2034 at a CAGR of 5.06% during the forecast period 2026-2034.
- Automotive metal stamping transforms flat sheet metal into automotive components including body panels, chassis components, brackets, and structural elements using a stamping press.
- Blanking dominates the technology segment, hot stamping dominates the process segment, and Asia-Pacific is the highest shareholder in the global market.
- Growing automotive production serves as a fundamental driver, with new vehicle sales/registrations reaching 18.8 million units in Q1 2021, representing a growth of over 26% compared to Q1 2020.
- Emerging markets like India, China, and Brazil are experiencing rapid industrialization and urbanization, further propelling automotive production and demand for stamped metal components.
- Technological disruptions like 3D printing and composite materials pose restraints by offering alternatives to conventional metal stamping with advantages such as greater design flexibility and reduced material waste.
- The advent of electric vehicles (EVs) presents a significant opportunity as they require specialized components including lightweight yet durable body structures and battery enclosures.
- Global EV sales exceeded 11 million in 2022, with forecasts predicting a 35% growth in 2023 to reach a total of 14 million units.
- The proportion of the global vehicle market allocated to electric cars rose from around 4.0% in 2020 to 14% in 2022 and further to 18% in 2023.
- Hot stamping, also known as press hardening, involves heating high-strength steel to around 900°C and rapidly stamping it into a die for lightweight yet strong safety components like car body reinforcements.
- Commercial vehicles require durable and precisely engineered stamped parts such as chassis, body panels, brackets, and structural parts to withstand rigorous use and ensure safety standards.
- Asia-Pacific is expected to expand substantially, driven by established automotive manufacturing hubs in China, Japan, and South Korea and rising EV demand.
- China led the electric vehicle industry in 2022 with an 82% surge in EV sales, accounting for almost 60% of global EV purchases.
- China currently possesses nearly 50% of the total number of electric vehicles worldwide and represented 35% of global EV exports in 2022.
- North America represents a dynamic market fueled by robust manufacturing activities in the United States and Mexico, with established OEMs and a dense network of suppliers.
- The U.S. automotive industry accounts for approximately 3-3.5% of the country's GDP, with 15.8 million units sold in 2021, making it the second-largest automobile market globally.
- In August 2023, Willis & Smith Capital and its affiliates officially announced the acquisition of Performance Stamping, LLC, a prominent metal stamping company based in Carpentersville, Illinois.
- Performance Stamping serves domestic and international original equipment manufacturers in sectors including automotive, appliances, water treatment, oil & gas, utilities, industrial services, and HVAC.
- Key players in the market include Aro Metal Stamping Co., Kenmode Precision Metal Stamping, Martinrea International Inc., Shiloh Industries, Inc., Acro Metal Stamping Co., Manor Tool & Manufacturing Company, American Industrial Company, Wisconsin Metal Parts, Inc., and Clow Stamping Co.
- In July 2023, ArcelorMittal Europe - Flat Products entered into a contract with Gestamp Metal Forming GmbH to manage the processing of Gestamp's ferrous scrap metal.
- In January 2024, GSC Steel Stamping, LLC acquired the assets of Dixien, LLC.
- GSC Steel Stamping operates a facility in Lake City, GA, spanning 200,000 square feet, making it the largest automotive stamping plant owned by a minority group worldwide.
- In March 2024, Tenral, a Chinese metal stamping company, plans to enhance its capacity and elevate its metal stamping operations.
