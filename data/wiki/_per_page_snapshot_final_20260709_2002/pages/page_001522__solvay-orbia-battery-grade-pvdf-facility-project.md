# Solvay-Orbia Battery-Grade PVDF Facility Project

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2023-11-06

**Source:** [Solvay and Orbia progress plans for new PVDF facility in Georgia - Chemical Engineering | Page 1](https://www.chemengonline.com/solvay-and-orbia-progress-plans-for-new-pvdf-facility-in-georgia/)  ·  `page_001522`

## Facts

- Solvay S.A., based in Brussels, Belgium, confirms plans to build a new battery-grade PVDF facility in Augusta, Georgia.
- More than half of U.S. car sales are projected to be electric by 2030.
- The facility will produce U.S.-made PVDF, a thermoplastic fluoropolymer, to supply the rapidly growing EV battery market and meet domestic energy storage needs.
- At full capacity, the new operations will provide material for more than 5 million EV batteries per year.
- The project will create hundreds of jobs throughout the value chain.
- Solvay and Orbia have signed a joint venture agreement for this project.
- The partnership secures Orbia's supply of needed materials for Solvay to manufacture suspension-grade polyvinylidene fluoride (PVDF).
- Suspension-grade PVDF is used as a lithium-ion binder and separator coating in electric vehicle batteries.
- Solvay will contribute its process technology and global market know-how to the venture.
- The combined efforts leverage Solvay’s Solef PVDF innovations and Orbia’s raw material assets and production expertise.
- The delivered PVDF enables electric vehicles to travel farther on each charge, extends battery life, and improves battery safety.
- Solvay and Orbia intend to utilize two production sites in the southeastern U.S. for this project.
- One of the two production sites will be dedicated to raw materials, while the other will produce finished products.
- Both plants are expected to become operational in 2026.
- Solvay and the U.S. Department of Energy’s Office of Manufacturing and Energy Supply Chains finalized an agreement for a $178 million grant to Solvay.
- The $178 million grant will help build the facility at Solvay's existing site in Augusta, GA.
- The grant was awarded under the U.S. Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act (IIJA).
- The funding aims to expand U.S. domestic manufacturing of batteries for electric vehicles and the electrical grid.
- The project will fill a significant supply gap in the domestic market.
- The project builds upon favorable regulatory conditions that promote regional production and material security.
