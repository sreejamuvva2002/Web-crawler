# Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution Joint Venture

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-08

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution to Invest Additional $2B in Bryan County – Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority](https://savannahjda.com/2023/08/hyundai-motor-group-and-lg-energy-solution-to-invest-additional-2b-in-bryan-county/)  ·  `page_029867`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution (LGES) will invest an additional $2 billion in their battery cell manufacturing joint venture at the Metaplant in Bryan County, raising the JV’s total investment value to more than $4.3 billion.
- This expansion will create another 400 new jobs.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that in a single year, they broke ground on the largest project in state history, landed multiple suppliers across the state for Hyundai’s Metaplant, and welcomed LGES to Bryan County.
- Governor Kemp aims to make Georgia the e-mobility capital of the nation through these investments.
- Governor Kemp noted that major investments ultimately go to hardworking Georgians in the form of paychecks, improved schools and infrastructure, and thanked Hyundai Motor Group and LGES for recognizing Georgia as the No. 1 state for business.
- José Muñoz, president and global COO of Hyundai Motor Company and president and CEO of Hyundai and Genesis Motor North America, stated the incremental investment reflects a continued commitment to create a more sustainable future powered by American workers.
- José Muñoz expressed pride in partnering with LGES and gratitude for the support of Governor Kemp and Georgia communities in driving operations as a global leader in the electrified mobility industry.
- In May 2023, Hyundai Motor Group and LGES signed a memorandum of understanding establishing LGES as the partner for Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America’s onsite battery cell manufacturing joint venture.
- With this announcement, the EV manufacturing facility and the battery joint venture represent an estimated $7.59 billion in investment.
- The combined projects will create 8,500 new jobs for the region over the next eight years.
- Dong-Myung Kim, president and head of the Advanced Automotive Battery Division of LG Energy Solution, stated the investment underscores a dedication to driving America’s EV transition while bolstering the local economy through quality jobs.
- Dong-Myung Kim thanked the State of Georgia for its support and expressed excitement to bring top-quality products and operational experience to grow with local communities.
- The 30 GWh facility will be able to support the production of 300,000 units of EVs annually at full operations.
- Hyundai Mobis will assemble battery packs using cells from the plant and supply them to Hyundai Motor Group’s U.S. manufacturing facilities for production of Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis EV models.
- Carter Infinger, Chairman of the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority, stated the announcement brings 400 additional well-paying jobs for those in the Savannah region.
- Carter Infinger noted that continued investment by Hyundai Motor Group and announced suppliers like LGES will be transformative for the region.
- Alyce Thornhill, Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy, represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s Global Commerce team on this competitive project in partnership with the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority, Georgia Power, Georgia Ports Authority, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated Governor Kemp set a goal of Georgia becoming the top location for EV manufacturing in the United States.
- Pat Wilson highlighted Georgia’s focus on building an ecosystem that supports the entire EV supply chain to bring jobs home to Georgians statewide.
- Pat Wilson credited state and community partners like the Savannah-area JDA, utility providers, Georgia Ports Authority, and Georgia Quick Start for creating a collaborative, business-friendly environment.
- Since 2018, Georgia has pursued job creation along the entire EV supply chain, resulting in more than $25 billion in investments and the creation of over 30,000 jobs.
- Battery related projects have accounted for at least $11 billion in investment in Georgia.
- In fiscal year 2023, job creation in the automotive industry increased by 324 percent when compared to FY21.
