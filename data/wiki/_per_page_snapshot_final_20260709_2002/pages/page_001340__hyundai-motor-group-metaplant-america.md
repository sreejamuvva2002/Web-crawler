# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-07-22

**Source:** [As workers train, Hyundai Metaplant salary picture takes shape - The Current](https://thecurrentga.org/2024/07/18/as-workers-train-hyundai-metaplant-salary-picture-takes-shape/)  ·  `page_001340`

## Facts

- The Hyundai Metaplant is a $7.56 billion electric vehicle factory located in rural Bryan County.
- More than 1,471 workers have been hired at the Hyundai Metaplant.
- The Korean manufacturer is pushing to get the first car off the assembly line by December.
- Georgia and southeast coastal counties are subsidizing Hyundai and its suppliers with $2.1 billion in tax breaks, construction costs, discounted land, and other perks.
- Gov. Brian Kemp announced the subsidy deal would produce good-paying jobs for Georgians.
- Hyundai Metaplant, the LG joint-venture battery facility, and five affiliated Hyundai suppliers must collectively invest $5.545 billion.
- The entities must hire at least 8,500 workers by 2031.
- The hired workers must have an average annual salary of $58,105.
- The created jobs must be retained until 2048.
- Only two of the eight publicly available economic agreements for companies at or near the Metaplant site promise average salaries higher than $58,000.
- The two companies promising salaries over $58,000 are Hyundai Metaplant and Hyundai Mobis.
- Six other companies bound by the state development benefits have average annual salaries lower than the suggested retail price of the electric SUVs their workers build.
- Two seat-building workers at the Metaplant earn $20 per hour.
- The Hyundai subsidiary aims to produce 300,000 electric vehicles per year.
- The annualized pay for these workers equals $41,600.
- The 2024 Hyundai IONIQ 5 electric SUV, which will be built at the plant, has a suggested retail price of $41,800.
- The $58,105 figure represents an average annual salary plus benefits for all workers on site.
- The $58,105 average pay scale is above the average worker salaries in Coastal Georgia counties.
- 2022 U.S. Census Bureau data reported average worker salaries in Coastal Georgia counties ranging from $30,686 in Bulloch County to $46,305 in Effingham County.
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) pays production workers a base salary of $22.40 per hour, or about $46,592 annually.
- HMGMA maintenance positions start at $30.70 per hour.
- HMGMA implemented a new wage structure for hourly employees in January 2024.
- The new structure is designed to help workers reach an annual salary of $62,524 within four years.
- Six other companies in the Metaplant economic agreement set their pay independently of HMGMA.
- HMGMA will not collect salary data from on-site affiliates until March 2025.
- The first annual report to the state regarding salaries is due in March 2025.
- Bianca Johnson is an HMGMA spokesperson.
- HMGMA does not coordinate with its affiliates on pay and has no plans to do so.
- Of the 1,471 new workers reported in HMGMA's biannual count on June 30, 859 are employed directly by HMGMA.
- The remaining 616 new hires work for affiliated companies.
- If the seven companies fail to create 8,500 jobs averaging $58,105 by 2031, HMGMA risks losing billions in public aid.
- Failure to meet job and salary targets could force HMGMA to repay part of its incentive package.
- HMGMA is confident average pay will reach or exceed $58,105 by December 31, 2031.
- The performance period for the job and salary requirements ends on December 31, 2031.
- More than a half-dozen other suppliers have promised to create almost 4,500 additional jobs in separate state agreements.
- Hyundai-affiliated parts manufacturer Hyundai Mobis promises the highest average pay of $61,642 annually plus benefits, or nearly $30 per hour.
- South Korean company Seohan Auto Georgia will pay workers an average salary of $31,304 annually plus benefits, or about $15 per hour.
- Seohan Auto Georgia's Liberty County plant will build Hyundai's brake systems and drive shafts.
- The seat-building team's pay temporarily increased from $20 to $22 per hour without warning.
- Two weeks later, an HR representative informed the 35-person seat-building team that the $2/hour raise was an error.
- The company stated the salary bump would be deducted from future paychecks.
- The Hyundai Metaplant site spans 3,000 acres and remains an active construction zone.
- Workers are forced to use a makeshift parking lot at the site.
- Industrial nails litter the site, causing vehicle damage like punctured tires.
- The company does not provide a safe parking zone or offer to pay for vehicle damages.
- The company does not provide shuttle transportation for workers.
- Many seat-building workers have finished repaying the mistaken raise, and their pay has returned to $20 per hour.
- Seohan Auto Georgia employs 188 workers averaging $31,304 a year plus benefits ($15/hr).
- PHA employs 402 workers averaging $37,440 a year plus benefits ($18/hr).
- Seoyon E-Hwa Savannah employs 500 workers averaging $37,440 a year plus benefits ($18/hr).
- Sewon America employs 740 workers averaging $37,440 a year plus benefits ($18/hr).
- Joon Georgia employs 630 workers averaging $45,000 a year plus benefits ($21.65/hr).
- Ecoplastic Corporation employs 456 workers averaging at least $45,000 a year plus benefits ($21.65/hr).
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (including affiliate suppliers and the joint-venture battery facility) employs 8,500 workers averaging $58,105 a year plus benefits ($28/hr).
- Hyundai Mobis employs 1,578 workers averaging $61,642 a year plus benefits ($30/hr).
