# Stryten Energy LLC

## Facts

- Stryten Energy LLC is a U.S.-based energy storage solutions provider headquartered in Alpharetta, Georgia.
- The company debuted Reluctance, an innovative mobile microgrid example of a resilient energy ecosystem, at CES 2025 in Las Vegas.
- Reluctance is a modified concept Jeep that operates as a hybrid diesel-electric vehicle.
- The vehicle is enabled by proprietary software that efficiently manages a variety of energy sources, both carbon-based and renewable, and effectively stores that power for use on demand during prolonged power outages.
- Mike Judd, President and CEO of Stryten Energy, stated that Reluctance challenges the status quo with an alternative approach to energy resiliency.
- Judd emphasized the need to capture all energy produced from renewables and hydrocarbons in stationary energy storage solutions and mobile systems like Reluctance.
- The company utilizes a technology-agnostic Earth to Energy™ model that focuses on building a sustainable, domestic supply chain for each battery chemistry in its product portfolio.
- Stryten uses the circular economy of lead batteries as the model for lithium and vanadium energy storage solutions.
- The company is creating an energy ecosystem of the future with a battery-first design that provides grid stability and drives efficient use of power generated from renewable and carbon-based sources.
- This approach enables long-duration energy storage that can be scaled in size and duration to meet the needs of utilities, commercial and industrial, military bases, and emergency response applications.
- Stryten Energy's stored energy technologies include advanced lead, lithium, and vanadium redox flow batteries, intelligent chargers, and energy performance management software.
- The company powers applications ranging from submarines to subcompacts, microgrids, warehouses, distribution centers, cars, trains, and trucks.
- Stryten Energy will host two panel discussions at CES with industry experts and government officials to explore grid modernization, battery supply chains, and energy storage systems.
- The first panel, Earth to Energy: Reimagining the Future of Energy Resilience, will examine how the U.S. energy industry is keeping up with skyrocketing demand and what the future of the battery supply chain should look like to ensure energy security and independence.
- The Earth to Energy panel is scheduled for Tuesday at 2 pm in the Stryten booth.
- Panelists for the Earth to Energy session include Eric Hsieh, Deputy Assistant Secretary for Energy Storage at the U.S. Department of Energy Office of Electricity; Dr. Vick Singh, Senior Vice President of Technology of Dragonfly Energy; and Scott Childers, Vice President of Essential Power of Stryten Energy.
- The second panel, Scaling Energy: Myths, Microgrids and The Race for Resilience, will focus on current and future power demands on U.S. grid infrastructure and what the energy ecosystem should look like to solve resilience challenges.
- The Scaling Energy panel is scheduled for Wednesday at 11 am in the Stryten booth.
- Panelists for the Scaling Energy session include Dr. Erik Spoerke, Materials Scientist and Battery Materials Lead at DOE Sandia National Laboratories; Dr. Vick Singh, Senior Vice President of Technology of Dragonfly Energy; and Scott Childers, Vice President of Essential Power of Stryten Energy.
- Mike Judd stated that batteries are critical to achieving energy resilience and that no single technology can do it all.
- Judd noted that Stryten Energy's domestic, vertically integrated supply chain is the foundation for the energy ecosystem of the future that will set the U.S. on the path to energy independence.
- The company partners with some of the world's most recognized companies to meet the growing demand for reliable and sustainable energy storage capacity.
- Stryten Energy is backed by more than a century of expertise in delivering top-performing energy solutions.
