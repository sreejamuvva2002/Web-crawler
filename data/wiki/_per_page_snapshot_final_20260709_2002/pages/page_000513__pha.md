# PHA

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-03

**Source:** [Hyundai supplier PHA to create more than 400 jobs in Chatham County - SEDA](https://seda.org/2023/03/hyundai-supplier-pha-to-create-more-than-400-jobs-in-chatham-county/)  ·  `page_000513`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that PHA, a global auto parts manufacturer that will supply the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America in Bryan County, will create 402 new jobs and invest more than $67 million in a new facility in Chatham County.
- In a single month prior to this announcement, Georgia’s economic development community had announced more than 1,900 new jobs for hardworking Georgians, with the bulk of those jobs related to the Hyundai Metaplant, according to Governor Brian Kemp.
- Governor Brian Kemp thanked partners in the local communities represented by the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority for their ongoing commitment to seeing businesses and Georgians prosper together.
- PHA is a Tier-1 automotive parts supplier for Hyundai Motor Group, Kia, and many other original equipment manufacturers (OEMs).
- PHA is a Korea-based company with locations throughout the world, including multiple facilities within the United States.
- Since its establishment in 1985, PHA has grown into a global company in the field of automotive door moving systems through research and development, innovative technology, and aggressive investment.
- Sangtae Kim, Chairman of PHA, stated it is an honor for PHA to establish a new production facility in Chatham County, Georgia.
- PHA produces innovative products for the automotive world and the growing EV market.
- PHA expressed gratitude to Chatham County and the State of Georgia for their support in making the partnership happen and launching it to bring new jobs and advance regional economic development.
- PHA’s new facility will be located at the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center, a Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) Certified site.
- The new manufacturing facility will produce door modules, tailgate latches, and hood latches for OEMs, including Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- Operations at the new facility are expected to begin in 2024.
- Savannah Economic Development Authority President and CEO Trip Tollison announced that PHA is the second Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America supplier to locate in the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center.
- Trip Tollison noted that PHA is well-regarded in the industry and expressed anticipation for working with them as they build their new facility and begin operations in the Savannah region.
- Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy Alyce Thornhill represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project in partnership with the Savannah Economic Development Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia Power.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson welcomed PHA to Georgia as the second Hyundai supplier at the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson emphasized that projects like Hyundai and its suppliers underscore the importance of site preparation to attract jobs, noting companies prioritize locations with completed GRAD certification to meet demand and accelerate speed-to-market.
- On October 25, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America officially broke ground in neighboring Bryan County on its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility.
- Offsite suppliers connected to the Hyundai Metaplant project were predicted to invest approximately $1 billion in other locations around the state.
- Projects announced to date for the Hyundai Metaplant ecosystem have exceeded the $1 billion supplier investment projection by more than $800 million.
- Georgia is positioned as a hub for the electric mobility industry, building on the assets that make the automotive industry successful.
- Since 2020, more than 35 EV-related projects have announced more than $21 billion in investment and 27,400 jobs in Georgia.
