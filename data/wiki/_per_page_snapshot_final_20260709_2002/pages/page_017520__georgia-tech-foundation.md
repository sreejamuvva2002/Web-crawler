# Georgia Tech Foundation

## Facts

- Georgia Tech President Ángel Cabrera described Tech Square in the 1990s as a brownfield of chain-link fences, broken windows, and abandoned buildings.
- Tech Square is now recognized nationally as a premier innovation district with the highest density of startups, corporate innovators, academic researchers, and students in the southeastern United States.
- In 2020, Cabrera announced a 10-year strategic plan to turn Atlanta into one of the top five cities for startups in the United States.
- More than 140,000 students are enrolled in colleges across Atlanta, with over 13,000 graduating from tech-focused programs each year.
- Atlanta hosts corporate innovation centers, incubators, and accelerators including Atlanta Tech Park, the Curiosity Lab, and Atlanta Tech Village, which is the largest tech entrepreneurial hub in the Southeast and the fourth largest in the country.
- A Boston Consulting Group (BCG) study found that Atlanta's venture capital funding declined more than 40 percent from 2021 to 2022.
- In 2022, Atlanta brought in $1.9 billion in venture capital funding, ranking 12th among the top 14 U.S. cities, falling short of Austin at $3.7 billion and Miami at $5.6 billion.
- The Georgia Tech Foundation (GTF) has been investing in private equity since the late 1980s.
- In 2019, GTF noticed local startups such as Flock Safety and Stord were seeking funding outside Atlanta due to a lack of local VCs for seed to early-stage companies.
- In 2020, GTF committed $20 million to five local venture capital firms and one direct Georgia Tech-related startup.
- In 2024, GTF made another $25-$30 million commitment, with a portion going to new firms that have established themselves in Atlanta.
- GTF requires its supported VC firms to be based in Atlanta, focus on startups in Atlanta and the Southeast, and maintain a fund size of less than $100 million.
- BCG estimates that about 2,000 startups need to be launched annually in Atlanta to create a self-sustaining entrepreneurial flywheel effect.
- Last year, Georgia Tech was named a top 20 school for entrepreneurship by The Princeton Review and Entrepreneur magazine.
- GTF aims to keep talent in Atlanta and the state of Georgia through its U.S.-centric portfolio strategy.
