# Kia Georgia

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-21

**Source:** [Leadership MAX Class of 2025, presented by Amazon, Enjoys a Day with Kia Georgia and Troup County Leaders](https://metroatlantaexchange.org/2025/09/21/leadership-max-class-of-2025-presented-by-amazon-enjoys-a-day-with-kia-georgia-and-troup-county-leaders/)  ·  `page_009502`

## Facts

- Members of the Leadership MAX Class of 2025 visited Kia Georgia, suppliers Hyundai Mobis and Hyundai Transys Georgia Powertrain, and local leaders in Troup County.
- The field trip was conceptualized by Marion Phillips, Research Manager at Georgia Power Economic Development, and Chris Kidd, Director of Economic Development at the University System of Georgia.
- Local host Meghan Richardson, Director of Economic Development with the City of West Point, Georgia, coordinated the visit.
- The field trip was sponsored by Samir Abdullahi, Director at Select Fulton, and MAX board member Brett Lacy, formerly with Select Fulton.
- Kia Georgia is responsible for creating over 14,000 jobs in the region.
- Leadership MAX Class of 2025 program chairs are Breezy Straton, Vice President at Elevate Douglas, and John Helton, President & CEO at CareerRise.
- Atlanta Technical College provided parking for trip participants; its leaders include Dr. Victoria Seals, President, and Yulonda Beauford, Vice President of Economic Development.
- Stuart Countess serves as President & CEO of Kia Georgia.
- Class members witnessed advanced manufacturing at the plant through high-tech stamping, robotic welding, and automated assembly processes.
- Vehicles manufactured at the Kia Georgia facility include the Telluride, Sorento, Sportage, K5, and electric SUVs.
- Suppliers Hyundai Mobis and Hyundai Transys Georgia Powertrain work in seamless tandem with Kia to ensure parts and components are delivered just-in-time.
- Kia and its suppliers clearly offer employee-centered job opportunities across the skills spectrum in a clean, modern, and ergonomical environment.
- Jeff Lukken, Director of the Wilkinson Family Servant Scholars Program and Assistant Professor at LaGrange College (formerly Mayor of LaGrange), spoke about supporting Kia Georgia's ongoing development.
- Connie Hensler, President of the LaGrange-Troup County Chamber of Commerce, participated in discussions regarding Kia Georgia's ongoing development.
- Nicki Barradas, Assistant Manager/Human Resources at Hyundai Transys Georgia Powertrain, shared insights on supporting Kia Georgia.
- Jamey Jackson, Founder of TalentKinect, addressed the group regarding workforce support for Kia Georgia.
- Jeff Matthews, Vice President of Operations at Fokker Services Group, discussed his role in supporting Kia Georgia's development.
- Eric McDonald, Regional Economic Development Manager for the West Region at Georgia Power, spoke about supporting Kia Georgia.
- Jennifer Pike, CTAE Director at Troup County Schools, participated in the afternoon session on supporting Kia Georgia.
- Julie Post, President of West Georgia Technical College, addressed the group regarding workforce and educational support for Kia Georgia.
- Amazon served as the presenting sponsor for the Leadership MAX Class of 2025 field trip.
- Goodwill of North Georgia served as a silver sponsor for the inaugural cohort of Leadership MAX.
- Georgia Power and Select Fulton served as program sponsors for the inaugural cohort of Leadership MAX.
- Leadership MAX is a cohort-based leadership development program designed for workforce developers to grow into peer leaders who solve problems together.
