# Rivian Automotive

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2021-12-16

**Source:** [Rivian will build its next EV manufacturing plant in Georgia](https://www.teslarati.com/rivian-new-production-facility-georgia/)  ·  `page_059035`

## Facts

- Rivian Automotive has chosen Georgia as the location for its next United States production plant.
- The company confirmed the site selection in its Q3 2021 Earnings Call Shareholder Deck on December 16, 2021.
- The facility is a recently announced second domestic manufacturing plant located near Atlanta, Georgia.
- The Georgia plant has a targeted annual production capacity of 500,000 vehicles.
- The facility will eventually include co-located cell production.
- Production at the new Georgia facility is slated to begin in 2024.
- Rivian had just started production and deliveries of its R1T pickup, which was the company’s first vehicle and the first all-electric pickup to hit the U.S. automotive market.
- Initial R1T units were produced at the company’s main manufacturing facility in Normal, Illinois, where the company is based.
- The decision to proceed with the next facility was driven by demand for the growing EV sector and initial interest in Rivian’s products.
- Rivian’s second domestic facility has been internally named “Project Terra.”
- Fort Worth, Texas, and an unconfirmed location in Georgia were mentioned as top contenders during site selection speculation.
- A Fort Worth City Council Work Study proposed that the plant would contribute 7,500 new jobs to the area by the end of 2027.
- The Fort Worth proposal included average wages of $56,000 and required a $5 billion investment from the City, payable back in 8.1 years.
- Rivian stated that the company “is in discussions with multiple locations as part of a competitive process for siting a second manufacturing facility.”
- Rivian noted that involvement in public-facing processes at potential locations does not indicate a final decision.
- In November, the Atlanta Journal-Constitution reported Rivian was in late-stage discussions to open a plant along Interstate 20, east of Atlanta.
- The Georgia plant location was rumored to be in Morgan, Newton, or Walton county.
- The State of Georgia heavily recruited Rivian for the large-scale EV manufacturing project.
- Georgia state officials were interested in bringing thousands of manufacturing jobs to the area.
- Georgia is already home to the Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia facility, which produces 360,000 cars per year across 2.2 million square feet of manufacturing space.
