# SK Battery America

**Source:** [SK Battery America to allocate 75% capacity to Hyundai and Kia - Automotive Technology Insight | Forecasts | Industry News | Supply Chain](https://autotechinsight.spglobal.com/news/5280461/sk-battery-america-to-allocate-75-capacity-to-hyundai-and-kia)  ·  `page_001113`

## Facts

- SK Battery America (SKBA), the U.S. subsidiary of South Korean battery manufacturer SK On, is expanding its battery production capacity to support Hyundai and Kia’s growing North American electric vehicle lineup.
- SKBA plans to begin producing batteries for Hyundai and Kia EVs as early as March on 9 out of 12 production lines across its Georgia U.S. Plant 1 and Plant 2.
- This allocation will convert approximately 75% of SKBA’s total U.S. battery production capacity for use by Hyundai and Kia.
- The expansion aligns with Hyundai and Kia’s plans to ramp up U.S. electric vehicle manufacturing at their new, highly automated Metaplant America facility.
- Hyundai and Kia are preparing to begin full-scale mass production of the Ioniq 5 electric vehicle starting in March.
- SKBA’s Georgia plant location enables convenient and timely battery deliveries to Hyundai’s Metaplant facility located in the same state.
- SKBA can deliver batteries to Hyundai’s Metaplant within four hours via land transportation.
- The local supply arrangement reduces transportation time and logistics costs compared to the previous method of importing batteries from South Korea and Europe.
- SKBA’s Georgia plant has a production capacity of approximately 16.5 GWh of batteries per year, which is estimated to power about 200,000 electric vehicles.
- Expanding local battery operations will increase benefits from the Advanced Manufacturing Production Credit (AMPC) under the U.S. Inflation Reduction Act (IRA).
- In 2024 alone, SK On received approximately $202 million (292.4 billion won) in AMPC tax credit benefits.
