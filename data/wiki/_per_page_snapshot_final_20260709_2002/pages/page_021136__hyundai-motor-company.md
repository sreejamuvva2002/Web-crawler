# Hyundai Motor Company

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-03-26

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Company CEO talks automaker's $21 billion US investment | Fox Business](https://www.foxbusiness.com/economy/hyundai-motor-company-ceo-talks-21-billion-investment-us)  ·  `page_021136`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Company CEO Jose Munoz appeared Wednesday on 'The Claman Countdown' where he talked about Hyundai Motor Group’s newly-announced massive investment in the U.S.
- His appearance came the same day the automaker marked the official grand opening for its Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) near Savannah, Georgia.
- Hyundai revealed it will put $21 billion over the next few years toward boosting manufacturing in the U.S.
- CEO Jose Munoz stated that HMGMA Metaplant America is comprised of $12.6 billion dollars.
- The facility includes one assembly plant with a capacity of 300,000 that will get to as much as 500,000 and two battery plants.
- Hyundai's executive chair, Mr. Chung, announced before President Trump that the company is planning to invest $21 billion between now and 2028 in a number of mega projects.
- One mega project is the new Electric Arc Furnace plant in Louisiana.
- The $21 billion investment will include $9 billion to bring annual U.S. production capacity to 1.2 million vehicles.
- The investment includes $6 billion for 'enhancing parts localization and logistics'.
- The investment includes $6 billion towards more 'collaboration in future industries' like autonomous driving, robotics, and energy infrastructure.
- Munoz told Claman that the company 'took a big decision to invest in America' during the prior Trump administration.
- Munoz noted that America is their largest market and they decided to make the largest investment in the world with this $12.6 billion.
- Localizing production in America is intended to address potential tariffs and bring jobs.
- Hyundai produces not only cars, but steel and many other things.
- The new investments will bring in total about 670,000 jobs, direct and indirect.
- Vehicles that Hyundai will manufacture at its Savannah, Georgia plant 'probably they will have to pay no tariffs.'
- Hyundai is the latest among a growing number of companies announcing plans to increase investment in the U.S. under the Trump administration.
- The Trump administration has been using tariffs to encourage companies to bring manufacturing back to American soil and reduce reliance on foreign goods.
- Trump implemented 20% levies on goods imported into the U.S. from China.
- Mexico and Canada were hit with tariffs, but Trump later introduced exemptions for those two countries on goods under the USMCA until April.
- Imports of steel and aluminum have become subject to new tariffs.
