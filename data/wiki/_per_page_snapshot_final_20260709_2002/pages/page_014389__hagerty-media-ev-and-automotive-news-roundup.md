# Hagerty Media EV and Automotive News Roundup

**Type:** event  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-01-18

**Source:** [Volvo splits North American operations, Alfa Tonale pricing revealed, Mazda's most powerful engine yet - Hagerty Media](https://www.hagerty.com/media/news/the-manifold/2023-01-18/)  ·  `page_014389`

## Facts

- BYD is the world’s largest manufacturer of electric vehicles, producing 1.86 million electric and plug-in hybrids last year.
- The Inflation Reduction Act offers a $7500 rebate on EVs but imposes battery material sourcing rules that disqualify EVs produced outside North America from receiving it.
- BYD previously planned to display its cars and technology at the Consumer Electronics Show in Las Vegas but cancelled the appearance.
- A source indicated that selling vehicles with a $7500 disadvantage compared to domestic competitors is a major barrier to U.S. market entry.
- BYD already manufactures electric buses at a California factory.
- BYD is backed by Warren Buffet’s investment firm and may eventually scale production to three million electric vehicles per year.
- Alfa Romeo will offer a single plug-in hybrid powertrain for the 2024 Tonale in the United States market.
- The Tonale PHEV combines a 1.3-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine driving the front wheels with a 90-kW electric motor driving the rear wheels for a total of 285 horsepower.
- The Tonale uses a 15.5-kWh battery to achieve a pure electric range of more than 30 miles in the U.S. market.
- Alfa Romeo removed a planned 2-liter turbocharged four-cylinder non-hybrid option from the U.S.-spec Tonale lineup, though Canadian and Mexican buyers will retain that choice.
- Mazda’s 2024 CX-90 crossover will feature a 3.3-liter turbocharged inline-six engine producing up to 340 hp and 369 lb-ft of torque when using premium fuel.
- The CX-90 powertrain incorporates 48-volt hybrid technology, though specific details regarding motor placement and battery type remain undisclosed.
- Mazda will offer a plug-in hybrid variant of the CX-90 that likely utilizes a 17.8-kWh battery system to deliver up to 35 miles of electric-only range.
