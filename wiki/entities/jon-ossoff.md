# Jon Ossoff

**Type:** Person  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[battery-cell-manufacturing|Battery Cell Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** Georgia, United States

## Overview

U.S. Senator Jon Ossoff’s three-year report highlights his leadership in attracting electric-vehicle battery manufacturing and component production to Georgia, securing major federal investments, and advancing related infrastructure, workforce, and policy initiatives.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Led efforts that resulted in a partnership between LG and Hyundai to build an electric-vehicle battery plant (Hyundai MetaPlant) in Bryan County and secured a new manufacturing facility in Bainbridge for EV components, positioning Georgia as a hub for EV supply-chain development.

## Key facts

- Delivered $1.3 billion in broadband expansion funding to bring high-speed internet to thousands of Georgia families.
- Secured $30 million for pedestrian and bike safety improvements in downtown Atlanta under the Safe Streets for All program.
- Obtained historic $8 million investment to begin planning passenger rail between Atlanta and Savannah, plus additional grants for Atlanta-Charlotte and Atlanta-Nashville corridors.
- Helped Georgia pecan growers gain access to the Indian market, lowering tariffs by 70% in early 2023.
- Delivered a $117 million bipartisan infrastructure grant to build an EV components manufacturing facility in Bainbridge.
- Passed legislation expanding Head Start, Early Head Start and Pre-K funding with over $22 million allocated statewide.
- Secured federal resources for pediatric hospitals at Emory University and Scottish Rite Children’s Medical Center to train and retain pediatric physicians.
- Championed the selection of Moody Air Force Base for the next F-35A mission, strengthening Georgia’s defense aerospace presence.
- Led bipartisan investigations that resulted in reforms to military family housing and veteran health-care access.
