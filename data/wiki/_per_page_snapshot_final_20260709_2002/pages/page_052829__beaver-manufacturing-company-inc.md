# Beaver Manufacturing Company, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Beaver Manufacturing expands with acquisition of Intercord Thüringen](https://www.innovationintextiles.com/industrial/beaver-manufacturing-expands-with-acquisition-of-intercord-thringen/)  ·  `page_052829`

## Facts

- Beaver Manufacturing Company, Inc. (BMC) is a North American supplier of technical fibre reinforcements for hoses and other industrial applications.
- BMC announced plans to acquire Intercord Thüringen GmbH of Germany to expand its global presence.
- The acquisition adds RFL treating technology to BMC's product line.
- The deal gives BMC a third production plant globally, rounding out its footprint with existing plants in the US and Mexico.
- The new manufacturing facility is located in Mühlhausen Thüringen, Germany.
- Michael Dubin, Sr. Vice President & Chief Operating Officer of BMC, stated the acquisition makes a significant expansion into the European market and enters the tyre and belt industry.
- The German plant will continue to produce RFL treated yarns and cords, as well as hose products that incorporate Beaverloc treatments.
- Intercord’s technology has produced cords for tyres, hoses, and transmission belts in the automotive and industrial industries for more than 20 years.
- Ramazan Yasbay, who will continue as Managing Director of the German facility for BMC, stated that adding Beaver Manufacturing's technology and product line will increase innovation in the industry throughout Europe and the Americas.
- Despite a sluggish global economy during the pandemic, BMC executives stated the partnership would continue growth, increase production, and promise job creation into 2021.
- The acquisition comes only two years after BMC opened its manufacturing site in Mexico.
- Executives involved in the deal include Craig Chandler (Vice President of Finance), Michael Dubin (Sr. Vice President & Chief Operating Officer), Ramazan Yasbay (Managing Director for Germany), and Walter vom Stein (former owner of Intercord).
- The article is published from Mansfield, GA.
