# Hollingsworth & Vose

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2023-01-03

**Source:** [Global Advanced Materials Manufacturer Hollingsworth & Vose To Expand In Virginia | Textile World](https://www.textileworld.com/textile-world/2023/01/global-advanced-materials-manufacturer-hollingsworth-vose-to-expand-in-virginia/)  ·  `page_008623`

## Facts

- Governor Glenn Youngkin announced that Hollingsworth & Vose (H&V), a global manufacturer of advanced materials used in filtration, battery, and industrial applications, will invest $40.2 million to expand its operation in Floyd County, Va.
- The company will add more than 28,000 square feet to its facility at 365 Christiansburg Pike Northeast to accommodate new production equipment to meet increased demand.
- Virginia successfully competed with Georgia for the project, which will create 25 new jobs.
- Governor Glenn Youngkin stated that H&V has generated positive economic impact and job opportunities in Floyd County for more than four decades, and this significant investment further solidifies the company’s commitment to Virginia.
- Governor Youngkin noted that businesses with a long history of expansion in the Commonwealth offer powerful testimonials on why a Virginia location is a foundation for success, and expressed a look forward to continued partnership with H&V.
- Secretary of Commerce and Trade Caren Merrick stated that H&V has played a role in the success of Virginia’s advanced materials industry over the past 46 years, and they are thrilled to support this important corporate partner’s expansion in Floyd County.
- Secretary Merrick highlighted the company’s continued investment as a strong testament to the New River Valley’s strategic location and business-friendly environment, thanking H&V for creating high-quality jobs in Floyd County.
- CEO Josh Ayer stated that they have been part of the Floyd, Virginia community since 1976 and that this facility is essential to serving both global and domestic customers.
- CEO Ayer explained that they chose Virginia for this expansion because of its positive business environment and strong support from the Commonwealth of Virginia and Floyd County.
- Joe Turman, Chairman of the Floyd County Board of Supervisors, stated that they are delighted to continue supporting H&V’s growth in Floyd County and noted their new investment is over three times bigger than the largest private investment in the county’s 191-year history.
- Delegate Marie March stated that the expansion will provide crucial jobs for Floyd families, enable keeping more families working at home, and welcomed the investment to their economy, noting private enterprise ventures reward and benefit the local community.
- H&V was incorporated in 1892 and is headquartered in East Walpole, Massachusetts.
- H&V’s advanced materials contribute to a cleaner world through products that provide clean air, clean liquids, and energy storage.
- The company has been family-owned for seven generations.
- In 1976, H&V established its first Virginia facility in Floyd County and currently employs over 200 workers.
- The Virginia Economic Development Partnership worked with Floyd County to secure the project for Virginia.
- Governor Youngkin approved a $558,700 grant from the Commonwealth’s Opportunity Fund to assist Floyd County with the project.
