# Hyundai Metaplant America (Joint Venture with LG Energy Solution)

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-06

**Source:** [ICE arrests at a Georgia Hyundai plant create new tension with South Korea - TPR: The Public's Radio](https://thepublicsradio.org/npr/ice-arrests-at-a-georgia-hyundai-plant-create-new-tension-with-south-korea/)  ·  `page_046674`

## Facts

- South Korea’s foreign minister is considering a trip to the U.S. to meet with the Trump administration after hundreds of South Korean nationals were arrested in Georgia this week at an electric vehicle battery plant.
- Foreign Minister Cho Hyun expressed deep concern and a heavy sense of responsibility over the arrests of South Korean nationals during an emergency meeting in Seoul.
- South Korean President Lee Jae Myung called for all-out efforts to swiftly resolve the matter, stressing that the rights and interests of South Korean nationals and the business operations of South Korean companies investing in the United States must not be infringed upon.
- U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement agents arrested 475 people, most of them South Korean nationals, in a raid Thursday at an electric vehicle battery plant under construction in Ellabell, Ga., near Savannah.
- The plant is a joint venture between Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution.
- ICE released video showing officers shackling workers and loading them onto Georgia prison buses during what officials say is the largest single-site enforcement operation in the history of the Homeland Security Investigations.
- Steven Schrank, special agent in charge of homeland security investigations for Georgia and Alabama, stated the operation underscores a commitment to protecting jobs for Georgians and Americans and sends a clear message that those who exploit the workforce or violate federal laws will be held accountable.
- Schrank clarified the arrests were the result of a months-long investigation, not a random roundup, and that the workers were either illegally present in the U.S. or working unlawfully.
- President Trump characterized the arrested individuals as "illegal aliens" and stated that ICE was just doing its job during a White House press conference on Friday.
- Democratic U.S. Sen. Raphael Warnock of Georgia raised questions about the president’s immigration strategy and how the firms were able to allegedly employ workers without proper documentation.
- Warnock stated companies should not be able to get rich in Georgia by exploiting immigrant workers, calling the situation bad for the economy, Georgia’s workers, and law-abiding families living in fear of this administration.
- Hyundai Motor Co. stated it is working to understand the specific circumstances of the arrests and noted that based on current understanding, none of those detained are directly employed by Hyundai Motor Company.
- Hyundai promised an internal investigation into employment compliance and reiterated its zero tolerance policy for those who don't follow the law.
- LG Energy Solution suspended all business trips to the U.S. and instructed workers visiting the U.S. to stay in their accommodations or return to South Korea immediately.
- LG Energy Solution's chief human resources officer is heading to the U.S. to address the issues on site, with the company promising to make every effort to promptly resolve the situation and ensure the speedy and safe return of all employees.
- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp has touted the Hyundai Metaplant America site as a game changer for the state that has provided historic benefits and new opportunities for workers.
- A spokesperson for Gov. Kemp stated the state will always enforce the law, including federal immigration law, and that all companies operating within Georgia must follow the laws of the state and nation.
- Republican U.S. Senate candidate and Congressman Buddy Carter, whose district includes the Ellabell plant site, applauded the Trump Administration and law enforcement for taking bold action to put American workers first and protect communities from illegal immigration.
- The arrests occur at a time when President Trump has been pushing for trade deals and more South Korean investment in the U.S.
- Hyundai also operates an auto plant in Montgomery, Ala., and has announced plans to invest more than $5 billion in a steel plant in Louisiana.
