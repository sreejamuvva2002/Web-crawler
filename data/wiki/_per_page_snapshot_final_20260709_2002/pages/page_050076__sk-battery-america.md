# SK Battery America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-01-30

**Source:** [SK Battery America Exceeds Hiring Goal, On Track to Reach 3,000 Workers - AllOnGeorgia](https://allongeorgia.com/georgia-business/sk-battery-america-exceeds-hiring-goal-on-track-to-reach-3000-workers/)  ·  `page_050076`

## Facts

- SK Battery America has exceeded its hiring goal of 2,600 employees two years ahead of schedule for its two lithium-ion battery manufacturing facilities in Commerce, Georgia.
- The company met its hiring goal at the end of 2022 and is further expanding its workforce to approximately 3,000 over the next year to meet growing demand for electric vehicles.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that SK Battery America and the State of Georgia share a common trait of never resting on their laurels and always working to reach the next milestone.
- Governor Brian Kemp thanked SK Battery America for going above and beyond in delivering on its commitments.
- Governor Brian Kemp noted SK Battery America is one of the first companies to put Georgia on the e-mobility map, with a relationship that continues to bring opportunity to communities across the state.
- SK Battery America announced its first project in Georgia in 2018, with an initial planned investment of $1.6 billion.
- Since 2019, the company has invested $2.6 billion to build two battery manufacturing facilities in Jackson County to supply electric vehicles, including the Ford F-150 Lightning and the Volkswagen ID.4.
- SK Battery America started mass production in early 2022.
- SK Battery America CEO Timothy Jeong credited the state’s Quick Start program for helping attract and train workers with a speed and scale difficult for any company to achieve on its own.
- More than 300,000 new electric vehicles a year will be on American roads because of the work being done at the Georgia facilities.
- SK Battery America has worked with Georgia Quick Start, the state’s top-ranked job training program through the Technical College System of Georgia, since announcing its first project in 2018.
- In July 2022, SK Battery America announced a partnership with the Georgia National Guard’s Work for Warriors Georgia program to focus on hiring veterans, servicemembers, retirees, reservists, and their families.
- The company has held numerous career fairs, visited schools, and held community events in recent months to attract talent.
- To attract the highest-quality workforce, SK Battery America has consistently improved wages, benefits, and its overall work environment.
- Tom Crow, Chairman of the Jackson County Board of Commissioners, appreciated SK Battery America’s commitment to provide high quality jobs within the county.
- Tom Crow expressed gratitude for SK Battery America’s substantial financial investment and looked forward to seeing the benefits the operation will continue to bring to the community.
- The company is still hiring for jobs in production, quality, utility, logistics, ERT, maintenance, and other areas.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson praised SK Battery America’s commitment to providing high-skilled, high-paying jobs for Georgians and meeting standing commitments and aggressive hiring needs.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson commended SK Battery America’s dedication to the state, its adopted community, and the people of Georgia.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson noted SK Battery America’s out-front role in the development of the electric mobility ecosystem in the state and participation in every opportunity presented.
- SK On is the parent company of SK Battery America and a global leading electric vehicle battery developer, manufacturer, and solutions provider.
- As SK On’s U.S. production base, SK Battery America produces one of the world’s safest, fastest charging and longest-lasting batteries.
- The batteries produced at the Georgia facilities are currently installed in the Ford F-150 Lightning and Volkswagen ID.4.
- The combined capacity of the Georgia facilities will make 22 GWh of battery cells a year.
- This production capacity is enough to power the equivalent of about 300,000 EVs annually.
- Since 2019, SK Battery America has invested a total of $2.6 billion to build EV battery plants in Commerce, Georgia.
