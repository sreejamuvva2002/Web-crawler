# Adient

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2021-06-28

**Source:** [Adient Malaysia announces new RM10 million facility in Melaka - to export headrests to Thailand, Indonesia - paultan.org](https://paultan.org/2021/06/28/adient-malaysia-announces-new-rm10-million-facility-in-melaka-to-export-headrests-to-thailand-indonesia/)  ·  `page_016703`

## Facts

- Adient Automotive Seating announced that its latest foam plant in Alor Gajah, Melaka has begun exporting head restraint systems to Thailand and Indonesia.
- The new RM10 million facility will serve both the local and export markets, and is fitted with state-of-the-art foam manufacturing technology.
- The facility is equipped with a rainwater harvesting system and solar panels to supplement energy generation.
- The plant features five-star power rated equipment that are low in carbon emissions, and the plant is completely lit via LED.
- The company claims its manufacturing facility is the first in Malaysia to be equipped with the Hydra Manufacturing System, a smart technology that collects vast amounts of data from various equipment in real time.
- Data from the system is accessible either onsite or remotely, allowing workers to quickly identify potential defects and their root causes.
- Company managing director AJ Dabydin stated that people are at the centre of designing the automation despite the technology-driven approach.
- Operators were reskilled and upskilled for effective man-machine interaction, and the plant layout was designed to allow for one-metre physical distancing and carefully planned logistic routes to keep workers safe.
- Since 1983, Adient has been consistently investing to improve and expand the capability and capacity of its manufacturing operations in Malaysia.
- With the new foam plant in Alor Gajah, Adient is elevating its Malaysian operations to become an automotive export hub for the Southeast Asia region.
- Through this initiative, Adient will drive parts localisation, develop local suppliers, and create more job opportunities and export revenues for Malaysians and Malaysia.
