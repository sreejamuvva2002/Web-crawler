# Sunmax Tech

**Type:** Company  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[power-electronics|Power Electronics]]  ·  **Location:** Adairsville, Georgia, United States

## Overview

Sunmax Tech Inc., an energy component manufacturer and supplier to Tesla and Hanwha (parent of QCells), is establishing a 300,000-square-foot manufacturing operation base in Adairsville, Georgia, investing $193 million and creating at least 242 jobs across three phases producing solar junction boxes, cables, EVA film, aluminum frames and copper wire.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Supplier of solar components that support renewable-energy infrastructure for electric vehicles, including photovoltaic junction boxes used in EV charging stations.

## Key facts

- Sunmax Tech Inc. is opening a 300,000-square-foot manufacturing operation base in Adairsville, Georgia.
- The company supplies solar components to Tesla and Hanwha (parent of QCells).
- Sunmax plans to invest $193 million and create at least 242 new jobs over a three-phased plan.
- Phase 1 will acquire equipment for solar junction boxes and cables; Phase 2 will expand production to EVA film, cable line plastic material, aluminum solar frames and copper wire.
- The company targets $100 million in annual sales by 2027 and aims to capture 40% of the North American photovoltaic junction box and connectors market share.
- Georgia’s growing solar panel manufacturing industry is creating jobs and opportunities for many communities, especially in Northwest Georgia, said Kristi Brigman.
