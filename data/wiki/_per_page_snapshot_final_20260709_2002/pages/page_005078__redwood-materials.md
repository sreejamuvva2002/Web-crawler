# Redwood Materials

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [LPO Offers Conditional Commitment to Redwood Materials to Produce Critical Electric Vehicle Battery Components From Recycled Materials | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/edf/articles/lpo-offers-conditional-commitment-redwood-materials-produce-critical-electric-vehicle)  ·  `page_005078`

## Facts

- DOE's Loan Programs Office (LPO) offered a conditional commitment to lend up to $2 billion to Redwood Materials.
- The funding is for the construction and expansion of a battery materials campus in McCarran, Nevada.
- Once fully operational, the project will be the first domestic facility to support production of anode copper foil and cathode active materials in a fully closed-loop lithium-ion battery manufacturing process by recycling end-of-life battery and production scrap.
- Redwood Materials is expected to create approximately 3,400 good-paying construction jobs.
- The project will employ approximately 1,600 full-time employees, including labor, technical staff, and on-site management.
- The construction workforce will be comprised of union, Minority and/or Woman-Owned Business Enterprises.
- LPO works with borrowers to create good-paying jobs with strong labor standards during construction, operations, and throughout the life of the loan.
- Borrowers must adhere to a strong Community Benefits Plan.
- This commitment builds upon recent Advanced Technology Vehicles Manufacturing (ATVM) loan announcements for Syrah Vidalia, Ultium Cells, and Rhyolite Ridge.
- The project supports the Biden Administration’s target of making half of all new vehicles sold in 2030 zero-emissions vehicles, including battery electric, plug-in hybrid electric, or fuel cell electric vehicles.
- At full production capacity, the project’s anode copper foil and cathode active material output is anticipated to support the production of more than 1 million EVs per year.
- The project will reduce annual gasoline consumption by over 395 million gallons.
- The project will avoid more than 3.5 million tons of CO2 emissions each year.
- Redwood Materials will use both new and recycled feedstocks comprised of critical materials like lithium, nickel, and cobalt.
- The facility will produce approximately 36,000 metric tons per year of ultra-thin battery-grade copper foil for use as the anode current collector.
- The facility will produce approximately 100,000 metric tons per year of cathode active materials.
- Anode copper foil and cathode active material are the two most critical and valuable components of lithium-ion battery manufacturing for EVs.
- Onshoring production of these components is critical to America’s energy independence while ensuring the long-term success and sustainability of an advanced battery manufacturing industry domestically.
- To date, nearly all anode and cathode production supporting U.S. battery cell manufacturers occurs in Asia.
- This conditional commitment makes Redwood Materials one of the first domestic projects that produces cathode materials at scale for battery cell manufacturing.
- The project will support a domestic, sustainable supply of anode copper foil as U.S. EV adoption grows exponentially.
- The share of annual battery-powered electric vehicles is expected to rise from about 2% in 2020 to 35% by 2030.
- On average, Redwood can recover greater than 95% of critical battery elements in an end-of-life battery (including lithium, nickel, cobalt, manganese, and copper).
- Recovered metals are used to manufacture anode and cathode components domestically for U.S. battery cell manufacturers.
- The Nevada operation recycles end-of-life batteries from consumer electronics like cell phone batteries, laptop computers, power tools, and other electronic waste.
- Lowering the cost of critical materials using recycled materials has the potential to make electric vehicles more accessible and affordable to consumers.
- Redwood Materials currently has partnerships with several existing and prospective EV battery manufacturers in the United States.
- Off-takers include Panasonic Energy of North America, which is located nearby at the Tesla Gigafactory.
- Panasonic Energy has another facility under construction in Kansas that will source Redwood Materials’ cathode and anode materials for battery cell production.
- Other partnerships include key U.S.-based and global automakers, domestic suppliers of end-of-life batteries and electronic waste, and others.
- Proximity to battery manufacturers’ facilities will drive down logistics costs and emissions.
- Proximity will enable faster learning cycles between battery makers and Redwood Materials.
- Proximity will accelerate production efficiencies and reduce costs in these component technologies.
- The project helps meet the goals put forth by the Biden Administration’s Executive Order 14017 on America’s Supply Chains.
- Goals include the promotion of domestic critical materials recycling and manufacturing.
- Domestic production of these battery components is paramount to national security, building the supply chain, and strengthening the economy long-term.
- Shifting the domestic critical materials supply for lithium-ion batteries to meet future demands using recycled materials will help decouple U.S.-based battery production from a shock-prone international supply chain.
- This shift will enable the growth of reliable, large-scale U.S.-based EV battery cell production.
- While the conditional commitment demonstrates intent to finance, several steps remain for the project to reach critical milestones.
- Certain conditions must be satisfied before the Department issues a final loan.
