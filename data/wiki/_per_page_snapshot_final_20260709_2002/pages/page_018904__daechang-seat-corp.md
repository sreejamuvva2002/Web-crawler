# Daechang Seat Corp.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-10-24

**Source:** [South Korean auto parts maker plans $72.5M plant near new Hyundai facility in Georgia, hiring 500 - Statesboro Herald](https://www.statesboroherald.com/local/south-korean-auto-parts-maker-plans-725m-plant-near-new-hyundai-facility-georgia-hiring-500/)  ·  `page_018904`

## Facts

- A South Korean company that makes automotive seat frames will open a factory in Georgia to supply a new Hyundai Motor Group plant.
- Daechang Seat Corp., based in Gyeongju, South Korea, announced plans to invest $72.5 million in a plant in Savannah and hire more than 500 people.
- The company is the latest to announce a plant to supply Hyundai's $7.6 billion plant to assemble electric vehicles and batteries in Ellabell, near Savannah.
- Hyundai's Ellabell plant was announced last year and could grow to 8,500 employees and begin producing vehicles as early as 2024.
- Suppliers to the Hyundai plant have pledged to invest nearly $2.3 billion and hire nearly 5,800 people.
- Daechang Seat opened a factory in Phenix City, Alabama, in 2019.
- The company announced a $23.4 million expansion in Phenix City last year, which includes building a new facility and hiring an additional 300 people.
- Daechang Seat currently employs more than 5,000 people across seven countries.
- Jinsuk Lee, CEO of Daechang Seat Savannah Corp., stated that the company's primary objective is to drive growth through facility expansions to transform DSC into a global entity.
- Workers at the new Georgia plant will earn an average wage of more than $18 per hour, excluding bonuses and benefits.
- Angela Hendrix serves as a spokesperson for the Savannah Economic Development Authority.
- The state of Georgia will pay to train workers for the new facility.
- Daechang Seat could qualify for $10 million in state income tax credits, valued at $4,000 per job over five years, provided workers earn at least $31,300 annually.
- Local officials have granted a 15-year property tax reduction projected to save the company $5.8 million.
- Local officials are selling land for the plant at a reduced rate.
