# Hyundai EV Metaplant

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Construction Nearing Its Finish at Hyundai EV Metaplant Near Savannah : CEG](https://www.constructionequipmentguide.com/construction-nearing-its-finish-at-76b-hyundai-ev-metaplant-near-savannah-ga/65399)  ·  `page_000611`

## Facts

- Hyundai is constructing an electric vehicle assembly factory near Savannah, Georgia.
- Company officials broke ground on the facility in October 2022.
- The project is on pace to begin rolling finished vehicles off the assembly line by fall, roughly two years after the start.
- Test production is already underway in the main plant as construction finishes, according to a July 22 report by the Atlanta Journal-Constitution.
- Hyundai has hired and is training its initial shift of assembly workers.
- Hyundai has a stated goal of introducing 17 new electric vehicle models and manufacturing 1.44 million battery-powered cars annually by 2030.
- Company officials refer to the facility as the "Metaplant," reflecting their view of "the future of mobility."
- Carter Infinger, an elected leader in Bryan County, Georgia, where the plant is located, stated, "Our friends from Korea only know one speed: Full throttle."
- Hyundai is investing $7.6 billion at the 2,906-acre site.
- The assembly facility plans to produce up to 300,000 vehicles per year.
- An EV battery manufacturing plant and a worker training center are also under construction on the site and are scheduled to open in 2025.
- Altogether, the project is the largest in Georgia economic development history.
- The property is located along Interstate 16 behind a truck weigh station near the town of Ellabell, approximately 25 miles west of downtown Savannah.
- A highway interchange is located just west of the site, and another exit is planned immediately to the east with a projected opening date in 2027.
- The property was formerly known as the Bryan County Megasite and was first prepared for development a decade ago when Volvo considered opening a factory in the Savannah area.
- State officials expanded the size of the site in 2021 after selling part of another nearby industrial parcel to Amazon.
- Hyundai inquired about the property in January 2022 and announced plans to build the factory in southeast Georgia within five months.
- Site preparation began in August 2022, and vertical construction started in early 2023.
- The Metaplant will launch operations with Hyundai's best-selling IONIQ 5 EV model.
- The assembly facility is designed for flexibility, moving vehicles through the factory on a conveyor belt.
- Automated guided vehicles will deliver parts and perform tasks alongside workers, whom Hyundai refers to as "metapros."
- The facility's design allows for the production of more than one vehicle model on the same assembly line.
- The plant will eventually manufacture vehicles for Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis brands.
- Hyundai already operates two other factories in the region: one producing Hyundais in Montgomery, Alabama, and another producing Kias in West Point, Georgia.
- The West Point, Georgia facility recently began production of the Kia EV9 automobile.
- Hyundai is also building a second battery factory near Cartersville, Georgia.
- The metro Savannah facility could produce hybrid and plug-in hybrid cars in addition to EVs.
- The Bryan County onsite battery manufacturing facility is intended to shorten the supply chain.
- As part of a $1.8 billion incentives package signed with state and local officials, Hyundai agreed to employ 8,500 workers at the Savannah factory.
- The battery plant near Cartersville is expected to hire an additional 3,500 people.
- Hyundai began hiring within months of announcing the project.
- The company reached 500 production workers as of February and has since grown to over 850 total employees.
- More than 80 percent of the initial hires and 96 percent of production line employees live within 60 miles of the factory site.
- This hiring occurred despite a historically low unemployment rate of 3 percent for the greater Savannah area, according to the Georgia Department of Labor.
- Hyundai recently paused recruitment of assembly workers but continues to seek engineers and other professionals.
- The automaker will resume hiring production personnel once it expands manufacturing beyond one shift.
- Hyundai is training its workers through Georgia Quick Start, a state program specializing in advanced manufacturing.
- Training also occurs at coastal Georgia technical colleges, including Savannah Tech and Ogeechee Tech in Statesboro.
- Georgia Quick Start operates a training center in the Savannah suburb of Pooler and has leased additional space to handle Hyundai hires.
- Hyundai's supplier network spans southeast Georgia and other areas, including metro Atlanta.
- Seventeen suppliers manufacturing components ranging from seats to climate control parts have been announced, with 11 located near the Metaplant.
- The supplier firms are projected to employ about 7,000 workers and invest $2.7 billion in new facilities.
- Hyundai maintained its rapid construction pace despite EV sales underperforming expectations, partly due to federal electric vehicle incentive restrictions.
- Only EVs manufactured in the United States qualify for the $7,500 purchase rebate incentive, though there is a loophole for leased vehicles.
- The IONIQ 5s manufactured in Georgia will be eligible for the rebates once the battery factory becomes operational next year.
- The Georgia-made Hyundais are intended for domestic sale rather than export.
- The Georgia Ports Authority auto terminal in Brunswick, the second-busiest in the country, does not expect a Hyundai-related increase in business once the factory comes online.
- The Georgia Ports Authority is handling imports of parts and materials for the Hyundai plant through its Savannah terminals.
