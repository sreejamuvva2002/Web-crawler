# LGES-HMGMA Battery Cell Manufacturing Joint Venture

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hyundai and LG Energy Solution up US Investment for Battery JV - World-Energy](https://www.world-energy.org/article/35967.html)  ·  `page_001246`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution (LGES) will invest an additional $2 billion in their battery cell manufacturing joint venture (JV).
- The JV is located at the Metaplant in Bryan County, Georgia.
- The additional investment raises the JV's total investment value to more than $4.3 billion.
- The new investment will create another 400 new jobs.
- In May 2023, Hyundai Motor Group and LGES signed a memorandum of understanding establishing LGES as the partner for Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America’s (HMGMA) onsite battery cell manufacturing JV.
- The combined EV manufacturing facility and battery JV represent an estimated $7.59 billion in total investment.
- The combined projects will create 8,500 new jobs for the region over the next eight years.
- The battery cell manufacturing facility has a capacity of 30 GWh.
- At full operations, the facility will support the production of 300,000 EV units annually.
- Hyundai Mobis will assemble battery packs using cells from the Bryan County plant.
- The assembled battery packs will be supplied to Hyundai Motor Group’s U.S. manufacturing facilities.
- The supplied batteries will be used for the production of Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis EV models.
- Georgia State Governor Brian Kemp stated that they broke ground on the largest project in state history in a single year.
- Governor Brian Kemp noted that multiple suppliers were landed across the state for Hyundai’s Metaplant.
- Governor Brian Kemp welcomed LGES to Bryan County.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated the goal is to make Georgia the e-mobility capital of the nation.
- Governor Brian Kemp claimed major investments ultimately go to hardworking Georgians in the form of paychecks, improved schools and infrastructure.
- José Muñoz, president and global COO of Hyundai Motor Company and president and CEO of Hyundai and Genesis Motor North America, stated the incremental investment reflects a continued commitment to create a more sustainable future powered by American workers.
- José Muñoz expressed gratitude for the support of Governor Kemp and communities throughout Georgia.
- Dong-Myung Kim, president and head of the Advanced Automotive Battery Division of LG Energy Solution, stated the investment underscores a dedication to driving America’s EV transition.
- Dong-Myung Kim noted the investment aims to bolster the local economy through the creation of quality jobs.
- Dong-Myung Kim thanked the State of Georgia for its support in bringing top-quality products and operational experience to grow with local communities.
