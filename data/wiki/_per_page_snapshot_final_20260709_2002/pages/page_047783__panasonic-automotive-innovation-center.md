# Panasonic Automotive Innovation Center

**Type:** research_center  ·  **Role:** research_and_development  ·  **Published:** 2012-05-17

**Source:** [Panasonic to establish auto innovation center in Atlanta | The Citizen](https://thecitizen.com/2012/05/17/panasonic-establish-auto-innovation-center-atlanta/)  ·  `page_047783`

## Facts

- Panasonic Automotive Systems Company of America announced on May 4 that it will establish the Panasonic Automotive Innovation Center in the Technology Square area of Midtown Atlanta on the campus of the Georgia Institute of Technology.
- The center will be an extension of the Design Engineering Centers located in Peachtree City, Ga., and Southfield, Mich.
- The center is expected to begin operations at the end of May.
- The facility will serve as an incubator for next-generation automotive infotainment technologies.
- The new Panasonic Automotive Innovation Center facility will be located in Centergy One, a LEED Silver-certified office tower.
- The Panasonic offices are designed to maximize natural light and enable open collaboration for projected 40 software, electrical, systems, mechanical, and other additional support personnel.
- The center expects to offer at least 15 co-op positions a year to Georgia Tech students.
- Automakers and suppliers like Panasonic prioritize developing sophisticated connected car systems with internet and smartphone connectivity due to consumer demand for seamless in-vehicle lifestyles.
- Tom Gebhardt, President of Panasonic Automotive Systems Company of America, stated the Atlanta location allows the company to tap into the engineering talent of one of the fastest growing high-technology areas in the nation.
- Atlanta currently hosts over 13,000 technology companies employing nearly 200,000 technology workers.
- A primary driver for locating the Innovation Center in Technology Square was Panasonic's interest in enhancing its partnership with the Georgia Institute of Technology.
- Panasonic aims to develop a strong partnership with the Georgia Tech Enterprise Innovation Institute and the University’s Division of Professional Practice for employing co-ops.
- Atlanta Mayor Kasim Reed stated that Panasonic's decision to open an office in Atlanta testifies to the city's strength as a technology hub, citing its educated workforce and high quality of life.
- Stephen E. Cross, executive vice president for research at Georgia Institute of Technology, noted collaboration with state and local economic development organizations to identify Georgia Tech resources supporting Panasonic's objectives.
- A key goal of the partnership is involving Georgia Tech students in Panasonic’s research and development program to provide real-world industrial experience.
- Hans Gant, senior vice president of economic development for the Metro Atlanta Chamber, stated the R&D center showcases Atlanta’s strengths as a global technology hub and congratulated Panasonic on new jobs and collaborative efforts with Georgia Tech.
- The Metro Atlanta Chamber collaborated with partners at the Georgia Department of Economic Development, Invest Atlanta, Georgia Power, and Georgia Tech to help bring about the project.
- Chris Cummiskey, commissioner of the Georgia Department of Economic Development, highlighted how Georgia’s pipeline of knowledge-based talent helps global businesses like Panasonic grow and remain competitive.
- Panasonic Automotive Systems Company of America is based in Peachtree City with Detroit-area operations located in Southfield, Mich.
- Panasonic Automotive Systems Company of America is a division company of Secaucus, New Jersey-based Panasonic Corporation of North America, the principal North American subsidiary of Panasonic Corporation of Osaka, Japan.
