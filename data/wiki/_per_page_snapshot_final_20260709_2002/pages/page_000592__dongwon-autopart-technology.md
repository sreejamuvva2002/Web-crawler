# Dongwon Autopart Technology

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-02

**Source:** [Auto supplier to invest $30M in rural Georgia county](https://www.ajc.com/business/2026/02/korean-automotive-supplier-promises-200-jobs-in-rural-georgia-county/)  ·  `page_000592`

## Facts

- Korean automotive supplier Dongwon Autopart Technology plans to invest $30 million into a new production facility in South Georgia.
- The project promises to create 200 jobs.
- Dongwon Autopart Technology manufactures parts including door, seat, and battery frames.
- The new facility will be Dongwon's second Georgia location, situated in Emanuel County.
- Emanuel County is located approximately 175 miles southeast of Atlanta.
- Gov. Brian Kemp announced the project on Wednesday.
- Ken Warnock, CEO of the Swainsboro-Emanuel County Chamber of Commerce and Development Authority, stated that it is literally the biggest new industrial development project ever in Emanuel County.
- Emanuel County has historically been known as a hub for metal working.
- Dongwon Autopart Technology was drawn to the county specifically because of its local metal-working expertise.
