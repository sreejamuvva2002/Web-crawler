# Volkswagen Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-03

**Source:** [Volkswagen Canada Offers the ID.4 EV with a CO2 Heat Pump](https://naturalrefrigerants.com/news/volkswagen-canada-offers-the-id-4-ev-with-a-co2-heat-pump/)  ·  `page_055685`

## Facts

- More than 1 million Volkswagen electric vehicles (EVs) on the road are equipped with CO2 (R744) heat pumps.
- A handful of these EVs with CO2 heat pumps are located in Canada.
- Volkswagen Canada has offered the ID.4 model since 2021.
- Many ID.4 vehicles sold in Canada have been equipped with a CO2 heat pump.
- The CO2 heat pump unit is manufactured by Hanon Systems, a South Korean provider of automotive thermal management systems.
- The heat pump unit features a 5.5kW (1.6TR) CO2 compressor.
- The optional heat pump costs CA$1,500 (US$1,057/€918).
- The ID.4 model is built on the Volkswagen Group’s modular electric drive matrix (MEB) platform.
- The MEB platform supports a wide range of EVs including the ID.3, ID.5, Audi Q4 e-tron, Q4 Sportback e-tron, Škoda Enyaq, Enyaq Coupé, Cupra Born, and Tavascan.
- These vehicles are all available with an optional R744 heat pump.
- Volkswagen sold 4,600 ID.4s in Canada in 2025.
- The first ID.4s were manufactured in Germany and imported to Canada.
- Since 2022, Volkswagen has built the ID.4 at its plant in Chattanooga, Tennessee.
- In April 2026, Volkswagen announced it would end ID.4 production at the Chattanooga facility.
- The decision is part of a strategy to prioritize production of what the company called “high-demand vehicles.”
- A future version of the ID.4 is planned for the North American market, with further details to be announced later.
- Because Volkswagen sells CO2-equipped vehicles in Canada, its dealers are required to have R744 air-conditioning service equipment to maintain them.
- In the U.S., Volkswagen dealers are not subject to this requirement as they do not offer EVs with CO2 heat pumps.
- Audi dealers in the United States are apparently required to have CO2 service equipment.
- Audi sells four EVs in the U.S., none of which are currently offered with CO2 heat pumps.
- At the ATMO MAC Summit 2025 × TU Berlin, Volkswagen shared how it is improving the efficiency of its CO2 heat pumps.
- Johannes Schwerter, a Test Engineer in Volkswagen’s R&D division, stated that the first-generation system was “over-designed” to account for higher temperatures and pressures CO2 operates at.
- The first CO2 compressor weighed 7.6kg [16.8lbs].
- A second version of the compressor has been introduced that is a half kilogram [1.1lbs] lighter, bringing the weight to 7.1kg [15.7lbs].
- The design of the gas cooler is being examined to address ice building caused by harvesting heat from ambient air.
- A vertical rather than horizontal orientation of the gas cooler tubes is being considered to allow melted frost to run off the unit.
- Professor Stefan Elbel, Head of the Department of Heat Transfer and Heat Conversion at Technische Universität Berlin, stated that CO2 and propane (R290) are critical for the future of MAC.
- The shift to natural refrigerants is driven by environmental necessity and regulatory forces.
- In May 2026, Sanden announced it is co-developing a propane-based integrated thermal management system for EVs with a “major” Chinese automaker.
- China is expected to lead the adoption of natural refrigerants in EVs, according to S&P Global Mobility.
- Vehicle HVAC manufacturer Aurora Netherlands has developed a dual-secondary loop propane heat pump HVAC system for electric buses.
- Aurora Netherlands had sold 165 units as of November 2025.
- In 2024, Ford released an electric version of its Explorer SUV with an optional CO2 heat pump.
- The electric Explorer is also built on Volkswagen’s MEB platform.
