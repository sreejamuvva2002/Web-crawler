# Freyr Battery

## Facts

- Freyr Battery is a Norwegian battery company that could receive more than $358 million in grants, tax breaks, and other inducements from state and local governments.
- The planned Coweta County factory will produce giant energy storage systems for utilities and other clients.
- Details of the incentives were obtained by The Atlanta Journal-Constitution through the Georgia Open Records Act.
- Freyr announced plans in November for a $2.57 billion factory in Coweta County that will employ 724 workers making energy storage systems and lithium-ion batteries.
- Freyr is among many companies moving to Georgia to produce electrified products, which is a priority for state leaders.
- Freyr cited Georgia's education system and ability to recruit clean-tech workers as deciding factors for choosing Georgia over more than two dozen states for its first North American factory.
- The package of incentives offered by Georgia and Coweta leaders helped tip the scales for Freyr's location decision.
- Greg LeRoy, executive director of the incentives watchdog Good Jobs First, expressed concern that Coweta County residents may have to cover local government costs for Freyr's new factory until the tax abatement ends.
- LeRoy stated that the county will need to hire more teachers, handle more trash collection, widen roads, and manage more 911 calls due to the factory.
- Pat Wilson, commissioner of the Georgia Department of Economic Development, stated that Freyr represents a new niche in the electric battery and vehicle ecosystem and helps diversify Georgia's sector.
- Wilson noted that Freyr's technology is vital to the transition to electrification and fills a gap in both Georgia's and the U.S.'s battery sectors.
- Freyr CEO Tom Einar Jensen stated that Georgia's increasing talent pool cemented Coweta County as the company's final location choice.
- Freyr stated that talent was the most important factor for selecting its first U.S. plant location.
- The company acknowledged that the total package of financial incentives for its "Giga America" project played an important role in the decision.
- The largest component of Freyr's incentives is approximately $228 million in local property tax breaks spread over 20 years.
- Coweta County offered Freyr a $20 million grant.
- Georgia will provide nearly $90 million in sales tax exemptions for qualified machinery and construction materials for the factory.
- Freyr will receive nearly $11 million in state tax credits for new jobs created.
- Georgia will provide a $7 million state grant to offset land costs.
- The total incentive package could increase in value over time based on future expenditures.
- No specific value was assigned to potential tax breaks for clean room equipment, manufacturing energy use, and other incentives based on future expenditures.
- The planned factory, named Giga America, will be located near Amazon and Shopify distribution centers off I-85 near Newnan.
- Sarah Jacobs, president of the Coweta County Development Authority, stated that Freyr will add industrial diversity to an area dominated by logistics and shipping companies.
- As part of the incentive package, Freyr is required to employ 724 workers at the new factory with an average salary exceeding $60,000.
- This average salary represents 127% of the average wage in Coweta County.
- Coweta County and Arizona were the final two location options considered by Freyr.
- Freyr's incentive package is smaller than those offered to Hyundai Motor Group ($1.8 billion) and Rivian ($1.5 billion) for their EV factories.
- Greg LeRoy questioned the rationale for financially incentivizing job creation in Georgia while unemployment remains near record lows.
- LeRoy stated that most new hires will likely come from outside the area or enter the labor market for the first time.
- LeRoy calculated that the incentives average roughly $490,000 per job and questioned whether each employee could generate enough local spending and tax revenue to justify it.
- Freyr was founded in 2018 and is simultaneously constructing a plant in Norway.
- Freyr's energy storage systems utilize lithium-ion batteries to store electricity from renewable sources like wind and solar.
- Freyr, which has been publicly traded since last year, reported a $93.9 million net loss in the third quarter.
- Koch Industries supports Freyr and its Georgia project.
- Koch Industries has become a major player in battery technology, and the project qualifies for incentives under President Joe Biden's Inflation Reduction Act.
- The 870,000-square-foot facility is scheduled to open in 2026.
- Phase one of the project will focus on initial battery cell production.
- A second phase of cell production is expected to be operational by 2029.
- Pat Wilson stated that Freyr's presence will help attract suppliers of battery components like cathodes and anthodes.
- Qcells confirmed a $2.5 billion expansion of its Georgia production on Wednesday.
- Officials described this Qcells expansion as the largest-ever investment in U.S. clean energy manufacturing.

- The total value of the incentive package is $358.5 million.
- The package includes a $7 million grant from the Regional Economic Business Assistance (REBA) program.
- The package includes $3.3 million from the Georgia Quick Start program.
- The incentive values listed are rounded.
