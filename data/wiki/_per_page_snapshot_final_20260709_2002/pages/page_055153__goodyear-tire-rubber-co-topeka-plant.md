# Goodyear Tire & Rubber Co. Topeka Plant

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Field Update: AAM Visits the Goodyear Plant in Kansas - Alliance for American Manufacturing](https://www.americanmanufacturing.org/blog/field-update-aam-visits-the-goodyear-plant-in-kansas/)  ·  `page_055153`

## Facts

- AAM Field Coordinators Mike Mitchell and Meghan Hasse recently toured the Goodyear Plant in Topeka, Kan.
- The Goodyear Tire & Rubber Co. plant in Topeka originally opened in 1944 to help with the American war effort during World War II.
- The federal government picked Topeka for the plant because of its location near several military installations and because it is 140 miles from the exact center of the United States, making it safer from attack by foreign armies.
- When the war ended in 1945, the federal government sold the plant to Goodyear to manage.
- Since its sale, the plant has served as one of the biggest private employers in the state.
- At one point, the Topeka plant made every tire that Goodyear produced, including for automobiles, bicycles, and aircraft.
- The plant hit its peak employment at about 4,400 people in the 1970s.
- Today, about 1,550 employees work at the plant on a 6 2/3 schedule, meaning operations run 24 hours a day except for a brief period on Sundays.
- Workers at the plant make a range of tires, including for buses, off-road vehicles, medium radial truck tires for semi-trucks, and military vehicles.
- Goodyear also makes the largest tire in the world at the plant — the 59/80R63, which is 13 feet tall and weighs close to 12,000 pounds.
- The Topeka plant is the second largest tire plant in the world at 3 million square feet, which equals 69 acres under roof.
- Topeka’s workers are represented by the USW Local 307 and the NCFO SEIU Local 7.
- Collectively, workers at the plant make $150 million each year in wages and benefits.
- More than $24 million a year returns to the community in local spending from employees, not including capital projects.
- Employees have given more than $1.3 million to the United Way since 2008.
- Goodyear Topeka is one of the largest supporters of Junior Achievement in the state.
- Employees hold four blood drives every year.
- At one time, it looked like Goodyear would close the plant.
- Since 2008, Goodyear has invested more than $600 million in this plant.
- Company officials maintain that Goodyear is committed to “making its operations work in North America.”
