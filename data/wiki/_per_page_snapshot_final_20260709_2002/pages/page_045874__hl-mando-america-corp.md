# HL Mando America Corp.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2024-08-06

**Source:** [HL Mando America Corp. recognized for support of Auburn mechanical engineering](https://www.eng.auburn.edu/news/2024/08/hl-mando-gift.html)  ·  `page_045874`

## Facts

- HL Mando America Corp. was recognized for its support of Auburn University, the Samuel Ginn College of Engineering, and the Department of Mechanical Engineering.
- HL Mando committed $200,000 to support up to four graduate students in an accelerated bachelor’s and master’s degree program in mechanical engineering at Auburn University.
- The funded graduate students must have a special interest in automotive technologies.
- HL Mando is based in Seoul, South Korea, and operates as an electric vehicle and autonomous driving solutions company specializing in core components for vehicle safety.
- HL Mando is a global leader in key enabling technologies for future mobility, including redundancy concept and by-wire technology.
- HL Mando is strengthening eco-friendly vehicle solutions such as e-Drive and hydrogen fuel cell converters.
- HL Mando America is the US-based subsidiary of HL Mando.
- HL Mando America has manufacturing operations in Opelika and Hogansville, Georgia.
- HL Mando America produces brake calipers, electric steering systems, and suspension systems.
- HL Mando America executives CEO Bruce Kim and Head of Americas Region Yun Lee attended a reception on Monday, August 5, hosted by Mario Eden, dean of engineering.
- During the reception, HL Mando presented Auburn with a ceremonial check for its educational support commitment.
- Yun Lee stated that HL Mando considers it a blessing to have Auburn University in their vicinity and has had great experiences partnering with the Department of Mechanical Engineering and the Office of International Programs.
- Yun Lee expressed hope that HL Mando’s contributions will help prepare tomorrow’s leaders in the automotive industry, both locally and globally.
- HL Mando has established a long-standing relationship with Song-Yul Choe, the William B. and Elizabeth Reed Professor in Auburn’s Department of Mechanical Engineering.
- Song-Yul Choe’s research interests include modeling/control of power sources, piezoelectric devices, high powered connectors, solenoid valves, and tires.
- Mario Eden, dean of engineering, stated that industry support from leaders like HL Mando is vital for preparing graduate students to enter the workforce as leaders.
