# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-05-20

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Group to invest $5.54 billion in an electric vehicle and battery manufacturing facility in Bryan County](https://www.wtoc.com/2022/05/20/hyundai-motor-group-invest-554-billion-electric-vehicle-battery-manufacturing-facility-bryan-county/)  ·  `page_021248`

## Facts

- Governor Brian Kemp and Hyundai Motor Group Executive Char Euisun Chung announced that Hyundai Motor Group will open its first fully dedicated electric vehicle and battery manufacturing facility in Georgia.
- The facility will be located at the megasite in Bryan County, Ga.
- Hyundai Motor Group will invest $5.54 billion to open the factory.
- Other suppliers will invest around another $1 billion in the project.
- The project will deliver approximately 8,100 new jobs to the area.
- Construction on the facility is expected to begin in January 2023.
- Full production is expected in the first half of 2025.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated the project will be a huge economic boost to Bryan County and the entire state of Georgia.
- Governor Kemp said, “This is going to be the largest economic plan we have ever done in the state’s history. From a state perspective, it’s just incredible even bigger than the Rivian plant, that we announced several months ago. 8,100 jobs, $5.5 billion in initial investment. We know there will be an addition $1billion dollars from Suppliers who are going to bring, hundreds and hundreds, if not thousands of other jobs.”
- Members of the Richmond Hill Bryan County Chamber of Commerce expressed excitement about the announcement.
- Chamber chairwoman Emily Cannon noted that new businesses directly related to the EV plant at the megasite will be expected, along with businesses for workers such as restaurants.
- Emily Cannon said, “And then after the direct industry comes, then is when you get additional restaurants and more mom and pop shops that join in and fill in with the community. So we can expect down the road to see this slow and homogenous growth that comes around a large investment like this.”
- County leaders stated that economic incentives for Hyundai are not finalized yet but should be announced in June.
- The megasite development footprint grew by about 700 acres to fit development needs following last year's land purchase announcement.
- Trip Tollison, President and CEO of the Savannah Economic Development Authority (SEDA), explained the megasite expansion.
- Trip Tollison said, “We’re going to, part of the announcement is an extra billion dollars of investment with a bunch of jobs attached to that that are coming as well. So we’re still getting all those details out. But we’ll see a lot of supplier visits over the next several months as they look to also locate here as well.”
- The overall job count and investment associated with the venture will grow over time.
- The Kia plant in West Point, Ga., serves as an example of how automotive plants boost local economies.
- Mayor Steve Tramell noted that West Point's downtown was 90 percent empty fifteen years ago but is now 90 percent full due to the Kia plant.
- Mayor Tramell said, “Fifteen years ago, this downtown was 90 percent empty. Empty shops every where you could see. Now, it’s 90 percent full.”
- West Point has a nighttime population of about 3,600 that swells to 10-12,000 daily due to plant workers.
- The Kia plant brought restaurants on nearly every block and several hiring agencies to West Point.
- Local businesses in West Point adapted to the plant's presence, such as shoe stores selling steel-toed shoes to plant employees.
- Communities along the road from the Kia plant have seen economic growth.
- Mayor Tramell said, “Everything you plan for, you get something else. It might not be what you want it to be. But what comes in, is a good thing......”
- Rep. Buddy Carter attended the announcement and released a statement supporting Hyundai's choice of Georgia.
- Rep. Carter said, “I am thrilled that Hyundai chose Georgia as the home for its latest factory. Not only will this investment bring thousands of jobs to the First District, but it will also boost America’s manufacturing economy, spurring innovation and global economic competitiveness within the region. With this investment, Georgia will be a leader in the electric vehicle industry. The future of transportation is right here in Bryan County.”
- Georgia Senator Ossoff released a statement supporting the announcement.
- Senator Ossoff led an economic delegation to South Korea back in November.
- Senator Ossoff said, “When I led an Economic Delegation to South Korea last fall, attracting Hyundai’s investment in Georgia was a key goal. This multibillion-dollar opportunity for Georgia will create thousands of jobs and grow Georgia’s reputation as a world leader in automotive and clean energy manufacturing.”
