# Toyota Motor Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-10-09

**Source:** [Toyota is set to release the "world's first" all-solid-state batteries for electric vehicles | УНН](https://unn.ua/en/news/toyota-to-unveil-solid-state-battery-electric-vehicle)  ·  `page_013664`

## Facts

- Plans to release its first electric vehicle with a solid-state battery in 2027-2028.
- Promises increased range and faster charging for the new EV technology.
- Collaborating with Sumitomo Metal Mining Co. for the mass production of cathode materials.
- Aims to achieve the world's first practical application of all-solid-state batteries in electric vehicles.
- New batteries use a cathode, anode, and solid electrolyte instead of existing liquid electrolyte solutions.
- Claims the next-generation battery technology unlocks potential for reduced size, increased power output, and extended lifespan.
- First electric car with a semi-solid-state battery went on sale on August 14, 2025.
- Has been developing cathode materials for solid-state EV batteries with Sumitomo since 2021.
- Using Sumitomo Metal Mining's proprietary powder synthesis technology to develop a high-strength cathode material.
- New agreement was concluded after Toyota received METI certification for the production of new batteries in Japan last September.
- Collaborating with several partners in Japan, including oil giant Idemitsu Kosan, to bring new EV batteries to market.
- Idemitsu announced plans to build a large plant for the production of lithium sulfide, a raw material used in EV batteries.
- Once operational, Idemitsu's plant will be able to produce 1,000 tons of lithium sulfide per year.
- Idemitsu also plans to begin mass production of all-solid-state batteries in 2027.
- New batteries are part of Japan's plans to establish a domestic supply chain and reduce dependence on China and South Korea.
- Toyota is among several Japanese companies collectively investing $7 billion (1 trillion yen) in battery production domestically.
- Other companies betting on the new technology include Mercedes-Benz, BMW, Volkswagen, and Honda.
- Mercedes put the first car with a lithium-metal battery on the road in February.
- Last month, Mercedes drove an EQS equipped with solid-state batteries almost 1205 km.
- Mercedes technical director Markus Schäfer calls the new battery technology a turning point in the history of electric vehicles.
- Mercedes plans to launch mass production of solid-state batteries by the end of the decade.
- CATL and BYD, which already dominate the global battery market, plan to introduce new technology around 2027.
- SAIC MG unveiled the new MG4 in August, calling it the world's first mass-produced electric vehicle with semi-solid-state batteries.
