# Stellantis

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-01-10

**Source:** [Stellantis to manufacture electric air taxis for Archer Aviation | Smart Cities Dive](https://www.smartcitiesdive.com/news/stellantis-manufacture-electric-air-taxis-for-archer-aviation-eVTOL/640072/)  ·  `page_016883`

## Facts

- Stellantis announced it would help Archer Aviation produce its flagship electric air taxi, which is designed to carry passengers short distances in urban areas.
- Through an expanded partnership, Stellantis will help stand up Archer’s planned manufacturing facility in Covington, Georgia.
- Stellantis will contribute advanced manufacturing technology, expertise and personnel to the project.
- Stellantis will contribute up to $150 million in capital.
- The collaboration is meant to enable Archer to quickly scale to meet its production targets without spending hundreds of millions of dollars.
- Stellantis and Archer plan to start manufacturing the aircraft in 2024.
- Stellantis is expected to mass produce Archer’s electric vertical takeoff and landing (eVTOL) aircraft Midnight as the startup’s exclusive contract manufacturer.
- The automaker has been a strategic partner to Archer since 2020, allowing Archer to leverage its manufacturing, supply chain and design expertise.
- The expanded partnership comes as automakers increasingly position themselves as mobility companies rather than just auto companies.
- Michelle Krebs, executive analyst for Cox Automotive, noted that Stellantis CEO Carlos Tavares stated at CES this week that his company is becoming a mobility tech company.
- Historically, automotive companies have ventured into aircraft development; Henry Ford developed aircraft and Chrysler, part of Stellantis, once owned Gulfstream.
- An investor in Archer since 2021, Stellantis is now freeing up $150 million in equity capital for use by Archer so long as certain milestones are reached.
- Carlos Tavares stated that deepening the partnership with Archer as a strategic investor demonstrates how Stellantis is pushing boundaries to provide sustainable freedom of mobility from the road to sky.
- The aircraft is designed for back-to-back short distance trips, with a range of 100 miles and a charging time of approximately 10 minutes.
- United Airlines has partnered with Archer on its first commercial route from downtown Manhattan to Newark Liberty International Airport once the air taxis are operational.
- With Stellantis’ help, Archer will build out a 350,000-square-foot facility capable of producing 650 aircraft per year.
- The facility is expected to be completed in the first half of 2024.
- The facility will have room to expand to 550,000 square feet and support production of up to 2,300 aircraft annually.
- Carlos Tavares stated that supporting Archer with manufacturing expertise is another example of how Stellantis will lead the way the world moves.
