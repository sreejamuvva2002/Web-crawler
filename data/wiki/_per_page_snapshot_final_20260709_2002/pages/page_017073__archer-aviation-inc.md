# Archer Aviation Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-03-31

**Source:** [Archer Aviation - Archer Announces First Quarter 2026 Results, Highlighting Record FAA Certification Progress With Initial US Operations Expected In 2026](https://investors.archer.com/news/news-details/2026/Archer-Announces-First-Quarter-2026-Results-Highlighting-Record-FAA-Certification-Progress-With-Initial-US-Operations-Expected-In-2026/)  ·  `page_017073`

## Facts

- US operations for the Midnight aircraft are expected to begin this year under the White House’s eVTOL Integration Pilot Program (eIPP) and in preparation for the LA28 Olympic Games.
- Archer expects Midnight operations in American cities to begin this year as part of the White House’s eIPP and in preparation to serve as the Official Air Taxi Provider of the LA28 Olympic Games, in coordination with the DOT and FAA.
- Archer was recently selected as an air taxi partner in 3 winning applications encompassing 8 states, including Florida, Texas, and New York.
- In April, Archer became the first eVTOL company to close Phase 3 of the FAA’s 4-phase Type Certification process for the Midnight aircraft.
- Archer has been driving progress in Phase 4 of the FAA certification process, where Midnight's compliance with FAA airworthiness requirements will be demonstrated through formal testing and analysis.
- Archer’s flight test program expanded this quarter, with piloted VTOL and CTOL flights across its expanded fleet occurring nearly every day, often multiple times a day.
- Archer took over operations of Hawthorne Airport in LA to develop the site into an innovation hub for next-generation aviation technologies and anchor planned LA air taxi operations.
- Hawthorne Airport is located near LAX and several major LA sports and entertainment venues.
- Archer continues work alongside Anduril on a dual-use, hybrid, autonomous aircraft, aiming to deliver one of the most sophisticated vertical lift platforms in this category.
- Archer anticipates beginning to win phased government awards for its dual-use aircraft program this year.
- NVIDIA is integrating its IGX Thor platform to power safety-capable onboard compute and autonomy-ready flight systems for Archer's AI stack.
- Starlink will deliver high-speed, low-latency LEO satellite connectivity for Midnight aircraft.
- Palantir was downselected as a finalist for the FAA's SMART AI project, which is instrumental to the DOT's ~$20B air traffic control modernization effort.
- Archer ended Q1 2026 with $1,775.9 million of cash, cash equivalents, and short-term investments on its balance sheet and an additional $7.3 million of restricted cash.
- Q1 2026 cash, cash equivalents, and short-term investments decreased by $188.8 million from Q4 2025, primarily due to $149.1 million used in operating activities and $32.6 million used for property and equipment purchases.
- Q1 2026 Revenue increased by $1.3 million from Q4 2025 to $1.6 million as operations expanded at the Hawthorne Airport in LA.
- Q1 2026 Operating Expenses increased by $21.5 million from Q4 2025, driven by continued investment in expanding flight testing, certification efforts, and production activities for the Midnight aircraft, along with design and development efforts for the hybrid aircraft.
- Q1 2026 Net Loss increased by $28.8 million from Q4 2025, primarily driven by the $21.5 million increase in operating expenses, a $7.3 million decrease in non-cash gain within other income (expense), net, and a $1.2 million decrease in interest income, net, offset by a $1.3 million increase in revenue generated.
- Q1 2026 Adjusted EBITDA was a loss of $172.5 million, which falls within the guidance range of $160 million to $180 million.
- Archer’s financial estimates for Q2 2026 project an Adjusted EBITDA loss of $170 million to $200 million.
- Founder and CEO Adam Goldstein stated that defense and AI software efforts are advancing quickly, opening up a bigger future for the company beyond air taxis.
