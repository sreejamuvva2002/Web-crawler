# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-23

**Source:** [Georgia Builds on Record Momentum While Advancing Long-Term Growth - Expansion Solutions](https://www.expansionsolutionsmagazine.com/georgia-economic-development-long-term-growth/)  ·  `page_009036`

## Facts

- Georgia’s economic development strategy has been driven by consistency, collaboration, and competitiveness for more than a decade.
- The state maintains a steady approach anchored by disciplined fiscal policy, targeted infrastructure and workforce investment, and a focus on business climate benefits.
- Pratt & Whitney announced a $200 million expansion in Columbus, following a prior expansion that added 400 new jobs and 81,000 square feet to its Columbus Engine Center.
- Georgia has been ranked the No. 1 state for business for 12 consecutive years.
- State leaders approved legislation reducing Georgia’s corporate income tax rate to 4.99 percent, retroactive to January 1, 2026.
- The tax legislation establishes a structured pathway for annual reductions of 0.125 percent until the rate reaches 3.99 percent.
- Existing incentives include the Job Tax Credit and other performance-based incentives.
- Georgia Power’s Integrated Resource Plan supports meeting projected growth of 8,500 megawatts by 2030.
- Utilities are diversifying the energy mix with investments in solar capacity, storage, and Plant Vogtle, the first new nuclear plant to open in the United States in three decades.
- UCB announced a $2 billion investment in a new manufacturing facility in Georgia, creating 330 jobs.
- Unified Legacy announced a $125 million investment in Macon-Bibb County, which will create 500 new jobs.
- Qcells is expanding its solar manufacturing footprint in northwest Georgia, strengthening the state’s role in the clean energy supply chain.
- Salesforce maintains a significant presence in Atlanta, with Site Lead Tanya Counter highlighting local talent and deepening investment.
- Glytec CEO Patrick Cua cited favorable business environment, global connectivity, and high quality of living as reasons for selecting Georgia.
- Duracell located its global research and development headquarters in Atlanta.
- HK USA CEO Michael Holley credited workforce partners including Georgia Quick Start, the Technical College System of Georgia, and the Choose Columbus retention program.
- Georgia Quick Start has been recognized by Area Development magazine as the No. 1 workforce training program for 14 consecutive years.
- The General Assembly passed HB 1302 (Education and Workforce Strategy Act) to strengthen coordination across agencies, align workforce planning with employer demand, and expand apprenticeship pathways.
- Georgia’s infrastructure includes the Ports of Savannah and Brunswick, Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport, and an extensive interstate and rail system.
- A Georgia Tech study released in April found that the Port of Savannah saves shippers more than $1,000 per container to Atlanta, Memphis, and Nashville compared to West Coast gateways.
- Independent research by Georgia Tech’s Supply Chain and Logistics Institute estimated shipping through Savannah offers a 32 percent cost savings over the West Coast.
- The GRAD (Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development) program and RSDI (Rural Site Development Initiative) prepare industrial sites in advance to reduce development timelines.
- Preciball USA utilized RSDI funding on a GRAD-certified site in coastal Georgia, where local authorities pre-built a spec building that shaved months off the development timeline.
- Atlanta’s airport connects the state to more than 80 international and 160 domestic destinations via nonstop service.
