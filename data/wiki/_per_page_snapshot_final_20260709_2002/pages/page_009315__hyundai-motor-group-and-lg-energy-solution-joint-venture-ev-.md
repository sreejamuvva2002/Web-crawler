# Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution Joint Venture EV Battery Plant

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Work begins on EV battery plant in Indonesia](https://www.aa.com.tr/en/asia-pacific/work-begins-on-ev-battery-plant-in-indonesia/2365290)  ·  `page_009315`

## Facts

- Construction on a $1.1 billion plant to make batteries for electric vehicles (EVs) started in Indonesia's Karawang New Industry City, near the capital Jakarta, on Wednesday.
- Indonesian President Joko Widodo spoke at a news briefing after the ground-breaking ceremony.
- President Joko Widodo stated the plant is the first EV battery facility in Southeast Asia.
- The facility is a joint venture by Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution.
- President Widodo said the factory is a manifestation of the government's seriousness to tap its rich nickel reserves.
- Indonesia holds 24% of the world's total nickel reserves.
- President Widodo stated Indonesia will become the main producer of nickel-based goods, adding that the factory will attract investment.
- The Indonesian government fully supports the development of the battery and electric vehicle industry ecosystem.
- The Indonesian government continues to roll out structural reforms to ensure ease in doing business.
- Jong-hyun Kim, president and CEO of LG Energy Solution, said today marks a significant step in starting a new era of the EV battery industry in Indonesia with the establishment of its first battery cell manufacturing plant in the country.
- Through the joint venture partnership, the companies stated they are now a step closer to establishing the world’s first EV comprehensive supply chain.
- According to a joint statement by the two companies, plant construction will be completed by the first half of 2023.
- Mass production of battery cells in the new facility is expected to commence in the first half of 2024.
- When fully operational, the facility is expected to annually produce 10-gigawatt hours (GWh) of NCMA (nickel-cobalt-manganese-aluminum) lithium-ion battery cells to power more than 150,000 EVs.
- The facility will be ready to increase its production capacity up to 30 GWh to meet future growth.
