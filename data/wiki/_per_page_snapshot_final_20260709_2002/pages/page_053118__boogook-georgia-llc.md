# Boogook Georgia, LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-06

**Source:** [Korean Automotive Supplier Boogook Industries to Redevelop Existing Building in Macon - Middle Georgia CEO](https://middlegeorgiaceo.com/features/2023/06/korean-automotive-supplier-boogook-industries-redevelop-existing-building-macon-georgia/)  ·  `page_053118`

## Facts

- Boogook Georgia, LLC, a Korean automotive supplier specializing in fluid-carrying systems for vehicles, announced it will launch its first U.S. location in Macon, Ga.
- The company will redevelop an existing building within the Macon-Bibb County Industrial Authority (MBCIA) Industrial Park at 2600 Weaver Road.
- Boogook Georgia plans $300,000 in leasehold improvements at the former B&W property.
- The company will make a total capital investment of $6.8 million to support its operation.
- Joong Suk Oh, CEO of Boogook Georgia, LLC, stated that leadership recognized a unique opportunity to support Korean automotive manufacturing partners more locally.
- Expanding operations to Macon will drive the company's ability to serve the Southeast U.S., according to leadership.
- The decision to locate in Macon was driven in part by proximity to Hyundai Motor's $5.5 billion Metaplant America in Savannah and Kia's West Point manufacturing facility.
- The location at 2600 Weaver Road offers quick highway access via I-16, which is conveniently located less than two miles from the company's new site.
- This strategic positioning ensures seamless connectivity to key transportation routes, further enhancing the company's ability to serve its customers effectively with improved access to critical parts.
- Macon-Bibb County Mayor Lester M. Miller stated that Georgia's location and highway access make it a prime spot for industries needing quick product movement.
- Mayor Lester M. Miller welcomed Boogook Georgia as the newest member of Team Macon-Bibb.
- Boogook Georgia will create 80 full-time jobs upon opening in 2024, with additional jobs expected later.
- The new roles will include professional positions in management, manufacturing, assembly, quality and assurance, and delivery functions within the automotive sector.
- Stephen Adams, Executive Director of the MBCIA, stated that Boogook's location decision reinforces the value of growing regional automotive industry partners.
- The MBCIA views Middle Georgia as an extremely attractive and competitive area for automotive companies and anticipates the industry will drive future economic and job growth.
- Boogook Georgia, LLC is a subsidiary of Boogook Industries CO., LTD, which is headquartered in Ulsan, South Korea.
- The parent company is a global leader in the automotive tube industry, specializing in parts and materials that carry fluids within vehicle systems.
- Boogook Industries supplies products and services to major global automakers, including Hyundai Motor Corp. and Kia Motor Corp.
