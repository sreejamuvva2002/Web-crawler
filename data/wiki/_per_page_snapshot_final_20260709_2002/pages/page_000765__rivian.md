# Rivian

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-08-20

**Source:** [Rivian moving forward on $5B Georgia EV plant | Construction Dive](https://www.constructiondive.com/news/rivian-georgia-plant-construction-restart/758066/)  ·  `page_000765`

## Facts

- Rivian will host two special events next month to mark the restart of construction of its $5 billion Georgia electric vehicle factory.
- A community event is scheduled for Sept. 14.
- A formal kickoff ceremony will be held on Sept. 16 to celebrate the partnership with Georgia and mark the planned resumption of construction in 2026.
- The Sept. 16 kickoff ceremony will be attended by stakeholders, government officials, and Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp.
- The Georgia plant is located near Social Circle.
- The facility is expected to begin production in 2028.
- The facility will have an annual production capacity of approximately 200,000 units.
- In March 2024, Rivian announced it was halting construction on the Georgia factory to save money.
- The company decided to shift production of its upcoming R2 SUV to its plant in Normal, Illinois, to expedite sales.
- Rivian secured final approval for a $6.6 billion Department of Energy loan in January.
- The DOE loan funds are available through September 2028 but cannot be accessed until construction resumes on the Georgia factory.
- In July, Rivian announced it would open a new East Coast headquarters in Atlanta later this year.
- The Atlanta headquarters will initially employ 100 people and ultimately house 500 workers to support operations in Georgia.
