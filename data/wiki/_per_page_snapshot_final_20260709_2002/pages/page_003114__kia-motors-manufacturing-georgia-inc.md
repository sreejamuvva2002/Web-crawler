## Facts

- An application was submitted to the Executive Secretary of the Foreign-Trade Zones Board by the Georgia Foreign-Trade Zone, Inc., grantee of FTZ 26, requesting temporary/interim manufacturing (T/IM) authority within FTZ 26 at the Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia, Inc. facility in West Point, Georgia.
- The application was filed on May 7, 2008.
- The KMMG facility is located at 700 Kia Parkway in West Point (Troup County), Georgia, designated as Site 1 T1.
- The KMMG facility employs about 2,500 people.
- Under the requested T/IM procedures, KMMG would produce up to 350,000 light-duty passenger vehicles (sedans, sport utility vehicles, minivans) annually for the U.S. market and export.
- The produced vehicles fall under HTSUS codes 8703.23 and 8703.24.
- Foreign components used in production represent about 25% of total material inputs at the facility.
- Included foreign components are oils (HTSUS 2710.11), paints (3208.10, 3209.90), plastic tubes/pipes/hoses (3917.31, 3917.40), plastic sheets/strips/plates (3919.90, 3921.90), rubber tubes/hoses (4009.11, 4009.31), rubber belts (4010.31, 4010.33), tires (4011.20), gaskets/washers/o-rings (4016.93, 4016.99), carpet sets (5703.20), safety glass (7007.11, 7007.21), mirrors (7009.10), tube fittings (7307.22, 7307.99), fasteners (7318.14), locks/keys (8301.20, 8301.40), engines (8407.34), engine parts (8409.91), pumps (8413.30), valves (8481.80), and bumpers (8708.10).
- The duty rates for the listed foreign components range from free to 8.6%.
- FTZ procedures could exempt KMMG from customs duty payments on foreign components used in export production, which is estimated to be 10% of plant shipments.
- For domestic sales, KMMG would be able to choose the duty rate that applies to finished passenger vehicles (2.5%) for foreign inputs that have higher rates.
- Customs duties could possibly be deferred or reduced on foreign status production equipment.
- Public comment is invited from interested parties, with submissions due by June 12, 2008.
- A copy of the application will be available for public inspection at the Office of the Foreign-Trade Zones Board's Executive Secretary.
- The notice was dated May 7, 2008, and signed by Andrew McGilvray, Executive Secretary.
