# U.S. Battery Mfg. Augusta Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-09

**Source:** [Battery Manufacturer Cited for Lead Violations - EHSLeaders](https://ehsleaders.org/2023/09/battery-manufacturer-cited-for-lead-violations/)  ·  `page_000881`

## Facts

- Faces $160,727 in Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA) fines for exposing employees to unsafe levels of lead.
- OSHA announced the fines on September 27 based on personal air monitoring of workers and lead accumulation on respirators and counters in lunch break areas.
- Cited for two repeat violations after inspections found the company failed to have engineering and work practice controls in place to reduce exposures to lead.
- Failed to prevent lead from accumulating on surfaces at the facility.
- Cited for two serious violations after finding that the company exposed employees to lead levels in concentrations above the permissible exposure limit (PEL).
- Did not ensure employees’ respirators were properly cleaned and disinfected.
- Since 2018, federal safety inspections at the Augusta facility have identified numerous violations related to lead exposures.
- In 2018, OSHA issued citations for nine serious violations, one repeat violation, and one other-than-serious violation at the facility.
- In a 2019 follow-up inspection, OSHA found one serious, one repeat, and one other-than-serious violation.
- Founded in 1926, designs and manufactures deep-cycle batteries for scissor lifts, golf carts, boats, and other uses.
- Company has locations in Corona, California, and Evans and Augusta, Georgia.
- OSHA’s Atlanta-east area office director Josh Turner stated that elevated lead levels can cause debilitating and permanent health issues and employers are legally responsible for minimizing exposure via engineering controls, safe work practices, and clean protective equipment.
