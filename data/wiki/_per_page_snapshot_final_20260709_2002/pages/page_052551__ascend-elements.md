# Ascend Elements

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Why We Invested in Ascend Elements to Accelerate Our Vision for Sustainable Energy Transformation | TDK Ventures](https://tdk-ventures.com/news/insights/why-we-invested-in-ascend-elements-to-accelerate-our-vision-for-sustainable-energy-transformation/)  ·  `page_052551`

## Facts

- TDK Ventures was founded in 2019 with a mission to build a sustainable future and help usher in the energy transformation needed.
- In early 2020, TDK Ventures invested in Ascend Elements (then known as Battery Resourcers) for their lithium-ion battery recycling technology.
- The United States has vowed a 50% reduction in greenhouse gas emissions by 2030 and launched multiple government initiatives to fund progress.
- The European Union announced a benchmark of zero carbon emissions by 2035 and launched multiple government initiatives to fund progress.
- BMW, Volvo, and Jaguar/Land Rover have all committed to going 100% electric in the next 10 years.
- GM, Ford, Toyota, Nissan, and others are declaring targets of up to 1/3rd of the market share along the same timeline.
- Lithium-ion batteries are projected to see a demand of 9.3 TWh of energy in 2030.
- Industry and government are rushing to address technical gaps with more than $500 bn in promised funds and a pledged 4.5 TWh of increased capacity by that timeline.
- Lithium-ion batteries require raw materials including cobalt, nickel, and lithium carbonate, which are processed into cathode components.
- Sourcing these materials presents a critical supply chain problem due to expensive and volatile pricing from unsustainable mining practices and sociopolitical relations.
- Lithium is often found raw as lithium carbonate, which must be chemically refined into lithium hydroxide for use in batteries.
- Cathode production costs alone make up 40–50% of the price for each battery.
- Ascend Elements developed a novel process to convert used Li-ion batteries and related manufacturing scrap into EV-ready grade cathode active materials, cathode precursors, and anode materials.
- The technology allows battery manufacturers and OEMs to skip refining and cathode/anode production steps from virgin materials.
- Introducing recycled materials has the potential for a 59% reduction in unit cost.
- There is an estimated 93% reduction in carbon footprint impact due to reduced processing requirements compared to using virgin materials.
- Ascend commenced construction of two recycling facilities, including a 154,000 sq. ft. plant in Covington, Georgia which will be capable of processing up to 30,000 metric tons per year.
- Ascend is also constructing a 500,000 sq. ft. facility in Hopkinsville, Kentucky capable of equipping over 250,000 electric vehicles per year with recycled batteries.
- Value extraction from end-of-life lithium-ion batteries has been shown to result in more than 10x more revenue and almost 3x the margin.
- Testing has demonstrated that their recycled cathodes outperform those produced by traditional means.
- Ascend has established a recycling process that is material agnostic, ensuring relevance as future manufacturing methods develop.
- Prior to 2021, Ascend had already established partnerships with both the US Department of Energy (DOE) and the National Science Foundation.
- In 2022, the DOE followed up with $480 million to directly support Ascend production and operation.
- Industry partners include Honda, Hitachi, SK Ecoplant, Jaguar Land Rover, EcoPro, and many other industrial leaders and battery manufacturers.
- The Battery Business Unit Manager at Jaguar Land Rover stated they considered 14 recyclers for partners but chose Ascend due to high-value products, technological sophistication, and ease of doing business.
- The same manager noted that Ascend's unique selling point is that their leading technology actually works and gives OEMs options to choose from.
- Ascend has quadrupled its team size since the partnership with TDK Ventures.
- There is a projected $6.5 billion market for the battery recycling market by 2025, representing an 87% CAGR.
- Ascend is positioned to be the leading player in North America's battery recycling sector.
