# State of Georgia Advanced Manufacturing Ecosystem

**Type:** unknown  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2022-06-01

**Source:** [High Tech Makers | Research](https://research.gatech.edu/high-tech-makers)  ·  `page_059249`

## Facts

- Parts holding rockets together are made in Dodge County, Georgia.
- Carbice in Atlanta produces thermal tape for satellites.
- Saft America in Valdosta makes advanced batteries that power airplanes, satellites and race cars.
- Manufacturing of all types employs over 11,000 companies with an estimated 393,500 workers in 2021 – about 9% of Georgia’s workforce.
- Approximately three-quarters of Georgia's manufacturing workers are production workers.
- Companies employing advanced manufacturing practices had a total output of nearly $62 billion in 2019, representing approximately 10% of Georgia’s Gross State Product according to National Association of Manufacturers (NAM) data.
- John Morehouse is the director of the Georgia Center of Innovation for Manufacturing at the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD).
- Georgia's strategic location allows companies to reach 80% of the U.S. market via truck, rail or plane.
- The Port of Savannah serves as a gateway to and from the world for Georgia manufacturers.
- Georgia Quick Start is the nation’s No. 1 workforce training program that has provided customized training for more than 1 million workers.
- John Avery is the director of the Advanced Technology Development Center (ATDC) at Georgia Tech.
- Southwire Company, a maker of wire and cable, is in the midst of a $1 billion investment to add capacity and improve factories.
- Rich Stinson is the CEO of Southwire Company.
- William Berry is the Senior Vice President at Southwire Company.
- Southwire has developed better control of motors and products such as servo motors that rotate machine parts with high efficiency.
- GDEcD’s innovation center has teams working in agricultural technology, energy technology, information technology, logistics, manufacturing, and aerospace to connect companies to academic experts, other companies, or government agencies.
- Georgia is pushing adoption of Manufacturing 4.0 (Industry 4.0), integrating technologies like the Internet of Things (IoT), cloud computing, and artificial intelligence (AI) into operations.
- Georgia Tech’s Advanced Manufacturing Pilot Facility (AMPF) is a 20,000-square-foot, reconfigurable R&D facility in Midtown Atlanta that provides space for industrial, academic, and government agencies to develop innovative manufacturing projects.
- AMPF serves as a teaching laboratory for Georgia Tech graduate students.
- AMPF was made possible by a $3 million donation from the Delta Air Lines Foundation.
- The facility can house advanced manufacturing innovation projects including industrial robotics and additive, hybrid, composite, and digital manufacturing.
- Aaron Stebner is an associate professor in the George W. Woodruff School of Mechanical Engineering at Georgia Tech working on research integrating data informatics and machine learning for new manufacturing processes.
- Georgia Tech launched the Georgia Manufacturing 4.0 Consortium to work with businesses in defense and related industries.
- The Georgia Manufacturing 4.0 initiative emphasizes helping small manufacturers adopt Industry 4.0 technologies and provides assistance with workforce training.
- Gulfstream equips workers with wearable devices to provide remote step-by-step instructions when supervisors are not on hand.
- Shreyes Melkote is a mechanical engineering professor at Georgia Tech and executive director of the Novelis Innovation Hub at Georgia Tech.
- The partnership between Georgia Tech and aluminum manufacturer/recycler Novelis focuses on basic and applied research related to sustainable processes.
- ProcessMiner opened in 2014 with an original goal to optimize manufacturing processes for the pulp and paper industry.
- ProcessMiner’s platform now predicts problems in real time using AI within the manufacturing process for companies in the pulp, paper, plastics, water treatment, and energy sectors.
- Kamran Paynabar is the co-founder and chief science officer at ProcessMiner.
- Karim Pourak is the co-founder and CEO at ProcessMiner.
- Rayonier Advanced Materials is developing high-tech applications for wood byproducts, such as purified cellulose used in baby diapers, adult incontinence products, plastics, run-flat tires, sausage casings, timed-release pharmaceuticals, and LCD screens.
- Larissa Fenn is the director of research and development into new high-purity cellulose (HPC) products at Rayonier’s Marketing and Research Center.
- Rayonier partnered with the University of Georgia’s poultry science department to test a wood-derived prebiotic product in chickens to increase gut health and disease resistance.
- Carbice is an Atlanta-based start-up that spun out of a lab at Georgia Tech to address heat control problems for satellite circuit boards.
- Carbice produces thermal tape and created a first-of-its-kind Space Pad that adheres to heat sources on satellites, reducing application time from three weeks to three minutes.
- CEO Baratunde Cola states Carbice’s material will improve performance across space, power trains in electric vehicles, semiconductor manufacturing, data centers, cloud computing, graphics cards, and high-performance gaming.
- The Atlanta Drone Group uses advanced manufacturing processes including 3D printing of prototype and production parts, 3D scanning of sensors, and cutting-edge carbon fiber lay-up methods for its unmanned aircraft.
- Matt Sloane is the CEO of the Atlanta Drone Group.
- Eastman’s Kencoa Aerospace is a supplier of multi-axis precision-machined and sheet metal-fabricated components serving customers including Lockheed Martin, Gulfstream Aerospace, and Boeing.
- Aerotech Machining is located in Chatham County near the Port of Savannah and designs/produces parts using CNC machine tools for aerospace, agricultural, power generation, and rail industries.
- Joey Jones is the President of Aerotech Machining.
- PHX Holdings owns Phoenix Stamping Group, which makes sheet metal and rod component parts.
- Brandyn Chapman is the CEO of PHX Holdings and states the company started its lean manufacturing journey in 2003 and implemented an ERP system to streamline and automate functions.
- Saft America is a wholly owned subsidiary of France’s TotalEnergies that specializes in advanced technology battery solutions for industry, with products used in satellites and airplanes where reliability is critical.
- Kirk Rosenlund is the general manager at Saft America, noting that more than 500 of their batteries have been in space since 1966.
