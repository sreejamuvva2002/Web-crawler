# Club Car LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-15

**Source:** [Who Owns Club Car? Platinum Equity’s $1.7B Acquisition - LegalClarity](https://legalclarity.org/who-owns-club-car-platinum-equitys-1-7b-acquisition/)  ·  `page_018410`

## Facts

- Platinum Equity acquired Club Car from industrial conglomerate Ingersoll Rand in 2021 for approximately $1.7 billion.
- The acquisition moved Club Car from a division inside a publicly traded corporation to a standalone business under private ownership.
- Tom Gores founded Platinum Equity in 1995, building it into a firm managing roughly $48 billion in assets across about 60 operationally complex businesses.
- Club Car had been a subsidiary of Ingersoll Rand since 1995, operating within its broader portfolio for over 25 years before the 2021 sale.
- Under Platinum Equity ownership, the company's entire budget and strategic focus revolve around the small-vehicle market rather than competing for capital with other business units.
- In April 2022, Club Car announced a deal to acquire Garia A/S, a Denmark-based electric low-speed vehicle manufacturer, from Lars Larsen Group.
- The April 2022 acquisition also included Melex, a Polish manufacturer of lightweight utility vehicles that Garia had acquired months earlier.
- Mark Wagner, who led Club Car as President and CEO through the transition from Ingersoll Rand to Platinum Equity, retired effective August 1, 2025.
- Craig Scanlon replaced Mark Wagner as President and CEO on June 9, 2025.
- Craig Scanlon spent 18 years at Polaris, rising to Chief Marketing Officer, and most recently served as CEO of K&N Engineering before joining Club Car.
- Scanlon actually started his career in 1998 selling Club Car Carryall vehicles when the company was still part of Ingersoll Rand.
- Club Car traces its origins to 1958 when Landreth Machine was founded in Houston, Texas.
- In 1962, Bill Stevens Sr. acquired the company and relocated operations to Augusta, Georgia, officially launching Club Car LLC.
- Club Car's global headquarters and primary manufacturing operations remain in Augusta, Georgia, where the company has been based for over 60 years.
- The Augusta facility handles assembly and engineering for the company's full vehicle lineup, from golf cars to commercial utility vehicles.
- Despite the change to private equity ownership, Club Car's manufacturing footprint stayed in Augusta, and the plant remains a significant employer in the area.
- With the Garia and Melex acquisitions, Club Car now has production capacity in Denmark and Poland, giving it a three-continent manufacturing presence.
- The Augusta site remains the center of gravity for engineering, design, and the bulk of production volume.
- Club Car's product line spans three broad categories: personal vehicles (Onward series in standard and street-legal LSV configurations, CRU, XRT utility model), golf course operators (Tempo fleet integrating with Visage GPS and connectivity platform), and commercial side (Carryall utility and transport vehicles for campuses, resorts, airports, and industrial facilities).
- The company has been pushing hard into lithium-ion battery technology across its lineup.
- Club Car's lithium-ion platform is available on Tempo, Carryall, and Onward vehicles, featuring a steel, watertight battery enclosure that meets IP67 waterproofing standards.
- The lithium-ion system includes smart charging that allows off-peak programming and extended storage without monthly state-of-charge checks.
- For fleet operators, the reduced maintenance and longer lifespan of lithium batteries compared to traditional lead-acid packs can meaningfully lower total cost of ownership over time.
- Private equity firms typically hold portfolio companies for five to seven years before selling, focusing on growing revenue, expanding margins, and building value for an eventual exit through a sale or IPO.
