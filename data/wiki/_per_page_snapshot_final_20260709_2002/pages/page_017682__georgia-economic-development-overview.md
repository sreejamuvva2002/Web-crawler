# Georgia Economic Development Overview

**Type:** event  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-03-31

**Source:** [Economic Development Around the State - Georgia Trend Magazine](https://www.georgiatrend.com/2026/03/31/georgia-economics-jobs-in-goergia/)  ·  `page_017682`

## Facts

- A recent report from the Global Business Alliance reveals that Georgia is a key location for international investment.
- Approximately 1,500 international investors employ more than 300,400 people in Georgia.
- One-third of the jobs supported by international investors in Georgia are in manufacturing.
- Companies from Canada, the U.K., and Japan support the largest number of jobs in Georgia.
- Georgia outpaces the national average for the proportion of employment derived from foreign direct investment with 7.2% in Georgia vs. 6% nationally.
- Preciball USA is opening a new production facility in Screven County.
- The Preciball USA facility represents a $17.6 million investment that will create 65 new jobs over the next several years.
- Preciball USA manufactures precision ball bearings for industrial use.
- French company Saint-Gobain recently celebrated the conclusion of a $140 million expansion at its CertainTeed roofing facility in Peachtree City, Fayette County.
- The Saint-Gobain/CertainTeed expansion doubled the facility's capacity to make roof shingles.
- Atlanta Gas Light is building a liquefied natural gas facility in Lowndes County to help strengthen the region’s energy capacity and support more industrial growth, particularly related to food production.
- The Atlanta Gas Light LNG facility represents a $50 million investment that will create nearly 100 jobs during construction.
- The Atlanta Gas Light project will create several highly skilled positions once operational in 2027.
- Pratt & Whitney celebrated a recent expansion that created 400 new jobs.
- Pratt & Whitney is investing more than $200 million in yet another expansion in Columbus, initially creating 15 new jobs with more to follow.
- Pratt & Whitney designs, manufactures and services aircraft engines and auxiliary power units for military, commercial and civil aviation.
- The former CNN Center in Downtown Atlanta is being revived as The Center through a $65 million-plus project.
- The Center redevelopment will include a food hall, Maestro’s Ocean Club as an anchor restaurant and the largest bar in the city.
- The Center will serve as the home base for the Atlanta Host Committee during the FIFA World Cup 2026 and the event’s volunteer headquarters.
- CTR Culture, in partnership with a local community-focused arts group, will feature public art, experiential activations and cultural programing at The Center.
