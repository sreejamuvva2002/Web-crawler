# Kia Georgia

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2009-11-16

**Source:** [Kia Georgia Starts 2027 Telluride Hybrid | REVBUZZ](https://revbuzz.com/content/6559/kia-georgia-starts-2027-telluride-hybrid)  ·  `page_008196`

## Facts

- Kia Georgia announced the start of 2027 Telluride production in West Point.
- The milestone marks the plant's 5 millionth vehicle built since mass production began on November 16, 2009.
- The 2027 Telluride is the first hybrid electric vehicle assembled in the state of Georgia.
- It also marks Kia's first hybrid model produced in the United States.
- The West Point facility is located on approximately 2,200 acres with an annual production capacity of up to 350,000 units.
- The plant currently builds the Telluride, Sorento, and Sportage (excluding their HEV and PHEV versions).
- The plant also builds the all-electric EV6 and EV9.
- Georgia Governor Brian P. Kemp attended the ceremony underscoring the event's regional significance.
- Since its debut in 2019, the Telluride has been exclusively Georgia-built and has become a flagship SUV for the brand in North America.
- In certain years, U.S. sales of the Telluride have exceeded 90,000 to 100,000 units.
- The launch of hybrid production reflects Kia's global electrification strategy and expansion of its electrified vehicle lineup.
- Localizing hybrid assembly in the United States signals a shift of key technologies closer to one of Kia's most important markets.
- Reaching the five-million-vehicle milestone took less than two decades, highlighting sustained demand for Georgia-built vehicles.
- Company leadership attributes this achievement to the plant's workforce and its ability to produce gasoline-powered models, electric vehicles, and now hybrids within the same facility.
- The start of 2027 Telluride hybrid production marks the beginning of Kia's hybrid manufacturing era in the United States.
