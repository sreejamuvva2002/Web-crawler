# Envisics

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [This UK startup empowers car dashboards to display information, directions and points of interest as holograms - UKTN](https://www.uktech.news/news/uk-startup-envisics-partners-with-panasonic-20210111)  ·  `page_047816`

## Facts

- UK-based holographic technology startup Envisics debuted with the intention to revolutionise the in-car experience with its technology.
- During the ongoing Consumer Electronics Show (CES), Envisics announced a partnership with Panasonic Automotive Systems to develop and commercialise a new generation of head-up displays for cars, SUVs and trucks.
- Panasonic Automotive Systems is a tier 1 automotive supplier and an arm of Panasonic Corporation of North America.
- Envisics' head-up displays are units integrated in vehicles’ dashboards that project images into the windshield to help drivers with navigation and other alerts.
- As a result of the partnership, Panasonic HUDs will use Envisics' holographic technology.
- The new partnership combines Panasonic’s expertise in optical design and its global reach to bring holography technology into the mainstream.
- Envisics intends to begin mass production of its technology in 2023.
- Envisics was founded by Jamieson Christmas in 2018.
- Envisics secured $50M (nearly £37M) Series B funding in a round led by Hyundai Mobis, General Motors’ GM Ventures, SAIC Capital, the venture arm of SAIC Motors, and Van Tuyl Companies (VTC).
- The investment was raised in October last year.
- The raised investment is supposed to be used for continued development of its technology and to bring fresh iterations of the same into the market.
- Hyundai Mobis, a branch of the Korean car maker, will integrate Envisics’ AR HUDs as part of its autonomous driving development.
- At the time of investment, it was announced that the fund will be used to integrate the technology in the upcoming Cadillac Lyriq electric vehicle.
- Envisics focuses solely on the automotive applications of holography technology.
- The first generation of this tech is already integrated in over 150,000 Jaguar Land Rover vehicles.
- Jamieson Christmas stated that the Series B funding round was about scaling the business and enabling them to move forward as they enter the market.
- Part of the plan included a commitment to engage in partnerships with Tier ones to deliver products to market.
- Envisics will develop head-up displays with high resolution, wider color gamut and larger images that can be projected upon reality.
- The technology can simultaneously project information at multiple distances.
- This capability can be used for night vision applications such as overlaying enhanced information upon a dark road to clearly show where the road is heading and the obstacles on the path.
