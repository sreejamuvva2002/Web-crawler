# IONNA

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** charging_infrastructure  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- Every IONNA Rechargery offers both CCS and NACS connectors.
- IONNA supports the use of adapters purchased from dealers for charging.
- IONNA charges all EVs, including vehicles that do not come from its founding automaker brands.
- Remote charging sessions at IONNA Rechargeries can be started using apps including ChargeHub, ChargePoint, EV Connect, Ford, Kia Access, Lexus, Mercedes Benz, MINI, My BMW, myCadillac, myChevrolet, myGMC, MyHyundai with Bluelink, My Porsche, Presto, Rivian, Stellantis Free2Move Charge, Toyota and Volvo Cars.
- Payment for charging at IONNA Rechargeries can be made using the same list of apps mentioned for starting sessions.
- Charging can also be paid for via credit card (tap, swipe, or insert), Plug & Charge, Google Pay, and Apple Pay.
- IONNA Rechargery locations support Tesla charging with both NACS and CCS charging bays.
- Tesla models before 2021 may require an upgrade to charge at IONNA stations, and owners should check their manual for third-party charging details.
- Drivers can route to the closest IONNA station from BMW, Ford, General Motors, Hyundai, Kia, Mercedes, Porsche, Rivian, Stellantis, and Toyota EVs.
- ChargePoint's "Tap to Charge" feature and ChargePoint Card (RFID card) do not currently work at IONNA stations.
- All BMW and MINI drivers who initiate charging with Plug & Charge or the My BMW App receive 20% off charging sessions through September 30, 2026.
- Plug & Charge is available for Volvo EX60 and EX90 vehicles at IONNA Rechargeries nationwide.
- 2025 Volvo EX90 vehicles may require a free hardware upgrade from the dealership to use Plug & Charge.
- Plug & Charge is available for 2025 and newer KIA EV6 and 2026 and newer KIA EV9 at IONNA Rechargeries nationwide.
