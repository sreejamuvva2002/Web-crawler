# Rivian Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2021-12-16

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp Welcomes Rivian for Single-Largest Economic Development Project in State History | Governor Brian P. Kemp Office of the Governor](https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases/2021-12-16/gov-kemp-welcomes-rivian-single-largest-economic-development-project)  ·  `page_000098`

## Facts

- Rivian Inc. will invest $5 billion in a carbon-conscious campus in Georgia for its electric adventure vehicles.
- Across operations, Rivian will create approximately 7,500 jobs on just under 2,000 acres located at the site known as the East Atlanta Megasite.
- The site is represented by the Joint Development Authority of Jasper, Morgan, Newton, and Walton counties.
- Once manufacturing operations are fully ramped up, the Georgia facility will be capable of producing up to 400,000 vehicles per year.
- Rivian’s manufacturing plant represents the single-largest economic development project in state history.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Georgia will now be home to Rivian's largest manufacturing facility.
- The investment establishes Georgia's leading role in the booming automotive manufacturing industry for generations to come.
- Georgia's long-term focus on logistics, talent, workforce, and responsive government significantly contributed to the announcement.
- Governor Kemp's administration emphasized innovation and development in the electric mobility ecosystem to deliver wins for Georgians.
- Georgia is considered the top state for business.
- Georgia Quick Start workforce training resources, world-class higher education institutions, and statewide logistics infrastructure assets are prepared to meet Rivian's production and research and development needs.
- Construction on the facility, located at Stanton Springs North, is expected to begin in summer 2022.
- The start of production is slated for 2024.
- Rivian’s technology centers at the new campus will support the company’s research and development programs.
- The company is actively working to ensure that both the construction process and future facility meet Rivian’s high standards of conservation and sustainability for a cleaner, wilder future.
- The entire project will support the expansion of the U.S. electric vehicle industry and will serve to decarbonize the transportation and energy sectors.
- Rivian is the latest success in Georgia’s push to recruit the entire electric mobility ecosystem to the state.
- In August, Governor Kemp announced the launch of the Electric Mobility and Innovation Alliance.
- The Electric Mobility and Innovation Alliance is a statewide initiative between government, industries, electric utilities, nonprofits, and other relevant stakeholders focused on growing the electric mobility ecosystem in the state and strengthening Georgia’s position in electrification-related manufacturing and innovation.
- Georgia is committed to ensuring the state’s existing industry and accompanying 55,000 automotive manufacturing jobs receive the support for retraining and expansions needed to transition to meet the new demands of electrified transportation.
- Rivian Chief People Officer Helen Russell stated that Georgia will serve as Rivian's next manufacturing site to meet demand for Rivian products and scale its business rapidly.
- Rivian's work in Georgia is rooted in collaboration, transparency, fairness, and a strong sense of belonging.
- Helen Russell expressed confidence that alongside Illinois, Georgia is the place for Rivian to scale and thrive.
- Rivian has developed and vertically integrated a connected electric platform that can be flexibly applied to a range of applications, including its adventure products as well as B2B products such as its last-mile delivery vans.
- The last-mile delivery vans were launched in partnership with Amazon.
- Rivian’s launch products are the R1T and R1S.
- The R1T and R1S provide an unmatched combination of performance, off-road capability, and utility.
- Earlier this week (relative to Dec 2021), the Rivian R1T all-electric truck earned the coveted 2022 truck of the year from MotorTrend.
- MotorTrend called the R1T “the most remarkable pickup truck we’ve ever driven!”. 
- Joint Development Authority of Stanton Springs Chairman Jerry Silvio welcomed Rivian to the country’s premier joint economic development partnership.
- Rivian will join two revolutionary leaders in their respective fields to make east Atlanta a beacon of technology and employment potential.
- The Joint Development Authority of Stanton Springs began over 25 years ago with the goal of creating high-tech, regional-focused jobs for generations to come.
- Economic development directors Shane Short and Serra P. Hall, and attorney Andrea Gray provided dedication and steadfast focus to ensure the vision is met and exceeded.
- The four counties and utility partners have planned strategically to ensure minimal impact on the local environment.
- Continued collaboration between the State of Georgia, the JDA, and Rivian will be unprecedented.
- Rivian’s strategic location on the I-20 corridor will enable the company to access resources and speed its products to market.
- Georgia offers 1,200 miles of highway and 5,000 miles of rail to move product quickly and efficiently.
- Investments such as the newly opened Mason Mega Rail Terminal at the Port of Savannah will allow Rivian to efficiently tap into supply chain needs.
- The Mason Mega Rail Terminal is the largest on-terminal rail facility in North America.
- Griff Lynch, Executive Director for the Georgia Ports Authority, expressed excitement to welcome Rivian to the Peach State’s manufacturing community.
- The Georgia Ports Authority is expanding facilities for processing containers and finished vehicles to provide supply chain solutions to Rivian.
- Russell McMurry, Commissioner of the Georgia Department of Transportation, stated that Georgia’s exceptional multimodal transportation network is foundational for the future of transportation.
- This network has led to Georgia being recognized as a national leader in EV corridor readiness.
- The Georgia Department of Transportation looks forward to providing the transportation infrastructure necessary to make this project successful.
- Rivian’s just under 2,000-acre parcel will include abundant natural space.
- Mike Hopkins, Executive Director of the Newton County Water and Sewerage Authority, expressed gratitude for partnering with Rivian and the Joint Development Authority of Stanton Springs.
- The partnership is viewed as a once-in-a-lifetime moment where water must be managed to become the standard that all future projects aspire to achieve.
- The State of Georgia is recognized as a beacon of preservation and steward for water protection.
- The water system is uniquely designed to support citizens, businesses, and the natural environment in the Southeast.
- Rivian’s focus on environmental excellence fits perfectly within the vision here.
- Georgia’s strong and diverse talent pool is bolstered by one of the largest state university systems in the country.
- Eighty-five accredited public and private universities in the state award more than 132,000 degrees and certificates to its graduates annually.
- The Technical College System of Georgia consists of 22 colleges offering education and training in numerous industries that keep Georgia competitive in the global economy.
- Metro Atlanta Chamber President and CEO Katie Kirkpatrick stated that Metro Atlanta is ideally suited for Rivian’s new operations.
- The region offers a diverse and highly skilled workforce, access to innovative businesses, and collaborative higher education institutions.
- To further assist with workforce requirements, Georgia Quick Start will build and operate a state-of-the-art manufacturing training center.
- The training center will provide Rivian with a fully customized training program that meets the company’s start-up needs.
- This initiative will also build capacity and curricula within the Technical College System of Georgia to maintain a long-term pipeline of highly skilled employees trained specifically in these operations.
- The Georgia Department of Labor will assist the company in identifying and recruiting workers.
- Hank Evans, Director of the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Aerospace, Defense, and Advanced Manufacturing team, represented GDEcD’s Global Commerce division on this competitive project.
- Partners include the Joint Development Authority (JDA) of Jasper, Morgan, Newton, and Walton Counties; the Georgia Ports Authority; the Georgia Department of Transportation; Georgia Quick Start; the Metro Atlanta Chamber of Commerce; and Georgia Power.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Rivian’s decision to locate in Georgia will have a tremendous impact on the entire state.
- Pat Wilson noted that more change in the automotive industry will occur in the next 10 years than in the past 100.
- With this announcement, Georgia will be home to one of the main drivers of this transformation.
- Rivian carefully plans construction efforts at the site to preserve and protect the beauty and uniqueness of their new home.
- Rivian will be an outstanding corporate citizen and community partner, creating jobs and investment that will change lives and accelerate Georgia’s sustainable and electrified future.
- Georgia is at the center of the rapidly expanding Southeast Automotive Alley.
- Rivian’s investment accelerates the evolution of Georgia’s automotive ecosystem.
- Within the last five years, 78 automotive-related companies have located or expanded operations in Georgia, creating thousands of new jobs.
- Existing companies in Georgia have expanded their portfolios to include parts for EVs.
- Companies from Korea, Germany, Turkey, and the Netherlands have also recently announced plans to build EV-related facilities in the state.
- Georgia’s prime location, extensive infrastructure, skilled workforce, and business-friendly climate has made it an attractive location for a diverse array of rapidly developing industries focused on creating a sustainable future.
- The state is leading in the development and use of emerging technologies for the evolving automotive and mobility industries through fostering healthy industry practices and encouraging collaboration and innovation.
- Rivian exists to create products and services that help the planet transition to carbon neutral energy and transportation.
- Rivian designs, develops, and manufactures category-defining electric vehicles and accessories and sells them directly to customers in the consumer and commercial markets.
- Rivian complements its vehicles with a full suite of proprietary, value-added services that address the entire lifecycle of the vehicle and deepen customer relationships.
