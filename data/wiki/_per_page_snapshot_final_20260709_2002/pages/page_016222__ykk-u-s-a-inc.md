# YKK (U.S.A.) Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2024-06-20

**Source:** [Japanese company YKK celebrates 50 years in Macon, Georgia. | Macon Telegraph](https://www.macon.com/news/business/article289359915.html)  ·  `page_016222`

## Facts

- YKK is a Japanese company celebrating 50 years since opening its doors in Macon, Georgia.
- YKK first entered the U.S. market in 1960 as a zipper distributor in New York City.
- Production started at YKK's textile and metal zipper plant in Macon in 1974 with approximately 50 employees.
- YKK founder Tadao Yoshida and a site selection team explored locations in Tennessee, North and South Carolina, Alabama, and Georgia before settling in Georgia.
- Georgia was chosen due to its robust infrastructure, including four interstate highways, the deep-water port of Savannah, and a major international airport.
- A meeting arranged by Cloyd Hall, a Macon native and special assistant to former Gov. Jimmy Carter, led to a connection between Yoshida and Carter that clinched the decision to settle in Georgia.
- YKK officials stated the strategic decision to establish its manufacturing facility just south of Macon in the newly developed Ocmulgee East Industrial Park heralded a deep partnership with the region.
- Under the leadership of Yoshinori Kitano, construction and operation strategies were crafted for the Macon facility.
- Newly hired engineers, including future YKK Corporation of America CEO Alex Gregory, were trained in Japan to transfer specialized manufacturing techniques to the Macon facility.
- The facility initially focused on producing zippers for clothing, furniture, and outdoor equipment tailored to the American market.
- Over the decades, the factory has expanded dramatically and now employs over 600 local residents.
- YKK has diversified its advanced solutions into industries including automotive, government, and medical sectors.
- Marty Timms, YKK (U.S.A.) Inc. vice president of manufacturing, cited the mutual commitment between the company and the Macon community as key to the company's success.
- Timms noted that Macon provides tremendous community support and a great workforce to draw from.
- Timms highlighted the outstanding industrial authority and chamber of commerce in Macon as always supportive and eager to help with needed resources.
- YKK has made significant contributions to local community projects and events for decades, exemplifying its commitment to corporate social responsibility.
- In 1977, YKK played a pivotal role in establishing a sister city relationship between Kurobe, Japan, and Macon.
- Over the past four decades, the sister city partnership has fostered cultural exchange with more than 1,100 citizens from both cities visiting each other’s countries.
- Officials said YKK has been a supporter of the Macon Cherry Blossom Festival through sponsorships and participation in festival activities.
- In 1991, YKK (U.S.A.) Inc. donated $300,000 toward the construction of a dedicated building for the festival organizers.
- The city of Macon complemented the donation by providing $60,000 worth of land for the festival building.
- Chairman Tadahiro Yoshida, son of founder Tadao Yoshida and recipient of the Order of the Rising Sun with Gold and Silver Stars from the Japanese government, was present for the 1991 dedication.
- YKK (U.S.A.) Inc. is committed to sustaining its founder's legacy by enhancing Macon's prosperity, enriching Georgia, and advancing the global textile industry.
- The company operates under a “Cycle of Goodness” philosophy where no one prospers without rendering benefit to others.
- YKK (U.S.A.) Inc. plans to hold a celebration for its employees later in 2024.
