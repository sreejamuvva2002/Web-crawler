# MICHELIN® Retread Technologies

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Explore MICHELIN® Retread Technologies: Sustainable & Cost-Effective Tire Solutions](https://business.michelin.ca/en/michelin-retread-technologies)  ·  `page_046540`

## Facts

- Michelin® Retread Technologies offer a sustainable and cost-effective alternative to new tires, providing significant environmental benefits and cost savings.
- Advanced technology and rigorous manufacturing processes ensure Michelin retreads meet the highest standards of safety and performance.
- Retreads extend tire life and improve fuel efficiency to maximize total cost of ownership while boosting the bottom line and reducing waste.
- Retreads reduce tire expenses significantly, decrease waste and carbon emissions, meet high safety standards, and offer a lifespan comparable to new tires.
- The trucking industry saves billions each year by retreading, with smart owners investing in high-quality tires instead of new ones.
- Michelin's innovative retread process utilizes new-tire design technology, proven tread designs, and new-tire manufacturing standards to deliver reliable retreads designed for specific applications.
- The MICHELIN® Retread Technologies (MRT) process delivers low rolling resistance, SmartWay® verified retreads for maximum fuel efficiency.
- Michelin proprietary technology yields multiple retreads from a single casing.
- The manufacturing process is monitored and audited, using a 9-step procedure that delivers uniform results.
- Bib Tread mobile on-site digital app provides increased accountability of tire assets.
- A consistent, nationwide retread warranty is backed by Michelin.
- Step 1 (Initial Inspection) inspects every casing and detail to enable trained operators to detect subtle irregularations indicating internal damage such as zippers.
- Step 2 (Buffing) is computer automated, guided by a casing-specific database and sensors to provide an under tread of optimal profile, depth, and texture.
- Step 3 (Casing Integrity) uses SHEAROGRAPHY, a two-step laser photography performed on every tire to reveal belt separations or blisters within the belts or other not visible areas.
- Step 4 (X-Ray) is available and used when needed to allow visual inspection of the steel belts within the casing.
- Step 5 (Repair) utilizes best practice repairs where thermal curing along with mechanical pressure exceeds industry standards.
- Step 6 (Tread Building) uses MICHELIN® tire tread, combining applying hot cushion gum and pre-molded tread into one operation.
- Step 7 (Double-Enveloping) uniquely applies an inner envelope to pressurize both sides of the repair, ensuring every retread surface undergoes uniform pressure and temperature.
- Step 8 (Curing) is computer controlled for minimum temperature variations to achieve optimal uniformity and enhance full integration of all repairs.
- Step 9 (Final Inspection) thoroughly inspects the crown, sidewall, bead, and interior to ensure the best retread every time.
- Michelin® retreads help reduce waste by extending the life of original tires and decrease carbon emissions through improved fuel efficiency.
- Retreads are significantly less expensive than new tires, reducing overall tire expenses and cost per mile.
- Michelin® retreads meet rigorous safety and performance standards, making them suitable for various vehicle types including commercial trucks.
- Michelin® retreads are designed to provide nearly the same lifespan as new tires when maintained properly.
- Not all tires are suitable for retreading; tires must pass a thorough inspection to ensure they meet the criteria for retreading.
