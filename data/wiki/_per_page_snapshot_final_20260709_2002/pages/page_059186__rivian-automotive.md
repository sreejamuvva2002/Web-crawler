# Rivian Automotive

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-06

**Source:** [Should You Buy Rivian While It's Below $20? | The Motley Fool](https://www.fool.com/investing/2026/07/06/should-you-buy-rivian-while-its-below-20/)  ·  `page_059186`

## Facts

- Rivian recently began selling its new R2 vehicle.
- The R2 lineup consists of smaller SUVs that are part of a larger plan to gradually roll out a lower-priced vehicle to expand Rivian's customer base.
- The new R2 starts around $58,000 right now.
- By late 2027, a base version of the vehicle will be on sale for just $45,000.
- The $45,000 price means the R2 will cost roughly $4,000 less than the average new vehicle price.
- Rivian needs to sell a lot of R2s, share parts and technology across its vehicle lineup, and produce vehicles efficiently to generate recurring profits.
- Rivian has retooled its R1S and R1T vehicles to reduce parts and equipment.
- Rivian has improved sourcing to lower costs and lowered other expenses.
- Rivian formed a joint venture with Volkswagen in 2024.
- Under the joint venture, Rivian provides software to Volkswagen in return for funding.
- The joint venture deal helped Rivian report $1.6 billion in sales from its software and services segment last year.
- The joint venture deal helped Rivian report $576 million in gross profit from its software and services segment last year.
- The software and services segment performance helped Rivian achieve two quarters of gross profitability.
- R2 sales and production are currently underway.
- Rivian delivered 12,194 vehicles in the first quarter.
- First-quarter deliveries outpaced Rivian's guidance of 9,000 to 11,000 vehicles.
- Rivian raised its production guidance for 2026 from a previous estimate of between 62,000 and 67,000 to a new range of 65,000 to 70,000.
- Many traditional automakers have backed away from ambitious EV plans because government tax incentives for buying an electric vehicle were prematurely eliminated.
- Rising consumer costs due to tariffs and high interest rates are dampening sales.
- Many automakers are releasing more hybrid models, which have proved appealing to car buyers and have likely cut into some EV sales.
