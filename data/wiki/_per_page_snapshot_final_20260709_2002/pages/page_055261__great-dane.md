# Great Dane

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2017-05-01

**Source:** [Great Dane tops in Georgia - Statesboro Herald](https://www.statesboroherald.com/local/great-dane-tops-in-georgia/)  ·  `page_055261`

## Facts

- Great Dane employs more than 400 people at its Statesboro plant.
- Great Dane brings more than $25 million in payroll to the Bulloch County area each year.
- Great Dane was named Georgia Manufacturer of the Year in the medium category, which covers plants with 150-499 employees.
- Gov. Nathan Deal presented the awards Thursday during the Manufacturing Appreciation Week luncheon at the Georgia International Convention Center in Atlanta.
- Great Dane was nominated for the award by Ogeechee Technical College, which nominates at least one manufacturer from its three-county service area each year.
- Great Dane was founded in Savannah in 1900.
- The company developed its first refrigerated trailer in 1940, which was also the first refrigerated trailer in the industry according to Brian Sage, Great Dane's current vice president of manufacturing.
- Great Dane opened its 450,000-square-foot Statesboro factory in Bulloch County's Gateway Industrial Park in April 2012.
- Brian Sage stated that Great Dane has deep roots in Georgia and chose the Statesboro location because of community support, the partnership with Ogeechee Technical College, and its strategic location.
- Great Dane is an international leader in the trailer industry and is now headquartered in Chicago while maintaining a corporate campus in Savannah.
- Representatives from Ogeechee Tech, the Statesboro-Bulloch Chamber of Commerce, the Bulloch County Development Authority, Bulloch County government, the city of Statesboro, and the Evans County Industrial Development Authority attended the luncheon.
- Several agencies contributed letters supporting Ogeechee Tech's nomination of Great Dane.
- Previous Manufacturer of the Year winners nominated by Ogeechee Tech were engine manufacturer Briggs & Stratton (large category, 2002) and architectural glass fabricator Viracon (medium category, 2005).
- Both previous winning companies' Georgia plants are located in Bulloch County.
- Ogeechee Tech and Bulloch County had a first-time winner in the Manufacturing Appreciation Week student design contest this year.
- Bethany Boyer, a seventh-grader at William James Middle School, won statewide first place among middle school artists (grades six through eight).
- Ogeechee Technical College President Lori Durden praised Boyer for competing against hundreds of state middle schoolers and her insight into manufacturing in Georgia.
- Boyer received a $500 scholarship check presented by the governor.
- The 2017 Manufacturer of the Year winner in the large manufacturer category (over 500 employees) was King's Hawaiian bakery in Flowery Branch, near Gainesville.
- The winner with fewer than 150 employees was Grenzebach Corporation, an international maker of specialized equipment with a plant in Newnan.
- Award categories are based on the workforce of the nominated Georgia-based location instead of overall company size.
- OTC Vice President for Economic Development Jan Moore, who is also Statesboro's mayor, called it a wonderful day for the community.
- Manufacturing and its economic impact are vital to Statesboro and Bulloch County, according to Moore.
- Great Dane employs a number of Ogeechee Tech graduates.
- Aspects of Ogeechee Tech's welding program have been geared to meet Great Dane's needs.
- Lori Durden noted that Ogeechee Tech and Great Dane have had a great partnership since day one, with Great Dane utilizing office space on the college campus before opening its plant and thousands crowding the campus to apply for positions.
- Great Dane has contributed to the college, including giving a trailer for use in the Commercial Truck Driving program.
- Gov. Nathan Deal's proclamation designated April 24-28 as Manufacturing Appreciation Week.
- The proclamation stated that manufacturing makes a tremendous contribution to Georgia's economic well-being.
- About 10,059 manufacturing facilities are located in Georgia.
- These facilities provide roughly 386,600 jobs, generate nearly $20.3 billion in wages, and contribute more than $100 billion to the state's economy each year.
- Gov. Deal acknowledged and honored the award-winning manufacturers at the luncheon.
- The event was cohosted by the Technical College System of Georgia and the Georgia Department of Economic Development.
- The 2017 awards selection and luncheon constituted Georgia's 23rd annual observance of Manufacturing Appreciation Week.
- Sponsors included the Georgia Association of Manufacturers, the Georgia Manufacturing Extension Partnership with the Georgia Institute of Technology, Georgia Power, and the accounting and business advisory firm Aprio (previously HA&W).
- Ogeechee Tech and schools in Bulloch County participate in a national Manufacturing Day each fall.
- Local industries that hosted about 100 eighth-graders for tours in October were Briggs & Stratton, Viracon, and Great Dane.
