# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-17

## Facts

- U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement raided Hyundai Motor Group's Metaplant America in eastern Georgia on Sept. 4.
- The agency detained 475 workers on allegations that they were employed in the United States illegally.
- The raid shocked the auto industry as automakers have already been grappling with tariff turmoil due to the Trump administration's stance on trade.
- Hyundai Motor Company calls its Georgia plant a "key pillar" of the group's $12.6 billion investment in Georgia.
- The vehicle assembly and battery plant was set to produce up to 500,000 hybrid and electric vehicles for the company's portfolio of brands.
- Hyundai committed to an additional $21 billion investment in U.S. manufacturing growth from 2025 to 2028.
- The decision to raid the South Korean automaker's plant puzzled some political observers considering President Donald Trump's push for private and foreign investment.
- On Sept. 11, a plane from Atlanta carrying over 300 Hyundai employees headed to South Korea.
- Hyundai Motor Company President and CEO Jose Munoz said production at the battery plant will likely be delayed 2 to 3 months.
- A production delay could mean that the plant won't produce as many of their popular EVs such as the Hyundai Ioniq 5 and Kia EV6.
- The delay could also cause a delay in production of planned new electric vehicle nameplates for 2026.
- Delayed battery production may force Hyundai to import batteries and parts, which could have cost implications for the automaker and consumers.
- As of August 2025, Hyundai sold 32,683 Ioniq 5 small electric SUVs.
- As of August 2025, Hyundai sold 8,318 Ioniq 6 small electric sedans.
- August Ioniq 5 sales increased a whopping 61% compared to 2024, according to sales report data.
- August Ioniq 6 sales increased by 30% compared to August 2024.
- Hyundai introduced the Ioniq 9 electric midsize SUV this year and sold 3,102 units for the year as of August.
- Tesla remains the undisputed sales champ in the EV space.
- Chevrolet's Equinox EV has been a success for the U.S. brand.
- Competitors from rivals Honda, General Motors, and Ford continue to gain momentum in the EV market.
