# Yachiyo Manufacturing of America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2014-08-01

**Source:** [Governor speaks at Carrollton Yachiyo opening | News | times-georgian.com](https://www.times-georgian.com/news/governor-speaks-at-carrollton-yachiyo-opening/article_12ba8219-dbf4-54f1-9aae-26a503981c11.html)  ·  `page_015604`

## Facts

- Georgia Gov. Nathan Deal visited Carrollton Friday for the grand opening at the Yachiyo auto parts manufacturing plant.
- The company made a $30 million investment to construct the new facility.
- The project will create more than 200 new jobs.
- Yachiyo is a supplier for Honda automotive vehicles.
- The new manufacturing facility is located in the Carrollton Industrial Park, off Columbia Drive.
- The plant will manufacture plastic gas tanks and sun roofs for Honda automobiles.
- The company hired its first 60 employees for the facility.
- Production is expected to begin on Aug. 25.
- Hiroshi Sasamoto serves as president of Yachiyo Industry Japan.
- Tetsuya Kitamura serves as president of Yachiyo of America Inc.
- Tomohisa Kobori serves as president of Yachiyo Manufacturing of America.
- Daniel Jackson is the president of the Carroll County Chamber of Commerce.
- Mike Oatridge is the president of Honda Manufacturing of Alabama.
- Japan is the largest international investor in Georgia in terms of dollar value.
- There are approximately 400 Japanese organizations and companies located in the state of Georgia.
- These Japanese companies employ 25,000 individuals in Georgia.
- The Georgia General Assembly removed sales tax on energy used in manufacturing to attract foreign industry investment.
- Gov. Nathan Deal stated that these policies resulted in an explosion of manufacturing jobs coming to Georgia.
- Georgia is now designated as the number one state in the country to do business.
- Chris Carr is the commissioner of the Georgia Department of Economic Development.
- The Quick Start program and Georgia Department of Labor were involved in facilitating the project.
- Rob Davyduck is the manager of Yachiyo's Carrollton plant.
- Yachiyo was founded in 1947 in Japan as a paint company.
- The company became an auto parts supplier in 1953.
- Robyn Miller is the senior manager for the Carrollton plant.
- The initial 60 employees were hired from 908 applicants who attended a job fair earlier this year.
- Recruitment efforts for Yachiyo to Carrollton began in October 2012 when the company's first team visited.
- Brian Gill, a Carroll County Chamber of Commerce vice president, handled overseas recruiting efforts.
- Donna Lackey, a Carroll County Chamber of Commerce vice president, provided assistance and offices to help the company get started.
- Carrollton Mayor Wayne Garner stated that local officials get along with each other and that city and county government work together well, which is beneficial for the community.
- Marty Smith is the chairman of the county Board of Commissioners.
- A special ceramic tray created by Carrollton artist Don McWhorter was presented to Yachiyo plant officials during the ceremony.
- Local government and company officials dedicated the planting of five trees near the building after the indoor ceremony.
