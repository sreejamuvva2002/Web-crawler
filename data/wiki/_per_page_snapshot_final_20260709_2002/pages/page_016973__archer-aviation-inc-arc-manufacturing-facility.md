# Archer Aviation Inc. ARC Manufacturing Facility

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-06

**Source:** [Archer Finishes Construction on GA Manufacturing Facility – Silicon Valley Daily](https://svdaily.com/2025/01/02/archer-finishes-construction-on-ga-manufacturing-facility/)  ·  `page_016973`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation Inc. has received the certificate of occupancy for its high-volume manufacturing facility, ARC.
- ARC is connected to the Covington Municipal Airport in Georgia.
- Archer is currently working to complete the tooling load-in for the initial manufacturing line at ARC.
- Production at ARC is planned to begin in early 2025.
- Archer aims to ramp production to a rate of two aircraft per month by the end of 2025.
- Archer built ARC in close partnership with Stellantis.
- Archer and Stellantis are continuing to progress their plans to mass-produce Archer’s Midnight aircraft.
- Stellantis is contributing capital, advanced manufacturing technology and expertise, and experienced personnel to the facility.
- The partnership aims to scale ARC's production capacity to 650 aircraft annually by 2030.
- ARC will serve as the production site for Archer's all-electric vertical take-off and landing (eVTOL) aircraft, named Midnight.
- The Midnight aircraft is designed to offer passengers a sustainable, low-noise, and safe alternative to ground transportation in congested cities around the world.
- Adam Goldstein, Archer’s founder and CEO, stated that construction on ARC is complete and the project was delivered quickly and on budget.
- Adam Goldstein noted that the facility's completion marks an industry shift from research and development into commercialization.
- Arnaud Deboeuf, Stellantis Chief Manufacturing and Supply Chain Officer, stated that supporting Archer’s electric aircraft growth leverages Stellantis's deep manufacturing expertise.
- Arnaud Deboeuf described the milestone as a significant step in taking clean mobility to the sky.
