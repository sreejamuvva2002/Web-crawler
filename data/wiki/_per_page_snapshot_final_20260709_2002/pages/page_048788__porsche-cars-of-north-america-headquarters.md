# Porsche Cars of North America Headquarters

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- The project location is Atlanta, GA.
- The project status is completed.
- The structure type is an office building.
- The facility targets and achieved LEED Gold certification.
- Whiting-Turner Contracting Co. served as the general contractor for the project.
- The client for the headquarters project was Porsche Cars North America.
- The project involved developing a 27-acre site into a 200,000 SF state-of-the-art facility.
- The headquarters consists of a building, an experience center, a business center, and an adjacent structure.
- The experience center includes a 1.6-mile drive development test track that runs through the courtyard of the complex.
- The test track includes six driving modules to demonstrate the experiences of different Porsche vehicles.
- The facility features a simulator lab where guests can race on 15 tracks in a virtual setting.
- The building provides modern facilities for 400 employees.
- Employee facilities include a technical service training center, a Heritage Center with galleries of vintage automobiles, a garage to work on concept vehicles, and an employee cafeteria.
- The training center uses advanced laboratory testing equipment to assess an individual’s physiological reaction to exercise.
- Assessments from the training center are used to formulate unique training plans that maximize efficiency.
- The facility includes Restaurant 356, which is named after the first automobile to carry the Porsche family name.
- The project was built on a remediated brownfield site previously occupied by a Ford Motor Company automobile plant.
- The facility was constructed at the end of an active runway at Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport.
- The airport is perennially deemed to be the world’s busiest airport, which required extensive coordination efforts for construction.
- The project received the 2015 Project of the Year award from the Construction Management Association of America – South Atlantic Chapter.
- The project received the 2015 Project of the Year award from ENR Southeast.
- The project received the 2015 Best Projects Award from ENR Southeast.
