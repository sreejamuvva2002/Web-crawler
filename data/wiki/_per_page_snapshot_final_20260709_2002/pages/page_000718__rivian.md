# Rivian

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-11-14

**Source:** [JDA, Rivian to close on Stanton Springs location - The Covington News](https://www.covnews.com/news/jda-rivian-close-stanton-springs-location/)  ·  `page_000718`

## Facts

- The Joint Development Authority (JDA) of Jasper, Morgan, Newton and Walton counties officially closed on bonds issued to finance the Rivian electric vehicle plant in Stanton Springs.
- A special called meeting was held on Nov. 6 where the JDA voted to approve the closing in a near unanimous decision.
- Only one JDA member, Ben Riden from Morgan County, opposed the closing approval.
- Rivian executed an exclusive rental agreement which will give the electric vehicle company long-term rights for the Stanton Springs property.
- The JDA stated that closing and issuing bonds for the Rivian project is a great day in Georgia.
- Renting the site to Rivian is the next step in delivering a generational opportunity for Georgians in Jasper, Morgan, Newton, and Walton counties and beyond.
- The project will bring $5 billion in investment to the region.
- The project will create 7,500 good-paying jobs.
- Grading work at the site will be completed by both the JDA and the state of Georgia in the weeks to come.
- Construction on the electric vehicle manufacturing facility will begin by early 2024.
- Rivian will continue efforts to grow its local presence by opening a new office in Covington.
- An apprenticeship program has been announced for the Stanton Springs location.
- Tuition for the apprenticeship will be covered by the state of Georgia’s HOPE grants.
- All other expenses for the apprenticeship will be paid for by Rivian.
- The goal of the apprenticeship is to assemble an initial group to support future industrial maintenance needs.
