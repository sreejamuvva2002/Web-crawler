# Georgia Department of Transportation

**Type:** event  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2022-05-05

**Source:** [Georgia Department of Transportation Demonstrates Safer, Connected Roadways with Kia Georgia and Panasonic Smart Mobility Technology on "The Ray Highway"](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/georgia-department-of-transportation-demonstrates-safer-connected-roadways-with-kia-georgia-and-panasonic-smart-mobility-technology-on-the-ray-highway-301541049.html)  ·  `page_029046`

## Facts

- On May 5, 2022, the Georgia Department of Transportation (GDOT), The Ray, Kia Georgia, and Panasonic demonstrated a fully operational connected vehicle environment along 18 miles of Interstate 85 known as "The Ray Highway."
- The demonstration utilized the Cirrus by Panasonic connected vehicle data management platform to detect real-time roadway events and communicate safety-critical messages to travelers about driving conditions.
- The partnership between GDOT, The Ray, Kia Georgia, and Panasonic is focused on raising awareness of the life-saving potential of connected vehicle technology and deploying real-world use cases with measurable benefits.
- GDOT and Kia Georgia vehicles equipped with vehicle-to-everything (V2X) technology were showcased during the demonstration.
- GDOT can now deliver real-time situational awareness to drivers on equipped roadways in this key logistics and manufacturing corridor, a fast-growing region of West Georgia.
- GDOT, The Ray, and Panasonic are providing "ride-along" experiences for stakeholders where drivers will be warned of hazardous conditions ahead on the roadway before they come into view.
- These ride-along experiences showcase how connected vehicle technology can detect and warn drivers about weather events, work zones, hard braking, and vehicle crashes in a real-world highway environment.
- The experience includes an in-vehicle visualization of traveler warnings, V2X technology capabilities installed on and along roadways, digital communication enabling connected roadway intelligence, and the Cirrus cloud platform's point of view for managing GDOT data to detect events and provide real-time alerts.
- Russell McMurry, Commissioner of the Georgia Department of Transportation, stated that it is important for GDOT to lead in deploying transportation technology that benefits all drivers, helps move freight across the region more efficiently and safely, and advances Georgia as the technology epicenter of the east coast.
- Russell McMurry emphasized that connected vehicle technology will save lives and expressed determination to see it come to life in Georgia as soon as possible through partnerships with Kia, The Ray, and Panasonic.
- Stuart Countess, President and CEO of Kia Georgia, stated that Kia Georgia is proud to partner with the organizations while showcasing products built by the Kia team in West Point, Georgia.
- Stuart Countess noted that customer safety is Kia Georgia's number one priority and described the V2X program as a forward-thinking initiative putting Kia at the heart of solutions where vehicles and infrastructure communicate seamlessly to enhance travel safety and efficiency.
- Allie Kelly, Executive Director of The Ray, stated that connected vehicle technology creates an opportunity to increase travel efficiency and driver safety simultaneously.
- Allie Kelly highlighted the responsibility to invest in physical and digital infrastructure necessary for connected and autonomous vehicles, noting that GDOT and The Ray have made those investments to modernize Georgia's transportation infrastructure.
- Allie Kelly explained that these investments offer travelers real-time warnings about critical driving conditions such as crashes, construction work zones, hard braking, and weather impacts like black ice, flooding, and hydroplaning.
- Chris Armstrong, Vice President of Cirrus at Panasonic's Smart Mobility Office, stated that the tech industry focuses on building technology with purpose rather than for its own sake.
- Chris Armstrong expressed pride in partnering with mission-driven organizations (GDOT, The Ray, Kia Georgia) to solve transportation challenges by bringing perspectives from public, private, and philanthropic sectors united in improving safety.
- Chris Armstrong emphasized the unique role each partner plays in championing the wide-scale adoption of this life-saving technology as quickly as possible.
- Panasonic Corporation of North America is based in Newark, NJ, and is a leading provider of Consumer Lifestyle technologies, Smart Mobility, Sustainable Energy, Immersive Experiences, and Integrated Supply Chain solutions.
- Panasonic is the principal North American subsidiary of Osaka, Japan-based Panasonic Holdings Corporation.
- Panasonic was named one of Interbrand's Top 100 Best Global Brands of 2021.
- Panasonic serves as a leading technology partner and integrator to businesses, government agencies, and consumers across the region.
