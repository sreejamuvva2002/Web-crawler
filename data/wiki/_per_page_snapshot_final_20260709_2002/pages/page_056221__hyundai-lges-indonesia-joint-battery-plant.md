# Hyundai-LGES Indonesia Joint Battery Plant

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2021-09-15

**Source:** [Hyundai, LGES start construction of Indonesia joint battery plant - The Korea Times](https://www.koreatimes.co.kr/business/companies/20210915/hyundai-lges-start-construction-of-indonesia-joint-battery-plant)  ·  `page_056221`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution officially kicked off construction of their joint electric vehicle battery plant in Indonesia.
- A groundbreaking ceremony was held at the construction site for the battery plant in Karawang New Industry City near Jakarta and Hyundai's existing car factory.
- Indonesian President Joko Widodo and several ministers and government officials attended the ceremony in person.
- Hyundai Motor Group Chairman Chung Euisun and LGES CEO Kim Jong-hyun attended the ceremony online due to the COVID-19 pandemic.
- Chairman Chung stated that Hyundai Motor Group is focusing on securing future competitiveness in the EV sector to become a global leader.
- Chung noted that as part of this effort, they decided to construct a joint Indonesia battery cell plant with LG Energy Solution.
- CEO Kim described the day as meaningful, stating that Indonesia's first next-generation battery cell plant will open the battery industry's future.
- Kim added that the joint plant gets them one step closer to establishing the world's first EV-integrated supply chain system.
- The two companies aim to complete construction of the plant in the first half of 2023.
- Mass production is scheduled to start in the first half of 2024.
- The plant will have an annual capacity of 10 gigawatts, enough to power approximately 150,000 EVs.
- The plant could be expanded to increase production capacity by 30 gigawatts when needed to meet a rise in EV demand.
- The plant is aimed at securing a stable supply of EV batteries for Hyundai Motor Co. and Kia Corp. starting in 2024.
- The batteries produced at the plant will embed LGES's latest battery technologies that maximize the properties of nickel, cobalt and manganese.
- These batteries will reduce chemical uncertainties by adding in high-performance aluminum.
- Hyundai Motor Group aims to expand its EV lineup to more than 23 models by 2025.
- Hyundai Motor Group plans to sell 1 million EVs by 2025.
- The batteries will be installed as part of Hyundai and Kia's E-GMP platform.
- In the first half of the year, the two companies agreed to construct a joint battery plant, with each taking half of the factory stakes.
- In July, the two companies signed a cooperation deal with the Indonesian government to inject $1.1 billion for the plant.
- The following month, the two companies established the joint venture.
- The two companies believe the Indonesian battery plant will be able to meet the drastically rising global demand for EVs.
- In 2019, the Indonesian government started to exempt luxury taxes for EVs and provide incentives pushing to foster the EV industry and supply chain, ranging from raw materials mining to manufacturing EVs and batteries.
