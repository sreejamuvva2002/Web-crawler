# Enchem Co.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-05

**Source:** [Korea’s battery material maker Enchem scraps $143 mn US plant plan - Open Korea](https://openkorea.org/feeds/koreas-battery-material-maker-enchem-scraps-143-mn-us-plant-plan/)  ·  `page_008008`

## Facts

- Enchem Co., a South Korean battery material maker, has scrapped its plan to invest $143 million to build a plant in the US.
- The scrapping is due to the protracted downturn in the world's third-largest electric vehicle market.
- Enchem is the world's No. 4 electrolyte manufacturer.
- Enchem cancelled its plan to spend 200 billion won ($142.7 million) to construct a production base in Tennessee.
- The Tennessee plant was intended to supply electrolytes to BlueOval SK LLC.
- BlueOval SK LLC is a joint venture between South Korea's battery maker SK On Co. and US automaker Ford Motor Co.
- Supply deals for the Tennessee plant were expected to be signed last year but were repeatedly delayed.
- Ford's EV business remains sluggish.
- Ford's EV division has lost $12 billion in total.
- Ford's EV division lost $2.2 billion in the first half of 2025.
- US President Donald Trump's policies are boosting internal combustion engine vehicles and hurting the EV industry.
- The US administration eliminated the tax credit benefit of up to $7,500 for EV buyers starting this month.
- Ford CEO Jim Farley stated that the elimination and softening of emissions rules will sharply curtail EV demand in the US.
- Jim Farley predicted the share of zero-emission vehicles, currently around 10% of the domestic market, could fall by half.
- The policies are expected to affect both the EV industry and the battery sector.
- BlueOval SK's total capacity is likely to rise to 127 GWh by next year.
- BlueOval SK is predicted to underutilize its facilities.
- An industry source in Seoul stated that all companies involved in the local EV value chain with Ford are now revising their strategies.
- Instead of the Tennessee project, Enchem plans to focus investments on its Georgia facility.
- Enchem's Georgia facility supplies electrolytes to LG Energy Solution Ltd.
- Enchem also has an Indiana plant that serves Samsung SDI Co.
