# Kia

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-04-25

**Source:** [Kia commits $1M to UGA's initiative on e-mobility](https://news.uga.edu/kia-commits-1m-to-ugas-initiative-on-e-mobility/)  ·  `page_009993`

## Facts

- Kia provided a $1 million gift to the UGA College of Engineering and the Georgia Network for Electric Mobility.
- The University of Georgia established the Georgia Network for Electric Mobility (GNEM) in early 2023.
- GNEM focuses on enhancing research, education, and outreach opportunities in e-mobility across the state of Georgia.
- GNEM brings together industry, academia, government, and communities to aid workforce development, economic development, and research.
- The network takes special care to involve rural and underserved communities and small businesses in its initiatives.
- The million-dollar commitment will provide endowed support for undergraduate scholarships, graduate research fellowships, community engagement programs, and experiential learning opportunities related to e-mobility.
- Experiential learning opportunities supported by the gift include co-ops, internships, and capstone projects for students in the College of Engineering.
- Kia established a state-of-the-art production facility in West Point, Georgia in 2009.
- Since 2009, Kia Georgia has helped create more than 14,000 jobs at its West Point facility.
- Since 2009, Kia Georgia has produced more than 4.2 million vehicles at its West Point facility.
- One of the vehicles soon to be produced at the West Point facility will be Kia’s all-electric vehicle, the EV9.
- As a founding partner of GNEM, Kia is providing support in perpetuity for educational pursuits, research productivity, and experiential learning efforts related to electric mobility careers.
- Kia was a premier sponsor of the University of Georgia’s third annual Electric Mobility Summit, which took place April 23-24.
- The Electric Mobility Summit focused on the adoption of electric mobility in Georgia, educational programs to support industry growth, and the future of e-mobility in Georgia and beyond.
- UGA President Jere W. Morehead stated that the university is thrilled to partner with Kia on the electric mobility initiative to advance technology, help students become leaders, and strengthen Georgia’s economy.
- Kia North America President and CEO Sean Yoon stated that Kia is committed to future e-mobility initiatives promoting sustainable transportation solutions in Georgia and across the nation.
- Kia Georgia President and CEO Stuart Countess stated that programs like GNEM are timely and critical for establishing a cohesive strategy for development and implementation of new initiatives within Georgia.
- College of Engineering Dean Donald Leo described Kia’s support as a testament to the power of collaboration between industry and academia.
- The gift aligns with UGA’s land-grant mission to serve all populations within Georgia by expanding GNEM’s impact on rural and underserved communities.
