# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-05-02

**Source:** [Inside Hyundai’s Massive Robot Push and What It Means for Car Buyers](https://www.autobroadcast.com/2026/05/02/inside-hyundais-massive-robot-push-and-what-it-means-for-car-buyers/)  ·  `page_001211`

## Facts

- Hyundai plans to order tens of thousands of Boston Dynamics robots over the next few years.
- Atlas humanoids are heading to a Georgia plant in 2028, scaling to 30,000 units a year.
- Hyundai Motor Group acquired Boston Dynamics in 2021 for $1.1 billion.
- Hyundai Motor Group announced it will invest $21 billion in the U.S., including a $6 billion push to drive new ideas and expand partnerships with American companies.
- Part of the investment is earmarked for hardware that does not punch a time clock, with plans to purchase tens of thousands of robots over the next few years.
- Hyundai will fold Boston Dynamics into its broader manufacturing operation to help become the world’s top maker of advanced mobile robots.
- Hyundai laid out its robotics timeline at CES 2026.
- The roadmap includes plans to build 30,000 Atlas humanoid robots a year by 2028 at its sprawling “Metaplant” near Savannah.
- The robots will be put to work in factories and warehouses worldwide.
- Deployment will start gradually in 2028 at the Savannah plant, where Hyundai builds electric and hybrid vehicles like the Ioniq 5 and Ioniq 9.
- Atlas will initially focus on parts sequencing, making sure vehicle components land in the correct spots on the line when needed.
- By 2030, Hyundai will start using Atlas for more complex tasks, including component assembly and jobs involving repetitive motions or heavy lifting.
- Spot, the four-legged robot dog, is already operational.
- Spot robots are inspecting vehicles on the assembly line at Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- The Atlas humanoid can charge itself and keep working without stopping.
- Atlas runs at a steady pace and automatically walks itself over to a charging station to swap out its own batteries when power is low.
- Once a single Atlas learns a new task, that task can be copied across the entire fleet.
- The deployment aims to catch defects early and keep tolerances tight on every car that rolls off the line.
- Better quality control at the plant means fewer warranty claims, fewer recalls, and stronger long-term durability.
- The impact of pouring money into robotics will likely be felt beyond new vehicle production, shaping factory floor inspections and the long-term reliability of certified pre-owned cars.
- Tesla plans to reveal the latest version of its Optimus robot in February or March, with production slated to begin by the end of 2026.
- Mercedes-Benz bought a stake in robotics firm Apptronik and is testing its robots in a couple of its German factories.
- BMW has been trying out Figure AI’s humanoid robots at its Spartanburg, South Carolina plant.
- Former employees say Hyundai is pressuring Boston Dynamics to produce “tens of thousands” of robots in the next few years for its automotive plants.
- Boston Dynamics is reportedly making just four Atlas robots per month as it figures out how to ramp up large-scale manufacturing.
- The production climb to 30,000 a year has reportedly contributed to executive shake-ups inside Boston Dynamics.
- Tighter assembly tolerances are expected on Ioniq EVs first, followed by similar gains across Kia and Genesis lineups.
- Hyundai will use its Hyundai Mobis auto parts arm and Hyundai Glovis logistics firm to build an end-to-end robotics value chain that can churn out 30,000 robots a year.
- Vehicles produced in 2030 are expected to share factory floors with thousands of humanoid coworkers.
