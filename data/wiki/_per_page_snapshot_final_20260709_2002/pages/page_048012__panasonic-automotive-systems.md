# Panasonic Automotive Systems

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2021-01-10

**Source:** [Panasonic Automotive Systems: Startup partners with Panasonic to provide a better in-car experience with holographic technology, ETAuto](https://auto.economictimes.indiatimes.com/news/auto-technology/startup-partners-with-panasonic-to-provide-a-better-in-car-experience-with-holographic-technology/80198453)  ·  `page_048012`

## Facts

- UK-based holographic technology startup Envisics reached an agreement with Panasonic Automotive Systems to jointly develop and commercialize a new generation of head-up displays for cars, trucks, and SUVs.
- Panasonic Automotive Systems is a Tier 1 automotive supplier and a division of Panasonic Corporation of North America.
- The head-up displays are units integrated into the dash of a vehicle that project images onto the windshield to aid drivers with navigation and provide other alerts.
- The Panasonic HUDs will use Envisics holographic technology.
- The deal was announced ahead of the virtual 2021 CES tech trade show.
- The announcement follows Envisics' $50 million Series B funding round, which brought the company a valuation of more than $250 million.
- Investors in Envisics' funding round included Hyundai Mobis, GM Ventures, SAIC Ventures, and Van Tuyl Companies.
- Envisics' technology will be integrated into the upcoming Cadillac Lyriq electric vehicle.
- Envisics is solely focused on the automotive application of holography.
- Envisics' first generation holographic technology is already integrated in more than 150,000 Jaguar Land Rover vehicles.
- The new agreement aims to combine Panasonic's expertise in optical design and its global reach as a Tier 1 supplier with Envisics' technology to bring holography into the mainstream.
- Mass production of vehicles using the combined technology is slated for 2023.
- The head-up displays will feature high resolution, wide color gamut, and large images that can be overlaid upon reality.
- The technology can project information at multiple distances simultaneously.
- Potential applications include night vision by overlaying enhanced information upon a dark road to clarify the path and obstacles.
- Short-term applications will include augmented reality features like navigation, lane highlighting, and safety applications.
- Future applications for autonomous driving could unlock opportunities in entertainment and video conferencing.
