# Archer Aviation Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-07-09

**Source:** [HeliHub.com : Stellantis Invests $55M more into Archer - HeliHub.com](https://www.helihub.com/2024/07/09/stellantis-invests-55m-more-into-archer/)  ·  `page_016909`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation received an additional $55 million investment from Stellantis under the companies’ strategic funding agreement.
- The additional investment follows Archer's achievement of its transition flight test milestone last month (June 2024).
- This latest investment builds on a previously announced series of open market purchases by Stellantis of 8.3 million shares of Archer’s stock in March 2024.
- During 2023, Stellantis invested $110 million into Archer through a combination of open market stock purchases and investments under the strategic funding agreement.
- Archer remains on track to complete construction of its high-volume manufacturing facility in Georgia later this year (2024).
- The first phase of the facility build-out is a ~350,000 square foot facility located on an ~100 acre site.
- The facility is designed to support production of up to 650 aircraft annually.
- This capacity would make it one of the largest manufacturing facilities by volume in the aircraft industry.
- Archer aims to establish a factory that supports its planned commercial ramp by leveraging Stellantis as its contract manufacturer.
- Stellantis has served as a strategic partner to Archer since 2020 through various collaboration initiatives.
- Stellantis has been an investor in Archer since 2021.
- Archer leveraged Stellantis’ deep manufacturing, supply chain, and design expertise to design, develop, and commercialize its eVTOL aircraft.
- Carlos Tavares, Stellantis CEO, stated: “Few things rival the excitement of seeing a dream take flight. I applaud the innovation, expertise and hard work of the engineering and manufacturing teams from Stellantis and Archer.”
- Carlos Tavares added: “With this additional investment in Archer, we remain on course for a future where freedom of mobility extends beyond today’s roads.”
- Adam Goldstein, Archer CEO, stated: “The commitment by Stellantis to Archer has been unrivaled, from its foresight to provide the manufacturing expertise and capital needed to accelerate Archer’s business objectives, to the strategic vision and steadfast support from Stellantis CEO Carlos Tavares.”
- Adam Goldstein added: “Together, we’re working to redefine urban transportation, opening new worlds of opportunity for citizens across the globe by providing more efficient access to people, places, and events across the regions they live in.”
- Archer’s goal is to transform urban travel by replacing 60–90-minute commutes by car with estimated 10–20-minute electric air taxi flights that are safe, sustainable, low noise, and cost-competitive with ground transportation.
- Archer’s Midnight aircraft is a piloted, four-passenger aircraft designed to perform rapid back-to-back flights with minimal charge time between flights.
