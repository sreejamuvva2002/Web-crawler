# Georgia Department of Economic Development

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2020-08-17

**Source:** [GA Dept. of Economic Development announces new international trade representative](https://valdostatoday.com/business-2/2020/08/ludovic-ortuno-named-new-gdecd-canadian-trade-representative/)  ·  `page_045706`

## Facts

- GDEcD announced Ludovic Ortuno as the new Canadian trade representative on August 17, 2020.
- Ortuno is the co-founder and chief operating officer for CIDEP.
- Ortuno will direct a team of four other specialists to continue the state’s international trade representation in Canada.
- Canada has been Georgia's largest export customer for more than two decades.
- Gail Morris, Georgia's longtime Canadian representative in Trade, Foreign Direct Investment (FDI), and Tourism, recently retired after 10 years.
- Ortuno will serve as Managing Director leading a team of export and investment attraction specialists at CIDEP.
- The team will advance Georgia's relationship with Canada, support existing Canadian companies in the state, and explore opportunities under the United States-Mexico-Canada Agreement (USMCA).
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson welcomed Ortuno to the strategic international team.
- Wilson thanked Morris for her 10 years of service as Georgia's managing director in Canada.
- The new Canada team consists of five professionals serving from CIDEP’s headquarters in Montreal, Québec.
- Montreal is a partner to Georgia in both the Southeastern United States-Canadian Provinces (SEUS-CP) Alliance and the Regional Leaders Summit (RLS).
- Through the RLS, seven international regions from five different continents exchange best practices on topics of mutual interest.
- Other RLS members include Upper Austria, Austria; São Paolo, Brazil; Shandong, China; Bavaria, Germany; and Western Cape, South Africa.
- The team will advance Georgia's role as a founder of the SEUS-CP Alliance to present opportunities for businesses to enhance commercial exchanges and promote two-way trade among six Southeastern states and six Canadian provinces.
- The inaugural SEUS-CP Conference was hosted by Georgia in Savannah in 2008.
- Since 1973, Georgia has been home to the Canadian Consulate General, one of 16 Canadian diplomatic offices in the U.S.
- The consulate promotes business development, investment, tourism, culture, and information exchange between Canada and the Southeastern U.S.
- In addition to Georgia, the consulate serves Alabama, Mississippi, North Carolina, South Carolina, and Tennessee.
- Nadia Theodore, Consul General of Canada to the Southeast U.S., welcomed Ortuno's appointment as a sign of the continued importance of the Canada-Georgia relationship.
- Georgia is home to the Québec Government Office, established in 1978, which promotes economic and political ties between Québec and 11 Southeastern states.
- Donald Leblanc, delegate of the Québec Government Office in Atlanta, stated that annual trade between Georgia and Québec now surpasses $2.5 billion.
- Georgia and Québec are actively involved as co-founders in the SEUS-CP Alliance and the Regional Leaders Summit.
- In 2019, Georgia exports to Canada, its No. 1 export market, were valued at $5.9 billion.
- Top exports from Georgia to Canada included motor vehicles and vehicle parts and accessories, civilian aircraft, and carpet.
- Georgia leads the nation in the export of carpets, acrylic polymers, and poultry.
- In 2019, Georgia imports from Canada totaled $5.1 billion, putting Canada at No. 7 among Georgia’s importer nations.
- Top imports from Canada included powered aircraft, turbojets and parts, ethylene polymers, and wood.
- CIDEP’s team will provide support to Canadian companies in Georgia such as Coreslab Structures, Decostar, Magna International, and Irving Tissue.
- Ortuno and his colleagues will assist in attracting new companies to invest in and bring their operations to the state.
- The team will provide guidance to nearly 90 Georgia companies in Canada, including The Home Depot, Marble Slab Creamery, Arby’s, and Cox Enterprises.
- CIDEP was founded in 2006 with offices in Montréal and Toronto.
- CIDEP's mission is to support economic development, trade organizations, and corporations in attracting global investments and export opportunities.
- GDEcD serves as the state’s sales and marketing arm and the lead agency for attracting new business investment, encouraging industry expansion, locating markets for Georgia products, attracting tourists, and promoting the state for arts, film, music, and digital entertainment projects.
