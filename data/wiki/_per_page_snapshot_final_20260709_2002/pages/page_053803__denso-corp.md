# DENSO Corp.

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** workforce_training  ·  **Published:** 2020-02-10

**Source:** [DENSO Opens Central Tech Training Center In TN](https://www.aftermarketnews.com/denso-opens-central-tech-training-center-in-tn/)  ·  `page_053803`

## Facts

- DENSO announced the opening of its Central Technical Training Center at its facility in Maryville, Tennessee.
- The state-of-the-art technical training center was designed by DENSO to educate employees about the latest technologies and best practices in advanced manufacturing.
- The initiative aims to ensure the company and its workforce remain leaders in manufacturing as it becomes more automated, connected, and data-rich.
- The development is part of DENSO’s 2017 commitment to invest $1 billion in the Maryville location.
- That investment has helped DENSO in Maryville expand its production of vehicle safety and electrification systems.
- The investment has helped create 1,000 jobs at the Maryville location.
- The investment established new employee training opportunities and reinforced the site’s ability to be a primary manufacturing center for DENSO in North America.
- All of this supports DENSO’s Long-Term Policy 2030, which aims to create new value for advanced mobility.
- Kenichiro Ito, senior executive officer of DENSO Corp. and CEO of DENSO’s North American headquarters, stated that DENSO’s manufacturing philosophy is to produce products in the region, for the region.
- Ito noted that as a leading mobility supplier, DENSO manufactures products and systems for all major automakers in North America.
- Ito emphasized it is crucial to remain at manufacturing’s leading edge to help customers realize new mobility solutions, with the technical training center helping accomplish that.
- DENSO in Maryville plans to educate more than 1,500 employees annually at the training facility.
- The facility features labs focused on electrical, robotic, and mechanical applications in manufacturing.
- DENSO employees will have access to more than 50 hands-on courses in production-related areas, including electrical, mechanical, and mechatronic technologies, and automation fundamentals.
- Future course programming will expand into rapidly evolving fields, including cybersecurity, artificial intelligence, and data analytics.
- DENSO collaborates with local colleges and high schools to better prepare students interested in manufacturing for successful and rewarding careers in the industry.
- Jack Helmboldt, president emeritus at DENSO in Maryville, stated they have manufactured technology in east Tennessee that makes cars safer and cleaner for more than 30 years.
- Helmboldt noted that sustained production is impossible without the support and skill of the local community.
- Helmboldt added that opening the Central Technical Training Center demonstrates commitment to employees and to maintaining DENSO and east Tennessee’s position as a manufacturing leader in North America.
- Speakers at the training facility’s grand opening included Ito and Bob Booker, senior manager of Legal Services and Corporate Compliance at DENSO in Maryville.
- Blount County Mayor Ed Mitchell and Commissioner of the Tennessee Department of Economic and Community Development Bob Rolfe also spoke at the grand opening.
- The Central Technical Training Center’s opening follows DENSO investing $1.95 million last year in its thermal manufacturing facility in Battle Creek, Michigan to open the North Technical Training Center.
- Both training centers bolster DENSO’s manufacturing expertise across North America, according to the company.
- DENSO’s Maryville location has nearly 5,000 employees and specializes in production of vehicle safety systems and electrification components.
- DENSO has other Tennessee locations in Athens, Mount Juliet, and Jackson.
