# Novelis Innovation Hub at Georgia Tech

**Type:** research_center  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2019-08-22

**Source:** [Novelis and Georgia Tech Establish Novelis Innovation Hub | Supply Chain and Logistics Institute](https://www.scl.gatech.edu/news/novelis-and-georgia-tech-establish-novelis-innovation-hub)  ·  `page_047401`

## Facts

- Atlanta-based Novelis Inc. and Georgia Tech have announced a new collaboration to establish the Novelis Innovation Hub at Georgia Tech.
- Novelis has committed $2.5 million to initiate research, faculty, student, and educational program support.
- The collaboration will promote basic and translational research, innovative business models, and related educational endeavors at Georgia Tech.
- The hub will serve as a cross-functional hub connecting Novelis’ technical and business innovators with Georgia Tech’s students and faculty.
- Steve Fisher, president and CEO of Novelis, stated that Georgia Tech is one of the world’s premier research institutions in their hometown.
- Fisher noted the opportunity to collaborate with Georgia Tech to help train a new generation of students poised to advance engineering and research frontiers.
- With Novelis funding, Georgia Tech intends to recruit a senior interdisciplinary faculty member to serve as the director of the Novelis Innovation Hub.
- The hub director is expected to be identified and in place by the 2020-21 academic year.
- Chaouki Abdallah, Georgia Tech’s executive vice president for research, stated the collaboration demonstrates a focus on providing students and faculty with unmatched academic and practical experiences through innovative corporate relationships.
- Abdallah highlighted Georgia Tech’s expertise in materials science, advanced manufacturing, and business and AI systems as among the best in global higher education.
- Under the agreement terms, Novelis will annually identify sponsored research and provide a list of innovation needs for Georgia Tech faculty to propose solutions.
- An advisory board comprised of representatives from Georgia Tech and Novelis will evaluate proposals utilizing Novelis funding and external funding opportunities.
- The advisory board will also foster future technology collaborations.
- Todd Summe, chief research and development officer at Novelis, stated establishing closer ties with Georgia Tech provides tremendous benefits for students and the company.
- Summe noted combined efforts will advance Novelis’s mission to lead the aluminum industry as the partner of choice for innovative solutions.
- The partnership aims to fill the industry’s talent pipeline with outstanding innovators.
- Summe described this as the next step in a partnership that can serve as a model for transformative innovation across Novelis, the Aditya Birla Group, and the industry at large.
- Novelis is building upon longstanding ties with Georgia Tech, having historically provided internships and full-time employment as well as mentorship to more than 50 graduate students over the years.
- Novelis has consistently funded sponsored research as well as research equipment at Georgia Tech.
- Most recently, Novelis co-invested in additive manufacturing in the Advanced Manufacturing Pilot Facility.
- The company provides funds for graduate student research with renowned faculty and for Georgia Tech’s InVenture Prize, a student competition fostering creativity, invention, and entrepreneurship.
- Novelis Inc. is a global leader in innovative products and services and the world's largest recycler of aluminum.
- The company is headquartered in Atlanta, Georgia.
- Novelis operates 23 facilities in nine countries.
- Novelis has approximately 11,000 employees.
- Novelis recorded $12.3 billion in revenue for its 2019 fiscal year.
- Novelis is a subsidiary of Hindalco Industries Limited, an industry leader in aluminum and copper.
- Hindalco Industries Limited is the metals flagship company of the Aditya Birla Group, a multinational conglomerate based in Mumbai, India.
