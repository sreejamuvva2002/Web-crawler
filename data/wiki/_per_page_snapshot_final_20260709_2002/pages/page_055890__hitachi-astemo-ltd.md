## Facts

- In January 2021, Hitachi Automotive Systems, Keihin, Showa, and Nissin Kogyo completed a business merger to form the global mega-supplier Hitachi Astemo.
- Hitachi Astemo is headquartered in Tokyo, Japan, and operates as a joint venture between Hitachi, Ltd. and Honda Motor Co.
- Brice Koch serves as President & CEO of Hitachi Astemo, Ltd.
- Gary Plover of Hitachi Astemo Americas, Inc. has been appointed to CAWA's Manufacturers' Advisory Council.
- Gary Plover was named Head of Aftermarket Division for Hitachi Astemo Americas in April 2022.
- Hannah Hua was appointed as Marketing Manager of Aftermarket Sales at Hitachi Astemo Americas.
- On September 26, 2022, Hitachi Astemo received EV e-Axle orders from Honda and announced plans to further expand its electrification business.
- On October 31, 2022, Hitachi Astemo announced it will provide inverters and motors to JATCO Ltd., which will supply e-Axles to Nissan's future e-POWER and battery electric vehicles.
- For Nissan's electric axles, Hitachi Astemo will deliver efficient, low-loss motors using square wire technology, along with efficient inverters.
- Hitachi Astemo develops in-house electric axles intended for sale to various automobile manufacturers globally.
- The company developed a Steer-by-Wire prototype with advanced steering and failsafe functions, announced on October 3, 2022.
- On October 20, 2022, Hitachi Astemo developed a prototype of a new steer-by-wire steering device that expands cabin space.
- On October 21, 2022, Hitachi Astemo developed a prototype 360-Degree Stereo Vision system with multi-camera 3D sensing.
- Hitachi Astemo's product segments include engine management systems, electric powertrain systems, drive control systems, and car information systems.
- The company is committed to achieving carbon neutrality at its business sites (factories and offices) by FY2030.
- Hitachi Astemo reduced CO2 emissions at factories and offices by 11% in FY2021 compared to FY2010.
- A facility named Hitachi Astemo Rock Spring, Inc. is located at 11782 in Rock Spring, Georgia, United States, with job listings for a Machining General Associate (3rd Shift) role.
- Hitachi Astemo Americas, Inc. maintains an office at 10833 Valley View St. Suite 400, Cypress, CA 90630 U.S.A., formerly known as Hitachi Automotive Systems Americas, Inc.
- Hitachi America, Ltd. was established in 1959 as a regional subsidiary, with its first U.S. manufacturing facility opening in 1977.
- Hitachi Astemo's entry into the Americas began in 1972 with a subsidiary established in Brazil.
- The company expanded ignition coil coverage to 260 million VIO in April 2022.
