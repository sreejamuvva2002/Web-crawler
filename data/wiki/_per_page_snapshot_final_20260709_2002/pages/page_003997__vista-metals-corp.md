# Vista Metals Corp.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2024-03-19

**Source:** [Gov. Beshear: Vista Metals To Invest $60 Million in Aluminum Casting Facility in Bowling Green, Creating Over 50 Full-Time Jobs | Kentucky Cabinet for Economic Development](https://newkentuckyhome.ky.gov/Newsroom/NewsPage/20240319_VistaMetals)  ·  `page_003997`

## Facts

- Gov. Andy Beshear congratulated Vista Metals as Kentucky’s metals industry continues to grow at a rapid pace.
- Vista Metals is a market leader in the manufacturing of specialty aluminum products primarily supporting aerospace, commercial aircraft, defense, automotive, and general industrial applications.
- Vista Metals plans to locate a new specialty aluminum products operation in Bowling Green, creating over 50 well-paying full-time jobs behind an approximate $60 million investment.
- Initial site preparation for the new facility is already underway.
- Vista Metals will commence with a phased build-out to support growth in its core markets.
- Gov. Andy Beshear stated that Kentucky’s metals sector growth is not slowing down and highlighted Warren County as among the leading communities for industry growth.
- Vista Metals plans to acquire more than 100 acres for the new facility.
- The company will construct a 75,000-plus-square-foot facility.
- Jobs created at the facility will include roles in operations, planning, engineering, and site management.
- Andy Primack, CEO of Vista Metals, announced the new Kentucky location and emphasized Bowling Green’s strong industrial business base, qualified manufacturing talent pipeline, business-education interaction, and long-term community development plan.
- Kelly Thomas, president of Vista Metals, stated the investment underscores a commitment to the aerospace industry and robust supply chains as OEMs look to more than double commercial aircraft build rates over the next two decades.
- Vista Metals intends to enable customers to test and qualify the new facility ahead of projected growth surges while continuing ongoing investments in people, processes, and equipment at existing facilities.
- VMC Specialty Alloys LLC (dba Vista Metals Georgia) operates under a licensing agreement with Fontana, California-based Vista Metals Corp.
- Along with Vista Progressive Metals LLC, which produces aluminum lithium products, the group of companies are market leaders in specialty aluminum products for aerospace, commercial aircraft, defense, automotive, and general industrial applications.
- Warren County Judge/Executive Doug Gorman noted the region’s strength in supporting new business and highlighted Warren County’s position as a hub for innovation and manufacturing excellence.
- Bowling Green Mayor Todd Alcott stated the community has the resources, skilled workforce, and robust infrastructure needed to ensure the company’s success.
- Ron Bunch, CEO of the Bowling Green Area Chamber of Commerce, thanked project partners including Atmos Energy, TVA, Warren County Water District, Warren RECC, Scotty’s Contracting & Stone, and Presidents Club investors.
- Vista Metals’ investment builds on the best four-year period for economic growth in Kentucky state history.
- Since the beginning of Gov. Beshear’s administration, more than 1,000 private-sector new-location and expansion projects have been announced totaling over $30.3 billion in investments and creating more than 52,000 jobs.
- This investment figure is the highest secured during the tenure of any governor in Kentucky’s history.
- The average incentivized hourly wage in Kentucky topped $26 in consecutive years for the first time in 2022 and 2023.
- Gov. Beshear has solidified Kentucky as the electric vehicle battery production capital of the United States through several major projects.
- Ford Motor Co. and SK On announced a transformative $5.8 billion, 5,000-job BlueOval SK Battery Park in Hardin County.
- AESC announced a $2 billion, 2,000-job gigafactory project in Warren County.
- Toyota announced a $1.3 billion investment in Scott County.
- INFAC North America announced a $53 million investment in Taylor County.
- The Governor’s administration secured the largest General Fund budget surplus and Rainy Day Fund, as well as the most jobs filled in state history.
- Kentucky set the record for the longest period with the lowest unemployment rates in state history last year.
- Kentucky secured rating increases from major credit rating agencies Fitch Ratings and S&P Global Ratings.
- Moody’s Investors Service upgraded Kentucky’s credit outlook from stable to positive.
- Site Selection magazine ranked Kentucky third nationally and first in the South Central region for economic development projects per capita in its 2023 Governor’s Cup rankings.
- Previously, Site Selection placed Kentucky first in the South Central region and top 5 nationally in its 2023 Prosperity Cup ranking for state-level economic development agencies.
- Gov. Beshear announced a “Supply Kentucky” initiative aimed at boosting job growth, reducing costs, and providing more security in the supply chains of Kentucky companies.
- The Kentucky Economic Development Finance Authority (KEDFA) preliminarily approved a 15-year incentive agreement with Vista Metals under the Kentucky Business Investment program.
- The performance-based agreement can provide up to $2.5 million in tax incentives based on capital investment and job creation.
- KEDFA also approved Vista Metals for up to $500,000 in tax incentives through the Kentucky Enterprise Initiative Act (KEIA).
- KEIA allows approved companies to recoup Kentucky sales and use tax on construction costs, building fixtures, equipment used in research and development, and electronic processing.
- Vista Metals is entitled to receive resources from Kentucky’s workforce service providers, including no-cost recruitment and job placement services, reduced-cost customized training, and job-training incentives.
