# Daechang Seat Corp. USA

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-10-23

**Source:** [Automotive Supplier Daechang Seat Corp. USA to Create 500 Jobs in Chatham County - Charge Forward Georgia](https://www.chargeforwardga.com/news-events/news/automotive-supplier-daechang-seat-corp-usa-to-create-500-jobs-in-chatham-county/)  ·  `page_018885`

## Facts

- On October 23, 2023, Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Daechang Seat Corp. USA will invest $72.5 million in the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center.
- The facility is located in close proximity to Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA).
- The project will create over 500 jobs.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that suppliers for the HMGMA project alone reach at least nine counties, and DSC is being added to the growing manufacturing base centered around Chatham County and the Savannah region.
- Daechang Seat Corp. (DSC) was established in 1979 in Kyungju, South Korea.
- Globally, DSC employs more than 5,000 people across seven countries.
- The company’s production process includes fully automatic lines with arc welding and spot-welding machines.
- Jinsuk Lee, CEO of Daechang Seat Savannah Corp., expressed appreciation to the State of Georgia, its local communities, and the City of Savannah for their support.
- DSC's primary objective is to drive growth through facility expansions to transform into a global entity.
- Presently, DSC holds a prominent partnership with Hyundai Transys and Hyundai Mobis at HMGMA.
- DSC hopes to collaborate with even more projects in the future.
- DSC’s new facility will be located at the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center.
- The company will hire for assembly operators, equipment technicians, mechanical engineers, and electrical engineers.
- Hiring will occur through local job fairs and website postings in the future.
- Griff Lynch, Georgia Ports Authority President and CEO, noted the facility is only minutes from the Port of Savannah, ideally located to leverage Georgia’s world-class connectivity.
- The Georgia Ports Authority aims to provide the best global gateway for U.S. supply chains and welcomes DSC as the latest job creator in Georgia’s automaking sector.
- Trip Tollison, Savannah Economic Development Authority President and CEO, announced that Daechang Seat Corp. USA will be the third Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America supplier to locate in the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center.
- The project brings 500 new jobs and $72.5 million in capital investment to Chatham County.
- Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy Alyce Thornhill represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The GDEcD partnered with the Savannah Economic Development Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start for this project.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that countless partners are helping transform Georgia into a center of the automotive and mobility industry.
- Chatham County has leveraged Savannah's status as an established global gateway to create a bustling community for residents and workers.
- As the market transitions to electric vehicles, Georgia has pursued the entire supply chain.
- Georgia has created more than $25 billion in investments and 30,000 jobs since 2018.
- In fiscal year 2023, job creation in the automotive industry increased by 324 percent compared to FY21.
- Suppliers attracted by Georgia’s mobility original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) resulted in over $2 billion in investment across the state in FY23.
- Daechang Seat Co. is recognized for its quality and technological expertise in the automotive seat sector.
- DSC has recently ventured into the electric vehicle sector and continues to expand its scope.
- The completion of new manufacturing facilities will provide larger installation spaces, anticipating greater opportunities for growth.
