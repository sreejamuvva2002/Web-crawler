# Astemo Americas, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-03-27

**Source:** [Automotive Components Manufacturer to Shut Tallapoosa Facility, Lay Off 60](https://whatnow.com/news/trending/automotive-components-manufacturer-to-shut-tallapoosa-facility-lay-off-60/)  ·  `page_008750`

## Facts

- Astemo Americas, Inc. filed a WARN notice on March 27, 2026.
- The company will permanently close its manufacturing facility at 550 Honda Parkway, Tallapoosa, Georgia 30176.
- Out of the total 89 employees at the facility, 60 will be laid off.
- The layoffs are expected to take effect on or after April 3, 2026.
- The plant closure is scheduled for on or around May 1, 2026.
- The company cited unforeseen business circumstances for the sudden closure and layoffs.
- Due to these circumstances, the company could not provide the standard 60 days’ advance notice required by the federal Worker Adjustment and Retraining Notification (WARN) Act.
- On March 25, a key customer informed the company that it would be cancelling its contract regarding all four vehicle model parts produced at the facility.
- Severe business disruption from the cancelled contract led to the decision to close the facility.
- Astemo Americas, Inc. is a subsidiary of Astemo, Ltd., a global advanced automotive technologies company formed in 2021 through the merger of Hitachi Automotive Systems, Keihin, Showa, and Nissin Kogyo.
- The company was previously named Hitachi Astemo before being rebranded to Astemo.
- Astemo provides automotive components and solutions, advanced Chassis systems, and electric powertrain systems.
- In its filing, the company stated that the closure decision could not be predicted earlier and that it provided as much notice as practicable under the circumstances.
