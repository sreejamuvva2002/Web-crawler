# Georgia Department of Economic Development

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2025-02-14

**Source:** [Georgia Breaks Export Records, Rises in Total Trade | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-releases/2025/georgia-breaks-export-records-rises-total-trade)  ·  `page_002433`

## Facts

- The State of Georgia surpassed $53.1 billion in exports in 2024.
- Georgia's export growth represented a year-over-year increase of 6.4%, outpacing the national average of 2.3%.
- Georgia moved up to rank sixth in the United States for the dollar value of total trade.
- The state facilitated more than $198.7 billion in trade with 222 unique countries and territories.
- More than 87% of Georgia exporters are small businesses.
- In 2024, Georgia exported to 219 unique destinations.
- Georgia retained its No. 12 ranking in the United States for the dollar value of exports.
- Civilian aircraft and ancillary parts remained Georgia's No. 1 export category.
- Other top export categories included motor vehicles, data processing machines (computers), electrical apparatus for line telephony, and medical devices.
- Markets with full-time Georgia international trade representatives accounted for 66% of exports and 83% of bilateral trade in 2024.
- Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport is the busiest and most efficient airport in the world.
- The Port of Savannah is the fastest growing and third-busiest container gateway in the United States.
- The Port of Brunswick serves as one of the nation's busiest gateways for Roll-on/Roll-off cargo.
- The Georgia Ports Authority reported twelve consecutive months of year-over-year container volume growth.
- Georgia has more rail miles than any other state in the Southeast.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated that strategic investments and commitments to job creators are delivering results for Georgians.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson noted that Georgia offers extensive partnerships, expert guidance on export strategies, and top-tier infrastructure including deepwater ports, railways, highways, and airports.
- Deputy Commissioner of Trade Lizann Grupalo highlighted that international representatives provide on-the-ground insights to navigate the global environment for Georgia-based small business exporters.
- GDEcD's annual International Trade Report tracks merchandise trade using four-digit Harmonized System (HS) codes and origin of movement via data from Trade Data Monitor.
- GDEcD's Trade team includes international representatives located in more than a dozen strategic global markets to assist Georgia companies with expanding sales worldwide.
