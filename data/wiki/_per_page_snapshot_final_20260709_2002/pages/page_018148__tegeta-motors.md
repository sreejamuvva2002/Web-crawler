# Tegeta Motors

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2025-11-20

**Source:** [Tegeta Motors Signs Agreement With Bridgestone to Build a State-of-the-art Bandag Plant • Forbes Georgia](https://forbes.ge/en/tegeta-motors-signs-agreement-with-bridgestone-to-build-a-state-of-the-art-bandag-plant/)  ·  `page_018148`

## Facts

- The Bandag plant will be built in Rustavi, representing a $2 million investment.
- A landmark agreement between Tegeta Motors and Bridgestone will see a Bandag plant built in the Caucasus for the first time.
- Tegeta Motors will become the exclusive regional operator of the Bandag plant.
- The plant will use Bandag’s cutting-edge tire retreading technology to extend the life of truck and bus tires.
- Bandag is a Bridgestone subsidiary that produces millions of high-quality retreaded tires annually for global markets.
- The agreement was signed in Tbilisi at Tegeta Motors’ multi-functional service center.
- Jacques Fourier, President of Bridgestone Core Tire Business Africa & Middle East, flew to Georgia to attend the signing event.
- Ekaterine Kavtaradze serves as Chief Executive Officer of Tegeta Holding.
- The partnership between Tegeta Motors and Bridgestone spans 27 years and is entering a new phase with this agreement.
- Tegeta Motors currently serves as the regional distributor for the Bridgestone brand in Georgia.
- At the initial stage, the plant aims to produce up to 5,000 retreaded tires per year in the region.
- Production capacity is planned to double over the next five years.
- $2 million has been invested in the project thus far.
- Initially, Tegeta Motors will retread tires for all types of trucks and buses in full compliance with international standards.
- The holding plans to export retreaded tires to Armenia and Azerbaijan in the next stage.
- Each retreaded tire costs 30%-40% less than purchasing a new tire.
- Refurbished tires have a service life of up to 5 years.
- Construction of the Bandag plant in Rustavi is scheduled to begin this year, with completion targeted for late 2026.
- Representatives from Bridgestone and Bandag will oversee construction to ensure compliance with global standards.
- The facility will be outfitted with state-of-the-art Bandag machinery sourced from Europe and the US.
- Specialists from Bridgestone will provide targeted technical training for the Georgian team before production begins.
- Six months of on-site supervision by Bridgestone and Bandag specialists will follow once the plant becomes operational.
- The retreading process uses a shearograph to detect internal flaws in tire casings with exceptional precision.
- Tegeta Motors possesses the only shearograph of its kind available in the region, which will be exclusive to the Rustavi plant.
- Once structural integrity is confirmed, the remaining tread is replaced with new, high-quality material.
- The cost per kilometer of a refurbished tire is significantly lower than that of a new tire while delivering comparable performance.
- Bandag’s retreading technology requires less raw material and energy, reducing waste generation and carbon emissions.
- Tegeta Motors emphasizes environmental sustainability and eco-friendly solutions as core priorities for the facility.
- Future plans include building a tire recycling facility capable of managing the entire tire life cycle and minimizing waste.
- Tegeta Motors has operated since 1995 as part of Tegeta Holding and is a leading company in Georgia’s automotive sector.
- The company provides comprehensive services for cars, minibuses, trucks, buses, and special-purpose equipment across 28 locations nationwide.
- Tegeta Motors serves approximately 500,000 retail customers and 35,000 corporate clients.
- The company represents over 300 international brands in Georgia, including Bridgestone, Michelin, Varta, Shell, Motul, and Hengst.
