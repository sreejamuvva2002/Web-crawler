# Honda

## Facts

- Honda associates Jessika Laudermilk and Tim Leopold were named 2024 Rising Stars by Automotive News.
- Laudermilk was acknowledged for her role in directing marketing operations for both the Honda and Acura brands.
- In June 2024, Laudermilk assumed a new role as assistant vice president of Honda National Sales.
- Laudermilk is the company’s youngest-ever executive to lead U.S. sales activities for the Honda automobile brand and the first woman to serve in this position.
- Tim Leopold is an associate chief engineer at Honda Development & Manufacturing of America (HDMA) in Ohio.
- Leopold serves as project leader for the Marysville Auto Plant (MAP) Battery EV Infrastructure.
- In this role, Leopold is responsible for the introduction of EV production at MAP.
- The Marysville Auto Plant was established in 1982 as the first Honda auto plant in North America and will become the first Honda facility to begin EV production.
- The Automotive News Rising Star awards annually honor automaker and supplier leaders based in the U.S. and under age 45 who have the talent and dedication necessary to become industry leaders of tomorrow.
- Automotive News has published its list of up-and-comers annually since 2014.
- Leopold and Laudermilk were two of 21 honorees from disciplines including executive management, purchasing, engineering, mobility, and design.
- Laudermilk joined Honda in 2018 within the Honda Auto Sales Division.
- After leading Honda Marketing, she became assistant vice president of the Marketing Division in 2022, where she led marketing for both the Honda and Acura brands.
- Under Laudermilk’s leadership, Honda ranked as the number one auto brand in America in the 2024 Axios Harris Poll.
- Leopold joined Honda in 2002 and has worked to innovate production capabilities in North America.
- Leopold was instrumental in the planning, development, and global introduction of the first 10-speed automatic transmission at the Honda Transmission Plant Georgia.
- The work at the Honda Transmission Plant Georgia demonstrated the ability of a U.S. facility to lead global production of a brand-new powertrain.
- This 10-speed automatic transmission is now featured in a number of Honda and Acura vehicles.
- Leopold took a senior role within the new Honda EV Hub in 2022 to prepare Honda for EV production at MAP.
