# Hitachi Automotive Systems Americas Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2019-05-20

**Source:** [Hitachi Automotive Undertakes $100M Plant Expansion in Monroe, Georgia - REBusinessOnline](https://rebusinessonline.com/hitachi-automotive-undertakes-100m-plant-expansion-in-monroe-georgia/)  ·  `page_008473`

## Facts

- Hitachi Automotive Systems Americas Inc. is a global supplier of automotive parts.
- Hitachi Automotive Systems Americas Inc. announced a massive, multi-year expansion of its manufacturing plant in Monroe, Georgia.
- The Monroe facility is located approximately 45 miles east of Atlanta.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp announced on Friday, May 17 that Hitachi will invest anywhere from $100 million to $330 million in expanding its existing facility.
- The expansion project is expected to bring an additional 100 jobs to Walton County.
- Hitachi's parent company, Hitachi Automotive Systems Ltd., is headquartered in Tokyo.
- Hitachi Automotive Systems Ltd. manufactures engine powertrain systems, electric powertrain systems, and integrated vehicle control systems.
- Hitachi opened its first facility in Georgia in 1997.
- Hitachi currently employs more than 900 people in the Peach State (Georgia).
- Hitachi is the largest employer in Walton County.
- The expansion project will be unveiled in phases over the next five years.
- Parts produced at the Monroe plant will continue to be sold to major automotive companies including Ford, General Motors, Honda, and Nissan.
- Hitachi cited larger growth in the automotive industry and the strength of the local workforce as key incentives behind the expansion project.
- Pat Wilson, commissioner of the Georgia Department of Economic Development, stated that partnering with a company like Hitachi helps bring jobs and investment opportunities to Georgia while taking an active role in the community.
- A timeline for the initial groundbreaking of the project was not released.
