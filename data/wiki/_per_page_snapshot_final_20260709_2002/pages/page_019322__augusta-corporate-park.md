# Augusta Corporate Park

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2023-06-05

**Source:** [South Augusta’s surge in development: Part one - Augusta Business Daily](https://augustabusinessdaily.com/south-augustas-surge-in-development-part-one-2/)  ·  `page_019322`

## Facts

- South Augusta is experiencing a surge of economic development totaling $3.2 billion.
- Some of the largest companies in the area will be located in the Augusta Corporate Park off Mike Padgett Highway.
- The Augusta Corporate Park is a “Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) Certified” site.
- A Starbucks plant has been in operation since July 2012 as the first company-owned facility to produce soluble products.
- Starbucks' soluble products are used in instant coffee and as the coffee base for many bottled and canned beverages.
- The Starbucks plant has roughly doubled in size from when it opened.
- Starbucks has put in $330 million in capital investment at its Augusta facility.
- Starbucks employs about 350 people at its Augusta location.
- Starbucks added additional roasters to produce prepackaged coffee sold in stores and retail outlets.
- Cal Wray serves as President of the Augusta Economic Development Authority (AEDA).
- District Six Commissioner Brandon Garrett oversees all the development happening in his district.
- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp announced in late 2021 that Aurubis would build a state-of-the-art recycling and smelting facility.
- Gov. Kemp called the Aurubis project the single-largest investment made by a German company in the State of Georgia.
- Gov. Kemp cited Georgia's ports system, highly skilled workforce, and pro-business environment as key attractions for Aurubis.
- Aurubis will be part of the growing electric mobility chain in Georgia.
- Aurubis will produce materials found in lithium-ion batteries and other electronics.
- The announced investment for Aurubis is $690 million.
- Aurubis will create about 250 jobs once its two phases are fully constructed.
- Aurubis is based in Germany and recycles copper, precious metals, and non-ferrous materials nationwide.
- Aurubis is one of the largest producers of copper in the world and one of the largest producers of gold and platinum.
- Aurubis processes metals from both aggregates (rocks) and recycled products, putting them back into a reusable form.
- Augusta is positioned to play a big part in Gov. Kemp's push for EV type investments around the state.
- Denkai America is planning to build its North American headquarters just past the Aurubis construction in the industrial park.
- Denkai America's initial investment is $150 million on a 115-acre tract.
- Denkai America is ultimately expected to grow to a $430 million investment to construct a headquarters and manufacturing plant.
- Denkai America will create 250 jobs over the next five years.
- Gov. Kemp announced the Denkai America facility in July 2022.
- Gov. Kemp stated Georgia's selection as home of Denkai America strengthens its status in emerging electric mobility and existing aerospace, defense, and technology industries.
- Denkai America produces copper foil for the automotive and aerospace industry.
- Denkai America has not started construction yet and is still in the design phase.
- Construction work on Denkai America is expected to start by the end of the year.
- Denkai America is Japanese-owned.
- Denkai America makes copper foil using a process similar to papermaking, resulting in a very thin product that goes into batteries and aerospace applications.
- Aurubis helped recruit Denkai to Augusta due to how impressed they were with the local economic development team.
- PureCycle Technologies, Inc. will also be coming to the Augusta Corporate Park.
- PureCycle converts #5 plastic waste to a renewable resource.
- PureCycle announced on May 31 that it has received approval from AEDA to proceed to closing on the project site.
- The AEDA approval preserves financial and tax incentives offered to PureCycle and allows it to move forward with development.
- PureCycle CEO Dustin Olson stated the approval resulted from months of hard work securing funding for at least one purification line and collaboration with the local community.
- PureCycle's facility will have an initial investment of $440 million on a 200-acre site.
- PureCycle will create over 80 manufacturing jobs.
- E-Z-Go/Textron is located along Marvin Griffin Road and is one of the longest employers in the area.
- E-Z-Go/Textron is the largest employer in the region, with 1,600 to 1,700 people headquartered there depending on the day.
- Ferrara and Prayon are also located along Marvin Griffin Road alongside other manufacturers and suppliers.
- Solvay Specialty Polymers is located along Tobacco Road.
- Two FedEx warehouses are also situated in the southern portion of Richmond County.
- Jobs at existing facilities and new plants in Augusta are not minimum-wage jobs, with many starting at $15 per hour and higher.
- In the Augusta MSA, there are currently between 350 and 400 open positions.
