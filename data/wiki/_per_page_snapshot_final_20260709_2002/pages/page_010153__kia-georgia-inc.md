# Kia Georgia Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** workforce_training  ·  **Published:** 2025-06-17

**Source:** [UGA and Kia combine efforts to boost Georgia’s economy - Give to UGA](https://give.uga.edu/uga-and-kia-combine-efforts-to-boost-georgias-economy/)  ·  `page_010153`

## Facts

- UGA and Kia Georgia have a partnership that continues to advance several of Georgia’s workforce development initiatives and overall economic growth.
- The two-pronged collaboration supports university programs and prepares the next generation of leaders in industry and policy at UGA Public Service and Outreach.
- Kia Georgia has contributed $75,000 since 2020 as a presenting sponsor of the Biennial Institute through UGA’s Carl Vinson Institute of Government.
- The Biennial Institute provides legislative training for members of the Georgia General Assembly with valuable time for learning, networking and exploring state issues prior to the upcoming General Assembly session.
- Kia Georgia sponsored Georgia’s Workforce Conference in 2023 and 2024.
- Georgia’s Workforce Conference is hosted annually by the UGA Institute of Government.
- Stuart Countess, President and CEO of Kia Georgia Inc., was a keynote speaker at the 2023 conference event.
- Stuart Countess helped to moderate and organize the Strategies to Retain and Engage Employees plenary session in 2024.
- Matt Bishop, interim vice president for Public Service and Outreach at UGA, stated that Kia Georgia’s continued partnership is expanding the impact of key university initiatives like Georgia’s Workforce Conference and the Biennial Institute for Georgia Legislators.
- Matt Bishop noted that Kia Georgia’s commitment to innovation and community investment is helping to strengthen the state’s workforce and drive economic growth.
- The partnership extends to UGA’s College of Engineering where Kia Georgia supports the Georgia Network for Electric Mobility (GNEM).
- Kia Georgia also supports the Annual E-mobility Summit and a campus-wide e-mobility initiative at UGA.
- Kia Georgia made a $1 million founding gift to GNEM.
- GNEM is a public-private partnership network established by UGA in early 2023.
- GNEM seeks to enhance research, education and outreach opportunities for students interested in a career in e-mobility across the state.
- GNEM envisions Georgia as a world leading ecosystem for electric mobility.
- GNEM accelerates electric and future mobility innovation through workforce and economic development, research and thought leadership, and key industry partnerships.
- GNEM aims to further advance Georgia’s nation-leading momentum in EV manufacturing across the entire ecosystem, involving rural and underserved communities and small businesses.
- Stephen Durham, interim dean for UGA’s College of Engineering, stated that Kia Georgia’s support as a founding partner has helped students become leaders in e-mobility and create new opportunities to strengthen Georgia’s economy.
- Since opening its West Point facility in 2009, Kia Georgia has created over 14,000 jobs at the plant and nearby suppliers.
- To date, Kia Georgia has produced more than 4.7 million vehicles.
- Kia Georgia produced the state’s first all-electric vehicle, the EV9.
- Stuart Countess stated that Kia Georgia is driven by a commitment to shape the future of mobility and is proud to work in a state that values innovation and forward-thinking partnerships.
- Countess noted that UGA’s support of GNEM reflects a shared vision that ultimately helps position Georgia as a global leader in e-mobility.
- As Georgia’s land-grant university, UGA is uniquely positioned to partner with Kia Georgia to provide resources and expertise for workforce development and economic growth.
