# South Metro Atlanta

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2015-03-31

**Source:** [South Metro: Start Your Engines - Georgia Trend Magazine](https://www.georgiatrend.com/2015/03/31/south-metro-start-your-engines/)  ·  `page_048898`

## Facts

- South Metro Atlanta, comprising the southern end of Fulton along with Clayton, Coweta, Fayette, Henry and Spalding counties, has seen increased economic activity and development following the recession.
- Porsche Cars North America Inc. is building its new headquarters adjacent to Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport as the first company to locate within the Aerotroplis Atlanta business district.
- The Porsche facility is constructed on a 26.4-acre site and includes a Technical Service and Training Center plus a Customer Experience Center featuring a 1.6-mile test track and handling road course.
- Walmart plans to invest $108 million and employ 400 people over the next three years in a new fulfillment center near Hartsfield-Jackson.
- Walmart's 1.2-million-square-foot facility is located at the Majestic Airport Center IV Industrial Park in Union City and will open in late 2015.
- The Atlanta Aerotropolis Alliance, convened by the Atlanta Regional Commission, is comprised of public and private sector leaders fostering development around Hartsfield-Jackson.
- Fulton County partnered with Majestic Realty to create a $120-million privately financed real estate development initiative at Charlie Brown Airfield.
- The Charlie Brown Airfield project plans to deliver about 2.8 million square feet of state-of-the-art industrial logistics, warehousing and distribution space supporting about 1,200 jobs.
- Clayton County can offer new businesses that want to expand as much as $4,000 per qualified job for five years.
- Kroger announced plans to build a 1-million-square-foot distribution center at the former Fort Gillem property in Forest Park.
- Kroger will hire 120 workers and invest more than $175 million over five years for its Forest Park distribution center.
- State and local leaders see the Kroger deal as the first step to turning the abandoned army post into a major distribution hub for the metro area and the Southeast.
- Southern Crescent Technical College’s new satellite campus opened last summer on a 25-acre site adjacent to Henry County High in McDonough, featuring laboratories for CISCO networking, computers, life science and an MRI/CT simulator.
- The Lakes at Green Valley is the first and only “eco-friendly” industrial park in Georgia, located in Spalding County.
- Otsuka Chemical Co. Ltd., a maker of titanate friction material for disc brakes, invested $15 million and hired 32 workers at The Lakes at Green Valley.
- Toppan Printing Co. Ltd. made a $100-million investment in Spalding County, yielding 80 jobs to produce plastic film for food packaging.
- Marukan invested approximately $13 million in Spalding County as a vinegar brewery, expected to add 15 to 20 jobs.
- 1888 Mills consolidated several plants into a single location in Spalding County, keeping 200 jobs and adding another 100 to its payroll while purchasing and retrofitting a 500,000-square-foot former NACOM facility on Airport Road in Griffin.
