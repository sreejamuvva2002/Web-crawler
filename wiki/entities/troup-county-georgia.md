# Troup County, Georgia

**Type:** Government Program  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[vehicle-manufacturing|Vehicle Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** Georgia, United States

## Overview

Troup County is a west-central Georgia county that has evolved from an agrarian and textile economy to a hub for advanced automotive manufacturing, battery production, logistics, and emerging data-center development.

## EV supply-chain relevance

High - host of Kia Motors' EV9 production plant, battery manufacturer Duracell, and other automotive supply-chain facilities.

- **Caterpillar Heavy Equipment Production Site** — Manufactures heavy equipment for regional and national markets.

- **UPS Distribution Center** — Supports regional freight operations.
- **Project West Data-Center Campus (proposed)** — $9.7 billion data-center campus proposal announced March 2025, expected to generate hundreds of high-wage jobs.

## Timeline

- **February 12, 1825** — Treaty of Indian Springs signed, ceding ~4.5 million acres to Georgia.
- **June 9, 1826** — Georgia General Assembly created Troup County.
- **March 12, 1827** — First land lot drawn in Mountville during the settlement lottery.
- **December 16, 1828** — LaGrange incorporated as the county seat of Troup County.
- **1846** — Troup Factory established on Flat Shoals Creek - Georgia's second cotton textile mill.
- **January 19, 1861** — Georgia adopted the ordinance of secession; later that fall the Troup Light Artillery was formed.
- **April 17, 1865** — Union forces invaded LaGrange, burning factories, stores, and the railroad depot.
- **1888** — First cottonseed-oil mill opened in LaGrange.
- **1895** — Dixie Mills founded as the county's second cotton textile manufacturer.
- **1916** — Joseph E. Dunson funded the county's first public hospital.
- **1943** — Callaway Foundation established by Fuller E. Callaway.
- **2006** — Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia selected West Point for a $1.1 billion assembly plant.
- **December 2009** — Kia plant opened, creating 2,700 direct jobs.
- **2020** — Hyundai TRANSYS announced a $240 million transmission facility in the county.
- **2022** — Hyundai TRANSYS transmission facility became operational.
- **2023** — $200 million investment for EV9 electric-vehicle production added nearly 200 jobs at the Kia plant.
- **March 2025** — Project West data-center campus proposal announced, targeting $9.7 billion in development.

## Key facts

- Troup County's economy transitioned from a textile-dominated base to advanced automotive manufacturing.
- The Kia plant created 2,700 direct jobs and spurred over 15,000 indirect positions across the region.
- Investments exceeding $800 million in related industries have been made by the early 2020s.
- Population grew from 58,779 in 2000 to over 71,500 by 2023, driven largely by manufacturing expansion.
- Median household income was $54,905 in 2023, a 2.44 % increase year-over-year.
- Unemployment rate was 3.2 % in 2023, rising to 3.6 % in 2024 amid shifting manufacturing employment.
- Duracell maintains a battery manufacturing presence within the county, supporting EV supply chains.
- Project West aims to create a $9.7 billion data-center campus that would generate hundreds of high-wage technology jobs.
