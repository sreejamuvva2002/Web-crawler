# HL-GA Battery Company

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-05

**Source:** [Massive Federal Raid at Hyundai's Metaplant Complex In Georgia, 475 Arrested](https://www.motortrend.com/news/feds-raid-hyundai-metaplant-georgia-arrest-475)  ·  `page_001137`

## Facts

- U.S. officials stated that the majority of the 475 arrestees were Korean nationals, prompting concern from the South Korean government.
- Several federal and state law enforcement agencies raided the construction site of a Hyundai joint-venture in Georgia, arresting 475 individuals for alleged immigration violations.
- The EV battery plant is known as HL-GA Battery Company.
- HL-GA Battery Company is a joint venture between Hyundai and LG Energy Solution.
- The plant is located next to Hyundai’s “Metaplant” in Ellabell, Georgia, southwest of Savannah.
- The plant was slated to open by the end of this year or early 2026.
- The plant will supply batteries for the Hyundai Ioniq 5 and Ioniq 9 EV SUVs.
- DHS spokesperson Steven Schrank stated the arrests were made for entering the U.S. unlawfully, overstaying visas, and working unlawfully.
- No charges have been filed yet as the investigation continues.
- The raid is part of President Donald Trump’s controversial crackdown on unlawful immigration.
- A spokesperson for LG told the New York Times that both LG employees and Hyundai employees were arrested.
- Hyundai told the New York Times separately that it believed none of its employees were arrested.
- Hyundai told Fox News that as of today, it understands that none of those detained are directly employed by Hyundai Motor Company.
- Hyundai stated it prioritizes the safety and well-being of everyone working at the site and complies with all laws and regulations wherever it operates.
- Schrank said some of the arrested individuals worked for subcontractors rather than directly for either parent company.
- Schrank said some number of U.S. citizens and lawful residents were detained and are now being released.
- DHS spokesperson Steven Schrank stated the operation underscores a commitment to protecting jobs for Georgians and Americans, ensuring a level playing field for compliant businesses, safeguarding economic integrity, and protecting workers from exploitation.
- DHS told CNN it executed a search warrant as part of an ongoing criminal investigation into allegations of unlawful employment practices and other serious federal crimes.
- ICE officials told the BBC the raid culminated a multi-month criminal investigation involving developed evidence, conducted interviews, gathered documents, and presented evidence to a court to obtain an official search warrant.
