# Pirelli SpA

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2010-12-14

**Source:** [Pirelli: On 5th Avenue and on the Move in North America - Tire Review Magazine](https://www.tirereview.com/pirelli-on-5th-avenue-and-on-the-move-in-north-america/)  ·  `page_048444`

## Facts

- Pirelli is strengthening its position in the North American market by committing to additional production capabilities with a new tire plant scheduled to be on line by the first half of 2012.
- The new greenfield plant is located in Silao, Mexico, about 350 miles south of the Texas border in the Mexican state of Guanajuato.
- The Silao facility has a price tag of roughly $210 million.
- The Silao plant will have an initial daily production capacity of 10,500 passenger and light truck tires.
- By 2015, Pirelli estimates the Silao plant will be able to produce 14,000 tires per day, or a maximum of 5 million tires annually.
- Additional land has been purchased at the Silao site to add capacity down the line.
- Mauro Pessi serves as President & CEO of Pirelli Tire North America.
- Pessi defined Silao as a location with good infrastructure, a qualified and capable workforce offering competitive labor rates compared to other Latin American countries, and solid transportation corridors with tariff-free status under NAFTA.
- About 80% of the tires manufactured at the new Silao facility will go to the U.S. and Canada.
- Tom Gravalos is vice president of marketing, motorsports and original equipment for Pirelli Tire North America.
- The Silao plant will employ conventional tire production technology rather than the Modular Integrated Robotic System (MIRS) featured in Pirelli’s Rome, Ga., facility.
- MIRS is designed for shorter runs of high performance tires.
- The 120,000-square-meter Mexican facility is part of Pirelli SpA’s five-year industrial plan designed to support projected growth for high performance tires in North America.
- The five-year industrial plan calls for added investments in Latin America and the Asia-Pacific region to achieve new sales targets producing an annual average of 8% growth through 2015.
- The Mexico facility will be Pirelli’s eighth tire manufacturing facility in Latin America.
- Pessi stated that along with the Rome, Ga., facility, the Silao plant sends a strong message to customers that Pirelli is committed and focused.
- Pirelli will invest $12 million in the Rome, Ga. plant to add a new compound mixing facility.
- The Rome, Ga. investment will assist in supporting market gains and a 30% sales increase made over the past three years in the U.S. and Canada.
- Pirelli is budgeting a total of $2.65 billion globally for modernization of its manufacturing facilities over the next five years.
- This budgeted modernization cost is 27% higher than what Pirelli spent between 2006 and 2010.
- Pirelli agreed to take over the assets of Sibur Russian Tyres after signing a memorandum of understanding with Russian Technologies and oil major Sibur Holding in Moscow on Nov. 26.
- The Russia deal is expected to close by June 2011.
- Pirelli launched the 38th edition of its high-profile calendar in Moscow, titled “Mythology,” featuring creative direction by Karl Lagerfeld.
