# Robert Bosch GmbH

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** power_electronics  ·  **Published:** 2022-12-31

**Source:** [Rising demand for SiC chips: Bosch plans to acquire U.S. chipmaker TSI Semiconductors - Bosch Media Service US](https://us.bosch-press.com/pressportal/us/en/press-release-20224.html)  ·  `page_018024`

## Facts

- Bosch is expanding its semiconductor business with silicon carbide chips.
- The technology company plans to acquire assets of the U.S. chipmaker TSI Semiconductors, based in Roseville, California.
- TSI Semiconductors has a workforce of 250 and operates as a foundry for application-specific integrated circuits, or ASICs.
- Currently, TSI mainly develops and produces large volumes of chips on 200-millimeter silicon wafers for applications in the mobility, telecommunications, energy, and life sciences industries.
- Over the next years, Bosch intends to invest more than 1.5 billion USD in the Roseville site.
- Bosch plans to convert the TSI Semiconductors manufacturing facilities to state-of-the-art processes.
- Starting in 2026, the first chips will be produced on 200-millimeter wafers based on the innovative material silicon carbide (SiC).
- As of Dec. 31, 2022, the Bosch Group employs nearly 37,000 associates in more than 100 locations in North America.
- According to preliminary 2022 figures, Bosch generated consolidated sales of $15 billion in the U.S., Canada, and Mexico.
- The Bosch Group employs roughly 420,000 associates worldwide as of Dec. 31, 2022.
- The company generated sales of $93.1 billion in 2022.
- Bosch's operations are divided into four business sectors: Mobility Solutions, Industrial Technology, Consumer Goods, and Energy and Building Technology.
- At 128 locations across the globe, Bosch employs roughly 84,800 associates in research and development, of which more than 44,000 are software engineers.
- In North America, Bosch employs approximately 3,500 associates in research and development.
