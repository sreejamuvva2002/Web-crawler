# Daimler Truck North America LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-08-17

**Source:** [Daimler Truck North America Executive Joins U.S. Government’s Electric Vehicle Working Group - School Transportation News](https://stnonline.com/industry-releases/daimler-truck-north-america-executive-joins-u-s-governments-electric-vehicle-working-group/)  ·  `page_053594`

## Facts

- Rakesh Aneja, head of eMobility at DTNA, has been appointed to the newly founded Electric Vehicle Working Group (EVWG) by the U.S. Department of Energy and U.S. Department of Transportation.
- The EVWG was established by the Joint Office of Energy and Transportation to provide vital recommendations to the U.S. Secretaries of Energy and Transportation for integrating light-, medium, and heavy-duty electric vehicles into the U.S. transportation and energy systems.
- The EVWG collaborates closely with federal interagency working groups, especially on fleet conversion, to ensure effective coordination and insightful consultation.
- The EVWG will regularly report on barriers and opportunities to scale up EV adoption to the U.S. Secretaries of Energy and Transportation and key congressional committees.
- Under the guidance of the U.S. Secretaries of Energy and Transportation, the EVWG comprises 25 members, encompassing 6 federal stakeholders and 19 non-governmental stakeholders.
- The non-governmental participants represent diverse backgrounds, experiences, and viewpoints across geographically diverse regions in the United States, spanning rural, urban, and suburban contexts.
- Rakesh Aneja stated he is incredibly honored and enthusiastic to accept the invitation to serve as a Committee member of the U.S. Government’s Electric Vehicle Working Group, calling it a significant recognition of DTNA's strides in eMobility and underscoring their commitment to leading sustainable transportation.
- DTNA has been a pioneer in commercial electric vehicle development.
- DTNA’s portfolio of electric vehicles includes the Jouley, an all-electric bus from Thomas Built Bus, available since 2020.
- DTNA’s portfolio includes the FCCC MT50e walk-in van chassis for final mile delivery, available since 2021.
- DTNA’s portfolio includes the Class 8 Freightliner eCascadias, available since 2022.
- DTNA’s portfolio includes the Freightliner eM2, set to debut later in 2023.
- Beyond vehicle manufacturing, DTNA is proactively fostering the transition to zero-emission commercial vehicles, including infrastructure and holistic ecosystem initiatives.
- DTNA established "Electric Island" in collaboration with Portland General Electric in 2021 as the first of its kind commercial vehicle charging site to drive the development, testing, and deployment of zero-emission commercial vehicles.
- In partnership with NextEra Energy and Blackrock Alternatives, DTNA made substantial investments through the Greenlane joint venture launched in April 2023 to address the urgent need for a publicly available, nationwide electric charging infrastructure for commercial vehicles.
- DTNA is the nation’s leading manufacturer of Class 6-8 commercial vehicles.
- DTNA collaborates with federal and state regulatory agencies to bolster U.S. competitiveness and achieve shared goals of improved fleet efficiency and reduced greenhouse gas emissions.
- DTNA remains steadfast in its long-term ambition to transition all new vehicle sales to exclusively carbon-neutral (in driving operation) by 2039.
- DTNA emphasizes the importance of investing in clean energy and domestic clean energy industries to decarbonize commercial vehicles.
- Daimler Truck North America LLC is headquartered in Portland, Oregon, and designs, engineers, manufactures and markets medium- and heavy-duty trucks, school buses, vehicle chassis and their associated technologies and components under the Freightliner, Western Star, Thomas Built Buses, Freightliner Custom Chassis Corp and Detroit brands.
- Daimler Truck North America LLC is a subsidiary of Daimler Truck Holding AG (DTG), one of the world’s leading commercial vehicle manufacturers.
