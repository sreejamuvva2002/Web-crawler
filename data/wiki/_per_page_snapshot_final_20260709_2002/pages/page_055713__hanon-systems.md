# Hanon Systems

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** thermal_management  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hanon Systems introduces new heat pump system for electric vehicles](https://inspenet.com/en/noticias/hanon-systems-and-heat-pump-system/)  ·  `page_055713`

## Facts

- Hanon Systems has unveiled the development of the first fourth-generation heat pump system designed for electric vehicles.
- The thermal management system has been commercially available since July.
- The new pump system could be the first to employ a simultaneous heat recovery method.
- The system allows both waste heat from the engine and battery, as well as external heat from the air, to be reused for multiple applications.
- Recovered heat is redirected for vehicle heating and cooling, and simultaneously for battery thermal management.
- Constant improvement in the company’s heat pump system design has enabled optimization of energy efficiency and reduction of consumption, thereby extending the range of electric vehicles.
- Hanon Systems is the world’s second largest thermal management supplier.
- The company remains committed to developing lightweight, highly efficient components through continuous innovation.
- Subu Nagasubramony, co-CEO of Hanon Systems, stated that constant improvement in design has enabled optimization of energy efficiency and reduction of consumption.
- The heat pump system features a modular design incorporating several patent-protected or patent-pending features.
- The system integrates the refrigerant control module (RCM) and refrigerant circuit control module (CCM) into a unified control unit for comprehensive thermal system management.
- Hanon Systems’ track record in heat pump production dates back to 2015, when it began mass production of systems with waste heat recovery.
- In 2018, the company introduced its second-generation system, which added battery thermal management functions and became the first integrated system to improve electric vehicle range.
- In 2021, Hanon Systems developed a third-generation system implemented on a platform dedicated to electric vehicles, featuring an 800 V electric compressor to reduce charging times and energy consumption.
- The fourth-generation heat pump system has initially been implemented in the Kia EV3 in South Korea.
- Hanon Systems plans to expand production of the fourth-generation system to the United States and Europe.
- Yoon Ho Wang serves as vice president of the AP Innovation Center at Hanon Systems Research Institute.
- The company has launched Thin HVAC (Heating, Ventilation and Air Conditioning) technology, marking a world first.
- Thin HVAC is a reduced HVAC system that decreases the overall size of the system by 30%, providing more legroom for passengers.
