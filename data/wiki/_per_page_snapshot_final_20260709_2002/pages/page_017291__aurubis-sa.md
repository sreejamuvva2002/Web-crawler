# Aurubis SA

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2022-12-23

**Source:** [As Georgia’s Battery Sector Gains Steam, German Copper Recycler Aurubis Doubles Its Planned Investment - Global Atlanta](https://www.globalatlanta.com/as-georgias-battery-sector-gains-steam-german-copper-recycler-aurubis-doubles-its-planned-investment-in-georgia/)  ·  `page_017291`

## Facts

- Georgia’s largest-ever single German investment is getting even larger.
- Aurubis SA, the Hamburg-based company set to put a recycling facility in Augusta by 2024, announced a second phase that will significantly increase its planned expenditure, provide 80 more jobs and double its output of blister copper used as a raw material across many pivotal industries.
- Aurubis initially announced in November that it would invest 300 million euros (about $340 million at the time) and hire 125 people for the first phase of the greenfield site.
- Much of the infrastructure work at the Augusta site has been completed since a groundbreaking in June.
- The company announced on Wednesday that it would add another 250 million euros (about $264.5 million) to the project as demand outstrips projections from when the project was first envisioned 18 months ago.
- Aurubis’s board approved an additional 90 million euros to adjust for higher-than-expected construction costs.
- Upon completion, Aurubis will have invested a total of 640 million euros (about $679 million) and will employ 200 people at the site in Richmond County.
- CEO Roland Harings stated that because Aurubis produces raw metals rather than finished goods, the expansion was not directly aimed at qualifying for incentives under the Inflation Reduction Act.
- Domestically produced copper and other metals are essential to shoring up supply chains and helping EV battery companies qualify for federal tax credits, which bolsters Aurubis's rationale for expanding its U.S. presence.
- CEO Roland Harings stated that the world needs recyclable metals like copper for cars, mobile phones, batteries, cables, and computers, and today's high recycling demand cannot be met by existing facilities.
- Harings noted that Aurubis's increased commitment to the U.S. will be a game-changer for the environment, manufacturers, and the transition to a renewable economy.
- In Augusta, Japanese copper foil supplier Denkai announced a $400 million investment following Aurubis's announcement.
- Recycling demand continues to grow at more than 5 percent per year in the U.S.
- Hyundai Motor announced a $5.5 billion Meta Plant for batteries and EV assembly near Savannah.
- Hyundai later agreed to a $4 billion-plus joint-venture battery facility with SK On in Bartow County.
- Norway's FREYR battery is building a $2.6 billion plant in Newnan.
- An electric vehicle uses more than twice as much copper as a gas-burning vehicle, partly due to more wiring and cathode material inside the battery.
- Harings stated that massive investments are building up positive neighborhoods, environments, and industrial value chains in the country, and Aurubis is convinced it can play an important role.
- Aurubis will use massive rotating smelters called top blown rotary converters (two planned for each phase) in a pyrometallurgical process to separate materials.
- The facility's first phase is set to start production in the second half of 2024 with an output of 90,000 tons of blister copper.
- By 2026, when the second phase comes online, capacity will double to 180,000 tons.
- The goal is to establish a modular system that allows Aurubis to add capacity when opportunities arise.
- Construction has faced challenges due to labor shortages and rising costs, though foundation work and pilings are nearly finished and drainage preparation is almost complete.
- CEO Roland Harings noted that the project is on track despite real challenges in the construction supply chain because many other companies are also building.
- Aurubis's gross profit before taxes reached 532 million euros during 2021-22, representing a 40 percent increase largely driven by rising metal prices.
- The company is financing its expansion through its own cash flow and existing credit facilities, absorbing no debt for the Georgia project.
- Aurubis faces no exchange rate worries because it has ample access to U.S. dollars through a "natural hedge" from selling metals on global markets denominated in dollars.
- Harings stated that the company likes a strong U.S. dollar versus the euro.
- Aurubis eventually plans to build a solar farm on or near its Georgia site, similar to practices at some of its European plants.
- Getting the factory up and running and delivering metals to market currently takes priority over the solar farm objective.
- A recycling infrastructure ecosystem, including business partners that sort printed circuit boards, wiring, and other materials, will set up near the Aurubis plant to extend its economic impact.
