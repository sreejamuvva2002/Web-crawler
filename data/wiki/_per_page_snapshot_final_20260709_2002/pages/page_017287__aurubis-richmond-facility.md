# Aurubis Richmond Facility

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-25

**Source:** [Aurubis now producing copper in Georgia - Recycling Today](https://www.recyclingtoday.com/news/aurubis-copper-wire-circuit-board-recycling-georgia-usa-germany/)  ·  `page_017287`

## Facts

- Germany-based metals producer Aurubis AG says the melt shop at its Aurubis Richmond facility in Augusta, Georgia, has started producing recycled-content nonferrous metals.
- Aurubis refers to the new site as the first United States multimetal recycling plant.
- The facility recovers strategic metals for U.S. energy infrastructure and the defense industry.
- The project strengthens Western supply chains for copper, nickel, tin and precious metals.
- Aurubis CEO Toralf Haag stated that with Aurubis Richmond, the company is the frontrunner in multimetal recycling and processing in the U.S., a position it already holds in Europe.
- Toralf Haag noted that the company will continue expanding its expertise in producing key strategic raw materials in the U.S., citing high availability of recycling material and good local conditions as appealing prospects for the American market.
- The company states that demand for strategic metals such as copper is rising steadily worldwide.
- U.S. industry currently needs about 1.8 million tons of copper per year.
- As of now, the U.S. imports about half of the copper it consumes.
- Multimetal recycling can close this supply gap quickly, in part due to the company's $800 million secondary smelter in Georgia.
- The plant has been designed to process up to 180,000 tons of end-of-life materials annually.
- End-of-life materials processed include printed circuit boards and copper wire and cable.
- The facility recovers several nonferrous metals from the processed materials.
- A “First Melt” ceremony was held in Augusta the week of Sept. 22.
- Participants at the ceremony included U.S. Rep. Rick W. Allen (R-Georgia) and Jens Hanefeld, Germany’s ambassador to the U.S.
- U.S. Rep. Rick W. Allen described Aurubis Richmond as a strategic site in Georgia’s 12th District that reinforces America’s ability to supply itself with metals for national security, infrastructure, and technology leadership.
- Allen added that Aurubis exemplifies how international partnerships can contribute to solidifying America’s industrial foundation and strengthening economic independence.
- Aurubis Richmond President and Managing Director David Schultheis expressed pride in the team that developed and built the state-of-the-art recycling plant.
