# Kia Georgia, Inc.

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-02-24

**Source:** [Kia Georgia celebrates trio of major milestones, including Start of Production for 2027 Kia Telluride AUTOJUNCTION - Autojunction](https://autojunction.org/kia-georgia-celebrates-trio-of-major-milestones-including-start-of-production-for-2027-kia-telluride-autojunction/)  ·  `page_001085`

## Facts

- On Feb. 24, 2026, Kia Georgia team members marked a historic day at its West Point facility with the start of production for the all-new 2027 Kia Telluride.
- The ceremonial 2027 Kia Telluride represented two additional major milestones: the first hybrid electric vehicle (HEV) assembled in the state of Georgia and the 5 millionth vehicle produced at Kia Georgia.
- A ceremony for the milestone was attended by Georgia Governor Brian P. Kemp, team members, and state/community leaders.
- Sean Yoon, President and CEO of Kia North America and Kia America, stated that as the only plant in the world that builds the Telluride, the launch reflects the strength of the West Point team, customer trust, and Kia’s commitment to building the future of mobility in Georgia.
- Sean Yoon noted that reaching five million vehicles and introducing Kia’s first US-assembled HEV is a testament to Kia’s history and direction going forward.
- Since beginning production in 2009, Kia Georgia has grown into one of the region’s largest employers and a cornerstone of automotive manufacturing in the Southeast.
- The Telluride is one of the industry’s most awarded vehicles, has become a flagship model for the brand, and is a symbol of quality built in West Point.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp stated that since its introduction in 2019, the Telluride has been exclusively Georgia-made and has become one of Kia's most successful vehicles.
- Governor Kemp attributed the Telluride's success to a proven partnership approach creating long-lasting relationships between Kia Georgia, the local community, and the state.
- Stuart Countess, Kia Georgia’s President and CEO, emphasized that the day’s milestones were made possible by the dedication of team members at Kia Georgia.
- Stuart Countess stated that five million vehicles are the result of famous teamwork and highlighted the plant's ability to produce world-class gasoline-powered vehicles, EVs, and now HEVs as a testament to versatility and talent in West Point.
- Kia Georgia, Inc. is the first manufacturing site in North America for Kia Corporation, which is based in Seoul, Korea.
- Kia Georgia is located on 2,200 acres in West Point, Ga., and has an annual capacity of 350,000 units.
- Kia Georgia began mass production on Nov. 16, 2009.
- Kia Georgia produces the Telluride, Sorento (excludes HEV and PHEV models), Sportage (excludes HEV and PHEV models) SUVs, and the all-electric EV9 and EV6.
