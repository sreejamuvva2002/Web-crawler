# Work Opportunity Tax Credit (WOTC) Program

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2025-12-31

**Source:** [Learn About Tax Credits and Incentives | Georgia Department of Labor](https://dol.georgia.gov/learn-about-tax-credits-and-incentives)  ·  `page_004321`

## Facts

- The Georgia Department of Labor (GDOL) coordinates the federal Work Opportunity Tax Credit Program (WOTC).
- The program is a federal tax credit available to employers who hire and retain eligible individuals from certain targeted groups with significant barriers to employment.
- Employers claim about $1 billion in tax credits each year under the WOTC program.
- WOTC reduces an employer's cost of doing business by decreasing their federal income tax liability between $1,200.00 and $9,600.00 per employee, depending on the target group identified and hours worked during the first year of employment.
- For employers to earn the tax credit, the new employee must work at least 120 hours during the first year of employment and have not worked previously for the employer.
- Employers can claim the WOTC on an unlimited number of qualified employees each year.
- The Work Opportunity Tax Credit (WOTC) is authorized through December 31, 2025 (Section 113 of Division EE of P.L. 116-260, Consolidated Appropriations Act, 2021).
- The legislative authority for all WOTC targeted groups will expire on December 31, 2025, unless reauthorized by Congress.
- At this time, the U.S. Department of Labor (DOL) Employment and Training Administration (ETA) has not indicated whether Congress will pass timely legislation to reauthorize WOTC.
- In the event of a lapse in WOTC authorization, Georgia WOTC will accept and fully process all timely submitted WOTC certification requests for individuals with start dates on or before December 31, 2025.
- Georgia WOTC will accept, date stamp, log, and retain certification requests submitted using the required IRS and ETA WOTC processing forms (Forms 8850 and 9061) for individuals with start dates on or after January 1, 2026, provided they are submitted within the DOL 28-day timeliness requirement.
- Georgia WOTC will not release certification request determinations for individuals with start dates on or after January 1, 2026, until after Congressional reauthorization.
- The GA WOTC Online System will remain functional and will accept certification requests submitted within the DOL 28-day timeliness requirement.
- No determinations will be released for WOTC applications with a start date on or after January 1, 2026.
- Employers can register for an account at the GA WOTC Employer Portal and will receive a confirmation email upon approval.
- Upon approval, employers can log in to enter WOTC applications, upload supporting documentation, check application status, and print determinations once processed.
- By submitting an application electronically, the employer is the designated legal forms holder and must maintain original signature documents (IRS Form 8850, ETA Form 9061), any supporting documentation, and certification letters received from the GA WOTC Coordinator.
- As the legal forms holder, employers are authorized to enter data from the original signature documents and submit this information electronically to the Georgia WOTC Program.
- Consultants submitting WOTC applications on behalf of an employer must have a completed Form 9198 (Employer Representative Declaration Form) on file before entering applications per USDOL TEGL 16-20, change 1, effective 10/1/2024.
- Form 9198 can be uploaded through the GA WOTC Consultant Portal for staff review and approval.
- Employers must complete IRS Form 8850, 'Pre-Screening Notification and Certification Request'.
- Employers must complete ETA Form 9061, 'Individual Characteristics Form.'
- Employers must submit completed and signed Forms 8850 and 9061 electronically within 28 calendar days of the new employee start date.
- Hard copies of Forms 8850 and 9061 are retained by employers for their records.
- If mailing WOTC applications, the postmarked date must be within 28 calendar days of the new employee’s start date.
- The mailing address is Georgia Dept of Labor, WOTC Unit, 148 Andrew Young International Blvd. NE, Atlanta, GA 30303.
- Access to WOTC forms and additional program information includes IRS Form 8850 and instructions, USDOL ETA Form 9061, USDOL Form 9175, USDOL ETA Form 9198, and IRS Form 5884.
- A Georgia WOTC Flyer (DOL-4468) is available.
- After receiving a certification, employers may file for the tax credit with the IRS.
- Generally, an employer elects to take the credit by filing IRS Form 3800 and IRS Form 5884.
- A tax-exempt organization that hires an employee in the WOTC veteran target group should use IRS Form 5884-C.
- Employers must meet requirements for the Minimum Employment Period, which is at least 120 hours in the first year of employment before they can file and qualify for the tax credit.
- WOTC applies only to new employees; rehires do not qualify.
- Temporary Assistance for Needy Family / Qualified IV-A recipients qualify for a $2,400 credit if they are members receiving TANF for any 9 months during the 18-month period ending on the hiring date.
- Veterans qualify if they served on active duty for more than 180 days or were discharged/released for a service-connected disability, and did not have active duty (excluding training) of more than 90 days ending in the 60-day period before hiring.
- Veteran/SNAP recipients qualify for $2,400 if they received SNAP benefits for at least a 3-month period during the 15 months ending on the hire date.
- Unemployed Veteran/4 Weeks qualifies for $2,400 if unemployed for at least 4 weeks in the 1-year period ending on the hiring date.
- Unemployed Veteran/6 Months qualifies for $5,600 if unemployed for at least 6 months in the 1-year period ending on the hiring date.
- Disabled Veteran qualifies for $4,800 if entitled to compensation for a service-connected disability and hired not more than 1 year after discharge/release.
- Disabled Unemployed Veteran qualifies for $9,600 if entitled to compensation for a service-connected disability and unemployed for at least 6 months in the 1-year period ending on the hiring date.
- Recently released Ex-felons / Qualified ex-felon qualify for $2,400 if convicted of a felony under federal or state law and hired not more than 1 year after conviction or release from prison.
- Designated community residents qualify for $2,400 if at least age 18 but not yet 40 on the hiring date and live within an empowerment zone (EZ) or rural renewal county (RRC).
- Currently, Randolph and Stewart counties are the only RRCs located in Georgia.
- Vocational Rehabilitation referrals qualify for $2,400 if they have a physical or mental disability resulting in a substantial handicap to employment and were referred by an approved rehabilitation agency, Ticket to Work employment network, or Department of Veterans Affairs.
- Summer Youth Employees qualify for $1,200 if they perform services between May 1 and September 15, are at least age 16 but not yet 18 on the hiring date (or May 1), have never worked for the employer before, and live within an empowerment zone.
- SNAP Recipients qualify for $2,400 if they are 18-39 years old on the hiring date and received SNAP benefits for the 6-month period ending on the hiring date, or were ineligible under section 6(o) but their family received SNAP for at least 3 months of the 5-month period ending on the hiring date.
- Supplemental Security Income/SSI recipients qualify for $2,400 if receiving SSI benefits for any month ending during the 60-day period ending on the hiring date.
- Long-term family assistance recipients qualify for $9,000 if they received TANF payments for at least 18 consecutive months ending on the hiring date, or received TANF for any 18 months after August 5, 1997, with the earliest period ending in the past 2 years, or stopped being eligible due to federal/state law limits and are hired not more than 2 years after eligibility ended.
- Long-term unemployment recipients qualify for $2,400 if in a period of unemployment not less than 27 consecutive weeks that includes a period receiving unemployment compensation under state or federal law.
- A tax credit may not be claimed for relatives.
- A tax credit may not be claimed for individuals who have previously worked for the employer.
- A tax credit may not be claimed for federally subsidized, on-the-job training, though wages paid after the subsidy expires can qualify.
- A tax credit may not be claimed for a new employee who works less than 120 hours in the first year of employment.
- The program offers a $1,200 - $9,600 tax credit for each new hire meeting certification and working requirements.
- Employers make the hiring decision.
- There is no limit to the number of new hires who can qualify for the tax credit.
- The program requires minimal paperwork.
- Applications are submitted electronically.
