# Archer Aviation Inc.

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-12

**Source:** [Archer Aviation Completes Georgia Air Taxi Factory - Middle Georgia CEO](https://middlegeorgiaceo.com/news/2024/12/archer-aviation-completes-georgia-air-taxi-factory/)  ·  `page_016760`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation Inc. announced that it received the certificate of occupancy for its high-volume manufacturing facility, ARC, earlier this week.
- ARC is connected to the Covington Municipal Airport in Georgia.
- Archer is currently working to complete the tooling load-in for the initial manufacturing line at this facility.
- Production is planned to begin in early 2025.
- The goal is to ramp to a rate of two aircraft per month by the end of the year.
- Archer built ARC in close partnership with Stellantis, and the two companies are continuing to progress their plans to mass-produce Archer’s Midnight aircraft.
- Stellantis is contributing capital, advanced manufacturing technology and expertise and experienced personnel with the goal of scaling this facility to 650 aircraft annually by 2030.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp commented that the announcement marks the next step in a journey reaffirming Georgia's commitment to working with innovative companies like Archer to create quality jobs and opportunities for years to come.
- Congressman Mike Collins of Georgia, who sits on the House Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure and Committee on Science, Space, and Technology, stated that the completion of the facility highlights Georgia’s leadership in innovation, advanced manufacturing and job creation.
- Adam Goldstein, Archer’s founder and CEO, noted that the completion is a testament to the industry shifting from R&D into commercialization and praised the team for getting construction done fast and on budget.
- Arnaud Deboeuf, Stellantis Chief Manufacturing and Supply Chain Officer, commented that supporting Archer’s electric aircraft growth with deep manufacturing expertise is a great opportunity for both companies as they take clean mobility to the sky.
- ARC will be where Archer plans to produce its all-electric vertical take-off and landing aircraft, Midnight.
- The Midnight aircraft is designed to offer passengers a sustainable, low-noise and safe alternative to ground transportation in congested cities around the world.
