# Kia Georgia Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-01-12

**Source:** [Kia Promotes Plant Veteran as New CEO for Georgia Operations  - Global Atlanta](https://www.globalatlanta.com/kia-promotes-plant-veteran-as-new-ceo-for-georgia-operations/)  ·  `page_010013`

## Facts

- Korean car maker Kia Motors announced a leadership transition at its Georgia manufacturing arm, Kia Georgia Inc., in mid-December.
- Jangsoo (Jason) Shin led Kia Georgia starting in 2017.
- Under Mr. Shin’s watch, the company introduced two new models: the Telluride SUV and the K5 sedan.
- The Georgia factory began producing the latest generation of the Sorento mid-size SUV under Mr. Shin’s watch.
- Starting Jan. 1, Jangsoo (Jason) Shin was replaced as CEO by Stuart Countess.
- Stuart Countess is the former chief operating officer who will also hold the role of president and already played a more visible role in the state’s manufacturing community.
- Mr. Countess joined the company in 2008, after Kia had announced the factory in Georgia but before it had broken ground.
- After production began in 2009, Mr. Countess moved steadily up the ranks, working in manufacturing and quality and taking up the chief administrative officer position before becoming COO.
- Stuart Countess said: “I am honored to have the opportunity to lead Kia Georgia’s world class team in my new capacity.”
- Stuart Countess said: “Kia is looking to the future with great confidence in our contribution to the automotive industry, and Kia Georgia intends to be an integral part of our North American strategy.”
- In response to industry trends, Kia is looking to electrify more of its lineup.
- Officials hope Kia's electrification efforts will lead to more investment at the Georgia plant.
- Where Kia’s localized EV production will be placed is an open question.
- Kia Georgia announced recently that it would add a fourth model to its Georgia-made lineup: the Kia Sportage crossover.
- Mr. Countess assumes leadership during a tough time for the auto sector, with rising wages and inflation and snarled supply chains forcing closures at many plants built on the just-in-time model of inventory management.
- Kia Georgia reportedly has weathered a few brief closures due to shortages of semiconductors.
- The investment that Kia pioneered as the state’s only vehicle assembly plant has continued to pay dividends through suppliers and more recently, other OEMs following Kia into the state.
- Georgia announced in December that Rivian, an electric truck maker, would put a $5 billion plant in Covington.
