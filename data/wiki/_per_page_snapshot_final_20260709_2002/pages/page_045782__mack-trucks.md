# Mack Trucks

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-25

**Source:** [Lehigh Valley’s Mack Trucks a presidential favorite for economic stumping | Pennsylvania | thecentersquare.com](https://www.thecentersquare.com/pennsylvania/article_c744940c-c79b-4663-885e-768b16c925c1.html)  ·  `page_045782`

## Facts

- President Donald Trump visited the Lehigh Valley on Tuesday to laud his administration’s tariff policies for reviving U.S. manufacturing.
- Former President Joe Biden previously visited the Macungie, Pa., facility and promised regulatory changes requiring government contractors to buy “substantially” American-made products would support the company’s growth.
- As of 2026, the “Buy American” rule requires 65% of materials used to be domestically produced.
- The domestic material requirement under the “Buy American” rule rises to 75% in 2029.
- This threshold expands upon a rule change from Trump’s first term that increased the requirement from 50% to 55%.
- Mack Trucks has been manufacturing in Pennsylvania for more than a century.
- The “Buy American” rule makes Mack Trucks an appealing choice for government contractors, including ongoing negotiations for a supply of 15,000 trucks.
- The potential cost of the 15,000-truck contract could not be confirmed.
- During his July 2021 visit, Biden stated: “We brought this economy back from the brink, checks in people’s pockets, shots in people's arms, tax cuts for working families with children.”
- Biden also stated during that visit: “We designed our strategy to not only to provide a temporary boost but to lay the foundation for a long-term boom that brings everyone along.”
- In 2021, Biden’s $1 trillion infrastructure spending plan was intended to “rebuild the backbone of the country” amid pandemic-induced unemployment and supply chain shortages.
- Criticism of the former administration’s recovery plans helped flip the swing region back to Trump's favor after Biden won it in 2020.
- On Tuesday, Trump stated: “You are the ones who are making America great again and you’re building the heavy-duty machinery that keeps our economy rolling, our factories moving and our industries roaring all across the nation.”
- Company President Stephen Roy stated that Mack Trucks now supports 2,800 employees.
- Mack Trucks has invested more than $400 million in facilities over the past decade.
- Mack Trucks has invested more than $1 billion in new truck lines.
- Mack Trucks recalled more than 150 workers amid strong demand.
- Republican officials and administration representatives present at the visit included State Treasurer Stacy Garrity, U.S. Reps. Dan Meuser and Ryan Mackenzie, NASA Administrator Jared Isaacman, and Acting U.S. Secretary of Labor Keith Sonderling.
- These officials echoed the message that tax cuts, trade policy, energy production, and workforce development are helping revive American industry.
- Rep. Ryan Mackenzie pointed to $47 million he helped secure for Mack Defense to build more heavy-duty trucks in the Lehigh Valley.
- The $47 million funding protects union jobs, strengthens domestic supply chains, and supports skilled manufacturing workers.
- Trump stated his administration’s trade policies are designed to protect companies like Mack from foreign competition.
- Tariffs on foreign steel, aluminum, copper, automobiles, and heavy-duty trucks are used as tools to encourage building in the U.S. and hiring American workers.
- In early 2025, months into Trump’s second term, Mack Trucks announced layoffs of up to 350 workers amid tariff uncertainty.
- Ultimately, 170 workers were laid off.
- 150 laid-off workers were brought back later in 2025.
