# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-04-22

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Group Secures Renewable Energy for Its U.S. Manufacturing Facilities](https://www.hyundai.com/worldwide/en/newsroom/detail/hyundai-motor-group-secures-renewable-energy-for-its-u.s.-manufacturing-facilities-0000000749)  ·  `page_002900`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group signed a 15-year power purchase agreement (PPA) with Matrix Renewables for its U.S. manufacturing facilities.
- Matrix Renewables is an energy development company based in Madrid, Spain.
- The Group will purchase 147 MW of generation capacity from Matrix Renewables’ Stillhouse Solar Project in Bell County, Texas.
- The Stillhouse Solar Project has a total generation capacity of 210 MW.
- Through this project, the Group expects to secure approximately 378 GWh of electricity annually.
- The initiative anticipates a yearly reduction of 140,000 tons of carbon emissions.
- The secured solar energy will support sustainable manufacturing at several Group-affiliated plants in the U.S., including the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA).
- HMGMA is a factory dedicated to electric vehicles (EVs).
- Other supported U.S. plants supply parts, steel plates, and sheets for the Group’s EVs.
- Affiliates collectively joining the agreement include HMGMA, Hyundai Mobis North America Electrified Powertrain LLC, Hyundai Steel Georgia Inc., and Hyundai Transys Georgia Powertrain Inc.
- These affiliates will strengthen a sustainable supply chain with renewable energy from parts to vehicles.
- Starting with this PPA, the Group will undertake investments and contracts for renewable energy conversion at its U.S. facilities in 2024.
- The Group plans to convert to renewable energy at its manufacturing facilities around the globe through collaboration with diverse parties.
- HMGMA is Hyundai Motor Group’s first dedicated EV mass-production plant located in Bryan County, Georgia.
- HMGMA will start producing EVs for automotive affiliates, including Hyundai Motor Company and Kia Corporation, in the fourth quarter of 2024.
