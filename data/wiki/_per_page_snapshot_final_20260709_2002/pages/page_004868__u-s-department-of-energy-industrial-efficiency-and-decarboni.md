# U.S. Department of Energy Industrial Efficiency and Decarbonization Funding Opportunity Announcement (FOA DE-FOA-0002804)

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2023-06-15

**Source:** [Funding Selections: Industrial Efficiency and Decarbonization FOA | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/cmei/ito/funding-selections-industrial-efficiency-and-decarbonization-foa-0)  ·  `page_004868`

## Facts

- On June 15, 2023, the U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) announced $135 million in funding for 40 projects to reduce the carbon footprint of the industrial sector and move the U.S. toward a net-zero emissions economy by 2050.
- The selected projects are primarily funded through DOE’s Industrial Technologies Office (ITO).
- The projects will support research, development, and pilot-scale demonstrations to reduce energy usage and emissions from high-emitting industrial subsectors.
- Award and cost share amounts are subject to change pending negotiations.
- Topic 1: Decarbonizing Chemicals includes 9 projects with an award amount of $38.3 million, focusing on unit operations like advanced separations, advanced reactors, alternative production, and process heating technologies.
- Topic 2: Decarbonizing Iron and Steel includes 10 projects with an award amount of $31.9 million, collaboratively funded with the Hydrogen and Fuel Cell Technologies Office, focusing on decarbonization in ore-based or scrap-based iron and steelmaking operations and converting ancillary/thermal processes to clean fuels or electricity.
- Topic 3: Decarbonizing Food and Beverage Products includes 3 projects with an award amount of $11.4 million, focusing on innovative technologies for process heating operations within the food and beverage sector.
- Topic 4: Decarbonizing Cement and Concrete includes 5 projects with an award amount of $16.4 million, focusing on next-generation cement formulations, process routes, and carbon capture/utilization technologies to address process-related and energy-related CO2 emissions.
- Topic 5: Decarbonizing Paper and Forest Products includes 6 projects with an award amount of $16.2 million, focusing on increasing renewable energy share, electrification, carbon capture, energy efficiency, novel paper/wood drying technologies, and innovative pulping/paper forming technologies.
- Topic 6: Cross-sector Decarbonization Technologies includes 7 projects with an award amount of $20.4 million, focusing on innovations like industrial heat pump technologies and power generation from low temperature waste heat.
