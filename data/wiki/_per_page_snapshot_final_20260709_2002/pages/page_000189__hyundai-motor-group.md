# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-01-05

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Group Announces AI Robotics Strategy to Lead Human-Centered Robotics Era at CES 2026](https://www.hyundai.com/worldwide/en/newsroom/detail/hyundai-motor-group-announces-ai-robotics-strategy-to-lead-human-centered-robotics-era-at-ces-2026-0000001100)  ·  `page_000189`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group unveiled its AI Robotics Strategy at CES 2026 under the theme ‘Partnering Human Progress’.
- The strategy is built on three foundational partnerships: partnering humans with co-working robots, partnering the Group Value Network with Boston Dynamics, and partnering with AI leaders.
- Physical AI refers to tangible technologies that collect data using hardware in real-world environments to make autonomous decisions, spanning robotics, smart factories, and autonomous driving.
- Hyundai Motor Group plans to establish a Physical AI Application Center to advance the Physical AI ecosystem.
- The Group plans to build a robot manufacturing and foundry plant based on customized robotics technologies developed through Physical AI.
- Boston Dynamics robots have been deployed in industrial settings to enhance safety and improve efficiency.
- Spot, an agile quadruped robot, is operational in more than 40 countries for data collection and safety monitoring in industrial sites.
- Stretch, a warehouse robot, has unloaded more than 20 million boxes globally since its launch in 2023.
- The Group aims to combine its manufacturing data and expertise with Boston Dynamics’ R&D leadership to establish safe human-robot collaboration starting in manufacturing environments.
- The Atlas prototype was unveiled at CES 2026 as a general-purpose humanoid research model designed for industrial applications and built to integrate with existing facilities.
- Atlas is equipped with advanced rotational joints and sensors, features 56 degrees of freedom (DoF) with most being fully rotational joints, and has human-scale hands with tactile sensing.
- Atlas supports applications including material sequencing, assembly, and machine tending while prioritizing safety, reliability, and collaboration in shared workspaces.
- Hyundai Motor Group expects humanoids to become the largest segment of the Physical AI market and aims to mass-produce the Atlas product model for deployment across industrial sites.
- The Group plans to integrate Atlas across its global network, including Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) in Savannah, Georgia, to perform real-world tasks on-site.
- Atlas deployment will expand process-by-process through validation, beginning in 2028 with processes like parts sequencing that have proven safety and quality benefits.
- By 2030, Atlas applications will extend to component assembly, and over time it will handle repetitive motions, heavy loads, and other complex operations.
- The Group envisions a human-centered automation model where robots handle labor-intensive or high-risk tasks while humans focus on training robots and providing oversight.
- Hyundai Motor Group is building a Group Value Network as its central AI Robotics Strategy leveraging Group capabilities.
- At the core of this network is an End-to-End (E2E) AI Robotics Value Chain leveraging automotive production infrastructure, safety know-how, and affiliate technological capabilities.
- Atlas robots will undergo extensive training in the Group’s manufacturing environment to ensure safe readiness for real-world applications.
- The Group has a unique advantage in extending its electrification value chain and applying AI-based software from Software-Defined Vehicles (SDVs) to robots and other Physical AI products.
- By 2028, the Group aims to establish a scalable production system capable of manufacturing 30,000 robot units annually.
- Boston Dynamics announced a strategic partnership with Google DeepMind to accelerate technological development for next-generation humanoid robots.
- This collaboration will integrate Boston Dynamics’ robotics expertise with Google DeepMind’s Gemini Robotics AI foundation models built on the Gemini multimodal generative AI model.
- The partnership aims to accelerate research on AI models to control complex robots for high-impact tasks and scale robot adoptions safely and efficiently.
- Zachary Jackowski, Vice President and General Manager of Atlas at Boston Dynamics, stated that combining capabilities with Google DeepMind is a significant step toward redefining the industry paradigm.
- Carolina Parada, Senior Director of Robotics at Google DeepMind, expressed excitement to explore possibilities with the new Atlas robot while developing new models to expand robotics impact safely and efficiently.
- From January 6–9, 2026, Hyundai Motor Group’s CES 2026 exhibition booth at the Las Vegas Convention Center featured live demonstrations of Spot, Stretch, Atlas, X-ble Shoulder, ACR, MobED, IONIQ 5 Robotaxi, AMR, Cobot, and Parking Robot.
- MobED won the CES 2026 Best of Innovation Award.
- Hyundai Motor Group unveiled an official brand video for CES 2026 showcasing human-centered AI Robotics technologies on its official YouTube channel.
- Additional CES 2026 updates will be shared through the Group’s official social media channels.
- Hyundai Motor Group is a global enterprise with about 250,000 employees worldwide and mobility brands including Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis.
