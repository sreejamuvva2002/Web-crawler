# Porsche Taycan Turbo GT Lap Record Event at Road Atlanta

**Type:** event  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-06-17

**Source:** [Taycan Turbo GT sets new production EV lap record at Road Atlanta - EIN Presswire](https://www.einnews.com/pr_news/822961534/taycan-turbo-gt-sets-new-production-ev-lap-record-at-road-atlanta)  ·  `page_048812`

## Facts

- The Taycan Turbo GT set a new benchmark lap time for a production EV at Road Atlanta.
- Professional race car driver and Porsche Cars North America brand ambassador Patrick Long completed the record lap in 1:27.15 minutes.
- The run utilized available Pirelli P Zero Trofeo RS NF0 summer performance tires developed and homologated specifically for the Taycan Turbo GT.
- The vehicle was equipped with the optional Weissach Package.
- The Taycan Turbo GT beat the previous record set by the Taycan Turbo S in 2020 by 6.73 seconds.
- A Taycan Turbo S previously completed the lap in 1:33.88 minutes on the 2.54-mile track at the end of 2020.
- The record was set on May 28, 2025 under continuously changing weather conditions.
- At the time of the run, temperatures were in the mid 70s with moderate cloud cover and winds of 3-5 mph from the North.
- Persistent morning rain left track conditions patchy damp in some spots but mostly dry during the record run due to a break in clouds and a consistent breeze.
- Patrick Long stated after his first lap: “The new Taycan was so easy to get up to speed, and the brakes were amazing, there was absolutely no compromise in feel or power.”
- After the record run, Long noted he expected steering feel, connection, and feedback but did not expect how powerful the Active Ride suspension would be in counteracting pitch, roll, and squat.
- Long added that Road Atlanta puts a lot of load into a car, and the hydraulics kept up without issues to ensure tires were always at their best.
- To ensure safety, the team experimented with traction and stability control settings, including Sport PSM on and off, finding the car worked fine in Sport mode with seamless operation from the driver's seat.
- Long described Attack Mode as intense, stating he had never felt such a smooth but intense burst of usable power in anything, including race cars.
- The record-setting vehicle was a stock production 2025 Porsche Taycan Turbo GT equipped with the Weissach Package and Pirelli Trofeo RS tires.
- The car featured an exterior color of Purple Sky and the GT Interior Package with accents in GT Silver.
- The vehicle was also optioned with the Illuminated Light Strip.
- Starting tire pressures were set to 37psi for both front and rear tires.
- The starting battery State of Charge was 97%.
- Track Endurance Mode was enabled prior to the run to reduce the starting temperature of the battery to 68 degrees F.
- Two attempts were made to break the record, with the second run being the faster one.
