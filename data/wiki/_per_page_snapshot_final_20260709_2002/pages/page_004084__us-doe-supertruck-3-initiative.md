# US DOE SuperTruck 3 Initiative

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-06-10

**Source:** [FOTW #1346, June 10, 2024: US DOE SuperTruck 3 Initiative Seeks to Reduce Medium/Heavy Truck Greenhouse Gas Emissions by 75% | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/cmei/vehicles/articles/fotw-1346-june-10-2024-us-doe-supertruck-3-initiative-seeks-reduce)  ·  `page_004084`

## Facts

- The US DOE SuperTruck 3 initiative seeks to reduce medium/heavy truck greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions by 75% on a lifecycle basis.
- The initiative aims to reduce the total cost of truck ownership.
- SuperTruck 3 runs from 2023 to 2027 and targets Class 4-6 and Class 7/8 trucks.
- Participants in SuperTruck 3 are concentrating on electric and fuel cell applications for medium- and heavy-duty (MD/HD) trucks.
- The first SuperTruck initiative was launched in 2009 with four industry participants successfully demonstrating new efficient technologies for Class 8 trucks, many of which were commercialized.
- SuperTruck 2 ran from 2016 to 2023 and had five industry participants succeed with increasing Class 8 truck freight efficiency and engine brake thermal efficiency.
- The initiative is ongoing as of June 10, 2024.
- Information provided by Siddiq Khan of the U.S. Department of Energy Vehicle Technologies Office in 2024.
