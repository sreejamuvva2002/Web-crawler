# SK Battery America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- SKBA is a security facility that strictly controls outsiders' access and serves as an intensive force of advanced battery technology.
- The factory requires workers to wear dustproof suits, masks, and gloves, tolerating even minor dust.
- Production involves applying cathode and anode materials evenly on metal foil, drying them, and cutting them to specific thickness and density according to customer requests.
- SK-on's core Z-stacking method completes pouch-type battery cells by cross-laying electrode plates like folding paper.
- Robot arms and artificial intelligence (AI) inspection systems are used to ensure accurate interlocking of electrode plates and separators for stable performance.
- Each cell is assembled with surgical precision.
- Real-time production yields and targets are updated in minutes and seconds on large monitors installed for each line.
- Monitor screens show yield data at levels where almost no defective products are found, such as 98 percent and 99 percent.
- Achieving results required training over 3,000 unskilled local workers in Georgia, which was difficult for middle-class U.S. workers mainly from the service industry to transition to manufacturing.
- SKBA introduced a two-week "boot camp" training system covering basic safety education and basic manufacturing training before entering the production line.
- Many applicants left during the first month of boot camp due to high initial dropout rates, requiring additional hiring.
- Choi Yoon-sang, head of SKBA, noted that Americans were not used to working weekends if necessary without being late.
- A strikeout system allowing workers to be fired after three instances of lateness was introduced to inject Korean-style production DNA.
- Production equipment and process technology originally designed for Korean body types were modified to suit Americans, including raising machine screen eye levels and enlarging buttons for larger hands.
- Non-technical improvements minimizing human errors from operator manipulation are considered equally important to machine and technology improvements for maximizing yield.
- Climate challenges include annual typhoons causing factory difficulties in controlling humidity.
- Battery quality can be affected if humidity increases by only 1-2 percent.
- Flexibility to respond appropriately through comprehensive judgment is essential when external variables like humidity cannot be eliminated.
- Efforts to improve quality led to favorable reviews from local automakers and positive regional perceptions, creating a virtuous cycle.
- SKBA is working on more than 100 production improvement projects to further boost yields in the second half of this year.
- A "language service team" will be organized to solve language problems and facilitate communication between engineers and workers to harmonize culturally.
- The good performance of SK-on's North American production base is expected to provide an opportunity for SK Group to rebound in its overall performance.
- The facility has begun deriving tangible indicators and numbers in the battery business sector, positioning it as a future growth driver rather than a concern for the group.
