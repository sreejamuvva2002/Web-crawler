# U.S. Department of Energy

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [U.S Department of Energy Releases Updated Report on Pathways to Commercial Liftoff for Clean Hydrogen | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/edf/articles/us-department-energy-releases-updated-report-pathways-commercial-liftoff-clean)  ·  `page_005417`

## Facts

- The U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) released an updated Pathways to Commercial Liftoff report on clean hydrogen.
- The original Clean Hydrogen report was first released in March 2023 at the start of the Liftoff effort.
- The U.S. clean hydrogen market is poised for rapid growth, accelerated by historic commitments like the Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act’s Regional Clean Hydrogen Hubs.
- Growth is also accelerated by the Inflation Reduction Act’s Clean Hydrogen Production Tax Credit (45V).
- DOE has ongoing programs for research, development, demonstration, and deployment of clean hydrogen technologies.
- The Loan Programs Office made a $1.04 billion conditional commitment to a natural gas pyrolysis project with Monolith.
- The Loan Programs Office provided a $504.4 million loan guarantee to an electrolysis and energy storage facility with Mitsubishi Power Americas, Magnum Development, and Haddington Ventures.
- The Loan Programs Office announced a $1.66 billion loan guarantee to finance up to six electrolysis projects nationwide with Plug Power.
- The U.S. is on track to reach 7-9 MMTpa in operational production capacity by 2030.
- Developers face higher production cost estimates due to higher electricity prices, installation costs, and cost of capital.
- Tax credits like 45V or 45Q can help offset some or all of these increases.
- Industrial sectors continue to pull near-term demand for clean hydrogen, although an increasing number of projects will service export markets.
- Starting in 2030, new end-use applications for clean hydrogen will emerge.
- Promising clean hydrogen production technologies continue to develop, including methane pyrolysis and drilling for geologic hydrogen.
- This update marks the fifteenth installment in the Department of Energy’s Pathways to Commercial Liftoff series.
- The Liftoff series combines best-available data from across DOE and other federal agencies with on-the-ground insight from the private sector to develop a roadmap for transformative clean energy technologies.
- These reports serve as “living documents.”
- This update serves as an analytical refresh of the original Clean Hydrogen Liftoff report published in March 2023.
- DOE develops its Pathways to Commercial Liftoff reports with extensive stakeholder engagement and system modeling.
- DOE continues to solicit input through industry forums, requests for information, and regular interaction.
- Direct public input can be submitted via official DOE channels.
- The analysis in this report was completed following the publication of the Notice of Proposed Rulemaking (NPRM) for 45V.
- The analysis was completed prior to the publication of final rules, which were issued by the United States Treasury Department and Internal Revenue Service on January 3, 2025.
