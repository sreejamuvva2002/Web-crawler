# Aurubis AG

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2021-11-10

**Source:** [Aurubis to Open State-of-the-Art Recycling and Copper Smelting Facility in Augusta • The Georgia Virtue](https://www.thegeorgiavirtue.com/local-news-south-georgia/aurubis-to-open-state-of-the-art-recycling-and-copper-smelting-facility-in-augusta/)  ·  `page_017313`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Aurubis will invest $340 million (€300 million) in a state-of-the-art recycling and secondary smelting facility for multi-metal recycling in Augusta.
- The new facility will be the very first of its kind in the U.S.
- The facility will create 125 jobs in the Augusta area.
- Governor Kemp stated that the investment marks the single-largest investment made by a German company in the State of Georgia.
- Aurubis will join more than 500 German companies with operations in Georgia.
- Aurubis’ commitment to sustainable development and raw material security will play a role in building Georgia’s electric mobility value chain.
- From the Augusta facility, the company plans to produce materials found in lithium-ion batteries and other electronics.
- Roland Harings, CEO of Aurubis AG, stated that the company will become a forerunner for multi-metal recycling in the U.S., a role it already holds in Europe.
- Aurubis will build its facility on 150 acres at Augusta Corporate Park, which is a “Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) Certified” site.
- Operations are expected to begin by the first half of 2024.
- The company plans to begin hiring for positions as soon as summer 2022.
- Steven Kendrick, Chairman of the Development Authority of Augusta, stated that Aurubis will build a world-class advanced manufacturing facility and bring skilled jobs to Augusta.
- The Augusta Economic Development Authority stands ready to provide a foundation for helping Aurubis succeed over the next several decades.
- GPA Executive Director Griff Lynch stated that the Georgia Ports Authority is playing a role in bringing jobs and investment to Georgia.
- Exports produced by the Aurubis recycling operation will strengthen the state’s global position via Savannah, which is already one of the nation’s most balanced ports in terms of import-export trade.
- Senior Project Manager Fernanda Kirchner represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce division on this project in partnership with the Augusta Economic Development Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, and Georgia Power.
- Managing Director for the State of Georgia’s Europe Office Sérgio Domingues played a supporting role in attracting Aurubis to Georgia.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Aurubis will build the first multi-metal recycling plant in the United States in Richmond County.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson thanked partners in Augusta and state leadership for supporting one of the largest German investments in Georgia.
- Augusta Corporate Park has completed due diligence and other specific certification requirements prior to visits by prospective businesses as a GRAD Certified park.
- Receiving a GRAD certification with GDEcD is a proactive way for community planners and landowners to help catalyze economic growth and industrial development.
- Thousands of Georgia jobs have been created on GRAD sites since the program’s inception in 2008.
