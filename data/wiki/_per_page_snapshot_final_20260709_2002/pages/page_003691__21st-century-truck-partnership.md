# 21st Century Truck Partnership

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** research_and_development  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [21st Century Truck Partnership | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/cmei/vehicles/21st-century-truck-partnership)  ·  `page_003691`

## Facts

- The 21st Century Truck Partnership addresses national challenges related to medium-duty and heavy-duty truck efficiency, safety, and emissions by fostering research collaboration and technical innovation among government and industry partners.
- Industry partners include Allison Transmission Inc., Cummins, Inc., Daimler Trucks North America, Eaton, Ford Motor Company, General Motors Research, GILLIG LLC, International Motors, LLC, Oshkosh Corporation, PACCAR Inc, Robert Bosch NA, Stellantis, and Volvo Trucks.
- Federal government partners include the U.S. Department of Defense, U.S. Department of Energy, U.S. Department of Transportation, and U.S. Environmental Protection Agency.
- Participating national laboratories are Argonne National Laboratory, Idaho National Laboratory, National Energy Technology Laboratory, National Laboratory of the Rockies, Oak Ridge National Laboratory, Pacific Northwest National Laboratory, and Sandia National Laboratories.
- Medium-duty and heavy-duty trucks and buses serve as the backbone of America's economy for moving freight and passengers.
- These vehicles are essential for construction, building and servicing water and electricity infrastructure, collecting refuse, and maintaining roadways and highways.
- The partnership's overall vision is for U.S. trucks and buses to safely and cost-effectively move larger volumes of freight and greater numbers of passengers while emitting little or no pollution.
- The partnership accelerates technology development through collaborative, pre-regulatory, and pre-competitive R&D in component and system-level projects.
- The partnership focuses R&D efforts on topics of broad interest by providing a discussion forum and organizing consensus building tools such as roadmaps and position papers to help members agree on R&D topics and goals.
- The partnership conducts information exchange and dissemination through regular conference calls, meetings, and information dissemination tools to help partners access current industry and government activities.
- Technical sector focus areas include Internal Combustion Engine Powertrains, covering engines, fuel, emissions equipment, transmissions, drive axles, and hybrid drive systems.
- Technical sector focus areas include Electrification Technologies, covering traction motors, power electronics, energy storage, and charging and infrastructure.
- Technical sector focus areas include Freight Operational Efficiency, covering vehicle duty cycle, automation and connectivity, and driver impacts.
- Technical sector focus areas include Safety, covering advance driver assistance systems or ADAS, automated driving systems, and safety communications.
- The partnership has developed four all-new roadmap documents to provide the technical foundation for research priorities within these technical areas.
