# TI Automotive

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2004-07-07

**Source:** [TI Automotive Breaks Ground for New Plant in Georgia - AftermarketNews](https://www.aftermarketnews.com/ti-automotive-breaks-ground-for-new-plant-in-georgia/)  ·  `page_011699`

## Facts

- TI Automotive is investing $30 million to build a new manufacturing facility in Hart County, Georgia.
- The new facility will be 145,000 square feet.
- The facility will produce automotive fuel-tank systems, including blow-molded plastic fuel tanks and filler pipes.
- The components are for vehicles built in nearby southern states by BMW and an Asia-based automaker.
- The plant will initially employ 100 people.
- The facility is expected to produce 350,000 automotive fuel-tank systems annually.
- Hiring is scheduled to begin in March 2005.
- A majority of the new employees will be hired from the Hart County region.
- The State of Georgia is offering TI Automotive incentives including state job tax credits totaling $3,000 per employee per year during the facility’s first five years of operation.
- TI Automotive can spread out the state income tax credits over a 10-year period.
- New employees will be trained for many of the facility’s high-tech operations through the state’s Quick Start program at North Georgia Technical College.
- The company expects to reach full production of fuel-system components for the two automakers in 2006.
