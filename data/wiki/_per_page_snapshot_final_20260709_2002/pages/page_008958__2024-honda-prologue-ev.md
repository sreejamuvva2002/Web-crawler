# 2024 Honda Prologue EV

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-02-22

**Source:** [2024 Honda Prologue EV combines Honda look and feel with GM Ultium batteries and motors - AOL](https://www.aol.com/2024-honda-prologue-ev-combines-140020969.html)  ·  `page_008958`

## Facts

- Honda's first modern electric vehicle is the 2024 Prologue midsize SUV, arriving in dealerships in spring 2024.
- The Prologue shares many systems with the Chevrolet Blazer EV, including batteries, motors, and platform.
- General Motors engineered the shared batteries, motors, and platform systems for the Prologue and Blazer.
- GM has struggled to build vehicles using these systems; the Blazer EV was withdrawn from sale in early January due to owner complaints about charging issues alongside navigation, entertainment, and other features.
- Blazer EV sales remain on hold as of February 16.
- Honda engineers monitored quality for the Prologue during development.
- The Acura ZDX EV will be built by GM alongside the Cadillac Lyriq in another plant.
- Chief engineer John Hwang confirmed Honda's standard quality gate system is in place for Prologue development and production.
- Honda engineers and designers were involved from the start of the three-year program that led to the Prologue and Blazer.
- The Prologue exterior shares no body panels with the Blazer, with visual differentiation created by varying proportions within an inch in most dimensions.
- Honda calls the Prologue design "neo-rugged," evoking a sporty wagon rather than traditional Honda SUV styling.
- The Prologue interior features unique controls, displays, colors, materials, and brand-correct narrow horizontal air vents instead of Chevrolet's circular vents.
- Competitors include the Chevrolet Blazer EV, Ford Mustang Mach-E, Hyundai Ioniq 5, Kia EV6, Nissan Ariya, Polestar 3, Tesla Y, Toyota BZ4X, Volvo XC40 Recharge, and VW ID4.
- The Prologue is 192 inches long (2.9 inches longer than the Honda Passport), has a roof 64.7 inches off the ground (7.5 inches lower than the Passport), and is 0.5 inches narrower.
- The Elite model features 21-inch tires and an unrevealed curb weight.
- Multilink front and rear suspensions absorb bumps and maintain stability in quick maneuvers.
- Controls include an 11.3-inch touch screen, dials for temperature and volume, physical switches for climate controls, and buttons for parking brake, lane keeping, and sport mode on the lower instrument panel.
- Honda engineers tuned the suspension and steering for quick, direct steering and a smooth ride.
- The Elite model includes a hotel-style key card and a sport mode that tweaks steering, pedal response, and ambient lighting.
- The Prologue will offer either front-wheel drive (212 hp, 236 lb-ft torque from a single front motor) or all-wheel drive (288 hp, 333 lb-ft torque from dual motors).
- Honda hopes both FWD and AWD models will be available when sales begin, though only AWD models were available for testing at the time of writing.
- The EPA rates the standard 85 kWh battery at 296 miles range for FWD models and 281 or 273 miles for AWD models depending on trim.
- Honda did not provide charging speed details but noted Level 2 240-volt home chargers will be included for overnight charging.
- The battery supports up to 150 kW DC fast charging, with Honda citing the ability to add 65 miles of range in 10 minutes under ideal circumstances.
- Honda will offer adapters to use Tesla chargers sometime this year.
- Honda is participating in a seven-automaker program to add thousands of new DC fast chargers along major highway routes.
- The Prologue will initially be sold in 18 states following California emission standards: California, Colorado, Connecticut, Delaware, Maryland, Maine, Massachusetts, Minnesota, Nevada, New Mexico, New York, New Jersey, Oregon, Pennsylvania, Rhode Island, Vermont, Virginia, and Washington.
- Prices start at $47,400 for the FWD EX trim and top out at $57,900 for the AWD Elite trim, excluding a $1,395 destination charge.
- The Prologue offers features including a panoramic opening sunroof, heated front seats, Bose audio, wireless device charging, head-up display, wireless Apple CarPlay and Android Auto, power tailgate, four USB-C ports, Google built-in assistant, and key fob/card access.
- Honda expects the Prologue to qualify for the $7,500 federal tax credit along with state EV incentives.
- Trim levels and prices include: EX FWD ($47,400), EX AWD ($50,400), Touring FWD ($51,700), Touring AWD ($54,700), and Elite AWD ($57,900).
- The Prologue includes standard Apple CarPlay and Android Auto with voice recognition and a Google-powered app store.
- GM offers only Google built-in in its EVs that share the same hardware as the Prologue.
- Google Maps calculates optimal charging stops in real-time, and the system preconditions the battery for quickest and most efficient charging stops.
- The Prologue is assembled in Ramos Arizpe, Mexico.
