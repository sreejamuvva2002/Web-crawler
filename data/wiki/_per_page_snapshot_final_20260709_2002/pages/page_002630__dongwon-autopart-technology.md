# Dongwon Autopart Technology

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-02-04

**Source:** [200 New Jobs Headed to Emanuel County | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-releases/2026/200-new-jobs-headed-emanuel-county)  ·  `page_002630`

## Facts

- Dongwon Autopart Technology plans to invest $30 million in a new production facility in Emanuel County.
- The automotive project is anticipated to create 200 new jobs over the next several years.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced the investment on February 04, 2026.
- Dongwon Autopart Technology was established in 1971.
- The company supplies components such as doorframes, seat frames, and battery frames to automotive original equipment manufacturers (OEMs).
- Dongwon Autopart Technology supports more than 500 direct jobs in the Southeast.
- The company maintains operational facilities in Hogansville, Georgia.
- Dongwon Autopart Technology has been in the United States since 2003.
- Construction on the new manufacturing facility will be located at the Hwy 297 Industrial Park near Swainsboro.
- The company will hire for positions including management, system leads, and production personnel.
- Hiring is expected to begin in 2026.
- Representative Butch Parrish, Chairman of the House Rules Committee, stated that Emanuel County’s work alongside state partners is delivering jobs to Georgians in the region.
- Will Donaldson serves as Chairman of the Emanuel County Development Authority.
- Ken Warnock is the CEO of the Emanuel County Development Authority and noted this will be the single largest new economic development project for Emanuel County in over 30 years.
- Alyce Thornhill, Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy, represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on the project.
- The GDEcD partnered with the Emanuel County Development Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start for this competitive project.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that the automotive industry is expanding outward along the I-16 corridor as suppliers choose strategic locations with access to OEMs and Georgia’s ports.
- Dongwon Autopart Technology’s second location in Georgia demonstrates the robust growth of Georgia’s automotive industry, which has been creating jobs across the state for more than 100 years.
