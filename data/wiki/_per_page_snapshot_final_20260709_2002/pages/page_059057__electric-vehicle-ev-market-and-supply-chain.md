# Electric Vehicle (EV) Market and Supply Chain

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-06-17

**Source:** [Opinion: Booming EV market is complicating Republicans message](https://www.pressherald.com/2023/06/17/opinion-booming-ev-market-is-complicating-the-gops-message/)  ·  `page_059057`

## Facts

- Electric vehicles are one of the biggest economic vulnerabilities that Republicans have in the battleground states such as Georgia, Michigan and Arizona.
- EV sales in the U.S. reached 325,000 in 2020, accounting for 2.4% of all passenger vehicle sales.
- EV sales are projected to rise in 2024 to almost 2.5 million vehicles, or 17.0% of all sales, according to BloombergNEF’s Electric Vehicle Outlook report.
- The projected 2024 EV sales volume would exceed the 1.8 million vehicles Toyota Motor Corp. sold in the U.S. in 2022.
- Between August 2022 and May 2023, $70 billion of investments were announced in the EV supply chain.
- Battleground states Georgia, Michigan, Arizona, Nevada and North Carolina each received billions of dollars in projects during that period.
- In Georgia, Rivian Automotive Inc. is building an EV production facility east of Atlanta.
- In Georgia, Hyundai Motor Co. is building an EV production facility outside of Savannah.
- Each of the Georgia EV production facilities will be capable of producing hundreds of thousands of vehicles annually.
- President Biden is expected to conduct hard-hat tours and ribbon-cuttings at these facilities over the next 16 months, touting them as outcomes made possible by his signed legislation.
- Donald Trump stated in Georgia that he would scrap support for EVs on his “first day in office” should he win the election.
- Florida Governor Ron DeSantis awarded money for electric transit buses and discussed efforts to bolster growing electric vehicle usage, but Florida has not attracted high-profile EV supply chain investments comparable to other states.
- Automakers are committing tens of billions of dollars to shift production to EVs and will not change their minds based on the outcome of a presidential election.
- China surpassed Japan to become the world’s largest exporter of automobiles in the first quarter.
- China’s BYD Co. is competing with Tesla Inc. to become the most important EV company.
- China already dominates the EV battery supply chain.
