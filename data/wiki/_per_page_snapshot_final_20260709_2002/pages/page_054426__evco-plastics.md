# EVCO Plastics

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [EVCO Plastics at 50: Still Thinking Big | Plastics Technology](https://www.ptonline.com/articles/evco-plastics-at-50-still-thinking-big)  ·  `page_054426`

## Facts

- EVCO Plastics was founded in 1964 by Donald Evans with a half-ounce injection press in a basement.
- EVCO Plastics is now a $130 million company.
- The company operates 148 injection machines and employs 900 people across nine plants in the U.S., Mexico, and China.
- Donald Evans, age 87, serves as chairman of EVCO Plastics.
- Dale Evans, Don Evans' son, is the company president.
- Don Evans has two other sons who work at the company.
- Anna Bartz, Dale Evans' daughter, serves as marketing director.
- Spencer Wright is the Oshkosh plant manager; his father previously held that position.
- EVCO Plastics has pursued an aggressive expansion program, averaging a major addition about every three years, including during the 2001 and 2009 recessions.
- The original 1,200 ft² headquarters plant in DeForest, Wis., has grown to three facilities totaling 156,000 ft².
- EVCO Plastics operates a 100,000 ft² facility in Oshkosh, Wis.
- EVCO Plastics operates a 70,000 ft² facility in Calhoun, Ga.
- EVCO Plastics operates 188,000 ft² across three plants in Monterrey and Juarez, Mexico.
- EVCO Plastics operates a 73,000 ft² facility in Shenzhen, China.
- EVCO started globalizing with a Chinese moldmaking joint venture in 1989 and a molding plant in 2000.
- The Chinese moldmaking and molding operations were combined into a wholly owned molding and moldmaking operation in 2011.
- EVCO Plastics de Mexico opened three plants in 2001, 2005, and 2007.
- Dale Evans states the company quotes the same jobs for production in the U.S., China, and Mexico to give customers a choice.
- The company's philosophy is to locate relatively small plants with 15 to 20 machines close to customers.
- Some customers have parts molded in both the U.S., Mexico, and China.
- China tends to have the lowest price but longest lead times.
- As China raises taxes and labor rates, Mexico becomes increasingly competitive for some jobs that start out in China.
- EVCO targets three primary markets: packaging, medical parts, and large parts.
- Packaging is a target market due to customers' need for quick time to market and the cost of shipping air in hollow parts.
- Medical parts are targeted because the market is heavily regulated by the FDA.
- EVCO MED (Medical & Electronic Devices) started in 1996.
- EVCO MED has 15 injection presses, all under 200 tons, located in a 34,000 ft² Class 100,000 clean room.
- EVCO plans to more than double its medical facility size with a 50,000 ft² clean-room addition next year.
- EVCO's Shenzhen plant has a 1,500-ton press capable of molding sizeable parts.
- EVCO also has presses up to 3,000 tons in Mexico.
- EVCO is qualifying a medical clean room at the Shenzhen plant to Class 100,000 standards.
- EVCO plans to move one medical molding and assembly cell from DeForest to Shenzhen.
- EVCO plans to seek ISO 13485 medical quality certification for its Shenzhen plant.
- EVCO states that 70% of a part's cost is determined by the design.
- EVCO emphasizes early involvement in project development and has ample design resources at its technical center in DeForest.
- The firm uses over 100 materials, primarily engineering resins like PPS, PC, ABS, PC/ABS, nylon, TPE, and acrylic, as well as PP, PE, and PS.
- EVCO prides itself on expertise in metal-to-plastic conversions.
- One conversion example is a foot well for construction equipment converted from metal to 23 lb of glass-filled nylon.
- Another example is a rotary debris-removal filter for agricultural equipment originally requiring an assembly of 15 metal and plastic parts and 144 operations, now reduced to four parts and one operation.
- A robot loads two halves of a 52-in.-diam. metal screen and a metal hub into a mold where they are overmolded and bonded together by glass-filled PP.
- EVCO uses rapid-prototyping technologies through outside service bureaus and its own 3D printer at the tech center.
- The company's 3D printer is a Projet3500 HD Max from 3D Systems that uses inkjet technology to deposit droplets of UV-curable liquid polymer.
- EVCO builds 30% of its own molds in Wisconsin and Georgia, plus another 20-30% in China.
- Dale Evans states the company controls the steel to ensure good quality.
- EVCO can build a wide variety of injection molds for large and small parts up to 300 cavities.
- The company builds hot-runner, insert, two-shot, gas-assist, thin-wall, and family molding molds, as well as stack molds.
- EVCO can build quick-turn prototype molds from aluminum using the same steel, gating, and side actions as multi-cavity production molds.
- Prototype molds are instrumented with cavity-pressure sensors and engineered with Scientific Molding techniques for scalability.
- More than a dozen EVCO employees have received training in Scientific Molding from RJG Inc. in Traverse City, Mich.
- EVCO has at least one mobile RJG monitoring system at every plant that can be moved from press to press for mold setup and qualification.
- The company incorporates provisions for sensors on every mold rather than putting cavity-pressure sensors on every mold.
- In some cases, EVCO uses in-mold temperature sensors to set up tools for glass-filled materials requiring a smooth resin-rich surface.
- EVCO keeps an eye on processes with Syscon-PlantStar monitors on every machine in North America.
- Dale Evans can monitor production from his office desk, including remotely checking the Georgia plant's machine status.
- The Syscon-PlantStar system is used for scheduling and monitoring cycle times, rejects, downtime, job completion, utilities (amperage, water temperatures), and chiller/compressor alarms.
- The system sends emails to appropriate personnel if a problem arises and allows maintenance staff to log in to check status.
- Operators can use the touchscreen to page supervisors, material handlers, or quality inspectors.
- At Oshkosh, the system is currently monitoring part weight, with plans to upgrade this year to include fill time, cavity pressure, and mold temperature monitoring.
- EVCO is certified to ISO 9001 at six plants in the U.S., China, and Mexico.
- EVCO is certified to the ISO 13485 medical quality standard at EVCO MED.
- EVCO is certified to the ISO/TS 16949 automotive quality standard at DeForest and Mexico.
- Dale Evans notes that in China, the company had its first external reject in six years.
- EVCO has 10 coordinate measuring machines (CMMs), leak and pressure testing equipment, and instruments for measuring melt flow, volume, tensile properties, gloss, color, and ash content.
- Every plant has a color spectrophotometer to maintain precisely matched molded-in color.
- All QA people undergo yearly testing and training to determine their ability to see color differences caused by varying heat histories on different machines.
- At the Oshkosh plant, robots drop parts onto weigh scales sensitive to 0.01 lb to check part weight as a key quality variable.
- EVCO has more than 104 robots, including three-axis Cartesian types, six-axis articulated units, SCARA pick-and-place units, and high-speed side-entry models.
- Dale Evans built EVCO AMP (Automated Molding Plant) in DeForest in 1989 to compete with Japanese plants.
- A fully automated cell at EVCO AMP uses a Husky Hylectric hybrid press and CBW Automation robotics to mold Redbox DVD cases with in-mold labeling.
- Another machine molds small round containers in a 48-cavity mold, with a six-axis robot grabbing 64 at a time for layer packing.
- At EVCO MED, a SCARA robot picks up 16 cannulas, submits them to a laser scan, and loads them into a rotary-table vertical press for overmolding with PS.
- Another cell with 26 automated stations produces a testing device that enters an enclosed assembly system after molding, where feeders add o-ring seals and apply adhesive labels without human handling.
- A third cell produces an electronic water control valve of PPS converted from stainless steel, using a robot to load six metal inserts of brass and stainless steel.
- After molding, parts pass through a cooling conveyor to automated inspection stations for optical flash checks and pressurized air gauge orifice diameter checks.
- Dale Evans states that exact tolerances cannot be achieved with hand labor in China, necessitating automation.
- EVCO uses its 3D printer to build fixtures for automation cells and robot end-of-arm tooling (EOAT).
- Plastic EOAT is lighter than metal, allows faster robot movement, channels air/vacuum without tubing, prevents mold damage during crashes, and can be built quickly and inexpensively.
- EVCO offers capabilities for product design, material selection, mold design, mold building, prototyping, and full contract manufacturing including assembly and decorating.
- Multicomponent molding example: EVCO made toothbrush handles by molding a rigid body, pad printing, then overmolding handles with two TPE materials in a second tool.
- Insert molding example: A large PC part for an ice machine inner door panel was molded on a 2,000-ton press with a robot loading 39 brass inserts at once.
- Another door assembly part was molded with 31 inserts.
- IMD/IML is a core competency available at U.S. and Chinese plants for packaging, medical devices, warning labels, and decoration on large parts.
- A glass-filled PET frozen-food tray application uses a four-sided in-mold label that provides an oxygen barrier and is selectively metalized for microwave reheating.
- A high-speed side-entry robot with a linear motor inserts the label and removes the box in under 9 seconds.
- EVCO is building its third cell for the frozen-food tray job.
- EVCO has 23 presses from 1,000 to 3,500 tons at seven plants in DeForest, Oshkosh, Calhoun, Mexico, and China.
- Large presses are used for appliances, agricultural equipment, off-road vehicles, outboard marine motor covers, riding lawnmower hoods, pallets, and more.
- The stable of big machines includes several Husky presses (1,000, 1,500, and 3,000 tons), a 750-ton Toyo press, and an Engel 3,500-tonner that arrived at Oshkosh last April.
- A 2,500-ton Engel is coming in June.
- The Engel 3,500-tonner features a robot with 300-lb capacity that travels the length of the machine to save floorspace.
- Magnetic platens are standard on big machines at Oshkosh for faster and safer mold mounting.
- Mold changes on a 3,300-ton press using quick-connects can be accomplished in as little as 1 hour.
- EVCO believes in continuing education and cycles people through its tech center to improve process understanding.
- The company sent eight people to the K 2013 show in Dusseldorf in October.
- EVCO has sent as many as 120 staffers to NPE shows.
- Dale Evans' philosophy is to keep learning, stating, "Today is the dumbest I'm ever going to be."
