# Rivian

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-05-01

**Source:** [Rivian Boosts Georgia Plant Capacity to 300,000 Vehicles Annually](https://driveteslacanada.ca/news/rivian-georgia-plant-capacity-increase/)  ·  `page_011881`

## Facts

- Rivian announced plans to increase the initial production capacity of its upcoming Georgia plant by 50%, bringing it to 300,000 vehicles per year.
- The capacity increase was revealed as part of the company’s latest earnings report.
- The update comes as the electric vehicle maker prepares for growth centered around its upcoming R2 platform.
- The Georgia facility is located near Stanton Springs.
- The facility is expected to play a key role in scaling production of Rivian’s more affordable, mass-market vehicles.
- Originally planned with a lower starting capacity, Rivian says the increase is designed to improve cost efficiency from day one.
- By building more vehicles at launch, the automaker aims to better absorb fixed costs across higher volumes—an important factor as they work toward profitability.
- The plant is still on track to begin vehicle production in late 2028.
- The expanded capacity applies to the first phase of development.
- Rivian says the site will also allow for additional growth in later phases, giving it room to scale even further if demand for its vehicles continues to rise.
- Supporting the project is a revised loan agreement with the U.S. Department of Energy.
- Rivian now expects to access up to $4.5 billion in financing for the Georgia facility, down from earlier projections of approximately $6.6 billion.
- Despite the lower total, the company says the updated structure offers more efficient capital deployment.
- Rivian anticipates drawing its first funds from the loan as early as 2027, earlier than previously expected.
- The Georgia plant will complement Rivian’s existing manufacturing operations in Normal, Illinois, where the company currently produces its R1T, R1S, commercial delivery vans.
- As of last month, Rivian has also begun early production of the R2 at its Illinois facility, with deliveries to employees already underway and customer deliveries expected to begin in a few weeks.
