# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-05-20

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Group to Establish First dedicated EV Plant and Battery Manufacturing Facility in the U.S.](https://www.hyundai.com/worldwide/en/newsroom/detail/hyundai-motor-group-to-establish-first-dedicated-ev-plant-and-battery-manufacturing-facility-in-the-u.s.-0000000068)  ·  `page_000124`

## Facts

- On May 20, 2022, Hyundai Motor Group announced an agreement with the State of Georgia to build its first dedicated full electric vehicle and battery manufacturing facilities in the U.S.
- The investment supports Hyundai's goal of becoming a leader in electric mobility in the U.S. market.
- The official signing ceremony was held in Bryan County, attended by Georgia Governor Brian P. Kemp, other Georgia officials, Hyundai Motor Company President and CEO Jaehoon Chang, Hyundai Motor President and Chief Operating Officer José Muñoz, and Executive Chair Euisun Chung (who joined virtually).
- Georgia Governor Brian P. Kemp called the project the largest in the state's history, delivering high-quality jobs on the leading edge of mobility to hardworking Georgians.
- Governor Kemp noted it is incredible that within a year of Georgia's investment in the Bryan County Megasite, they are locating a world-class project with a company with great Georgia history.
- Hyundai Motor Group Executive Chair Euisun Chung expressed pride in sharing plans to open the first dedicated full EV and battery manufacturing facilities in the U.S., highlighting the U.S.'s importance to the Group's global strategy and partnership with Georgia for electrified mobility.
- The new EV plant and battery manufacturing facilities represent an investment of approximately USD 5.54 billion.
- The new facility will break ground in early 2023 and is expected to begin commercial production in the first half of 2025.
- The facility will have an annual capacity of 300,000 units.
- The battery manufacturing facility will be established through a strategic partnership, with details to be disclosed at a later stage.
- The Group plans to produce a wide range of full electric vehicles for U.S. customers at the new Georgia EV plant, with production model details to be shared later.
- Local EV production aims to increase U.S. consumer accessibility to the Group's innovative EVs.
- Through the battery manufacturing facility, the Group aims to establish a stable supply chain and build a healthy EV ecosystem in the U.S.
- The EV and battery manufacturing plant will be located on a dedicated 2,923-acre site in Bryan County, Georgia.
- The site has immediate access to I-95 and I-16 highways, creating easy access to 250 major metro areas.
- The site is less than 50 kilometers from the Port of Savannah, the single-largest and fastest-growing container terminal in the U.S., with two Class I rail facilities on-site provided by Georgia Central Railway.
- West Point, Georgia is already home to the Group's Kia manufacturing hub in the US.
- Hyundai Motor Group selected Georgia due to favorable business conditions including speed-to-market, a talented workforce, and an existing network of Group affiliates and suppliers.
- Georgia is the Southeastern leader for EV registration per 1,000 registered automobiles and offers more publicly available EV charging outlets per capita than anywhere else in the Southeast.
- The Group's new investment will help accelerate the state's sustainable shift to electrification.
- The project is expected to create about 8,100 new jobs.
- The EV plant investment is part of the Group's 2021 announcement to invest USD 7.4 billion by 2025 to foster future mobility in the U.S., including production of EVs and offering smart mobility solutions.
- Hyundai Motor Group aims to lead the U.S. auto market's EV transition with the new facility.
- The Group has a global target to sell 3.23 million full electric vehicles annually by 2030.
- To realize this goal, the Group plans to establish a global EV production network to ensure a stable supply of EVs around the globe.
- Hyundai Motor President and CEO Jaehoon Chang stated the decision to build the first dedicated EV plant in the U.S. was driven by America embracing change and innovation, and that the plant will meet growing demands for leading edge design, safe, zero-emissions vehicles.
- With additional EV and battery production capabilities in the U.S., the Group aims to become one of the top three EV providers in the U.S. by 2026.
- The Group plans to lead the EV market not just in sales, but also in terms of design, technologies, and mobility solutions optimized for the electric mobility era.
- The investment aligns with the U.S. government's roadmap to accelerate electrification as global OEMs expand local EV production in the U.S.
- Last year, the U.S. government announced an initiative under which sales of zero-emission vehicles should account for at least half of OEM's new vehicle sales by 2030.
- The new plant will feature a highly connected, automated, and flexible manufacturing system that organically connects all elements of the EV ecosystem to realize customer value.
- The Group plans to implement advanced intelligent manufacturing technologies currently under test at the Hyundai Motor Group Innovation Center in Singapore (HMGICS).
- All production processes, including order collection, procurement, logistics, and production, will be optimized utilizing AI and data.
- The innovative manufacturing system will create a human-centered work environment with robots assisting human workers.
- As part of its commitment to sustainability, the plant will mainly rely on renewable energy sources to power the facility and use emission-reduction technologies to meet RE100 requirements.
