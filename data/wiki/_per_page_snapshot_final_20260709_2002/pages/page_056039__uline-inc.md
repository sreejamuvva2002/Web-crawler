# Uline, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Uline - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Uline)  ·  `page_056039`

## Facts

- Uline, Inc. is a private, family-owned company offering shipping and business supplies throughout North America.
- The company was founded in 1980 by Richard and Elizabeth Uihlein from their basement in Lake Forest, Illinois, using start-up funds from Edgar Uihlein.
- Its first product was the H-101 carton sizer.
- Operations expanded to Minnesota, California, and New Jersey during the 1980s and 1990s.
- In the 2000s, Uline opened distribution centers in Illinois, Texas, Georgia, Wisconsin, and Pennsylvania.
- The company's catalog grew to 450 pages offering over 17,000 products before later expanding further.
- In 2008, Uline announced construction of a new headquarters in Pleasant Prairie, Wisconsin, motivated by the founders' ties to the state.
- Wisconsin Governor Jim Doyle expected 1,000 jobs would move to Southeastern Wisconsin and pledged $6 million to support the headquarters move.
- The 200-acre headquarters opened in summer 2010, featuring offices and a 1-million square foot warehouse supplying distribution centers.
- An expansion with a second office building and warehouse was announced in 2014, with construction beginning in early 2016 and completing in 2017, bringing about 800 additional jobs to Pleasant Prairie.
- In 2019, Uline began considering a second major expansion with two more distribution centers totaling over 1.7 million square feet to complement existing centers in Pleasant Prairie.
- In 2021, Uline opened a warehouse in Reno, Nevada, to service Nevada and Northern California.
- Elizabeth Uihlein serves as president and chief executive officer, while Richard Uihlein is chairman of the board of directors.
- All three of their children serve as company executives: Duke Uihlein (Minnesota branch manager), Freddy Goldenberg (corporate planning manager), and Brian Uihlein (vice president of merchandising).
- Richard Uihlein's brother Steve also serves as a vice president.
- The headquarters consists of two buildings on 200 acres, including a 279,000 square foot facility and a newer 2017 building of about the same size.
- Plans announced in January 2024 to begin work on a third corporate campus building of 325,000 square feet.
- The company moved from Waukegan, Illinois, to Pleasant Prairie, Wisconsin, in 2010 in return for up to $18.6 million in state incentives.
- Uline regularly holds career fairs at its warehouses and uses workers on H-1B and temporary work visas.
- In 2015, Uline began using hydrogen-powered forklifts in its Pleasant Prairie facility, expanding their use to five other facilities between then and 2024, with four more facilities announced in 2024.
- The company's product catalog is mailed twice a year, is 800 pages long, and advertises over 37,500 products.
- In 2014, the company was estimated to have $2 billion in revenue, with ProPublica estimating nearly a billion dollars in profit in 2018.
- As of February 2020, the company reported over 6,700 employees and over $5.8 billion in revenue.
- In 2023, Uline reported US$11 billion in revenue and had 9,392 employees.
- The company donated $3 million to the city of Kenosha, Wisconsin, in 2023 to help build housing.
- Elizabeth and Richard Uihlein are major donors to far-right conservative and Republican causes, with the company and employees contributing over $31 million to political campaigns before June 2020.
