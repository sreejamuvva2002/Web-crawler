# Lear Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Lear Corp Earnings Call Highlights Margin Momentum - TipRanks.com](https://www.tipranks.com/news/company-announcements/lear-corp-earnings-call-highlights-margin-momentum)  ·  `page_057010`

## Facts

- Lear Corp. held its Q1 earnings call.
- Management emphasized solid growth, expanding margins, and a string of new business wins that underscore rising competitiveness.
- Executives acknowledged tariff-driven revenue noise, lingering E-Systems fixes, and macro uncertainty that kept them from raising full-year guidance despite a strong start to 2026.
- Lear reported Q1 2026 sales of $5.8 billion, up 5% from a year ago as the company continued to outgrow underlying auto production.
- Organic sales rose 3%, powered by higher volumes on existing Lear platforms and new Seating program wins, partly offset by tariff-related revenue adjustments that weighed on the reported top line.
- Core operating earnings climbed 10% to $297 million in the quarter.
- Adjusted earnings per share surged 24% year over year to $3.87, the best quarterly EPS since Q1 2019, signaling operating leverage as volumes grow and efficiency programs gain traction.
- The Seating business delivered $4.4 billion in sales, a 6% year-over-year increase.
- Adjusted earnings in Seating rose 9% to $305 million, and adjusted operating margins reached 6.9%, reflecting mix benefits and cost savings even as the company navigates commodity and production volatility.
- E-Systems posted $1.4 billion in revenue, up 1% with flat organic sales, but profit improvement was the standout.
- Adjusted earnings in E-Systems increased to $86 million, lifting margins to 6.1% from 5.2% a year ago, helped by higher volumes, net performance actions, and favorable foreign exchange.
- Management highlighted a series of high-profile commercial wins that bolster visibility into future growth.
- Lear secured a GM full-size SUV wire harness award.
- The company won multiple power distribution and high-voltage PDU deals including with Audi.
- Strong Chinese automaker wins pushed the 2026–2027 backlog up roughly $250 million, about $400 million over three years.
- Lear expanded its customer mix beyond traditional Western automakers in China.
- The company secured about $280 million in new business awards in China across Seating and E-Systems, split roughly evenly between wire-harness programs and complete-seat awards.
- Some volume from Chinese business awards flows through joint ventures.
- Lear’s Idea by Lear, automation, and net-performance programs produced about $70 million of savings in Q1.
- Management expects roughly $75 million of incremental savings ahead.
- The company is targeting full-year net-performance margin gains of 40 basis points in Seating and 80 basis points in E-Systems.
- Operating cash flow swung to a $98 million inflow compared with a use of cash last year.
- The company repurchased $75 million of shares in Q1.
- Lear is on track to buy back more than $300 million in 2026.
- The company aims for free cash flow conversion above 80%, extending a four-year track record of returning over $1.8 billion to shareholders.
- New U.S. tariff rules and a one-time reversal under earlier policy created a sizable drag on reported revenue without affecting earnings.
- Q1 saw a one-time $175 million year-over-year revenue reduction.
- Management expects a $285 million revenue headwind for 2026 versus last year, about $385 million worse than its February planning assumptions.
- Leaders refrained from lifting full-year guidance due to global economic softness and heightened geopolitical risks, including tension in the Middle East.
- Global vehicle production was down 3% on a calendar basis in Q1.
- Lear’s sales-weighted global production was up 3%.
- China vehicle production was down 5% on a sales-weighted basis.
- E-Systems organic sales were flat as prior operational issues and weaker EV-related volumes weighed on the top line.
- Remediation efforts, including fixes in Mexico, have largely addressed E-Systems problems.
- Some Q1 margin strength reflected temporary accounting effects rather than purely structural gains.
- Tariff-related accounting boosted margins by about 20 basis points in Seating and 40 basis points in E-Systems.
- An inventory revaluation benefit on copper aided results, prompting a more measured view of the run-rate.
- Rising input costs remain a manageable but real headwind, particularly for copper, foam chemicals, and steel.
- Timing lags and pricing frictions created roughly a $10 million earnings headwind versus prior expectations in the quarter.
- Several major awards are set to launch in late 2026 or 2027.
- The company must navigate compressed sourcing-to-launch cycles, especially in China, making on-time launches and smooth ramps critical to realizing the backlog’s full earnings potential.
- For 2026, Lear maintained its full-year outlook, noting that Q1 performance tracks between the midpoint and high end of guidance if trends hold.
- Near term, Q2 revenue is projected at $6.1–$6.2 billion with Seating margins in the mid-6% range, E-Systems in the low-5% range, and continued net-performance margin expansion despite tariff-related revenue headwinds.
