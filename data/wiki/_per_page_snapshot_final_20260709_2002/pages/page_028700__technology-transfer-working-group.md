# Technology Transfer Working Group

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [Winners of the 2022 Technology Transfer Working Group Awards! | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/technologycommercialization/articles/winners-2022-technology-transfer-working-group-awards)  ·  `page_028700`

## Facts

- Transitioning to electric vehicles (EVs) is transforming the automotive supply chain, requiring new innovations in battery storage technology.
- Oak Ridge National Laboratory (ORNL), the Tennessee Valley Authority, and the Tennessee Department of Economic & Community Development collaborated to drive home why EV battery firms should locate their innovation operations in Tennessee.
- Tennessee is the location of nearly 40 percent of the nation's EV jobs and investments.
- The collaboration team targeted a dozen automotive and EV battery companies.
- A key win included a $2.3 billion commitment by GM-LG Energy Solution JV to build a battery cell plant in Spring Hill, TN.
- The Spring Hill facility will be the second battery cell plant for the JV in the country.
- The investment will bring 1,300 new jobs to Tennessee.
- Jesse Smith and Jeffrey Cornett received the Economic Development award for leading this project.
- The project is titled 'ORNL's and Partners' $2B Win Is Largest in Tennessee's History'.
