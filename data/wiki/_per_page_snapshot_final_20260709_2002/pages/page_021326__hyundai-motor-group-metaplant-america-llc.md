# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America LLC

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hyundai’s new American dream comes with new plant, more spending - KED Global](https://www.kedglobal.com/automobiles/newsView/ked202503270007)  ·  `page_021326`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America LLC (HMGMA) was opened in Ellabell, Georgia by executive chairman Chung Euisun.
- The HMGMA project represents Hyundai Motor Group's largest US investment and the largest economic development project in Georgia's history.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp stated at the opening that the company is “not just cutting ribbons anymore. It’s breaking new ground.”
- Hyundai Motor Group completed construction of an $8 billion electric vehicle plant in Georgia with an annual production capacity of 300,000 vehicles.
- When combined with its two other US plants, the Georgia facility will allow Hyundai to manufacture at least 1 million cars annually.
- Days before the opening, executive chairman Chung visited the White House at President Donald Trump's request to unveil a $21 billion investment package.
- The $21 billion package includes the HMGMA expansion and a new vertically integrated electric steel mill built by Hyundai Steel Co. in Louisiana.
- Hyundai aims to control its supply chain "from molten metal to mobility" on US soil through these investments.
- Hyundai chairman stated during the inauguration ceremony: “With the rich history of craftsmanship and manufacturing in this community, together with the talented workforce at HMGMA, we are building the future of mobility with America, in America.”
- Event attendees included Georgia Gov. Brian P. Kemp, US Representative Buddy Carter, Hyundai Motor Group Vice Chair Chang Jae-hoon, Hyundai Motor CEO José Muñoz, Kia CEO Song Ho-sung, and HMGMA employees referred to as “Meta Pros.”
- The plant opening coincides with President Trump's announcement of a 25% tariff on imported vehicles taking effect on April 3.
- Analysts say Hyundai is preemptively scaling up US-based manufacturing to offset the negative impact of auto tariffs and improve price competitiveness.
- Hyundai Motor Group sold 1.71 million vehicles in the US last year, ranking fourth behind General Motors (2.69 million), Toyota (2.33 million), and Ford (2.07 million).
- With the Georgia plant, Hyundai aims to overtake Ford and break into America’s automotive Big Three.
- Globally, Hyundai ranks third in vehicle sales behind Toyota and Volkswagen.
- The HMGMA facility spans 11.8 million square meters, roughly four times the size of Seoul’s Yeouido financial district.
- The plant will be ramped up to produce 500,000 units a year within the next few years.
- Combined with Hyundai's Alabama factory (360,000 unit capacity) and Kia's Georgia plant in West Point (340,000 unit capacity), the group is poised to build up to 1.2 million vehicles annually in the US.
- This combined US production capacity will be equivalent to 70% of Hyundai's 2024 sales in the market.
- In October 2024, less than two years after breaking ground, the first electric vehicle (a Hyundai IONIQ 5) rolled off HMGMA’s assembly line.
- The Hyundai IONIQ 9 three-row electric SUV is now in production at HMGMA and will be joined in 2026 by the first Kia model built at the facility.
- In addition to EVs for Hyundai, Genesis, and Kia, the plant is equipped to produce hybrid vehicles.
- Kia CEO Song stated that Kia will account for roughly 40% of HMGMA’s production, mostly hybrids.
- All HMGMA processes including order collection, procurement, logistics, and production are optimized using AI and real-time data.
- The facility integrates cutting-edge robotics and vision systems to enhance automation, including automated guided vehicles for logistics.
- Boston Dynamics’ robot dog Spot is used for exterior quality inspection in the weld shop, and parking robots operate in the assembly shop.
- Automation in HMGMA’s assembly line is nearly double that of Hyundai's existing facilities.
- A solar parking lot at the site generates 5.2 megawatts of energy, accounting for roughly 5% of Metaplant’s total power consumption.
- Hyundai's investment in Georgia related to HMGMA and joint battery ventures with LG Energy Solution Ltd. and SK On Co. totals $12.6 billion.
- The project includes a total of 8,500 jobs at HMGMA by 2031, according to Hyundai.
- Total investments in Georgia are expected to create nearly 40,000 direct and indirect jobs and generate $4.6 billion in individual earnings every year, according to the Center for Automotive Research.
- Under the announced $21 billion US investment plan through 2028, $9 billion will be used to expand HMGMA facilities.
- $6 billion will be allocated to enhance parts and logistics business and establish an advanced steel mill in the US with an annual production capacity of 2.7 million tons.
- The new steel mill will supply high-quality automotive steel plates to HMGMA when commercial production begins in 2029.
- An additional $6 billion will drive innovation and expand strategic partnerships with US companies in autonomous driving, robotics, AI, and advanced air mobility (AAM).
- Through the $21 billion commitment, Hyundai expects to create 14,000 new direct full-time jobs in the US by 2028.
- The overall economic impact is expected to generate more than 100,000 direct and indirect job opportunities across related industries.
