# Hella

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2022-10-24

**Source:** [Hella opens e-mobility development centre in Romania - electrive.com](https://www.electrive.com/2022/10/24/hella-opens-e-mobility-development-centre-in-romania/)  ·  `page_055780`

## Facts

- Automotive supplier Hella operates under the umbrella brand Forvia.
- Hella is expanding its European R&D network with two new facilities in the Romanian cities of Iași and Oradea.
- The focus of the new facilities will be on autonomous driving and electromobility.
- The Technical Center in Oradea will primarily concentrate on developing products for electric vehicles, including battery management systems and voltage converters.
- In Iași, software development and testing for 77 GHz radar sensors is initially entrusted to the new location.
- The 77 GHz radar sensors are described as a central key technology for assisted and automated driving.
- Both locations have already started operations in autumn 2022 with a core team of around ten engineers each.
- Hella plans to create a total of up to 300 new jobs at the two locations over the next three to four years.
- The planned jobs are not broken down between the topics of electromobility and autonomous driving.
- Forvia is the name of the group of companies since the takeover of Hella by the French supplier Faurecia last year.
- Björn Twiehaus serves as Managing Director Electronics at Hella.
- Björn Twiehaus stated that Romania has already been represented for many years with essential high-performance electronics sites where highly sophisticated control units are developed.
- Björn Twiehaus said it was obvious to build on this network and further strengthen Hella's presence in the country.
