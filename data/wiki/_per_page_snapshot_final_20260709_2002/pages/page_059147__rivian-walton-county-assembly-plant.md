# Rivian Walton County Assembly Plant

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-02-14

**Source:** [Community concerned about proposed Rivian electric car plant in Walton County | KRDO](https://krdo.com/news/2022/02/14/community-concerned-about-proposed-rivian-electric-car-plant-in-walton-county/)  ·  `page_059147`

## Facts

- The proposed Rivian electric car plant in Walton County, Georgia, is touted as the state’s largest economic development project ever.
- Community members are concerned about the project, with some feeling robbed of their rights as property owners.
- Chas Moore, a resident of Rutledge and part of the group Our Communities Oppose Rivian Assembly Plant, stated that locals have not been spoken to by Rivian despite potential partnership benefits.
- Only one meeting for homeowners with officials has occurred since the project's December announcement.
- The proposed plant will span 20 million square feet, an area large enough to fit almost 10 Mercedes Benz stadiums inside the site.
- Locals are concerned the plant site sits on land that refills their groundwater water supply.
- Chas Moore noted that residents outside the city of Madison rely entirely on private wells and lack infrastructure to connect to city water in case of emergencies.
- The Jasper, Morgan, Newton & Walton Development Authority, a collaborative group formed by members from those four counties, is listed as the developer of the site.
- The development authority states groundwater will be protected through storm water runoff management, use of water and sewer service from the Newton County Water & Sewerage Authority, and strict adherence to state and federal soil erosion guidelines.
- Rivian would receive the biggest tax break ever offered by the state for this project.
- The Georgia government claims the plant will bring in 7,500 jobs.
- Chas Moore observed that there is already a substantial amount of unfilled industrial jobs available in the surrounding area.
- Rivian's vice president of public policy expressed hope to start construction on the plant this summer.
