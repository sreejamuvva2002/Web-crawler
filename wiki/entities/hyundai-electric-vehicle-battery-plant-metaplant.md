# Hyundai electric vehicle battery plant (Metaplant)

**Type:** Facility  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[battery-cell-manufacturing|Battery Cell Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** Ellabell, Georgia, Bryan County, Georgia, United States

## Overview

A Hyundai-owned electric-vehicle battery manufacturing facility in Ellabell, Georgia that was the target of the largest U.S. immigration raid in September 2025 and is a centerpiece of Georgia’s EV supply-chain incentives.

## EV supply-chain relevance

The plant produces battery cells for electric vehicles, representing the largest EV battery investment in the state and a key node of the U.S. EV supply chain.

## Investment

- **$1.8 billion** (2022) — State incentives from Georgia to build the Metaplant
  - Georgia offered $1.8 billion in subsidies to attract Hyundai’s battery plant, the largest economic-development project in the state’s history.

## Jobs

- **over 40,000 jobs (including supplier jobs)** — plant expected to be fully operational in 2026
  - Areas: battery cell manufacturing, assembly, logistics, support services
  - At the time of the raid the plant had created 2 824 jobs, with 73 % held by local residents; once complete about 99 % of the projected 8 000 workers will be Americans.

## Timeline

- **4 September 2025** — Federal immigration agents raided Hyundai’s electric-vehicle battery plant in Ellabell, Georgia, arresting 475 workers.
- **9 September 2025** — Crowds protested outside the U.S. Embassy in Seoul following the raid.
- **October 2022** — Groundbreaking ceremony for Hyundai’s Metaplant battery facility in Ellabell, Georgia.
- **2023** — Georgia approved $1.8 billion in incentives to support the Metaplant project.
- **2026 (planned)** — Projected full operational start of the Hyundai battery plant, creating over 40,000 jobs.

## Key facts

- The raid was the largest immigration enforcement action at a single U.S. location in history.
- 475 workers were detained, including 300 Korean nationals; most had valid work visas.
- The incident strained U.S.-South Korea relations and prompted diplomatic talks between President Lee Jae Myung and President Donald Trump.
- Hyundai’s Metaplant was marketed as ‘Where the Future of Mobility Begins’ and is the biggest economic-development project in Georgia’s history.
- State incentives totaling $1.8 billion were offered to Hyundai, making it a flagship EV battery investment for the state.
- The raid delayed construction by several months, pushing back the plant’s 2026 opening timeline.
- Hyundai has a documented history of labor controversies, including safety violations and alleged use of child or prison labor at U.S. suppliers.
- Three worker deaths were reported at the Metaplant site in the two years preceding the raid.
- At the time of the raid the plant had created 2 824 jobs, with 73 % held by local residents; once complete about 99 % of the projected 8 000 workers will be Americans.
- The South’s low-wage, union-free environment was a key factor in Hyundai’s decision to locate the battery plant in Georgia.
