## Facts

- Founder, CEO & Chairman of the Board Robert Scaringe celebrated the start of saleable R2 production last week.
- Rivian has started R2 deliveries to its employees.
- Rivian expects R2's bill of materials and non-BOM costs to be about half those of R1.
- Rivian anticipates short-term gross profit pressure from launch complexity before margins improve in the fourth quarter.
- The R2 is positioned as a catalyst toward long-term profitability.
- Rivian maintains unchanged guidance of 62,000 to 67,000 vehicle deliveries for the full year.
- Rivian expects Q2 deliveries to be 9,000-11,000.
- Back-half of the year will be weighted toward R2 ramps.
- Management acknowledges macro and geopolitical uncertainties as risks.
- Recent tornado damage is noted as a risk.
- Commodity cost pressures are noted as a risk.
- Rivian highlights proactive supply-chain actions and alternative sourcing to contain disruptions.
- These supply chain actions were factored into unchanged guidance.
- The company is scaling its Georgia phase 1 capacity to 300,000 units.
