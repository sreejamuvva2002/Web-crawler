# Lumpkin County, Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2009-01-01

**Source:** [Lumpkin County: From Tourism To High Tech - Georgia Trend Magazine](https://www.georgiatrend.com/2009/01/01/lumpkin-county-from-tourism-to-high-tech/)  ·  `page_059998`

## Facts

- Mohawk closed its Dahlonega carpet fiber plant in spring, where some 366 were employed, including two for more than 50 years.
- The former Mohawk facility covered 35 acres and occupied a 365,000-square-foot building now available for $3.5 million.
- Lumpkin County formed the North Georgia Network (NGN) with Forsyth, Dawson, White, and Union counties to run fiber-optic cable up Highway 400 to the North Carolina state line.
- The NGN received a $240,000 grant from Gov. Perdue on Dec. 1 to fund a feasibility study for the fiber-optic project, with Lumpkin serving as the lead applicant due to its high poverty rate.
- Lumpkin County is pursuing a strategy to build a fiber-optic network to attract tech-dependent businesses and retain its trained manufacturing workforce.
- Georgia Tech is contracted as a contractor for the fiber-optic infrastructure project, alongside a national consulting firm assisting with infrastructure development and recruiting programs.
- North Georgia College and State University (NGCSU) is partnering in creating the workforce to support the region's tech-dependent economic shift.
- NGCSU currently has an enrollment of 5,500 students and graduates between 65 to 80 computer science majors annually.
- In August, the NGCSU Mike Cottrell School of Business opened with undergraduate courses in accounting, finance, management, and marketing, plus an MBA program in Forsyth County.
- Mike Cottrell, whose company Cottrell Inc. is the nation’s largest manufacturer of car-haulers, donated $10 million to establish the NGCSU business school.
- Dr. Max Burns was named dean of the NGCSU Mike Cottrell School of Business.
- The U.S. Army has charged NGCSU with graduating 100 commissioned lieutenants per year.
- New manufacturing companies relocating to or operating in Lumpkin County include RefrigiWear, The Louver Shop (shifting its manufacturing line to the county), and Polly Portables (which manufactures 15 to 20 percent of all portable toilets and chemicals).
- Timken U.S. Corporation, Southern Switches, Walmart, Chestatee Regional Hospital, and The Home Depot are listed as major employers in Lumpkin County.
- Lumpkin County passed a Special Purpose Local Option Sales Tax (SPLOST) last Spring to fund water and sewer treatment capacity upgrades, with revenues holding steady despite the economic downturn.
- The city of Dahlonega is constructing a new water treatment plant to increase capacity for future residents.
- Lumpkin County's population has grown by approximately 1,000 new residents annually since 2000, reaching 26,554 in 2007.
- The county's per capita income was $25,591 in 2006, compared to the Georgia state average of $30,914.
- Unemployment in Lumpkin County stood at 7.8 percent in October 2008, slightly above the Georgia state rate of 7 percent.
- Former Mohawk manufacturing jobs paid approximately $10 per hour, highlighting the wage gap the county aims to address with tech and higher-paying industries.
- The Dahlonega-Lumpkin County Chamber of Commerce holds an Entrepreneur Friendly Community designation, one of only 80 in the state, aimed at attracting small businesses and IT-type companies.
- Tourism remains a major economic engine for Lumpkin County, with Gold Rush Days drawing a record 200,000 visitors and hotel/B&B occupancy increasing by 9 percent.
- Five wineries have opened in Lumpkin County, leveraging the region's climate and land slope for grape cultivation and wedding tourism.
- Liquor by the drink was passed last November in the county to support potential boutique hotel development along Hwy. 16 and Ga. 400.
