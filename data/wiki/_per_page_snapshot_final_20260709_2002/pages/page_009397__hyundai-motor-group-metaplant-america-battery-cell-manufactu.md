# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America Battery Cell Manufacturing Joint Venture

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-09

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution to invest additional $2 billion at Bryan County Megasite - SEDA](https://seda.org/2023/09/hyundai-motor-group-invest/)  ·  `page_009397`

## Facts

- In May 2023, Hyundai Motor Group and LGES signed a memorandum of understanding establishing LGES as the partner for Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America’s (HMGMA) onsite battery cell manufacturing JV.
- The additional investment announced on Aug. 31, 2023 is $2 billion.
- The EV manufacturing facility and the battery JV represent an estimated $7.59 billion in total investment.
- The project will create 8,500 new jobs for the region over the next eight years.
- Dong-Myung Kim, president and head of the Advanced Automotive Battery Division of LG Energy Solution, stated the investment underscores dedication to driving America’s EV transition while bolstering the local economy through the creation of quality jobs.
- Dong-Myung Kim noted thanks to support from the State of Georgia, they are excited to bring top-quality products and storied operational experience to grow with local communities.
- The 30 GWh facility will be able to support the production of 300,000 units of EVs annually at full operations.
- Hyundai Mobis will assemble battery packs using cells from the plant, then supply them to the Hyundai Motor Group’s U.S. manufacturing facilities for production of Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis EV models.
- Carter Infinger, Chairman of the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority, stated the announcement will bring 400 additional well-paying jobs for those in the Savannah region.
- Carter Infinger noted the continued investment by Hyundai Motor Group and announced suppliers like LG Energy Solution is truly remarkable and will be transformative for the region.
- Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy Alyce Thornhill represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project in partnership with the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority, Georgia Power, Georgia Ports Authority, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated Governor Kemp set a goal of Georgia becoming the top location for EV manufacturing in the United States, and the state is in a great position to claim that distinction with this announcement.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated through focus on building an ecosystem supporting the entire EV supply chain, Georgia continues adding to industry-leading success and bringing future jobs to Georgians in every corner of the state.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson noted that state and community partners like the Savannah-area JDA, utility providers, Georgia Ports Authority, and Georgia Quick Start create a collaborative, business-friendly environment that sets Georgia up for success.
- Georgia has pursued job creation along the entire supply chain, resulting in more than $25 billion in investments and the creation of over 30,000 jobs since 2018.
- Battery related projects have accounted for at least $11 billion in investment in Georgia.
- In fiscal year 2023, job creation in the automotive industry increased by 324 percent when compared to FY21.
