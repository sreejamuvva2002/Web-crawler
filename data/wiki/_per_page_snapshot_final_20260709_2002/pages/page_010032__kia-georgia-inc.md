# Kia Georgia, Inc.

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-04-24

**Source:** [VPS and Kia Georgia Complete Landmark Automotive Hail Protection and Solar Canopy Project](https://www.kiamedia.com/us/en/media/pressreleases/24633/vps-and-kia-georgia-complete-landmark-automotive-hail-protection-and-solar-canopy-project)  ·  `page_010032`

## Facts

- Kia Georgia completed a large-scale hail protection and solar canopy system at its West Point manufacturing facility in Georgia.
- The project was announced on April 24, 2026, with an official ribbon-cutting ceremony scheduled for the same day.
- The installation spans 3.2 million square feet of vehicle protection area.
- The system generates 10 megawatts of on-site solar power capacity used exclusively by Kia Georgia.
- It is one of the largest behind-the-meter solar canopy projects in the Southeast.
- The project was developed to address increasing weather volatility and protect high-value vehicle inventory following a 2023 hailstorm that damaged more than 13,000 vehicles at the facility.
- VPS (Vehicle Protection Structures) delivered the turnkey system, which provides continuous vehicle protection while converting previously unused space into an energy asset.
- The solar panels integrated into the canopy were manufactured by Georgia-based Qcells, supporting regional supply chains.
- The on-site renewable power generation reduces Kia Georgia's reliance on external energy sources and stabilizes long-term operating costs.
- Wade White, Executive Vice President at VPS, stated that combining large-scale protection with on-site solar generation turns the investment into a source of ongoing value and reflects a new standard for manufacturer risk management.
- Stuart Countess, President and CEO of Kia Georgia, Inc., noted the solution will protect vehicles, support the team, generate power on-site, and position the company for a more efficient and sustainable future.
- Cheryl Davis, Vice President of Customer Service at Georgia Power, highlighted that the project demonstrates strategic energy thinking and helps customers meet renewable energy and sustainability goals while solving unique business needs.
- The completion of the project highlights a growing trend among manufacturers adopting integrated infrastructure solutions to address multiple challenges simultaneously, reduce financial exposure, improve efficiency, and better utilize existing space.
- VPS, a PlayPower brand and leader in automotive hail, heat, and UV protection since 1991, provided full-service design-build support from initial survey and design through engineering, fabrication, construction, and post-project service.
