# Lehigh Technologies

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Michelin acquires Lehigh Technologies | European Rubber Journal](https://www.european-rubber-journal.com/article/2065805/michelin-acquires-lehigh-technologies)  ·  `page_057131`

## Facts

- Michelin has acquired US scrap tire and rubber recycling company Lehigh Technologies.
- The acquisition was announced by Michelin North America on 18 Oct.
- Terms of the deal were not disclosed.
- Lehigh is based in Tucker, near Atlanta, Georgia.
- Lehigh has developed a patented cryogenic turbo mill technology to transform rubber from end-of-life tires and industrial goods into materials for new tires and other products.
- The 'micronised' powder (MRP) end-product is offered as a low-cost, sustainable substitute for conventional oil- and rubber-based materials.
- Applications for MRP encompass tires, plastics, asphalt and construction materials.
- Customers include some of the world’s largest tire companies.
- Michelin stated it is 'always looking for ways to achieve more sustainable mobility, including by using high-technology recycled materials, without compromising safety or other performances.'
- Christophe Rahier, senior VP of strategic planning materials for Michelin Group, said the acquisition demonstrates Michelin’s strategic intent to foster the use of advanced rubber recycled materials in the tire and non-tire industries.
- Alan Barton, CEO of Lehigh, stated the acquisition provides Lehigh with an 'incredible opportunity' to continue its growth.
- Barton added that with Michelin's backing, Lehigh can expand capabilities, execute its global growth strategy, and pursue new market opportunities.
- Lehigh claims its US facility in Tucker is the world’s largest MRP manufacturing plant.
- The Tucker facility has an annual production capacity of 140 million pounds (around 64 kilotonnes).
- Lehigh also set up a factory in Murillo el Fruto, northern Spain to serve European markets.
- The Spanish facility was originally due for start-up in 2016 and then early 2017, though there has been no recent announcement about its status.
- The Spanish facility is equipped with a 10,000-tonne a year capacity cryogenic turbo mill to produce MRP from end-of-life tires, buffings and post-industrial scrap.
