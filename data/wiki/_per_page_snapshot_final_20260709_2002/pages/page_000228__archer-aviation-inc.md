# Archer Aviation Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-11-14

**Source:** [Archer to Create 1,000 Jobs in Covington | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-releases/2022/electric-vertical-aircraft-manufacturing-company-archer-create-1000-jobs-covington)  ·  `page_000228`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Archer Aviation Inc. will create 1,000 jobs and invest $118 million over 10 years in a new state-of-the-art electric vertical takeoff and landing aircraft (eVTOL) manufacturing facility.
- The facility will be located alongside the Covington Municipal Airport in Newton County.
- Georgia has made fostering innovation a priority to bring the jobs of the future to the No. 1 state for business.
- Georgia's established leadership in the aerospace industry was essential to landing Archer Aviation in Newton County.
- Archer is based in Santa Clara, CA and is currently developing and manufacturing eVTOL aircraft for use in Urban Air Mobility networks.
- Adam Goldstein, Archer’s Founder and CEO, stated that Archer decided to locate its first manufacturing facility in Covington as it progresses towards commercialization.
- Archer's goal is creating Urban Air Mobility networks across major cities nationwide, which requires ramping production of its aircraft.
- Glen Burks, Archer’s Vice President of Manufacturing, noted the new manufacturing facility is a crucial next step in bringing their vision for eVTOL travel to life.
- Archer plans to break ground on the facility in the coming months.
- Archer plans to construct the facility adjacent to the Covington Municipal Airport in Newton County, which was established in 1963 as part of a long-term airport master plan.
- The site is strategically accessible by Georgia’s robust interstate system via I-20, and large adjacent parcels of land were set aside for future development that the new facility will use.
- Archer’s new facility will consist of three buildings across more than 96 acres.
- Construction is expected to begin in December 2022.
- The buildout will follow a phased approach for eVTOL aircraft production, with eventual capacity able to produce up to 2,300 aircraft per year.
- Hiring is expected to begin in late 2023.
- The facility is slated to open in the first half of 2024.
- Mayor Steve Horton expressed excitement for Archer Aviation to join Covington's business community and noted that Covington has always focused on quality jobs and new technology.
- Lanier Sims, Chairman of the Newton County Industrial Development Authority, stated that Archer fits perfectly with Newton County's current industry mix and will be an asset to the airport in Covington.
- The Newton County Industrial Development Authority is committed to ensuring the workforce is prepared for the community's future and ready for jobs from Archer Aviation.
- Metro Atlanta Chamber President and CEO Katie Kirkpatrick welcomed Archer Aviation to metro Atlanta, citing the region's aviation industry expertise and support from academic, business, civic, and government partners.
- The Global Commerce Industrial Projects team represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) on this competitive project.
- Partners supporting the project include the Newton County Industrial Development Authority, Metro Atlanta Chamber of Commerce, the Georgia Center of Innovation’s Aerospace industry team, Georgia EMC, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia Tech.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson highlighted that Archer Aviation is on the forefront of a technological evolution in aviation using an innovative eVTOL aircraft design to sustainably tackle mobility issues in urban centers.
- The GDEcD Center of Innovation’s specialized Aerospace team has been involved in fostering Georgia’s aerospace and defense industries.
- Home to more than 800 aerospace companies, Georgia maintains a robust aerospace education pipeline including a dozen high schools with training programs, five technical colleges with aviation programs, and several universities offering degrees in aerospace engineering.
- The Georgia Center of Innovation for Aerospace is a strategic arm of the GDEcD that has been cultivating an ecosystem for innovative companies like Archer Aviation for more than 10 years.
- The Georgia Center of Innovation for Aerospace builds and connects Unmanned Aerial Systems (UAS) networks for the commercialization of Advanced Air Mobility and UAS Systems.
- Archer (NYSE: ACHR) is designing and developing electric vertical takeoff and landing aircraft for use in urban air mobility networks, with a mission to unlock the skies and reimagine how people move.
- Archer's team is based in Santa Clara, CA.
