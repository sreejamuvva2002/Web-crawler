# Hyundai Metaplant Bryan

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-05-05

**Source:** [Georgia Governor Bryan Kemp signing ceremony Hyundai Metaplant Bryan](https://www.savannahnow.com/story/news/2023/05/05/georgia-governor-bryan-kemp-signing-ceremony-hyundai-metaplant-bryan/70187060007/)  ·  `page_060350`

## Facts

- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp signed a sales use tax exemption for projects of regional significance at the future site of the Hyundai Metaplant in Bryan County.
- The South Korean automaker's electric vehicle and battery manufacturing facility is scheduled to open in late 2024.
- The facility expects to employ 8,100 workers.
- Kemp was joined for the signing ceremony by Georgia Speaker of the House Jon Burns, an Effingham County native who owns a farm near the site.
- Kemp was also joined by Georgia Sen. Ben Watson, a Savannahian whose district includes Bryan County.
- In addition to the tax exemption, Kemp signed the state's Fiscal Year 2024 budget into law at the event.
- Georgia's state budget tops $32.4 billion.
- House Bill 19 centers on raises for law enforcement and teachers, with teachers receiving a $2,000 raise and law enforcement seeing a $6,000 increase.
- Kemp stated: “We are investing in a pipeline, from the classroom to the boardroom,” noting more money is being put towards education than ever before in state history.
- Kemp announced the Hope Scholarship will be restored to 100% of tuition for students attending public universities and technical colleges.
- The budget includes $52 million for Georgia Pathways to Coverage, the state's Medicaid waiver program.
- Individuals earning less than 100% of the federal poverty level are eligible for Georgia’s Pathways to Coverage if they work, attend school, or perform community service at least 80 hours a month.
- Kemp stated the administration is working to successfully launch the Medicaid program on July 1 of this year.
- Another key aspect of the bill includes hiring an analyst to work alongside the prosecution unit tasked with tackling human trafficking in Georgia.
- House Bill 408 extends a sales use tax break for regional projects meant to spur investment.
- Kemp called Hyundai Metaplant America and another battery plant coming to Bartow County “transformational projects” and cited them as reasons why House Bill 408 is imperative.
- Kemp noted that most communities benefiting from the tax exemption tool are located outside the metro Atlanta area.
- Kemp reminded attendees that suppliers related to the Hyundai project exceeded financial projections.
- Kemp announced over 4,500 new jobs in roughly $1.96 billion dollars of investment for the Hyundai project's site suppliers.
- The announced supplier investment number is nearly double the original estimate because the company chose Georgia.
