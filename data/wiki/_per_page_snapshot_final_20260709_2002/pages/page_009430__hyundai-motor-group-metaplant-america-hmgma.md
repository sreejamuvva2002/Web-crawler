# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA)

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-02

**Source:** [Hyundai's new Metaplant in Georgia gives Millions of Dollars in Social Impact - AutoNews-Wire.com](https://autonews-wire.com/hyundai-metaplant-georgia-millions-dollars-social-impact)  ·  `page_009430`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group is building its largest and most modern electrified car manufacturing plant in the United States in Savannah, Georgia.
- The modern metaplant is expected to be ready to make cars by the end of 2024 or beginning of 2025.
- Hyundai will produce a diverse range of innovative Hyundai, Genesis, and Kia electric vehicles (EVs) at the facility.
- The plant will also build the most important component of electric cars, the battery, in a joint venture with LGES, LG Energy Solution.
- Vehicles expected to be built locally include Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis EV models like the IONIQ 5, IONIQ 6, GV60, and Electrified GV70.
- The hydrogen-powered XCIENT Fuel Cell Truck will be used for logistics needs between Hyundai’s manufacturing facilities and suppliers around the metaplant.
- The new car manufacturing plant will be open to the public, offering free tours to see the facility.
- The plant site, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America, LLC (HMGMA), is located at 10484 US Highway 280, Ellabell, GA 31308.
- The site is located just about 40 minutes drive from Savannah, Georgia.
- The new plant covers a site of about 3,000 acres.
- Hyundai named the facility "Metaplant" to signify transformation or transcending, focusing on electrification, social impact, employee motivation, and being eco-friendly.
- The official name of the factory in Bryan County, Georgia, is Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA).
- It is Hyundai Motor Group’s largest plant in the United States.
- The project is supported by José Muñoz, President and Global Chief Operating Officer for Hyundai Motor Company and also President and CEO for Hyundai, and Genesis Motor North America.
- CEO Jose Muñoz has given guidelines to give back to the community and invest in the well-being of children, education, and the environment.
- "Georgia is like a second home to us," said Jose Muñoz.
- Hyundai’s future global goals include becoming the world’s leading mobility solutions provider, implying social responsibility and producing a positive impact in the Georgia community.
- Joe LaMuraglia, Communications, and PR Manager for HMGMA, discussed benefits including donations to charity organizations with tons of social impact.
- The venture includes educational programs with high schools and colleges.
- The plant will provide training for new employees.
- The project will create more than 8,000 new jobs for the area.
- Joint ventures and third-party suppliers will help the local economy grow.
- Brandon Ramirez, Director of Corporate Social Responsibility and External Relations at Hyundai Motor America, highlighted building a bridge between the plant and the Georgia community.
- Hyundai has donated more than one and half million dollars to charity initiatives in Georgia through Hyundai Hope, Hyundai Hope On Wheels, Genesis Gives, Genesis Inspiration Foundation, and Kia Accelerate the Good.
- Recent beneficiaries of Hyundai Motor America’s donations include The Salvation Army of Savannah, Frank Callen Boys & Girls Club, Bulloch County Boys & Girls Club, and Coastal Empire Habitat for Humanity.
- Hyundai Motor America provided STEM scholarships to Georgia Southern University.
- The Hyundai Hydrogen STEM Program has come to action in Bryan County and Effingham County near the new Metaplant.
- Through this program, middle school students learn about hydrogen, build their own hydrogen vehicles, and race them on a Hyundai track.
- Hyundai has already invested more than one million dollars in different organizations in Georgia near Bryan County and Savannah.
- Vanessa Perez and Ciara Calub from HMNA (Hyundai Motor North America) provided feedback on benefits and cultural influences the plant will bring to the area.
- The new Metaplant is targeting production starting end of 2024 into 2025 with an annual capacity of 300,000 cars.
- Production capacity can be expanded to almost double if the market requires it.
- The investment into this Georgia facility is part of Hyundai’s pledged $10 billion investment into the U.S. mobility market.
- Approximately $1 billion is anticipated to be invested by non-affiliated, HMGMA suppliers in connection with the project in other locations.
- More production in the United States means less production in South Korea, Hyundai's homeland.
- Hyundai has Headquarters in California and two large plants in Alabama and Georgia.
- Hyundai plans to lead the EV market in sales, design, technologies, EV and battery production capabilities, and mobility solutions.
- Hyundai wants to evolve beyond an automaker into the world’s leading mobility solutions provider.
- The new Metaplant consolidates Hyundai Motor Group as one of the leaders in the U.S. auto market’s EV transition.
- Hyundai maintains its position as one of the top three EV providers in the U.S.
- The Group’s electrification goal includes a global target to sell 3.23 million fully electric vehicles annually in the next few years.
- Employees at the plant are called "Meta Pros" by Hyundai Motor Group.
- The Metaplant is expected to employ more than eight thousand people from Bryan County, Georgia, and Savannah, Georgia.
- Partner companies working next to the Metaplant will be a source of employment for small businesses and independent contractors.
- Hyundai partnered with four technical colleges in the Savannah area to train students in the Electrical Vehicle Professional (EVP) Technical Certificate of Credit (TCC).
- After graduation from the EVP TCC program, students have the opportunity to be employed at the new Hyundai Metaplant with higher wages and positions.
- The short 8-credit-hour certificate includes 3 courses: Automotive Technology Introduction, Automotive Electrical Principles, and Introduction to EV/Hybrid Vehicles and Safety Protocols.
- Savannah Technical College offers the program at its Savannah and Liberty County Campuses.
- Southeastern Technical College offers the program at its Vidalia Campus.
- Coastal Pines Technical College offers the program at its Baxley and Waycross Campuses.
- The certificate program prepares students for entry-level employment in the electrified vehicle production industry, focusing on safe shop operations, electrical principles, and EV operation/service.
- Hyundai’s first U.S. assembly plant is Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Alabama, LLC (HMMA) in Montgomery, Alabama.
- The new Bryan County, GA facility is larger and smarter than the Alabama plant and is completely oriented towards electrified cars, electric batteries, and components.
- The new MetaPlant will cooperate with the previous Montgomery, Alabama facility, which produces over 300,000 vehicles annually.
- This new facility will double Hyundai’s current vehicle output in the U.S.
- The Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) under the Biden administration influenced the decision to build the plant in the U.S.
- The IRA stipulates that EV vehicles must be domestically produced in the U.S. to qualify for federal incentives.
- Tax incentives from the act allow manufacturers to transfer savings to consumers, offering lower prices and winning competition.
- Producing cars locally in Georgia will help lower transportation costs and speed up inventory delivery to car dealers in the U.S.
- Local production ensures parts, batteries, and important electric car components become available much faster than if produced in Mexico or Asia.
