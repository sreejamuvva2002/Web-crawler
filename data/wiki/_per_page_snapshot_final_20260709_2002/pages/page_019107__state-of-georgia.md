# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-06

## Facts

- Georgia stands at the forefront of automotive industry evolution, capturing significant investment from leading entities within the electric vehicle (EV) sphere.
- Doowon Climate Control America pledges over $30 million toward the establishment of a new production facility in Metter, Candler County.
- The Doowon Climate Control America initiative is poised to create 200 employment opportunities.
- The project will strategically bolster supply chains for Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia (Kia Georgia) and Hyundai Motor Group.
- Governor Brian Kemp’s office champions the Doowon Climate Control America development as a pivotal stride in fortifying Georgia’s automotive sector.
- DAS Corp announced a $35 million investment into a Metter facility last September, engendering 300 jobs.
- Georgia’s deliberate focus on its comprehensive logistics network, replete with an extensive assembly of rail lines and highways, has been instrumental in attracting such ventures.
- Governor Kemp accentuated the logistics infrastructure’s role in propelling the state towards unprecedented economic milestones, facilitating seamless connectivity for companies to vital markets globally and within the U.S.
- From 2020 onwards, Georgia has emerged as a pivotal hub for mobility and battery supply chain investments, with figures surpassing $27.3 billion.
- These investments have generated upward of 32,000 new positions in the state.
- Hyundai Motor Group is constructing its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing plant in Georgia.
- Rivian is committing to establishing its most extensive manufacturing site in Georgia, projected to commence production in 2026.
- The Rivian facility will have an annual output capacity of 400,000 vehicles.
- Georgia’s strategic embrace of EV manufacturing and supply chain investments reflects a deliberate stride towards minimizing environmental footprints and championing eco-friendly transport solutions.
- Investments by Doowon Climate Control America and DAS Corp, alongside upcoming EV plants by Hyundai and Rivian, highlight Georgia’s dedication to innovation, environmental stewardship, and economic growth.
