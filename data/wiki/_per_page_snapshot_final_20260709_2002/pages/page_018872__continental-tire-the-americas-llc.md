# Continental Tire the Americas, LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2025-07-01

**Source:** [Continental Announces Leadership Transition in its Replacement Tires Business in the US | Continental Tire](https://continentaltire.com/learn/continental-announces-leadership-transition-its-replacement-tires-business-us)  ·  `page_018872`

## Facts

- Continental Tire the Americas, LLC announced a key leadership transition for its U.S. PLT replacement business on June 5, 2025, from Fort Mill, S.C.
- Bill Caldwell is retiring in June 2025 after more than two decades with the company and over 20 years of leadership at Continental Tire the Americas.
- Chris Charity will succeed Bill Caldwell as the leader of Continental’s U.S. PLT Replacement Business effective July 1, 2025.
- Bill Caldwell previously served as Senior Vice President of Sales and Marketing for the U.S. PLT replacement business.
- Chris Charity currently serves as Vice President of Sales for the U.S. PLT replacement business.
- Caldwell has led the U.S. PLT Replacement Market since 2009.
- Prior to leading the PLT Replacement Market, Caldwell led Continental’s OE Key Account Management team in Auburn Hills.
- Under Caldwell's guidance, Continental and General Tire brands experienced significant growth in the United States.
- Chris Charity joined Continental in April 2002 as a quality engineer before transitioning into sales leadership.
- Tansu Işık serves as Head of Continental’s Business Area Replacement Tires the Americas.
- Bill Caldwell stated, “I’m incredibly proud of what we’ve accomplished together over the years,” and expressed confidence in Chris Charity's leadership.
- Chris Charity said, “I’m grateful for the opportunity to step into the role and build on the strong foundation Bill has laid.”
- Tansu Işık stated, “Bill has made a lasting contribution to Continental’s success in the Americas,” and welcomed Charity by noting he brings “the right mix of experience, vision, and dedication.”
- Continental develops pioneering technologies and services for sustainable and connected mobility of people and their goods.
- In fiscal 2024, Continental's global Tires group sector generated sales of 13.9 billion euros.
- Continental's tire division employs more than 57,000 people worldwide across 20 production and 16 development sites.
- Continental Tire the Americas, LLC is based in Fort Mill, S.C., where it manufactures and distributes a complete premium line of passenger, light truck, and commercial tires for original equipment and replacement markets.
- Continental’s premium products are distributed through leading independent tire dealers, car dealers, online tire dealers, and mass retail companies across North America.
- Continental Tire supports multiple organizations including Alive at 25 (National Safety Council), BMW Car Club of America, BMW Performance Driving Centers, Major League Soccer, ROUSH Performance, Saleen Automotive, Toyota’s GR Cup, Universal Technical Institute, and the USF Pro Championships.
