# Dalton-Whitfield Joint Development Authority

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-10-31

**Source:** [Dalton | Whitfield County: Talent Hub - Georgia Trend Magazine](https://www.georgiatrend.com/2024/10/31/dalton-whitfield-county-talent-hub/)  ·  `page_020602`

## Facts

- Qcells is a clean-energy producer and manufacturer of solar cells that added a factory in Dalton in 2019.
- Qcells completed its Dalton factory expansion last year and created 510 additional clean energy jobs in the area.
- Qcells began constructing another facility in Cartersville last year, which is expected to be fully operational in 2025.
- Together, Qcells’ manufacturing operations in Georgia are projected to generate roughly 4,000 jobs in Bartow and Whitfield Counties.
- GEDIA Automotive Group, a German company, invested $85 million in a new manufacturing plant in the Carbondale Business Park.
- The GEDIA Automotive Group plant opened in 2022, adding 200 jobs.
- Carl Campbell is executive director of the Dalton-Whitfield Joint Development Authority and says his biggest challenge is figuring out how to make room for housing the growing workforce.
- Campbell notes that Dalton’s location near the interstate makes it attractive to businesses, and its technical skill development and career progression opportunities help retain talent.
- Campbell states that Dalton has multiple facilities with over 1 million square feet and is rich in resources for business growth.
- The Northwest Georgia College and Career Academy offers high school students a curriculum integrating core academics with career-technical education to build a local talent pipeline.
- Stephani Womack, chief talent officer for the Greater Dalton Chamber of Commerce, conducts bus tours with Campbell to expose nonresidents to career opportunities in advanced manufacturing and other sectors beyond flooring.
- Gov. Brian Kemp awarded a $1.5 million grant to Dalton from the Rural Workforce Housing Initiative earlier this year to help add 39 homes to Dalton’s market by funding street and stormwater drainage for the 3-acre South Hamilton Residential Infill Project.
- The city of Dalton pledged $622,300 toward infrastructure design and construction for the housing project, which was unanimously approved by the Dalton City Council in September.
- Homes on donated city land will range from 850 to 1,400 square feet with one to three bedrooms, priced between $125,000-$290,000 per state mandate to address lower-end and upper-middle income market needs.
- Believe Greater Dalton, a strategic plan led by the Greater Dalton Chamber of Commerce, focuses on expanding housing choice, neighborhood revitalization, increasing affordability, and continuing downtown development to attract workers.
- Co-chair Anna Adamson says the board uses resources to identify specific housing needs for the local workforce, including multigenerational families and single dwellers.
- A $100,000 grant from the Truist Charitable Fund helped Flooring Capital Development Corporation pilot sustainable homes furnished with structurally insulated panels to reduce construction timelines and utility costs.
- Dalton State College President John Fuchko III says the college prioritizes preparing students for the workforce through bachelor’s degrees in fields like engineering and accounting, and expects to integrate AI into all programs over time to produce AI-literate graduates for emerging industries leveraging AI.
- Roughly half of incoming Dalton State students are first-generation college students, prompting the college to offer peer mentor programs and the six-week Roadrunner Scholars Summer Bridge Program providing intense English and math training at no dorm cost.
- The college gives well over a quarter million dollars a year in private scholarships to students, with more than half receiving need-based aid and around nine in 10 receiving any sort of financial aid.
- Stephani Womack says Project Purpose was piloted last year as a two-week program connecting high school students with employers, exposing them to industries like advanced manufacturing while training them in communication, conflict management, and financial literacy.
- All 18 students who participated in Project Purpose this year were hired after taking industrial maintenance classes at Georgia Northwestern Technical College, receiving professional interview attire, and touring local employers.
- Whitfield County Administrator Robert Sivick says his participation in recent training qualifies the county to apply for $50,000 in federal funds to deliver reliable broadband internet coverage to rural areas, benefiting businesses that use cashless payment systems and school-aged children.
