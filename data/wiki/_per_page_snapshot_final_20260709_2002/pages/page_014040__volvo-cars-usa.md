# Volvo Cars USA

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Supply Chain Now Radio - Episode-167 - Supply Chain Now](https://supplychainnow.com/episode-167/)  ·  `page_014040`

## Facts

- Volvo Cars USA operates its first U.S. plant in Charleston, South Carolina.
- The project kicked off in 2015 after the company realized the need for a North American presence following its acquisition by Geely in 2010.
- Full scale production has been going for one year at the time of the interview.
- Around one hundred eighty two cars roll off the line every day.
- The first vehicle produced in Charleston is the S60 sedan.
- The second vehicle, referred to as the XY 90, will be produced in 2022 and 2023.
- Volvo depends heavily on the Port of Charleston for material imports from suppliers and exporting completed cars globally.
- The plant represents a one point one billion dollar economic input to the Charleston area.
- The impact from suppliers and others is over four billion dollars.
- Approximately fifteen hundred people currently work at the facility.
- With the launch of the second car, the company aims to have around 4000 employees.
- Sustainability initiatives include reducing the carbon footprint from suppliers and achieving full traceability for cobalt sourcing by the end of this year.
- Volvo is introducing 25 percent of plastics made from recycled material, targeting completion by 2025.
- The company is focusing on industry trends including electrification (fully electric vehicles and hybrids), sustainability, and autonomous drive.
- Volvo attended the AIAG SCAC Supply Chain and Quality Conference in North Charleston to identify new vendors and develop workforce partnerships with South Carolina.
- Kyle Hooper serves as manager of procurement and excellence at Volvo Cars USA, managing process management, sustainability, and digitalization for supplier collaboration.
