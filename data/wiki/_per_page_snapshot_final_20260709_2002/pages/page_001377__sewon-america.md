# Sewon America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Sewon America Will Create 740 New Jobs In Georgia - Automotive - Business Facilities Magazine](https://businessfacilities.com/sewon-america-will-create-740-new-jobs-in-georgia/)  ·  `page_001377`

## Facts

- Sewon America is a subsidiary of Korean-based Sewon Precision Industry Co.
- Sewon America will create 740 new jobs in Effingham County, Georgia.
- Sewon America will invest more than $300 million in a new manufacturing facility.
- The project is the largest known private investment in the City of Rincon to date.
- The project fulfills the commitment to create jobs and investment in all four Savannah Harbor Interstate-16 Joint Development Authority (JDA) counties.
- This JDA commitment results from Hyundai’s Metaplant location in Bryan County, which is the largest economic development project in state history.
- Suppliers for the Metaplant have now committed over $1.8 billion in investment, surpassing initial estimates by at least $800 million.
- Sewon America operates one of the largest automobile body parts manufacturing plants in North America.
- The company opened a manufacturing facility in LaGrange in 2008, which has since doubled in size.
- Following the completion of the Rincon facility, Sewon is expected to support 1,600 jobs in Georgia.
- Vice Chairman Kim stated that Sewon America was founded 15 years ago in west Georgia and expressed excitement about building another facility in east Georgia as one family in two locations.
- The growth is attributed to the Sewon team, Governor Kemp, the Georgia Department of Economic Development, and Effingham County.
- Sewon’s new facility will be located at the Grande View industrial park in Rincon.
- The new manufacturing location will produce electric vehicle (EV) body parts for original equipment manufacturers (OEMs), including Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- Operations are expected to begin in 2025.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp noted that his first international trip as governor to Korea included congratulating Chairman Kim on a recent expansion and discussing continued collaboration, which led to this announcement.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) worked with the Effingham County Industrial Development Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia EMC to support the project.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Sewon has integrated into Georgia’s automotive community through work with local colleges, Georgia Quick Start, and statewide initiatives like Georgia Made.
- The project won the 2022 Deal Of The Year: Bronze Award.
- Chairman of the Effingham County Industrial Development Authority (IDA) Troy Smith stated that Sewon selected Grande View industrial park for significant future growth investment.
- The IDA and Sewon’s investment will allow current and future generations to have fulfilling, well-paying careers without leaving Effingham County.
- The project was made possible with help from the City of Rincon and the Effingham County Board of Commissioners.
- Executive Director of the Georgia Ports Authority Griff Lynch stated that the announcement is terrific news for Effingham County and the State of Georgia, and that the Hyundai announcement continues to bring new economic opportunities to the Peach State.
- On October 25, 2022, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America officially broke ground in neighboring Bryan County on its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility.
- Since 2020, more than 35 EV-related projects have announced more than $21 billion in investment and 26,700 jobs in Georgia.
