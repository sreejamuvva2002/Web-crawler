# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-09

**Source:** [Explainer: What Hyundai companies are part of the Savannah-area car plant? | Georgia Public Broadcasting](https://www.gpb.org/news/2025/09/09/explainer-what-hyundai-companies-are-part-of-the-savannah-area-car-plant)  ·  `page_001206`

## Facts

- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp announced two years ago that South Korea’s Hyundai Motor Group was going to build an electric vehicle plant near Savannah.
- The deal promised nearly $8 billion in investment.
- The deal promised to bring more than 8,000 well-paying jobs for Georgians.
- A federal raid occurred last week at the Hyundai Metaplant and the EV battery facility, which is a joint venture between Hyundai and Korean company LG Energy Solution.
- Federal officials detained approximately 475 foreign workers, approximately 300 of whom were Korean, during an ongoing investigation into illegal labor practices.
- The Current GA has published numerous stories about misleading wage claims and workplace safety problems at the sprawling site.
- The plant sits on a 2,300-acre site where Hyundai has assembled a complete supply chain of companies.
- These supply chain companies are separate legal entities but all have a role in meeting the employment and wage targets that Georgia received in exchange for $2 billion in state tax credits and abatements agreed to between the Kemp administration and Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA).
- Prior to last week’s raid, HMGMA said it was not responsible for the actions of its suppliers and subcontractors that The Current’s reporting showed were allegedly in violation of federal workplace safety laws.
- On Friday, Hyundai announced that it was bringing a senior executive to supervise the firms amid the ongoing federal investigation at the battery plant.
- As of Monday, no arrests have been made, and it is still unclear who employed those detained.
- The search warrant authorizing the raid cited the battery plant as a target, along with five other companies that appear to have served as subcontractors for the plant.
- Operations at the Hyundai site are intertwined, meaning a worker in one company may have been recruited by a different company or subcontractor.
- To build EVs, Hyundai has organized its supply chain at the site, including Hyundai Mobis (power electric systems), Ecoplastic Corporation (plastic components), Seoyon E-HWA (door trim, seats, bumpers), and Sewon America (body components).
- Other companies at the site include Hanon Systems, PHA, and Daechang Seat Corp. USA.
- Hyundai’s own affiliates present at the site include Hyundai Glovis, Hyundai Steel, and Hyundai Transys.
- The joint venture battery factory with LG Energy Solution is also located at the site.
- Hyundai, the battery plant, and five affiliated Hyundai suppliers must together invest $5.545 billion and hire at least 8,500 workers by 2031 (making an average of $58,105) to receive the tax credits and tax deferments offered by the state.
- As of mid-March, Hyundai told The Current that approximately 1,200 people were employed across the site, not counting Koreans.
