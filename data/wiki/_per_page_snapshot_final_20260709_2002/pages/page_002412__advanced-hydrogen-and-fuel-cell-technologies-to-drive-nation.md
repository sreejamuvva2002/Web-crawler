# Advanced Hydrogen and Fuel Cell Technologies to Drive National Decarbonization

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [DOE: CMEI eXCHANGE: Funding Opportunities](https://eere-exchange.energy.gov/Default.aspx?Search=3438&SearchType=)  ·  `page_002412`

## Facts

- The Office of Energy Efficiency and Renewable Energy (EERE) intends to issue, on behalf of the Hydrogen and Fuel Cell Technologies Office (HFTO), a Notice of Funding Opportunity (NOFO).
- The NOFO is entitled “Advanced Hydrogen and Fuel Cell Technologies to Drive National Decarbonization.”
- Clean hydrogen and fuel cell technologies are important elements for hard-to-decarbonize sectors such as heavy-duty transportation and industrial applications.
- These technologies enable long duration energy storage for a clean electric grid.
- Advancing clean hydrogen and fuel cell technologies supports the Biden-Harris Administration’s goals of achieving carbon pollution-free electricity by 2035.
- The administration's goal includes net-zero emissions economy-wide by no later than 2050.
- The NOFO supports the U.S. National Clean Hydrogen Strategy and Roadmap vision of affordable clean hydrogen for a net-zero carbon future.
- The roadmap envisions a sustainable, resilient, and equitable economy.
- The NOFO will target research, development, and demonstration (RD&D) topics critical to scaling hydrogen infrastructure.
- It targets enabling increased adoption of clean hydrogen across sectors, particularly in heavy-duty vehicles and other heavy-duty transportation applications.
- Increased adoption of hydrogen technologies supports the Department of Energy’s Regional Clean Hydrogen Hubs (H2Hubs) Program.
- The H2Hubs Program is an $8 billion federal investment to create networks of hydrogen producers, consumers, and local connective infrastructure.
- The program aims to accelerate the use of hydrogen as a clean energy carrier and a carbon-free input to chemical and industrial processes.
- Activities funded under this NOFO will align with the H2@Scale Initiative.
- The H2@Scale Initiative aims to advance affordable hydrogen production, transport, storage, and utilization.
- The initiative targets enabling decarbonization and revenue opportunities across multiple sectors.
- The NOFO’s objectives support DOE’s Hydrogen Shot goal.
- The Hydrogen Shot goal targets affordable clean hydrogen production at $1/kg within a decade.
- Projects funded under this NOFO will be expected to advance diversity, equity, inclusion, and accessibility (DEIA).
- Projects must contribute to the Justice40 Initiative and other considerations linked with energy and/or environmental justice.
- Projects must invest in America’s workforce.
- Applications must include an RDD&D Community Benefits Plan that addresses DEIA, Justice40, and workforce investment objectives.
- EERE plans to issue the NOFO before or in October 2024 via the EERE eXCHANGE website.
- Applicants wishing to receive official notifications and information regarding this NOFO should register in EERE eXCHANGE.
- When the FOA is released, applications will be accepted only through EERE eXCHANGE.
- Interested parties can navigate to TPL-0000058 in EERE Exchange to access the Teaming Partner List for this solicitation.
- This notice of intent (NOI) informs interested parties that EERE intends to issue the NOFO in the near term.
- All information contained in this NOI is subject to change.
