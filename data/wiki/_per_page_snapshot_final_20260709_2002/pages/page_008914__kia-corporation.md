## Facts

- Kia maintains manufacturing facilities in eight countries along with research centres in South Korea, the United States, Japan, and Germany.
- In 2022, Kia announced an additional $480 million to open at least five new production facilities while $67 million of the existing investment will be used to improve commuting conditions.
- Kia Manufacturing Georgia (KMMG), Kia's plant in West Point, Georgia, is capable of producing 350,000 cars annually for the North American and global markets.
- It was announced in 2006 and went online in 2010, with the state of Georgia providing significant incentives.
- At an initial cost of $1 billion (US) the facility comprises 2.2 million-square-feet on more than 2,200 acres (8.9km2) of land near Interstate 85.
- In addition to four main areas (stamping, welding, paint, and assembly), the facility also includes a transmission shop, module shop, and a two-mile (3km) test track.
- The assembly area features more than a half mile of height-adjustable conveyors and wood flooring.
- Kia Georgia is home of the Telluride SUV, Sorento SUV, Sportage SUV, all-electric Kia EV6 SUV, and the all-electric EV9 SUV.
- This advanced manufacturing facility represents a $2.8 billion investment on a 2,200-acre site, operating 24 hours a day.
- Kia's Georgia plant recorded cumulative production of 1 million units in 2013 and exceeded 3 million units in September 2019.
- On December 31, 2021, President Jangsu Shin retired and a new CEO, Stuart Countess, was appointed effective January 2022.
- On May 20, 2022, Kia's parent company Hyundai announced plans to construct a new electric vehicle plant near Savannah, Georgia.
- Construction of the sprawling site took two years, with production starting in late 2024, and the site is now dubbed the Hyundai Metaplant.
