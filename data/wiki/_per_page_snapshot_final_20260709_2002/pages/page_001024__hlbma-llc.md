# HLBMA LLC

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-11-21

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor and LG Energy Solution name Georgia battery joint venture HLBMA LLC - CHOSUNBIZ](https://biz.chosun.com/en/en-industry/2025/11/21/L3A4YJTZM5BNRIY2L6GIN62WNQ/)  ·  `page_001024`

## Facts

- LG Energy Solution announced on Nov. 21 that the name of the joint venture for building the Georgia joint plant with Hyundai Motor is "HLBMA LLC."
- Until now, the joint venture had been called "LGES-HMG Battery JV."
- The two companies signed a contract in 2023 regarding the establishment of a battery joint venture in North America to build the "HL-GA Battery Company," a joint battery plant in Georgia.
- Hyundai Motor Group's U.S. unit "HMG Global LLC" and LG Energy Solution's Michigan branch will jointly invest $4.3 billion (about 6.3275 trillion won).
- Each partner will hold 50% equity in the joint venture.
- The joint plant is being built to a scale capable of mass-producing about 30 gigawatt-hours (GWh) annually.
- The annual capacity is enough for battery cells for about 300,000 electric vehicles.
- The joint plant sits at one end of the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) site.
- The HMGMA site spans about 3 million pyeong in Ellabell, Georgia.
- EV battery cells produced at the joint plant will be moved to Hyundai Mobis within the HMGMA site.
- Hyundai Mobis will assemble the cells into battery packs.
- The assembled battery packs will be supplied in full to electric vehicles produced at Hyundai Motor's Alabama plant.
- The battery packs will also be supplied to Kia's Georgia plant and other U.S. plants of Hyundai Motor Group.
- Initially, the two companies aimed to start production at the end of 2025.
- The plant construction plan is facing setbacks due to a Sept. 4 sweep by U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) and Homeland Security Investigations (HSI) for undocumented immigrants at the construction site.
- During the Sept. 4 sweep, U.S. immigration authorities arrested a total of 475 people and detained them for seven days.
- Of the arrested personnel, 317 were from Korea.
- The Korean detainees included 47 from LG Energy Solution, 210 employees of LG Energy Solution partners, and 60 from Hyundai Engineering.
- The remainder of the arrested personnel were foreign nationals.
