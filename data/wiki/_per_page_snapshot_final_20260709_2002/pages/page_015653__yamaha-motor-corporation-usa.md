# Yamaha Motor Corporation, USA

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Yamaha Funds Six All Kids Bike Programs in Georgia and California](https://sg.finance.yahoo.com/news/yamaha-funds-six-kids-bike-163500283.html)  ·  `page_015653`

## Facts

- Yamaha Motor Corp., USA announced $30,000 in funding to All Kids Bike for six programs teaching kids in Georgia and California public schools' kindergarten PE classes how to ride bikes.
- The grant is provided through the Yamaha Outdoor Access Initiative (OAI).
- Yamaha employees from Marietta, Georgia, and Cypress, California corporate offices, plus YMMC personnel in Newnan, Georgia, volunteered to build and deliver bikes and helmets to local schools.
- Steve Nessl, Yamaha's Motorsports Marketing Manager, stated that giving employees the opportunity to contribute time is meaningful and valuable.
- The All Kids Bike program is now in 350 schools across 45 states, with another 50 schools in training to have the program by year-end.
- Full program deliveries included 24 balance bikes, pedal conversion kits, helmets, and a teacher's bike to Elm Street Elementary in Newnan, Georgia, A.L. Burruss Elementary School in Marietta, Georgia, and Juliet Morris Elementary School in Cypress, California.
- Three additional schools in Newnan, Marietta, and Cypress are guaranteed funding through the Yamaha grant for this year.
- Bob Brown, Vice President of Finance and Operations Support at YMMC, highlighted that these are the first schools in Georgia to receive the All Kids Bike program.
- Ryan McFarland, founder of All Kids Bike and CEO of Strider Sports International, Inc., stated he grew up on Yamaha and helped deliver bikes to Morris Elementary in Cypress.
- YMMC facility in Newnan, Georgia, was founded in 1986 and produces golf cars, personal watercraft, Side-by-Side vehicles, and all-terrain vehicles (ATVs).
- The YMMC factory holds ISO9001 Quality Management System and ISO14001 Environmental Management System certifications.
- YMUS operates corporate offices in California and two in Georgia, with facilities in Wisconsin and Alabama, and factory operations in Tennessee and Georgia.
- Yamaha OAI has contributed more than $4.5 million to nearly 400 projects nationwide over more than 12 years.
