# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-04-22

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor to power US EV plant with renewable energy - The Korea Herald](https://www.koreaherald.com/article/3376415)  ·  `page_009550`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group inked a 147-megawatt power purchase agreement with Matrix Renewables, a Spanish renewable energy company, to power its electric vehicle plant currently under construction in Georgia.
- The deal marks the largest single power purchase agreement signed by a Korean company in the US.
- Four Hyundai subsidiaries joined the contract: Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (the EV plant), Hyundai Mobis North America Electric, Hyundai Steel Georgia, and Hyundai Transys Georgia Powertrain.
- The deal establishes a renewable energy system for both the auto plant and its supply chains.
- Hyundai Motor Group stated the deal signifies a pan-conglomerate step to secure renewable energy.
- Overseas offices pushed for a joint purchase agreement instead of individual deals, saving on renewable energy purchasing costs.
- North American offices of Hyundai Mobis, Hyundai Steel, and Hyundai Transys will utilize the secured solar power to supply automation parts, EV steel sheets, and seats to HMGMA.
- The PPA delivers power from a new solar power plant rather than existing renewable facilities, resulting in better impacts on reducing global carbon emissions.
- The solar power supply is expected to decrease carbon emissions by 140,000 tons per year.
- This emission reduction equals absorbing the carbon emissions produced by about 84,000 mid-sized sedans annually.
- Matrix Renewables is constructing a 210-megawatt solar power plant named Stillhouse Solar in Bell County, Texas.
- The Stillhouse Solar plant aims to start operating in October 2025.
- Hyundai Motor has secured about 70 percent of the generation capacity.
- Hyundai Motor will obtain about 378 gigawatt-hours per year from the deal.
- The secured power is enough to supply approximately 100,000 households for a year.
- A Hyundai Motor Group official stated they will conduct investments and deals to transition all US operations to renewable energy starting with this PPA.
- The company will continue pushing for cooperation between its companies to secure renewable energy efficiently.
- Hyundai Motor's EV-dedicated plant in Georgia's Bryan County is expected to begin operations in October 2024.
- The plant's start date is three months ahead of the initial planned timeline.
