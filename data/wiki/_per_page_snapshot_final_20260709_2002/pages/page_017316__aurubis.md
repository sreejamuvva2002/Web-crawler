# Aurubis

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2025-04-15

**Source:** [Aurubis Starts Production at US Copper Recycling Plant - World-Energy](https://www.world-energy.org/article/53930.html)  ·  `page_017316`

## Facts

- Aurubis has started production at its new U.S. copper recycling plant in Richmond.
- The facility represents an investment of approximately $800 million.
- The plant will process up to 180,000 metric tons of complex waste material annually.
- Input materials include printed circuit boards, copper cables, and other metal-bearing products.
- The plant will produce 70,000 tons of high-grade blister copper with a purity of 98-99%.
- Production outputs also include nickel, tin, and other precious metals.
- An Aurubis spokesperson stated the company intends to sell the copper primarily to U.S. customers.
- The initiative aims to meet the growing demand for strategic metals such as copper in the U.S. and elsewhere.
- The U.S. currently has an annual copper demand of around 1.8 million tons.
- Approximately half of the U.S. copper demand is imported.
- The Richmond plant aims to address this import gap by providing a faster method to reduce imports.
- Blister copper produced at the Richmond facility can be further processed into high-purity copper and copper products at Aurubis' smelting network in Europe.
- Integration with European operations allows for efficient distribution and added value to the materials produced.
- Aurubis CEO Toralf Haag stated the company may consider additional projects in the United States.
- Haag cited the high availability of recycling materials and favorable local conditions as reasons for considering further U.S. expansion.
- Haag noted that the American market offers Aurubis appealing prospects that will continue to be considered closely in the future.
- The new plant is a significant step for Aurubis to strengthen its presence in the U.S. market.
