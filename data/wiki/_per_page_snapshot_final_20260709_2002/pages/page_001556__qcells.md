# Qcells

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2026-03-06

**Source:** [US Customs delays force solar giant Qcells to furlough 1,000 workers [update] | Electrek](https://electrek.co/2026/03/06/us-customs-delays-force-solar-giant-qcells-to-furlough-1000-workers/)  ·  `page_001556`

## Facts

- In early November 2025, solar panel giant Qcells announced it was temporarily furloughing 1,000 US workers (25% of its workforce) and reducing pay and shifts at its Georgia factories due to supply chain delays caused by US Customs.
- On March 6, 2026, Qcells announced that its solar panel output is back to normal production.
- Qcells spokesperson Marta Stoepker stated on March 6, 2026, that the company aims to build a complete American solar supply chain and will soon be back on track delivering American-made energy with its Georgia team.
- Qcells expects its Cartersville, Georgia, factory to manufacture 3.3 GW of ingots, wafers, and cells by the end of 2026.
- Solar module production at Cartersville’s 3.3 GW facility began in April 2025.
- Qcells' Dalton, Georgia, factory came online in 2019 and has an output capacity of 5.1 GW.
- By the end of 2026, the combined Cartersville and Dalton factories will produce 8.4 GW of solar panels and key components annually with a workforce of nearly 4,000 people.
- On November 7, 2025, Qcells reported that supply chain delays hindered its ability to import components, preventing its two Georgia factories from operating at full capacity for several months.
- Furloughed workers will retain full benefits and not be laid off, but Qcells will temporarily stop using staffing agency employees in Georgia.
- Qcells scaled back production while shipments into the US were delayed in the customs clearance process as it introduced new supply chains for its Georgia facilities.
- As supply chain operations normalized, Qcells informed employees that HR actions are necessary to improve operational efficiency until production capacity returns to normal.
- Spokesperson Stoepker expects furloughed workers to return “in the coming weeks and months.”
- In January 2023, Seoul-headquartered Qcells announced a $2.5 billion investment to build a solar supply chain in Georgia, marking the largest-ever investment in US clean energy manufacturing at the time.
- The January 2023 investment plan included expanding the Dalton solar factory and constructing a fully integrated solar supply chain factory in Cartersville to manufacture ingots, wafers, cells, and finished panels.
- The Georgia factories' total output of 8.4 GW annually is equivalent to nearly 46,000 panels per day.
- The annual solar production capacity will be enough to power approximately 1.3 million homes each year.
