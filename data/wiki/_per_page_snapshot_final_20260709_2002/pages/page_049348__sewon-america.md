# Sewon America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-03-09

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp: Sewon America Announces 740 New Jobs in Effingham County - Effingham County GA](https://effinghamcountyga.com/gov-kemp-sewon-america-announces-740-new-jobs-in-effingham-county/)  ·  `page_049348`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Sewon America, a subsidiary of Korean-based Sewon Precision Industry Co., will create 740 new jobs in Rincon.
- Sewon America will invest more than $300 million in a new Effingham County manufacturing facility.
- This project is the largest known private investment in the City of Rincon to-date.
- The announcement fulfills the commitment to create jobs and investment in all four Savannah Harbor Interstate-16 Joint Development Authority (JDA) counties as a result of Hyundai’s Metaplant location in Bryan County.
- Suppliers for the Metaplant have now committed over $1.8 billion in investment, surpassing initial estimates by at least $800 million.
- Governor Brian Kemp's first international trip as governor was to Korea, where he congratulated Chairman Kim on the company’s expansion and discussed future collaboration.
- Speaker of the House Jon Burns, who represents part of Effingham County, played a role in securing the project and engaging Sewon America executives.
- Lt. Governor Burt Jones commended the announcement, noting it builds on Georgia’s commitment to remain the No. 1 state in which to do business while attracting a robust workforce.
- Sewon America operates one of the largest automobile body parts manufacturing plants in North America.
- The company opened a manufacturing facility in LaGrange in 2008, which has since doubled in size.
- Following the completion of the Rincon facility, Sewon is expected to support 1,600 jobs in Georgia.
- Vice Chairman Kim stated that Sewon America was founded 15 years ago in west Georgia and expressed excitement about building another facility in east Georgia as one family in two locations.
- Sewon’s new facility will be located at the Grande View industrial park in Rincon.
- The new manufacturing location will produce electric vehicle (EV) body parts for original equipment manufacturers (OEMs), including Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- Operations at the new facility are expected to begin in 2025.
- Rincon Mayor Ken Lee welcomed Sewon America, calling it the largest employment and private investment project in the city’s history.
- Chairman of the Effingham County Industrial Development Authority (IDA) Troy Smith stated that the IDA selected Grande View industrial park as the best location for Sewon’s significant investment.
- Executive Director of the Georgia Ports Authority Griff Lynch called the announcement terrific news for Effingham County and the State of Georgia.
- Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy Alyce Thornhill represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on the project in partnership with the Effingham County IDA, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia EMC.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson noted that Sewon has integrated into Georgia’s automotive community through working with local colleges, Georgia Quick Start, and statewide initiatives like Georgia Made.
- On October 25, 2022, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America officially broke ground in neighboring Bryan County on its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility.
- Since 2020, more than 35 EV-related projects have announced more than $21 billion in investment and 26,700 jobs in Georgia.
- Sewon America was established in 2008 in LaGrange as a Georgia company.
- Sewon primarily operates in the Motor Vehicles and Car Bodies industry within the Transportation Equipment sector, producing stamped body components.
