# Advanced Technology Services

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** workforce_training  ·  **Published:** 2014-08-01

**Source:** [This firm helps others by growing its own workforce](https://www.cnbc.com/2014/08/01/this-firm-helps-others-by-growing-its-own-workforce.html)  ·  `page_051610`

## Facts

- At a factory 10 minutes from the famed Augusta National Golf Club in Georgia, brightly colored golf carts are being assembled, inspected and prepped for shipment.
- E-Z-GO carts come in vivid reds, blues, greens, classic whites and a subtle gold color as part of Textron Specialized Vehicles' expanding product line.
- The expansion was made possible in part by the firm's decision to cut its maintenance staff and hire Advanced Technology Services (ATS) to handle that work instead.
- Over the last five years, Textron Specialized Vehicles launched 40 new vehicles to market and expanded its engineering workforce by 138 percent, according to plant manager Kerri Thompson.
- In July of 2003, the Textron unit decided it was not very good at doing internal maintenance required in its Augusta, Georgia plant and hired ATS to maintain and repair machines that build E-Z-GO golf carts, Cushman industrial carts, and Bad Boy Buggies all-terrain vehicles.
- The production facility is 750,000 square feet.
- Brian Rabe, ATS's vice president of heavy equipment industry and international operations, stated that demand for their product has been accelerating over the last five years.
- Spun off from Caterpillar in 1985, ATS is looking to add 500 jobs this year to its 3,000-person workforce.
- Finding workers with basic mechanical, electrical, hydraulic, and computer skills can prove challenging for ATS.
- ATS developed an internal recruiting team that relies on social media, job postings, and current employees to advertise available jobs while monitoring plants being shut down.
- An ATS technician will earn between $21 to $51 an hour with full benefits.
- Due to high demand for skilled workers, ATS pays a premium in areas with lots of manufacturing or remote locations where attracting workers is difficult.
- Over the last five years, pay has increased in these regions by 10 percent to 20 percent.
- Nationally, ATS said wages have been flat because of labor slack in other parts of the country.
- Jonathan Register, a 48-year-old Georgia native hired by ATS after it took over maintenance at his former employer Georgia Iron Works, noted he gets more support and training working for a firm where maintenance is one of many services rather than the only service.
- Peoria, IL-based ATS assesses potential employees' skills and provides additional training to fill gaps before sending them to customer factories.
- Micah Statler, head of training programs for ATS, stated they work closely with clients to understand their equipment and processes and tailor training to meet specific needs.
- Training needs are met by ATS's 300 mobile training simulators that are shipped to clients' factories for in-house training.
- Today, ATS supports over 100 factories in the U.S., the U.K., Mexico, and Canada for clients including Dean Foods, Georgia Pacific, and Eaton Corporation.
- ATS technicians work in teams of six to 60 and typically start at a client's factory under a three-year contract, with fees depending on the type of jobs taken over.
- Brian Rabe noted that reducing machine downtime from 5 percent to 1 percent allows customers to make product and profits.
