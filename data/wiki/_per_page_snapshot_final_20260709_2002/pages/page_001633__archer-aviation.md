# Archer Aviation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-07-31

**Source:** [Archer Aviation nears completion of eVTOL mega-facility](https://www.aerotime.aero/articles/archer-aviation-covington-facility-evtol)  ·  `page_001633`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation is nearing completion of its brand-new electric vehicle takeoff and landing (eVTOL) manufacturing facility in Covington, Georgia.
- Once up and running, Archer Aviation plans to build up to 650 Midnight eVTOL aircraft each year at the facility in partnership with long term collaborators Stellantis.
- In an update on July 31, 2024, Archer said work on the first build phase was completed earlier this year.
- Phase two of construction, which includes erection of the exterior walls and roof, is nearing its end as of July 31, 2024.
- The third phase of construction will focus on the building’s interior.
- The final phase involves installing equipment and power on the facility.
- All remaining construction phases should be completed in the coming months, with completion targeted by the end of 2024.
- The 400,000 square foot complex will become one of the largest manufacturing facilities by volume in the aviation industry.
- Archer is continuing to carry out test flight of its Midnight eVTOL aircraft, having already completed 233 flight tests this year.
- More than 70 flights have been conducted since the Midnight's transition flight.
- Each flight produces critical data that teams use to evaluate aircraft loads, vibrations, performance and handling qualities.
- Archer aims to carry out over 400 test flights by the end of 2024.
- Adam Goldstein, CEO and founder of Archer, stated that thanks to the hard work of the Archer and Stellantis teams, they are tracking towards completing the manufacturing facility by the end of the year.
- Once complete, Archer will focus on ramping production up to 650 aircraft a year, with the goal of being the first in the eVTOL industry to achieve scaled manufacturing.
