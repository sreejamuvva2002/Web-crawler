# Archer Aviation

## Facts

- Final assembly is now complete on Archer’s first Midnight aircraft.
- Archer is preparing for its planned first flight this summer.
- The Midnight aircraft has recently garnered significant attention from the U.S. Department of Defense given its payload capabilities.
- This Midnight aircraft will enable Archer to perform critical “company testing” to accelerate and reduce risk on its certification program with the Federal Aviation Administration (FAA).
- The company plans to begin “for credit” certification testing early next year with piloted Midnight aircraft.
- Archer’s strategy allows it to fly many of the same test points needed during piloted “for credit” flight testing to validate the aircraft before FAA witnesses and participates in the testing.
- Component manufacturing is already underway for Archer’s conforming Midnight aircraft.
- Archer is targeting completion of final assembly of its initial conforming Midnight aircraft in Q4 2023.
- Archer is targeting beginning piloted flight test operations in early 2024.
- Adam Goldstein, Founder and CEO of Archer, announced the completion of final assembly and stated the aircraft will accelerate and reduce risk on the certification program.
- The goal is to enter into service in 2025.
- Key strategic partners advancing aircraft development and commercial operations include Stellantis and United Airlines.
- The company has established a significant lead over industry peers on manufacturing and commercial operations fronts with the build out of its high-volume manufacturing facility.
- Archer’s Midnight aircraft has a range of up to 100 miles.
- The aircraft is designed to perform rapid back-to-back flights with minimal charge time in between.
- Archer aims to transform inter-city travel by replacing 60-90 minute commutes by car that can take over an hour in traffic with ~10-20 minute electric air taxi flights.
- The electric air taxi flights are described as safe, sustainable, low noise and cost competitive with ground transportation.
- Archer is designing and developing electric vertical takeoff and landing aircraft for use in urban air mobility networks.
