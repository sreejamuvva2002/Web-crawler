# Daechang Seat Savannah Corp.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-10

**Source:** [Automotive supplier Daechang Seat Savannah Corp. to create 500 jobs in Chatham County - SEDA](https://seda.org/2023/10/automotive-supplier-daechang-seat-corp-usa-to-create-500-jobs-in-chatham-county/)  ·  `page_018887`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Daechang Seat Savannah Corp., a supplier specializing in automotive seat frames, will invest $72.5 million in the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center, in close proximity to Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA), creating over 500 jobs.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated that suppliers for the Hyundai Metaplant project alone reach at least nine counties and expressed excitement about adding Daechang Seat Savannah Corp. to the growing manufacturing base centered around Chatham County and the Savannah region.
- Daechang Seat Corp. (DSC) was established in 1979 in Kyungju, South Korea, and has become one of the largest seat frame producers in the world.
- Globally, DSC employs more than 5,000 people across seven countries.
- The company’s production process includes fully automatic lines with arc welding and spot-welding machines.
- Jinsuk Lee, CEO of Daechang Seat Savannah Corp., expressed appreciation to the State of Georgia, local communities, and the City of Savannah for their support.
- Jinsuk Lee stated that DSC takes great pride in creating improved opportunities and employment prospects within Chatham County and the Savannah area, and is honored to maintain its partnership with the City of Savannah.
- Jinsuk Lee noted that DSC’s primary objective is to drive growth through facility expansions to transform into a global entity.
- Jinsuk Lee confirmed that DSC currently holds a prominent partnership with Hyundai Transys and Hyundai Mobis at HMGMA and hopes to collaborate with more projects in the future.
- DSC’s new facility will be located at the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center.
- The company will hire for assembly operators, equipment technicians, mechanical engineers, and electrical engineers through local job fairs and website postings.
- Griff Lynch, Georgia Ports Authority President and CEO, noted that DSC is located only minutes from the Port of Savannah to take advantage of Georgia’s world-class connectivity.
- Griff Lynch stated that the Georgia Ports Authority is focused on providing the best global gateway for U.S. supply chains and welcomed DSC as the latest job creator in Georgia’s automaking sector.
- Trip Tollison, Savannah Economic Development Authority President and CEO, announced that Daechang Seat Savannah Corp. will be the third Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America supplier to locate in the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center, bringing 500 new jobs and $72.5 million in capital investment to Chatham County.
- Trip Tollison described Daechang Seat Savannah Corp. as a well-established global company and expressed excitement for them to grow and become part of the community.
- Alyce Thornhill, Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy, represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project in partnership with the Savannah Economic Development Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that countless partners are helping transform Georgia into a center of the automotive and mobility industry and welcomed DSC to Savannah.
- Pat Wilson highlighted that Savannah is an established global gateway and Chatham County has leveraged it to create a bustling community for living, working, and enjoying.
- As the market transitions to electric vehicles, Georgia has pursued the entire supply chain, creating more than $25 billion in investments and 30,000 jobs since 2018.
- In fiscal year 2023, job creation in the automotive industry increased by 324 percent compared to FY21.
- Suppliers attracted by Georgia’s mobility original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) resulted in over $2 billion in investment across the state in FY23.
- Daechang Seat Co. (DSC) has recently ventured into the electric vehicle sector and continues to expand its scope beyond automotive seat frames.
- DSC anticipates greater opportunities for growth with the completion of new manufacturing facilities, which will provide larger installation spaces.
