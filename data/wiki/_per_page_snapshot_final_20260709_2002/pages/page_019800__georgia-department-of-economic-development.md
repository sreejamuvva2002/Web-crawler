# Georgia Department of Economic Development

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2020-08-06

**Source:** [GA: July saw $574 Million in new investments, 3,629 jobs](https://valdostatoday.com/business-2/2020/08/ga-july-saw-574-million-in-new-investments-3629-jobs/)  ·  `page_019800`

## Facts

- On August 6, 2020, Governor Brian P. Kemp and the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) announced a strong start to the first month of fiscal year 2021 with new investment projects in Georgia.
- In July, Georgia announced roughly $574 million in new investments and 3,629 new jobs created in nearly every region of the state.
- Governor Kemp stated that jobs at every skill level were created across advanced manufacturing to software development in nearly every region of the state during July.
- Governor Kemp thanked state and local economic development partners and companies establishing or expanding operations in Georgia during an unprecedented economic climate.
- Existing companies in Georgia prior to July, including Frito-Lay, ThredUP, and Airman USA, created roughly 65% of the new jobs and represented 75% of the investments as they expanded operations.
- German automotive parts manufacturer GEDIA was one of nine companies deciding to establish operations in Georgia for the first time.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson was traveling in Germany to meet with government officials, tourism, and trade partners when the announcement was made.
- Commissioner Wilson's trip to Germany was supported by Georgia’s government partners in the Free State of Bavaria, a strategic partner region.
- Commissioner Wilson met at GEDIA headquarters and visited other companies in the final stages of deciding to enter the U.S. market.
- The investment data follows Atlanta being named the No. 1 Tech Hub in the United States by Business Facilities Magazine.
- GDEcD announced that female-founded gaming software company Amebous Labs, founded by Georgia Tech graduate Annie Eaton, will create more new jobs opening a virtual reality studio in Atlanta.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that July's numbers provide hope and opportunity to counter economic uncertainties through jobs and investment.
- Commissioner Wilson noted that Georgia remains the best state in the nation to do business for seven consecutive years despite fighting the spread of COVID-19.
- The state announced that residential affordable-housing brand Common is investing $7 million to establish its second headquarters in Atlanta, delivering 274 career opportunities in architecture, interior design, software engineering, operations, and more.
