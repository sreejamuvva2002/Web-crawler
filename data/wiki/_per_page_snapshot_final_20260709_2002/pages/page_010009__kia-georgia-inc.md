# Kia Georgia, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-02-24

**Source:** [Kia Georgia celebrates trio of major milestones, including Start of Production for 2027 Kia Telluride | lifestyle.ribbon.co](https://lifestyle.ribbon.co/story/513394/kia-georgia-celebrates-trio-of-major-milestones-including-start-of-production-for-2027-kia-telluride/)  ·  `page_010009`

## Facts

- On Feb. 24, 2026, Kia Georgia celebrated the start of production for the all-new 2027 Kia Telluride at its West Point facility.
- The milestone event marked the production of the state of Georgia’s first-ever hybrid electric vehicle (HEV).
- The event also commemorated the plant’s 5 millionth vehicle produced.
- A ceremony for the milestones was attended by Georgia Governor Brian P. Kemp, team members, and state/community leaders.
- Sean Yoon, President and CEO of Kia North America and Kia America, stated that Kia Georgia is the only plant in the world that builds the Telluride.
- Yoon noted that reaching five million vehicles and introducing Kia’s first US-assembled HEV reflects Kia's commitment to building the future of mobility in Georgia.
- Kia Georgia began production in 2009, with mass production officially starting on Nov. 16, 2009.
- Since opening in 2009, Kia Georgia has grown into one of the region’s largest employers and a cornerstone of automotive manufacturing in the Southeast.
- The Telluride was introduced in 2019, is exclusively made in Georgia, and has become one of the most successful vehicles in Kia’s lineup.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp praised the proven partnership approach between Kia Georgia, the local community, and the state for creating long-lasting relationships.
- Stuart Countess, President and CEO of Kia Georgia, highlighted that the plant's ability to produce world-class gasoline-powered vehicles, EVs, and HEVs demonstrates its versatility and talent.
- Kia Georgia, Inc. is the first manufacturing site in North America for Kia Corporation, which is based in Seoul, Korea.
- The West Point facility sits on 2,200 acres and has an annual production capacity of 350,000 units.
- The plant currently manufactures the Telluride, Sorento SUV (excluding HEV and PHEV models), Sportage SUV (excluding HEV and PHEV models), and the all-electric EV9 and EV6.
