# JBM Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [JBM Grp acquires 60% stake in $700m German auto co Linde Wiemann - Times of India](https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/business/india-business/jbm-grp-acquires-60-stake-in-700m-german-auto-co-linde-wiemann/articleshow/68159725.cms)  ·  `page_057217`

## Facts

- JBM Group, a $1.5 billion auto component major, acquired around 60% stake in Germany-based Linde-Wiemann for an undisclosed sum.
- The acquisition represents JBM Group's biggest deal to date.
- The transaction was executed through JBM’s privately held group company Neel Metal Products and financed by ICICI Bank.
- Linde-Wiemann is a privately-held German component company valued at $700 million.
- Linde-Wiemann operates 17 plants in 8 countries and has 2,300 employees.
- The deal will increase JBM Group's combined turnover to $2.2 billion.
- The acquisition will provide JBM with critical global customers and future technology in lightweighting, aluminium usage, and electric vehicle and battery components.
- JBM Auto stock price rose 3.5% on the BSE on Monday to close at Rs 272.3.
- Nishant Arya is the executive director of JBM Group.
- Arya stated this is the first acquisition of this size for the company and that the acquired technology is critical.
- Although majority of the deal is financed through debt, JBM maintains a strong cash position with debt levels well below bank requirements.
- JBM has been clocking around 12% EBITDA margin, while Linde-Wiemann is strong in multiple technologies relevant to OEMs launching global platforms.
- The acquisition will earn JBM new products, global customers, and new technologies.
- JBM does not intend to replace Linde-Wiemann's current management, though a future merger remains possible.
- Neel Metal is the largest company in the JBM group and has the financial ability to take on this transaction without burdening listed entities.
- Linde-Wiemann is headquartered in Dillenburg, Germany.
- Linde-Wiemann specializes in hot forming, tubular assemblies, and usage of alternate materials such as aluminium resulting in lightweighting in auto systems.
