# Hanwha Q CELLS USA Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2026-04-11

**Source:** [Q CELLS Solar Panels Review 2026: Quality at Mid-Range Prices | Green Energy Calculators](https://greenenergycalc.com/guides/q-cells-solar-panels-review-2026/)  ·  `page_000912`

## Facts

- Q CELLS shipped roughly 20 gigawatts of solar panels globally in 2024, making it one of the ten largest module manufacturers in the world.
- The brand is most commonly quoted by US installers looking to split the difference between budget Chinese imports and premium European glass.
- Q CELLS was founded in 1999 in Bitterfeld-Wolfen and built a strong European reputation before being acquired by South Korean conglomerate Hanwha Group in 2012.
- Manufacturing is currently split across South Korea, Malaysia, and a facility in Dalton, Georgia.
- The Dalton, Georgia facility produces Q.PEAK DUO series panels, which qualify for the full 30% federal Investment Tax Credit under the Inflation Reduction Act’s domestic content adder.
- For most homeowners, Q CELLS represents a reliable tier-one option priced between $0.28 and $0.38 per watt at the module level, before installation labour.
- The flagship residential line in 2026 is the Q.PEAK DUO BLK ML-G10+, a monocrystalline PERC panel available from 395W to 430W.
- Panel efficiency for the Q.PEAK DUO BLK ML-G10+ sits between 20.4% and 21.6% depending on the specific wattage bin.
- The Q.PEAK DUO L-G10.3 (non-black-frame version) tops out at 425W with a cell efficiency of 21.4%.
- Temperature coefficient for power (Pmax) is -0.34%/°C on the G10+ series, which is better than the industry average of roughly -0.38%/°C.
- Q CELLS markets low-light performance citing its proprietary Q.ANTUM cell technology, which uses a passivated rear surface and fine-line front metallisation to improve current collection under diffuse irradiation.
- NREL testing has broadly validated that PERC rear-passivation improves output at low angles and under cloud cover compared to standard BSF cells, with gains typically 2–4% relative to conventional mono cells.
- The 400W Q.PEAK DUO BLK ML-G10+ measures 1,755 × 1,038 × 32 mm and weighs 20.9 kg (46 lb).
- Wind load rating is 2,400 Pa front and 2,400 Pa rear, and snow load is 5,400 Pa.
- The panel carries UL 61730 and IEC 61215 certifications, both required by most US utilities and local authorities before interconnection approval.
- Module-only pricing for Q.PEAK DUO BLK ML-G10+ panels in Q1 2026 sits at approximately $0.30–$0.36 per watt through US distributors.
- For a typical 8 kW residential system (20 panels), module cost lands between $2,400 and $2,900, representing about 18–22% of the full installed system cost, which averages $24,000–$28,000 before incentives nationally according to SEIA data.
- After the 30% federal Investment Tax Credit, an 8 kW system falls to roughly $16,800–$19,600.
- Homeowners whose installers use domestically manufactured Q CELLS panels from the Dalton, Georgia facility may also qualify for an additional 10% domestic content adder under IRA rules, bringing the effective credit to 40% and reducing net cost further to around $14,400–$16,800.
- California installers typically quote Q CELLS systems at $2.70–$3.20 per watt all-in.
- Texas installers often come in at $2.40–$2.90 per watt due to a more competitive and lower-labour-cost market.
- Massachusetts homeowners tend to pay a premium of $3.00–$3.50 per watt but benefit from the state’s SMART programme, which can add meaningful ongoing income on top of net metering.
- Payback periods for a Q CELLS system typically run 7–10 years in high-electricity-cost states and 9–13 years in lower-rate markets, based on current NREL modelling.
- Q CELLS offers a 25-year product warranty covering manufacturing defects and a 25-year linear performance warranty on the Q.PEAK DUO series.
- The performance warranty guarantees no more than 2% degradation in year one and no more than 0.54% per year thereafter, with a guaranteed output of at least 86% of rated power at year 25.
- The 0.54% annual degradation figure is slightly better than the industry standard of 0.55–0.60% per year that NREL cites as typical for monocrystalline PERC panels in its Long-Term PV Degradation Study.
- Over 25 years, a 400W panel should still produce a minimum of 344W — or about 860 kWh annually on a south-facing roof in a mid-sun location.
- The product warranty is honoured by Hanwha Q CELLS USA Inc., a US legal entity, which reduces insolvency risk compared to manufacturers backed by entities without US assets.
- The company explicitly directs warranty claims to their US operations and maintains a dedicated US claims portal.
- At the premium end, SunPower Maxeon 6 panels deliver 22.8% efficiency with a 40-year performance warranty, but module pricing of $0.55–$0.70 per watt makes a typical 8 kW system cost $4,400–$5,600 more at the panel level alone before installation labour.
- Canadian Solar’s HiKu6 series is priced at $0.26–$0.30 per watt with efficiency up to 20.9%, slightly below Q CELLS on both metrics.
- Panasonic EverVolt HK Black panels achieve 22.2% efficiency at $0.42–$0.48 per watt and carry an industry-leading temperature coefficient of -0.26%/°C.
- Jinko Solar’s Tiger Neo series offers strong efficiency at the lowest price point — around $0.24–$0.28 per watt — but lacks US-based warranty support and domestic manufacturing, which can complicate ITC domestic content claims and long-term recourse.
- Independent performance monitoring data from projects on the PVOutput community database and EnergySage’s installer network shows systems using Q.PEAK DUO panels in North Carolina and across the Sun Belt generally perform within 3–5% of their modelled PVWatts output.
- Q CELLS rates its Q.PEAK DUO series as PID-resistant per IEC 62804 testing, which is increasingly important as string inverter system voltages exceed 1,000V in larger residential and commercial installations.
- Panels without PID resistance can lose 5–30% output over time when installed in high-voltage strings, particularly in humid climates.
- Field failure rate data from EnergySage’s 2024 installer survey places Q CELLS in the lower-failure-rate tier among leading brands, with reported defect rates under 0.1% over 5-year installation windows.
- This aligns with Q CELLS’ Bloomberg NEF Tier 1 classification, held continuously since 2013.
- Tier 1 status measures bankability and manufacturing quality control, not raw panel performance, but it correlates with consistent module quality over time.
