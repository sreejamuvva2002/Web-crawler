# SK Innovation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2019-03-22

**Source:** [Jackson County Due to See an Influx of 2,000 Jobs from Battery Manufacturer, SK Innovation - Location Georgia](https://www.locationgeorgia.com/2019/03/22/jackson-county-due-to-see-an-influx-of-2000-jobs-from-battery-manufacturer-sk-innovation/)  ·  `page_000581`

## Facts

- SK Innovation intends to build a $1.67 billion factory to supply batteries for electric-powered vehicles in Jackson County, Georgia.
- Forecasts indicate that more than 7 to 8 million EVs could be on the road within five years.
- The deal between SK Group and Georgia was originally announced in November 2018.
- This investment is the biggest since Kia Motors opened its doors in West Point about ten years ago.
- The project will inject an estimated 2,000 jobs into the Jackson County and state market.
- Full job potential for the project is expected to be reached by 2025.
- Georgia has been chosen as the top state for businesses six times in a row.
- Georgia is home to Fortune 500 companies as well as international businesses.
- Georgia is in close proximity with many car manufacturers in the South.
- The Sun Belt area, which includes South Carolina, Tennessee, Alabama, and Georgia, has a high concentration of automakers.
- Automakers with shops in the region include Volkswagen, Daimler, Hyundai, Volvo, Kia, and BMW.
- SK Innovation selected Georgia for its skilled workforce and favorable employment environment.
- CEO Jun Kim stated that the company was looking for a place that would allow it to grow together with its community.
- CEO Jun Kim noted that Georgia's passion for building a business-friendly environment influenced their decision.
- The professionalism of state and local officials in Commerce and Jackson County also influenced the decision to set up a factory in the state.
- SK Innovation demonstrated its desire for a mutually-beneficial and long-term partnership by making a donation to the Empower College and Career Center in Jackson County.
- A groundbreaking ceremony was held on March 19, 2019.
- More than 200 guests attended the groundbreaking ceremony.
- Executive Vice President of SK Group Jae-Won Chey attended the groundbreaking ceremony.
- CEO and President of SK Innovation Jun Kim attended the groundbreaking ceremony.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp attended the groundbreaking ceremony.
- Commerce Secretary Wilbur Ross attended the groundbreaking ceremony.
- Other elected officials, state and local economic development leaders, and corporate representatives also attended the groundbreaking ceremony.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated, "Today is a remarkable day for Georgia," and thanked SK Innovation for a historic investment that will provide immeasurable economic opportunity for thousands of Georgia families.
- Governor Kemp expressed his expectation to work together in the years to come to strengthen the relationship and ensure a brighter future for Georgia.
