# Link Snacks, Inc. (Jack Link’s)

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2022-04-26

**Source:** [Jack Link’s to Create 800 Jobs with Facility in Houston County | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-releases/2022/jack-links-create-800-jobs-georgia-manufacturing-facility-houston-county)  ·  `page_002427`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Jack Link’s will create 800 jobs and invest $450 million for a new manufacturing facility in Perry, GA.
- The announcement was made with company, state, and local officials at an event at the future home of Jack Link’s.
- Governor Kemp stated he promised to bring opportunities to rural communities overlooked in the past and highlighted the 800 quality jobs and $450 million investment.
- Governor Kemp noted the company will take advantage of Georgia’s award-winning workforce program, Quick Start, to quickly fill available positions with Georgians.
- Jack Link’s was founded in 1985 in Minong, Wisconsin, and is a family-owned and operated company known for high-quality meat snacks and environmentally friendly manufacturing processes.
- The company operates multiple sites throughout the Midwest and has facilities in New Zealand, Brazil, and Germany.
- Troy Link, CEO of Jack Link’s, expressed excitement to partner with the State of Georgia and Houston County on a state-of-the-art facility and commitment to becoming a strong community advocate.
- Jack Link’s selected a 120-acre site in the I-75 GRAD Certified Site in Houston County located at 720 Perry Parkway in Perry for the new production facility.
- The facility is slated to be operational at the end of 2023.
- Ben Hulbert, Chairman of the Development Authority of Houston County, praised the company's relationship-driven approach and alignment with community values.
- Hulbert noted the project involves partners including Houston County, the City of Perry, the Houston County School District, Central Georgia Technical College, and other key stakeholders aiming to bring quality jobs to citizens.
- Career opportunities at the facility will include positions in management, production and production supervision, quality, maintenance, engineering, and general office support.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) was represented by Global Commerce Senior Project Manager Fernanda Kirchner, partnering with the Development Authority of Houston County, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson welcomed Jack Link’s to Georgia, emphasizing that economic investment and job growth are crucial for the long-term health and prosperity of rural communities.
- Houston County’s I-75 GRAD Site is a Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) Certified park, requiring due diligence and specific certification requirements prior to business visits to catalyze economic growth.
- Link Snacks, Inc. (Jack Link’s) is the #1 Meat Snack brand in the U.S., headquartered in Minong, Wisconsin, with a portfolio including Jack Link’s®, Lorissa’s Kitchen®, Wild River®, Golden Island®, MATADOR®, BiFi®, and Peperami®.
- The company's market position is ranked by NielsenIQ TSR RMS for 52 weeks ending 3/26/2022 in Total US Convenience, Super Category: Salty Snacks by Brand Low.
