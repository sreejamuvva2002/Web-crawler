# Wabash National Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2014-08-01

**Source:** [Wabash to build new plant; appoints new COO - Truck News](https://www.trucknews.com/transportation/wabash-to-build-new-plant-appoints-new-coo/1003121936/)  ·  `page_014613`

## Facts

- Wabash National plans to cut the cost of building new trailers with a new state-of-the-art production facility in Franklin, Ky., and changes to its plant in Lafayette, Ind.
- These efforts are focused on optimizing existing production capabilities and do not involve the addition of new capacity.
- The initiatives are expected to streamline production processes and improve manufacturing cost efficiencies in Lafayette.
- The initiatives will establish a strategically located, cost-leading, state-of-the-art manufacturing facility in Kentucky.
- The initiatives will gain access to an expanded labor pool to meet future workforce growth needs.
- The initiatives will provide manufacturing capabilities in closer proximity to new customers.
- The new facility and changes to the existing plant will drive margin expansion by reducing the overall average production cost per trailer.
- The initiatives will provide future opportunities to expand capacity at a lower cost point.
- The initiatives will open manufacturing space in Lafayette for future growth initiatives.
- Wabash National President and CEO Dick Giromini stated that while the trailer market faces current headwinds, the company is taking steps to improve its long-term cost position.
- Giromini noted that even at cyclically low production levels, these initiatives are expected to favorably impact margins.
- Giromini added that when demand for trailers improves over the next 12-24 months as industry experts expect, Wabash will be positioned to take full advantage of the benefits resulting from these initiatives.
- Current plans are to begin construction of the $25 million Kentucky facility in late 2008 or early 2009, dependent on market conditions.
- The 300,000-sq ft facility will produce both DuraPlate and FreightPro dry van trailers.
- Wabash named Joseph M. Zachman to senior vice president and chief operating officer of its executive management team.
- Zachman has over 24 years of manufacturing experience including serving as Vice President and General Manager for TTM Technologies, President of CDR Corporation, and Director of Operations of Sanmina Corporation.
- Zachman began his career with Delco Electronics Corp.
