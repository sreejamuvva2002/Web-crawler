# SK-Hyundai Joint Battery Plant in Bartow County

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-04-08

**Source:** [SK, Hyundai Joint Battery Plant in Bartow to Hit $5B, Filing Shows](https://georgialanddevelopment.com/2024/04/08/sk-hyundai-joint-battery-plant-in-bartow-to-hit-5b-filing-shows/)  ·  `page_021147`

## Facts

- An electric vehicle battery plant set to be built by two Korean giants in Bartow County by 2025 will hit $5 billion in investment value.
- The plant will generate $40 million in tax revenue per year.
- Details were outlined in a Development of Regional Impact filing submitted to the Georgia Department of Community Affairs.
- SK Battery America Inc., which already operates twin battery plants in Commerce, logged the filing to outline how its investment will impact the local community.
- The state’s community affairs department oversees the granting of tax incentives that help facilitate inbound investments.
- SK is partnering on the new plant with Hyundai Motor Group.
- Hyundai Motor Group is developing its own $5.54 billion “meta plant” in Bryan County and has already begun attracting a parade of suppliers from Korea.
- Hyundai’s facility near Savannah is set to turn out 300,000 electric models per year at launch.
- Hyundai’s production volume is expected to increase to 500,000 vehicles shortly after launch.
- All those cars will require batteries built in the United States using materials sourced domestically or from free-trade partners to qualify for federal sales tax incentives of $7,500.
- Hyundai and SK signed a memorandum of understanding to work together on a U.S. battery plant in November.
- Gov. Brian Kemp announced in December that the investment would come to Cartersville and total between $4 billion and $5 billion, creating 3,500 jobs.
- The February filing disclosed new details about the plant, which at the outlined spending level will be the third $5 billion project in Georgia’s history.
- Previous $5 billion projects in the state include Rivian’s Covington factory and Hyundai’s Bryan County plant, both announced within the last 18 months.
- The factory will occupy 3.3 million square feet.
- The site is located on Highway 411 on a 1,119-acre plot owned by an entity called Two Run Ranch LLC.
- According to the filing, 60 percent of the plot will be covered in impermeable surfaces when construction is completed.
- The plant is expected to use 3.6 million gallons of water per day.
- The plant is expected to generate 1 million gallons per day of sewage flow.
- The water and sewage requirements necessitate plans for a new treatment plant and service lines that will be built by the county.
