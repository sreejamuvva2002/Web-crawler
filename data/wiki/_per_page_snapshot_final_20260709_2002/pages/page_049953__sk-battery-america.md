# SK Battery America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2021-03-05

**Source:** [Georgia Battery plant's destruction of evidence 'extraordinary,' still pinning hopes on Biden | FOX 5 Atlanta](https://www.fox5atlanta.com/news/ga-battery-plants-destruction-of-evidence-extraordinary-still-pinning-hopes-on-biden)  ·  `page_049953`

## Facts

- The SK Battery America plant in Commerce, Ga. (Jackson County) already employs 250 people.
- The company plans to eventually hire 10,000 workers to provide batteries for American-built vehicles like the electric Ford F-150 pickup and a new Volkswagen electric SUV.
- On Feb. 10, the US International Trade Commission issued a ban on much of the technology SK plans to use at the Commerce plant.
- The ITC agreed that SK intentionally destroyed evidence after rival company LG Energy Solution complained SK stole its trade secrets.
- The ruling threatens the future of the plant and all associated green energy jobs.
- Deputy Secretary of Transportation nominee Polly Trottenberg promised to speak to President Joe Biden about the importance of the SK Battery plant.
- Georgia Sen. Raphael Warnock won a pledge from a Transportation Department nominee to explain to Biden what is at stake regarding the plant.
- The president has 60 days to either amend the ban or let it move forward.
- During a confirmation hearing for Deputy Secretary of Transportation Polly Trottenberg, Warnock stated that losing 2,600 clean energy jobs in the short term and 10,000 in the long term would be absolutely critical for the people of Georgia.
- Warnock pointed out that Biden had pledged to create 10 million clean energy jobs.
- Trottenberg agreed the issue was important and committed to explaining it to the senator, noting the department is already aware of the issue with the plant and starting to look into it.
- The ITC released public findings stating SK’s destruction of evidence in the case was extraordinary, ordered at a high level, and carried out by department heads throughout SK.
- The ITC found that even after receipt of the complaint, SK scrambled to destroy more records.
- The ITC noted it was not enough for SK to destroy or hide records once, as SK then collected more LG proprietary information only to destroy or hide those records as well.
- LG Energy Solution, a rival Korean battery manufacturer, accused SK of stealing their battery secrets and publicly urged SK to negotiate a financial settlement that would guarantee the Jackson County plant opens on schedule.
- In a statement, SK argued that no judicial process or legal authority has ever found that SK stole any technology, and urged the president to undo the damage from the ITC decision.
- Neither company confirmed whether settlement negotiations have begun.
- LG’s intellectual property attorney Song Jung stated they must first address the issue of stolen technology to compensate LG before SK can maintain the plant in Georgia.
- The ITC decision allows SK to produce Ford electric F-150 batteries for four years and VW batteries for two years as a temporary reprieve.
