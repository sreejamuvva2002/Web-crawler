# SK Battery America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-03-12

**Source:** [Nearly 1,000 workers laid off at Georgia EV battery plant | ESG Dive](https://www.esgdive.com/news/nearly-1000-workers-laid-off-at-georgia-ev-battery-plant/814535/)  ·  `page_060592`

## Facts

- SK Battery America is a subsidiary of South Korea-based SK On.
- SK Battery America is laying off workers from its production facility in Commerce, Georgia.
- The layoffs coincide with slowing electric vehicle battery demand and drying up federal incentives.
- The lithium-ion battery producer filed a Worker Adjustment and Retraining Notification on Friday.
- The notification informed Georgia of job cuts affecting 958 people.
- Affected workers will receive pay and benefits for the next 60 days.
- A spokesperson confirmed the severance details to ESG Dive sister publication Manufacturing Dive via email.
- Friday, March 6 was the last day of work for the affected employees.
- The Commerce site previously employed 2,566 workers.
- The layoffs reduced the facility's headcount to 1,600.
- Joe Guy Collier serves as SK Battery America’s spokesperson.
- Collier stated the company will work with affected workers during the transition period.
- SK Battery America remains committed to Georgia and building a robust U.S. supply chain for advanced battery manufacturing.
- The company is pursuing future customers in the Battery Energy Storage System arena.
- The EV battery industry is currently navigating demand challenges.
- Many automakers and battery companies are making cutbacks due to slower sales and a pullback of federal incentives under the current administration, according to the Federal Reserve Bank of Dallas.
- Companies are shifting focus to cater to growing data center demand for backup power and energy storage systems.
- In December, Ford Motor Co., a customer of SK Battery America, announced it was shifting electrification plans to include hybrid vehicles and smaller, more affordable EVs.
- Ford is also expanding its energy storage business.
- The automaker expects to record a $19.5 billion write-down due to the strategic shift.
- Ford dissolved its BlueOval SK battery joint venture with SK On as it shifts away from EVs.
- Ford canceled the electric F-150 Lightning due to sluggish sales.
- In 2023, SK Battery America announced plans to expand its Commerce site workforce by several hundred employees to meet rising EV demand.
- The planned expansion exceeded the company's initial goal of 2,600 employees.
- The Occupational Safety and Health Administration later fined the battery maker twice for exposing workers to respiratory hazards.
- Law firm Strauss Borrelli announced an investigation into SK Battery for allegedly failing to notify the 958 employees per the WARN Act.
- The WARN Act mandates that employers notify workers in writing at least 60 days before a plant closure or job cuts take effect.
- Collier stated that SK Battery America’s reduction complies with WARN guidelines.
