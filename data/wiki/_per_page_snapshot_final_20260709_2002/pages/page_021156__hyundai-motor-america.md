# Hyundai Motor America

**Source:** [Hyundai Announces 2024 Kona Electric Pricing | Automotive Fleet](https://www.automotive-fleet.com/news/hyundai-announces-2024-kona-electric-pricing)  ·  `page_021156`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor America announced Dec. 11 the pricing and equipment details for the all-new 2024 Hyundai Kona Electric SUV.
- The 2024 Kona Electric comes in three price trims and an estimated EPA range of more than 250 miles.
- This second-generation model adds improved cabin and cargo space, up to 261 miles of EPA-estimated range, and faster charging speeds with an upgraded 11-kW Level 2 onboard charger.
- The SE trim features a 99-kW (133-HP) Electric Motor with an MSRP of $32,675.
- The SEL trim features a 150-kW (201-HP) Electric Motor with an MSRP of $36,675.
- The Limited trim features a 150-kW (201-HP) Electric Motor with an MSRP of $41,045.
- The 2024 Kona Electric comes standard with Hyundai SmartSense advanced driver assistance systems (ADAS).
- Standard ADAS features include forward collision-avoidance assist with junction-turning and direct-oncoming (FCA), blind-spot collision warning (BCW), lane keeping assist (LKA), rear cross-traffic collision-avoidance assist (RCCA), driver attention warning (DAW), safe exit warning (SEW), and rear occupant alert (ROA).
- The vehicle includes a haptic-feedback steering wheel that alerts drivers of possible dangers, as well as second-row seat belt reminders.
- The Kona Electric comes standard with a 12.3-inch digital instrument cluster integrated with Hyundai’s next-generation 12.3-inch touchscreen infotainment system.
- The infotainment system delivers a new graphic user interface, faster processing speeds, customizable layouts, and EV-specific menus for charging preferences and finding charging stations.
- Advanced connectivity features include standard Wireless Android Auto and Apple CarPlay, over-the-air (OTA) software updates, and Wi-Fi hotspots.
- The new i-PEDAL feature enables a one-pedal driving mode that allows drivers to accelerate, decelerate, and stop using only the accelerator when desired.
- The Smart Regenerative System adjusts regenerative braking levels based on real-time data from forward traffic flow to optimize efficiency.
- Electric features include standard LED projector headlights, LED daytime running lamps and taillights, and an available “Seamless Horizon” LED DRL array.
- The Kona Electric has grown nearly six inches in length, the second row has gained three inches in legroom, and the cargo area has grown by 33%.
- Standard wheels on this SUV are 17-inch aero alloys which can help to improve aerodynamic efficiency.
- The 2024 Kona Electric features Bluelink+, a connected car services program that Hyundai intends to include for all 2024 model vehicles.
- Bluelink+ gives original owners of all 2024 model-year Hyundai vehicles complimentary access to all services included in the previous Bluelink connected care, remote and guidance subscription packages (4G LTE network and technology dependent).
- The Kona Electric features an expansion of the recently launched Hyundai Pay platform to enable in-vehicle payments.
- In addition to parking, Kona Electric owners can pay for charging at select locations through Hyundai Pay.
