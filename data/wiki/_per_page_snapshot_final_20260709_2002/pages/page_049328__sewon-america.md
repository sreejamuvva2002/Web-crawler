# Sewon America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-02-21

**Source:** [Sewon America to Create 740 New Jobs in Effingham County - AllOnGeorgia](https://allongeorgia.com/georgia-business/sewon-america-to-create-740-new-jobs-in-effingham-county/)  ·  `page_049328`

## Facts

- Sewon America is a subsidiary of Korean-based Sewon Precision Industry Co.
- Sewon America will create 740 new jobs in Rincon.
- Sewon America will invest more than $300 million in a new Effingham County manufacturing facility.
- The project is the largest known private investment in the City of Rincon to date.
- The announcement fulfills a commitment to create jobs and investment in all four Savannah Harbor Interstate-16 Joint Development Authority (JDA) counties.
- This commitment stems from Hyundai’s Metaplant location in Bryan County, which is the largest economic development project in Georgia state history.
- Suppliers for Hyundai’s Metaplant have committed over $1.8 billion in investment.
- This supplier investment surpasses initial estimates by at least $800 million.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that the announcement celebrates delivering on a promise to create jobs in all four JDA counties and the growth of a valued business partner.
- Governor Kemp congratulated Chairman Kim on the company's recent expansion during his first international trip as governor to Korea.
- Governor Kemp discussed continuing to work together with the company during that trip, which led to the current announcement.
- Speaker of the House Jon Burns, who represents part of Effingham County, played a role in securing the project and engaging Sewon America executives.
- Speaker Jon Burns celebrated the creation of 740 new jobs in Effingham County and coastal Georgia.
- Lt. Governor Burt Jones commended Governor Brian Kemp, Sewon America, and stakeholders on the historic economic development announcement for the City of Rincon.
- Lt. Governor Jones noted that investments by companies like Sewon build on Georgia's commitment to remain the No. 1 state for doing business and attracting a robust workforce.
- Lt. Governor Jones stated that the more than $300 million investment in Effingham County will positively impact the local community and the state.
- Sewon America operates one of the largest automobile body parts manufacturing plants in North America.
- Sewon America opened a manufacturing facility in LaGrange in 2008.
- The LaGrange facility has since doubled in size.
- Following the completion of the Rincon facility, Sewon America is expected to support 1,600 jobs in Georgia.
- Vice Chairman Kim of Sewon America stated that Sewon America was founded 15 years ago in west Georgia.
- Vice Chairman Kim expressed happiness at building another Sewon facility in east Georgia and excitement about the company's future as one family operating in two Georgia locations.
- Vice Chairman Kim credited the company's team members, Governor Kemp, the Georgia Department of Economic Development, and Effingham County for supporting the growth.
- Vice Chairman Kim stated Sewon America will continue as an excellent corporate and community partner and looks forward to continued success in Effingham County.
- Sewon's new facility will be located at the Grande View industrial park in Rincon.
- The new manufacturing location will produce electric vehicle (EV) body parts for original equipment manufacturers (OEMs).
- The facility will supply parts to Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- Operations at the new facility are expected to begin in 2025.
- Rincon Mayor Ken Lee welcomed Sewon America to the community.
- Mayor Lee commended the Effingham County Industrial Development Authority for bringing the largest employment and private investment project in the city's history.
- Mayor Lee expressed looking forward to working with Sewon to ensure its long-term success in the community.
- Chairman of the Effingham County IDA Troy Smith stated Sewon America selected Grande View industrial park as the best location for its future growth.
- Chairman Smith stated that Sewon America's and the IDA's investments will allow current and future generations to have fulfilling, well-paying careers without leaving Effingham County.
- Chairman Smith credited the City of Rincon and the Effingham County Board of Commissioners for their help and leadership in making the project possible.
- Executive Director of the Georgia Ports Authority Griff Lynch called the announcement terrific news for Effingham County and the State of Georgia.
- The Georgia Ports Authority is excited to support Sewon's growth in the new location.
- Lynch noted that the Hyundai announcement continues to bring new economic opportunities to Georgia.
- Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy Alyce Thornhill represented the GDEcD's Global Commerce team on the project.
- The project was developed in partnership with the Effingham County Industrial Development Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia EMC.

- Commissioner Wilson expressed excitement that Sewon is reinvesting in Georgia while supporting Hyundai Motor Group's operations in Effingham County.
- Commissioner Wilson noted that Effingham County has a skilled workforce, infrastructure, and collaborative spirit to support the company's growth.
- Commissioner Wilson congratulated Sewon and Effingham County and looked forward to the opportunities created for the company and community.
