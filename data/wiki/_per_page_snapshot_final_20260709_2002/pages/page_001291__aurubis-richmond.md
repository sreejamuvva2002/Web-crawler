# Aurubis Richmond

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2024-09-21

**Source:** [Aurubis celebrates ribbon-cutting at the first multimetal recycling plant in the United States](https://www.aurubis.com/en/media/press-releases/press-releases-2024/aurubis-celebrates-ribbon-cutting-at-the-first-multimetal-recycling-plant-in-the-united-states)  ·  `page_001291`

## Facts

- Aurubis AG marked the official ribbon-cutting ceremony for its state-of-the-art recycling facility in Augusta, Georgia on Saturday, September 21, 2024.
- The facility, named Aurubis Richmond, is the first-ever secondary smelter of its kind for multimetal recycling in the United States.
- Construction of the plant took over two years, following a groundbreaking in June 2022.
- At the end of 2022, Aurubis made a strategic decision to fast-track the expansion of the plant and invest a total of around $800 million.
- The expanded facility will be able to process up to 180,000 tons of complex recycling materials annually.
- The processed materials include circuit boards, copper cables, and other metal-containing products.
- Aurubis Richmond is expected to provide over 230 high-quality jobs in the greater Augusta area.
- The company pledged a community benefit agreement with Augusta Technical College, Aiken Technical College, and the Richmond County School System.
- As part of the community benefit agreement, Aurubis Richmond will invest in scholarships and job training programs for young workers.
- Aurubis Richmond's recycled metals are essential for key industries including renewable energy, electric vehicles, and battery manufacturing.
- The facility is equipped with advanced technology that meets environmental standards set by both the State of Georgia and federal regulators.
- Aurubis has a strategic vision to be carbon-neutral well before 2050.
- Governor Brian Kemp and First Lady Marty Kemp attended the ribbon-cutting ceremony alongside Aurubis leaders and local officials.
- Aurubis CEO Toralf Haag stated that Aurubis Richmond will keep strategically important metals in the economy, making US supply chains more independent.
- David Schultheis, President and Managing Director of Aurubis Richmond, described the facility as a major player in the North American multimetal supply chain.
- Governor Brian Kemp noted that Aurubis's goals aligned with Georgia's to create good-paying jobs and position the state as a leader in an emerging electric industry.
- German-based parent company Aurubis AG is a leading global provider of non-ferrous metals and one of the largest copper recyclers worldwide for over 150 years.
- COO Inge Hofkens participated in conversations with Governor Brian Kemp during the event.
