# IMTS Weekly Manufacturing Investments Roundup

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [10 New Manufacturing Investments This Week: June 29 | IMTS September 14 - 19, 2026](https://www.imts.com/read/article-details.cfm?articleid=2365&type=1)  ·  `page_054776`

## Facts

- Yancey Engineered Solutions will spend $5.7 million to build a new factory in Cordele, Georgia.
- The factory will produce power generation packaging systems.
- The facility is expected to employ 300 people once fully operational.
- LS Electric held a groundbreaking ceremony for an expansion of its power solutions manufacturing facility in Cedar City, Utah.
- LS Electric's Utah facility will increase from approximately 142,000 to nearly 854,000 square feet, representing a sixfold increase.
- A total of 250 billion won will be invested in the LS Electric expansion project.
- The new LS Electric plant is scheduled to begin operations in early 2027.
- Amthor, the nation's largest tanker truck manufacturer, officially opened its second manufacturing facility in Gretna, Virginia.
- The new Amthor facility spans 33,000 square feet and serves as a mounting facility for custom-built tanks installed on truck chassis.
- Amthor is actively hiring and expects the Gretna facility to create between 50 and 75 new positions.
- Fibrosan Inc. plans to construct a new fiberglass manufacturing facility in Cassopolis, Cass County, Michigan.
- The Fibrosan project will generate more than $16.8 million in capital investment and create 32 new jobs.
- The Fibrosan investment will support continued growth in the North American recreational vehicle market.
- InterConnect Wiring has leased 136,000 square feet in Fort Worth, Texas, to meet demand driven by increased defense spending and domestic aerospace manufacturing.
- InterConnect manufactures electrical wiring harnesses for military and aerospace applications, cockpit panels, circuit breaker panels, fiber-optic cable assemblies, ground support equipment, and sheet metal assemblies.
- InterConnect supplies blue-chip companies including Lockheed Martin, Bell, Boeing, L3Harris, and Northrop Grumman.
- TTM Technologies Inc. celebrated the grand opening of its new Ultra-HDI PCB manufacturing facility in Syracuse, New York.
- The TTM facility spans 215,000 square feet on its existing Syracuse campus and is purpose-built for Ultra-HDI PCB and advanced packaging production.
- The TTM Technologies investment will create up to 400 new engineering and manufacturing jobs.
- L3Harris Technologies is expanding missile propulsion manufacturing capacity with two new facilities in Arkansas.
- One L3Harris facility is a roughly 75,000-square-foot building for casting, curing, and final assembly, and the other is a nearly 70,000-square-foot case preparation facility.
- The expansion supports growing production of the U.S. Army’s PAC-3 missile propulsion systems and accelerates production of propulsion components for the PAC-3 Missile Segment Enhancement interceptor.
- Lockheed Martin received a seven-year Pentagon contract worth up to $35 billion to produce hundreds of THAAD interceptors per year.
- Lockheed will receive an initial $842.9 million for the THAAD contract, according to the Missile Defense Agency.
- THAAD interceptors can neutralize ballistic missiles outside the Earth’s atmosphere and cost more than $12 million each to produce.
- Gulf Coast Tactical LLC will invest $6.1 million in Iberia Parish, Louisiana, to expand production of unmanned surface vehicles, patrol craft, and other maritime platforms.
- The Gulf Coast Tactical project is expected to create 65 new jobs and advance autonomous vessel technology.
- Sandvik and Alpha Metallurgical Resources are investing $25 million to open a new manufacturing facility at the Waterways Industrial Park near Poca, West Virginia.
- The Sandvik and Alpha Metallurgical joint venture, called Centauri Ground Support, will generate more than 100 new jobs and produce rock bolts and resin capsules used in underground mining.
