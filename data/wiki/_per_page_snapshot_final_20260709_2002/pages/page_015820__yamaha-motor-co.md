# Yamaha Motor Co.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-03-10

**Source:** [Yamaha Motor Corp. moving its U.S. headquarters from California to Georgia - CBS Atlanta](https://www.cbsnews.com/atlanta/news/yamaha-motor-corp-us-headquarters-california-kennesaw-georgia/)  ·  `page_015820`

## Facts

- Yamaha Motor Co. is moving its U.S. headquarters from California to Georgia after nearly half a century.
- Gov. Brian Kemp announced that the Japanese company would be relocating to Kennesaw in the next few years.
- The relocation will begin at the end of 2026 and last until the end of 2028.
- The company's previous headquarters was located in Cypress, California, where it had been based since 1979 after acquiring the land in 1978.
- Yamaha manufactures ATVs, boat engines, personal watercraft, and other motorized products.
- Yamaha previously relocated its marine business to Kennesaw in 1999.
- Yamaha previously relocated its motorsports business to Kennesaw in 2019.
- Gov. Brian Kemp stated: "After many years of great partnership, we are honored and proud to welcome Yamaha's American headquarters to the No. 1 state for business."
- Gov. Brian Kemp added: "This is another loud and clear testament to what we offer job creators from around the world. To any other California-based companies looking for a better home, we'll give you plenty of reasons to keep Georgia on your mind."
- Yamaha currently employs over 2,300 people in Georgia.
- The majority of Yamaha's Georgia employees work at its factory in Newnan.
- The company stated the move is part of a structural reform aimed at improving the profitability of its U.S. operations.
- The restructuring responds to cost increases resulting from U.S. tariffs and changes in the market environment.
