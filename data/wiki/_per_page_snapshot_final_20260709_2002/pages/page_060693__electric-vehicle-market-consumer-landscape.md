# Electric Vehicle Market & Consumer Landscape

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-08

**Source:** [Before you buy an EV, consider these 5 questions](https://www.usatoday.com/story/cars/shopping/evs/2025/09/08/buying-an-electric-car-what-to-know/84038237007/)  ·  `page_060693`

## Facts

- EVs continue to grow as a share of the market with new models arriving or on the way and deals available.
- Recent uproar targeting Tesla over CEO Elon Musk’s political activities impacts the market.
- The expected elimination of federal EV tax credits is anticipated.
- Recent sales declines have occurred in certain EV segments.
- The average daily commute is less than 30 miles, making maximum range potentially overkill for many buyers.
- Many EVs promise battery ranges of 300 miles or more.
- Stephanie Valdez-Streaty, director of industry insights for Cox Automotive, notes that more range comes with more price.
- Cold weather conditions are harder on EV batteries and can affect range.
- Most people charge their EVs at home, though multiunit building residents may find this challenging.
- The U.S. Energy Department notes many EV owners meet daily driving range requirements by charging overnight with Level 1 equipment requiring no additional cost or installation if a dedicated branch circuit outlet is available near their parking location.
- Level 2 (240 volt) chargers can be installed at home to speed up the charging process.
- Level 2 charging provides 25 miles of range per hour according to the Energy Department.
- Some utility companies offer special rebates for installing Level 2 chargers as well as programs that can save money on charging.
- The Energy Department lists almost 73,000 publicly accessible EV charging stations with one or more ports on its website.
- DC Fast Charging provides 100-200 plus miles of range per 30 minutes of charging according to the Energy Department.
- Tesla’s Supercharger network promises more than 60,000 global superchargers for drivers of its vehicles with a recharge rate of up to 200 miles in 15 minutes.
- Many charging stations are located along prime corridors such as the Ohio Turnpike.
- Waffle House signed a deal with an arm of BP to bring fast charging to its restaurants.
- Stephanie Valdez-Streaty suggests prime EV candidates have shorter commutes, live where gas is expensive, and have good home or work charging access.
- People who regularly drive long distances, have consistently long commutes, live in rural areas with limited charging infrastructure, or need large trucks for hauling may not be ideal EV drivers.
- In April, the average transaction price for a new EV was $59,255 and the average listing price for a used EV was $35,874 according to Cox Automotive.
- AAA notes it costs less to charge an EV than a gas-powered vehicle.
- Some maintenance needs differ for EVs, such as no longer requiring oil changes.
- The expected end of federal tax credits for EV purchases would eliminate one type of incentive, but state and local jurisdictions could fill that gap.
- A large number of used EVs are expected to come off lease, leading to lower-priced options.
- Stephanie Valdez-Streaty remains bullish on EVs in the long run, stating the future is still electric.
