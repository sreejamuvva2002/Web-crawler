# Volvo Cars

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-24

**Source:** [Volvo Targets US Market With Next-Gen Made-In-America Hybrid](https://www.slashgear.com/1977743/volvo-next-generation-hybrid-us-market-announcement/)  ·  `page_014293`

## Facts

- Over the last few years, Swedish automaker Volvo has been investing more in electric vehicles and hybrid cars.
- This past year alone, Volvo released the updated XC90 plug-in hybrid SUV and the 2025 EX30 twin motor EV.
- Volvo is broadening its footprint in the United States to skirt stringent import tariffs.
- Volvo announced it would invest heavily into its shared factory with Polestar in Ridgeville, South Carolina, expanding the facility's size and scope.
- A large portion of the Ridgeville, South Carolina expansion will focus on manufacturing a brand-new American-made hybrid vehicle.
- Production of the new American-made hybrid vehicle is projected to begin sometime before 2030.
- Volvo has not divulged details or shown a prototype for the upcoming hybrid vehicle.
- Volvo states that hybrid technology demand in the U.S. currently exceeds electric vehicle demand.
- Hakan Samuelsson, chief executive of Volvo Cars, stated that electrification has not picked up as fast as anticipated a few years ago.
- Hakan Samuelsson stated that Volvo believes it will need hybrid cars longer.
- Volvo aims to increase its overall U.S. sales by about 60% in the next five years.
- The targeted sales increase represents an additional 125,000 units compared to last year's sales figures.
- Expanding the scale and operations of the South Carolina manufacturing plant will help Volvo recoup losses from import tariffs instituted this year.
- Imports of Volvo vehicles from Sweden are currently subject to a 15% levy.
- The 15% tariff rate could change as new deals are finalized with the United States government.
