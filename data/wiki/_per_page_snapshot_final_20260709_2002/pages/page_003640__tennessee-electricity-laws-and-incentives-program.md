# Tennessee Electricity Laws and Incentives Program

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Alternative Fuels Data Center: Electricity Laws and Incentives in Tennessee](https://afdc.energy.gov/fuels/laws/ELEC?state=TN)  ·  `page_003640`

## Facts

- The Tennessee Department of General Services (DGS) must ensure at least 25% of newly purchased passenger motor vehicles procured for use in ozone nonattainment areas are all-electric, hybrid electric, natural gas, or propane powered vehicles if available.
- If EVs, HEVs, NGVs, or propane vehicles are unavailable in ozone nonattainment areas, conventional gasoline vehicles achieving at least 25 mpg may satisfy the 25% requirement.
- In areas not designated as ozone nonattainment areas, at least 25% of newly purchased passenger motor vehicles must be EVs, HEVs, NGVs, propane powered vehicles, or conventional gasoline vehicles achieving at least 25 mpg if available at competitive prices.
- For non-passenger vehicles, Tennessee state fleets must make a reasonable effort to purchase at least 5% as natural gas or propane vehicles.
- State fleets must make every effort to ensure 100% of newly purchased motor vehicles are energy-efficient vehicles.
- Energy-efficient vehicles are defined as passenger vehicles using alternative fuels per the Energy Policy Act of 1992, HEVs, conventional gasoline vehicles achieving at least 25 mpg, or ultra-low sulfur diesel vehicles achieving at least 30 mpg.
- DGS must inventory the state’s passenger vehicle fleet and prepare annual progress reports detailing energy-efficient state fleet vehicle purchases by category and total counts by efficiency category.
- EV owners in Tennessee must pay an annual fee in addition to standard registration fees, increasing on a set schedule.
- The annual EV fee is $200 from 2024 to 2026.
- The annual EV fee increases to $274 in 2027.
- The annual EV fee remains at $274 for 2028 and later, adjusted for annual inflation.
- HEV and PHEV owners are subject to an annual fee of $100 from 2024 to 2028.
- The annual HEV and PHEV fee remains at $100 for 2028 and later, adjusted for annual inflation.
- Low-speed and medium-speed vehicles are exempt from the EV/HEV/PHEV annual fees.
- EV chargers providing electricity exclusively for EVs are not considered public utilities within the Tennessee Valley Authority (TVA) service area.
- EV chargers located outside of TVA’s territory may be subject to different regulations.
- NGVs and EVs may exceed Tennessee’s gross and axle vehicle weight limits by up to 2,000 pounds.
- The maximum gross vehicle weight for NGVs and EVs cannot exceed 82,000 lbs under the weight exemption.
- The vehicle weight exemption applies on all interstate highways.
- The U.S. Department of Transportation’s NEVI Formula Program required the Tennessee Department of Transportation (TDOT) to submit an annually updated EV Infrastructure Deployment Plan for fiscal years 2022 through 2026.
- TDOT's NEVI plans must describe how the state intends to distribute NEVI funds and outline physical and cybersecurity strategies.
- TDOT's NEVI plans must include a Community Engagement Outcomes Report and address updated NEVI Guidance.
- The Tennessee Department of Environment and Conservation (TDEC) provides funding for repowering or replacing Class 4-8 school, shuttle, and transit buses with alternative fuel or all-electric models.
- TDEC provides funding for replacing Class 4-7 local freight trucks and Class 8 local freight trucks and port drayage trucks with alternative fuel or all-electric models.
- Alternative fuels eligible for TDEC funding include compressed natural gas, propane, and hybrid electric technologies.
- TDEC also provides funding for light-duty EV charging infrastructure.
- Private, public, and non-profit organizations, including state, local, and tribal governments, are eligible for TDEC vehicle and charging funding.
- In partnership with the Tennessee Valley Authority (TVA), TDEC provides funding for the Fast Charge TN Network to support DC fast charging station deployment.
- Eligible applicants for the Fast Charge TN Network include TVA-served Local Power Companies whose service territory is located along corridors.
- The TDEC grant programs are funded in part by Tennessee’s portion of the Volkswagen Environmental Mitigation Trust.
