# Hollingsworth & Vose

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2023-01-03

**Source:** [H&V to Make Historic Investment in Floyd County, Virginia - Yes Floyd Virginia](https://yesfloydva.org/hv-to-make-historic-investment-in-floyd-county-virginia/)  ·  `page_008592`

## Facts

- Governor Glenn Youngkin announced on January 3, 2023 that Hollingsworth & Vose (H&V) will invest $40.2 million to expand its operation in Floyd County, Virginia.
- Floyd County successfully competed with Georgia for this project.
- The company will add over 28,000 square feet to its facility at 365 Christiansburg Pike Northeast to accommodate new production equipment to meet increased demand.
- The expansion will create 25 new jobs that will average about $50,000 per year in wages.
- Josh Ayer, CEO of H&V, stated the facility is essential to serving global and domestic customers and noted the company has been part of the Floyd community since 1976.
- Joe Turman, Chairman of the Floyd County Board of Supervisors, stated the new investment is over three times bigger than the largest private investment in the county's 191-year history.
- H&V was incorporated in 1892, is headquartered in East Walpole, Massachusetts, and has been family-owned for seven generations.
- The company manufactures advanced materials used in filtration, battery, and industrial applications that provide clean air, clean liquids, and energy storage.
- H&V currently employs over 200 workers at its Floyd County facility, which was established in 1976 as its first Virginia location.
- The Virginia Economic Development Partnership worked with Floyd County to secure the project for Virginia.
- Governor Youngkin approved a $558,700 grant from the Commonwealth’s Opportunity Fund to assist Floyd County with the project.
- Floyd County is offering performance-based incentives that will rebate a portion of new taxes paid on the new building and equipment for 5 years after the company makes investments and creates jobs.
- These local incentives provide the required 1-to-1 match for the state incentives.
- The Economic Development Authority of Floyd County (EDA) is offering stormwater capacity to support the H&V Plant 2 expansion.
- Jon Beegle, chairman of the local EDA, highlighted access to talent from the Floyd County Collaboration and Career Development Center, New River Community College, and Virginia Tech.
- The EDA sold H&V a 3.8-acre graded site adjacent to Plant 2 at the Floyd Regional Commerce Center in December 2021.
- The EDA and county are currently grading three smaller sites and bidding work to begin Phase 2 of the Floyd Regional Commerce Center in January 2023.
- Lydeana Martin, Floyd County Community and Economic Development Director, stated the project will provide new tax revenue and living wage opportunities for residents.
