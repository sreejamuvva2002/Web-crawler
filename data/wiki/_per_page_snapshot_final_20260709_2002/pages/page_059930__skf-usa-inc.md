# SKF USA Inc.

**Source:** [SKF – Customer Story | DMG MORI - DMG MORI Australia and New Zealand](https://au.dmgmori.com/news-and-media/customer-stories/skf-usa-inc)  ·  `page_059930`

## Facts

- SKF was founded in Gothenburg, Sweden, in 1907 by Swedish engineer Sven Gustaf Wingqvist, who invented the self-aligning ball bearing.
- The company provides an extensive range of products and services in rolling bearings, seals, lubrication, and mechatronics.
- SKF supports customers across aerospace, medical, semiconductor, automotive, and energy sectors.
- SKF maintains a US manufacturing site in Sumter, South Carolina.
- At the Sumter site, complete production of thin-section bearings was implemented on three automated DMG MORI DMU 340 FD machines.
- DMG MORI integrated grinding and gear milling by means of gearSKIVING at the facility.
- Autonomous and process-reliable tool handling is provided by TH-AGV at the Sumter site.
- Marcus Jakob, Director of Operations at SKF in Sumter, stated that their products enable precise, low friction operation of rotating equipment and machine elements.
- Jakob noted that a focus on reduced energy consumption for customers puts exciting challenges ahead.
- SKF continuously invests in the further development of its rolling bearings, slewing rings, and seals.
- Customer demands include tighter tolerances, reduced cost, and higher performance, requiring continuous production adaptation.
- To stay ahead of competition, SKF depends on high-precision multi-tasking machining centers and efficient manufacturing processes.
- The three DMU 340 FDs were selected for their machining versatility through technology integration.
- The XXL machining centers are equipped with 5-axis milling and turning in one workspace, plus exclusive DMG MORI technology cycles for Grinding and gearSKIVING.
- This allows all machining steps to be performed on any of the models for which at least two additional machines were previously needed.
- There has been a drastic reduction in throughput times in grinding and gear milling.
- SKF maximizes machine tool utilization by eliminating waiting times that were previously unavoidable when changing to different machines.
- Setup of components was accelerated with the help of robotic automation from Fastems.
- The automation model was based on a plant established by Jakob in his previous role as Aerospace Process Development Manager involving robot-assisted production of smaller bearing rings for the aerospace sector.
- In Sumter, SKF extended this principle to XXL machining while adding best in class workholding and automation principles.
- Automation ensures continuous manufacturing operation and maximizes machining center utilization, allowing the team to fully concentrate on preparing new orders and quality control.
- Tool management addressed a challenge of 60 tool changes per shift, which is a huge, time consuming task.
- The TH-AGV ensures autonomous transport of all tools to the respective machine.
- The TH-AGV can accommodate tools weighing up to 50 kg with diameters of 400 mm and lengths of 380 mm.
- The maximum loading weight with 16 tools is 480 kg.
- Safety for people and machines is ensured by scanners and sensors that permanently monitor the AGV travel path.
- SKF uses RFID chips in central tool management to assign tools to orders and machines.
- End-to-end digitization from tool presetting to machining ensures no incorrect tools are changed nor incorrect offsets entered into the controller.
- The optimized processes at the Sumter site serve as a good role model within the entire SKF Group, with potential future implementation on a DMU 210 FD in France.
- SKF USA Inc. is located at 925 Corporate Circle, Sumter, 29154 South Carolina, USA.
