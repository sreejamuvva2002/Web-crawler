# Panasonic Automotive Systems Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** research_and_development  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Considering “Joy in Motion” from a UX Perspective Professor Masaya Ando Describes People’s Relationship with Cars | Panasonic Automotive Systems Co., Ltd.](https://automotive.panasonic.com/en/studio/story/09)  ·  `page_047848`

## Facts

- Panasonic Automotive Systems Executive Officer Keiko Ohta discussed future car design and UX with Professor Masaya Ando of Chiba Institute of Technology following the company's exhibit at Tokyo Auto Salon.
- The company showcased concept vehicles including the "WELL Cabin Luxe" and "WELL Cabin Craie2" to demonstrate tangible mobility experiences.
- Panasonic introduced a large transparent display in its concept cars to present information on a large scale within the vehicle.
- The company demonstrated AI technology capable of analyzing finger gestures for in-car interaction.
- Panasonic's UX design philosophy emphasizes that a car must be driven safely before any convenient features are added, making safety the foundational premise of automotive UX.
- Context of use is identified as the most important factor in UX design, with different experiences required for weekend drives versus shuttling family members.
- Designers aim to shape how people spend time in the vehicle, such as creating spaces for solitary enjoyment or deep conversation between passengers.
- Automotive UX must account for finite human attention resources, noting that listening to music while driving uses about 30% of attention while driving uses 70%, whereas conversation splits attention fifty-fifty and can delay reactions to unexpected events.
- Interface design must be rooted in an understanding of human cognitive characteristics to prevent increased cognitive load from excessive information display.
- The company prioritizes maintaining the driver's psychological "sense of competence" rather than entrusting all driving tasks to technology.
- Panasonic is developing a non-commercial AI project called "Human Insight Intelligence" that combines human cognition models with external sensor data.
- Human Insight Intelligence uses AI to estimate risk based on sensor information and identifies cognitive gaps where drivers may fail to notice hazards, such as objects moving at a constant speed.
- The system aims to provide safety guidance by gently supplementing what the driver has not noticed, rather than constantly notifying them or creating warning displays.
- Panasonic has researched Human Insight Technology for many years to understand which environments and stimuli give people a sense of reassurance and comfort.
- The company is committed to examining factors that produce comfort and reassurance factor-by-factor to establish logic and evidence for situation-aware design.
- Future potential exists in using personal data sensed by cars not only within the vehicle but also in daily life or other industries to create new value.
