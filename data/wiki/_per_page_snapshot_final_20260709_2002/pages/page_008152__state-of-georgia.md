# State of Georgia

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2021-07-01

**Source:** [Georgia: Corporate Headquarters, Development-Ready Sites Foster Continued Wave of Development | Trade and Industry Development](https://www.tradeandindustrydev.com/tid-issue/georgia-corporate-headquarters-development-ready-31250)  ·  `page_008152`

## Facts

- Georgia’s pro-business environment is consistently recognized for its supply of development-ready sites, workforce development programs and strong logistics infrastructure.
- Corporations have found Georgia’s low corporate tax rate and access to quality talent to be just a few of the benefits to establishing headquarters in the state.
- Georgia smashed previous economic records – including for foreign direct investment – in fiscal year 2022, cementing itself as a leader in development across many thriving industries.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that from July 1, 2021, through June 30, 2022, investments and job creation were up again – reaching 94 percent and 53 percent above prior economic development records, respectively.
- More than 51,000 jobs were created in Georgia from July 1, 2021, through June 30, 2022.
- $21.2 billion was invested in expansions and new locations in Georgia during the period of July 1, 2021, through June 30, 2022.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development runs the “Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development” (GRAD) Certification Program to offer businesses a portfolio of pre-qualified industrial sites with completed due diligence.
- Companies including Amazon, Facebook/Meta, Starbucks and Qcells have benefited from choosing GRAD Certified properties in Georgia.
- Georgia has a labor force of 5.3 million strong.
- Georgia Quick Start is the nation’s number one workforce training program and has trained over one million employees for companies expanding or locating in the state.
- The Georgia Quick Start program began more than 55 years ago and provides tailored training for specific industries or companies in conjunction with the University System of Georgia and the Technical College System of Georgia.
- Underemployed workers in other fields can undergo upskilling training through the Georgia Quick Start program.
- More than 132,000 degrees and certificates are awarded to graduates from public and private universities in Georgia each year.
- According to U.S. News & World Report, Georgia is home to two of the nation’s top 20 public universities: the Georgia Institute of Technology and the University of Georgia.
- Georgia is home to two of the nation’s top five HBCUs: Spelman College and Morehouse College.
- Georgia Tech graduates the most female and underrepresented minority engineers of any college in the nation.
- Georgia’s HOPE Scholarship is the first merit-based scholarship in the U.S., providing tuition assistance to recent high school graduates and those pursuing higher education later in their careers.
- Georgia has the fifth-largest military population in the country, with almost 700,000 former servicemembers and over 101,000 military retirees currently living in the state.
- In April 2022, Governor Kemp created the first military retirement income tax exemption in state history to retain retiring military servicemembers in Georgia's workforce.
- The state’s corporate tax rate sits at 5.75 percent, and new and expanding companies can earn Job Tax Credits for creating new jobs in Georgia.
- Atlanta ranks third among U.S. cities for the number of Fortune 500 headquarters locations.
- Georgia is home to 18 of the country’s top 500 companies and 33 of the top 1,000.
- Georgia continues to invest in targeted industries including advanced manufacturing, electric vehicles and logistics.
- In July 2022, copper foil manufacturer Denkai America announced it will locate a new manufacturing facility and North American headquarters in Augusta.
- Denkai America’s materials are used in the lithium-ion batteries that power electric vehicles.
- Denkai America plans to invest a total of $430 million over the next five years.
- Denkai America plans to create 250 new jobs over the next five years.
- Georgia serves as the North American headquarters for many advanced manufacturing companies due to its strong logistics network and skilled workforce.
- Georgia outpaces the rest of the nation in 10-year GDP growth, particularly in machinery, electrical equipment and components, and fabricated metals manufacturing.
- Success in several manufacturing sectors resulted in a GDP of $64.9 billion for Georgia in 2021.
- The Georgia Department of Labor estimated there were 411,746 manufacturing jobs in the state in the second quarter of 2022.
- Georgia has welcomed recent investments in advanced manufacturing industries including electric vehicle batteries, electric vertical aircraft, high-end ammunition, clothing, furniture and building materials.
- Manufacturers can access the Georgia Center of Innovation’s expert manufacturing team at no cost to assist with market expansion, product development, and strategic partnerships.
- Georgia has been an established automotive manufacturing center since 1909, when the first automobile was assembled in the state.
- In 2021, more than 4,000 EVs were sold in Georgia, representing a total annual growth of 20 percent.
- As of September 2022, more than 42,500 electric vehicles were registered in Georgia.
- Dozens of EV-related projects have popped up all over the state since 2020.
- Rivian is planning to invest over $5 billion into EV manufacturing in Georgia.
- Hyundai Motor Group is planning to invest over $5 billion into EV manufacturing in Georgia.
- SK Battery America is investing at least $2.6 billion in two battery manufacturing facilities in Georgia.
- Korean lithium-ion battery recycler SungEel HiTech announced its first U.S. recycling facility in Georgia.
- Joon Georgia, an automotive parts manufacturer, was announced as the first confirmed supplier for the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- Georgia provides a seamless system of connected ocean, rail, truck and air transportation to support burgeoning industries like EVs.
- Atlanta’s Hartsfield-Jackson International Airport is known as the busiest and most efficient airport in the world.
- Georgia’s three inland ports provide regions across the state with direct paths to the Port of Savannah and Brunswick.
- The state has 5,000 miles of rail, creating the most extensive rail system and largest intermodal hub in the Southeast.
