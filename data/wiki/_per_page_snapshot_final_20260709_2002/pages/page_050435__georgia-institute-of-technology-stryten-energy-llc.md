# Georgia Institute of Technology / Stryten Energy LLC

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** research_and_development  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-04

**Source:** [Georgia Tech, Stryten Launch Lead Battery Research System](https://theenergydata.com/georgia-tech-stryten-launch-lead-battery/)  ·  `page_050435`

## Facts

- Georgia Institute of Technology partnered with Stryten Energy LLC, a U.S.-based leader in energy storage solutions, to install a Lead Battery Energy Storage System (BESS).
- The BESS was installed at Georgia Tech’s Carbon Neutral Energy Solutions Laboratory (CNES).
- CNES is located in the North Avenue Research Area of Georgia Tech’s campus.
- The facility houses Georgia Tech’s Strategic Energy Institute (SEI), an interdisciplinary research hub focused on accelerating discoveries in energy systems, sustainable practices, and resilient infrastructure.
- Faculty, postdocs, and students working on renewable energy systems, microgrids, storage technologies, and decarbonization strategies conduct research at CNES.
- The installed system will support a wide array of research initiatives related to Distributed Energy Resources (DERs), energy reliability, peak load management, and microgrid design.
- The system simultaneously provides functional value by enhancing the building’s own energy infrastructure.
- Stryten’s Lead BESS offers Georgia Tech researchers the ability to simulate, model, and monitor real-time energy storage and discharge behaviors in a campus-integrated environment.
- The system was selected for its well-documented advantages in cost-efficiency, safety, recyclability, and operational performance.
- Lead batteries are described as a mature, proven, and circular solution specifically suited for medium-duration energy storage.
- The installed BESS features bi-directional charging capabilities to absorb electricity from the grid or renewable sources like solar panels and release stored energy for peak loads or backup power.
- The system performs critical functions including load shifting, frequency regulation, and demand response to balance renewable energy integration.
- The BESS is designed to seamlessly integrate with Georgia Tech’s existing solar photovoltaic (PV) array.
- Excess energy during high solar generation periods is stored and deployed later during late afternoon hours or overnight to advance energy independence and emissions reduction.
- Christine Conwell serves as interim executive director of the Strategic Energy Institute at Georgia Tech.
- Christine Conwell stated that Georgia Tech’s strategic plan envisions the campus as a dynamic laboratory where sustainable practices are integrated into operations, creating mini ecosystems for situational data to guide innovative energy research beyond the campus.
- Scott Childers holds the title of vice president of essential power at Stryten Energy.
- Scott Childers noted the deployment signifies an evolution in viewing energy storage as scalable solutions for commercial, institutional, and behind-the-meter use cases rather than just utility-scale grid components.
- Scott Childers stated that with the introduction of this lead battery BESS, behind-the-meter applications are getting their day in the sun, expressing particular excitement about deploying units in commercial and industrial microgrids paired with EV charging stations to help the U.S. achieve its energy goals.
- Richard Simmons is the director of research and studies at SEI.
- Richard Simmons explained the system allows researchers to precisely control and study charging and discharging cycles in close coordination with the building’s rooftop solar PV array and a new electric vehicle (EV) charging test bed.
- Researchers can time-shift solar input to align with demand patterns like late-afternoon building loads and store renewable electricity to charge vehicles at night.
- Georgia Tech is planning a broader suite of energy storage technologies as part of its living lab ecosystem.
- Richard Simmons stated that regional partners are exploring R&D and testing projects involving flow batteries for longer-duration storage and lithium-ion BESS integrating second-life EV battery modules for grid resilience.
- The multi-technology approach aims to position Georgia Tech as a national leader in battery innovation, systems integration, and sustainable energy design.
- The project trains students and supports cutting-edge research while delivering tangible environmental and operational benefits.
