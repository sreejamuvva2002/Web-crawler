# Ascend Elements

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2024-05-10

**Source:** [Ascend Elements & Call2Recycle launch collaboration in the US - electrive.com](https://www.electrive.com/2024/05/10/ascend-elements-call2recycle-launch-collaboration-in-the-us/)  ·  `page_001492`

## Facts

- The collaboration combines Ascend Elements’ electric vehicle battery recycling infrastructure and technologies with Call2Recycle’s collection, logistics and tracking capabilities.
- The program provides online access to GreenTraxEV, Call2Recycle’s cloud-based all-in-one platform designed to ship mid-life, end-of-life and damaged-defective EV batteries.
- The companies plan to collect and recycle the equivalent of over 300,000 electric vehicle battery packs by 2030.
- Ascend Elements operates a facility in the US state of Georgia with processing capacity to handle 30,000 metric tons per year.
- Ascend Elements' Georgia facility is one of the largest EV battery recycling facilities in North America.
- Leo Raudys, CEO of Call2Recycle, stated the partnership aims to build infrastructure for electric vehicle battery transportation and recycling.
- The commercial agreement enables a convenient collection process through Call2Recycle's GreenTraxEV logistics platform.
- Ian Braime, Chief Commercial Officer at Ascend Elements, stated the expanded partnership will offer a comprehensive EV battery recycling and tracking program to customers.
- The new program is designed to help automotive dealerships, repair shops, and recyclers prepare for the expected wave of EV batteries in the coming years.
- Ascend Elements previously announced a similar cooperation with SK Ecoplant last September.
- Ascend Elements recently opened a plant in Poland.
