# YKK (U.S.A.) Inc.

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Macon-Bibb County Government](https://www.maconbibb.us/ykk-usa-celebrates-50-years-of-innovation-and-excellence-at-macon-factory/)  ·  `page_016064`

## Facts

- YKK (U.S.A.) Inc. announced the 50th anniversary of its Macon Factory, marking half a century of commitment to quality, innovation, sustainability, and community in Georgia.
- On November 9, YKK celebrated the milestone with a Family Day event for employees featuring speeches from notable figures.
- Attendees at the celebration included Mayor of Macon-Bibb County Lester Miller, Consul General of Japan Mio Maeda, YKK Corporation Chairman Masayuki Sarumaru, President of YKK Corporation of America Jim Reed, and President of YKK (U.S.A.) Inc. John Smith.
- President John Smith highlighted the pivotal role of the Macon Factory in embodying the company’s philosophy and its enduring commitment to the community over fifty years.
- Japan-based YKK first entered the U.S. market in 1960 as a zipper distributor in New York City, strategically located near the garment district.
- Founder Tadao Yoshida envisioned a vertical integration model epitomizing the “Cycle of Goodness” philosophy to control production and ensure superior quality and cost efficiency.
- A site selection team explored locations in Tennessee, North Carolina, South Carolina, Alabama, and Georgia before settling on Georgia.
- Georgia emerged as a frontrunner due to robust infrastructure including four interstate highways, the deep-water port of Savannah, and a major international airport.
- The decision to locate in Georgia was clinched by a meeting arranged by Cloyd Hall, a Macon native and Special Assistant to then-Governor Jimmy Carter.
- YKK established its manufacturing facility just south of Macon in the newly developed Ocmulgee East Industrial Park.
- The plant opened in 1974 after construction and operation strategies were crafted under Yoshinori Kitano.
- Newly hired engineers, including future YKK Corporation of America CEO Alex Gregory, were trained at YKK’s manufacturing center in Kurobe, Japan to transfer specialized techniques to the Macon facility.
- The facility initially focused on producing zippers tailored for the American market, including varieties for clothing, furniture, and outdoor equipment.
- Over the decades, the factory has expanded dramatically and now employs over 600 local residents.
- The Macon facility has diversified into advanced solutions for industries including automotive, government, and medical sectors.
- In 1977, YKK played a pivotal role in establishing a sister city relationship between Kurobe, Japan, and Macon, Georgia.
- Over the past four decades, this partnership facilitated cultural exchange with more than 1,100 citizens visiting each other’s countries.
- YKK has been a stalwart supporter of the annual Macon Cherry Blossom Festival through substantial sponsorships and active participation.
- In 1990, YKK (U.S.A.) Inc. donated $300,000 toward the construction of a dedicated building for festival organizers, complemented by $60,000 worth of land donated by the city of Macon.
- Chairman Tadahiro Yoshida personally attended the 1990 groundbreaking ceremony for the festival building.
- YKK was founded in Tokyo in 1934 and operates in over 70 countries/regions with a product portfolio including zippers, plastic hardware, hook and loop fasteners, webbing tapes, and snap buttons.
