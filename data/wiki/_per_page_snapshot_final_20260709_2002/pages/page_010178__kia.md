# Kia

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-07-12

**Source:** [Kia invests $200 million in producing electric vehicles in Georgia - WDEF](https://www.wdef.com/kia-invests-200-million-in-producing-electric-vehicles-in-georgia/)  ·  `page_010178`

## Facts

- Around 200 new jobs are coming to Georgia as Kia plans to assemble new electric vehicles, according to an announcement by Governor Brian Kemp on Wednesday.
- Kia will be manufacturing new 2024 EV9 SUVs with three rows of seats at Kia Georgia.
- The company is investing more than $200 million in this new venture.
- Assembly of these new vehicles should begin in the second quarter of 2024, according to Governor Kemp.
- This adds to Kia Georgia’s current manufacturing of the Telluride, Sorento and Sportage SUVs, as well as the K5 sedan.
- Over 40% of Kias sold in the U.S. are manufactured in West Point.
- Kia ships vehicles from Georgia to markets all over the world, including Canada, Mexico, the Caribbean, Middle East and Pacific countries.
- The EV9 is Kia’s first three-row EV SUV.
- It is also the first electric vehicle Kia has assembled in North America.
- Kia has currently invested nearly $2 billion into Georgia.
- Kia has added over 14,000 jobs in the region, according to officials in a press release.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Georgia’s longstanding partnership with Kia has led to generational job creation and growth for the West Point area.
- Governor Brian Kemp expressed pride in Kia’s continued impact on the Peach State as a national leader in the automotive industry.
- Governor Brian Kemp noted that the project will bring more opportunity to hardworking Georgians and help propel the state toward its goal of becoming the e-mobility capital.
