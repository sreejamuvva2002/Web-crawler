# Dana Incorporated

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-11

**Source:** [Dana Incorporated Announces Agreement to Combine with Eaton's Mobility Business, Strengthens Dana's Position as a Leading Global Powertrain Systems Provider](https://www.dana.com/newsroom/press-releases/dana-incorporated-announces-agreement-to-combine-with-eatons-mobility-business-strengthens-danas-position-as-a-leading-global-powertrain-systems-provider/)  ·  `page_019634`

## Facts

- Dana Incorporated announced a definitive agreement with Eaton Corporation plc to combine with Eaton's Mobility business in a transaction valued at approximately $5.1 billion.
- The transaction is structured as a Reverse Morris Trust, resulting in Eaton shareholders owning at least 50.1% and Dana shareholders owning approximately 49.9% of the combined company at close.
- Under the agreement terms, Eaton will receive a cash distribution of approximately $1.1 billion (subject to adjustments for cash and indebtedness), funded through new debt raised prior to closing.
- The combined company is expected to achieve $250 million in annual run-rate synergies within 24 months following closing, driven by reduced structural costs, purchasing scale, manufacturing optimization, and engineering efficiencies.
- On a fully synergized, pro forma 2026 estimated basis, the combined company will have approximately $11 billion in sales and approximately $1.7 billion adjusted EBITDA (approximately 15% adjusted EBITDA margin).
- The transaction values Eaton Mobility at approximately $5.1 billion on a cash-free, debt-free basis, representing approximately 8.3x estimated 2026 pro forma adjusted EBITDA before synergies, or approximately 5.9x including run-rate synergies.
- Dana's 2030 strategy targets are increased to $14–$15 billion in sales, approximately 18% adjusted EBITDA margin, and an 8%–9% adjusted free cash flow margin by 2030.
- Prior to the combination, Dana's 2030 targets included approximately $10 billion in sales, 14% to 15% adjusted EBITDA margins, and a 6% adjusted free cash flow margin.
- Following closing, the combined company is expected to maintain a strong balance sheet with net leverage of approximately 1.2x on a fully synergized pro forma 2026 estimated basis, and Dana is expected to maintain its current credit rating.
- R. Bruce McDonald will serve as Executive Chairman of the combined company with responsibility for integration and synergy realization, and Byron Foster will serve as Chief Executive Officer, with both assuming their roles on July 1, 2026.
- Timothy Kraus will continue as Chief Financial Officer, and Eaton's Erin Rowse, Senior Vice President Human Resources, Industrial, will serve as Chief Human Resources Officer at closing.
- The combined company's Board of Directors will be comprised of all the members of Dana's Board of Directors and three Eaton designees.
- The combination will integrate Dana's global powertrain, thermal, and sealing technologies with Eaton Mobility's commercial vehicle transmissions, engine and emissions products, and advanced electrification capabilities.
- Byron Foster, Dana's incoming Chief Executive Officer, stated the transaction marks an important milestone in Dana's transformation and positions it as a leading, scaled provider of powertrain solutions.
- Paulo Ruiz, Eaton Chief Executive Officer, stated the combination delivers significant value to Eaton and its shareholders, aligns with powerful megatrends, supports Eaton's 2030 growth strategy, and creates a leading global engineering solutions partner for commercial and light vehicle markets.
- Timothy Kraus, Dana's Chief Financial Officer, stated the transaction meaningfully enhances long-term financial outlook and enables significantly increasing Dana 2030 targets.
- The combined company will continue to operate under the Dana Incorporated name and retain its listing on the New York Stock Exchange.
- The transaction is expected to be tax-free to Dana and Eaton shareholders for U.S. federal income tax purposes and is expected to close in the first quarter of 2027, subject to approval by Dana shareholders, receipt of regulatory approvals, and other customary closing conditions.
- Goldman Sachs & Co. LLC is serving as exclusive financial advisor, Kirkland & Ellis LLP is serving as legal counsel to Dana, and Ernst and Young LLP is serving as transaction advisor.
- Goldman Sachs Bank USA is serving as sole underwriter of the committed financing for the transaction.
- Dana will host a conference call at 8:30 a.m. EDT on Thursday, June 11, to discuss the transaction.
- Dana Incorporated is headquartered in Maumee, Ohio, USA, reported sales of $7.5 billion in 2025, and employs 27,000 people in 24 countries across six continents.
- The company supplies leading vehicle manufacturers and related aftermarkets with industry-defining drive systems, electrodynamic technologies, and thermal and sealing solutions.
