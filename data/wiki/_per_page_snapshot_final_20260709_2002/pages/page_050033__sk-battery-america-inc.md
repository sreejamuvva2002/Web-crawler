# SK Battery America Inc.

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-03

**Source:** [SK lays off nearly 1,000 workers at Georgia plant amid cooling automaker EV plans - WTOP News](https://wtop.com/national/2026/03/sk-lays-off-nearly-1000-workers-at-georgia-plant-amid-cooling-automaker-ev-plans/)  ·  `page_050033`

## Facts

- SK Battery America Inc. laid off nearly 1,000 workers at its manufacturing plant northeast of Atlanta on Friday.
- The last working day for 958 plant employees marked about 37% of the workforce.
- A Worker Adjustment and Retraining Notification (WARN) notice was filed by human resources chief Chuck Moore.
- Impacted workers will be paid through May 6.
- The Commerce, Georgia plant will continue to employ approximately 1,600 workers.
- SK opened the $2.6 billion battery plant in Commerce, Georgia, in January 2022.
- The facility notably supplied batteries for the Ford F-150 Lightning electric pickup truck.
- Ford announced plans to cancel the fully electric version of the F-150 Lightning in December.
- SK Americas spokesperson Joe Guy Collier stated the workforce reduction was made to align operations with market conditions.
- Collier noted SK remains committed to Georgia and building a robust U.S. supply chain for advanced battery manufacturing.
- SK is pursuing future customers, including opportunities in the Battery Energy Storage System arena.
- The City of Commerce and Jackson County commission chair did not immediately respond to requests for comment.
- Ford scrapped its fully-electric F-150 version in December and opted for an extended-range version instead.
- A Ford spokesperson declined to comment on supplier personnel actions.
- SK and Ford previously invested $11.4 billion together in joint battery plants in the U.S. before ending the joint venture in December.
- SK also supplies batteries to Volkswagen.
- Georgia U.S. Sen. Jon Ossoff (Democrat) stated that the layoffs represent lost battery manufacturing jobs and criticized federal EV policy shifts.
- SK had previously announced plans in June 2020 to invest $940 million to expand its battery manufacturing presence in Atlanta.
- At the time of the 2020 expansion announcement, Gov. Brian Kemp’s office projected it would create 600 jobs.
- SK and Hyundai are still jointly building a $5 billion battery factory near Cartersville, northwest of Atlanta.
- Georgia has attracted other major EV manufacturing investments, including Rivian’s $5 billion factory and Hyundai’s own $7.6 billion factory complex.
- Georgia led the nation with 33 additional green energy projects announced by the end of 2024, according to environmental business group E2.
- Projects in Georgia topped $20 billion in investment, pledging more than 25,000 jobs.
- Qcells, a unit of South Korea’s Hanwha Solutions, resumed normal production on Friday after temporarily reducing hours and pay due to U.S. customs detaining imported solar panel components.
- A spokesperson for Gov. Kemp’s office highlighted Georgia’s robust job market, noting over 219,700 new jobs announced since 2019.
- EVs accounted for about 8% of new vehicle sales in the U.S. in 2025, matching the previous year's rate.
- Automakers including Ford, General Motors, and Stellantis have reneged on factory, investment, and product plans due to mounting financial losses and shifting demand.
- Many automakers across the EV supply chain are pivoting production toward hybrid and plug-in hybrid electric vehicles instead of fully electric models.
- Consumer concerns over EV driving range and charging infrastructure availability are making hybrids and efficient gasoline vehicles more popular.
- Under President Donald Trump, Congress eliminated federal tax credits of up to $7,500 for consumers purchasing new or used EVs.
- The Trump administration announced plans to weaken fuel economy and greenhouse gas emissions rules for automakers, removing federal incentives for cleaner vehicle fleets.
