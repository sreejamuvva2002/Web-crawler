# State of Georgia

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [2022 Tax Incentives | Atlanta CPA Firm](https://moorecolson.com/news-insights/seven-tax-incentives-georgia-businesses-should-consider/)  ·  `page_007891`

## Facts

- The state of Georgia offers tax incentives that make investing and expanding business in Georgia attractive.
- New and expanding companies may earn the Georgia Job Tax Credit for creating new full-time jobs in Georgia.
- A qualifying job must have a 35-hour work week, offer health insurance, and pay more than the county’s average wage with the lowest average wage in the state being $602/week as of June 2022.
- The employer generating the jobs must be in a specified industry including manufacturing, warehousing, software development, data centers, contact centers, telecommunications, and research and development.
- Employers can use the Georgia Job Tax Credit to offset income tax or to reduce a payroll withholding obligation.
- The county in which the job is created determines the amount of the credit, which ranges from $750 - $4,000.
- The Investment Tax Credit is available to existing manufacturing and telecommunication companies.
- Depending on the county, the Investment Tax Credit ranges from 1% - 5% of qualified investment expenses.
- Eligible expenses for the Investment Tax Credit include land acquisitions, improvements, buildings, machinery and equipment.
- To qualify for the Investment Tax Credit, a company must have operated a manufacturing or telecommunications facility in Georgia for at least three years and make a minimum $100,000 investment.
- Companies in certain designated counties may be able to apply excess Investment Tax Credits to payroll withholding.
- The Retraining Tax Credit can mitigate the cost of a company’s investment in new technologies and retraining existing employees on the technology or software.
- The Retraining Tax Credit is calculated as 50% of the employer’s direct retraining costs, up to $500 per employee per approved retaining program.
- The total amount of the Retraining Tax Credit per employee can be at most $1,250 per year.
- The Technical College System of Georgia must approve the training program for it to be eligible for the Retraining Tax Credit.
- The Research & Development Tax Credit is available to companies performing research and development in Georgia.
- To qualify for the Research & Development Tax Credit, the Georgia facility must be engaged in, or the headquarters of, a specified industry including manufacturing, warehousing and distribution, tourism, telecommunications, broadcasting, and research and development.
- The value of the Research & Development Tax Credit is determined based on qualifying expenditures each year in Georgia.
- Qualifying expenditures for the Research & Development Tax Credit can include the purchase of materials and supplies, fees for Georgia-based contract work, and the portion of wages and salaries of personnel, direct management, and direct support dedicated to R&D activities that occur in the state.
- The Research & Development Tax Credit equals 10% of qualified R&D expenditures over a base amount.
- The base amount is calculated by multiplying the company’s current year Georgia sales by the average of the ratios of its aggregate qualified R&D expenses to sales in Georgia for the preceding three taxable years, or 0.300, whichever is less.
- If the company had no sales in Georgia in one or more of the three preceding tax years, then multiply the current year Georgia sales by 0.300.
- The Employer Child Care Assistance / Facilities Tax Credit is available to employers who provide or sponsor childcare at a state-licensed facility.
- The Employer Child Care Assistance / Facilities Tax Credit is equal to 75% of the direct costs associated with the childcare services.
- Georgia provides an exemption from sales and use tax for certain expenditures that manufacturing facilities, data centers and distribution centers must make for their operations.
- Expenditures eligible for the sales and use tax exemption include manufacturing machinery and equipment, repair to industrial machinery, consumables, energy used in manufacturing, primary material handling equipment, pollution control, clean room equipment, and data centers.
- Many counties and municipalities offer a personal property tax exemption for inventory held within Georgia under the Freeport Exemption.
- The Freeport Exemption generally applies to manufacturers, distributors and fulfillment centers.
- The exemption amount for the Freeport Exemption varies by county and municipality.
- Companies can claim the Freeport Exemption with the filing of their annual business personal property tax return.
