## Facts

- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp announced an additional $2 billion investment for Hyundai Metaplant on Thursday.
- Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solutions will invest the additional $2 billion in their battery cell manufacturing joint venture at the Metaplant in Bryan County.
- The added investment will create 400 more jobs at the facility.
- The facility is expected to create 8,500 new jobs for the region in the coming years.
- With the announcement, the electric vehicle manufacturing facility and the battery joint venture represent about $7.59 billion in total investment.
- The project will create 8,500 new jobs for the region over the next eight years.
- Once fully operational, the facility will be able to support the annual production of 300,000 electric vehicle units.
- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp stated that in a single year, they broke ground on the largest project in state history, landed multiple suppliers across the state for Hyundai's Metaplant, and welcomed LGES to Bryan County.
- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp stated that they are building on that success as they continue to make Georgia the e-mobility capital of the nation.
- St. Joseph's/Candler is opening a site at Hyundai Metaplant.
