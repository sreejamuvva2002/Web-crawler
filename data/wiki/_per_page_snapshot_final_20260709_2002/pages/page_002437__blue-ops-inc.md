# Blue Ops, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2026-04-20

**Source:** [Red Cat’s Blue Ops, Inc. to Bring Hundreds of New Jobs with Lowndes Co | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-releases/2026/red-cats-blue-ops-inc-bring-hundreds-new-jobs-lowndes-county-manufacturing-facility)  ·  `page_002437`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced on April 20, 2026, that Blue Ops, Inc. will invest $30 million in a new manufacturing facility in Valdosta.
- The investment is expected to create 200+ jobs over the next several years in Lowndes County.
- Blue Ops operates as the maritime division of Red Cat Holdings, Inc. (Nasdaq: RCAT), a U.S.-based provider of advanced all-domain drone and robotic solutions for defense and national security.
- Blue Ops is leasing the former Regal Boats facility located at 601 Gil Harbin Industrial Boulevard in Valdosta.
- The leased manufacturing facility spans 155,000 square feet.
- The facility will manufacture the company’s uncrewed surface vessels (USVs).
- Blue Ops plans to hire up to 100 people in 2026.
- Red Cat recently announced a partnership with HADDY to accelerate Blue Ops’ USV manufacturing capacity.
- The HADDY partnership will create demand for hiring personnel to complete boat production after hulls are built using large-scale 3D printers.
- Barry Hinckley, President of Blue Ops, stated the investment aims to build a modern, scalable, and domestic USV manufacturing base from the ground up.
- Blue Ops is leveraging advanced production technologies to accelerate delivery of mission-ready maritime systems for defense and national security customers.
- The company will create high-quality local jobs, reinforcing Red Cat’s commitment to a secure, domestic supply chain.
- Valdosta Mayor Scott James Matheson welcomed Blue Ops to Valdosta, citing the area's strong workforce, strategic location, and powerful partnerships driving local economic growth.
- Niki Ogletree, president and CEO of the Valdosta-Lowndes County Development Authority, stated the announcement marks a significant community milestone.
- Valdosta-Lowndes County has experienced tremendous economic momentum over the past several years by attracting major investments and creating hundreds of new jobs.
- Bringing Blue Ops to the region diversifies the local economy, creates higher-wage opportunities, and positions the community for continued growth in advanced manufacturing and technology.
- Senior Regional Project Manager Andrea Taylor represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development on this competitive project.
- The project was developed in partnership with the City of Valdosta, Valdosta-Lowndes County Development Authority, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson highlighted that expanding the defense and national security manufacturing base is critically important.
- Georgia maintains a military presence across nine installations statewide, providing a stable foundation for companies in this ecosystem to grow and compete.
- Blue Ops’ location decision highlights the depth and diversity of innovation occurring across Georgia's defense sector.
- Through wholly owned subsidiaries Teal Drones and FlightWave Aerospace, Red Cat develops American-made hardware and software supporting military, government, and public safety operations across air, land, and sea.
- Red Cat’s family of systems delivers tactical capabilities in small, unmanned aircraft systems (sUAS).
- Through Blue Ops, Inc., Red Cat is expanding into the maritime domain to innovate uncrewed surface vessels (USVs) that deliver integrated platforms enhancing safety and multi-domain mission effectiveness.
