# American Trailer Manufacturers Coalition

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Feds investigate unfair foreign truck trailer pricing » Land Line Media](https://landline.media/feds-investigate-trailer-imports-tied-to-dumping-margins-as-high-as-1400/)  ·  `page_013249`

## Facts

- The U.S. International Trade Commission is investigating truck trailer imports from Canada, China, and Mexico for unfair trade practices.
- Foreign manufacturers are accused of selling trailers in the United States at less than fair value, known as dumping.
- Those foreign trailers are allegedly receiving government subsidies from Canada, China, and Mexico.
- The investigation stems from an antidumping/countervailing petition submitted by the American Trailer Manufacturers Coalition.
- The coalition is an ad hoc group that includes Great Dane, Stoughton, and Wabash.
- U.S. truck trailer manufacturers claim unfair practices have injured the domestic trailer industry and its workers.
- The coalition states foreign manufacturers have gained a significant and increasing share of the U.S. market at the expense of the U.S. industry.
- U.S. manufacturers have experienced significant drops in production, shipments, performance, and employment due to these imports.
- Nearly 10,000 jobs are directly supported by the trailer industry.
- Robert E. DeFrancesco III, counsel to the American Trailer Manufacturers Coalition, stated that trade relief is necessary to remedy injurious effects of unfairly traded van trailers from Mexico, China, and Canada.
- He added that applying antidumping and countervailing duties will level the playing field and save America’s trailer manufacturing industry.
- Hyundai Translead and Vanguard National Trailer Corporation are the primary focus of the probe according to the petition.
- In 2016, Hyundai Translead added a fourth Mexican manufacturing facility.
- From 2017 through 2024, truck trailer imports from Mexico averaged nearly 57,000 units per year, which was twice the rate from 2010 to 2015.
- Vanguard’s parent company, China International Marine Containers, ramped up subassembly imports in China.
- Following Section 301 tariffs on China in 2018, China International Marine Containers announced a Canadian facility in 2020.
- Assembly operations at the Canadian facility began in 2021.
- Canadian truck trailer imports rose 147% from 2021 to 2022 following the new facility's operations.
- The coalition claims truck trailer purchases surpassed freight demand needs, with purchasers overstocking on low-priced imports in 2022 and 2023.
- New trailer orders plummeted in 2024 due to this supply overhang.
- Demand is expected to remain suppressed until the supply overhang is fully absorbed.
- Subject imports continued using unfair pricing to win sales and capture remaining demand in 2024 and Hl 2025.
- Dumping margins have been as high as nearly 300% from Canada, 1,400% from China, and more than 430% from Mexico.
- Any eventual duties would apply to all van trailers imported from Canada, China, and Mexico, not just the focused companies.
- A final decision on the investigation is not expected until next year, but relief can arrive sooner.
- If dumping is found, the Department of Commerce can impose antidumping duties to offset the difference between fair value and sale price.
- Countervailing duties will similarly offset foreign government subsidies.
- The International Trade Commission's preliminary determination is due Jan. 5.
- The Department of Commerce's preliminary determination is due in mid-2026.
- A final determination is scheduled for the end of next year, though duties can be imposed at any time during the investigation.
- The American Trailer Manufacturers Coalition was formed to protect the U.S. van trailer industry from unfair foreign trade practices.
- The group's first public activity occurred earlier this year.
- In May, the group asked the Department of Commerce to include truck trailers as steel and aluminum derivative products under Section 232 tariffs.
- That request was granted in August.
- A 25% tariff applies to the value of the steel and aluminum within the trailer, not the entire trailer.
- Neither Hyundai Translead nor Vanguard could be reached for comment.
