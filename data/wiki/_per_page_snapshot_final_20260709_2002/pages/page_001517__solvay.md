# Solvay

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2023-11-02

**Source:** [Solvay to build PVDF facility | Solvay](https://www.solvay.com/en/press-release/solvay-confirms-plans-create-largest-north-american-production-facility-electric)  ·  `page_001517`

## Facts

- Solvay confirmed plans to build a new battery-grade polyvinylidene fluoride (PVDF) facility in Augusta, Georgia.
- The project is pivotal for the U.S. EV battery supply chain and benefits from a grant from the U.S. Department of Energy and a joint venture with Orbia.
- More than half of U.S. car sales are projected to be electric by 2030, driving demand for U.S.-produced PVDF to meet the growing EV battery and domestic energy storage markets.
- At full capacity, the new operations will provide material for more than 5 million EV batteries per year.
- The project will create hundreds of jobs throughout the value chain.
- Solvay and Orbia have signed a joint venture agreement for the project.
- The partnership secures Orbia's supply of needed materials for Solvay to manufacture suspension-grade PVDF.
- Suspension-grade PVDF is used as a lithium-ion binder and separator coating in electric vehicle batteries.
- Solvay will bring its process technology and global market know-how to the joint venture.
- The combination of Solvay’s Solef® PVDF innovations and Orbia’s raw material assets and production expertise will deliver PVDF that extends EV range, battery life, and battery safety.
- Ilham Kadri, CEO of Solvay, stated the project is a key milestone in Solvay's electrification strategy and emphasizes its global commitment to sustainable mobility.
- Ilham Kadri noted that DOE support demonstrates Solvay’s pivotal role in advancing technologies that meet market demand and improve energy storage, safety, and power of electric vehicles.
- Ilham Kadri added that the partnership with Orbia positions Solvay to shore up an independent, sustainable EV battery supply chain in North America and create clean energy jobs.
- Sameer Bharadwaj, CEO of Orbia, stated the partnership underscores Orbia's commitment to enabling the clean energy transition through investments in energy materials.
- Sameer Bharadwaj noted Orbia has a unique 'mine-to-market' position with integration in all key materials needed to bolster North America's EV supply chain and comply with the Inflation Reduction Act.
- Solvay and Orbia intend to use two production sites in the southeastern U.S., one for raw materials and one for finished products.
- Both plants are expected to become operational in 2026.
- Solvay and the U.S. Department of Energy’s Office of Manufacturing and Energy Supply Chains finalized an agreement for a $178 million grant to help build the facility at its site in Augusta, Georgia.
- The grant was awarded under the U.S. Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act (IIJA) to expand domestic manufacturing of batteries for EVs and the electrical grid.
- The project will fill a significant supply gap and build upon favorable regulatory conditions promoting regional production and material security.
