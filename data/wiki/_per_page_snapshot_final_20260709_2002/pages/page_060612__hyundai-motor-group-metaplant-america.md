# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-02

**Source:** [State Leaders Celebrate Hyundai Motor Group’s Historic Impact on Georgia - Rome CEO](https://romeceo.com/features/2024/02/state-leaders-celebrate-hyundai-motor-groups-historic-impact-georgia/)  ·  `page_060612`

## Facts

- Governor Brian Kemp and First Lady Marty Kemp joined President and Global COO of Hyundai Motor Company and President and CEO of Hyundai and Genesis Motor North America José Muñoz to celebrate Hyundai Motor Group’s historic impact on Georgia.
- An update was provided on the progress of Hyundai Motor Group's Metaplant America facility.
- Hyundai Motor Group's Metaplant America facility will officially start vehicle production in the fourth quarter of 2024.
- Hyundai Motor Group has been a partner to Georgia for decades, helping recruit businesses, giving back to communities, and working with colleges and universities to connect Georgians with opportunities.
- Hyundai and Genesis electric vehicles including the IONIQ 5, IONIQ 6, GV60, and Electrified GV70 were displayed outside the State Capitol.
- The hydrogen-powered XCIENT Fuel Cell Truck was displayed outside the State Capitol and will be used for logistics between Hyundai’s manufacturing facilities and suppliers.
- Governor Kemp officially declared February 26 as Hyundai Day throughout the state.
- The Georgia General Assembly passed a Resolution commending Hyundai Motor Group’s dedication to Georgia and recognizing February 26 as Hyundai Day at the State Capitol.
- Hyundai’s investments in Georgia will create tens of thousands of great-paying American jobs.
- Hyundai’s investments in Georgia will bring tens of billions of dollars in economic impact for decades to come.
- Since the Metaplant America project was announced in May 2022, LG Energy Solution was named as the joint venture partner in the manufacture of battery components.
- An additional expansion of the LG Energy Solution battery facility was announced.
- Hyundai and SK On are building an EV battery manufacturing facility in Bartow County that will supply Hyundai Motor Group’s U.S. plants.
- Since the initial announcement, Hyundai Motor Group’s planned investment in Georgia has reached more than $12.6 billion.
- The planned investment brings with it the creation of 12,000 direct, long-term jobs.
- Lieutenant Governor Burt Jones, House Speaker Jon Burns, legislative leaders, GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson, and officials from the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority joined the celebration.
- Partnerships with the University System of Georgia, especially Georgia Tech, have enabled Hyundai's positive impact and job creation in Georgia.
- Up to 300,000 Hyundai, Genesis, and Kia EVs are expected to be produced annually at the Metaplant.
- Seventeen HMGMA suppliers have announced projects across 10 Georgia counties spanning beyond southeast Georgia.
- Further supplier announcements are expected.
- Supplier facilities are expected to create more than 6,700 new jobs and more than $2.5 billion in investment.
- Supplier facilities will be operational prior to the opening of HMGMA.
- The Metaplant will have a generational impact as a force multiplier for additional investments in education, healthcare, housing, and transportation.
- The nearly 3,000-acre Metaplant site has four building exteriors complete with outfitting for internal operations underway.
- The Metaplant’s architecture incorporates technologically advanced elements, design features representing the Savannah area, Hyundai’s brand identity, nature, and clean energy.
- HMGMA is hiring full-time employees rapidly for training and skills on machinery, office and clerical work, and numerous other employment roles.
- HMGMA currently has 566 full-time employees.
- Approximately 75 percent of the current workforce is from the Greater Savannah area.
- An additional 37 employees are from Georgia but outside the local area.
- Georgia Quick Start broke ground on its customized training center at the Metaplant site on February 8, 2024.
- HMGMA partnered with local technical colleges to prepare students for entry-level employment at the Metaplant.
- Electrical Vehicle Professional Technical Certificate of Credit (EVP TCC) training is available at Coastal Pines Technical College’s Baxley and Waycross campuses, Southeastern Technical College’s Vidalia campus, Savannah Technical College, and Ogeechee Technical College.
- In September 2023, Georgia Institute of Technology and Hyundai Motor Company signed a memorandum of understanding to collaborate on research and applications for sustainable mobility.
- The collaboration areas include developing a hydrogen economy, workforce development, and making cities smarter and more sustainable.
- U.S. News & World Report ranked Georgia Tech as the top public university and No. 3 nationally in energy and fuel research.
- Since 1998, Hyundai has directed over $6 million towards local Georgia communities through philanthropic programs and partnerships.
- Hyundai is promoting STEM programs in local K-12 schools and hosting educational programs on alternative energy sources.
- Hyundai supports local organizations dedicated to affordable homeownership and focuses on environmental stewardship in the region.
- Hyundai has given more than $1 million to regional nonprofits and formed key partnerships with local educational institutions.
- Hyundai committed to $7.59 billion in capital investment in Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- The project will create 8,500 jobs.
- More than 550 Meta Pros have already been hired, with 75 percent coming from the Savannah region.
- Beginning with mass production, the electricity used to power the Metaplant will come from 100 percent clean power sources.
- The clean power initiative aligns with Hyundai’s commitment to carbon neutrality by 2045.
- A 41-acre park will be featured in front of the plant, including trails, running tracks, environmental art displays, sports fields, and amenities for employee use.
