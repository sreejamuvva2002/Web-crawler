# Archer Aviation Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Big Order Anchors Big Plan for eVTOL Developer | Archer Aviation | American Machinist](https://www.americanmachinist.com/news/article/55133250/big-order-anchors-big-plan-for-evtol-developer-archer-aviation)  ·  `page_001661`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation Inc. is building a manufacturing site in Georgia.
- Archer has a complex business deal involving a high-volume order for its electric vertical take-off and landing (eVTOL) air taxis and a partnership with the buyer to establish air-mobility services globally.
- Andrew Cummins, director of Business Development at Archer, stated that the new agreement grows their prospective order book to nearly $6 billion and opens up Archer’s global reach to premium international markets.
- Future Flight Global (FFG) and Archer have a memorandum of understanding for FFG to purchase up to 116 Archer Midnight aircraft, which could be worth up to $580 million.
- The buyer has made an initial deposit on the purchase, and Archer and FFG are finalizing a definitive aircraft purchase agreement.
- FFG is a company formed by executives at Titan Aviation, a business-jet operator, and has a joint venture to operate advanced air mobility assets globally.
- The plan includes FFG operating selected routes within Archer’s planned air-taxi service and providing service in other high-demand locations, including Southeast Asia, Europe, and the Middle East.
- FFG will deploy Midnight eVTOLs to provide on-demand transit to high-value clients such as businesses, governments, or individuals.
- Archer and FFG will collaborate to mature the eVTOL commercial market in areas including ecosystem development, infrastructure, regulatory engagement, and demand generation.
- FFG founder and CEO Karan Singh stated that they are partnering with Archer to provide tailored, flexible aviation services to top clients, noting the Midnight’s versatility for diverse routes and operational efficiency attractive to corporate clients.
- Archer is one of several companies planning to produce electric-powered aircraft capable of hovering, taking off, and landing vertically.
- The Archer Midnight eVTOL is powered by lithium-ion battery packs that provide 142 KWh of energy and a maximum power of 1,300 KW, capable of carrying four passengers and/or cargo over a range of 100 100 miles.
- Commercial-scale production of the Archer Midnight is expected to start soon at a new manufacturing plant in Covington, Georgia.
- Stellantis, a major investor in Archer, agreed to finance approximately $370 million of manufacturing labor costs as production ramps up to 650 aircraft per year.
- The Stellantis financing will be provided in exchange for a quarterly distribution of Archer shares based on the labor costs incurred.
- Stellantis will also contribute up to $20 million for initial manufacturing costs.
