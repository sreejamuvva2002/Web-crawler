# Saft

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [SAFT](https://franceforbatteries.fr/saft/)  ·  `page_059218`

## Facts

- Saft is a pioneering French company specialized in the design and manufacture of high-performance industrial batteries.
- The company's capabilities span cells, modules, pack assembly, battery management systems (BMS), and recycling.
- Saft has 100 years of expertise in developing tailor-made battery solutions.
- Solutions are designed for customers in multiple segments including aviation, aerospace, maritime, rail, and energy storage.
- Saft is a wholly owned subsidiary of TotalEnergies.
- The company offers nickel-based battery ranges for professional and industrial applications with elements ranging from 0.1 to 1,500 Ah.
- Nickel batteries are noted for robustness, reliability, and longevity, serving as the reference for difficult or demanding applications.
- Nickel batteries provide excellent performance at very low temperatures and are suitable for prolonged storage with limited or non-existent maintenance.
- Approximately 80% of all large commercial aircraft use Saft's Ni-Cd batteries.
- Saft's nickel batteries are also used in industrial standby applications, on board trains, on offshore platforms, and to power telecom masts.
- The primary-lithium battery range consists of three main electrochemistries: Li-SOCl2, Li-SO2, and Li-MnO2.
- Primary-lithium batteries provide high-energy and high-power solutions and can last more than 20 years.
- Lithium-ion battery solutions are available with different electrochemistries including NMC, NCA, NMC/NCA, and SLFP.
- Saft's lithium-ion batteries can discharge in less than 10 minutes.
- The company provides emergency power for the Joint Strike Fighter Aircraft F-35 at temperatures as low as -40°C.
- Recent Battery Storage Systems (BESS) projects include a Microsoft data center in Sweden that replaced a diesel backup system.
- A BESS project at the Myrtle solar farm in Houston, Texas has a total storage capacity of 225 megawatt hours (MWh) using 114 containers.
- The Grandpuits solar farm in Ile-de-France features a BESS with a storage capacity of 43 megawatt hours (MWh).
- Saft has a global presence across 19 countries.
- The company operates 16 factories located abroad and in France.
- Saft employs 4,300 people worldwide.
- The company maintains two research centers located in Bordeaux and Cockeysville (Maryland, US).
- Corporate headquarters is located at 26, quai Charles Pasqua 92300 Levallois-Perret France.
- Manufacturing plants are located at Georges Leclanché Street 86000 Poitiers, 111-113 Blv Alfred Daney 33074 Bordeaux, and 10 rue Ampère Zone industrielle 16440 Nersac.
