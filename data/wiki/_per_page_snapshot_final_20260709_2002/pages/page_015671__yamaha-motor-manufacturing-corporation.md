# Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Yamaha Breaks Ground on SMART Warehouse in Georgia | ATV Rider](https://www.atvrider.com/story/news/yamaha-breaks-ground-smart-warehouse-georgia/)  ·  `page_015671`

## Facts

- Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation (YMMC) broke ground on June 9 at its Newnan, Georgia location for a new SMART warehouse.
- The SMART warehouse is the first of its kind for Yamaha Motor Company worldwide.
- SMART stands for Sequenced Material and Reduced Transportation.
- The new facility spans 200,000 square feet.
- The warehouse will optimize SMART capabilities to reduce dealer order fulfillment response time by more than 50 percent for ATVs, WaveRunners, side-by-sides, and golf cars.
- Mike Chrzanowski, president and CEO of Yamaha Motor Corporation, stated that demand for all outdoor recreational products produced at the factory continues to exceed supply.
- The SMART warehouse will feature an automated storage and retrieval system to significantly improve medium and small parts delivery capabilities.
- The new system will reduce forklift usage by 50 percent by utilizing automatic conveyors and tuggers to move parts through the SMART center to factories.
- Mike Chrzanowski stated that the warehouse will improve overall operational efficiency and material flow velocity to factory assembly lines, allowing products to reach dealers and customers faster.
- The facility represents a significant investment by Yamaha, underscoring a long-term commitment to YMMC employees, the City of Newnan, and Coweta County.
- The SMART warehouse will bring higher-paid and more skilled jobs into Coweta County, including roles for robotics experts, software developers, and systems engineers.
