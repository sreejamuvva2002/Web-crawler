## Facts

- Archer Aviation (NYSE: ACHR) stock was up 8.2% as of 3:30 p.m. ET on Tuesday.
- Stellantis made a new $55 million investment in Archer Aviation.
- The $55 million investment follows Stellantis' purchase of 8.3 million Archer shares in March.
- The $55 million investment follows a $110 million stock and strategic-funding deal made in 2023.
- Stellantis is the auto company responsible for brands including Dodge, Chrysler, Fiat, Jeep, and Maserati.
- Archer confirmed it is on track to complete its manufacturing facility in Georgia later this year.
- The Georgia manufacturing facility should be able to support the production of up to 650 eVTOL aircraft a year.
- Archer's Midnight electric-vertical-take-off-and-landing (eVTOL) aircraft recently completed a successful flight test.
- Archer Aviation stock is down roughly 39% year to date.
- The company posted a net loss of $116.5 million in the first quarter of this year.
- Archer closed out the first quarter with cash and equivalents totaling roughly $406 million.
- The business remains in a pre-revenue state.
- Archer will likely need to sell more stock or take on debt to continue funding its operations.
