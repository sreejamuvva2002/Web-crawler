# Georgia

**Source:** [Georgia points to logistics prowess as auto manufacturing sector grows | The Supply Chain Xchange](https://www.thescxchange.com/articles/9580-georgia-points-to-logistics-prowess-as-auto-manufacturing-sector-grows)  ·  `page_019161`

## Facts

- Automotive industry companies are bringing new manufacturing centers to Georgia as demand grows for electric vehicle (EV) cars and parts, according to state officials.
- Doowon Climate Control America Inc., a Korean auto parts manufacturer and supplier, will invest more than $30 million into a new manufacturing facility in Metter, a town in Candler County.
- The Doowon investment will create 200 new jobs.
- Doowon Group specializes in designing and manufacturing automotive climate control systems.
- Doowon will be a key supplier for Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia (Kia Georgia) and Hyundai Motor Group.
- In September 2023, automotive parts company DAS Corp. invested $35 million in a manufacturing facility in Metter.
- The DAS Corp. investment created 300 new jobs.
- State officials pointed to Georgia's logistics networks as a reason the region was attracting new manufacturing centers.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that suppliers for the Hyundai Metaplant resulted in over $2 billion in investment last fiscal year alone, helping Georgia achieve a third straight year of record-breaking economic growth.
- Kemp noted that Georgia's logistics assets, including a reliable network of rail lines and highways, connect companies like DAS to key business partners across the state and to markets worldwide.
- Overall, mobility and battery supply chain companies have announced more than $27.3 billion in investments in Georgia since 2020.
- These investments have created 32,000 new jobs according to the governor's office.
- South Korea-based Hyundai Motor Group (HMG) is constructing its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility in Georgia.
- U.S.-based Rivian announced its largest manufacturing facility in Georgia.
- Rivian's Georgia facility is slated to start production in 2026.
- Rivian's Georgia facility will have an annual capacity of 400,000 vehicles.
