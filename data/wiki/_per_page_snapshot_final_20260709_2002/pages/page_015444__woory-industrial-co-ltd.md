# Woory Industrial Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-06-08

**Source:** [Woory to Open EV Parts Manufacturing, R&D Facility in Georgia - Expansion Solutions](https://www.expansionsolutionsmagazine.com/woory-ev-parts-manufacturing-georgia/)  ·  `page_015444`

## Facts

- Woory Industrial Co., Ltd. will establish a new manufacturing facility in Dublin, Georgia.
- The facility will create more than 130 new jobs.
- The project represents an estimated $18 million investment in Dublin-Laurens County.
- Governor Brian Kemp welcomed Woory as the latest job creator to help Georgia become the e-mobility capital of the nation.
- Woory Industrial Company was established in 1989 and specializes in heating, ventilation, and air conditioning components and systems for the global automotive industry.
- The company is concentrating on the development of new products for electric vehicles (EVs) and hydrogen-fueled cars.
- CEO Jungwoo Kim stated that the transition to electric vehicles is the future and the U.S. EV market is the key market among all automotive markets.
- Over the past 10 years, Woory has grown into a specialized EV component company through continuous innovation.
- The new facility will be located at 404 Kellam Road in Dublin.
- This site will be Woory's first U.S. manufacturing location.
- The facility will produce electrical heaters, control units, and actuators for EV and internal combustion carmakers in North America.
- Operations at the new facility are expected to begin in November 2023.
- Unlike internal combustion engine vehicles, electric vehicles do not have a heating source, requiring heaters for cabin heating and battery temperature maintenance.
- Woory's pioneering development of electric heaters over the last 10 years has made it a leader in this growing market segment.
- Woory will be hiring personnel from all fields necessary for an automotive parts manufacturing plant.
- Dublin Mayor Joshua Kight noted that Woory's commitment to place an existing manufacturing building back into service promotes infill development and creates new career paths.
- Dr. Fred Williams, Chair of the Board of Directors of the Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority, cited Dublin and Laurens County's rapidly growing automotive sector due to its location on Interstate 16 and access to a skilled workforce.
- Woory will partner with area education systems and training providers to ensure training programs are in place to fill its talent pipeline.
- Project Manager Jacob Lee represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on the project.
- The project was developed in partnership with the Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson highlighted that projects like Woory create jobs and opportunities in rural communities, which is a priority for the department.
- From July 1 through December 31, 2022, the automotive industry was the second-largest source of new jobs and investment in Georgia.
- Since 2020, Georgia's pursuit of job creation along the EV supply chain has resulted in over $22.7 billion in investment and the creation of more than 28,400 jobs.
- Woory operates production plants around the world employing over 1,300 people.
- The company's business focuses on the development and production of HVAC auto components for all types of vehicles, including electric, internal combustion engine, eco-friendly, and hydrogen-fueled cars.
- Woory has been nominated as a supplier for Tesla, Rivian, Lucid, and Canoo.
- Woory is currently supplying the HV heater system used to heat the cabins of EVs.
