# Hyundai Glovis

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2025-10-20

**Source:** [What is Customer Demographics and Target Market of Hyundai Glovis Company? – MatrixBCG.com](https://matrixbcg.com/blogs/target-market/hyundai-glovis)  ·  `page_001203`

## Facts

- Hyundai Glovis operates a logistics base in West Point, Georgia.
- The West Point, Georgia base is crucial for the company's involvement in the US automotive supply chain, supporting inland transportation and complete knock-down (CKD) parts distribution.
- Hyundai Glovis primarily serves its parent group affiliates, Hyundai Motor Co. and Kia Corp., managing logistics for their finished vehicles and CKD/SKD parts for international production facilities.
- The company is targeting Chinese electric vehicle manufacturers, exemplified by a September 2024 memorandum of understanding with BYD Co. to optimize vessel capacity.
- Hyundai Glovis aims to expand non-affiliated sales to over 40% by 2030, moving beyond its historical reliance on the Hyundai Motor Group.
- The company is exploring collaborations with other global automakers, such as Volkswagen AG.
- Hyundai Glovis handles specialized transportation for finished vehicles, raw materials, industrial equipment, and breakbulk cargo like heavy machinery.
- In 2024, the distribution segment (including used car trading and CKD parts) generated KRW 13.3 trillion in revenue.
- The logistics segment reported Q1 2025 revenue of KRW 2.458 trillion, an 8.1% increase.
- SKD-related revenue increased by 75% between 2020 and 2024.
- Overseas logistics revenue surged by 93% during the same period (2020-2024).
- Hyundai Glovis is investing approximately $750 million for six new LNG dual-fuel vessels to be delivered by the end of 2028.
- The company made a $110 million investment in Asiana Airlines' cargo division in 2024 to expand air logistics capabilities.
- Hyundai Glovis operates a global network with 55 bases in Korea and 97 overseas bases, supporting integrated logistics across America, Asia, Europe, the Middle East, and Oceania.
- The company is venturing into waste EV battery businesses to cater to market trends towards sustainability.
- Hyundai Glovis supports increased overseas production by Hyundai Motor Group, particularly in the US, which boosts CKD and inland transportation volumes.
