# Yamaha Motor Corp. USA

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-03-09

**Source:** [Yamaha Motor Co. leaving longtime Cypress headquarters for Georgia – Daily News](https://www.dailynews.com/2026/03/09/yamaha-motor-co-leaving-longtime-cypress-headquarters-for-georgia/)  ·  `page_015832`

## Facts

- Yamaha Motor Corp. USA is moving its headquarters from Cypress, California to Kennesaw, Georgia.
- The company has called Cypress home for nearly a half-century.
- The company has been moving divisions to Georgia for years.
- The corporate exit from California comes as Yamaha undertakes “structural reforms” aimed at improving profitability in response to cost increases from the Trump administration’s tariffs and “changes in the market environment.”
- The company’s departure will happen at the end of 2026 and continue until late 2028, according to Yamaha.
- Company spokesman Bob Starr said the move would affect about 250 workers at the Cypress facility.
- Bob Starr stated, “In terms of efficiency, to have us all together in Georgia, all the functions of the business, it makes a lot of sense.”
- Yamaha is the second big company to exit Cypress in recent years.
- In 2019, Mitsubishi Motors North America left its Cypress headquarters after 31 years and relocated to Franklin, Tennessee.
- The automaker cited lower operating costs in Tennessee for roughly 200 employees at the time of its departure.
- Iwata-based parent Yamaha Motor Co. Ltd. will sell the 25-acre office campus on Katella Avenue.
- The Cypress campus serves as an administrative hub with corporate functions and financial services for its motorsports operations.
- Yamaha's motorsports products include all-terrain vehicles, golf carts, motorcycles, and motorboats.
- Yamaha does not perform any manufacturing in Cypress.
- Commercial brokerage firm Avison Young has been tapped to market the Yamaha facilities.
- The facilities include industrial, flex-warehouse, and office buildings in a combined 278,964 square feet.
- The property occupies an entire city block bound by Katella Avenue, Holder Street, and Yamaha Way.
- Avison Young says the Yamaha property is one of the largest industrial redevelopment opportunities available in Southern California.
- Besides tariffs, Yamaha also said the move is the result of changes in the company’s business focus within the U.S. market.
- The move to Georgia follows the relocation of Yamaha's marine business there in 1999 and its motorsports business in 2019.
- Alicia Velasco, director of planning and community development for Cypress, said the city regrets Yamaha’s decision to relocate its headquarters to Georgia.
- Yamaha was one of the first companies to locate in the Cypress Business Park in 1980 and has been an important part of the business community for more than 40 years.
- The city thanks Yamaha for its long-standing partnership and looks forward to working with management to transition to new ownership.
- In the past decade, there have been several corporate departures from California for a variety of reasons, though many have cited lower labor costs and taxes.
- Other notable departures included Pfizer’s sale of its San Diego campus, Hewlett Packard’s 2020 move to Spring Texas, and Toyota’s 2014 shift to Plano, Texas.
- In 2021, electric vehicle maker Tesla moved its headquarters to Austin, and three years ago opened a global engineering headquarters in Palo Alto.
- Billionaire founder Elon Musk moved his residence from California to Texas, where there is no state personal income tax.
- Tesla’s automaking plants still operate in Northern California.
- The Buena Park-based Yamaha Corp. of America, which makes musical instruments and sound gear, is unaffected by the relocation.
