# Aspen Aerogels Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** thermal_management  ·  **Published:** 2025-02-15

**Source:** [EV supplier cancels plan to build plant near Statesboro - The Current](https://thecurrentga.org/2025/02/15/ev-supplier-cancels-plan-to-build-plant-near-statesboro/)  ·  `page_000447`

## Facts

- A Massachusetts-based electric vehicle supplier has abandoned plans to build a manufacturing facility in Bulloch County.
- Aspen Aerogels Inc. manufactures thermal barriers that insulate the batteries on electric cars from fires.
- Aspen Aerogels announced on Thursday via an investor call that it will cancel its plan to build a second plant near Statesboro, Georgia.
- Instead of the Statesboro facility, Aspen Aerogels will increase production capacity at its East Providence, R.I., plant.
- Governor Brian Kemp originally announced the Statesboro project in 2022.
- The original 2022 plan called for a $325 million investment in the Statesboro plant.
- The project was expected to create more than 250 jobs.
- Production at the Statesboro plant had initially been scheduled to begin in late 2023.
- Aspen Aerogel’s chief financial officer and treasurer, Ricardo Rodriguez, stated that in early 2023 the company decided to "right-time the construction" of the planned second aerogel manufacturing facility in Statesboro, Georgia.
- Following the decision to delay construction, the company ramped up its external manufacturing capacity.
- Rodriguez noted that the external manufacturing model has fully demonstrated its ability to efficiently increase aerogel supply.
- The company expressed confidence that a capital-light and modular capacity plan provides the most efficient path to creating value.
- Demand for electric vehicles has not grown as much in recent years as EV manufacturers originally expected.
- Slower EV demand is attributed to high sticker prices, an insufficient number of charging stations, and government policies that have discouraged car buyers from purchasing EVs.
- The Trump administration recently froze a $5 billion program aimed at funding the construction of EV charging stations.
