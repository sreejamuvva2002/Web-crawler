# Foreign-Owned Factories in Georgia

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2016-09-22

**Source:** [Foreign-Owned Factories Turn to Connectivity to Improve Efficiency - Global Atlanta](https://www.globalatlanta.com/foreign-owned-factories-turn-connectivity-improve-efficiency/)  ·  `page_015804`

## Facts

- Foreign-owned affiliates are heavy investors in Georgia and are increasingly relying on communication between shop floor devices and mainline computer systems to prevent problems, allocate resources, and react to customer preferences.
- Alcon/Novartis manufactures contact lenses with layers thinner than a human hair.
- Elesys produces sensors that help cars avoid collisions.
- Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corp. of America manufactures golf cars, Waverunners, and four-wheelers.
- The Sept. 22 Next Generation Manufacturing annual conference focused on foreign ownership and a commitment to efficient manufacturing through the use of technology.
- A panel discussion at the conference was part of a broader program focused on getting Georgia manufacturers to share best practices on strategic innovations.
- Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corp. of America operates a factory in Newnan, Georgia.
- A decade ago, Yamaha's Newnan factory required technicians to periodically take laptops to production lines to download machine information; now operations happen in real time.
- Yamaha Vice President Phil Trickey stated the company cut its downtime in half by getting ahead of problems through machines communicating with them.
- Phil Trickey added that connected systems can help forecast supply-chain gaps and material shortages.
- Alcon Inc., now part of Swiss drug maker Novartis AG, collects data on multiple points along its complex production line for an FDA-regulated medical device product.
- Alcon's head of engineering, Ireland native Shay Foley, stated the 'Internet of Things' provides information but the challenge is making it actionable to add value.
- Shay Foley noted that customer feedback channels exist in each country to keep the company updated on consumer complaints and preferences.
- Alcon's massive plant is located in Johns Creek, Georgia, and can react quickly to data generated from new contact lens colors in Asia or patient discomfort sources.
- Alcon took over the former CIBA Vision facility after it merged with Novartis.
- Mike Price of Elesys, a subsidiary of Japan's Nidec, stated connectivity enables firms to centralize processes and trim costs across global operations.
- Elesys supplies automotive sensors to manufacturers like Honda.
- Mike Price noted that collecting data instantaneously from plants allows one core group to analyze it and send directions, avoiding duplication of effort at each plant.
- Nearly 5 million units per year roll off Elesys's assembly lines in Suwanee, Georgia.
- Suwanee is one of four factory locations in Elesys's international system.
- Connectivity helps keep manufacturing in high-cost places like the U.S. versus low-cost locales by allowing companies to hold less inventory.
- Yamaha foresees a shift from a 'push' business model selling through dealer networks to a 'pull' business where consumers demand customized colors and styles due to online sales.
- Some companies see cybersecurity and difficulty finding an appropriate workforce as hindrances to introducing more connectivity to their plants.
- Other companies see their parent companies' legacy systems holding them back from further connectivity adoption.
- Workforce development was a key theme of the sixth annual Next Generation Manufacturing conference held on Sept. 22.
- Gulfstream Aerospace, the state's largest manufacturer, highlighted apprenticeships with 60 high schoolers a year and a co-op program through which nearly all technicians are hired.
- A tribute was held for Randy Jackson, the late chief administrative officer for Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia, who died in May.
- Randy Jackson was esteemed for his long-term view of workforce challenges facing Troup County and led the company's investment in a college and career academy.
- Randy Jackson prodded county officials to improve early childhood education to ensure Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia's access to a 'world-class' labor supply.
- Former Troup county commission Chairman Ricky Wolfe stated in a video that Jackson was always happy with reports but never satisfied.
- The Pendleton Group is the presenting sponsor of Global Atlanta's Economic Development Channel.
