# SK On and Hyundai Motor Group EV Battery Manufacturing Facility

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-12-12

**Source:** [Bartow County to get $5 billion EV battery facility](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/bartow-county-5-billion-ev-221700673.html)  ·  `page_050130`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group and SK On selected a site in Bartow County for a new electric vehicle battery manufacturing facility that will supply Hyundai Motor Group's plants in the U.S.
- Stakeholders estimate the project will create more than 3,500 new jobs through approximately $4-5 billion of investment in Bartow County.
- Hyundai Motor Group and SK On recently signed a memorandum of understanding regarding the partnership for a new EV battery facility in the U.S., with details still in development.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp stated that Hyundai Motor Group and SK On are valued partners and key players in the state's automotive industry, praising their investment in the "No. 1 state for business."
- Established in 2021, SK On is the lithium-ion battery subsidiary branch of SK Innovation and currently employs more than 2,000 Georgians at its SK Battery America facility in Commerce.
- In July, SK Battery America announced it would partner with the Work for Warriors Georgia program to hire veterans, servicemembers, and their families at its Georgia operations.
- Subject to execution of relevant agreements and final board decisions, the new facility will be located at Bartow Centre, a zoned manufacturing and industrial site on Highway 411, aiming to begin operations in 2025.
- Bartow County Sole Commissioner Steve Taylor expressed pleasure with the decision, noting the project will benefit regional job seekers and establish Bartow in Georgia's EV ecosystem.
- Director of Korean Investment Yoonie Kim represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development's Global Commerce team on the project, partnering with Georgia EMC, Development Authority of Bartow County, Cartersville-Bartow County Department of Economic Development, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson noted HMG and SK have been pioneering partners for decades, driving Georgia's automotive renaissance, and highlighted efforts to create a fully integrated supply chain connecting battery manufacturers with recyclers.
- Excluding this announcement, EV-related projects announced in Georgia since 2020 total approximately $17 billion in investment and more than 22,800 new jobs.
- Separately, Hyundai Motor Group announced in May plans to invest $5.54 billion in Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America, an EV manufacturing facility in Bryan County.
- Hyundai Motor Group broke ground on the Bryan County facility in October.
