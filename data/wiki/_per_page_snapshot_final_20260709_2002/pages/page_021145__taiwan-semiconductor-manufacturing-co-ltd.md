# Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Co Ltd

## Facts

- TSMC warned that first-quarter revenue could fall by up to 5% due to softer demand from a slowing global economy.
- Annual capital expenditure for 2023 will be reduced to $32-36 billion, down from $36.3 billion in 2022.
- Fourth-quarter profit increased by 78%, highlighting a sharp slowdown in the global technology sector grappling with inflation and rising interest rates.
- CEO C.C. Wei forecasts the semiconductor cycle to bottom sometime in first half 2023 and sees a recovery in second half 2023.
- The economic rebound is expected to be boosted by new product launches such as artificial intelligence-enabled goods.
- Revenue is expected to fall by a mid- to high-single-digit percentage in the first half of 2023.
- First-quarter revenue is expected to be in the $16.7 billion to $17.5 billion range, down from $17.57 billion a year ago.
- TSMC produces some of the most advanced chips for high-end customers like Apple Inc.
- The current quarter is likely to mark the company's first sales decline in four years.
- Chief Financial Officer Wendell Huang stated that the fourth quarter was dampened by end-market demand softness and customers' inventory adjustment, adding that such conditions would continue into the first quarter.
- TSMC is backed by billionaire Warren Buffett's investment conglomerate Berkshire Hathaway Inc.
- The company has repeatedly stated that it will benefit from a mega-trend of demand for high-performance computing chips for 5G networks and data centers, as well as increased use of chips in gadgets and vehicles.
- TSMC announced plans to expand production outside of Taiwan as various governments offer incentives to boost chip manufacturing in their respective countries.
- At least one-fifth of its 28 nanometre (nm) and more advanced node capacity could be deployed overseas within five years or more.
- TSMC began construction on a second chip factory in Arizona late last year, which will begin production in 2026 using advanced 3 nm technology.
- The total investment for the Arizona project in the United States is $40 billion.
- CEO Wei stated that TSMC was considering building a second fab in Japan.
- TSMC was also considering building a speciality fab focused on the auto industry in Europe.
- The company expects the auto chip shortage to be relaxed quickly.
- TSMC reported a record net profit of T$295.9 billion ($9.72 billion) from October to December, up from T$166.2 billion the previous year.
- Fourth-quarter revenue increased 26.7% to $19.93 billion, exceeding the previous forecast range of $19.9 billion to $20.7 billion.
- The share price of TSMC fell 27.1% in 2022 but is up 8.5% this year, giving the company a market value of $412.78 billion.
