# Joon Georgia, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp: First Hyundai Motor Group Supplier to Build Facility in Bulloch County - EIN Presswire](https://www.einnews.com/pr_news/600084463/gov-kemp-first-hyundai-motor-group-supplier-to-build-facility-in-bulloch-county)  ·  `page_001437`

## Facts

- Joon Georgia, Inc., an automotive parts manufacturer, will invest $317 million in Bulloch County.
- Joon Georgia, Inc. will create 630 new jobs with competitive benefits in Bulloch County and the State of Georgia.
- The company’s new manufacturing facility plans mark the first confirmed supplier for Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America in Bryan County.
- Joon Georgia is part of Ajin USA, which is a current supplier for multiple Hyundai Motor Group facilities.
- The company produces a variety of automotive body parts and electronics for original equipment manufacturers.
- Jung Ho Sea, CEO of Ajin USA, stated the company is excited to be one of the first Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America automotive suppliers to build an automotive parts manufacturing facility in Bulloch County and its first plant in Georgia.
- Joon Georgia’s new facility will be located at Bruce Yawn Commerce Park, previously known as the Southern Gateway Commerce Park, near Statesboro and in close proximity to the Port of Savannah.
- Operations at the Bruce Yawn Commerce Park are expected to begin in mid-2024.
- The company will begin hiring for the new facility in mid-2023.
- Billy Allen, Chair of the Development Authority of Bulloch County, welcomed Joon Georgia to the Statesboro-Bulloch County community and highlighted the site's proximity to the new Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- The Development Authority of Bulloch County will partner with Georgia Southern University and Ogeechee Technical College to provide critical workforce support for Joon Georgia.
- Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy Alyce Thornhill represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this project in partnership with the Development Authority of Bulloch County, Georgia Quick Start, Georgia Ports Authority, and Georgia EMC.
- Workforce development for the new facility will be supported by Quick Start, the top-ranked workforce training program in the nation.
- Through a partnership with the Technical College System of Georgia, Quick Start helps assess workers, train new employees on unique processes, and develop customized job-specific training using the most current techniques and media.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson described Joon Georgia’s announcement as a landmark moment driving Georgia’s automotive industry into the future.
- Since 2020, more than 30 EV-related projects have contributed over $13.3 billion in investment and announced more than 18,100 jobs in Georgia.
- On October 25, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America officially broke ground on its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility in neighboring Bryan County.
- Non-affiliated suppliers connected to the Hyundai project are predicted to invest approximately $1 billion in other locations around the state.
- Georgia is positioned as a hub for the electric mobility industry, building on assets that make the automotive industry successful.
