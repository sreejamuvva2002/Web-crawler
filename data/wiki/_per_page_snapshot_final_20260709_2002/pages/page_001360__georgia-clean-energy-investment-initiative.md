# Georgia Clean Energy Investment Initiative

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Clean Energy Investments Are Revitalizing Georgia Communities | Climate Power](https://climatepower.us/research-polling/clean-energy-investments-are-revitalizing-georgia-communities/)  ·  `page_001360`

## Facts

- Since the passage of the clean energy plan, Georgia has seen 41 new clean energy projects invest $23.88 billion.
- These projects have created over 30,600 jobs across the state.
- Many of these clean energy jobs pay a higher average salary and are unionized positions.
- Companies have partnered with local schools to establish workforce training and skills development programs.
- Qcells has made several investments in northwest Georgia to boost U.S. solar manufacturing.
- The company said the passage of the Inflation Reduction Act was “crucial” to its success and decision to expand manufacturing in the U.S.
- Since the clean energy plan passed, Qcells has added over 500 new jobs at its Dalton manufacturing facility.
- Qcells announced plans to create 2,000 new jobs at a manufacturing facility in Cartersville.
- Qcells is creating 3,800 direct jobs across Bartow and Whitfield counties.
- Qcells is supporting nearly 3,000 additional indirect jobs in surrounding communities.
- The two factories are projected to create over $2 billion in total sales/output in Bartow and Whitfield Counties annually.
- Qcells is actively working to attract and train a local workforce.
- Qcells partnered with Georgia Quick Start to provide customized job training programs.
- Training sessions emphasize worker safety.
- Qcells is attracting workers from the region’s once-thriving carpeting industry.
- Qcells is offering opportunities for growth.
- In March 2023, Hanwha Advanced Materials announced a $147 million investment to open a solar encapsulent materials manufacturing facility in Bartow County.
- The facility will supply Qcell’s nearby Cartersville factory.
- Production begins at the end of 2024.
- The new factory will employ 160 workers.
- It will be the only U.S. manufacturer of ethylene vinyl acetate, which increases solar panel durability.
- In November 2022, Hyundai and SK On signed a memorandum of understanding to open an EV battery cell plant in Bartow County.
- The plant aims to meet demand for battery cells that qualify for Inflation Reduction Act tax incentives.
- The two companies formalized the agreement in May 2023.
- They announced they would invest $5 billion in the facility.
- The plant will create 3,750 jobs.
- Jobs have an average annual salary of over $53,000.
- This salary is higher than the current average income in Bartow County.
- The plant opens in the second half of 2025.
- It will produce enough batteries to support 300,000 EVs per year.
- In February 2024, SolarCycle announced plans to invest $344 million in a new solar glass manufacturing facility in Cedartown.
- The company plans to create more than 600 new jobs at the facility.
- It will be the first in the U.S. to use recycled materials from retired solar panels to make new solar glass.
- The Chair of Polk County’s Development Authority celebrated the investment, saying it would have a positive economic impact by providing quality jobs and strengthening opportunities in housing and retail development in the area.
- SolarCycle chose to invest in Northwest Georgia because of its proximity to major domestic solar panel manufacturers.
- SolarCycle chose the location for access to key shipping infrastructure.
- The company announced its Cedartown facility after receiving a contract from Qcells to recycle decommissioned panels.
- In partnership with Georgia Northwestern Technical College, the State of Georgia, and other community organizations, SolarCycle will offer 60 apprenticeship positions.
- Apprenticeships ensure training is available for students of all economic backgrounds.
- The City of West Point was on a downward economic slide in the 2000s, driven by losses in the textile industry before Kia announced it would open an auto manufacturing plant in the city in 2006.
- In July 2023, Kia announced a $200 million investment in its West Point manufacturing facility.
- The investment will create 200 jobs.
- The facility includes the Kia Georgia Training Center.
- The training center includes robotics, welding and electronics labs, classrooms, and equipment for training on state-of-the-art programmable logic controllers.
- In May 2023, Anovion announced its plans for its first synthetic graphite anode materials manufacturing facility in Bainbridge.
- The facility represents an $800 million investment.
- It will create over 400 high-paying quality jobs.
- Anovion’s average wage is $63,000 a year.
- Anovion will provide scholarships for students in the area to attend Southern Regional Technical College.
- Bainbridge’s Executive Director of the Developmental Authority, Rick McCaskill, said it is difficult to keep young people in the city after they graduate.
- New high-paying clean energy jobs and scholarships are hoped to be an incentive to have residents develop their careers in the community.
- In January 2024, Imola Automotive USA announced it would open an EV manufacturing facility in Fort Valley.
- When operational, the plant would employ 7,500 workers.
- The plant will provide a technology training program for EV workers.
- The majority of the jobs created would pay $46 per hour.
- Jobs only require a G.E.D or high school diploma.
- There are opportunities for training and career advancement.
- These jobs would raise the median income in Fort Valley.
- Imola Automotive Group signed a neutrality agreement with the UAW.
- The company said allowing workers to unionize was “in line” with its priorities.
- In April 2024, Syensqo broke ground on a battery-grade polyvinylidene fluoride (PVDF) manufacturing facility near Augusta.
- PVDF is a critical battery component that helps EVs go further.
- The plant was partially supported by a $178.2 million Bipartisan Infrastructure Law-funded Department of Energy grant to support the U.S. battery supply chain.
- Syensqo created 100 highly skilled jobs.
- Syensqo supported 500 local construction jobs.
- Syensqo announced it would invest $1 million to support workforce development and training programs for local economically disadvantaged, underrepresented, and rural communities.
- As part of that investment, Syensqo will make contributions to local higher education institutions, including Augusta Technical College.
- In February 2023, Seoyon E-HWA announced a new EV components manufacturing facility in Savannah.
- The company broke ground on the $76 million facility in April 2023.
- Production is scheduled for October 2024.
- The new facility will supply parts for the nearby Hyundai Metaplant.
- It will create 740 jobs, including 500 permanent roles.
- In September 2023, Seoyon announced a partnership with Savannah Technical College to provide job training programs.
- Seoyon E-Hwa President Chung Yun Kim said it was a great opportunity for the company to “hire very skilled employees from the esteemed Savannah Technical College.”
- As of March 2024, Seoyon is actively hiring.
- Seoyon participated in a job fair for Hyundai suppliers.
- In February 2023, Sewon America announced a $300 million investment to open an EV body part manufacturing facility in Rincon.
- This project is the largest known private investment in the City of Rincon.
- The facility will create 740 new jobs.
- Sewon America is collaborating with Savannah Technical College on a workforce training program.
- The training program graduated its first students in January 2024.
- Sewon America contributed to local educational and infrastructure funds in the county.
- As of March 2024, Sewon is already actively hiring in the community.
- Sewon participated in a job fair for Hyundai suppliers in Georgia.
