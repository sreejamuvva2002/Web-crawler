## Facts

- SK Innovation and Hyundai Motor Group plan a sprawling electric vehicle battery plant in Bartow County, northwest of Atlanta.
- The project is expected to create more than 3,500 jobs.
- The project will rank as the third-largest corporate investment in state history.
- Gov. Brian Kemp’s office announced the project on Thursday.
- The joint venture is between SK subsidiary SK On and Hyundai.
- The project adds to Georgia’s stature as an emerging EV and battery manufacturing hub.
- Georgia’s status as an emerging EV and battery manufacturing hub shows no signs of slowing down.
- Automakers are betting their futures on electrification.
- SK Innovation previously announced a $5.8 billion investment in Atlanta, Georgia on February 7, 2025.
- Hyundai announced a $4.5 billion investment in automotive components manufacturing for two new plants.
- The joint venture between SK On and Hyundai announced the project at a press conference in Bartow County, Georgia on February 11, 2025.
- Gov. Brian Kemp said one of the largest investments in the state will be made by Hyundai and SK On in automotive manufacturing.
- The company will also add three new jobs for the first three years of this project and up to 845,000 jobs over the lifetime of the project.
- The project is in addition to multibillion-dollar investments SK and Hyundai have already announced in Georgia.
