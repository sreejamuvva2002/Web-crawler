# Hyundai Motor Company

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-04

**Source:** [Hyundai Doubles Down: $2.7 Billion Georgia Expansion Proves ICE Raid Won’t Stop EV Push - DealerBar](https://dealerbar.com/2025/09/25/hyundai-doubles-down-2-7-billion-georgia-expansion-proves-ice-raid-wont-stop-ev-push/)  ·  `page_008859`

## Facts

- Hyundai announced a $2.7 billion expansion at its Georgia megasite two weeks after an immigration raid detained 475 workers.
- The September 4, 2025 raid at the Hyundai-LG battery plant in Ellabell, Georgia was the largest single-location immigration raid in U.S. history.
- The raid detained over 300 South Korean nationals and sparked diplomatic tensions between Washington and Seoul under Trump’s second term.
- Federal agents descended on the Ellabell site with military equipment, helicopters, and ATVs during the raid.
- Some detained workers held valid work permits according to leaked documents.
- The Hyundai-LG battery plant opening was pushed from 2025 to 2026 due to the raid.
- Seoul sent a charter plane to bring its citizens home following the detention.
- Hyundai confirmed it will stick with the expansion plan first revealed at the plant’s March grand opening.
- The expansion adds 200,000 units of annual production capacity over the next three years.
- Total production capacity will jump from 300,000 to 500,000 vehicles annually by 2028.
- The plant will build 10 different electric and hybrid models by 2028, up from two currently rolling off the line.
- The expansion includes a new mid-sized pickup truck for Hyundai.
- Hyundai currently employs 3,129 people at the Ellabell site.
- Hyundai commits to reaching 8,500 workers by 2031, adding 3,000 new jobs.
- Georgia promises $2.1 billion in tax incentives, which Hyundai must unlock by reaching the 8,500 worker threshold by 2031.
- By 2030, Hyundai plans to build more than 80% of U.S.-sold vehicles domestically.
- American content in Hyundai's U.S. vehicles will jump from 60% to 80%.
- Rivian broke ground on a separate $5 billion EV plant east of Atlanta that will eventually produce 400,000 vehicles annually starting in 2028.
- Georgia has attracted 22 EV-related facilities since the Inflation Reduction Act passed, representing $24.4 billion in investment and 26,400 jobs.
- Governor Brian Kemp described Georgia's positioning as the 'electric mobility capital of America.'
- President Trump posted on Truth Social calling for foreign companies to legally bring skilled workers while defending ICE's actions at the plant.
- South Korean President Lee Jae Myung warned the raid could make Korean companies hesitant about U.S. investments.
- South Korean Foreign Minister Cho Hyun fielded angry questions from lawmakers demanding retaliation.
- America currently lacks enough workers who know how to build EV batteries at scale, exposing a workforce gap for foreign-owned facilities.
