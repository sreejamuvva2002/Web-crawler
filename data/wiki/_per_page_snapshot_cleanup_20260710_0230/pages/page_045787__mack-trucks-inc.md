# Mack Trucks, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 1900-07-26

**Source:** [Mack Trucks - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mack_Trucks)  ·  `page_045787`

## Facts

- Mack Trucks, Inc. is an American truck manufacturing company and a former manufacturer of buses and trolley buses.
- Founded in 1900 as the Mack Brothers Company, it adopted its present name in 1922.
- Since 2000, Mack Trucks has been a subsidiary of Volvo, which purchased Mack and its former parent company Renault Véhicules Industriels.
- Headquarters moved to Greensboro, North Carolina in 2009.
- Key people include Stephen Roy (President).
- Products include heavy and medium-duty trucks.
- Manufacturing facilities are located at the Lehigh Valley Operations facility / Macungie Assembly Operations Plant in Macungie, Pennsylvania.
- Mack Trucks is the fourth-largest employer in the Lehigh Valley region as of 2024.
- Mack vehicles are produced in Lower Macungie, Pennsylvania, and Salem, Virginia.
- Powertrain products are produced in its Hagerstown, Maryland plant, which also produces and designs all Mack engines, including Mack Defense engines used in tanks and other military applications.
- In 2021, Mack completed an upgrade of its facility with $84 million in funding.
- In July 2021, US President Joe Biden visited the Mack Trucks plant in Pennsylvania, where he was shown Mack's fully electric Class 8 truck, the Mack LR Electric.
- In July 2021, Mack Trucks said it would hire 400 more employees at its facility in Lower Macungie, adding to a 2,500 overall workforce.
- In August 2021, it was reported that Mack had to suspend "production periodically" in July 2021 due to chip shortages.
- A 2008 restructuring plan included relocating the head office, product development, and most support/purchasing functions to Greensboro, North Carolina in 2009.
- The 2008 restructuring plan also included assembly of all produced Mack highway vehicles in Macungie, Pennsylvania, starting in 2008.
- Mack's testing facility in Allentown, Pennsylvania was converted into a "customer demonstration and reception center" in 2010.
- The company maintains a remanufacturing center in Middletown, Pennsylvania.
- Parts for right-hand-drive vehicles are produced in Brisbane, Australia for worldwide distribution.
- Assembly for South America is performed in Macungie alongside North American trucks.
- In October 2010, Mack announced that a version of its Terrapro Cabover would run on natural gas using a Cummins Westport engine.
- In March 2008, Mack introduced the Titan, a heavy duty model with a 16-liter big-block MP10 engine producing 515, 565, and 605 horsepower.
- In 2014, Mack offered three engine series: the 11 L MP 7, 13 L MP8, and 16 L MP10, with power ranging from 325hp to 605hp and torque from 1,200lb⋅ft to 2,060lb⋅ft.
- Mack is one of the top producers in the vocational and on-road vehicle market, class 8 through class 13.
- On April 8, 2025, Mack announced a brand new class 8 tractor, the Pioneer, in Brooklyn, New York.
