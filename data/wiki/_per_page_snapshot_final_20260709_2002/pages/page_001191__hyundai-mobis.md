# Hyundai Mobis

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2024-11-19

**Source:** [Hyundai Mobis Outlines Value-Up Strategy at CEO Investor Day - fintechfilter](https://fintechfilter.com/hyundai-mobis-outlines-value-up-strategy-at-ceo-investor-day/)  ·  `page_001191`

## Facts

- Hyundai Mobis is aiming for an average annual revenue growth of 8% and an operating margin of 5~6% by 2027.
- The company targets increasing its global automaker customer share in auto component manufacturing from the current 10% to 40% by 2033.
- Hyundai Mobis plans to achieve a Total Shareholder Return (TSR) of over 30% by leveraging leading technological competitiveness and improving business structure centered on profitability.
- The company is strengthening high-value lineups such as electronic braking systems (EMB) and steering systems (SBW) that utilize electrical signals to attain a 10% global chassis safety market share by 2030.
- Hyundai Mobis hosted its 2024 CEO Investor Day on November 19 at the Fairmont Hotel in Yeouido, Seoul, with investors, analysts, and credit-rating agents in attendance.
- Lee Gyusuk serves as President and CEO of Hyundai Mobis and unveiled the company’s mid-to-long-term growth direction and comprehensive strategies.
- The company declared its intention to raise its average annual revenue growth rate to over 8% by 2027, marking the company’s 50th anniversary.
- Hyundai Mobis plans to expand its current TSR of 20% to over 30% within the next three years as part of a balanced and proactive shareholder return policy.
- As part of the shareholder return initiative, Hyundai Mobis revealed plans to cancel its currently held treasury shares over the next three years.
- The company's strategy to secure market-leading technological competitiveness is grounded in an efficient response to the Software-Driven Vehicles (SDV) mobility trend and electrification.
- Hyundai Mobis plans to consolidate its leadership in the electrification sector by proactively developing optimized products for Extended Range Electric Vehicles (EREV) and Economical Electric Powertrain (e-PT) systems.
- The EREV product is currently undergoing design verification and evaluation, with mass production targeted for the end of 2026.
- Hyundai Mobis intends to develop an economical 120kW e-PT system by next year, which will be priced at approximately 70% of the current 160kW systems dominating the electric vehicle market.
- The development of the 120kW e-PT system targets concentrated efforts in small EV-centric markets such as Europe and India.
- A 250kW e-PT system for large EVs is in the final stages of development, with the company actively pursuing orders from premium global automakers.
- Hyundai Mobis focuses on providing innovative hardware and software solutions, such as integrated control platforms and unified infotainment systems, to enable flexible responses to SDV demands.
- The company plans to optimize its business portfolio by categorizing it into growth businesses (electrification and electronics) and stabilization businesses (modules, chassis, safety, lamps, and service components).
- Hyundai Mobis announced a commitment to ESG management aiming to realize mobility through responsible innovation and clean technology.
- The company plans to achieve a 35% renewable energy transition rate by 2025.
- Hyundai Mobis will increase the sustainability audit rate of its manufacturing sites to 100% by 2025 and its component sites by 2027.
- From 2025 to 2027, Hyundai Mobis will disclose the results of external evaluations related to board management to expand communication with stakeholders.
