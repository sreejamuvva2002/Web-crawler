# Kia Motors Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Georgia at center of Kia’s EV campaign with new electric SUV](https://www.ajc.com/news/breaking-kia-to-manufacture-new-electric-suv-at-georgia-plant/URVZWPRGWND37NEHVXV7A2JTB4/)  ·  `page_000296`

## Facts

- Kia unveiled a new electric vehicle Wednesday, which the automaker says it will manufacture at its existing Georgia factory in West Point.
- The EV9 will be the Korean automaker’s first three-row electric SUV, mirroring the company’s gas-powered West Point-built Telluride model.
- The new EV was shown off Wednesday for the first time in North America at the 2023 New York International Auto Show.
- Kia says the EV9 will go on sale in the U.S. by the end of this year, and the model will be built in Georgia starting in 2024.
- Sean Yoon, president and CEO, Kia North America and Kia America, stated: “With the launch of the EV9, we are incorporating our experience in producing brilliantly designed, best-in-class SUVs with our deep expertise in developing the industry’s leading EVs.”
- Sean Yoon described the EV9 as a credible, high-value SUV that encapsulates Kia’s approach to bringing innovative, consumer-focused products to market with a serious commitment to electrification.
- Kia announced a $5.5 billion investment in Georgia for its Kia Motors Innovation Hub and a partnership with Savannah State University.
- The EV9 model is expected to create between 3 million to 5 million jobs.
- Kia announced a $12 billion investment for its Georgia EV plant construction on December 5, 2022.
- A California-based EV charging network operator is part of the plant's supply chain for electric vehicle components.
- The West Point plant will manufacture both the gas-powered Telluride and the new EV9.
- Kia is exploring facility expansion and maximizing local manufacturing talent for career opportunities in the region.
