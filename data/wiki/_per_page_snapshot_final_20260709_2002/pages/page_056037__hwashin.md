# Hwashin

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-10-31

**Source:** [New Hwashin Plant Promises To Bring 450 Jobs to Middle Georgia](https://thegeorgiasun.com/business-economy/new-hwashin-plant-promises-to-bring-450-jobs-to-middle-georgia/)  ·  `page_056037`

## Facts

- Governor Brian Kemp announced that automotive parts supplier Hwashin will set up a new manufacturing facility in Dublin.
- The new facility will create 460 jobs.
- Hwashin will invest more than $176 million in Laurens County.
- Hwashin is a Tier-1 supplier known for chassis and body parts.
- Production at the new Georgia facility is scheduled to begin by late 2025.
- The facility will be located at the Georgia Highway 257 Rail Served Site.
- The site is the state's first Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development Select site.
- Hwashin currently operates production facilities across Asia, South America, and the United States.
- Hwashin supplies chassis components to Hyundai, Kia, and other OEMs.
- The new Georgia facility will primarily focus on producing chassis components for Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America and Kia Georgia.
- In fiscal year 2023, Georgia saw a 324 percent increase in job creation in the automotive industry compared to FY21.
- The project is part of Georgia's broader aim to become a hub for the electric vehicle industry.
- The establishment of the plant is expected to impact the Dublin-Laurens County community with job creation and potential growth in housing and retail.
