# Macon-Bibb County Industrial Authority

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-02-28

**Source:** [Macon | Bibb County: Forward Together - Georgia Trend Magazine](https://www.georgiatrend.com/2024/02/28/macon-bibb-county-forward-together/)  ·  `page_053111`

## Facts

- Korean automotive supplier Boogook Industries plans to open at the former Brown & Williamson Tobacco facility in 2024, creating 80 full time jobs.
- The Macon-Bibb County Industrial Authority (MBCIA) reports that the county has seen almost $2 billion in private investment since 2017.
- The 120-acre Airport North Industrial Park was certified as Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD), allowing businesses to hit the ground running and save time and money.
- Industrial Realty Group purchased the 2 million-square-foot former Brown & Williamson Tobacco property in 2021 and created a multitenant option for MBCIA to market.
- The former Brown & Williamson Tobacco facility was once Macon’s largest employer before its redevelopment.
- Greater Macon Chamber of Commerce launched the Greater Career Works digital platform to assist employers with hiring and help job seekers explore careers, registering 13,000 users over the past year.
- The chamber developed Future Workforce Fridays, a program livestreamed every other Friday into middle and high school classrooms to make kids aware of job possibilities.
- Bibb County schools executive director Cassandra Washington used a $15,000 grant to launch the CD2 program, training counselors to connect students with local industrial parks and career pathways.
- The chamber dove into workforce development in 2022 to address the need for a skilled labor pool to sustain incoming industrial opportunities.
- Bibb County schools hopes through various workforce programs to touch at least 2,500 students a year and increase the number of local people going into community industries.
