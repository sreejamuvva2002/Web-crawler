# SK Innovation

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-03-31

**Source:** [SK Innovation's Georgia plants will make batteries for 300,000 EVs annually](https://www.greencarreports.com/news/1129698_sk-innovation-s-georgia-plants-will-make-batteries-for-300-000-evs-annually)  ·  `page_000562`

## Facts

- SK Innovation estimates that a pair of factories on a common site in Commerce, Georgia, will build batteries for 300,000 electric cars annually once full capacity is reached.
- SK Innovation announced its first hires for the Georgia site.
- SK Innovation planned to create 1,000 jobs by 2021 at the Georgia site.
- SK Innovation planned to create over 2,600 jobs by 2024 at the Georgia site.
- An SK Innovation press release stated that the two stages of plant construction would make Georgia one of the largest hubs of EV battery manufacturing in the world.
- An SK Innovation press release stated that the project constitutes one of the largest economic projects in Georgia's history.
- SK Innovation recently completed the exterior of its first factory at the Commerce site.
- SK Innovation broke ground on its second factory at the Commerce site.
- The Commerce site is located about 70 miles northeast of Atlanta.
- The first factory is scheduled to begin initial operations in 2021.
- The first factory is scheduled to scale up to mass production in 2022.
- The second factory will begin mass-producing batteries in 2023.
- Construction and hiring are proceeding amidst a legal dispute with rival Korean battery maker LG Chem.
- LG Chem has alleged trade-secret violations by SK Innovation.
- LG Chem reportedly is seeking to block the factories.
- Volkswagen is depending on the two Georgia factories for battery supply for its Tennessee assembly of the ID.4 crossover and another electric model.
- Volkswagen's battery supply from the Georgia factories will start in 2022.
- In documents related to the legal dispute with LG Chem, Ford indicated it would use SK Innovation battery cells from Georgia in its F-150 Electric pickup truck.
- Kia currently uses SK Innovation cells in some of its products, including its Niro EV.
- The SK Innovation cells used by Kia were the source of the dispute with LG Chem.
- LG Chem claimed that the SK Innovation cells used by Kia were essentially knockoffs of its technology.
- Hyundai, Kia's parent company, recently announced a partnership with SK to look at the entire battery ecosystem.
- The Hyundai-SK partnership includes looking at leasing models for battery packs and second-life uses.
