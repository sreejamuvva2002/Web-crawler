# Aurubis Richmond

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2023-11-09

**Source:** [First Lady of the United States visits Aurubis Richmond, the first secondary smelter for complex recycling materials in North America](https://www.aurubis.com/en/media/press-releases/press-releases-2023/first-lady-of-the-united-states-visits-aurubis-richmond-the-first-secondary-smelter-for-complex-recycling-materials-in-north-america)  ·  `page_017331`

## Facts

- Aurubis AG is a leading global provider of non-ferrous metals and one of the largest copper recyclers worldwide.
- First Lady Jill Biden visited Aurubis Richmond's new site in Augusta (Richmond County), Georgia.
- The company is investing around US$ 700 million to build the first secondary smelter for complex recycling materials in North America.
- Commissioning of the secondary smelter is planned for the second half of the coming year.
- An expansion of the plant is already under construction.
- The expanded plant will allow Aurubis Richmond to process a total of 180,000 tons of complex recycling materials per year as of 2026.
- Complex recycling materials processed include printed circuit boards and insulated copper cable.
- Dr. Biden visited as part of the Biden-Harris Administration’s Investing in America agenda.
- Augusta is one of five focus regions – known as Workforce Hubs – where a large number of jobs for skilled workers are to be created as a result of significant federal and private-sector investments.
- Aurubis COO Multimetal Recycling Inge Hofkens stated the plant creates long-term, secure jobs for more than 200 people in an industry strategically important to the US.
- The metals returned to circulation are essential to the mobility and energy transition and an important contribution to the electrical vehicle ecosystem.
- This project fits perfectly with Georgia’s Electric Mobility and Innovation Alliance initiative.
- The Workforce Hub initiative supports Aurubis's commitment to training workers for the challenging tasks in its secondary smelter.
- Aurubis has pledged a community benefit agreement with the Technical Colleges in Augusta and Aiken (South Carolina) as well as with the Richmond County School System.
- As part of this agreement, Aurubis will invest more than US$ 200,000 in scholarships and job training programs for young workers over the next five years.
- The company actively promotes women in the metal industry with its international Women4Metals initiative.
- Georgia offers ideal conditions for tapping the great growth potential of the U.S. recycling market.
- Increasing awareness for sustainability and a significant jump in collection volumes are generating roughly 6 million tons of recyclable, metal-bearing materials in the U.S. every year.
- This volume is a main driver for Aurubis's investment decision.
- The goal is to establish a reliable and sustainable supply chain by returning more of these recycling materials to local cycles.
- Experts estimate that the recycling volume in North America will increase by an average of 5% annually in the coming years.
- Georgia offers a pro-business climate, excellent infrastructure including logistics, and a stable energy supply at competitive prices.
- Aurubis CEO Roland Harings emphasized support from Georgia State officials and opportunities to contribute expertise and become a multi-metal recycling pioneer in the U.S.
- The Inflation Reduction Act offers great potential to accelerate Aurubis's future U.S. growth and investment, helping position the Southeast as a leading ecosystem for EV and multi-metal recycling.
- Aurubis is in a strong financial position and ready to invest more in the U.S. in keeping with its 'Metals for Progress: Driving Sustainable Growth' strategy.
