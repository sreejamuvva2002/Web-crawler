# Saft America Battery Manufacturing Facility

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Saft America Battery Manufacturing Facility | Haskell](https://www.haskell.com/project/saft-america-battery-manufacturing-facility/)  ·  `page_000926`

## Facts

- Saft is a leading lithium-ion battery developer and manufacturer.
- Haskell combined manufacturing, administrative, and support areas into a smart, sustainable structure for the facility.
- The facility produces 4,800,000 cells annually.
- The facility includes a two-story administrative office.
- The facility includes clean/dry rooms class 1,000 (ISO Class 6), certified with -40ºC and -27ºC return dew points.
- The facility includes coating ovens, formation area (charging) and open circuit voltage testing area, final assembly area.
- The facility includes electrical, chillers and air compressor rooms, maintenance and support areas, and shipping and receiving docks.
- The Saft plant’s sustainable design elements earned LEED silver certification.
- The facility features photovoltaic electricity generation.
- The facility features stormwater management.
- The facility features a reduced heat island effect.
- The facility features reduced parking.
- The facility uses captured rainwater.
- The facility features fundamental refrigerant management.
- The facility features high-efficiency HVAC equipment.
- LEED Silver certification was achieved with 37 points under v2.2.
- Water conservation is achieved through captured rainwater and low-flow fixtures, allowing a 100% reduction in irrigation.
- Energy conservation reduced energy use by 18%.
- Alternative transportation features include bicycle racks, showers, lockers, changing facilities, and preferred parking for high efficiency vehicles.
- 26% of building materials came from recycled sources.
- Nearly 41% of building materials were produced regionally.
- Many operations materials are recycled on-site.
- The photovoltaic system is the largest private photovoltaic installation in the Southeast at 1-MW (mega-watt) of power.
- The manufacturing facility size is 235,200 SF.
- Haskell team members worked closely with process and commissioning subject matter experts to understand lithium-ion construction needs.
- Haskell challenged Saft and Jacksonville city officials to ensure safe and viable structures were produced.
