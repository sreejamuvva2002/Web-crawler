# Commonwealth of Pennsylvania

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Alternative Fuels Data Center: Electricity Laws and Incentives in Pennsylvania](https://afdc.energy.gov/fuels/laws/ELEC?state=pa)  ·  `page_003273`

## Facts

- EVs that are subject to the annual EV registration fee are exempt from the Alternative Fuels Tax in Pennsylvania.
- The Pennsylvania Department of Environmental Protection (DEP) AFV Program offers rebates to assist eligible residents with the cost of purchasing or leasing new or qualifying pre-owned alternative fuel vehicles, including all-electric vehicles (EVs), plug-in hybrid electric vehicles (PHEVs), compressed natural gas (CNG) vehicles, electric motorcycles, and propane vehicles.
- Applicants for the DEP AFV rebate program must meet income eligibility requirements and the eligible AFV purchase price cannot exceed $45,000.
- Rebate amounts under the DEP AFV program are $3,000 for new or pre-owned EVs, $1,500 for new or pre-owned PHEVs, and $500 for new or pre-owned CNG, propane, or electric motorcycles.
- An additional rebate of $1,000 is available for all applicants that meet the low-income requirement as defined by the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services.
- DEP AFV rebate applications must be received within six months of vehicle purchase and are awarded on a first-come, first-served basis.
- The Alternative Fuels Incentive Grant (AFIG) Program provides financial assistance for innovative, advanced fuel and vehicle technology projects in Pennsylvania.
- AFIG program selection favors projects that result in product commercialization and the expansion of Pennsylvania companies.
- Eligible AFIG applicants include school districts, municipal authorities, political subdivisions, non-profits, corporations, limited liability companies, or partnerships incorporated or registered in the Commonwealth.
- AFIG grants can cover incremental cost expenses for retrofitting vehicles to operate on alternative fuels, purchasing alternative fuel vehicles, installing fleet- or home-refueling equipment, or performing research, training, development, and demonstration of new applications or next-phase technology related to alternative fuel vehicles.
- The Pennsylvania Department of Environmental Protection (DEP) administers the Pennsylvania State Clean Diesel Grant Program for diesel emission reduction projects.
- DEP Clean Diesel grants are funded by Pennsylvania’s portion of the Volkswagen Environmental Mitigation Trust and the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency’s Diesel Emission Reduction Act (DERA) Program.
- The DEP offers Heavy-Duty Emission Reduction Grants to repower or replace ferries, tugboats, and freight switcher locomotives with any new U.S. EPA or California Air and Resource Board-certified diesel, alternative fuel, or all-electric equivalent.
- The DEP provides Off-Road Electric Equipment Grants for the replacement of airport ground support equipment, forklifts, and port cargo handling equipment with an all-electric equivalent.
- All funds for the DEP Off-Road Electric Equipment Grants have been awarded.
- Pennsylvania’s NEVI Formula Program requires the Pennsylvania Department of Transportation (PennDOT) to submit an annually updated EV Infrastructure Deployment Plan for fiscal years 2022 through 2026.
- The submitted NEVI plans must describe how the state intends to distribute funds, outline physical and cybersecurity strategies, and include a Community Engagement Outcomes Report.
- Alternative fuels used to propel vehicles of any kind on public highways in Pennsylvania are taxed at a rate determined on a gasoline gallon equivalent basis.
- EV owners in Pennsylvania must pay an annual fee of $200 in 2025 and $250 in 2026, while plug-in hybrid electric vehicle owners must pay an annual fee equal to 25% of the EV fee.
- Pennsylvania signed a memorandum of understanding with multiple other states to support the deployment of medium- and heavy-duty zero emission vehicles (MHD ZEVs) through a Multi-State ZEV Task Force.
- In July 2022, the Multi-State ZEV Task Force published an action plan that includes limiting all new MHD vehicle sales in signatory states to ZEVs by 2050.
- The MHD ZEV deployment action plan aims to accelerate ZEV deployment and ensure it benefits disadvantaged communities.
- A Neighborhood Electric Vehicle (NEV) is defined as a four-wheeled electric vehicle that has a maximum speed greater than 20 miles per hour but not more than 25 mph.
- NEVs must comply with safety standards established in Title 49 of the U.S. Code of Federal Regulations, section 571.500, and drivers must maintain a defined level of insurance coverage for all registered vehicles.
- In Pennsylvania, a person, corporation, or entity that owns or operates an EV charging station open to the public is not defined as a public utility.
- Each electric distribution company in Pennsylvania must address third-party owned and operated EV charging stations in its tariff.
- Pennsylvania state agencies must replace 25% of their passenger car fleets with EVs by 2025.
- State agencies must collectively reduce all energy consumption by 3% annually, achieving a 21% reduction from a 2017 baseline by 2025.
- The Governor’s Green Government Council was reestablished to work with state agencies to oversee the development and implementation of procedures to reduce greenhouse gas emissions and energy usage.
