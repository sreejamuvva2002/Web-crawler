# Battery Resourcers

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2022-01-05

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp: Battery Resourcers to Open North America’s Largest Lithium-ion Battery Recycling Facility in Georgia, Create 150 Jobs | Governor Brian P. Kemp Office of the Governor](https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases/2022-01-05/gov-kemp-battery-resourcers-open-north-americas-largest-lithium-ion)  ·  `page_000074`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Battery Resourcers, a lithium-ion battery recycling and engineered materials startup, will invest $43 million to open its first commercial-scale battery recycling plant in Covington.
- The processing facility will become the largest of its kind in North America when fully operational in August 2022.
- The facility will create at least 150 jobs in Newton County.
- Governor Kemp stated that Battery Resourcers is the latest job creator to move to Georgia due to the state's leadership position in the electric vehicle manufacturing space.
- The strategically located facility will have the capacity to process 30,000 metric tons of discarded lithium-ion batteries and scrap annually.
- The annual processing capacity is equivalent to 70,000 vehicle batteries per year.
- The recycling process will return battery-grade lithium, cobalt, and nickel back into the battery supply chain.
- Major brand investors in Battery Resourcers include Jaguar Land Rover, Orbia, TDK, TRUMPF Group, Doral Energy, and Hitachi.
- Recycling batteries is one component of Battery Resourcers' technology and marks a vital step in the company's strategic expansion plans.
- Battery Resourcers has engineered a process to turn recyclables back into critical battery materials, specifically nickel-manganese-cobalt cathodes.
- The produced nickel-manganese-cobalt cathode materials are sold back to battery manufacturers.
- Michael O’Kronley, CEO and Director of Battery Resourcers, stated the company is bringing 150 tech jobs to the Covington area.
- O’Kronley noted that automotive manufacturers currently have very few options for responsible and cost-effective disposal of discarded batteries and scrap.
- The company aims to provide a sustainable solution that minimizes the need for mining and returns valuable, battery-grade materials back into the lithium-ion supply chain.
- Battery Resourcers will operate out of an existing 154,000-square-foot building located at 9172 Industrial Drive Northeast in Covington.
- Operations are expected to begin in spring 2022.
- The company has already begun hiring for the facility.
- Lanier Sims, Chairman of the Newton County Industrial Development Authority, welcomed Battery Resourcers to the area's business mix.
- The Newton County Industrial Development Authority is partnering with Battery Resourcers and the State of Georgia to make the I-20 corridor a business and technology-focused hub.
- Mayor Steve Horton stated that Battery Resourcers is an outstanding addition to Covington's business ecosystem and will complement regional businesses in Georgia.
- When fully operational, the facility will be located in the heart of Covington.
- Metro Atlanta Chamber President and CEO Katie Kirkpatrick stated that Metro Atlanta is an ideal location for EV and clean energy businesses due to local university and technical college engineering/manufacturing expertise, market proximity, and rapid industry growth.
- Project Manager Virginia Sengewald represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce division on the competitive project.
- The project was developed in partnership with the Newton County Industrial Development Authority, the Metro Atlanta Chamber, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia Power.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that battery recycling has become a vital part of the supply chain as the EV industry grows.
- Wilson emphasized the importance of cutting reliance on unstable areas of the globe for battery materials.
- The announcement follows Rivian's recent announcement to make Georgia the location of its second EV manufacturing facility and campus.
