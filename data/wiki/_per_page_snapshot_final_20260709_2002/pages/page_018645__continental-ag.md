# Continental AG

## Facts

- German auto supplier Continental announced a €4-billion (RM18.7-billion) deal to sell its ContiTech division to US investment group Lone Star Funds as part of a major restructuring.
- The group has been slimming down operations to focus on its traditional tyre business amid a crisis in the wider European auto sector.
- Continental could receive up to €250 million more in coming years depending on the business’s performance.
- The deal is expected to be finalised by the end of 2026.
- Funds from the sale will pay down debt, with around €2.5 billion distributed to shareholders.
- Continental last year spun off a division focused on car components including braking systems and sensors as a separate entity called Aumovio.
- The group has announced thousands of job cuts in recent years alongside other auto suppliers and manufacturers in Germany.
- The European auto sector is facing fierce Chinese competition, weak demand in key markets, and a troubled shift to electric vehicles.
