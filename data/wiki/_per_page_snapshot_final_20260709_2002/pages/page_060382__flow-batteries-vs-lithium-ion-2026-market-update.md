# Flow Batteries vs Lithium-Ion 2026 Market Update

**Type:** research_center  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Flow Batteries vs Lithium-Ion 2026: Cost, LCOS & Grid Storage Economics | Energy Solutions Intelligence](https://energy-solutions.co/articles/sub/flow-batteries-grid-storage-vs-lithium)  ·  `page_060382`

## Facts

- The stationary storage market has shattered early-2026 forecasts.
- Lithium-ion (dominated by LFP chemistry) has officially surpassed 400 GWh in cumulative global utility-scale installations.
- Lithium-ion deployment is driven by an aggressive 150+ GWh build-out in China and IRA-backed deployment in the US.
- Flow battery deployments have stabilized at roughly 8 GWh.
- The flow battery pipeline has quadrupled as specific Long-Duration Energy Storage (LDES) tenders mandate non-lithium solutions capable of 8-12 hour discharge without degradation.
- Lithium-ion (specifically LFP) utterly dominates the 1-4 hour duration market.
- For 4-12 hour applications, flow batteries can cut lifetime Levelized Cost of Storage (LCOS) by 10-25% compared with lithium.
- Energy Solutions Intelligence benchmarked 80+ grid-scale projects using both chemistries across North America, Europe, and Asia updated to July 2026.
- Vanadium Redox Flow Batteries (VRFB) offer 20+ year lifespans with virtually zero electrolyte degradation.
- At durations beyond 6-8 hours, flow battery economics eclipse lithium-ion due to the ability to scale energy capacity simply by increasing tank size.
- High upfront CAPEX for flow batteries ranges from $260-$360/kWh for an 8h system.
- Flow batteries have lower round-trip efficiency (70-75%) compared with lithium-ion (88-92%).
- Targeted long-duration energy storage (LDES) mandates in California, the UK, and China are providing revenue certainty to commercialize large flow projects.
- Lithium-Ion stores energy in solid electrodes where power and energy are tightly coupled.
- Adding duration to lithium-ion systems requires adding more complete battery packs, including cells, BMS, and enclosures, making long-duration scaling linear and expensive.
- The solid lattice of lithium-ion degrades with every charge/discharge cycle.
- Flow Batteries (VRFB/Zn-Br) store energy in liquid electrolytes in external tanks pumped through a central cell stack.
- In flow batteries, power and energy are decoupled; sizing the cell stack determines power (kW) and tank/fluid volume determines energy (kWh).
- Falling lithium carbonate prices in 2024-2025 brought 4h Li-ion systems down significantly.
- Installed Cost (CAPEX) for Utility Lithium-Ion (LFP - 4h) is $220 - $320 / kWh.
- Round-trip Efficiency for Utility Lithium-Ion is 88% - 92%.
- Usable Lifetime Cycles for Utility Lithium-Ion are ~5,000 - 7,000 (Requires Augmentation).
- O&M Costs for Utility Lithium-Ion are $5 - $9 per kW-year.
- Installed Cost (CAPEX) for Vanadium Flow Battery (VRFB - 8h) is $260 - $360 / kWh.
- Round-trip Efficiency for VRFB is 70% - 78%.
- Usable Lifetime Cycles for VRFB are 15,000 - 20,000+ (No Cell Augmentation).
- O&M Costs for VRFB are $10 - $18 per kW-year.
- Lithium-ion has very high land footprint/energy density in compact containers.
- Flow batteries have low energy density requiring large external tanks.
- Lithium-ion carries high safety risk requiring HVAC and fire suppression.
- Flow batteries carry zero fire risk due to aqueous electrolyte.
- LCOS per Stored MWh for heavy cycle lithium-ion is baseline (1.0x).
- LCOS per Stored MWh for heavy cycle VRFB is ~0.75x - 0.90x.
- Invinity was formed by the merger of redT and Avalon.
- Invinity is a VRFB provider with major deployments across the UK, North America, and Australia.
- ESS pioneers an iron-based flow battery that eliminates vanadium cost volatility using abundant iron, salt, and water.
- A Japanese industrial giant built some of the earliest and largest VRFB projects in the world, including a massive 15MW/60MWh system in Hokkaido.
- CellCube is based in Austria and has over 130 deployments globally focusing on commercial and industrial microgrids.
- Tesla (Megapack), Fluence, Wärtsilä, and Sungrow utterly dominate global lithium-ion market share.
- Tesla's Megapack alone accounts for tens of GWh of capacity.
- The processing and refining of Lithium, Nickel, and Cobalt remain overwhelmingly concentrated in China.
- The US IRA (Inflation Reduction Act) has spurred domestic battery factories.
- Vanadium is primarily produced as a co-product of steel manufacturing.
- China, Russia, and South Africa control the majority of virgin vanadium production.
- Vanadium electrolyte can be leased and retains full residual value at the end of a 20-year project for recovery or resale.
- Iron-flow batteries rely on iron, salt, and water, offering supply chain sovereignty without reliance on critical mineral cartels.
- Lithium megapacks dominate California and Texas stationary storage markets.
- Flow battery pilot projects are scaling in China, the UK, and Australia.
