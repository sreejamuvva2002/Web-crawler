# Northwest Georgia Economic Development Region

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2023-03-29

**Source:** [Northwest Georgia: Steadily Pushing Forward - Georgia Trend Magazine](https://www.georgiatrend.com/2023/03/29/2023-economic-yearbook-northwest-georgia/)  ·  `page_020601`

## Facts

- The mountainous terrain of Northwest Georgia makes for a steep hill to climb in terms of developing this region.
- Billions of dollars in investment is headed for the hills this year in a variety of industries including solar, ecommerce, car manufacturing and product distribution.
- In the last year, this region has been grappling with workforce shortages and supply-chain delays that interrupted project plans.
- The region has exponential growth in clean-energy industries with two major electric vehicle operations that opened in the last two years.
- A $6.5 million facility from Turkish company TEKLAS opened in Gordon County.
- An $85 million facility from German company GEDIA opened in Whitfield County.
- The region is attractive to EV developers and traditional auto parts suppliers due to its proximity to car manufacturers in the state, such as Kia, and others in the Southeast such as BMW in South Carolina and Volkswagen in Tennessee.
- Kathy Johnson is president and CEO of the Gordon County Chamber of Commerce.
- Bartow County has seen billions of dollars in growth in green industries.
- SK On and Hyundai Motor Group announced in December 2022 that they will be investing $4 billion to $5 billion to develop an electric vehicle battery plant in Bartow County.
- The SK On and Hyundai Motor Group battery plant project will create more than 3,500 new jobs.
- The SK On and Hyundai Motor Group battery plant project is one of the largest economic development projects in state history.
- South Korean solar energy manufacturer Qcells announced a $2.5 billion expansion in Bartow County.
- The Qcells expansion will create 2,500 new jobs for Bartow County.
- Together, these two projects will boost Bartow’s employment population by more than 10%.
- Melinda Lemmon is executive director of the Cartersville-Bartow County Department of Economic Development.
- Duluth Trading Company established a distribution and fulfillment facility in Bartow County in 2022.
- Hyundai and SK On operate as a joint venture for their battery plant project.
- Shaw Industries in Whitfield County is the third-largest manufacturing facility in the state with more than 6,000 employees.
- Shaw Industries wrapped up a $20 million, three-phase expansion last year, creating an additional 55 jobs in Catoosa County.
- Mohawk Industries in Gordon County has 3,200 employees.
- Mohawk Industries is one of the largest floorcovering manufacturers in the country, producing carpeting, vinyl and hardwood flooring among other floorcovering materials.
- Mohawk Industries has called Calhoun home since 1992.
- Floorcovering companies in Gordon County are shifting to having in-house design and marketing roles for photography, videography, catalog design and print products.
- The recent TEKLAS site and the proximity to the I-75 corridor has helped attract other industries to Gordon County.
- Gordon County expects close to 15 million square feet of distribution facilities will be ready for move-in between Q4 of this year and Q1 of 2024.
- Pam Powers-Smith is president of the Rome-Floyd Chamber of Commerce.
- The Rome-Floyd Chamber of Commerce had almost 65 ribbon-cuttings last year.
- Floyd County is unveiling a workforce strategic plan for the community in 2023, which includes a new workforce website in partnership with Georgia Power that will connect employers with prospective employees.
- Many communities are putting new emphasis on infrastructure such as expanded broadband, highway access and workforce training to address labor shortages.
- Companies in Gordon County are looking at ways to address labor shortages from an educational standpoint, but also through automation and artificial intelligence resources.
- Catoosa County is preparing to unveil its Catoosa County College and Career Academy in August 2023, which will partner with area businesses to train high school students for local careers.
- Amy Jackson is president and CEO of the Catoosa County Chamber of Commerce.
- The Catoosa County College and Career Academy board has been working with area businesses for the last three years to form partnerships for internships and potential jobs upon graduation.
- More than 82% of the workforce in Catoosa County commutes out of the county to get to their jobs.
- Meghan Trusley is executive administrative assistant to the Catoosa County manager.
- Marianne Wood is executive director of the Polk County Chamber of Commerce.
- Chris Thomas is president and CEO of the Polk County Development Authority.
- Polk County has two college and career academies that it is trying to connect directly to major employers in the area, such as Parker Meggitt, Tip Top Poultry and HON.
- Parker Meggitt is an aircraft fuel-tank manufacturer located in Polk County.
- HON is an office chair manufacturer located in Polk County.
- Polk County has GRAD-certified 240 acres of industrial land that is seeing increased interest from EV manufacturers following the SK On and Hyundai development announcement.
- Chattooga County will spend much of this year rebuilding after significant flooding in late 2022.
- Cindy Rivers McGraw is executive director of the Chattooga County Chamber of Commerce.
- Gov. Brian Kemp called the late 2022 flooding in Chattooga County a 500-year storm when he visited the county last September.
- The late 2022 flooding forced dozens of businesses to close their doors in Chattooga County.
- Chattooga County has been able to help 25 households through local flood-relief grants.
- Blake Elsberry is Chattooga County sole commissioner.
- Chattooga County is developing affordable housing and opening the first freestanding rural emergency room in the state, which is an $18 million project.
- The 50-acre North Georgia Business Park in Catoosa is located directly off of I-75 and has room for two medium-sized industrial or manufacturing facilities.
- Dan Wright is Catoosa County manager.
- A new Love’s Travel Center is being built adjacent to the North Georgia Business Park site, which will create 50 to 60 jobs.
- Chattanooga-based White Oak Enterprises teamed with Ohio-based Tenby Partners to develop a Class A industrial park near Chattanooga that can accommodate up to 1.1 million square feet of built-out space.
- Cloud Springs I-75 Industrial Park is about 40 minutes away from the Appalachian Regional Port in Murray County.
- Jeff Londis is co-founder of White Oak Enterprises.
- The goal for the Cloud Springs I-75 Industrial Park development is to support the onshoring of industries, particularly in the EV space.
- Based on an economic impact study, White Oak Enterprises and Tenby Partners anticipate a capital investment of more than $100 million by the time the property is fully developed.
