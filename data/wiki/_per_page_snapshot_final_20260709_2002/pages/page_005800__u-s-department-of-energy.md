# U.S. Department of Energy

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [U.S. Department of Energy Awards $2.7 Billion to Restore American Uranium Enrichment | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/articles/us-department-energy-awards-27-billion-restore-american-uranium-enrichment)  ·  `page_005800`

## Facts

- The U.S. Department of Energy announced $2.7 billion to strengthen domestic enrichment services over the next ten years.
- The funding announcement was made on January 5, 2026.
- The investment supports President Trump’s commitment to enhance energy security and reduce reliance on foreign suppliers.
- The historic investment expands U.S. capacity for low-enriched uranium (LEU).
- The investment jumpstarts new supply chains and innovations for high-assay low-enriched uranium (HALEU).
- The goal of the investment is to create American jobs and usher in the nation’s nuclear renaissance.
- Secretary of Energy Chris Wright stated that President Trump is catalyzing a resurgence in the nation’s nuclear energy sector to strengthen American security and prosperity.
- The Administration is committed to restoring a secure domestic nuclear fuel supply chain capable of producing the nuclear fuels needed to power the reactors of today and the advanced reactors of tomorrow.
- Last year, the DOE signed contracts with a total of six companies for LEU and HALEU enrichment that allowed them to bid on future work.
- The task order awards will transition the United States away from foreign sources of uranium and diversify the nation’s domestic fuel supply.
- Developing new domestic production capacity for LEU and HALEU ensures an adequate fuel supply is available to maintain operations of the nation’s 94 commercial reactors.
- The new capacity builds a strong base to supply future deployments of advanced nuclear reactors.
- To ensure accountability, the awards will be distributed to companies under a strict milestone approach.
- American Centrifuge Operating was awarded a $900 million task order to create domestic HALEU enrichment capacity.
- General Matter was awarded a $900 million task order to create domestic HALEU enrichment capacity.
- Orano Federal Services was awarded a $900 million task order to expand U.S. domestic LEU enrichment capacity.
- The DOE awarded an additional $28 million to Global Laser Enrichment to continue advancing next generation uranium enrichment technology for the nuclear fuel cycle.
- The $28 million award is the result of a competitive solicitation issued last December.
