# Volvo Car AB

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-05-26

**Source:** [Volvo Wins U.S. Clearance Over China-Tied Vehicle Ban - TT](https://www.ttnews.com/articles/volvo-us-china-vehicle-tech)  ·  `page_014180`

## Facts

- Volvo Car AB received Commerce Department authorization on May 26 to continue importing and selling connected passenger vehicles in the U.S.
- The authorization allows Volvo to avoid a U.S. ban on connected vehicles tied to China that would have taken effect with the 2027 model year.
- Volvo is majority-owned by China’s Zhejiang Geely Holding Group.
- Volvo builds vehicles at a plant in Charleston, S.C., where it has invested more than $1.3 billion.
- The company imports models from Sweden, where it is headquartered.
- Volvo started assembling one of its better-selling SUVs in Belgium last year in part due to the U.S. raising tariffs on cars manufactured in China.
- The U.S. ban on connected vehicles and related hardware and software technology with ties to China takes effect starting with the 2027 model year.
- The rule implementing the ban was finalized in January 2025, shortly before President Donald Trump began his second term in office.
- The ban rule went into effect in March of that year after Trump was inaugurated.
- U.S. officials stated the approval comes as Volvo awaits 2027 shipments and does not signal approvals for other Chinese-owned automakers.
- Volvo said the authorization followed constructive discussions with the U.S. Department of Commerce and other U.S. officials regarding governance, technology, and data security.
- With this specific authorization, Volvo Cars can continue its growth plans in the U.S., according to the company.
- Volvo Car CEO Håkan Samuelsson told Bloomberg earlier this year that it would be 'unthinkable' for Volvo to be banned from selling in the U.S.
- Samuelsson stated the company was working to receive approval and expected the Commerce Department to announce a decision by summer.
- In March, Samuelsson noted that the authorization process focused on demonstrating how data is handled and confirming that U.S. customer data is not transferred to China.
