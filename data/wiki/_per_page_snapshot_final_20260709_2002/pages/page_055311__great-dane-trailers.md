# Great Dane Trailers

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2010-10-22

**Source:** [Great Dane Trailers breaks ground on new Southeast plant](https://www.fleetequipmentmag.com/great-dane-trailers-breaks-ground-on-new-southeast-plant/)  ·  `page_055311`

## Facts

- Great Dane Trailers officially broke ground on the site of its new refrigerated trailer manufacturing plant in Statesboro, Ga.
- The groundbreaking ceremony included local business representatives, state and local political leaders, and company executives.
- Georgia Gov. Sonny Perdue, Statesboro Mayor Joe Brannen, and Bulloch County Commission Chairman Garrett Nevil hailed the cooperation among the state, local economic developers, and Great Dane as key reasons for the success of the site selection process.
- The 118-acre Statesboro site was chosen because it is located just northwest of Savannah and remains close to where Great Dane was founded in 1900.
- Bill Crown, president and CEO of CC Industries and CEO of Great Dane Limited Partnership, stated that continuing Great Dane’s legacy by keeping it close to the company’s deep roots in Savannah was just as important in the site selection.
- The plant will encompass 450,000 sq. ft., including office and show room space.
- The facility will have the capacity to produce more than 5,000 trailers annually.
- The plant will employ more than 400 people.
- Phill Pines, president and COO of Great Dane Trailers, stated that the refrigerated trailers built there will be complex and detailed products.
- The new reefer plant will feature the latest technology, making it the most modern, efficient plant in the world.
- Construction is expected to get underway later this year to bring the plant into production in 2012.
