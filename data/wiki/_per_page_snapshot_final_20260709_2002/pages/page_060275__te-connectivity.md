## Facts

- TE Connectivity is a Tier 2 supplier to global automotive supply chains.
- TE Connectivity operates a manufacturing plant in the North-Western Hungarian city of Esztergom.
- The Hungarian facility employs more than a thousand people.
- Passive electrical connectors and connector housings are manufactured at the facility using plastic injection moulding.
- These products are used in vehicles from leading auto makers including the Volkswagen Group, PSA, and Mercedes.
- In 2019, the Hungarian plant experienced material handling problems because existing systems reached their capability limits.
- Labour shortages worsened the situation as interest in low-skilled warehouse operator positions declined sharply.
- Industry 4.0-driven transformation is part of TE's corporate goals, prompting a decision to automate internal logistics using AMRs.
- TE turned its Hungarian plant into an industrial test environment to experiment with multiple parallel AMR and MRE solutions simultaneously.
- The company concluded that the MiR200 AMR combined with a TMC300 top module, cart, and docking station from ROEQ best suited its needs.
- TE's fleet consists of six MiR200s, most carrying raw materials and finished products between the warehouse and value streams.
- The plant operates as a semi-automatic system where employees launch missions and manually move carts connected to robots in production areas and warehouses.
- Gábor Miszler serves as the Continuous Improvement Analyst at TE.
- The ROEQ TMC300 top module, cart, and docking station provided a standardized solution easily integrated due to full compatibility with MiR robots and ROEQ Assist software.
- ROEQ Assist software is fully integrated into the robots' fleet management program, eliminating the need for employees to learn separate programs.
- Regularly occurring tasks can be pre-programmed, allowing missions to be launched quickly without special expertise.
- Employees were initially averse to robots but accepted the technology after recognizing its ability to make jobs easier.
- Two warehouse workers per shift learned to operate the robots at a basic level and correct simple errors like being stuck or lost.
- The patented locking mechanism of the carts allows precise and safe connection to both the robot and the docking station.
- ROEQ Assist software provides continuous tracking, allowing staff to check at any time which robot or dock a cart is connected to.
- Staff can easily disconnect carts from robots at mission endpoints in the production area and warehouse, then move them manually.
- ROEQ carts are remarkably stable and safe, maintaining a zero accident and zero property loss record for two years since deployment.
- The ROEQ cart solution increases AMR payload, improving overall efficiency and throughput of the material handling process.
- TE Connectivity purchased ROEQ's modules and carts after the outbreak of the Covid-19 epidemic.
- Gábor Schvamberger serves as the Logistics Analyst at TE.
- Travel restrictions prevented engineers from the Danish plant from traveling to Esztergom to assist with deployment.
- TE set up and coordinated the devices and programs themselves using easy implementation, detailed manuals, and remote support from the manufacturer and distributor.
- ROEQ top modules and equipment are extremely reliable, with quick support available for repairs or maintenance.
- The distributor and ROEQ engineers provided immediate assistance during an initial challenge, discovering the cause and providing a permanent technical solution.
- VFP Systems, the ROEQ partner in Hungary, supported TE during the acquisition of robotic solutions.
- András Dobner serves as the Head of Internal Logistics at VFP Systems.
- The TE Esztergom plant currently uses 3 ROEQ TMC300 top modules, 15 ROEQ 300E Easy-Pull-Out carts, and 10 easy locking docking stations for material handling tasks.
- The company is building a new production hall optimized for AMR characteristics to leverage fleet capabilities more effectively and increase AMR utilization.
- The payback time for the automation investment is estimated at around 2-3 years, requiring only half of the average payback time in logistics.
- TE Connectivity is a USD 14 billion technology and manufacturing company.
- TE Connectivity's products have supported the development of transport, industrial applications, and communication industries for more than 75 years.
