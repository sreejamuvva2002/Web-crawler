# Valeo

## Facts

- Valeo is a French global automotive supplier headquartered in Paris.
- The company supplies a wide range of products to automakers and the aftermarket.
- The Group employs 113,600 people in 29 countries worldwide.
- It operates 186 production plants, 66 R&D centers, and 15 distribution platforms.
- In 2018, Valeo's sales rose 4% to €19.1 billion.
- The company frequently ranks as France's leading patent filer.
- The company was founded in 1923 in Saint-Ouen, near Paris, originally as the Société Anonyme Française de Ferodo.
- In 1932, the company began producing clutches.
- In 1932, it was listed on the Paris Stock Exchange.
- The firm produced braking systems in 1961, thermal systems in 1962, and lighting and electrical systems between 1971-1978 and in 1984.
- International development began in the 1960s, expanding to Spain, Italy, Brazil (1974), the United States (1980), Tunisia (1984), South Korea and Turkey (1988), Mexico (1989), China (1994), India (1997), Central and Eastern Europe, and Egypt (2005), reaching 33 countries by the end of 2018.
- In May 1980, the company was renamed Valeo, which means "I am well" in Latin.
- The company acquired German automotive suppliers Peiker and Spheros in 2015, followed by FTE automotive in 2017.
- In the early 2000s, Valeo produced parking assistance systems using ultrasonic sensors.
- In 2017, Valeo presented a low-voltage prototype electric vehicle at the Consumer Electronics Show in Las Vegas.
- In February 2021, Valeo announced an investment of $5 million to open a new assembly plant in Bessemer, Alabama, expected to create 70 jobs.
- Since 2009, CEO Jacques Aschenbroich's strategy has focused on innovation linked to CO2 emission reduction, intuitive driving, and geographical expansion in high-growth potential countries like Asia and emerging markets.
- From 2009 to 2018, the number of employees increased from 45,000 to 113,600.
- From 2009 to 2018, the Group's sales rose from €7.5 billion to €19.1 billion with strong development in China.
- Valeo invests 11.8% of its sales in R&D and innovation.
- The company employs 17,900 research and development staff.
- In 2016 and 2017, the Group ranked as France's leading patent filer.
- In November 2025, Valeo announced its 'Elevate 2028' strategy.
- The strategy targets steadily increasing profit from 2022 onwards, generating higher cash from 2025 onwards, and returning to sales growth from 2027 onwards.
- The goal is to consolidate its position as a global leader in key car technologies across regions like China, India, and North America.
- The strategy fully aligns the company for a future of electrified, safer, and software-defined cars.
- The company is structured around four business groups: Comfort & Driving Assistance Systems, Powertrain Systems (automotive powertrain components and vehicle electrification), Thermal Systems, and Visibility Systems.
- Its Valeo Service activity supplies original equipment spares to automakers and replacement parts to the independent aftermarket.
- Original equipment sales by destination region are distributed as follows: Europe and Africa at 46%, Asia at 32%, North America at 20%, and South America at 2%.
- In 2006, Valeo, its directors, and its corporate medical function were indicted for homicide, unintentional injury, and failure to assist regarding former asbestos use that caused the death of hundreds of employees.
- The group has set up high health-security standards and thoroughly monitors employees and retired personnel.
- This policy was ratified in a national agreement with the French government in April 2009.
- The group secured four former industrial sites with asbestos-related issues.
