# South System Expansion 4 Project

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [$3 Billion Natural Gas Pipeline Expansion to Add 1.3 Bcf Capacity in Southeast Region](https://pgjonline.com/news/2025/january/3-billion-natural-gas-pipeline-expansion-to-add-13-bcf-capacity-in-southeast-region)  ·  `page_046936`

## Facts

- Kinder Morgan has proposed the $3 billion South System Expansion 4 project.
- The project aims to add 1.3 billion cubic feet per day of natural gas capacity across 279 miles in the Southeast.
- The expansion is intended to enhance energy supply for homes, businesses, and manufacturing.
- The project could be operational by 2028 or 2029, pending approval.
- An open house event for the initiative was held at Bethel Missionary Baptist Church in Tuskegee.
- The proposed route spans 279 miles, incorporating new pipelines alongside existing ones and enhancing compressor stations to maintain gas flow.
- Alabama Power is a part of this project and has indicated a need for additional natural gas capacity for the next 20 to 30 years.
- Allen Fore, Kinder Morgan’s vice president of public affairs, confirmed Alabama Power's involvement and needs.
- Kinder Morgan oversees approximately 3,000 miles of pipeline infrastructure across Alabama.
- The expansion will include parallel pipelines, known as loop lines, and upgrades to existing infrastructure.
- Landowners impacted by the project will receive compensation, with no relocations required.
