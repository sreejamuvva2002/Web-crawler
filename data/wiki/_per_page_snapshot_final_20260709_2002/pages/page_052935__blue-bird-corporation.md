# Blue Bird Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-07-15

**Source:** [Blue Bird in Georgia to expand EV bus production with $80m grant, create over 400 jobs](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/blue-bird-georgia-expand-ev-090000396.html)  ·  `page_052935`

## Facts

- Blue Bird Corporation in Macon received an $80 million grant to convert a prior manufacturing site in Fort Valley into a "state-of-the-art" electric school bus production site.
- The grant funding comes from the U.S. Department of Energy.
- The project will bring over 400 new manufacturing jobs to Middle Georgia.
- The conversion will take place at an approximately 600,000-square-foot facility.
- The federal grant is part of the U.S. administration's Domestic Automotive Manufacturing Conversion Grants program.
- The grants program represents a nearly $2 billion investment to convert long-standing automotive facilities to manufacturing electric vehicles, retain good-paying manufacturing jobs and bolster domestic supply chains.
- Blue Bird is one of 11 firms selected for the federal grant program.
- Phil Horlock serves as CEO of Blue Bird Corporation.
- Horlock stated that the company is delighted the DOE supported its conversion project over many other submitted applications.
- Horlock noted the grant will enable Blue Bird to reinvest in middle Georgia on a site that previously manufactured high-end motorhomes.
- Horlock added the grant will allow Blue Bird to expand its workforce and invest in a community integral to its history and continued success.
- Horlock indicated the company is looking forward to fully participating in the next phase of the negotiation process with the DOE.
- The facility will increase production of Type-D school buses to meet rising nationwide demand for clean student transportation.
- Type-D school buses require low maintenance costs, seat up to 87 passengers, and run up to 120 miles on a single charge.
- Blue Bird is recognized as the leader in electric school buses with nearly 2,000 zero-emission vehicles currently on the road.
- The new Fort Valley facility will allow Blue Bird to retain and expand its existing workforce of nearly 2,000 employees.
- U.S. Secretary of Energy Jennifer M. Granholm commented that losing jobs to foreign competition and a changing industry is very hard for manufacturing communities.
- Granholm stated that even as competitors invest heavily in electric vehicles, these grants ensure the automotive industry stays competitive in supporting communities and workforces.
