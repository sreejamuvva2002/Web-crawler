# Hyundai-LG EV Battery Plant

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hyundai, LG to build $4.3B battery plant near Savannah](https://www.ajc.com/news/breaking-hyundai-lg-to-build-43-billion-ev-plant-near-savannah/6RTYCW7645DKFABA6PCQRZPAUY/)  ·  `page_001575`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solutions announced plans late Thursday to spend $4.3 billion to build an EV battery manufacturing plant in Bryan County near Savannah.
- The project is described as the largest economic development project in Georgia's state history.
- The battery plant will be built adjacent to Hyundai’s massive electric vehicle “Metaplant” under construction in Ellabell.
- State officials say the project is part of a previously announced plan by Hyundai to spend $5.5 billion on the new complex and create 8,100 jobs.
- The battery plant will account for 3,000 of those jobs.
- Batteries made at the forthcoming factory will power Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis models produced at U.S. facilities.
- The South Korean-based companies made the announcement in a release.
- Georgia is positioning itself as a monolithic green hub for the largest electric vehicle manufacturing complex in the state's history.
- The project will receive incentives in exchange for creating local battery and electric vehicle supply chain partnerships.
- A Korean American Workers Association was established to create a new workforce development program.
- A supplier of local electric vehicle components will have access to all project resources and grants.
- The initiative aligns with the Biden Administration's push for domestic battery manufacturing and supply chain partnerships.
