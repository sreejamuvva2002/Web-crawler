# Daimler Truck North America (DTNA)

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-06

**Source:** [Daimler Truck North America Invests in Electric Vehicle Infrastructure and Engineering in Portland](https://evmagz.com/daimler-truck-north-america-invests-in-electric-vehicle-infrastructure-and-engineering-in-portland/)  ·  `page_053624`

## Facts

- Daimler Truck North America (DTNA) has announced significant investments in its headquarters complex on Swan Island in Portland, Oregon.
- The company is set to establish a new Electric Vehicle Supply Equipment (EVSE) training center adjacent to Electric Island.
- The new EVSE training center aims to accelerate the testing and development of charging infrastructure for commercial vehicles.
- The training center will serve as a resource center for the DTNA dealer network and community stakeholders.
- The training center is expected to cost around $3 million.
- Funding for the training center is partly provided through a forgivable loan from Prosper Portland, the city’s economic development agency.
- DTNA is constructing a new engineering facility covering approximately 110,000 ft² at the DTNA campus.
- The new engineering facility will consolidate testing activities and focus on research and development for emerging propulsion technologies, including battery electric and hydrogen-fueled vehicles.
- The State of Oregon is supporting the construction of the engineering facility with a $700,000 forgivable loan from the State’s Strategic Reserve fund.
- John O’Leary, President and CEO of DTNA, stated: “We appreciate the City and State’s support of our expansion plans to further promote the development and proper testing of advanced technology.”
- Portland City Commissioner Carmen Rubio praised DTNA’s investment, stating: “I am thrilled by DTNA’s decision to invest significant resources and continue to lead heavy-duty vehicle technology right here in Portland.”
- Portland has been a key location for DTNA, with recent milestones including the start of customer deliveries of the Freightliner eM2 electric truck.
- DTNA introduced the eCascadia in 2022.
- The company electrified its logistics at the facility towards the end of 2023.
