# Turner County, Georgia

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Incentives and Advantages](https://turnergrowth.com/incentives-and-advantages/)  ·  `page_047034`

## Facts

- Site Selection Magazine named Georgia the country’s #1 State for Business due to lucrative financial incentives.
- Turner County offers Georgia’s Top Tier 1 financial incentives, which are the highest the state offers.
- Costs of living and doing business are attractively low in Turner County.
- ROI is rapid and accrues benefits rapidly and for years to come for operations in Turner County.
- Custom-tailored incentives, a custom-trained workforce, financing, and fast-track permitting are available to help organizations succeed.
- The Tier 1 Job Tax Credit offers up to $4,000 per qualified job created, per year, for up to 5 years.
- Creating a minimum of two jobs allows a company to offset up to 100% of their annual state corporate income tax liability using the “Payroll Withholding” option.
- Any leftover Job Tax Credits can be carried forward for up to 10 years from the time the job was created.
- The Freeport Exemption provides a 100% ad valorem tax exemption on inventories for manufacturers, distributors, wholesalers and warehouse operations as defined by Georgia law.
- Three classes of exempt personal property include: (1) manufacturer’s raw materials and goods-in-process inventory; (2) manufacturer’s finished goods held by the original manufacturer; and (3) warehouse, distribution or manufacturing firms with finished goods destined to be shipped outside of Georgia.
- Bond Financing is issued through the Adel Industrial Development Authority, providing tax-exempt and taxable revenue bonds for low-interest financing of real estate, construction and manufacturing equipment.
- Land price from the Turner County Development Authority is determined by the total capital investment of the project and the number of jobs to be created.
- An Ad valorem Tax Incentive/PILOT program may be available to qualifying companies as an incentive for job creation and capital investment.
- Turner County is one of the few Constitutional Authorities in Georgia, giving it more flexibility with incentives than traditional authorities.
- Fast Track Permitting provides a streamlined and simplified permitting process to accommodate developing and expanding businesses.
- Projects may qualify for a Build to Suit/Lease Back Arrangement based on capital investment and jobs created.
- Low-cost utilities feature competitive rates enhanced by “customer choice” of providers for users requiring 900 kw loads or larger.
- Qualifying companies may be eligible for Infrastructure Financial Assistance.
- Georgia’s corporate tax rate is a flat 6%.
- Georgia’s state and local taxpayer burden ranks as the 16th lowest in the nation.
- Municipally sourced utilities keep operational expenses significantly lower while maintaining high productivity.
- Turner County maintains a right-to-work, ready-to-work labor force that keeps quality high and costs low.
- Georgia’s cost of living is 91.5% of the U.S. average.
- Turner County’s cost of living is 81.7% of the national average.
- Turner County has superb public schools, keeping the ratio of public versus private school attendance higher than the state average.
- Recreation options include a sprawling state park and a municipal golf course that offer highly pleasurable experiences without high prices.
- Turner County’s convenient hub location places top vacation spots like Disney World and Atlantic and Gulf beaches within just a few hours’ drive.
