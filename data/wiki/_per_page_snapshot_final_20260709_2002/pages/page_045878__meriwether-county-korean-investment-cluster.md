# Meriwether County Korean Investment Cluster

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2018-07-03

**Source:** [Korean Investment Leaves Big Impression on West Georgia County  - Global Atlanta](https://www.globalatlanta.com/korean-investment-leaves-big-impression-on-west-georgia-county/)  ·  `page_045878`

## Facts

- Korean investment has been a boon for Georgia, especially in the automotive sector in the decade after the announcement of Kia Motors’ West Point factory.
- Consul General Young-jun Kim cited 33,000 jobs at a recent Asia summit as part of this investment impact.
- Meriwether County has access to Interstate 85 and sits 22 miles from the Kia plant.
- Three out of the top four employers in the county of 22,000 people hail from Korea and form an integral part of the Kia supply chain.
- Dongwon Autopart Technology employs 300 people making structural panels for vehicles.
- Dongwon Autopart Technology trails only a Georgia-Pacific plywood plant in providing jobs for the county.
- Korea’s Mando Corp., which supplies both Hyundai and Kia, has two separate subsidiary plants in the county.
- Mando America employs about 420 people making brake/steering components.
- Mando Casting Facility has 195 workers, for a total of 615 employees at Mando.
- Mando Corp. will become the largest manufacturer in the county with more than 1,000 employees when it reaches full capacity.
- The investments represent more than $300 million in capital investment, all concentrated in Meriwether Park.
- Meriwether Park sits just 0.2 miles from Interstate 85 and only 40 miles from Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport.
- The location's proximity to nonstop flights to Seoul is of particular importance to Korean executives.
- Delta Air Lines Inc. and its Skyteam partner, Korean Air, offer two separate nonstop flights between the area and Seoul.
- Jane Fryer has headed up Meriwether County’s industrial recruitment since 2011.
- As executive director of the Meriwether County Industrial Development Authority, Ms. Fryer oversees the influx of Korean investment and sees potential given the county’s proximity to an automotive corridor running from east Alabama into Georgia where Korean investment is booming.
- Ms. Fryer spent 2016-17 heading up the Southeast U.S. Korea Chamber of Commerce.
- Meriwether County’s IDA has become the exclusive sponsor of Global Atlanta’s Korea coverage, highlighting the Georgia commercial and cultural relationship with South Korea.
