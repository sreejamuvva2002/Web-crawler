# Hyundai Industrial Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-11-08

**Source:** [Hyundai, Kia supplier to invest in manufacturing facility in Coweta  - Grice Connect](https://www.griceconnect.com/local-news/hyundai-kia-supplier-to-invest-in-manufacturing-facility-in-coweta-7768013)  ·  `page_021100`

## Facts

- Last week, Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Hyundai Industrial Co., Ltd., a global auto parts supplier, will create 100 new jobs and invest more than $24 million in a new manufacturing facility near Newnan in Coweta County.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that partners like Kia and Hyundai are attracting a large network of suppliers, creating jobs and opportunity for hardworking Georgians across the entire state.
- Suppliers have now committed to bringing over 5,300 jobs to Georgia, mostly to communities outside of large urban areas, since last year's Hyundai Metaplant announcement.
- Hyundai Industrial was established in 1978 and is headquartered in Ulsan, Korea.
- The company specializes in manufacturing car seats, arm rests, and head rests.
- Mr. Woosuck Kim, Team Leader at HDI America Inc., stated that Hyundai Industrial has more than four decades of experience providing comfortable and safe experiences for drivers and passengers with some of the world’s biggest automotive brands.
- Hyundai Industrial expressed excitement to be part of the e-mobility transition happening in Georgia and look forward to working with Coweta County to create mutually beneficial growth for the community and future employees.
- Hyundai Industrial has purchased an existing building at 83 Amlajack Way in Coweta County.
- The purchased building will be retrofitted to produce arm and head rests for OEMs, including Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- Hyundai Industrial will focus on filling manufacturing production roles.
- Job openings will be posted on Indeed or an online portal when available.
- Coweta County Board of Commissioners Chairman John Reidelbach welcomed Hyundai Industrial to Coweta County, noting it joins the area's thriving manufacturing industries.
- The new facility will have close proximity to I-85, making transport to nearby automobile plants effortless.
- Director of Korean Investment Yoonie Kim represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The project was completed in partnership with the Coweta County Development Authority and Georgia EMC.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson noted that with Kia located in West Georgia and Hyundai on the eastern coast, suppliers like Hyundai Industrial can locate almost anywhere in the state and remain central to at least two major manufacturers.
- Commissioner Wilson highlighted that this relationship between Coweta County and Hyundai Industrial is a classic example of Georgia’s partnership approach to economic development.
- Georgia has positioned itself as a leader in developing and harnessing emerging technologies for the evolving automotive and mobility industry.
- Since 2020, Georgia has created more than $25.1 billion in investments and 29,000 jobs by pursuing the entire electric vehicle supply chain.
- In fiscal year 2023, job creation in Georgia's automotive industry increased by 324 percent compared to fiscal year 2021.
- Suppliers attracted by Georgia’s mobility original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) resulted in over $2 billion in investment across the state in FY23.
