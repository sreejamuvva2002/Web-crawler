# Aurubis AG

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2023-03-27

**Source:** [Aurubis opts to double capacity at Georgia plant - Recycling Today](https://www.recyclingtoday.com/news/aurubis-georgia-usa-sms-recycling-capacity-doubled-copper/)  ·  `page_017306`

## Facts

- Aurubis AG signed an equipment and technology contract with Germany-based SMS Group GmbH for a second plant module at its Richmond, Georgia scrap-fed copper refinery.
- The company announced additional capital expenditures for 2023 that included a project designed to double the capacity of the Georgia facility.
- The additional equipment will increase the capacity of the metal recycling plant in Richmond, currently under construction, from an annual 90,000 tons of complex metal scrap materials to 180,000 tons per year.
- Aurubis CEO Roland Harings stated the expansion delivers on the company’s “Driving Sustainable Growth” strategy while keeping mineral raw materials that are both strategically important and in high demand in the resource cycle.
- The SMS modular technology allows for flexible operations and quick growth in the dynamic United States recycling market.
- The SMS technology includes a top-blown rotary converter (TBRC), which is state-of-the-art equipment used for processing complex recycling materials so that raw materials like copper, nickel, tin, precious metals and platinum group metals can be recovered in additional downstream stages.
- Aurubis CEO Roland Harings noted that bundling SMS Group’s extensive plant know-how with Aurubis’ unique expertise creates an environment for the highly efficient recovery of metals.
- Michael Rzepczyk, a member of the SMS executive board, stated the partnership helps Aurubis assume a leading role in multimetal recycling in the U.S. and takes recycling to the next level with the planned doubling of production volume at the new site.
- The modular system allows additional components to be added to the Richmond plant to precisely meet any future requirements.
- Aurubis CEO Roland Harings added that the modular system enables a prudent market strategy to respond flexibly to U.S. needs and benefit from constant market growth in the country.
- Aurubis assigned a total project price tag of about 640 million euros ($692 million) for both phases of the project.
- The facility is described as the first secondary smelter specializing in multimetal recycling in the U.S., representing a targeted investment in expanding its recycling business.
- In a 2022 interview, Aurubis CEO Roland Harings stated the Richmond plant was initially slated to process 90,000 metric tons of circuit boards, copper cable and other recyclables that contain metal annually to produce 35,000 metric tons of blister copper annually.
- Both the annual input scrap volume (90,000 metric tons) and output blister copper volume (35,000 metric tons) are presumed to double when the plant runs at full capacity.
- Stage 1 of the Richmond plant is scheduled to go online in the first half of 2024.
- The equipment and technology contract was signed in late March, with an anticipated second-stage production startup sometime in 2026.
