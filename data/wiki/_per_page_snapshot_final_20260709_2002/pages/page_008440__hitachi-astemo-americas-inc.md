# Hitachi Astemo Americas Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-01

## Facts

- Hitachi Astemo Americas was founded in 2021 and is based in Farmington, Michigan.
- The company provides advanced mobility solutions within the automotive and motorcycle systems sectors.
- Offerings include autonomous driving and advanced driver assistance systems, connected car solutions, software, powertrain and chassis systems, products for motorcycles and industrial equipment, and aftermarket products for various vehicles.
- The company's headquarters is located at 34500 Grand River Avenue, Farmington, Michigan, 48335, United States.
- Hitachi Astemo Americas has filed 5 patents.
- The three most popular patent topics include computer vision, electric power conversion, and electrical engineering.
- On July 1, 2026, Astemo Americas Inc. in Farmington Hills announced a collaboration with California’s NVIDIA as a Hyperion Tier 1 partner.
- The partnership involves developing dual NVIDIA DRIVE AGX Thor electronic control units (ECUs) for automotive OEMs.
- This collaboration supports the industry’s transition from Level-2 to Level-4 software-defined ready vehicles.
- NVIDIA DRIVE Hyperion is a reference architecture and ecosystem designed to help automakers develop and deploy highly automated and autonomous driving systems.
- Astemo Americas’ work will focus on dual SoC development based on NVIDIA DRIVE AGX Thor to support the high-performance compute required for next-generation vehicle platforms.
- Through the NVIDIA DRIVE Hyperion ecosystem, Astemo Americas is strengthening its role in the global shift toward software-defined, electrified, and increasingly autonomous vehicles.
- As a DRIVE Hyperion Tier-1 partner, Astemo Americas is expected to bring integration expertise in electrification, software-defined vehicle technologies, and advanced mobility systems to support automakers in bringing advanced autonomous driving technologies from development to production.
- Mathieu Devillard, executive vice president and head of the Electrification Business Division for Astemo, stated that “Astemo’s electrification and software-defined vehicle capabilities are central to where mobility is headed.”
- Rishi Dhall, vice president of automotive at NVIDIA, stated that “As a DRIVE Hyperion Tier-1 ecosystem partner developing dual NVIDIA DRIVE AGX Thor ECUs for automotive OEMs, Astemo is helping bring scalable autonomous driving platforms closer to production.”
- Backed by decades of ADAS expertise, Astemo states it delivers functionally safe, OTA-ready technologies proven in real-world driving environments.
- Through its partnership with NVIDIA, the company enables SDV-ready intelligence powered by advanced AI compute for conversational AI and MPU workloads.
- According to Astemo, its flexible-choice architecture is designed to deliver scalable central computing tailored to platform needs, complemented by efficient high-performance computing optimized for power and thermal management.
- Competitors of Hitachi Astemo Americas include Delphi Technologies, Nexteer Automotive, Valeo, Hwashin, Tachi-S, Avanti R&D, Dana, Marelli, DENSO US/Canada, ZF, and Webasto.
