# LG Energy Solution and Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-09-01

**Source:** [LG Energy, Hyundai to invest additional $2B in Georgia battery cell plant | Manufacturing Dive](https://www.manufacturingdive.com/news/lg-energy-hyundai-additional-2b-ellabell-savannah-georgia-joint-battery-cell/692549/)  ·  `page_001244`

## Facts

- LG Energy Solution and Hyundai Motor Group plan to invest an additional $2 billion to expand their joint battery cell manufacturing plant in Georgia.
- The investment adds to the $4.3 billion announced for the project in May.
- The expansion will add 400 jobs, according to Gov. Brian Kemp.
- The factory will be adjacent to Hyundai’s upcoming $5.5 billion manufacturing campus in Ellabell, Georgia.
- Production at the facility is slated to begin at the end of 2025.
- The plant will have enough capacity to produce 300,000 EVs annually.
- Dong-Myung Kim, president and head of the advanced automotive battery division of LG Energy Solution, stated that the investment underscores their dedication to driving America’s EV transition while bolstering the local economy through the creation of quality jobs.
- The two South Korea-based companies will each take a 50% stake in the venture and facility.
- Hyundai will assemble battery packs using cells from the plant.
- The battery packs will be sent to the automaker’s U.S. manufacturing sites to produce Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis EV models.
- Hyundai and LG Energy Solution have been working together in the EV battery sector since 2009, when Hyundai introduced its Elantra Hybrid EV.
- LG Energy Solution provided batteries for the Elantra, along with Hyundai’s Kona Electric and IONIQ 6 EVs.
- This investment marks the second joint venture between the two companies.
- The two companies broke ground on a battery cell manufacturing plant in Karawang, Indonesia, in September 2021.
- The $1.1 billion Indonesian plant is set to start production in the first half of 2024.
- Hyundai’s upcoming EV campus has drawn a host of suppliers to the area.
- On Tuesday, Daesol Ausys Georgia unveiled plans to invest $72 million in a manufacturing facility in West Point.
- The auto part maker will produce luggage boards and covers at its new facility.
- Daesol Ausys Georgia is also a key supplier for Kia and General Motors.
