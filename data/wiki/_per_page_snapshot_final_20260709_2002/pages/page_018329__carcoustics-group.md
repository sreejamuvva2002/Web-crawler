# Carcoustics Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2017-06-13

**Source:** [Carcoustics to Create 200 Jobs in Gwinnett County - Gwinnett Citizen](https://gwinnettcitizen.com/local-news/local-news-local-news/carcoustics-to-create-200-jobs-in-gwinnett-county/)  ·  `page_018329`

## Facts

- Georgia offers automotive industry suppliers like Carcoustics resources necessary to compete worldwide, according to Deal.
- By choosing Georgia, Carcoustics will benefit from a well-trained workforce, a thriving automotive industry, and the nation’s top business environment.
- Deal expressed encouragement at seeing another German company locate in Georgia, citing successful efforts to build international relationships.
- A new 130-square-foot manufacturing facility is being built at 4620 Thompson Mill Road to manufacture solution-oriented acoustic and thermally effective components for the automotive industry.
- Stefan Vallentin, project manager at Carcoustics, stated the new plant location in Georgia offers great international transport connections and is logistically positioned between BMW in South Carolina and Mercedes in Alabama.
- Potential customers for the facility include Automotive Tier I/Tier II suppliers, Volkswagen, Mercedes Vans, Volvo, and Asian OEMs like Nissan, Honda, Toyota, Kia, and Hyundai.
- The location provides new possibilities for Carcoustics' growth strategy, particularly in the U.S. market.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson expressed excitement over continued economic development opportunities in Gwinnett County.
- Carcoustics is joining a growing number of German automotive manufacturers that have established operations in Georgia.
- The international Carcoustics Group model integrates a central research center with a manufacturing structure designed for proximity to major markets to enable rapid global product availability.
- Gwinnett County Board of Commissioners Chair Charlotte Nash welcomed Carcoustics to Gwinnett County, noting it joins a strong base of international companies that find the county's talent, resources, and location ideal.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) Director of International Investment Nico Wijnberg represented the Global Commerce division in partnership with Partnership Gwinnett, the Metro Atlanta Chamber, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- Buford City Manager Bryan Kerlin called the project a great win for the City of Buford and welcomed Carcoustics as one of its newest businesses.
- Bryan Kerlin expressed confidence that Carcoustics will find the necessary support and resources in Buford to remain competitive globally.
- Partnership Gwinnett project manager Deven Cason expressed excitement over Carcoustics choosing to locate in Gwinnett County.
- The new Buford facility is strategically located to serve Carcoustics' rapidly growing global customer base.
- Carcoustics will join nearly 100 other German companies operating in Gwinnett County, alongside 500 internationally owned and operated firms, reinforcing the county's status as a leader in international business.
