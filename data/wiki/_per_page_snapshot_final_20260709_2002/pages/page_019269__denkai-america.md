# Denkai America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2022-07-20

**Source:** [Copper foil maker to build Augusta plant with 250 jobs](https://www.wrdw.com/2022/07/20/copper-foil-maker-build-augusta-plant-with-250-jobs/)  ·  `page_019269`

## Facts

- Denkai America, a manufacturer of copper foil used in printed electronics, will locate its new manufacturing facility and North American headquarters in Richmond County.
- Gov. Brian Kemp announced the company's selection on Wednesday.
- The company’s initial investment will be $150 million to establish an “electrodeposited” copper foil production facility on a 115-acre site.
- Denkai America aims to invest a total of $430 million and create 250 jobs over the next five years.
- The plant will focus foil for electric vehicle batteries.
- Denkai America’s materials have traditionally been used in printed circuit board applications, primarily for the aerospace and defense industries.
- The company's materials are also now used in lithium-ion batteries powering electric vehicles.
- Construction is expected to begin in September at the Augusta Corporate Park in south Augusta.
- The Augusta facility will be the first part of a three-phase investment.
- The facility will be fully operational by December 2024.
- Wednesday’s announcement switches the location for the Nippon Denkai plant from one originally announced next to the company’s existing plant in Camden, S.C.
- The company says it could eventually triple production at the Georgia site.
- Denkai says the Augusta site has room for expansion, allows for more advanced equipment and has cheaper electricity prices.
- Expanding battery manufacturing will drive a big increase in demand for copper foil.
- The company will be hiring for skilled industrial technicians, production managers, and engineers.
- Operating for more than 60 years, Denkai has team members worldwide with operations in Japan and North America.
- Gov. Brian Kemp stated: “Georgia is proud to be selected as the home of Denkai America, further strengthening our state’s status as a leader in the emerging electric mobility and existing aerospace, defense, and technology industries.”
- Steven Kendrick, chairman of the Augusta Economic Development Authority, said the authority is excited to welcome Denkai America and its North American Headquarters to the Augusta Corporate Park and expects them to bring high-skilled jobs.
- The Augusta Corporate Park has been a magnet for new industry in the past couple of years.
- Metal recycler Aurubis is breaking ground on a plant at the park that will bring 125 jobs.
- Plastic recycler PureCycle Technologies is planning a plant at the park that will create 80 to 100 jobs.
- Georgia officials are close to finalizing a deal with automaker Hyundai to build a $5.5 billion electric car plant near Savannah.
- An economic development agency representing four Savannah-area counties approved its portions of the Hyundai agreement, including an economic incentives package.
- Details of the Hyundai deal won’t be released until state officials and Hyundai also sign it.
