# Archer Aviation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-12-11

**Source:** [Electric Air Taxi Company Sets Sights on Georgia Base](https://www.govtech.com/fs/electric-air-taxi-company-sets-sights-on-georgia-base)  ·  `page_000227`

## Facts

- Santa Clara, California-based electric aircraft developer Archer Aviation announced last year plans to build a manufacturing facility in Covington with hundreds of jobs.
- State and local officials have offered millions of dollars in tax incentives to Archer for the project.
- The incentives include a $3 million grant for machinery and equipment, contingent on job creation.
- Plans are underway to train people to work in the factory building a new generation of aircraft.
- The decision to choose Covington over 28 other sites in consideration around the country was led by Archer vice president of manufacturing Glen Burks.
- Burks spent time in Marietta and Atlanta while in the Marine Corps.
- Archer met with Newton County Industrial Development Authority officials two years ago during the site selection process.
- Glen Burks stated that when he first sat down with Covington officials in August 2021, they were very excited about Archer.
- The remarks were made during a luncheon at the Atlanta Aero Club in September.
- Covington was one of three finalists for the Archer Aviation site, along with Tucson, Arizona, and the Alliance Airport in Fort Worth, Texas.
- Elements that made Covington attractive included its location on an airport, affordability, and the town’s growth aspirations.
- With plans to hire hundreds of hourly workers for the factory, Burks prioritized a low cost of living due to previous experiences with high housing costs in Maryland.
- Georgia and local officials are eager for projects like Archer, which was code-named Project Jane early on.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development global commerce division director Bob Kosek stated that the state and Gov. Brian Kemp have identified e-mobility as a critical industry to Georgia’s future.
- Kosek noted that Georgia is creating the jobs of tomorrow by building on existing industries, including aerospace.
- Kosek called Archer a natural fit for the community’s tech ecosystem, which includes Meta, Rivian, and Takeda.
- Archer’s most important goal is working toward certification of its Midnight aircraft in the new electric Vertical Takeoff and Landing (eVTOL) category.
- The certification process is expected to stretch into 2025.
- Archer aims to get a production certificate to open the Covington facility in the middle of next year and begin building aircraft on an assembly line.
- The first six to eight aircraft are being produced at San Jose for testing.
- Employees working on the initial aircraft will move to Covington when the new plant opens.
- These employees are being trained with a three-week curriculum developed in partnership with Georgia Piedmont Technical College.
- In the first few years, Archer plans to ramp up to a few hundred workers at the Covington facility.
- The workforce is expected to grow to about 650 workers by 2027.
- Employment could reach as many as 1,500 workers by 2030.
- Archer plans to help develop a certificate program at Georgia Piedmont Technical College to train an eVTOL manufacturing workforce.
- The company will also use the state’s workforce development program Georgia Quick Start.
- Chief people officer Tosha Perkins stated she does not plan to list job openings until sometime in the first half of next year when the company is confident it will get the production certificate around June.
- Perkins noted that attracting people to Covington for an entirely new industry may be difficult due to its small size.
- It will be important for the community to add enough housing for hundreds more workers and ensure the area is alluring enough to draw people willing to move there and raise a family.
- Perkins stated that Covington is not yet built up in a way where it could currently support that influx of residents.
