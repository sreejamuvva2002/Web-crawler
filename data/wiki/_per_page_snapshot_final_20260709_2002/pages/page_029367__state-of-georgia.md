# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Infrastructure | Georgia](https://georgia.org/competitive-advantages/infrastructure)  ·  `page_029367`

## Facts

- Georgia ranks No. 1 in Logistics and Infrastructure according to Area Development (2025).
- Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport serves more than 150 U.S. destinations, 70 international destinations, and 40 countries.
- The Port of Savannah hosts more than 30 ship calls per week.
- The Port of Brunswick ranks No. 1 for roll-on/roll-off cargo.
- Georgia has over 4,600 miles of active rail lines and two Class I railroads, boasting one of the most extensive freight rail systems in the U.S.
- Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport is the world’s busiest and most efficient passenger airport, with an average of 2,100 arrivals and departures moving 286,000 passengers daily.
- The airport's cargo operation includes three main air cargo complexes totaling 1.8 million square feet of cargo handling space.
- Each airport cargo complex is equipped with its own refueling and support systems as well as direct access to four interstates: I-75, I-85, I-285, and I-20.
- Delta Air Lines is headquartered in Atlanta.
- Delta serves seven of Georgia’s nine regional commercial airports and offers nonstop flights to more than 200 domestic and international destinations.
- The Port of Savannah is the western-most port on the Atlantic, offering faster access to major inland markets and the largest refrigerated capacity on the East Coast.
- The Port of Brunswick is the nation’s No. 1 Ro/Ro port, with 1,700 acres of dedicated auto and heavy equipment processing space.
- Georgia has more than 20,000 miles of major interstate-connected highway.
- Georgia's inland ports extend the reach of deepwater seaports by shifting freight to rail and lowering transportation costs.
- Appalachian Regional Port provides a direct CSX rail link between the Port of Savannah and the Southeast.
- The Blue Ridge Connector is a Norfolk Southern-served terminal opening in 2026 that will connect directly to the Port of Savannah.
- Georgia has the eighth-largest refrigerated space capacity in the U.S. at 300+ million cubic feet, allowing perishables and temperature-sensitive products to move safely and efficiently.
- Half percent of the world’s top 3PLs operate in Georgia, including Americold.
- Georgia was the first state in the U.S. to precisely map the availability of broadband services and identify areas that need greater access.
- State-led and regional initiatives are aimed at closing broadband access gaps across the state.
- Two of the nation’s largest fiber optic trunk lines intersect in Atlanta, along with two major research lines.
- This broadband access directly facilitates the continuing growth of Georgia’s “Transaction Alley,” which extends from Columbus in west Georgia, across Atlanta, to Georgia’s eastern border in Augusta.
- Georgia’s three primary utility providers are Georgia Power, Georgia Electric Membership Corporation (Georgia EMC), and Electric Cities of Georgia (ECG).
- These utilities offer reliable, affordable energy to businesses of all sizes with long-term, advanced planning to accommodate large load users and provide responsive service crews.
- Through the Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) program and the Rural Site Development Initiative (RSDI), Georgia maintains a portfolio of development-ready sites statewide.
- These site readiness programs reduce risk, minimize delays, and support long-term operational stability in both urban and rural markets.
