# Lehigh Technologies Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2013-11-15

**Source:** [Lehigh Technologies Adds Industry Vets to its Ranks - Tire Review Magazine](https://www.tirereview.com/lehigh-technologies-adds-industry-vets-to-its-ranks/)  ·  `page_057088`

## Facts

- Lehigh Technologies Inc. announced that Jim MacMaster, former executive vice president and chief operating officer at Yokohama Tire Corp. North America, has joined its board of directors.
- Lehigh also appointed Adel Halasa, a polymer scientist formerly with Goodyear Tire & Rubber Co., to its advisory board.
- The company has been continuing to drive adoption of its micronized rubber powder (MRP) material in high-value applications.
- Lehigh is now expanding its presence in Asia and Europe.
- CEO Alan Barton stated that having an executive with Jim MacMaster's level of experience in a key market segment will help deliver the compelling value proposition of MRP to the tire industry.
- MacMaster began his career with Yokohama in 1981 in sales and rose through multiple commercial roles to become vice president of the company’s North American Commercial Tire Division.
- In 2001, MacMaster was named executive vice president at Yokohama.
- As a new Advisory Board member, Adel Halasa will provide guidance to Lehigh’s technical team.
- Halasa was a research and development fellow at Goodyear from 1983 to 2009.
- He has since served as an adjunct professor at the University of Akron.
- Halasa has been listed on more than 300 patents.
- He was recognized in 1997 with the Charles Goodyear Medal for his work developing anionic solution polymerization of styrene-butadiene rubber (SBR).
- Tom Rosenmayer, vice president of technology at Lehigh Technologies, stated that the company's technology roadmap for the tire industry is based upon driving higher loading levels of MRP and developing MRPs with chemical reactivity.
- Halasa’s depth of experience and knowledge of polymers and their structure-property relationships will help Lehigh develop new products faster as a key part of its growth strategy.
