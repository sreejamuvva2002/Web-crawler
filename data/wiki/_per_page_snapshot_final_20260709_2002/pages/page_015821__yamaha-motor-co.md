# Yamaha Motor Co.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Yamaha Motor Co. Moving HQ From California To Kennesaw, Georgia](https://www.bisnow.com/atlanta/news/economic-development/yamaha-motor-co-moving-hq-to-kennesaw-133589)  ·  `page_015821`

## Facts

- Yamaha Motor Co.’s U.S. subsidiary will be moving its headquarters from Cypress, California, to Kennesaw, Georgia over at least a two-year period.
- California has been the company’s corporate home for half a century.
- Yamaha currently owns and occupies 1270 Chastain Road, a 459K SF office building in Kennesaw, where it opened a 75K SF Marine Innovation Center in 2023.
- Yamaha has operated a 1.3M SF Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation of America factory in Newnan since 1986.
- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp announced the move and highlighted it as a signal that companies prefer the state’s business-friendly policies.
- Kemp spokesperson Carter Chapman said the state did not provide any grants or Quick Start funds to lure Yamaha to the state.
- It is unclear how many jobs will be added to Georgia, but the move will affect around 250 employees in California.
- In November, the company leased 25K SF at TownPark Commons, a four-building, 350K SF office park located more than 30 miles north of Downtown Atlanta.
- RGRE purchased the office park out of foreclosure nearly a year ago for $42M.
- Yamaha is seeking another 50K to 75K SF of office in Kennesaw over the next two years.
- Yamaha spokesperson Bob Starr stated the move to Metro Atlanta was part of a structural reform to boost profits as costs and tariff pressures climbed.
- The company plans to lease back its sprawling 25-acre headquarters campus at 6555 Katella Ave. in Orange County, California, which includes 279K SF in three buildings, during the transitional period while pursuing future redevelopment entitlements.
- Avison Young was tapped to sell Yamaha’s sprawling 25-acre headquarters campus at 6555 Katella Ave. in Orange County, California.
