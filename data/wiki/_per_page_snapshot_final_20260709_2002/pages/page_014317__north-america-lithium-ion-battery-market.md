# North America Lithium-ion Battery Market

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [North America Lithium-ion Battery Market Size, Outlook 2034](https://www.imarcgroup.com/north-america-lithium-ion-battery-market)  ·  `page_014317`

## Facts

- The North America lithium-ion battery market size was valued at USD 22.1 Billion in 2025.
- IMARC Group estimates the market to reach USD 52.9 Billion by 2034, exhibiting a CAGR of 9.86% from 2026-2034.
- Market growth is driven by growing adoption of electric vehicles and expanding energy storage systems deployment.
- Growth is further expanded by domestic lithium-ion battery manufacturing, increasing investment in battery recycling and second-life applications, and advancements in battery technology and alternative chemistries.
- Base year for market analysis is 2025, with forecast years 2026-2034 and historical years 2020-2025.
- Rising acceptance of electric vehicles and government emissions regulations prompting acquisition incentives are major causes supporting the North American lithium-ion battery market.
- The Biden administration has provided funding for charging stations within domestic battery manufacturing boundaries to boost electric vehicle growth.
- Leading automakers Tesla, General Motors, and Ford have ramped up production of electric vehicles.
- In December 2024, Volkswagen and PowerCo joined to invest $48 million in Patriot Battery Metals to ensure a long-term North American lithium supply for EV batteries.
- Battery technology improvements toward better energy density and faster charging are making electric cars competitive with internal combustion engine vehicles.
- Growing deployment of energy storage systems supports the integration of variable renewable energy sources.
- In November 2024, EDF Renewables secured a 20-year power purchase agreement with APS for the 250 MW Beehive BESS in Arizona.
- Construction of the Beehive BESS facility is anticipated to initiate in 2025, focusing on storing and discharging renewable energy during peak-demand periods.
- The Beehive BESS facility will encompass enclosures of lithium-ion batteries.
- Lithium-ion batteries are selected for ESS applications due to elevated energy density, long life cycles above 5000 cycles, and decreasing costs.
- Government programs supporting energy storage deployment and rising private sector investments aid market growth.
- Requirements for residential, commercial, and bulk storage systems are anticipated to chart an upward growth trend.
- North America is undergoing a significant ramp-up of lithium-ion battery manufacturing supported by government initiatives and private sector investments.
- The US Inflation Reduction Act and Bipartisan Infrastructure Law provide incentives for domestic battery production and reduce foreign supply chain reliance.
- Tesla, Panasonic, and LG Energy Solution are ramping up gigafactory building programs across the U.S. and Canada.
- In November 2024, Asahi Kasei Battery Separator Corporation and Honda began constructing a lithium-ion battery separator plant in Ontario, Canada.
- The Ontario separator plant is set to produce 700 million square meters per year by 2027.
- Battery recycling and second-life uses are rapidly evolving into main sustainability strategies due to the magnifying need for lithium-ion batteries.
- Companies invest in recycling technologies to recover valuable materials like nickel, lithium, and cobalt while reducing environmental disposal burdens.
- In December 2024, American Battery Technology Company received a USD 144 million grant from the U.S. Department of Energy for a lithium-ion battery recycling plant in conjunction with Argonne National Laboratory.
- Government policies favoring a circular economy and new business models for second-life battery applications support industry changes.
- Redwood Materials and Li-Cycle are spearheading efforts to set up large-scale recycling infrastructure in North America.
- Industry innovations emphasize energy density, charging speed, and safety advancements.
- Next-generation battery chemistries like solid-state batteries and lithium-iron-phosphate (LFP) are trending due to improved thermal stability and cost-effectiveness.
- LFP batteries are being adopted by automakers and energy storage companies for stationary storage and entry-level EVs, while high-performance applications see solid-state development.
- In September 2024, BMW North America collaborated with Redwood Materials to recycle lithium-ion batteries, targeting a closed-loop system reintroducing 95-98% of critical minerals into the supply chain.
- Lithium Cobalt Oxide (LCO) is the preferred cathode material for consumer electronics in North America due to high energy density and stable performance.
- LCO adoption for EVs is complicated by high costs and cobalt supply chain risks, forcing manufacturers to search for alternative chemistries with little or no cobalt and higher nickel.
- Lithium Iron Phosphate (LFP) is emerging as a new technology for North American EVs, energy storage systems, and industrial applications due to long cycle life, thermal stability, and lower cost compared to cobalt-based chemistries.
- Tesla and Ford are increasingly implementing LFP in affordable electric vehicle models to meet sustainability goals and increase domestic battery production.
- Lithium Nickel Manganese Cobalt (NMC) predominates in the North American EV scene due to high energy density and balanced performance.
- General Motors, Tesla, and Rivian utilize NMC for electric vehicles requiring longer range.
- Battery makers are optimizing high nickel levels in NMC variants to lessen cobalt dependency, improve cost efficiency, and enhance sustainability and energy output.
- Lithium Manganese Oxide (LMO) is used for hybrid vehicles, power tools, and medical equipment across North America due to high thermal stability and safety.
- Despite lower energy density, LMO's cost and safety make it preferred for short-range electric vehicles and backup power systems.
- 0 to 3000mAh lithium-ion batteries are used in North America for consumer electronics like cell phones, wearables, and wireless accessories.
- Batteries ranging from 3000mAh to 10000mAh are used in North America for laptops, tablets, drones, medical gadgets, and power banks.
- 10,000-60,000mAh lithium-ion batteries support electric two-wheelers, power tools, industrial tools, and energy storage systems in North America.
- Batteries greater than 60,000mAh are needed for EVs, grid storage, and heavy-duty industrial applications in North America.
- Automakers Tesla, General Motors, and Ford are scaling up battery production using LFP and NMC chemistries to balance costs with sustainable practices.
- Government incentives and ever-growing charging infrastructure provide impetus for accelerated EV market adoption in North America.
- Lithium-ion battery storage systems in North America provide backup power, grid stabilization, and renewable energy integration for utilities and enterprises.
- The United States dominates the North American lithium-ion battery market due to demand stimulated by EV manufacturing, energy storage, and consumer electronics.
- Incentives from the Inflation Reduction Act support domestic battery manufacturing and supply chain development in the U.S.
- Tesla, LG Energy Solution, and Panasonic have expanded their gigafactories in the U.S. to gain an advantage in the EV and ESS markets.
- Canada plays a major role in the lithium-ion battery industry due to critical mineral supplies such as cobalt, lithium, and nickel.
- Investments are being made in battery manufacturing and recycling in Canada to strengthen its supply chain for EVs and energy storage.
- Lion Electric and BASF Canada are creating battery production hubs for sustainable mobility and energy storage solutions.
- Mexico is rapidly becoming a major lithium-ion battery manufacturing center due to increased automotive production and the USMCA.
- BMW and Ford are ramping up EV battery assembly in Mexico.
- Mexico is exploring domestic lithium extraction from deposits in Sonora with the objective of integrating with the North American battery supply chain.
- Companies are investing in gigafactories to localize production and mitigate supply chain risks.
- In May 2024, Li-Cycle partnered with Daimler Truck North America to recycle lithium-ion batteries from electric vehicles to support a circular economy.
- Key players in the North American lithium-ion battery market include A123 Systems, Duracell Inc., Tesla Inc., Saft America, Johnson Controls, CANBAT Technologies Inc., Corvus Energy, Prime Power, and Panasonic Corporation.
