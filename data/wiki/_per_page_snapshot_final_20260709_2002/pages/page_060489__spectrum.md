# Spectrum

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-01

**Source:** [Local group opposes permit for one of the world’s largest wood pellet plants in predominantly Black and Hispanic neighborhood - Southern Environmental Law Center](https://www.selc.org/press-release/local-group-opposes-permit-for-worlds-largest-wood-pellet-plant-in-predominantly-black-and-hispanic-neighborhood/)  ·  `page_060489`

## Facts

- On Aug. 5, SELC filed a petition on behalf of Concerned Citizens of Cook County with the Georgia Office of State Administrative Hearings to appeal an air quality permit for Spectrum's wood pellet facility in Adel, GA.
- The proposed facility is located adjacent to a predominantly Black and Hispanic neighborhood in west Adel.
- Wood pellet plants release air particles that create air quality problems and are linked to serious health issues, like asthma and heart disease.
- EPD’s issuance of the permit violates the Clean Air Act and threatens the health and welfare of local residents who have voiced concerns.
- EPD’s decision circumvents EPA non-discrimination goals by allowing construction before testing.
- In 2021, EPD issued a permit to Renewable Biomass Group for a facility slated to produce 450,000 metric tons per year of industrial wood pellets.
- Dr. Treva Gear, founding member of 4C, stated: 'Our community deserves better. Economic progress should not equate to our Black, Hispanic, and impoverished neighborhoods being treated as a sacrifice zone for repeated environmental injustices.'
- Chandra Taylor-Sawyer, leader of SELC’s Environmental Justice Initiative, stated: 'EPD has a golden opportunity to do right by those living near Spectrum’s proposed plant and revoke the permit.'
- The petition was amended on September 7, resulting in a stay until the Georgia Office of State Administrative Hearings details next steps.
- On Jan. 28, 2021, EPD denied environmental groups' request to comment on a draft permit for Renewable Biomass Group's new wood pellet plant in Adel.
- In Oct. 2021, Spectrum submitted a permit application for a facility with Phase 1 capacity of 600,000 tons per year and Phase 2 capacity of 1,320,000 tons per year.
- At full capacity, Spectrum would become the largest wood pellet manufacturing facility in the world.
- On Nov. 19, 2021, 4C and 14 other public interest organizations submitted comments opposing the application due to incomplete information.
- In Dec. 2021, Spectrum submitted a revised permit application.
- On Mar. 28, 2022, EPD solicited public comments on Spectrum's revised draft permit.
- On May 2, 2022, 4C and 13 other public interest organizations submitted comments citing insufficient air pollution controls.
- On Jul. 8, 2022, the permit was approved for two phases of construction and operation without notifying the 14 commenting groups or providing a hearing deadline.
- Concerned Citizens of Cook County (4C) is a frontline community organization based in Adel, GA focused on equity and environmental justice.
