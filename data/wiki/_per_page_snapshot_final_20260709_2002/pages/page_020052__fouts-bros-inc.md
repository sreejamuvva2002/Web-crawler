# Fouts Bros Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- Fouts Bros Inc. hosted its inaugural “Home Day” event at the company’s corporate headquarters in Milledgeville, Georgia, on Thursday, November 7th, 2024.
- The event was attended by state and federal government officials, industry leaders, and local community members to highlight the company’s long-standing commitment to manufacturing life-saving equipment for fire service and emergency responders across North America.
- Fouts Bros Inc. is a valued member of the Fire Apparatus Manufacturers’ Association (FAMA).
- The Honorable Austin Scott, U.S. Congressman for District 8, delivered the keynote address recognizing Fouts Bros Inc.’s significant contributions to Georgia’s workforce and its ongoing dedication to keeping first responders equipped with necessary tools.
- Attendees participated in guided tours of the Fouts Bros manufacturing facility to witness state-of-the-art fire apparatus, emergency vehicles, and specialized equipment being manufactured on-site.
- Nearly 1,000 Georgia residents are employed by the company in the manufacturing and distribution of fire and emergency service equipment, underscoring its role as a major local employer and economic contributor.
- Scott Edens, Fouts Bros Inc. President and CEO, stated that the event showcased the company’s progress over the past seven decades as a family-owned business and expressed pride in the dedicated team manufacturing safety equipment.
- A key focus of Home Day was federal and state funding for fire departments, emergency service agencies, and first responder training facilities, with special attention given to the Assistance to Firefighters Grants (AFG) and SAFER Grants.
- Fouts Bros Inc. leadership shared recent advocacy efforts during “Hill Day” in Washington, DC, where they worked alongside nearly 200 fire chiefs and industry peers to ensure continued funding for AFG and SAFER programs.
- Gary Pacilio, Executive VP of Operations at Fouts Bros Inc. and current President of FAMA, noted that more than 70% of firefighters and first responders across the country are volunteers and advocated for congressional appropriation of critical grant funding to support their equipment and assets.
