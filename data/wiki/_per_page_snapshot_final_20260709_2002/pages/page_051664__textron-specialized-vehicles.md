# Textron Specialized Vehicles

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- After an 18-month process to evaluate expansion opportunities, Textron Specialized Vehicles announces it is purchasing 235 acres in Augusta, Ga.
- The new site is located a half mile away from the company's current operations and near a facility currently owned by Procter and Gamble.
- The purchased facility is about 600,000 square feet.
- Textron Specialized Vehicles plans to add 400 jobs at the new location within the next 5 years.
- The expansion investment is estimated at $40 million.
- Textron Specialized Vehicles manufactures commercial, recreational, off-road utility and light transportation vehicles for golf courses, consumers, aviation, government and industrial users under the E-Z-GO, Cushman, Bad Boy Buggies and TUG brands.
- Textron President Kevin Holleran stated that the expansion "gives us room to not only move into today, but it will allow us to grow into in the future, it's more than we need today."
