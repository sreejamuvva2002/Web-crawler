# Archer Aviation, Inc.

## Facts

- Archer Aviation is an eVTOL startup company led by CEO Adam Goldstein.
- United Airlines and the U.S. Military are key partners of Archer Aviation, starting with deliveries to the USAF.
- Grizzly Research published a bear report titled "We Believe Archer Aviation, Inc is a Lame Duck" on August 15, 2023.
- Grizzly claimed Archer's DoD contract was noncompetitive with only $1.3 million in potential revenue for 2023.
- Grizzly accused Archer of misrepresenting flight activity and recycling old video footage to exaggerate progress.
- Grizzly criticized Archer's partnerships with United Airlines and Stellantis as being secured by giving out millions of shares and warrants for little compensation.
- Grizzly claimed FAA experts estimated Archer would not receive Type Certification until at least 2028.
- Grizzly claimed Archer settled a legal dispute with Wisk/Boeing by giving $100 million in free shares to Boeing.
- Grizzly claimed Archer's San Jose lab and small-scale production facility was incomplete as of July 2023.
- Archer delivered its first Midnight eVTOL aircraft to the U.S. Air Force on August 15, 2024, under a $142 million contract via the AFWERX Agility Prime program.
- The U.S. Air Force plans to use the Midnight aircraft for personnel transport, logistics, and rescue operations.
- The DoD contract includes pilot training, maintenance, and certification support.
- Archer continued manned test flights of the Midnight eVTOL aircraft post-August 2023.
- United Airlines made a $10 million pre-delivery payment for 100 of Archer’s eVTOLs.
- Stellantis committed $150 million in equity in January 2023 and became Archer's exclusive manufacturing partner for the Midnight eVTOL.
- Stellantis led a $215 million funding round for Archer as of August 2023.
- In July 2024, N.V. Stellantis bought 17 million shares of Archer for $54 million.
- The FAA issued final airworthiness criteria for the Midnight aircraft on May 23, 2024.
- Archer received a Part 145 Maintenance & Repair Certificate and a Part 135 Air Carrier Certificate from the FAA.
- As of September 2024, Archer successfully completed 402 test flights ahead of schedule.
- For-credit flight testing was expected to begin in early 2024.
- The settlement with Wisk/Boeing evolved into a collaboration agreement making Wisk Archer's exclusive provider of autonomous flight technology.
- Boeing made a significant investment in Archer during the $215 million funding round alongside United Airlines and ARK Invest.
- Archer completed installation of its high-volume battery pack manufacturing line at the San Jose Integrated Test Lab in May 2024, with capacity for up to 15,000 battery packs per year.
- Archer's first conforming Midnight aircraft was expected to be completed in Q4 2023.
- Archer began construction on a high-volume manufacturing facility in Covington, Georgia in March 2023.
- The Covington, Georgia facility is projected to support production of up to 650 eVTOL aircraft per year.
- The Covington facility is on track for completion in 2024.
- The Covington facility is nearly 400,000 square feet.
- Construction of the Covington facility was expected to be completed by the end of 2024 as of July 2024.
- The Midnight eVTOL's planned entry into service is in 2025.
- Stellantis increased its investment in Archer by $55 million following successful flight milestones.
- Short interest in Archer Aviation stock dropped from nearly 30% to 22.28%.
- Benchmark reaffirmed a "buy" rating with a $12.00 price objective on August 9th.
- Cantor Fitzgerald reaffirmed an "overweight" rating with a $10.00 price objective.
- JPMorgan Chase & Co. increased its price objective from $5.00 to $6.00 and gave an "overweight" rating on July 24th.
- HC Wainwright started coverage with a "buy" rating and $12.50 target price on September 3rd.
- Archer Aviation has a consensus rating of "Moderate Buy" and a consensus target price of $9.00 according to MarketBeat.com.
- Christopher Coes, acting under secretary of transportation for policy at the U.S. Department of Transportation, announced at Honeywell’s Advanced Air Mobility (AAM) Summit on Sept. 19 that the final special federal aviation regulation (SFAR) for powered-lift pilot certification and operations is expected to be published in the "next month."
- The FAA technically has until Dec. 16 to finalize the SFAR regulation.
- First movers like Joby Aviation and Archer Aviation are aiming for a 2025 commercialization of eVTOLs.
