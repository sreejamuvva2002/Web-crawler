# Rivian Automotive, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-02-19

**Source:** [Rivian (RIVN) eyes first positive gross profit in Q4 earnings](https://electrek.co/2025/02/19/rivian-rivn-eyes-first-positive-gross-profit-q4-earnings/)  ·  `page_060559`

## Facts

- Rivian expects to achieve its first positive gross profit in Q4 2024.
- Rivian delivered 14,183 vehicles in the final three months of 2024, bringing the annual total to 51,579.
- The delivery total met the company's full-year guidance of 50,500 to 52,000 vehicles.
- Rivian delivered slightly more than the 50,122 vehicles it delivered in 2023.
- A supply shortage beginning in Q3 caused Rivian to cut its 2024 full-year production target to 47,000 to 49,000 vehicles, down from an original 57,000.
- Rivian produced 49,476 units at its Normal, IL plant in 2024, topping its revised target.
- Deliveries and production include the R1S, R1T, and electric delivery and commercial vans.
- The previously discussed shortage of a shared component on the R1 and RCV platforms is no longer a constraint on production.
- CFO Claire McDonough told analysts Rivian expects "a modest gross profit" in the final three months of 2024.
- The positive gross profit will be mainly driven by regulatory credit sales, lower costs thanks to plant upgrades and improved supply contracts, and other revenue outside vehicle sales.
- Rivian's net loss fell to $1.1 billion in Q3, with a gross profit loss of $392 million.
- The company lost around $39,000 on each vehicle delivered in Q3, a drastic improvement from losing over $139,000 per unit in 2022.
- Including a $1 billion convertible note from Volkswagen, Rivian ended Q3 with $6.7 billion in cash and equivalents.
- Wall Street expects Rivian to post Q4 revenue of $1.4 billion, up from $1.3 billion in Q4 2023, and a loss of $0.68 per share compared to a loss of $1.36 per share last year.
- Rivian launched a new joint venture with Volkswagen worth up to $5.8 billion, which CEO RJ Scaringe called a "meaningful financial opportunity."
- Chief Software Officer Wassym Bensaid stated that other OEMs are now "knocking on our door" about similar supply deals for EV tech and software.
- Rivian is preparing to launch its mid-size electric SUV, the R2, early next year.
- The R2 will initially be built at Rivian's Normal, IL facility, but production is expected to significantly expand with plans to open a second plant in Georgia.
- The R2 will start at around $45,000, or nearly half the cost of the current R1T ($71,700) and R1S ($77,700).
- Rivian will also build a smaller, more affordable R3 crossover and high-performance R3X at the Georgia facility.
- Rivian plans to build the Georgia plant in two stages, each adding 200,000 units of annual production capacity.
- Rivian says the R2 and R3 are "critical drivers in the company's long-term growth and profitability."
- Rivian secured a $6.6 billion federal loan for the new EV plant just before Donald Trump took office.
- The funding is now in jeopardy after the Administration announced plans to freeze federal loans.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp told Channel 2 news that Rivian "secured that loan at the tail end of the Biden administration and, you know, I think there's no secret that the Trump administration is taking a look at all those things," adding, "So I don't really know where that stands right now."
- Rivian is confident the funds will be there next year when they go to draw them.
- A Rivian spokesperson said the company is "working hard to onshore US manufacturing, providing thousands of American jobs here in Georgia."
- Rivian's stock is down 12% over the past 12 months and 90% from its all-time high shortly after going public in November 2021.
