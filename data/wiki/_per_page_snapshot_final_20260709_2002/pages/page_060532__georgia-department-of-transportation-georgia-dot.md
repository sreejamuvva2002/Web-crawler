# Georgia Department of Transportation (Georgia DOT)

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** charging_infrastructure  ·  **Published:** 2024-02

**Source:** [Georgia DOT Continues to Advance the Deployment of Electric Vehicle Infrastructure - Columbus CEO](https://thecolumbusceo.com/news/2024/02/georgia-dot-continues-advance-deployment-electric-vehicle-infrastructure/)  ·  `page_060532`

## Facts

- Georgia DOT is advancing the federally approved Georgia National Electric Vehicle Infrastructure (NEVI) Deployment Plan, which has reached a significant milestone.
- Georgia DOT conditionally awarded apparent successful proposers for five locations during Round 1 of Georgia NEVI deployment.
- The electric vehicle chargers will be deployed through a Public-Private-Partnership (P3) utilizing federal and private funding.
- Under the P3 agreement, each team will design, install, finance, operate, and maintain EV charging stations equipped with Direct Current Fast Charging (DCFC) ports for a minimum of five years in compliance with federal rules.
- The Tifton location (PI. No. 0019828) is awarded to EnviroSpark Networks, Inc. at Waffle House (706 7th Street West, Tifton, GA 31794).
- The Fort Valley location (PI. No. 0019829) is awarded to Francis Energy Charging, LLC at Wendy’s (318 GA-49, Byron, GA 31008).
- The Dublin location (PI. No. 0019830) is awarded to Silver Comet Energy, Inc. at RaceTrac Gas Station (2262 US-441, Dublin, GA 31021).
- The Metter location (PI. No. 0019831) is awarded to EnviroSpark Networks, Inc. at Shell Super Stop Gas Station (1205 S Lewis Street, Metter, GA 30439).
- The Brunswick location (PI. No. 0019832) is awarded to Love’s Travel Stops & Country Stores, Inc. at Love’s Travel Stop (2766 Hwy 17, Brunswick, GA 31523).
- Georgia DOT Commissioner Russell R. McMurry, P.E. stated that Georgia's mission is to provide a transportation system focused on innovation, safety, sustainability, and mobility.
- Commissioner McMurry noted Georgia is a leader in E-Mobility with KIA in West Point producing the EV9, as well as the Hyundai KIA Genisis Meta plant and Rivian plant both under construction.
- Commissioner McMurry said deploying the first five charger locations is a good start to ensure fast charging infrastructure is in place as more electric vehicles are on the road.
- Commissioner McMurry expressed that Georgia DOT looks forward to the next round of procurements starting very soon.
- Georgia is among the top 10 states for EV sales.
- Three of the top five largest investments in the EV supply chain are in Georgia.
- Georgia has approximately 1,193 publicly available EV charging stations.
- Only 14 stations have DCFC ports that meet both distance and power requirements under federal rules (minimum of four 150 kW ports and located within 1 mile of an Alternative Fuel Corridor).
- Georgia DOT Deputy Chief Engineer Andrew Heath, P.E., stated the Georgia NEVI Deployment Program outlines the state's approach to using federal funding to address EV infrastructure gaps in the state’s Alternative Fuel Corridors.
- Andrew Heath noted that to be successful, Georgia DOT is turning to the private sector to deliver innovative infrastructure at strategic locations across the state through Public-Private Partnerships.
- Georgia DOT developed the Georgia NEVI implementation approach with broad industry stakeholder input for EV charging infrastructure across the state in accordance with federal law.
- The program aims to establish an interconnected EV charging network that meets customer demands, reduces range concerns, facilitates data collection, and ensures secure, convenient access to publicly available charging infrastructure.
- The Georgia NEVI Deployment Program is derived from the 2021 Bipartisan Infrastructure Law enacted as the Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act (IIJA).
- The program provides nearly $5 billion nationally over five years, which started in the fall of 2022.
- Georgia’s allotment from the formula program is approximately $135 million to develop its portion of the national network.
- The Georgia NEVI Deployment Plan covers the entire state of Georgia and garnered approval from the Federal Highway Administration (FHWA) in January 2023.
- Georgia DOT developed the state’s NEVI plan in consultation with 57 different entities, including other state agencies, MPOs, utility providers, private businesses, and other regional/national groups.
