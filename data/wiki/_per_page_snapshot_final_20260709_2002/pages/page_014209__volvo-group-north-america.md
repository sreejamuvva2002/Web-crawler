# Volvo Group North America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Volvo’s Maryland Facility Highlighted by DOE for Efficiency | Heavy Duty Trucking](https://www.truckinginfo.com/336292/volvos-maryland-facility-highlighted-by-doe-for-its-efficiencies)  ·  `page_014209`

## Facts

- Volvo Group North America’s Hagerstown, Maryland powertrain facility was recently toured by the U.S. Department of Energy’s Better Buildings Better Plants Summit.
- Pierre Jenny, vice president and plant manager of the Hagerstown facility, stated that the DOE visit provided an opportunity to spotlight the plant's efficiencies.
- The company retrofitted crankshaft grinders with Computer Numerically Controlled grinding machines, replacing 13 machines with three.
- The new energy-efficient machines process more crankshafts per day while reducing electricity consumption by 56%.
- The new machines reduce water consumption by 53% and coolant chemicals by 60%.
- The new grinding machines generate less waste, enabling the facility to divert 68 tons from the landfill.
- The project was completed in 2014 as part of the ISO 50001 EnMS and SEP certification.
- Achieved savings from the project helped the facility achieve platinum level Superior Energy Performance certification.
- The company met its first Better Buildings, Better Plants goal of a 25% reduction in energy consumption at U.S. facilities five years ahead of schedule.
- The company set a new goal of achieving 25% more savings by 2024.
- In the first four years of the new challenge, the Volvo Group reduced energy consumption by 17.5% compared with a 2014 baseline.
- Three of the company’s manufacturing sites—Lehigh Valley Operations, New River Valley, and Hagerstown—are platinum-level partners in the DOE’s Superior Energy Performance program.
