# Oyakhire Lab

**Type:** Research Center  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[research-and-development|Research & Development]]  ·  **Location:** Georgia Institute of Technology, Atlanta, Georgia, United States

## Overview

The Oyakhire Lab studies and controls ion and electron transfer at solid-liquid interfaces in electrochemical systems to advance batteries, electrocatalysis, and electrochemical separations.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Research on battery interfacial charge-transfer processes that directly impact electric-vehicle energy storage performance and safety.

## Key facts

- The Oyakhire Lab studies and controls ion and electron transfer at solid-liquid interfaces in electrochemical systems.
- These interfacial charge-transfer processes determine the macroscopic performance of batteries, electrocatalytic cells, and related technologies.
- During charging, lithium- and sodium-metal batteries undergo electrically driven metal deposition and growth; the lab elucidates key steps in metal growth to enable long-lasting, stable batteries.
- The lab develops operando spectroscopy and acoustic sensing methods that provide real-time insights into interphase evolution, guiding design of passivation layers that enhance battery cycle life.
- Research includes recycling minerals from waste using precise electrochemical free-energy control for resource recovery and environmental stewardship.
- The lab created Hypothesis-Informed, Physics-coupled Artificial Intelligence (HIP-AI) to design new molecules for batteries and resource-recovery cells.
- First PhD students Gene Koifman and Luke Cravey joined the lab on 10/01/2025.
- The lab received a Schmidt Science Fellows Catalyst Grant on 12/01/2025.
- The lab received an ACS Sustainable Futures Early Career Postdoctoral-Faculty Bridge Grant on 02/27/2026.
