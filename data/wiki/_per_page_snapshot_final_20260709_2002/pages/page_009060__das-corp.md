# DAS Corp.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [GA: Auto Parts Manufacturer DAS to Establish Facility in Metter, Creating 300 Jobs in Candler County | Trade and Industry Development](https://www.tradeandindustrydev.com/region/georgia/news/ga-auto-parts-manufacturer-das-establish-facility-32205)  ·  `page_009060`

## Facts

- Georgia Gov. Brian P. Kemp announced that DAS Corp. (DAS), an automotive parts company, will establish a new manufacturing facility in Metter.
- The new facility will create 300 new jobs.
- The project will generate more than $35 million in investment in Candler County.
- The initiative will also involve collaborative indirect investments.
- DAS was established in Korea in 1987 and specializes in designing and manufacturing seating systems, safety seating components, and seating structures.
- DAS is a key supplier for Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia (KMMG) and Hyundai Motor Group.
- The new DAS manufacturing facility will be located in the Candler County Industrial Park in Metter.
- Operations at the new facility are projected to begin in the latter half of 2024.
- Over the course of the next five years, DAS will recruit for assembly technician roles.
- DAS intends to establish collaborations with local employment services as part of its hiring process.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that suppliers for the Hyundai Metaplant resulted in over $2 billion in investment last fiscal year alone, helping Georgia achieve a third straight year of record-breaking economic growth.
- Governor Brian Kemp noted that Georgia's logistics assets, including a reliable network of rail lines and highways, connect companies like DAS to key business partners statewide and to global markets.
- Sean Kim, Chief Operating Officer at DAS, said the strategic location near prominent cities like Savannah and Macon facilitated convenient access and played a pivotal role in the decision-making process.
- Sean Kim added that DAS deemed Metter an ideal location in anticipation of its role as a global electric vehicle production hub due to its close proximity to Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- Metter Mayor Ed Boyd stated that the City of Metter is proud to welcome DAS and expects a transformative impact on the community, stimulating investment in housing, creating other business opportunities, and improving socioeconomic wellbeing.
- Glyn Thrift, Chairman of the Candler County Board of Commissioners, thanked DAS for choosing Candler County as its new home and stated they will provide a boost to spur more economic development.
- Griff Lynch, President and CEO of the Georgia Ports Authority, expressed pride in supporting the growing automotive manufacturing presence in the state and driving economic activity and jobs.
- Alyce Thornhill, Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy at the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team, represented the agency on this competitive project.
- The GDEcD project was executed in partnership with the Candler County Industrial Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, Electric Cities of Georgia, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Georgia is well-positioned for the arrival of Hyundai’s supplier network due to years of consistent investment in site preparation, workforce solutions, and connectivity.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson described DAS as another exciting addition to Georgia’s automotive community and congratulated the company along with partners in the City of Metter and Candler County.
