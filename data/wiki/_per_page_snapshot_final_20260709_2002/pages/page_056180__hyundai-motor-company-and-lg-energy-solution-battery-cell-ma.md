# Hyundai Motor Company and LG Energy Solution Battery Cell Manufacturing JV

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hyundai, LG Energy Solution Launch Battery Cell Manufacturing JV In US With $4.3B Investment - Hyundai Mo - Benzinga](https://www.benzinga.com/markets/equities/23/05/32590920/hyundai-lg-energy-solution-launch-battery-cell-manufacturing-jv-in-us-with-4-3b-investment)  ·  `page_056180`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Company and LG Energy Solution announced an EV battery cell manufacturing joint venture in the U.S.
- Hyundai Group and LG Energy Solution will each hold a 50% stake in the joint venture.
- The joint venture involves an investment of over $4.3 billion (KRW 5.7 trillion).
- The annual production capacity of the new joint venture facility will be 30 GWh.
- The 30 GWh capacity will be able to support the production of 300,000 EV units annually.
- The manufacturing facility will be located in Bryan County, Savannah, Georgia.
- The site is adjacent to the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America, which is currently under construction.
- Construction on the joint venture facility is scheduled to start in the second half of 2023.
- The joint venture plans to begin battery production at the end of 2025 at the earliest.
- Hyundai Mobis will assemble battery packs using cells from the new plant.
- The assembled battery packs will be supplied to Hyundai Motor Group's U.S. manufacturing facilities for production of Hyundai, Kia and Genesis EV models.
- Jaehoon Chang, President and CEO of Hyundai Motor Company, stated that Hyundai Motor Group is focusing on its electrification efforts to secure a leadership position in the global auto industry.
