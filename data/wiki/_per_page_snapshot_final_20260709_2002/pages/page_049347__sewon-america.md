# Sewon America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-02

**Source:** [Sewon America new GA facility will create 740 jobs](https://valdostatoday.com/news-2/region/2023/02/sewon-america-new-ga-facility-will-create-740-jobs/)  ·  `page_049347`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Sewon America will invest over $300 million in a new manufacturing facility creating 740 new jobs in Georgia.
- Sewon America is a subsidiary of Korean-based Sewon Precision Industry Co.
- The company will create 740 new jobs in Rincon, Effingham County.
- This project is the largest known private investment in the City of Rincon to-date.
- The announcement fulfills the commitment to create jobs and investment in all four Savannah Harbor Interstate-16 Joint Development Authority (JDA) counties as a result of Hyundai’s Metaplant location in Bryan County.
- Suppliers for Hyundai's Metaplant have now committed over $1.8 billion in investment, surpassing initial estimates by at least $800 million.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp previously congratulated Chairman Kim on the company’s expansion during his first international trip as governor to Korea.
- Speaker of the House Jon Burns represents part of Effingham County and played a role in securing the project and engaging Sewon America executives.
- Lt. Governor Burt Jones commended Governor Brian Kemp, Sewon America, and stakeholders on the historic economic development announcement for Rincon.
- The investment of more than $300 million in Effingham County will positively impact the local community and state.
- Sewon America operates one of the largest automobile body parts manufacturing plants in North America.
- The company opened a manufacturing facility in LaGrange in 2008, which has since doubled in size.
- Following the completion of the Rincon facility, Sewon is expected to support 1,600 jobs in Georgia.
- Sewon America was founded 15 years ago in west Georgia.
- Vice Chairman Kim expressed excitement about building another Sewon facility in east Georgia and credited the growth to the Sewon team, Governor Kemp, the Georgia Department of Economic Development, and Effingham County.
- Sewon’s new facility will be located at the Grande View industrial park in Rincon.
- The new manufacturing location will produce electric vehicle (EV) body parts for original equipment manufacturers (OEMs), including Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- Operations at the new facility are expected to begin in 2025.
- Rincon Mayor Ken Lee welcomed Sewon America to the community and noted the project will be the largest employment and private investment project in the city’s history.
- Chairman of the Effingham County Industrial Development Authority (IDA) Troy Smith stated that Sewon selected Grande View industrial park for significant investment.
- The IDA partnered with the City of Rincon and the Effingham County Board of Commissioners to secure the project.
- Executive Director of the Georgia Ports Authority Griff Lynch expressed excitement to support Sewon’s growth alongside Hyundai’s announcement.
- Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy Alyce Thornhill represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The project was a partnership with the Effingham County Industrial Development Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia EMC.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson noted that Sewon integrated into Georgia’s automotive community through local colleges, Georgia Quick Start, and statewide initiatives like Georgia Made.
- Sewon will support Hyundai Motor Group’s operations from Effingham County, which has a skilled workforce, infrastructure, and collaborative spirit to support the company's growth.
- On October 25, 2022, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America officially broke ground in neighboring Bryan County on its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility.
- Georgia is positioned as a hub for the electric mobility industry.
- Since 2020, more than 35 EV-related projects have announced more than $21 billion in investment and 26,700 jobs in Georgia.
- Sewon America was established in 2008 in LaGrange as a Georgia company.
- Sewon primarily operates in the Motor Vehicles and Car Bodies industry within the Transportation Equipment sector, producing stamped body components.
- Sewon Group is celebrated for high-quality automotive parts and will continue its tradition of excellence in the new Rincon facility.
