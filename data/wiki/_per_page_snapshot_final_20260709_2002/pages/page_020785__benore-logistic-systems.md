# Benore Logistic Systems

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2025-04-11

**Source:** [Benore adds 14 hydrogen Hyundai trucks to fleet, now ferrying EVs in Georgia - Driving Hydrogen](https://drivinghydrogen.com/2025/04/11/benore-adds-14-hydrogen-hyundai-trucks-to-fleet-now-running-routes-in-georgia/)  ·  `page_020785`

## Facts

- Benore Logistic Systems has added 14 hydrogen-powered Hyundai Xcient trucks to its fleet.
- The vehicles are now in daily service around Savannah, Georgia.
- The trucks are being used on dedicated routes to support Glovis EV America’s logistics operation for Hyundai’s new Metaplant.
- The rollout began quietly in January with four trucks, and by March, all 14 were up and running.
- Benore is based in Spartanburg County, South Carolina.
- The company says the trucks offer zero tailpipe emissions, efficient refuelling, and performance expected in heavy-duty logistics.
- The hydrogen trucks join eight battery-electric trucks already in the company’s fleet.
- The hydrogen trucks are running full-time, hauling freight on fixed routes, Just-in-Time.
- Benore’s involvement started with Glovis, Hyundai’s logistics arm, through a long-standing partnership in Georgia.
- That relationship expanded into work with Glovis EV America and led to the company’s role supporting Hyundai’s EV production at the Savannah Metaplant.
- Hyundai Motor Company manufactures the Xcient trucks.
- Deployment is handled by HTWO Logistics, a joint venture between Hyundai and Glovis America.
- Benore manages the fleet and day-to-day operations for these hydrogen trucks.
- Drivers have completed specific training for the hydrogen trucks ahead of launch.
- Dennis Kunz is Vice President of Revenue Strategy and Operation Development at Benore.
- Dennis Kunz stated that the hydrogen fuel cell trucks represent a significant step forward for Benore and its ability to deliver innovative, sustainable logistics solutions.
- Dennis Kunz noted that partnerships with both Hyundai and Glovis are key to the company’s mission and values around sustainability and exploring advanced technology.
- Benore’s trucks follow Hyundai’s own earlier deployment of 21 Xcient fuel cell trucks at the same site.
- The Hyundai fleet is operated by Glovis America and was the first major deployment of Hyundai’s hydrogen trucks in the U.S.
- Together, the two fleets are now a regular presence around the HMGMA site in Savannah.
- Hyundai sees the Xcient as a ready-for-work fuel cell solution, capable of quick refuelling and uptime logistics operators rely on.
- Benore describes the vehicles as part of its push toward cleaner transport, aligned with a broader strategy to adopt advanced vehicle technology.
