# Novelis Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Novelis Seeks $15M For Recycle Facility Construction Delays - Law360](https://www.law360.com/articles/2320991/novelis-seeks-15m-for-recycle-facility-construction-delays)  ·  `page_047349`

## Facts

- Novelis filed a lawsuit in Georgia federal court against Denham-Blythe Co. Inc. alleging mismanagement of a recycling facility intended to serve as a sister facility to an automotive aluminum plant in Guthrie, Kentucky.
- Denham-Blythe was reportedly obliged to indemnify Novelis from losses caused by construction failures but allegedly failed to pay damages even after a third-party engineer found it at fault.
- Novelis stated that Denham-Blythe repeatedly breached contracts, failed to indemnify Novelis for claims, failed to cure breaches, and persistently delayed final completion, denying Novelis full use of its plant as planned.
- Novelis made costly repairs of Denham-Blythe's defective work and breaches, and the delays caused improper liens to be filed against Novelis's project and tens of millions in ongoing and increasing damages.
- Denham-Blythe did not immediately respond to a request for comment on Friday.
- Novelis set out several years ago to construct a sprawling recycling facility that would furnish aluminum for its auto finishing plant, with an intended investment of $365 million per the company's website.
- The facility was expected to produce 240,000 tons of aluminum sheet metal per year at peak capacity.
- Denham-Blythe was tasked with the overall design and engineering of the project and held final responsibility for its outcome, though it could subcontract portions.
- During construction, Denham-Blythe allegedly botched key parts, including improperly installing the electrical system of a shredder that turns aluminum scrap into fines.
- When attempting to repair the mistake, Denham-Blythe sent metal shavings into the shredder's transformer, causing a serious failure of the system in July 2024.
- This failure occurred when the facility was expecting to start ramping up production, forcing Novelis to buy scrap materials from third parties at a premium.
- Later that year, an overhead crane within the factory allegedly broke and failed, causing a serious safety event.
- An independent review commissioned after the incident found that Denham-Blythe's installation failed to meet industry standards for construction.
- Despite its own investigation findings and notice from Novelis, Denham-Blythe failed to indemnify or compensate Novelis for repairs associated with the crane rail failure or losses due to inability to produce ingot.
- Novelis alleged two counts of breach of contract against Denham-Blythe, noting screwups were not limited to those two incidents but included a range of other delays and faulty installations.
- Novelis is seeking recovery of $15 million in damages it says it sustained, along with attorney fees and other litigation costs.
- Novelis is represented by Michael J. Breslin, Daniel B. Swaja, Daniel K. Johnson, and Kurtis G. Anderson of Kilpatrick Townsend & Stockton LLP.
- The case is Novelis Corporation v. Denham-Blythe Company Inc., case number 1:25-cv-01748, in the U.S. District Court for the Northern District of Georgia.
