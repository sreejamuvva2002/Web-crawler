# Bonnell Aluminum

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2013-01-22

**Source:** [Bonnell Aluminum | Business World Magazine](https://www.businessworldmag.com/business-profiles-and-featured-articles/canadian-manufacturing/bonnell-aluminum/)  ·  `page_053083`

## Facts

- Bonnell Aluminum has its headquarters in Newnan, Georgia.
- The company operates state-of-the-art manufacturing facilities in Carthage, Tennessee; Niles, Michigan; and Elkhart, Indiana.
- Bonnell Aluminum employs a team of more than 1,300 people.
- The company specializes in the production of mill finish, anodized, painted, fabricated, and thermal-barrier assemblies of customized aluminum extrusions.
- Product offerings include applications up to 16-inch diameter circles with mirror-like surface finishing.
- Aluminum extrusions are used in construction, pleasure crafts, heat sinks for L.E.D. lighting, photovoltaic panels, automotive engineering, walkways, windows, and shower enclosures.
- The company's work is visible at the Georgia Aquarium, Luxor’s pyramid-styled hotel & casino in Las Vegas, and the Philadelphia Eagles football stadium.
- William Bonnell acquired his first extrusion press in 1939, making his firm the first in the industry to have in-house extrusion capability.
- Scientists working with the U.S. Government in the Manhattan Project turned to William Bonnell for help extruding radioactive uranium to develop what became the world’s first atomic bomb.
- Bonnell was the originator of more than 500 shapes which are still in use today.
- In 1955, the William L. Bonnell Company emerged after buying out patents and trademarks affiliated with Trimedge.
- The company expanded plant operations in Georgia under William Bonnell's leadership until his death in 1960.
- In 1965, the company became a wholly owned subsidiary of Ethyl Corporation.
- Ethyl Corporation opened the Tennessee facility in 1969 and later acquired an aluminum window fabricator formerly known as Capitol Products.
- By the late 80s, Ethyl consolidated its aluminum, plastics, and energy holdings into Tredegar Corporation, where Bonnell Aluminum functions as a core business unit.
- Duncan Crowdis serves as President of Bonnell Aluminum and leads an experienced group of executives.
- During a period when Chinese imports captured up to 20% of America's market due to heavy government subsidies, Bonnell joined other extruders to file petitions with the U.S. Department of Commerce citing unfair trade practices.
- Working with support from the U.S. Trade Commission, the team successfully secured an ultimate decision by the Department of Commerce to apply duties to extruded product imports from China.
- As a result of these duties, Chinese imports have been reduced to a trickle compared to 2009 levels.
- Following the recent economic downturn and diminished construction rates, Bonnell strategically diversified by acquiring Indiana-based extruder AACOA.
- The company is pursuing opportunities in the automotive industry driven by a push for vehicle light-weighting to meet Federal fuel efficiency guidelines implemented by 2025.
- Aluminum is utilized in automotive applications because it is one-third the weight of steel but offers appropriate strength for many applications.
- Transportation will provide a significant future direction for the extrusion industry, including Bonnell Aluminum.
- Bonnell Aluminum works in conjunction with the Aluminum Extruder’s Council to educate designers on the benefits and opportunities of using aluminum extrusions.
- The company's leadership emphasizes securing long-term client relationships and retaining long-serving employees through a culture of high integrity and trust.
- Bonnell Aluminum's vision is to act like a small, regional provider that genuinely wants customer business while leveraging its capabilities as a large extruder.
