# Georgia Manufacturing Extension Partnership

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2025-07

**Source:** [Georgia Manufacturing Extension Partnership at Georgia Tech Celebrates 65 Years of Service - Augusta CEO](https://augustaceo.com/features/2025/07/georgia-manufacturing-extension-partnership-georgia-tech-celebrates-65-years-service/)  ·  `page_012571`

## Facts

- The Georgia Manufacturing Extension Partnership is a program of the Enterprise Innovation Institute at Georgia Tech.
- Gov. Brian P. Kemp recognized GaMEP for 65 years of service to the manufacturing industry at the Georgia State Capitol on Monday, July 14th, 2025.
- The commendation acknowledges GaMEP's commitment to supporting manufacturers across the state through educational opportunities and technical assistance.
- GaMEP leverages world-renowned expertise and resources to advance manufacturing and economic prosperity across Georgia.
- According to the National Associations of Manufacturers, the manufacturing industry adds $82 billion to the Georgia economy and employs 425,000 residents.
- By 1960, more than 4,500 manufacturers had established operations in Georgia due to strategic efforts by state leaders and economic development assistance from Georgia Tech.
- The Georgia General Assembly voted to establish the Georgia Tech Industrial Extension Service (now known as GaMEP) to provide technical support to manufacturers facing new challenges from industry growth.
- GaMEP created statewide field offices that deliver on-site technical expertise to help manufacturers thrive and maintain a direct link between industry and innovation.
- Tim Israel, GaMEP director and EI2 associate vice president — corporate engagement/firm-based programs, stated that the program's role is to support manufacturers to grow the state's economy.
- In 2024, GaMEP generated a return on investment of $294 for every state dollar allocated to manufacturing projects.
- Over the past decade, GaMEP provided assistance and education to more than 3,900 manufacturers across 144 counties.
- During that same decade, GaMEP helped manufacturers create or retain 14,500 jobs.
- Over the past decade, GaMEP helped manufacturers invest $1 billion in capital improvements.
- Over the past decade, GaMEP helped manufacturers realize $3.5 billion in sales.
- Over the past decade, GaMEP helped manufacturers save nearly $450 million in costs.
- GaMEP primarily serves small- to medium-sized manufacturers, with 75% of its clients employing less than 250 workers.
- GaMEP's top-served industries include fabricated metal product, food, machinery, chemical, and transportation equipment manufacturing.
- David Bridges, EI2 vice president, noted that when Georgia manufacturers become more productive and profitable, they hire more people, pay better wages, and stabilize local economies, especially in rural and underserved areas.
- David Bridges also stated that GaMEP's work creates pathways for career advancement for frontline workers who might not have had previous opportunities.
- GaMEP's reach spans 10 regions across the state, each led by a dedicated region manager who lives and works locally to connect manufacturers with expert teams and resources.
- Lloyd Avram, Georgia Association of Manufacturers CEO and president, called GaMEP a trusted collaborator and valued partner in strengthening manufacturing across Georgia.
- Lloyd Avram stated that together they have supported thousands of manufacturers statewide to embrace innovation, improve operations, and remain competitive.
- Coastal Region: GaMEP served 269 manufacturers across Bryan, Bulloch, Camden, Chatham, Effingham, Glynn, Liberty, Long, McIntosh, and Screven counties, creating/retaining 283 jobs, $23,171,292 in capital improvements, saving $1,645,061 in costs, and realizing $13,965,000 in sales, with Roger Wood Foods as a success story.
