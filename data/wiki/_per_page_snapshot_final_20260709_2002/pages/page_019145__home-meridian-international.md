# Home Meridian International

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- Home Meridian International is investing $23.5 million in a Georgia expansion.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp welcomed the company to Georgia and confirmed it will build a new distribution center in Liberty County.
- The selected site for the expansion is Tradeport East Business Center, which holds a GRAD (Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development) rating.
- The GRAD certification program is administered by the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) to help network planners and landowners catalyze economic expansion.
- According to the GDEcD, more than 8,000 jobs have been created at GRAD sites.
- Liberty County Development Authority President Allen Brown stated that HMI selected Tradeport East Business Center as the most productive location on the Georgia coast for its long-term growth.
- In September, the Port of Savannah handled the largest shipment ever made on the east coast of the United States.
- The Georgia Port Authority announced that August was its most productive month and that it was granted the prestige of being the country’s main port for U.S. exports.
- Griff Lynch, Executive Director of the Georgia Port Authority, stated that Georgia’s unmatched connectivity via roads and railroads, combined with Savannah’s global port network, makes it ideal for logistics operations like IHM.
- Hank Evans, Director of GDEcD’s Global Trade Division for Logistics, Energy, Agribusiness and Food Projects, collaborated with the Liberty County Development Authority, Georgia Port Authority, and Georgia Power on the project.
- GDEcD commissioner Pat Wilson stated that HMI’s decision to expand in Georgia is supported by the state's logistics network, including its ports, road infrastructure, rail formula, and the world’s busiest airport.
