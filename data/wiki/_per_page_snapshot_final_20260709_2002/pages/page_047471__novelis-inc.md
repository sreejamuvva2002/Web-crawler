# Novelis Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-05-31

**Source:** [Novelis reports $84M loss in Q4 after Oswego fires but signals how soon the plant could reopen | Oswego County | nny360.com](https://www.nny360.com/news/oswegocounty/novelis-reports-84m-loss-in-q4-after-oswego-fires-but-signals-how-soon-the-plant/article_e959400e-55be-5173-a204-30ed2ed31b48.html)  ·  `page_047471`

## Facts

- Novelis Inc. reported an $84 million loss in its fourth quarter due to two fires at its aluminum plant in Oswego County last year.
- The company reported a loss for the three months ended March 31, compared with a profit of $294 million in the same period a year earlier.
- Novelis attributed the drop in profits primarily to $630 million in pre-tax losses related to the fires.
- For fiscal 2026, Novelis saw its profit fall 98% to $15 million from the previous year, primarily because of the two fires, restructuring charges, and unrealized derivative losses.
- CEO Steve Fisher stated the company is focused on restarting the plant in Scriba within the next few weeks, well ahead of the previous estimate of the end of June.
- A large portion of the plant has been closed since the fires.
- A blaze on Sept. 16 struck the roof of the plant’s hot mill and caused extensive damage.
- The second fire occurred on Nov. 20 in a location where repair work from the September fire was taking place, further damaging the mill.
- Workers safely evacuated the plant during both fires with no injuries reported.
- Novelis stated that commissioning activities are ramping up and expects the hot mill to be back in service in the next few weeks to support pent-up demand and normalize shipments over time.
- The company believes several factors contributed to the September fire, including the composition and characteristics of the aluminum coil in the mill, as well as lubricants in the mill and surrounding structure.
- The plant is a major producer of aluminum sheet for the auto industry, and its hot mill shutdown has impacted automakers.
- Novelis's biggest customer, Ford Motor Co., reported taking a $2 billion hit to its earnings last year because of the disruption the fires caused to its aluminum supply.
- With more than 1,120 workers, Novelis is the largest manufacturing employer in Oswego County.
