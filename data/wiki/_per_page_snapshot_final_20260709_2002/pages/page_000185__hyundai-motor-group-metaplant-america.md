# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-10-25

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America Celebrates Anniversary of Groundbreaking](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/hyundai-motor-group-metaplant-america-celebrates-anniversary-of-groundbreaking-301966947.html)  ·  `page_000185`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) celebrated the one-year anniversary of its groundbreaking in Bryan County on Oct. 25, 2023.
- The company officially released the latest design renderings of the electric vehicle and battery manufacturing facility, also known as the Metaplant.
- Oscar Kwon is the CEO of HMGMA.
- Production at the facility is on track to start in early 2025, or possibly sooner.
- José Muñoz is a colleague of Oscar Kwon at HMGMA who commented on the production timeline.
- Approximately 2,000 construction workers are working at the nearly 3,000-acre megasite.
- Construction has already begun on all HMGMA production-related buildings.
- 99.9 percent of all foundation work is complete.
- An estimated 81 percent of steel framing has been installed, using 27,045 tons of steel.
- Roof, floor, and wall construction have begun and are in varying stages of completion.
- Architects designed the Metaplant to incorporate technologically advanced elements and features representing Hyundai's brand identity, the greater Savannah area, nature, and renewable energy.
- The exhibition hall at the front of the plant was designed as a square to pay homage to historic squares in downtown Savannah.
- The building will feature an exhibition space with its own central courtyard and water features for artful exhibitions and tour meetings.
- All work zones, from office to production areas, are designed to encourage communication and team collaboration.
- Designs will feature open layouts with ample daylight and indoor landscaping.
- Production areas such as the Assembly Building will have respite zones with naturally lit skylights at the center for employee breaks and meetings.
- A solar parking lot at the front of the site will provide 5.2 megawatts of energy.
- Solar panels will be positioned over the top of 1,878 vehicle spots to protect vehicles from direct sun while generating power.
- Beginning with mass production, electricity used to power the Metaplant will come from 100 percent renewable sources.
- This aligns with Hyundai's commitment to carbon neutrality by 2045.
- The 3,050-foot-long front exterior of the building will be lit in two parallel lines inspired by a stream.
- The lighting will outline an area where interstate travelers can view vehicles as they travel by conveyor from paint to general assembly.
- A 41-acre ecological park will be prominently featured in front of the Metaplant, including trails, running tracks, environmental art displays, sports fields, picnic areas, and a pond for employees.
- HMGMA is hiring for all positions from production to administrative roles.
- The company has currently hired 290 employees (referred to as Meta Pros), consisting of 182 salary and 108 hourly workers.
- An additional 53 South Korean expatriates are assisting with initiating operations.
- At full capacity, 8,500 employees will be employed on-site between HMGMA, the battery JV, and on-site suppliers.
- In total, 14,476 people will be employed between the Metaplant campus and all off-site suppliers.
- HMGMA is Hyundai Motor Group's first dedicated Electric Vehicle mass-production plant located in Bryan County, GA.
- The new plant will feature a highly connected, automated, and flexible manufacturing system that organically connects all elements of the EV ecosystem.
- The Georgia facility will operate as an intelligent manufacturing plant where order collection, procurement, logistics, and production are optimized utilizing AI and data.
- The innovative manufacturing system will create a human-centered work environment with robots assisting human workers.
- Hyundai Motor Group is a global enterprise with about 250,000 employees worldwide.
- Hyundai Motor Group's mobility brands include Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis.
