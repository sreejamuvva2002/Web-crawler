# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hydrogen trucks have been deployed by Hyundai for Georgia EV plant logistics - Hydrogen and Carbon Capture Technology World Expo 2026](https://www.hydrogen-worldexpo.com/industry-news/hydrogen-trucks-deployed-hyundai-georgia-ev-plant-logistics)  ·  `page_020774`

## Facts

- Hyundai will be using hydrogen fuel cell heavy-duty trucks to bring parts to its US EV plant in Georgia through a partnership with a Californian-based logistics firm.
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) has placed a preliminary order of 21 Class 8 XCIENT trucks onto the road.
- The purpose of these vehicles is to transport vehicle parts from suppliers to the factory on a daily basis.
- HTWO Logistics, a joint venture between Hyundai and Glovis, has planned to install a hydrogen refuelling station at the Megasite to support this deployment.
- The parties involved are currently waiting for the hydrogen production capacity to be announced soon.
- Due to the trucks connecting suppliers and HMGMA's on-site consolidation centre, the firms have released a plan to expand the logistics to a broader network of suppliers.
- HTWO Logistics was set up earlier this year as a brand that will enable each stage of the entire hydrogen value chain, from production and storage to transportation and utilisation.
- Hyundai's North America commercial vehicle and hydrogen business boss Jim Park stated that HMGMA's deployment of XCIENT for logistics and operations is one of the main initiatives of HTWO, delivering on their strategy to showcase a closed-loop hydrogen logistics ecosystem and improve manufacturing environmental sustainability.
