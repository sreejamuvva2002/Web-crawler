# Electric Vehicle (EV) Industry & Policy Landscape

**Source:** [Tesla's Ongoing Austerity Measures, Rivian-Apple Partnership Buzz, Biden Administration's Clampdown: Biggest EV Stories Of The Week — TradingView News](https://www.tradingview.com/news/benzinga:cc045f18b094b:0-tesla-s-ongoing-austerity-measures-rivian-apple-partnership-buzz-biden-administration-s-clampdown-biggest-ev-stories-of-the-week/)  ·  `page_059085`

## Facts

- Electric vehicle stocks retreated in the week ending May 10 amid profit taking, with earnings reports weighing down several stocks.
- Tesla executive Rich Otto, head of product, departed after a seven-year stint, citing that recent layoffs rocking the company and its morale have thrown harmony out of balance.
- Tesla removed most job postings from its website, with North American sites listing just three open manufacturing jobs as of May 1 (down from 3,400 openings in the U.S., Mexico, and Canada).
- Tesla nearly disbanded its Supercharger network team but may expand the network according to CEO Elon Musk.
- Elon Musk stated Tesla will spend well over $500M expanding the Supercharger network to create thousands of new chargers this year.
- Musk noted that the $500M expansion cost covers only new sites and expansions, not counting operations costs which are much higher.
- Tesla shipped 62,617 made-in-China EVs in April, down 18% year-over-year according to China Passenger Car Association data.
- Of the April shipments, 30,746 units were exports while domestic sales totaled 31,421 units, marking a decrease of approximately 50% from the previous month and over 21% compared to last year.
- Tesla sent former China head Tom Zhu back to China as it struggles with sagging sales amid competitive pressure.
- Tom Zhu was promoted to Tesla’s top leadership team last year and was stationed in Austin before his return to China.
- Rivian reported first-quarter revenue that exceeded estimates but widened loss per share compared to a year ago.
- Rivian maintained its 2024 production forecast of 57,000 units.
- Rivian CFO Claire McDonough stated the 2024 capital expenditure guidance was reduced by $550 million to $1.2 billion due to moving the first R2 production line to the Normal, Illinois plant.
- DigiTimes reported Apple is assessing a partnership with U.S. EV startup Rivian, though neither company clarified the rumor.
- Fisker's Austrian subsidiary Fisker GmbH voluntarily filed for bankruptcy protection on Tuesday, clarifying no other Fisker entities are included in the restructuring.
- NHTSA opened another investigation into potential issues with the Fisker Ocean electric SUV's automatic emergency braking.
- The Biden administration plans to announce tariffs on Chinese imports in strategic sectors including EVs to protect the domestic EV industry from competition.
- The U.S. will maintain existing tariffs on many Chinese goods set by former President Donald Trump while adding new tariffs to semiconductors and solar equipment.
- The government is reportedly planning to hike EV tariffs, which are likely to quadruple.
- Ford Motor Co. reversed its plan to fully transition to battery EVs in Europe by 2030 due to softer-than-expected demand for pureplay EVs.
- Martin Sander, head of Ford’s passenger cars business in Europe, stated the company will continue selling internal combustion engine cars beyond 2030 if customers demand them and will focus on plug-in hybrids.
- The KraneShares Electric Vehicles and Future Mobility Index ETF (KARS) ended Friday down 1.87% at $22.14, falling 2.55% for the week.
- EV stock performances for the week included Tesla (-7.02%), Nio (-8.98%), XPeng (-14.68%), Li Auto (-4.68%), Fisker (-32.69%), Workhorse Group (+12.50%), Hyzon Motors (-12.50%), Canoo (-7.58%), Rivian (-0.79%), Lucid Group (-4.49%), Faraday Future (+12.71%), Nikola Corp. (-18.33%), and VinFast Auto (+4.15%).
