# Continental AG

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2025-11-14

**Source:** [Continental to Discontinue Operations at Textile Plant Aldora Mills in Barnesville, Georgia - Continental AG](https://www.continental.com/en/press/press-releases/20251114-aldora-mills-closure/)  ·  `page_018626`

## Facts

- Continental will discontinue operations at its Aldora Mills textile manufacturing plant in Barnesville, Georgia by the end of 2026.
- The Aldora Mills facility employs approximately 230 people.
- The facility produces tire cord fabric, hose yarn, and knitted fabric used exclusively within Continental’s Tires and ContiTech group sectors.
- Continental acquired the Aldora Mills facility in 1988.
- The decision to discontinue operations follows a comprehensive business review to safeguard the company’s profitability, competitiveness, and business performance in the Americas region.
- The plant has not been cost competitive for many years despite continuous turnaround efforts.
- Ongoing changes in global market dynamics and supply chains have made it necessary to take this decision.
- Continental will support affected employees during the transition by offering career counseling and access to employment resources in collaboration with local partners.
- The U.S. remains a key market for Continental.
- Continental has invested around 1.5 billion USD in its tire manufacturing operations in the United States over the last decade alone.
- Continental operates tire manufacturing plants in Plymouth, Indiana; Clinton, Mississippi; Sumter, South Carolina; and Mount Vernon, Illinois.
- Continental’s U.S. tire plants have an annual production capacity of more than 16 million tires.
- Continental employs more than 8,800 people in the U.S. across its Tires and ContiTech group sectors.
- In October, the company announced a more than 85 million USD investment to expand manufacturing operations at its ContiTech plant in Mount Pleasant, Iowa.
- In September 2024, Continental broke ground on its first fully owned tire distribution center in the Dallas–Fort Worth metroplex.
- The new Dallas–Fort Worth distribution center is scheduled to open in early 2026.
- The new facility will enhance service levels and customer support across the region.
- The new facility complements seven partner-owned distribution centers that currently serve the U.S. market.
- Allison Zora serves as Head of PR & Brand Communication for Continental Tires in the Americas.
