# Continental Tire the Americas, LLC d.b.a. General Tire

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 1915-09-29

**Source:** [General Tire - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/General_Tire)  ·  `page_018834`

## Facts

- Continental Tire the Americas, LLC, d.b.a. General Tire, is an American manufacturer of tires for motor vehicles, and semi trucks.
- Founded in 1915 in Akron, Ohio, by William Francis O'Neil, Winfred E. Fouse, Charles J. Jahant, Robert Iredell, and H.B. Pushee as The General Tire & Rubber Company using funding from Michael O'Neil's department store.
- Initial capitalization included $200,000 USD borrowed from the store.
- Early products included the General Jumbo premium replacement tire for Ford Model Ts, a low-pressure General Balloon Jumbo tire, and Dual 90 tires.
- By 1930, the company had 14 retail stores and about 1.8% of the tire market.
- During the depression, The General Tire & Rubber Company bought out Yale Tire and Rubber Company and India Tire and Rubber Company, increasing market share to 2.7% by 1933.
- In 1984, the company diversified into a conglomerate (GenCorp, Inc.) with holdings in tire manufacturing, rubber compounds (DiversiTech General), rocketry and aeronautics (Aerojet), and broadcasting (RKO General).
- The compounds division was spun off and became OMNOVA Solutions Inc.
- The rocketry and aeronautics business was kept and expanded, eventually becoming Aerojet Rocketdyne Holdings, Inc.
- The company's tire division was sold to Germany's Continental AG in 1987, becoming Continental General Tire Corp. before its re–incorporation again to its current name.
- Headquarters moved to Fort Mill, South Carolina in 2008 and remains there as of the page's publication.
- General Tire is the official tire supplier of the ARCA Menards Series and NASCAR Canada Series.
- General Tire is an official partner of Major League Fishing and Jeep Jamboree USA.
