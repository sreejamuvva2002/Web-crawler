# SungEel Recycling Park Georgia, LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2023-06-01

**Source:** [Progress Being Made On Bringing New Lithium-Ion Battery Plant - 92.1 WLHR](https://921wlhr.com/progress-being-made-on-bringing-new-lithium-ion-battery-plant/)  ·  `page_050796`

## Facts

- Stephens County is one step closer to adding another international company to its roster of industrial/manufacturing facilities in the Haystone Brady Business Park.
- At a recent Stephens County Development Authority meeting, a motion was approved to authorize the SCDA chairman to sign a Memorandum of Understanding with Korean lithium-ion battery recycling company SungEel HighTech.
- SCDA President and CEO Brittany Ivey is working to bring the community’s MOU in line with the MOU that SungEel has already signed with the State of Georgia.
- The MOU between SungEel and the State of Georgia, signed April 17 of this year, stipulates that a REBA Grant in the amount of $700,000 will be awarded to SungEel through the Stephens County Development Authority.
- REBA Grant funding comes from the Georgia Department of Community Affairs and is an incentive program used to help close deals when companies consider Georgia versus another state or country for location or expansion.
- REBA funds may finance various fixed-asset needs including infrastructure, real estate acquisition, construction, or machinery and equipment.
- The state is only legally allowed to offer incentives for projects when the company is also considering locations in at least one other state.
- Local incentives included in the SCDA and SungEel MOU will not be made public until after both parties sign it.
- The facility will operate under the name SungEel Recycling Park Georgia, LLC.
- Stephens County has changed its land sale strategy since 2017 by asking for full price for the land in Haystone Brady instead of giving it away.
- Under the current MOU terms, the company will be placed on the tax digest within the first two years of operation.
- The project will bring additional jobs to Stephens County with higher than average wages.
- An August 2022 press release from Georgia Governor Brian Kemp stated SungEel Recycling Park Georgia, LLC will locate its first U.S. recycling facility in Georgia as a subsidiary of Korea-based global industry leader SungEel HiTech Co., Ltd.
- The company will invest more than $37 million in the new facility and create 104 jobs in Stephens County.
- The SCDA has been working on bringing this facility to Stephens County since November of 2021.
- After a site visit by SungEel company representatives, a contract was signed for the company to purchase Haystone Brady lots 3 and 4, located behind the NIFCO KTW facility and next to the SKAPS building on Haystone Brady Road.
- The lots were chosen because they are pad ready and already have sewer utility access, which will result in a quicker project completion timeline than undeveloped parcels.
- The SungEel facility will be part of the company’s electric vehicle supply chain according to company executives.
- Suk Jae Yim, Representative of SungEel Recycling Park Georgia, stated that SungEel HiTech’s entry into Georgia is the last piece of the puzzle to build a sustainable ecosystem of Georgia’s electric vehicle supply chain.
- SungEel Recycling Park Georgia will conduct its full responsibility to build a U.S. eco-friendly industrial ecosystem in line with the expectations of the State of Georgia and Stephens County.
- WNEG will continue to follow the story and report on details of the MOU between the SCDA and SungEel once the document is signed and made public.
