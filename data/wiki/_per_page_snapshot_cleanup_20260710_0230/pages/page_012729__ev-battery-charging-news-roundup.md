# EV Battery & Charging News Roundup

## Facts

- Zum announced that Oakland Unified School District (OUSD) will be the first major school district in the U.S. to transition to a 100% electrified school bus system with groundbreaking vehicle-to-grid technology.
- Zum is providing a fleet of 74 electric school buses and bidirectional chargers in Oakland, managed through its AI-enabled technology platform.
- The all-EV fleet will play a critical dual role as a Virtual Power Plant (VPP), giving 2.1 gigawatt hours of energy back to the power grid at scale annually.
- Student transportation is the largest mass transit system in the nation, moving 27 million students twice daily.
- Over 90% of the nation’s 500,000 school buses run on carbon-based fuels, releasing over 8.4 million tons of greenhouse gases annually.
- EVgo announced it will begin to deploy North American Charging Standard (NACS) connectors – currently in the process of being standardized as SAE J3400 – on its fast charging network later this year.
- Stations included in the EVgo roll-out will serve both CCS and NACS vehicles.
- EVgo’s overall network utilization in Q1 2024 was approximately 19%, up from approximately 9% in Q1 2023.
- EVgo CEO Badar Khan stated that the seismic shift in the competitive landscape for EV charging opened new opportunities to drive usage and accelerate network expansion by fostering site host partnerships.
- EVgo had previously identified more than 100,000 potential stalls with strategic site host partners.
- Nearly 40% of EVgo’s stalls are powered by a 350kW charger – almost double the percentage a year ago.
- Asahi Kasei announced it will construct its previously announced integrated lithium-ion battery (LIB) separator plant in Port Colborne, Niagara region, Ontario, Canada.
- The new manufacturing facility will operate as Asahi Kasei Battery Separator Canada and is expected to create highly skilled, good paying jobs in manufacturing and construction.
- Commercial production at the Canadian facility is currently slated for 2027.
- Asahi Kasei President & Representative Director Koshiro Kudo attended the location announcement event.
- The Hipore™ wet-process separators to be produced at the Port Colborne facility are among the most highly engineered and critical components of LIBs used in EVs and other energy storage applications.
- On April 25, 2024, Asahi Kasei announced an initial investment of approximately 180 billion JPY (CAD$ 1.56 billion) to install approximately 700 million square meters of annual Hipore™ separator capacity at the new Canadian facility.
- Asahi Kasei will receive support for the project from the provincial government of Ontario through Invest Ontario and benefit from the federal government’s Clean Technology Manufacturing Investment Tax Credit (ITC).
- Ionic Mineral Technologies announced a major expansion of its production facility in Provo, Utah.
- The expansion includes 37,000 square feet of advanced manufacturing facilities, 7,000 square feet of Class 1 office space, and 5,000 square feet of R&D laboratories.
- Ionic MT’s total facility footprint will bring to over 74,000 square feet.
- The expansion commercializes Ionisil™, a nano-silicon anode material engineered for next-generation lithium-ion batteries.
- Ionisil™ is compatible with existing lithium-ion battery cell production lines and promises to enhance anode capacity up to sevenfold and significantly reduce charging time.
- The facility expansion is scheduled for completion by September 2024.
- The new space provides line-of-sight for 7,000 tons of annual capacity.
- At the leading energy density offered by Ionisil™, this represents one of the nation’s largest anode material facilities.
- By replacing a portion of graphite anode material, Ionic MT can supply over 1,000,000 EVs with next-generation materials for fast-charging and extended-range.
- Ionic MT CEO Andre Zeitoun stated that expanding facilities underscores dedication to innovation and establishing a robust, domestic supply chain for critical battery materials.
- Ionic MT employs a patented, environmentally friendly continuous production process that facilitates efficient production scaling while minimizing costs and environmental impact.
- The facility will process Halloysite feedstock from Ionic MT’s vertically integrated, IRA-compliant mineral reserve.
- Ionic MT is strategically located less than 40 miles away from permitted mineral reserves, removing thousands of miles from the typical anode material supply chain centered around China.
- Westwater Resources acknowledged its support of recent U.S. Trade Representative and White House actions related to EV and critical mineral tariffs.
- On May 14, 2024, the White House announced results of a four-year review of Section 301 tariffs on Chinese products.
- The announcement includes an increase from 25% to 100% on imported Chinese EVs in 2024, and a 25% tariff on natural graphite imports from China beginning in 2026.
- Westwater Resources Executive Chairmen Terence J. Cryan stated that the company is 100% focused on producing battery-grade natural graphite anode material in the U.S. for non-Chinese EVs and supports actions aiding domestic supply chains.
- Westwater Chief Commercial Officer Jon Jacobs stated that the tariffs are what the new U.S.-based natural graphite industry needed to compete with China's monopoly.
- US Customs and Border Protection selected Verdek’s EV Oasis™ Off-Grid DC Fast Charging Solutions to energize its Ford F150 Lightnings monitoring the southern borders of New Mexico and Arizona.
- The EV Oasis™ is a mobile, fully off-grid charging station with dual ports, powered by renewable energy for remote or off-grid locations.
- Its design allows rapid deployment, bypassing complex infrastructure upgrades, permitting, and civil works.
- Penske Truck Leasing joined Powering America’s Commercial Transportation (PACT) as a Charter Member.
- As a Charter Member, Penske will hold a permanent seat on the Board of Directors, filled by Paul Rosa, Senior Vice President of Procurement and Fleet Planning.
- Penske currently operates and maintains a fleet of more than 445,800 vehicles across North America.
- PACT members include ABB E-mobility, BC Hydro, Burns & McDonnell, Chateau Energy Solutions, Cummins, Daimler Truck North America, EV Realty, Geotab, Greenlane™, InductEV, J.B. Hunt Transport, Inc., Mortenson Construction, Navistar, Inc., Pitt Ohio, Pilot Travel Centers, Prologis Inc., Voltera, Volvo Group North America, WattEV, and Zeem Solutions.
- Adden Energy announced its record-breaking lithium metal batteries can now maintain extreme-fast-charging (EFC) of less than 10 minutes at room temperature.
- Adden Energy CEO Will Fitzhugh stated that slow charge rates of Li-ion batteries made EVs inaccessible to many, and their technology makes EV charging as fast as gas.
- Adden Energy’s all-solid-state pouch cell batteries (ASSB) use lithium metal anodes and high nickel NMC cathodes, enabling energy densities up to 500+ Wh/kg.
- Patented innovations include a multi-electrolyte separator and porous 3D lithium metal anode, enabling simultaneous EFC with industry-leading energy densities.
- In 2022, Adden Energy raised a $5.15M seed round to transition technology from academic coin cells to commercially-viable pouch cells for the EV industry.
- Based at its laboratory in Waltham, MA, Adden Energy passed testing criteria for EV manufacturer adoption, including reducing EFC temperature to room temperature and maintaining conventional charging down to below -20°C.
- Adden Energy CTO Luhan Ye explained that their porous anode technologies allow high cathode loadings in excess of 4 mAh/cm2 and multi-electrolyte separators stop dendrite formation at high current densities.
- Adden Energy co-founder and Chairman Xin Li stated that maintaining commercial level cathode loadings, a lithium metal anode, room temperature operation, and 10-minute charge is unparalleled.
- Blink Charging secured a contract as one of the official EV charging providers for the state of New York.
- The agreement allows Blink to offer advanced electric vehicle charging stations (Level 2 and DC fast chargers) on the OGS award for state and municipal fleets and public charging solutions.
- Blink will provide site assessment, installation, maintenance, repair, parts, supplies, warranties, and product training.
- The contract designates Blink as a provider of EV charging network services including monitoring, reporting, billing, support, and integration of third-party fleet/building management systems.
- Blink Chief Revenue Officer Jim Nemec stated the state of New York and Blink are focused on leading transportation electrification together.
- Penske Truck Leasing will begin to offer Powered by REE EVs to its customers for demos and orders, adding momentum to REE’s current $50 million order book value.
- Zero Emission Industries unveiled its FTcase, a portable hydrogen refueling solution barely larger than carry-on luggage.
- The FTcase eliminates complexities of gaseous hydrogen refueling and enables rapid deployment of hydrogen-powered vehicles and heavy-duty equipment.
- ZEI invented field-validated, software-controlled technology to ensure safety of every fueling operation, integrating advanced “fail-safe” systems.
- ZEI Chief Strategy Officer John Motlow highlighted that trained hydrogen technicians are rare, and FTcase ensures top-notch safety with no technical staff involved.
- The FTcase can transform any gaseous hydrogen supply into a complete hydrogen fueling station and refuel anything from boats to cars, heavy-duty equipment, commercial hauling, transport trucks, work trucks, locomotives, buses, and stationary power systems.
- The technology behind the FTcase has been iterated and actively used over the last four years, performing as a sole fueling solution in commercial maritime environments and trials with Class 8 commercial trucks.
- ZEI offers the FTcase as hardware-as-a-service, providing continuous service, automatic updates, and lowering total cost of ownership.
- EV+ announced a preferred partner agreement with CBRE to deploy electric vehicle charging systems at 10,000 U.S. commercial properties over the next five years.
- The partnership focuses on multifamily complexes, hotels, and healthcare-related facilities that have been critically underserved.
- Turnkey EV+ charging stations will be installed ready to operate with no ongoing responsibilities for property owners.
- Valeo’s 48V eAccess technology will power Toyota APM electric shuttles in Paris this summer.
- The electric shuttle can embark 6 persons including the driver, has foldable seats for wheelchair transport, and is available in a relief version for medical assistance.
- Toyota chose Valeo eAccess Belt 14, an all-in-one solution (motor, inverter, reducer) offering operation up to 20 kph with a gross vehicle mass of 1390 kg.
- Valeo’s 48V eAccess combines a 48 Volts 13.5 kW generator, gear reducer, and innovative ribbed elastic belt to create a full eDrive propulsion system.
- The solution can reach speeds up to 100 kph and an electric autonomy average of 100 km.
- Valeo’s eAccess technology is already powering the urban car Citroën Ami, Atul 3-Wheelers, BMW e-motorbike CE02, and other applications.
- Toyota plans to provide 250 vehicles for the major sport event in Paris next summer.
- Parkopedia teamed up with Irdeto to introduce the first true multi-contract solution designed to simplify the EV charging process.
- The joint solution leverages Parkopedia’s EV Charging service and Irdeto’s CrossCharge technologies to offer a seamless Plug and Charge experience.
- Utilizing Irdeto’s Public Key Infrastructure (PKI) expertise, the innovation bolsters security and introduces flexibility and compatibility in EV charging based on ISO 15118 standards.
- The Plug and Charge solution extends the utility of legacy vehicles by making them compatible with multiple contracts for access to public chargers and cheapest tariffs.
- The new solution simplifies compliance with ISO 15118 for vehicle manufacturers, reducing development burden and providing OEMs with a multi-contract solution.
