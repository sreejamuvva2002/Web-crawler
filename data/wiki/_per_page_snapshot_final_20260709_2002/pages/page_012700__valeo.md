# Valeo

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-06

**Source:** [Valeo plans €200 million India investment, targets €700 million revenue by 2028 - Business News | The Financial Express](https://www.financialexpress.com/business/news/valeo-plans-200-million-india-investment-targets-700-million-revenue-by-2028/4147425/)  ·  `page_012700`

## Facts

- Valeo plans to invest more than €200 million in India under its global Elevate 2028 roadmap.
- Valeo targets a jump in India revenues from roughly €220 million currently to €700 million by 2028.
- Valeo has a longer-term ambition of crossing €1 billion revenue beyond the 2028 period.
- A large share of the €200 million investment will flow into Valeo’s Power division.
- Valeo’s Power division houses electrification technologies including electric motors, inverters, on-board chargers, DC-DC converters and battery cooling systems.
- EV penetration in India is currently at around 4 per cent.
- EV penetration in India is projected to rise sharply over the next decade.
- Valeo expects electrified vehicles, including hybrids, to account for about 25% of the Indian market by 2032.
- Around 25% of Valeo India’s Power division revenue already comes from electrified products.
- Localisation levels in electrification stand at about 50%, compared with 80 to 90% in legacy ICE products.
- Valeo is expanding capacity at its Pune and Chennai plants.
- Valeo is deepening domestic sourcing as volumes build.
- Valeo’s Brain division is focused on ADAS technologies such as parking sensors, cameras and radar.
- India’s tightening safety regulations are driving higher demand for advanced safety systems.
- Rising SUV penetration in India is now above 60% of new orders.
- Valeo has installed electronic parking sensor lines locally.
- Valeo is industrialising camera production, with manufacturing expected to begin in 2026.
- Radar localisation is planned as market readiness improves.
- Regulatory changes around 2028 are expected, including features such as automatic emergency braking and lane departure warning systems, to further accelerate ADAS adoption.
- Valeo operates six production sites in India across Pune, Chennai and Sanand.
- Valeo is supported by a major R&D centre in Chennai.
- Valeo employs over 7,000 people in India.
- Valeo’s Indian workforce includes more than 3,000 engineers and over 1,000 software specialists.
- Under its local-for-local strategy, Valeo aims to manufacture in India everything it sells in India.
- Valeo works with more than 200 Indian suppliers.
- Valeo is expanding its footprint in two-wheelers.
- Valeo has partnered with Hero to introduce advanced rider assistance systems, adapting ADAS technologies to enhance motorcycle safety.
- India currently contributes around 1% to Valeo’s global revenue.
- Under Elevate 2028, Valeo intends to significantly raise India's share of global revenue.
- Christophe Périllat, CEO of Valeo, stated: “We are absolutely determined to grow in India,” underscoring confidence in the country’s accelerating technology shift.
