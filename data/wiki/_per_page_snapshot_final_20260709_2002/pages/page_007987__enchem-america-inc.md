# Enchem America Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [TN: Enchem America Inc. to Locate U.S. Manufacturing Operations in Brownsville, Creating 190 Jobs | Trade and Industry Development](https://www.tradeandindustrydev.com/region/tennessee/news/tn-enchem-america-inc-locate-us-manufacturing-31945)  ·  `page_007987`

## Facts

- Tennessee Gov. Bill Lee, Department of Economic and Community Development Commissioner Stuart McWhorter and Enchem America Inc. officials announced the company will invest $152.5 million to establish electrolyte manufacturing operations in Brownsville, Tennessee.
- The new facility is located at the I-40 Advantage Industrial Park, a Select Tennessee Certified Site.
- The Brownsville facility will be Enchem’s second U.S. plant.
- The project will create 190 new jobs in Tier 4, At-Risk Haywood County.
- The Brownsville facility will operate similarly to Enchem’s U.S. headquarters in Commerce, Georgia.
- The facility will manufacture electrolytes for electric vehicle (EV) batteries as well as other EV battery-related services.
- Enchem America Inc. is a subsidiary of Enchem Co., Ltd., which is based in South Korea.
- The company specializes in the development and manufacturing of electrolytes and other additives for rechargeable batteries.
- With the additional Tennessee operations, Enchem will be able to further support its EV partners across the Southeast.
- Since 2019, TNECD has supported more than 50 economic development projects in the Southwest Tennessee region, resulting in nearly 9,300 job commitments and $7.2 billion in capital investment.
- Gov. Bill Lee stated that Enchem’s decision to invest more than $150 million is a testament to Tennessee’s unmatched business climate, highly skilled workforce and position as a leader in the automotive industry.
- TNECD Commissioner Stuart McWhorter noted that South Korea is one of Tennessee’s top 10 foreign direct investment partners and that Enchem is boosting the EV supply chain across Tennessee by investing in an At-Risk county.
- Enchem Co. Ltd. CEO and founder Jung Kang Oh thanked Haywood County, the City of Brownsville and all professionals that assisted Enchem in expanding its presence in North America.
- Jung Kang Oh stated that the growth of the EV market in recent years has been truly remarkable and indications suggest the trend will continue at an accelerated pace over the coming decade.
- Jung Kang Oh expressed confidence that Enchem is well positioned to further capitalize on the growth of the EV market and looks forward to exploring new avenues of innovation and partnership opportunities with Haywood County and the City of Brownsville.
- Haywood County Mayor David Livingston stated that Enchem’s economic opportunity will help transform Haywood County and all West Tennessee with great, new high paying jobs.
- Brownsville Mayor Bill Rawls said the announcement brings to fruition years of work and investment in I-40 Advantage Industrial Park fulfilling the goal of bringing high quality job opportunities to the city.
- Bill Rawls stated that Brownsville looks forward to working with Enchem to continue to build the advanced manufacturing workforce of the future right here in Brownsville, Tennessee.
- TVA senior vice president of economic development John Bradley stated that TVA and Brownsville Energy Authority are proud to partner with HTL Advantage, Brownsville-Haywood County Industrial Development Corporation, City of Brownsville and TNECD to support companies creating jobs and investment in the region.
- Sen. Page Walley (R-Savannah) stated that Enchem’s investment will bring many jobs, boost the regional economy, and further establish Haywood County as a hub of innovation at the forefront of this rapidly growing industry.
- Rep. Johnny Shaw (D-Bolivar) stated that BlueOval City lit the fuse for an economic explosion in Brownsville, Haywood County and West Tennessee, citing the more than $152 million investment as an example of unprecedented growth.
