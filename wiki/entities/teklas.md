# TEKLAS

**Type:** Company  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[automotive-components|Automotive Components]]  ·  **Location:** Calhoun, Georgia, Gordon County, Georgia, United States

## Overview

TEKLAS is a Turkish advanced research and development, manufacturing company that designs, develops and manufactures fluid circulation systems for cooling and heating applications in electric vehicles. In November 2020 the company announced a $6.5 million investment to open its first North American facility and headquarters in Calhoun, Georgia, creating 120 jobs.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Supplier of electric vehicle fluid circulation systems and a new EV parts manufacturer establishing a production hub in Georgia.

## Key facts

- TEKLAS designs, develops, and manufactures fluid circulation systems for cooling and heating applications in electric vehicles.
- The company uses highly innovative and automated manufacturing processes with advanced water molding technology.
- The planned Calhoun facility will be 200,000 square feet.
- $6.5 million investment is expected to create 120 jobs in Gordon County.
- TEKLAS will hire workers at various skill levels including advanced manufacturing, management, and supervision.
- Governor Kemp highlighted Georgia’s skilled workforce, logistics network, and pro-business environment as reasons for TEKLAS’ location choice.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson emphasized the state’s focus on attracting future jobs and fostering an ecosystem of EV manufacturers and suppliers.
- The Center of Innovation for Logistics provided freight analysis to support project planning.
- TEKLAS designs, develops, and manufactures fluid circulation systems for cooling and heating applications, including hoses and pipes from rubber, plastic, and metal for the global automotive industry.
- The new Georgia facility will be 200,000 sq ft and include assembly lines to serve prominent original equipment manufacturers (OEMs).
- Governor Kemp highlighted Georgia’s skilled workforce, logistics network, and pro-business environment as reasons for TEKLAS’ decision.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson emphasized that the project aligns with Georgia’s strategy to attract future jobs and grow an EV supplier ecosystem.
