# TDK Components USA, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2008-08-19

**Source:** [08/19/2008: TDK Receives EPA Award for Reducing Its Metro Atlanta Manufacturing Facility’s Environmental Footprint](https://www.epa.gov/archive/epapages/newsroom_archive/newsreleases/c3e16131a4273ada852574aa005648ad.html)  ·  `page_029810`

## Facts

- TDK Components USA, Inc. was recognized by EPA Regional Administrator Jimmy Palmer as a recipient of the 2008 Performance Track Environmental Performance Award.
- TDK is one of two companies in the Southeast and just five nationwide to receive this award.
- TDK was also recognized for acceptance to the highest level of the Partnership for a Sustainable Georgia, an environmental leadership program for Georgia businesses.
- Jimmy Palmer stated that TDK's outstanding commitment to consistently exceed environmental requirements allowed it to continuously improve on its environmental performance and reduce its environmental footprint.
- Suzanne Burnes, Manager of the Partnership for a Sustainable Georgia, noted that members like TDK lead in implementing sound environmental practices while recognizing that what is good for the environment can also be good for the bottom line.
- TDK manufactures multi-layer ceramic chip capacitors at its Peachtree City, Ga., facility.
- From 2003-2006, TDK increased production by 28 percent while achieving specific environmental results.
- During that period, TDK reduced total water use by 38 percent, or more than 1.1 million gallons, by analyzing electroplating process line rinse flow rates.
- TDK reduced non-transportation energy use by 17 percent and associated greenhouse gas emissions by nearly 700 tons of carbon dioxide through the installation of a more efficient lighting system, upgrading to more efficient equipment, and consolidating operations to two buildings.
- TDK reduced the use of copper paste by 36 percent, nearly 3,500 pounds, and the use of caustic soda by 53 percent, about 21,600 pounds.
- TDK President Masatoshi Fujimoto stated that being selected among peers to receive the award represents confirmation of numerous improvement activities implemented at TDK toward reducing their environmental footprint.
- Since its inception in June 2000, Performance Track membership grew to more than 500 facilities in 49 states and Puerto Rico, with members making more than 3,500 commitments to the environment.
- Through combined environmental efforts, Performance Track member facilities reported cumulative reductions in water use of 5.2 billion gallons, greenhouse gas reductions of 309,780 metric tons of carbon dioxide equivalent, increases in recycled materials use by 559,991 tons, and conservation of 16,809 acres of habitat.
- The Performance Track program encourages facilities with strong environmental records to exceed legal requirements, typically setting four public, measurable goals to improve air, water, and land quality.
- The Partnership for a Sustainable Georgia was established by the Pollution Prevention Assistance Division of the Georgia Department of Natural Resources to foster environmental leadership and recognize superior environmental performance.
- The Partnership offers incentives including cost savings through increased efficiencies, potential regulatory flexibility, free technical assistance and training, and access to networking and mentoring among peers, state and local officials.
