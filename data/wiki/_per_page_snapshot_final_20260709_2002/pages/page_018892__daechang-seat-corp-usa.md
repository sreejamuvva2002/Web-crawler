# Daechang Seat Corp. USA

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-10-23

**Source:** [Hundreds of jobs are coming to Chatham County through auto supplier](https://www.savannahnow.com/story/business/2023/10/23/hundreds-of-jobs-to-chatham-county-coming-through-automotive-supplier/71287853007/)  ·  `page_018892`

## Facts

- Daechang Seat Corp. USA is an automotive supplier specializing in seat frames.
- The company will locate at the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center in Chatham County.
- The facility will be situated within reach of the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center megasite.
- Daechang Seat Corp. USA will invest $72.5 million in the new facility.
- The investment is expected to create over 500 jobs in Chatham County.
- This marks the third supplier to establish operations in Chatham County.
- No production start date or timetable was provided for the new facility.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that the news highlights the broader impact of projects like the Hyundai Metaplant beyond their initial investment.
- Daechang Seat Corp. USA was founded in 1979 in South Korea.
- The company has grown to become one of the largest seat frame manufacturers globally.
- Daechang Seat Corp. USA currently employs more than 5,000 people across seven countries.
- Savannah Economic Development Authority (SEDA) President and CEO Trip Tollison stated that Daechang Seat Corp. USA is a well-established global company and expressed excitement about welcoming them to the community.
- A job fair will be scheduled in the future to recruit for the new positions.
- Open positions at the facility include assembly operators, equipment technicians, mechanical engineers, and electrical engineers.
- The announcement comes weeks after Governor Kemp's office celebrated Georgia being ranked the No. 1 state to do business for 10 consecutive years.
- Georgia has attracted more than $25 billion in investments and created thousands of jobs in EV manufacturing over the last five years.
- Savannah has become a main player in the state's aggressive approach to EV manufacturing.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that countless partners are helping transform Georgia into a center of the automotive and mobility industry.
- Pat Wilson highlighted that Chatham County has leveraged Savannah's status as an established global gateway to create a bustling community for residents and workers.
