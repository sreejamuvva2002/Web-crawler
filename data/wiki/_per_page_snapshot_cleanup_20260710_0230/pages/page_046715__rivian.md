## Facts

- A Morgan County board signed off Wednesday on a property rental agreement that is a centerpiece of the $1.5 billion incentive package offered to electric vehicle maker Rivian.
- The Morgan County Board of Assessors voted 4-1 to rule that the rental agreement, known as a usufruct, does not make Rivian’s interest in the property taxable.
- The decision allows Rivian to rent the land and future factory buildings on the nearly 2,000-acre site without paying typical property taxes.
- The rental agreement is one of several components of Rivian’s incentive package and is by far the largest.
- The agreement provides some $700 million in property tax savings over the 25-year term.
- Under the terms of the agreement, Rivian will pay more than $300 million over 25 years to local governments and school systems under a payment in lieu of taxes agreement or PILOT.
- The site is located in Madison, Georgia.
