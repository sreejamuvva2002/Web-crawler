## Facts

- EVCO Plastics was founded in 1964 by Don Evans as a custom plastics and contract manufacturing company on DeForest’s Main Street.
- The company has expanded to 13 sites across the United States, China, and Mexico.
- EVCO Plastics is marking its 60-year milestone in 2024.
- The company remains family-owned and operated, with third-generation leadership roles held by Dale Evans' daughters and two cousins on the board of directors.
- Dale Evans took over the company in the 1980s and continues to chair the board of directors.
- Kate Bashir, Dale Evans' daughter, serves as CEO and oversees engineering and sales.
- Anna Evans Bartz is EVCO’s vice president of communications.
- EVCO employs 400 people at its three DeForest plants.
- EVCO's facilities in Oshkosh, Wisconsin, and Calhoun, Georgia, employ an additional 850 people.
- The company employs a total of 2,100 associates worldwide when including locations in China and Mexico.
- EVCO specializes in plastic injection molding for complicated products and works with top 500 companies across various industries.
- In 2018, EVCO built a 50,000-square-foot addition to its MED facility on EVCO Drive in DeForest.
- In 2020, the company began a two-year process of moving its corporate headquarters from Main Street to the EVCO Drive location.
- The EVCO Drive building houses a medical manufacturing facility that creates components for medical devices.
- During the COVID-19 pandemic, EVCO manufactured components for respirators and diagnostic equipment, including COVID testing kits.
- In 2019, EVCO expanded its Oshkosh campus by adding two new buildings totaling 117,473 square feet.
- The Oshkosh expansion houses a 4,400-ton press, which is the largest of its machines at the facility, along with other equipment and warehousing space.
- EVCO operates 270 injection molding machines and presses ranging from 28 tons to 4,400 tons in Oshkosh.
- Kate Bashir anticipates hiring someone to oversee sales as she transitions more fully into the CEO role for the U.S. side.
- Dale Evans has served the company for 50 years and is expected to retire once Bashir completes her transition.
- The company fosters a culture of curiosity and aims to support employee development and retention.
- EVCO anticipates a successful 2025.
