## Facts

- Lehigh Technologies is a green materials company that manufacturers micronized rubber powders made from end-of-life tires and other post-industrial rubber.
- Lehigh’s micronized rubber material is used in a wide range of consumer and industrial applications.
- Lehigh Technologies is located in Tucker, Georgia, United States.
- Lehigh Technologies has raised a total of $29M.
- On 03.09.2014, Lehigh Technologies announced the closing of an $8 million strategic financing round.
- On 21.08.2012, Lehigh Technologies completed $16 million in financing.
- On 27.07.2012, Lehigh Technologies completed a $5 million financing round.
- Investors in Lehigh Technologies include Western Tech, Centrecour, Trinity Cap, Horizon Tech, and NGP Energy.
- Backers of the $8M funding round announced on 03.09.2014 included JSR Corp, Leaf Clean Energy, Kleiner Perkins Caufield and Byers, Index Ventures, and Florida Gulfshore Capital.
- The $16M financing round completed on 21.08.2012 was led by Leaf Clean Energy, with participation from Kleiner Perkins Caufield Byers, Index Ventures, and NGP Energy Technology Partners.
- On 04.12.2009, Lehigh Technologies was named one of the World Economic Forum’s Technology Pioneers for 2010 for recycling rubber.
- Lehigh Technologies was one of only 26 companies selected to the 2010 class out of over 300 contestants.
- Indugarbi NFU will produce green tires with the support of Lehigh Technologies.
