# Hyundai TRANSYS Georgia Seating System, LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2021-04-05

**Source:** [Hyundai TRANSYS Georgia Seating System, LLC Expands Manufacturing Operations in Georgia | Governor Brian P. Kemp Office of the Governor](https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases/2021-04-05/hyundai-transys-georgia-seating-system-llc-expands-manufacturing)  ·  `page_002955`

## Facts

- Hyundai TRANSYS Georgia Seating System, LLC will invest $9 million in expanding their seating manufacturing operations in West Point, Georgia.
- The expansion will create more than 150 jobs in Troup County.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced the investment and stated that Georgia's highly skilled workforce continues to attract manufacturing jobs in every corner of the state.
- Hyundai TRANSYS Georgia Seating System manufactures seats and seat foam pads for the Hyundai Santa Fe, KIA Sorento, and other models.
- In addition to providing seating structures for automobile manufacturers, the company also supplies seats to leading electric vehicle makers.
- Hyundai TRANSYS Georgia Seating System President and CEO Sangkil Jung said they are excited for the new opportunity ahead as they expand their presence in the West Point community.
- The expansion will take Hyundai TRANSYS’ employment total in Georgia to more than 1,890 jobs.
- Hyundai TRANSYS Georgia Seating System is continuing to hire for careers in production seat assembly, foam, quality, and logistics.
- Kevin Patrick, Chairman of the West Point Development Authority, thanked Hyundai TRANSYS Georgia Seating System for its continued investment and noted that automotive and existing industries are the backbone of local economic success.
- Senior Project Manager Amanda Fields represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s Global Commerce division on this competitive project in partnership with the West Point Development Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that contributing more than $3 billion to Georgia’s economy, the state is committed to growing Georgia’s robust automotive ecosystem and creating solid manufacturing jobs of the future.
- Wilson extended thanks to partners at local, state, and federal levels who have worked to foster Georgia’s pro-business environment, which has helped give automotive partners confidence to continue investing in the state.
- Georgia is home to more than 200 automotive-related facilities and remains a national leader in advanced manufacturing.
- Georgia is outpacing the United States in 10-year GDP growth in the manufacture of products including machinery, electrical equipment and components, and fabricated metals.
- Hyundai TRANSYS is an automotive parts company that produces critical automobile parts, including transmissions, axles, and seats.
- It is the only company in the world specializing in transmissions.
- The company produces all types of automatic transmissions, manual transmissions, dual clutch transmissions (DCT), and continuously variable transmissions.
- Hyundai TRANSYS also develops and produces top quality seats for Genesis, Santa Fe, Sorento, and other models.
