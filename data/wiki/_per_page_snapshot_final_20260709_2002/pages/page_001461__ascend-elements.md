# Ascend Elements

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2023-03-30

**Source:** [Ascend Elements opens EV battery recycling facility in Georgia](https://www.recyclingproductnews.com/article/40103/ascend-elements-opens-its-first-commercial-scale-lithium-ion-battery-recycling-facility)  ·  `page_001461`

## Facts

- Ascend Elements has opened its first commercial-scale lithium-ion battery recycling facility.
- The $50 million Base 1 facility is located in Covington, Georgia.
- The Base 1 facility is one of the largest electric vehicle battery recycling facilities in North America.
- Lithium-ion battery recycling is becoming an increasingly important part of the EV battery materials supply chain as demand for electric vehicles increases.
- The facility began partial operations in August 2022.
- The facility has an annual capacity to process 30,000 metric tons of used lithium-ion batteries and manufacturing scrap.
- The processing capacity is equal to 70,000 electric vehicle batteries per year.
- Ascend Elements CEO Mike O'Kronley stated that the facility is an important part of America's EV battery infrastructure and that it is just the start.
- Ascend Elements CEO Mike O'Kronley emphasized the industry's need to continue building a closed-loop battery materials supply chain to make electric vehicle batteries cleaner and more sustainable.
- Ascend Elements recovers up to 98 percent of the critical battery metals in used electric vehicle batteries and gigafactory manufacturing scrap.
- By using its Hydro-to-Cathode process to recycle used lithium-ion batteries and scrap, Ascend Elements can reduce the carbon footprint of new electric vehicle battery cathode materials by up to 90 percent.
- The facility is powered by renewable energy.
- The facility features onsite wastewater recycling and treatment systems.
- The facility currently employs about 100 team members.
- Ascend Elements plans to increase the facility's headcount to 185 by 2024.
