# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-10-08

**Source:** [Hyundai begins producing EVs at Bryan Co. Metaplant - Statesboro Herald](https://www.statesboroherald.com/local/hyundai-begins-producing-evs-at-bryan-co-metaplant/)  ·  `page_008647`

## Facts

- Hyundai has begun producing electric SUVs in Georgia less than two years after breaking ground on its sprawling, $7.6 billion manufacturing plant west of Savannah.
- Hyundai's Georgia factory held an employee-focused celebration when its first EV for commercial sale rolled off the assembly line.
- The event occurred on a Thursday.
- Bianca Johnson is the spokesperson for Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- The statement regarding the production start was provided to The Associated Press on Monday.
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America validated its production processes to ensure vehicles meet Hyundai Motor Group's high quality standards.
- Initial production of customer vehicles has started ahead of schedule.
- A grand opening celebration at the Georgia plant is expected in the first quarter of 2025.
- Hyundai and battery partner LG Energy Solution plan to employ a total of 8,500 workers at the Bryan County site in Ellabell once the plant is fully operational.
- Hyundai plans to produce up to 300,000 EVs per year in Georgia, as well as the batteries that power them.
- The plant's vehicle production areas have been completed and are being staffed by more than 1,000 workers.
- The battery-making facilities at the plant remain under construction.
- The first vehicles being produced at the Georgia site are 2025 models of Hyundai's Ioniq 5 electric SUVs.
- The American-made EVs are expected to arrive at U.S. dealerships before the end of 2024.
- During the first half of 2024, the Ioniq 5 was America's second-best-selling electric vehicle not made by Tesla.
- Hyundai broke ground on its Georgia plant in late October 2022.
- The project is the largest economic development project the state of Georgia has ever seen.
- The project received $2.1 billion in tax breaks and other incentives from state and local governments.
- Hyundai accelerated the plant's timeline to start making EVs within two years of groundbreaking due to federal electric vehicle incentives rewarding domestic production.
- The Inflation Reduction Act was passed in 2022 with provisions intended to combat climate change.
- The law includes a tax credit that saves EV buyers up to $7,500.
- The $7,500 tax credit applies only to cars made in North America with domestic batteries.
- Hyundai executives initially complained that the Inflation Reduction Act was unfair.
- Hyundai President and Global Chief Operating Officer Jose Munoz stated that the law caused the automaker to push to open the Georgia plant sooner.
