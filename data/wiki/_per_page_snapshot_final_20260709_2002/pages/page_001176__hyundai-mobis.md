# Hyundai Mobis

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** power_electronics  ·  **Published:** 2022-11-23

**Source:** [New Hyundai Metaplant to bring 1,500 jobs to Bryan Co., Gov. Kemp says](https://www.yahoo.com/news/hyundai-metaplant-bring-1-500-205746932.html)  ·  `page_001176`

## Facts

- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp announced that Hyundai Mobis would invest $926 million in an Electric Vehicle Power Electric system plant in Bryan County.
- The project will hire at least 1,500 people.
- Gov. Kemp stated the groundbreaking of Hyundai’s new electric vehicle and battery manufacturing facility in Bryan County would unleash transformational job creation and investment in that region.
- This announcement marks the second supplier to locate in the area within two weeks.
- Hyundai Mobis has operated a manufacturing factory in West Point with almost 1,200 employees since 2009.
- The West Point factory is the largest Tier 1 supplier to Kia Georgia.
- The West Point plant supplies car manufacturers with complete cockpit modules, chassis modules, and bumper assemblies as part of the Hyundai Mobis family.
- H.S. Oh, Vice President of Hyundai Mobis Electric Powertrain Business Unit, stated the investment reflects an acceleration in the development of the EV supply chain in Georgia’s auto industry.
- H.S. Oh noted Hyundai Mobis will be a major production player in the EV market and will trigger more growth within the sector.
- H.S. Oh added that Mobis is looking forward to providing high-quality work opportunities to the growing local workforce.
- The new 1,200,000-square-foot building is expected to provide over 900,000 EV Power Electric systems and 450,000 Integrated Charging Control units annually.
- Production will be supplied to several factories in Georgia and one factory in Alabama.
- Construction is expected to begin as early as January 2023 at the Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development Certified Belfast Commerce Park in Richmond Hill.
- Construction officials predict production will begin sometime in 2024.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated suppliers like Hyundai Mobis are transitioning to the jobs of the future and becoming part of Georgia’s growing EV ecosystem.
- In May, GDEcD predicted that non-affiliated HMGMA suppliers in connection with the project would invest an additional $1 billion into Georgia.
- GDEcD reported that the $1 billion prediction had already been blown past in about two weeks.
- GDEcD mentioned utilizing partners like Georgia Quick Start and sister agencies to prepare Georgia’s workforce for the jobs being created.
- Officials congratulated Bryan County, the City of Richmond Hill, the Georgia Ports Authority, and Hyundai Mobis on the project.
