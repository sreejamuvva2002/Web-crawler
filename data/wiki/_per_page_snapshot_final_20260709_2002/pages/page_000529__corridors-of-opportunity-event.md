# Corridors of Opportunity Event

**Type:** event  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2023-08-16

**Source:** [Smart Growth Stressed at Corridors of Opportunity Event - MOSAIC at Chatham Park](https://www.mosaicatchathampark.com/news/smart-growth-stressed-at-corridors-of-opportunity-event/)  ·  `page_000529`

## Facts

- The Corridors of Opportunity event was hosted by the Triangle Business Journal on June 23rd.
- Panelists included Karen Howard, chair of the Chatham County Board of Commissioners; Michael Cline, North Carolina’s state demographer; Michael Smith, president of Chatham Economic Development Corp.; and Julian “Bubba” Rawl, co-owner of Chatham Park and Preston Development Company.
- The event was sponsored by MOSAIC and Chatham Park.
- The event was held at the Chatham County Agriculture & Conference Center in Pittsboro.
- Growth over the past decade now accounts for 40% of the total Chatham County population.
- Major companies such as Vietnamese carmaker VinFast plan to build substantial manufacturing facilities in the area.
- North Carolina-based silicon carbide company Wolfspeed plans to build a substantial manufacturing facility in the area.
- Margaret Chadwick, chief human resources officer at Wolfspeed, stated there is much more opportunity for collaborations and partnerships than competition.
- Julian “Bubba” Rawl and partner Tim Smith were attracted to the Pittsboro region by its beauty, landscape, downtown, culture, and history, aiming to create amenities that make Chatham Park a special place.
- Social media and local news media amplified buzz from the stage and audience at the event.
- The Chatham News & Record reported on Michael Cline’s facts and figures regarding the county’s population growth.
