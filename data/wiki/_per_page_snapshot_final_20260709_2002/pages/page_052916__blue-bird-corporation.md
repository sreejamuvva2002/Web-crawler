# Blue Bird Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Workers at Georgia school bus maker Blue Bird approve their first union contract | AP News](https://apnews.com/article/school-bus-contract-blue-bird-4dbd2d08fd9f0d57c0830417e5f484ff)  ·  `page_052916`

## Facts

- Workers at Blue Bird’s assembly plants and warehouse in Fort Valley, Ga., have approved their first union contract a year after voting to unionize.
- The three-year contract is between Blue Bird Corp. and the United Steelworkers union.
- The contract covers all 1,500 workers at the Fort Valley facilities.
- All covered workers will receive at least a 12% raise, with some of the lowest-paid workers receiving raises of more than 40%.
- Blue Bird will contribute to a retirement plan for workers, share profits, and improve health and safety under the new contract.
- Blue Bird received $40 million in federal aid to build electric school buses.
- The negotiations were closely watched by President Joe Biden’s administration due to the company's receipt of federal aid for electric school bus production.
- Acting Labor Secretary Julie Su helped bring the contract negotiations to a successful agreement.
- Blue Bird is one of three major school bus manufacturers in the United States, alongside Thomas Built Buses (a subsidiary of Daimler Truck AG) and IC Bus (a unit of Navistar International).
- Blue Bird had previously urged employees to reject unionization last year but CEO Phil Horlock stated contract talks were “very collaborative” and expressed looking forward to a strong partnership.
- The company stated that higher pay, benefits, and career development opportunities will help attract workers.
- CEO Phil Horlock said the agreement provides positive outcomes for all parties and will bolster Blue Bird’s position as an employer of choice in the region.
- Blue Bird is a publicly held company based in Macon with about 2,000 employees overall.
- Blue Bird has long been the largest private employer in Peach County, Georgia.
- The unionization vote was considered a notable win for organized labor in the traditionally unfriendly Deep South.
- United Steelworkers District 9 Director Dan Flippo stated that federal investments must come with a seat at the table for workers and criticized corporations trying to run away from unions in the South.
- The nationwide share of unionized workers dipped to 10% last year, with private sector workers at 6%, according to the federal Bureau of Labor Statistics.
- Only 4.4% of Georgia workers are union members, and state business leaders have historically been hostile to unions.
- Republican Gov. Brian Kemp pushed through a law this year barring companies taking state incentives from recognizing unions without a formal secret-ballot election.
