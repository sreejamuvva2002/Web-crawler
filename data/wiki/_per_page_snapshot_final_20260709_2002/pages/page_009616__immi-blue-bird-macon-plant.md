# IMMI-Blue Bird Macon Plant

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [IMMI, Blue Bird Open New Manufacturing Plant in Georgia | School Bus Fleet](https://www.schoolbusfleet.com/news/immi-blue-bird-open-new-manufacturing-plant-in-georgia)  ·  `page_009616`

## Facts

- IMMI’s new 75,000 square-foot facility in Macon will produce school bus seats for Georgia-based Blue Bird Corporation.
- The new manufacturing plant creates 80 American manufacturing jobs.
- IMMI and Blue Bird celebrated the grand opening of their joint manufacturing facility in Macon, Georgia on Oct. 2.
- IMMI’s new 75,000 square-foot plant will support Blue Bird buses after the company announced equipping select models with safety upgrades.
- Blue Bird announced equipping select models with three-point seat belts as standard protection for all student passengers.
- Blue Bird also announced equipping select models with a steering wheel-deployed air bag to safeguard school bus drivers.
- Larry Gray serves as CEO of IMMI.
- Norm Gould III serves as school bus president and IMMI CFO.
- IMMI’s 4Front airbag safety system will be standard safety equipment on Blue Bird’s Vision school buses beginning this month.
- John Wyskiel serves as president and CEO of Blue Bird Corporation.
- Under the brand SafeGuard, IMMI’s Macon facility manufactures state-of-the-art school bus seats.
- Blue Bird was the first school bus manufacturer to announce in 2024 that lap-shoulder belts would be standard safety equipment on their school bus seats.
- The National Highway Traffic Safety Administration and the National Transportation Safety Board have called for school bus manufacturers to provide lap-shoulder belts for the 25 million plus children who ride on buses every year.
- As demand increases for IMMI SafeGuard’s lap-shoulder belt seats, IMMI expects to add more jobs to its workforce in Macon.
- Illinois recently passed legislation mandating lap-shoulder belts on new school buses, joining eight other U.S. states.
- IMMI CEO Larry Gray expects other states to follow suit as they recognize that seat belts continue to save lives and reduce injuries on school buses.
