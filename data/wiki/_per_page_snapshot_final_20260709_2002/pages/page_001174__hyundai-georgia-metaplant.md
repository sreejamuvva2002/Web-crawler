# Hyundai Georgia Metaplant

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-03-31

**Source:** [Kia EVs will also be made at Hyundai’s Georgia “Metaplant”](https://www.greencarreports.com/news/1137620_kia-evs-us-made-at-hyundai-georgia-metaplant)  ·  `page_001174`

## Facts

- Hyundai broke ground on its $5.5 billion “Metaplant” near Savannah, Georgia.
- Kia confirmed that it will have upcoming electric vehicles made at the Hyundai Metaplant facility.
- Hyundai announced in May plans to produce up to 300,000 EVs annually at the Georgia plant.
- The Georgia plant will produce models from Hyundai and Genesis, as well as Kia.
- Batteries are to be manufactured separately at a corresponding battery venture that has not yet been detailed.
- Hyundai’s companion parts and components arm, Hyundai Mobis, is reportedly looking for nearby sites.
- Kia already operates a facility in West Point, Georgia, which manufactures Telluride, Sorento, and Sportage SUVs, as well as the K5 sedan.
- The new Savannah plant will accommodate U.S. EV assembly and potentially qualify the brand’s future EVs under the stricter requirements of the Inflation Reduction Act and its revamped EV tax credit.
- Production at the Savannah plant for Kia is scheduled to start in the first half of 2025.
- The Hyundai Ioniq 7, previewed by the Seven concept, is one of the prime candidates for manufacture at the new plant.
- Kia’s EV9 electric SUV reaches the U.S. in 2023 but might potentially be shifted to the Savannah plant.
- Kia is planning an electric pickup truck that would be a likely candidate for production at the facility.
- Rivian aims to produce 450,000 electric trucks per year in Georgia, with its plant due to start production in 2024.
- According to the Atlanta Journal-Constitution, citing Hyundai and Genesis Motor North America CEO José Muñoz, the Hyundai plant could be expanded to 500,000 units annually.
- The expanded Hyundai plant would involve “maybe five or six” upcoming EV models.
- Georgia is being cast as a hub for EV manufacturing due to these recent decisions.
- Battery maker SK Innovation is building dual battery plants in Georgia that will support 300,000 EVs per year.
- SK Innovation is ramping up production to supply Ford and Volkswagen.
