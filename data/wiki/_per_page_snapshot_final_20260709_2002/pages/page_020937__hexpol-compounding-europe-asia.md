# HEXPOL Compounding Europe & Asia

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2024-03-18

**Source:** [HEXPOL Compounding Invests in Devulcanization, Supports Transition to Circular Economy - Wire & Cable India](https://www.wirecable.in/hexpol-compounding-invests-in-devulcanization/)  ·  `page_020937`

## Facts

- HEXPOL Group's rubber compounding arm announced an investment in a devulcanization line in Europe.
- The investment enables HEXPOL Compounding in Europe to produce several thousand tonnes of compounds with devulcanized rubber content per year.
- The new extruder will be installed at the HEXPOL Compounding site in the Czech Republic in late 2024.
- The devulcanization process uses mechanical forces like shear, compression, and temperature to break down crosslinking bonds of cured rubber with a specific extruder, adding no additional chemicals.
- Devulcanized material retains the polymer, carbon black or other fillers, and plasticizer of the original compound, creating a raw material base for new rubber parts.
- HEXPOL Compounding will provide devulcanization services to other European HEXPOL Compounding sites to serve a broad customer base.
- The company adjusts compound recipes based on devulcanized rubber and other renewable raw materials to achieve the same performance as compounds based on standard raw materials.
- Carsten Rüter, President of HEXPOL Rubber Compounding Europe & Asia, stated HEXPOL's target is to reprocess cured rubber from post-industrial sources for typical rubber applications in the automotive or building and construction industry.
- Ralph Wolkener, President HEXPOL Compounding Europe & Asia, said customers are becoming business partners by collecting and sorting production side products until HEXPOL transforms them into devulcanized raw materials.
- Norbert Niemand, Managing Director at HEXPOL Lesina, confirmed adjusting compound recipes based on devulcanized rubber is part of HEXPOL's service.
