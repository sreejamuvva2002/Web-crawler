# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-05

**Source:** [Georgia Scores Top Spot in Competitive Economic Rankings - Rome CEO](https://romeceo.com/features/2024/05/georgia-scores-top-spot-competitive-economic-rankings/)  ·  `page_052347`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp, in conjunction with the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD), today announced that the state earned the No. 1 spot in Site Selection’s annual Prosperity Cup rankings for successful investment recruitment in 2023.
- Criteria for this national award include the total values of new and expanded facilities in the state, business-climate attractiveness, and workforce readiness.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that companies choosing Georgia get access to the global market through logistics assets like Hartsfield-Jackson International Airport and some of the fastest-growing ports in the nation.
- In 2023, Georgia attracted 318 new and expanded facilities to secure the No. 1 ranking.
- The award includes an expanded scope for the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA), which is the largest project in state history.
- HMGMA announced an additional 400 new jobs and $2 billion in investment last year.
- HMGMA’s total investment is now $7.59 billion and will net 8,500 new jobs for Georgians.
- Anovion Technologies selected southwest Georgia for its $800 million, 400-job synthetic graphite manufacturing facility in May.
- Anovion Technologies broke ground at the site in June of last year.
- Meissner Corporation plans to create more than 1,700 new jobs at a new campus in northeast Georgia that will more than double the company’s manufacturing footprint in the U.S.
- Site Selection’s Mark Arend stated that the Prosperity Cup recognizes state-level economic development competitiveness and that Georgia’s consistent delivery of location attributes demonstrates it competes to win.
- On the heels of Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America’s groundbreaking in October 2022, Georgia welcomed more than 10 Hyundai and automotive suppliers in 2023 reaching middle Georgia and beyond.
- These suppliers will ultimately create over 4,300 new jobs and bring $1.2 billion in combined investment.
- Kia’s facility in West Point announced an expansion during 2023.
- Toyota’s campus in Pendergrass announced an expansion during 2023.
- Kia and Toyota expansions created a combined 440 new jobs and $286 million in investment.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Georgia’s economic development team is truly collaborative, including sister state agencies, utility partners, local economic developers, and many others who work to help companies and communities find the right match for mutual success.
- Wilson noted that decades of partnerships, intentional economic development from the community level to the state level, combined with consistent pro-business policies, have led to these positive results.
