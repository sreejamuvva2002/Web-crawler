## Facts

- SK Battery America, Inc. announced March 6 it would lay off 958 workers from its electric vehicle battery plant in Commerce, Georgia.
- The Associated Press reports that the laid-off workers make up roughly 37% of the company’s entire workforce.
- The workers finished their last day of work on March 6 but will receive pay through May 6.
- The layoffs were disclosed via a Worker Adjustment and Retraining Notification notice filed with the state.
- The Georgia plant cost $2.6 billion to build and opened in January 2022.
- The plant initially supplied batteries for the Ford F-150 Lightning electric pickup truck.
- SK Battery America, Inc. was formed as a joint venture with Ford.
- Ford discontinued its partnership with SK On.
- Ford announced it would cease production of the F-150 Lightning in December 2025.
- Pratt & Whitney is spending $200 million to expand its Georgia aircraft engine factory.
- The Pratt & Whitney expansion is expected to initially create 15 new jobs.
- Preciball USA announced a new $17 million factory in Georgia.
- The Preciball USA factory will produce industrial balls for bearings and valves and create 65 new jobs.
- Dongwon Autopart Technology is building a new $30 million factory in Georgia that will create 200 new jobs.
- Dongwon Autopart Technology, a Korean auto parts manufacturer, already has about 500 employees in the region.
