# Seohan Auto Georgia

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-04-11

**Source:** [Hyundai supplier Seohan sets $72M, 180-job Georgia plant | AP News](https://apnews.com/article/seohan-hyundai-supplier-plant-georgia-0e3bda0aa8cd28d89d7b0129d207f930)  ·  `page_059437`

## Facts

- A South Korean company will build a $72 million factory to make half shafts, axles and brake systems for a Hyundai Motor Group auto assembly plant in coastal Georgia.
- Seohan Group announced plans to build the plant near Midway, south of Savannah, with plans to hire at least 180 new employees.
- Seohan Auto Georgia is the seventh major supplier to locate in the region after Hyundai said in May it would build a $5.5 billion plant to assemble electric vehicles and batteries in Ellabell, Georgia.
- The Hyundai EV/battery plant site could grow to 8,100 employees and is slated to begin producing vehicles in 2025.
- Seohan and six other suppliers have pledged to invest nearly $2 billion and hire more than 4,600 people.
- Seoul-based Seohan already supplies a Hyundai assembly plant in Montgomery, Alabama and a Kia plant in West Point, Georgia.
- Seohan makes axles and driveshafts at two plants in Auburn, Alabama, that opened in 2008 and 2014.
- The company said last year it would invest $13.5 million in the Auburn, Alabama plants, which jointly employ more than 200 people, to make parts for electric vehicles.
- The company stated it needs additional capacity to make parts as automakers convert to electric propulsion.
- Seohan Auto Georgia Corp. CEO Jung Kee Koo stated, “Sustained growth of the EV market over the past few years suggests that accelerated changes to the automotive market is inevitable.”
- Jung Kee Koo added, “We believe Georgia will be the center of the EV industry, and will be a new frontier for Seohan’s future with limitless opportunities and potential.”
- The new plant will be located in a business park off Interstate 95 near Midway, in Liberty County.
- The company plans to begin production at the new Georgia plant in late 2024.
- Seohan could qualify for $2.7 million in state income tax credits, at $3,000 per job over five years, provided workers make at least $31,300 a year.
- The state of Georgia will also pay to train the plant's workers.
