# City of Atlanta

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** charging_infrastructure  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Georgia Procurement Registry](https://ssl.doas.state.ga.us/gpr/eventDetails?eSourceNumber=PE-66062-NONST-2026-000000294&sourceSystemType=gpr20)  ·  `page_046051`

## Facts

- The City of Atlanta is seeking submissions from qualified firms to procure, install, and maintain Direct Current Fast Charging (DCFC) infrastructure at four City-owned sites.
- The procurement is part of the federally funded EcoFleet ATL initiative.
- The project will replace seven internal combustion engine (ICE) vehicles with electric heavy-duty vehicles.
- The initiative will deliver the necessary electric vehicle supply equipment (EVSE) infrastructure to support the operation of the new electric vehicles.
- The initiative includes integration with the City's fleet management platform, workforce development, and emissions reduction tracking.
- Buyer contact for the procurement is Bonnie Davis.
- NIGP Code 06009 applies, covering Batteries and Charging Stations For Electric Automobiles, Including Recycled Types.
- NIGP Code 20314 applies, covering Battery Chargers, Computer and Peripheral, Environmentally Certified Products.
- NIGP Code 91238 applies, covering Construction Services, Hazardous Waste, Including Site Management.
