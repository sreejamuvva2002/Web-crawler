# Stryten Energy LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** research_and_development  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Stryten Energy Opens Expanded, State-of-the-Art Battery Testing Lab](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20240828728862/en/Stryten-Energy-Opens-Expanded-State-of-the-Art-Battery-Testing-Lab)  ·  `page_029416`

## Facts

- Stryten Energy LLC is a U.S.-based energy storage solutions provider headquartered in Alpharetta, Georgia.
- The company added to its in-house battery testing and analysis capabilities with a new laboratory space at its new corporate headquarters.
- The facility includes dedicated space for testing lead, lithium, and vanadium batteries and materials.
- A ribbon-cutting celebration for the battery lab opening was held on August 26.
- Ribbon-cutting participants included Roger Miksad (President & Executive Director, Battery Council International), Tim Vargo (Chairman of the Board, Stryten Energy), Mike Judd (CEO and President, Stryten Energy), Nathan Barker (Legislative Director for U.S. Representative Rich McCormick, GA-06), and Ronnie Joyner (Senior Manager, Laboratory, Stryten Energy).
- Event attendees included staff members from the offices of Senator Jon Ossoff, Senator Raphael Warnock, Representative Rich McCormick, Representative Andrew Clyde, and local community leaders.
- Attendees toured the new battery lab and an onsite working solar plus energy storage microgrid.
- Lieutenant Governor Burt Jones issued a proclamation recognizing the celebration.
- In his proclamation, Lieutenant Governor Burt Jones stated that Stryten Energy is helping shape the future of energy storage technology in Georgia important for national U.S. energy independence and security, while forging economic development and innovation in the state.
- Mike Judd, CEO and President of Stryten Energy, stated that achieving long-term U.S. energy resiliency and security requires a range of solutions across battery chemistries.
- Mike Judd noted that the expanded lab will allow the company to stress test lead and lithium batteries, achieve market readiness for newer chemistries like vanadium, and collaborate with customers to find additional applications for these chemistries.
- The lab supports Stryten Energy’s engineering, research and development, and quality assurance departments, as well as manufacturing facilities, in new product development, technology evaluations, warranty investigations, and root cause analysis.
- The Corporate Materials Lab performs tests on battery materials including electrolyte, polypropylene, lead, and lead alloys for automotive, heavy-duty commercial, industrial, lithium, and vanadium redox flow batteries.
- The Lead Battery Test Lab performs electrical performance and teardown testing on automotive, industrial, and heavy-duty commercial batteries, including enhanced flooded (EFB), conventional flooded, and absorbed glass mat (AGM) batteries.
- The Lithium Battery Test Lab is the newest addition to the facility and focuses on non-destructive functional charge/discharge, environmental, and UL-related testing on lithium battery products.
- The new lab will serve as a model to other local energy technology companies, further strengthening Georgia's claim as a key power player in the clean energy sector.
- Stryten Energy provides energy storage solutions across the Essential Power, Motive Power, Transportation, Military, and Government sectors.
- The company's stored energy technologies include advanced lead, lithium, and vanadium redox flow batteries, intelligent chargers, and energy performance management software.
- Stryten Energy powers applications ranging from submarines to subcompacts, microgrids, warehouses, distribution centers, cars, trains, and trucks.
