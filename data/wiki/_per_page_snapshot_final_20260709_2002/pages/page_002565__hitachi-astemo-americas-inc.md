# Hitachi Astemo Americas Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-07-11

**Source:** [Hitachi Astemo Americas to Expand in Berea with $153Million Investment Creating 167 Full-Time Jobs | Kentucky Cabinet for Economic Development](https://ced.ky.gov/Newsroom/NewsPage/20230711_Hitachi)  ·  `page_002565`

## Facts

- Hitachi Astemo Americas Inc. announced an expansion of its operation in Madison County with a $153 million investment.
- The investment will create 167 new full-time jobs for Kentuckians.
- Funds will support site improvements and renovations, including additional production lines and equipment to address increased market demand and growth within the EV industry.
- Expansion brings company's total Kentucky employment across Harrodsburg and Berea facilities to over 2,100 workers.
- Tim Clark serves as president of Hitachi Astemo Americas.
- In January 2021, Hitachi Automotive Systems, Keihin, Showa, and Nissin Kogyo completed a business merger to form the global mega-supplier Hitachi Astemo.
- Company provides advanced mobility solutions including electric powertrains for automobiles, advanced chassis, autonomous driving/advanced driver assistance systems, and motorcycle systems.
- Hitachi Astemo is headquartered in Tokyo, Japan, entered the Americas market in 1972 with a Brazil subsidiary, and expanded into the U.S. in the mid-1980s with its first Kentucky factory.
- Company operates more than 20 manufacturing sites across the United States, Canada, Mexico, and Brazil, including two locations in Kentucky.
- Madison County Judge/Executive Reagan Taylor expressed support for the expansion, citing positive community relationships and quality of life.
- Berea Mayor Bruce Fraley noted Hitachi Astemo is the city's largest employer and highlighted local workforce talent and economic climate.
- Donna Angel, business development director for the Berea Business and Economic Development Office, stated the company develops newest technology in parts for automotive and other EV engines.
- Kentucky has seen nearly 600 private-sector new-location or expansion projects commit to invest over $22.9 billion and create more than 30,000 jobs administration-to-date.
- Gov. Andy Beshear announced Kentucky has filled more than 2 million jobs, the most in state history, with 57,000 new jobs filled since the beginning of his administration.
- On May 18, Gov. Beshear announced Kentucky set a historic low unemployment rate of 3.7%.
- Since the beginning of his administration, Gov. Beshear has announced more than 860 private-sector new-location and expansion projects totaling $26.8 billion in announced investments.
- The average incentivized hourly wage in Kentucky was $26.78 before benefits in 2022, representing an 11.5% increase over the previous year.
- In September 2021, Gov. Beshear and leaders from Ford Motor Co. and SK On celebrated a $5.8 billion investment to create 5,000 jobs in Hardin County.
- In April 2022, leadership at AESC announced a $2 billion investment to create 2,000 jobs in Warren County.
- In May 2023, Gov. Beshear and Toyota leaders announced a $591 million investment across multiple projects for Georgetown's first battery electric vehicle production.
- In June 2023, Gov. Beshear joined INFAC North America to break ground on a $53 million investment for EV-related production in Campbellsville.
- On May 12, Gov. Beshear announced Kentucky received its first state-level credit rating upgrade in 13 years from Fitch Ratings.
- On June 29, the commonwealth received its first-ever rating increase from S&P Global Ratings.
- Site Selection magazine ranked Kentucky first in the South Central region and top-5 nationally for its 2023 Prosperity Cup ranking.
- Site Selection placed Kentucky second nationwide and first in the South Central region for its 2022 Governor’s Cup ranking.
- Gov. Beshear recently announced a new “Supply Kentucky” initiative to boost job growth, reduce costs, and secure supply chains for Kentucky companies.
- In March 2023, the Kentucky Economic Development Finance Authority (KEDFA) preliminarily approved a 10-year incentive agreement with Hitachi Astemo under the Kentucky Business Investment program.
- The performance-based agreement can provide up to $2.4 million in tax incentives based on annual investment targets.
- KEDFA also approved Hitachi Astemo for up to $200,000 in tax incentives through the Kentucky Enterprise Initiative Act (KEIA) to recoup sales and use tax on construction costs, building fixtures, equipment used in R&D, and electronic processing.
- Eligible incentives can be claimed against income tax liability and/or wage assessments.
- Hitachi Astemo is eligible for resources from Kentucky’s workforce service providers, including no-cost recruitment and job placement services, reduced-cost customized training, and job-training incentives.
