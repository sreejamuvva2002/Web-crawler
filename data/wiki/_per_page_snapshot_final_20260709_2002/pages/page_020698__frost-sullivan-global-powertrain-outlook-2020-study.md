# Frost & Sullivan Global Powertrain Outlook 2020 Study

**Type:** research_center  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2020-11-16

**Source:** [Global Powertrain Scenario - Auto Components India](https://autocomponentsindia.com/global-powertrain-scenario/)  ·  `page_020698`

## Facts

- Frost & Sullivan released the "Global Powertrain Outlook, 2020" study examining global supply and demand trends for efficient powertrains.
- Research Analyst Naga Karthik Voruganti stated that growth opportunities in key regional markets will vary considerably.
- Original Equipment Manufacturers (OEMs) are banking on the success of full hybrids, which have complied with stricter regulations and demonstrated growing customer confidence.
- Engine downsizing will continue while highly efficient Gasoline Direct Injection (GDI) engines witness increased adoption.
- Integration of Gasoline Particulate Filters (GPFs) and three-way Catalytic Converters (3WCs) is expected to increase substantially in 2020 as OEMs seek WLTP certification for gasoline vehicles.
- Mild-hybrid powertrains and standardization of exhaust after-treatment technologies, including Selective Catalytic Reduction (SCR) and coated GPF, are highlighted as major trends impacting the powertrain industry in 2020.
- Hybrid vehicle sales in India recorded a 75 percent growth from 2018.
- The rollout of Bharat Stage VI emission norms is expected to negatively impact diesel vehicle prices, causing them to rise.
- In the United States, 1.3 million electric and hybrid vehicles are expected to sell in 2020 alone, representing a 4.7 percent growth driven by Battery Electric Vehicles (BEVs) and mild and full hybrids.
- European electric vehicle (xEV) sales are expected to grow by 5.3 percent, with EVs projected to register a 27.5 percent growth despite moderate Covid-19 impacts.
- Latin America countries saw a spike in EV sales driven by hybrids, with EVs and hybrids expected to hold a 1.2 percent market share in 2020.
- Despite overall vehicle sales degrowth in China, the EV share is expected to grow from 4.9 percent of sales in 2019 to 5.6 percent in 2020.
- In South Korea, mild hybrid electric vehicles (MHEV) in the 48V segment enjoyed a 283 percent growth with sales quadrupling in 2019 following the end of a temporary tax cut in August.
- Car sales in Indonesia are expected to register a degrowth of 17.3 percent in 2020 due to Covid-19 impacting the global supply chain.
- Japanese brand new passenger car sales increased by 1.9 percent compared to foreign brand sales declining by 3.3 percent, alongside a general trend of vehicle sharing and fading appeal of cars among younger Japanese consumers.
- Global automotive sales are predicted to decline by 14.9 percent due to Covid-19 by the end of 2020 as manufacturing facilities and supply chains were hit by worldwide lockdowns, though consumer purchase trends are projected to remain unaffected.
- In advanced markets like Europe, demand for diesel engines is expected to decline by 30 percent by the end of 2020 as WLTP commences.
- Global EV sales are projected to increase by 3.4 percent, inducing demand for gasoline-hybrid and fully-electric powertrains.
