# Magna International

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2018-10-05

**Source:** [Magna to invest 150 million dollars and strength relations between Coahuila and Canada](https://www.promexicoindustry.com/en/article/magna-to-invest-150-million-dollars-and-strength-relations-between-coahuila-and-canada)  ·  `page_057488`

## Facts

- The Canadian automotive company Magna announced an investment of more than 150 million dollars and the generation of more than 3,000 jobs in Coahuila.
- Coahuila has consolidated as the main production bastion for Magna, having almost half of its national operations in the entity.
- The announcement resulted from a meeting between Governor Miguel Riquelme Solis and his delegation with Magna executives during an economic promotion tour in Canada.
- Executives stated that the place for the expansion is still being analyzed, with the Laguna Region being the tentative location due to its recent industrial growth.
- The new investment will exceed previous job expectations for all of Magna's plants in Coahuila, increasing employment from more than 14,000 currently to around 17,400 with the new positions.
- Governor Miguel Riquelme reiterated his government's support for new projects by Magna and other companies to grow employment and competitiveness for thousands of families in Coahuila.
- The governor highlighted that economic development in Coahuila is possible due to skilled labor, technical and higher-level schools, high schooling and worker specialization, as well as road, railway, and airport infrastructure.
- Jaime Guerra Perez, head of the Ministry of Economy and Tourism of Coahuila (SEDECT), stated that there are 28 Canadian companies with 47 plants in the automotive, mining, chemical industry, and metal-mechanic manufacturing sectors mainly in the state.
- Jaime Guerra Perez noted that Magna is the main Canadian investor in Coahuila, where it has 14 plants out of a total of 32 across all of Mexico.
- The Secretary of Economy added that in addition to the announced 150 million dollars, the company is fulfilling more studies to bring more investment to the state, showing confidence in the entity.
- Magna is analyzing the possibility of creating an engineering center, which could be located in Coahuila.
- Headquartered in Aurora, Ontario, Magna is the largest manufacturer of components for automobiles in Canada and owns Magna Steyr, a manufacturer of automobiles located in Steyr, Austria.
- Globally, Magna operates as an automotive supplier with more than 335 manufacturing operations and 102 product development, engineering, and sales centers in 29 countries, employing more than 170,000 people.
- In North America, the auto-parts company operates 137 manufacturing plants: 32 in Mexico, 50 in Canada, and 55 in the United States.
- Magna has 25 engineering, product development, or sales centers in North America: 13 in the United States, 11 in Canada, and one in Mexico.
- Magna was established in Ramos Arizpe in 1999 and currently employs more than 14,000 people across its 14 factories that produce a wide range of automotive components for the world.
- The company offers complete vehicle engineering services, with product capabilities including body, chassis, exterior, seats, power train, active driver assistance, vision, locking systems, and roof.
- Magna also has electronic and software capabilities in many of these areas.
- Although it supplies parts to major OEMs, the majority of its sales are to six customers: General Motors, Ford, Fiat Chrysler Automobiles, Daimler, BMW, and Volkswagen.
- The company's origins trace back to Frank Stronach, an Austrian who migrated to Canada in 1954 with 200 dollars.
- Frank Stronach turned a common workshop of paint and parts manufacturing in Toronto into one of the largest suppliers of automotive equipment in the world.
- Stronach was born on September 6th, 1932, in Weiz, southern Austria, left school at age 14, and migrated to Canada with only apprenticeship training in a tool-making workshop.
- After changing his name and doing different jobs, he launched his own company, Multimatic, in 1957, initially as the only employee.
- In 1967, he merged Multimatic with another company to register Magna Electronics Corp on the stock exchange, which became Magna International in 1973.
- By the end of the 1970s, sales of control panels and brake systems exceeded 150 million dollars.
- A week-long economic promotion tour in Canada aimed to attract investments to Coahuila, generate well-paid jobs, diversify the industry, increase competitiveness, and provide development opportunities.
- The promotional meetings highlighted Coahuila's competitive advantages, including its strategic position, security, educational level, qualified workforce, leader status in the automotive sector, and 68 industrial parks.
- The state's potential in manufacturing and clean energy, infrastructure development, connectivity, and natural resources in mining, agriculture, livestock, milk, brewing, and wine were also exposed to investors.
- During the tour, visits and interviews with Canadian companies in Toronto and Montreal led to agreements for an immediate visit to Coahuila's soil by businessmen.
- The Coahuila delegation included Governor Miguel Angel Riquelme, mayors of Saltillo (Manolo Jimenez), Monclova (Alfredo Paredes Lopez), Frontera (Florencio Siller Linaje), and CANACINTRA Centro Region president Rolando Rivero.
- Achievements from the tour included investment announcements, expansion projects, commitments, trade agreements, and educational/training partnerships.
- The delegation met with universities and businessmen from the Auto-parts Manufacturers Association, Mold Manufacturers Association of Canada, Ontario Teachers' Pension Plan, Samuel Associated Tube Group, Centennial College, Linamar, Conestoga College, The Woodbridge Group, Canimex Group, RDV Soucy International, Espacio por la vida, Montreal Metropolitan Chamber of Commerce, Commission for Environmental Cooperation, and Trans-Pro Logistics.
- Work meetings were held with Aero Montreal, an aerospace sector cluster, to reach joint projects, highlighting Coahuila's existing aerospace companies in engineering service, parts machining, security systems, heat treatment of metals, harnesses, and connectors.
