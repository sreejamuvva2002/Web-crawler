# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Georgia - Israel Cooperation | Jewish Virtual Library](https://jewishvirtuallibrary.org/jsource/states/GA.html)  ·  `page_053920`

## Facts

- Georgia exported $182,249,506 worth of goods to Israel in 2015.
- Total exports from Georgia to Israel between 1996 and the present totaled $2,801,689,491.
- Israel ranked as Georgia's 41st trade partner in 2015.
- Military contracts with Israel in 2015 were valued at $46,232,283.
- The American-Israel Chamber of Commerce Southeast Division (AICC-SD) was established in Atlanta in 1992 to help Israeli businesses explore markets and develop relationships with companies in Georgia, Alabama, Mississippi, North Carolina, South Carolina, and Tennessee.
- AICC-SD boasts over 450 members and has facilitated completed transactions valued at over $700 million.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) established an Israel Office in Jerusalem in 1994 to promote trade, investment, and tourism.
- Governor Zell Miller created the Georgia-Israel Exchange in 1992 to explore emerging technology in industry and agriculture and enhance trade.
- In March 2011, the GDEcD planned a mission to Israel focusing on multi-industry trade opportunities in environmental, telecom, advanced manufacturing, and alternative energy industries.
- Governor Sonny Perdue led a delegation of 36 Georgians on a technology and trade mission to Israel in November 2005.
- Georgia is one of 33 states that have cooperative agreements with Israel.
- In 2012, Georgia exported over $158,802,552 worth of manufacturing goods to Israel.
- Since 1996, Georgia exports to Israel totaled more than $2,392,988,141, making Israel Georgia's 15th leading trade partner.
- In 2012, Georgia received over $97,403,798 in foreign military financing (FMF) for U.S. military aid to Israel.
- Companies that received FMF funding include GE Intelligence Platforms Technology in Atlanta, Hewlett-Packard Company in Atlanta, and Horthrop Grumman Support Services in Warner Robins.
- More than 100 Georgia companies do business in Israel, including BellSouth, CocaCola, Home Depot, and Holiday Inn.
- Apica Cardiovascular, a combined Georgia Tech and Emory University medical-device startup, received a $5.1 million investment in June 2010 from investors including TriVentures of Herziliyah, Israel.
- In January 2011, Canton-based J&S Chemicals formed a distribution agreement for specialty chemical products in the Israeli market through Ra'anana-based Orian International Marketing, Ltd.
- Ami Bental, President of Systematic Controls in Kennesaw, worked out a partnership agreement to manufacture instrumentation and control systems for Israeli industry with a kibbutz on the Golan Heights.
- BellSouth was part of a consortium that won the tender to operate the second cellular phone network in Israel, providing service to approximately 900,000 subscribers.
- Advance Building Supply opened the first Ace hardware store in Israel.
- At least three industry-specific exchanges between Israel and Georgia have been organized by the AICC to promote health care, telecommunications, and software incubators.
- 26 Israeli companies now have U.S. or regional headquarters in Georgia.
- The Binational Industrial Research and Development Foundation (BIRD) funds joint U.S.-Israeli teams in developing innovative technological products.
- Since its inception, BIRD has funded more than 800 joint high-tech R&D projects through conditional grants totaling more than $210 million.
- Products from BIRD ventures have generated more than $8 billion in revenues and created an estimated 20,000 American jobs.
- Georgia companies have benefited from close to one million dollars in BIRD grants over the last three decades.
- Four Georgia companies have received BIRD grants.
- ARRIS (Suwanee) and Resolute Networks Ltd. developed an integrated end-to-end solution for Multi-Service Operators based on a BIRD Foundation grant.
- The Binational Science Foundation (BSF), established in 1972, supports collaborative research projects between U.S. and Israeli scientists.
- Since its inception, BSF has awarded some $480 million through more than 4,000 grants.
- Institutions in Georgia have shared with their Israeli counterparts nearly $3 million in BSF grants awarded since 1996 alone.
- The University of Georgia, Georgia Tech, and Emory are among the BSF grant recipients.
- Albert Padwa of Emory has collaborated with a collaborator from Bar-Ilan University on alkaloid synthesis for cancer inhibitors and antibiotic properties.
- The Binational Agricultural Research and Development Fund (BARD) was created in 1978 to fund programs between U.S. and Israeli scientists for agricultural research.
- Since its inception, BARD has funded more than 1,000 projects with a total investment of more than $250 million.
- Georgia institutions have shared grants worth more than $2.6 million since 1979, with the University of Georgia receiving the largest share.
- Edward Law, a biological and agricultural engineer at the University of Georgia's Athens campus, received two BARD grants for an electrostatic sprayer system and research into pollen transfer.
