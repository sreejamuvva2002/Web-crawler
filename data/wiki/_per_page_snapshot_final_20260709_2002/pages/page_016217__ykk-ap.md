# YKK AP

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2022-08-16

**Source:** [YKK AP Plans New Factory in Macon, 100 New Jobs Created | Choose Macon](https://choosemacon.com/ykk-ap-plans-new-factory-in-macon-100-new-jobs-created/)  ·  `page_016217`

## Facts

- YKK AP will build a new state-of-the-art factory at the Macon-Bibb I-75 Industrial Park in Macon, Ga., according to a release from the Macon-Bibb County Industrial Authority.
- The new plant is part of an expansion that will include additional machinery and equipment in Bibb County.
- YKK AP plans to add 100 jobs in addition to its 250 jobs currently located in Macon.
- Governor Brian Kemp announced the deal on Tuesday, which includes up to a $125 million investment from YKK in the new location.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that being the No. 1 state for business means prioritizing ensuring companies already operating in Georgia can grow and thrive alongside attracting new investments.
- Governor Brian Kemp said he is excited YKK AP recognizes the advantages of doing business in middle Georgia and has chosen to expand its relationship with the Macon community through 100 new jobs.
- After completion, YKK AP will relocate its operations and employees from its current site on Ocmulgee Boulevard to the new factory.
- The company’s residential division, which manufactures vinyl windows and doors for builders and homeowners, will be located at the new Macon location.
- Macon-Bibb County Mayor Lester M. Miller stated it is an especially great day in Macon-Bibb County when one of their longest-running business partners decides to make another investment in the community and bring an additional 100 jobs to the area.
- Mayor Lester M. Miller noted that the new state-of-the-art facility will be used as a blueprint for future facilities, illustrating trust in Macon-Bibb County and Georgia as the best place to do business.
- YKK AP expects operations at the new site to begin in early 2024.
