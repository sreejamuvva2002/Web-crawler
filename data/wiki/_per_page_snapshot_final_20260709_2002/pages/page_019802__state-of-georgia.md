# State of Georgia

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2018-11-05

**Source:** [Site Selection ranks Georgia's business climate No. 1 for sixth straight year - Location Georgia](https://www.locationgeorgia.com/2018/11/05/site-selection-ranks-georgias-business-climate-no-1-for-sixth-straight-year/)  ·  `page_019802`

## Facts

- Gov. Nathan Deal announced that Georgia’s business climate has been ranked No. 1 in the nation for the sixth consecutive year by Site Selection.
- Georgia is the first state to hold this ranking for six consecutive years under leadership by the same governor.
- Governor Deal stated that earning the distinction six consecutive times speaks to the strength of the business-friendly environment and strategic investments made across the state.
- The announcement resulted from collaboration between public and private sector partners alongside conservative policies attracting business.
- Georgia has cut taxes and made deeper investments in its workforce since first earning the distinction.
- Site Selection releases its business climate rankings each November based on a survey of corporate real estate executives and site consultants.
- The ranking index uses tax burden criteria from the Tax Foundation and KPMG’s Location Matters analysis, plus qualifying projects from the Conway Projects Database tracking worldwide facility activity.
- Georgia has been ranked among the top 10 states throughout the last decade and held the top spot for five years prior to this announcement.
- Site Selection editor-in-chief Mark Arend noted a six-year top business climate streak is highly unusual and credits Governor Deal’s early commitment to making Georgia competitive.
- Capital investors report that Georgia delivers fiscal soundness, logistics infrastructure, competitive business costs, and required workers.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson, former GDEcD Commissioners Attorney General Chris Carr, and Chris Cummiskey joined Governor Deal at the news conference.
- Over the past eight years, Georgia has seen the creation of roughly 750,000 new private sector jobs.
- Georgia’s unemployment rate is at 3.7 percent, its lowest point since 2001.
- In October 2018 alone, Governor Deal and GDEcD announced the creation of more than 2,500 jobs and over $215 million in private sector investment across Cobb, Emanuel, Fulton, Hall, and Troup counties.
- Since 2011, GDEcD has worked on more than 2,900 economic development locations, bringing roughly 214,000 jobs and $39.6 billion in investment to Georgia communities.
- GDEcD’s Global Commerce Division helped bring in $5.56 billion in new investments for Georgia during the last fiscal year.
- Overall exports from Georgia have increased by 28.8 percent since 2010.
- Since 2012, 74 percent of GDEcD location projects were located outside the metro Atlanta area.
- When state tax cuts passed earlier this year are fully implemented, Georgia’s corporate and individual income tax rates will be reduced from 6 percent to 5.5 percent.
- State tax reforms will double the standard deduction for individuals and families from $3,000 to $6,000.
- Federal and state tax reforms could save Georgians up to $5 billion over the next five years.
- These tax reforms are estimated to lead to a $4,200 increase in wage and salary income for the average household.
- The percent of Georgia’s population living below the poverty line has decreased by 2.1 percent since January 2017, reaching its lowest point since 2006.
- Monthly initial claims for unemployment insurance in Georgia recently fell to their lowest level since 1974.
- Georgia has maintained a AAA bond rating for 21 consecutive years.
- The state’s Rainy Day Fund has topped $2.5 billion, ranking it the highest in the Southeast and one of the top ten highest in the nation.
- Site Selection is published by Conway Data Inc. and delivers expansion planning information to 48,000 executives of fast-growing firms.
- Site Selection publishes or co-publishes several e-newsletters including The Site Selection Dispatch, Life Sciences Report, Aerospace Report, Energy Report, Site Selection International, The FDI Report, TrustBelt.com, and the OnSITE Travel blog.
