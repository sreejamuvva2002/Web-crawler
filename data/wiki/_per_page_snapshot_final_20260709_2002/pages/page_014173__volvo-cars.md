# Volvo Cars

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-23

**Source:** [Volvo Cars to produce new US model amid tariff pressure – Wall Street Observer](https://wallstreetobserver.com/volvo-cars-to-produce/)  ·  `page_014173`

## Facts

- Volvo Cars announced it will begin producing a new hybrid model in the United States by the end of this decade to adjust to President Donald Trump’s tariff policies.
- The new hybrid model will be designed specifically for the U.S. market to help boost capacity utilization at Volvo's South Carolina plant.
- Volvo Cars CEO Hakan Samuelsson stated that it will take time until America is fully electric.
- CEO Hakan Samuelsson described the new larger hybrid model as primarily electric with a backup combustion engine that activates when the battery is depleted.
- Automakers worldwide are rethinking production strategies to address President Trump's duties on foreign cars as part of a broader policy to boost U.S. manufacturing.
- In April, Volvo Cars' CEO stated the company would increase U.S. vehicle production, and in July, the company announced plans to begin producing the XC60 mid-size SUV at its South Carolina plant starting in late 2026.
- The South Carolina plant currently produces only the fully electric EX90 SUV and the Polestar 3, utilizing only a small fraction of its 150,000 vehicle annual capacity.
- The announcement of the new hybrid model coincided with Volvo's 70th anniversary in the United States.
- Volvo previously aimed to phase out all non-electric models by 2030 but reversed course last year to keep hybrids in its lineup.
- Luis Rezende, president of Volvo Cars' Americas operation, stated that adding another model to local production will help realize the full potential of their U.S. manufacturing investments and workforce.
- A July trade deal with the European Union reduced U.S. tariffs on European cars to 15%, though it has not taken effect and vehicles currently face a 27.5% levy.
- CEO Hakan Samuelsson noted that eliminating 10% duties on auto imports from the U.S. to Europe makes selling the U.S.-produced EX90 abroad more attractive.
- Samuelsson stated that the 10% tariff reduction provides an opportunity for growth in Volvo's European operations.
