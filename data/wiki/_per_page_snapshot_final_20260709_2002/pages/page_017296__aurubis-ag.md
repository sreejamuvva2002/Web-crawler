# Aurubis AG

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2022-12-21

**Source:** [Aurubis adopts € 530 million investment package to accelerate growth and sustainability](https://www.aurubis.com/en/media/press-releases/press-releases-2022/aurubis-adopts-530-million-investment-package-to-accelerate-growth-and-sustainability)  ·  `page_017296`

## Facts

- Aurubis AG's Supervisory Board approved an additional comprehensive investment package of around €530 million.
- The investment package includes accelerated expansion of a modular recycling system and doubling of processing capacity at the Aurubis Richmond site in Georgia, USA.
- A second recycling module with an investment volume of around €250 million is slated to be added to the Aurubis Richmond plant currently under construction in Georgia.
- An additional €90 million was approved for a capital expenditure adjustment covering infrastructure requirements and inflation for the current project.
- Production at the expanded Aurubis Richmond site is scheduled to begin in 2026.
- Recycling in the USA is growing by 5% every year, resulting in rapid increases in the availability of complex recycling materials.
- The new expansion stage will double throughput volume from the previously planned 90,000 tons of complex recycling materials to 180,000 tons per year.
- The additional plant at the Richmond site will be built in accordance with the highest environmental standards for environmentally friendly recycling of input materials.
- Aurubis anticipates creating around 80 additional jobs in the region due to the expansion.
- Once both recycling modules are completed, Aurubis anticipates employing around 200 people at the Richmond site.
- The expansion leverages a high level of synergy and limited need for additional space by using existing infrastructure designed for expansion.
- Starting in 2025/26, Aurubis anticipates additional EBITDA totaling around €90 million per year from the approved expansion phase.
- The Richmond plant will benefit from attractive local electricity and natural gas prices that will positively impact cost efficiency.
- Construction is already underway on the first plant at the Richmond site.
- Together with the planned expansion, Aurubis anticipates total investments of €640 million for the Richmond site.
- The expanded Richmond site is projected to deliver an additional contribution to EBITDA of around €170 million starting in fiscal year 2026/27.
- Another part of the investment package involves securing the core business with an investment of around €190 million in the Complex Recycling Hamburg (CRH) project at the Hamburg site.
- The CRH project will increase capacity to process around 30,000 more tons of recycling material and internal complex smelter intermediary products annually on a larger scale.
- The CRH process was developed in-house and considerably broadens metallurgical capabilities in the Aurubis smelter network while optimizing utilized capacity for existing plants.
- The CRH project will improve the processing of precious metals with an even shorter process run time.
- Once the CRH project is ramped up in 2025, the company forecasts an annual earnings contribution of around €40 million.
- The third approved project is the expansion of the existing photovoltaic plant at the Pirdop site in Bulgaria by an additional 14 MW.
- An initial investment volume of around €12 million will be used to increase the Pirdop solar park's output from 14 MW to 24 MW.
- Solar panels for future enlargement of the Pirdop solar park will also be purchased for €8 million.
- The current expansion employs stationary, double-sided modules that produce more than conventional PV modules.
- The expanded Pirdop solar plant is scheduled to be commissioned as early as 2024.
- Once complete, the expanded solar park will generate enough electricity to power 9,000 households or a small city every year.
- Compared to coal-fired power generation, the expanded solar park will save 34,000 tons of CO2 emissions per year, or over 500,000 tons for its planned 15-year operating period.
- The in-house solar park expansion advances Aurubis' sustainability goals calling for a 50% reduction of Scope 1 + 2 emissions by 2030.
- The three strategic projects are projected to deliver an annual earnings contribution of around €130 million (EBITDA) in total.
- Aurubis plans to finance the approved projects primarily out of current cash flow with no need for a capital increase.
- The Supervisory Board and Executive Board will recommend a dividend of €1.80 per share, which is the highest recommended dividend since the company's IPO.
- The previous year's dividend was €1.60 per share.
- Aurubis reported an outstanding 2021/22 operating EBT of €532 million, compared to a previous year adjusted figure of €381 million.
- For the current fiscal year 2022/23, the company projects an operating EBT of between €400 and €500 million.
- The Supervisory Board also agreed to move forward with a comprehensive modernization project of the company’s IT landscape, including migration to the S/4HANA SAP platform.
- CEO Roland Harings stated that the new resolutions show the company is delivering on its “Driving Sustainable Growth” strategy and accelerating growth in recycling at its US plant.
- COO Heiko Arnold noted that the CRH project retains much more value creation in-house, highlights research and development expertise, and creates a foundation for additional strategic projects at the Hamburg plant.
