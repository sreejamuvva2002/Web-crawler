# Stryten Energy LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-08-16

**Source:** [Stryten Energy Applauds Investments in Energy Security and U.S. Manufacturers with the Passage of the Inflation Reduction Act](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/stryten-energy-applauds-investments-energy-192100837.html)  ·  `page_050744`

## Facts

- Stryten Energy LLC is a U.S.-based energy storage solutions provider headquartered in Alpharetta, Georgia.
- The company offers advanced vanadium redox flow battery (VRFB) technology that provides medium and long-duration energy storage (LDES).
- VRFB technology helps ensure grid stability and facilitates increased utilization of solar and wind for businesses and consumers across the U.S.
- The Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) includes vital Investment Tax Credits (ITC) for domestically sourced and manufactured standalone clean energy storage intended to lower costs, increase energy efficiency, and strengthen energy security for America.
- Tim Vargo, Chief Executive Officer of Stryten Energy, stated that the legislation's long-term, standalone energy storage investment tax credits and its ten-year runway will help customers incorporate medium and long-duration energy storage such as VRFB batteries into their operations more economically.
- Leveraging domestic VRFB technology and other long-term energy storage solutions will enable reliable access to clean power and help the U.S. achieve energy security as it transitions to a clean energy economy, according to Vargo.
- Scott Childers, Vice President of Stryten Energy's Essential Power Division, stated that Stryten's patented flow battery technology is uniquely positioned to provide safe, long-term, reliable on-demand energy needed to build a new economy powered by clean energy.
- The Inflation Reduction Act is expected to accelerate the demand for domestically sourced energy storage, especially for commercial applications such as charging stations, manufacturing plants, and businesses with 24/7 energy requirements, according to Childers.
- Stryten's VRFB technology uses domestically sourced vanadium electrolyte that is easily recycled.
- The VRFB technology features increased power density and reduced battery downtime.
- The batteries offer the potential of limitless cycle life with proper maintenance.
- VRFB provides high-temperature ratings and is thermally safe.
- The technology is scalable to meet varying energy storage capacity needs.
- Stryten's VRFB technology is ideal for applications that require more than four hours of storage capacity to deploy clean energy on demand from renewable sources such as solar and wind energy.
- Target applications for the VRFB include utility-scale storage, microgrids, and military bases.
- Stryten Energy provides energy storage solutions across the Essential Power, Motive Power, Transportation, Military, and Government sectors.
- The company powers applications ranging from submarines to subcompacts, microgrids, warehouses, distribution centers, cars, trains, and trucks.
- Stryten's stored energy technologies include advanced lead, lithium, and vanadium redox flow batteries, intelligent chargers, and energy performance management software.
- Stryten is an industry leader backed by more than a century of expertise.
