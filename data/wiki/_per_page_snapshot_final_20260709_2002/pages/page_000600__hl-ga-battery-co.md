# HL-GA Battery Co.

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-10

**Source:** [Georgia's Electric Mobility Dream Tested After Hyundai Raid - TT](https://www.ttnews.com/articles/georgia-hyundai-kemp-factory)  ·  `page_000600`

## Facts

- Immigration authorities raided a battery plant construction site and detained foreign workers.
- Some 475 people were detained in the raid, including more than 300 South Koreans.
- The raid occurred at the construction site for a battery plant that will be operated by HL-GA Battery Co., a joint venture between Hyundai and LG Energy Solution.
- The battery plant is slated to open next year.
- The battery plant is part of a $7.6 billion complex planned to eventually employ 8,500 people.
- The complex was announced and then expanded after trips by Gov. Kemp to South Korea to court officials at Hyundai and other firms.
- Hyundai stated it is investing $26 billion in the United States from 2025 to 2028.
- Hyundai's U.S. investments include an EV plant in Ellabell, Ga., located about 50 miles west of Savannah.
- Hyundai's U.S. investments include a separate $5 billion battery joint venture in Cartersville, Ga.
- Hyundai's U.S. investments include a steel mill in Donaldsonville, La.
- Hyundai's U.S. investments include a robotics hub and an expansion of the Hyundai plant in Montgomery, Ala.
- Kia Corp. has a plant in West Point, Ga. as part of these regional investments.
- The governor’s office stated: “In Georgia, we will always enforce the law, including all state and federal immigration laws. All companies operating within the state must follow the laws of Georgia and our nation.”
- Protesters staged a rally against the detention of South Korean workers near the U.S. Embassy in Seoul on Sept. 9.
- On Sept. 5, Kemp's office stated that the state Department of Public Safety coordinated with U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement to support the raid.
- Kemp is in his second term as governor and has declared a goal of making Georgia the “electric mobility capital of America.”
- In a 2018 ad, Kemp said: “I’ve got a big truck, just in case I’ve got to round up some criminal illegals and take them home myself.”
- Kemp backed a state law requiring local jails to check the immigration status of prisoners.
- Kemp announced he would mobilize 75 National Guard soldiers to support ICE operations in Georgia last month.
- Kemp announced he would send more than 300 National Guard members to take part in Trump’s law enforcement takeover in Washington.
- Kemp has journeyed twice to South Korea to meet with Hyundai and LG Group.
- Kemp disputes claims that tax dollars were subsidizing the jobs of detained workers.
- His administration says none of the $2.1 billion in projected state and local incentives for the Hyundai complex directly benefits subcontractors who employ detainees.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development stated: “Without exception, the Department of Economic Development expects anyone doing business in Georgia to follow federal and state laws.”
- Kemp has clashed with Democratic U.S. Sen. Jon Ossoff over credit for Georgia’s green energy boom.
- Kemp disputes that incentives signed by President Joe Biden spurred investment, saying industries were already coming to Georgia before the Inflation Reduction Act passed in 2022.
- The rush of projects accelerated after Biden signed his climate law.
- According to E2, 33 additional green energy projects were announced in Georgia by the end of 2024, the most nationwide.
- Projects in Georgia top $20 billion, pledging more than 25,000 jobs.
- Hyundai has continued investing even as Trump and congressional Republicans rolled back electric vehicle incentives.
