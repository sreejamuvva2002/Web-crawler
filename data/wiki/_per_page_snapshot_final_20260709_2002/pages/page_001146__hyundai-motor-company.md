# Hyundai Motor Company

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-11

**Source:** [Hyundai Raid](https://www.barks.com/post/hyundai-raid)  ·  `page_001146`

## Facts

- Immigration enforcement agents stormed a Hyundai electric vehicle battery production plant in Georgia last Friday, citing a shockingly large number of illegal workers and raising concerns about workforce security.
- Hyundai Motor Company stated it is aware of the immigration enforcement action that took place at the construction site of its supplier, HL-GA Battery Company in Bryan County, Georgia.
- Hyundai stated it is "closely monitoring the situation while working to understand the specific circumstances."
- Based on Hyundai's current understanding, none of those detained are directly employed by Hyundai Motor Company.
- Hyundai committed to full compliance with all laws and regulations in every market where it operates, including employment verification requirements and immigration laws.
- Hyundai expects the same commitment to legal compliance from all its partners, suppliers, contractors, and subcontractors.
- Hyundai stated that the safety and well-being of everyone working at its sites is a top priority.
- Hyundai views incidents like this as reminders of the importance of robust oversight throughout its entire supply chain and contractor network.
- Hyundai is reviewing its processes to ensure all parties working on its projects maintain high standards of legal compliance, including thorough vetting of employment practices by contractors and subcontractors.
- North America Chief Manufacturing Officer Chris Susock will now assume governance of the entire megasite in Georgia.
- Hyundai will conduct an investigation to ensure all suppliers and their subcontractors comply with all laws and regulations.
- Hyundai stated it has zero tolerance for those who don't follow the law.
- Hyundai continues to invest in American manufacturing and create thousands of jobs in full accordance with U.S. law, reflecting values of treating all people with dignity and respect.
