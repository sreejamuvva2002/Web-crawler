# Ascend Elements

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2022-02-28

**Source:** [Economic Development Around the State - Georgia Trend Magazine](https://www.georgiatrend.com/2022/02/28/economic-development-around-the-state-60/)  ·  `page_052614`

## Facts

- Lithium-ion battery recycling and engineered materials startup Ascend Elements (formerly Battery Resourcers) is investing $43 million to open its first commercial-scale battery recycling plant in Newton County.
- The Ascend Elements facility will create 150 jobs.
- When fully operational in August, the 154,000-square-foot facility will be the largest of its kind in North America.
- Roper Corp., a subsidiary of GE Appliances, is investing $118 million to expand its cooking products manufacturing operations in LaFayette.
- The Roper Corp. expansion will create 600 new jobs.
- Roper Corp. has been in Georgia since 1973 and employs 2,000 people in the region.
- The Georgia Institute of Technology is expanding its biosciences and health innovation district, Technology Enterprise Park.
- The five phases of development would add more than 365,000 square feet of life sciences-focused laboratory and office space and 280 apartments.
- The Georgia Tech project has the potential to create 3,500 prominent jobs for the area, generating upwards of $8 billion annually.
- The nation’s largest vertical farming company Bowery Farming is opening an indoor vertical farm in Locust Grove, creating more than 100 jobs.
- Bowery’s farms are powered by 100% renewable energy and grow pesticide-free greens and herbs for more than 800 U.S. grocery stores and e-commerce sites.
- Titan Steel Door is investing more than $11 million to expand operations in Hart County.
- The 145,000-square-foot Titan Steel Door expansion will increase capacity of its existing lines and add a new product line of detention walls and ceilings, creating 90 new jobs.
- Atlanta-based T5 Data Centers is building a 140-acre government and enterprise cloud data center campus in Augusta across from Fort Gordon.
- In 2021, the Port of Savannah moved a record 5.6 million twenty-foot equivalent container units (TEUs), 20% more than in 2020.
- Colonel’s Island Terminal in Brunswick saw an increase of 10.6% in volume of cars, trucks and machinery.
