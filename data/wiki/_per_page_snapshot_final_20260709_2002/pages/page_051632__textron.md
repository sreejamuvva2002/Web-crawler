# Textron

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-04-30

**Source:** [Textron (TXT) Q1 2026 Earnings Call Transcript | The Motley Fool](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/04/30/textron-txt-q1-2026-earnings-call-transcript/)  ·  `page_051632`

## Facts

- Textron announced its intent to separate the Industrial segment within 12 to 18 months through a potential sale or tax-free spin-off into a stand-alone company, with alternatives under evaluation.
- The Industrial segment is composed of Kautex and Textron Specialized Vehicles.
- Kautex operates as a Tier 1 auto supplier whose primary product line is fuel systems for the automotive industry.
- Kautex has built a meaningful position in hybrid fuel tanks, which is a growing part of the industry.
- The Pentatonic battery and closure business supports EV and hybrid platforms, including the Rivian R1.
- Pentatonic supports a major European OEM start of production plan for 2027.
- Allegro cleaning systems is a growth platform focused on solutions to clean autonomous vehicle cameras and sensors.
- Kautex secured its largest award to date for its hybrid plastic fuel tank offering.
- Textron is repositioning some of its R&D funding into the supply chain and focusing on making factories much more effective.
- Management intends to invest in enabling the workforce with better tools and capabilities to improve production output and yield.
- Kautex revenues were $486 million, up $36 million or 8% from a year ago, primarily due to a favorable impact of $20 million from foreign exchange rate fluctuations and higher volume and mix.
- Industrial segment revenues were $786 million, down $6 million from last year's first quarter on a reported basis, but up 4% organically once accounting for the powersports divestiture.
- Textron is reallocating some of its R&D investment into supply chains and factories to drive growth and enhance profitability across its business.
