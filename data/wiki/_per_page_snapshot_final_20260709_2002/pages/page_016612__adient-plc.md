# Adient plc

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Flow Racks Put Automotive Supplier in the Driver’s Seat | ASSEMBLY](https://www.assemblymag.com/articles/97590-flow-racks-put-automotive-supplier-in-the-drivers-seat)  ·  `page_016612`

## Facts

- Based in Plymouth, MI, Adient plc is the world’s largest manufacturer of automotive seating.
- The company accounts for one-third of the global market and provides components for 25 million vehicles.
- Adient was founded as a spin-off from Johnson Controls in 2016.
- Today, the company works with all major automakers and vehicle classes.
- Adient operates some 200 assembly plants in 30 countries.
- One of those factories is a 125,000-square-foot plant in West Point, GA.
- At the West Point factory, seats, door panels and other interior parts are assembled for the Kia Sorento.
- Adient began building the Georgia plant in 2009 while it was still part of Johnson Controls.
- Todd Emmons is an engineer in the automotive business unit for Adient.
- Subassemblies for some 500 cars a day move through the West Point facility.
- The plant completes 55 jobs an hour, with a goal to step that up to 60 jobs an hour.
- John Maicki is president of DR Storage Systems.
- Adient required durable, ergonomically sound assembly line technology capable of accommodating various product sizes and being moved, adjusted, and reused as production demands changed.
- The company previously used tubular pipe structures on assembly lines in some plants but found them ineffective for the Georgia facility due to customization and mobility limitations.
- Adient selected UNEX Manufacturing’s Flow Cell workstations after conducting a thorough analysis, obtaining vendor quotes, and developing prototypes.
- Each workstation at the West Point plant has 84 inches of pick space.
- UNEX’s Flow Bed carton flow accommodates seats, door handles, power controls, air bags, brackets, buttons, and other materials.
- The Georgia plant operates on a just-in-time basis to deliver products exactly when the nearby Kia facility is ready.
- Reconfiguring the Flow Cells at the West Point plant is three times faster than reconfiguring other types of rack products.
- Adient plans to soon add new car parts to the production line for other types of vehicles.
- Adient has ordered 41 Flow Cells and assembled them using UNEX instructions in less than half an hour each.
- John Maicki first recommended the Flow Cell product to a customer in 2003.
- Customers are constantly reconfiguring products and updating standards, requiring plants to remain flexible and efficient.
