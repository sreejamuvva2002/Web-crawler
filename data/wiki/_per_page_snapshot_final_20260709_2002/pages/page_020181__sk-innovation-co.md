# SK Innovation Co.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2021-03-01

**Source:** [Pair of South Korean EV battery makers seek to overturn ITC ruling against Georgia factory | Fox Business](https://www.foxbusiness.com/energy/pair-of-south-korean-ev-battery-makers-seek-to-overturn-itc-ruling-against-georgia-factory)  ·  `page_020181`

## Facts

- SK Innovation Co. is seeking White House intervention to overturn a U.S. International Trade Commission (ITC) ruling that would force it to abandon its $2.6 billion battery factory complex under construction in Georgia, which it had hoped to open later this year.
- The Georgia battery complex, located northeast of Atlanta in Commerce, Ga., is expected to create 2,600 jobs and supply electric-car batteries to Ford Motor Co. and Volkswagen AG.
- SKI disclosed previously unreported plans to expand the factory complex by investing an additional $2.4 billion and creating 3,400 more jobs by 2025.
- Rival battery maker LG Chem met with administration trade officials Friday to pitch for preserving the ITC ruling against SKI.
- Nearly two years ago, LG Chem filed a complaint with the ITC accusing SKI of stealing trade secrets related to lithium-ion battery production, allegations SKI has denied.
- In a Feb. 10 ruling, the ITC upheld an earlier judge's determination that SKI destroyed evidence in the case and prohibited SKI from importing batteries and components needed for its U.S. factory production for a decade.
- The ITC allowed SKI to fulfill existing contracts with Ford and Volkswagen for four and two years, respectively, to give those automakers time to find alternative battery suppliers.
- The ruling initiated a 60-day review period during which the White House could overrule the decision on policy grounds.
- LG Chem stated it can increase its U.S. battery-making capacity to accommodate Ford and Volkswagen during SKI's import ban and is open to accepting financial restitution from SKI to settle the dispute.
- A spokesman for the Office of the U.S. Trade Representative declined to comment on discussions with the two battery makers.
- The legal dispute underscores the high stakes for automakers in securing batteries to meet electric vehicle growth targets amid tightening tailpipe emission restrictions and government commitments to phase out gas-powered vehicles.
- The case aligns with President Biden's policy priorities of transitioning transportation to electric vehicles, creating jobs, and building domestic battery production capacity to reduce reliance on foreign manufacturers.
- On Wednesday, the Biden administration ordered a 100-day review of electric vehicle battery supply chains, as well as those for semiconductor chips, pharmaceuticals, and rare-earth minerals, to spur U.S. industry growth.
- South Korean Prime Minister Chung Sye-kyun called the dispute embarrassing on Jan. 28 and stated he had spoken with the heads of both companies to urge resolution.
- Ford Chief Executive Jim Farley tweeted that a settlement between SKI and LG Chem would be in the best interests of U.S. manufacturers and workers.
- Ford and Volkswagen told the ITC last year that sanctions against the SKI plant could significantly hinder their efforts to build electric vehicles in the U.S.
- SKI is set to supply batteries for an electric version of Ford's F-150 pickup truck, which is expected to launch next year.
- Volkswagen plans to begin building its ID.4 crossover in Tennessee in 2022 using batteries supplied from SKI.
- SKI's presentation to administration officials clarified that the ITC judgment was based on evidence destruction rather than a finding of trade-secret theft.
- The presentation highlighted other SK Group investments in U.S.-related businesses, such as last year's $9 billion acquisition of Intel Corp.'s flash-memory manufacturing business by subsidiary SK Hynix Inc.
- Presidential intervention in ITC rulings has historical precedent, including the Obama administration's 2013 overruling of a ban on Apple Inc. importing iPhones and iPads following patent infringement allegations from Samsung Electronics Co.
