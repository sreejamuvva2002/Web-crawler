# Kumho

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2008-03-18

**Source:** [City's Reserves Might Help Fund Tire Plant - Tire Review Magazine](https://www.tirereview.com/city-s-reserves-might-help-fund-tire-plant/)  ·  `page_048626`

## Facts

- Korean tire manufacturer Kumho is expected to bring more than 400 jobs to Bibb County.
- Local officials promised to waive permits and fees related to the plant’s construction as part of the deal.
- Economic development officials compiled a nearly $12.5 million pot of money to help pay for site acquisition and preparation, infrastructure improvements, and other startup costs.
- Macon generally does not forego permit fees as policy, so it will contribute cash to the pile of incentives and in-kind work instead.
- The incentive pool includes contributions from state and federal grants, the Bibb County Commission, the Macon Water Authority, plus other public and private entities.
- The project requires about $280,000 to be paid to Macon’s Bureau of Inspection and Fees as well as the Macon-Bibb County Planning & Zoning Commission.
- Chip Cherry, president of both the Macon Economic Development Commission and the Greater Macon Chamber of Commerce, stated that competition for the plant was intense and necessitated 'extraordinary incentives' including the agreement to waive permit fees.
- The project was one of the state’s largest when negotiations began last year.
- Through sales tax revenue on equipment and energy used, Kumho’s presence in Bibb County will directly repay Macon within four to five years.
- Development officials estimate a net economic benefit of $7.6 million for the region over the next 20 years from the company’s facility investment and high-paying skilled jobs.
- The city's contribution will be paid from Macon’s unallocated reserve account, causing the fund balance to dip down to $90,000.
- Human Resources Director Ben Hubbard stated that about $500,000 in salary savings from vacant positions is being transferred into the reserve fund to replenish it.
- Appropriations Chairman Mike Cranford called Kumho a 'wonderful project' and a unique opportunity for the city after his committee unanimously approved the payment.
