# Hyundai Motor Group EV and Battery Metaplant

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-07-05

**Source:** [5 things: Hyundai development | Georgia Public Broadcasting](https://www.gpb.org/news/2023/07/05/5-things-hyundai-development)  ·  `page_001429`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group says its arrival will mean 8,100 new jobs at its site at Interstate 16 and Georgia 280 in Bryan County by the time the electric vehicle and battery plant are fully running.
- Official announcements for the development include at least $5.5 billion in construction spending and suppliers that plan to hire thousands more employees.
- The metaplant's staff count would put it among the biggest employers in the region, where only 11 private companies employ more than 1,000 people in Chatham, Bryan and Effingham counties according to Georgia Department of Labor’s 2022 data.
- Gulfstream is the largest employer in the area with roughly 11,500 workers around Savannah and is looking for more staff.
- The Savannah-Chatham Public School System has about 5,500 staff.
- Some 3,500 civilians work at Fort Stewart, while 640 civilians are employed at Hunter Army Air Field.
- Approximately 27,000 people work in the hospitality industry in Chatham, Bryan and Effingham counties, spread among some 1,600 relatively small businesses.
- Hyundai and the next-door battery plant will pay at least $58,105 on average, plus benefits, for each job.
- The average wage is required to qualify for all promised state and local grants, tax breaks and land, which amount to public funds worth about $228,000 per job.
- Hyundai suppliers can cut separate pay, investment and subsidy deals with local governments.
- Ecoplastic’s plant near Register needs to provide 456 jobs in eight years when fully functional making plastic parts for cars.
- Ecoplastic workers need to be paid at least the county’s average wage of $21.65 an hour as of the February 2023 agreement with Bulloch County.
- At 40 hours per week, that wage comes to about $43,300 per year for Ecoplastic workers.
- In return for building in Bulloch County, Ecoplastic received a $2.3 million discount on the purchase price of its site and a discount on county property taxes for 15 years.
- The Bulloch County tax discount does not apply to school and fire district taxes.
- Ecoplastic may also receive a $1.6 million state grant.
- Joon Georgia has a similar contract with Bulloch County and the state, promising an hourly rate matching the county average for 630 jobs it will create in its new facility.
- In return, Joon Georgia received a $3.1 million discount on land near I-16, a $2.7 million state grant and a 10-year county property tax break.
- The Joon Georgia county property tax break does not apply to school and fire rates.
- The Hyundai Metaplant sits on more than a mile of Black Creek, about three miles before the tributary joins the Ogeechee River.
- Hyundai and its suppliers are setting up near interstates and the waterways of the Ogeechee watershed.
- A new wastewater treatment plant for the site and surrounding areas will discharge into the Ogeechee River.
- The wastewater treatment plant across the interstate from Hyundai will treat the plant’s wastewater and north Bryan’s sewage before pouring it into the Ogeechee.
- Environmentalists at the Ogeechee Riverkeeper have started tracking water quality in both the creek and the river due to distrust of state pollution monitoring.
- Eight Hyundai suppliers will be spread out around Bryan, Bulloch, Chatham, Effingham and Liberty counties.
- None of the supplier factories are on public transport lines, requiring a car for access.
- Hyundai production is set to start in the first half of 2025.
- The Metaplant site sits on the south side of I-16, about 22 miles northwest of Savannah.
- Site clearance is already visible from satellite photos.
- Satellite photos show the build-out of industrial sites in the area since 2005.
- Daniel Defense and ORAFOL are located just across the interstate from the site.
- The team that wooed Hyundai and suppliers was made up of state and county leaders as well as the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority.
- Georgia competed against regional states for the development, meeting manufacturer expectations of being offered billions of dollars in subsidies.
- Georgia’s Department of Economic Development led its offer letter to Hyundai with the Best State for Business honorific from a niche business publication.
- The publication cites the area’s outstanding logistics, well-trained and educated workforce, low business costs, pro-business climate and high quality of life.
- Coastal Georgia laborers can expect to benefit quickly from tens of millions of dollars that the state is investing in job-training specific to the needs of the new employers.
