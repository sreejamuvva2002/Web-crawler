# Toyota Motor Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-08-08

**Source:** [Toyota Offering to Buy Back Electric Vehicles After Issuing Startling Warning to Stop Driving SUV Immediately – Long Island, NY](https://www.longisland-ny.com/2022/08/09/toyota-offering-to-buy-back-electric-vehicles-after-issuing-startling-warning-to-stop-driving-suv-immediately/)  ·  `page_000804`

## Facts

- In June, Toyota warned buyers of its bZ4X electric SUV not to drive their vehicles for fear the wheels could fall off.
- Toyota has not yet found a solution to the problem and is offering to buy back the SUV from its owners.
- Toyota stated it appreciates customer loyalty and is supporting them through the recall, but will offer to repurchase the bZ4X if customers do not want other options.
- Owners who keep their vehicles get free use of another Toyota vehicle until a fix is found.
- Toyota will pay $5,000 toward an owner’s car payments or as a partial refund for those keeping the vehicle.
- Toyota will extend the factory warranty on the bZ4X by the length of time before the owner gets their vehicle back.
- The offer includes free EV charging once owners get the vehicle back and covers gasoline costs for loaner vehicles.
- It was unclear how owners would handle dealer markups, as the vehicle's price starts at $43,215.
- In June, Toyota announced that all 2,700 of its new electric bZ4X SUVs were a danger to drivers, with only 260 delivered in the United States to date.
- Toyota stated that after low-mileage use, hub bolts on the wheel can loosen enough for the wheel to detach, potentially causing loss of control and crashes.
- The cause of the issue and driving patterns are under investigation, and no one should drive these vehicles until a remedy is performed.
- No remedy was available at the time of the June release.
- The bZ4X debuted in Japan in June.
- Toyota president Akio Toyoda said in December that the company planned to roll out 30 BEV models by 2030.
- David Leggett, automotive editor at GlobalData, noted Toyota has been under pressure to up its game in EVs and will be disappointed by the recall on its first mass-market electric cars.
- Anjani Trivedi of Bloomberg wrote that if traditional auto giants commit to this level of quality and safety, investors and regulators should increase scrutiny.
