# Designing Materials to Revolutionize and Engineer our Future (DMREF) Program Solicitation 23-530

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** research_and_development  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [DOE: CMEI eXCHANGE: Funding Opportunities](https://eere-exchange.energy.gov/Default.aspx?Search=dmref&SearchType=)  ·  `page_003558`

## Facts

- The announcement serves as a Teaming Partner List to help prospective applicants for NSF Program Solicitation 23-530 identify potential DOE National Laboratory partners within DOE-EERE, including the Energy Materials Network consortia.
- Applicants can select "Cross-Cutting" for the Area of Expertise if no specific option fits their needs.
- The Office of Science and Technology Policy (OSTP) Subcommittee on the Materials Genome Initiative (MGI) is a federal partnership between NSF and DOE.
- The MGI vision aims to enable discovery, development, manufacturing, and deployment of advanced materials at least twice as fast at a fraction of the cost compared to traditional methods.
- DOE's EERE is building a clean energy economy that encompasses energy efficiency, renewable energy, and sustainable transportation.
- EERE's primary interface with DMREF is through the Energy Materials Network (EMN), which accelerates solutions to materials challenges in the energy sector.
- Teams partnering with this option must agree to participate in technical review meetings, and funds may be budgeted for this purpose.
- "EERE" must be included as a Key Word in the Project Summary.
- DMREF is the principal NSF program responsive to the MGI.
- Over its inaugural decade, the MGI has driven a transformational paradigm shift in materials research philosophy.
- DMREF supports the 2021 MGI Strategic Plan goals: unifying the materials innovation infrastructure; harnessing the power of materials data; and educating, training, and connecting a world-class materials R&D workforce.
- The DMREF solicitation is open to Institutions of Higher Education.
- This solicitation enables DMREF teams to collaborate with MGI-related efforts to bolster scientific/engineering aspects of the iterative feedback loop for accelerated materials research, facilitate translation of fundamental research toward application, and provide education, training, and workforce development opportunities.
- Federal partners must not be listed on the Cover Page of proposals.
- Letters of Collaboration are not required from DMREF's federal partners, but the collaborating PI must be identified in the Project Description to explain how collaboration benefits both parties.
- Proposals collaborating with personnel from DMREF's federal partners like EERE may budget funds for student support or travel to enable these activities.
- No funds may be budgeted or sub-awarded to federal partners, as these personnel will participate as unfunded collaborators.
- Proposals partnering with EERE may budget funds specifically for travel to technical review meetings.
- EERE and DMREF share common interests through the EMN in developing materials, processes, and advanced manufacturing technologies for the energy sector.
- Specific topics of interest to both EERE and DMREF include catalysis; materials for extreme environments; accelerated materials design and discovery by artificial intelligence and autonomous experimentation; multiscale modeling; photovoltaic materials; and lightweight and durable materials.
- The Teaming Partner List allows EERE partners, including the EMN, to network with prospective DMREF applicants.
- Proposals are due March 13, 2023, closing by 5 PM submitter's local time.
