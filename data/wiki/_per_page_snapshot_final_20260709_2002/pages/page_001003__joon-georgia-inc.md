# Joon Georgia Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2022-11-12

**Source:** [Hyundai's first supplier has arrived: Joon Georgia Inc. to build facility in Bulloch County - AOL](https://www.aol.com/news/hyundais-first-supplier-arrived-joon-110016296.html)  ·  `page_001003`

## Facts

- Auto parts maker Joon Georgia Inc. announced they will build a new manufacturing facility in Bulloch County.
- The project will create more than 600 jobs.
- The company is investing $317 million into the county.
- Joon Georgia Inc. is the first supplier for Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America in Bryan County.
- The facility will manufacture the frame for the vehicles at the Hyundai EV plant in Black Creek.
- Joon Georgia Inc. is part of Ajin USA, who is already a supplier for several Hyundai Motor Group facilities.
- Gov. Brian Kemp stated that less than two weeks after the groundbreaking of Hyundai Motor Group’s innovative facility, they are already seeing regional impact.
- Gov. Brian Kemp noted Joon Georgia will be the first of many companies drawn to Georgia to support the Metaplant, creating jobs and opportunity for generations of hardworking Georgians.
- The facility will be located seven miles outside Statesboro in the Bruce Yawn Commerce Park.
- The unit is currently planned for 866,000 square feet.
- The square footage could change as the facility is still in the design phase.
- Savannah Joint Development Authority President and CEO Trip Tollison said this announcement marks the first of many supplier announcements to come as Hyundai Motor Group moves into the market.
- The Savannah JDA consists of Bryan, Bulloch, Chatham and Effingham counties.
- Benjy Thompson is the CEO of the Bulloch County Development Authority.
- Thompson described the site as the city’s “industrial jewel” and noted it took years to develop.
- Joon Georgia Inc. reached out to Thompson in June.
- Thompson stated they put resources on the property at the interstate to make it marketable.
- Thompson mentioned spending a significant amount of money making their entire site pad ready.
- Thompson expects more suppliers to build facilities in Bulloch County but could not provide further details.
- The hiring process is expected to begin mid-2023.
- Operations at the facility are expected to start in 2024.
- Thompson noted their workforce and proximity away from the plant worked in their favor.
- Thompson stated the cost of labor in Bulloch County is probably a little less than the Savannah metro area.
- Thompson explained they can draw the workforce to the west, whereas to the east of Savannah is the ocean.
- Ajin USA’s CEO Jung Ho Sea said they are eager to partner with the county and state and are committed to being the “best employer” for the community.
- Sea stated they are excited to be one of the first Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America automotive suppliers to build an automotive parts manufacturing facility in Bulloch County and for their first plant in Georgia.
- Sea expressed a desire to be the best corporate citizen and give back to the community of Bulloch County and the city of Statesboro.
