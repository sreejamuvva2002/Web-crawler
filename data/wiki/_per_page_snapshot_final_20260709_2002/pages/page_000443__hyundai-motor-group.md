# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- Hyundai is constructing facilities in Savannah, Georgia, dedicated to the production of electric vehicles (EVs) and batteries.
- The new facility will be Hyundai’s first EV-only plant in the United States.
- Hyundai anticipates investing $5.5 billion in the new facility.
- Suppliers will contribute an additional $1 billion to the project.
- The facility is located on a 2,923-acre site.
- Construction of the facility began in early 2023.
- Hyundai anticipates that production will begin on the site in the first half of 2025.
- The EV facility is expected to produce 300,000 vehicles per year.
- The project will create approximately 8,100 new jobs.
- Hyundai does not identify which specific EV models will be produced at the factory.
- Hyundai stated that a “broad range” of vehicles will be produced on Georgia’s assembly lines.
- Details about the battery-building plant are limited to it “will be built through a strategic collaboration.”
- Hyundai's current electric vehicle lineup includes the Kona Electric, Ioniq 5, and the Nexo powered by a hydrogen fuel cell.
- Hyundai-owned Kia offers the all-electric EV6 and Niro.
- Hyundai’s luxury Genesis brand offers the GV60, GV70, and GV80 EVs.
- In addition to the new Georgia facilities, Hyundai declared it will invest more than $10 billion in the United States by 2025.
- The broader U.S. investment will explore diverse technologies such as autonomous driving, robotics, artificial intelligence, and advanced air mobility.
- The $5.5 billion set out for the new Georgia EV factory is part of that reserve.
- The state of Georgia signed a deal with Rivian earlier this month offering $1.5 billion in tax breaks to entice the company to relocate to the state.
- Rivian's planned factories will employ 7,500 people by 2028 and generate 400,000 EVs each year.
- Toyota is constructing a $1.29 billion battery factory in North Carolina.
- General Motors intends to bring its third EV battery manufacturing plant to Michigan.
- Stellantis, the parent company of Jeep, Dodge, and Chrysler, is planning to develop an EV manufacturing plant in the United States, though the location has yet to be determined.
- Ford and SK Innovation are bringing new EV-focused plants to Tennessee and Kentucky.
- Tesla already has numerous factories in the United States dedicated to making batteries and EVs.
- Tesla recently established another factory in Austin, Texas.
