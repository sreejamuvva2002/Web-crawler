# Rivian

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-16

**Source:** [Georgia Today: New Rivian electric vehicle plant; Kemp responds to Hyundai raid; Vaccine access | Georgia Public Broadcasting](https://www.gpb.org/news/2025/09/16/georgia-today-new-rivian-electric-vehicle-plant-kemp-responds-hyundai-raid-vaccine)  ·  `page_018784`

## Facts

- State leaders celebrated the groundbreaking of a new Rivian electric vehicle factory east of Atlanta on Tuesday, September 16th.
- The facility will be built on a 2,000-acre site.
- When completed, the facility will employ 7,500 people to build electric vehicles.
- The project represents a $5 billion investment.
- The $5 billion project was originally announced in December 2021.
- At the time of its announcement, it was the largest economic investment in Georgia state history.
- Speakers at the groundbreaking ceremony included Gov. Brian Kemp, Morgan County officials, and Rivian founder R.J. Scringe.
- Rivian is actively working to find local sources for car parts to keep prices down.
- Rivian founder R.J. Scringe stated they have been working closely with the state to bring suppliers to Georgia or work with new suppliers based in the state, calling it a really big part of the effort.
- The first vehicles built at this factory are expected to be available in 2028.
- Gov. Brian Kemp stated that an immigration raid resulting in the arrest of around 500 people at a battery plant in Southeast Georgia will not deter foreign investors from locating companies in the state.
- Kemp has had conversations with the White House regarding the need for visas for immigrant workers and expressed hope for a solution.
- Many workers taken into ICE custody during the raid were South Korean, leading to concern from the South Korean government.
- ICE stated the arrested workers came to the U.S. illegally or overstayed their visas, while lawyers for some workers claim they were lawfully present.
