# Hitachi Astemo Americas, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2021-07-12

**Source:** [Hitachi Astemo Americas And Light Collaborate On Development Of Adas Technology: Hitachi Global](https://www.hitachi.com/en-us/press/hitachi-astemo-americas-and-light-collaborate-on-development-of-adas-technology/)  ·  `page_008487`

## Facts

- Hitachi Astemo Americas, Inc. and Light have begun “proof of concept” development of a forward-facing perception system for use with advanced driver assistance systems (ADAS).
- The two companies will begin field testing the new multi-view, camera-based sensing technology later in July 2021.
- Light’s Clarity™ depth perception platform integrates seamlessly into Hitachi Astemo’s ADAS and engine control unit (ECU) applications to work directly with other ADAS systems.
- John Nunneley serves as senior vice president of Design Engineering at Hitachi Astemo Americas, Inc.
- Light is based in Santa Clara, California.
- Light’s Clarity platform uses cameras to detect 3D structures on the road from 10 centimeters to 1,000 meters away.
- The Clarity platform offers three times the range and 20 times the detail of best-in-class LiDAR sensors.
- The technology enables safer automatic emergency braking, evasive steering control for a wider variety of objects, adaptive suspension adjustment for road hazards such as potholes, and smoother adaptive cruise-control and lane-keeping systems.
- Dave Grannan is co-founder and CEO of Light.
- Hitachi Astemo Americas, Inc. manufactures and markets engine management, electric powertrain, and integrated vehicle control systems for major automotive manufacturers worldwide.
- Hitachi Astemo adheres to ISO/TS:16949:2009 industry standards in its manufacturing and marketing operations.
- Light combines computational imaging breakthroughs with multi-camera calibration to provide accurate real-time depth perception at near and far distances.
