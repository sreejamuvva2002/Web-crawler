# Hyundai Motor Group / Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-11-01

**Source:** [Pucks, Pluck, and EV Power - Atlanta Magazine](https://www.atlantamagazine.com/article/pucks-pluck-and-ev-power/)  ·  `page_001533`

## Facts

- Hyundai celebrated the groundbreaking for its 3,000-acre, $5.54 billion, 8,100-job EV manufacturing complex in Bryan County on Oct. 25.
- The ceremony eased fears that a tiff over federal tax credits would slam the brakes on the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America project.
- South Korea’s ambassador to the United States used the event to lobby for Congress to delay a domestic assembly requirement for the $7,500 credit.
- The Savannah-area plant is due to go live in 2025.
- Georgia Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson said it already is producing “opportunities in the EV revolution for Georgians.”
- On Oct. 19, a $178.2 million grant from the U.S. Energy Department was awarded for a proposed Augusta plant.
- Solvay Specialty Polymers, a European conglomerate with an EV-related innovation center in Alpharetta, was one of 21 winners of grants totaling $2.8 billion to support domestic EV battery production.
- Solvay would have to match the grant and has yet to announce that it will move forward.
- The Augusta plant would employ 100 people to produce polyvinylidene fluoride, known as PVDF, a coating material that extends battery life and driving range.
- Solvay and Boston-based Orbia, which supplies the raw materials needed to produce PVDF, announced a joint venture Nov. 3 to invest about $670 million on top of the federal grant to build the Augusta plant.
- Operations at the Augusta plant should begin in 2026.
- The Energy Department says the plant could annually produce enough PVDF for more than 5 million batteries.
- For perspective, the Hyundai Metaplant has a target of 300,000 vehicles a year.
- Atlanta-based Delta Air Lines is investing $60 million in California’s Joby Aviation so premium customers can fly from home to the airport as well as between airports.
- Joby’s zero-emission electric aircraft takes off and lands vertically, can carry four passengers up to 150 miles at 200 mph.
- Delta’s initial investment, announced Oct. 11, could grow to $200 million and buys five years of U.S. and British partnership exclusivity from the time the joint service starts.
- The plan is to begin in New York and Los Angeles at a date uncertain, although Joby aims to launch an independent air taxi service as early as 2024.
- The Russell Innovation Center for Entrepreneurs in Atlanta is partnering with Sandy Springs–based UPS to provide data, services, and market access to Black-owned small businesses.
- The UPS Logistics Launchpad opened at RICE on Oct. 11, providing a UPS Store and warehouse and fulfillment services run by UPS-owned Ware2Go.
- UPS supply chain and logistics experts are on site at the launchpad.
- RICE CEO Jay Bailey said in a press release that Atlanta’s corporations can have powerful inspiration on entrepreneurs.
