# Procter & Gamble Distributing, LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2022-05-13

**Source:** [Procter & Gamble to Create 350 New Jobs with $205 Million New Facility | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-releases/2022/procter-gamble-create-350-new-jobs-205-million-new-facility-butts-county)  ·  `page_004654`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Procter & Gamble Distributing, LLC is planning to invest $205 million in a new distribution facility in Jackson, Georgia.
- The new facility will create 350 new jobs in Butts County.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated his administration has been laser-focused on creating jobs and opportunities in rural Georgia.
- Procter & Gamble Company is headquartered in Cincinnati, Ohio, and specializes in personal healthcare and hygiene products.
- P&G currently operates a manufacturing facility in Albany and a distribution center outside of Atlanta.
- Ignacio Arranz, Senior Vice President of P&G Physical Distribution Innovation, stated the company is expanding its Georgia footprint to better meet product demand.
- The new distribution center will be located at 950 Logistics Parkway in Jackson, Georgia.
- The state-of-the-art, automated facility will exceed 1 million square feet upon completion.
- Butts County Board of Commissioners’ Chair Joe Brown welcomed P&G as a corporate citizen to the county.
- Metro Atlanta Chamber President and CEO Katie Kirkpatrick noted the announcement bolsters Georgia’s reputation and leadership in the supply chain sector.
- Griff Lynch, Executive Director of the Georgia Ports Authority, highlighted Georgia’s logistics network ability to reach major domestic and global markets efficiently.
- Bob Kosek, Director of Industrial Projects, represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s Global Commerce team on the project.
- The project was executed in partnership with the Development Authority of Butts County, Metro Atlanta Chamber, Georgia Ports Authority, and Georgia Power.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated Georgia’s logistics infrastructure includes highways connected to 80% of the U.S. market by less than two days of driving.
- The announcement was made on May 13, 2022, in Atlanta.
