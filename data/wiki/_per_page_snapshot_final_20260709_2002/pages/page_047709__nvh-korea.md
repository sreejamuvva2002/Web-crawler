# NVH Korea

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Atlanta Trend - Connecting People. Processing News](https://atlantatrend.org/post/news/552)  ·  `page_047709`

## Facts

- Automotive parts manufacturer NVH Korea will establish a new facility in Locust Grove.
- The project will create over 160 new jobs.
- The project will bring $72 million in investment to Henry County.
- This is the second economic development project Governor Kemp has announced this week, alone.
- Governor Kemp is continuing to promote Georgia to international job creators through a packed agenda while abroad.
- NVH Korea chose the State of Georgia as its company’s first EV battery component manufacturing site.
- Cooperation between state agencies and NVH Korea staff helped guide them throughout this project.
- Ja Kyum Koo is Chairman and CEO of NVH Korea.
- This is the first step of NVH Korea’s effort to contribute to the electrification of the mobility industry.
- NVH Korea is an automotive parts manufacturer based in the City of Ulsan.
- NVH Korea was established in 1984.
- The company specializes in automotive noise, vibration, and heat-control parts.
- NVH Korea currently produces these parts in Korea, Europe, and India.
- The company operates a manufacturing facility that produces floor mats, cargo mats, and cargo trays in Georgia.
- The new facility will supply battery parts for electric vehicles (EVs) in the United States.
- The new facility will supply parts to manufacturers such as Hyundai Mobis, Kia Georgia, and Hyundai Motor Group.
- NVH Korea’s new facility will be located at Gardner Logistics Park on Colvin Drive in Locust Grove.
- The facility will produce EV battery system components that protect, connect, and sense the performance of battery cells.
- Operations are expected to begin in the second quarter of 2024.
- Mayor Robert Price is the Mayor of the City of Locust Grove.
- Carlotta Harrell is Chair of the Henry County Board of Commissioners.
- Project Manager Jacob Lee represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The project was in partnership with the Henry County Development Authority, Georgia EMC, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that an economic transformation is happening across Georgia as manufacturers create jobs of the future in line with cutting-edge technologies and industry trends.
- GDEcD welcomes innovators establishing their first U.S. or e-mobility manufacturing presence, like NVH Korea.
- For over a century, Georgia has fostered healthy industry practices, encouraged collaboration and innovation, and positioned the state as a leader in developing and harnessing emerging technologies for the evolving automotive and mobility industry.
- From July 1 through December 31, 2022, the automotive industry was the second-largest source of new jobs and investment.
- Georgia has pursued job creation along the entire EV supply chain as the market continues to grow.
- Since 2020, Georgia's pursuit of EV supply chain job creation has resulted in more than $22.7 billion in investments and over 28,400 jobs created.
