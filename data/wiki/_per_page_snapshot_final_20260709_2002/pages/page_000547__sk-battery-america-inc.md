# SK Battery America Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-03-06

**Source:** [SK Battery America lays off nearly 1,000 workers at Commerce plant | FOX 5 Atlanta](https://www.fox5atlanta.com/news/sk-battery-america-lays-off-nearly-1000-workers-commerce-plant)  ·  `page_000547`

## Facts

- SK Battery America Inc. announced the layoffs of 958 employees at its Commerce, Georgia plant.
- The company cited shifts in automakers’ electrification plans and uncertain consumer demand for electric vehicles (EVs) as reasons for the layoffs.
- A Worker Adjustment and Retraining Notification (WARN) notice was filed by human resources chief Chuck Moore.
- The final day of work for these employees was today.
- Impacted workers will continue to be paid through May 6.
- SK opened the $2.6 billion battery facility in Commerce in January 2022.
- The plant notably supplied batteries for the Ford F-150 Lightning electric pickup truck.
- Ford announced plans to cancel the fully electric version of the truck this past December.
- The layoffs follow a significant shift in federal policy under the Trump administration, which has steered support away from electrification toward laxer automotive emissions policies and oil/gas industries.
- Congress has eliminated consumer tax credits of up to $7,500 for the purchase of new or used EVs.
- The administration announced plans to weaken fuel economy and greenhouse gas emissions rules, which essentially eliminates federal incentives for auto companies to prioritize cleaner vehicle fleets.
- SK Battery America stated it remains committed to Georgia and its U.S. operations, framing the workforce reduction as a response to current market conditions aligned with long-term success.
- SK Battery America is pursuing future customers in the Battery Energy Storage System arena.
- Sen. Jon Ossoff blamed the current White House administration for the layoffs, stating that Trump’s war on electric vehicles is hurting Georgia’s economy and causing Georgians to lose jobs.
- Sen. Raphael Warnock stated that the administration slashed clean energy investments that brought good-paying manufacturing jobs to Georgia and made manufacturing in Georgia more expensive through harmful tariff taxes.
