# Hyundai TRANSYS

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2020-12

**Source:** [Hyundai TRANSYS to Invest More Than $240 Million in West Point Expansion, Create 678 Jobs - Georgia_CEO](https://goldenislesceo.com/news/2020/12/hyundai-transys-invest-more-240-million-west-point-expansion-create-678-jobs/)  ·  `page_009523`

## Facts

- Hyundai TRANSYS will invest more than $240 million in expanding its operations in Georgia.
- The investment will create 678 full-time long-term positions at a new manufacturing facility in the West Point area.
- The brand-new facility will be a 620,000-square-foot plant located near the company’s current location on the Kia campus.
- Hyundai TRANSYS is the only automobile manufacturing company in the world that specializes in transmissions.
- The new West Point plant will produce eight-speed transmissions for a global automotive leader.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced the investment, citing Georgia's business climate, robust logistics network, and top-ranked workforce training programs as key factors attracting and retaining global companies.
- Sangkil Jung, President of Hyundai TRANSYS Georgia Powertrain, stated that trust built through years of capital investment, employment, and training support, along with unmatched opportunities, made Georgia a natural choice for the expansion.
- The company plans to create 165 supplementary construction handling and inspection jobs for the project.
- This expansion will bring Hyundai TRANSYS’ total employment in Georgia to 1,741 jobs.
- Mayor Steve Tramell of West Point expressed support for the long-term investment and new jobs, thanking the West Point Development Authority, State of Georgia, and Troup County for their contributions.
- Patrick Crews, Chairman of Troup County, noted that the significant capital investment will strengthen the local economy and create economic opportunities for residents.
- Senior Project Manager Amanda Fields represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce division on the project in partnership with the West Point Development Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson emphasized the state's commitment to supporting long-term growth for investing companies and thanked Governor Kemp and the General Assembly for their continued support of business recruitment efforts.
- In 2020 alone, the State of Georgia facilitated more than $1 billion in investment and more than 2,000 jobs from South Korean companies.
- Georgia is home to more than 200 automotive-related facilities and remains a national leader in advanced manufacturing.
- Georgia has outpaced the United States in 10-year GDP growth in the manufacture of machinery, electrical equipment and components, and fabricated metals.
- Individuals interested in career opportunities with Hyundai TRANSYS are encouraged to check the company’s website for updated information.
