# Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia, Inc.

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2010-02-26

**Source:** [KIA CELEBRATES GRAND OPENING OF $1 BILLION STATE-OF-THE-ART AUTOMOBILE MANUFACTURING PLANT IN GEORGIA](https://www.kiamedia.com/us/en/media/pressreleases/3682/kia-celebrates-grand-opening-of-1-billion-state-of-the-art-automobile-manufacturing-plant-in-georgia)  ·  `page_010084`

## Facts

- Officials with Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia (KMMG), Inc. celebrated the official grand opening of Kia’s first-ever U.S. automobile manufacturing plant in West Point, Georgia on Feb. 26, 2010.
- The plant is capable of producing 300,000 cars annually for the North American and global markets.
- The ceremony was attended by approximately 500 VIPs from Korea and the U.S., including Chairman of the Hyundai-Kia Automotive Group Mong-Koo Chung; Group President and CEO of KMMG and Kia Motors America (KMA) Byung-Mo Ahn; Georgia Governor Sonny Perdue; and other high-ranking Korean government and State of Georgia officials.
- Group President and CEO of KMMG and Kia Motors America (KMA) Byung-Mo Ahn stated that Kia is proud to call West Point, Georgia home and looks forward to many years of growing together.
- Continued diligent work has helped elevate Kia to one of the fastest growing automobile brands around the globe, according to Byung-Mo Ahn.
- The successful launch of the 2011 Kia Sorento CUV and establishment of Kia’s first manufacturing plant in the United States position the Kia brand for further growth and to become a worldwide leader in quality and innovation, according to Byung-Mo Ahn.
- The project represents a $1 billion investment by Kia Motors Corporation.
- The sprawling, new automobile manufacturing plant is 2.2 million square feet and situated on more than 2,200 acres of land between Exit 2 and Exit 6 along Interstate 85 in West Point.
- KMMG consists of four main shops: stamping, welding, paint and assembly.
- The site also includes a transmission shop, module shop and a two-mile test track where every vehicle produced by KMMG will be thoroughly tested.
- KMMG’s general assembly area is outfitted with more than a half mile of height-adjustable conveyors to achieve the most favorable installation position for each team member and reduce physical fatigue.
- The general assembly area is outfitted with wood flooring, which provides a more comfortable and forgiving work environment for team members.
- Georgia Governor Sonny Perdue noted that today’s event meant the continuation of a mutually beneficial partnership between the State of Georgia and Kia that will mean many great things for the State in years to come.
- Kia Motors has brought thousands of new jobs to the West Point area and is truly delivering as a tremendous corporate citizen, according to Governor Sonny Perdue.
- The State of Georgia pledges to continue providing specialized training through the Kia Georgia Training Center that will guarantee a quality workforce and the future success of this plant in Georgia, according to Governor Sonny Perdue.
- The massive Kia automobile manufacturing plant and its supplier companies have already provided thousands of jobs.
- KMMG currently employs more than 1,200 team members and is expected to nearly double that number by the end of the year (relative to Feb. 26, 2010).
- Onsite and nearby suppliers will create up to 7,500 additional jobs in the region.
- Randy Jackson, director of human resources and administration at KMMG, said he was glad that KMMG could help give back to the local community through job creation, community development and tourism.
- The Grand Opening marks a new milestone for Kia, according to Randy Jackson.
- Beginning this spring (relative to Feb. 26, 2010), KMMG will open its doors to the community to show firsthand the quality and craftsmanship that is built into every Kia vehicle, according to Randy Jackson.
- KMMG hopes every Kia owner, and anyone thinking about purchasing a Kia, will visit them, according to Randy Jackson.
- Mass production of the all-new 2011 Kia Sorento CUV began on Nov. 16, 2009 with the product officially going on sale in the U.S. in January of this year (2010).
- The 2011 Sorento sold 7,398 units in its first month, making it Kia’s best-selling vehicle in January (2010).
- The outstanding early performance of the first vehicle produced from the West Point, Georgia plant is a significant achievement for KMMG, KMA and the Kia brand as a whole, according to Byung-Mo Ahn.
- As the company grows, it will continue to expand its line of vehicles here and abroad while staying true to the Kia brand tenets of value, quality, safety and technology, according to Byung-Mo Ahn.
- KMMG will play a prominent role in achieving these goals, according to Byung-Mo Ahn.
- Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia, Inc. (KMMG) is the first manufacturing site in North America for Kia Motors Corporation based in Seoul, Korea.
- At full capacity, the plant will have the ability to build 300,000 vehicles annually from U.S. and globally sourced parts.
