# FREYR Battery, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Energy, Automobile, EV, Renewable News | FREYR Battery Reports Fourth Quarter and Full Year 2023 Results](https://oilandgaspress.com/freyr-battery-reports-fourth-quarter-and-full-year-2023-results/)  ·  `page_055012`

## Facts

- FREYR Battery, Inc. is a developer of clean, next-generation battery cell production capacity.
- The company is commencing development of the Giga America battery manufacturing project in Coweta County, Georgia, U.S.
- Over the initial phase, Giga America is expected to generate hundreds of jobs in Coweta County, GA and surrounding areas.
- FREYR’s Asset Mo team at the Customer Qualification Plant (“CQP”) conducted successful automated anode casting trials with active anode slurry in the second half of February 2024.
- Cathode casting trials were successfully completed earlier in February 2024 at the CQP.
- The remaining major milestone before expected start of automated production at the CQP is integration of casting webs across cathode, anode, and merge units using the Multi-Carrier System (“MCS”).
- FREYR expects to commence production of functional battery cells for customer samples using full automation of the CQP in H1 2024.
- CQP progress is being used to support a U.S. Department of Energy Title 17 application.
- FREYR is positioning itself to qualify for a conditional commitment from the DOE Loan Programs Office by year-end 2024.
- The DOE Title 17 application is tied to development of Giga America using the 24M Technologies (“24M”) U.S.-based SemiSolidTM platform.
- FREYR launched the FREYR 2.0 growth initiative, encompassing pursuit of five major opportunities tied to distinct projects using either the SemiSolid platform or conventional technology.
- The FREYR 2.0 initiative targets more than 100 GWh of production capacity in the U.S. and Europe.
- Prospective end markets for the 2.0 initiative include Energy Storage Systems (“ESS”) and passenger EV applications, with possibility to produce both LFP and NMC cells.
- The 2.0 initiative includes separate potential inorganic opportunities to accelerate path to market and first revenues.
- Birger Steen serves as FREYR’s Chief Executive Officer.
- On February 7th, 2024, FREYR published an operations update indicating automatic casting of electrodes with active electrolyte slurry in a dry room environment at the CQP in Mo i Rana, Norway.
- Year-end 2023 cash balance was $275.7 million in cash, restricted cash and equivalents.
- FREYR has a two-year cash runway before any new financings associated with the DOE Title 17 process and/or prospective project-level equity raise for Giga America.
- 2024 spending will focus on achieving production using full automation at CQP with 24M technology in H1 2024, delivering testable cells to customers, continued development of Giga America including costs for conditional credit approval in 2024 from DOE Title 17 loan program, and pursuing a conventional technology agreement.
- On January 2nd, 2024, FREYR announced completion of redomicile from Luxembourg to the United States following a shareholder vote on December 15, 2023.
- The Board believes redomiciling will enhance shareholder value by simplifying corporate structure and reporting requirements, expanding eligibility for equity indexes, aligning with NYSE listing standards and SEC governance requirements, and positioning FREYR to better respond to U.S. incentive programs for battery manufacturers.
- As of publication, FREYR completed handovers of 363 of 388 (94%) discrete production line equipment commissioning and testing packages at the CQP.
- The remaining 6% of commissioning packages will be finalized upon final handover of Casting & Unit Cell Assembly equipment later this year, expected after commencement of functional cell production.
- Customer validation of the SemiSolid production process at giga scale and acceptance of product performance are key precursors to anticipated customer offtake conversions and unlocking potential project-level equity and debt financing for Giga America.
- FREYR targets completion of the DOE Title 17 application process and securing a conditional commitment from the Loan Programs Office before year-end 2024.
- FREYR targets formalization and announcement of an agreement with a conventional battery technology solutions provider potentially in H1 2024.
- FREYR seeks to serve primary markets of ESS and commercial mobility, and maintains ambition to serve the passenger electric vehicles market.
- Investor contact is Jeffrey Spittel, Head of Investor Relations.
- Media contact is Amy Jaick, Global Head of Communications.
