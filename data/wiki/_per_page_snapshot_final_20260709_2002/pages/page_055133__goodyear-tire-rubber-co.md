# Goodyear Tire & Rubber Co.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2025-03-10

**Source:** [Goodyear Tire's transformation plan is underway — in the sky and on the ground](https://www.nbcnews.com/business/autos/goodyear-tires-transformation-plan-underway-sky-ground-rcna195663)  ·  `page_055133`

## Facts

- Goodyear Tire & Rubber Co. is executing a two-year transformation plan called "Goodyear Forward" that ends in December.
- The plan aims to double operating income margin to 10%.
- The plan includes enacting top-line and cost reductions of $1.5 billion.
- The plan targets bringing in gross proceeds of $2 billion from business asset sales.
- The plan reduces the company's debt load by $1.5 billion, net of approximately $1.1 billion for restructuring.
- Goodyear is investing in and deploying artificial intelligence technologies and 3D-printing for components such as tread teeth.
- The company uses simulation technology to speed the development and production of its products.
- CEO Mark Stewart stated that Goodyear is ahead of schedule for its benchmarks, including upping cost cuts by $200 million.
- Goodyear has reported five consecutive quarters of margin growth and its best retail performance in more than 20 years.
- Shares of Goodyear received a 17% boost after the company reported its 2024 and fourth-quarter results.
- Elliott Investment Management revealed a stake in the company in 2023 and retains a roughly 9% stake, adding three directors to its board.
- Goodyear employs about 68,000 people globally.
- The company manufactures its products in 53 facilities across multiple countries, with major operations in North and South America, Asia-Pacific, and Europe.
- Goodyear operates eight tire plants in the U.S., two plants in Canada, two in Mexico, and one plant each in Brazil, Chile, Colombia, and Peru.
- Manufacturing operations in the Americas represented roughly half of Goodyear's tire sales in 2024.
- Goodyear is investing an unspecified amount into a facility in Oklahoma to expand production by 10 million units annually and modernize the plant.
- Goodyear's Asia-Pacific segment operating income increased by 37% last year to $277 million, with an operating margin of 11.4%.
- Low-cost imported tires from Southeast Asia grew 11% in the U.S. last year, undercutting Goodyear ahead of potential tariffs.
- Goodyear's largest global competitors are France-based Michelin, Japan-based Bridgestone Corp., and Germany-based Continental.
- The company is expanding automation to its U.S. facilities, contrasting with traditional processes at its historic Akron headquarters.
- Goodyear invested millions in a simulation machine at its headquarters to cut R&D costs and time while improving product profits.
- The simulation facility features vehicle cockpits for a hatchback and pickup truck surrounded by 270 degrees of screens for virtual tire testing.
- Goodyear has worked with major automakers on virtual development, including Ferrari.
- Goodyear is expanding its top-tier products to include massive tires for off-road vehicles like the Jeep Wrangler, Ford Bronco, Tesla Cybertruck, and large SUVs with 22-inch or 24-inch wheels like the Cadillac Escalade.
- CEO Mark Stewart plans to get aggressive about growth in retail and service, as well as high-end tires.
- Goodyear inked a high-profile business deal for its first launch in decades on a Ferrari sports car.
- The company is evaluating the potential impact of President Donald Trump's tariffs on Canada and Mexico for automakers and suppliers on its North American operations.
- CFO Christina Zamarro stated during the quarterly earnings call that low-end imports outperformed the U.S. industry last year and grew 11%.
- Argus analyst Bill Selesky upgraded Goodyear to buy in a Feb. 14 investor note, citing fourth quarter 2024 results as an inflection point.
- Nine analysts compiled by FactSet rate Goodyear's stock as overweight with a target price of $11.47 a share.
