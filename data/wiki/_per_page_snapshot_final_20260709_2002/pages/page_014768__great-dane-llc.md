# Great Dane LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-12

**Source:** [Savannah's Great Dane wins Chinese tariff battle - The Current](https://thecurrentga.org/2026/06/12/savannahs-great-dane-wins-chinese-tariff-battle/)  ·  `page_014768`

## Facts

- U.S. customs officials are poised to levy new duties on semitruck trailers made in China and Mexico after a yearlong legal battle fought by Savannah’s Great Dane LLC and two other American manufacturers.
- The decision marks a small victory for the American employers against an onslaught of cheaper, less safe Chinese trailers that are heavily subsidized by Beijing.
- Chris Hammond serves as executive vice president of Great Dane and is the great-great grandson of the 125-year-old company’s founder.
- Great Dane originated as a Savannah-based factory that helped the U.S. Army transport heavy equipment between the U.S. mainland and European battlefields in World War II.
- Great Dane has grown into one of the largest suppliers of refrigerated trailers in the nation.
- The company operates 10 manufacturing facilities across the country, including a factory in Statesboro.
- Great Dane supplies semitrailers to retail companies such as Walmart, Home Depot and Amazon with the semitrailers they need to move products around the country.
- Great Dane, along with Wabash National Corp. and Stoughton Trailers LLC, presented data to the U.S. International Trade Commission regarding market conditions.
- The three companies claim recent market headwinds are due to an unfair playing field rather than superior products or marketing by competitors.
- The companies estimate that about 40% of the U.S. market for van trailers is supplied by China, Mexico and Canada combined.
- Import data suggests foreign suppliers, mainly Chinese, boosted their sales in America from about 48,000 van trailers annually between 2015-2017 to 80,600 between 2022 and 2024.
- Hammond attributes this competition to loopholes in trade law and a lack of enforcement by U.S. officials against state subsidies and exploitative wage practices by Chinese rivals.
- According to Hammond, this competition has caused many other American trailer manufacturers out of business.
- Hammond stated that Great Dane makes the safest and best products and is doing its level best to support good jobs, good manufacturing jobs, for Georgians.
- Hammond emphasized that the company is simply asking for a level playing field.
- The American Trailer Manufacturers Coalition, the lobbying organization for these three companies, petitioned the U.S. government to support U.S. manufacturers in two ways.
- One petition goal was imposing countervailing duties on foreign rivals to close the price gap and offset foreign government subsidies or unfair wage competition.
- The ITC determined that the U.S. trailer manufacturers have been materially injured by imports.
- The Commerce Department instructed customs authorities to add duties ranging from 82.3% to 128.7% for trailers from China and 1.9% to 1.95% for trailers from Mexico.
- A second Commerce decision set additional antidumping duties on specific Chinese companies intended to offset the amount by which a product is sold at less than fair value in America.
- Those antidumping duties were set at 130% in a preliminary decision that is expected to be finalized later this summer.
- Hammond said U.S. government action is a boon for his company and will enable Great Dane to hire more Americans and expand its output.
- Hammond noted the decisions are important for national security considerations, as a thriving U.S. trucking trailer industry is necessary for America’s economy.
- Hammond asserted that it is the job of the government to help businesses that have critical national security implications.
