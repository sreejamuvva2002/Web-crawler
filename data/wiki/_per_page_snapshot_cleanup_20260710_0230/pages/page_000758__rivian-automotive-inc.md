# Rivian Automotive, Inc.

## Facts

- Rivian Automotive, Inc. is an American electric vehicle manufacturer and automotive technology company founded in 2009.
- It produces an electric sport utility vehicle (SUV) and a pickup truck on a skateboard platform that can support future vehicles and be adopted by other companies.
- The company produces an electric delivery van, the Rivian EDV, which is sold to Amazon for deliveries.
- Rivian started deliveries of its R1T pickup truck in late 2021.
- The company planned to build an exclusive charging network in the United States and Canada by the end of 2023.
- Rivian is based in Irvine, California, with its manufacturing plant in Normal, Illinois, and other facilities in Palo Alto, California; Carson, California; Plymouth, Michigan; Burnaby, British Columbia; Wittmann, Arizona; Woking, England; and Belgrade, Serbia.
- Rivian has plans to build another US$5 billion factory in Social Circle, Georgia.
- The company raised over US$13.5 billion in financing following its initial public offering in November 2021.
- The company was founded in Rockledge, Florida in 2009 as Mainstream Motors by RJ Scaringe.
- After being renamed Avera Automotive or Avera Motors, the company became Rivian Automotive in 2011.
- Rivian's first car model was intended to be a sports car dubbed the R1, which was shelved in late 2011.
- In 2015, Rivian received a large investment and opened research facilities in Michigan and the San Francisco Bay Area.
- Shortly after 2015, Rivian began working exclusively on electric autonomous vehicles.
- By September 2016, Rivian was negotiating to buy a manufacturing plant formerly owned by Mitsubishi Motors in Normal, Illinois.
- In January 2017, Rivian acquired the Normal plant and its machinery for $16 million to become its primary North American manufacturing facility.
- In December 2017, Rivian revealed its first two products: an electric five-passenger pickup truck and an electric seven-passenger SUV, provisionally named A1T and A1C.
- In November 2018, the truck and SUV were renamed R1T and R1S and unveiled at the LA Auto Show.
- Production for the R1T and R1S was scheduled to begin in 2020.
- Rivian had 250 employees at the start of 2018, growing to 750 by February 2019, over 3,000 by November 2020, and over 9,000 by November 2021.
- In late 2020, Rivian planned to begin R1T shipments in June 2021, which slipped to August and was delayed again due to a global microchip shortage.
- In September 2021, Rivian became the first automaker to bring a fully electric pickup to the consumer market.
- In October 2021, Rivian began delivering the R1T truck to customers.
- On November 10, 2021, Rivian went public via an IPO, selling 153 million shares at $78.00 and valuing the company at $66.5 billion.
- Rivian's stock began trading on Nasdaq under the ticker RIVN and closed its first day at $100.73 per share, valuing the company at just under $100 billion.
- On November 19, 2021, Ford and Rivian announced they no longer planned to co-develop an electric vehicle, with Ford retaining a 12 percent stake valued at over $10 billion.
- Ford sold around 90 percent of its Rivian stake for $3 billion by the end of 2022, up from its initial $1.2 billion investment.
- In December 2021, as Rivian began delivering R1S SUVs and ramping production, COO Rod Copes stepped down and retired.
- On March 1, 2022, Rivian announced price increases of 17 percent for the R1T and 20 percent for the R1S due to semiconductor shortages and higher component costs.
- Rivian later apologized for the retroactive price increases and honored original configured prices for existing preorders.
- On March 14, 2022, Rivian hired Frank Klein as COO, who began on June 1, 2022.
- In March 2022, Rivian was named to TIME's List of 100 Most Influential Companies.
- On July 27, 2022, Rivian announced a 6 percent workforce reduction in response to high inflation, rising interest rates, and increased parts prices.
- In September 2022, Rivian signed an MoU with Mercedes-Benz Group for a joint venture to invest in and operate a European factory for large commercial electric vans.
- Rivian cancelled the Mercedes-Benz partnership in December 2022 but stated future collaborations may be possible.
- In October 2022, Rivian issued a voluntary recall of 13,000 vehicles due to seven reports of loose torque bolts.
- In January 2023, reports indicated the departure of multiple top executives during a challenging period for the company.
- On June 20, 2023, Rivian announced it would incorporate Tesla's NACS charging standard into R1T and R1S vehicles in 2025 and its upcoming R2 platform.
- As early as Q2 2024, Rivian owners may be able to use adapters to connect to Tesla's Supercharger network.
- On June 21, 2023, Rivian announced the acquisition of Swedish mapping company Iternio and its ABRP app, integrating it into its in-car navigation system.
- In late 2023, Amazon's total EDV fleet size reached 10,000 units.
- In October 2023, Rivian ended its exclusivity deal with Amazon, allowing it to supply other commercial customers with electric delivery vehicles.
- In December 2023, AT&T announced it would take delivery of Rivian trucks for a fleet trial process.
- In October 2023, Rivian announced plans to open a new Georgia manufacturing facility with an annual production capacity of 400,000 units in early 2024.
- In March 2026, plans for vertical construction to begin by the end of 2026 were announced for the Georgia facility at a Joint Development Authority meeting.
- On February 21, 2024, Rivian announced it would lay off 10 percent of its salaried workers starting February 23, 2024.
- In March 2024, the Georgia factory project was put on hold for financial reasons but restarted in November 2024 following a $6 billion federal loan announcement.
- On March 7, 2024, Rivian unveiled the R2 SUV starting from $45,000 and announced an even less expensive R3 model.
- On June 25, 2024, Volkswagen Group announced an intention to invest up to $5 billion in Rivian, with an initial $1 billion and additional $4 billion anticipated by 2026.
- The Volkswagen investment includes a prospective joint venture aimed at developing electrical architecture and software technology.
- In July 2024, Germany's competition authority gave approval for the form of the Rivian-Volkswagen joint venture.
- In February 2025, Rivian opened orders for its Electric Commercial Van (ECV) to business fleet customers.
- In July 2025, Rivian announced plans to establish an East Coast headquarters at the Junction Krog District in Atlanta, Georgia, with a 45,000 ft2 office opening in late 2025 and plans to accommodate 500 employees by the end of 2026.
- For Q1 2026, Rivian produced 10,236 vehicles and delivered 10,365, reaffirming annual delivery guidance of between 62,000 and 67,000 vehicles for the 2026 fiscal year.
- In December 2025, Rivian announced its first Autonomy and AI Day to showcase autonomous capabilities with an end-to-end AI-centric approach.
- On December 9, 2025, CEO RJ Scaringe announced his goal of creating an autonomous vehicle capable of running and coordinating tasks and errands for people.
- Both R1 models are designed to be capable off-road with 14 inches of ground clearance.
- The R1T truck was claimed to accelerate from 0-60 mph in under 3 seconds, wade through 3 ft 7 in of water, and climb a 45-degree incline.
- The R1T has a motor at each of its four wheels, with each wheel controllable independently.
- Most expensive R1 models reach around 450 miles on a charge and feature an 800HP electric motor.
- Rivian designed its vehicles to facilitate carsharing with autonomous features.
- The Rivian EDV is designed in three sizes capable of carrying 500, 700, or 900 cubic feet of packages.
- All three EDV sizes share their basic electrical and network architecture, ECUs, and battery packs with the R1 models.
- In December 2021, Rivian reported over 71,000 pre-orders for its R1 models.
- The company produced a total of 24,337 cars in 2022.
- The R1T features four electric motors producing a combined 835 hp and 908 lb-ft of torque, with a claimed 0-60 mph time of 3.0 seconds.
- The R1T is offered with three battery sizes: 105 kWh, 135 kWh, or 180 kWh, with projected ranges of 230, 314, and over 400 miles respectively.
- In December 2021, the R1T was named Motor Trend Truck of the Year.
- In August 2022, Rivian eliminated the base Explore package of the R1T due to low customer demand to streamline supply chain issues.
- The R1S aims for 91 percent shared components with the R1T, sharing a chassis that includes braking, suspension, and cooling systems with a central battery.
- In February 2019, Rivian entered a commercial agreement with Amazon to design, produce, and purchase 100,000 electric delivery vehicles.
- As of 2019, Amazon expected up to 10,000 electric vans in operation by 2022, with full delivery of the 100,000 contract by 2030.
- In November 2022, Rivian announced it had delivered 1,000 EDVs to Amazon.
- In 2026, Amazon announced it reached half its goal with over 50,000 EV delivery vans in its fleet as part of a plan to convert its delivery fleet to 100 percent renewable energy by 2030.
- The EDV assembly line is designed as a low-feature-content line to keep costs down and allow Amazon to reduce costs and shrink its carbon footprint.
- A 150-mile prototype for the Amazon EDV was tested on public roads in early 2021 across multiple U.S. cities including Los Angeles, San Francisco, Colorado, Oklahoma, and Michigan.
- Deployment of electric delivery vans began in nine U.S. cities in July 2022.
- Amazon also sources electric vans from other OEMs such as Stellantis, Mercedes-Benz Group, and Mahindra.
- In early 2023, Amazon notified Rivian it wanted to buy about 10,000 vans that year, prompting Rivian to seek removal of exclusivity terms.
- By January 2024, Amazon agreed to end the exclusivity arrangement, allowing Rivian to seek new commercial customers.
- The R2 is a smaller, less-expensive SUV on a new platform launched in mid-year 2026 with initial production at the Normal, IL facility.
- In May 2023, a shrouded clay model of the R2 was shown, with expected launch costs between $40,000 and $60,000 and a potential pickup truck version alongside the SUV.
- In March 2021, Rivian announced plans for a public charging network across the U.S. and Canada by the end of 2023, initially restricted to owners but open to any EV with a CCS port or adapter as of 2026.
- The charging network targets 600 Adventure Network sites with 3,500 DC fast chargers at high-traffic locations, starting at 200 kW with a target of 300 kW.
- Rivian planned 10,000 destination chargers called Rivian Waypoints at retail, lodging, dining, and parks, usable by all EVs with a J1772 connector.
- In July 2021, Rivian announced plans to install Waypoint chargers at all 56 Tennessee state parks, with the first site unveiled on October 1, 2021.
- In June 2023, it was reported that Rivian adopted NACS for its vehicles in North America beginning in the 2026 model year.
- As of 2026, Rivian had more than 50,000 CCS DC fast chargers across the United States, all powered by renewable energy.
- Rivian's headquarters in Irvine, California focuses on vehicle engineering and design, propulsion and battery system development, and commercial functions.
- The sole production factory in Normal, Illinois manufactures components like battery packs in a 2.6-million-square-foot plant equipped with paint shops, robotics, stamping machines, and injection molding equipment.
- In July 2020, plans for 576,000 ft2 of additions to the Normal facility were approved by the town council.
- In October 2021, Rivian announced plans for a 623,000 ft2 addition to the Normal facility, bringing total size to 3,900,000 ft2.
- In December 2021, Rivian announced plans for a $5 billion battery and assembly plant east of Atlanta with construction planned for summer 2022 and production starting in 2024.
- The Georgia plant was projected to employ 7,500 workers with possible growth to 10,000 workers.
- Some locals opposed the Rivian plant due to environmental concerns and government costs, including Georgia proposing to allocate $125 million for associated factory costs.
- In early 2024, Rivian announced it was putting construction of the Georgia plant on hold, noting the land had been at least 90 percent graded, while all vehicles continued to be made in Normal.
- Cumulative vehicle production reached 132,048 by Q4 2024 and 152,638 by Q2 2025.
- As of December 2021, Rivian is a public company trading under the symbol RIVN on Nasdaq.
- From seed stage to its final $2.5 billion round in 2021, Rivian secured 10 rounds of private funding.
- In January 2017, Rivian received a $1 million grant and five-year tax abatement from Normal, Illinois contingent on employment targets and a $40.5 million investment over five years.
- Rivian also received $49.5 million in tax credits from the Illinois state government contingent on meeting employment targets and investing at least $175 million into the site by 2024.
- In December 2017, Sumitomo Corporation made a large strategic investment in Rivian, though the amount was undisclosed.
- On May 23, 2018, Rivian closed on $200 million in debt financing from Standard Chartered Bank, bringing total raised funds to upwards of $450 million.
- On February 15, 2019, Amazon led an investment round of $700 million into Rivian.
- On April 24, 2019, Ford Motor Company invested $500 million at a pre-money valuation of $4.5 billion.
- On September 10, 2019, Cox Automotive invested $350 million into Rivian and planned to add a representative to its board.
- In December 2019, Rivian raised US$1.3 billion in a round led by T Rowe Price with Ford and Amazon.
- On January 19, 2021, Rivian raised $2.65 billion led by T. Rowe Price alongside multiple institutional investors including Soros Fund Management, Fidelity, Coatue Management, BlackRock, Abdul Latif Jameel, Amazon Climate Pledge Fund, and D1 Capital Partners.
- On July 23, 2021, Rivian raised $2.5 billion in a round led by Amazon's Climate Pledge Fund, D1 Capital Partners, and Ford, with new investors Third Point Ventures and Dragoneer Investment Group joining.
- In August 2021, Rivian filed for an IPO seeking a valuation as high as $80 billion after raising approximately $11 billion total.
- Amazon purchased $200 million of stock in the IPO, raising its total stake in Rivian to 22 percent.
- Rivian's stock peaked at $146.07 on November 13, 2021, before falling continuously during the early 2022 tech sell-off to $30.68 on April 26, 2022.
- The market value declined to roughly $29 billion by the end of April 2022, a loss of 79 percent from its peak.
- At the beginning of May 2022, Rivian's stock price fell further to an all-time low of $22.78 on May 9, 2022, a decline of nearly 85 percent from the November 2021 peak.
- Rivian's stock recovered significantly through the first half of 2023, reaching a period high of $28.06 on July 27, 2023.
- Momentum reversed with a poorly received $1.5 billion convertible green note, causing the share price to decline to a daily low of $15.88 on October 26, 2023.
- Around 28 percent of Rivian shares are held by insiders, with the 10 largest institutional shareholders holding significant portions.
