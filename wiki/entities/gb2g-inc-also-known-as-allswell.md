# GB2G, Inc. (also known as Allswell)

**Type:** Company  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[workforce-training|Workforce Training]]  ·  **Location:** Newnan, Georgia, Coweta County, Georgia, United States

## Overview

Allswell is a Newnan-based staffing agency that provides engineering and production labor to automotive parts manufacturers in Georgia. The agency has been accused of using the TN visa program to recruit Mexican engineers for engineering positions that turned out to be manual assembly-line work.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Supplies labor to auto parts manufacturers (Hyundai Mobis, Mando, Hyundai Glovis) that produce components for Kia vehicles assembled in Georgia, a key part of the state's emerging electric-vehicle supply chain.

## Workforce & partners

- TN Visa Program — Visa category used by Allswell to recruit Mexican engineers for U.S. employment.. The TN visa is intended for high-skill professionals; the agency allegedly misused it for manual assembly work.

## Key facts

- Allswell provided engineering staffing solutions and claimed full compliance with federal, state, and local laws.
- Six Mexican nationals were recruited through SPJ Connect and placed by Allswell at auto parts manufacturers in West Point, Georgia.
- Workers performed manual assembly-line tasks such as lugging bumpers and transmissions weighing over 100 pounds, contrary to the engineering roles promised.
- Salaries offered ($40,000-$44,000 annually) were far below the regional average for mechanical engineers (~$80,000).
- Workers lived in overcrowded housing; one was placed on a house floor shared with seven other TN workers.
- The TN visa program is intended for high-skill professionals; using it for production line work may constitute immigration fraud and human trafficking.
- Federal agents performed on-site inspections at Allswell-related facilities and found no violations, according to the company’s statement.
- Labor advocates warn that reliance on low-wage foreign workers can depress wages for local Georgia residents.
- The article highlights a broader pattern of visa misuse in the Southeast auto sector, with multiple recruiters and staffing firms implicated.
