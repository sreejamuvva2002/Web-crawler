# Global Automotive OEMs and Tier 1/2/3 Suppliers

**Type:** supplier_relationship  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2013-07-12

**Source:** [Automotive OEMs tell suppliers to be prepared to play, or go home](https://www.plasticstoday.com/automotive-mobility/automotive-oems-tell-suppliers-to-be-prepared-to-play-or-go-home)  ·  `page_060575`

## Facts

- The automotive industry in North America has rebounded significantly with new models hitting showroom floors and sales ticking upward.
- OEMs are concerned about whether their suppliers can keep up with increasing demand.
- New car sales in the U.S. were on pace to exceed 15 million in 2013, according to an IHS SupplierBusiness editorial.
- North American vehicle production was expected to exceed 16 million in 2013 and 17 million in 2015, per IHS Automotive.
- Michael Robinet, managing director for IHS Automotive Consulting, forecasted about 135 vehicle launches per year through 2016.
- Vehicle launches averaged 85 per year in 2010 and 2011, compared to the projected 135 annually through 2016.
- Suppliers face significant pressure due to a strained supply base, with potential for launch delays if they cannot handle the pace.
- Many suppliers are reluctant to expand capacity due to fears of another automotive fiscal cliff.
- Larger Tier 1 suppliers are responding to OEM demands for expanded capacity and capabilities, while smaller Tier 2 and 3 suppliers struggle to maintain their presence.
- Smaller suppliers face increasing difficulty as a small group of huge global suppliers secure larger contracts from OEMs.
- Large global Tier 1 suppliers possess the financial resources to meet OEM demands for both capacity expansion and enhanced R&D capabilities.
- OEMs are demanding increased supplier investment in research and development of new technologies and international facilities.
- International Automotive Components (IAC) Group, a large global Tier 1 supplier with over 90 manufacturing locations, developed new plastics processing technologies for vehicle interiors.
- IAC provides instrument panel solutions including air distribution ducts, air outlets, glove boxes, decorative appliqués, and other trim components.
- IAC developed unique surface materials like SprayPUR, TAC 2, and FastCast to meet consumer demand for comfort and craftsmanship.
- Smaller suppliers unable to provide high-level R&D or meet capacity demands are becoming targets as OEMs consolidate their supplier pools.
- Ford allocated 65% of its global purchasing to companies on its Aligned Business Framework list of 104 preferred suppliers in the prior year, up from 55% in 2010.
- Ford aims to continue increasing this share while reducing its total number of suppliers from 1,250 to 750.
- OEMs are requiring suppliers to accept shared responsibility for potential product liability, warranty, and recall claims.
- The emphasis on shared responsibility has extended from automakers down through all three tiers of the supply chain.
- Mold manufacturers face increased risk of being dragged into product liability lawsuits due to today's consumer advocacy climate.
- Tier 1 suppliers are questioning whether their Tier 2 and 3 suppliers are financially viable to cover warranty exposures or claims.
- Most suppliers were running three crews or shifts to meet current demand, leaving little slack in the system.
- Rapid supplier expansion carries pitfalls, including compromised part quality that could lead to increased product liability claims.
- Tier 2 and 3 suppliers must focus on reducing risk within their automotive customer base as OEMs and Tier 1 suppliers work to mitigate supply chain risks.
