## Facts

- Ascend Elements, a Massachusetts-based battery recycling startup, filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy protection.
- The company had raised more than $500 million prior to its bankruptcy filing.
- A critical Department of Energy grant that Ascend Elements was counting on to fund its commercial-scale operations was canceled.
- Lithium carbonate prices peaked above $80,000 per ton in late 2022 but now trade around $12,000.
- Nickel and cobalt battery material prices followed a similar downward trajectory over the past two years.
- Honda was a strategic investor in Ascend Elements.
- South Korean battery giant SK On was a strategic investor in Ascend Elements.
- Ascend Elements developed a proprietary "Hydro-to-Cathode" recycling process.
- Construction had begun on Ascend Elements' planned battery recycling facility in Hopkinsville, Kentucky.
- The Hopkinsville facility site spans 140 acres.
- The Hopkinsville plant was projected to process enough batteries annually to recover materials for 250,000 electric vehicles.
- The Hopkinsville project was supposed to create 400 jobs in the region.
- Local officials in Hopkinsville celebrated Ascend Elements' arrival as a clean energy investment that would anchor their economic future.
- The Inflation Reduction Act provided billions for domestic battery manufacturing, though fund deployment has been slower and more selective than anticipated.
- Ascend Elements' recycling process could recover up to 98% of critical materials from spent lithium-ion batteries.
- Testing showed that Ascend Elements' recycled cathode material performed identically to virgin material.
- Global EV sales growth slowed from the breakneck pace seen between 2020 and 2022.
- China has flooded global markets with cheap battery materials, leveraging its control of processing capacity to undercut Western competitors.
- Battery giants CATL and LG Energy Solution might see value in acquiring Ascend Elements' technology and partial facilities at bankruptcy prices.
- Redwood Materials, founded by former Tesla CTO JB Straubel, has raised over $2 billion and is better capitalized to weather the battery recycling downturn.
- Li-Cycle had to dramatically scale back expansion plans in 2023 due to its own financial challenges.
- The battery recycling sector is currently overcrowded relative to current demand for recycled materials.
- Ascend Elements' bankruptcy filing will give the company breathing room to restructure debt and potentially find new investors or acquirers.
