# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Shaping The Region: Hussey Gay Bell Helps Bring the Hyundai Metaplant to Life - Hussey Gay Bell](https://husseygaybell.com/shaping-the-region-hussey-gay-bell-and-hyundai-motor-group/)  ·  `page_000413`

## Facts

- Georgia reached a historic milestone in EV manufacturing with the grand opening of the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) in Bryan County.
- The facility was completed just two and a half years after breaking ground.
- HMGMA stands as the largest economic development project in Georgia’s history.
- In 2022, Hyundai Motor Group announced a landmark $5.5 billion investment to establish its first U.S.-based EV mass production facility on a 3,000+ acre site in Bryan County, Georgia.
- The total project investment has grown to over $8 billion, encompassing HMGMA and its network of Tier 1 suppliers.
- The initiative is projected to create more than 12,000 direct, long-term jobs across coastal Georgia.
- Commercial production at the facility officially began in March 2025.
- The Hyundai Metaplant integrates cutting-edge smart factory technology and sustainability initiatives.
- The project was delivered through a Construction Manager at Risk (CMAR) approach to ensure feasibility, efficiency, and cost-effectiveness from concept to completion.
- Hussey Gay Bell served as the architecture, engineering, and surveying partner for the HMGMA project.
- Hussey Gay Bell acted as both prime consultant and subconsultant across multiple Tier 1 supplier projects related to the initiative.
- Hussey Gay Bell provided comprehensive professional services to facilitate seamless development and integration within Hyundai’s advanced EV network.
- Hussey Gay Bell played a vital role in the design and development of on-site facilities for the project.
- Hussey Gay Bell contributed to several off-site Tier 1 supplier developments designed and constructed in parallel with the Hyundai Metaplant.
- Each facility was engineered to align with Hyundai’s next-generation EV production standards to ensure efficiency, sustainability, and long-term operational success.
- The project's success resulted from a public–private partnership among Hyundai Motor Group, the State of Georgia, the Georgia Department of Economic Development, and regional development authorities.
- Georgia has held the title of America’s #1 State for Business for over a decade.
