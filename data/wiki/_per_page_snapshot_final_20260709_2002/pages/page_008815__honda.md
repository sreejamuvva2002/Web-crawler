# Honda

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-03-31

**Source:** [12 things to know about next-gen Honda EVs arriving in 2026](https://www.greencarreports.com/news/1142042_12-things-to-know-about-next-gen-honda-evs-arriving-in-2026)  ·  `page_008815`

## Facts

- Honda provided a first look at future EV models planned for 2026 with the Saloon and Space-Hub concepts at CES in Las Vegas.
- Both the Saloon and Space-Hub concepts are envisioned for Honda's 0 Series (Zero Series) platform.
- At least some of these upcoming models will be U.S.-made and will arrive in the U.S. before Japan, China, and Europe.
- The vehicles will feature a not-yet-detailed Motion Management System for ride and handling.
- Honda's future EVs will emphasize efficiency and use lightweight, energy-dense battery packs.
- Batteries to be used in later versions of the 0 Series platform will charge from 15-80% in just 10-15 minutes.
- The batteries will lose no more than 10% of original capacity (and range) after 10 years of use.
- The 0 Series platform consists of multiple layers including an EV propulsion platform, an E&E electrical and electronic architecture, a software platform, an application layer, a sensing suite, and Honda Connect telematics and data/software delivery.
- Initial 0 Series production models will start in the mid-large segment, with concepts appearing to be around 190 inches long.
- Honda declined to quote dimensions for the concepts but plans to introduce different-sized EV and propulsion platforms over time to span from small to large vehicles.
- A production model based on the Saloon concept will launch in 2026, though Honda has not yet decided on a specific production volume.
- There will be multiple production Honda EVs based on the 0 Series building blocks.
- Honda has committed to 40 gigawatt-hours annually with LG as part of a joint-venture battery-supply agreement, enough to produce 350,000 to 400,000 vehicles.
- The 0 Series lineup will not use existing Honda brand names like Accord, Civic, or CR-V.
- Honda does not expect an affordable small EV much before 2030, as adding EV versions of legacy models proved more difficult than anticipated.
- The Afeela electric sedan, set to be sold by Sony-Honda in 2026, will use the 0 Series underpinnings as common building blocks but will include additional components to enhance vehicle value.
- Honda's technical approach for the 0 Series emphasizes thin, light, and wise design, starting with reducing battery height at the bottom of the vehicle by about 10%.
- Creative director Daisuke Tsutamori stated that reducing battery height frees up design choices on the way to production.
- Honda developed the Zero series with a target of 300 miles or more as the optimal range for the U.S. market production models.
- General manager Koji Nakano confirmed the 300-mile range target was chosen combined with a thin battery form factor to minimize losses and optimize efficiency.
- Honda will not use carbon fiber or carbon-fiber-reinforced plastic for these vehicles, despite BMW's past use of it in the i3.
- Nakano could not reveal whether the vehicles will be made from steel, aluminum, or other materials.
- Production models will be developed with bidirectional charging capability in mind.
- The Space-Hub concept appeared to feature a mobile power pack charging between the front and rear seats, potentially tying into Honda's Motocompacto electric scooter.
- Honda is working on SAE Level 3 city-driving capability for the 0 Series, aiming to deploy it over-the-air in the late 2020s.
- Honda aims to make steer-by-wire systems and assisted driving features more widely available and affordable.
- The steer-by-wire system will allow for assisted driving plus increased packaging flexibility.
- Aerodynamics is a key part of efficiency and real-world range, with Honda focusing on the coefficient of drag in modeling the Saloon concept's shape.
- Honda plans to bring an EV based closely on the Saloon model to production, though it would not reveal drag numbers as they are still concepts.
- The 0 Series design emphasizes simple, clean aesthetics mixed with elements from earlier Honda designs like the first-generation Civic front end, which houses radar, lidar, and other sensors instead of a grille.
- Neither concept vehicle features a rear window, a design choice Honda would like to make to expand rear passenger space, pending market-by-market regulatory checks as models evolve toward production.
