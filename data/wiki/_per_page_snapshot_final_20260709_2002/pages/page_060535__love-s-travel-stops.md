# Love's Travel Stops

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** charging_infrastructure  ·  **Published:** 2023-09-13

**Source:** [Love's Travel Stops Installing EV Charging Stations In Over A Dozen States - Clean Energy Revolution](https://cleanenergyrevolution.co/2024/08/22/loves-travel-stops-installing-ev-charging-stations-in-over-a-dozen-states/)  ·  `page_060535`

## Facts

- Love’s Travel Stops is an Oklahoma City-based company investing in electric vehicle (EV) charging infrastructure.
- The company broke ground on EV charging stations at two locations in New York.
- Each New York location will be equipped with two dual-port Level 3 DC fast chargers, offering four charging spots with power outputs of 160 kilowatts.
- Love’s currently offers more than 100 chargers at 28 stores in 11 states.
- The company first started in the EV space in 2017 and has continued to grow its network since then.
- Love’s announced groundbreaking on additional charging stations in Pennsylvania and Kentucky later this year (2023).
- In September 2023, Love’s was awarded $4.8 million in federal funding to build EV charging stations at eight of its locations in Pennsylvania and Colorado.
- The award was part of the Federal Highway Administration’s National Electric Vehicle Infrastructure (NEVI) Formula Program.
- Trillium Energy Solutions, a member of the Love’s Family of Companies that designs, builds, and maintains clean fueling stations, is partnering with Love’s on the Pennsylvania and Colorado projects.
- Construction of EV charging stations is set to begin in Oklahoma, Texas, Alabama, Arkansas, Colorado, Georgia, Indiana, Kansas, Michigan, Ohio, Tennessee, Virginia, and Washington in 2025.
- Gas stations and truck stops together host almost 54 percent of NEVI-funded charging stalls, with the largest chains dominating.
- Love’s and Pilot Flying J are each slated to host 39 charging plazas.
- Truck stops and gas station chains won $92.1 million out of a total of $265 million awarded by the infrastructure law to date.
- Shane Wharton, President of Love’s, stated that expanding the EV charging network reinforces the company's commitment to meeting customers’ needs regardless of fuel type.
