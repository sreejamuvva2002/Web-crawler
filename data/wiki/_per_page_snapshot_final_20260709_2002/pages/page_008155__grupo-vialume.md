# Grupo Vialume

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2025-12

**Source:** [Grupo Vialume to invest $4.4M in new GA manufacturing operations](https://valdostatoday.com/news-2/region/2025/12/grupo-vialume-to-invest-4-4m-in-new-ga-manufacturing-operations/)  ·  `page_008155`

## Facts

- Governor Brian Kemp announced that Grupo Vialume plans to invest $4.4 million in a new Georgia manufacturing operations.
- The investment will establish U.S. manufacturing operations in Colquitt-Miller County, creating 150 new jobs over approximately seven years.
- Grupo Vialume is based in the state of São Paulo, Brazil, and is a second-generation, family-owned enterprise founded in the 1980s.
- The company has grown into one of the largest global manufacturers of advanced visibility solutions for transportation safety.
- Its flagship business, Vialume, is Brazil’s largest producer of reflective raised pavement markers and is recognized as one of the top five manufacturers worldwide.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated he met with Grupo Vialume in Brazil over the summer and expressed excitement about welcoming them to Georgia.
- CEO João Paulo Moura cited growing demand for sustainable products, the strength of the U.S. supplier base, and greater ease of exporting to commercial partners as key factors for the expansion.
- The company also noted attractive tax incentives, the stability of the U.S. dollar, and the security of investing in a strong market as reasons for choosing Colquitt.
- Grupo Vialume’s new integrated manufacturing and recycling hub will be located on E. Crawford Street in the City of Colquitt.
- The facility will produce both plastic raised pavement markers and precision-engineered reflective lenses.
- The hub will pioneer new circular-economy processes by transforming materials once considered unrecyclable, including textile offcuts and peanut-shell byproducts, into high-value products and raw materials.
- Upgrades to the existing facilities are expected to be completed in 2026.
- The company will be hiring for production operators, injection machinists, salespersons, logistics experts, and management positions.
- Colquitt Mayor Preston Clenney highlighted the positive economic development and collaboration that helped nurture the project with support from the State of Georgia and the City Development Authority.
- Miller County Board of Commissioners Chairman Ladon Calhoun noted the investment is breathing new life into a building underutilized for over a decade and will benefit residents and neighboring communities in southwest Georgia.
- Senior Project Manager Mellissa Takeuchi represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development on this project in partnership with Colquitt Downtown Development Authority and Electric Cities of Georgia.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson emphasized Georgia’s long-term international partnerships, noting the department celebrated 30 years of representation in Brazil over the summer.
