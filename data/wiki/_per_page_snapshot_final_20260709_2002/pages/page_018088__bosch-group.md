# Bosch Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [The Robert Bosch story: in the footsteps of an automotive legend - Bosch Media Service Hungary](https://boschmediaservice.hu/en/press_release/bosch_heritage_podcast_2024-418.html)  ·  `page_018088`

## Facts

- Robert Bosch founded his first company in 1886 at age 25 with a business partner as a precision mechanics workshop.
- The early company fabricated bells, telephones, and sold medical equipment.
- Robert Bosch invented magnetic ignition, which was essential for high-revving engines and enabled the birth of the automotive industry.
- Bosch established a team of innovation experts in the 1930s to monitor industry trends and suggest areas for investment, invention, and acquisition.
- The company diversified its product range early on to cover almost everything powered by a motor, including hair trimmers, power tools, and refrigerators.
- Robert Bosch placed great emphasis on the salary, well-being, and training of associates, founded a hospital and sanatorium with 400 beds, and introduced the 8-hour working day.
- Bosch has been present in Hungary since 1898 with its products.
- After re-establishment as a regional trading company in 1991, Bosch grew into one of Hungary’s largest foreign industrial employers with currently eight subsidiaries.
- In fiscal 2023, Bosch Hungary had total net sales of 2.207 billion forints and consolidated sales to third parties on the Hungarian market of 343 billion forints.
- The Bosch Group in Hungary employs more than 18,300 associates as of December 31, 2023.
- The Bosch Group is a leading global supplier of technology and services that employs roughly 429,000 associates worldwide as of December 31, 2023.
- The company generated sales of 91.6 billion euros in 2023.
- Operations are divided into four business sectors: Mobility, Industrial Technology, Consumer Goods, and Energy and Building Technology.
- Bosch aims to use technology to help shape universal trends such as automation, electrification, digitalization, connectivity, and an orientation to sustainability.
- The company uses expertise in sensor technology, software, services, connectivity, and artificial intelligence to develop user-friendly, sustainable products.
- The Bosch Group comprises Robert Bosch GmbH and roughly 470 subsidiary and regional companies in over 60 countries.
- Including sales and service partners, the global manufacturing, engineering, and sales network covers nearly every country in the world.
- At 136 locations across the globe, Bosch employs some 90,000 associates in research and development, of which nearly 48,000 are software engineers.
