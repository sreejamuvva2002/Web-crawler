# Savannah Economic Development Authority

**Type:** Government Program  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[workforce-training|Workforce Training]]  ·  **Location:** Savannah, Georgia, Georgia, United States

## Overview

The Savannah Economic Development Authority (SEDA) held its annual meeting to discuss the region’s workforce demand, highlighting a projected deficit of over 1,400 industrial workers by 2025 and outlining plans to boost the local labor pool through recruitment, school partnerships, new incentive programs, and the creation of a dedicated workforce organization.

## EV supply-chain relevance

SEDA is coordinating workforce planning for upcoming electric vehicle and battery manufacturing facilities in Georgia, including Hyundai’s Bryan County EV and battery processing plant.

## Key facts

- SEDA officials say boosting the area’s workforce is among their biggest priorities in 2024.
- Trip Tollison emphasized the need to recruit and work with schools and other institutions to increase the workforce because many jobs need to be filled quickly.
- A study by the Savannah Harbor - Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority projects a deficit of more than 1,400 industrial workers in the area by 2025.
- Manufacturers like Hyundai Motor Group plan to hire thousands of employees locally in the coming years.
- SEDA announced the creation of a new organization tasked with boosting regional workforce numbers through six key areas.
- In 2023, approximately 3,700 jobs were created with $2.4 billion in regional investment.
- The upcoming focus will shift to securing more manufacturing businesses in the area through new incentive programs.
