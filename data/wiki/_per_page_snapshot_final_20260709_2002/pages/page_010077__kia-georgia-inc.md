# Kia Georgia, Inc.

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-05-31

**Source:** [Kia Georgia Assembles 2025 EV9 SUV - The EV Report](https://theevreport.com/kia-georgia-assembles-2025-ev9-suv)  ·  `page_010077`

## Facts

- The 2025 Kia EV9 is the first saleable electric vehicle assembled in the state of Georgia.
- The 2025 Kia EV9 joins Kia’s lineup of award-winning SUVs at the West Point facility.
- The historic milestone event was celebrated with notable attendees including Governor Brian Kemp and Kia executives.
- The introduction of the EV9 is part of a broader $200 million expansion project.
- The expansion has created approximately 200 new jobs at the plant.
- Kia’s investment in e-mobility highlights its ongoing dedication to the U.S. market and Georgia.
- SeungKyu (Sean) Yoon, President and CEO of Kia North America and Kia America, attended the milestone event.
- James Watson, Vice President of Production at Kia Georgia, attended the milestone event.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that the expansion is an example of state and local partners working with a valued job creator.
- Governor Brian Kemp thanked the Kia Georgia team for choosing the Peach State as they work to become the e-mobility capital of the nation.
- SeungKyu (Sean) Yoon stated that the EV9 launch is an example of Kia’s commitment to the U.S. market and Georgia.
- Through Plan S, Kia aims to be a leader in the e-mobility sector for years to come.
- The EV9 has won accolades including World Car of the Year, World EV of the Year, and North American Utility Vehicle of the Year.
- Stuart Countess is the President and CEO of Kia Georgia.
- Stuart Countess credited the Kia Georgia team for their dedication and ability to turn challenges into opportunities.
- The first Kia vehicle was assembled at the plant on November 16, 2009.
- Kia Georgia, Inc. is Kia Corporation’s first manufacturing site in North America.
- Kia Corporation is based in Seoul, Korea.
- The facility has an annual production capacity of 350,000 units.
- The facility is located on 2,200 acres in West Point, Georgia.
- The facility began mass production on November 16, 2009.
- The facility produces the Telluride SUV.
- The facility produces the Sorento SUV, excluding HEV and PHEV models.
- The facility produces the Sportage SUV, excluding HEV and PHEV models.
- The facility produces the all-electric EV9 SUV.
- The expansion allows Kia Georgia to assemble both electric vehicles and traditional products on the same assembly line.
- The expansion increases the plant’s flexibility and capacity.
