# Qcells

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2023-01-11

**Source:** [Solar power company plans $2.5B Georgia expansion, hiring 2,500 new workers | FOX 5 Atlanta](https://www.fox5atlanta.com/news/qcells-cartersville-dalton-factory-new-jobs-georgia)  ·  `page_010467`

## Facts

- Qcells, a unit of Hanwha Solutions, plans to invest more than $2.5 billion to build factories in Georgia.
- The company will hire 2,500 new employees for its Georgia operations.
- A new factory in Cartersville will employ 2,000 people.
- Construction on the Cartersville factory is set to start within weeks of the announcement.
- Production at the Cartersville factory is expected to begin before the end of 2024.
- Qcells announced a third expansion phase for its existing Dalton plant.
- The Dalton plant is currently the largest manufacturer of solar panels in the Western Hemisphere.
- Qcells will add nearly 500 jobs at the Dalton facility.
- Total employment at the Dalton plant will exceed 1,500 once all expansions are complete.
- Qcells CEO Justin Lee stated the company aims to expand low-carbon solar investments and lead the industry toward fully American-made clean energy solutions.
- Republican Georgia Governor Brian Kemp celebrated the expansion as an example of Georgia being the No. 1 state for business.
- Kemp cited Georgia's robust logistics infrastructure, top-ranked workforce training program, and collaborative approach as providing a business-friendly environment.
- Democratic President Joe Biden described the announcement as a win for workers, consumers, and the climate.
- Biden stated the project would provide good jobs, reduce American reliance on other countries for solar components, lower solar panel costs, and help lower carbon emissions.
- The Cartersville plant fulfills one of the aims of the climate change and health care bill that Biden signed in August.
- The bill includes provisions from Georgia Senators Jon Ossoff and Raphael Warnock allowing companies making solar panel components to claim tax credits.
- The Cartersville plant will assemble solar panels, silicon ingots and wafers, as well as solar cells.
- Qcells will source polysilicon from an REC Silicon plant in Moses Lake, Washington.
- Hanwha purchased a 21% stake in REC last year, whose shares are listed in Norway.
- Qcells currently produces solar modules with an annual capacity of 1.7 gigawatts at its Dalton plant.
- Following expansions, total module production will reach 8.4 gigawatts, equivalent to approximately 10,000 solar panels.
- The expanded capacity will be split between 5.1 gigawatts in Dalton and 3.3 gigawatts in Cartersville.
- Senator Jon Ossoff stated his goal remains to make Georgia the world leader in advanced energy production.
- Qcells currently employs 750 workers at its existing Dalton factory.
- Last year, Qcells announced a second expansion phase for Dalton valued at $171 million, which included hiring 470 additional workers.
- Senators Warnock and Ossoff along with Biden administration officials stated that Biden’s strategy is enhancing the nation’s manufacturing base as part of the clean energy transition.
- Biden clean energy adviser John Podesta projected that by 2027, the Qcells expansion will supply about 30% of total U.S. demand for solar panels.
- Podesta described the deal as President Biden’s vision come to life.
- The Biden administration states its policies have driven $300 billion in private investment across sectors like semiconductors, clean energy, EVs, and batteries.
- Nearly $25 billion of that private investment has been directed to Georgia.
- Georgia's investments include two electric vehicle plants valued at over $5 billion each, plus a battery plant announced for Cartersville in December valued at over $4 billion.
- Hyundai Motor Group is constructing one of the $5 billion-plus EV plants in Georgia.
- Hyundai Motor Group is partnering with South Korean firm SK Group to build the Cartersville battery plant.
- Georgia economic development officials have particularly promoted electric vehicles and batteries.
- State Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson stated Qcells solidified Georgia’s place as a leader in renewable energy and sustainable technology when it opened the largest solar panel manufacturing facility in the Western Hemisphere in 2019.
- National Climate Adviser Ali Zaidi stated U.S. solar panel manufacturing is on track to exceed 33.5 gigawatts annually by 2024, more than quadrupling the 7 gigawatts at the start of Biden's term.
- Zaidi noted this output would be sufficient to power clean solar energy for about 5 million homes per year.
- The exact total incentive package from state and local governments was not immediately disclosed.
- Qcells may qualify for over $65 million in state income tax credits, calculated at $5,250 per job over five years.
- Eligibility for the tax credits requires workers to earn at least $31,300 annually.
- Local officials reported that starting wages for Dalton workers are $17 per hour.
