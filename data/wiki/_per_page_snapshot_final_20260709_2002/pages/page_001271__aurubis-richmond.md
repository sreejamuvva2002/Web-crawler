# Aurubis Richmond

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-03

**Source:** [Aurubis Launches First U.S. Multimetal Recycling Plant In Georgia](https://www.rttnews.com/3576893/aurubis-launches-first-u-s-multimetal-recycling-plant-in-georgia.aspx)  ·  `page_001271`

## Facts

- Aurubis AG has officially commenced operations at its first U.S. multimetal recycling plant in Richmond County, Georgia.
- The facility will produce strategic metals including copper, nickel, tin, and precious metals.
- These recovered metals play a key role in expanding data centers, AI applications, energy infrastructure, high-tech manufacturing, and the defense industry.
- Global demand for strategic metals, especially copper, continues to surge.
- The U.S. industry requires approximately 1.8 million tons of copper annually.
- Experts forecast a nearly 30% increase in strategic metal demand over the next five years.
- Currently, the U.S. imports about half of the copper it processes.
- Aurubis Richmond aims to close the domestic supply gap by leveraging advanced multimetal recycling technologies to enhance the resilience and independence of American supply chains.
- Aurubis is investing around $800 million into the Richmond facility.
- The investment will create over 240 new jobs in Georgia.
- The plant is designed to process up to 180,000 tons of complex recycling materials each year.
- Processed inputs will include printed circuit boards, copper cables, and other metal-bearing products.
- An expansion phase for the facility is scheduled to begin in 2026.
- Aurubis Richmond will recover critical metals directly from recycled materials, effectively closing the value chain on site.
- By sourcing materials locally, the plant helps retain valuable raw resources within the U.S. market, supporting sustainability and supply chain security.
