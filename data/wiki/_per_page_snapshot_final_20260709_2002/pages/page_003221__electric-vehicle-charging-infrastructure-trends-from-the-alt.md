# Electric Vehicle Charging Infrastructure Trends from the Alternative Fueling Station Locator: Third Quarter 2022

**Type:** charging_infrastructure  ·  **Role:** charging_infrastructure  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Alternative Fuels Data Center: Publications](https://afdc.energy.gov/publications?page=7&tags[]=electricity)  ·  `page_003221`

## Facts

- The U.S. Department of Energy's Alternative Fueling Station Locator contains information on public and private nonresidential alternative fueling stations in the United States and Canada.
- The locator currently tracks ethanol (E85), biodiesel, compressed natural gas, electric vehicle (EV) charging, hydrogen, liquefied natural gas, and propane stations.
- EV charging continues to experience rapidly changing technology and growing infrastructure.
- This report provides a snapshot of the state of EV charging infrastructure in the United States in the third calendar quarter of 2022 (Q3).
- Using data from the Station Locator, the report breaks down the growth of public and private charging infrastructure by charging level, network, and location.
- The report measures the current state of charging infrastructure compared with two different 2030 infrastructure requirement scenarios.
- This information is intended to help transportation planners, policymakers, researchers, infrastructure developers, and others understand the rapidly changing landscape of EV charging infrastructure.
- This is the eleventh report in a series on electric vehicle charging infrastructure trends.
- The report was published on March 9, 2023.
- Authors include A. Brown, J. Cappellucci, E. White, A. Heinrich, and E. Cost.
