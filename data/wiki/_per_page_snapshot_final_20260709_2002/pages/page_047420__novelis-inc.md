# Novelis, Inc.

## Facts

- Novelis, Inc. is a leader in producing sustainable aluminum products, aluminum rolling and aluminum recycling.
- The Company focuses on providing solutions for the beverage can, automotive, aerospace and high-end specialty markets.
- The Company is a subsidiary of Hindalco Industries Limited, the metals flagship company of the Indian multinational company Aditya Birla Group.
- The Company is organized into four operating segments based on geographical area as follows: North America, Europe, Asia, and South America
- The Company supplies rolled aluminum components for automotive bodies including body-in-whites, body panels and side walls, doors, hoods and trunks, wings, spoilers and fins, fenders, and liftgates.
- The Company supplies structural components including engine supports, cross members, crash-resistant passenger compartments, platforms to mount drive units and axle modules, peripheral energy-conversion areas, stabilizer bars, frame rails, bracket frames, gussets, and bumpers.
- The Company supplies car trim and sealings, heat exchangers (aluminum fin stocks, air conditioners, brazing sheets, radiators), electric vehicle battery enclosures, and transportation components.
- Transportation components supplied include flexible tubes, heat protection tubes, air ducts, exhaust pipes, thermal insulation hoses, energy absorption elements, tubes for hot and cold air distribution, heat protection mats and sleeves, hoses, silencers, tanks and rigid containers.
- The Company was created in 2005 as a spin-off from aluminum producer Alcan Inc.
- In May 2007, the company was acquired by Hindalco Industries Limited.
- In May 2013, the company signed a cooperation agreement with ThyssenKrupp Tailored Blanks for the development of tailored aluminum blanks.
- In May 2013, the company Europe qualified its rolling and finishing facility in Gottingen, Germany to produce automotive aluminum sheet.
- In July 2013, the company announced the opening of its expanded aluminum rolling operations in Pindamonhangaba, Sao Paulo, Brazil.
- In October 2013, the company completed a two-year, USD 400 million expansion program in South Korea.
- In October 2013, the company commissioned a USD 200 million expansion of its rolling operations in Oswego, New York, U.S.
- In December 2013, the company announced an investment of an additional USD 205 million to further expand global manufacturing operations serving the automotive market in U.S. and Germany.
- In April 2014, the company held a groundbreaking ceremony of a new aluminum automotive heat treatment line in Nachterstedt, Germany.
- In April 2014, the company signed an agreement with Changzhou National Hi-Tech District to build China's first aluminum automotive sheet manufacturing facility with an annual capacity of 120,000 metric tons.
- In January 2018, the company announced an investment of USD 305 million to locate a new processing plant in Guthrie, Kentucky to produce automotive-grade aluminum sheet.
- In February 2018, the company signed a binding agreement to acquire operating facilities and manufacturing assets at its Sierre plant from Constellium for EUR 200 million, expected to close in the first quarter of fiscal 2019.
- In April 2020, the company completed an acquisition of Aleris Corporation, a global supplier of rolled aluminum products, for approximately USD 2.8 billion.
- In January 2021, the company and Constellium launched Alumobility, a non-profit organization focused on providing innovative implementation-ready solutions to advance the adoption of aluminum automotive body sheet.
- In October 2021, the company established a new operation USD 300 million automotive aluminum finishing in Guthrie, Kentucky which employs over 150 people.
- In May 2022, the company broke ground on a new USD 365 million aluminum recycling center in Guthrie, Kentucky which will serve the automotive market with an annual capacity of 240,000 tons.
