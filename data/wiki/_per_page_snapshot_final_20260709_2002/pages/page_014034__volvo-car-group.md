# Volvo Car Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Volvo Cars continues to invest in South Carolina plant | Volvo Cars Media Intl](https://www.volvocars.com/intl/media/press-releases/ED74D24FBD001BA9/)  ·  `page_014034`

## Facts

- Volvo Cars will continue to invest in its US car plant in Ridgeville outside Charleston, South Carolina, with the aim of reaching full utilisation of the factory in coming years.
- Volvo Cars has already invested USD 1.3 billion in the plant in the last decade to make it ready for the future.
- Through these investments, Volvo Cars intends to deliver both volume and financial growth as well as using the plant’s capacity.
- The Ridgeville plant currently has an installed production capacity of 150,000 cars per year.
- Together with its factories in Europe and China, the South Carolina facility plays an important part in Volvo Cars’ future growth plans and its increased focus on regional tailoring of products.
- The South Carolina facility currently produces the fully electric Volvo EX90 SUV, as well as the Polestar 3.
- A first step towards better capacity utilisation was announced earlier this year, when Volvo Cars said it will introduce its best-selling XC60 mid-size SUV to the South Carolina production line from late 2026 onwards.
- The company has sold over 27,000 XC60s in the US in the first eight months of 2025, an increase of almost 20 per cent compared to the same period in 2024.
- Håkan Samuelsson, chief executive, stated that the investment plans reinforce Volvo Cars’ long-term commitment to the US market and its manufacturing operations in South Carolina.
- Volvo Cars is celebrating 70 years of presence in the United States this year.
- The company has sold over 5 million cars in the US and plans to sell many more in years to come.
- Henry McMaster, Governor of South Carolina, stated that Volvo Cars' decision to further invest in Ridgeville reinforces South Carolina's position as a national leader in automotive manufacturing and highlights confidence from top global companies.
- Volvo Cars broke ground on its US manufacturing plant in 2015.
- The Ridgeville facility is a high-capacity plant with advanced capabilities in terms of multiple platforms, technologies and models.
- Before 2030, Volvo Cars plans to add a new, next-generation hybrid model to the production line of the Ridgeville plant tailored to specific US market demands.
- Luis Rezende, Volvo Cars’ president of Americas, stated that adding another model to the Charleston plant is foundational to their strategic growth plan for the US and will accelerate progress toward commercial targets.
- Volvo Cars has adopted a regionalisation strategy that applies a more focused approach for each sales region regarding product, technology, manufacturing and commercial operations.
- For the full year 2024, Volvo Car Group recorded a core operating profit of SEK 27 billion.
- Volvo Car Group's revenue in 2024 amounted to an all-time high of SEK 400.2 billion, while global sales reached a record 763,389 cars.
- Volvo Cars was founded in 1927 and is listed on the Nasdaq Stockholm exchange under the ticker “VOLCAR B”.
- Volvo Cars aims to become a fully electric car maker and targets net-zero greenhouse gas emissions by 2040 through an ongoing reduction of its carbon footprint.
- As of December 2024, Volvo Cars employed approximately 42,600 full-time employees globally.
- Volvo Cars' head office, product development, marketing and administration functions are mainly located in Gothenburg, Sweden.
- Volvo Cars operates production plants in Gothenburg, Ghent (Belgium), South Carolina (US), Chengdu, Daqing and Taizhou (China).
- The company also has R&D and design centres in Gothenburg and Shanghai (China).
