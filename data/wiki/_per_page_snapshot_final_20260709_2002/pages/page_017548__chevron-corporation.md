# Chevron Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2023-10-23

**Source:** [KeyfactsEnergy](https://www.keyfactsenergy.com/news/24636/view/)  ·  `page_017548`

## Facts

- Chevron announced an expected organic capital expenditure range of $15.5 to $16.5 billion for consolidated subsidiaries and an affiliate capital expenditure budget of approximately $3 billion for 2024.
- Approximately $2 billion of the upstream and downstream budgets is designated as lower carbon capital expenditure to lower the carbon intensity of traditional operations and grow new energy business lines.
- Chevron’s Geismar renewable diesel expansion project is expected to start-up in 2024.
- Chevron Chairman and CEO Mike Wirth stated that the company is maintaining capital discipline in both traditional and new energies, with investments expected to underpin durable free cash flow growth to support returning more cash to shareholders.
- Following the acquisition of PDC Energy, Chevron announced an annual capital expenditure guidance range of $14 to $16 billion through 2027.
- On October 23, 2023, Chevron announced it had entered into an agreement to acquire Hess Corporation, expected to close in the first half of 2024 subject to shareholder and regulatory approvals.
- Following the closing of the Hess acquisition, Chevron’s annual capital expenditure budget is expected to increase to between $19 and $22 billion.
- Chevron aims to grow its traditional oil and gas business while lowering carbon intensity and growing new lower carbon businesses in renewable fuels, hydrogen, carbon capture, offsets and other emerging technologies.
