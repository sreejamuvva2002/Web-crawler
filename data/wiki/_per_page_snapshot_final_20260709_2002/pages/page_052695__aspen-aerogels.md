# Aspen Aerogels

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2029-08-19

**Source:** [Aspen Closes on $125MM Term Loan Facility and $100MM Credit Facility with MidCap - ABF Journal](https://www.abfjournal.com/aspen-closes-on-125mm-term-loan-facility-and-100mm-credit-facility-with-midcap/)  ·  `page_052695`

## Facts

- Aspen Aerogels, a technology company in sustainability and electrification solutions, closed on a $125 million term loan facility and $100 million capacity asset-based revolving credit facility with MidCap Financial.
- MidCap Financial is a commercial finance company managed by Apollo Capital Management, focused on middle market transactions.
- The collateral structure of the financing agreement explicitly excludes the assets of Aspen’s wholly-owned subsidiary, Aspen Aerogels Georgia.
- The excluded collateral specifically covers the company’s planned second aerogel manufacturing facility in Statesboro, Georgia.
- Proceeds from the transaction will be utilized for the full cash redemption of the company’s legacy convertible note and for general corporate purposes.
- The term loan has a maturity date of Aug. 19, 2029.
- Aspen Aerogels president and CEO Donald R. Young stated MidCap Financial is an outstanding partner for building a highly valuable and profitable business by leveraging the Aerogel Technology Platform into high-growth markets.
- Young noted the transaction was made possible by Aspen’s dedicated focus on generating near-term profitability.
- Aspen Aerogels CFO and treasurer Ricardo C. Rodriguez stated the combined $225 million debt facilities put the company on a path to further reduce its cost of capital.
- Rodriguez added the financing provides access to additional liquidity to support profitable revenue growth.
- The collateral structure is specifically optimized to fit into the anticipated structure of a previously disclosed potential debt option for accelerating the longer-term construction of the second aerogel manufacturing facility in Statesboro, Georgia.
- Rodriguez stated eliminating the potential overhang of existing convertible debt near or above the conversion share price of approximately $29.94 is an added benefit that can enable value creation.
