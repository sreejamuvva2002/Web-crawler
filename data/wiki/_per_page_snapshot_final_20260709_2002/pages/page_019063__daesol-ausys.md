# Daesol Ausys

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-08-29

**Source:** [South Korean auto supplier plans $72 million plant in Georgia to build electric vehicle parts](https://www.yahoo.com/news/south-korean-auto-supplier-plans-221903671.html)  ·  `page_019063`

## Facts

- Plans to build a $72 million factory in Georgia to make parts for electric vehicles.
- Will hire more than 140 workers.
- Announced plans on Tuesday (August 29, 2023) to build the factory in West Point, Georgia.
- The new facility will produce luggage boards and covers for compartments in electric vehicles.
- Supplies parts to Hyundai, Kia, and General Motors.
- This will be the third factory in the same West Point industrial park for members of the company's corporate family.
- Daehan Solution Co. opened a $1 million factory in the same industrial park in 2008 to make headliners and other sound insulation parts for the nearby Kia plant.
- Daesol Material opened a $35 million factory in the same industrial park in 2017 to make automotive fiber padding.
- Daehan Solution currently employs 350 full-time workers.
- Daesol Material currently employs 50 workers.
- Meghan Richardson serves as an economic developer for the city of West Point.
- Daehan Solution also operates a plant near Montgomery, Alabama, supplying parts to the Hyundai assembly plant there.
- The announcement follows a wave of EV supplier plant announcements in Georgia after Hyundai announced in 2022 plans to build a $5.5 billion plant to assemble electric vehicles and batteries in Ellabell, Georgia, near Savannah.
- The Hyundai Ellabell site could grow to 8,100 employees and is slated to begin producing vehicles in 2025.
- EV suppliers have pledged to invest nearly $2.2 billion and hire 5,000 people for the Georgia region.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp stated in a statement: 'Our state's automotive industry employs over 50,000 hardworking Georgians and will continue to grow as e-mobility suppliers choose all corners of the state for the jobs of the future.'
- Daesol Ausys is scheduled to begin production at the new West Point factory in December 2024.
- The company will pay workers $15 to $20 an hour.
- The state of Georgia will cover the cost of training workers for the new facility.
- The state provided $500,000 last year to help Harris County clear and grade the site and build a road, which the county matched using sales tax collections.
- The city of West Point and Harris County are granting eight years of property tax breaks worth a projected $5.7 million.
- Daesol Ausys could qualify for $875,000 in state income tax credits, calculated at $1,250 per job over five years, contingent on workers earning at least $31,300 annually.
