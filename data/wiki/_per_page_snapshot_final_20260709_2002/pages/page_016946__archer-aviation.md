# Archer Aviation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-08-01

**Source:** [Archer's eVTOL Aircraft Factory Takes Shape in Georgia  | Aviation International News](https://www.ainonline.com/aviation-news/futureflight/2024-08-01/archers-evtol-aircraft-factory-takes-shape-georgia)  ·  `page_016946`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation has erected the walls of a 400,000-sq-ft factory in Covington, Georgia.
- The Silicon Valley-based aircraft developer plans to mass-produce its four-passenger Midnight eVTOL air taxi at the facility.
- Construction is on track for completion by the end of this year.
- Archer completed the first build phase, involving grading and foundation work, earlier this year.
- Phase two, the construction of the facility’s exterior walls and roof, is nearing completion.
- Archer is working towards completing FAA type certification in time for commercial operations to begin in 2025.
- The manufacturing facility is located at Covington Municipal Airport, approximately 40 miles east of Atlanta.
- The high-volume manufacturing facility will initially target a production rate of 650 Midnight aircraft per year.
- Archer eventually hopes to more than double the factory’s floorspace.
- Archer aims to ramp up production rates to around 2,300 aircraft per year after expansion.
- Archer has partnered with European automotive group Stellantis to help achieve its production targets.
- Stellantis formed in 2021 from the merger between carmakers Fiat Chrysler and Peugeot.
- Stellantis is a major investor in Archer.
- Stellantis has agreed to provide Archer with up to $150 million in equity financing.
- Archer founder and CEO Adam Goldstein stated that the company is tracking towards completing the manufacturing facility by the end of the year.
- Once complete, Archer will focus on ramping production up to 650 aircraft a year.
- Archer's goal is to be the first in the eVTOL industry to achieve scaled manufacturing.
- Last week, Archer showed off an example of the Midnight aircraft to a group of government officials.
- The showcase event was held at Atlantic Aviation’s new FBO at DeKalb–Peachtree Municipal Airport near Atlanta.
