# SK On-Hyundai EV Battery Plant

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-03-15

**Source:** [411 Corridor: This Is Only The Beginning |  | northwestgeorgianews.com](https://www.northwestgeorgianews.com/411-corridor-this-is-only-the-beginning/article_e9e0a77c-f165-11ef-95eb-bbf8a2a928b2.html)  ·  `page_000622`

## Facts

- The $5 billion SK On-Hyundai Motor Group electric vehicle battery plant is anchoring economic development along the 411 corridor between Rome and Cartersville.
- The massive battery plant operates as a partnership between Hyundai Motor Group and SK On.
- The development site is located east of the Floyd/Bartow county line off the north side of U.S. 411 near Bartow Centre.
- The project is expected to add $40 million a year in tax revenue.
- The plant will rival the SK plant located off I-85 in the Commerce area on the other side of the state.
- First-tier supplier businesses to the SK plant need to be within a certain distance radius from the facility, according to Rome Floyd Development Authority Chair Missy Kendrick.
- Second-tier suppliers to the SK plant need to be within about 50 miles of the facility, according to Rome Floyd Development Authority Chair Missy Kendrick.
- The region will continue to see huge amounts of economic interest regardless of the exact distance suppliers need to be from the SK plant, according to Rome Floyd Development Authority Chair Missy Kendrick.
- The Rome Floyd Development Authority purchased property for the Enterprise Corner development on the Bartow County border, which will soon have water and sewer access.
- A $7.9 million allocation for water and sewer access for the Enterprise Corner Development was approved as part of a special purpose local option sales tax referendum passed by voters in 2023.
- Interest in the Enterprise Corner Development saw a significant jump once the SPLOST package passed, as the lack of infrastructure previously inhibited economic development in that area, according to Rome Floyd Development Authority Chair Missy Kendrick.
- West Cartersville is expected to become a huge growth area for the next several years, experiencing both industrial and significant residential growth, according to Bartow County Commissioner Steve Taylor.
- Bartow County is building a 2 million gallon per day wastewater treatment plant on the Etowah River south of Neel’s Landing.
- Plans are already in place to expand the Bartow County wastewater treatment plant to 4 million gallons per day.
- The Bartow County wastewater treatment plant project costs between $70 and $80 million, according to Bartow County Commissioner Steve Taylor.
- There are hardly any large plots of land available near I-75 all the way up to Chattanooga, which is driving interest in the corridor between Cartersville and Rome, according to Bartow County Commissioner Steve Taylor.
- It is difficult to find 100-acre plots of land anywhere near I-75 until reaching Tennessee, making the 411 corridor highly attractive for development, according to Bartow County Commissioner Steve Taylor.
- Floyd, Bartow, and Polk Counties have agreed to join forces to create a joint development authority to work together on large business development opportunities.
- A multi-county development authority functions as an independent government agency designed to promote economic and industrial development across county lines.
- The Gordon-Floyd Joint Development Authority previously served as an example of a multi-county entity responsible for recruiting Synthica Energy to locate in its jurisdiction.
- Rome Floyd Development Authority Chair Jimmy Byars stated late last year that the joint development authority is a great initiative because Bartow and Floyd counties are growing toward each other and the 411 corridor is highly active.
