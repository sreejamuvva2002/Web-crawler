# Pirelli US Plant in Rome, Georgia

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [SUV tyres production: the primacy is in Rome, Georgia | Pirelli](https://www.pirelli.com/global/en-ww/life/innovation/industry/suv-tyres-production-the-primacy-is-in-rome-georgia-49750/)  ·  `page_048429`

## Facts

- Pirelli's US plant is located in Rome, Georgia, approximately 70 miles northwest of Atlanta.
- The first tire at the Rome facility was cured in 2002.
- The plant is one of the smaller facilities in the Pirelli group.
- Production focuses on bigger Premium sizes aimed at the North American premium car and especially the SUV market.
- The Rome plant's production is supported by a new plant in Guanajuato, Mexico.
- The facility employs 226 people, including expatriates from Italy, Brazil, Germany, England, and Venezuela.
- Pirelli chose the Rome location because several of its major customers were moving their manufacturing to the Southeast of the USA.
- Major customers that manufacture in the area include BMW, Audi, Porsche, and Mercedes.
- Pirelli tires are manufactured to be precisely the same in every facility worldwide and are fitted as standard equipment on many European car models.
- The Rome plant produces approximately 450,000 pieces (gross) per year.
- Products include premium tires for cars, light trucks, and SUVs, ranging in sizes from 17 inches up to 30 inches.
- Each tire has an average weight of 17 kg, which is the highest average weight for any factory in the Pirelli group.
- Almost 70% of the plant's production goes to premium carmakers such as BMW, Mercedes Benz, and Cadillac.
- A significant portion of the remaining production is exported outside the USA.
