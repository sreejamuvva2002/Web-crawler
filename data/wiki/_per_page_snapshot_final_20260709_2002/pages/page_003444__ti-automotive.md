# TI Automotive

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2004-07-01

**Source:** [TI Automotive - Hart County, Georgia](https://hartcountyga.gov/tiautomot.html)  ·  `page_003444`

## Facts

- TI Automotive will invest $30 million to build a new manufacturing facility in Hart County, Georgia.
- The plant will initially manufacture blow-molded plastic fuel tanks and filler pipes for vehicles built in nearby southern states by BMW and an Asia-based automaker.
- Groundbreaking ceremonies for the 145,000-square-foot plant took place on July 1, 2004.
- The facility is located in the Gateway High Tech Industrial Park off Interstate 85 at Exit 177 in northeast Georgia.
- Site preparation, including grading and installation of water and sewer lines, began last month at the 25-acre plot.
- Construction was scheduled to begin within several weeks of the July 1, 2004 groundbreaking.
- The TI Automotive plant will initially employ 100 people.
- The facility will produce 350,000 automotive fuel-tank systems annually upon initial operation.
- Hiring will begin in March 2005 with the start of training and prototype assembly.
- A majority of the new employees will be hired locally.
- Training for high-tech operators will be provided by the state’s Quick Start program through North Georgia Technical College.
- Brian Lindsay, TI Automotive managing commercial director for Global Fuel Systems, expects full production of fuel-system components for BMW and the Asia-based automaker in 2006.
- The site was developed through the Joint Development Authority of Hart, Franklin, and Stephens counties.
- Development was funded in part by a State of Georgia Edge Grant of $650,000 provided through the One Georgia agency.
- Officials at the groundbreaking included Craig Lesser (Georgia Department of Economic Development), Mike Beatty (Georgia Department of Community Affairs), Nancy Cobb (OneGeorgia Authority executive director), Mark Lytle and Kevin Langston (Georgia Department of Economic Development), Buddy Glazner (Georgia Power), and Jack Edmunds (chairman of the Joint Development Authority).
- Local officials in attendance included State Representatives Jeanette Jamieson and Alan Powell, Dan Reyen (Hart County Board chairman), Sammy Elrod (Franklin County Board chairman), Hartwell Mayor Matt Beasley, Lavonia Mayor Ralph Owens, and Hart County Administrator John Caime.
- Brian Lindsay noted the warm reception and strong spirit of cooperation for the site selection in Hart, Franklin, and Stephens counties.
- Georgia Governor Sonny Perdue stated that TI Automotive’s investment means more jobs for citizens and demonstrates Georgia is a great place to do business.
- The facility was designed and engineered by O’Neal Constructors of Atlanta, which also serves as the general contractor.
- Paul Darden, O’Neal Constructors vice president, stated the building will use significant glass for natural light, be fully air-conditioned, and feature flexible space with minimal interior partitions.
- TI Automotive will lease the Gateway Park site from the tri-county Joint Development Authority.
- Incentives include state job tax credits totaling $3,000 per employee per year during the first five years of operation, which can be spread over a 10-year period.
- Manouchehr Kambaksh, TI Automotive vice president of Global Advanced Engineering, stated the facility will have two blow-molding press lines with an annual capacity to produce one million fuel tanks.
- The plant will use an innovative blow-molded filler-pipe process, which is a first for a North American automotive supplier.
- This Hart County plant is the first TI Automotive facility to incorporate the assembly of fuel tank components, filler pipes, and tanks under one roof.
- TI Automotive is the world’s leading supplier of fluid storage, transfer, and delivery systems for brake, fuel, and air conditioning applications.
- The company is based in Warren, Michigan, employs over 20,000 people at more than 130 facilities across 29 countries on six continents, and reported nearly $2.5 billion in sales in 2003.
