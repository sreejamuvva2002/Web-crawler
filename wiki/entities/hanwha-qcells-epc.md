# Hanwha Qcells EPC

**Type:** Company  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[battery-cell-manufacturing|Battery Cell Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** Dalton, Georgia, Georgia, United States

## Overview

Hanwha Qcells EPC (Qcells) is a U.S.-based solar panel manufacturer and turnkey utility-scale developer that has partnered with LG Energy Solution Vertech to deliver up to 5 GWh of American-made lithium-ion energy storage projects across the United States.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Domestic solar panel production and battery storage manufacturing support electric-vehicle adoption by providing clean electricity and locally sourced lithium-ion cells for charging infrastructure.

## Timeline

- **2022** — LG Energy Solution purchased NEC Energy Solutions and established LG Energy Solution Vertech Inc.
- **May 2024** — Qcells entered into battery purchase agreements with LG Energy Solution Vertech totaling 4.8 GWh for U.S. energy storage projects.
- **2024** — Hanwha Qcells and LG Energy Solution Vertech sign a multiyear agreement to deliver up to 5 GWh of utility-scale lithium-ion energy storage projects.
- **2028-2030** — Qcells EPC’s engineering, procurement, and construction (EPC) projects scheduled for delivery.
- **End of 2026** — LG Energy Solution plans to ramp battery cell production to around 50 GWh across North America.

## Key facts

- The partnership includes up to 5 GWh of lithium-ion energy storage projects for utility-scale sites.
- Qcells operates the largest solar panel manufacturing facility in the United States, located in Dalton, Georgia.
- Qcells is building a second factory in Cartersville, Georgia to produce ingots, wafers, cells and finished panels.
- When fully operational, Qcells’ annual solar panel production capacity will reach 8.4 GW-enough to power more than 1.3 million American homes each year.
- LG Energy Solution manufactures up to 16.5 GWh of battery energy storage cells in Holland, Michigan, with plans to increase to ~50 GWh by the end of 2026.
- The agreement supports U.S. domestic content requirements and creates manufacturing jobs across the supply chain.
- "Qcells is proud to partner with LG Energy Solution on a project that shows what’s possible when energy development supports U.S. manufacturing," said Chris Hodrick, CEO of Qcells EPC.
- "We’re excited to continue our partnership with Qcells as we begin a new phase together," said Jaehong Park, CEO and President of LG Energy Solution Vertech.
