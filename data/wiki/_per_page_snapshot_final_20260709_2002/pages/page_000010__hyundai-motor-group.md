# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-05-20

**Source:** [NEWS: Sen. Ossoff Helps Secure Historic Korean Investment in Georgia Electric Vehicle Manufacturing – U.S. Senator for Georgia Jon Ossoff](https://www.ossoff.senate.gov/press-releases/news-sen-ossoff-helps-secure-historic-korean-investment-in-georgia-electric-vehicle-manufacturing/)  ·  `page_000010`

## Facts

- Following months of behind-the-scenes work by U.S. Senator Jon Ossoff, Hyundai Motor Company formally announced a historic new electric vehicle and electric vehicle battery manufacturing plant in Bryan County, Georgia.
- According to Hyundai, the investment amount is $5.54 billion.
- The investment will create roughly 8,100 jobs in Georgia.
- Sen. Ossoff was widely credited for helping broker a deal between SK Innovation and LG in April 2021 to save an electric vehicle battery production plant in Commerce, Georgia.
- According to reports, Hyundai will partner with SK to power select Hyundai EVs with Georgia-made SK batteries.
- Hyundai Chairman Euisun Chung thanked Sen. Ossoff for his work and support.
- Over the last year, Sen. Ossoff worked closely with Hyundai Chairman Euisun Chung and other Hyundai officials to urge further investment in Georgia and secure this deal.
- The deal followed a settlement between SK Innovation and LG that saved the $2.6 billion SK electric vehicle battery plant in Commerce, Georgia.
- Sen. Ossoff led an economic delegation to Seoul last November with a goal of securing additional investment in Georgia.
- During the delegation, he met with Hyundai Chairman Euisun Chung and top officials, as well as other key Korean companies including SK, LG, and Hanwha.
- Hanwha operates the Q Cells solar module manufacturing plant in Dalton, Georgia.
- Sen. Ossoff stated that attracting Hyundai’s investment in Georgia was a key goal of his economic delegation to South Korea last fall.
- He noted the multibillion-dollar opportunity will create thousands of jobs and grow Georgia’s reputation as a world leader in automotive and clean energy manufacturing.
- Hyundai Motor Group Executive Chair Euisun Chung stated that Hyundai is proud to share its plan to open its first dedicated full EV and battery manufacturing facilities in the U.S.
- Chung noted that the U.S. holds an important place in Hyundai's global strategy and expressed excitement to partner with the State of Georgia for electrified mobility and sustainability.
- He appreciated the continued support of Governor Brian Kemp, Senators Jon Ossoff and Raphael Warnock, and other Georgia elected officials.
- According to Hyundai, the EV and battery manufacturing plant will be located on a dedicated 2,923-acre site in Bryan County, Georgia.
- The site has immediate access to I-95 and I-16 highways, providing easy access to 250 major metro areas.
- The site is located less than 50 kilometers from the Port of Savannah.
- The Port of Savannah is the single-largest and fastest-growing container terminal in the U.S.
- The port has two Class I rail facilities on-site provided by Georgia Central Railway.
- West Point, Georgia, is already home to Kia’s manufacturing hub in the U.S.
- Sen. Ossoff has long championed making Georgia the clean energy manufacturing hub in the United States and making the U.S. the world leader in clean energy manufacturing.
- Sen. Ossoff worked personally with Hyundai Chairman Euisun Chung and top executives over the last several months to secure this investment in Georgia.
- Last November, Sen. Ossoff led an economic delegation to the Republic of Korea where he met with top executives, including Hyundai Chairman Euisun Chung and other Hyundai officials.
- During their meeting, Sen. Ossoff and Hyundai executives discussed further investments in clean energy technology manufacturing in Georgia.
- The possibility of further investments became more attractive thanks to the SK electric vehicle battery plant in Commerce, which Sen. Ossoff helped save.
- After being widely credited for helping negotiate a settlement between SK Innovation and LG last April that saved the 2,600-job electric vehicle battery plant in Commerce, Sen. Ossoff began courting additional Korean foreign direct investment in Georgia and the United States.
- Hyundai and SK announced a joint venture last year.
- In the weeks and months after Sen. Ossoff’s economic delegation, he and his staff remained in close contact with Hyundai officials in Korea and the U.S. to urge them to choose Georgia for their historic investment.
