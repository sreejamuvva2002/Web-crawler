# Bosch Hydrogen Powertrain Systems (Chongqing) Co. Ltd.

## Facts

- Bosch and Qingling Motors established a joint venture in China named Bosch Hydrogen Powertrain Systems (Chongqing) Co. Ltd.
- The new company will develop, assemble, and market fuel cell systems, called Fuel Cell Power Modules, for the Chinese market.
- The goal is to pool the technology and market expertise of both partners and contribute to the development of the Chinese fuel cell market and the transformation of the automotive industry there.
- According to the Energy Saving and New Energy Vehicle Technology Roadmap 2.0 by the China Society of Automotive Engineers (China-SAE), more than one million vehicles with fuel cell drive systems could be registered in China by 2030.
- The joint venture aims to provide all Chinese vehicle manufacturers with fuel cell systems.
- Components required for the systems, such as the fuel cell stack, air compressor with power electronics, and control unit with sensors, come from Bosch, mainly from the plant in Wuxi.
- Small-scale production of these components will start at the Wuxi plant this year.
- In 2021, a test fleet of 70 Qingling trucks equipped with Bosch's Fuel Cell Power Module will hit the road.
- The market launch of the fuel cell system is planned for 2022/2023.
- Mobility is the largest Bosch Group business sector, generating sales of 55.8 billion euros in 2025 and contributing around 61 percent of total sales.
- Bosch Group employs roughly 413,000 associates worldwide as of December 31, 2025, with sales of 91 billion euros in 2025.
- Bosch's operations are divided into four business sectors: Mobility, Industrial Technology, Consumer Goods, and Energy and Building Technology.
- Bosch employs some 82,000 associates in research and development.
- The Bosch Group comprises Robert Bosch GmbH and its roughly 500 subsidiary and regional companies in over 60 countries.
