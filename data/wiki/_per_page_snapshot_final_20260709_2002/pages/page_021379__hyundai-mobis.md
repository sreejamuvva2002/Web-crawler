# Hyundai Mobis

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** power_electronics  ·  **Published:** 2022-11-23

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp: Second Global Automotive Supplier for Hyundai Metaplant To Create 1,500 Jobs - Richmond Hill Lifestyle](https://livingrichmondhillga.com/gov-kemp-second-global-automotive-supplier-for-hyundai-metaplant-to-create-1500-jobs/)  ·  `page_021379`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Hyundai Mobis plans to invest $926 million in an Electric Vehicle (EV) Power Electric system plant in Bryan County.
- The new plant will eventually employ at least 1,500 people.
- The EV Power Electric system plant will expand Hyundai Mobis’ current footprint in Georgia.
- Hyundai Mobis has operated a manufacturing facility with almost 1,200 employees in West Point since 2009.
- The West Point facility is the largest Tier 1 supplier to Kia Georgia.
- The West Point plant supplies automotive manufacturers with complete cockpit modules, chassis modules, and bumper assemblies.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that the recent groundbreaking of Hyundai’s new EV and battery manufacturing facility in Bryan County was expected to unleash transformational job creation and investment in the region.
- Governor Kemp noted this announcement marks the second supplier to locate in the area in just two weeks, signaling a growing list of future job creators.
- H.S. Oh, Vice President of Hyundai Mobis Electric Powertrain Business Unit, stated the investment reflects an acceleration in the development of the EV supply chain in Georgia’s auto industry.
- Hyundai Mobis plans to become a major production player in the EV market and provide high-quality work opportunities to the growing local workforce.
- Once at full production, the new facility will be 1,200,000 square feet.
- The facility will supply over 900,000 EV Power Electric systems annually to Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) in Bryan County, Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Alabama (HMMA) in Montgomery, and the Kia Georgia plant.
- The facility will also supply 450,000 Integrated Charging Control Units annually to the same customers.
- Construction on the new facility is expected to begin as early as January 2023.
- Production at the new facility is expected to begin in 2024.
- The new facility will be located at the Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) Certified Belfast Commerce Park in Richmond Hill.
- Jon Seagraves, Chairman of the Development Authority of Bryan County, noted the site was purchased about two years ago and highlighted its proximity to available housing in Heartwood, a top-performing public school system, interstate access, and rail.
- The project received support and partnership from Bryan County, the City of Richmond Hill, and Raydient Places.
- Alyce Thornhill, Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy, represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on the project.
- The GDEcD partnered with the Development Authority of Bryan County, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia Power for this competitive project.
- Pat Wilson, GDEcD Commissioner, stated that in May they predicted non-affiliated HMGMA suppliers would invest an additional $1 billion into Georgia, a prediction already blown past in about two weeks.
- Since 2020, more than 30 EV-related projects have contributed over $13.3 billion in investment and announced more than 18,100 jobs in Georgia.
- On October 25, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America officially broke ground on its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility in Bryan County.
- Offsite suppliers connected to the HMGMA project are predicted to invest approximately $1 billion in other locations around Georgia.
