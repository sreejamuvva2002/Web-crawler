# Hyundai Mobis

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** power_electronics  ·  **Published:** 2022-11-23

**Source:** [Second Global Automotive Supplier for Hyundai Metaplant To Create 1,500 Jobs | Development Authority of Bryan County](https://bryancountyga.com/news/article/second-global-automotive-supplier-for-hyundai-metaplant-to-create-1500-jobs)  ·  `page_001090`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Hyundai Mobis plans to invest $926 million in an Electric Vehicle (EV) Power Electric system plant in Bryan County.
- The new plant will eventually employ at least 1,500 people.
- Hyundai Mobis currently operates a manufacturing facility with almost 1,200 employees in West Point since 2009.
- The existing West Point facility is the largest Tier 1 supplier to Kia Georgia.
- The existing facility supplies automotive manufacturers with complete cockpit modules, chassis modules, and bumper assemblies.
- H.S. Oh, Vice President of Hyundai Mobis Electric Powertrain Business Unit, stated the investment reflects an acceleration in the development of the EV supply chain in Georgia’s auto industry.
- The new facility will be 1,200,000 square feet.
- At full production, the facility will supply over 900,000 EV Power Electric systems and 450,000 Integrated Charging Control Units annually to Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America in Bryan County, Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Alabama in Montgomery, and the Kia Georgia plant.
- Construction on the new facility is expected to begin as early as January 2023.
- Production at the new facility is expected to begin in 2024.
- The facility will be located at the Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) Certified Belfast Commerce Park in Richmond Hill.
- Jon Seagraves, Chairman of the Development Authority of Bryan County, noted the site's proximity to available housing in Heartwood, a top-performing public school system, interstate access, and rail.
- Alyce Thornhill, Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy, represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The project is being developed in partnership with the Development Authority of Bryan County, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia Power.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that in May they predicted non-affiliated HMGMA suppliers would invest an additional $1 billion into Georgia, a prediction that was blown past in about two weeks.
- Since 2020, more than 30 EV-related projects have contributed over $13.3 billion in investment and announced more than 18,100 jobs in Georgia.
- On October 25, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America officially broke ground on its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility in Bryan County.
- Offsite suppliers connected to the HMGMA project are predicted to invest approximately $1 billion in other locations around the state.
