# TEKLAS

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2020-11

**Source:** [Electric Vehicle Manufacturer TEKLAS to Open North American Headquarters in Georgia, Create 120 Jobs in Calhoun - Augusta CEO](https://augustaceo.com/news/2020/11/electric-vehicle-manufacturer-teklas-open-north-american-headquarters-georgia-create-120-jobs-calhoun/)  ·  `page_000454`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that TEKLAS, a Turkish advanced research and development, manufacturing company and supplier of electric vehicle (EV) parts, will invest $6.5 million in opening their first North American facility and headquarters in Gordon County.
- The project will deliver 120 jobs to Calhoun.
- Founded in Turkey in 1971, TEKLAS designs, develops, and manufactures fluid circulation systems for cooling and heating applications.
- TEKLAS produces hoses and pipes from rubber, plastic, and metal for the global automotive industry.
- The company uses highly innovative and automated manufacturing processes using advanced water molding technology.
- TEKLAS USA will include assembly lines to serve the most prominent original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) in the industry.
- Operations at the new facility are scheduled to begin in the spring of 2021.
- TEKLAS is headquartered in Turkey and maintains locations in Bulgaria, Serbia, Mexico, and China, along with sales offices in Germany and France.
- Regional Director Arcan Ergur stated that the Calhoun location will help the company be closer to its main customers.
- The 200,000-square-foot facility and headquarters will be located at 320 South Industrial Boulevard in Calhoun, Georgia.
- TEKLAS will hire for positions at a variety of skill levels, including advanced manufacturing, management, and supervision.
- Individuals interested in careers with TEKLAS are encouraged to visit the company website for additional information.
- Larry Roye, chairman of the Development Authority of Gordon County, applauded state and local partners who helped encourage the company to come to Calhoun.
- Gordon County Chamber of Commerce President and CEO Kathy Johnson commended TEKLAS for their research and advancements in electric vehicle manufacturing and contribution to the expansion of the automotive sector of the community's manufacturing base.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) Project Manager Fernanda Kirchner represented the Global Commerce division on this competitive project.
- The project was developed in partnership with the Economic Development Authority of Gordon County and Georgia Power.
- The Center of Innovation for Logistics, a division of GDEcD, provided freight analysis to support the project and company planning.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that TEKLAS choosing to establish their North American headquarters and build their very first U.S. advanced manufacturing facility in Calhoun exemplifies Georgia's efforts to attract and grow jobs of the future.
- Wilson noted that as the automotive industry continues to transform toward electric vehicles and e-mobility, Georgia will work to foster an ecosystem of manufacturers and suppliers on the leading edge of the industry’s transition.
