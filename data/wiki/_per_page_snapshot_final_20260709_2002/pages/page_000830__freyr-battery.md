# FREYR Battery

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-11-11

**Source:** [GLOBAL LITHION-ION BATTERY MANUFACTURER TO INVEST $2.57+ BILLION IN COWETA COUNTY - Coweta County Development Authority](https://choosecoweta.com/2022/11/16/global-lithion-ion-battery-manufacturer-to-invest-2-57-billion-in-coweta-county/)  ·  `page_000830`

## Facts

- FREYR Battery, a developer of clean, next-generation battery cell production capacity, announced the selection and purchase of a site in Coweta County, Georgia for its planned Giga America battery plant on November 11, 2022.
- The company plans to create 723 new jobs through the project.
- FREYR Battery will invest more than $2.57 billion in Coweta County through 2029.
- The total investment in Coweta County could be well over $2.57 billion, including planned value-enhancing upstream and downstream modules.
- The new flagship manufacturing plant will be located at the Bridgeport Industrial Park.
- FREYR Battery is based in Luxembourg.
- The company’s battery cells have a variety of uses, including stationary energy storage (ESS) and electric mobility.
- To allow for an expedited start to construction, FREYR is creating a strategy to carry out the project in a phased approach.
- The new project execution plan takes into account the recently passed U.S. Inflation Reduction Act and soaring incremental customer demand for clean, next-generation batteries.
- The project is expected to be developed in multiple phases beginning with an initial battery cell production module of approximately 34 GWh.
- SemiSolidTM technology from FREYR’s American licensing partner, 24M Technologies, will serve as the foundation for the project.
- Lithium-ion battery manufacture can be done at scale with a high level of capital efficiency thanks to the simplified SemiSolidTM platform.
- With local utility providers involvement, FREYR is currently reviewing clean power supply options for the project.
- The long-term goal of the company is to adopt the same approach used at Giga Arctic to create some of the cleanest next-generation batteries.
- Coweta County Board of Commissioners Chairman Bob Blackburn welcomed FREYR Battery to the community, stating that with its addition, Coweta County is quickly becoming a premier destination for top-tier manufacturing industries that support the U.S. economy and beyond.
- The site of the new FREYR facility will be in the Bridgeport Industrial Park, where a mix of heavy and light industrial has been permitted since 2014.
- FREYR chose to invest in Coweta County after an extensive and competitive site selection process.
- The County’s workforce, location, and business-friendly climate helped it stand out from other locations.
- Coweta County has a strong manufacturing workforce with the ability to attract talent from universities and technical colleges.
- The robust transportation infrastructure of highway and rail provides easy access to ATL and the Port of Savannah.
- Coweta County Development Authority Chairman John Daviston stated that collaboration was integral to the success of this project and that teamwork and intelligence sharing among local and statewide stakeholders built credence in the location business case.
- FREYR Battery is looking to fill openings for both administrative, technical, and managerial positions.
- FREYR’s Co-Founder and Chief Executive Officer Tom Einar Jensen commented that expanding into the U.S. has been a foundational aspect of FREYR’s long-term strategy from its inception.
- FREYR Battery aims to provide industrial scale clean battery solutions to reduce global emissions.
- FREYR Battery is listed on the New York Stock Exchange.
- FREYR intends to install 50 GWh of battery cell capacity by 2025.
- FREYR intends to install 100 GWh annual capacity by 2028 and 200 GWh of annual capacity by 2030.
- FREYR has commenced building the first of its planned factories in Mo i Rana, Norway.
- FREYR announced potential development of industrial scale battery cell production in Vaasa, Finland, and the United States.
