# Yamaha Motor Company

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2016-08-05

**Source:** [Yamaha-Yanmar form alliance for US recreational off highway vehicle biz, ETAuto](https://auto.economictimes.indiatimes.com/news/yamaha-yanmar-form-alliance-for-us-recreational-off-highway-vehicle-biz/53555162)  ·  `page_015711`

## Facts

- Japanese automaker Yamaha Motor Company and autoparts manufacturer Yanmar Company announced an alliance for the United States recreational off-highway vehicle (ROV) business.
- The aim of this business alliance is to expand sales in the growing US ROV market by leveraging Yamaha’s technical capabilities and manufacturing know-how alongside Yanmar’s farmer-oriented dealer network in the US.
- The companies will conclude a contract by the end of 2016 for the OEM supply of Yamaha’s multi-purpose VIKING and VIKING VI ROV models to Yanmar starting in December 2016.
- Yanmar will sell these supplied products under its own brand name beginning in January 2017.
- The two companies share values of creating and developing markets through Monozukuri (engineering, manufacturing, and marketing) and have previously collaborated on industrial-use unmanned helicopters, generators, and snow blowers, according to an official statement from the automobile manufacturer.
- Demand for ROVs in the US market has been increasing in recent years, with applications ranging from practical work such as agriculture and dairy farming to leisure-oriented purposes.
- Yamaha launched the VIKING, a 3-seater multi-purpose model, in 2013.
- Yamaha launched the VIKING VI, a 6-seater model, in 2014.
- The two ROV models supplied on an OEM basis will feature an exterior design refreshed to Yanmar specifications through new coloring and graphics.
- The products will be sold under the Yanmar brand and model name as new gasoline engine-powered vehicles.
- Manufacturing of these ROV models will be carried out in the state of Georgia by Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation of America.
