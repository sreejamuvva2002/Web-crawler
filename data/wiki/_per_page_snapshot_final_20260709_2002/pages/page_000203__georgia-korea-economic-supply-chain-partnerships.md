# Georgia-Korea Economic & Supply Chain Partnerships

**Type:** supplier_relationship  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-05-19

**Source:** [Korea Connections: Successful Business Built on Strong Relationships | Georgia](https://georgia.org/blogs/korea-connections-successful-business-built-strong-relationships)  ·  `page_000203`

## Facts

- Strong economic ties between Georgia and Korea are founded on personal relationships between leaders, policy makers, and representatives crucial to building long-lasting global partnerships.
- The State of Georgia recently welcomed a delegation of high-ranking officials from the Korean province of Gyeong sang Buk-Do to discuss future business opportunities.
- The State of Georgia and Korea have a long history of mutually beneficial partnerships, investments, and cultural exchange.
- In March, GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson and Mr. Soo Deuk Sohn (Senior Executive Vice President of KOTRA) celebrated the opening of the new KOTRA overseas office in Atlanta.
- The new KOTRA office is the eighth in the United States and the tenth in North America.
- KOTRA’s overseas offices host teams dedicated to facilitating trade and investment opportunities between host states and Korea.
- The automotive industry has been a cornerstone for the economic relationship between Georgia and Korea, with environmentally friendly and sustainable technology companies from Korea finding a home in the state.
- Relationships developed by Georgia’s international office in Seoul led to the Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia (KMMG) project announcement just six months after Kia requested a special meeting with GDEcD officials.
- Since beginning operations in 2009, Kia Georgia has attracted numerous suppliers including HYUNDAI TRANSYS, Sewon Precision, DAEHAN Solution, and Powertech America between 2009 and 2014.
- Kia’s relationships in Georgia span more than a dozen local entities, with state partners including the Departments of Labor, Transportation, and Community Affairs, the Georgia Ports Authority, the Attorney General’s office, and the state’s technical college system.
- The Technical College System of Georgia’s Quick Start program partnered with Kia to construct and operate the 70,000-square-foot Kia Georgia Training Center for customized advanced industrial training.
- Quick Start developed a maintenance certification program at West Georgia Technical College to help students fine-tune industrial maintenance knowledge and open employment opportunities with large manufacturers like Kia Georgia.
- Kia Georgia sponsors local school system STEM programs as well as Troup County’s THINC College and Career Academy, a model facility for exploring automotive and manufacturing career opportunities.
- The Ray, a section of West Georgia’s I-85 serving as a proving ground for evolving transportation technology, is a community project supported by Kia Georgia.
- As part of the corridor, Kia Georgia sponsors a free solar panel charging center and tire inspection center at the state-operated West Point Visitor Information Center.
- Kia Georgia is a proud inaugural partner in Georgia Made™, a program requiring 50% of the final product to be manufactured in Georgia that provides logistics, manufacturing, and marketing support to qualifying companies.
- In 2018, the announcement that SK On would manufacture EV batteries in Jackson County attracted global electric mobility suppliers like Duckyang, Enchem Ltd, TEKLAS, GEDIA, and others.
- SKC, a subsidiary of SK Group, located in Georgia in 1996 as the first South Korean company to establish a manufacturing plant in the state.
- SKC’s original Newton County facility became one of the major polyester films (PET film) producers in North America, providing high-quality, environmentally friendly service as a base-film market leader.
- In 2021, SKC and several business partners announced plans to manufacture glass-based substrates for semiconductor chips in Covington with an investment of more than $473 million to create more than 400 new jobs.
- Dr. Sung Jin Kim, SKC Director of New Business Development, stated that Georgia will be a basecamp for SKC’s AI and high-speed data center semiconductor applications.
- Georgia Tech played a key role in the venture through research collaboration with SKC over the last decade and a half on advanced circuitry research and methods for packaging fragile technology as part of its Packaging Research Center, launched in 1993 by Georgia Research Alliance Eminent Scholar Rao Tummala.
- In addition to the Covington location, SK On is investing nearly $2.6 billion in two battery manufacturing facilities in Jackson County to supply electric vehicles including the Ford F-150 Lightning.
- Founded in 1976, SK Group is one of the largest conglomerates in South Korea, and its new facility represents the third major investment by SK Group in the State of Georgia.
- Qcells was attracted to Georgia’s emerging EV ecosystem after SK On's 2018 announcement and played a role in securing new investments in the state.
- During its nationwide site-selection process, Meta Platforms Inc. announced it would require 100% new renewable energy sources to power its planned $1 billion state-of-the-art facility.
- In 2018, Meta announced its choice of Stanton Springs, Georgia for the facility.
- A large part of Meta’s decision to locate in Newton County was that Georgia’s utility providers had found a way to meet the renewable energy requirement using solar panels manufactured at Qcells plant in Dalton just up I-20 and I-75, which is the largest solar panel manufacturing facility in the western hemisphere.
- The solar panels were installed and maintained in south Georgia’s Early County.
- Qcells’ 300,000-square-foot facility in Dalton employs more than 700 local workers and produces 1.7 GW of solar module capacity annually.
- The Qcells facility opened in 2019 and produces 12,000 panels a day.
- Leadership from the State of Georgia and Qcells agreed that Georgia was positioned to continue growing as a leader in the solar and renewable power industries.
- Creating a local supply chain that can power an industry ecosystem is important to attracting opportunities, and Qcells has become a key source of renewable energy options for new or expanding businesses.
- The State of Georgia hosts a Korean consular presence since 1971 and maintains continuous representation in Seoul since 1985.
- The state has a dedicated Director of Korean Investment based in the State of Georgia.
- In 2019, Governor Kemp made an economic development mission to Korea his very first as Governor.
