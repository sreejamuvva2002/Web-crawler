# Ascend Elements

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2026-04-14

**Source:** [Ascend Elements Files Chapter 11 to Bolster Finances - Battery-Tech Network](https://battery-tech.net/battery-markets-news/ascend-elements-files-chapter-11-to-bolster-finances/)  ·  `page_052560`

## Facts

- Ascend Elements, a lithium-ion battery recycling company based in Westborough, Massachusetts, has filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy protection.
- On April 9, President and CEO Linh Austin stated in a LinkedIn post that the Chapter 11 filing aims to place the company on firmer financial footing, maximize stakeholder value, and allow operations to continue under existing management.
- Linh Austin was appointed CEO in March 2025 and has acknowledged the company's history of fiscal and operational challenges since taking the role.
- The company demonstrated commercial-scale production at its Base 1 facility in Covington, Georgia, but CEO Linh Austin stated that financial difficulties were too great to overcome without restructuring despite assembling an experienced team.
- Ascend's Base 1 plant in Covington, Georgia, began operations in 2022 and has a capacity to process 30,000 metric tons of end-of-life lithium-ion batteries and manufacturing scrap annually.
- Since late 2022, Ascend has been constructing its Apex 1 facility on a 140-acre site in Hopkinsville, Kentucky.
- The Base 1 and Apex 1 projects together represent more than $1 billion in investment.
- Ascend secured a grant offer of up to $320 million from Poland’s Ministry of Economic Development and Technology for a precursor cathode active material (pCAM) plant in Europe called Apex 2.
- Founded in 2015, Ascend has scaled its patented Hydro-to-Cathode synthesis process to recover and manufacture NMC (nickel, manganese, cobalt) precursor cathode active material (pCAM) and cathode active material (CAM).
- Ascend also produces lithium carbonate and various sulfate products from black mass.
- To date, Ascend has raised over $1.1 billion from investors and received grants from the U.S. Department of Energy under the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law.
- Ascend previously returned a $164 million DOE grant for CAM infrastructure at Apex 1 due to changing market conditions and experienced a partial cut of a remaining $316 million DOE award in late 2025.
- Ascend has pursued alternative funding sources to bridge the financial gap left by the returned and reduced DOE grants.
- In 2025, Ascend reported strong momentum, including a take-or-pay lithium carbonate offtake agreement with Trafigura, successful commercial-scale production in Georgia, and a multiyear supply contract with a leading automaker.
- Ascend's Apex 2 project was recognized as Manufacturing Investment of the Year by the Polish Investment and Trade Agency in early 2026.
- CEO Linh Austin emphasized that the Chapter 11 filing is a restructuring step rather than a relinquishment of the company's mission.
- Ascend remains committed to building a domestic, circular supply chain for critical battery materials and fulfilling existing offtake and customer agreements during the court-supervised restructuring process.
