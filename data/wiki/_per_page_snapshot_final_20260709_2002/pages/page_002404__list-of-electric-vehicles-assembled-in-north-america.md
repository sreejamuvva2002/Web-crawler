# List of Electric Vehicles Assembled in North America

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Alternative Fuels Data Center: Details for Manufacturers Submitting Information on Electric Vehicles Assembled in North America](https://afdc.energy.gov/laws/manufacturer-details-on-electric-vehicles-for-tax-credit)  ·  `page_002404`

## Facts

- The U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) publishes a list of Electric Vehicles (EVs) with Final Assembly in North America.
- The list is based on Fuel Economy label data submitted to the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA).
- The list also relies on Part 583 vehicle identification reports submitted to the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA).
- Manufacturers can use a website contact form to request changes to the vehicle list, such as adding new vehicles or modifying existing ones.
- Requests to change the list must include the vehicle make/manufacturer, model name, model year, electric vehicle status, and country of final assembly.
- Manufacturers must submit or update their Fuel Economy label data to the EPA through the EV-CIS portal.
- Manufacturers must send new or revised Part 583 reports to NHTSA reflecting current electric vehicle makes, models, and applicable final assembly points.
- Submitting updated data is important for DOE to confirm reported information when evaluating requests for the list of EVs assembled in North America.
- The Engines and Vehicles Compliance Information System (EV-CIS) is EPA's database where manufacturers submit certification and Fuel Economy labeling information.
- Under 40 C.F.R. § 600.313-08(a), EPA requires manufacturers to submit Fuel Economy labeling information no later than five working days before the date the model type is initially offered for sale.
- Manufacturers must follow EPA's Fuel Economy labeling requirements under 40 C.F.R. Part 600 to ensure qualified vehicles are included on the list of EVs assembled in North America in a timely manner.
- Under the American Automobile Labeling Act, implemented by NHTSA in 49 C.F.R. Part 583, manufacturers must report information about each model year and carline of new passenger motor vehicles they produce.
- This reporting to NHTSA must be submitted no later than the date the first vehicle of the carline is offered for sale to the ultimate purchaser (per 49 C.F.R. § 583.17).
- Required information for Part 583 reports includes the location of the final assembly point, which encompasses the city, state (for U.S.-assembled vehicles), and country.
- NHTSA defines a vehicle's final assembly point further in 49 C.F.R. § 583.4(b)–4) - (b)(5).
- To expedite review for the list of EVs assembled in North America, manufacturers should include common electric and hybrid vehicle descriptors (EV or PHEV) at the end of each carline name when resubmitting Part 583 reports.
- Adding EV/PHEV descriptors helps NHTSA identify whether final assembly information reported for an entire carline applies to electric or hybrid versions of those models.
- The public can use NHTSA's VIN decoder to confirm the general build location for a vehicle model.
- The VIN decoder's final assembly information relies on build plant data submitted by manufacturers through NHTSA's Product Information Catalog and Vehicle Listing (vPIC) system.
- Manufacturers should ensure build plant data is up to date, especially for carlines spanning multiple points of final assembly.
