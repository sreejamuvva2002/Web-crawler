# Aurubis Richmond Facility

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2024-09-20

**Source:** [Norddeutsche Affinerie AG (ETR:NDA) |](https://www.webdisclosure.com/press-release/norddeutsche-affinerie-ag-etr-nda-aurubis-celebrates-ribbon-cutting-at-the-first-multimetal-recycling-plant-in-the-united-states-ToCNiOnYTy9)  ·  `page_017362`

## Facts

- Aurubis AG celebrated the official ribbon-cutting ceremony for its new multimetal recycling facility in Augusta, Georgia, on September 20, 2024.
- The facility, named Aurubis Richmond, is the first-ever secondary smelter of its kind for multimetal recycling in the United States.
- Construction of the plant required a total investment of around $800 million.
- Groundbreaking occurred in June 2022, followed by a strategic decision at the end of 2022 to fast-track the expansion.
- Following over two years of construction, the state-of-the-art facility is now operational.
- The plant has a capacity to process up to 180,000 tons of complex recycling materials annually.
- Recycled materials will include circuit boards, copper cables, and other metal-containing products.
- Aurubis Richmond will provide over 230 high-quality jobs to the greater Augusta area.
- The company pledged a community benefit agreement with Augusta Technical College, Aiken Technical College, and the Richmond County School System.
- As part of the agreement, Aurubis Richmond will invest in scholarships and job training programs for young workers.
- Georgia Governor Brian P. Kemp attended the ribbon-cutting ceremony to celebrate the facility's economic impact and partnership with the state.
- Aurubis CEO Toralf Haag stated that the plant will keep strategically important metals in the U.S. economy, making American supply chains more independent.
- David Schultheis, President and Managing Director of Aurubis Richmond, described the facility as a major player in the North American multimetal supply chain.
- The new Richmond facility is equipped with advanced technology that meets environmental standards set by both the State of Georgia and federal regulators.
- Aurubis aims to be carbon-neutral well before 2050, aligning the plant's operations with its sustainability mission.
- The recycled metals will supply essential materials to key industries including renewable energy, electric vehicles, and battery manufacturing.
- German-based Aurubis AG is a leading global provider of non-ferrous metals and one of the largest copper recyclers worldwide for over 150 years.
- Aurubis produces more than 1 million tons of copper cathodes annually, along with wire rod, continuous cast shapes, profiles, flat rolled products, precious metals, selenium, lead, nickel, tin, and zinc.
