# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-06-11

**Source:** [| PressReleasePoint](https://www.pressreleasepoint.com/governor-and-first-lady-kemp-reinforce-economic-partnerships-korea)  ·  `page_047710`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp and First Lady Marty Kemp announced they are leading a delegation of state officials on an international mission to the Republic of Korea.
- The mission focuses on reinforcing strategic economic development partnerships.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that the Republic of Korea was the very first country he visited when he became governor due to incredible partners located there.
- Governor Kemp noted that the trip aims to reinforce friendships bringing historic investments and jobs to communities across Georgia.
- Governor Kemp highlighted that Georgia’s connections to Korea go back decades and will further strengthen relationships having a generational impact on the state being No. 1 for business.
- The state delegation consists of President Pro Tem John Kennedy, Representative Soo Hong, and Representative Lehman Franklin.
- The delegation includes representatives from the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD), Georgia Environmental Protection Division, and Georgia Ports Authority.
- Governor and Mrs. Kemp and the delegation will conduct meetings with existing industries operating or under construction in Georgia.
- Industries to be met include Hyundai Motor Group, Kia, CJ Foodville, LG Group, SK Group, and Hanwha Qcells.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that meeting with leadership of existing industries at their headquarters provides a unique opportunity to learn about industry perspectives and future directions.
- Commissioner Wilson emphasized that keeping lines of communication open helps stay ahead of industry shifts and positions Georgia at the forefront of new opportunities.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson said, "Connectivity leads to business, and relationships build the foundations of mutual progress."
- The State of Georgia has maintained continuous representation in Korea since 1985.
- Korea’s Consular Office with jurisdiction across the Southeast, Puerto Rico, and U.S. Virgin Islands has been located in Georgia since 1976.
- Korea has been one of the top sources of Foreign Direct Investment and international job creation in Georgia for the last three years.
- In fiscal year 2023 alone, Korean companies announced more than $10 billion in investments and the creation of 12,605 jobs across the state.
- Total trade between the State of Georgia and Korea totaled over $15.8 billion in calendar year 2023.
- Trade with Korea accounted for nearly 10 percent of the state’s total trade in calendar year 2023.
