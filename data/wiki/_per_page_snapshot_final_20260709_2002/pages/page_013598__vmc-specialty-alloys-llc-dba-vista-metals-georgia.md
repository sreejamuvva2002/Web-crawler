# VMC Specialty Alloys LLC (dba Vista Metals Georgia)

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2024-03-19

**Source:** [News & Press Releases | Vista Metals](https://www.vistametals.com/about/news)  ·  `page_013598`

## Facts

- Vista Metals Georgia announced the completion of its 7th cast-house in Adairsville, Georgia on April 30, 2024.
- The cast-house completion is a significant milestone in the company’s ongoing $100 million long-range capital plan.
- This expansion represents the second phase of the capital plan and includes the installation of melting furnaces, homogenizing ovens, and support equipment at the Adairsville facility.
- Kelly Thomas serves as President of Vista Metals Georgia.
- Kelly Thomas stated that Vista’s investment in the expansion will further support growth in core markets including outer space, commercial aviation, defense, and industrial applications.
- Kelly Thomas added that final equipment installation is underway in a new world-class finishing facility, which is a critical part of this expansion phase.
- On March 19, 2024, Vista Metals announced an investment in a new location to serve the aerospace industry with specialty aluminum products.
- The new facility will be located in Bowling Green, Kentucky.
- The Kentucky facility is part of Vista Metals’ long-range investment plan and will expand upon existing capabilities in California and Georgia.
- The initial investment for the new Kentucky site is nearly $60 million.
- The new site will primarily support downstream processing and value-added products serving aerospace extrusion, forging, and rolling markets.
- In addition to commercial aviation, the Kentucky location will support defense, automotive, and general industrial applications.
- Initial site preparation for the Kentucky facility is underway as of the March 19, 2024 announcement.
- Vista Metals plans to commence a phased build-out at the Kentucky site to support growth in its core markets.
- Kentucky Governor Andy Beshear commented on the continued growth of Kentucky’s metals sector and thanked Vista Metals leaders for their commitment to the state.
- Andy Primack serves as CEO of Vista Metals.
- Andy Primack stated that the company plans to bring over 50 new jobs to the Bowling Green area.
- Andy Primack noted that Bowling Green has a strong industrial business base, a proven track record of attracting qualified manufacturing talent, and close interaction between business and education.
- Kelly Thomas, President of Vista Metals, stated that the Kentucky opening underscores the company’s commitment to the aerospace industry and robust supply chains.
- Kelly Thomas noted that OEMs look to more than double commercial aircraft build rates over the next two decades, prompting investments to enable customers to test and qualify the facility ahead of projected growth.
- VMC Specialty Alloys LLC operates under a licensing agreement with Fontana, California-based Vista Metals LLC.
- Vista Progressive Metals LLC produces aluminum lithium products.
- The group of companies are market leaders in manufacturing specialty aluminum products supporting aerospace, commercial aircraft, defense, automotive, and general industrial applications.
