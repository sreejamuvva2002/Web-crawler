# Neways

## Facts

- Neways develops and manufactures mission-critical electronics for semiconductor, defense, and mobility customers.
- Neways is positioned to double its revenue within five years.
- The MPS and S&OP infrastructure is in place across the group’s operating companies.
- A new Supply Chain Excellence Manager role has been created to focus on continuous improvement, optimizing local processes, sharpening consolidation, and ensuring supply chain performance matches market demands.
- Neways has over fifty years of experience and strong in-house engineering capability.
- Neways serves as a technological partner for microchip production, advanced defense technologies, and electric vehicle charging applications.
- The company employs over 2,500 specialists across multiple countries who design, prototype, and scale up highly complex electronics.
- Products include advanced modules and assemblies, specialized cables, wiring, and cutting-edge microelectronics solutions.
- Since 2021, Neways has made substantial investments in its footprint, capacity, and capabilities.
- Semiconductor demand is accelerating globally, prompting ambitious growth targets for the years ahead.
- The organization values long-term partnerships, technical depth, and accountability over hierarchy.
- Each operating company runs its own MPS and S&OP process, which are already consolidated at the group level.
- The new role aims to drive continuous improvement through sharper local planning cycles, optimized ERP tools, and a consolidated group-level MPS and S&OP.
- The Supply Chain Excellence Manager will connect operating companies, implement best practices, and leverage systems and processes to advance the planning function group-wide.
- One core workstream is programmatic: continuously improving local MPS and S&OP processes across all operating companies by strengthening planning, deepening ERP utilization, professionalizing consolidation tooling, and rolling out best practices.
- The second workstream focuses on the consolidated group-level MPS and S&OP: challenging the order book monthly, flagging capacity constraints early, and providing management information to the Supply Chain Director.
- The ideal candidate requires serious experience in supply chain planning, procurement, and inventory management, along with relevant manufacturing experience.
- The role reports directly to the Supply Chain Director.
- Toine Verbruggen is the Supply Chain Director at Neways.
- A new ERP system has just been rolled out across the company.
- The consolidated S&OP process is currently under active development.
- Automotive and defense are identified as two major market segments that are growing simultaneously for Neways.
- The successful candidate will have a direct hand in defining the S&OP function for the next three years, with monthly visibility at board level.
- Supply Chain Director Toine Verbruggen stated: “A smooth MPS and S&OP is critical in realizing our ambitions, and that starts with someone who connects the processes across our operating companies. You bring the group perspective in, standardize processes, focus on improvements and maintain the rhythm month after month. Get this right, and you will see it in the numbers: faster decisions at the top and a supply chain that keeps pace with the market.”
