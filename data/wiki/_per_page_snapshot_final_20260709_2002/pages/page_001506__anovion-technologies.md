# Anovion Technologies

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2023-05-16

**Source:** [Anovion to build battery material plant in Georgia - electrive.com](https://www.electrive.com/2023/05/16/anovion-to-build-battery-material-plant-in-georgia/)  ·  `page_001506`

## Facts

- US battery materials company Anovion Technologies is building a production facility for its graphite anode material for use in lithium-ion batteries in Decatur County in southwest Georgia.
- The project involves an initial investment of $800 million.
- Anovion is based in Chicago, Illinois, and has been producing anode-grade synthetic graphite near Niagara Falls, New York, and Clarksburg, West Virginia, since 2021.
- In October 2022, Anovion received a grant for a total of 117 million US dollars from the US Department of Energy to expand its production capacities.
- The new plant in Georgia is planned to start with an annual production capacity of 40,000 metric tonnes.
- Information on the schedule for the Georgia facility is not yet available.
- The initially 1,500,000 square foot facility was chosen based on its proximity to existing and planned low- and carbon-free energy sources for power, short supply chains, access to existing rail infrastructure, and a highly valuable skilled workforce.
- Eric Stopka, Chief Executive Officer of Anovion, stated that the company conducted a thorough search to identify a location addressing energy, transportation, logistics, and human capital needs to ensure an ecosystem for producing the highest quality and sustainable synthetic graphite anode material on the market today.
- Eric Stopka noted that existing infrastructure makes Southwest Georgia attractive and that proximity to other battery and EV manufacturing plants will allow Anovion to further reduce its carbon footprint.
- Chip Dunn, Executive Chairman of Anovion, stated the company's long-term strategic vision is to focus on growth and innovation, positioning Anovion as a leader in the US energy transition and securing a domestic source for critical materials required for US lithium-ion battery production.
- Chip Dunn added that from continued research and development in electrochemistry to technology improvements in production processes and climate tech initiatives, the goal is to become carbon-neutral over time, mitigate greenhouse gas emissions, and position Anovion as the climate tech leader of the future.
