# Georgia’s e-mobility and battery ecosystem

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-02

**Source:** [Unlocking Innovation in Georgia’s $27 Billion Mobility Industry - Rome CEO](https://romeceo.com/news/2024/02/unlocking-innovation-georgias-27-billion-mobility-industry/)  ·  `page_010112`

## Facts

- The first Georgia-made electric car is the EV9, which prepares to roll off the line in 2024 at Kia Georgia in West Point.
- Total planned investments in Georgia’s e-mobility and battery ecosystem topped $27.3 billion over the five-year period from 2018 to 2023.
- Georgia is historically a top destination for job seekers looking to reduce living expenses.
- The state works with employers to identify opportunities to attract and train the local labor pool.
- Georgia Quick Start is helping the state meet the demands of the clean energy boom.
- Georgia Quick Start recently unveiled plans to support the training of more than 8,500 Georgians for Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA).
- CNBC ranked Georgia as the No. 1 state for infrastructure in the U.S. in 2023.
- Georgia serves as a global gateway to the U.S.
- The state has two deepwater ports, with one ranked among the fastest growing and the other a top port for automotive Ro/Ro in the country.
- The busiest airport in the world, Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport, is located near the center of the state.
- Infrastructure assets are connected to cities across the state via a seamless network of interstates, highways, and rail lines.
- Reliable energy grids are diversifying Georgia’s energy mix through renewable options, including solar energy and Plant Vogtle.
- The state is supporting the availability of low-cost energy, including solar and hydrogen.
- The state is bringing together energy technology thought-leaders to foster innovative ecosystems.
- Georgia’s mobility ecosystem consists of original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) Kia, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA), Rivian, and Archer Aviation.
- The ecosystem includes battery manufacturers such as SK Battery America and LG Energy Solution.
- The ecosystem includes metal and battery-material recyclers such as Aurubis AG and Ascend Elements.
- The ecosystem includes hydrogen fuel cell manufacturer Plug Power.
- The ecosystem includes synthetic graphite manufacturer Anovion Technologies.
- Georgia’s college and university systems serve as a prime incubator for collaboration and innovation to support the electric mobility field.
- At CES 2024 in Las Vegas, Hyundai Motor Group (HMG) unveiled plans for the Clean Logistics Project.
- The Clean Logistics Project involves HMGMA deploying XCIENT Fuel Cell tractors to manage logistics at the new Metaplant in coastal Georgia.
