# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2022-05-17

**Source:** [Connecting Cultures: The State of Georgia and Korea | Georgia](https://georgia.org/blogs/connecting-cultures-state-georgia-and-korea)  ·  `page_002211`

## Facts

- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson met with Senior Vice Chairman of SK Inc. during a mission to Korea in early March.
- The mission schedule included a tour of Kia Vision Square with Kia leadership.
- The mission also included a meeting with the President of the Korea Trade-Investment Promotion Agency (KOTRA).
- In April, Georgia welcomed a delegation of high-ranking officials from the Korean province of Gyeong sang Buk-Do to discuss future business opportunities between Georgia and Korea.
- Georgia Governor Brian P. Kemp made Korea his first economic development mission as Governor in 2019.
- KOTRA celebrated the opening of its Atlanta office on March 31, which serves as its eighth U.S. office and tenth in North America.
- KOTRA's overseas offices host teams dedicated to facilitating trade and investment opportunities between host states like Georgia and Korea.
- Georgia and KOTRA signed a Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) affirming support for each other’s missions and promoting cooperation.
- Georgia has maintained continuous economic representation in South Korea since 1985, including an office in Seoul.
- GDEcD maintains a dedicated Director of Korean Investment based in Georgia.
- The Republic of Korea established a consular presence in Georgia in 1971, with Mr. Yoonjoo Park serving as Consul General today.
