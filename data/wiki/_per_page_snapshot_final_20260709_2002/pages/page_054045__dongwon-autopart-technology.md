# Dongwon Autopart Technology

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-02-04

**Source:** [200 New Jobs Headed to Emanuel County, FL - Southern Economic Development Council](https://www.sedc.org/news/200-new-jobs-headed-to-emanuel-county-fl)  ·  `page_054045`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Dongwon Autopart Technology plans to invest $30 million in a new production facility in Emanuel County.
- The automotive project is anticipated to create 200 new jobs over the next several years.
- Dongwon Autopart Technology was established in 1971 and supplies components such as doorframes, seat frames, and battery frames to automotive original equipment manufacturers (OEMs).
- The company currently supports more than 500 direct jobs in the Southeast, including at facilities in Hogansville, Georgia, which will remain operational.
- Dongwon Autopart Technology has operated in the United States since 2003.
- Construction on the new manufacturing facility will be located at the Hwy 297 Industrial Park near Swainsboro.
- The company will hire for positions including management, system leads, and production personnel.
- Hiring is expected to begin in 2026.
- Representative Butch Parrish, Chairman of the House Rules Committee, highlighted state commitment to bringing high-quality jobs to rural communities and praised Emanuel County’s collaboration with state partners.
- Emanuel County Development Authority Chairman Will Donaldson welcomed the company and noted the success of local economic development investments.
- Ken Warnock, CEO of the Emanuel County Development Authority, described the project as the single largest new economic development project for Emanuel County in over 30 years.
- Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy Alyce Thornhill represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project alongside partners including the Emanuel County Development Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson noted the automotive industry is expanding outward along the I-16 corridor as suppliers seek strategic locations with access to OEMs and Georgia’s ports, citing Dongwon’s second Georgia location as evidence of over 100 years of robust industry growth in the state.
