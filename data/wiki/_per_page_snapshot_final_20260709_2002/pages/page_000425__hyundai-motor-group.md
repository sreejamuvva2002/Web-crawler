# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hyundai’s EV Factory Draws Three More Suppliers to Georgia | ASSEMBLY](https://www.assemblymag.com/articles/98157-hyundais-ev-factory-draws-three-more-suppliers-to-georgia)  ·  `page_000425`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group’s Georgia supplier list has grown with three additional auto parts suppliers announcing plans to establish assembly plants in the state.
- Hyundai Industrial Co., a subsidiary of Hyundai Motor Group, is spending more than $24 million to acquire a building in Newnan, GA.
- Hyundai Industrial Co. plans to retrofit the acquired facility to produce armrests and headrests for Hyundai’s EV assembly plant under construction in Ellabell, as well as for other OEMs.
- Hyundai’s EV assembly plant under construction in Ellabell has an estimated cost of $5.5 billion.
- The new Hyundai Industrial Co. factory is expected to create 100 jobs.
- South Korean company Hwashin plans to establish a $176 million plant in Dublin, GA.
- Hwashin’s new plant will create more than 460 jobs.
- Hwashin is a Tier 1 supplier that specializes in crafting chassis and body parts for Hyundai and its subsidiary Kia, as well as for other OEMs.
- Production at Hwashin’s Dublin plant is expected to begin in late 2025.
- Daechang Seat Corp. USA announced it is investing $72.5 million to build an assembly plant in Savannah, GA.
- Daechang Seat Corp. USA’s new plant will create more than 500 jobs.
- Daechang Seat Corp. USA specializes in automotive seat frames.
- Daechang Seat Corp. USA will join two other auto parts manufacturers, Seoyon E-HWA and PHA, at the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center.
- The latest investments bring the Peach State an estimated $2.5 billion from a total of 14 suppliers.
- The growing EV network is also creating more than 6,000 jobs.
- Hyundai Motor Group invested additional funds to increase its supplier network amid the ongoing construction of its EV manufacturing campus.
- In September, Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution invested an additional $2 billion toward the $4.3 billion of their joint battery cell manufacturing plant.
- Production at the joint battery cell manufacturing plant is slated to begin at the end of 2025.
- The joint battery cell manufacturing plant will have enough capacity to produce 300,000 EVs annually.
- Hyundai broke ground on its manufacturing campus over a year ago.
- Construction has recently begun on Hyundai’s production-related buildings at the campus.
- The nearly 3,000-acre megasite will use renewable resources to power its mass production.
- Using renewable resources for mass production will help Hyundai reach its goal of becoming carbon neutral by 2045.
