# Electric Vehicle (EV) Market and Supply Chain

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Can Enthusiasm About Electric Vehicles Trigger Global Growth? – Candela Green Energy](https://candela.com.my/can-enthusiasm-about-electric-vehicles-trigger-global-growth/)  ·  `page_050024`

## Facts

- The expansion of the electric vehicle (EV) market has brought with it nuanced opportunities to create a cleaner and transformative new energy carrier.
- Demand for EVs is projected to continue to expand globally.
- Higher oil prices, spurred by the Russian invasion of Ukraine, have made EVs seem suddenly appealing to many car consumers, accelerating adoption globally.
- The US Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) includes $369 billion in renewable energy investment and tax credits for EVs, which should put automakers on a growth path for the next decade or so.
- Sales of internal combustion engine vehicles declined by 8.5% year-over-year during the first half of 2022, while sales of EVs grew by 60%+, according to LMC Automotive, led by BYD and Tesla.
- Bloomberg New Energy Finance (BNEF) projected EV sales to hit 10.7 million in 2022 and 20.6 million by 2025.
- Surges in EV sales are driven by policy support, improvements in battery technology, more charging infrastructure, and new compelling models from automakers.
- Electrification is spreading to new segments of road transport.
- China and Europe will account for nearly 80% of EV sales by 2025, while the US is projected to represent 15%.
- The major gap in EV adoption is supply, with demand so strong that some models are effectively sold out and there are long waits for others.
- Carmakers, battery suppliers, and semiconductor chip manufacturers must build and equip new factories to meet demand.
- The sweeping new US climate bill apportions nearly $400 billion over 10 years to encourage the clean energy transition and the growth of factories.
- Tesla CEO Elon Musk admitted on a recent earnings call that the company does not have a demand problem but a production problem, reflecting an industry-wide focus on scaling operations and improving supply chain management.
- Production is taking place at a Georgia factory spanning 3 football fields, where SK Battery America employees work 24/7.
- The new Ford F-150 Lightning is highlighted as part of an all-around win for manufacturing revitalization, gas savings, and curbing the transportation sector's leading 27% share of US greenhouse gas emissions.
- China’s EVE Energy will supply BMW with large cylindrical batteries for its electric cars in Europe, with contracts signed to be BMW’s primary supplier of battery cells in Europe for new EVs hitting the market from 2025.
- Metals are an important component of EV batteries, with prices rising but expected to trend downward due to new supply coming to market.
- The most common EV battery type uses a combination of nickel, cobalt, and magnesium in lithium-ion cells, while other metals feature in the revolution including graphite, silicon, tin, copper (in charging stations), and aluminium and lightweight steels (in car bodies).
- Long-term prospects stay healthy for commodities like nickel and lithium used in EV batteries.
- At least 40% of critical metals in batteries (lithium, nickel, cobalt, and manganese) must come from the US or a Free Trade Agreement partner to comply with the IRA, rising to 80% in 2026, necessitating more factories within US borders.
- Commodity suppliers have to open new mines and build refineries, with mining companies already reacting to high prices by announcing expansions to stabilize prices in the next few years.
- Charging companies are struggling to install stations fast enough, which is especially important for fleet owners representing more than 2.5 million vehicles on the road and claiming more than $1.1 trillion in annual revenue.
- Tesla is researching methods to cull out multiple uses from battery cells and expanding its R&D in 4680 batteries, including for energy storage to improve range.
- A managed EV battery charging and discharging profile with the national grid, known as the Vehicle-to-Grid system (V2G), is projected to reduce renewable energy intermittency impacts, with Australia’s REVS project serving as an example of commercially available EVs and chargers contributing to energy stability.
- Technological changes in the automobile industry accelerate automated driving development, including a project at Cornell University where researchers developed a way to help autonomous vehicles create “memories” of previous experiences for future navigation in adverse weather.
- The emergence of plastics in EV development is considered pivotal for efficiency and profitability, giving engineers freedom to design various parts and sizes by replacing bulkier materials.
