# Sejong Industrial Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2013-12-31

**Source:** [Sejong Industrial Co., Ltd. Business Report up until FY ended 2013 - MarkLines Automotive Industry Portal](https://www.marklines.com/en/top500/cf/s500_362_hl2013)  ·  `page_059282`

## Facts

- Sejong America, Inc. established Sejong Georgia, LLC in Georgia, U.S.A. to manufacture auto parts for Kia's transplant in Georgia.
- The new subsidiary Sejong Georgia, LLC is capitalized at 974 million won, which was fully funded by Sejong America.
- The Company will invest 27.8 million USD to establish Sejong Georgia LLC. on the former factory-site of Federal Mogul in LaGrange, Georgia, U.S.A.
- The new plant will begin producing automotive muffler and exhaust systems in Oct. 2009 and supply them to Kia Motors' plant in Georgia.
- The Georgia facility will become Sejong's second plant in North America, following the Sejong Alabama plant.
- The Company acquired the remaining 50 percent equity stake that ArvinMeritor had in ArvinMeritor Sejong, located in Alabama U.S.A.
- ArvinMeritor Sejong supplies exhaust systems to Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Alabama (HMMA) and other companies.
- Having acquired 100 percent of the equity in the joint-venture company, Seijong changed the name of the Alabama-based company to Sejong Alabama LLC.
- The Company conducts research and development activities at its Sejong USA R&D Center in Detroit, Michigan, U.S.A.
- Sejong Industrial Co., Ltd. supplies mufflers and catalytic converters for commercial vehicles as a Tier 1 supplier to the Hyundai and Kia Group.
- The Company developed exhaust systems for multiple Hyundai and Kia vehicle models including Sonata, Tucson, Grandeur, Santa Fe, Libero, Genesis, Equus, Genesis Coupe, Avante, i30, Successor model to Tucson, Successor model to New Sonata, Semi large sedans, Santa Fe Facelift, Successor model to Avante HD, Strategic vehicles for the European market, Sorento, Grandeur TG, Opirus, Minivan, and Small car.
- The Company developed exhaust gas purification catalysts and purifying devices compliant with Euro lV and Euro V emission standards under the G7 project.
- The Company developed canning for plasma reaction for exhaust system development promotion projects.
- The Company developed exhaust gas after-treatment devices for heavy-duty diesel engine vehicles compliant to Euro V standard under the Eco-Star project.
- The Company developed technology to predict product life of automotive exhaust manifolds based on studying thermal fatigue levels.
- The Company developed glucose sensors with electrodes made of copper, nickel, and gold.
- The Company developed hydrogen gas leak detectors to detect gas leakage in fuel cell vehicles.
- The Company developed silencers and flexible tubes for fuel cell vehicles, completing the first phase by Oct. 2009 and the second phase by Oct. 2011.
- The Company developed technology to optimize the mounting position of the urea injection device to enhance exhaust purification performance of selective catalytic reduction systems.
- The Company developed hydrogen supply systems based on heat and mass transfer characteristics, completing the first phase by Sep. 2011 and the second phase by Sep. 2013.
- The Company developed variable valves to enhance engine performance and reduce noise, vibration, and harshness.
- The Company developed non fusion joining technology for stainless steel.
- The Company developed regenerated energy application systems to improve vehicle fuel efficiency.
- The Company developed low-cost variable valves.
- The Company developed low-cost hydrogen supply devices for fuel cell vehicles.
- The Company developed sensor systems for exhaust aftertreatment technology.
- The Company developed exhaust gas recirculation systems and exhaust gas purification systems for global automakers.
- The Company is developing blower motors for rechargeable battery cooling.
- The Company developed 6 degrees of freedom (DOF) load cells for exhaust systems.
- The Company is studying electric pulse forming of tailor welded blanks aimed at developing lightweight and high-strength resonators.
- The Company developed hydrogen sensors.
- The Company is developing catalytic exhaust purification devices.
- The Company is developing engine power generating systems for range-extended EVs.
- The Company is developing system control units for hybrid power packs and power converters.
- The Company is studying thermoelectric generation systems to develop automotive exhaust heat recovery systems.
- The Company is developing turbochargers integrated with stainless exhaust manifolds.
- The Company is developing injection modules for urea SCR systems used on clean diesel passenger vehicles.
- The Company is developing converters that meet the Euro6 standards.
- The Company is developing elemental technologies for high-performance green engines.
- The Company is developing pumps for fuel-cell systems.
- The Company announced it had established Sejong Czech S.R.O to supply auto parts to the local facilities of Hyundai Motor and Kia Motors, investing 3 million dollars in the new subsidiary.
- The Company announced the establishment of Sejong Slovakia S.R.O in Slovakia, fully owned by the Company, to supply principally to Kia Motors.
- The Company established a subsidiary, Sejong Rus LLC, in Russia in order to supply auto parts to Hyundai Motor's new assembly plant in the country, which is scheduled to launch production in 2010.
- Sejong Rus started mass production in January 2011.
- In July 2013, the Company established Sichuan Sejong Auto Parts Co., Ltd., a muffler production company, in Sichuan Province, China, capitalized at 1.67 million won with ownership split between SEWOOM (40%), Beijing Sejong Auto Parts (30%), and Yancheng Sejong Auto Parts (30%).
- In July 2013, the Company and the Chongqing Qinchuan Industry Group jointly established Chongqing Qinchuan Sejong Auto Parts Co. Ltd., a muffler production company, in Chongqing City, China, capitalized at 2.22 million won with ownership split between Sejong (10%), Taicang Sejong Auto Part (39%), and Chongqing Qinchuan Sejong Auto Parts (51%).
- Yancheng Sejong Auto Parts' new plant started operations in October 2011.
- Beijing Sejong Auto Parts established its second plant in June 2011.
- In April 2011, the Company established Taicang Sejong Auto Parts Co., Ltd., a joint-venture company to manufacture and sell automotive parts in China, capitalized at 852 million won with 50 percent from Beijing Sejong Auto Parts Co., Ltd. and 50 percent from a Chinese company.
- In December 2013, the Company established Shanghai SJG R&D Center, a research base in China, capitalized at KRW 173 million, fully invested by Sejong (China) Auto Parts Technology R&D Co., Ltd.
- In April 2012, the Company established Sejong (China) Auto Parts Technology R&D Co., Ltd. in Taicang, Jiangsu, China, wholly investing all of the 3,412 million won in capital.
- The Company conducted research and development activities also at its Production Technology Research Center established in February 2009.
- The Company established an R&D center in Suwon City, Gyeonggi-do in July 2008.
- The Company established an R&D center in Ulsan City in June 1995.
- Korea's Ministry of Knowledge Economy announced it will invest over the next year or two approximately 155 billion won in several growth areas, including green cars led by Hyundai Mobis, Korea Electric Terminal Co., Ltd., and Daewon Kangup Co., Ltd., with Sejong Industrial Co., Ltd. participating in application studies on renewable energy alongside Halla Climate Control Corp. and Inzi Controls Co., Ltd.
- Ulsan City announced that the city will promote development of NOx sensors for clean diesel engines that meet the Euro 6 emissions standards, led by Sejong Industrial Co., Ltd., with an estimated investment of approximately 3,132 million won.
- The Ministry of Knowledge Economy of Korea announced it has implemented eight strategic tasks in renewable energy, including commercializing polymer electrolyte fuel cells for transportation vehicles, led by Hyundai Motor with Sejong Industrial Co., Ltd. joining to develop this technology over three years with 14.9 billion won from the ministry and 13.6 billion won from the private sector.
- The Company's FY ended Dec. 31, 2013 sales were 1,093,718 million won, with mufflers & catalytic converters accounting for 1,073,985 million won (98.2%) and body parts accounting for 3,978 million won (0.4%).
- The Company's FY ended Dec. 31, 2013 operating income was 57,470 million won and net income was 46,464 million won.
- In August 2013, the Company announced that its subsidiary SEWOOM will invest approximately 25 billion won to expand production capacity at its Jeongup Plant in Jeollabuk-do, adding 4,160 square meters of building area to be completed in March 2015.
- The Company's R&D expenditure for FY ended Dec. 31, 2013 was 26,593 million won, representing 5.7% of sales.
