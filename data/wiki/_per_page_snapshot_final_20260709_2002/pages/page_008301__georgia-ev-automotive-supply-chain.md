# Georgia EV & Automotive Supply Chain

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-11-01

**Source:** [Georgia Trend Daily - Nov. 1, 2023 - Georgia Trend Magazine](https://www.georgiatrend.com/2023/11/01/georgia-trend-daily-nov-1-2023/)  ·  `page_008301`

## Facts

- F&P Georgia announced a $22 million expansion to its Rome facility due to contracts with automakers Honda and Tesla.
- F&P’s facility is located on 51 acres of the Floyd County Industrial Park in Rome.
- F&P operates as a tier one automotive supplier.
- The Rome plant expansion follows a series of previous expansions at the company’s facility.
- International automotive parts company Hwashin plans to open a new manufacturing facility in Dublin, Laurens County.
- The Hwashin facility will create around 460 jobs.
- The project brings more than $176 million investment to Laurens County.
- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp’s Office announced the Hwashin details via a news release.
- The Georgia Environmental Finance Authority (GEFA) and a coalition of partners announced a $250 million grant from the Department of Energy on Oct. 18.
- The funding supports the Grid Resilience and Innovation Partnerships (GRIP) program.
- The GRIP program aims to enhance the resiliency and flexibility of the electric grid.
- GRIP is a product of the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law signed by President Biden in Nov. 2021.
- The program seeks to ensure the reliability of the power sector’s infrastructure so all communities have access to affordable, reliable, clean electricity.
- Qcells operates a solar panel manufacturing facility in Dalton, Georgia.
- Qcells donated a total of $64,000 to local chapters of the Boys & Girls Clubs in Georgia.
- The Dalton and Cartersville locations each received $32,000 from the donation.
- Gov. Brian Kemp urged Georgia’s congressional delegation to support a request by the Georgia Ports Authority (GPA) for a study of the economic and environmental impacts of another deepening of Savannah Harbor.
- The ports authority is asking Congress to approve the study as part of the Water Resources Development Act federal lawmakers are due to consider next year.
