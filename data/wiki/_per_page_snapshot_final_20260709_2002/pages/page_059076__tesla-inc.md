# Tesla, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-03

**Source:** [Tesla, BYD, or Rivian: Which Stock Currently Has the EV Crown? | The Motley Fool](https://www.fool.com/investing/2026/07/03/tesla-byd-or-rivian-which-stock-currently-has-the/)  ·  `page_059076`

## Facts

- Tesla was previously the undisputed leader in the EV space while competitors tried to catch up.
- BYD is now the world's largest producer of battery-electric and plug-in hybrid vehicles (BEVs and PHEVs).
- Rivian is carving out a niche in the premium electric truck and SUV market.
- In 2025, BYD sold 4.6 million BEVs and PHEVs, representing a 7.7% increase from the previous year.
- BYD vertically integrates by manufacturing many of its own batteries, semiconductors, electric motors, and power electronics.
- This vertical integration provides BYD with greater cost control and the ability to respond quickly to demand changes.
- BYD is expanding aggressively outside China by opening new manufacturing facilities and increasing exports to Europe, Latin America, and Southeast Asia.
- Nearly 16.4 million vehicles were sold in the U.S. last year, making it the world's second-largest automotive market.
- Rivian generated its first full-year gross profit in 2025 while narrowing annual losses and improving manufacturing efficiency.
- Rivian's revenue reached approximately $5.38 billion in 2025.
- Rivian is preparing to launch its lower-priced R2 SUV, which is expected to become the company's highest-volume product.
- Rivian expects to deliver between 62,000 and 67,000 vehicles in 2026.
- Tesla operates more than 80,000 Superchargers worldwide, running one of the world's largest fast-charging networks.
- Tesla's energy generation and storage business is a meaningful revenue contributor, with Megapack battery deployments growing as utilities invest in grid-scale storage.
- Tesla is investing heavily in autonomous driving, robotics, artificial intelligence, and manufacturing automation.
- Tesla generated $6.2 billion in free cash flow during 2025.
- Tesla ended Q1 2026 with more than $44 billion in cash, cash equivalents, and short-term investments.
- Tesla continues to produce more than 1.6 million vehicles annually.
- Tesla's margins have compressed due to intensifying pricing competition, and its delivery growth has slowed.
- Tesla remains profitable while many EV competitors continue to burn cash.
- The EV industry focus has shifted from vehicle volume to building the strongest ecosystem around vehicles.
