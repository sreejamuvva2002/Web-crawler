# Vale S.A.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2025-12-31

**Source:** [Vale Releases 2025 Annual Report highlighting progress in safety, sustainability, and value creation - Vale](https://www.vale.com/w/vale-releases-2025-annual-report-highlighting-progress-in-safety-sustainability-and-value-creation)  ·  `page_012854`

## Facts

- Vale published its 2025 Annual Report, a document that brings together the company’s financial, operational, environmental, social, and governance information for the period from January 1 to December 31, 2025.
- The report reflects the evolution of the former Integrated Report and reinforces a unified view of performance, risk management, and long-term value creation.
- Among the highlights of 2025, Vale recorded USD 38.4 billion in net revenue, USD 15.5 billion in adjusted EBITDA, and USD 4.8 billion in recurring free cash flow.
- Iron ore production reached 336 million tons, the highest level since 2018.
- The Base Metals business saw significant growth, particularly in copper and nickel, strategic minerals for the energy transition.
- Vale completed the implementation of the Global Industry Standard for Tailings Management (GISTM) at all dams and ended the year with no structures at emergency level 3.
- The company achieved 63% completion of the upstream dam decharacterization program and fulfilled 81% of obligations under the Brumadinho Comprehensive Remediation Agreement.
- Vale expanded its circular mining initiatives, producing 26 million tons of ore from circular sources.
- Approximately 60,000 people participated in initiatives to combat extreme poverty in 2025.
- The company exceeded diversity targets, with women making up 28% of the workforce, above the commitment set for 2026.
- Vale voluntarily published the first edition of its ISSB Report in 2025, disclosing financial information related to sustainability, risks, and opportunities prepared in accordance with International Sustainability Standards Board standards.
- Grazielle Parenti is Vale’s Executive Vice President of Sustainability.
