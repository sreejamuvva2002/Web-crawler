# Yamaha Motor Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2026-03-10

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp’s Office Trolls California, Linking Japanese HQ Move to October Trip  - Global Atlanta](https://www.globalatlanta.com/gov-kemps-office-trolls-california-linking-japanese-hq-move-to-october-trip/)  ·  `page_015674`

## Facts

- After 50 years in California, Yamaha Motor Co. Ltd. is moving its U.S. base to Kennesaw.
- By 2028, it plans to complete a slow migration of its headquarters.
- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp hopes the move will be repeated by other companies — perhaps more quickly in the future.
- Yamaha already employs 2,300 people in the Peach State through various entities.
- Some 2,000 employees work at the Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corp. of America factory in Newnan.
- The Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corp. of America factory in Newnan opened 40 years ago.
- Other Yamaha affiliates have followed the company to Georgia over time.
- Gov. Kemp headlined the opening of a new marine innovation center in 2023.
- The governor's office pointed to Georgia’s No. 1 state for business ranking to hint that other firms should follow suit.
- Gov. Kemp stated, “This is another loud and clear testament to what we offer job creators from around the world. To any other California-based companies looking for a better home, we’ll give you plenty of reasons to keep Georgia on your mind.”
- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp’s first trip to Japan last fall included a full slate of company visits and prospect meetings, with the Yamaha headquarters move proving particularly fruitful.
- The governor met with Yamaha executives during his first trip to Japan last fall for the SEUS-Japan alliance annual meeting.
- It’s unclear whether the meeting accelerated the company’s plans to move its headquarters.
- Yamaha said the move was an effort to streamline operations amid tariffs and market changes.
- Kennesaw for many years has been “where all the action is” for Yamaha Motor USA, according to Chief Executive Mike Chrzanowski in early 2025.
