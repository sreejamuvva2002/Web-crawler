# Kumho Tire Co., Inc.

## Facts

- Kumho Tire operates a tire manufacturing plant in Macon, Georgia.
- In May 2019, an administrative law judge with the National Labor Relations Board found that Kumho Tire had engaged in 'pervasive' illegal conduct during a unionization campaign at the company's tire manufacturing plant in Macon, Georgia.
- At least 12 managers, including the company's CEO, issued illegal threats to lay off employees or close the plant if it unionized.
- The company illegally interrogated employees and created an impression of surveillance during the unionization campaign at the Macon, Georgia facility.
- The NLRB required Kumho Tire to read a notice to all workers at the Macon, Georgia plant outlining how the company violated their rights.
- Kumho Tire supplies OEM tires for vehicle manufacturers including Audi, BMW, Chrysler, Fiat, General Motors, Genesis, Hyundai, Kia, Mercedes-Benz, Nissan, and Volkswagen.
- In July 2018, Chinese company Doublestar finalized a deal to acquire a 45 percent stake and control of Kumho Tire from the Kumho Asiana Group for $607 million.
- Kumho Tire manufactures a full range of tires under the Kumho, Marshal, and Zetum brands.
- The company operates three research and development centers, with the largest in Gwangju, South Korea, and additional centers in Akron, Ohio, and Birmingham, England.
- In October 2024, Kumho Tire won the grand prize at the '2024 Global Top Excellent Environmental Technology Awards' for developing low-wear-low-carbon tires to reduce fine dust and carbon dioxide.
- Kumho Tire was previously operated as a business unit of the Kumho Asiana Group.
- Kumho Tire exports tires worldwide and maintains a global network of sales organizations.
