# Volvo Cars

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-23

**Source:** [Volvo Cars to produce new US model amid tariff pressure](https://bluewaterhealthyliving.com/news/business-and-economy/volvo-cars-to-produce-new-us-model-amid-tariff-pressure/)  ·  `page_014169`

## Facts

- Volvo Cars said it will begin producing a new hybrid model in the United States by the end of this decade as the Swedish auto maker continues to adjust to President Donald Trump’s tariff policies.
- The new model will be designed for the U.S. market and help Volvo boost capacity utilisation at its South Carolina plant.
- Automakers worldwide are scrambling to rethink their production strategies to contend with President Trump’s duties on foreign cars, part of his wider policy to boost U.S. manufacturing.
- Volvo Cars’ CEO said in April the company would produce more vehicles in the U.S.
- The group announced in July that it plans to start producing its popular XC60 mid-size SUV at the South Carolina plant from late 2026.
- The factory currently builds only Volvo’s fully electric EX90 SUV and the Polestar 3.
- Production data shows that these models take up only a small fraction of the site’s annual capacity of 150,000 vehicles.
- News of the new hybrid model coincided with Volvo’s 70th anniversary in the United States.
- Volvo, long positioned as an EV trailblazer with the aim of phasing out all non-electric models by 2030, last year reversed course and said hybrids would remain part of its lineup.
- Luis Rezende, president of Volvo Cars’ Americas operation, stated that adding another model into production will take a big step toward realising the full potential of local manufacturing investments and workforce.
- A trade deal struck in July with the European Union cut U.S. tariffs on European cars to 15%, but the agreement has yet to take effect.
- Vehicles continue to face a 27.5% levy while awaiting the new tariff agreement's implementation.
