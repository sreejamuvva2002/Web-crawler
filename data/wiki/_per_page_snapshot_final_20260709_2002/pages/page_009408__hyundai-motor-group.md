# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [page_009408](https://www.cbtnews.com/georgia-gives-1-8-b-in-tax-breaks-to-hyundai-to-build-first-ev-plant-in-us/)  ·  `page_009408`

## Facts

- The state of Georgia and local governments will provide Hyundai Motor Group with $1.8 billion in tax breaks and other incentives.
- The incentives are in exchange for Hyundai constructing its first U.S. plant specifically for electric vehicles.
- The plant will be located close to the port city of Savannah.
- Hyundai is required by the agreement to hire 8,100 people.
- Hyundai is required by the agreement to invest $5.5 billion in its Georgia plant.
- This represents the most significant economic investment in the state of Georgia.
- A few months prior to this announcement, Georgia closed a deal with electric vehicle manufacturer Rivian to establish a factory.
- Pat Wilson, the state’s Economic Development Commissioner, approves of both the Hyundai and Rivian deals and incentives.
- Wilson stated the deals would boost interest in electric vehicles while providing Georgians across the state with reliable jobs in the future.
- The project site is an expansive 2,900-acre location in Bryan County, west of Savannah.
- Hyundai officials and Governor Brian Kemp announced the agreement at the site.
- Hyundai intends to build the facility in the coming year.
- The plant is scheduled to produce up to 300,000 vehicles annually in 2025.
- EV batteries will also be manufactured at the new factory.
- The deal was made public on Friday.
- The Hyundai incentives are worth approximately $300 million more than the incentives Rivian received.
- Georgia and four counties near Savannah are giving Hyundai $228,000 for each new job created.
