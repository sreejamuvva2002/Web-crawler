# Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia, Inc.

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2010-02-26

**Source:** [Kia Celebrates Grand Opening of $1 Billion State-of-the-Art Automobile Manufacturing Plant in Georgia - Kia Georgia](https://www.kiageorgia.com/kia-celebrates-grand-opening-of-1-billion-state-of-the-art-automobile-manufacturing-plant-in-georgia/)  ·  `page_001094`

## Facts

- Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia (KMMG) celebrated its official grand opening on February 26, 2010, in West Point, Georgia.
- The ceremony was attended by officials from KMMG, Hyundai-Kia Automotive Group, the State of Georgia, local city and county officials, and approximately 500 VIPs from Korea and the U.S.
- Attendees included Hyundai-Kia Automotive Group Chairman Mong-Koo Chung, Group President and CEO of KMMG and Kia Motors America Byung-Mo Ahn, and Georgia Governor Sonny Perdue.
- Byung-Mo Ahn stated that Kia is proud to call West Point, Georgia home and looks forward to many years of growing together.
- Byung-Mo Ahn noted that continued diligent work has helped elevate Kia to one of the fastest growing automobile brands around the globe.
- Byung-Mo Ahn stated that with the successful launch of the 2011 Kia Sorento CUV and establishment of their first U.S. manufacturing plant, the Kia brand is well positioned for further growth and to become a worldwide leader in quality and innovation.
- The project represents a $1 billion investment by Kia Motors Corporation.
- The automobile manufacturing plant spans 2.2 million square feet and is situated on more than 2,200 acres of land between Exit 2 and Exit 6 along Interstate 85 in West Point.
- KMMG consists of four main shops: stamping, welding, paint, and assembly.
- The site also includes a transmission shop, a module shop, and a two-mile test track where every produced vehicle is thoroughly tested.
- KMMG’s general assembly area features more than a half mile of height-adjustable conveyors to optimize installation positions and reduce physical fatigue for team members.
- The assembly area is outfitted with wood flooring to provide a more comfortable work environment.
- Georgia Governor Sonny Perdue noted the event continues a mutually beneficial partnership between the State of Georgia and Kia that will benefit the state in years to come.
- Governor Perdue stated that Kia has brought thousands of new jobs to the area and is delivering as a tremendous corporate citizen.
- Governor Perdue pledged to continue providing specialized training through the Kia Georgia Training Center to guarantee a quality workforce and future plant success.
- KMMG currently employs more than 1,200 team members.
- KMMG is expected to nearly double its workforce by the end of the year.
- Onsite and nearby suppliers are expected to create up to 7,500 additional jobs in the region.
- Randy Jackson, director of human resources and administration, highlighted KMMG's role in job creation, community development, and tourism.
- The plant will open its doors to the community beginning in spring for tours to see the quality and craftsmanship of Kia vehicles.
- Mass production of the all-new 2011 Kia Sorento CUV began on November 16, 2009.
- The 2011 Sorento officially went on sale in the U.S. in January 2010.
- The 2011 Sorento sold 7,398 units in its first month, making it Kia’s best-selling vehicle in January.
- Byung-Mo Ahn noted the outstanding early performance of the first vehicle from the West Point plant is a significant achievement for KMMG and the Kia brand.
- Kia plans to continue expanding its line of vehicles at the facility while adhering to brand tenets of value, quality, safety, and technology.
- KMMG is the first manufacturing site in North America for Seoul-based Kia Motors Corporation.
- At full capacity, the plant will be able to build 300,000 vehicles annually using U.S. and globally sourced parts.
