# Mercedes-Benz USA

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-01

**Source:** [Mercedes-Benz USA Reports 2025 Year-Over-Year Retail Sales Growth - Metro Atlanta CEO](https://metroatlantaceo.com/news/2026/01/mercedes-benz-usa-reports-2025-year-over-year-retail-sales-growth/)  ·  `page_046361`

## Facts

- Mercedes-Benz USA reported annual retail sales of 303,200 passenger cars in 2025.
- MBUSA passenger car sales represent a 1% increase compared to 2024.
- Mercedes-Benz Vans reported 40,000 units retailed in 2025.
- Combined total retail sales for MBUSA in 2025 reached 343,200 units.
- Significant demand across the entire Mercedes-Benz SUV model portfolio contributed to growth.
- Continued robust interest in Mercedes-AMG performance luxury models contributed to growth.
- Adam Chamberlain is President and CEO of Mercedes-Benz USA.
- Adam Chamberlain expressed gratitude for employee and dealer partner dedication in navigating an unprecedented year.
- Adam Chamberlain stated the company enters 2026 with a steadfast, customer-centric focus on excellence and commitment to innovation.
- Adam Chamberlain noted the company will mark its 140th anniversary throughout 2026 by introducing new products.
- Mercedes-AMG recorded its best year on record with a 12% increase in retail sales year-over-year.
- The G-Class achieved its most successful year ever, with a 26% increase in sales compared to 2024.
- The CLE experienced 53% growth in 2025.
- GLE SUV sales rose 14% for the year, marking its best retail sales year ever.
- The GLE is proudly manufactured in the U.S. in Tuscaloosa, Alabama.
- GLC sales were up 20% from last year.
- The GLA led the entry segment with a 21% increase in retail sales year-over-year.
- Retail sales for Vans totaled 12,400 units for Q4 2025.
- This represents a 1% increase compared to Q4 2024.
- Mercedes-Benz Group sold 2,160,000 cars and vans globally in 2025.
- Global sales included a 7% surge in Mercedes-AMG deliveries.
- Global sales marked the best-ever sales for the G-Class.
- Q4 global sales reached 558,400 cars and vans, marking the strongest quarter of 2025 (+6% vs. Q3 2025).
- Ola Källenius is Chairman of the Board of Management of Mercedes-Benz Group AG.
- Ola Källenius stated that in 2025, Mercedes-Benz kicked off its biggest product and tech launch program with cutting-edge electric-drive systems and intelligent software.
- The all-new CLA was highlighted as underscoring the depth of quality and innovation.
- Ola Källenius noted that in 2026, 140 years after inventing the automobile, the company continues its launch program to build the world’s most desirable cars.
- MBUSA Passenger Cars Q4 2025 sales were 79,350 units (+8% vs. Q3, -12% vs. Q4 2024).
- MBUSA Vans Q4 2025 sales were 12,400 units (+24% vs. Q3, +1% vs. Q4 2024).
- All reported figures are preliminary and subject to change pending final reports.
- Mercedes-Benz reports retail sales at the market level and group sales at a global level.
- The Top-End segment includes Mercedes-AMG, Mercedes-Maybach, G-Class, S-Class, GLS, EQS and EQS SUV.
- The Core segment includes all derivatives from C- and E-Class, including EQE and EQE SUV.
- The Entry segment includes all derivatives from A- and B-Class, including EQA and EQB.
- Metris and Sprinter gas engines were discontinued for the U.S. market.
