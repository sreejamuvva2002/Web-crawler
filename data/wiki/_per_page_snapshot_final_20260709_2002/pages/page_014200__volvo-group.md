# Volvo Group

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2022-02-24

**Source:** [Volvo Group Invests $41 Million in North Carolina - TT](https://www.ttnews.com/articles/volvo-group-invests-41-million-north-carolina)  ·  `page_014200`

## Facts

- Volvo Group announced a $41 million investment in North Carolina’s Piedmont Triad region.
- The investment will purchase and expand the building housing its U.S. Uptime Center.
- The expanded facility will now also serve as the global headquarters for Volvo Financial Services (VFS).
- Martin Weissburg, chairman of Volvo Group North America and president of Mack Trucks, described the Uptime Center as a strategic site for Volvo Group North America.
- Weissburg stated the center plays a critical role in keeping customers and their vehicles on the road, delivering goods, transporting people, and servicing communities throughout the year.
- The purchase and expansion of the facility reinforces Volvo Group's commitment to the great communities and residents of the Piedmont Triad.
- Volvo Group’s investment includes the acquisition of the Uptime Center building, which opened in 2014 and was previously leased by the company.
- The investment also funds a 62,000-square-foot expansion of the Uptime Center to house Volvo Financial Services’ global headquarters.
- VFS is the captive finance arm of the Volvo Group.
- VFS provides financial services for customers of Mack Trucks, Volvo Trucks, Volvo Construction Equipment, Nova buses, Prevost coaches, and Volvo buses in North America.
- VFS also provides financial services for other Volvo Group brands around the world.
- VFS operates in 50 countries globally with approximately 1,700 colleagues worldwide.
