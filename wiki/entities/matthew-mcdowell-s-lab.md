# Matthew McDowell's Lab

**Type:** Research Center  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[battery-materials|Battery Materials]]  ·  **Location:** Georgia Institute of Technology, Georgia, United States

## Overview

A research group at the Georgia Institute of Technology focused on understanding material behavior and transformations in energy storage devices to engineer improved battery materials.

## EV supply-chain relevance

The lab conducts fundamental research on next-generation battery materials, solid-state battery interfaces, sustainable and low-temperature batteries that are critical for electric vehicle energy storage.

## Related organizations & people

- [[georgia-institute-of-technology|Georgia Institute of Technology]] — Parent university
- Prof. Matthew McDowell — Lab director / Principal Investigator

## Key facts

- Materials in energy storage devices are dynamic, undergoing mass/electron transport, high temperatures, and reactive environments.
- The group uses in situ experimental techniques such as X-ray tomographic imaging and electron microscopy to observe real-time structural, chemical, and morphological transformations.
- Project 1 investigates transformation and degradation mechanisms in next-generation battery materials, including lithium-ion, sodium-ion, and multivalent systems.
- Project 2 focuses on understanding and improving interfaces in solid-state batteries to enhance safety and energy density.
- Project 3 develops sustainable, lower-cost battery materials for various technologies, including grid-scale flow batteries.
- Project 4 explores high-capacity electrode materials that operate at low temperatures, with potential applications in spaceflight and electrified aircraft.
