# Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-04

**Source:** [Kia Production Announcement Brings GA’s EV Efforts Full Circle - Middle Georgia CEO](https://middlegeorgiaceo.com/features/2023/04/kia-production-announcement-brings-gas-ev-efforts-full-circle/)  ·  `page_060444`

## Facts

- Kia officially announced at the New York Auto Show that its newest EV, the Telluride-sized EV9, will be built at its plant in West Point, Georgia.
- Hyundai plans to build 300,000 electric vehicles at their Bryan County facility.
- The announcement ratifies statements and actions of the Georgia Department of Economic Development regarding strategic courtship of EV and supplier manufacturers.
- At a legislative study committee meeting in August 2022, Commissioner Pat Wilson testified that Georgia’s strategy to attract EV and supplier manufacturers aimed to retain existing auto manufacturing jobs during the industry's transition to new technologies.
- Georgia had 55,000 auto and supplier jobs tied to traditional internal combustion engine (ICE) vehicles prior to the transition.
- World’s largest automakers are shifting R&D efforts toward EVs and away from ICE, creating an opportunity to capture plant and equipment investment while retaining current jobs.
- Hyundai Motor Group has begun production of its electric GV70 model for the Genesis luxury brand alongside other ICE vehicles at its Montgomery, Alabama plant.
- Hyundai selected Bryan County for its first dedicated EV plant and third Southeastern factory, forming a critical mass capable of producing over one million vehicles per year.
- Commissioner Wilson testified that Hyundai expects to help co-locate more than 3 dozen new suppliers to support manufacturing efforts in Bryan County.
- Hyundai’s official target from the Bryan County selection announcement was $1 billion in additional capital investment from suppliers.
- As of March 2023, announced projects totaled $1.8 billion, with more supplier location decisions still pending.
- Much of the costs and value added for EV manufacturing will come from battery unit production.
- The Bryan County factory will include an on-site battery production facility.
- SK Innovation has been producing EV batteries in Northeast Georgia for several years.
- SK Innovation currently employs 2,000 Georgians near Commerce with a goal to reach 3,000 employees by year-end 2023.
- A subsidiary of SK Innovation announced an additional battery manufacturing facility northwest of Atlanta in Bartow County.
- The Bartow County facility represents $4.5 billion in new investment with a goal of employing 3,500 Georgians.
- SK Innovation is establishing an IT office in Roswell to create 200 high-tech jobs.
- American companies are diversifying supply chains away from China due to economic and national security concerns following the pandemic, the war in Ukraine, and tensions over Taiwan.
- Korea is highlighted as a long-standing US ally with aligned national security interests, increasing its importance as an Asian trading partner for Georgia.
- Georgia Ports remain a major economic driver for the state with a balanced mix of two-way trade and are among the ports with the highest exports compared to imports.
- Yoonie Kim serves as the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s point person on Korean investment.
- Governor Brian Kemp gave Yoonie Kim a name-check during his State of the State address, followed by a standing ovation from legislators for landing billions in investment and thousands of jobs while maintaining the current employment base.
- Georgia previously lost a Ford plant in Hapeville and GM plants at Lakewood and Doraville.
- Kia put Georgia back on the automotive manufacturing map by building an SUV there beginning in 2009.
- Kia’s decision to build a dedicated EV model at the same West Point plant a decade and a half later supports Georgia’s growing manufacturing and trading future.
