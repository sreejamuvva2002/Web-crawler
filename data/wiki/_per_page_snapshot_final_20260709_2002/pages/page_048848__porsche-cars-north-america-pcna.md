# Porsche Cars North America (PCNA)

**Source:** [Porsche Cars North America Sold a Record 76,167 New Vehicles in the U.S. in 2024 - Rennlist](https://rennlist.com/articles/porsche-cars-north-america-sold-a-record-76167-new-vehicles-in-the-u-s-in-2024/)  ·  `page_048848`

## Facts

- Porsche Cars North America sold a record 76,167 new vehicles in the U.S. in 2024.
- The 2024 sales figure represents a one percent increase over the previous sales record set in 2023.
- Timo Resch serves as the President and CEO of PCNA.
- PCNA introduced unprecedented new ICE, Hybrid, and BEV models including the new 911, Panamera, Taycan, and Macan.
- The 911 model set an all-time sales record with 14,128 retail sales in 2024.
- The 911's 2024 sales represent a 20.8 percent increase compared to 2023.
- The 718 model sold 5,698 units in 2024.
- The 718's 2024 sales represent a 25.9 percent increase compared to 2023.
- The 911 and 718 models combined accounted for 26 percent of all Porsche vehicles sold in the U.S. in 2024.
- The Cayenne model achieved record sales with 22,432 units sold in 2024.
- The Cayenne's 2024 sales represent a 9.6 percent increase over 2023.
- Porsche Approved Certified Pre-Owned (CPO) sales totaled 43,249 vehicles in 2024.
- CPO sales in 2024 represent a 12 percent increase compared to the prior year.
- The Taycan model saw a 37.3 percent decrease in sales in 2024.
- Taycan sales dropped from 7,570 units in 2023 to 4,747 units in 2024.
- Panamera sales decreased by 5.3 percent in 2024, dropping from 4,205 units to 3,982 units.
- Macan sales decreased by 6.6 percent in 2024, dropping from 26,947 units to 25,180 units.
- Global EV sales are slowing down, contributing to the decline in Taycan sales.
- Porsche is introducing a new EV Macan model.
- An EV Cayenne line is expected from Porsche.
- An EV 718 line is expected from Porsche.
- A local Porsche dealer offered a brand-new Macan 4 EV for 10 percent off MSRP.
- Porsche CFO Lutz Meschke stated the company is considering hybrid drive or combustion engines for originally planned all-electric vehicles.
- Porsche is currently making conceptual decisions regarding powertrain options for its future vehicles.
- Porsche confirmed it will stick with the combustion engine for much longer than originally planned.
