# Honda Motor Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-02-02

**Source:** [Honda's new EV production revolution begins with $1 billion in Ohio](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/02/02/hondas-new-ev-production-revolution-begins-with-1-billion-in-ohio.html)  ·  `page_008928`

## Facts

- Honda is in the midst of completing more than $1 billion in new investments in Ohio this year, upped from an initially announced $700 million.
- The investment upgrades notably include installing six “giga presses” (6,000-ton high-pressure die cast machines) for megacasting materials.
- Honda is establishing a new “cell” manufacturing system for its upcoming electric vehicle battery cases.
- The company’s emerging EV hub in Ohio will serve as the flagship for Honda’s global manufacturing operations.
- Honda’s Marysville Auto Plant will be capable of producing traditional vehicles, hybrids and EVs on the same assembly line.
- Mike Fischer, North American lead for Honda’s battery-electric vehicle projects, stated that the Ohio EV hub is establishing the global standard for EV production for people, technology, and processes.
- The Ohio investments were initially announced in October 2022 as part of the Biden administration’s push to on-shore manufacturing.
- Honda produced more than 1 million vehicles at five U.S. assembly plants in 2024, with about 64% sold in the U.S. and the remainder exported.
- Once completed, Honda will be able to produce roughly 220,000 vehicles annually at its Marysville plant, located in central Ohio outside of Columbus.
- The 4 million-square-foot Marysville facility currently produces several Honda and Acura vehicles and is expected to join production of an all-electric Acura RSX crossover later this year.
- The all-electric Acura RSX crossover will be the first EV produced by Honda in the U.S.
- The new Acura crossover will be followed by the Honda 0 SUV and Honda 0 Saloon EV prototypes debuted at CES in Las Vegas.
- Aluminum battery packs for the new EVs will be produced at Honda’s nearby engine complex in Anna, Ohio, which is the company’s largest engine facility globally and spans more than 2.8-million-square feet.
- Tim Stroh, EV battery case project leader, stated that the goal is to roll out the large aluminum production technology for all Honda products and factories across the world.
- The massive gigacasting machines use enormous pressure to form parts, theoretically slashing per-unit manufacturing costs by eliminating the welding of dozens of body parts to cast one single module.
- Once cast, battery packs will be shipped from Anna to Marysville and other plants to have battery cells from Honda’s joint venture operations with LG Energy Solution installed before final EV assembly.
- To combine battery cells and packs in Marysville, Honda is installing nearly 60 flexible manufacturing “cells” or zones for battery assembly that run parallel to the main line to prevent slowdowns.
- Bob Schwyn, senior vice president of Honda Development and Manufacturing of America, referred to the transition to electric vehicles as Honda’s “second founding” and stated they are using the opportunity to reimagine their approach to manufacturing.
- Despite slower-than-expected EV adoption in the U.S., Honda maintains its previously announced goal of achieving zero environmental impact by 2050 through carbon neutrality, clean energy, and resource circulation.
- Honda’s goals also include exclusively selling zero-emissions vehicles by 2040.
- The more than $1 billion investments include new manufacturing processes to lower emissions and waste, including using a special form of structural aluminum for EV battery packs that can be recycled and reused.
- Schwyn stated the company is implementing strategies to recapture products at end-of-life and recycle or reuse 100% of materials, especially finite materials for EV batteries, to essentially make new Hondas out of old Hondas.
