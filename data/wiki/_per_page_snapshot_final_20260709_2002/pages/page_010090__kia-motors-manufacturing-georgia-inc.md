# Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia, Inc.

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2009-09-03

**Source:** [Kia On Track For November Production Start - Kia Georgia](https://www.kiageorgia.com/kia-on-track-for-november-production-start/)  ·  `page_010090`

## Facts

- As of September 3, 2009, the first car has yet to roll off the assembly line of Kia Motors’ West Point, Ga., plant.
- Progress toward the projected mid-November launch date is moving at full throttle.
- J. Randy Jackson, Kia Motors’ human resource director and administrator, shared progress updates at the Opelika Chamber of Commerce’s Business Over Breakfast event at the Saugahatchee Country Club.
- “We’re the fastest growing auto company in the world and that’s something I’m proud of,” said Jackson.
- The West Point, Ga. plant is scheduled to begin production in mid-November 2009.
- At launch, the plant will manufacture 2011 model vehicles, including the popular Sorento CUV.
- At full production, the plant hopes to produce 300,000 vehicles a year, according to Kia Motors public relations specialist Patrick Sands.
- After January 2010, the plant will begin shipping vehicles by rail and truck to dealership locations in the U.S., Canada, and Puerto Rico.
- In August 2009, Kia Motors reported a 60.4 percent increase in vehicle sales.
- The opening of Kia’s West Point auto assembly plant is on schedule.
- When complete, the plant will sit on a 2,200-acre site, with only about 650 acres designated for the main pad.
- “Typically you don’t see an OEM (original equipment manufacturer) site larger than about 1,100 acres but there’s a reason we’re located on such a large site,” said Jackson.
- “We hope to expand and we hope to do so quickly,” added Jackson regarding the plant's large footprint.
- When Kia Motors began accepting applications for positions at its West Point plant, more than 43,000 people applied from January to February 2008.
- As of September 2009, 1,138 persons have been hired to staff the West Point, Ga. plant.
- There are about 100 salaried position openings remaining at the plant, according to Jackson.
- Jackson hopes to hire more workers in the future.
- Opelika Mayor Gary Fuller said he was “impressed with the information Jackson shared” regarding the plant's progress.
- “Kia has and will continue to have a significant, positive economic impact on the Opelika and East Alabama communities,” said Fuller.
- Fuller hopes the West Point, Ga. plant will serve as an employment option for workers affected by the closing of BFGoodrich’s Opelika plant.
- “We’re not going anywhere... We’re here to stay,” said Jackson regarding Kia's commitment to the West Point, Ga. site.
- Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia, Inc. (KMMG) is the first manufacturing site in North America for Kia Motors Corporation, which is based in Seoul, Korea.
- KMMG is located on 2,200 acres in West Point, Georgia.
- The plant was scheduled to begin production in the last quarter of 2009.
- At full capacity, the plant will have the ability to produce 300,000 vehicles annually and employ approximately 2,500 team members.
