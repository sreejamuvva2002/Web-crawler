# GDEcD State of Small Business Outreach Program

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2022-03

**Source:** [GDEcD State of Small Business: A Roundtable Opportunity - Albany CEO](https://albanyceo.com/news/2022/03/state-small-business-roundtable-opportunity/)  ·  `page_013475`

## Facts

- Governor Kemp announced a new position to support and empower Georgia’s small business community less than a year prior to March 2022.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) established a Director of Small Business Outreach based on a recommendation from Governor Kemp’s Georgians First Commission.
- In Georgia, a small business is defined as independently owned and operated with fewer than 300 employees or less than $30 million in yearly gross receipts.
- More than 1.6 million Georgians are employed by these small businesses across sectors including manufacturing, retail, arts, hospitality, film production, and more.
- Director of Small Business Outreach Allen Fox travels the state to discuss GDEcD services and support available to small businesses.
- Approximately 80% of GDEcD’s work involves small businesses through its Global Commerce, International Trade, Tourism, Center of Innovation, Entertainment, Georgia Council for the Arts, and International Relations teams.
- In November 2021, Fox launched the “State of Small Business” program featuring a series of regional roundtables connecting GDEcD team members with small business stakeholders.
- The inaugural roundtable event was held in Macon to allow state and local organizations to hear directly from small business owners about their efforts, needs, and concerns.
- Additional “State of Small Business” roundtables have been presented in Augusta, Butts County, Columbus, the Georgia Black Chamber, Rome, and other locations.
- The program continues to bring together businesses, academic institutions, chambers, and organizations to support local small businesses across Georgia.
- Interested Georgia communities or organizations can host a “State of Small Business” presentation by contacting Allen Fox via email.
- GDEcD plans to celebrate Georgia’s 2022 Small Business Rock Stars during the week of May 2–7, 2022, highlighting outstanding and impactful small businesses in the state.
