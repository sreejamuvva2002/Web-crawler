## Facts

- U.S. President Donald Trump warned foreign companies to hire and train American workers following the Immigration Enforcement Operation on South Korean carmaker Hyundai Motor Company’s battery plant in Georgia.
- Trump said in a post on Truth Social that he is calling on all Foreign Companies investing in the United States to please respect the Nation’s Immigration Laws.
- Trump encouraged foreign companies to LEGALLY bring very smart people with great technical talent to build World Class products, and stated that what is asked in return is that they hire and train American Workers.
- Last week, U.S. federal agents detained 475 people including about 300 from South Korea at the battery plant construction site in Georgia.
- U.S. authorities said these people were “illegally” working at the battery plant in Georgia.
- In a statement on September 5, 2025, Hyundai Motor Company said it is aware of the immigration enforcement action that took place at the construction site of its supplier, the HL-GA Battery Company in Bryan County, Georgia.
- Hyundai Motor Company stated it is closely monitoring the situation while working to understand the specific circumstances.
- Based on current understanding, none of those detained is directly employed by Hyundai Motor Company.
- Hyundai Motor India said it is committed to full compliance with all laws and regulations in every market where it operates.
- This includes employment verification requirements and immigration laws, and Hyundai expects the same commitment from all partners, suppliers, contractors, and subcontractors.
- The safety and well-being of everyone working at sites, whether directly employed or working for suppliers, is a top priority.
- Incidents like this remind us of the importance of robust oversight throughout the entire supply chain and contractor network.
- Hyundai said it is reviewing its processes to ensure that all parties working on its projects maintain high standards of legal compliance.
- This includes thorough vetting of employment practices by contractors and subcontractors.
- The South Korean auto major said it will conduct an investigation to ensure all suppliers and their subcontractors comply with all laws and regulations.
- As the company continues to invest in American manufacturing and create thousands of jobs, it will do so in full accordance with U.S. law and in a manner that reflects values of treating all people with dignity and respect.
