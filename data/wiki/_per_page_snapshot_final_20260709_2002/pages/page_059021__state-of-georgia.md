# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2023-12

**Source:** [GDEcD: 50 Years of International Presence - Rome CEO](https://romeceo.com/features/2023/12/50-years-japan-and-europe/)  ·  `page_059021`

## Facts

- The State of Georgia has become a center of international business, with Atlanta and Savannah serving as primary global gateways for people and products through air, sea, and rail.
- Decades of strategic decisions by state leaders are delivering generational investments that continue to keep Georgia at the forefront of global trends and stretching into new markets.
- Fifty years ago in 1973, then-Governor Jimmy Carter began to establish Georgia’s physical presence abroad.
- Coryn Marsik serves as Director of International Investment at the Georgia Department of Economic Development.
- Coryn Marsik stated that Georgia having international representation for over 50 years has helped give its economy remarkable resiliency, and that these offices recruit diverse investments to create jobs for Georgians and help companies reach new global markets.
- The State of Georgia’s first international office was established in Tokyo in 1973.
- Japanese companies continue to be the top international employer in Georgia to this day.
- Active Japan-Georgia organizations include the Japan-America Society of Georgia and the Japan External Trade Organization.
- Georgia is a charter member of the Southeast U.S.-Japan Alliance.
- The State of Georgia’s Europe Office was established shortly after the 1973 Tokyo office opening, with professionals serving the European market from Munich, Germany.
- Germany is one of Georgia’s top investor nations and has a robust mobility industry.
- In 2020, Governor Brian Kemp and a State of Georgia delegation met with automotive companies headquartered in Germany to discuss electrification and other alternative fuels.
- Following the 2020 trip, Georgia set a new goal to identify opportunities at every step of the supply chain.
- The mobility and battery supply chain have announced more than $25.5 billion in investments and an estimated 30,700 new jobs over the next few years.
- European-based companies enhancing Georgia’s mobility ecosystem include GEDIA Automotive, Aurubis AG, and Heliox.
- Governor Brian Kemp served on a panel at the 2023 World Economic Forum in Davos-Klosters, Switzerland, sharing that one can count on stability and a great economy and business environment in Georgia.
- Ellen Kraft serves as Managing Director of the Europe Office.
- Ellen Kraft stated that the European market is full of opportunity for attracting innovative companies, helping Georgia businesses connect with new customers, and establishing new ties, noting the expansion of relationships with partners like the new North Rhine-Westphalia (NRW) office in Atlanta.
- International businesses operating in Georgia tap into university-backed research, innovative ecosystems, and solutions-oriented partnerships.
- Commissioner Pat Wilson explains how Georgia is reaping the seeds sown decades ago by visionary leaders.
