# Archer Aviation's Covington Manufacturing Facility

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-07-31

**Source:** [Archer's Electric Aircraft Factory in Georgia Is Nearly Complete | Design and Development Today](https://www.designdevelopmenttoday.com/industries/aerospace/news/22916496/archers-electric-aircraft-factory-in-georgia-is-nearly-complete)  ·  `page_001636`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation provided an update on the construction progress of its high-volume manufacturing facility in Covington, GA, reaffirming an expected 2024 completion date.
- The facility's first build phase, inclusive of grading and foundation work, was completed earlier this year.
- Phase two, the erection of the exterior walls and roof, is nearing completion.
- Phase three (interior fit out) and phase four (equipment load in and power on) are on track to be completed in the coming months.
- Once complete, the facility will be nearly 400,000 square feet.
- The facility will support Archer’s planned production of up to 650 Midnight aircraft annually.
- This production volume would make it one of the largest manufacturing facilities by volume in the aviation industry.
- Archer’s buildout is taking place in close partnership with Stellantis, with both companies continuing to progress plans to mass-produce the Midnight aircraft together.
- The Midnight is an electric vertical take-off and landing (eVTOL) aircraft built around Archer’s proprietary electric powertrain.
- The Midnight is designed for back-to-back flights of 20-50 miles with minimal charge time in between.
- The Midnight travels at speeds up to 150 mph, turning hour-long ground commutes into minutes in the air.
- The Midnight operates at 100x quieter than a helicopter at cruising altitudes, making it nearly inaudible from the streets below.
- The Midnight features redundant systems across the aircraft allowing Archer to target similar levels of safety as commercial airliners.
- The Midnight is designed to carry a pilot, up to four passengers, and carry-on luggage.
- Last week, Archer showcased its Midnight aircraft at Atlantic Aviation’s new terminal at DeKalb–Peachtree Municipal Airport.
- Guests at the showcase included government officials from Georgia’s Office of the Governor, Georgia’s Congressional Offices, the City of Atlanta, Georgia’s State Legislature, the Federal Aviation Administration, and other local governments.
