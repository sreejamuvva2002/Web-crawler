# State of Georgia / Georgia Department of Economic Development

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2024-04-29

**Source:** [Five Georgia Manufacturers Share Secrets to Success | Georgia](https://georgia.org/blog/five-georgia-manufacturers-share-secrets-success)  ·  `page_002574`

## Facts

- Verescence North America is the only specialized manufacturer of glass perfume bottles in the United States.
- The French company has over 120 years of industry experience and was one of the first glassmakers to establish U.S. production facilities in 1996.
- Verescence operates two North American production facilities in Georgia: a glass bottle manufacturing facility in Covington and a decorative/printing facility in Sparta, Georgia.
- Verescence partnered with Georgia Quick Start to develop customized training materials for new employees to safely operate equipment and work with molten glass.
- Takeda’s facility is located at the GRAD (Georgia Ready Accelerated Development) certified Stanton Springs industrial park east of Atlanta.
- Takeda’s $1.7 billion, more than 1 million-square-foot facility manufactures two types of plasma-derived therapies used to combat over 400 primary immune deficiency diseases.
- Takeda co-founded the global CoVIg-19 Plasma Alliance and manufactured its investigational hyperimmune globulin medicine at the Georgia facility.
- Ethicon, a subsidiary of Johnson & Johnson, operates a medical device facility in Cornelia, Georgia, founded in 1946 as a J&J plant and co-located with Janssen on a campus that has since expanded to include an Athens location.
- Johnson & Johnson attributed its decision to expand in Georgia to a business-friendly environment and an available, skilled workforce.
- Communities around metro Atlanta are investing in workforce and talent solutions such as Georgia Quick Start’s Georgia BioScience Training Center and collaboration opportunities.
- The University of Georgia (UGA) broke ground in April 2024 on its new independent School of Medicine to expand existing talent pipelines and research.
- UGA President Jere W. Morehead stated the new School of Medicine will expand the pool of medical professionals, attract top-tier scientists and researchers to the state, and produce more physicians for underserved and rural Georgia communities.
- Creature Comforts Brewing Co. was founded in Athens in 2014 and operates three production facilities in revitalized industrial spaces, including the original location at the old Snow Tire Building and a second facility at the historic Southern Mill property in Athens.
- Starbucks located an additional production facility in Augusta to produce at-home ground blends, K-Cup pods, and cold brew concentrates closer to new markets.
- Starbucks cited reliable partners, power and utilities, and a trained workforce as key factors for its Augusta location.
- Georgia offers more rail miles than any other state in the Southeast at 5,000 miles.
- Georgia has 20,000 miles of federal and state highways connecting companies to top-ranked logistics assets.
- Georgia’s connectivity through Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport and two deepwater ports enabled the state to move more than $186 billion in U.S. trade in 2023.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development’s International Relations team organized a 2024 International VIP Tour that stopped in Athens, Augusta, Covington, Oconee, and Monroe to engage the Consular Corps with existing industries and local communities.
