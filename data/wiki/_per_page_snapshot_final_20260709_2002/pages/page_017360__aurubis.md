# Aurubis

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2023-02

**Source:** [Accelerated Investment in Aurubis’ Augusta Multi-metal Recycling Facility Strengthens Georgia’s Position in EV, Sustainability - Columbus CEO](https://thecolumbusceo.com/news/2023/02/accelerated-investment-aurubis-augusta-multi-metal-recycling-facility-strengthens-georgias-position-ev-sustainability-state/)  ·  `page_017360`

## Facts

- German multi-metal provider and recycling company Aurubis was drawn to Georgia for its pro-business environment, spirit of innovation, and focus on building out a world-class electric mobility value chain.
- Aurubis announced in November 2021 that it would build a recycling and secondary smelting facility for multi-metal recycling in Augusta, Georgia.
- Leaders from the Georgia Department of Economic Development were at company headquarters in Hamburg, Germany, when the investment was announced.
- Aurubis announced in December 2022 that it would increase its investment total to €640 million.
- Aurubis projects it will double its throughput volume from the previously announced 90,000 tons of complex recycling materials to 180,000 tons per year.
- The facility is expected to create 200 jobs.
- The announcement marked the single-largest investment of a German company in Georgia.
- It is the first facility of its kind in the U.S.
- This is also the first greenfield project in the U.S. for Aurubis.
- Aurubis plans to produce materials that can be used in lithium-ion batteries and other electronics valuable in the EV industry.
- Not long after the December 2022 announcement, Georgia Governor Brian Kemp and state leaders were again on the ground in Germany to celebrate the company’s commitment to Georgia, the e-mobility and renewable energy ecosystems, and the jobs it would bring.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp said the project was attracted by Georgia's ports system, highly skilled workforce, and pro-business environment that prioritizes partnership and innovation.
- Aurubis will now join more than 500 German companies with operations in Georgia.
- Roland Harings, CEO of Aurubis, said today’s high recycling demand cannot be met by existing facilities.
- Roland Harings stated the world needs metals such as copper, which can be recycled without loss of quality or performance, for cars, mobile phones, batteries, cables, and computers.
- Roland Harings added that Aurubis's increased commitment to the U.S. will be a game-changer for the environment, manufacturers, and the transition to a renewable economy.
- Aurubis broke ground on the recycling plant in June 2022.
- The first phase is expected to come online in mid-2024.
- The expansion is expected to be completed in 2026.
- The company was able to quickly start work on the site due to its status as a “Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) Certified” site.
- Cal Wray, president of the Augusta Economic Development Authority, expressed excitement over the speed at which Aurubis is building the facility and executing further expansions.
- Cal Wray noted that Aurubis will provide great jobs and be a wonderful addition to the Augusta community.
- Germany is one of Georgia’s top three investment partners.
- Germany was Georgia’s second-largest job-creating country for Foreign Direct Investment in FY22.
- At least 513 German facilities operate in Georgia, employing more than 25,000 Georgians.
- Georgia is celebrating its 50th year of continuous representation in Germany in 2023.
