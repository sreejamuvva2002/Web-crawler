# Bridgestone Bandag

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [TX: Bridgestone Bandag Breaks Ground on $60 Million Expansion in Abilene, Creating 25 New Jobs | Trade and Industry Development](https://www.tradeandindustrydev.com/region/texas/news/tx-bridgestone-bandag-breaks-ground-60-million-31767)  ·  `page_018183`

## Facts

- Bridgestone Bandag broke ground on the expansion of its retread tire plant in Abilene, Texas.
- The project marks a $60 million capital investment into Abilene.
- The expansion will add 50,000 square feet to their existing 200,000 square-foot facility.
- Misty Mayo, President and Chief Executive Officer of the DCOA, stated that Bridgestone Bandag has continued to invest in Abilene for over 50 years and prioritizes building long-term partnerships with local existing companies.
- The expansion is expected to retain 179 full-time employees and create an additional 25 full-time positions for Abilene.
- Over the next 10 years, the $60 million project is estimated to add over $21 million in taxes for the City of Abilene, Taylor County, and Abilene Independent School District.
- Abilene Mayor Weldon Hurt stated that the project is a tremendous opportunity for the community and expressed pride in Bridgestone Bandag's continued growth and investment.
- Over the next 10 years, the total estimated indirect and direct economic impact of retaining and expanding the company is $1.81 billion.
- Sam Vinson, DCOA Board Chair, noted that the expansion brings immense value because Bridgestone Bandag chose to stay, grow, and prosper in Abilene, Texas.
- As a leading manufacturer of tire retreads, the expansion will focus on developing an automated operation for rubber mixing and production.
- Billy Fralick, Plant Manager at the Abilene Plant, stated the expansion will allow growth of production capabilities to meet customer needs and thanked the community for support over the last 52 years.
- The plant expansion supports Bridgestone’s E8 Commitment, which consists of eight values starting with “E” (Energy, Ecology, Efficiency, Extension, Economy, Emotion, Ease, and Empowerment) to realize a sustainable society.
- Chris Daniel, Executive Director of Bandag Manufacturing at Bridgestone Americas, stated the expansion is driven by growing demand for products and the team’s commitment to delivering innovation and superior quality globally.
- The company plays a vital role in the transformation of mobility by building greener retreads and helping maximize fuel efficiency for fleets.
- The project taps into Abilene’s skilled labor force in manufacturing, supported by four universities, a technical college, a community college, and state-of-the-art training facilities for high school and college students.
- Abilene boasts robust infrastructure and a strategic location in the south-central United States, securing it as a premier location for manufacturing.
