# Blue Ridge Manufacturing LLC

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2014-01-23

**Source:** [Osha Says Blue Ridge Manufacturing Violated Safety Rules, Put Georgia Workers At Risk Of Serious Workplace Injury | Georgia Personal Injury Lawyer Blog | January 23, 2014](https://www.parsonslawgroup.com/blog/osha-says-blue-ridge-manufacturing-violated-safety-rules-put-geo/)  ·  `page_017888`

## Facts

- The federal Occupational Safety and Health Administration fined Blue Ridge Manufacturing LLC $64,400 for 20 serious safety and health violations at its plant in Blue Ridge, Ga.
- OSHA inspected the plant in March following a complaint against Blue Ridge Manufacturing LLC.
- Safety inspectors found unguarded equipment, missing safety equipment, and unsafe noise levels exposing manufacturing workers to risk of common injuries or fatal workplace accidents.
- Violations included failure to provide workers with lockout/tagout training to protect them during maintenance and service work on dangerous equipment.
- Violations included failure to train workers to run powered industrial trucks.
- Violations included failure to inspect a crane used for picking up steel parts.
- Violations included failure to ensure exits were unlocked and clearly marked.
- Inspectors found workers could be injured by being struck or caught by unguarded equipment, electrical shock and burn hazards.
- Dust levels at the plant were six times higher than allowable limits.
- The company failed to keep required injury and illness logs showing where injuries occurred.
- Georgia has more than 250 automotive and parts manufacturing facilities that employ nearly 18,000 workers, according to Georgia.org.
- Kia opened its first U.S. manufacturing operation in West Point, Georgia.
- Porsche, Lotus, and BBS located their North American headquarters in Georgia.
- General Motors launched a GM Innovation Center in conjunction with the Georgia Institute of Technology.
- The developments are encouraging for the state’s economy, ensuring the state will be able to offer high-tech and industrial jobs for many years.
- The automotive industry is incumbent on focusing on worker welfare to ensure work atmospheres are free of risks that could cause injury or death.
