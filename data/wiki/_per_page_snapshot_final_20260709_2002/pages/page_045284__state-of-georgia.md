# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** workforce_training  ·  **Published:** 2024-09-13

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp Celebrates Top State for Business Ranking at Workforce Summit - Location Georgia](https://www.locationgeorgia.com/georgia-named-top-state-for-business-september-2024/)  ·  `page_045284`

## Facts

- Georgia was named the No. 1 state for business for the 11th year in a row by Area Development magazine, with workforce development being a major contributor.
- Georgia earned a No. 1 ranking for the 15th year in a row for its nationally recognized workforce development program, Georgia Quick Start.
- The state’s economic development efforts in fiscal year 2024 resulted in $20.3 billion in investments and plans for 26,900 new jobs statewide.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp delivered the keynote address at the second annual Governor’s Workforce Summit, during which he announced that Georgia has been named the No. 1 state for business for the 11th consecutive year by Area Development magazine.
- Governor Kemp provided an update on the state of Georgia MATCH and other state efforts to strengthen workforce development while putting forth the goal of making Georgia the Top State for Talent.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that the recognition underscores their pro-business approach to economic development creating opportunities for Georgians in every corner of the state.
- Governor Kemp thanked the state economic development team and community partners, noting continued efforts through Georgia MATCH which has already seen a notable increase in enrollment at both USG and TCSG institutions.
- Leaders from across the public and private sector remain engaged in solving the need for more workers, as observed at today’s summit.
- No other state has earned the distinction of Top State for Business for so many years.
- Area Development’s annual poll considers approximately 50 leading site consulting firms from across the U.S. and evaluates 14 different factors to determine the ranking.
- Governor Kemp hosted the second annual Governor’s Workforce Summit at the Georgia World Congress Center.
- The summit brought together leaders from business, education, and public policy to align these sectors in preparing Georgians for available jobs statewide.
- The highlight of last year’s summit was the announcement of Georgia MATCH, the largest direct college admission program in the nation.
- Georgia MATCH sent a letter to every high school senior in the state informing them of spots at Georgia’s University System and Technical College System institutions.
- Following last year’s rollout, both systems saw a preliminary year-over-year increase in enrollment this fall of 9.3% for TCSG and 6.2% for USG.
- This enrollment increase occurred despite challenges created by the federal government’s mishandling of this year’s FAFSA rollout.
- Dennis Shea, Publisher of Area Development, stated that Georgia has maintained top 10 rankings in each survey subcategory for over a decade, including available workforce, cost of doing business, cooperative and responsive state government, energy availability and costs, logistics and infrastructure, and site readiness programs.
- Site selectors recognize that Georgia provides the whole package for companies choosing to grow or expand in the state.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Georgia does economic development differently by prioritizing partnerships and investments in infrastructure, education, and workforce development.
- Between July 1, 2023, and June 30, 2024, GDEcD’s Global Commerce team supported 429 facility expansions and new locations totaling more than $20.3 billion in investment, resulting in plans for 26,900 new jobs statewide.
- Area Development is considered the leading executive magazine covering corporate site selection and relocation, founded in 1965, publishing quarterly with a website receiving over one million visitor sessions annually.
