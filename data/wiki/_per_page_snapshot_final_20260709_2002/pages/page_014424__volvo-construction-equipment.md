# Volvo Construction Equipment

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Volvo at CONEXPO | Volvo Construction Equipment Global](https://www.volvoce.com/global/en/news-and-events/news-and-stories/2026/ambitious-solutions-for-the-toughest-challenges-volvo-at-conexpo-2026/)  ·  `page_014424`

## Facts

- Volvo CE arrived at CONEXPO 2026 under the theme "Power Your Ambition" with a 55,000-square-foot booth at the Festival Grounds.
- The company showcased a next-generation lineup of more than 20 machines, including 14 new or first-look models across electric and conventional power.
- The booth featured live demos, hands-on operating experiences, and integrated displays from Volvo Trucks and Volvo Financial Services.
- Melker Jernberg, President of Volvo CE, stated that CONEXPO is about listening to customers to support their ambitions and that the company brought machines and services ready to deliver today rather than just showing future concepts.
- Approximately 40% of the Volvo Group portfolio has been renewed since the industry last gathered in Las Vegas.
- Volvo CE alone has updated more than 35% of its machines using a multi-technology approach that includes battery-electric, internal combustion engine, and grid-connected solutions.
- The biggest product updates are across excavators, wheel loaders, and articulated haulers, which together represent around 70% of the global construction market.
- Volvo CE experts demonstrated how tools like Load Ticket, Connected Map, and ActiveCare® Direct transform raw data into uptime and safety inside an immersive "site office."
- The company highlighted its growing Equipment as a Service (EaaS) model, which offers guaranteed availability and predictable costs through a simple pay-for-use structure.
- Scott Young, President of Volvo Construction Equipment North America, noted that combining renewed machines with services and digital tools helps customers work safer, more productively, and with lower total cost of ownership.
- The Volvo Group reinforced its long-term commitment to North America through $1.2 billion USD in strategic investments designed to strengthen manufacturing capabilities, regionalize supply chains, and support sustainable technologies.
- A centerpiece of this strategy is a $40 million USD investment in the Shippensburg, Pa., facility.
- The Shippensburg investment brings production of excavators and four large wheel loader models to the U.S. in early 2026.
- This facility expansion is part of a significant Volvo CE investment in the global excavator manufacturing footprint.
- Attendees operated compact diesel and mid-size electric excavators, including the 23-ton EC230 Electric excavator, noting that its performance matches expectations for demanding applications without compromise.
- Appearances by "Gold Rush" star Tony Beets, YouTuber LetsDig18, and other influencers helped connect Volvo CE innovations to industry professionals.
- Melker Jernberg emphasized that meeting customers where they are and leveraging the full strength of the Volvo Group ensures customer success, which in turn benefits communities.
