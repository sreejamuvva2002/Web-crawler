# Hyundai Industrial Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-11-03

**Source:** [Governor Kemp announces Hyundai Industrial investment of $24 million in Georgia, creating 100 new jobs - Peach Tree Times](https://peachtreetimes.com/governor-kemp-announces-hyundai-industrial-investment-of-24-million-in-georgia-creating-100-new-jobs)  ·  `page_021115`

## Facts

- Hyundai Industrial intends to invest over $24 million in a new manufacturing plant in Coweta County, Georgia, near Newnan.
- The investment is expected to create 100 new jobs.
- Governor Kemp affirmed that this project will continue to boost the auto industry and overall state economy.
- A partnership with auto giants Kia and Hyundai has drawn many suppliers resulting in job creation across Georgia.
- Governor Kemp stated: 'Partners like Kia and Hyundai are attracting a large network of suppliers, creating jobs and opportunity for hardworking Georgians across the entire state.'
- Suppliers have pledged nearly 6,000 jobs for Georgia since last year's announcement about the Hyundai Metaplant.
- These supplier jobs especially benefit communities outside major urban areas.
- Hyundai Industrial is a Korean firm established in 1978 with headquarters in Ulsan.
- The company specializes in producing car seats, armrests, and headrests for some of the world's leading auto brands.
- Hyundai Industrial expressed excitement about contributing to the e-mobility transition in Georgia and fostering mutually beneficial growth with Coweta County.
- Coweta County Board of Commissioners Chairman John Reidelbach welcomed Hyundai Industrial Co., Ltd. to Coweta County.
- Chairman Reidelbach stated: 'We are thrilled to welcome Hyundai Industrial Co., Ltd. to Coweta County.'
- Reidelbach highlighted that access to I-85 will make transport seamless to nearby automobile plants.
- Georgia fosters industry practices, collaboration, and innovation, putting it at the forefront of the automotive and mobility industry.
- Georgia has invested heavily across the EV supply chain, resulting in over $25.1 billion in investments and 29,000 job additions since 2020.
- Automotive sector jobs went up by 324 percent in fiscal year 2023 compared to fiscal year 2021.
- Suppliers flocking to Georgia's mobility original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) contributed over $2 billion in investments.
