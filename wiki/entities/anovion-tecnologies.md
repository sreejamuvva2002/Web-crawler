# Anovion Tecnologies

**Type:** Company  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[battery-materials|Battery Materials]]  ·  **Location:** Bainbridge, Decatur County, Georgia, United States

## Overview

Anovion Tecnologies, a maker of synthetic graphite for electric vehicle batteries, announced an $800 million investment to build a new graphite factory in Bainbridge, Decatur County, Georgia, creating 400 jobs and targeting a production capacity of 44,000 tons by 2025.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Supplier of synthetic graphite anodes used in lithium-ion batteries for electric vehicles.

## Key facts

- Anovion aims to become a major American-based supplier of graphite for electric batteries.
- The Georgia plant will have a capacity of 44,000 tons (40,000 metric tonnes) of synthetic graphite.
- The company plans to hire 400 workers for the new factory.
- Anovion seeks carbon neutrality by using renewable energy and a more efficient furnace.
- Synthetic graphite is made from petroleum coke, requiring large amounts of electricity; power costs are a key consideration in locating factories.
- Georgia offers tax incentives including a state income-tax credit of $3,500 per job annually for five years, potentially worth $7 million, plus property-tax breaks.
- Anovion’s CEO Eric Stopka said the company was attracted by Georgia’s workforce, research universities and growing solar energy production.
- Georgia has announced more than 40 electric-vehicle-related projects since 2020, pledging $22 billion of investment and 28,000 jobs.
- The company described planned pay levels as “competitive.”
- Anovion stated it will provide solutions to further secure the country’s electrified future with support from partners in Georgia and the federal government.
