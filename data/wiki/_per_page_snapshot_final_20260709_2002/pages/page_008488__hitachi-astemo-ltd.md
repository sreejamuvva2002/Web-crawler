# Hitachi Astemo Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hitachi Astemo Pushes the Envelope on EV and AV Technology | ASSEMBLY](https://www.assemblymag.com/articles/98342-hitachi-astemo-pushes-the-envelope-on-ev-and-av-technology)  ·  `page_008488`

## Facts

- Hitachi Astemo Ltd. is owned by Hitachi Ltd. and Honda Motor Co. and was created in 2021 from the merger of Hitachi Automotive Systems with Keihin, Nissin Kogyo, and Showa.
- The company focuses on electrification, comfort, safety, and sustainability for mobility applications.
- Hitachi Astemo is investing heavily in e-axles, inverters, and electric motors to balance traditional ICE and EV automaker demands.
- Engineers developed e-axles featuring efficiency, low vibration, low noise, modular design, and optimization control software for motors and gears.
- The company developed an inverter with industry-leading power density using a unique cooling module and thin, highly heat-dissipating insulation mounting technology.
- Inverters consist of power modules with built-in power semiconductors, control boards, capacitors, and current sensors.
- Hitachi Astemo leverages legacy Hitachi technologies in material development, analysis, sound-reduction, and manufacturing to create magnetic circuits for electric motors using magnets, electromagnetic steel sheets, and windings.
- At the Japan Mobility Show in Tokyo, the company showcased advanced driver assistance systems including smart brakes and steer-by-wire technology.
- The company unveiled 360-degree sensing technology that uses multiple stereo cameras combined with long-range detection and wide viewing angles to detect pedestrians and objects using AI and machine learning.
- Hitachi Astemo operates assembly plants in Georgia, Indiana, Kentucky, and Ohio in North America, plus facilities in Canada and Mexico, with its U.S. headquarters in Farmington Hills, MI.
- The company's Berea, KY plant conducted a smart factory experiment with Ericsson and Amazon Web Services using Hitachi video analytics over a private 5G network to detect defects earlier and reduce wasted material.
- John Nunneley serves as senior vice president of design engineering and IT at Hitachi Astemo Americas Inc.
- The company functions independently of Hitachi and Honda but collaborates on R&D projects, leveraging Hitachi's digital technology and former Honda-owned component suppliers to act as a systems supplier for OEMs.
- Hitachi Astemo has no plans to exit the ICE market, which generates the bulk of its revenue, acknowledging a long transition period from ICE to hybrid to fully electric vehicles.
- The company expects an 85 percent power density increase in its inverters over the next five years.
- Hitachi Astemo is combining inverters, motors, power electronics, gearboxes, and software to create three-in-one e-axles that simplify EV drivetrain integration.
- Production of the three-in-one e-axle is currently ramping up, with pilot production scheduled for this year and commercial production next year.
- The three-in-one e-axle will be built in North America at Hitachi Astemo's factory in Tallapoosa, GA.
- Motors and power inverters are larger and more complex than brake and suspension parts, requiring single-station batch builds in early stages before transitioning to new Industry 4.0-equipped assembly lines for higher EV volumes.
- Battery pack control modules for EVs can be manufactured on the same surface-mount assembly lines as ICE engine controllers.
- The company is electrifying components from hydraulic brakes to smart brakes and brake-by-wire, while also developing steer-by-wire systems and active suspension systems.
- A smart chassis system ties these components together dynamically using data from stereo cameras and other sensors to adapt to road conditions like potholes or ice.
- Target production timing for the next-generation AI-enhanced 360-degree stereo camera is 2025, with initial assembly in Japan followed by North American production as volumes ramp up.
- The Berea, KY plant currently produces suspension products and has started producing electric motors in 2022, with production ramping up over the next few years alongside new EV product assembly lines.
- Harrodsburg, KY is Hitachi Astemo's largest U.S. plant, primarily producing components for electronic control units, electronic throttle bodies, fuel rails, and mass air flow sensors.
- The Sunbury, OH plant assembles suspension products.
- The Greenfield, IN facility, a former Keihin factory making ICE components, is being converted to produce EV products such as power inverters.
- All U.S. production sites are implementing Industry 4.0 tools, including data tracking and equipment observation to identify bottlenecks and improve overall equipment effectiveness.
- The company is creating an end-to-end digital thread from sales to engineering to production to aftermarket and implementing a standard enterprise resource planning system across global sites.
