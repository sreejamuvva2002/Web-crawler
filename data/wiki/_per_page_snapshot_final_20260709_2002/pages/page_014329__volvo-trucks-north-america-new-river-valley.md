# Volvo Trucks North America New River Valley

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Governor Northam Announces Volvo Group to Create 777 New Jobs in Pulaski County Expansion | Virginia Economic Development Partnership](https://www.vedp.org/press-release/2019-06/volvo-group-nrv)  ·  `page_014329`

## Facts

- Governor Ralph Northam announced that Volvo Group will invest nearly $400 million to expand its Volvo Trucks North America New River Valley (NRV) assembly operation in Pulaski County.
- Major components of the investment include a new 350,000-square-foot building that will ultimately house truck cab welding operations.
- The expansion includes improvements to the existing plant's paint operations and overall material/production flow.
- Equipment upgrades include installation of several state-of-the-art dynamometers for vehicle testing.
- Volvo considered sites in multiple other states for the expansion project.
- The project will create 777 new jobs within approximately six years.
- Volvo Trucks is the leading private employer in the New River Valley.
- Volvo Trucks has been a bedrock of the community for more than forty years and has fueled the regional economy.
- The expansion represents one of the largest capital investments in the history of Southwestern Virginia.
- Volvo Trucks provides complete transport solutions for customers, offering a full range of medium to heavy duty trucks.
- Customer support is secured via a global network of 2,100 dealers and workshops in more than 130 countries.
- Volvo trucks are assembled in 15 countries across the globe.
- In 2018, more than 127,000 Volvo trucks were delivered worldwide.
- Volvo Trucks is part of Volvo Group, one of the world’s leading manufacturers of trucks, buses, construction equipment, and marine and industrial engines.
- The Group also provides solutions for financing and service.
- Volvo Trucks’ work is based on the core values of quality, safety, and environmental care.
- The Volvo Trucks facility in Pulaski County is its largest in the world, with 1.6 million square feet on nearly 300 acres and 3,500 employees.
- Secretary of Commerce and Trade Brian Ball stated that Volvo’s historic investment signifies the plant’s future growth and strengthens Virginia’s corporate partnership with Volvo Trucks North America.
- The New River Valley is a hub for manufacturers from around the world due to its access to markets, robust workforce, and pro-business climate.
- Franky Marchand is Vice President and General Manager of the NRV plant.
- The investment will give employees tools to continue providing customers with the highest quality products.
- The Virginia Economic Development Partnership worked with Pulaski County, the General Assembly’s Major Employment and Investment (MEI) Project Approval Commission, and Onward New River Valley to secure the expansion for Virginia.
- The Volvo Group will be eligible to receive post-performance MEI custom grant payments totaling up to $16.5 million over 10 years.
- Grant eligibility is based upon achieving pledged net new job creation and investment commitments.
- New job creation will be on top of the baseline employment level of 3,219 in place when project negotiations began in 2018.
- The company is also eligible to receive state benefits from the Virginia Enterprise Zone Program, administered by the Virginia Department of Housing and Community Development.
- Chair of the Pulaski County Board of Supervisors Andy McCready expressed excitement to partner with the state to support Volvo Trucks and its workforce.
- Senator Frank Ruff is Chairman of the MEI Project Approval Commission.
- The project will enable the company to add nearly 800 new jobs to its existing workforce.
- Delegate Nick Rush is Vice Chair of the MEI Project Approval Commission.
- The NRV assembly plant is Volvo Trucks’ largest manufacturing facility in the world.
- The company has been able to grow in Pulaski County for decades with support from local, regional, and state economic development partners.
- Senator Benton Chafin highlighted the opportunity for hard-working people of Southwest Virginia and the New River Valley.
- The investment creates nearly 800 manufacturing jobs.
