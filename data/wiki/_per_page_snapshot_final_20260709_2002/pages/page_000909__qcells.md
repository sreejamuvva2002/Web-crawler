# Qcells

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-09

**Source:** [Qcells begins production of solar cells in Cartersville, Georgia - pv magazine USA](https://pv-magazine-usa.com/2026/06/09/qcells-begins-production-of-solar-cells-in-cartersville-georgia/)  ·  `page_000909`

## Facts

- Qcells is a South Korean solar manufacturer with facilities in the U.S. and Malaysia.
- Qcells has begun manufacturing solar cells at its Cartersville, Georgia facility.
- Manufacturing solar cells allows Qcells to produce the major components of a solar module all under one roof at the Cartersville site.
- Qcells expects to reach full production at its Cartersville facility by Q3 2026.
- At full capacity, the Cartersville facility will produce an estimated 3.3 GW each of ingots, wafers, and cells annually.
- The Cartersville facility will also produce 3.5 GW of solar modules per year at full capacity.
- Qcells operates a separate manufacturing facility in Dalton, Georgia.
- Qcells' Dalton facility tripled its module capacity to 5.1 GW in late 2023.
- Combined, Qcells' two Georgia facilities can produce 8.6 GW of solar modules annually at full capacity.
- The combined annual output of the two Georgia facilities equals approximately 47,000 panels.
- Andy Park, Global CEO of Qcells, stated that producing the first solar cells at Cartersville is a milestone for Qcells and American manufacturing.
- Andy Park noted that a dependable domestic supply chain creates thousands of good-paying jobs and gives customers greater certainty on price, supply, tariffs, and product trust.
- By the end of 2026, Qcells expects to employ a combined workforce of nearly 4,000 people across its two Georgia sites.
- The addition of 3.3 GW of cell capacity to the U.S. solar supply chain coincides with record-high electricity demand and solar projects rushing to meet tax credit eligibility deadlines.
- Jade Jones, senior director of solar products manufacturing and supply chain for SEIA, described the current state of U.S. solar manufacturing as 'an exciting story' during a recent webinar.
- Jones noted that solar manufacturing is taking place in 43 states and Puerto Rico.
- Jones stated that for some solar products, domestic capacity is sufficient to meet domestic demand.
- Jones observed that while there are upstream gaps, upstream manufacturing buildout typically follows downstream buildout.
- According to SEIA, U.S. solar module manufacturing grew from 8 GW prior to federal manufacturing tax credits to 69.9 GW as of June 2026, an increase of over 750%.
- In 2024, U.S. solar cell manufacturing was onshored for the first time since 2019.
- SEIA reports that about 30 GW of solar manufacturing is currently under construction in the U.S.
- While U.S. solar module manufacturing has grown steadily since 2018, ingot, wafer, and cell manufacturing has only recently begun to rise.
- Scott Moskowitz, vice president of market strategy and industrial affairs at Qcells, stated in a press conference last year that the company invested over $3 billion to build out its entire solar supply chain on U.S. soil.
- Qcells has expanded beyond vertically integrated solar manufacturing into recycling and energy storage.
- Qcells' first recycling operation is located at the Cartersville facility and is expected to recycle approximately 250 MW of solar panels annually, or about 500,000 panels per year.
- Qcells launched a new business division called 'Qcells New Homes' to provide builders with turnkey solutions for integrating solar and battery storage into new construction projects.
- Moskowitz told pv magazine USA that with storage, EPC, and solar recycling in its portfolio, Qcells is building the clean energy infrastructure the country needs.
