# Hitachi Astemo Americas, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2025-08-26

**Source:** [Hitachi Astemo Americas Sees Industrial Automation and IoT Innovation as Key to the Future of Manufacturing - Industrial Innovation](https://www.industrial-innovation.com/hitachi-astemo-americas-sees-industrial-automation-and-iot-innovation-as-key-to-the-future-of-manufacturing/)  ·  `page_008494`

## Facts

- Hitachi Astemo Americas, Inc. is a newly created company with 90,000 employees.
- The company manufactures and markets engine management, electric powertrain, and integrated vehicle controls for major automotive manufacturers worldwide.
- Hitachi Astemo is a leader in adhering to ISO/TS:16949:2009 industry standards.
- Challenges from the global pandemic, including work-from-home shifts and supply-chain problems, are spurring Hitachi Astemo to accelerate its move to more data-driven production processes in the U.S. and globally.
- The company announced investments in robotics, artificial intelligence, and other data-driven technologies to implement sweeping changes at manufacturing plants worldwide.
- Sudhanshu Gaur serves as Hitachi America vice president and chief architect, Smart Manufacturing at Hitachi Astemo.
- Gaur stated that the global pandemic, changes in work habits, and recent supply chain problems are accelerating adoption of data-driven production processes supported by machine learning, artificial intelligence, time-sensitive networking, and collaborative robotics.
- Gaur said, “We are standing at the cusp of a fourth industrial revolution driven by exponential growth in IT technologies.”
- Gaur noted there is a great opportunity for manufacturers to embrace these changes to unlock greater business value while bringing more flexibility and resiliency into their operations.
- Gaur is responsible for the digital transformation of Hitachi Astemo’s global manufacturing operations to improve overall productivity and ensure continued leadership in an Industry 4.0 vision.
- Gaur was recently named one of the top 25 leaders in the transformation of manufacturing by the Society of Manufacturing Engineers.
- Gaur served as keynote speaker at The Assembly Show held at the Donald E. Stephens Convention Center in Rosemont, Illinois.
- Gaur predicted that Hitachi Astemo’s nearly 140 manufacturing plants around the world will benefit from access to Astemo’s Global IoT platform and services within the next four years.
- During his keynote address titled “Smart Manufacturing – Today and Tomorrow,” Gaur outlined a vision of digital platforms connecting all aspects of manufacturing operations, including production, engineering, quality, inventory, and supply-chain management.
- He noted that digital platforms introduce additional flexibility into production processes and allow companies to more quickly address disruptions caused by VUCA (volatility, uncertainty, complexity, and ambiguity).
- Gaur explained that while fully automated plants are more efficient, they pose risks because tightly linked systems are generally not designed to handle unpredictability, complex situations, and rare events.
- He advocated for a gradual adoption of advanced digital technologies, bringing necessary automation while “upskilling” the workforce to manage and leverage complex systems.
- The cost for implementing a data-driven system at a medium-size U.S. manufacturing plant with 1,000 employees and $200 million in annual sales is estimated at $20,000 in one-time implementation charges plus $60,000 or more in annual running costs.
- For a large global enterprise with about 100 plants, 60,000 employees, and $10 billion in annual sales, implementing a fully digital platform could cost $1 million or more in one-time charges plus more than $3 million in annual running costs.
- Successful implementation of smart manufacturing strategies involves multiple layers of the Industrial Internet of Things (IIoT), including fog computing and edge intelligence, as well as 5G and cloud computing.
- Hitachi Astemo was formed by the recent merger of Hitachi Automotive Systems with Honda’s Keihin, Showa, and Nissin groups.
- Hitachi holds a 66.6 percent share of voting rights in Hitachi Astemo, while Honda holds the remaining 33.4 percent.
- The company holds leadership positions in AD/ADAS, software, powertrain systems, chassis systems, and motorcycle systems.
