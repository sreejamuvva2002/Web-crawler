# Quick Start Program (Technical College System of Georgia)

**Type:** workforce_training  ·  **Role:** workforce_training  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Georgia Supercharges Growth Through Worker Training – Site Selection Magazine](https://siteselection.com/georgia-supercharges-growth-through-worker-training/)  ·  `page_010155`

## Facts

- During fiscal year 2022, economic development projects in Georgia set an all-time record for job creation and investment.
- The 358 located projects supported by the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team will result in more than 51,132 new jobs and $21.2 billion in investment for the state.
- Gov. Brian Kemp has touted Georgia as the best place to do business, with Site Selection magazine ranking it as having the nation’s best business climate for most of the past decade.
- Rick Douglas, director of people and culture for Kia Georgia Inc., stated that GDEcD made it easy for the Korean automaker to select West Point, Georgia, in March 2006 for its automotive assembly plant.
- The Kia Georgia facility employs over 3,000 Georgians, covers 2,200 acres, and represents a total capital investment of $1.8 billion.
- Before the ink was dry on Kia’s announcement to build in Georgia, the Technical College System of Georgia (TCSG) dispatched its Quick Start program to create a training program for the 3,000 automotive assembly jobs.
- Jay Neely, Vice President of Law & Public Affairs at Gulfstream Aerospace, noted that Georgia’s significant investment in the Quick Start training center adds significantly to its capabilities, citing the Quick Start Advanced Manufacturing Center in Savannah as an example.
- The U.S. labor force participation rate has never been lower, with millions of pandemic-era dropouts yet to return, and some states' community college education inadequately aligned to employer needs.
- Georgia’s Quick Start program is ranked the No. 1 worker training program in the nation for 13 years in a row.
- Early in its operation, Kia Georgia faced significant difficulty recruiting maintenance team members with automation and control equipment experience, which Quick Start and TCSG resolved by creating a Maintenance Certification Program based on Kia’s needs.
- The customized training assessment for Kia was developed by Quick Start before moving to West Georgia Technical College (WGTC), where 85 Kia Georgia participants have enrolled and the program was added to WGTC’s public curriculum.
- Steven Jahng, director of external affairs for SK Battery America Inc. in Jackson County, identified the Georgia Quick Start program as the key incentive for the Korean-owned EV battery producer.
- SK Battery picked a site just off Interstate 85 in Commerce, Georgia, in November 2018 for a $1.67 billion plant, with its incentives agreement based on hiring 2,600 people.
- According to Jahng, SK Battery is ahead of schedule toward its 2,600-employee target, and its hiring plans have accelerated.
- Jahng stated that proximity to Atlanta (a 40-mile radius covering Gwinnett County and Athens) sold them on the Jackson County site due to population exposure.
- Gov. Brian Kemp and GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson provided leadership and support that sealed the deal for SK Battery.
- EV sector announcements in Georgia include Illinois-based Rivian building a $5 billion EV plant near Social Circle and Madison (about 45 miles east of Atlanta).
- Korean-based Hyundai Motor Group is spending $5.5 billion to build battery and EV plants in Savannah.
- Norwegian company FREYR Battery is investing $2.57 billion to construct an electric car battery plant in Newnan, Coweta County (just southwest of Atlanta).
- GDEcD’s Wilson noted that Quick Start has been operating since 1968 and provides a track record of preparing Georgians to fill available jobs, with companies able to leverage the program repeatedly during ramp-up.
- For Kia’s initial need to hire 1,000 people, there were 30,000 applications; Quick Start identified the best candidates and created a training program specific to Kia’s needs.
- Rodger Brown, executive director of Quick Start (overseeing the process for 21 years), stated that for the SK Battery project, they visited facilities in Korea, documented equipment/tasks, and fabricated training stations that mirrored the plant's workstations rather than just simulating them.
- Brown noted that Quick Start has worked with Kia for 15 years and will build, design, equip, and operate a training center for Hyundai in Bryan County.
- Gulfstream Aerospace is investing $150 million to expand its manufacturing plant in Savannah, adding a 262,000-square-foot facility with G400 production and wing lines plus new material delivery and distribution areas.
- As many as 200 new positions could be created by Gulfstream’s expansion, with Quick Start assisting in training.
- Jay Neely highlighted Gulfstream’s long working relationship with Georgia Quick Start for decades, praising its flexibility and ability to tailor training to specific employer requirements.
- Wilson added that transportation sector companies need a skilled workforce and can leverage Quick Start's integrated technical college system across rural communities.
