# Robert Bosch GmbH

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Bosch has set a course for the future in the difficult 2025 financial year - Bosch Media Service US](https://us.bosch-press.com/pressportal/us/en/press-release-29632.html)  ·  `page_018038`

## Facts

- Bosch reported preliminary 2025 sales revenue of 91 billion euros (up slightly from 90.3 billion in 2024), with a 4.2 percent growth after adjusting for exchange-rate effects.
- Bosch's 2025 EBIT margin from operations was around 2 percent, below the 3.5 percent achieved in 2024 and below expectations.
- Stefan Hartung, chairman of the board of management of Robert Bosch GmbH, stated that 2025 was a difficult and sometimes painful year for Bosch due to economic reality.
- Sluggish growth in the past financial year was mainly due to a weak economic environment, challenging market conditions, lack of margins from lower sales, increased tariffs, and provisions for structural adjustments and personnel measures.
- Bosch aims to generate annual sales growth of 6 to 8 percent with a margin of at least 7 percent to remain economically robust and financially independent long-term.
- Given the current environment, Bosch now expects to achieve its target margin of 7 percent in 2027 at the earliest, instead of 2026.
- Preliminary figures for 2025 indicate sales to third parties in North America were 18.7 billion USD (16.6 billion euros), an increase of approximately eight percent year-over-year.
- Bosch continues to focus on growth in the U.S. market, where it will celebrate its 120-year anniversary in 2026.
- Paul Thomas, president of Bosch in North America and president of Bosch Mobility, Americas, stated that the Bosch business in the U.S. is healthy and poised for continued growth supported by local investments.
- Bosch employs approximately 20,000 associates in the U.S., including high-quality, high-value jobs in engineering, manufacturing, software, and research and development.
- Bosch currently maintains 20 manufacturing locations in the U.S. across Arkansas, California, Florida, Illinois, Indiana, Kansas, Kentucky, North Carolina, Michigan, Minnesota, Oklahoma, Pennsylvania, South Carolina, and Tennessee.
- Since 2023, Bosch has announced more than $6 billion in U.S.-based acquisitions, resulting in more than 5,000 U.S. jobs.
- The structural shift toward electromobility and extremely high price and competitive pressure in the global automotive industry is resulting in an annual cost gap of around 2.5 billion euros worldwide in the Mobility business sector alone relative to the target margin.
- Last year, Bosch announced it needed to cut about another 13,000 jobs globally due to the shift toward electromobility and competitive pressures.
- Hartung emphasized that Bosch aims to implement job cuts in close consultation with employee representatives and in as socially acceptable a manner as possible, even if it initially incurs high costs.
- Bosch sees opportunities in software-driven mobility, assuming market momentum will initially be restrained but accelerate significantly in the coming decade.
- The Vehicle Motion Management software for central control of brakes, steering, powertrain, and chassis is already being very well received on the market.
- Last year, Bosch won customer orders for solutions for automated driving, requisite sensor technology, and central vehicle computers worth 10 billion euros.
- Bosch plans to invest a total of 2.5 billion euros in AI by the end of 2027, which is already in use throughout the company.
- Markus Forschner, member of the board of management and chief financial officer of Robert Bosch GmbH, noted that despite considerable uncertainties and trade barriers, Bosch has held its own in most markets.
- Sales revenue in the Mobility business sector was 56 billion euros in 2025, up slightly year on year by 0.3 percent (or 3.1 percent adjusted for exchange-rate effects).
- Worldwide, the Bosch Group employed some 412,400 associates as of December 31, 2025, which is about 5,400 (around 1 percent) fewer than the previous year due to job cuts and the sale of Building Technologies.
- Bosch does not expect the situation to ease in 2026, expecting global economy growth of 2.3 percent, with increased competitive/price pressure and tariffs having their full impact for the first time.
- Hartung stated that Bosch is working hard on material costs, making even more intensive use of AI to increase productivity, and weighing up every investment more carefully than before to secure competitiveness and investment capacity long-term.
- At the recent U.S. electronics trade show CES, Bosch presented an AI-enabled high-performance computer for making the AI-controlled car cockpit a reality.
