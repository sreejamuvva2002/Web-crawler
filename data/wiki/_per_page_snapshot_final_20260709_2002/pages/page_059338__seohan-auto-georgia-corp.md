# Seohan Auto Georgia Corp.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-04-13

**Source:** [Another Hyundai Supplier Lands in Coastal Georgia, Invests $72M - Global Atlanta](https://www.globalatlanta.com/another-hyundai-supplier-lands-in-coastal-georgia-invests-72m/)  ·  `page_059338`

## Facts

- South Korean parts manufacturer Seohan Auto Georgia Corp. is investing $72 million in coastal Georgia.
- Seohan Auto Georgia Corp. is the first supplier of the Hyundai Meta Plant to land in Liberty County.
- The Hyundai Meta Plant represents a $5.54 billion investment.
- The Meta Plant will be Hyundai's first purpose-built electric-vehicle factory in the United States.
- The Meta Plant broke ground last October.
- Since breaking ground, the Meta Plant has attracted seven suppliers to the area.
- These seven suppliers have pledged about $2 billion of additional investment.
- These seven suppliers have pledged to create 4,500 jobs.
- Seohan operates as a tier-one supplier.
- Seohan's products include actuators, pumps, halfshafts, crankshafts, and other parts that feed directly into Hyundai and other brands' manufacturing processes.
- The parent Seohan Group posted sales of 2.8 trillion won ($2.1 billion) in 2022.
- The Seohan Group also manufactures bearings for wind turbines.
- The Seohan Group is headquartered in Seoul and manages 16 domestic companies in Korea.
- One of Seohan's seven international affiliates is located in Auburn, Alabama.
- Seohan USA established its Auburn facility in 2008 to serve Hyundai's first U.S. plant.
- A second Seohan USA location was added in 2014.
- Both Alabama facilities received a $28.5 million expansion in 2019.
- The latest expansion at the Alabama facilities, focused on EVs, added $13.5 million in investment.
- The latest EV-focused expansion added 12 jobs last year.
- The new Georgia plant will employ 180 workers.
- The Georgia plant will focus on manufacturing electric-vehicle parts including halfshafts, axles, and brake systems.
- The company's website refers to Georgia as the "new hub of future car production."
- Jung Kee Koo is the CEO of Seohan Auto Georgia.
- Jung Kee Koo stated that sustained EV market growth suggests accelerated changes to the automotive market are inevitable.
- Jung Kee Koo believes Georgia will be the center of the EV industry and a new frontier for Seohan's future.
- The quote was issued in a news release by Gov. Brian Kemp’s office.
- Seohan's chosen site is located within the Tradeport East Business Center.
- Liberty County is the first county to secure supplier investment outside of Bryan, Bulloch, Chatham, and Effingham counties, which formed a joint development authority for the Hyundai plant.
