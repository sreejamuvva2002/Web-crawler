# Buildings Energy Efficiency Frontiers & Innovation Technologies (BENEFIT) – 2022/2023

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [DOE: CMEI eXCHANGE: Funding Opportunities](https://eere-exchange.energy.gov/Default.aspx?Search=DE-FOA-0002788&SearchType=)  ·  `page_003432`

## Facts

- The Office of Energy Efficiency and Renewable Energy (EERE) is issuing, on behalf of the Building Technologies Office (BTO), a Funding Opportunity Announcement (FOA) titled “Buildings Energy Efficiency Frontiers & Innovation Technologies (BENEFIT) – 2022/2023”.
- The 2022/2023 BENEFIT FOA will invest up to $15.35M - $45.2M across 5 topic areas to allow all interested parties to research and develop high-impact, cost-effective technologies and practices that will reduce carbon emissions, improve flexibility and resilience, as well as lower energy costs.
- Topic 1 covers Heating, Ventilation, and Air Conditioning and Water Heating: Technologies with improved materials, components, equipment design and engineering, lower cost manufacturing processes, and easier installation.
- Topic 2 covers Thermal Energy Storage (TES): Development and validation of next generation plug-and-play TES products with improved cost and performance and ease of installation to accelerate adoption of TES in HVAC applications.
- Topic 3 covers Battery Energy Storage Systems (BESS): Development, validation, and demonstration of product innovations that reduce the cost of BESS integration, improve the coordination between distributed BESS and the electrical grid, as well as help meet building decarbonization targets.
- Topic 4 covers Plug Loads/Lighting: Integration of plug load controls with connected lighting systems in commercial buildings with minimal cost and complexity to support building electrification.
- Topic 5 covers Opaque Building Envelope: Development, validation, and demonstration of high-impact, affordable opaque building envelope retrofit and diagnostic technologies.
- BTO is compiling a “teaming” partner list to facilitate widespread participation in this FOA, allowing organizations with expertise in the topics to express their interest to potential applicants and explore potential partnerships.
- The eXCHANGE system is currently designed to enforce hard deadlines for Concept Paper and Full Application submissions, with APPLY and SUBMIT buttons automatically disabling at the defined submission deadlines.
- Applicants that experience issues with submissions PRIOR to the FOA Deadline should contact the eXCHANGE helpdesk for assistance.
- Applicants that experience issues with submissions that result in a late submission should contact the eXCHANGE helpdesk for assistance, which may include finalizing the submission on behalf of the applicant with concurrence.
- DOE will only accept late applications when the Applicant has encountered technical difficulties beyond their control, contacted the eXCHANGE helpdesk for assistance, and submitted the application through eXCHANGE within 24 hours of the FOA's posted deadline.
