# Hitachi Astemo Americas, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2021-07-12

**Source:** [Hitachi Astemo Americas And Light Collaborate On Development Of ADAS Technology](https://www.advfn.com/stock-market/stock-news/85565789/hitachi-astemo-americas-and-light-collaborate-on-d)  ·  `page_008540`

## Facts

- Hitachi Astemo Americas, Inc. and Light have begun "proof of concept" development of a forward-facing perception system for use with advanced driver assistance systems.
- The two companies will begin to field test the new multi-view, camera-based sensing technology later in July 2021.
- Light's Clarity™ systems integrate seamlessly into Hitachi Astemo's advanced vehicle assistance systems (ADAS) to work directly with other ADAS and ECU (engine control unit) applications.
- John Nunneley serves as senior vice president of Design Engineering at Hitachi Astemo Americas, Inc.
- Hitachi Astemo Americas aims to create the next generation of leading ADAS and autonomous driving solutions in collaboration with Light.
- Clarity is an industry-first multi-view perception platform using cameras designed to see any 3D structures in the road from 10 centimeters to 1,000 meters away.
- The Clarity platform provides three times the range and 20x the detail of best-in-class LiDAR.
- Clarity outperforms existing sensing solutions by seamlessly combining visual information from cameras with measured depth so that longer distances and greater detail can be measured rather than only inferred by machine learning.
- Benefits of the technology include safer automatic emergency braking, evasive steering control for a wider variety of objects, adaptive suspension adjustment for road hazards such as potholes, and smoother adaptive cruise-control and lane-keeping systems.
- Dave Grannan serves as Light's co-founder and CEO.
- Hitachi Astemo Americas, Inc. manufactures and markets engine management, electric powertrain, and integrated vehicle control for major automotive manufacturers worldwide.
- Hitachi Astemo adheres to ISO/TS:16949:2009 industry standards.
- Light is pioneering real-time 3D perception by combining computational imaging with multi-camera calibration to provide accurate depth at both near and far distances in real-time.
