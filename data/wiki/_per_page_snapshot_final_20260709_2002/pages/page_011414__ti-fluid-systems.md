# TI Fluid Systems

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** thermal_management  ·  **Published:** 2023-04-17

**Source:** [PMM visits TI Fluid Systems - Professional Motor Mechanic](https://pmmonline.co.uk/article/pmm-visits-ti-fluid-systems/)  ·  `page_011414`

## Facts

- TI Fluid Systems is a maker of all fluid-related transport systems within cars.
- The company opened a new e-mobility ‘Innovation Centre’ in Rastatt.
- Thermal management is integral to regulating temperatures in electric vehicles to prevent overheating risks.
- CEO Hans Dieltjens stated that electrification is being driven by efficiency and timing, noting the European Union's decision that no more combustion engine cars will be sold by 2035.
- The company concentrated on simulation capabilities at the new facility, offering 2D and 3D simulation options to ensure efficient flow temperature at every spot in the car.
- The facility includes manufacturing of components directly on-site and prototype constructs for quick testing and real-life testing with direct feedback loops for engineers.
- TI Fluid Systems claims its approach can deliver up to 15 per cent efficiency improvements, up to 30 per cent cost improvements, and another 20 to 30 per cent in weight savings.
- Chief technology officer Johannes Helmich stated the Mobility Innovation Center was developed as a collaborative space for teams to design, visualise, prototype and test next-generation thermal management systems, modules, and components for electric vehicles.
- New products start with a need from vehicle manufacturers for greater efficiency, lighter products that take up less space.
- The product development process includes virtual engineering where a vehicle’s thermal management system is mapped out digitally with CGI graphics of the interior.
- Products are designed using CAD drawings to very strict tolerances, with virtual testing built into the process before reaching the prototype stage.
- Prototypes are made using 3D printing and brought as close to perfection as possible before progressing to manufacture.
- All products undergo extensive testing before launch, including vibration, shock and endurance testing.
