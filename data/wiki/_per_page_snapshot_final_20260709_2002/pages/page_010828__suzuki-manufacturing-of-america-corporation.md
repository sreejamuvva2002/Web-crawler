# Suzuki Manufacturing of America Corporation

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-06-14

**Source:** [Suzuki Celebrates 500,000th ATV Milestone at U.S. Plant - Gearhead Daily](https://gearheaddaily.com/suzuki-celebrates-500000th-atv-milestone-at-u-s-plant)  ·  `page_010828`

## Facts

- Suzuki Motor USA (SMO) announced the production of its 500,000th All-Terrain Vehicle (ATV) at the SMAC facility in Rome, Georgia.
- The SMAC facility has been assembling ATVs for 22 years since opening in 2002.
- Prior to 2002, Suzuki’s adult-sized ATVs were produced in Japan.
- The 500,000th ATV rolled off the assembly line is the KingQuad 750AXi, Suzuki’s flagship model known for its power and reliability.
- Masami Haga, President of SMO and SMAC, expressed pride in the achievement of building a half million ATVs in the United States.
- Masami Haga stated that North America represents the largest ATV market in the world and emphasized the importance of building these products here by skilled American workers.
- Masami Haga highlighted the role of U.S.-based suppliers in providing components essential to Suzuki’s ATVs.
- Suzuki introduced the first four-wheel ATV in 1982 and has maintained its leadership in the market for over four decades.
- Suzuki ATVs are sold through a network of over 700 franchised dealers across the United States.
- Suzuki ATVs are distributed in nearly 25 countries worldwide.
- All-Terrain Vehicles produced by Suzuki are used for recreational trail riding, hunting, agricultural work, and light construction.
- Suzuki ATVs are especially valued for their reliability and durability, making them a trusted choice for various outdoor and industrial activities.
- To commemorate the milestone, Suzuki plans to display the 500,000th ATV at its SMAC headquarters.
- Suzuki’s U.S. sales and distribution headquarters are located in Brea, California.
- SMAC operates as a subsidiary of SMO.
