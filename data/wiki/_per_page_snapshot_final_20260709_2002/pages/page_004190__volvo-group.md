# Volvo Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Volvo Group Heads to ORNL for Energy Network North America | Better Buildings & Better Plants Initiative](https://betterbuildingssolutioncenter.energy.gov/beat-blog/volvo-group-heads-ornl-energy-network-north-america)  ·  `page_004190`

## Facts

- The Volvo Group is one of the world’s leading manufacturers of trucks, buses, construction equipment and marine and industrial engines.
- In the U.S., its subsidiary Volvo Group North America (which includes Mack Trucks) has committed to improving competitiveness through energy efficiency.
- The Group operates two remanufacturing plants in the U.S. that prepare engines, transmissions, and other parts for a second life.
- The company met its first goal in the Better Plants Challenge in 2014, five years ahead of schedule.
- The company set an ambitious new goal of achieving 25% more energy savings by 2024.
- Volvo has leveraged the Better Plants program's resources through four in-plant trainings and multiple IAC assessments.
- In May of this year, the Volvo Group organized its Energy Network North America Meeting (VENNA) at Oak Ridge National Lab (ORNL).
- The VENNA meeting brought energy management staff from 8 Volvo manufacturing facilities and 6 Prevost service centers to ORNL.
- The meeting focused on Volvo’s 2017 Better Plants annual energy performance reporting activities, baselining analysis, and plant-level performance tracking.
- ORNL staff scientists Sachin Nimbalkar and Thomas Wenning introduced Volvo staff to DOE programs, including 50001 Ready for establishing energy management systems and self-attesting to ISO 50001.
- During the meeting, Volvo members shared their energy management status and discussed their 2018 energy efficiency action plan.
- Oak Ridge National Lab (ORNL) is the largest science and energy national laboratory in the DOE system by surface area and annual budget.
- ORNL is home to the world's most powerful supercomputer and pioneers new energy sources, technologies, materials, and scientific research.
- The Volvo Group team toured the Distributed Energy Communications and Controls (DECC) Laboratory and the Building Technologies Research & Integration Center (BTRIC).
- At the DECC Lab, ORNL scientists demonstrated testing of dynamic voltage and power factor controls from Distributed Energy resources and expanded capabilities for microgrid control and communications testing.
- The BTRIC tour covered multi-zone environmental chambers, building envelope research, ultrasonic drying technology, and advanced refrigerant research.
- ORNL provided a tour of the X-10 Graphite Reactor, the world's second artificial nuclear reactor and the first designed for continuous operation.
- The Better Plants program operates as a broad partner community committed to sharing energy reduction solutions.
- While in Tennessee, ORNL staff facilitated a tour of an Arconic plant (formerly part of ALCOA) for the Volvo team.
- Adam Vucelich, Director of Arconic’s Energy Services, presented Arconic’s Energy Intelligence System, which tracks energy performance at both facility and corporate levels.
- Other Arconic staff shared environmental, safety, and health best practices with the visiting Volvo team.
- Similar energy network meetings are available to all Better Plants members through their Technical Account Managers.
