# Volvo Cars

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-24

**Source:** [Volvo Planning To Max Out South Carolina Factory Production - American Cars And Racing](https://americancarsandracing.com/2025/09/24/volvo-south-carolina-factory/)  ·  `page_013958`

## Facts

- Volvo Cars is increasing its financial investment into its South Carolina manufacturing operation, signaling confidence in American-built vehicles and growing U.S. demand for luxury SUVs.
- The automaker has already invested $1.3 billion into its Ridgeville plant outside Charleston over the past decade.
- Volvo plans to continue funding the facility to reach “full utilization” in the coming years.
- The South Carolina facility currently has an annual production capacity of 150,000 vehicles.
- The Ridgeville plant currently manufactures the all-electric EX90 SUV and the Polestar 3 for both domestic and export markets.
- Volvo announced it will begin producing its best-selling XC60 SUV at the South Carolina facility starting in late 2026.
- Volvo sold 27,000 XC60s through the first eight months of 2025.
- The 2025 XC60 sales figure represents a nearly 20% increase over the same period in 2024.
- CEO Håkan Samuelsson stated that Volvo’s investment plans reinforce its long-term commitment to the U.S. market and South Carolina manufacturing operations.
- The company is celebrating 70 years of selling cars in America, with over five million vehicles delivered to U.S. customers to date.
- Volvo plans to introduce a brand-new hybrid model to the South Carolina production line before 2030, specifically designed to meet American customer preferences.
- Details regarding the new hybrid model remain undisclosed.
- The expansion aligns with Volvo’s broader strategy of manufacturing vehicles closer to their sales markets in response to the new U.S. tariff environment.
- Luis Rezende, Volvo’s president of Americas, stated that the Charleston plant is foundational to the company’s strategic growth plan for the U.S.
- Luis Rezende added that adding another model to local production will take a significant step toward realizing the full potential of Volvo’s local manufacturing investments in South Carolina.
