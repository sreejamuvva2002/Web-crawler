# Hyundai Mobis

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-05

**Source:** [Hyundai Mobis to invest nearly $1B in new supply plant in Bryan County - The Christian Index](https://christianindex.org/stories/hyundai-announces-new-georgia-plants-1b-parts-supplier,38952)  ·  `page_000374`

## Facts

- South Korean automaker Hyundai announced a second large supplier for its new electric car plant on the Georgia coast.
- Hyundai Mobis plans to invest nearly $1 billion in a new facility in Bryan County.
- The facility could employ around 1,500 workers, according to Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp.
- The plant will be located in Richmond Hill.
- The plant will make powertrains for Hyundai's electric vehicles manufactured at its new plant scheduled to open in 2025 about 30 miles west of Savannah.
- The plant will also manufacture components for a Kia plant in West Point.
- The plant will also manufacture components for another Hyundai plant in Montgomery, Alabama.
- Earlier this month, Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp announced that Joon Georgia would invest $317 million and hire 630 workers for a Hyundai parts plant in Statesboro.
- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp stated: “When we celebrated the groundbreaking of Hyundai’s new electric vehicle and battery manufacturing facility in Bryan County, we knew it would unleash transformational job creation and investment in that entire region of our state.”
- In October, Kemp attended a groundbreaking ceremony for the new Hyundai plant in Bryan County.
- The company projects the new Hyundai plant will make up to 300,000 vehicles a year.
- Georgia officials say it’s the largest economic development project the state has ever seen.
