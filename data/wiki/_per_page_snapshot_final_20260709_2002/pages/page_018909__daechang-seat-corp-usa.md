# Daechang Seat Corp. USA

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-10-25

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp: Daechang Seat Corp. USA to create 500 jobs in Chatham County  - Grice Connect](https://www.griceconnect.com/business/gov-kemp-daechang-seat-corp-usa-to-create-500-jobs-in-chatham-county-7723240)  ·  `page_018909`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Daechang Seat Corp. USA will invest $72.5 million in the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center, creating over 500 jobs.
- The new facility will be located in close proximity to Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA).
- Daechang Seat Corp. USA is a supplier specializing in automotive seat frames.
- Suppliers for the Hyundai Metaplant project alone reach at least nine counties.
- The company is being added to a growing manufacturing base centered around Chatham County and the Savannah region.
- Daechang Seat Corp. (DSC) was established in 1979 in Kyungju, South Korea.
- DSC has become one of the largest seat frame producers in the world.
- Globally, DSC employs more than 5,000 people across seven countries.
- The company's production process includes fully automatic lines with arc welding and spot-welding machines.
- Jinsuk Lee, CEO of Daechang Seat Savannah Corp., expressed appreciation to the State of Georgia, local communities, and the City of Savannah.
- DSC's primary objective is to drive growth through facility expansions to transform into a global entity.
- DSC currently holds a prominent partnership with Hyundai Transys and Hyundai Mobis at HMGMA.
- The company will hire for assembly operators, equipment technicians, mechanical engineers, and electrical engineers through local job fairs and website postings in the future.
- The facility is located only minutes from the Port of Savannah.
- Daechang Seat Corp. USA will be the third Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America supplier to locate in the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center.
- The project was supported by a partnership including the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s Global Commerce team, Savannah Economic Development Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- Alyce Thornhill, Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy at GDEcD, represented the state on this competitive project.
- As the market transitions to electric vehicles, Georgia has pursued the entire supply chain, creating more than $25 billion in investments and 30,000 jobs since 2018.
- In fiscal year 2023, job creation in Georgia's automotive industry increased by 324% compared to FY21.
- Suppliers attracted by Georgia’s mobility original equipment manufacturers resulted in over $2 billion in investment across the state in FY23.
