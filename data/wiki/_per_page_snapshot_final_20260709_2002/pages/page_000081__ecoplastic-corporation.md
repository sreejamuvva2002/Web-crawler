# Ecoplastic Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-01-05

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp: Longtime Kia and HMG Supplier to Build Manufacturing Facility in Bulloch County | Governor Brian P. Kemp Office of the Governor](https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases/2023-01-05/gov-kemp-longtime-kia-and-hmg-supplier-build-manufacturing-facility)  ·  `page_000081`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Ecoplastic Corporation will create 456 new jobs and invest approximately $205 million in a new manufacturing facility in Bulloch County.
- Ecoplastic Corporation was established in June 1984 as Apollo Ind Co., Ltd.
- Ecoplastic is a Korea-based company that manufactures plastic automobile parts for original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) and other suppliers.
- Ecoplastic is a Tier-1 supplier for Hyundai Motor Group (HMG) and Kia.
- The company engages in the manufacturing of automotive interior and exterior plastic parts, including bumpers, consoles, trims, and plastic molds.
- Shang Han serves as President and CEO of Ecoplastic.
- Ecoplastic's new facility will be located at 4822 Highway 301 South Register near Statesboro.
- The new plant will be capable of injection molding and painting.
- The facility will produce exterior parts, bumpers and garnishes, interior parts, consoles, and trims for electric vehicles.
- The company expects to begin operations by October 2024.
- Ecoplastic will be hiring for HR, finance, parts development, quality control, facility maintenance, injection, and paint production positions.
- Billy Allen serves as Chair of the Development Authority of Bulloch County.
- Alyce Thornhill, Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy at the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team, represented the agency on this project.
- The project was developed in partnership with the Development Authority of Bulloch County, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia EMC.
- Pat Wilson serves as GDEcD Commissioner.
- Since 2020, more than 30 EV-related projects have announced more than $21 billion in investment and more than 26,300 jobs in Georgia.
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America officially broke ground on October 25 in neighboring Bryan County for its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility.
- Offsite suppliers connected to the Hyundai project were predicted to invest approximately $1 billion in other locations around the state.
- Ecoplastic currently employs more than 770 people.
- Ecoplastic recorded sales revenue of more than $700 million in 2021.
- Ecoplastic established its first Technical Research Center in 1987.
- Ecoplastic was publicly listed on the Korean Stock Exchange in March 2000.
