# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-08-27

**Source:** [Hyundai’s Georgia EV plant faces permit review over water impact concerns](https://koreajoongangdaily.joins.com/news/2024-08-27/business/industry/Hyundais-Georgia-EV-plant-faces-permit-review-over-water-impact-concerns/2121468)  ·  `page_001017`

## Facts

- A U.S. agency plans to reexamine the environmental permit for Hyundai Motor Group’s EV factory in Georgia ahead of its planned opening in October.
- The U.S. Army Corps of Engineers (Usace) agreed to reassess the permit for Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (Hmgma) after a conservation group alleged that the impact of the plant on the local water supply was not properly assessed.
- Hmgma released a statement saying it is “aware of the request” and will cooperate with the reassessment.
- Hmgma stated it is confident that Usace will supply the relevant data in a timely manner and will assist them as needed.
- Hmgma added that it adheres to internationally recognized standards and guidelines to reduce environmental impacts and protect communities.
- The plant is built on a 2,923-acre site in Bryan County, Georgia.
- The HMGMA plant is scheduled to start production in early 2025 or sooner.
- The plant is set to have an annual capacity of 300,000 vehicles, including Hyundai, Genesis and Kia EVs.
- A letter from Usace said that the plant’s 2022 permit did not address the factory’s plans to withdraw up to 6.6 million gallons of local underground water per day.
- The plan to withdraw water emerged earlier this year when the Georgia Environmental Protection Division considered a proposal to provide four new wells for the factory’s water supply.
- Usace stated that the "permit is still valid [at this time]" and that the agency "has not requested that the permittee stop work."
- The new factory is expected to create about 8,100 new jobs, according to Hmgma.
- Georgia officials say it’s the largest economic development project the state has ever seen.
