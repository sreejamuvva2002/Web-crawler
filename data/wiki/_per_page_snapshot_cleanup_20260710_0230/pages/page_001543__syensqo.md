# Syensqo

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2023-12-11

**Source:** [Syensqo - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Syensqo)  ·  `page_001543`

## Facts

- Syensqo is a Belgian multinational chemicals and advanced materials company founded in December 2023 through a spin-off from Solvay.
- The company's CEO is Mike Radossich, who succeeded Ilham Kadri in January 2026, with Heike Van de Kerkhof serving as Chair of the Board.
- Syensqo went public on December 11, 2023, listing on Euronext Brussels and joining the BEL20 index before voluntarily delisting from Euronext Paris on September 16, 2024.
- The company operates globally with 54 industrial sites, 12 major R&D centers, and 13,000 employees across 30 countries, reporting €5,969 million in revenue for 2025.
- Syensqo's business focuses on specialty polymers, composites, and specialty chemicals, with growth platforms in batteries, green hydrogen, thermoplastic composites, renewable materials, and biotechnology.
- The company produces high-performance composite materials including thermoset and thermoplastic composites, carbon fiber prepregs, resin systems, and adhesives for automotive, aerospace, and energy applications.
- Syensqo manufactures specialty polymers such as fluoropolymers (PVDF, ECTFE, PTFE), fluoroelastomers (FKM), perfluoroelastomers (FFKM), and fluorinated fluids (PFPE) used in electronics, energy, chemical processing, and semiconductor manufacturing.
- The company introduced fluorinated elastomers manufactured without certain fluorosurfactants associated with PFAS chemistry.
- Syensqo produces perfluorosulfonic acid (PFSA) ionomers used as ion-conducting materials in electrochemical technologies like fuel cells and water electrolysis.
- Electroactive polymers (EAPs) produced by the company are utilized in sensors, actuators, and related devices.
- Syensqo supplies specialty chemical reagents used in the recovery and separation of critical battery metals including lithium, nickel, and cobalt.
- The company develops bio-based and circular materials incorporating renewable or recycled feedstocks for applications in automotive, transportation, and industrial sectors.
- In the United States, Syensqo operates 26 production sites and 9 R&D centers across locations including California, Minnesota, Ohio, Tennessee, and Georgia.
- The company maintains facilities in Alpharetta and Winder, Georgia, alongside a dedicated site in Augusta, Georgia that supports materials used in electric-vehicle battery applications.
- Syensqo became a sponsor of the DS Penske Formula E racing team in January 2024 through a three-year partnership agreement.
- The company's bio-based carbon fiber composites have been utilized in Mercedes-AMG's W16 race car.
- In May 2024, Syensqo served as the main partner alongside Mohammed VI Polytechnic University and OCP Group for the Climate Impulse project, aiming to fly a green hydrogen-powered aircraft around the world by 2028.
- The company established a Scientific Advisory Board in June 2024 featuring academics Ben Feringa, Avelino Corma, Karim Zaghib, and Juan de Pablo to support innovation projects.
- Syensqo manufactures components for the Hermes 450 drone at its UK facility for UAV Tactical Systems, a company co-owned by Elbits Systems Ltd and Thales.
- The company recognizes scientific advancements through the Science for the Future Ernest Solvay Prize, with recent recipients including Xiaowei Zhuang (2026), Omar Yaghi (2024), Katalin Karikó (2022), and others.
