# Dongwon Autopart Technology

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-02-04

**Source:** [$30M project to bring 200 new jobs to Emanuel County](https://www.wrdw.com/2026/02/04/30m-project-bring-200-new-jobs-emanuel-county/)  ·  `page_053952`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Dongwon Autopart Technology plans to invest $30 million in a new production facility in Emanuel County.
- The project is anticipated to create 200 new jobs over the next several years.
- Dongwon Autopart Technology was established in 1971.
- The company supplies components such as doorframes, seat frames and battery frames to automotive original equipment manufacturers.
- The company supports more than 500 direct jobs in the Southeast, including at facilities in Hogansville, Ga., which will remain operational.
- Dongwon Autopart Technology has been in the United States since 2003.
- Construction on the new manufacturing facility will be located at the Highway 297 Industrial Park near Swainsboro.
- The company will be hiring for positions such as management, system leads and production personnel, with hiring expected to begin in 2026.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Georgia’s thriving automotive industry is creating new opportunities for Georgians statewide and expressed excitement for the project's impact on Emanuel County.
- Heather Hollstein, Human Resources Manager at Dongwon Autopart Technology, said the company is proud to be part of growing the state’s automotive industry and remains committed to supplying internal and external customers with high-quality products and service.
- Will Donaldson, Chairman of the Emanuel County Development Authority, welcomed Dongwon Autopart Technology to Emanuel County and noted that their time, energy, and investment in economic development have paid off for the community.
