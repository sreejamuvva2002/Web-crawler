# SungEel Recycling Park Georgia, LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2023-09-14

**Source:** [SCDA releases MOU with SungEel outlining incentives - WNEG](https://wnegradio.com/scda-releases-mou-with-sungeel-outlining-incentives-announces-construction-schedule/)  ·  `page_050820`

## Facts

- SungEel Recycling Park Georgia, LLC is a Korean-based lithium battery recycler that has purchased property in the Hayestone Brady Industrial Park to locate a new recycling facility.
- The company will pay ad-valorem taxes on the property this year, marking the first time the county has collected taxes on the property for many years.
- Stephens County Development Authority (SCDA) does not pay taxes on property it owns, but SungEel paid full price for its property and will pay taxes this year.
- The company will receive a tax abatement in the coming years as part of an incentive package completed and recorded with the SCDA.
- SCDA President and CEO Brittany Ivey explained that local incentives are necessary for the state to offer incentives, and without them, Stephens County would not have been in the running for the development.
- The tax abatement follows a declining schedule: 0 percent in year one of operation, 20 percent in year two, adding another 20 percent each year until year six, when the company will pay 100 percent of taxes due.
- The tax abatement incentive is legally tied through the MOU to financial investment levels and employment figures.
- The city has agreed to waive tap fees for city utilities in exchange for the company obtaining utilities through the city where possible.
- An MOU between SungEel and the State of Georgia was signed on April 17 of this year.
- The state MOU stipulates a REBA Grant in the amount of $700,000 will be awarded to SungEel through the Stephens County Development Authority.
- REBA Grant funding comes from the Georgia Department of Community Affairs and is used to help close deals when companies consider Georgia versus other locations or countries.
- REBA funds may finance fixed-asset needs including infrastructure, real estate acquisition, construction, or machinery and equipment.
- WNEG News will publish a multi-part series on facility safety questions posed to SungEel officials earlier in the month and their responses.
