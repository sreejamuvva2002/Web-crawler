# Electric Mobility and Innovation Alliance (EMIA)

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2021-08-06

**Source:** [Governor Kemp, Georgia Department of Economic Development Announce Leadership of Electric Mobility and Innovation Initiative | Governor Brian P. Kemp Office of the Governor](https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases/2021-08-06/governor-kemp-georgia-department-economic-development-announce-leadership)  ·  `page_000091`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp and First Lady Marty Kemp, in conjunction with the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD), held the official launch of the Electric Mobility and Innovation Alliance (EMIA) at the Governor’s Mansion.
- The EMIA launch included partners from the private and public sectors who will work on five committees to develop a multi-step approach setting state objectives for Supply Chain, Infrastructure, Workforce, Innovation, and Policy/Initiatives.
- Stuart Countess, COO of Kia Georgia, was announced as the chair of the Supply Chain committee.
- Jannine Miller, Director of Planning for the Georgia Department of Transportation, was announced as the chair of the Infrastructure committee.
- Randall “Randy” Hatcher, President of MAU Workforce Solutions, was announced as the chair of the Workforce committee.
- State Representative Bruce Williamson was announced as the chair of the Policy and Initiatives committee.
- Cynthia Curry, Director of Smart Cities Ecosystem Expansion at the Metro Atlanta Chamber, was announced as the chair of the Innovation committee.
- The Carl Vinson Institute of Government at the University of Georgia will host EMIA’s future committee meetings and integrated research forums.
- Leaders from the Carl Vinson Institute were introduced at the launch event.
- Governor Kemp stated that the Georgia Electric Mobility and Innovation Alliance’s recommendations will help make strategic decisions that align with industry needs.
- Governor Kemp stated that maintaining business-friendly policies will ensure Georgians have the greatest opportunities for the jobs of the future.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that the alliance announcement is already garnering attention in the mobility industry.
- Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that the diverse group of experts leading EMIA and their knowledge, experience, and ideas will help position Georgia to further develop the electric mobility ecosystem.
- Governor Kemp announced the formation of the Electric Mobility and Innovation Alliance in July 2021.
- EMIA will offer policy recommendations intended to further enhance the state’s attractiveness to the electric mobility industry and foster innovation in related fields, including drones, autonomous vehicles, connected vehicles, battery technology, and more.
- In conjunction with the initiative, GDEcD launched new website assets for the program.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) serves as the state’s sales and marketing arm and the lead agency for attracting new business investment, encouraging industry expansion, locating new markets, promoting tourism, and planning/mobilizing state resources for economic development.
