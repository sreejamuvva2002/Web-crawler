# Yamaha Motor Co.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-03-10

**Source:** [Yamaha Motor Co. to relocate U.S. HQ to Kennesaw - Rough Draft Atlanta](https://roughdraftatlanta.com/2026/03/10/yamaha-kennesaw-georgia-move/)  ·  `page_015663`

## Facts

- Yamaha Motor Co. will relocate its U.S. headquarters to Kennesaw, Georgia.
- The relocation follows more than 50 years of operations in California.
- The move comes after a meeting between Gov. Brian Kemp and Yamaha executives last October at the Southeast U.S./Japan (SEUS-Japan) annual gathering in Tokyo.
- Yamaha is a manufacturer of ATVs, boat engines, personal watercraft, and other motorized products.
- Yamaha currently employs over 2,300 Georgians across its operations in the state.
- The headquarters relocation will begin this year (2026) and is expected to continue through 2028.
- Gov. Brian Kemp stated he is honored and proud to welcome Yamaha’s American headquarters to the No. 1 state for business.
- Kemp noted the move serves as a testament to what Georgia offers job creators from around the world.
- Kemp encouraged other California-based companies looking for a better home to keep Georgia on their mind.
- The 1.3-million-square-foot Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation of America factory in Newnan opened in 1986.
- The Newnan factory currently employs roughly 2,000 Georgians.
- Yamaha relocated its marine division to Kennesaw in 1999.
- The Kennesaw facility was expanded with a 75,000-square-foot Marine Innovation Center in 2023.
- In 2019, the company moved its motorsports operations to Georgia to benefit from proximity to existing facilities.
