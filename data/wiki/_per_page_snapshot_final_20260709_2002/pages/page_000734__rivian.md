## Facts

- Electric vehicle maker Rivian has pressed pause on the construction of a major manufacturing plant in Stanton Springs, Georgia.
- The company selected Clayco and Skidmore, Owings & Merrill to deliver the 1,800-acre complex.
- In March, the company announced a pause in construction of the $5 billion facility.
- Rivian stated that the plant remains an extremely important part of its strategy to scale production of R2 and R3 car models.
- The timing for resuming construction is expected to be later to focus its teams on the capital-efficient launch of R2 in Normal, Illinois.
- An email sent from the company in April underscored a commitment to the project.
- During the pause period, Rivian anticipates performing some limited site development activities.
- The depth and breadth of this work has not yet been determined.
