# Wabash National

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Wabash Makes Big Changes That Are Starting to Pay Off | IndustryWeek](https://www.industryweek.com/profiles-in-leadership/article/21277290/wabash-makes-big-changes-that-are-starting-to-pay-off)  ·  `page_014820`

## Facts

- Wabash National is a truck-trailer manufacturer that narrowed its portfolio and shed product lines including aircraft fueling equipment and healthcare containment systems.
- The company streamlined from three sometimes-redundant divisions to one under President and CEO Brent Yeagy, who has led since 2018.
- Wabash saw record earnings in 2022 under challenging transportation market conditions.
- Recognizing the truck market has become less cyclical due to the boom in on-demand e-commerce delivery, Wabash inked longer-term relationships with key suppliers Hydro for aluminum and Ryerson for steel.
- The company secured a customer relationship with J.B. Hunt for 15,000 trailers and partnered with technology provider Fernweh for digital service and e-commerce.
- Yeagy and Chief Strategy Officer Dustin Smith led an organizational realignment that ushered in a new C-suite of leaders who had been with the company for an average of eight to 10 years.
- The company deviated from its previous strategy of becoming a diversified industrial manufacturer to focus on transportation, logistics and distribution, a pivot the team recognized as necessary as early as 2015.
- Wabash moved away from strategic business units to a "OneWabash" approach involving hybrid centralization, changing the roles of almost 40 people while only Yeagy's role remained unstructured.
- The previous organizational design was rife with waste and duplicity, stacking dollars and people on non-value-added activity, which the reorganization freed up to redeploy for greater value over the next three to four years.
- Centralization allowed the company to pull leadership away from market details to see broader opportunities and reallocate resources quickly in an agile fashion.
- Dustin Smith was moved from a strategic business unit leader with dedicated cross-functional resources to a strategy role to aggregate manufacturing and focus on future strategic actions, innovation, and value creation.
- Under the new model, sales leaders sell the entire portfolio instead of one product, and engineers design products for multiple segments rather than being trapped in specific product areas like flatbeds.
- In 2020, Wabash had deployed over $700 million over the previous eight years to add tanks and truck bodies through acquisition without organizing those business units to serve customers.
- The company implemented a new commercial strategy called "first to final mile," bundling product services, pricing, and value creation to increase both growth and profitability simultaneously.
- Wabash is partnering with impactful suppliers to provide a total enterprise supply chain, creating five-year-plus agreements on the customer side and five- to 10-year agreements on the supplier side that include demand and capacity planning, advanced product development, and engineering.
- Approximately 18 to 20% of annual dry van trailer revenue is allocated to long-term-agreement customers, representing $500 to $600 million a year, with expectations for this to grow slightly to maybe 30% of total dry van trailer revenue.
- Product innovation focuses on advanced material science for lightweighting, durability/asset-life, and thermal efficiency in full-chain products.
- The company is focusing on electrification and autonomy to aid EV technology adoption and become a leader in autonomous adoption.
- In the near-term, Wabash is working through which EV providers customers need to do business with for vocational and delivery vehicles to create the right relationships.
- Mid-term focus involves adapting truck bodies, trailers, and tankers for near-term regulatory and weight-related innovation to offset battery weight and maintain revenue-carrying capacity.
- Long-term focus includes exploring solar technology, scavenging energy off vehicle movement, and advancing hybrid electric technology to increase EV adoption scale rapidly.
- Supply chain challenges have shifted from large-scale disruption to a lack of resiliency across the overall supply chain, extending from tier one to tier four.
- The efficiency of the pre-pandemic supply chain has been broken, leaving tier three, four, and five suppliers at an economic disadvantage in an inflationary environment.
- Wabash is at the tail end of a freight recession that has lasted about 18 to 20 months and should conclude in the first half of 2024, with demand down year over year but not negative.
- The company is at its best balance-sheet perspective ever, coming on record profitability and record earnings per share, enabling continued investment in its strategic plan for ‘25 and ‘26.
