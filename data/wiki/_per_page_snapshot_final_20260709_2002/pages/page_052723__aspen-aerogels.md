# Aspen Aerogels

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** thermal_management  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Aspen Aerogels (ASPN) Q1 2025: EV Thermal Barrier Revenue Drops 25% as Inventory Destocking Reshapes Outlook | EarningsIQ](https://www.earningsiq.co/articles/aspen_aerogels_aspn_q1_2025_ev_thermal_barrier_revenue_drops)  ·  `page_052723`

## Facts

- EV thermal barrier revenue dropped 25% in Q1 2025 as OEM production schedules reset and distributors cleared excess inventory.
- Total company revenue fell 17% year-over-year in Q1 2025.
- The EV thermal barrier segment contributed $48.9 million to Q1 2025 revenue, representing roughly 62% of total company revenue.
- Gross margins in the EV segment dropped to 23%, missing a 35% target, due to suboptimal fixed cost absorption and strategic pricing actions.
- Company-wide gross profit fell 35% year-over-year in Q1 2025.
- Adjusted EBITDA for Q1 2025 was positive but modest at $4.9 million.
- Working capital gains from a $31.9 million reduction in accounts receivable were offset by $13 million in CapEx.
- The $13 million in CapEx was largely allocated to ongoing obligations in Georgia and equipment for future launches.
- Strategic cost actions aim to reduce the revenue required for EBITDA breakeven by $90 million.
- Fixed cost reductions have lowered the revenue threshold needed for positive operating income to $245 million.
- Management aims to deliver at least $20 million of adjusted EBITDA on annualized revenues as low as $250 million.
- Aspen Aerogels secured OEM awards with General Motors, Mercedes-Benz, and Volvo Truck for next-generation battery platforms.
- The industry shift from pouch to prismatic cell batteries is structurally reducing EV thermal barrier content per vehicle from over $1,000 to $200-$250.
- Management notes that the mix shift toward prismatic cells allows for better equipment utilization across multiple OEMs.
- Energy industrial revenue grew 2% year-over-year to $29.8 million in Q1 2025 as distributors cleared excess inventory.
- Aspen targets a full-year energy industrial revenue outcome similar to 2024's $145.9 million.
- The company utilizes dual sourcing and regionalized production across its U.S. and Mexican operations, plus an external manufacturing facility (EMF), to mitigate tariff risks.
- Most of Aspen's EV thermal barrier products are USMCA compliant and exempt from tariffs.
- Targeted exemptions for energy industrial products cap raw material tariff exposure at under $4.5 million in 2025.
- Aspen Aerogels is actively monetizing its Georgia facility, aiming to recoup cash quickly through equipment sales and potential site disposition.
- Record quoting activity and new OEM engagements with Korean cell makers indicate a robust medium-term commercial pipeline.
- Management expects additional OEM wins to begin impacting financial results meaningfully from 2027 onward.
- European customers are currently supplied from Mexico to leverage cost advantages and minimize risk.
- Aspen guided for Q2 2025 revenue of $70 to $80 million and adjusted EBITDA between breakeven and $7 million.
- Full-year 2025 baseline expectations include at least $280 million in revenue and $20 million in adjusted EBITDA.
- CapEx for the full year is tightly controlled, targeting less than $25 million (excluding Georgia demobilization costs).
- Don Young serves as President and Chief Executive Officer of Aspen Aerogels.
- Ricardo Rodriguez serves as Chief Financial Officer and Treasurer of Aspen Aerogels.
