# HLI Green Power

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-06

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor completes EV ecosystem in Indonesia - THE INVESTOR](https://m.theinvestor.co.kr/article/3428319)  ·  `page_009321`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group launched a new joint battery cell manufacturing facility with LG Energy Solution in Indonesia.
- The facility is set to supply cells for Hyundai's upcoming Kona Electric.
- This makes Hyundai the first automaker in Indonesia with a fully localized production ecosystem for electric vehicles, spanning from battery cells to finished vehicles.
- Hyundai Motor Group celebrated the opening of the joint HLI Green Power plant in Indonesia's Karawang New Industry City on Wednesday.
- The event was attended by Hyundai Motor Group Executive Chair Chung Euisun, LG Energy Solution CEO Kim Dong-myung, and Indonesian President Joko Widodo.
- The event marked the beginning of mass production for the new Kona Electric in Hyundai’s existing Indonesian car plant.
- Hyundai Motor Group Executive Chair Chung Euisun stated that the opening marks a major step forward in collaboration with Indonesia and is a key milestone in developing a sustainable and robust EV ecosystem.
- The facility is a key part of Hyundai's strategy to build a strong electric vehicle ecosystem in Southeast Asia.
- Hyundai has already made large investments in EV infrastructure in Korea and is now expanding these efforts to Indonesia.
- The goal is to create a comprehensive local EV ecosystem that includes sourcing raw materials, producing batteries and vehicles, supporting charging networks, and recycling batteries.
- HLI Green Power, a joint venture between Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution, started in September 2021.
- The plant began producing battery cells in the second quarter of this year after trial production in late 2022.
- The plant covers 320,000 square meters.
- The facility can produce 10 gigawatt-hours of battery cells a year.
- This capacity is sufficient to power over 150,000 EVs.
- The cells are high-performance nickel, cobalt, manganese and aluminum lithium-ion cells.
- These cells enhance power output and reduce chemical instability.
- The cells are intended for use in Hyundai and Kia models including the new Kona Electric.
- The new Kona Electric is scheduled for launch in Indonesia on July 17.
- The Kona Electric is the second electric vehicle to be manufactured at Hyundai's Indonesian facility, following the Ioniq 5.
- Hyundai aims to establish Kona Electric as a flagship model in Indonesia's expanding electric vehicle market.
- Indonesia is advancing its EV industry to achieve carbon neutrality by 2060.
- Indonesia targets an annual production of 600,000 EVs by 2030.
- The Indonesian government plans to convert over 10,000 fleet vehicles to EVs each year from 2021 to 2030, totaling more than 130,000 vehicles.
- Currently, EV demand in Indonesia is low, with only 18,000 units sold in 2023.
- This represents about 2 percent of the total automotive market.
- Hyundai projects this share will reach double digits by 2030.
- The projected growth is spurred by local initiatives and competition from Chinese EV manufacturers.
