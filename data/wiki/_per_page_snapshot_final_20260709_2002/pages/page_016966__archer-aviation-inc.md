# Archer Aviation Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-07-31

**Source:** [Archer Aviation showcases Midnight aircraft in Atlanta as it nears completion of high-volume manufacturing facility in Covington, Georgia | Press Release | Business Air News](https://www.businessairnews.com/hb_news_story.html?release=92517)  ·  `page_016966`

## Facts

- On July 31, 2024, Archer Aviation Inc. issued a press release providing an update on the construction progress of its high-volume manufacturing facility in Covington, Georgia.
- Archer reaffirmed an expected 2024 completion date for the Covington facility.
- In preparation for this facility's completion, last week Archer showcased its Midnight aircraft at Atlantic Aviation's new FBO terminal at DeKalb–Peachtree Municipal Airport in Atlanta.
- Guests included government officials from Georgia's Office of the Governor, Georgia's Congressional Offices, the City of Atlanta, Georgia's State Legislature, the Federal Aviation Administration and other local governments.
- Archer completed the first build phase earlier this year, inclusive of grading and foundation work.
- The company is nearing completion of phase two, the erection of the exterior walls and roof.
- Phase three, comprising interior fit out, and phase four, the equipment load in and power on, are on track to be completed in the coming months.
- Once complete, this facility will be nearly 400,000 square feet.
- The facility will support Archer's planned production of up to 650 Midnight aircraft annually.
- This production volume would make it one of the largest manufacturing facilities by volume in the aviation industry.
- Archer's buildout is taking place in close partnership with Stellantis.
- Archer and Stellantis are continuing to progress their plans to mass-produce Archer's Midnight aircraft together.
- Adam Goldstein, CEO and founder of Archer, stated that thanks to the hard work of the Archer and Stellantis teams, they are tracking towards completing this manufacturing facility by the end of the year.
- Goldstein noted that once complete, Archer will focus on ramping production up to 650 aircraft a year and aims to be the first in the eVTOL industry to achieve scaled manufacturing.
- Midnight is Archer's electric vertical take-off and landing aircraft.
- The Midnight aircraft is built around Archer's proprietary electric powertrain.
- Midnight is designed to offer passengers a sustainable, low-noise and safe alternative to ground transportation.
- The aircraft travels at speeds up to 150 mph, turning hour-long ground commutes into minutes in the air.
- Midnight is designed for back-to-back flights of 20-50 miles with minimal charge time in between.
- The aircraft is 100 times quieter than a helicopter at cruising altitudes and nearly inaudible from the streets below.
- Midnight features redundant systems across the aircraft allowing Archer to target similar levels of safety as commercial airliners.
- The Midnight is designed to carry a pilot, up to four passengers and carry-on luggage.
