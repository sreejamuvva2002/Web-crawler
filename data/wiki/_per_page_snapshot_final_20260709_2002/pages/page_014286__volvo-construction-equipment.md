# Volvo Construction Equipment

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Volvo Construction Equipment to Expand Production in North America](https://www.volvoce.com/united-states/en-us/about-us/news/2025/volvo-ce-to-expand-production-in-north-america/)  ·  `page_014286`

## Facts

- Volvo Construction Equipment (Volvo CE) is making a significant investment to expand production worldwide.
- Approximately $261 million will be invested across three main sites for crawler excavator production: Shippensburg, Penn., USA; Changwon, South Korea; and a location in Sweden.
- In Shippensburg, Volvo CE will add crawler excavator production and expand wheel loader production to include large wheel loaders.
- The Shippensburg factory currently manufactures soil and asphalt compactors and mid-size wheel loaders.
- Updates to the existing Shippensburg factory space will include installing assembly lines, integrating more automation technologies into the manufacturing process, and training employees.
- The goal is to be production-ready in the first half of 2026.
- Melker Jernberg, Head of Volvo CE, stated that strategically enhancing production capacity and flexibility will allow the OEM to meet current and future customer demands more effectively in a rapidly evolving market.
- Expanding production capabilities in key markets will reduce Volvo CE's dependency on any single site and decrease reliance on long-distance logistics.
- Supply chain risks will be mitigated by expanding domestic supplier bases, allowing the OEM to more nimbly manage economic or regulatory challenges.
- Jernberg noted that fostering collaboration with local suppliers and customers will better position the company for sustained growth and innovation without compromising Volvo CE equipment's high standards.
- Scott Young, Head of Region North America, stated that bringing excavator production to North America and growing the range of wheel loader models built there has always been part of their long-term industrial plan.
- The increase in production capacity means that over 50% of Volvo CE's North American machine supply can be built in Shippensburg, resulting in shorter lead times while creating opportunities for supplier growth.
- Volvo CE acquired the Shippensburg site in 2007 from Ingersoll Rand and relocated its regional headquarters there in 2012.
- On top of recent investments, Volvo CE will invest approximately $40 million locally over the next five years.
