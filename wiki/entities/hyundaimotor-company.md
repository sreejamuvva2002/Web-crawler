# HyundaiMotor Company

**Type:** Company  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[vehicle-manufacturing|Vehicle Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** Bryan County, Georgia, Bryan County, Georgia, United States

## Overview

Hyundai Motor Company announced a historic $5.54 billion investment to build its first dedicated full electric-vehicle and battery manufacturing facilities in the United States, located on a 2,923-acre site in Bryan County, Georgia.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Construction of an EV assembly line and battery cell plant that will produce vehicles and batteries for the U.S. market.

## Key facts

- Hyundai announced a $5.54 billion investment that will create roughly 8,100 jobs in Georgia.
- The plant will be Hyundai’s first dedicated full EV and battery manufacturing facilities in the United States.
- Located on a 2,923-acre site with immediate access to I-95, I-16, near the Port of Savannah and served by two Class I rail lines.
- Hyundai will partner with SK Innovation to power select Hyundai EVs with Georgia-made SK batteries.
- Sen. Ossoff’s economic delegation to Seoul was a key factor in attracting the investment.
- Governor Brian Kemp, Senator Jon Ossoff, and Senator Raphael Warnock publicly supported the project.
- The site’s proximity to major transportation corridors is intended to support logistics for vehicle and battery distribution.
