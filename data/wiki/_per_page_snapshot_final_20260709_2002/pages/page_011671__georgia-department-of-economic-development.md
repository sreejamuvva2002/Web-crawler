# Georgia Department of Economic Development

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2023-08-09

**Source:** [Kemp and GDEcD announce results for FY23](https://www.wuga.org/local-news/2023-08-09/kemp-and-gdecd-announce-results-for-fy23)  ·  `page_011671`

## Facts

- Georgia Governor Brian P. Kemp and the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) announced record-breaking economic development results for Fiscal Year 2023 (FY23).
- The state achieved more than $24 billion in investments for facility expansions and new locations.
- These investments created 38,400 new jobs through 426 projects supported by GDEcD's Global Commerce team.
- Excluding the two most significant projects from the previous year, Georgia still exceeded the last job creation record by over 2,800 new positions.
- The state's annual investment totals have risen 131 percent over the past three years.
- Around 82 percent of the new jobs and over $20 billion in investments are heading to communities outside the 10-county Atlanta region.
- Metro Atlanta attracted $3.6 billion in investment during the previous fiscal year.
- Expansion projects accounted for 71 percent of the total projects.
- New locations contributed to 65 percent of new job announcements.
- Governor Kemp's administration focused on the future of mobility, with over 40 e-mobility projects announced between 2018 and 2023.
- These e-mobility projects resulted in over 30,000 jobs and more than $25 billion in investments.
- The automotive industry saw a 324 percent increase in job creation compared to FY21, driven by e-mobility and cleantech projects.
- Investments in aerospace-related projects increased by over 1,000 percent since FY21.
- Initiatives like the 'Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development' (GRAD) certification and Georgia Quick Start programs contributed to a 112 percent growth in advanced manufacturing investments since FY21.
- Foreign investments were attracted from countries including South Korea, Finland, Norway, Japan, Switzerland, and the Netherlands.
- Governor Kemp and GDEcD officials conducted international economic development missions to strengthen relationships and foster economic opportunities.
