# Southern Company

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2025-11-05

**Source:** [Georgia voters oust GOP utility commissioners over rising electricity rates - AOL](https://www.aol.com/articles/georgia-voters-oust-gop-utility-172520734.html)  ·  `page_012072`

## Facts

- Georgia voters unseated two Republican utility commissioners over escalating electricity rates on Tuesday night.
- The election outcome could signal an early consumer backlash against data centers and Republican President Donald Trump's aggressive push to develop nuclear power.
- AI-fueled data centers' energy needs are forcing utility regulators nationwide to address the cost of building billions of dollars in infrastructure projects for new generation and upgraded transmission lines.
- Consumers have faced higher rates as demand outpaces available power supply.
- Jefferies downgraded Southern Co. stock to a "Hold" rating from a "Buy" rating following the special election outcome.
- Jefferies analysts called the election outcome a "resounding Republican loss" and stated it boosts the risk profile of Georgia Power, a unit of Southern Co., making it harder to increase electricity usage rates.
- Summer electric bills for Georgia Power customers using 1,000 kilowatts increased 41% to nearly $190 per month compared to the summer of 2021, according to a survey by the Georgia Public Service Commission.
- Democrats Alicia Johnson and Peter Hubbard easily unseated Republican incumbent commissioners, each winning more than 60% of the votes cast.
- Johnson and Hubbard will take seats on Georgia's Public Service Commission, which regulates major utilities and sets electric rates.
- Both candidates ran campaigns championing fair electric rates and more emphasis on renewable energy.
- Electricity rates have increased six times in the past two years, according to their online campaign platforms.
- Georgia electric bills partly reflect massive cost overruns from the construction of the two Vogtle nuclear reactors, which were about seven years behind schedule and cost about $35 billion.
- The Vogtle project cost was more than double the original estimate of $14 billion, according to Georgia regulators.
- Patty Durand, director of nonprofit Georgians for Affordable Energy, called the election a referendum on nuclear power.
- The Trump administration announced an $80 billion deal to buy nuclear reactors from Westinghouse last week.
- Westinghouse previously landed in bankruptcy in 2017 because of cost overruns at the Georgia nuclear projects.
- A January 2023 report to the Georgia Public Service Commission estimated electric customers would pay an additional $36 billion to $43 billion over the 60-year lifecycle of the two Vogtle reactors compared to an alternative gas-fired fuel source.
- Durand stated that "Georgia electric customers are getting electric bills that look like monthly car payments."
