# Rivian Manufacturing Facility (Stanton Springs North, Georgia)

**Source:** [Rivian Boosts Georgia EV Plant Capacity to 300,000 Units | EV.com](https://ev.com/news/rivian-boosts-georgia-ev-plant-capacity-to-300000-units)  ·  `page_001047`

## Facts

- The move underscores Rivian’s long-term growth ambitions as it prepares to launch higher-volume vehicles and expand domestic manufacturing.
- Rivian is expanding the planned capacity of its Georgia factory to 300,000 vehicles annually, a 50% increase aimed at lowering costs and scaling U.S. EV production.
- Rivian confirmed that its future facility in Stanton Springs North, Georgia will now target 300,000 units annually in its first phase, up from the originally planned 200,000.
- CEO RJ Scaringe said the increased capacity will improve cost efficiency and support the company’s expansion as it ramps production of next-generation models like the Rivian R2.
- The company is working with the U.S. Department of Energy to update a loan package of up to $4.5 billion, designed to support construction and manufacturing.
- Rivian expects to begin drawing on the loan in early 2027, with vehicle production scheduled to start in late 2028.
- Construction is set to accelerate in 2026, with key infrastructure such as stamping operations already in preparation as the site transitions into vertical build-out.
- The Georgia plant is expected to create thousands of jobs and play a central role in Rivian’s broader strategy to scale production while maintaining cost discipline.
- Increasing initial capacity allows the company to better position itself for higher demand and future expansion phases.
- Through its partnership with Uber Technologies, the automaker plans to produce up to 50,000 robotaxis at the Georgia site starting in 2028.
- The expanded plant capacity reflects a shift toward higher-volume manufacturing and long-term competitiveness in the global EV market.
