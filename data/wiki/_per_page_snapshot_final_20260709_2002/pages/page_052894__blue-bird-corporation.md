# Blue Bird Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Blue Bird Corporation — Grokipedia](https://grokipedia.com/page/Blue_Bird_Corporation)  ·  `page_052894`

## Facts

- Blue Bird Corporation conducts its primary bus assembly at the Fort Valley, Georgia facility, operational as the company headquarters since 1946.
- The Fort Valley plant produces Type C and Type D school buses encompassing diesel, propane, natural gas, and electric variants.
- In May 2023, Blue Bird inaugurated an Electric Vehicle Build-up Center within the Fort Valley complex.
- The EV Build-up Center facilitates a six-fold expansion in electric bus production capacity to a targeted 5,000 units per year on a long-term basis.
- By August 2024, the company had delivered its 2,000th electric school bus from this facility.
- The Fort Valley operations employ over 1,000 personnel.
- Blue Bird's total workforce exceeds 1,500 across Georgia-based sites.
- Corporate administrative functions are managed from Macon, Georgia, approximately 25 miles northeast of the manufacturing site.
- In October 2025, partner IMMI opened an ancillary manufacturing site for school bus seating in Macon, spanning 75,000 square feet and focusing on compartmentalized seats with three-point belts.
- Blue Bird's joint venture Micro Bird established a new U.S. assembly site in 2025 to double output and add over 350 jobs, tied to New York State development announcements from late 2024.
- A planned 600,000 square foot facility aims to accommodate projected growth in alternative-fuel demand without specified timelines or locations as of mid-2025.
- Blue Bird utilizes a make-to-order production approach at its Fort Valley facility, featuring dedicated lines for chassis fabrication and body construction.
- In 2019, the company commissioned a 60,000-square-foot advanced paint facility equipped with robotic systems to automate and standardize painting.
- The EV Build-up Center handles final integration for electric models including the Vision and All American series, incorporating power systems like the Accelera by Cummins PowerDrive 7000.
- Subsidiary Blue Bird South in Georgia focuses on prefabricating bus materials, including sheet metal components, to support main-line assembly.
- The company implemented a multi-source strategy for procuring critical components during fiscal 2022 to counter disruptions from global events and semiconductor shortages.
- In March 2022, Blue Bird appointed an executive vice president to lead global supply chain efforts and develop a stable supplier network.
- Logistics optimization includes a partnership with Uber Freight that yielded over $3 million in savings by fiscal 2023 through consolidated shipping and carrier management.
- Blue Bird launched the Vision Electric school bus in the early 2020s with up to 130 miles of range per charge and 6-8 hour overnight recharging capability.
- In July 2023, Blue Bird collaborated with Accelera by Cummins to introduce a next-generation electric platform prioritizing enhanced performance for zero-emission student transport.
- By March 2025, the firm debuted a standardized electric battery pack featuring the Accelera BP97E to optimize fleet integration and scalability.
- The Vision Electric Type C battery-electric school bus entered limited production in 2018 with full-scale manufacturing commencing in 2019.
- The Vision Electric utilizes a 196 kWh lithium-ion battery pack, delivering up to 140 miles of range on a single charge under typical school routes.
- The electric bus supports regenerative braking and thermal management for battery longevity.
- Powertrains for the electric models are sourced from Cummins (now Accelera).
- The Vision Electric accommodates 72 passengers and features quiet operation aiding driver focus and pedestrian safety alerts for low-speed maneuvers.
- The All American Electric provides a Type D transit-style alternative with similar zero-emission capabilities, integrated into Blue Bird's electrification strategy since the 2020s.
- In July 2024, Blue Bird was awarded an $80 million grant to expand EV production capacity.
- A 2023 repower program allows for converting recent gasoline and propane Vision buses to electric.
- Approximately 20% of Blue Bird's sales involve propane-fueled buses, with over 1.3 million students daily riding propane models across more than 1,100 U.S. districts as of 2025.
- CNG-powered options were expanded in 2019 with ultra-low NOx engines certified 90% cleaner than federal diesel standards.
- Blue Bird maintains a dominant position in the North American school bus market, commanding approximately 33% share as of early 2025.
- The company projects overall school bus deliveries to expand at a 6% compound annual growth rate through 2030 to 41,700 units annually.
- In December 2023, Blue Bird formed the Clean Bus Solutions joint venture with Generate Capital to market electrification-as-a-service models and mitigate upfront costs for districts.
- Over 20,000 cumulative low-emission units have been sold by the company.
- Fiscal 2024 net sales reached $1.35 billion, an 18.92% increase from $1.13 billion the prior year.
- Fiscal 2025 third-quarter net sales hit a record $398 million, up 20% year-over-year, supported by 2,467 buses sold representing a 14.7% rise in units and 5.4% increase in average sales price.
- Adjusted EBITDA for the third quarter stood at $58 million, yielding a 14.7% margin.
- The company raised full-year guidance to $1.45 billion in net revenue and $205-215 million in adjusted EBITDA, alongside a $100 million share repurchase authorization.
- The company holds $173 million in cash offsetting $91.5 million in debt by quarter-end.
