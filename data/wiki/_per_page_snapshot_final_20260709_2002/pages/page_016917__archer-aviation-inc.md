# Archer Aviation Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-12-20

**Source:** [Archer Aviation opens new high-volume aircraft facility in Georgia](https://www.airport-technology.com/news/archer-aircraft-facility-georgia/)  ·  `page_016917`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation has completed the construction of its high-volume manufacturing facility, ARC, in Georgia, US.
- The ARC facility is situated adjacent to the Covington Municipal Airport and covers an area of about 400,000ft².
- The aircraft manufacturing facility is set to start production in early 2025, aiming to achieve a production rate of two aircraft per month by year’s end.
- Archer received the certificate of occupancy for ARC earlier this week, marking a significant milestone towards commencing the tooling load-in for the initial manufacturing line.
- Archer founder and CEO Adam Goldstein stated that the completion of the facility is a testament to the industry shifting from R&D into commercialization.
- With construction on ARC complete, Archer's team is focused on the start of production planned for early next year, followed by execution and scaling.
- Archer teamed up with automobile manufacturing company Stellantis to build ARC, which is focused on mass-producing Archer’s Midnight all-electric vertical takeoff and landing aircraft (eVTOL).
- The Midnight aircraft is designed to provide a sustainable, low-noise, and safe mode of transportation.
- The Midnight aircraft offers an alternative to traditional ground transport in densely populated urban areas.
- The facility is the result of an expanded tie-up between Stellantis and Archer Aviation.
- Both companies are working to finalize an agreement making Stellantis the exclusive contract manufacturer for the aircraft at the ARC facility.
- Stellantis is contributing capital, advanced manufacturing technology, and experienced personnel to scale production to 650 aircraft annually by 2030 at the new facility.
- Stellantis chief manufacturing and supply chain officer Arnaud Deboeuf stated that supporting Archer’s electric aircraft growth with deep manufacturing expertise is a great opportunity for both companies.
- Archer announced it successfully raised an additional $430m in equity capital this month from longtime strategic partners Stellantis and United Airlines, along with new investors Wellington Management and 2PointZero.
- Earlier this year, Archer received almost $400m in further funding from Stellantis to fund the labor costs for its eVTOL aircraft manufacturing program.
