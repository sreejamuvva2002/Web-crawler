## Facts

- Archer Aviation is positioning itself as a pioneer in the urban air mobility sector, which is projected to reach $1.5 trillion by 2040.
- The company's flagship Midnight eVTOL aircraft completed its first transition flight in 2024, achieving speeds over 100 mph and vertical landing capabilities.
- Archer aims to secure FAA Type Certification for the Midnight by late 2025, with three of four required licenses already secured.
- Commercial operations for the Midnight are planned to begin as early as 2026 upon certification.
- A 400,000-square-foot Georgia manufacturing facility began output in early 2025.
- The Georgia facility targets an initial production rate of 2 aircraft per month, ramping to over 100 aircraft per month by 2027.
- Production scaling leverages automotive expertise and supply chain best practices from a partnership with Stellantis.
- Stellantis could provide up to $400 million in manufacturing support for the Georgia facility.
- Archer's defense division, Archer Defense, secured a $142 million contract with the U.S. Air Force's Agility Prime program to fund hybrid VTOL military aircraft development.
- The defense market for such aircraft is projected to hit $79 billion by 2033.
- United Airlines placed a $300 million order for 250 Midnight aircraft and took a $50 million equity stake in Archer.
- Analysts estimate the Midnight's price at $5 million per unit, with economies of scale expected to drive down costs as production ramps.
- The Midnight features electric propulsion and Distributed Electric Propulsion (DEP) with twelve independent propellers for redundancy and noise reduction.
- The aircraft utilizes a dual VTOL/CTOL (conventional takeoff and landing) capability to extend range and reduce operational expenses.
- Archer's strategy includes repurposing existing airports and helipads to avoid costly vertiport construction and accelerate deployment.
- Archer holds a $1.1 billion cash balance following a $301.8 million capital raise in early 2025, which should fund operations through mid-2026.
- Net losses widened to $198.1 million in Q4 2024, highlighting the need for cost discipline during scaling.
- Current valuation stands at approximately $2 billion, with stock volatility driven by regulatory updates and partnership news.
- Competitors advancing similar eVTOL programs include Joby Aviation and Airbus.
