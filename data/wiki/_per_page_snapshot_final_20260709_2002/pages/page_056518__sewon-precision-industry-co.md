# Sewon Precision Industry Co.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-02-27

**Source:** [Hyundai supplier plans Georgia plant](https://readmagazine.com/industries/automotive/hyundai-supplier-plans-georgia-plant/)  ·  `page_056518`

## Facts

- A company that stamps metal auto parts will invest $300 million to build a 740-employee plant near Savannah.
- Sewon Precision Industry Co. is becoming the fifth Hyundai Motor Group supplier to locate in the region.
- Sewon Precision Industry Co. said last week that it would locate its plant in Rincon, a Savannah suburb in Georgia’s Effingham County.
- Sewon already has a stamping plant in LaGrange, near the Kia plant in West Point and the Hyundai plant in Montgomery, Ala.
- The South Korean company expects to have 1,600 employees between the two plants once the Rincon factory is complete.
- Hyundai said in May that it would build a $5.5 billion plant to assemble electric vehicles and batteries in Ellabell.
- That Hyundai plant could grow to 8,100 employees.
- The Hyundai plant is supposed to begin producing vehicles in 2025.
- Hyundai suppliers have now announced $1.8 billion of investments in the region.
- The state said this investment amount is more than the $1 billion that it had projected.
- With last week’s announcement, suppliers are locating plants in all four of the local counties helping to subsidize the Hyundai plant.
- The state will pay to train workers.
- The total incentive package from state and local governments wasn’t immediately clear.
- Sewon could qualify for nearly $6.5 million in state income tax credits.
- The tax credits are valued at $1,750 per job over five years.
- Workers must make at least $31,300 a year to qualify for the tax credits.
- Ken Horton is the company’s human resources manager.
- Ken Horton said Sewon will provide “competitive pay rates.”
- Ken Horton did not say how much workers would make.
- Seoyon E-HWA plans a $76 million plant near Savannah to make interior and exterior trim parts.
- Seoyon E-HWA plans to hire 730 employees for the plant.
- Hyundai Mobis announced a $926 million powertrain plant in Richmond Hill.
- The Hyundai Mobis plant will employ 1,500 people.
- Joon Georgia plans a $317 million plant near Statesboro.
- The Joon Georgia plant will employ 630 people.
- Ecoplastic plans a $205 million plant in Register.
- The Ecoplastic plant will employ 456 people.
