# SK Battery America

**Type:** Company  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[battery-cell-manufacturing|Battery Cell Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** Commerce, Georgia, Jackson County

## Overview

SK Battery America (SKBA) is the U.S. subsidiary of South Korea’s SK On and operates two large lithium-ion battery manufacturing plants in Jackson County, Georgia, together delivering about 22 GWh of capacity - enough to power roughly 300,000 electric vehicles each year. The company invested a total of $2.6 billion since 2019 to build the Commerce facilities and an additional $19 million for a new IT Regional Center in Roswell that will support battery-manufacturing execution systems across the United States. SKBA exceeded its original hiring goal of 2,600 workers two years ahead of schedule, reaching about 3,000 employees before announcing layoffs of 958 staff in early 2024 as EV demand slowed and federal incentives were reduced. Workforce development has been supported by Georgia Quick Start, Work for Warriors Georgia, Lanier Technical College and the state’s Economic Development team, while partnerships with Ford, Volkswagen, Hyundai and other OEMs drive demand for its cells. Safety investigations by OSHA have identified serious violations at the Commerce plant following a 2023 fire, resulting in fines exceeding $77,000 and highlighting ongoing regulatory challenges.

## Timeline

- **2017** — Company founded (as SK Battery America Inc.)
- **2018** — SK Battery America announced its first project in Georgia with an initial planned investment of $1.6 billion.
- **2019** — SK On established SK Battery America as its U.S. production base and began a $2.6 billion investment for two Jackson County battery plants.
- **Early 2022** — Mass production started at the two battery manufacturing facilities in Jackson County.
- **End of 2022** — Company met its hiring goal of 2,600 employees two years ahead of schedule.
- **January 30, 2023** — Governor Brian Kemp announced SK Battery America exceeded its hiring goal and is on track to reach 3,000 workers.
- **July 2022** — Partnered with the Georgia National Guard’s Work for Warriors Georgia program to focus on hiring veterans.
- **January 26, 2023** — Governor Kemp announced opening of a regional IT center in Roswell creating 200 high-tech jobs.
- **March 6, 2024** — SK Battery America filed WARN notice and laid off 958 workers (37% of workforce) at the Commerce plant.
- **October 2023** — Lithium-battery fire at the Commerce plant triggered OSHA investigations.
- **April 8, 2024** — U.S. Department of Labor released OSHA findings and proposed $77,200 in penalties.

## Key facts

- SK Battery America invested a total of $2.6 billion to build two lithium-ion battery plants in Jackson County, Georgia.
- The combined annual capacity of the plants is about 22 GWh - enough for roughly 300,000 electric vehicles per year.
- Hiring goal of 2,600 employees was exceeded two years ahead of schedule; workforce later expanded toward ~3,000 before a 2024 layoff of 958 workers.
- Georgia Quick Start and Work for Warriors Georgia helped attract and train the early workforce.
- The company opened an IT Regional Center in Roswell with a $19 million investment to support battery-manufacturing execution systems.
- OSHA cited serious safety violations after a 2023 fire, proposing more than $77,000 in penalties.
- Key automotive customers include Ford (F-150 Lightning), Volkswagen (ID.4) and Hyundai (future plant).
- $300 million in state and local incentives were awarded to support the Georgia facilities.
- SK Battery America’s operations are part of a broader Southeast battery belt that includes lithium mining, cell fabrication, vehicle assembly and recycling.
