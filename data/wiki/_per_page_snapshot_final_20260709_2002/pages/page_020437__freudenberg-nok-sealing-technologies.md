# Freudenberg-NOK Sealing Technologies

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2015-01-05

**Source:** [Freudenberg-NOK Renews Support For Collegiate Fuel Economy Competition](https://www.import-car.com/freudenberg-nok-renews-support-for-collegiate-fuel-economy-competition/)  ·  `page_020437`

## Facts

- Freudenberg-NOK Sealing Technologies announced it will support college students designing single-passenger vehicles for the 2015 SAE Supermileage Competition with custom seals and engineering resources.
- Freudenberg-NOK will support the Supermileage event for a third year as both a Gold Sponsor and a component supplier.
- The company engineered a new series of custom Energy Saving Seals (ESS) to fit the latest Briggs & Stratton engine used by students to power their Supermileage cars.
- The ESS seals are part of the company’s Low Emission Sealing Solutions (LESS) portfolio and are custom-designed to fit the crankshaft on the small four-cycle engine.
- Global automotive manufacturers are intensively focused on improving fuel economy and lowering vehicle emissions, mirroring the goals of the student vehicles.
- Luis Lorenzo, senior vice president, Technology and Innovation at Freudenberg-NOK, stated that Supermileage is a prime example of what the auto industry is pursuing and expressed excitement about helping students achieve their goals.
- In the previous year's competition, Freudenberg-NOK supplied ESS seals that reduced friction by 57 percent.
- Because a newer engine model will be used in the 2015 competition, Freudenberg-NOK will design and produce new ESS seals to fit the updated single cylinder engine.
- Lorenzo added that the company is actively pursuing trends like light weighting and fuel saving technologies, and Supermileage allows them to work on these major automotive industry challenges with up-and-coming engineers.
- In addition to providing friction-reducing seals, Freudenberg-NOK will sponsor two students and their vehicle from the top-performing team that uses the seals at the Center for Automotive Research’s Management Briefing Seminars (CAR MBS) in August.
- In the 2014 competition, the top team was Concordia University from Montreal, Quebec, Canada, which received the Endurance Award for achieving the highest average fuel economy with at least five complete runs.
- Students will have the opportunity to participate in the CAR MBS Student Program with all expenses paid.
- Alex Laroche, a Concordia University engineering student and 2014 CAR MBS participant, said Freudenberg-NOK gave them the opportunity to start a relationship with auto industry leaders and build their network, and noted that publicity from an Autoline.tv interview was appreciated by their university and sponsors.
- SAE International’s Supermileage Competition takes place June 4-5 at Eaton Corp.’s proving grounds in Marshall, Mich.
