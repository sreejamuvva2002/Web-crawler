# Denkai America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2022-07-20

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp: Copper Foil Manufacturer Denkai America to Locate New Manufacturing Facility, North American Headquarters in Richmond County | Governor Brian P. Kemp Office of the Governor](https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases/2022-07-20/gov-kemp-copper-foil-manufacturer-denkai-america-locate-new-manufacturing)  ·  `page_005552`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Denkai America, the only North American manufacturer of high-quality electrodeposited (ED) copper foil used in printed electronics, will locate its new manufacturing facility and North American Headquarters in Richmond County.
- The company’s initial investment will be $150 million to establish an ED copper foil production facility.
- The facility will focus on electric vehicle (EV) battery foil.
- The project is located on a 115-acre site.
- The goal is to invest a total of $430 million over the next five years.
- The goal includes creating 250 new jobs over the next five years.
- Governor Kemp stated Georgia is proud to be selected as the home of Denkai America, further strengthening the state's status as a leader in the emerging electric mobility and existing aerospace, defense, and technology industries.
- Governor Kemp emphasized the focus on bringing quality jobs to highly-trained Georgians across the state.
- Governor Kemp highlighted Georgia’s top-ranking pro-business environment.
- Denkai America’s materials have traditionally been used in printed circuit board (PCB) applications, primarily for the aerospace and defense industries.
- The materials are now also used in lithium-ion batteries powering electric vehicles.
- Denkai has operated for more than 60 years.
- Denkai has team members worldwide with operations in Japan and North America.
- Heday Nakajima, Ph.D., Denkai Group President and CEO, stated the investment highlights their commitment to leading ED battery foil manufacturing in North America for many years to come.
- Heday Nakajima expressed gratitude to the teams in Richmond County and the State of Georgia for making the project possible.
- Nobu Masuda, Denkai America’s President and CEO, stated they are thrilled to expand their manufacturing presence into Augusta.
- Nobu Masuda noted Augusta has the skilled and talented workforce needed for company growth.
- Construction is expected to begin in September 2022 as the first part of a three-phase investment.
- The facility will be fully operational by December 2024.
- The company will be hiring for skilled industrial technicians, production managers, and engineers.
- Steven Kendrick, Chairman of the Augusta Economic Development Authority, stated the authority is excited to welcome Denkai America and its North American Headquarters to the Augusta Corporate Park.
- Steven Kendrick noted they will bring high-skilled jobs to the growing list of companies in Augusta and the Augusta Corporate Park.
- Steven Kendrick thanked the Augusta Commission and the Augusta Utilities Department for their support and hard work.
- Barton Lowrey, Global Commerce Director of Corporate Solutions and Cyber Security at GDEcD, represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development on this competitive project.
- The project was in partnership with the Augusta Economic Development Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- Georgia Quick Start is a nationally ranked workforce training program by the Technical College System of Georgia.
- Georgia Quick Start has provided specialized training tailored to business needs for more than 40 years.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that as electric mobility adoption becomes widespread, Georgia is committed to harnessing this opportunity to ensure Georgians in the automotive industry have access to future jobs at home.
- Pat Wilson noted Denkai America plays a vital role in the EV ecosystem.
- Pat Wilson highlighted Georgia’s commitment to building lasting international relationships driving economic partnerships across the state, including recruiting the wider electric mobility value chain.
- Georgia’s prime location, extensive infrastructure, skilled workforce, and business-friendly climate have made it attractive for diverse industries focused on a sustainable future.
- Georgia is cultivating a vertically integrated supply chain to help companies increase efficiencies by reducing reliance on imported materials.
- EV-related projects have contributed more than $12.6 billion in investments to Georgia since 2020.
- EV-related projects have contributed more than 17,800 new jobs to Georgia since 2020.
- Augusta Corporate Park is a Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) Certified park.
- To earn GRAD certification, a property must undergo due diligence and complete specific requirements prior to a prospective business visit.
- Completing the GRAD certification program with GDEcD helps catalyze economic growth and industrial development for regions.
- Denkai America is a leader in manufacturing high-quality electrodeposited copper foils for PCB, industrial, and energy storage applications.
- Denkai America delivers application-specific copper foils required by technology-leading companies.
- Denkai America operates with the strength of domestic manufacturing backed by a global presence.
