# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-04-24

**Source:** [Inside Hyundai’s $12.6 Billion Georgia Metaplant](https://www.autoweek.com/news/a71122214/hyundai-billion-dollar-georgia-metaplant/)  ·  `page_000988`

## Facts

- The Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) is located in Ellabell, Georgia.
- The plant produced its first car in 2024.
- The facility consists of 11 buildings with 7.5 million square feet of space under roof.
- Current output consists of two electric vehicles: the Ioniq 5 and Ioniq 9.
- The plant is set to eventually have 10 models on site, including hybrids and entries from Kia and Genesis.
- A 2.5-million-square-foot LG joint venture battery plant is now under construction at the site.
- The complex includes a training center and an ecological park.
- Hyundai has made a $12.6 billion investment in Georgia.
- In 2025, Hyundai announced it would expand the plant’s output from 300,000 annually to 500,000 vehicles.
- The made-in-Korea Ioniq 6 sedan will not be produced at the Georgia plant.
- Only the Ioniq 6N performance iteration will be available in the US market.
- The Metaplant complements Hyundai Motor Manufacturing in Montgomery, Alabama.
- The Alabama facility produces about 360,000 vehicles annually.
- Hyundai is also building a steel mill in Louisiana.
- The heavily robotized plant currently has 1,623 people directly working for HMGMA.
- It will have 2,600 employees when fully staffed.
- The total workforce at the 3,000-acre complex, including suppliers, will reach 8,500.
- Salaries average $56,000 annually.
- Average salaries are 25 percent higher than is common in the surrounding area.
- The highest number of workers (32 percent) come from Chatham County, which includes Savannah.
- At the time of the visit on April 23, 150 to 175 workers were at the on-site training center learning core skills.
- The Hyundai Mobility Training Center is operated by Georgia Quick Start, the state’s workforce development program.
- Guide Susan Williams of Quick Start noted that workers must repeat tasks like installing doors if they do not understand them.
- One training task had workers standing on a moving conveyer traveling at line speed to identify key car parts.
- Other training covered how to use a torque wrench, fasten grommets, and install fasteners at awkward angles.
- Judging by first quarter 2026 sales figures, Hyundai produces approximately five times more Ioniq 5s (9,790 units) at the plant than Ioniq 9 SUVs (1,990 units).
- The plant's size necessitates travel via golf cart.
- A fleet of Hyundai Xcient hydrogen fuel-cell big rigs brings in parts from suppliers on service roads.
- Rows of Ioniq 5s headed for distant locations are lined up at rail sidings.
- Vehicles destined for less than 500 miles are shipped by truck.
- Ioniq bodies in white start at stamping and move through welding, the paint shop, then general assembly on motorized autonomous carts with pre-programmed routes.
- The plant eliminates traditional assembly lines in favor of automated guided vehicles.
- Everything is described as sparkling clean with a noise level far from traditional auto plants.
- Workers at HMGMA are called Meta Pros.
- Line people work alongside huge robots that lift car bodies.
- Boston Dynamics robotic quality control “dogs” named Spot roam around and check welds.
- Robots handle back-breaking drudge work to keep head count down and do not add to the $497 million annual payroll.
- HMGMA has its own Georgia Power substation.
- The plant has 1,878 solar parking spots that contribute five percent of the plant’s energy needs.
- Miles Johnson, Hyundai senior manager of public relations, stated the company signed a 15-year power purchase agreement with Matrix Renewables in 2024 to supplement the plant with renewable energy credits.
- Sister company Kia recently completed installation of a 10-megawatt solar array at its West Point plant that doubles as hail protection.
- Completed cars go through rigorous obstacle courses on a quality control test track to search for buzz, squeaks, and rattles.
- The auto hold feature gets checked, as does the vehicle’s willingness to travel a straight line.
- Tester Vanessa Walker tests maybe 35 to 38 cars daily on Impact and Hill Test Roads.
- Ninety five percent of tested cars are fine, but the other five percent may have issues like loose clips in the tailgate, dropped bolts under carpet, or wiper blades needing replacement.
- A known issue with the Ioniq 5 has been failure of the critical Integrated Charging Control Unit (ICCU).
- Hyundai has resolved backorder issues with the ICCU part, which is replaced under warranty.
- Due to the ICCU issue, Hyundai issued a warranty extension covering the part for 15 years or 180,000 miles.
- Hyundai spokesman Michael Stewart said the company’s Q1 2026 sales in the US set a record.
- This complements five years of retail sales increases.
- Electrified sales were 33.8 percent of the Q1 volume.
- Hyundai is celebrating 40 years in the US, beginning in 1986 with the $4,995 Excel.
- Dan Hwang, senior manager for advanced vehicle technical planning, said more than half of Ioniq 9 owners use their third row at least once a month.
- A subset of Ioniq 9 owners uses the third row a few times per week.
- Hyundai has a robotaxi partnership with Waymo, with IONIQ 5-based vehicles now under testing in San Francisco.
- The Waymo car is 90 to 95 percent made by Hyundai.
- Its Motional-partnered robotaxi, equipped with more than 30 sensors, is a 100 percent company product.
- The Motional robotaxi is in a program with Uber that has geofenced vehicles running on the Vegas strip and downtown.
- The Ioniq 5 off-road GTX model was demonstrated in an autocross, showing EV capability beyond paved trails.
