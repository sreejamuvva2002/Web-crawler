## Facts

- WIKA USA teams from around the country convened in north Georgia for the 2024 national sales meeting.
- Bill Edinger serves as vice-president of sales and marketing at WIKA USA.
- Jeff Christian serves as senior sales director at WIKA USA.
- About 80 WIKA sales and marketing employees gathered at Lanier Islands’ Legacy Lodge in Buford, Georgia, over four days in late January and early February.
- The meeting agenda focused on getting back to the basics of supporting customers with exceptional service and products.
- WIKA USA subsidiaries and sales reps had not convened together since 2022 because 2023’s gathering was devoted to training on a new management platform.
- Todd Gardner serves as president of WIKA USA and executive vice-president of WIKA Group – Americas.
- Todd Gardner discussed WIKA USA’s performance, vision, customer care, support, and plans for the next level of innovation.
- Innovation initiatives include digital sales with a new product configurator and a focus on the industrial internet of things (IIoT).
- Christian Payne serves as head of global product management – Gauges at WIKA Group.
- Thomas Hasenoehrl serves as vice president of IIoT systems and solutions at WIKA Group.
- Bill Edinger and Drew Firestone, vice-president of strategy and marketing, led sessions on go-to-market strategies for OEM, sales partners, and distribution.
- The hydrogen economy and off-highway vehicles received extra attention as growth markets in the coming years.
- Representatives from the WIKA USA branch in Houston, which produces diaphragm seals and electronic temperature measurement devices, attended the meeting.
- Representatives from WIKA Sensor Technology, which focuses on force measurement in greater Columbus, Ohio, attended the meeting.
- Representatives from WIKA Mobile Control, which manufactures safety devices of lifting machines in Chambersburg, Penn., attended the meeting.
- Representatives from WIKA IIoT Solutions, which provides wireless sensing solutions in Boulder, Colo., attended the meeting.
- Company leadership initiated a charity initiative to enhance team cohesion while supporting Rainbow House, an organization dedicated to safeguarding children in central Georgia.
- WIKA USA contributed $2,500 toward Rainbow House through a creative fundraising endeavor and a notable donation from the executive team.
- Jason Dean serves as sales development specialist at WIKA Mobile Control.
