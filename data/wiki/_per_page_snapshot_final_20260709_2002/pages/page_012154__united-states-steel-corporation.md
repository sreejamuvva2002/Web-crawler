# United States Steel Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2023-08-01

**Source:** [U.S. Steel Expects Dip in 2024 Capex](https://www.industrialinfo.com/news/article/us-steel-expects-dip-in-2024-capex--322493)  ·  `page_012154`

## Facts

- Executives with United States Steel Corporation expect to maintain capital expenditures of $2.5 billion for 2023 but project a lower spend for 2024.
- Chief Financial Officer Jessica Graziano discussed the company's spending plans on the second-quarter earnings conference call held on July 27, 2023.
- The company forecasts about $800 million of additional strategic capex in 2024.
- Early read on sustaining capex is probably in the neighborhood of $600 million to $700 million.
- Total projected capex for 2024 is somewhere in the neighborhood of $1.4 billion to $1.5 billion.
- Capex from existing strategic projects will be substantially done in 2024.
- The company aims to maintain a strong balance sheet while it invests and executes its strategy, with potential for a new sizable repurchase authorization and a fresh review of its dividend policy.
- Industrial Info Resources is tracking 61 active projects from U.S. Steel worth nearly $7 billion.
- The $3 billion Big River 2 minimill in Osceola, Arkansas, remains on track for a startup later in 2024.
- CEO David Burritt noted that 87% of the spend for the Big River 2 minimill has already been committed and 59% of the project is complete.
- Once complete, the Big River 2 minimill combined with existing Big River Steel will form a 6 million-ton mega mill supplying advanced and sustainable steels in North America.
- A non-grain-oriented (NGO) electrical steel line at Big River Steel operations is on track to launch in the third quarter of 2023.
- U.S. Steel has received its first customer orders for NGO steels in both industrial and electric vehicle markets.
- The NGO steels are strategically located to support manufacturing concentration in the U.S., Canada, and Mexico.
- Thinner NGO steels will allow electric vehicle motors to go further between charges.
- U.S. Steel announced a supply agreement with General Motors Company to supply the EV automaker with a sustainable-steel product from Big River.
- Construction is underway at the Big River Steel site for a 325,000-ton-per-year galvanizing line.
- Completion of the new galvanizing line is planned for the second quarter of 2024.
