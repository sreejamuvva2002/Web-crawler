# TEKLAS

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Georgia Chron - Electric Vehicle Manufacturer TEKLAS to Open North American Headquarters in Georgia, Create 120 Jobs in Calhoun - 10089519](https://georgiachron.com/ganews/electric-vehicle-manufacturer-teklas-to-open-north-american-headquarters-in-georgia-create-120-jobs-in-calhoun-georgiachron-10089519)  ·  `page_051279`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that TEKLAS, a Turkish advanced research and development, manufacturing company and supplier of electric vehicle (EV) parts, will invest $6.5 million in opening its first North American facility and headquarters in Gordon County.
- The new facility will deliver 120 jobs to Calhoun.
- Governor Kemp cited Georgia's highly skilled workforce, top-notch logistics network, and pro-business environment as key factors for TEKLAS's decision to join the state's growing electric vehicle parts industry.
- TEKLAS was founded in Turkey in 1971.
- The company designs, develops, and manufactures fluid circulation systems for cooling and heating applications, including hoses and pipes made from rubber, plastic, and metal for the global automotive industry.
- TEKLAS utilizes highly innovative and automated manufacturing processes using advanced water molding technology.
- TEKLAS USA will include assembly lines to serve prominent original equipment manufacturers (OEMs).
- TEKLAS USA operations are scheduled to start in the spring of 2021.
- TEKLAS is headquartered in Turkey with locations in Bulgaria, Serbia, Mexico, and China, along with sales offices in Germany and France.
- Regional Director Arcan Ergur stated that the new Calhoun, Georgia plant will help the company be closer to its main customers.
- The new facility and headquarters will occupy 200,000 square feet.
- The facility will be located at 320 South Industrial Boulevard in Calhoun, Georgia.
- TEKLAS will hire for positions across a variety of skill levels, including advanced manufacturing, management, and supervision.
- Larry Roye, chairman of the Development Authority of Gordon County, applauded the efforts of state and local partners who helped encourage the company to choose Calhoun.
- Gordon County Chamber of Commerce President and CEO Kathy Johnson commended TEKLAS for its research and advancements in electric vehicle manufacturing.
- The Calhoun and Gordon County community recognizes TEKLAS's contribution to the expansion of the automotive sector of the local manufacturing base.
- GDEcD Project Manager Fernanda Kirchner represented the Global Commerce division on this competitive project.
- The project was completed in partnership with the Economic Development Authority of Gordon County and Georgia Power.
- The Center of Innovation for Logistics, a division of GDEcD, provided freight analysis to support the project and company planning.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that the State of Georgia is focused on attracting and growing the jobs of the future.
- TEKLAS will establish its North American headquarters and build its very first U.S. advanced manufacturing facility in Calhoun.
- Commissioner Wilson noted that as the automotive industry transforms toward electric vehicles and e-mobility, the state will foster an ecosystem of manufacturers and suppliers on the leading edge of this transition.
- TEKLAS is a global automotive fluid circulation system supplier for major car original equipment manufacturers (OEMs).
- The company has more than 45 years of experience serving customers with design, development, and production capabilities.
- TEKLAS provides innovative solutions for thermoplastics, rubber, and metal products to its customers.
