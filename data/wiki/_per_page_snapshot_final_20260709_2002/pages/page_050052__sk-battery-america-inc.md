# SK Battery America Inc.

**Source:** [SK lays off nearly 1,000 workers at Georgia plant amid cooling automaker EV plans - Loonie Politics](https://looniepolitics.com/sk-lays-off-nearly-1000-workers-at-georgia-plant-amid-cooling-automaker-ev-plans/)  ·  `page_050052`

## Facts

- SK Battery America Inc. laid off nearly 1,000 workers at its manufacturing plant northeast of Atlanta on Friday amid automakers’ changing electrification plans and uncertain consumer demand for EVs.
- The last working day for 958 impacted plant employees was Friday, according to a Worker Adjustment and Retraining Notification (WARN) notice filed by human resources chief Chuck Moore.
- Impacted workers will be paid through May 6.
- SK opened its $2.6 billion battery manufacturing plant in Commerce, Georgia, in January 2022.
- The Korean company previously supplied batteries for the Ford F-150 Lightning electric pickup truck.
- Ford announced plans to cancel the fully electric version of the F-150 Lightning in December.
- The layoffs occur as the U.S. electric vehicle market faces a standstill due to the Trump administration steering federal support away from electrification toward laxer automotive emissions policies and oil/gas industry support.
- SK Americas spokesperson Joe Guy Collier stated the workforce reduction was made to align operations with market conditions.
- Collier stated, “SK Battery America remains committed to Georgia and to building a robust U.S. supply chain for advanced battery manufacturing.”
- Collier added that the company is pursuing future customers in the Battery Electric Storage System arena.
- The City of Commerce and Jackson County commission chair did not immediately respond to requests for comment regarding the layoffs.
- Ford decided in December to scrap the fully-electric F-150 pickup truck in favor of an extended-range version.
- A Ford spokesperson declined to comment on supplier personnel actions related to the plant.
- SK and Ford previously invested $11.4 billion together in joint battery plants across the U.S.
- SK ended its joint venture with Ford in December.
- SK has made significant investments in Jackson County, Georgia, as automakers developed EV plans supported by the former Biden administration's domestic supply chain efforts.
- In June 2020, SK announced plans to invest $940 million to expand its battery manufacturing presence in Atlanta.
- Governor Brian Kemp’s office stated that the June 2020 expansion would create 600 jobs.
- SK and Hyundai are jointly building a $5 billion battery factory near Cartersville, northwest of Atlanta.
- Georgia has attracted other major EV manufacturing investments, including Rivian’s $5 billion factory and Hyundai’s $7.6 billion factory complex.
- U.S. EV demand has not met automakers' expectations despite still growing, with EVs accounting for about 8% of new vehicle sales in the U.S. in 2025, matching the prior year.
- Automakers are reevaluating multibillion-dollar electrification plans due to mounting financial losses and shifting consumer demand.
- Manufacturers including Ford, General Motors, Stellantis, and others have reneged on factory and investment plans, laid off workers, and pivoted some efforts toward hybrid and plug-in hybrid electric vehicles.
- Hybrids and more efficient gasoline-powered vehicles are gaining preference among mainstream buyers concerned about EV driving range and charging infrastructure availability.
- Under President Donald Trump, Congress eliminated consumer tax credits of up to $7,500 for purchases of new or used EVs.
- The Trump administration announced plans to weaken fuel economy and greenhouse gas emissions rules for automakers, effectively removing federal incentives for cleaner vehicle fleets.
