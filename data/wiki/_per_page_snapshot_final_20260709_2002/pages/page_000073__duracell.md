# Duracell

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-02-19

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp: Duracell Selects Georgia for New R&D Headquarters | Governor Brian P. Kemp Office of the Governor](https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases/2025-02-19/gov-kemp-duracell-selects-georgia-new-rd-headquarters)  ·  `page_000073`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced on February 19, 2025 that Duracell will establish its new Global Headquarters for Research and Development in Georgia.
- The project involves creating 110 jobs and investing approximately $56 million.
- Duracell's new Global Headquarters for Research and Development will be located at 101 Nerem Street NW in Atlanta.
- The headquarters site is adjacent to Georgia Tech’s Midtown Atlanta campus within Science Square, an 18-acre multi-phase development featuring more than 1.8 million square feet of lab and office space.
- Duracell currently operates a manufacturing facility in LaGrange, Georgia, that has been in operation since 1980.
- The LaGrange facility currently supports approximately 400 jobs.
- Duracell operates a logistics and distribution plant in Fairburn, Georgia, that began operations in 2020.
- The Fairburn plant supports an additional 275 jobs.
- Duracell is an American manufacturer of alkaline, lithium coin, and hearing aid batteries.
- The Duracell brand and company was started in the 1920s.
- Duracell was acquired by Berkshire Hathaway Inc. (NYSE-BRK.A, BRK.B) in 2016.
- Berkshire Hathaway Inc. is a $250 billion holding company owning subsidiaries that engage in diverse business activities.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated Georgia has set itself apart as a leader in attracting innovative companies with its research institutions, world-class logistics network, and pro-business environment.
- Mayor Andre Dickens stated Atlanta’s transportation infrastructure, diverse talent pool from top-tier universities, and a thriving tech ecosystem make the city an ideal environment for corporate innovation and growth.
- Katie Kirkpatrick, President & CEO of the Metro Atlanta Chamber, stated Duracell's decision strengthens the region’s reputation as a hub for innovation and furthers Georgia's growing battery ecosystem.
- Robb Pitts, Chairman of the Fulton County Board of Commissioners, stated Duracell’s choice solidifies Fulton’s leadership in innovation and talent attraction.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated research and development is critical to Duracell's long-term success and that locating the new Global Headquarters in Atlanta shows Georgia as a long-term partner.
- Dr. Liben Hailu, Chief Technology Officer at Duracell, stated the move to Atlanta will strengthen their position as a global leader in the industry and drive innovation in battery technology.
- Assistant Director of Statewide Projects John Soper represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this project in partnership with Invest Atlanta, Select Fulton, Metro Atlanta Chamber, Georgia Power, and the University System of Georgia.
- The 110 new innovation jobs will provide opportunities for more Atlanta residents to build promising careers.
- Duracell is locating literally next door to the world-class talent at Georgia Tech and in close proximity to other tens of thousands of new graduates in the region.
