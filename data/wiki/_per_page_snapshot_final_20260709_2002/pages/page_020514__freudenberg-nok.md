# Freudenberg-NOK

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2015-05-04

**Source:** [Freudenberg-NOK plans to expand Findlay, Ohio facility](https://www.mobilehydraulictips.com/freudenberg-nok-plans-to-expand-findlay-ohio-facility/)  ·  `page_020514`

## Facts

- Freudenberg-NOK officially announced a 43,000-foot factory expansion at its Findlay, Ohio plant.
- The expansion could result in retaining 148 jobs and creating 25 new jobs at the facility.
- Final company decisions are dependent on formal approval of all State of Ohio workforce and incentive programs.
- The company expects to invest approximately $8.6 million in construction and equipment upgrades.
- Expansion and construction will be complete by winter, 2015.
- Dr. Theodore G. Duclos, president of Freudenberg-NOK, stated the investment aims to better serve existing and new customers with quality sealing products.
- Freudenberg-NOK leaders worked with JobsOhio, state officials, and local officials on incentives to support the expansion.
- JobsOhio Managing Director Glenn Richardson noted the decision demonstrates how companies view Ohio as a great investment location.
- JobsOhio and the Regional Growth Partnership will work closely with Freudenberg-NOK as they expand their presence in Hancock County.
- Findlay-Hancock County Economic Development Director Anthony Iriti cited the positive business climate and outstanding workforce in Findlay and Hancock County as key factors.
- Findlay has become the company’s lead facility for adopting Single Cavity Net Shape Molding, a breakthrough manufacturing approach.
- The single cavity approach uses compact, custom-engineered one-cavity injection and compression molding machines rather than multi-cavity machines to produce crucial thermoplastic parts.
- Roy Schroeder, Lead Center Manager for Fluid Power Automotive in Findlay, highlighted the plant's pursuit of world-class manufacturing processes and market expansion.
- The expansion will help increase compression and injection molding product production, including seal rings, thrust washers, pistons, and assemblies.
- The plant currently manufactures 185 million seals and related components annually.
- The new addition is anticipated to increase annual production by approximately 25 percent.
