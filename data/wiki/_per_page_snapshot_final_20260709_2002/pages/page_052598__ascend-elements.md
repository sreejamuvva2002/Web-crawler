# Ascend Elements

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [North American Clean Energy - Ascend Elements Closes 2025 with Strong Business Momentum and Commercial Scale Production of Critical Minerals for Battery Production](https://www.nacleanenergy.com/energy-storage/ascend-elements-closes-2025-with-strong-business-momentum-and-commercial-scale-production-of-critical-minerals-for-battery-production)  ·  `page_052598`

## Facts

- Ascend Elements announced a series of year-end milestones that cap a transformational 2025 and position the company for accelerated growth through 2026 and beyond.
- Ascend Elements stands alone as the proven, US- and EU-based answer to delivering circularity and security from core battery material recycling to engineered-component production at scale.
- Linh Austin serves as President and CEO of Ascend Elements.
- In May, Ascend Elements secured a $340M non-dilutive grant commitment from the Republic of Poland to support development of advanced pCAM and lithium processing capacity in Europe.
- In September, Ascend Elements became America’s first commercial-scale producer of recycled lithium carbonate with greater than 99% purity.
- Production of recycled lithium carbonate is currently located in Georgia.
- Near-term expansion of production is planned for Poland.
- The company plans to deliver more than 15,000 tons of recycled Li₂CO₃ annually by 2027.
- This output directly addresses critical mineral shortages in the US and Europe.
- In November, Ascend Elements executed a 15,000 metric ton take-or-pay lithium carbonate agreement with Trafigura.
- The agreement assures multi-year revenue and reinforces market confidence in its lithium-first strategy.
- The company executed a multi-year, nearly $1B supply contract with a leading global automaker.
- This demonstrates clear OEM demand for certified, circular, domestic supply at scale.
- Ascend is the only Western firm delivering full, commercial-scale vertical integration from recycling to cathode-active pCAM and battery-grade lithium carbonate today, not in pilot.
- The company utilizes a patented Hydro-to-Cathode® Process that upgrades recycled feedstock straight into high-value components including Lithium Carbonate and pCAM.
- This process cuts cost and steps while reducing CO₂e by up to 90% over mining-based approaches.
- Commercial production of recycled lithium carbonate is already operational in the U.S.
- Upon financial close expected in Q1/Q2 2026, Ascend will scale its Kentucky and Poland facilities.
- The scaling aims to answer critical mineral supply shortages with low-carbon throughput for North America and Europe.
- The company's growth plan will power robust revenue and EBITDA expansion through the decade while supporting national security, energy transition, and climate targets.
- President and CEO Linh Austin stated that 2025 proved circularity works at commercial scale, establishing Ascend Elements as North America’s most advanced platform for integrated, domestic production of essential battery ingredients with industry-leading cost and carbon footprint reduction.
- Austin noted that company momentum underscores global partner confidence in its advanced recovery and manufacturing technologies and its ability to supply high-quality materials with transparency and reliability.
- Austin added that the company is building a new era of energy independence, industrial sustainability, and regional security for the US and EU by looping more rather than mining more.
- Austin emphasized that Ascend Elements is setting the standard for how a resilient, low-carbon, and transparent battery economy is built.
