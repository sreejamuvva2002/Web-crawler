# Woory Industrial Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-06

**Source:** [Woory to Open EV Parts Manufacturing, R&D Facility in Laurens County - Metro Atlanta CEO](https://metroatlantaceo.com/news/2023/06/woory-open-ev-parts-manufacturing-rd-facility-laurens-county/)  ·  `page_015443`

## Facts

- Governor Brian Kemp announced that Woory Industrial Co., Ltd. will establish a new manufacturing facility in Dublin, Georgia on Thursday, June 8th, 2023.
- The facility will create more than 130 new jobs and an estimated $18 million in investment in Dublin-Laurens County.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Woory Industrial is the latest job creator helping the state on the path to becoming the e-mobility capital of the nation.
- Woory Industrial Company, known as Woory, was established in 1989 and specializes in heating, ventilation, and air conditioning components and systems for the global automotive industry.
- Woory is currently concentrating on the development of new products for electric vehicles (EV) and hydrogen-fueled cars while maintaining its traditional product lines.
- Jungwoo Kim, CEO of Woory Industrial Company, stated that the automobile industry is facing a revolution for the first time in 100 years and the transition to electric vehicles is the future.
- The U.S. EV market is the key market among all automotive markets, according to Jungwoo Kim.
- Over the past 10 years, Woory has successfully grown into a specialized EV component company through continuous innovation.
- Georgia will serve as an opportunity for Woory to take another step forward as part of the vanguard of EV development, according to Jungwoo Kim.
- Woory's new facility is located at 404 Kellam Road in Dublin and will be the company’s first U.S. manufacturing location.
- The facility will produce electrical heaters, control units, and actuators for EV and internal combustion carmakers in North America.
- Operations at the new facility are expected to begin in November 2023.
- Unlike internal combustion engine vehicles, electric vehicles do not have a heating source, so heaters for EVs are required for cabin heating and maintaining battery temperature.
- Woory's pioneering development of electric heaters over the last 10 years has led the company to become a leader in this growing market segment.
- Woory will be hiring personnel from all fields necessary for an automotive parts manufacturing plant.
- Dublin Mayor Joshua Kight stated that fostering quality and sustainable growth is a goal of the City of Dublin.
- Mayor Joshua Kight noted that Woory’s commitment to place an existing manufacturing building back into service helps promote infill development while creating new career paths for the community.
- Dr. Fred Williams, Chair of the Board of Directors of the Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority, stated they are excited to welcome Woory to the Dublin-Laurens County community.
- Dublin and Laurens County has a rapidly growing automotive sector due to its geographic location on Interstate 16 and access to a skilled and trained workforce, according to Dr. Fred Williams.
- Partnerships with area education systems and training providers will ensure training programs are in place to fill Woory's talent pipeline for years to come, according to Dr. Fred Williams.
- Project Manager Jacob Lee represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project in partnership with the Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that projects like Woory's first U.S. home in Dublin, Georgia, are why they have made it a priority to be on the cutting-edge of this transformational opportunity in manufacturing to create jobs and opportunities in rural communities.
- Georgia has invested for decades to match competitive projects with prepared communities and sites, according to GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson.
- For over a century, Georgia has fostered healthy industry practices, encouraged partnership and innovation, and positioned the state as a leader in developing emerging technologies for the evolving automotive and mobility industry.
- From July 1 through December 31, 2022, the automotive industry was the second-largest source of new jobs and investment in Georgia.
- The state has pursued job creation opportunities along the entire EV supply chain, resulting in over $22.7 billion in investment and the creation of more than 28,400 jobs since 2020.
