# Bipartisan Infrastructure Law - Battery Materials Processing and Battery Manufacturing Funding Opportunity Announcement

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2022-08-24

**Source:** [DOE: CMEI eXCHANGE: Funding Opportunities](https://eere-exchange.energy.gov/Default.aspx?Search=DE-FOA-0002678&SearchType=.)  ·  `page_005955`

## Facts

- The Office of Manufacturing and Energy Supply Chains (MESC) and Office of Energy Efficiency and Renewable Energy (EERE) are issuing Funding Opportunity Announcement No. DE-FOA-0002678.
- The FOA is entitled Bipartisan Infrastructure Law - Battery Materials Processing and Battery Manufacturing Funding Opportunity Announcement.
- The FOA supports the Bipartisan Infrastructure Legislation goals of delivering more clean energy, creating new good-paying jobs, and lowering costs for American families and workers.
- The initiative aims to guide the nation towards a one hundred percent carbon pollution-free electricity sector by 2035 and a net-zero economy by 2050.
- This amendment changes the Letter of Intent requirement from mandatory to strongly encouraged.
- The amendment clarifies the content and process for a Letter of Intent.
- The amendment clarifies the requirements for Areas of Interest (AOIs) 3, 4, 5, and 10.
- The amendment corrects language in Criterion 2a, Section V.A.
- Reviewer comments were emailed to the Business Point of Contact and Technical Point of Contact on August 24, 2022.
- Replies to reviewer comments are only accepted in Exchange from August 24, 2022, at 5:00 PM ET until September 1, 2022, at 5:00 PM ET.
- The deadline for submitting replies to reviewer comments will not be extended and cannot be submitted through any other methods.
- Providing a reply to reviewer comments is not a mandatory requirement.
- Submissions must conform to content and form requirements, including maximum page lengths described in Section IV.E of the FOA.
