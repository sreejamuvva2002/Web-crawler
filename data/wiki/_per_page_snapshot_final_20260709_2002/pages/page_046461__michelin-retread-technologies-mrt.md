# MICHELIN® Retread Technologies (MRT)

## Facts

- Michelin Retread Technologies helps fleets maximize casing value, lower cost per mile, and support sustainability goals.
- The retread process is designed to deliver performance, savings, and environmental benefits.
- A Michelin retread specialist can review a fleet's operation to identify opportunities to reduce tire costs, extend casing life, improve sustainability metrics, and maximize return on tire investments.
- Retreads offer a sustainable and cost-effective alternative to new tires with significant environmental benefits and cost savings.
- Advanced technology and rigorous manufacturing processes ensure Michelin retreads meet the highest standards of safety and performance.
- Retreads extend tire life and improve fuel efficiency to maximize total cost of ownership.
- Retreading reduces tire expenses significantly, decreases waste and carbon emissions, meets high safety standards, and offers a lifespan comparable to new tires.
- The trucking industry saves billions each year by retreading.
- Michelin's innovative retread process utilizes new-tire design technology, proven tread designs, and new-tire manufacturing standards.
- MICHELIN® Retread Technologies (MRT) delivers reliable retreads designed for specific applications to maximize tire assets.
- Features include low rolling resistance and SmartWay® verified retreads for maximum fuel efficiency.
- Michelin proprietary technology yields multiple retreads per casing.
- The process is monitored and audited, using a 9-step manufacturing process that delivers uniform results.
- Asset accountability is increased with the Bib Tread mobile on-site digital app.
- A consistent, nationwide retread warranty is backed by Michelin.
- Retreading is often one component of a broader strategy to reduce tire cost per mile, alongside tire selection, casing durability, fuel efficiency, and maintenance practices.
- Step 1 (Initial Inspection) involves inspecting every casing every time to enable trained operators to detect subtle irregularities indicating internal damage such as zippers.
- Step 2 (Buffing) is computer automated and guided by a casing-specific database and sensors to provide an under tread of optimal profile, depth, and texture.
- Step 3 (Casing Integrity) uses shearography, a two-step laser photography process performed on every tire, to reveal belt separations or blisters within the belts or other not visible areas.
- Step 4 (X-Ray) is available and used when needed to allow visual inspection of the steel belts within the casing.
- Step 5 (Repair) utilizes best practice repairs where thermal curing along with mechanical pressure exceeds industry standards.
- Step 6 (Tread Building) uses MICHELIN® tire tread, combining two operations into one by applying hot cushion gum and pre-molded tread.
- Step 7 (Double-Enveloping) uniquely uses an inner envelope to apply pressure to both sides of the repair, ensuring every retread surface undergoes uniform pressure and temperature.
- Step 8 (Curing) is computer controlled for minimum temperature variations to achieve optimal uniformity and enhance full integration of all repairs.
- Step 9 (Final Inspection) provides assurance of the best retread through thorough inspection of crown, sidewall, bead, and interior.
- Michelin retreads help reduce waste by extending the life of original tires and decrease carbon emissions through improved fuel efficiency.
- Retreads are significantly less expensive than new tires, reducing overall tire expenses and cost per mile.
- Michelin retreads meet rigorous safety and performance standards, making them suitable for various vehicle types including commercial trucks.
- Michelin retreads are designed to provide nearly the same lifespan as new tires when maintained properly.
- Not all tires are suitable for retreading; tires must pass a thorough inspection to ensure they meet the criteria for retreading.
