# NIFCO KTW

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2015-06-09

**Source:** [North Georgia Nets Another German Auto Supplier - Global Atlanta](https://www.globalatlanta.com/north-georgia-nets-another-german-auto-supplier/)  ·  `page_058138`

## Facts

- Gov. Nathan Deal announced a new $27 million investment in the sector that will provide 200 jobs over the next five years.
- NIFCO KTW, a German company, will make injection-molded parts in Toccoa for customers like Mercedes-Benz USA and BMW.
- Mercedes-Benz USA and BMW have plants in Alabama and just across the border in South Carolina, respectively.
- Nico Wijnberg, project manager on the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s international investment team for Europe, stated that German automotive suppliers are looking at Georgia because it is located right in the middle of German OEMs in the Southeast.
- Promoting these assets to the automotive audience at Automotive News Europe puts Georgia on the radar for companies looking to expand to the U.S.
- KTW is based in the city of Weissenberg and has plants in 14 countries.
- The company will start with a 32-acre site in the Hayestone Brady Business Park in Stephens County.
- KTW will build an initial 130,000-square-foot facility and plans to expand in the future.
- Auto suppliers make up a big part of Georgia’s foreign-investment outreach.
- About one-fourth of the department’s German projects are auto-related.
- The proportion of auto-related projects is at 41 percent for Japanese projects and 50 percent for Korean projects.
