# Freudenberg Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2025-07-01

**Source:** [Change at the helm of the Freudenberg Group](https://www.freudenberg.com/en/company/press-media/news-detail/2024-10-08-change-at-the-helm-of-the-freudenberg-group)  ·  `page_020477`

## Facts

- Dr. Mohsen Sohi is retiring on June 30, 2025, and will be succeeded by Claus Möhlenkamp as Chairman of the Management Board and CEO of Freudenberg SE on July 1, 2025.
- Claus Möhlenkamp will simultaneously join the Management Board of Freudenberg SE and Freudenberg & Co. Kommanditgesellschaft on January 1, 2025, serving as Deputy Chairman.
- Dr. Mohsen Sohi is American and held leadership positions in U.S. companies in the aerospace, automotive, and electronics industries for a decade and a half before joining Freudenberg.
- In 2003, Sohi was named President and CEO of the Freudenberg-NOK General Partnership in Plymouth, Michigan, USA.
- The Freudenberg-NOK General Partnership is active in the sealing business and is owned by Freudenberg and its longtime Japanese partner NOK.
- Sohi strategically realigned the joint venture and expanded activities into new sectors including medical technology and the oil and gas industry.
- In 2010, Sohi was appointed to Freudenberg Group’s Management Board in Weinheim as a board member.
- Sohi was named Chief Executive of Freudenberg & Co. Kommanditgesellschaft and the newly formed Freudenberg SE in 2012.
- Over the past 10 years, the Freudenberg Group has more than doubled its revenue and profits.
- The company has increased its research and development investments each year by more than 10 percent to more than 600 million euros annually.
- Claus Möhlenkamp has worked in various roles at Freudenberg for more than 30 years.
- As CEO of Freudenberg’s largest business group, Freudenberg Sealing Technologies, Möhlenkamp played an important role in the company's development.
- Möhlenkamp joined Freudenberg Sealing and Vibration Control Technology as a key account manager for Mercedes-Benz in 1994.
- In 2001, Möhlenkamp assumed management responsibilities at the Freudenberg-NOK General Partnership in the United States.
- By 2005, he returned to Europe and joined the Management Board of Freudenberg Sealing and Vibration Control Technologies.
- In 2007, Möhlenkamp was named CEO of Freudenberg Sealing and Vibration Control Technologies.
- In 2011, Möhlenkamp took over global business responsibility for the newly formed Freudenberg Sealing Technologies following the merger of European and American sealing activities.
- Under Möhlenkamp’s leadership, Freudenberg Sealing Technologies played a major role in shaping the progress and transformation of the auto industry.
- Numerous Freudenberg innovations aim to increase the efficiency of internal combustion engines and sustainably reduce their emissions.
- For many years, Freudenberg Sealing Technologies has supported the trend to electric mobility with intelligent solutions.
- Freudenberg Sealing Technologies made the transition from a component manufacturer to a system manufacturer in the fuel cell and battery fields, which are key technologies for the future.
- Since 2022, Freudenberg has conducted its fuel cell and battery business through the newly formed business group Freudenberg e-Power Systems.
- Möhlenkamp is a member of the Freudenberg Executive Council, which takes Business Group views into account at the Group level.
- Mathias Thielen serves as Chairman of the Board of Partners for Freudenberg.
- The company's sustainability strategy outlines a step-by-step path to climate neutrality by 2045.
- Freudenberg & Co. KG is headquartered at Hoehnerweg 2-4, 69469 Weinheim, Germany.
