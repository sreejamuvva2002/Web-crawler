# Qcells

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-05-11

**Source:** [Qcells partners Jabil in US for domestic content residential batteries - Energy-Storage.News](https://www.energy-storage.news/qcells-partners-jabil-in-us-for-domestic-content-residential-battery-storage-manufacturing/)  ·  `page_001569`

## Facts

- Qcells has introduced its first domestically assembled residential battery energy storage system (BESS) to the US market.
- The announcement for the BESS was made on 7 May.
- The system is assembled at Jabil’s Auburn Hills facility in Michigan.
- The BESS is a domestic content eligible version of Qcells’ third-generation residential ESS, known as ‘Q.HOME CORE G3’.
- The system uses lithium iron phosphate (LFP) cells and has a storage capacity ranging from 13.1kWh-26.2kWh.
- Qcells was originally a German solar manufacturer.
- Qcells was purchased out of bankruptcy in 2012 by South Korea’s Hanwha Group after Chinese PV cells flooded the market, effectively ending the European industry.
- In addition to home energy and energy management solutions, Qcells develops, invests in, and delivers PV and energy storage projects through its engineering, procurement, and construction (EPC) and development businesses in the US.
- In February, LG ES Vertech announced a multi-year partnership with Qcells for utility-scale projects.
- Under the partnership, LG ES Vertech will provide 5GWh of US-manufactured energy storage products and lifecycle services to Qcells to support its EPC projects from 2028-2030.
- Qcells stated that while some sites may feature integrated Qcells solar PV equipment alongside LG ES Vertech battery storage, they won’t necessarily always integrate both on the same projects.
- The announcement builds on an existing collaboration where Jabil assembles Qcells microinverters in Michigan for Qcells’ AC solar PV module products.
- To date, Qcells and Hanwha claim to have invested a total of US$17 million in residential solar-plus-storage manufacturing in Michigan.
- The collaboration reflects Qcells' commitment to onshoring its renewables supply chain.
- In Georgia, Qcells has invested nearly US$3 billion in building a US solar supply chain.
- Qcells is closing in on completing the construction of a factory in Cartersville, Georgia that will build critical solar components – ingots, wafers, cells and modules – under one roof.
- Q.HOME CORE G3 pairs with Qcells’ AC module products (Q.PEAK DUO AC and Q.TRON AC), which are both assembled in Georgia, as well as integrated microinverters manufactured in Michigan.
- The Biden administration moved to strengthen domestic supply chains through investment incentives and tariffs on Chinese batteries.
- The Trump administration altered production tax credit (PTC) and investment tax credit (ITC) eligibility rules last year.
- HR 1, the ‘One, Big, Beautiful Bill Act’ (OBBBA), imposed limits on material assistance from foreign entities of concern (FEOC), namely Chinese or Chinese-owned firms, for companies seeking PTC and ITC benefits.
- This policy shift has created an opportunity for South Korean battery makers like LG ES, Samsung SDI, and SK On, which had already committed to US EV battery production facilities.
- In March 2025, Nofar USA and Qcells signed an agreement to develop and construct two BESS projects in Texas totalling 350MW/700MWh.
