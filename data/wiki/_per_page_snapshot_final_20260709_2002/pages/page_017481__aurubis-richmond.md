# Aurubis Richmond

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-24

**Source:** [Press release: Aurubis Richmond - First US multimetal recycling plant starts ... | Presseportal](https://www.presseportal.de/en/pm/109932/6124769)  ·  `page_017481`

## Facts

- Aurubis AG is a leading global provider of non-ferrous metals and one of the largest copper recyclers worldwide.
- The new US site Aurubis Richmond ramped up operations today, September 24, 2025.
- The facility produces key strategic metals including copper, nickel, tin, and precious metals in Georgia.
- These recovered metals are essential for American energy infrastructure, data centers, AI applications, high-tech products, and the defense industry.
- American industry currently requires approximately 1.8 million t of copper per year.
- Experts estimate that copper demand will increase nearly 30% over the next five years.
- The US currently imports about half of the copper it processes.
- Aurubis Richmond operates as the first and most technologically advanced secondary smelter for multimetal recycling in the United States.
- The “First Melt” celebration attendees included US Representative Rick W. Allen for Georgia’s 12th District, German Ambassador to the United States Jens Hanefeld, and high-profile guests from politics and business.
- Dr. Toralf Haag serves as CEO of Aurubis AG.
- Dr. Toralf Haag stated that Aurubis Richmond makes the company the frontrunner in multimetal recycling and processing in the US, a position it already holds in Europe.
- Dr. Toralf Haag noted that high availability of recycling material and good local conditions offer appealing prospects for continued expansion in the American market.
- Georgia Governor Brian P. Kemp stated that Aurubis Richmond is now a significant employer for Georgia and plays a major role in the American multimetal supply chain, standing as a testament to American manufacturing, independence, and resiliency.
- The project involves an investment of about $800 million.
- Aurubis is creating more than 240 jobs in Georgia through this facility.
- The plant will process up to 180,000 t of complex recycling material annually.
- An expansion stage for the facility is scheduled to begin in 2026.
- Input materials include printed circuit boards, copper cable, and other metal-bearing products.
- The plant recovers critical metals on-site, effectively closing the value chain locally.
- The facility will be supplied with recycling materials from local sources to keep valuable raw materials within the domestic market.
- US Representative Rick W. Allen emphasized that Aurubis Richmond is a strategic site in Georgia’s 12th District that reinforces America’s ability to supply itself with metals for national security, infrastructure, and technology leadership.
- David Schultheis serves as President and Managing Director of Aurubis Richmond.
- David Schultheis expressed pride in the team's achievement and thanked Georgia policymakers and governmental authorities for strong support, smooth approval processes, and enabling quick settlement.
- The plant utilizes cutting-edge technology that fulfills the latest environmental standards enacted by Georgia state and federal authorities to ensure high consideration for water, air, and soil.
- The facility is designed as a flexible “one-stop shop” that will receive a broad range of metal-bearing recycling materials from US suppliers.
