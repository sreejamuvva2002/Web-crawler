# Honda Precision Parts of Georgia (HPPG)

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2021-05-21

**Source:** [Honda transmission plant in Georgia takes first steps to electric vehicles](https://www.yahoo.com/news/honda-transmission-plant-georgia-takes-234700884.html)  ·  `page_008893`

## Facts

- Honda's transmission plant in Tallapoosa, Ga., is taking its first steps toward producing components for electric vehicles as part of Honda's electrification efforts.
- The facility is responsible for producing the two-motor hybrid system used in the Accord Hybrid, CR-V Hybrid, and Insight.
- The plant was formerly known as Honda Precision Parts of Georgia (HPPG) and originally began production with 5-speed automatic transmissions.
- The facility added 6-speed automatic transmission production in 2012.
- In 2017, the plant added a second assembly line and became the first Honda plant globally to produce the 10-speed automatic transmission (10AT).
- Earlier in 2021, the plant began replacing its original production line to increase 10AT production.
- Gerald Bruch, vice president of Honda Development and Manufacturing of America, LLC (HDMA), stated that Honda's Georgia and Ohio plants form a transmission powerhouse creating advanced technology components for Honda and Acura products.
- Honda's first dedicated U.S. transmission plant in Russells Point, Ohio, will mark its 25th anniversary in July.
- Honda has manufactured over 21 million transmissions in the U.S. since beginning automatic transmission production in Ohio in 1989.
- U.S. transmission production initially began at Honda's Anna Engine Plant in Ohio in 1989.
- In 1996, transmission manufacturing lines were relocated from the Anna Engine plant to Bellemar Parts Industries in Russells Point, Ohio.
- Bellemar was incorporated as Honda Transmission of America in 1997.
- Both Honda transmission plant locations were combined in the spring under a new company named Honda Development & Manufacturing of America, LLC (HDMA).
- HDMA includes all Honda automobile manufacturing facilities in the U.S. related to frame, engine, transmission, and related engineering and purchasing operations.
- The Georgia transmission plant began operations in May 2006.
- It is one of two Honda automobile transmission plants in the U.S. and one of 12 major Honda manufacturing facilities operating in America.
- The Tallapoosa plant employs more than 500 associates.
- It serves as Honda's lead global facility for producing the 10-speed automatic transmission for various Honda and Acura models.
- Cumulative production of the 10-speed automatic transmission at the facility is soon to surpass four million units.
- The plant uses domestic and globally sourced parts for its transmissions.
