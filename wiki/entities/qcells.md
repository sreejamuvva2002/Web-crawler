# Qcells

**Type:** Company  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[unknown|Uncategorized]]  ·  **Location:** Dalton, Georgia, Whitfield County, Georgia, United States

## Overview

Qcells is a solar panel manufacturer operating the largest silicon solar panel manufacturing facility in the United States (Dalton, GA) and building a second factory in Cartersville, GA. It has entered into multi-year battery energy storage system (BESS) supply agreements with LG Energy Solution Vertech to install American-made storage products on its development projects.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Provides grid-scale battery storage systems that support electric vehicle charging infrastructure and improve grid reliability for EVs.

- **Qcells Dalton Phase 1 Facility** — Opened January 2019; employed more than 800 workers by the end of 2019; three original production lines.
- **Qcells Dalton Expansion Facility** — Construction broke ground February 2023; completed August 2023; added four new production lines and manufacturing space equal to two football fields.
- **Qcells Cartersville Facility** — Conditional $1.45 billion loan guarantee from the U.S. Department of Energy on August 8 (2023); will add wafer-cutting, cell-making and module-assembly steps.
- **Qcells Dalton Manufacturing Facility** — Largest silicon solar panel manufacturing facility in the United States.
- **Qcells Cartersville Factory (under construction)** — Will manufacture ingots, wafers, cells and finished panels; will increase annual solar panel production capacity to 8.4 GW.
- **Qcells Manufacturing Facility** — Facility is being expanded to meet a historic 2.5 million-panel order for community solar.
- **Cartersville Facility** — First recycling operation expected to recycle ~250 MW of panels annually (~500,000 panels).

## Key facts

- The Dalton complex employs about 2,000 full-time workers and produces 5.1 GW of solar panels per year - the highest output of any U.S. site.
- Entry-level wages at Qcells range from $17.50 to $22 per hour ($36,400-$45,760 annually), higher than other local factories.
- The factory’s roof is covered with dozens of air-conditioning units that keep the climate-controlled production floor comfortable for workers.
- Workers can move from low-skill positions to higher-skill roles operating and maintaining robots on the line.
- Qcells relied on Georgia’s Quick Start program for pre-opening worker training funding.
- Project Purpose, a crash-course run by the Greater Dalton Chamber, placed 12 graduates into Qcells jobs starting July 1.
- The 2023 expansion added manufacturing space equivalent to two football fields.
- Four new production lines increase output 1.5× compared with the original three lines.
- Qcells’ presence has lifted local wages and helped revitalize downtown Dalton’s restaurants, hotels, and housing market.
- Local Class I railroads CSX and Norfolk Southern serve the area, supporting logistics for the plant.
- Qcells operates the largest silicon solar panel manufacturing facility in the United States, based in Dalton, Georgia.
- Qcells is building a second factory in Cartersville, Georgia to manufacture ingots, wafers, cells and finished panels.
- The partnership secures energy storage systems that meet domestic content requirements, with LG’s batteries made in Michigan.
- The collaboration aims to create American jobs, strengthen supply chains, and deliver reliable, affordable power for communities.
- "Qcells is proud to partner with LG Energy Solution on a project that shows what’s possible when energy development supports U.S. manufacturing," said Chris Hodrick, CEO of Qcells EPC.
- "We’re excited to continue our partnership with Qcells as we begin a new phase together," said Jaehong Park, CEO and President at LG Energy Solution Vertech.
- Largest community solar purchase in U.S. history - 2.5 million panels ordered for Georgia.
- Expanding access to solar energy through a massive community-solar project.
- Historic expansion of clean-energy manufacturing at Qcells' Whitfield County facility.
- Creation of green jobs in a traditionally deep-red part of Georgia.
- Layered state and federal incentives are supporting the clean-tech investment.
- Qcells is now manufacturing solar cells, a critical step toward producing all major components of a solar module under one roof.
- Full production at Cartersville expected by Q3 2026, delivering 3.3 GW each of ingots, wafers and cells and 3.5 GW of modules annually.
- Combined capacity of Cartersville and Dalton facilities can produce 8.6 GW of solar modules (~47,000 panels) per year.
- "Producing the first solar cells at Cartersville is a milestone for Qcells and for American manufacturing," said CEO Andy Park, highlighting job creation and supply-chain reliability.
- By the end of 2026, Qcells expects a combined workforce of nearly 4,000 people across its two Georgia sites.
- The addition of 3.3 GW of cells to the U.S. solar supply chain comes at a critical time when electricity demand is at an all-time high and projects are racing to meet tax-credit deadlines.
- SEIA notes that domestic solar module manufacturing capacity has risen from 8 GW pre-tax credits to 69.9 GW as of June 2026.
- Qcells invested over $3 billion to build a fully U.S.-based, vertically integrated solar supply chain.
- The Cartersville facility will host Qcells' first recycling operation, capable of processing ~250 MW of panels (~500,000 panels) per year.
- "With storage, EPC, and solar recycling also in our portfolio, we are far more than a panel company," said Scott Moskowitz, announcing the new "Qcells New Homes" division for turnkey solar-plus-battery solutions.
