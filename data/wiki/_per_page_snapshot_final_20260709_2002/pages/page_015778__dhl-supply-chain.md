# DHL Supply Chain

## Facts

- DHL Supply Chain and Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation (YMMC) are progressing with studies, permitting, and filings that could lead to the construction of a new distribution facility on YMMC’s Newnan, Georgia campus.
- The proposed facility is 784,700 square feet.
- The project represents an estimated $60–90 million investment.
- The facility would be solely dedicated to YMMC operations.
- If approved, the new facility is expected to improve efficiency while also benefitting the community.
- Currently, freight traffic flows between YMMC’s main plant and off-site distribution centers, contributing to congestion along Highway 34 and surrounding intersections.
- Consolidating logistics operations on the YMMC campus could reduce truck traffic on local roads, ease congestion, improve safety, and come at no added cost to taxpayers.
- Bob Brown serves as President of YMMC.
- Bob Brown stated that the project is about strengthening the supply chain and being a good neighbor while carefully navigating studies and permitting.
- A key potential benefit is a dedicated, world-class logistics hub to support Yamaha’s manufacturing operations.
- Another benefit is reduced truck traffic on local roads by consolidating operations on-campus.
- The site design will be thoughtful to maintain the character of the surrounding area.
- The project would invest in roadway improvements such as new turn lanes and upgraded intersections to improve traffic flow and safety.
- Sustainable features will include stormwater management systems, tree buffers, and pervious surfaces to reduce environmental impact.
- Pending successful completion of studies and required approvals, construction could begin as early as 2026.
- Operations could potentially start in the first quarter of 2028.
- Dave Rumler serves as President of Automotive Engineering and Manufacturing at DHL Supply Chain North America.
- Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Company (YMMC) employs more than 2,000 metro Atlanta residents to design and build recreational vehicles at its Newnan manufacturing facilities.
- All of the world’s Yamaha golf cars, Side-by-Sides, as well as most of its WaveRunners and ATVs are manufactured at the plant in Coweta County, Georgia.
