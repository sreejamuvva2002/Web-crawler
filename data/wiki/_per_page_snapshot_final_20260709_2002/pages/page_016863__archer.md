# Archer

## Facts

- Archer has facilities in Georgia and California where several Midnight aircraft are in various stages of completion.
- Archer plans to expand its Midnight fleet and flight envelope throughout 2026 across its Georgia and California facilities.
- The Georgia and California facilities will support Archer's planned Type Inspection Authorization (TIA) activities with the FAA as soon as this year.
- Archer has submitted applications for the White House's eVTOL Integration Pilot Program (eIPP) across California, Florida, Texas, Georgia, and New York.
- Archer plans to deploy additional piloted Midnight aircraft in American cities through the eIPP later this year.
- President Trump signed an Executive Order titled 'Unleashing Drone Dominance' that established a White House mandate to accelerate air taxi deployments in American cities.
- Archer is working directly with the DOT and FAA to help ensure the eIPP positions the U.S. to lead in the new air taxi category.
- Archer's order book is in the billions, with seven of the world's largest airlines choosing to partner with Archer.
- In Q4, Saudi Arabia's PIF partnered with Archer to deploy aircraft, starting with Red Sea Global.
- Korean Air selected Archer as its exclusive partner.
- A consortium with Japan Airlines was selected to support commercialization in both Osaka and Tokyo.
- The FAA confirmed final acceptance of 100% of the 797 Means of Compliance for the Midnight aircraft, making Archer the first eVTOL manufacturer to achieve this milestone.
- Completing the Means of Compliance unlocks Archer's ability to finalize remaining certification plans with the FAA, which are expected to be resolved in the coming quarters.
- Type Inspection Authorization (TIA) work on the Midnight program is expected to begin as soon as this year.
- Archer designed the Midnight aircraft to be as close to a Part 23 airplane as possible and vertically integrated only the powertrain and flight control software due to a lack of existing supply base, partnering with Tier 1 aerospace suppliers for the rest.
- The Midnight aircraft requires only 17 issue papers for certification, with 13 being industry-standard and 4 unique to its design and battery pack.
- Archer chose a four-blade aft propeller design for Midnight to reduce complexity, simplify certification, and maintain manufacturability with minimal performance impact.
- The Midnight aircraft is designed to certify both vertical takeoff and landing (VTOL) and conventional takeoff and landing (CTOL), providing safety and operational flexibility advantages.
- Archer's defense strategy centers on a partnership with Anduril to design an autonomous, hybrid-electric VTOL aircraft for dual use as a loyal wingman for defense and cargo/medevac for commercial customers.
- Archer opened a new engineering hub in Bristol, UK, and has hired a team of 20+ local engineers.
- In November, Archer announced its first third-party powertrain deal with Anduril and EDGE Group to power their Omen autonomous air vehicle.
- President Trump's One Big Beautiful Bill allocated a historic $12.5 billion to modernize the U.S. air traffic control system.
- Archer is working to build a full-stack autonomy solution for aviation and plans to unveil its first software product in this category later this year.
- Archer acquired Hawthorne Airport to use as a testbed for autonomy technologies.
- Archer partnered with Palantir to build the AI foundation for next-generation air traffic control, movement control, and route planning.
- Archer is working with NVIDIA to integrate their IGX Thor platform into Midnight for real-time onboard computing for safety-critical autonomy applications.
- Archer is working with SpaceX's Starlink to bring high-speed, low-latency connectivity to its aircraft.
- Archer ended Q4 with approximately $2 billion in liquidity.
- Adam Goldstein serves as the Founder & Chief Executive Officer of Archer.
