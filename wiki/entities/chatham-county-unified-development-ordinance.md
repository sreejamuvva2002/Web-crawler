# Chatham County Unified Development Ordinance

**Type:** Policy / Incentive  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[government-support|Government Support]]  ·  **Location:** Chatham County, Georgia, United States

## Overview

A comprehensive set of regulations adopted by Chatham County to guide growth, development, sustainability, and land use while balancing economic development with environmental protection.

## EV supply-chain relevance

The ordinance includes a forward-looking requirement that new developments provide electric vehicle (EV) charging stations, aiming to prepare the county for increased EV adoption.

## Key facts

- Businesses expanding more than 10% must bring the entire building up to current code, a requirement that could pose significant financial challenges for small and medium-sized businesses.
- Large commercial buildings of 100,000 square feet or more are mandated to install solar panels under the UDO.
- New developments are required to invest in landscaping that includes 10-foot trees and irrigation systems, adding potentially hundreds of thousands of dollars to project costs.
- Parking lots must be surrounded by hedges and other vegetation, which raises concerns about security due to possible hiding spots for criminals.
- The ordinance requires electric vehicle charging stations in new developments, a provision Andrews argues may be premature given current EV demand.
- Only 10% of riparian buffers can be counted as open space, limiting the amount of land developers can use and potentially driving up housing costs.
- Andrews warns that the UDO could stifle growth, increase expenses for businesses, and make housing less affordable for residents.
- The UDO aims to preserve Chatham County's rural character while promoting sustainability through measures such as renewable energy incentives and open-space protection.
