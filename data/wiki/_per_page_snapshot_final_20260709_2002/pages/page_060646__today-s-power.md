# Today's Power

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** charging_infrastructure  ·  **Published:** 2022-03-10

**Source:** [Economic Development 2022: Jennah Denney, Today’s Power](https://armoneyandpolitics.com/economic-development-2022-jennah-denney-todays-power/)  ·  `page_060646`

## Facts

- Over the past six years, solar, energy storage, and electric vehicles have grown at an exponential rate.
- Renewable energy technologies provide economic advantages by creating jobs and using primarily indigenous resources, allowing most energy dollars to stay in the country.
- Arkansas' economic potential is increasing because of transportation electrification.
- Electric vehicles are laying the groundwork for a strong Arkansas EV economy by lowering the cost of ownership and operation, and benefit from some of the lowest electric rates in the country.
- Electric vehicle charging serves as a cheaper alternative to fueling up at the pump.
- Today's Power works with cities and utilities that strive to lead by example on behalf of their communities, demonstrating that promoting low-carbon projects such as solar and EV charging fosters clean energy, efficiency, good employment, investment, and environmental friendliness.
- The recently passed infrastructure act will provide more Americans, particularly those in rural and underserved areas, with opportunities to benefit from the renewable energy economy.
- Arkansas is set to receive approximately $54 million to build out a statewide network of EV chargers to support EV adoption across the state.
- With ample charging infrastructure and the availability of larger EV models such as electric trucks and SUVs, utilization rates of EVs and other renewable energy technologies are expected to increase over the next few years.
- The top two technological advances expected over the next five years are improved large-scale EVs and more cost-effective battery energy storage.
- Personal EVs are grabbing market share, and commercial fleets and municipalities could rapidly follow suit.
- The total cost of ownership (TCO) for electric delivery trucks could soon be on par with diesel-run trucks due to increasingly cost-competitive and available EV infrastructure.
- E-buses have higher acquisition prices due to upfront battery costs but offer a lower TCO due to independence from pricey diesel.
- E-buses eliminate local particulates, including SOx, NOx, and CO2, which are major issues in most cities today.
- The new era of EVs has rapidly expanded the market for lithium and cobalt batteries while significantly lowering their cost.
- Expanded battery markets are increasingly being used as energy-storage solutions by industry and utilities beyond just EVs.
- Falling battery prices are proving useful in lowering power costs, increasing reliability and resiliency, and making power systems more flexible to operate.
