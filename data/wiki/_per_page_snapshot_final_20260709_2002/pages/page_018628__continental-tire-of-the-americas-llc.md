# Continental Tire of the Americas LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-02-03

**Source:** [German Tire Giant Set to Close Georgia Textile Plant, Shedding 235 Jobs  - Global Atlanta](https://www.globalatlanta.com/german-tire-giant-set-to-close-georgia-textile-plant-shedding-235-jobs/)  ·  `page_018628`

## Facts

- Continental Tire of the Americas LLC is closing a plant in Barnesville, Ga., by the end of 2026.
- The closure will cost Lamar County 235 jobs.
- The Barnesville plant has been part of Continental's portfolio since 1988.
- The facility is situated alongside a railroad track on Aldora Street in Barnesville.
- The plant produces tire cord fabric, hose yarn, and knitted fabric used in tires and ContiTech unit products for non-automotive industries.
- In a November release, Continental attributed the closure to “ongoing changes in global market dynamics and supply chains.”
- Continental stated the Barnesville plant “has not been cost competitive for many years despite continuous turnaround efforts.”
- Spokesperson Allison Zora declined to comment on whether recent trade upheaval directly influenced the closure decision.
- Zora stated the decision resulted from a comprehensive review of operations and supply chain aimed at strengthening Continental’s long-term competitiveness, efficiency, quality, and sustainability.
- Continental emphasized the Barnesville closure will not reduce its commitment to the U.S. market.
- The company has made $1.5 billion in investments in U.S. tire manufacturing facilities located in South Carolina, Illinois, Mississippi, and Indiana.
- These U.S. facilities produce 16 million tires per year.
- Continental employs a total of 8,800 people across its operations.
- The company recently completed an $85 million investment to upgrade a ContiTech factory in Iowa.
- A new tire distribution center is scheduled to open in Dallas.
- ContiTech continues to maintain some operations in Georgia, but Continental Tire operates no tire facilities within the state.
- Layoffs at the Barnesville plant will be phased in starting in March, with final separation dates occurring on the last day of 2026.
- The company filed a WARN notice regarding the closures with the Georgia Department of Labor in January.
- Affected employees will receive career counseling and access to employment resources.
- On-site resume writing and informational workshops are scheduled for February 10-12.
- A job fair for displaced workers will be held on March 25.
