# SK Battery America Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-11-07

**Source:** [SK Battery America cited for safety, health hazard violations at Georgia plant | WardsAuto](https://www.automotivedive.com/news/sk-battery-america-safety-health-hazard-violations-osha-commerce-georgia/705616/)  ·  `page_050072`

## Facts

- The U.S. Department of Labor cited SK Battery America for seven health and safety violations at its Commerce, Georgia, facility.
- The Department of Labor proposed $75,449 in penalties against the battery maker.
- SK Battery America employs about 3,100 people at its two battery manufacturing plants in Commerce.
- SK Battery America is a subsidiary of South Korea-based electric vehicle battery manufacturer SK On.
- OSHA inspectors investigated the Commerce facility between June 26 and Nov. 7, 2023.
- Inspectors found that the Commerce facility exposed workers to respiratory hazards when working with cobalt, nickel and manganese.
- Cobalt, nickel and manganese are key components in the company’s electric vehicle batteries.
- The company failed to complete a workplace hazard assessment for using the chemicals.
- The company did not provide employees with proper safety equipment, such as eyewash stations and emergency showers.
- The company did not ensure that respirators used when working with the materials were properly cleaned and stored.
- OSHA Area Office Director Joshua Turner stated that the lithium battery industry has experienced unprecedented growth, bringing heightened responsibility to ensure worker safety.
- Turner noted that materials used to produce lithium batteries can cause debilitating and permanent health issues.
- Inspectors found that the Commerce facility exposed workers to unsafe noise levels.
- The agency cited the plant for failure to develop a hearing monitoring program as required by law.
- SK Battery contested OSHA’s findings before the federal agency’s review commission.
- A company spokesperson stated that safety remains the utmost priority for workers at SK Battery America.
- The spokesperson noted that SK Battery America has procedures in place that comply with workplace safety regulations and is reviewing the findings while working closely with officials to address concerns.
