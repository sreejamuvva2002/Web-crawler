# PIOLAX, INC.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [PIOLAX, INC. Business Report FY2006 - MarkLines Automotive Industry Portal](https://www.marklines.com/en/top500/cf/s500_489_hl2006)  ·  `page_048378`

## Facts

- FY2006 sales were 42,418 million JPY, a 3.2% increase from FY2005, supported by strong overseas sales.
- Operating income was 2,340 million JPY (0.1% increase), with gains offset by soaring material prices and increased depreciation costs from vigorous capital spending.
- Ordinary income was 3,114 million JPY (1.1% increase).
- Current net income was 1,981 million JPY (20.4% increase).
- Japan sales amounted to 34,176 million JPY (up 679 million JPY), with operating income down 3 million JPY to 1,920 million JPY due to material price hikes and higher fixed costs.
- North America sales increased 79 million JPY to 6,215 million JPY, driven by U.S. subsidiary marketing targeting major Japanese carmakers, the Big Three of the USA, and Tier-1 suppliers.
- North America operating income was 199 million JPY (up 68 million JPY), impacted by soaring material prices and special costs for expanding the Mexico plant.
- Other regions sales reached 4,682 million JPY (up 989 million JPY), with operating income up 102 million JPY to 177 million JPY.
- Production structure strengthening in Japan involved integrating precision spring production and augmenting fuel system components and open-close mechanism parts volume.
- Major overseas investment activities included a plant expansion in Mexico and land purchase in China prior to an expansion/relocation project starting in the first half of 2007.
- Sales to Hyundai Motor Co. (Korea) and Ford Motor Co. (U.S.A.) increased by 1.5-fold compared to the previous term.
- Overseas sales have more than doubled since the Nissan Revival Plan period, when 90% of sales were from Nissan and the domestic market.
- In FY2006, 15% of sales came from North America, while Europe and Asia sales reached two-digit percentages for the first time.
- Overseas sales amount doubled the 2000 results.
- The medium-term business plan aims to expand Asian region sales by 2.2-fold from 2005 to 4 billion JPY over three years.
- The company targets large demand increase in Korea, marking the 10th year of business there, with a target of 1 billion JPY sales for the term ending March 2007.
- In February 2007, Piolax decided to acquire all stocks of Sankyo Hatsujo Co., Ltd. (Oume City, Tokyo), making it a wholly-owned subsidiary and merging it with P.N.S Co., Ltd. (Yokohama City, Kanagawa Pref.) on April 1, 2007, to consolidate precision spring production.
- R&D expenses were 421 million JPY in FY2006, with 306 million JPY allocated to automotive products.
- Automotive R&D focused on precision springs (compression coil springs for engines/transmissions), composite springs, and hexavalent-chromium-free surface treatments to meet EU End-of-Life Vehicle Directive, RoHS, REACH, and Japan Automobile Manufacturers Association regulations.
- Joint development activities with Japanese carmakers were reinforced, along with technological ties to a Korean car maker and joint development with Japanese automakers in China leading to mass production.
- Industrial fasteners R&D developed products for various sheet thicknesses, easy assembly to auto bodies, high strength, easy scrapping, and odor-reducing interior materials.
- Compact units R&D included new lock mechanisms for glove boxes (mounted on vehicles launched January 2003), new dampers (developed with domestic and overseas manufacturers in Europe, U.S., Korea), hinge mechanism parts for console box lids/floor boards using oil dampers, and better performance filtering elements for transmissions.
- Fuel systems R&D developed in-tank evaporator systems for metal tanks to meet USA evaporator regulations, valves (rollover and inlet check valves) for plastic tanks, and products for alternative fuels like bio diesel and alcohol fuel.
- Licensing of technology from Norma Germany GmbH covers automotive fuel delivery components and plastic fuel tank components via approved patents, renewed every 5 years starting August 18, 1995 and December 13, 2001.
- Licensing of technology to other companies includes Sanjia Industrial Co., Ltd. (Taiwan) for metallic/plastic fasteners, Taiwan Houmu Industrial Co., Ltd. for automotive clutch/torsion/valve springs, and various Piolax subsidiaries in USA, UK, Korea, Thailand, and China for precision plastic slips/springs/assembly products under indefinite or annual renewal terms.
- FY2006 capital investments totaled 3,986 million JPY, mainly for automotive parts production facilities, with 3,006 million JPY in Japan and 980 million JPY overseas.
- New facility investments include Yokohama Technical Center (49,811 thousand JPY, April 2007-March 2008), Mooka Plant (100,750 thousand JPY, November 2007-March 2008), Fuji Plant (1,000 thousand JPY plant + 188,950 thousand JPY facilities, April 2007-March 2008), and Kyushu Plant (114,400 thousand JPY plant + 150,555 thousand JPY facilities + 53,900 thousand JPY molds, April 2007-March 2008).
- Piolax Corp. Head Office/Plant in Georgia, USA has an expected investment of 9,900 thousand JPY for the plant, with production facilities at 185,460 thousand JPY and molds at 91,850 thousand JPY, scheduled to start in January 2007 and complete in December 2007.
- Additional new facility investments include Lancashire UK (88,400 thousand JPY plant + 19,700 thousand JPY facilities), Inchon Korea (55,200 thousand JPY facilities + 83,000 thousand JPY molds), Rayong Thailand (360 thousand JPY buildings + 106,607 thousand JPY facilities + 112,226 thousand JPY molds), and Dongguan China (30,339 thousand JPY plant + 280,610 thousand JPY facilities + 193,943 thousand JPY molds), all starting January 2007 and completing December 2007.
