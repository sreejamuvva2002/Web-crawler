# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-04-24

**Source:** [A Look Inside Hyundai's Metaplant - CleanTechnica](https://cleantechnica.com/2026/04/24/a-look-inside-hyundais-metaplant/)  ·  `page_000282`

## Facts

- The Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) is the official name of the new factory.
- The facility is located a few minutes outside Savannah in Ellabell, Georgia.
- HMGMA represents the largest investment ever made in the state of Georgia.
- The investment is part of Hyundai’s broader expansion of its US manufacturing footprint.
- The company owns nearly 3,000 acres of land at the site and continues to build out additional business units as operations expand.
- The initial production scope was set at 300,000 vehicles per year.
- Just last year, Hyundai committed to expanding operations and increasing its total output capacity to 500,000 vehicles per year.
- The facility is currently operational and producing the IONIQ 5 and IONIQ 9 for the US market.
- Hyundai has created 8,500 direct jobs at the facility.
- Hyundai has created 6,950 indirect jobs through regional suppliers and supporting businesses.
- The factory plan includes co-locating core vendors at the same site to optimize logistics and increase partner agility.
- Co-located vendors include Hyundai conglomerate companies like Hyundai Steel as well as external vendors building their own factories within the Metaplant.
- Co-locating vendors creates a shorter supply chain that ties up less money and inventory, enabling faster response times to changes.
- The plant utilizes autonomous guided vehicles (AGVs) instead of traditional massive conveyors to carry vehicles through the factory floor.
- AGVs provide greater flexibility for vehicle queuing and production line changes but require individual maintenance, charging, and direction.
- Hyundai’s stamping press features extensive noise dampening and shock absorption, allowing workers to operate in the area without hearing protection.
- The body shop welds stamped panels together using a fleet of robots and checks them for quality.
- The state of Georgia was instrumental in securing the large parcel of land for the Hyundai Metaplant.
- Georgia built a state-owned and run training facility that provides initial training modules for Hyundai’s new hires.
- This state support enabled Hyundai to invest significantly in Georgia with confidence in building a sizable local workforce.
- Hyundai’s CEO stated he sees significant potential for the company to expand in the US market in the near term.
