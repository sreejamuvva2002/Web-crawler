## Facts

- On January 16, 2025, Rivian and the U.S. Department of Energy finalized a loan agreement to support a future Georgia manufacturing site.
- On April 30, 2026, Rivian announced an optimized capacity plan for its Georgia plant.
- On November 12, 2024, Rivian and Volkswagen Group announced the launch of their joint venture.
- On March 19, 2026, Uber and Rivian partnered to deploy up to 50,000 fully autonomous robotaxis.
- On June 9, 2026, Rivian began R2 public customer deliveries and opened orders for reservation holders.
- On March 12, 2026, Rivian introduced the R2 lineup with full trims and pricing.
- On December 11, 2025, Rivian unveiled custom silicon, a next-gen autonomy platform, and deep AI integration at its inaugural Autonomy & AI Day.
- Rivian founder and CEO RJ Scaringe conducted a virtual tour of the manufacturing facility in Normal, Illinois, explaining progress in producing electric vehicles at scale.
- The Normal, Illinois manufacturing facility includes Stamping, Body, Paint, Propulsion, General Assembly, and End of Line shops where skilled teams assemble the R1T, R1S, and commercial vans.
- Rivian is based in Southern California with additional offices in Northern California, Michigan, Arizona, Vancouver, the Netherlands, and the United Kingdom.
- Rivian was incorporated in the state of Delaware on March 26, 2015.
- Rivian trades on the Nasdaq Global Select Market under the symbol “RIVN.”
- Rivian’s independent registered public accounting firm is KPMG LLP.
- Rivian priced its initial public offering on November 9, 2021 and began trading on Nasdaq on November 10, 2021.
- Rivian has never declared or paid any cash dividends on its capital stock and does not anticipate declaring or paying any dividends in the foreseeable future.
