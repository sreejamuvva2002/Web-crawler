## Facts

- Shiroki North America, Inc. in Tennessee, U.S.A. recorded its highest-ever sales of 28,558 million yen, up 5.7 percent from the previous year's results.
- Higher sales at Shiroki North America, Inc. were driven by greater production volume of door sashes for the Nissan Altima assembled in the U.S.A.
- Total U.S. sales reached 28,552 million yen, up 5.7 percent year-on-year, with operating profit increasing to 555 million yen from 3 million yen the previous year.
- The Group strengthened its production structure in North America in response to increased requirements from Japanese automakers in the region.
- Shiroki-GA, LLC in Georgia completed a facility expansion project and launched door sash production.
- Shiroki GT, LLC in Tennessee began manufacturing window regulators.
- The Company optimally allocated production among its three U.S. facilities, with Shiroki North America, Inc. specializing in seat components, Shiroki-GA, LLC specializing in door sashes, and Shiroki GT, LLC specializing in window regulators.
- A centralized production system across the three U.S. facilities was implemented to enhance production efficiency.
- Shiroki North America, Inc. (Tennessee, USA) planned to install manufacturing equipment with an estimated investment of 276 million JPY from April 2008 to March 2009.
- Shiroki-GA, LLC (Georgia, USA) planned to install manufacturing equipment with an estimated investment of 495 million JPY from January 2008 to December 2008.
- Shiroki-GT, LLC (Tennessee, USA) planned to install manufacturing equipment with an estimated investment of 261 million JPY from January 2008 to December 2008.
