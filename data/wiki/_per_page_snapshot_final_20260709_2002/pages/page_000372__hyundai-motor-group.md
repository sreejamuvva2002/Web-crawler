# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hyundai confirms details of electric vehicle factory to be built in South Georgia](https://www.ajc.com/news/hyundai-ev-factory-to-be-announced-near-savannah/U6QAEVY72NGZBNJGZQFUHNJJ5U/)  ·  `page_000372`

## Facts

- South Korean automotive giant Hyundai Motor Group confirmed plans to build a roughly $5.5 billion electric vehicle assembly and battery plant near Savannah.
- The facility will eventually employ about 8,100 workers.
- This marks the second major electric vehicle factory announcement in Georgia since December.
- State economic development officials are working to turn Georgia into an important manufacturing hub for battery-powered automobiles.
- Hyundai officials and Gov. Brian Kemp outlined future operations during a ceremony near the Georgia coast.
- The plant will be located on a nearly 3,000-acre, state-owned megasite along I-16 in Bryan County.
- Hyundai officials stated they will break ground in 2023 and start production in 2025.
- The facility will have an annual capacity for 300,000 EVs.
- The new plant will be highly automated and feature state-of-the-art technology with high automation levels.
- Hyundai Motor Co. CEO Jaehoon Chang said the facility will be one of the most advanced EV plants in the world.
- The project includes a battery manufacturing facility.
- Georgia and South Korea share a long history of economic cooperation.
- Hyundai has invested $5.1 billion in the U.S. since 2013.
- The deal follows pushback over Rivian's planned plant in Bryan County.
- Rivian's Georgia plant was initially planned for Bryan County but faced opposition from local residents concerned about traffic and infrastructure impacts.
- No cars will roll off the line at the Hyundai plant in Bryan County until 2025.
- The project is part of a broader push to attract automotive and battery manufacturing to Georgia.
- Rivian, an electric vehicle startup, announced it would build its first U.S. factory in Georgia.
