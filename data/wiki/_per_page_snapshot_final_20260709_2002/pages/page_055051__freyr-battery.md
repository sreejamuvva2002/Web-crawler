# Freyr Battery

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-02-07

**Source:** [Battery firm abandons plan for a $2.6 billion plant in Georgia](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/battery-firm-abandons-plan-2-204124656.html)  ·  `page_055051`

## Facts

- Freyr Battery abandoned its plan to build a giant electric battery factory in Atlanta's suburbs after shifting focus to buy a solar panel plant in Texas.
- The company informed officials in Newnan on Thursday that it would not proceed with a $2.6 billion plant planned to hire more than 700 people.
- Freyr sent a Jan. 21 letter to the Coweta County Development Authority announcing its plans to end the project.
- The planned factory was designed to build batteries to store electricity produced by renewable sources and release it later.
- When announced in 2023, the facility would have been the second-largest battery factory worldwide.
- Freyr, a startup founded in 2018, never began construction on the 368-acre (149-hectare) site.
- The company moved its corporate headquarters from Norway to Newnan in part to maximize eligibility for U.S. tax benefits under President Joe Biden's climate law.
- Freyr shifted its focus to a newly opened solar panel factory that it bought last year for $340 million from top Chinese solar panel maker Trina Solar.
- Freyr spokesperson Amy Jaick stated the company is grateful for support and partnership in Coweta County and throughout Georgia.
- The company noted in a December release that it is currently focusing on its solar module manufacturing facility in Texas.
- Freyr senior vice president of business development Jason Peace met with local officials on Thursday.
- Freyr cited rising interest rates, falling battery prices, a change in company leadership, and a shift in goals as drivers for the decision.
- Coweta County Development Authority President Sarah Jacobs reported the meeting and reasons in an email on Friday.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development conveyed a $7 million grant to purchase a site for Freyr in Newnan, located about 35 miles (55 kilometers) southwest of Atlanta.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development spokesperson Jessica Atwell stated the state and company are working together to ensure the grant money is repaid expeditiously.
- Freyr may also owe money to Coweta County.
- Atwell stated that Georgia's incentives process protects taxpayers and ensures discretionary incentives are repaid when a company's plans change.
- Coweta County Development Authority President Sarah Jacobs said planning for the project made the county a stronger candidate for future projects.
- Freyr previously planned to build battery factories in Norway and Finland but announced in November that it would try to sell its European business.
- The company terminated its license for battery manufacturing technology, paying $3 million to the licensing company.
- Then-CEO Tom Einar Jensen told investors in August that raising money for battery production had become difficult due to a surplus of lower-cost Chinese batteries.
- Freyr switched its strategy to businesses allowing it to raise cash, including solar panel manufacturing.
- The company's cash on hand fell from $253 million at the end of 2023 to $182 million on Sept. 30.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp has targeted the recruitment of the electric vehicle industry.
- Korean firm SK Innovation built a $2.6 billion battery plant in Commerce, northeast of Atlanta, hired 3,000 workers, and later laid off or furloughed some workers.
- Hyundai Motor Group has started production at a $7.6 billion electric vehicle and battery plant near Savannah, with plans to hire 8,500 workers.
- Electric truck maker Rivian revived plans to build a plant east of Atlanta after receiving a $6.6 billion loan from the Biden administration.
