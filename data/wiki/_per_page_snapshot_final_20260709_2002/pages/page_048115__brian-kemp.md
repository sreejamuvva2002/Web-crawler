# Brian Kemp

**Type:** person  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-02-05

**Source:** [Brian Kemp on Georgia Economy and Small Business Growth](https://www.forbes.com/sites/rhettbuttle/2026/02/05/business-owner-to-governor-brian-kemp-on-georgias-economic-future/)  ·  `page_048115`

## Facts

- Governor Brian Kemp is the 83rd governor of Georgia.
- Early in his first term, Governor Kemp created the “Georgians First Commission” to make Georgia the top state for small business in the country.
- For the 12th consecutive year, Area Development magazine has named Georgia the No. 1 state for business.
- In Fiscal Year 2025, the Georgia Department of Economic Development Global Commerce team supported 423 facility expansions and new locations.
- The FY2025 facility expansions and new locations represented more than $26.3 billion in investment, a new record.
- The FY2025 facility expansions and new locations created 23,200 new private-sector jobs.
- In Fiscal Year 2025, 64% of new jobs were created outside the 10-county metro Atlanta area.
- In Fiscal Year 2025, 75% of total investments were made outside the 10-county metro Atlanta area.
- The state prioritizes stability and speed-to-market to create a fertile environment for growth across every corner of Georgia.
- Georgia's business climate formula focuses on trusting job creators and getting government out of the way.
- The state tackles three main deal killers: a burdensome tax code, slow permits, and lack of available sites.
- Georgia has slashed red tape to make permitting faster for developers and manufacturers.
- Georgia is aggressively returning money to taxpayers by accelerating its largest income tax cut to 4.99% three years ahead of schedule.
- The state invested heavily in the Rural Site Development Initiative, partnering with local development authorities to grade land and install infrastructure.
- Through the Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) program, the state performs upfront heavy lifting to create ready-made sites before companies look.
- In 2025, Governor Kemp signed major tort reform legislation, Senate Bills 68 and 69, to address lawsuit abuse.
- The 2025 tort reform legislation aims to give entrepreneurs confidence to hire and expand without fear of frivolous lawsuits.
- The tort reform also provided tools for the Insurance Commissioner to fight against out-of-control insurance rate increases.
- Georgia is investing in workforce development through the Technical College System of Georgia and its Quick Start program.
- These workforce investments ensure skilled Georgians are ready to work when new companies open their doors.
- In 2025, Georgia officially reached 5 million Georgians working for the first time.
- At the annual Governor's Workforce Summit in October, educators and business leaders were brought together to operationalize the "Top State for Talent" initiative.
- The state is treating its workforce pipeline like a supply chain by using a new High Demand Career List.
- The High Demand Career List connects K-12 and technical education directly with industry needs so students graduate with in-demand skills.
- Governor Kemp is cracking down on criminal street gangs to ensure the physical and legal safety of small business owners.
- Governor Kemp is proposing the new DREAMS Scholarship, a need-based aid program to remove financial barriers for students.
- Atlanta will host World Cup games this year, prompting state investments in keeping the capital city clean and safe for vendors and small businesses.
- Governor Kemp established the Georgia US250 Commission to ensure the U.S. 250th anniversary celebration reaches every county and drives tourism revenue.
- The state is making historic investments in high-speed broadband and infrastructure to unlock a "rural renaissance" for manufacturing and business growth.
- These rural infrastructure investments level the playing field so small businesses in rural Georgia have the same digital access to the global economy as firms in Midtown Atlanta.
- Governor Kemp warns that complacency is the biggest risk to Georgia's business environment over the next five years.
- Georgia is in real competition with other Republican states for jobs and cannot afford to abandon long-term strategies.
