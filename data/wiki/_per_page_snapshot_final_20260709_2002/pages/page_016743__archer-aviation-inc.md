# Archer Aviation Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Archer Aviation Selects Georgia For Manufacturing Facility Next to Covington Municipal Airport – Candela Green Energy](https://candela.com.my/archer-aviation-selects-georgia-for-manufacturing-facility-next-to-covington-municipal-airport/)  ·  `page_016743`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation Inc., a pioneer in electric vertical takeoff and landing (eVTOL) aircraft, announced plans to build its manufacturing facility in Covington, Georgia, next to the Covington Municipal Airport.
- Archer announced a new partnership with Molicel to supply battery cells for Archer’s Midnight Aircraft.
- The manufacturing facility will be located on a 96-acre property.
- Archer intends to initially develop a 350,000-square-foot plant.
- The initial plant capacity is projected to produce up to 650 aircraft annually.
- Over the long run, the eVTOL aircraft production plant is expected to provide over 1,000 jobs.
- Construction of the facility is expected to be finished in the first half of 2024.
- Initial production is projected to start in the second half of 2024.
- The facility will have the capacity to grow by an additional 550,000 square feet.
- The expanded capacity is estimated to enable the production of up to 2,300 aircraft annually.
- Archer Aviation thoroughly investigated all available locations across the US to choose the site for its first manufacturing facility.
- Important factors in site selection included the availability of skilled workers on the local labor market, accessibility of utilities, capability of conducting smooth flight test operations, construction expenses, and logistics.
- A grant from the Georgia REBA is part of the incentive package Archer will receive.
- The incentive package also includes tax breaks and land conveyance.
- Archer believes Synovus, the biggest bank with its headquarters in Georgia, will offer finance for the project.
- Adam Goldstein, Archer’s CEO, stated that after many months of discussion and evaluation, they are excited to select Georgia as the location for their manufacturing facility.
- Adam Goldstein noted that the U.S. has long led the world in aerospace technology and Georgia has played a vital role in that industry.
- Adam Goldstein stated that eVTOL technology can transform how urban and rural communities live and commute, and the factory can create pathways to highly skilled manufacturing jobs and other ladders of social and economic opportunity.
- Adam Goldstein expressed gratitude to Governor Kemp along with Bob Kosek, Sara Gershon, Serra Hall, Frank Turner, their teams, and Archer colleagues for pioneering innovative aviation technologies.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp commented that Georgia has made nurturing innovation a priority as they work with businesses to define the future.
- Governor Kemp noted that Georgia's established leadership in the aerospace industry was essential to landing Archer Aviation in Newton County.
- Governor Kemp highlighted that Archer can take advantage of Georgia's signature workforce training program Quick Start to help fill over 1,000 jobs in Covington in the long term.
- Georgia is home to more than 800 aerospace businesses.
- The state maintains a strong pipeline for aerospace education with 12 high schools providing industry training, 5 technical institutions offering aviation programs, and 7 universities granting degrees in aerospace engineering.
- For more than 10 years, the Georgia Center of Innovation for Aerospace, a strategic division of the Georgia Department of Economic Development, has fostered an ecosystem of opportunity for cutting-edge businesses like Archer.
