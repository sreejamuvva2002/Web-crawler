# EnviroSpark

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** charging_infrastructure  ·  **Published:** 2024-03-08

**Source:** [Charged EVs | EnviroSpark wins Georgia NEVI awards to install DC fast chargers at Waffle House and Shell station - Charged EVs](https://chargedevs.com/newswire/envirospark-wins-georgia-nevi-awards-to-install-dc-fast-chargers-at-waffle-house-and-shell-station/)  ·  `page_060533`

## Facts

- EnviroSpark won a National Electric Vehicle Infrastructure (NEVI) funding award from Georgia to build eight DC fast chargers.
- Four of the new DC fast chargers will be installed at a Waffle House in Tifton, Georgia.
- The remaining four DC fast chargers will be installed at a Shell station in Metter, Georgia.
- EnviroSpark secured two of the five eligible sites awarded in Phase 1 of the state’s NEVI awards.
- All eight stations will be hosted on EnviroSpark's national charging network.
- Construction and installation of the chargers will begin later this year.
- The project marks the second collaboration between EnviroSpark and Waffle House.
- In February, the Tennessee DOT selected EnviroSpark to build stations at a Waffle House in Lakeland, Tennessee.
- Stephanie Luque, co-founder of EnviroSpark who also leads the company’s federal initiatives, stated they are honored to partner again with Waffle House.
- Luque noted that NEVI-funded projects represent huge steps toward reducing drivers’ range anxiety and moving closer to mass EV adoption.
- According to GDOT, Georgia is among the top 10 states for EV sales.
- GDOT reported that three of the top five largest pending investments in the growing EV supply chain are in Georgia.
- Georgia has around 1,200 public charging stations.
- Only 14 stations currently meet both the distance and power requirements under federal rules (minimum of four 150 kW ports and located within one mile of an Alternative Fuel Corridor).
- Andrew Heath, Georgia DOT Deputy Chief Engineer, stated that the Georgia NEVI Deployment Program outlines the state’s approach to using federal funding to address EV infrastructure gaps in Alternative Fuel Corridors.
- Heath noted that Georgia is turning to the private sector to deliver innovative infrastructure at strategic locations across the state through public-private partnerships.
