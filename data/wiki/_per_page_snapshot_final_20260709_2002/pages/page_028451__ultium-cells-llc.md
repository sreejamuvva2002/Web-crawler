# Ultium Cells LLC

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [U.S. Department of Energy Announces $2.5 Billion Loan to Ultium Cells for Three Domestic Battery Cell Manufacturing Facilities | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/articles/us-department-energy-announces-25-billion-loan-ultium-cells-three-domestic-battery-cell)  ·  `page_028451`

## Facts

- The U.S. Department of Energy (DOE), through its Loan Programs Office (LPO), announced the closing of a $2.5 billion loan to Ultium Cells LLC on December 12, 2022.
- The loan will help finance the construction of new lithium-ion battery cell manufacturing facilities in Ohio, Tennessee, and Michigan.
- Ultium Cells is a joint venture between General Motors and LG Energy Solution that will manage battery cell production at the three facilities to address growing U.S. consumer demand for electric vehicles (EVs).
- The project is expected to create approximately more than 11,000 good-paying jobs across the three facilities, including 6,000 construction jobs and 5,100 operations jobs.
- The newly-organized Warren, Ohio facility will include more than 700 United Auto Worker jobs.
- In October, President Biden launched the American Battery Materials Initiative alongside $2.8 billion in grants from DOE to build out the battery mineral and material supply chain.
- The loan supports President Biden’s goals to reach net zero emissions by 2050 and have EVs make up half of all new vehicle sales by 2030.
- U.S. Secretary of Energy Jennifer M. Granholm stated that the loan will jumpstart domestic battery cell production needed to reduce reliance on other countries, support widespread EV adoption, and cut carbon pollution from gas-powered vehicles.
- U.S. Senator Sherrod Brown (OH) stated that investing in American production and Ohio workers is part of a pro-American, pro-worker industrial policy and will help Ohio lead the country and world in producing sustainable technology and electric vehicles.
- U.S. Senator Debbie Stabenow (MI) stated she authored the Advanced Technology Vehicles Manufacturing Program to ensure clean vehicles and innovative technologies are made at home, noting the investment will create thousands of good-paying jobs, strengthen the supply chain, and help address the climate crisis.
- U.S. Representative Tim Ryan (OH-13) stated his advocacy for the Mahoning Valley to become the nation’s hub of electric vehicle production yielded a major DOE investment in the workforce.
- In July, LPO announced a conditional commitment for the loan to Ultium Cells to manufacture large format, pouch-type cells that use state-of-the-art chemistry to deliver more range at less cost.
- The battery cells can be arranged in different combinations to provide clean, reliable energy for pickups, SUVs, family vehicles, luxury vehicles, and commercial vehicles.
- Ultium Cells plans to use this technology in coordination with General Motors’ work to eliminate 100% of tailpipe emissions from its new U.S. light-duty vehicles by 2035.
- The project supports GM’s plans to install capacity to produce more than one million EVs annually in North America and make its global products and operations carbon neutral by 2040.
- Today’s announcement marks LPO’s first closed loan exclusively for a battery cell manufacturing project under the Advanced Technology Vehicles Manufacturing (ATVM) program.
- Financing from the ATVM program complements historic investments from the President’s Bipartisan Infrastructure Law, including $7.5 billion for EV charging infrastructure and more than $7 billion for critical minerals supply chains necessary for batteries, components, materials, and recycling.
- The Inflation Reduction Act appropriates $3 billion for the costs of direct loans to remain available through September 30, 2028, and expands uses to medium- and heavy-duty vehicles, maritime vessels, aviation, and other transportation modes.
- The Inflation Reduction Act provides a clear demand signal for EV batteries as the auto industry builds future vehicles in America.
- As of the end of October, DOE attracted 98 active applications across all LPO’s new and improved programs, totaling over $104 billion in requested loans and loan guarantees for projects across the country.
