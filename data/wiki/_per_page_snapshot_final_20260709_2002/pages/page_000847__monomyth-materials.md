# Monomyth Materials

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2026-05-18

**Source:** [Charged EVs | Monomyth Materials acquires NanoGraf to pair SiOx silicon anodes with its domestic synthetic graphite platform - Charged EVs](https://chargedevs.com/newswire/monomyth-materials-acquires-nanograf-to-pair-siox-silicon-anodes-with-its-domestic-synthetic-graphite-platform/)  ·  `page_000847`

## Facts

- Monomyth Materials has acquired the assets of NanoGraf.
- NanoGraf is a Chicago-based silicon anode company spun out of Northwestern University and Argonne National Laboratory.
- The acquisition pairs SiOx technology and a defense-validated IP portfolio with Monomyth's domestic synthetic graphite business.
- Financial terms for the acquisition were not disclosed.
- The NanoGraf assets become the foundation of a new subsidiary, M2Innovations.
- M2Innovations is focused on advanced battery materials and prototype cell development from R&D through pilot scale.
- M2Innovations sits alongside M2Graphite, Monomyth's synthetic graphite business.
- M2Graphite was simultaneously rebranded from Anovion Technologies.
- M2Graphite handles commercial-scale production.
- The combination covers essentially the full active anode stack.
- Graphite and silicon together make up virtually all of the active material in a lithium-ion anode.
- Both are currently dominated by Chinese supply.
- Over 90% of active anode material is sourced from China today.
- China accounts for more than 60% of global SiOx capacity.
- NanoGraf's SiOx technology is already deployed in consumer electronics and military applications.
- The technology has validation from federal defense and energy agencies, including the Department of Energy.
- M2Graphite was assembled from graphite assets formerly held by Amsted Industries and Pyrotek.
- Amsted remains a minority shareholder.
- The Bainbridge, Georgia site is slated to be the first of several facilities.
- The site is backed by three planned capital programs of more than $1 billion each.
- Monomyth has invested more than $100 million in the platform since 2022.
- Francis Wang, who led NanoGraf through its growth stage, will join the M2Innovations advisory board.
