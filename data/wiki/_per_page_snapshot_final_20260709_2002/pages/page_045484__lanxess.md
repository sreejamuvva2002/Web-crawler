# LANXESS

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_material  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- The feasibility of plastic enclosures for high-voltage batteries in electric vehicles has been proven.
- A technology demonstrator made from polyamide 6 passes all important mechanical and thermal tests.
- Prototype testing is being conducted in an electric test vehicle.
- Development projects for series production with automotive manufacturers are being launched.
- LANXESS was responsible for the material development of the jointly developed technology demonstrator.
- Kautex Textron was responsible for the engineering, design, and manufacturing process of the demonstrator.
- Dr. Christopher Hoefs is the Project Manager e-Powertrain at LANXESS.
- The near-series demonstrator passes all mechanical and thermal tests that are relevant for such enclosures.
- Solutions for the thermal management and leak tightness of the enclosure have been developed.
- An enclosure prototype is being road tested in a test vehicle to verify its suitability for daily use.
- LANXESS and Kautex Textron are currently jointly tackling the first series-production development projects with automotive manufacturers.
- Felix Haas is the Director Product Development at Kautex Textron.
- Calculations revealed that the carbon footprint of the plastic enclosure is over 40 percent smaller compared to an aluminum design.
- The lower energy use in the production of polyamide 6 compared with metal helps minimize the carbon footprint.
- The omission of time-consuming cathodic dip painting to prevent corrosion where steel is used helps minimize the carbon footprint.
- The thermoplastic component design makes recycling the enclosure easier compared with thermoset materials such as sheet molding compounds (SMC).
- Tests on the technology demonstrator were carried out in accordance with internationally recognized standards for battery-powered electric vehicles such as ECE R100 from the Economic Commission for Europe.
- Tests also followed the Chinese standard GB 38031.
- The large-format all-plastic enclosure measures around 1,400 millimeters in both length and width.
- The enclosure meets the requirements of the mechanical shock test, which is used to examine the component’s behavior in the event of severe shocks.
- The enclosure meets the requirements of the crush test, which examines the resistance of the battery enclosure in the event of slow deformation.
- Results of the drop and vibration tests were positive.
- Results of the bottom impact test, which examines the stability of the batteries in the event of ground contact or impacts from sizeable stones, were positive.
- All test results corroborate the previous simulations and calculations.
- A critical failure of the plastic enclosure would not have occurred in any of the load cases.
- The demonstrator proved its resistance to external sources of fire underneath the vehicle in accordance with ECE R100 (external fire).
- The demonstrator was developed based on the aluminum battery housing of a mid-size electric vehicle and designed for mass production.
- It is manufactured in a single-stage compression molding process with a molding compound based on the polyamide 6 compound Durethan B24CMH2.0 from LANXESS.
- The manufacturing process does not require any further rework.
- Crash-relevant areas are specially reinforced with locally placed blanks made from the continuous-fiber-reinforced, polyamide 6-based composite Tepex dynalite 102-RGUD600.
- Compared with an aluminum design, there is a weight saving of around 10 percent.
- The weight saving is advantageous for the range and therefore the carbon footprint of the vehicle.
- Integration of functions such as fasteners, reinforcing ribs, and components for thermal management reduces the number of individual components significantly compared with the metal design.
- Reducing the number of individual components simplifies assembly and logistical effort and reduces manufacturing costs.
- LANXESS is a leading specialty chemicals company.
- LANXESS had sales of EUR 6.8 billion in 2019.
- LANXESS currently has about 14,400 employees in 33 countries.
- The core business of LANXESS is the development, manufacturing, and marketing of chemical intermediates, additives, specialty chemicals, and plastics.
- LANXESS is listed in the leading sustainability indices Dow Jones Sustainability Index (DJSI World and Europe) and FTSE4Good.
