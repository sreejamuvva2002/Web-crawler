# GEIGER Automotive, USA, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [GA: GEIGER Automotive to Create 120 Jobs at New U.S. Mfg Facility | Trade and Industry Development](https://www.tradeandindustrydev.com/industry/plastics/news/ga-geiger-automotive-create-120-jobs-new-us-mfg-10300)  ·  `page_058232`

## Facts

- The Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) announced that Germany-based GEIGER Automotive will open a U.S. manufacturing facility in Gwinnett County.
- The new facility will create as many as 120 jobs over the next several years.
- To better reach its growing customer base in the Southeast, the company will move into a 48,000-foot facility in Suwanee.
- The new facility will manufacture plastic automotive components for engine compartment powertrain applications.
- GEIGER produces highly engineered products for cooling and brake systems in relation to air and fluid management, as well as many other functional products under the hood.
- The new jobs will be in injection molding and assembly manufacturing, as well as customer support positions.
- GDEcD Commissioner Chris Carr stated that GEIGER Automotive’s decision underscores Georgia’s booming auto hub and the growth of German companies in the state.
- Georgia’s strategic location to large OEMs, including the Volkswagen Chattanooga Assembly Plant, coupled with a highly skilled workforce, creates an ideal location for GEIGER Automotive.
- Ralph Ralston, president of the NAFTA region at GEIGER Automotive, USA, Inc., said the location was strategically selected to optimize supply to current automotive customers in the southeast U.S. and noted increasing interest from automotive OEMs in the region with high growth potential in Gwinnett County.
- GEIGER has grown to become one of the leading partners of the automotive industry, with other state-of-the-art production locations in Germany, Mexico, and China manufacturing precision components and subassemblies.
- Since June 2014, German automotive companies have invested more than $160 million and created more than 1,400 jobs in Georgia.
- Nico Wijnberg, GDEcD international project manager, supported the company on behalf of Georgia, along with Lonnie Smallwood, project manager at Electric Cities of Georgia and Partnership Gwinnett.
- Gwinnett Board of Commissioners Chairman Charlotte Nash welcomed GEIGER to the growing international business community and noted Gwinnett’s commitment to supporting international business expansion.
- Suwanee Mayor Jimmy Burnette called the facility a great win for the City of Suwanee and expressed confidence that GEIGER will find necessary support and resources in the community.
- GEIGER Automotive is headquartered in Murnau, Germany, and supplies primarily German automotive manufacturers with highly engineered plastic components for powertrain applications.
- The company operates three production facilities in Germany, one in the USA, and is opening new facilities in Mexico and China in 2015.
- GEIGER Automotive employs a workforce of more than 700 people globally.
- GEIGER Automotive is wholly owned by Sanoh Industrial Co., Ltd.
