# SK On / Hyundai Motor Group Bartow County EV Battery Facility

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-12-08

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp: Hyundai Motor Group and SK On To Build EV Battery Facility in Bartow County | Governor Brian P. Kemp Office of the Governor](https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases/2022-12-08/gov-kemp-hyundai-motor-group-and-sk-build-ev-battery-facility-bartow)  ·  `page_000070`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Hyundai Motor Group (HMG) and SK On have selected a site in Bartow County for a new electric vehicle (EV) battery manufacturing facility.
- The new facility will supply Hyundai Motor Group’s plants in the U.S.
- The project is considered one of the largest economic development projects in Georgia state history.
- Stakeholders estimate the project will create more than 3,500 new jobs.
- The project involves approximately $4-5 billion of investment in Bartow County.
- Hyundai Motor Group and SK On recently signed a memorandum of understanding (MOU) regarding the partnership for a new EV battery facility in the U.S.
- Details of the partnership are still in development.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Hyundai Motor Group and SK On are valued partners and key players in Georgia's ever-growing automotive industry.
- Governor Kemp noted his administration has been focused on bringing jobs and opportunity to communities across the state that may have been overlooked in the past.
- Governor Kemp said SK and HMG share the goal of bringing jobs and opportunity and are proud they are choosing to invest further in Georgia, the No. 1 state for business.
- SK On was established in 2021 as the lithium-ion battery subsidiary branch of SK Innovation.
- SK On currently employs more than 2,000 Georgians at its SK Battery America facility in Commerce.
- In July, SK Battery America announced it would partner with the Work for Warriors Georgia program to hire veterans, servicemembers, and their families at its Georgia operations.
- The new facility's development is subject to execution of relevant agreements and HMG and SK On's final board decisions.
- The new facility will be located at Bartow Centre, a zoned manufacturing and industrial site located on Highway 411.
- The project is aiming to begin operations in 2025.
- Bartow County Sole Commissioner Steve Taylor expressed pleasure with the decision of Hyundai Motor Group and SK On.
- Commissioner Taylor thanked the owners of the former Bartow Centre Industrial property and noted they pursued the extraordinary economic development project together.
- Commissioner Taylor stated the project will have regional benefit for job seekers in the area and establishes Bartow in Georgia’s EV ecosystem in a significant way.
- Commissioner Taylor cited Bartow’s strong history and talent-base built upon advanced manufacturing, the automotive industry, and innovation as reasons the battery partnership should thrive.
- Director of Korean Investment Yoonie Kim represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The project was pursued in partnership with Georgia EMC, Development Authority of Bartow County, the Cartersville-Bartow County Department of Economic Development, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated HMG and SK have been pioneering partners for Georgia for decades as major drivers for Georgia’s automotive renaissance.
- Commissioner Wilson noted HMG was the first Korean manufacturer to locate in the state.
- Commissioner Wilson said supporting cooperation across Georgia's growing EV ecosystem is creating a fully integrated supply chain for automotive OEMs while connecting battery manufacturers with recyclers to close the loop on battery manufacturing.
- Commissioner Wilson expressed excitement for the jobs of the future this will create for Bartow County and northwest Georgia.
- Georgia’s prime location, major ports system, extensive infrastructure, skilled workforce, and pro-business climate have made it an attractive location for rapidly developing industries focused on creating a sustainable future.
- Building on assets that make the automotive industry successful, Georgia is positioned as a hub for the electric transportation industry.
- Excluding this announcement, EV-related projects announced in Georgia since 2020 total approximately $17 billion in investment and more than 22,800 new jobs.
- Hyundai Motor Group separately announced in May its plans to invest $5.54 billion in Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America, an EV manufacturing facility in Bryan County.
- Hyundai Motor Group broke ground on the Bryan County facility in October with Governor Kemp, other state leaders, and local and federal officials.
- Hyundai Motor Group is a global enterprise that has created a value chain based on mobility, steel, construction, logistics, finance, IT, and service.
- Hyundai Motor Group has about 250,000 employees worldwide.
- Hyundai Motor Group's mobility brands include Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis.
- SK On is a global leading electric vehicle (EV) battery developer, manufacturer, and solutions provider.
- SK On's mission is to make the world a cleaner and more convenient place as an electrification linchpin.
- SK On was launched as an independent company in 2021 after SK Innovation, South Korea’s largest energy company, decided to split off its battery business.
- SK On aims to become a world leader in the clean energy industry by leveraging its global production base and R&D capabilities, as well as its production and quality management know-how.
- SK On is headquartered in Seoul, South Korea.
- SK On has a worldwide presence with battery plants currently operating or in construction across the United States, Europe, and Asia.
