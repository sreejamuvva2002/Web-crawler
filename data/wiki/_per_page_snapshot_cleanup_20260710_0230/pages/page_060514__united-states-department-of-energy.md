# United States Department of Energy

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 1977-08-04

**Source:** [United States Department of Energy - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Energy)  ·  `page_060514`

## Facts

- The United States Department of Energy (DOE) is an executive department that oversees national energy policy, energy production, nuclear power research and development, naval reactor production, energy-related research, and energy conservation.
- The DOE was created in 1977 following the 1973 oil crisis and began operations on October 1, 1977.
- Current Secretary is Chris Wright, who has served since February 2025.
- Headquarters are located at the James V. Forrestal Building in Washington, D.C., with additional offices in Germantown, Maryland.
- The department employs 14,382 civil servants (as of 2018) and 93,094 contractors (as of 2008), with an annual budget of $45.7 billion (2023).
- Title XVII of the Energy Policy Act of 2005 authorizes the DOE to issue loan guarantees for projects that avoid, reduce, or sequester air pollutants or greenhouse gas emissions and employ new or significantly improved technologies.
- In December 2024, the Loan Programs Office announced a $15 billion low-interest loan to Pacific Gas & Electric to enhance transmission lines critical for renewable energy integration, data center operations, and the growing fleet of electric vehicles.
- In June 2026, the DOE announced $17.5 billion in loans to accelerate construction of 10 new large nuclear reactors across the U.S., utilizing Westinghouse’s AP1000 design, with each reactor capable of generating 1.1 GW and combined output projected to power nearly 10 million households.
- In November 2025, an executive order launched the Genesis Mission, involving the DOE and national laboratories to apply artificial intelligence and advanced computing to accelerate scientific research and energy capabilities.
- On December 18, 2025, the DOE announced collaboration agreements with 24 organizations to support the Genesis Mission initiative.
- The Advanced Research Projects Agency-Energy (ARPA-E) was created in 2007 and received an initial budget of about $400 million from the February 2009 economic stimulus bill.
- The 2009 DOE budget allocated $280 million to fund eight Energy Innovation Hubs focused on smart materials for buildings, solar energy conversion, advanced energy storage, and smart grid technologies.
- In 2012, the DOE awarded $120 million to the Ames Laboratory to establish the Critical Materials Institute, aimed at improving the supply of rare earth elements.
- The Grid Deployment Office is a division dedicated to spreading adoption of grid-enhancing technologies and improving transmission permitting.
- The department includes the Office of Clean Energy Demonstrations and the Loan Programs Office as key organizational components.
- The Fusion Energy Sciences program researches nuclear fusion, with a 2020 yearly budget of $670 million, including $250 million allocated to ITER.
