# Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Young people from all around Coweta County, with the help of Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation, are gearing up once again for a big day at the races. | Yamaha](https://www.yamaha-motor-georgia.com/node/63)  ·  `page_015616`

## Facts

- Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation (YMMC) employs more than 2,200 metro Atlanta residents to design and build recreational vehicles at its Newnan manufacturing facilities in Coweta County, Georgia.
- The Newnan facility manufactures all of the world’s Yamaha golf cars, WaveRunners and Side-by-Sides, as well as most of its ATVs.
- YMMC sponsored the second annual 'Yamaha Downhill Dreams Academy,' a program designed to engage at-risk students in STEM activities and potentially inspire careers in those fields.
- The Academy involved youth from the Newnan Coweta Boys and Girls Club designing, building, and testing soap box vehicles for the Coweta Grand Prix scheduled for April 20, 2024.
- This year's Academy expanded to include community business partners Golf Rider and Veritiv serving as mentors and fabricators for each participant’s racing team.
- Bob Brown, Vice President of Manufacturing at Yamaha, stated that being involved in something like this is invaluable for a young person who might not otherwise have the opportunity.
- Bob Brown also noted that real-world applications of STEM principles in such an exciting setting can make quite an impression at this age.
- The Coweta Grand Prix event took place on April 20 at 11am on LaGrange Street, utilizing a track approximately 600 feet long stretching from the Carnegie Library to City Hall.
- Workshop activities for the Academy included design sessions where students drew concept designs, and assembly sessions where participants applied finishing touches like paint, decals, and wheels.
- Testing day for the Academy occurred on April 15, allowing racers to practice driving their designed and assembled vehicles.
- The majority of participants in this year's design and build event were girls.
