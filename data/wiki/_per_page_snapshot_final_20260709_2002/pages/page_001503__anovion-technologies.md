# Anovion Technologies

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2023-05-15

**Source:** [Maker of EV battery materials coming to Southwest Georgia with $800 million investment](https://www.yahoo.com/news/maker-ev-battery-materials-coming-161858951.html)  ·  `page_001503`

## Facts

- A manufacturer of materials for lithium-ion batteries used to power electric vehicles will invest $800 million in a plant in Southwest Georgia.
- The new plant will create more than 400 jobs.
- Gov. Brian Kemp announced the investment on Monday.
- Chicago-based Anovion Technologies supplies premium synthetic graphite.
- Synthetic graphite is the largest battery material used in EVs by mass, exceeding copper, nickel, manganese, cobalt, and lithium.
- The new plant will be located in Bainbridge.
- The plant will span 1.5 million square feet.
- The plant will be the first of its size and scale in North America.
- Anovion CEO Eric Stopka stated that this is the company's first large-scale expansion manufacturing facility in Georgia.
- The company cites Georgia's strong technical workforce and top-tier schools as factors that will help advance research partnerships and contribute to a highly skilled talent pipeline.
- Gov. Brian Kemp stated that Georgia is fast becoming the e-mobility capital of the nation.
- Gov. Brian Kemp noted that job creators are recognizing that every corner of Georgia has the resources and workforce needed for success.
- The two largest economic development projects in Georgia history are electric vehicle manufacturing plants.
- Hyundai is investing $5.5 billion in a plant near Savannah in Bryan County.
- The Hyundai plant is expected to generate 8,100 jobs when fully built out.
- Rivian is building a $5 billion plant east of Atlanta.
- The Rivian plant will create 7,500 jobs.
- Suppliers of electric vehicle batteries are springing up across Georgia to serve the two major EV manufacturing plants.
- Anovion will be hiring for technical, production, and engineering jobs, as well as administrative and management positions.
- The Bainbridge plant is expected to begin operations in late 2025.
