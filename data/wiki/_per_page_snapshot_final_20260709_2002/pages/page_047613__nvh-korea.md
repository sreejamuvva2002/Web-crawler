# NVH Korea

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-06-22

**Source:** [EV Parts Supplier NVH Korea to Invest $72M in Henry County - Expansion Solutions](https://www.expansionsolutionsmagazine.com/ev-parts-supplier-nvh-korea-to-invest-72m-in-henry-county/)  ·  `page_047613`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that automotive parts manufacturer NVH Korea will establish a new facility in Locust Grove during an economic development trip to the Paris Air Show.
- The project will create over 160 new jobs.
- The project will bring $72 million in investment to Henry County.
- This is the second economic development project Governor Kemp has announced this week while promoting Georgia to international job creators abroad.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated that reliable infrastructure and a great workforce allow job creators to find success in every community in Georgia.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp cited the incredible growth of the e-mobility and battery industries in Georgia as a testament to supporting large suppliers like NVH Korea.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp welcomed NVH Korea as the latest partner in making Georgia the epicenter of the industrial revolution of their time.
- NVH Korea is an automotive parts manufacturer based in the City of Ulsan.
- NVH Korea was established in 1984.
- The company specializes in automotive noise, vibration, and heat-control parts.
- NVH Korea currently produces these parts in Korea, Europe, and India.
- The company operates a manufacturing facility that produces floor mats, cargo mats, and cargo trays in Georgia.
- The new facility will supply battery parts for electric vehicles (EVs) in the United States.
- NVH Korea will supply battery parts to manufacturers such as Hyundai Mobis, Kia Georgia, and Hyundai Motor Group.
- Ja Kyum Koo, Chairman and CEO of NVH Korea, stated they chose Georgia as the company’s first EV battery component manufacturing site.
- Ja Kyum Koo noted that cooperation between state agencies and company staff helped guide the project.
- Ja Kyum Koo described this as the first step in contributing to the electrification of the mobility industry.
- Ja Kyum Koo expressed looking forward to joining the community of Locust Grove and Henry County.
- NVH Korea’s new facility will be located at Gardner Logistics Park on Colvin Drive in Locust Grove.
- The facility will produce EV battery system components that protect, connect, and sense the performance of battery cells.
- Operations are expected to begin in the second quarter of 2024.
- Interested individuals can learn more about NVH Korea online.
- Mayor Robert Price of the City of Locust Grove stated NVH’s investment will increase manufacturing jobs and make them part of Georgia’s electric vehicle ecosystem.
- Mayor Robert Price expressed looking forward to a successful partnership with NVH and the opportunities this investment creates.
- Carlotta Harrell, Chair of the Henry County Board of Commissioners, stated today’s announcement will add quality manufacturing jobs for citizens and continue to diversify the economy.
- Carlotta Harrell expressed excitement to welcome NVH and for the community to join Georgia’s growing electric vehicle manufacturing base.
- Project Manager Jacob Lee represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The project was developed in partnership with the Henry County Development Authority, Georgia EMC, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated there is an economic transformation happening as manufacturers create jobs of the future aligned with cutting-edge technologies.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson welcomed innovators establishing their first U.S. or e-mobility manufacturing presence, like NVH Korea.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson noted these companies enrich the local industry mix, provide well-paying jobs for Georgians, and build on the statewide mobility ecosystem.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson thanked NVH Korea for selecting Georgia and congratulated community partners in Henry County.
- For over a century, Georgia has fostered healthy industry practices, encouraged collaboration and innovation, and positioned itself as a leader in developing emerging technologies for the automotive and mobility industry.
- From July 1 through December 31, 2022, the automotive industry was the second-largest source of new jobs and investment in Georgia.
- Since 2020, Georgia has pursued job creation along the entire EV supply chain, resulting in more than $22.7 billion in investments and the creation of over 28,400 jobs.

- Carter Chapman serves as Deputy Press Secretary for the Office of Governor Brian P. Kemp.
