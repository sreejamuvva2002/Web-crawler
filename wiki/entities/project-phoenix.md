# Project Phoenix

**Type:** Government Program  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[battery-cell-manufacturing|Battery Cell Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** Atlanta, Fulton County, Georgia, United States

## Overview

A state-led recruitment effort to attract an undisclosed battery manufacturer to establish a 110-worker innovation center in Atlanta, known by the code name “Project Phoenix.”

## EV supply-chain relevance

The proposed innovation center would add a battery manufacturing project to Georgia’s growing e-mobility and electric vehicle supply chain.

## Investment

- **$870,000** — Tax incentive for battery manufacturer
  - Develop Fulton is considering a nearly $870,000 tax break for the company.

## Jobs

- **110 workers**
  - The planned innovation center would employ approximately 110 staff members.

## Related organizations & people

- Georgia Department of Economic Development — State agency leading recruitment effort
- Develop Fulton (Development Authority of Fulton County) — County agency evaluating tax incentives
- Fulton County — Local jurisdiction providing potential tax incentives
- Atlanta Journal Constitution — Media outlet reporting on the initiative

## Key facts

- Georgia leaders are attempting to recruit a battery manufacturer to open an innovation center in Atlanta.
- The deal could involve a Fulton County agency granting the company hundreds of thousands of dollars in tax incentives.
- "Project Phoenix" is the code name for the recruitment effort.
- The proposed center would employ about 110 workers.
- It would add another battery-related project to Georgia’s fast-growing e-mobility and electric vehicle sector.
