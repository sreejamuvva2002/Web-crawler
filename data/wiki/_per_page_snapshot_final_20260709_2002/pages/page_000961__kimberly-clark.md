# Kimberly-Clark

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2020-02-26

**Source:** [Kimberly-Clark, United Renewable Energy® LLC and NextEra Energy Resources Team Up to Construct Solar Project in LaGrange, Georgia – United Renewable Energy](https://u-renew.com/kimberly-clark-united-renewable-energy-nextera-solar-project-lagrange-georgia/)  ·  `page_000961`

## Facts

- A 3MW solar photovoltaic (PV) project has been completed and commercialized by United Renewable Energy at Kimberly-Clark’s facility in LaGrange, Georgia.
- The project was a collaboration between Kimberly-Clark’s facility, United Renewable Energy LLC as the project developer and builder, and NextEra Energy Resources, LLC through a subsidiary acting as the long-term project owner.
- All output from the new solar facility and all renewable energy credits (RECs) are sold to Georgia Power as part of Georgia Power’s Renewable Energy Development Initiative.
- The associated RECs will be retired on behalf of all Georgia Power customers.
- The 3MW solar project includes over 8,600 solar panels.
- Kimberly-Clark leases the land for the project and entered a separate agreement to purchase RECs from NextEra Energy Resources to offset its greenhouse gas emissions.
- Jeremy Cannady serves as mill manager at Kimberly-Clark’s LaGrange facility.
- Kimberly-Clark is pursuing a global goal of a 40% offset in greenhouse gas emissions by 2022.
- William Silva is the CEO of United Renewable Energy.
- Matt Ulman is the vice president of distributed generation for NextEra Energy Resources.
- NextEra Energy Resources has approximately 21,000 megawatts of net generating capacity as of year-end 2018.
- NextEra Energy Resources operates clean, emissions-free nuclear power generation facilities in New Hampshire, Iowa and Wisconsin.
- NextEra Energy Resources is a subsidiary of Juno Beach, Florida-based NextEra Energy, Inc.
