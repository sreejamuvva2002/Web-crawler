# Georgia Department of Economic Development

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-07-01

**Source:** [Georgia in 2026: Strategic Growth Through Partnerships, Preparedness, Global Connectivity | Trade and Industry Development](https://www.tradeandindustrydev.com/region/georgia/georgia-2026-strategic-growth-through-partnerships-35579)  ·  `page_010169`

## Facts

- Fiscal Year 2025 spans July 1, 2024 to June 30, 2025.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development’s Global Commerce team secured 423 new locations and expansions during Fiscal Year 2025.
- These efforts resulted in a record $26.3 billion in total capital investment.
- Approximately 23,200 projected new private sector jobs were created alongside the investments.
- Approximately three-quarters of total investment occurred outside the 10-county Atlanta region.
- The state utilizes the Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) program to help communities complete environmental reviews, infrastructure planning, and engineering analyses in advance of project selection.
- GRAD preparation minimizes delays and cost risks by allowing businesses to move smoothly from site selection to construction.
- The state-backed Rural Site Development Initiative (RSDI) provides grant funding to communities for site studies, land grading, infrastructure improvements, and GRAD certification preparation.
- Recent RSDI grants have improved access roads and expanded utilities in previously untapped regions to attract new business.
- Georgia leverages Quick Start, a customized workforce training program, alongside technical colleges and universities to prepare employees for industry-specific requirements.
- Quick Start tailors curriculum to accelerate employee readiness across advanced manufacturing, logistics, technology, and corporate services.
- Companies consistently cite access to skilled talent and flexible training as key factors in choosing or expanding operations in Georgia.
- The state offers access to one of the nation’s busiest airports, major port facilities including the Port of Savannah and the Port of Brunswick, and a robust interstate and rail system.
- Ongoing infrastructure investments include highway expansions and freight/rail improvements to support supply chain efficiency and global trade.
- Strategic planning in Georgia’s energy sector focuses on stability, low costs, and reliable power to support energy-intensive growth.
- Key industrial sectors reinforced by the state's economic strategy include automotive, aerospace, food processing, advanced manufacturing, technology, life sciences, logistics, and corporate services.
- The state actively builds supplier networks and sustains industry clusters to strengthen its competitive advantage in dynamic global markets.
- Industry experts consistently rank Georgia highly for business climate and competitiveness due to its workforce development, site readiness, infrastructure, and economic planning.
- As the state enters 2026, it continues investing in site readiness, workforce development, infrastructure, and energy to position companies for scaling operations.
