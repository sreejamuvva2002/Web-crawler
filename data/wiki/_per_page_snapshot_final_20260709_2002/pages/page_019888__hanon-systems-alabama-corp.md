# Hanon Systems Alabama Corp.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** thermal_management  ·  **Published:** 2023-07-06

**Source:** [Bulloch Co. Commissioners Approve 10-Year Tax Abatement, Host of Other Incentives for South Korean Manufacturer • The Georgia Virtue](https://www.thegeorgiavirtue.com/bulloch-local-government/bulloch-co-commissioners-approve-10-year-tax-abatement-host-of-other-incentives-for-south-korean-manufacturer/)  ·  `page_019888`

## Facts

- Bulloch County Commissioners voted to finalize a 10-year tax abatement for a manufacturer coming to Bulloch County as part of the Hyundai Meta Plant.
- The approval comes weeks after commissioners voted to approve the FY 2024 budget and announced that an increase in the county’s millage rate is likely forthcoming.
- Hanon Systems Alabama Corp., a South Korea-based operation, announced its intention to come to Bulloch County in May of 2023.
- The company proclaimed its new facility will offer 160 jobs to the community.
- The facility is located at the Gateway Regional Industrial Park north of I-16.
- The project is supposedly a $40 million project.
- According to Global Atlanta, the company is a provider of automotive thermal management products and HVAC parts like vents, compressors, cooling fans and condensers.
- Documents provided to commissioners said the Development Authority entered into a Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) with CG Statesboro, LLC and Hanon Systems Alabama Corp. for the location of a project in Bulloch County to supply vehicular HVAC components for Hyundai.
- The Development Authority, CG Statesboro, LLC, and Hanon Systems Alabama Corp. are the parties to the MOU.
- The Board of Commissioners and Board of Tax Assessors were requested to acknowledge the MOU and agree to applicable provisions.
- As an inducement for location of the project in Bulloch County, the MOU includes a 100% abatement of County ad valorem taxes for 10 years.
- The tax abatement does not include school taxes or taxes for the County’s fire district.
- Wednesday evening, Commissioners voted on a tax abatement of 100% for ten years.
- The Bulloch County Development Authority agreed to a discounted land price from $1,700,000 to $850,000 for the bond lease with the Development Authority.
- The project will have availability of $17,500,000 for Real Estate Bonds for the Real Estate Assets and $22,500,000 for the Personal Property Bonds for the Personal Property Assets of the project.
- The Real Estate Bond lease is the responsibility of CG Statesboro, LLC (out of Alabama) while the Personal Property Bond Lease is the responsibility of Hanon Systems Alabama Corp, though the Development Authority will facilitate both.
- The Development Authority promised to apply for a $525,000 grant with the state on behalf of the companies.
- There is a commitment to provide $20,000 in training to employees of Hanon Systems via Ogeechee Technical College.
- There is a commitment to provide up to $10,000 worth of free office space to Hanon Systems.
- Due diligence and geotechnical report costs of $17,625 paid to Terracon Consultants, Inc. will be covered to save Hanon Systems the expense.
- The Development Authority will provide concierge services to the companies to assist in setting up utilities, permits, and other project needs.
- The contract with the Development Authority lists ‘Year 1’ as no later than 2026.
- The only instance in which the company would not be eligible for the tax abatement would be if the investment goal shortfall for jobs was more than 20% of what was promised.
- Approval by staff was recommended and commissioners approved the abatement unanimously with no discussion as part of the consent agenda.
