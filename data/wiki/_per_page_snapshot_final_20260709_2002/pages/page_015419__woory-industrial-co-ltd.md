# Woory Industrial Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-06

**Source:** [Woory Industrial Co. to open EV parts facility in GA](https://valdostatoday.com/news-2/region/2023/06/woory-industrial-co-to-open-ev-parts-facility-in-ga/)  ·  `page_015419`

## Facts

- An automotive parts manufacturer will open a new EV parts manufacturing facility in Georgia, creating over 130 new jobs.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Woory Industrial Co., Ltd. will establish a new manufacturing facility in Dublin, Georgia.
- The facility will create more than 130 new jobs and an estimated $18 million in investment in Dublin-Laurens County.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated pride in welcoming Woory Industrial as a job creator helping Georgia become the "e-mobility capital of the nation."
- Governor Kemp cited historic levels of new jobs and investment brought to communities by the emerging industry, thanks to the state's highly-skilled workforce and reliable infrastructure network.
- Governor Kemp expressed confidence that Woory will have a long and beneficial partnership with Dublin-Laurens County.
- Woory Industrial Company (known as Woory) was established in 1989 and specializes in heating, ventilation, and air conditioning components and systems for the global automotive industry.
- Woory currently maintains its traditional product lines while concentrating on developing new products for electric vehicles (EVs) and hydrogen-fueled cars.
- CEO Jungwoo Kim stated that the auto industry is facing a revolution for the first time in 100 years and identified the U.S. EV market as the key market for the transition to electric vehicles.
- Over the past 10 years, Woory has successfully grown into a specialized EV component company through continuous innovation.
- Jungwoo Kim noted that Georgia offers a strategic opportunity for Woory to advance as part of the vanguard of EV development and thanked the State of Georgia and City of Dublin for their partnership.
- The new facility, located at 404 Kellam Road in Dublin, will be Woory's first U.S. manufacturing location.
- The facility will produce electrical heaters, control units, and actuators for EV and internal combustion carmakers in North America.
- Operations are expected to begin in November 2023.
- Unlike internal combustion engine vehicles, EVs lack a built-in heating source, making EV heaters necessary for cabin heating and battery temperature maintenance.
- Woory's pioneering development of electric heaters over the last 10 years has established it as a leader in this growing market segment.
- Dublin Mayor Joshua Kight stated that Woory's commitment to repurposing an existing manufacturing building promotes infill development and creates new career paths for the community.
- Dr. Fred Williams, Chair of the Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority Board, highlighted Dublin and Laurens County's rapidly growing automotive sector driven by its location on Interstate 16 and access to a skilled workforce.
- Dr. Williams noted that partnerships with area education systems and training providers will ensure training programs are in place to fill Woory’s talent pipeline for years to come.
- Project Manager Jacob Lee represented the GDEcD Global Commerce team on the project, which was developed in partnership with the Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that projects like Woory's first U.S. facility in Dublin justify prioritizing the cutting-edge manufacturing transformation to create jobs and opportunities in rural communities.
- Commissioner Wilson noted that Georgia has invested for decades to match competitive projects with prepared development sites and congratulated Dublin-Laurens County on bringing future e-mobility jobs to middle Georgia.
- For over a century, Georgia has fostered industry practices, partnership, and innovation to position itself as a leader in developing emerging technologies for the automotive and mobility industry.
- From July 1 through December 31, 2022, the automotive industry was the second-largest source of new jobs and investment in Georgia.
- Since 2020, Georgia's pursuit of EV supply chain job creation opportunities has resulted in over $22.7 billion in investment and the creation of more than 28,400 jobs.
- Woory began nearly 35 years ago as a small factory producing fuel senders in Suwon, South Korea, and has grown into an international enterprise with global production plants employing over 1,300 people.
- Woory's current business focuses on developing and producing HVAC auto components for all vehicle types, including EVs, internal combustion engines, eco-friendly cars, and hydrogen-fueled cars.
- Woory has been nominated as a supplier for Tesla, Rivian, Lucid, and Canoo, and currently supplies the HV heater system used to heat EV cabins.
