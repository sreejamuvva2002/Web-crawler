# Aurubis AG

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2017-04-12

**Source:** [Aurubis capex plans include added US capacity – Carolina Recycling Association](https://www.cra-recycle.org/2022/12/aurubis-capex-plans-include-added-us-capacity/)  ·  `page_017346`

## Facts

- Germany-based copper producer and recycler Aurubis AG has listed a doubling of capacity at its under-construction smelter in Richmond, Georgia, among its capital expenditure (capex) plans for the current fiscal year.
- Aurubis announced three additional strategic capex projects with an investment volume of around 530 million euros ($563 million).
- One of these new projects involves investing to expand the modular recycling system at the U.S. site in Georgia, which will double processing capacities at the location with a second recycling module.
- Further investment adjustments for infrastructure requirements and inflation were approved for the ongoing Georgia smelter project.
- Aurubis is implementing its “Driving Sustainable Growth” strategy, citing the United States plant Aurubis Richmond in Georgia alongside recycling projects ASPA and BOB at two Belgian locations as examples currently being implemented.
- The company achieved operating earnings before taxes (EBT) of 532 million euros ($565 million) in its fiscal year 2021/22, which closed at the end of September.
- For the upcoming 2022/2023 fiscal year, Aurubis forecasts profits in the 400 million euros ($425 million) to 500 million euros ($531 million) range, representing a decline in EBT of from 6 percent to 24 percent.
- During the Oct. 1, 2021, to Sept. 30, 2022, fiscal year, the company benefited from significantly higher metal prices, especially for industrial metals such as copper, tin and nickel.
- The former Metallo facilities now part of the Aurubis portfolio have increased its presence in the tin and nickel markets.
- Aurubis CEO Roland Harings stated that the company has the metallurgical expertise to offer process and recycling solutions to recover strategically necessary future metals for the circular economy.
- Aurubis remains optimistic for the new 2022/23 financial year despite a general economic slowdown and higher burdens from rising energy prices.
