# Absolics, Inc.

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2022-11-01

**Source:** [Absolics Breaks Ground on Planned $600 Million Manufacturing Site in Georgia for Breakthrough Semiconductor Material](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/absolics-breaks-ground-on-planned-600-million-manufacturing-site-in-georgia-for-breakthrough-semiconductor-material-301665192.html)  ·  `page_000028`

## Facts

- Absolics, Inc., a subsidiary of SKC Co. Ltd., broke ground on Nov 1, 2022, on a planned $600 million manufacturing facility in Covington, Ga.
- The investment represents an increase from the $473 million originally announced in October 2021 due to the need for more advanced manufacturing equipment to meet anticipated demand.
- The facility will create more than 400 high-skilled jobs in Georgia.
- Absolics plans to build the facility in two phases at SKC's existing campus in Covington.
- Phase one is a $240 million investment expected to be completed by the end of 2023, creating 140 jobs and focusing on small-volume manufacturing.
- Mass production at the facility is expected to start in the second quarter of 2024.
- Phase two is an additional $360 million investment that will create another 270 jobs and ramp up to high-volume manufacturing, anticipated to be completed over the next three to five years.
- The facility will produce a glass substrate, a thin layer of glass approximately 3-by-3 inches used to package different semiconductor components together.
- Processing, memory, and logic chips can be mounted next to each other on the glass to create the core of a computing system.
- The new glass material replaces both silicon imposer and plastic substrate layers, simplifying manufacturing and reducing package thickness by almost half.
- Multi-layer ceramic capacitors (MLCC) can be embedded in the glass substrate, freeing more surface space for chips.
- Absolics' product allows larger processors and up to four times more chips to be mounted in the same space compared to standard packaging.
- The energy efficiency of the glass substrate reduces overall power consumption by up to 50 percent.
- Phase one is expected to have an annual production capacity of 12,000 square meters.
- Phase two will ramp up to an annual production capacity of 72,000 square meters.
- The glass substrate technology was originally developed by SKC and Absolics as part of a research consortium with the Georgia Institute of Technology.
- Absolics is expected to be the first company in the world to mass produce this material.
- Groundbreaking ceremony attendees included Senator Jon Ossoff and Georgia Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson.
- Dr. Woncheol Park, President and Chief Executive Officer of SKC, stated that Absolics will be an integral part of the U.S. semiconductor ecosystem and praised the factory's job creation impact on Covington and Georgia.
- Senator Jon Ossoff stated he is working to bring jobs and business to Georgia, noting the plant will create Georgia jobs, support U.S. national security, and bring advanced manufacturing to the state.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson highlighted the development as an opportunity to grow the semiconductor industry in Georgia and the U.S., linking it to planning for an increasingly electrified future.
- Absolics was established last year by SKC, which remains a majority owner of the company.
- SKC is an operating company of SK Group, South Korea's second-largest conglomerate with global companies in clean energy, semiconductors and life sciences.
- SKC was founded in 1976 and established a presence in Georgia in 1996 that grew into one of North America's largest manufacturers of polyester films.
- SKC's Semiconductor Material Business develops and manufactures semiconductors, specialty materials, chemical mechanical polishing pads and slurry, and copper foil for electric vehicle battery anodes.
- SK Group plans to increase its total investments and assets in the U.S. to more than $50 billion by the end of 2025, focusing on clean energy, semiconductors, and life sciences.
- Earlier this year, SK Siltron CSS opened a new facility in Michigan as part of a $300 million investment to increase manufacturing capacity for semiconductor wafers used in electric vehicle components.
- SK On is investing $2.6 billion in a site in Commerce, Ga., to manufacture batteries for electric vehicles.
- SK Group employs more than 100,000 people worldwide and has $139 billion in global annual revenue.
- SK companies are investing billions of dollars in expanding their U.S. presence with business operations or partnerships in hydrogen energy and fuel cells, EV battery manufacturing and technology, energy storage solutions, pharmaceutical manufacturing and development, semiconductors, and advanced materials.
