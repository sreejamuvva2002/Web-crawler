# Hühoco Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2017-01-26

**Source:** [Hühoco Group to build a new facility in Cartersville-Bartow County Company will invest $24 million in community | WBHF](https://wbhfradio.org/huhoco-group-to-build-a-new-facility-in-cartersville-bartow-county-company-will-invest-24-million-in-community/)  ·  `page_054798`

## Facts

- Hühoco Group announced plans to build a new greenfield facility in Cartersville, Bartow County, Georgia on January 26, 2017.
- The company will invest $24 million in the new facility and community.
- The project is expected to create 200 jobs total.
- Hühoco will add 100 new jobs to the new facility over the next three years.
- Hühoco currently operates a facility in Mableton, Georgia under the name First American Resources.
- The Mableton facility employs around 100 people.
- First American Resources specializes in decorative and functional coating systems.
- The company's two primary markets are domestic residential building products and automotive.
- Hühoco Group worked with Atlanta-based site selection firm Pegasus Partners for the project.
- The Cartersville-Bartow County Department of Economic Development partnered with Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) Director of International Investment Nico Wijnberg and Senior Project Manager Walt Farrell.
- Georgia Power was also a partner in the development process.
- Hühoco Group's product portfolio includes cold strips, paints for coating processes, colour concentrates for plastics, coil coating, lancing and stamping processes, and parts manufactured on multiple Bihler presses.
- The company has expanded its portfolio to include noise and damping materials (Anti-Noise Technology) for coated metal strips.
- Hühoco Group products are utilized in more than 30 industrial sectors globally, including automotive, human and veterinary medicine, and electronics.
- Cartersville Mayor Matt Santini welcomed First American Resources to the community and praised Georgia as a great place for business.
- Hühoco CEO Christoph Koslowski stated the company is renewing its commitment to Georgia by adding jobs and investing in an entirely new facility.
- Pegasus Partners' Benedikt Ibing emphasized that workforce priorities were central to the site selection process.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson noted that continued German investment in Georgia's manufacturing sector validates the state's international relationship efforts.
