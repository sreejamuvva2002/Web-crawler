# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-10-25

**Source:** [Company says Metaplant employees can expect annual pay of $58k - Bryan County News](https://www.bryancountynews.com/news/company-says-metaplant-employees-can-expect-annual-pay-58k/)  ·  `page_019854`

## Facts

- Not all of the 8,500 employees expected to work at Hyundai Motor Group’s Metaplant America will be working on the first day of production.
- Almost 6,000 additional jobs are projected for electric vehicle and battery manufacturing suppliers across Georgia.
- Area economic development officials expect at least a five-year ramp-up to reach full job creation numbers.
- Trip Tollison, president and CEO of the Savannah Economic Development Authority, stated it will be a five- to seven-year hire-up process.
- The plant is not expected to hire 14,000 people in one year.
- During a tour of the construction site in northern Bryan County on Oct. 25, 2023, HMG Metaplant CEO and President Oscar Kwon said the plant is on track to start production in early 2025.
- Hyundai Motor Company Global President and Chief Operating Officer Josè Muñoz added that production could start sooner than early 2025.
- The groundbreaking ceremony for the project took place on Oct. 25, 2022.
- The complex is called a “meta” plant because it will include several HMG-affiliated suppliers and another corporation’s battery factory alongside the vehicle assembly plant.
- Officials say the facility may produce electric vehicles under Hyundai, Kia, Genesis, and Ioniq labels.
- The Savannah Economic Development Authority, based in Chatham County, and development authorities in Bulloch, Bryan, and Effingham counties participate in the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority (JDA).
- The JDA, together with the state of Georgia, purchased and provided the site for the project.
- The incentive package is the largest in Georgia’s history and includes $2.1 billion worth of tax breaks.
- Eric Johnson, a retired architect and former state senator, serves as project director at the site for the JDA and the state.
- When first announced in May 2022, the HMG plant was predicted to create about 8,100 jobs through a $5.54 billion investment.
- The addition of an on-site battery plant in a joint venture with LG Energy Solution adds 400 expected jobs.
- The total expected investment for the project was boosted to $7.59 billion.
- Off-site suppliers represent a further $2.2 billion investment across nine different counties.
- The total investment from Hyundai Motor Group and its suppliers is approaching $10 billion.
- Parts suppliers are projected to employ about 6,000 workers.
- HMG states that the Metaplant and off-site suppliers together will create a total of 14,476 jobs at full production.
- Company representatives reported that 290 people have been hired for the HMG Metaplant so far.
- The current hires include 182 salaried and 108 hourly employees.
- Some hired workers are in training at other HMG plants, including Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Alabama in Montgomery and facilities in South Korea.
- The count of 290 hires does not include 53 South Korean nationals assigned to the plant, including CEO Oscar Kwon.
- Both HMG and its battery joint venture partner LG Energy Solution are headquartered in South Korea.
- Approximately 2,000 contractor employees are currently working to build the manufacturing complex, excluding them from job-creation figures.
- The average salary for production jobs, including those at the HMG plant, battery plant, and all affiliates, will be $58,105.
- This predicted average salary is 25% higher than the current average Bryan County annual wage of $47,000.
- Bulloch County’s comparable average salary is $45,032, based on a $21.65 average hourly wage from Department of Labor reports.
- The predicted Metaplant wage is 29% higher than Bulloch County’s average.
- As of the article's publication date, the Metaplant’s career job search page listed 95 job descriptions.
- A “production team member” job listing stated a salary of from $22.40 per hour.
- Benjy Thompson, CEO of the Development Authority of Bulloch County, agreed with the five- to seven-year timeline to achieve full job numbers.
- HMG supplier factories are being located in nine counties, including areas far from the four JDA counties and their immediate neighbors.
- Supplier announcements have been made in Henry County (McDonough) and Dublin.
- Three Hyundai suppliers are building plants in Bulloch County along U.S. Highway 301 south of Statesboro and near the I-16 interchange.
- Joon Georgia Inc. is predicted to create 630 jobs.
- Ecoplastic America Corporation is projected to create 456 jobs.
- Hanon Systems is projected to create 160 jobs.
