# Woory Industrial Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [GA: Woory to Open EV Parts Manufacturing, R&D Facility in Laurens County, Creating 130+ Jobs | Trade and Industry Development](https://www.tradeandindustrydev.com/region/georgia/news/ga-woory-open-ev-parts-manufacturing-rd-facility-31846)  ·  `page_015384`

## Facts

- Georgia Gov. Brian P. Kemp announced that Woory Industrial Co., Ltd., an automotive parts manufacturer, will establish a new manufacturing facility in Dublin, Georgia.
- The facility will create more than 130 new jobs.
- The project involves an estimated $18 million in investment in Dublin-Laurens County.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated that Woory Industrial is helping the state become the 'e-mobility capital of the nation.'
- Governor Brian P. Kemp credited Georgia's highly-skilled workforce and reliable infrastructure network for bringing historic levels of new jobs and investment to communities across the state.
- Woory Industrial Company, known as Woory, was established in 1989.
- Woory specializes in heating, ventilation, and air conditioning components and systems for the global automotive industry.
- Woory is concentrating on developing new products for electric vehicles (EV) and hydrogen-fueled cars.
- Jungwoo Kim, CEO of Woory Industrial Company, stated that the transition to electric vehicles represents a revolution in the automobile industry and that the U.S. EV market is key among all automotive markets.
- Jungwoo Kim noted that over the past 10 years, Woory has grown into a specialized EV component company through continuous innovation.
- Jungwoo Kim expressed confidence that Woory's experience and technology will contribute to community prosperity and appreciated the partnership of the State of Georgia and the City of Dublin.
- Woory's new facility is located at 404 Kellam Road in Dublin.
- The facility will be Woory's first U.S. manufacturing location.
- The facility will produce electrical heaters, control units, and actuators for EV and internal combustion carmakers in North America.
- Operations at the new facility are expected to begin in November 2023.
- Woory's pioneering development of electric heaters over the last 10 years has positioned the company as a leader in this growing market segment.
- Woory will be hiring personnel from all fields necessary for an automotive parts manufacturing plant.
- Dublin Mayor Joshua Kight stated that fostering quality and sustainable growth is a goal of the City of Dublin.
- Mayor Joshua Kight noted that Woory's commitment to repurposing an existing manufacturing building promotes infill development and creates new career paths for the community.
- Dr. Fred Williams, Chair of the Board of Directors of the Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority, welcomed Woory to the Dublin-Laurens County community.
- Dr. Fred Williams highlighted Dublin and Laurens County's rapidly growing automotive sector, citing its geographic location on Interstate 16 and access to a skilled workforce.
- Dr. Fred Williams stated that partnerships with area education systems and training providers will ensure training programs are in place to fill Woory's talent pipeline for years to come.
- Project Manager Jacob Lee represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The project is a partnership between Woory, the Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that creating jobs and opportunities in rural communities is a priority for matching competitive projects with prepared sites.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson congratulated Dublin-Laurens County on bringing e-mobility jobs to middle Georgia.
- For over a century, Georgia has fostered industry practices, partnership, and innovation, positioning the state as a leader in emerging technologies for the automotive and mobility industry.
- From July 1 through December 31, 2022, the automotive industry was the second-largest source of new jobs and investment in Georgia.
- Since 2020, Georgia has pursued job creation opportunities along the entire EV supply chain, resulting in over $22.7 billion in investment and more than 28,400 jobs created.
