# Fox Factory Holding Corp.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-02-26

**Source:** [Fox Factory's Reckoning: Huge Loss Triggers Aggressive Turnaround Plan - BriefGlance.com](https://briefglance.com/articles/fox-factorys-reckoning-huge-loss-triggers-aggressive-turnaround-plan)  ·  `page_020182`

## Facts

- Fox Factory Holding Corp. is headquartered in Duluth, GA.
- The company reported a net loss of $544.6 million for fiscal 2025.
- The net loss was driven by a $557.3 million non-cash goodwill impairment charge.
- $295.2 million of the goodwill impairment charge occurred in the fourth quarter alone.
- Adverse changes to U.S. tariff policies and a sustained decline in the company’s stock price triggered the goodwill write-down.
- The non-deductible nature of the impairment negatively impacted the company's effective tax rate.
- Full-year net sales increased by 5.3% to $1.467 billion, compared to a $6.6 million net income in the prior year.
- CEO Mike Dennison cited persistent cyclical and tariff headwinds affecting the business.
- The Specialty Sports Group (SSG) includes high-performance bicycle components and the Marucci baseball brand.
- The bicycle industry faces ongoing tariff pressures and high inventory levels squeezing OEM partners.
- Marucci's profitability was impacted by tariffs and strategic investments aimed at future growth.
- The Powered Vehicles Group (PVG) saw nearly flat sales, with Marzocchi acquisition contributions offsetting a slowdown in demand for automotive original equipment (OE) products.
- High interest rates and elevated vehicle costs have dampened consumer appetite and created inventory challenges for dealers in the powersports sector.
- The Aftermarket Applications Group (AAG) grew sales by 12.5% in the fourth quarter, driven by strong demand for upfitting product lines and aftermarket suspension products.
- Fox Factory is launching Phase 2 of its turnaround plan, targeting an additional $40 million in savings for fiscal 2026.
- The company aims for a total realized savings of approximately $50 million for the year, building on $25 million saved from Phase 1 in 2025.
- Cost-cutting initiatives target business line rationalization, supply chain and materials cost productivity, and operating expense reductions.
- The Board of Directors is reviewing strategic alternatives for Marucci and other non-core assets to shed unprofitable business lines.
- Fox Factory plans to divest its Phoenix, Arizona-based AAG operations, including the Upfit UTV, Geiser, and Shock Therapy businesses.
- The company expects to complete the Phoenix operations sale by the end of the first quarter of 2026.
- All proceeds from the asset sales will be dedicated to paying down $673.5 million in total debt.
- Fox Factory's stock declined in after-hours trading following the announcement.
- Q4 revenue of $361.1 million slightly beat analyst expectations, but adjusted earnings per share of $0.20 fell short of consensus estimates.
- The company projects fiscal 2026 net sales between $1.328 billion and $1.416 billion, reflecting divestitures and a subdued market outlook.
- Fox Factory expects a 200 basis point improvement in its adjusted EBITDA margin for fiscal 2026.
