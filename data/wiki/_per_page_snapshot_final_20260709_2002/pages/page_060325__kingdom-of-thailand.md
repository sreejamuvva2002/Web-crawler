# Kingdom of Thailand

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-07

**Source:** [Thailand Attracts $4.1 Billion in EV Supply Chain Investments Across 198 Projects - Responsibleus](https://responsibleus.com/thailand-attracts-41-billion-in-ev-supply-chain-investments-across-198-projects)  ·  `page_060325`

## Facts

- Thailand has secured more than $4.1 billion in electric vehicle supply chain investments across 198 projects.
- The investments expand battery production, charging infrastructure, and vehicle manufacturing.
- The investments strengthen Thailand's position as Southeast Asia's automotive hub.
- According to the Board of Investment (BOI), Thailand has gained over $4.1 billion (137 billion baht) worth of investments from its EV supply chain.
- The 198 approved projects encompass vehicle manufacturing, batteries, charging stations, and essential parts.
- The investments occur as global car manufacturers continue to produce more in Southeast Asia by diversifying supply chains and adding electric vehicle manufacturing capacity.
- Narit Therdsteerasukdi, Secretary-General of the BOI, stated that Thailand’s EV strategy caters to hybrid electric vehicles (HEVs), plug-in hybrid electric vehicles (PHEVs), and battery electric vehicles (BEVs).
- Thailand’s EV strategy does not focus on a single technology type.
- At the International Electric Vehicle Technology Conference (iEVTech) in Bangkok, Narit stated the strategy is designed to benefit both incumbent auto brands and new entrants while developing Thailand’s automotive supply chain.
- BEV-related projects total about $1.18 billion (39.5 billion baht) and include 18 projects.
- The projected annual production capacity for BEV projects is over 370,000 units.
- Investments in HEV and PHEV projects total about $1.18 billion (39.3 billion baht) across 14 projects.
- Battery and energy storage system manufacturing accounts for $1 billion (33.5 billion baht) through 57 projects.
- Another $373 million (12.5 billion baht) has been committed to 49 projects producing components such as drive motors, battery management systems, and power control units.
- Charging infrastructure projects represent $292 million (9.8 billion baht) across 42 projects.
- These charging projects support the rollout of more than 22,900 charging stations, including over 10,000 DC fast chargers.
- Mercedes-Benz started local battery electric vehicle production in Thailand in 2022.
- BYD, Great Wall Motor, SAIC Motor, and Aion opened assembly operations in Thailand in 2024.
- Changan Auto and EV Primus began production in Thailand in 2025.
- Hyundai Mobility and Omoda & Jaecoo are scheduled to begin production in Thailand in 2026.
- The BOI stated that these manufacturing projects have created more than 16,000 jobs.
- The BOI has organized 18 supplier matchmaking sessions bringing together multinationals and Thailand's component suppliers.
- Over 800 local suppliers have attended the matchmaking events.
- The events resulted in over 1,200 deals.
- According to the BOI, the collaboration will lead to a procurement value of approximately $1.79 billion (60 billion baht).
- Thailand created the BOI in 1966 to encourage investments and develop its industries.
