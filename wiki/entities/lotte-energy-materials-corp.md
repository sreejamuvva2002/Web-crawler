# Lotte Energy Materials Corp.

**Type:** Company  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[battery-materials|Battery Materials]]  ·  **Location:** Georgia, United States

## Overview

South Korean battery materials maker Lotte Energy Materials Corp. plans to build a copper foil plant in the United States to supply electric-vehicle makers, with Georgia among the states under consideration.

## EV supply-chain relevance

The planned copper foil plant will produce elecfoil used as anode and cathode collector in lithium-ion batteries for EVs, addressing growing North American demand.

## Key facts

- Lotte Energy Materials is one of South Korea’s leading battery materials makers.
- The company plans a U.S. copper foil plant to meet growing demand from North American EV manufacturers.
- Potential sites include Michigan, Kentucky, Tennessee, and Georgia where U.S. and Korean EV makers operate.
- Initial investment is projected at 700-800 billion won ($526-$601 million).
- The project is a response to the US Inflation Reduction Act, which favors domestic EV and battery production.
- Copper foil (elecfoil) is essential for lithium-ion batteries, serving as anode and cathode collector.
- SNE Research forecasts U.S. high-end copper foil market growth from 10,000 tons in 2022 to 100,000 tons by 2026.
- Lotte currently operates factories in Korea (20,000 tons) and Malaysia (40,000 tons), with plans to raise Malaysian capacity to 60,000 tons.
- A 30,000-ton plant is also being built in Spain with a 560 billion won investment.
- Clients include Chinese EV maker BYD, Korea’s LG Energy Solution Ltd., and Samsung SDI Co.
- The U.S. plant will initially produce 30,000 tons of copper foil, focusing on higher-value 6-micrometer products.
- "The US market is critical to our global expansion plan," said Lotte Energy CEO Kim.
