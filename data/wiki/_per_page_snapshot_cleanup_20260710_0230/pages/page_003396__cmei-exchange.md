## Facts

- TechnipFMC is a leading technology provider to the traditional and new energies industry delivering fully integrated projects, products, and services.
- The company operates within three New Energy pillars: Offshore Hydrogen, Green House Gas removal, and Floating Renewables.
- TechnipFMC generates hydrogen offshore by electrolyzing seawater using renewable power.
- The company captures surplus renewable energy as hydrogen for later electricity production to provide stable zero-emission power.
- TechnipFMC provides offshore integration abilities in production systems and umbilicals, risers, and flowlines to store hydrogen subsea and transport it ashore.
- Contributions to greenhouse gas removal include carbon transportation and storage (CTS) leveraging existing equipment and integration expertise.
- TechnipFMC offers Dynamic Inter Array Cable Systems for transferring power from floating offshore wind farms to the onshore grid.
- The company has experience delivering large-scale industrialized subsea projects and manufacturing capacity transferable from the oil and gas industry.
- PC&S Company LLC is interested in partnering with DOE Labs for feedstocks for sustainable aviation fuel (SAF), marine, railway, and land transportation.
- Feedstock options include lignin, bamboo tree, jatropha, camilina, and coconut oil.
- The company notes an abundant supply of raw materials to produce lignin by volume for jet fuel production.
- Bamboo trees can grow in the USA and be used to produce methanol and lignin.
- There is a big market for methanol supply from shipping cargo companies in Europe that currently buy from China.
- Shipping cargo engines are powered by M.A.N. shipping engines running on 100% methanol.
- Polykala Technologies has worked on critical materials (including rare earth metals) recovery and production from scraps and post-consumer products for over 3 years.
- The company uses a pilot plant scale hydrogen processing facility for this work.
- It also processes critical minerals recovery with a custom-made plasma facility at a current scale of 50 kg/day.
- Other business areas include catalyst and membrane manufacturing.
- Hydroplane Ltd. develops and tests low temperature PEM fuel cell membrane electric assemblies.
- The company develops and tests low temperature PEM fuel cell technology from component to stack level.
- It develops and tests fuel cell balance of plant technology including thermal management, power distribution, and software control.
- Hydroplane develops electric propulsion powered by fuel cell technology for aviation, maritime, and ground vehicles.
- The company performs aircraft system integration and test with electric propulsion.
- It conducts fuel cell stack system integration and test including up to 200 kW load bank and humidification systems.
- Hydroplane designs and tests low temperature PEM thermal management.
- The company designs, fabricates, and tests fuel cell gaskets.
- It performs endurance testing of H2 fuel cell stacks.
- Hydroplane designs liquid hydrogen tanks and fuel delivery systems.
- Brookhaven National Laboratory's Interdisciplinary Science Department performs research to help meet the Nation's exponentially growing energy needs.
- The Energy Systems Division conducts research for electric grid modernization, renewable energy system deployment, energy system efficiency and emissions, geothermal energy system materials, and advanced manufacturing methods.
- Energy Conversion research offers advanced technical solutions to reduce fossil fuel use in residential and commercial building energy applications, focusing on biofuel end use, combustion efficiency and emissions, and system concepts.
- Geothermal Materials research focuses on developing new materials for geothermal well applications to improve system performance.
- Renewables and Grid Modernization work supports development of solutions for deployment, grid integration, and operation of renewable energy technologies in the US.
- The Energy Storage Division conducts leading-edge research into promising energy storage technologies for consumer electronics, vehicle technologies, and grid-level storage.
- The lab has dry room facilities to assemble pouch cells up to 2Ah.
- It has expertise in mechanistic characterization of electrochemical energy storage processes from material to systems level.
- Vehicle Technologies research includes study and preparation of novel materials and electrodes for high energy, high power, and safe batteries capable of fast charge and function in extreme environments.
- Grid-level Storage research focuses on developing electrochemical energy storage systems based on sustainable materials for safe, long-life batteries.
- Shorepower Technologies provides expertise in Electric Vehicle Service Equipment design, charging equipment options, installation at community and interstate site-host locations, battery-energy-storage for grid balancing, engineering, project management, procurement, networked connectivity, customer payment options, 24/7 public access operations, and utilization/environmental benefits reporting.
- The company's existing Truck Stop Electrification sites already have infrastructure available to tie-in EV charging equipment.
- Shorepower saves on project time and costs, allowing for more EV spaces and L2 and/or DC-Fast charging equipment in project budgets.
- As a minority-owned business enterprise (MBE), Shorepower helps organizations meet diversity goals.
- The company has support from the DOT-DOE Joint Office/ MBDA agency to advance public-private partnerships.
- DRG is a full-service engineering and research firm focused on the electric utility and infrastructure sector.
- Areas of expertise include transmission and distribution planning, grid automation, reliability, and resiliency.
- Specific capabilities include Electric Power System research, Electric Distribution Engineering Design, Electrical Substation Design, Electric Grid Automation, advanced sensing and fault detection for Electric Power systems, situational awareness for Electric Power systems, HPC high-performance computing Electric Power supply, electrical protection and controls, AI and Machine Learning for Electric Power systems, renewable generation (pv, wind, hydro, nuclear, energy storage, EV charging stations), and Electrical Power Quality and Monitoring.
- ORNL hosts the Battery Manufacturing Facility (BMF).
- The BMF analyzes every aspect of battery cell development from raw materials and electrode dispersion preparation to finished product and performance testing.
- The facility houses equipment for researching advanced materials, electrolyte development, electrode formulation chemistry, slurry rheology, innovative coating technology, and high-performance electrode architectures.
- Resources include three coating lines, a 1,400 ft2 dry room, and a cell assembly line.
- The BMF can produce pouch cells up to 66 × 99 × 12 mm and 6 Ah capacity.
- The facility houses state-of-the-art characterization capabilities including XPS, XRD, SEM, GC-MS, and electrochemical performance testing with several Maccor and Biologic cyclers.
- The University of Maine's Center for Research on Sustainable Forests and Forest Policy and Economics Lab researches integrated approaches to forest resource issues.
- The lab conducts economic analysis of climate change mitigation and adaptation.
- It has developed forest biomass and carbon models of varying spatial and temporal scales.
- Models investigate estimating global timber supply and carbon stocks under different socioeconomic and policy conditions.
- Research includes understanding how landowners prioritize forest management for ecosystem services like carbon and biomass.
- The lab is interested in partnering with DOE labs to integrate remote sensing and economic modeling tools to improve quantifying forest carbon stocks and fluxes throughout the US.
- It aims to advance forest economic models to identify, assess, and address economic drivers in forest management regarding carbon impact.
- Models can estimate forest carbon, timber market, and ecosystem impacts at stand, regional, national, and international scales.
- Recent projects evaluated the impact of forest carbon offsets on timber supply, biomass energy, and wood product markets.
- The lab has conducted analyses on forest carbon market concerns about leakage, additionality, and permanence.
- The Agile BioFoundry (ABF) is a consortium of seven U.S. DOE national laboratories operating as a distributed biofoundry with industry and academia.
- It is funded by DOE’s Bioenergy Technologies Office (BETO).
- ABF work supports BETO’s goals to develop sustainable aviation fuels and decarbonize energy-intensive industries.
- The consortium has developed an extensive design-build-test-learn platform with scale-up, techno-economic and life-cycle analyses, and onboarding capabilities.
- Goals include actively developing funds-in and BETO-funded projects for technology transfer to industry.
- ABF aims to develop engineering biology, computational, and bioprocess tools to advance strain performance for sustainable fuel and chemical production.
- It develops innovative pathways, strains, and processes for sustainable aviation fuels.
- It develops innovative pathways, strains, and processes for direct replacement and performance-advantaged chemicals.
- SLAC focuses on reducing carbon emissions in industrial processes like steelmaking, cement, and chemical production.
- The lab develops, understands, and integrates materials and devices for electrocatalysis and battery technologies.
- It constructs a toolbox of innovative techniques including synchrotron X-ray spectroscopy and imaging to understand sustainable manufacturing, electrocatalysis, and battery technologies.

- The organization is rapidly electrifying and demonstrating vehicle to grid (V2G) and vehicle to building (V2B) technology.
- As a non-profit, it is organized to innovate for its industry and supports demonstrations of new technology, provides operator-level insights, and helps introduce new technologies.
- EVenergi streamlines the transition to zero-emission vehicles with intelligent software solutions for planning and managing fleets.
- The company developed and markets the BetterFleet platform in the United States, Europe, and Asia Pacific.
- EVenergi's vision is to accelerate the transition to zero emission fleets efficiently from financial, operational, and environmental perspectives.
- Founded in 2016, the company serves over 200 complex fleets across North America, Europe, Singapore, and Australia.
- Using real-world data and road-tested algorithms, BetterFleet simplifies planning through automation in charger and vehicle selection, utility infrastructure sizing, and operational planning optimization.
- The platform manages live charging and operations to meet financial and sustainability goals without disrupting core duties or impacting the electricity grid.
- BetterFleet uses "digital twin" and machine learning technologies to simulate fleet operations accurately and optimize performance.

- It is interested in combining power conversion with advanced control and optimization methods to achieve high energy efficiency, durability, and reliability of energy storage systems.
