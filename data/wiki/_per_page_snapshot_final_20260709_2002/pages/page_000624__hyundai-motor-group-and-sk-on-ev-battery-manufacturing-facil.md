# Hyundai Motor Group and SK On EV Battery Manufacturing Facility

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Governor Announces Plans For EV Battery Manufacturing Facility In Bartow County | AM 1180 Radio](https://chattooga1180.com/governor-announces-plans-for-ev-battery-manufacturing-facility-in-bartow-county/)  ·  `page_000624`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Hyundai Motor Group (HMG) and SK On have selected a site in Bartow County for a new electric vehicle (EV) battery manufacturing facility that will supply Hyundai Motor Group’s plants in the U.S.
- Stakeholders estimate the project will create more than 3,500 new jobs through approximately $4-5 billion of investment in Bartow County.
- Hyundai Motor Group and SK On recently signed a memorandum of understanding (MOU) regarding the partnership for a new EV battery facility in the U.S., with details of the partnership still in development.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Hyundai Motor Group and SK On are valued partners and key players in Georgia’s ever-growing automotive industry, and his administration has focused on bringing jobs to overlooked communities while sharing their goal to invest further in the No. 1 state for business.
- SK On was established in 2021 as the lithium-ion battery subsidiary branch of SK Innovation.
- SK On currently employs more than 2,000 Georgians at its SK Battery America facility in Commerce.
- In July, SK Battery America announced it would partner with the Work for Warriors Georgia program to hire veterans, servicemembers, and their families at its Georgia operations.
- The new facility is subject to execution of relevant agreements and Hyundai Motor Group and SK On’s final board decisions.
- The new facility will be located at Bartow Centre, a zoned manufacturing and industrial site located on Highway 411.
- The project is aiming to begin operations in 2025.
- Bartow County Sole Commissioner Steve Taylor expressed pleasure with the decision of Hyundai Motor Group and SK On and thanked the owners of the former Bartow Centre Industrial property for pursuing this extraordinary economic development project together.
- Commissioner Taylor stated the project will have regional benefit for job seekers, establish Bartow in Georgia’s EV ecosystem significantly, and thrive due to Bartow’s strong history and talent base built upon advanced manufacturing, the automotive industry, and innovation.
- Director of Korean Investment Yoonie Kim represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The GDEcD partnered with Georgia EMC, Development Authority of Bartow County, the Cartersville-Bartow County Department of Economic Development, and Georgia Quick Start on the project.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated Hyundai Motor Group and SK On have been pioneering partners for Georgia for decades as major drivers for Georgia’s automotive renaissance and the first Korean manufacturer to locate in the state, respectively.
- Commissioner Wilson noted that supporting cooperation across Georgia’s growing EV ecosystem creates a fully integrated supply chain for automotive OEMs while connecting battery manufacturers with recyclers to close the loop on battery manufacturing.
- Commissioner Wilson expressed excitement for the jobs of the future this will create for Bartow County and northwest Georgia, and gratitude for community and utility partner support.
- Georgia’s prime location, major ports system, extensive infrastructure, skilled workforce, and pro-business climate have made it an attractive location for diverse rapidly developing industries focused on creating a sustainable future.
- Building on assets that make the automotive industry successful, Georgia is positioned as a hub for the electric transportation industry.
- Excluding this announcement, EV-related projects announced in the state since 2020 total approximately $17 billion in investment and more than 22,800 new jobs in Georgia.
- Hyundai Motor Group separately announced in May its plans to invest $5.54 billion in Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America, an EV manufacturing facility in Bryan County.
- Hyundai Motor Group broke ground on the Metaplant America facility in October with Governor Kemp, other state leaders, and local and federal officials.
