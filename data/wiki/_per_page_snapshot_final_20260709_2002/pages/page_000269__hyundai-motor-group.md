# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-04-27

**Source:** [Hyundai taps Texas for solar power 'credit' at Georgia EV facility](https://www.savannahnow.com/story/news/environment/2024/04/27/hyundai-taps-texas-for-solar-power-credit-at-georgia-ev-facility/73473599007/)  ·  `page_000269`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group plans to support sustainable manufacturing at its $7.6 billion Georgia electric-vehicle plant and related manufacturing facilities by buying enough solar power annually to electrify 31,000 typical American homes for a year.
- The South Korean automaker announced a deal with Spanish energy developer Matrix Renewables.
- Hyundai committed to purchasing 378 gigawatt hours of electricity annually through the agreement.
- Maggie Shober, research director at the Southern Alliance for Clean Energy, explained that Texas operates on a self-contained energy grid while Georgia is part of the Eastern Interconnection, so the power will not physically flow from the Stillhouse Solar Project in Bell County, Texas, to Hyundai's EV factory in Bryan County, Georgia.
- Hyundai will accumulate renewable energy credits for its share of the electricity over a 15-year agreement via a virtual power purchase agreement.
- Under the arrangement, Hyundai will sell the generated electricity to a utility or other provider on the Texas grid.
- Company spokeswoman Dain Kang confirmed the details of the virtual power purchase agreement.
- Shober noted that buying power from another state illustrates a hierarchy in corporate energy goals, adding that the best scenario for offsetting load is on-site or within the same service territory and state.
- Shober stated that Hyundai must ensure its outside energy purchases do not make Georgia Power's generation mix dirtier.
- Shober observed that Hyundai did not consider potential increases in emissions from Georgia Power due to its load when announcing that the project would decrease emissions.
- Georgia Power is responding to projected increased electricity demand from a flood of new manufacturing facilities, including Hyundai, and energy-intensive data centers by building three new gas-fired power plants recently approved by the state Public Services Commission.
- The combined capacity of the three new gas-fired power plant units will be as much as 1,400 megawatts.
- New federal emissions guidelines require new gas-fired power plants to capture 90% of carbon emissions, but utilities are not required to fully employ the technology until 2032.
- Hyundai's share of the Stillhouse Solar Project will prevent an estimated 140 tons of heat-trapping carbon dioxide from entering the atmosphere in Texas annually.
- The prevented emissions are equivalent to what 84,000 typical American cars release into the atmosphere in one year.
- Codi Norred, executive director of Georgia Interfaith Power and Light, stated that buying clean energy from across the country is backward and inequitable, allowing Georgia Power to burn more fossil fuels while Hyundai calls its energy clean.
- Hyundai has pledged to reach net-zero in climate-impacting pollution by 2045.
- The Texas solar deal allows Hyundai to shrink its emissions footprint without impacting its actual energy mix, which is significant because 60% of Georgia's energy is still produced by burning fossil fuels.
- Critics argue that Georgia should have demanded large-scale in-state solar investment in exchange for providing Hyundai with the state's largest-ever incentive package.
- Jen Hilburn, north coast advocate for One Hundred Miles, stated that Georgia gave Hyundai $2 billion in tax abatements rather than asking them to invest in community infrastructure.
- Hyundai is installing a solar array on a rooftop over a covered parking lot on its campus, but the panels will supply only a fraction of the plant's electricity.
- Heavy energy users including Walmart and Google asked the Public Service Commission to reject Georgia Power's request for more natural gas capacity, as did the U.S. Department of Defense.
- Of Georgia Power's four rate hikes since the beginning of last year, the largest led to a nearly $16 spike in the average residential customer's monthly bill, which was tied to soaring fuel prices in 2022.
- Two additional rate increases have been approved for the next eight months, which will add $44 a month to the average residential bill.
- Spain-based Matrix Renewables purchased the Texas solar project from developer OCI Solar Power.
- Hyundai will purchase 70% of the Stillhouse Solar Project's capacity.
