# Suzuki Manufacturing of America Corporation (SMAC)

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-03-05

**Source:** [[roadracingworld.com] - Suzuki Manufacturing Of America Becomes Subsidiary Of Suzuki  - ninjette.org](https://www.ninjette.org/forums/showthread.php?t=408862)  ·  `page_010841`

## Facts

- Suzuki Manufacturing of America Corporation (SMAC) has become a 100% subsidiary of Suzuki Motor USA, LLC (SMO) effective March 1, 2024.
- SMAC is based in Rome, Georgia and was founded in 2001.
- SMAC serves as Suzuki’s All-Terrain Vehicle (ATV) manufacturing facility.
- For over two decades, all Suzuki adult KingQuad ATVs have been manufactured at the Rome, Georgia location.
- Suzuki ATVs are sold in the United States, Canada, and nearly 25 countries worldwide.
- Prior to the change, SMAC stock was owned by Suzuki Motor Corporation (SMC) (20%) and Suzuki Motor of America, Inc. (SMAI) (80%).
- SMAI is owned by SMC and acts as the holding company for Suzuki companies operating in the United States.
- The organizational change consolidates ATV sales and production arms to strengthen the overall ATV business in the United States and globally.
- A key objective of the move is strengthening the supply chain and purchasing policies for locally sourced parts.
- SMO and SMAC leaders expect improved responsiveness to the ATV market by more accurately linking production to sales demand.
- SMO plans to improve inventory management for both the company and its dealer body to protect sales margins, flooring costs, and profitability.
- The restructuring will create better, more direct communication between SMO and SMAC, fostering a collaborative environment between Rome- and Brea-based teams.
- Masami Haga, President of SMO, stated that expanding commitment to the growth of the entire powersports business is a primary goal of the change.
- The move sets the stage for new Suzuki ATV offerings and enhancements to existing products in the future.
- Suzuki’s United States distribution headquarters are located in Brea, California, where the company relocated in 1981 after first entering the U.S. market in 1963.
- Suzuki is credited with introducing the first four-wheel ATV in 1982 with the LT125 model.
- ATV industry sales are generally steady throughout the calendar year with demand typically increasing in the fall months.
