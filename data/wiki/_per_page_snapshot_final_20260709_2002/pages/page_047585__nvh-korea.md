# NVH Korea

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-06-22

**Source:** [EV Parts Supplier NVH Korea to Create Over 160 Jobs, Invest $72M in Henry County • The Georgia Virtue](https://www.thegeorgiavirtue.com/georgia-news/ev-parts-supplier-nvh-korea-to-create-over-160-jobs-invest-72m-in-henry-county/)  ·  `page_047585`

## Facts

- Governor Brian Kemp announced that automotive parts manufacturer NVH Korea will establish a new facility in Locust Grove during an economic development trip to the Paris Air Show.
- The project will create over 160 new jobs.
- The project will bring $72 million in investment to Henry County.
- This is the second economic development project Governor Kemp has announced that week while promoting Georgia to international job creators abroad.
- NVH Korea is an automotive parts manufacturer based in the City of Ulsan and was established in 1984.
- The company specializes in automotive noise, vibration, and heat-control parts.
- The company currently produces these parts in Korea, Europe, and India.
- The company already operates a manufacturing facility in Georgia that produces floor mats, cargo mats, and cargo trays.
- The new facility will supply battery parts for electric vehicles (EVs) in the United States.
- Customers for the new facility include Hyundai Mobis, Kia Georgia, and Hyundai Motor Group.
- Ja Kyum Koo is the Chairman and CEO of NVH Korea.
- The State of Georgia will serve as the company’s first EV battery component manufacturing site.
- Cooperation between state agencies and company staff helped guide the project.
- This marks the first step in the company's effort to contribute to the electrification of the mobility industry.
- NVH Korea’s new facility will be located at Gardner Logistics Park on Colvin Drive in Locust Grove.
- The facility will produce EV battery system components that protect, connect, and sense the performance of battery cells.
- Operations are expected to begin in the second quarter of 2024.
- Robert Price is the Mayor of the City of Locust Grove.
- Carlotta Harrell is the Chair of the Henry County Board of Commissioners.
- Jacob Lee is the Project Manager representing the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The project was executed in partnership with the Henry County Development Authority, Georgia EMC, and Georgia Quick Start.
- Pat Wilson is the GDEcD Commissioner.
- From July 1 through December 31, 2022, the automotive industry was the second-largest source of new jobs and investment in Georgia.
- Since 2020, Georgia has pursued job creation along the entire EV supply chain, resulting in more than $22.7 billion in investments and the creation of over 28,400 jobs.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Georgia's reliable infrastructure network and great workforce allow job creators to find success in every community.
- Governor Brian Kemp cited the incredible growth of Georgia's e-mobility and battery industries as a testament to the state's ability to support large suppliers like NVH Korea.
- Ja Kyum Koo stated that choosing Georgia was the first step in contributing to the electrification of the mobility industry and expressed excitement about joining the Locust Grove and Henry County community.
- Mayor Robert Price stated that NVH's investment will increase the manufacturing jobs base and integrate the city into Georgia’s electric vehicle ecosystem.
- Carlotta Harrell stated that the announcement will add quality manufacturing jobs for citizens and continue to diversify the local economy.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that an economic transformation is happening across Georgia as manufacturers create future jobs aligned with cutting-edge technologies, and welcomed innovators establishing their first U.S. or e-mobility presence in the state.
