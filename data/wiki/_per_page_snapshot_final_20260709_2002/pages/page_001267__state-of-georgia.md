# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2022-06-30

**Source:** [Scoring Goals While Going Green - Atlanta Magazine](https://www.atlantamagazine.com/article/scoring-goals-while-going-green/)  ·  `page_001267`

## Facts

- Georgia announced a $5 billion Rivian electric truck plant in December.
- Hyundai Motor Group unveiled a $5.5 billion electric vehicle and battery facility in May.
- Korean solar energy company Qcells announced a $171 million expansion of its photovoltaic module manufacturing operation in Dalton.
- Construction on the Qcells expansion is due to start in the fall, bringing 470 jobs to Whitfield County when the plant opens in mid-2023.
- Qcells currently employs 750 workers at a nearby plant it opened in 2019.
- Together, Qcells' Dalton facilities will represent one-third of the solar module production capacity in the United States.
- Qcells CEO Justin Lee stated that 'Georgia has become the clean energy manufacturing heart of America.'
- German metal supplier Aurubis broke ground on June 17 on a $320 million recycling facility in Augusta (Richmond County).
- The Aurubis Richmond facility will be the nation’s largest metal recycling facility and feature the first large secondary smelter for multimetal recycling.
- When fully operational in mid-2024, the Aurubis plant will extract copper, tin, nickel, and other metals from 90,000 tons of materials such as circuit boards each year.
- The Aurubis facility is expected to bring 120 jobs to Richmond County.
- Georgia unveiled the 1,113-acre Middle Georgia Megasite near Fort Valley on June 16 as a partnership between the state and the Development Authority of Peach County.
- The megasite project was funded by proceeds from selling a Chatham County site to Amazon last year.
- Georgia Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that the joint effort will create opportunities for the entire region and that projects attracted to megasites create a ripple effect of growth that crosses county lines.
- Governor Brian Kemp announced on June 13 that Georgia maintained its top-level AAA bond rating from FitchRatings, Moody’s Investors Service, and S&P Global Ratings.
- Georgia used its AAA rating to sell general obligation bonds due July 7 to finance $754 million in capital projects, including economic development.
