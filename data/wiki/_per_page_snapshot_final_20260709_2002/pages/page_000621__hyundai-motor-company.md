# Hyundai Motor Company

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-09-26

**Source:** [Hyundai's massive new Georgia EV plant adds its 18th supplier](https://electrek.co/2024/09/26/hyundais-massive-new-georgia-ev-plant-adds-18th-supplier/)  ·  `page_000621`

## Facts

- Hyundai’s $7.6 billion EV plant in Georgia gained its 18th supplier as the automaker builds an extensive US supply chain.
- The plant is slated to open as early as October and will produce US-made Hyundai electric vehicles, starting with the updated 2025 IONIQ 5.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp announced that Shinsung Petrochemical will invest $11.2 million in a new manufacturing facility in Toombs County.
- Shinsung is a leading auto sealant company and will be a key supplier for Hyundai’s new Metaplant in Bryan County.
- Governor Kemp stated, “We are excited that Shinsung is joining that growing network of suppliers locating in rural communities.”
- The Shinsung facility is scheduled to open in 2025 and will be the first in Toombs County, creating over 30 new jobs.
- Shinsung is the 18th supplier to invest in Hyundai’s new Georgia EV plant.
- Hyundai is investing $7.6 billion to develop the facility, directly creating 8,500 jobs in the state.
- Another $5 billion EV battery plant with SK On is being built in Bartow County, GA, creating an additional 3,500 positions.
- Since 2018, Georgia has attracted over $28 billion in investments to build electric vehicles, batteries, and other clean energy initiatives.
- The funding has already attracted over 36,000 new jobs in the state.
- According to a recent Center for Automotive Research study, Hyundai’s plant will create more than 58,200 new jobs while attracting over $12.6 billion in investments in Georgia.
- The first model to roll off the assembly line will be the updated 2025 IONIQ 5, which will have more range, a sleek new design, and a Tesla NACS charging port.
- Hyundai will also launch its first three-row electric SUV, the IONIQ 9, which will also be built at the facility.
- Initially, vehicles built at the facility will only be eligible for a partial $3,750 tax credit.
- Hyundai expects its US-made EVs to qualify for the full $7,500 once the battery unit comes online.
- Hyundai’s 2025 IONIQ 5 gained a new XRT variant designed for off-road use with “XRT-exclusive” front and rear bumpers, side skirts, and other aggressive design elements.
- The AWD Limited SE/SEL XRT trim has a targeted new range of +250 to +280 miles.
- The RWD SE/SEL/Limited Standard Range trims have targeted new ranges of +310 miles and +240 miles, respectively.
- Hyundai topped Ford and GM in US electric vehicle sales in the second quarter.
- According to Motor Intelligence, Hyundai and Kia accounted for 10% of US electric vehicle sales in Q2, outpacing Ford (7.4%) and GM (6.3%).
- With US production and advanced new EVs on the way, Hyundai wants an even bigger share of the market.
