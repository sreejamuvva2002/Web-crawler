# State of Georgia Logistics Network

**Type:** unknown  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Logistics in Georgia | Georgia](https://georgia.org/center-of-innovation/logistics-1/georgia-center-innovation-host-2022-logistics-summit)  ·  `page_002201`

## Facts

- Georgia is a premier distribution and logistics hub in the heart of the Southeast.
- Industry leaders including Amazon, Cargill, Hapag-Lloyd, Hyundai Motor Group, SAIA, and UPS have been attracted to Georgia’s logistics strengths.
- In 2025, the state’s exports reached 213 countries and territories, with total trade hitting $210.7 billion.
- Ongoing infrastructure expansions include increased warehouse space and extensive cold storage development for perishable goods and agricultural sectors.
- Georgia ranks nationally for efficient movement of goods by Area Development in 2025.
- The Port of Savannah is the fastest-growing single-terminal port in the nation, according to Georgia Ports Authority.
- More than 80% of the U.S. market is within a two-hour flight or two-day truck drive from Georgia.
- The Port of Savannah ranks among the fastest-growing deepwater container ports in the U.S., with extensive on-terminal rail access and strong capacity for refrigerated containers.
- The Port of Brunswick is the No. 1 port for automotive and heavy machinery Ro/Ro.
- Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport, the world’s busiest, also operates as a major air-cargo hub.
- The airport features cargo complexes exceeding 2 million square feet.
- The airport features the Southeast’s only USDA-approved on-terminal perishable-goods facility.
- The airport handles over 650,000 metric tons of cargo annually.
- Georgia's rail network is the largest in the Southeast, backed by Class I providers CSX and Norfolk Southern.
- Georgia's highway network includes 1,200 miles of interstate highways and 20,000 miles of federal and state roads.
- Half of the world’s top 100 third-party logistics (3PL) providers operate in Georgia.
- Georgia’s warehousing footprint spans hundreds of millions of square feet, including specialized cold storage that supports perishables and high-value products.
- One in nine jobs in the state are tied to the logistics industry.
- The state consistently ranks high for access to qualified talent, especially in logistics, transportation, and supply chain functions.
- Georgia invests heavily in workforce development to support industry growth.
- A broad network of logistics programs across the University System of Georgia, technical colleges, and career academies trains students on logistics operations, freight management, supply chain analytics, and more.
- Strategic partnerships between educators, employers, and state workforce initiatives ensure that training aligns with industry needs, helping companies recruit and retain skilled workers for today and tomorrow.
