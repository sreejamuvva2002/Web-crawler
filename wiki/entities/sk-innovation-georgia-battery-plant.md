# SK Innovation Georgia Battery Plant

**Type:** Facility  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[battery-cell-manufacturing|Battery Cell Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** Commerce, Georgia, Georgia, United States

## Overview

A settlement between SK Innovation and LG allows the completion of a $2.6 billion battery manufacturing facility in Commerce, GA that will supply batteries for Volkswagen vehicles and the electric Ford F-150.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Battery manufacturing facility supplying EV batteries for major automakers.

## Key facts

- The $1.8 billion settlement will enable SK Innovation to complete a $2.6 billion battery factory in Commerce, Georgia that will supply batteries for Volkswagen vehicles and the electric Ford F-150.
- President Joe Biden said the deal is a positive step toward building a strong, diversified and resilient U.S.-based electric vehicle battery supply chain and creating good-paying jobs.
- The plant is expected to provide 2,600 jobs in Commerce, Georgia.
- SK Innovation retained former EPA Administrator Carol Browner and former Deputy Attorney General Sally Yates to lobby the administration on the dispute.
- LG agreed to resolve the IP dispute and will not sue SK for ten years in exchange for the settlement payment and a running royalty on batteries produced.
- Georgia Senators Raphael Warnock and Jon Ossoff praised the settlement as protecting workers and jobs in Commerce.
