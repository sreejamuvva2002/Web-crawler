# Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-02

**Source:** [Kia Georgia celebra tres hitos importantes, incluido el inicio de la producción del Kia Telluride 2027   - AutoNews-Wire.com](https://autonews-wire.com/kia-georgia-celebra-tres-hitos-importantes-incluido-el-inicio-de-la-produccion-del-kia-telluride-2027)  ·  `page_010208`

## Facts

- Kia Georgia celebrated a historic day at its West Point facility with the start of production for the new 2027 Kia Telluride.
- The ceremonial vehicle represented the first hybrid electric vehicle (HEV) assembled in the state of Georgia.
- The event marked the 5 millionth vehicle produced at Kia Georgia.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp, team members, and state and community leaders attended the ceremony.
- Sean Yoon, president and CEO of Kia North America and Kia America, stated that the plant is the only one in the world that manufactures the Telluride.
- Sean Yoon noted that reaching five million vehicles and presenting the first HEV assembled in Georgia (and the first Kia HEV assembled in the U.S.) testifies to Kia's rich history and future direction.
- Since production began in 2009, Kia Georgia has become one of the region's most important employers and a pillar of automotive manufacturing in the Southeast.
- The Telluride, launched in 2019, is manufactured exclusively in Georgia and has become one of the most successful vehicles on the Kia lineup.
- Stuart Countess, president and CEO of Kia Georgia, emphasized that the day's milestones were made possible by the dedication of the Kia Georgia team members.
- Stuart Countess stated that producing gasoline, electric, and world-class hybrid vehicles demonstrates the plant's versatility and talent.
- Attendees at the ceremony included Consul General Joon-ho Lee, State Representative Vance Smith, Technical College System of Georgia Commissioner Greg Dozier, Department of Community Affairs Commissioner Chris Nunn, Department of Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson, First Lady Marty Kemp, HATCI President John Robb, Georgia Chamber of Commerce President Chris Clark, and Hyundai Motor Group Washington Office Senior Vice President Drew Ferguson.
- The Telluride has become a flagship model for the brand and a symbol of vehicle quality manufactured in West Point.
