# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Strong Currents - Georgia Quick Start](https://www.georgiaquickstart.org/showcase/hyundai-motor-group-metaplant-america-hmgma/)  ·  `page_000104`

## Facts

- In May 2022, Hyundai Motor Group announced it was investing $5.4 billion to build a smart factory dedicated to making electric vehicles in Georgia.
- The facility will be built on a 2,900-acre megasite in Bryan County, located just a 30-minute drive from the downtown historic district of Savannah, Ga.
- The project agreement signed by State of Georgia officials and Hyundai executives will create more than 8,000 jobs in the region.
- This project is considered the largest economic development project in the state’s history.
- Georgia Quick Start is committed to providing customized workforce training for Hyundai, affiliated companies, and supplier companies.
- Georgia Quick Start will design, build, and operate an 80,000-sq. ft. training center directly adjacent to the new facility.
- Georgia Quick Start will construct a second building at its Advanced Manufacturing Training Center in Pooler, Georgia, to support training for supplier companies.
- The project involves overcoming unknown challenges as this type of megaproject had never been done before.
- Hyundai’s “Metaplant” concept is a vision of mobility where journeys are measured by more than just mileage.
- Georgia Quick Start is training future General Assembly Meta Pros to be ready for the production floor on day one.
- The Georgia Quick Start team visited South Korea and spent days in discussion with Hyundai Motor Group to develop company-specific training ahead of the megaproject.
- The facility will include the Hyundai Mobility Training Center of Georgia, where thousands of future Meta Pros and supplier employees will be trained.
- Hyundai is partnering with dozens of suppliers for the project, including Mobis, which is receiving customized training from Quick Start.
- Georgia Quick Start continues its partnership with HYUNDAI TRANSYS, which will supply powertrains and seating systems to both Kia in West Point, Ga., and HMGMA.
- Oscar Kwon, CEO of HMGMA, stated that Hyundai’s mission is “progress for humanity” and part of a shared vision for a brighter, more innovative future.
