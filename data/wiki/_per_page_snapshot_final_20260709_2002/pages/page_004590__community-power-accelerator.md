# Community Power Accelerator

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Home | Community Power Accelerator](https://communitypower.energy.gov/)  ·  `page_004590`

## Facts

- The National Laboratory of the Rockies (NLR, formerly NREL) is sharing an update regarding the future of the Community Power Accelerator (CPA) platform.
- Beginning in March 2026, the CPA platform will transition to the Milken Institute’s Community Infrastructure Center (CIC).
- CPA will continue to operate as a free, public-facing platform while benefiting from expanded resources, partnerships, and long-term support.
- The integration of CPA into CIC is designed to preserve tools users rely on today while expanding access to capital, expertise, and knowledge across a broader range of infrastructure projects.
- All core CPA functionality will remain intact.
- Existing project information and user activity will be preserved.
- Continued support for distributed solar and storage projects under 5 megawatts.
- Free access to the platform for all users.
- A broader national network of capital providers, technical assistance partners, and project sponsors will be added.
- Enhanced grant and financing matching tools will be available.
- An expanded knowledge base will include new templates, case studies, and resources.
- Opportunities to participate in financing forums and deal-focused convenings will be offered.
- Ongoing platform enhancements and feature development will continue.
- The Community Infrastructure Center supports infrastructure projects nationwide—well beyond solar and storage—and connects communities to a wide range of capital pathways.
- In early March 2026, users will receive instructions to reset their password and activate their account on the CIC platform.
- A live onboarding and platform demo will be held on April 7, 2026, at 12:00 PM MT.
- Registration details for the live session will be shared soon.
- All existing project information, user profiles, and activity histories will be preserved and available on the CIC platform.
- CPA will continue to support distributed solar and storage projects under 5 MW.
- Access to the CPA platform through CIC remains free.
- Users will receive instructions to reset their password and activate their existing account in CIC rather than creating a new one.
- Users gain access to a larger national network of funders and partners, improved matching tools, expanded learning resources, and new financing convening opportunities.
