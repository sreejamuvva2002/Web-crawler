# Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia, Inc.

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2010-02-27

**Source:** [Kia Celebrates Grand Opening of Georgia Plant](https://www.autotalk.com/kia-celebrates-grand-opening-of-georgia-plant-3255/)  ·  `page_060437`

## Facts

- Officials with Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia (KMMG), Inc. celebrated the official grand opening of Kia’s first-ever U.S. automobile manufacturing plant in West Point, Georgia.
- The plant is capable of producing 300,000 cars annually for the North American and global markets.
- The ceremony was attended by approximately 500 VIPs from Korea and the U.S.
- Attendees included Chairman of the Hyundai-Kia Automotive Group Mong-Koo Chung; Group President and CEO of KMMG and Kia Motors America (KMA) Byung-Mo Ahn; Georgia Governor Sonny Perdue; and other high-ranking Korean government and State of Georgia officials.
- Byung-Mo Ahn stated that Kia is proud to call West Point, Georgia home and looks forward to many years of growing together.
- Byung-Mo Ahn noted that the successful launch of the 2011 Kia Sorento CUV and establishment of their first U.S. manufacturing plant positions the Kia brand for further growth and to become a worldwide leader in quality and innovation.
- The project represents a $1 billion investment by Kia Motors Corporation.
- The plant is a sprawling, new 2.2 million-square-foot automobile manufacturing facility situated on more than 2,200 acres of land between Exit 2 and Exit 6 along Interstate 85 in West Point.
- KMMG consists of four main shops: stamping, welding, paint, and assembly.
- The site also includes a transmission shop, module shop, and a two-mile test track where every vehicle produced by KMMG will be thoroughly tested.
- KMMG’s general assembly area is outfitted with more than a half mile of height-adjustable conveyors to achieve the most favorable installation position for each team member and reduce physical fatigue.
- The assembly area is outfitted with wood flooring, which provides a more comfortable and forgiving work environment for team members.
- Georgia Governor Sonny Perdue noted the event marks the continuation of a mutually beneficial partnership between the State of Georgia and Kia that will benefit the state in years to come.
- Governor Perdue stated that Kia Motors has brought thousands of new jobs to the area and is truly delivering as a tremendous corporate citizen.
- Governor Perdue pledged to continue providing specialized training through the Kia Georgia Training Center to guarantee a quality workforce and the future success of the plant in Georgia.
- The massive Kia automobile manufacturing plant and its supplier companies have already provided thousands of jobs.
- KMMG currently employs more than 1,200 team members.
- KMMG is expected to nearly double its headcount by the end of the year.
- Onsite and nearby suppliers will create up to 7,500 additional jobs in the region.
- Randy Jackson, director of human resources and administration, expressed gladness that KMMG could help give back to the local community through job creation, community development, and tourism.
- Randy Jackson stated that the Grand Opening marks a new milestone for Kia.
- Beginning in spring, KMMG will open its doors to the community to see firsthand the quality and craftsmanship built into every Kia vehicle.
- Mass production of the all-new 2011 Kia Sorento CUV began on Nov. 16, 2009.
- The 2011 Sorento officially went on sale in the U.S. in January of this year.
- The 2011 Sorento sold 7,398 units in its first month, making it Kia’s best-selling vehicle in January.
- Byung-Mo Ahn stated that the outstanding early performance of the first vehicle produced from the West Point, Georgia plant is a significant achievement for KMMG, KMA and the Kia brand as a whole.
- Kia plans to continue expanding its line of vehicles here and abroad while staying true to the Kia brand tenets of value, quality, safety, and technology.
- KMMG will play a prominent role in achieving Kia's goals.
