# Technical College System of Georgia (TCSG)

**Type:** workforce_training  ·  **Role:** workforce_training  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp and TCSG Announce Winners of 2024 Governor’s Workforce Summit Awards – TCSG | Technical College System of Georgia](https://www.tcsg.edu/gov-kemp-and-tcsg-announce-winners-of-2024-governors-workforce-summit-awards/)  ·  `page_012934`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp and the Technical College System of Georgia (TCSG) announced the winners of the 2024 Governor’s Workforce Summit Awards.
- The awards were presented during the annual two-day Governor’s Workforce Summit held at the Georgia World Congress Center in Atlanta.
- The summit brings together more than 1,000 educators, business and industry leaders, government officials, and community leaders to highlight and strengthen workforce initiatives across Georgia.
- Aurubis Richmond was named Business Partner of the Year.
- Floyd County College and Career Academy was named College and Career Academy of the Year.
- Jesse Price from Houston College and Career Academy and Northside High School won Work-Based Learning Student of the Year, with the award sponsored by the Georgia Foundation for Public Education.
- Cullee Peavey Richardson from Ware County High School won Youth Apprenticeship Completer of the Year, with the award sponsored by the Georgia Foundation for Public Education.
- Jayden Davis from Athens Technical College Academy won Dual Achievement Program Student of the Year, with the award sponsored by the TCSG Foundation.
- Moatapari Agbere from Chattahoochee Valley College and Career Academy won Dual Enrollment Student of the Year, with the award sponsored by the TCSG Foundation.
- Joshua Cain from Rockdale Career Academy won College and Career Academy Student of the Year, with the award sponsored by Georgia Power and The Munn Firm.
- TCSG Commissioner Greg Dozier stated that the award winners demonstrate the power of strong partnerships between education and industry in creating opportunities for students and building a highly skilled workforce.
- The Technical College System of Georgia consists of 22 colleges and serves as Georgia’s top resource for skilled workers.
- TCSG offers world-class training in more than 600 associate degree, diploma, and certificate programs.
- Students are trained on state-of-the-art equipment by instructors who are experts in their fields.
- TCSG houses Georgia’s Office of Adult Education, which promotes and provides adult literacy and education programs, including the GED® testing program, throughout the state.
- TCSG partners with companies through Quick Start, described as the nation’s top customized workforce training program.
- Individual TCSG colleges work with local industry to provide workforce and training solutions.
