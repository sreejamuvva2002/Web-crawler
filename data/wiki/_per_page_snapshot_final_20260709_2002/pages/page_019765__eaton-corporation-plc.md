# Eaton Corporation plc

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-11

**Source:** [ETN SEC Filings - Eaton 10-K, 10-Q, 8-K Forms](https://www.stocktitan.net/sec-filings/ETN/)  ·  `page_019765`

## Facts

- Eaton Corporation plc announced a proposed transaction to combine its Mobility Group with Dana Incorporated in a Reverse Morris Trust transaction.
- Definitive agreements were dated June 10, 2026, and customer FAQs describing leadership plans were posted on June 11, 2026.
- The combined automotive and commercial-vehicle business is expected to generate approximately $11 billion in revenues.
- Eaton expects the transaction to close in Quarter 1, 2027, subject to customary regulatory approvals, Dana shareholder approval, and other closing conditions.
- The companies will operate independently until closing, with the Mobility Group planned to transition to the Dana brand upon completion.
- Eaton's Mobility Group is valued at about $5.1 billion, while the combined company is valued at over $10 billion.
- The combined business is expected to generate roughly $11 billion in 2026 pro forma revenue and $1.7 billion in estimated adjusted EBITDA.
- Run-rate cost synergies are estimated at $250 million.
- At closing, Eaton will receive approximately $1.1 billion in cash distribution.
- Eaton shareholders are expected to own at least 50.1% of the combined company's shares.
- The deal is intended to be tax-free for U.S. federal income tax purposes.
- Leadership appointments for the combined company include Byron Foster as incoming Chief Executive Officer and Timothy Kraus as Chief Financial Officer.
- Erin Rowse will serve as Chief Human Resources Officer upon close.
- Dana's eight-member board will be expanded to include three additional directors designated by Eaton.
- The combined Mobility/Dana company will create a larger engineered solutions provider for commercial and light vehicle OEMs and aftermarket channels.
- The transaction is framed as part of Eaton’s 2030 growth strategy.
- After closing, Eaton will focus on its Electrical and Aerospace businesses.
- The combined company will operate as Dana Incorporated and continue to trade under the ticker DAN.
- Upcoming SEC filings include a Form 10, Form S-1/S-4, Schedule TO, and Form S-4.
- Company statements direct employees to virtual information sessions and a Mobility Group Transition Hub for updates.
- The communication includes customary forward-looking cautionary language about approvals, integration risks, financing, and other uncertainties.
