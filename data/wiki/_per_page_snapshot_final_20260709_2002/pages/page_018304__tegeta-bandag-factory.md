# Tegeta Bandag Factory

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Bandag To Build State-Of-The-Art Retreading Factory In Georgia In Collaboration With Tegeta Motors And Bridgestone](https://menafn.com/1110480008/Bandag-To-Build-State-Of-The-Art-Retreading-Factory-In-Georgia-In-Collaboration-With-Tegeta-Motors-And-Bridgestone)  ·  `page_018304`

## Facts

- Bandag signed an agreement with Tegeta Motors to build a state-of-the-art Tegeta Bandag Factory in Rustavi, Georgia.
- The project marks an important step in expanding sustainable retreading solutions across the Caucasus region.
- The facility is supported by an initial investment of USD 2 million.
- The plant aims to produce up to 5,000 retreaded tyres annually.
- Capacity is expected to double within five years.
- The new facility will be the first Bandag retreading factory in the Caucasus.
- Construction is scheduled to begin this year.
- Completion is targeted for late 2026.
- The project marks a significant milestone in Bandag's commitment to advancing circular mobility, promoting environmental responsibility, and delivering innovative fleet solutions.
- The project strengthens Tegeta Motors' position as a leading provider of advanced automotive services in the region.
- The facility will begin by retreading tyres for buses and trucks.
- Every product will meet international safety and performance standards.
- The approach is expected to significantly reduce environmental impact and lower operational costs for corporate and commercial customers.
- Representatives from Bridgestone and Bandag will provide construction oversight to ensure the facility adheres to global quality standards.
- The plant will be equipped with state-of-the-art Bandag machinery sourced from Europe and the United States.
- The retreading process reinforces the casing of a used tyre with the same premium materials used in new tyre manufacturing.
- Retreaded tyres cost 30-40% less than new tyres.
- Retreaded tyres deliver comparable performance to new tyres.
- Retreaded tyres provide a service life of up to five years.
- Bandag's retreading technology requires less raw material and energy.
- The technology extends a tyre's life through multiple cycles, reduces waste generation, minimizes carbon emissions, and supports more efficient use of natural resources.
- Ekaterine Kavtaradze, CEO of Tegeta Motors, stated that the launch advances fleet solutions, promotes sustainable mobility, strengthens their presence in the Caucasus region, and is positioned to drive innovation and deliver long-term value for customers and communities.
- Tegeta Motors will integrate the retreading operations into its existing ecosystem, which includes tyre services, oil and battery operations, and recycling activities.
- Bridgestone MEA continues to provide technical expertise and innovation support throughout the factory's development as Bandag's supporting entity in the Caucasus.
- The Tegeta Bandag Factory marks an important chapter in Bandag's regional growth and sustainability journey.
- Bandag is creating a strong foundation for cleaner, more efficient, and more reliable mobility solutions for fleets across the Caucasus through its partnership with Tegeta Motors and support of Bridgestone MEA.
