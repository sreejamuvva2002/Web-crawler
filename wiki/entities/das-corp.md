# DAS Corp.

**Type:** Company  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[automotive-components|Automotive Components]]  ·  **Location:** Metter, Candler County, Georgia, United States

## Overview

DAS Corp., an automotive parts company specializing in seating systems and safety components, will establish a new manufacturing facility in Metter, Georgia to produce automotive seat structures, creating 300 jobs and generating more than $35 million in investment.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Produces automotive seat structures for Hyundai Motor Group’s EV production hub, supporting Georgia’s electric-vehicle supply chain.

## Key facts

- The new DAS facility will primarily produce automotive seat structures, supporting Hyundai Motor Group’s EV production hub in Georgia.
- Operations are projected to begin in the latter half of 2024.
- DAS will recruit assembly technicians over a five-year period, aiming for 300 new jobs.
- Strategic location near Savannah and Macon provides convenient access to logistics assets such as rail lines and highways.
- Governor Kemp highlighted that suppliers for the Hyundai Metaplant generated over $2 billion in investment last fiscal year, contributing to record economic growth.
- Mayor Ed Boyd said the project will be transformative for Metter’s community, stimulating housing investment and new business opportunities.
- Candler County Chairman Glyn Thrift thanked DAS for choosing the county, noting the boost to economic development.
- Georgia Ports Authority President Griff Lynch expressed pride in supporting Georgia’s growing automotive manufacturing presence.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson noted Georgia is well-positioned for Hyundai’s supplier network due to years of investment in site preparation, workforce solutions, and connectivity.
- Since 2018, Georgia has attracted more than $25 billion in investments and created 30,000 jobs across the automotive supply chain.
