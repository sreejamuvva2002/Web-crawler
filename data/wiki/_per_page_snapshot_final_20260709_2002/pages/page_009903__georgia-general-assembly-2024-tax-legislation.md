# Georgia General Assembly 2024 Tax Legislation

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-05-07

**Source:** [Georgia’s 2024 Tax Legislation | Individual Income Tax Reduction](https://www.wilsonlewis.com/georgias-2024-tax-legislation/)  ·  `page_009903`

## Facts

- The 2024 legislative session in Georgia concluded on March 28, 2024.
- The General Assembly passed several tax bills, including a substantial $500 million in tax cuts for both individual and corporate taxpayers in Georgia.
- HB 1181 revises the tax incentive framework by reducing the carryforward period for key income tax credits from 10 years to five years.
- HB 1181 affects essential credits like research and development, jobs, and investment tax credits, shortening the time they can be used against future tax liabilities.
- HB 1181 sets sunset dates starting in 2025 for these and other specific incentives, requiring renewal through future legislation.
- Both income tax rate bills apply to the entire 2024 tax year but take effect on July 1, 2024.
- HB 808 raises the exemption threshold for tangible personal property taxes from $7,500 to $20,000, benefiting businesses by lowering tax liabilities and supporting investment.
- HB 1162 aligns Georgia’s state income tax laws with the federal Internal Revenue Code (IRC), adopting the same tax rules and regulations as the federal government for that year.
- HR 598 and HB 1267 lay the groundwork for creating a dedicated tax court in Georgia, with an operational start date of January 1, 2026, and beginning to accept cases on August 1, 2026.
