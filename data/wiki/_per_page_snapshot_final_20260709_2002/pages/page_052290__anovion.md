# Anovion

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Decatur County, Ga., Chosen for Lithium-Ion Battery Materials Factory : CEG](https://www.constructionequipmentguide.com/decatur-county-ga-chosen-as-site-for-new-lithium-ion-battery-materials-factory/61883)  ·  `page_052290`

## Facts

- Anovion chose southwest Georgia to invest an initial $800 million for its 1.5-million-sq.-ft. plant.
- Site selection factors included proximity to existing and planned low- and carbon-free energy sources, short supply chains, access to existing rail infrastructure, a highly valuable skilled workforce, and a business-friendly environment.
- Decatur County is seen as an ideal fit for Anovion's new expansion by company officials.
- CEO Eric Stopka stated Anovion conducted a thorough search to address energy, transportation, logistics, and human capital needs to produce the highest quality and sustainable synthetic graphite anode material.
- CEO Eric Stopka noted that existing infrastructure makes southwest Georgia attractive and proximity to other battery and electric vehicle manufacturing plants will help reduce their carbon footprint.
- Anovion began commercial production of lithium-ion battery materials in early 2021.
- Anovion is among a limited number of graphite anode producers in America qualified for EV applications.
- In October 2022, Anovion was awarded a $117 million grant from the U.S. Department of Energy under the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law to scale production capacity and construct a large-scale factory in the Southeast.
- The Georgia facility will be the first of its scale for Anovion.
- Anovion views the Georgia plant as a crucial first step to reshoring production of synthetic graphite anode, which is favored by EV and battery manufacturers for consistency and performance.
- The new Georgia plant is expected to initially produce 40,000 metric tons per year once fully operational.
- The facility will create hundreds of high-quality local clean energy jobs for the surrounding region.
- Gov. Brian Kemp welcomed Anovion to Georgia, calling it the No. 1 state for business and fast becoming the e-mobility capital of the nation.
- Gov. Brian Kemp stated that job creators recognize every corner of Georgia has the resources and workforce needed for success.
- Gov. Brian Kemp expressed looking forward to celebrating their partnership with Georgians in Decatur County after meeting the company's team personally.
- Anovion plans to use innovative, sustainable, and technologically advanced manufacturing processes at the new Decatur County facility.
- The facility will rely on the region's rapidly decarbonizing energy mix to power manufacturing.
- The facility will introduce proprietary furnace technologies that substantially reduce greenhouse gas emissions.
- These furnace technologies provide a clear roadmap for Anovion to achieve a carbon-neutral footprint over time.
- Executive Chairman Chip Dunn stated their long-term strategic vision is to focus on growth and innovation, positioning Anovion as a leader in the U.S. energy transition and securing a domestic source for critical materials for U.S. lithium-ion battery production.
- Chip Dunn expressed excitement to bring their full arsenal of innovation to the Georgia facility to execute their long-term business strategy and combat the climate crisis.
- In addition to Georgia, Anovion is expanding its Anovion Center of Excellence.
- The company is also expanding a hydro-powered sister manufacturing facility in Sanborn, N.Y., near Niagara Falls.
- Sanborn, N.Y. is one of the only qualified U.S. sources of battery-grade synthetic graphite to commercially ship product today.
