# Hyundai Motors Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-05-19

**Source:** [Inside Hyundai’s Wild Georgia Metaplant: EV Production, Jobs, and Factory Tour Insights](https://www.motortrend.com/features/hyundai-metaplant-georgia-ev-production-jobs-robots)  ·  `page_009257`

## Facts

- The full name of the sprawling complex is Hyundai Motors Group Metaplant America, or HMGMA for short.
- The facility is located in Ellabell, Georgia, just outside of Savannah.
- The site covers a 2,900-acre megasite.
- The plant is six times larger than Disneyland, plus California Adventure and Downtown Disney.
- A future nature preserve with a walking path for employees will be located on the campus.
- A water tower on the campus features a livery designed by local art students.
- HMGMA represents the single largest investment by any company in the history of Georgia.
- In May 2022, Hyundai pledged a $7.6B expenditure for the project.
- The investment quickly grew to $12.6B, which includes the addition of a separate battery facility joint venture with SK On located on the same megasite.
- In September 2025, the company spent another $2.7B on the project.
- The first Ioniq 5 rolled off the production line in October 2024.
- The first Ioniq 5 was covered in the signatures of the "Meta Pros," which is HMGMA-speak for factory workers.
- The company hopes the Metaplant will eventually have the capacity to build up to 500,000 BEVs and EREVs per year.
- The eventual production capacity will span across the Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis brands.
- By 2030, Hyundai aims for 80 percent of its vehicles sold in the U.S. to be built in the U.S.
- Hyundai is promising 36 new models by 2030 as part of a new product offensive.
- More than 30 percent of Hyundai’s U.S. sales in 2025 were electrified in some capacity.
- The company presumes at least 80 percent of the 36 new models promised by 2030 will be built in the United States.
- A new body-on-frame midsize truck is being developed to compete with the Colorado, Ranger, and Tacoma.
- The brand showed a production-intent-looking Bronco/Wrangler competitor called the Boulder at the New York Auto Show.
- Hyundai bowed a conceptual off-roader called the Crater at last November’s L.A. Auto Show, which appears to be a tributary flowing off the Ioniq 5 platform.
- Hyundai Motor Group managed to move 984,017 units in the U.S. in 2025.
- Of those 2025 units, 772,712 were retail sales.
- Q1 of 2026 represents an all-time sales record for the Hyundai brand.
- Q1 2026 also marked 40 years since Hyundai sold its first car in the U.S., which occurred in February 1986.
- Hyundai saw a nearly 40 percent increase in EV sales from February to March of 2026, attributed to the war in Iran spiking gas prices.
- Hyundai CEO José Muñoz is the 2025 MotorTrend Person of the Year.
