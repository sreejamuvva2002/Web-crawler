# Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [FinancialContent - Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation Breaks Ground on Innovative SMART Warehouse in Newnan, Georgia](https://markets.financialcontent.com/stocks/article/bizwire-2022-6-10-yamaha-motor-manufacturing-corporation-breaks-ground-on-innovative-smart-warehouse-in-newnan-georgia)  ·  `page_015713`

## Facts

- Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation (YMMC) broke ground on a new, advanced, and innovative “SMART” Warehouse in Newnan, Georgia.
- The SMART Warehouse is the first-of-its-kind for Yamaha Motor Company worldwide.
- The new 200,000 square foot facility will use state-of-the-art automated storage and retrieval systems.
- The warehouse will reduce response time for fulfilling dealer orders for Yamaha WaveRunners, Golf Cars, ATVs, and Side-by-Sides by more than 50 percent.
- Groundbreaking attendees included YMMC President Taka Imanishi, YMMC VP Supply Chain Wayne Pierce, YMUS President and CEO Mike Chrzanowski, Newnan Mayor Keith Brady, and Georgia State Senator Matt Brass.
- Mike Chrzanowski stated the facility will improve overall operational efficiency and material flow velocity to factory assembly lines to get product to dealers and customers faster.
- Demand for all outdoor recreational products produced at the YMMC factory continues to exceed supply.
- SMART is an acronym for Sequenced Material and Reduced Transportation.
- The medium parts automated storage system will retrieve and put away 240 containers per hour, compared to the current rate of 170 containers per hour.
- The small part automated system will handle 2400 containers per hour, in comparison to the current capability of 550 containers per day.
- New systems will enable Yamaha to reduce forklifts by 50 percent using automatic conveyors and tuggers.
- The investment underscores the company’s long-term commitment to YMMC employees, the City of Newnan, and Coweta County.
- The facility will bring higher paid and more skilled jobs into Coweta County, including robotics experts, software developers, and systems engineers.
- The warehouse was designed by SSI SCHAEFER.
- The facility will include an observation platform for groups such as students from local schools to learn about manufacturing and automated processes.
- Yamaha expects to complete the SMART Warehouse in 2024.
- YMUS products include Motorcycles, Scooters, ATVs, Side-by-Side vehicles, Snowmobiles, WaveRunners, Boats, Outboard Motors, Golf Cars, and Power Assist Bicycles, sold through a nationwide network of distributors and dealers.
- YMUS has corporate offices in California and Georgia, facilities in Wisconsin and Alabama, and factory operations in Tennessee and Georgia.
- YMUS owns additional U.S.-based subsidiaries including Yamaha Marine Systems Company, Skeeter Boats, and Yamaha Precision Propeller.
