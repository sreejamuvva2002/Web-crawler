# Rideout Arsenal

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-10

**Source:** [Rideout Arsenal Bringing $22M, 120 New Jobs to Thomas Co. | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-releases/2026/rideout-arsenal-bringing-22m-120-new-jobs-thomas-co)  ·  `page_002359`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Rideout Arsenal is investing $22 million in a new manufacturing facility in Thomasville.
- The investment will create 120 new jobs over the next several years.
- Rideout Arsenal specializes in high-performance, high-end competition pistols and is based in the United States.
- The company utilizes a localized supply chain built around trusted domestic partners, suppliers, and skilled labor.
- Recent anti-gun legislation in Virginia created significant uncertainty for the company, prompting its relocation to Georgia.
- The new Rideout Arsenal facility will be located within the Plantation Oak Industrial Park.
- Additional buildings are planned throughout the next few years at the Thomas County site.
- Rideout Arsenal is actively hiring for production, engineering, administrative, and management positions.
- Thomasville Mayor Scott Chastain welcomed the company to the community, citing new highly skilled jobs, partnerships, careers, and local momentum.
- Shelley Zorn, President and CEO of the Thomasville Payroll Development Authority, noted that the arrival of new industry attracts investment, innovation, and opportunity to Thomasville.
- Tina Herring, Assistant Director of Existing Industry and Regional Recruitment, represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) on the project.
- The project was executed in partnership with the Thomasville-Thomas County Payroll Development Authority (PDA) and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson emphasized that Georgia’s workforce training and development for companies of every size is a key reason businesses choose the state.
- The investment strengthens integrated supply chains, creating additional opportunities for local businesses, workers, and communities throughout the region.
