# Hyundai Mobis

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hyundai Mobis secures $940 million to build US factories](https://www.newsarticleinsiders.com/news/articleView.html?idxno=1946)  ·  `page_021313`

## Facts

- South Korea’s leading car component maker Hyundai Mobis announced on Nov. 8 that it had secured $940 million to build U.S. plants through its first overseas green loan.
- The funds will be used for the firm’s plan of investing $1.3 billion through 2030 to build new production hubs for electrification in four locations, including Alabama and Georgia.
- The facilities will include battery system assembly lines and power electric system factories aimed at meeting the growing demand for electric cars in the United States.
- Hyundai Mobis achieved long-term financing with a maturity of 10 years by liaising with seven international financial institutions.
- The Seoul-based company raised the funds through its first overseas green loans, which are funding methods for eco-friendly businesses like EVs and renewable energy.
- Hyundai Mobis currently operates electrification production hubs in nine locations globally.
- Hyundai Mobis is establishing six new electrification production hubs in South Korea, North America, and Indonesia.
- A Hyundai Mobis official stated the company remains committed to enhancing its competitiveness in the electrification business through a strategy of selectivity and focus as it transitions into a future mobility technology specialist.
- Observers point out that Hyundai Mobis is expected to further increase its investments in the U.S. market.
- Daelim University automotive professor Kim Pil-soo stated that Hyundai Mobis is now the world’s sixth-largest auto part maker and must make its presence felt in the U.S. market for EVs to become a bigger player.
- Observers expect the company to channel more funds and energies into the U.S. market down the road than it has already announced.
- The share price of Hyundai Mobis went down 0.67 percent on the South Korean Stock Exchange on Nov. 8.
