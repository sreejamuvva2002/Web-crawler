# Novelis Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-06-01

**Source:** [Novelis Enters Long - Beijing Ferrochrome Co.,Ltd](https://www.tjjyly.com/blog/novelis-enters-long.html)  ·  `page_047525`

## Facts

- Novelis Inc. signed a new long-term contract with Coca-Cola Bottlers' Sales & Services Company, the contracting agent for The Coca-Cola Company's authorized North American bottlers.
- Under the agreement, Novelis will supply Coca-Cola's authorized North American bottlers with aluminum can sheet for The Coca-Cola Company's family of iconic brands.
- Supply will come from Novelis' plant in Bay Minette, Ala., which is currently under construction and expected to begin commissioning in 2025.
- The Coca-Cola North American bottlers have committed to purchasing a confidential volume of aluminum can sheet over a multi-year period through the agreement.
- Steve Fisher, President and CEO of Novelis, stated the contract builds on a decades-long relationship with the Coca-Cola system and strengthens Novelis' position as the leading provider of aluminum for beverage cans.
- The contract validates Novelis' investment in expanding rolling and recycling capacity in North America.
- The agreement includes closed-loop recycling, where Novelis takes back manufacturing scrap from the can making process, recycles it, and converts it into new can sheet.
- Novelis recycles more than 80 billion used beverage cans per year into new aluminum for beverage packaging.
- Recycling aluminum is approximately 95% less energy intensive than making primary aluminum, resulting in 95% less carbon emissions.
- Novelis expects demand for aluminum beverage can sheet to grow at a 3% compounded annual growth rate from 2022 to 2031.
- Demand growth is driven by consumer preference for sustainable products and size variety, as well as more beverage types being packed in cans including water, energy drinks, soda, beer, wine, hard seltzers and ready-to-drink cocktails.
- Dan Coe, Chief Procurement Officer of The Coca-Cola Company, stated the goal is to build a resilient domestic supply chain for sustainable long-term growth and recognize Novelis as a sustainability leader supporting 2030 World Without Waste Targets.
- The majority of the Bay Minette plant's capacity for beverage can is already contracted.
- Novelis' new low-carbon facility in Bay Minette will be the first fully integrated aluminum manufacturing and recycling plant built in the U.S. in nearly 40 years.
- The Bay Minette facility is expected to create up to 1,000 new jobs.
- The facility boasts an initial capacity of 600,000 tons of finished goods for the North American can and automotive markets.
- The facility will be powered by renewable energy, use recycled water, and operate as a zero-waste facility.
- Novelis is working toward a goal of achieving net carbon neutrality at Bay Minette.
- Novelis expects to commission the Bay Minette plant in mid-2025.
- Novelis Inc. is a global leader in innovative aluminum products and the world's largest recycler of aluminum.
- Novelis aims to be the leading provider of low-carbon, sustainable aluminum solutions and achieve a fully circular economy across North America, Europe, Asia, and South America.
- Novelis had net sales of $18.5 billion in fiscal year 2023.
- Novelis is a subsidiary of Hindalco Industries Limited, the metals flagship company of the Aditya Birla Group, a multinational conglomerate based in Mumbai.
- Novelis serves customers in the aerospace, automotive, beverage can, and specialties industries.
