# Reaching Potential Through Manufacturing

**Type:** workforce_training  ·  **Role:** workforce_training  ·  **Published:** 2017-05-15

**Source:** [RPM To Provide New Path To Graduation For Richmond County High Schoolers | Georgia Public Broadcasting](https://www.gpb.org/blogs/education-matters/2017/05/25/rpm-provide-new-path-graduation-for-richmond-county-high)  ·  `page_051597`

## Facts

- Beginning this August, Richmond County high school students seeking an alternative path to completing their secondary education will have one.
- Reaching Potential Through Manufacturing (RPM) is a partnership between Textron Specialized Vehicles Inc. (TSV) and the Richmond County School System.
- The RPM program offers students the ability to make up lost ground toward a high school diploma while earning income and valuable work experience in a manufacturing facility.
- The cooperative education program combines an instructional day with a four-hour shift at a manufacturing facility that produces components and subassemblies for products manufactured by TSV.
- TSV constructs vehicles under the E-Z-GO®, Cushman®, Bad Boy®, TUG™, and Douglas™ brands.
- The program will draw students from all Richmond County high schools.
- Applicants for the RPM program are selected based on need and suitability.
- Depending on their specific needs, students will have a choice between attending classes at their home high school and taking coursework at the RPM facility.
- Classroom and educational space is available on site at the RPM facility.
- Besides traditional academic classes, RPM students will also receive training in life skills and tutoring resources as needed.
- Kevin Holleran, president of TSV, stated that RPM will give students a unique educational path to reach their goal while providing training in life skills and vocational work experience they can leverage to start careers afterward.
- Dr. Angela Pringle, superintendent of Richmond County Schools, said the partnership will provide an unprecedented experience for students who enroll in the manufacturing career pathway.
- RPM is modeled after “12 For Life,” a highly successful program of Southwire Company LLC and the Carroll County School System.
- Since its inception eight years ago, more than 870 students have graduated from the 12 For Life program.
- Sister programs for 12 For Life have been created in Monroe, Georgia and Florence, Alabama.
- Georgia 12 For Life has received national attention and acclaim.
- Southwire and Carroll County Schools officials provided valuable knowledge and assistance in the creation of RPM.
- The Richmond County School System is home to over 32,000 students in 56 schools.
- The Richmond County School System is the 10th largest school district in Georgia.
- The Richmond County School System is the third largest employer in Augusta-Richmond County with over 4,000 employees.
- RCSS boasts having the oldest public school in the south, and the fifth oldest public high school in America.
- Several of its schools are on the National Register of Historic Places.
- Although steeped in history, Richmond County strives to move education forward and to provide students with opportunities to succeed.
- Textron Specialized Vehicles Inc. is a leading global manufacturer of golf cars, utility and personal transportation vehicles, and ground support equipment.
- TSV's vehicles are found in environments ranging from golf courses to factories, airports to planned communities, and theme parks to hunting preserves.
- Textron Inc. (NYSE: TXT) is a multi-industry company that leverages its global network of aircraft, defense, industrial and finance businesses to provide customers with innovative solutions and services.
- Textron is known around the world for its powerful brands such as Bell Helicopter, Cessna, Beechcraft, Hawker, Jacobsen, Kautex, Lycoming, E-Z-GO, Greenlee, Textron Systems, and TRU Simulation + Training.
