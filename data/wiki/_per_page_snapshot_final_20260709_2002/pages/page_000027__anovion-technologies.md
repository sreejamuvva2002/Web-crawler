# Anovion Technologies

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2023-05-15

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp: Anovion Technologies to Create Over 400 Jobs in Bainbridge, Invest $800M in Manufacturing Facility | Governor Brian P. Kemp Office of the Governor](https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases/2023-05-15/gov-kemp-anovion-technologies-create-over-400-jobs-bainbridge-invest-800m)  ·  `page_000027`

## Facts

- Anovion Technologies is a supplier of premium synthetic graphite anode materials for lithium-ion batteries.
- Anovion will build a new manufacturing facility in Bainbridge.
- The project will create more than 400 jobs.
- The project involves over $800 million in investment in Decatur County.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced the project on May 15, 2023.
- Georgia is fast becoming the e-mobility capital of the nation.
- Anovion is a climate tech-driven advanced materials company.
- Anovion is one of the first qualified U.S. suppliers of synthetic graphite anode materials for use in e-mobility applications.
- Graphite is the largest battery material used in electric vehicles by mass, more than copper, nickel, manganese, cobalt, and lithium.
- Eric Stopka is the Chief Executive Officer of Anovion.
- The Bainbridge facility will be Anovion's first large-scale expansion manufacturing facility in Georgia.
- Georgia's strong technical workforce and top-tier schools will help advance research partnerships and contribute to a highly skilled talent pipeline for Anovion.
- The region's growing availability of clean energy will result in increased sustainability for Anovion's manufacturing process.
- The new facility will be 1,500,000 square feet.
- The facility will utilize Anovion's proprietary furnace technologies.
- The facility is located at Downrange Industrial Park in Bainbridge.
- The facility will be the first of its size and scale in North America.
- The facility will produce synthetic graphite for use in batteries that power EVs, electric storage systems, electronics, military applications, and other products.
- Operations are expected to begin in late 2025.
- The company will be hiring for technical, production, engineering, administrative, and management positions.
- Bainbridge Mayor Edward Reynolds welcomed Anovion to the city.
- Pete Stephens is Chairman of the Decatur County Board of Commissioners.
- Decatur County leadership has focused on attracting quality companies and jobs to diversify its industry mix.
- Taylor Walden served as Project Manager representing the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s Global Commerce team.
- The project was developed in partnership with the City of Bainbridge, Development Authority of Bainbridge, Decatur County, University System of Georgia, Georgia EMC, and Georgia Quick Start.
- Pat Wilson is the GDEcD Commissioner.
- Much of the critical supply chain for e-mobility and manufacturing is currently sourced outside of the U.S.
- Anovion fills that need and delivers another critical component of the supply chain to Georgia.
- Georgia is positioned as a hub for the electric mobility industry.
- Since 2020, 40 EV-related projects have announced more than $21.9 billion in investment and 28,000 jobs in Georgia.
- More than 10 of these projects are battery manufacturers, recyclers, and suppliers.
- These battery projects represent approximately $8.5 billion in investment and 6,000 jobs.
- Anovion Technologies is headquartered in Chicago, Illinois.
- Anovion is a U.S.-owned and U.S.-based advanced materials business.
- Anovion is America’s first commercial-scale manufacturer of premium, cleaner anode-grade synthetic graphite across its existing footprint near Niagara Falls, New York, and Clarksburg, West Virginia.
- Anovion began commercial production in early 2021.
- Anovion is among a limited number of graphite anode producers in America to have successfully gained qualification for EV applications.
