# West Point Kia Plant

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-02-24

**Source:** [West Point KIA plant celebrates 5 millionth vehicle, 2027 Telluride start of production](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/west-point-kia-plant-celebrates-232613235.html?fr=sycsrp_catchall)  ·  `page_010028`

## Facts

- Georgia state leaders and Kia executives celebrated a milestone at the West Point, Ga. plant on a Tuesday morning.
- The plant celebrated the production of its 5 millionth vehicle.
- The plant also celebrated the start of production for the 2027 Kia Telluride.
- The 2027 Kia Telluride is the first U.S.-built Kia hybrid model.
- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp called the facility "the crown jewel of our entire economic mission" at the event.
- Gov. Kemp stated that the Telluride has become a symbol of success because it is prevalent on roads in Georgia and across the country.
- Kia Tellurides are exclusively manufactured at the West Point plant.
- The Kia Telluride model was originally launched in 2019.
- Kia Georgia CEO and President Stuart Countess stated that since its 2019 launch, the Telluride has become one of the brand's most popular models.
- Gov. Kemp highlighted efforts to align Georgia's workforce pipeline from K-12 through higher education to prepare locals for future success.
- The state and Kia are working together to provide Georgians with the skills and knowledge needed to fill good-paying jobs at the Kia plant.
- Kia employs roughly 14,000 people across Georgia.
- Approximately 3,200 of those employees work at the West Point plant.
- Kia is collaborating with the local school system, particularly middle schools, to expose students to good-paying job opportunities in the field.
- Kia aims to show potential future talent that they can enter the workforce directly, attend a technical college, or attend a four-year institution.
- Kia has created programs designed to give students the skills needed to enter the workforce, regardless of whether they can afford or choose to attend a four-year institution.
