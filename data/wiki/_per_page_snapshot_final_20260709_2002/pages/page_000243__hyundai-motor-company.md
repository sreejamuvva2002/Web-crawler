# Hyundai Motor Company

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-09-12

**Source:** [Hyundai’s Georgia EV supplier network adds 3 more manufacturers | Manufacturing Dive](https://www.manufacturingdive.com/news/hyundai-georgia-supplier-network-nvh-korea-daesol-ausys-das-corp-manufacturers/693349/)  ·  `page_000243`

## Facts

- Three Hyundai suppliers plan to add manufacturing plants in Georgia for a combined investment of nearly $180 million.
- Gov. Brian Kemp announced the supplier investments.
- Automotive parts manufacturer NVH Korea will invest over $72 million in a new manufacturing facility in Locust Grove, Georgia.
- NVH Korea will produce EV battery system components that protect, connect, and monitor battery cells' performance.
- NVH Korea's new facility is expected to create 160 jobs.
- NVH Korea's operations are anticipated to begin in Q2 of 2024.
- The Locust Grove plant will be NVH Korea's second Georgia facility.
- NVH Korea's subsidiary, AFS America, operates a facility in Columbus that produces floor and carbo mats and cargo trays.
- South Korea-based Daesol Ausys will establish a $72 million facility in West Point.
- Daesol Ausys's new plant will create 140 jobs across production, quality control, maintenance, human resources, and management.
- Daesol Ausys's production is expected to begin in December 2024.
- Daesol Ausys will produce luggage boards and covers.
- Daesol Ausys supplies General Motors and Kia.
- Kia operates a manufacturing facility in West Point.
- In July, Kia announced a $200 million investment to expand its West Point plant to produce a new electric SUV model beginning in Q2 of the following year.
- Car seat maker Das Corp will establish a $35 million plant in Metter.
- Das Corp's new facility will produce automotive seat structures when operations begin in the second half of 2024.
- Das Corp's investment will create 300 jobs.
- The three new factories follow Hyundai's announcement of a $5.5 billion manufacturing campus in Ellabell, which is the largest economic development project in Georgia’s history.
- Gov. Brian Kemp stated that suppliers for the Hyundai Metaplant resulted in over $2 billion in investment last fiscal year alone, helping Georgia achieve a third straight year of record-breaking economic growth.
- In late May, Hyundai and LG Energy Solution struck a joint venture deal to build a $4.3 billion battery cell plant adjacent to Hyundai’s EV campus.
- In early September, Hyundai and LG Energy Solution announced an additional $2 billion investment toward the upcoming plant.
- The total joint venture investment will reach $6.3 billion.
- Hyundai and LG Energy Solution will each hold a 50% stake in the venture and facility.
- Hyundai will assemble battery packs using cells from the joint venture plant to send to its U.S. manufacturing sites.
- A state official told Manufacturing Dive that the total $6.3 billion investment will create 8,500 jobs.
