# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2023-05-30

**Source:** [In Georgia, Collaboration Equals Solutions - Expansion Solutions](https://www.expansionsolutionsmagazine.com/in-georgia-collaboration-equals-solutions/)  ·  `page_052615`

## Facts

- Georgia’s largest economic development project to date demonstrates how longstanding relationships can lead to the greatest success stories.
- Over two decades ago, the Kia Georgia automobile manufacturing plant opened in West Point, Georgia.
- The Kia plant delivered thousands of high-quality jobs to a rural community and attracted numerous suppliers to the region.
- Over a five-year period, Kia’s investment revitalized the community and helped the company reach new goals.
- Kia has announced new product lines, including most recently the highly anticipated Kia EV9 SUV starting 2024.
- Essential factors for the location’s success are partnership and workforce, which Georgia has in abundance.
- These are exhibited through the state-of-the-art 70,000-square-foot Kia Georgia Training Center.
- The training center was developed to provide advanced industrial training for employees in collaboration with the Technical College System of Georgia.
- This approach played a major role in a speedy six-month negotiation period leading to Hyundai Motor Group’s plan for the $5.54 billion electric vehicle Metaplant at the Bryan County Megasite near Savannah, Georgia.
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) has remained on the fast track, breaking ground on the facility five months after its announcement.
- This new OEM is already anchoring a supplier network worth an estimated $2 billion that extends across coastal Georgia.
- More suppliers are expected to locate in other corners of the state.
- In Commerce, Georgia, SK Battery America’s (SKBA) 2018-announced facility is also thriving.
- As of January 2023, batteries were being shipped to customers from the SKBA facility.
- The company was two years ahead of schedule to meet its hiring goal of 2,600 employees.
- Over the next year, SKBA plans to further expand its workforce to handle growing market demand at its two existing lithium-ion (Li-ion) battery manufacturing facilities.
- Following SK Battery America’s opening, Li-ion battery recycling and engineered materials startup Ascend Elements announced a new facility in Georgia in January 2022.
- Within months, Ascend Elements confirmed a partnership with SKBA.
- When Ascend Elements celebrated its grand opening only a year later, it had already attained a full 50 percent of its hiring goal ahead of schedule.
- This led the company to announce a workforce expansion beyond its original plans.
- Global leader Aurubis will focus on recycling copper and other precious metals for sustainable technologies such as electric vehicles, batteries, and solar panels.
- An existing advantage in the solar manufacturing industry is cementing Georgia’s place on the cutting-edge of the generational transformation of clean energy.
- Qcells North America, an affiliate of Korean-based Hanwha, began solar panel production in northeast Georgia in 2019.
- The company has seen accelerated growth since then, announcing major expansions of the original facility and an additional $2 billion facility in a nearby county.
- Suppliers have also begun to locate in the area.
- The state is adapting for a seamless transition to sustainable technology by fostering fully integrated ecosystems through its support for organic partnerships.
- Growth during the past five years has been rapid, and Georgia has continued to meet company needs thanks to years of foresight and investment in speed-to-market solutions for businesses.
- Solutions range from megasites capable of supporting OEMs and their suppliers.
- Pre-certified “Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development” sites fast-track construction timelines.
- The state develops workforce pipelines and training through the Technical College System of Georgia, College and Career Academies, tapping into the large population of veterans, and through more traditional methods.
- The growth of e-mobility extends to the aerospace industry, which has traditionally been drawn to Georgia for the highly qualified talent produced at its research universities.
- Archer Aviation has announced a new state-of-the-art electric vertical takeoff and landing aircraft (eVTOL) manufacturing facility located alongside one of Georgia’s nine regional commercial airports in Covington, Georgia.
- Universities like the Georgia Institute of Technology and the University of Georgia draw companies to the Atlanta region, where creating research and development (R&D) space can provide opportunities for collaboration and innovation.
- Anduril Industries acquired then Marietta-based Area-I as a way to enter Georgia’s aerospace market.
- The company reinforced the public-private partnerships that had been developed through years of working with the Georgia Center of Innovation’s aerospace industry team.
- Anduril elected to transform two buildings near Georgia Tech’s Tech Square in Midtown into mixed-use office, R&D, manufacturing, and production space.
- Georgia’s technology and software development talent is as diverse as it is well-educated.
- Cisco and Micron Technology each cited diversity as part of their decisions to locate in Atlanta.
- Georgia took steps early to expand internet access across the state to allow companies and employees more choices for where they can live and work.
- The State of Georgia’s marketing arm, the Georgia Department of Economic Development, works with communities to identify strategic markets for growth and hone their existing resources for interested companies.
- The Technical College System of Georgia and Georgia Quick Start prepare the workforce with both customized training as well as holistic, concept-based manufacturing training to educate employees on the “bigger picture” of their role within a system.
- This training provides employers with capable, informed team members who are ready to perform at a high level and lead with confidence.
- This broader education also equips Georgians with transferrable skills that make them more capable of crossing industries.
- Further, Quick Start’s training programs identify opportunities to upskill workers across industries, leading to a nimbler workforce.
- Companies prioritizing logistics are not limited to Atlanta and the world’s busiest commercial airport.
- Coastal communities offer access to both the fastest-growing port in the nation in Savannah and the Port of Brunswick, which is the second-busiest port in the U.S. for total Ro/Ro cargo.
- Communities located more centrally in the state are connected through an inland port system that boosts the use of rail cargo transportation.
