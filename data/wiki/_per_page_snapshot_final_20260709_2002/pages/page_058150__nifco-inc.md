# Nifco Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2025-03-31

**Source:** [What is Brief History of Nifco Company? – MatrixBCG.com](https://matrixbcg.com/blogs/brief-history/nifco)  ·  `page_058150`

## Facts

- Nifco Inc. pioneered the use of plastic fasteners, transforming industries, especially automotive.
- Founded in 1967 as Nippon Industrial Fastener Corporation, originally focusing on plastic fasteners for automotive and home electronics.
- Established on February 13, 1967, as a venture co-founded by Nichiei Bussan Co., Ltd. (holding 60%) and ITW (holding 40%).
- Initial capital investment was 48 million yen.
- Core vision was to provide efficient and cost-effective fastening solutions by replacing metal fasteners with plastic alternatives.
- First product was the 'Plasti Rivet', designed to contribute to labor savings and cost reductions in manufacturing processes.
- In December 1970, Nippon Industrial Fastener Corporation was renamed Nifco Inc.
- In 1972, entered technology transfer agreements with America's 'Big Three' automotive manufacturers (GM, Chrysler, and Ford).
- In October 1977, merged with Nichiei Bussan Co., Ltd., increasing capital to 1 billion yen.
- Listed on the Second Section of the Tokyo Stock Exchange in July 1979, with capital increasing to 1.18 billion yen.
- First overseas venture was co-founding Nifco Taiwan Corporation in Taiwan in May 1983.
- Listed on the First Section of the Tokyo Stock Exchange in March 1984.
- Co-founded Korea Industrial Fastener Corporation (now Nifco Korea Inc.) in South Korea in 1985 and listed on the First Section of the Osaka Securities Exchange.
- In 1990, acquired Elta Plastics Ltd. (now Nifco U.K. Ltd.) in England and co-founded Nifco (Malaysia) Sdn. Bhd. in Malaysia.
- By 1999, sales reached approximately ¥45.7 billion.
- Pioneered the world's first mini-damper, popularizing the 'push-open' mechanism globally.
- Engineered parts for fuel tanks to meet environmental regulations and developed sophisticated engine transmission components.
- Received the Intellectual Property Achievement Award in 2006.
- Established the Nifco Technology Development Center (NTEC) as its central hub for advanced technical development in 2013.
- Achieved total sales revenue of ¥186 billion for the fiscal year ending March 2024, a 12% year-over-year increase.
- Reported operating income of ¥23 billion for FY ending March 2024, a 15% increase from the previous year.
- Recorded net income of ¥16 billion for FY ending March 2024, a 10% rise compared to the prior year.
- Experienced a 5% decrease in net sales to ¥353,038 million for the fiscal year ending March 31, 2025, while operating profit increased by 12% to ¥49,200 million.
- Consolidated net sales were ¥353,038 million for the fiscal year ending March 31, 2025.
- Market capitalization was approximately $2.39 billion as of July 25, 2025.
- Established Nifco America Corporation in the United States in 1987.
- Went public with an IPO priced at ¥1,300 per share in 2014.
- Moved head office to Yokosuka in 2015.
- Achieved ISO 9001 certification in 1998.
- Established a subsidiary in Germany in 2006.
- Targets a 20% increase in international sales by 2025, with projected revenue contribution rising to approximately ¥31.2 billion.
- Aims to reduce its carbon footprint by 25% by 2025, having already achieved a 10% reduction by 2023.
- Makes investments in renewable energy, including solar panels and self-delivering solar power plants, to support carbon reduction goals.
- Concentrating on developing products for electric vehicles (EVs) and advanced driver-assistance systems (ADAS).
- Strategically responding to the automotive industry's push for lightweighting to drive growth in the plastic fasteners market.
- Developing advanced plastic components that contribute to fuel efficiency and reduced emissions.
- The global automotive plastic fasteners market is projected to rise from USD 5.03 billion in 2025 to USD 8.56 billion by 2034.
- The automotive plastic fasteners market is projected to reach USD 7.89 billion by 2033.
