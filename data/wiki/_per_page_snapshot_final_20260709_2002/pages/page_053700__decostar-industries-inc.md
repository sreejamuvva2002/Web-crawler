# Decostar Industries, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2014-10-27

**Source:** [Decostar Industries to Pay $38,500 to Settle EEOC Religious Discrimination Suit | U.S. Equal Employment Opportunity Commission (EEOC) - JDSupra](https://www.jdsupra.com/legalnews/decostar-industries-to-pay-38-500-to-22479/)  ·  `page_053700`

## Facts

- Decostar Industries, Inc. is a manufacturer and supplier of automotive parts based in Carrollton, Ga.
- The company will pay $38,500 and provide other relief to settle a religious discrimination lawsuit filed by the U.S. Equal Employment Opportunity Commission (EEOC).
- The EEOC charged that employee Dina Lucas Velasquez made numerous requests to be excused from Decostar's requirement that all employees work overtime hours on designated Saturdays because her religious beliefs prohibited her from working during the Sabbath.
- Decostar initially approved Velasquez's request for accommodation until January 2014, when a new supervisor took over her department and repeatedly denied her ongoing request for a religious accommodation.
- Decostar eventually discharged Velasquez on Oct. 27, 2014, after she refused to violate her religious beliefs.
- The consent decree settling the lawsuit requires Decostar to adopt and implement a new policy for employees to request a religious accommodation for their bone fide religious beliefs.
- The decree also requires that the company provide annual equal employment opportunity training to its managers.
- The two-year decree further requires the company to post a notice to its employees about the lawsuit and to provide periodic reporting to EEOC about disability discrimination complaints.
- Antonette Sewell, regional attorney for the agency's Atlanta District Office, stated that it is unconscionable and unlawful for employers to force members of their workforce to choose between their livelihood and their religion.
