# Rivian

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-11-26

**Source:** [Vivek Ramaswamy Is Mad About Rivian's Georgia Factory Because He Doesn't Know How Loans Work](https://www.jalopnik.com/vivek-ramaswamy-is-mad-about-rivians-georgia-factory-be-1851712206/)  ·  `page_020191`

## Facts

- Rivian secured a $6.6 billion loan from the Biden administration to fund construction of its Georgia factory.
- The electric startup had to pause its initial plans to bring the plant online this year.
- The approved loan will be a key part of Rivian's plans to expand its product lineup beyond the R1T and R1S models.
- Vivek Ramaswamy, co-lead of the advisory committee for Elon Musk's Department of Government Efficiency, criticized the loan approval on Twitter/X.
- Ramaswamy claimed the $6.6 billion loan implies a cost of $880k per job based on a projected 7,500 jobs at the Georgia plant.
- Ramaswamy characterized the loan as a "political shot across the bow" targeting Elon Musk and Tesla.
- The article clarifies that the federal government is not spending $880,000 per job because Rivian must repay the loan with interest.
- A second factory will allow Rivian to build several new, more affordable models and significantly increase overall sales.
- Rivian is projected to sell about 50,000 vehicles this year.
- Tesla remains the most valuable automaker in the world despite being described as "hilariously overvalued" and not needing government assistance.
