## Facts

- Essex Furukawa released its second annual sustainability report today in collaboration with Vision 2030: A Sustainable Future, Our Driving Force.
- The 29-page document highlights the company's increased use of renewable energy and successes in reducing carbon dioxide emissions.
- It shows progress toward 'Zero Waste to Landfill' in global production and outlines future initiatives regarding Scope-3 emissions, supply chain data, and ESG projects.
- Austin Robinson, Global Corporate Sustainability Manager at Essex Furukawa, believes the results were very encouraging.
- Robinson and her team compiled the data with help from company leaders across the organization and external verification partners.
- Robinson stated that what they achieved is only possible through strong commitment, noting consistent changes across the company and ongoing exchange/implementation of best practices.
- Robinson noted a noticeable increase in social sustainability as more employees become aware of their role for the future of the environment and local communities.
- Essex Furukawa developed its holistic sustainability program with three core values: disruptive innovation, environmentally friendly production, and agile digitalization, which serve as pillars to drive change and innovate the magnet wire industry without losing sight of environmental impacts.
- The report presents last year's results and shows how they impact Essex Furukawa's progress in implementing its holistic Vision 2030 program.
- The document addresses goals for zero waste to landfill, reducing energy consumption, renewable energy, and CO2 reduction regarding environmentally friendly production.
- The report covers how Essex Furukawa prioritizes agile digitalization goals while collaborating with customers and their R&D leaders to drive disruptive innovations in the magnet wire industry.
- Daniel Choi, CEO of Essex Furukawa, said that a leading role in environmental, social, and governance (ESG) requires the transparency provided by the report.
- Choi stated that the ESG field is constantly evolving and they are committed to stakeholders to remain flexible and accountable, claiming success across most perspectives and metrics.
- Choi added that the company wants to set the standard rather than just follow it in ESG practices.
- Superior Essex is located at 5770 Powers Ferry Rd. NW Suite 400, Atlanta, GA 30327 – USA.
