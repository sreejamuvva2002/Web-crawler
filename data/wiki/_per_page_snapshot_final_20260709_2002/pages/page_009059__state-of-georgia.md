# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2023-06-05

**Source:** [Be Careful What You Wish For, Electric Vehicle Edition](https://cleantechnica.com/2023/06/05/be-careful-what-you-wish-for-electric-vehicle-edition/)  ·  `page_009059`

## Facts

- Right-to-work laws are clustered mainly in the conservative Southeast and up through the nation’s midsection, used for decades to lure automakers and manufacturers with cheap, compliant labor.
- The same right-to-work labor pool is now attracting electric vehicle stakeholders, putting red state officials in the awkward position of leading the decarbonization movement while continuing to rail against it.
- Georgia was among the first wave of states to adopt anti-union, right-to-work legislation shortly after World War II.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) continues to pitch its anti-union status to the auto industry.
- Only 4.7% of workers in the private sector in Georgia are unionized, which is well below the national average according to GDEcD.
- GDEcD positions Georgia as a hub for the electric transportation industry under an elaborately detailed section titled “Electric Mobility Manufacturing.”
- Since 2018, 35 EV-related projects have contributed $23 billion in investments in Georgia, with GDEcD highlighting Hyundai Motor Group’s recent investments.
- A new $4.3 billion joint venture between Hyundai Motor Group and electric vehicle battery maker LG Energy Solution is the latest clean tech investment in Georgia.
- GDEcD leads the Georgia Electric Mobility and Innovation Alliance (EMIA), which is dedicated to supporting the growth of the entire electric mobility industry, fostering innovation, creating a business-friendly environment, and promoting favorable public policy.
- Georgia is among 25 or Republican-dominated states that pitched a lawsuit last January against new federal Department of Labor rules supporting pension fund managers who ascribe to ESG (environment, social, governance) investing principles.
- The anti-ESG movement focuses on protecting fossil energy stakeholders from having to compete in a low-carbon economy and uses stirring up public opinion against the “woke mob” as part of its strategy.
- Georgia State Attorney General Christopher M. Carr joined the anti-ESG lawsuit on behalf of Georgia.
- Republican Georgia Governor Brian Kemp signed onto a joint anti-ESG statement with 18 other governors dated March 16, railing against ESG-friendly rules.
- The joint letter states that the proliferation of ESG is a direct threat to the American economy, individual economic freedom, and our way of life, putting investment decisions in the hands of the woke mob to bypass the ballot box and inject political ideology into investment decisions, corporate governance, and the everyday economy.
- GM is pumping $1 billion or more into Phase II of its “Ultium CAM” joint venture with Korean firm POSCO Future M to onshore the battery materials part of its EV supply chain.
- The Ultium CAM joint venture is majority owned by POSCO and aims to support GM’s Ultium lithium-ion EV battery platform.
- CAM stands for Cathode Active Materials, a combination of lithium and other raw materials that go into lithium-ion EV batteries; Phase II integrates pCAM, referring to the engineering of precursor materials for producing CAM.
- With CAM production concentrated mainly in Asia, GM and other US automakers are on the prowl for opportunities to shore up their North American supply chains, which the Phase II POSCO hookup will accomplish.
- The Ultium CAM joint venture will support production of approximately 360,000 Chevrolet, Cadillac, GMC, Buick and BrightDrop vehicles annually in the 2025-2030 timeframe in North America.
- The announcement left out the location of the new integrated CAM/pCAM processing facility; GM has dozens of manufacturing facilities around the US including two EV battery cell factories in Michigan, but Georgia has come up empty-handed so far.
- GM also has battery cell plants in Ohio and Tennessee.
- The POSCO partnership angle hints that GM is eyeballing solid-state EV battery technology; GM announced plans for a joint venture with POSCO Chemical in 2021 to launch Phase I of the Ultium CAM project and tapped POSCO as one of its six strategic supply chain partners last fall.
- GM China Science Lab in Shanghai received the 2022 R&D 100 Award for its new solid-state battery technology, which GM has described as “game-changing.”
- The high-power bipolar solid-state battery technology offers excellent thermal stability and abuse tolerance compared with conventional liquid electrolyte.
- Its unique bipolar stacking and proprietary interfacial ionic booster greatly enhance all-climate serviceability and give it a simplified configuration.
- Dr. Helen Liu, manager of the Battery Lab Group at GM China Science Lab, stated that the highly safe battery innovation offers huge potential applications for promoting electrification inside and outside the automotive industry.
- Governor Kemp launched the Electrification and Innovation Alliance in 2021; the alliance notes that advancements in battery technology, flying vehicles, autonomous driving and unmanned aircraft systems are driving the electric mobility industry.
- Governor Kemp agreed in a 2021 press release announcing the launch of EMIA that the alliance will ensure Georgia is positioned to continue leading the nation in the rapidly growing electric mobility industry.
