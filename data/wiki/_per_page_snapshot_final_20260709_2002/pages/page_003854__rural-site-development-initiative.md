# Rural Site Development Initiative

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-05-15

**Source:** [Rural Site Development Initiative NOFA | Georgia Department of Community Affairs](https://dca.georgia.gov/public-notice/2026-05-15/rural-site-development-initiative-nofa)  ·  `page_003854`

## Facts

- On May 15, 2026, the OneGeorgia Authority, housed within the Georgia Department of Community Affairs (DCA), released a Notice of Funding Availability for the Rural Site Development Initiative.
- The initiative will provide approximately twenty million dollars ($20,000,000) in state funding to assist local communities with developing sites crucial to economic development across Georgia.
- Funding will be awarded at regular semi-annual intervals through an ongoing, competitive grant cycle, subject to funding availability.
- The Authority reserves the right to fund any, all, or none of the applications submitted and may waive minimum and maximum funding limits at its discretion.
- Applications for less than fifty thousand dollars ($50,000) will not be accepted.
- Only one application will be considered per applicant, per cycle.
- Projects must demonstrate the ability to address a lack of available industrial sites for recruitment and retention of employment opportunities.
- Projects must demonstrate the ability to address the difficulty of satisfying project sites in communities or regions with high unemployment.
- Projects must demonstrate the ability to address a community or regional commitment to expand and improve existing sites.
- Studies for due diligence on site development are limited to no more than two hundred fifty thousand dollars ($250,000).
- Site Improvement grants are limited to no more than two million dollars ($2,000,000) per application and development.
- Applicants located in an OneGeorgia Eligible community should generally contribute at least a ten percent (10%) match to the amount of grant funds requested.
- Applicants located in an OneGeorgia Conditionally Eligible community should contribute at a minimum twenty-five percent (25%) match to the amount of grant funds requested.
- Communities located in an OneGeorgia Non-Eligible county are not allowed to apply or receive grant funds.
- Applications are expected to leverage other sources of funding such as private debt or equity, philanthropic support, or other public funds.
- Eligible applicants include units of general-purpose local government (e.g., city, county, consolidated government), as well as local, regional, and state authorities.
- Prospective applicants must be located in OneGeorgia Eligible and Conditionally Eligible counties to submit applications for activities within those areas.
- Local government and authority applicants must comply with audit report and grant certification form submission requirements contained in O.C.G.A. §§ 36-81-7 and 86-81-8.1.
- Applicants must be current and compliant with all reporting, audit, and survey requirements as required by statute.
- Prior recipients of OneGeorgia funding must resolve any outstanding audit and/or compliance monitoring findings or deficiencies prior to submitting an application.
- Eligible infrastructure and construction costs include grading, clearing, grubbing, infrastructure development, road improvements, accel/decel lanes, engineering, purchase of adjacent or expansion property, easements, and due diligence studies.
- Sites must be publicly controlled at the time of application submission and must be a minimum of fifty (50) contiguous acres.
- If a site is not publicly owned, the development authority must hold an option to purchase extending for a period of no less than three (3) years for an application to be considered.
- Applicants seeking Site Improvement Grants must be GRAD certified or GRAD select at the time of application.
- Applicants seeking Due Diligence grant funding must commit to applying for GRAD certification within one year.
- If a site is not GRAD certified, completion of due diligence activities and receiving GRAD certification are both prerequisites to the Authority considering the site for Site Development funding.
- Should an applicant receive a Due Diligence award and achieve GRAD certification on said property, the applicant may pursue Site Improvement grant funding in any subsequent funding cycle.
- Funding for a second location will not be considered by the Authority prior to an original funded site receiving GRAD certification (if not already achieved), all infrastructure installed within the industrial park, and marketing of such property being undertaken.
- Sale or lease activities not compliant with the NOFA parameters are subject to penalties addressed in award documentation.
- Restrictions on OneGeorgia-improved property prohibit use for solar farm activities, data centers, or mini-storage facilities.
- Developments must comply with state environmental and historic preservation requirements.
- A pre-application is due thirty (30) days prior to the full application deadline and should be submitted via email.
- Each completed application will first be reviewed to reconfirm eligibility and determine completeness, then thoroughly vetted and competitively scored.
- The full application should include a preliminary engineering report and commitment letters for all local funding match.
- For Fiscal Year 2027, the submission deadline is October 9, 2026, with a December/January award announcement.
- For Fiscal Year 2027, the second submission deadline is April 9, 2027, with a June/July award announcement.
- Successful applicants will receive a written notice of award including grant or loan documents, requirements, timelines, and other details.
- OneGeorgia funds must generally be expended within two years from the date of the award.
- Completion is defined as all activities for which funds were received being complete, all awarded funding having been expended, and all award close-out procedures being finalized.
