# Toccoa, Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2010-03-30

**Source:** [Toccoa, Georgia - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Toccoa,_Georgia)  ·  `page_018012`

## Facts

- Stephens County Development Authority (SCDA) was established in 1965 to continue and sustain the growth of Northeast Georgia.
- SCDA is responsible for the recruitment of new businesses such as industrial, manufacturing, distribution, corporate and regional headquarters and customer service centers.
- SCDA serves the following cities: Toccoa, Eastanollee, Martin, and Avalon.
- Major industrial parks in the area are Toccoa Industrial Park, Meadowbrook Industrial Park, and Hayestone Brady Business Park.
- The top Stephens County employers in descending order include Caterpillar, Patterson Pump, ASI (GEM Industries), American Woodmark Corp., Standard Register, Sage Automotive Interiors, Habersham Plantation, Toccoa Falls College, Coats & Clark, Eaton Corporation, and PTL Company.
- PTL Company is an elevator fixtures and parts manufacturer.
- Sage Automotive Interiors is listed among the top employers in Stephens County.
- Eaton Corporation and Caterpillar are listed among the top employers in Stephens County.
- The nearest interstate highway to Toccoa is Interstate 85.
- State Route 17 bypasses Toccoa, with an alternate route through the city, and U.S. Route 123/State Route 365 runs through Toccoa as well.
- The Amtrak line serving Toccoa is shared with the Norfolk Southern Railway.
- In 2008, downtown redevelopment efforts attracted private investment of $3.5 million, resulting in 11 new businesses and 68 full-time equivalent jobs retained.
- Toccoa partnered with six state agencies, including the Georgia Department of Transportation, to secure $1.3 million for infrastructure improvements, supplemented by $552,000 in local funding.
