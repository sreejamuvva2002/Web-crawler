# Anovion Technologies

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2023-05-15

**Source:** [Anovion Technologies Announces Plans for New Manufacturing Facility in Georgia](https://www.m2graphite.com/news/anovion-technologies-announces-plans-for-800-million-initial-investment-in-new-manufacturing-facility-in-southwest-georgia/)  ·  `page_000343`

## Facts

- Anovion Technologies selected Decatur County in Southwest Georgia as the location of its first large-scale expansion of manufacturing capacity for production of its premium synthetic graphite anode materials.
- The new facility will be 1,500,000 square feet.
- The facility is expected to initially produce 40,000 metric tonnes per annum of synthetic graphite anode material for lithium-ion batteries once fully operational.
- Anovion announced an $800 million initial investment for the new Georgia manufacturing facility.
- The project will create over 400 high-paying, quality jobs for the surrounding region.
- In October 2022, Anovion was awarded a grant totaling $117 million from the U.S. Department of Energy under the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law to help secure the domestic battery manufacturing supply chain.
- Anovion chose Decatur County based on its proximity to existing and planned low- and carbon-free energy sources for power, short supply chains, access to existing rail infrastructure, a highly valuable skilled workforce, and a business-friendly environment.
- Eric Stopka, Chief Executive Officer of Anovion, stated the company conducted a thorough search to identify a location that addressed its energy, transportation, logistics, and human capital needs.
- Eric Stopka noted that proximity to other battery and EV manufacturing plants in Southwest Georgia will allow Anovion to further reduce its carbon footprint.
- Governor Brian Kemp welcomed Anovion to Georgia, calling it the No. 1 state for business that is fast becoming the e-mobility capital of the nation.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that job creators are recognizing that every corner of the state has the resources and workforce needed for success.
- Bainbridge Mayor Edward Reynolds welcomed Anovion to Bainbridge, noting the $800 million investment and creation of over 400 high-paying, quality jobs as a huge win for the community.
- Chip Dunn, Executive Chairman of Anovion, stated the company's long-term strategic vision is to focus on growth and innovation while positioning Anovion as a leader in the U.S. energy transition.
- The new facility will rely on the region's rapidly decarbonizing energy mix to power its manufacturing process.
- Anovion will introduce proprietary furnace technologies at the Georgia facility that substantially reduce greenhouse gas emissions and lay out a clear roadmap for achieving a carbon-neutral footprint over time.
- Chip Dunn emphasized continued research and development in electrochemistry, technology improvements in production processes, and ambitious climate tech initiatives to mitigate greenhouse gas emissions.
- Anovion is expanding its Anovion Center of Excellence and its hydro-powered sister manufacturing facility near Niagara Falls, NY.
- The Niagara Falls facility is one of the only qualified U.S. sources of battery-grade synthetic graphite that is commercially shipping product today.
- Anovion Technologies is headquartered in Chicago, Illinois, and is a U.S.-owned and U.S.-based advanced materials business.
- Anovion currently operates an existing footprint near Niagara Falls, NY and Clarksburg, WV.
- Anovion began commercial production of premium, cleaner anode-grade synthetic graphite in early 2021.
- Anovion is among a limited number of graphite anode producers in America to have successfully gained qualification for EV applications.
- In 2022, Anovion was awarded the $117 million DOE grant to rapidly scale production capacity with the construction of a large-scale factory in the southeastern U.S. and investment in its relocated Anovion Center of Excellence in Sanborn, NY.
- Anovion is commencing a multi-year expansion program of 150,000 metric tonnes of annual capacity for anode-grade synthetic graphite.
