# Yachiyo Industry Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2019-03-31

**Source:** [Yachiyo Industry Co., Ltd. Business Report FY ended Mar. 2019 - MarkLines Automotive Industry Portal](https://www.marklines.com/en/top500/cf/s500_209_hl2018)  ·  `page_015562`

## Facts

- Yachiyo Industry Co., Ltd. reported revenue of 161,160 million JPY for FY ended Mar. 31, 2019, a 2.7% decrease from the prior year.
- Operating profit fell 92.5% to 701 million JPY due to lower revenue, expenses to stabilize production at its US subsidiary, and recall costs for faulty fuel tanks.
- Profit before tax was 928 million JPY, a 89.9% decrease from the prior year.
- Profit for the year attributable to owners of the parent was (4,335) million JPY compared to 4,796 million JPY in the prior year.
- The company transferred its vehicle assembly business to Honda Motor Co., Ltd. in April 2018 to consolidate managerial resources into parts businesses for sustainable growth.
- Yachiyo Industry will strengthen development capabilities at its Zhongshan, Guangdong Province subsidiary to enhance local automaker support, aiming to increase stationed employees by March 2020.
- The company's two Chinese subsidiaries in Wuhan and Zhongshan mainly produce fuel tanks and sunroofs, supplying Geely Automobile in addition to Honda Motor Co., Ltd.
- US Yachiyo, Inc., a U.S.-based production subsidiary, will invest about JPY 2.8 billion to expand its plant building and install new forming equipment for plastic fuel tanks.
- US Yachiyo boosted production with aging equipment, which caused earnings deterioration, before implementing the expansion.
- US Yachiyo will start operation of the new forming equipment at the beginning of 2020.
- Plastic fuel tanks have become lighter and functionally sophisticated in areas such as fuel transpiration prevention.
- The company is considering developing and producing plastic fuel tanks for plug-in hybrid and electrified vehicles.
- A flexible production system for high-performance plastic tanks will be established in the U.S.
- Yachiyo Industry aims to develop two or three types of new exterior plastic automotive parts and establish mass-production technology by 2025, focusing on weight reduction as vehicle electrification accelerates.
- The company is focusing on developing plastic tank production technology for fuel cell vehicles in Japan.
- R&D activities include developing metal-honeycomb, high-precision fuel-cell carriers that comply with exhaust-gas regulations worldwide.
- R&D activities include work on plastic fuel tanks with new structural designs.
- R&D activities include work on new types of sunroofs and environmental technology for automotive parts.
- R&D activities include work on special-needs vehicles and driver-assist systems.
- R&D facilities include the Tochigi R&D Center (Tochigi Pref., Japan) for fuel tanks and sunroof systems for mass-market vehicles.
- The Saitama R&D Center (Saitama Pref., Japan) was established in April 2012 for next-generation product R&D.
- Yachiyo of America Inc. is located in Ohio, U.S., handling design and testing of sunroofs and fuel tanks manufactured in North America since its establishment in July 2008.
- R&D expenditure was 4,282 million JPY for FY ended Mar. 31, 2019, down slightly from 4,305 million JPY in the prior year.
- Overall capital expenditure reached 13,227 million JPY for FY ended Mar. 31, 2019, with the Americas region accounting for 3,580 million JPY.
- Planned capital investments allocate 628 million yen to the Kashiwabara Plant in Saitama Pref., Japan from Mar. 2018 to Mar. 2020 to respond to new orders and renew facilities.
- Planned capital investments allocate 504 million yen to the Suzuka Plant in Mie Pref., Japan from Mar. 2019 to Mar. 2020 to renew facilities and respond to new orders.
- Planned capital investments allocate 461 million yen to the Kameyama Branch in Mie Pref., Japan starting May 2019 and ending Mar. 2020 to increase production capacity and renew facilities.
- Headquarters in Saitama Pref., Japan will receive 148 million yen for operational management facilities from Jun. 2019 to Mar. 2020.
- The Tochigi R&D Center will receive 145 million yen for R&D facilities from Sep. 2018 to Jan. 2020 to install testing facilities.
- The Saitama R&D Center in Sayama, Saitama Pref. will receive 578 million yen for R&D facilities from Oct. 2018 to Mar. 2020 to install testing facilities and reduce energy consumption.
- Goshi Giken Co., Ltd. in Kumamoto Pref., Japan will receive 892 million yen for production and R&D facilities from Apr. 2019 to Mar. 2020.
- Uemura Tec Co., Ltd. in Kumamoto Pref., Japan will receive 146 million yen for production facilities from Apr. 2019 to Mar. 2020 to reduce energy consumption and streamline operations.
- Goshi Tec Co., Ltd. in Kumamoto Pref., Japan will receive 305 million yen for production facilities from Apr. 2019 to Mar. 2020.
- Planned capital investments allocate 4,084 million yen to US Yachiyo Inc. in Ohio, USA for production facilities from Mar. 2018 to Jan. 2020.
- Yachiyo Manufacturing of America, LLC in Georgia, USA is allocated 1,241 million yen for production facilities from Mar. 2019 to Dec. 2020 to renew facilities, reduce energy consumption, and streamline operations.
- Planned capital investments allocate 500 million yen to AY Manufacturing Ltd. in Ohio, USA for production facilities from Apr. 2019 to Mar. 2020.
