# Stow Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Belgium’s Stow Group Invests $36 Million to Create US Manufacturing Hub in Georgia - Food and Beverage Business](https://www.foodandbeverage.business/supply-chain/belgiums-stow-group-invests-36-million-to-create-us-manufacturing-hub-in-georgia/)  ·  `page_017943`

## Facts

- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp announced that Belgium-based Stow Group, a leader in industrial storage and automation solutions, will invest US$36 million (£26.8 million) to establish a new manufacturing facility in Gordon County, Georgia.
- The strategic investment will result in a 240,000 square foot manufacturing site located along Highway 41 near Adairsville.
- The facility is scheduled to commence operations in 2026.
- The new facility is poised to create approximately 200 jobs in the local community.
- Stow Group plans to hire for various production roles, including profiling, stamping, welding, powder coating, automation, and assembly.
- Additional job opportunities will be available in logistics, administration, and management.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Georgia’s reliable infrastructure network and strategic location ensure the state remains a gateway to opportunity, citing companies like Stow Group as a big reason why Georgia's robust logistics industry continues to deliver results across the state.
- Governor Kemp emphasized looking forward to the impact of this job creator on the community and critical industries throughout Georgia.
- Jos De Vuyst, CEO of Stow Group, stated that the new production facility in Georgia will further strengthen Stow’s presence and support network in North America and bring the company closer to existing and new customers.
- Expanding its production footprint in the United States is a key milestone for Stow Group, with the new Gordon County facility allowing the company to serve customers faster, enhance local partnerships, and continue its commitment to innovation and sustainability.
