# HEXPOL

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2018-09-12

**Source:** [HEXPOL acquires Kirkhill Rubber, a well-known U.S. Rubber Compounder - HEXPOL](https://news.cision.com/hexpol/r/hexpol-acquires-kirkhill-rubber--a-well-known-u-s--rubber-compounder,c2610479)  ·  `page_020981`

## Facts

- HEXPOL announced the acquisition of Kirkhill Rubber from founder Edward Reker, his family, and co-owners.
- Kirkhill Rubber is an industry leader with experience in aerospace, automotive, medical, and other demanding industry segments.
- Kirkhill Rubber has most recently expanded into silicone and perfluoroelastomer compounds.
- Kirkhill Rubber has annual sales of approximately 46 million USD.
- Kirkhill Rubber brings extensive knowledge in highly engineered elastomers, specialty equipment, and complete laboratory testing facilities.
- Kirkhill Rubber's state-of-the-art facility in Long Beach, California will be its sole manufacturing plant.
- Production at Kirkhill Rubber's Downey, California location will be transferred to Long Beach, California.
- Production at Kirkhill Rubber's Athens, Georgia location will be transferred to other HEXPOL sites in the US.
- The facilities in Downey and Athens are not included in the acquisition transaction.
- The acquired business has an EBITDA margin below the HEXPOL Group but is expected to positively impact earnings per share.
- Tracy Garrison, President of HEXPOL Compounding NAFTA, stated the acquisition complements HEXPOL Compounding in the US and strengthens their presence with rubber compounds through highly skilled people and advanced processes.
- Kirkhill's recent purchase of the Long Beach, California facility increases the ability to provide superior products and service to customers.
- Edward Reker, President of Kirkhill Rubber, will continue with HEXPOL after the acquisition.
- Edward Reker noted immediate benefits from access to HEXPOL’s global material reach, expanded development resources, and advanced quality systems.
- Mikael Fryklund, CEO of HEXPOL Group, described the acquisition as a step in expanding and strengthening the group within its core business.
- The acquisition price amounts to approximately 49 million USD on a cash and debt-free basis.
- The transaction is funded by a combination of cash and existing bank facilities.
- The acquired business will be consolidated into HEXPOL from September 2018.
- A smaller performance-based consideration will be paid later if certain criteria are met.
- HEXPOL is a world-leading polymers group with positions in advanced polymer compounds, gaskets for plate heat exchangers, and plastic/rubber materials for truck and castor wheel applications.
- HEXPOL's customers include systems suppliers to the global automotive and engineering industry, construction industry, energy/oil and gas sector, medical equipment manufacturers, and OEM manufacturers of plate heat exchangers and forklifts.
- HEXPOL is organized into two business areas: HEXPOL Compounding and HEXPOL Engineered Products.
- HEXPOL Group sales in 2017 amounted to 12,230 MSEK.
- HEXPOL Group has approximately 4,400 employees across eleven countries.
