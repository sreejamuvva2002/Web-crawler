# Yancey Engineered Solutions

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-25

**Source:** [Yancey Engineered Solutions Bringing 300 New Jobs to Crisp Co. | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-releases/2026/yancey-engineered-solutions-bringing-300-new-jobs-crisp-co)  ·  `page_002423`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Georgia-based engineering and manufacturing company Yancey Engineered Solutions will invest $5.7 million in a new manufacturing facility in Cordele.
- The investment will create 300 new jobs over the next several years in Crisp County.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Yancey provides solutions and services that keep Georgia moving, supporting everything from small businesses to large manufacturers across every industry.
- Governor Brian Kemp noted the new facility will provide direct opportunity for hardworking Georgians in Crisp County and help drive further growth statewide.
- Yancey Engineered Solutions was founded in 2004 and provides power generation packaging systems.
- Yancey Engineered Solutions is a division of Yancey Bros. Company, the authorized Caterpillar dealer for Georgia.
- Trey Googe, Yancey’s CEO, stated that Yancey Bros. has served Cordele and Crisp County for decades primarily with Caterpillar earthmoving equipment, parts, and service.
- Trey Googe expressed excitement to add new teammates from the Cordele/Crisp community and manufacture products here that help fill a need in a growing and important market.
- Yancey’s new facility will be located at 502 South Midway Road in Cordele.
- The company plans to refurbish the former Big Tex manufacturing facility.
- Yancey Engineered Solutions plans to hire welders, quality control technicians, assembly workers, and drivers.
- Cordele Mayor and Crisp County-Cordele Industrial Development Authority Vice-Chair Wesley Rainey stated that Cordele is excited by Yancey’s decision to redevelop the Big Tex facility and hopes to rehire many former employees of Big Tex.
- Wesley Rainey described the project as a great development for Cordele.
- James R. Dowdy, III, Chairman of the Crisp County Board of Commissioners and the Cordele-Crisp Industrial Development Authority, stated that the community is thankful for Yancey’s decision to land there.
- James R. Dowdy, III noted the project demonstrates that economic development efforts are paying dividends and are consistent with citizens’ visions of growth.
- Senior Regional Project Manager Christy Bozeman represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development on this competitive project in partnership with the Cordele-Crisp Industrial Development Authority, Crisp County Power Commission, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Yancey Bros. has shaped Georgia’s story for a century and is continuously evolving to serve industry needs.
- Pat Wilson noted that Yancey Engineered Solutions represents an exciting addition to Yancey’s portfolio that helps provide reliable energy to users.
- Yancey Bros. Co. is headquartered in Austell, Ga., and is recognized as the nation’s oldest Caterpillar dealer.
- Yancey Bros. Co. celebrated its Centennial anniversary in 2014 and has been family owned and operated for 107 years.
- Yancey Bros. Co. offers Caterpillar, Weiler, and Blue Bird equipment plus many other products, parts and services across the state of Georgia.
- The company operates through 29 facilities in 21 Georgia communities.
