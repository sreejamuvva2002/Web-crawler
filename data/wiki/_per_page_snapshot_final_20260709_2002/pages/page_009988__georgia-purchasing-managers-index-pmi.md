# Georgia Purchasing Managers Index (PMI)

**Type:** research_center  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2016-07-01

**Source:** [Georgia manufacturing remains sluggish in June - Coles College of Business](https://www.kennesaw.edu/coles/news/stories/07-01-2016-georgia-manufacturing-remains-sluggish-in-june.php)  ·  `page_009988`

## Facts

- Georgia manufacturing saw its second consecutive month in decline according to the Purchasing Managers Index (PMI) released by Kennesaw State University’s Econometric Center in June 2016.
- June’s PMI reading was 49.3, falling below the 50-point benchmark for growth for the second consecutive month.
- The decline was driven by anemic new orders and downward adjustments for production and employment.
- Higher monthly employment fell from 31 percent in April to 6.7 percent in June.
- Lower monthly employment rose from 6.3 percent in April to 13.3 percent in June.
- Higher reported production fell from 52.4 percent in March to 26.7 percent in June.
- Higher new orders, which were at 47.6 percent in March, fell to 26.7 percent in June.
- Don Sabbarese, director emeritus of the Econometric Center and professor of economics at Kennesaw State University, stated that improved consumer spending had not yet reached Georgia manufacturers.
- One third of PMI respondents reported lower new orders and production, while only 6.7 percent reported increased hiring.
- June’s new orders, production, and employment metrics registered 11.2, 14.1, and 8.1 points below their respective six-month averages.
- The National PMI reading for June was 53.2, an increase of 1.9 points from the previous month.
- The national report identified 13 industries expanding in June compared to 12 in May.
- June’s national PMI registered increases across all five of its underlying variables.
- A PMI reading above 50 indicates that manufacturing activity is expanding, while a reading below 50 indicates it is contracting.
