# Ecoplastic Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-01-05

**Source:** [Longtime Kia and HMG Supplier to Build Manufacturing Facility in Bulloch County | Development Authority of Bulloch County](https://advantagebulloch.com/news/article/longtime-kia-and-hmg-supplier-to-build-manufacturing-facility-in-bulloch-county)  ·  `page_000477`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced on January 5, 2023 that Ecoplastic Corporation will create 456 new jobs in a new manufacturing facility in Bulloch County.
- The company will invest approximately $205 million in the new facility.
- The company will serve the Hyundai Metaplant from Statesboro, where it will have close proximity to Georgia's ports system.
- Ecoplastic Corporation was established in 1984 and is a Korea-based company that manufactures plastic automobile parts for original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) and other suppliers.
- Ecoplastic is a Tier-1 supplier for Hyundai Motor Group (HMG) and Kia, engaging in the manufacturing of automotive interior and exterior plastic parts including bumpers, consoles, trims, and plastic molds.
- Shang Han, President and CEO of Ecoplastic, stated the company is establishing a production facility for electric vehicle parts in Bulloch County to provide a stable supply of high-quality products to client HMGMA.
- Ecoplastic’s new facility will be located at 4822 Highway 301 South Register near Statesboro.
- The new plant will be capable of injection molding and painting, producing exterior parts, bumpers and garnishes, interior parts, consoles, and trims for electric vehicles.
- The company expects to begin operations by October 2024.
- Ecoplastic will be hiring for HR, finance, parts development, quality control, facility maintenance, injection, and paint production positions.
- Billy Allen serves as the Development Authority of Bulloch County Chair.
- Alyce Thornhill, Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy at the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team, represented the agency on this competitive project.
- The project was developed in partnership with the Development Authority of Bulloch County, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia EMC.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Ecoplastic has decades of experience as a supplier for Kia and Hyundai and will help build the supplier network for the Hyundai metaplant in Bryan County.
- Georgia is positioned as a hub for the electric mobility industry, with more than 30 EV-related projects announcing more than $21 billion in investment and more than 26,300 jobs since 2020.
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America officially broke ground on October 25 on its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility in neighboring Bryan County.
- Offsite suppliers connected to the Hyundai project were predicted to invest approximately $1 billion in other locations around the state, and projects announced to date have already exceeded this amount.
- Ecoplastic Corporation was established in June 1984 as Apollo Ind Co., Ltd. and established its first Technical Research Center in 1987.
- The company was publicly listed on the Korean Stock Exchange in March 2000.
- Ecoplastic currently employs more than 770 employees and recorded sales revenue of more than $700 million in 2021.
