# U.S. Primary and Downstream Aluminum Industry

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_material  ·  **Published:** 2018-03-08

**Source:** [Aluminum tariffs have led to a strong recovery in employment, production, and investment in primary aluminum and downstream industries | Economic Policy Institute](https://www.epi.org/publication/aluminum-tariffs-have-led-to-a-strong-recovery-in-employment-production-and-investment-in-primary-aluminum-and-downstream-industries/)  ·  `page_053044`

## Facts

- Elixir Extrusions announced an extrusion press investment in Douglas-Coffee County, Ga., with $8 million in capital investment and 130 jobs created.
- Ford Motor Co. warned that rising costs for raw materials like steel and aluminum would add $1.6 billion to its costs for the year.
- U.S. manufacturing sector added approximately 176,000 jobs (including 2,700 in iron and steel production) since February 2018.
- Motor vehicles and parts industry employment increased by 8,500 jobs between February and October 2018.
- U.S. primary aluminum production is projected to increase by 67 percent (500,000 tons per year) between 2017 and the end of 2018.
- Three smelters are being restarted and another has announced a capacity expansion, bringing seven smelters in total to operation by the end of 2018.
- Restart and expansion projects in primary aluminum will create over 1,000 new jobs and generate over $100 million in new investment.
- 22 new and expansion projects in downstream aluminum industries will employ over 2,000 additional workers, generate $3.3 billion in new investments, and add nearly 1,000,000 tons of annual rolling and extrusion capacity.
- Shipments of all extruded products increased by 6.3 percent (279.8 million pounds) year-to-date in 2018 compared to the same period in 2017.
- Total sheet and plate shipments increased by 4.6 percent (336.4 million pounds) year-to-date in 2018.
- U.S. output of raw alumina and refined & processed aluminum increased 9.8 percent between February 2018 and October 2018.
- Output of rolled and extruded aluminum products increased 9.1 percent from February to September 2018.
- U.S. employment in the aluminum industries increased by 300 jobs since tariffs were imposed, with planned restarts and capacity expansions expected to create more than 3,000 jobs total.
- Critics claimed tariffs would eliminate hundreds of thousands of jobs in downstream industries, but no evidence of negative downstream effects was found in the U.S. economy to date.
- Can stock shipments declined by 33.3 million pounds (1.0 percent), a trend attributed to shifting consumer preferences rather than tariff impacts.
- The beer industry's private consultant report claimed the aluminum tariff would cost more than 20,000 jobs, but aluminum for beverage cans represents only 5.7 percent of manufacturers' cost of beer in cans.
- A Trade Partnership study predicted manufacturing employment would drop by 2,612 jobs due to tariffs, but actual data showed a gain of 176,000 jobs.
- Nonferrous metals employment dropped by 1,300 jobs, while iron & steel production gained 2,700 jobs between February and October 2018.
- Fabricated metals industry gained 28,600 jobs, and other transportation equipment gained 26,900 jobs in the same period.
