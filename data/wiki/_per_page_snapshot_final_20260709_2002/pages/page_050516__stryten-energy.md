# Stryten Energy

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-09-14

**Source:** [Stryten Energy vanadium redox flow battery on path to commercialization - pv magazine Global](https://www.pv-magazine.com/2023/09/14/stryten-energy-vanadium-redox-flow-battery-on-path-to-commercialization/)  ·  `page_050516`

## Facts

- Stryten Energy is a US-based battery technology company.
- The company recently installed a pilot-sized version of its vanadium redox flow battery (VRFB) at a facility operated by Snapping Shoals EMC, an electricity cooperative in Georgia, United States.
- The installed battery is a 20 kW/120 kWh VRFB with a recharge time of 7.5 hours and connected to the grid at 480V.
- The system consists of several modules with built-in safety features, each operating independently at 6.67 kW/40 kWh per cycle.
- Scott Childers, Vice President of Essential Power at Stryten Energy, stated the system was specifically designed and sized for an initial evaluation phase with Snapping Shoals to conduct long-duration testing of six hours or more.
- The vanadium electrolyte aqueous solution is a proprietary combination of sulfuric acid and vanadium oxides.
- The electrolyte is stable up to 50 C, but the system can be designed to mitigate external temperature effects beyond this range.
- The VRFB system is supported by remote management software that tracks and stores performance data.
- As product commercialization matures, Stryten Energy’s VRFB will be highly scalable to serve kW to MW needs for commercial, industrial customers, and full-scale utilities.
- The VRFB technology was originally developed with Storion Energy, a US-based startup that Stryten acquired last year.
- A prototype VRFB system is installed at a PV plant in the Colorado test facility known as Solar Technology Acceleration Center (SolarTAC).
- The SolarTAC prototype has already dispatched 30 MWh of energy over 1,100 cycles, demonstrating capability to store solar energy for on-demand deployment.
- Stryten Energy anticipates the VRFB will be commercially available in January 2025.
- The company plans a complete US-based supply chain and manufacturing of all components of the VRFB system, including the vanadium electrolyte.
- A manufacturing location has not yet been selected.
