# General Motors and Honda Alliance

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2020-09-03

**Source:** [GM and Honda Team Up to Build at Least Four Cars Together](https://www.thedrive.com/news/36155/gm-and-honda-team-up-to-build-at-least-four-cars-together)  ·  `page_008962`

## Facts

- Ford has Volkswagen as an EV partner.
- FCA will form Stellantis with the PSA Groupe.
- General Motors and Honda announced plans to link up in North America and jointly develop future products.
- The two automakers signed a preliminary, non-binding memorandum of understanding to form a business and technical alliance.
- The proposed partnership involves sharing vehicle platforms and propulsion systems, notably internal combustion, electric, and hydrogen fuel cell powertrains.
- This announcement expands on an April deal wherein GM agreed to lend its Ultium EV platform to Honda for two EVs aimed at the North American market.
- The alliance could signal the introduction of additional GM-based Honda EVs or vice versa.
- A GM spokesperson confirmed that Ultium will account for one of at least four platforms the two automakers will share.
- Powertrains for the shared platforms will likely match their company of origin.
- GM and Honda expect to work on more than four vehicle architectures together, with some led by GM and others by Honda.
- GM powertrains will be used in GM-led programs and vice versa.
- Planning discussions for vehicles to be sold under both GM and Honda brands will begin immediately.
- Engineering work is expected to begin in early 2021.
- Honda statements strongly suggest an effective merger of the two automakers' research and development wings is on the table.
- Collaborative R&D could also be used to advance other fields of automotive technology, specifically Advanced Driver Assist Systems (ADAS), infotainment, and vehicle connectivity.
- Groundwork for integrating each automaker's connected car systems was laid in April when GM confirmed the pair of Ultium-based Hondas would utilize HondaLink rather than OnStar.
- Honda will explore further co-development opportunities for future connected services, including GM's OnStar and infotainment.
- Consolidation of behind-the-scenes operations such as manufacturing and purchasing is acknowledged as a possibility, with Honda citing significant potential cost savings.
- James Cain serves as GM's Senior Manager of Communications.
