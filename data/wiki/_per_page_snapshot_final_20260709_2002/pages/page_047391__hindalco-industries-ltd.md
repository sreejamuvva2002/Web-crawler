# Hindalco Industries Ltd

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-07

**Source:** [Hindalco profit slumps on heat from Novelis fire | Company Business News](https://www.livemint.com/companies/hindalco-profit-slumps-on-heat-from-novelis-fire-11779464023292.html)  ·  `page_047391`

## Facts

- Hindalco Industries Ltd reported an almost 16% drop in net profit attributable to owners to ₹13,391 crore from ₹16,001 crore in FY25 for FY26.
- The company's FY26 earnings missed Street expectations despite higher metal prices.
- Profit fell well short of the ₹16,164 crore estimate in a Bloomberg poll of 28 analysts.
- In the fourth quarter, net profit attributable to owners dropped 51% to ₹2,597 crore, down from ₹5,283 crore a year ago.
- The decline stemmed mainly from ₹6,963 crore in one-off expenses linked to the Oswego plant disruption in New York last year.
- Operations of Novelis, a key supplier of rolled aluminium to can makers and auto companies, were affected by the fire.
- Novelis accounted for 59% of Hindalco’s revenue in FY26.
- Hindalco reported a 15% jump in consolidated revenue from operations of ₹2,74,944 crore on the back of higher aluminium sales and moderate copper sales in the domestic market.
- Managing director Satish Pai stated that the outage was largely timing-related, with current year headwinds expected to substantially recover in the next fiscal year.
- The company reported a record India business Ebitda of ₹22,671 crore in FY26, up 6% from FY25.
- Novelis saw Ebitda decline by 5% to ₹14,546 crore in FY26 as the fire incident shrank volumes.
- In the June quarter, Hindalco expects near-term input cost escalations of about 5% sequentially due to the West Asia war.
- Analysts expect higher metal prices to cushion the impact of cost escalations.
- Aditya Welekar, metals and mining analyst at Axis Securities, noted that earnings were largely impacted by the one-time fire incident at Novelis’ Oswego plant.
- Management has guided for a $1.7 billion restructuring expense, which will weigh on free cash flow, although 70–80% is expected to be recovered through insurance.
- The Oswego plant is set to restart, putting the company on a gradual path to recovery.
- In India, aluminium cost pressures are expected to rise in Q1 due to higher furnace oil and coal tar pitch prices, even as realizations remain firm.
- The near-term outlook remains steady as strong aluminium and copper prices should offset cost pressures while operations gradually stabilize.
- Hindalco plans a ₹12,000 crore capex in India for FY27.
- Novelis capex will be $2.3-2.4 billion, mostly at Bay Minette.
- Bay Minette is the site of a new $5.5 billion low-carbon aluminum recycling and rolling plant being built by Novelis Inc, expected to be completed this year.
- Satish Pai indicated that going into FY28, Novelis capex will sharply drop once Bay Minette is commissioned, shifting to maintenance capex.
- India capex will be much higher than ₹12,000 crore in the future due to ramp-ups in the copper smelter and other projects.
- In FY26, Novelis shipments declined to 3,557 kt from 3,757 kt in FY25, reflecting the impact of the Oswego plant disruption.
- The copper business reported a marginal dip of 1% in volumes, with metal sales at 487 kt versus 491 kt a year ago.
- Aluminium upstream operations showed a modest improvement, with shipments rising to 1,350 kt from 1,327 kt in FY25.
- Downstream aluminium shipments were up 11% to 446 kt, supported by steady operational performance and capacity utilization.
