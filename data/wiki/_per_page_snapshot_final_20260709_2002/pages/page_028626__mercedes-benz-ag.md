# Mercedes-Benz AG

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Mercedes-Benz Announces Expansion in Atlanta: Company to Establish Atlanta as North American Headquarters with Centralization of Key Corporate Functions and Investment in New Research & Development Hub](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20250521238035/en/Mercedes-Benz-Announces-Expansion-in-Atlanta-Company-to-Establish-Atlanta-as-North-American-Headquarters-with-Centralization-of-Key-Corporate-Functions-and-Investment-in-New-Research-Development-Hub)  ·  `page_028626`

## Facts

- Mercedes-Benz will move up to 500 corporate, technical and high-tech roles to its existing “1MB” facility in Sandy Springs.
- Mercedes-Benz will move up to 500 corporate, technical and high-tech roles to a new, multi-million dollar Research & Development hub in Metro Atlanta.
- The announcement establishes Atlanta as Mercedes-Benz’s headquarters in North America.
- This centralizes and unites key corporate functions driving the success of the U.S. market.
- Mercedes-Benz will establish Atlanta as its headquarters in North America by moving additional corporate roles to the “1MB” facility in Sandy Springs, Ga. from Farmington Hills, Mich.
- The company is making a multi-million dollar investment in a state-of-the-art Research & Development hub in Metro Atlanta for technical teams from various U.S. locations.
- The move of up to 500 roles further expands Mercedes-Benz’s longstanding presence in the Atlanta area.
- Jason Hoff, CEO, Mercedes-Benz North America, thanked the State of Georgia for its support in deepening Mercedes-Benz’s roots in the Atlanta area.
- The move is expected to be completed by August 2026.
- Mercedes-Benz opened its 1MB facility in 2018.
- Currently, there are approximately 800 Mercedes-Benz employees in Atlanta.
- With today’s announcement, up to 500 additional roles will move to Georgia.
- Brian Kemp, Governor of the State of Georgia, stated that Georgia continues to lead the way in the future of mobility and technical innovation, attracting world-class companies like Mercedes-Benz.
- Mercedes-Benz is co-locating its Research & Development and customer lifecycle-focused activities in the U.S.
- Financial services teams and corporate functions based in Farmington Hills will be co-located with current sales teams in Sandy Springs.
- Technical teams from various U.S. locations will move to a new Research & Development hub near Sandy Springs.
- The strategic investment in the Atlanta region will allow the company to leverage the rich engineering talent pool around institutions like Georgia Institute of Technology.
- The company aims to create new opportunities to collaborate with the region’s thriving startup ecosystem and established tech sectors.
- Mercedes-Benz announced it will bring production of the GLC SUV to the company’s Tuscaloosa, Ala. facility.
- The U.S. is a vital market for Mercedes-Benz, with sales of 374,100 vehicles, both passenger cars and vans, last year.
- The company is responsible for 11,100 direct jobs in the U.S., of which around 8,000 are in manufacturing.
- There are approximately 112,000 jobs across Mercedes-Benz’s business suppliers and service providers.
- Mercedes-Benz has a heritage dating back to 1888 in Long Island City, NY.
- As a major local investor, Mercedes-Benz has committed over $10 billion to production, infrastructure, R&D, design, and the dealer network.
- This includes $7+ billion in its Tuscaloosa, Ala., site and $560+ million in its Charleston, S.C., plant.
- The company supports more than 163,000 jobs across the country, including 11,100 direct jobs (of which around 8,000 are in manufacturing) and 28,000 dealership employees.
- Its nationwide presence currently spans 385 dealer partners and 24 corporate locations in 13 states.
- More than 4.5 million vehicles have left the Tuscaloosa plant over the past 28 years.
- Roughly sixty percent of annual output from Tuscaloosa is exported.
- The United States is the second-largest global market for Mercedes-Benz Sprinter vans.
- Since 2006, Mercedes-Benz Vans, LLC (MBV Charleston) has assembled more than 400,000 vans for delivery across North America.
- Mercedes-Benz works with around 400 suppliers in the United States.
- The company is sourcing local demand for steel and aluminum almost exclusively in the United States.
- The Tuscaloosa operation is the global hub for its SUVs (GLE, GLS, GLE Coupe, and Mercedes-Maybach GLS as well as the EQS SUV, EQE SUV, and the Mercedes-Maybach EQS SUV).
- Mercedes-Benz is making electric vehicle (EV) infrastructure investments of approximately $1.2 billion to expand a high-speed charging network in North America over the next six to seven years.
- The company launched its first EV charging location in Sandy Springs, Ga. in November 2023.
- In 2024, Mercedes-Benz sold 324,500 passenger cars and 49,600 vans in the U.S.
- The United States is the second-largest global market for Mercedes-Benz cars and vans.
- Mercedes-Benz currently supports over 700,000 finance and lease customers in the United States.
- The company maintains a portfolio of over $40 billion.
- The company has filed nearly 100 technology patents from the U.S.
- Mercedes-Benz introduced DRIVE PILOT, the first SAE-Level 3 system for conditionally automated driving approved for Nevada and California.
- The company actively supported communities with nearly 40,000 hours of company-supported volunteer work in 2024 alone.
- Mercedes-Benz AG is part of the Mercedes-Benz Group AG with a total of around 175,000 employees worldwide.
- Ola Källenius is Chairman of the Board of Management of Mercedes-Benz AG.
- The company focuses on development, production and sales of passenger cars, vans and vehicle-related services.
- It aspires to be the leader in electric mobility and vehicle software.
- Product portfolio includes Mercedes-AMG, Mercedes-Maybach, G-Class (all-electric models), and smart brand.
- In 2024 it sold around 2.4 million passenger cars and vans globally.
- It has more than 30 production sites on four continents.
- It is constructing and extending its global battery production network on three continents.
