# Novelis Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2022-01-11

**Source:** [Novelis to Build $365 Million Recycling Center to Support North American Automotive Customers and Reduce Carbon Emissions by More Than One Million Tons a Year :: Novelis Inc.](https://investors.novelis.com/news-events/press-releases/detail/65/novelis-to-build-365-million-recycling-center-to-support-north-american-automotive-customers-and-reduce-carbon-emissions-by-more-than-one-million-tons-a-year)  ·  `page_047403`

## Facts

- Novelis Inc. announced it will invest $365 million to build a highly advanced recycling center for automotive in North America.
- The new facility will have an annual casting capacity of 240 kt of sheet ingot.
- The facility is expected to reduce the company's carbon emissions by more than one million tons each year.
- The new recycling facility will be built adjacent to Novelis' existing automotive finishing plant in Guthrie, Kentucky.
- The center will enable Novelis to grow its closed-loop-recycling programs with more automotive customers in North America.
- Through closed-loop recycling, the company takes back aluminum remaining after automotive parts are stamped from sheets and remakes it into the same product for new vehicle production.
- The center will also have the capability to process aluminum from vehicles at the end of their lifecycle.
- Using recycled aluminum as input material requires only 5 percent of the energy used to make primary aluminum, thus avoiding 95 percent of the carbon emissions associated with production.
- Steve Fisher serves as President and CEO of Novelis, Inc.
- Novelis aims to be the world's leading provider of low-carbon, sustainable aluminum solutions that advance its business, industry and society toward the benefits of a circular economy.
- The new recycling center will be equipped with advanced shredding and sorting technology.
- The facility will include energy-efficient innovations to support Novelis' sustainability goal to reduce energy intensity by 10 percent by 2026.
- Groundbreaking for the recycling center is scheduled for early 2022.
- Commissioning of the facility is expected in 2024.
- When fully operational, the expansion will add approximately 140 new jobs in Guthrie.
- Novelis grand opened its automotive finishing plant in Guthrie in October prior to this announcement.
- The Guthrie automotive finishing plant currently employs 150 people and will grow to 190 over the next two years.
- Tom Boney serves as Executive Vice President and President of Novelis North America.
- Kentucky Gov. Andy Beshear stated that the investment helps Novelis achieve sustainability goals and supports carbon-reduction targets for automotive customers.
- Gov. Beshear noted that Novelis is proving to be a great fit for Todd County and the surrounding area.
- Novelis has a 37-year history of creating jobs in Kentucky and investing in local communities.
- Novelis employs 1,600 people in the state of Kentucky.
- In addition to the Guthrie automotive finishing facility, Novelis operates an aluminum beverage can recycling plant in Berea.
- Novelis also operates the Logan Aluminum joint venture in Russellville.
- Novelis Inc. is a global leader in the production of innovative aluminum products and solutions and the world's largest recycler of aluminum.
- Novelis had net sales of $12.3 billion in fiscal year 2021.
- Novelis is a subsidiary of Hindalco Industries Limited, an industry leader in aluminum and copper.
- Hindalco Industries Limited is the metals flagship company of the Aditya Birla Group, a multinational conglomerate based in Mumbai.
- The announcement contains forward-looking statements regarding the investment, emission reductions, and job creation, with risks detailed in Novelis' Form 10-K for the fiscal year ended March 31, 2021.
