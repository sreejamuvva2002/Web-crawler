# Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution Joint Venture

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-08-31

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution to Invest Additional $2B in | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-releases/2023/hyundai-motor-group-and-lg-energy-solution-invest-additional-2b-bryan-county)  ·  `page_000036`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution will invest an additional $2 billion in their battery cell manufacturing joint venture at the Metaplant in Bryan County.
- The additional investment raises the JV’s total investment value to more than $4.3 billion.
- The expansion will create another 400 new jobs.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that in a single year, they broke ground on the largest project in state history, landed multiple suppliers across the state for Hyundai's Metaplant, and welcomed LGES to Bryan County.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Georgia continues to make itself the e-mobility capital of the nation.
- Governor Brian Kemp noted that major investments ultimately go to hardworking Georgians in the form of paychecks, improved schools and infrastructure, and more.
- Governor Brian Kemp thanked Hyundai Motor Group and LGES for recognizing that Georgia is the No. 1 state for business.
- José Muñoz, president and global COO of Hyundai Motor Company and president and CEO of Hyundai and Genesis Motor North America, stated the incremental investment reflects a continued commitment to create a more sustainable future powered by American workers.
- José Muñoz stated Hyundai is proud to partner with LGES and grateful for the support of Governor Kemp and Georgia communities in driving operations as they work to be a global leader in the electrified mobility industry.
- In May 2023, Hyundai Motor Group and LGES signed a memorandum of understanding establishing LGES as the partner for Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America’s (HMGMA) onsite battery cell manufacturing JV.
- The EV manufacturing facility and the battery JV now represent an estimated $7.59 billion in investment and will create 8,500 new jobs for the region over the next eight years.
- Dong-Myung Kim, president and head of the Advanced Automotive Battery Division of LG Energy Solution, stated the investment underscores a dedication to driving America’s EV transition while bolstering the local economy through quality jobs.
- Dong-Myung Kim thanked the State of Georgia for support and expressed excitement to bring top-quality products and operational experience to grow with its communities.
- The 30 GWh facility will be able to support the production of 300,000 units of EVs annually at full operations.
- Hyundai Mobis will assemble battery packs using cells from the plant, then supply them to Hyundai Motor Group’s U.S. manufacturing facilities for production of Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis EV models.
- Carter Infinger, Chairman of the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority, stated the additional investment will bring 400 additional well-paying jobs for those in the Savannah region.
- Carter Infinger noted that continued investment by Hyundai Motor Group and announced suppliers like LG Energy Solution is truly remarkable and transformative for the region.
- Alyce Thornhill, Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy, represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project in partnership with the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority (JDA), Georgia Power, Georgia Ports Authority, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated Governor Kemp set a goal of Georgia becoming the top location for EV manufacturing in the United States, and the state is in a great position to claim that distinction.
- Pat Wilson stated Georgia continues to add to its industry-leading success by building an ecosystem that supports the entire EV supply chain and bringing jobs home to Georgians in every corner of the state.
- Pat Wilson noted that state and community partners like the Savannah-area JDA, utility providers, Georgia Ports Authority, and Georgia Quick Start create a collaborative, business-friendly environment for success.
- Since 2018, Georgia has pursued job creation along the entire EV supply chain, resulting in more than $25 billion in investments and the creation of over 30,000 jobs.
- Battery related projects have accounted for at least $11 billion in investment.
- In fiscal year 2023, job creation in the automotive industry increased by 324 percent when compared to FY21.
- Hyundai Motor Group is a global enterprise with about 250,000 employees worldwide and mobility brands including Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis.
- LG Energy Solution (KRX: 373220) is a split-off from LG Chem and a leading global manufacturer of lithium-ion batteries for electric vehicles, mobility, IT, and energy storage systems.
- LG Energy Solution has 30 years of experience in battery technology and is the top battery-related patent holder in the world with over 25,000 patents.
- LG Energy Solution's global network spans North America, Europe, Asia, and Australia, including JV facilities with General Motors, Stellantis N.V., Hyundai Motor Group, and Honda Motor Co., Ltd.
- LG Energy Solution aims to achieve carbon neutral operations by 2050.
