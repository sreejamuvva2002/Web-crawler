# Rivian Stanton Springs EV Plant

**Type:** Facility  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[vehicle-manufacturing|Vehicle Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** Stanton Springs, Georgia, Georgia, United States

## Overview

A new Rivian electric-vehicle manufacturing plant to be built on the Stanton Springs site in Georgia, financed by bonds issued by the Joint Development Authority of Jasper, Morgan, Newton and Walton counties.

## EV supply-chain relevance

The facility will produce electric vehicles in Georgia, adding significant manufacturing capacity to the state's EV supply chain.

## Investment

- **$5 billion** — Construction and operation of the Rivian electric vehicle plant

## Jobs

- **7,500 good paying jobs** — Construction begins early 2024; jobs created as plant ramps up

## Timeline

- **Nov. 6** — Special called meeting of the JDA voted to approve closing on bonds for the Rivian project (near unanimous decision).
- **Early 2024** — Construction on the electric vehicle manufacturing facility is scheduled to begin.

## Related organizations & people

- Joint Development Authority (JDA) — Financing authority / bond issuer
- [[rivian|Rivian]] — Parent company / project developer
- State of Georgia — Government agency providing grading work and HOPE grant funding
- Ben Riden — JDA member who opposed the bond closing vote
- HOPE Grants — State-funded scholarship program covering apprenticeship tuition
- Covington, Georgia — Location of new Rivian office announced in the statement

## Workforce & partners

- Stanton Springs Apprenticeship Program — Workforce training partner (Rivian and State of Georgia). Tuition covered by Georgia's HOPE grants; all other expenses paid by Rivian; goal to assemble an initial group to support future industrial maintenance needs.

## Key facts

- $5 billion investment and 7,500 good-paying jobs expected from the Rivian plant.
- Rivian secured an exclusive rental agreement granting long-term rights to the Stanton Springs property.
- Grading work will be completed by both the JDA and the State of Georgia in the weeks following bond closing.
- A new Rivian office will be opened in Covington, Georgia.
- Apprenticeship program tuition funded by Georgia’s HOPE grants; other expenses covered by Rivian.
- "It’s a great day in Georgia as we close and issue the bonds for the Rivian project," said the JDA statement.
