# Commonwealth of Kentucky EV Manufacturing Ecosystem

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2025-08-25

**Source:** [Kentucky becomes EV capital of America after $13 billion auto overhaul](https://www.usatoday.com/story/cars/technology/electric-vehicles/2025/08/25/kentucky-becomes-ev-capital-of-america/85824161007/)  ·  `page_052567`

## Facts

- Kentucky has attracted over $13.1 billion in electric vehicle (EV) related investments since 2020.
- Over the past five years, Kentucky has emerged as "the EV capital of the United States."
- The effort to bring electric vehicle manufacturing, battery making and other parts of the EV supply chain to the commonwealth has been a cornerstone of Gov. Andy Beshear's two terms in office.
- Since 2020, the state has seen more than $13.1 billion in EV-related investments, bringing a slew of promised jobs, as well, Beshear said Aug. 11.
- Ford Motor Co. announced a $2 billion investment into the Louisville Assembly Plant to create a new Universal EV Platform for assembly, bring a new four-door midsize truck EV, and secure 2,200 hourly jobs at the factory.
- Gov. Andy Beshear said: "Together, Kentucky and Ford are bringing the future of the EV industry to this world."
- Ascend Elements is a battery material manufacturer that landed a multimillion-dollar investment from the federal government to go toward production of battery materials for electric vehicles.
- Ascend Elements scaled back its plans for the Hopkinsville plant, canceling a $164 million federal grant for cathode active material manufacturing while retaining a larger grant for cathode precursor production.
- The EV battery company has faced legal disputes from contractors on the project due to payment issues.
- Ascend Elements expects to start operations in late 2026.
- Ford's $2 billion August announcement marks the first time in Louisville Assembly Plant's history that the plant will produce an electric vehicle, a midsize four-door EV pickup truck with a $30,000 starting price coming to consumers in 2027.
- Ford said the new-to-market truck will be "as fast as a Mustang EcoBoost" in acceleration of 0-60 miles per hour and have more passenger space than the competitor Toyota RAV4.
- Like many EVs, the truck will also feature a "frunk" or front trunk and have a truck bed.
- Ford announced the Louisville Assembly Plant will be home to its newest iteration of the assembly line, which the company has dubbed the "assembly tree."
- Ford is creating a new Universal EV Platform that will debut at the Louisville Assembly Plant and allow a family of "affordable" vehicles to be produced and scaled to meet customer demand.
- BlueOval SK is a nearly $6 billion joint venture EV battery production company bringing together the automotive history and innovation of Ford combined with the technology of South Korean SK On.
- BlueOval SK announced full production started Aug. 19.
- The facility is located in Hardin County and has promised to employ 5,000 workers.
- As of May there were roughly 1,400 employees at the battery park, according to documents filed with the federal government.
- The battery park is a twin plant facility, with Kentucky 1 currently employing people.
- Kentucky 2 is currently delayed as Ford previously said consumer demand for EVs was not where the automaker anticipated it would be.
- Ahead of starting full production in August, the plant faced a slew of health and safety concerns filed with state government agencies, including concerns over chemical usage, adequate PPE and a bat infestation.
- In 2022, Bowling Green was named as the future home for a planned $2 billion EV-related project.
- Envision AESC, a global battery technology company now known as just AESC, planned to produce battery cells and modules for multiple automakers.
- Envision AESC was expected to start producing batteries to power up to 300,000 electric vehicles annually by 2027.
- While the building shell has been constructed, the project has essentially been abandoned, with promised jobs and output not yet materialized in the commonwealth.
- Rivian, an electric vehicle manufacturer that builds sport utility vehicles and trucks, invested $10 million in Bullitt County in 2023 to expand its parts operations and remanufacturing facility.
- The Rivian facility is where employees reconstruct vehicle parts such as electrical hardware, drive units and battery systems.
- State law previously blocked direct-to-consumer sales from automakers in Kentucky.
- In late 2024, Tesla found a caveat to the state law and was able to obtain a dealer license.
- As of this summer, Tesla has been selling vehicles and offering leasing options from its Louisville location.
