## Facts

- Georgia plans to announce next week it has landed a factory from well-financed electric vehicle startup Rivian.
- Officials hope the deal will make the state a leader in the next generation of the automotive industry.
- Gov. Brian Kemp and Rivian officials are expected to hold a ceremony next Thursday to outline the project.
- Rivian has been in talks to develop a site along I-20 straddling Walton and Morgan counties about an hour’s drive east of Atlanta, between the cities of Social Circle and Rutledge.
- The deal would allow the company to ultimately produce 2,000,000 vehicles per year.
- The plant is expected to be built on a site that was previously used as a manufacturing facility for automotive automation robot workers.
