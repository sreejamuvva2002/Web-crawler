# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-10-26

**Source:** [Hyundai breaks ground on $5.5B electric car plant in Georgia | Taiwan News | Oct. 26, 2022 06:04](https://www.taiwannews.com.tw/en/news/4697952)  ·  `page_009215`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group broke ground on its first U.S. plant dedicated to making electric vehicles.
- The groundbreaking ceremony took place in Ellabelle, Ga.
- A robotic dog delivered a champagne toast at the ceremony.
- Georgia's governor and junior U.S. senator shared a brief bipartisan celebration ahead of high-stakes elections.
- Hyundai announced the $5.5 billion manufacturing plant in Bryan County west of Savannah five months prior to the groundbreaking.
- Vast areas of the site have already been cleared of trees.
- Euisun Chung, executive chairman of Hyundai Motor Group, said the plant will be the envy of the industry.
- The South Korean company plans to open the factory in 2025.
- The plant will employ at least 8,100 employees.
- The plant will produce up to 300,000 electric vehicles per year.
- Georgia officials say it's the largest economic development project the state has ever seen.
- Gov. Brian Kemp said: “This is transformational for the region, not just for people's children now, but for their grandchildren.”
- Gov. Brian Kemp added: “This is going to be a project that will resonate for generations to come.”
- The groundbreaking ceremony was held two weeks before the Nov. 8 election.
- Both Gov. Brian Kemp and Sen. Raphael Warnock face formidable challengers at the top of the ballot in the upcoming election.
- After the robotic dog delivered champagne flutes, officials joined other dignitaries in picking up shovels to turn ceremonial scoops of dirt.
- Kemp and Warnock shared a brief handshake after the ceremony.
- Sen. Raphael Warnock said: “I’d like to see more of this kind of collaboration, quite frankly.”
- Warnock noted that at the end of the day, they want to see more jobs in Georgia.
- His race with Republican Herschel Walker will help determine which party controls the U.S. Senate.
- Kemp worked with state and local officials to lure Hyundai to Georgia with a package of $1.8 billion in tax breaks and incentives.
- Kemp faces an Election Day rematch with Democrat Stacey Abrams.
- President Joe Biden has embraced boosting U.S. production and sales of electric vehicles as a key part of his strategy for slowing climate change and adding manufacturing jobs.
- The White House recently announced $2.8 billion in grants to ramp up production of EV batteries in 12 states.
- A sweeping climate and health care law passed by congressional Democrats in August includes incentives for manufacturing and purchasing electric vehicles.
- The law includes tax credits for EV buyers worth up to $7,500.
- Changes made to the tax credit by Congress limit eligibility only to electric vehicles produced in North America.
- Hyundai EVs will no longer qualify for the tax credit until the company starts production in Georgia.
- South Korea's ambassador to the U.S. told dignitaries at the Tuesday event that his country believes the revised EV tax credit unfairly excludes Hyundai as it commits to expanding EV production in America.
- Ambassador Tae-yong Cho said: “Korean companies are now at risk of being disadvantaged by the electric vehicle tax credit provision.”
- Ambassador Cho added: “I believe it is not good for Korea’s partnership, for the state of Georgia, and not even for the common cause of climate change by limiting the consumer choices.”
- Warnock voted in support of the Inflation Reduction Act that contained the revised tax credit.
- Warnock defends the law overall, saying it shows the U.S. is serious about investing in EV manufacturing.
- Warnock has introduced a bill that would push back the date the tax credit revisions take effect.
- Warnock has asked the Treasury Department to use “maximum flexibility” in adopting regulations to carry out the new criteria.
- Warnock said after the ceremony: “I will continue to work with Ambassador Cho as well as with other officials to do everything we can to perfect what is already clearly a win.”
- Georgia's other big deal for a $5 billion EV plant east of Atlanta has run into deeper problems.
- Electric truck maker Rivian announced last year plans to employ 7,500 workers in Georgia.
- A Morgan County judge last month rejected local governments’ plan to exempt Rivian from an estimated $700 million in property taxes.
- Rivian agreed to pay $300 million in lieu of taxes.
- State and local officials are weighing an appeal of the judge’s decision.
- The property tax break was a major piece of $1.2 billion in incentives offered to Rivian.
- Rivian is a California automaker that has seen its stock value decline sharply this year.
- Rivian recently announced a big recall to tighten a loose fastener that could affect drivers’ ability to steer.
- When asked if he thinks Rivian's Georgia plant will still happen, Kemp said: “I’m very confident.”
- Kemp added: “The vast majority of people want the Rivian project to come.”
- Kemp stated: “We’re excited about the whole EV marketplace here in Georgia.”
