# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-10-26

**Source:** [A Year After Groundbreaking, Hyundai Ramps Up Hiring at Electric Vehicle Factory - Global Atlanta](https://www.globalatlanta.com/a-year-after-groundbreaking-hyundai-ramps-up-hiring-at-electric-vehicle-factory/)  ·  `page_060611`

## Facts

- A year after breaking ground, Hyundai Motor Group has hired about 290 people at its Metaplant near Savannah.
- The company announced the hiring progress as it released photos and renderings of the factory being built along Interstate 16.
- Hired employees are referred to as “Meta Pros” in company parlance and are direct employees of Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA).
- These hires represent the first fruits of a promised 8,500 jobs on the site.
- The site will include a joint-venture battery facility with LG that accounts for the bulk of the financial investment.
- The employee tally does not include the 2,000 construction workers laboring to build the facility in Bryan County.
- Hyundai says supplier jobs will boost its total employment impact to 14,476 jobs.
- The company is racing to meet demand for made-in-America vehicles that qualify for the $7,500 tax credit outlined in the Inflation Reduction Act.
- A U.S. manufacturing base will provide regulatory certainty under the Biden administration.
- The Korean auto maker could face a changing market and new political risks when it comes online by early 2025.
- EV sales globally have cooled since the company announced its $5.54 billion investment in May 2022.
- Hyundai describes the new plant on its careers website as an idyllic place for employees who want to forge the future of car manufacturing.
- The company is hiring as construction continues.
- The plant will feature a highly-connected, automated and flexible manufacturing system where all processor production — order collection, procurement, logistics and production — will be optimized utilizing AI and data.
- Hyundai eschewed terms like “factory” in favor of “Metaplant,” according to the Atlanta Journal-Constitution.
- The Atlanta Journal-Constitution compared the Georgia plant with a much smaller Singapore factory sharing some of the same features.
- The company latched on to “Meta” because in science it can mean transformation or transcending, aiming to transform what an automotive plant is and transcend employee perceptions.
- The factory design includes parallel lights along the front of the building to allow interstate travelers to see vehicles moving from the paint area to general assembly by conveyor.
- An exhibition hall designed in a square pays homage to Savannah’s historic squares, complete with a courtyard and water features.
- The production floor features open layouts, indoor landscaping and respite zones with skylights designed to enhance collaboration.
- A solar parking lot with 1,878 spaces will produce 5.2 megawatts of energy, helping the plant reach its goal of being 100 percent powered by renewable energy at the start of mass production.
- The site includes a 41-acre ecological park with trails, tracks and sports fields.
- Construction progress shows that 27,045 tons of steel have been used in competing 81 percent of the facility’s framing.
- Oscar Kwon, CEO of HMGMA, stated they are extremely pleased with the design of their state-of-the-art smart plant and are eager to see the finished product.
- The 290-employee figure breaks down to 182 salaried and 108 hourly workers.
- So far, 53 Korean expatriates are on site to aid with the construction process and commissioning aspects of the plant.
