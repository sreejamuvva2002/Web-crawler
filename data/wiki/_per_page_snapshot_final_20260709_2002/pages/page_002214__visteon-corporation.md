# Visteon Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2014-05-02

**Source:** [EX-99.1](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1111335/000119312514187379/d721408dex991.htm)  ·  `page_002214`

## Facts

- Visteon Corporation announced an agreement to divest the majority of its global automotive interiors business to an affiliate of Cerberus Capital Management, L.P.
- The transaction represents the second phase in a three-phase exit strategy, with 90 percent of the interiors business now under contract, offer, or sold.
- The first transaction, involving the sale of Duckyang shares, has been completed.
- The divestiture aligns with prior guidance to fully exit the interiors business with an aggregate neutral value impact to Visteon.
- The move supports Visteon's strategic focus on core growth businesses: thermal management and cockpit electronic ecosystems.
- The transaction is subject to regulatory reviews and other conditions, with completion expected by December 31, 2014.
- Visteon completed the sale of its 50 percent ownership stake in a Korean automotive interiors joint venture, Duckyang Industry Co., Ltd., as part of the divestiture.
- The agreement to sell the Duckyang stake was announced on March 19 for approximately $24.1 million, plus approximately $6 million in dividends.
- The sold interiors operations generated revenues of approximately $1 billion in 2013.
- These operations produce automotive interior products including cockpit modules, instrument panels, door panels, and floor consoles supplied to global vehicle manufacturers.
- The divested operations encompass 15 manufacturing plants, five just-in-time assembly plants, and six engineering centers located in Europe, Asia, and South America.
- As part of the transaction, the Cerberus affiliate made an irrevocable offer to purchase the interiors business in France, prompting Visteon France to begin consultation with employee representatives.
- More than 4,000 manufacturing, engineering, and administrative employees are part of the business involved in the transaction.
- Under the agreement terms, Visteon will divest the interiors operations for nominal consideration and contribute up to $95 million to the business.
- The Cerberus affiliate will assume approximately $20 million of pension and other liabilities associated with the business.
- Visteon will retain ownership in certain real estate and other assets in South America and Europe valued at approximately $35 million in aggregate.
- Visteon will provide support services to the Cerberus affiliate following the transaction.
- Visteon agreed to provide a seller-backed revolving credit facility to bridge any shortfall of a targeted $90 million in external credit facilities if not available by closing, repayable by the buyer once additional committed facilities are secured.
- The $90 million financing is not anticipated to be utilized at closing and serves only to add liquidity to the business.
- The potential amount of the Visteon-backed revolver, if drawn, is difficult to assess but is not expected to exceed $20 million once all external credit facilities are in place.
- Timothy D. Leuliette, Visteon president and CEO, stated the transaction supports the intention to divest the interiors business and focus on core thermal management and cockpit electronic ecosystems businesses that enjoy market-leading positions in faster-growing segments.
- Dev Kapadia, a Cerberus managing director, expressed pleasure in making a long-term investment in Visteon's interiors business, citing its strong products, skilled workforce, excellent customer relationships, and solid manufacturing operations, including in emerging markets.
- Cerberus plans to use the acquired business as a platform to pursue additional acquisitions in the automotive interiors space after completing the transaction.
- Visteon designs, engineers, and manufactures innovative components and systems for virtually every vehicle manufacturer worldwide.
- Visteon maintains corporate offices in Van Buren Township, Michigan; Shanghai, China; and Chelmsford, UK.
- Visteon operates facilities in 29 countries and employs approximately 24,000 people globally.
- Visteon reported sales of $7.4 billion in 2013.
- Cerberus Capital Management, L.P., established in 1992, manages over $25 billion across distressed securities, private equity, commercial lending, and real estate strategies.
- Cerberus operates from headquarters in New York City with affiliate and advisory offices across the U.S., Europe, and Asia.
