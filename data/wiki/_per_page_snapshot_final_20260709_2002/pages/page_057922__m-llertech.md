# MöllerTech

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2016-10

**Source:** [MöllerTech plans $46M Alabama auto parts plant with 222 jobs - Made in Alabama](https://www.madeinalabama.com/2016/10/mollertech/)  ·  `page_057922`

## Facts

- German automotive supplier MöllerTech announced it will invest $46.3 million to build a manufacturing facility in Bibb County.
- The project will create 222 jobs in Alabama’s auto sector.
- Governor Robert Bentley stated that attracting investment and new jobs to rural counties is a key priority in Alabama's economic development strategy.
- Governor Robert Bentley noted that MöllerTech’s Bibb County facility represents the type of project the state has been targeting.
- Governor Robert Bentley mentioned new tools are available to help rural counties overcome economic development obstacles.
- MöllerTech plans to construct a 150,000-square-foot facility in the Scott G. Davis Industrial Park.
- The site is located near Interstate 20/59 and close to the Woodstock community in northern Bibb County.
- The facility will manufacture interior parts for Mercedes-Benz’s next-generation SUVs.
- These SUVs will be produced at Mercedes-Benz’s Alabama assembly plant following a $1.3 billion expansion.
- MöllerTech North American President and CEO Steve Jordan said the Bibb County facility will serve as the company’s flagship U.S. manufacturing facility.
- The facility will incorporate new ideas and best practices from other operations in the company’s global manufacturing footprint.
- After visiting various states and counties, the company chose to settle in Bibb County due to a good fit with MöllerTech's culture centered on people and local communities.
- Financial and personal support from the state and county helped influence the decision to locate in Bibb County.
- The Birmingham region competed for the project, which is codenamed “Helios,” against other sites across the Southeast.
- The Bibb County Commission, Alabama Department of Commerce, Economic Development Partnership of Alabama, Alabama Power Co., and Birmingham Business Alliance collaborated to bring MöllerTech to the region.
- Greg Canfield, secretary of the Alabama Department of Commerce, joined company leaders and local officials at an announcement ceremony at the Bibb County Board of Education.
- Bibb County provided 20 acres of land in the Scott G. Davis Industrial Park for the facility.
- Bibb County provided an abatement of non-educational sales and use taxes and property taxes.
- The state provided jobs and investment credits plus AIDT workforce training, all valued at $12.3 million over a 15-year period.
- Bibb County Commissioner Ricky Hubbard stated the project will be a great boost for the county and its workforce.
- MöllerTech will become the second largest manufacturing employer in Bibb County and the third largest overall employer.
- MöllerTech originated as a family copper trading and processing business in 1730.
- It represents the 18th German corporate investment in the seven-county Birmingham metro area, according to the Birmingham Business Alliance.
- These 18 companies will employ approximately 1,700 people once MöllerTech is operational.
- Brian Hilson, president and CEO of the BBA, expressed excitement about the project's impact on the local workforce and families.
- MöllerTech is the automotive arm of Germany-based MöllerGroup, which remains family-owned with 2,300 employees worldwide.
- Besides Mercedes-Benz, MöllerTech develops and produces interior parts for automakers including Audi, BMW, General Motors, Honda, and Toyota.
- The company has existing U.S. facilities in Elberton, Georgia, and Shelby Township, Michigan.
- The Alabama facility is located just six miles from the Mercedes plant in Vance.
- The company hopes to reach full production at the Alabama facility by the end of 2019.
