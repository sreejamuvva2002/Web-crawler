# Daechang Seat Corp. USA

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-10-23

**Source:** [Automotive Supplier Daechang Seat Corp. USA to Create 500 Jobs in Chatham County • The Georgia Virtue](https://www.thegeorgiavirtue.com/georgia-news/automotive-supplier-daechang-seat-corp-usa-to-create-500-jobs-in-chatham-county/)  ·  `page_018882`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Daechang Seat Corp. USA will invest $72.5 million in the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center.
- Daechang Seat Corp. USA is a supplier specializing in automotive seat frames.
- The new facility will be located in close proximity to Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA).
- The investment will create over 500 jobs.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated that the announcement highlights the broader impact of projects like the Hyundai Metaplant beyond their initial investment.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp noted that suppliers for the Hyundai Metaplant project currently reach at least nine counties.
- The manufacturing base is centered around Chatham County and the Savannah region.
- Daechang Seat Corp. (DSC) was established in 1979 in Kyungju, South Korea.
- DSC has become one of the largest seat frame producers in the world.
- Globally, DSC employs more than 5,000 people across seven countries.
- The company’s production process includes fully automatic lines with arc welding and spot-welding machines.
- Jinsuk Lee is the CEO of Daechang Seat Savannah Corp.
- Jinsuk Lee expressed appreciation to the State of Georgia, local communities, and the City of Savannah for creating employment prospects in Chatham County and the Savannah area.
- DSC's primary objective is to drive growth through facility expansions to become a global entity.
- DSC currently holds a prominent partnership with Hyundai Transys and Hyundai Mobis at HMGMA.
- The company will hire for assembly operators, equipment technicians, mechanical engineers, and electrical engineers.
- Hiring will occur through local job fairs and website postings.
- Griff Lynch is the President and CEO of the Georgia Ports Authority.
- Griff Lynch stated that the facility is located only minutes from the Port of Savannah.
- The Georgia Ports Authority aims to provide a global gateway for U.S. supply chains.
- Trip Tollison announced that Daechang Seat Corp. USA is the third Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America supplier to locate in the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center.
- Trip Tollison noted the project will bring 500 new jobs and $72.5 million in capital investment to Chatham County.
- Trip Tollison is the President and CEO of the Savannah Economic Development Authority.
- Alyce Thornhill is the Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy.
- Alyce Thornhill represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this project.
- The project was developed in partnership with the Savannah Economic Development Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- Pat Wilson is the GDEcD Commissioner.
- Pat Wilson stated that partners are helping transform Georgia into a center of the automotive and mobility industry.
- Savannah is considered an established global gateway.
- Chatham County has leveraged its location to create a community for living and working.
- Since 2018, Georgia has created more than $25 billion in investments for its EV supply chain.
- Since 2018, Georgia's EV supply chain pursuit has created 30,000 jobs.
- In fiscal year 2023, job creation in Georgia's automotive industry increased by 324 percent compared to FY21.
- Suppliers attracted by Georgia’s mobility OEMs resulted in over $2 billion in investment across the state in FY23.
