# Aurubis Richmond

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2024-09-30

**Source:** [Aurubis starts up Georgia smelter - Resource Recycling](https://resource-recycling.com/e-scrap/2024/09/30/aurubis-starts-up-georgia-smelter/)  ·  `page_001296`

## Facts

- German company Aurubis has brought its multi-metal recycling facility in Georgia online after two years of work.
- The $800 million facility, named Aurubis Richmond, is located in Augusta.
- It is the first-ever secondary smelter of its kind for multi metal recycling in the United States, according to a press release.
- The plant will have a processing capacity of more than 180,000 tons of complex materials annually, including circuit boards, copper cables and other metal-containing products.
- The plant will produce blister copper, which will later be processed into industrial and precious metals at the company’s European smelter sites.
- Some of the final product will be sold back into the U.S. market.
- Aurubis CEO Toralf Haag said the smelter will allow them to keep strategically important metals in the economy, making U.S. supply chains more independent.
- Haag added that the new site shows how Aurubis combines profitable growth and sustainable business activity, following their ambition to responsibly transform raw materials into value for an innovative and sustainable world.
- The original plan called for a smaller capacity, but in 2023 Aurubis decided to double the plant’s metals recovery capacity by adding a second recycling module.
- This first stage of the plant was scheduled to come online in the first half of 2024.
- The second stage is slated for 2026.
- David Schultheis, president and managing director of Aurubis Richmond, said the facility is going to become a major player in the North American multimetal supply chain.
