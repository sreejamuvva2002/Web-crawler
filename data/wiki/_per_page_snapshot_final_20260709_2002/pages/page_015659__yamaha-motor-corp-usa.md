# Yamaha Motor Corp. USA

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-02-01

**Source:** [Yamaha Motor's Impact in Georgia Outlined at Cobb International Reception  - Global Atlanta](https://www.globalatlanta.com/yamaha-motors-impact-in-georgia-outlined-at-cobb-international-reception/)  ·  `page_015659`

## Facts

- Yamaha has approximately 2,600 employees in Georgia.
- The company has operated in Georgia for nearly 40 years since opening its first factory there.
- In 1997, Yamaha spun off its music unit and created Yamaha Motor USA, which remains technically based in Cypress, Calif.
- The Georgia manufacturing facility is named Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corp. of America and is located in Newnan, Ga.
- The Newnan factory initially produced golf carts before expanding to wave runners, all-terrain vehicles, and side-by-side off-roaders.
- To date, the Georgia factory has produced 4.5 million units.
- North American CEO Michael Chrzanowski began his tenure at the Georgia facility more than 20 years ago.
- When appointed CEO of the North American operation in 2021, Mr. Chrzanowski chose to remain in Kennesaw rather than relocate to California.
- Sales and marketing functions for Yamaha's marine division relocated to Georgia due to proximity to the boating community and the state's welcoming business environment.
- A former parts warehouse on Chastain Road now serves as the hub for Yamaha's U.S. marine, motorsports, and golf cart operations, which are its three largest business units.
- The Kennesaw facility also functions as a major technical training hub.
- Upon taking the CEO role in 2021, Mr. Chrzanowski was told the company's footprint across two facilities in Kennesaw and Marietta would be sufficient, but space constraints emerged within two weeks.
- In April 2023, the company inaugurated a 75,280-square-foot Marine Innovation Center attached to its Kennesaw facility.
- The Marine Innovation Center trains representatives from 3,000 nationwide dealers and partners, as well as 2,000 technical specialists annually.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp attended the April 2023 opening of the Marine Innovation Center to celebrate Yamaha's commitment to leadership and innovation.
- The company operates 13 facilities across the United States, including three campuses in Georgia.
- Each Georgia Department of Natural Resources trailer is equipped with a Yamaha portable generator.
- The company has donated six G3 boats to the state of Georgia.
- Vehicles are donated annually from the Newnan plant to local communities, including ATVs to fire departments and golf carts for schools.
- In September, following Hurricane Helene, Yamaha donated 1,000 generators to assist residents in eastern Georgia and the Carolinas.
- Mr. Chrzanowski attributes Cobb County's selection for the company's initial investment to its educated workforce and strong transportation infrastructure.
- The Cobb Chamber’s annual International Company Reception featured trade representatives from Ireland and Japan, along with diplomats from the Bahamas, Hungary, Norway, Sweden, Slovakia, and Japan.
- Deputy Consul General Hiroyuki Kaneda praised Cobb County as one of Japan's most important partner counties in Georgia.
- The Georgia Japanese Language School, established in 1974 to help expatriates maintain their mother tongue, is based in Cobb County.
- The reception event was sponsored by The Manely Firm and Freeman Mathis Decisions.
