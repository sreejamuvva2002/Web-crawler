# Kia Georgia Inc. / Hyundai Mobis Wage Suit Settlement

**Type:** event  ·  **Role:** workforce_training  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Ex-Kia, Hyundai Workers Score $11.5M Deal In Wage Suit - Law360](https://www.law360.com/articles/2414376/ex-kia-hyundai-workers-score-11-5m-deal-in-wage-suit)  ·  `page_021356`

## Facts

- A joint motion filed Thursday by former production line employees, Kia Georgia Inc., Hyundai Mobis, staffing agencies Allswell and SPJ Connect, and SPJ Connect CEO Youngjin Lee asked a federal judge for preliminary approval of a settlement ending the workers' lawsuit.
- The lawsuit included Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA) and racketeering claims.
- Subject to court approval, the defendants reached a nonreversionary settlement of $11.5 million for approximately 614 class members.
- The $11.5 million settlement total includes $3.45 million in attorney fees and expenses.
- FLSA settlement collective members, including named plaintiffs, will receive an average of $1,672.61, totaling $999,870.00.
- The $999,870.00 payment is a pro-rated amount based on the number of workweeks each collective member worked for the defendants.
- The parties described the proposed settlement as fair and reasonable, resulting from lengthy settlement negotiations.
- Separately, the workers and third defendant staffing agency Job Knowledge filed a motion Friday seeking approval for a separate settlement providing $7,500 to named plaintiff Aaron Hernán Pérez Salazar and $5,000 to each remaining plaintiff with claims against Job Knowledge, totaling $27,500.
- The parties argued the separate Job Knowledge settlement is reasonable, provides substantial recovery, and would reduce litigation complexity, expense, and duration.
- The original suit was initially filed in August 2022 with only one named plaintiff: engineer and electromagnetics technician Jorge Oswaldo Aquino Martinez.
- Aquino Martinez alleged that Allswell and SPJ Connect helped him secure a nonimmigrant TN visa, which is reserved for Canadians and Mexicans working in the U.S. under the United States-Mexico-Canada Agreement.
- He alleged he came to the U.S. based on promises to work at Hyundai as an engineer but was assigned manual labor installing car dashboards and paid "substantially less per hour" than his white, American co-workers.
- The latest version of the complaint alleges violations including an FLSA collective action claim and federal and state Racketeering Influenced and Corrupt Organizations Act (RICO) claims.
- Representatives for the parties did not respond immediately to requests for comment.
- The workers are represented by Daniel Werner and James Radford of Radford Scott LLP, Rachel B. Benjamin and Brian J. Sutherland of Beal Sutherland Berlin & Brown LLC, Christopher B. Hall of Hall & Lampros LLP, and Julia Solórzano and Abigail R. Kerfoot of Centro De Los Derechos Del Migrante Inc.
- Hyundai Mobis is represented by Jon M. Gumbel, Ingu Hwang, Michael L. Lucas, and Allison Hawkins of Burr & Forman LLP.
- Kia Georgia Inc. is represented by Sarah M. Phaff and Frank B. Shuster of Constangy Brooks Smith & Prophete LLP.
- Allswell and SPJ Connect are represented by John S. Gibbs II, Charles E. Peeler, and Emily E. Schifter of Troutman Pepper Locke LLP.
- Job Knowledge is represented by Charles K. McKnight Jr. and Nicholas F. McDaniel of Taylor Duma LLP.
- The case is Martinez et al. v. Mobis Alabama LLC et al., case number 3:22-cv-00145, in the U.S. District Court for the Northern District of Georgia.
