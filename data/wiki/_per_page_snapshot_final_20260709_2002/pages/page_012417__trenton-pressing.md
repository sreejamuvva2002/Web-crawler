# Trenton Pressing

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Trenton Pressing Success Story | GaMEP at Georgia Tech](https://gamep.org/success-story/trenton-pressing/)  ·  `page_012417`

## Facts

- Trenton Pressing is a metal stamping, welding, and assembly company located in Trenton, Georgia.
- The company operates with 220 employees.
- It supplies a number of large automotive OEMs.
- It also supplies customers in other industries, such as the recreational vehicle industry.
- In 2020, current owners purchased the company with the intent to modernize, streamline, and make it more profitable while maintaining jobs for the existing workforce.
- Hans Pfunder serves as COO of Trenton Pressing.
- John Zegers is the Northwest Georgia region manager for Georgia Manufacturing Extension Partnership (GaMEP) at Georgia Tech.
- Pfunder explained plans to implement new technology to improve efficiency and communication.
- Zegers connected Trenton Pressing with Sean Madhavaraman, a GaMEP technology project manager.
- Madhavaraman identified an opportunity to connect legacy machinery to a Manufacturing Execution System (MES) using sensors and other Industry 4.0 technologies.
- The company utilized various types of stamping presses, robotic welding cells, and other machinery that required real-time monitoring for output, efficiency, and downtime.
- Machinery inherited from previous owners lacked real-time monitoring capabilities.
- Pfunder worked with Madhavaraman to create a roadmap to connect the machinery and train employees on using the new data.
- Engineering and maintenance teams installed new monitoring equipment and utilized software to allow real-time data tracking.
- The collected data was used to predict equipment failure before it occurred.
- This capability allowed the maintenance team to implement preventative maintenance and reduce downtime.
- The data was also used to optimize production planning and improve employee experience.
- Pfunder stated that employees like knowing how they are performing and how their work contributes to overall company success.
- Using the new technology improved employee work/life balance by eliminating unexpected schedule changes and weekend shifts, according to Pfunder.
- Operational improvements allowed the company to ship substantially more units.
- The improvements freed up additional capacity for the company to take on new customers.
- Marco Linner serves as director of business development at Trenton Pressing.
- During a check-in visit, Linner discussed an idea to diversify their business by taking on more complex, lower-volume projects that could develop into long-term partnerships.
- Within a week, Zegers learned that Plotmaster Systems, an agricultural equipment company in Sandersville, Georgia, was looking for a contract manufacturer.
- Plotmaster had been working on product design with Bob Wray, a GaMEP technology project manager.
- Plotmaster requested help sourcing a manufacturing partner with the right capabilities to start production quickly.
- Zegers connected Trenton Pressing and Plotmaster Systems.
- Linner met with Plotmaster and confirmed the product was a good business fit.
- The product is now in production at Trenton Pressing.
- The first units were delivered just three months after initial introduction, ahead of schedule.
- Both companies hope to produce more products together in the future.
- By working with GaMEP, Trenton Pressing implemented an MES and used collected data to improve OEE by nearly 40 percentage points in one year.
- The company increased units shipped from its largest production line by 50%.
- Shifts were reduced from two full shifts per day to one and a quarter.
- Trenton Pressing connected with a new strategic customer and began producing products together within three months.
- The company invested $12 Million in a new 100,000 sq. ft. facility to support future growth plans.
- Pfunder stated that process transparency helped by GaMEP allows them to calculate ROI much better.
- Pfunder noted they now have a world-class OEE, enabling them to service customers better and take on new projects.
- Linner stated that GaMEP helped them become a better manufacturer and offer their capabilities to others.
- Linner expressed pride in manufacturing in the USA and helping other innovative companies produce products locally.
