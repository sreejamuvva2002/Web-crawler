# Novelis Inc.

## Facts

- Novelis Inc. is a leading sustainable aluminum solutions provider and the world leader in aluminum rolling and recycling.
- Steve Fisher serves as president and CEO of Novelis Inc.
- Novelis reported fourth quarter and full fiscal year 2026 financial results.
- Net sales for the fourth quarter of fiscal year 2026 increased 4% versus the prior year period to $4.8 billion, mainly driven by higher average aluminum prices.
- Rolled product shipments decreased 12% to 844 kilotonnes in the fourth quarter of fiscal year 2026.
- Lower fourth quarter shipments were driven by Oswego production disruption and softness in some specialty markets due to geo-political conditions.
- Net loss attributable to common shareholders was $84 million in the fourth quarter of fiscal year 2026, compared to a net income of $294 million in the prior year period.
- The quarterly net loss decrease was primarily due to $630 million in pre-tax net losses related to the Oswego fires, partially offset by favorable metal price lag from rising average local market aluminum premiums.
- Net income attributable to common shareholders, excluding special items, was down 13% year-over-year to $227 million in the fourth quarter.
- Adjusted EBITDA decreased 3% year-over-year to $459 million in the fourth quarter of fiscal year 2026.
- Fourth quarter adjusted EBITDA was impacted by an estimated negative $53 million resulting from production interruptions at Oswego and a net negative tariff impact, partially offset by lower aluminum scrap prices and higher Sierre flood insurance recoveries.
- Net sales increased 7% versus the prior year to $18.4 billion in full fiscal year 2026, primarily driven by higher average aluminum prices.
- Rolled product shipments decreased 5% to 3,557 kilotonnes for full fiscal year 2026 due mainly to the Oswego fires.
- Fiscal year 2026 net income attributable to common shareholders decreased 98% versus the prior year to $15 million, primarily driven by the Oswego fires, restructuring charges, and unrealized derivative losses.
- Net income attributable to common shareholders, excluding special items, was down 38% year-over-year to $476 million for full fiscal year 2026.
- Adjusted EBITDA decreased 9% to $1.6 billion in fiscal year 2026 driven by an estimated negative $104 million resulting from the Oswego fires and $143 million from tariffs.
- Full year adjusted EBITDA was partially offset by higher product pricing, lower SG&A costs, and favorable foreign exchange.
- Net cash used in operating activities was an outflow of $193 million in fiscal year 2026, compared to a net cash inflow of $951 million in the prior year period.
- The operating cash flow outflow was largely related to impacts from the Oswego fires and higher working capital from rising aluminum prices.
- Adjusted free cash flow was an outflow of $2.4 billion in fiscal year 2026, compared to a prior year period outflow of $737 million.
- The reduction in adjusted free cash flow is mainly driven by impacts from the Oswego fires and a 39% increase in total capital expenditures to $2.3 billion in fiscal year 2026.
- Total capital expenditures were mainly related to the Company's U.S. greenfield rolling and recycling plant in Bay Minette, Alabama.
- The Bay Minette plant is expected to commission in the second half of this calendar year.
- The Company had a net leverage ratio (Adjusted Net Debt / trailing twelve months Adjusted EBITDA) of 4.1x at the end of fiscal year 2026.
- Total liquidity stood at $2.8 billion as of March 31, 2026, consisting of $1.3 billion in cash and cash equivalents and $1.5 billion in availability under committed credit facilities.
- Dev Ahuja serves as executive vice president and CFO of Novelis Inc.
- Novelis expects to return to positive free cash flow by the end of fiscal 2027, setting a firm path towards deleveraging.
- A fire broke out at the Novelis plant in Oswego, New York on September 16.
- A second significant fire occurred at the Oswego plant on November 20 in a location where repair work from the September fire was taking place.
- Everyone working at the Oswego plant was safely evacuated with no injuries to employees, contractors or first responders during either event.
- Both Oswego fire events were contained to the hot mill area and did not impact the rest of the plant.
- Novelis expects the Oswego hot mill to be back in service in the next few weeks, positioning the company to support pent-up demand and normalize shipments over time.
