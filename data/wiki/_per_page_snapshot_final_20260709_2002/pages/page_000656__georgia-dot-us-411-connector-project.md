# Georgia DOT US 411 Connector Project

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2010-03-18

**Source:** [Open house for proposed U.S. 411 connector in Bartow Co. | WGAA Radio](https://www.wgaaradio.com/open-house-for-proposed-us-411-connector-in-bartow-co/)  ·  `page_000656`

## Facts

- Georgia DOT scheduled a public information open house for its proposed US 411 Connector project in Bartow County on Thursday, March 18, 2010, from 4 to 7 p.m.
- The meeting was held at the Cartersville Civic Center, located at 435 West Main Street in Cartersville.
- The proposed project calls for a new US 411 to I-75 connection by constructing a rural freeway on a new location between the existing US 411/US 41 interchange and State Route (SR) 20 east of I-75.
- Plans include a new I-75 interchange as part of the project.
- Interchange improvements are also planned at US 41.
- The project would begin at the intersection of US 411 and US 41 and extend to SR 20 east of I-75, covering a distance of 7.31 miles.
- Bartow and Floyd County residents were encouraged to attend the meeting to express their thoughts and preferences on the proposed project.
