# Boogook Industries

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-06-28

**Source:** [South Korean factory should bring 80 jobs to Macon. Here’s where, when it will open](https://www.yahoo.com/news/south-korean-factory-bring-80-180714829.html)  ·  `page_053108`

## Facts

- A South Korean automotive supply company announced it would open a facility in Macon, according to a press release from the Macon-Bibb County Industrial Authority.
- Boogook Industries will redevelop the old Brown and Williamson cigarette plant on Weaver Road near the Macon Downtown Airport in the industrial area of east Bibb.
- The plant is expected to open in 2024.
- The facility will create 80 full-time jobs in Macon.
- Job fields will include management, manufacturing, assembly, and delivery.
- Boogook committed to investing a total of $6.8 million into the project.
- Boogook specializes in fluid containers for cars.
- The company chose the Macon location because of the nearby Hyundai plant in Savannah and Kia plant in West Point.
- This facility will be the first American plant for Boogook.
- The former cigarette factory was one of the largest employers in Macon, with Brown and Williamson employing 3,000 workers at its peak in 1997.
- Mayor Lester Miller stated that the location's access to interstates and highways makes it a prime spot for industries needing quick and efficient product movement.
- Mayor Lester Miller thanked the Industrial Authority and welcomed Boogook Georgia as the newest member of Team Macon-Bibb.
- Joong Suk Oh, Chief Executive Officer of Boogook Georgia, stated that leadership recognized an opportunity to support Korean automotive manufacturing partners more locally.
- Joong Suk Oh noted that expanding operations to Macon will drive their ability to serve the Southeast U.S. and expressed anticipation for a successful partnership with the MBCIA.
- The former cigarette plant closed roughly 16 years ago prior to this announcement.
- The property was sold to Los Angeles-based Industrial Realty Group for redevelopment in 2021.
- It remains unclear how Industrial Realty Group developed the property.
