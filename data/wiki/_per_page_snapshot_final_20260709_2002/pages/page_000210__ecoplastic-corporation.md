# Ecoplastic Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-01

**Source:** [Longtime Kia and HMG Supplier to Build Manufacturing Facility in Bulloch County – Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority](https://savannahjda.com/2023/01/longtime-kia-and-hmg-supplier-to-build-manufacturing-facility-in-bulloch-county/)  ·  `page_000210`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Ecoplastic Corporation will create 456 new jobs.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Ecoplastic Corporation will invest approximately $205 million in a new manufacturing facility in Bulloch County.
- Ecoplastic Corporation is an automotive parts manufacturer.
- Ecoplastic Corporation was established in 1984.
- Ecoplastic Corporation is a Korea-based company.
- Ecoplastic manufactures plastic automobile parts for original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) and other suppliers.
- Ecoplastic is a Tier-1 supplier for Hyundai Motor Group (HMG) and Kia.
- Ecoplastic engages in the manufacturing of automotive interior and exterior plastic parts, including bumpers, consoles, trims, and plastic molds.
- Shang Han, President and CEO of Ecoplastic, stated the company is honored to establish a production facility for electric vehicle parts in Bulloch County, Georgia.
- Shang Han noted the facility will increase new jobs within the area and provide a stable supply of high-quality products to client HMGMA.
- Shang Han expressed appreciation for the support of State and County governments.
- Ecoplastic’s new facility will be located at 4822 Highway 301 South Register near Statesboro.
- The new plant is capable of injection molding and painting.
- The plant will produce exterior parts, bumpers and garnishes, interior parts, consoles, and trims for electric vehicles.
- The company expects to begin operations by October 2024.
- The company will be hiring for HR, finance, parts development, quality control, facility maintenance, injection, and paint production positions.
- Development Authority of Bulloch County Chair Billy Allen stated they are honored to welcome Ecoplastic to Bulloch County.
- Billy Allen stated they are pleased that the important Hyundai supplier has chosen their community.
- Alyce Thornhill, Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy, represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The project was in partnership with the Development Authority of Bulloch County, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia EMC.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated Ecoplastic has decades of experience as a supplier for Kia and Hyundai.
- Pat Wilson noted they are building the supplier network for the Hyundai metaplant in Bryan County.
- Pat Wilson highlighted the project provides well-paying, high-tech manufacturing jobs for Georgians.
- Georgia is positioned as a hub for the electric mobility industry.
- Since 2020, more than 30 EV-related projects have announced more than $21 billion in investment and more than 26,300 jobs in Georgia.
- On October 25, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America officially broke ground in neighboring Bryan County on its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility.
- Offsite suppliers connected to the Hyundai project were predicted to invest approximately $1 billion in other locations around the state.
- Projects announced to date have already exceeded this predicted amount.
- Ecoplastic Corporation was established in June 1984 as Apollo Ind Co., Ltd.
- It established its first Technical Research Center in 1987.
- It was publicly listed on the Korean Stock Exchange in March 2000.
- It currently employs more than 770 employees.
- It recorded sales revenue of more than $700 million in 2021.
