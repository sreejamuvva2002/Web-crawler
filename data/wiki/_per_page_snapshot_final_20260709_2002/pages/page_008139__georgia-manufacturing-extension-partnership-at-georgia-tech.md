# Georgia Manufacturing Extension Partnership at Georgia Tech

**Type:** workforce_training  ·  **Role:** workforce_training  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Georgia Manufacturing Extension Partnership at Georgia Tech](https://gamep.org/)  ·  `page_008139`

## Facts

- GaMEP works with manufacturers across Georgia to implement on-site projects, conduct training, and build connections with Georgia Tech resources and partners throughout the state.
- The organization divides the state into ten regions led by region managers who live and work within the region they serve.
- GaMEP project managers stay updated on the latest tools and technology to help manufacturers think strategically, operate more efficiently, and grow at a sustainable pace.
- Services offered include Business Health & Strategic Planning, Leadership & Team Development, Human Resources, and Technology.
- Technology project managers provide expertise in robotics, automation, artificial intelligence, sensors and data, and more.
- The program assists small manufacturers with few existing technology applications as well as mid-size to large manufacturers looking to add advanced systems to their plants.
- GaMEP offers Advanced Manufacturing services to help companies successfully implement and sustain advanced manufacturing automation and equipment projects.
- Industry 4.0 experts assist manufacturers in identifying and applying data and artificial intelligence solutions to maximize productivity and boost revenue growth.
- Cybersecurity services help companies identify electronic security risks and develop a cybersecurity plan.
- In 2025, GaMEP reported 1,043 jobs created and retained.
- In 2025, GaMEP reported $366 Million in new and retained sales.
- In 2025, GaMEP reported $15 Million in reduced operating costs.
- In 2025, GaMEP reported $43 Million in plant investments.
