# Aspen Aerogels, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2026-04-20

**Source:** [Earnings call: Aspen Aerogels raises outlook after strong Q3 performance By Investing.com](https://www.investing.com/news/transcripts/earnings-call-aspen-aerogels-raises-outlook-after-strong-q3-performance-93CH-3712830)  ·  `page_052726`

## Facts

- Aspen Aerogels reported Q3 2024 revenues of $117.3 million, marking a 93% increase year-over-year.
- The company raised its full-year 2024 revenue outlook to $450 million and adjusted EBITDA to $90 million.
- Gross profit margin improved to 42%, with adjusted EBITDA of $25.4 million for the quarter.
- The company reported a net loss of $13 million for Q3 2024, including a $27.5 million loss on debt extinguishment.
- Aspen Aerogels announced a new OEM supply agreement with Mercedes-Benz for PyroThin Thermal Barriers, with production expected to begin in 2027.
- The Mercedes-Benz award is through ACC, a battery cell joint venture between Stellantis, Saft, TotalEnergies, and Mercedes-Benz.
- Earlier awards from ACC are for Stellantis' STLA medium platforms, expected to ramp in late 2025.
- The company aims to double its Energy Industrial business size within five years.
- EV Thermal Barrier revenue reached $90.6 million in Q3, up 176% year-over-year.
- Aspen Aerogels has over $113 million in cash and a conditional commitment for a $670 million loan from the U.S. Department of Energy's Loan Programs Office.
- The company is preparing for future EV demand, noting that Plant 2 requires an additional $200 million of demand to be viable.
- Project costs for Plant 2 in Georgia have risen to an estimated $800 million to $960 million.
- Initial production expectations for the Audi platform have been delayed to the second half of next year.
- The U.S. Department of Energy awarded Aspen Aerogels a $7.3 million R&D grant dedicated to advancing aerogel technology for battery materials, specifically fast-charging high-power LFP cathode materials.
- Aspen is partnering with Oak Ridge National Laboratory under the DOE grant to validate and scale the fast-charging LFP technology from lab to pilot.
- The Statesboro aerogel manufacturing plant (Plant 2) is expected to have a revenue capacity between $1.2 billion and $1.6 billion.
- Plant 2 is designed to be accretive to margins and generate positive cash flow at moderate capacity utilization levels, requiring only $200 million of revenues within the EV Thermal Barriers segment to break even.
- The company spent $316.3 million in cumulative capital expenses on Plant 2 in Georgia to date.
- Aspen Aerogels spent $36 million year-to-date advancing construction of Plant 2 in Statesboro, Georgia.
- The company is targeting Q1 of next year (2025) to close the DOE loan and restart construction on the Statesboro plant.
- On October 21, Aspen Aerogels closed an underwritten public offering with net proceeds of $93.2 million.
- As of October 31, the company's cash balance was $203 million.
- The company expects its EV Thermal Barrier segment to deliver $315 million of revenues in 2024, a 31% increase over the prior outlook.
- The Energy Industrial segment is expected to supply $135 million of product in 2024, with at least $42 million in Q4.
- General Motors confirmed plans to produce 200,000 EVs for its own brands in 2024, with Aspen leading Ultium-based production expectations unchanged at 225,000 vehicles.
- The company's diluted EPS outlook improved by $0.02 per share to $0.11 per share on a post-offering share count of 84 million shares.
- CAPEX excluding Plant 2 is expected to be reduced by $5 million to $40 million for the year.
- The company's market capitalization stands at $1.29 billion.
- Don Young serves as President and CEO of Aspen Aerogels.
- Ricardo C. Rodriguez serves as Chief Financial Officer and Treasurer.
- Neal Baranosky is the Senior Director, Head of Investor Relations and Corporate Strategy.
