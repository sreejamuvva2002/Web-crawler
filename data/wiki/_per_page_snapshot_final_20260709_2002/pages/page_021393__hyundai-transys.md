## Facts

- Hyundai Transys is a global automotive parts manufacturer and a subsidiary of the Hyundai Motor Group.
- Hyundai Transys specializes in chassis systems, drivetrain components, and automotive seating systems.
- Hyundai Transys operates a Georgia-based seating production facility located in Montgomery County near Atlanta.
- The Georgia facility supports just-in-time manufacturing logistics by supplying completed seat assemblies directly to final assembly lines.
- The facility supplies factory-installed seats for vehicles assembled at the Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Alabama (HMMA) plant in Montgomery.
- There is a high probability that original seats for Hyundai Sonata, Santa Fe, or Palisade models built in Alabama were produced by Hyundai Transys at its Georgia plant.
- The domestic seating supplier reduces shipping time and cost between parts suppliers and final assembly plants.
- Factory-integrated seats meet strict OEM specifications for fit, durability, and safety compliance.
- The facility ensures trim-level consistency across models with leather, heated, ventilated, or power-adjustable features.
- Hyundai Transys does not sell seats directly to consumers but supplies automakers.
- Engineers collaborate with Hyundai Transys early in the development cycle to integrate seating into overall interior architecture.
- Seating integration decisions include frame structure and mounting points, adjustment mechanisms (manual, electric, memory functions), material selection (fabric, synthetic leather, genuine leather), advanced features like lumbar support, heating, ventilation, and massage, and compatibility with airbag systems and seatbelt pre-tensioners.
- Differences in wiring harnesses, bracket alignment, and electronic controls between trims can prevent direct seat swaps without modification.
- Power seat motors may use different pin configurations, heated seat modules may require ECU reprogramming, and folding mechanisms in rear seats may vary between trims.
- Replacement seats are available through Hyundai dealerships or OEM parts distributors using the vehicle's VIN to match factory-original specifications exactly.
- Factory-recycled programs operated by Hyundai Motor Group provide another sourcing channel for replacement seats.
- Used and refurbished OEM seats are available through platforms like eBay, Car-Part.com, and regional auto recyclers.
- Some salvage yards specialize in HMMA-built vehicles, increasing the likelihood that recovered seats originated from the same Hyundai Transys Georgia line.
- Aftermarket companies like Katzkin and ShearComfort offer custom-fit seat covers or reupholstery solutions designed specifically for Hyundai models whose original seats were built under Hyundai Transys specifications.
- Not all Hyundai seats sold in the U.S. come from the Georgia plant; other seats come from facilities in Mexico, South Korea, or third-party suppliers depending on the model and production schedule.
- Some Elantra and Kona models built outside the U.S. may have seats produced elsewhere.
- All Hyundai Transys plants adhere to the same global quality standards, meaning location does not imply superiority.
- Post-2020 supply chain constraints increased lead times for OEM replacement seats, with some dealers reporting waits of 6–8 weeks for backordered items.
- Production prioritizes new vehicle output over spare parts, which explains why certain seat configurations take longer to source.
- The Ergo Motion Driver’s Seat, available on select Palisade and Genesis models, uses adjustable lateral support and dynamic cushion extension to reduce fatigue, with actuators and control logic integrated at the factory level by Hyundai Transys.
- The Relaxation Comfort Seat, found in higher-trim SUVs, simulates a zero-gravity position by adjusting seatback angle and leg support, requiring firmware coordination between the seat module and infotainment system.
- Ventilated and heated seats are standard or optional on SEL, Limited, and Calligraphy trims, with cooling fans and heating elements embedded during initial assembly.
- Retrofitting advanced seating features often requires dealership-level programming for software integration even if hardware can be installed.
- New OEM seats purchased through dealers typically carry a 12-month/unlimited-mile warranty.
