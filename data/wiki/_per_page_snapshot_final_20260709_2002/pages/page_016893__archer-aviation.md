## Facts

- Archer Aviation stock closed out the last week of trading up 10%.
- The broader market saw big sell-offs after the Federal Reserve gave a more hawkish forecast for interest rates in 2025.
- The Fed announced a 25-basis-point rate cut but Chair Jerome Powell issued new guidance expecting only two 25-basis-point cuts next year instead of four.
- Archer Aviation completed construction and received the certificate of occupancy for its manufacturing plant in Georgia.
- The company expects to begin producing its Midnight flying electric vehicles at the beginning of 2025.
- By the end of next year, management expects the Georgia plant will be producing two aircraft a month.
- Archer Aviation stock is up 54.5% across 2024's trading and has a market capitalization of roughly $4.1 billion.
- The company remains in a pre-revenue state with a highly growth-dependent and speculative valuation.
- Archer still needs to obtain regulatory approvals to begin commercial operations for its Midnight vehicles.
- Archer already has $6 billion in orders for its Midnight flying taxis.
- CEO Adam Goldstein recently stated he expects to begin commercial flights next year.
- There appears to be a visible path to receiving the necessary regulatory approvals, with sales potentially ramping rapidly.
- Archer has announced it is entering the defense industry.
- The company is partnering with defense-tech innovator Anduril to develop aircraft for military applications.
