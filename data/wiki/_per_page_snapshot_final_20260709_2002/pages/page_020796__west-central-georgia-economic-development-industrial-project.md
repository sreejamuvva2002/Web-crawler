# West Central Georgia Economic Development & Industrial Projects

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2022-03-30

**Source:** [West Central: Enduring Action, Economic Edge - Georgia Trend Magazine](https://www.georgiatrend.com/2022/03/30/west-central-enduring-action-economic-edge/)  ·  `page_020796`

## Facts

- West Central region of Georgia is experiencing economic movement, sustainability projects, and initiatives propelling it forward.
- Rusty Warner serves as executive director of the Sumter County Development Authority.
- Americus Solar broke ground on the first phase of a $750 million project expected to become the largest solar farm east of the Mississippi River.
- The three-phase Americus Solar project will produce nearly 1,850 megawatts of energy.
- NanoPV opened a $36 million solar panel factory in the Ted Baldwin Business and Technology Park, located northeast of downtown Americus.
- NanoPV's new factory will bring more than 500 jobs to the Ted Baldwin Business and Technology Park.
- NanoPV utilizes patented technology for high-energy efficiency, fully certified solar panels expected to be 99% recyclable.
- New Jersey-based NanoPV already has preorders from Asia and Africa.
- NanoPV joined Sumter County in 2021.
- Georgia ranks 10th nationally in solar installation with 3,260.4 megawatts, enough to power nearly 416,000 homes, according to the Solar Energy Industries Association.
- South Georgia Technical College's alternative energy program and the state’s Quick Start program help Sumter County meet specialized employment needs for the solar industry.
- In January, Canadian-based crypto mining operations company Quarry Dynamics (QDI) announced a move to Sumter County.
- QDI's move represents a reportedly $10 million investment for a 40,500-square-foot Bitcoin mining facility capable of providing up to 19 megawatts of power using renewable energy initiatives.
- Hardee Fresh announced plans to invest $42 million in a new 350,000-square-foot facility later this year (2022).
- The Hardee Fresh facility will employ at least 84 people in the production of indoor, organic, pesticide-free, USDA-certified vegetables.
- The Sumter County facility will be known as Americus Fresh.
- Muscogee County is one of the top 10 most populous counties in Georgia.
- Uptown Columbus reports more than 30 major projects totaling nearly $275 million completed, underway, or announced since 2013.
- Ed Wolverton serves as president and CEO at Uptown Columbus and Columbus Business Improvement District.
- A $26 million project in 2013 transformed an industrial river focused on textile power generation into one adding outdoor recreation as a new economic driver for Uptown.
- The Chattahoochee River flow changes will host the 2022 World Cup and the 2023 World Championships in freestyle kayaking, only the second time these events are held in the United States.
- Three new hotel openings occurred in Uptown Columbus in 2021, almost doubling the hotel inventory.
- City Mills is a $12 million transformation of a historic riverside mill into a 64-room luxury boutique hotel adding a riverfront lawn, event space, and restaurant opening in spring 2022.
- A Hampton Inn was under construction in Uptown Columbus at the time of publication.
- In October 2020, the Environmental Protection Agency awarded a $300,000 Brownfield Assessment Grant to the City of Columbus for community engagement, site assessments, and cleanup planning for the Mill District.
- The Mill District cleanup project leveraged approximately $26.2 million in public and private investment for redevelopment.
- About 42 acres of the Mill District became ready for reuse according to Terracon.
- Terracon is a consulting engineering firm that worked with the City of Columbus on the Mill District cleanup and served as the project's environmental consultant.
- Jerald Mitchell serves as CEO of the Greater Columbus Georgia Chamber of Commerce.
- Highside Market is an adaptive reuse mixed-use development by The Cotton Companies in Columbus opening this summer (2022).
- Highside Market will offer 20,000 square feet of retail, 20,000 square feet of restaurants, 15,000 square feet of office space, event and green space, and outdoor lounge areas.
- Two empty buildings in Greenville, Meriwether County’s seat, found new life in 2021, translating into approximately 35 to 40 jobs this year.
- Fellers Holdings bought and renovated the two Greenville buildings for custom cabinet production and distribution.
- Jane Fryer serves as executive director of the Meriwether County Development Authority.
- Remington Firearms will relocate its global base of operations to LaGrange, marking the 30th headquarters move to Georgia in the last five years.
- America’s oldest firearms manufacturer will invest $100 million and create 856 jobs over a five-year period in Troup County.
- The Remington Firearms relocation includes opening a new advanced manufacturing operation and a world-class research and development center.
- Scott Malone serves as president of the Development Authority of LaGrange.
- Meriwether Park is an industrial park located in northern Meriwether County that has become a popular destination for automobile suppliers.
- Companies at Meriwether Park manufacture door frames, electronic steering, brake pads, and many other parts for General Motors, Ford, Honda, Tesla, Kia, and other production plants.
- A significant portion of the companies located in Meriwether Park are based in South Korea.
- KB AutoSys Manufacturing will add 180 jobs to the existing 800 Korean automotive industry jobs with its first U.S. advanced manufacturing facility at Meriwether Park.
- Global brake pad and lining supplier KB AutoSys is infusing $38 million of investment in Meriwether County via 108,000 square feet of new construction scheduled for completion in the second half of 2022.
- KIA Georgia’s production facility is located in West Point, spanning both Troup and Harris counties, and accounts for almost 40% of the automaker’s sales in the United States.
- Meghan Duke serves as economic development director for the city of West Point.
- Hyundai TRANSYS Georgia Seating System is infusing $9 million into Troup County with an expansion creating at least 150 more jobs in West Point.
- Glovis Georgia is building a 300,000-square-foot light assembly facility to support its logistics operations.
- The Glovis Georgia project involves a $34 million capital investment that will create 70 new jobs in Troup County this year.
- Carolyn McKinley heads both Meriwether County’s chamber of commerce and tourism, and co-chairs the “Get Reading” campaign leveraging arts to promote literacy.
- Dan Gilbert has over 40 years in the whitewater rafting business, served as a venue advisor for the 1996 Olympics, brought the kayaking World Cup and World Championships to Columbus, and his two sons have joined the family business Whitewater Express.
