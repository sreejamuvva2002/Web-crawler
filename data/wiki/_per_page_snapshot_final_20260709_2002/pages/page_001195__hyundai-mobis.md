# Hyundai Mobis

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2025-01-14

**Source:** [Hyundai Mobis and ZEISS Unveil Holographic Windshield Display - Highways Today](https://highways.today/2025/01/14/holographic-windshield-display/)  ·  `page_001195`

## Facts

- Hyundai Mobis, a global leader in advanced automotive technology, introduced its revolutionary Holographic Windshield Display at CES 2025.
- The holographic windshield display was developed in collaboration with ZEISS, a world-renowned German optics company.
- The technology transforms the entire windshield into an immersive, transparent screen that blends safety, convenience, and aesthetics.
- The system was demonstrated in a Kia EV9 electric vehicle.
- It projects critical driving information, including navigation prompts, speed, and multimedia options, directly onto the windshield.
- The display eliminates the need for physical dashboard screens to offer a seamless and distraction-free interface.
- From the outside, the display looks like an ordinary piece of glass.
- The system delivers crystal-clear visuals in bright sunlight due to high brightness and precise colour reproduction.
- A specially embedded Holographic Optical Element (HOE) uses light diffraction to project images straight to the driver’s eyes.
- Hyundai Mobis CEO Lee Gyu Suk and ZEISS CEO Karl Lamprecht held a high-profile meeting at CES to discuss the future of their partnership.
- Both CEOs expressed optimism about scaling the technology for mass production.
- The partners aim to roll out the holographic windshield display globally by 2027.
- Lee Gyu Suk remarked that the synergy between Hyundai Mobis’s innovative vision and ZEISS’s optical precision will bring the futuristic concept to the roads.
- The technology is currently in the pre-production phase.
- Hyundai Mobis aims to complete the final stages of development by mid-2026.
- A full-scale market launch is planned for 2027.
- Hyundai Mobis also showcased other next-generation innovations at CES 2025, including advanced electrification components for EVs and hybrid vehicles.
- Other showcased technologies include vehicle electronics for smarter driving, chassis systems for improved performance and comfort, and innovative lighting solutions.
- The holographic display reduces driver distraction by keeping critical information in the driver’s line of sight.
- The innovation aligns with the broader push for autonomous and semi-autonomous vehicles requiring clear, real-time information displays.
- Experts have hailed the holographic windshield as a game-changer with potential applications extending to public transport and aviation.
- Developing the technology for mass production faces challenges related to manufacturing scalability and cost efficiency.
