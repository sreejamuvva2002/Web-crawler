# BYD

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-02-05

**Source:** [BYD Quietly Halts $1B Plant Progress in Turkey, Formal Announcement Expected: Report | EV](https://eletric-vehicles.com/byd/byd-quietly-halts-1b-plant-progress-in-turkey-formal-announcement-expected-report/)  ·  `page_012753`

## Facts

- BYD might be halting its planned $1 billion investment in its passenger car plant in Turkey.
- The deal for the factory to be located in the Manisa Organized Industrial Zone was signed in July 2024 by Turkish President Tayyip Erdoğan and BYD Chief Executive Officer Wang Chuanfu.
- The Turkish plant is intended to have an annual production capacity of 150,000 vehicles.
- BYD has been granted incentives by the Turkish government, including tax exemptions.
- 18 months after the deal was signed, BYD has yet to begin construction of the Turkish facility.
- No timeline has been shared with the public regarding the Turkish plant despite recent media reports.
- CHP lawmaker Sevda Erdan Kilic stated last week that no timeline has been shared.
- Reuters reported in July that BYD was postponing mass production at its first European passenger-car plant in Hungary until 2026.
- The delay to the Hungarian plant was said to reflect a shift in focus toward BYD’s planned Turkish factory, where production was expected to start sooner due to lower labor costs.
- Construction equipment was reportedly spotted at the planned Turkish site in mid-January, suggesting work could finally be starting.
- Local media outlet PA Turkey reported on Thursday that BYD has allegedly suspended the investment, citing sources within the industry.
- Ali Suat Ertosun, a former member of Turkey’s Supreme Court of Appeals and current president of the Manisa Association for the Protection of Cultural and Natural Assets, is among the cited sources.
- A formal announcement on the Turkish project's status is expected as early as this month or March.
- Turkish Minister of Industry and Technology Mehmet Fatih Kacır was questioned in the Grand National Assembly last week about the project's status.
- The opposition Republican People’s Party (CHP) submitted a 10-question parliamentary inquiry addressing how much of the $1 billion investment has been carried out, land allocation status, tax exemption details, and potential sanctions for non-completion.
- Kacır stated the project remains under active supervision within Turkey’s investment incentive legislation, with oversight and compliance checks continuing in full.
- Kacır added that if the investment is not completed as planned, sanctions set out in the incentive regulations would apply.
- Kacır said: “These measures are valid for the BYD project as well. In addition, safeguard mechanisms ensure that our national interests are fully protected.”
- Besides domestic production in China, BYD currently operates factories in Thailand, Uzbekistan and Brazil.
- The Chinese new energy vehicle maker is also building new plants in Indonesia, Malaysia and Cambodia.
- BYD has recently begun trial production at its European plant in Hungary following delays in completing construction of the passenger vehicle factory.
- Manufacturing fully electric vehicles in Europe will allow the automaker to avoid the European Commission tariffs imposed in October 2024 on imported EVs.
- Full production at the Hungarian plant is expected to begin in the second quarter and will ramp up to its planned capacity of 200,000 vehicles over several years.
- Employment at the Hungarian plant will expand in parallel with production ramp-up.
- Last year, BYD established its European headquarters in Hungary, saying the office was expected to create around 2,000 jobs.
- A third European factory is under consideration according to several reports, though no location has been revealed yet.
- The company aims to achieve 1.3 million sales outside China this year, representing a 24.3% increase year over year.
- Last month, BYD’s sales more than doubled in Germany and the UK.
- BYD continues to struggle to gain market share in Scandinavia.
- BYD’s vehicle registrations declined overall in January, leading its stock down in both the Shenzhen and Hong Kong markets earlier this week.
