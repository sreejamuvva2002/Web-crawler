# Lear Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 1999-03-16

**Source:** [Lear Corporation - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lear_Corporation)  ·  `page_056937`

## Facts

- Lear Corporation is a public manufacturing company formerly known as American Metal Products.
- Founded in Detroit, Michigan in 1917, originally manufacturing tubular, welded and stamped assemblies for aircraft and automobile industries.
- Headquarters located in the United States.
- Operates 257 locations across 39 countries as of 2017.
- Serves an international area served.
- Key executives include Raymond E. Scott (President, CEO, and Director) and Terry Larkin (Executive Vice President, Business Development and General Counsel).
- Provides automotive seating and electrical systems to original equipment manufacturers (OEMs).
- Reported revenue of US$23.3 billion in 2024.
- Reported US$888 million and US$507 million in additional 2024 financial metrics.
- Holds total assets of US$14.0 billion and total equity of US$4.45 billion as of 2024.
- Employs 173,700 people as of 2024.
- Organized into Seating and Electrical divisions.
- Ranked #148 on the Fortune 500 list in 2018 and #147 in 2019.
- Grew during the 1980s and 1990s through acquisitions to supply complete interior automotive systems (seating, electrical, flooring, interior trim, instrument panels) to OEM auto companies.
- On March 16, 1999, announced the acquisition of United Technologies Automotive for $2.3 billion.
- Completed the acquisition of United Technologies Automotive on May 4, 1999; the subsidiary produced dashboards, electrical distribution systems, motors, air-flow parts, interior door panels and switches.
- Became the first large corporation to achieve ISO/TS 16949:1999 worldwide across 280 global facilities, managed by Bill Cooper (Global Quality Systems Senior Quality Manager).
- On April 5, 2004, announced a $220 million acquisition of Grote & Hartmann, a Wuppertal, Germany-based maker of electrical components.
- Completed the Grote & Hartmann transaction on July 6, 2004.
- By late 2005, most OEM auto companies stopped purchasing total interiors from single companies, making Lear's interior systems division unprofitable and prompting a divestiture search.
- In early 2007, transferred its former North American Interior Systems Division to International Automotive Components Group (IAC), a joint venture of Lear, WL Ross & Co., and Franklin Mutual Advisers.
- The IAC transfer deal involved 26 manufacturing plants and two Chinese joint ventures, with Lear contributing $27 million in cash for a 25 percent interest plus warrants for an additional 7 percent.
- In 2007, Lear's board agreed to a $2.3 billion takeover offer from American Real Estate Partners (an affiliate of Carl Icahn), which included a $100 million fee for Icahn if another offer was accepted; the deal was later voted down by shareholders.
- On July 2, 2009, Bloomberg News reported Lear planned to file for Chapter 11 bankruptcy after reaching an agreement with secured lenders and bondholders.
- Announced emergence from bankruptcy on November 9, 2009.
- On August 10, 2011, announced that CFO Matt Simoncini was elected CEO and president, effective September 1, 2011.
- In 2012, won the Automotive News PACE Award for its Solid State Smart Junction Box (S3JB), which features integrated solid state fuse technology eliminating relays, a non-driver-compartment accessible package reducing warranty returns, smart software logic for fail-safe operation, and a patented thermal single state board design.
- In May 2012, acquired automotive and specialty fabrics company Guilford Mills for $257 million; Guilford produces fabrics for water filtration, window covering, performance apparel, medical and industrial applications.
- In August 2014, announced the acquisition of automotive leather supplier Eagle Ottawa LLC for $850 million.
- In May 2015, announced plans to open a new automotive plant in Gostivar, Republic of North Macedonia in summer 2015, marking Lear's first facility in North Macedonia.
- In February 2017, signed a definitive agreement to acquire Grupo Antolin's automotive seating business for €286 million on a cash and debt-free basis, funded with cash on hand.
- Served as a backer for the Jaguar Racing Team in the 2001 Formula One World Championship and currently supports the Formula E Competition.
- In November 2022, acquired Novi-based supplier InTouch Automation, which provides Industry 4.0 technologies and automated testing equipment used in automotive seat production.
