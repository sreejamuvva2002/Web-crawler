# Hyundai Mobis

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** power_electronics  ·  **Published:** 2022-11-23

**Source:** [Second Global Automotive Supplier for Hyundai Metaplant To Create 1,500 Jobs - Expansion Solutions](https://www.expansionsolutionsmagazine.com/second-global-automotive-supplier-for-hyundai-metaplant/)  ·  `page_001180`

## Facts

- Hyundai Mobis plans to invest $926 million in an Electric Vehicle (EV) Power Electric system plant in Bryan County that will eventually employ at least 1,500 people.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that celebrating the groundbreaking of Hyundai’s new electric vehicle and battery manufacturing facility in Bryan County would unleash transformational job creation and investment in that region of the state.
- The announcement marks the second supplier to locate in the area within two weeks.
- Hyundai Mobis has operated a manufacturing facility with almost 1,200 employees in West Point since 2009.
- The West Point plant is the largest Tier 1 supplier to Kia Georgia and supplies automotive manufacturers with complete cockpit modules, chassis modules, and bumper assemblies.
- H.S. Oh, Vice President of Hyundai Mobis Electric Powertrain Business Unit, said the Bryan County investment reflects an acceleration in the development of the EV supply chain in Georgia’s auto industry.
- Hyundai Mobis aims to be a major production player in the EV market, which will trigger more growth within the sector and provide high-quality work opportunities to the growing local workforce.
- Once it reaches full production, the 1,200,000-square-foot facility will supply over 900,000 EV Power Electric systems annually.
- The facility will also supply 450,000 Integrated Charging Control Units annually to Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) in Bryan County, Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Alabama (HMMA) in Montgomery, and the Kia Georgia plant.
- Construction on the new facility is expected to begin as early as January 2023.
- Production at the new facility is expected to begin in 2024.
- The facility will be located at the GRAD Certified Belfast Commerce Park in Richmond Hill.
- Development Authority of Bryan County Chairman Jon Seagraves noted the site's close proximity to available housing in nearby Heartwood, a top-performing public school system, interstate access, and rail.
- The site for the new facility was purchased about two years ago.
- Jon Seagraves expressed gratitude to Bryan County, the City of Richmond Hill, and Raydient Places for their support and partnership on the project.
- Alyce Thornhill, Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy, represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The GDEcD partnered with the Development Authority of Bryan County, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia Power for this project.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that suppliers like Hyundai Mobis are transitioning to the jobs of the future and becoming part of Georgia’s growing EV ecosystem.
- In May, officials predicted that non-affiliated HMGMA suppliers in connection with the project would invest an additional $1 billion into Georgia.
- That prediction was blown past in about two weeks.
- GDEcD is working with partners like Georgia Quick Start and sister agencies to prepare Georgia’s workforce of tomorrow for the jobs being created now.
- Since 2020, more than 30 EV-related projects have contributed over $13.3 billion in investment and announced more than 18,100 jobs in Georgia.
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America officially broke ground on October 25 in Bryan County on its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility.
- Offsite suppliers connected to the HMGMA project are predicted to invest approximately $1 billion in other locations around the state.
