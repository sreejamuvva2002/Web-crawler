## Facts

- Mercedes-Benz USA maintains its corporate office in Atlanta, Georgia.
- The company is hiring across 17 different brands, with job openings in 15 different categories nationwide.
- Technician opportunities are available at Mercedes-Benz dealerships nationwide, with an education training program to become a Certified Technician.
- The MB STAR Internship Program targets undergraduate college juniors and seniors for summer real-world experience, skills, and insights.
- Employee benefits include quality medical, dental and vision plans; life insurance and disability plans; a best-in-class 401k; flexible spending accounts and health savings accounts; paid vacation days; paid time off for life events and volunteerism; and performance-based merit increases and bonuses.
- As part of Daimler’s Ambition 2039 initiative, Mercedes-Benz aims to offer a carbon-neutral fleet of new vehicles less than 20 years from now.
- Where market conditions allow, Mercedes-Benz USA will be ready to go all electric by the end of the decade.
- All newly launched vehicle architectures will be electric-only from 2025 onwards.
- In 2021, the company introduced the record-breaking EQS, its first fully-electric luxury sedan.
- In 2022, Mercedes-Benz USA is introducing multiple additional all-electric SUVs and sedans that combine industry-leading standards with Mercedes-EQ’s electric powertrain and groundbreaking technologies.
- The company's founder, Carl Benz, invented the automobile in 1886.
