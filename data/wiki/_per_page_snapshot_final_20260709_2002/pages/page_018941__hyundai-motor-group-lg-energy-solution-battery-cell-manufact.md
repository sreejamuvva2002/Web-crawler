# Hyundai Motor Group & LG Energy Solution Battery Cell Manufacturing Joint Venture

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Business Development – SEDA : Annual Report](https://www.sedaannualreport.com/2023/business-development/)  ·  `page_018941`

## Facts

- On Feb. 1, HMGMA supplier Seoyon E-HWA announced it would locate in the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center (SCMC), bringing 740 new jobs and $76 million in capital investment.
- Seoyon E-HWA manufactures door trims, headlining seats, bumpers, and interior and exterior components.
- On March 6, PHA announced it would create 402 new jobs and invest $67 million in capital for a new location in SCMC.
- PHA manufactures door, tailgate, and hood latches.
- On Oct. 23, Daechang Seat Savannah Corp. announced it would locate in SCMC to make automotive seat frames for HMGMA, creating 500 new jobs and $72.5 million in capital investment.
- On April 6, Gulfstream Aerospace Corp. selected Chatham County for expansions that will bring 1,600 new jobs and $150 million in capital investment.
- The Gulfstream projects include construction of a new Customer Support service center and expansion of its manufacturing facility for the G400, G500, and G600 aircraft.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Gulfstream joined the Savannah community in 1967 and partners with technical college systems and local schools to create opportunity while participating in programs like Georgia Made.
- Gulfstream flew its first purpose-built business aircraft in 1958 and relocated to Savannah in 1967, with over 3,000 aircraft currently in service worldwide.
- Gulfstream currently employs more than 11,000 people in Chatham County.
- SEDA President and CEO Trip Tollison noted that the Gulfstream announcement was a competitive project and thanked the company for choosing Savannah.
- Savannah Airport Commission Executive Director Greg Kelly stated the partnership resulted in one of the largest aeronautical complexes in the world at SAV, with new facilities underway and future growth planned.
- On Aug. 31, Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution (LGES) announced an additional $2 billion investment in their battery cell manufacturing joint venture at HMGMA in Bryan County, raising the JV's total investment to over $4.3 billion.
- The Hyundai/LGES expansion will create another 400 new jobs.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Georgia broke ground on the largest project in state history, landed multiple suppliers for Hyundai's Metaplant, and welcomed LGES to Bryan County.
- Hyundai President and Global COO José Muñoz stated the incremental investment reflects a continued commitment to creating a sustainable future powered by American workers.
- In May 2023, Hyundai Motor Group and LGES signed an MOU establishing LGES as the partner for HMGMA's onsite battery cell manufacturing JV.
- The EV manufacturing facility and battery JV represent an estimated $7.59 billion in investment and will create 8,500 new jobs over the next eight years.
- LG Energy Solution President and Head of the Advanced Automotive Battery Division Dong-Myung Kim stated the investment underscores dedication to driving America's EV transition and bolstering the local economy through quality jobs.
- The 30 GWh facility will support production of 300,000 EV units annually at full operations.
- Hyundai Mobis will assemble battery packs using cells from the plant and supply them to Hyundai Motor Group's U.S. manufacturing facilities for Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis EV models.
- Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority Chairman Carter Infinger stated the announcement will bring 400 additional well-paying jobs to the Savannah region.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated Governor Kemp set a goal of Georgia becoming the top location for EV manufacturing in the U.S., highlighting partners like the Savannah-area JDA, utility providers, Georgia Ports Authority, and Georgia Quick Start.
- On Nov. 14, VendTech International (Naturals2Go) announced it would relocate its headquarters, training center, assembly manufacturing, and global distribution/technology development facility to SCMC, creating 125 new jobs and contributing over $20 million in capital investment.
- Naturals2Go focuses on unattended retail space with healthy product choices and cashless payment systems technology.
- SEDA COO Jesse Dillon stated the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center was created to attract companies like Naturals2Go and bring their assembly manufacturing, headquarters, and training center to the region.
- On Nov. 30, Governor Brian Kemp announced Plastic Express would build a second shipping facility in Chatham County, creating an estimated 200 new jobs and $80 million in investment.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that more than 70 percent of the previous year's economic development projects were expansions of businesses already operating in Georgia.
- Plastic Express is headquartered in Pasadena, Texas, founded in 1970, and offers bulk trucking, terminal, packaging, and warehousing services for the plastics industry.
- The company operates two existing facilities in Georgia that support more than 200 jobs.
- Plastic Express President and CEO Ray Hufnagel stated Savannah presents a solid opportunity for investment in export logistic centers to complement aggressive capability expansions at the port.
- Plastic Express Chief Marketing Officer Jennifer Pittman Fussell stated the new location is strategically located adjacent to the CSX operating yard and within five miles of the port.
- Plastic Express will construct its second facility at Rockingham Farms Industrial Park, a 980,000-square-foot site served by CSX with 104 dock doors and 7,200 container spots.
- The new Plastic Express packing lines will be capable of packaging 4 million pounds of resin per day, with operations expected to begin mid-2024.
- SEDA President and CEO Trip Tollison noted Plastic Express will be the first employer to locate at Rockingham Farms.
- The Savannah Logistics Innovation Center (SLIC) graduated its second cohort of startup companies in 2023, bringing the total alumni count to 25.
- Companies that have gone through the SLIC accelerator program have raised a total of $80 million.
- Einride, a Swedish freight mobility company and part of the first SLIC cohort, signed a partnership with Keen Transport to develop and provide battery-electric vehicle trucks for a shuttle between the Port of Savannah and a Wallenius Wilhelmsen processing facility in Pooler.
- Einride is supporting the deployment of the trucks by providing driver training, charging infrastructure, and data insights.
