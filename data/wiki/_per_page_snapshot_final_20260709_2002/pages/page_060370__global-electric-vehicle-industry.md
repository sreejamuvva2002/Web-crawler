# Global Electric Vehicle Industry

**Type:** event  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [What electric car slowdown? Global EV industry is breaking records - Adamas Intelligence](https://www.adamasintel.com/global-ev-industry-breaks-records/)  ·  `page_060370`

## Facts

- Volkswagen, Ford and others rolled back spending on new battery plants and curbed production of some models, notably the F-150 Lightning pickup.
- Volkswagen idled workers at its largest EV plant in Europe.
- EV startups Enovate, Levdeo, Singulato and WM Motor filed for bankruptcy in China during Q4.
- The Chinese market has 110 other EV brands to evaluate.
- Share prices of electric spinoffs like Volvo’s Polestar declined, while erstwhile highflyers Vinfast, Lucid, Rivian and Fisker also saw declines.
- Fisker's share price is down 90% in value from its 2023 peak.
- EV startup Arrival filed for bankruptcy before selling its first vehicle after being valued as much as $15 billion.
- Ampere’s IPO was shelved, with Adamas battery capacity data showing Renault-Dacia EV brands were not making headway in their home base.
- Renault's idea that its EV unit would fetch more than its parent’s $10 billion valuation was considered fanciful.
- Tesla shares doubled in value in 2023.
- BYD surpassed Tesla in terms of sales last year (2023).
- Over 19.2 million EVs, including plug-in and conventional hybrids, were registered globally last year, which is 4.6 million more than in 2022.
- The global EV industry grew at 32% on an annual basis.
- In 2023, EVs represented 23% of all passenger vehicle sales around the world.
- December was a record month in terms of total battery capacity (GWh) deployed onto roads globally in newly sold EVs, growing by 12% over the previous record month of November.
- For the 2023 calendar year, 689.2 GWh was added to the electric car parc, an expansion of just under 200 GWh or 40% compared to 2022.
- Some 37% of all power-hours deployed over the past decade were rolled onto roads in 2023 alone.
- More gigawatt-hours were deployed in 2023 than in the eight years from 2014 to 2021 combined.
- The GWhs deployed in December 2023 alone were more than the entire 2018 calendar year.
- Germany, the fourth largest EV market worldwide, saw passenger EV battery capacity deployment drop 46% year-over-year in December.
- Germany's decline was mainly due to the axing of government subsidies a full year early.
- Germany's December 2022 outcome was distorted by an exceptional month where more than 7.3 GWh were added to autobahns on the back of buoyant Tesla Model Y and 3 sales.
- The Asia Pacific region saw combined battery capacity of 48.8 GWh deployed in December, a new record for the region and up 45% over the same month the year before.
- In the Americas, 12.6 GWh were deployed onto roads in newly sold passenger EVs in December, a 34% year-over-year gain marking a new all-time monthly high.
- The US and Canada make up more than 96% of the Americas regional market.
- In Europe, total battery capacity deployment shrank by 39% in December 2023 compared to the same month a year earlier.
- Historically, December has been Europe's strongest month, but its best month came as far back as March when 16.1 GWh hit roads versus 15.9 GWh in December 2023.
- In France, the third largest EV market in the region in GWh terms, December battery capacity deployment rocketed by 47%, smashing all records and surpassing the UK only for the third time ever on a monthly basis.
- On an annual basis in 2023, Europe powered ahead 32% year-on-year with 156.2 GWh deployed.
- Asia Pacific saw 40% growth in 2023 with 405.6 GWh deployed.
- The Americas saw 49% growth in 2023 where 117.0 GWh was deployed.
- Across Europe and the UK, December was the 2023 high mark for EV adoption with more than one-third of passenger vehicles leaving showrooms as full electric or hybrid vehicles.
- Germany accounts for a fifth of the European regional market, and December was its second best month of the year for EVs relative to gas-powered passenger vehicles.
- The only market in Europe that contracted on an annual basis in 2023 was Norway, with buyers accumulating 24% less battery power than the year before.
- 84% of all vehicles sold in Norway last year were EVs, which is on par with 2022.
- More than half of cars sold in Finland, Sweden, Denmark and Netherlands in 2023 were EVs, with Belgium reaching a similar level by the end of the year.
- In Iceland, EV penetration rates ran north of 90% since August and hit over 99% of sales in December last year, matching the previous year.
