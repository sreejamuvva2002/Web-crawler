# Anovion Technologies

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Anovion Technologies: Job Creation and U.S. Battery Leadership](https://www.batterytechonline.com/industry-outlook/anovion-technologies-job-creation-and-u-s-battery-leadership)  ·  `page_001499`

## Facts

- Anovion Technologies is a climate tech-driven advanced materials company focused on clean energy innovation.
- The company produces synthetic graphite anode materials for lithium-ion batteries.
- Eric Stopka serves as the Chief Executive Officer of Anovion.
- Anovion announced plans to build a new manufacturing facility in Georgia.
- The Georgia facility will initially produce 40,000 metric tons of synthetic graphite anode materials annually.
- Total operational capacity for the Georgia facility is expected to be achieved by late 2025.
- The manufacturing facility in Georgia will span 1,500,000 square feet.
- Anovion expects to create approximately 400 high-quality local clean energy jobs at the Georgia site.
- Hiring for the Georgia facility is scheduled to begin in late 2025.
- Eric Stopka cited Georgia's strong pipeline of technical workers as a key factor in the site selection.
- Georgia was chosen for its existing and planned proximity to low- and carbon-free energy sources, access to rail infrastructure, highly skilled workforce, and business-friendly environment.
- Anovion views Georgia as part of a green energy corridor focused on job creation, community development, and investment in people.
- The company gained qualification for electric vehicle (EV) applications for its synthetic graphite products.
- Commercial production of Anovion's synthetic graphite began in early 2021.
- The new Georgia facility will utilize state-of-the-art processes and proprietary furnace technologies to reduce greenhouse gas emissions.
- The facility will leverage the decarbonizing energy mix in the Southeast to enhance sustainability.
- Anovion's expansion strategy includes the Anovion Center of Excellence (ACE) in Sanborn, New York.
- Anovion also operates a hydro-powered manufacturing facility near Niagara Falls.
- These domestic expansions support Anovion's goal of securing critical materials for US lithium-ion battery production.
- Anovion received a $117M grant from the US Department of Energy to scale production capacity and construct the Georgia factory.
- Synthetic graphite produced by Anovion powers electric vehicles on the road today, as well as energy storage systems, personal e-mobility, medical devices, military and aerospace applications, and other industrial uses.
- Anovion's in-house testing capabilities allow it to understand how manufacturing parameters and methods impact battery performance from raw materials to finished batteries.
- Historically, much of the graphite used by the US for lithium-ion batteries has been produced overseas, a paradigm Anovion aims to change.
- Growing consumer demand for EVs and other electrified applications, along with government policies like the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law and Inflation Reduction Act, are driving increased investment in a clean energy economy.
- Anovion is a founding member of the Battery Materials & Technology Coalition (BMTC), which advocates for significant investments and smart policy to strengthen the sustainable domestic upstream battery supply chain in North America.
