# Anovion Technologies

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2023-05-15

**Source:** [Anovion Technologies to Create Over 400 Jobs in Bainbridge | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-release/anovion-technologies-create-over-400-jobs-bainbridge-invest-800m-manufacturing)  ·  `page_000005`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Anovion Technologies (Anovion), a supplier of premium synthetic graphite anode materials for lithium-ion batteries, will build a new manufacturing facility in Bainbridge.
- The project will create more than 400 jobs and over $800 million in investment in Decatur County.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated Georgia is fast becoming the e-mobility capital of the nation.
- Governor Kemp noted job creators are recognizing that every corner of the state has the resources and workforce needed for success.
- Governor Kemp mentioned meeting Anovion's team personally and looking forward to celebrating their partnership with Georgians in Decatur County.
- Anovion is a climate tech-driven advanced materials company.
- Anovion is one of the first qualified U.S. suppliers of synthetic graphite anode materials for use in e-mobility applications.
- Graphite is the largest battery material used in electric vehicles by mass, more than copper, nickel, manganese, cobalt, and lithium.
- Eric Stopka, Chief Executive Officer of Anovion, stated they are thrilled to be opening their first large-scale expansion manufacturing facility in Georgia.
- Stopka noted the state’s strong technical workforce and top-tier schools will help advance research partnerships and contribute to a highly skilled talent pipeline for Anovion’s innovation and climate technology jobs.
- Stopka mentioned encouragement by the region’s growing availability of clean energy, resulting in increased sustainability for Anovion’s manufacturing process.
- Stopka expressed looking forward to providing solutions to secure the country’s electrified future with support from partners in Georgia and the Federal government.
- Anovion’s new facility will be 1,500,000-square-foot.
- The facility will use proprietary furnace technologies.
- The facility is located at Downrange Industrial Park in Bainbridge.
- It will be the first of its size and scale in North America.
- The facility will produce synthetic graphite for use in batteries that power EVs, electric storage systems, electronics, military applications, and other products.
- Operations are expected to begin in late 2025.
- The company will be hiring for a variety of technical, production, and engineering roles, as well as administrative and management positions.
- Bainbridge Mayor Edward Reynolds welcomed Anovion to Bainbridge.
- Reynolds noted the $800 million investment and creation of over 400 high-paying, quality jobs as a huge win for the community.
- Reynolds stated the local workforce is ready to embrace the opportunity and that everyone will grow and prosper together.
- Pete Stephens, Chairman of the Decatur County Board of Commissioners, noted Decatur County leadership has focused on attracting quality companies and jobs for many years.
- Stephens stated diversifying the industry mix is needed to remain competitive and grow.
- Stephens described today’s announcement as a step in that direction, noting green industry jobs represent a growing segment of the economy and will provide quality employment opportunities for decades to come.
- Stephens expressed pride in welcoming Anovion to Decatur County and its family of industries.
- Project Manager Taylor Walden represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The project was done in partnership with the City of Bainbridge, Development Authority of Bainbridge and Decatur County, University System of Georgia, Georgia EMC, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated much of the critical supply chain for e-mobility and manufacturing is currently sourced outside the U.S.
- Wilson described Anovion as an innovative industry leader that fills that need and delivers another critical component to Georgia.
- Wilson noted a diverse mix of public-private partnerships are coming together to support the next revolution in the automotive industry, from working with utility partners and establishing infrastructure to attracting and retaining top-talent through the University System of Georgia.
- Wilson expressed excitement for the hope and opportunity Anovion will create for Bainbridge and Decatur County.
- Building on assets that make the automotive industry successful, Georgia is positioned as a hub for the electric mobility industry.
- Since 2020, 40 EV-related projects have announced more than $21.9 billion in investment and 28,000 jobs in Georgia.
- More than 10 of these projects are battery manufacturers, recyclers, and suppliers, representing approximately $8.5 billion in investment and 6,000 jobs.
- Anovion Technologies is headquartered in Chicago, Illinois.
- Anovion Technologies is a U.S.-owned and U.S.-based advanced materials business.
- It is currently America’s first commercial-scale manufacturer of premium, cleaner anode-grade synthetic graphite across its existing footprint near Niagara Falls, New York, and Clarksburg, West Virginia.
- As a leader in innovation and production of lithium-ion battery materials, Anovion began commercial production in early 2021.
- Anovion is among a limited number of graphite anode producers in America to have successfully gained qualification for EV applications.
