# Hanon Systems

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** thermal_management  ·  **Published:** 2023-05

**Source:** [Hanon Systems invest over $40M in GA facility](https://valdostatoday.com/news-2/region/2023/05/hanon-systems-invest-over-40m-in-ga-facility/)  ·  `page_000487`

## Facts

- Hanon Systems, a global automotive thermal and energy management solutions provider, invested over $40 million in a new Georgia facility.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced the investment while on an economic development trip to Israel.
- Hanon Systems will create 160 new jobs.
- The new manufacturing facility is located in Bulloch County, Georgia.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Georgia’s automotive and e-mobility industries continue to grow as they welcome Hanon Systems to the state.
- Governor Brian Kemp noted that Georgia is furthering its position as the EV capital of America, with new jobs and opportunities coming to hardworking Georgians across the state.
- Min Sung, President and CEO of Hanon Systems, stated the company is pleased to contribute to Bulloch County’s local economy through job creation.
- Min Sung thanked the State of Georgia and the cooperation between its agencies for guiding the project.
- Min Sung said the announcement aligns with Hanon Systems’ strategy to support the industry shift toward electrification while growing its North America footprint.
- Hanon Systems will build a new facility at Gateway Regional Industrial Park in Bulloch County to assemble automotive thermal solutions.
- Operations at the new facility are expected to begin in May 2024.
- Billy Allen, Chair of the Development Authority of Bulloch County, stated Statesboro-Bulloch County is honored by Hanon Systems’ decision to locate its new facility there.
- Billy Allen expressed excitement to welcome another automotive supplier to the community, particularly one working closely with the Hyundai Metaplant and the electric vehicle industry.
- Billy Allen cited the decision as an example of the resources offered by their community and region to help companies succeed.
- Griff Lynch, Executive Director of the Georgia Ports Authority (GPA), stated the announcement further solidifies Georgia’s position as a growing hub for auto manufacturing.
- Griff Lynch noted that GPA is proud to support the operations of Hyundai Motor Group and its suppliers to deliver jobs and opportunity for Georgia residents.
- Alyce Thornhill, Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy, represented the GDEcD’s Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The GDEcD partnered with the Development Authority of Bulloch County, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start for this project.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that suppliers like Hanon Systems can successfully support larger industry ecosystems in Georgia through partnership-driven solutions ranging from workforce training to logistics systems efficiencies.
- Pat Wilson noted that Bulloch County, as part of the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority, has been part of this growing success story from the beginning.
- Pat Wilson highlighted how Georgia’s history of partnership continues to create opportunity for current and future generations.
- Georgia is positioned as a hub for the electric mobility industry, building on assets that make the automotive industry successful.
- Since 2020, over 40 EV-related projects have announced more than $22.7 billion in investment and 28,400 jobs in Georgia.
- On October 25, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America officially broke ground in neighboring Bryan County on its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility.
- Hanon Systems was formed in 1986 and has grown into a leading supplier of automotive thermal and energy management solutions for electrified and conventional vehicles.
- Hanon Systems’ offerings include heating ventilation and air conditioning, powertrain cooling, compressor, fluid transport, and electronics and fluid pressure solutions.
