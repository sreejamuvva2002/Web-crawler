# U.S. Battery Manufacturing Co.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2019-03-06

**Source:** [page_000882](https://www.asihealthservices.com/osha-cites-battery-manufacturer-for-lead-hazards)  ·  `page_000882`

## Facts

- U.S. Battery Manufacturing Co. was cited by OSHA for exposing employees to lead exceeding permissible exposure limits at its Augusta, Georgia facility.
- The company was also cited for exposing employees to arsenic above the action level.
- OSHA cited the manufacturer for failing to record on OSHA's 300 log that medically removed employees from exposure.
- The company failed to conduct required training.
- The company failed to implement a medical surveillance program.
- The facility was cited for having unguarded machinery.
- The facility was cited for failing to properly guard equipment.
- OSHA proposed $115,594 in penalties against U.S. Battery Manufacturing Co.
- The investigation was initiated under OSHA's Regional Emphasis Program on Lead.
- The company has 15 business days from receipt of the citations and proposed penalties to comply, request an informal conference with OSHA’s area director, or contest the findings before the Occupational Safety and Health Review Commission.
- OSHA Atlanta-East Area Director William Fulcher stated that elevated lead levels can cause debilitating and permanent health issues.
- Under OSHA's lead standard, employers must minimize workers' exposure using engineering controls, safe work practices, and protective clothing and equipment.
- The Occupational Safety and Health Act of 1970 holds employers responsible for providing safe and healthful workplaces, with OSHA setting and enforcing standards while providing training, education, and assistance.
