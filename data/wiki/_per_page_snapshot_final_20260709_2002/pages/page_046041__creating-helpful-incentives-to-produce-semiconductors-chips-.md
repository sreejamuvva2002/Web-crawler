# Creating Helpful Incentives to Produce Semiconductors (CHIPS) and Science Act

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Failure to Repeal R D Expenditure Capitalization Requirements Spoils CHIPS and Science Act | UHY](https://uhy-us.com/insights/news/2022/october/failure-to-repeal-r-d-expenditure-capitalization-requirements-spoils-chips-and-science-act)  ·  `page_046041`

## Facts

- The Creating Helpful Incentives to Produce Semiconductors (CHIPS) and Science Act was signed into law by President Joe Biden in August 2022.
- The legislation contains $280 billion in funding designed to boost the domestic manufacture of semiconductor products.
- The package contains billions of dollars allocated to improve domestic chip capabilities.
- The package includes a new program to incentivize research and development (R&D) in advanced computer technology.
- The legislation did not repeal IRC Section 174 R&D expenditure capitalization requirements contained in the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act of 2017 (TCJA).
- TCJA eliminated the ability for companies to deduct R&D expenses in the year they are incurred, effective for tax years beginning in 2022.
- The failure to repeal these requirements could be a major barrier to investment in critical R&D activities.
- IRC Section 174 has allowed deductions for research and experimental (R&E) expenditures as current business expenses since 1954.
- Section 174 defines these expenditures as costs related to the development or improvement of a product, including direct expenses such as salaries, supplies, materials, operating costs, patent expenses, and costs of hiring outside contractors or engineering organizations.
- Section 174 originally allowed an option to capitalize and amortize R&E expenditures over five years from when a company first received economic benefit, or a write-off over 10 years from when costs were incurred.
- Starting in tax year 2022, domestic research costs must be capitalized and amortized over a five-year period starting with the midpoint of the year they were incurred.
- Costs for research conducted outside the U.S. must be amortized over 15 years.
- Companies with significant R&D investments may see a significant increase in taxable income on their 2022 returns due to these changes.
- The changes couple increased taxable income with increased administrative responsibility to comply with new amortization requirements.
- Companies are hoping for congressional intervention to delay or repeal the TCJA changes through year-end tax extenders legislation.
- Companies must identify a method for tracking expenditures and where research takes place, as these are now subject to amortization.
- Many companies may already track R&D costs to utilize Section 41 R&D tax credit, but Section 174 amortization requirements are more far-reaching.
- Further guidance does not exist to clarify or further define the new requirements.
- It may be worthwhile for companies to shift the location of some research projects to qualify for the shorter domestic R&D expenses amortization.
