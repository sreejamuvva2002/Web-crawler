# Hyundai-LG Georgia EV Battery Plant Partnership

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hyundai and LG’s $2B Investment Accelerates Georgia’s Role in the EV Revolution](https://www.reportlinker.com/article/3841/)  ·  `page_001232`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution announced a significant $2 billion investment in their battery manufacturing collaboration at Hyundai’s new EV plant in Georgia.
- The partnership between Hyundai and LG Energy Solution was initiated just three months prior to the announcement.
- The investment aims to enhance battery production capabilities in anticipation of Hyundai’s EV production start in 2025.
- The additional investment is expected to create hundreds of jobs.
- The initiative will bolster battery manufacturing facilities at the Metaplant in Bryan County.
- LG Energy Solution has agreements with Toyota to supply lithium batteries.
- LG Energy Solution announced a $3 billion investment in a Michigan plant for EV battery production.
- Georgia’s favorable business environment, skilled workforce, and strategic location have made it an attractive destination for companies looking to capitalize on the EV boom.
- The investments align with U.S. government objectives to accelerate the transition to electric vehicles, enhance energy storage solutions, and reduce carbon emissions.
- These developments are aligned with initiatives like the Inflation Reduction Act.
- Hyundai and LG Energy Solution’s expansion in Georgia contributes to the creation of a sustainable and resilient EV supply chain in the U.S.
- Strategic partnerships between Hyundai, LG Energy Solution, and other major players like Toyota signal a significant shift towards a robust EV ecosystem in the United States.
- Automotive giants and battery manufacturers are increasing their footprint in the U.S., driven by growing demand for electric vehicles and the strategic imperative to localize supply chains for better resilience and efficiency.
- Georgia is positioned at the forefront of the transformative wave toward electric mobility in the U.S.
