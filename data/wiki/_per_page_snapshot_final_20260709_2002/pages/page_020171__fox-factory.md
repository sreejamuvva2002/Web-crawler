# FOX Factory

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Fox Factory - Fabrication and Assembly Facility - BRPH](https://www.brph.com/portfolio-items/fabrication-and-assembly-facility/)  ·  `page_020171`

## Facts

- FOX Factory relocated their assembly facilities from Southern California to Gainesville, Georgia.
- The new facility represents a $47 million investment.
- The plant is vertically integrated and energy efficient for manufacturing shocks and suspension products for off-road powered vehicles.
- BRPH designed a 300,000 state-of-the-art facility.
- The facility provides more than 176,000-square-feet of assembly area.
- The building includes clean rooms, an anodizing line, CNC machining areas and shipping/receiving rooms.
- The facility supports 200 employees on site.
- Interior design features include branding, themed murals and product displays in and around the large cafeteria, locker rooms, and all-hands meeting areas to elevate company culture.
- This facility serves as the first phase of their relocation strategy.
- The second level temporarily houses corporate offices and support areas with views down into the assembly areas.
- An integrated tour experience is included in the office spaces aimed at interacting with vendors, customers, athletes, local partners and industry sponsors.
- This is FOX’s first vertically integrated factory, allowing products to move from materials arrival on one side of the building to finished products and shipping on the other.
- Locating manufacturing, anodizing, pre-assembly, full-assembly, and shipping within the same building reduces the need to outsource activities in their overall shock production process.
- Tooling includes an anodizing line, CNC machining and lathes.
- The facility also includes two clean rooms and nine loading docks.
- BRPH received the DBIA National Award of Merit in 2021 and the Distinguished Engineering Project Achievement Award from The Engineers’ Council in 2021 for this project.
