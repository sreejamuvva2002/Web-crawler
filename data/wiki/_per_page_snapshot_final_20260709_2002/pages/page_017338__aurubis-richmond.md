# Aurubis Richmond

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-24

**Source:** [Aurubis Richmond: First US multimetal recycling plant starts production of strategic metals](https://www.aurubis.com/richmond/media/Press-Releases/aurubis-richmond-first-us-multimetal-recycling-plant-starts-production-of-strategic-metals)  ·  `page_017338`

## Facts

- The press release announcing the start of production was dated September 24, 2025.
- Aurubis Richmond is located in Augusta, Georgia.
- Aurubis Richmond has started production as the first US multimetal recycling plant.
- The facility recovers strategic metals including copper, nickel, tin, and precious metals from complex recycling materials.
- The recovered metals are intended for American energy infrastructure, data centers, AI applications, and the defense industry.
- Aurubis AG is a leading global provider of non-ferrous metals and one of the largest copper recyclers worldwide.
- American industry currently requires approximately 1.8 million tons of copper per year.
- Experts estimate that demand for strategic metals like copper will increase nearly 30% over the next five years.
- The US currently imports about half of the copper it processes.
- Multimetal recycling is positioned to quickly close the domestic supply gap by processing complex recycling materials.
- The “First Melt” celebration attendees included US Representative Rick W. Allen for Georgia’s 12th District, German Ambassador to the United States Jens Hanefeld, and high-profile guests from politics and business.
- Aurubis CEO Dr. Toralf Haag stated that Aurubis Richmond makes the company the frontrunner in multimetal recycling in the US, matching its position in Europe.
- Dr. Toralf Haag indicated plans to continue expanding expertise in producing key strategic raw materials in the US due to high availability of recycling material and favorable local conditions.
- Georgia Governor Brian P. Kemp stated in a video message that Aurubis Richmond is now a significant employer for Georgia and plays a major role in the American multimetal supply chain.
- Governor Kemp described the facility as a testament to American manufacturing, independence, and resiliency.
- The project involves an investment of about $800 million.
- Aurubis is creating more than 240 jobs in Georgia.
- The plant will process up to 180,000 tons of complex recycling material annually.
- An expansion stage for the facility is scheduled to begin in 2026.
- Input materials for the plant include printed circuit boards, copper cable, and other metal-bearing products.
- Aurubis Richmond recovers critical metals on-site, effectively closing the value chain locally.
- The facility will be supplied with recycling materials from local sources to keep valuable raw materials in the domestic market.
- US Representative Rick W. Allen emphasized that the facility reinforces America’s ability to supply itself with metals for national security, infrastructure, and technology leadership.
- Representative Allen noted that Aurubis exemplifies how international partnerships can solidify America’s industrial foundation and strengthen economic independence.
- David Schultheis, President and Managing Director of Aurubis Richmond, stated the facility is now an important player in the North American multimetal supply chain.
- Schultheis thanked Georgia policymakers and governmental authorities for strong support, smooth approval processes, and enabling quick settlement.
- The new plant utilizes cutting-edge technology that meets the latest environmental standards enacted by the state of Georgia and federal authorities.
- Facility operations are designed with high consideration for water, air, and soil protection.
- Aurubis Richmond is designed as a flexible “one-stop shop” to receive a broad range of metal-bearing recycling materials from US suppliers.
