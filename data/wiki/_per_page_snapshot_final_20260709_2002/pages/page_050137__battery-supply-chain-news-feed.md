## Facts

- Ford and South Korea’s SK Innovation (SKI) announced they will manufacture next-generation battery cells and arrays in the US.
- Canada’s First Quantum Minerals is in talks with South Korea’s Posco Group about a potential joint battery venture.
- Norway’s Morrow Batteries will jointly develop anode materials for its planned lithium-ion battery cell facility.
- Australia-listed Jadar Resources and European Lithium will work together on the development of their respective lithium projects.
- Canadian cathode materials producer Nano One entered an advanced lithium-ion battery cathode materials coating development agreement.
- International separator group Entek is acquiring the majority stake in the lead battery separator business of Tokyo-based Nippon.
- Canadian Pacific (CP) will retrofit a line-haul diesel freight locomotive with hydrogen fuel cells and Leclanché’s European-manufactured lithium-ion batteries.
- Leclanché will deploy proprietary energy management software for the CP project to evaluate combined technologies’ readiness for the freight rail sector.
- CP operates a 21,000 km rail network across Canada and the US that serves deep-water ports on the Atlantic and Pacific Oceans.
- CP will conduct rail service trials and qualification testing once the locomotive is operational.
- Leclanché CEO Anil Srivastava stated battery systems and energy management software significantly increase total energy efficiency compared to standalone fuel cell modules, reducing total cost of ownership.
- Leclanché is evaluating the freight train market in Europe, Australia, Brazil and South Africa for diesel stock transformation.
- Leclanché unveiled a high-energy, 65 Ah lithium-ion pouch cell designed to meet demands of bus and truck manufacturers for increased range and operating time.
- Solid Power’s Series B investment round will enable production of full-scale automotive batteries and expand in-house production capabilities.
- Auto manufacturers aim to utilize Solid Power’s low-cost, high-energy all solid-state technology in forthcoming electric vehicles and have expanded existing joint development agreements.
- Solid Power is producing 20 ampere hour (Ah) multi-layer all solid-state batteries on a continuous roll-to-roll production line utilizing industry standard lithium-ion processes.
- CEO Doug Campbell plans to start producing automotive-scale batteries on the pilot production line in early 2022.
- Solid Power delivered hundreds of production line-produced battery cells validated by Ford and BMW late last year, formalizing commercialization plans.
- Ford and BMW will receive full-scale 100 Ah cells for automotive qualification testing and vehicle integration beginning in 2022.
- Volta Energy Technologies CEO Dr Jeff Chamberlain noted Volta invested early in Solid Power due to promising technology and a fundamental focus on manufacturability.
- Volta is a venture capital firm focused on breakthrough energy storage and battery innovations, spun out of the US Department of Energy’s Argonne National Laboratory.
- Leipzig is BMW’s pioneer plant for electromobility, building the BMW i3 since 2013, and will now produce electric drive train components.
- Battery modules will be produced in a 10,000 sqm production area previously reserved for the BMW i8 at Leipzig.
- BMW’s first of four coating lines for fifth-generation high-voltage batteries went on stream at its Regensburg plant, occupying over 40,000 sqm.
- High-voltage batteries will be produced in Regensburg from 2022.
- BMW battery modules are produced in a highly-automated process with plasma cleaning and specialized coating for optimal insulation.
- BMW uses different battery cells from supply partners depending on which provides the best properties for each vehicle concept.
- Michael Nikolaides, senior VP of production engines and e-drives at BMW, expects at least 50% of vehicles delivered worldwide to be fully electric by 2030.
- BMW is investing more than €250m (£217m) in Regensburg and Leipzig locations to supply electrified vehicles with high-voltage batteries.
- BMW produces high-voltage batteries and components at three German locations: Dingolfing, Leipzig, and Regensburg.
- Akasol, being acquired by US-headquartered BorgWarner, has an additional supply deal worth the high double-digit-million-euro range from mid-2022 until at least 2024.
- The Akasol order volume could increase to a low three-digit-million-euro amount if a potential option for further battery systems is taken up by 2027.
- Akasol CEO Sven Schulz stated the order for AKM CYC lithium-ion ultra-high energy systems is the second extension of cooperation with its long-term electric bus manufacturing customer.
- The Akasol systems use new cylindrical-cell battery modules allowing electric vehicles to increase range by more than 60%.
- The module achieves an energy density of approx. 221 Wh/kg with liquid-cooled, scalable battery modules at the system level.
- Commissioned Akasol battery systems will be produced on fully-automated lines at Gigafactory 1 in Darmstadt.
- Gigafactory 1 will have a production capacity of 2.5 GWh from that year onwards, potentially increasing up to 5 GWh.
- Akasol senior VP Klaus-Dieter Nagel stated follow-up orders will gradually increase production capacities at Gigafactory 1 over the coming quarters.
- Gigafactory 1 lines can produce up to 70 ultra-high-energy battery systems daily, each with about 100 kWh storage capacity.
- Porsche CEO Oliver Blume stated a factory is to be built in the southwest of Germany.
- Blume said Porsche will purchase battery cells from the Volkswagen Group but require high-performance cells.
- Porsche is researching high-performance batteries with silicon instead of graphite anodes to achieve higher energy density and better fast-charging capability.
- The new Porsche batteries will be produced in Europe and initially used in limited-production, high-performance vehicles and customer motorsport.
- BASF will hold a 51% stake in a venture with Shanshan to produce cathode active materials (CAM) and precursors (PCAM) in China for e-mobility and consumer electronics markets.
- The BASF-Shanshan move strengthens position in Asia and increases annual CAM capacity to 160 kilotons by 2022, with further expansions under way.
- BASF catalysts division president Dr Peter Schuhmacher stated the partnership brings two powerhouses in the battery materials market together.
- Shanshan chairman Yonggang Zheng stated the deal strengthens competitiveness in the Chinese market and accelerates integration into the global market.
- Shanshan’s portfolio includes CAM and PCAM for lithium-ion batteries, a raw materials supply chain, and a battery recycling operation.
- Shanshan operates four production sites for CAM and PCAM in Hunan and Ningxia, China, with an annual capacity of 90 kilotons by 2022.
- The BASF-Shanshan partnership deal is set for completion later this summer, subject to various approvals.
