# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-08

**Source:** [Explainer: What Hyundai companies are part of the Savannah-area car plant? - The Current](https://thecurrentga.org/2025/09/08/explainer-hyundai-metaplant-project/)  ·  `page_000278`

## Facts

- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp announced two years ago that South Korea’s Hyundai Motor Group was going to build an electric vehicle plant near Savannah.
- The deal promised nearly $8 billion and more than 8,000 well-paying jobs for hard-working Georgians.
- A federal raid occurred last week at the Hyundai Metaplant and the EV battery facility that is a joint venture between Hyundai and Korean-company LG Energy Solution.
- Federal officials detained approximately 475 foreign workers during an ongoing investigation into illegal labor practices.
- Approximately 300 of the detained workers were Korean.
- The Current GA has published numerous stories about misleading wage claims and workplace safety problems at the sprawling, 2,300-acre site in Bryan County.
- Hyundai assembled a complete supply chain of companies on the site that are separate legal entities but all have a role in meeting employment and wage targets for Georgia.
- Georgia provided $2 billion in state tax credits and abatements to Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America, the company that oversees production at the metaplant.
- Prior to the raid, HMGMA said it was not responsible for the actions of affiliates, suppliers, and subcontractors allegedly violating federal workplace safety laws.
- Hyundai announced on Friday that it was bringing a senior executive to supervise the firms amid the ongoing federal investigation at the battery plant.
- As of Monday, no arrests have been made regarding the detained workers.
- It is still unclear who employed those detained.
- The search warrant authorizing the raid cited the battery plant as a target, along with five other companies that appear to have served as subcontractors for the plant.
- Operations at the Hyundai site are intertwined, meaning a worker in one company may have been recruited by a different company or subcontractor.
- To build EVs, Hyundai organized its whole supply chain at the site, including affiliates Hyundai Material, Hyundai Mobis, Hyundai Glovis, Hyundai Steel, and Hyundai Transys.
- The supply chain also includes the battery factory with LG Energy Solution.
- Together, those companies must invest $5.545 billion and hire at least 8,500 workers by 2031 making an average of $58,105 in order to get tax credits and tax deferments offered by the state.
- As of mid-March, Hyundai told The Current that approximately 1,200 people were employed across the site, not counting Koreans.
- More than a dozen suppliers have set up shop in surrounding counties to make components such as brakes and bumpers.
- The Current has published investigative stories revealing alleged violations of labor and workplace safety laws at the plant.
