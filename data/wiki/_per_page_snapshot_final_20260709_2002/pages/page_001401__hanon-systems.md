# Hanon Systems

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** thermal_management  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp: Automotive Supplier Hanon Systems to Create 160 N](https://bxjmag.com/gov-kemp-automotive-supplier-hanon-systems-to-create-160-new-jobs-in-bulloch-county-georgia/)  ·  `page_001401`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Hanon Systems will create 160 new jobs and invest more than $40 million in a new manufacturing facility in Bulloch County.
- The announcement was made on May 31, 2023, while Governor Kemp was on an economic development trip to Israel.
- Hanon Systems is a global provider of automotive thermal and energy management solutions for electrified and conventional vehicles.
- The company's offerings include heating ventilation and air conditioning, powertrain cooling, compressors, fluid transport, electronics, and fluid pressure solutions.
- Hanon Systems will build its new facility at Gateway Regional Industrial Park in Bulloch County to assemble automotive thermal solutions.
- Operations at the new facility are expected to begin in May 2024.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated that Georgia’s automotive and e-mobility industries continue to grow as the state welcomes Hanon Systems.
- Governor Kemp noted that Georgia is furthering its position as the EV capital of America, with new jobs and opportunities coming to Georgians statewide.
- Min Sung, President and Chief Executive Officer of Hanon Systems, said the company is pleased to contribute to the Bulloch County economy through job creation.
- Min Sung thanked the State of Georgia and the cooperation between its agencies for guiding the project.
- Min Sung stated that the announcement aligns with Hanon Systems' strategy to support the industry shift toward electrification while growing its North America footprint.
- Billy Allen, Chair of the Development Authority of Bulloch County, said Statesboro-Bulloch County is honored by Hanon Systems’ decision to locate its new facility there.
- Billy Allen expressed excitement about welcoming an automotive supplier that will work closely with the Hyundai Metaplant and the electric vehicle industry.
- Billy Allen cited the decision as an example of the resources offered by the community and region to help companies succeed.
- Griff Lynch, Executive Director of the Georgia Ports Authority (GPA), said the announcement further solidifies Georgia’s position as a growing hub for auto manufacturing.
- Griff Lynch stated that GPA is proud to support the operations of Hyundai Motor Group and its suppliers in delivering jobs and opportunity for Georgia residents.
- Alyce Thornhill, Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy for the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team, represented GDEcD on this competitive project.
- The project was developed in partnership with the Development Authority of Bulloch County, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- Pat Wilson, GDEcD Commissioner, stated that suppliers like Hanon Systems successfully support larger industry ecosystems through partnership-driven solutions ranging from workforce training to logistics systems efficiencies.
- Pat Wilson noted that Bulloch County is part of the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority and has been part of Georgia’s growing success story in automotive manufacturing.
- Georgia is positioned as a hub for the electric mobility industry, building on assets that make the automotive industry successful.
- Since 2020, over 40 EV-related projects have announced more than $22.7 billion in investment and 28,400 jobs in Georgia.
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America officially broke ground on October 25 for its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility in neighboring Bryan County.
- Hanon Systems was formed in 1986 and has grown into a leading supplier of automotive thermal and energy management solutions.
