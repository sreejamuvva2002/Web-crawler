# Daimler Truck North America LLC (DTNA)

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Daimler Truck invests $285M to upgrade automotive manufacturing plant in Michigan | Plant Services](https://www.plantservices.com/industry-news/news/55261239/daimler-truck-invests-285m-to-upgrade-automotive-manufacturing-plant-in-michigan)  ·  `page_053542`

## Facts

- Daimler Truck North America LLC (DTNA) has announced plans to upgrade its manufacturing plant in Redford Township, Michigan.
- The plant is part of Detroit Diesel Corporation, a DTNA subsidiary.
- The company will invest up to $285 million into the project.
- The investment will create 400 new jobs.
- Funds will be invested in DTNA’s internal combustion engine (ICE) platform and research and development facilities.
- DTNA hopes the investment will help maintain its current production capacity.
- DTNA aims to increase innovation of ICE components through this project.
- The investment will support growing demand for cleaner, more fuel-efficient engines and components.
- Construction on the project is expected to begin this year.
- The Michigan Economic Development Corporation (MEDC) has offered DTNA a $27.7 million performance-based Critical Industries Program grant to help aid the project.
- DTNA has received Michigan Strategic Fund support for a State Essential Services Assessment abatement with an estimated value of up to $3.29 million.
- Matt Pfaffenbach, vice president of powertrain operations, stated that the Detroit Manufacturing Plant has a legacy of over 85 years in Michigan and stands as a testament to innovation and progress.
- Pfaffenbach noted the expansion is about creating job opportunities and upskilling the workforce to meet the demands of tomorrow’s technology.
- Pfaffenbach said the project will help stabilize the heavy-duty supply chain, strengthen DTNA's role in the industry, and bring significant growth to the community.
- Quentin Messer, Jr., CEO of the Michigan Economic Development Corporation and chair of the Michigan Strategic Fund Board, thanked Detroit Diesel for its commitment to Michigan’s hardworking mobility industry workers employed at its Redford Township facility.
- Messer stated that Michigan’s workforce, supply chain, and R&D strength in the mobility sector are the best in North America.
- Messer added that the project embodies the ‘Make It In Michigan’ economic development strategy focused on People, Places, and Projects.
- Hemlock Semiconductor received $325 million from the CHIPS Act to increase production of hyper-pure polysilicon in Michigan.
- The Hemlock Semiconductor project will generate 180 new jobs for the surrounding area.
- Saab is investing $75M to open a new Michigan manufacturing facility to support the U.S. military.
- Construction on the Saab facility will begin later this year, with production beginning in 2026.
- NanoGraf was awarded $60M to build a silicon anode battery materials manufacturing plant in Michigan.
- The NanoGraf facility will produce 2,500 tons per year of proprietary silicon anode material.
