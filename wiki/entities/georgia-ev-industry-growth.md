# Georgia EV Industry Growth

**Type:** Government Program  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[government-support|Government Support]]  ·  **Location:** Georgia, United States

## Overview

Georgia has become a major hub for electric-vehicle design, assembly and battery manufacturing, attracting more than $27 billion in private investment and multiple large-scale facilities across the state.

## EV supply-chain relevance

High - the article details numerous EV vehicle and battery projects, investments, jobs and supply-chain developments within Georgia.

## Workforce & partners

- Quality Jobs Tax Credit — State incentive. Offsets capital costs for multibillion-dollar facilities.
- Investment Tax Credit — State incentive. Provides tax relief to attract EV and battery manufacturers.
- Quick Start workforce program — State workforce training initiative. Allows companies to train employees before plants open; cited as one of the most effective in the country.

## Key facts

- Georgia has secured more than $27 billion in private investment tied to electric vehicles and battery manufacturing.
- The Hyundai Metaplant is a $7.59 billion integrated EV ecosystem designed to produce up to 300,000 vehicles per year.
- Rivian’s planned campus near Social Circle will have a projected workforce of 7,500 and capacity for 400,000 vehicles annually.
- Kia invested $200 million to retrofit its West Point plant, with the EV9 SUV now rolling off the line.
- SK Battery America built a $2.6 billion battery complex in Commerce supplying cells for Ford F-150 Lightning and Volkswagen ID.4.
- Around the Hyundai metaplant, more than $2.5 billion in additional supplier investments have been announced across multiple counties.
- State incentives such as the Quality Jobs Tax Credit, Investment Tax Credit, and Quick Start workforce program have been critical to attracting EV projects.
- Georgia’s universities and technical colleges, led by Georgia Tech, are aligned with EV sector talent needs, creating accelerated programs tied directly to employers like Rivian.
- Logistics firms DHL, UPS and GXO Logistics are expanding capacity to support the flow of parts between suppliers and assembly lines.
- By 2030, Georgia could be producing hundreds of thousands of electric vehicles annually, potentially rivaling Michigan in battery production capacity.
