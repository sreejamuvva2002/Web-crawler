# Anovion Technologies

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2023-06-28

**Source:** [Anovion Technologies holds groundbreaking ceremony | The Post-Searchlight](https://thepostsearchlight.com/2023/06/28/anovion-technologies-holds-groundbreaking-ceremony/)  ·  `page_052371`

## Facts

- A sizable crowd gathered off of Newton Road on Tuesday for the groundbreaking ceremony for Anovion Technologies.
- The company had previously announced its plan for a Bainbridge facility last month.
- Anovion Technologies is headquartered in Chicago.
- The company specializes in the production of synthetic graphite, a key component in lithium-ion batteries.
- According to Anovion CEO Eric Stopka at the initial announcement, this facility will be the first large-scale factory of its kind in North America.
- Development Authority Vice Chairman Andy Bell took to the podium first at the ceremony.
- County Commission Chair Pete Stephens delivered the invocation.
- Mayor Edward Reynolds spoke at the event and introduced Governor Kemp.
- Mayor Edward Reynolds stated that throughout Governor Kemp’s first term, Georgia has consistently broken economic records with jobs and investment coming to every corner of the state, including Bainbridge and Decatur County.
- Mayor Edward Reynolds touted the mayor’s record during his introduction, including tax cuts, teacher pay raises, and a crackdown on human trafficking.
- Governor Kemp thanked officials present before discussing Anovion at the podium.
- Governor Kemp stated that the event represents many great victories for the state and is a cumulation of hard work by local communities.
- Governor Kemp noted that projects like this result from months of careful talks and efforts at the state level through the Department of Economic Development and support from members of the general assembly.
- Governor Kemp praised Anovion’s business and its stated commitment to build up the American supply chain for lithium-ion batteries, noting much of it is currently based in Asia.
- Governor Kemp reiterated that the facility will create 400 new jobs.
- Governor Kemp reiterated that the project represents an $800 million investment.
- Governor Kemp stated that with the project’s groundbreaking, all four corners of Georgia have seen major investment and significant job creation as a result of leading the next Industrial Revolution in the state.
- Anovion Executive Chairman Chip Dunn spoke after Governor Kemp.
- Chip Dunn recounted how he met Governor Kemp and how Anovion was persuaded to come to Georgia.
- Chip Dunn stated that factors contributing to the decision included Bainbridge’s inland port and access to waterway and land transport.
- Chip Dunn stated that Anovion is the “only commercial sale battery-grade synthetic graphite company in North America.”
- Chip Dunn discussed the particulars of which components in lithium-ion batteries use synthetic graphite.
- Chip Dunn emphasized Anovion’s commitment to being climate-focused.
- Chip Dunn focused on the company’s commitment to reduce emissions associated with producing synthetic graphite.
- Chip Dunn stated: “Importantly we’re focused on shifting the narrative of a rather environmentally unfriendly process, if we’re being honest, in China, and demonstrate what our vision is, and what it truly means to be climate tech-driven through our state-of-the-art manufacturing facility.”
- Anovion hopes to reduce its production’s carbon footprint to zero over time.
- Chip Dunn stated: “We think that we can be the first major manufacturing business in the world to be carbon-free. It’s an ambition, but we think we have a chance to make it.”
- Congressman Sanford Bishop spoke about how state and national government were involved in bringing Anovion to the state.
- Congressman Sanford Bishop discussed how the state has benefitted from federal manufacturing incentives.
- Congressman Sanford Bishop stated that federal resources combined with resources from the governor and general assembly are making historic improvements in high-speed internet connecting families, schools, and businesses.
- Congressman Sanford Bishop stated: “The Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act is improving our roads, our ports, our railways, bridges and airports, the arteries through which our commerce flows.”
- SRTC President Jim Glass delivered the closing remarks at the ceremony.
- Jim Glass revealed that Anovion will be working to offer scholarships in the future, with updates to come later.
- The facility is projected to be operational by 2025.
