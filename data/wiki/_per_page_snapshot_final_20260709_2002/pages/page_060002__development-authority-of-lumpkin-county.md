# Development Authority of Lumpkin County

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Key Industries and Leading Employers – Development Authority of Lumpkin County](https://picklumpkincounty.org/key-industries/)  ·  `page_060002`

## Facts

- The Development Authority of Lumpkin County focuses daily on recruiting, assisting, and supporting industries related to Light Manufacturing, Headquarters, Healthcare & Social Services, and Hospitality.
- Lumpkin County contains a total of 38 manufacturing businesses with a workforce attractive to employers due to a balanced mix of skilled trade workers and advanced degree holders.
- The manufacturing industry accounts for 7.5% of jobs in Lumpkin County and is the number one employer among all goods-producing industries.
- Regional educational institutions provide training and degrees that support local manufacturing operations.
- Northeast Georgia Medical Center (NGMC) – Lumpkin campus was announced to open in 2024, becoming the fourth campus of the Northeast Georgia Health System.
- Lumpkin County has 73 establishments in the Healthcare and social assistance industry, accounting for 12.1% of county jobs.
- In partnership with the Lumpkin County School System, University of North Georgia, Lanier Technical College, and Northeast Georgia Health Systems, the county provides talent to support life science sectors including health information technology, research & development, and medical manufacturing.
- The region’s demographics supply the life sciences industry with a young workforce and a growing local market for healthcare services.
- Georgia is consistently ranked as one of the best states for business in the nation.
- Lumpkin County offers a high quality of life, competitive business environment, and supportive infrastructure, making it an attractive destination for corporate headquarters, regional offices, and government department centers.
- The community features diverse housing stock, family-friendly amenities, outdoor recreation opportunities, and excellent schools to attract businesses and employees.
- Dahlonega-Lumpkin County includes a historic downtown, plentiful outdoor recreation (kayaking/canoeing, fishing, mountain biking, running), the Appalachian Trail, and numerous wineries.
- The City of Dahlonega hosts arts venues, holiday/community events, and outdoor music throughout the year.
- Lumpkin County was named one of the South’s Best Mountain Towns by Southern Living and is located in the foothills of the Appalachian Mountains in Northeast Georgia.
- Lumpkin County has a retail gap of approximately $21 million within the Food Service & Drinking subsector.
- Retail and Accommodations & Food Service are two of the top employment industries in Lumpkin County.
- Retail options for dining and shopping continue to grow and diversify, particularly along the State Route 400 (SR 400) corridor.
- There is opportunity for unique retail, eating, and entertainment amenities in the award-winning Historic Downtown Dahlonega.
- Retail and entertainment traffic is primarily generated by Lumpkin County residents, tourists, and University of North Georgia faculty, staff, students, alumni, and parents.
- Lumpkin County hosts highly diversified and international companies with national and global recognition that produce high-quality products within a competitive and business-friendly community and state.
