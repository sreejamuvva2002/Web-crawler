# Novelis Inc

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2021-08-04

**Source:** [Novelis Inc (NVL) Q1 2022 Earnings Call Transcript - Alphastreet](https://news.alphastreet.com/novelis-inc-nvl-q1-2022-earnings-call-transcript/)  ·  `page_047380`

## Facts

- Novelis captured strong demand for aluminum flat rolled products across end markets, including electric vehicles.
- Growing consumer demand for vehicles using a higher share of aluminum, such as XUVs, trucks, and electric vehicles, supports a robust mid to long-term automotive outlook.
- The global semiconductor shortage impacting the automotive industry is affecting near-term demand for aluminum sheet.
- Novelis expanded its total automotive finishing capacity to approximately 1 million tons globally.
- Novelis recently completed its newest automotive finishing lines in the U.S. and China, which are now capable of producing commercial shipments.
- Novelis is working closely with customers on material qualification to meet strong demand for these new lines.
- Novelis plans a $375 million capital investment to expand capacity in Zhenjiang, China.
- The China investment will produce automotive coils to feed existing automotive finishing cash lines in Changzhou, located about 80 kilometers away.
- The investment entails a new automotive casthouse including recycling and re-melt capabilities, expanded hot mill capacity, and a new technologically advanced cold mill.
- Novelis estimates the China integration will unlock over $100 million in synergies.
- Novelis forecasts total acquisition synergy benefits to exceed $220 million on a run rate basis.
- Novelis achieved $100 million in run rate combination synergy savings by the end of Q1 FY2022, ahead of its original base case estimate of $85 million.
- Novelis expects its China automotive finishing lines to reach full utilization within about a year and a half.
- Novelis U.S. automotive finishing lines will take a few years to reach full utilization due to contractual commitments.
- Novelis EBITDA per ton at levels above $500 is very well sustainable.
- Industry hot mill capacity for aluminum is becoming extremely tight outside of China.
- Sustainability trends are driving continued growth in automotive and packaging demand.
- Novelis has identified approximately $1.5 billion in capex projects over the next five years, including debottlenecking, capacity expansion, and recycling initiatives.
- Novelis plans to spend an average of about $300 million per annum on maintenance over the next five years.
