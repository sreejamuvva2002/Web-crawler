# Anovion Technologies

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2025-07-16

**Source:** [Anovion still aiming for 2026 construction | The Post-Searchlight](https://thepostsearchlight.com/2025/07/16/anovion-still-aiming-for-2026-construction/)  ·  `page_052148`

## Facts

- Anovion Technologies officially broke ground for its new Bainbridge facility just over two years ago.
- There has been little visible construction at the industrial park site since groundbreaking, though developments have remained ongoing in the background.
- Patrick Donnelly serves as Anovion’s Chief Commercial Officer and provided updates on the progress of the Bainbridge facility.
- During a December 2024 Bainbridge city council meeting, Chairman Chip Dunn informed the council that Anovion planned to begin construction on a “mini-mill” by mid-2025, to be completed by 2026.
- Full production for the facility was scheduled at 2028.
- Donnelly stated that the company is still targeting 2026 to kick off the “mini-mill” as well as production in 2028 for the full facility.
- Donnelly noted that exact start and completion dates are dependent on several variables and he could not provide precise dates.
- In November of last year, Anovion was announced as being in talks with the Export-Import Bank of the United States for $400 million in project financing.
- Donnelly stated that they are still working with the Ex-Im Bank to meet the criteria to secure the financing.
- The financing process is dependent on factors, some of which are not completely under Anovion’s control.
- Donnelly stated that Anovion remains confident that this financing source will be part of the final package to build out the Bainbridge facility.
- Anovion’s main product is synthetic graphite, a key component in electric vehicles, and federal policy decisions and bills have impacted the company.
- The most recent influencing factors include the One Big Beautiful Bill Act, as well as tariffs.
- Donnelly stated that Anovion is still assessing the impacts of the OBBBA, with some positives and negatives for battery cell producers.
- A positive impact identified was restrictions placed on the use of foreign entity of concern (FEOC) material assistance.
- A negative result identified is the loss of EV tax credits that had been a part of the Biden administration’s Inflation Reduction Act.
- Donnelly stated that as 95%+ of synthetic graphite used for battery cells currently comes from China, there should be tailwinds for Anovion’s value proposition to domestic cell makers coming out of the OBBBA.
- Donnelly added that ongoing uncertainty around tariffs and trade policy plays a significant role in the timing of the project and contracts.
- Donnelly concluded that Anovion wants to get into business at scale in Bainbridge as fast as prudently possible to address both the national security needs of the US and build out a critical part of the battery supply chain of the energy transition throughout North America.
