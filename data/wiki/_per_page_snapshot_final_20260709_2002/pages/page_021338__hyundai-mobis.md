# Hyundai Mobis

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** thermal_management  ·  **Published:** 2024-12-20

**Source:** [Will This Device Solve EV Battery Heat Issues?](https://www.autoweek.com/news/a63249725/hyundai-mobis-battery-cooling-technology/)  ·  `page_021338`

## Facts

- Hyundai Mobis has developed a new battery-cell cooling device dubbed Pulsating Heat Pipe (PHP), designed to transfer heat from between cells to the exterior more efficiently.
- The PHP device is composed of aluminum alloy and refrigerant and is positioned between battery cells to dissipate heat during DC fast-charging.
- Hyundai Mobis says PHP offers 10 times the heat transfer performance of common aluminum, lowering charging time by permitting faster charging cycles.
- The pipes function as metal-tube-shaped thermal conductors that diffuse heat through vibration while circulating refrigerant internally to move heat from the cells to the exterior.
- Hyundai Mobis is optimistic about putting PHPs into production with minimal effort at the manufacturing level.
- The company applied a press process enabling large-scale continuous production in the manufacturing stage, simplifying the PHP manufacturing process and reducing production costs.
- Lower battery temperatures will permit faster charging speeds and reduce the time owners need to spend at the charging station.
- PHP technology could address EV battery weight issues by making electrically driven cooling fans unnecessary or less bulky.
- Solid-state designs are noted as a potential competitor promising greater thermal safety, but such technology remains largely confined to the lab for now.
- Hyundai Mobis aims to secure world-class battery cooling technology and commercialize it to enhance its competitiveness in the future mobility market.
