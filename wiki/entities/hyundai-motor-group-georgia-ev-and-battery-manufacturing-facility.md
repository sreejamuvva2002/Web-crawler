# Hyundai Motor Group Georgia EV and Battery Manufacturing Facility

**Type:** Facility  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[vehicle-manufacturing|Vehicle Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** Georgia, United States

## Overview

The first fully dedicated electric vehicle and battery manufacturing plant announced by Hyundai Motor Group in the state of Georgia, slated for production start in early 2025 with an annual capacity of 300,000 vehicles.

## EV supply-chain relevance

A dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility that expands Georgia's electric vehicle supply chain and creates high-skill jobs.

- **Hyundai Motor Group Georgia EV and Battery Manufacturing Facility** — Announced May 2022; construction began Jan 2023; production expected first half of 2025.

## Timeline

- **May 2022** — Hyundai Motor Group announced plans for its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility in Georgia.
- **January 2023** — Construction began on the Hyundai Georgia EV and battery plant.
- **First half of 2025** — Projected start of full production at the Hyundai Georgia facility with an annual capacity of 300,000 units.

## Workforce & partners

- Electric Vehicle Career Pathway — Workforce training partner. Developed by Georgia Department of Education in partnership with industry leaders to provide EV-specific coursework for high-school students.

## Key facts

- Since 2018, 35 EV-related projects have contributed $23 billion in investments in Georgia.
- Georgia is sixth in the nation for public EV charging stations, offering more than 1,500 individual outlets.
- The state has over 1,200 miles of federally designated EV-ready Alternative Fuel Corridors along its interstates and highways.
- Georgia is due to receive $135 million in federal funds from the infrastructure spending bill to build additional EV charging stations.
- Georgia’s workforce includes engineers, electricians, machinists, software developers, and cybersecurity specialists ready for EV manufacturing jobs.
- The Georgia Department of Education, working with industry leaders, is developing an Electric Vehicle Career Pathway for high-school students.
