# Target Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2024-06-24

**Source:** [Target’s fourth ‘flow’ distribution center opens in Georgia | Supply Chain Dive](https://www.supplychaindive.com/news/target-new-supply-chain-facility-georgia/719557/)  ·  `page_011895`

## Facts

- Target opened a new distribution center in Hampton, Georgia.
- The new facility is Target’s fourth full-service “flow” center.
- The facility helps replenish stores and ship products to customers with more speed and efficiency.
- Target opened its first such facility in New Jersey in 2018.
- Target now has seven supply chain facilities in Georgia.
- The newest Hampton facility will support more than 400 jobs.
- The new facility allows the company to improve its shipping efficiency in the Southeast.
- Target CEO Brian Cornell told analysts during a Q4 2023 earnings call that the retailer planned to add 10 new supply chain facilities to its distribution network over the next 10 years.
- Cornell said in a Q1 2024 earnings call that the company is continuing to introduce new sortation centers meant to enhance its last-mile delivery capabilities.
- New facilities, such as the recently opened flow center in Hampton, can upgrade Target’s ability to offer additional delivery options.
- Earlier this year, Target announced plans to open more than 300 new stores in addition to expanding its owned brands and membership rewards program.
- Brian Cornell said in the Q4 2023 earnings call: “Without huge investments in stores, supply chain, and tech, there is no drive up or order pickup.”
- As of February, Target had 58 total supply chain facilities with a combined footprint of 61.5 million square feet, according to a securities filing.
- According to Gretchen McCarthy, Target’s chief supply chain and logistics officer, Georgia “has become an increasingly important location within Target’s supply chain network.”
- McCarthy added that Target has “spent millions of dollars over the last several years expanding our presence” in Georgia.
- The Peach State’s significance as a supply chain hub has been on the rise recently.
- The Port of Savannah posted record growth in fiscal 2023.
- The Georgia Ports Authority has continued to report increases in cargo volumes as companies like Hyundai invest in manufacturing plants in the state.
