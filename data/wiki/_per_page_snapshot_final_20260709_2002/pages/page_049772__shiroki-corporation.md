# Shiroki Corporation

## Facts

- Sales were 123,425 million JPY in FY2008, a 14.0% decrease year-on-year due to a substantial slowdown in the global economy and decreased vehicle sales in the USA and Japan.
- Operating income for FY2008 was an operating loss of 31 million JPY.
- Ordinary income for FY2008 was a loss of 773 million JPY.
- Net income for FY2008 was a loss of 2,190 million JPY.
- Shiroki North America Inc. reduced production due to a substantial decrease in orders from automakers caused by the US economic slowdown and foreign exchange losses.
- North American sales were 22 billion 735 million yen, a 20.4% year-on-year decrease, with an operating loss of 490 million yen.
- The company developed the world's lightest weight wind regulator jointly with Toyota and supplied it for Toyota's small car 'iQ'.
- Technico Industries, Ltd. in Gurgaon, Haryana, India won a contract to supply all window regulators for Toyota's newly developed small model starting production in India in 2010.
- Technico Industries will build a new plant in the suburbs of Bangalore near Toyota's existing production facilities.
- Shiroki Corporation decided to increase its investment ratio in Technico Industries, Ltd.
- Domestic plants in Japan switched to permanent one-shift operation starting early 2009 due to automaker production cutbacks and globally shrinking demand.
- The company will review employee allocation and make facility investments for higher efficiency based on one-shift operations, incorporating this into 'Shiroki Vision 2012'.
- Shiroki Corporation made an additional capital injection of 175 million rupees (approx. 437 million yen) in Technico Industries Ltd. in India.
- Technico Industries manufactures and sells automotive parts including window regulators, door hinges, and gear shift levers.
- The company aims to better serve customers planning to increase production in India and explore opportunities in India, China, Thailand, and other regions for new supply contracts.
- R&D expenses for the automotive components business were 2,358 million JPY in FY2008.
- Recent R&D achievements include next-generation power seats, next-generation manual seats, new wire-type regulators, new arm-type regulators, and new door frames.
- Shiroki has a technical licensing-in partnership with Keiper-Recaro GmbH & Co. (Germany) for seat recliners from Apr 1, 1988 to Mar 31, 2010.
- Shiroki has technical licensing-out partnerships with NHK Spring (Thailand) for seat recliners, Summit Laemchabang Auto Body Co. (Thailand) for door frames, and Asan Co., Ltd. (Korea) for automotive door frames.
- Overall capital expenditure was 6,631 million JPY in FY2008, mostly devoted to the automotive component business.
- Shiroki North America, Inc. in Tennessee, USA installed manufacturing equipment with an estimated investment of 134 million JPY from Apr 2009 to Mar 2010.
- Shiroki-GA, LLC in Georgia, USA installed manufacturing equipment with an estimated investment of 105 million JPY from Apr 2009 to Mar 2010.
- Shiroki-GT, LLC in Tennessee, USA installed manufacturing equipment with an estimated investment of 30 million JPY from Apr 2009 to Mar 2010.
- Guangzhou Shiroki Corp. in Guangdong, China installed manufacturing equipment with an estimated investment of 375 million JPY from Apr 2009 to Mar 2010.
