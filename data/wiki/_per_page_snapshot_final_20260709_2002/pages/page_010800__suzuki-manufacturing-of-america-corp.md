# Suzuki Manufacturing of America Corp.

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-03-01

**Source:** [Suzuki Manufacturing of America Corp. Becomes Subsidiary of Suzuki Motor USA, LLC - Motorcycle & Powersports News](https://www.motorcyclepowersportsnews.com/suzuki-manufacturing-america-corp-becomes-subsidiary-suzuki-motor-usa-llc/)  ·  `page_010800`

## Facts

- Suzuki Manufacturing of America Corp. (SMAC) became a 100% subsidiary of Suzuki Motor USA, LLC (SMO) effective March 1, 2024.
- SMAC was founded in 2001 and is based in Rome, Georgia.
- SMAC serves as Suzuki’s All-Terrain Vehicle (ATV) manufacturing facility.
- For over two decades, all Suzuki adult KingQuad ATVs have been manufactured at the Rome, Georgia location.
- Suzuki ATVs are sold in the U.S., Canada, and nearly 25 countries worldwide.
- Prior to this change, SMAC stock was owned by Suzuki Motor Corp. (SMC) at 20% and Suzuki Motor of America, Inc. (SMAI) at 80%, with SMAI owned by SMC as the holding company for Suzuki’s U.S. operations.
- The organizational change consolidates ATV sales and production arms to strengthen Suzuki’s overall ATV business in the U.S. and globally.
- Masami Haga, president of SMO, stated that a primary goal is to expand commitment to the growth of the entire powersports business and build on relationships with Suzuki dealers and SMAC vendor partners.
- Strengthening the supply chain and purchasing policies for locally sourced parts are objectives of the move.
- Integrated production and sales functions are expected to improve responsiveness to the ATV market by more accurately linking production to sales demand.
- SMO aims to improve inventory management for both the company and its dealer body to protect sales margins, flooring costs, and profitability.
- ATV industry sales are generally steady throughout the calendar year with demand typically increasing in the fall months.
- The change will result in better, more direct communication between SMO and SMAC, improving staff interaction between the two companies.
- A more collaborative environment will be created between the production and sales groups of Suzuki ATVs, fostering better connection between the Rome- and Brea-based teams.
- Suzuki’s U.S. distribution headquarters are located in Brea, California, where the company relocated in 1981 after first entering the U.S. market in 1963.
- SMAC becoming a subsidiary of SMO sets the stage for new Suzuki ATV offerings and enhancements to existing products in the future.
