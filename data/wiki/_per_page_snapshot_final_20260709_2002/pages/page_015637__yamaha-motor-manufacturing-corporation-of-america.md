# Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation of America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Yamaha Celebrates 35 Years | Yamaha](https://www.yamaha-motor-georgia.com/node/50)  ·  `page_015637`

## Facts

- Plant construction for Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation of America started in 1986 in Newnan, Georgia.
- The company began operations in 1988 with a small crew assembling golf cars on a 280-acre campus comprising six plants.
- The first unit to roll off the manufacturing line was a golf car in June of 1988.
- WaveRunner production was added by January of the following year (1989).
- Over the years, production gradually expanded to encompass ATVs and Side-by-Side Recreational Offroad Vehicles.
- The facility currently employs over 2000 team members.
- Yamaha has produced over four million recreational vehicles across its product lines.
- Every WaveRunner, Golf Car, and side-by-side ROV that Yamaha markets is manufactured in Newnan, Georgia.
- Most of the world’s Yamaha ATVs are also assembled in Newnan, Georgia.
- Original team member Ken Ellerbee started at the plant in January 1988 as an electronics technician working on robotics systems for welding and has since advanced to SMART Construction Manager.
- The company maintains a commitment to environmental health and safety, holding ISO 9001 and 14001 certifications.
- Yamaha received the Georgia State Natural Leadership Award for Continual Environmental Improvement.
- The facility was named Coweta Water Authority Plant of the Year.
- Every dollar collected from Yamaha’s extensive recycling program is reinvested into corporate social responsibility initiatives, including scholarships, science education for local students, and disaster response.
- Yamaha regularly sponsors entire classes from Title 1 schools to experience a science classroom on the lake through the Chattahoochee Riverkeeper’s floating classroom program for water quality and conservation experiments.
- The company is investing in a new SMART Warehouse facility spanning 200,000 square feet to improve manufacturing efficiencies.
- The SMART Warehouse utilizes high-speed automation technology to immediately send parts to team members on the production floor.
- Yamaha has adopted a Carbon Neutral program aimed at reducing the carbon footprint for its entire campus by 2035.
- Progress toward the carbon neutral goal is being driven through the installation of solar panels across the rooftop of Plant 2.
- Bob Brown serves as Vice President of finance and operational support at Yamaha and is a Chamber Board member.
