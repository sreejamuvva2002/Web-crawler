# Central Piedmont Community College Automotive Systems Technology Program

**Type:** workforce_training  ·  **Role:** workforce_training  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-03

**Source:** [Automotive Systems Technology | Central Piedmont](https://www.cpcc.edu/programs/automotive-systems-technology)  ·  `page_058865`

## Facts

- The automotive systems technology program equips students with technical knowledge and skills for employment in the transportation industry.
- Training includes classroom and lab experiences teaching how to repair, service, and maintain automobiles of all different makes and models.
- Class time covers theory and the servicing/operation of brakes, electrical/electronic systems, engine performance, steering/suspension, automatic transmission/transaxles, engine repair, climate control, and manual drive trains.
- Upon completion, students are prepared to take the ASE (Automotive Service Excellence) exam and enter the workforce as full-time automotive service technicians in dealerships, fleet shops, or independent repair shops.
- The program is certified by the National Automotive Technicians Education Foundation (NATEF).
- The program has secured partnerships with transport systems businesses nationwide that sponsor students.
- The program hosts specialized manufacturer-based training programs for BMW, GM, and Honda.
- It became the first community college in the country to host the Tesla START program, providing skills for job placement as service technicians at Tesla service centers.
- The degree program includes a work-based learning component allowing students to gain career experience while earning money.
- Successful graduates of the program are often employed before graduation.
- Students have the opportunity to participate in the Skills USA - Automotive student organization.
- Program options include an Associate in Applied Science, a diploma, and several certificates in automotive technology.
- In-person classes and labs are held on the Levine Campus and the Merancas Campus.
- Classes are delivered via in-person or combined classroom/lab and online formats, with schedules changing each semester.
- The college offers a Commercial Driving Licensure (CDL) program providing hands-on experience operating tractor trailers.
- Dual-enrolled students can pursue ACE certifications while finishing their senior year in Charlotte-Mecklenburg Schools.
- Financial aid and scholarships are available for the affordable, hands-on education and career training provided by the college.
