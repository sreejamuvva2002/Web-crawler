# Archer Aviation Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** power_electronics  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Archer to Supply Its Proprietary Electric Powertrain to Third Parties, Starting with Anduril and EDGE Group for their Recently Announced Omen Autonomous Air Vehicle](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20251117709771/en/Archer-to-Supply-Its-Proprietary-Electric-Powertrain-to-Third-Parties-Starting-with-Anduril-and-EDGE-Group-for-their-Recently-Announced-Omen-Autonomous-Air-Vehicle)  ·  `page_004750`

## Facts

- Archer is making its proprietary, dual-use advanced powertrain technology (currently used on its Midnight eVTOL aircraft) available to third parties for the first time, introducing a new revenue stream.
- Archer strategically vertically integrated the design and manufacturing of its battery pack to meet rigorous aviation safety, performance, and reliability standards.
- The Omen, a hover-to-cruise autonomous air vehicle co-developed by Anduril and EDGE Group in the UAE, will be the first third-party product to receive Archer's powertrain technology.
- Archer manufactures its electric powertrain across nearly 1,000,000 square feet of manufacturing and test facilities in the U.S., applying automation to key areas for reliable, scalable production.
- Archer Aviation Inc. (NYSE: ACHR) announced an agreement to supply its dual-use electric powertrain technology to Anduril Industries and EDGE Group to accelerate development and scaled production of the Omen autonomous air vehicle system.
- The UAE has committed to an initial acquisition of 50 Omen systems, creating a demand signal for both the vehicle and Archer's powertrain technology.
- Archer has invested heavily in developing a proprietary, best-in-class electric powertrain highly optimized for commercial and defense aviation use cases.
- Archer's expansion into supplying electric powertrains for defense applications builds on its accumulated experience in battery and electric engine technologies.
- Archer founder and CEO Adam Goldstein stated that the company views its Midnight eVTOL as a platform for various aerospace technologies and expects the Anduril powertrain deal to be the first of many similar collaborations.
- Anduril SVP of Engineering Shane Arnott noted that combining Archer's powertrain expertise with five years of internal work has matured their propulsion solution to meet required ranges, speeds, and payload capacity for dual-use missions.
- Archer announced an exclusive agreement with Korean Air to commercialize aerial mobility (AAM) technology across multiple markets and use cases.
- The cities of Osaka and Tokyo have selected Archer and its local partner to lead aerial mobility deployments in Japan.
- Archer's Midnight aircraft is a piloted design capable of carrying four passengers with rapid back-to-back trips and minimal charge times between flights.
- The Midnight aircraft aims to replace 60–90 minute car commutes with 10–20 minute electric air taxi flights that are safe, sustainable, low-noise, and cost-competitive with ground transportation.
- EDGE Group, headquartered in Abu Dhabi, capital of the UAE, consolidates more than 35 entities into five core clusters: Platforms & Systems, Missiles & Weapons, Space & Cyber Technologies, Technology & Innovation, and Homeland Security.
