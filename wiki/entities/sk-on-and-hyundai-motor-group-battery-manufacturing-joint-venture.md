# SK On and Hyundai Motor Group Battery Manufacturing Joint Venture

**Type:** Joint Venture  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[battery-cell-manufacturing|Battery Cell Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** Bartow County, Georgia, United States

## Overview

A joint venture between SK On and Hyundai Motor Group to build an electric vehicle battery manufacturing facility in Georgia, projected to create 3,500 jobs and attract significant state incentives.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Battery manufacturing for electric vehicles within the Georgia supply chain.

## Facilities

| Facility | Location | County | Status | Capacity |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Hyundai Battery Plant (Bryan County) | Along I-16, Bryan County, Georgia | Bryan County | Groundbreaking completed; under construction | 300,000 electric vehicles annually in Phase 1, expanding to 500,000 later |
| Bartow County Battery Facility (Proposed) | Bartow County, Georgia | Bartow County | Planned / pending incentives | — |

- **Hyundai Battery Plant (Bryan County)** — Planned hiring of 8,100 workers; part of a $1.8 billion state stimulus package.
- **Bartow County Battery Facility (Proposed)** — Expected to receive a similar incentive package including grants, tax breaks, free infrastructure and worker training.

## Key facts

- More than 548,000 electric vehicles were sold in the United States during the first nine months of this year, up 70% from the same period in 2021.
- EVs accounted for about 5.4% of all new car sales in the first three quarters of the year, double the share from the previous year.
- Georgia is positioning itself as a major EV hub, having recruited Rivian for a $5 billion factory that will employ 7,500 workers.
- State and local leaders highlighted Hyundai’s commitment to create thousands of jobs at suppliers around Georgia as justification for a record $1.8 billion stimulus package.
- The Bartow County battery facility is expected to receive grants, tax breaks, free infrastructure, and worker-training support similar to the Bryan County plant.
- Good Jobs First reported that state and local governments have contributed nearly $13.8 billion in incentives to at least 51 EV and battery manufacturers nationwide, with Georgia setting aside about $3.3 billion for Hyundai and Rivian.
- "The population in the Southeast is growing, drawing people from all over the country and the world," said David Clayton, emphasizing a strong labor pool for EV-related businesses.
