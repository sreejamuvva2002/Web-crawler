# Hyundai Motor

## Facts

- José Muñoz serves as Hyundai Motor president.
- Hyundai Motor emphasized investment plans for the U.S. state of Georgia, where about 300 Koreans were detained.
- In an interview published on the 15th (local time) with the Atlanta Business Chronicle, Muñoz said he would invest $26 billion (about 38 trillion won) in the United States over four years.
- Muñoz stated that the investment policy remains unchanged.
- Hyundai will maximize the productivity of the Georgia plant through robotic automation and artificial intelligence (AI).
- Hyundai will localize automobile production and the logistics supply chain to reduce expense and increase competitiveness.
- Hyundai will continue hiring until it reaches maximum production.
- Hyundai will create 40,000 direct and indirect jobs in Georgia.
- On tariffs, Muñoz stated that localization is a simple solution.
- Hyundai has discussed this localization solution for some time and is implementing it now.
- In September, U.S. immigration authorities arrested about 300 Korean workers at the construction site of the Hyundai Motor Group–LG Energy Solution battery joint-venture plant in Georgia.
- The detained workers were released following South Korea–U.S. diplomatic talks.
- Local media reported that factors such as insufficient local hiring may have provided grounds for the crackdown.
