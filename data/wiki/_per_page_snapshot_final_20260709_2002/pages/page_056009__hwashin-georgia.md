# Hwashin Georgia

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2024-09-23

**Source:** [VSU, Hwashin Collaboration Helps Innovative Ideas Become Sustainable Businesses - Valdosta State University](https://www.valdosta.edu/about/news/releases/2024/09/vsu-hwashin-collaboration-helps-innovative-ideas-become-sustainable-businesses.php)  ·  `page_056009`

## Facts

- Valdosta State University has partnered with Hwashin Georgia to promote entrepreneurship and small business development by supporting the creation of ventures conceived, managed, and owned by students.
- Yohan Yeon serves as supply chain manager for Hwashin Georgia.
- Sungkee Kim is the chief executive officer of Hwashin Georgia.
- Hwashin Georgia donated $10,000 to support the J. Donald Lee Center for Entrepreneurship’s annual Business Plan Competition.
- The Business Plan Competition is open to all VSU students, regardless of major or business experience.
- Participating students develop their understanding of a successful business plan and receive expert feedback from industry leaders.
- Students have the opportunity to win seed money to help turn their ideas into reality.
- Dr. Byeonghwa Park, assistant professor of management for VSU’s Harley Langdale Jr. College of Business Administration and director of the J. Donald Lee Center for Entrepreneurship, stated that the competition helps students gain real-world experience, enhances entrepreneurial skills, and provides a chance to win financial support for their ventures.
- The competition promotes student engagement with industry leaders and potential future employers like Hwashin.
- The J. Donald Lee Center for Entrepreneurship will begin accepting applications for this year’s Business Plan Competition in November.
- Students have nearly three months to register for the event and submit their initial business ideas.
- The final round of the competition takes place in April, when finalists present their full business plans to a panel of judges.
- Hwashin is a specialized manufacturer of chassis and body parts for automobiles.
- Hwashin was established in Korea in 1975.
- Following its successful expansion into Greenville, Alabama, in 2003, the company is now making a significant new investment in Dublin, Georgia.
- Hwashin aims to begin mass production by December 2025 as part of its Dublin, Georgia expansion.
- Sungkee Kim stated that Hwashin looks forward to a strong partnership with Valdosta State University and anticipates positive outcomes from the Business Plan Competition supported by Hwashin.
- Sungkee Kim stated that the company aspires to be a valuable contributor to the economic growth of the Georgia region.
- VSU’s Harley Langdale Jr. College of Business Administration opened the J. Donald Lee Center for Entrepreneurship in early 2023.
- The center is located in Pound Hall on the Rea and Lillian Steel North Campus at Valdosta State University.
- The center brings emerging student entrepreneurs, faculty, staff, and industry leaders together to ignite innovation and foster connections across the university, region, and world.
- The center offers a wide range of resources, technology, equipment, and mentorship needed to unlock the potential of the next startup generation.
