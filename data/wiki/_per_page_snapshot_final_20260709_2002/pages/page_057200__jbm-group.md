# JBM Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2019-02-25

**Source:** [JBM Group Acquires Majority Stake in Germany's Linde-Wiemann | Business News - News18](https://www.news18.com/news/business/jbm-group-acquires-majority-stake-in-germanys-linde-wiemann-2047879.html)  ·  `page_057200`

## Facts

- JBM Group is an Indian auto component major.
- JBM Group has acquired a majority stake in Germany's Linde-Wiemann GmbH KG.
- The acquisition is being executed through JBM Group's flagship company, Neel Metal Products.
- The deal will provide JBM with access to global platforms and German technology inheritance for its Indian operations.
- JBM did not disclose the financial value of the acquisition deal.
- Linde-Wiemann (L+W) generates revenue of USD 700 million.
- L+W operates 17 plants across eight countries: Germany, US, China, Spain, Turkey, Czech Republic, Hungary, and South Africa.
- L+W employs over 2,300 people globally.
- JBM Group Executive Director Nishant Arya stated the acquisition is a strategic fit perfectly in sync with their existing scope of operations and solutions.
- Arya noted the acquisition will further consolidate JBM's global footprint.
- JBM intends to leverage L+W's European experience combined with its own auto component expertise.
- The combined entity will focus on developing new products that improve vehicle safety and performance through light-weighting.
- The combination aims to minimize time to market for original equipment manufacturers (OEMs).
- The combination will offer an integrated one-stop solution to customers.
- Through the acquisition, JBM gains access to L+W's capabilities in electric vehicle components and systems.
- Through the acquisition, JBM gains access to L+W's capabilities in battery structure and systems.
- Linde-Wiemann CEO Ulrich Schoof stated the new business model will allow L+W to unlock substantial growth by adapting product competencies in chassis and suspension.
- Schoof added that L+W will extend its holistic system understanding in e-mobility through knowledge exchange with JBM's electrical bus division.
- Linde-Wiemann is headquartered in Dillenburg, Germany.
- L+W specializes in hot forming, tubular assemblies, and the use of alternate materials like aluminium for auto system light-weighting.
- L+W supplies parts to global automotive OEMs including BMW, Volkswagen, Audi, Skoda, Porsche, Daimler, Ford, and PSA.
