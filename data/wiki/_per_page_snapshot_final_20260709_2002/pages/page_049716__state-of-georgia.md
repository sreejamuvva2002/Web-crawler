# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Georgia: For Businesses Wishing to Compete on a Global Scale | Trade and Industry Development](https://www.tradeandindustrydev.com/region/georgia/georgia-businesses-wishing-compete-global-scale-542)  ·  `page_049716`

## Facts

- Georgia is positioned as an ideal location for businesses wishing to compete on a global scale.
- Georgia has two deep-water ports located in Brunswick and Savannah.
- Hartsfield-Jackson International Airport serves as a major logistics hub with more than 2,700 daily departures and arrivals.
- Eighty percent of the U.S. market can be reached within a two-hour flight from Atlanta via Hartsfield-Jackson.
- Hartsfield-Jackson is the country’s 10th busiest airport in terms of cargo volume.
- The airport's cargo handling areas provide convenient access to Interstates 75, 85, 285 and 20.
- Atlanta is one of five U.S. cities with three cross-country interstates (Interstates 20, 75 and 85) intersecting near downtown.
- Georgia’s five interstates, including 16 and 95, consistently rank highly for their condition.
- Freight can reach 80 percent of U.S. markets within two days from Georgia due to its central location.
- Georgia has 4,700 miles of rail, making it the best-connected state in the Southeast.
- Two of the country’s largest Class 1 rail systems, CSX and Norfolk Southern, provide the majority of rail service in Georgia.
- Twenty-four short-line rail companies operate on almost 1,500 miles of tracks in Georgia.
- Georgia’s ports are the country’s fastest-growing at a rate of more than 20 percent.
- The Port of Savannah is now the fourth busiest container terminal in the U.S.
- In 1996, Atlanta was designated as the first U.S. Customs Inland Port to prepare for the Summer Olympic Games.
- This designation made Atlanta a Foreign Trade Zone, allowing containers to travel inbound duty-free from coastal ports.
- Georgia offers a performance-based package of business assistance designed to foster success for companies and their communities.
- The state has one of the lowest corporate tax rates in the nation at 6 percent.
- Georgia provides corporate tax credits including single factor corporate tax, job tax credits, existing industry and headquarters tax credits, and research and development incentives.
- Tax credit amounts depend on the “tier status” of the community, which is an annual four-tier ranking of economic vitality across Georgia’s 159 counties.
- The highest tax credits are available in counties with the greatest need (Tiers 1 and 2).
- Georgia offers sales and use tax exemptions and inventory tax exemptions for qualified equipment purchases and leases.
- More than 100 Georgia counties offer up to 100 percent exemptions for qualified raw material, work-in-process and finished goods under the local-option “Freeport” law.
- The state Department of Labor assists companies in recruitment by posting job notices, collecting and screening applications, providing interview space, scheduling interviews and hosting job fairs.
- The state’s Technical College System hosts Quick Start, an award-winning customized workforce training program provided at no cost to employers.
- Georgia’s Intellectual Capital Partnership Program (ICAPP) taps into public colleges and universities to tailor programs for highly skilled workers.
- Georgia’s HOPE Scholarship and Grant provides free tuition at state public colleges, universities and technical schools for graduating high school seniors with a B average or higher.
- Georgia is number one in the U.S. for attracting the highly-educated 25-34 demographic.
- The state turns out more than 44,000 graduates each year from its large, high-quality university system.
- In 2006, Kia Motors decided to build its first U.S. manufacturing facility in Georgia.
- Many automotive supplier companies followed Kia to Georgia, including Hyundai Mobis, Dongwon Autopart Technology, Glovis, Kumho Tire, Johnson Controls, Sewon America Inc., Sejong Georgia, DongNam Tech and DAEHAN Solution.
- Advanced manufacturing is designated as one of the state’s strategic industries.
- More than 60 countries have consular, trade or bi-national chambers of commerce offices in the state.
- The state is home to more than 1,600 internationally-owned facilities representing at least 43 countries.
- Georgia has 10 international offices, including a business center in China, to help companies find new markets.
- The state employs strategically placed regional staff to keep companies apprised of new incentives and programs and help them find new facilities for expansion.
- Twenty-six Fortune 1000 list companies have their corporate homes in Georgia.
