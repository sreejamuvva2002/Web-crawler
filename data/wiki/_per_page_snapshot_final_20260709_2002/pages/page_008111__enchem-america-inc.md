# Enchem America, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2025-10-14

**Source:** [EV Battery Component Company Backs Out Of Plans For $152M Haywood County Facility | Tennessee Conservative](https://tennesseeconservativenews.com/ev-battery-component-company-backs-out-of-plans-for-152m-haywood-county-facility/)  ·  `page_008111`

## Facts

- Enchem America, Inc. is a South Korean battery component maker.
- Enchem America backed out of plans to build a $152.5 million electrolyte manufacturing facility in Haywood County.
- The withdrawn project would have brought 190 jobs to Haywood County.
- Enchem America announced its plan to build at the “shovel ready” I-40 Advantage Industrial Park in Brownsville in 2023.
- The site is roughly 16 miles from BlueOval City, an electric vehicle manufacturing campus.
- BlueOval City is slated to produce Ford Motor Company’s new all-electric pickup truck and electric vehicle batteries.
- BlueOval City will be produced in partnership with SK On.
- The BlueOval City campus is expected to serve as manufacturing space for BlueOval SK, Ford, and electric vehicle component suppliers.
- BlueOval City is expected to inject jobs and residents into the surrounding rural West Tennessee communities.
- The Enchem facility would have produced electrolytes used for electric vehicle batteries.
- The facility was expected to magnify Enchem’s ability to supply and support its electric vehicle partners in the United States.
- Enchem decided to withdraw from the project in mid-September 2025.
- The withdrawal followed “careful consideration and an extensive review of (their) North American business strategy,” according to Brownsville-Haywood County Economic Development Corporation Executive Director Aaron Stewart.
- Stewart communicated the withdrawal to the corporation’s board members and West Tennessee mayors on Sept. 26 via email.
- The decision was “primarily driven by a recent shift in demand from (their) key North American customers,” according to the company.
- Enchem America did not respond to a request for comment.
- Ford Motor Company announced it will delay mass production of the next-generation pickup truck at BlueOval City until 2028.
- Ford still plans to start producing prototypes of the truck at BlueOval City in 2027.
- The Brownsville-Haywood Economic Development Corporation is working to terminate the state incentives Enchem was set to receive for the project.
- Enchem received a $2 million recruitment grant in 2023, according to a state database.
- Stewart will work with the state and the Tennessee Valley Authority (TVA) to put the site back on the market.
- The TVA has contributed grant funds to prepare the industrial park for future development.
- Enchem completed preliminary site grading but had not yet begun construction.
- Aaron Stewart stated that Enchem is not abandoning a facility.
- The site will take time to get back on the market, but it “always generated interest because of its proximity to Memphis and Jackson.”
- All site preparation was completed as part of the state’s Select Tennessee Certified Sites program.
- Stewart suspects a new tenant will draw interest due to BlueOval City’s production beginning in 2027 and mass production in 2028.
- More details about the site’s future will be discussed at the Brownsville-Haywood Economic Development Corporation board meeting on Oct. 8.
- Stewart is not concerned by uncertainty in the electric vehicle market, which is still evolving.
- Stewart compared navigating the automotive industry to looking into a crystal ball.
- Navigating policies from Washington, international supply chains for rare earth metals and aluminum production, tariffs, and trade agreements makes it difficult for automakers to chart a course for the next 12 months, let alone five to 10 years.
- Stewart highlighted a $5.6 billion investment in the BlueOval City campus.
- The BlueOval City campus has nearly 11 million square feet under a roof.
- Machinery and equipment at BlueOval City is still being installed and calibrated, albeit at a slower pace.
