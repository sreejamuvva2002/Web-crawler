## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp and First Lady Marty Kemp joined state and local leaders to celebrate the groundbreaking of Anovion Technologies' new manufacturing facility.
- The facility will supply premium synthetic graphite anode materials for lithium-ion batteries.
- The project will create more than 400 jobs.
- The project will involve over $800 million in investment in Decatur County.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated the event is the culmination of months of hard work by the local community and sustained talks and efforts at the state level, noting Georgia is fast becoming the e-mobility capital of the nation.
- Governor Kemp noted this project represents a milestone on Georgia's path to becoming the e-mobility capital of the nation.
- Governor Kemp stated that literally all four corners of the state have seen major investment and job creation from the industry.
- Governor Kemp emphasized commitment to building relationships with job creators like Anovion and bringing jobs and investments to all Georgians no matter their zip code.
- Since Governor Kemp took office in 2018, EV-related projects have committed over $24 billion in investment to communities across Georgia.
- Since Governor Kemp took office in 2018, EV-related projects have committed more than 31,000 jobs to communities across Georgia.

- The State of Georgia has been recognized as the No. 1 state for business for nine consecutive years.
- Georgia's QuickStart workforce training program is consistently cited as a leading cause of the explosive growth Georgia has enjoyed in this sector.
- The Department of Economic Development is consistently cited as a leading cause of the explosive growth Georgia has enjoyed in this sector.
- Anovion’s new facility will be 1,500,000 square feet in size.
- The facility will utilize proprietary furnace technologies.
- The facility is located at Downrange Industrial Park in Bainbridge.
- The facility will be the first of its size and scale in North America.
- The facility will produce synthetic graphite for use in batteries that power EVs, electric storage systems, electronics, military applications, and other products.
- Operations at the new facility are expected to begin in late 2025.
- Major EV-related projects are now announced, under construction, or operational in all four corners of Georgia (southwest, southeast, northeast).
