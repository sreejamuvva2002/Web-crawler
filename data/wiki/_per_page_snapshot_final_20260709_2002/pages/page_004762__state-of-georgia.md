# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-07-30

**Source:** [Georgia: A Premier Destination for Aerospace and Defense Opportunities | Georgia](https://georgia.org/blog/georgia-premier-destination-aerospace-and-defense-opportunities)  ·  `page_004762`

## Facts

- Georgia is the fifth-largest aerospace exporter in the U.S., with exports totaling $11.1 billion in 2023.
- According to PwC, Georgia is ranked second for Aerospace Manufacturing Readiness.
- The state is home to industry giants such as Gulfstream Aerospace Corporation and Lockheed Martin.
- The state supports dynamic startups like Archer Aviation and Hermeus.
- Recent investments by major players include StandardAero, Pratt & Whitney, Anduril Industries, and Embraer.
- The University System of Georgia and the Technical College System of Georgia offer comprehensive programs that address the entire design, manufacturing, and maintenance value chain.
- Four out of five military branches operate in the state, providing a significant workforce advantage.
- Robins Air Force Base exemplifies public and private sector collaboration to build a skilled workforce.
- Middle Georgia State University (MGSU)’s FAA-certified aviation programs, strong ties with local aerospace companies, and partnerships with Robins Air Force Base contribute to a labor pool of over 5,000 professionals in Macon-Bibb County.
- The Robins North Facility is a collaboration between Robins Air Force Base Central Georgia Technical College and Macon-Bibb County Industrial Authority that enhances training and streamlines the transition of students into the aerospace workforce.
- The Georgia Institute of Technology alone is home to the Georgia Tech Research Institute (GTRI), Center for Urban and Regional Air Mobility, Georgia AI Manufacturing (GA-AIM), and Advanced Manufacturing Pilot Facility (AMPF).
- The Georgia Center of Innovation, part of the Georgia Department of Economic Development, provides industry-specific expertise and fosters collaboration within the aerospace ecosystem.
- Georgia’s investments in infrastructure include an expanded $1.5 billion budget for statewide airport improvements to support continued growth in the aerospace sector.
- Strategic development sites such as the Innovation Isles Aerospace Park and the Eastman Aerospace Park are certified as “Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD)-Select.”
- Archer Aviation chose a site in Covington for its new electric vertical takeoff and landing (eVTOL) manufacturing facility.
- Businesses can meet with Georgia Department of Economic Development representatives at events such as Eurosatory, Farnborough International Airshow, Paris Air Show, and the annual SelectUSA Investment Summit.
