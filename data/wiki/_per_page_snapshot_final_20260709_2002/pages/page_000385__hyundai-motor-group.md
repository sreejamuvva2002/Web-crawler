# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-05

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Group to Invest $5.54B in Georgia at First Fully Dedicated Electric Vehicle & Battery Manufacturing Facility - Savannah CEO](https://savannahceo.com/features/2022/05/hyundai-motor-group-invest-554-billion-georgia-first-fully-dedicated-electric-vehicle-and-battery-manufacturing-facility/)  ·  `page_000385`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp and Hyundai Motor Group Executive Chair Euisun Chung announced at a signing ceremony that Hyundai Motor Group will open its first fully dedicated electric vehicle (EV) and battery manufacturing facility in the State of Georgia.
- Hyundai Motor Group will invest $5.54 billion in opening a state-of-the-art U.S. smart factory at the Bryan County Megasite.
- Non-affiliated Hyundai Motor Group suppliers will invest approximately another $1 billion in the project.
- The project will deliver approximately 8,100 new jobs to Georgia’s coastal region.
- Governor Kemp was joined by Jay Chang, President and CEO of Hyundai Motor Company, and José Muñoz, President and COO of Hyundai Motor Company, along with Hyundai Motor Group executives and state and local economic development leaders at the premier 2,923-acre site.
- The megasite was purchased jointly by the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority (JDA) and the State less than a year ago.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that welcoming Hyundai Motor Group to Georgia is part of forging an innovative future together, citing initial conversations on his economic development mission to Korea and Georgia’s investment in the Bryan County Megasite as long-term preparations for this opportunity.
- Governor Kemp described the announcement as the largest economic development project in Georgia's history and a commitment to making the state a premier destination for quality companies creating jobs.
- Hyundai Motor Group Executive Chair Euisun Chung stated that the U.S. holds an important place in the Group’s global strategy and expressed excitement about partnering with Georgia to achieve shared goals of electrified mobility and sustainability.
- Hyundai Motor Group expects to begin construction on the new facility in January 2023.
- Full production is expected in the first half of 2025.
- The facility will have an annual capacity of 300,000 units.
- Wages throughout the project will be well-paying advanced manufacturing positions competitive in the local market.
- Jaehoon (Jay) Chang, President and CEO of Hyundai Motor Company, stated that America embraces change and drives innovation, making it the location for their first dedicated EV plant in the U.S.
- Chang noted the new EV plant is the future of their business and will help meet growing demands of U.S. customers for leading edge design, safe, zero-emissions vehicles.
- Hyundai Motor Group cited speed-to-market, workforce, and the state’s ability to meet the company’s carbon neutrality standards as reasons for choosing Georgia.
- Georgia is home to an existing network of Hyundai subsidiaries and suppliers.
- The Bryan County Megasite was developed by the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor JDA over the last several years in partnership with the state.
- The megasite offers the needed speed-to-market requirements that reduce barriers to speedy operations.
- Industrial utilities are adjacent to the site, and extensive due diligence reports have been completed.
- José Muñoz, President and COO of Hyundai Motor Company, stated they partnered with Governor Kemp and the Savannah Joint Economic Development Authority to bring a flexible EV smart factory to Bryan County.
- Muñoz said the plant will position them well to win in a competitive market with consumers demanding smart, sustainable mobility solutions.
- Hyundai Motor Group is headquartered in Seoul, Korea, and employs 250,000 people worldwide.
- The Group’s vehicles are sold in 193 countries through 5,000 dealerships and showrooms.
- At the new Georgia highly connected, automated, and flexible EV smart factory, Hyundai Motor Group plans to produce a diverse range of innovative full electric vehicles.
- Details of production models will be shared at later dates.
- Through the battery manufacturing facility, the Group aims to establish a stable supply chain for EV battery and other EV components in the U.S. market.
- When it nears planned 2025 start of operations, Hyundai Motor Group will seek local vendors and suppliers.
- Carter Infinger, Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority Board of Directors Chairman, stated the announcement is transformative and that the jobs being created and the investment being made by the Hyundai Motor Group will reverberate for years and generations to come.
- Infinger noted this is a great day for Bryan, Bulloch, Chatham and Effingham counties as well as the entire region and the State of Georgia.
- Bryan County is the fastest-growing county in the state.
- The strategic purchase of the Bryan County Megasite in 2021 was the largest in state history.
- The site is adjacent to Interstate 16 with immediate access from I-95 and I-16 to 250 major metro areas.
- It is less than 30 miles from the Port of Savannah, the single-largest and fastest-growing container terminal in the U.S.
- The site has two Class I rail facilities on-site provided by Georgia Central Railway.
- Russell McMurry, Georgia Department of Transportation Commissioner, stated Hyundai's announcement is evidence that Georgia’s collective focus on supporting the electric mobility future is working and exemplifies the power of partnership and preparation.
- McMurry noted Georgia’s planning ensures logistics networks are safe, sustainable, innovative, and ready to support economic growth.
- Griff Lynch, Georgia Ports Authority Executive Director, stated Hyundai's new location provides stellar access to global and domestic markets for both assembly components and finished vehicle marketing.
- Lynch said the decision to expand in Georgia is a clear endorsement of the Peach State as a dynamic center for cutting-edge manufacturing.
- Building on existing automotive assets and developing workforce, Georgia is consistently welcoming increased investment in the electric mobility ecosystem and supply chain across the state.
- Georgia is the Southeastern leader for EV registrations per 1,000 registered automobiles.
- Georgia offers more EV charging outlets per capita than anywhere else in the Southeast.
- The Georgia Electric Mobility and Innovation Alliance is a public-private-nonprofit initiative focused on growing the electric mobility ecosystem in the state and strengthening Georgia’s position in electrification-related manufacturing and innovation.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) was represented by Project Manager Taylor Walden in partnership with the Department’s Georgia-based Director of Korean Investment Yoonie Kim.
- Representatives also included the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor JDA, which includes Bryan County, Bulloch County, Chatham County, and Effingham County; the Georgia Department of Transportation; the Georgia Ports Authority; Georgia Quick Start; and Georgia Power.
- Pat Wilson, Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner, stated under Governor Kemp’s leadership they have focused on putting Georgia at the forefront of electric mobility.
- Wilson said the announcement solidifies Georgia's spot at the vanguard of the EV transition and welcomes the Hyundai Group to celebrate the incredible investment.
- Wilson noted the state-of-the-art facility will create exciting new possibilities for all Georgians and transform an entire region.
- At the new facility, Hyundai Motor Group plans to implement many of its advanced intelligent manufacturing technologies that are currently under test at the Group’s innovation hub.
- Technologies include intelligent A.I.-equipped manufacturing and data-driven processes to best meet demands of customers.
- The plant will help support Hyundai Motor Group’s efforts to achieve its carbon neutrality target through the active use of renewable energy for the facility.
- Since 2020 alone, Georgia has announced more than 20 EV-related projects that will invest more than $13.54 billion and create nearly 18,000 jobs in the state.
