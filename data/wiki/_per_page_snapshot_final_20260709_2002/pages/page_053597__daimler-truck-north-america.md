# Daimler Truck North America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-05-12

**Source:** [Daimler Truck picks Portland for new engineering facility, EVSE training center](https://electriccarsreport.com/2024/05/daimler-truck-picks-portland-for-new-engineering-facility-evse-training-center/)  ·  `page_053597`

## Facts

- Daimler Truck North America (DTNA) announced two significant investments at its headquarters complex on Swan Island: a new Electric Vehicle Supply Equipment (EVSE) training center adjacent to Electric Island and a new engineering facility at the DTNA campus.
- The new EVSE training center addition to Electric Island represents the next phase to accelerate testing and development of charging infrastructure for commercial vehicles since the first-of-its-kind public charging station was built in partnership with Portland General Electric and opened in 2021.
- The new training center will be used for the DTNA dealer network and as a resource center for community stakeholders interested in the clean transportation industry.
- The anticipated EVSE training center project will cost $3 million, with half of the costs funded through a forgivable loan from Prosper Portland, the City of Portland’s economic development agency.
- The new DTNA engineering facility will be approximately 110,000 sq-ft and will consolidate testing activities under one roof with a focus on research and development for emerging propulsion technologies, such as battery electric and hydrogen-fueled vehicles.
- The anticipated engineering facility project is expected to cost $40 million and will be partially funded by the State of Oregon with a $700,000 forgivable loan from the State’s Strategic Reserve fund.
- John O’Leary, president and CEO of DTNA, stated that DTNA is proud to call Portland, Oregon home and appreciates the City and State’s support for expansion plans to further promote the development and proper testing of advanced technology.
- John O’Leary expressed looking forward to collaborating for many more years on training and employing a future-ready workforce.
- Business Oregon Director Sophorn Cheang stated that Oregon is the location of choice for businesses leading clean technology innovation in the U.S.
- Sophorn Cheang noted that the partnership with local and regional economic development leaders to support DTNA’s investment adds to the region’s competitiveness and will provide opportunities for skilled workforce in manufacturing and engineering for years to come.
- Portland City Commissioner Carmen Rubio expressed thrill at DTNA’s decision to invest significant resources and continue leading heavy-duty vehicle technology right here in Portland.
- Carmen Rubio thanked Governor Kotek for her commitment to this effort and to building the clean economy.
- Carmen Rubio recognized Prosper Portland Board Chair Kimberly Branam and the outstanding team at Prosper Portland that has worked closely with DTNA for many years.
- Carmen Rubio noted that Portland is where Freightliner was founded more than 80 years ago, DTNA is an anchor employer in the city, and today’s announcement is a proud moment for all involved.
- Carmen Rubio stated that the future of heavy-duty vehicle technology in Portland is very bright and very green.
- DTNA’s history in Portland dates back to the founding of the Freightliner brand in the city in 1942.
- When Freightliner was acquired by the former Daimler AG in 1981, the manufacturer established its North American headquarters for medium- and heavy-duty trucks in Portland.
- Since 1981, DTNA has based all research and development activities for North America in the region.
- The Portland Truck Manufacturing Plant is located on Swan Island and has been in continuous operation since 1969.
- The Portland Truck Manufacturing Plant currently produces the battery-electric Freightliner eCascadia and eM2 as well as the Western Star X-Series.
- DTNA employs over 3,000 people in the Portland region.
