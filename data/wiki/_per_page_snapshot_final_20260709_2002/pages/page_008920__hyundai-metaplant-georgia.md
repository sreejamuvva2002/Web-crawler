# Hyundai Metaplant Georgia

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-03-26

**Source:** [Hyundai's massive new plant celebrating opening today in south Georgia | FOX 5 Atlanta](https://www.fox5atlanta.com/news/hyundais-massive-new-plant-celebrating-opening-today-south-georgia)  ·  `page_008920`

## Facts

- Hyundai has officially opened its new electric vehicle assembly plant in Bryan County, marking the largest single economic development project in Georgia's history.
- Before reaching full capacity, the company announced a plan for expansion at the facility.
- The Hyundai Metaplant, located just outside Savannah, is Hyundai’s first dedicated mass-production facility for electric and hybrid vehicles.
- The facility is expected to create 8,500 jobs over the next few years.
- The project represents more than $12 billion in investment from Hyundai in Georgia.
- The plant operates as a joint venture with LG Energy Solution, which will supply battery technology for the electric vehicles.
- Hyundai officials state the facility will help the company reach its goal of selling more than 3 million fully electric vehicles per year by 2030.
- Euisun Chung, executive chair of Hyundai, stated: "I have never been more optimistic about building the future of mobility with America, in America."
- Company executives joined local and state leaders for the grand opening ceremony on Wednesday afternoon.
- State officials have touted the project as a major economic win for Georgia, positioning the state as a key player in the nation’s growing EV industry.
- Kemp stated: "This is yet another historic investment and a testament of Hyundai's belief in Georgia. A belief in our people."
- The plant is already operational with vehicles rolling off the assembly line.
- The facility was originally expected to produce over 300,000 vehicles annually.
- During the grand opening, the company announced it could beat the original production target at 500,000 vehicles annually.
- Hyundai Metaplant is currently producing the IONIQ 5 and IONIQ 9 models.
- As of March 24, a little over 1,200 employees (excluding Korean expats) had been hired to work at the plant.
- Total employees as of March 24: 1,232.
- Employees from Georgia: 1,077.
- Employees from out-of-state: 155.
- Of the 1,077 Georgia-based employees, 33% come from Chatham County, 15% from Bulloch County, 8% from Effingham County, and 7% from Bryan County.
- The facility is currently operating on one shift.
- The goal is to eventually run the facility 24/7.
