# Hanon Systems

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-05-23

**Source:** [Automotive Supplier Hanon Systems to Create 160 New Jobs in Bulloch County • The Georgia Virtue](https://www.thegeorgiavirtue.com/bulloch-local-news/automotive-supplier-hanon-systems-to-create-160-new-jobs-in-bulloch-county/)  ·  `page_055729`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Hanon Systems will create 160 new jobs and invest more than $40 million in a new manufacturing facility in Bulloch County.
- The announcement was made while Governor Kemp was on an economic development trip to Israel.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Georgia’s automotive and e-mobility industries continue to grow as the state welcomes Hanon Systems.
- Governor Brian Kemp noted that Georgia is furthering its position as the EV capital of America, with new jobs and opportunities coming to Georgians across the state.
- Min Sung, President and Chief Executive Officer of Hanon Systems, expressed pleasure in contributing to the Bulloch County local economy through job creation.
- Min Sung thanked the State of Georgia and the cooperation between its agencies for guiding the project.
- Min Sung stated that the announcement aligns with Hanon Systems' strategy to support the industry shift toward electrification while growing its North America footprint.
- Hanon Systems will build a new facility at Gateway Regional Industrial Park in Bulloch County.
- The new facility will assemble automotive thermal solutions.
- Operations at the new facility are expected to begin in May 2024.
- Billy Allen, Chair of the Development Authority of Bulloch County, stated that Statesboro-Bulloch County is honored by Hanon Systems’ decision to locate its new facility there.
- Billy Allen noted excitement over welcoming another automotive supplier that will work closely with the Hyundai Metaplant and the electric vehicle industry.
- Billy Allen cited the decision as an example of the resources offered by the community and region to help companies succeed.
- Griff Lynch, Executive Director of the Georgia Ports Authority (GPA), stated that the announcement further solidifies Georgia’s position as a growing hub for auto manufacturing.
- Griff Lynch noted that GPA is proud to support the operations of Hyundai Motor Group and its suppliers in delivering jobs and opportunity for Georgia residents.
- Alyce Thornhill, Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy, represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The GDEcD partnered with the Development Authority of Bulloch County, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start for this project.
- Pat Wilson, GDEcD Commissioner, stated that suppliers like Hanon Systems successfully support larger industry ecosystems in Georgia through partnership-driven solutions ranging from workforce training to logistics systems efficiencies.
- Pat Wilson noted that Bulloch County, as part of the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority, has been part of a growing success story from the beginning.
- Pat Wilson expressed that Georgia’s history of partnership continues to create opportunity for current and future generations.
- Georgia is positioned as a hub for the electric mobility industry, building on assets that make the automotive industry successful.
- Since 2020, over 40 EV-related projects have announced more than $22.7 billion in investment and 28,400 jobs in Georgia.
- On October 25, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America officially broke ground in neighboring Bryan County on its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility.
- The article is a press release from the Governor’s Office.
