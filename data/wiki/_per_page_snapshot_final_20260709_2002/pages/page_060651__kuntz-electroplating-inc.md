# Kuntz Electroplating Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Canada Invests in Kitchener-Based Company's Cleaner Electric Vehicle Manufacturing | Mirage News](https://www.miragenews.com/canada-invests-in-kitchener-based-companys-1043930/)  ·  `page_060651`

## Facts

- Kuntz Electroplating Inc. (KEI) will receive a $3-million Government of Canada investment.
- The investment aims to strengthen KEI's manufacturing capabilities in the automotive and Electric Vehicle (EV) industry.
- The project will create 45 new jobs in the automotive and EV industry.
- KEI is one of Kitchener's longest-running family-owned businesses.
- KEI is one of North America's leading manufacturers of electroplated and powder-coated exterior components for the automotive and motorcycle industries.
- The investment will help KEI modify its existing production lines and add new equipment to meet growing demand from the EV market.
- The project will help KEI enter the clean technology sector.
- KEI will fulfill larger volume orders and serve new clients by offering more environmentally sustainable solutions, including in the EV sector.
- The investment will strengthen the manufacturing sector in Kitchener and position KEI for long-term growth.
- Growing demand for greener alternatives gives Ontario's manufacturers immense potential to harness expertise and new technologies to support the rapidly growing Electric Vehicle (EV) market.
- Ontario's manufacturing sector employs thousands of workers and is a significant contributor to Canada's economy.
- The Honourable Filomena Tassi, Minister responsible for the Federal Economic Development Agency for Southern Ontario (FedDev Ontario), announced the investment.
- Valerie Bradford, Member of Parliament for Kitchener South-Hespeler, joined the announcement.
- Tim Louis, Member of Parliament for Kitchener-Conestoga, joined the announcement.
- Minister Tassi met with Jan Liggett, Mayor of Cambridge; Susan Foxton, Mayor of North Dumfries; and Dorothy McCabe, Mayor of the City of Waterloo, to discuss regional economic growth opportunities.
