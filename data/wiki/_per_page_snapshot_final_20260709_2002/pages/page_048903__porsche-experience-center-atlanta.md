# Porsche Experience Center Atlanta

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-05

**Source:** [Porsche's Atlanta experience center reshaped Southside over 10 years](https://www.ajc.com/news/2025/05/how-porsches-north-american-hq-and-test-track-have-reshaped-atlantas-southside/)  ·  `page_048903`

## Facts

- German luxury automaker Porsche is celebrating the 10th anniversary of its Atlanta experience center this summer (2025).
- The investment combines test tracks with Porsche's North American headquarters.
- It is one of the largest investments made in Atlanta’s Southside since the turn of the century.
- The project revitalized the site of the shuttered Ford Motor Co. plant near Hapeville.
- For roughly 60 years, airplane drone near the world’s busiest airport was countered by sounds of automobiles coming off assembly lines at the Ford plant.
- In 2006, factory lines at Ford Motor Co.’s plant near Hapeville went quiet.
- Alan Hallman, Atlanta’s mayor since 2003, called the Ford factory’s shuttering “an economically devastating event.”
- Mayor Hallman stated that Porsche's investment “transformed that area” and “woke that area up again after Ford’s departure.”
- The Porsche Experience Center is located in Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport / I-57, Atlanta, Georgia.
- The project represents a $1.2 billion investment announced in 2015.
- The site employs more than 1,000 employees.
- The facility serves as Porsche's North American headquarters and includes a unique experience center combining test tracks and driving.
- Mayor Hallman referred to the project as “a third crown jewel” for Atlanta.
- The investment is expected to serve as a major economic driver for the Southside area.
- The site includes a test track and driving experiences for customers and employees.
