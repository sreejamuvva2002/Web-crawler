# Chattanooga Area

**Source:** [Chattanooga job growth expected to jump four-fold in next five years | Chattanooga Times Free Press](https://www.timesfreepress.com/news/2015/dec/13/chattanooga-job-growth-expected-jump-four-fold-nex/)  ·  `page_013157`

## Facts

- Volkswagen announced a $900 million expansion in July 2014 that will add 2,000 jobs at its Chattanooga assembly plant.
- Eighteen new business additions or expansions were announced during 2015, collectively projected to invest another $456.8 million and add 2,451 jobs in the Chattanooga area.
- Gestamp automotive supplier expansion is projected to cost $180 million and create 510 jobs.
- Vanguard National Trailer Corp. announced a plant for semi-trailers costing $30 million with 400 new jobs.
- Amazon expanded its Chattanooga fulfillment center, adding 400 jobs.
- Yanfeng Automotive Interiors supply plant investment is valued at $55 million and will add 325 jobs.
- Schnellecke Logistics USA established an automotive support operation adding 300 jobs.
- West Star Aviation aircraft maintenance facility investment totals $22.3 million and adds 200 jobs.
- McKee Foods Corp. expansion costs $102.35 million and adds 50 jobs.
- Since 2009, Davidson County employment grew 14.1 percent, adding 41,560 jobs.
- Hamilton County employment grew 2.4 percent since 2009, adding 3,573 jobs.
- Shelby County employment was virtually flat since 2009, adding just 157 jobs.
- Knox County employment fell 0.1 percent since 2009, falling by 148 jobs.
- Employment in the rest of Tennessee outside the biggest counties grew by 1.9 percent, or 32,602 jobs, since 2009.
- The Chattanooga area has yet to regain all jobs lost during the Great Recession that began nearly eight years ago.
- An influx of automotive suppliers and growth in tech startups and existing businesses are expected to push employment in metropolitan Chattanooga to a record high in 2016.
- A new study suggests job growth in Hamilton County, already the second highest among Tennessee's counties over the past five years, will grow nearly four times faster in the next five years.
