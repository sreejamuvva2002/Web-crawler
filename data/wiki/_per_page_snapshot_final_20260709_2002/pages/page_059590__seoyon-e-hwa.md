# Seoyon E-Hwa

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-02-01

**Source:** [Seoyon E-HWA Expands with Manufacturing Facility in Chatham Co - AllOnGeorgia](https://allongeorgia.com/georgia-business/seoyon-e-hwa-expands-with-manufacturing-facility-in-chatham-co/)  ·  `page_059590`

## Facts

- Seoyon E-HWA is a global auto parts supplier that manufactures both interior and exterior components.
- Seoyon E-HWA will create 740 new direct and indirect jobs.
- Seoyon E-HWA will invest almost $76 million in a new manufacturing facility in Chatham County.
- Governor Brian Kemp announced the largest economic development project in Georgia history last year.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Georgia is working to ensure its state is the e-mobility capital of the nation.
- Governor Brian Kemp noted that projects like this will continue to choose Georgia as the No. 1 state for business and benefit communities in just about every zip code of Georgia.
- Seoyon E-Hwa was established in 1972.
- Seoyon E-Hwa operates 32 corporations in North America, South America, Europe, and Asia.
- Seoyon E-Hwa supplies door trim, headlining, seats, C/PAD, bumpers, and other parts to Hyundai Motor Group, Kia, Mercedes-Benz, Ford, Volkswagen, and more.
- Seoyon’s LaGrange location will continue to support 630 full time jobs.
- Seoyon E-Hwa Chairman Mr. Ryu Yang-seok stated that Seoyon E-Hwa is an automobile manufacturer in various countries such as Korea, Europe, and India.
- Chairman Mr. Ryu Yang-seok expressed honor in supplying electric vehicle parts in Chatham County, Georgia.
- Chairman Mr. Ryu Yang-seok stated that with the establishment of Seoyon E-Hwa Savannah, they hope to provide new jobs in the region and contribute to making Savannah a central city for automobile development and production.
- Chairman Mr. Ryu Yang-seok committed that Seoyon E-Hwa will cooperate continuously with the local government.
- Seoyon’s new facility will be located at the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center, a Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) Certified site.
- The new manufacturing facility will produce automotive interior parts such as door trims, headlining, and tailgate trims.
- Production is expected to begin in October 2024.
- More than two-thirds, or approximately 500, of the announced jobs will be permanent roles in human resources, development, quality, and production personnel.
- Savannah Economic Development Authority President and CEO Trip Tollison expressed delight with the announcement that Seoyon E-HWA will be locating in the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center.
- Trip Tollison noted the site ideally positions them in close proximity to the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America, the Port of Savannah, Interstates I-16 and I-95, and more.
- Trip Tollison stated Seoyon E-HWA has a proven track record of supplying the automotive industry and will provide well paying jobs for the region.
- Georgia Ports Authority Executive Director Griff Lynch stated that Seoyon is a valued customer using the Port of Savannah to supply its manufacturing facility in West Georgia.
- Griff Lynch expressed excitement to help support Seoyon’s growth in this new location as the Hyundai announcement continues to have positive ripple effects throughout the regional economy.
- Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy Alyce Thornhill represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The project was completed in partnership with the Savannah Economic Development Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia Power.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that with its second Georgia facility, Seoyon is committing to nearly doubling the number of jobs it supports in the state.
- Pat Wilson noted this extends Seoyon's partnership with the business community.
- Pat Wilson stated that Hyundai’s new EV facility is drawing an experienced and dedicated supplier network to the region, adding to the state’s industry expertise and shaping a larger skilled workforce.
- Pat Wilson expressed excitement to work with partners across the state and near Savannah to create the jobs of the future.
- Georgia is positioned as a hub for the electric mobility industry, building on assets that make the automotive industry successful.
- Since 2020, more than 35 EV-related projects have announced over $21 billion in investment and 26,700 jobs in Georgia.
- On October 25, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America officially broke ground in neighboring Bryan County on its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility.
- Offsite suppliers connected to the Hyundai project around the state have already exceeded the $1 billion in investment predicted from other locations by more than $600 million.
