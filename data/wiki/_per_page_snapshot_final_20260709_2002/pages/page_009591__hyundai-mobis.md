# Hyundai Mobis

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** power_electronics  ·  **Published:** 2022-11-23

**Source:** [New Hyundai Metaplant to bring 1,500 jobs to Bryan Co., Gov. Kemp says – WSB-TV Channel 2 - Atlanta](https://www.wsbtv.com/news/local/atlanta/new-hyundai-metaplant-bring-1500-jobs-bryan-co-gov-kemp-says/DSDYVFQJZJFMFK2J6IQHTENOAI/)  ·  `page_009591`

## Facts

- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp announced that Hyundai Mobis will invest $926 million in a new Electric Vehicle Power Electric system plant in Bryan County.
- The new plant is expected to hire at least 1,500 people.
- Governor Kemp stated that the groundbreaking of Hyundai's new electric vehicle and battery manufacturing facility in Bryan County was expected to unleash transformational job creation and investment in the region.
- This announcement marks the second supplier to locate in the area within just two weeks.
- Hyundai Mobis has operated a manufacturing factory in West Point since 2009 with almost 1,200 employees.
- The West Point factory is the largest Tier 1 supplier to Kia Georgia.
- The West Point plant supplies car manufacturers with complete cockpit modules, chassis modules, and bumper assemblies as part of the Hyundai Mobis family.
- H.S. Oh, Vice President of Hyundai Mobis Electric Powertrain Business Unit, stated that the investment reflects an acceleration in the development of the EV supply chain in Georgia's auto industry.
- H.S. Oh noted that Hyundai Mobis will be a major production player in the EV market and will trigger more growth within the sector.
- The new facility will be a 1,200,000-square-foot building.
- The plant is expected to produce over 900,000 EV Power Electric systems annually.
- The plant is expected to produce 450,000 Integrated Charging Control units annually.
- Production will be supplied to several factories in Georgia and one factory in Alabama.
- Construction is expected to begin as early as January 2023.
- The site for the new plant is the Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development Certified Belfast Commerce Park in Richmond Hill.
- Construction officials predict production will begin sometime in 2024.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that suppliers like Hyundai Mobis are transitioning to the jobs of the future and becoming part of Georgia's growing EV ecosystem.
- In May, GDEcD predicted that non-affiliated HMGMA suppliers would invest an additional $1 billion into Georgia in connection with the project.
- That $1 billion investment prediction was blown past in about two weeks.
- The project involves support from partners including Georgia Quick Start, sister agencies, Bryan County, the City of Richmond Hill, and the Georgia Ports Authority.
