## Facts

- Starting in late 2026, Swedish automaker Volvo will begin building the XC60 at its plant in Ridgeville, South Carolina.
- The XC60 is Volvo's global best-seller and most popular model in the United States.
- This production move strengthens Volvo’s manufacturing footprint in the U.S. to stay competitive against compact luxury SUVs dominated by BMW, Mercedes-Benz, and Lexus.
- Currently, most XC60s are manufactured at Volvo's Torslanda plant in Sweden and Ghent facility in Belgium.
- XC60 sales in the U.S. surged nearly 23 percent in the first half of 2025.
- Volvo Car Americas reported 40,675 vehicle sales in the second quarter of 2025.
- Electrified models made up 40.8 percent of that total.
- The XC60 accounts for over a third of Volvo’s U.S. sales this year.
- A quarter of those sales come from plug-in hybrids.
- Globally, the XC60 just overtook the brand’s iconic 240 wagon to become Volvo’s best-selling model of all time.
- Over 2.7 million cars are now on the road globally.
- Håkan Samuelsson, CEO of Volvo Cars, stated that adding the XC60 to the Charleston production line will strengthen its market position and support/create American manufacturing jobs.
- The South Carolina plant already builds Volvo’s fully electric flagship EX90 SUV.
- The South Carolina plant also builds the Polestar 3.
- More than $1.3 billion has been invested over the last decade since it commenced operations in 2015 with production of the S60 sedan.
- The 2.3 million sq.ft facility is equipped with a revamped body and paint shop.
- The facility includes a state-of-the-art battery pack line.
- It has the capacity to handle multiple platforms and technologies.
- Production of the XC60 will be in both mild hybrid and plug-in hybrid variants.
- Luis Rezende, President of Volvo Cars Americas, stated that the XC60 is the right car for this market.
- He added that it offers the best of Volvo in a versatile size with powertrain options to suit U.S. customers.
- The U.S. raised tariffs on the import of cars and spare parts earlier this year.
- Tariffs on Chinese-made electric vehicles went from 25 percent to 100 percent, forcing many automakers to rethink their supply chains.
- Volvo, owned by China’s Geely but headquartered in Sweden, has largely sidestepped that impact by manufacturing most U.S.-bound vehicles in Europe.
- Polestar, also under Geely, has localized production of the Polestar 3 at the South Carolina plant.
- Volvo marks 70 years in the American market with over 5 million cars sold since 1955.
- The Swedish giant is renowned for Scandinavian design and some of the safest cars on the road.
