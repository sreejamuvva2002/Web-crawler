# Georgia

**Source:** [Georgia Clean-Energy Boom Producing Jobs, Housing Shortfalls | Climate Central](https://www.climatecentral.org/partnership-journalism/georgia-clean-energy-boom-producing-jobs-housing-shortfalls)  ·  `page_052203`

## Facts

- Olivia Amyette is the founding owner of Infinite Energy Advisors and the Solar Knowledge Institute in Cleveland, Georgia.
- Infinite Energy Advisors is a solar energy company located in Cleveland, Georgia, a small town about an hour and a half northeast of Atlanta.
- The Solar Knowledge Institute (SKI) is a workforce development program that prepares workers for careers in the solar industry.
- Amyette stated that the overall success of her company would not really be likely if the Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) wasn't in place, noting it drives demand for solar.
- QCells employs nearly 1,800 people in Dalton, Georgia, producing 30,000 solar panels a day.
- QCells employs another 750 workers at a plant in Cartersville, Georgia (population 23,100).
- A 2024 expansion at the QCells Cartersville plant was powered largely by IRA tax credits incentivizing domestic manufacturing of clean energy products.
- The Cartersville facility manufactures finished solar panels and semiconductive wafers.
- When fully operational, QCells expects to produce 8.4 gigawatts of solar in Georgia, equal to about 16 million solar panels.
- The IRA expanded the Investment Tax Credit (ITC) for clean energy projects, providing tax credits from about a quarter to a third over the coming decade.
- Georgia does not offer consumers one-to-one net metering for solar panels.
- Businesses in Georgia rely heavily on tax incentives to grow solar investments; Amyette estimates 90% of her clients would not switch to solar without the tax credit.
- Georgia ranked in the top five states nationally for total clean investment from early 2023 to early 2024, with more than half of that $12.4 million going to manufacturing facilities.
- The state has created at least 17,421 clean energy jobs across Georgia, according to Climate Power.
- Rural Georgia has seen 13 projects resulting in over $2.23 billion invested and 3,479 jobs created in the two years since the IRA was enacted.
- SKI is the only apprenticeship program with a specific focus on solar energy construction registered with the Georgia Department of Labor.
- SKI offers a two-year, 4,000-hour apprenticeship program combining classroom instruction and on-the-job learning, plus a-la-carte courses in topics like sales.
- Since launching in 2022, SKI has trained about 24 individuals, half from the Cleveland area.
- Cartersville is expected to see 5,750 new jobs between the QCells Plant and the Hyundai factory.
- QCells hired 600 people within the first six months of opening their Dalton plant.
- Georgia has attracted more than a dozen manufacturing projects for electric vehicles, school buses, and batteries since 2018.
- Rivian proposed a plant on a 2,000-acre parcel where four counties meet, expected to create 7,500 jobs; production was initially expected by the end of 2024 but is currently on hold.
- Kia in West Point, Georgia (population ~3,500) is expanding its automotive manufacturing plant to add a line for electric vehicles.
- Gov. Brian Kemp has promoted Georgia's resources and workforce for projects like the 1.5 million-square-foot Anovion Technologies facility, which will make battery components in southeastern Georgia.
- In 2023, the Georgia General Assembly began funneling state money to the Rural Workforce Housing Initiative to provide 0-3% interest construction loans.
- The initiative has awarded $46 million to housing and infrastructure loans, including single-family homes, multi-family senior apartments, and water/sewer capacity projects.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated these grants are the first step in meeting community needs amid incredible growth.
- The Dalton-Whitfield County Joint Development Authority received $1.5 million for street and stormwater drainage construction for 39 new homes.
- The Stephens County Development Authority received $478,400 to build a sanitary sewer lift station to allow constructing 318 housing units near an expanding regional industrial park; SungEl has announced plans to begin manufacturing batteries in Toccoa, contributing to the project.
- Hedge funds are buying properties with reasonable mortgages around $850 per month and renting them back at $1,600 a month, squeezing the rental market.
- Most housing for new workers is expected to be built closer to Atlanta and Augusta, each about an hour's drive away from rural sites.
- Community leaders often aren't aware of constraints in rental housing supply or how lower-income residents might be affected, according to research by Kim Skobba at the University of Georgia’s Housing and Demographics Research Center.
- A survey of 164 decision-makers in rural, small town Georgia found that many are unaware of rental housing constraints.
