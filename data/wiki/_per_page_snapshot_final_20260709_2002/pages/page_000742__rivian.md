# Rivian

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-08-20

**Source:** [Rivian moving forward on $5B Georgia EV plant | Manufacturing Dive](https://www.manufacturingdive.com/news/rivian-kickoff-georgia-plant-gov-brian-kemp/758069/)  ·  `page_000742`

## Facts

- Rivian will host two special events next month to mark the restart of construction of its $5 billion Georgia electric vehicle factory.
- A community event is set for Sept. 14.
- A formal kickoff ceremony is set for Sept. 16.
- The kickoff ceremony will be held “in celebration of our strong partnership with Georgia and the planned resumption of construction in 2026.”
- The kickoff ceremony will be attended by stakeholders and government officials, including Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp.
- The plant near Social Circle is expected to start production in 2028.
- The plant will have an annual capacity of about 200,000 units.
- In March 2024, Rivian announced it was stopping construction on the Georgia factory project to save money.
- Rivian decided to move production of its upcoming R2 SUV to its plant in Normal, Illinois, to expedite sales.
- Rivian got the project back on track after winning final approval for a $6.6 billion Department of Energy loan in January.
- Rivian cannot access the DOE loan funds until it resumes work on the Georgia factory.
- The DOE loan funds are available through September 2028.
- In July, Rivian announced it was opening a new East Coast headquarters in Atlanta later this year.
- The new Atlanta headquarters will initially employ 100 people.
- The new Atlanta headquarters will ultimately house 500 workers to support its operations in the state.
