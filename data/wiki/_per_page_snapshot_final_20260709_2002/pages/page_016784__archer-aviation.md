## Facts

- Archer Aviation is an eVTOL air taxi company that has been compared by short seller Grizzly Research to troubled truck start-up Nikola, being called the “Nikola of the Skies.”
- Grizzly Research issued a report questioning Archer's future, citing weak orders, a quiet factory, and reliance on publicity rather than progress.
- Archer values its order book at $6 billion, but Grizzly claims many of the orders are linked to partners with weak financials or limited aviation experience.
- An order tied to Air Chateau is considered unlikely to be real because the group has only ever bought one helicopter in the past.
- Another order tied to Future Flight Global comes from a small firm with no track record.
- A deal with KakaoMobility in Korea has already fallen apart but still shows up in Archer's order pipeline.
- Archer often highlights its work with the U.S. Air Force, yet only a small part of the full contract has been awarded so far.
- Grizzly argues that Archer’s Midnight aircraft is flawed and unlikely to be cleared for flight by regulators due to design hurdles that cannot be fixed with minor changes.
- Archer maintains that certification for its Midnight aircraft is on track, contrasting with Grizzly's claims.
- Grizzly says that Archer’s Covington, Georgia factory shows little sign of mass production.
- Investigators visited the Covington, Georgia site over the summer and reported empty lots and no sign of scaled output.
- Archer has stated that its Covington, Georgia site can support up to 650 aircraft per year.
- Grizzly argues that Archer's target of 650 aircraft per year at the Georgia facility is unrealistic.
- The report claims that Archer leans heavily on events and photos to shape its story rather than demonstrating progress.
- One example cited is a “Launch Edition” in Abu Dhabi where the aircraft shown was not new but a reused unit meant for display.
- Grizzly states that Archer has even “delivered” the same aircraft more than once, raising questions about its disclosure practices.
- Rival Joby Aviation (JOBY) is making faster gains across certification and commercial preparation compared to Archer’s reliance on future promises.
- Archer continues to promote its path toward both urban flight and defense applications.
- Grizzly frames Archer’s move into defense as a late attempt to shift focus, but notes investors will have to decide if that pivot has real depth.
- ACHR shares have dropped 11% year-to-date and more than 8% in the last five trading sessions.
- Wall Street analysts maintain a “Strong Buy” consensus on Archer Aviation based on seven recent ratings.
- The average 12-month price target for Archer Aviation is $13.14, implying a 52.44% upside from the current price.
