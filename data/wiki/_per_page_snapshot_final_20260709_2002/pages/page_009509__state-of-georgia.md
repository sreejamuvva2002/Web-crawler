# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2021-04-11

**Source:** [Georgia Poised to Power Up the Southeast’s Electric Vehicle Market – Site Selection Magazine](https://siteselection.com/georgia-poised-to-power-up-the-southeasts-electric-vehicle-market/)  ·  `page_009509`

## Facts

- LG Energy Solution and SK Innovation resolved an intellectual property dispute via mutual agreement on April 11, 2021.
- The dispute resolution occurred prior to a deadline that would have required intervention by the U.S. president.
- Jong Hyun Kim is CEO and president of LG Energy Solution.
- Jun Kim is CEO and president of SK Innovation.
- LG Energy Solution pledged to invest over $4.5 billion in its own U.S. business by 2025.
- LG Energy Solution is close to finalizing a second joint venture plant with GM.
- SK Battery America is constructing a $1.67 billion manufacturing facility near Commerce, Georgia, in Jackson County.
- SK Battery America announced an additional $940 million investment this year (2021) to accommodate increasing demand.
- One of SK Battery America's primary customers is VW in Chattanooga.
- VW announced plans to expand its production site to assemble all-electric SUVs in 2022.
- The International Trade Commission ruled on February 10 that SK Innovation had stolen trade secrets from LG Energy Solutions.
- SK Innovation has vigorously denied the ITC claim as without basis.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp sought relief from the new U.S. president regarding the ITC ruling.
- By law, President Biden has 60 days to alter or block the ITC ruling.
- If the ruling stands, SK could make batteries for VW for two years and for Ford for four years before being banned from importing necessary components for 10 years.
- A representative of SK Innovation stated the company will immediately enter discussions with customers Ford Motor Company and Volkswagen regarding commercial and operational implications.
- SK Innovation believes the ITC ruling could have a serious adverse impact on President Biden’s policies to combat climate change and expand U.S. auto fleet electrification.
- Construction on the SK plant in Commerce began nearly two years ago (relative to April 2021).
- The SK plant is slated to start producing samples in the first half of 2021.
- Mass production at the SK plant is expected to begin in the latter half of 2021.
- SK Battery America expects to employ nearly 2,000 people by 2025.
- Construction of SK Battery America's second plant began in July 2020.
- The second SK plant is expected to start mass production by 2023.
- Upon completion of both plants, SK Battery America will supply enough batteries for 310,000 vehicles (21.5GWh) annually.
- SK Battery America's site selection in Commerce was driven by three factors: location, workforce access, and support from Team Georgia.
- The Jefferson micropolitan area (Jackson County) is ranked No. 2 in the nation in the year’s Top Micropolitan rankings.
- Georgia is situated at the center of the growing automobile industry in the Southeast, surrounded by Tennessee, Alabama, and South Carolina.
- Accessibility to Atlanta-Hartsfield International Airport, the busiest airport in the world, enhances SK's location choice.
- Growth of the Savannah Port and Port of Brunswick, plus future development of the Gainesville Inland Port, will improve logistical capabilities.
- North Georgia provides access to 1.6 million workers within a 45-minute drive from the Commerce plant.
- Atlanta and Athens are within reach for workforce access.
- SK Battery America partners with Lanier Technical College, North Georgia Technical College, Athens Technical College, and Gwinnett Technical College for workforce development.
- The Georgia Quickstart program trains SK's initial workforce with precision for immediate job start.
- Georgia ranked among the country’s top 10 exporting states for the first time last year (2020).
- Total trade reached $137.7 billion in 2020, spanning 221 countries and territories.
- Georgia’s top five export markets in 2020 were Canada, Germany, China, Mexico, and Singapore.
- Motor vehicles are Georgia’s No. 2 top exported product, representing $1.9 billion.
- Pat Wilson is the Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner.
- In 2020, Georgia saw more than $1 billion in foreign investments and more than 2,000 jobs from South Korean companies.
- KB Autosys announced a $38 million investment in Lone Oak in Meriwether County in February 2021.
- KB Autosys will build its first U.S. manufacturing facility in Georgia to meet product demand from General Motors, Hyundai, and Kia Motors.
- Kia Motors’ plant in West Point, Georgia, is located just 30 minutes south on I-85 from Lone Oak.
- Richard A. Kaatz is vice president of KB Autosys America.
- Hyundai TRANSYS announced plans to invest more than $240 million in its West Point, Georgia, operations.
- Hyundai TRANSYS produces eight-speed transmissions at the West Point facility.
- The Hyundai TRANSYS expansion will create 678 full-time, long-term positions at a new 620,000-sq.-ft. (57,598-sq.-m.) manufacturing facility near its current Kia campus location.
- This expansion will take Hyundai TRANSYS’ employment total in Georgia to 1,741 jobs.
- Sangkil Jung is the Hyundai TRANSYS Georgia Powertrain President.
- GEDIA Automotive Group announced plans in August 2020 to build an advanced manufacturing facility focused on EV parts.
- GEDIA will invest $85 million in the project, delivering 200 jobs to the greater Dalton area.
- GEDIA will build and occupy a 180,000-sq.-ft. (16,722-sq.-m.) facility on a Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development Certified property in Carbondale Business Park.
- GEDIA operations are expected to begin in the third quarter of 2021.
- GEDIA supplies lightweight structural automotive parts to VW, BMW, and Mercedes-Benz.
- This investment marks GEDIA’s first site in the Southeast and its second facility in the United States.
- GEDIA’s new facility will be located just down I-75 from VW’s Chattanooga plant.
- Markus Schaumburg is one of two GEDIA Automotive Group CEOs.
