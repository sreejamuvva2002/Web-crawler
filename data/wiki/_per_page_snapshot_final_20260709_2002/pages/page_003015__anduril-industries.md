# Anduril Industries

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2022-07-14

**Source:** [Anduril Industries to Invest $60M, Create 180 New Jobs in Fulton | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-releases/2022/anduril-industries-invest-60-million-create-180-new-jobs-fulton-county)  ·  `page_003015`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Anduril Industries will invest $60 million in a new manufacturing and research facility in Atlanta.
- The investment will create more than 180 new jobs in Fulton County over the next three years.
- Anduril Industries is a defense technology company that specializes in building advanced technology for US and allied militaries.
- Governor Kemp stated that Georgia is well established as a leader in aerospace and defense due to its top research universities, colleges, technical colleges, and large active military and veteran populations.
- Anduril’s new Atlanta office will be home to Area-I, a Georgia-based manufacturer of advanced unmanned aircraft systems.
- Anduril acquired Area-I in 2021, and the company operates as a wholly owned subsidiary of Anduril.
- The new facility is located at 1435 Hills Place Northwest in Atlanta.
- The facility includes more than 180,000 square feet of industrial buildings that are being transformed into mixed-use office, research and development, manufacturing, and production space across two buildings.
- Area-I is hiring for a variety of engineering disciplines including aerospace, mechanical, software, electrical, and systems backgrounds for research and development.
- The increased space for production will enable more hires for skilled mechanical and avionics technicians.
- Matt Grimm, Chief Operating Officer of Anduril, stated that Atlanta has a highly skilled and educated workforce in aerospace, engineering, and robotics.
- Matt Grimm noted that growing their presence in Georgia will allow the company to attract world-class talent to support its mission to transform defense capabilities for the US and its allies.
- Aerospace products are Georgia’s top export at $9.19 billion in 2021.
- The aerospace sector is the state’s second largest manufacturing industry, generating a $57.5 billion economic impact.
- Georgia is home to more than 800 aerospace companies.
- Georgia maintains a robust aerospace education pipeline with a dozen high schools that offer training in the field.
- There are five technical colleges in Georgia with aviation programs.
- Several universities in Georgia offer degrees in aerospace engineering.
- Atlanta Mayor Andre Dickens stated that Atlanta continues to attract global companies looking to fuel their growth through its highly skilled, diverse tech talent and innovation ecosystem.
- Robb Pitts, Chairman of the Fulton County Board of Commissioners, called the Anduril Industries announcement a big deal that speaks to the diversity of people, communities, and industries in Fulton County.
- Metro Atlanta Chamber President and CEO Katie Kirkpatrick stated that Anduril is a welcome addition to the region's thriving community of knowledge-based businesses and will benefit from the innovative cluster of software-, hardware-, and engineering-driven businesses in metro Atlanta.
- Project Manager Kasey Smith represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The project was completed in partnership with Select Fulton, Metro Atlanta Chamber, Invest Atlanta, the Georgia Center of Innovation’s Aerospace industry team, and Georgia Power.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Anduril Industries is a fantastic fit for Georgia’s aerospace and advanced manufacturing ecosystems.
- Area-I has over a decade of history with the Georgia Center of Innovation and is a longstanding member of its Unmanned Systems Working Group.
- Anduril Industries’ family of systems is powered by Lattice OS, an AI-powered operating system that turns thousands of data streams into a realtime, 3D command and control center.
- Anduril is committed to bringing cutting-edge AI, computer vision, sensor fusion, and networking technology to the military in months, not years.
