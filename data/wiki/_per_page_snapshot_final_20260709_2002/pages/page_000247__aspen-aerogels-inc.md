# Aspen Aerogels Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** thermal_management  ·  **Published:** 2025-02

**Source:** [EV Supplier Cancels Plan to Build Plant Near Statesboro - Columbus CEO](https://thecolumbusceo.com/news/2025/02/ev-supplier-cancels-plan-build-plant-near-statesboro/)  ·  `page_000247`

## Facts

- Aspen Aerogels Inc., a Massachusetts-based electric vehicle supplier, has abandoned plans to build a manufacturing facility in Bulloch County near Statesboro, Georgia.
- The company announced on Thursday during an investor call that it will instead increase production capacity at its East Providence, R.I., plant.
- Governor Brian Kemp originally announced the Statesboro project in 2022.
- At the time of the 2022 announcement, the company was planning to invest $325 million in the plant and create more than 250 jobs.
- Production had been expected to start late in 2023.
- Aspen Aerogels manufactures thermal barriers that insulate electric car batteries from fires.
- CFO and Treasurer Ricardo Rodriguez wrote that in early 2023, pre-empting a reset in EV demand expectations, the company decided to right-time the construction of its planned second aerogel manufacturing facility in Statesboro, Georgia, and subsequently ramped up external manufacturing capacity.
- Rodriguez stated that the external manufacturing model has fully demonstrated its ability to efficiently increase aerogel supply, making a capital-light and modular capacity plan the most efficient path to creating value.
- Demand for electric vehicles has not grown as much in recent years as EV manufacturers expected.
- Factors contributing to slower EV demand include high sticker prices, an insufficient number of charging stations, and government policies that have discouraged car buyers from purchasing EVs.
- The Trump administration recently froze a $5 billion program aimed at funding the construction of EV charging stations.
