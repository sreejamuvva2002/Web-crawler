# Hyundai-LGES Georgia EV Battery Plant

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-06-28

**Source:** [Hyundai-LGES announces $4.3B EV battery plant: ‘Huge win’](https://www.thecooldown.com/green-business/hyundai-lges-ev-georgia-manufacturing-plant-battery/)  ·  `page_056198`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solutions announced a joint venture in late May 2023 to build a $4.3 billion state-of-the-art battery manufacturing plant in the U.S.
- Hyundai President and CEO Jaehoon Chang stated the partnership aims to "create a strong foundation to lead the global EV transition" and that with LGES, they are "ready to drive the EV transition in America."
- The facility will be located adjacent to the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America near Savannah, Georgia.
- Upon projected completion in 2025, the plant is expected to support the production of 300,000 EVs each year.
- The project has the potential for future expansion.
- This will be Hyundai's second battery manufacturing plant in Georgia and LGES's seventh in the U.S.
- Incentives from the Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) are drawing clean energy business to the U.S., providing tax credits for companies and consumers.
- To make products eligible for IRA tax credits, manufacturers must meet specific sourcing requirements for EV battery components.
- To make products eligible for IRA tax credits, manufacturers must complete the final assembly process in North America.
- Batteries from the new plant will supply U.S. manufacturing facilities producing new Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis EV models.
- As of mid-June, the Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis EV models were ineligible for IRA tax credits.
- The initiative aims to accelerate EV adoption with a goal of EVs or plug-in hybrids contributing 50% of new vehicle sales in the U.S. by 2030.
- The project aims to reduce the nation's reliance on Chinese battery supply chains.
- The region could see up to 8,100 new jobs available between the Hyundai Metaplant and the new battery manufacturing facility.
- Senator Jon Ossoff of Georgia led a trade mission to South Korea and praised the announcement as "yet another huge win for Georgia."
- Senator Ossoff stated that "the IRA's manufacturing incentives continue to bring jobs and investment."
