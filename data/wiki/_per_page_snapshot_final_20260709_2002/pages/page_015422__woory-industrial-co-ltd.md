# Woory Industrial Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp: Woory to Open EV Parts Manufacturing, R&D Facility in Laurens County](https://www.einpresswire.com/article/638187597/gov-kemp-woory-to-open-ev-parts-manufacturing-r-d-facility-in-laurens-county)  ·  `page_015422`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Woory Industrial Co., Ltd. will establish a new manufacturing facility in Dublin, Georgia.
- The new facility will create more than 130 new jobs.
- The project involves an estimated $18 million in investment in Dublin-Laurens County.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated that Woory Industrial is the latest job creator helping Georgia become the e-mobility capital of the nation.
- Governor Kemp noted the emerging industry is bringing historic levels of new jobs and investment to communities across the state thanks to a highly-skilled workforce and reliable infrastructure network.
- Governor Kemp expressed confidence that Woory will have a long and beneficial partnership with Dublin-Laurens County.
- Woory Industrial Company was established in 1989.
- Woory specializes in heating, ventilation, and air conditioning components and systems for the global automotive industry.
- Woory is concentrating on developing new products for electric vehicles (EVs) and hydrogen-fueled cars.
- Jungwoo Kim, CEO of Woory Industrial Company, stated the automobile industry is facing a revolution for the first time in 100 years.
- Jungwoo Kim said the transition to EVs is the future and the U.S. EV market is the key market among all automotive markets.
- Jungwoo Kim noted that over the past 10 years, Woory has grown into a specialized EV component company through continuous innovation.
- Jungwoo Kim stated Georgia will be a good opportunity for Woory to take another step forward as part of the vanguard of EV development.
- Jungwoo Kim expressed confidence that their experience and technology will contribute to community prosperity and appreciated the partnership of the State of Georgia and the City of Dublin.
- Woory's new facility is located at 404 Kellam Road in Dublin.
- The facility will be Woory's first U.S. manufacturing location.
- The facility will produce electrical heaters, control units, and actuators for EV and internal combustion carmakers in North America.
- Operations are expected to begin in November 2023.
- Unlike internal combustion engine vehicles, electric vehicles do not have a heating source, so EV heaters are required for cabin heating and maintaining battery temperature.
- Woory's pioneering development of electric heaters over the last 10 years has made it a leader in this growing market segment.
- Woory will be hiring personnel from all fields necessary for an automotive parts manufacturing plant.
- Dublin Mayor Joshua Kight said fostering quality and sustainable growth is a goal of the City of Dublin.
- Mayor Kight noted Woory's commitment to place an existing manufacturing building back into service helps promote infill development while creating new career paths.
- Dr. Fred Williams, Chair of the Board of Directors of the Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority, said they are excited to welcome Woory to the community.
- Dr. Williams noted Dublin and Laurens County has a rapidly growing automotive sector thanks to geographic location on Interstate 16 and access to a skilled and trained workforce.
- Dr. Williams stated their partnership with area education systems and training providers will ensure training programs are in place to fill Woory's talent pipeline for years to come.
- Project Manager Jacob Lee represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The project is in partnership with the Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson said projects like Woory's first U.S. home are why they prioritized being on the cutting-edge of this transformational opportunity in manufacturing.
- Commissioner Wilson noted these projects are creating jobs and opportunities in rural communities.
- Commissioner Wilson stated Georgia has invested for decades to match competitive projects with prepared communities and sites, and is thrilled to congratulate Dublin-Laurens County on bringing e-mobility jobs to middle Georgia.
- For over a century, Georgia has fostered healthy industry practices, encouraged partnership and innovation, and positioned the state as a leader in developing emerging technologies for the automotive and mobility industry.
- From July 1 through December 31, 2022, the automotive industry was the second-largest source of new jobs and investment in Georgia.
- The state has pursued job creation opportunities along the entire EV supply chain, resulting in over $22.7 billion in investment and the creation of more than 28,400 jobs since 2020.
- Woory grew from a small factory producing fuel senders in Suwon, South Korea almost 35 years ago into an international enterprise with production plants around the world.
- Woory employs over 1,300 people globally.
- Woory's business focuses on development and production of HVAC auto components for all types of vehicles, including electric, internal combustion engine, eco-friendly, and hydrogen-fueled cars.
- Woory has been nominated as a supplier for Tesla, Rivian, Lucid, and Canoo.
- Woory is currently supplying the HV heater system used to heat the cabins of EVs.
