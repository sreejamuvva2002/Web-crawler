# SKF

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2024-10-31

**Source:** [SKF Selling Pennsylvania Aerospace Supply Unit for $220M - Modern Distribution Management](https://www.mdm.com/news/operations/earnings/skf-selling-pennsylvania-aerospace-supply-unit-for-220m/)  ·  `page_059974`

## Facts

- Sweden-based bearings maker SKF is set to divest its south-central Pennsylvania-based ring and seal business unit to exit non-core aerospace markets.
- The Pennsylvania operation, based in Hanover, will be sold to Carco PRP Group subsidiary PCTI for $220 million.
- The Hanover facility makes mechanical seals and rings and had 2023 annual sales of approximately $65 million.
- The pending divestment deal was announced Oct. 29 and is expected to close in 1Q25.
- SKF is also exploring options to divest its elastomeric device (PED) aerospace operation in Elgin, IL, which it considers non-strategic and smaller than the Hanover unit.
- The company stated that the PED exit process is 'proceeding according to plan.'
- On Oct. 31, SKF announced it completed the acquisition of the lubrication and flow management business of John Sample Group (JSG).
- JSG is a lubrication system and full-service solutions provider with operations in Australia, New Zealand, Indonesia, and Singapore.
- Terms for the JSG acquisition were not disclosed, but SKF noted JSG had approximately $51 million in its latest fiscal year sales and employs 85 people.
- The JSG business will be integrated into SKF’s existing lubrication management business to strengthen offerings for customers in engineered solutions, heavy industries, and mobile equipment.
- On Sept. 17, SKF announced its plan to separate its Automotive business as a standalone entity to become more industrial-focused.
- As of an Oct. 31 report update, SKF has 'kick-started' separation planning and formed a dedicated project organization to list the Automotive business on Nasdaq Stockholm by the first half of 2026.
- SKF shared its Q3 2024 financial results on Oct. 30, showing annual sales declines in both its Industrial and Automotive units.
- The company posted total Q3 sales of $2.22 billion, down 4.4% year-over-year organically, with Industrial organic sales falling 4.6% and Automotive organic sales falling 4.0%.
- SKF expects a mid-single-digit organic Q4 sales decline year-over-year, as well as for full-year organic sales.
