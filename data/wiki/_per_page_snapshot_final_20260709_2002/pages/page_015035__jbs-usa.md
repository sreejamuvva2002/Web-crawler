# JBS USA

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-12

## Facts

- JBS USA announced the planned closure of two facilities in Pennsylvania and Tennessee.
- One closed facility is a beef production facility in Souderton, Pennsylvania (a suburb of Philadelphia).
- The other closed facility is a value-added facility in Memphis, Tennessee.
- CEO Wesley Batista Filho stated the decisions directly affect team members and communities.
- JBS USA is implementing transition plans that include opportunities for team members to apply for open roles at other company facilities across the United States.
- Transition plans include on-site support and resources to assist team members through the transition.
- Transition plans include continued engagement with local stakeholders and workforce partners.
- The closures are part of a broader strategy focused on growth, modernization, and long-term competitiveness in the United States.
- Over the past year, JBS USA has made significant investments in new facilities and improvements across the United States, including major expansions in Texas, Georgia, and Iowa.
- These projects are focused on growing prepared foods and value-added capabilities, modernizing operations, and enhancing the company’s ability to serve customers in the years ahead.
- Earlier this year, JBS USA combined its beef and case-ready businesses into a more integrated platform designed to improve efficiency, enhance productivity, and expand value-added capabilities across its network.
- Production from the affected facilities will be absorbed into other operations across its network, ensuring continuity of supply and service for customers.
- JBS USA remains committed to U.S. agriculture and rural communities, working closely with producers and grower partners across the country.
- CEO Wesley Batista Filho said demand for high-quality protein continues to grow and the company is committed to meeting that demand as a reliable partner to customers, producers, and communities.
