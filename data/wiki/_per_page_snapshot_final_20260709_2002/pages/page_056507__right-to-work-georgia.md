# Right to Work Georgia

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Right to Work Georgia Excited to Welcome New Business! - NRTWC](https://nrtwc.org/right-to-work-georgia-excited-to-welcome-new-business/)  ·  `page_056507`

## Facts

- Right to Work Georgia reports an increase in economic activity with businesses adding new locations and expanding across the state.
- Companies making investments in Right to Work Georgia include Norma Precision, Joon Georgia, Grupo Bimbo, and King’s Hawaiian.
- The listed companies will collectively create a total of 1,372 new jobs for Georgia.
- Norma Precision is investing $60 million to add a new location in Bryan County, Georgia.
- Norma Precision's investment will create 600 new jobs in Bryan County, Georgia.
- Norma Precision previously manufactured ammunition in the United States for 12 years under the name RUAG Ammotec USA.
- Norma Precision has experienced steady growth over the past four years.
- The economic impact of Norma Precision's new facility will be felt across county lines through hiring and partnering with local suppliers.
- Bryan and Chatham counties collaborated on an innovative solution to secure Norma Precision's new jobs for the region.
- GPA welcomed Norma Precision's growth, citing Georgia's broad logistics network as key to reaching major domestic and global markets efficiently.
- Joon Georgia is investing $317 million to build a new facility in Bulloch County, Georgia.
- Joon Georgia's new facility will create 317 new jobs in Bulloch County, Georgia.
- Joon Georgia's announcement is described as a landmark moment for driving Georgia's automotive industry into the future.
- Hyundai Motor Group, its suppliers, and the Savannah area JDA are collaborating to create generational opportunities for Georgia communities.
- Joon Georgia's facility will be located in Bruce Yawn Commerce Park.
- Joon Georgia's site is located in proximity to the new Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- Georgia Southern University and Ogeechee Technical College are expected to provide critical workforce support for Joon Georgia.
- Grupo Bimbo is investing $200 million to add a new facility in Valdosta, Georgia.
- Grupo Bimbo's new facility will create 295 new jobs in Valdosta, Georgia.
- Grupo Bimbo previously joined as an international company in Georgia last year and has been a strong community partner and premier employer in the Valdosta region.
- Grupo Bimbo's expansion represents a long-term partnership with the Valdosta-Lowndes community.
- King’s Hawaiian is investing $85 million to expand its operations in Oakwood, Georgia.
- King's Hawaiian's expansion will create 160 new jobs in Hall County, Georgia.
- Georgia has been an important part of King's Hawaiian for more than a decade.
- King's Hawaiian has worked with the University System of Georgia to offer learning and career development opportunities for students.
- King's Hawaiian supports community programs including the Boys and Girls Clubs, Junior Achievement, and Youth Leadership Hall in Hall County and Northeast Georgia.
