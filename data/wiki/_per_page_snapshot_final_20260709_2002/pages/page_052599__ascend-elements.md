# Ascend Elements

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Ascend Elements to Relocate and Expand Corporate Headquarters and R&D Tech Center to Devens, Massachusetts](https://www.bldup.com/posts/ascend-elements-to-relocate-and-expand-corporate-headquarters-and-r-d-tech-center-to-devens-massachusetts)  ·  `page_052599`

## Facts

- Ascend Elements announced plans to relocate its corporate headquarters and research & development center from Westborough, Massachusetts to Devens, Massachusetts.
- The new facility will be a 101,000 square foot space located at 39 Jackson Road in the Pathway Devens campus.
- The facility is owned and operated by King Street Properties in Boston.
- Ascend Elements has experienced over 500% headcount growth in the last 18 months.
- The company currently employs approximately 350 team members globally.
- Ascend Elements employs 140 people in Massachusetts.
- The relocation from Westborough to Devens is planned for September 2024.
- CEO Mike O'Kronley stated the relocation will be a milestone for the company's rapid growth in the engineered battery materials industry.
- Pathway Devens is a 45-acre, purpose-built development in Devens that includes an 8,300 square foot amenity center and taproom opening in Q1 2024.
- The Pathway Devens amenity center features a fitness center, lounge area, game room, and café to serve workers and the community.
- Other businesses located on the Pathway Devens campus include Electric Hydrogen, Commonwealth Fusion Systems, and Azzur Group.
- Tyson Reynoso, Managing Director & Partner at King Street Properties, noted that Ascend Elements' mission aligns with King Street's goal of creating science campuses to solve health and climate issues.
- Tyson Reynoso added that Ascend Elements' addition solidifies Devens as the top destination in Greater Boston for innovators engaged in advanced manufacturing.
- Ascend Elements is a leader in the electric vehicle battery materials and battery recycling industry.
- The company uses its patented Hydro-to-Cathode® direct precursor synthesis process to transform used lithium-ion batteries and gigafactory manufacturing scrap into engineered battery materials.
- Engineered battery materials produced by Ascend Elements include cathode active materials (CAM) and cathode precursor (pCAM).
- The patented process eliminates several intermediary steps in traditional cathode manufacturing, providing significant economic and carbon-reduction benefits.
- Ascend Elements' customers include Honda, SK Battery America, Freudenberg e-Power Systems, and others.
- Ascend Elements recently closed a $542 million funding round.
- The company received $480 million in U.S. Department of Energy grant awards.
- The DOE grants will accelerate construction of a $1 billion engineered battery materials manufacturing campus in Hopkinsville, Kentucky.
- Ascend Elements operates one of North America's largest electric vehicle battery recycling facilities in Covington, Georgia.
- CEO Mike O'Kronley stated that central Massachusetts is home to the company, noting its technology was invented at Worcester Polytechnic Institute and its first location was in Worcester.
- O'Kronley emphasized the company's strong ties to central Massachusetts as it expands in the southeast United States.
