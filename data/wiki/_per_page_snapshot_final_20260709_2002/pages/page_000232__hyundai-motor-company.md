# Hyundai Motor Company

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-04

**Source:** [Unpacking claims Hyundai is closing Georgia plant following ICE raid | Snopes.com](https://www.snopes.com/news/2025/09/10/ice-hyundai-plant-georgia/)  ·  `page_000232`

## Facts

- Claims that car manufacturer Hyundai would be closing down a sprawling auto plant "megasite" located in rural Georgia spread across the internet in early September 2025.
- The rumor spread following a raid by U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) on Sept. 4, 2025, at a Hyundai manufacturing facility in Ellabell, Georgia, located outside Savannah.
- ICE detained 475 people during the raid, most of them South Korean nationals.
- Social media users alleged the company was pulling out of the area, claiming "8500 American jobs and 4.3 billion dollar investment from South Korea... gone."
- The Ellabell plant currently employs about 1,200 people with plans to eventually employ at least 8,100 workers.
- The raid focused on a construction site for HL-GA Battery Company, a joint partnership between Hyundai and LG Energy Solution announced in 2023 to produce electric vehicle battery cells.
- HL-GA was touted as an additional $2 billion investment following Hyundai's larger $5.5 billion electric vehicle plant announced in 2022.
- Steven Schrank, special agent in charge of Homeland Security Investigations in Georgia and Alabama, stated detained employees worked for various companies on the site, including subcontractors, not just the parent company.
- There was no evidence that Hyundai was closing the Georgia facility in light of the raid.
- The Associated Press reported HL-GA would be pausing construction of the battery plant, which is scheduled to open in 2026.
- Michael Stewart, director of public relations and communications at Hyundai North America, stated there have been no changes to Hyundai's operations in the U.S.
- Hyundai's official statement claimed none of those detained are directly employed by Hyundai Motor Company and that the company has zero tolerance for law violators.
- Hyundai is aware of the immigration enforcement action at its supplier HL-GA Battery Company in Bryan County, Georgia, and is closely monitoring the situation.
- Hyundai expects all partners, suppliers, contractors, and subcontractors to comply with employment verification requirements and immigration laws.
- North America Chief Manufacturing Officer Chris Susock will assume governance of the entire megasite in Georgia and conduct an investigation to ensure supplier and subcontractor compliance.
- CNN reported the raid's impact on the local community was less visible than in other immigration crackdowns because many detainees were temporary employees during construction.
- Few permanent employees expected to fill 8,500 slots have been hired, and the bulk of the current workforce consists of transient single men on temporary visas or contracts rotating through monthly.
- A local business owner reported an overnight drop in business at Viet Huong Supermarket in Ellabell, noting only three Korean customers instead of the usual 10 to 15.
- One local resident stated Hyundai's facility has not yet brought many jobs to the community and felt more construction jobs at the battery plant should have gone to local residents.
