# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-10-27

**Source:** [Hyundai breaks ground on $5.5B electric car manufacturing plant in Georgia | FOX 5 Atlanta](https://www.fox5atlanta.com/news/hyundai-electric-car-plant-georgia-jobs-development)  ·  `page_009233`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group broke ground on its first U.S. plant dedicated to making electric vehicles in Bryan County west of Savannah, Ga.
- The groundbreaking ceremony took place two weeks before the Nov. 8 election.
- A robotic dog delivered champagne flutes for Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp and top Hyundai executives during the celebration.
- Euisun Chung, executive chairman of Hyundai Motor Group, said the plant will be the envy of the industry.
- Hyundai announced a $5.5 billion manufacturing plant in Bryan County five months prior to the groundbreaking.
- Vast areas of the site have already been cleared of trees following the announcement.
- Hyundai plans to open the factory in 2025.
- The facility will employ at least 8,100 workers.
- The plant is designed to produce up to 300,000 electric vehicles per year.
- Georgia officials state the project is the largest economic development project the state has ever seen.
- Gov. Brian Kemp called the project transformational for the region and said it will resonate for generations to come.
- The groundbreaking ceremony occurred ahead of high-stakes elections where both Kemp and Sen. Raphael Warnock face formidable challengers.
- Kemp and Warnock shared a brief handshake after officials turned ceremonial scoops of dirt.
- Sen. Raphael Warnock expressed a desire to see more collaboration and emphasized the need for more jobs in Georgia.
- Kemp worked with state and local officials to secure a package of $1.8 billion in tax breaks and incentives to lure Hyundai to Georgia.
- President Joe Biden has embraced boosting U.S. production and sales of electric vehicles as part of his strategy for slowing climate change and adding manufacturing jobs.
- The White House recently announced $2.8 billion in grants to ramp up EV battery production in 12 states.
- A sweeping climate and health care law passed by congressional Democrats in August includes incentives for EV manufacturing and purchasing.
- The federal law provides tax credits for EV buyers worth up to $7,500.
- Congressional changes to the tax credit limit eligibility only to electric vehicles produced in North America.
- Hyundai EVs will not qualify for the tax credit until the company starts production in Georgia.
- South Korea’s ambassador to the U.S., Tae-yong Cho, told dignitaries that his country believes the revised EV tax credit unfairly excludes Hyundai.
- Ambassador Cho stated Korean companies are at risk of being disadvantaged by the provision and criticized limiting consumer choices for climate change efforts.
- Warnock voted in support of the Inflation Reduction Act containing the revised tax credit but defends it as proof of U.S. seriousness on EV manufacturing.
- Warnock introduced a bill to push back the effective date of the tax credit revisions.
- Warnock asked the Treasury Department to use maximum flexibility in adopting regulations for the new criteria.
- Warnock stated he will continue working with Ambassador Cho and other officials to perfect the project.
- Rivian announced plans last year for a $5 billion EV plant east of Atlanta that would employ 7,500 workers.
- A Morgan County judge rejected local governments' plan to exempt Rivian from an estimated $700 million in property taxes.
- Rivian agreed to pay $300 million in lieu of the exempted property taxes.
- State and local officials are weighing an appeal of the judge's decision regarding Rivian's tax break.
- The property tax break was a major piece of a $1.2 billion incentive package offered to California automaker Rivian.
- Rivian has seen its stock value decline sharply this year.
- Rivian recently announced a big recall to tighten a loose fastener that could affect drivers' ability to steer.
- Gov. Kemp expressed confidence that the Rivian Georgia plant will still happen despite the tax ruling.
- Kemp stated the vast majority of people want the Rivian project to come and expressed excitement about the whole EV marketplace in Georgia.
