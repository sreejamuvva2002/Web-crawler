# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-01-15

**Source:** [Governor Kemp's 2026 State of the State Address | Governor Brian P. Kemp Office of the Governor](https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases/2026-01-15/governor-kemps-2026-state-state-address)  ·  `page_000080`

## Facts

- Over the last seven years, Georgia has seen over 219,700 new jobs and more than 118 billion dollars in investment from projects worked by the state economic development team.
- 64 percent of those jobs and 75 percent of those investment dollars are going to communities outside the metro-Atlanta region.
- Georgia has maintained its streak as the number one state for business for an unprecedented 12 years in a row.
- Since 2019, Georgia has welcomed the three largest economic development projects in state history.
- The Hyundai Metaplant is cited as an example of a large-scale project representing a generational opportunity for Georgia families and communities.
- Over the last seven years, the state has invested more money toward education and workforce training programs than ever before in its history.
- The state has fully funded Quality Basic Education (QBE) seven times and added an additional 368 million dollars to programs and initiatives that directly prepare workers for good jobs.
- Georgia is proposing a further 20-basis point reduction in the state’s personal and corporate income tax rate, bringing it to just 4.99 percent.
- From the 2024 budget through the proposed Amended 2026 budget, 4.1 billion dollars in capital projects have been funded through cash instead of debt.
- The state has invested over 6 billion dollars in water, sewer, and transportation projects across Georgia.
- The state has put 2 billion additional dollars toward higher education than when the governor first took office.
- This year’s budget proposals include 40.7 million dollars for healthcare education facilities and medical workforce training programs.
- The state has spent over 26 million dollars for medical residency slots, adding 897 positions for a grand total of 2,523 residency slots as of this fiscal year.
- This year’s budget adds another 2.1 million dollars to expand medical residency slots.
- The governor’s amended budget proposal invests 325 million dollars in the University System of Georgia’s DREAMS Scholarship program, the first need-based scholarship of its kind in state history.
- The Georgia MATCH program, launched in 2023, has seen a 5.9 percent rise in University System of Georgia (USG) enrollment and a 7.1 percent increase in Technical College System of Georgia (TCSG) enrollment over its first two years.
- Tuition costs at Georgia public universities have increased by just five percent between 2019 and the current academic year, compared to roughly 19 percent at the University of California system.
- The HOPE Scholarship has been returned to 100 percent of its promise while tuition costs were held in check across the Board of Regents system.
- Georgia is recognized as one of the top states in the country for four-year public university affordability according to National Center for Education Statistics data.
- La Regina, an Italian food production company with a facility in Alma, Georgia, employs 110 workers and moves goods through the Port of Savannah, serving as an example of economic development revitalizing former facilities.
