# Valeo Service UK

**Type:** workforce_training  ·  **Role:** workforce_training  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- Valeo Service UK has released a new free training module on its Valeo Tech Academy platform offering technicians an introduction to battery electric vehicle (BEV) air conditioning systems.
- The session is presented by Valeo technical trainer Stewart Lynch.
- The course highlights the critical function of EV air conditioning in battery thermal management, emphasizing that regulating battery temperature is essential for performance, safety, and longevity.
- The module breaks down core differences in compressor technology, noting that BEVs rely on electrically driven compressors powered by the high-voltage battery rather than internal combustion engine belt-driven compressors lubricated with PAG oil.
- These electric compressors offer greater precision and energy efficiency thanks to sensor-driven input and inverter-based control.
- Temperature, solar, and humidity sensors feed data into an electronic control unit that works with the inverter to modulate compressor speed and protect against voltage spikes and overheating.
- The new module forms part of a growing library of free and IMI-certified training available on Valeo Tech Academy.
- The platform covers traditional maintenance and repair, hybrid and electric vehicle systems, as well as advanced driver assistance systems.
- Courses feature interactive, gamified content designed to make learning more engaging and effective.
- Pre- and post-assessments enable technicians to monitor their progress and measure skill development.
- Technicians can find additional resources on the Valeo Tech Assist website, including fitting guides and technical bulletins available at no cost.
- Technicians fitting Valeo products can scan barcodes to earn rewards through the Valeo Specialist Club, which are redeemable in the Valeo online shop.
