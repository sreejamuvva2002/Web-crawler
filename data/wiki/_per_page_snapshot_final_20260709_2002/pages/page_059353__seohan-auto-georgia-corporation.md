# Seohan Auto Georgia Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-04-11

**Source:** [Seohan Auto Georgia Announces 180 New Jobs in Liberty Co.](https://www.wtoc.com/2023/04/11/seohan-auto-georgia-announces-180-new-jobs-liberty-co/)  ·  `page_059353`

## Facts

- Seohan Auto Georgia will create at least 180 new jobs and invest more than $72 million in a new manufacturing facility in Liberty County, Ga.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp announced the project on Tuesday.
- Seohan Auto Georgia Corporation is a Tier-1 automotive parts supplier for Hyundai Motor Group America based in South Korea.
- The company has production facilities around the world, including multiple facilities in the United States.
- Jung Kee Koo, CEO of Seohan Auto Georgia Corporation, stated that sustained growth of the EV market over the past few years suggests accelerated changes to the automotive market are inevitable and believes Georgia will be the center of the EV industry and a new frontier for Seohan's future.
- The new facility is scheduled to begin production in late 2024.
- The facility will be located at the Dunwoody Court Site in the Tradeport East Business Center, a Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) Certified site.
- The new manufacturing facility will produce H/shafts, axles, and brake systems for OEMs, including Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- Allen Brown, Chairman of Liberty County Development Authority, stated that Seohan and its affiliated enterprises are responsible, thorough, and progressive organizations striving to advance innovative technology.
- Alyce Thornhill, Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy, represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project in partnership with the Liberty County Industrial Development Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia Power.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that the ongoing transition to e-mobility and sustainable technology creates a window of opportunity for companies and states remaining on the forefront.
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America officially broke ground in Bryan County on October 25, 2022, marking their first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility.
- Offsite suppliers connected to the Hyundai Metaplant project were predicted to invest approximately $1 billion in other locations around Georgia.
- Projects announced to date for the Hyundai ecosystem have already exceeded the predicted investment by nearly $1 billion, according to the release.
- Since 2020, more than 35 EV-related projects have announced more than $21 billion in investment and 27,800 jobs in Georgia.
