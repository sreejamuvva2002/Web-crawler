# SK Battery America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-04-02

**Source:** [Leading EV Battery Company Joins the Bust - Master Resource](https://www.masterresource.org/ev-battery-sector/battery-companies-join-the-bust/)  ·  `page_060602`

## Facts

- The domestic EV market and the battery industry serving it are in steep decline.
- SK Battery America cut 958 jobs at its Georgia plant amid an EV sales slowdown.
- The South Korean battery manufacturer reduced its workforce at its Commerce, Georgia, facility by about 37% as EV demand weakens and policy changes reshape the market.
- Author Ashby Lincoln explained how shifting market and political winds resulted in the malinvestment.
- SK Battery America stated it is still committed to long-term battery investments for the U.S. with business from Volkswagen and Hyundai models.
- Volkswagen has pumped the brakes on major US factory plans.
- Hyundai and Kia have cut and delayed EV models.
- In all, 18 EV carmakers have announced cutbacks or other retreats.
- SK Battery America’s website currently claims it has solidified supply agreements with top-tier global automotive leaders at the forefront of the electric vehicle revolution.
- The company’s website states its commitment to expanding the electric vehicle battery industry extends to collaboration across diverse sectors in the U.S. and maintains enduring partnerships with prominent companies rooted in advanced technology and steadfast reliability.
- The mega-agreement with Ford is kaput.
- The advertised workforce of 2,600 on the company’s website is incorrect.
- The company’s planned new battery plant in Tennessee is at risk.
- A recent study by the Federal Reserve Bank of Dallas warned that projects under construction reflect a significant amount of potential capacity, especially when considering those focused on EVs.
- The fate of these plants hangs in the balance, whether they will be canceled or postponed indefinitely in 2026 due to the changing dynamics of the U.S. battery market.
