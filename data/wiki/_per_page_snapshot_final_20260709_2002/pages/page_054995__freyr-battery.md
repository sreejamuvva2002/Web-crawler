# Freyr Battery

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-12-15

**Source:** [Freyr Battery Receives Shareholder Approval For Redomicile to USA](https://www.highnorthnews.com/en/freyr-battery-receives-shareholder-approval-redomicile-usa)  ·  `page_054995`

## Facts

- Freyr Battery is two steps closer to Giga America, its US-based battery factory, as it leaves Europe behind.
- Freyr received shareholder approval for all proposals at its extraordinary general meeting (EGM) on December 15, 2023.
- Freyr expects to complete the process to redomicile from Luxembourg to the U.S. by the end of 2023.
- Tom Einar Jensen is Freyr's Executive Chairperson.
- Giga America is currently undergoing initial development in Coweta County, Georgia.
- The U.S. Department of Energy (DoE) invited Freyr to submit the Part II loan application under the DoE Title 17 Clean Energy Financing Program for the Giga America project in Georgia.
- Birger Steen is Freyr's Chief Executive Officer.
- Freyr will utilize the U.S.-based 24M Technologies SemiSolidTM platform for the manufacture of utility scale energy storage LFP battery systems.
- The DoE loan application process is a key element of Freyr’s capital formation strategy to fund construction of the Giga America project.
- Freyr continues to engage with potential investors in project-level equity to finance the equity component of anticipated capital expenditures and organizational development associated with the project.
- The company plans to continue advancing the DoE Title 17 process during 2024.
- In November, Freyr announced plans to cut costs at its battery venture in Mo i Rana, Northern Norway, in 2024.
- The Giga factory had been referred to as potentially one of the largest investments in mainland Norway in recent times.
- Freyr chose to minimize Giga Arctic spending for 2024 due to the U.S. Inflation Reduction Act (IRA).
- The company stated that Norway and Europe have yet to offer a competitive response to the US IRA or similar incentives from Canada.
- Freyr plans to minimize spending on the Norwegian project in 2024, secure the asset with remaining committed capital spending, and work with stakeholders in Norway and Europe to develop a mutually attractive policy solution.
- Almost 80 employees were laid off from the company shortly after the IRA-related announcements.
