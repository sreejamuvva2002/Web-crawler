# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-10-25

**Source:** [As Metaplant rises in Ellabell, regional officials expect a 5- to 7-year hire-up to the Hyundai Motor Group’s 8,500 jobs and suppliers’ nearly 6,000 - Statesboro Herald](https://www.statesboroherald.com/local/as-metaplant-rises-in-ellabell-regional-officials-expect-a-5-to-7-year-hire-up-to-the-hyundai-motor-groups-8500-jobs-and-suppliers-nearly-6000/)  ·  `page_019856`

## Facts

- Not all of the 8,500 employees expected to work at Hyundai Motor Group’s Metaplant America will be working at those locations on the first day of production.
- Almost 6,000 more projected for the electric vehicle and battery manufacturing site’s suppliers across Georgia will not be working there on day one.
- Area economic development officials expect at least a five-year ramp-up to the full job creation numbers.
- Trip Tollison is president and CEO of the Savannah Economic Development Authority.
- Trip Tollison told reporters on Oct. 25, 2023 that it will be a five- to seven-year hire-up.
- Officials stress that Hyundai is not going to hire 14,000 people in one year.
- Reporters and news cameras were given a tour of the Metaplant construction site in northern Bryan County on Oct. 25, 2023.
- The tour marked the first anniversary of the groundbreaking ceremony.
- HMG Metaplant CEO and President Oscar Kwon told reporters the plant is “on track to start production in early 2025”.
- Hyundai Motor Company Global President and Chief Operating Officer Josè Muñoz added, “if not sooner.”
- The complex is called a “meta” plant because it will include several HMG-affiliated suppliers and another corporation’s battery factory.
- It will make electric vehicles under all of Hyundai’s labels: Hyundai, Kia, Genesis and Ioniq.
- The Savannah Economic Development Authority is based in Chatham County.
- Development authorities in Bulloch, Bryan and Effingham counties participate in the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority (JDA).
- The JDA, together with the state of Georgia, purchased and provided the site.
- The incentive package is the largest in Georgia’s history.
- It included $2.1 billion worth of tax breaks, according to an Associated Press report in September.
- Eric Johnson is a retired architect and former state senator who serves as project director at the site for the JDA and for the state.
- When the HMG plant was first announced in May 2022, the prediction was about 8,100 jobs would be created there through a $5.54 billion investment.
- The addition of the on-site battery plant in Hyundai’s joint venture with LG Energy Solution was announced in September.
- The battery plant adds 400 expected jobs and boosts the total expected investment to $7.59 billion.
- Off-site suppliers represent a further $2.2 billion investment in nine different counties, for a total investment approaching $10 billion from Hyundai Motor Group and its suppliers.
- Parts suppliers are now projected to employ about 6,000 workers.
- HMG states that the Metaplant and off-site suppliers together will create a total of 14,476 jobs at full production.
- So far, 290 people have been hired to work at the HMG Metaplant, including 182 salaried and 108 hourly employees.
- Some hires are in training at other HMG plants, such as Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Alabama in Montgomery and even in South Korea.
- Sources said they couldn’t predict how long those workers will work or train elsewhere before being needed in production at Metaplant America.
- HMG representatives repeated the “early 2025 … if not sooner” forecast and didn’t provide an estimate of how many people will be working there at start-up.
- The count of 290 hires does not include 53 South Korean nationals, Kwon being one of them, reportedly assigned to the plant.
- Both HMG and its battery joint venture partner LG Energy Solution are headquartered in South Korea.
- Oscar Kwon thanked the tour group for their hospitality during the first year and noted most staff are only there for a short time.
- Approximately 2,000 employees of contractors are now working to build the manufacturing complex, but those workers aren’t included in the job-creation figures.
- For production jobs including those within the HMG plant, the battery plant and all affiliates, the average salary will be $58,105.
- This average salary was stated to be 25% higher than an average current Bryan County annual wage of $47,000.
- Bulloch County’s comparable average salary is $45,032, based on a $21.65 average hourly wage used in Department of Labor reports.
- The predicted Metaplant wage would be 29% higher than Bulloch’s average.
- As of Thursday, the Metaplant’s career job search page listed 95 job descriptions.
- A “production team member” listing stated the salary as “from $22.40 per hour.”
- Benjy Thompson, CEO of the Development Authority of Bulloch County, agreed with the prediction of five to seven years to achieve the full number of jobs in a phone interview.
- Thompson noted that the nine counties where HMG supplier factories are being located include a few far afield of the four JDA counties and their immediate neighbors.
- There has been one supplier announcement in Henry County and another in Dublin.
- Henry County’s county seat is McDonough, which is north of Macon and south of Atlanta.
- Tollison called the suppliers “scattered all over the region and some outside of the region.”
- Three Hyundai suppliers are now building plants in Bulloch County at sites along U.S. Highway 301 south of Statesboro and near the I-16 interchange.
- Joon Georgia Inc. is predicted to create 630 jobs.
- Ecoplastic America Corporation is projected for 456 jobs.
- Hanon Systems is projected for 160 jobs.
