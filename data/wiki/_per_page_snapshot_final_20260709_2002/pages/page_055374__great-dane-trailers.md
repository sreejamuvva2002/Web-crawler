# Great Dane Trailers

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2008-11-11

**Source:** [Great Dane Trailers to close manufacturing facility in Savannah, Ga. | Commercial Carrier Journal](https://www.ccjdigital.com/business/article/14912059/great-dane-trailers-to-close-manufacturing-facility-in-savannah-ga)  ·  `page_055374`

## Facts

- Great Dane Trailers announced plans to close its original manufacturing facility in Savannah, Ga., effective Jan. 9.
- The company cited the effect of the extended economic downturn on the transportation industry coupled with the need for more efficient production as the key factors behind closing the refrigerated trailer plant.
- About 270 plant hourly and office employees will be affected by this closing.
- Corporate office roles unaffected include sales and marketing, design engineering, manufacturing engineering, research and development, retail accounting, MIS, corporate quality, and customer service.
- The Savannah plant was built in 1919 for the expanding Savannah Blowpipe Co. before becoming the starting ground for the first Great Dane trailers.
- Phillip Pines, chief operating officer of Great Dane Limited Partnership, stated that the closing is a sad day affecting many family and friends, and emphasized that the plant has remained pivotal in the company's history.
- Pines noted that the closing is no reflection on the caliber of the people or their work, but rather that the company has exhausted the ability of this plant to produce refrigerated trailers at a competitive price.
- Plans for replacing the lost capacity are underway to meet customer expectations while remaining price-competitive.
- Advancements in technology for trailer design and production have placed increased demands on manufacturing, necessitating a modernized facility to maintain Great Dane’s position as the leader in the refrigerated trailer market.
- Until a new plant is established, the Savannah plant’s production will be transferred to Great Dane’s two other refrigerated plants in Wayne, Neb., and Brazil, Ind.
