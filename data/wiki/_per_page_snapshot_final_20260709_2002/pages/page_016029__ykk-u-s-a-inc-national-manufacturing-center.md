# YKK (U.S.A.) Inc. National Manufacturing Center

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Sustainable Manufacturing at YKK (U.S.A.) Inc. in Macon, GA - YKK Americas](https://ykkamericas.com/ykk-u-s-inc-national-manufacturing-center-macon-georgia-implements-sustainable-manufacturing-processes/)  ·  `page_016029`

## Facts

- The YKK (U.S.A.) Inc. National Manufacturing Center is located in Macon, Georgia.
- The facility produces zippers and other fastening products.
- The company installed a building automation system called TREND to monitor and control the facility’s electrical, water, and gas consumption within its plants.
- The TREND system contains a dashboard with charts showing the facility’s current water, electricity, and gas consumption.
- Dustin Miller, YKK (U.S.A.) Inc. senior manager of facilities engineering, stated that monitoring water usage at each plant and specific processes allowed them to eliminate wasted usage during down periods.
- The system enabled the company to reduce the amount of water required to run certain processes.
- The facility caught an excessive flow of water during a shutdown period caused by various equipment and valve failures using the monitoring system.
- Through the use of TREND, the company reduced the number of air compressors used in one plant from four to two and a half based on measured air flow demand in cubic feet per minute.
- Reducing the air compressors decreased the horsepower required to supply the plant and significantly reduced the cost to run them.
- The TREND system is described as a continuous monitoring platform.
- The facility operates an extensive recycling program with containers inside plants for plastic bottles, cans, white paper, and magazines.
- Recycling containers outside the plants are used for scrap metal, cardboard, and brass.
- The company participates in the annual Ocmulgee Alive! River Cleanup event every October to help clean up the Ocmulgee River in Macon, Georgia.
