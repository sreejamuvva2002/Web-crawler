## Facts

- Central Georgia is an eleven-county region anchored by both the Macon and Warner Robins metropolitan areas.
- The economy in Central Georgia tends to cluster around five areas: aerospace, healthcare and hospitals, kaolin, warehousing and distribution, and heritage and ecotourism.
- In August 2017, Canadian-based Irving Consumer Products announced plans to build a manufacturing plant in Macon that would create additional jobs.
- The Central Georgia Business and Technology Park in Thomaston is a 240-acre facility that houses tenants such as Southern Company, Solutions Pest & Lawn, Criterion Technologies, and Chief Manufacturing.
- Houston County contains Georgia's largest industrial complex at Warner Robins Air Force Base and has more than 3,000 acres of land for industrial development with one of the lowest property tax rates in Central Georgia.
- Blue Bird Corporation is listed as a major employer in the region.
- The Macon Economic Development Commission recruits new businesses and industries to assist in business growth and development.
- The region is only hours away from the Port of Savannah, a major U.S. seaport.
- Major freeways and highways in the region include Interstate 16, Interstate 75, Interstate 475, US23, US41, US80, US129, US341, and US441.
