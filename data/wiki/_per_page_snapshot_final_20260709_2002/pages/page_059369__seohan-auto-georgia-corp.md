# Seohan Auto Georgia Corp.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-04-11

**Source:** [Hyundai supplier Seohan set to build $72M, 180-job Georgia plant | Fox News](https://www.foxnews.com/us/hyundai-supplier-seohan-set-72m-180-job-georgia-plant)  ·  `page_059369`

## Facts

- Seohan Group announced plans to build a $72 million factory that will produce axles, half shafts, and brake systems for Hyundai Motor Group.
- The South Korean company announced Tuesday it would build the plant near Midway, south of Savannah, with plans to hire at least 180 new employees.
- Seohan Auto Georgia is the seventh major supplier to locate in the region following Hyundai's May announcement to build a $5.5 billion plant to assemble electric vehicles and batteries in Ellabell, Georgia.
- Hyundai's Ellabell site could grow to employ 8,100 workers and is slated to begin producing vehicles in 2025.
- Seohan and six other suppliers have since pledged to invest nearly $2 billion and hire more than 4,600 people.
- Seoul-based Seohan already supplies a Hyundai assembly plant in Montgomery, Alabama and a Kia plant in West Point, Georgia.
- Seohan currently makes axles and driveshafts at two plants in Auburn, Alabama that opened in 2008 and 2014.
- The company said last year it would invest $13.5 million in the Auburn plants, which jointly employ more than 200 people, to make parts for electric vehicles.
- Seohan stated it needs additional capacity to make parts as automakers convert to electric propulsion.
- Seohan Auto Georgia Corp. CEO Jung Kee Koo said sustained growth of the EV market over the past few years suggests accelerated changes to the automotive market is inevitable.
- Jung Kee Koo stated, "We believe Georgia will be the center of the EV industry, and will be a new frontier for Seohan’s future with limitless opportunities and potential."
- The new plant will be located in a business park off Interstate 95 near Midway in Liberty County.
- The company plans to begin production at the new Georgia facility in late 2024.
- Seohan could qualify for $2.7 million in state income tax credits, calculated at $3,000 per job over five years.
- To qualify for the tax credits, workers must make at least $31,300 a year.
- The state will also pay to train workers for the new Georgia plant.
