# Valeo

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2020-12-16

**Source:** [Valeo is revolutionising e-bike drivetrains - Auto Components India](https://autocomponentsindia.com/valeo-is-revolutionizing-e-bike-drivetrains/)  ·  `page_012736`

## Facts

- Valeo is the world leader in automotive electrification.
- Valeo has adapted its 48V motors to bikes, resulting in a high-performance electric assistance system for bikes.
- Jacques Aschenbroich serves as Valeo’s Chairman and Chief Executive Officer.
- One of Valeo’s key strategic focuses is accelerating expansion in emerging markets for new “zero-emissions” mobility, including electric small city vehicles, electric motorcycles and scooters, last-mile autonomous delivery droids, and electric bikes.
- Valeo is leveraging and adapting technological platforms developed for the automotive industry, specifically ADAS (advanced driver assistance systems) and low-voltage electrification (48V).
- The Valeo Smart e-Bike System is the first solution in the world to integrate both an electric motor and an adaptive automatic transmission in the pedal assembly.
- With this new electric assistance system, gears change automatically and algorithms instantly adapt to the amount of electric assistance needed from the first pedal stroke.
- Valeo's technology was developed in partnership with Effigear.
- The technology comprises a 48V electric motor and a seven-speed automatic, adaptive gearbox in a single unit located in the pedal assembly.
- The motor’s torque is 130 Newton meters, multiplying the cyclist’s effort by eight.
- Other systems on the market offer up to a fivefold boost compared to Valeo's system.
- Used on a cargo bike, Valeo’s electric assistance allows a cyclist carrying a 150kg load to climb a 14% gradient without breaking a sweat.
- Valeo’s electric motor is more efficient than the 24V or 36V motors installed on the vast majority of e-bikes available today.
- The adaptive automatic transmission uses an algorithm to understand and instantly adjust to each person’s needs, taking into account riding style and journey.
- Gears shift smoothly with no buttons or levers needing to be pressed.
- The Valeo Smart e-Bike System includes an anti-theft function integrated directly into the pedal assembly that blocks bike use when activated.
- The system boasts a pedestrian push-assist function useful for carrying heavy loads.
- It includes a boost function to make it easier to overtake other cyclists and climb hills.
- Valeo developed the system from scratch, eliminating the need for derailleurs, sprockets, handlebar shifters, cables, or a traditional bike chain.
- The traditional chain is replaced by a cleaner belt system.
- Electric bikes using this technology no longer need around 50 parts that are often fragile and require constant maintenance.
- On average, a traditional model needs to be serviced at least once a month.
- Valeo aims solely to equip bikes with its electric assistance solution rather than build bikes itself.
- Three prototypes were developed to show adaptation to all types of models: a city bike, a mountain bike, and a cargo bike for transporting loads.
- The cargo bike prototype features unique reversing and braking energy recovery functions.
- The high-end solution meets the best and most demanding automotive standards in terms of quality, robustness, durability, and safety.
- Valeo's major assets include its 48V technological platform, which enables meeting a broad range of needs from a single technology base.
- Valeo has very high volume production capacity delivering significant economies of scale, producing around 30 million electric machines each year.
- The electric bike market is booming, with a fifteen-fold expansion expected over the next ten years.
- Global sales in the electric bike market are expected to bring 270 million euros in 2030 (McKinsey/Center For Future Mobility -2020).
- The market for electric cargo bikes is set to grow substantially as regulations prohibiting access to city centers for polluting vehicles gradually come into force.
- In 2019, 57% of Valeo’s Group sales were generated from technologies aimed at reducing CO2 emissions.
