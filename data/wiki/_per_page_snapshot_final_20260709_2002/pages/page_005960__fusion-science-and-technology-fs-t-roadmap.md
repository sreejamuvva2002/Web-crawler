# Fusion Science and Technology (FS&T) Roadmap

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [Energy Department Releases Finalized Fusion Science and Technology Roadmap to Accelerate Commercial Fusion Power | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/articles/energy-department-releases-finalized-fusion-science-and-technology-roadmap-accelerate)  ·  `page_005960`

## Facts

- The U.S. Department of Energy released the finalized Fusion Science and Technology (FS&T) Roadmap on June 9, 2026.
- The roadmap serves as a national strategy to accelerate the development and commercialization of fusion energy on a rapid timeline.
- It aims to support fusion pilot plants and commercial fusion power in the mid-2030s.
- The initiative advances President Trump’s energy dominance agenda and reinforces commitments to expanding reliable American energy production, strengthening domestic supply chains, and maintaining U.S. leadership in critical technologies.
- DOE Under Secretary for Science Dr. Darío Gil stated that fusion energy has entered a new era defined by extraordinary scientific progress and public-private momentum.
- The roadmap was developed with input from more than 800 scientists and engineers across the public and private sectors.
- Contributions to the roadmap came from more than 15 private companies, over 10 National Laboratories, and more than 70 universities.
- The document identifies critical science and technology gaps that must be closed to realize fusion pilot plants and strengthen U.S. leadership in the global fusion industry.
- It establishes a unified strategy built around three primary drivers: building critical infrastructure to close fusion materials and technology gaps, innovating through advanced research/high-performance computing/AI, and growing the U.S. fusion ecosystem through public-private partnerships/supply chain development/workforce growth/commercialization pathways.
- The roadmap aligns with DOE’s Genesis Mission and will be implemented through the DOE's newly established Office of Fusion.
- More than $10 billion in private investment is already advancing fusion technologies and demonstration projects.
- DOE is coordinating a national effort to close remaining technical gaps needed to commercialize fusion energy.
- The roadmap's ability to support milestones and timelines is contingent on future public-private partnerships and Congressional appropriations, and it does not commit DOE to specific funding levels.
