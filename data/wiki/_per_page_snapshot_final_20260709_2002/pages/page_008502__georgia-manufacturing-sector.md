# Georgia Manufacturing Sector

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-10-04

**Source:** [Celebrating Manufacturing Excellence in Georgia Energy Cities - Location Georgia %](https://www.locationgeorgia.com/manufacturing-day-october-2024/)  ·  `page_008502`

## Facts

- Manufacturing Day 2024 was celebrated on October 4, 2024, highlighting manufacturers in Georgia's Energy Cities.
- Georgia has had the second-highest manufacturing employment rate in the Southeast for more than a decade, except for April and May 2020, when it was third.
- As of Q1 2024, there are currently more than 435,000 people employed in Manufacturing in the State of Georgia.
- The manufacturing industry has grown by more than 70,000 people over the past decade.
- Manufacturing employment is forecasted to grow by another 30,500 over the next ten years.
- Manufacturing employment makes up 8.3% of total employment in Georgia.
- The average manufacturing wage in Georgia is $68,975.
- There are more than 23,700 manufacturing employers in Georgia.
- Mohawk Industries in Calhoun pioneers sustainable practices by integrating renewable energy sources and recycling initiatives into its operations to reduce its carbon footprint.
- Duracell in LaGrange has committed to energy-efficient manufacturing processes, contributing to both cost savings and environmental preservation.
- Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia (KMMG) in West Point invests heavily in workforce development through training programs and partnerships with local educational institutions.
- Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation in Newnan provides state-of-the-art manufacturing jobs and engages in significant community development initiatives.
- Hitachi Astemo Americas in Monroe specializes in automotive components and is driving the future of electric and hybrid vehicle technologies.
- Erdrich USA, Inc. in Dublin has adapted to shifts in market demand by developing innovative automotive components that meet the evolving needs of the industry.
- Caterpillar Inc. in Griffin remains a steady force, actively participating in local community initiatives while maintaining a commitment to sustainable manufacturing practices.
- Check-Mate Industries in Thomasville is committed to providing quality jobs and products while focused on benefiting the community through continued involvement in local initiatives.
- National Manufacturing Day is celebrated annually on the first Friday in October to showcase modern manufacturing, inspire the future workforce, and dispel common misconceptions about the industry.
- Georgia is recognized as the No. 1 state for business and a leader in the development of manufacturing career pathways.
