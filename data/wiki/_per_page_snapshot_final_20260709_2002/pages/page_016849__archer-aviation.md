# Archer Aviation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-04-28

**Source:** [Archer Aviation’s Stock Tumbles: Time to Sell the eVTOL Stock or Stay the Course? - 24/7 Wall St.](https://247wallst.com/investing/2025/07/22/archer-aviations-stock-tumbles-time-to-sell-the-evtol-stock-or-stay-the-course/)  ·  `page_016849`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation is an electric vertical takeoff and landing (eVTOL) aircraft manufacturer.
- Stellantis serves as a major backer of Archer Aviation.
- Stellantis reported preliminary first-half 2025 earnings showing a $2.68 billion loss, raising fears the automaker might scale back its support for Archer to cut costs.
- A Delaware Chancery Court ruling allows a shareholder lawsuit related to Archer’s 2021 SPAC merger to proceed, alleging misrepresentation of share value and development progress.
- Archer secured its Part 135 Air Carrier Certificate in June 2024, marking a critical step toward commercial operations.
- Archer's Midnight eVTOL is a four-seat, zero-emission aircraft designed for short urban trips.
- Archer is on track to complete FAA Type Certification later this year (2025).
- Completion of FAA Type Certification would enable revenue-generating flights in the United Arab Emirates by late 2025 and in the U.S. by early 2026.
- Archer's Georgia manufacturing facility, backed by Stellantis, aims to produce two aircraft per month by the end of 2025.
- Archer's Georgia manufacturing facility aims to scale production to 650 aircraft annually by 2030.
- United Airlines placed a $1 billion order for 200 Midnight eVTOL aircraft.
- Archer plans to develop air taxi networks in cities including New York and Los Angeles.
- Archer conducted recent test flights in Abu Dhabi.
- Archer plans to participate in the 2028 Olympics in Los Angeles.
- Archer raised $850 million in capital in June, bringing its total liquidity to approximately $2 billion.
- Archer's financial position supports a $6 billion order backlog and funds production scaling.
- Archer reported a first-quarter loss of $0.17 per share, beating analyst expectations of $0.28 per share.
- Archer outsources its manufacturing to Stellantis, contrasting with competitor Joby Aviation's vertically integrated model.
- The eVTOL market is projected to grow from $760 million in 2024 to $170.6 billion by 2034.
- Morgan Stanley projects the U.S. air taxi industry could be worth $1 trillion by 2040 and $9 trillion by 2050.
