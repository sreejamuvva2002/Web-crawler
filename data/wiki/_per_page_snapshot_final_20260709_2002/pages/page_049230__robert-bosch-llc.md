# Robert Bosch LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2010-12-03

**Source:** [Bosch Announces Expansion and Green Initiative in Dorchester County - Dorchester County Economic Development](https://www.dorchesterforbusiness.com/news/bosch-announces-expansion-and-green-initiative-in-dorchester-county/)  ·  `page_049230`

## Facts

- Over the next five years, Bosch will invest approximately $125 million in its manufacturing facility in Dorchester County.
- The investment will create approximately 300 new jobs.
- Job creation based on this investment is expected to begin in the second half of 2011.
- Bosch will add personnel and equipment to support production of its latest passenger car diesel and gasoline fuel injectors for the North American automotive market.
- Bosch will add personnel and equipment to support production of next generation anti-lock brake and electronic stability control systems for the North American automotive market.
- Dorchester County plans to provide reclaimed water for the company’s manufacturing processes as part of a green initiative.
- The reclaimed water initiative will conserve 80,000 gallons of wastewater per day.
- The sustainable water alternative will be developed with assistance from a grant from the U.S. Department of Commerce, Economic Development Administration (EDA) and matching funds from Dorchester County.
- The EDA grant for the reclaimed water initiative is currently pending final approval.
- The facility is located at 8101 Dorchester Road in Dorchester County, South Carolina.
- The facility currently employs more than 1,500 people.
- The facility produces a wide range of diesel, gasoline, and chassis products.
- The Dorchester County facility has been in operation since 1974 and was the company’s first operation in South Carolina.
- Gene Oswalt, vice president and commercial plant manager at Robert Bosch LLC, stated the investment is designed to strengthen the business at the plant and support Bosch’s global focus on environmental sustainability.
- South Carolina Governor Mark Sanford noted that the state has added 4,500 manufacturing jobs over the past seven months and highlighted Bosch’s confidence in South Carolina’s workforce.
- Joe Taylor, Secretary of Commerce for South Carolina, stated that the company’s decision to expand reiterates Bosch’s confidence in the state’s low taxes and regulatory environment.
- Jamie Feltner, Dorchester County Council Chairman, noted that Bosch has called Dorchester County home since 1974.
- Sean Bennett, chairman of the Charleston Regional Development Alliance, stated that the expansion proves the region's competitiveness for world-class manufacturers.
- The Bosch Group is a leading global supplier of technology and services with approximately 275,000 associates and $53 billion in sales for fiscal 2009.
- The Bosch Group spends more than $5 billion annually on research and development and applies for approximately 3,800 patents worldwide each year.
- In the U.S., Canada, and Mexico, the Bosch Group employs over 20,000 associates in more than 70 locations with reported sales of $7.3 billion in fiscal 2009.
- The announcement was made by Gov. Mark Sanford, the South Carolina Department of Commerce, Dorchester County, the Charleston Regional Development Alliance, and Robert Bosch LLC on December 3, 2010.
