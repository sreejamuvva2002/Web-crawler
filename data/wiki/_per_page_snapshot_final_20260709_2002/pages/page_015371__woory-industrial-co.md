# Woory Industrial Co.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** thermal_management  ·  **Published:** 2023-06-08

**Source:** [South Korean company investing $18M in Georgia plant to make heaters for electric vehicles | FOX 5 Atlanta](https://www.fox5atlanta.com/news/woory-industrial-plant-georgia-jobs-investment)  ·  `page_015371`

## Facts

- South Korean company investing $18 million in Georgia plant to build heaters for electric vehicles.
- Woory Industrial Co., based in Yongin, South Korea, will open the plant in Dublin, Georgia, in November.
- The company plans to hire 130 people for the new facility.
- The plant will manufacture electric heaters and control units for electric and internal combustion automakers.
- This marks Woory's first U.S. manufacturing location.
- The company currently operates existing factories in South Korea, China, Thailand, Vietnam, India, Slovakia, and Mexico.
- Woory has been developing electric heaters over the last 10 years with hopes of becoming a leader in the market.
- Electric heaters are needed in cold weather to keep passengers warm and to keep electric batteries warm for better performance.
- Woory is the ninth supplier to locate in the region following Hyundai's 2022 announcement to build a $5.5 billion plant to assemble electric vehicles and batteries in Ellabell, Georgia.
- The Hyundai site in Ellabell could grow to 8,100 employees and is slated to begin producing vehicles in 2025.
- Suppliers for the regional project have pledged to invest more than $2 billion and hire nearly 4,700 people.
- Woory could qualify for $2.6 million in state income tax credits, calculated at $4,000 per job over five years, contingent on workers earning at least $31,300 a year.
- The state will cover the cost to train workers for the facility.
- The company could qualify for additional incentives, including property tax breaks from Dublin and Laurens County.
