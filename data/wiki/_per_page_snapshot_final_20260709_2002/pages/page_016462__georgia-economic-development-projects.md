# Georgia Economic Development Projects

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2018-01-31

**Source:** [Economic Development Around the State - Georgia Trend Magazine](https://www.georgiatrend.com/2018/01/31/economic-development-around-the-state-18/)  ·  `page_016462`

## Facts

- German real estate company Newport U.S. RE is investing $500 million into redeveloping nearly 2 million square feet of downtown Atlanta.
- Newport U.S. RE has acquired 47 buildings between Atlanta City Hall and Mercedes-Benz Stadium.
- A new IKEA store is coming to Marietta’s Franklin Gateway Corridor.
- The 300,000-square-foot IKEA store will employ from 250 to 300 people.
- Wisconsin’s Modutek International LLC, manufacturer of modular buildings and power systems, is investing $6 million on a manufacturing and distribution facility in Soperton that will create 70 new jobs.
- New York-based risk management company Assurant Inc. is investing $2.2 million into a technical support center in Duluth, creating 335 jobs.
- CyrusOne is investing $200 million over the next seven years into a data center campus in Douglas County in which other businesses will locate.
- The CyrusOne facility is expected to generate an additional $600 million in investment and create 38 jobs.
- Graphic Packaging International is investing $136 million to update its mill in Macon-Bibb County.
- The Graphic Packaging International facility makes paperboard for food and beverage packaging and supports 460 manufacturing jobs.
- S&S Activewear is creating 300 jobs in McDonough over the next four years.
- The imprintable apparel wholesaler will add a 505,000-square-foot warehouse that will be operational by the second quarter of 2018.
- Automotive fiber-padding manufacturer Daesol Material Georgia LLC invested $35 million into a Harris County facility, creating 110 jobs.
