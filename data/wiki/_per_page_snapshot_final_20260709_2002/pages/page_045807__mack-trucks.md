# Mack Trucks

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Mack Debuts All-New Keystone Vocational Tractor, Unveils Reimagined Granite at ConExpo 2026 | Heavy Duty Trucking](https://www.truckinginfo.com/news/mack-debuts-all-new-keystone-vocational-tractor-unveils-reimagined-granite-at-conexpo-2026)  ·  `page_045807`

## Facts

- Mack has debuted an all-new Class 8 tractor and an updated Granite model ahead of ConExpo-Con/Agg 2026.
- The Mack Keystone is a purpose-built Class 8 tractor aimed squarely at construction, agriculture, and severe-duty on/off-road applications.
- The Keystone represents an all-new model line for Mack and signals the OEM’s push to unify its highway and vocational platforms under a common architecture that blends durability, advanced safety and driver-focused design.
- Alongside the Keystone, Mack introduced a fully redesigned Granite vocational truck, expanding the brand’s reach across the Class 8 work spectrum.
- The new truck is named after Pennsylvania, the historic home to Mack Trucks and the site of its Lehigh Valley Operations in Macungie, where all North American Class 8 models are built.
- Stephen Roy, president of Mack Trucks, stated the Mack Keystone represents a commitment to providing customers in agriculture, construction and vocational markets with a truck that’s as capable on rough terrain as it is on the highway.
- Roy said the Keystone builds on more than two decades of Pinnacle production but moves to Mack’s latest common platform shared with the Pioneer, Anthem and the newly redesigned Granite.
- Vocational tractor buyers will now get the same advanced safety suite, digital architecture and interior refinements found in Mack’s newest highway offerings without sacrificing heavy-duty capability.
- For the first time, Mack will offer this class of vocational tractor in select Latin American markets, expanding the Keystone beyond North America.
- The Keystone is engineered for operators hauling logs out of forests, livestock across rural highways, equipment to construction sites or commodities across mixed terrain.
- Key features include higher ground clearance and improved approach angle, robust suspension options including Mack’s MaxRide system, axle-forward and axle-back configurations, and rear axle offerings in 6x4, 8x4 and 8x6.
- Cab configurations include a day cab, 44-inch sleeper and 64-inch sleeper, each available in both axle positions.
- Fernando Couceiro, vice president of highway trucks at Mack, said the Keystone is designed for terrain versatility, thriving where roads are unpaved, uneven or nonexistent before transitioning seamlessly to highway driving.
- Powering the Keystone is Mack’s MP13 engine, rated up to 540 hp and 1,950 lb.-ft. of torque, paired with the mDRIVE HD automated manual transmission.
- Mack says the powertrain delivers up to 6% improved fuel efficiency compared to the Pinnacle.
- The Keystone comes standard with Mack’s integrated uptime tools, including a five-year subscription to Mack Connect telematics for remote diagnostics and proactive service planning.
- The cab features wrap-around controls, high-visibility sightlines, ergonomic grab handles, premium seating options, a flat-bottom steering wheel, electronic braking system, available digital mirror system, and integrated ELD plate and device management solutions.
- Jonathan Randall, president of Mack Trucks North America, stated customers wanted a truck that could handle the toughest jobs without compromising driver comfort or modern technology, which the Keystone delivers.
- The new Granite receives an all-new galvanized steel cab that is nine inches wider at the B-pillar, an updated MP13 engine rated up to 540 hp, faster-shifting mDRIVE transmission options and Mack’s proprietary MaxRide air load distributing suspension.
- The Granite offers the Mack Protect safety suite with 270-degree radar coverage, including both driver- and passenger-side radar systems, marking a first for the segment.
- The Granite also introduces an electronic park brake with rollaway protection and standard E911 auto-call capability triggered by airbag deployment or rollover detection.
- The Granite will be available in day cab and 44-inch sleeper configurations, with axle-forward and axle-back options.
- Production of the new Granite is scheduled to begin in January 2027 at Lehigh Valley Operations.
- The all-new Mack Keystone will be available for order through Mack dealers in late Q3 2026.
- Granite orders will open in the second half of the year.
