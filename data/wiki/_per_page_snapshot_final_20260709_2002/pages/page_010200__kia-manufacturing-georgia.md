# Kia Manufacturing Georgia

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-05-20

**Source:** [Kia car manufacturing plant in Georgia transforms the region](https://www.wtoc.com/2022/05/20/kia-plant-georgia-transforms-region/)  ·  `page_010200`

## Facts

- In 2009, Hyundai Motor Group opened its first Kia car manufacturing plant in the U.S. near a small town on the Georgia-Alabama border.
- The project promised thousands of jobs and regional transformation for an area historically predominantly textiles.
- Kia spokesperson Randy Jackson stated that the economic impact will change the face of the region and make it better for everyone, with more hotels, restaurants, and housing developments coming in five to ten years from now.
- Hyundai Motor Group built a plant 250 miles west of Savannah in West Point, Georgia.
- The deal became the largest in Georgia’s history.
- In 2005, Jane Fryer was then president of the LaGrange Chamber of Commerce and Economic Development Authority when she took the first call from the state about an undisclosed company interested in a 2,200-acre site in West Point, Georgia.
- The LaGrange Development Authority owned land in the area, but the company wanted land farther down where no industrial land was available at the time.
- The project turned into reality three years later in 2009 when Kia began production at its first U.S. car manufacturing plant.
- Fourteen years ago, the site was all farmland owned by 30 property owners who agreed to sell their generation-owned land for the plant.
- The facility has grown to a $1.8 billion investment off Interstate 85.
- The plant employs about 3,000 people.
- The plant produces about 340,000 cars each year.
- Rick Douglas, Senior Manager Public Relations, stated that the area unemployment rate dropped from 12 percent at the time to three percent now.
- The plant started manufacturing the Kia Sorento in November 2009.
- It now produces three more models including the flagship Telluride.
- Ninety percent of the cars made here go to dealerships in the U.S.
- Kia suppliers are spread along I-85 from Valley, Alabama all the way east to Greenville, Georgia.
- These suppliers have created another 14,000 jobs to support this particular operation.
- The plant has led to strong partnerships with area schools, technical colleges and universities to develop an automotive workforce.
- There has been a dramatic turn toward technology and smart manufacturing as a result of companies coming into the area.
- The project led to a melding of American and Korean culture in the region while producing the best quality vehicles built in North America.
