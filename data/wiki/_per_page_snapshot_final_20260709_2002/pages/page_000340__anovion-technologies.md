# Anovion Technologies

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2023-05-15

**Source:** [Electric battery component maker plans $800M Georgia factory, hiring hundreds | FOX 5 Atlanta](https://www.fox5atlanta.com/news/anovion-technologies-electric-battery-component-georgia-factory-hiring-hundreds)  ·  `page_000340`

## Facts

- Anovion Technologies, based in Chicago, announced plans to invest $800 million to build a factory in Bainbridge, Georgia.
- The company will hire 400 workers for the new facility.
- Anovion will manufacture synthetic graphite anode for lithium-ion batteries at the plant.
- Production of the synthetic graphite anode is scheduled to start in 2025.
- The factory supports Georgia's broader initiative to recruit electric vehicle and supplier manufacturers.
- Republican Governor Brian Kemp stated the announcement furthers his goal of making Georgia the nation's electric mobility capital.
- Most battery graphite is currently manufactured outside the United States.
- Georgia economic development officials report that more than 40 electric vehicle-related projects have been announced in Georgia since 2020, pledging $22 billion in investment and 28,000 jobs.
- Anovion was formed last year by combining the graphite businesses of Pyrotek and Amsted Graphite Materials, along with new investment from private equity firm Monomyth Group.
- The company operates existing facilities in Sanborn, New York, and Clarksburg, West Virginia.
- Anovion previously announced plans to build a graphite factory with a 35,000-ton yearly capacity near Muscle Shoals, Alabama.
- The company won a $117 million grant under the new federal infrastructure law last year to help finance the Alabama plant and improvements at its New York facility.
- CEO Eric Stopka cited Georgia's workforce, research universities, and growing solar energy production as key attractions for the project.
- Stopka stated, "We are looking forward to providing solutions that will further secure the country’s electrified future with the support of our partners in Georgia and the federal government."
- Anovion executives aim to reach 150,000 tons of production capacity in coming years, starting from near zero.
- Synthetic graphite is produced by heating carbon products like petroleum coke (an oil refining byproduct) to super-high temperatures for extended periods.
- The manufacturing process requires furnaces that consume huge amounts of electricity, making power costs a critical factor in site selection.
- Anovion may qualify for a Georgia state income tax credit allowing an annual deduction of $3,000 per job from state taxes for five years, contingent on workers earning at least $31,300 annually.
- The potential state income tax incentive could be worth up to $6 million.
- The company may also qualify for property tax breaks from the city of Bainbridge and Decatur County.
- This announcement marks the second major industrial project in two years for Bainbridge, located in southwest Georgia.
- In 2021, Danimer Scientific announced plans to build a $700 million factory in the area, also hiring 400 employees.
- Decatur County Commission Chairman Pete Stephens stated, "We have always known that in order to remain competitive and to grow, we needed to diversify our industry mix."
