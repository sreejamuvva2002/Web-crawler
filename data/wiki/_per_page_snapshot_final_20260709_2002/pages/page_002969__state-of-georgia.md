# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-08-29

**Source:** [Gov Kemp: Georgia Job Creation Remains Strong | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-releases/2024/gov-kemp-georgia-job-creation-remains-strong)  ·  `page_002969`

## Facts

- Between July 1, 2023, and June 30, 2024, GDEcD’s Global Commerce team supported 429 facility expansions and new locations resulting in more than $20.3 billion in investment and the commitment of 26,900 new, private sector jobs statewide.
- 83% of expansions and new locations are on their way to communities outside the 10-county Atlanta region, representing more than 18,300 new jobs and $12.5 billion in investment.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that the state is committed to continuing momentum by investing in key areas like education, workforce development, and infrastructure.
- Metro Atlanta attracted nearly $7.8 billion in investments as a logistics and business hub.
- Statewide, new locations account for $12.8 billion in investments and expansions are responsible for 9,800 new jobs.
- Lt. Governor Burt Jones emphasized the need to ensure businesses have the necessary workforce, citing the Technical College System of Georgia, University System of Georgia, College and Career Academies, and private-public partnerships as building the workforce pipeline.
- Speaker of the House Jon Burns highlighted historic investments in the education system, workforce development, and infrastructure as part of sustained economic development success.
- Governor and First Lady Kemp led economic development missions to Korea and Switzerland in FY2024 focusing on strengthening relationships with Hyundai Motor Group and its supplier network.
- Georgia maintained its position as a global gateway for business, landing more than 8,100 new jobs and $5.9 billion in investments from international companies expanding or locating in the U.S. market this past fiscal year.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson noted that Georgia's strong partnerships and diverse industry landscape are major advantages for businesses.
- The State of Georgia celebrated more than 50 years of continuous representation in Europe and Japan, which remain among the top sources of foreign direct investment.
- The coming fiscal year will mark 40 years of continuous representation in Korea, a partnership that earned GDEcD the James A. Van Fleet Award from the Korea Society.
- All markets where Georgia maintains representation in business and trade accounted for 29% of total new jobs and investments in FY2024.
- Manufacturing, including the aerospace, automotive, and e-mobility sectors, accounted for over 50% of expansions or new locations in FY2024.
- Kia Georgia added the EV9 to its lineup in Troup County.
- Toyota Industries Corporation established a campus in Jackson County.
- StandardAero operates in Richmond County.
- International life sciences company Gerresheimer announced its most recent expansion in Fayette County.
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America suppliers announced new locations in Bryan, Candler, Chatham, Coweta, Effingham, Harris, and Laurens County.
- SOLARCYCLE, an innovator in solar panel recycling, announced plans for a new location in Polk County to support solar panel manufacturer Qcells’ operations.
- Investments in food processing and agribusiness totaled more than $1.3 billion in FY2024.
- Japanese probiotic beverage company Yakult located in Bartow county.
- Korean bakery CJ Foodville located in Hall county.
- Walmart will be sourcing milk from local dairy farmers at a future dairy processing facility in Lowndes County.
- Technology and corporate hubs are creating at least 4,400 new jobs as a result of wins in FY2024.
- Cargill Corporation is establishing a new office hub in the City of Atlanta at the intersection of multiple key industries for the state.
- U.S. Soccer’s future headquarters and national training center will be located in Fayette County.
- Georgia earned the rank of No. 1 state for business from Area Development magazine for 10 consecutive years.
- Georgia earned the No. 1 spot in Site Selection magazine’s annual Prosperity Cup rankings for successful investment recruitment in 2023.
- Georgia was awarded a Gold Shovel by Area Development for achieving significant success in attracting new business investment and job creation in 2023.
- Business Facilities magazine ranked Georgia as the No. 1 state for EV investments as well as a top 10 state for food processing, cybersecurity, aerospace and defense, and AI growth hubs.
