# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor opens 3rd U.S. production plant in Georgia | Yonhap News Agency](https://en.yna.co.kr/view/AEN20250327003100320)  ·  `page_001022`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group celebrated the opening of its new electric vehicle (EV) factory in Georgia on Wednesday (U.S. time).
- HMGMA will serve as a strategic production base to drive the company's global growth and play a key role in realizing the future of mobility.
- The facility will act as a hub to strengthen economic cooperation between Korea and the United States.
- Key company officials at the opening ceremony included Hyundai Motor Group Executive Chairman Euisun Chung and Hyundai Motor Co. Chief Executive Officer (CEO) Jose Munoz.
- Local government officials Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp and Rep. Buddy Carter attended the opening ceremony.
- Executive Chairman Euisun Chung stated, "Standing here today, I can say I have never been more confident about building the future of mobility with America, in America," and added that Hyundai has come to Georgia "to stay, to invest and to grow."
- HMGMA is located on an 11.83 million-square-meter site in Ellabell, Georgia.
- The plant has an annual capacity of 300,000 cars, with plans to increase production to 500,000 units per year.
- HMGMA is Hyundai Motor Group's third production facility in the U.S., following Hyundai Motor's Alabama plant and Kia Corp.'s Georgia factory.
- With the completion of HMGMA, Hyundai Motor Group now has a total production capacity of 1 million cars in the U.S. per year.
- Last year, Hyundai and Kia sold a combined 1.7 million vehicles in the U.S.
- Before the official opening, the plant had been in partial operation since late last year.
- During partial operation, the plant produced two EV models: the Ioniq 5 and Ioniq 9.
- Some Kia models will be produced at HMGMA next year.
- HMGMA is designed as a software-driven factory (SDF) that utilizes data from the entire production process.
- The facility incorporates the latest automation and artificial intelligence (AI) technologies.
- AI analyzes production big data to detect abnormalities in advance at the plant.
- Advanced robots handle high-weight, high-risk processes and inspections at HMGMA.
- The opening of HMGMA came a few days after Chung announced a US$21 billion investment plan in the United States over the next three years at the White House with President Donald Trump.
- The investment plan also includes a $5.8 billion steel mill in Louisiana to produce auto parts for Hyundai Motor Group's assembly plants in Georgia and Alabama.
- President Trump announced a 25 percent tariff on all imported cars starting next Wednesday.
- The U.S. government is set to impose country-by-country reciprocal tariffs on the same day.
- Chung called for concerted efforts by the South Korean government and carmakers to seek favorable deals in upcoming tariff negotiations with Washington.
- Chung told reporters, "After the announcement, the government will lead tariff negotiations, and companies also have to be engaged. That's the beginning," and added, "I know our investment plan can hardly affect Washington's tariff policies. We are just a company. It's a country-to-country issue."
