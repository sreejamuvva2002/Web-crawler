# Electric Mobility and Innovation Alliance

**Type:** Government Program  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[government-support|Government Support]]  ·  **Location:** Atlanta, GA, Georgia, United States

## Overview

A statewide initiative launched by Governor Brian P. Kemp and driven by the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) to accelerate Georgia’s electric mobility industry and supply chain through a public-private partnership that includes government, industry, utilities, education, nonprofits and other stakeholders.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Statewide alliance focused on advancing the electric mobility industry and its supply chain in Georgia.

## Timeline

- **July 20, 2021** — Governor Brian P. Kemp announced the Electric Mobility and Innovation Alliance statewide initiative.
- **2018** — Blue Bird introduced its all-electric buses in Georgia.
- **2021-07-20** — Governor Brian P. Kemp announced the statewide Electric Mobility and Innovation Alliance initiative.
- **2018** — Blue Bird introduced its all-electric school buses.
- **July 2021** — Governor Kemp announced the formation of the Electric Mobility and Innovation Alliance.
- **August 6, 2021** — Official launch of EMIA at the Governor’s Mansion; chairs for five committees were announced.

## Key facts

- The EMIA brings together private and public sector partners to advance Georgia’s electric mobility industry and supply chain.
- GDEcD launched a dedicated website for the alliance at georgia.org/EMIA.
- Governor Kemp highlighted Georgia’s history of early investment in resources and infrastructure that create jobs of the future.
- EMIA will develop policy recommendations to enhance state attractiveness and foster innovation in drones, autonomous vehicles, connected vehicles, battery technology, and more.
- Five EMIA committees will set objectives for Supply Chain, Infrastructure, Workforce, Innovation, and Policy/Initiatives.
- The Carl Vinson Institute at the University of Georgia will host EMIA’s committee meetings and integrated research forums.
- Georgia hosts nearly $2.6 billion in SK Innovation EV battery manufacturing facilities in Jackson County.
- International partners include Dutch charging-systems leader Heliox, Turkish EV-parts maker TEKLAS, German-owned GEDIA, and Korean supplier EnChem.
- Blue Bird introduced all-electric school buses in 2018 and recently reported its 500th delivery or sale of electric buses.
- A report by the Alliance for Automotive Innovation projects automakers will invest $250 billion in electrification by 2023.
- IHS Markit forecasts 130 EV models will be available in the U.S. by 2026.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson said EMIA will give Georgia a comprehensive strategy for the wave of electrification across industry sectors.
- EMIA includes partners from government, industry, electric utilities, education, nonprofits, and other stakeholders.
- The alliance will develop policy recommendations across Supply Chain, Infrastructure, Workforce, Innovation, and Policy/Initiatives.
- Five EMIA committees will set objectives for each of those focus areas.
- Carl Vinson Institute at the University of Georgia will host EMIA’s committee meetings and integrated research forums.
- International investments include Heliox (Netherlands), TEKLAS (Turkey), GEDIA (Germany), and EnChem (Korea).
- Blue Bird reported its 500th delivery or sale of electric buses.
- Automakers are planning to invest $250 billion in electrification by 2023 (Alliance for Automotive Innovation report).
- IHS Markit predicts 130 EV models will be available in the U.S. by 2026.
- GDEcD launched new website assets at georgia.org/mobility to support the initiative.
- EMIA will develop a multi-step approach that sets state objectives for Supply Chain, Infrastructure, Workforce, Innovation, and Policy/Initiatives.
- Five committee chairs were appointed: Stuart Countess (Supply Chain), Jannine Miller (Infrastructure), Randall "Randy" Hatcher (Workforce), State Rep. Bruce Williamson (Policy & Initiatives), Cynthia Curry (Innovation).
- The Carl Vinson Institute of Government at the University of Georgia will host EMIA’s future committee meetings and integrated research forums.
