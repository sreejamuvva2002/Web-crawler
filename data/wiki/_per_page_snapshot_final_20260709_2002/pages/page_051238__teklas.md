# TEKLAS

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2021-01-15

**Source:** [Electric Vehicle Manufacturer TEKLAS to Open North American Headquarters in Georgia - Expansion Solutions](https://www.expansionsolutionsmagazine.com/electric-vehicle-manufacturer-georgia/)  ·  `page_051238`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that TEKLAS will invest $6.5 million in opening their first North American facility and headquarters in Gordon County.
- The new facility will deliver 120 jobs to Calhoun, Georgia.
- TEKLAS is a Turkish advanced research and development, manufacturing company and supplier of electric vehicle (EV) parts.
- Founded in Turkey in 1971, TEKLAS designs, develops, and manufactures fluid circulation systems for cooling and heating applications.
- The company produces hoses and pipes from rubber, plastic, and metal for the global automotive industry.
- TEKLAS uses highly innovative and automated manufacturing processes using advanced water molding technology.
- TEKLAS USA will include assembly lines to serve the most prominent original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) in the industry.
- Operations at the new Georgia facility are scheduled to start in the spring of 2021.
- TEKLAS is headquartered in Turkey and maintains locations in Bulgaria, Serbia, Mexico, and China, along with sales offices in Germany and France.
- Regional Director Arcan Ergur stated that the Calhoun location will help the company be closer to its main customers.
- The 200,000-square-foot facility and headquarters will be located at 320 South Industrial Boulevard in Calhoun, Georgia.
- TEKLAS will hire for positions at a variety of skill levels, including advanced manufacturing, management, and supervision.
- Larry Roye, chairman of the Development Authority of Gordon County, applauded the efforts of state and local partners who helped encourage the company to come to this decision.
- Kathy Johnson, president and CEO of the Gordon County Chamber of Commerce, commended TEKLAS for its research and advancements in electric vehicle manufacturing and for its contribution to the expansion of the automotive sector of the local manufacturing base.
- GDEcD Project Manager Fernanda Kirchner represented the Global Commerce division on this competitive project.
- The project was executed in partnership with the Economic Development Authority of Gordon County and Georgia Power.
- The Center of Innovation for Logistics, a division of GDEcD, provided freight analysis to support the project and company planning.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that the State of Georgia is focused on attracting and growing the jobs of the future and welcomed TEKLAS to the diverse family of EV suppliers in Georgia.
- TEKLAS serves major car original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) with more than 45 years of experience in design, development, and production capabilities.
- The company provides innovative solutions for thermoplastics, rubber, and metal products to its customers.
