# SK Battery America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2020-08-19

**Source:** [How will cheating allegations affect massive EV battery plant in Jackson County? | FOX 5 Atlanta](https://www.fox5atlanta.com/news/how-will-cheating-allegations-affect-massive-ev-battery-plant-in-jackson-county)  ·  `page_049972`

## Facts

- Political leaders from Georgia are awaiting a decision in Washington this fall regarding the massive EV battery plant under construction in Jackson County and whether SK Innovation will be punished over cheating allegations.
- In March 2019, SK Innovation broke ground on a $2.6 billion EV battery plant in Jackson County, Georgia.
- SK Battery America promises to hire 2,600 people to manufacture batteries for the electric vehicle market at the Jackson County facility.
- The factory is planned to open in 2022.
- State and local governments provided $300 million in tax breaks and donated the land for the factory site.
- Five weeks after the groundbreaking, rival company LG Chem accused SK Innovation of stealing trade secrets and attempting to cover it up, with allegations tied directly to the Jackson County plant.
- Congressman Doug Collins attended the groundbreaking to welcome SK Battery America but stated he had no idea at the time that the company was suspected of stealing trade secrets from a competitor.
- LG Chem filed lawsuits against SK Innovation in federal court and with the International Trade Commission (ITC), prompting concern among Georgia elected leaders.
- The ITC complaint alleges that SK Innovation's alleged cheating intensified as it planned to enter the U.S. market and build the Jackson County factory.
- Prior to the allegations, SK Innovation had already signed a deal to provide batteries to Volkswagen's factory in Chattanooga, Tennessee.
- LG Chem accused SK Innovation of interviewing LG employees for jobs, extracting proprietary information, and deliberately deleting company emails to conceal the misconduct.
- ITC investigators recovered numerous deleted emails containing directives such as “Please delete everything in the emails that discuss L-Company,” “You know why,” “Delete every material related to the rival company…,” and “Delete this email after completing this directive.”
- An ITC law judge ruled against SK Innovation without holding a hearing, citing the blatant nature of the cover-up.
- Congressman Doug Collins stated, “They need to be open and honest with their case and make sure they’re not doing something they shouldn’t be doing,” while acknowledging he was unaware of the trade secret theft complaints during the groundbreaking.
- The ITC is scheduled to decide SK Innovation's fate by October, with a worst-case scenario being an exclusion order that would forbid the company from operating in the United States.
- Local union leaders are meeting to determine how to secure more construction jobs for Americans at the SK Battery Georgia plant.
- State and local leaders find themselves in an uncomfortable position, writing letters of support to the ITC for SK Innovation while attempting to avoid condoning its actions, with some letters citing the local construction jobs already generated by the project.
- Union leader David Cagle dismissed claims about job creation as “a joke” and convinced Congressman Collins to ask Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) to review who is actually being hired for construction work.
- Cagle complained that American workers are being passed over for standard construction jobs in favor of Korean workers who may not be legally in the country.
- A portion of a letter from the Jackson County Board of Commissioners to the ITC urges that SK Battery Georgia be allowed to remain open.
- In May, U.S. Customs intercepted 33 Koreans at Hartsfield-Jackson International Airport carrying “fraudulent employment letters” intended to secure jobs building the battery plant.
- David Cagle remarked, “My personal opinion is there’s a lot of politicians that’s going to have egg on their face,” regarding the situation.
- Business analysts in Korea predict LG Chem and SK Innovation will ultimately reach a financial settlement, allowing the Jackson County plant to open on schedule in 2022.
- SK Battery America stated there is no change to the 2022 opening timetable and told FOX 5 I-Team that it “remains confident in the merits of our case.”
- In a statement to FOX 5 I-Team, the company said, “SK Innovation will vigorously defend ourselves in this matter to ensure a robust, innovative EV battery industry in the U.S.,” with the statement notably omitting any mention of the deleted emails.
