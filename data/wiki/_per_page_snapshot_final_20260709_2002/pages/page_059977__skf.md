# SKF

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-04-09

**Source:** [SKF relocates automotive operations from Monterrey to Puebla and La Silla](https://www.promexicoindustry.com/en/article/skf-relocates-automotive-operations-from-monterrey-to-puebla-and-la-silla)  ·  `page_059977`

## Facts

- SKF announced a reconfiguration of its operations in Mexico as part of the separation of its automotive business.
- The company aims to strengthen efficiency and competitiveness across the region.
- SKF will shift production from Monterrey to facilities in Puebla and La Silla, consolidating capabilities in strategic locations.
- The Monterrey plant operated as a shared facility between SKF’s Industrial and Automotive divisions.
- The Monterrey plant was originally designed to support projected growth in electric vehicles across the Americas.
- Slower-than-expected momentum in the EV segment, combined with the business separation, prompted a reassessment of installed capacity.
- Under the new configuration, SKF will optimize its manufacturing footprint and concentrate operations to improve overall operational performance.
- The company will retain the flexibility to respond to a potential rebound in automotive electrification.
- The adjustment includes the elimination of approximately 390 positions in Monterrey.
- The adjustment includes the creation of around 100 new jobs in Puebla and La Silla.
- These locations will centralize operations, reinforcing their role within SKF’s regional manufacturing strategy.
- This decision is part of a broader consolidation process aimed at aligning production with current market conditions.
- The move strengthens SKF’s presence in Mexico as a key node within its manufacturing network in the Americas.
- The restructuring is expected to cost approximately 500 million Swedish kronor.
- The restructuring costs will be recorded in the second quarter of 2026.
- The restructuring costs have already been incorporated into the company’s annual financial guidance.
- Mexico remains a strategic destination for advanced manufacturing, supported by infrastructure and talent that enable companies to reorganize and sustain competitiveness.
