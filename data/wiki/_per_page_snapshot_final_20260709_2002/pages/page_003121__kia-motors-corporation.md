# Kia Motors Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Kia’s EV Revolution: From Georgia to the Pacific Northwest | Dick Hannah Kia](https://www.dickhannahkia.com/kias-ev-revolution-from-georgia-to-the-pacific-northwest/)  ·  `page_003121`

## Facts

- Kia recently invested over $200 million to expand its West Point, Georgia plant to build electric vehicles like the new EV9 SUV.
- The Kia EV9 began rolling off the Georgia assembly line in 2024.
- The EV9 is the first-ever Kia EV assembled in North America.
- Kia announced that its award-winning EV6 crossover will also be built in West Point starting in early 2025.
- The EV6 production follows a $217 million plant expansion that created around 200 new jobs.
- These moves are part of a broader push to localize EV production to qualify for federal EV tax credits.
- Kia’s Georgia plant, officially named Kia Georgia, Inc., has undergone a major transformation to accommodate EV production alongside traditional models.
- The factory already produces the Telluride, Sorento, and Sportage SUVs before adding EV assembly.
- Kia added approximately 200 employees during the expansion.
- Facilities were upgraded so that EVs and gasoline models can be built on the same line.
- Total employment at the plant was brought to over 3,200 workers.
- The first Georgia-built EV9 rolled out just months after the expansion.
- Governor Brian Kemp noted that over $22.7 billion has been invested in Georgia’s EV supply chain since 2020.
- This investment wave has created more than 28,000 jobs in Georgia’s EV supply chain.
- As of 2025, Kia has both the EV6 and EV9 in full production in Georgia.
- Hyundai Motor Group is opening a massive new “Metaplant” and battery facilities in Georgia.
- The Hyundai Metaplant and battery facilities will supply local battery packs to boost output.
- Kia joined other automakers in July 2023 to announce a joint venture that will build thousands of high-speed chargers across North America.
- Under its “Plan S” electrification strategy, Kia plans to launch 14-15 new EV models globally by 2027.
- The current Kia EV6 and EV9 use an 800-volt electrical architecture that allows ultra-fast charging and improved efficiency.
- The EV9 is the first Kia to use a fourth-generation battery technology that boosts energy density for more range in the same space.
- Every Kia EV battery and powertrain comes with a 10-year/100,000-mile limited warranty.
- The bumper-to-bumper warranty is 5 years/60,000 miles.
- Kia earned the title of top mass-market brand in J.D. Power’s 2023 Vehicle Dependability Study.
- The 2023 Kia EV6 earned the IIHS Top Safety Pick+ rating for its safety and build quality.
- Kia partnered with Electrify America to provide EV6 and Niro EV owners with free fast-charging sessions totaling up to 500 kWh in the first three years.
- Starting with new models in 2024, Kia EVs will come with a NACS charging port, giving access to 12,000+ Tesla Supercharger stalls in the U.S. and Canada.
- Kia’s EVs feature Vehicle-to-Load (V2L) capability, which turns the car into a mobile power source.
- The EV9 is designed to eventually offer Level 3 autonomous driving capabilities using lidar-based sensors for hands-free driving in certain conditions.
