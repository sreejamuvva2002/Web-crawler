# VinFast

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-07-28

**Source:** [VINFAST BREAKS GROUND AT NORTH CAROLINA ELECTRIC VEHICLE FACILITY | VinFast](https://vinfastauto.us/newsroom/press-release/vinfast-breaks-ground-at-north-carolina-electric-vehicle-facility)  ·  `page_000501`

## Facts

- On July 28, 2023, VinFast officially broke ground on its electric vehicle manufacturing site in Raleigh, North Carolina.
- The manufacturing site is located within the Triangle Innovation Point in Chatham County, North Carolina.
- This project marks the first electric vehicle manufacturing facility in North Carolina and the largest economic development initiative in the state's history.
- The groundbreaking ceremony was attended by North Carolina Governor Roy Cooper, Vietnamese Ambassador to the US Nguyen Quoc Dzung, representatives from state and federal governments, and VinFast’s partners, customers, industry experts, and journalists.
- Phase 1 of the plant involves a total investment of up to $2 billion USD.
- The facility spans an area of approximately 1,800 acres.
- The plant is divided into five main production areas: a body shop, general assembly, press shop, paint shop, and an energy center.
- Other functional facilities will also be located on the factory premises.
- In its initial phase, the factory will focus on building VinFast’s VF 7, VF 8, and VF 9 electric vehicles.
- The expected production capacity for the facility is 150,000 vehicles per year.
- The supplier base for components and materials will be primarily concentrated in the US, Vietnam, and a few other countries.
- Future expansion and updates for the factory will be identified in the next phase.
- VinFast has appointed Clayco as the general contractor and construction management company to lead the project.
- VinFast will begin the bidding process soon to select subcontractors for the construction project.
- North Carolina Governor Roy Cooper stated that North Carolina’s skilled and diverse workforce is the greatest strength and that the state has the largest manufacturing workforce in the Southeast.
- Governor Roy Cooper noted that the VinFast factory will create thousands of good-paying jobs in the state, along with a healthier environment as more electric vehicles take to the road.
- Madam Le Thi Thu Thuy, CEO of VinFast, stated that the event marks a new milestone affirming VinFast’s commitment to the North American market.
- Madam Le Thi Thu Thuy added that the project will create thousands of jobs, contribute to North Carolina’s economic development, bring additional economic benefits to consumers, and help develop the North Carolina community during production and business operations.
- The VinFast electric vehicle manufacturing plant is expected to commence production in 2025.
- The facility will create an ecosystem of suppliers and thousands of jobs.
- This manufacturing complex is VinFast’s first electric vehicle factory outside of Vietnam.
- The project plays an important role as VinFast strives to reach world-class levels of sustainable mobility on a global scale.
- VinFast is a member of Vingroup, established in 2017, and envisions driving the global smart electric vehicle revolution.
- VinFast owns a state-of-the-art automotive manufacturing complex with up to 90% automation in Hai Phong, Vietnam.
