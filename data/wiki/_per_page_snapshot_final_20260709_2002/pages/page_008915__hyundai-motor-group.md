## Facts

- Kia’s parent firm is planning a second automobile factory in Georgia.

- Details of the project, such as projected costs, were not immediately known at the time of reporting.

- The Atlanta Journal-Constitution (AJC) first reported details about the location and number of jobs for the new plant.
- Kia Motors is investing $6.2 billion in its EV business through 2030.
- A 2022 rift occurred between Kia and the Georgia state government over the capacity of Kia's first Georgia plant.
- The company requested an additional $65 million in incentives for its Georgia plant during the rift.
- Kia's new second plant will be located on 2,200 acres near Ellabell, off I-16 in Bryan County.
- The site for the new plant was previously used as a military base.
- Kia Motors Georgia VE project is projected to create 8,500 jobs and is located in Bryan County.
- Hyundai Motor Group's South Georgia project is projected to create 7,500 jobs and is located near Savannah.
- Hyundai Motor Group's South Georgia project has an investment of $5.1 billion.
- KS Battery America project is projected to create 3,000 jobs and is located in Jackson County.
