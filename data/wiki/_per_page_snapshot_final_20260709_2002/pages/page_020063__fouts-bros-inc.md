## Facts

- Fouts Bros Inc. is a family-owned American manufacturer of commercial automotive equipment, including a wide range of trucks for various public and private sectors.
- The company was founded in 1952 by brothers CJ and Jerry Fouts, who initially purchased a Sinclair service station in Smyrna, Georgia.
- Over the years, the company expanded its business interests into auto and truck dealerships, commercial body manufacturing, and real estate.
- The company entered the fire apparatus market in 1989 with the introduction of a 3000-gallon tanker.
- The Fouts Fire division produces a standardized line of fire tankers, pumpers, light rescues, and brush trucks.
- The company's business model focuses on streamlining production and costs by building a limited, standardized line of apparatus to keep stock units readily available for fast delivery.
- A typical lead time from purchase order to delivery is four to six months.
- The company is now in its third generation of family management.
- The company is led by President and CEO Scott Edens, grandson of co-founder C.J. Fouts.
- Heather Edens serves as the Executive Vice President.
- In 2019, Fouts Bros moved its headquarters and primary production from Smyrna to a 700,000-square-foot facility in Milledgeville, Georgia.
- The company operates a second factory in La Grange, Texas.
- This move allowed for a significant expansion of their manufacturing capabilities.
- The Fouts Bros Inc. family of brands includes Fouts Fire, Fouts Commercial Vehicles Inc., Smyrna Truck, Fouts Defense, Big Deal Parts, and Electric Commercial Vehicles Inc.
- The Fouts Defense division is led by industry veteran George Bergamini.
- The Fouts Defense division specializes in equipping U.S. military and government agencies with commercial and fire apparatus.
- The company's product lineup includes tankers of various sizes (2000, 3000, and 4000 gallons).
- The product lineup includes commercial pumpers like the FB-94.
- The product lineup includes the versatile Fouts FOUR (First Out Utility Rescue) vehicle.
