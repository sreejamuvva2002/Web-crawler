# Southern Business and Development April 2023 News Roundup

**Type:** event  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [April 2023 News | Southern Business and Development](https://sb-d.com/news/article/937)  ·  `page_059662`

## Facts

- German automaker Audi is considering building a U.S. factory to produce electric vehicles, following several automakers and battery manufacturers announcing plans over the past three years to spend billions on new facilities in the Southern Automotive Corridor.
- Tennessee Governor Bill Lee and Ford Motor Company marked significant progress on construction at the historic BlueOval City complex in West Tennessee during the spring quarter.
- The BlueOval City project is the single largest investment in Tennessee history, involving a $5.6 billion commitment from Ford Motor Company and SK On announced in 2021 to build a 3,600-acre mega-campus at the Megasite of West Tennessee.
- Production of Ford’s second generation electric trucks at BlueOval City is scheduled to begin in 2025.
- The BlueOval City project is expected to create 30,000 jobs in West Tennessee anchored by Memphis, including auto parts suppliers and other support projects, while construction is expected to create 6,000 jobs.
- A study conducted by the Kentucky League of Cities and Murray State University projects the BlueOval SK Battery Park will have a yearly value-added economic impact of nearly $800 million on local governments in Hardin County and the surrounding region once at full employment in 2025.
- Massachusetts-based Ascend Elements opened a $50 million lithium-ion battery recycling facility in Covington, Georgia, located near Rivian’s planned $5 billion electric vehicle plant in Social Circle, Ga.
- Ascend Elements currently employs 100 workers at its Covington facility and plans to increase its headcount to 185 by 2024, calling the project the largest of its kind in North America.
- Electric vehicle plants like Rivian and Hyundai in Georgia make up seven of the 10 largest economic development projects as of April, with the Hyundai project worth billions being one of the largest announced in the South.
- Microvast Advanced Membrane is investing $504 million in Hopkinsville, Kentucky, to build the world’s first mass production facility for its polyaramid separator technology, which will be integrated into EV battery designs including Microvast’s cells and those of third-party manufacturers, creating 562 full-time jobs.
- Toyota Boshoku America Inc. will invest more than $225 million to construct a new 365,400-square-foot facility on 49 acres in Christian County, Kentucky, creating 157 quality jobs and establishing its first globally recognized “Smart Plant” utilizing advanced robotics and automation.
- EnerVenue will establish a new manufacturing facility in Shelby County, Kentucky, with a $264 million Phase 1 investment for energy storage products and systems, creating 450 full-time jobs with potential for further investment.
- Charlotte-based Albemarle is investing at least $1.3 billion and creating more than 300 new jobs to construct a nearly 800-acre “Mega-Flex” lithium hydroxide processing facility near Richburg in Chester County, South Carolina.
- The Albemarle facility will process diverse lithium feedstock, including lithium from recycled batteries, to support the surging demand for electric vehicles and energy storage applications.
- The Albemarle facility is expected to annually produce approximately 50,000 metric tons of battery-grade lithium hydroxide from multiple sources, with the potential to reach up to 100,000 metric tons.
- Cirba Solutions announced plans in the spring to build a state-of-the-art integrated lithium-ion battery materials campus in Richland County, South Carolina, encompassing an initial investment of over $300 million and creating more than 300 new jobs.
- The Cirba Solutions campus will feature an approximately 400,000-square-foot facility focusing on processing end-of-life hybrid and EV batteries and gigafactory scrap to extract critical materials such as nickel, cobalt, and lithium.
- Microvast is investing $150 million to expand its Clarksville, Tennessee facility, doubling the plant’s capacity, with commitments for close to 290 jobs at the first factory and another 600 to 700 at a potential second factory nearby.
- Microvast reported 35 percent revenue growth from 2021 to 2022, with its backlog of orders more than tripling to $410.5 million.
- Korea-based PHA announced plans in March to build a new plant near the Hyundai facility in Chatham County, Georgia, involving a $67 million investment that will generate more than 400 new jobs.
- Tin Thanh Group Americas is establishing its first U.S. tire manufacturing operations in Allendale County, South Carolina, with a $68 million investment creating 1,031 new jobs while utilizing recycled energy and closed industry-agriculture practices.
- German auto parts manufacturer STS Group AG will establish its North American headquarters in Salem, Virginia, moving into an existing facility near Roanoke with a $39 million investment that will create 120 jobs.
- The Tennessee Department of Economic and Community Development reported in the spring quarter that suppliers for Ford’s electric vehicle facility in Stanton, Tennessee (located just outside Memphis) are considering sites across the state for the 11-million-square-foot plant shared by Ford and battery maker SK.
- Alabama’s four major assembly plants continue to grow, with Mazda Toyota adding jobs in Huntsville, Hyundai (Montgomery) and Mercedes-Benz (Tuscaloosa County) expanding their electric vehicle lineups, and Honda planning updates on several models at its Lincoln plant.
- Nokian Tyres is expanding its workforce in Dayton, Tennessee, generating 125 new jobs.
- Korean automaker Hyundai rolled out its first electric vehicle, the Genesis Electrified GV70 SUV, at its large plant in Montgomery, Alabama, ten months after announcing plans to build the Electrified GV70 and a hybrid Santa Fe there.
- Hyundai invested $300 million and added 200 jobs to install an EV line at its Montgomery plant.
- Georgia announced that Sewon America will invest more than $300 million into a new manufacturing facility in Rincon in Effingham County, generating more than 700 jobs as a supplier for Hyundai’s massive Georgia complex.
- South Carolina remains the nation’s top exporter of completed passenger motor vehicles and tires, with 2022 export sales totaling $31.5 billion (up 6 percent over 2021) according to Gov. Henry McMaster, SC Commerce, and SC Ports.
- Tier 1 supplier Samkee Corp., which supplies Hyundai’s plant in Montgomery, will open a $128 million factory in Tuskegee, Alabama, creating 170 new jobs as its first U.S. operation.
- Ford Motor Co. announced it will build a $3.5 billion electric vehicle battery plant in Michigan housing about 2,500 workers, after Virginia dropped out of the running for the deal in February.
- Pallidus, a silicon carbide (SiC) wafer semiconductor manufacturer, announced plans to relocate its corporate headquarters and manufacturing operations to York County, South Carolina, with a $443 million investment creating 405 jobs to produce wafers used in semiconductors via proprietary M-SiC technology.
- Global auto parts supplier Seoyon E-HWA will invest almost $76 million in a new manufacturing facility in Chatham County, Georgia, creating 740 new direct and indirect jobs for interior and exterior components as another Hyundai supplier.
