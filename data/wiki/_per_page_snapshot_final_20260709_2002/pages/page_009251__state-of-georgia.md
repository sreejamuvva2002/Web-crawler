# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2023-07-01

**Source:** [Georgia Job Creation & Momentum Remains Strong - Valdosta CEO](https://valdostaceo.com/features/2024/08/georgia-job-creation-momentum-remains-strong/)  ·  `page_009251`

## Facts

- Governor Brian Kemp, in conjunction with the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD), announced sustained momentum for business recruitment and expansions in Fiscal Year 2024.
- Between July 1, 2023, and June 30, 2024, GDEcD’s Global Commerce team supported 429 facility expansions and new locations resulting in more than $20.3 billion in investment and the commitment of 26,900 new private sector jobs statewide.
- 83% of expansions and new locations are directed to communities outside the 10-county Atlanta region, representing more than $12.5 billion in investment and over 18,300 new jobs.
- Metro Atlanta attracted nearly $7.8 billion in investments as a logistics and business hub during FY2024.
- Statewide, new locations accounted for $12.8 billion in investments while expansions were responsible for 9,800 new jobs.
- Lt. Governor Burt Jones highlighted the Technical College System of Georgia, University System of Georgia, College and Career Academies, and private-public partnerships as key builders of Georgia's workforce pipeline for expanding businesses.
- Governor and First Lady Kemp led economic development missions to Korea and Switzerland during FY2024, focusing on strengthening relationships with key investors like Hyundai Motor Group and its supplier network.
- Georgia landed more than 8,100 new jobs and $5.9 billion in investments from international companies expanding or locating in the U.S. market during the past fiscal year.
- Manufacturing, including the aerospace, automotive, and e-mobility sectors, accounted for over 50% of expansions or new locations in FY2024.
- Kia Georgia added the EV9 to its lineup at its facility in Troup County.
- Toyota Industries Corporation established a campus in Jackson County.
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America suppliers announced new locations in Bryan, Candler, Chatham, Coweta, Effingham, Harris, and Laurens County.
- SOLARCYCLE, an innovator in solar panel recycling, announced plans for a new location in Polk County to support solar panel manufacturer Qcells’ operations.
- Business Facilities magazine ranked Georgia as the No. 1 state for EV investments.
- GDEcD Commissioner Kristi Brigman noted that FY24 saw achievements across mobility and technology sectors, celebrating project milestones across the state.
