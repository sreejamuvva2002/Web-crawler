# Mercedes-Benz USA

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2015-01-08

**Source:** [16442557-3-things-the-mercedes-hq-move-tells-us-about-incentives - Smart Incentives](https://smartincentives.org/16442557-3-things-the-mercedes-hq-move-tells-us-about-incentives/)  ·  `page_046404`

## Facts

- Mercedes-Benz USA recently announced it would move its headquarters from Montvale, NJ to the Atlanta area.
- The relocation will bring at least 800 jobs to Georgia.
- Large incentive packages dominated the discussion for other recent automotive headquarters moves, including Subaru’s commitment to Camden, NJ and Toyota’s planned move to Plano, TX.
- Mercedes-Benz USA cited locating closer to the strong and growing customer base in the southeast as a key factor supporting its location choice.
- Additional factors cited by Mercedes-Benz USA included talent and quality of life, cost savings, and business efficiency.
- CEO Steve Cannon stated that incentives are just a small piece of the picture when making a 50-year decision, noting that "a pile of incentives in Year One, Two or Three over a 50-year decision doesn’t make a gigantic impact."
- The company's strategy emphasizes a long-term outlook, growth especially among younger, millennial buyers, emphasis on customer service and digital platforms, constant focus on efficiency, and a recent pattern of consolidating regional operations.
- Economic developers aware of the company’s non-cash, non-incentive factors would be better prepared to present their communities in a light more appealing than simply offering a “pile of incentives.”
- Many articles cited New Jersey’s tax climate and high costs as the reason behind Mercedes-Benz USA's move.
- Georgia has lower taxes than New Jersey, but the Tax Foundation ranks the state 32nd in the country for tax climate for business.
- Last summer, the New Jersey Assembly approved a bill related to manufacturer-dealership regulations that angered New Jersey-based car companies.
- Jaguar Land Rover stated that if the NJ bill was enacted, “we would be actively looking at whether there is a more business-friendly environment in a neighboring state.”
- The CEOs of other car companies based in New Jersey all signed a letter opposing the bill, which did not become law.
- Focusing economic development efforts exclusively on incentives and taxes will mean missed opportunities and lost business.
