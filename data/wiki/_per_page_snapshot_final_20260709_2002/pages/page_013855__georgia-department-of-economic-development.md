# Georgia Department of Economic Development

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2015-09-24

**Source:** [Gov. Deal Visits Germany During Turbulent Time for Volkswagen, Porsche - Global Atlanta](https://www.globalatlanta.com/gov-deal-visits-germany-during-turbulent-time-for-volkswagen-porsche/)  ·  `page_013855`

## Facts

- Gov. Nathan Deal and top leaders from the Georgia Department of Economic Development traveled to Germany to thank Porsche and Mercedes-Benz for recent Atlanta headquarters investments while meeting with other prospects.
- The visit occurred as Porsche’s parent company, Volkswagen AG, faced a scandal where U.S. investigators found the automaker installed software on diesel models to falsify emissions test results on up to 11 million vehicles worldwide.
- Volkswagen Group CEO Martin Winterkorn announced his resignation, apologizing for the error without admitting personal wrongdoing, as company shares tumbled.
- Volkswagen manufactures diesel-powered Passats in Chattanooga, Tenn., and German automakers supplying that factory have been among many recent investors in Georgia.
- Volkswagen halted sales of affected models but reportedly reassured Chattanooga leaders that the scandal would not diminish their plant’s importance to U.S. market expansion plans.
- Porsche AG CEO Matthias Müller, who visited Atlanta in May to open a new headquarters near Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport, is reportedly being tapped to become VW’s next chief executive.
- A Georgia Department of Economic Development spokeswoman stated it was unclear if the scandal's effects for Georgia were discussed during meetings between Porsche officials and Gov. Deal, Commissioner Chris Carr, and Deputy Commissioner Tom Croteau.
- Keith Barclift, project manager for the Northwest Georgia Joint Development Authority promoting four counties bordering Tennessee, noted that automotive is a small part of the region’s industrial mix and that the area has no German suppliers.
- Barclift stated that none of the regional suppliers are specifically trying to serve just VW, so they are not worried about layoffs or other impacts.
- Philipp Schulz, president of Austrian-based voestalpine Automotive Body Parts Inc., which makes structural steel components for cars, noted the company has a diversified customer base including other German automakers around the South.
- voestalpine launched its second phase of expansion at its $62 million Cartersville plant last year to serve buoyant regional demand.
- Schulz stated the company chose Georgia because it is centrally located between its customer base and that they supply all German transplants and others in the region.
- Georgia officials celebrated the state's business climate outside Porsche headquarters in Stuttgart to attract strong international investors.
- Officials marked Area Development magazine’s ranking of Georgia as the top state for business for the second straight year.
- The state is home to nearly 500 German companies employing 23,250 people.
- Mr. Deal’s office announced that Sandler AG, a textile manufacturer, will invest $30 million to build a plant in Perry, Ga., to supply non-wovens to the U.S. market and eventually hire 140 employees.
- Georgia’s exports to Germany totaled $1.33 billion in 2014, according to the department.
- Mr. Deal is slated to visit Munich during the business mission, where the department’s continental Europe trade and investment office is located.
- Georgia and the state of Bavaria are partners in the Regional Leaders Summit, a forum of states and provinces with members from around the world.
- The Pendleton Group is the presenting sponsor of Global Atlanta's Economic Development Channel.
