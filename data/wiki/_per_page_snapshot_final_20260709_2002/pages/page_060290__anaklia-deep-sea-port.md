# Anaklia Deep Sea Port

## Facts

- Georgian Economy Minister Mariam Kvrivishvili stated that the modernization of Georgia’s transport and logistics network aims to ensure uninterrupted transit of growing trade flows between Europe and Asia.
- Georgia has named the development of the Middle Corridor an important priority to improve regional connectivity, develop transport systems, and accelerate logistics processes.
- Major infrastructure projects being implemented include the active construction of the East-West Highway and the development of the Anaklia deep-sea port project.
- The Anaklia Deep Sea Port is considered one of Georgia’s most important strategic projects to strengthen the country's economy and reinforce its strategic position.
- Active construction is currently underway at the Anaklia Deep Sea Port, with progress open for public site visits.
- The Anaklia Deep Sea Port is expected to receive its first vessel in 2029.
- The Georgian Government decided to develop the Anaklia Deep-Sea Port under a landlord model, whereby the state will retain ownership of the core maritime and port infrastructure while overseeing its development and management.
- The Anaklia Deep Sea Port will remain under state ownership, with international companies involved only in managing terminal infrastructure.
- The development model ensures state leadership in strategic infrastructure while creating a competitive environment that accommodates the interests of multiple partner countries and international companies.
