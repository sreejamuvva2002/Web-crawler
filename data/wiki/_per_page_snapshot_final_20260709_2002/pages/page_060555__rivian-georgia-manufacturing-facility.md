## Facts

- The U.S. Department of Energy gave final approval Thursday to electric vehicle maker Rivian for a $6.6 billion loan to support its stalled Georgia factory.
- The loan provides financial backing to build the plant in southern Morgan and Walton counties, roughly an hour east of Atlanta.
- The project was first proposed in late 2021.
- The project was initially supposed to open in 2024.
- Delays mounted as Rivian faced supply chain issues and other challenges.
- Rivian also plans to expand its Georgia plant with a second line.
- The T1R, S1R, and R2 models are expected to start production in the coming years for export.
- The $5.1 billion project is key to Rivian's future.
