# FREYR Battery

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-11-09

**Source:** [FREYR Battery (NYSE:FREY) Q3 2023 Earnings Call Transcript - Insider Monkey](https://www.insidermonkey.com/blog/freyr-battery-nysefrey-q3-2023-earnings-call-transcript-1221078/)  ·  `page_055020`

## Facts

- FREYR Battery reported a net loss of $10 million or $0.07 per share for the quarter ended September 30, 2023.
- The company's net loss for the same period last year was $94 million.
- Net income from last year's period included a $70 million non-cash loss on warrant liability fair value adjustment due to changes in stock price.
- For Q3 of this year, FREYR recognized a $24 million non-cash gain on the warrant liability item.
- The company reported a net loss of $48 million or $0.34 per share for the nine months ended September 30, 2023.
- Net cash spent during the first nine months of 2023 was $235 million, including $169 million on capital expenditures.
- During Q3, FREYR spent $41 million on capital expenditures, with $32 million allocated to Giga Arctic and about $7.5 million to the customer qualification plan and test center.
- Capital expenditures were partly offset by a receipt of a $3.5 million grant in the United States.
- FREYR ended Q3 2023 with $328 million in cash, cash equivalents, and restricted cash, and holds no debt.
- The company is advancing its Giga America battery manufacturing project located in Georgia.
- Giga America will utilize a 24M semi-solid platform technology as one of two parallel development tracks.
- A second track for Giga America involves leveraging conventional battery technology to mitigate technology risk and potentially accelerate US market entry.
- The Georgia site is large enough to accommodate both a conventional and a 24M production facility with plenty of room to spare.
- FREYR terminated its previously discussed two-line fast track project (Phase 1A) for Giga America due to decreased value of the time advantage related to CQP delays.
- The company is now focusing on the larger Phase 1B project at the Georgia site, which aligns with its DOE financing plan.
- FREYR aims to achieve a potential Final Investment Decision (FID) for the larger 24M-based project, along with DOE and equity financing, sometime late in 2024.
- The company is progressing through the US Department of Energy Title 17 loan process for Giga America and is awaiting invitations to part two of the application.
- Section 45X of the Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) provides annual production tax credits that support the financing of Giga America.
- FREYR is staying vigilant for applicable federal grant opportunities in the US for its business.
- The company plans to redomicile from Luxembourg to the United States, specifically Delaware, to expand equity index inclusion potential and align with the country offering the highest bids for battery manufacturing at scale.
- A special shareholder meeting to approve the redomicile transaction is set for December 15, requiring a 50% vote of outstanding shares and a two-thirds majority of those voting in favor.
- FREYR expects to close the redomicile transaction by year-end 2023.
- The company initiated a cost rationalization program to reduce its total run rate cash spending by over 50% in 2024.
- This cost-cutting initiative extends the company's liquidity runway to two-plus years and into 2026.
- FREYR will not authorize any significant new capital expenditures in 2024 until new project-level financing is secured for Giga America.
- The company targets an annual cash burn rate in 2024 less than half of that in 2023, with a quarterly burn rate well under $30 million.
- FREYR will not raise common equity from shareholders in 2024 due to its liquidity position and lower burn rate.
- Project-level equity financing for Giga America remains available but is tied to getting the CQP up and running with testable batteries expected again in 2024.
- The company is pursuing conventional technology partnerships to complement its 24M semi-solid technology and unlock avenues to financing and commercial development.
- FREYR is leveraging its Energy Transition Acceleration Coalition (ETAC) to explore partnership-based upstream opportunities and address supply chain decarbonization.
- Jeremy Bezdek serves as Executive VP of Corporate Development and President of FREYR Battery US, leading the Giga America update.
- Birgir Steen serves as Chief Executive Officer of FREYR Battery.
- Oscar Brown serves as Chief Financial Officer of FREYR Battery.
- Jan Arve Haugan serves as Chief Operating Officer of FREYR Battery.
- Jeffrey Spittel serves as VP of Investor Relations for FREYR Battery.
- Dr. Dan Steingard, a FREYR Board member and co-director of the Columbia University Electric Chemical Energy Center, leads a newly formed technology advisory board to assist with scaling challenges at the Customer Qualification Plan (CQP).
- The company has implemented changes in project governance and heightened coordination with vendors and ecosystem partners at the CQP to address delays in achieving automated production of inspect cells.
- Commissioning of the casting unit cell assembly equipment at the CQP is proving more difficult and time-consuming than previously envisaged.
- The timeline to achieve automated production of inspect cells has pushed beyond the previous goal of the fourth quarter 2023.
- FREYR is not currently forecasting any capital expenditures for Giga Arctic in 2024, prioritizing liquidity for the CQP scale-up and Giga America development.
- The company recently announced it was awarded a €100 million grant for Giga Arctic by the European Innovation Fund (EUIF), though no cash has been received yet.
- FREYR will continue to work with Norwegian and European government stakeholders to establish framework conditions that place Giga Arctic on globally competitive economic terms.
- The company's strategic focus includes advancing the commissioning and 24M scale-up processes at the CQP while pursuing conventional technology partnerships and US redomicile.
