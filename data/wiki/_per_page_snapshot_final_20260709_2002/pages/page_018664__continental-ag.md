# Continental AG

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2024-03-07

**Source:** [Continental increases earnings in 2023 and targets further improvement this year | Automotive World](https://www.automotiveworld.com/news-releases/continental-increases-earnings-in-2023-and-targets-further-improvement-this-year/)  ·  `page_018664`

## Facts

- Continental achieved its financial targets in the past fiscal year, taking the DAX company a step closer to its mid-term targets.
- Geopolitical situation, additional inflation-related costs of around €1.4 billion, exchange-rate effects and high costs for special freight had a considerable impact on performance.
- Continental presented its strategy for increasing value creation and achieving its mid-term targets at its Capital Market Day in December 2023.
- For the current fiscal year, Continental is targeting consolidated sales of around €41.0 billion to €44.0 billion.
- Continental is targeting an adjusted EBIT margin of around 6.0 to 7.0 percent for the current fiscal year.
- Continental expects the margin to improve further in the Automotive group sector, thanks mainly to cost-cutting measures, price adjustments and efficiency improvements adopted last year.
- The goal is also to achieve above-market growth.
- Continental CEO Nikolai Setzer stated at the annual press conference in Hanover that the past fiscal year posed major challenges with geopolitical uncertainties, persistent strains on the supply of semiconductors, and inflation.
- Nikolai Setzer added that Continental achieved its goals for 2023 and made improvements toward achieving its mid-term targets despite challenging conditions.
- Nikolai Setzer thanked around 200,000 employees worldwide for making substantial progress.
- Nikolai Setzer stated that in 2024, the company will tackle challenges head on and pursue goals with determination.
- Adjusted operating result increased by 31.6 percent in 2023.
- In the past fiscal year, Continental achieved consolidated sales of €41.4 billion (2022: €39.4 billion, +5.1 percent).
- Its adjusted EBIT was €2.5 billion (2022: €1.9 billion, +31.6 percent), corresponding to an adjusted EBIT margin of 6.1 percent (2022: 4.9 percent).
- All three group sectors contributed to the improved adjusted EBIT margin.
- Net income increased to €1.2 billion in 2023 (2022: €67 million, +1,635 percent).
- Adjusted free cash flow was €1.3 billion (2022: €200 million, +547 percent).
- Continental CFO Katja Garcia Vila stated that an improvement in operating earnings and a reduction in inventories and receivables significantly increased adjusted free cash flow.
- Katja Garcia Vila noted that the company slightly exceeded its cash flow target, speaking to financial strength.
- Based on the updated distribution strategy of 20 to 40 percent of net income, the Continental Executive Board will propose a €0.70 increase in the dividend to €2.20 per share for the past fiscal year.
- The distribution to shareholders is at around 38 percent, at the upper end of the defined range, and would amount to around €440 million in total.
- In 2024, Continental expects the global production of passenger cars and light commercial vehicles to change by -1 to 1 percent.
- Last year, global vehicle production increased by almost 10 percent compared with 2022 to more than 90 million vehicles.
- For the global tire-replacement business, Continental expects sales volumes to develop by 0 to 3 percent.
- Higher costs for wages and salaries, expected to amount to around €500 million, will weigh heavily on the earnings position in fiscal 2024.
- Around half of the wage and salary cost increases are attributable to the Automotive group sector.
- Continental anticipates consolidated sales for 2024 of around €41.0 billion to €44.0 billion and an adjusted EBIT margin of around 6.0 to 7.0 percent.
- Continental expects the Automotive group sector to generate sales of around €20.0 billion to €22.0 billion and an adjusted EBIT margin of around 3.0 to 4.0 percent.
- For the Tires group sector, Continental forecasts sales of around €14.0 billion to €15.0 billion and an adjusted EBIT margin of around 13.0 to 14.0 percent.
- Continental anticipates sales in the ContiTech group sector of around €6.6 billion to €7.0 billion and an adjusted EBIT margin of around 6.5 to 7.5 percent.
- Capital expenditure before financial investments is forecast to be around 6.0 to 7.0 percent of sales.
- Adjusted free cash flow is expected to be around €0.7 billion to €1.1 billion.
- Katja Garcia Vila stated that the company is targeting an increase in sales and adjusted EBIT margin for the Continental Group in the current year.
- Despite high special expenses, Continental expects the corridor for adjusted free cash flow to be only slightly below that of the previous year.
- Payments for the buyback of shares in ContiTech AG will impact free cash flow by around €500 million in 2024.
- In 2009, the company sold 24.9 percent of its shares in ContiTech AG to Continental Pension Trust e. V. to strengthen liquidity during the financial market crisis.
- The shares were bought back to restore the original ownership structure, meaning Continental AG now fully owns ContiTech AG, which operates under the name ContiTech Deutschland GmbH.
- Continental anticipates additional special expenses of around €500 million related to the carve-out of Original Equipment Solutions (ContiTech) and User Experience (Automotive) business areas.
- Additional special expenses also relate to measures already announced in the Automotive group sector to strengthen competitiveness.
- In total, Continental expects special expenses of around €1 billion, which will impact adjusted free cash flow in fiscal 2024.
- In the Automotive group sector, sales increased by 10.8 percent to €20.3 billion (2022: €18.3 billion).
- Automotive significantly increased its adjusted EBIT margin compared with the previous year to 1.9 percent (2022: -0.3 percent).
- The improvement in earnings was attributable to higher production efficiency thanks to a more stable material supply, strict cost discipline and a lower special freight volume.
- Continental further increased order intake in the Automotive group sector, generating orders worth around €27.1 billion last year.
- Year-on-year, the order volume increased by around 16 percent (2022: more than €23 billion).
- The order from the exclusive partnership with Aurora contributed to the increased order intake.
- Continental and Aurora will bring autonomous trucking systems to the mass market, starting with the USA by 2027.
- Continental and Aurora recently defined the design and architecture of the future driverless system, reaching this development milestone less than a year after their partnership began.
- The Tires group sector delivered stable profitability despite weak tire-replacement markets in Europe and North America, achieving sales of €14.0 billion (2022: €14.0 billion, -0.3 percent).
- At 13.5 percent, the adjusted EBIT margin was higher than in the previous year (2022: 13.1 percent).
- The margin improvement was mainly due to the continued high share of premium tires and a strong end-of-year business in Europe.
- With the UltraContact NXT, Continental launched the most sustainable production tire currently on the market last year.
- The UltraContact NXT has been available to tire dealers in Europe since July 2023.
- The UltraContact NXT comprises up to 65 percent renewable and recycled materials.
- It combines a high share of sustainable materials with maximum safety and performance, holding the highest possible rating (“A”) of the EU tire label in all performance categories.
- Continental has established a strong position in the tire original equipment segment for electric vehicles.
- The 10 highest-volume manufacturers of electric vehicles currently all place their trust in tires from Continental.
- The ContiTech group sector performed positively despite a weak industrial environment, generating sales of €6.8 billion (2022: €6.6 billion, +3.7 percent).
- ContiTech's adjusted EBIT margin was up significantly year-on-year at 6.7 percent (2022: 4.7 percent).
- In May last year, ContiTech launched a strategic realignment aimed at enhancing impact and efficiency, improving customer and market proximity and further expanding its industrial business.
- ContiTech strengthened its industrial business through company takeovers, fully acquiring the printing technology business of Trelleborg.
- This acquisition expanded Continental’s range of surface solutions in printing technology while boosting business with industrial clients.
- Continental also acquired the conveyor and maintenance company Vertech AB, a leading service provider for the mining industry in Sweden and Norway.
- The Vertech AB acquisition marks an important next step toward ContiTech’s goal of offering industrial customers in Sweden and other northern European countries a comprehensive package of products, services and solutions.
- At the end of 2023, Continental had around 200,000 employees (+1.9 percent compared with the end of 2022).
- The number of software and IT specialists increased slightly to around 22,000 worldwide.
