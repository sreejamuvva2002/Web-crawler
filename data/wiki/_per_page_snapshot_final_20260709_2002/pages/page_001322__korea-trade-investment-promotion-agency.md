# Korea Trade-Investment Promotion Agency

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2022-04-01

**Source:** [Korea Trade-Investment Promotion Agency Opens First Atlanta Office - Expansion Solutions](https://www.expansionsolutionsmagazine.com/korea-trade-investment-promotion-agency-opens-first-atlanta-office/)  ·  `page_001322`

## Facts

- Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) Commissioner Pat Wilson joined Mr. Soo Deuk Sohn, Senior Executive Vice President of KOTRA, to announce the opening of the new KOTRA overseas office in Atlanta.
- KOTRA is the Government of Korea’s trade and investment organization.
- Both parties signed a Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) affirming their dedication to support each other’s services and networks and to promote cooperation in economic relations, trade, and investment between Georgia and Korea.
- Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Korea is an established strategic market for investment, trade, and tourism for Georgia, and the new Atlanta office will strengthen these relationships.
- Existing connectivity includes daily direct flights between Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport and Incheon International Airport.
- Long-standing agreements exist between the Georgia ports of Savannah and Brunswick and five Korean ports to facilitate joint efforts to create an innovative electric mobility ecosystem in Georgia.
- On a mission to Korea in early March, Commissioner Wilson met with KOTRA President and CEO Mr. Jeoung Yeol Yu and key business representatives including those from Kia and SK Group.
- The mission re-confirmed the State of Georgia’s commitment to its Korean companies and international partners.
- Mr. Soo Deuk Sohn stated that KOTRA Atlanta will serve as an outpost to support Korean business in the Southeastern United States and expand cooperation in many industrial fields between Korea and the U.S.
- KOTRA helps Korean companies settle in new markets like Georgia by acting as the first point of contact and providing investment and support resources, including information on programs from state or local agencies like GDEcD.
- The new KOTRA office is located at 950 East Paces Ferry Road NE, Suite 999 in Atlanta.
- Atlanta will host the eighth overseas KOTRA office in the United States and the 10th in North America.
- The Republic of Korea has maintained a consular presence in Georgia since 1971, with Mr. Yoonjoo Park serving as Consul General since June 2021.
- Georgia has maintained continuous representation in Korea since 1985 with an office in Seoul headed by Managing Director Peter Underwood.
- The state also has a dedicated Georgia-based director of Korean Investment, Yoonie Kim.
- Several Georgia companies including American Megatrends, First Advantage Corporation, InComm, Novelis, Unicoi Systems, and UPS have operations in Korea.
- Georgia is home to at least 100 Korean facilities that currently employ more than 9,400 Georgians.
- Korean-affiliated companies in Georgia include Kia, SK Battery America, Sangsin Technology America, and Q CELLS Korea.
- Since 2020, numerous Korean companies have announced projects in Georgia, including SK Group subsidiary SKC manufacturing glass-based substrates for semiconductor chips in Covington with several business partners.
- SUNGDO ENG USA announced its corporate U.S. headquarters in the state to provide highly technical construction services to Georgia-based clients.
- Electric mobility parts and storage systems supplier for SK Group, Duckyang, is opening a new facility in Jackson County.
- In west Georgia, brake pad supplier KB Autosys will open its first U.S. advanced manufacturing facility to better serve regional customers such as GM, Hyundai, and Kia.
- Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport offers daily direct flights from Atlanta to Seoul.
- The Georgia Ports Authority (GPA) in Savannah has an established relationship with Korean ports and offers seven regular shipping services to the Port of Busan.
- GPA’s Brunswick Port also offers shipping services to Incheon, Masan, Pyongtaek, and Kunsan.
- In 2021, Georgia exports to Korea totaled $860.59 million, making Korea the 10th largest export market for Georgia.
- Top exports from Georgia to Korea include gas turbines, aircraft, medical instruments, chemical woodpulp, and chemical analysis instruments.
- Georgia imports from Korea totaled $8.83 billion in 2021, with Korea ranking third among Georgia’s importer nations.
- Top imports from Korea to Georgia include motor vehicles, tractor parts, bulldozers, engines, and fork-lift trucks.
- KOTRA was established in 1962 to contribute to the development of the national economy by promoting trade, facilitating investment between domestic and foreign companies, and supporting industrial technology cooperation.
- KOTRA is headquartered in Seoul but operates over 127 Overseas Trade Centers in 84 countries.
- On March 31, 2022, leaders from GDEcD and KOTRA announced the opening of the new KOTRA overseas office in Atlanta and signed an MOU affirming dedication to cooperation between Georgia and Korea.
