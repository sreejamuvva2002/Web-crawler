# Norfolk Southern Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2024-04-18

**Source:** [DEFA14A](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/702165/000119312524100915/d830417ddefa14a.htm)  ·  `page_028351`

## Facts

- Norfolk Southern originates more automotive traffic than any other Class I Railroad.
- The company has the most extensive intermodal network in the eastern U.S.
- Norfolk Southern helps its customers avoid approximately 15 million tons of yearly carbon emissions by shipping via rail.
- The company serves a majority of the country’s population and manufacturing base, with connections to every major container port on the Atlantic coast as well as major ports in the Gulf of Mexico and Great Lakes.
- Norfolk Southern is targeting a sub-60% operating ratio in three to four years to close the margin gap with peers.
- The company's plan includes capturing 400 basis points of productivity savings and upcycle improvement.
- In 2024, the plan targets delivering 100-150 basis points of operating ratio improvement year-over-year.
- There is a line of sight to 400-450 basis points of improvement in the second half of 2024 compared to the prior year period.
- CEO Alan Shaw was appointed in 2022 to leverage Precision Scheduled Railroading (PSR) to deliver top-tier revenue and earnings growth with industry-competitive margins.
- COO John Orr joined the company four weeks ago as of April 2024 and has improved Merchandise velocity by 8% and terminal dwell by 8%.
- Within the next 60 to 90 days, the company expects to reduce terminal dwell in two major yards by 30%, reduce overtime by 20%, reduce recrew rate by 20%, and increase on-time connections system wide by 10%.
- Following the East Palestine incident, Norfolk Southern reduced its mainline accident rate by 38% year-over-year in 2023, achieving the lowest rate since 1999.
- The company increased train speed by 22% and decreased terminal dwell by 11% since Shaw became CEO.
- In 2022, the company delivered record revenues, closed the margin gap to Class I peers with an operating ratio in the low 60s, and achieved the second highest five-year total shareholder returns among Class I peers.
