# Joon Georgia

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2022-11-08

**Source:** [Hyundai Ev Plant: Supplier to hire 630 workers near Hyundai's EV plant in Georgia, ETAuto](https://auto.economictimes.indiatimes.com/news/auto-components/supplier-to-hire-630-workers-near-hyundais-ev-plant-in-georgia/95368337)  ·  `page_056517`

## Facts

- Joon Georgia will invest $317 million to produce parts in Bulloch County.
- The supplier plans to hire 630 workers at a new factory in southeast Georgia.
- The new factory will be located roughly 30 miles (50 kilometers) west of the site where Hyundai executives broke ground on its new EV plant two weeks prior to the announcement.
- Joon Georgia is expected to open its factory near Statesboro in mid-2024.
- The company will supply Hyundai Motor Group's first U.S. electric vehicle plant, which is under construction nearby.
- Gov. Brian Kemp stated that Joon Georgia is "the first of many" suppliers expected to come to Georgia to support the $5.5 billion Hyundai plant in Bryan County.
- Hyundai plans to open its Georgia plant in 2025, with production capacity of up to 300,000 electric vehicles per year.
- Joon Georgia is a subsidiary of Ajin USA, which supplies parts to other Hyundai plants.
- Joon Georgia already operates an existing facility in Cusseta, Alabama, near the Georgia border that manufactures parts for Hyundai's plant in Montgomery, Alabama, and Kia's auto plant in West Point, Georgia.
- State and local officials in Georgia secured tax breaks and incentives worth $1.8 billion to lure Hyundai, marking the state's largest economic development deal.
- Gov. Brian Kemp's office announced the investment details in a news release.
- Pat Wilson, commissioner of the Georgia Department of Economic Development, called the announcement "a landmark moment as we drive Georgia's automotive industry into the future."
- In addition to Hyundai hiring 8,100 workers, suppliers are expected to create thousands of additional jobs in the state.
