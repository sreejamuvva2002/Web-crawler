# Archer Aviation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-05-17

**Source:** [US firm's air taxi set to fly at 2028 LA Olympics, help dodge traffic](https://interestingengineering.com/innovation/ARCHER-AVIATION-EVTOL-FLY-2028-LA-OLYMPICS)  ·  `page_017012`

## Facts

- San Jose-headquartered Archer Aviation has been chosen as the official air taxi partner for the upcoming 2028 Olympic Games in Los Angeles.
- The company’s ‘Midnight’ electric Vertical Take-Off and Landing (eVTOL) will ferry VIPs, fans, and athletes throughout the event.
- The Midnight is Archer’s flagship four-passenger, one-pilot eVTOL.
- This craft is much quieter than helicopters and other similar traditional air taxis, and is designed specifically for short-range urban trips.
- The eVTOL aircraft is built with redundant, fault-tolerant systems, including 12 total engines and propellers.
- Archer targets certification with the FAA at similar levels of safety as commercial airliners.
- The Midnight is currently being produced on a small scale at Archer’s plants in California and Georgia.
- The upcoming eVTOL will operate from vertiports at key Olympic locations, such as SoFi Stadium, LAX, and Los Angeles Memorial Coliseum.
- Adam Goldstein, Archer’s CEO and founder, stated the goal is to transform how people get around Los Angeles and leave a legacy shaping the future of transportation in America during the LA28 Games.
- Goldstein expressed excitement about seeing Midnight flying passengers over Los Angeles with the Team USA logo and Olympic/Paralympic rings.
- Vertiports are to be set up at key venues including a stadium in Inglewood, the LA Memorial Coliseum on the USC campus, LAX Airport, Hollywood Burbank, Orange County, and Santa Monica visitor hubs.
- The partnership with the Olympic committee and Team USA makes Archer a front-runner in implementing air taxis as public service.
- The collaboration provides a significant marketing boost for the eVTOL industry and helps normalize flying taxis in urban settings.
- The initiative aligns with LA's push for green transit and the Olympics' spotlight on innovation, offering clean, futuristic transport.
- The 2028 Olympic Games are expected to welcome over 15 million visitors, some of whom may experience the first-ever air taxi rides in the US.
- Archer Aviation hopes to receive FAA certification to offer commercial flights en masse.
- Establishing large-scale vertiport infrastructure across LA over the coming years is a critical milestone.
- It remains uncertain if the public will trust and use the technology.
- The company announced international partnerships with Japan and the United Arab Emirates to provide zero-emission air taxi networks.
- Casey Wasserman, LA28 Chairperson and President, stated Archer was partnered with for innovation and creativity, aiming to reimagine the Olympic experience.
