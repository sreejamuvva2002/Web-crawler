# LG Energy Solution

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-10-10

**Source:** [LG Energy Solution invests $3B in US battery manufacturing expansion | Utility Dive](https://www.utilitydive.com/news/toyota-lg-energy-solution-lithium-battery-supply-deal-michigan-factory-expansion/696126/)  ·  `page_001256`

## Facts

- LG Energy Solution will invest $3 billion in its Holland, Michigan battery manufacturing plant.
- The investment is part of a new lithium battery supply deal with Toyota.
- Batteries from the Michigan plant will be shipped to Toyota’s largest vehicle manufacturing plant in Georgetown, Kentucky.
- The batteries will be used in Toyota’s new, North American-assembled battery-powered EV.
- This agreement is LG Energy Solution’s largest supply deal outside a joint venture and is scheduled to begin in 2025.
- LG Energy Solution is planning an annual battery production capacity of 20 gigawatts.
- The supply deal supports Toyota’s goal to produce 3.5 million battery EVs annually by 2030.
- Toyota announced plans to triple its annual EV production target to 600,000 vehicles in 2025.
- Toyota aims to compete against global EV makers like Tesla and China-based BYD.
- Toyota announced plans to update and streamline its EV manufacturing process to reduce production costs.
- Toyota’s North American segment sold 64,298 EVs in September, up 81.5% year-over-year.
- LG Energy Solution has become a major player in U.S. battery manufacturing over the past year.
- LG Energy Solution is partnering with Hyundai to build a $6.3 billion battery manufacturing campus in Savannah, Georgia.
- LG Energy Solution is partnering with Honda to build a facility worth up to $4.4 billion in Fayette County, Ohio.
- LG Energy Solution and GM’s joint venture Ultium are building three battery production sites across Ohio, Tennessee, and Michigan.
- LG Energy Solution is independently investing $5.5 billion in a Queen Creek, Arizona complex to produce cylindrical EV batteries and lithium iron phosphate batteries.
- Young Soo Kwon, CEO of LG Energy Solution, stated the company will leverage 30 years of lithium-ion battery experience to support Toyota’s EV expansion.
- Kwon noted the agreement strengthens LG Energy Solution’s North American production capacity and advances regional electrification progress.
