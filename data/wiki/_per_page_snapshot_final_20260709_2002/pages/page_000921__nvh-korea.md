## Facts

- An automobile parts supplier plans to build a new factory in Henry County to supply Georgia’s electric vehicle industry, namely cars made by Hyundai Motor Group.
- NVH Korea will invest $72 million in the new plant in Locust Grove, south of Atlanta.
- The plant is expected to employ 160 workers.
- The investment and employment details were announced in a Thursday news release.
- NVH Korea produces a variety of noise, vibration, and heat-control parts.
- The new facility will focus on supplying battery parts to Hyundai and its subsidiaries, including Kia and parts supplier Hyundai Mobis.
- Gov. Brian Kemp made the announcement while in France for the Paris Air Show.
- During his trip to France, Gov. Kemp is touting Georgia for aerospace industry expansion.
- The NVH Korea auto parts plant is the second large jobs recruitment announcement to come during his trip to France.
- Pratt & Whitney’s $206 million commitment to expand its Columbus operation and add 400 jobs preceded the NVH Korea announcement on this trip.
