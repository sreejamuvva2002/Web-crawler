# One Porsche Drive (Porsche Experience Center Atlanta and North American Headquarters)

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [One Porsche Drive, Porsche North America Headquarters | Kimley-Horn](https://www.kimley-horn.com/project/one-porsche-drive/)  ·  `page_048782`

## Facts

- Porsche Cars North America developed a new 27.7-acre complex, dubbed One Porsche Drive, to serve as its new North American headquarters.
- The complex spans across Fulton and Clayton Counties in the cities of Atlanta and Hapeville, Georgia.
- The site includes the Porsche Experience Center Atlanta and Headquarters featuring office space, workshops, training facilities, restaurants, a museum, a green roof, and the Porsche Driver Experience.
- Kimley-Horn provided land development, landscape architecture, and environmental services for the new headquarters.
- Kimley-Horn's project scope included demolition, site plan creation, grading, utility design services, stormwater conveyance, private pump station design, permitting, and construction phase services.
- Kimley-Horn designed, permitted, and assisted Porsche in the construction and testing of state-of-the-art fast-charging stations.
- The fast-charging stations can fully charge the electric Porsche 918 Spyder Supercar in less than 15 minutes.
- The charging stations will also be installed at the Porsche Experience Center Los Angeles.
- The facility provides office space for more than 400 employees.
- One Porsche Drive contains 13,000 square feet of conference and event space.
- The site features a 1.6-mile driver development track and handling course.
- A training center for technicians is located on-site.
- The complex includes a driver-simulator lab and a classic car restoration workshop.
- Additional facilities include a customer center, a fine-dining restaurant, and three levels of subsurface parking.
- The $100-million facility represents Porsche's largest foreign investment in its nearly 90-year history.
- One Porsche Drive attracts over 30,000 visitors per year.
- The complex invites customers, business partners, and automotive enthusiasts to experience Porsche.
- One Porsche Drive, in conjunction with the new Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Terminal, acts as a catalyst for the redevelopment of Atlanta, Hapeville, and unincorporated Clayton County.
- The project marks the beginning of a larger movement toward sustainable development surrounding the world's busiest airport.
