# Rivian Automotive

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Rivian Automotive plans to unveil massive electric vehicle plant and track east of Atlanta — Motor World](https://motorworld.app/rivian-automotive-plans-to-unveil-massive-electric-vehicle-plant-and-track-east-of-atlanta/)  ·  `page_059126`

## Facts

- U.S. electric vehicle maker Rivian Automotive plans to start the structure of its production plant in Georgia in 2022.
- Site maps discovered through Rivian Forums revealed the California-based automaker was looking to build a 20 million-square-foot facility on about 2,000 acres.
- The facility will be five times the length of Rivian’s first plant in Normal, Illinois.
- Electrek reported that the massive plant will be 30 minutes east of Atlanta.
- Concept plans received via Rivian Forums show the complex stretching on both sides of the line between Walton and Morgan counties, just north of Interstate 20, between Social Circle and Madison, Georgia.
- Electrek first reported in December that Rivian had targeted northern Georgia for a second, larger facility.
- Rivian announced that it plans to begin electric car production at the Peach State’s $5 billion plant in 2024.
- The plant will employ more than 7,500 workers.
- The plant represents a key next step as Rivian aggressively moves toward higher capacity production for its next generation of products.
- Rivian Forums members reported that the Georgia facility will have thirteen separate buildings plus an R space.
- Planning notes indicate the facility is legal under Level 1 zoning and may include garage space, accessory buildings, offices, a medical clinic, recreational facilities, a restaurant, education rooms, a nursery, a hotel, and a gym.
- Solar, wind, and environmental energy production can also be built on the site.
- Rivian's Illinois facility was originally a Mitsubishi factory before Rivian acquired it in 2017.
- Four years after acquiring the Illinois plant, Rivian delivered its first electric passenger vehicle.
- The Illinois facility has received two city-approved additions totaling 672,000 square feet, bringing its total domain to nearly 3.9 million square feet.
- Rivian is about to deliver its moment of two iconic electric cars in the Illinois plant’s R1S SUV.
- Electrek noted that Rivian has neither shown nor denied the plans, but they fit many recent company descriptions.
