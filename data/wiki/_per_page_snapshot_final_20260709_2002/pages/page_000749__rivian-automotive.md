# Rivian Automotive

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09

**Source:** [‘Do Or Die’ | News, Sports, Jobs - Post Journal](https://www.post-journal.com/news/business/2025/09/do-or-die-2/)  ·  `page_000749`

## Facts

- Rivian broke ground on a $5 billion electric vehicle plant in Social Circle, Georgia.
- The facility is located east of Atlanta.
- The groundbreaking occurred on a Tuesday.
- Rivian is a California-based company that is currently money-losing.
- President Donald Trump successfully pushed to roll back electric vehicle tax credits.
- Starting Sept 30, buyers will no longer qualify for savings of up to $7,500 per car.
- CEO RJ Scaringe stated Rivian will sell its R2 sport utility vehicles because it is a superior vehicle, not for environmental or tax incentive reasons.
- Scaringe described the R2 as an incredible five-passenger offroad machine that happens to be electric.
- The Georgia plant was first announced in 2021.
- The Georgia plant is key to Rivian's profitability goals.
- Rivian currently makes the high-end R1T pickup truck and R1S SUV in Normal, Illinois.
- Rivian also manufactures delivery vans for Amazon and others.
- R1T prices start at $71,000.
- The Illinois plant will begin making the smaller R2 next year.
- R2 prices will start at $45,000.
- An expanded Illinois plant will be able to assemble 215,000 vehicles yearly.
- If the R2 is a hit and Rivian successfully produces an even smaller R3, it will need more capacity.
- The Georgia operation will be able to make 200,000 vehicles yearly starting in 2028.
- Scaringe called the Georgia operation the foundation for growth.
- Rivian plans another 200,000 in capacity in phase two.
- Phase two volume would spread fixed costs over many more vehicles.
- Rivian expects to deliver between 40,000 and 46,000 vehicles this year.
- Vehicle deliveries are down from 52,000 last year.
- The company is limiting production in part to launch 2026 models.
- Alex Oyler, North American director of auto research firm SBD Automotive, said it is do-or-die time for Rivian.
- Oyler noted that Tesla showed the key to profitability is scale.
- Oyler stated you cannot scale if your cheapest vehicle is $70,000.
- Oyler said they need the plant online to achieve a level of scale for the R2 and ultimately R3.
- Sales growth for electric vehicles in the United States rose only 1.5% in 2025's first half, according to Cox Automotive.
- Tesla accounted for almost 45% of U.S. electric vehicle sales in that period, according to Cox.
- General Motors' slice of American EV sales has climbed to 13%.
- Rivian had a 3% share in the first half of the year, behind Tesla and six traditional automakers.
- Excluding Tesla, Rivian is the most successful startup automaker.
- Rivian initially tapped a largely unfilled niche: demand for electric pickups and SUVs.
- Competition now includes Ford’s F-150 Lightning and the electric Chevrolet Silverado.
- After an initial public offering in 2021, Rivian shares have fallen by more than 80%.
- Automaker shares overall have outpaced the broader stock market.
- Rivian lost $1.66 billion in 2025’s first half.
- Stellantis canceled Ram’s electric truck program last week.
- Ford has delayed production at a new Tennessee plant.
- General Motors abandoned plans to build electric vehicles in suburban Detroit.
- Sam Fiorani, vice president at AutoForecast Solutions, said slowing EV growth and competition do not play in Rivian's favor.
- Fiorani noted there still is an EV market out there.
- Georgia has pledged $1.5 billion of incentives to Rivian in exchange for 7,500 company jobs.
- The 7,500 jobs will pay at least $56,000 a year on average.
- Rivian cannot benefit from most incentives unless it meets employment goals.
- Georgia is already spending $175 million to buy and grade land and improve roads.
- Georgia Republican Gov. Brian Kemp wants to make Georgia the electric mobility capital of America.
- Kemp acknowledged the road to get here has not been smooth.
- Kemp voiced confidence that Rivian can deliver an innovation revolution benefitting Georgians.
- Tesla has thousands of employees in California and Texas.
- Two separate EV makers that hoped to assemble vehicles in a former GM plant in Lordstown, Ohio, went bankrupt.
- Georgia’s Hyundai complex near Savannah is faring better, with production underway.
- A battery plant at the Hyundai complex has been delayed by U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement arresting 475 people on site.
- The arrests included more than 300 South Koreans.
- Rivian was supposed to be making trucks by now at the 2,000-acre (800-hectare) site near Social Circle.
- The site is about 45 miles (70 kilometers) east of Atlanta.
- As the company burned through cash in 2024, it paused construction.
- German automaker Volkswagen agreed to invest $5.8 billion in Rivian in exchange for software and electrical technology.
- Then-President Joe Biden’s administration agreed to loan Rivian $6.6 billion to build the Georgia plant.
- Scaringe said Rivian has built a very close relationship with the U.S. Department of Energy.
- Scaringe said the company’s goals align with some of Trump’s big ones, including U.S. manufacturing, U.S. technology, U.S. technology that supports global business, and leading in all those areas.
- Kemp said he has urged Energy Secretary Chris Wright to back Rivian.
- Rivian faces opposition from some residents who say the plant is an inappropriate neighbor to farms and will pollute groundwater.
- Resident Eddie Clay, who lives less than a mile away, questioned why the biggest project in Georgia has to go next door.
- Clay says his well water turned mud-choked after excavation at the Rivian site.
- Challenges include tariffs costing $2,000 per vehicle.
- The Trump administration ending a tax-credit program will cost the company $140 million in revenue this year.
- Long-term threats come from low-priced, cutting-edge Chinese EVs.
- Scaringe said the start of construction shows Rivian is working through its challenges.
- Scaringe stated these are not kinds of things you start without having clear sight to be able to fully finish and launch.
