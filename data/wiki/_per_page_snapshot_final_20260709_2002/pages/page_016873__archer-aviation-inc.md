## Facts

- Archer Aviation is finishing the build of its production facility in Covington, GA.
- The company is spending more on testing efforts as part of its fourth-quarter operating expenses.
- Operating expenses increased by 15% year-over-year from $107.30 million to $124.20 million.
- Archer Aviation is developing the Midnight eVTOL (electric vertical take-off and landing) vehicle, also referred to as flying taxis.
- The company is in the final stage of the Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) regulatory process for its aircraft.
- In late February, the FAA gave Archer Aviation approval to launch its pilot training academy.
- The pilot training academy will train personnel in preparation for commercial services.
- Archer Aviation has partnerships with Southwest Airlines Co. and United Airlines Holdings Inc. to facilitate mobility networks in Los Angeles and San Francisco.
- Abu Dhabi Aviation, the Middle East’s largest commercial helicopter operator, plans to deploy the first of Archer's flying taxis later in 2025.
- Archer Aviation raised $301.80 million in capital in February.
- The company now has approximately $1 billion on its balance sheet, which will be enough to start production.
