# Kia Georgia Plant

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-02-25

**Source:** [Kia Georgia Plant Tops 5 Million Vehicles, Starts Production of 2027 Telluride Hybrid | Aju Press](https://www.ajupress.com/view/20260225085470236)  ·  `page_010095`

## Facts

- Kia's Georgia plant reached the 5 million production mark as of February 24 local time.
- The milestone vehicle was the 2027 model-year all-new Telluride hybrid, marking Kia's first hybrid vehicle produced in Georgia.
- A ceremony celebrating the milestone was held with Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp, local government officials, community leaders, and employees attending.
- The Georgia operation began production in 2009 and has grown into a major local employer and a core manufacturing base for the auto industry in the U.S. Southeast.
- The Telluride built at the West Point plant is a strategic model developed around North American consumer demand and U.S. driving conditions, which Kia calls a symbol of the brand's growth in the U.S. market.
- The first-generation Telluride won major awards in 2020, including North American Car of the Year, World Car of the Year, and MotorTrend SUV of the Year, helping raise the brand's profile in North America.
- Yoon Seung-kyu, head of Kia’s North America regional headquarters and CEO of its U.S. sales unit, stated that Kia Georgia is the only plant in the world that produces the Telluride and that this production start carries meaning beyond the rollout of a new vehicle.
- Gov. Brian Kemp said the Telluride has been produced only in Georgia since its 2019 launch and has become one of Kia's most successful models, adding that the achievement is the result of a close partnership with the community and the state government.
- Stuart Countess, head of Kia’s Georgia manufacturing unit, said the 5 million milestone reflects employees' dedication and teamwork, noting that the plant's flexibility and technical capabilities were proven again as it adds hybrid production alongside internal combustion and electric vehicles.
