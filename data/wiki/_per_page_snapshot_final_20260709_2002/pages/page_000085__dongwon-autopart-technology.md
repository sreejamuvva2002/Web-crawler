# Dongwon Autopart Technology

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-02-04

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp: 200 New Jobs Headed to Emanuel County | Governor Brian P. Kemp Office of the Governor](https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases/2026-02-04/gov-kemp-200-new-jobs-headed-emanuel-county)  ·  `page_000085`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Dongwon Autopart Technology plans to invest $30 million in a new production facility in Emanuel County.
- The automotive project is anticipated to create 200 new jobs over the next several years.
- Dongwon Autopart Technology was established in 1971.
- The company supplies components such as doorframes, seat frames, and battery frames to automotive original equipment manufacturers (OEMs).
- Dongwon Autopart Technology currently supports more than 500 direct jobs in the Southeast.
- Existing facilities in Hogansville, Georgia will remain operational.
- Dongwon Autopart Technology has been operating in the United States since 2003.
- Construction on the new manufacturing facility will be located at the Hwy 297 Industrial Park near Swainsboro.
- The company will be hiring for positions such as management, system leads, and production personnel.
- Hiring is expected to begin in 2026.
- Representative Butch Parrish, Chairman of the House Rules Committee, stated the Georgia House of Representatives is committed to bringing high-quality jobs to rural communities.
- Chairman Will Donaldson of the Emanuel County Development Authority welcomed Dongwon Autopart Technology to Emanuel County and noted their economic development investments paid off for the community.
- Ken Warnock, CEO of the Emanuel County Development Authority, called the project the single largest new economic development project for Emanuel County in over 30 years.
- Alyce Thornhill, Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy, represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The project was developed in partnership with the Emanuel County Development Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated the automotive industry is expanding outward along the I-16 corridor as suppliers choose strategic locations with access to OEMs and Georgia’s ports.
- Dongwon Autopart Technology’s new facility will be its second location in Georgia.
