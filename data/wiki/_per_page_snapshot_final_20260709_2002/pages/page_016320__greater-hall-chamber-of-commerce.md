# Greater Hall Chamber of Commerce

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Business Taxes & Incentives - Greater Hall Chamber of Commerce](https://www.ghcc.com/economic-development/business-taxes-incentives/)  ·  `page_016320`

## Facts

- Gainesville-Hall County, Georgia maintains a stable business tax environment and offers attractive incentives to new and existing businesses to encourage the creation of new jobs and investment.
- Businesses located in a Georgia Opportunity Zone may qualify for the state’s maximum job tax credit of $3,500 per job when creating two or more jobs; credits may be applied against state corporate income or withholding tax.
- The West Gainesville/Atlanta Highway area in the Gainesville-Hall County MSA is designated as a Georgia Opportunity Zone.
- Gainesville-Hall County has three census tracts designated as Federal Opportunity Zones.
- The Federal Opportunity Zone distinction was created by the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act to spur economic growth in low-income areas by allowing investors to temporarily defer and, ultimately, exempt federal capital gains on investments made in the Federal Opportunity Zone.
- Qualified businesses in Gainesville-Hall County may qualify for the Job Tax Credit Program if they create at least 25 new jobs in a calendar year; Job Tax Credits are $1,250 each year for five years for each new full time job created and maintained.
- Creating 25 new jobs over five years yields $156,250 in tax credits under the Job Tax Credit Program example calculation.
- A Port Job Tax Credit Bonus of $1,250 per job is available for businesses that already qualify for the job tax credits and have a 10% increase in Georgia Port traffic over the previous year.
- Businesses qualify for Quality Job Tax Credits if they create a minimum of 50 new jobs that pay at least 110% of the county average (est. $61,032 annually).
- Quality Job Tax Credits are awarded per job, per year, for up to 5 years based on an increasing scale system.
- Direct investment for employee retraining for quality and productivity enhancements may qualify for credits of 50% of the employer’s direct cost of up to $1,250 per employee under Retraining Tax Credits & WIOA Incumbent Worker programs.
- Additional tax credits are available for equipment, capital investments, retraining employees, childcare, and R&D.
- Job, Investment, and Retraining Credits earned in Hall County may be used to offset up to 50% of a company’s Georgia corporate income tax liability in any taxable year following the year in which the credits are earned.
- Credits not used may be carried forward and applied for up to 10 years.
- Georgia’s Corporate Income Tax rate is 4.99%.
- The “Single Factor Gross Receipts” apportionment formula results in a substantial and permanent reduction of state income taxes for companies that manufacture products within the state of Georgia and sell those products or services to customers in other states.
- This formula is designed to encourage manufacturing, distribution and service companies to invest or expand in Georgia.
- Gainesville-Hall County and State of Georgia Sales Tax total 7%.
- Georgia Law provides a 100% sales and use tax exemption for qualified purchases including Manufacturing Machinery & Equipment Purchase & Repair, Raw Materials & Packaging, Energy Used in Manufacturing, Primary Material Handling Equipment, Pollution Control Equipment, and Consumables.
- Effective property tax rate for the City of Gainesville is 1.137% of Fair Market Value (FMV).
- Effective property tax rate for Hall County (Unincorporated Areas) is 0.952% of FMV.
- Effective property tax rate for the City of Oakwood is 1.131% of FMV.
- Effective property tax rate for the City of Flowery Branch is 1.095% of FMV.
- Effective property tax rate for the City of Buford is 0.825% of FMV.
- Effective property tax rate for the City of Lula is 0.964% of FMV.
- Effective property tax rate for the Town of Braselton is 1.164% of FMV.
- Depreciation percentage for personal property in GROUP 1 during the first year of business is 84%, with a typical economic life of 5-7 years.
- Depreciation percentage for personal property in GROUP 2 during the first year of business is 91%, with a typical economic life of 8-12 years.
- Depreciation percentage for personal property in GROUP 3 during the first year of business is 95%, with a typical economic life of 13 years or more.
- Depreciation percentage for personal property in GROUP 4 during the first year of business is 76%, with a typical economic life of 1-4 years.
- Companies operating 85% of total available time over a 12-month period may be eligible for accelerated depreciation on equipment under the Multi-Shift program.
- A 100% Freeport Tax Exemption (ad valorem) with annual filing is available for raw materials and goods in process of manufacture, finished goods held by the manufacturer, and finished goods destined for shipment out-of-state.
- Tax exempt bond financing is available for companies seeking to finance the purchase of land, buildings and equipment for eligible manufacturing projects through Industrial Revenue Bond (IRB) Financing.
- Rates for tax-exempt bonds are market-based and established on the credit and strength of the individual company and projects.
- Tax-exempt IRB financing can be used for projects with capital investment between $1 million and $20 million.
