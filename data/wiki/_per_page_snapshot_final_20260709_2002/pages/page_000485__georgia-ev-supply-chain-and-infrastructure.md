# Georgia EV Supply Chain and Infrastructure

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Senator and congressman weigh in on Georgia's economic future - WJCL](https://www.wjcl.com/article/electric-vehicle-future-georgia/63875463)  ·  `page_000485`

## Facts

- Former President Joe Biden initiated a $5 billion program in 2022 aimed at building 500,000 electric vehicle chargers across the nation.
- President Trump signed the "Unleashing American Energy" executive order terminating the EV charger program earlier this month.
- WJCL 22 News interviewed Rep. Buddy Carter of Georgia's 1st Congressional District regarding the impact of federal decisions on Georgia's economic outlook.
- Rep. Buddy Carter stated that the current administration has "hit the ground running" and had a positive effect on Georgia's economy.
- Trump's administration signed 68 executive orders, including one to eliminate the EV vehicle goal by 2030.
- The National Electric Vehicle Infrastructure Formula Program, which would have facilitated nationwide construction of EV chargers, was eliminated.
- Hyundai is producing EV vehicles in Georgia, bringing scrutiny to the state's future EV infrastructure.
- Rep. Buddy Carter stated that the future of EVs in Georgia is strong and bright, predicting a market will exist for them.
- Rep. Buddy Carter criticized government intervention in consumer choices, stating that when the government picks winners and losers, consumers lose.
- EV supplier Aspen Aerogels halted production at a potential new manufacturing plant in Bulloch County.
- Rep. Buddy Carter expressed disappointment that Aspen Aerogels decided not to pursue their project further.
- WJCL 22 News asked Sen. Jon Ossoff about the Trump administration's actions damaging Georgia's economy through the repeal of energy and manufacturing policies.
- Rep. Buddy Carter noted that a frequent question he gets is "What's next?" and remarked that with Donald Trump, it changes every day and every minute.
- The United States Department of Energy maintains a map of all EV charging stations nationwide.
