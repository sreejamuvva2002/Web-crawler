# Novelis Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2025-11-04

**Source:** [Novelis Reports Second Quarter Fiscal Year 2026 Results :: Novelis Inc.](https://investors.novelis.com/news-events/press-releases/detail/1407/novelis-reports-second-quarter-fiscal-year-2026-results)  ·  `page_047396`

## Facts

- Novelis Inc. is constructing a state-of-the-art greenfield rolling and recycling plant in Bay Minette, Alabama.
- The company is making a significant investment in the U.S. to bring needed capacity for infinitely recyclable, lightweight aluminum to an undersupplied domestic market.
- Total capital expenditures increased 27% to $913 million for the first six months of fiscal year 2026, primarily due to strategic investments in new rolling and recycling capacity under construction, most notably the Bay Minette plant.
- Start-up costs related to the construction of the Bay Minette rolling and recycling plant are included in Selling, general and administrative expenses.
- Demand for infinitely recyclable, lightweight aluminum continues to grow as a fundamental material in modern transportation.
- Slightly higher automotive and aerospace shipments were offset by lower beverage packaging and specialty shipments in the second quarter of fiscal year 2026.
- Novelis serves customers in the aerospace, automotive, beverage packaging, and specialties industries throughout North America, Europe, Asia, and South America.
- Novelis Inc. is a subsidiary of Hindalco Industries Limited, which is the metals flagship company of the Aditya Birla Group.
- Q2 fiscal year 2026 net sales increased 10% versus the prior year period to $4.7 billion.
- Adjusted EBITDA for Q2 fiscal year 2026 was $422 million, down 9% year-over-year.
- Rolled product shipments totaled 941 kilotonnes in Q2 fiscal year 2026, in line with the prior year period.
- Net income attributable to common shareholders increased 27% versus the prior year to $163 million in Q2 fiscal year 2026.
- Novelis had net sales of $17.1 billion in fiscal year 2025.
