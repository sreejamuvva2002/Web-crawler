# BlueOval SK Battery Park

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-12-05

**Source:** [Ford, SK On Making Progress with BlueOval SK Battery Park Construction - The EV Report](https://theevreport.com/ford-sk-on-making-progress-with-blueoval-sk-battery-park-construction)  ·  `page_050069`

## Facts

- BlueOval SK Battery Park is a joint venture between Ford and SK On located in Glendale, Kentucky.
- The joint venture has invested $5.8 billion to produce advanced batteries for future Ford and Lincoln electric vehicles.
- Production at the site is slated to start in 2025.
- The project will create 5,000 new jobs in Kentucky.
- BlueOval SK Battery Park consists of two massive battery manufacturing facilities capable of collectively producing more than 80 gigawatt hours annually.
- The site covers 1,500 acres and spans a 2.3-square-mile campus.
- Ford aims to reach an annual run rate of 2 million electric vehicles globally by the end of 2026, with this project bringing it closer to that target.
- SK On president and CEO Jee Dong-seob stated that BlueOval SK Battery Park will be at the core of the electrification of the North American auto market.
- Jee Dong-seob added that they expect SK On and Ford’s leadership in the global electric vehicle market to be solidified through BlueOval SK.
- Ford EV Industrialization vice president Lisa Drake said BlueOval SK will help Ford lead the EV revolution by bringing thousands of new, high-tech jobs to Kentucky.
- Lisa Drake noted that Ford is building on more than a century of investment in Kentucky and its workforce.
- BlueOval SK CEO David Hahm stated that the park is building state-of-the-art batteries for future Ford and Lincoln electric vehicles and a bright future for the Kentucky workforce.
- David Hahm emphasized the need for a premier workforce trained in the latest battery and advanced manufacturing technology to produce these batteries.
- The ECTC BlueOval SK Training Center will be located on the 1,500-acre site to train 5,000 new workers.
- Kentucky Commonwealth invested $25 million into the training center.
- The ECTC BlueOval SK Training Center is the only co-branded learning facility within the Kentucky Community College System.
- The training facility spans 42,000 square feet and will support battery knowledge, roles, and skills.
- BlueOval SK will train employees in SK On’s proprietary technical, quality, and manufacturing processes at the center.
- Training facilities include virtual reality labs, an industrial maintenance lab, a work simulation lab, and ergonomics techniques classrooms.
- The Kentucky Community and Technical College System (KCTCS) began construction of the training center in 2023, with completion scheduled for 2024.
- Kentucky Governor Andy Beshear stated that the partnership between Kentucky and Ford dates back more than a century to the dawn of the automobile industry.
- Gov. Beshear noted that breaking ground on BlueOval SK Battery Park in Hardin County cements Kentucky’s status as the electric vehicle battery production capital of the United States.
- Gov. Beshear described the project as both the largest economic development project in Kentucky’s history and part of the biggest investment ever by Ford.
- Construction crews have moved 4.3 million cubic yards of soil at the site, enough to fill 200 American football stadiums.
- Crews have laid 283,000 tons of stone, equivalent to the weight of nearly 1,350 locomotives.
- Crews have poured 66,000 cubic yards of concrete, enough to fill 356 backyard swimming pools.
- Crews have installed 3,300 tons of rebar ties to reinforce concrete, equivalent to the weight of more than 470 elephants.
- Crews have installed 1,300 deep foundations, equal to the height of nearly 60 Empire State Buildings stacked end to end.
- Crews have erected 7,900 tons of structural steel, equivalent to the weight of nearly 400 fire trucks.
- Ford and its construction partners provided nearly $40,000 and manpower to the city of Glendale through donations, sponsorships, and volunteer efforts.
- Construction partners include Barton Malow Company and Gray Construction.
- The team refreshed many of Glendale’s parks and sidewalks, some not replaced in over 100 years.
- Partners installed handicap-accessible ramps throughout Glendale.
- Ford supported local projects with the United Way of Central Kentucky, Feeding America, and Habitat for Humanity.
- Ford donated $42,000 to the Glendale Volunteer Fire Department, increasing its budget by more than 50 percent.
- Ford sponsored this year’s Glendale Crossing Festival.
- Hardin County Schools Transition Readiness Director Lisa Slaven stated that Ford has been a pillar of the Kentucky community for over a century.
- Lisa Slaven viewed the building of BlueOval SK Battery Park as synonymous with creating new learning opportunities and skill sets for students, the community, and Kentucky.
