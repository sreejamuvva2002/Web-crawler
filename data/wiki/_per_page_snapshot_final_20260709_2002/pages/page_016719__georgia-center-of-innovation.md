# Georgia Center of Innovation

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2021-11-02

**Source:** [GDEcD Georgia Center of Innovation Launches New Website Following Record Year - AllOnGeorgia](https://allongeorgia.com/georgia-business/gdecd-georgia-center-of-innovation-launches-new-website-following-record-year/)  ·  `page_016719`

## Facts

- The Georgia Center of Innovation is a strategic arm of the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD).
- The Center launched new website assets following a record year of impact on Georgia businesses.
- During fiscal year 2021, the Georgia Center of Innovation had more than 700 engagements with Georgia businesses and industry organizations, representing an increase of over 16% from the previous fiscal year.
- The Center added a new area of expertise focused on Agricultural Technology (AgTech).
- Teams at the Center represent some of the state’s leading industries, including manufacturing, logistics, IT, aerospace, and energy technology.
- Staff with the Center are located strategically around Georgia to partner with businesses and provide expert advice and resource connections.
- Services provided by the Center are complimentary and considered a concierge service provided by the state.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated the Center is proud to be the only state to provide this benefit, connecting small, large, and medium-sized businesses with solutions to source materials, offer guidance on implementing technology, and provide support for growth.
- The Center expanded its IT team by establishing a partnership with Augusta University and the Georgia Cyber Center to create a shared position delivering on-the-ground support for companies in Augusta’s cybersecurity ecosystem.
- The Center added support for Georgia’s growing digital media industry to its IT team.
- The AgTech team supports Georgia’s No. 1 industry by focusing on precision agriculture, controlled environment agriculture, and food product innovation.
- Experts at the Center consistently support GDEcD business recruitment efforts by providing critical insights and aiding in ecosystem development statewide.
- Past assistance examples include helping Thomasville’s Sweet Grass Dairy reach new markets, assisting Skyfire with securing drone service contracts, and ensuring Carbice reached full-scale production for nanotech thermal tape.
- During the COVID-19 pandemic, the Center led Georgia’s effort to meet PPE needs by identifying and coordinating businesses that manufacture, store, and distribute supplies, utilizing a COVID-19 Suppliers Interactive List and Map.
- Executive Director David Nuckolls described the Center as an extra complimentary resource available to Georgia businesses that offers direct advice or connections to address business challenges.
- GDEcD serves as the state’s sales and marketing arm, acting as the lead agency for attracting new business investment, encouraging industry expansion, locating markets for Georgia products, promoting tourism, and planning/mobilizing state resources for economic development.
