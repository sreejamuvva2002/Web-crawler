## Facts

- NHTSA issued a consent order with $130 million in total civil penalties to Volvo Group North America.
- The NHTSA investigation found the company failed to recall heavy-duty trucks and buses in a timely fashion.
- The company failed to comply with other recall and reporting requirements, including notifying owners of recalls and reporting death and injury incidents.
- The total penalties are one of the largest-ever for violations of the Vehicle Safety Act.
- Volvo Group North America violated the act by untimely filing certain recalls.
- Volvo Group North America violated the act by including inaccurate information in its DIRs.
- Volvo Group North America violated the act by failing to comply with requirements for notifying owners of a recall.
- Volvo Group North America violated the act by failing to submit certain quarterly recall reports.
- Volvo Group North America violated the act by failing to submit certain manufacturer communications.
- Volvo Group North America violated the act by failing to comply with early warning reporting requirements, not reporting certain death and injury incidents, and not submitting certain field reports.
- The consent order includes both monetary and non-monetary provisions designed to improve compliance with the law and safety practices.
- Volvo Group North America agreed to oversight by an independent third-party auditor.
- The company will meet regularly with NHTSA to ensure it addresses any potential safety issues.
- The auditor will review processes to assess compliance with the Vehicle Safety Act, regulations, and the consent order.
- The auditor will recommend and oversee implementation of organizational and process improvements to enhance Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standards compliance and certification.
- The auditor will oversee a review of past submissions to NHTSA for accuracy and completeness.
- Volvo Group North America will develop and implement a safety data analytics infrastructure to enhance its ability to detect and investigate potential safety defects.
- The company will develop written procedures and training for employees on compliance with the Vehicle Safety Act, regulations, and the consent order.
- The company will implement a training schedule to ensure employees are on-boarded and trained appropriately.
- Volvo Group North America will launch a user interface on its websites to allow users to search vehicle identification numbers for open recalls.
- The company will implement a system to support NHTSA’s VIN lookup tool.
- The term of the consent order is three years, which NHTSA may extend for up to two additional years if warranted.
- The consent order requires an upfront payment of $65 million.
- The company must spend an additional $20 million on creating a safety data analytics infrastructure.
- The consent order includes an additional $45 million deferred penalty that may become payable under specified circumstances.
