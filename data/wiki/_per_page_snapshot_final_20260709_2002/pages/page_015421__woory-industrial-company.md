# Woory Industrial Company

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Woory Industrial Looks Toward Right to Work Georgia | NILRR](https://nilrr.org/woory-industrial-looks-toward-right-to-work-georgia/)  ·  `page_015421`

## Facts

- Woory Industrial is a manufacturing company for auto parts looking to Right to Work Dublin, Georgia, for its new location.
- The company is investing a total of $18 million in the project.
- The project will create 130 new jobs.
- CEO Jungwoo Kim stated that the company is facing a revolution in the automobile industry for the first time in 100 years.
- Jungwoo Kim noted that the transition to electric vehicles is the future and the U.S. EV market is the key market among all automotive markets.
- Over the past 10 years, Woory has successfully grown into a specialized EV component company through continuous innovation.
- Georgia will be a good opportunity for Woory to take another step forward as part of the vanguard of EV development.
- The company is confident its experience and technology will contribute to the prosperity of the community and appreciates the partnership of the State of Georgia and the City of Dublin.
- State officials prioritize attracting projects like Woory's first U.S. home in Dublin, Georgia, because they create jobs and opportunities in rural communities.
- Georgia has invested for decades to match competitive projects with communities and sites that are prepared for development.
- Officials congratulated Dublin-Laurens County on bringing these e-mobility jobs of the future to middle Georgia.
- Dublin and Laurens County have a rapidly growing automotive sector thanks to their geographic location on Interstate 16.
- The region has access to a skilled and trained workforce.
- Woory's partnership with local education systems and training providers will ensure training programs are in place to help fill Woory’s talent pipeline for years to come.
