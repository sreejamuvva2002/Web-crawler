# Alvin W. Vogtle Electric Generating Plant

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2023-07-31

**Source:** [Plant Vogtle: Alvin W. Vogtle Nuclear Power Plant in Georgia](https://www.southernnuclear.com/our-plants/plant-vogtle.html)  ·  `page_012749`

## Facts

- Plant Vogtle is located in Waynesboro, Georgia, specifically in Burke County near Waynesboro along the Savannah River.
- The facility features four reactors and is co-owned by Georgia Power, Oglethorpe Power Corporation, Municipal Electric Authority of Georgia, and Dalton Utilities.
- It is one of Georgia Power's two nuclear facilities and one of three nuclear facilities in the Southern Company system.
- Unit 1 began commercial operation in 1987.
- Unit 2 began commercial operation in 1989.
- Unit 3 entered commercial operation on July 31, 2023.
- Unit 4 entered commercial operation on April 29, 2024.
- Units 3 and 4 are the first new nuclear units to be constructed in the U.S. in the last 30 years.
- The plant is now the largest generator of clean energy in the country.
- Units 1 and 2 utilize Westinghouse 4-loop Pressurized Water Reactors.
- Units 3 and 4 utilize Westinghouse AP1000 2-loop Pressurized Water Reactors.
- The total generating capacity is approximately 4,800 megawatts.
- Units 1 and 2 each generate around 1,150 MW of power.
- Units 3 and 4 each generate around 1,117 MW of power.
- Over 1,700 people oversee the plant's safe and reliable operations 24 hours a day, 365 days a year.
- The workforce includes engineers, mechanics, control room operators, chemists, electricians, security officers, and others.
- Plant Vogtle has been a certified wildlife habitat by the Wildlife Habitat Council since 1993.
- The site is situated on approximately 3,000 acres, with hundreds of acres replanted with loblolly and native longleaf pines.
- Management programs enhance habitats for species such as bluebirds, wood ducks, wild turkey, and the red-cockaded woodpecker.
- The plant generates electricity through a fission process where the nucleus of an atom splits into smaller parts, causing a chain reaction that releases heat.
- Water is pumped through the reactor core, heated by fission, circulated through steam generators, and returned to the reactor in a closed loop.
- Cooler water in the steam generator meets hot tubes to turn to steam, which spins turbine blades connected to an electric generator.
- The three water systems used to generate electricity are separate and do not mix with each other.
- Nuclear power plants at this facility have minimal environmental impact and produce carbon-free energy around the clock.
- The plant's ability to generate consistent power contributes to reducing greenhouse gas emissions and strengthening U.S. energy independence.
- Safety systems include defense-in-depth designs with multiple passive and active systems to protect the reactor and surrounding public.
- Passive safety barriers include fuel zirconium alloy cladding, reactor vessel thickness, and concrete containment.
- Active safety systems ensure continuous core cooling and safe plant shutdown, including a reactor protection system, multiple core cooling systems, and containment isolation systems.
- A person living within 50 miles of the plant receives an average of about 0.01 millirem of radiation per year.
- The facility is named for Alvin W. Vogtle Jr., past Chairman of the Board of Southern Company, who was elected President in 1969, CEO in 1970, and Chairman in 1983.
- Alvin W. Vogtle Jr. was a prisoner of war during World War II and escaped six times before reaching Switzerland.
