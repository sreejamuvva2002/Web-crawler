# Middle Georgia Megasite

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2022-06-16

**Source:** [Governor Kemp Announces Purchase of Middle Georgia Megasite | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-releases/2022/governor-kemp-announces-purchase-middle-georgia-megasite-peach-county)  ·  `page_003793`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced the purchase of a more than 1,100-acre economic development site in Peach County on June 16, 2022.
- The Middle Georgia Megasite was acquired through a partnership between the State of Georgia and the Development Authority of Peach County.
- The site is strategically located adjacent to GA Hwy 96 with immediate access via Interstate 75 to multiple major metro areas across the American South and Midwest.
- The site is less than three hours by truck from the Port of Savannah, which is the single-largest and fastest-growing container terminal in the U.S. and has two Class I rail facilities on-site.
- Both the Port of Savannah and Port of Brunswick can be reached in less than 5 hours by rail.
- Rail service to the site is provided by Norfolk Southern Class I rail on its north side and an additional rail line on its west side, creating multiple access and design possibilities for loading cars.
- The site is a 90-minute drive from Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport, which is described as the busiest and most efficient airport in the world.
- Two regional airports are located within 25 minutes of the Middle Georgia Megasite.
- Industrial utilities are adjacent to the site, and extensive due diligence reports have been completed to reduce barriers to speedy operations.
- The purchase was funded using proceeds from the sale of the Chatham County Economic Development Site, which Amazon purchased in 2021 for a new major distribution facility.
- The Middle Georgia Megasite includes four parcels of land, with approximately 1,100 acres designated as developable.
- Martin Moseley, Chairman of the Peach County Board of Commissioners, stated that the megasite will position the Middle Georgia region for significant investment and new jobs, calling it transformative for a rural community.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson noted that preparing sites of this scale to meet company timelines keeps Georgia competitive and creates a ripple effect of growth across county lines.
- Governor Kemp emphasized that Georgia’s superior infrastructure, including its robust highway system, rail lines, record-setting port, and top-ranked airport, makes the state a competitive choice for companies worldwide.
