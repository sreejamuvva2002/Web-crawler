# Archer Aviation Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Archer Aviation selects site in Georgia to build eVTOL manufacturing facility | CompositesWorld](https://www.compositesworld.com/news/archer-aviation-selects-site-in-georgia-to-build-evtol-manufacturing-facility)  ·  `page_001660`

## Facts

- Electric vertical takeoff and landing (eVTOL) aircraft manufacturer Archer Aviation Inc. (Santa Clara, Calif., U.S.) has announced plans to locate its eVTOL manufacturing facility in Covington, Ga., U.S., at a site adjacent to the Covington Municipal Airport.
- Archer plans to initially build out a 350,000-square-foot facility on a 96-acre site capable of producing up to 650 aircraft per year.
- The planned eVTOL aircraft manufacturing facility is expected to create more than 1,000 jobs over the long term.
- Construction is expected to be completed in the first half of 2024 with initial production expected to begin in the second half of 2024.
- The facility will be capable of being expanded by an additional 550,000 square feet, which is estimated to support production of up to 2,300 aircraft per year.
- In selecting the location for its first manufacturing facility, Archer completed a comprehensive survey of potential sites across the U.S.
- Key considerations for site selection included the availability of talent in the local labor market, utility availability, ability to conduct seamless flight test operations, construction costs and logistics.
- Archer will receive an incentive package that includes land conveyance, tax incentives and a Georgia REBA grant.
- The company anticipates that financing for the project will be provided by Synovus, the largest bank headquartered in the state.
- Adam Goldstein, Archer’s CEO, stated that the U.S. has long led the world in aerospace technology and Georgia has played a vital role in that industry.
- Adam Goldstein noted that their eVTOL technology can transform how urban and rural communities live and commute and that the factory can create pathways to highly skilled manufacturing jobs and other ladders of social and economic opportunity.
- Home to more than 800 aerospace companies, Georgia maintains a robust aerospace education pipeline with a dozen high schools that offer training in the field, five technical colleges with aviation programs and several universities offering degrees in aerospace engineering.
- The Georgia Center of Innovation for Aerospace, a strategic arm of the Georgia Department of Economic Development, has been cultivating an ecosystem of opportunities for innovative companies like Archer for more than 10 years.
