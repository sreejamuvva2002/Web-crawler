# Rivian Automotive

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-17

**Source:** [It’s ‘do or die’ as Rivian breaks ground on a $5B Ga. plant](https://www.wrdw.com/2025/09/17/its-do-or-die-electric-vehicle-maker-rivian-it-breaks-ground-5-billion-plant/)  ·  `page_000746`

## Facts

- Rivian breaks ground on a long-delayed $5 billion electric vehicle plant in Georgia east of Atlanta.
- Construction begins despite President Donald Trump's successful push to roll back electric vehicle tax credits.
- Starting Sept 30, buyers will no longer qualify for savings of up to $7,500 per car.
- Rivian is a money-losing California-based company.
- The Georgia plant was first announced in 2021 and is key to Rivian reaching profitability.
- Rivian currently manufactures the high-end R1T pickup truck and R1S SUV in Normal, Illinois, as well as delivery vans for Amazon and others.
- R1T prices start at $71,000.
- The Illinois plant will begin producing smaller R2 SUVs next year with prices starting at $45,000.
- An expanded Illinois plant will be able to assemble 215,000 vehicles yearly.
- Rivian's Georgia operation will be able to make 200,000 vehicles yearly starting in 2028.
- The company plans another 200,000 in capacity in phase two to spread fixed costs over more vehicles.
- These projections represent a leap from the 40,000 to 46,000 vehicles Rivian expects to deliver this year, down from 52,000 last year.
- The company is limiting production now in part to launch 2026 models.
- Alex Oyler, North American director of auto research firm SBD Automotive, stated that for Rivian it is do-or-die time and scale is key to profitability.
- EV sales growth in the United States rose only 1.5% in 2025's first half according to Cox Automotive.
- Tesla accounted for almost 45% of U.S. electric vehicle sales in that period, while General Motors climbed to 13%.
- Rivian held a 3% share in the first half of the year, behind Tesla and six traditional automakers.
- Excluding Tesla, Rivian is the most successful startup automaker.
- Rivian initially targeted demand for electric pickups and SUVs but now faces competition from Ford’s F-150 Lightning and the electric Chevrolet Silverado.
- After an initial public offering in 2021, Rivian shares have fallen by more than 80%.
- Rivian lost $1.66 billion in 2025's first half.
- Some automakers are cooling on EVs, including Stellantis canceling Ram’s electric truck program, Ford delaying production at a new Tennessee plant, and GM abandoning plans for a suburban Detroit plant.
- Sam Fiorani, vice president at AutoForecast Solutions, noted that slowing EV growth and competition do not play in Rivian's favor but affirmed an EV market still exists.
- Georgia has pledged $1.5 billion in incentives to Rivian in exchange for 7,500 jobs paying at least $56,000 a year on average.
- Rivian cannot benefit from most incentives unless it meets employment goals.
- The state is already spending $175 million to buy and grade land and improve roads for the project.
- Georgia Republican Gov. Brian Kemp wants to make Georgia the electric mobility capital of America and remains confident Rivian can fulfill its promises despite facing bumps.
- Two separate EV makers hoping to assemble vehicles in a former GM plant in Lordstown, Ohio, went bankrupt.
- Georgia’s Hyundai complex near Savannah is producing vehicles, though a battery plant there was delayed by ICE arresting 475 people on site, including over 300 South Koreans.
- Rivian was supposed to be making trucks by now at the 2,000-acre site near Social Circle, about 45 miles east of Atlanta.
- The company paused construction in 2024 as it burned through cash.
- German automaker Volkswagen agreed to invest $5.8 billion in Rivian in exchange for software and electrical technology.
- Then-President Joe Biden’s administration in November agreed to loan Rivian $6.6 billion to build the Georgia plant.
- Rivian hopes the U.S. Department of Energy will distribute the loan money, arguing it will boost domestic manufacturing.
- Some neighbors oppose the plant, citing concerns about pollution and inappropriate proximity to farms.
- Resident Eddie Clay, who lives less than a mile away, reported his well water turned mud-choked after excavation at the site.
- Rivian faces additional challenges including tariffs costing $2,000 per vehicle.
- The Trump administration ending a tax-credit program will cost the company $140 million in revenue this year.
- Long-term threats include low-priced, cutting-edge Chinese EVs.
- Chief Policy Officer Alan Hoffman stated Rivian is in it for the long haul and believes it can compete with anyone and excel when given the opportunity.
