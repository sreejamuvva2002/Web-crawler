# SK On

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [SK On Nears Additional ESS Orders as Georgia LFP Line Prepares for October Start < Battery < 기사본문 - The Elec Inc.](https://www.thelec.net/news/articleView.html?idxno=6492)  ·  `page_001121`

## Facts

- SK On is close to securing additional large-scale energy storage system (ESS) orders in North America.
- SK On is accelerating preparations for mass production of ESS batteries.
- According to industry sources on April 10, SK On is in talks with multiple customers for supply deals totaling several tens of gigawatt-hours (GWh).
- There is a high likelihood of contracts being finalized.
- SK On entered the ESS market later than rivals LG Energy Solution and Samsung SDI.
- SK On has begun to gain traction in the ESS market since the second half of last year.
- SK On is building a lithium iron phosphate (LFP) battery production line for ESS at its standalone plant in Georgia (SKBA).
- Mass production at the Georgia plant is scheduled to begin in October.
- Key material supplies for the Georgia plant are expected to start in early second half.
- A source familiar with SK On's ESS business said the company is actively negotiating with multiple U.S.-based firms.
- Demand for domestically produced batteries is rising rapidly due to proposed legislation restricting imports of Chinese-made ESS products.
- Demand is also rising due to the impact of investment tax credits (ITC).
- Existing supply chains are struggling to keep pace with demand growth.
- SK On has established its ESS supply chain framework ahead of production.
- Major material suppliers are set to begin deliveries between late May and early June.
- Cathode supplier is L&F.
- Separator maker is WCP.
- Electrolyte provider is Enchem.
- Chinese suppliers such as BTR may be used for anode materials.
- SK On has completed the configuration of ESS systems.
- SK On is working with DeltaX on battery module design.
- Key components such as structures and containerized direct current (DC) blocks are being supplied in partnership with Seojin System.
- In February, SK On signed a procurement contract worth about 2 trillion won with Seojin System for ESS components through 2030.
- SK On is applying electrochemical impedance spectroscopy (EIS) technology to detect battery anomalies at an early stage.
- SK On's battery management system (BMS) is developed by Hunate.
- Key diagnostic semiconductors are sourced from Analog Devices.
- SK On's ESS order pipeline includes a global target of 20 GWh for 2026.
- SK On has secured a supply agreement of up to 7.2 GWh with U.S. renewable energy developer Flatiron Energy Development.
- SK On secured 284 megawatts (MW) in South Korea's second centralized ESS contract market.
- SK On secured 60 megawatt-hours (MWh) for a carbon-neutral industrial complex in Gumi.
- SK On secured 500 MWh for a distributed energy project in Busan.
- SK On's first North American ESS contract came in September last year with Flatiron for 1 GWh.
- The Flatiron contract was later expanded to a potential 7.2 GWh over four years.
- Domestically, SK On secured 284 MW—about 50.2% of total capacity—in a 565 MW ESS bidding program overseen by the Korea Power Exchange.
- SK On secured government-backed projects in Gumi and Busan.
- Beyond the Georgia plant, SK On plans to expand ESS production capacity.
- SK On's Seosan Plant 2 will convert two electric vehicle battery lines, totaling 3 GWh, for ESS production.
- Mass production at Seosan Plant 2 is scheduled for early next year.
- SK On plans to utilize its Tennessee facility to meet North American demand.
- The Tennessee facility was secured after restructuring the BlueOval SK joint venture with Ford.
- SK On is expected to post an operating loss of around 300 billion won in the first quarter.
- The operating loss is due to costs associated with slowing electric vehicle demand and production restructuring.
- Earnings improvement is anticipated in the second half.
- Earnings improvement will be supported by increased EV sales from key customers Hyundai Motor and Kia.
- Earnings improvement will also be supported by the ramp-up of ESS production lines.
