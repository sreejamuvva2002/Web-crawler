# Aurubis AG

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-24

**Source:** [Aurubis Opens New Smelter in Augusta, GA | K2 Castings](https://www.k2castings.com/aurubis-opens-new-smelter-in-augusta-ga/)  ·  `page_017365`

## Facts

- Aurubis AG, one of the largest copper recyclers worldwide, announced the opening of its Aurubis Richmond (Augusta, GA) facility September 24, 2025.
- The facility is the first U.S. multimetal recycling and smelter plant of its kind.
- The facility is already in operation and is producing key strategic metals such as copper, nickel, tin, and precious metals for American manufacturers.
- Naveed Moghadam, Commercial Director at Aurubis, stated at ReMA’s September 2025 Copper Roundtable that the company wants to be a first mover against its competition.
- Moghadam stated, “We see the U.S. as a key and growing market where development is happening.”
- Moghadam added, “We invested in the site because we wanted to be one of the first players to support manufacturing being reshored to North America.”
- A company press release noted that the demand for strategic metals, such as copper, is rising steadily worldwide.
- American industry alone currently needs about 1.8 million tons of copper per year.
- Experts estimate demand will rise nearly 30% in the next five years.
- As of now, the US imports about half of the copper it processes.
- Multimetal recycling can close this gap quickly, and as the first and most technologically advanced secondary smelter, Aurubis Richmond reinforces the independence of American supply chains by processing complex recycling materials.
- The new facility is the nation’s largest and most advanced secondary smelter and will provide metals for AI and data centers, energy infrastructure, electrification, and defense.
- The company invested $800M in building the new site.
- The company has added 240 high-quality industrial jobs to the local area.
- ReMA Copper Roundtable participants agreed that copper is a critical material for the future and that demand is great.
- A January 26, 2025 copper update noted that AI and data centers are two main drivers of copper demand.
- The International Energy Agency (IEA) projects that electricity consumption from data centers is expected to double by 2026.
- David Brooks, Global Head of Metals at Argus Media, said while EV sales in the U.S. increased 7.3% in 2024, consumers have been slower to adopt these vehicles than the rest of the world.
- According to the IEA’s “Trends in electric car markets” report, global electric car sales exceeded 17 million in 2024, totaling 20% of new car market.
- Global EV sales were 25% higher in 2024 than 2023.
- Approximately 1.3 million EVs were sold in the U.S. during 2024, a 7.3 increase over the previous year and roughly 12% of global sales.
- On June 30, the White House announced tariffs on all copper products, excluding copper cathodes, which the market needs.
- David Brooks stated that the tariff on copper products is designed to encourage new mills in the U.S. and more domestic production.
