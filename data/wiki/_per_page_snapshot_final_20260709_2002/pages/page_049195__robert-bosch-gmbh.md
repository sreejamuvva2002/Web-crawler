# Robert Bosch GmbH

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- Robert Bosch GmbH is a German company founded in 1886 by Robert Bosch to repair electrical equipment.
- The company is Europe’s largest auto-parts brand manufacturer and one of the world’s leading makers of auto ignition, fuel injection, and antilock braking systems.
- Bosch also produces industrial hydraulic and pneumatic equipment, telecommunications equipment and systems, power tools, household appliances, radios, television sets, and audio-visual equipment.
- The company's headquarters are located in Stuttgart.
- Subsidiaries of the company operate throughout Europe, North and South America, Asia, and Australia.
- Bosch’s magneto and his partner’s spark plug became the basis for the company’s line of auto parts.
- The company began selling auto parts to the American automotive market around 1900.
- By 1914, two-thirds of the company’s sales were in the United States.
- The majority of Bosch’s American businesses were expropriated by the U.S. government in 1918 during World War I.
- Bosch’s American operations were taken over again during World War II.
- By the 1950s, the company had resumed sales to American and other foreign automotive makers.
- In the 1950s, Bosch began diversifying into household appliances, power tools, and hydraulic equipment.
- Advanced fuel-injection systems developed by Bosch engineers from 1949 were widely adopted by automakers in the 1970s and ’80s for reducing fuel consumption and minimizing exhaust pollution.
- Antilock braking systems pioneered by Bosch came into wide use on European and American cars during the 1980s and ’90s.
- In the early 1990s, the company experienced a decline in revenues accompanied by large layoffs.
- The company subsequently regained its financial health after the early 1990s revenue decline.
- Bosch’s stock was publicly held between 1917 and 1937.
- The company is now primarily owned by the Robert Bosch Foundation.
