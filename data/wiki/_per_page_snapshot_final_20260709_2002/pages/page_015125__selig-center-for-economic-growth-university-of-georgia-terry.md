# Selig Center for Economic Growth, University of Georgia Terry College of Business

**Type:** research_center  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-01-23

**Source:** [2024 Overall U.S. and Georgia Economic Outlook | CAES Field Report](https://fieldreport.caes.uga.edu/publications/AP130-2-01/2024-overall-u-s-and-georgia-economic-outlook/)  ·  `page_015125`

## Facts

- Electric vehicle manufacturers are building large facilities in Georgia.
- Reshoring production has helped make supply chains more secure.
- Reshoring is especially important to industries favored by recent shifts in U.S. industrial policies.
- More emphasis on near sourcing and friend sourcing will help reduce supply chain stress and improve the prospects for the growth of Georgia’s large transportation and logistics industry.
- A substantial increase in U.S. vehicle sales is expected, which bodes well for Georgia’s vehicle and vehicle parts manufacturing industries.
- Georgia’s economic developers broke records for the 3rd year in a row, landing 426 projects in fiscal year 2023 compared to the record-setting 358 projects announced in 2022.
- Site selection professionals ranked Georgia as the No. 1 state for business for the 10th consecutive year in 2023.
