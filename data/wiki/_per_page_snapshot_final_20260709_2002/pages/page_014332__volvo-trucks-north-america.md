# Volvo Trucks North America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Volvo Trucks leads heavy-duty electrification](https://www.volvotrucks.us/news-and-stories/press-releases/2025/october/volvo-trucks-leads-heavy-duty-electrification/)  ·  `page_014332`

## Facts

- Volvo Trucks North America continues to lead the transition to electromobility.
- More than 700 Volvo VNR Electric trucks are now operating across the United States and Canada.
- These vehicles have logged more than 20 million zero-tailpipe-emission miles.
- This eliminates an estimated 34,000 metric tons of CO₂, equivalent to taking more than 7,000 passenger cars off the road for a full year.
- Since launching its first electric trucks in 2019, Volvo has delivered more than 5,700 electric vehicles across 50 countries.
- These globally delivered vehicles have collectively driven 250 million kilometers (155 million miles).
- Volvo Trucks maintains an average of over 30% market share in the North American electric truck segment over the past five years.
- Peter Voorhoeve serves as president of Volvo Trucks North America.
- The Certified EV Dealer network has expanded to 83 locations across 33 U.S. states and four Canadian provinces.
- Georgia is included among the 33 U.S. states with certified dealer locations.
- Four new states recently added to the certified dealer network are Colorado, Louisiana, Maryland, and Nevada.
- Four new dealer groups have joined the program.
- Another 13 dealer locations are currently in the certification pipeline.
- Certified dealers complete extensive EV training and invest in facility upgrades to deliver full sales and service support for battery-electric trucks.
- Recent certified dealer locations include Bergey’s Truck Centers (West Milton, PA and Carlisle, PA), Kriete Truck Center (Franksville, WI), Nextran Truck Center (Commerce City, CO), Old River Truck Sales (Saint Rose, LA), Potomac Truck Center (Hyattsville, MD), TEC Equipment (Las Vegas, NV), Transource Inc. (Raleigh, NC), and Worldwide Equipment (Cincinnati, OH).
- Volvo Trucks offers eight fully electric truck models globally: FL Electric, FE Electric, FM Electric, FM Low Entry, FMX Electric, FH Electric, FH Aero Electric, and VNR Electric.
- These electric models serve diverse applications including urban distribution, waste management, regional haul, and construction.
- The $90 million public-private partnership known as the Volvo LIGHTS Project laid the foundation for widespread adoption of heavy-duty battery-electric trucks.
- Volvo Trucks is a key partner in California’s Joint Electric Truck Scaling Initiative (JETSI), which deployed Class 8 battery-electric trucks across Southern California.
- Volvo Trucks North America is headquartered in Greensboro, North Carolina.
- Uptime Services are delivered by a network of nearly 400 authorized dealers across North America and the 24/7 Volvo Trucks Uptime Center.
- Every Volvo truck is assembled at the Volvo Trucks New River Valley manufacturing facility in Dublin, Virginia.
- The Dublin, Virginia manufacturing facility meets ISO 9001 standards for quality and ISO 14001 standards for environmental care.
- The Dublin, Virginia facility holds dual ISO 50001/Superior Energy Performance certification at the platinum level for energy management.
- Volvo Trucks provides complete transport solutions via a global dealer network with 2,200 service points in about 130 countries.
- Volvo trucks are assembled in 12 countries across the globe.
- In 2025, approximately 120,000 Volvo trucks were delivered worldwide.
- Volvo Trucks is part of the Volvo Group, which also provides financing and service solutions.
