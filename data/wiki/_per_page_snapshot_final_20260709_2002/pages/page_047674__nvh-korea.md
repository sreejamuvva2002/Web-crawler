# NVH Korea

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-07

**Source:** [S Korean firm plans $72M Georgia plant to make battery parts, hiring 140 | World News - Business Standard](https://www.business-standard.com/world-news/s-korean-firm-plans-72m-georgia-plant-to-make-battery-parts-hiring-140-123062300096_1.html)  ·  `page_047674`

## Facts

- NVH Korea announced plans to invest $72 million in a new manufacturing plant located in Locust Grove, south of Atlanta.
- The company plans to hire 140 workers for the new facility.
- The plant will manufacture sensors, connections, and protectors specifically for electric vehicle batteries.
- Operations at the new Georgia plant are scheduled to begin in 2024.
- Chairman and CEO Ja Kyum Koo stated that producing electric battery components marks NVH Korea's first venture into this sector.
- NVH Korea is headquartered in Ulsan, South Korea.
- The Locust Grove facility will be the 10th supplier to locate in the region following Hyundai's 2022 announcement of a $5.5 billion electric vehicle and battery assembly plant in Ellabell, Georgia, near Savannah.
- Hyundai's Ellabell site is projected to eventually grow to 8,100 employees.
- Hyundai's Ellabell plant is slated to begin producing vehicles in 2025.
- Suppliers for the regional automotive ecosystem have collectively pledged to invest nearly $2.1 billion.
- Regional suppliers have pledged to hire nearly 4,900 people.
- The Locust Grove project marks NVH Korea's second plant in Georgia.
- NVH Korea's subsidiary, AFS America, is located in Columbus and manufactures floor mats and cargo trays for Hyundai's Montgomery, Alabama plant, Kia's West Point, Georgia plant, and General Motors.
- NVH Korea operates a total of 19 plants worldwide across South Korea, North America, China, India, Europe, and Russia.
- The company's broader product portfolio includes sound-dampening parts and materials, floor mats, headliners, insulators, and components for managing engine and cooling system temperatures.
- The state of Georgia will fund the training of NVH Korea's new workforce.
- NVH Korea may qualify for up to $1.2 million in Georgia state income tax credits, calculated at $1,750 per job over a five-year period.
- Eligibility for the state income tax credits requires employees to earn a minimum annual wage of $31,300.
- The company may also be eligible for additional incentives, including property tax breaks from the city of Locust Grove and Henry County.
