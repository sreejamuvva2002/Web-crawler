# Joon Georgia Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2022-11-14

**Source:** [With Plans to Invest $317M and Hire 630, First Hyundai Supplier Is No Stranger to the Southeast - Global Atlanta](https://www.globalatlanta.com/with-plans-to-invest-317m-and-hire-630-first-hyundai-supplier-is-no-stranger-to-the-southeast/)  ·  `page_000602`

## Facts

- Joon Georgia Inc. is the first announced supplier for Hyundai’s new electric-vehicle and battery plant in Bryan County.
- Joon Georgia Inc. said it would invest $317 million.
- Joon Georgia Inc. said it would hire 630 people.
- The investment and hiring will take place in Bulloch County, where Statesboro is the county seat.
- Joon Georgia Inc. is a subsidiary of Korea’s Ajin Industrial Co. Ltd.
- In 2008, the company set up shop near the Georgia-Alabama border to supply body components to Hyundai’s landmark Montgomery factory.
- Ajin USA’s first plant is located in Cusseta, Ala., along the Korean automotive corridor that emerged between Montgomery and the Kia Motors plant in West Point, Ga.
- The first plant operated under the name Joon LLC, churning out stamped metal assemblies and automotive electronic components.
- Joon Georgia's announcement came two weeks after groundbreaking on the $5.54 billion Hyundai Metaplant.
- The Hyundai Metaplant will include a battery joint venture.
- The Hyundai Metaplant is reportedly considering assembling multiple models in the Hyundai, Kia and Genesis electric-vehicle lineup.
- Joon Georgia became the first in what is expected to be a wave of Korean suppliers headed to coastal Georgia.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Hyundai Motor Group, its suppliers, and the Savannah area joint development authority are creating generational opportunities for communities in Georgia.
- Total supplier investment factored in for the new plant is $1.1 billion.
- Hyundai said the new plant will bring about 8,100 jobs for Georgians.
- The Hyundai Metaplant will sit on a 2,923-acre site jointly purchased by the state of Georgia and the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority.
- The Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority includes the counties of Bryan, Bulloch, Chatham and Effingham.
- Clearing on the Hyundai Metaplant site is nearing completion.
- Construction on the Hyundai Metaplant is set to begin in January.
- Hyundai plans to begin making vehicles at the site by 2025, if not sooner.
- Ajin’s new site will be located in the Bruce Yawn Commerce Park in Statesboro.
- Georgia Quick Start, the state’s workforce training agency, is set to assist the company with a customized training package to fill available positions.
- Hiring for Joon Georgia Inc. is expected to begin in the middle of next year, a year before the planned start of production.
- Ajin Industrial Co. Ltd. is based in Gyeongsan, just outside of the metropolitan city of Daegu.
- Daegu is an auto parts powerhouse in the southern part of Korea and a sister city of Atlanta.
- Ajin USA CEO Jung Ho Sea stated his company’s commitment is to not only be the best parts supplier for automotive and EV companies like Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America, but to also be the best employer and corporate citizen and give back to the great community of Bulloch County and the City of Statesboro.
