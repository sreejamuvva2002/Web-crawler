# PIOLAX, INC.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [PIOLAX, INC. Business Report FY2008 - MarkLines Automotive Industry Portal](https://www.marklines.com/en/top500/cf/s500_489_hl2008)  ·  `page_048377`

## Facts

- PIOLAX, INC. reported FY2008 sales of 40,677 million JPY, representing a 16.7% decrease from FY2007.
- PIOLAX, INC. reported FY2008 operating income of 838 million JPY, a 79.4% decrease from FY2007.
- PIOLAX, INC. reported FY2008 ordinary income of 1,210 million JPY, a 75.2% decrease from FY2007.
- PIOLAX, INC. reported FY2008 current net income of 500 million JPY, an 84.8% decrease from FY2007.
- The company acquired a new order from Ford Motor Company for pickup truck components.
- PIOLAX is sending engineers to Ford R&D departments to win more orders by directly engaging in development activities.
- The company will begin supplying fasteners to clip both exterior and interior parts to bodies of new pickup trucks planned by Ford for release in 2010.
- Fastener production for Ford will begin in the latter half of 2009 at the company's Thai plant.
- PIOLAX spent 515 million JPY on overall R&D in FY2008, with 396 million JPY allocated to automotive products.
- R&D activities are conducted by the design department, development groups in each SBU, and the development division of subsidiary Pilolax Medical Devices, Inc.
- The company conducts joint development with universities and external research institutes.
- PIOLAX developed compressed coil spring materials used in engines and gears, commercializing products that are more compact and lighter in weight.
- The company strengthened development activities on complex springs to enable easier installation.
- PIOLAX worked on developing surface coatings for spring materials that do not contain hexavalent chromium.
- The company completed development of hexavalent chromium free surface treatment, complying with EU directives on end-of-life vehicles and restriction of hazardous substances in electrical equipment.
- PIOLAX is working to make products meet Japan Automobile Manufacturers Association restrictions on environmentally hazardous substances including hexavalent chromium, vinyl chloride, and VOC.
- The company started development activities on products that comply with the new REACH Regulation in Europe.
- PIOLAX is developing versatile fasteners for thin and thick metal plates, fasteners requiring less installation force, fasteners with powerful fastening capabilities, and fasteners that are easier to disassemble during vehicle scrapping.
- The company is considering migrating to materials that emit less odor in vehicle interiors while developing environmentally friendly products that enhance interior comfort.
- PIOLAX is currently developing high-strength, user-friendly net hooks used in car interior parts.
- The company developed side locks for glove boxes released in January 2003, which are now being equipped on an increasing number of vehicle models to improve performance and workability.
- PIOLAX worked on developing new dampers.
- The company is currently developing hinge parts to open/close the console box lid and floor board in the rear luggage compartment.
- In fuel systems, PIOLAX enhanced the performance and reduced manufacturing costs of rollover valves and inlet check valves for plastic tanks.
- PIOLAX licensed-in patent use for automotive fuel delivery components and plastic fuel tank components from Norma Germany GmbH under contracts with terms spanning from 1995 through 2006, renewing annually.
- The company licensed-out manufacturing technology for metallic and plastic fasteners to Piolax of Taiwan Inc. from October 1987 through October 1992, renewable every 10 years.
- PIOLAX licensed-out manufacturing technology for pressure springs, torsion springs, and valve springs to Taiwan Houmu Industrial Co., Ltd. from November 1978 through October 1983, renewable every 5 years.
- The company licensed-out technology, engineering, and marketing services for precision plastic slips, precision springs, and related assembly products to Piolax Corp.(USA) for an indefinite period starting April 1, 1993.
- PIOLAX licensed-out similar technology, engineering, and marketing services to Piolax Ltd. (UK) for an indefinite period starting August 8, 1995.
- The company licensed-out related services to Piolax Co., Ltd. (Korea) from June 20, 1995 through June 19, 2006, renewable each year.
- PIOLAX licensed-out similar services to Piolax (Thailand) Ltd. for an indefinite period starting August 10, 2000.
- The company licensed-out related services to Dongguan Piolax Co., Ltd. from July 1, 2004 through June 30, 2014, renewable each year.
- PIOLAX spent 2,876 million JPY on capital investment for automotive products in FY2008.
- The company spent a total of 1,805 million yen in Japan and 1,129 million yen outside Japan on capital investments.
- Piolax Corp. established a Head Office/Plant in Georgia, USA, focused on production facilities and dies.
- The expected investment for the Georgia facility is 295 thousand yen.
- Construction of the Georgia facility started in January 2009 and is expected to complete in December 2010.
- Other new automobile-related facilities under development include locations in Yokohama City, Tochigi Prefecture, Fuji City, Fukuoka Prefecture, Inchon, Rayong, and Dongguan, all starting construction in April or January 2009 with completion expected in March or December 2010.
