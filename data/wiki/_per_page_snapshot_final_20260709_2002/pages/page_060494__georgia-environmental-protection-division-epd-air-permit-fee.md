# Georgia Environmental Protection Division (EPD) Air Permit Fee Structure

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2025-07-01

**Source:** [Big Increase in Georgia EPD's Air Permit Fees - Trinity Consultants](https://trinityconsultants.com/resources/big-increase-in-georgia-epds-air-permit-fees/)  ·  `page_060494`

## Facts

- Effective July 1, 2025, the Georgia Environmental Protection Division (EPD) will implement significant increases in both air permit application fees and annual operating permit emissions fees.
- The fee adjustments are applicable to the 2024 calendar year emissions data.
- EPD states these adjustments address funding shortfalls resulting from the retirement of several large facilities.
- The adjustments aim to ensure the continued effectiveness of the state’s air quality programs.
- The revised fee structure aims to cover reasonable costs associated with reviewing and acting upon permit applications, implementing and enforcing permit terms, and other related activities.
- Increased annual air permit fees for calendar year 2024 emissions are due September 2, 2025.
- Flat annual permit fees represent a 30% increase from the previous year.
- Per-ton-based annual permit fees represent an approximate 20% increase from the previous year.
- The updated NSPS Fee is $2,470.
- The updated Synthetic Minor Source Fees is $2,730.
- The updated Title V Sources – Maintenance Fee is $1,300.
- The updated Title V Source – Minimum Fee is $5,850.
- The updated Title V Sources per ton of emissions (PM, VOC, NOX, and SO2) for coal-fired electric generating units is $45.37.
- The updated Title V Sources per ton of emissions (PM, VOC, NOX, and SO2) for all other sources is $43.13.
- Permit application fees represent a 50% increase over the existing rates.
- The Generic (Minor or Synthetic Minor) Permit application fee is $0.
- The Title V Renewal application fee is $0.
- The Off-Permit Change Request application fee is $0.
- The No Permit Required Exemption application fee is $0.
- The Name / Ownership Change application fee is $750.
- The Minor Source Permit or Amendment application fee is $2,000.
- The Permit-by-Rule application fee is $2,000.
- The Synthetic Minor Source Permit or Amendment application fee is $3,000.
- The Title V Minor Modification with Construction application fee is $6,000.
- The Title V Minor Modification without Construction application fee is $6,000.
- The Title V Significant Modification with Construction application fee is $6,000.
- The Title V Significant Modification without Construction application fee is $6,000.
- The Title V 503(b)(10) Permit Amendment application fee is $6,000.
- The Major Source Permit not PSD or 112(g) application fee is $6,000.
- The PSD Permit application fee is $22,500.
- The 112(g) Permit application fee is $22,500.
- EPD increased the expedited permit application fees earlier this year.
- Standard air permit application fees are increasing effective July 1, 2025, to support the costs associated with reviewing and processing permit applications.
- Application fees are assessed per application and are separate from annual emissions fees.
- Permittees subject to Title V (Part 70) permitting must calculate their annual fees based on the total tons of regulated air pollutants emitted, using the current per-ton fee rate set annually by EPD.
- Facilities must use the most recent emissions data and multiply the total eligible emissions by the published fee rate.
- Synthetic minor sources pay a flat annual fee.
- Title V sources also pay a maintenance fee in addition to their emissions-based fee.
- Certain NSPS sources pay a flat fee, in addition to any other fee.
- Reviewing fee calculations annually helps avoid discrepancies and potential enforcement actions when submitting emissions inventories or permit renewal documents.
- The cost of an expedited application for a synthetic minor permit amendment in July 2025 is $13,000, up from $7,000 a year ago.
- Applicants should not submit payment until prompted by EPD staff after application submission.
- Payments can be made via check or credit card through the Georgia Environmental Connection Online (GECO) system.
