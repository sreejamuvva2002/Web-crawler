# Hitachi Astemo Americas Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-07-11

**Source:** [page_002564](https://www.kentucky.gov/Pages/Activity-stream.aspx?n=GovernorBeshear&prId=1860)  ·  `page_002564`

## Facts

- Hitachi Astemo Americas Inc. announced an expansion of its operation in Madison County with a $153 million investment creating 167 new, quality Kentucky jobs.
- The project will see company leaders invest in the current manufacturing operation in Berea to support site improvements and renovations.
- Renovations include additional production lines and equipment in response to increased market demand and growth within the EV industry.
- Hitachi’s investment will create 167 full-time jobs for Kentuckians, bringing the company’s total Kentucky employment across the Harrodsburg and Berea facilities to over 2,100 workers.
- Tim Clark, president of Hitachi Astemo Americas, stated the announcement is a bold step in the direction of vehicle electrification.
- In January 2021, Hitachi Automotive Systems, Keihin, Showa and Nissin Kogyo completed a business merger to form the global mega-supplier Hitachi Astemo.
- The company provides advanced mobility solutions including electric powertrains for automobiles, advanced chassis, autonomous driving/advanced driver assistance systems, and systems for motorcycles.
- Headquartered in Tokyo, Japan, the company’s entry into the Americas began in 1972 with a subsidiary established in Brazil.
- Hitachi expanded into the U.S. market in the mid-1980s with its first factory in Kentucky.
- The company serves as a global mega-supplier to some of the largest automobile manufacturers in the world and has more than 20 manufacturing sites in the United States, Canada, Mexico and Brazil.
- Hitachi Astemo operates two locations in Kentucky.
- Madison County Judge/Executive Reagan Taylor expressed pleasure at the company's decision to expand its Berea facility.
- Berea Mayor Bruce Fraley noted the decision speaks to the talent of the local workforce and economic climate.
- Donna Angel, business development director for the Business and Economic Development Office of the city of Berea, highlighted the investment will bring quality jobs and economic sustainability to the community.
- Kentucky’s administration has seen nearly 600 private-sector new-location or expansion projects commit to invest over $22.9 billion and create more than 30,000 jobs.
- Gov. Andy Beshear announced there are more than 2 million jobs filled in Kentucky, the most in state history.
- 57,000 new jobs have been filled since the beginning of Gov. Beshear’s administration.
- Kentucky set a historic low unemployment rate of 3.7% on May 18.
- Since the beginning of his administration, Gov. Beshear has announced more than 860 private-sector new-location and expansion projects totaling $26.8 billion in announced investments.
- The average incentivized hourly wage in Kentucky in 2022 was $26.78 before benefits, an 11.5% increase over the previous year.
- Gov. Beshear and leaders from Ford Motor Co. and SK On celebrated a transformative $5.8 billion investment that will create 5,000 jobs in Hardin County in September 2021.
- Gov. Beshear joined leadership at AESC to announce a $2 billion investment that will create 2,000 jobs in Warren County in April 2022.
- These announcements solidified Kentucky as the EV battery production capital of the United States.
- In May 2023, Gov. Beshear and leaders at Toyota announced a $591 million investment across multiple projects as Georgetown introduces its first battery electric vehicle.
- In June, Gov. Beshear joined INFAC North America to break ground on a $53 million investment to support EV-related production in Campbellsville.
- KEDFA preliminarily approved a 10-year incentive agreement with Hitachi Astemo under the Kentucky Business Investment program in March 2023.
- The performance-based agreement can provide up to $2.4 million in tax incentives based on annual targets of maintaining 167 Kentucky-resident, full-time jobs for 10 years and paying an average hourly wage of $25.71 including benefits.
- KEDFA approved Hitachi Astemo for up to $200,000 in tax incentives through the Kentucky Enterprise Initiative Act (KEIA).
- KEIA allows approved companies to recoup Kentucky sales and use tax on construction costs, building fixtures, equipment used for research and development, and electronic processing.
- The company may claim eligible incentives against its income tax liability and/or wage assessments.
- Hitachi Astemo can receive resources from Kentucky’s workforce service providers, including no-cost recruitment and job placement services, reduced-cost customized training, and job-training incentives.
