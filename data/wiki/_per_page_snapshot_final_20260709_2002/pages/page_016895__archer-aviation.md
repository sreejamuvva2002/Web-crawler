# Archer Aviation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-06-20

**Source:** [Archer Aviation wants 'hundreds' of eVTOLs in the air by 2028 Olympics](https://interestingengineering.com/transportation/archer-aviation-evtols-2028-olympics)  ·  `page_016895`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation and Stellantis are ramping up production of their "Midnight" eVTOL air taxi to deploy "hundreds or thousands" of aircraft for the 2028 LA Olympics.
- Archer Aviation, a Silicon Valley-based electric vertical take-off and landing (eVTOL) vehicle developer, is moving at full speed toward full-scale production by the 2030s.
- The company and its main investor Stellantis unveiled the "Midnight" four-seater air taxi at this year's Paris air show.
- Archer's Midnight air taxi has a range of up to 87 nautical miles (161 kilometers) but is specifically optimized for flights between 17 and 43 nautical miles.
- The aircraft is engineered to reach speeds of up to 130 knots (241 kilometers per hour).
- Archer CEO and co-founder Adam Goldstein aims to have "hundreds or thousands" of eVTOL aircraft flying over the Los Angeles Olympic Games in 2028.
- According to a June 19th statement by Goldstein, operations will transition from only a few test flights per day to thousands per month.
- Stellantis has increased its strategic shareholding in Archer by purchasing Archer stock in the open market.
- The world's first high-volume eVTOL aircraft manufacturing facility is currently being constructed on an approximately 100-acre campus in Covington, Georgia.
- Stellantis CEO Carlos Tavares stated that both teams are moving full speed ahead to bring scalable manufacturing of Archer's aircraft online in mid-2024.
- Tavares noted the partnership is setting the standard for cross-industry collaboration and remains a key pillar of Stellantis' strategy to lead global mobility.
- Stellantis personnel are embedded across all areas of Archer's operations, including manufacturing, engineering, supply chain, quality, facilities, and human resources.
- Experienced personnel from Stellantis are working full-time with the Archer team to support operational readiness, focusing on manufacturing automation and component sourcing.
- The manufacturing facility is set to go online in mid-2024.
- Archer's manufacturing operations can produce 650 air taxis annually, with potential for expansion to support production of up to 2,300 aircraft annually.
- Both companies aim to become the world's top aircraft manufacturing facility in terms of volume.
- Tavares and Goldstein believe the necessary technology, public approval, and updated air traffic control infrastructure to integrate the aircraft into the National Airspace System will be available by the target timeline.
- Goldstein emphasized that high-volume manufacturing is critical to commercialization at scale and that partnering with Stellantis helps realize the opportunity to redefine urban transportation.
