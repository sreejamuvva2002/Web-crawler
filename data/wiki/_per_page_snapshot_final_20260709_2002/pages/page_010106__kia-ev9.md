# Kia EV9

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [The all-new, all-electric Kia EV9 | Kia Mall of Georgia](https://www.kiamallofga.com/EV9.htm)  ·  `page_010106`

## Facts

- The 2024 Kia EV9 lineup features EPA-estimated all-electric ranges of 230 miles for Light RWD, 304 miles for Light Long Range RWD, 280 miles for Wind AWD, 280 miles for Land AWD, and 270 miles for GT-Line AWD.
- The EV9 utilizes a purpose-built EV platform with an available 99.8 kWh battery and dual motor e-AWD.
- It supports charging from 10-80% in as little as 24 minutes using a 350kW DC fast charger.
- Kia has teamed up with ChargePoint to offer drivers an available Level 2 at-home charging system for an additional cost, backed by a 3-year limited warranty.
- The EV9 comes with 1,000 kWh of charging credits on the Electrify America network, which is equivalent to about 3,000 miles of driving range.
- An optional portable Webasto Go charger is available for an additional cost, fitting in the front storage area with adaptors for both 120V and 240V outlets.
- Starting MSRP for the Light RWD trim is $54,099, featuring a 76.1 kWh battery.
- Starting MSRP for the Light Long Range RWD trim is $59,200, featuring a 99.8 kWh battery.
- Starting MSRP for the Wind AWD trim is $63,900.
- Starting MSRP for the Land AWD trim is $69,900.
- Starting MSRP for the GT-Line AWD trim is $73,900.
- The vehicle offers up to 5,000 lbs. of towing capacity with a self-leveling rear suspension and dedicated trailering mode.
- It includes an onboard power generator delivering up to 1,920 watts for external power needs.
- Vehicle-to-home (V2H) backup power capabilities are planned for the future, allowing the vehicle to reverse energy flow to properly equipped homes.
- The EV9 features a heat pump to help preserve range in colder climates and a battery warmer to enhance charging performance.
- Over-the-air updates can be performed automatically to enhance infotainment features and battery management systems.
