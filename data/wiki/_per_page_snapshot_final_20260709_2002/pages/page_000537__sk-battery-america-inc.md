# SK Battery America Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-03-06

**Source:** [Nearly 1,000 workers laid off at Georgia battery plant as companies cancel EVs and lose incentives | Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/03/06/sk-battery-america-laid-off-nearly-1000-workers-georgia-electric-vehicles/?ref=biztoc.com)  ·  `page_000537`

## Facts

- SK Battery America Inc. laid off nearly 1,000 workers at its manufacturing plant northeast of Atlanta on Friday amid automakers’ changing electrification plans and uncertain consumer demand for EVs.
- Friday marked the last working day for exactly 958 plant employees, according to a Worker Adjustment and Retraining Notification (WARN) notice filed by human resources chief Chuck Moore.
- Impacted laid-off workers will be paid through May 6.
- SK Battery America Inc. opened its $2.6 billion battery manufacturing plant in Commerce, Georgia, in January 2022.
- The Commerce plant notably supplied batteries for the Ford F-150 Lightning electric pickup truck.
- Ford announced plans to cancel the fully electric version of the F-150 Lightning in December.
- The U.S. electric vehicle market is currently at a standstill as the Trump administration steers federal support away from electrification toward laxer automotive emissions policies and broader support for oil and gas industries.
- In December, Ford announced it would scrap the fully electric F-150 Lightning in favor of an extended-range version of the vehicle.
- SK Battery America Inc. and Ford had jointly invested $11.4 billion in U.S. battery plants before the battery maker ended their joint venture in December.
- SK Battery America Inc. has made significant investments in Jackson County, Georgia, in recent years as automakers planned billions for EV development and the Biden administration supported domestic EV supply chain efforts.
- In June 2020, SK Battery America Inc. announced plans to invest $940 million to expand its battery manufacturing presence in Atlanta.
- At the time of the 2020 expansion announcement, Governor Brian Kemp’s office stated it would create 600 jobs.
- SK Battery America Inc. and Hyundai are currently jointly building a $5 billion battery factory near Cartersville, Georgia (northwest of Atlanta).
- Georgia has attracted other major EV manufacturing investments, including Rivian’s $5 billion factory and Hyundai’s own $7.6 billion factory complex.
- EV demand has not met automakers' ambitious expectations, accounting for about 8% of new U.S. vehicle sales in 2025, matching the previous year's rate.
- Automakers are reevaluating multibillion-dollar electrification plans due to mounting financial losses and shifting consumer demand.
- Manufacturers including Ford, General Motors, Stellantis, and other EV supply chain companies have reneged on factory, investment, and product plans, laid off workers, and pivoted efforts toward hybrid and plug-in hybrid electric vehicles.
- Mainstream buyers are increasingly favoring hybrids and efficient gasoline-powered vehicles due to concerns over EV driving range and charging infrastructure availability.
- The U.S. Congress under President Donald Trump has eliminated consumer tax credits of up to $7,500 for purchasing new or used EVs.
- The Trump administration has announced plans to weaken federal fuel economy and greenhouse gas emissions rules for automakers, effectively eliminating federal incentives for producing cleaner vehicle fleets.
