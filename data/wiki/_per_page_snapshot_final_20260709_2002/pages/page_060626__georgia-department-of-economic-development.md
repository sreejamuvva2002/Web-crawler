# Georgia Department of Economic Development

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-08-29

**Source:** [Georgia Celebrates Fiscal Year 2024 Business Boom, $20 Billion](https://hoodline.com/2024/08/georgia-celebrates-fiscal-year-2024-business-boom-20-billion-investment-and-26-900-new-jobs-statewide/)  ·  `page_060626`

## Facts

- GDEcD's Global Commerce team claims involvement in 429 facility expansions and new locations during Fiscal Year 2024.
- These facility expansions and new locations translate into an investment north of $20 billion across Georgia.
- The investments created approximately 26,900 new private sector jobs statewide.
- A substantial 83% of the expansions and new ventures are located beyond the 10-county Atlanta area.
- Rural areas outside Atlanta generated over 18,300 jobs and more than $12.5 billion in investment.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated: “For years, we've worked closely with local and state partners all across Georgia to bring historic job creation to our communities, especially in rural areas.”
- Metro Atlanta cemented its status as a logistic and business heavyweight with nearly $7.8 billion in investments.
- New state locations were tied to investments reaching $12.8 billion.
- Expansions brought forth 9,800 new jobs, according to Lt. Governor Burt Jones.
- Lt. Governor Burt Jones emphasized the importance of a skilled workforce to support business growth and praised educational institutions and public-private partnerships for preparing the workforce.
- Speaker of the House Jon Burns reflected on the legislature's collaboration with Gov. Kemp to sustain Georgia’s economic stride.
- Jon Burns stated: "From cutting taxes and burdensome regulations to making historic investments in our education system, workforce development and infrastructure, we have delivered real results for the people of Georgia—and we will continue to do just that in the months and years ahead."
- Manufacturing took the lead among industry sectors receiving boosts in FY2024.
- Kia Georgia launched the EV9 vehicle model during FY2024.
- Companies making investments in Georgia include Toyota, StandardAero, and Gerresheimer.
- Investments topped $1.3 billion in the food processing and agribusiness sector.
- International companies Yakult and CJ Foodville opted for Georgia to prop up their East Coast operations.
- The technology and corporate hub sphere experienced growth, adding at least 4,400 new jobs.
- Gov. Kemp and the state conducted economic development missions to Korea and Switzerland to tighten bonds with key investors.
- GDEcD received a Gold Shovel award from Area Development magazine.
- GDEcD received a top ranking for EV investments in Business Facilities magazine.
