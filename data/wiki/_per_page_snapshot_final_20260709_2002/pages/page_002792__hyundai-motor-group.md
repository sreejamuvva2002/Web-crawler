# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** research_and_development  ·  **Published:** 2026-04-29

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Group and Georgia Tech Sign MOU to Further Collaborate on Hydrogen Mobility Development  | Press Releases & Insights | Worldwide | HTWO](https://www.htwo.hyundai.com/en/worldwide/press-releases/article/hyundai-motor-group-and-georgia-tech-sign-mou-to-further-collaborate-on-hydrogen-mobility-development)  ·  `page_002792`

## Facts

- On April 29, 2026, Hyundai Motor Group and Georgia Tech announced an expansion of their collaboration to advance hydrogen-powered transportation, deepen applied research and education, and accelerate the use of zero-emissions vehicles in Georgia.
- The partnership will bring hydrogen fuel cell vehicles and fueling infrastructure to campus, turning Georgia Tech into one of the nation’s most prominent campus-based examples for hydrogen mobility.
- Ken Ramírez, Executive Vice President and Head of Global Energy and Hydrogen Business at Hyundai Motor Group (a Georgia Tech alumnus, class of 1991, and current member of the Georgia Tech Advisory Board), stated the collaboration combines university research with Hyundai’s global experience to create real-world solutions for the energy transition and inspire future mobility leaders.
- Randy Parker, President and CEO of Hyundai Motor North America, donated a handful of NEXO fuel cell SUVs to expand the campus clean mobility footprint and provide real-world experiences with zero-emissions transportation technology.
- Ángel Cabrera, President of Georgia Tech, stated the expanded partnership advances hydrogen research, reduces emissions on their campus, and strengthens Georgia’s role in the future of clean mobility.
- The partnership includes the donation of four Hyundai NEXO fuel cell electric SUVs by Hyundai Motor North America.
- A hydrogen electrolyzer project will be installed at Georgia Tech’s North Avenue Research Area (NARA).
- The donated vehicles and installed infrastructure will support campus operations and interdisciplinary research focusing on engineering, sustainability, energy systems, and public policy related to hydrogen adoption.
- Faculty and students will study the real-world performance of hydrogen technology, infrastructure requirements for large-scale deployment, and environmental impacts of hydrogen energy systems.
- Insights gathered from the initiative aim to inform and accelerate the widespread use of hydrogen technology in campuses, fleets, cities, and freight corridors.
- The initiative supports Georgia Tech’s strategic plan goal of expanding the use of zero-emissions vehicles powered by sustainable energy sources.
- Beyond hydrogen energy, the partnership aims to advance innovation in autonomous driving, electric vehicle (EV) batteries, charging infrastructure, materials science, and cybersecurity.
- Georgia is home to the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) and serves as a hub for zero-emission transportation through HTWO Logistics, a clean logistics partnership operating Hyundai XCIENT fuel cell heavy-duty trucks in logistics operations near Savannah.
- The collaboration reinforces the link between education, research, and Hyundai’s long-term goal of achieving carbon neutrality by 2045.
- The partnership aims to further clean mobility research, generate workforce talent, and position Georgia as a national leader in hydrogen and zero-emissions transportation.
- Additional announcements regarding the partnership’s research projects, educational programs, and vehicle deployment are expected in the coming months.
