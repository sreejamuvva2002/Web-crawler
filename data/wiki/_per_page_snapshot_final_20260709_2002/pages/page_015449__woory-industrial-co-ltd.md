# Woory Industrial Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-06

**Source:** [Woory To Open EV Parts Manufacturing, R&D Facility In Laurens County – Georgia Daily Independent](https://georgiadailyindependent.com/2023/06/woory-to-open-ev-parts-manufacturing-rd-facility-in-laurens-county/)  ·  `page_015449`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Woory Industrial Co., Ltd. will establish a new manufacturing facility in Dublin, Georgia.
- The facility will create more than 130 new jobs and an estimated $18 million in investment in Dublin-Laurens County.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that the company is helping Georgia on the path to becoming the e-mobility capital of the nation.
- Woory Industrial Company was established in 1989 and specializes in heating, ventilation, and air conditioning components and systems for the global automotive industry.
- The company is concentrating on the development of new products for electric vehicles (EV) and hydrogen-fueled cars.
- Jungwoo Kim, CEO of Woory Industrial Company, stated that the transition to electric vehicles is the future and the U.S. EV market is the key market among all automotive markets.
- Over the past 10 years, Woory has successfully grown into a specialized EV component company through continuous innovation.
- The new facility located at 404 Kellam Road in Dublin will be the company’s first U.S. manufacturing location.
- The facility will produce electrical heaters, control units, and actuators for EV and internal combustion carmakers in North America.
- Operations at the new facility are expected to begin in November 2023.
- Unlike internal combustion engine vehicles, electric vehicles do not have a heating source, so heaters for EVs are required for heating the cabin and maintaining battery temperature.
- Woory’s pioneering development of electric heaters over the last 10 years has led the company to become a leader in this growing market segment.
- Woory will be hiring for positions in personnel from all fields necessary for an automotive parts manufacturing plant.
- Dublin Mayor Joshua Kight stated that Woory’s commitment to place an existing manufacturing building back into service helps promote more infill development while creating new career paths for the community.
- Dr. Fred Williams, Chair of the Board of Directors of the Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority, noted that Dublin and Laurens County has a rapidly growing automotive sector thanks to its geographic location on Interstate 16 and access to a skilled and trained workforce.
- The project involves a partnership with the Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- Project Manager Jacob Lee represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Georgia has invested for decades to match competitive projects with communities and sites that are prepared for development.
- From July 1 through December 31, 2022, the automotive industry was the second-largest source of new jobs and investment in Georgia.
- Since 2020, Georgia has pursued job creation opportunities along the entire EV supply chain, resulting in over $22.7 billion in investment and the creation of more than 28,400 jobs.
