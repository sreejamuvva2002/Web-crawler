# DENSO Manufacturing Michigan, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-04-20

**Source:** [DENSO Manufacturing Michigan Graduates Largest-Ever Skilled Trades Apprentice Class | The AI Journal](https://aijourn.com/denso-manufacturing-michigan-graduates-largest-ever-skilled-trades-apprentice-class/)  ·  `page_053811`

## Facts

- DENSO Manufacturing Michigan, Inc. (DMMI) celebrated the graduation of its largest-ever class of skilled trades apprentices on April 20, 2026.
- The milestone coincides with DENSO celebrating 40 years of manufacturing in Battle Creek, Michigan.
- Eleven apprentices graduated from the DENSO apprenticeship program, marking a commitment to continuous skills development and manufacturing excellence.
- DMMI operates a U.S. Department of Labor Registered Apprenticeship Program supported by the Michigan Department of Labor and Economic Opportunity (LEO).
- DMMI was recognized as a Registered Apprenticeship Champion in 2024 by LEO.
- The rigorous, four-year program combines classroom instruction with extensive hands-on training under experienced skilled trades professionals.
- The program is supported by Michigan Works! Southwest and the State of Michigan.
- Graduates are now credentialed journeymen prepared for expanded roles as tool and die makers, machine repairmen, or electricians supporting DENSO’s manufacturing operations.
- Since the program's inception in 2003, DENSO has graduated 77 apprentices.
- Todd Greer, vice president of manufacturing at DMMI, stated that skilled trades professionals are vital to advanced manufacturing and that the graduating class's success reflects their dedication and DENSO’s long-term investment in people.
- Brian Boylan, director of skilled trades and maintenance at DMMI, stated that advanced manufacturing depends on people willing to keep learning and growing, and expressed excitement about graduates advancing safety, quality, and production.
- DENSO is globally headquartered in Kariya, Japan, and is a $47.9 billion leading mobility supplier developing advanced technology and components for nearly every vehicle make and model.
- The company invests in around 180 facilities worldwide to produce cutting-edge electrification, powertrain, thermal, and mobility electronics products.
- DENSO employs 158,000 global employees who are working toward a mobility future that improves lives, eliminates traffic accidents, and preserves the environment.
- DENSO spent around 8.6 percent of its global consolidated sales on research and development in the fiscal year ending March 31, 2025.
- In North America, DENSO is headquartered in Southfield, Michigan, and employs 23,000+ team members across nearly 50 sites in the U.S., Canada, and Mexico.
- In the fiscal year ending March 31, 2025, DENSO North America generated $12.5 billion in consolidated sales.
