# Novelis Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2014-12-02

**Source:** [Nestlé, JLL, Novelis Inc., and Levi Strauss & Co Join 223 Companies in Supporting EPA’s Clean Power Plan - We Mean Business Coalition](https://www.wemeanbusinesscoalition.org/blog/nestle-jll-novelis-inc-and-levi-strauss-co-join-223-companies-in-supporting-epas-clean-power-plan/)  ·  `page_047425`

## Facts

- Novelis Inc. is the world’s largest manufacturer of rolled aluminum and the global leader in aluminum recycling.
- Novelis has U.S. manufacturing plants in Georgia, Indiana, Kentucky, New York, Ohio, and West Virginia.
- Novelis VP and Chief Sustainability Officer John Gardner stated that delivering technologically advanced, low-carbon solutions is a source of competitive advantage for Novelis and its customers.
- Novelis supports the principles of the EPA Clean Power Plan as an important step in providing greater energy choices and helping the U.S. achieve needed emissions reductions.
- 223 companies announced support for the EPA’s proposed carbon standard for electric power plants via a letter to the EPA, Obama Administration, and congressional leaders.
- The letter was coordinated by the nonprofit sustainability advocacy organization Ceres.
- Signatory companies include industry giants such as IKEA, Mars Inc., VF Corporation, Nestlé, JLL, and Levi Strauss & Co.
- Dan Probst, Chairman of Energy and Sustainability Services at JLL, stated that achieving proposed carbon reductions through energy efficiency retrofits will reduce greenhouse gas emissions, lower costs for power companies and consumers, and help drive the economy by creating jobs.
- Tim Brown, President and CEO of Nestlé Waters North America, commented that Nestlé places great importance on environmental responsibility and focuses on efficiency and renewable energy incorporation aligned with the Clean Power Plan.
- IKEA has set a goal to use 100 percent renewable energy for its global facilities by 2020.
- IKEA recently announced the purchase of a 165-megawatt Texas wind farm, boosting its total wind production capacity to nearly 1,000 gigawatts of electricity per year.
- This IKEA wind capacity is equivalent to the annual electricity consumption of about 90,000 American households.
- JLL manages more than 3 billion square feet of building space globally.
- JLL has helped commercial real estate clients reduce greenhouse gas emissions by 12 million metric tons while saving them $2.5 billion in energy costs over the past seven years.
- The EPA Clean Power Plan is the nation’s first comprehensive effort to reduce carbon pollution from existing electric power plants, which are the single largest source of global warming pollution in the U.S.
- EPA estimates the plan will reduce carbon emissions from power plants by 30 percent by 2030, from a 2005 baseline.
- The public comment period for the proposed standard closed on December 1, 2014.
- EPA is expected to announce a final rule in June 2015.
- More than 270 companies and investors have expressed support for the EPA Clean Power Plan.
- In June 2014, 49 investors managing $800 billion in assets sent letters of support to the Obama Administration and congressional leaders.
- The sign-on letter was open to energy users only, though some large electric power utilities also support the rule.
- Tom King, US President of National Grid, stated that National Grid supports EPA’s efforts to reduce emissions through sensible regulation and looks forward to working with the Obama Administration and EPA Administrator Gina McCarthy to reduce emissions from the energy sector.
- Ceres directs the Investor Network on Climate Risk (INCR), a network of over 100 institutional investors with collective assets totaling more than $13 trillion.
- Ceres also directs Business for Innovative Climate and Energy Policy (BICEP), an advocacy coalition of nearly 30 businesses committed to working with policymakers on energy and climate legislation.
- Burton President Donna Carpenter stated that the company backs the EPA’s Clean Power Plan due to experiencing climate change effects personally.
- Letitia Webster, director of global sustainability at VF Corporation (a North Carolina-based apparel company), stated that the EPA clean power plan provides long-term certainty for VF to continue investing in clean energy solutions.
- Nicole Peill-Moelter, Senior Director of Environmental Sustainability at Akamai Technologies, stated that Akamai is committed to driving energy efficiency and decarbonizing electricity consumption, viewing EPA guidelines as a good start to accelerating industry-wide innovation for clean energy.
- Andrew Lemley, Government Affairs Representative with New Belgium Brewing, stated the company has committed to protecting its future by saving energy and investing in renewable sources, pleased to see U.S. steps toward a clean energy economy.
- Amy Davidsen, Executive Director of The Climate Group North America, stated that power plants account for nearly 40 percent of US carbon emissions and commended the Administration's step to make them cleaner and safer.
- Lance Pierce, President of CDP North America, stated that companies supporting the plan are successful, competitive, bottom-line oriented firms that have analyzed why supporting low-carbon power makes good sense for their business and consumers.
