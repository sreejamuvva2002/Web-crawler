# Valeo

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [48 VOLTS AFFORDABLE ELECTRIFICATION | Valeo Service](https://www.valeoservice.co.uk/en-uk/passenger-car/car-alternators-and-starters/48-volts-affordable-electrification)  ·  `page_012813`

## Facts

- The 2030 CO2 reduction targets are unreachable without hybridization.
- The Valeo 48 Volts powertrain system architecture is a hybridization solution suited to all types of vehicle, allowing significant gains compared to a 12 Volts hybridization system and a full electrification solution for light vehicles.
- Reaching the voltage level to 48 Volts maximises the hybrid system benefits in terms of CO2 reduction and fuel economy.
- This powertrain system, which can be used in either 100% electric is on average 20% more affordable than high-voltage motors (greater than 60 Volts), thanks to a simplified architecture that reduces safety equipment requirements.
- Valeo offers two levels of 48 Volts hybridization: Level 1 provides 5% fuel savings with increased comfort and power compared to a 12 Volts system.
- Level 1 includes regenerative braking, which stores energy in the 48 Volts lithium-ion battery.
- Level 1 includes torque assist, where the 48 Volts starter generator uses recovered energy to assist the internal combustion engine when accelerating.
- Level 1 includes electrical supply, where energy stored in the 48-volt lithium-ion battery is fed through the 48 Volt converter to power vehicle accessories like the car radio and windscreen wipers.
- Level 2 provides hybrid with 100% electric propulsion, offering around 15% fuel savings with greater comfort and power.
- Level 2 builds on Level 1 by adding a 48 Volts electric propulsion engine.
- Combined with a high-capacity battery in Level 2, the vehicle can be driven in 100% electric mode.
- Level 2 delivers similar performance to a High Voltage hybrid system.
- Valeo’s 48 Volts starter-alternator ranges from 4 to 6 kW and has already been sold in more than 1 million units by Valeo.
- The 48 Volts starter-alternator was developed using know-how acquired with the 12 Volts starter-alternator.
- The 48 Volts 15 kW electric motor is oil-cooled and can be fitted to a hybrid dual-clutch gearbox, with series production scheduled for 2021.
- The 48 Volts 25kW water-cooled electric motor can be positioned on the rear axle of the vehicle to provide an electric 4WD function and enhance the driving experience.
- Valeo is producing the 48 Volts power converter (DC/DC converter) that transfers energy stored in the battery to the vehicle’s 12 Volts on-board power supply.
- Series production of the air-cooled version of the 48 Volts power converter began in 2017, meeting cost-performance requirements.
- A new generation of the 48 Volts power converter launched in 2021 is capable of meeting all energy needs thanks to its adaptable air or water cooling.
- Valeo solutions include an additional 48 Volts lithium-ion battery, a 48 Volts converter, a new 48 Volts starter generator, and a range of electric motors incorporating regenerative braking, torque assist, and electrical supply functions.
- Valeo offers the very first 48 Volts low-voltage rechargeable vehicle featuring a Four-Wheel drive electric architecture with a 48 Volts starter-alternator and a 48 Volts electric motor on the rear axle.
- This four-wheel drive electric architecture significantly improves handling in hard conditions such as snowy environments.
- By combining 48 Volts hybridization with a larger 48 Volts battery, this plug-in hybrid solution offers an electric range of up to 80 km.
- The plug-in hybrid solution guarantees a drastic overall reduction in CO2 emissions.
- This solution enables all-electric travel for around forty kilometers, with the thermal engine taking over for longer journeys.
- Using geolocation, the 100% electric mode is automatically activated when the vehicle enters a low emission zone.
- When a driver enters a route into the GPS, the vehicle can adapt the use of the electric drive system accordingly.
- Compared to conventional high-voltage plug-in hybrid vehicles, the Valeo 48 Volts plug-in hybrid is more economical, as the average cost of the 48 Volts technology is around 20% lower.
- Valeo proposes to reuse the 48 Volts technology initially developed for hybrid vehicles for new mobility applications such as light 2, 3, or 4 wheels.
- Valeo is supplying the engine for the Citroën AMI.
- The 100% electric prototype operating at low voltage in 48 Volts is the very first 48 Volts democar powered entirely by Valeo, excluding batteries.
- This two-seater electric vehicle can reach a maximum speed of 100km/h and has a range of 150km.
- Its small size and weight make it suitable for short journeys and city trips, and it can be recharged at any electric charging point.
- Valeo’s 48 Volts 100% electric vehicle is more economical (20% less expensive) than a current all-electric solution because its low-voltage system does not require specific protection, whereas high-voltage electric vehicles are subject to restrictive regulations and accreditations.
- The cost of a small 48 Volts 100% electric vehicle could be around €7,500 after deduction of purchase subsidies.
- This type of vehicle equipped with Valeo’s solution gave manufacturers the impulse for the new era of low-voltage urban mobility, which arrived on the streets of French cities in 2020 with the very first full electric 48 Volts Citroën AMI.
