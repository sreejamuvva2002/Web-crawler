# Hyundai Motor's Georgia plant

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Three workers died at Hyundai’s Georgia plant since 2022, before US immigration raid, WSJ reports | Salem News Channel](https://salemnewschannel.com/three-workers-died-at-hyundais-georgia-plant-since-2022-before-us-immigration-raid-wsj-reports)  ·  `page_009947`

## Facts

- Three workers died at Hyundai’s Georgia plant since construction began in 2022.
- The Wall Street Journal reported the deaths, citing a review of federal records.
- Dozens of current and former workers, many of them safety coordinators who helped oversee construction, told the WSJ that the work environment involved many inexperienced immigrant laborers.
- Workers cited lax safety standards and frequent accidents at the site.
- The plant is operated through a joint venture between Hyundai and South Korea’s LG Energy Solution.
- The facility has been in the limelight since an immigration raid last month that detained hundreds of South Korean workers.
- The immigration raid was the largest single-site enforcement operation in the U.S. Department of Homeland Security’s history.
- Apart from the deaths, more than a dozen workers have suffered serious injuries.
- Injuries included falling without wearing harnesses and getting crushed by forklifts.
- Workers interviewed said Hyundai did not ensure that people were properly trained.
- Safety regulators did little to prevent worksite violations, according to the report.
- Reuters could not immediately confirm the report.
- Hyundai did not immediately respond to a Reuters request for comment.
- Hyundai stated it is committed to following immigration laws and does not compromise safety for the sake of speed.
- Hyundai took steps to address safety issues in response to incidents during construction.
- Hyundai CEO Jose Munoz told the newspaper: “We acted immediately and comprehensively to prevent anything like this from happening again.”
- Jose Munoz traveled to Georgia to tell the team directly that their safety comes before production schedules, costs, profits, and everything else.
- Construction on the electric vehicle and battery plant is ongoing.
- The plant is located in Bryan County about 30 miles west of Savannah.
- Hyundai started construction of its $7.6 billion auto plant in Georgia in 2022.
