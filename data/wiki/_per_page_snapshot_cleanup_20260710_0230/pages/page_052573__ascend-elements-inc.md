# Ascend Elements, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2025-11-12

**Source:** [Case Summary: Ascend Elements Chapter 11](https://bondoro.com/ascend-elements/)  ·  `page_052573`

## Facts

- Ascend Elements, Inc. is a lithium-ion battery recycler headquartered in Westborough, MA.
- The company recovers critical minerals from end-of-life EV batteries and gigafactory scrap.
- It converts recovered materials into battery-grade products including black mass, precursor cathode active material (pCAM), and lithium carbonate.
- The company's proprietary Hydro-to-Cathode® process combines hydrometallurgy with direct precursor synthesis and is licensed from Worcester Polytechnic Institute (WPI).
- The technology underpins a worldwide intellectual property portfolio of 154 granted and pending patents.
- Ascend operates an active commercial facility in Covington, GA (Apex 0).
- Apex 0 is a 154,000-square-foot plant located approximately 35 miles east of Atlanta.
- The Covington facility was originally announced in January 2022 as a $43 million investment.
- During the August 2025 facility restart, CEO Linh Austin disclosed that over $120 million had been invested in Apex 0, representing a near-threefold overrun relative to the original budget.
- Apex 0 is designed to process approximately 30,000 metric tons of battery feedstock per year into 20,000–30,000 metric tons of black mass.
- In August 2025, Apex 0 produced what the company described as the first commercial-scale recycled lithium carbonate (>99% pure) in U.S. history.
- The Covington facility experienced fourteen fire-related incidents between March 2023 and February 2025.
- Operations at Apex 0 were suspended in February 2025 under pressure from the Covington Fire Department.
- Apex 0 operations restarted on August 25, 2025, following safety upgrades and a near-total site management replacement.
- Utility-connection upgrades remain necessary for commercially viable lithium carbonate output at Apex 0.
- Ascend Elements filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy protection on April 9, 2026, in the U.S. Bankruptcy Court for the Southern District of Texas.
- The company reported $1 billion to $10 billion in assets and $500 million to $1 billion in liabilities at filing.
- Ascend is pursuing an expedited 363 sale of substantially all assets.
- Apex 1 in Hopkinsville, KY is a 60% complete, construction-paused pCAM plant targeting pCAM for roughly 750,000 EVs per year.
- Apex 1 was announced in August 2022 as a Phase 1 $310 million investment with eventual buildout up to $1 billion.
- The company operates a 50/50 Polish joint venture with Elemental Strategic Metals in Zawiercie (AE Elemental) that commercially opened September 19, 2024.
- AE Elemental operates a 12,000 MT/year battery metals recovery facility.
- Ascend has an early-stage wholly-owned Polish lithium carbonate and pCAM extraction project (Apex 2) in the Wałbrzych Special Economic Zone.
- The company also operates an R&D facility in Westborough, MA, and a cathode-finishing lab in Novi, MI.
- Headcount was approximately 102 at the petition date, with estimated 2024 revenue of approximately $35 million.
- Ascend has a November 2025 multi-year offtake agreement with Trafigura Group for 15,000 metric tons of lithium carbonate between 2027 and 2031.
- The Trafigura contract is described by Ascend as a "take-or-pay" agreement.
- In December 2025, Ascend signed a multi-year supply contract valued at approximately $1 billion with an unnamed leading global automaker.
- In June 2023, Ascend signed a pCAM supply contract valued at approximately $1 billion (with an option to expand up to $5 billion) for an unnamed major U.S. company, sourced from Apex 1.
- On March 14, 2023, Ascend signed an agreement with Koura (Orbia's fluorinated solutions business) for up to 5,000 metric tons per year of recycled lithium carbonate.
- Orbia Ventures led the company's 2021 Series B financing.
- Ascend claims more than $2 billion in aggregate future commitments, with each of the three largest contracts structurally dependent on Apex 1 completion.
- A non-binding February 2023 basic agreement was signed with Honda.
- The company made one-time commercial deliveries to Navitas Systems for an undisclosed DoD project and to Freudenberg e-Power Systems.
- Under CEO Mike O'Kronley, the company raised approximately $1 billion of equity over three years (2021–2024).
- A $300 million October 2022 Series C financing included $200 million in equity led by Fifth Wall Climate, with SK ecoplant joining as a strategic investor.
- A $542 million September 2023 Series D was led by Decarbonization Partners (a BlackRock/Temasek JV), Temasek, and Qatar Investment Authority, with Fifth Wall, BHP Ventures, Tenaska, and Alliance Resource Partners participating.
- A $162 million February 2024 round came from Just Climate, Clearvision Ventures, and IRONGREY.
- SK ecoplant was Ascend's largest individual equity holder, committing $60.84 million across Series C and a follow-on, and taking a board seat.
- On September 9, 2024, SK ecoplant sold its entire stake (~7.7% of shares) to Seoul-based SKS Private Equity Co., Ltd. for approximately $98.23 million.
- SK ecoplant separately sold its waste- and water-treatment business to KKR for approximately $1.1–$1.2 billion in August 2025.
- Battery Resourcers, LLC was founded in 2015 as a WPI spinout by Dr. Yan Wang (Chief Scientist), Dr. Eric Gratz (CEO), and Dr. Diran Apelian (Board Chair).
- Mike O'Kronley was appointed CEO in March 2020 after joining the board in 2017.
- The company rebranded as Ascend Elements in January 2022.
- O'Kronley departed on or about March 20, 2025.
- Linh Austin was appointed President and CEO on March 20, 2025, after joining the board in September 2024.
- Eight of eleven senior leaders have joined since 2025.
- Adam Titus served as interim CFO beginning January 2026 and transitioned to Chief Restructuring Officer on April 3, 2026.
- Ahmed Allouache was appointed permanent CFO on March 21, 2026.
- Lithium carbonate prices peaked at approximately $80,000 per metric ton in November 2022 before collapsing to roughly $8,259 per ton by June 2025.
- As of 2023, China controlled nearly 85% of global battery cell production capacity by monetary value and over two-thirds of the world's cobalt and lithium processing capacity as of 2022.
- Spot battery-grade lithium carbonate roughly doubled in Q1 2026, rising from approximately $13,433 per metric ton in early December 2025 to approximately $26,278 per metric ton by late January 2026.
- USTR Section 301 tariffs on Chinese lithium-ion EV batteries rose from 7.5% to 25% effective September 27, 2024.
- The One Big Beautiful Bill Act, signed July 4, 2025, terminated Section 30D's $7,500 consumer EV tax credit for vehicles acquired after September 30, 2025.
- The act also narrowed Section 45X advanced manufacturing credits with new Foreign Entity of Concern restrictions.
- The Section 30D termination triggered a 28% year-over-year collapse in Q1 2026 U.S. new EV sales.
- In October 2022, the DOE awarded Ascend two Battery Materials Processing grants totaling approximately $480 million under the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law.
- A $316.2 million grant for pCAM infrastructure and a $164 million grant for CAM manufacturing infrastructure were awarded.
- The $164 million CAM grant was mutually canceled on February 27, 2025.
- The $316.2 million pCAM grant was unilaterally canceled by the DOE on or about October 7, 2025.
- Approximately $207 million had been disbursed at termination, leaving approximately $110 million unfunded.
- DOE stated the canceled projects missed milestones, were not economically viable, and would not provide a positive return on investment of taxpayer dollars.
- The DOE retains an undivided reversionary interest in equipment acquired with grant funds.
