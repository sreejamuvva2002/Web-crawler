# Hollingsworth & Vose

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2023-01-05

**Source:** [Hollingsworth & Vose to Expand Virginia Site - Nonwovens Industry](https://www.nonwovens-industry.com/contents/view_breaking-news/2023-01-05/hollingsworth-vose-to-expand-virginia-site/)  ·  `page_008583`

## Facts

- Hollingsworth & Vose (H&V) is a global manufacturer of advanced materials including nonwovens used in filtration, battery and industrial applications.
- The company will invest $40.2 million to expand its operation in Floyd County, VA.
- The expansion will add more than 28,000 square feet to an existing facility to accommodate new production equipment.
- The project is intended to meet increased demand for the company's battery and industrial applications.
- Virginia successfully competed with Georgia for the project.
- The expansion will create 25 new jobs in Floyd County.
- Governor Glenn Youngkin announced the investment and job creation.
- Josh Ayer, CEO of H&V, stated the facility is essential to serving global and domestic customers and that Virginia was chosen for its positive business environment and strong state and county support.
- The company has been part of the Floyd, VA community since 1976.
- Joe Turman, Chairman of the Floyd County Board of Supervisors, noted the new investment is over three times bigger than the largest private investment in the county's 191-year history.
- The Virginia Economic Development Partnership worked with Floyd County to secure the project for Virginia.
- Governor Youngkin approved a $558,700 grant from the Commonwealth’s Opportunity Fund to assist Floyd County with the project.
- Caren Merrick, Secretary of Commerce and Trade for Virginia, highlighted the company's role in Virginia's advanced materials industry over the past 46 years and thanked H&V for creating high-quality jobs.
