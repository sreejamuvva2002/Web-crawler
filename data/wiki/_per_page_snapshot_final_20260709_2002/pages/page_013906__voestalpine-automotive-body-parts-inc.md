# voestalpine Automotive Body Parts Inc.

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2014-06

**Source:** [Parker Poe Assists Austrian Company on Launch of First North American Automotive Parts Facility  |  Parker Poe](https://www.parkerpoe.com/news/2014/06/parker-poe-assists-austrian-company-on-launch-of)  ·  `page_013906`

## Facts

- voestalpine Automotive Body Parts Inc. in Cartersville, Georgia, recently held the grand opening of its new North American production operation.
- The facility represents an investment of about $70 million.
- The project will create 220 new jobs.
- The plant will manufacture ultra-high strength body-in-white components for premium car manufacturers in the region.
- The opening represents the completion of the first phase of voestalpine’s extensive internationalization program in the automotive sector.
- Site selection was driven by logistical advantages to meet critical delivery schedules for automotive OEM customers, a well-trained workforce, and pro-business state and local partners in Bartow County willing to support short- and long-term strategic growth plans.
- The site selection was first announced by Georgia Gov. Nathan Deal in August 2012.
- The facility is part of voestalpine’s Metal Forming Division.
- It is centrally located to the company’s automotive customers across the South.
- Several long-term contracts are already in hand, with expectations for more as demand continues for its high-quality components.
- Philipp Schulz, CEO of voestalpine Automotive Body Parts, stated that Cartersville was chosen from over 30 candidates due to its willingness to work with the company and make necessary accommodations possible.
- Parker Poe Adams & Bernstein LLP assisted voestalpine Group in developing its 17th production site in the United States.
- The facility is a state-of-the-art operation located in Cartersville, Bartow County, Georgia.
