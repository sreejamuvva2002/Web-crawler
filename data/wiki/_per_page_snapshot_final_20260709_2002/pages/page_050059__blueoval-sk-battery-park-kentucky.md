# BlueOval SK Battery Park Kentucky

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Electric VehicleBlueOval SK Battery Park Kentucky - Barton Malow](https://www.bartonmalow.com/projects/blueoval-sk-battery-park-kentucky/)  ·  `page_050059`

## Facts

- Barton Malow partnered with Gray Construction as design-build contractors to deliver two battery plants in Glendale, Kentucky.
- The facilities total 9 million square feet under roof and are built on BlueOval SK Battery Park Kentucky’s 1,500-acre campus.
- The project includes 1.8 million square feet of highly controlled cleanroom space across two floors.
- The project includes essential outbuildings for hazardous storage, waste management, security, and testing.
- Construction began in early 2022.
- Construction reached substantial completion in February 2025.
- The project met an aggressive speed-to-market schedule for a project of unprecedented scale and technical complexity.
- The first concrete placement occurred in August 2022.
- The first steel column was erected ahead of schedule in October 2022.
- Cleanroom certification was achieved by July 2024.
- At peak, 4,700 craft professionals were engaged on site.
- The team utilized advanced scheduling, earned value management, and real-time coordination across trades.
- The construction team moved 4.3 million cubic yards of soil.
- The team laid 283,000 tons of stone.
- The team poured 66,000 cubic yards of concrete.
- The team erected over 40,000 tons of steel while staying under budget.
- Barton Malow partnered with local organizations to support food insecurity relief, park revitalization, workforce development, and environmental stewardship.
- In 2025, the GE2 plant was awarded The Association of Union Constructors’ (TAUC) Joseph R. La Rocca Project of the Year Award in the “Above $50 Million” category.
- The award recognizes outstanding work performed under labor-management cooperation regarding collaboration and teamwork, quality and craftsmanship, execution, and safety.
- The park is part of Ford and SK On’s $5.8 billion manufacturing investment to bring EVs at scale to American consumers.
- This represents the single largest manufacturing investment at one time by any automaker in the nation.
