# Volvo Group Powertrain Operations

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** ['Made in America': President Biden touts American manufacturing at Volvo plant in Hagerstown - CBS Baltimore](https://www.cbsnews.com/baltimore/news/president-biden-touts-economy-at-volvo-plant-in-hagerstown/)  ·  `page_014376`

## Facts

- President Joe Biden visited the Volvo Group Powertrain Operations in Hagerstown on a Friday afternoon to deliver remarks about the state of the U.S. economy and thank local manufacturers.
- Biden praised the manufacturing team for building heavy-duty engines, transmissions, axles for trucks and buses, and parts for electric cars in the future.
- The president spoke for about 22 minutes, addressing the goal of "building the economy from the bottom up and middle out."
- Biden's visit followed similar economic trips to Ohio and New York and highlighted his recent signing of the Chips and Science Act to spur semiconductor production in the U.S.
- The visit took place on National Manufacturing Day, which is Oct. 7.
- Martin Weissburg, chairman of Volvo Group North America and Mack Trucks president, stated that the Hagerstown site exemplifies modern manufacturing by providing advanced transportation technologies to meet customer needs today and in the future.
- Over the past 10 years, the Volvo Group has invested nearly $320 million in the site.
- The Hagerstown campus produces engines, transmissions, and axles for Mack Trucks, Volvo Trucks, Prevost motorcoaches and Volvo Buses sold in North America.
- The facility recently began production of the Modular Power Boxes (MPB) that are at the heart of the Group's new zero-emission battery-electric vehicles, the Volvo VNR Electric and Mack LR Electric models.
- Biden opened his remarks with "Made in America" and asked, "Where is it written that America can't be the leader in manufacturing?"
- The president emphasized bringing manufacturing back to the U.S., citing competition with China as a matter of national security.
- Biden declared himself the "most pro-union president in American history," stating that unions are the "single hardest workers around."
- During the visit, Biden met with executives, toured the facility, and met with employees.
