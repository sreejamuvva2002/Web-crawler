# TEKLAS

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2020-11-10

**Source:** [Electric Vehicle Manufacturer to Open North American Headquarters in Georgia, Create Jobs in Calhoun - AllOnGeorgia](https://allongeorgia.com/gordon-local-news/electric-vehicle-manufacturer-to-open-north-american-headquarters-in-georgia-create-jobs-in-calhoun/)  ·  `page_051252`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that TEKLAS, a Turkish advanced research and development, manufacturing company and supplier of electric vehicle (EV) parts, will invest $6.5 million to open its first North American facility and headquarters in Gordon County.
- The project will deliver 120 jobs to Calhoun.
- Governor Kemp cited Georgia's highly skilled workforce, top-notch logistics network, and pro-business environment as key factors for TEKLAS joining the state's growing electric vehicle parts industry.
- TEKLAS was founded in Turkey in 1971.
- The company designs, develops, and manufactures fluid circulation systems for cooling and heating applications, including hoses and pipes made from rubber, plastic, and metal for the global automotive industry.
- TEKLAS utilizes highly innovative and automated manufacturing processes that employ advanced water molding technology.
- TEKLAS USA will feature assembly lines designed to serve prominent original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) in the industry.
- Operations at the new facility are scheduled to begin in the spring of 2021.
- TEKLAS is headquartered in Turkey and maintains existing locations in Bulgaria, Serbia, Mexico, and China, along with sales offices in Germany and France.
- Regional Director Arcan Ergur stated the company is excited to open a new plant in Calhoun, Georgia.
- Regional Director Arcan Ergur stated that the new Calhoun location will help the company be closer to its main customers.
- The 200,000-square-foot facility and headquarters will be located at 320 South Industrial Boulevard in Calhoun, Georgia.
- TEKLAS will hire for positions across a variety of skill levels, including advanced manufacturing, management, and supervision roles.
- Larry Roye, chairman of the Development Authority of Gordon County, applauded the state and local partners for helping encourage TEKLAS to choose Calhoun.
- Gordon County Chamber of Commerce President and CEO Kathy Johnson commended TEKLAS for its research, advancements in EV manufacturing, and contribution to expanding the local automotive manufacturing base.
- GDEcD Project Manager Fernanda Kirchner represented the Global Commerce division on this competitive project in partnership with the Economic Development Authority of Gordon County and Georgia Power.
- The Center of Innovation for Logistics, a division of GDEcD, provided freight analysis to support the project and company planning.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson highlighted TEKLAS's decision to establish its North American headquarters and first U.S. advanced manufacturing facility in Calhoun as a successful example of Georgia's efforts to attract and grow future jobs.
- Commissioner Wilson stated that Georgia will work to foster an ecosystem of manufacturers and suppliers on the leading edge of the automotive industry's transition to electric vehicles and e-mobility.
- Commissioner Wilson welcomed TEKLAS to the diverse family of EV suppliers in Georgia.
