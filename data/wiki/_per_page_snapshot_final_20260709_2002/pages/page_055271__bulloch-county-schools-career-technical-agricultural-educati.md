# Bulloch County Schools Career Technical & Agricultural Education (CTAE) Program

**Type:** workforce_training  ·  **Role:** workforce_training  ·  **Published:** 2022-01-27

**Source:** [State leaders visit Bulloch County Schools • The Georgia Virtue](https://www.thegeorgiavirtue.com/bulloch-local-news/state-leaders-visit-bulloch-county-schools/)  ·  `page_055271`

## Facts

- An Economic Development Partnership team from the Georgia Department of Education (GaDOE) visited Bulloch County Schools on Friday, January 21.
- Patrick Ledford, an economic development liaison with the GaDOE’s Career, Technical, and Agricultural Education division, described the visit as a fantastic experience and highlighted strong stakeholder relationships in the district.
- The eight-hour site visit was a key part of a year-long process for Bulloch County Schools to seek a district-wide designation from the state’s Economic Development Partnership program.
- The designation is endorsed by the Georgia Economic Developers Association.
- The GaDOE team visited to learn about the school district’s Career Technical & Agricultural Education (CTAE) program and its collaboration with local post-secondary institutions, businesses, industries, economic developers, and workforce development partners.
- The school district was admitted into the Economic Development Partnership program in 2021 after an application process that required input and support from local partners including the Development Authority of Bulloch County and Great Dane.
- Bethany Gilliam, the school district’s CTAE director, stated the district believes it has a strong chance of obtaining the designation, which would benefit the community by helping compete for new business and industry.
- The GaDOE team toured career pathway classes and labs at Portal Middle High School, Southeast Bulloch High School, and Statesboro High School to visit with teachers and students.
- The team met one-on-one with multiple school district partners and heard presentations about the district’s Workforce Development Plan and opportunities within its CTAE program.
- Gilliam noted the GaDOE team was very complimentary of the work in CTAE classrooms and labs, as well as the district-wide approach to employability skills training.
- The Economic Development Partnership Designation program is a district-wide process that prepares school districts to adopt best practices supporting economic development, strengthen programs, and align education with key regional industries.
- In terms of economic development, the Partnership elevates the capability of school systems to fill a key role in business recruitment and expansion and talent development for their communities.
- Fostering strong relationships between education, business and industry, and economic development stakeholders ensures districts continue offering high-quality industry-relevant programs equitable for all students and supportive of local talent pipeline development.
- Dr. Julie Chance, executive director of Program Improvement for Bulloch County Schools, said it is an honor to be accepted into the 2021-2022 Economic Development Partnership Designation program and highlighted ongoing collaboration with local partners to prepare students for successful careers and build an educated, skilled workforce for economic growth.
- Bulloch County Schools’ CTAE program helps prepare students for college enrollment, military enlistment, or employment in in-demand, high-wage careers after high school.
- The district’s state-approved career pathway courses leverage partnerships with industry and higher education to ensure students acquire skills needed for immediate and future employment.
- The program offers students courses in 13 different career clusters and 23 high school career pathways and three middle school career pathways.
- Work Based Learning opportunities are offered for students at all three of the school district’s high schools.
