# Hollingsworth & Vose

## Facts

- Hollingsworth & Vose (H&V) is a manufacturer of advanced materials for filtration, energy and industrial applications.
- Over its more than 100 years in business, the company has undergone numerous expansion efforts.
- Today, H&V maintains 13 facilities in strategic locations across three continents, North America, Europe and Asia.
- In each location, the company aims to hire locally to raise new opportunities to further its commitment to these regions.
- H&V adjusts and aligns its operations to meet customer demands as markets mature or customers relocate.
- The company's vertical integration assists with the improvement of costs when it comes to the sourcing and distribution of products, among other extensive cost savings.
- H&V is set up to achieve a more economic operation and allows full control when changes and enhancements are necessary.
- This established global presence makes a significant impact on the efficiencies of the international supply chain.
- Hollingsworth & Vose opened with a single location in East Walpole, Massachusetts, which remains its company headquarters.
- Key expansion efforts include erecting a facility in New York state in the 1950s.
- A facility was built in Virginia in the 1970s.
- A joint venture was formed in Mexico in 1981.
- The company established a presence in the United Kingdom while simultaneously creating new facilities in the southern and northwestern United States from the 1980s to the 2000s.
- Facilities were constructed in Germany throughout the 2000s.
- A facility was opened in Suzhou, China in 2004.
- Today, H&V utilizes its 15 R&D and manufacturing facilities strategically placed across the globe to develop and deliver advanced materials that contribute to a cleaner world.
- H&V products commonly find use in devices and systems that provide clean air/liquids and energy storage.
- The company maintains a facility in Hawkinsville, GA at 106 Industrial Boulevard, Hawkinsville, GA 31036 USA.
- Zachery Hollingsworth and Charles Vose formed their partnership in 1881.
