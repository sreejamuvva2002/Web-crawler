# Enchem America Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2023-06-26

**Source:** [Governor Lee, Commissioner McWhorter Announce Enchem America Inc. to Locate US Manufacturing Operations in Brownsville - TNECD](https://tnecd.com/news/governor-lee-commissioner-mcwhorter-announce-enchem-america-inc-to-locate-us-manufacturing-operations-in-brownsville/)  ·  `page_007948`

## Facts

- Enchem America Inc. will invest $152.5 million to establish electrolyte manufacturing operations in Brownsville, Tennessee.
- The new facility will be located at the I-40 Advantage Industrial Park, a Select Tennessee Certified Site.
- The project will create 190 new jobs in Tier 4, At-Risk Haywood County.
- The Brownsville facility will operate similarly to Enchem’s U.S. headquarters in Commerce, Georgia.
- The facility will manufacture electrolytes for electric vehicle (EV) batteries as well as other EV battery-related services.
- Enchem America Inc. is a subsidiary of Enchem Co., Ltd., which is based in South Korea.
- The company specializes in the development and manufacturing of electrolytes and other additives for rechargeable batteries.
- With the additional Tennessee operations, Enchem will be able to further support its EV partners across the Southeast.
- Since 2019, TNECD has supported more than 50 economic development projects in the Southwest Tennessee region, resulting in nearly 9,300 job commitments and $7.2 billion in capital investment.
- Gov. Bill Lee stated that Enchem’s decision to invest more than $150 million in Tennessee is a testament to the state's unmatched business climate, highly skilled workforce, and position as a leader in the automotive industry.
- Gov. Bill Lee thanked Enchem for choosing Tennessee to establish its second U.S. facility and for creating 190 new jobs to provide greater opportunity to Tennesseans across Haywood County.
- TNECD Commissioner Stuart McWhorter noted that South Korea is one of Tennessee’s top 10 foreign direct investment partners.
- McWhorter stated Enchem is investing in one of Tennessee’s At-Risk counties and boosting the EV supply chain across Tennessee.
- Jung Kang Oh, CEO and founder of Enchem Co. Ltd., expressed gratitude to Haywood County, the City of Brownsville, and professionals who assisted Enchem in expanding its North American presence.
- Jung Kang Oh highlighted that the growth of the EV market has been remarkable and will continue at an accelerated pace over the coming decade.
- Jung Kang Oh stated confidence that the company is well positioned to capitalize on EV market growth and explore new innovation and partnership opportunities with Haywood County and Brownsville.
- Haywood County Mayor David Livingston welcomed Enchem, stating the association signifies a commitment to making the EV revolution a reality in Haywood County.
- Livingston noted the economic opportunity will transform Haywood County and West Tennessee with great, new high-paying jobs.
- Brownsville Mayor Bill Rawls welcomed Enchem America to the city, noting the announcement fulfills years of work and investment in I-40 Advantage Industrial Park to bring high-quality job opportunities.
- Rawls expressed looking forward to working with Enchem to build the advanced manufacturing workforce in Brownsville, Tennessee.
- TVA and Brownsville Energy Authority congratulated Enchem America on establishing operations in Haywood County.
- John Bradley, TVA senior vice president of economic development, stated helping companies like Enchem create jobs and investment is fundamental to TVA’s mission of service.
- Bradley noted pride in partnering with HTL Advantage, Brownsville-Haywood County Industrial Development Corporation, City of Brownsville, and TNECD for the announcement.
- Sen. Page Walley (R-Savannah) welcomed Enchem America to Haywood County, stating the investment will bring jobs, boost the regional economy, and establish the community as an innovation hub in the rapidly growing industry.
- Rep. Johnny Shaw (D-Bolivar) noted the prospect of a more than $152 million investment is an example of unprecedented growth in Brownsville, Haywood County, and West Tennessee.
- Shaw stated BlueOval City lit the fuse for what continues to be an economic explosion for the area.
- Shaw thanked Enchem America and local/state officials for keeping momentum going in Haywood County.
