# Cherokee County Office of Economic Development

## Facts

- Advanced manufacturing integrates technology into production processes to increase quality and meet industry specifications or standards.
- Cherokee County has a strong advanced manufacturing sector that includes automotive, aerospace, and medical industries.
- The county takes advantage of its proximity to global markets, a skilled workforce, and transportation opportunities to expand manufacturer reach and productivity.
- Cherokee County is an ideal destination for advanced manufacturing companies, offering a combination of a skilled workforce, superior infrastructure, and strategic market access.
- Businesses can draw from a labor force of nearly 2.5 million from within Cherokee’s labor market area.
- 46.4% of residents in Cherokee County hold a bachelor’s degree or higher.
- Chattahoochee Technical College provides targeted training in manufacturing, engineering technologies, and transportation.
- Georgia’s Quick Start program offers one of the nation’s top workforce training resources.
- Established automotive suppliers operating in Cherokee County include Inalfa Roof Systems and Piolax Corporation.
- Georgia has been ranked the best state to do business for ten years in a row.
- Cherokee County offers strategic access to major highways and rail services through Patriot Rail.
- The county is located just 45 minutes from Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport, providing connections to 80% of the U.S. market within a two-hour flight.
- A recent $34 million expansion at Cherokee County Regional Airport underscores the area’s commitment to supporting logistical efficiency for manufacturing operations.
- Georgia is home to the North American headquarters of industry leaders like Porsche and Lotus.
- Atlanta ranks as the number one metro area for foreign investment by average project value, offering advanced manufacturing companies unparalleled opportunities for growth and global connectivity.
