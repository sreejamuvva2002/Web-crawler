# Rivian Automotive, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-12-03

**Source:** [Rivian to Receive $6.6B Conditional Loan from US DOE](https://www.aftermarketnews.com/rivian-conditional-loan-u-s-doe/)  ·  `page_000731`

## Facts

- Rivian Automotive has received a conditional commitment from the U.S. Department of Energy’s Advanced Technology Vehicle Manufacturing (ATVM) loan program for a loan of up to $6.6 billion (including $6 billion of principal and approximately $600 million of capitalized interest).
- The loan is intended to accelerate Rivian's growth and leadership in electric vehicle design, development, and manufacturing in the United States.
- If finalized, the loan would support the construction of Rivian’s next facility in Stanton Springs North, near the city of Social Circle, Georgia.
- The new facility will substantially expand Rivian’s domestic production capacity to support demand from U.S. and international markets.
- The funding will provide significant support for the production of Rivian's midsize platform, which underpins the R2, a midsize SUV, and the R3/R3X, a midsize crossover.
- Rivian states that its R2 and R3 vehicle lines will be critical drivers in the company’s long-term growth and profitability.
- Rivian plans to build the facility in two phases, with each phase resulting in 200,000 units of annual production capacity for a total of 400,000 units annually.
- Phase 1 of the project is expected to start production in 2028.
- Rivian expects to create approximately 7,500 operations jobs through 2030 at the future Georgia manufacturing facility.
- The facility will be located at the Stanton Springs Site, less than one hour’s drive from downtown Atlanta.
- Rivian plans to design and build a fully modern manufacturing facility employing modern construction techniques and advanced environmental management while preserving natural spaces and investing actively in surrounding communities.
- Over the history of the ATVM loan program, DOE has provided billions in financing for American EV manufacturing, including loans to General Motors and Tesla.
- The conditional commitment indicates DOE’s intent to finance the project, but technical, legal, environmental, and financial conditions must be satisfied before definitive financing documents are executed and funds are disbursed.
- If finalized, the loan would be secured by all assets of the project, fixed assets, and guarantees of parent company Rivian Automotive, Inc. and certain of its subsidiaries.
