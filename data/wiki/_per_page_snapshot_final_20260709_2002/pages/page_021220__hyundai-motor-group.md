# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-05-22

**Source:** [Biden highlights Hyundai announcement of $10B US investment | Fox Business](https://www.foxbusiness.com/technology/biden-hyundai-announcement-us-investment)  ·  `page_021220`

## Facts

- Hyundai pledged to invest at least $10 billion in electric vehicles and related technologies in the United States.
- Hyundai's investment includes $5.5 billion for an electric vehicle and battery factory in Georgia.
- Hyundai CEO Euisun Chung stated his company would spend another $5 billion on artificial intelligence for autonomous vehicles and other technologies.
- President Joe Biden showcased the pledge during a three-day visit to South Korea, highlighting how longstanding U.S.-South Korea military ties are being leveraged into a broader economic partnership.
- Hyundai's Georgia factory is expected to employ 8,100 workers and produce up to 300,000 vehicles annually.
- Construction for the Georgia factory is planned to begin early next year, with production set to start in 2025 near the unincorporated town of Ellabell.
- Biden earlier tried to link EV production to automakers with unionized workers via a $1.85 trillion spending proposal that stalled in the Senate, which would have provided extra tax credits for buyers of EVs made by unionized factories.
- During a visit to a Samsung computer chip plant, Biden called on Korean companies building plants in the U.S. to hire union workers and urged them to enter partnerships with American union members.
- There is no guarantee that Hyundai's Georgia plant's workers will be unionized.
- Katie Byrd, press secretary for Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp, noted in an email that Georgia is a "Right-to-Work" state, meaning workers may not be required to join a union or make payments to a union as a condition of employment.
- A Hyundai spokesperson did not respond to an email asking if the Georgia plant would be unionized.
- A senior Biden administration official, briefing reporters on condition of anonymity, stated there was no contradiction between Biden encouraging investors to embrace union workforces while his administration does "whatever it can" to encourage investment and bring jobs to the U.S.
- Samsung has a deal in place with Stellantis to build an electric vehicle battery manufacturing plant in the U.S.
