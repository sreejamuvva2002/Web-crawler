# Hyundai Motor Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-04-20

**Source:** [Three workers died at Hyundai’s Georgia plant since 2022, before US immigration raid, WSJ reports By Reuters](https://www.investing.com/news/stock-market-news/three-workers-died-at-hyundais-georgia-plant-since-2022-before-us-immigration-raid-wsj-reports-4282981)  ·  `page_009948`

## Facts

- Three workers have died since Hyundai Motor started construction of its $7.6 billion auto plant in Georgia in 2022, the Wall Street Journal reported on Sunday, citing a review of federal records.
- Apart from the deaths, more than a dozen workers have suffered serious injuries, including from falling without wearing harnesses and getting crushed by forklifts, according to the WSJ.
- A spokesperson for Hyundai Motor North America stated the company conducted a comprehensive audit and strengthened safety oversight across the site.
- The company enhanced contractor vetting, training, and accountability to ensure all partners meet Hyundai’s standards and legal requirements.
- Dozens of the company’s current and former workers, many of them safety coordinators who helped oversee construction, told the newspaper in interviews that the work environment involved many inexperienced immigrant laborers, often lax safety standards and frequent accidents.
- The plant is operated through a joint venture between Hyundai and South Korea’s LG Energy Solution.
- The facility has been in the limelight since an immigration raid last month that detained hundreds of South Korean workers in the largest single-site enforcement operation in the U.S. Department of Homeland Security’s history.
- Workers interviewed by the WSJ said Hyundai did not ensure that people were properly trained, and safety regulators did little to prevent worksite violations.
- Hyundai CEO Jose Munoz told the newspaper he acted immediately and comprehensively to prevent anything like this from happening again.
- Jose Munoz traveled to Georgia to tell his team directly that their safety comes before production schedules, before costs, before profits, before everything.
- Construction on the electric vehicle and battery plant is ongoing.
- The plant is located in Bryan County about 30 miles west of Savannah.
