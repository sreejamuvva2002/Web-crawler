# Hanon Systems

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hanon Systems joins Georgia EV industry with $40M plant](https://theconstructionbroadsheet.com/hanon-systems-joins-georgia-ev-industry-with-m-plant-p1362-174.htm)  ·  `page_001389`

## Facts

- South Korea-based automotive firm Hanon Systems is joining Georgia's EV industry with a factory investment of more than $40 million.
- Hanon will assemble auto cooling and heating systems at its new facility in Bulloch County, located about 55 miles from the city of Savannah.
- Hanon expects to start operations at the new location in May 2024, according to Gov. Brian Kemp's office.
- Min Sung, Hanon's president and CEO, stated that the company is pleased to contribute to the local economy of Bulloch County through job creation.
- Min Sung thanked the State of Georgia and the cooperation between its agencies for guiding the project.
- Min Sung said the announcement aligns with Hanon's strategy to support the industry shift toward electrification while growing its North America footprint.
- Hanon is locating near the under-construction Hyundai Motor Group "Metaplant" in Bryan County, Georgia.
- The Hyundai Metaplant will manufacture Hyundai, Genesis, and Kia EVs and EV batteries.
- In October, when Hyundai broke ground on the Metaplant project, company officials said its own investment plus that of affiliated suppliers would exceed $5.5 billion.
- Non-affiliated Hyundai suppliers are expected to add at least another $1 billion of facility investments in support of the plant.
- Hyundai Mobis is spending $926 million on a new EV power system plant near the Metaplant.
- Sewon America is investing $300 million in an auto body parts facility near the Metaplant.
- Since 2020, the governor's office reported more than 40 EV-related project announcements in Georgia worth an estimated $22.7 billion.
- Billy Allen, chair of the Development Authority of Bulloch County, said they are excited to welcome another automotive supplier to their community that will work closely with the Hyundai Metaplant and the electric vehicle industry.
- Billy Allen stated that Hanon's decision is another example of the resources offered by their community and region to help companies succeed.
