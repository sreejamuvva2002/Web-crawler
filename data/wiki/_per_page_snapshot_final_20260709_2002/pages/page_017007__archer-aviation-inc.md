# Archer Aviation Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Archer Aviation Showcases Midnight Aircraft In Atlanta As It Nears Completion Of Its High-Volume Manufacturing Facility In Covington, Georgia | FinancialContent](https://www.financialcontent.com/article/bizwire-2024-7-31-archer-aviation-showcases-midnight-aircraft-in-atlanta-as-it-nears-completion-of-its-high-volume-manufacturing-facility-in-covington-georgia)  ·  `page_017007`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation Inc. is building a nearly 400,000 square foot high-volume manufacturing facility in Covington, GA.
- The facility is located adjacent to the Covington Municipal Airport.
- Construction phase one, inclusive of grading and foundation work, was completed earlier this year.
- Phase two, involving the erection of exterior walls and roof, is nearing completion.
- Phase three (interior fit out) and phase four (equipment load in and power on) are on track to be completed in the coming months.
- Archer reaffirmed an expected construction completion date by the end of 2024.
- The facility will support planned production of up to 650 Midnight aircraft annually in partnership with Stellantis.
- This production volume would make it one of the largest manufacturing facilities by volume in the aviation industry.
- Archer recently showcased its Midnight aircraft at Atlantic Aviation’s new terminal at DeKalb–Peachtree Municipal Airport.
- Guests at the showcase included government officials from Georgia’s Office of the Governor, Georgia’s Congressional Offices, the City of Atlanta, Georgia’s State Legislature, the Federal Aviation Administration, and other local governments.
- Adam Goldstein, CEO and founder of Archer, stated that the company is tracking towards completing the manufacturing facility by the end of the year.
- Once complete, Archer will focus on ramping production up to 650 aircraft a year.
- Archer aims to be the first in the eVTOL industry to achieve scaled manufacturing.
- The Midnight aircraft is an electric vertical take-off and landing (eVTOL) aircraft built around Archer’s proprietary electric powertrain.
- The Midnight is designed to offer passengers a sustainable, low-noise, and safe alternative to ground transportation.
- The Midnight travels at speeds up to 150 mph, turning hour-long ground commutes into minutes in the air.
- The Midnight is designed for back-to-back flights of 20-50 miles with minimal charge time in between.
- The Midnight is 100x quieter than a helicopter at cruising altitudes and nearly inaudible from streets below.
- The Midnight features redundant systems allowing Archer to target similar safety levels as commercial airliners.
- The Midnight is designed to carry a pilot, up to four passengers, and carry-on luggage.
- Archer is continuing to progress plans with Stellantis to mass-produce the Midnight aircraft together.
