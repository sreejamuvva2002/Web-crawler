# Passive Component Suppliers

**Type:** supplier_relationship  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** ['Passive' but not inert - EDN](https://www.edn.com/passive-but-not-inert/)  ·  `page_047133`

## Facts

- Top passive component suppliers are investing in new-product development and information technology to automate communications with their supply chain partners.
- Cost-reduction initiatives have translated into plant closings, factory consolidation, manufacturing shifts to low-cost-labor regions in China and Eastern Europe, layoffs, and reduced capital spending.
- Glyndwr Smith serves as assistant to the chief executive and senior vice president at Vishay Intertechnology Inc., located in Malvern, Pa.
- Sandy Beck is vice president of sales and marketing at Kemet Corp., located in Greenville, S.C.
- Inventory levels remain higher than necessary to support current consumption, with excess stock persisting despite not reaching previous highs.
- Lead times for standard products range from four to eight weeks, while delivery times for specialty products range from about eight to ten weeks.
- Prices for capacitors and resistors have continued to drop steadily over the past 12 months, with some tags approaching manufacturing costs.
- Pockets of growth are emerging in end markets such as automotive, game consoles, cell phones, and laptop computers.
- AVX Corp., located in Myrtle Beach, S.C., posted net sales of $294.9 million in its fiscal first quarter ended June 30, with net income of $1.3 million.
- AVX reported unit volume sales were higher than expected because price pressure slowed as production capacity utilization increased.
- Kemet reported $124 million in net sales for the June quarter, a 5% sequential increase, with net earnings of $3.4 million.
- Kemet's unit shipments have increased for three consecutive quarters, while average selling prices declined approximately 5% in the June quarter.
- Kemet has moved ahead with plans to close a plant in Greenwood, S.C., and another in Matamoros, Mexico.
- Kemet lowered raw materials inventories during the quarter by $11 million and finished goods stores by $7.9 million.
- In the past two years, Kemet cut its workforce to 6,500 from a high of about 16,000.
- Vishay expected aggregate unit shipments for its passives, semiconductors, and optoelectronic devices rose 3% to 5% compared with the March quarter.
- Vishay will drop expenditures, including R&D, to approximately $100 million from $160 million last year.
- Taiwan-based Yageo Corp. is expected to cut its capital budget to just $30 million, down from $160 million in 2001.
- About 30% of the EMS industry's total production is manufactured in low-cost-labor centers, including China and elsewhere in Asia, Eastern Europe, and Mexico.
- Many top-tier players want to migrate more than 70% of total production to low-cost regions by 2004.
- The manufacturing shift has resulted in a decline in spending in North America and Europe, leaving the United States as a center for design and new-product development.
- China's passives suppliers are expected to chalk up resistor and capacitor sales of $1.6 billion in 2003, giving the country an 8.9% share of the global market.
- Key Chinese players to watch include Chengdu, Fenghua, Man Yue, Nanyang, and Shenzhen.
- Global suppliers retain a competitive edge because China's passives vendors currently lack advanced products and manufacturing tools, global support services, and brand recognition.
- Production costs of components in China are less than those in Western regions and Japan, but total cost of acquisition associated primarily with shipping costs gives China a significant advantage over Western-based suppliers.
- Vishay has several factories and a strong distribution network in China.
- Japan's Murata Manufacturing Co. Ltd. struck its first joint venture in the early 1990s and has since opened two additional factories with major sales organizations.
- John Denslinger is senior vice president of marketing at Murata Electronics North America Inc., located in Smyrna, Ga.
- Murata Electronics North America is well equipped to handle expansion in China due to its global structure for sales, marketing, and supply chain.
- Rohm Co. Ltd. has shifted manufacturing to China and is making investments to increase production capacity.
- AVX is establishing a facility in China that will come on line in the third quarter to support multinational companies moving into the region.
- Willie King is division vice president of product marketing at AVX.
- Kemet remains one of the few top-tier passives companies with no manufacturing presence in China but has distribution hubs in Asia, including China.
- Kemet expects to have a manufacturing presence in Asia in the not-too-distant future to support existing customers moving to China and introduce itself to local customers.
- Epcos A.G., based in Munich, Germany, is moving production to low-cost areas in both China and Eastern Europe, including Hungary and the Czech Republic.
- Denny Salmang is president and chief executive of Epcos Inc., located in Iselin, N.J.
- Vishay last year acquired two companies, Tansitor and Mallory (a North American capacitor maker), to build up its passives portfolio.
- Vishay plans to roll out 200 new products this year, of which 60 will be passive devices.
- Yageo in 2000 acquired two passive component divisions from Royal Philips Electronics N.V., now marketed under the brand names Ferroxcube and Phycomp.
- Allan H. Cole is chief executive of Yageo America Inc., located in Addison, Texas.
- KOA Corp. of Japan acquired specialty resistor manufacturer Tama Electric Co. Ltd. in 2001.
- KOA now offers metal oxide varistors, resettable fuses, three-pole capacitors, inductors, and ferrite beads to tap into emerging gigahertz designs.
- Jeff Rice is vice president of sales and marketing at subsidiary KOA Speer Electronics Inc., located in Bradford, Pa.
- Kemet's differentiator includes a full complement of surface-mount capacitor technologies, including solid-state aluminum and ceramic and tantalum dielectrics.
- Kemet has continued to expand its high-frequency tantalum and high-capacitance ceramic products.
- New-product introductions have generated significant revenue for Kemet over the past year.
- Kemet is in discussions to supply products to the medical sector by targeting nontraditional markets.
- Yageo's new-product development centers on lead-free components, a new line of miniaturized solid-state antennas, and X2Y attenuators primarily used for filtering in high-frequency applications.
- Suppliers are creating customer and supplier Web portals to make it easier to do business with them.
- AVX, Kemet, and Vishay have the ability to check distributors' inventory via the Web.
- Taiyo Yuden (U.S.A.), located in Schaumburg, Ill., began offering an inventory check function on its Website in July that allows users to retrieve franchised distributor inventory information updated daily.
- Customers can view available quantity, see when inventory information was last updated, download distributor contact information, and send requests for quotes or place purchase orders through the platform.
- Vishay's new MLCC modeling program provides data that engineers can use for complex development of capacitor/circuit behavior.
- AVX and Kemet both support engineers on the Web with SPICE models and software.
- Vishay supported its EMS customers with account managers at the senior management level to coordinate component requirements.
- Murata Electronics North America is focusing on design issues and strengthening its engineering and product management as business shifts from North America and Eastern Europe to Asia.
- Kemet endorses systems that allow communication with end users to provide visibility up and down the supply chain.
- Kemet and passives distributor TTI Inc., located in Fort Worth, Texas, began using RosettaNet Partner Interface Process 3A1 earlier this year to conduct automated quoting.
- The goal of the RosettaNet implementation is to reduce cycle time for quoting and increase accuracy of information exchanged between companies.
