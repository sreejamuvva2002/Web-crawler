# Pat Wilson

**Type:** person  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2017-01-28

**Source:** [Q&A with January/February Cover Feature: GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson – Engineering Georgia Magazine](https://www.engineeringga.com/meet-pat-wilson/)  ·  `page_012018`

## Facts

- Commissioner Pat Wilson accepted his current role at the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) in November 2016.
- Wilson first joined the GDEcD in 2010 as Deputy Commissioner for Global Commerce.
- A year after joining, he became Chief Operating Officer, managing daily operations and serving as the Department’s legislative liaison.
- Wilson is a native of Franklin Springs in Franklin County and a graduate of the University of Georgia.
- Former Governor Sonny Perdue suggested Wilson move into economic development after their conversation following Perdue's last legislative session.
- Governor Perdue believed Wilson would excel at selling Georgia due to his years of lobbying for public policy in Washington and under the Gold Dome.
- Governor Deal has been supportive of Wilson's career path and growing jobs in Georgia.
- Governors and General Assemblies across party lines have a long history of being pro-business in Georgia.
- Governor Deal and his team bolstered Georgia's pro-business reputation, leading to it being named the No. 1 state in the nation to do business for the last four years by Site Selection magazine.
- Post-recession growth has spurred companies to focus on and invest funds into R&D and innovation.
- This investment in R&D and innovation has spurred a rebirth of advanced manufacturing and advanced distribution jobs across Georgia.
- Innovation-related jobs have become a major growth sector for the state.
- The flooring industry has experienced a vibrant rebirth in northwest Georgia.
- The high-tech automotive industry is present in northeast and west Georgia.
- Innovations are occurring in food processing and distribution.
- FinTech, cyber security, and advanced computing-related jobs are growing in the state.
- These industry shifts are directly related to companies placing greater emphasis on innovation, competitiveness, and positioning for prosperity over the next 20 to 30 years.
- Wilson hopes Georgia continues to diversify by leveraging assets such as Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport, the Ports of Savannah and Brunswick, its interstate system, and unrivaled transportation network.
- Wilson also hopes to leverage academic assets produced by technical colleges and universities to continue job growth across industries.
- Wilson would love to see strengthening job growth in rural Georgia.
- Governor Deal tasked the GDEcD with strategically and aggressively pursuing opportunities to grow jobs and investment specifically in rural Georgia.
- All divisions of the department have been working to find growth opportunities in rural communities.
- Consistency in job growth is the department's goal, and they are committed to pursuing that objective.
