# Internal Revenue Code Section 30D Clean Vehicle Tax Credit

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2022-08-16

**Source:** [Credits for new electric vehicles purchased in 2022 or before | Internal Revenue Service](https://www.irs.gov/credits-deductions/credits-for-new-electric-vehicles-purchased-in-2022-or-before)  ·  `page_004201`

## Facts

- A clean vehicle tax credit of up to $7,500 is available under Internal Revenue Code Section 30D for new qualified plug-in electric vehicles purchased in 2022 or before.
- The credit equals $2,917 for a battery capacity of at least 5 kWh plus $417 for each additional kWh over 5 kWh.
- The maximum credit is $7,500 and is nonrefundable, meaning taxpayers cannot receive more back than they owe in taxes or apply excess credits to future tax years.
- The credit is available to individuals and businesses purchasing a qualified new car or light truck for their own use primarily in the U.S.
- Qualified vehicles must have an external charging source, a gross vehicle weight rating of less than 14,000 pounds, and be made by a manufacturer that has not sold more than 200,000 EVs in the U.S.
- Vehicles purchased between August 17, 2022, and December 31, 2022, must undergo final assembly in North America to qualify.
- Buyers who entered a written binding contract after December 31, 2021, but before August 16, 2022, may elect to claim the credit under prior rules if delivery occurs on or after August 16, 2022.
- A written binding contract is generally enforceable under state law and does not limit damages for breach, often indicated by a significant non-refundable deposit or down payment.
- Taxpayers must file Form 8936 with their 2022 tax return to claim the credit for vehicles taken possession of in 2022, including providing the vehicle's VIN.
- The credit for qualified 2-wheeled plug-in electric vehicles expired in 2022, though those purchased in 2021 but placed in service during 2022 may still be eligible.
