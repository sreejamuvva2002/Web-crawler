# Valeo

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Valeo to invest €44M in Autonomous Vehicle RD&I, creating 50 jobs in its R&D centre  in Tuam, Irelan | IDA Ireland](https://www.idaireland.com/latest-news/press-release/valeo-to-invest-€44m-in-autonomous-vehicle-rd-i,-creating-50-jobs-in-its-r-d-centre-in-tuam,-irelan)  ·  `page_012653`

## Facts

- On March 13th 2019, Valeo announced it is to invest €44M in a major RD&I investment programme.
- The investment builds on the Group’s strong capability in Advanced Driver Assistance Systems (ADAS).
- The programme positions Valeo at the forefront of developments in autonomous vehicles.
- The investment will lead to the creation of 50 new R&D roles over the next three years.
- The Irish R&D centre will grow to over 500 people and become one of the largest R&D facilities in Ireland.
- The project is supported by the Irish Government through IDA Ireland.
- Marc Vrecko, Valeo Comfort and Driving Assistance Systems Business Group President, stated that the Tuam facility is one of the leading Research centres in the Group.
- With IDA Ireland support, Valeo continues to have a sustained and rewarding investment in Ireland.
- The expansion aims to build on proven innovation in Product and Process R&D.
- The investment will help fuel the global growth of the Vision Product Line.
- The investment extends Valeo as a global leader for ADAS systems and related MaaS products.
- Sean Canney TD, Minister of State for Rural Affairs & Natural Resources, welcomed the announcement.
- Valeo is described as a substantial and valued employer in Tuam, Galway, and the West region.
- The announcement demonstrates significant commitment by the company to the Tuam site.
- The site positions itself at the leading edge of the development of autonomous vehicles.
- The project shows that global companies can find skilled workers, necessary infrastructure, services, and a pro-business environment in regional locations.
- IDA Ireland CEO Martin Shanahan described the project as focused on new emerging and disruptive advances in Autonomous Vehicles.
- Valeo is characterized as a technology leader in autonomous technologies with a global remit.
- The project helps in the development of a cluster in Ireland around AV.
- The project will add significant value to the company’s operations in Tuam and benefit Tuam, Galway, and the West region substantially.
- Valeo is an automotive supplier at the heart of three revolutions shaping the automotive industry: autonomous car, electrification of the powertrain, and digital mobility.
- As a technology company, Valeo proposes innovative products and systems that contribute to the reduction of CO2 emissions and to the development of autonomous and connected cars.
- In 2018, the Group generated sales of 19.3 billion euros.
- In 2018, the Group invested 13% of its original equipment sales in Research and Development.
- At December 31, 2018, Valeo had 186 plants, 21 research centers, 38 development centers, and 15 distribution platforms.
- At December 31, 2018, Valeo employed 113,600 people in 33 countries worldwide.
- Valeo is listed on the Paris stock exchange and is a member of the CAC 40 index.
- Valeo is the global market leader and supplier of choice for sensors, including vision cameras and ADAS systems.
- Automotive cameras act as the eyes of the vehicle, combined with advanced software algorithms and artificial intelligence to enable vehicles to make the right decision while driving.
- The R&D centre in Tuam is the Group’s worldwide reference centre for vision systems.
- The site includes a state of the art production facility alongside an R&D Centre of Excellence.
- The Tuam centre has responsibility for the development and manufacture of automotive camera vision systems for global markets.
- Products developed by the R&D centre in Tuam are also in production across the world at Valeo sites in Germany, Hungary, Mexico, and China.
- Valeo is a major employer in the West region with a total workforce of over 1,000 people.
- The 450-strong R&D centre consists of many highly skilled engineers and scientists across a range of disciplines.
- The Tuam R&D team works on the latest technologies in Artificial intelligence and Deep Learning for application at the cutting edge of advancements in autonomous vehicles.
