# Roper Corp Cooking Products Manufacturing Facility

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2025-06-24

**Source:** [GE Appliances completes $180M Georgia plant expansion | Manufacturing Dive](https://www.manufacturingdive.com/news/ge-appliances-roper-corp-completes-georgia-cook-tops-ovens-plant-expansion/751344/)  ·  `page_008184`

## Facts

- GE Appliances has completed a $180 million expansion of subsidiary Roper Corp.’s cooking products manufacturing facility in LaFayette, Georgia.
- The investment represents an increase from the company's initial $118 million announcement made in 2021.
- An additional $60 million was allocated toward purchasing additional equipment and expanding production capacity.
- Luther Ingram, president and executive director of Roper, confirmed the investment details via email.
- The combined investment created more than 600 jobs at the facility.
- The expansion enables the addition of automated technology to production lines, including robotic cells that assemble glass cooktops, program control boards, and rotate units.
- Robotics use at the Roper facility has more than tripled since work began in 2023.
- The factory has currently implemented robotics in only 30% of its operations, leaving room for further expansion.
- Roper has focused on upskilling the plant’s workforce alongside investments in robotics and digitization.
- In 2023, Roper obtained grant money from the state of Georgia and partnered with Georgia Northwestern Technical College to fund an apprenticeship program training 45 employees in robotics.
- The new production lines and robotics allow GE Appliances to expand capacity for manufacturing gas, electric, and induction ranges to meet customer demand.
- The investment will accelerate the launch of new range products, specifically the smart-feature GE Profile induction line, which is positioned as more accessible and affordable.
- The Roper facility originally opened in 1973 and currently employs approximately 2,500 people.
- The plant operates as a vertically integrated site incorporating stamping, paint, enamel, welding, graphics, and production processes.
- GE Appliances is a subsidiary of China-based Haier Smart Home, which acquired the company from General Electric in 2016.
- GE Appliances operates 11 manufacturing facilities and microfactories across the United States.
- Five of these U.S. facilities are located at the company’s headquarters in Louisville, Kentucky.
- The remaining four U.S. plants are located in Alabama, Tennessee, South Carolina, Indiana, and Connecticut.
