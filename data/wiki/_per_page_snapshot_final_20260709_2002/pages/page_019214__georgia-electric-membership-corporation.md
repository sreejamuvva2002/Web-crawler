# Georgia Electric Membership Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2025-04-08

**Source:** [Georgia EMC Announces New Leadership for Community and Economic Development Team - Georgia Electric Membership Corporation](https://georgiaemc.com/news/698056/Georgia-EMC-Announces-New-Leadership-for-Community-and-Economic-Development-Team.htm)  ·  `page_019214`

## Facts

- Georgia Electric Membership Corporation (EMC) promoted Bradley Harris to vice president, community and economic development.
- Dennis Chastain serves as president/CEO of Georgia EMC.
- Harris joined Georgia EMC in July 2003.
- For the last several years prior to his promotion, Harris served as Georgia EMC’s manager of economic development.
- Harris leads Georgia EMC’s economic development team that partners with the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) in marketing the state to new business and industry.
- Over the last 22 years, Harris has supported GDEcD on hundreds of projects that have created thousands of new jobs for Georgia citizens.
- Prior to his time at Georgia EMC, Harris served as a project manager for the Georgia Department of Industry, Trade & Tourism.
- Harris is an active member of several national, regional, and state professional organizations, including the Industrial Asset Management Council (IAMC) and the Georgia Economic Development Association (GEDA).
- Harris serves on the board of directors of the Southeast US Korean Chamber of Commerce.
- Last year, Harris was named one of North America’s Top 50 Economic Developer Award recipients by Consultant Connect.
- Georgia EMC is the statewide trade association representing the state’s 41 electric cooperatives, Oglethorpe Power Corp., Georgia Transmission Corp. and Georgia System Operations Corp.
- Collectively, Georgia’s customer-owned co-ops provide electricity and related services to 4.4 million people, nearly half of Georgia’s population, across 73 percent of the state’s land area.
