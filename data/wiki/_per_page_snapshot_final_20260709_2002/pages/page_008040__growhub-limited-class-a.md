# GrowHub Limited Class A

## Facts

- On May 28, 2026, GrowHub Limited signed a legally binding summary term sheet with EnChem America for a reverse triangular merger.
- Under the deal structure, EnChem will merge into a newly formed GrowHub subsidiary and become the surviving entity.
- EnChem’s shareholders are slated to receive 85% of the combined company’s equity on a fully diluted basis, leaving existing GrowHub holders with 15%, subject to customary regulatory and shareholder approvals, including Nasdaq rules on new share issuance.
- The deal values EnChem at a minimum of $400 million and treats it as the accounting acquirer.
- The transaction represents a transformative reverse takeover that would effectively pivot GrowHub into the battery electrolyte materials sector.
- The structure includes a valuation floor that allows EnChem to walk away if GrowHub’s trading price falls below $4 before closing.
- The deal is designed to support substantial post-merger capital raising via a large shelf registration and follow-on financings once the combined company is in place.
- GrowHub Limited is a Cayman Islands–incorporated holding company listed on the Nasdaq Capital Market under the symbol TGHL and reporting as a foreign private issuer.
- GrowHub serves as a public vehicle for corporate combinations and capital-raising transactions and is now positioning itself as the listed platform for EnChem America, a U.S. subsidiary of Korea’s EnChem Co., focused on battery electrolyte materials.
