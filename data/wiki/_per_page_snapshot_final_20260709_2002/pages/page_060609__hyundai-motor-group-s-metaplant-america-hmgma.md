# Hyundai Motor Group’s MetaPlant America (HMGMA)

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-03-30

**Source:** [Georgia’s HMGMA: Hyundai’s vision for automated, flexible car production](https://www.chosun.com/english/industry-en/2025/03/30/AN73FG6EIJF4XLBXEMP7ZJEHRA/)  ·  `page_060609`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group’s new MetaPlant America (HMGMA) is located in Ellaville, Georgia.
- The plant began construction in October 2022.
- HMGMA was completed after just two and a half years of construction.
- The facility departs from the conventional car factory model by combining human labor with advanced automation.
- Approximately 880 human workers are employed at the plant.
- Over 950 robots are deployed across the facility.
- More than 200 autonomous transport robots are used for parts transportation and vehicle handling.
- 48 parking robots are deployed at the site.
- 700 robots are involved in welding and painting tasks.
- A line of over 10 waist-height robots can be seen moving parts across the factory floor upon entering.
- Autonomous mobile robots (AMRs) communicate with a central control system to track production progress and optimize movements in real-time.
- Unlike traditional plants where presswork and car body construction are automated, component sorting and transportation at HMGMA are handled by AMRs.
- A company official stated: “This plant embodies Hyundai’s vision for the future and its manufacturing capabilities.”
- Boston Dynamics' four-legged robot “Spot” inspects the vehicle’s exterior in the final assembly stage by capturing images and comparing them with reference points to identify defects.
- Hyundai has introduced the world’s first system for automatically removing and installing vehicle doors using robots equipped with AI-powered camera systems.
- Dozens of cameras have been integrated into the press process to detect imperfections in finished panels.
- The painting process incorporates an innovative system that captures and analyzes around 50,000 images per vehicle to ensure quality.
- These technological advancements are part of Hyundai’s ongoing efforts to experiment with future mobility solutions at HMGMA.
- The plant is designed to support “low-volume, high-variety production,” reflecting a shift from mass production to autonomous driving, connected cars, and in-car technologies.
- Some areas of the assembly plant have no conveyor belts, instead using autonomous transport robots to carry car bodies for a flexible production flow that adapts to different models and options.
- A company official stated: “By overcoming the limitations of conveyor belts, we expect our production system to evolve, bypassing unnecessary steps and streamlining processes.”
- The company plans to pilot the “All-New Atlas” humanoid robot, developed by its robotics affiliate Boston Dynamics, in the near future.
