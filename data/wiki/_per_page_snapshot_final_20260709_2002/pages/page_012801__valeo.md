# Valeo

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** thermal_management  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Valeo's Heat Pump Strategy for EV Winter Range](https://thestorytellingtribe.com/valeo-ev-range-winter-heat-pump-eesm/)  ·  `page_012801`

## Facts

- Claudine Rochette serves as VP of Strategy and Communication for Valeo’s Power Division.
- Rochette joined Johnson Controls in 2003 as a systems integration engineer, surviving its division's acquisition by Valeo in 2005.
- She has led hybrid platforms, 48V electronics, and full EV powertrain programmes from inside the industry.
- Thermal management is identified as the defining frontier of EV performance rather than battery chemistry.
- Electric cars lose winter range primarily because they lack a combustion engine’s waste heat.
- Valeo’s integrated heat pumps recover over 25% of lost winter range by redistributing thermal energy already present in the vehicle.
- Over 90% of EV motors today depend on rare earth magnets primarily sourced from China.
- Valeo’s EESM technology and the Valeo-MAHLE iBEE system eliminate rare earth dependency for vehicles up to 350 kW.
- OTA updates allow continuous motor efficiency improvement for 10 to 15 years after a vehicle leaves production.
- Purchase price parity between EVs and petrol cars is now an active engineering target rather than a distant aspiration.
- On first-generation electric cars, winter heating could decrease range by half, with a car rated for 400 to 500 kilometres delivering barely 200 on a January commute.
- This range loss is caused by a thermal architecture designed around a heat source that no longer exists, not battery degradation.
- Valeo formed its Power Division in 2024 by merging its Powertrain Systems and Thermal Systems business groups for engineering reasons.
- The Power Division’s integrated system simultaneously handles electrical energy, mechanical energy, and thermal energy across the vehicle.
- More than 90% of EV motors are permanent magnet synchronous motors (PMSM) using heavy rare earths, creating structural supply chain vulnerabilities for European and American OEMs due to China's concentration of processing and production.
- Rare earth extraction and processing generate significant CO2 emissions, resulting in a bad carbon footprint.
- Valeo’s EESM (Externally Excited Synchronous Motor) generates its electromagnetic field using copper windings and an induced electrical current in the rotor instead of permanent magnets.
- The EESM technology cuts the electric motor's carbon footprint by more than 40% compared to an equivalent permanent magnet motor.
- Renault and BMW have used variants of EESM technology.
- Supplying current to a high-speed spinning rotor traditionally uses brushes, which require roughly 5 centimetres of extra packaging space and wear out over the vehicle's lifetime.
- Valeo and MAHLE jointly announced the iBEE (Inner Brushless Electrical Excitation) system in October 2024 to eliminate physical brushes.
- The iBEE system uses inductive power transfer, the same physics as wireless phone charging, to supply current to the rotor without physical contact.
- MAHLE contributes its Contactless Transmitter (MCT) technology for the brushless rotor, while Valeo provides the inverter, motor control algorithms, and high-efficiency power electronics.
- The combined system targets peak power of 220 to 350 kW for premium segment vehicles.
- This partnership extends a programme Valeo began in 2022 with an unnamed European OEM.
- The first EESM validation program covered B and C segment vehicles, completed more than six months of testing, and exceeded initial targets on both power density and efficiency.
- Valeo now operates software maintenance contracts with OEMs that extend active development work up to 10 to 15 years beyond a vehicle's Start of Production.
- Approximately 25% of Tier 1 automotive software coding is now AI-assisted, a figure that stood at zero in 2016.
- Valeo is developing a software layer called Valeo Predict for Range that draws on weather data, GPS routing, driving patterns, and vehicle sensor inputs to optimize energy management in real time.
- EVs are more reliable than conventional cars due to simpler construction, architecture, and fewer actuators or sensors.
- US-based taxi company Tesloop documented individual Tesla vehicles exceeding one million kilometres.
- OEMs actively monitor battery state of health (SOH) through OTA telemetry to detect degradation before it affects the driver.
- Renault managed early battery reliability by leasing the battery pack for the first-generation Zoe so it could be replaced if SOH declined below an acceptable threshold.
- Battery pack costs have fallen sharply due to volume scaling and manufacturing efficiency, eliminating the need for a chemistry breakthrough to reach viability.
- Similar cost reductions have flowed through Valeo’s own product lines, including inverters, onboard chargers, and motor systems.
- The upfront purchase price for EVs currently remains 20 to 30% higher than a comparable combustion vehicle, but achieving parity is now an active commercial objective.
