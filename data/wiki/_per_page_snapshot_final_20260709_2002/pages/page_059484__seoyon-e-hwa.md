# Seoyon E-Hwa

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [GA: Auto Parts Manufacturer Seoyon E-HWA to Create 740 Jobs With Chatham County Facility | Trade and Industry Development](https://www.tradeandindustrydev.com/region/georgia/news/ga-auto-parts-manufacturer-seoyon-e-hwa-create-740-31371)  ·  `page_059484`

## Facts

- Georgia Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Seoyon E-HWA will create 740 new direct and indirect jobs.
- Seoyon E-HWA will invest almost $76 million in a new manufacturing facility in Chatham County.
- Seoyon E-HWA is a global auto parts supplier that manufactures both interior and exterior components.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp referenced his announcement of the largest economic development project in Georgia history from the previous year.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated that Georgia aims to be the e-mobility capital of the nation and remains the No. 1 state for business.
- Seoyon E-Hwa was established in 1972.
- Seoyon E-Hwa operates 32 corporations across North America, South America, Europe, and Asia.
- Seoyon E-Hwa supplies door trim, headlining, seats, C/PAD, bumpers, and other parts to Hyundai Motor Group, Kia, Mercedes-Benz, Ford, Volkswagen, and others.
- Seoyon’s existing LaGrange facility in Georgia will continue to support 630 full-time jobs.
- Seoyon E-Hwa Chairman Ryu Yang-seok stated the company manufactures automobiles in Korea, Europe, and India.
- Seoyon E-Hwa will supply electric vehicle parts at its new Chatham County, Georgia facility.
- The company hopes its new Savannah facility will make Savannah a central city for automobile development and production.
- Seoyon E-Hwa plans to continuously cooperate with the local government regarding its Savannah expansion.
- Seoyon’s new manufacturing facility will be located at the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center.
- The Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center is a Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) Certified site.
- The new facility will produce automotive interior parts including door trims, headlining, and tailgate trims.
- Production at the new facility is expected to begin in October 2024.
- Approximately 500 of the 740 announced jobs will be permanent roles in human resources, development, quality, and production.
- Savannah Economic Development Authority President and CEO Trip Tollison noted the new facility's location is ideally positioned near Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America, the Port of Savannah, and Interstates I-16 and I-95.
- The new facility will provide well-paying jobs for the Savannah region.
- Seoyon currently uses the Port of Savannah to supply its existing manufacturing facility in West Georgia.
- The Georgia Ports Authority supports Seoyon's growth, noting positive ripple effects from the Hyundai announcement on the regional economy.
- GDEcD Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy Alyce Thornhill represented the agency on this project.
- The project was developed in partnership with the Savannah Economic Development Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia Power.
- This will be Seoyon’s second facility in Georgia.
- The new facility will nearly double the number of jobs Seoyon supports in Georgia.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated Hyundai’s new EV facility is drawing an experienced supplier network to the region.
- The growth is expected to add to the state’s industry expertise and shape a larger skilled workforce.
- Georgia is positioned as a hub for the electric mobility industry.
- Since 2020, more than 35 EV-related projects have announced over $21 billion in investment in Georgia.
- Since 2020, more than 35 EV-related projects have announced the creation of 26,700 jobs in Georgia.
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America officially broke ground on October 25 for its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility in neighboring Bryan County.
- Offsite suppliers connected to the Hyundai project have already exceeded the predicted $1 billion in investment by more than $600 million.
