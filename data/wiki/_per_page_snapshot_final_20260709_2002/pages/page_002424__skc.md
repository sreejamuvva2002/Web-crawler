# SKC

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** power_electronics  ·  **Published:** 2021-10-28

**Source:** [SK Group to Locate Glass-based Semiconductor-part Venture in Covington | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-releases/2021/sk-group-locate-first-its-kind-glass-based-semiconductor-part-venture-covington)  ·  `page_002424`

## Facts

- SKC, a subsidiary of SK Group, will manufacture glass-based substrates for semiconductor chips in Covington, Georgia.
- The company will invest more than $473 million in this unprecedented venture.
- The project will create more than 400 new jobs in Newton County.
- Production is expected to ramp up by late summer 2023.
- The new facility will be developed on existing SKC property located at 3000 SKC Drive in Covington.
- SKC will primarily hire high-tech engineers, skilled technicians, and other semiconductor field-experienced talent.
- Dr. Sung Jin Kim, SKC Director of New Business Development, served as a research professor at the Georgia Institute of Technology from 2012 to 2015.
- Professor Kim helped develop the glass substrate technology through research conducted at the Georgia Tech Packaging Research Center with the Center Team and global semiconductor supply chain companies.
- SKC was founded in 1976 as a specialty material company and established its Newton County, Georgia facility in 1996.
- The existing Covington facility is one of the major Polyester films (PET film) producers in North America.
- During the COVID-19 pandemic, the Covington facility pivoted manufacturing to produce face shields and other PPE safety products.
- SKC has expanded its business to copper foil and next-generation cathode/anode materials for rechargeable batteries.
- The company developed the world’s first glass substrate technology for high-performance semiconductors, following high-end blank masks and CMP pads.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced the project and signed a ceremonial Memorandum of Understanding with SKC to solidify the location.
- Lanier Sims, Chairman of the Newton County Industrial Development Authority, highlighted SK’s long-term partnership with the county.
- Ángel Cabrera, President of Georgia Tech, noted that 25-30 years of Georgia Tech’s investment in packaging research and advanced circuitry contributed to Georgia being selected as the best location.
- Yoonie Kim, Director of Korean Investment at the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD), represented the Global Commerce division on the project.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated the investment will directly support desperately needed domestic manufacturing of semiconductors.
- SK Group is one of the largest conglomerates in South Korea, and this facility marks its third major investment in Georgia.
- The announcement notes that SK Innovation is investing nearly $2.6 billion to develop two battery manufacturing facilities in Jackson County to supply electric vehicles.
- Governor Kemp described the announcement as a prime example of Georgia addressing a pressing U.S. supply chain roadblock affecting manufacturers.
- Dr. Kim stated the new technology will enable utmost performance with minimal power consumption for high-performance computing and high-speed communication applications, serving as a basecamp for SKC’s AI and data center semiconductor applications.
