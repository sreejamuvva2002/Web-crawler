# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-02

**Source:** [State Leaders Celebrate Hyundai Motor Group’s Historic Impact on Georgia - Athens CEO](https://athensceo.com/features/2024/02/state-leaders-celebrate-hyundai-motor-groups-historic-impact-georgia/)  ·  `page_060608`

## Facts

- Governor Brian Kemp and First Lady Marty Kemp joined President and Global COO of Hyundai Motor Company and President and CEO of Hyundai and Genesis Motor North America José Muñoz to celebrate Hyundai Motor Group’s historic impact on Georgia and provide an update on Metaplant America progress.
- The Metaplant America facility will officially start vehicle production in the fourth quarter of this year.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated Hyundai Motor Group has been an incredible partner to Georgia for decades and highlighted the historic benefits the project brings to Georgians.
- Governor Kemp noted Hyundai helps recruit businesses, gives back to communities, and partners with world-class colleges and universities to connect Georgians with opportunities.
- Hyundai and Genesis electric vehicles on display included the IONIQ 5, IONIQ 6, GV60, Electrified GV70, and the hydrogen-powered XCIENT Fuel Cell Truck used for logistics between Hyundai’s manufacturing facilities and suppliers.
- Governor Kemp officially declared February 26 as Hyundai Day throughout the state of Georgia.
- The Georgia General Assembly passed a Resolution commending Hyundai Motor Group’s dedication to the State of Georgia and recognizing February 26 as Hyundai Day at the State Capitol.
- José Muñoz stated Hyundai’s investments in Georgia will create tens of thousands of great paying American jobs and bring tens of billions of dollars in economic impact for decades to come.
- Since the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America project was announced in May 2022, LG Energy Solution was named as the joint venture partner in the manufacture of battery components.
- An additional expansion of the LG Energy Solution battery facility was announced.
- Hyundai and SK On are building an EV battery manufacturing facility in Bartow County that will supply Hyundai Motor Group’s U.S. plants.
- Since the initial announcement of HMGMA’s location, Hyundai Motor Group’s planned investment in Georgia has reached more than $12.6 billion.
- The project brings with it the creation of 12,000 direct, long-term jobs.
- Lieutenant Governor Burt Jones and House Speaker Jon Burns joined legislative leaders, GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson, local elected officials from the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority, and company representatives at the celebration.
- Lt. Governor Burt Jones applauded Governor Brian Kemp, state agencies, and the University System of Georgia (especially Georgia Tech) for their partnership with Hyundai.
- Lt. Governor Jones stated partnerships foster a thriving environment to grow Georgia's businesses and workforce.
- Up to 300,000 Hyundai, Genesis, and Kia EVs are expected to be produced annually at the Metaplant.
- 17 HMGMA suppliers have announced projects across 10 Georgia counties spanning beyond southeast Georgia.
- Further supplier announcements are expected.
- Supplier facilities are expected to create more than 6,700 new jobs and more than $2.5 billion in investment.
- Supplier facilities will be operational prior to the opening of HMGMA.
- Speaker of the House Jon Burns credited Governor Kemp and the General Assembly for sound policy direction supporting Hyundai's Southeast Georgia investment.
- Speaker Burns stated Hyundai's Metaplant will have a generational impact as a force multiplier for investments in education, healthcare, housing, and transportation.
- The nearly 3,000-acre Metaplant site has four building exteriors complete with outfitting for internal operations underway.
- The Metaplant’s architecture incorporates technologically advanced elements plus design features representing the Savannah area, Hyundai’s brand identity, nature, and clean energy.
- HMGMA is hiring full-time employees, known as Meta Pros, at a rapid pace for training on machinery, office/clerical work, and other roles.
- HMGMA currently has 566 full-time employees.
- Approximately 75 percent of the Meta Pro workforce is from the Greater Savannah area.
- An additional 37 employees are from Georgia but outside the local area.
- Georgia Quick Start broke ground on its customized training center at the Metaplant site on February 8.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated Georgians will be trained in high-skill careers as Meta Pros at one of the most advanced vehicle assembly and battery plants in the world.
- HMGMA partnered with local technical colleges to prepare students for entry-level employment at the Metaplant.
- Electrical Vehicle Professional Technical Certificate of Credit (EVP TCC) training is available at Coastal Pines Technical College’s Baxley and Waycross campuses, Southeastern Technical College’s Vidalia campus, Savannah Technical College, and Ogeechee Technical College.
- TCSG Commissioner Greg Dozier stated technical colleges are providing comprehensive training programs tailored to the growing EV industry in Georgia.
- In September 2023, Georgia Institute of Technology (Georgia Tech) and Hyundai Motor Company signed a memorandum of understanding to collaborate on research for sustainable mobility, including developing a hydrogen economy, workforce development, and smart city initiatives.
- U.S. News & World Report ranked Georgia Tech as the top public university and No. 3 nationally in energy and fuel research in February 2024.
- Georgia Tech President Ángel Cabrera stated the strategic partnership with Hyundai is one of their most significant corporate relationships and aims to advance science for emerging technologies and produce the next-generation workforce.
- Since 1998, Hyundai has directed over $6 million towards local Georgia communities through philanthropic programs and partnerships.
- Hyundai is promoting STEM programs in local K-12 schools.
- The company hosts educational programs on alternative energy sources.
- Hyundai supports local organizations dedicated to affordable homeownership.
- The company focuses on environmental stewardship while championing the regional community.
- Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority’s Trip Tollison stated Hyundai has given more than $1 million to regional nonprofits and formed key partnerships with local educational institutions.
- Trip Tollison cited a commitment of $7.59 billion in capital investment in Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- Trip Tollison stated the project will create 8,500 jobs.
- More than 550 Meta Pros have already been hired, with 75 percent coming from the Savannah region.
- Beginning with mass production, the electricity used to power the Metaplant will come from 100 percent clean power sources.
- This aligns with Hyundai’s commitment to carbon neutrality by 2045.
- A 41-acre park will be featured in front of the plant, including trails, running tracks, environmental art displays, sports fields, and other amenities for employee use.
