# Erdrich Umformtechnik GmbH

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Erdrich Umformtechnik GmbH | Unternehmen | Historie](https://www.erdrich.de/en/unternehmen/historie/)  ·  `page_019991`

## Facts

- Michael Weis becomes the new CEO of the Erdrich Group in 2023, forming a new management team with CFO Joachim Schulz.
- Franz-Bernd Schenke joins the Management Board in 2020, taking over from Dr. Jürgen Simon, who moves to lead the Technology division.
- Georg Erdrich passes company succession to the next generation in 2017, with Nicolas Erdrich, Joachim Schulz, and Dr. Jürgen Simon assuming management.
- Erdrich establishes a new plant in Changzhou, China in 2015, where suspension arms and a broad range of formed parts for the auto industry are produced.
- Erdrich expands into international markets in 2013 by establishing a new plant in Dublin, Georgia, USA.
- In 2011, the SANDER-Firmengruppe merges production facilities Erdrich and SANDER under the brand name ERDRICH Umformtechnik.
- The SANDER group of companies establishes a branch in Taicang, China in 2010.
- In 2006, the SANDER group builds a plant in Krnov, Czech Republic, developing one of the most modern brake piston plants there.
- Due to the success of steel brake pistons, Erdrich establishes a second plant at the Sömmerda site in 2003, doubling capacity.
- Sander Automation GmbH becomes an independent part of the SANDER group in 2003.
- Erdrich Umformtechnik GmbH & Co. KG becomes independent in 2002 with shareholders Nicolas and Georg Erdrich, remaining part of the SANDER group alongside SANDER Umformtechnik.
- Further development of SANDER transfer devices allows their use in warm and hot working of metals for the first time in 1995.
- Adolf Hättig retires in 1993, allowing Georg Erdrich to assume sole management.
- A subsidiary designated Erdrich Umformtechnik GmbH & Co. is established in Thuringian Sömmerda in 1992.
- The first large press is installed at the company in 1987.
- SANDER Automation GmbH, which produces transfer units, is established as a subsidiary in Renchen-Ulm in 1979.
- The parent company begins developing smaller devices with intermediary speeds for forming sheet metal in 1979.
- Georg Erdrich and Adolf Hättig assume management of the company in 1977.
- Due to company growth, headquarters are relocated from Oberkirch to Renchen-Ulm in 1975, establishing the company's reputation in the auto industry as an efficient supplier.
- Rolf Sander and Adolf Hättig lay the foundation of SANDER Umformtechnik GmbH & Co. KG in Oberkirch in 1962, initially producing cutting and punching tools for chipless sheet forming before expanding to punching and bending parts in series.
