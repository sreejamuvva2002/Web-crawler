# Doowon Climate Control America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2024-02-12

**Source:** [Doowan Climate Control America’s new Georgia factory to supply Hyundai | Manufacturing Dive](https://www.manufacturingdive.com/news/hyundai-supplier-doowon-climate-control-america-metter-georgia/707170/)  ·  `page_019230`

## Facts

- Auto parts manufacturer Doowon Climate Control America will build a $30 million factory in Metter, Georgia.
- Gov. Brian Kemp announced the project in a Feb. 6 state release.
- The Metter facility will be the South Korea-based company’s first U.S. site.
- The facility will manufacture climate control systems for gas-powered and electric vehicles.
- The investment will create over 200 jobs, including injection molding technicians, assembly operators, quality, materials, and production specialists and engineers.
- Operations at the Metter facility are expected to begin in 2026.
- Doowon is the second Hyundai supplier to establish a facility in Metter to serve the automaker’s upcoming $5.5 billion EV manufacturing site in Ellabell, Georgia.
- In September, car seat maker Das Corp. unveiled plans to establish a $35 million plant in Metter.
- Das’ facility will produce automotive seat structures when operations begin in the second half of 2024.
- Georgia has welcomed more than $2.5 billion in investment from a total of 15 manufacturers that will supply Hyundai’s plant in Ellabell.
- The growing manufacturing network is creating over 6,000 jobs, according to Gov. Brian Kemp in November.
- Hyundai is building a massive EV manufacturing campus in Georgia.
- In September, Hyundai and LG Energy Solution invested an additional $2 billion in their joint battery cell factory, bringing the total investment to $6.3 billion.
- The joint battery cell plant will be adjacent to Hyundai’s EV campus.
- The push for domestic EV manufacturing has attracted a host of foreign auto suppliers to the U.S. to serve major automakers.
- Ford’s upcoming EV manufacturing campus in Tennessee has attracted several auto parts manufacturers.
- Canada-based Magna International plans to invest $790 million to build three facilities in Tennessee, two of which will be part of Ford’s BlueOval City supplier park.
- Germany-based auto part supplier ZF Group is investing $500 million to expand its transmission facility in Gray Court, South Carolina, to serve customers like BMW, Tesla and Mercedes-Benz.
