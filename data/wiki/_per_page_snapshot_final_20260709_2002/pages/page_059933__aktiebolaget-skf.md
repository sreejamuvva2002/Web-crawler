# Aktiebolaget SKF

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-25

**Source:** [SKF invests in startup Anferra to transform industry’s toughest waste problems into circular resources - SKF](https://news.cision.com/skf/r/skf-invests-in-startup-anferra-to-transform-industry-s-toughest-waste-problems-into-circular-resourc,c4367605)  ·  `page_059933`

## Facts

- SKF announced a strategic investment, together with Stephen Industries and Chalmers Ventures, in startup Anferra AB to tackle hazardous steel grinding sludge.
- Grinding sludge is a finely powdered mixture of metal particles, abrasives, and contaminated grinding emulsion generated during metal grinding operations.
- Globally, around 12 million tonnes of grinding sludge are produced each year, with most non-recycled volumes handled through incineration or landfill.
- Anferra's innovative process converts steel grinding sludge into ferric chloride, a common chemical agent used for wastewater and water treatment.
- Another by-product of the process is hydrogen gas, which can be further used as an energy carrier contributing to green energy solutions.
- The technology enables a potential net climate benefit of negative 470 kg CO₂-equivalents per tonne of sludge compared to conventional disposal.
- This climate benefit equals driving an electric car for 7,500 km per tonne of sludge processed.
- The process achieves up to 90% iron recovery with significantly lower energy demand.
- The technology has the potential to shift grinding sludge from being treated as hazardous waste towards becoming a secondary raw material.
- This shift aligns with EU efforts to harmonise waste regulations and build a stronger market for secondary raw materials.
- Mikael Krook, Director of SKF Ventures, stated that Anferra's approach represents a smarter way forward for circularity and decarbonization in the steel and bearing industry.
- This investment is the first initiative coming out of SKF Ventures, SKF's initiative to accelerate new innovations.
- Ebba Adolfsson, CEO and Co-founder of Anferra, expressed happiness at deepening collaboration with SKF to accelerate industrial implementation globally.
- Stephen Industries brings expertise in scaling high-impact deeptech and greentech ventures.
- SKF contributes industrial application knowledge, waste stream volume, and decades of experience in sustainability leadership.
- Chalmers Ventures provides venture building capabilities and close connection to the research ecosystem for commercialization.
- SKF applies circularity principles across its operations, including remanufacturing bearings and circular oil use via RecondOil.
- Other SKF initiatives include exploring recycled steel solutions with ultra-low emissions and investigating hydrometallurgical methods to minimize resource loss.
- SKF aims to accelerate the transition to a net-zero, more circular industrial ecosystem.
- Since 1907, SKF has produced bearings, seals, lubrication systems, condition monitoring solutions, and services to reduce friction and save energy.
- SKF is represented in approximately 130 countries with around 17,000 distributor locations worldwide.
- SKF's annual sales in 2025 were SEK 91,583 million, with 37,271 employees.
