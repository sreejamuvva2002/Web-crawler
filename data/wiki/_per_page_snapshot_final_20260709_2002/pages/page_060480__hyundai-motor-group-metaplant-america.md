# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Group breaks ground on Metaplant America dedicated to EV and battery production](https://www.evspecifications.com/en/news/0b4d6a7)  ·  `page_060480`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group and its affiliate suppliers announced a $5.54 billion investment five months prior to the article's publication date of 25 October 2022.
- The project includes plans to produce a diverse range of innovative Hyundai, Genesis, and Kia electric vehicles (EVs) alongside a new battery manufacturing facility.
- HMGMA will create more than 8,100 jobs over the next few years.
- The company aims to establish a stable supply chain for EV batteries and other EV components in the U.S. market through this project.
- HMGMA's new smart factory is expected to begin commercial production in the first half of 2025.
- Hyundai Motor Group Executive Chair Euisun Chung stated that Hyundai and the people of Georgia share qualities including respect for history, ingenuity, creativity, and determination to improve the next generation's world.
- Euisun Chung noted that Hyundai's EVs are recognized as best in class and expressed determination to become a global leader in electrification, safety, quality, and sustainability through this partnership.
- Euisun Chung added that with the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America, the company will evolve beyond an automaker into a leading global mobility solutions provider.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp stated that the state's economy is reaching new heights due to record jobs, investment, workforce development programs, and infrastructure benefiting Georgians.
- Governor Brian Kemp said the partnership with Hyundai Motor Group and the groundbreaking of this innovative facility exemplify Georgia's unprecedented economic success.
- Governor Brian Kemp expressed anticipation for continuing the partnership and observing its positive impact on the local community and the entire state.
- Approximately $1 billion is anticipated to be invested by non-affiliated HMGMA suppliers in connection with the project in other locations.
- Hyundai Motor Group aims to lead the U.S. auto market's EV transition with the new facility.
- The Group has a global target to sell 3.23 million full electric vehicles annually by 2030 as part of its accelerated electrification efforts.
- To achieve its sales target, the Group plans to establish a global EV production network ensuring a stable worldwide supply of EVs.
- With additional U.S. EV and battery production capabilities, the Group aims to maintain its position as one of the top three EV providers in the United States.
- The Group plans to lead the EV market through advancements in design, technologies, and mobility solutions optimized for the electric mobility era.
- The investment aligns with the U.S. government's roadmap to accelerate electrification, coinciding with other global OEMs expanding local U.S. EV production.
- Last year, the U.S. government announced an initiative requiring zero-emission vehicle sales to account for at least half of OEMs' new vehicle sales by 2030.
- The new plant will feature a highly connected, automated, and flexible manufacturing system that organically connects all elements of the EV ecosystem to realize customer value.
- The Georgia facility will operate as an intelligent manufacturing plant.
- All production processes, including order collection, procurement, logistics, and production, will be optimized using AI and data.
- The innovative manufacturing system will create a human-centered work environment where robots assist human workers.
- As part of its sustainability commitment, the plant will primarily rely on renewable energy sources and use emission-reduction technologies to meet RE100 requirements.
- In addition to job creation, HMG announced new connections with the local economy to enrich community health through corporate social responsibility programs.
- Corporate social responsibility donations to date total $1.54 million.
