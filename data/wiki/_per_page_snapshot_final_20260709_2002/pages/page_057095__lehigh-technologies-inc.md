# Lehigh Technologies Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2017-10-18

**Source:** [Michelin Acquires Lehigh Technologies, a Specialty Materials Company | www.inknowvation.com](https://www.inknowvation.com/sbir/story/michelin-acquires-lehigh-technologies-specialty-materials-company)  ·  `page_057095`

## Facts

- Michelin acquired Lehigh Technologies, a specialty materials company based in Tucker, Ga., near Atlanta.
- The acquisition was completed on Oct. 13.
- Lehigh Technologies uses patented cryogenic turbo mill technology to transform rubber from end-of-life tires and industrial goods into materials for new tires and other products.
- This process reduces the amount of raw materials initially needed, such as elastomers and fillers from oil- and rubber-based sources.
- Lehigh Technologies employs about 100 people.
- The firm produces highly engineered, versatile raw materials called micronized rubber powder (MRP).
- MRP is a low-cost, high-performance, sustainable material that substitutes for other oil- and rubber-based materials used in manufacturing tires, plastics, asphalt and construction materials.
- Its customers include some of the largest tire companies in the world, as well as companies in construction materials, asphalt modification and other markets.
- Pete Selleck, chairman and president of Michelin North America, stated that the acquisition demonstrates a strategic intent to achieve safer and more sustainable mobility using high-technology recycled materials without compromising safety or performance.
- Christophe Rahier, senior vice president of strategic planning materials for the Michelin Group, noted that the acquisition extends Michelin's materials expertise beyond tires to foster advanced rubber recycled materials in tire and non-tire industries.
- Alan Barton, CEO of Lehigh Technologies, said the deal provides an opportunity to continue growth, expand capabilities, execute a global growth strategy, and pursue new market opportunities with full backing from Michelin.
- Michelin North America is headquartered in Greenville, S.C., employs about 22,700 people, and operates 19 major manufacturing plants.
- Michelin designs, manufactures and sells tires for every type of vehicle, including airplanes, automobiles, bicycles, earthmovers, farm equipment, heavy-duty trucks and motorcycles.
