# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-05-05

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp Signs FY24 Budget to Invest in Public Safety, Healthcare, the Workforce, and More | Governor Brian P. Kemp Office of the Governor](https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases/2023-05-05/gov-kemp-signs-fy24-budget-invest-public-safety-healthcare-workforce-and)  ·  `page_000079`

## Facts

- Gov. Brian P. Kemp signed FY24 Budget (HB 19) and HB 408 on May 5, 2023, at a signing ceremony on the site of the Hyundai Metaplant in Bryan County, Ellabell, GA.
- Georgia has been named the number one state to do business for nine consecutive years.
- Georgia's unemployment rate is 3.1 percent.
- In less than 365 days prior to May 2023, four of the largest economic development projects in Georgia history were announced.
- Hyundai announced a project bringing 8,100 jobs and over $5.54 billion in investment to Bryan County.
- Suppliers for the Hyundai Metaplant are relocating to Georgia, collectively announcing over 4,500 new jobs and roughly $1.96 billion in investment.
- The supplier investment figure is nearly double the original estimate, driven by Hyundai's decision to locate in Georgia.
- Hyundai has formed a joint venture with SK On in Bartow County for an EV battery facility that will bring 3,500 new jobs and roughly $5 billion in investment to Georgia.
- HB 408 extends the sales and use tax exemption for projects designated as competitive projects of regional significance.
- Most communities benefiting from the HB 408 tax exemption tool are located outside metro Atlanta.
- Representative Bruce Williamson introduced HB 408, with co-sponsors Representatives Shaw Blackmon and Matt Gambill, and Senator Chuck Hufstetler carried it in the Senate.
- FY24 Budget (HB 19) funds economic development priorities and aims to maintain Georgia's status as the No. 1 state for business.
