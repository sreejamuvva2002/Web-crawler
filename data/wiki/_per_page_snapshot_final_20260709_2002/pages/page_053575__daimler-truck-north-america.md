# Daimler Truck North America

## Facts

- Daimler Truck North America announced the appointment of Joanna Buttler as General Manager, product strategy and market development.
- In her new role, Joanna Buttler will oversee the development of DTNA’s product portfolio, both short and long-term.
- Buttler will spearhead all go-to-market activities for the company.
- Her responsibilities include product marketing, brand communications, sales training, and digital sales tools.
- Joanna Buttler’s team represents the Freightliner, Western Star, and Detroit brands.
- She will report directly to David Carson, senior vice president, sales and marketing and member of DTNA’s Operating Committee.
- Buttler brings nearly 20 years of experience with Daimler Truck to her new position.
- She began her career in 2006 as a Marketing Analyst and Sales Support Specialist focused on Detroit powertrain.
- Her journey within the company includes roles such as project controller, manager of IT Controlling, and support manager for Martin Daum, former president and CEO of DTNA.
- With Martin Daum’s promotion to CEO of Daimler Truck and Buses in 2017, Buttler became chief of staff.
- She was subsequently appointed into strategic executive roles within the Autonomous Technology Group.
- Most recently, Buttler served as head of DTNA’s Global Autonomous Technology Group.
- In that role, she set the global autonomous technology strategy for Daimler Truck.
- She led the post-merger integration within Torc Robotics.
- She led the autonomous-ready Cascadia program.
- She led key strategic partnerships in the autonomous vehicle industry.
- According to DTNA, her efforts have been instrumental in forming strong partnerships and leading innovation programs within the transportation industry.
- David Carson stated that Buttler’s extensive experience and proven leadership make her the ideal leader to drive product strategy and market development forward.
- Carson added that her innovative approach and dedication to excellence will continue to strengthen DTNA's position in the industry.
- Daimler Truck will continue along its autonomous roadmap, with a succession plan to be announced in the near future.
