# Greater Hall Chamber of Commerce 2018 Industry of the Year Awards

**Type:** event  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2018-11-13

**Source:** [2018 Industry of the Year Awards - Greater Hall Chamber of Commerce](https://www.ghcc.com/chamber-presents-2018-industry-of-the-year-awards/)  ·  `page_052054`

## Facts

- The Greater Hall Chamber of Commerce presented its 2018 Industry of the Year Awards at the 10th Annual Industry Appreciation Luncheon on November 13, 2018.
- As reported by the Gainesville-Hall County Economic Development Council, 2018 year-to-date announcements in Gainesville-Hall County included 17 new and expanded businesses with 400 new jobs and $200 million in capital investment.
- Since 2013, 150 new and expanded businesses in Gainesville-Hall County have announced 5,400 jobs and $1 billion in capital investment.
- ALBAform, Inc. employs 98 people and manufactures precision metal components, primarily wire frames for seating for the automotive industry.
- In the last 12 months prior to November 2018, ALBAform grew its customer base by 17% and added eight robotic machines.
- ALBAform created new jobs in the last 12 months and offers employees company paid benefits, Paid Time Off, Tuition Assistance, and an attendance recognition program.
- ABB's local manufacturing facility in Flowery Branch produces over 60 highly custom engineered electric motors per day.
- ABB has been employing Work Based Learning High School students since 2015 as well as college interns in the fields of design and manufacturing engineering.
- ABB is actively engaged with the STEM program at Flowery Branch High School.
- Kubota Manufacturing of America employs over 1,500 local area residents in Gainesville.
- Kubota employs up to 30 high school students each year in Work Based Learning and 15 College Internships.
- Kubota partnered with the University of North Georgia to offer a 12 month management development course for new managers.
- Kubota recently made a $1 million dollar donation to Lanier Technical College.
- Wilheit Packaging participated in a partnership with Southern Waste & Recycling, Kubota Manufacturing of America, and the Hall County Recycling Center.
- The Wilheit Packaging partnership diverted 1,500 tons of fiber and 160 tons of plastic from the landfill in nine months.
- Lanier Technical College sponsored the Greater Hall Chamber’s 2018 Industry of the Year Awards program.
- Jan Vintrlik serves as President of ALBAform, Inc.
- Monika Vintrlikova serves as Vice President of ALBAform, Inc.
- Alex Villalobos serves as Production Manager at ALBAform, Inc.
- Mike Holman is the Plant Manager at ABB's Flowery Branch facility.
