# DOE VTO Fiscal Year 2025 Program Wide Funding Opportunity

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [Funding Notice: Fiscal Year 2025 Vehicle Technologies Office (VTO) Program Wide Notice of Funding Opportunity | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/cmei/vehicles/articles/funding-notice-fiscal-year-2025-vehicle-technologies-office-vto-program-wide)  ·  `page_002758`

## Facts

- Up to $88 million in funding is available for advanced vehicle research and development.
- The funding program is administered by the Vehicle Technologies Office (VTO).
- The NOFO number is DE-FOA-0003514.
- The U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) announced the Fiscal Year 2025 VTO Program Wide funding program.
- This funding opportunity will award up to $88 million for projects seeking innovative transportation solutions for on- and off-road vehicles.
- DOE is committed to pushing the frontiers of science and engineering, catalyzing domestic jobs through research, development, demonstration, and deployment.
- Topic areas under this funding will advance affordable and reliable energy innovation in mobility and improve U.S. competitiveness.
- The NOFO covers 10 distinct topic areas.
- Topic Area 1 focuses on technologies for enhanced lithium-ion cell safety, specifically improving abuse tolerance of Li-ion cells with graphite anodes and mass-produced cathodes for plug-in electric vehicles.
- Topic Area 2a seeks low-cost production of battery-grade lithium with reduced energy consumption, improved yield, and minimal environmental impact.
- Topic Area 2b seeks novel materials to form a beneficial passivation layer on the surface of Li foil for rechargeable Li batteries containing liquid or solid-state electrolytes.
- Topic Area 3a aims to accelerate a domestic battery industry supporting lower cost options for long-haul U.S. trucks by seeking Li-ion battery chemistry innovations and design solutions for multilayer pouch, cylindrical, or prismatic cells with extended cycle life.
- Topic Area 3b supports innovative standardized modules or scaled packs (cell-to-pack design) suitable for Class 8 long-haul trucks.
- Topic Area 4 focuses on thermal technologies for zero-emission vehicles (ZEVs), aiming to decrease on-board energy use of vehicle thermal systems for medium- and heavy-duty on-highway ZEVs.
- Topic Area 5 aims to research, develop, and validate tools leveraging Advanced Metering Infrastructure (AMI) and smart metering to improve EV charging infrastructure energization, accelerate deployment, and provide an efficient EV load management platform.
- Topic Area 6 (CROP) focuses on co-located renewable fuel and off-road vehicle pilots for farms and agricultural communities, demonstrating locally produced renewable fuels from agricultural waste and animal manure.
- Topic Area 7 aims to conduct life cycle analysis (LCA) including energy usage, critical materials requirements, and greenhouse gas emissions on up to four vehicles.
- Topic Area 8 focuses on quality improvements of battery busbar joining methods for “foil-to-tab” manufacturing in EVs and real-time monitoring of joining performance.
- Topic Area 9 targets electric vehicle workforce development through local, state, regional, or national outreach, awareness, and partnership building efforts.
- VTO highly encourages project teams for Topic Area 9 to include one or more Clean Cities and Communities coalition.
- Topic Area 10 is an open topic for exploring novel solutions to transportation and related energy and infrastructure challenges through demonstration and deployment projects to spur market development.
- Project teams for Topic Area 10 must include at least one active and DOE-designated Clean Cities and Communities coalition with a significant project role.
- The NOFO Issue Date is January 17, 2025.
- The Submission Deadline for Concept Papers is April 1, 2025, at 5:00 p.m. ET.
- The Submission Deadline for Full Applications is June 18, 2025, at 5:00 p.m. ET.
- The Expected Date for EERE Selection Notifications is November 5, 2025, at 5:00 p.m. ET.
- The Expected Timeframe for Award Negotiations is February 2026.
