# Aspen Aerogels Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** thermal_management  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Aspen Aerogels Cancels Construction Project in Georgia](https://www.concretegeorgia.com/aspen-aerogels-cancels-georgia-construction/)  ·  `page_052649`

## Facts

- Aspen Aerogels Inc. has canceled plans for a proposed 500,000-square-foot manufacturing plant in Statesboro, Georgia.
- The canceled project was expected to create over 250 jobs.
- The facility was projected to cost $325 million.
- The plant was slated to produce thermal barriers for electric vehicle batteries.
- The project was planned for a 90-acre site in Bulloch County, southeast Georgia.
- Turner Construction Co. was brought on board to manage the construction of the plant.
- On February 12, Aspen Aerogels announced it would cancel the Statesboro project and shift focus to maximizing output at its existing facility in East Providence, Rhode Island.
- The CEO stated that streamlining operations in Rhode Island became a priority due to growing uncertainties in the electric vehicle market.
- The cancellation was primarily influenced by slower-than-expected demand for electric vehicles and rolled-back EV promotion policies from the Biden administration.
- A recent memo from the Federal Highway Administration announced the suspension of a $5 billion program aimed at funding EV chargers nationwide, which contributed to the company reevaluating its expansion plans.
- Aspen is planning to relocate some equipment from the canceled Statesboro site to further enhance its Rhode Island facility.
- The cancellation aligns with a broader industry trend, as other corporations like Freyr Battery have also put their Georgia expansion plans on hold.
- Local authorities in Statesboro and Bulloch County expressed disappointment over the cancellation but remain determined to pursue other economic opportunities in the area.
