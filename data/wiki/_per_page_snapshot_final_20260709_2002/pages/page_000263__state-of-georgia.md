# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [10 Stats About Manufacturing in Georgia Over The Next Decade  - Unmudl](https://unmudl.com/blog/10-stats-about-manufacturing-in-georgia)  ·  `page_000263`

## Facts

- Over $27.3 billion in e-mobility investments have been announced in Georgia since 2018.
- Georgia has emerged as the undisputed leader in electric vehicle manufacturing investment across the United States.
- Major players like Hyundai Motor Group, Kia, and Rivian have chosen Georgia as their home base for electric vehicle production.
- The investments create opportunities in battery technology, charging infrastructure, and advanced manufacturing processes.
- Manufacturing is Georgia's fourth-largest and sixth fastest-growing industry.
- There is a projected 10% growth in manufacturing jobs over the next 10 years (till 2034).
- The projected job growth encompasses traditional assembly roles to high-tech positions in quality control, process engineering, and advanced manufacturing systems management.
- Georgia has an abundant workforce of approximately 289,000 production workers across multiple sectors.
- These workers generate a $59.5 billion annual output, according to the Georgia Department of Economic Development.
- This translates to over $205,000 in annual output per production worker.
- The workforce diversity spans automotive assembly, aerospace components, food processing, textiles, and emerging technologies.
- 70% of occupations in the manufacturing industry require only some college or form of technical training.
- Manufacturing careers offer average wages of $84,000 per worker.
- Technical colleges, apprenticeship programs, and industry certifications provide direct routes into well-paying careers.
