# Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Georgia & Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-08

**Source:** [Insights & Observations: Inside Hyundai’s Meta & Kia’s West Point Plants - Autoline](https://www.autoline.tv/industry-interviews/insights-observations-inside-hyundais-meta-kias-wet-point-plants/)  ·  `page_011298`

## Facts

- The state of Georgia is quickly turning into an automotive powerhouse.
- Hyundai operates a brand-new manufacturing plant near Savannah called the Meta plant.
- Kia operates an established assembly plant in West Point near Atlanta.
- Hyundai's Meta plant exterior buildings are painted light sage green to blend with rural surroundings.
- Every building at the Meta plant has rounded corners intentionally chosen to look more natural and less hard-edged.
- The original plan for the Meta plant was to only make electric cars starting with the Hyundai Ioniq 5 and Ioniq 9.
- EV sales did not materialize as expected, so Kia added the Sportage to the Meta plant for manufacturing volume.
- The Meta plant is highly automated and described as the most automated facility visited by the author.
- The Meta plant is currently operating at very low volume with lines moving at a snail's pace.
- Production at the Meta plant started in October 2024.
- Full line speed at the Meta plant is not expected until 2028.
- The Meta plant needs more models to fill assembly lines and its advanced automation requires tweaking and adjustment.
- Metal stamping at the Meta plant uses one massive transfer press line made by Rotem, a division of Hyundai.
- There is only one transfer press line at the Meta plant with no backup if it goes down.
- The single press line makes all panels for three models at the Meta plant.
- Major A-class panels made on-site at the Meta plant include body sides, roofs, floors, and fenders.
- Smaller stampings for the Meta plant are shipped in from suppliers.
- No forklifts are used to move panel racks at the Meta plant; everything is handled by Automated Guided Vehicles (AGVs) and heavy-duty electric tuggers.
- Welding at the Meta plant uses robotic automation with huge Fanuc robots picking up entire body-in-white structures.
- The Meta plant is a brand-new facility currently ironing out operational kinks.
- Line workers at the Meta plant were observed hammering door sills and Korean technicians discussing body-in-white assemblies.
- Several bodies were pulled off the line at the Meta plant for repair loops.
- Cars spend six to eight hours in the paint shop at the Meta plant using one gallon of paint per vehicle.
- The paint shop at the Meta plant is not accessible on tours to prevent contamination from dust and dirt.
- Final assembly at the Meta plant uses robots to take doors off for interior assembly and put them back on later.
- Every car at the Meta plant rides on an AGV equipped with an automatic up-and-down lift to set ergonomic height at every workstation.
- Hyundai Steel makes the steel used at the Meta plant.
- Hyundai Rotem makes the presses used at the Meta plant.
- Hyundai Glovis handles shipping, receiving, and logistics for the Meta plant.
- Hyundai Mobis handles battery pack assembly for the Meta plant.
- The Meta plant currently employs about 1,800 people.
- The Meta plant is aggressively hiring to add a second shift, which will push employment to 2,600.
- The Meta plant shipped about 6,000 cars the month before the visit.
- At full capacity, Kia will receive about 40% of production from the Meta plant.
- Current capacity at the Meta plant is 300,000 units.
- Hyundai plans to boost Meta plant capacity to 500,000 units.
- Hyundai is planning to add a second plant by knocking out an exterior wall to add another 250,000 units of capacity.
- The extra space at the Meta plant will likely be tooled for upcoming midsize body-on-frame trucks and SUVs from Hyundai and Kia.
- Kia's West Point plant began making cars 16 years ago.
- The West Point plant spans roughly 1.7 million square feet under roof including stamping, paint, body shop, and final assembly.
- The West Point plant is tooled to make 350,000 vehicles a year.
- A single line at the West Point plant runs the Telluride, Sorento, Sportage, EV6, and EV9.
- The West Point plant builds gas, hybrid, and pure EVs sequentially on one line.
- The West Point plant employs 3,200 employees working three shifts, eight hours a day, five days a week.
- The West Point plant does on-the-fly handoffs from shift to shift so the line never stops.
- Daily maintenance at the West Point plant is squeezed into brief 30-minute lunch breaks and weekends.
- The parking lot at the West Point plant is jam-packed due to no breaks between shifts, making it hard to find parking.
- The West Point stamping shop runs two Rotem transfer presses.
- Die sets for each vehicle at the West Point plant are color-coded.
- Storage racks for stamped panels at the West Point plant are color-coded to each specific model.
- Robots at the West Point plant pick panels off the press line and keep a last hit panel on a rack for each model to verify quality.
- The West Point plant maintains about 36 hours of inventory in stamped panels.
- The West Point body shop has zero batch building and builds different bodies one right after another to match sales orders.
- A modular body buck at the West Point plant adapts on the fly to accommodate different models.
- General assembly at the West Point plant has open sightlines with parts racks no higher than chest-high.
- The West Point plant uses two separate trim lines where human operators assisted by mechanical lift equipment handle doors-on and doors-off processes.
- Workers on the final line at the West Point plant rotate jobs every two hours to prevent fatigue and boredom.
- The West Point plant paints 14 different colors, mostly white, black, and silver.
- The final assembly line at the West Point plant is tightly packed with only about four feet of clearance between each car.
- The West Point plant pumps out 1,500 cars a day.
- Every vehicle at the West Point plant goes through a test on a 2.2-mile on-site track vital for testing lane-keeping system calibration.
- The West Point plant features an on-site transmission assembly plant.
- Kia sells transmissions from the West Point plant to outside competitors like Nissan and Volkswagen.
- The West Point plant has a massive 13,000-spot parking lot with protective roofs built over part of the staging areas.
- Protective roofs were added at the West Point plant after a severe hail storm caused massive inventory damage last year.
- Solar panels were installed on the protective roofs at the West Point plant while management assesses cost-effectiveness of covering all cars.
- The Meta plant is vast, highly automated, whisper-quiet, and utilizes AGVs for moving stampings and carrying cars.
- The Meta plant is running at a snail's pace while slowly ramping up production and dialing in quality.
- The West Point plant relies on hyper-disciplined human logistics and flexible tooling to churn out high-margin SUVs at maximum velocity.
- The West Point plant uses about 17 labor-hours to make each vehicle including stamping, which is extremely efficient.
- Hyundai and Kia are on track to make more than 1.4 million vehicles a year in the U.S.
- This production volume would be more than what Toyota or Honda currently make in the U.S.
- The plants demonstrate the kind of ambition the Hyundai Group has for the American market.
