# Valeo

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2021-11-23

**Source:** [With its new generation LiDAR, Valeo makes autonomous mobility a reality](https://www.newswire.ca/news-releases/with-its-new-generation-lidar-valeo-makes-autonomous-mobility-a-reality-819209196.html)  ·  `page_012834`

## Facts

- Valeo is the global leader in driving assistance systems (ADAS).
- Valeo presented its third generation scanning LiDAR on Nov. 23, 2021.
- The third generation scanning LiDAR is set to make its market debut in 2024.
- The new technology offers significantly enhanced performance, makes autonomous mobility a reality, and provides previously unseen levels of road safety.
- Geoffrey Bouquot serves as Valeo's Senior Vice-President, R&D and Strategy.
- Bouquot stated that Valeo's third generation LiDAR is a major technological advance toward the autonomous vehicle.
- Bouquot noted the upgrade strengthens Valeo's technological and industrial leadership in the field.
- At the time of the announcement, Valeo was already the only supplier on the market currently series-producing an automotive-grade LiDAR scanner.
- Bouquot stated that Valeo's number one goal with the device is to save lives on the road.
- The new LiDAR delivers unrivaled performance in range, resolution, and frame rate.
- It reconstructs a 3D real-time image of the vehicle's surroundings at a rate of 4.5 million pixels and 25 frames per second.
- Compared to the previous generation, the resolution has been increased 12-fold.
- The range has been increased 3-fold compared to the previous generation.
- The viewing angle has been increased 2.5-fold compared to the previous generation.
- The LiDAR can see things that humans, cameras, and radars cannot.
- This capability allows driving to be delegated to the vehicle in many situations (level 2 automation and above), including on the highway at speeds of up to 130km/h.
- A vehicle fitted with the third-generation scanning LiDAR can manage emergency situations autonomously.
- The scanning LiDAR detects, recognizes, and classifies all objects located around the car.
- If objects are moving, the LiDAR measures their speed and direction.
- The scanning LiDAR can adapt to all light conditions, including dazzlingly bright or pitch black environments.
- It measures the density of raindrops to calculate the right braking distance.
- It tracks nearby vehicles even when they are no longer in the driver's line of sight.
- It uses algorithms to anticipate trajectories and trigger necessary safety maneuvers.
- The scanning LiDAR protects people inside the car and those around it, including pedestrians, cyclists, and other road users.
- Beyond the vehicles it equips, the LiDAR will alert other vehicles of road hazards via the Cloud.
- Valeo designs and manufactures the entire system, including hardware, software, and associated artificial intelligence.
- The AI acts as the "brain" that combines collected data and enables the vehicle to instantly make the right decision.
- The software automatically adapts to the environment and improves performance over time through regular updates.
- Valeo's LiDARs are produced in Germany at Valeo's Wemding plant in Bavaria.
- Components at the Wemding plant are assembled with a micron level of precision.
- The plant's production lines draw on Valeo's state-of-the-art expertise in optics, mechanics, and photonics.
- 300 engineers at Valeo are dedicated solely to this LiDAR technology.
- Over 500 patents have already been filed for this technology.
- Valeo was the first, and remains the only, company to produce a scanning LiDAR on an industrial scale.
- Valeo has already produced over 150,000 units of its scanning LiDAR.
- 99% of cars equipped with a scanning LiDAR scanner worldwide are equipped with a Valeo scanning LiDAR.
- Up to 30% of premium new vehicles are set to reach level 3 automation by 2030.
- Vehicles reaching level 3 automation will need to be equipped with LiDAR technology.
- Autonomous shuttles, robotaxis, delivery droids, autonomous trucks, and the agriculture, mining, and infrastructure sectors will also need to be equipped with one or more LiDARs.
- The LiDAR market is expected to represent more than $50 billion by 2030.
- Valeo's new third generation LiDAR will be a key contributor to this change in scale.
