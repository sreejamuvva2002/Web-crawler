# Daimler Truck North America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Daimler Truck laying off 573 at North Carolina plant as economic uncertainty slows sales :: WRAL.com](https://www.wral.com/story/daimler-truck-laying-off-573-at-north-carolina-plant-as-economic-uncertainty-slows-sales/22086012/)  ·  `page_053551`

## Facts

- Daimler Truck plans to lay off 573 employees in North Carolina.
- The layoffs are due to struggles to grow North American sales amid uncertainty over the economy and U.S. trade policy.
- Daimler Truck North America notified state officials in a July 11 letter about the planned layoffs.
- The layoffs will affect mostly truck assemblers at a plant in Mount Holly.
- Layoffs are scheduled to start on Friday.
- The company cited a sustained reduction in orders and a diminished build rate as reasons for the layoffs.
- Workers can be paid through Sept. 9 in lieu of notice.
- The notification letter was sent to the North Carolina Department of Commerce.
- The company stated the layoffs are expected to be temporary, but of an unknown duration.
- A company spokesperson did not immediately respond to a request for additional information.
- The layoff notification was required under a federal law intended to give workers advance notice to plan job transitions.
- WRAL received the letter through a public records request.
- Daimler Truck makes commercial vehicles under the Freightliner, Western Star, and Thomas Built Buses brands.
- Many of these commercial vehicles are made in North Carolina.
- The company’s North American sales were down 18% during the first half of 2025.
- The sales decline was due in part to uncertainty over the U.S. economy.
- The company had lowered its North American sales forecast in May.
- The company expects to maintain a profit margin of 11% to 13% for the unit.
- The company expected to sell between 260,000 and 290,000 units in North America this year.
- This sales forecast is down from a previous forecast of between 280,000 and 320,000 units.
- Daimler mainly sells its trucks to other businesses.
- Customers have delayed purchases while assessing how U.S. trade policies affect their industries.
- The company’s customers haul products including steel, produce, petroleum, and more.
- These products could be affected by tariffs, executive orders, or provisions of President Donald Trump’s One Big Beautiful Bill Act.
- John O’Leary, chief executive of Daimler Truck North America, stated that people are looking for certainty regarding tariffs or other economic policy.
- O’Leary noted customers are in wait-and-see mode because their end customers generating freight moves and rates are also waiting for certainty.
- Customers are currently on the sidelines waiting for signs of certainty and not buying trucks.
- Trump has increased tariffs on nearly every other country in the world.
- This tariff policy is expected to drive up prices for goods but supporters say it might lead to a resurgence in American manufacturing.
- Critics argue tariffs don't make sense in the modern global economy.
- Mike Pence, Trump’s former vice president, traveled to Raleigh in May to make the case against tariffs.
- Pence stated that Americans will ultimately pay the cost of tariffs in higher prices, lower wages, and lost jobs.
- Daimler Truck has been analyzing the potential impact of tariffs on truck manufacturing and sales.
- O’Leary told equity analysts in North Carolina last week that there is still a lot of clarity that needs to be provided from the government regarding tariffs.
- O’Leary added that tariffs would have an inflationary effect on the industry.
- Added costs from tariffs would likely be pushed out to customers.
- Daimler Truck North America executives have tried to dissuade the federal government from imposing tariffs affecting truck manufacturers.
- Carsten Kirchholtes, DTNA’s general manager of procurement and supply chain, and Sean Waters, the company’s vice president of product integrity, wrote a May public comment letter to officials at the U.S. Department of Commerce’s Bureau of Industry and Security.
- The bureau has been investigating import policies across a variety of sectors.
- O’Leary told analysts last week that the company has the flexibility to shift some North American production from Mexico to the U.S. if needed.
- O’Leary stated the company has game-planned different scenarios but it is still not clear, and he cannot wait for clarity.
- The temporary layoffs in North Carolina come less than two months after Daimler Truck Financial Services USA announced plans to consolidate and relocate its headquarters to Charlotte.
- The financial services relocation is expected to create up to 276 high-paying jobs in Mecklenburg County.
- Commerce officials approved an incentives package of about $5.2 million for that expansion, including a jobs grant worth up to $4.2 million.
- Daimler Truck Financial Services USA plans to invest more than $7.8 million to relocate its headquarters.
- The financial services headquarters are currently split between offices in Redford Township, Michigan, and Fort Worth, Texas.
- The new North Carolina headquarters is expected to house administration, human resources, and financial operations.
- The factory layoffs are not expected to affect DTFS’s relocation plans, according to a spokesperson for the state Department of Commerce.
