# Rivian

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-11-15

**Source:** [Rivian Can Begin Construction on $5B Manufacturing Plant in Georgia - Autobody News](https://www.autobodynews.com/news/rivian-can-begin-construction-on-5b-manufacturing-plant-in-georgia)  ·  `page_060563`

## Facts

- The plant will be Rivian's second production facility in the U.S., joining its existing one in Normal, IL.
- EV maker Rivian gained approval to start construction on a new manufacturing plant outside of Atlanta, GA.
- Rivian was granted final approval for the project by the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDECD) and the Joint Development Authority (JDA) of Jasper, Morgan, Newton and Walton counties.
- The GDECD and JDA announced the final approval on Nov. 9 via WSB-TV.
- The approval gives Rivian long-term rights to construct an automotive plant in the Stanton Springs industrial area east of Atlanta.
- Plans for the facility were first set in motion in 2021.
- The JDA and GDECD stated that they are closing and issuing bonds for the Rivian project.
- Renting the site to Rivian is considered the next step in delivering a generational opportunity for Georgians in Jasper, Morgan, Newton and Walton counties.
- The factory is expected to manufacture Rivian’s R2 next-generation platform for future vehicles.
- The site is located near the I-20 corridor to Atlanta’s east, near the historic Walton County community of Social Circle and touching on Morgan County.
- The facility will join Rivian's first factory in Normal, IL, which produces the R1T pickup and the R1S SUV.
- Rivian gained approval for its Economic Development Agreement (EDA) on the factory earlier this year.
- The new approval allows the automaker to officially begin construction.
- Shortly after Rivian’s initial commitment from Georgia for the Stanton Springs North site in 2021, the state approved a $43 million EV battery recycling plant nearby.
- The Rivian location includes just under 2,000 acres at a site previously called the East Atlanta Megasite.
- The facility is expected to bring as many as 7,500 jobs to the area.
- Rivian will invest $5 billion into building the facility.
- Rivian is targeting 2026 for initial production.
- The company eventually expects to reach an annual volume production of around 400,000 vehicles.
- In addition to building the manufacturing facility, Rivian is expected to accelerate charging station deployment in the area in nearby shopping centers, state parks, and near its Ponce City Market showroom.
- The company’s production facility has forced Georgia lawmakers to consider direct sales laws in the state.
- Georgia currently bans the sales of automobiles that aren’t done through a dealership.
- Tesla is exempt from the direct sales ban, though it is limited to five statewide sales sites as of last year.
- Rivian is introducing an apprenticeship program in which the company will select local students for a 24-month training to support the Stanton Springs North facility.
- The Georgia HOPE Grants will provide tuition for the apprenticeship program.
- Rivian plans to fund additional expenses for the apprenticeship, including textbooks and other learning materials.
