# Hollingsworth & Vose

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hollingsworth & Vose to Expand in Virginia | Virginia Economic Development Partnership](https://www.vedp.org/press-release/2023-01/hollingsworth-vose-floyd-county)  ·  `page_008665`

## Facts

- Hollingsworth & Vose (H&V) is a global manufacturer of advanced materials used in filtration, battery, and industrial applications.
- H&V will invest $40.2 million to expand its operation in Floyd County, Virginia.
- The expansion will add over 28,000 square feet to the company's facility located at 365 Christiansburg Pike Northeast.
- The added space will accommodate new production equipment to meet increased demand.
- Virginia successfully competed with Georgia for this project.
- The expansion is expected to create 25 new jobs.
- Governor Glenn Youngkin announced the investment and highlighted that H&V has generated positive economic impact and job opportunities in Floyd County for more than four decades.
- Secretary of Commerce and Trade Caren Merrick stated that H&V has played a role in the success of Virginia’s advanced materials industry over the past 46 years.
- CEO Josh Ayer said the company chose Virginia for this expansion because of its positive business environment and strong support from the Commonwealth of Virginia and Floyd County.
- Joe Turman, Chairman of the Floyd County Board of Supervisors, stated that the new investment is over three times bigger than the largest private investment in the county’s 191-year history.
- Delegate Marie March welcomed the investment, noting it will provide crucial jobs for local families and keep them working at home.
- H&V was incorporated in 1892 and is headquartered in East Walpole, Massachusetts.
- The company has been family-owned for seven generations.
- H&V established its first Virginia facility in Floyd County in 1976.
- The Floyd County facility currently employs over 200 workers.
- The Virginia Economic Development Partnership worked with Floyd County to secure the project for Virginia.
- Governor Youngkin approved a $558,700 grant from the Commonwealth’s Opportunity Fund to assist Floyd County with the project.
