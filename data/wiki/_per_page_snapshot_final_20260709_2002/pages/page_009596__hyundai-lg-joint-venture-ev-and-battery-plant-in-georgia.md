# Hyundai-LG Joint Venture EV and Battery Plant in Georgia

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-10-12

**Source:** [3 workers died at Hyundai's Georgia plant since 2022, before US immigration raid: report](https://nypost.com/2025/10/12/business/3-workers-died-at-hyundais-georgia-plant-since-2022-before-us-immigration-raid-report/)  ·  `page_009596`

## Facts

- Three workers have died since Hyundai Motor started construction of its $7.6 billion auto plant in Georgia in 2022.
- Dozens of current and former workers, many of them safety coordinators who helped oversee construction, reported that the work environment involved many inexperienced immigrant laborers.
- Workers reported lax safety standards and frequent accidents at the site.
- The plant is operated through a joint venture between Hyundai and South Korea’s LG Energy Solution.
- An immigration raid last month detained hundreds of South Korean workers at the facility.
- The immigration raid was the largest single-site enforcement operation in the U.S. Department of Homeland Security’s history.
- More than a dozen workers have suffered serious injuries on site.
- Injuries included falls without wearing harnesses and being crushed by forklifts.
- Workers stated that Hyundai did not ensure that people were properly trained.
- Safety regulators reportedly did little to prevent worksite violations.
- The Wall Street Journal reported the findings, citing a review of federal records.
- Reuters could not immediately confirm the report.
- Hyundai stated it is committed to following immigration laws.
- Hyundai stated it does not compromise safety for the sake of speed.
- Hyundai took steps to address safety issues in response to incidents during construction.
- Hyundai CEO Jose Munoz traveled to Georgia to tell the team directly that their safety comes before production schedules, costs, profits, and everything else.
- The facility is located in Bryan County about 30 miles west of Savannah.
- Construction on the electric vehicle and battery plant is ongoing.
