# Clayco

**Type:** Company  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[vehicle-manufacturing|Vehicle Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** Chicago, Illinois, Georgia, United States

## Overview

Chicago-based construction firm selected to build a $5 billion electric-vehicle manufacturing plant for Rivian in Stanton Springs, Georgia.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Construction of a major EV vehicle-manufacturing facility in Georgia for Rivian.

## Timeline

- **2023** — Electrical machinery construction, including EV battery plants, reached $35.2 billion (≈47% of overall manufacturing construction).
- **2024** — Dodge Construction Network projected a 15% increase in overall manufacturing construction despite automaker slowdown.
- **December 2021** — Georgia Governor Brian Kemp announced the $5 billion Rivian campus as part of the state’s electric-mobility ecosystem push.
- **August** — Clayco kicked off phase one of a $4 billion VinFast EV manufacturing campus in North Carolina.
- **2025** — Projected start of production at the VinFast plant in North Carolina with 150,000 vehicles per year capacity.
- **2026** — Projected start of vehicle production at Rivian’s Stanton Springs plant.

## Key facts

- Electrical machinery construction, which includes EV battery plants, hit $35.2 billion in 2023, roughly 47% of overall manufacturing construction.
- Clayco recently won the contract to build Rivian’s $5 billion EV plant in Stanton Springs, Georgia.
- JE Dunn will deliver Dongwha Electrolyte’s first North America project, a $70 million battery production facility in Clarksville, Tennessee.
- Economists anticipate continued growth in manufacturing construction with a projected 15% increase in 2024.
- The number of EVs qualifying for clean vehicle tax credits dropped from 35 to 14 in 2024 under the Inflation Reduction Act.
- The 1,800-acre complex will produce about 400,000 vehicles per year when fully complete.
- Clayco expects the Rivian plant to begin vehicle production in 2026.
- Georgia’s push aims to recruit the entire electric-mobility ecosystem to the state.
- The project marks Clayco’s continued involvement in EV manufacturing construction.
- Phase one of the VinFast campus includes a body shop, general assembly, press shop, paint shop and an energy center.
- VinFast’s North Carolina plant is expected to start production in 2025 with a capacity of 150,000 vehicles per year.
