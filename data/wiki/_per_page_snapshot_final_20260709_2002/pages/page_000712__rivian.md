# Rivian

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-03-08

**Source:** [Rivian halts construction on $5B Georgia EV facility | Manufacturing Dive](https://www.manufacturingdive.com/news/rivian-halts-construction-5-billion-georgia-ev-facility-r2-reveal/709726/)  ·  `page_000712`

## Facts

- Rivian has paused construction of its $5 billion manufacturing facility near Social Circle, Georgia.
- Founder and CEO RJ Scaringe announced the pause at the company’s R2 reveal event on Thursday.
- The decision to halt construction at the Stanton Springs North site will reduce the amount of capital needed for Rivian to launch and deliver its R2 model to the market.
- Instead of Georgia, R2 production will start at Rivian's facility in Normal, Illinois.
- The Normal, Illinois plant's capacity is being expanded to 215,000 units a year.
- Production of the R2 model at the Illinois facility is expected to begin in the first half of 2026.
- RJ Scaringe stated that the Georgia site remains core to scaling across all vehicles between the R2, R3, and R3X models.
- RJ Scaringe expressed excitement about leveraging the Illinois facility's team and skills to bring the R2 to market as quickly as possible.
- Rivian estimates total savings of over $2.25 billion compared to initial plans to launch the first R2 production line at the Stanton Springs North facility.
- The expected savings will come from capital expenditures, product development investment, and supplier sourcing opportunities.
- Rivian has been cutting costs over the last year to conserve cash.
- Last month, Rivian announced plans to reduce its salaried workforce by 10%.
- In February 2023, Rivian laid off 6% of its workforce.
- Rivian's stock is down over 40% since the start of the year.
