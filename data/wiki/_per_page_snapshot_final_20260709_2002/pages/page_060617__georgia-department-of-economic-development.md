## Facts

- The Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) is the state's sales and marketing arm and the lead agency for attracting new business investment, encouraging the expansion of existing industry and small businesses, locating new markets for Georgia products, and planning and mobilizing state resources for economic development.
- GDEcD serves as the central hub for strategic planning, policy development, and administrative operations, coordinating statewide economic development initiatives, business recruitment, international trade, and arts promotion.
- GDEcD is located in Atlanta's Technology Square, a vibrant innovation district adjacent to Georgia Institute of Technology, fostering collaboration with academia, research institutions, and technology industries.
- GDEcD maintains a robust global presence through a network of strategically located international offices in key markets across Asia, Europe, and the Americas.
- GDEcD's international offices actively promote Georgia as a premier business destination, assist Georgia-based companies with export development and market access, and work to attract foreign direct investment into the state.
- GDEcD provides services including market intelligence, business matchmaking, site selection assistance, and support for international delegations.
- GDEcD headquarters is located at 75 Fifth Street N.W., Suite 1200, Atlanta, Georgia, USA.
- GDEcD operates a Europe Office at Opernplatz 14, 60313 Frankfurt am Main, Germany, serving as the primary point of contact for European businesses looking to expand or relocate to Georgia and for Georgia companies seeking to enter or expand in the European market, covering the entire European region.
- GDEcD operates a China Office at Room 1805, Central Tower, 555 Huaihai Middle Road, Shanghai 200020, P.R. China, strengthening economic ties between Georgia and China and facilitating foreign direct investment and export opportunities.
- GDEcD operates a Korea Office at #1010, Gwanghwamun Officia Building, 92 Saemunan-ro, Jongno-gu, Seoul 03186, Korea, building strategic economic partnerships with South Korea and leveraging strong existing business relationships in key industries.
- GDEcD operates a Japan Office at Yamate Mansion, #101, 19-5 Uguisudani-cho, Shibuya-ku, Tokyo 150-0032, Japan, managing and expanding the economic relationship with Japan to attract significant foreign direct investment.
- As of April 2025, GDEcD's senior leadership team has shown stability, with no significant new hires or departures at the Commissioner or Deputy Commissioner level reported between mid-2023 and mid-2024.
- On June 20, 2024, Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Cirba Solutions will invest over $300 million to expand its lithium-ion battery recycling operations in Columbia County, creating more than 100 new jobs.
- GDEcD played a key role in attracting the Cirba Solutions project, showcasing Georgia's commitment to the EV and sustainable technology sectors.
- On May 22, 2024, the State of Georgia, alongside the Georgia Ports Authority and local officials, broke ground on an $18 million expansion of the Appalachian Regional Port (ARP) in Murray County.
- Supported by GDEcD, the ARP expansion aims to enhance logistics and supply chain capabilities in Northwest Georgia, attract investment, create jobs, and improve connectivity to the Port of Savannah.
