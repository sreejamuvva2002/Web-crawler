# Top 10 Golf Car Companies Based in the United States of America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-03-28

## Facts

- The golf car industry in the United States has seen remarkable growth, driven by advancements in electric mobility, sustainable energy solutions, and expanding usage beyond golf courses.
- Golf carts are widely used in resorts, gated communities, airports, industrial facilities, and recreational areas.
- Club Car is located in Augusta, Georgia, USA, established in 1958 as a manufacturer of electric and gas-powered golf cars, utility vehicles, and fleet golf cars.
- Club Car is known for advanced technology including connected fleet management systems and lithium-ion batteries, with customizable options for personal and commercial use.
- EZ-Go is located in Augusta, Georgia, USA, established in 1954 as a manufacturer of golf cars, personal transport vehicles, and utility vehicles.
- EZ-Go is a subsidiary of Textron Inc., a global leader in aerospace and industrial technologies.
- EZ-Go introduced the ELiTE lithium-powered golf carts, which deliver faster charging, longer battery life, and increased efficiency.
- Yamaha Golf Car Company is located in Kennesaw, Georgia, USA, established in 1979 as a division of Yamaha Motor Corporation offering electric and gas-powered golf cars.
- Yamaha models feature advanced suspension systems, fuel-efficient engines, and innovative power management technologies for smooth performance and long-term reliability.
- Cushman is located in Augusta, Georgia, USA, established in 1901 as a manufacturer now part of Textron Inc., specializing in rugged golf cars and industrial transport vehicles.
- Cushman vehicles include gas, electric, and diesel-powered options catering to golf courses, hospitality businesses, and commercial industries.
- Polaris Industries Inc. is located in Medina, Minnesota, USA, established in 1954, producing golf cars under the GEM (Global Electric Motorcars) brand alongside ATVs and UTVs.
- Polaris’ GEM golf cars are street-legal low-speed vehicles (LSVs) designed for residential, commercial, and recreational use, featuring safety certifications, modern styling, and long-lasting battery performance.
- MMC Golf Carts is located in Bradenton, Florida, USA, operating as a distributor and custom dealer specializing in refurbished, street-legal, and customized golf carts with aftermarket modifications like high-performance batteries.
- Star EV is located in Greenville, South Carolina, USA, established in 2003 and owned by JH Global Services, focusing on high-performance eco-friendly electric vehicles including lithium-ion-powered golf carts with faster charging times and zero emissions.
- Tomberlin is located in Leesburg, Florida, USA, established in 2006 as a brand under the Columbia Vehicle Group manufacturing street-legal electric golf cars and neighborhood electric vehicles (NEVs) with standard features like seat belts, turn signals, LED lighting, and power steering.
- Garia Luxury Golf Car is headquartered in Houston, Texas, USA (originally founded in Denmark in 2005), specializing in luxury electric golf carts featuring smart connectivity, ergonomic seating, and eco-friendly lithium battery systems.
- Ruike is located in China & USA, established in 2009, manufacturing affordable high-performance electric golf carts and low-speed vehicles that integrate advanced battery technology and high-quality motors.
- The top 10 companies offer a variety of models including electric, gas-powered, and solar-assisted vehicles designed for performance, comfort, and durability.
- A well-maintained golf cart can last 10-15 years, with battery replacement needed every 4-6 years.
- Top brands like Club Car, Yamaha, and EZ-Go offer warranties ranging from 2 to 5 years depending on the model.
- Many golf car manufacturers and dealers offer financing plans, leasing options, and installment payments for purchasing carts.
- Many companies offer fleet packages for resorts, golf courses, gated communities, and corporate campuses.
- Brands like Garia, Ruike, Polaris GEM, and Star EV focus on energy-efficient lithium-ion batteries and sustainable production.
