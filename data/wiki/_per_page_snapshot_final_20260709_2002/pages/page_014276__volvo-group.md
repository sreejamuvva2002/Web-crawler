# Volvo Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Volvo Q2 Profit Halves on US Truck, Battery-Electric Weakness – WTA Global Inc](https://wtaglobalinc.com/archives/82222)  ·  `page_014276`

## Facts

- Volvo posted a profit of $771 million in Q2 2025, less than half its $1.6 billion profit in the year-ago period.
- Revenue at Volvo decreased 12% year over year to $12.59 billion from $14.36 billion a year earlier.
- The company’s revenue from truck sales declined 7% to $8.37 billion in the three months that ended June 30, down 14% from 9.74 billion krona in the year-ago period.
- Volvo posted an operating margin of 8.1% in Q2 2025, compared with 14.5% in Q2 2024.
- CEO Martin Lundstedt stated that the societal transformation to zero-emission vehicles is slower than previously anticipated.
- Costs of $460 million related to compensation for lower battery volume commitments and impairment of some battery-electric assets negatively impacted reported operating income.
- Renegotiated battery delivery contracts saw Volvo pay suppliers $297 million in compensation, with $195 million taken against Q2 earnings.
- The company took a $164 million charge against earnings on the value of its battery-electric assets.
- CEO Martin Lundstedt warned that enabling conditions such as charging infrastructure and stimulus of demand have not yet been put in place, calling for action from policymakers, state leaders, and other stakeholders.
- Volvo sold 52,764 trucks globally in Q2 2025, down 10% from 58,935 trucks in the same period a year earlier.
- In the first six months of 2025, Volvo sold 101,597 trucks, a decrease of 11% from 114,405 trucks a year earlier.
- Demand in North America has been weak due to uncertainty surrounding tariffs and EPA 2027 emissions regulations, prompting Volvo to reduce production capacity there.
- VTNA and Mack will lay off 1,000 staff at manufacturing plants in the U.S., as announced by Lundstedt on April 23.
- Volvo peers Traton Group and Daimler Truck have also laid off or are about to lay off employees in North America due to soft demand for Class 8 on-highway vehicles.
- Daimler Truck North America will lay off 2,000 production staff temporarily, with returns dependent on an upturn in orders.
- The parent company of Freightliner and Western Star sold 38,580 trucks and buses in Q2 2025, down 20% from 48,246 a year earlier.
- North America’s over-the-road freight market is in a recession due to lower freight volume and rates, while the vocational market is relatively better.
- Overall industrywide heavy-duty truck retail sales in North America through the end of June were down 6% at 135,723, compared with 145,056 in the first half of 2024.
- VTNA’s sales in North America decreased by 20% to 12,981 trucks from 16,234.
- Order intake in North America across VTNA and Mack decreased by 16% to 8,243 trucks due to a wait-and-see stance by carriers.
- VTNA’s orders rose 29% to 4,131 trucks from 3,193 in the year-ago period, while Mack’s orders totaled 4,080 trucks, a 38% slump from 6,556 a year earlier.
- VTNA’s North American heavy-duty truck market share was 7.6% in Q2 2025, down from 9.5%, while Mack’s market share rose to 7.2% from 6.1%.
- Volvo Group paid $40 million in October 2024 to bring all of Mack’s cab assembly in-house after buying Commercial Vehicle Group’s Kings Mountain, N.C., plant.
- VTNA’s on-highway lineup saw a redesign of its flagship VNL tractor and the regional haul VNR tractor.
- Mack launched a new flagship on-highway tractor called the Pioneer and repositioned its Anthem predecessor to the regional haul market.
- At Volvo Group’s 2024 Capital Markets Day in Dublin, Va., executives announced plans for 25% North American heavy-duty truck market share by 2030.
