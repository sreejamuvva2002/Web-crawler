# Valeo

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-06

**Source:** [Valeo Secures Nearly $1 Billion Contract to Supply Electric Drives for Mahindra's "Born Electric" SUVs](https://evmagz.com/valeo-secures-nearly-1-billion-contract-to-supply-electric-drives-for-mahindras-born-electric-suvs/)  ·  `page_012818`

## Facts

- Valeo inked a contract worth “almost one billion US dollars” with Indian car manufacturer Mahindra.
- The deal involves supplying electric drives integral to Mahindra’s “Born Electric” SUVs.
- The partnership signals a shift away from Mahindra's initially planned collaboration with Volkswagen.
- The commissioned electric drivetrain encompasses an electric motor, inverter, transmission, and bidirectional power electronics.
- Specific details regarding the system remain undisclosed.
- Production will take place in close proximity to Mahindra’s facility in Pune, Maharashtra.
- Mahindra's decision to partner with Valeo stems from anticipated delays in the supply of Modular Electric Drive Kit (MEB) components from Volkswagen.
- The original collaboration was set to equip Mahindra’s Inglo electric vehicle platform.
- In August, Mahindra opted for Valeo's agile and reliable solutions to meet its electrification goals.
- Xavier Dupont, President of the Valeo Powertrain Systems Business Group, emphasized that locally developed solutions would enable higher voltage and longer-range electric vehicles for the Indian market.
- The partnership is positioned as a significant stride for Valeo in the burgeoning Indian electric vehicle (EV) sector.
- Valeo has plans for investments to localize production in India.
- Mahindra’s “Born Electric” vehicles will rely on Valeo’s electric drives, while Mahindra retains responsibility for chassis, bodywork, interior, and infotainment.
- The collaboration is aligned with Mahindra’s commitment to providing sustainable and innovative mobility solutions globally.
- The shift away from Volkswagen involves Mahindra sourcing battery cells from BYD and electric motors from Valeo.
- Delays in Volkswagen’s delivery of battery cells and electric motors are now projected for 2026 or 2027.
- The first set of electric SUVs under Mahindra’s “Born Electric” initiative, including models XUV.e8 and XUV.e9, is slated for launch between 2024 and 2026.
- These vehicles will address both domestic and international markets.
