# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Electric Transportation Investments in Georgia | Select Georgia](https://www.selectgeorgia.com/blog/georgia-all-in-on-ev/)  ·  `page_029852`

## Facts

- Georgia is attracting a significant national market share of investment in Electric Transportation (ET).
- In 2018, SK Innovation announced a $2.6 billion battery plant in Commerce, Georgia.
- The SK Innovation plant includes two phases of production investment.
- The SK Innovation plant will produce enough batteries for 200,000 electric vehicles (EV).
- The SK Innovation plant will employ 3,000 workers by 2023, an increase in staffing from initial figures.
- Rivian announced an EV plant in Social Circle.
- The Rivian EV plant will employ 7,500 workers.
- Hyundai Motor Group announced plans to build their first fully dedicated electric vehicle (EV) and battery manufacturing facility in the Savannah region.
- The Hyundai Motor Group facility adds $5.54 billion of capital investment.
- The Hyundai Motor Group facility will create approximately 8,100 jobs.
- Total employment in Georgia's ET space is nearly 20,000 jobs.
- Total capital investment for Georgia's ET space is $14 billion.
- Georgia has been supporting the growth of its local EV industry for consumers and manufacturers for several years.
- Collaboration exists between government, businesses, electric utilities, universities, nonprofits, and other relevant stakeholders to support industry growth.
- Last year, groups mobilized to form The National Electric Highway Coalition (NEHC).
- Last year, groups also formed The Georgia Electric Mobility and Innovation Alliance (EMIA).
- The NEHC is a collaboration among electric companies committed to providing EV fast-charging stations along major U.S. travel corridors.
- EMIA was announced by Governor Brian Kemp in July 2021.
- EMIA is led by the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD).
- EMIA constitutes a statewide initiative between government, industries, electric utilities, nonprofits, and other relevant stakeholders.
- The EMIA partnership focuses on growing the electric mobility ecosystem in the state and strengthening Georgia’s EV ecosystem.
- Pat Wilson, Commissioner of the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD), stated at the time of the EMIA announcement: “By bringing together industry leaders and our public and private sector partners, we are proactively developing a roadmap that will keep Georgia at the forefront of electric mobility and innovation."
- SK announced plans to work with Georgia Tech to advance next-generation batteries.
- University research is playing a big part in attracting Georgia as a hub for ET.
- Partnerships and proactive planning have helped Georgia become a top destination for EV manufacturers and suppliers.
- The state is already the heart of the Southeastern Automotive Corridor.
- Georgia is racing ahead as the epicenter for electric transportation technology.
- Since 2018, EV companies have created approximately 19,900 jobs in Georgia.
- Since 2018, EV companies have generated $13.78 billion in capital investment in Georgia.
- EV manufacturers from Korea, Germany, Turkey, and beyond have opened EV facilities in Georgia.
- More EV companies are on their way to Georgia.
- Ashley Varnum, Business Recruitment Director, stated: "The collaboration between the State, local communities, and utility partners in Georgia is unparalleled. Many people dedicated endless hours to supporting Hyundai Motor Group during the selection process in Georgia, and we all look forward to working with them as they grow in Georgia."
- At Georgia Power, the team is collaborating statewide and beyond to support the EV industry.
- Georgia Power is promoting available sites for future facilities.
- Georgia Power is investing an additional $6 million over three years in fast-charging infrastructure.
