# Rivian

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-05-01

**Source:** [Ford In Talks With Trump Administration About Getting Into The War Business](https://autos.yahoo.com/policy-and-environment/articles/ford-talks-trump-administration-getting-135537470.html)  ·  `page_020222`

## Facts

- Rivian is cutting back on output plans for its future plant in Georgia from 400,000 vehicles per year to 300,000.
- The capacity reduction follows a federal loan for the project shrinking by about $2 billion.
- Rivian will be able to draw on the funds in 2027, which is earlier than expected.
- The company will use the $4.5 billion loan for a single phase with an annual capacity of 300,000 vehicles instead of two phases with a capacity of 200,000 each.
- Originally, President Biden's Department of Energy planned to lend Rivian $6.6 billion as part of a program to boost production of its electric trucks and batteries.
- The Georgia plant is scheduled to open in late 2028.
- The facility will build the R2 midsize crossover and future vehicles on the R2 platform, including the smaller R3 crossover.
- CEO RJ Scaringe stated that the strategic decision increased the production capacity of the first phase of the Georgia plant by 50 percent to 300,000 units.
- The Georgia factory site near Atlanta has room for future expansion but no immediate plans for an additional phase of construction were announced.
- CFO Claire McDonough said Rivian's total production capacity between its two plants would rise to 515,000 vehicles per year.
- McDonough declined to say whether Rivian approached the Department of Energy or vice versa regarding changes to the loan terms.
- Rivian reported a Q1 2026 net loss that narrowed to $416 million, representing a 23% improvement from the same time last year.
- The company's revenue rose 11% to $1.38 billion in Q1 2026.
- Rivian reported a 20% gain in Q1 deliveries year-over-year, with 10,365 vehicles delivered between January and March.
- The company maintained its 2026 delivery guidance of somewhere between 62,000 and 67,000 vehicles.
