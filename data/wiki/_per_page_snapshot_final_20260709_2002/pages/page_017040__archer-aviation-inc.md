# Archer Aviation Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Archer Aviation - Archer Aviation Selects Site in Georgia Adjacent to the Covington Municipal Airport for its Manufacturing Facility](https://investors.archer.com/news/news-details/2022/Archer-Aviation-Selects-Site-in-Georgia-Adjacent-to-the-Covington-Municipal-Airport-for-its-Manufacturing-Facility/default.aspx)  ·  `page_017040`

## Facts

- Archer plans to initially build out a 350,000 square-foot facility on a 96-acre site adjacent to the Covington Municipal Airport in Covington, Georgia (Newton County).
- The planned eVTOL aircraft manufacturing facility is capable of producing up to 650 aircraft per year.
- Construction is expected to be completed in the first half of 2024, with initial production expected to begin in the second half of 2024.
- The facility will be expandable by an additional 550,000 square feet, which is estimated to support production of up to 2,300 aircraft per year.
- Archer intends to invest $118M over the next 10 years for the project.
- Archer will receive an incentive package totaling 33% of its capital investment commitment, which includes land conveyance, tax incentives, and a Georgia REBA grant.
- The facility is expected to create over 1,000 jobs over the long term.
- Archer completed a comprehensive survey of potential sites across the United States, evaluating local labor market talent availability, utility availability, ability to conduct seamless flight test operations, construction costs, and logistics.
- Archer has entered into an LOI with Synovus Bank, the largest bank headquartered in Georgia, for project financing.
- Adam Goldstein, Archer's CEO, stated that Georgia played a vital role in the aerospace industry and that the factory can create pathways to highly skilled manufacturing jobs and other ladders of social and economic opportunity.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp commented that Georgia's established leadership in the aerospace industry was essential to landing Archer Aviation in Newton County and welcomed the company to utilize the Quick Start workforce training program for the long-term jobs.
- Georgia is home to more than 800 aerospace companies and maintains a robust aerospace education pipeline including a dozen high schools with training, five technical colleges with aviation programs, and several universities offering aerospace engineering degrees.
- The Georgia Center of Innovation for Aerospace, a strategic arm of the Georgia Department of Economic Development, has cultivated an ecosystem for innovative companies for more than 10 years.
- Archer is designing and developing electric vertical takeoff and landing (eVTOL) aircraft for use in urban air mobility networks, with its team based in Santa Clara, CA.
