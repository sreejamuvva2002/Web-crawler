## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Company is aware of the immigration enforcement action that took place at the construction site of its supplier, the HL-GA Battery Company in Bryan County, Georgia.
- Hyundai is closely monitoring the situation while working to understand the specific circumstances.
- Based on current understanding, none of those detained are directly employed by Hyundai Motor Company.
- Hyundai is committed to full compliance with all laws and regulations in every market where it operates.
- This commitment includes adherence to employment verification requirements and immigration laws.
- Hyundai expects the same commitment from all partners, suppliers, contractors, and subcontractors.
- The safety and well-being of everyone working at Hyundai sites, whether directly employed or working for suppliers, is a top priority.
- Hyundai takes its responsibility as a corporate citizen seriously regarding incidents like this.
- Incidents like this highlight the importance of robust oversight throughout the entire supply chain and contractor network.
- Hyundai is reviewing its processes to ensure that all parties working on its projects maintain the same high standards of legal compliance demanded of itself.
- This review includes thorough vetting of employment practices by contractors and subcontractors.
- North America Chief Manufacturing Officer Chris Susock will now assume governance of the entire megasite in Georgia.
- Hyundai will conduct an investigation to ensure all suppliers and their subcontractors comply with all laws and regulations.
- Hyundai has zero tolerance for those who don’t follow the law.
- Hyundai continues to invest in American manufacturing and create thousands of jobs.
- These investments and job creations will be done in full accordance with U.S. law.
- The approach reflects values of treating all people with dignity and respect.
