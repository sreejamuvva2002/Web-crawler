# SungEel Recycling Park Georgia, LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2022-08-19

**Source:** [Korean Lithium-Ion Battery Recycler SungEel HiTech to Build First U.S. Recycling Facility in Stephens County - 92.1 WLHR](https://921wlhr.com/korean-lithium-ion-battery-recycler-sungeel-hitech-to-build-first-u-s-recycling-facility-in-stephens-county/)  ·  `page_050807`

## Facts

- Lithium-ion battery recycler and raw materials provider SungEel Recycling Park Georgia, LLC., will locate its first U.S. recycling facility in Georgia.
- The company is a subsidiary of the Korea-based global industry leader SungEel HiTech Co., Ltd.
- The company will invest more than $37 million in the new facility.
- The new facility will create 104 jobs.
- SungEel Recycling Park Georgia’s new facility will be located at the Hayestone Brady Business Park, a Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) Certified site in Toccoa.
- The GRAD Program is a proactive effort by Georgia’s economic development community to develop a portfolio of available sites ready for fast-track development.
- Brian Akin serves as Chairman of the Stephens County Development Authority and has worked with SungEel HiTech over the past eight months.
- Brian Akin stated that investments in infrastructure, workforce development, and education were significant factors in SungEel HiTech's selection process to expand to Toccoa-Stephens County.
- With a base in Stephens County, the plant will be located near SK Battery America in Commerce, another South Korean company.
- Operations at the new facility are expected to begin in early 2024.
- Over the next few years, the company will be hiring for technicians, operators, administrative, and sales roles.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that SungEel is a company at the cutting edge of sustainable technology.
- Pat Wilson believes SungEel’s proximity to other members of Georgia’s electric mobility ecosystem will lead to amazing success.
- Pat Wilson noted that preparing key industries like the auto industry for the next technological revolution means creating the jobs of the future and protecting the opportunities of today.
- Pat Wilson expressed gratitude to partners in Stephens County for their investment in speed-to-market solutions like site preparation.
- With more than 10 years of patented technology, SungEel specializes in complete recycling technology using a 100 percent full circulatory system.
- The recycling system processes end-of-life batteries and battery manufacturing scrap with a metal recovery rate of more than 95 percent.
- Recovered metals from the process include nickel, cobalt, and lithium.
- SungEel Representative Suk Jae Yim noted that the company’s entry into Georgia is the last piece of the puzzle to build a sustainable ecosystem of Georgia’s electric vehicle supply chain.
- Suk Jae Yim stated that his company will build a U.S. eco-friendly industrial ecosystem in line with the expectations of the State of Georgia and Stephens County.
- Georgia’s prime location, extensive infrastructure, skilled workforce, and business-friendly climate have made it an attractive location for rapidly developing industries focused on creating a sustainable future.
- Georgia is cultivating a vertically integrated supply chain to help companies increase efficiencies by reducing reliance on imported materials.
- EV-related projects have contributed more than $13 billion in investments and more than 18,100 new jobs to Georgia since 2020.
- SungEel HiTech Co., Ltd. (KOSDAQ, KQ 365340) is headquartered in Korea.
- SungEel HiTech operates facilities in South Korea, China, Malaysia, India, and Hungary.
- SungEel HiTech has new facilities underway in Poland and Germany.
- SungEel HiTech’s objectives are to further improve the environment and create value for customers, stockholders, and communities through innovation in recycling technology, material processing, and products.
