# NeoVolta Power

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [NeoVolta Power JV: Georgia BESS Manufacturing Plant Set for Mid-2026 Production - News and Statistics - IndexBox](https://www.indexbox.io/blog/neovolta-power-jv-launches-georgia-battery-manufacturing-plant-for-2026/)  ·  `page_060331`

## Facts

- NeoVolta has formed a joint venture to develop a U.S. battery energy storage system manufacturing platform in Pendergrass, Georgia.
- The JV is named NeoVolta Power.
- The JV partners are NeoVolta, system integrator PotisEdge, and solar PV company Longi.
- In November 2025, Longi acquired approximately 62% of PotisEdge, gaining sole control.
- The acquisition marks the Chinese solar manufacturer's entry into the energy storage space.
- Longi and PotisEdge characterized the deal as a partnership to launch Longi's Energy Storage One-Stop Solution.
- The solution is initially implemented in key markets including the UK, Germany, Italy, and Spain.
- Also in November 2025, NeoVolta entered into agreements for a US$13 million private placement financing anchored by Infinite Grid Capital.
- The financing will be used in part to advance the BESS manufacturing plant in Georgia.
- The Georgia facility is built for an initial annual production capacity of 2GWh.
- The facility has the potential to scale up to 8GWh.
- It is scheduled to start mass production by mid-2026.
- The plant will initially concentrate on assembling prismatic-cell battery packs and integrating DC containers.
- The plant supports around 89 workers at its steady-state initial capacity.
- NeoVolta holds a 60% controlling interest in the NeoVolta Power JV.
- PotisEdge holds a 20% ownership interest in the JV.
- The remaining 20% of the JV is held by a group of strategic investors providing additional technical and operational support.
- Initial production is anticipated to focus more on utility-scale systems.
- Commercial and industrial systems are expected to capture an increasing share as demand rises.
- The platform is built to accommodate various system configurations and product formats.
- The facility will not produce battery cells directly.
- NeoVolta did not specify the source of the cells to be assembled.
- PotisEdge does not manufacture battery cells.
- Longi, as of Q3 2025, was unprofitable.
- Under FEOC rules, U.S. downstream projects or upstream manufacturing cannot receive material support from prohibited foreign entities above specific thresholds if they wish to qualify for tax credits.
