# Georgia Quail Hunt

**Type:** event  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2023-02

**Source:** [Georgia Chamber Hosts Business Prospects for Annual ‘Georgia Quail Hunt’ in Albany - Savannah CEO](https://savannahceo.com/features/2023/02/georgia-chamber-hosts-business-prospects-annual-georgia-quail-hunt-albany/)  ·  `page_010159`

## Facts

- The Georgia Chamber of Commerce hosted its annual Georgia Quail Hunt in Albany on Friday, February 10th, 2023.
- Business leaders representing state recruitment and expansion prospects joined local and state economic development officials, including Lt. Governor Burt Jones and University System of Georgia Chancellor Sonny Perdue.
- The event is now in its 34th year and serves as the state’s premiere economic development recruitment event.
- Since 1994, business guests of the Georgia Quail Hunt have invested upwards of $2.5 billion and created more than 10,000 jobs in the state as a result of corporate locations or expansions.
- Industry locations in Albany-Dougherty County facilitated through the hunt include Outdoor Network, PowerSports Plus and WebstaurantStore.
- Corporate sponsors for the event include Bergara, BPI Outdoors, Glock, Kia Georgia, Inc., Molson Coors, PowerSports Plus, Remington Firearms, Taurus Holdings and Trulock Choke Tubes.
- 2023 Georgia Quail Hunt Chairman Daryl Ingram serves as chief external officer and senior vice president of Electric Cities of Georgia.
- Event organizers attribute the hunt's success to partnerships with Locate South Georgia, Albany-Dougherty Economic Development Commission, Albany Area Chamber of Commerce, and local volunteers.
- Area Development magazine has named Georgia the nation’s “Top state for doing business” for nine consecutive years, citing competitive advantages in tax environment, talent availability, training access, and transportation infrastructure.
- Chris Clark, president and CEO of the Georgia Chamber, stated that for more than 30 years the hunt has helped facilitate thousands of jobs and tens of billions in investment in Georgia’s economy.
