# SRG Global Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2011-02-22

**Source:** [SRG to open new Mexican molding site](https://www.plasticstoday.com/blow-molding/srg-to-open-new-mexican-molding-site)  ·  `page_060125`

## Facts

- SRG Global Inc. has broken ground on an injection molding, top coat, chrome-plating, and assembly plant in Irapuato, Mexico.
- The company estimates that the initial phase of the project will cost between $25 and $35 million.
- The facility is expected to be completed by the first quarter of 2012.
- At this point in time, the facility's final size and machine count are to be determined.
- The build-out for the new facility is expected to last one year.
- The company expects to install injection molding machines ranging from 250 to 3000 tons, but that could be subject to change.
- SRG currently supplies "many" of the OEMs in Mexico, shipping product from its sites in Kentucky, Indiana, Georgia, Tennessee, and Missouri.
- The Irapuato site is located east of Guadalajara and northwest of Mexico City in the Mexican state of Guanajuato.
- The site will serve a variety of automotive and non-automotive OEMs and customers.
- Initial plans call for staffing up with 220 direct employees.
- There is potential to bring on an additional 220 indirect employees.
- Construction of the Mexico facility is part of SRG Global's business strategy to maintain local and regional production capabilities.
- SRG Global's other recent investments include its Advanced Development Center in Taylor, MI, which opened in 2010.
- SRG Global also opened a production facility in Boleslawiec, Poland, in 2009.
- Within the automotive sector, SRG supplies front and rear end components including grilles, emblems/nameplates, liftgates, deck lids, and spoilers.
- SRG supplies body side parts such as rocker panels, body side moldings, fuel filler caps, wheel flares, door handles, and wheel covers/caps.
- SRG supplies a variety of interior components including pillars, window surround moldings, cowls, door belt moldings, parts for door panels, center consoles, instrument panels, and steering wheels.
- The current company was formed by the former Guardian's acquisition of Siegel-Robert Automotive in 2008.
- SRG Global was created in 2009 by combining the two companies' trim operations, with headquarters in Warren, MI.
- In a 2008 presentation, Bill Conwell, VP of planning and strategic initiatives at Tier One supplier Lear, noted that there were 13 OEM plants in Mexico spread among 10 states, including Baja, Sonora, Coahuila, Aguascalientes, Jalisco, Guanajuato, Puebla, Morelos, and San Luis Potosi.
- Conwell stated that OEMs operating in Mexico at that time included GM, Ford, Chrysler, Toyota, Nissan, Honda, and Volkswagen.
- By 2011, Conwell forecast there would be 12 OEMs in the country spread among 18 plants, with an influx of Chinese carmakers including Geely, Chery, and ZhongXing, in addition to Fiat.
