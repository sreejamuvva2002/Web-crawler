# Ascend Elements

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2022-10-27

**Source:** [Ascend Elements raises $300 million - electrive.com](https://www.electrive.com/2022/10/27/ascend-elements-raises-300-million/)  ·  `page_052552`

## Facts

- US-based Ascend Elements has raised funding totalling $300 million.
- The company began building its factory to produce sustainable battery materials for electric vehicles in southwestern Kentucky a few days ago.
- The funding raised includes $200 million in investments as part of the company’s Series C financing round led by Fifth Wall Climate.
- SK Ecoplant, a unit of the South Korean conglomerate SK Group, joined the round as a new investor and has reportedly invested $50 million alone.
- Previous investors including Hitachi and Jaguar Land Rover also participated in the latest financing round.
- Ascend Elements recently received two grants from the US Department of Energy totalling $480 million.
- Just over $310 million of the DOE grants is earmarked for the construction of the Kentucky factory.
- The Hopkinsville plant, construction of which has now begun, will use the company’s “hydro-to-cathode” technology.
- The technology processes black matter from recycled electric vehicle batteries directly into new cathode materials without the usual intermediate step of hydrometallurgically splitting the black matter into individual materials.
- Ascend Elements CEO Mike O’Kronley stated that their patented, closed-loop process produces sustainable, high-performance cathode active materials that can go directly back into new EV batteries.
- The company has already started generating revenue by processing end-of-life batteries and manufacturing scrap at its “Base 1” facility in Georgia.
- The Georgia facility is meant to have 30,000 metric tons of EV battery recycling capacity by the end of 2022.
- The Georgia facility should eventually feed the Apex 1 facility in Kentucky.
- The company aims to recycle more than 150,000 metric tons of lithium-ion batteries per year globally by 2026.
- Fifth Wall Partner and Co-Lead of the Climate Investment team Peter Gajdoš stated that securing the recyclability of critical, valuable metals in batteries is an essential avenue to achieve energy independence from fossil fuels.
