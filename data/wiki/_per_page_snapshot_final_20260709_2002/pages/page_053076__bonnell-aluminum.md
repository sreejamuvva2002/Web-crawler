# Bonnell Aluminum

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Bonnell Aluminum investment](https://aluminiumtoday.com/news/bonnell-aluminum-investment)  ·  `page_053076`

## Facts

- Bonnell Aluminum is a subsidiary of Tredegar Corporation (NYSE:TG).
- Bonnell Aluminum announced it will invest approximately $18 million over the next 12 months.
- The investment will fund an expansion project at its AACOA facility in Niles, Michigan.
- The expansion includes the purchase of a new aluminum extrusion line.
- Planned startup for the new line is the second quarter 2017.
- Brook Hamilton serves as President and General Manager for Bonnell Aluminum.
- Hamilton stated that growing customer demand prompted the investment to satisfy opportunities with high-quality products and services.
- Hamilton noted the Niles facility is ideally suited for the project due to a well-run operation and a great team in place.
- Ira Endres serves as Vice President of Sales and Marketing.
- Endres stated AACOA has a solid market reputation and is highly regarded as a leader in consumer durables and machinery/equipment markets.
- The Niles facility serves the automotive market due to its regional location.
- Endres cited customer demand as the reason for increasing capacity in Niles.
- The new line will be comprised of a 3,600-ton extrusion press.
- The press will house a 9-inch container, handling systems, and ancillary equipment.
- Capacity is estimated at approximately 16 million pounds.
- The project includes additional floor space to accommodate future value-added fabrication capacity.
- Hamilton added that the new line will provide new and broader product line capabilities for customers.
