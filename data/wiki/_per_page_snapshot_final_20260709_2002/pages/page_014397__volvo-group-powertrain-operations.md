# Volvo Group Powertrain Operations

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2022-10-08

**Source:** [President Biden Visits Volvo Group Powertrain Operations in Hagerstown, Maryland | Markets Herald](https://marketsherald.com/president-biden-visits-volvo-group-powertrain-operations-in-hagerstown-maryland/)  ·  `page_014397`

## Facts

- President Joe Biden visited the Volvo Group Powertrain Operations facility in Hagerstown, Maryland, for National Manufacturing Day on October 7.
- During the visit, Biden met with Volvo Group executives, toured the facility, and spoke with employees.
- Martin Weissburg, chairman of Volvo Group North America and Mack Trucks president, stated that the Hagerstown site exemplifies modern manufacturing by providing advanced transportation technologies to meet customer needs today and in the future.
- Over the past 10 years, the Volvo Group has invested nearly $320 million in the Hagerstown site.
- The Hagerstown campus produces engines, transmissions, and axles for Mack Trucks, Volvo Trucks, Prevost motorcoaches, and Volvo Buses sold in North America.
- The facility recently began production of Modular Power Boxes (MPB) that are at the heart of the Group’s new zero-emission battery-electric vehicles, the Volvo VNR Electric and Mack LR Electric models.
- President Biden toured the MPB subassembly, assembly, and testing areas while on campus.
- Marcus Minkkinen, vice president and general manager at the Volvo Group Powertrain Operations in Hagerstown, stated that the site offers great jobs in a modernized facility that is transforming the transportation industry and highlighted the importance of manufacturing to the U.S. economy.
