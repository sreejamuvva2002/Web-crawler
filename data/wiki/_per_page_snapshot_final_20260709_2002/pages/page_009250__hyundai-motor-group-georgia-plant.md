# Hyundai Motor Group Georgia Plant

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-19

**Source:** [Hyundai moving forward with $2.7 billion Georgia plant expansion after ICE raid - CBS Atlanta](https://www.cbsnews.com/atlanta/news/hyundai-will-spend-2-7-billion-expanding-georgia-plant-raided-by-ice/)  ·  `page_009250`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group confirmed it is moving forward with plans to expand its Georgia auto plant just weeks after an immigration raid delayed the startup of an electric vehicle battery facility on site.
- The Ellabell plant, located west of Savannah, will receive a $2.7 billion investment.
- The investment will increase annual production by 200,000 vehicles over the next three years.
- Total production capacity at the facility will reach 500,000 vehicles per year.
- Hyundai plans to produce 10 models of electric and hybrid gas-electric vehicles in Georgia, increasing from the current two models.
- The expansion supports Hyundai's broader goal to manufacture 5.6 million vehicles worldwide annually by 2030.
- The facility will begin production of a mid-sized pickup truck for the first time.
- The plant will also produce models like the Santa Cruz compact pickup, which Hyundai began selling in 2021.
- Hyundai states that more than 80% of vehicles sold in the U.S. will be produced domestically by 2030.
- This shift will increase domestic vehicle content from 60% to 80%.
- An immigration raid resulted in the arrest of more than 300 South Korean nationals.
- The raid delayed the opening of the on-site battery plant by at least two to three months.
- The battery plant startup timeline has been pushed to the first half of 2026.
- Hyundai CEO José Muñoz and company spokesperson Michael Stewart emphasized that the broader Georgia expansion remains on track despite the delay.
- Georgia officials, including Governor Brian Kemp, have actively supported the company by emphasizing the state's business advantages and promising visa issues will be resolved to facilitate operations.
- State and local governments have pledged $2.1 billion in tax breaks and incentives for the project.
- Hyundai plans to increase its Ellabell workforce from 3,129 employees to at least 8,500 by 2031.
- The Georgia expansion is the largest component of Hyundai's global growth strategy.
- Hyundai's global growth strategy also includes production increases in India, South Korea, Saudi Arabia, Vietnam, and North Africa.
- By 2027, Hyundai plans to introduce extended-range electric vehicles capable of traveling more than 600 miles on a single battery-gasoline hybrid system.
