# Georgia EV and Battery Supply Chain

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-03-01

**Source:** [Georgia’s Ins and Outs - Atlanta Magazine](https://www.atlantamagazine.com/article/georgias-ins-and-outs/)  ·  `page_059500`

## Facts

- Georgia totaled record international trade of more than $196 billion in 2022 while racing to become a global electric vehicle hub.
- The state’s exports set a record of more than $47 billion in 2022, up $5 billion from 2021.
- Automotive exports slipped from $4.5 billion to $4.1 billion in 2022, including nearly $1 billion in parts and accessories.
- Sewon America announced on Feb. 21 that it is bringing 740 jobs to the town of Rincon (30 minutes northeast of Savannah) with a $300 million plant.
- The Sewon America plant will make chassis pieces for electric vehicles, especially those built at Hyundai Motor Group’s Metaplot America in Bryan County.
- Seoyon E-Hwa announced on Feb. 1 that it is bringing 740 direct and indirect jobs to Savannah with a $76 million plant.
- Seoyon E-Hwa is an American subsidiary of a South Korean company with an existing facility supplying the Kia factory in LaGrange.
- The Seoyon E-Hwa plant will make interior items such as door and tailgate trims for Metaplant America.
- Currently, 500 of the Seoyon jobs are described as permanent.
- The advent of the Sewon facility in Effingham County means that all four counties under the economic development authority for the Savannah region—Bryan, Bulloch, Chatham, and Effingham—have manufacturing projects for Hyundai suppliers.
- These Hyundai supplier projects represent $1.8 billion in investments across the four counties.
- Construction on the Hyundai Metaplot plant began in early January.
- Chatham County Economic Development Authority President and CEO Trip Tollison says the facility’s capacity is expanding from 300,000 to 500,000 vehicles per year.
- The capacity increase reflects rising EV demand.
- SK Battery America operates two battery plants for Ford and Volkswagen EVs in Commerce.
- SK originally committed to hiring 2,600 workers by the end of 2024, but met that goal last year.
- SK intends to expand to 3,000 employees this year.
- SK is planning a bigger manufacturing center in Bartow County and has an information technology hub in Roswell.
- Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson says, “Today’s success is the result of a decades-long vision shared by Georgia leaders since the first international trade offices were opened 50 years ago.”
