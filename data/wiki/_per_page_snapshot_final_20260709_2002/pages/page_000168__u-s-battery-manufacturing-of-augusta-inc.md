# U.S. Battery Manufacturing of Augusta Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-09-27

**Source:** [US Department of Labor finds Augusta battery manufacturer exposing employees to unsafe lead levels, other hazards | Occupational Safety and Health Administration](http://www.osha.gov/news/newsreleases/atlanta/20230927)  ·  `page_000168`

## Facts

- U.S. Battery Manufacturing of Augusta cited for 4 violations, faces $160K in penalties.
- A federal workplace safety investigation found an Augusta battery manufacturer exposed employees to unsafe levels of lead based on personal air monitoring of workers and lead accumulation on respirators and counters in areas where workers took lunch breaks.
- OSHA issued two repeat citations after May 2023 inspections found the company failed to have engineering and work practice controls in place to reduce exposures to lead.
- Inspections found the company did not prevent lead from accumulating on surfaces.
- OSHA issued two serious citations after finding U.S. Battery exposed employees to lead levels in concentrations greater than permissible.
- The agency found the company did not ensure employees’ respirators were cleaned and disinfected.
- OSHA proposed $160,727 in penalties.
- OSHA Area Office Director Josh Turner stated that elevated lead levels can cause debilitating and permanent health issues and that employers are legally responsible for minimizing workers' exposure using engineering controls, safe work practices, and clean protective equipment.
- Since 2018, federal safety inspections at U.S. Battery's Augusta facility have identified numerous violations related to lead exposure.
- In 2019, OSHA issued citations for one serious, one repeat, and one other-than-serious violation at the facility.
- In 2018, OSHA found nine serious violations and one repeat and one other-than-serious violation at the facility.
- Founded in 1926, U.S. Battery Mfg. Augusta Inc. designs and manufactures deep cycle batteries for scissor lifts, golf carts, boats, and other uses by numerous industries in the U.S. and abroad.
- The company has locations in Corona, California, and in Evans and Augusta, Georgia.
- The company has 15 business days from receipt of its citations and penalties to comply, request an informal conference with OSHA's area director, or contest the findings before the independent Occupational Safety and Health Review Commission.
