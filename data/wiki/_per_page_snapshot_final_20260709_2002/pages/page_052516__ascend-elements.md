# Ascend Elements

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2024-12-10

**Source:** [Ascend Elements | Ascend Elements to Increase U.S. Production of Lithium Carbonate By 60% With Start of New Lithium Recovery Line in 2025](https://ascendelements.com/covington-lithium-carbonate/)  ·  `page_052516`

## Facts

- Ascend Elements plans to increase U.S. production of lithium carbonate by 60% with the start of a new lithium recovery line in 2025.
- The company will begin producing >99% pure lithium carbonate (Li2CO3) recovered from used lithium-ion batteries at its facility in Covington, Ga. in 2025.
- Ascend Elements plans to produce up to 3,000 metric tons of domestic Li2CO3 per year.
- Currently, recycled Li2CO3 is not produced at a commercial scale anywhere in the United States.
- The only other domestic source of Li2CO3 is from a mining operation in Nevada.
- Eric Gratz, co-founder and CTO of Ascend Elements, stated this is the first new, domestic source of lithium carbonate in the 21st Century.
- Gratz noted that this new domestic supply of a critical battery material will help U.S. industries meet growing demand while avoiding the possibility of tariffs on imported materials.
- Li2CO3 is used to make advanced batteries for grid-scale energy storage applications as well as electric vehicles, boats and aircraft.
- Ascend Elements’ innovative lithium recovery process produces a remarkably low level of carbon dioxide emissions compared to conventional methods like mining and brine extraction.
- The company’s lithium extraction process produces just 2.27 kg of CO2 emissions per 1 kg of Li2CO3 produced.
- Spodumene mining produces approximately 16.7 kg of CO2 emissions per 1 kg of Li2CO3.
- Chilean brine extraction produces approximately 3.6 kg of CO2 emissions per 1 kg of Li2CO3.
- These preliminary emission comparisons are from a life cycle assessment (LCA) not yet critically reviewed.
- Gratz stated that the current process of lithium extraction from used lithium-ion batteries is about 86% less carbon-intensive compared to spodumene mining and 37% less carbon-intensive compared to Chilean brine extraction.
- The Ascend Elements facility in Covington is one of North America’s largest lithium-ion battery recycling facilities.
- The plant has been in operation since August 2022.
- The Covington plant has the capacity to recycle up to 30,000 metric tons of lithium-ion battery materials per year.
- This recycling capacity equates to approximately 70,000 EV battery packs annually.
- Ascend Elements is a North America- and Europe-based battery materials leader that converts spent lithium-ion batteries and manufacturing scrap into premium precursor cathode material (pCAM) and battery-grade lithium carbonate through its patented Hydro-to-Cathode® process.
