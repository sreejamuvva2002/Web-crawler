# Novelis

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2026-03-03

**Source:** [Novelis' Greensboro, Georgia, plant site of fire - Recycling Today](https://www.recyclingtoday.com/news/novelis-greensboro-georgia-plant-site-of-fire/)  ·  `page_047317`

## Facts

- Fire broke out at Novelis’ facility in Greensboro, Georgia, following an explosion the afternoon of Sunday, March 1.
- Greene County Fire Public Safety, Emergency Management/Homeland Security Director Joe Bashore told The Union-Recorder that he heard the significant explosion from 5 miles away and it rattled his house.
- Novelis confirmed that an explosion occurred in a piece of equipment at its Greensboro recycling plant in the late afternoon of Sunday, March 1, leading to a fire in the equipment.
- There were no injuries to employees or first responders resulting from the incident.
- The Greensboro plant is currently shut down while the company cleans the impacted areas.
- Novelis stated that it has recycling and casting capacity at its Berea, Guthrie, and Logan facilities in Kentucky, its Oswego plant in New York, and access to third-party casting centers, so it does not expect a significant impact on customers.
- The Greensboro facility was the first stand-alone used beverage can (UBC) recycling facility for Novelis and now pioneers many of the company's recycling technologies.
- In late 2025, Novelis experienced two major fires at its Oswego plant’s hot mill that affected recovered aluminum demand.
- The Oswego plant was previously the biggest producer of auto body sheet and a large producer of can sheet.
- Some of Novelis' rolling mills have shifted their manufacturing lines away from other end markets, including beverage cans, to support Ford and other automotive companies.
