# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Third Fatality Recorded at Hyundai’s $7.6B Metaplant in Georgia | Engineering News-Record](https://www.enr.com/articles/60802-third-fatality-recorded-at-hyundais-76b-metaplant-in-georgia)  ·  `page_009428`

## Facts

- A forklift incident on May 20 resulted in the death of a construction worker at the Hyundai Metaplant complex near Savannah, Ga.
- This fatality marks the third recorded at the electric vehicle production campus in just over two years.
- The incident was the second jobsite death in the past two months.
- Local media reports cite the Bryan County Sheriff’s Office regarding the incident location.
- The plant is being constructed for HL-GA Battery Company, a joint venture formed by Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solutions to produce batteries for the automaker’s electric vehicle manufacturing operations at the site.
- The victim was identified as Allen Kowalski, 27, who worked for a subcontractor at the project.
- He reportedly died after being struck by a load that unexpectedly fell as it was being unloaded from the forklift.
- The victim’s employer was not identified.
- No other injuries were reported from this incident.
- The U.S. Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA) is investigating the incident.
- LG Energy Solutions issued a statement expressing condolences to the worker’s family, friends and colleagues.
- LG Energy Solutions stated that “the health and safety of our larger team is our highest priority, and we are committed to following all Occupational Industrial Safety and Health Policies.”
- LG Energy Solutions noted they are fully supporting a thorough investigation with their subcontractor and relevant authorities to determine the root cause and prevent future accidents.
- The fatality occurred less than two months after another forklift-related incident at the battery plant resulted in the death of a 45-year-old subcontractor employee.
- That previous incident also remains under investigation.
- Hyundai is listed as architect and general contractor for the $7.6-billion Metaplant’s 11 facilities.
- Construction on the Metaplant broke ground in late 2022.
- Approximately six months after groundbreaking, in April 2023, 34-year-old Victor Javier Cajija Gamboa died while accessing the top beam of an open-web structural steel frame for the 16-million-sq-ft EV assembly plant.
- Gamboa was an employee of steel contractor Eastern Constructors Inc. of Geismar, La.
- According to an OSHA investigation report, Gamboa lost his balance while walking along a steel I-beam.
- The beam cut his personal fall arrest lanyard, causing him to fall 60 ft to the ground.
- The OSHA report concluded Gamboa was killed due to blunt force trauma.
- Eastern Constructors was penalized more than $20,000 for safety violations, which included one willful and one serious violation.
- OSHA added Eastern Constructors to its Severe Violator Enforcement Program.
- Local media report that OSHA has opened at least 15 investigations related to activities at the 2,906-acre site.
- In addition to the three fatalities, incidents reportedly have included employees struck by moving objects, falls, an electrocution, and a pipe explosion in March of this year that caused serious but non-life-threatening injuries.
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America held the facility's grand opening on March 26.
- The first Hyundai vehicles have already rolled off the assembly line.
- The facility has an initial annual production capacity of 300,000 units, plus the capability for further expansion.
- The HL-GA battery plant is scheduled to begin full operation next year.
- According to Hyundai, the Metaplant will be powered entirely by renewable energy, including 5.2 MW of solar power generated by solar panels positioned by the nearly 1,900-vehicle parking area.
