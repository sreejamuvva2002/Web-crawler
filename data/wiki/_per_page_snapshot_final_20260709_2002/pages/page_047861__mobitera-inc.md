## Facts

- In April 2027, the company will change its name from Panasonic Automotive Systems Co., Ltd. to Mobitera Inc.
- CEO Masashi Nagayasu explained the core sentiments behind the rebranding process through dialogue about who the company is and hopes for the new name.
- Over several months, the company reviewed roughly 800 proposed new names before finalizing the decision.
- The name change decision was made with an eye toward a future IPO to grow as an autonomous company amid a major transformation in the automotive industry.
- The company aims to shift from simply manufacturing products to designing 'experiences' that make mobility comfortable and enrich lives.
- The new 'Mobitera' logo places the letter 'i' at its center to symbolize keeping individuals at the forefront of all operations.
- The brand's character was refined around core values: integrity, teamwork, adaptability, challenge, and productivity.
- These values were broken down into specific daily actions (DO) and non-actions (DON'T) to translate abstract commitments into concrete behavior.
- To survive in a rapidly changing industry, the company plans to accelerate decision-making and increase the flexibility of its capital policy.
- The company's unchanging core DNA includes its founding principles and a relentless commitment to quality in safety and security.
- The new name 'Mobitera' combines the Japanese words for opening the road ('Iter') and illuminating ('Tera').
