## Facts

- Dana Johnson was newly named Executive Director of Select Cobb at the time of the interview.
- Dana Johnson has worked in the community for 18 years and received her Master's degree from Georgia Tech in 2000.
- The Battery Atlanta development is located at SunTrust Park and serves as a year-round entertainment destination attracting residents and visitors.
- Investments by the Atlanta Braves and Cobb County into SunTrust Park and The Battery have driven increases in retail business, SPLOST, and new tax revenue.
- Five new office towers were built in the Cumberland submarket as part of the development's broader economic impact.
- Strategic planning for growth in the Cumberland submarket over the last 20 years prepared the area for major developments including the Braves, The Battery, and thyssenkrupp.
- A dedicated transportation network was created to provide excess capacity for the development's traffic demands.
- Game start times were adjusted to allow interstates sufficient time to move people through the area.
- Technology, transit systems, and intersection improvements are utilized to manage traffic and pedestrian flows around the development.
- The staggered arrival and departure patterns of visitors help reduce congestion compared to traditional game-day traffic surges.
- Cobb County has evolved from a bedroom community into an economic powerhouse supported by towns like Marietta and Smyrna.
- Cobb County government is characterized as efficient and focused on facilitating business development in the community.
- Economic success in Cobb County is driven by collaboration between the chamber, county government, cities, and CIDs.
- Major corporate partners in Cobb County include the Atlanta Braves, Home Depot, Novelis, and thyssenkrupp.
- Georgia Power supports economic development by gathering research for customers or prospects, providing leads, and helping visualize building placements on sites.
- Georgia Power is recognized as a critical partner in the statewide economic development process.
