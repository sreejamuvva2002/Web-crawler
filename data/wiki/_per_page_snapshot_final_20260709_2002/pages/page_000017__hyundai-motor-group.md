# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-05-20

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Group to Invest $5.54 Billion in Georgia at First Fully  | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-releases/2022/hyundai-motor-group-invest-554-billion-georgia-first-fully-dedicated-electric)  ·  `page_000017`

## Facts

- On May 20, 2022, Governor Brian P. Kemp and Hyundai Motor Group Executive Chair Euisun Chung announced that Hyundai Motor Group will open its first fully dedicated electric vehicle (EV) and battery manufacturing facility in Georgia.
- Hyundai Motor Group will invest $5.54 billion to open a state-of-the-art U.S. smart factory at the Bryan County Megasite.
- Non-affiliated Hyundai Motor Group suppliers will invest approximately another $1 billion in the project.
- The project will deliver approximately 8,100 new jobs to Georgia’s coastal region.
- The megasite is a premier 2,923-acre site purchased jointly by the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority (JDA) and the State less than a year ago.
- Construction on the new facility is expected to begin in January 2023.
- Full production is expected in the first half of 2025, with an annual capacity of 300,000 units.
- Wages throughout the project will be well-paying advanced manufacturing positions competitive in the local market.
- Hyundai Motor Group cited speed-to-market, workforce, and the state’s ability to meet the company’s carbon neutrality standards as reasons for choosing Georgia.
- Georgia is home to an existing network of Hyundai subsidiaries and suppliers.
- The Bryan County Megasite was developed by the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor JDA over several years in partnership with the state to reduce barriers to speedy operations.
- Industrial utilities are adjacent to the site, and extensive due diligence reports have been completed.
- Hyundai Motor Group is headquartered in Seoul, Korea, and employs 250,000 people worldwide.
- The Group’s vehicles are sold in 193 countries through 5,000 dealerships and showrooms.
- At the new Georgia facility, Hyundai Motor Group plans to produce a diverse range of innovative full electric vehicles, with details of production models to be shared later.
- Through the battery manufacturing facility, the Group aims to establish a stable supply chain for EV battery and other EV components in the U.S. market.
- When nearing the planned 2025 start of operations, Hyundai Motor Group will seek local vendors and suppliers.
- Bryan County is the fastest-growing county in Georgia, and the strategic purchase of the Bryan County Megasite in 2021 was the largest in state history.
- The site is adjacent to Interstate 16 with immediate access from I-95 and I-16 to 250 major metro areas.
- The site is less than 30 miles from the Port of Savannah, the single-largest and fastest-growing container terminal in the U.S., with two Class I rail facilities on-site provided by Georgia Central Railway.
- Georgia is the Southeastern leader for EV registrations per 1,000 registered automobiles and offers more EV charging outlets per capita than anywhere else in the Southeast.
- The Georgia Electric Mobility and Innovation Alliance is a public-private-nonprofit initiative focused on growing the electric mobility ecosystem in the state and strengthening Georgia’s position in electrification-related manufacturing and innovation.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) was represented by Project Manager Taylor Walden and Director of Korean Investment Yoonie Kim, along with the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor JDA, Georgia DOT, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia Power.
- Under Governor Kemp’s leadership, GDEcD has focused on putting Georgia at the forefront of electric mobility.
- At the new facility, Hyundai Motor Group plans to implement advanced intelligent manufacturing technologies currently under test at the Group’s innovation hub, including intelligent A.I.-equipped manufacturing and data-driven processes.
- The plant will support Hyundai Motor Group’s efforts to achieve its carbon neutrality target through the active use of renewable energy for the facility.
- Since 2020, Georgia has announced more than 20 EV-related projects that will invest more than $13.54 billion and create nearly 18,000 jobs in the state.
- Hyundai Motor Group's mobility brands include Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis.
