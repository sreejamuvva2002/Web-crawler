# Hyundai Motor Company and General Motors Strategic Collaboration

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-08-07

**Source:** [Hyundai and GM to launch jointly-developed EV in 2028 - electrive.com](https://www.electrive.com/2025/08/07/hyundai-and-gm-to-launch-jointly-developed-ev-in-2028/)  ·  `page_021159`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Company and General Motors confirmed plans to co-develop five new vehicles under their ongoing strategic collaboration.
- The collaboration includes a jointly developed electric commercial van aimed at the North American market, expected to launch as early as 2028.
- The remaining four co-developed vehicles will feature either internal combustion or hybrid drivetrains.
- The jointly developed electric commercial van will be manufactured in the U.S. as early as 2028.
- Hyundai will take the lead on the development of the co-developed vehicles.
- GM refers to the jointly developed electric van as a smaller sibling to Chevrolet’s BrightDrop vans.
- In April, GM paused production of Chevrolet’s BrightDrop vans at its Canadian plant for six month due to weak demand.
- The collaboration will yield four flexible-platform vehicles for Central and South American markets, including a compact SUV, a car and pickup, and a mid-size pickup, capable of supporting internal combustion or hybrid powertrains.
- The four flexible-platform vehicles for Central and South American markets are scheduled to reach market in 2028, with development efforts currently in progress.
- Hyundai and GM will share platforms for all five co-developed vehicles while developing unique interiors and exteriors consistent with their respective brands.
- Hyundai and GM expect to surpass combined annual sales of 800,000 units once full-scale production is achieved.
- The two manufacturers are aligning operations across their supply chains and sustainability initiatives.
- Joint sourcing programs are planned across North and South America for materials, transport, logistics, and complex systems.
- The companies will explore using low-carbon emissions steel to support decarbonized manufacturing processes.
- The announcement follows a framework agreement signed between GM and Hyundai in September 2024.
- At the time of the September 2024 framework agreement, GM and Hyundai indicated potential collaboration on development and production, as well as joint procurement of battery materials and steel.
- José Muñoz, President and CEO of Hyundai Motor Company, stated that the strategic collaboration will help deliver value and choice to customers across multiple vehicle segments and markets.
- Muñoz noted that combined scale in North and South America will help efficiently provide customers with beautifully designed, high-quality, safety-focused vehicles with appreciated technology.
- Shilpan Amin, GM Senior Vice President and Global Chief Procurement and Supply Chain Officer, highlighted the market relevance of the upcoming models.
- Amin stated that partnering together will allow GM and Hyundai to bring more choice to customers faster and at a lower cost.
- Amin noted that the first co-developed vehicles demonstrate how GM and Hyundai will leverage complementary strengths and combined scale.
