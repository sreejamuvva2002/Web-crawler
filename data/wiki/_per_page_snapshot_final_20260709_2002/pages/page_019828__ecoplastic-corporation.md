# Ecoplastic Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-02

**Source:** [Georgia Governor Announces New Manufacturing Facility Will Bring 456 New Jobs And Invest $205 Million | Doc and the Block](https://docandtheblock.com/georgia-governor-announces-new-manufacturing-facility-will-bring-456-new-jobs-and-invest-205-million/)  ·  `page_019828`

## Facts

- Republican Georgia Governor Brian Kemp announced that a new manufacturing facility in Bulloch County will create 456 new jobs and invest about $205 million.
- The facility will be opened by Ecoplastic Corporation, an automotive parts manufacturing company.
- Governor Kemp stated the company will serve the Hyundai Metaplant well from Statesboro due to its close proximity to Georgia's ports system.
- Ecoplastic was established in 1984 and is a Korea-based company that manufactures plastic automobile parts for original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) and other suppliers.
- Ecoplastic is a Tier-1 supplier for Hyundai Motor Group (HMG) and Kia.
- The company engages in the manufacturing of automotive interior and exterior plastic parts, including bumpers, consoles, trims, and plastic molds.
- Ecoplastic’s new facility will be located at 4822 Highway 301 South Register near Statesboro.
- The new plant is capable of injection molding and painting.
- The facility will produce exterior parts, bumpers and garnishes, interior parts, consoles, and trims for electric vehicles.
- The company expects to begin operations by October 2024.
- The company will be hiring for HR, finance, parts development, quality control, facility maintenance, injection, and paint production positions.
- Shang Han serves as the President and CEO of Ecoplastic.
- Shang Han stated that Ecoplastic is honored to establish a production facility for electric vehicle parts in Bulloch County, Georgia.
- Shang Han noted that by increasing new jobs within the area and providing a stable supply of high quality products to client HMGMA, the company looks forward to contributing to the economic development of the State of Georgia and Bulloch County.
- Shang Han expressed appreciation for the support of the State and County governments and stated Ecoplastic will do its best to preserve a continuous and mutually beneficial relationship.
