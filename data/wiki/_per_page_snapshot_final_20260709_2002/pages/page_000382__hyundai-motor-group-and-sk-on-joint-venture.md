# Hyundai Motor Group and SK On Joint Venture

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-05

**Source:** [Hyundai and SK On to build electric vehicle battery manufacturing facility in Bartow County](https://evmagz.com/hyundai-and-sk-on-to-build-electric-vehicle-battery-manufacturing-facility-in-bartow-county/)  ·  `page_000382`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Hyundai Motor Group (HMG) and SK On have selected a site in Bartow County for a new electric vehicle (EV) battery manufacturing facility that will supply Hyundai Motor Group’s plants in the U.S.
- The project is considered one of the largest economic development projects in Georgia state history.
- Stakeholders estimate it will create more than 3,500 new jobs through approximately $4-5 billion of investment in Bartow County.
- Hyundai Motor Group and SK On recently signed a memorandum of understanding (MOU) regarding the partnership for a new EV battery facility in the U.S., with details still in development.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Hyundai Motor Group and SK On are valued partners and key players in Georgia’s ever-growing automotive industry, emphasizing his administration's focus on bringing jobs to overlooked communities and praising their investment in the No. 1 state for business.
- SK On was established in 2021 as the lithium-ion battery subsidiary branch of SK Innovation.
- SK On currently employs more than 2,000 Georgians at its SK Battery America facility in Commerce.
- In July, SK Battery America announced it would partner with the Work for Warriors Georgia program to hire veterans, servicemembers, and their families at its Georgia operations.
- Subject to execution of relevant agreements and final board decisions, the new facility will be located at Bartow Centre, a zoned manufacturing and industrial site on Highway 411.
- The project is aiming to begin operations in 2025.
- Bartow County Sole Commissioner Steve Taylor expressed pleasure with the decision, thanked the former property owners, and noted the project will establish Bartow in Georgia’s EV ecosystem while leveraging the area's advanced manufacturing talent base.
- Director of Korean Investment Yoonie Kim represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on the competitive project.
- The project involved partnerships with Georgia EMC, Development Authority of Bartow County, Cartersville-Bartow County Department of Economic Development, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson highlighted that HMG and SK have been pioneering partners for decades, driving Georgia’s automotive renaissance, and noted the partnership helps create a fully integrated supply chain connecting battery manufacturers with recyclers.
- Georgia’s prime location, major ports system, extensive infrastructure, skilled workforce, and pro-business climate position it as a hub for the electric transportation industry.
- Excluding this announcement, EV-related projects announced in Georgia since 2020 total approximately $17 billion in investment and more than 22,800 new jobs.
- Hyundai Motor Group separately announced in May plans to invest $5.54 billion in Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America, an EV manufacturing facility in Bryan County.
- Hyundai Motor Group broke ground on the Metaplant America facility in October with Governor Kemp and other state, local, and federal officials.
- Hyundai secures EV battery supply from SK On for its plants in North America.
