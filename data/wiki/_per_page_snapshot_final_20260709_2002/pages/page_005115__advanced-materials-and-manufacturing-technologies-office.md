# Advanced Materials and Manufacturing Technologies Office

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [Advancing Manufacturing for Our Clean Energy Future: 2023 in Review | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/cmei/ammto/articles/advancing-manufacturing-our-clean-energy-future-2023-review)  ·  `page_005115`

## Facts

- AMMTO invested in five groundbreaking funding opportunities during its inaugural year.
- AMMTO launched four prize competitions during its inaugural year.
- AMMTO renewed three Clean Energy Manufacturing Innovation Institutes and the Critical Materials Innovation Hub during its inaugural year.
- The Next Generation Materials and Processes team, led by Program Manager Huijuan Dai, invested in 14 projects across 10 states to receive a total of $45 million in federal funding.
- AMMTO allocated $15 million to a team led by GE Research for developing, building, and refining manufacturing practices of large-scale machinery to manufacture wind and hydropower technology using 3D printing.
- AMMTO allocated a combined $30 million for 13 projects committed to advancing innovative practices that will reshape the design, materials, and sustainability of manufacturing for wind turbine equipment.
- AMMTO renewed the Clean Energy Smart Manufacturing Innovation Institute (CESMII) for an initial $6 million with potential additional funding across four subsequent fiscal years.
- CESMII aims to advance widespread adoption of smart manufacturing to accelerate domestic competitiveness in clean energy manufacturing and the nation’s transition to a net-zero carbon economy.
- AMMTO renewed the Institute for Advanced Composites Manufacturing Innovation (IACMI) for an initial $6 million and potential funding across four subsequent fiscal years.
- IACMI aims to further technological R&D and accelerate commercialization in the domestic composites manufacturing sector.
- AMMTO announced a $4.7 million request for proposals through the Cybersecurity Manufacturing Innovation Institute (CyManII) to fortify the cybersecurity landscape within American manufacturing.
- The Energy Technology and Manufacturing and Workforce team, led by Acting Program Manager Tina Kaarsberg, renewed DOE’s first Clean Energy Manufacturing Innovation Institute, PowerAmerica, for an initial $8 million, with potential funding across four subsequent fiscal years.
- PowerAmerica aims to advance domestic manufacturing of next-generation wide bandgap semiconductors for power electronics to aid economy-wide decarbonization and electrification.
- AMMTO selected eight projects for a total of $2 million to drive innovation in lithium-ion battery rejuvenation, recycling, and reuse.
- AMMTO awarded eight $75,000 prizes through Phase I of the Microbattery Design Prize to accelerate commercialization of innovative microbattery designs for clean energy manufacturing.
- AMMTO secured 61 pledges across leading private industry, national labs, and academia to double semiconductor energy efficiency every two years.
- This effort aims to increase economic competitiveness of American semiconductor manufacturers and strengthen domestic clean energy supply chains through the Energy Efficiency Scaling for 2 Decades (EES2) national initiative.
- AMMTO is awarding funds through the Community Energy Innovation Prize, soliciting submissions for the prize’s Manufacturing Ecosystem Track.
- AMMTO announced a 2023 cohort of 21 Lab-Embedded Entrepreneurship Program (LEEP) innovators to develop next-generation technologies for a clean energy future.
- AMMTO opened applications for the coming year’s LEEP cohort.
- The new LEEP cohort will spend two years embedded within four DOE national energy labs as fellows: West Gate at NREL, Cyclotron Road at LBNL, Chain Reaction Innovations at ANL, or Innovation Crossroads at ORNL.
- Program Manager Kate Peretti and the Secure and Sustainable Materials team published a historic DOE Critical Materials Assessment and corresponding list of Critical Materials to inform government-wide approach to clean energy technology elements, including research into alternatives, substitutes, and recycling.
- AMMTO launched a Critical Materials Collaborative to integrate critical materials RD&D across DOE, government, industry, and academia to accelerate domestic critical material supply chains.
- AMMTO announced a FOA to fund up to $10 million to establish a Critical Materials Accelerator for early-stage science and technology solutions research toward commercialization.
- These critical minerals and materials solutions will help the manufacturing sector deliver clean energy technologies needed to reach DOE and Biden-Harris Administration net-zero goals.
- AMMTO rebranded and renewed the Critical Materials Innovation Hub (formerly the Critical Materials Institute) for $30 million in the first year with potential additional funding across subsequent four fiscal years.
- The Critical Materials Innovation Hub aims to accelerate innovative scientific and technological solutions to develop resilient and secure supply chains for materials critical to clean energy technologies.
- AMMTO launched the Re-X Before Recycling clean energy prize, which will award a total of $5.6 million to teams developing ways to extend product or parts lifetimes via re-using, repairing, refurbishing, remanufacturing, or repurposing before recycling.
- AMMTO plans to launch a series of strategies and roadmaps in 2024 to lay out materials and manufacturing advances guiding the clean energy future.
