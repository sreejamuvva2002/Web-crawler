# Georgia Manufacturing Charges Regional Battery Industry Growth - C2ES Roundtable

**Type:** Event  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[unknown|Uncategorized]]  ·  **Location:** Atlanta, Georgia, Georgia, United States

## Overview

A roundtable hosted by the Center for Climate and Energy Solutions (C2ES) with Georgia Tech Strategic Energy Institute on November 5, 2025 to discuss Georgia’s rapid growth in electric vehicle manufacturing and battery supply-chain development, workforce needs, commercialization pathways, and policy support.

## EV supply-chain relevance

The event highlights Georgia’s leadership in EV manufacturing investment, the state’s role as a key market for locally produced batteries, and strategies to expand the regional battery supply chain.

## Key facts

- Georgia is the Southeast’s leader in electric vehicle manufacturing investment and a key market for locally produced batteries.
- Rapid growth has attracted suppliers and signaled strong demand for a regional battery industry.
- Success in attracting large-scale final assembly plants creates opportunities for other states to develop complementary parts of the supply chain.
- Workforce development is identified as a linchpin for long-term industry growth, with calls for expanded training programs and outreach to students.
- The battery sector offers new career pathways in advanced manufacturing and innovation for jobseekers unfamiliar with the industry.
- Participants highlighted gaps in translating lab technologies to commercial products due to limited early-stage investors.
- Near-term policy support is needed for battery material manufacturers to compete globally, including low-cost renewable energy and preferential financing.
- Recommendations include providing access to low-cost renewable power, offering favorable funding terms for new manufacturing lines, and supporting high-volume automated production.
