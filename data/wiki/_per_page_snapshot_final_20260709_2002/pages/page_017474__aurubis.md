# Aurubis

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2022-06-17

**Source:** [Georgia unveils Aurubis' first U.S. Multimetal Recycling Plant | News | wfxg.com](https://www.wfxg.com/news/georgia-unveils-aurubis-first-u-s-multimetal-recycling-plant/article_9ce37e58-7795-11ef-82b0-6360bea87673.html)  ·  `page_017474`

## Facts

- Aurubis Richmond initiated a multimetal recycling plant project in Augusta, Georgia, in 2022 as a $340-million investment.
- By the end of 2022, Aurubis expanded its plans and doubled the investment for the Augusta facility.
- The officially open multimetal recycling plant represents an $800-million project and is the first of its kind in the United States.
- The facility grinds up electronic material and metals to create new metals for other uses.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp attended the ribbon-cutting ceremony at the Augusta plant.
- The project will bring approximately 235 jobs to the area.
- Governor Kemp emphasized that the company focuses on bringing jobs paying over the average county wage, confirming these positions meet that standard.
- Aurubis Health and Safety Coordinator Tessie Wilkerson stated the facility employs people from everywhere, including experts from Germany.
- Tessie Wilkerson noted there is significant room for growth and expansion in the near future at the plant.
- Augusta Mayor Garnett Johnson attended the ribbon-cutting alongside current employees and local leaders.
- Mayor Johnson recalled a ground-breaking ceremony held on June 17, 2022, in an open field over two years prior to the article's publication.
- Mayor Johnson described Aurubis as a community partner that utilizes world-class technology, protects the environment, and creates jobs.
- Aurubis CEO Toralf Haag identified Georgia as an ideal location for growth due to government support and local colleges supplying the workforce.
- Toralf Haag cited additional advantages including good construction support, a strong labor market, a favorable environment, and proximity to ports, interstate highways, and the Augusta airport.
- The plant plans to process more than 180,000 tons of materials annually.
- Governor Kemp stated the facility will supply rare earth minerals, copper, aluminum, and other materials needed to build vehicle batteries.
- The German-based company aims to continue its sustainability projects and expand throughout the United States.
