# Georgia Electric Vehicle and Battery Supply Chain

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2021-02-25

**Source:** [Biden’s Energy Policies Help Keep Georgia Manufacturing Humming | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/articles/bidens-energy-policies-help-keep-georgia-manufacturing-humming)  ·  `page_002468`

## Facts

- President Biden’s Investing in America agenda is delivering industries, jobs, and opportunities to communities across the South.
- The cornerstone of that agenda is a suite of generous federal tax credits making the U.S. an irresistible destination for companies to manufacture electric vehicles, batteries, semiconductors, solar panels, and other clean energy technologies.
- These policies are turning industrial hubs across the Southeast into supermagnets for new investment.
- Georgia is Exhibit A of this manufacturing resurgence.
- Since President Biden took office, companies have planned $32 billion in electric vehicle, battery and clean energy supply chain investments in the Peach State.
- In Dalton, QCells is building America’s first-ever fully integrated solar supply chain.
- The QCells project in Dalton is creating 2,500 jobs.
- In Bryan County, Hyundai broke ground on its very first U.S. electric vehicle and battery plant.
- The Hyundai plant in Bryan County is creating more than 8,000 jobs.
- Jennifer M. Granholm, former Secretary of the U.S. Department of Energy, observed that Americans of all stripes want to land future industries, jobs, and opportunities in their communities.
- The federal tax credits are designed to ensure American products are made on American soil with American workers.
