# Aurubis AG

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2024-09-28

**Source:** [Aurubis Ribbon Cutting: Germany’s Largest Investor in Georgia Focuses on American Security, Sustainability  - Global Atlanta](https://www.globalatlanta.com/aurubis-ribbon-cutting-germanys-largest-investor-in-georgia-focuses-on-american-security-sustainability/)  ·  `page_001262`

## Facts

- Germany’s largest investor in Georgia declared intentions for an $800 million investment in the state with U.S. interests at heart.
- Aurubis AG is focused on “metals for America” at a new smelting operation just south of Augusta.
- A ribbon-cutting ceremony was held Sept. 20 with patriotic flair including a marching-band welcome, a national anthem rendition by a classically trained singer, and a James Brown impersonator.
- The facility will soon house equipment for sampling material to determine its metal content.
- Aurubis Richmond joins a network of plants employing 7,000 people from Germany to Bulgaria and beyond.
- It is the first major secondary smelter in the U.S.
- New Aurubis global CEO Toralf Haag stated the investment will make supply chains more independent by keeping strategically important metals in the loop for the U.S. economy and its customers.
- Aurubis uses shredding and smelting technology with massive rotating furnaces installed by German supplier SMS to separate copper and other metals from printed circuit boards and insulated copper wire.
- The resulting slag material fuels up to half of the facility’s own fuel needs.
- CEO Toralf Haag stated American copper stays here, powers lives here, and drives futures here, strengthening American industry and entrepreneurship.
- Copper can be “infinitely” recycled, creating a closed-loop system that cuts emissions drastically versus mining when paired with the right energy mix.
- CEO Toralf Haag stated today's penny could be tomorrow's transmission line, mobile phone, or medical device, and their metals will drive economic growth across Georgia and the U.S.
- Aurubis claims its smelters emit half the greenhouse gases of competitors.
- The company aims to be carbon-neutral before 2050 with the use of new hydrogen technology.
- In December 2022, Aurubis announced a second phase in Augusta, doubling capacity to 180,000 tons of copper through a commitment of a second $400 million that brought the investment to its record-breaking level.
- Third and fourth phases are possible, with the company currently only using about a third of the 150 acres it purchased.
- Colin Kelly, chair of the Recycled Materials Association (ReMA), said the industry is now viewed as “sustainable, resilient and essential.”
- Colin Kelly stated there is no decarbonization or clean economy without this sector, and the facility brings needed materials.
- Jasjit Singh, executive director of SelectUSA, said Aurubis exemplifies policies prioritizing the intersection of clean-energy and national security.
- Jasjit Singh noted the U.S. has been the No. 1 foreign investment destination for 12 years running, and Georgia has been wonderfully placed over recent years.
- Before opening the first phase, the company hired more than 150 of the 230 people it plans to employ.
- Aurubis invested heavily in community engagement, helping set up an apprenticeship program and hosting oversubscribed job fairs that helped grow local technical college enrollment and change perceptions around manufacturing.
- The company put $200,000 into workforce training and has been an ally in promoting manufacturing as a career path.
- Augusta Technical College President Jermaine Whirl noted Aurubis hammered home messages with parents and counselors, and Augusta Tech faculty spent time in Germany learning processes to teach a growing pipeline of students.
- Augusta is a beneficiary of federal policies aimed at driving domestic resiliency in the clean-energy sector.
- The Inflation Reduction Act provides incentives for domestically sourced materials for electric-vehicle batteries.
- The IRA has been a key driver for companies like Denkai, a Japanese producer of copper films, and Syensqo, the Belgian plastics firm that got a $178 million U.S. Department of Energy Grant to make separator films for EV batteries.
- Augusta Chamber of Commerce President Angie Cox described the influx of foreign firms as “transformative.”
- Augusta is set to welcome an estimated 100,000 guests for the Arts in the Heart festival over that weekend.
- Ms. Cox noted Augusta positions itself as a clean energy, EV battery and recycling hub, especially with the Workforce Hub designation from the Biden-Harris administration.
- She added the city enjoys a strong industry mix, especially with Fort Eisenhower (formerly Fort Gordon) designated as a U.S. cybersecurity hub.
- Gov. Brian Kemp returned to the Aurubis plant two years after a groundbreaking under the hot Georgia sun.
- Gov. Kemp said the investment is evidence of the business-friendly environment his government helped create.
- Area Development magazine named Georgia its top spot for business for the 11th straight year.
- Gov. Kemp stated he talks about this company and project all the time as one of his favorites, showing the value of the state’s international relationships and local community diligence.
- Gov. Kemp noted hearing the American national anthem reminded him of going to the symphony in Hamburg while visiting the Aurubis facility on a European mission.
- David Schultheis is president and managing director of Aurubis Richmond.
- Mr. Schultheis stated American employees join a team with 61 nationalities around the world, many represented at the ribbon-cutting.
- He expressed love for working with an international and diverse team, having a foot in both German and American cultures.
- Aurubis Richmond used 4,000 tons of structural steel in the building process.
- 1 million working hours were spent since groundbreaking two years ago.
- 130 feet is the height of the tallest structure on the premises.
