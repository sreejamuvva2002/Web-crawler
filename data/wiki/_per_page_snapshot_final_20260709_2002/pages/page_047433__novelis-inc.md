# Novelis Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2025-02-20

**Source:** [Significant step toward carbon-neutral production: Novelis successfully tests industrial scale use of hydrogen in a recycling furnace | Novelis Recycling UK](https://www.novelisrecycling.co.uk/significant-step-toward-carbon-neutral-production-novelis-successfully-tests-industrial-scale-use-of-hydrogen-in-a-recycling-furnace/)  ·  `page_047433`

## Facts

- Novelis Inc. is a leading sustainable aluminium solutions provider and the world leader in aluminium rolling and recycling.
- Novelis successfully tested using hydrogen fuel to power a recycling furnace at its UK plant in Latchford, Warrington.
- The tests were carried out as part of a UK government decarbonization programme and in collaboration with Progressive Energy, an independent UK energy company.
- The project required the installation of new burners, regenerators, and furnace lining material.
- Using hydrogen instead of the same amount of natural gas when operating a melting furnace can reduce CO2e emissions by up to 90%.
- Emilio Braghi, Executive Vice President of Novelis Inc. and President of Novelis Europe, stated that exploring renewable energy sources like hydrogen, making first mover investments, and reducing energy intensity are part of the company's 3×30 vision to advance aluminium as the material of choice with circular solutions.
- The expansion of local recycling capacity at the Latchford site is transforming it into a prototype for high-recycled content and decarbonized aluminium production.
- Safety was the top priority during the hydrogen pilot project, with several series of tests conducted by blending different percentages of hydrogen with natural gas ranging from 30% to 100%.
- The trial campaign involved remelting and casting several hundred tonnes of 3000 series scrap aluminium alloy into sheet ingots.
- All relevant parameters were measured during the trial to assess any impact on the product, process, operating environment, and environmental emissions.
- Further downstream processing, including rolling and finishing, will be completed at other Novelis plants in Europe to establish the real end-to-end parameters of a hydrogen-based recycled alloy production process.
- A report detailing the full post-trial evaluation and assessments will be released as part of the UK government’s Industrial Fuel Switching programme later this year.
- Allan Sweeney, Plant Manager at Novelis Latchford, noted that the use of hydrogen is not common in the aluminium industry today and expressed pride in being one of the pioneers to test this new fuel at an industrial scale in a real-world environment.
- The results from the Latchford trial will drive further research into the potential deployment of hydrogen in Novelis' recycling operations worldwide.
- The demonstration project is part of the UK government’s Industrial Fuel Switching Competition programme and is supported with a grant of £4.6 million.
- The grant funding comes as part of the £1 billion Net Zero Innovation Portfolio and the wider regional HyNet project.
- The programme is designed to support industry in decarbonising operations through a switch from natural gas to low carbon hydrogen.
- HyNet aims to decarbonise industry across the North West and North Wales through the production, transportation, and storage of low-carbon hydrogen, and by capturing industry’s carbon dioxide emissions through Carbon Capture Solutions.
- Novelis has been a partner in the HyNet project since 2017 and is supporting the development of the regional infrastructure project.
- Novelis is conducting its own technical feasibility studies into using hydrogen as a direct replacement for natural gas.
- Novelis research and development teams worldwide continue to investigate the possibility of using plasma and electricity to supply energy to its production plants.
- In July 2024, Novelis announced an investment of approximately $90 million to double the recycling capacity for used beverage cans (UBCs) at its Latchford plant.
- The UBC recycling expansion project will increase the facility’s capacity by 85 kilotonnes per year and decrease Novelis Europe’s CO2e by more than 350,000 tonnes annually.
- The Latchford UBC recycling expansion project is expected to begin commissioning in December 2026.
- As part of its 3×30 vision, Novelis has set sustainability targets to achieve by the end of 2030, including increasing recycled content to 75% from today’s 63%.
- Another 2030 target is reducing carbon emissions to less than 3 tonnes CO2e per tonne of aluminium shipped.
- Novelis aims to continue leading the industry to circularity through first-mover investments and achieve carbon neutrality by 2050 or sooner.
- Novelis is a global leader in the production of innovative aluminum products and solutions, and the world’s largest recycler of aluminum.
- The company partners with suppliers and customers in the aerospace, automotive, beverage can, and specialties industries throughout North America, Europe, Asia, and South America.
- Novelis reported net sales of $16.2 billion in fiscal year 2024.
- Novelis is a subsidiary of Hindalco Industries Limited, an industry leader in aluminum and copper, and the metals flagship company of the Aditya Birla Group, a multinational conglomerate based in Mumbai.
- Novelis Latchford in Warrington, UK, is one of Europe’s largest aluminium used beverage cans recycling plants.
- The Latchford facility is Europe’s largest closed-loop recycling operation for automotive aluminium rolled products with an annual recycling capacity of up to 195,000 tonnes.
- The plant has enough capacity to recycle every aluminium beverage can sold in the UK.
- Novelis collaborated with Jaguar Land Rover (JLR) to create the first closed-loop system in Europe for automotive aluminium rolled products.
- Novelis operates across borders in well-established and efficient supply chains due to the high level of economic integration and geographical proximity between the EU, UK, and Switzerland.
- This interconnectedness creates important economies of scale that benefit value chains across Europe and enables Novelis to maximize the use of recycled inputs for low-carbon, sustainable aluminum solutions.
