# Brian P. Kemp

**Type:** person  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2025-01

**Source:** [Governor Kemp to Reinforce Economic Partnerships in Germany and Poland - Augusta CEO](https://augustaceo.com/features/2025/01/governor-kemp-reinforce-economic-partnerships-germany-and-poland/)  ·  `page_018379`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced an international mission to Europe on January 21, 2025, to strengthen economic and cultural ties in Germany and Poland while developing new relationships for Georgians.
- Over the last six years, international companies have invested over $38 billion in Georgia, according to Governor Kemp.
- These investments created more than 58,000 new opportunities for Georgians in every corner of the state, according to Governor Kemp.
- Communities outside of metro Atlanta attracted over 80% of those jobs, according to Governor Kemp.
- Georgia’s partnership with Germany is instrumental to its economic success, as Germany is one of the state's largest trade partners and a major contributor to job creation and investment, stated Governor Kemp.
- The mission aims to reinforce collaboration with Germany while opening dialogues with Poland, an emerging market, to ensure a brighter future for Georgians, stated Governor Kemp.
- As we prepare to return money to Georgia taxpayers and invest in key areas like public safety and education, this mission is an opportunity to reinforce that collaboration while also opening dialogues with an emerging market, ensuring a brighter future for all Georgians, stated Governor Kemp.
- Governor Kemp and representatives from the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) will conduct meetings with firms already operating or under construction in Georgia.
- Notable German companies on the itinerary include BASF, Gerresheimer, Carcoustics, KIRCHHOFF Automotive, and GEDIA Automotive Group.
- German companies excel in industries like life sciences and automotive manufacturing, and Georgia serves as a strategic gateway for them and other European businesses entering the U.S. market, stated GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson.
- Over the past two decades, German automotive firms alone have contributed approximately 2,200 jobs and $830 million in investments to Georgia communities, according to Commissioner Wilson.
- The growth of life sciences companies like Gerresheimer demonstrates how international businesses become integral to the state's economy, according to Commissioner Wilson.
- Exploring emerging markets such as Poland allows Georgia to stay ahead of global trends, build new partnerships, and support local companies seeking to expand internationally, according to Commissioner Wilson.
- The State of Georgia has maintained continuous representation in Europe since 1973.
- Germany is the fourth largest export and import market for the State of Georgia.
- More than $12.8 billion in total trade moved between Georgia’s global gateways and Germany in 2023.
- German companies have invested more than $3.9 billion in Georgia since 2010 through projects with state involvement.
- An estimated 650 German-owned facilities support approximately 42,000 jobs for Georgians across the state.
- Georgia-based companies such as Alimera Sciences, Bridgestone Golf, Delta Air Lines, Eversheds Sutherland, Graphic Packaging Holding, Novelis, Porex Corporation, WestRock, and others maintain operations in Germany.
- BASF’s manufacturing presence in Georgia includes operations in Attapulgus, Edgar, and Savannah.
- Gerresheimer first established a production facility in Peachtree City in 1993.
- Gerresheimer expanded its Peachtree City location in 2009 with the establishment of a Technical Competence Center (TCC).
- Gerresheimer announced additional expansions in Georgia in December 2023 and May 2024.
- In 2017, Carcoustics established Carcoustics USA South Inc. in Buford, Georgia, including a central research center and a manufacturing presence.
- KIRCHHOFF Automotive opened an assembly facility in Lawrenceville in 2023.
- In 2020, GEDIA Automotive Group announced an advanced manufacturing facility that will begin production with a focus on parts for electric vehicles in the greater Dalton area.
