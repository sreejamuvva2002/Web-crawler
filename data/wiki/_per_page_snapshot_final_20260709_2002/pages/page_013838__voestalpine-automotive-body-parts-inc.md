# voestalpine Automotive Body Parts Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2016-08-16

**Source:** [voestalpine Automotive Body Parts To Expand In Bartow County | Cartersville, GA Patch](https://patch.com/georgia/cartersville/voestalpine-automotive-body-parts-expand-bartow-county)  ·  `page_013838`

## Facts

- Georgia Governor Nathan Deal announced that voestalpine Automotive Body Parts Inc. will expand its existing operation in Bartow County, Georgia.
- The expansion involves a $50 million investment in the local community.
- The investment will result in the creation of 150 new jobs.
- The company currently employs approximately 100 Georgians at the Cartersville facility.
- voestalpine Automotive Body Parts Inc. serves as the supply base for the German-based voestalpine Group.
- The company produces ultra-high strength body-in-white components for premium car manufacturers.
- The newly created jobs will range from advanced manufacturing to hot-forming and assembly positions.
- The facility offers extensive training opportunities, including an apprenticeship program with students of the Bartow County College & Career Academy in Cartersville.
- Gov. Nathan Deal stated: “voestalpine’s decision to expand operations in Bartow County strengthens Georgia’s growing automotive industry and highlights the state’s extensive manufacturing capabilities.”
- Gov. Nathan Deal added: “When we met with voestalpine executives in Germany, we discussed the benefits that Georgia can continue to offer the growing company. The business environment in Bartow County and throughout the state will help voestalpine maintain its position as a leader in automotive supply, while bolstering Georgia’s reputation as a top state for business.”
- Philipp Schulz is the managing director of voestalpine Automotive Body Parts Inc.
- Philipp Schulz stated: “This investment is an important step in the growth strategy of our company in the U.S. and positions us well in the center of the southern automotive hub.”
- Philipp Schulz added: “We would especially like to thank state and local officials as well as the Georgia Department of Economic Development for their assistance in this project. We are very excited about expanding operations in Cartersville, where our company has found great partners for our operations.”
- Bartow County Commissioner Steve Taylor stated: “We are so proud and thankful for voestalpine’s investment in Bartow County.”
- Steve Taylor added: “They were the first company to build in Highland 75 and their multiple additional phases, continued investment and creation of jobs shows that Bartow is a great location to do business.”
- Steve Taylor noted the success of the Highland 75 location is a testament to collaboration between the county, the city of Cartersville, state officials, and corporate partners.
- voestalpine Automotive Body Parts Inc. is a subsidiary company of the Metal Forming Division of the voestalpine Group.
- The Metal Forming Division produces highly refined sections, tubes, precision strip steel products, and ready-to-install components made of pressed, stamped, and roll formed parts.
