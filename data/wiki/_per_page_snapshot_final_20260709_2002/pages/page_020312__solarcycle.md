# Solarcycle

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2024-02-15

**Source:** [Company plans $344 million Georgia factory to make recycled glass for solar panels](https://www.wuga.org/local-news/2024-02-15/company-plans-344-million-georgia-factory-to-make-recycled-glass-for-solar-panels)  ·  `page_020312`

## Facts

- Arizona-based Solarcycle announced plans to build a $344 million factory in northwest Georgia.
- The new facility marks the company's first expansion into manufacturing new glass for solar panels from recycled materials.
- Solarcycle will hire more than 600 workers at the Cedartown, Georgia site.
- The Cedartown factory is scheduled to open in 2026.
- Cedartown is located approximately 50 miles (80 kilometers) northwest of Atlanta.
- Solarcycle was founded in 2022 and previously opened its first facility in Odessa, Texas.
- The company opened a headquarters and research lab in Mesa, Arizona earlier this month (February 2024), hiring more than 100 people.
- Solarcycle can extract materials worth 95% of a solar panel's value, including silver, silicon, copper, and aluminum.
- The Georgia factory will produce enough glass annually to manufacture solar panels capable of generating 5 gigawatts of electricity.
- The company plans to sell the manufactured glass to solar panel producers in the United States.
- South Korean-owned Qcells, which manufactures solar panels in nearby Dalton, contracted Solarcycle to recycle decommissioned Qcells panels in the United States.
- Solarcycle has similar recycling contracts with more than 40 other solar energy companies.
- The company has raised tens of millions of dollars from private investors for expansion.
- Solarcycle received $1.5 million from the U.S. Department of Energy to fund research and development.
- The facility could qualify for $9 million in state income tax credits, calculated at $3,000 per job over five years.
- To qualify for the state income tax credits, workers must earn at least $31,300 annually.
- Cedartown and Polk County will provide property tax breaks to the company, though no specific estimate was provided.
- Georgia Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Solarcycle approached state economic recruiters at a trade show.
- Chris Thomas, president and CEO of the Development Authority of Polk County, confirmed the property tax breaks but did not provide an estimate.
- Wilson stated that “Solarcycle provides a critical piece to the integrated solar supply chain we are building in Georgia.”
- U.S. Sen. Raphael Warnock credited President Joe Biden's Inflation Reduction Act with spurring Solarcycle's investment in Georgia.
