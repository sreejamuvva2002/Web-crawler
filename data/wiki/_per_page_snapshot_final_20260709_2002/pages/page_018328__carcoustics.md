# Carcoustics

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2017-06-13

**Source:** [Carcoustics To Bring 200 Jobs To Gwinnett | Dacula, GA Patch](https://patch.com/georgia/dacula/carcoustics-bring-200-jobs-gwinnett)  ·  `page_018328`

## Facts

- Carcoustics, an auto-industry supply company based in Germany, will spend $6 million on a new manufacturing facility in Buford, Georgia.
- The company plans to create 200 jobs over the next few years.
- Governor Nathan Deal announced the project on Tuesday.
- Carcoustics develops and produces acoustic and thermally effective components for the automotive industry and other companies.
- Its customers include major manufacturers of cars, trucks, domestic appliances, agricultural machinery, and HVAC systems.
- Stefan Vallentin, project manager at Carcoustics, stated the company is happy to locate its new plant in Georgia due to great international transport connections and a logistical location between BMW in South Carolina and Mercedes in Alabama.
- The company identified potential customers throughout the South, including Volkswagen, Mercedes, Volvo, Nissan, Honda, Toyota, Kia, and Hyundai.
- Gwinnett County Board of Commissioners Chair Charlotte Nash welcomed Carcoustics to Gwinnett County, noting it joins a strong and growing base of international companies in the area.
- Governor Deal emphasized that Georgia offers automotive industry suppliers resources to compete worldwide, citing a well-trained workforce, a thriving automotive industry, and the nation’s top business environment.
- Governor Deal highlighted efforts to build international relationships as yielding positive results with German companies locating in Georgia.
