# Amazon Web Services (AWS)

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2025-08-18

**Source:** [AWS Commits  Billion to AI and Cloud Expansion in Georgia](https://www.timesofai.com/news/aws-11b-ai-cloud-expansion-georgia/)  ·  `page_017558`

## Facts

- Amazon Web Services has unveiled plans to invest $11 billion in Georgia to enhance its AI and cloud computing infrastructure.
- The investment will fund the construction of cutting-edge data centers in Butts and Douglas counties.
- The project is intended to create 550 direct high-tech jobs.
- The project will also create many indirect construction and supply chain jobs.
- Roger Wehner, vice president of economic development at AWS, stated that AWS's ongoing infrastructure investments across the United States demonstrate a commitment to powering customers' digital innovation through cloud and AI technologies.
- AWS expects the planned multibillion-dollar investment to create more than 550 well-paying jobs and drive significant economic growth for Georgia.
- The initiative showcases Georgia's increasing relevance as a cutting-edge, digital hub.
- The infrastructure will support workloads for various AI applications, including machine learning models and generative AI applications.
- Dr. Romona Jackson Jones, Douglas County Commission Chairwoman, welcomed AWS bringing secure, resilient, and advanced cloud computing technology to the county.
- Dr. Jackson Jones noted the investment will bring hundreds of high-paying, high-tech jobs that will benefit the community and citizens long-term.
- Dr. Jackson Jones described the initial investment as a game-changer that will place the community on the forefront of innovation and establish Georgia as a leader in technology, innovation, and artificial intelligence.
- AWS has contributed investments of over $18.5 billion to Georgia since 2010.
- Beyond job creation, AWS has promised disaster relief initiatives.
- AWS has pledged renewable energy projects, including the Bulldog Solar farm.
- AWS has committed to STEM education programs such as Girls’ Tech Day and Think Big Spaces.
- Russ Crumbley, chairman of the Butts County Board of Commissioners, expressed pleasure that Amazon Web Services chose to locate new data centers in Butts County.
- Crumbley stated the investment will be the most significant in Butts County history and represents a collaborative effort involving local government entities.
- The expansion aligns with AWS’s wider strategy of innovating and sustaining operations across its platform.
- AWS plans to utilize robust infrastructure, skilled workforce development, and educational partnerships to solidify Georgia's position as a leading technology state.
