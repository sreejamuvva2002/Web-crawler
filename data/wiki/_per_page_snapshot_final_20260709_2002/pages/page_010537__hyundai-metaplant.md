# Hyundai Metaplant

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Chatham County prepares for impact from new Hyundai Metaplant - WJCL](https://www.wjcl.com/article/chatham-county-hyundai-metaplant-housing-roads/45865646)  ·  `page_010537`

## Facts

- Hyundai expects production to begin at its new electric vehicle manufacturing plant in Bryan County by early 2025.
- Chatham County leaders expect thousands of people to move to the area as a result of the Hyundai Metaplant and other similar projects.
- Trip Tollison, President and CEO of the Savannah Economic Development Authority, stated that adding the Hyundai Project announced last year turned local growth from steady to explosive.
- Chatham County Commission Chairman Chester Ellis said every day involves planning for the project's effects on Chatham County.
- Every community in Chatham County, from Bloomingdale to Tybee, will be affected by the project.
- Chatham County leaders are addressing housing concerns for incoming residents by focusing on income-based housing to ensure affordability for apartments, single-family homes, and multifamily units.
- Savannah Economic Development Authority President and CEO Trip Tollison expects new housing developments and planned communities to come online to address housing deficiencies.
- Trip Tollison noted that zoning will be used to protect environmentally sensitive areas from new housing developments.
- Chatham County leaders are working to prepare roads for an influx of vehicles related to the Hyundai Metaplant.
- Chatham County Commission Chairman Chester Ellis said officials are coordinating with federal, state, and county transportation agencies to secure federal funding for widening Quacco Road, Little Neck Road, and John Carter Road where they cross federal highways.
- Trip Tollison stated that major changes will occur in the Highway 280 corridor, including a brand new interchange on I-16 related to the Hyundai Metaplant and other improvements under the I-16 Corridor Improvement Plan.
- Chatham County leaders are addressing potential parking issues for the area.
- Trip Tollison stated that handling increased parking demand is a certainty, not just a possibility.
- Trip Tollison described the Hyundai Metaplant as the largest project in Georgia's history in the realm of economic development.
- The Hyundai Metaplant will have a workforce of 8,500 employees.
