# Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution Battery Joint Venture

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Group And LGES Confirm 30 GWh Battery JV In Georgia - AutoMoto Tale](https://automototale.com/car-reviews/hyundai-motor-group-and-lges-confirm-30-gwh-battery-jv-in-georgia/)  ·  `page_056186`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution officially announced a plan to establish a 50/50 EV battery joint venture in the United States.
- The two South Korean companies signed a memorandum of understanding with an intention to build a 30-gigawatt-hour (GWh) battery factory in Bryan County, Savannah, Georgia.
- The joint venture will involve an investment of over $4.3 billion (KRW 5.7 trillion).
- Construction of the battery plant is targeted to start in the second half of the publication year.
- The construction site is located on a plot adjacent to the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America site, which is currently under construction.
- Battery production at the new facility is targeted to start before the end of 2025.
- The plant's annual capacity of 30 GWh is expected to be sufficient for approximately 300,000 electric vehicles.
- The 30 GWh capacity indicates an average battery pack size of roughly 100-kilowatt-hours (kWh).
- Several weeks prior to this announcement, Hyundai Motor Group signed a similar agreement with SK Innovation’s SK On to build a 35-GWh battery joint venture in Bartow Centre, Georgia.
- Both the new LG Energy Solution JV and the SK On JV will supply battery cells for the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America plant.
- The two battery factories will also supply cells for Hyundai Motor Group's other manufacturing sites.
- Battery packs for these vehicles will be assembled by Hyundai Mobis.
- Jaehoon Chang, President and CEO of Hyundai Motor Company, stated that Hyundai Motor Group is focusing on electrification efforts to secure a leadership position in the global auto industry.
- Chang added that the group will create a strong foundation to lead the global EV transition through establishing a new EV battery cell plant with LG Energy Solution.
- Youngsoo Kwon, CEO of LG Energy Solution, stated that two strong leaders in the auto and battery industries have joined hands to drive the EV transition in America.
- Kwon noted that LG Energy Solution will commit its best efforts to offering ultimate sustainable energy solutions by advancing product competitiveness and global operational expertise.
- Hyundai Motor Group, which includes the Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis brands, is preparing for high-volume electric vehicle production in the US.
- The group aims for 600,000 annual EV units based on the recently announced battery plants.
- LG Energy Solution noted that the new JV with Hyundai is its seventh battery plant project in the US, which are currently operating or under construction.
- The total output of LG Energy Solution's seven US battery plants is roughly 250-300 GWh annually.
- Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution have been partnering on electrification for many years, starting with a first non-rechargeable hybrid in 2009.
- Their partnership includes several all-electric models, including the Hyundai Kona Electric and Hyundai Ioniq 6.
- Both companies are also working on another joint battery project for an upcoming plant in Indonesia with a capacity of 10 GWh annually.
- The new Georgia facility will produce lithium-ion cells specifically for electric vehicles.
- Construction start is targeted for the second half of 2023 according to the article's summary section.
- The facility is expected to supply battery cells to Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America in Bryan County, Georgia.
- The facility is also expected to supply Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Alabama (HMMA) factory in Montgomery, Alabama.
- The facility is expected to supply the Kia Georgia Plant (KaGA).
