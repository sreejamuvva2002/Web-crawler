# Martinez v. Mobis Alabama, LLC et al.

**Type:** event  ·  **Role:** workforce_training  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Home - Martinez v. Mobis Alabama LLC et al.](https://www.mobisalsettlement.com/)  ·  `page_046808`

## Facts

- A purported class action lawsuit was filed against Mobis Alabama, LLC d/b/a Hyundai Mobis, Kia Georgia, Inc., GB2G, Inc. d/b/a Allswell, SPJ Connect, Inc., Total Employee Solution Support, LLC (TESS), and Youngjin Lee.
- The lawsuit alleges the defendants misrepresented job terms offered to TN visa holders and violated state and federal statutes regarding recruiting, employment, and workplace practices.
- Affected workers were recruited by TESS, SPJ, or Allswell and assigned to work at Kia Georgia’s or Mobis’s West Point, Georgia locations.
- The relevant period for affected workers who received wages from Allswell is August 11, 2018 through the present.
- Eligible class members may receive an approximate settlement payment of between $4,100.00 and $16,800.00.
- The defendants disagree on whether their recruiting and employment practices were proper but agreed to resolve the dispute through a settlement.
- The U.S. District Court for the Northern District of Georgia (Newnan Division) granted final approval of the settlement on May 29, 2026.
- Class members have legal options to remain in the settlement class, exclude themselves from the agreement, object to it in writing, or attend a hearing.
