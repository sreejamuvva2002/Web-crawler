# Stryten Energy LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** research_and_development  ·  **Published:** 2024-08

**Source:** [Stryten Energy Opens Expanded, State-of-the-Art Battery Testing Lab - Metro Atlanta CEO](https://metroatlantaceo.com/news/2024/08/stryten-energy-opens-expanded-state-art-battery-testing-lab/)  ·  `page_050507`

## Facts

- Stryten Energy LLC is a U.S.-based energy storage solutions provider.
- The company added to its in-house battery testing and analysis capabilities with a new laboratory space.
- A ribbon-cutting celebration for the new battery lab was held on August 26 at Stryten Energy’s new corporate headquarters.
- Attendees at the event included staff members from the offices of Senator Jon Ossoff, Senator Raphael Warnock, Representative Rich McCormick, Representative Andrew Clyde, and local community leaders.
- Event attendees toured the new battery lab and an onsite working solar plus energy storage microgrid.
- Lieutenant Governor Burt Jones issued a proclamation recognizing the celebration.
- In his proclamation, Lieutenant Governor Burt Jones stated: “Through innovative projects, Stryten Energy is helping shape the future of energy storage technology in Georgia that will be important on a national scale for U.S. energy independence and security. Stryten Energy forging a brighter future for economic development in Georgia and fulfilling a shared commitment to innovation and growth, harnessing collective talents for the prosperity of the state of Georgia.”
- Mike Judd serves as CEO and President of Stryten Energy.
- Mike Judd stated: “For the U.S. to achieve long-term energy resiliency and energy security, we’ll need a range of solutions across battery chemistries,” and emphasized the need to stress test lead and lithium batteries to achieve market readiness for newer chemistries such as vanadium.
- With the expanded lab, Stryten Energy will collaborate with new and existing customers to find additional applications for various battery chemistries and strengthen existing ones.
- The lab supports Stryten Energy’s engineering, research and development, and quality assurance departments, as well as manufacturing facilities, in new product development, technology evaluations, warranty investigations, and root cause analysis.
- The expanded facility includes a Corporate Materials Lab that performs tests on various battery materials, including electrolyte, polypropylene, lead, and lead alloys for use in automotive, heavy-duty commercial, industrial, lithium, and vanadium redox flow batteries (VRFB).
- The facility includes a Lead Battery Test Lab that performs electrical performance and teardown testing on automotive, industrial, and heavy-duty commercial batteries, including enhanced flooded (EFB), conventional flooded, and absorbed glass mat (AGM) batteries.
- The facility includes a Lithium Battery Test Lab, the newest addition, which focuses on conducting non-destructive functional charge/discharge, environmental, and UL-related testing on lithium battery products.
- The new lab will serve as a model to other local energy technology companies and further strengthen Georgia’s claim as one of the key power players in the clean energy sector.
