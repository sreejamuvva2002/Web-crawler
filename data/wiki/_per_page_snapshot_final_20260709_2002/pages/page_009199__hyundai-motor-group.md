# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-20

**Source:** [Hyundai to Invest $2.7B in Georgia, Showing Commitment Despite Immigration Raid - Global Atlanta](https://www.globalatlanta.com/hyundai-to-invest-2-7b-in-georgia-showing-commitment-despite-immigration-raid/)  ·  `page_009199`

## Facts

- Even after a Sept. 4 immigration raid that threw a wrench into its operations, Hyundai Motor Group said it would invest $2.7 billion more in Georgia.
- The investment clarifies plans to create thousands more jobs at its Metaplant near Savannah.
- Hyundai announced earlier this year that it would basically double down on the $20.5 billion the conglomerate has already invested in the United States.
- The announcement had not specified how its Georgia car facilities would be affected.
- The timing of the new investment was announced at a global investor conference held for the first time in the U.S.
- Executives said the commitment to Georgia remains solid despite the immigration snafu.
- The raid has played out on television screens across Asia with horror stories shared by returned detainees, shaking investor confidence in the U.S.
- In March, Hyundai said it would put $9 billion more into U.S. automotive manufacturing.
- This boosts domestic production capacity to 1.2 million units.
- It is part of a $21 billion U.S. investment plan that also included a steel mill in Louisiana.
- In August, as Korean President Lee Jae-myung visited the White House, Hyundai added $5 billion more to the overall total.
- The latest Georgia investment will create 3,000 “direct and indirect jobs” on and around the site.
- Tami Overby is a Korea trade expert at DGA Group.
- Hyundai Motor Chairman Chung Eui-sun was noticeably absent during the parade of business leaders traveling with President Lee to the White House in August.
- His absence may be a sign of discontent over 25 percent auto tariffs imposed in April, days after he joined President Trump to announce the U.S. investment plan.
- Hyundai’s U.S. subsidiaries spent $1 million to be present at President Trump’s inauguration.
- This was the first time Tami Overby remembers seeing Korean companies getting involved in American politics.
- Tami Overby stated she is not sure Americans realized Chairman Chung might be unhappy and understands why.
- Hyundai clarified it would raise production capacity in Georgia to 500,000 units per year in the second phase of its plant over the next three years.
- CEO Jose Muñoz sees this ramp-up as being driven mainly by hybrids.
- This is a shift from initial plans for the plant to operate as a pure-electric factory.
- With the repeal of incentives and flagging demand for EVs, Hyundai is leaning more heavily into hybrids.
- More than 18 new hybrid models will be introduced globally by 2030.
- 10 hybrid and EV models are set to be made at Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America by 2030.
- By 2030, 60 percent of vehicles sold globally will be electrified, a category that includes hybrids and battery-electric vehicles.
- New models will include a mid-size pickup truck for the U.S.
- Extended-range EVs launching in 2027 will achieve 600 miles of range by combining smaller “in-house” batteries with conventional motors.
- The goal is to eliminate range anxiety.
- Plans for next-generation batteries in the U.S. could hinge on Hyundai getting its joint-venture battery factory with LG up and running.
- That $4.3 billion site was targeted in the ICE raid and has been delayed for at least two months.
- Tami Overby said restoring confidence will involve reassuring investors that the raid was a “one-time mistake.”
- Mr. Kemp posted a brief statement on X welcoming Hyundai’s new commitment to Georgia.
- His statement read: “We look forward to working with them on their continued growth and success for years to come!”
- Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson shared a statement on LinkedIn celebrating Hyundai's continuing historic investments in its state-of-the-art Georgia facility.
- His statement read: “Today is a day to celebrate – Hyundai is continuing its historic investments in its state-of-the-art Georgia facility. From the beginning, Hyundai and our state and local partners have made this partnership a mission to maximize the possibilities for the future.”
