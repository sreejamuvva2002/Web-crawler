# US Battery-Building Announcements (BMW, Envision AESC, Hyundai)

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [US Battery-Building: Updates from BMW, Envision, Hyundai](https://www.batterytechonline.com/automotive-mobility/bmw-envision-hyundai-more-us-battery-factories-coming)  ·  `page_060484`

## Facts

- The US battery-building spree continues as BMW and Hyundai each made major announcements this month.
- BMW Group announced a $700 million investment to build a new high-voltage battery assembly facility in Woodruff, SC.
- The new facility is located close to the company’s Spartanburg automobile manufacturing facility.
- Plant Spartanburg will receive an influx of $1 billion to make it EV production-ready.
- The new Woodruff facility will encompass over 1 million sq. ft.
- The facility will produce next generation batteries for fully electric vehicles.
- Around 300 new jobs will be created on site at the Woodruff facility.
- Plant Spartanburg currently produces lithium-ion battery modules for the BMW X3 xDrive30e and BMW X5 xDrive45e plug-in hybrid electric vehicles.
- The Woodruff facility will leverage the experience and expertise developed in Plant Spartanburg’s existing operations.
- BMW concurrently announced an agreement with battery-maker Envision AESC to build a new battery cell factory in South Carolina to supply Plant Spartanburg.
- Envision will produce newly developed round lithium-ion battery cells specifically designed for the sixth generation of BMW eDrive technology.
- The new cells will be used in next generation electric vehicles.
- The annual capacity of the Envision battery cell factory will be up to 30 GWh.
- The new battery format is said to increase energy density by more than 20%.
- The new battery format is said to improve charging speed by up to 30%.
- The new battery format is said to enhance range by up to 30%.
- CO2 emissions from cell production will be reduced by up to 60% through the partial use of secondary lithium, cobalt, and nickel material.
- CO2 emissions from cell production will also be reduced by up to 60% through the use of renewable energy for production.
- On October 25, Hyundai Motor Group officially broke ground on a new manufacturing plant, ‘Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America’ (HMGMA), in Bryan County, GA.
- The $5.54 billion investment by Hyundai and its affiliate suppliers was announced five months prior to the groundbreaking.
- HMGMA plans include producing a new battery manufacturing facility alongside facilities for making a range of Hyundai, Genesis, and Kia EVs.
- HMGMA will create more than 8,100 jobs over the next few years.
- The company aims to establish a stable supply chain for EV battery and other EV components in the U.S. market through HMGMA.
- HMGMA’s new smart factory is expected to begin commercial production in the first half of 2025.
- Approximately $1 billion is anticipated to be invested by non-affiliated HMGMA suppliers in connection with the project in other locations.
- The new plant will feature a highly connected, automated, and flexible manufacturing system that organically connects all elements of the EV ecosystem.
- All production processes—including order collection, procurement, logistics, and production—will be optimized utilizing AI and data.
- The manufacturing system will help create a human-centered work environment with robots assisting human workers.
- The Georgia facility will primarily rely on renewable energy sources to power operations.
- The plant will use emission-reduction technologies to meet the RE100 requirements.
