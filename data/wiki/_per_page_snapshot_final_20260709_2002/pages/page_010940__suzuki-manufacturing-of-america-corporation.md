# Suzuki Manufacturing of America Corporation

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-05-23

**Source:** [Suzuki Cycles](https://suzukicycles.com/news/2022/05/23/16/32/suzuki-celebrates-20-years-of-atv-manufacturing-in-the-united-states)  ·  `page_010940`

## Facts

- Suzuki Motor USA, LLC is celebrating 20 years of manufacturing KingQuad ATVs at the Suzuki Manufacturing of America Corporation (SMAC) facility.
- KingQuad ATVs first rolled off the assembly line in Rome, Georgia in May 2002 and have continued to be built exclusively in the United States ever since.
- Suzuki has been building the award-winning KingQuad ATVs at the SMAC facility since 2002.
- The SMAC facility is a multi-million dollar, 35-acre manufacturing site located in Rome, Georgia.
- The facility's location in the center of the biggest ATV market in the world allows access to real-world rider feedback for product refinement and timely delivery of KingQuad ATVs to Suzuki dealers worldwide.
- Suzuki founded its engineering commitment in 1909 and led the industry by creating the first ever four-wheel ATV in 1983.
- Kerry Graeber, Vice President and Division Manager MC/ATV Sales and Marketing at Suzuki, stated that building KingQuad ATVs in America for the past 20 years is an extremely important milestone representing the commitment, hard work, and dedication of the Rome, Georgia facility's workforce.
- The SMAC facility is a key component to the continued success of Suzuki’s global ATV business, with KingQuad ATVs shipped to U.S. Suzuki dealers and nearly 30 countries worldwide.
- KingQuad ATVs are built in the USA using both U.S. and imported parts.
