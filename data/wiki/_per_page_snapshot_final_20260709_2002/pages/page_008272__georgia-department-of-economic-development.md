# Georgia Department of Economic Development

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-07-01

**Source:** [GDEcD investment breaks new record](https://valdostatoday.com/news-2/region/2025/09/gdecd-investment-breaks-new-record/)  ·  `page_008272`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) investment broke a new record in Fiscal Year 2025.
- Between July 1, 2024, and June 30, 2025, GDEcD’s Global Commerce team supported 423 facility expansions and new locations.
- These commitments represent more than $26.3 billion in investment, a new record.
- The projects will create 23,200 new private-sector jobs over the next few years.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Georgia has been the No. 1 state for business for over a decade due to its unmatched ability to attract new companies and foster growth for existing employers.
- Expansions at existing Georgia companies accounted for 74% of the FY25 projects.
- These expansions build on an estimated 50,000 jobs already employing Georgians.
- Lt. Governor Burt Jones highlighted investments in workforce programs with partners including the University System of Georgia, the Technical College System of Georgia, and College Career Academies to ensure a steady talent pipeline.
- 77% of all FY25 expansions and new locations occurred in communities outside the 10-county Atlanta region.
- Metro Atlanta remains one of the Southeast’s top business hubs.
- Companies like TriNet, A.I.G., CRH, Duracell, and Mercedes-Benz established or expanded key corporate functions in the 10-county Atlanta region.
- Speaker Jon Burns cited Georgia’s ports at Savannah and Brunswick, extensive rail network, vast energy infrastructure, and world-class roads as key business advantages.
- Georgia attracted more than 6,500 new jobs from international companies expanding or locating in the U.S.
- The Republic of Korea, Japan, and Canada were the top sources of international investments.
- International companies committed to more than $3 billion in investment, with 64% slated for expansions within Georgia.
- Notable international investments include Irving Tissue’s expansion in Macon, GreenBox Systems’ new location in Butts County, and KISS USA’s expansion in Bryan County.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson noted that Georgia’s stable, reliable business climate gives companies confidence to plan for the long term.
- Georgia remains the No. 1 state for business according to Area Development magazine, a title held for over a decade.
- The state is investing over $18 million to help rural communities prepare new industrial sites through the Rural Site Development Initiative.
- These grants support site improvements and add locations to the “Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development” (GRAD) Program.
- The GRAD Program now includes more than 70 certified sites.
- Deputy Commissioner of Global Commerce Misti Martin stated that site-readiness certification and programs like the Rural Site Development Initiative help communities attract new jobs and investment with speed-to-market solutions.
