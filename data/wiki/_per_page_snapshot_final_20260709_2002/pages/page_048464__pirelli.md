# Pirelli

## Facts

- The Italian government stepped in to limit China’s control over Pirelli.
- Pirelli has been given the go-ahead to begin manufacturing its latest series of tires in the United States with help from the Italian government.
- Pirelli’s plant in Rome, Georgia will soon start manufacturing connected tires using Cyber Tyre technology.
- The Cyber Tyre tech includes sensors that can collect data and work in conjunction with a vehicle’s on-board electronics, including the ABS and stability control systems, to enhance driving dynamics and safety.
- For more than a year, there had been concerns that Pirelli would be blocked from manufacturing in the US because the Chinese company Sinochem holds a 34.1 percent stake in the tire giant.
- With the US government clamping down on Chinese technology, it seemed likely Pirelli would have to build these tires elsewhere.
- The Italian government stepped in using its ‘golden powers’ rule established to protect companies that are of national strategic interest.
- Last month, Italy imposed several curbs on Sinochem, noting that none of the eight board members that Sinochem has previously appointed will be allowed to hold top corporate positions like chairman or chief executive.
- Sinochem has been instructed that it cannot exert any influence over Pirelli, and Pirelli does not need to share sensitive information with the Chinese company.
- Pirelli’s plant in Georgia can currently produce up to 400,000 tires annually.
- The company did not disclose how many tires using its Cyber Tyre tech will be added to its production.
- Pirelli North America chief executive Claudio Zanardo said the start of Cyber Tyre production in the Rome, Georgia plant is a significant milestone for Pirelli in this country.
- Zanardo stated the move reflects Pirelli’s commitment to bringing advanced technologies like Cyber Tyre closer to the market, further strengthening its industrial footprint and innovation capabilities in the United States.
