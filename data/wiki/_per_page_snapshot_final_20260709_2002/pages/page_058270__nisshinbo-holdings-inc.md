# Nisshinbo Holdings Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-11-30

**Source:** [Nisshinbo Holdings Inc. - MarkLines Automotive Industry Portal](https://www.marklines.com/en/top500/nisshinbo-holdings?&sitesearchKey=thailand)  ·  `page_058270`

## Facts

- The company is a global manufacturer with a focus on wireless, communications, microdevices, and other electronics businesses, as well as operations in other fields.
- The company's business is divided into seven segments: wireless communications, microdevices, brakes, precision instruments, chemicals, textiles, and real estate.
- Nisshinbo announced it will transfer all outstanding shares of TMD Friction Group S.A., a main pillar of its automotive brake business, along with loan receivables against TMD and its subsidiary TMD Friction Holdings GmbH, to German company AEQUITA SE & Co. KGaA.
- The transaction includes four TMD subsidiaries: TMD Friction Holdings (UK) Limited, TMD Friction UK Limited, TMD Friction do Brasil S.A., and TMD Friction Mexico S.A. de C.V.
- The date of the TMD transaction is scheduled to be November 30, 2023.
- The wireless and communications segment produces communication systems for ADAS, ETC, locators, and GNSS receivers.
- High power transformers, reactors, and choke coils are applied to DC-DC converters for 12V output, electric inverters for compressors, EV battery chargers, electric power steering, on-board chargers, and DC/AC inverters.
- GPS modules are produced as part of the wireless and communications segment.
- The microdevices segment produces electronic devices for car audio, power control units, EPS, BMS, and parking assist systems.
- ICs and operational amps for automobiles are applied to power control units (PCU), electric power steering (EPS), body control modules (BCM), safety/clash avoidance and prevention (ADAS), parking assist systems, entertainment/car navigation/car audio, battery management systems (BMS), LED headlight ECUs, vehicle-to-everything (V2X), TFT LCDs, corner sensor ECUs, and center consoles.
- Power management ICs for automotive applications include LDO regulators, watchdog timers, LED controllers, reset ICs, DC-DC converters, and USB switches.
- The automobile brakes segment produces brake linings, disc pads, drum brakes, and drum/brake linings for medium- and heavy-duty trucks.
- Brake components produced include brake master cylinders, proportioning valves, disc brake calipers, wheel cylinders, and joint brackets.
- Precision instruments division produces molding products such as cross flow fans, Sirocco fans, propeller fans, interior/exterior decorative parts, console parts, seat plastic parts, luggage trim, back door trim, pillar garnish, front grills, wire harnesses, lighting/functional parts, and HV high-voltage cable protectors.
- Precision parts for automobiles include EBS valve blocks, housings and cylinders for diesel fuel-injection management pumps, EGR valves, air control valves, supercharged air control valves, exhaust brake valves, intake-air shutter valves, exhaust shutter valves, actuators, check/delay valves, clutch master cylinders, clutch release cylinders, filler caps, fuel cut valves, hoses with check valves, and control valves for automotive steering systems.
- The chemicals segment produces bipolar plates for fuel cells and fuel cell separators.
- Nisshinbo Automotive Corporation was established in the U.S.A. in February 1995.
- Nisshinbo Automotive Manufacturing Inc., a wholly-owned subsidiary, was established in the U.S.A. in March 1997.
- The company acquired Enjiasheng Electronic Ltd. in China through a joint investment with Continental Teves AG & Co. oHG in March 2004.
- Nisshinbo Brake Inc. and Saeron Automotive Corporation jointly established Nisshinbo Saeron Automotive Changshu Corp. in China in February 2011.
- The company purchased all outstanding stock shares in TMD Friction Group S.A. in November 2011, making it a consolidated subsidiary.
- In April 2009, the company spun off five business sectors (Textiles, Automobile Brakes, Papers, Mechatronics and Chemicals) and transformed into a holding company, changing its corporate name to Nisshinbo Holdings Inc.
- The company is listed on the Prime Market of the Tokyo Stock Exchange as of December 31, 2024.
- Major shareholders include The Master Trust Bank of Japan, Ltd. (14.91%), Fukoku Mutual Life Insurance Co. (5.73%), Custody Bank of Japan, Ltd. (4.52%), STATESTREETBANKANDTRUST COMPANY (3.76%), and Teijin Ltd. (2.01%).
