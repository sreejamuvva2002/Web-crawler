# Joon Georgia, Inc.

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2022-11-07

**Source:** [Press release, 2022 November 7C - Digital Library of Georgia](https://dlg.usg.edu/record/dlg_ggpd_i-ga-bg600-b-pp1-bp7-b2022-s11-h7c-belec-p-btext)  ·  `page_001434`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Joon Georgia, Inc., an automotive parts manufacturer, will create 630 new jobs and invest $317 million in Bulloch County.
- The company's new manufacturing facility plans mark the first confirmed supplier for Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America in Bryan County.
- Joon Georgia is part of Ajin USA, a current supplier for multiple Hyundai Motor Group facilities.
- The company produces a variety of automotive body parts and electronics for original equipment manufacturers.
- Jung Ho Sea, CEO of Ajin USA, stated that Joon Georgia will create approximately 630 new jobs with competitive benefits in Bulloch County and the State of Georgia.
- Joon Georgia's new facility will be located at Bruce Yawn Commerce Park, previously known as the Southern Gateway Commerce Park, near Statesboro.
- The site is in close proximity to the Port of Savannah, allowing both Hyundai and Joon Georgia to access global markets quickly and efficiently.
- Operations at the Bruce Yawn Commerce Park are expected to begin in mid-2024.
- The company will begin hiring for the new facility in mid-2023.
- Billy Allen, Chair of the Development Authority of Bulloch County, noted that education resources including Georgia Southern University and Ogeechee Technical College will provide critical workforce support.
- Alyce Thornhill, Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy, represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development's Global Commerce team on this project in partnership with the Development Authority of Bulloch County, Georgia Quick Start, Georgia Ports Authority, and Georgia EMC.
- Workforce recruitment will be supported by Quick Start, described as the top-ranked workforce training program in the nation.
- Through a partnership with the Technical College System of Georgia, Quick Start will help assess workers, train new employees on unique processes, and develop customized job-specific training.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Hyundai Motor Group, its suppliers, and the Savannah area JDA are creating generational opportunities for communities in Georgia.
- Since 2020, more than 30 EV-related projects have contributed over $13.3 billion in investment and announced more than 18,100 jobs in Georgia.
- On October 25, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America officially broke ground on its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility in neighboring Bryan County.
- Non-affiliated suppliers connected to the Hyundai project are predicted to invest approximately $1 billion in other locations around the state.
