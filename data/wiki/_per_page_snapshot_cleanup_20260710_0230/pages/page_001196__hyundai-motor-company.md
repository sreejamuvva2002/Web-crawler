# Hyundai Motor Company

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 1967-12-29

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Company - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hyundai_Motor_Company)  ·  `page_001196`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Company is a South Korean multinational automotive manufacturer headquartered in Seoul, South Korea.
- The company owns 33.88 percent of Kia Corporation and owns the Genesis luxury car subsidiary, with all three brands making up the Hyundai Motor Group.
- In May 2023, Hyundai established a joint venture with LG Energy Solution to manufacture electric vehicle batteries in the US.
- In October 2024, Hyundai Motor signed a 610-gigawatt-hour (GWh) power purchase agreement (PPA) with SK E&S and other partners for its renewable energy transition efforts.
- In November 2024, Hyundai announced that Global Chief Operating Officer Jose Munoz would become global co-CEO beginning January 1, 2025, a promotion credited to his strategies in pushing record sales in the North America region.
- Hyundai incorporated a new manufacturing facility, Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Alabama, in April 2002.
- The Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Alabama plant is located in Montgomery, Alabama, was completed during 2004 at a cost of $1.7 billion, and started production in May 2005.
- The Montgomery, Alabama plant employed more than 3,000 workers in 2012.
- Hyundai operates research and development centers in South Korea, Germany, the United States (California), Japan, India, and China.
- Hyundai established its Design Center in Fountain Valley, California in 1990, which moved to a new $30 million facility in Irvine, California in 2003.
- The Hyundai America Technical Center, Inc., responsible for all engineering activities in the US for Hyundai, moved to a new 200,000-square-foot, $117 million headquarters in Superior Township, Michigan in 2005.
- In 2004, the Hyundai America Technical Center completed construction of its Hyundai/Kia proving ground in California City, California, a 4,300-acre facility built at a cost of $50 million.
- Hyundai established Supernal, an urban air mobility (UAM) subsidiary, in the United States in 2020.
- On November 8, 2023, Hyundai announced plans to build a plant in the US to produce flying taxis under its Supernal subsidiary.
- As of February 2025, a full-scale technology demonstrator for the Supernal flying taxi was undergoing ground testing.
- The Supernal headquarters is located in Washington, D.C., with an R&D facility in Fremont, California, and engineering headquarters in Irvine, California.
- Hyundai began selling cars in the United States on February 20, 1986, with a single model, the Hyundai Excel.
- In its first year of business in the United States in 1986, Hyundai set a record for selling the most automobiles compared to any other car brand, with total sales of 168,882.
- The company added free maintenance for the first 2 years or 24,000 miles for all its new cars sold in the United States, starting with the 1992 model year.
- Hyundai added a 10-year or 100,000-mile (160,000km) powertrain warranty to its vehicles sold in the United States.
- In 2004, Hyundai tied with Honda for initial brand quality in a J.D. Power and Associates survey for having 102 problems per 1000 vehicles.
- The company placed third overall in J.D. Power's 2006 Initial Quality Survey, behind only Porsche and Lexus.
- In 2009, the Hyundai Genesis luxury sedan was named 2009 North American Car of the Year.
- In January 2012, the Hyundai Elantra was named the North American Car of the Year at the North American International Auto Show, having sold more than 200,000 cars since its redesigned debut.
- As of November 2024, Hyundai is the world's third-largest carmaker in terms of production, behind competitors Toyota and Volkswagen.
- In 2024, Hyundai produced 4,146,335 vehicles.
- The company employs approximately 75,000 people worldwide.
