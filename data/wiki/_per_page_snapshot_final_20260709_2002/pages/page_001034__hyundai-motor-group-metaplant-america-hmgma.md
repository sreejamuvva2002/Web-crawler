# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA)

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-03

**Source:** [Kia Begins Sportage Hybrid Production in Georgia](https://thekoreancarblog.com/kia-begins-production-of-sportage-hybrid-at-hyundai-metaplant-in-georgia/)  ·  `page_001034`

## Facts

- Kia America has officially started production of the Sportage Hybrid (HEV) at Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA).
- The production start marks three major milestones for HMGMA: its first Kia model assembly, its first hybrid electric vehicle assembly, and the third vehicle to join the plant's production line.
- The Sportage Hybrid joins the fully electric Hyundai IONIQ 5 and IONIQ 9 at the Savannah facility.
- HMGMA was designed to integrate diverse powertrains with minimal modifications.
- HMGMA has the structural capacity to build up to 10 different vehicle models across the Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis brands.
- The Metaplant represents Kia’s second major industrial investment in Georgia, complementing its existing assembly plant in West Point.
- With the addition of HMGMA, Kia now commands a total annual production capacity of up to 550,000 vehicles in the United States.
- Sean Yoon, President and CEO of Kia North America and Kia America, stated that the Metaplant in Savannah is Kia's second major investment in Georgia and a testament to confidence in the state’s future as an automotive powerhouse.
- Sean Yoon expressed confidence that Metaplant will continue the strong momentum achieved by the Sportage Hybrid since it was updated last year to quickly become one of Kia's highest volume models.
- To prepare for production, HMGMA personnel underwent extensive training, including travel to Kia’s West Point facility and operations in South Korea to align with global manufacturing standards.
- HMGMA was engineered from day one with a highly flexible manufacturing system that allows seamless integration of different powertrains with minimal modifications.
- The start of local hybrid assembly allows Kia to optimize its logistics and expand the volume of SUVs built directly within the U.S. market.
- Production duties across Georgia are split between two facilities.
- Kia West Point assembles the internal combustion (ICE) versions of the Sportage and Sorento, alongside the Telluride, EV6, and EV9.
- HMGMA in Savannah assembles the Sportage Hybrid (HEV), alongside the Hyundai IONIQ 5 and IONIQ 9.
- During the official launch ceremony, the first production Sportage Hybrid was delivered to the stage via an Autonomous Mobile Robot (AMR), showcasing the factory's advanced automation systems.
- Company officials stated that future product allocation plans for the plant are not being released at this time.
