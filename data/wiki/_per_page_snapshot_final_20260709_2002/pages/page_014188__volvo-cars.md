# Volvo Cars

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-01-07

**Source:** [Volvo Cars reports new global sales record in 2024 - Volvo Car AB (publ)](https://news.cision.com/volvo-car-ab--publ-/r/volvo-cars-reports-new-global-sales-record-in-2024,c4088306)  ·  `page_014188`

## Facts

- Volvo Cars set a new global sales record with full-year 2024 sales of 763,389 cars, an increase of 8 percent compared to 2023.
- December 2024 global sales reached 73,804 cars, a decrease of 3 percent compared to December 2023.
- Full-year 2024 fully electric car sales totaled 175,194 units, an increase of 54 percent compared to 2023.
- Full-year 2024 plug-in hybrid car sales totaled 177,593 units, a 16 percent increase compared to 2023.
- Electrified models accounted for 46 percent of all Volvo cars sold globally during 2024.
- Fully electric cars accounted for 23 percent of global sales in 2024, up from 16 percent in 2023.
- European sales reached 369,685 cars in 2024, a 25 percent increase compared to 2023.
- Electrified models accounted for 65 percent of all cars sold in Europe during 2024.
- US sales for 2024 amounted to 125,243 cars, down 3 percent compared to the full year of 2023.
- Electrified model sales in the US increased by 20 percent and accounted for 34 percent of all cars sold in the US during 2024.
- Sales in China decreased 8 percent compared to 2023, resulting in 156,370 sold cars during 2024.
- Overall sales of electrified models in China grew 3 percent during the January to December 2024 period.
- The Volvo XC60 was the top-selling model globally in 2024 with sales of 230,853 cars.
- The XC40/EX40 was the second top-selling model with total sales of 173,890 cars.
- The XC90 had sales of 108,621 cars in 2024.
- Volvo Cars' production plants are located in Gothenburg, Ghent (Belgium), South Carolina (US), Chengdu, Daqing and Taizhou (China).
- As of December 2023, Volvo Cars employed approximately 43,400 full-time employees.
- Volvo Cars' head office, product development, marketing and administration functions are mainly located in Gothenburg, Sweden.
- Volvo Cars has R&D and design centres in Gothenburg and Shanghai (China).
- Volvo Cars is listed on the Nasdaq Stockholm exchange under the ticker “VOLCAR B”.
- Volvo Cars aims to become a fully electric car maker and achieve net-zero greenhouse gas emissions by 2040.
