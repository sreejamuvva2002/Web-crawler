## Facts

- Georgia has over 50 years of international partnerships to help companies scale up quickly in the United States.
- International companies accounted for one in four new locations or expansions in FY 2025.
- Georgia facilitated more than $210.7 billion in international trade in 2025.
- The Port of Savannah is the fastest growing single-terminal port in the nation.
- The Port of Brunswick is the No 1. Ro/Ro port for automotive and heavy equipment.
- Georgia has connections via inland ports and the most extensive rail system in the Southeastern United States.
- Products can reach 80% of the U.S. market by a two-day drive via highways.
- Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport serves more than 150 U.S. destinations and more than 70 international destinations in 43 countries.
- Georgia consistently ranks as the No. 1 State for Business due to its business-friendly environment and smooth permitting process.
- More than 60 international diplomatic and trade offices are located in Georgia.
- Georgia maintains international representatives in more than a dozen locations covering over 60 markets worldwide.
- The State of Georgia established its first international offices in Japan and Europe more than 50 years ago.
- Georgia offers a skilled workforce pipeline, low cost of living, and robust infrastructure for moving goods globally.
