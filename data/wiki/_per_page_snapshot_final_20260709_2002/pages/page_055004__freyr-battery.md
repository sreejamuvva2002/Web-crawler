# FREYR Battery

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-11-11

**Source:** [$2.57 billion Freyr battery plant will bring 723 jobs to Coweta County](https://thegeorgiasun.com/business-economy/2-57-billion-freyr-battery-plant-will-bring-723-jobs-to-coweta-county/)  ·  `page_055004`

## Facts

- FREYR Battery, a Europe-based producer of next-generation battery cells, will build a manufacturing plant in Coweta County.
- The project represents a $2.57 billion investment that will create 723 jobs during the next seven years.
- Gov. Brian Kemp announced the project on Friday, November 11, 2022.
- Gov. Brian Kemp stated: “Job creators and innovators from all over the world bring their operations to Georgia because they know they will have success here.”
- Gov. Brian Kemp added: “We’re proud to welcome FREYR to the Peach State as the latest company to bring transformational investments and opportunity to our communities.”
- FREYR is dedicated to supporting a domestic supply chain for renewable power sources.
- The company’s battery cells can be used for stationary energy storage, electric mobility, and additional applications.
- Tom Einar Jensen, co-founder and CEO of FREYR, stated: “At FREYR, we are deeply committed to the ambition we share with our U.S. partners to decarbonize the transportation and energy sectors.”
- Tom Einar Jensen noted that as they advance their U.S. expansion plan in cooperation with key stakeholders, they expect to make meaningful investments to spur job creation and the eventual development of localized, decarbonized supply chains in the U.S. to enhance energy security and economic activity.
- The announcement comes as Georgia ramps up to become a leading producer of electric vehicles and EV batteries.
- During the past year, Rivian announced plans for a huge EV manufacturing plant near Covington.
- During the past year, Hyundai announced plans for a huge EV manufacturing plant near Savannah.
- The Rivian and Hyundai projects are described as the two largest economic development projects in Georgia’s history.
- SK Innovation has built two EV battery manufacturing facilities in Northeast Georgia.
- FREYR’s new facility will be called Giga America.
- The planned first phase production of the Giga America facility will be about 34 gigawatt hours.
- The company is currently evaluating clean power supply solutions for the new plant, including the potential use of renewable energy from solar installations.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development’s Global Commerce team worked to land the project in partnership with the Coweta County Development Authority.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development’s Global Commerce team worked to land the project in partnership with the Metro Atlanta Chamber.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development’s Global Commerce team worked to land the project in partnership with Georgia EMC.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development’s Global Commerce team worked to land the project in partnership with the state Department of Labor’s Quick Start program.
