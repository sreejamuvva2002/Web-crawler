# Honda Motor Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2025-12-16

**Source:** [Form 6-K](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/715153/000119312525319940/d67992d6k.htm)  ·  `page_002582`

## Facts

- Honda Motor Co., Ltd. has resolved to acquire an additional 21% equity interest in Astemo, Ltd. from Hitachi, Ltd.
- The acquisition will make Astemo a consolidated subsidiary of Honda.
- Astemo was formed in January 2021 through the management integration of Hitachi Automotive Systems, Ltd., and three Honda subsidiaries: Keihin Corporation, Showa Corporation, and Nissin Kogyo Co., Ltd.
- In October 2023, JIC Capital, Ltd. joined as a new joint partner, establishing a shareholding structure of 40% for Honda, 40% for Hitachi, and 20% for JICC.
- Astemo is leveraging strong software development capabilities and accelerating investment in advanced technology areas to achieve sustainable growth as a global mega supplier.
- The automotive industry is expected to continue an accelerated shift toward software-defined vehicles (SDVs), where vehicle value relies more on software technologies for autonomous driving and over-the-air updates than primarily hardware technologies.
- Honda aims to enhance its SDV development capabilities and cost competitiveness by leading Astemo's transformation toward high-speed, high-efficiency AI and software technology development.
- Post-acquisition, the shareholding structure will change to 61% for Honda, 19% for Hitachi, and 20% for JICC.
- Honda plans to maintain consideration for an IPO of Astemo while leading its growth as a global supplier.
- Astemo's business includes the development, manufacture, sales, and services of automotive components, transportation related components, industrial machines, and systems.
- Astemo is located at Shin-Otemachi Building, 2-1, Otemachi 2-chome, Chiyoda-ku, Tokyo.
- Kohei Takeuchi serves as the Representative Director, President & CEO of Astemo.
- Astemo's stated capital was 129.1 billion JPY as of March 31, 2025.
- Astemo was established on July 1, 2009.
- Prior to the transaction, Honda held 40% voting rights, equivalent to 425,916 shares of Astemo's common stock.
- Astemo's consolidated sales revenue was 1,920,067 million yen for the fiscal year ended March 2023, 2,190,332 million yen for March 2024, and 2,186,537 million yen for March 2025.
- Astemo's consolidated operating profit was 7,320 million yen for the fiscal year ended March 2023, 67,293 million yen for March 2024, and 67,400 million yen for March 2025.
- Hitachi, Ltd. is the counterparty to the share acquisition and develops, produces, sells, and provides services for products related to digital systems & services, energy, mobility, and connective industries.
- Hitachi's stated capital was 464,384 million yen as of March 31, 2025.
- The total acquisition price is 152.3 billion yen, determined through analysis by independent financial advisor Nomura Securities Co., Ltd. and subsequent negotiations.
- Honda will acquire 223,605 shares, representing 223,605 voting rights units.
- Post-acquisition, Honda will hold 61% voting rights, equivalent to 649,521 shares of Astemo.
- The agreement was signed on December 16, 2025.
- The share transfer execution date is scheduled for the first quarter of the fiscal year ending March 31, 2027, subject to competition law procedures.
- Toshihiro Mibe serves as Director, President and Representative Executive Officer of Honda Motor Co., Ltd.
- Koji Ito is the General Manager of the Finance Division at Honda.
- Masao Kawaguchi is the Head of Accounting and Finance Unit at Honda.
