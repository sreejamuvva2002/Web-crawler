# King's Hawaiian

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2025-07-22

## Facts

- King’s Hawaiian has announced its investment of US$54 m to expand its plant in Oakwood, Georgia.
- A new manufacturing line is set to be installed at the 150,000-square-foot Oakwood South Industrial Park facility.
- The project is expected to generate 135 new jobs.
- Hiring will focus on roles in management, maintenance, food safety, and quality control.
- Operations for the new production line are scheduled to begin in the second quarter of 2026.
- Plans include introducing additional flavors of King’s Hawaiian Pretzel Bites.
- King’s Hawaiian was established in 1950 by the Taira family in Hilo, Hawaii.
- The company is now a member of the Irresistible Foods Group (IFG), alongside brands like Grillo’s Pickles.
- Since expanding operations to Georgia in 2010, King’s Hawaiian has reportedly created over 800 jobs statewide.
- The details of the investment were shared last week through an official announcement from Georgia Governor Brian Kemp.
- CEO Mark Taira stated that the expansion represents a major milestone in their journey and that Georgia has been essential to their success for 15 years.
- Governor Brian Kemp noted that King’s Hawaiian has proven to be an incredible partner in creating quality jobs in northeast Georgia over nearly 15 years.
- Governor Brian Kemp added that expansions like these are an important part of economic development work, helping create further opportunities in growing communities.
- King’s Hawaiian’s latest investment reinforces the state’s reputation as a hub for innovation, workforce development, and community-driven growth.
- The expansion allows the company to scale its production and deepen its legacy in the state of Georgia, also known as the Peach State.
