# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-25

**Source:** [Make it 12 in a row for business success - Augusta Business Daily](https://augustabusinessdaily.com/make-it-12-in-a-row-for-business-success/)  ·  `page_017482`

## Facts

- Aurubis and other recent CSRA business expansions have generated an $800 million economic impact in the region.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp, in conjunction with the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD), has announced that the state has been recognized as the No. 1 state for business by Area Development magazine for the 12th year in a row.
- The recognition marks an unmatched record of success and consistency in economic development for the state.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that site selectors who decide where companies invest have voted Georgia as the ‘Top State for Business’ in this annual poll from the leading publication for these decisionmakers for 12 straight years.
- Governor Kemp noted that while the ranking has been earned for over a decade, it is not guaranteed and reflects a sustained commitment to excellence and a team approach to economic development.
- Governor Kemp thanked the GDEcD team, legislative partners, and local leaders and economic developers across the state for making the achievement possible on behalf of hardworking Georgians.
- The 2025 Top States for Doing Business rankings from Area Development are determined by scores in 15 categories based on input from approximately 50 leading site consultants nationwide.
- Lt. Governor Burt Jones stated that the announcement reflects the partnership between Governor Kemp’s Office, the legislature, state agencies, and local leaders and economic developers across the state.
- Lt. Governor Jones expressed gratitude for all of the hardworking Georgians who continue to show up every day and invest in their future and the betterment of all Georgians.
- Lt. Governor Jones highlighted that Georgia's workforce continues to excel and serves as an example of why Georgia is the best state to live, work, and raise a family.
- Georgia ranked in the top five in 10 categories and claimed the overall No. 1 ranking.
- The Top States for Doing Business Survey is the 16th in the annual series.
- Building on this recognition, Georgia continues investing in future opportunities through the Rural Site Development Initiative, which helps local communities prepare development-ready industrial sites.
