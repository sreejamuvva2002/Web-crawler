# Astemo Americas, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2025-06-09

**Source:** [Changes Are Underway at Astemo Americas - A Leading Auto Supplier](https://www.theautochannel.com/news/2025/06/09/1540173-changes-are-underway-astemo-americas-leading-auto-supplier.html)  ·  `page_008469`

## Facts

- Major changes are taking place at Astemo Americas, part of the Astemo Group – one of the world's largest automotive suppliers.
- Astemo Americas is introducing new mobility technology to its North American customers, expanding its footprint in the Americas and making name and branding changes as well.
- The Astemo Group has 80,000 employees worldwide, including more than 40 offices and manufacturing sites with more than 14,000 employees in the Americas.
- Astemo Americas CEO and President Tim Clark states the company aims to strengthen its position as a leader in electrification and mobility intelligence as the industry moves into the software-defined vehicle age.
- The company is accelerating investment in advanced mobility, including electrification, advanced driver-assistance systems, autonomous driving, and advanced chassis technology.
- Astemo has outgrown its technical center and headquarters in Farmington Hills, Michigan, and plans to expand to a new location in the near future.
- Astemo is showcasing next-generation autonomous driving, EV, chassis, suspension, steering, and brake technologies to major OEM customers in the Americas this summer.
- Technologies on display include 3D sensing-and-control, semi-active suspension, steer-by-wire, and brake-by-wire for use on future new-model vehicles sold in the Americas.
- Astemo Americas Vice President for Advanced Engineering Harsha Badarinarayan highlights that new technologies being previewed include advanced AI-based multi-camera systems gaining wide acceptance among automakers for AD/ADAS applications.
- The company has been developing automated driving solutions using proprietary AI-based stereo vision technology that generates a 360° denser point cloud than conventional LiDAR along with color and brightness information.
- A full suspension system with AI-powered control logic using deep-learning algorithms delivers ride-and-handling improvements at a lower cost compared to similar systems today.
- The new suspension system is equipped with a preview camera to prepare for bumps and potholes in the road ahead.
- Additional suspension technologies being showcased include a performance series damper system focused on Off-Road vehicles and a Virtual Development Lab to help customers eliminate physical prototypes.
- Astemo's latest generation of steer-by-wire technology features a hand-held device designed to replace a vehicle's steering wheel.
- The Astemo Smart Steer-by-Wire System's software and hardware provide a pathway to the auto industry's move toward autonomous driving.
- Shown for the first time in North America, Astemo's electro-mechanical brake-by-wire system, Smart Brake, reduces lag-time between driver input and brake response.
- The system's e-pedal eliminates the traditional hydraulic connection between the brake pedal and the vehicle's brakes.
- The new brake system simplifies vehicle assembly, extends battery range, and reduces maintenance by eliminating the use of brake fluid.
- Astemo's Smart Brake is an innovative project supported financially by the French Government as part of the France 2030 investment plan.
- Formerly known as Hitachi Astemo Americas, the Tier One auto supplier's name has officially changed to Astemo Americas following its corporate headquarters' name change from Hitachi Astemo, Ltd. to Astemo Ltd.
- The new branding message Astemo, Mobility Beyond – From the Core of Mobility, Freedom is featured on the company's global website and marketing materials.
- The name Astemo is derived from Advanced Sustainable Technologies for Mobility, underscoring a focus on cutting-edge, sustainable mobility solutions.
- Astemo is headquartered in Tokyo, Japan, and operates as a joint venture between Hitachi, Ltd., Honda Motor Co., and JIC Capital, Ltd.
- The company's Electric Business Division, Vehicle Business Division, and Motorcycle Business Division are engaged in the development, manufacture, sales, and service of automotive parts as well as transportation and industrial machinery and systems.
- Astemo Americas, Inc. manufactures and markets engine management, electric powertrain, and integrated vehicle controls for major automotive manufacturers worldwide.
- The company adheres to ISO/TS:16949:2009 industry standards.
