# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2022-07-18

**Source:** [Georgia Emerges as Hub for Sustainability | Georgia](https://georgia.org/blog/georgia-emerges-hub-sustainability)  ·  `page_003723`

## Facts

- Companies have invested over $11.5 billion in Georgia since 2018 to lower emissions and reduce carbon footprint for themselves and their customers.
- Georgia’s competitive advantages and responsiveness to rapidly evolving business needs have attracted a range of manufacturers, recyclers, and innovators leading the way to a greener future.
- Ascend Elements, formerly known as Battery Resourcers, invested $43 million to open a commercial-scale lithium-ion battery recycling plant in Georgia.
- The Ascend Elements facility will extract critically scarce materials like lithium, cobalt, and nickel from batteries.
- The Ascend Elements facility will become the largest of its kind in North America when fully operational later this year.
- Roger Lin is Ascend Elements’ Vice President of Marketing and Government Relations.
- Roger Lin stated Georgia was selected primarily because of the growing electric vehicle and battery ecosystem in the state.
- Proximity to partners and customers is essential in the sustainable battery materials industry, according to Lin.
- The Southeast is a mecca for the electric vehicle and battery industry, with Georgia close to factories like SK Battery America and others, minimizing transportation costs.
- Ascend Elements benefits from proximity to top talent from nearby universities and access to renewable energy.
- Korea’s SK Battery America is investing nearly $2.6 billion in two facilities near Atlanta.
- SK Battery America will supply batteries to Ford, Volkswagen, and other electric vehicle makers.
- SK Battery America made its first announcement regarding the Georgia investment in 2018.
- Since the company’s first announcement in 2018, more businesses focused on EV sustainability have invested in the state.
- Plug Power recently announced a new $84 million, zero-emission hydrogen fuel production facility near I-95 and Georgia’s deepwater ports.
- Plug Power provides fuel cell systems for e-mobility that deliver efficiency, fast fueling, and low cost to customers.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp launched the Electric Mobility and Innovation Alliance.
- The Electric Mobility and Innovation Alliance is a statewide initiative between government, industries, electric utilities, nonprofits, and other stakeholders focused on growing the electric mobility ecosystem in the state.
- The initiative aims to strengthen Georgia’s position in electrification-related manufacturing and innovation.
- In 2021, EV manufacturer Rivian announced an investment of $5 billion to build a carbon-conscious facility.
- The Rivian facility will make vertically connected electric platforms for trucks, sport/utility vehicles, and delivery vans.
- Hyundai Motor Group will open its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility in the State of Georgia.
- Hyundai announced a $5.54 billion investment with operations slated to begin in the first half of 2025.
- The Hyundai facility will have an annual capacity of 300,000 units.
- Many other Georgia companies are already making parts for EVs.
- Companies from Korea, Germany, Turkey, and the Netherlands have announced plans to build EV-related facilities in the state.
- Site Selection Magazine recently ranked Georgia No. 3 for sustainability in the South Atlantic region.
- The Atlanta metro ranked in the Top 10 nationally for sustainability.
- Since 2009, Georgia has increased net electricity generation from renewable sources by more than 93%.
- Georgia’s electricity mix currently consists of nearly 12% renewables.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that renewable energy capacity installation in Georgia was driven organically by companies and consumers, accelerated through economic attractiveness rather than mandate.
- Smart policies have allowed Georgia to promote renewable power development and offer a comprehensive portfolio of clean energy options that give the state a competitive edge.
- Solar PV is Georgia’s fastest-growing source of renewable energy.
- NanoPV will manufacture solar panels and equipment in south Georgia’s Sumter County.
- Qcells is investing more than $320 million in its solar panel assembly plant in northern Georgia.
- The Qcells plant is the largest in the Western hemisphere.
- German copper recycler Aurubis announced plans to locate in Georgia.
- Electronic waste recycler Igneo announced plans for Georgia.
- Danimer Scientific, a manufacturer and developer of biodegradable materials, announced plans for Georgia.
- Recycled furniture maker SoPoly announced plans for Georgia.
- In Atlanta, EF Block turns everyday recycled Styrofoam into earth-friendly, stronger, sustainable walls that replace traditional building materials.
- With support from GDEcD’s Center of Innovation and the City of Atlanta, EF Block launched a city-wide Styrofoam recycling initiative.
- NANTRenewables is opening a plant on Georgia’s coast to manufacture biodegradable products like plates, straws, cutlery, trays, and cups.
- NANTRenewables provides sustainable products to companies within the food, agriculture, and pharmaceutical industries.
- Harold Meherg is a NANT Renewables senior executive.
- Harold Meherg stated SeaPoint Industrial Terminal Complex in Savannah was selected for their new biogenic fillers and bioplastics manufacturing facility due to shared sustainability values and development-ready infrastructure including roads, rail access, sea access, and nearby utility connections.
- Companies leading the charge in mobility research & development, battery manufacturing and recycling, and electric vehicle production all call Georgia home.
- Ascend Elements’ Lin stated they are pleased to be in a strategic location with the critical mass of factories that are locating or already operating in the area.
- Lin described Georgia as a very friendly, welcoming place to open and grow a business with large facilities like theirs.
