# Hyundai Mobis

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** power_electronics  ·  **Published:** 2022-11-23

**Source:** [Second Global Automotive Supplier for Hyundai Metaplant in Bryan Co. to Create 1,500 Jobs • The Georgia Virtue](https://www.thegeorgiavirtue.com/local-news-south-georgia/second-global-automotive-supplier-for-hyundai-metaplant-in-bryan-co-to-create-1500-jobs/)  ·  `page_021376`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Hyundai Mobis plans to invest $926 million in an Electric Vehicle (EV) Power Electric system plant in Bryan County.
- The new plant will eventually employ at least 1,500 people.
- This announcement marks the second supplier to locate in the Bryan County area in just two weeks.
- The EV Power Electric system plant will expand Hyundai Mobis’ current footprint in Georgia.
- Hyundai Mobis has operated a manufacturing facility with almost 1,200 employees in West Point since 2009.
- The West Point facility is the largest Tier 1 supplier to Kia Georgia.
- The plant supplies automotive manufacturers with complete cockpit modules, chassis modules, as well as bumper assemblies.
- H.S. Oh, Vice President of Hyundai Mobis Electric Powertrain Business Unit, stated the project reflects an acceleration in the development of the EV supply chain in Georgia’s auto industry.
- Once it reaches full production, the 1,200,000-square-foot facility will be able to supply over 900,000 EV Power Electric systems and 450,000 Integrated Charging Control Units annually.
- The annual output will be supplied to the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) factory in Bryan County, the Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Alabama (HMMA) factory in Montgomery, and the Kia Georgia plant.
- Construction on the new facility is located at the Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) Certified Belfast Commerce Park in Richmond Hill.
- Construction is expected to begin as early as January 2023.
- Production is expected to begin in 2024.
- Jon Seagraves, Chairman of the Development Authority of Bryan County, noted the site was purchased about two years ago and highlighted its proximity to available housing in nearby Heartwood, a top-performing public school system, interstate access, and rail.
- The project involves nearly $1 billion of investment and the creation of over 1,500 good-paying jobs.
- Support for the project came from Bryan County, the City of Richmond Hill, and Raydient Places.
- Alyce Thornhill, Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy, represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The project was developed in partnership with the Development Authority of Bryan County, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia Power.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that suppliers like Hyundai Mobis are transitioning to the jobs of the future and becoming part of Georgia’s growing EV ecosystem.
- In May, GDEcD predicted that non-affiliated HMGMA suppliers in connection with the project would invest an additional $1 billion into Georgia.
- That prediction was already exceeded in about two weeks following the announcement.
- Since 2020, more than 30 EV-related projects have contributed over $13.3 billion in investment and announced more than 18,100 jobs in Georgia.
- On October 25, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America officially broke ground in Bryan County on its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility.
- Offsite suppliers connected to the project are predicted to invest approximately $1 billion in other locations around the state.
