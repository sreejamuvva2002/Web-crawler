# Duckyang Ind. Co. Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2021-05

**Source:** [New auto manufacturing facility to arrive in Braselton](https://valdostatoday.com/business-2/2021/05/new-auto-manufacturing-facility-to-arrive-in-braselton/)  ·  `page_001683`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Duckyang will open its first U.S. facility in Jackson County.
- Duckyang is an electric mobility parts supplier of automotive battery modules and energy storage systems to SK Battery America (SKBA).
- The company will invest $10 million in the new manufacturing facility.
- The new facility will create 285 jobs in Braselton, Georgia.
- Governor Kemp stated that Duckyang is creating high-paying, advanced automotive manufacturing jobs for Georgians and praised Georgia's workforce training programs and its status as the number one state for business for eight years.
- Duckyang is headquartered in Ulsan, Korea.
- Duckyang supplies cockpit modules to customers around the world including Hyundai, Kia, Renault Samsung Motors, Genesis, and Mobis.
- Cockpit modules are integrated auto parts comprised of more than 130 subcomponents found near the driver’s seat of a vehicle.
- The Georgia facility will allow Duckyang to better serve several of its automotive customers within the region.
- Dong-in Son, CEO of Duckyang Ind. Co. Ltd., stated that entering the U.S. electric vehicle battery market with SKBA provides opportunities to cooperate with Hyundai and Kia Motors and expand business to supply EV parts, interior parts, cockpit module, door trim panel module, and other flagship parts to global automakers in the U.S.
- The new facility will be 230,000-square-foot.
- The facility address is 984 Broadway Avenue in Braselton.
- Duckyang will be hiring for positions in production, maintenance, logistics, and warehouse management.
- Jennifer Scott, Town Manager and Clerk of Braselton, expressed delight at welcoming a new manufacturing facility to the town.
- John Scott, Director of Economic Development for the Jackson County Area Chamber of Commerce, noted that companies like Duckyang are helping secure Jackson County and Georgia's place in the electric automotive industry's future.
- Yoonie Kim, Director of Korean Investment, represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce division on the project in partnership with the Jackson County Industrial Development Authority, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia EMC.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Georgia is focused on growing the entire electric mobility supply chain and highlighted strong relationships with SK Battery America and Duckyang as examples of bringing suppliers to the state to secure local supply.
- The State of Georgia facilitated more than $1.3 billion in investment and more than 2,700 jobs from South Korean companies during 2020 alone.
- Georgia remains a national leader in advanced manufacturing and continues to attract business globally.
