# Hitachi Astemo, Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2021-10-27

**Source:** [News Releases : October 27, 2021 : Hitachi Astemo, Ltd.](https://www.hitachi-automotive.us/about/press/10-27-2021.htm)  ·  `page_008504`

## Facts

- On October 27, 2021, Hitachi Astemo announced that robotics, artificial intelligence, and data-driven technologies are driving sweeping changes in global manufacturing plants.
- Sudhanshu Gaur, Hitachi America vice president and chief architect – Smart Manufacturing at Hitachi Astemo, stated that the global pandemic, changes in work habits, and recent supply chain problems are accelerating the shift to data-driven production processes supported by machine learning, artificial intelligence, time-sensitive networking, and collaborative robotics.
- Gaur noted that manufacturers have a great opportunity to embrace these technological changes to unlock business value while increasing operational flexibility and resiliency.
- Gaur is responsible for the digital transformation of Hitachi Astemo’s global manufacturing operations to improve productivity and maintain leadership in an Industry 4.0 vision, and was recently named one of the top 25 leaders in manufacturing transformation by the Society of Manufacturing Engineers.
- Speaking as a keynote speaker at The Assembly Show in Rosemont, Illinois, Gaur predicted that Hitachi Astemo’s nearly 140 global manufacturing plants would benefit from its Global IoT platform and services within the next four years.
- During his Oct. 27 keynote on “Smart Manufacturing – Today and Tomorrow,” Gaur outlined a vision for digital platforms connecting production, engineering, quality, inventory, and supply-chain management.
- He explained that digital platforms introduce flexibility into production processes and help companies manage disruptions caused by volatility, uncertainty, complexity, and ambiguity (VUCA).
- Gaur warned that while fully automated plants are more efficient, tightly linked systems pose risks because they are not designed to handle unpredictability, complex situations, or rare events.
- He advocated for a gradual adoption of advanced digital technologies to bring necessary automation while upskilling the workforce to manage complex systems.
- Gaur estimated that implementing a data-driven system at a medium-size U.S. manufacturing plant with 1,000 employees and $200 million in annual sales would incur approximately $20,000 in one-time implementation charges and $60,000 or more in annual running costs.
- He estimated that a large global enterprise with about 100 plants, 60,000 employees, and $10 billion in annual sales could face one million dollars or more in one-time implementation charges and over three million dollars in annual running costs for a fully digital platform.
- Successful smart manufacturing implementation will involve layers of the Industrial Internet of Things (IIoT), including fog computing and edge intelligence, as well as 5G and cloud computing.
- Hitachi Astemo was formed by the merger of Hitachi Automotive Systems with Honda’s Keihin, Showa, and Nissin groups, with Hitachi holding a 66.6% share of voting rights and Honda holding 33.4%.
- The newly formed company employs 90,000 people globally and holds leadership positions in AD/ADAS, software, powertrain systems, chassis systems, and motorcycle systems.
- Hitachi Astemo Americas, Inc. manufactures and markets engine management, electric powertrain, and integrated vehicle controls for major automotive manufacturers worldwide, adhering to ISO/TS:16949:2009 industry standards.
- Hiromi Mizuno serves as the corporate contact for Hitachi Astemo Americas, Inc., and Elaine Cozzetto of AutoCom Associates serves as the media contact.
