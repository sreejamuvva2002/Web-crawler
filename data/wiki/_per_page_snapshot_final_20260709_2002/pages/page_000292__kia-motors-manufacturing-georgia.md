# Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-06-06

**Source:** [Kia creates first-ever Georgia electric vehicle at West Point plant | Columbus Ledger-Enquirer](https://www.ledger-enquirer.com/news/business/article289058894.html)  ·  `page_000292`

## Facts

- Kia at its West Point, Georgia plant rolled out the first-ever electric vehicle manufactured in the state of Georgia, an EV9 SUV.
- The rollout occurred just 10 months after Kia invested $200 million to expand operations at its West Point, Georgia plant.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp drove the newly assembled EV9 off the production line and congratulated the Kia Georgia team for boosting the state’s EV portfolio, strengthening the battery belt, and advancing the climate agenda.
- The fossil-fuel, emission-free vehicle went on sale at the end of 2023.
- Prior to Georgia production, the vehicles were shipped from South Korea and sold in all 50 states.
- The EV9 has received industry accolades including World Car of the Year, World EV of the Year, and North American Utility Vehicle of the Year.
- At the ceremony, U.S. Senator Jon Ossoff congratulated the Kia Georgia team on the “huge milestone.”
- Ossoff stated that Georgians are proud of these beautiful cars, produced in Georgia and made in America.
- Manufacturing the vehicle on American soil makes it at least partially eligible for a $7,500 EV tax credit under the Inflation Reduction Act.
- The EV tax credit requires the vehicle to be made in America and meets specific battery component requirements.
- Anne Blair, VP of the Electrification Coalition, noted that fifty percent of an EV's mineral components must now be sourced and processed in the U.S. or a trade partner country.
- Kia has not confirmed whether the Georgia-assembled EV9 will qualify for the full tax credit.
- Kia Georgia spokesman Patrick Sands stated the company expects the Georgia-assembled EV9 to qualify for available incentives in the near future and will provide additional details at a later date.
- According to Sands, the EV9 batteries take eight hours to charge on a 220v level 2 circuit.
- The EV9 battery comes with a 10-year/100,00- mile warranty, while federal regulation mandates a minimum of an eight-year warranty.
- The MSRP for the EV9 ranges between $55,000 and $70,000, accounting for incentives and trim level.
- Kia Georgia spokesman Patrick Sands did not address whether Georgians will receive a hometown discount on the vehicle.
- The plant's expansion last July added 200 jobs to support EV production alongside the current Telluride, Sorento, and Sportage lineup.
- The West Point plant is listed as the fifth largest employer on the ‘Choose Columbus’ website.
- Approximately 86 people in Columbus work for Kia Georgia at the West Point plant, according to LinkedIn.
