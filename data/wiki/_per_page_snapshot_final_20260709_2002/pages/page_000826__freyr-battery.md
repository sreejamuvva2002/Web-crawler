# FREYR Battery

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Battery Manufacturer to invest $2.57 Billion in Coweta County – Winters Media](https://www.wintersmedia.net/battery-manufacturer-to-invest-2-57-billion-in-coweta-county/)  ·  `page_000826`

## Facts

- FREYR Battery, a developer of clean, next-generation battery cell production capacity, announced the selection and purchase of a site in Coweta County, Georgia for its planned Giga America battery plant.
- The company plans to create 723 new jobs and invest more than $2.57 billion in Coweta County through 2029.
- The new flagship manufacturing plant will be located at the Bridgeport Industrial Park near Moreland.
- FREYR is a company based in Luxembourg committed to assisting the local supply chain for renewable energy sources.
- The company’s battery cells have a variety of uses, including stationary energy storage (ESS) and electric mobility.
- To allow for an expedited start to construction, FREYR is creating a strategy to carry out the project in a phased approach.
- The new project execution plan takes into account the soaring incremental customer demand for clean, next-generation batteries and the recently passed U.S. Inflation Reduction Act.
- FREYR’s Co-Founder and Chief Executive Officer Tom Einar Jensen commented that expanding into the U.S. has been a foundational aspect of the company's long-term strategy from inception.
- The project is expected to be developed in multiple phases beginning with an initial battery cell production module of approximately 34 GWh.
- The entire investment in Coweta County could be well over $2.57 billion, including planned, value-enhancing upstream and downstream modules.
- The SemiSolidTM technology of FREYR’s American licensing partner, 24M Technologies, will serve as the foundation for the project.
- Lithium-ion battery manufacture can be done at scale with a high level of capital efficiency thanks to the simplified SemiSolidTM platform.
- With local utility providers involvement, FREYR is currently reviewing clean power supply options for the project.
- The long-term goal of the company is to adopt the same approach used at Giga Arctic to create some of the cleanest next-generation batteries.
- Coweta County Board of Commissioners Chairman Bob Blackburn welcomed FREYR Battery to the community, noting it is quickly becoming a premier destination for top-tier manufacturing industries that support the U.S. economy.
- The site will be in the Bridgeport Industrial Park, where a mix of heavy and light industrial has been permitted since 2014.
- FREYR chose Coweta County after an extensive and competitive site selection process, citing the county’s workforce, location, and business-friendly climate.
- Coweta County has a strong manufacturing workforce with the ability to attract talent from universities and technical colleges.
- The robust transportation infrastructure of highway and rail provides easy access to Atlanta and the Port of Savannah.
- Coweta County Development Authority Chairman John Daviston stated that collaboration was integral to the success of the project and credited teamwork among local and statewide stakeholders.
- FREYR Battery is looking to fill openings for administrative, technical, and managerial positions.
- FREYR aims to provide industrial scale clean battery solutions to reduce global emissions and is listed on the New York Stock Exchange.
- FREYR has commenced building the first of its planned factories in Mo i Rana, Norway.
- The company announced potential development of industrial scale battery cell production in Vaasa, Finland, and the United States.
- FREYR intends to install 50 GWh of battery cell capacity by 2025, 100 GWh annual capacity by 2028, and 200 GWh of annual capacity by 2030.
