# Harris County

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2022-04-30

**Source:** [Harris County: Driven to Success - Georgia Trend Magazine](https://www.georgiatrend.com/2022/04/30/harris-county-hamilton-driven-to-success/)  ·  `page_019023`

## Facts

- KIA’s sole U.S.-based manufacturing plant is in Troup County, and Harris County is reaping rewards as secondary and tertiary suppliers to the automaker fill up the more than 450 acres at the county-owned Northwest Harris Business Park.
- Companies operating in Northwest Harris Business Park include Adient (maker of automotive seat systems and door panels), Daehan Solution Georgia (automotive noise reduction systems producer), Daesol Material Georgia (manufacturer of automotive fiber padding), Kopla (specializing in automotive plastic injection molding), and Glovis Georgia (automotive parts consolidation and inventory management company).
- Collectively, the companies currently operating in Northwest Harris Business Park employ about 800 to 1,000 workers.
- The county will use a $500,000 One Georgia grant as part of a larger plan to fund the construction of a new road, grading of the new site and additional utilities at the business park.
- The overall plan will prepare another 35-acre site for a future tenant and have another 60 acres in reserve in the next phase.
- Harris County has collected about $1 million in TSPLOST (Transportation Special Purpose Local Options Sales Tax) funds each year since voters in the 16-county River Valley region approved the referendum for a 1% tax in 2013.
- TSPLOST funds have been used to resurface county roads, repair county bridges, construct portions of the Man O’ War Railroad Recreation Trail, install turn lanes into Ellerslie Park and widen U.S. Highway 27.
- The initial TSPLOST referendum lasts through the end of 2022, and voters in May will be asked once again to approve a 10-year TSPLOST.
- If approved, Harris County is expected to receive about $14 million over the 10-year collection period.
- If voters approve the TSPLOST referendum, another $5 million allotment will enable the 16-county River Valley Regional Commission to continue the trail’s development.
- The Mulberry Grove master-planned community at the southwestern tip of Harris County is zoned for 2,198 residential units and more than 2.4 million in commercial square footage, including retail, office, highway commercial and industrial.
- Stone Martin Builders started constructing houses in Mulberry Grove in 2021.
- Construction began last year on Greystone at Mulberry Grove, a luxury multifamily community consisting of 223 units, developed by Greystone Properties, LLC in partnership with Woodruff Real Estate Services, LLC.
- Hughston Homes has constructed about 300 homes at the south end of Harris County over the past four years and will put up another 75 over the next 12 months.
- Hughston Homes reports an average home price point of just over $400,000 and notes a shift in buyers to include out-of-state residents and move-up buyers from Columbus.
- The Harris County Chamber of Commerce had a record year for chamber memberships last year, with about 35 new businesses coming on board.
- Mercer’s School of Medicine cut the ribbon on a new clinic in Harris County this spring that employs a doctor and a nurse practitioner and offers basic health care services, telehealth and x-ray and lab services.
