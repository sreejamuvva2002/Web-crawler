# Continental Tire the Americas, LLC

## Facts

- CTA determined that certain Continental VanContact A/S tires do not fully comply with paragraph S5.5(b) of FMVSS No. 139.
- CTA filed a noncompliance report dated September 10, 2021, pursuant to 49 CFR part 573.
- CTA petitioned NHTSA on September 30, 2021, for an exemption from notification and remedy requirements on the basis that the noncompliance is inconsequential to motor vehicle safety.
- Notice of receipt of CTA's petition was published with a 30-day public comment period on April 18, 2024; no comments were received.
- Approximately 419 Continental VanContact A/S tires are potentially involved in the noncompliance.
- The subject tires are size LT245/70R17 119/116 Q, manufactured between May 31, 2020, and May 22, 2021.
- The noncompliance consists of the tire size designation on both sidewalls missing the prefix “LT”.
- Sidewalls incorrectly read “245/70R17 119/116 Q” instead of the required “LT245/70R17 119/116 Q.”
- CTA states that aside from the missing marking, the tires contain all required markings and meet FMVSS 139 performance requirements for LT 245/70R17 119/116 Q, Load Range E tires.
- CTA contends the missing markings do not impact operational safety because correct load index and load capacities for single and dual fitments are marked.
- No higher load tire is specified for size 245/70R17 in the TRA yearbook or ETRTO, meaning the tires could not be mistakenly placed in an overloaded application.
- CTA cites a similar noncompliance petition granted to Michelin North America Incorporated (78 FR 30963, May 23, 2013) as supporting precedent.
- The subject tires were installed on pre-serial production vehicles that were not sold to end consumers or released into the replacement tire market.
- All remaining CTA inventory will be scrapped, and no additional noncompliant tires will be manufactured or sold.
- In response to NHTSA email correspondence on November 7, 2022, CTA clarified that Rivian owned, controlled, and operated the subject vehicles.
- The tires were sent to Toyota Arizona Proving Grounds for prototype vehicle testing.
- NHTSA granted the petition, exempting CTA from notification and remedy obligations under 49 U.S.C. 30118 and 30120.
- The exemption only applies to subject tires CTA no longer controlled at the time of the noncompliance determination.
- The decision does not relieve tire distributors and dealers of prohibitions on selling or introducing noncompliant tires into interstate commerce after being notified.
- Otto G. Matheke, III serves as Director of the Office of Vehicle Safety Compliance at NHTSA.
