# Morgan Stanley

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2023-07-27

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp Applauds Morgan Stanley’s Commitment to Georgia Job Creation | Governor Brian P. Kemp Office of the Governor](https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases/2023-07-27/gov-kemp-applauds-morgan-stanleys-commitment-georgia-job-creation)  ·  `page_028842`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp applauded Morgan Stanley’s continued progress in growing its workforce across several locations in the metro Atlanta area.
- The company confirmed it is close to reaching its goal set in 2020 of adding 1,800 Georgians to its workforce.
- Many of these new positions are located at Morgan Stanley's Alpharetta campus.
- More than 4,000 Georgians currently work across the firm’s offices in metro Atlanta and statewide in a variety of roles and functions.
- Roles include Technology, Research, Human Resources and Operations and support roles for Morgan Stanley at Work and E*TRADE from Morgan Stanley.
- Governor Kemp stated Georgia is the No. 1 state for business for nine years running and continues to attract companies from diverse industries that bring opportunity to communities all across the state.
- Kemp noted Morgan Stanley continues to grow its presence in Georgia because the state's workforce and partnership approach lead to success, ensuring Georgia remains at the cutting edge of the financial services industry.
- Morgan Stanley is a leading global financial services firm providing investment banking, securities, wealth management, and investment management services.
- The company has offices in more than 42 countries, with employees serving clients worldwide including corporations, governments, institutions, and individuals.
- The company’s presence in Georgia was bolstered by its acquisition of E*TRADE Financial Corporation in 2020.
- Ekene Ezulike, Global Head of Corporate & Enterprise Services at Morgan Stanley, stated Alpharetta expansion will play a key role in growing and broading their talent base to support operations.
- Ezulike appreciated the support of the State of Georgia and looked forward to deepening commitment to the Atlanta Metropolitan area.
- Katie Kirkpatrick, President and CEO of the Metro Atlanta Chamber, welcomed Morgan Stanley's growth in metro Atlanta, citing the strength it adds to both the fintech and financial services community.
- Kirkpatrick committed to working with partners to ensure success and seamless integration into the region while continuing to build the top business ecosystem in the country.
- Alpharetta Mayor Jim Gilvin stated Alpharetta was selected for Morgan Stanley's growth due to shared core values including 'do the right thing', and looked forward to strong community connections.
- Robb Pitts, Chairman of the Fulton County Board of Commissioners, called Fulton County a leading destination for the nation’s best talent and noted the announcement speaks to community quality and workforce strength.
- Director of Statewide Projects Barton Lowrey and Senior Project Manager John Soper represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce division on this project with the City of Alpharetta, Select Fulton, Metro Atlanta Chamber, the University System of Georgia, and Georgia Power.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated it is gratifying when a company heavily invested in the state doubles down and chooses to grow again.
- Wilson noted Morgan Stanley’s decision to significantly expand their campus and add well-paying jobs to the fintech ecosystem testifies to diverse talent from education systems, and exemplifies the state’s strength in attracting and retaining talent due to high quality of life.
