# Ateios Systems

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2026-03-23

**Source:** [Ateios Systems Closes $7.25M Series A to Scale Next-Generation Battery Electrode Manufacturing - Athens Banner-Herald](https://www.onlineathens.com/press-release/story/48147/ateios-systems-closes-7-25m-series-a-to-scale-next-generation-battery-electrode-manufacturing/)  ·  `page_000771`

## Facts

- Ateios Systems has closed $7.25 million in Series A funding to scale production of its next-generation lithium-ion battery electrodes and expand supply to global battery manufacturers.
- The funding round was led by TitletownTech, with participation from existing investors Elevate Ventures, VisionTech Partners, Good Growth Capital, Lateral Capital, Techstars, and Keshif Ventures, as well as new investors JHH Ventures, Stonecast Ventures, Zaka Ventures, Carat Venture Partners, and E8 Angels.
- Ateios’ high-speed electrode manufacturing lines are powered by the company’s solvent-free RaiCure™ electron-curing platform.
- The technology enables battery manufacturers to modify existing assembly lines to qualify RaiCore™ electrodes toward gigawatt-hour production.
- The new capital will support the expansion of Ateios’ commercial engagements with battery and advanced materials partners, including ongoing work with companies such as Eastman Kodak and Vianode.
- The funding will enable Ateios to increase production capacity and further advance its manufacturing capabilities as it moves toward supplying battery manufacturers at larger commercial scale.
- Dr. Rajan Kumar is the Founder and CEO of Ateios Systems.
- In September, Ateios and Eastman Kodak demonstrated a top speed for a solvent-free electrode production process.
- The system achieved coating speeds of 80 meters per minute, nearly three times faster than the industry standard.
- The process produces PFAS-free, solvent-free electrodes compatible with lithium-ion battery chemistries, including LCO, LFP, NMC, and synthetic graphite.
- Craig Dickman is a Managing Partner at TitletownTech and an Ateios board member.
- Ateios Systems was founded by Dr. Rajan Kumar, whose background in nanoscale science and nanoengineering led to the development of printable energy composites.
- The company advanced its technology through the Department of Energy’s Innovation Crossroads program at Oak Ridge National Laboratory and incubation at the Battery Innovation Center.
- Ateios earned industry recognition including Battery Manufacturer of the Year at The Battery Show North America in 2025.
- The company recently showcased production-grade RaiCore™ electrodes, including third-party verification of PFAS-free battery materials, alongside its manufacturing partners at the 43rd International Battery Seminar & Exhibit in Orlando.
