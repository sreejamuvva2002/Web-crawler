# NVH Korea

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-06-23

**Source:** [South Korean firm plans $72M Georgia plant to make battery parts, hiring 140 – WSB-TV Channel 2 - Atlanta](https://www.wsbtv.com/news/local/henry-county/south-korean-firm-plans-72m-georgia-plant-make-battery-parts-hiring-140/D4NXHCL7IRBBDHV7HQILLLE3XY/)  ·  `page_047712`

## Facts

- A South Korean company will open a factory in suburban Atlanta to make parts for electric batteries.
- NVH Korea plans to invest $72 million in a plant located in Locust Grove, south of Atlanta.
- The new plant will hire 140 workers.
- The facility will manufacture sensors, connections, and protectors for electric vehicle batteries.
- Operations at the new plant are scheduled to begin in 2024.
- Chairman and CEO Ja Kyum Koo stated that this marks NVH Korea’s first venture into manufacturing components for electric batteries.
- Based in Ulsan, South Korea, NVH Korea is the 10th supplier to locate in the region.
- Hyundai announced in 2022 that it would build a $5.5 billion plant to assemble electric vehicles and batteries in Ellabell, Georgia, near Savannah.
- The Hyundai plant site could eventually grow to employ 8,100 workers.
- The Hyundai plant is slated to begin producing vehicles in 2025.
- Suppliers for the region's EV projects have pledged to invest nearly $2.1 billion.
- Suppliers for the region's EV projects have pledged to hire nearly 4,900 people.
- This facility will be NVH Korea’s second plant in Georgia.
- NVH Korea’s subsidiary, AFS America, is located in Columbus and manufactures floor mats and cargo trays for the Hyundai plant in Montgomery, Alabama, the Kia plant in West Point, Georgia, and General Motors.
- NVH Korea operates 19 plants worldwide across South Korea, North America, China, India, Europe, and Russia.
- NVH Korea manufactures sound-dampening parts and materials, floor mats, headliners, insulators, and components for managing engine and cooling system temperatures.
- The state of Georgia will fund the training of NVH Korea’s workers at the new facility.
- The company could qualify for $1.2 million in state income tax credits, calculated at $1,750 per job over five years, provided workers earn at least $31,300 annually.
- The company could qualify for additional incentives, including property tax breaks from the city of Locust Grove and Henry County.
