# GF Casting Solutions AG

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2024-05-03

**Source:** [Georgia Lands 350 New Jobs in E-mobility Manufacturing | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-releases/2024/georgia-lands-350-new-jobs-e-mobility-manufacturing)  ·  `page_002431`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced on May 3, 2024 that GF Casting Solutions AG will invest more than $184 million in a new manufacturing facility in Augusta.
- GF Casting Solutions AG is a division of Georg Fischer AG, located in Schaffhausen, Switzerland.
- The company produces lightweight components for the mobility and energy industries.
- The project will create 350 new jobs for Richmond County.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated Georgia is proud to carry the title of the No. 1 state for business to companies across the globe.
- GF Casting Solutions develops and produces parts made from aluminum, magnesium, iron, and super alloy for light vehicles, trucks, aerospace, energy, off-highway vehicles, and industrial applications.
- Carlos Vasto, President of GF Casting Solutions, stated building a High Pressure Die Casting Facility is a very long-term investment to complete their footprint in the global e-mobility market.
- The new facility will be state-of-the-art and LEED-certified at the Augusta Corporate Park, a Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) site.
- The facility will produce cast aluminum parts with a special focus on large structural components for the automotive industry.
- Operations are expected to begin in 2027.
- GF Casting Solutions will hire for roles in manufacturing and automation over the next few years.
- Jobs will be posted as they become available.
- Augusta Mayor Garnett Johnson stated this is the second large European investment in the Augusta Corporate Park and highlighted city investments in water, sewer, roads, and workforce growth.
- Development Authority of Augusta Chairman Wayne Gossage thanked the Augusta Economic Development Authority for bringing in industries with new jobs and ensuring existing industries have resources to grow.
- Assistant Director Virginia Sengewald and Project Manager Caroline Knowles represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on the project.
- The project was completed in partnership with the Augusta Economic Development Authority, Georgia Power, State of Georgia Europe Office, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated European companies demonstrate a wealth of business experience combined with an intentional focus on innovation driving the market forward.
- Since 2018, Georgia has attracted more than 32,200 new jobs and over $27.3 billion in investments in the e-mobility, clean energy, and battery-related supply chains.
