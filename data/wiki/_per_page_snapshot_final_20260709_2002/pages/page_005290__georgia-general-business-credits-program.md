# Georgia General Business Credits Program

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [General Business Credits - FAQ | Department of Revenue](https://dor.georgia.gov/general-business-credits-faq)  ·  `page_005290`

## Facts

- Benefits of tax credits earned by S-corporations or partnerships are usually passed through to the shareholders or partners.
- The law limits the amount of job tax credit that may be claimed against withholding by excluding Port Traffic Credit and Joint Development Authority credit amounts from being applied to withholding.
- If a company claims Port Traffic or Joint Development Authority credits in addition to the Job Tax withholding credit, those specific amounts may only be applied against income tax liability.
- For flow-through entities making an irrevocable election to claim the withholding credit, amounts that cannot be applied to withholding are allowed to flow through.
- A taxpayer may apply the 50% limitation of one credit and the 50% limitation of another credit to offset up to 100% of income tax liability, though some credits like research have requirements that may prevent a full 100% offset.
- Investment tax credits require an application process that must be approved before the credit can be claimed on a tax return.
- The regulation requires the investment tax credit application to be filed within 45 days of project completion, which for most taxpayers means within 45 days of the close of the tax year in which the project was completed.
- Applicants should allow a minimum of 60 days for the investment tax credit application to be processed.
- Applications filed after the 45-day period must petition the Commissioner for approval to file late.
- An investment tax credit application must be signed and dated by a corporate officer, include a descriptive project narrative, and contain a spreadsheet detailing assets to be claimed with descriptions, manufacturing process explanations, dates placed into service, and purchase prices.
- If a consultant files an investment tax credit application on behalf of a taxpayer, a properly executed Power of Attorney is required; otherwise, any questions or correspondence will go directly to the taxpayer at the address or phone number provided on the application.
- Only real and personal property qualify for the investment tax credit.
- The Port Traffic Credit is claimed on the form for the tax credit it accompanies or replaces, requiring all requirements of the original credit to be met first.
- Claiming the Port Traffic Credit requires attaching a statement to the Georgia return reflecting base year and prior year traffic to substantiate the increase; failure to provide this disallows the credit.
- For job tax credit purposes, the port traffic increase must be established in the year of eligibility with sustained increases shown for subsequent years.
- Qualifying for the Georgia Low Income Housing Credit requires the qualified Georgia project to be eligible for the federal housing credit under Section 42 of the IRC of 1986.
- Claiming the Georgia Low Income Housing Credit on a tax return requires submitting a partnership K-1 (if claiming as a flow-through), the associated Federal Form 8609 tax credit certificate, and a schedule with building-by-building allocation for each claimed property.
