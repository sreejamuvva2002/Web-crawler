## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that SungEel Recycling Park Georgia, LLC., will locate its first U.S. recycling facility in Georgia.
- SungEel Recycling Park Georgia, LLC is a subsidiary of the Korea-based global industry leader SungEel HiTech Co., Ltd.
- The company will invest more than $37 million in the new facility.
- The project will create 104 jobs in Stephens County.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that companies like SungEel are evidence that the rapidly growing electric mobility ecosystem continues to generate new jobs for hardworking Georgians across the state.
- Governor Brian Kemp noted that Korea has been a key partner in Georgia’s growing sustainable technology industry.
- SungEel has more than 10 years of patented technology.
- SungEel specializes in complete recycling technology using a 100 percent full circulatory system.
- The recycling system recycles end-of-life batteries and battery manufacturing scrap.
- The system has a metal recovery rate of more than 95 percent.
- Recovered metals include nickel, cobalt, and lithium.
- Suk Jae Yim is the Representative of SungEel Recycling Park Georgia.
- Suk Jae Yim stated that SungEel HiTech’s entry into Georgia is the last piece of the puzzle to build a sustainable ecosystem of Georgia’s electric vehicle supply chain.
- SungEel Recycling Park Georgia will conduct its full responsibility to build a U.S. eco-friendly industrial ecosystem in line with the expectations of the State of Georgia and Stephens County.
- The new facility will be located at the Hayestone Brady Business Park, a Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) Certified site, in Toccoa.
- The GRAD Program is a proactive effort by Georgia’s economic development community to develop a portfolio of available sites ready for fast-track development.
- Operations are expected to begin in early 2024.
- Over the next few years, the company will be hiring for technicians, operators, administrative, and sales roles.
- Brian Akin is Chairman of the Stephens County Development Authority.
- Brian Akin stated that investments made in infrastructure, workforce development, and education were significant factors in the selection process.
- Yoonie Kim is Director of Korean Investment at the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD).
- Yoonie Kim represented GDEcD on this competitive project in partnership with the Stephens County Development Authority and Georgia EMC.
- Pat Wilson is GDEcD Commissioner.
- Pat Wilson stated that SungEel is a company at the cutting edge of sustainable technology.
- Pat Wilson believes SungEel’s proximity to other members of Georgia’s electric mobility ecosystem will lead to amazing success.
- Pat Wilson stated that creating the jobs of the future and protecting opportunities means preparing key industries, such as the auto industry, for the next technological revolution.
- GDEcD expressed gratitude to partners in Stephens County for their investment in speed to market solutions like site preparation.
- Georgia’s prime location, extensive infrastructure, skilled workforce, and business-friendly climate have made it an attractive location for a diverse array of rapidly developing industries focused on creating a sustainable future.
- Georgia is cultivating a vertically integrated supply chain that will help companies increase efficiencies by reducing reliance on imported materials.
- EV-related projects have contributed more than $13 billion in investments and more than 18,100 new jobs to Georgia since 2020.
