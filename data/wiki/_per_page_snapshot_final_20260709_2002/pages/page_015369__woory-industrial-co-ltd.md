# Woory Industrial Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp: Woory to Open EV Parts Manufacturing, R&D Facility in Laurens County - EIN Presswire](https://www.einnews.com/pr_news/638187597/gov-kemp-woory-to-open-ev-parts-manufacturing-r-d-facility-in-laurens-county)  ·  `page_015369`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Woory Industrial Co., Ltd. will establish a new manufacturing facility in Dublin, Georgia.
- The facility will create more than 130 new jobs and an estimated $18 million in investment in Dublin-Laurens County.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated the company is helping Georgia become the 'e-mobility capital of the nation' and highlighted historic levels of new jobs and investment brought by the emerging EV industry.
- Woory Industrial Company was established in 1989 and specializes in heating, ventilation, and air conditioning components and systems for the global automotive industry.
- The company is concentrating on the development of new products for electric vehicles (EV) and hydrogen-fueled cars.
- Jungwoo Kim, CEO of Woory Industrial Company, stated the U.S. EV market is the key market among all automotive markets and that Georgia will be a good opportunity for Woory to take another step forward as part of the vanguard of EV development.
- Woory's new facility is located at 404 Kellam Road in Dublin and will be the company’s first U.S. manufacturing location.
- The facility will produce electrical heaters, control units, and actuators for EV and internal combustion carmakers in North America.
- Operations are expected to begin in November 2023.
- Unlike internal combustion engine vehicles, electric vehicles do not have a heating source, so heaters are required for cabin heating and maintaining battery temperature.
- Woory's pioneering development of electric heaters over the last 10 years has made it a leader in this growing market segment.
- Woory will be hiring personnel from all fields necessary for an automotive parts manufacturing plant.
- Dublin Mayor Joshua Kight stated that Woory’s commitment to place an existing manufacturing building back into service helps promote infill development and creates new career paths for the community.
- Dr. Fred Williams, Chair of the Board of Directors of the Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority, noted that Dublin and Laurens County have a rapidly growing automotive sector due to their geographic location on Interstate 16 and access to a skilled workforce.
- The partnership with area education systems and training providers will ensure training programs are in place to fill Woory's talent pipeline for years to come.
- Project Manager Jacob Lee represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this project in partnership with the Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Georgia has invested for decades to match competitive projects with communities and sites prepared for development, and congratulated Dublin-Laurens County on bringing e-mobility jobs to middle Georgia.
- From July 1 through December 31, 2022, the automotive industry was the second-largest source of new jobs and investment in Georgia.
- Since 2020, Georgia's pursuit of job creation opportunities along the EV supply chain has resulted in over $22.7 billion in investment and the creation of more than 28,400 jobs.
- Woory began operating a small factory producing fuel senders in Suwon, South Korea almost 35 years ago and has grown into an international enterprise with production plants around the world employing over 1,300 people.
- Woory's business is focused on the development and production of HVAC auto components for all types of vehicles, including electric, internal combustion engine, eco-friendly, and hydrogen-fueled cars.
- Woory has been nominated as a supplier for Tesla, Rivian, Lucid, and Canoo.
- Woory is currently supplying the HV heater system used to heat the cabins of EVs.
