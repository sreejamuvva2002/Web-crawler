# YKK AP America Inc.

## Facts

- YKK AP America Inc. installed a new goods-to-person (GTP) automation system at its residential window plant in Macon, Georgia.
- The GTP system was installed at the 400,000-square-foot purpose-built facility’s shipping department to further reduce the manual movement of heavy windows.
- Dennis Howard serves as senior manager of transportation and logistics, and Daymon Loyd serves as director of residential manufacturing for YKK AP.
- The GTP system improves operations and quality and reduces time to load by 39%.
- The system uses autonomous robots to move finished products from the end of the line to material storage units and into a fenced GTP field that houses final products.
- YKK AP is making strategic investments in automation to reduce risk and improve safety across its operations.
- For residential manufacturing, YKK AP's goal was to reduce material handling to improve quality, starting with the shipping department where most material handling occurs.
- When the Macon facility opened in early 2024, YKK AP aimed for zero injuries, zero backorders, and 100% on-time delivery.
- The facility currently achieves a 97% on-time delivery rate.
- YKK AP invested in the GTP system to close the gap from 97% to 100% on-time delivery.
- The facility handles 80% of its products with the GTP system, while the remaining 20% are too large for the material storage units.
- Integrating the new autonomous system with YKK AP’s IT infrastructure was identified as the most significant deployment challenge.
- Training for the system interface required an aggressive schedule focused on troubleshooting technical issues as they arose.
- The Macon facility's residential manufacturing team made its first deployment of GTP technology, requiring flexibility to adjust original plans to accommodate the technology correctly.
- Much of the capital equipment was staged on the manufacturing floor before activation, requiring time and trial-and-error for deployment.
