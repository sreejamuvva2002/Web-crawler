## Facts

- The U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) operates the Critical Minerals and Materials Program with a vision to build reliable, resilient, affordable, and secure domestic critical mineral and materials supply chains that support the energy, manufacturing, and transportation economies while promoting safe solutions to meet current and future needs.
- The program's strategy focuses on four pillars: Broaden and Expand Supply by securing resources from primary and secondary sources, co-produced materials, and international partners; Develop Alternatives to produce new materials with less disruption potential and design parts requiring little to no critical materials; Improve Materials and Manufacturing Efficiency through atom economy, waste reduction, and efficient mining/manufacturing/recycling; and Reuse and Recycle by remanufacturing, refurbishing, repairing, reusing, recycling, and repurposing materials.
- DOE enables these strategy pillars through four cross-cutting functions: Analysis and advanced tools, Market assessment and development, International engagement and standards, and Education and workforce development.
