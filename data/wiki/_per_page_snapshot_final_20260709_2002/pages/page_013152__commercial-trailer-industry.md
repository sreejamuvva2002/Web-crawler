# Commercial Trailer Industry

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2012-09-03

**Source:** [July Trailer Orders Rise 0.2% as Fleets Remain Cautious - TT](https://www.ttnews.com/articles/july-trailer-orders-rise-02-fleets-remain-cautious)  ·  `page_013152`

## Facts

- Orders for new trailers rose a slight 0.2% in July from a year earlier to 13,339, according to ACT Research.
- Trailer orders increased year-over-year in 16 of the past 19 months but were down 3.2% to 128,144 through the first seven months of 2012.
- The July order total was 2% below the 13,615 orders recorded for June.
- Frank Maly, director of commercial vehicle transportation analysis at ACT, reported softness on the truck and trailer orders side over recent months due to marketplace uncertainty.
- Fleets are exercising caution on investments due to a wait-and-see attitude regarding the economy, November elections, and fuel prices.
- Chris Hammond, vice president of dealer sales at Great Dane Trailers in Savannah, Ga., noted that while the election is keeping some buyers on the sideline, there remains significant interest in upgrading fleets.
- Jeff Bennett, vice president of engineering at Utility Trailer Manufacturing Co. in City of Industry, Calif., cited summer doldrums, slowing global and domestic economic activity, and rising fuel costs as factors indicating trailer demand will not grow substantially.
- Refrigerated fleets are analyzing new reefer units ahead of new Environmental Protection Agency emissions standards taking effect on Jan. 1.
- Hammond stated that flatbeds are picking up, vans are good, truckload reefers have slowed, and he remains optimistic about next year.
- Mike Cobb, sales manager at Vanguard National Trailer Corp. in Monon, Ind., explained that potential buyers are waiting for 2013 vendor pricing from suspension and tire makers before placing orders.
- David Giesen, vice president of sales and marketing at Stoughton Trailers LLC. in Stoughton, Wis., described the period as a usual summer slowdown but noted good quote and order activity relative to the season.
- Maly observed that most trailer buyers are keeping existing orders on the books but are unwilling to significantly expand commitments into the future.
- The July cancellation rate stood at 1.2% of manufacturers' backlogs, down from 1.8% in June.
- A recent uptick in cancellations was driven by liquid and dry bulk trailers as the market adjusted to changes in energy exploration expenditures.
- Manufacturers' backlogs stood at 92,214 at the end of July, down from 97,935 at the end of June.
- Maly characterized the backlog decline as typical for summer and predicted order intake would kick up around the September and October timeframe.
- Utility Trailer announced plans to open a 22,000-square-foot expansion at its Paragould, Ark., dry van plant in September.
- Utility Trailer is constructing a 50,000-square-foot modification center for high option and complex refrigerated trailers at its Marion, Va., plant.
- Bennett stated that expanding production facilities is necessary to meet goals, improve labor efficiency, and increase production even if trailer demand continues to slip.
- Wabash National Corp. and Hyundai Translead declined to comment on market conditions.
