# Fouts Bros. Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-11-13

**Source:** [Event showcases growth at Milledgeville Fouts plant | The Union-Recorder](https://unionrecorder.com/2024/11/13/event-showcases-growth-at-milledgeville-fouts-plant/)  ·  `page_020128`

## Facts

- Fouts Bros. Inc. remains a leader in building quality fire trucks and is one of the fastest growing industrial employers in Milledgeville and Baldwin County.
- The company operates out of the old Rheem plant off Roberson Mill Road in Milledgeville, Georgia.
- John Slawson serves as executive vice president of sales and marketing and business development at Fouts Bros. Inc.
- Slawson opened the November 13, 2024 event by stating, “Thank you all for coming; this is the Fouts Bros. Inc. Fouts Fire First Home Day.”
- Georgia’s 8th District U.S. Rep. Austin Scott attended the company's "Fouts Fire First Home Day" event on November 13, 2024.
- Scott Edens serves as president and chief executive officer of Fouts Bros. Inc., while his wife Heather serves as executive vice president.
- The organization employs nearly 420 people across its operations.
- C.J. Fouts founded the business in 1952; he was a longtime car dealer in Smyrna, Georgia, and operated a Sinclair Gas Station in downtown Smyrna before his death in 2005.
- Scott Edens is a third-generation family member who joined the company in his early 20s after graduating from the University of Georgia.
- Edens acquired Fouts Bros., Inc. in 2013 and moved its headquarters from Smyrna to Milledgeville in 2019.
- The company's current Milledgeville facility contains nearly 700,000 square feet of space.
- Since relocating to Milledgeville in 2020, Fouts Bros., Inc. has increased its production volume of various fire truck types by more than 100%.
- The company's growth and market reach have expanded both domestically and internationally.
- Gary Pacilio serves as executive vice president of operations at Fouts Bros., Inc., and concurrently serves as president of the Fire Apparatus Manufacturer’s Association (FAMA).
- Pacilio highlighted the critical importance of Assistance to Firefighters Grants (AFG) and SAFER Grants for supporting first responders.
- Edens stated that the company currently supports fire services with approximately 4,500 trucks.
- Based on industry fire surveys, the fire service sector requires about 6,000 new trucks annually to address population growth and natural disaster response needs.
- Earlier in 2024, Edens traveled to Washington, D.C., to meet with Congressional lawmakers, including Rep. Austin Scott, to discuss industry priorities.
- Rep. Austin Scott told The Union-Recorder, “They’re doing an amazing job here at their Milledgeville plant and I couldn’t be more proud of a business that’s growing and becoming more and more successful with every passing year.”
- Attendees toured the plant and production area, which is scheduled for expansion to increase annual fire apparatus output once construction is completed.
