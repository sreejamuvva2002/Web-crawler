# Adient

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2025-10-13

**Source:** [Adient and Autoliv Unveil Breakthrough in Dynamic Seat Safety So](https://www.gurufocus.com/news/3140344/adient-and-autoliv-unveil-breakthrough-in-dynamic-seat-safety-solutions)  ·  `page_016637`

## Facts

- Adient and Autoliv have co-developed innovative safety solutions that significantly enhance occupant protection in deeply reclined seating positions, commonly referred to as zero-gravity.
- The co-developed solutions are now ready for mass production.
- The new seating concept combines Adient's Z-Guard with Autoliv's advanced safety technologies.
- Zero-gravity seating is gaining popularity in mid- to high-end vehicle models due to its ergonomic benefits.
- Deeply reclined seating postures can compromise the effectiveness of conventional safety systems typically designed for standard upright seating.
- Adient's Z-Guard is built around the principle of Multi-Dimensional Collaborative Protection, integrating advanced constraint and energy-absorbing technologies into a dynamic safety system.
- A key innovation in Z-Guard is the Active Cushion Collapse Mechanism, which rapidly collapses the seat frame in a crash to absorb energy, adjust posture, reduce spinal impact, and protect vital body regions.
- Another key innovation is the Adjustable Seat Belt Outlet, which adapts to various passenger postures and body types to enhance comfort while maximizing precise restraint and minimizing shoulder slippage risks.
- Autoliv's complementary technologies include an Integrated Seatbelt System for improved stability, a Dynamic Lumbar Retractor for controlled restraint force, a Pelvic Cushion Airbag to reduce pelvic impact, and a Head Side Airbag for enhanced side collision protection in reclined positions.
- Together, the combined systems safeguard critical areas including the head, neck, chest, and pelvis.
- The Z-Guard concept enables deep integration with vehicle driver assistance systems tailored to OEM requirements and real-world scenarios.
- Leveraging predictive signals, the system utilizes high-speed, motor-driven seat repositioning to adjust occupant posture before a collision to reduce injury risk.
- If a vehicle cannot detect a crash in time or lacks active repositioning capabilities, Z-Guard provides robust protection through dual pretensioners, cushion buffering, and a collapse mechanism to prevent forward movement and submarining.
- The new seating concept is designed to meet the evolving demands of cockpit electronic architecture in the era of software-defined vehicles.
- Adient is leading the commercialization of Z-Guard.
- Z-Guard is scheduled for production in a high-volume model from a major global OEM.
- Adient (NYSE: ADNT) is a global leader in automotive seating with around 70,000 employees in 29 countries.
- Adient operates more than 200 manufacturing and assembly plants worldwide and produces/delivers automotive seating for all major OEMs.
- Adient's expertise spans from complete seating systems to individual components, covering the entire automotive seat-making process from research and design to engineering and manufacturing.
