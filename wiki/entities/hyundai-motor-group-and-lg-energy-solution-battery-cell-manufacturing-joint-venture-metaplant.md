# Hyundai Motor Group & LG Energy Solution Battery Cell Manufacturing Joint Venture (Metaplant)

**Type:** Joint Venture  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[battery-cell-manufacturing|Battery Cell Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** Bryan County, Georgia, Bryan County, Georgia, United States

## Overview

A battery cell manufacturing joint venture between Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution located at the Metaplant in Bryan County, Georgia. The JV has received a total investment of more than $7.59 billion, including an additional $2 billion announced on August 31, 2023, to expand capacity and create hundreds of new jobs.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Manufactures lithium-ion battery cells that power Hyundai, Kia and Genesis electric vehicles, supporting the EV supply chain in Georgia.

- **Metaplant Battery Cell Manufacturing Facility** — The facility will enable Hyundai Mobis to assemble battery packs and supply them to Hyundai, Kia and Genesis U.S. manufacturing plants.

## Investment

- **$2 billion** (August 31, 2023) — Additional investment in Metaplant JV expansion
  - Raises total JV investment to more than $4.3 billion and creates an additional 400 jobs.
- **$7.59 billion** (May 2023) — Estimated total investment for the EV manufacturing facility and battery JV
  - Projected to create 8,500 new jobs over the next eight years.

## Jobs

- **400 new jobs**
  - Additional well-paying positions for the Savannah region as part of the August 2023 investment announcement.
- **8,500 new jobs** — next eight years
  - Total job creation expected from the $7.59 billion EV manufacturing and battery JV project.

## Timeline

- **May 2023** — Hyundai Motor Group and LGES signed a memorandum of understanding establishing LGES as the partner for Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America’s onsite battery cell manufacturing JV.
- **August 31, 2023** — Governor Brian P. Kemp announced an additional $2 billion investment in the Metaplant JV, raising total investment to over $4.3 billion and creating 400 new jobs.

## Key facts

- The 30 GWh facility will be able to support production of 300,000 electric vehicles annually at full operations.
- Hyundai Mobis will assemble battery packs using cells from the plant and supply them to Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis U.S. manufacturing facilities.
- Georgia has attracted more than $25 billion in EV-related investments since 2018, creating over 30,000 jobs; battery projects alone account for at least $11 billion of that investment.
- In fiscal year 2023, automotive industry job creation increased by 324 percent compared with FY21.
- Governor Kemp stated Georgia is becoming the e-mobility capital of the nation and aims to be the top location for EV manufacturing in the United States.
