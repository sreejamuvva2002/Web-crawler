# Vanguard National

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Vanguard National seeks geographic leverage with new dry van plant | Trailer Body Builders](https://www.trailer-bodybuilders.com/trailers/article/21741559/vanguard-national-seeks-geographic-leverage-with-new-dry-van-plant)  ·  `page_013165`

## Facts

- Vanguard National is building a new dry van trailer plant in Trenton, Georgia.
- The company has been manufacturing van trailers in Monon, Indiana for its second decade of operations.
- Management identified the Southeast United States as having zero dry-freight van manufacturing plants, creating a significant market opportunity.
- Charlie Mudd, president of Vanguard National, stated that the Southeast was underserved due to the lack of dry van manufacturing plants.
- Vanguard began searching for a Southeast location approximately three years prior to the article's publication.
- The company evaluated current demand, future scalability, and overall trailer industry capacity when determining the appropriate plant size.
- Trailer manufacturers set a production record in 2015, with the last time over 300,000 trailers were built in a year occurring in 1999.
- Vanguard designed the new facility to produce 10,000 trailers per year to avoid diluting overall industry capacity.
- The plant has been in operation since August and was designed exclusively for dry freight vans.
- Vanguard acquired assets from HPA Monon, manufacturers of Monon Trailers, in 2003.
- Mark Roush, Vanguard’s vice-president of engineering, noted the new plant consolidates operations under one roof, improving efficiency compared to the company's multi-building Monon campus.
- The new plant features a U-shaped material flow with special carts carrying trailers through the assembly process.
- The facility includes new automated equipment and 70 feet between support columns to accommodate potential 60-ft trailers.
- Vanguard selected an industrial park site in Trenton, Georgia, located just off Interstate 59, 20 miles southwest of Chattanooga, Tennessee.
- Charlie Mudd stated there was strong support from the state of Georgia, Dade County, and the city of Trenton for the project.
- At full capacity, the plant will contribute 400 jobs to the local area.
- Employment at the time of publication was 90 people, with plans to hire approximately 90 more.
- Kevin Black serves as the plant manager for the new facility.
- The company planned a gradual ramp-up to full production over a three-year period.
- Vanguard broke ground on the project on September 3, 2015.
- The 356,000-square-foot plant began manufacturing trailers less than a year after groundbreaking.
- The project was completed on time and under a $36 million budget.
- The estimated production capacity of 10,000 trailers aims to shrink customer backlogs that can stretch up to a year during periods of high trailer demand.
- Vanguard designed and planned the entire project internally without external contractors.
- Vanguard held an open house for dealers and suppliers on November 3 to celebrate the plant's opening.
