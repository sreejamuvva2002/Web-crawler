# Hyundai Motor Group

**Source:** [Hyundai's $5.5B Georgia EV Plant Jobs | PDF | Taxes | Joint Venture](https://www.scribd.com/document/584099418/Hyundai-Motor-Group-Economic-Development-Agreement-Executive-Summary)  ·  `page_009365`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group is headquartered in Seoul, Korea, employs 250,000 people worldwide, and sells vehicles in 193 countries through 5,000 dealerships and showrooms.
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America LLC (HMGMA), its affiliate suppliers, and a battery joint venture will invest $5.545 billion to open Georgia’s first fully dedicated electric vehicle manufacturing facility and joint venture battery manufacturing facility.
- The project will create 8,100 jobs with an average salary of $58,105 plus benefits at a state-of-the-art smart factory located at the Bryan County Megasite in Georgia’s coastal region.
- Approximately another $1 billion is anticipated to be invested by non-affiliated HMGMA suppliers in connection with the project in other locations.
- Construction on the new facility is expected to begin in early 2023, with full production expected in the first half of 2025 and an expected annual production of approximately 300,000 units.
- Production plans at the new Georgia smart factory include a diverse range of innovative fully electric vehicles, with details on specific models to be shared at later dates.
- The establishment of the JV battery manufacturing facility is intended to enhance the stabilization of the supply chain for EV battery components in the U.S. market.
- Hyundai Motor Group’s corporate social responsibility programs focus on enriching lives, improving societal wellbeing, and environmental sustainability through time, talent, and resources.
- Hyundai Hope programs are focused on improving health, safety, and supporting access to food and nutrition, while Genesis Gives supports STEAM education programs and youth sports.
- Hyundai is currently coordinating with several local non-profit organizations that support community-based programming.
- Decades of history exist between Hyundai Motor Group and the State of Georgia, including the Kia Georgia facility in West Point established in 2009 with a pledge to create 2,500 jobs and invest $1.2 billion.
- The Kia West Point facility now operates four production lines for the Telluride, K5, Sorento, and Sportage models, covers 2,200 acres, employs approximately 2,700 workers, and has a capacity to produce 340,000 automobiles annually.
- The Kia West Point plant and its suppliers have an estimated annual economic impact of $4 billion.
- Hyundai Dymos Georgia, Glovis Georgia, Hyundai Transys, Mobis America, and other HMG-affiliated companies also operate across the state of Georgia.
- HMGMA will receive a 26-year property tax abatement for its investment, with leasehold ad valorem property tax payments beginning in calendar year 2026 after initial construction is complete.
- HMGMA’s estimated leasehold ad valorem property tax payments in 2026 are $12.696 million, which is more than 320 times the property taxes estimated to be currently generated on the property based on 2021 values of $38,748.29.
- Over the 26-year abatement period, HMG is estimated to pay over $357 million in leasehold ad valorem property tax payments.
- The leasehold ad valorem tax payments increase over time, with Year 12 payments estimated at over $15 million, Year 19 over $20 million, and Year 24 over $23 million.
- Local communities will receive an approximate five-fold return on the Savannah JDA’s investment through leasehold ad valorem property tax payments alone, with the initial investment estimated to be recouped by Year 10.
- In addition to leasehold ad valorem payments, local communities will benefit from increased sales tax revenues, new job-related benefits, and growth in housing, retail, and commercial sectors.
- The Economic Development Agreement (EDA) is a binding contract between the State of Georgia, the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority (Savannah JDA), and HMGMA to ensure all parties abide by agreed-upon terms.
- Georgia’s Constitution requires a balanced budget every year and does not allow the state to give unrestricted cash directly to companies; instead, dollars are directed into project solutions to aid speed-to-market.
- HMGMA commits to a minimum of $5.545 billion investment for the EV and battery manufacturing facilities and the creation of 8,100 full-time jobs by December 31, 2031, with continued maintenance of those jobs and investment through 2048.
- Repayments to the State and Savannah JDA are due in any year if the investment and full-time jobs maintenance fall below 80% of the commitments.
- The EDA requires adherence to environmental state and federal regulations, payment for certain overages on costs relating to changes in site plans, and annual compliance reports.
- By December 31, 2031, HMGMA, affiliate suppliers, and the battery JV must collectively meet 80% of the $5.545 billion investment commitment and 8,100 job commitment, maintaining them through 2048.
- If combined job and investment performance falls below 80%, a proportional repayment of total JDA property, state property, state land improvements, and estimated tax savings is due.
- If jobs attainment falls to 20% or less in any given year during the measurement period, additional payments are due to the JDA and the state.
- Statutory incentives available to HMGMA include tax credits for job creation, retraining, machinery, construction development, R&D, sales/use tax, and raw materials, which are non-discretionary and based on job creation.
- The Mega Project Tax Credit allows companies to proactively claim credits based on a commitment to capital investment and jobs, requiring maintenance of those jobs for at least 5 years after reaching statutory thresholds or triggering repayment if failed.
- Georgia and the Savannah JDA will partner with HMGMA to deliver a site ready for accelerated development, including a 2,967-acre site with all due diligence completed, clearing and grading, wetlands permitting and mitigation, and roadway/access route improvements.
- A REBA grant of $50,000,000 will be spread over two annual installments of $25,000,000 each to assist with site development, building construction, or machinery and equipment purchases.
- A separate REBA grant of approximately $112,608,688 will be provided to the Savannah JDA for additional land acquisition, wetlands mitigation costs, and site clearing and grading, reflecting price increases since the original offer.
- When recruiting Kia in 2006, Georgia provided a $46,000,000 REBA Grant that adjusts to approximately $66.9 million with inflation, making the HMGMA grant higher in real terms.
- The Georgia Department of Transportation (GDOT) will fund public road improvements at an estimated $200,000,000, including a new I-16 interchange and site access road.
