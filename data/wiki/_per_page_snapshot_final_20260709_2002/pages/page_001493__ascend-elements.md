# Ascend Elements

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- Ascend Elements announced plans to relocate its corporate headquarters and research & development center from Westborough, Mass. to a 101,000 square foot facility at 39 Jackson Road in the Pathway Devens campus in Devens, Mass.
- The new facility is owned and operated by King Street Properties in Boston.
- Ascend Elements has experienced over 500% headcount growth in the last 18 months.
- Ascend Elements currently employs approximately 350 team members globally, including 140 people in Massachusetts.
- The 31-mile corporate relocation is planned for September 2024.
- Mike O’Kronley serves as the CEO of Ascend Elements.
- Pathway Devens is a 45-acre, purpose-built development in Devens that includes an 8,300 square foot amenity center and taproom scheduled to open in Q1 2024.
- Other cleantech and biotech tenants on the Pathway Devens campus include Electric Hydrogen, Commonwealth Fusion Systems, and Azzur Group.
- Tyson Reynoso is the Managing Director & Partner at King Street Properties.
- Ascend Elements recently closed a $542 million funding round.
- Ascend Elements received $480 million of U.S. Department of Energy grant awards to accelerate construction of a $1 billion engineered battery materials manufacturing campus in Hopkinsville, Kentucky.
- Ascend Elements operates one of North America’s largest electric vehicle battery recycling facilities in Covington, Georgia.
- CEO Mike O’Kronley stated that while the company is expanding in the southeast United States, its technology was invented at Worcester Polytechnic Institute and its first location was in Worcester, maintaining strong ties to central Massachusetts.
