# Kia Georgia, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-02-01

**Source:** [GEORGIA LAWMAKERS COMMEND KIA FOR MANUFACTURING AND ITS IMPACT TO THE STATE](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/georgia-lawmakers-commend-kia-for-manufacturing-and-its-impact-to-the-state-301735751.html)  ·  `page_003126`

## Facts

- Georgia lawmakers recognized January 31 as "Kia Day," commending Kia's vehicle manufacturing and its impact to the state.
- Kia made one of the biggest single foreign investments in Georgia's history in 2006 by establishing a state-of-the-art vehicle assembly facility in West Point.
- Kia Georgia was Kia Corporation's first manufacturing plant in North America.
- Kia Georgia assembles nearly 40-percent of all Kia vehicles sold in the U.S.
- To date, Kia has invested more than $1.9 billion in the state.
- Kia Georgia is responsible for more than 14,000 plant and supplier jobs in the region.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated that Kia chose Georgia because they saw something in them – their great workforce, quality of life, and pro-business approach.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated that Georgia saw something in Kia – a shared vision for transformational growth and a commitment to the hardworking people of the state.
- Kia is a dynamic part of the world's third-largest automaker and continues to invest in Georgia.
- In October, it was revealed that assembly of Kia electric vehicles (EVs) is expected to begin in the first half of 2025 at Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA).
- HMGMA officially broke ground near Savannah, Georgia.
- HMGMA will create more than 8,100 jobs over the next few years.
- Sean Yoon, president and CEO of Kia North America and Kia America, stated that Kia was the first to open an auto manufacturing plant in Georgia because they saw the talented workforce, sense of community, respected pro-growth business climate and commitment to innovation that is prevalent there.
- Sean Yoon stated that Kia is focused on becoming a leader in sustainable mobility by popularizing EVs and looks forward to working with and bringing new opportunities to Georgia.
- Kia Georgia is home to the K5 midsize sedan and various models of the Telluride, Sorento and Sportage SUVs.
- More than 90-percent of the vehicles assembled at Kia Georgia are destined for dealerships across America.
- Kia Georgia's total production is anticipated to surpass 4 million vehicles in April of 2023.
- The West Point facility has provided over $20 million in support of Georgia communities since 2009.
- Over $6 million was provided to support educational programs such as the THINC College & Career Academy in LaGrange and SAE's "A World in Motion."
- Stuart Countess, president and CEO of Kia Georgia, stated that Georgia's array of resources to promote economic growth and workforce development enabled Kia to focus on the key fundamentals of building a state-of-the-art facility and the necessary skill sets for hundreds of team members new to automotive manufacturing.
- Stuart Countess stated that as Kia looks ahead with excitement, they are confident in their ability to adapt and lead the evolving automotive industry.
- Stuart Countess stated that Kia's experience in the state of Georgia is a huge part of building that confidence.
- Kia America is headquartered in Irvine, California.
- Kia America continues to top automotive quality surveys and is recognized as one of the 100 Best Global Brands.
- Kia serves as the "Official Automotive Partner" of the NBA.
- Kia offers a range of gasoline, hybrid, plug-in hybrid and electrified vehicles sold through a network of over 750 dealers in the U.S.
- Several cars and SUVs are proudly assembled in America.
- The K5, Sportage, Sorento, and Telluride (excludes HEV and PHEV models) are assembled in the United States from U.S. and globally sourced parts.
- Kia Georgia, Inc. is the first manufacturing site in North America for Kia Corporation based in Seoul, Korea.
- With an annual capacity of 340,000 units, Kia Georgia is located on 2,200 acres in West Point, GA.
- Kia Georgia began mass vehicle assembly on Nov. 16, 2009.
- Kia Georgia is home to the Telluride SUV, Sorento SUV, Sportage SUV (excluding HEV and PHEV models) and the K5 mid-size sedan.
