# Michelin North America, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2018-06-21

**Source:** [Michelin Celebrates 10 Years of a Closed-Loop Process Using Micronized Rubber Powder in North America :: Michelin North America, Inc.](https://michelinmedia.com/pages/blog/detail/article/c/a749/)  ·  `page_057042`

## Facts

- Michelin North America retread business celebrated a decade of business with Lehigh Technologies, a Georgia-based company that manufactures Micronized Rubber Powders.
- The partnership with Lehigh Technologies began early in the specialty chemical company’s history to manufacture Micronized Rubber Powder (MRP) for reusing end-of-life tire materials.
- Lehigh Technologies was acquired by the Michelin Group in October 2017.
- Lehigh is a key element of Michelin’s ambition to make tires with 80 percent sustainable materials by 2048.
- Gary Scheide, responsible for materials manufacturing for Michelin North America, stated that Michelin and its customers will benefit from the closed-loop approach where tires are recycled into tires with Lehigh Technologies.
- Gary Scheide described the Lehigh team as a great partner and reliable supplier to Michelin’s plants in North America.
- Lehigh’s proprietary technology converts rubber materials into a high technology powder incorporated in new retread compounds for higher performance levels.
- MRP replaces oil- and rubber-based feedstocks in industrial and consumer applications including high-performance tires, plastics, consumer goods, coatings, sealants, construction materials, and asphalt.
- Lehigh technical experts collaborate with customers to optimize products for each application.
- Michelin North American retread facilities operate as zero-waste operations due to the closed-loop model.
- This circular economy approach reduces carbon dioxide load and delivers economic advantages to the company.
- Lehigh is now part of Michelin’s High Technology Materials Business Unit.
- The acquisition supports Michelin’s Sustainable Mobility vision and its ambition to recycle 100 percent of tires by 2048.
- Michelin plans to be the global leader in using MRP in new tires.
- Working with Lehigh ensures supply chain efficiencies are maximized and material specifications exactly fit retread compound performance requirements.
- Christophe Rahier, director of the High Technology Materials Business Line for the Michelin Group, noted that markets must improve sustainability profiles and reduce carbon-based material consumption.
- Christophe Rahier stated Lehigh’s products enable rubber powders from end-of-life tires to deliver cost savings, performance, and sustainability to customers in tire, asphalt, construction, plastics, and coatings markets.
- Michelin is committed to growth plans for Lehigh’s PolyDyne™ and MicroDyne™ products across multiple global high-performance markets.
- The value proposition for MRP has been proven in the tire industry, prompting Michelin to support wide adoption of the technology for sustainability and environmental performance leadership.
- For Europe, Michelin is working with Lehigh to build the first MRP plant outside the U.S., a 10,000-metric-ton facility in Navarra, Spain.
- The Spanish MRP plant will be commissioned in summer 2018.
