# Delta Air Lines

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2020-02-29

**Source:** [Sustainable Georgia Roundup - Georgia Trend Magazine](https://www.georgiatrend.com/2020/02/29/sustainable-georgia/)  ·  `page_052960`

## Facts

- Atlanta-based Delta Air Lines signed an agreement to purchase 10 million gallons a year of advanced renewable biofuel from Gevo Inc.
- Gevo Inc. is a renewable chemicals and biofuels company based in Colorado.
- Delta estimates the sustainable aviation fuel will have a carbon footprint that is up to 75% less than conventional jet fuel.
- Gevo says its renewable biofuel performs as well as or better than traditional fossil-based fuels in jet engines.
- The deal follows Delta’s $2-million investment in Northwest Advanced Bio-Fuels for a feasibility study of a potential biofuel facility in Washington state.
- These efforts are part of the airline’s stated goal of reducing emissions 50% by 2050.
- Since 2005, Delta estimates it has reduced emissions by 11% as a result of reducing jet fuel consumption.
- The company has been investing in fuel-efficient aircraft.
- Delta was the first U.S. airline to recycle aluminum cans, plastics, newspapers, and magazines.
