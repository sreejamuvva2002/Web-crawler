# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-05-22

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Group - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hyundai_Motor_Group)  ·  `page_009164`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group is a South Korean chaebol headquartered in Seoul, South Korea.
- The group was formed in 1998 through the purchase of 51% of Kia by Hyundai Motor Company.
- As of 2011, Hyundai owns 33.7% of Kia Motors.
- In June 2021, Hyundai Motor, Hyundai Mobis, Hyundai Glovis, and Executive Chair Chung Eui-sun collectively purchased an 80% stake in Boston Dynamics from SoftBank Group.
- On May 22, 2022, the group announced an additional $5 billion investment in the United States by 2025 to strengthen collaboration with U.S. firms in urban air mobility, autonomous driving, artificial intelligence, and robotics.
- The May 2022 investment announcement occurred during a visit to South Korea by former President Joe Biden.
- On December 10, 2024, Hyundai allowed customers to purchase Hyundai cars on Amazon for pickup at local dealerships.
- Hyundai Motor Company and Kia are the largest and second-largest car manufacturers in South Korea, respectively.
- The group sold 8.01 million vehicles in 2015, falling short of its sales target.
- In 2017, the group sold 7.25 million vehicles, marking the lowest sales in five years at that time.
- According to OICA, Hyundai Motor Group was the world's third-largest vehicle manufacturer by production volume in 2017, behind Toyota and Volkswagen Group.
- On June 17, 2024, Hyundai Motor India announced the commencement of an IPO process to list on the Bombay Stock Exchange.
- In 2024, Hyundai Motor Group announced plans to invest a record $16.7 billion in South Korea, focusing on green technologies and future mobility.
- Chairman Chung Eui-sun announced an investment of 31 trillion won in the U.S. over four years, prompting a response from Trump that he would exempt the company from tariffs.
- Affiliates include Ionna (a joint venture), Motional, Supernal, Hyundai AutoEver, and Hyundai Glovis.
- On September 7, 2021, Hyundai held the Hydrogen Wave global online forum to outline its hydrogen business vision and Hydrogen Vision 2040.
- The group unveiled the Vision FK, a high-performance hydrogen fuel cell vehicle developed in collaboration with Limak.
- The Vision FK utilizes second-generation fuel cell stacks averaging 85kW with a maximum of 95kW, along with two 2kg hydrogen fuel tanks and a PE System comprising motor drives, a decelerator, inverter, and battery.
- The total output of the rear-wheel motor drives on the Vision FK exceeds 500kW.
- In December 2021, Hyundai suspended development of its Genesis hydrogen cars and possibly other hydrogen vehicles.
- Key executives include Chung Mong-koo (honorary chairman), Chung Eui-sun (Executive Chairman & CEO), and Chang Jae-hoon (Vice Chairman).
- The group reported production output of 3,890,726 vehicles in 2021 and had 292,575 employees as of 2021.
