## Facts

- WIKA is a leading manufacturer of gauge thermometers and machine glass thermometers for industrial and process applications, from HVAC and refrigeration to pharmaceuticals and oil & gas.
- Many of WIKA's industrial temperature gauges are produced in the Atlanta metro area.
- Customers of WIKA's Atlanta-produced gauges are assured of industry-leading workmanship, competitive pricing, responsive customer service, and fast delivery times.
- WIKA offers a portfolio of bimetal temperature gauges, gas-actuated temperature gauges, and expansion temperature gauges.
- WIKA's temperature measuring instruments feature simple, robust, and cost-effective designs frequently used for industrial measuring tasks in heating, ventilation and air-conditioning, machine building, plant/vessel/pipeline/apparatus construction, and chemical/petrochemical industries.
- WIKA provides a complete range of measuring instruments for electrical temperature measurement including thermocouples, resistance thermometers, temperature switches, and temperature transmitters.
- The core of WIKA's electronic temperature measurement instruments is a sensor that converts measured temperature into an electrical signal.
- WIKA thermocouples consist of two wires of different material welded together, with the welding point representing the actual measuring point.
- WIKA resistance thermometers use platinum resistors (Pt100 and Pt1000) where measured resistance changes depending on temperature.
- WIKA temperature switches provide control and analogue output signals to the process alongside temperature measurement.
- WIKA temperature transmitters are mounted either on a DIN rail in a control cabinet or in a connection head directly at the measuring point.
- WIKA's mechanical temperature measurement range comprises bimetal, gas-actuated, and expansion thermometers based on the expansion of temperature-dependent materials.
- Bimetal thermometers from WIKA are composed of two bonded metal strips with differing expansion coefficients, where resulting bending measures temperature.
- Gas-actuated thermometers from WIKA are filled with an inert gas or gas mixture, using temperature-dependent change in gas pressure to lead via a capillary to the temperature display.
- Expansion thermometers from WIKA contain a liquid-filled measuring system that generates a pressure change when temperature changes, displayed via an integrated movement.
- Mechatronic temperature measurement instruments from WIKA have additional equipment such as alarm contacts and switch contacts that trigger a switching operation when a pre-defined measured value is reached.
- WIKA's mechatronic measuring instruments can be used for control and regulation processes via transmitted output signals, including mechanical temperature measurement with electrical output signals or switch contacts and mechanical temperature switches.
