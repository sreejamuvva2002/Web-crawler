## Facts

- Georgia is home to more than 200 automotive-related facilities.
- The automotive industry contributes more than $3 billion to the state’s economy.
- GaMEP at Georgia Tech supports the automotive industry by providing specialized assistance and expertise.
- GaMEP helps companies meet quality, efficiency, and sustainability goals.
- Quality Core Tools are available for planning, launching, and validating automotive products and processes.
- Automotive manufacturers and suppliers must meet ISO 9001 quality system requirements in addition to IATF 16949 specific requirements.
- GaMEP provides training on Advanced Product Quality Planning (APQP) for designing and developing new automotive products.
- GaMEP provides training on Failure Mode Effects Analysis (FMEA) to determine potential failures with parts and processes.
- GaMEP provides training on the Production Part Approval Process (PPAP) to prove reliable and consistent parts meet customer requirements.
- GaMEP provides training on Statistical Process Control to verify if processes are operating in a controlled state and meeting customer requirements.
- GaMEP provides training on Measurement System Analysis to recognize gauge variation and ensure calculations capture true process variations.
- Receiving Quality Core Tools training helps reduce production defects, meet buyer demands, acquire new business, and increase profit margins.
- The Georgia Tech Manufacturing 4.0 Consortium provides access to state-of-the-art advanced manufacturing research facilities to deploy manufacturing technologies and innovation into the market.
- GaMEP offers customized automotive industry services including lean manufacturing implementation.
- Customized services include Single Minute Exchange of Dies (SMED).
- Customized services include quality management systems for ISO/IATF 16949 compliance.
- Customized services include automation and Industry 4.0 technologies.
- Customized services include supply chain and logistics optimization.
