# Plug Power Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2025-03-03

**Source:** [Plug Power Inc. | Plug Power Announces Continued Cash Flow Improvement and Progress on Path to Profitability](https://www.ir.plugpower.com/press-releases/news-details/2025/Plug-Power-Announces-Continued-Cash-Flow-Improvement-and-Progress-on-Path-to-Profitability/default.aspx)  ·  `page_012542`

## Facts

- Plug Power Inc. is a global leader in the hydrogen economy and is building an end-to-end green hydrogen ecosystem from production, storage, and delivery to energy generation.
- 2024 marked a pivotal commercial inflection point for Plug as it advanced its hydrogen generation platform and scaled new product offerings such as electrolyzer solutions.
- In response to evolving market conditions in 2024, Plug streamlined its workforce, consolidated facilities, increased pricing on certain offerings, reduced working capital, and reprioritized hydrogen and new product investments to improve margins and cash flows.
- Plug initiated Project Quantum Leap, which includes additional workforce reductions, rooftop facility consolidations, discretionary spending cuts, inventory reduction, and limiting capital expenditures to near-term critical requirements.
- Project Quantum Leap is targeted to reduce annual expenses in the range of $150 million to $200 million.
- In the fourth quarter of 2024, Plug reported revenue of $191.5 million, reflecting electrolyzer deployment growth and hydrogen network expansion.
- Customer warrant charges for the fourth quarter of 2024 totaled $22.7 million, driven by updated forecasts and customer program timing.
- Operating cash flow improved by 25% quarter-over-quarter and 46% year-over-year in the fourth quarter of 2024, with full-year 2024 burn improving 34% compared to 2023.
- Capital expenditures were down by 56% quarter-over-quarter and 52% for the full year of 2024 compared to 2023 due to limiting incremental investments.
- Plug reported a gross margin loss of 122% in the fourth quarter of 2024, including $104.2 million in inventory valuation adjustments linked to a strategic slowdown in mobility, high-power stationary applications, and other products.
- For the full year of 2024, results included an estimated reduced amount of labor, overhead, and outside services of approximately $42 million from targeted cost reductions.
- In the fourth quarter of 2024, Plug recorded $971.3 million in non-cash charges for asset impairments and bad debt provision, which will reduce future depreciation and amortization by an estimated $55 million to $60 million in 2025.
- Electrolyzer revenue increased 583% year-over-year in the fourth quarter of 2024, driven by 5-megawatt system sales and a large-scale order deployment.
- Plug announced a purchase agreement with Allied Green Ammonia to supply three gigawatts of electrolyzer capacity for a green hydrogen-to-ammonia plant in Australia.
- To date, Plug has secured more than 8 GW in global Basic Engineering and Design Package contracts.
- A joint venture hydrogen plant with Olin Corporation in Louisiana is on track for full operation with final commissioning nearly complete.
- The Louisiana plant expansion is expected to increase Plug's hydrogen nameplate network capacity to over 39 tons per day.
- The expanded hydrogen network will provide vertically integrated hydrogen solutions to partners including Amazon and Walmart.
- Plug anticipates its material handling business to grow approximately 10-20% year-over-year, with a major customer placing an approximate $10 million order in the fourth quarter of 2024 positioning over $200 million in equipment opportunities.
- Plug closed 2024 with over $200 million in unrestricted cash on its balance sheet, with cash burn expected to continue significantly improving in 2025.
- Plug completed the transfer of approximately $30 million in energy storage investment tax credits related to a hydrogen liquefier at its hydrogen generation plant in Woodbine, GA.
- The company plans to leverage a similar ITC strategy for its Louisiana and Texas hydrogen plants and pursue sales of other asset ITC opportunities.
- Plug closed the $1.66 billion Department of Energy Loan Guarantee program, having invested over $250 million to date with an estimated additional required investment of approximately $600 million.
- The DOE loan is targeted to cover approximately $400 million of the additional investment, with the remainder to be covered through project finance and equity investors.
- Construction of the DOE-funded project is expected to take approximately 18 months once the EPC contractor is mobilized.
- CEO Andy Marsh stated that hydrogen plays a crucial role in energy diversification and contributes to economic growth and job creation, and Plug remains committed to scaling its solutions.
- Plug has deployed more than 72,000 fuel cell systems and over 275 fueling stations globally and is the largest buyer of liquid hydrogen.
- Plug plans to operate a green hydrogen highway across North America and Europe and built a state-of-the-art Gigafactory to produce electrolyzers and fuel cells.
- The company delivers green hydrogen solutions directly to customers and through joint venture partners in material handling, e-mobility, power generation, and industrial applications.
