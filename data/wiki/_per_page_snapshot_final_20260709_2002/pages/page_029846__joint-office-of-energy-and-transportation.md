# Joint Office of Energy and Transportation

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Technical Assistance and Resources for States · Joint Office of Energy and Transportation](https://driveelectric.gov/state-plans/)  ·  `page_029846`

## Facts

- The Joint Office has electric transportation experts available to help answer questions on alternative fuel vehicles and fueling infrastructure, including EV charging.
- The Joint Office is tasked with helping to ensure that individual efforts in the National Electric Vehicle Infrastructure (NEVI) Formula Program, Charging and Fueling Infrastructure (CFI) Discretionary Grant Program, and other related grant programs contribute toward a convenient, reliable, and affordable national EV charging network.
- EV charging projects developed under the NEVI Formula Program and CFI Discretionary Grant Program must comply with minimum standards for federally funded EV charging infrastructure projects.
- EV charging projects developed under the NEVI Formula Program and CFI Discretionary Grant Program must comply with the Build America, Buy America implementation plan for EV charging equipment.
- EV charging projects developed under the NEVI Formula Program and CFI Discretionary Grant Program must comply with the NEVI Formula Program Interim Final Guidance, updated 8/11/2025.
- The Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act (IIJA) established the NEVI Formula Program.
- The NEVI Formula Program provides funding to states to deploy electric vehicle (EV) charging infrastructure and establish an interconnected network to facilitate data collection, convenience, and reliability.
- Under the NEVI program, each state is required to submit an annual EV Infrastructure Deployment Plan that describes how the state intends to use its apportioned NEVI Formula Program funds in accordance with federal guidance.
- The Charging and Fueling Infrastructure (CFI) Discretionary Grant Program from the Federal Highway Administration provides funding to strategically deploy public EV charging and hydrogen, propane, and natural gas refueling infrastructure.
- Created by IIJA, the CFI Program provides two funding categories of grants: Community Charging and Alternative Fueling Grants (Community Program) and Charging and Alternative Fuel Corridor Grants (Corridor Program).
- The Alternative Fuel Life-Cycle Environmental and Economic Transportation (AFLEET) CFI Emissions Tool is a resource to guide the buildout of alternative fueling infrastructure.
- The AFLEET CFI Emissions Tool can assess estimated emissions reductions from EV charging infrastructure and hydrogen, propane, and natural gas fueling infrastructure along designated AFCs.
- As part of technical assistance resources, the Joint Office connects states with critical data and tools to understand where EV charging and alternative fueling infrastructure is installed and where existing AFCs are designated.
- The Alternative Fueling Station Locator can be used to find existing EV charging and alternative fueling infrastructure.
- The Alternative Fuel Corridors website can be used to learn about AFCs and see existing corridors.
- The Station Data for Alternative Fuel Corridors tool allows users to download data and analyze stations that meet the criteria for corridors and view potential EV charging and AFCs.
- Contact information for technical assistance on EV chargers and alternative fueling infrastructure is available through the Joint Office.
- The Alternative Fuels Data Center provides resources and connects users with seasoned experts to answer technical questions about alternative fuels, advanced vehicles, and alternative fueling infrastructure.
- The Public EV Charging Infrastructure Playbook provides interactive resources to help communities and other stakeholders plan and build infrastructure to support a modern transportation system.
- EV Charging Community Readiness provides a summary of resources, tools, and publications designed to help communities prepare and plan for the expansion of EVs and charging infrastructure.
- Planning and Zoning Guidance for Electric Vehicle Charger Deployment is a guidance document published by Rocky Mountain Institute, along with the Interstate Renewable Energy Council and the Sustainable Energy Action Committee to support local code officials with planning and zoning for EV charger deployment.
- The EV States Clearinghouse serves as a repository for state EV program documents, current state-level EV roadmaps, and other resources to support EV infrastructure program development.
