# Archer Aviation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-03-08

**Source:** [Archer begins construction on Covington manufacturing facility | Revolution.aero](https://www.revolution.aero/news/2023/03/08/archer-begins-construction-on-covington-manufacturing-facility/)  ·  `page_016775`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation has begun construction on its Georgia-based manufacturing facility.
- The company closed the local incentive transaction for the facility.
- The planned facility is approximately 350,000 sqft.
- The facility is located adjacent to the Covington Municipal Airport.
- The facility will be capable of producing up to 650 aircraft per year.
- Archer stated this type of high-volume manufacturing facility is believed to be a first for the eVTOL industry.
- The factory can be expanded by an additional 800,000 sqft.
- The expansion could support long-term production targets of up to 2,300 aircraft per year.
- Archer recently announced it had expanded its relationship with automaker Stellantis, which owns brands like Jeep, Ram and Maserati.
- Under the partnership, Archer and Stellantis will join forces to manufacture Archer’s production aircraft, Midnight.
- Adam Goldstein serves as Archer’s founder and CEO.
- Goldstein stated they are working closely with Stellantis to ramp up high-volume manufacturing capabilities at the new Covington, GA facility.
- Goldstein emphasized that scale is key to unlocking the promise of what the eVTOL aircraft industry can bring.
- Goldstein highlighted the support provided by the State of Georgia and Newton County to this first of its kind facility in a new industry.
- Stellantis will contribute advanced manufacturing technology, expertise, personnel and capital to Archer.
- The partnership is intended to enable the rapid scaling of aircraft production.
- The partnership allows Archer to strengthen its path to commercialization by avoiding millions of dollars of spending during the ramp up phase.
- Stellantis is planned to mass produce Archer’s eVTOL aircraft as its exclusive contract manufacturer.
