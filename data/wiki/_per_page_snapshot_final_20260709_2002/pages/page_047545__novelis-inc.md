# Novelis Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2021-09-08

**Source:** [Novelis Expands Research Partnership with Georgia Tech :: Novelis Inc.](https://investors.novelis.com/news-events/press-releases/detail/74/novelis-expands-research-partnership-with-georgia-tech)  ·  `page_047545`

## Facts

- Novelis Inc. announced the expansion of its research partnership with the Georgia Institute of Technology (Georgia Tech) Novelis Innovation Hub.
- The initiative focuses on materials and process research to help Novelis reach a net zero carbon footprint by 2050.
- Extended cooperation builds on programs to accelerate innovation via high throughput development methodologies coupled with artificial intelligence.
- Programs aim to develop new generations of electric vehicle (EV) batteries, making use of aluminum to improve their performance.
- Extended programs will target improved performance with higher amounts of recycled aluminum in batteries.
- Philippe Meyer, Senior Vice President and Chief Technology Officer of Novelis Inc., stated the collaboration provides an opportunity to accelerate R&D and boost EV battery performance (energy density) and recyclability, while lowering costs for automakers and battery manufacturers.
- Meyer stated they want to partner closely with Georgia Tech to help transform their industry to reach committed objectives toward sustainability.
- The expanded partnership continues ongoing technical research while initiating new strategic programs.
- New strategic programs include circular economy solutions or de-carbonization of the industrial footprint.
- Programs also promote entrepreneurship and incubation of new ventures.
- This will drive accelerated commercialization of new products, resulting in broader relationships with Georgia Tech's business and commercialization groups.
- Raj Gopalaswamy, Global Technology Director of New Domains at Novelis Inc., stated the aluminum industry must increase the pace of innovation to develop solutions demonstrating aluminum's superior sustainability benefits.
- Gopalaswamy noted research partnerships with institutions like Georgia Tech help fulfill growing needs for aluminum applications that help customers meet sustainability goals faster and more efficiently.
- Shreyes Melkote, Executive Director of the Novelis Innovation Hub at Georgia Tech, stated collaboration between academia and industry will help speed development of transformative solutions to meet sustainability challenges.
- Melkote expressed looking forward to expanding the partnership with Novelis to address critical scientific, technological and workforce needs by leveraging Georgia Tech faculty and students in sustainable technologies, advanced materials and manufacturing, artificial intelligence, data sciences and robotics.
- Julia Kubanek, Vice President for Interdisciplinary Research at Georgia Tech, views Novelis as a critical partner for Georgia Tech to accelerate product innovation across multiple sectors.
- Kubanek stated the Institute remains committed to fostering world-class partnerships collaborating with Novelis, citing mutual interests in sustainability, clean energy and creative design of materials.
- Novelis Inc. is driven by its purpose to shape a sustainable world together.
- Novelis is a global leader in innovative products and services and the world's largest recycler of aluminum.
- Novelis partners with customers in the aerospace, automotive, beverage can and specialties industries.
- Novelis delivers solutions maximizing lightweight aluminum benefits throughout North America, Europe, Asia and South America.
- Novelis is a subsidiary of Hindalco Industries Limited.
- Hindalco Industries Limited is an industry leader in aluminum and copper, and the metals flagship company of the Aditya Birla Group.
- The Aditya Birla Group is a multinational conglomerate based in Mumbai.
