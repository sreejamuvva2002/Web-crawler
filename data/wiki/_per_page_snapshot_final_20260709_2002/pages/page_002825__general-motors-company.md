# General Motors Company

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-04-29

**Source:** [DEF 14A](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1467858/000119312522131642/d215687ddef14a.htm)  ·  `page_002825`

## Facts

- General Motors is executing an unprecedented transformation focused on an accelerated transition to electric and autonomous vehicles.
- GM invested billions to develop the Ultium Platform, a combined EV architecture and propulsion system that enables EVs at scale for every lifestyle and price point.
- GM delivered its first Ultium-powered EVs to customers.
- Ultium-powered vehicles include the GMC HUMMER EV Pickup, BrightDrop Zevo 600 and Zevo 400, Wuling Hong Guang Mini EV (the best-selling EV in China), Cadillac LYRIQ, Chevrolet Silverado EV, and Chevrolet Equinox EV.
- By the end of 2025, GM expects to have more than 2 million units of EV capacity globally, with 1 million in North America and another 1 million in China.
- GM recently announced investments of nearly $7 billion for a significant expansion of battery cell and EV assembly capacity in the United States.
- The $7 billion U.S. expansion is expected to create 4,000 new jobs and retain 1,000 others.
- This investment is part of GM's larger commitment to invest more than $35 billion in EVs and AVs from 2020 to 2025.
- GM is investing aggressively in other growth platforms and new businesses, including Cruise, BrightDrop, GM Defense, OnStar Insurance, and Ultifi.
- GM named Kyle Vogt as the CEO of Cruise.
- Under Kyle Vogt's leadership, driverless Cruise test vehicles are rapidly accumulating miles and transporting members of the public throughout San Francisco.
- Cruise is one permit away from being able to provide paid ride-hailing services in San Francisco.
- GM recently increased its stake in Cruise.
- GM announced plans to be carbon neutral in its global products and operations by 2040.
- GM plans to eliminate tailpipe emissions from new light-duty vehicles globally by 2035.
- GM set SBTi-validated science-based emission reduction targets for scope 1, 2, and 3 emissions that align with the most ambitious goals of the Paris Agreement.
- GM announced a plan to source 100 percent renewable energy to power its U.S. facilities by 2025.
- The target to source 100 percent renewable energy for U.S. facilities is five years ahead of GM's previous target and 25 years ahead of its initial target.
- GM aims to source 100 percent renewable energy globally by 2035.
- GM updated its executive compensation program to better link the long-term compensation of executives to its EV strategy.
- Jane Mendillo informed GM she will not stand for re-election and will retire from the Board following the Annual Meeting, having provided insights that helped accelerate the Company's EV future.
