# Magna

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [A $42B Auto Giant You've Never Heard of Is Building AI Into Factories - Business Insider](https://www.businessinsider.com/auto-giant-magna-ai-factories-2026-5)  ·  `page_057452`

## Facts

- Magna is a 66-year-old Canadian firm and one of the largest automotive parts suppliers in the world.
- The company builds everything from seats and mirrors to advanced driver-assistance systems for major car companies.
- Magna supplies components to at least 59 global automakers, including BYD, Tesla, Hyundai, Ford, Volkswagen, and Xiaopeng.
- The company has hand-built entire vehicles, including the Mercedes-Benz G-Wagen.
- Magna operates 330 manufacturing and assembly plants spanning 28 countries.
- The firm reports roughly $42 billion in annual sales.
- Magna is currently embedding artificial intelligence across its global manufacturing footprint.
- Sharath Reddy, the company's SVP of R&D, stated that AI is already embedded across multiple layers of Magna's supply chain and manufacturing operations, adding that "We don't view it as a stand-alone technology."
- Magna's AI investments focus on five areas: product quality, equipment maintenance, factory safety, energy reduction, and output speed.
- For customers, the company deploys an AI-powered vision inspection system that uses high-resolution scanners and machine learning to detect parts defects and irregularities in real time.
- Sharath Reddy noted that the clearest payoff from AI comes from applications "closest to the physical operation" rather than through sweeping, end-to-end automation.
- Magna uses AI systems that monitor vibration, temperature, and pressure to predict equipment failures before they happen, helping plants avoid costly downtime.
- The company is deploying autonomous mobile robots to move heavy materials between workstations.
- Magna uses machine learning to track energy use, water consumption, and industrial waste across facilities, flagging anomalies and identifying ways to reduce costs.
- The company is working toward a "unified factory" where data, software, and automation systems are connected across factory-wide operations.
- Reddy explained that the unified system's value shows up across scheduling, material flow, and decision-making rather than in a single metric, stating, "The value is real, but it's more distributed."
- Magna uses AI to monitor global supply chain disruptions caused by factors like tariffs, trade tensions, material supply shortages, and uneven global EV demand.
- Reddy stated that AI does not replace the fundamentals of supply chain management but instead acts as an "amplifier" for potential threats.
- Regarding near-term impacts, Reddy said AI will provide better visibility and faster decision-making through earlier signals, stronger scenario modeling, and more coordinated response.
- Magna's factories are becoming more software-defined step by step, with intelligence layered into the parts of the system where it can reliably deliver results.
