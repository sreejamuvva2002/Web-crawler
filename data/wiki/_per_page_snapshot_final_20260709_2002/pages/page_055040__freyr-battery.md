# Freyr Battery

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2021-02-01

**Source:** [Norway's Freyr merges to list on NYSE - electrive.com](https://www.electrive.com/2021/02/01/battery-cell-start-up-freyr-merges-to-make-it-on-nyse/)  ·  `page_055040`

## Facts

- Norway’s battery cell start-up Freyr is about to go public on the US stock exchange through merging with Alussa Energy Acquisition Corporation.
- Upon closing, the combined company will be renamed Freyr Battery and listed on NYSE under the ticker symbol ‘FREY’.
- Following Northvolt, Freyr is another Nordic company planning to build battery cell factories in the gigawatt range.
- The merger is expected to provide equity proceeds of approximately $850 million.
- This includes a $600 million PIPE at $10.00 per share with Koch Strategic Platforms and Glencore having already joined the investment.
- Koch Strategic Platforms represents Koch Industries’ attempt to buy into new technology.
- Glencore is connected to the gas and oil industry.
- Freyr signed an LOI with Glencore to supply up to 3,700 tonnes of high-grade sustainably-sourced cobalt metal cut cathodes produced at Nikkleverk, Glencore’s Norwegian operations.
- Freyr plans to install a battery cell production capacity of up to 43 GWh in four years.
- The company previously hoped to get 32 GWh online by 2023 as reported in August 2019.
- Earlier plans included building fast-track production plants with a capacity of 2 GWh per year alongside local site partner Mo Industrial Park.
- Daniel Barcelo, Chief Executive Officer and Founder of Alussa Energy, stated that Norway with entrepreneurial cities like Mo i Rana provides a great foundation for Freyr’s Gigafactories.
- Barcelo noted they evaluated over 75 investment opportunities across the global energy and energy transition sectors before selecting Freyr as a frontline player.
- The CEO stressed a commitment to environmentally-friendly production, claiming they would build one of the most environmentally friendly battery cells in the world.
- Torstein Dale Sjøtveit, Founder and Executive Chairman of Freyr, stated that growth potential is enabled by cutting-edge technology and access to clean, renewable energy.
- Freyr intends to supply battery cells at more competitive prices than other suppliers using energy from renewable sources.
- In January, Freyr signed a licensing agreement with cell specialist 24M Technologies.
- The US American spin-off formerly known as A123 develops semi-solid technology and granted Freyr rights to unlimited battery cell production based on current and all future technologies.
- Freyr expects the partnership with 24M Technologies to materially reduce manufacturing costs and provide a highly competitive market position.
- Freyr estimated the total required capital at $4.5 billion or 40 billion crowns (4.5 billion US dollars or 3.4Bn euros).
- To start construction that year, the company stated it would need two billion dollars or 1.6 billion euros.
- After the merger, the pro forma equity value of the combined company was expected to approximate $1.4 billion.
- Freyr introduced Siemens Energy and Elkem as interested customers in October 2020.
- A memorandum of understanding is in place with both groups covering cell delivery and the supply of anode active materials.
- Elkem plans to produce anode materials in Herøya Industrial Park as part of a joint commitment to develop and test new anode active materials.
- Freyr Battery or Pubco’s common stock is expected to start trading on NYSE as FREY in the second quarter of 2021.
- Current shareholders will own approximately 30% of the combined company after transaction close.
- The boards of directors of both Alussa Energy and Freyr have approved the proposed business combination, which is now subject to shareholder approval.
