# Porsche

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-11-23

**Source:** [Mixing it up: Porsche unveils a new gas-powered 911 Carrera T and electric Taycan GTS - AOL](https://www.aol.com/mixing-porsche-unveils-gas-powered-160019163.html)  ·  `page_048832`

## Facts

- Porsche originally planned for 80% of its cars to be EVs by the end of the decade, including an all-electric Macan and replacements for the 718 Boxster and Cayman.
- Porsche has backed away from its ambitious electrification plans due to the general EV market not growing as expected and luxury brands like Mercedes and Audi seeing EV sales plummet.
- Taycan EV sales slipped in 2024, which Porsche attributed to supply chain issues and customers waiting for the updated Taycan.
- Porsche unveiled the new Taycan alongside the new gas-powered 911 Carrera T to meet driver demand for both performance-oriented EVs and gas-powered sports cars.
- Porsche North America product specialist Frank Wiesmann stated that a Porsche is meant to be an engaging, practical driver's car and that an electric car can fulfill those roles.
- Wiesmann noted Porsche remains passionate about analog cars like the 911 with manual transmissions and gas engines.
- The new 911 Carrera T features lightweight glass, less sound-deadening material, standard rear-wheel steering, a lower stance, better brakes, and is manual transmission only.
- Porsche reported that 70% of US buyers of the prior Carrera T selected it with a seven-speed manual transmission; the new model uses a six-speed shifter with a sportier short-throw design and new linkages.
- Wiesmann stated the US is Porsche's biggest market for manual transmission vehicles, as purist drivers value the engaging element of shifting themselves.
- The Taycan GTS features Porsche Active Ride suspension to smooth road bumps, new aero and design elements, and nearly 100 more horsepower, totaling 690 hp.
- The Taycan starts at $99,400, with the new GTS trim starting at $149,895.
- The Taycan competes against EVs like the Tesla Model S and Lucid Air, as well as gas-powered cars and hybrids.
- Porsche has adopted a multi-energy strategy including hybrid Cayenne SUVs and Panamera sedans, with the 911 also available in a hybrid Carrera GTS variant.
- Wiesmann explained Porsche's flexible powertrain strategy allows customers to choose EVs, hybrids, or gas engines based on their home charging capabilities and preferences.
- The 2025 911 Carrera T and Taycan GTS will arrive in dealerships next year (2025), with the Carrera T starting at $135,995.
