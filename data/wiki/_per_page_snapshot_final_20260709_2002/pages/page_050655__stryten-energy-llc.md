# Stryten Energy LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-04-20

## Facts

- Stryten Energy's stored energy solutions are built in the U.S. and power applications ranging from forklifts and distribution centers to cars, trains, trucks, microgrids, and telecom networks.
- Stryten Energy manufactures advanced lead, lithium, and vanadium batteries and intelligent chargers, and provides cloud-based software for smart fleet design.
- Stryten Energy installed a lead Battery Energy Storage System (BESS) at the VICARS Community Center in Southwest Atlanta to provide power resiliency.
- Stryten Energy introduced the E-Series AGM160/190 multi-terminal batteries for telecommunications and utility applications, featuring spill-proof designs and suitability for harsh environments and long-duration discharge.
- Stryten Energy developed a mobile microgrid solution designed for remote power and disaster relief.
- At CES 2026, Stryten Energy convened experts to discuss how batteries can address energy demand challenges in hyperscale data centers driven by AI adoption.
- At NAATBatt 2026, Stryten Energy highlighted its domestically sourced and manufactured battery energy storage solutions to meet growing energy demand.
- At RE+ 2025, industry experts discussed technical and economic hurdles in scaling solar-plus-storage systems, including software interoperability and supply chain constraints.
- At CES 2026, Stryten Energy unveiled lead, lithium, and vanadium redox flow battery technologies for grid resilience and renewable integration, with scalable BESS solutions supporting data centers, manufacturing, and EV charging.
- On October 27, 2025, Stryten Energy announced it is delivering battery-first technologies to BCI Flow Batteries North America for commercial, industrial, and utility-scale applications.
- Stryten Energy installed a lead BESS at the Georgia Institute of Technology to support the university's carbon-neutral initiatives alongside existing solar infrastructure.
- On September 4, 2025, Stryten Energy announced it would feature BESS solutions at RE+ 2025 to deliver reliable power and business continuity during outages and peak demand.
- Scott Childers serves as Vice President of Essential Power at Stryten Energy, while Dr. Vick Singh is Chief Operating Officer at Dragonfly Energy; both discussed energy resilience strategies.
- On April 23, 2025, Georgia Tech and Stryten Energy announced the successful installation of a lead battery energy storage system for advanced research in Atlanta.
- Stryten Energy highlighted the development of a domestic lithium supply chain, noting that lithium batteries offer higher energy density, efficiency, cycle life, and calendar life.
- Stryten Energy partnered with Storion Energy to address cost barriers for vanadium redox flow battery long-duration energy storage, offering domestic BESS and vanadium electrolyte.
- Stryten Energy formed a joint venture with Largo to launch Storion Energy in Alpharetta, Georgia, aiming to deliver price-competitive vanadium electrolyte for LDES via a leasing model.
- Stryten Energy noted that the Inflation Reduction Act's Advanced Manufacturing Production Credit (AMPC) provides incentives for domestic clean energy manufacturing investment.
- Stryten Energy cited U.S. power demand projections reaching 4,185 billion kWh in 2025, up from 4,067 billion kWh in 2022.
- On February 12, 2025, Stryten Energy highlighted its technology-agnostic battery-first approach at NAATBatt 2025 in Orlando, Florida, to address increasing U.S. energy demand.
- At CES 2025, Stryten Energy discussed energy resilience as a key innovation area for meeting U.S. energy security and independence goals.
