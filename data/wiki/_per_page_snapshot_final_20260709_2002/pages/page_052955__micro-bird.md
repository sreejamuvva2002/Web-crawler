# Micro Bird

## Facts

- Blue Bird Corporation (Nasdaq: BLBD) is the leader in electric and low-emission school buses.
- Micro Bird is a 50/50 joint venture of Blue Bird Corporation and Girardin.
- Micro Bird has confirmed the purchase of the Nova Bus facility in Plattsburgh, NY.
- Micro Bird plans to begin production at the new facility in mid-2025.
- The investment will enable Micro Bird to double its production capacity to better meet the sustained and growing demand for its full line of school buses, school activity buses, multi-purpose vehicles and commercial buses.
- Micro Bird is poised to create more than 350 full-time jobs over the next several years at its Plattsburgh facility.
- The project is supported by Empire State Development with nearly $10 million in performance-based Excelsior Jobs Program tax credits.
- The project is supported by a $2.5 million capital grant from the North Country Regional Economic Development Council.
- Phil Horlock serves as President and CEO of Blue Bird Corporation.
- Eric Boulé serves as President and CEO of Micro Bird.
- Micro Bird will progressively hire the skilled and experienced employees currently working at Nova Bus Plattsburgh.
- Site preparation will begin in January 2025, including hiring of employees.
- Start of production is scheduled for the summer of 2025.
- Blue Bird Corporation was founded in 1927 and designs, engineers, and manufactures school buses with a singular focus on safety, reliability, and durability.
- School buses carry 25 million children twice a day.
- Blue Bird is the proven leader in low- and zero-emission school buses with more than 20,000 propane, natural gas, and electric powered buses in operation today.
- Girardin is a third-generation family business specializing in school bus transportation for over 65 years.
- Girardin has its head office in Drummondville, Quebec (Canada) for almost 60 years.
- Girardin provides the widest selection of electric school buses together with individualized charging infrastructure assistance.
- Girardin is a manufacturer, distributor, and operator of school buses and electric school buses.
- Girardin's more than 40 operating divisions employ close to 3,000 employees.
