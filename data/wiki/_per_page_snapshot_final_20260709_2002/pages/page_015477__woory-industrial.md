# Woory Industrial

**Type:** supplier_relationship  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Woory Industrial chooses aXiom for Hyundai Genesis GV80 HVAC solution | Electronic Specifier](https://www.electronicspecifier.com/industries/automotive/woory-industrial-chooses-axiom-for-hyundai-genesis-gv80-hvac-solution)  ·  `page_015477`

## Facts

- Woory Industrial selected TouchNetix's aXiom AX54A chip for the heating, ventilation, and air conditioning (HVAC) touch and force controls system in the Hyundai Genesis GV80.
- The aXiom AX54A chip combines high-performance multi-touch sensing with safety-enhancing force sensing technology in one chip.
- This integration helps avoid accidental user interaction by requiring additional intent from users to operate the HVAC controls.
- The single-chip solution saves space on the Printed Circuit Board (PCB) and reduces overall costs for the customer.
- Moon Soo Byeon, Director of Research Engineering at Woory Industrial, stated that TouchNetix’s dedicated engineering support was pivotal from design through to mass production.
- Vegard Wollan, CEO at TouchNetix, noted that TouchNetix is thrilled to be selected by Woory Industrial and Hyundai for the project.
- The aXiom AX54A chip supports 3D proximity and hover sensing for air gestures.
- The chip offers multi-force sensing and low-latency haptic triggering.
- It includes integrated support for passive and active Dial-on-Display.
- The chip is compatible with curved shapes, thick lenses, and finger guiding overlays.
- It is suitable for smart surfaces such as wood, plastic, foam, and leatherette.
- The chip provides parallel multi-touch and multi-force capabilities for safety-enhancing systemic redundancy.
- The aXiom AX54A chip is available and in stock.
- The Genesis GV80 is currently available for sale.
