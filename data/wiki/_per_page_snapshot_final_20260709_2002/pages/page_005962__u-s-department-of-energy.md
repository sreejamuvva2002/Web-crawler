# U.S. Department of Energy

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [Energy Department Announces $500 Million to Strengthen Domestic Critical Materials Processing and Manufacturing | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/articles/energy-department-announces-500-million-strengthen-domestic-critical-materials-processing)  ·  `page_005962`

## Facts

- The U.S. Department of Energy’s Office of Critical Minerals and Energy Innovation announced a Notice of Funding Opportunity for up to $500 million to expand U.S. critical mineral and materials processing and derivative battery manufacturing and recycling.
- Funding will expand domestic manufacturing of battery supply chains for defense, grid resilience, transportation, manufacturing and other industries.
- Assistant Secretary of Energy (EERE) Audrey Robertson is currently in Japan meeting with regional allies at the Indo-Pacific Energy Security Ministerial and Business Forum to advance shared efforts on supply chain resilience and energy security issues.
- Her engagements at the forum underscore the importance of close cooperation with partners as the United States strengthens its supply chain through this NOFO.
- U.S. Energy Secretary Chris Wright stated that for too long, the United States has relied on hostile foreign actors to supply and process the critical materials that are essential in battery manufacturing and materials processing.
- Chris Wright credited President Trump’s leadership for the Department of Energy playing a leading role in strengthening domestic industries that will position the U.S. to win the AI race, meet rising energy demand, and achieve energy dominance.
- Audrey Robertson stated she is delighted to be in Japan meeting with allies, underscoring the important connection between critical materials and energy security.
- Robertson noted that critical minerals processing is a vital component of the nation’s critical minerals supply base.
- Boosting domestic production, including through recycling, will bolster national security and ensure the United States and its partners are prepared to meet the energy challenges of the 21st century.
- Funding awarded through this NOFO will support demonstration and/or commercial facilities for processing, recycling, or utilizing for manufacturing of critical materials.
- Eligible critical materials may include traditional battery minerals such as lithium, graphite, nickel, copper, aluminum, as well as other minerals that are contained within commercially available batteries.
- This is the third round of funding issued through DOE’s Battery Materials Processing and Battery Manufacturing and Recycling programs.
- DOE is seeking projects in Domestic Critical Minerals Processing from Raw Feedstocks to increase U.S. processing capacity for critical minerals and materials for use in advanced batteries.
- DOE is seeking projects in Domestic Critical Materials Recycling to increase recovery of battery critical minerals through recycling of manufacturing scrap and/or off-specification or end-of-life batteries.
- DOE is seeking projects in Domestic Battery Materials and Component Manufacturing to increase domestic manufacturing capacity for strategic battery materials, components and technologies.
- A webinar with additional information on this funding opportunity will be held at 1:00 PM ET on March 26, 2026.
- Non-binding letters of intent are requested by 5:00 p.m. ET on March 27, 2026, to assist the Department in planning for the review process.
- Applications must be submitted by 5:00 p.m. ET on April 24, 2026.
