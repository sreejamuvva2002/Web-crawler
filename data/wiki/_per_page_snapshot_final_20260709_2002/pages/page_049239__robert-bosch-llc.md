# Robert Bosch LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Bosch fosters new-school startup culture - Automotive News](https://www.autonews.com/article/20180731/OEM10/180739889/bosch-fosters-new-school-startup-culture/)  ·  `page_049239`

## Facts

- Robert Bosch LLC is a nearly $14 billion behemoth supplier adapting to the digital age.
- The company is fostering a startup culture in its workforce to help make the transition and accelerate innovation.
- CFO Maximiliane Straub stated, "We have to increase speed in our innovation and become very customer-centric from the beginning."
- Bosch takes an "inside-out and outside-in" approach to fostering new ideas.
- Through an internal accelerator program called Innovation Framework, Bosch employees are encouraged to develop new ideas.
- The company looks at everything from ideation to scaling of the business.
- Teams are encouraged to experiment with ideas and not be afraid to fail or pivot.
- Straub said, "We want them to learn how to fail early and do it with very little money."
- Fostering a startup culture within the organization serves as an employee recruitment and retention tool.
- Teams receive mentoring from top management and get access to marketing, IT and other resources from across the company.
- Bosch has coached about 350 associates so far through the program.
- Associates who participate often come back to become coaches themselves.
- Straub noted that participants "don't get a lot of money, but they get a lot of our time."
- Bosch's innovation efforts are generating financial dividends.
- The company has secured about $106 million in contracted new revenue from the Innovation Framework.
- Fifteen business units have participated in the Innovation Framework.
- Employees bring the innovation culture back to their respective businesses.
- Bosch is partnering with startups, established companies and universities through hackathons and accelerators to develop new products and tap into emerging technologies.
- Straub stated, "It's not just about buying startups, but having cooperation."
- The company co-develops products, has R&D contracts, and often acts as a mentor to partners.
- Last year, Bosch launched the Connectory in downtown Chicago.
- The Connectory functions as part incubator and part co-working space.
- The initiative allows Bosch to tap into the region's startup ecosystem and talent pool.
- Bosch has similar hubs in Stuttgart and Guadalajara, Mexico.
