# Linde + Wiemann

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-29

**Source:** [German Auto Giant Linde + Wiemann Ends Türkiye Partnership](https://autoworldjournal.com/german-auto-giant-linde-wiemann/)  ·  `page_057195`

## Facts

- German auto supplier Linde + Wiemann has concluded its partnership with Türkiye’s Opsan.
- The decision cites global challenges in the automotive sector.
- The Turkish Competition Authority approved the transfer of Linde + Wiemann’s shares in Linde Opsan Otomotiv to the Ersu family on Thursday.
- The Ersu family’s KEB Metal Investment now holds full control of the company.
- Opsan is one of Türkiye’s leading automotive suppliers, instrumental in producing sheet metal and welded assembly parts for the sector.
- Opsan was established in 1963 in Istanbul.
- Opsan initially focused on manufacturing pressure vessels and sheet metal products.
- Opsan transitioned to the automotive sector in 1976, producing chrome-plated bumpers for Oyak-Renault.
- In 2010, Linde + Wiemann partnered with the Ersu family to form Linde Opsan.
- The joint venture launched mass production in 2012.
- Linde Opsan supplies components for projects like the Ford Tourneo and Ford Transit.
- Linde Opsan has facilities spanning 25,000 square meters.
- Linde Opsan has a workforce of 475 employees.
- Linde Opsan produces 130 million spot welds and 55 million fastener welds annually.
- Linde Opsan has collaborated with prominent brands including Türkiye’s domestic electric vehicle (EV) Togg, Ford Otosan, TOFAS, Stellantis, and Magna.
- Linde + Wiemann was founded in 1939 in Dillenburg, Germany.
- Linde + Wiemann operates production facilities in Spain, the Czech Republic, Hungary, China, South Africa, and Türkiye.
- Despite ending its partnership with Opsan, Linde + Wiemann continues to maintain a strong international presence.
