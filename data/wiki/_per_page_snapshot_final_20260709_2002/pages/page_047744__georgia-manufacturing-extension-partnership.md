# Georgia Manufacturing Extension Partnership

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [You asked. We listened. - Enterprise Innovation Institute](https://innovate.gatech.edu/you-asked-we-listened/)  ·  `page_047744`

## Facts

- Manufacturing represents 10 percent of Georgia's state gross domestic product.
- Total manufacturing GDP totaled nearly $62 billion in 2019.
- The manufacturing industry accounts for 92 percent of exports across the state.
- Manufacturing employs more than 380,000 people across Georgia.
- Georgia's location and logistics network allows companies to reach 80 percent of the United States in hours by truck, train, or plane.
- Companies can access the world via Georgia's ports.
- According to the biannual Georgia Manufacturing Survey, over the past 12 years manufacturers have continuously identified marketing as one of their top needs.
- GaMEP is a program of Georgia Tech’s Enterprise Innovation Institute.
- GaMEP has been in operation since 1960 with the singular goal of enhancing global competitiveness for Georgia manufacturers.
- GaMEP's existing service toolkit includes leadership development, technology adaptation, quality systems, energy, sustainability, and implementation of lean tools to improve processes across the plant.
- GaMEP has added marketing services covering everything from strategic plan development through tactical execution and improving a company’s digital presence.
- Katie Takacs serves as the GaMEP group manager for marketing.
- Many small to mid-size manufacturers either don’t have an internal marketing team, have one person supporting marketing, or are project managing multiple agencies.
- Oneda Corp. is a Columbus, Georgia-based precision metal stamping manufacturer primarily for the automotive and heating and air industries.
- Oneda Corp. worked with GaMEP to develop and implement a strategic marketing plan.
- The Oneda project grew to include writing customer success stories, launching a new website with improved usability and content, planning, creating LinkedIn content, and training Michael Brooks to manage social media.
- Michael Brooks is Oneda’s manager of marketing, customer service, and logistics.
- Katie Takacs stated, “What we’ve found is that many small to mid-size manufacturers either don’t have an internal marketing team, have one person supporting marketing, or the company is project managing multiple agencies.”
- Michael Brooks noted, “If you’re not on social media, and you’re not keeping up with that, you will lose your audience,” adding that younger audiences check websites and social postings to determine if content meets their needs before deciding on a business relationship.
