# Hyundai Mobis

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** power_electronics  ·  **Published:** 2022-11-29

**Source:** [Second Automotive Supplier for Hyundai Metaplant In Georgia To Create 1,500 Jobs - CleanTechnica](https://cleantechnica.com/2022/11/29/second-automotive-supplier-for-hyundai-metaplant-in-georgia-to-create-1500-jobs/)  ·  `page_000279`

## Facts

- Hyundai Mobis will invest $926 million in an EV Power Electric system plant in Bryan County.
- The new plant is expected to hire and employ at least 1,500 people.
- This investment expands Hyundai's current operations which currently employ almost 1,200 people.
- Governor Brian Kemp announced the job creation for the state.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that celebrating the groundbreaking of Hyundai’s new electric vehicle and battery manufacturing facility in Bryan County showed it would unleash transformational job creation and investment in that region.
- Governor Brian Kemp noted this is the second supplier announced in just two weeks to locate in that area, expressing excitement for its impact on surrounding communities and other upcoming job creators.
- The EV Power Electric system plant will increase Hyundai Mobis's geographic reach in Georgia.
- Hyundai Mobis serves as the primary Tier 1 supplier to Kia Georgia.
- Hyundai Mobis has operated a manufacturing facility with almost 1,200 workers in West Point since 2009.
- The West Point facility provides bumper assemblies, chassis modules, and whole cockpit modules to the car industry.
- H.S. Oh, Vice President of Hyundai Mobis Electric Powertrain Business Unit, stated the investment reflects an acceleration in the development of the EV supply chain in Georgia’s auto industry.
- H.S. Oh stated Hyundai Mobis will be a major production player in the EV market, which will trigger more growth within the sector.
- H.S. Oh stated Mobis looks forward to providing high-quality work opportunities to the growing local workforce.
- At full capacity, the 1,200,000-square-foot Hyundai Metaplant facility will supply the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) factory in Bryan County, the Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Alabama (HMMA) factory in Montgomery, and the Kia Georgia plant with over 900,000 EV Power Electric systems each year.
- At full capacity, the facility will also supply over 450,000 Integrated Charging Control Units each year to those same factories.
- The new factory will be built in Richmond Hill’s Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) Certified Belfast Commerce Park.
- Construction is scheduled to start in January 2023.
- Manufacturing at the new facility will start in 2024.
- Jon Seagraves, Development Authority of Bryan County Chairman, stated they could not be more excited to welcome Mobis to their community.
- Jon Seagraves cited the close proximity of Belfast Commerce Park to available housing in nearby Heartwood, a top-performing public school system, interstate access, and rail as site advantages.
- Jon Seagraves noted the site was purchased about two years ago with the potential to attract a special project.
- Jon Seagraves highlighted nearly $1 billion of investment and the creation of over 1,500 good-paying jobs as reasons Mobis is special.
- Jon Seagraves expressed gratitude to Bryan County, the City of Richmond Hill, and Raydient Places for their support and partnership on the project.
- The Development Authority of Bryan County, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia Power teamed up on this competitive project.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team represented the state, with Alyce Thornhill as director of project implementation and supplier strategy.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated suppliers like Hyundai Mobis are transitioning to the jobs of the future and becoming part of Georgia’s growing EV ecosystem.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson noted that in May they predicted non-affiliated HMGMA suppliers would invest an additional $1 billion into Georgia, a prediction already blown past in about two weeks.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson expressed excitement to prepare Georgia’s workforce of tomorrow for the jobs being created now with support from partners like Georgia Quick Start and sister agencies.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson congratulated Bryan County, the City of Richmond Hill, the Georgia Ports Authority, and Hyundai Mobis.
- Georgia is positioned as a hub for the electric mobility industry by building on the strengths that the automotive sector offers.
- In Georgia, more than 18,100 jobs have been announced as part of more than 30 EV-related projects that have invested over $13.3 billion since 2020.
- In Bryan County, the first dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility of Hyundai Motor Group — Metaplant America — was fully opened on October 25.
- Off-site suppliers involved in the Metaplant America project are expected to make about $1 billion in investments in other areas across the state.
- Georgia has positioned itself to be a major player in electric vehicles by recruiting at least 51 EV and electric vehicle battery plants so far with billions in incentives in recent years.
- Hyundai Mobis will probably be eligible for various tax credits depending on its new jobs produced and other investments, but specific information on potential incentives was not provided.
- Georgia is home to more than 800 aerospace companies, including Archer Aviation, which will provide over 1,000 jobs.
