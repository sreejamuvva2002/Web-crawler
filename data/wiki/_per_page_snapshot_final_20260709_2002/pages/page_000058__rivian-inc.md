# Rivian Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2021-12-16

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp Welcomes Rivian for Single-Largest Project in State History | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-release/governor-kemp-welcomes-rivian-single-largest-economic-development-project-state)  ·  `page_000058`

## Facts

- Rivian Inc. will invest $5 billion in a carbon-conscious campus in Georgia for its electric adventure vehicles.
- Across operations, Rivian will create approximately 7,500 jobs on just under 2,000 acres at the East Atlanta Megasite.
- The site is represented by the Joint Development Authority of Jasper, Morgan, Newton, and Walton counties.
- Once manufacturing operations are fully ramped up, the Georgia facility will be capable of producing up to 400,000 vehicles per year.
- Rivian’s manufacturing plant represents the single-largest economic development project in state history.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp cited Georgia's long-term focus on logistics, talent, workforce, and responsive government as contributing factors to the announcement.
- Governor Kemp noted his administration's recent emphasis on innovation and development in the electric mobility ecosystem equipped the state for this pivotal announcement.
- Georgia Quick Start workforce training resources are prepared to meet Rivian's production and research and development needs.
- World-class higher education institutions and statewide logistics infrastructure assets are prepared to meet Rivian's production and R&D needs.
- The Electric Mobility and Innovation Alliance is a statewide initiative between government, industries, electric utilities, nonprofits, and other relevant stakeholders focused on growing the electric mobility ecosystem in Georgia.
- Construction on the facility at Stanton Springs North is expected to begin in summer 2022.
- The start of production is slated for 2024.
- Rivian’s technology centers at the new campus will support the company’s research and development programs.
- The entire project will support the expansion of the U.S. electric vehicle industry and decarbonize the transportation and energy sectors.
- In August, Governor Kemp announced the launch of the Electric Mobility and Innovation Alliance.
- Georgia is committed to ensuring existing industry and accompanying 55,000 automotive manufacturing jobs receive support for retraining and expansions to transition to electrified transportation.
- Rivian Chief People Officer Helen Russell stated alongside Illinois, Georgia is the place for Rivian to scale and thrive.
- Rivian has developed and vertically integrated a connected electric platform flexibly applied to adventure products and B2B products like last-mile delivery vans launched in partnership with Amazon.
- Rivian’s launch products are the R1T and R1S.
- The Rivian R1T all-electric truck earned the 2022 truck of the year from MotorTrend.
- Joint Development Authority of Stanton Springs Chairman Jerry Silvio welcomed Rivian to the country’s premier joint economic development partnership.
- The Joint Development Authority of Stanton Springs began over 25 years ago with the goal of creating high-tech, regional-focused jobs for generations.
- Economic development directors Shane Short and Serra P. Hall and attorney Andrea Gray provided dedication to ensure the vision is met.
- Four counties and utility partners planned strategically to ensure minimal impact on the local environment.
- Rivian’s strategic location on the I-20 corridor will enable access to resources and speed products to market.
- Georgia offers 1,200 miles of highway and 5,000 miles of rail.
- The newly opened Mason Mega Rail Terminal at the Port of Savannah is the largest on-terminal rail facility in North America.
- Griff Lynch, Executive Director for the Georgia Ports Authority, stated the authority will expand facilities for processing containers and finished vehicles to provide supply chain solutions to Rivian.
- Russell McMurry, Commissioner of the Georgia Department of Transportation, stated Georgia is recognized as a national leader in EV corridor readiness.
- Mike Hopkins, Executive Director of the Newton County Water and Sewerage Authority, partnered with Rivian and the Joint Development Authority of Stanton Springs for a landmark opportunity.
- The water system is uniquely designed to support citizens, businesses, and the natural environment in the Southeast.
- Georgia’s talent pool is bolstered by one of the largest state university systems in the country.
- Eighty-five accredited public and private universities in the state award more than 132,000 degrees and certificates annually.
- The Technical College System of Georgia consists of 22 colleges offering education and training in numerous industries.
- Metro Atlanta Chamber President and CEO Katie Kirkpatrick stated Metro Atlanta is ideally suited for Rivian’s new operations due to a diverse and highly skilled workforce.
- Georgia Quick Start will build and operate a state-of-the-art manufacturing training center providing a fully customized training program for Rivian’s start-up needs.
- This training initiative will build capacity and curricula within the Technical College System of Georgia to maintain a long-term pipeline of highly skilled employees.
- The Georgia Department of Labor will assist in identifying and recruiting workers.
- Hank Evans, Director of GDEcD’s Aerospace, Defense, and Advanced Manufacturing team, represented GDEcD’s Global Commerce division on this project.
- Partners include the Joint Development Authority (JDA) of Jasper, Morgan, Newton, and Walton Counties; Georgia Ports Authority; Georgia Department of Transportation; Georgia Quick Start; Metro Atlanta Chamber of Commerce; and Georgia Power.
- Pat Wilson, Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner, stated more change in the automotive industry in the next 10 years than in the past 100.
- Within the last five years, 78 automotive-related companies have located or expanded operations in Georgia, creating thousands of new jobs.
- Existing companies in Georgia have expanded portfolios to include parts for EVs.
- Companies from Korea, Germany, Turkey, and the Netherlands have recently announced plans to build EV-related facilities in the state.
- Rivian designs, develops, and manufactures electric vehicles and accessories, selling directly to consumer and commercial markets.
