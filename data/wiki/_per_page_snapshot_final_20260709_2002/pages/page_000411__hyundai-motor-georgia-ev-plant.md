# Hyundai Motor Georgia EV Plant

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hyundai’s $5B EV plant is ahead of schedule. So are its suppliers](https://www.ajc.com/news/hyundais-5b-ev-plant-is-ahead-of-schedule-so-are-its-suppliers/Z6YDB2L3TBF7BENHFT5CZBQASU/)  ·  `page_000411`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group is constructing a $5.54 billion electric vehicle plant.
- The plant site covers nearly 3,000 acres of Bryan County soil, located 30 miles west of Savannah along I-16.
- The factory will be 17 million square feet, which is nine times the size of the Mall of Georgia.
- Construction is months ahead of schedule according to Pat Wilson, Georgia’s top economic development official.
- Hyundai officials are targeting the first half of 2025 for production start.
- Production could begin as early as October 2024.
- The plant will produce battery-powered Genesis, Hyundai, and Kia vehicles.
- Supplier LG Energy Solution is investing $2.1 billion in a battery cell manufacturing facility.
- Supplier SK On is investing $2.6 billion in a battery cell manufacturing facility.
- Supplier Hyundai Mobis is investing $750 million in an automotive components plant.
- Kia is investing $900 million in an EV parts plant.
- The project will create 25,000 jobs directly and indirectly.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp stated the project positions Georgia as a global leader in battery and EV manufacturing.
- Pat Wilson noted Hyundai is building an entire ecosystem for suppliers and workforce development.
- Construction crews are working around the clock to meet the accelerated timeline.
- The site includes infrastructure for logistics, warehousing, and supplier parks.
