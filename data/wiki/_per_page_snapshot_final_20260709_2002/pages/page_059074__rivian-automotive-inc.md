# Rivian Automotive Inc.

## Facts

- Rivian hired Arnhelm Mittelbach as the first manager of its upcoming Georgia plant.
- Arnhelm Mittelbach is a former Mercedes-Benz executive with over two decades of experience.
- Construction on Rivian's new Georgia plant is set to commence early next year.
- The new Georgia plant will house Rivian's next-generation R2 products.
- Rivian's current R1T electric truck and R1S models start at over $70,000.
- Rivian CEO RJ Scaringe stated in June that the R2 lineup will have a starting price of around $40,000 to $45,000.
- The unveiling of Rivian's R2 lineup is expected next year.
- The first production phase of the Georgia plant is slated to begin next year.
- The Georgia plant will have an estimated annual capacity of 200,000 vehicles once fully operational.
- Rivian intends to ramp up the Georgia plant's annual EV capacity to 400,000 by the end of the decade.
- Arnhelm Mittelbach started his role as Georgia plant manager on November 1.
- The Georgia plant manager reports directly to Rivian COO Frank Klein.
- Arnhelm Mittelbach previously served as plant manager at Mercedes-Benz in East London, South Africa.
- Arnhelm Mittelbach previously served as president and CEO of Mercedes-Benz Vans in Charleston, SC.
- Rivian recently launched an apprenticeship program to support staff development at its future plant site in Stanton Springs North, Georgia.
- Rivian plans to employ around 7,500 people at the Georgia plant site by 2030.
- Rivian's Normal, Illinois plant has begun charging every new electric vehicle with 100% clean energy.
