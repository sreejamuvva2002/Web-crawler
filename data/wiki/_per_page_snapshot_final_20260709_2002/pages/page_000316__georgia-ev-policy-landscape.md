# Georgia EV Policy Landscape

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-10-10

**Source:** [Georgia EV Policy | Electrification Coalition](https://electrificationcoalition.org/work/state-ev-policy/georgia/)  ·  `page_000316`

## Facts

- Georgia is becoming a critical state for EV and battery manufacturing.
- Significant investment from automakers, battery suppliers, energy companies, and government funding is driving economic output in the state.
- As of August 2024, $24.4 billion in private investments have been poured into the Georgia EV manufacturing industry.
- These investments were spurred by approximately $347 million in federal funding.
- The investments will result in the creation of more than 26,40o jobs for Georgians.
- Prominent locations of these jobs are shown on a map on the page.
- Investments in Georgia’s EV economy are bolstering American manufacturing and supply chains at a time when China has a head start in the global transition to electric transportation.
- The City of Atlanta developed the 2025 EV Readiness Workbook in close consultation with the Electrification Coalition (EC).
- The workbook serves as a practical guide for property owners, developers, fleet managers, and community partners to accelerate EV readiness.
- Georgia is currently a leader in electric vehicle adoption within the state.
- There are more than 100,000 EV drivers on the road in Georgia.
- The state has more than 2,200 publicly available EV charging stations.
- The National Electric Vehicle Infrastructure (NEVI) program provides federal funds to build a reliable, accessible network of public EV charging stations across all 50 states.
- Georgia’s Department of Transportation was allotted $135 million in NEVI funds to build chargers along major highways and in communities across the state through public-private partnerships.
- NEVI funds were frozen in February 2025.
- The Federal Highway Administration issued updated guidance in August 2025, allowing states to regain access to their allotted funds.
- The new NEVI guidance will streamline the program and provide states with greater flexibility when deciding how to use the funds.
- Georgia submitted an updated state plan for NEVI in September 2025.
- Georgia announced a second round of NEVI awards in November 2025.
- The Electrification Coalition will continue working alongside the state and NEVI awardees to support the construction of a robust national charging network.
- Outdated laws in Georgia currently restrict consumers’ freedom to buy vehicles of their choice.
- These vehicle purchase restrictions are obstructing economic growth and blocking Georgians’ access to the latest automotive technologies.
- The Electrification Coalition’s policy priorities for EV expansion in Georgia include supporting the expansion of medium- and heavy-duty electric vehicle adoption.
- EC priorities also include promoting EV-friendly rates and rate design best practices for charging, including the sale of electricity by kilowatt hours.
- Additional EC priorities involve supporting utility transportation electrification planning and effective implementation of the state’s NEVI plan.
- The EC aims to support effective implementation of federal funding opportunities for school bus and transit electrification.
- The coalition supports direct-to-consumer vehicle sales and service to expand vehicle choices and provide greater access to maintenance.
- The EC opposes punitive EV taxes that discourage EV adoption.
- On August 7, 2025, regional leaders and industry experts gathered at the Savannah Visitors Center in Savannah, GA, for an event titled "How EV Investments Are Powering Prosperity in Georgia".
- This event highlighted the region’s emergence as a national hub for EV innovation, manufacturing, and workforce development.
- On October 10, 2024, the EC hosted a workshop on the future of port and medium- and heavy-duty (MHD) vehicle electrification in Georgia and Florida.
- On October 4, 2023, the EC convened policymakers and industry representatives from across the Southeast in Charlotte, NC, for a Rural Roundtable discussing rural transportation electrification in Florida, Georgia, and the Carolinas.
- The October 4, 2023 roundtable focused on workforce development, the role of utilities in the transition, battery recycling, and related issues.
- On May 24, 2023, the EC hosted a roundtable on vehicle electrification in rural Georgia to discuss opportunities and challenges associated with electric transportation investment and how rural communities can benefit.
