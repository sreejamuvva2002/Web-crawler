# Valeo SA

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-02-26

**Source:** [Latest Company News - Investing.com](https://www.investing.com/news/company-news/valeo-2025-slides-record-cash-flow-amid-automotive-headwinds-93CH-4529257)  ·  `page_012644`

## Facts

- French automotive supplier Valeo SA presented its full-year 2025 results on February 26, 2026.
- Total sales reached €20.9 billion with a 0.5% like-for-like growth.
- Operating margin improved to 4.7% from 4.3% in 2024.
- Free cash flow after net financial interest reached a record €371 million, marking a 50% increase year-over-year.
- Adjusted EBITDA margin reached 14.7%, surpassing the guided range of 13.5-14.5%.
- Operating margin of 4.7% landed within the 4.5-5.5% target range.
- Operating margin increased from 1.7% in 2021 to 4.7% in 2025, driven by gross margin expansion of 1.2 percentage points versus 2024 and 2.3 percentage points versus 2023.
- Selling, general and administrative expenses declined 5% compared to 2024 and 10% versus 2023.
- Net attributable income reached €200 million, up 23% year-over-year.
- The company faced a 43% effective tax rate partly due to cash repatriation taxes.
- Free cash flow after net financial interest evolved from negative €146 million in 2021 to positive €371 million in 2025.
- Improvement stems from higher profitability with adjusted EBITDA reaching 14.7% of sales, capital expenditure reduction of 30% or €348 million versus 2024 to 3.8% of sales, and gross R&D reduction of 7.3% or €193 million year-over-year.
- The company generated €756 million in free cash flow before exceptional restructuring costs, comfortably within the €700-800 million guidance range.
- Net debt declined to €4.0 billion at year-end from €4.2 billion at mid-year.
- The company maintains a leverage ratio of 1.3x and gearing ratio of 121%.
- Valeo holds €2.5 billion in cash and cash equivalents alongside €1.6 billion in undrawn credit lines.
- Valeo secured €24.6 billion in order intake during 2025, with €11.8 billion in the first half and €12.8 billion in the second half.
- The cumulative 2022-2025 order intake ratio reached 1.5x, with Power division representing 38%, Brain 37%, and Light 25% of the total.
- China emerged as a strategic bright spot despite near-term sales challenges.
- The company achieved a 2.8x order intake ratio with Chinese OEMs, with these manufacturers now representing 63% of China order intake compared to 54% of current OEM sales.
- Major contract wins included an innovative dual-layer HVAC system, 5-in-1 power electronics module, pillar-to-pillar head-up display, and new generation dual inverter solution.
- Valeo secured its first €225 million business in Battery Energy Storage Systems (BESS), marking entry into an adjacent growth market.
- The Power division generated €10.5 billion in sales with 2% like-for-like OEM growth, underperforming the market by 2 percentage points.
- Power division operating margin improved significantly to 4.2% from 2.9% in 2024.
- The Brain division reported €5.0 billion in sales with a 6% like-for-like decline in OEM sales, underperforming by 10 percentage points.
- Brain division operating margin compressed slightly to 5.4% from 5.8%, impacted by delayed production starts in North America.
- The Brain division secured key positions in software-defined vehicles through large contracts.
- The Light division posted €5.4 billion in sales with flat like-for-like OEM growth, underperforming by 4 percentage points.
- Light division operating margin reached 5.0% versus 5.5% in 2024.
- The Light division experienced successful product launches in Europe and China with momentum accelerating in the second half.
- Valeo achieved its 2025 ESG objectives across multiple dimensions, earning an A rating from CDP for both climate change and water security.
- CO2 emissions reached 39.4 MtCO2 equivalent with an intensity ratio of 1.88 ktCO2 per million euros.
- The company achieved 27% women representation in management committees and a frequency rate of 0.69 for workplace safety incidents, both meeting 2025 targets.
- Valeo's One site, One cause initiative proved successful, with 92% of suppliers assessed on sustainability practices.
- Valeo maintains strong ESG ratings including MSCI AA, S&P Global 72/100, and Sustainalytics low risk at 17.3.
- For 2026, Valeo projects sales of €20-21 billion with flat organic growth in OEM sales.
- Operating margin is expected to range between 4.7-5.3% for 2026, maintained through price discipline and continued cost reduction.
- Free cash flow after net financial interest is guided above €400 million for 2026 with potential further R&D efficiency gains.
- The company remains focused on its Elevate 2028 strategic plan targeting €22-24 billion in sales, 6-7% operating margin, and over €500 million in free cash flow after net financial interest.
- The Elevate 2028 plan aims to achieve a leverage ratio below 1.0x adjusted EBITDA, positioning Valeo for investment-grade credit metrics.
- The restructuring program continues with confirmed savings of €300 million annual run rate by 2026.
- Restructuring costs in P&L are expected to decline to approximately €20 million in 2026 from €110 million in 2025.
- Cash outflows for restructuring will remain elevated at approximately €150 million before declining to €100 million in 2027.
- Valeo proposed a dividend of €0.44 per share for 2025, continuing the progressive dividend policy that has seen payouts increase from €0.35 in 2021.
- The dividend will be submitted for approval at the May 21, 2026 shareholders meeting.
- Valeo stock declined 1.75% following the results presentation, closing at €13.13.
- Market reaction likely reflects concerns about flat sales guidance for 2026 and ongoing challenges in China, where OEM sales declined 7% like-for-like despite 10% production growth.
- The company faces continued headwinds including supply chain disruptions, particularly component shortages, and market volatility affecting production volumes.
- Geographic market dynamics require careful strategic execution navigating the rapidly evolving Chinese automotive landscape dominated by local manufacturers.
