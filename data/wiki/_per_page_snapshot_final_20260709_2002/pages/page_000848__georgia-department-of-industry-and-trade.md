# Georgia Department of Industry and Trade

**Source:** [Georgia progress - Digital Library of Georgia](https://dlg.usg.edu/record/dlg_ggpd_i-ga-bc500-pi5-bp1-bp7-bv-p-b1-s2)  ·  `page_000848`

## Facts

- Georgia Progress magazine, compiled monthly by the Georgia Department of Industry and Trade, was launched on October 14, 1965, to coordinate federal programs and stimulate state and local economic development.
- Thompson Industries' Metal Products Division at its Valdosta plant is completing a 70,000 sq. ft. structure to fabricate automobile trim and molding, creating 100 new jobs.
- Thermo King Corporation, a Westinghouse division in Louisville, is increasing its floor space by over 30 percent to manufacture refrigeration units for the trucking industry and air conditioners for automobiles, currently employing 180 persons across three shifts.
- Auto Ventshade, Inc. in Chamblee purchased a five and two-tenths acre tract of land for future plant expansion due to increased demand requiring additional production and warehouse space.
- Lockheed-Georgia Company of Marietta is competing with three other companies for a $1 billion plus contract from the Air Force for the construction of the C-5-A supersonic jet, with construction of its new tandem wind tunnel scheduled to begin shortly and complete early in the fall of 1966.
- Southern Iron and Equipment Company of Chamblee manufactures railroad cars and giant gantry cranes, with President Tom C. Campbell participating in a November 25 trade mission to Japan, Hong Kong, Singapore, and Manila to expand international sales.
- The U.S. Rubber Company at Hogansville announced a $400,000 expansion and modernization program for its local plant to offer a wider variety of products at a greatly increased volume.
