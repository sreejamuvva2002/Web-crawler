# Amara Industries

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Amara Industries Acquires Thomson Plastics](https://www.amara.industries/post/amara-industries-acquires-thomson-plastics-1)  ·  `page_011242`

## Facts

- Amara Industries announced the acquisition of Thomson Plastics on Feb 23.
- Thomson Plastics is a recognized leader in large tonnage injection molding in the Southeastern United States.
- The strategic acquisition enhances Amara Industries’ manufacturing footprint, automation capabilities, and technical depth.
- The deal strengthens Amara Industries’ position as a key player in the North American injection molding industry.
- Thomson Plastics has clamping force capacities up to 3,700 tons.
- Thirty percent of Thomson Plastics' fleet exceeds 1,000 tons in clamping force capacity.
- Thomson Plastics is known for its scale, precision, and ability to meet the demands of major customers across multiple facilities.
- Thomson Plastics utilizes advanced robotics and automation to deliver consistent performance and efficiency across high-volume production environments.
- Thomson Plastics’ technical portfolio includes injection molding, gas-assisted molding, over-molding, insert molding, in-mold labeling, robotic part picking, pad printing, and heat transfer decorating.
- Vince Orlando, Co-President at Amara Industries, stated that the addition of Thomson Plastics represents a strategic milestone for the organization.
- The unified company will meet rising demand for high-performance molded products in key sectors including automotive, consumer goods, industrial, and commercial markets.
- The expansion into the Southeastern United States strengthens Amara Industries’ ability to provide customers with faster delivery, greater flexibility, and improved supply chain efficiency.
