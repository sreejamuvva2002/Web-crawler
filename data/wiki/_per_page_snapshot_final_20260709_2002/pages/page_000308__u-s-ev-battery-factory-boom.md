# U.S. EV Battery Factory Boom

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Tracking the EV Battery Factory Boom in U.S. - OCBridge](https://www.ocbridge.ai/tracking-the-ev-battery-factory-construction-boom-across-north-america/)  ·  `page_000308`

## Facts

- In 2019, the U.S. had only two operational EV battery factories, with another two under construction.
- As of the article's publication, there are 32 battery factories either planned, under construction, or already operational across the country.
- The Inflation Reduction Act (IRA), passed in 2022, provided significant incentives including tax credits for EV buyers and manufacturers to invest in U.S.-based facilities.
- Donald Trump's victory in the 2024 elections has introduced uncertainty regarding the future pace of investments and regulatory support for the EV battery sector.
- By 2021, many automakers began announcing plans to build EVs and batteries closer to home to avoid supply chain disruptions like those seen during the COVID-19 pandemic.
- Over $112 billion in investments have been pledged for new plants and facilities by 2030.
- These facilities are expected to produce up to 1,200 gigawatt-hours of battery capacity annually, enough to power about 18 million EVs.
- SK Battery America’s Plant in Commerce, Georgia, received a $2.6 billion investment, started production in 2021, and created 2,600 jobs.
- Hyundai’s SK On facility in Bartow County, Georgia, has a $5 billion investment with production beginning in 2025, generating 8,100 jobs.
- Hyundai’s HMG-LGES Battery JV in Savannah, Georgia, involves a $4.3 billion investment with production starting in 2025 and creating 2,000 jobs.
- Key states seeing massive investments in battery manufacturing capacity include Michigan, Georgia, Tennessee, Kentucky, and Indiana.
- By 2028, over 1,000 gigawatt-hours of annual battery production capacity are expected to be in operation, enough to power 10 million electric vehicles.
- A Financial Times report from August 2024 highlighted that 40% of over 100 clean energy projects, including battery plants, have been delayed.
- By 2028, announced EV battery manufacturing projects are expected to support nearly 30,000 new jobs spread across the Southeast, Midwest, and Southwest regions.
- EPA projections indicate that electric vehicles accounted for 12% of all U.S. vehicles produced in 2023, marking a 70% increase from the previous year.
