# Daesol Ausys

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-08-29

**Source:** [Automotive Supplier Announces $72 Million Investment in Georgia - Tennessee Star](https://tennesseestar.com/news/automotive-supplier-announces-72-million-investment-in-georgia/khousler/2023/08/30/)  ·  `page_010171`

## Facts

- Daesol Ausys, a South Korean automotive supplier, announced it will invest $72 million to establish a new manufacturing facility in Harris County.
- The company specializes in designing and manufacturing motor and electric vehicle interior components and accessories.
- According to the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD), Daesol Ausys is a “key supplier” for Kia Georgia, Hyundai Motor Group, and General Motors.
- The new facility will be located in West Point, Georgia, specifically within the Northwest Harris Business Park.
- The facility will specifically produce luggage boards and covers.
- The project is expected to create more than 140 new jobs in Harris County.
- Operations at the new West Point facility are expected to begin in December 2024.
- Governor Brian Kemp announced that Daesol Ausys Georgia will establish the new manufacturing facility in West Point, Georgia.
- Min Ho Kwon, CEO of Daesol Ausys, stated the company is committed to delivering world-class services and is excited about the project in the Northwest Harris Business Park and continuing partnerships with the State of Georgia and Harris County.
- Governor Brian Kemp noted that West Point has become an automotive capital in its own right and expressed excitement that Daesol Ausys will join other innovators in Harris County.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Georgia’s automotive industry employs over 50,000 hardworking Georgians and will continue to grow as e-mobility suppliers choose all corners of the state for future jobs.
- South Korea was one of the top three international sources of job creation in Georgia during Fiscal Year 2023.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that economic development is driven at the local level and congratulated Harris County and community partners for helping bring Daesol Ausys to West Georgia.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson added that the automotive industry is providing generational change in the region and across the state, and expressed excitement for the opportunities Daesol Ausys will provide to people of Harris County.
