# Panasonic Energy Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-06

**Source:** [Tesla supplier Panasonic breaks ground on 30 GWh EV battery plant in Kansas](https://www.teslarati.com/panasonic-2170-production-groundbreaking-kansas-plant/)  ·  `page_047929`

## Facts

- Tesla battery cell supplier Panasonic broke ground on a new lithium-ion battery manufacturing facility in De Soto, Kansas.
- The facility contributes approximately 30 GWh of annual capacity in North America.
- The facility will focus on rapidly ramping up the manufacture of 2170 cylindrical cells to match surging domestic demand within the United States.
- Panasonic is planning to mass produce the battery cells by March 2025.
- Upon completion, the facility will add 30 GWh of capacity of battery supply to EV companies in the United States.
- Kazuo Tadanobu serves as President and CEO of Panasonic Energy Co., Ltd.
- Tadanobu stated that the groundbreaking is an important step towards expanding production capacity in North America to meet rapidly growing demand for lithium-ion batteries.
- The construction of the additional battery production plant in Kansas reflects Panasonic’s intention to dominate as an EV battery supplier for numerous automotive companies in the United States.
- Panasonic already supplies batteries for Tesla through Gigafactory Nevada.
- The Kansas production facility is expected to funnel cells to Gigafactory Texas, where Tesla utilizes 2170 and 4680 cells for recent Model Y builds.
- Panasonic selected Kansas as the plant location in July after potentially considering land plots in nearby Oklahoma.
- Kansas offered advantages including a diverse talent pool expected to fulfill 4,000 jobs at the factory.
- Panasonic is investing $4 billion into the plant.
- Tadanobu said the facility will help strengthen production capacity in an increasing demand market.
- While only explicitly mentioning 2170 cells, the company is expected to eventually transition to produce 4680 cells at the plant.
- Tesla and Panasonic have been working together to develop the 4680 cell, which Tesla unveiled at its Battery Day event in late 2020.
- Panasonic reportedly sent 4680 samples to Tesla in June.
- Large-scale prototype production of the 4680 cells is expected to launch in Japan in May 2023, according to Tadanobu.
- Tesla issued an open challenge called JUNI x Tesla Battery Cell Giga Challenge targeting startups to bring battery technology solutions directly to Gigafactory Berlin.
- The startup challenge opened applications with a deadline of July 24, 2026.
- The program targets startups with solutions that can make battery cell manufacturing faster, cheaper, safer, and more scalable at an industrial level.
- On May 12, 2026, Giga Berlin plant manager André Thierig announced a $250 million investment to scale the factory’s annual 4680 cell production capacity from 8 GWh to 18 GWh.
- The expansion is expected to create more than 1,500 new jobs.
- Combined with a previously announced battery investment at the Grunheide site, Tesla's total approaches $1.2 billion.
- The challenge looks specifically for startups with proven solutions across five categories: materials, equipment, operations, automation, and artificial intelligence.
- Applications are screened directly by Tesla’s cell manufacturing team in Grunheide, moving through technical discussions, a pitch day, and potentially a paid pilot project.
- Tesla requires applicants to demonstrate working prototypes, test data, or prior pilots before being considered.
- Elon Musk first announced plans for what he called the world’s largest battery cell production facility alongside Giga Berlin in 2020, targeting up to 250 GWh of annual capacity.
- Those initial plans were shelved in 2022 when Tesla shifted its battery investment focus to the United States to take advantage of Inflation Reduction Act incentives.
- The revival of cell production at Giga Berlin is backed by over $1 billion in committed capital.
- The 4680 format is central to Tesla’s long-term cost reduction strategy across vehicles, energy storage, the Tesla Semi, and Cybercab.
- Tesla demonstrated its Cybercab robotaxi at the National Federation of the Blind Annual Convention in Austin, Texas on July 3.
- The Cybercab demonstration highlighted accessibility features including Braille lettering on physical controls, space for service animals, and wheelchair-height seating.
