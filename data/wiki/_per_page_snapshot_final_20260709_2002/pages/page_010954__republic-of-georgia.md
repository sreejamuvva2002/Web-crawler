## Facts

- Manufacturing GDP is projected to rise by 2.5% over the course of 2023.
- Employment levels in manufacturing are exceeding those last seen in 2008.
- EU industrial production went up 8% in 2021 compared with 2020, and increased by another 5% in 2022 compared with 2021.
- A National Association of Manufacturers survey found only 67% of US manufacturing directors were positive about their company’s prospects, down from nearly 75% in Q1 2023.
- A Reuters poll in April showed optimism among EU decision makers slipped for a third straight month due to gloomy production expectations.
- Concerns around supply chain resilience, legislative burdens, and talent shortages have left many manufacturers reluctant to invest.
- Georgia has risen as a national leader in advanced manufacturing.
- Output growth is currently in double-digits for critical sectors including machinery, electrical equipment, components, and fabricated metals.
- The country boasts an impressive $8bn yearly output driven by a workforce of nearly 152,000 workers.
- More than 5,000 students are enrolled in engineering, manufacturing, and construction programmes to shore up the future workforce.
- Georgia is situated at the crossroads of Europe, Asia, and the Middle East, providing unparalleled access to key markets for swift distribution and efficient logistics.
- A service-oriented approach to customs and seamless trade is a top policymaking priority.
- The FDI grant programme offers cashback for both investment costs and staff training expenses.
- Georgia operates a flat tax economy with corporate profit rates of just 15%.
- Retained and reinvested profit in Georgia is subject to 0% tax.
- The World Bank has celebrated Georgia as the third least tax-burdened nation globally.
- Recent reforms have slashed the business incorporation process to two mandatory documents and reduced licensing requirements from over 900 to 150.
- Georgia enjoys unparalleled energy independence with renewables, primarily hydropower, dominating its electricity mix.
- Strategic partnerships with Azerbaijan guarantee businesses a stable, cost-efficient supply of energy regardless of demand or timing.
- Kutaisi Auto Mechanical Plant specializes in crafting spare parts for agricultural machinery, ropeways, railways, and mining equipment.
- Tbilisi Aircraft Manufacturing (TAM) produces, overhauls, and repairs military aircraft and creates various military products.
- Elmaval-Mshenebeli is a regional leader in the assembly of electric vehicles.
- Native companies producing microchips, home appliances, and machinery are rapidly expanding.
- AIONRISE, an industry leader in high-efficiency photovoltaic solar modules, operates the largest manufacturing facility under a single roof in Georgia.
- French conglomerate Groupe Atlantic has extended operations to Georgia to produce electric heaters for domestic consumption and international export.
- Between 2016 and 2022, electronics exports rose from $20m to more than $110m according to the UN Comtrade database.
- Machinery exports surged from around $30m to nearly $140m between 2016 and 2022.
- Durbek Fattakhov, CEO of AIONRISE, states that Georgia offers the best advantages compared to other central and eastern European countries due to low utility costs and no bureaucracy.
