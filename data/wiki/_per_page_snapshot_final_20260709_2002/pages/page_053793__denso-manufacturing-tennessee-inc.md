# DENSO Manufacturing Tennessee, Inc.

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2015-08-25

**Source:** [DENSO Manufacturing Tennessee, Inc. to Expand Maryville Operations - www.blountpartnership.com](https://blountpartnership.com/denso-manufacturing-tennessee-inc-to-expand-maryville-operations/)  ·  `page_053793`

## Facts

- DENSO Manufacturing Tennessee will expand its automotive components and systems manufacturing plant in Maryville, making it one of the largest operating campuses in DENSO Corporation’s worldwide operations.
- DENSO Manufacturing Tennessee has a workforce of more than 3,100 employees and is Blount County’s largest employer.
- The company will create 500 new jobs as part of the expansion.
- The company will invest $400 million to expand its manufacturing capabilities and construct a new warehouse.
- Due to expanded production needs at two of the company’s manufacturing plants, the existing warehouse will be converted to manufacturing space.
- A new consolidated warehouse will be built on the company’s current campus.
- Tennessee Gov. Bill Haslam congratulated DENSO on its expansion and the creation of 500 new jobs in Maryville and Blount County.
- Gov. Haslam stated the announcement indicates momentum Tennessee has in the automotive manufacturing sector and that DENSO supports numerous families throughout East Tennessee.
- TN Economic and Community Development Commissioner Randy Boyd praised DENSO as an outstanding corporate citizen in Tennessee.
- Commissioner Boyd noted this is the second expansion DENSO has made at a Tennessee facility in less than a year since he became commissioner.
- DENSO Manufacturing Tennessee President Jack Helmboldt, who is also a DENSO Corporation global executive director, credited skilled associates and thanked state/local leaders, the Blount Chamber Partnership, and TVA for their support.
- DENSO Manufacturing Tennessee produces starters, alternators, instrument clusters, and a wide range of electronic components and systems as a key supplier to the automotive industry.
- The Maryville campus consists of four manufacturing plants on 194 acres and has undergone eight major expansions.
- U.S. Sen. Lamar Alexander stated the DENSO plant was "bursting at seams" last year.
- Sen. Alexander noted the auto industry has spread into 80 of Tennessee’s 95 counties and is the main contributor to raising family incomes in the state.
- Blount County Mayor Ed Mitchell highlighted collaborative efforts by the Blount Partnership, the state of Tennessee, and the cities of Alcoa and Maryville.
- Maryville City Mayor Tom Taylor described DENSO as a great community partner impacting jobs and giving back to the community.
- To celebrate its 25 years in Maryville, DENSO Manufacturing Tennessee donated $50,000 to Blount Memorial Hospital and $50,000 to East Tennessee Children’s Hospital.
- Alcoa City Mayor Don Mull welcomed the opportunity to sustain DENSO's success for the next 25 years.
- TVA Senior Vice President of Economic Development John Bradley congratulated DENSO on adding 500 new jobs and noted partnership with TN ECD and Blount Partnership.
- Blount County Economic Development Board Chairman Fred Lawson stated the investment nearly doubles the capital investment Blount County has enjoyed since 2010.
- Maryville and Blount County are represented by Sen. Doug Overbey, Rep. Art Swann, and Rep. Bob Ramsey in the Tennessee General Assembly.
- DENSO Corp. is headquartered in Kariya, Aichi prefecture, Japan.
- DENSO is a leading global automotive supplier of advanced technology, systems, and components in thermal, powertrain control, electronics, information, and safety.
- DENSO's customers include all the world’s major carmakers.
- Worldwide, DENSO has more than 200 subsidiaries and affiliates in 38 countries and regions.
- DENSO employs more than 140,000 people worldwide.
- DENSO's consolidated global sales for the fiscal year ending March 31, 2015, totaled US$35.9 billion.
- DENSO spent 9.2 percent of its global consolidated sales on research and development in the last fiscal year.
- In North America, DENSO employs more than 22,000 people at 30 consolidated companies and affiliates.
- Of DENSO's North American facilities, 25 are manufacturing facilities located in the United States, Canada, and Mexico.
- In the United States alone, DENSO employs more than 14,000 people in California, Michigan, North Carolina, South Carolina, Tennessee, Kentucky, Georgia, Iowa, Ohio, Pennsylvania, Texas, and Arkansas.
- DENSO’s North American consolidated sales totaled US$8.0 billion for the fiscal year ending March 31, 2015.
- The Tennessee Department of Economic and Community Development's mission is to develop strategies to make Tennessee the No. 1 location in the Southeast for high quality jobs.
- Tennessee was named 2014 and 2013 “State of the Year” for economic development by Business Facilities magazine.
