# Volvo Cars

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-23

**Source:** [Volvo to build new hybrid model in US amid Trump tariff shift](https://www.usatoday.com/story/cars/news/2025/09/23/volvo-new-hybrid-model-in-us/86315134007/)  ·  `page_014066`

## Facts

- Volvo Cars announced it will begin producing a new hybrid model in the United States by the end of this decade.
- The production shift is part of Volvo's strategy to adjust to President Donald Trump's tariff policies on foreign cars.
- The new hybrid model details have not been released but will be designed specifically for the U.S. market.
- Producing the new model aims to boost capacity utilization at Volvo's South Carolina plant.
- Automakers worldwide are rethinking production strategies in response to Trump's duties on foreign cars and his policy to boost U.S. manufacturing.
- In April, Volvo Cars' CEO stated the company would produce more vehicles in the U.S.
- In July, Volvo announced plans to start producing its XC60 mid-size SUV at the South Carolina plant starting in late 2026.
- The South Carolina plant currently produces only the fully electric EX90 SUV and the Polestar 3.
- Production data indicates that current models utilize only a small fraction of the South Carolina plant's annual capacity of 150,000 vehicles.
- The announcement coincided with Volvo's 70th anniversary in the United States.
- Volvo previously aimed to phase out all non-electric models by 2030 but reversed course last year to keep hybrids in its lineup.
- Luis Rezende, president of Volvo Cars' Americas operation, stated: "By adding another model into production here, we'll take a big step toward realizing the full potential of our local manufacturing investments and workforce."
- A July trade deal with the European Union reduced U.S. tariffs on European cars to 15%, but the agreement has not yet taken effect.
- Vehicles continue to face a 27.5% tariff levy while awaiting the implementation of the EU trade deal.
