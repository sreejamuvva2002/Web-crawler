# Hyundai-LG Energy Solution Battery Plant

**Type:** Facility  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[battery-cell-manufacturing|Battery Cell Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** Ellabell, Georgia, Georgia, United States

## Overview

The Hyundai-LG Energy Solution battery plant under construction in Ellabell, Georgia was the target of a large immigration raid that halted work on the $4.3 billion EV battery project.

## Key facts

- The raid halted work on the $4.3 billion EV battery project.
- Most detained workers were South Korean nationals employed by subcontractors rather than Hyundai or LG themselves.
- It was the largest single-site immigration raid in HSI history.
- Diplomatic talks between U.S. and South Korean officials led to charter flights repatriating many detainees.
- President Trump said: “Your Investments are welcome… What we ask in return is that you hire and train American Workers.”
- Hyundai Motor Group stated it has a “zero tolerance” policy for unlawful employment and will review contractor vetting.
- The United Auto Workers condemned Hyundai for unsafe conditions and the use of immigrant labor, urging OSHA and NLRB oversight.
- Experts estimate unauthorized labor accounts for about 23% of construction workforces on large projects.
- VisaVerge.com advises firms to map contractor networks and assign clear compliance owners.
- The raid signals tougher enforcement across contracting chains for major foreign-invested sites under Trump’s second term.
- Work stoppage may delay the battery plant project and impact the local economy in Ellabell and surrounding communities.
