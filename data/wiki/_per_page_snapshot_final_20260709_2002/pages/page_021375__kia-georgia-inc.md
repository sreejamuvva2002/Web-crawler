# Kia Georgia Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2018-08-11

**Source:** [Kia Georgia, Hyundai Mobis $11.5M TN Visa Settlement](https://www.claimdepot.com/settlements/mobis-al-settlement)  ·  `page_021375`

## Facts

- Employees recruited as TN visa holders by Total Employee Solution Support LLC, SPJ Connect Inc., or GB2G Inc. (d/b/a Allswell) were assigned to work at Mobis Alabama LLC d/b/a Hyundai Mobis or Kia Georgia Inc. at their West Point, Georgia locations.
- Kia Georgia Inc., Mobis Alabama LLC d/b/a Hyundai Mobis, GB2G Inc. d/b/a Allswell, SPJ Connect Inc., and Youngjin Lee agreed to pay $11.5 million to settle a class action lawsuit alleging they misrepresented job terms and violated state and federal statutes affecting TN visa workers at their Georgia facilities.
- The class includes approximately 614 individuals, including the named plaintiffs.
- Class members are divided into four groups based on days worked: Group 1 (1-60 days), Group 2 (61-120 days), Group 3 (121-400 days), and Group 4 (more than 400 days).
- Under the FLSA settlement, each eligible class member receives $30 per week worked at Hyundai Mobis or Kia Georgia between Aug. 11, 2019, and the present.
- The average class member worked 54 weeks, resulting in an average payment of $1,672.61.
- Half of the FLSA settlement payment is reported as W-2 wages with tax withholding, and half is reported as 1099 liquidated damages with no withholding.
- Group 1 class members receive $4,112.50 for working 1-60 days under the Rule 23 settlement.
- Group 2 class members receive $8,000 for working 61-120 days under the Rule 23 settlement.
- Group 3 class members receive $16,800 for working 121-400 days under the Rule 23 settlement, minus $2,500 if assigned after Dec. 31, 2023.
- Group 4 class members receive $11,100 for working more than 400 days under the Rule 23 settlement, minus $2,500 for 100 members assigned after Dec. 31, 2023.
- All Rule 23 settlement payments are reported as IRS Form 1099 income.
- The defendants denied all wrongdoing but agreed to settle to avoid the uncertainty and expense of continued litigation.
- The class action lawsuit alleged that Kia Georgia Inc., Mobis Alabama LLC d/b/a Hyundai Mobis, GB2G Inc. d/b/a Allswell, SPJ Connect Inc., and Youngjin Lee misrepresented job terms and wages to TN visa holders recruited to work at their West Point, Georgia facilities.
- Plaintiffs claimed the defendants violated federal and Georgia RICO statutes and federal wage laws under the Fair Labor Standards Act.
- The $11,500,000 settlement fund includes $3,450,000 allocated for attorneys' fees and costs, with the remainder going to eligible class members.
- Settlement administration costs are not specified in the agreement.
- Class members must submit a settlement distribution form online or via mail/email to the settlement administrator.
- The settlement administrator is Atticus Administration, located at P.O. Box 64053, Saint Paul, MN 55164.
- A fairness hearing is scheduled for May 29, 2026.
- The deadline to submit a request for exclusion from the class is May 14, 2026.
- Payouts will be distributed on or before the later of 60 days after the final settlement date or 60 days after receipt of the settlement distribution form.
- Payout options include interbank electronic transfer (U.S. bank account preferred), wire transfer for international recipients, and paper checks sent via FedEx, UPS, or U.S. First Class Mail.
- Class members must provide name, address, date of birth, gender, Social Security number, residency status, bank/payment details, Medicare beneficiary status, signature, and date to file a claim.
