# Kia

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Kia Accelerates Plans for EV9 Production in the U.S. For $7,500 EV Tax Credit | EV.com](https://ev.com/news/kia-accelerates-plans-for-ev9-production-in-the-u-s-for-7500-ev-tax-credit)  ·  `page_010188`

## Facts

- Kia announced plans to commence production of its EV9 electric SUV in the United States by May.
- The move aims to capitalize on growing U.S. EV demand and qualify for the country’s federal EV tax credit.
- The EV9 is Kia’s first three-row electric SUV, which debuted in the U.S. late last year.
- Over 3,800 units of the EV9 have been delivered since its launch.
- The EV9's starting price is under $55,000.
- The EV9 won the 2024 World Car of the Year and World Electric Car awards at the New York Auto Show.
- Kia is relocating EV9 production from South Korea to its West Point, Georgia plant.
- Production in Georgia is expected to make the EV9 eligible for the full $7,500 federal EV tax credit by early 2025.
- James Bell, Kia America’s head of corporate communications, stated that the EV9’s battery is made in South Korea and China.
- Moving production to the Georgia plant will require that 50% of the value of battery components be produced or manufactured in North America.
- Kia acknowledges the importance of incentives for driving consumer EV adoption and aims to compete with established players like Tesla.
- Kia is not deterred by competition from cheaper EVs rolling in from China, viewing new market entrants as a way to accelerate the transition to electric mobility.
- Kia is offering a $5,000 customer cash incentive on all 2024 EV9 trims to offset the EV tax credit.
- The EV9’s starting price of $54,900 (excluding $1,495 destination fees) is reduced to $51,395 with the incentive.
- Kia expects full U.S. production and tax credit eligibility by 2025 to solidify its position in the growing EV landscape.
