# Panasonic Automotive Systems Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Entering the Era of Choosing Mobility Based on the Experience It Offers The Future of “Joy in Motion” design and Car Interior UX | Panasonic Automotive Systems Co., Ltd.](https://automotive.panasonic.com/en/studio/story/08)  ·  `page_047920`

## Facts

- Panasonic Automotive Systems Co., Ltd. is applying its “Joy in Motion” design perspective to create new mobility value by focusing on the riding and driving experience rather than just vehicle specifications.
- The company develops In-Vehicle Infotainment (IVI) systems, which include car navigation systems and audio systems used to provide information and entertainment inside vehicles.
- User evaluations show that IVI quality and reliability significantly affect customer satisfaction with vehicles.
- Panasonic Automotive Systems aims to provide consistent user experiences across software-defined vehicles (SDVs) instead of competing based on the number of added functions.
- The company is dedicating efforts to Human Insight Technology to understand individual users and make vehicle interiors more comfortable, coordinating with universities and other partners for this research.
- Panasonic conducts fundamental research into how the brain recognizes visual information through sensation sensors to inform interface design.
- The company designs vehicle interfaces to capture no more attention than necessary, creating a “low distraction” state that reduces driver frustration and stress.
- Panasonic Automotive Systems is developing Unified HMI technology to integrate vehicle instrument panels, heads-up displays, and monitors into a cohesive framework.
- Unified HMI aims to eliminate hesitation or confusion in the vehicle interior as screen numbers increase and will be opened up to others to improve development efficiency.
- The company plans to use AI to support users by automating recommendations through emotional and action inferencing, reducing the need for instruction manuals.
- AI integration strategies include using facial recognition or smartphone Bluetooth for authentication, automatically adjusting GUIs per driver, enabling individual seat audio zones, prioritizing alerts over music based on driver focus, and using thermography sensing to automatically adjust seat temperatures.
- Panasonic Automotive Systems leverages its strengths in software and computing, which are vital for the development of software-defined vehicles (SDVs).
- The company plans to expand its contributions beyond traditional cars to total mobility experiences, including Mobility as a Service (MaaS), which uses apps for transportation stages like searching, reservations, and payment.
- Masashige Mizuyama serves as Representative Director, Executive Vice President, and Chief Technology Officer (CTO) at Panasonic Automotive Systems Co., Ltd.
- Keiko Ohta serves as Executive Officer and Japan Chief Executive Officer (Japan CEO) at Panasonic Automotive Systems Co., Ltd.
