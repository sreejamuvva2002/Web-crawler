# Kubota Manufacturing of America Corporation

## Facts

- Kubota Manufacturing of America was formed in 1988 as Kubota’s North American manufacturing base.
- The company manufactures over 200,000 units across its five main manufacturing facilities.
- KMA manufactures and assembles lawn tractors, zero-turn mowers, sub-compact tractors, and utility vehicles.
- The Gainesville/Jefferson facility offers 3.2 million feet of manufacturing space.
- The company employs over 3500 employees.
- Approximately one-half of all Kubota branded equipment sold in the United States is manufactured or assembled in the state of Georgia.
- KMA operates a Work Based Learning Program to train individuals without prior skills.
- Eric Ramey serves as Maintenance Supervisor at KMA in Jefferson, having started his career there in 2012 as a high school junior and intern.
- Eric Ramey progressed through roles including High School Intern, Maintenance Apprentice, Mechanical Technician, Electrical Technician, and Maintenance Lead before becoming Maintenance Supervisor.
- Eric Ramey stated, “Kubota is like a family and all of our team members look after each other.”
- Brian Arnold serves as President of KMA.
- Kevin Strike, Ryan Owen, and Yuichiro Inoue are listed among KMA's leadership team.
- KMA leaders bring decades of industry experience and proven expertise to drive progress.
- Brian Arnold stated, “Through the products we produce we are able to delight our customers and provide bright futures for our team members. It’s both a pleasure and great honor to serve in this role.”
