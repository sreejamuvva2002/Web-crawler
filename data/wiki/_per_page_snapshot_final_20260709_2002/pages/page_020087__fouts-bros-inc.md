# Fouts Bros. Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-09-26

**Source:** [Fouts Brothers to expand in Milledgeville and Baldwin County](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/fouts-brothers-expand-milledgeville-baldwin-035900163.html?fr=sycsrp_catchall)  ·  `page_020087`

## Facts

- Fouts Bros. Inc. moved its company headquarters to Milledgeville from Smyrna a few years ago.
- Scott Edens serves as the president and chief executive officer of the company.
- The company was founded in 1952 by C.J. Fouts, who ran day-to-day operations until his passing.
- The company manufactures fire and fire rescue trucks.
- When Edens first arrived in Milledgeville, the facility was building 150 trucks annually.
- Edens set a goal to double annual truck production to 300 as soon as operations got underway.
- The company recently announced a local expansion that will bring new jobs and investment to the community.
- Sharon Seymour serves as chairwoman of The Development Authority of Milledgeville and Baldwin County (DAMBC).
- Jonathan Jackson serves as executive director of DAMBC.
- The company is rapidly becoming one of the fastest-growing employers in Milledgeville and Baldwin County.
- During a DAMBC board meeting, Jackson shared that the expansion includes $4 million in investment for this year and next.
- The company is adding 96 jobs in 2023.
- The company forecasts adding 70 more jobs in 2024.
- These employment figures will bring the company's total workforce to nearly 500 employees, making it one of Baldwin County's top employers.
- The company is currently using only about half of the space at its facility, which was formerly home to Rheem.
- Fouts Bros. has plans to build on the back 30 acres of the property due to business expansion.
- County commissioners recently toured the facility and observed impressive fire trucks and rescue vehicles being built.
- Employees at the facility are making good wages.
- The site was previously a Brownfield site requiring well monitoring and sub-surface ventilation due to Rheem's operations.
- Through cooperation between Fouts Bros. and Rheem, the company is no longer required to monitor groundwater or perform underground ventilation for the plant.
- Fouts Bros. continues to conduct environmental work at the site.
- The property was removed from the Georgia Hazardous Sites list, serving as a textbook example of how Georgia's Brownfield program works through the EPD.
- Jackson noted that while hiring has been a challenge for the company, the local community is better suited to handle it than their previous location.
