# Aurubis

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Stop Aurubis - Savannah Riverkeeper](https://www.savannahriverkeeper.org/aurubis.html)  ·  `page_017271`

## Facts

- German company Aurubis Richmond has filed a request with the Georgia Department of Environmental Protection (GA-EPD) for a permit that would allow the Aurubis to open a copper smelting plant in South Augusta.
- Aurubis is asking Georgia’s Environmental Protection Division for permission to release toxins into Augusta’s air, including more than 30 dangerous chemicals.
- The requested chemical releases include heavy metals, high amounts of particulate matter, arsenic, and o-xylene.
- The proposed facility would be located on Valencia Way in South Augusta.
- The site location is in a community that is over 80% African-American.
- The South Augusta area already suffers from relic heavy metal toxicity, high particulate matter pollution, and numerous still-contaminated abandoned sites.
- According to the EPA’s National Air Toxic Assessment, the Augusta area has a 95 to 99 percent cancer risk rate.
- This cancer risk rate ranks Augusta as high as cities like Atlanta and New York.
- Savannah Riverkeeper’s Research and Advocacy Unit identified federal Resource Conservation and Recovery Act (RCRA) violations filed against Aurubis.
- Those RCRA violations at Aurubis’ Buffalo, New York facility indicate that the company’s handling of toxic chemicals was unsafe, presenting a danger to the local community and employees.
- The United States Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) considers Aurubis a “significant” violator.
- Augusta’s air quality is ranked 28th in the nation for year-round particulate pollution, which is worse than its previous ranking of 36th on the American Lung Association’s list.
- Tonya Bonitatibus, executive director of Savannah Riverkeeper, stated that the community cannot afford more contamination and does not want known polluters like Aurubis as neighbors in exchange for a few jobs.
- Tonya Bonitatibus also stated that the South Augusta community and Augusta’s future do not need known polluters as neighbors and deserve better.
