# Volvo Cars

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-07-28

**Source:** [Volvo Cars to produce new US model amid tariff pressure](https://www.streetinsider.com/Reuters/Volvo+Cars+to+produce+new+US+model+amid+tariff+pressure/25362723.html)  ·  `page_014167`

## Facts

- Volvo Cars will begin producing a new hybrid model in the United States by the end of this decade.
- The new model details have yet to be released but will be designed for the U.S. market.
- The new model is intended to help Volvo boost capacity utilization at its South Carolina plant.
- Volvo Cars CEO Hakan Samuelsson stated, “It will take time until America is electric everywhere.”
- Samuelsson described the larger hybrid model as "more an electric car, but with a Plan B. When the battery is flat, you have a combustion engine kicking in, but normally you'd drive it electric."
- Auto makers worldwide are scrambling to rethink production strategies to contend with President Donald Trump's duties on foreign cars, part of his wider policy to boost U.S. manufacturing.
- Volvo Cars' CEO announced in April that the company would produce more vehicles in the U.S.
- The group announced in July that it plans to start producing its popular XC60 mid-size SUV at the South Carolina plant from late 2026.
- The South Carolina factory currently builds only Volvo's fully electric EX90 SUV and the Polestar 3.
- Production data shows that the EX90 and Polestar 3 take up only a small fraction of the site's annual capacity of 150,000 vehicles.
- The announcement coincided with Volvo's 70th anniversary in the United States.
- Volvo, long positioned as an EV trailblazer with the aim of phasing out all non-electric models by 2030, last year reversed course and said hybrids would remain part of its lineup.
- Luis Rezende, president of Volvo Cars' Americas operation, stated, "By adding another model into production here, we'll take a big step toward realising the full potential of our local manufacturing investments and workforce."
- A trade deal struck in July with the European Union cut U.S. tariffs on European cars to 15%, but the agreement has yet to take effect, with vehicles continuing to face a 27.5% levy.
- Samuelsson said the 10% duties eliminated on auto imports from the U.S. into Europe will make selling its U.S.-produced EX90 vehicle abroad more attractive.
- Samuelsson added regarding Volvo's operations in Europe, "With this 10% reduction, we have the possibility to grow."
