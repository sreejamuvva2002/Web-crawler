# Georgia Department of Economic Development

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2022-12-31

**Source:** [Key Industries Sustain Momentum for State's Economic Development - Albany CEO](https://albanyceo.com/features/2023/01/gov-kemp-key-industries-sustain-momentum-states-economic-development/)  ·  `page_055038`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp, in conjunction with the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD), announced that Georgia sustained momentum through economic development projects in key industries during the first half of Fiscal Year 2023 (July 1 through December 31, 2022).
- The 218 locations or expansions supported by GDEcD’s Global Commerce division are expected to create approximately 17,500 new jobs.
- These projects represent more than $13 billion in investment for the state.
- Both the number of locations and total investment amount increased over the same period last year.
- Areas outside the 10-county metro Atlanta region accounted for 85 percent of jobs created and 92 percent of investments from economic development projects during the period.
- Four of Georgia’s largest industries – advanced manufacturing, automotive, aerospace, and food processing – accounted for nearly 75 percent of new jobs and 84 percent of investments.
- Planned investments resulting from these projects increased by more than 38 percent compared to the same time last year.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson highlighted proven solutions such as the job training program Georgia Quick Start and GRAD certified sites that support accelerated project timelines.
- The automotive industry was the second-largest source of new jobs and investment, after advanced manufacturing.
- In October, state and local officials joined company representatives, including Korean Ambassador to the U.S. Tae-yong Cho, for the groundbreaking of the new Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) EV facility.
- The HMGMA EV facility will support more than 8,100 direct jobs in Bryan, Bulloch, Chatham and Effingham Counties.
- Three off-site HMGMA suppliers announced locations following HMGMA’s groundbreaking.
- These supplier additions swiftly surpassed the initial $1 billion in investment predicted to come from them.
- International companies accounted for 23 percent of state projects since July 1, 2022.
- Historic project examples include the partnership between SK On and Hyundai Motor Group in Bartow County.
- Next-generation developer FREYR Battery announced a Coweta County facility.
- Both the SK On/Hyundai partnership and the FREYR Battery facility are in the top five largest projects by investment in the state’s history.
- Battery recyclers Sung-Il Hitech and Denkai America joined Georgia’s growing electric mobility ecosystem.
- The aerospace and defense industries continued to benefit from Georgia’s logistics infrastructure and engineering talent pool, creating more than 1,500 new jobs.
- Archer Aviation Inc., an aerospace company advancing sustainable urban air mobility, announced a facility near airports.
- Anduril Industries, a defense technology company, announced a facility near airports.
- Both companies will benefit from nearby top-ranked technical colleges and four-year universities.
- Major investments or expansions announced thus far in FY 2023 include YKK AP in Macon.
- Beretta Holding S.A. announced a new facility in Bryan County through its subsidiary Norma Precision Inc.
- The advanced manufacturing sector has announced more than 7,700 new jobs in the state since July 1, 2022.
- Germany-based Haering Precision celebrated the grand opening of its Lavonia facility in November with a two-day event.
- The Haering Precision event included a “Future Day” for approximately 400 local high school students and members of the community.
- Companies in the food processing industry announced more than $695 million in investments, largely through expansions.
- King’s Hawaiian®, a Georgia partner for more than a decade, announced another expansion of its Oakwood facility.
- This marks King's Hawaiian's second expansion in less than a year.
- GDEcD Deputy Commissioner for Global Commerce Kristi Brigman noted that working with the Georgia Quick Start program and letting students see jobs in-person helps companies build talent from the ground up.
- These programs help Georgians upskill, including those part of Georgia’s strong inbound domestic migration trends.
- Two industries with rapidly growing footprints in Georgia -- life sciences and film -- welcomed additional facilities that will support industry breakthroughs and learning opportunities for students.
- Georgia Tech hosted a groundbreaking for its Science Square purpose-built district in August.
- The Rowen Foundation’s 2,000-acre science and learning campus broke ground in early December.
- Athena Studios announced it will add an additional 350,000 square feet to its Athens facility.
- The Athena Studios facility includes a structure dedicated to training the local workforce through a partnership with the University of Georgia and Georgia Film Academy.
- At the former Fort Gillem outside of Atlanta, BlueStar Studios announced more than 600,000 square feet of new stage space.
- BlueStar Studios will also add an additional 45,000 square feet dedicated to technical resources and amenities, which will include refurbished buildings that preserve the base’s historic architecture.
