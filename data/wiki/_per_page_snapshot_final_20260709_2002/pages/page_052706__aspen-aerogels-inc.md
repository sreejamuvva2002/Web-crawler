## Facts

- Aspen Aerogels designs, develops, manufactures, and sells aerogel materials primarily for use in the energy industrial, sustainable insulation materials, and electric vehicle (EV) markets in the United States, Canada, Asia, Europe, and Latin America.
- The company operates through two segments: Thermal Barrier and Energy Industrial.
- Aspen offers PyroThin aerogel thermal barriers for use in battery packs in EVs.
- Aspen offers Pyrogel XTE for refineries and petrochemical facilities.
- Aspen offers Pyrogel HPS for high temperatures and in demanding thermal applications in refining and chemical processing systems.
- Aspen offers Pyrogel XTF for protection against fire.
- Aspen offers Cryogel Z for sub-ambient and cryogenic applications.
- Aspen offers Spaceloft Subsea for pipe-in-pipe applications in offshore oil production.
- Aspen offers Cryogel X201 for cold system designs.
- The company serves EV manufacturers, automotive suppliers, lithium-ion battery manufacturers, and e-mobility and energy storage companies.
- Aspen Aerogels faces significant challenges as General Motors reduces EV production targets, forcing the company to recalibrate its near-term prospects and financial guidance.
- Analysts anticipate a gradual recovery for Aspen as it adapts, with EPS projections at -$0.32 for FY1 and -$0.18 for FY2.
- Barclays Capital provides price targets ranging from $4.00 (Underweight) to $7.00 (Equal Weight) for Aspen's stock.
- Aspen's Thermal Barrier segment struggles while its Energy Industrial division demonstrates resilience, providing crucial stability to the company's overall financial picture.
- The company was founded in 2001 and is headquartered in Northborough, Massachusetts.
