# Adient plc

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-09-30

**Source:** [Adient plc - Annual Report: 2023 (Form 10-K) | Annual Statements](https://annual-statements.com/company/adient-plc/annual-report-2023-form-10k-368451)  ·  `page_016607`

## Facts

- Adient plc is a global leader in the automotive seating supply industry with leading market positions in the Americas, Europe and China.
- Adient operates more than 200 wholly- and majority-owned manufacturing or assembly facilities, with operations in 29 countries.
- Adient has more than 70,000 employees operating in its global manufacturing and assembly plants.
- Adient designs, manufactures and markets a full range of seating systems and components for passenger cars, commercial vehicles and light trucks, including vans, pick-up trucks and sport/crossover utility vehicles.
- Adient's proprietary technologies extend into virtually every area of automotive seating solutions, including complete seating systems, frames, mechanisms, foam, head restraints, armrests and trim covers.
- Adient maintains longstanding relationships with premier global OEM customers including BMW, Mercedes-Benz Group, Ford Motor Company, General Motors Company, Honda Motor Company, Hyundai Motor Company, Jaguar Land Rover, Kia Corporation, Mazda Motor Corporation, Mitsubishi Motor Corporation, Nissan Motor Corporation, Renault Group, Stellantis N.V., Suzuki Motor Corporation, Toyota Motor Corporation, Volkswagen Group and Volvo Car Group.
- Adient also supplies regional OEMs such as Beijing Automotive Group CO., Ltd., Changan Automobile (Group) Co., Ltd., FAW Group Corporation, Proton Holdings Berhad, Ashok Leyland, Tata Motors Limited and Zhejiang Geely Holding Group Co., Ltd., as well as newer auto manufacturers NIO and Xpeng Motors.
- Adient has more than 6 joint venture partnerships with key OEMs, including Guangzhou Automobile Group Co., Ltd., Beijing Automobile Group Co., Ltd. and FAW Group Corporation.
- Electric vehicles (“EVs”) continue to be a focus in the global automotive industry driven by product offerings from legacy manufacturers and new entrants.
- The rollout of EV platforms varies across regions with factors such as pricing, affordability, government incentives, infrastructure and overall consumer acceptance influencing adoption pace.
- While seating systems are not largely impacted by the shift to EVs, key attributes of seat design are evolving as the market pivots toward EVs.
- This EV market movement provides Adient with opportunities to provide value-added solutions through its Evolution of Seating Systems Sustainability (“ES3”) and to capture market share through new entrants based on its existing leading market position.
- Adient is developing interiors concepts for autonomous driving that address major seating and interior trends expected to drive the automotive industry of the future.
- Adient will continue to partner with OEMs and other customers in the development of Advanced Driver Assist Systems (“ADAS”) and Automated Driving Systems (“ADS”) concepts.
- Raw materials used by Adient include steel, aluminum, polyurethane chemicals, fabrics, leather, vinyl and polypropylene.
- In fiscal year 2021, Adient set a goal of reducing its scope 1 and 2 greenhouse gas emissions 75% by 2030 (with 2019 as the base year).
