## Facts

- Georgia Quick Start was ranked the No. 1 workforce training program in Area Development magazine’s 2024 survey of site location consultants.
- This marks the 15th consecutive year that Georgia Quick Start has won the top spot in the Workforce Training Programs category.
- In its inaugural 2010 survey, Area Development magazine asked leading site location consultants to identify the best state for workforce training programs, and Georgia Quick Start won the No. 1 spot for Georgia.
- The program maintained its top ranking annually from 2011 through 2024.
- The September 2024 survey cites Georgia Quick Start as the best customized workforce training program in the U.S.
- Area Development editors stated that states excelling in this category invest heavily in customized training programs that meet specific business and industry needs, crediting Georgia's leadership to the highly acclaimed Georgia Quick Start program.
- Georgia Quick Start operates within the Technical College System of Georgia and offers customized workforce training to qualified companies as a discretionary incentive to support investment and job creation in the state.
- Area Development’s annual state rankings are considered the gold standard for assessing state appeal and are the only ranking based on surveys of site location professionals who directly influence investment and job creation decisions.
- Georgia ranked No. 1 overall as the Best State for Doing Business for the 11th consecutive year in this survey.
- Magazine editor Andy Greiner attributed Georgia's top overall ranking to its robust pro-business environment and consistent performance across categories, noting it remains a perennial favorite for businesses expanding or relocating.
- Greiner highlighted Quick Start’s pivotal role in establishing the state's reputation, stating it is a key factor in Georgia’s sustained success.
- According to Greiner, Georgia Quick Start has been pivotal in attracting companies requiring tech-savvy workers with specific skills and bolstering the state’s competitive labor market.
