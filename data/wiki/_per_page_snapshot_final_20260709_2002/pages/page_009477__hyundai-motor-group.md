# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-02-26

**Source:** [Hyundai Announces Earlier Production Start Date at Georgia EV Plant - Autobody News](https://www.autobodynews.com/regional/northeast-regional-news/hyundai-announces-earlier-production-start-date-at-georgia-ev-plant)  ·  `page_009477`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group plans to begin production at its upcoming Metaplant America facility in Bryan County, GA -- its first dedicated EV mass-production plant -- in the last quarter of this year.
- Previously, Hyundai said it would start production in the first quarter of 2025.
- The announcement was made during an event celebrating the Korean automaker's impact on Georgia, attended by state officials and Hyundai executives.
- The General Assembly passed a resolution recognizing Feb. 26, 2024, as Hyundai Day at the State Capitol.
- Hyundai and Genesis electric vehicles, including the IONIQ 5, IONIQ 6, GV60 and electrified GV70, were on display outside of the State Capitol.
- The hydrogen-powered XCIENT Fuel Cell Truck will be used for logistics needs between Hyundai’s manufacturing facilities and suppliers.
- José Muñoz, president and global COO of Hyundai Motor Company and president and CEO of Hyundai and Genesis Motor North America, stated that Georgia is like a second home to the company.
- Hyundai's investments in Georgia are expected to create tens of thousands of great paying American jobs and bring tens of billions of dollars in economic impact for decades to come, according to José Muñoz.
- Gov. Brian Kemp stated that Hyundai Motor Group has been an incredible partner to the state for decades and will help recruit businesses, give back to communities, and work with colleges and universities to connect Georgians with opportunities.
- Hyundai Motor Group is investing $7.6 billion to develop the Metaplant America facility.
- The Metaplant America facility will create 8,500 direct jobs.
- Hyundai and SK On are building a $5 billion EV battery cell plant in Bartow County that will employ 3,500 workers.
- According to a recent Center for Automotive Research study, Hyundai’s investments in Georgia are expected to create nearly 40,000 jobs and create $4.6 billion in individual earnings every year.
- The Metaplant America facility is continuing its hiring efforts and has developed educational partnerships with area colleges to create a pipeline for future employment.
- Hyundai has partnered with four area technical colleges to offer the Electric Vehicle Professional Technical Certificate (EVP TCC).
- Students who successfully complete the EVP TCC program receive preferred status when applying for a position at the new plant.
