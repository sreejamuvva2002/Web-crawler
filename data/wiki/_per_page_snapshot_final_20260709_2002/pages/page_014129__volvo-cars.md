# Volvo Cars

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-23

**Source:** [Volvo Cars to produce new vehicle in US as tariffs pressure auto supply chains](https://nypost.com/2025/09/23/business/volvo-cars-to-produce-new-vehicle-in-us-as-tariffs-pressure-auto-supply-chains/)  ·  `page_014129`

## Facts

- Volvo Cars announced plans to produce a new hybrid model in the US.
- The automaker aims to add a next-generation hybrid model to the production line at its Ridgeville, South Carolina plant near Charleston before 2030.
- Luis Rezende, president of Volvo Cars Americas, stated that the Charleston plant is foundational to the company's strategic growth plan for the US.
- Rezende added that adding another model will take a big step toward realizing the full potential of local manufacturing investments and workforce, accelerating progress toward ambitious commercial targets in the coming years.
- Like other global automakers, Volvo Cars has been scrambling to rearrange its supply chain to avoid the brunt of President Trump’s tariffs.
- The White House struck a trade deal with the 27-nation European Union in July that will slash tariffs on foreign cars to 15%.
- In the meantime, imported vehicles are facing a 27.5% duty.
- After Trump unveiled sweeping tariffs in April, Volvo Cars announced plans to ramp up its vehicle production in the US.
- In July, Volvo said it will start producing its popular XC60 mid-size SUV at the South Carolina plant starting late 2026.
- Currently, the Ridgeville factory only produces Volvo’s fully electric EX90 SUV and the luxury electric Polestar 3.
- Volvo emerged as a major name in the EV industry with plans to phase out all non-electric models by 2030.
- The company reversed course on its phase-out plan last year and pledged to keep hybrid models among its lineup.
