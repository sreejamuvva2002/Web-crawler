# Daesol Ausys

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Daesol Ausys To Invest $72 Million In Georgia’s Harris County - Advanced Manufacturing - Business Facilities Magazine](https://businessfacilities.com/daesol-ausys-to-invest-72-million-in-georgias-harris-county/)  ·  `page_019044`

## Facts

- An automotive company with roots in Korea will bring more than 140 new jobs to Harris County, Georgia, as part of a $72 million project.
- The Daesol Ausys Georgia manufacturing facility in West Point will produce luggage boards and luggage covers.
- The facility will offer employment in production, purchasing, quality control, maintenance, human resources, and management roles.
- Min Ho Kwon serves as the CEO of Daesol Ausys.
- Min Ho Kwon stated, “We’re committed to delivering world-class services that exceed our clients’ requirements and expectations.”
- The project is located in the Northwest Harris Business Park.
- Min Ho Kwon expressed excitement about the project and looking forward to continuing the partnership with the State of Georgia and Harris County.
- Gov. Brian Kemp stated that West Point has become an “automotive capital.”
- Georgia employs more than 50,000 people in the automotive industry.
- Pat Wilson of the Georgia Department of Economic Development Commission stated that the automotive industry provides “generational change” both regionally and statewide.
- Pat Wilson expressed excitement about the continuation of growth and the opportunities Daesol Ausys will provide to the people of Harris County.
- Daesol Ausys was established in 2017 in Korea.
- Daesol Ausys specializes in designing and manufacturing motor and electric vehicle interior components and accessories.
- Daesol Ausys is a key supplier for Kia Georgia, Hyundai Motor Group, and General Motors.
- Craig Greenhaw serves as the Chairman of the Harris County Development Authority.
- Craig Greenhaw stated that Harris County looks forward to a “long and mutually beneficial” partnership with Daesol Ausys.
- Craig Greenhaw noted that West Point and Harris County continue to work well together and that the venture brings jobs and capital investment into the region.
- Operations at the Harris County site are expected to begin in December 2024.
- Alyce Thornhill serves as the Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy.
- Alyce Thornhill represented the GDEcD Global Commerce team on this project in partnership with the Harris County Commission, Harris County Development Authority, City of West Point, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
