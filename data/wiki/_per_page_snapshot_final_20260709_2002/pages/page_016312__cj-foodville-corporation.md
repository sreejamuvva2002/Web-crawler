# CJ Foodville Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2023-09-05

**Source:** [South Korean Food Processing Company Announces $47 Million Investment in Georgia | The Star News Network](https://thestarnewsnetwork.com/2023/09/06/south-korean-food-processing-company-announces-47-million-investment-in-georgia/?redirected=1)  ·  `page_016312`

## Facts

- CJ Foodville Corporation announced it will invest more than $47 million to build a new bakery and food processing facility in Gainesville.
- A subsidiary of CJ Group, CJ Foodville is one of the largest food service and food processing companies in South Korea.
- Governor Brian Kemp announced the investment on September 5, 2023.
- The project will create an estimated 285 jobs in Hall County.
- The company made its first overseas expansion and entered the U.S. market in 2004 with its TOUS les JOURS bakery café brand.
- The TOUS les JOURS brand currently has four storefronts in Georgia.
- The new facility will be located at the Gainesville Business Park northeast of Atlanta.
- The facility is expected to be operational in 2025, according to the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD).
- Once operational, the facility is expected to produce more than 19,000 tons of products per year at full capacity.
- Tony Hunsoo Ahn, CEO of CJ Foodville USA, stated the decision was based on strong profitability to enhance production and supply capabilities for the expanding TOUS les JOURS business.
- Ahn noted the company is committed to promoting the unique competitiveness of the K-Bakery, built upon excellent product quality in a diverse range of bread and cakes.
- The ultimate goal is to establish the TOUS les JOURS brand as a globally cherished bakery brand that transcends borders.
- South Korea was one of the top three international sources of job creation in Georgia during Fiscal Year 2023.
- In August 2023, two other South Korean companies announced investment projects in Georgia: Duckshin Housing will invest more than $15 million for its first U.S. manufacturing presence in Athens, and Daesol Ausy will invest $72 million for a new manufacturing facility in Harris County.
- Half of the United States’ top 100 food processing companies operate in Georgia.
- Locations of new and expanding food processing companies ranked among the greatest number of new industrial projects during fiscal year 2022 and resulted in more than $923 million in investment in fiscal year 2023, according to GDEcD.
