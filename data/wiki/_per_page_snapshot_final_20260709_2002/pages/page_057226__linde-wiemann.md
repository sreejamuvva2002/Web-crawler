# LINDE + WIEMANN

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [LINDE + WIEMANN | Quality assurance with automated component testing](https://www.zeiss.com/metrology/us/industries/success-stories/linde-wiemann.html)  ·  `page_057226`

## Facts

- LINDE + WIEMANN combines profiling, stamping, joining and gluing technology to drive innovations in structural assemblies for the automotive industry.
- The company aims to increase vehicle safety and reduce vehicle weight through its products.
- Its German site in Hagenbach produces ready-to-install assemblies of high-strength steel.
- LINDE + WIEMANN supplies these assemblies to global vehicle manufacturers and automotive suppliers.
- Automated 3D measurement technology solutions from ZEISS support the tier-1 supplier throughout the entire manufacturing process.
- Three integrated ScanBox systems scan measuring data and submit it directly to upstream production using ZEISS PiWeb.
- Deviations are detected quickly so appropriate corrective measures can be initiated.
- As a close partner of the automotive industry, LINDE + WIEMANN has faced increasing customer demands and ever more complex geometries for many years.
- This development requires constant adaptation of production and quality assurance processes.
- The automotive supplier regularly shares its measuring data with customers.
- The parallel operation of the ScanBox systems accelerates the measuring procedures significantly.
- Quality management receives valid information on part quality during ongoing production.
- Peter Kluge, Head of Quality Management of LINDE + WIEMANN, explains that they pass on measuring results to the manufacturing department promptly so decisive measures can be taken directly and unnecessary quality costs avoided.
- ZEISS optical measuring machines offer significant advantages for automotive suppliers in terms of accuracy.
- ATOS sensors digitize the entire surface of the structural components to be inspected within seconds via fringe projection.
- Every measurement identifies possible deviations between the actual 3D coordinates and the CAD data, which are displayed through a color comparison in the ZEISS INSPECT software.
- Michael Kray, Quality Manager at LINDE + WIEMANN, explains that their parts are very complex but optical 3D metrology from ZEISS makes measuring them easy and efficient.
- The company benefits from the flexibility of the automated systems.
- In everyday production, the low set-up time of the ScanBox system enables the inspection of other structurally relevant components within just a few minutes.
