# Inalfa Roof Systems

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Auto Supplier Opens Fourth U.S. Plant in Georgia | IndustryWeek](https://www.industryweek.com/expansion-management/article/21960601/auto-supplier-opens-fourth-us-plant-in-georgia)  ·  `page_045126`

## Facts

- Inalfa Roof Systems is a supplier of vehicle roofing systems.
- Inalfa Roof Systems will open a manufacturing plant in Cherokee County, Geogia.
- The plant represents a $17.1 million investment.
- The plant will create 300 jobs for Georgians.
- Governor Nathan Deal stated that Inalfa joins the dynamic auto industry Georgia has cultivated for more than 100 years.
- Governor Nathan Deal noted that Georgia's central location in the Southeast, deep workforce resources and logistics infrastructure have attracted well over 300 companies and continue to give the state a competitive advantage in this manufacturing sector.
- Inalfa Roof Systems supplies sunroofs and open roofing systems Hyundai Kia, Ford, General Motors, Volvo, Chrysler Group and BMW Group.
- Danny Colaris, vice president of North American Operations for Inalfa Roof Systems, stated that after a four-year search for a location in the South, the company found its ideal site in Cherokee County.
- Danny Colaris noted that state and local economic development teams, as well as Georgia Power, have all been extremely helpful in introducing the company to this strategic location and the advantages of doing business in Georgia.
- This facility will be Inalfa's fourth plant in the U.S.
- This facility will be Inalfa's first plant in the Southeast.
- Inalfa has its global headquarters based in Venray, The Netherlands.
- Inalfa has a global workforce of 3,000 employees.
- Inalfa’s North American headquarters is located in Michigan.
- Inalfa operates a total of four plants in Michigan and Mexico.
- Worldwide, Inalfa Roof Systems also has facilities in Slovakia, South Korea, China, Brazil and Japan.
- Inalfa is currently also expanding its footprint in Poland.
- Chris Cummiskey, Commissioner of the Georgia Department of Economic Development, stated that investment by international companies creates jobs and contributes to the state’s economy.
- Chris Cummiskey noted that commitments from top-notch businesses like Inalfa help other investors understand how Georgia can help them become successful in the U.S. market.
