# Lear Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-01-28

**Source:** [GM Seat Supplier Lear To Open Dark Factory Near Orion Plant](https://gmauthority.com/blog/2026/01/gm-seat-supplier-lear-to-open-dark-factory-near-orion-plant/)  ·  `page_056941`

## Facts

- Lear Corporation secured a contract to supply seats for General Motors’ next-generation pickup trucks and SUVs.
- Lear plans to open a highly automated “dark factory” near GM’s Orion Assembly plant.
- The new seating operation will occupy 440,000 square feet in Auburn Hills.
- The Auburn Hills facility is strategically positioned to support just-in-time delivery for Orion Assembly.
- GM confirmed the Orion plant will begin building the next-generation Chevy Silverado 1500 and GMC Sierra 1500 in early 2027.
- These trucks are currently built in Mexico and Canada, but GM is moving to build more USDM trucks in the U.S. to avoid tariff costs.
- Production of the ICE-powered Cadillac Escalade is moving from Texas to Michigan.
- The Cadillac Escalade move makes room for more Chevy Tahoe/Suburban and GMC Yukon/Yukon XL production at the Arlington Assembly plant.
- Lear’s win represents a significant setback for competitor Magna International, which had previously announced a $100 million seating plant and 500 jobs to supply the now-canceled EV program at Orion.
- The canceled Orion EV program left Magna with stranded capital, mirroring broader pain across the supply base as automakers recalibrate electrification timelines.
- GM attributed over half of a recent $7 billion in EV-related charges to supplier contract cancellations and settlements.
- Lear’s winning strategy relies on aggressive automation to make U.S.-based production cost-competitive.
- Lear recently showcased a “lights out manufacturing” center in Rochester Hills, demonstrating fully automated production.
- Lear executives position leveraging automation and digital strategy as essential for winning onshoring business.
- Lear CFO Jason Cardew stated during the company’s October earnings call: “We see leveraging automation and our unique position with automation and our digital strategy as a way to earn higher returns on our seat business that we’re conquesting.”
- The automated approach is expected to keep capital investment and job creation for the Auburn Hills facility lower than traditional operations.
- Lear is expected to provide further details on the landmark GM contract during its fourth-quarter earnings conference call scheduled for February 4th.
