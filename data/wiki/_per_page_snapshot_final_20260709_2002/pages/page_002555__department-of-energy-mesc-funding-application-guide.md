# Department of Energy MESC Funding Application Guide

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [Application Guide | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/cmei/manufacturing/application-guide)  ·  `page_002555`

## Facts

- The guide explains the steps needed to get started on a funding application for DOE opportunities.
- Each funding opportunity is labeled as either a Funding Opportunity Announcement (FOA) or a Partnership Intermediary Agreement (PIA) on the funding page.
- Applicants are recommended to use only one account per organization or business unit as the contact point for each submission when creating an account with Infrastructure eXCHANGE.
- Questions or technical difficulties with submissions should be directed to the Infrastructure eXCHANGE helpdesk.
- Each applicant must register with the System for Award Management (SAM) to obtain a Unique Entity ID (UEI) before submitting an application, unless excepted under 2 CFR 25.110.
- The SAM account must be maintained with up-to-date information in order for a federal award to be made.
- Applicants who have not fully complied with SAM requirements by the time DOE is ready to make a federal award will not receive a federal award.
- Designating an Electronic Business Point of Contact and obtaining a Marketing Partner ID Number (MPIN) are important steps in SAM registration.
- Entity legal business name and address validations for UEI requests and SAM registrations are taking longer than expected due to high volume.
- Entities should start the UEI and SAM registration process as soon as possible.
- Technical difficulties with UEI validation or SAM registration should be addressed using the HELP feature on SAM.gov.
- SAM.gov will address service tickets in the order in which they are received, and entities should not create multiple service tickets for the same request or technical issue.
- Applicants must register with Grants.gov to receive email updates on FOA amendments, though applications will not be accepted through Grants.gov.
- Applying to a Funding Opportunity Announcement is a multi-step process that can take several weeks.
- Applicants should review the relevant Funding Notice on Infrastructure eXCHANGE for all submission deadlines and resources.
- Joining the ENERGYWERX ecosystem provides notifications and updates about funding opportunities, with a recommendation to use only one account per organization or business unit per submission.
- Questions regarding ENERGYWERX should be directed to ENERGYWERX directly.
- Applications for MESC funding opportunities can be found via links on the funding page, with the application type listed as PIA if applying through ENERGYWERX.
- Applicants using ENERGYWERX must pay close attention to provided instructions, which vary per funding opportunity.
- The 48C tax credit applies to investments in advanced energy projects as defined in 26 USC § 48C(c)(1).
- The Energy Efficient Transformer Rebate Program and Extended Product System Rebate Program support the installation of energy efficient distribution transformers and extended product systems that use equipment with electric motors, such as pumps, air compressors, and fans.
- These rebate programs aim to help domestic manufacturers, utilities, tribes, hospitals, schools, and other operators finance equipment upgrades to conserve energy, reduce costs, and slash greenhouse gas emissions across multiple sectors of the economy.
- The funding page hosts all MESC funding opportunities, tax credits, rebates, requests for information, notices of intent, and selections, which can be filtered by content type, status, funding source, and program area.
- By default, funding opportunities that are open for applications are listed on the top of the page and clearly marked as "Open".
- Requests for Information (RFIs) represent the initial fact-finding and need-identifying phase, with MESC periodically announcing them to collect information about market dynamics and test program design concepts.
- Perspectives supported by facts and evidence carry the most weight among RFI submissions.
- RFI statuses include Open (public can submit input), In Review (input is under consideration), and Closed (next steps have been taken; process is complete).
- A Notice of Intent (NOI) serves as a hypothetical heads-up for applicants to understand MESC's areas of interest for a potential funding opportunity, though one is not guaranteed, has no deadline, and requires no required action.
- FOAs can be applied to through Infrastructure eXCHANGE, while PIAs are accessed through ENERGYWERX.
- Funding opportunity statuses include Open (open for applications), In Review (no longer open for application; MESC is reviewing submitted applications), and Selected (MESC has selected applicants as potential awardees; applicants will undergo negotiation).
- Tax credits managed by MESC can have statuses of Open, In Review, or Closed (no longer active).
- Rebates managed by MESC can have statuses of Open, In Review, or Closed.
