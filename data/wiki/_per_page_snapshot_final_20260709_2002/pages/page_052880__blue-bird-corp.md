# Blue Bird Corp.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2014-10-02

**Source:** [Custom-made corrections buses roll off Blue Bird line in Fort Valley  | Macon Telegraph](https://www.macon.com/news/business/article30146397.html)  ·  `page_052880`

## Facts

- State and Middle Georgia officials gathered at the Blue Bird plant in Fort Valley to celebrate the completion of five custom-made buses for the Georgia Department of Corrections.
- The event included a partial tour of the enormous bus manufacturing plant located on Ga. 49.
- Gov. Nathan Deal attended the event, making several stops in Houston and Peach counties that day.
- CEO and President Phil Horlock stated this is the second order of corrections transport buses placed with Blue Bird by the state.
- Horlock noted the new bus design was launched in 2012, with the Georgia Department of Corrections serving as its first customer.
- Corrections Commissioner Brian Owens reported the department operates a fleet of 24 buses used statewide and is about halfway through replacing them with Blue Bird buses.
- Owens specified that despite modifications like white paint, window cages, and toilets, the department prioritizes quality, safety, reliability, and longevity.
- Owens stated that among different manufacturers, Blue Bird sets the standards in those four key areas.
- Two transport officers participated in the bus design process and attended the event.
- Horlock explained that Blue Bird offers 25,000 different parts options, enabling complete customization to buyer specifications.
- Following an initial order of 10 transport buses two years ago, orders have come from corrections departments in other states.
- Owens commented that with only about 50 corrections commissioners in the U.S., positive product feedback spreads quickly among them.
- Blue Bird is expected to grow following its announcement to go public and trade on the Nasdaq exchange.
- Texas-based Hennessy Capital Acquisition Corp. plans to acquire Blue Bird in a deal valued at $490 million, including $255 million in cash and stock, while assuming approximately $235 million of Blue Bird’s debt.
- Hennessy was established this year as a special purpose acquisition corporation trading under the ticker HCAC.
- Upon consummation expected by the end of the year, Hennessy stock will convert to Blue Bird stock.
- No operational changes are expected at the company, which currently employs about 1,600 workers.
- Blue Bird Corp. manufactures a complete line of Type A, C, and D school buses with various options and configurations.
- The company maintains a global presence with sales in more than 60 countries across Africa, Asia, the Caribbean, Latin America, Europe, and the Middle East.
