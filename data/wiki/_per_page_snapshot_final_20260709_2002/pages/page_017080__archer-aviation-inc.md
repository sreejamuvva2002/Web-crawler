# Archer Aviation Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-01-12

**Source:** [Air taxi supply shortage may slow eVTOL adoption, Archer Aviation exec says at CES | Smart Cities Dive](https://www.smartcitiesdive.com/news/air-taxi-supply-shortage-slow-evtol-adoption-archer-aviation-ces/640090/)  ·  `page_017080`

## Facts

- Panelists at the CES trade show in Las Vegas discussed that air taxi adoption may be delayed as manufacturers ramp up production and public officials figure out regulation and planning.
- Air taxi companies and government officials are shifting focus from approving eVTOL aircraft and vertiport guidelines to implementation challenges including route planning, manufacturing and distribution, and workforce development.
- Archer Aviation Chief Operating Officer Tom Muniz stated that original equipment manufacturers will be supply constrained for a long time and must be thoughtful about the best markets to introduce these new products.
- Advanced air mobility has the potential to transform the transportation sector because eVTOLs may allow movement of people or goods currently underserved.
- Unlike electric vehicles that largely replace existing gas- and diesel-powered cars and trucks, eVTOLs could enable longer commutes to work, improve access to emergency medical services in rural areas, and directly connect urban downtowns to major airports.
- Aircraft manufacturers might need to produce tens of thousands of eVTOLs but currently lack the capability, as commercial jet manufacturers make hundreds of aircraft per year.
- There is not much precedent for producing tens of thousands of aircraft in aerospace manufacturing outside of World War Two.
- Bob Brock, Kansas director of aviation and advanced air mobility, noted that many regions lack the supply chains, distribution networks, and other resources needed to build and deliver eVTOLs.
- Brock recommended that air taxi manufacturers establish operations in locations that can help them achieve the economies of scale needed to succeed.
- Auto manufacturer Stellantis announced it would help Archer Aviation produce its flagship Midnight electric air taxi.
- Archer Aviation CEO Adam Goldstein explained that Archer and Stellantis began working together in 2020 on engineering projects, including ways to optimize the vehicle around high-volume manufacturing.
- Goldstein likened the Archer-Stellantis relationship to Apple and Foxconn, where Archer designs the product and Stellantis manufactures it at scale.
- During the ITS World Congress meeting in Los Angeles last September, local government officials, transportation researchers, and urban planning consultants said cities must start planning for air taxis to prevent them from wreaking havoc on urban transportation systems due to their complexity and need for greater coordination.
