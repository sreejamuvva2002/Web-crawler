# T1 Energy Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-03-03

**Source:** [Freyr Battery rebrands as T1 Energy - Energy Storage](https://www.ess-news.com/2025/02/19/freyr-battery-rebrands-as-t1-energy/)  ·  `page_054999`

## Facts

- Freyr Battery has announced its rebranding as T1 Energy Inc., marking a shift away from battery manufacturing to solar PV production and storage.
- The company cancelled its $2.6 billion Georgia battery gigafactory plans.
- T1 Energy will trade under the ticker “TE” on the NYSE starting March 3, 2025.
- The abandonment of the Georgia battery factory plans follows the company's previous aims of 200 GWh of battery production by 2030.
- The company is selling the site for the abandoned Georgia battery factory.
- In late 2024, T1 Energy acquired a 5 GW solar cell facility in the U.S. from Trina Solar for $340 million.
- T1 Energy continues to advance a battery facility in Norway following previous cutbacks to its size and scope.
- The company has not provided significant new information on the Giga Arctic project beyond its October investor presentation.
- In the October investor presentation, the company detailed that it has continued to advance value optimization with leading strategic, industrial, and financial stakeholders based on less capital-intensive business models.
- These value optimization efforts include high-value adjacencies to the battery value chain such as digital battery platforms and data center opportunities for Giga Arctic balanced by Battery Energy Storage Solutions.
- Multiple industry moves highlight difficulties in entering the competitive battery manufacturing sector while competing with China's rapidly growing sector.
- According to Benchmark Mineral Intelligence, 2024 saw record global battery gigafactory cancellations exceeding 312 GWh.
- T1 Energy's new operational focus centers on its solar module facility in Wilmer, Texas, dubbed “G1 Dallas,” which employs over 1,000 people.
- The company is planning a second U.S. solar cell manufacturing facility, “G2,” with site selection expected in Q1 2025.
- T1 Energy will have its headquarters in Austin, Texas.
- The company is positioning itself to leverage U.S. tax benefits under the Inflation Reduction Act while focusing on domestic solar manufacturing capacity.
- Daniel Barcelo serves as T1’s chairman of the board and CEO.
- Daniel Barcelo stated that the rebirth as T1 Energy is about American energy, jobs, and advanced manufacturing, emphasizing the need for domestic supply chains to harness abundant solar resources.
- Barcelo noted that more than 80% of new U.S. electricity capacity in 2024 was solar and batteries.
- T1 Energy plans to lead in the solar and battery markets by enabling energy supply growth to support AI, data centers, reshoring, and electrification to restore American industrial capacity and leadership.
