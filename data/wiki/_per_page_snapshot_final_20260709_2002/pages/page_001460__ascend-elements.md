# Ascend Elements

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2023-03-30

**Source:** [Ascend Elements Opens EV Battery Recycling Facility in Georgia - Autobody News](https://www.autobodynews.com/news/ascend-elements-opens-north-america-s-largest-ev-battery-recycling-facility-in-georgia)  ·  `page_001460`

## Facts

- Ascend Elements celebrated the grand opening of its first commercial-scale lithium-ion battery recycling facility at the end of March.
- The $50 million Base 1 facility is located in Covington, GA.
- It is North America's largest electric vehicle battery recycling facility.
- Lithium-ion battery recycling is becoming an increasingly important part of the EV battery materials supply chain as demand for EVs increases.
- The facility began partial operations in August 2022.
- The facility now has an annual capacity to process 30,000 metric tons of used lithium-ion batteries and manufacturing scrap.
- This processing capacity is equal to 70,000 EV batteries per year.
- Ascend Elements CEO Mike O'Kronley stated the facility is an important part of America's EV battery infrastructure and just the start.
- O'Kronley emphasized that the industry needs to continue building a closed-loop battery materials supply chain to make electric vehicle batteries cleaner and more sustainable.
- Ascend Elements recovers up to 98% of critical battery metals in used EV batteries and gigafactory manufacturing scrap.
- The company uses its patented Hydro-to-Cathode™ process to recycle used lithium-ion batteries and scrap.
- This recycling process can reduce the carbon footprint of new EV battery cathode materials by up to 90%.
- The facility is powered by renewable energy.
- The facility features onsite wastewater recycling and treatment systems.
- The facility currently employs about 100 team members.
- Ascend Elements plans to increase the facility's headcount to 185 by 2024.
