# Solvay Specialty Polymers

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2022-10-19

**Source:** [Augusta polymers plant wins $178 million grant to build electric-vehicle battery parts](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/augusta-polymers-plant-wins-178-194639704.html)  ·  `page_001266`

## Facts

- Solvay Specialty Polymers received a $178 million federal grant to build a companion facility to produce battery components for electric vehicles in Augusta.
- The proposed new plant could bring as many as 500 construction jobs and 100 manufacturing jobs to the Augusta area.
- U.S. Sen Raphael Warnock's office announced the job projections in a statement on Wednesday.
- Solvay won the grant through the Battery Materials Processing and Battery Component Manufacturing and Recycling program.
- The program is funded through the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law enacted in 2021, according to the U.S. Department of Energy.
- Cal Wray, executive director of the Augusta Economic Development Authority, stated that the real economic impact numbers for the Augusta-Richmond County Metropolitan Statistical Area will likely end up being higher than the grant amount.
- The Augusta Economic Development Authority wrote a letter to the DOE earlier this year in support of Solvay's grant application.
- Cal Wray noted that between a couple-hundred million investment and a couple-hundred jobs that are well-paid, he thinks it is great for the area.
- The Augusta-Richmond County Metropolitan Statistical Area has a concentration of about 23,000 people who work in manufacturing.
- Solvay provides some of the best paid manufacturing jobs in the area.
- The factory is expected to manufacture electric battery-grade binders and separator coatings.
- These components are crucial for lithium-ion batteries that power EVs.
- Belgium-headquartered Solvay acquired its current Augusta facility from BP Amoco in 2000.
- Since acquiring the facility, Solvay has regularly invested in increasing its product lines and capacity.
- In 2015, Solvay announced an $85 million local expansion at the Augusta facility.
- Cal Wray noted that Aurubis and Denkai are already in the same industry in Augusta alongside Solvay's continually growing facility.
- In June, German company Aurubis AG broke ground in south Richmond County on a $340 million copper recycling and smelting plant dubbed Aurubis Richmond.
- The Aurubis Richmond plant will process about 90,000 tons of complex recycling materials annually to extract about 35,000 tons of blister copper.
- Copper is one of the most in-demand finished products that goes into making EVs.
- In July, Japanese manufacturer Denkai America announced the construction of a $430 million south Augusta manufacturing plant.
- Denkai produces copper foil used in printed electronics such as circuit boards.
- U.S. Sen Raphael Warnock stated that Georgia is open for business and today's announcement is good news for both Georgia's growing clean energy economy and Augusta workers.
- Mike Finelli, president of Solvay Growth Platforms and chief North American officer, said the company is proud to receive the federal government's support for manufacturing technologies that will provide the U.S. with critical raw materials for building an independent, sustainable EV value chain.
- Finelli stated the grant reaffirms Solvay's leadership in developing a robust and resilient domestic battery supply chain that brings important clean energy jobs to the U.S.
