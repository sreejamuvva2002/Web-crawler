# Ecoplastic America Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-18

**Source:** [Proposed Ecoplastic land purchase could bring worker housing development to Bulloch County - Statesboro Herald](https://www.statesboroherald.com/local/proposed-ecoplastic-land-purchase-could-bring-worker-housing-development-to-bulloch-county/)  ·  `page_019823`

## Facts

- Ecoplastic America Corporation is looking to purchase roughly 7.3 acres of Development Authority-owned property located across U.S. Highway 301 South from its Bulloch County manufacturing facility.
- The proposed land sale was reviewed during the Development Authority of Bulloch County's June 9 meeting, but a vote to approve the agreement was tabled after questions were raised about potential residential development at the site.
- The property is located about a mile and a half north of the Interstate 16 exit onto U.S. Highway 301.
- Development Authority CEO Benjy Thompson stated that early conversations with Ecoplastic focused primarily on commercial uses, but company representatives later indicated interest in housing-related uses and sleeping quarters.
- Ecoplastic America Corporation manufactures injection-molded plastic automotive body parts for Hyundai Motor Group vehicles.
- The company has expressed interest in assigning ownership of the purchased property to an affiliated entity that could pursue commercial or residential projects.
- Development Authority board member Tal Johnson noted that Ecoplastic has a need to house workers, including local hires and employees coming from their home country.
- Board member Timmy Rushing questioned whether the proposed housing would resemble traditional apartments, company-sponsored workforce housing, or a transient arrangement.
- The Statesboro Herald reached out to Ecoplastic about the land sale and housing needs but did not receive a response in time for publication.
- Benjy Thompson noted that the housing component was unexpected but not surprising since Hyundai Motor Group announced the Ellabell Metaplant and suppliers began locating throughout the region.
- Hyundai suppliers in Bulloch County, including Ecoplastic, Ajin Georgia, and Hanon Systems, have committed to creating about 1,200 job positions.
- Many of the 1,200 jobs will be filled by local residents, but Hyundai suppliers have asked local officials about housing options for Korean employees who temporarily relocate to the United States to help establish and operate facilities.
- The Development Authority's role in housing issues is generally limited, although it has previously partnered on workforce housing initiatives including support for the Cornerstone Pointe workforce housing development on Highway 67 through a rural workforce housing grant program.
- Benjy Thompson stated that commercial development is lacking near the site and could be beneficial due to its proximity to Interstate 16.
- The site under consideration is currently zoned for highway commercial, and Ecoplastic would have to request rezoning if their plans do not align with current zoning ordinances.
- The property was originally purchased by the Development Authority as part of a long-term industrial recruitment strategy but is not located within an industrial park and has not received site preparation.
- SECO Ecoplastic had hoped to close on the property by the end of June, but Benjy Thompson indicated the timeline would be extended to allow sufficient time for due diligence.
- The proposed sale agreement includes a provision requiring construction to begin within 24 months, with a repurchase option for the Development Authority at the original sale price between $400,000 and $500,000 if construction does not start within that period.
- The repurchase option would be available during a six-month window after the 24-month construction deadline expires.
- The agreement references restrictive covenants to govern property use, which the Authority has not finalized but will likely apply requirements similar to those used on other development authority-owned industrial properties.
- Any final decision on the land sale will return to the Development Authority board for approval during a public meeting.
