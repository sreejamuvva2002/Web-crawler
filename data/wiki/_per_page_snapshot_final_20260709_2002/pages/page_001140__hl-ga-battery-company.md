# HL-GA Battery Company

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-05

**Source:** [Hundreds arrested in immigration raid at Hyundai plant site in Georgia - AOL](https://www.aol.com/hundreds-arrested-immigration-raid-hyundai-014752212.html)  ·  `page_001140`

## Facts

- Federal agents on Sept. 4 arrested hundreds of people at a sprawling battery plant construction site in Georgia in one of the Trump administration's largest immigration raids.
- Agents from the Department of Homeland Security, FBI, Drug Enforcement Agency, and Georgia State Patrol took part in the operation at Hyundai's battery plant in Bryan County, southwest of Savannah.
- Agents apprehended about 450 people suspected of being in the country illegally, according to an ATF X post.
- DHS stated that Immigration and Customs Enforcement's Homeland Security Investigations executed a judicial search warrant for an ongoing criminal investigation into alleged unlawful employment practices and other federal crimes.
- The agency did not clarify how many agents participated in the operation or the charges those detained face.
- Steven Schrank, special agent in charge of Homeland Security Investigations Atlanta, stated at a news conference that they are making many arrests of undocumented individuals.
- Schrank noted that many lawful employees, including United States citizens and lawful permanent residents, were encountered and released.
- Schrank confirmed there were no injuries or uses of force during the operation and said more details would be released later.
- The facility is the HL-GA Battery Company, a battery plant jointly created by LG Energy Solution and Hyundai Motor Group.
- The plant is under construction next to the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America at a site spanning nearly 3,000 acres.
- Hyundai's new $7.6 billion plant in a rural area near Savannah has promised to transform the local economy and workforce.
- The facility is estimated to create around 8,500 jobs by 2030.
- Hyundai spokesperson Bianca Johnson stated in an email that there was no impact to the metaplant or its production.
- Johnson confirmed the law enforcement action took place at the HL-GA battery plant's construction site.
- Johnson said Hyundai is cooperating with law enforcement and is committed to abiding by all labor and immigration regulations.
- Mary Beth Kennedy, spokesperson for the HL-GA Battery Company, stated the company is cooperating fully with appropriate authorities regarding activity at their construction site.
- Kennedy announced that they have paused construction to assist authorities' work but do not have further details at this time.
- The Savannah Morning News was told to exit the facility as state troopers blocked access during the raid.
- Images posted by ATF showed men and women in zip ties being escorted off the site.
- Georgia State Patrol Capt. Crystal Zion said the department assisted federal officials with serving a criminal search warrant at the Hyundai Metaplant, directing further inquiries to Homeland Security Investigations.
- The Georgia raid is one of the largest during President Donald Trump's second term as he promises a sweeping immigration crackdown using multiple federal agencies and local/state law enforcement assistance.
- Raids have taken place across the country on farms, construction sites, restaurants, and car washes.
- During Trump's first term in August 2019, agents arrested 680 people at seven Mississippi chicken processing plants, called the largest single-state immigration crackdown in a decade.
