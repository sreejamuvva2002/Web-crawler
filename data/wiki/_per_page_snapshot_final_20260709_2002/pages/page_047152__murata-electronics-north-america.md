# Murata Electronics North America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** power_electronics  ·  **Published:** 2025-04-11

**Source:** [Webinar: Murata: Tiny Parts, Huge Impact on Global Supply Chains - Georgia State Center for International Business Education & Research](https://ciber.gsu.edu/webinar-murata-tiny-parts-huge-impact-on-global-supply-chains/)  ·  `page_047152`

## Facts

- Murata became one of the earliest Japanese investors in Georgia in 1973, setting up a plant to make capacitors in car radios.
- The Kyoto-based company has global annual revenues of approximately $17 trillion yen.
- Murata holds about 40 percent of the world’s market share for capacitors, small ceramic parts produced by the trillions each year to store and release power within electronic devices.
- More than 1,000 of Murata's parts go into a typical smartphone.
- Potentially 30,000+ of Murata's parts are slated for a fully autonomous electric vehicle.
- Murata diversified its sourcing around the world, including in Southeast Asia, to be closer to end customers.
- Murata deepened research spending to ensure it could serve advanced industries, like increasingly computerized cars, with innovative solutions.
- Murata Electronics North America has a new headquarters in Atlanta, Ga.
- Murata Electronics North America maintains a distribution center in Rockmart, Ga.
- The company is dealing with increasing volatility in high-value industries where the smallest parts can make the biggest impact.
- A webinar hosted by GSU-CIBER and Global Atlanta on Thursday, April 24, 2025, from 11:00 AM to 12:00 PM EST will cover Murata's diversification of manufacturing locations in partnership with customers and its impact on U.S. operations.
- The webinar will cover Murata’s approach to continuous improvement in research and development, its moves up the value chain over the last 50 years, and efforts to showcase innovation through robots and consumer-facing items.
- The webinar will cover the company's approach to trade uncertainty and supply chain volatility and how it balances Japanese sensibilities, including long-term strategy, with a nimble approach in global markets.
- Speaker David Kirk serves as VP of Murata Manufacturing Co Ltd. and President & CEO of Murata Electronics North America.
- David Kirk is responsible for Sales/Marketing, Operations, Corporate Technology, and Innovation across the Americas.
- The Americas region is critical for design-in activity that results in global sales within the electronics industry.
- Murata has made multiple acquisitions and integrations globally, including Peregrine Semiconductor (pSemi), Vios Medical, Eta Wireless, Resonant, and RF Monolithics (RFM).
- David Kirk started his career as a Product Engineer with Murata and moved into the Director of Marketing role.
- He previously served as President and CEO of RFM, which was a public company from 1999 to 2011.
- Upon returning to Murata in 2012, Kirk was instrumental in RFM’s acquisition by the parent company in Japan.
- Kirk is the first non-Japanese person to lead Murata in the Americas and become an officer of the parent company, Murata Manufacturing Company headquartered in Kyoto, Japan.
