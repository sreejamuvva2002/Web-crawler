# High-Performance Computing for Energy Innovation (HPC4EI)

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [Funding Notice: Over $10 Million Available for High-Performance Computing Innovation Program | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/cmei/ammto/articles/funding-notice-over-10-million-available-high-performance-computing-innovation)  ·  `page_003086`

## Facts

- The U.S. Department of Energy’s Office of Critical Minerals and Energy Innovation (CMEI) announced a new solicitation for projects that improve America’s manufacturing competitiveness through the use of powerful supercomputers.
- DOE will award up to $400,000 per project.
- In total, DOE will make over $10 million available for potential selections.
- Eligible applicants include U.S.-based small and medium-sized manufacturers (SMMs), universities, institutes, and non-profit organizations.
- Selected teams will work with staff from one or more DOE National Laboratories to advance material designs and solve manufacturing challenges.
- The program is called the High-Performance Computing for Manufacturing (HPC4Mfg) program, which is part of the High-Performance Computing for Energy Innovation (HPC4EI) initiative.
- HPC4EI is funded by both DOE’s Advanced Materials and Manufacturing Technologies Office (AMMTO) and Industrial Technologies Office (ITO).
- The HPC4EI initiative is managed by Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory.
- Projects funded through this solicitation will accelerate the development of new materials and improve manufacturing and industrial processes through artificial intelligence, machine learning, and digital simulation.
- Topic Area 1 proposals should demonstrate credible pathways to advancing American manufacturing competitiveness, improving supply chain security, enhancing energy system reliability, and creating high-quality domestic jobs.
- Areas of interest for Topic Area 1 include the development of advanced materials to improve the performance, durability, and cost-effectiveness of energy systems and technologies.
- Areas of interest for Topic Area 1 also include manufacturing processes to strengthen U.S. industrial competitiveness and energy security.
- Topic Area 2 proposals should demonstrate potential to strengthen global competitiveness of the U.S. industrial sector, reduce the energy intensity of industrial processes, improve the reliability of energy infrastructure, and onshore industrial excellence.
- Areas of interest for Topic Area 2 include the development, optimization, scale-up, and integration of technologies that significantly improve productivity optimization in energy-intensive industries to bolster manufacturing competitiveness.
- Areas of interest for Topic Area 2 also include equipment and processes that improve industrial process heating, including reducing cost, improving efficiency, or enhancing product quality.
- The solicitation encourages applicants to partner with a range of universities, community colleges, and non-profit organizations.
- The funding opportunity was released on April 28, 2026.
- Concept papers are due by May 27, 2026, at 5:00 p.m. PT.
- Full applications will open in July 2026 and are due in August 2026.
- Selection notifications are expected in October 2026.
