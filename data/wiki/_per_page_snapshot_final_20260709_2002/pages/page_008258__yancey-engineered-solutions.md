# Yancey Engineered Solutions

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Yancey Engineered Solutions To Invest $5.7M In Georgia Manufacturing Facility - Business Facilities Magazine](https://businessfacilities.com/yancey-engineered-solutions-to-invest-5-7m-in-georgia-manufacturing-facility/)  ·  `page_008258`

## Facts

- Yancey Engineered Solutions will invest $5.7 million to establish a new manufacturing facility in Cordele, Georgia.
- The project will create 300 new jobs over the next several years in Crisp County.
- Yancey Engineered Solutions provides power generation packaging systems and operates as a division of Yancey Bros. Company (Yancey).
- Yancey Bros. Company is the authorized Caterpillar dealer for Georgia.
- Trey Googe, CEO of Yancey, stated that Yancey Bros. has served Cordele and Crisp County for decades primarily with Caterpillar earthmoving equipment, parts, and service.
- The new facility will be located at 502 South Midway Road in Cordele.
- The company plans to refurbish the former Big Tex manufacturing facility on the site.
- Hiring plans include welders, quality control technicians, assembly workers, and drivers.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp noted that Yancey provides solutions supporting everything from small businesses to large manufacturers across every industry in Georgia.
- Senior Regional Project Manager Christy Bozeman represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) on the project.
- The project is in partnership with the Cordele-Crisp Industrial Development Authority, Crisp County Power Commission, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Yancey Engineered Solutions represents an exciting addition to Yancey’s portfolio that helps provide reliable energy to users.
- Cordele Mayor and Crisp County-Cordele Industrial Development Authority Vice-Chair Wesley Rainey expressed excitement about redeveloping the Big Tex facility and hopes to rehire many of its former employees.
- James R. Dowdy, III, Chairman of the Crisp County Board of Commissioners and the Cordele-Crisp Industrial Development Authority, stated that the community is thankful for the decision and it demonstrates economic development efforts paying dividends.
