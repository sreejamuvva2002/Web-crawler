# Plug Power Energy Loan Borrower, LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [Plug Power | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/edf/plug-power)  ·  `page_004502`

## Facts

- In January 2025, the U.S. Department of Energy’s Loan Programs Office announced the closing of a $1.66 billion loan guarantee ($1.55 billion of principal and $107 million of capitalized interest) to Plug Power Energy Loan Borrower, LLC.
- The loan will help finance the construction of up to six projects to produce and liquify zero- or low-carbon hydrogen.
- The produced hydrogen will supply Plug Power’s national customer base with end-to-end clean hydrogen.
- The loan guarantee will support an estimated 100-200 jobs during the construction period.
- At full capacity, each location will have at least 50 new full-time jobs.
- Together with the DOE Regional Clean Hydrogen Hubs and ongoing research, development, and demonstration in the DOE Hydrogen Program, this project will help strengthen local economies.
- The project aims to create and maintain high-quality jobs, reduce greenhouse gas emissions in sectors critical to meeting U.S. net-zero goals, and enhance America’s manufacturing and industrial competitiveness.
- The hydrogen fuel is expected to be used for fuel cell electric material handling equipment and in the transportation and industrial sectors.
- The project is estimated to achieve an 84% reduction in greenhouse gas emissions compared to conventional hydrogen production, which derives hydrogen from natural gas (CH4) and ultimately produces carbon dioxide (CO2).
- Harnessing hydrogen fuel cells in applications such as material handling equipment includes enhanced operational efficiency, reduced environmental impact through zero-emission operations, and increased productivity due to faster refueling times compared to conventional batteries.
- Plug will initiate a community outreach program dedicated to promoting awareness, understanding, and utilization of hydrogen as a clean and sustainable energy source.
- The outreach aims to engage and empower communities by providing educational resources, interactive activities, and collaborative initiatives that highlight the benefits and potential applications of hydrogen technology.
- Plug has a comprehensive workforce strategy including utilizing apprentices at its New York location.
- All workforce development strategies and programs would be designed to utilize the comprehensive suite of services offered by the Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act (WIOA) network of One-Stop Career Centers.
- The project owners are Plug Power Energy Loan Borrower LLC.
- Project locations are throughout the U.S.
- The loan program is Title 17.
- The guaranteed loan amount is $1.66 billion.
- The issuance date is January 2025.
- Permanent U.S. jobs supported are estimated at up to 300.
- The approximate loan amount approved at closing includes principal and any capitalized interest.
- Job estimates were made at the time of closing.
