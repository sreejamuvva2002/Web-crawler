# Eaton

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** power_electronics  ·  **Published:** 2025-12-10

**Source:** [Governor Glenn Youngkin Announces Eaton Expansion with Over $50 Million Investment in Henrico County - Henrico EDA](https://henrico.com/press-release/governor-glenn-youngkin-announces-eaton-expansion-with-over-50-million-investment-in-henrico-county/)  ·  `page_019804`

## Facts

- Governor Glenn Youngkin announced Eaton, an intelligent power management company, will invest over $50 million to expand its existing manufacturing footprint in Henrico County, Virginia.
- Upon completion, the project is expected to create 200 new jobs in the Commonwealth.
- The company selected Henrico after assessing multiple locations across the country.
- Governor Glenn Youngkin stated: “Eaton’s latest investment in Henrico is proof that Virginia companies and workers are stepping up to meet our growing power needs.”
- Governor Glenn Youngkin added: “The only thing better than bringing a new business to Virginia is watching an existing Virginia business expand. By producing even more critical power equipment in the Commonwealth, Eaton is helping drive Virginia’s future and strengthening our competitive edge.”
- Secretary of Commerce and Trade Juan Pablo Segura stated: “Eaton’s expansion in Henrico is just another reason to celebrate this holiday season.”
- Secretary of Commerce and Trade Juan Pablo Segura noted that 200 more employees will soon work to meet the nation’s rising need for critical power equipment.
- Secretary of Commerce and Trade Juan Pablo Segura highlighted that Virginia’s manufacturing sector is surging from Southwest Virginia to Hampton Roads and the Greater Richmond region thanks to forward-looking companies like Eaton.
- Aidan Graham, senior vice president and general manager of Critical Power Solutions at Eaton, stated: “Eaton is continuing to invest in U.S. manufacturing and is thankful for the strong collaboration and support in Virginia.”
- Aidan Graham noted that the expansion builds on the company's history in the region and reflects the abilities of longtime local employees.
- Aidan Graham stated: “We’re uniquely positioned to help our data center customers meet the rapidly increasing power requirements for AI factories through our expansive manufacturing footprint and our focus on innovation and engineering excellence.”
- Eaton will open a new campus in Henrico, more than doubling its regional footprint.
- The company will occupy a to-be-built 350,000-square-foot facility.
- The expansion is expected to add 200 new employees, bringing Eaton’s local team to nearly 500 employees.
- This expansion will support production of static transfer switches, power distribution units, and remote power panels.
- These products are essential technologies that provide reliable, uninterrupted power.
- Eaton’s investment highlights its portfolio of intelligent power management solutions for the data center, utility, industrial, commercial, machine building, residential, aerospace and mobility markets.
- Henrico EDA Board of Directors Chair Edward S. Whitlock, III stated: “Eaton’s decision to expand in Henrico reinforces our reputation as a place where manufacturers truly succeed.”
- Henrico EDA Board of Directors Chair Edward S. Whitlock, III noted this is Eaton's second major investment in the past five years.
- Senator Lashrecse Aird stated: “Henrico continues to prove that when companies invest here, our people and our economy grow with them.”
- Senator Lashrecse Aird noted Eaton’s decision to expand again in Eastern Henrico is a clear vote of confidence in the workforce and the Commonwealth’s ability to support advanced manufacturing at a national scale.
- Senator Lashrecse Aird stated the project will create well-paying jobs and open the door to new opportunities for local families, strengthening both the community and the region’s economic competitiveness.
- Governor Youngkin approved a performance-based grant of $1,000,000 from the Virginia Investment Performance Grant to support Eaton’s investment.
- The Virginia Investment Performance Grant is an incentive that encourages continued capital investment by existing Virginia companies.
