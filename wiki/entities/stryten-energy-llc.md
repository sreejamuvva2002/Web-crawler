# Stryten Energy LLC

**Type:** Company  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[battery-materials|Battery Materials]]  ·  **Location:** Alpharetta, GA, Georgia, United States

## Overview

Stryten Energy is the only company with a complete offering of advanced lead, lithium and vanadium batteries, allowing customers to select the right chemistry or combination of chemistries to design a solution that best fits their energy needs.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Provides batteries for material-handling fleets, transportation vehicles, and military applications, supporting electric vehicle power and charging infrastructure across the state.

## Key facts

- Stryten offers a complete portfolio of advanced lead, lithium and vanadium batteries, allowing customers to select the optimal chemistry for their application.
- The Vanadium Redox Flow Battery provides 4-12 hours of medium-to-long duration storage suitable for microgrids, utility-scale projects, data centers and military bases.
- inCOMMAND software aggregates fleet performance data into a central dashboard, enabling real-time monitoring, modeling and management of motive-power batteries and chargers.
- All Stryten batteries are manufactured in the United States through a robust domestic supply chain, supporting national energy-storage goals.
- Stryten’s advanced lead batteries incorporate a circular-economy model for sustainable production and recycling.
- The company supplies batteries for essential power, motive power, transportation, military & government applications, as well as components for automotive and railway power systems.
- Stryten has announced plans to add 10 GW of new U.S. energy-storage manufacturing capacity to meet growing demand.
