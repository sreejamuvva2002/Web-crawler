# Bonnell Aluminum

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2025-12-16

**Source:** [Bonnell Aluminum Archives - Light Metal Age Magazine](https://www.lightmetalage.com/tag/bonnell-aluminum/)  ·  `page_053081`

## Facts

- Bonnell Aluminum is headquartered in Newnan, Georgia, and is a leading manufacturer of custom aluminum extrusions.
- The company has a 65-year history in the extrusion industry.
- Bonnell Aluminum completed the installation of an ASCONA promex EXPERT profile measuring system at its plant in Carthage, Tennessee, to improve extrusion quality.
- This is the second profile measurement system of this type installed by the company.
- Bonnell Aluminum, a subsidiary of Tredegar Corporation, plans to expand operations focused on producing TSLOTS aluminum structural framing systems.
- The company will add a manufacturing and distribution site for the TSLOTS framing system to its existing facility in Newnan, Georgia.
- This expansion will enable Bonnell to provide customers based in the Southeastern U.S. with expanded TSLOTS availability.
- Bonnell Aluminum is expanding its TSLOTS line of aluminum structural framing systems into the Midwest region of the U.S. with a new location and increased storage capacity.
- Customers in the Midwest and East have been requesting expanded TSLOTS availability as the product line has grown.
- Futura Industries, a subsidiary of Bonnell Aluminum, installed two new CNC machines due to increased demand for fabrication and precision milling.
- The new CNC machines will improve lead times and provide better fabrication speed and reliability.
- The 2026 ETF Rick Merluzzi Student Design Competition is sponsored by MI Metals, Inc., Pennex Aluminum, and Bonnell Aluminum.
- The AEC and ET Foundation announced the winners of the 2018 Aluminum Extrusion Design Competition at the EDU ’18 event held May 15-17 in Chicago, Illinois.
- Seven extrusion designs showcasing the versatility and advantages of aluminum extrusions were recognized at the 2018 competition.
