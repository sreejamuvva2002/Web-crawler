# Advanced Materials and Manufacturing Technologies Office (AMMTO) FY22 Multi-Topic Funding Program

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [Funding Selections: AMMTO-BTO and OE FY22 Multi-Topic FOA | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/cmei/ammto/funding-selections-ammto-bto-and-oe-fy22-multi-topic-foa)  ·  `page_005905`

## Facts

- The U.S. Department of Energy’s Office of Energy Efficiency and Renewable Energy (EERE) selected 31 projects led by national laboratories, industry, and academia.
- More than $61 million in federal funding is available for the selectees to drive innovation and progress next generation materials and manufacturing related energy technologies.
- The initiative aims to strengthen America’s economic competitiveness.
- The FOA selections seek to advance development of next generation structural and functional materials for energy technology.
- The selections also seek field validation and pilot demonstration of technologies for materials and manufacturing processes.
- Award and cost share amounts are subject to change pending negotiations.
- Topic 1: Next Generation Materials and Manufacturing includes 20 projects with an award amount of $27.6 million.
- Topic 1 focuses on cost-effective manufacturing processes and development of novel materials with improved properties such as high strength, enhanced conductivity, or high performance under extreme conditions.
- Topic 2: Secure and Sustainable Materials includes 4 projects with an award amount of $10.8 million.
- Topic 2 focuses on materials RD&D to support the establishment of reuse and recycling systems with an emphasis on material and product design, recycling technology development, and reverse supply chain logistics.
- Topic 3: Energy Technology Manufacturing includes 7 projects with an award amount of $22.6 million.
- Topic 3 focuses on energy technology manufacturing innovation to improve performance and address technical barriers.
