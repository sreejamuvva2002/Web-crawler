# State of Georgia

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Georgia Scores Top Spot in Competitive Economic Rankings | AM 1180 Radio](https://chattooga1180.com/georgia-scores-top-spot-in-competitive-economic-rankings/)  ·  `page_052348`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp and the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) announced that Georgia earned the No. 1 spot in Site Selection’s annual Prosperity Cup rankings for successful investment recruitment in 2023.
- The Prosperity Cup criteria include total values of new and expanded facilities, business-climate attractiveness, and workforce readiness.
- In 2023, Georgia attracted 318 new and expanded facilities to secure the No. 1 ranking.
- An expanded scope for Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) is included in the rankings as the largest project in state history.
- HMGMA announced an additional 400 new jobs and $2 billion in investment in 2023.
- HMGMA’s total investment is now $7.59 billion and will net 8,500 new jobs for Georgians.
- Anovion Technologies selected southwest Georgia in May for an $800 million, 400-job synthetic graphite manufacturing facility.
- Anovion Technologies broke ground on its synthetic graphite facility in June of last year (2023).
- Meissner Corporation plans to create more than 1,700 new jobs at a new campus in northeast Georgia.
- Meissner Corporation’s expansion will more than double the company’s manufacturing footprint in the U.S.
- Site Selection’s Mark Arend stated that the Prosperity Cup recognizes state-level economic development competitiveness and that Georgia’s consistent delivery of location attributes demonstrates it competes to win.
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America held its groundbreaking in October 2022.
- In 2023, more than 10 Hyundai and automotive suppliers reached middle Georgia and beyond following the HMGMA groundbreaking.
- These Hyundai and automotive suppliers will ultimately create over 4,300 new jobs and bring $1.2 billion in combined investment, with current numbers reported as even higher.
- Kia’s facility in West Point announced an expansion during 2023.
- Toyota’s campus in Pendergrass announced an expansion during 2023.
- The combined Kia and Toyota expansions created a total of 440 new jobs and $286 million in investment.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson highlighted the collaborative nature of Georgia’s economic development team, including sister state agencies, utility partners, and local economic developers.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that decades of partnerships, community-to-state groundwork, and consistent pro-business policies have led to positive economic results for Georgia families.
- Governor Brian Kemp noted that companies choosing Georgia gain access to global markets through logistics assets like Hartsfield-Jackson International Airport and some of the fastest-growing ports in the nation.
