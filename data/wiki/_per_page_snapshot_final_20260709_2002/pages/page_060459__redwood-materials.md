# Redwood Materials

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2026-04-21

**Source:** [Redwood Materials' Restructuring Exposes Deep Cost Pressures in the U.S. Battery Recycling Supply Chain | Energy Tech News](https://energytech-news.com/redwood-materials-restructuring-exposes-deep-cost)  ·  `page_060459`

## Facts

- Redwood Materials laid off approximately 135 employees on April 21, 2026.
- The layoffs affected multiple divisions including engineering and operations.
- The cuts came three months after the company closed a $425 million funding round at a valuation above $6 billion.
- This marks the second workforce reduction in five months, following a 5% cut in November 2025.
- The restructuring triggered the departure of Chief Operating Officer Chris Lister and several other senior executives in supply chain and mechanical engineering.
- Founder and CEO JB Straubel framed the layoffs as a deliberate realignment rather than a distress response.
- Straubel noted that "parts of the company have expanded faster than needed to support the direction" of the business.
- The company is reducing management layers to sharpen strategic focus.
- Straubel stated Redwood "continues to dominate the U.S. battery recycling market" while touting "great momentum" in its newer energy storage business.
- Cuts across engineering and operations aim to align headcount with the evolving priority of a second-life battery energy storage business growing faster than the core recycling vertical.
- Ascend Elements, a Massachusetts-based battery recycler that raised more than $1.1 billion in equity and grants since its 2015 founding, filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy on April 9, 2026.
- Ascend CEO Linh Austin described financial difficulties as "insurmountable," pointing to a canceled $316 million federal grant and broader softening of EV demand.
- SK Battery America cut nearly 1,000 jobs in Georgia in 2026 amid slowing EV production by U.S. automakers.
- Lithium carbonate peaked above $80,000 per metric ton in late 2022 and was trading near $12,000 per metric ton in early 2026, a decline of roughly 85%.
- Nickel and cobalt prices have followed similar downward trajectories.
- Federal grant cancellations and program reviews have removed funding that underpinned many recyclers' capital structures.
- Redwood launched its dedicated energy storage unit, Redwood Energy, in June 2025.
- Redwood Energy deployed North America's largest microgrid: a 12 MW / 63 MWh installation built from 805 retired EV battery packs to power a Crusoe AI modular data center in Sparks, Nevada.
- The system was constructed in under four months and described as profitable by Straubel.
- In April 2026, Redwood and Rivian announced the first repurposed battery energy storage system deployed at a U.S. automaker's manufacturing facility.
- The installation is 10 MWh at Rivian's Normal, Illinois, plant using more than 100 second-life Rivian battery packs.
- Redwood signed a battery repurposing MOU with General Motors.
- Redwood holds partnerships spanning Volkswagen Group, BMW, Toyota, Ford, and Panasonic.
- Redwood processes more than 20 GWh of batteries annually, equivalent to approximately 250,000 EVs.
- This represents about 90% of all lithium-ion batteries recycled in North America.
- Projections cited by Redwood indicate the U.S. will need more than 600 GWh of storage by 2030 to handle peak demand and AI-driven load growth.
- Redwood's South Carolina facility carries a $2 billion conditional DOE loan commitment.
- The South Carolina facility's first phase added an initial 20,000 metric tons of annual processing capacity in late 2025.
- Ascend's bankruptcy leaves Redwood as the dominant independent domestic recycler with scale.
- The absence of a meaningful competitor in high-purity cathode precursor production could affect pricing dynamics for automakers and cell manufacturers depending on recycled material inputs for domestic content compliance.
- Redwood has raised over $2 billion in private capital before grant dependency became critical.
- Companies can monetize packs before recycling by extracting storage value from batteries retaining 50% or more of usable capacity.
- Redwood's restructuring is a strategic realignment, not a signal of financial distress.
- The company's $6 billion-plus valuation and $425 million Series E position it among the best-capitalized players in the sector.
- Feedstock control is a structural moat; Redwood's dominance in North American battery collection provides a durable competitive advantage that commodity-price headwinds cannot easily erode.
