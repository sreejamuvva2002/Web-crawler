# Anovion Technologies

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2023-06-27

**Source:** [Georgia Gov. attacks Biden's electric vehicle policy at battery material maker](https://www.wtxl.com/news/local-news/georgia-governor-attacks-bidens-electric-vehicle-policy-at-federally-backed-battery-material-maker)  ·  `page_052411`

## Facts

- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp escalated his attack on President Joe Biden's electric vehicle policy at the groundbreaking for Anovion Technologies.
- Anovion Technologies received more than $100 million to refine graphite for electric batteries from the infrastructure law signed by President Biden.
- Gov. Kemp stated that Georgia's electric mobility boom is happening because the state is second to none for companies looking to invest, relocate, expand, and innovate, not because of federal government favoritism.
- The political remarks were unusually partisan for a factory groundbreaking.
- Anovion Technologies has an $800 million investment promise.
- Anovion promises 400 new jobs in rural Bainbridge, located in Georgia's far southwest corner.
- Georgia has been a top beneficiary of a nationwide electric vehicle investment boom, with more than 40 electric vehicle-related projects since 2020 pledging $22.7 billion of investment and 28,400 jobs in the state.
- Gov. Kemp remarked that when President Biden and others falsely try to take credit for Georgia's success, people should remember that next year is an election year.
- Democratic Sen. Jon Ossoff was likely to share his stage at the groundbreaking.
- Sen. Ossoff has been Georgia's most high-profile supporter of President Biden's electric vehicle policies.
- Gov. Kemp and Sen. Ossoff could be rivals for the U.S. Senate seat in 2026.
- Sen. Ossoff argued that Georgia's investment boom would not have happened without Democratic policies.
- Sen. Ossoff told The Associated Press it is bizarre to attend a groundbreaking and launch a political attack on the policy that made it possible, calling the governor's actions a panicked political tantrum over federal manufacturing policies' success.
- Gov. Kemp has always opposed the Inflation Reduction Act, which is pumping billions into electric vehicle subsidies.
- Gov. Kemp disagreed with the Inflation Reduction Act's domestic content standards, which are meant to increase America's clean-energy manufacturing capacity.
- These domestic content standards make tax incentives on electric vehicles available only when the vehicle, battery, and key raw materials are all made in the United States.
- Hyundai Motor Group is building a $5.5 billion plant to assemble electric vehicles and batteries in Ellabell, Georgia, near Savannah.
- Hyundai has stated that the tax credits are unfair because its electric vehicles are not currently eligible.
- Gov. Kemp referenced Hyundai's criticism in his speech, stating that 'that approach simply doesn't work.'
- Fellow Democratic senator Raphael Warnock proposed making the tax credits more flexible.
- Sen. Ossoff emphasized that benefits will be available to Hyundai once the Ellabell plant starts production.
- Gov. Kemp noted that Rivian Automotive announced a $5 billion plant east of Atlanta in December 2021, and Hyundai announced its project in May 2022, both before President Biden signed the Inflation Reduction Act.
- Gov. Kemp wants to make Georgia the 'e-mobility capital of the nation' as his second-term legacy.
- Sen. Ossoff has claimed credit for President Biden and Democrats, including for solar panel plant expansions in northwest Georgia.
- Hyundai and LG Energy Solution announced a $4.3 billion electric battery plant in May at Hyundai's new complex.
- Sen. Ossoff assertively trumpeted the Hyundai-LG battery announcement while Gov. Kemp was in Israel, which chafed some Kemp administration officials.
- Gov. Kemp attributed Anovion's choice of location to state and local officials, saying they 'don't posture or showboat, and they don't try to steal credit.'
- Many Republicans oppose electric vehicles; former President Donald Trump told the Georgia Republican convention he would abolish President Biden's electric vehicle policies on his first day in office.
- Gov. Kemp did not attend that convention due to dissatisfaction with state party leadership.
- Gov. Kemp has tried to persuade Republicans to break off their support for former President Trump while simultaneously opposing a Democratic president who is lavishing electric vehicle makers with billions in incentives.
- Gov. Kemp stated that unlike top-down systems like China's, Georgia is not dictating how growth happens or picking winners and losers, but is letting the market drive innovation and expansion.
- Anovion Technologies is a Chicago-based firm.
- Anovion's Georgia factory will produce synthetic graphite, a key ingredient for lithium batteries.
- The factory benefits from content standards that are boosting domestic graphite demand.
- Anovion won $117 million in federal financing to build and improve its factories.
- Anovion may be able to claim federal tax credits of 10% on the costs of producing graphite and 30% on its factory investment, both part of the Inflation Reduction Act.
- Sen. Ossoff stated that manufacturing is coming back to America and Georgia as intended when infrastructure and manufacturing policies were passed.
