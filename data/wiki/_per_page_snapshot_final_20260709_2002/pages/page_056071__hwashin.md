# Hwashin

**Source:** [GA: Auto Parts Supplier Hwashin to Bring 460 Jobs to Middle Georgia With New Facility in Dublin | Trade and Industry Development](https://www.tradeandindustrydev.com/region/georgia/news/ga-auto-parts-supplier-hwashin-bring-460-jobs-32420)  ·  `page_056071`

## Facts

- Georgia Gov. Brian P. Kemp announced that Hwashin, an automotive parts company, will establish a new manufacturing facility in Dublin.
- The project will create over 460 new jobs.
- The project involves more than $176 million in investment in Laurens County.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated the goal to become the e-mobility capital of the nation is bringing opportunity to communities across the state.
- Hwashin is the latest company to join Georgia's growing ecosystem of companies bringing good jobs in this industry.
- Hwashin stands as a Tier-1 supplier specializing in crafting chassis and body parts for Hyundai Motor Group, Kia, and international original equipment manufacturers (OEMs).
- Hwashin operates from its headquarters in Korea.
- Hwashin has established production facilities spanning the United States, Asia, and South America.
- Seo Jin Jung is the CEO of Hwashin.
- Hwashin aspires to shape a future global city focused on electric vehicles (EVs), drawing from its 50-year expertise and collaboration with Georgia and Laurens County.
- The alliance seeks to position the region at the forefront of the evolving automobile industry.
- Hwashin will take on the role of a reliable partner to customers and an engaged contributor to the local community.
- Hwashin believes in its capacity to contribute to the prosperity of communities and the American auto parts industry.
- Hwashin extends gratitude to the State of Georgia and Laurens County for their ongoing cooperation and support throughout this project.
- Hwashin's new Georgia facility is scheduled to begin production in late 2025.
- The facility will be located at the Georgia Highway 257 Rail Served Site.
- This site is the first Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) Select site in the state.
- A “GRAD Certified Select” status indicates a site has met or exceeded more rigorous certification requirements.
- The facility will be primarily focused on manufacturing chassis components for OEMs, including Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America and Kia Georgia.
- Dublin Mayor Joshua Kight welcomed Hwashin to the community.
- The staff and board of the Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority showed Hwashin why Dublin-Laurens County is a great place to do business.
- The company's manufacturing facility in Dublin will become an economic engine for the region, leading to more employment opportunities and quality growth.
- Jeff Davis is Chairman of the Laurens County Board of Commissioners.
- Hwashin is the seventh international company to call Dublin and Laurens County home.
- The project demonstrates the partnership and 'can-do attitude' between Laurens County, the City of Dublin, and the Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority.
- The project will change the landscape for Dublin-Laurens County, providing new career opportunities for area residents.
- The project will develop increased and far-reaching opportunities in areas such as housing and retail development.
- Griff Lynch is President and CEO of the Georgia Ports Authority.
- Significant investments from Hwashin and other automotive suppliers are delivering career opportunities that will allow Georgians to thrive right here at home.
- The Georgia Ports Authority supports economic development throughout the state and welcomes Hwashin as the newest member of its manufacturing community.
- The authority's mission is to efficiently connect companies like Hwashin to international markets.
- Alyce Thornhill is Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy at the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team.
- GDEcD worked on this competitive project in partnership with the Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia EMC, and Georgia Quick Start.
- Pat Wilson is a GDEcD Commissioner.
- From their chosen site, Hwashin will have direct interstate access to at least two long-term automotive partners.
- Hwashin will be able to pursue an accelerated construction timeline by locating on a GRAD Select site.
- Decades of elected leadership have supported infrastructure and workforce development in Georgia.
- For over a century, Georgia has fostered healthy industry practices, encouraged collaboration and innovation, and positioned the state as a leader in developing emerging technologies for the automotive and mobility industry.
- In fiscal year 2023, job creation in the automotive industry increased by 324 percent when compared to FY21.
- Suppliers attracted by Georgia’s mobility OEMs resulted in over $2 billion in investment across the state.
- Since 2020, Georgia has pursued the entire EV supply chain, creating more than $25.1 billion in investments and 29,000 jobs.
