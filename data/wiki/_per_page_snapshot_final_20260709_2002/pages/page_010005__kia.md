# Kia

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-04-25

**Source:** [Kia commits $1M to e-mobility initiative at UGA - Give to UGA](https://give.uga.edu/kia-commits-1m-to-e-mobility-initiative-at-uga/)  ·  `page_010005`

## Facts

- Kia committed a $1 million gift to the University of Georgia College of Engineering and the Georgia Network for Electric Mobility (GNEM).
- The $1 million commitment provides endowed support for undergraduate scholarships, graduate research fellowships, community engagement programs, and experiential learning opportunities related to e-mobility for students in the College of Engineering.
- Supported experiential learning opportunities include co-ops, internships, and capstone projects.
- Kia's gift supports the Georgia Network for Electric Mobility (GNEM), which was established by the University of Georgia in early 2023.
- GNEM focuses on enhancing research, education, and outreach opportunities in e-mobility across the state of Georgia.
- GNEM brings together industry, academia, government, and communities to aid workforce development, economic development, and research.
- GNEM takes special care to involve rural and underserved communities and small businesses in its initiatives.
- As a founding partner of GNEM, Kia will provide support in perpetuity for educational pursuits, research productivity, and experiential learning efforts for students interested in electric mobility careers.
- Kia established a state-of-the-art production facility in West Point, Georgia in 2009.
- Since the facility opened, Kia Georgia has helped create more than 14,000 jobs.
- The Kia Georgia facility in West Point has produced more than 4.2 million vehicles.
- One of the vehicles soon to be produced at the West Point facility will be Kia's all-electric vehicle, the EV9.
- Kia served as a premier sponsor of the University of Georgia's third annual Electric Mobility Summit.
- The Electric Mobility Summit took place on April 23-24 and focused on electric mobility adoption in Georgia, educational programs to support industry growth, and the future of electric mobility in the state and beyond.
- Kia's GNEM support aligns with UGA's land-grant mission to serve all populations within Georgia.
