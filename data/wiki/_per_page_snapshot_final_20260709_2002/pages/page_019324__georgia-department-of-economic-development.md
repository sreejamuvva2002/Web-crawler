# Georgia Department of Economic Development

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2022-12-31

**Source:** [Key Industries Sustain Momentum for State's Economic Development - Valdosta CEO](https://valdostaceo.com/features/2023/01/gov-kemp-key-industries-sustain-momentum-states-economic-development/)  ·  `page_019324`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp and the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) announced that Georgia sustained economic development momentum during the first half of Fiscal Year 2023 (July 1 through December 31, 2022).
- The 218 locations or expansions supported by GDEcD’s Global Commerce division are expected to create approximately 17,500 new jobs and more than $13 billion in investment for the state.
- Both the number of supported locations and total investment amount increased over the same period last year.
- Areas outside the 10-county metro Atlanta region accounted for 85 percent of jobs created and 92 percent of investments from economic development projects during the period.
- Four of Georgia’s largest industries – advanced manufacturing, automotive, aerospace, and food processing – accounted for nearly 75 percent of new jobs and 84 percent of investments.
- Planned investments resulting from these projects increased by more than 38 percent compared to the same time last year.
- The automotive industry was the second-largest source of new jobs and investment, after advanced manufacturing.
- In October, state and local officials joined company representatives, including Korean Ambassador to the U.S. Tae-yong Cho, for the groundbreaking of the new Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) EV facility.
- The HMGMA EV facility will support more than 8,100 direct jobs in Bryan, Bulloch, Chatham and Effingham Counties.
- Three off-site HMGMA suppliers announced locations following the groundbreaking, swiftly surpassing the initial $1 billion in investment predicted to come from such additions.
- International companies accounted for 23 percent of state projects since July 1, 2022, acting as valued partners in creating a closed-loop electric mobility supply chain.
- The partnership between SK On and Hyundai Motor Group in Bartow County is a historic project example in the top five largest projects by investment in the state’s history.
- Next-generation developer FREYR Battery’s Coweta County facility is also among the top five largest projects by investment in the state’s history.
- Battery recyclers Sung-Il Hitech and Denkai America joined Georgia’s growing electric mobility ecosystem.
- The aerospace and defense industries created more than 1,500 new jobs by benefiting from Georgia’s logistics infrastructure and engineering talent pool.
- Archer Aviation Inc., an aerospace company advancing sustainable urban air mobility, announced a facility near airports that will benefit from nearby top-ranked technical colleges and four-year universities.
- Anduril Industries, a defense technology company, also announced a facility near airports with similar benefits from nearby educational institutions.
- Advanced manufacturing is a cornerstone industry sector in Georgia’s middle and coastal regions, announcing more than 7,700 new jobs in the state since July 1, 2022.
- Major advanced manufacturing investments or expansions announced in FY 2023 include YKK AP in Macon and Beretta Holding S.A.’s new facility in Bryan County through its subsidiary Norma Precision Inc.
- Germany-based Haering Precision celebrated the grand opening of its Lavonia facility in November with a two-day event including a “Future Day” for approximately 400 local high school students and community members.
- Companies in Georgia’s food processing industry announced more than $695 million in investments, largely through expansions, supported by the state’s agricultural strength and logistics capabilities.
- King’s Hawaiian®, a Georgia partner for more than a decade, announced another expansion of its Oakwood facility, marking its second expansion in less than a year.
- GDEcD Deputy Commissioner for Global Commerce Kristi Brigman stated that companies choose to grow in Georgia because they are connected with community and state partners to solve workforce challenges.
- Brigman noted that companies utilize the Georgia Quick Start program to build talent from the ground up, helping Georgians upskill amid strong inbound domestic migration trends.
- Georgia Tech hosted a groundbreaking for its Science Square purpose-built district in August.
- The Rowen Foundation’s 2,000-acre science and learning campus broke ground in early December.
- Athena Studios announced it will add an additional 350,000 square feet to its Athens facility, including a structure dedicated to training the local workforce through a partnership with the University of Georgia and Georgia Film Academy.
- BlueStar Studios announced more than 600,000 square feet of new stage space and an additional 45,000 square feet dedicated to technical resources and amenities at the former Fort Gillem outside of Atlanta.
