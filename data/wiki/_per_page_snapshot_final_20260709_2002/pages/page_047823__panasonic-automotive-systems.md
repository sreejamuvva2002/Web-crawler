# Panasonic Automotive Systems

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-11-27

**Source:** [No big changes as Panasonic Automotive Systems sells majority stake to Apollo Global Management | The Citizen](https://thecitizen.com/2023/11/27/local-industry-headquarters-to-be-sold-jobs-retention-being-negotiated/)  ·  `page_047823`

## Facts

- Panasonic Automotive Systems headquarters is located in Peachtree City, Georgia.
- The company is selling a majority stake in its automotive systems unit to a US private equity fund managed by an affiliate of Apollo Global Management.
- There are no current discussions about closing any Panasonic Automotive facilities or leaving the Peachtree City area.
- Peachtree City is home to the North American Panasonic Automotive headquarters.
- The scope of this transaction is not yet fixed and will be determined based on further discussions.
- The company does not expect any layoffs due to this deal.
- Business operations will continue as is.
- Panasonic Holdings Corporation (PHD) signed a memorandum of understanding (MOU) with Apollo Global Management to become joint partners in the automotive business segment.
- The automotive industry is currently undergoing a once-in-a-century paradigm shift toward electrification and software defined vehicles, prompting the partnership to leverage external resources.
- Maintaining relationships with current customers, suppliers, stakeholders and community partners is a top priority during this transition.
- The company name, brand and structure will remain as it is today for the foreseeable future.
- No changes to current office locations are anticipated as a result of this announcement.
- The company anticipates reaching a definitive agreement in March of 2024.
- Talks are underway to sell a stake of 50 to 80 percent to the US equity firm, with the aim of finalizing an agreement by the end of March next year.
- No purchase price has been announced yet.
- Panasonic Automotive produces electronic parts and systems for cars and is wholly owned by Panasonic Holdings.
- The company has a global workforce of around 30,000 people.
- The local plant on Ga. Highway 74 South had a workforce in the past of several hundred people, but that number has gone down in recent years.
- Shares of Japan’s Panasonic Holdings have rallied about 10% since it announced the plan to sell a stake in its automotive systems business.
- Analysts said Panasonic could be following in the footsteps of Hitachi, which sold off several businesses to transform into a digitally driven service firm.
- One analyst said Panasonic needs to adjust nimbly as it faces disruption from competition in traditional areas of growth.
- Investors reacted positively to the potential sale, as the announcement was both unexpected and welcomed.
- The automotive unit makes cockpit and electronics systems for vehicles.
- Panasonic’s automotive unit is separate from its energy unit that makes batteries for electric vehicles, including those from Tesla.
