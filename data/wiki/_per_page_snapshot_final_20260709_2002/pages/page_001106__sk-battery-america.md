# SK Battery America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-03-09

**Source:** [SK Battery America lays off nearly 1,000 workers at Georgia plant | WardsAuto](https://www.wardsauto.com/news/sk-battery-america-lays-off-1000-workers-at-georgia-plant-ev/814128/)  ·  `page_001106`

## Facts

- SK Battery America, a subsidiary of South Korea-based SK On, is laying off hundreds of workers from its production facility in Commerce, Georgia.
- The layoffs are occurring as electric vehicle battery demand slows and federal incentives dry up.
- The producer of Nickel-Cobalt-Manganese lithium-ion battery cells filed a Worker Adjustment and Retraining Notification on Friday.
- The notification notified the state of Georgia of job cuts affecting 958 people.
- Impacted workers will receive pay and benefits for the next 60 days, confirmed by a spokesperson via email to Manufacturing Dive.
- Friday, March 6 was the last day of work for the affected employees.
- The Commerce site previously had 2,566 workers before reductions.
- Workforce reductions brought the Commerce site headcount down to 1,600.
- Joe Guy Collier serves as SK America’s spokesperson and stated the company will work with affected workers during the transition.
- SK Battery America issued a statement affirming its commitment to Georgia and building a robust U.S. supply chain for advanced battery manufacturing.
- The company is actively pursuing future customers in the Battery Energy Storage System arena.
- The EV battery industry is currently navigating demand challenges, with many automakers and battery companies implementing cutbacks due to slower sales and reduced federal incentives under the current administration, according to the Federal Reserve Bank of Dallas.
- Automakers and battery companies are also shifting focus to cater to growing data center demand for backup power and energy storage systems.
- In December, Ford Motor Co., a customer of SK, announced it was shifting its electrification plans to include hybrid vehicles and smaller, more affordable EVs.
- Ford is simultaneously expanding its energy storage business.
- The automaker expects to record a $19.5 billion write-down due to its strategic shift.
- Ford dissolved its BlueOval SK battery joint venture with SK On as it moves away from electric vehicles.
- Ford canceled the electric F-150 Lightning production due to sluggish sales.
- In 2023, SK America announced plans to expand its Commerce site workforce by several hundred employees to meet rising EV demand, exceeding an initial goal of 2,600 employees.
- The Occupational Safety and Health Administration previously fined the EV battery maker twice for exposing workers to respiratory hazards.
- Law firm Strauss Borrelli announced an investigation into SK Battery for allegedly failing to notify the 958 employees in compliance with the WARN Act.
- The WARN Act mandates that employers provide written notification to workers at least 60 days before a plant closure or job cuts take effect.
- Spokesperson Joe Guy Collier stated that SK America’s workforce reduction complies with WARN guidelines.
