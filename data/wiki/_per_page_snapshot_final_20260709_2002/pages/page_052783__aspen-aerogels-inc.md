# Aspen Aerogels, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2026-05-07

**Source:** [Aspen Aerogels, Inc. Reports First Quarter 2026 Financial Results and Recent Business Highlights](https://www.barchart.com/story/news/1766632/aspen-aerogels-inc-reports-first-quarter-2026-financial-results-and-recent-business-highlights)  ·  `page_052783`

## Facts

- The company previously planned a second manufacturing facility in Statesboro, Georgia.
- Aspen recorded a $286.6 million impairment charge related to the abandoned Statesboro, Georgia facility during the first quarter of 2025.
- Aspen's PyroThin® products are designed to enable solutions for thermal runaway challenges within the electric vehicle (EV) market.
- The company received a $37.6 million commercial settlement from General Motors in Q1 2026, with approximately $4.9 million to be recognized as revenue quarterly through the end of 2027.
- Thermal barrier segment revenue fell to $16.3 million in Q1 2026, reflecting a significant reduction in customer demand following changes in regulatory frameworks and incentive programs.
- Aspen is utilizing existing inventory and leveraging external manufacturing capacity to support customer demand while its East Providence facility undergoes a staged restart beginning in May 2026.
- The company's strategy involves partnering with industry leaders to leverage its Aerogel Technology Platform into high-value markets, including e-mobility and clean energy.
- EV thermal barrier customers retain the right to cancel contracts with Aspen at any time and without penalty.
- Aspen faces potential risks regarding costs, expenses, or investments incurred in excess of projections used to develop pricing under contracts with EV thermal barrier customers.
