# Qcells

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2022-05-26

**Source:** [Qcells Expands Manufacturing in Dalton, GA - Qcells North America](https://us.qcells.com/blog/qcells-announces-solar-panel-manufacturing-expansion-will-be-in-dalton-georgia/)  ·  `page_001582`

## Facts

- Qcells plans to bring nearly 500 additional jobs to Whitfield County as part of its long-haul commitment to the U.S. solar market.
- The investment marks Qcells’ latest efforts to rebuild the U.S. solar supply chain and help meet growing demand for decarbonized energy sources.
- Qcells announced that its new solar panel manufacturing facility will be located in Dalton, Georgia.
- A $171 million investment will boost production of advanced photovoltaic modules to help the U.S. achieve goals of decarbonizing the electric grid and creating good-paying manufacturing jobs.
- Qcells CEO Justin Lee stated that the additional investment in Dalton will help Qcells better serve the needs of U.S. customers with increased local manufacturing capacity.
- Justin Lee noted that Georgia has become the clean energy manufacturing heart of America, and Qcells is proud to contribute to the state’s advanced manufacturing economy.
- The new facility will produce 1.4 gigawatts of solar modules per year made with Qcells’ next generation photovoltaic cells using high efficiency tunnel oxide passivated contact technology (TOPCon).
- Located near the company’s existing 1.7-gigawatt factory in Dalton, the expansion will bring Qcells’ total U.S. capacity to 3.1 gigawatts, equivalent to one-third of the country’s solar module manufacturing capacity.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp expressed excitement over Qcells' continued expansion in Georgia, highlighting decades of outstanding partnership between the state and Korea focused on bringing jobs and opportunities to Georgians.
- Qcells’ Dalton expansion is expected to bring 470 new jobs to Whitfield County.
- With more than 750 staff currently employed at the existing factory, total local employees will exceed 1,000 when the expansion is complete.
- Groundbreaking for the facility is planned for fall 2022, with operations expected to commence within the first half of 2023.
- In March, Qcells’ parent company Hanwha Solutions became the largest shareholder of U.S. polysilicon manufacturer REC Silicon to secure reliable supply of raw material crucial to solar panel manufacturing.
- Senator Jon Ossoff appreciated Qcells’ sustained and growing investment in Georgia and the working relationship built to create jobs, pledging to continue making Georgia a world leader in renewable energy innovation and manufacturing.
- Qcells retains the largest market share in the U.S. commercial and residential markets.
- According to the Wood Mackenzie Q1 2022 U.S. PV Leaderboard report, Qcells obtained 24.1 percent market share in the residential sector in 2021.
- According to the Wood Mackenzie Q1 2022 U.S. PV Leaderboard report, Qcells obtained 20.6 percent market share in the commercial sector in 2021.
- Qcells is also a leading supplier to the utility solar sector.
- Senator Reverend Raphael Warnock commended Qcells’ smart investments in Georgia and pledged to work closely with the company to incentivize solar production and encourage additional economic investments creating good-paying jobs across North Georgia and the state.
- Qcells aspires to realize its vision of being a “completely clean energy solutions provider” by working with partners in the U.S. and elsewhere to accelerate the energy transition.
- Qcells provides low-carbon solar products, smart energy solutions, renewable electricity retail services, and packages to end customers worldwide.
- Qcells is headquartered in Seoul, South Korea (Global Executive HQ).
- Qcells maintains a Technology & Innovation HQ in Thalheim, Germany.
- Qcells operates diverse international manufacturing facilities in the U.S., Malaysia, China, and South Korea.
- Through a global business network spanning Europe, North America, Asia, South America, Africa, and the Middle East, Qcells provides services and long-term partnerships to customers in the utility, commercial, governmental, and residential markets.
