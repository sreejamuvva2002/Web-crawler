# Aspen Aerogels

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2025-02-13

**Source:** [Aspen Aerogels halts construction of Statesboro plant  - Grice Connect](https://www.griceconnect.com/business/aspen-aerogels-halts-construction-of-statesboro-plant-10228940)  ·  `page_052654`

## Facts

- Aspen Aerogels announced it is halting construction of its planned second aerogel manufacturing facility in Statesboro.
- CEO Donald R. Young made the announcement during an investor call on Thursday morning, February 13, 2025.
- The company will explore options to leverage its Statesboro assets, including potentially relocating some equipment to its existing plant for upgrades and expansion.
- To meet future aerogel demand, Aspen will focus on maximizing capacity at its East Providence facility and implementing a flexible supply strategy.
- The announcement comes nearly three years after Aspen unveiled plans for a $325 million, 500,000 square foot facility in the Bruce Yawn Industrial Park, promising 250 jobs.
- Aspen's decision to build in Statesboro came three months before Hyundai announced the construction of the Meta Plant in Bryan County, though the two projects were not connected.
- The project has faced numerous challenges, with the company previously announcing a “right-timing” of construction in 2023.
- CFO and Treasurer Ricardo C. Rodriguez stated that in early 2023, pre-empting a reset in EV demand expectations, the company decided to right-time the construction and subsequently ramped up external manufacturing capacity for its Energy Industrial business.
- Hopes for the Statesboro project were briefly rekindled late last year when Aspen announced a conditional commitment from the U.S. Department of Energy for a proposed loan of up to $670.6 million for a planned facility in Register, Georgia.
- The latest announcement dashes hopes for the Statesboro project.
- Benjy Thompson, CEO of the Development Authority of Bulloch County, stated that businesses often reevaluate their strategies and expressed disappointment over the cessation of construction.
- Thompson noted that taxpayer protection is a priority and clawback provisions are in place to recoup local investment should the project not move forward.
- The Development Authority, along with local and state partners, will continue to pursue long-term projects that bring good jobs, smart investment, and economic opportunities to Bulloch County citizens.
