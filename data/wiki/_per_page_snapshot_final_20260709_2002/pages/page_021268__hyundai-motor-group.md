# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-07

**Source:** [Hyundai, Czech govt. to sign formal plant contract in mid-May  - Tehran Times](https://www.tehrantimes.com/news/117165/Hyundai-Czech-govt-to-sign-formal-plant-contract-in-mid-May)  ·  `page_021268`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor chairman Chung Mong-Koo was arrested on embezzlement charges last week.
- The formal contract with the Czech government will be signed in mid-May.
- Vice-Chairman Kim Dong-Jin will likely sign the deal on behalf of Chung in a low-key manner.
- A Czech Republic delegation, including industry Minister Milan Urban, will travel to Seoul for the signing.
- Hyundai Motor postponed "indefinitely" a ground-breaking ceremony scheduled for May 17 for a one billion euro (1.26 billion dollar) plant at Nosovice in the northeast of the Czech Republic.
- Hyundai suffered a serious setback in domestic sales last month due to the slush fund scandal, while exports continue robustly.
- Chung, 68, was arrested and imprisoned Friday on charges of embezzlement and breach of trust following a month-long probe into a slush fund scandal.
- He is charged with raising about 130 billion won (138 million dollars) in slush funds through group affiliates since 2001.
- He is charged with causing 390 billion won in damages to the group through misconduct.
- Prosecutors are investigating allegations that a substantial portion of the slush fund was used to bribe government and financial officials for business favors.
- Chung is suspected of using part of the slush fund money to facilitate the transfer of corporate wealth and management control to his son, Chung Eui-Sun, president of Kia Motors.
- The Hyundai Motor group is the world's seventh largest auto maker and controls more than 70 percent of the South Korean car market.
- The planned Czech car plant should produce about 300,000 vehicles a year starting in 2008.
- Kia Motors, a unit of Hyundai, operates a plant in neighboring Slovakia.
- Citing the pending probe of its parent Hyundai Motor, Kia Motors last month put off a ground-breaking ceremony for a 1.2 billion dollar auto plant in West Point, Troup County, Georgia.
- Hyundai Motor reported total sales rose 2.5 percent year-on-year to 215,037 vehicles in April, but were down 12.8 percent from March.
- Domestic sales stood at 44,044 vehicles in April, the lowest level for the month since 2000, down 14.4 percent from March and 1.5 percent from a year earlier.
- Exports totaled 170,993 vehicles in April, up 3.6 percent year-on-year but down 12.4 percent month-on-month.
- Kia Motors sales were down 1.3 percent year-on-year to 113,406 vehicles in April, with domestic sales off 8.4 percent to 21,532 and exports posting a modest 0.5 percent rise to 91,874.
