# Rivian New Horizon LLC

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Extensive Ground Work Lifts 18-Story Miami Beach Tower  | Engineering News-Record](https://www.enr.com/articles/61576-extensive-ground-work-lifts-18-story-miami-beach-tower)  ·  `page_059053`

## Facts

- Rivian Automotive broke ground on its long-planned electric-vehicle factory in Georgia.
- The project is valued at $5 billion and is supported by one of the state’s largest incentive packages and a multibillion-dollar federal loan designed to anchor EV manufacturing in the Southeast.
- State leaders marked the official start of site development on Sept. 16.
- Construction mobilization is scheduled for 2026, with vehicle production beginning in 2028.
- The facility will be completed in two phases and span roughly 9 million sq ft.
- Production capacity aims to exceed 400,000 vehicles annually.
- Rivian's R2 and R3 crossover models will anchor production at the plant.
- Georgia officials estimate the value of state and local incentives at about $1.5 billion.
- An economic development agreement signed by Rivian and the state in 2022 outlines statutory tax credits, property tax abatements, workforce training, land and infrastructure improvements, and discretionary “speed-to-market” spending.
- The Georgia state legislature appropriated $112.6 million for land acquisition and site preparation.
- Site preparation includes a new interchange, a rail spur, and a training center.
- The U.S. Dept. of Energy closed on a loan of up to $6.57 billion to Rivian New Horizon LLC in January.
- The federal loan covers site development, construction, and manufacturing facilities.
- Rivian temporarily paused the project in 2024 to conserve cash while launching its R2 line at its Normal, Ill., plant.
- Project work restarted after securing federal financing and reconfirming the company's long-term strategy.
