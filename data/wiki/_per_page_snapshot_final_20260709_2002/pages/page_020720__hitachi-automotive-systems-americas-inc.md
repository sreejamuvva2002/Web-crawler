# Hitachi Automotive Systems Americas, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2019-05-17

**Source:** [Hitatchi Automotive Systems To Expand In Walton County – GeorgiaPol](https://www.georgiapol.com/2019/05/17/hitatchi-automotive-systems-to-expand-in-walton-county/)  ·  `page_020720`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Hitachi Automotive Systems Americas, Inc. plans to create 100 jobs and invest at least $100 million, with potential up to $330 million, in an expansion at its existing facility in Monroe, Georgia.
- Hitachi Automotive Systems Americas, Inc. is a Tier 1 supplier of world-class products to the global automotive market.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated that Hitachi has been a great partner to the Walton County community by investing in employees and creating jobs for more than twenty years.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp expressed confidence that the investment will positively impact the local community and encourage continued success in Georgia’s strong automotive industry.
- Hitachi Automotive Systems Americas, Inc. is a group company of Hitachi Automotive Systems, Ltd., which is headquartered in Tokyo.
- Hitachi Automotive Systems, Ltd. manufactures and markets engine powertrain systems, electric powertrain systems, and integrated vehicle control systems for major automotive original equipment manufacturers (OEM) worldwide.
- Hitachi Automotive Systems Americas, Inc. President and CEO Paul Carroll stated the company has been operating in Georgia for over twenty-two years.
- The Georgia factory supplies advanced technology products for major automotive OEMs including Ford Motor Company, General Motors, Nissan, Subaru, and Honda.
- Paul Carroll highlighted the partnership with stakeholders including the State of Georgia, Walton County, and the City of Monroe.
- Hitachi Automotive Systems Americas, Inc. opened its first facility in Georgia in 1997.
- The company currently employs 900 Georgians.
- The expansion aims to increase production capacity in response to the growth of the automotive industry.
- Walton County Commission Chairman Kevin Little stated that Hitachi has become the county's largest employer.
- Kevin Little expressed gratitude for the quality jobs and investment Hitachi provides in Walton County.
- City of Monroe Mayor John Howard described Hitachi as one of Monroe's finest corporations investing in high technology and top-quality jobs.
- Mayor John Howard praised the company's community support and involvement as second to none.
- GDEcD Sr. Project Manager Tammy Caudell represented the Global Commerce Division in partnership with the Development Authority of Walton County, Walton EMC, and the Georgia Department of Community Affairs.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that establishing relationships with job-creating companies like Hitachi elevates their work.
- Pat Wilson noted that companies like Hitachi have driven success and increased interest from Japanese companies in Georgia.
- Hitachi Automotive Systems, Ltd. is a wholly owned subsidiary of Hitachi, Ltd., headquartered in Tokyo, Japan.
- The company develops, manufactures, sells, and services automotive components, transportation related components, industrial machines and systems, and offers engine powertrain systems, electric powertrain systems, and integrated vehicle control systems.
