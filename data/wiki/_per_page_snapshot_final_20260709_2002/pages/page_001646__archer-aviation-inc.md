# Archer Aviation Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Energy, Automobile, EV, Renewable News | Archer Aviation Showcases Midnight Aircraft In Atlanta As It Nears Completion Of Its High-Volume Manufacturing Facility In Covington, Georgia](https://oilandgaspress.com/archer-aviation-showcases-midnight-aircraft-in-atlanta-as-it-nears-completion-of-its-high-volume-manufacturing-facility-in-covington-georgia/)  ·  `page_001646`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation Inc. (NYSE: ACHR) is nearing completion of its high-volume manufacturing facility in Covington, GA, reaffirming an expected 2024 completion date.
- Construction of the nearly 400,000 square foot facility will be completed by the end of the year.
- The facility will support Archer’s planned production of up to 650 Midnight aircraft annually in partnership with Stellantis.
- This output volume would make the Covington site one of the largest manufacturing facilities by volume in the aviation industry.
- Archer completed the first build phase earlier this year, which included grading and foundation work.
- The company is nearing completion of phase two, involving the erection of exterior walls and roof.
- Phase three (interior fit out) and phase four (equipment load in and power on) are on track to be completed in the coming months.
- Archer showcased its Midnight aircraft at Atlantic Aviation’s new terminal at DeKalb–Peachtree Municipal Airport to guests from Georgia’s Office of the Governor, Georgia’s Congressional Offices, the City of Atlanta, Georgia’s State Legislature, the Federal Aviation Administration, and other local governments.
- The Midnight is Archer’s electric vertical take-off and landing (eVTOL) aircraft, built around Archer’s proprietary electric powertrain.
- The Midnight is designed to travel at speeds up to 150 mph, turning hour-long ground commutes into minutes in the air.
- The aircraft is designed for back-to-back flights of 20-50 miles with minimal charge time in between.
- At cruising altitudes, the Midnight operates 100x quieter than a helicopter, making it nearly inaudible from the streets below.
- The aircraft features redundant systems across its design to target safety levels similar to commercial airliners.
- The Midnight is designed to carry a pilot, up to four passengers, and carry-on luggage.
- Adam Goldstein, CEO and founder of Archer, stated that the company’s goal is to be the first in the eVTOL industry to achieve scaled manufacturing once production ramps up to 650 aircraft a year.
- Archer describes itself as a leader in the electrification of aviation, designing and developing key enabling technologies and aircraft to power the next great transportation revolution.
