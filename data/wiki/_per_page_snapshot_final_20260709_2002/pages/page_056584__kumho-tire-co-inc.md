# Kumho Tire Co., Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Kumho Tire — Grokipedia](https://grokipedia.com/page/Kumho_Tire)  ·  `page_056584`

## Facts

- Kumho Tire traces its origins to 1960 in Gwangju, South Korea, founded by Incheon Park of the Kumho Asiana Group.
- Initially operated at a capacity of 20 tires per day under the original name Samyang Tire.
- First tires were produced in 1961 to meet nascent domestic demand for vehicle components.
- Exported to Thailand in 1965 totaling USD 7,700.
- Secured the DOT mark from the U.S. Department of Transportation and KS quality standard in 1966.
- Operational Songjeong plant began in 1971, with the Gwangju plant completed in 1974.
- Developed Korea's first radial and aircraft tires in 1975.
- Annual production surpassed 1 million units by 1976.
- Received the grand prize at the 5th National Quality Management Award in 1979.
- Achieved a USD 100 million export milestone trophy in 1980.
- Received approval from General Motors for supplying tires in the 1980s.
- Established the Gwangju Central Research Center in 1981 to strengthen domestic R&D capabilities.
- Merged with Kumho Industry Co., Ltd. in 1984, rebranding as Kumho Co., Ltd.
- Completed the Gokseong Plant proving ground in 1989.
- Established the Kumho America Technical Center (KATC) in Akron, Ohio in 1990 as its first overseas R&D facility.
- Ranked among the world's top 10 tire manufacturers by 1992 with cumulative production exceeding 100 million tires.
- Completed the Gokseong Plant and Proving Ground in 1990 to enhance testing capabilities.
- Initiated research into racing tires in 1990, positioning itself as a motorsport leader by the late 1990s.
- Developed the world's fourth run-flat tire technology in 1999.
- Received ISO 9001 certification in 1994 and ISO 14001 in 1996.
- Renamed to Kumho Tire Co., Ltd. in 1996.
- Completed the Tianjin Plant in China in 2006 to serve Asian demand.
- Completed the Changchun Plant in China in 2007.
- Started operations at Nanjing's truck and bus radial (TBR) facility.
- Opened a factory in Vietnam in 2008 to diversify production bases.
- Supplied tires for the Korea Super Prix F3 in 2000 and became the official supplier for the F3 Euro series in 2003.
- Captured 40% of the global F3 tire market by the end of the decade through specialized racing compounds.
- Pioneered the world's first 32-inch ultra-high-performance (UHP) tires in 2006 for luxury vehicles.
- Provided original equipment supply to Ford in 2008, integrating advanced materials for handling and durability.
- Entered a debt workout program in late 2009 amid financial distress of parent Kumho Asiana Group.
- By 2010, creditors including the Korea Development Bank converted portions of debt to equity, securing a combined 42% stake.
- Creditors injected approximately 3.9 trillion won in additional support over subsequent years to prevent insolvency.
- Balance sheet deteriorated by 2017 due to weak sales and high debt servicing.
- September 2017: Creditor consortium ousted Kumho Asiana's influence and assumed direct management.
- December 29, 2017: Formal separation from the Kumho Asiana Group occurred as part of creditor-directed reforms.
- March 2018: Qingdao Doublestar Co. acquired a controlling stake in Kumho Tire for $597 million.
- Under Doublestar's ownership via Xingwei Korea (holding about 45% as of mid-2025), the company focused on cost reductions and supply chain integration with the parent.
- Majority-owned by Xingwei Korea Company Limited, a South Korean subsidiary of China's Qingdao Doublestar Group, holding approximately 45% of shares as of recent filings.
- Other significant shareholders include IBK Asset Management Co., Ltd. (7.43%), The Korea Development Bank (7.43%), Woori Financial Group (3.95%), and the Government of South Korea (1.12%).
- Governance is overseen by a Board of Directors comprising seven members, including four independent directors.
- ESG Management Committee established in February 2023 as an independent board subcommittee chaired by CEO Jeong Il-taek.
- Operates eight manufacturing facilities across four countries, primarily producing passenger car (PC) tires.
- South Korean facilities include the Gwangju Plant (PC, T&B, combat plane tires), Goksung Plant (PC, T&B), and Pyungtaek Plant (PC).
- Chinese facilities include the Nanjing Plant (PC, T&B), Tianjin Plant (PC), and Changchun Plant (PC).
- Vietnam facility in Binh Duong Province produces PC tires.
- United States facility is the Georgia Plant in Macon, Georgia, producing PC tires and operational since 2016.
- Gwangju plant suffered a major fire on May 17, 2025, suspending operations due to damage to rubber compounding and blending lines.
- Production at Gwangju partially resumed in November 2025, with capacity restored to approximately 10,000 tires daily by March 2026.
- Global production capacity reached over 62 million tire units annually following expansions at Vietnam and Georgia facilities completed in 2023.
- This marked an increase from 55-57 million units prior to those projects.
- Gwangju plant has an annual capacity of 12 million tires, accounting for approximately 20% of total output.
- Vietnam plant was expanded to 12 million tires per year by mid-2025.
- Macon, Georgia plant is rated at 4 million tires annually.
- November 2024: Announced a further $300 million investment to expand the Vietnam facility by 36%, adding 4.5 million units of passenger car and light truck tire capacity.
- Modifications at the China plant aim to allocate 1.4 million units for new-energy vehicle tires by 2025.
- Global workforce totaled 10,240 employees as of the 2024 sustainability report.
- Georgia plant employs over 380 workers focused on tire production.
- North American headquarters supports an additional 50 staff for regional operations and sales.
- Office workers' unions in South Korea affiliated in April 2024 amid efforts to align compensation with welfare benefits.
- Core product lines include the Ecsta brand (high-performance/UHP), Solus series (grand touring/all-season), Road Venture range (SUV/light truck), and Marshal brand (value-oriented).
- Commercial offerings include the KRS03 all-position rib tire and SmartWay certified KLD11e drive axle tire for medium-duty trucks.
- Maintains a global R&D network including its Central Research and Development Center in Yongin, South Korea, plus facilities in China, the United States, and Germany.
- EV tire development is a core priority, with the 'EnnoV' brand launched in March 2024 for electric mobility innovations.
- In 2022, prioritized EV tire development among 257 projects, completing 171 focused on reducing rolling resistance and improving durability for heavier EV loads.
- Aims for 100% sustainable materials by 2045, incorporating R&D into raw material substitutions and end-of-life tire management.
- European R&D center in Frankfurt develops high-performance tires for diverse driving conditions and original equipment applications.
- Introduced a digital twin tire development system in November 2024 to accelerate design and lower costs through virtual simulations.
- 2023: Introduced the Solus TA51a grand touring all-season tire engineered for coupes, sedans, crossovers, and SUVs.
- 2024: Launched Ecsta Sport All-Season PA71 and Ecsta Sport Summer PS72 tires at the SEMA show in November.
- August 2025: Announced the Road Venture RT rugged-terrain light truck tire, available from September in 27 sizes for 17- to 22-inch rims.
- "Volume" and "Origami" conceptual tire designs earned recognition at the 2025 U.S. International Design Excellence Awards for speculative approaches to structure and sustainability.
- 2010 revenue was 2.702 trillion KRW, reflecting a 42.6% year-over-year increase after returning to operating profitability.
- Revenue grew to 4.04 trillion KRW with net income of 157.8 billion KRW in 2023.
- Preliminary 2024 revenue reached 4.53 trillion KRW with net income of 324.9 billion KRW.
