# Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2022-06-11

**Source:** [Yamaha breaks ground on SMART Warehouse in Newnan, Ga. | Powersports Business](https://powersportsbusiness.com/top-stories/2022/06/11/yamaha-breaks-ground-on-smart-warehouse-in-newnan-ga/)  ·  `page_015670`

## Facts

- Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation (YMMC), based in Newnan, Georgia, broke ground on a new advanced and innovative “SMART” Warehouse.
- The SMART Warehouse is the first-of-its-kind for Yamaha Motor Company worldwide.
- Using state-of-the-art automated storage and retrieval systems, the new facility will be 200,000 square feet.
- The warehouse will reduce response time for fulfilling dealer orders for Yamaha WaveRunners, ATVs, side-by-sides, and golf cars by more than 50 percent.
- Mike Chrzanowski, president and CEO of Yamaha Motor Corporation, stated the facility will improve overall operational efficiency and material flow velocity to factory assembly lines.
- Demand for all outdoor recreational products produced at the YMMC factory continues to exceed supply.
- SMART is an acronym for Sequenced Material and Reduced Transportation.
- The medium parts automated storage and retrieval system will retrieve and put away 240 containers per hour, compared to the current rate of 170 containers per hour.
- The small parts automated system will handle 2,400 containers per hour, compared to the current capability of 550 containers per day.
- New systems will enable Yamaha to reduce forklift usage by 50 percent using automatic conveyors and tuggers to move parts through the SMART center to factories.
- The investment underscores Yamaha’s long-term commitment to YMMC employees, the City of Newnan, and Coweta County.
- The facility will bring higher paid and more skilled jobs to Coweta County, including roles for robotics experts, software developers, and systems engineers.
- The warehouse was designed by SSI SCHAEFER.
- The facility will include an observation platform for groups such as students from local schools to learn about manufacturing and automated processes.
- Yamaha expects to complete the SMART Warehouse in 2024.
