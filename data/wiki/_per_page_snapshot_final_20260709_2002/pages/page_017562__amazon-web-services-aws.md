# Amazon Web Services (AWS)

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2025-01-07

**Source:** [AWS says it'll invest 'at least' $11B to expand data center infrastructure in Georgia | TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2025/01/07/aws-says-itll-invest-at-least-11b-to-expand-data-center-infrastructure-in-georgia/)  ·  `page_017562`

## Facts

- AWS plans to invest at least $11 billion in Georgia to expand its infrastructure to support various cloud computing and AI technologies.
- AWS estimates that its investment will create roughly 550 jobs in the state of Georgia.
- AWS stated in a press release that it is proud to expand operations in Georgia to help drive the next generation of cutting-edge technologies such as AI.
- AWS expressed gratitude to state and local leaders who have partnered with the company and looks forward to keeping Georgia at the leading edge of the digital age.
- AWS' investment infusion comes about eight months after the company said it would pour $11 billion into data centers in Indiana.
- The Indiana data center deal will create at least 1,000 jobs, according to an AWS pledge.
- Data center construction in the Atlanta market increased 76% year-over-year by midyear 2024.
- Georgia's cheap electricity, existing fiber-optic infrastructure, and state tax incentives are attractive to Big Tech companies.
- Google, Meta, Elon Musk’s X, and Microsoft are other major data center infrastructure spenders in the region.
- In 2023, Microsoft purchased hundreds of acres of land to build a data center expected to cost around $1.8 billion.
- Locals oppose the data center developments, arguing they compete with housing and other more pressing real estate needs.
- Last September, Atlanta’s city council banned new data centers from opening in neighborhoods near transit and the popular Beltline walking trail.
- Data centers tend to have high power requirements.
- Utility Georgia Power is leaning partly on fossil fuels to generate enough capacity to power current and future data centers.
- Environmental advocates have criticized Georgia Power's move to rely on fossil fuels for data center power generation.
- By 2028, the Atlanta metro’s data center inventory could grow to a power load of over 4,000 megawatts.
- The projected 2028 power load will be more than 30 times the metro’s data center load in 2012.
- The AI boom is responsible for driving many current data center expansion projects because AI requires lots of computing power to develop and run.
- Goldman Sachs estimates that AI will represent about 19% of data center power demand by 2028.
- Microsoft recently stated it was on track to invest $80 billion in AI data centers this year.
- A McKinsey report estimates capital spending on procurement and installation of mechanical and electrical systems for data centers could eclipse $250 billion in the next five years.
