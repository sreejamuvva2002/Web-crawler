# Volvo Cars

## Facts

- Volvo Cars said it will cut approximately 125 jobs, or 5% of the workforce, at its Charleston, South Carolina factory.
- The job cuts are being made as the company adjusts to shifting market conditions and evolving trade policies.
- The announcement was made on Wednesday as part of a broader effort to realign operations and sharpen its U.S. strategy.
- The production changes affect Volvo’s only American plant.
- The Charleston facility currently employs about 2,500 workers.
- The facility builds the EX90 electric SUV and Polestar’s Model 3.
- The plant has a capacity to produce 150,000 vehicles annually.
- Only 1,316 EX90s have been sold in the U.S. so far in 2024.
- Volvo is majority-owned by China’s Geely Holding.
- Volvo emphasized its long-term commitment to U.S. manufacturing.
- Volvo remains on track to create 4,000 jobs in South Carolina.
- Volvo plans to expand output at the Charleston site in the future.
- The Charleston job cuts are separate from broader global cost-saving measures announced last week in Volvo's Q1 earnings report.
- The global cost-saving plan includes a plan to reduce expenses by 18 billion Swedish crowns, approximately $1.88 billion.
- Volvo has not specified when it will share more details about the job cuts or which specific roles will be affected.
- Volvo has nearly 43,000 employees worldwide, with 29,000 in Europe, 10,000 in Asia, and 3,000 in the Americas.
- Volvo says the U.S. remains a core market for the company.
- Volvo reiterated that its focus remains on strengthening its American product lineup and manufacturing footprint.
