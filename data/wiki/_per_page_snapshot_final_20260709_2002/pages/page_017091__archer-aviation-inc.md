# Archer Aviation Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-12-19

**Source:** [Archer Aviation Completes Construction of Manufacturing Facility in Covington | News | jacksonprogress-argus.com](https://www.jacksonprogress-argus.com/news/archer-aviation-completes-construction-of-manufacturing-facility-in-covington/article_e4b8f81a-a0f7-54c1-a572-2bf1df7635ae.html)  ·  `page_017091`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation Inc.'s facility in Covington received a certificate of occupancy last week, signaling completion of construction for its high-volume manufacturing facility adjacent to Covington Municipal Airport.
- The company held a commemorative ribbon-cutting ceremony Tuesday for the approximately 400,000-square-foot facility, known as ARC.
- The ARC facility will manufacture Archer's Midnight eVTOL (electric vertical take-off and landing) aircraft.
- Archer is currently working to complete the tooling load-in for the initial manufacturing line.
- Production at the facility is expected to begin in early 2025.
- Archer aims to ramp up production to two aircraft per month by the end of 2025.
- Archer is finalizing an agreement with partner Stellantis, targeting a manufacturing capacity of 650 aircraft annually by 2030.
- Stellantis is contributing capital, advanced manufacturing technology and expertise, and experienced personnel to the project.
- The Midnight aircraft is designed to offer passengers a sustainable, low-noise, and safe alternative to ground transportation in congested cities around the world.
- Archer has invested more than $118 million in the manufacturing facility located on 96 acres at Covington Municipal Airport.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp praised Archer Aviation's decision to locate its manufacturing facility in Georgia.
- Governor Kemp stated that the announcement marks the next step in a journey reaffirming Georgia's commitment to working with innovative companies to create quality jobs and opportunities for years to come.
- Congressman Mike Collins of Georgia, who serves on the House Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure and the Committee on Science, Space, and Technology, highlighted Georgia's leadership in innovation, advanced manufacturing, and job creation due to the facility.
- Congressman Collins added that the project will drive progress, deliver economic benefits for Georgia, and create opportunity for everyone involved with and around the facility.
- Archer founder and CEO Adam Goldstein stated that the facility's completion is a testament to the industry shifting from R&D into commercialization.
- Goldstein noted that the team is focused on the start of production planned for early next year, followed by execution and scaling.
- Goldstein expressed pride in the Archer team and partners for completing construction fast and on budget.
- Stellantis Chief Manufacturing and Supply Chain Officer Arnaud Deboeuf stated that supporting Archer's electric aircraft growth with deep manufacturing expertise is a great opportunity for both companies.
- Deboeuf congratulated the Archer team on the milestone, calling it a significant step in taking clean mobility to the sky.
- Archer's goal is to transform urban travel by replacing 60-90 minute car commutes with estimated 10-20 minute electric aircraft flights.
- The Midnight aircraft is designed to be safe, sustainable, low noise, and cost competitive with ground transportation.
- The Midnight is a piloted, four-passenger aircraft designed to perform rapid back-to-back flights with minimal charge time between flights.
- The aircraft travels at speeds up to 150 mph, turning hour-long ground commutes into minutes in the air.
- The aircraft is designed for back-to-back flights of 20-50 miles with minimal charge time in between.
- The aircraft operates at 100x quieter than a helicopter at cruising altitudes, making it nearly inaudible from the streets below.
- The aircraft features redundant systems allowing Archer to target similar levels of safety as commercial airliners.
- The aircraft is designed to carry a pilot, up to four passengers, and carry-on luggage.
