# National Truck Equipment Association's Work Truck Show

**Type:** event  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Wrapping Up The Work Truck Show With Some Of The New Products Displayed - Ford Motor (NYSE:F) - Benzinga](https://www.benzinga.com/news/19/03/13326499/wrapping-up-the-work-truck-show-with-some-of-the-new-products-displayed)  ·  `page_014655`

## Facts

- The National Truck Equipment Association's Work Truck Show showcased vocational work truck packages and vehicles from major original equipment manufacturers.
- Freightliner displayed its eM2 electric truck, which is set to go on sale in 2021.
- The Class 6/7 Freightliner eM2 features a 325 kWh electric motor with an expected range of 230 miles and an 80 percent charge time of 60 minutes.
- The eM2 is designed for last-mile logistics and pickup & delivery service applications.
- Greg Treinen, advanced technology marketing manager for Daimler Trucks North America (DTNA), presented updates on the vehicle and DTNA's e-mobility platform.
- Freightliner is developing the truck through a co-creation process working directly with customers to gather feedback.
- The development process will lead to the Freightliner Truck Innovation Fleet, placing vehicles into customer hands for testing.
- Some testing customers will form the Electric Vehicle Council to share experiences, funding and infrastructure concerns, and maintenance and safety tips.
- The Electric Vehicle Council will serve as the basis for DTNA's e-consulting services designed for serial production customers.
- Peterbilt announced its Model 348 will be available with the Bendix Wingman Fusion advanced driver assistance system.
- The Bendix Wingman Fusion system integrates camera and radar technology to enable advanced collision mitigation features including object detection, lane departure warning, and overspeed alerts.
- Peterbilt is adding SmartLINQ remote diagnostics to Models 348, 337, 330, and 325 when selecting a Paccar PX engine.
- Customers selecting a natural gas engine will also have the option to add remote diagnostics.
- Kim Lawton, Medium Duty Marketing Manager at Peterbilt Motors Company, stated that extending remote diagnostics to medium duty products gives customers real-time information to maximize uptime.
- SmartLINQ provides real-time fleet health and generates notifications with severity, urgency, and recommended actions when a diagnostic code is generated.
- A two-year subscription is included with the SmartLINQ option.
- Isuzu introduced a new 12-foot Supreme service body for its NPR gas trucks as part of the Road-Ready Program.
- The 12-foot Supreme Spartan Service body is available for 109-inch-wheelbase NPR and NPR-HD models and includes eight compartments with adjustable shelving, stand-up height interior, stainless-steel D-ring handles, LED dome lights, optional plexiglass sliders, E-track, curbside pipe door, translucent roof, and drop-down double ladder rack.
- Isuzu announced three new Knapheide bodies for its N-Series trucks covering utility, dump, and landscaper applications.
- The Knapheide KUVcc enclosed utility body is available in an 11-foot version for 109-inch wheelbase NPR and NPR-HD gasoline-powered trucks with eight exterior compartments, two fixed interior shelves, conduit/pipe doors, dome lights, glass window, Master locks, and Knapliner-coated step bumper.
- The Knapheide 11-foot dump body is available on 109-inch wheelbase N-Series standard-cab trucks with GVWRs of 14,500 pounds and higher, featuring crossmemberless construction, fully-boxed long sills made of 7-gauge hot-rolled commercial-quality steel, an electric hoist powered by a 12-volt pump, and a versatile double acting tailgate.
- The Knapheide Landscaper body is available on 109- and 132.5-inch wheelbase N-Series standard-cab models with GVWRs of 14,500 pounds and higher, as well as 150- and 176-inch wheelbase crew-cab models with 14,500-lb GVWRs and higher.
- Ram Trucks announced updates to its chassis cab 3500, 4500, and 5500 models at the show.
- With the updates, a Class 3 chassis cab now has a max gross vehicle weight rating of 14,000 pounds, Class 4 is up to 16,000 pounds, and Class 5 is up to 19,500 pounds.
- Max payload on the updated Ram models is 12,510 pounds with a gross combined weight rating of 43,000 pounds, which is up 4,500 pounds from the 2018 model.
- The updated Ram chassis cabs have a towing capacity of 35,220 pounds.
- Ram introduced the Ram Chassis Cab Limited, described as a luxury version of its chassis cab models.
- Dave Sowers, head of commercial truck marketing at Ram, stated it will be the most upfit friendly chassis cab with surprising luxury.
- Ram also announced a Tradesman trim for its heavy duty models.
- Wabash National and Supreme showcased new truck body technologies including Wabash's new National Cold Chain product.
- Wabash's new technology uses molded structural composite material that delivers improved thermal efficiency, is lighter and stronger, and is two times more puncture resistant than truck bodies with aluminum side walls.
- Supreme's Kold King multi-temp solution has removed wood, steel, and rivets from its construction.
- Supreme showcased its Signature Van with DuraPlate Cell Core technology, which is up to 150 pounds lighter than traditional wall materials and up to five times more puncture resistant.
- Supreme's Iner-City cutaway with a flat floor is now available on the Ford Transit and will be upfit with a Ranger Design shelving package.
- Ranger Design announced its newest ladder rack, the Cargo +, designed for contractors using small commercial vans such as the Metris, Transit Connect, or Promaster City.
- The Cargo + rack is made of aluminum with an anodized finish, is rust-free and lightweight, has a carrying capacity up to 300 pounds, and includes a rolling bar at the rear.
- Nissan announced a new Rocky Ridge Titan model and Rocky Ridge upfit packages for several other models including the Frontier.
- The new Rocky Ridge Titan model will come with a full 5-year, 100,000-mile warranty.
- XL Hybrids is bringing a plug-in hybrid electric system (PHEV) to market for Ford F-250 Super Duty models.
- The PHEV system offers a 50 percent improvement in miles per gallon and a 33 percent reduction in CO2 emissions.
- The system features a 15 kWh lithium-ion battery pack that adds about 750 pounds in weight to the vehicle.
- Eric Fullmer, director of marketing at XL Hybrids, stated the system is designed for commercial operators and municipalities setting their own emissions reductions goals.
- The cost of the XL Hybrids PHEV system is likely to be around $25,000 when it launches later this year.
