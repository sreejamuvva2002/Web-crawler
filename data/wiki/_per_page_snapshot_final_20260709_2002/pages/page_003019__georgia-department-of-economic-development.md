# Georgia Department of Economic Development

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-24

**Source:** [Georgia No. 1 for Business for 12th Straight Year | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-releases/2025/georgia-no-1-business-12th-straight-year)  ·  `page_003019`

## Facts

- Georgia has been recognized as the No. 1 state for business by Area Development magazine for the 12th consecutive year.
- The announcement was made on September 24, 2025, by Governor Brian P. Kemp and the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD).
- Site selectors who decide where companies invest have voted Georgia as the 'Top State for Business' for 12 straight years in this annual poll from Area Development magazine.
- The 2025 Top States for Doing Business rankings are determined by scores in 15 categories based on input from approximately 50 leading site consultants nationwide.
- Georgia ranked in the top five in 10 categories and claimed the overall No. 1 ranking.
- The Top States for Doing Business Survey is the 16th in the annual series.
- Georgia continues investing in future opportunities through the Rural Site Development Initiative, which helps local communities prepare development-ready industrial sites.
- Lt. Governor Burt Jones stated that the designation reflects partnership between Governor Kemp's Office, the legislature, state agencies, and local leaders and economic developers across the state.
- Lt. Governor Burt Jones noted that Georgia's workforce continues to excel and serves as an example of why Georgia is the best state to live, work, and raise a family.
- Speaker of the House Jon Burns stated that the designation is the direct result of decades of strong leadership, forward-thinking policies, and strategic investments.
- Speaker Jon Burns highlighted that the accomplishment represents historic job creation and economic growth in every corner of the state.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that success in economic development takes years of planning, investment, and strong partnerships to achieve and sustain this level of success.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson noted that through support from the Georgia General Assembly, local leadership, and collaborations with sister state agencies, Georgia has been able to strengthen communities and prepare sites for future growth.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson emphasized combining these efforts with world-class workforce programs and unmatched global connectivity to ensure companies have what they need to thrive.
- Area Development magazine was founded in 1965 and is considered the leading executive magazine covering corporate site selection and relocation.
- Area Development is published quarterly and has a subscriber base of 41,000 senior executives among major U.S. corporations, leading site selection consultants, and other corporate advisors.
- Areadevelopment.com is viewed by more than 175,000 unique users each quarter.
- GDEcD serves as the state’s sales and marketing arm and lead agency for attracting new business investment, encouraging expansion of existing industry and small businesses, and locating new markets for Georgia products.
- GDEcD drives traveler visitation and promotes the state as a location for film and digital entertainment projects.
- GDEcD is responsible for planning and mobilizing state resources for economic development, fostering innovation and the arts to drive opportunity from the mountains to the coast.
