# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-08

**Source:** [From Global to Local: Industries that Put Georgia on the Map - Albany CEO](https://albanyceo.com/features/2024/08/global-local-industries-put-georgia-map/)  ·  `page_017463`

## Facts

- The State of Georgia maintains full-time representation in a dozen international markets.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development and its economic development partners capitalized on the 2024 SelectUSA Investment Summit to demonstrate why Georgia stands out to international companies seeking to locate or expand in the U.S.
- Georgia has emerged as a leader in the e-mobility sector, with more than $27.3 billion in investments since 2018, creating over 32,200 new jobs.
- Major Original Equipment Manufacturers (OEMs) making significant investments in Georgia include Kia, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA), Rivian, and Archer Aviation.
- Battery manufacturers at the forefront of battery innovation in Georgia include SK Battery America and LG Energy Solution.
- Recyclers and fuel cell manufacturers advancing recycling and hydrogen fuel technologies in Georgia include Aurubis AG, Ascend Elements, and Plug Power.
- The state’s traditional automotive sector exports reached $4.1 billion in 2023.
- The Georgia Ports Authority’s Brunswick facility is the second-busiest automotive Roll-on/Roll-off port in the U.S.
- Aerospace and defense products are Georgia’s top export, totaling $11.1 billion in 2023.
- The state ranks fifth in aerospace exports in the U.S.
- In 2023 alone, aerospace investments in Georgia surpassed $500 million.
- Notable aerospace giants contributing to the sector include Gulfstream Aerospace Corporation, Lockheed Martin, and Embraer.
- Innovative startups pushing boundaries in urban air mobility and advanced aerospace technologies include Archer Aviation and Hermeus.
- Defense leaders driving advancements in defense technology include Anduril Industries and Northrop Grumman.
- The state’s appeal is enhanced by its affordable cost of doing business, available sites, and robust workforce pipeline from institutions like the University System of Georgia and the Technical College System of Georgia.
- The Georgia Center of Innovation supports these sectors with industry-specific expertise and strategic partnerships.
- Georgia ranks among the Top 10 U.S. states for food processing GDP, contributing over $11 billion to the state’s economy.
- Major companies in Georgia’s food processing industry include Pilgrim’s, Chick-fil-A, The Coca-Cola Company, Yakult, Nestlé, and Ferrero.
- Baked goods are prepared fresh daily at King’s Hawaiian and CJ Foodville.
- Companies like Local Bounti and Pure Flavor are leading advancements in controlled environment agriculture (CEA), significantly reducing land and water use.
- Georgia’s food processing industry benefits from the eighth-largest refrigerated capacity space in the U.S.
- There are 22 technical colleges across the state ensuring a steady, skilled workforce pipeline.
- Georgia’s agricultural legacy includes leading production in peanuts, pecans, and poultry.
- For over a decade, Georgia has established itself as a prime location for global business growth, offering diverse opportunities across key industries.
