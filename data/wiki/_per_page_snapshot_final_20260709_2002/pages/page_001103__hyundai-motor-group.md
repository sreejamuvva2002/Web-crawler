## Facts

- Georgia Tech and Hyundai Motor Group signed a memorandum of understanding to collaborate on research and applications to support the future of sustainable mobility.
- The partnership will focus on developing a hydrogen economy, workforce development, and making cities smarter and more sustainable.
- Hyundai Motor Group is investing more than $12 billion in EV manufacturing and battery production in Georgia.
- Hyundai Motor Group is investing $12.6 billion in new dedicated electric vehicle and battery manufacturing facilities in Georgia.
- The investment includes the $7.59 billion Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) site in Bryan County, Georgia.
- The HMGMA site will create more than 8,500 direct jobs.
- The investment includes a $5 billion battery manufacturing facility joint venture with SK On in Bartow County, Georgia.
- The SK On joint venture facility will employ approximately 3,500 people.
- Proximity of the new facilities to Georgia Tech was one of the important considerations in deciding their locations.
- Kia Georgia’s West Point assembly plant began vehicle production in 2009.
- Kia Georgia's plant was established under the leadership of Honorary Chairman Mong-Koo Chung and Executive Chair Euisun Chung.
- University System of Georgia Chancellor Sonny Perdue participated in the HMG-Georgia Tech MOU ceremony.
- Sonny Perdue was the Governor of Georgia at the time of the Kia Georgia plant’s opening.
- A recent study by the Center for Automotive Research reveals Hyundai Motor’s automotive operations contributed $20.1 billion and 190,000 jobs to the U.S. economy in 2021 alone.
- According to the study, Hyundai’s investments in Georgia are expected to create nearly 40,000 jobs every year.
- According to the study, Hyundai’s investments in Georgia are expected to generate $4.6 billion in individual earnings every year.
- Hyundai’s economic impact in Georgia will be tens of billions of dollars in the years to come.
- The partnership with Georgia Tech builds upon Hyundai Motor Group’s long history in Georgia.
- The partnership is expected to last for decades and was one of the reasons Hyundai chose Georgia for its EV investments.
- Future mobility concepts explored through the collaboration include zero emissions hydrogen-powered vehicles on campus, advanced air mobility shuttles to Hartsfield International Airport, and hands-free autonomous vehicles on I-75 and I-85.
- The expanded relationship with Georgia Tech will include opportunities for student professional development and cooperative work programs.
- Hyundai will collaborate with Georgia Tech on joint research and development programs focused on the future of sustainable mobility.
- Joint R&D efforts will focus on battery technology and expanding the hydrogen economy.
- Georgia Tech and Hyundai will create technical training and leadership development programming for Hyundai employees.
- The partnership will initiate engagement activities to stimulate interest in STEM degrees among students.
- Hyundai will actively participate in career recruitment events at Georgia Tech.
- Hyundai will create learning programs for both undergraduate and graduate students.
- Hyundai’s partnership with Georgia Tech includes field-naming recognition at Bobby Dodd Stadium, which is now known as Bobby Dodd Stadium at Hyundai Field.
- The partnership provides a vital pathway for Georgia Tech to expand its stature as a leading research entity in the electrification of the automotive industry through world-class hydrogen research.
- The partnership offers an opportunity for Georgia Tech to contribute more broadly to Georgia’s commitment as a hub for electric vehicle production.
- The partnership will generate new revenue for Georgia Tech Athletics.
- This athletic revenue will provide student-athletes and teams with resources needed to compete at the highest levels athletically and academically.
