# U.S. Department of Energy

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [Energy Department Announces Actions to Secure American Critical Minerals and Materials Supply Chain | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/articles/energy-department-announces-actions-secure-american-critical-minerals-and-materials-supply)  ·  `page_004050`

## Facts

- The U.S. Department of Energy announced its intent to issue notices of funding opportunities totaling nearly $1 billion to advance and scale mining, processing, and manufacturing technologies across key stages of the critical minerals and materials supply chains.
- The proposed funding will accelerate the growth of the U.S. critical minerals and materials sector.
- The funding announcements were issued in accordance with President Trump’s Executive Order Unleashing American Energy.
- The initiative aims to ensure a more secure, predictable, and affordable supply of critical minerals and materials foundational to American energy dominance, national security, and industrial competitiveness.
- U.S. Secretary of Energy Chris Wright stated that the United States has long relied on foreign actors for critical materials essential to modern life and national security.
- Secretary Wright noted that the Energy Department will play a leading role in reshoring the processing of critical materials and expanding domestic supply under President Trump’s leadership.
- The Advanced Materials and Manufacturing Technologies Office expects to release a NOFO of up to $50 million early this fall through the Critical Minerals and Materials (CMM) Accelerator program.
- The CMM Accelerator promotes technology maturation to unlock capital investments and facilitate domestic commercialization.
- The CMM Accelerator NOFO addresses processes in the rare-earth magnet supply chain, refining and alloying gallium, gallium nitride, germanium, and silicon carbide for semiconductors, cost-competitive technologies for direct lithium extraction and separation, and critical-material separation technologies for co-producing useful products from byproducts and scrap.
- The Office of Fossil Energy and Carbon Management is announcing intent to issue a NOFO to support approximately $250 million of financial assistance for American industrial facilities producing valuable mineral byproducts from existing industrial processes.
- This program requires piloting technologies at an industrial scale in an industrial facility where material feedstocks can be processed to derisk technical uncertainty and financial risk for commercial deployment.
- The proposed NOFO addresses topic areas pertaining to both industry at large and the coal-based industry.
- The Office of Manufacturing and Energy Supply Chains (MESC) is announcing intent to issue a NOFO of up to $135 million to enhance domestic supply chains for rare earth elements (REEs).
- The REE initiative aims to reduce America’s dependence on foreign sources by demonstrating commercial viability of methods for domestically refining and recovering REEs from mine tailings, deleterious material, and waste streams.
- An academic partner is required as part of the project team for the REE initiative, and awards require a cost-share of at least 50% by the recipient.
- MESC is announcing intent to issue a NOFO of up to $500 million to expand U.S. critical mineral and materials processing and derivative battery manufacturing and recycling.
- This funding supports demonstration and/or commercial facilities processing, recycling, or utilizing for manufacturing critical materials including traditional battery minerals such as lithium, graphite, nickel, copper, aluminum, and other minerals contained within commercially available batteries like rare earth elements.
- Awards for the battery materials program require a cost-share of at least 50% by the recipient.
- The Advanced Research Projects Agency-Energy (ARPA-E) is planning to announce project selections early this fall for its $40 million RECOVER program.
- The RECOVER program aims to develop technologies to recover critical minerals from industrial wastewater, enabling the U.S. to reduce dependence on imports and replace them with secure domestic sources.
- Significant amounts of critical minerals exist in domestic wastewater systems, untreated and discarded.
- RECOVER technologies complement traditional mining operations to access materials and potentially meet a significant portion of America’s needs using supplies that might otherwise go to waste.
