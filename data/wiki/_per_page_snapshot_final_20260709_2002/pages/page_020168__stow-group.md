# Stow Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2025-08-21

**Source:** [European Firm to Invest $36M in Northwest Georgia Factory - Global Atlanta](https://www.globalatlanta.com/european-firm-to-invest-36m-in-northwest-georgia-factory/)  ·  `page_020168`

## Facts

- A European provider of warehousing and automation solutions is set to invest $36 million in a new factory in northwest Georgia.
- Stow Group says it will hire 200 workers at the facility in northwest Georgia.
- The region's reputation as the “carpet capital of the world” in the 1970s helped make Georgia the No. 1 destination for Belgian firms.
- Georgia set up an office in Brussels under then-Gov. Jimmy Carter, partly in response to flooring investments flocking to the region.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson cites the state's strong “manufacturing legacy” and proximity to an inland port as key factors.
- Stow’s main customers are warehouses involved in “intralogistics,” which have congregated near the inland port.
- Stow’s facility in Gordon County will focus on the “highly automated production” of racking systems as part of customized storage solutions for customers in logistics, food, life sciences, and more.
- Plant Manager Michael Morgenroth confirmed the facility's focus in a news release from Gov. Brian Kemp’s office.
- The investment does not appear to include Stow’s Movu Inc. autonomous mobile robot division, which lists an Illinois U.S. headquarters.
- Back in Belgium, Movu operates out of a separate innovation center opened in 2023 in Lokeren.
- The Georgia site will be Stow’s first facility in the United States.
- Stow is based in Spiere-Helkijn, Belgium, in the West Flanders province near the French border.
- The global company employs 2,000 “Rack Stars” across 11 factories and sales offices in 21 countries.
- Stow's international footprint includes 20 locations in Europe and one in Australia.
- The 45-year-old company’s products are sold in more than 60 countries.
- The investment comes as steel from Europe faces a 50 percent tariff.
- CEO Jos De Vuyst stated that expanding production in the U.S. is a key milestone to serve customers faster, enhance local partnerships, and commit to innovation and sustainability.
- Three open jobs are currently listed in Georgia, including two at the Adairsville plant and one supply chain and purchasing manager role in Atlanta.
- More production roles are to be announced in profiling, stamping, welding, powder coating, automation, and assembly.
- Logistics, administrative, and management roles are also on the way.
