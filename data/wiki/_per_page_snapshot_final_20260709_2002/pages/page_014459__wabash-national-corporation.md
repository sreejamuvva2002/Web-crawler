# Wabash National Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- Beckwood Press Company was awarded a contract by Wabash National Corporation to engineer and build a custom 3,500-ton hydraulic bulge forming press.
- The press is scheduled for delivery in October 2019.
- The machine will be used to form stainless steel and aluminum tank heads with diameters up to 156-in. for Wabash National’s tank and silo business.
- The 3,500 ton, 285-in. x 216-in. press will weigh approximately 650,000 lbs when fully assembled.
- The press will be installed in a 6-ft. deep pit.
- The 160-in. by 160-in. bolsters will contain machined t-slots for use with the Quick Die Change system.
- A fully adjustable 8-point square gib-guidance system will ensure bed-to-ram parallelism throughout the stroke.
- The press will accommodate Wabash National’s existing tooling and be able to bulge form using either air (pneumatic) or water (hydro).
- Beckwood will design a specialized closed-loop pneumatic control system for the bulge forming operation.
- The control system allows Wabash National to form parts to specific dome heights while utilizing up to 750psi of air pressure.
- An automated quick die change system with chain driven die clamps and a bed trolley will be included.
- The trolley will be capable of transporting dies up to 30,000 lbs across a 30-ft track via a push-button controls station.
- Polycarbonate guarding will be provided around the working area for added safety.
- Hydraulically-actuated die safety blocks will secure the ram during die changeover.
- A collision detection system will be installed on each end of the trolley.
- Wabash National Lead Manufacturing Engineer Barb Yocum stated that Beckwood has been engaged and supportive, with detailed and careful work from quoting to design.
- Beckwood president Jeffrey Debus stated the press is one of the largest and heaviest machines they have ever built.
- Since moving into a new facility last year, Beckwood has seen an increase in requests for presses of this magnitude.
- Extended ceiling heights and heavy crane capabilities allow Beckwood to assemble, program, and test these machines in-house, which greatly expedites delivery to customers.
- Wabash National Corporation (NYSE: WNC) is a diversified industrial manufacturer and a leading producer of semi-trailers, truck bodies and liquid transportation systems.
- The company was established in 1985 in Lafayette, Indiana.
- Wabash National manufactures dry freight and refrigerated trailers, platform trailers, bulk tank trailers, dry and refrigerated truck bodies, structural composite panels, trailer aerodynamic solutions, and specialty food grade and pharmaceutical equipment.
- Wabash National products are sold under the brand names: Wabash National®, Beall®, Benson®, Brenner® Tank, Bulk Tank International, DuraPlate®, Extract Technology®, Supreme®, Transcraft®, Walker Engineered Products, and Walker Transport.
