# Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation of America (YMMC)

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2021-08-12

**Source:** [Yamaha Celebrates 4 Million Units Assembled in the USA](https://www.globenewswire.com/en/news-release/2021/08/12/2280046/0/en/Yamaha-Celebrates-4-Million-Units-Assembled-in-the-USA.html)  ·  `page_004458`

## Facts

- Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation of America (YMMC) and Yamaha Motor Corp., USA (YMUS) celebrated the production of 4 million vehicles with the assembly of a new 2021 Wolverine RMAX2 1000 Side-by-Side at its U.S. manufacturing facility in Newnan, Georgia.
- The milestone celebration took place at the Newnan factory on August 12 to recognize employees and thank them for their dedication to superior manufacturing processes and high-quality vehicle production.
- YMMC serves as Yamaha’s hub for 4-wheel off-highway vehicle manufacturing, producing all of the company’s Side-by-Sides, full-size ATVs, WaveRunners, and Golf Cars for worldwide distribution.
- Over the past 20 years, Yamaha has invested more than $380 million into U.S. manufacturing to create a world-class facility in Newnan, according to Taka Imanishi, president of YMMC.
- The company strives to maintain the highest quality of production and environmental standards while giving back to the community through comprehensive corporate social responsibility programs.
- Since its founding in 1988, YMMC has made a significant impact within its community, covering 280 acres with more than 1.3 million square feet of manufacturing facilities in Newnan.
- Between the YMMC facility and YMUS corporate offices in Marietta and Kennesaw, Georgia, Yamaha now employs over 2,000 people in the state alone.
- This Georgia workforce encompasses more than half of the 4,000+ individuals the company directly employs in the United States.
- The city of Newnan named May 18th “Yamaha Day” in 2011 to celebrate the company’s positive economic impacts and ongoing job creation.
- Many of Yamaha’s community impact messages were highlighted in the season 19 premiere of “Modern Marvels: Machines” on the HISTORY® Channel, which went behind-the-scenes at the factory.
- Mike Martinez, YMUS vice president of motorsports, stated that Yamaha continues to add personnel and strengthen operations in Georgia as the Side-by-Side line expands with popular new models like the Wolverine RMAX 1000.
- With increasing demand for outdoor recreation, Yamaha expects continued growth of its operations in Georgia and across the country.
- The YMMC facility holds the highest certifications for quality, safety, and environmental manufacturing for producing golf cars, personal watercraft, Side-by-Side vehicles, and ATVs.
- The factory is ISO9001 Quality Management System and ISO14001 Environmental Management System certified.
- YMUS maintains a corporate office in California, two corporate offices in Georgia, facilities in Wisconsin and Alabama, and factory operations in Tennessee and Georgia.
- Additional U.S.-based subsidiaries include Yamaha Marine Systems Company with divisions Bennett Marine (Florida) and Kracor Systems (Wisconsin), Skeeter Boats (Texas) with division G3 Boats (Missouri), and Yamaha Precision Propeller (Indiana).
