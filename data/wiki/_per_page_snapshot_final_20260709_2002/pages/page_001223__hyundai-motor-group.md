# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2026-03-29

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Group expands energy-saving measures across affiliates](https://koreajoongangdaily.joins.com/news/2026-03-29/business/industry/Hyundai-Motor-Group-expands-energysaving-measures-across-affiliates-/2556228)  ·  `page_001223`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group is expanding its energy-saving measures across its affiliates amid ongoing tensions in the Middle East.
- The auto giant is expanding its five-day vehicle rotation system, which was previously imposed on Hyundai Motor and Kia headquarters, to all Hyundai Motor Group affiliates.
- The company will increase the number of commuter shuttle services to discourage the use of personal vehicles.
- Hyundai Motor Group’s measure comes amid continued conflict in the Middle East, which is affecting global supply chains, and rising fuel prices.
- The company will implement a new AI-based system that cuts off power and lighting in unused spaces like corridors, parking lots and empty rooms, based on feeds from closed-circuit cameras and motion sensors.
- Heating, cooling and computer power usage will also be regulated under the AI system.
- Hyundai Motor Group will minimize business trips within Korea, replacing them with online meetings to reduce car usage.
- When business trips are unavoidable, electric and hydrogen fuel cell vehicles will be prioritized.
- The firm will focus on improving the efficiency of various machines and upgrading its aging facilities, including expanded inspections for electrical or oil leaks on site.
- Logistics affiliate Hyundai Glovis will optimize maritime routes and reduce vessel speeds to conserve fuel.
- As part of long-term measures, Hyundai Motor Group will install solar panels on its production sites, parking lots and car maintenance shops to improve its energy storage system.
- The firm will expand its power purchase agreement, which allows Hyundai to purchase renewable energy at a fixed price.
- The automaker will gradually replace its commuter buses with hydrogen fuel-cell buses.
- Hyundai Motor Group stated in a press release on Sunday: “We have established practical energy-saving measures to help overcome the national energy crisis, and we plan to actively implement the plans across all our affiliates.”
- Hyundai Motor Group added: “Both the company and our employees will work together to participate in the government’s energy conservation policies.”
