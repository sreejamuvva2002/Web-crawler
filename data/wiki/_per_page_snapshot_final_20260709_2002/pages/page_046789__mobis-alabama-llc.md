# MOBIS Alabama, LLC

## Facts

- Hyundai MOBIS forms the parts and service arm for South Korean automakers Hyundai Motor Company, Genesis Motors, and Kia Motors.
- The company offers chassis, cockpit, and front-end modules; safety products including airbags, headlights, anti-lock brake systems, and electronic stability control products; steering parts; multimedia systems; UVO systems; injection-molded plastic parts such as instrument panels, carriers, and bumpers; and steel wheel rims and decks.
- Hyundai MOBIS supplies after-sales service parts for vehicles and concentrates its resources on after-sales parts, module parts manufacture, and parts export.
- Hyundai MOBIS has firmly established its position as one of the leading auto parts specialist companies in the world.
- Hyundai MOBIS’s Module Division, which includes MOBIS Alabama, LLC, supplies the chassis module, cockpit module, and front-end module to automakers using a just-in-sequence (JIS) system.
- The division participates in the initial stages of new vehicle development processes and performs auto-parts research and development, including designing and testing for production applications based on module specifications.
- Through modularization system integration, Hyundai MOBIS reduces total weight and the number of parts needed, enabling easier assembly, effective inventory control, and cost reduction.
- MOBIS Alabama, LLC was established in 2002 and operates on nearly 83 acres in Montgomery, Alabama.
- MOBIS Alabama, LLC is the largest Tier 1 supplier to both Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Alabama (HMMA) and KIA Motor Manufacturing Georgia (KMMG).
- The company holds ISO14001 and IATF16949 certifications for Environmental Management and Quality Management.
- Production begins in a 600,000 square-foot Plastics plant that uses injection molding to produce front and rear bumpers, instrument panel frames, and after-service parts.
- Bumpers are molded and painted in-house at the Alabama facility.
- Bumpers destined for HMMA are assembled in-house before being transferred to HMMA for final vehicle assembly.
- Bumpers destined for KMMG are sent to a sister plant in Georgia for assembly.
- After molding, instrument panel frames move to an adjacent Module plant where they receive additional parts to form complete Cockpit (dashboard) assemblies.
- The Module building also houses front and rear Chassis (vehicle suspension) assembly lines that assemble parts from Tier 2 and 3 suppliers into completed Chassis modules.
- Completed Chassis modules are transported to nearby HMMA for final vehicle assembly.
- A Redistribution Center (RDC) supplies genuine Hyundai and KIA after-service parts to dealerships, auto parts stores, and other distribution centers across North America.
- The company offers competitive wages and benefits including paid vacation and personal time off, 14 paid holidays, a 401(k) with company match, health/dental/vision insurance, life insurance, and disability insurance.
