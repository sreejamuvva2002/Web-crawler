# Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- Kia’s aim is to change the future for the better and be a respected company that people feel inspired by and want to work for.
- Georgia Quick Start was a major draw for Kia's decision to locate in Georgia.
- Former Chief Administrative Officer of Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia Randy Jackson stated that while other states typically provide a workforce training budget and cut a check, Georgia’s approach established the state as a true long-term partner in workforce development and training.
- In 2006, Kia Motors Corp. announced plans to build Georgia’s first new auto manufacturing facility in decades.
- The chosen location was West Point, a small town in West Georgia where textile manufacturing offshoring had decimated once-thriving communities.
- Hand-made yard signs reading “God Bless Kia” appeared when the announcement was made.
- Despite the Great Recession and hedge fund bankruptcies causing global economic uncertainty and skepticism, the community, company, Georgia Quick Start, and state economic development players continued forward.
- The all-new Kia Sorento, manufactured in West Point, became one of the most popular models on the road.
- Georgia Quick Start developed and delivered a pre-employment assessment program that allowed Kia to select the best candidates from an initial pool of 41,000 applicants.
- In 2008, a landmark moment in Georgia manufacturing occurred at the Georgia Ports Authority.
- Georgia Quick Start designed and built a training center to prepare Georgians for automotive manufacturing work, which remains operational.
