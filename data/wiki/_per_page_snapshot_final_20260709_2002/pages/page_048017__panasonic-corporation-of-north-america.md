## Facts

- GDOT, The Ray, Kia Georgia, and Panasonic demonstrated a fully operational connected vehicle environment along 18 miles of Interstate 85 known as “The Ray Highway.”
- The demonstration utilized the Cirrus by Panasonic® connected vehicle data management platform that detects real-time roadway events and communicates safety-critical messages to travelers about driving conditions.
- The partnership between the four organizations is focused on raising awareness of the life-saving potential of connected vehicle technology as well as deploying real-world use cases with measurable benefits.
- GDOT and Kia Georgia vehicles were equipped with vehicle-to-everything (V2X) technology for the demonstration.
- GDOT can now deliver real-time situational awareness to drivers on equipped roadways in West Georgia, a key logistics and manufacturing corridor.
- GDOT, The Ray, and Panasonic are providing “ride-along” experiences for stakeholders where drivers will be warned of hazardous conditions ahead on the roadway before they come into view.
- These “ride-along” experiences showcase how connected vehicle technology can detect and warn drivers about weather events, work zones, hard braking, and vehicle crashes in a real-world highway environment.
- The experience includes an in-vehicle visualization of traveler warnings.
- The experience showcases the capabilities of V2X technology installed on and along roadways.
- The experience highlights the digital communication that enables the intelligence of connected roadways.
- The Cirrus cloud platform manages data for GDOT to detect events and provide real-time alerts to drivers.
- Russell McMurry, Commissioner of the Georgia Department of Transportation, stated that it is important for Georgia DOT to lead in deploying transportation technology that benefits all drivers, moves freight across the region more efficiently and safely, and advances Georgia as the technology epicenter of the east coast.
- Russell McMurry noted that connected vehicle technology will save lives and expressed determination to see it come to life in Georgia as soon as possible.
- Russell McMurry emphasized the power of partnerships with organizations like Kia, The Ray, and Panasonic to advance roadways for Georgians.
- Stuart Countess, Kia Georgia’s president and CEO, stated that Kia Georgia is proud to partner with these organizations while showcasing outstanding products being built by the Kia team in West Point, Georgia.
- Stuart Countess emphasized that customer safety is the number one priority for Kia Georgia team members and called the V2X program a forward-thinking initiative putting Kia at the heart of solutions where vehicles and infrastructure communicate seamlessly to enhance travel safety and efficiency.
- Allie Kelly, Executive Director of The Ray, stated that connected vehicle technology creates an opportunity to increase travel efficiency and driver safety simultaneously.
- Allie Kelly noted that enabling connected and autonomous vehicles requires investments in physical and digital infrastructure, which Georgia DOT and The Ray have made to improve and modernize transportation infrastructure.
- Allie Kelly highlighted that these investments offer the traveling public real-time warnings about critical driving conditions such as crashes, construction work zones, hard braking, and weather impacts like black ice, flooding, and hydroplaning.
- Chris Armstrong, Vice President of Cirrus at Panasonic’s Smart Mobility Office, stated that the tech industry constantly talks about building technology with purpose rather than developing technology for technology’s sake.
- Chris Armstrong expressed pride in partnering with mission-driven organizations in Georgia DOT, The Ray, and Kia Georgia to solve transportation challenges by bringing perspectives from public, private, and philanthropic organizations united in improving safety.
- Chris Armstrong emphasized championing the wide-scale adoption of this life-saving technology as quickly as possible.
- Panasonic Corporation of North America is based in Newark, NJ, and is a leading provider of Consumer Lifestyle technologies, Smart Mobility, Sustainable Energy, Immersive Experiences, and Integrated Supply Chain solutions.
