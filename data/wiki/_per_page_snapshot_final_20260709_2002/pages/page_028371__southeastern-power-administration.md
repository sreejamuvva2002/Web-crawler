# Southeastern Power Administration

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-10-01

**Source:** [Southeastern Power Administration | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/sepa/southeastern-power-administration)  ·  `page_028371`

## Facts

- Proposes to revise existing schedules of rates and charges applicable to the sale of power from the Jim Woodruff Project, effective October 1, 2026, through September 30, 2031.
- Opportunities will be available for interested persons to review the present rates, supporting studies, and participate in public information and public comment forums.
- Objectives are to market the electric power and energy generated by Federal reservoir projects and encourage widespread use of the power at the lowest possible cost to consumers.
- Power rates are formulated based on sound financial principles.
- Responsibilities include negotiating, preparing, executing and administering contracts for the sale of electric power.
- Prepares wholesale rates and repayment studies for regional interconnected reservoir projects, supporting deliveries made to serve contractual loads.
- Responsible for scheduling hydropower generation at Corps facilities within the marketing area to ensure and maintain continuity of electric service for customers.
- Does not own transmission lines but contracts with neighboring utilities to provide transmission service for the delivery of Federal power.
- Currently has 471 wholesale customers that serve more than 12 million residential and industrial consumers.
- Leadership includes a vacant Vice President of National Relations position, Leon Jourolmon IV as General Counsel, Samuel Loggins as Assistant Administrator & Chief Operating Officer, Carter B. Edge as Assistant Administrator & Chief Financial Officer, and James D. Hall as Chief Information Officer.
- Located at 1166 Athens Tech Rd., Elberton, GA 30635-6711.
