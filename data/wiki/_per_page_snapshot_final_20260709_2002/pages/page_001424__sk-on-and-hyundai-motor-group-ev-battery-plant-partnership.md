# SK On and Hyundai Motor Group EV Battery Plant Partnership

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-01-12

**Source:** [South Korean conglomerates partner on EV battery plant in Bartow County, Ga.](https://canada.constructconnect.com/dcn/news/usa/2023/01/south-korean-conglomerates-partner-on-ev-battery-plant-in-bartow-county-ga)  ·  `page_001424`

## Facts

- Two South Korean conglomerates, Hyundai Motor Group (HMG) and SK On, are partnering to build a new battery manufacturing plant for electric vehicles (EVs).
- The facility is located at a site in Bartow County, northwest of Atlanta, Ga.
- The new facility is considered another big win for the Peach State which is positioning itself as a go-to venue for EV and battery manufacturing and suppliers.
- Since 2020, Georgia has attracted numerous EV-related projects with a total value of the investments amounting to approximately $17 billion.
- The number of new jobs created by these projects is expected to exceed 22,000.
- The price tag for the latest plant is $4 to $5 billion.
- The facility is expected to create more than 3,500 jobs.
- The plant will be built at Bartow Centre, a zoned manufacturing and industrial area located along Highway 411, just west of Cartersville.
- The plan is to get the massive facility built and up and running by 2025.
- The plant will be one of the largest economic development projects in state history.
- HMG and SK On recently signed a memorandum of understanding regarding the partnership for the new battery facility.
- The two companies are still working out specific details.
- SK On is the lithium-ion battery subsidiary branch of SK Innovation.
- SK On currently employs more than 2,000 Georgians at its SK Battery America facility in Commerce.
- Pat Wilson, commissioner of the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD), stated that HMG and SK have been pioneering partners for Georgia for decades as major drivers for Georgia’s automotive renaissance and as the first Korean manufacturer to locate in the state, respectively.
- GDEcD is supporting cooperation and partnerships across Georgia's growing EV ecosystem to create a fully integrated supply chain for automotive OEMs while connecting battery manufacturers with recyclers.
- The state maintains that Georgia’s prime location, major ports system, extensive infrastructure, skilled workforce, and pro-business climate have positioned it as a hub for the electric transportation industry.
- Bartow County commissioner Steve Taylor noted the county is very pleased with the decision of Hyundai Motor Group and SK On.
- Taylor thanked the owners of the former Bartow Centre industrial property.
- Taylor highlighted Bartow’s strong history and talent-base built upon advanced manufacturing, the automotive industry, and innovation as reasons the battery partnership should thrive.
- Director of Korean investment, Yoonie Kim, represented the GDEcD global commerce team on the project.
- The project is in partnership with Georgia EMC, the development authority of Bartow County, the Cartersville-Bartow County department of economic development, and Georgia Quick Start.
- Atlanta Gov. Brian Kemp stated that Hyundai and SK On are valued partners and key players in the state’s ever-growing automotive industry.
- Kemp noted his administration has been focused on bringing jobs and opportunity to communities across the state that may have been overlooked in the past.
- In November, Hyundai Mobis announced plans to invest $926 million in an EV power electric system plant in Bryan County.
- The Hyundai Mobis plant will eventually employ at least 1,500 people.
- Auto parts manufacturer Joon Georgia announced it will create 630 new jobs.
- Auto parts manufacturer Joon Georgia announced it will invest $317 million in a new manufacturing facility at the Bruce Yawn Commerce Park near Statesboro in Bulloch County to supply Hyundai.
- FREYR Battery announced it will invest $2.57 billion in a manufacturing facility in Coweta County that will create 723 new jobs.
- In October, HMG broke ground on a $5.54 billion EV plant in Bryan County near Savannah that will employ at least 8,100 workers.
- Rivian Automotive announced plans for a $5-billion electric truck plant east of Atlanta that’s expected to employ about 7,500 workers.
