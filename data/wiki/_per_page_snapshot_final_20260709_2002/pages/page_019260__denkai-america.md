# Denkai America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Printed Circuit Design & Fab Online Magazine - Denkai America to Invest $150M in ED Copper Plant near Atlanta](https://pcdandf.com/pcdesign/index.php/editorial/menu-news/fab-news/16683-denkai-america-to-invest-150m-in-ed-copper-plant-near-atlanta)  ·  `page_019260`

## Facts

- Denkai America will invest $150 million to establish an electrodeposited (ED) copper foil production facility on a 115-acre site near Atlanta.
- The company aims to invest a total of $430 million and create 250 new jobs over the next five years.
- The site will also serve as Denkai's North American headquarters.
- The ED copper foil materials are used in printed circuit boards, printed electronics, and increasingly in lithium-ion batteries for electric vehicles.
- Construction is expected to begin in September as the first part of a three-phase investment.
- The facility will be fully operational by December 2024.
- Denkai America will hire skilled industrial technicians, production managers, and engineers.
- Heday Nakajima, Ph.D., president and CEO of Denkai Group, stated the investment highlights their commitment to leading ED battery foil manufacturing in North America for many years to come.
- Nakajima expressed gratitude to the teams in Richmond County and the State of Georgia for making the project possible.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Georgia is proud to be selected as the home of Denkai America, further strengthening the state's status as a leader in emerging electric mobility and existing aerospace, defense, and technology industries.
- Denkai America’s materials have traditionally been used in PCB applications primarily for the aerospace and defense industries.
- The company has operated for more than 60 years with team members worldwide and operations in Japan and North America.
- Nobu Masuda, president and CEO of Denkai America, stated they are thrilled to expand manufacturing into Augusta due to its skilled workforce.
