# DENSO Manufacturing Tennessee

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2015-08-28

**Source:** [DENSO Adding 500 New Jobs in Maryville, TN - Expansion Solutions](https://www.expansionsolutionsmagazine.com/denso-adding-500-new-jobs-in-maryville-tn/)  ·  `page_053852`

## Facts

- Tennessee Gov. Bill Haslam and Economic and Community Development Commissioner Randy Boyd along with DENSO Manufacturing Tennessee officials recently announced the company will expand its automotive components and systems manufacturing plant in Maryville.
- The expansion makes the Maryville campus one of the largest operating campuses in DENSO Corporation’s worldwide operations.
- DENSO Manufacturing Tennessee has a workforce of more than 3,100 employees.
- DENSO Manufacturing Tennessee is Blount County’s largest employer.
- The company will create 500 new jobs.
- The company will invest $400 million to expand its manufacturing capabilities and construct a new warehouse.
- Due to expanded production needs at two of the company’s manufacturing plants, the existing warehouse will be converted to manufacturing space.
- A new consolidated warehouse will be built on the company’s current campus.
- Governor Haslam stated the announcement is another indication of the momentum Tennessee has in the automotive manufacturing sector.
- Commissioner Randy Boyd noted this is the second expansion DENSO has made at one of its Tennessee facilities in less than a year since he became commissioner.
- DENSO Manufacturing Tennessee President Jack Helmboldt, who is also a DENSO Corporation global executive director, thanked skilled associates, Governor Haslam, Commissioner Boyd, state and local leaders, the Blount Chamber Partnership, and TVA for their support.
- DENSO Manufacturing Tennessee is a key supplier of advanced technology to the automotive industry.
- The Maryville campus produces starters, alternators, instrument clusters, and a wide range of electronic components and systems.
- The Maryville campus consists of four manufacturing plants on 194 acres.
- The Maryville campus has undergone eight major expansions.
- U.S. Sen. Lamar Alexander noted the auto industry has spread into 80 of Tennessee's 95 counties to become the main contributor to raising family incomes.
- Blount County Mayor Ed Mitchell praised the collaborative efforts of the Blount Partnership, the state of Tennessee, and the cities of Alcoa and Maryville in producing a win-win environment for the regional community.
- Maryville City Mayor Tom Taylor highlighted DENSO's impact on the community with jobs and its way of giving back.
- To celebrate its 25 years in Maryville, DENSO Manufacturing Tennessee donated $50,000 to Blount Memorial Hospital.
- DENSO Manufacturing Tennessee donated a separate gift of $50,000 to East Tennessee Children’s Hospital.
- Alcoa City Mayor Don Mull welcomed the opportunity to further the relationship with DENSO for the next 25 years.
- TVA Senior Vice President of Economic Development John Bradley congratulated DENSO on adding 500 new jobs in Maryville and expressed pleasure in partnering with the State of Tennessee Department of Economic and Community Development and Blount Partnership.
- Blount County Economic Development Board Chairman Fred Lawson stated DENSO’s investment nearly doubles the amount of capital investment Blount County has enjoyed since 2010.
- The press release was sourced from the Tennessee Department of Economic and Community Development.
