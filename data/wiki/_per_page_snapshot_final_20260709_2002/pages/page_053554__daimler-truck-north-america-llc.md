# Daimler Truck North America LLC

**Source:** [Daimler Truck North America Invests in Clean Transportation Technology in Portland, Oregon | Daimler Truck North America](https://northamerica.daimlertruck.com/news-stories/2024/daimler-truck-north-america-invests-in-clean-transportation-technology-in-portland-oregon)  ·  `page_053554`

## Facts

- Daimler Truck North America LLC (DTNA) announced two significant investments at its headquarters complex on Swan Island in Portland, Oregon: a new Electric Vehicle Supply Equipment (EVSE) training center and a new engineering facility.
- The new EVSE training center is located adjacent to Electric Island and represents the next phase to accelerate testing and development of charging infrastructure for commercial vehicles.
- A first-of-its-kind public charging station on Electric Island was built in partnership with Portland General Electric and opened in 2021.
- The new training center will be used for the DTNA dealer network and as a resource center for community stakeholders interested in the clean transportation industry.
- The EVSE training center project is anticipated to cost $3 million, with half of the costs funded through a forgivable loan from Prosper Portland, the City of Portland’s economic development agency.
- The new DTNA engineering facility will be approximately 110,000 square feet.
- The engineering facility will consolidate testing activities under one roof with a focus on research and development for emerging propulsion technologies, such as battery electric and hydrogen-fueled vehicles.
- The engineering facility project is anticipated to cost $40 million and will be partially funded by the State of Oregon with a $700,000 forgivable loan from the State’s Strategic Reserve fund.
- John O’Leary, president and CEO of DTNA, stated that Daimler Truck North America is proud to call Portland, Oregon its home.
- John O’Leary expressed appreciation for the City and State’s support of expansion plans to further promote the development and proper testing of advanced technology.
- John O’Leary noted that DTNA looks forward to collaborating for many more years on training and employing a future-ready workforce.
- Business Oregon Director Sophorn Cheang stated that Oregon is the location of choice for businesses leading clean technology innovation in the U.S.
- Sophorn Cheang added that the partnership with local and regional economic development leaders to support DTNA’s investment adds to the region’s competitiveness.
- Sophorn Cheang noted that the investment will provide opportunities for a skilled workforce in manufacturing and engineering for years to come.
- Portland City Commissioner Carmen Rubio stated she is thrilled by DTNA’s decision to invest significant resources and continue to lead heavy-duty vehicle technology in Portland.
- Carmen Rubio acknowledged Governor Kotek’s commitment to building the clean economy and recognized the Prosper Portland Board, Kimberly Branam, and the Prosper Portland team.
- Carmen Rubio noted that Portland is where Freightliner was founded more than 80 years ago and that DTNA is an anchor employer in the city.
- Carmen Rubio stated that today’s announcement is a proud moment for all involved and that the future of heavy-duty vehicle technology in Portland is very bright and very green.
- DTNA's history in Portland dates back to the founding of the Freightliner brand in the city in 1942.
- When Freightliner was acquired by the former Daimler AG in 1981, the manufacturer established its North American headquarters for medium- and heavy-duty trucks in Portland.
- DTNA has based all research and development activities for North America in the Portland region since 1981.
- The Portland Truck Manufacturing Plant is located on Swan Island and has been in continuous operation since 1969.
- The Portland Truck Manufacturing Plant currently produces the battery-electric Freightliner eCascadia and eM2 as well as the Western Star X-Series.
- DTNA employs over 3,000 people in the Portland region.
- Daimler Truck North America LLC designs, engineers, manufactures, and markets medium- and heavy-duty trucks, school buses, vehicle chassis, and their associated technologies and components under the Freightliner, Western Star, Thomas Built Buses, Freightliner Custom Chassis Corp, and Detroit brands.
- DTNA is a subsidiary of Daimler Truck Holding AG (DTG), one of the world’s leading commercial vehicle manufacturers.
