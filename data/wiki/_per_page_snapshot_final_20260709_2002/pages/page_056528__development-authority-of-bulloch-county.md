# Development Authority of Bulloch County

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2023-10-03

**Source:** [News Archive | Development Authority of Bulloch County](https://advantagebulloch.com/?/news)  ·  `page_056528`

## Facts

- Bulloch County was awarded a $1,518,920 grant through the OneGeorgia Authority’s Rural Site Development Initiative for industrial site development on June 19, 2025.
- More than 350 job seekers attended the first hiring event for Bulloch County-based Hyundai suppliers at Ogeechee Technical College on April 17, 2024.
- revalyu Resources broke ground on its first advanced PET (polyethylene terephthalate) recycling facility in the United States in Statesboro, GA, on October 5, 2023.
- The Development Authority of Bulloch County joined state and local officials to celebrate revalyu's groundbreaking for its U.S. facility.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp and the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) announced on October 3, 2023, that Georgia achieved an unprecedented milestone by being named the No. 1 state for business for the 10th consecutive year.
- Rachel Barnwell was appointed as Vice President of the Development Authority of Bulloch County (DABC) on September 27, 2023.
- Governor Brian Kemp approved funding on June 7, 2023, to construct the Georgia Industrial Systems and Industrial Robotics Training Center at Ogeechee Technical College to provide training for employees of local industries.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced on May 23, 2023, that Hanon Systems, a global provider of automotive thermal and energy management solutions, will create 160 new jobs in Bulloch County.
- Executives from revalyu Resources, a German-owned plastics recycling company whose established plant is in India, launched construction of a new factory in Bulloch County on January 26, 2023.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced on January 5, 2023, that Ecoplastic Corporation, an automotive parts manufacturer, will invest approximately $205 million and create 456 new jobs in a new manufacturing facility in Bulloch County.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced on November 7, 2022, that Joon Georgia, Inc., an automotive parts manufacturer, will invest $317 million and create 630 new jobs in a new facility in Bulloch County.
