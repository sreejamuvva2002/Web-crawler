# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [page_008198](https://www.cbtnews.com/hyundais-metaplant-america/)  ·  `page_008198`

## Facts

- Bryan County, Georgia, is emerging as a critical hub for the future of U.S. auto manufacturing.
- Hyundai Motor Group has invested $7.6 billion into its Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- The facility is a nearly 3,000-acre site located outside Savannah.
- This is Hyundai’s first dedicated EV and battery plant in the U.S.
- Initial capacity is 300,000 vehicles annually with the ability to scale to 500,000.
- The plant uses advanced robotics and real-time production systems to improve quality and flexibility.
- On-site battery production is handled through a joint venture with LG Energy Solution.
- Hyundai expects to create more than 8,500 direct jobs.
- Nearly 7,000 additional roles tied to suppliers and logistics are expected.
- Hyundai entered the U.S. market in 1986 and has steadily expanded domestic production since then.
- About 80 percent of Hyundai’s U.S. lineup is now built in America.
- The MetaPlant will eventually support multiple brands, including Kia and Genesis.
- The Hyundai Mobility Training Center provides hands-on training for technicians and engineers in EV systems, diagnostics, and advanced vehicle technologies.
- The plant is already producing the 2026 Hyundai IONIQ 5.
- The 2026 Hyundai IONIQ 5 offers up to 318 miles of range and access to the Tesla Supercharger network through its NACS port.
- The plant will also produce the 2026 Hyundai IONIQ 9, a three-row electric SUV with up to 335 miles of range.
- The 2026 Hyundai IONIQ 9 features ultra-fast charging capability from 10 to 80 percent in about 24 minutes.
- Hyundai plans to expand production to include hybrid models and hydrogen fuel cell trucks.
- Hyundai is investing in autonomy through partnerships with Waymo and Motional, with additional support from Magna International and deployment through Uber.
