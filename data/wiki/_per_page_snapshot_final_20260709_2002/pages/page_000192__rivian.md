# Rivian

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-04-30

**Source:** [Rivian Announces Optimized Capacity Plan for Georgia Plant](https://rivian.com/newsroom/article/rivian-announces-optimized-capacity-plan-for-georgia-plant)  ·  `page_000192`

## Facts

- Rivian is increasing its initial production capacity to 300,000 vehicles annually for its future manufacturing facility in Stanton Springs North, Georgia.
- The updated capacity represents a 50 percent increase over the initially planned capacity of 200,000 units.
- The capacity increase will facilitate a lower cost per unit while providing significant room for future expansion in later phases.
- The expanded capacity aims to support thousands of U.S. manufacturing jobs and bolster American tech leadership on the global stage.
- Rivian Founder and CEO RJ Scaringe stated the company is excited to partner with the U.S. Department of Energy to grow its manufacturing footprint in Georgia.
- RJ Scaringe noted that the R2 vehicle model dramatically expands Rivian's market opportunity and that thousands of dedicated workers will be instrumental to scaling American manufacturing.
- Rivian worked with the U.S. Department of Energy’s Office of Energy Dominance Financing (EDF) to update its DOE loan aimed at bolstering American manufacturing and construction jobs.
- The total DOE loan is aligned with the updated facility design and roadmap at up to $4.5 billion, comprising $4,006 million in principal and $494 million in capitalized interest for the initial phase.
- Rivian expects to first draw on the DOE loan by early 2027.
- Vertical construction at the Georgia plant is expected to commence in spring 2026.
- Preparations are underway for the development of the stamping press area, which is one of the most capital-intensive and technically demanding projects within the plant from a construction perspective.
- Construction progress will accelerate this summer as the facility’s primary buildings begin to take shape.
- Vehicle production at the Georgia plant is expected to begin in late 2028.
- Rivian recently announced a partnership with Uber.
- Rivian is expected to build up to 50,000 robotaxis at its Georgia plant beginning in late 2028.
- Rivian (NASDAQ: RIVN) is an American automotive technology company that develops and manufactures electric vehicles as well as vertically integrated technologies and services.
- The company utilizes innovation across electrical architecture, end-to-end software, autonomous driving platform, artificial intelligence, and propulsion to create its vehicles.
- Rivian vehicles are manufactured in the United States and sold directly to consumer and commercial customers.
