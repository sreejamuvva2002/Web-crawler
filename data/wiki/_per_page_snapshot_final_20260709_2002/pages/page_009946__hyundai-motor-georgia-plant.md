# Hyundai Motor Georgia Plant

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-10-12

**Source:** [Three workers died at Hyundai's Georgia plant since 2022, before US immigration raid, WSJ reports](https://bluewaterhealthyliving.com/news/business-and-economy/three-workers-died-at-hyundais-georgia-plant-since-2022-before-us-immigration-raid-wsj-reports/)  ·  `page_009946`

## Facts

- Three workers have died since Hyundai Motor started construction of its $7.6 billion auto plant in Georgia in 2022, according to a Wall Street Journal review of federal records.
- More than a dozen workers have suffered serious injuries at the site, including from falling without wearing harnesses and getting crushed by forklifts.
- A Hyundai Motor North America spokesperson stated the company conducted a comprehensive audit and strengthened safety oversight across the site.
- The spokesperson added that contractor vetting, training, and accountability were enhanced to ensure all partners meet Hyundai’s standards and legal requirements.
- Dozens of current and former workers, many of them safety coordinators who helped oversee construction, told the WSJ that the work environment involved many inexperienced immigrant laborers.
- These workers reported lax safety standards and frequent accidents at the site.
- The plant is operated through a joint venture between Hyundai and South Korea’s LG Energy Solution.
- The facility has been in the limelight since an immigration raid last month that detained hundreds of South Korean workers.
- The immigration raid was the largest single-site enforcement operation in the U.S. Department of Homeland Security’s history.
- Workers interviewed by the WSJ said Hyundai did not ensure that people were properly trained on site.
- Safety regulators did little to prevent worksite violations, according to the report.
- Hyundai CEO Jose Munoz told the newspaper he acted immediately and comprehensively to prevent anything like this from happening again.
- Jose Munoz stated he traveled to Georgia to tell his team directly that their safety comes before production schedules, costs, profits, and everything else.
- Construction on the electric vehicle and battery plant is ongoing.
- The plant is located in Bryan County, about 30 miles west of Savannah.
