# Joon Georgia, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2022-11

**Source:** [First Hyundai Motor Group Supplier to Build Facility in Bulloch County - Athens CEO](https://athensceo.com/news/2022/11/gov-kemp-first-hyundai-motor-group-supplier-build-facility-bulloch-county-news/)  ·  `page_056511`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced on Monday that Joon Georgia, Inc., an automotive parts manufacturer, will create 630 new jobs and invest $317 million in Bulloch County.
- Plans for the company’s new manufacturing facility mark the first confirmed supplier for Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America in Bryan County.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that less than two weeks after the groundbreaking of Hyundai Motor Group’s innovative facility, the regional impact is already being seen.
- Governor Brian Kemp noted that Joon Georgia will be the first of many companies drawn to Georgia to support the Metaplant, creating jobs and opportunity for generations of hardworking Georgians.
- Joon Georgia is part of Ajin USA, which is a current supplier for multiple Hyundai Motor Group facilities.
- The company produces a variety of automotive body parts and electronics for original equipment manufacturers.
- Jung Ho Sea, CEO of Ajin USA, stated the company is excited to be one of the first Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America automotive suppliers to build an automotive parts manufacturing facility in Bulloch County and its first plant in Georgia.
- Jung Ho Sea noted that Joon Georgia will create approximately 630 new jobs with competitive benefits in Bulloch County and the State of Georgia.
- Jung Ho Sea expressed a commitment to be the best parts supplier for automotive and EV companies like Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America, as well as the best employer and corporate citizen giving back to the community of Bulloch County and the City of Statesboro.
- Joon Georgia’s new facility will be located at Bruce Yawn Commerce Park, previously known as the Southern Gateway Commerce Park, near Statesboro and in close proximity to the Port of Savannah.
- With connections to the port, both Hyundai and Joon Georgia will be able to access global markets quickly and efficiently.
- Operations at the Bruce Yawn Commerce Park are expected to begin in mid-2024.
- The company will begin hiring for the new facility in mid-2023.
- Billy Allen, Chair of the Development Authority of Bulloch County, stated the authority is honored and proud to welcome Joon Georgia to the Statesboro-Bulloch County community and region.
- Billy Allen noted that the site in Bruce Yawn Commerce Park and proximity to the new Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America will serve the company well.
- Billy Allen highlighted that education resources, including Georgia Southern University and Ogeechee Technical College, will provide critical workforce support to help Joon Georgia succeed.
- Alyce Thornhill, Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy, represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project in partnership with the Development Authority of Bulloch County, Georgia Quick Start, Georgia Ports Authority, and Georgia EMC.
- Workforce recruitment for the new facility will be supported by Quick Start, the top-ranked workforce training program in the nation.
- By partnering with the Technical College System of Georgia, Quick Start helps assess workers, train new employees on unique processes, and develop customized job-specific training using the most current techniques and media.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Joon Georgia’s announcement is a landmark moment as Georgia drives its automotive industry into the future.
- Pat Wilson noted that Hyundai Motor Group, its suppliers, and the Savannah area JDA are creating generational opportunities for communities in Georgia.
- Pat Wilson emphasized that supporting the jobs of tomorrow is a team effort and regional collaboration ensures benefits are shared across county lines.
- Georgia is positioned as a hub for the electric mobility industry.
- Since 2020, more than 30 EV-related projects have contributed over $13.3 billion in investment and announced more than 18,100 jobs in Georgia.
- On October 25, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America officially broke ground in neighboring Bryan County on its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility.
- Non-affiliated suppliers connected to the Hyundai project are predicted to invest approximately $1 billion in other locations around the state.
