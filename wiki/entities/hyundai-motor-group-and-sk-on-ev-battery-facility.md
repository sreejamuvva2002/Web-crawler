# Hyundai Motor Group & SK On EV Battery Facility

**Type:** Facility  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[battery-cell-manufacturing|Battery Cell Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** Bartow Centre, Highway 411, Bartow County, Georgia, United States

## Overview

A planned electric vehicle (EV) battery manufacturing facility in Bartow County selected by Hyundai Motor Group and SK On to supply HMG’s U.S. plants, representing a $4-5 billion investment and creation of more than 3,500 jobs.

## EV supply-chain relevance

The facility will produce EV batteries for Hyundai Motor Group’s U.S. vehicle plants, expanding Georgia’s battery manufacturing ecosystem.

## Key facts

- The project is expected to create more than 3,500 new jobs and involve $4-5 billion in investment.
- The facility will supply Hyundai Motor Group’s plants across the United States.
- Site selected is Bartow Centre, a zoned manufacturing and industrial property on Highway 411.
- Operations are targeted to begin in 2025.
- SK On employs more than 2,000 Georgians at its SK Battery America plant in Commerce.
- SK Battery America partnered with the Work for Warriors Georgia program to hire veterans, servicemembers, and their families.
- Governor Kemp highlighted Georgia as the No. 1 state for business and emphasized job creation for local communities.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson noted that the project helps create a fully integrated EV supply chain and connects battery manufacturers with recyclers.
- Excluding this announcement, EV-related projects announced in Georgia since 2020 total approximately $17 billion in investment and more than 22,800 new jobs.
- Hyundai Motor Group also announced a separate $5.54 billion investment for the Metaplant America facility in Bryan County.
- The project is one of the largest economic development projects in Georgia’s history.
- Stakeholders estimate the investment will create more than 3,500 new jobs and involve $4-5 billion of capital.
- The facility will supply batteries to Hyundai Motor Group's U.S. vehicle plants, strengthening domestic EV supply chains.
- Bartow County is positioned as a hub within Georgia’s growing EV ecosystem.
- Since 2020, Georgia has attracted approximately $17 billion in EV-related projects, creating over 22,800 jobs.
- "Hyundai Motor Group and SK On are valued partners and key players in our state's ever-growing automotive industry," said Governor Brian Kemp.
- "By supporting cooperation and partnerships across our growing EV ecosystem, we're creating a fully integrated supply chain for automotive OEMs while also connecting battery manufacturers with recyclers to close the loop on battery manufacturing," said GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson.
