# Joon Georgia Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2022-11-12

**Source:** [Hyundai's first supplier has arrived: Joon Georgia Inc. to build facility in Bulloch County](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/hyundais-first-supplier-arrived-joon-110016296.html)  ·  `page_056524`

## Facts

- Auto parts maker Joon Georgia Inc. announced they will build a new manufacturing facility in Bulloch County, creating more than 600 jobs and investing $317 million into the county.
- The company is the first supplier for Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America in Bryan County.
- The facility will manufacture the frame for the vehicles at the Hyundai EV plant in Black Creek.
- Joon Georgia Inc. is part of Ajin USA, who is already a supplier for several Hyundai Motor Group facilities.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that less than two weeks after the groundbreaking of Hyundai Motor Group's innovative facility, they were already seeing regional impact.
- Governor Kemp noted Joon Georgia will be the first of many companies drawn to Georgia to support the Metaplant, creating jobs and opportunity for generations of Georgians.
- The facility will be located seven miles outside Statesboro in the Bruce Yawn Commerce Park.
- The unit is currently planned for 866,000 square feet but could change as it is still in the design phase.
- Savannah Economic Development Authority President and CEO Trip Tollison said the announcement marks the first of many supplier announcements to come as Hyundai Motor Group moves into the market.
- The Savannah Joint Development Authority consists of Bryan, Bulloch, Chatham and Effingham counties.
- Bulloch County Development Authority CEO Benjy Thompson described the site as the city's "industrial jewel" that took years to develop.
- Joon Georgia Inc. reached out to Thompson in June.
- Thompson stated they spent a significant amount of money making their entire site pad ready to make it marketable.
- Thompson expects more suppliers to build facilities in Bulloch County but could not provide further details.
- The hiring process is expected to begin mid-2023 with operations starting in 2024.
- Thompson noted the cost of labor is probably a little less than the Savannah metro area and they can draw the workforce to their west, whereas to the east of Savannah is the ocean.
- Ajin USA CEO Jung Ho Sea said they are excited to be one of the first Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America automotive suppliers to build an automotive parts manufacturing facility in Bulloch County and for their first plant in Georgia.
- Sea stated they want to be the best corporate citizen and give back to the community of Bulloch County and the city of Statesboro.
