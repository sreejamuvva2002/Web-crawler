# Archer Aviation Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Archer Aviation Selects Site in Georgia Adjacent to the Covington Municipal Airport for its Manufacturing Facility | Aviation Pros](https://www.aviationpros.com/aircraft-maintenance-technology/aircraft-technology/business-general-aviation/press-release/21287218/archer-aviation-archer-aviation-selects-site-in-georgia-adjacent-to-the-covington-municipal-airport-for-its-manufacturing-facility)  ·  `page_017094`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation Inc., a leader in electric vertical takeoff and landing (eVTOL) aircraft, announced plans to locate its manufacturing facility in Covington, Georgia.
- The selected site is adjacent to the Covington Municipal Airport.
- Archer plans to initially build out a 350,000-square-foot facility.
- The facility will be located on a 96-acre site.
- The initial facility is capable of producing up to 650 aircraft per year.
- The planned eVTOL aircraft manufacturing facility is expected to create over 1,000 jobs over the long term.
- Construction is expected to be completed in the first half of 2024.
- Initial production is expected to begin in the second half of 2024.
- The facility will be capable of being expanded by an additional 550,000 square feet.
- The expansion is estimated to support production of up to 2,300 aircraft per year.
- In selecting the location for its first manufacturing facility, Archer completed a comprehensive survey of potential sites across the United States.
- Key site selection considerations included the availability of talent in the local labor market, utility availability, ability to conduct seamless flight test operations, construction costs, and logistics.
- Archer will receive an incentive package that includes land conveyance, tax incentives, and a Georgia REBA grant.
- Archer anticipates that financing for the project will be provided by Synovus, the largest bank headquartered in Georgia.
- Archer CEO Adam Goldstein stated that after many months of discussion and evaluation, they are excited to select Georgia as the location for their manufacturing facility.
- Goldstein noted that the U.S. has long led the world in aerospace technology and Georgia has played a vital role in that industry.
- Goldstein stated that Archer's eVTOL technology can transform how urban and rural communities live and commute, and the factory can create pathways to highly skilled manufacturing jobs and other ladders of social and economic opportunity.
- Goldstein expressed gratitude to Governor Kemp along with Bob Kosek, Sara Gershon, Serra Hall, Frank Turner, and their teams for working together to pioneer innovative aviation technologies.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp commented that Georgia has made nurturing innovation a priority as they work with businesses to define the future.
- Kemp stated that Georgia's established leadership in the aerospace industry was essential to landing Archer Aviation in Newton County.
- Kemp expressed thrill at welcoming Archer to Georgia, where they can take advantage of the signature workforce training program Quick Start to help fill over 1,000 jobs in Covington long-term.
- Georgia is home to more than 800 aerospace companies.
- Georgia maintains a robust aerospace education pipeline featuring a dozen high schools offering training in the field, five technical colleges with aviation programs, and several universities offering degrees in aerospace engineering.
- The Georgia Center of Innovation for Aerospace, a strategic arm of the Georgia Department of Economic Development, has been cultivating an ecosystem of opportunities for innovative companies like Archer for more than 10 years.
