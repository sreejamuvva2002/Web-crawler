# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-09-13

**Source:** [Hyundai could get another $290m in government aid | Fortune](https://fortune.com/2023/09/13/hyundai-plan-invest-2-billion-more-georgia-ev-battery-plant-290-million-government-aid/)  ·  `page_009167`

## Facts

- The state of Georgia and local governments are on track to give $2.1 billion in tax breaks and other incentives to Hyundai Motor Group.
- South Korean automaker Hyundai announced last month it will invest an additional $2 billion at an electric vehicle complex it is building in Georgia.
- Projected incentives will rise by more than $290 million from the $1.8 billion deal originally announced last year.
- Only $2.75 million of the incentive increase represents additional cash from the state, while the rest comes from increases in tax breaks.
- The deal calls for Hyundai and battery maker LG Energy Solution to invest $7.6 billion in the Georgia plant.
- The deal requires hiring 8,500 workers by the end of 2031, up from the original job projection of 8,100.
- The sprawling electric vehicle and battery complex is being built in Ellabell, west of Savannah, in Bryan County.
- It is the largest economic development deal in Georgia history and comes with the largest incentive package.
- Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that benefits to Georgia outweigh the incentives.
- Hyundai is projected to have a direct payroll of $4.7 billion over the next 10 years.
- The company has promised to pay workers a yearly average of $58,105 plus benefits.
- Pat Wilson said in a statement on Friday when the state released an amended incentive agreement: “As we work together to deliver a state-of-the-art facility in Bryan County that will provide well-paying jobs to Georgians, we know Hyundai Motor Group will give back to the region, investing in our schools, families, and communities, and we are grateful for those planned investments.”
- Local officials released property tax projections to the Associated Press on Tuesday.
- The Hyundai package has been described as the largest subsidy package a U.S. state has ever promised a single automotive plant, according to Good Jobs First.
- Local governments have agreed to abate property taxes on the assembly plant through 2048.
- Local governments have agreed to abate property taxes on the battery plant through 2049.
- During the tax abatement period, Hyundai is projected to pay $523 million in taxes while saving $669 million.
- The state projects it will waive an additional $81.8 million in sales taxes on construction materials and machinery.
- This brings Hyundai’s savings from sales tax exemptions to more than $478 million.
- The company is projected to receive an additional $10.5 million in state income tax credits at $5,250 per job over five years due to the increase in jobs.
- This brings Hyundai’s state income tax savings to $223 million.
- If Hyundai did not owe that much state corporate income tax, Georgia would instead give the company personal income taxes collected from Hyundai workers.
- The state will spend an additional $2.75 million to help fund construction, machinery and equipment, boosting that total to nearly $53 million.
- Unchanged parts of the deal include state and local governments spending more than $112 million to buy and prepare 2,913 acres (1,179 hectares) for the plant.
- The state will spend $175 million on water and sewer facilities.
- The state will spend $210 million on road construction and improvements.
- The state will spend more than $153 million to recruit and train workers.
- The deal requires Hyundai to pay back a portion of the incentives if the company falls below 80% of promised investment or employment.
- Hyundai plans to start EV production in 2025, initially making 300,000 vehicles per year.
- Production may possibly expand to 500,000 vehicles a year.
- Since the company announced its first U.S. plant solely dedicated to electric vehicles, suppliers have pledged to invest $2.2 billion and hire 5,300 people.
- The announcements are part of an electric vehicle and battery land rush across the United States.
- Under the U.S. Inflation Reduction Act, EVs must be assembled in North America to qualify for a full $7,500 EV tax credit.
- A certain percentage of battery parts and minerals must come from North America or a U.S. free trade partner to qualify for the full tax credit.
