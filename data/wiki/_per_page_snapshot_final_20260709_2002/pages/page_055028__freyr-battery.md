# FREYR Battery

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [FREYR Battery taps IRA as motivation to bring Battery Storage Manufacturing to Georgia | Energy Tech](https://www.energytech.com/energy-storage/article/21254716/freyr-battery-taps-ira-as-motivation-to-bring-battery-storage-manufacturing-to-georgia)  ·  `page_055028`

## Facts

- FREYR Battery has acquired land in Georgia to built a $1.7 billion battery cell manufacturing facility.
- The company partners with 24M to use and scale up the latter’s SemiSolid lithium-ion battery platform.
- FREYR announced plans for the Giga America production plant in Coweta County at the Bridgeport Industrial Park site.
- Norway-based FREYR Battery’s initial goal is create 34 GWh in battery cell production capacity at the industrial park site once production begins.
- Eventually, FREYR plans to spend close to $2.6 billion on several battery cell lines of production by 2029.
- Co-founder and CEO Tom Einar Jensen stated that expanding into the U.S. has been a foundational aspect of FREYR’s long-term strategy from our inception.
- Jensen noted that with the recent passage of the Inflation Reduction Act, they expected U.S. demand for energy storage systems, passenger EV and other electric mobility applications to grow rapidly over the next decade.
- The project could create more than 720 U.S. jobs, the company predicted.
- Both the state of Georgia and Coweta County offered financial incentives for the project.
- FREYR intends to apply with the U.S. Department of Energy for packages that could include a grant and/or direct loans to assist with the development of Giga America.
- The announcement follows several other battery manufacturing plans in the United States revealed since passage of the Inflation Reduction Act which offers incentives such as production tax credits for tightening the clean energy supply chain surrounding the U.S.
- Early this year, technology giant Honeywell detailed a deal to buy 19 GWh of FREYR battery cells between 2023 and 2030 for its energy storage applications.
- FREYR will also leverage Honeywell’s technology such as field instrumentation, integrated automation and security integration in the manufacturing process.
- Prices for battery energy storage systems are predicted to fall by many market observers, spurring greater market development.
- Early this decade, the energy storage industry forecasted some 100 GW of new BESS capacity by 2030 as developers increase solar power installations and seek balancing assets for intermittent renewable energies.
