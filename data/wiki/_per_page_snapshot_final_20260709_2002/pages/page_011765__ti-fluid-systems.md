# TI Fluid Systems

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [TI Fluid Systems points to sales slowdown in 2024  - Sharecast.com](https://www.sharecast.com/news/news-and-announcements/ti-fluid--16417827.html)  ·  `page_011765`

## Facts

- The company provides automotive fluid storage, carrying and delivery systems and thermal management products and systems.
- Reported an 11.1% increase in constant-currency revenues to €3.52bn.
- Revenue growth was helped by a recovery in industry volumes, inflationary cost recoveries and the launch of new products.
- Constant currency revenue growth was 14.2% in Europe and Africa and 14.3% in North America.
- Constant currency revenue growth in Asia Pacific was 4.5%, held back by weakness in China.
- Adjusted earnings before interest and tax (EBIT) surged 44.2% to €259.6m.
- Achieved a 190 basis-point improvement in the adjusted EBIT margin to 7.4%.
- Chief executive Hans Dieltjens attributed the margin improvement to 'unrelenting operational and commercial execution'.
- Adjusted free cash flow was up nearly 80% at €140.7m.
- Shareholders received a 169% increase in the full-year dividend per share to 6.83 cents.
- Maintained revenue guidance of generating €3.8-4.2bn by 2026.
- Guided for returning to a double-digit adjusted EBIT margin in the mid-term.
- Forecast 'flat to low-single digit constant currency revenue growth' in 2024.
- 2024 planning assumptions are based on a modest year-on-year industry volume decline.
- Automotive production volumes for 2024 are forecast to be slightly higher in North America.
- Automotive production volumes for 2024 are forecast to be flat in China.
- Automotive production volumes for 2024 are forecast to be slightly negative in Europe.
