# Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution Joint Venture for EV Battery Manufacturing in Georgia

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-05-27

**Source:** [Hyundai and LG partner to build a $4.3 billion-EV battery plant in US](https://interestingengineering.com/transportation/hyundai-lg-to-build-ev-battery-plant)  ·  `page_056168`

## Facts

- Korean firms Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution (LGES) have announced their collaboration to establish a state-of-the-art EV battery manufacturing plant in the United States.
- The joint venture aims to take advantage of tax breaks offered by the administration for green initiatives.
- The investment amount for the facility is $4.3 billion.
- Both LGES and Hyundai Motor Group will each hold a 50 percent stake in the joint venture.
- The facility will have a yearly production capacity of 30 GWh.
- The plant's capacity is estimated to power 300,000 EVs in North America.
- Jaehoon Chang, President and CEO of Hyundai Motor Company, stated the firms aim to create a strong foundation to lead the global EV transition through establishing the new EV battery cell plant with LG Energy Solution.
- The battery manufacturing facility will be located in Bryan County, Savannah, Georgia.
- The site is adjacent to Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America, which was under construction at the time of the announcement.
- To become eligible for tax rebates, manufacturers must fulfill new sourcing requirements concerning EV battery components and minerals.
- Meeting IRA sourcing requirements enables EV buyers to avail tax credits of up to $7,500.
- The regulations are part of President Joe Biden’s initiative to have 50% of new vehicle sales in the United States be EVs or plug-in hybrids by 2030.
- The initiative is intended to lessen the country’s reliance on China for EV battery supply chains.
- Construction of the new facility was planned to begin in the second half of 2023.
- The joint venture scheduled production to start at the end of 2025.
- This is the second JV concerning Hyundai and LG for battery manufacturing.
- In 2021, both parties began building a facility in Indonesia.
- Production at the Indonesian facility was anticipated to begin in the first half of 2024.
- The battery cells produced from the Georgia plant will be assembled in Hyundai Mobis.
- Assembled batteries would be shipped to the manufacturing facilities of Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis EV models.
- The new facility will help create a stable supply of batteries in the region and allow the Group to respond fast to soaring EV demand in the U.S. market.
- With this JV, LGES now has seven battery factories running or being built in the United States.
- LGES is focusing the majority of its efforts on increasing output and hopes to hasten the U.S.’s transition to clean energy.
- LGES and Hyundai Motor Group have long collaborated on the supply of EV batteries for vehicles including the Elantra Hybrid, Kona Electric, and IONIQ 6 dedicated EVs.
- The Hyundai Elantra Hybrid debuted in 2009 as the first electric model produced by the Group.
- The partnership is poised to help the brands further strengthen ties going forward with EV battery cooperation.
