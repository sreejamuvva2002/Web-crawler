# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Georgia: A Powerhouse for Advanced Manufacturing, Logistics | Trade and Industry Development](https://www.tradeandindustrydev.com/region/georgia/georgia-powerhouse-advanced-manufacturing-17807)  ·  `page_008255`

## Facts

- Georgia is named the number one state for business for eight consecutive years due to its diverse talent pipeline, strong logistics infrastructure, and pro-business environment.
- From July through December 2020, investments from economic development projects facilitated by the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) were up nearly 50 percent compared to the same time period in the previous year.
- Job creation facilitated by GDEcD was up nearly 40 percent during that same July through December 2020 period.
- These projects generated more than 16,000 jobs and nearly $6 billion in new investments.
- Food processing, manufacturing, and logistics and distribution industries created nearly 70 percent of the new jobs generated.
- Georgia's manufacturing GDP was $64.7 billion in 2019.
- As of December 2020, there were 389,400 manufacturing jobs in the state.
- Georgia Quick Start is ranked as the number one workforce training program in the U.S. and has trained more than one million workers.
- Georgia Quick Start partners with the University System of Georgia and the Technical College System of Georgia to provide industry-specific training for vehicle assembly, machinery manufacturing, vaccine production, and food handling.
- Georgia's accredited public and private universities award more than 132,000 degrees and certificates annually.
- Georgia is home to Morehouse College and Spellman College, two of the top five HBCUs in the U.S., and the Atlanta University Center, the largest consortium of HBCUs in the U.S.
- The Georgia Institute of Technology hosts the most diverse college of engineering in the nation and graduates the most women and underrepresented minority engineers.
- Apple and Southern Company each announced a $25 million investment in January 2021 to build the Propel Center at the Atlanta University Center as a technology and innovation hub for HBCU students.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development's Center of Innovation (COI) for Manufacturing provides cost-free technical expertise, cutting-edge research, and strategic partnerships to manufacturers.
- Georgia-based startup Carbice designs and produces thermal materials for electronic devices and used COI support to transition from R&D to commercial state production.
- The global projection of annual passenger electric vehicle (EV) sales in 2040 is 56 million EVs.
- SK Innovation is constructing a $1.67 billion lithium-ion battery manufacturing facility in northeast Georgia.
- SK Innovation announced an additional $940 million expansion in 2020 to accommodate demand for its battery facility.
- Volkswagen, a primary customer of SK Innovation, is expanding its production plant to assemble 100,000 all-electric SUVs annually beginning in 2022.
- In November 2020, TEKLAS, a Turkish advanced manufacturer and supplier of EV parts, announced plans to invest in opening its first North American facility and headquarters in northwest Georgia.
- In July 2020, GEDIA Automotive Group announced it will build an advanced manufacturing facility in Whitfield County, Georgia, focusing on lightweight structural automotive parts for electric vehicles.
- Companies and universities in Georgia utilize innovation centers to research driving range improvements, recharging speed increases, connectivity system enhancements, AI for autonomous vehicles, and nanotech materials in production.
- 'The Ray' is a public-private-philanthropic partnership in Georgia where leading industries and universities test technologies in energy, transportation, health, and the environment.
- Initial experiments at The Ray included trials of a solar energy-powered EV fast charger sponsored by Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia, a solar road installed by Wattway by Colas, and wireless tire pressure and tread measuring technology by Wheelright.
- Panasonic and 3M utilize an 18-mile stretch of highway at The Ray to test new products and technologies.
- Hartsfield-Jackson International Airport is consistently ranked as the busiest and most efficient airport in the world for passenger traffic, with 80 percent of the U.S. population within a two-hour flight from Atlanta.
- Georgia has deepwater ports in Savannah and Brunswick, plus inland ports including the Appalachian Regional Port, Northeast Georgia Inland Port, and Cordele Inland Port.
- Georgia companies rely on the state's logistics infrastructure to handle and haul more than $900 billion of cargo each year.
- The COI for Logistics helps companies improve supply chain efficiencies and support growth.
- During the last six months of 2020, foreign direct investment accounted for 28 percent of jobs created in Georgia, increasing by 46 percent compared to the first half of the previous fiscal year.
- In October 2020, La Regina Atlantica announced a $20 million investment in a new food processing facility in Bacon County, importing tomatoes from Italy through the Port of Savannah.
- More than 90 percent of Georgia's exports are advanced manufacturing-related, with manufacturing exports increasing by more than 30 percent since 2010.
- GDEcD's trade division has won the Presidential 'E Star' award four times and maintains international representatives in 12 strategic markets.
- Poriferous, a Newnan-based medical technology manufacturer, utilizes GDEcD's cost-free due diligence and research for market vetting and attends state-sponsored trade shows.
