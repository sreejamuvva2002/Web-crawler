## Facts

- Georgia Ports Authority (GPA) plays a crucial role for automakers, suppliers, manufacturers, and logistics operations considering job expansion in Georgia.
- Griff Lynch serves as the CEO and president of Georgia Ports Authority.
- Through collaboration between GPA and the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD), the state revealed 350 projects from potential port users in Fiscal Year 2023.
- These announcements are expected to generate over 33,000 jobs in manufacturing and logistics.
- These announcements are expected to attract investments totalling US$21 billion.
- The economic benefits will extend to 96 counties statewide.
- GPA, GDEcD, and the Georgia Department of Transportation are collaborating to foster responsible growth that delivers good jobs while preserving quality of life.
- Georgia Ports is investing $29 million in an overpass that will route trucks leaving its Ocean Terminal in Savannah directly onto U.S. 17, avoiding community streets.
- The expanding electric vehicle ecosystem in Georgia has made an impact throughout the entire state.
- This EV growth is primarily driven by announcements related to the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America has established operations in over 10 counties across Georgia.
- Companies in e-mobility are prioritizing speed-to-market solutions.
- Pat Wilson serves as a commissioner at the Georgia Department of Economic Development.
- Communities can prepare for new opportunities by investing in site development and obtaining “Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development” certification.
- Anovion Technologies, a synthetic graphite manufacturer, is creating 400 job opportunities in Southwest Georgia’s Decatur County.
- Chip Dunn serves as the Executive Chairman of Anovion Technologies.
- State recruitment efforts, including meetings with Gov. Brian Kemp, influenced Anovion’s decision to establish its presence in rural Georgia.
- The GPA’s inland port system comprises the Appalachian Regional Port in Crandall, Ga., and the ongoing construction of the Blue Ridge Connector rail yard near Gainesville, Ga.
- This inland port system facilitates the transportation of large, heavy products from centrally located communities to the state’s deepwater ports.
- In the Savannah region alone, the industrial property inventory now exceeds 11.5 million square meters.
- Over 1.3 million square meters of industrial property in the Savannah region was under construction as of the end of Calendar Year 2023.
- Stephen Ezelle serves as an industrial real estate broker at Cushman & Wakefield.
- Despite a slowing economy across most U.S. markets, the Savannah regional economy continues to expand and demonstrate robust absorption in the industrial sector.
- The outlook for Savannah’s industrial market remains favorable due to continued growth at Georgia Ports and conservative projections to eclipse 8 million TEUs of throughput by 2030.
- Operational efficiency offered by GPA and a shift in import cargo from the West Coast and NY/NJ are key drivers in the growth of Savannah’s industrial market.
- Savannah is poised for sustained, steady growth, largely attributed to the Hyundai announcement and GPA’s increasing national market presence.
- Savannah’s industrial sector concluded 2023 with a 7% overall vacancy rate.
- Stacy Watson serves as the director of economic and industrial development at Georgia Ports Authority.
- Properties located 40 or 50 miles away from Savannah remain competitive for drayage, lease rates, and overall cost of doing business due to the port’s location upriver of the Savannah population center and DOT efforts reducing truck route congestion.
- Efficient cargo movement to and from the marine terminal at the Port of Savannah is facilitated by direct truck routes connecting to Interstates 95 and 16.
- On-terminal service at the Port of Savannah is provided by two Class I railroads.
- Properties in Bryan, Liberty, and McIntosh counties, as well as those further west, offer access to interstate and rail connections linking to the port and vibrant inland markets.
