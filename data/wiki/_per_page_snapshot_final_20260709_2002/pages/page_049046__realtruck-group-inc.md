# RealTruck Group Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2025-01-21

**Source:** [RealTruck Group Inc.'s Proposed $525 Million Firs | S&P Global Ratings](https://www.spglobal.com/ratings/en/regulatory/article/-/view/type/HTML/id/3311098)  ·  `page_049046`

## Facts

- S&P Global Ratings assigned a 'B-' issue-level rating and '3' recovery rating to RealTruck Group Inc.'s proposed $525 million non-fungible first-lien term loan due January 2028.
- The company will use the proceeds from this issuance to fund its acquisition of Vehicle Accessories Group (VAI), a supplier of floor mats, running boards, and other automotive accessories.
- RealTruck forecasts its debt to EBITDA will remain high in the upper-8x area as of the close of the transaction and above 7x through 2026.
- The company’s leverage has been elevated since it was acquired by financial sponsor L Catterton in January 2021.
- At the time of the L Catterton acquisition, RealTruck's S&P Global Ratings-adjusted EBITDA margins exceeded 20%.
- RealTruck's EBITDA margins were roughly 17% for the trailing-12-months ended Sept. 30, 2024.
- The company's EBITDA margins have declined over the last couple of years due to weaker sales volumes from weaker consumer demand and increased competition in certain product categories, increased restructuring costs, and higher advertising spending.
- S&P expects RealTruck to improve its EBITDA margins to the 19%-20% range in 2025 and 2026 due to VAI’s higher-margin revenue mix and somewhat lower restructuring charges.
- The company forecasts organic sales volumes will increase by only 3% annually over the next couple of years because consumer demand remains somewhat weak.
- RealTruck's free operating cash flow was strained in 2024 due to weaker margins, increased working capital usage, and higher capital spending, leading to cash outflows of more than $50 million through the third quarter.
- S&P forecasts RealTruck will improve its operating cash flow in 2025 on stronger margins and steadier working capital following the ramp-up of production at its Mexico facility.
- The company expects higher capital spending in 2025 (nearly 4% of revenue) because it is expanding select manufacturing lines to prioritize high-volume products/stock-keeping units.
- After 2025, RealTruck expects to reduce its capital spending back toward the 2.5%-3.0% of sales range annually.
- The acquisition of VAI is consistent with management's strategy of expanding its product portfolio, gaining share in existing accessory categories, and entering desirable geographies.
- RealTruck has already integrated two acquisitions under its current financial sponsor and management team: GoRhino and MountainTop.
- Since its acquisition of GoRhino in late-2023, the company has shifted more of its manufacturing to facilities located in Mexico, which has improved its gross margins due to lower-cost labor.
- RealTruck's business model is differentiated from peers because it manufactures and assembles its products in North America, whereas competitors primarily manufacture and import from Asia.
- There is heightened risk that the new U.S. administration will place tariffs on Mexican- and Canadian-made products.
- RealTruck's larger in-house manufacturing footprint leads to a somewhat higher fixed-cost base that weighs on profitability when sales volumes decrease.
- As of the close of the transaction, the company has pro forma balance sheet cash of $18 million and nearly full access to a $235 million asset-based lending (ABL) revolver, with total liquidity of about $250 million.
- RealTruck does not face any near-term funded debt maturities until its first-lien term loan expires in January 2028, followed by senior unsecured notes in January 2029.
- S&P's simulated default scenario assumes a payment default in 2027 due to sustained economic downturn reducing consumer demand for new pickup trucks and Jeeps, lower-than-projected penetration for accessory products, intense pricing pressure, and potential loss of key distributor or installer customers.
- In the simulated default scenario, RealTruck's gross enterprise value at default is projected at $1,375 million.
- Total first-lien debt in the default scenario is projected at $2,218 million, with recovery expectations of 50%-70%.
- Total collateral value for unsecured debt is projected at $69 million, with unsecured debt claims of $1,741 million and a recovery expectation of 0%-10%.
