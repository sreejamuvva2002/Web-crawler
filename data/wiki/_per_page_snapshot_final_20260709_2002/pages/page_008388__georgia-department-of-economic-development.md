# Georgia Department of Economic Development

## Facts

- The Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) is a department of the State of Georgia headquartered at 75 Fifth Street, N.W., Suite 1200, Atlanta, Georgia.
- The agency was originally named the Georgia Department of Commerce and established by law in 1949.
- Governor Herman Talmadge appointed the first five-member board under Official Code of Georgia Annotated sections 50-7-1 through 50-7-41.
- George C. Gaines served as the first commissioner.
- Pat Wilson was appointed Commissioner of GDEcD by Governor Nathan Deal in November 2016.
- As Commissioner, Pat Wilson leads the state agency responsible for creating jobs and investment through business recruitment and expansion, international trade and tourism, and arts/film/music industries.
- Christopher M. Carr served as Commissioner from November 2013 until his appointment as state Attorney General in November 2016.
- GDEcD plans, manages, and mobilizes state resources to attract new business investment, drive industry expansion, locate markets for Georgia products, and promote the state for arts and entertainment projects.
- The department is divided into seven distinct divisions: Global Commerce, International Trade, Workforce, Georgia Centers of Innovation, Tourism, Film/Music & Digital Entertainment, and Georgia Council for the Arts, supported by Administration and Marketing/Communications divisions.
- The Global Commerce Division assists businesses with site location, employee training, market research, and export assistance.
- In FY2012, the Global Commerce division led to 403 announced projects and nearly $6B in investment.
- GDEcD supports small businesses (which make up 97% of all Georgia businesses) through direct company relationships, resource awareness, B-2-B opportunities, and local-level business development.
- The International Trade Division maintains representation in 11 markets including Brazil, Canada, Chile, China, Colombia, Germany, Japan, Korea, Mexico, Israel, and the United Kingdom.
- The Centers of Innovation (COI) program accelerates technology-based companies through six centers focused on agribusiness, aerospace, energy, life sciences, logistics, and manufacturing.
- COI centers are located in Atlanta, Augusta, Gainesville, Savannah, Tifton, and Eastman.
- Tangible services for COI include access to university-level research and development, product commercialization, industry-specific business counsel, matching research grants for qualified companies, networking opportunities, and connections to potential investor networks.
- Georgia Allies was formed in 1997 as a partnership between state government and private corporations.
- On May 12, 2008, Governor Sonny Perdue signed the Georgia Entertainment Industry Investment Act, increasing the state tax credit for qualified production and post-production expenditures by as much as 30%.
- The tax credit is available to traditional motion picture projects as well as new industries such as game development and animation.
- During FY2012, GDEcD led more than 100 feature films and television series produced in Georgia, ranking the state number one in the Southeast and among the top five states nationally for film and TV productions.
- The Film, Music & Digital Entertainment Division has helped secure Georgia as a location for over 700 film and television productions.
- Currently, more than 30 video game businesses operate in the state.
- Georgia's video game workforce benefits from 15 of the state's colleges and universities offering video game development courses or curriculums, including some graduate degree programs.
