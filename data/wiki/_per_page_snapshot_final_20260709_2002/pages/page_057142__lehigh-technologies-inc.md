# Lehigh Technologies, Inc.

## Facts

- Lehigh Technologies, Inc. is located in Tucker, Ga.
- The company produces a brand of vulcanized elastomer particles known as "PolyDyne" or "PD," which includes cured rubber particles, recycled rubber particles, ground tire rubber (GTR), and micronized rubber powder (MRP).
- PD80 conforms to conventional 80 mesh standards, while PD40 conforms to conventional 40 mesh standards.
- The company's micronized rubber powders can be produced via a cryogenic grinding system described by U.S. Pat. No. 7,445,170 and an impact mill described by U.S. Pat. No. 7,861,958.
- Reclaimed elastomer materials like GTR and MRP are used as fillers in elastomer compositions (e.g., tire tread compounds for vehicle tires) and plastics compositions (e.g., as fillers for polyolefins).
- Rubber powders, whether GTR or MRP, are typically significantly less expensive than virgin rubber or plastic, which tends to reduce the overall manufacturing cost of the composition.
- Reincorporating reclaimed materials into elastomer and plastic compositions reduces landfill waste and results in a more environmentally-friendly product.
- The use of recycled GTR or MRP provides a strategic supply chain hedge against petroleum-based supply chain price and supply volatility.
- Functionalized reclaimed elastomer material can be obtained from Levgum, Ltd., which has a principal place of business in Kanot, Israel.
- Devulcanization processes modify the chemistry of vulcanized particles by breaking chemical crosslinks formed during vulcanization through chemical, thermal, and/or mechanical treatment.
- One functionalization method subjects particles to shear at temperatures less than 100° C. using a two-roll mill with a 0.1 mm nip, where rolls rotate at different speeds (e.g., 25 rpm and 12.5 rpm).
- The reclaimed elastomer material used in experiments was made from vehicle tire buffings, butadiene rubber (BR), natural rubber (NR), or blends of NR and BR or SBR and NR.
- Experimental SBR/BR elastomer blend formulations were tested with 10% functionalized material at 10, 20, 40, and 80 mesh sizes.
- Samples comprising 10% 80 mesh functionalized MRP demonstrated an average tensile strength of 17.3 MPa and elongation of 613%.
- Samples comprising 10% 40 mesh functionalized MRP demonstrated an average tensile strength of 16.3 MPa and elongation of 599%.
- Testing showed that elastomer compositions comprising functionalized MRP of a particular mesh size exhibit tensile strength approximately 6% greater than compositions comprising functionalized powder one mesh size larger.
- Samples comprising functionalized MRP of smaller mesh size demonstrate superior elongation characteristics compared to samples with larger mesh functionalized GTR.
- The relative quality of tested samples was determined by taking the product of tensile strength and elongation for the respective sample and dividing that quantity by the product of the tensile strength and elongation for the control material.
- Functionalized 80 mesh MRP approaches a relative quality of approximately 0.9, making it suitable as a substitute in high-performance tire tread applications.
- An elastomer compound comprising these materials includes uncured elastomer, functionalized reclaimed elastomer material (between 3% and 30% by weight), a filler, and a crosslinking agent.
- The article of manufacture comprising the cross-linked elastomer compound can be a tire.
