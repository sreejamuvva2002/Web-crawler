# Augusta Economic Development Authority

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2023-07-24

**Source:** [Augusta | Richmond County: More than the Masters - Georgia Trend Magazine](https://www.georgiatrend.com/2023/07/24/richmond-county-more-than-the-masters/)  ·  `page_019319`

## Facts

- Augusta Mayor Garnett Johnson serves on the Georgia Department of Economic Development Board of Directors.
- A German copper recycler announced an almost $1 billion investment in the community that will create hundreds of good-paying jobs.
- In November 2021, Hamburg-based Aurubis announced it would invest $340 million in a recycling and copper smelting facility to be built on 150 acres in Richmond County’s Augusta Corporate Park and hire 125 employees.
- Roughly six months after breaking ground last June, the company announced that Georgia’s largest-ever single German investment was going to get even bigger.
- In December, Aurubis announced a Phase II bringing their total investment to $690 million and 250 jobs.
- The 150 acres are graded and two buildings are in the air for the Aurubis facility.
- The first building at the Aurubis site is expected to be completed in the third quarter.
- The first smelt at the Aurubis facility will be sometime in 2024.
- The Aurubis smelting facility will produce copper from recycled cable, e-waste and other metal-bearing material.
- Denkai America announced in July it will build its North American headquarters in Richmond County, making an initial investment of $150 million on a 115-acre site.
- Denkai is a Japanese copper-foil manufacturer that will be located in the industrial park.
- Ultimately, Denkai will invest $400 million in their new North American headquarters and manufacturing plant and create 250 jobs.
- In October, Belgian plastics and chemicals firm Solvay was announced to get a $178.2 million grant from the U.S. Department of Energy for construction of a production line for polyvinylidene fluoride (PVDF) at its Augusta site.
- PVDF is a polymer used as a binder and coating for the separator in lithium-ion batteries, semiconductors and other applications.
- Cal Wray, president of the Augusta Economic Development Authority, says they worked $35 billion worth of investment opportunities in the Augusta area last year.
- In the five years since his staff arrived, the Augusta EDA has announced just over $3 billion worth of investment.
- Existing companies and manufacturers continue to invest in Augusta and Richmond County, with almost all announcing new jobs, including E-Z-Go and Starbucks.
- E-Z-Go, owned by Textron, is a leading global manufacturer of golf cars, utility vehicles and personal transportation vehicles founded in Augusta in 1954.
- E-Z-Go is one of Augusta’s oldest corporate headquarters and employs 1,700 people.
- The Augusta area has one of the top 20 most diverse economies in the country.
- The Augusta area is also in the top 20 in concentration of engineers in the country.
- About 24,000 people are working in advanced manufacturing in the Augusta region according to Augusta Technical College President Jermaine Whirl.
- Augusta Technical College was awarded money from the governor and legislature to build an 81,000-square-foot advanced manufacturing and engineering technology training center.
- The college was awarded $2.7 million for design, $35 million for construction, and roughly $6.9 million for furniture, fixtures and equipment for the new training center.
- The new advanced manufacturing center is located off I-20 in Columbia County.
- The facility will house engineering technology programs including architectural, computer/electrical, mechanical, nuclear, machine tool/CNC, chemical technology, industrial automation, mechatronics/robotics, metrology and quality management, hydraulics/pneumatics, additive manufacturing/3D printing and welding programs.
- The training center has a three year build-out and is expecting to have over 1,300 new students.
- The facility will nearly double the number of graduates from Augusta Tech’s School of Aviation, Industrial and Engineering Technology, which will graduate 680 students at its summer commencement.
- The building will increase corporate training efforts for companies including John Deere, Club Car and Starbucks to train more incumbent workers.
- Manufacturing jobs in the region pay anywhere from $55,000 all the way up to six figures after two to three years on the job.
- A partnership between Augusta Technical College, the city of Augusta, First Tee of Augusta and Augusta National Golf Club will result in the purchase of the Augusta Municipal Golf Course (The Patch) on Jan. 1, 2025.
- The golf course purchase initiative was announced during Masters Week in April.
- Augusta Tech’s golf management program and turf-grass management program will be relocated to The Patch.
- Augusta National Golf Club has committed to updating current facilities at The Patch, including installing new greens and renovating the clubhouse.
- Augusta Advantage Program creates a streamlined pathway for students to transfer from Augusta Technical College to Augusta University, offering accelerated admissions into eight pathways: business administration, communication, computer science, kinesiology, nursing, political science, psychology, and social work.
- These transfer pathways were chosen based on significant workforce needs here and beyond according to Augusta University President Brooks Keel.
- Augusta University has a partnership with Augusta Technical College since 2015 for a Cybersecurity to Information Technology Pathway.
- Augusta University has an agreement with the Army Cyber Center of Excellence to offer several master’s degree programs to soldiers, including information security management and intelligence and security studies.
- The Army moved its entire cyber command to Fort Gordon three years ago.
- Augusta University worked with public and private partners to develop the Georgia Cyber Center to support the Army and businesses that support its mission.
- The Medical College of Georgia (MCG) is the state’s only public medical school and is the eleventh largest medical school in the country.
- A pending merger means Wellstar will take over the operations of the Augusta University Health System.
- One potential benefit of the Wellstar merger is that med students may be able to practice in other parts of the state where Wellstar has facilities.
- Augusta University had year-over-year enrollment growth of approximately 3% since 2015 and enrolled 9,800 students in the fall.
- Augusta University was one of only eight universities in the University System of Georgia that had an increase in enrollment last fall.
