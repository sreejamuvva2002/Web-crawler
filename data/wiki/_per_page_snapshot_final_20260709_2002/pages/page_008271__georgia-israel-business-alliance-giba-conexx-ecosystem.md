# Georgia Israel Business Alliance (GIBA) / Conexx Ecosystem

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Israeli Tech in Georgia: 33 Years, 50+ Companies, $5.4B in Bilateral Trade | 5W Research Report 2026](https://www.5wpr.com/research/israeli-tech-georgia-2026/)  ·  `page_008271`

## Facts

- Cumulative Georgia-Israel merchandise trade tracked by GDEcD since 1996 totals $5.4B.
- Georgia exported $374M in manufacturing goods to Israel in 2024.
- More than 50 Israeli-affiliated companies operate in Georgia, with Atlanta ranking as a top-five U.S. center for Israeli company activity.
- Georgia ranks 10th among U.S. states in exports to Israel.
- Georgia firms have received approximately $615M in U.S. Foreign Military Financing dollars routed to IDF contracts since 1996.
- Conexx operated for 33 years (1992-2025), facilitating over $1B in completed transactions and serving 140+ Israeli companies before voting to wind down in late 2024.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development opened its formal Israel office in 1994, one of the longest-running U.S. state economic-development presences in Israel, currently led by Sima Amir.
- In 2018, Southern Company signed an MoU with the Israel Innovation Authority establishing a joint R&D funding program covering vehicle automation, advanced materials, sustainable energy, public safety, smart homes, and distributed energy.
- The Year 1 contribution for the Southern Company-Israel Innovation Authority joint R&D program was $1M, matched by the IIA.
- Atlanta-headquartered Coca-Cola operated The Bridge startup accelerator from Tel Aviv alongside a sister BridgeCommunity program in Atlanta, serving as a consistent corporate gateway for Israeli technology into the enterprise ecosystem.
- Israel ranks first globally on the Anthropic AI Usage Index at 4.9x the global baseline, per the April 2026 5W & Louder study "Claude in Israel."
- The Georgia Israel Business Alliance (GIBA) launched in 2025 as the institutional successor to Conexx, incubated by Atlanta's Zalik Foundation, with a program calendar including trade missions, networking events, and sector-specific partnerships across defense, energy, cyber, and innovation.
- Hartsfield-Jackson Airport is consistently the busiest passenger airport in the world, positioning Atlanta as the de facto capital of the U.S. Southeast for finance, media, logistics, and Fortune 500 corporate presence.
- Governor Kemp has been a consistent public advocate for Georgia-Israel ties, with predecessors Barnes, Perdue, and Deal all leading economic missions to Israel, establishing a bipartisan and durable relationship across administrations.
- David Zalik chairs the Georgia Solidarity Network and was the driving force behind Delta's restoration of the ATL-TLV route, while his foundation incubated GIBA.
- Delta Air Lines announced in October 2025 plans to resume three-times-weekly nonstop Atlanta-Tel Aviv flights on the Airbus A350-900 with a launch date of April 15, 2026, though service was suspended through August 4-5, 2026 due to regional conflict escalation.
- Governor Kemp publicly stated regarding Delta's flight restoration: "This flight is important to so many in our state, further strengthening the cultural and economic ties between Georgia and Israel."
- Atlanta's enterprise customer base includes Truist, Aflac, Equifax, Home Depot, and UPS, forming a deep market for Israeli innovation in cybersecurity, fintech, logistics, and AI.
- Ronn Torossian, Founder and Chairman of 5W, noted that Israeli founders increasingly recognize Atlanta as a welcoming, well-built U.S. base with thirty years of formal economic representation and serious philanthropic backing.
- A comprehensive USIBA-style economic-impact study for Georgia has not yet been published, though triangulation against Florida's data suggests Georgia operates in the same tier as states reporting up to $19.5B in total output.
- The 2018 Southern Company-Israel Innovation Authority R&D framework remains active, continuing to support collaborative projects in energy and advanced technology.
- Conexx originally operated as the American-Israel Chamber of Commerce Southeast Division before evolving into a primary business organization connecting Israel with Georgia and the broader Southeast over three decades.
