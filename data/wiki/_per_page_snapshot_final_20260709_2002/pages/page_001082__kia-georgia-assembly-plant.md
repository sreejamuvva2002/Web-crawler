# Kia Georgia Assembly Plant

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-02-25

**Source:** [Kia Georgia Hits Triple Milestone: 5 Millionth Vehicle and First GA-Made Hybrid](https://thekoreancarblog.com/kia-georgia-hits-triple-milestone-5-millionth-vehicle-and-first-ga-made-hybrid/)  ·  `page_001082`

## Facts

- The Kia Georgia assembly plant celebrated a 'triple milestone' marking the rollout of the first 2027 Kia Telluride.
- The rolled-out vehicle is the 5 millionth vehicle produced at the West Point site.
- The rollout marks the official start of production for the redesigned 2027 model year.
- The 2027 Telluride is the first hybrid electric vehicle (HEV) ever assembled in the state of Georgia.
- The introduction of the 2027 Telluride HEV marks a major shift in Kia’s domestic manufacturing strategy from internal combustion engine production to its first U.S.-assembled hybrid.
- Sean Yoon, President and CEO of Kia North America and Kia America, stated that the plant is the only one in the world that builds the Telluride and highlighted Kia's commitment to building the future of mobility in Georgia.
- Reaching the 5-million-unit mark while debuting the state’s first HEV reflects Kia’s trajectory toward a diversified, electrified lineup.
- Since its inception in 2009, Kia Georgia has grown into a cornerstone of the Southeastern manufacturing economy.
- The Telluride has been exclusively manufactured at the West Point site since 2019.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp attended the milestone ceremony and highlighted the public-private partnership sustaining growth between Kia Georgia, the local community, and the state.
- Stuart Countess, President and CEO of Kia Georgia, noted that the plant’s ability to pivot between gasoline-powered vehicles, EVs, and HEVs is its greatest competitive advantage.
- The West Point facility is one of the largest employers in the Southeast automotive sector.
- Total production at the site reached 5,000,000 units from 2009 to present.
- The 2027 Telluride will begin its rollout to dealerships across North America.
