# Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD)

**Type:** Government Program  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[government-support|Government Support]]  ·  **Location:** Georgia, United States

## Overview

The Georgia Department of Economic Development promotes and supports manufacturing in the state, highlighting advanced manufacturing capabilities, workforce development, logistics infrastructure, and initiatives such as the Georgia Made® program.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Supports electric mobility manufacturers through state-wide advanced manufacturing resources and incentives.

## Timeline

- **2025** — Manufacturing accounted for 46% of new jobs announced by GDEcD (JobsEQ report).
- **Fiscal year 2025** — Reported outpacing the Southeast in manufacturing GDP and job growth over the last decade.

## Related organizations & people

- Georgia Made® program — State branding and marketing initiative for manufacturers. Promotes qualified manufacturers and connects them with GDEcD’s business support teams.
- Georgia Tech — Research institution partner. Ranks No. 1 among public universities for undergraduate engineering programs and supports manufacturers through research collaborations.

## Key facts

- Manufacturing excels in Georgia - outpacing the Southeast in manufacturing GDP and job growth over the last decade, according to the 2025 JobsEQ report.
- Georgia leads in advanced manufacturing by blending innovation, skilled talent, and global connectivity.
- Supported by top research institutions and a pro-business climate, the state helps manufacturers accelerate innovation and scale globally.
- Business Facilities continues to rank Georgia in the top five for aerospace and defense manufacturers.
- In fiscal year 2025, manufacturing accounted for 46% of new jobs announced by GDEcD.
