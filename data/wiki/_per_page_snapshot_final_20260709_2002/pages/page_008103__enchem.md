# Enchem

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Enchem Secures approximately $50 Million via US Tariff Refunds, Tax Credits as North American Bet Pays Off — BigGo Finance](https://finance.biggo.com/news/2Q-GyZ4BhIZ-5rXB9u9u)  ·  `page_008103`

## Facts

- Enchem (348370) is a specialist in secondary battery electrolytes.
- Enchem's US subsidiary secured large-scale tariff refunds and Advanced Manufacturing Production Credit (AMPC) benefits by leveraging its local production base in the United States.
- On the 15th, Enchem announced that its US subsidiary is undergoing a tariff refund process based on the International Emergency Economic Powers Act (IEEPA).
- The total amount filed for the tariff refund is $5.45 million (~8 billion won).
- Of the filed amount, 63%, or $3.44 million (~5 billion won), has already been refunded.
- The company expects the remaining balance to be returned sequentially according to US government administrative procedures.
- The refund follows a ruling by the US Court of International Trade (CIT).
- Resolving the tariff burden incurred during local distribution and import stages is expected to significantly improve the financial health and cash flow of the US subsidiary.
- The cumulative AMPC accumulated by the US subsidiary from 2023 to 2025 amounts to approximately $29.78 million (~45 billion won).
- An additional receipt of about $15 million (~23 billion won) is virtually confirmed for this year.
- The total cumulative benefit over four years is projected to reach $45 million (~68 billion won).
- An Enchem official stated that the tariff refunds and AMPC benefits are not simply a policy windfall, but "the fruit of a global supply chain strategy that took risks to preemptively establish a local electrolyte production system in Georgia, USA."
- The same official added that as the US battery industry prioritizes transparency in raw material procurement and supply chain stability centered on allied nations, "the value of Enchem's local production base will only increase day by day."
- Industry observers note these results mark the point where the financial outcomes of years of North American investment are beginning to materialize.
- The North American battery market is rapidly expanding beyond electric vehicles (EVs) into the energy storage system (ESS) sector.
- Demand for 'long-life, high-safety electrolytes'—which determine battery lifespan and safety—is exploding due to a surge in AI data center construction and power infrastructure investment.
- Enchem's strategy is to rapidly transfer its quality control capabilities and global operational experience, proven in the existing EV electrolyte market, to the ESS sector.
- A company official expressed confidence in the local production base's competitiveness, stating that "the number of companies capable of supplying high-quality electrolytes in a timely manner within North America is limited."
- Enchem was founded in 2012 and listed on the KOSDAQ market in 2021.
- The company's main business is the manufacture and sale of electrolytes and additives for secondary batteries and EDLCs.
- Enchem is pursuing a waste NMP (recycling) business generated after secondary battery manufacturing as a new growth engine.
- Industry analysts suggest this performance could be a structural turning point that strengthens Enchem's position within the North American supply chain.
- Potential changes in the US administration's trade policies or the possibility of AMPC system reform are cited as variables requiring continuous monitoring.
