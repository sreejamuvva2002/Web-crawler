# Kia EV6

## Facts

- Assembly takes place at Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia (KMMG) in West Point, Georgia.
- Manufacturing of most EV6 variants for the North American market is expected to move to Kia's West Point, Georgia assembly plant for the 2025 model year.
- Production is expected to begin in early calendar year 2025.
- Vehicles with the GT trim level will still be manufactured in Korea.
- This is expected to make the Georgia-made EV6 models eligible for the US federal EV tax credit.
- On Georgia-made vehicles, 2025 swaps the CCS charging port for the Tesla-style NACS / SAE J3400 port.
- On Georgia-made vehicles, the charge port location is moved from the rear passenger (right-hand) side to the rear driver (left-hand) side of the car.
