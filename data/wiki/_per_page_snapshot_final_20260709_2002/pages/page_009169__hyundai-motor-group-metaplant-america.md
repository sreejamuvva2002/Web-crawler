# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hyundai is rapidly building its first US electric vehicle plant, with production on track for 2025 | AP News](https://apnews.com/article/hyundai-georgia-electric-vehicle-ev-845e2729fd5220797e7984f424bc1d5c)  ·  `page_009169`

## Facts

- Hyundai is rapidly building its first US electric vehicle plant in Ellabelle, Georgia.
- The construction site spans more than a half mile and covers more than 2,900 acres (1,170 hectares) parallel to Interstate 16 west of Savannah.
- Hyundai Motor Group broke ground on the $7.6 billion vehicle and battery plant one year prior to the article's reporting.
- More than 2,000 people are working each week on the construction project.
- The site is dotted with cranes, bulldozers, and construction workers, resembling a small city.
- Foundation work for the factory’s main production buildings is almost finished.
- The framework of more than 27,000 tons (24,490 metric tons) of steel is more than 80% complete.
- Some buildings have roofs and floors, and exterior wall panels have begun to go up.
- Hyundai officials refer to the facility as its American “metaplant.”
- The plant will stamp, weld, paint, and assemble electric vehicles.
- Production is on track to start in early 2025, with possible acceleration to open before the end of next year.
- Construction was accelerated to take advantage of federal incentives that reward domestic production of EVs.
- Hyundai says it will build 300,000 EVs each year at the plant.
- The site will manufacture batteries to power those vehicles in a partnership with LG Energy Solution.
- Artist renderings show buildings with plentiful windows and skylights to maximize natural lighting indoors.
- A large covered parking lot for employees will be topped with solar panels.
- An elevated bridge with glass sides will allow visibility of unfinished cars moving by conveyor from the paint shop to the assembly plant.
- The plant will employ 8,500 workers upon completion.
- Suppliers opening shop in nine Georgia counties near the Hyundai plant are projected to create another 6,000 jobs.
- It is the largest economic development project in Georgia’s history.
- State officials and local governments offered a $2.1 billion tax break incentive package for the project.
- Georgia’s economic development commissioner Pat Wilson stated Hyundai is projected to have a direct payroll of $4.7 billion over the next 10 years.
- The company has promised to pay workers a yearly average of $58,105 plus benefits.
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America CEO Oscar Kwon told reporters visiting the site Wednesday that the project is advancing daily.
- Hyundai president and global chief operating officer Jose Munoz indicated production could open before the end of next year.
- Savannah Area Economic Development Authority president and CEO Trip Tollison noted the rapid progress made in just one year.
