# Tredegar Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2025-03-31

**Source:** [Tredegar Q1 Earnings Down Y/Y as Aluminum Costs Weigh on Margins — TradingView News](https://www.tradingview.com/news/zacks:627c963f1094b:0-tredegar-q1-earnings-down-y-y-as-aluminum-costs-weigh-on-margins/)  ·  `page_053052`

## Facts

- Tredegar reported first-quarter 2025 net income from continuing operations of $0.7 million ($0.02 per share), down from $2.6 million ($0.08 per share) in the first quarter of 2024.
- On an adjusted basis, Tredegar's first-quarter 2025 net income from ongoing operations fell to $3.6 million ($0.10 per share) from $4.7 million ($0.14 per share) a year ago.
- The company's first-quarter 2025 net sales reached $164.7 million, representing a 14.4% increase from $144 million in the year-ago quarter.
- Tredegar's Bonnell Aluminum segment reported net sales of $133.6 million, a 17% year-over-year increase driven by a 12% rise in sales volume and pass-through of higher metal costs.
- EBITDA from ongoing operations in the Bonnell Aluminum segment declined 27% to $9.2 million from $12.5 million in the prior-year quarter due to an unfavorable shift in sales mix, higher input costs, increased labor and maintenance expenses, and elevated SG&A costs.
- Specialty products within the Bonnell Aluminum segment led volume growth, jumping 52.8%, primarily due to increased shipments for solar panel applications and TSLOTS framing systems.
- The PE Films segment delivered an 8.9% improvement in EBITDA to $7.5 million despite a 4% drop in sales volume, with net sales edging up 3.2% to $25.5 million due to strength in surface protection films.
- CEO John Steitz noted that the recovery in Bonnell Aluminum is gaining traction, supported by a tenth consecutive quarterly increase in net new orders, which rose 36% year over year.
- Open orders for aluminum extrusions reached 25 million pounds, marking the highest level in two years.
- Management highlighted the benefit of new Section 232 tariffs, which raise the rate on aluminum imports from 10% to 25% and expand coverage to downstream products, helping restore market share previously lost to underpriced imports.
- The company has not yet seen a decline in demand stemming from U.S. tariff actions on imported electronics, though management remains cautious amid ongoing global trade developments.
- The decline in Aluminum Extrusions' EBITDA was also driven by a lower spread between selling prices and metal costs, unfavorable manufacturing yields, and higher expenses related to labor, maintenance, dies, and utilities.
- Timing mismatches in aluminum pricing under the FIFO method contributed a $1.7 million benefit versus a $1.3 million charge in the prior year.
- Increased SG&A costs in the Aluminum Extrusions segment (up 38.5%) stemmed mainly from compensation, travel, and consulting fees.
- PE Films benefited from $1.5 million in incremental contribution from surface protection, offset by a $0.7 million decline from overwrap films due to weaker volume and less favorable pricing.
- For 2025, Tredegar forecasts capital expenditures of $17 million for Bonnell Aluminum and $3 million for PE Films, largely directed toward productivity and operational continuity.
- Depreciation and amortization expenses are projected at $16 million and $2 million for Aluminum Extrusions, and $5 million for PE Films in 2025.
- Tredegar's total debt declined to $56.6 million as of March 31, 2025, from $61.9 million at year-end 2024, with net debt standing at $52.9 million and a net leverage ratio of 1.1x.
- The company refinanced its $125 million asset-based lending facility in May for a new five-year term to ensure continued liquidity.
- During the quarter, Tredegar completed the sale of its Terphane business and received $9.8 million from a post-closing settlement, contributing to a $9.4 million gain from discontinued operations.
- Restructuring activities included the closure of the Richmond, VA, PE Films technical center, which concluded at the end of the first quarter of 2024.
