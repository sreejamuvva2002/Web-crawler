# Hitachi Automotive Systems Americas, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Georgia Chron - Georgia: Hitachi Automotive Systems America to Expand in Walton County - 10018385](https://georgiachron.com/ganews/georgia-hitachi-automotive-systems-america-to-expand-in-walton-county-georgiachron-10018385)  ·  `page_020719`

## Facts

- Hitachi Automotive Systems Americas, Inc., a Tier 1 supplier of world-class products to the global automotive market, is planning to create 100 jobs and invest at least $100 million - with potential up to $330 million - in an expansion at their existing facility in Monroe, Georgia.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced the expansion plan for Hitachi Automotive Systems Americas, Inc.
- Governor Kemp stated that Hitachi has been a great partner to the Walton County community by investing in employees and creating jobs for more than twenty years.
- Governor Kemp expressed confidence that the investment will positively impact the local community and encourage continued success in Georgia's strong automotive industry.
- Hitachi Automotive Systems Americas, Inc. is a group company of Hitachi Automotive Systems, Ltd., which is headquartered in Tokyo.
- Hitachi Automotive Systems, Ltd. manufactures and markets engine powertrain systems, electric powertrain systems, and integrated vehicle control systems for major automotive original equipment manufacturers (OEM) worldwide.
- Hitachi Automotive Systems Americas, Inc. President and CEO Paul Carroll stated the company has been operating in Georgia for over twenty-two years.
- The Georgia factory supplies advanced technology products for major automotive OEMs including Ford Motor Company, General Motors, Nissan, Subaru, and Honda.
- Paul Carroll highlighted the company's partnership with stakeholders including the State of Georgia, Walton County, and the City of Monroe.
- Hitachi Automotive Systems Americas, Inc. opened its first facility in Georgia in 1997.
- The company currently employs 900 Georgians.
- The expansion aims to increase production capacity in response to the growth of the automotive industry.
- Walton County Commission Chairman Kevin Little described Hitachi as a great corporate partner and the county's largest employer.
- City of Monroe Mayor John Howard noted that Hitachi continues to invest in high technology and top-quality jobs for the community.
- GDEcD Sr. Project Manager Tammy Caudell represented the Global Commerce Division in partnership with the Development Authority of Walton County, Walton EMC, and Georgia Department of Community Affairs.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that companies like Hitachi create jobs, take an active role in the community, and drive interest from Japanese companies in Georgia.
- Hitachi Automotive Systems, Ltd. is a wholly owned subsidiary of Hitachi, Ltd., headquartered in Tokyo, Japan.
- Hitachi Automotive Systems, Ltd. is engaged in the development, manufacture, sales, and services of automotive components, transportation related components, industrial machines, and systems.
