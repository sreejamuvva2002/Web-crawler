# Georgia Ports Authority

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Georgia Ports highlights site selection opportunities | AJOT.COM](https://www.ajot.com/news/georgia-ports-highlights-site-selection-opportunities)  ·  `page_019328`

## Facts

- Georgia’s ports are a key factor for automakers, their suppliers, other manufacturers, and logistics operations deciding to bring jobs to the state.
- Griff Lynch serves as President and CEO of the Georgia Ports Authority.
- The Georgia Ports Authority's mission covers the entire state, extending beyond just Savannah or Atlanta.
- In partnership with the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD), the state announced 350 projects from potential port users in Fiscal Year 2023.
- These FY2023 announcements will bring more than 33,000 manufacturing and logistics jobs and $21 billion in investment to 96 counties across the state.
- The Georgia Ports Authority, GDEcD, and the Georgia Department of Transportation are working together to foster responsible growth that delivers good jobs while preserving quality of life.
- The Georgia Ports Authority is investing $29 million in an overpass that will route trucks leaving its Ocean Terminal in Savannah directly onto U.S. 17, avoiding community streets.
- According to the Global Commerce division of GDEcD, Georgia’s growing electric vehicle ecosystem has touched every corner of the state.
- Growth in the state's EV ecosystem is led by announcements associated with the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America, which have located in more than 10 counties across the state.
- Commissioner Pat Wilson at GDEcD stated that companies, especially in e-mobility, are prioritizing speed-to-market solutions.
- Investing in site development and obtaining “Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development” certification are ways communities can prepare for new opportunities.
- Anovion Technologies, a synthetic graphite manufacturer, is creating 400 jobs for Southwest Georgia in Decatur County.
- Anovion Executive Chairman Chip Dunn cited the state’s recruitment effort that swayed the company’s decision to locate in rural Georgia, including meetings with Gov. Brian Kemp and factors such as the GPA’s inland port system and access to waterway and land transport.
- The GPA’s inland port system includes the Appalachian Regional Port in Crandall, Ga., and the Blue Ridge Connector rail yard now under construction near Gainesville, Ga.
- The inland port system supports the movement of large, heavy products from centrally located communities to the state’s deepwater ports.
- In the Savannah area alone, the inventory of industrial property totals more than 124 million square feet, with more than 14 million square feet under construction at the close of Calendar Year 2023.
- Stephen Ezelle, a broker for industrial real estate firm Cushman & Wakefield, stated that the Savannah regional economy continues to experience expansion and strong absorption in the industrial sector despite a slowing economy across most U.S. markets.
- The outlook for Savannah’s industrial market remains favorable thanks to continued growth at the Georgia Ports with conservative projections to eclipse 8 million TEUs of throughput by 2030.
- Operational efficiency offered by the GPA and the shift in import cargo from the West Coast and NY/NJ are key drivers in the growth of Savannah’s industrial market.
- Despite moderation of container trade nationally, Savannah is expected to continue balanced expansion thanks to the Hyundai announcement and the GPA’s growing national market share.
- Ezelle predicts continued absorption of well-located light industrial warehouse space.
- Savannah’s industrial sector ended 2023 with an overall vacancy rate of 7 percent.
- Stacy Watson, director of economic and industrial development at the GPA, stated that being within 30 miles of the port is great but not required to be competitive in drayage, lease rates, and overall cost of doing business.
- Locations 40 or 50 miles away from Savannah are an easy drive, attributed to the port’s location upriver of the Savannah population center and GDOT efforts making truck routes less congested and easily accessible.
- Direct truck routes from the Port of Savannah connect to Interstates 95 and 16, while on-terminal service from two Class I railroads expedite cargo moving to and from the marine terminal.
- Available properties in Bryan, Liberty and McIntosh counties as well as points west offer interstate and rail links to the port and to thriving inland markets.
- Augusta is only a short drive from Savannah, and a majority of manufacturers in Augusta are port users.
- Cal Wray, president of the Development Authority of Augusta, Ga., stated that Augusta-Richmond County provides an ideal location for port users due to available, affordable industrial land, a key base for manufacturers with over 350 already calling Augusta home, and easy rail and highway connections to the port.
- Recent announcements in Augusta include Aurubis Richmond, a metal smelting operation bringing 250 jobs and $600 million in investment.
- Recent announcements in Augusta include Denkai America, a copper film producer providing 250 jobs and $440 million in investment.
- Recent announcements in Augusta include Purecycle Technologies, which recycles plastic into like-new pellets for reuse, bringing 100 jobs and $400 million in investment.
- A stand-out industry ecosystem in Augusta is electric vehicle manufacturing, featuring EZGo Textron and Club Car along with their related suppliers in Richmond and Columbia counties.
- Metals and chemical manufacturing are two other important industries in Augusta.
- The Georgia Ports Authority supports the efficient operation of existing industries as well as local economic development efforts and is a key partner in ongoing recruitment of new industry.
- The GPA serves as a great resource to existing manufacturers who have invested billions in the region already, many of which have announced significant further investments in the last five years.
