## Facts

- The automotive parts manufacturing industry in Alabama thrives as a crucial segment of the broader automotive ecosystem.
- Companies produce a wide range of vital components, from powertrain parts to interior elements, catering to major automotive manufacturers.
- Many firms are adopting more sustainable practices as technology advances.
- There is a growing emphasis on lightweight materials and electric vehicle components, reflecting a broader shift towards fuel efficiency and emissions reduction.
- The sector contributes significantly to the state's economy by creating jobs and positions Alabama as a key player in the automotive supply chain.
- The listed companies represent a mix of sizes and specialties, ranging from small operations to large corporations.
- These firms were founded between 1954 and 2017 and are mostly privately owned.
- Headquarters are located throughout various Alabama cities such as Montgomery, Auburn, and Opelika.
- Hyundai MOBIS Alabama, LLC operates from a nearly 83-acre facility in Montgomery, Alabama, established in 2002.
- Hyundai MOBIS is the largest Tier 1 supplier to Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Alabama and Kia Motor Manufacturing Georgia.
- Hyundai MOBIS manufactures complete cockpit modules, front and rear chassis modules, bumper assemblies, and instrument panels.
- Hyundai MOBIS manages a Redistribution Center supplying genuine Hyundai and Kia service parts across North America.
- Seohan Auto USA Corporation is located in Auburn, Alabama, established in 2007, and specializes in automotive parts, wind power components, and forging services.
- Seohan Auto USA focuses on high-quality driveline and powertrain parts, including solenoid valves, pumps, and actuators.
- Alto Products Corp is based in Atmore, Alabama, founded in 1954, and specializes in wet friction materials and clutches.
- Alto has produced over a billion friction clutches and serves automotive, heavy-duty, marine, and motorcycle sectors.
- Bocar US, Inc. is an automotive supplier in Tanner, Alabama, established in 2017, specializing in aluminum and plastic components.
- Bocar produces complex assemblies through high-pressure die casting and injection molding processes.
- Moriroku Technology North America - Rainsville Plant was established in 2000 in Rainsville, Alabama, producing interior and exterior components primarily for Honda.
- REGITAR USA, Inc. has been based in Montgomery, Alabama, since 1987, specializing in ignition and charging system parts, safety products, and power tools.
- TSE Brakes is a Marmon/Berkshire Hathaway Company based in Cullman, Alabama, operational since 1966, manufacturing brake chambers and slack adjusters for OEMs and the aftermarket.
- Hanwha Advanced Materials America, LLC was founded in 2007 in Opelika, Alabama, producing lightweight composite materials like StrongLite and BuffLite for automotive fuel efficiency.
- Hanwha Advanced Materials produces bumper beams, seat structures, underbody shields, and interior trim panels, and acquired the GMT business unit from Azdel.
- SJA INC. Sejin America is located in Dadeville, Alabama, founded in 2007, specializing in pillar trim, complete consoles, quarter glass, cockpit elements, and spoilers.
- Webb Wheel Products is based in Cullman, Alabama, dating back to 1946, specializing in brake drums, hubs, rotors, and calipers for heavy-duty transportation.
- Webb Wheel Products invests millions in research and development and utilizes a distribution network of over 8,000 warehouse distributors and OEM dealers.
- C,G & J Inc Heat Transfer dba Brice Thomas Radiator is based in Gadsden, Alabama, established in 1956, producing automotive and industrial cooling products like radiators and charge air coolers.
- APR LLC was founded in 1997 in Opelika, Alabama, specializing in aftermarket performance products for Volkswagen Group brands including Audi, Porsche, and Lamborghini.
- Amphenol Tecvox was founded in 2003 in Huntsville, Alabama, designing and manufacturing automotive connectivity solutions like media hubs, charge ports, and wireless chargers for infotainment systems.
- Tris USA Inc was founded in 1990 in Athens, Alabama, specializing in carbon brushes and components for electric motors used in starter motors, alternators, and fuel pumps.
- Whitesell Group was founded in 1971 in Muscle Shoals, Alabama, specializing in engineered metal parts, fasteners, and components with supply chain management services like kitting and third-party logistics.
- Cast Products Inc. is a family-owned manufacturer in Athens, Alabama, founded in 1979, with a production capacity of 1.5 million cast aluminum components per year.
- Stamped Products, Inc. has been based in Gadsden, Alabama, since 1962, specializing in metal stamping and welding services for the automotive industry.
- Associated MetalCast was founded in 1998 in Oxford, Alabama, specializing in precision machining and casting services with a certified quality management system.
- MotoBilt was founded in 2012 in Ozark, Alabama, manufacturing and retailing American-made parts and accessories for off-road vehicles like bumpers and suspension components.
- Cumberland Plastic Solutions operates a 155,000-square-foot facility in Opelika, Alabama, established in 2006, equipped with 33 injection molding machines for automotive trim and HVAC assemblies.
- DRPco (Diamond Rubber Products) is a family-owned manufacturer in Birmingham, Alabama, founded in 1956, producing custom rubber parts like bumpers, fenders, and wheel chocks for automotive and industrial markets.
- T&C Stamping, Inc. operates a 96,000-square-foot facility in Athens, Alabama, established in 1985, processing over 15,000 tons of raw materials annually to produce more than 130 million stamped parts each year.
- Changer & Dresser Corp. was established in 2001 in Anniston, Alabama, serving as the North American division of Kyokutoh and providing automation solutions like auto tip dressers and arc welding for automotive manufacturers.
