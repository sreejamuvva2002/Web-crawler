# Volvo Car USA LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-10-07

**Source:** [Volvo Car USA announces major upgrades and new models as part of its electric 2026 lineup | Volvo Cars Media US](https://www.volvocars.com/us/media/press-releases/A2ED18400A01D55B/)  ·  `page_014157`

## Facts

- Volvo Car USA announced details of its 2026 fully electric lineup for U.S. customers, highlighted by upgrades to the EX90 and new EX30 variants.
- The 2026 EX90 features a new 800-volt electrical architecture that delivers faster, more efficient charging.
- At compatible 800-volt charging stations, the upgraded EX90 adds up to 155 miles of range in just 10 minutes.
- Every 2026 EX90 gains a dual NVIDIA DRIVE AGX Orin-based core computer with 500 TOPS of computing power for advanced safety and driver-assist features.
- New safety capabilities include connected safety alerts for slippery roads and hazards, expanded automatic emergency steering in darkness, and Emergency Stop Assist with integrated automatic e-call.
- Owners of the 2025 EX90 will receive this computer upgrade at no charge in the coming months.
- The 2026 EX90 adds a Single Motor powertrain option alongside Twin Motor and Twin Motor Performance variants, with the latter producing 670-hp.
- The 2026 EX90 adds an available electrochromic panoramic roof with adjustable tint and ventilated Nappa leather upholstery in three colors.
- Volvo is adding the EX30 Single Motor to the U.S. market in Plus equipment level with a starting MSRP of $38,950.
- The EX30 Single Motor offers an EPA-estimated range of up to 261 miles and can charge from 10 to 80 percent in 28 minutes.
- The EX30 is designed with a focus on minimizing its carbon footprint across its complete lifecycle, featuring zero tailpipe emissions and recycled/renewable materials throughout the interior.
- Volvo is introducing the fully electric EX30 Cross Country for U.S. customers, featuring higher ground clearance, standard all-wheel drive, and available 18-inch all-terrain tires.
- The EX30 Cross Country battery can charge from 10 to 80 percent in as little as 27 minutes.
- The EX30 Cross Country will be available only as the Twin Motor Ultra model with a starting MSRP of $48,150.
- The popular Black Edition styling option is now available on the fully electric EX40 in the U.S.
- Volvo Car USA LLC operates a manufacturing campus in Ridgeville, South Carolina.
- The Ridgeville operations underscore Volvo's long-term commitment to the United States as a key market.
- Volvo Cars' production plants are located in Gothenburg, Ghent (Belgium), South Carolina (US), Chengdu, Daqing and Taizhou (China).
- Volvo Car USA maintains offices in California and parts distribution and training centers across the country.
- For the full year 2024, Volvo Car Group recorded a core operating profit of SEK 27 billion and revenue of SEK 400.2 billion.
- Global sales for Volvo Cars reached a record 763,389 cars in 2024.
- As of December 2024, Volvo Cars employed approximately 42,600 full-time employees globally.
