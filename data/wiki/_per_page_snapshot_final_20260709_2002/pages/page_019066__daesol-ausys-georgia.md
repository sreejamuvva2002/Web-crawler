## Facts

- A South Korean automobile parts supplier announced Tuesday it will build a new manufacturing facility near Kia’s factory in West Point.
- Daesol Ausys Georgia, a supplier for Kia, Hyundai Motor Group and General Motors, will build a $72 million factory in Harris County.
- The new factory is located roughly 80 miles southwest of downtown Atlanta.
- The plant will employ more than 140 workers.
- The plant will manufacture luggage boards and covers for electric vehicles.
- The manufactured components will be used for Kia’s new electric EV9 SUV.
- Kia’s EV9 SUV is expected to roll off the West Point production lines next year.
- Gov. Brian Kemp stated in a news release that the project continues the ring of growth around Kia’s auto plant.
- The state aims to recreate this ring of growth with two other massive EV factories.
- Gov. Brian Kemp said, “West Point has become an automotive capital in its own right.”
- Gov. Brian Kemp stated that Georgia’s automotive industry employs over 50,000 hardworking Georgians and will continue to grow as e-mobility suppliers choose all corners of the state for the jobs of the future.
- Daesol Ausys was founded in 2017.
- Daesol Ausys specializes in designing and manufacturing motor and EV interior components and accessories.
- Daesol Ausys plans to open its new facility at the Northwest Harris Business Park off Ga. 103 in December 2024.
- Kia expects to launch its three-row SUV called the EV9 next year.
- Launching the EV9 requires a $200 million expansion to Kia’s existing factory.
- Kia’s West Point plant opened nearly 15 years ago and became Georgia’s only vehicle manufacturing plant.
- Hyundai Motor Group, Kia’s parent company, is building its $5.54 billion “Metaplant” near Savannah.
- EV startup Rivian expects to begin construction soon on its $5 billion EV factory in southern Morgan and Walton counties.
- Rivian’s new factory is located an hour east of Atlanta.
- Georgia has emerged as a leader in EV production.
- Since 2018, Georgia has attracted $25 billion in investment and 30,000 jobs in the EV industry.
- This fiscal year, suppliers flocking to locate near the forthcoming Georgia factories represent $2 billion of that investment.
- Daesol Ausys will likely qualify for job creation tax credits and worker training through Georgia Quick Start.
- A Georgia Department of Economic Development spokesperson said negotiations involving discretionary incentives, such as grants, remain active.
