# Kia Georgia

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-06-07

**Source:** [Kia at West Point just made history, rolling out first-ever EV made in Georgia, an EV9 SUV | Georgia Public Broadcasting](https://www.gpb.org/news/2024/06/07/kia-at-west-point-just-made-history-rolling-out-first-ever-ev-made-in-georgia-ev9)  ·  `page_001076`

## Facts

- Kia invested $200 million to expand operations at its plant in West Point, Georgia.
- The expansion occurred 10 months prior to the release of the EV9.
- Kia released the EV9, the first electric vehicle manufactured in the state of Georgia.
- Gov. Brian Kemp drove the EV9 off the production line at the West Point plant on a Thursday.
- Gov. Brian Kemp congratulated the Kia Georgia team for boosting the state’s EV portfolio, strengthening the battery belt, and advancing the climate agenda.
- Transportation activities accounted for 37.4 percent of U.S. CO2 emissions from fossil fuel combustion in 2022, according to the EPA US Greenhouse Gas Inventory.
- Gov. Brian Kemp stated that Georgia is working to become the e-mobility capital of the nation.
- The EV9 went on sale at the end of 2023.
- Prior to Georgia production, EV9s were shipped from South Korea and sold in all 50 states.
- The EV9 has received industry accolades including World Car of the Year, World EV of the Year, and North American Utility Vehicle of the Year.
- Sen. Jon Ossoff congratulated the Kia Georgia team at the rollout ceremony.
- Sen. Jon Ossoff stated that Georgians are proud of the cars produced in Georgia and made in America.
- Manufacturing the vehicle on American soil makes it at least partially eligible for a $7,500 EV tax credit under the Inflation Reduction Act.
- The $7,500 EV tax credit requires the EV to be made in America.
- The second requirement for the tax credit relates to battery components.
- Anne Blair, VP of the Electrification Coalition, stated that 50% of mineral components in an EV must now be sourced and processed in the U.S. or a trade partner country.
- Kia has not confirmed whether the Georgia-assembled EV9 will qualify for the full $7,500 tax credit.
- Kia Georgia spokesman Patrick Sands stated the company expects the Georgia-assembled EV9 to qualify for available incentives in the near future.
- Kia will provide additional details regarding tax credit qualification at a later date.
- The EV9 batteries take eight hours to charge on a 220V Level 2 circuit.
- The EV9 battery comes with a 10-year/100,00-mile warranty.
- Federal regulations mandate a minimum eight-year warranty for EV batteries.
- The EV9's MSRP ranges from $55,000 to $70,000, depending on trim level and incentives.
- The plant's expansion in July added 200 jobs.
- The new jobs support EV production as well as the Telluride, Sorento, and Sportage lineups.
- The West Point plant is listed as the fifth largest employer on the 'Choose Columbus' website.
- Approximately 86 people from Columbus work at the Kia Georgia plant in West Point.
