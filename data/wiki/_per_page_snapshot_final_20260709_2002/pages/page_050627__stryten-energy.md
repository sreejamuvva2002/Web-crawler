# Stryten Energy

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-01-27

**Source:** [Lead battery firm Stryten shifts into vanadium flow battery technology – Batteries International](https://www.batteriesinternational.com/2022/01/27/lead-battery-firm-stryten-shifts-into-vanadium-flow-battery-technology/)  ·  `page_050627`

## Facts

- On January 19, 2022, lead and lithium battery maker Stryten Energy announced the acquisition of Storion Energy to take on vanadium flow battery technology.
- The acquisition marks a shift in markets toward redox flow batteries used for longer-duration storage applications such as utilities, microgrids, and military bases.
- Storion Energy is a commercial spin-off from technology firm ITN Energy, which began research into redox flow batteries in 2010 and formed Storion in 2015 to bring the technology to market.
- Stryten Energy states that Storion’s VRFB design solves the energy-to-power tradeoff for batteries by allowing operation for many hours without sacrificing power or release speed through simply adding more electrolytes.
- Stryten Energy is based in Georgia, US.
- Stryten Energy CEO Tim Vargo stated that the clean energy transition in the US requires reliable battery solutions across multiple chemistries to meet growing demand for renewable power.
- According to Tim Vargo, adding Storion’s proprietary vanadium redox flow battery technology to Stryten’s portfolio will expand capabilities to solve current and future US energy challenges.
- Mike Judd, president and chief operating officer at Stryten Energy, noted that the Storion facility will complement Stryten’s four R&D centres in North America that focus on lead and lithium storage solutions.
- Stryten has created an Essential Power division to provide advanced lead and lithium battery technologies and services for applications ranging from telecommunications to microgrids, railways, and renewable energy storage.
- The company states that Storion’s VRFB technology will expand the portfolio of Stryten’s Essential Power division.
