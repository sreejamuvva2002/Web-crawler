# Bosch Sensortec

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Bosch to integrate 9-axis inertial sensor complements - EDN Asia](https://www.ednasia.com/bosch-to-integrate-9-axis-inertial-sensor-complements/)  ·  `page_018036`

## Facts

- Bosch Sensortec plans to integrate complete 9-axis inertial sensor complements along with more powerful ARM-based microcontrollers into a single package while aiming for the same power envelope as the hub alone.
- Stefan Finkbeiner, chief executive officer and general manager of Bosch Sensortec, announced upcoming trends in micro-electro-mechanical systems (MEMS) at the MEMS Executive Congress in Scottsdale, Arizona.
- Bosch Sensortec plans to add environmental sensors including volatile-organic-compounds (VOCs), barometric pressure, humidity, and temperature into a 2.5-millimeter package.
- i-Blades has a smartphone case that includes Bosch's environmental sensors and is popular in regions with poor air quality like China.
- The i-Blades smartphone case won the MEMS & Sensors Technology Showcase, earning a place in the MEMS Hall of Fame.
- Bosch aims to make its environmental sensors cheap and small enough for factory inclusion in smartphones, as well as for use in air purifiers and thermostats by lowering their price/performance ratio.
- Bosch's consumer-grade MEMS division and its automotive division are sharing resources for integrated navigation modules that are small and cheap like consumer devices but meet higher automotive MEMS standards.
- The Bosch automotive division announced a new super-high precision SMP580 barometric pressure sensor that significantly reduces fuel consumption and emissions at the same price.
- The Bosch automotive division introduced a new SMP580 barometric pressure sensor designed for immersion in automatic transmission oil, which results in smoother shifting, fast sensing with a response time of less than 1ms, and a smaller package sealed for the lifetime of the car.
