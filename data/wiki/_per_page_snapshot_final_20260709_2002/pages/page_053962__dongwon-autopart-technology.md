# Dongwon Autopart Technology

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-02-05

**Source:** [Korean Auto Parts Maker to Invest $30M in Rural Georgia - Georgia Asian Times](https://gasiantimes.com/business/2026/02/05/korean-auto-parts-maker-to-invest-30m-in-rural-georgia/)  ·  `page_053962`

## Facts

- Dongwon Autopart Technology will invest $30 million to build a new manufacturing plant in Emanuel County.
- The project will bring 200 jobs to the rural Georgia community over the coming years.
- The South Korean automotive supplier has operated in the U.S. since 2003.
- The facility will be constructed at the Highway 297 Industrial Park near Swainsboro.
- Hiring is expected to start in 2026 for positions including management, system leads, and production workers.
- Dongwon Autopart Technology was founded in 1971.
- The company manufactures doorframes, seat frames, and battery frames for automotive companies.
- The company currently employs over 500 people in the Southeast.
- Dongwon operates an existing plant in Hogansville, Georgia, which will continue operations alongside the new facility.
- Heather Hollstein, Human Resources Manager at Dongwon, stated, “We are proud to be part of growing the state's automotive industry.”
- Heather Hollstein added, “Thank you to Emanuel County for welcoming us.”
- Ken Warnock, CEO of the Emanuel County Development Authority, described the project as Emanuel County's largest economic development win in more than 30 years.
- Hiring for management positions, system leads, and production personnel will occur as construction progresses.
- Governor Brian Kemp praised the investment as evidence of Georgia's expanding automotive sector reaching communities statewide.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated, “Georgia's thriving automotive industry is creating new opportunities for Georgians statewide, no matter their zip code.”
- Georgia officials highlighted the expansion as part of the state's booming automotive sector along the I-16 corridor.
- Suppliers in the region are positioning themselves near major manufacturers and the Port of Savannah.
