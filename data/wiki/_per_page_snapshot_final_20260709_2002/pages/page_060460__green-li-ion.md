# Green Li-ion

**Source:** [Green Li-ion's plant in Atoka, Oklahoma is a Groundbreaking Achievement in the Battery Recycling Industry - Green Li-ion](https://www.greenli-ion.com/post/green-li-ion-launches-north-americas-first-commercial-scale-pcam-producing-plant-in-atoka-oklahoma)  ·  `page_060460`

## Facts

- Green Li-ion launched North America’s first commercial-scale plant to produce battery-grade materials from recycled lithium-ion batteries.
- The facility is located in Atoka, Oklahoma.
- The plant utilizes Green Li-ion’s patented multi-cathode-producing GREEN HYDROREJUVINATION™ technology.
- This technology processes unsorted battery waste, or “black mass,” directly into battery-grade precursor cathode active material (pCAM) and anode materials.
- The approach eliminates the need to export materials overseas for further processing, significantly reducing production time and greenhouse gas emissions.
- Green Li-ion offers modular hardware solutions that allow clients to integrate the technology within existing facilities through licensing agreements.
- Clients can alternatively utilize Green Li-ion’s tolling services by sending black mass to Green Li-ion-owned plants.
- The technology enables rapid deployment, allowing operations to begin within a few months rather than years.
- Green Li-ion's technology converts black mass into high-purity pCAM in approximately 12 hours.
- The process generates up to 90% fewer greenhouse gas emissions compared to the production of virgin materials.
- The Atoka plant's initial production capacity can process four to six metric tons of end-of-life batteries per day.
- This daily processing volume is equivalent to 72,000 smartphone batteries.
- Plans are already in place to quadruple the plant's capacity within the calendar year.
- The facility closes a critical gap in North America’s battery recycling supply chain.
- By producing battery-grade materials domestically, the plant supports the growing demand for electric vehicles (EVs) and other battery-powered technologies.
- The advancement aligns with provisions in the Inflation Reduction Act, which incentivizes domestic battery manufacturing and the use of recycled materials through substantial tax credits.
- Establishing the Atoka plant has created over 20 local jobs.
- There are plans to expand the workforce as the facility grows.
- Green Li-ion is committed to using local suppliers and partnering with educational institutions to strengthen integration with the Atoka community.
- The plant boosts the local economy and enhances Atoka’s visibility on the international stage.
