# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [About Us - HMGMA](https://www.hmgma.com/our-facility/)  ·  `page_000987`

## Facts

- In May 2022, Hyundai Motor Group entered into an agreement with the State of Georgia to build its first dedicated fully electrified vehicle and battery manufacturing facilities in the United States.
- HMG selected a 2,900-acre megasite in Bryan County for the project due to the area's quality of life, regional connectivity, global logistical connections and infrastructure, and existing workforce training programs.
- In October 2022, HMG broke ground on the Metaplant, its first state-of-the-art U.S. smart factory.
- Production at the Metaplant began in October 2024.
- Once fully operational, more than 8,500 people will work on the megasite.
- An additional 6,900 jobs will be created at offsite suppliers.
- The economic development agreement totals more than $7.5 billion, making it the largest in Georgia state history.
- The first Hyundai IONIQ 5 rolled off the production line at HMGMA in October 2024.
- In March 2025, production expanded with the launch of the Hyundai IONIQ 9.
- In June 2026, HMGMA launched the Kia Sportage Hybrid, marking the plant's first Kia model and first hybrid electric vehicle (HEV).
- The facility is designed to accommodate a wide range of vehicle models and propulsion systems with minimal modifications.
- The facility has the capacity to produce up to 10 different vehicles.
- The Metaplant will ultimately manufacture vehicles for all three Hyundai Motor Group brands: Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis.
- The megasite campus includes HLBMA, a joint venture battery manufacturing plant.
- Five key affiliates on the campus are Hyundai Mobis, Hyundai Glovis, Hyundai Transys, Hyundai Steel, and Hyundai Material.
- Another 22 suppliers operate nearby, creating an integrated network supporting vehicle production from battery manufacturing to final assembly.
- A solar panel-covered parking area at the front of the campus generates approximately five percent of HMGMA’s electricity needs.
- Inside the plant, robots and Automated Guided Vehicles (AGVs) work alongside employees known as Meta Pros.
- Robots handle heavy lifting, while AGVs deliver parts throughout the facility to improve safety and efficiency.
- Every stage of the manufacturing process, from order collection and procurement to logistics and production, is optimized using artificial intelligence and data-driven systems.
- Every vehicle undergoes rigorous testing on HMGMA’s nearly two-mile test track before shipment.
- HMGMA utilizes XCIENT heavy-duty hydrogen fuel-cell electric trucks to transport vehicle parts from regional suppliers daily.
- Once complete, most vehicles are shipped to customers by rail, connecting the Metaplant to markets across the United States.
