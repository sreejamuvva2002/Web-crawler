# Dongwon Autopart Technology

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-02-04

**Source:** [New $30 million auto parts plant to add 200 jobs in Emanuel Co.](https://www.yahoo.com/news/articles/30-million-auto-parts-plant-153207540.html)  ·  `page_053987`

## Facts

- Dongwon Autopart Technology plans to invest $30 million in a new production facility in Emanuel County, Georgia.
- The automotive project is anticipated to create 200 new jobs over the next several years.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced the investment and stated that Georgia's thriving automotive industry is creating new opportunities for Georgians statewide regardless of zip code.
- Dongwon Autopart Technology was established in 1971 and supplies components such as doorframes, seat frames, and battery frames to automotive original equipment manufacturers (OEMs).
- The company currently supports more than 500 direct jobs in the Southeast, including at facilities in Hogansville, Georgia that will remain operational.
- Dongwon Autopart Technology has been operating in the United States since 2003.
- Heather Hollstein, Human Resources Manager at Dongwon Autopart Technology, stated the company is proud to be part of growing the state's automotive industry and remains committed to supplying internal and external customers with high-quality products and service.
- Construction on the new manufacturing facility will take place at the Hwy 297 Industrial Park near Swainsboro.
- The company will be hiring for positions including management, system leads, and production personnel.
- Hiring is expected to begin in 2026.
- Emanuel County Development Authority Chairman Will Donaldson welcomed Dongwon Autopart Technology to the county, noting that the project pays off the authority's time, energy, and investment in economic development.
- Ken Warnock, CEO of the Emanuel County Development Authority, assisted the company in locating the site and described the facility as the single largest new economic development project for Emanuel County in over 30 years.
- Alyce Thornhill, Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy at the Georgia Department of Economic Development's Global Commerce team, represented GDEcD on the competitive project.
- The project was developed in partnership with the Emanuel County Development Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that the automotive industry is expanding outward along the I-16 corridor as suppliers choose strategic locations with access to OEMs and Georgia's ports.
- Dongwon Autopart Technology's new facility will be its second location in Georgia, reflecting the robust growth of the state's automotive industry over more than 100 years.
