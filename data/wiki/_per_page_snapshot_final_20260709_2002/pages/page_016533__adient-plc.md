# Adient PLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2024-09-15

**Source:** [page_016533](https://www.aminext.com/blog/2019/1/4/am-i-next-no-love-at-adient)  ·  `page_016533`

## Facts

- Adient PLC is laying off 172 employees at its Riverside, Missouri, manufacturing facility in two phases: 109 employees on November 11, 2024, and the remaining 63 employees on January 6, 2025.
- The layoff decision was driven by the end of contract production for a single customer.
- Adient PLC is closing its automotive seating facility in Charlotte, Michigan and permanently laying off 212 employees as of January 4, 2019.
- According to an Adient spokesperson, the Charlotte plant's core capability is injection molding, which is not part of Adient's core product portfolio.
- The company was originally spun off from Milwaukee, Wisconsin-based Johnson Controls in 2016.
- Adient acquired the Futuris Group in 2017.
- Adient canceled its plans to move its Plymouth headquarters to Detroit, Michigan due to 'financial considerations'.
- All equipment and any existing contracts at the Charlotte facility will be sold.
