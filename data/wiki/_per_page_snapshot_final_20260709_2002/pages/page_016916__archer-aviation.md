# Archer Aviation

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-10-30

**Source:** [Archer’s High‑Volume Manufacturing Facility Energizes Newton County Economy](https://www.selectnewton.com/post/archer-highvolume-manufacturing-facility)  ·  `page_016916`

## Facts

- Construction is complete on Archer Aviation's high-volume manufacturing facility at the Covington Municipal Airport.
- The roughly 400,000-square-foot facility, codenamed ARC, is located in Covington, Georgia.
- The facility has received its certificate of occupancy.
- Tooling load-in will begin ahead of initial production efforts slated to start in early 2025.
- Archer's partner Stellantis plans to contribute manufacturing expertise, capital investment, and an experienced workforce to the facility.
- The goal is ramping production to 650 aircraft annually by 2030.
- The development will bring increased employment opportunities, supply-chain development, and enhanced regional prominence in high-tech sectors to Newton County.
- The timing and scale of the facility align with the goals of the Newton County Industrial Development Authority (NCIDA).
- The facility aims to provide high-quality jobs in production, engineering, and maintenance roles.
- The facility aims to expand the local supplier ecosystem to support global-scale manufacturing operations.
- The facility aims to elevate Newton County's infrastructure, talent base, and reputation for innovation.
- Governor Brian Kemp called this development “the next step in that journey” for Newton County’s economic growth.
- In early 2025, tooling load-in will transition toward manufacturing of Archer’s all-electric vertical take-off and landing aircraft, the Midnight model.
- Newton County will monitor local job creation and training opportunities in advanced manufacturing.
- Newton County will monitor growth of supply-chain firms locating or expanding operations in the region.
- Newton County will monitor impacts on the local economy from real-estate development to services supporting the facility’s workforce.
