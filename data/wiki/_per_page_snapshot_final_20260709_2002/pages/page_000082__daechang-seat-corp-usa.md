# Daechang Seat Corp. USA

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-10-23

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp: Automotive Supplier Daechang Seat Corp. USA to Create 500 Jobs in Chatham County | Governor Brian P. Kemp Office of the Governor](https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases/2023-10-23/gov-kemp-automotive-supplier-daechang-seat-corp-usa-create-500-jobs)  ·  `page_000082`

## Facts

- Daechang Seat Corp. USA will invest $72.5 million in the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center, in close proximity to Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA).
- The investment will create over 500 jobs in Chatham County.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that suppliers for the Hyundai Metaplant project alone reach at least nine counties.
- Daechang Seat Corp. USA is adding to Georgia's growing manufacturing base centered around Chatham County and the Savannah region.
- Daechang Seat Corp. (DSC) was established in 1979 in Kyungju, South Korea.
- DSC is one of the largest seat frame producers in the world.
- Globally, DSC employs more than 5,000 people across seven countries.
- The company's production process includes fully automatic lines with arc welding and spot-welding machines.
- CEO Jinsuk Lee expressed appreciation to the State of Georgia, local communities, and the City of Savannah for their support.
- DSC's primary objective is to drive growth through facility expansions to transform into a global entity.
- DSC currently holds a prominent partnership with Hyundai Transys and Hyundai Mobis at HMGMA.
- DSC's new facility will be located at the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center.
- The company will hire for assembly operators, equipment technicians, mechanical engineers, and electrical engineers through local job fairs and website postings.
- The facility is located only minutes from the Port of Savannah to leverage Georgia's world-class connectivity.
- Daechang Seat Corp. USA is the third Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America supplier to locate in the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center.
- The project was facilitated by the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s Global Commerce team in partnership with the Savannah Economic Development Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson highlighted that Chatham County has leveraged Savannah's status as an established global gateway to foster economic growth.
- As the market transitions to electric vehicles, Georgia has created more than $25 billion in investments and 30,000 jobs since 2018.
- In fiscal year 2023, automotive industry job creation in Georgia increased by 324 percent compared to fiscal year 2021.
- Suppliers attracted by Georgia's mobility original equipment manufacturers resulted in over $2 billion in investment across the state in FY23.
- DSC has recently ventured into the electric vehicle sector and continues to expand its scope.
