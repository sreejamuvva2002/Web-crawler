# Daesol Ausys

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-08-31

**Source:** [Daesol Ausys Auto: SK auto supplier, Daesol Ausys plans USD 72 million plant in Georgia to build EV parts, ETAuto](https://auto.economictimes.indiatimes.com/news/auto-components/sk-auto-supplier-daesol-ausys-plans-usd-72-million-plant-in-georgia-to-build-ev-parts/103239437)  ·  `page_018980`

## Facts

- Daesol Ausys, a South Korean auto supplier, plans to build a USD 72 million factory in Georgia to make parts for electric vehicles.
- The company will hire more than 140 workers for the new facility.
- Daesol Ausys announced its plans on Tuesday to build a factory in West Point, Georgia.
- The plant will manufacture luggage boards and covers for compartments in electric vehicles.
- The company supplies parts to Hyundai, Kia, and General Motors.
- This will be the third factory in the same West Point industrial park for members of the company's corporate family.
- Daehan Solution Co. opened a million factory in the same West Point industrial park in 2008 to make headliners and other sound insulation parts for the nearby Kia plant.
- Daehan Solution Co. currently employs 350 full-time workers.
- Daesol Material opened a USD 35 million factory in the same West Point industrial park in 2017 to make automotive fiber padding.
- Daesol Material currently employs 50 workers.
- These employment figures were reported by Meghan Richardson, an economic developer for the city of West Point.
- Daehan Solution Co. also operates a plant near Montgomery, Alabama, which supplies parts to the Hyundai assembly plant there.
- The announcement follows a wave of electric vehicle suppliers announcing plants in Georgia after Hyundai said in 2022 that it would build a USD 5.5 billion plant to assemble electric vehicles and batteries in Ellabell, Georgia, near Savannah.
- The Hyundai site in Ellabell could grow to 8,100 employees and is slated to begin producing vehicles in 2025.
- EV suppliers have pledged to invest nearly USD 2.2 billion and hire 5,000 people for the Georgia projects.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp stated that the state's automotive industry employs over 50,000 Georgians and will continue to grow as e-mobility suppliers choose locations across the state.
- Daesol Ausys is scheduled to begin production in December 2024.
- The company will pay workers USD 15 to USD 20 an hour.
- The state of Georgia will pay to train workers for the facility.
- The state gave USD 500,000 last year to help Harris County clear and grade the site and build a road, which the county matched using sales tax collections.
- The city of West Point and Harris County are granting eight years of property tax breaks worth a projected USD 5.7 million.
- Daesol Ausys could qualify for USD 875,000 in state income tax credits, calculated at USD 1,250 per job over five years.
- To qualify for the USD 875,000 in state income tax credits, workers must make at least USD 31,300 a year.
