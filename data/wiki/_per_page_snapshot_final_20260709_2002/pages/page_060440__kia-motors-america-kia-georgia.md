# Kia Motors America / Kia Georgia

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-07

**Source:** [KIA to invest more than $200M, hire nearly 200 as it starts ...](https://www.valleytimes-news.com/2023/07/kia-to-invest-more-than-200m-hire-nearly-200-as-it-starts-assembling-ev9/)  ·  `page_060440`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Kia will invest more than $200 million and create nearly 200 new jobs at its West Point plant to accommodate assembly of the all-electric 2024 EV9 three-row SUV.
- The production line transformation will make the EV9 the fifth model assembled at Kia Georgia, joining the Telluride, Sorento and Sportage SUVs, and the K5 mid-size sedan.
- EV9 assembly is expected to begin in the second quarter of 2024.
- Governor Kemp stated that Georgia's longstanding partnership with Kia has led to generational job creation and growth for the West Point area.
- Governor Kemp noted that the project will bring more opportunity to hardworking Georgians and help propel the state toward its goal of becoming the e-mobility capital.
- Currently, more than 40 percent of Kia vehicles sold in the U.S. are manufactured in West Point.
- The addition of the EV9 to the lineup will further increase the facility's output.
- The EV9 is the brand's first three-row EV SUV and the first Kia EV to be assembled in North America.
- The EV9 serves as the flagship vehicle for Kia's global Plan S strategy focused on sustainable mobility leadership.
- Sean Yoon, President & CEO of Kia Motors North America and Kia Motors America, stated that the EV9 has the potential to be another change catalyst for Kia like the Telluride.
- Sean Yoon described the EV9 as the most innovative vehicle Kia has ever built and a standout in the EV market and on the road.
- Kia Georgia is a certified Georgia Made manufacturer.
- Kia Georgia will continue to utilize the services of Georgia Quick Start, the nation's top-ranked workforce development program, which operates the Kia Georgia Training Center.
- Georgia Quick Start's comprehensive, customized training services will prepare new employees with the skills required to perform successfully on the new assembly line.
- Stuart Countess, President & CEO of Kia Georgia, stated that the Kia Georgia team looks forward to continuing its tradition of being the best of the best as the plant begins production of its first EV.
- Stuart Countess highlighted partnerships developed statewide with the Governor's Office, the Department of Economic Development, and Georgia QuickStart, as well as local partnerships with the City of West Point and Troup County.
- City of West Point Mayor Steve Tramell stated that Kia has been an outstanding corporate citizen and community partner for seventeen years and looks forward to a strong future together.
- Mayor Tramell noted that by providing employment opportunities and fostering economic growth, Kia has helped improve the livelihoods of many people in West Point.
- Senior Regional Project Manager Amanda Fields represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development's Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The project involved partnerships with the West Point Development Authority, the City of West Point, Troup County, Georgia Center of Innovation's Manufacturing and Energy teams, and Georgia Quick Start.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that since Georgia recruited Kia in 2006, Kia has exceeded expectations as a partner by giving back to its adopted community.
- Commissioner Wilson thanked Kia, partners in West Point, and state and local agencies for working together to meet the needs of today and tomorrow's leading industries.
- To date, Kia has invested more than $1.9 billion in Georgia.
- Kia Georgia is responsible for more than 14,000 plant and supplier jobs in the region.
- Earlier this year, Governor Kemp and state lawmakers recognized January 31 as "Kia Day" to commend Kia's vehicle manufacturing and impact since the 2006 announcement of a state-of-the-art vehicle assembly facility in West Point.
- The 2006 announcement marked one of the biggest single foreign investments in Georgia's history.
- From Georgia, Kia ships vehicles to sales markets around the globe, including the United States, Canada, Mexico, the Caribbean, Middle East, and Pacific nations.
- Georgia has pursued job creation along the entire EV supply chain, resulting in more than $22.7 billion in investment and the creation of over 28,400 jobs since 2020.
