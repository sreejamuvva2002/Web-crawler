# Amara Industries

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2025-12-09

**Source:** [Amara Forges a Molding Powerhouse with Thomson Plastics Acquisition - BriefGlance.com](https://briefglance.com/articles/amara-forges-a-molding-powerhouse-with-thomson-plastics-acquisition)  ·  `page_011285`

## Facts

- Amara Industries has acquired Thomson Plastics, a recognized leader in large-tonnage injection molding.
- The acquisition merges a newly-focused custom solutions provider with a veteran of high-tech manufacturing to create a formidable entity for high-performance molded products.
- Amara Industries was recently launched as an independent entity from the customs division of Gracious Living Corporation and now acts as the parent company to the Gracious Living group.
- This acquisition is Amara Industries' inaugural strategic milestone.
- The deal absorbs Thomson Plastics' two advanced facilities located in Georgia and North Carolina.
- The acquisition expands Amara Industries' manufacturing footprint to five locations.
- The move establishes a critical foothold for Amara in the Southeastern United States.
- The acquisition signals an aggressive growth strategy aimed at the industrial, automotive, and commercial sectors.
- Amara Industries provides end-to-end custom manufacturing solutions ranging from design and engineering to materials science.
- Thomson Plastics specializes in large-tonnage injection molding, a specialized field where less than 4% of U.S. plastics manufacturers compete.
- Thomson Plastics' machinery boasts clamping forces up to 3,700 tons.
- Nearly a third of Thomson Plastics' fleet exceeds the 1,000-ton mark.
- Thomson Plastics produces massive single-piece components essential for automotive body panels, industrial containers, and large consumer goods.
- Vince Orlando serves as Co-President at Amara Industries.
- Vince Orlando stated that the addition of Thomson Plastics represents a strategic milestone that will enhance their ability to serve customers with scale, speed, and precision across North America.
- Thomson Plastics operates with 95% automation, with its machinery integrated with 3- to 6-axis robotics.
- Thomson Plastics has developed in-house expertise for designing, customizing, and maintaining its robotic systems and critical end-of-arm tooling.
- The deep integration of robotics minimizes cycle times, reduces waste, and ensures manufacturing consistency.
- The acquired technical portfolio includes gas-assisted molding, which reduces the weight and cycle time of large, dense parts.
- The acquired technical portfolio also includes over-molding, a process for creating components with multiple materials such as soft-grip handles on power tools or automotive steering wheels.
- The acquisition establishes a major advanced manufacturing presence in Georgia and North Carolina.
- Since 2020, the Southeastern region has seen an influx of over 650,000 workers into production and warehouse roles, with job growth outpacing the national average.
- Placing these capabilities in the Southeast addresses supply chain fragility for automotive, appliance, and industrial sector customers concentrated in the region.
- Local sourcing in the Southeast enables faster delivery, reduced logistics costs, and greater agility to respond to market shifts.
- The acquisition strengthens regional supply chains, making them more resilient and less dependent on overseas production.
- The North American injection molding industry is projected to reach a value of over $81 billion by 2033.
- Market growth is fueled by relentless demand from the automotive sector for lightweight components to improve fuel efficiency, as well as innovation in consumer goods and packaging.
- The combined entity plans to integrate advanced materials such as composites and bioplastics to meet rising demands for customization and sustainability.
