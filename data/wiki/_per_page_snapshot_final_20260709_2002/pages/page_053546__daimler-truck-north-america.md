# Daimler Truck North America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-07-09

**Source:** [DTNA Aims to Increase Margins With Focus on Western Star - TT](https://www.ttnews.com/articles/dtna-aims-increase-margins)  ·  `page_053546`

## Facts

- DTNA aims to increase its margins in the second half of the 2020s.
- DTNA accentuates a position as the key engine of growth for parent company Daimler Truck.
- The margin push will be driven by an ever greater focus on the heavy-duty vocational truck sector through Western Star, increased parts sales, reduced costs, and a decrease in capital expenditure.
- Parent company Daimler Truck’s return on sales was 12.9% in 2024 and is expected to be 12% in 2025.
- By 2030, DTNA expects its North American unit’s margin to reach up to 14%, which is 2 percentage points higher than the ceiling forecast at Capital Market Day in 2023.
- DTNA’s margin floor for 2030 is rising to 10% from 9%.
- DTNA margins came in at 9.2% in 2021 and 10.8% in 2022.
- Daimler Truck’s ROS was 7.7% in 2022, 9.9% in 2023, and 8.9% in 2024.
- The parent company is aiming for an overall ROS of 12% from its truck and bus units by 2030, including Mercedes-Benz Trucks and Asian truck units.
- CEO John O’Leary stated DTNA has delivered consistently strong financial performance over the past years and is building on that momentum.
- The strategic focus will be on increasing the rate of vocational truck market growth and optimizing the product lineup.
- DTNA aims to increase its heavy-duty vocational truck sales by 60% by 2030.
- DTNA is aiming for a heavy-duty vocational truck market share of more than 35% by 2030.
- Western Star launched the X Series (47X, 49X and 57X) starting in 2020.
- Western Star upped its market share over the first half of the 2020s, benefiting from competitors’ constrained capacity starting in 2023.
- DTNA completely inverted its model at the Cleveland Truck Plant due to increased vocational orders and sales.
- Vocational trucks are high consumers of parts, which drives aftermarket sales and boosts margins.
- The shift is part of a companywide “Service First” program, one of five pillars of Daimler Truck’s “Stronger 2030” strategy unveiled at Capital Market Day 2025.
- DTNA aims to capture a greater share of the second life cycle of parts and increase its third and fourth customer share beyond dealerships.
- The Service First program will have three legs: stand-alone parts stores, direct fulfillment, and portfolio expansion.
- DTNA cut costs by 185 million euros post-COVID pandemic.
- In mid-2024, DTNA reduced its white-collar headcount by 200 employees.
- DTNA plans to return operating efficiency to pre-COVID levels, reduce non-value-added complexity, increase automation, and improve efficiency through AI-identified strategies.
- Companywide capital expenditure will peak in 2026.
- Freightliner launched the fifth-generation Cascadia in October.
- CEO Karin Radstrom said the parent company chose Cleveland partly because of pre-buy expectations, but market doubt and volatility increased.
- CFO Eva Scherer noted the order environment is very challenging and targets set at the start of the year are becoming distant.
- In May, Daimler Truck cut its 2025 North American truck sales guidance due to a weak U.S. freight market and trade policy uncertainty.
- Marketwide heavy-duty Class 8 sales forecast for 2025 is 260,000 to 290,000 vehicles, down from previous expectations of 280,000-320,000 trucks.
- DTNA is forecast to sell 155,000 to 175,000 trucks in 2025 across all classes, down from 180,000 to 200,000 before tariffs and reciprocal actions.
