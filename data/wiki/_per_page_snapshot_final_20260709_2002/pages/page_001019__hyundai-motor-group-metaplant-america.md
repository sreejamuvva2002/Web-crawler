# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-06-20

**Source:** [Hyundai to start Georgia EV plant, unlocking IRA benefits - The Korea Herald](https://www.koreaherald.com/article/3417221)  ·  `page_001019`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America is Hyundai Motor Group's new electric vehicle plant in Georgia.
- The plant will begin production later this year.
- Production start will allow Hyundai to leverage Inflation Reduction Act benefits to better compete in the US EV market.
- Hyundai announced that the inaugural model produced at the Georgia plant will be the 2025 Ioniq 5.
- The launch of the inaugural model is set for the fourth quarter.
- This announcement marks Hyundai's first official statement on the operational timeline and specific production plans for the new facility.
- The plant will manufacture all trims of the Ioniq 5 except the high-performance Ioniq 5 N.
- Industry expectations pinpoint October as the likely start date for production.
- HMGMA is located in Bryan County, Georgia.
- Hyundai Motor Group began constructing the dedicated EV plant in October 2022.
- The project was initially slated for completion in the first half of next year.
- The project timeline was accelerated by about three months to align with the Biden administration's IRA incentives.
- The IRA incentives provide substantial benefits for domestically produced electric vehicles.
- Once fully operational, HMGMA is expected to significantly boost Hyundai's market share in the US.
- Production of the 2025 Ioniq 5 in the US will make it eligible for the full $7,500 IRA subsidy.
- Currently, Hyundai does not qualify for the US government's IRA subsidy of up to $7,500 for electric vehicles.
- To compensate for not qualifying for the IRA subsidy, Hyundai offers $7,500 in cash incentives to customers purchasing the 2024 Ioniq 5.
- According to the Korea Automobile & Mobility Association, Hyundai and Kia collectively captured an 11.2 percent share of the US EV market from January to May this year.
- Hyundai and Kia ranked second behind Tesla in the US EV market with sales of 48,838 units out of 437,246 total electric passenger vehicles sold.
- Beyond the Ioniq 5, Hyundai Motor Group plans to produce six electric vehicles under the Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis brands at the HMGMA facility.
- The company intends to manufacture hybrid models at the plant due to growing demand for hybrid vehicles in the US.
- Hyundai Motor Group has invested $7.59 billion in the HMGMA project.
- The project is creating 8,500 jobs.
