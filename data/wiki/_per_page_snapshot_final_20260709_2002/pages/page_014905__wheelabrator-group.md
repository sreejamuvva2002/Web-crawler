# Wheelabrator Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- Industry veteran Mike Reaves returns to Wheelabrator to head up the North American EMP (“Equipment Modernization Program”) team as a technical specialist.
- Mike devoted nearly 20 years of his career to Wheelabrator, joining in 1997 and initially designing new equipment.
- He subsequently worked as engineering manager and across engineering services, quality assurance, and new product development.
- In his ultimate role as director of aftermarket engineering, he was part of the team behind the multi-patented EZEFIT® blast wheel (living on in today’s EZX® technology) and built out the equipment upgrade offering.
- He completed an MBA from Columbus State University, which prompted his move in 2017 into a chief operating officer role outside the world of shot blasting.
- Almost ten years later, he rejoins to shape the EMP offering for a new era – faster, smarter, more digital.
- Marcus Montie, Senior Vice President of Norican North America, comments that if machines last decades, innovation can’t wait until the next equipment purchase.
- Marcus Montie notes Mike saw this early on and built products and services around it, pushing the limits of what you can get out of existing machines and being part of some of the most successful aftermarket developments of the past decades.
- Marcus Montie adds that Mike rejoins at a time when digital tools and innovative wear parts enable continuous injection of new technology on existing shot blast equipment, and will ensure customers get maximum performance and value from installed machines.
