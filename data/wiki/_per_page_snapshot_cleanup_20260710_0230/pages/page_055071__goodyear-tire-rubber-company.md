# Goodyear Tire & Rubber Company

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 1898-08-29

**Source:** [Goodyear Tire and Rubber Company - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Goodyear_Tire_and_Rubber_Company)  ·  `page_055071`

## Facts

- Goodyear operates a tread rubber manufacturing facility in Social Circle, Georgia.
- Goodyear operates an aircraft tire retreading facility in Stockbridge, Georgia.
- Goodyear manufactures tires for passenger vehicles, commercial trucks, military and police vehicles, motorcycles, recreational vehicles, race cars, and heavy off-road machinery.
- Goodyear is the sole tire supplier for the NASCAR series.
- In 1989, President Tom Barrett began modernizing and expanding Goodyear plants in Scottsboro, Alabama, and Point Pleasant, West Virginia.
- Goodyear operates a passenger car tire facility in Fayetteville, North Carolina.
- Goodyear operates a passenger car tire facility in Gadsden, Alabama.
- In 2018, Goodyear and Bridgestone announced the creation of TireHub, a joint wholesale distribution network across the United States.
- Goodyear acquired Cooper Tire & Rubber Company for $2.5 billion, with the transaction closing in the second half of 2021.
- Goodyear's Eagle LS2000 tire uses BioTRED compounding technology, which replaces traditional carbon black and silica with filler materials derived from corn starch to increase flexibility and resistance to energy loss.
- Goodyear joined the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency's SmartWay Transport Partnership, providing Fuel Max linehaul tires that deliver up to 4% improved truck fuel economy and can save over $11,000 annually per tractor and trailer.
- Goodyear operates manufacturing facilities in Clarksdale, Mississippi; Danville, Virginia; Lawton, Oklahoma; Statesville, North Carolina; Topeka, Kansas; and Tupelo, Mississippi.
