# Robert Bosch GmbH

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Bosch focuses on growth in United States - Bosch Media Service US](https://www.bosch-presse.de/pressportal/us/en/press-release-27520.html)  ·  `page_018039`

## Facts

- Bosch posted consolidated sales of $17.3 billion USD (16 billion euros) and achieved total net sales of $18.6 billion USD including sales of non-consolidated companies and internal deliveries to affiliated companies in 2024.
- The North American region achieved the largest sales increase globally for the Bosch Group in 2024, emphasizing the company’s focus on the region and particularly the U.S. market as part of achieving regional balance globally.
- Stefan Hartung, chairman of Bosch, stated that by 2030 the company aims for the North American region to represent 20 percent of global sales, with the U.S. representing the majority.
- Over the past five years, Bosch has invested more than $2 billion in capital expenditures in the United States, representing more than 75 percent of its capital expenditures in the North American region over that time.
- Since 2023, Bosch has announced plans to invest around $6 billion in U.S.-based acquisitions.
- Paul Thomas, president of Bosch in North America and president of Bosch Mobility Americas, stated that Bosch is investing to grow in the U.S. market by developing regionally relevant products and solutions.
- Bosch has been in the United States since 1906 and will celebrate its 120-year anniversary in the U.S. in 2026.
- The company currently employs more than 20,000 associates and operates 15+ manufacturing sites across the United States.
- Both the number of associates and U.S. manufacturing sites are expected to grow in 2025 following the closure of a major planned acquisition.
- In 2024, Bosch's Mobility business in North America increased sales to third parties to $10.7 billion USD despite market challenges.
- Bosch continues to invest in and book customer wins for technologies supporting U.S. mobility trends, including software-defined vehicles (SDV), advanced-driver assistance systems (ADAS), hybrid technology, and vehicle motion control.
- Bosch has continued to innovate on internal combustion engine (ICE) technology, including technology to support hybrids, maintaining revenue as ICE vehicle production decreases and BEV vehicles increase.
- The company supports customers with battery electric and hydrogen solutions, providing both fuel cell and hydrogen combustion technology.
- Paul Thomas stated that Bosch's goal is to provide manufacturers and U.S. consumers with affordable options across its Mobility portfolio.
- Bosch's technology leadership in the U.S. mobility sector is realized through a workforce at nine locations in California, Indiana, Kentucky, Michigan, Minnesota, and South Carolina.
- Bosch plans to manufacture second-generation silicon carbide (SiC) chips on 200mm wafers in Roseville, California beginning in 2026.
- The transformation of the acquired Roseville facility represents an investment of up to $1.9 billion.
- During the Roseville facility transformation, Bosch has retained nearly all 250 associates and provided advanced training through its global manufacturing network.
- A major driver of growth for Bosch Mobility in North America in 2024 was the Cross-Domain Computing Solutions division, which delivers ADAS and vehicle computers to support U.S. automakers' software-defined focus.
- Bosch has expanded its software and hardware for assisted and automated driving to facilitate rapid development in ADAS, particularly Level 2 and Level 2++.
- For its new radar sensor, Bosch developed and manufactured all core elements in-house, including a computing chip (SoC) using RF CMOS technology with transistor structure sizes of only 22 nanometers.
- Bosch is the first Tier 1 supplier to introduce this 22-nanometer radar sensor technology in serial production.
- In the U.S., Bosch signed a major award with the Southeastern Pennsylvania Transportation Authority (SEPTA) to install tram forward collision warning systems across its entire fleet via retrofit, following a pilot program that began in 2022 on four vehicles.
- SEPTA serves a region of nearly four million people in Philadelphia and its surrounding counties.
- In 2024, Bosch announced plans to acquire the HVAC solutions business for residential and light commercial buildings from Johnson Controls, including brands like York and Coleman, with closure expected during 2025.
- The Bosch Group generated 90.3 billion euros in sales revenue in 2024, a decrease of 1.4 percent year-over-year (or 0.5 percent after adjusting for exchange-rate effects).
- Stefan Hartung stated that with normal inflation between 2 and 3 percent, Bosch aims to achieve annual growth of between 6 and 8 percent on average until 2030.
- Bosch increased its sales revenue by 4 percent in the first quarter of the year compared to the previous year.
- The company is aiming for a target margin of 7 percent in 2026, viewing it as an extremely challenging task given current market conditions.
- As one of Europe’s biggest corporate venture-capital investors, Bosch announced a new fund through its subsidiary Bosch Ventures providing around $270 million USD.
- Bosch expects developments in electromobility, hydrogen, and software-defined vehicles to be major stimuli for growth in its core Mobility business sector.
- Factory automation at Bosch is set to focus on growth areas such as battery, semiconductor, and consumer goods production.
- Bosch is underlining its commitment to climate action with new scope 3 targets aiming to bring down carbon emissions outside its direct sphere of influence further by 2030.
- The company wants to double its corresponding CO2 reduction target from 15 percent to 30 percent by 2030 compared to 2018.
