# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-03-26

**Source:** [Hyundai’s $7.6B Factory of the Future Opens in Georgia - Newsweek](https://www.newsweek.com/hyundai-metaplant-america-georgia-factory-2050288)  ·  `page_009228`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group (HMG) celebrated the opening of its new manufacturing facility outside Savannah, Georgia.
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) is described as the company's factory of the future for regional production of new vehicles, including the forthcoming Hyundai Ioniq 9 SUV.
- HMGMA is part of a $7.59 billion investment made in Bryan County, Georgia, by HMG.
- The facility includes a joint venture 30 gigawatt-hour battery plant with LG Energy Solution (LGES).
- It joins the company's SK On battery plant in Commerce, Georgia, as a producer of batteries for HMG's electric vehicles.
- The new site brings Hyundai Motor Group's total investment in Georgia to $12.6 billion.
- The new site is the largest economic development project in Georgia's history.
- Governor Brian Kemp attended the opening ceremony alongside Hyundai Motor Group Executive Chair Chung, U.S. Representative Buddy Carter, Hyundai Motor Group Vice Chair Jaehoon Chang, Hyundai Motor Company President and CEO José Muñoz, and Kia Corporation President and CEO Ho Sung Song.
- HMG is the parent company of the Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis vehicle brands, Hyundai Mobis, Hyundai Transys, Hyundai Glovis, and Hyundai Steel.
- It is Hyundai's second plant in Georgia; the other is Kia Georgia in West Point.
- Kia Georgia in West Point makes the Telluride, Sorento, Sportage, EV9, and EV6 SUVs.
- It is Hyundai's third plant in America's Southeast; Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Alabama in Montgomery makes the Hyundai Tucson, Santa Fe, Santa Fe Hybrid SUVs, Hyundai Santa Cruz truck, and Genesis GV70 SUVs.
- HMGMA features 16 million square feet of manufacturing space.
- The facility will have the ability to manufacture 500,000 units annually once fully operational.
- Construction of the facility began in January 2023.
- The site is strategically located off Interstate 16, close to Interstate 95 and the Port of Savannah, and linked to Class 1 rail.
- HMGMA features proof-of-concept manufacturing processes and technologies from Hyundai Motor Group Innovation Center Singapore, scaled to accommodate larger volume production.
- The facility's processes (order collection, procurement, logistics, and production) are optimized using artificial intelligence and real-time data gathering.
- Robots assist humans in the factory floor work, including Boston Dynamics' Spot robot which completes exterior inspection tasks.
- Hyundai acquired Boston Dynamics at a cost of $1.1 billion in 2021.
- The factory floor features open layouts with daylight streaming in via skylights and indoor landscaping to encourage communication and collaboration.
- Vehicles travel by conveyor belt from the paint shop to the general assembly area, visible to travelers on the interstate.
- HMGMA's parking lot generates solar energy via panels above its 1,878 spaces, accounting for 5 percent of the plant's total power consumption.
- The site features a 41-acre park with trails, running tracks, sports fields, and picnic areas for workers called Meta Pros.
- HMG will utilize 21 XCIENT Fuel Cell trucks, which run on hydrogen fuel and emit only water vapor, at the site for logistics operations.
- This truck deployment marks the first step in reducing emissions from Hyundai's internal logistics operations.
- The Ioniq 5 was the first vehicle produced at the factory, rolling off the assembly line on October 3, 2024.
- HMG predicts its investment in Georgia will result in 8,500 new jobs at HMGMA by 2031.
- These jobs are predicted to represent $4.6 billion in individual earnings annually, according to the Center for Automotive Research.
- Through the end of 2024, 6,900 jobs and capital investments totaling $2.5 billion have been announced by HMGMA suppliers in 12 of Georgia's 159 counties.
- The supplier job and investment numbers are more than double what HMG originally projected.
- The factory opening follows a Monday announcement that Hyundai will invest $21 billion in the U.S. from 2025 to 2028.
- This $21 billion amount is greater than the $20.5 billion Hyundai has invested in the U.S. to date since entering the market in 1986.
- Of the $21 billion, $9 billion will be spent to increase production capacity to 1.2 million vehicles annually across its three brands.
- Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Alabama and Kia Georgia will receive improved production facilities as part of this $9 billion allocation.
- The company's logistics operation will receive a $6 billion infusion to localize production of automotive components, including battery packs.
- Hyundai Steel will build a mill in Louisiana capable of producing millions of tons of steel annually using steel scrap.
- Key alliances with tech leaders including Boston Dynamics (robotics), NVIDIA (artificial intelligence solutions), Waymo (robotaxis), and Supernal (eVTOL development) will cost $6 billion.
- The final $6 billion of the $21 billion will go toward energy infrastructure investments linked to new business opportunities.
- Hyundai projects that the $21 billion investment will create more than 100,000 direct and indirect job opportunities.
- Stephanie Brinley, associate director of AutoIntelligence at S&P Global, stated that automakers and suppliers are expected to make investment announcements in this environment.
- Brinley noted that the HMG announcements align with previously announced projects and expected direction, except for U.S. steel production.
- She added that as Hyundai increases U.S. vehicle production, having access to its own regional steel gives it an advantage even without tariffs.
- Brinley explained that increased production capacity allows for more flexible sourcing and responsiveness to U.S. demand changes, particularly for electrified vehicles enabled by previous U.S. battery manufacturing investments.
- She concluded that these investments support building where volume increases is less costly and align with Hyundai's targeted growth for the U.S. market.
- An update on 3/26/2025 at 2:25 p.m. ET noted that the total number of units the facility will manufacture annually has been updated.
