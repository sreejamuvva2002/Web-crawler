# Tesla Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2020-06-29

**Source:** [Musk defends Tesla as Texas county considers millions in tax breaks to land 'gigafactory' | Fox Business](https://www.foxbusiness.com/lifestyle/elon-musk-tesla-texas-county-tax-breaks)  ·  `page_020346`

## Facts

- Elon Musk defended Tesla by stating the company 'receives the least subsidies of any automaker.'
- Musk tweeted that federal tax credits apply to other EVs but no longer to Tesla.
- Texans raised concerns at a public hearing regarding a school district's proposal to offer Tesla tens of millions in property tax breaks over the next decade.
- The proposed incentives are intended to attract Tesla to build a potential $1 billion 'gigafactory' in Travis County.
- Austin and Tulsa, Oklahoma are competing for Tesla's new U.S. assembly plant location.
- The new assembly plant is planned to manufacture the Cybertruck and Tesla Model Y.
- Tesla has stated a preference for locating the factory in the center of the country and closer to East Coast markets.
- State and local governments covet auto factories because they create many well-paying jobs that generate income and property taxes.
- The Del Valle Independent School District's proposal was disclosed in a Tesla tax application filed earlier this month with the Texas comptroller’s office.
- Travis County commissioners are considering a separate tax incentive package for Tesla.
- The overall proposal would provide Tesla $68 million in property tax breaks over the next decade to build a new plant off Texas 130 just north of the Colorado River on the southeastern outskirts of Austin.
- Comptroller documents indicate the real value of the proposed property would average about $600 million per year.
- Without the Del Valle district incentives, Tesla would be required to pay almost $8 million in property taxes per year.
- Some residents criticized asking a school district to 'hand over millions' during the coronavirus pandemic as 'simply ridiculous.'
- Other residents argued that the local tax breaks would come 'at the expense of children's education.'
- Supporters countered that the added jobs would stimulate economic development in the area, which is considered underserved and has a large minority population.
- The proposed plant would contain 4 million to 5 million square feet of space, making it Tesla's largest factory to date.
- The company previously stated the factory would create 5,000 'middle-skill' jobs and spur an additional 4,000 jobs in the surrounding area.
- If Austin is selected, the plant would span thousands of acres on land owned and operated by Martin Marietta.
- The existing Martin Marietta plant on the site would be relocated.
- Tesla has not announced a timeline for making its final decision on the location.
