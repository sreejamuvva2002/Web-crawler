# Volvo Group North America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-01-31

**Source:** [Volvo Group North America Recall Failures to Cost $130 Million | CarComplaints.com](https://www.carcomplaints.com/news/2023/volvo-group-north-america-recall-failures-130-million.shtml)  ·  `page_014391`

## Facts

- Volvo Group North America agreed to pay $130 million to the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration for alleged failures regarding vehicle recalls.
- The civil penalty is described by the government as "one of the largest-ever penalties for violations of the Vehicle Safety Act."
- NHTSA's investigation allegedly found that Volvo failed to properly report death and injury incidents.
- Safety regulators allege Volvo failed to recall vehicles in a timely fashion.
- Regulators also allege Volvo failed to properly notify owners about safety recalls.
- An independent third-party auditor will monitor Volvo as the automaker meets regularly with NHTSA about potential vehicle safety problems.
- The auditor's job requires monitoring Volvo for possible Safety Act regulations violations.
- As part of the consent order, employees will receive appropriate training about complying with federal safety regulations.
- The consent order requires the company to make an upfront payment of $65 million.
- Volvo must spend an additional $20 million on a specific performance obligation to create a safety data analytics infrastructure.
- The agreement includes an additional $45 million deferred penalty that may become payable under specified circumstances.
- The consent order is scheduled to have a three-year term unless NHTSA determines possible problems that could extend the order to five years.
