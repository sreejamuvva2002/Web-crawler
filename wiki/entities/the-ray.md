# The Ray

**Type:** Government Program  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[charging-infrastructure|Charging Infrastructure]]  ·  **Location:** West Point, GA, Georgia, United States

## Overview

An 18-mile stretch of rural Georgia highway transformed into a clean-energy living laboratory featuring solar-paved roadways, EV charging stations, tire-pressure monitoring kiosks and a pipeline of emerging technologies.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Hosts a solar-powered electric-vehicle charging station and is exploring wireless EV charging lanes and other EV-focused innovations.

## Key facts

- The Ray aims to be “a zero carbon, zero deaths, zero waste, zero impact highway.”
- Solar-paved roadway panels supply power to the Visitor Information Center.
- A solar-powered charging station can charge most electric vehicles 80% in less than 45 minutes.
- Roll-over tire pressure monitor kiosks provide free tire checks that improve fuel efficiency.
- Planned innovations include wireless EV charging lanes, noise-cancelling solar barriers, solar-powered smart studs, roadside wind turbines, wheat farms producing straw for toilet paper, pollinator gardens, drone road inspections, and repaving with asphalt mixed with recycled tires to reduce noise and increase durability.
- The Ray demonstrates Ray Anderson’s philosophy of “doing well by doing good.”
