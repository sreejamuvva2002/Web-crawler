# Milliken & Company

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2025-04-16

**Source:** [Milliken & Company Unveils Its 2024 Sustainability Report](https://www.globaltextiletimes.com/press-issues/milliken-company-unveils-its-2024-sustainability-report/)  ·  `page_046691`

## Facts

- Milliken & Company unveiled its seventh annual sustainability report on April 16, 2025.
- The report showcases performance against the 2025 Sustainability Goals and 2030 net-zero commitments.
- Halsey Cook, president and CEO of Milliken, stated that sustainability is at the core of Milliken’s business strategy and highlighted progress made with associates, customers, and partners.
- Milliken successfully reduced lost-time incidents in 2024 by prioritizing essential safety measures.
- The company achieved its target of 100,000 volunteer hours a year ahead of schedule, surpassing its initial 2025 volunteer goal by an additional 31,000 hours.
- In 2024, Milliken conducted sustainability assessments on 100% of new products, an increase from a 22% baseline in 2018.
- Milliken became the first textile manufacturer to provide non-PFAS materials for all three layers of firefighter turnout gear.
- The company launched its N/XT Life™ program alongside a partnership with The Recycling Partnership’s Center for Sustainable Behavior and Impact to support circular economy principles.
- Milliken exceeded its 2025 renewable energy target in 2022 and continues seeking solutions to enhance renewable energy usage to minimize emissions.
- Ongoing efforts include thermal drying biosolids from wastewater treatment and removing coal ash from steam generation to reduce waste.
- In 2024, Milliken optimized wastewater treatment processes and targeted various waste streams across its manufacturing sites, decreasing waste intensity compared to its 2018 baseline.
- The company achieved a 42% reduction in scope 1 and 2 emissions from its 2018 baseline in 2024, approaching its 2030 goal of a 50% reduction.
- Since 2018, Milliken has invested over $35 million in coal elimination initiatives, energy efficiency improvements, increased renewable electricity usage, and manufacturing modernization.
- Efforts to reduce scope 3 emissions involve engaging suppliers, enhancing product sustainability data, and sourcing lower-carbon materials.
- Milliken reaffirmed its dedication to the U.N. Global Compact, committing to uphold Ten Principles concerning human rights, labor standards, environmental protection, and anti-corruption.
- Kasel Knight, SVP, Chief Legal Officer, and head of sustainability at Milliken, remarked that the report reflects progress and a commitment to driving positive change.
