# US AV Jobs Coalition

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** workforce_training  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Policy — US AV Jobs Coalition](https://www.usavjobs.com/policy)  ·  `page_017528`

## Facts

- Autonomous vehicles (AVs) are defined as SAE International Levels 4- and 5-capable vehicles.
- AVs present a generational opportunity to create safer roads, promote accessibility, ease supply chain challenges, and create new jobs for American workers.
- A public policy framework is imperative to support a clear pathway to AV deployment and create jobs across the United States.
- Policy measures should take into account supportive actions for investment in training, transition and job creation programs, upskilling, and levers to encourage expansion of investment in AV technology, building, and deployment.
- Continued and expanded deployment across all autonomous vehicle modalities is inherent in solving for transitions and inspiring successful investments in the workforce.
- Market penetration and scaling of autonomous vehicles will happen over time, with any workforce transitions occurring gradually.
- Workforce impacts will not be uniform and will be unique to the type of AV deployed, geographic area, cargo or passenger type, and the operator’s commercial relationships with public and private sector partners.
- Enabling rules that address operational and safety issues should be developed on a parallel track with workforce programs or initiatives.
- Efforts to address workforce opportunities should be subject to open and transparent processes balancing government, worker, and industry representatives.
- States are recommended to establish a workforce innovation committee composed of a balanced group of government, worker, and industry representatives appointed by the state governor.
- This committee would serve as a partnership between government, workers, and industry to prepare workforce recommendations as the AV industry advances.
- The committee can be used to identify and accomplish workforce-related policy goals, including providing a venue for industry to share information with government and labor partners about existing and upcoming workforce needs.
- The committee can support workforce transition programs, including community and technical college programs, transition assistance, best practices, and research.
- The committee can advise on funding strategies for workforce programs.
- States should conduct and/or fund research projects in coordination with industry to analyze state-specific workforce needs and opportunities.
- Research may evaluate how AV jobs will be created due to factors such as geographic location, workforce strengths, access to ports and interstates, community college training programs, existing truck routes, and other special attributes of a state.
- States should identify ways to leverage these needs and opportunities to create jobs throughout the AV ecosystem.
- States should create new programs, curriculum, and/or enhance existing workforce programs across the state.
- States should leverage existing programs and funding to optimize workforce opportunities in the AV industry based on research, deployment timelines, and best practices.
- States should establish grant programs for community colleges, universities, and other educational institutions to implement autonomous vehicle-related training programs.
- These grant programs aim to ensure workers are taking advantage of high-quality jobs of the future.
