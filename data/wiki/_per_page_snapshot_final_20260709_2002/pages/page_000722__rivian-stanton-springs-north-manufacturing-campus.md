# Rivian Stanton Springs North Manufacturing Campus

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Rivian Stanton Springs North EV Plant — Industrial in Social Circle - Exchange](https://exchange.construction/projects/industrial/rivian-stanton-springs-georgia-ev-plant-2/)  ·  `page_000722`

## Facts

- Rivian is building a $5 billion manufacturing campus at Stanton Springs North, located east of Atlanta.
- The campus spans roughly 9 million square feet across 1,800 acres of greenfield land.
- Construction mobilization is scheduled to continue through 2026.
- Vehicle production at the facility is targeted for 2028.
- Rivian previously paused and then restarted the project to sharpen its capacity plan.
- Rivian selected Clayco as the general contractor to build the plant.
- Skidmore, Owings & Merrill (SOM) is serving as the design architect for the facility.
- Jacobs is acting as the engineer of record.
- The facility will be delivered in two phases using a design-build delivery method.
- The plant is designed with a production capacity aiming to top 400,000 vehicles per year.
- To capture its full state incentive package, Rivian must invest $5 billion and employ 7,500 workers at an average salary of $56,000 by the end of 2028.
- Site preparation across the 1,800-acre site is a major earthwork and logistics component that sets the schedule before the first structure goes vertical.
- The project is considered one of the largest economic-development projects in Georgia’s history.
- The plant's stop-start path makes it a case study in how fragile even mega-incentivized manufacturing can be.
- Rivian’s restart signals renewed confidence in U.S. EV demand at a time when the broader market has wobbled.
- For the region, the plant is reshaping the local labor market by pulling thousands of skilled trades into the corridor between Atlanta and Athens.
- The project reflects a wider reshoring wave concentrating the biggest U.S. construction jobs in advanced manufacturing, where only a handful of contractors can build at this scale.
- Rivian serves as both the developer and owner/client for the project.
- The current status of the facility is under construction.
- Funding for the project comes from private sources.
