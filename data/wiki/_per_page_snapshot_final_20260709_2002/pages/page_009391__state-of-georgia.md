# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2023-07-01

**Source:** [Georgia Job Creation & Momentum Remains Strong - Columbus CEO](https://thecolumbusceo.com/features/2024/08/georgia-job-creation-momentum-remains-strong/)  ·  `page_009391`

## Facts

- Between July 1, 2023, and June 30, 2024, GDEcD’s Global Commerce team supported 429 facility expansions and new locations resulting in more than $20.3 billion in investment and the commitment of 26,900 new, private sector jobs statewide.
- 83% of expansions and new locations are on their way to communities outside the 10-county Atlanta region, representing more than 18,300 new jobs and $12.5 billion in investment.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that the state is committed to continuing momentum by investing in key areas like education, workforce development and infrastructure.
- Lt. Governor Burt Jones emphasized ensuring businesses have the necessary workforce, noting that the Technical College System of Georgia, University System of Georgia, College and Career Academies and private-public partnerships are building Georgia's workforce pipeline with their workforce training programs.
- Speaker of the House Jon Burns highlighted historic investments in the education system, workforce development and infrastructure as part of sustained economic development success.
- During FY2024, Governor and First Lady Kemp led economic development missions to Korea and Switzerland that focused on strengthening existing relationships with key investors like Hyundai Motor Group and its supplier network.
- The prior year, Governor and First Lady Kemp led similar missions to Israel, France, and Switzerland.
- Georgia maintained its position as a global gateway for business, landing more than 8,100 new jobs and $5.9 billion in investments from international companies expanding or locating in the U.S. market this past fiscal year.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson noted that Georgia's strong partnerships and diverse industry landscape are major advantages for businesses and people.
- The State of Georgia celebrated more than 50 years of continuous representation in Europe and Japan in FY2024, which remain among the top sources of foreign direct investment.
- The coming fiscal year will mark 40 years of continuous representation in Korea, a partnership that earned the Department of Economic Development the James A. Van Fleet Award from the Korea Society.
- All markets where Georgia maintains representation in business and trade accounted for 29% of total new jobs and investments in FY2024.
- GDEcD Commissioner Kristi Brigman stated that the state is exploring new heights in mobility and technology, celebrating project milestones across the state during Fiscal Year 2024.
- Manufacturing, including the aerospace, automotive, and e-mobility sectors, accounted for over 50% of expansions or new locations in FY2024.
- Kia Georgia added the EV9 to its lineup in Troup County.
- Toyota Industries Corporation established a campus in Jackson County.
- StandardAero operates in Richmond County.
- International life sciences company Gerresheimer completed a recent expansion in Fayette County.
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America suppliers announced new locations in Bryan, Candler, Chatham, Coweta, Effingham, Harris, and Laurens Counties.
- SOLARCYCLE, an innovator in solar panel recycling, announced plans for a new location in Polk County to support solar panel manufacturer Qcells’ operations.
- Investments in food processing and agribusiness totaled more than $1.3 billion in FY2024.
- Japanese probiotic beverage company Yakult located in Bartow County, and Korean bakery CJ Foodville located in Hall County to support East Coast operations.
- Walmart will be sourcing milk from local dairy farmers at a future dairy processing facility in Lowndes County.
- Technology and corporate hubs are creating at least 4,400 new jobs as a result of wins in FY2024, including Cargill Corporation establishing a new office hub in the City of Atlanta.
- U.S. Soccer’s future headquarters and national training center in Fayette County reinforces Georgia’s position within entertainment, sports, and tourism.
- Georgia earned the rank of No. 1 state for business from Area Development magazine for 10 consecutive years.
- The state earned the No. 1 spot in Site Selection magazine’s annual Prosperity Cup rankings for successful investment recruitment in 2023.
- Georgia was awarded a Gold Shovel by Area Development for achieving significant success in attracting new business investment and job creation in 2023.
- Business Facilities magazine ranked Georgia as the No. 1 state for EV investments as well as a top 10 state for food processing, cybersecurity, aerospace and defense, and AI growth hubs.
