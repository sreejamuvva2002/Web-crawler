# Brian Marlowe

**Type:** person  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2020-10-21

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp announces leader for rural Georgia economic development effort | FOX 5 Atlanta](https://www.fox5atlanta.com/news/gov-kemp-announces-leader-for-rural-georgia-economic-development-effort)  ·  `page_020304`

## Facts

- Gov. Brian Kemp and Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson announced that Tift County’s Brian Marlowe will be the new deputy commissioner for rural Georgia.
- Marlowe has been the president and CEO of the Tift County Development Authority.
- Marlowe will lead the Governor’s Rural Strike Team, an initiative the governor announced last year to improve the economy in rural parts of the state.
- The initiative aims to market large rural industrial sites to new businesses.
- The effort involves creating specific marketing plans for a site and targeting specific industries.
- The program includes training local leaders in business recruitment.
- State rankings show Georgia's least developed areas, based on poverty rates, unemployment rates, and income, are overwhelmingly rural.
- The exception to the rural development ranking is Clarke, Clayton, and Dougherty counties.
- Almost all of those less-developed counties are located south of Interstate 20.
- Gov. Kemp stated he believes they can land big projects in rural communities across the state.
- Kemp expressed confidence that Marlowe will help continue to move the needle in rural Georgia.
- Kemp was elected in 2018 with overwhelming support from rural, white voters.
- The announcement coincided with Republican efforts to maintain their majority in the state House, including targeting a handful of rural districts.
- Marlowe has led business recruiting efforts in Tift County for 10 years.
- Before his role in Tift County, Marlowe worked in Grady County.
- Marlowe was a member of Kemp’s transition team.
- Kemp cited Marlowe's advocacy for improved internet service, health care, and worker training.
- Marlowe will be Wilson’s seventh deputy commissioner.
- Wilson cited Marlowe's success in Tifton, including Coca Cola Bottling Co. United's decision to locate a $60 million, 200-job distribution facility there.
- Wilson stated they needed someone with a passion for growing rural communities and providing hope and opportunity.
- Marlowe is scheduled to start his new role on Dec. 1.
- Wilson was unsure whether Marlowe’s office would be located in Atlanta or somewhere else.
