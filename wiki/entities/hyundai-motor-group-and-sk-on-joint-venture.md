# Hyundai Motor Group & SK On Joint Venture

**Type:** Joint Venture  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[battery-cell-manufacturing|Battery Cell Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** Bartow County, Georgia, Bartow County, Georgia, United States

## Overview

A 50/50 joint venture between Hyundai Motor Group and SK On to build an electric-vehicle battery cell plant in Bartow County, Georgia, with a total investment of about USD 5 billion.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Establishes domestic EV battery cell production in Georgia to supply Hyundai, Kia and Genesis electric-vehicle manufacturing in the United States.

- **EV Battery Cell Plant (Joint Venture)** — Expected to start manufacturing battery cells in the second half of 2025; capacity sufficient for 300,000 EVs per year.

## Investment

- **$5 billion (approx.)** (April 25, 2023) — Establish EV battery cell production joint venture
  - Each partner holds a 50 percent stake in the JV.

## Timeline

- **November 2022** — Hyundai Motor Group signed a memorandum of understanding with SK On to secure EV battery supply for North America.
- **April 25, 2023** — Press release announcing the formation of a 50/50 joint venture between Hyundai Motor Group and SK On to build an EV battery cell plant in Bartow County, Georgia.
- **Second half of 2025** — Planned start of battery cell manufacturing at the Bartow County facility.

## Key facts

- The joint venture will have an annual production capacity of 35 GWh, enough to support roughly 300,000 electric vehicles.
- Hyundai Mobis will assemble battery packs from the plant’s cells and supply them to Hyundai, Kia and Genesis EV models in the United States.
- Total investment is approximately USD 5 billion, with each partner holding a 50 percent stake.
- The project accelerates Hyundai Motor Group’s electrification strategy and strengthens its position as an EV leader in the U.S. market.
- SK On has been a strategic battery partner for Hyundai, collaborating on models such as the Hyundai IONIQ 5, Kia EV6 and Genesis GV60.
