# Rivian New Georgia Manufacturing Facility

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Rivian Automotive receives $6.6bn federal backing for new Georgia EV plant](https://business-news-today.com/rivian-automotive-receives-6-6bn-federal-backing-for-new-georgia-ev-plant/)  ·  `page_059093`

## Facts

- Rivian Automotive has announced a conditional commitment for up to $6.6 billion in funding from the U.S. Department of Energy’s (DOE) Advanced Technology Vehicle Manufacturing (ATVM) Loan Program.
- The loan package includes $6 billion in principal and approximately $600 million in capitalized interest.
- The funding is contingent upon Rivian meeting specific technical, legal, and environmental benchmarks set by the DOE.
- If finalized, the funds will support the construction of a new manufacturing facility at Stanton Springs North, near Social Circle, Georgia.
- The upcoming plant will serve as the cornerstone for producing the R2 and R3 vehicle lines, which are a midsize SUV and crossover, respectively.
- The facility is designed to achieve a total production capacity of 400,000 units annually, split across two phases with each phase contributing 200,000 units.
- Phase 1 production at the new Georgia facility is expected to commence in 2028.
- The project is forecast to create around 7,500 permanent jobs by 2030.
- The project will also create approximately 2,000 temporary construction roles.
- Rivian CEO RJ Scaringe stated that the DOE funding would allow the company to scale production of its affordable and high-performing midsize vehicles while reinforcing U.S. leadership in EV innovation.
- The Stanton Springs site is located less than an hour from Atlanta and will be designed with advanced environmental management practices.
- Rivian plans to invest in the surrounding communities to ensure the project’s long-term sustainability and positive local impact.
- Past beneficiaries of the DOE’s ATVM program include General Motors and Tesla.
- Before the loan is finalized, Rivian and the DOE must resolve all outstanding technical, legal, and financial conditions.
- Once approved, the loan will be secured against the project’s assets, as well as the fixed assets and guarantees of Rivian Automotive and its subsidiaries.
- Rivian’s existing manufacturing plant is located in Normal, Illinois.
