# Woodbridge Foam Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Woodbridge Foam Corporation - MarkLines Automotive Industry Portal](https://www.marklines.com/en/top500/woodbridge-foam?sitesearchKey=2023)  ·  `page_015262`

## Facts

- The Company provides material technologies for applications in a wide range of markets including automotive, commercial, recreational, packaging, healthcare and building products.
- It is a global leader in the development and production of automotive polyurethane products.
- For the automotive industry, the Company manufactures molded foam, expanded foam, chemicals, components, and engineered composites for applications including seating, structural support, occupant protection, headliner systems, exterior bumper assemblies, and NVH management.
- The Company is privately owned.
- Products include seatback panel assemblies, seat back bolsters, rear seat stacked interior components, flexible foam, climate-controlled seating foam, flexible foam lamination, low-emission flexible foam, and topper pads.
- Products include molded foam, adaptive comfort and support foams, trimvisible foam products enabling visual transition between exposed seating components, textured surface foams, high-performance foams, and multi-zone foams.
- Products include head restraints, head restraint assembly, energy management polyurethane foam, foam-in-place head restraint assembly, integral skin head restraint, foam-on-rod assemblies, high-resiliency foams, dual hardness foams, comfort safe head restraints, and head restraint mechanisms.
- Products include an encapsulated StructureLite Module combining seating support with urethane seating pad in a single module, integral skin seat armrests, foam-in-place armrest assemblies, structurelite structural seat foam, and trimvisible BIO reduced GWP foam.
- Interior products include cab back panel insulators, interior soft trim spacer foams, thin profile interior foam door armrests, thin profile interior foam console armrests, integral skin door armrests, integral skin console armrests, load floors, rear seat bolster foams, rear seat bolster assemblies, coverstock headliner foam, headliner composites, thin high-strength headliner composites, cargo management solutions, package trays, energy management foam, acoustical foam, and technical foams for filters or NVH applications.
- Exterior products include structural exterior foam bumper assemblies, underhood insulators, underbody shields, tailgate liners, compressor shields, acoustical heat shields, and wheel liners.
- The company began its business in 1978 as a privately owned Canadian supplier created by the purchase of the polyurethane foam division of Monsanto Corporation in Woodbridge, Ontario, Canada.
- The company experienced growth primarily through acquisitions in the early 1980s.
- In the late 1980s, the company expanded further by building new facilities strategically located near its customers.
- In March 1999, Group and Poliuretanos S.W. de C.V. announced the expansion of their Joint Venture with the addition of a third manufacturing facility in Mexico.
- The company opened a manufacturing facility in Sarnia, Ontario, Canada in April 1999.
- In September 1999, the company signed contracts with the Belgian Group Recticel for a 51% stake in a manufacturing joint venture, while retaining a 49% stake.
- In February 2000, the company acquired the Morval division of PFB Corporation of Calgary Alberta for CAD 17 million.
- In March 2001, Woodbridge and its European partner Recticel together acquired 50% of Gestind.
- In May 2001, the company signed a letter of intent to enter into a Joint Venture with Technicon Industries Inc. of North Carolina.
- The company established a joint venture in China with Chinese company GSK in September 2005.
- In May 2007, Johnson Controls joined with Proseat (a joint venture between Recticel and Woodbridge) to set up a new manufacturing joint venture for the production of molded foam seating cushions in Slovakia and Poland.
- In November 2007, the Group and Inoac Corp. announced the establishment of four joint venture companies in North America.
- In July 2008, Proseat signed an agreement for the takeover of all shares of Indepol, a Spanish moulded foam manufacturer.
- In July 2011, the company and Wuxi Jixing Auto Parts Co., Ltd. agreed to form a joint venture named Wuxi Woodbridge Jixing Advanced Polymer Technology Co, Ltd. in China.
- In August 2011, the company and Summa Group announced the expansion of their partnership with the establishment of a joint venture named DuraLite Formed Plastics in Leon, Mexico.
- In January 2018, the company and Inoac set up a new equal joint venture named Woodbridge INOAC Technical Products.
- In December 2018, the company announced it entered into an agreement to sell its stake in Proseat, its European joint venture, to partner Recticel S.A.
- In November 2021, the company completed the acquisition of Hematite, a company specializing in NVH technology development.
- In December 2023, the company formed a joint venture with TM Automotive Seating Systems called TMWB Foam Private Limited in India to produce seating systems for commercial vehicles.
- In November 2024, the company formed a joint venture with Chengpeng called Woodbridge (Changzhou) Automotive Components Co., Ltd. in China for commercial vehicle and passenger seat foam.
