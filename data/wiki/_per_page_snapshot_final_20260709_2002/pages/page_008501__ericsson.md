# Ericsson

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2023-08-29

**Source:** [Ericsson works with AWS and Hitachi America R&D to showcase smart factory potential](https://www.prnewswire.co.uk/news-releases/ericsson-works-with-aws-and-hitachi-america-rd-to-showcase-smart-factory-potential-301912437.html)  ·  `page_008501`

## Facts

- Ericsson, Amazon Web Services (AWS), and Hitachi America R&D enabled a private 5G infrastructure trial at Hitachi Astemo Americas' electric motor vehicle manufacturing plant in Berea, Kentucky, USA.
- The collaboration focuses on real-time digital video, artificial intelligence (AI), and edge-to-cloud technologies across a private 5G wireless network to transform manufacturing and improve productivity, efficiency, environmental impact, safety, and cost reduction.
- The solution leverages Ericsson Private 5G side by side with the AWS Snow Family to provide foundational private cellular networks for establishing machine learning (ML) models within the Hitachi manufacturing complex.
- Real-time video of a component assembly operation producing motor components for electric vehicles was fed across the Ericsson private 5G network to an AWS Snowball Edge device running Hitachi video analytics to detect defects earlier and reduce wasted material and lost production.
- The trial installation using 5G wireless was completed in three days by setting up cameras on a live production assembly line.
- Computer vision running on the private 5G network simultaneously inspected 24 assembly components, compared with one-by-one inspection using conventional approaches.
- High-definition 4K cameras enabled the computer vision configuration to observe defects at the sub-millimeter level, providing far greater visibility than the human eye.
- The high throughput and low latency of 5G was key to uploading huge volumes of video data from the cameras to an AWS Snow Family device for analysis to streamline decision-making.
- Ericsson Private 5G is built on Ericsson's 4G and 5G radio and dual mode core technology, providing secure and reliable connectivity through a single server dual mode core pre-integrated for rapid time to service.
- The combination of private 5G, cloud, and artificial intelligence/machine learning automated technologies has the potential to revolutionize manufacturing processes.
- The trial demonstrated that the combination of 5G and cloud technologies is now making full-scale, global deployment of digital production line applications viable, including defect detection, quality inspection, robotics automation, real-time machine control, and augmented reality.
- Ericsson hosted a webinar on Thursday, August 24, 2023, exploring the full deployment and highlighting how private 5G serves as a reliable, flexible, high-performing network for manufacturing use cases involving AI and machine learning automation models.
- Hitachi Astemo Americas manufactures and markets engine management, electric powertrain, and integrated vehicle controls for major automotive manufacturers worldwide, adhering to ISO/TS:16949:2009 industry standards.
- Thomas Noren, Head of PCN Commercial and Operations at Ericsson, stated that the collaboration demonstrates solutions deployable today in manufacturing and enterprise environments to deliver early adopter competitive advantages.
- Sudhanshu Gaur, Vice President of R&D for Hitachi America and Chief Architect at Hitachi Astemo Americas, noted that the team explored and validated new use cases enabled by private 5G to show how smart factories can already function.
- Chris McKenna, Global Lead for Private Wireless at AWS, explained that using the Ericsson Private 5G Network product and running AI/ML models on an AWS Snow Family device demonstrated a reliable and secure connection to run machine vision inferences on-site to detect defects earlier.
