# DAS Corp.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-09-06

**Source:** [DAS Corp. to Bring 300 Automotive Jobs to Metter](https://ourcommunitymedia.org/us-news/das-corp-to-bring-300-automotive-jobs-to-metter/)  ·  `page_019119`

## Facts

- Automotive parts company DAS Corp. will establish a new manufacturing facility in Metter, Georgia.
- The new facility will create 300 jobs.
- The facility will generate more than $35 million in investment.
- DAS specializes in designing and manufacturing seating systems, safety seating components, and seating structures.
- DAS is a key supplier for Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia (KMMG) and Hyundai Motor Group.
- The facility will primarily produce automotive seat structures.
- Operations are projected to begin in the latter half of 2024.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced the DAS Corp. facility establishment on September 6, 2023.
- The initiative will involve collaborative indirect investments.
- Suppliers for the Hyundai Metaplant resulted in over $2 billion in investment last fiscal year alone.
- This supplier investment helped Georgia achieve a third straight year of record-breaking economic growth.
- DAS was established in Korea in 1987.
- Sean Kim serves as the Chief Operating Officer at DAS.
- The strategic location near prominent cities like Savannah and Macon facilitated convenient access and played a pivotal role in DAS's decision-making process.
- Metter was deemed an ideal location in anticipation of its role as a global electric vehicle production hub due to its close proximity to Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- The new manufacturing facility is located in the Candler County Industrial Park in Metter.
- Over the course of the next five years, DAS will be recruiting for assembly technicians.
- DAS intends to establish collaborations with local employment services as part of its hiring process.
- Ed Boyd serves as the Mayor of Metter.
- Glyn Thrift serves as the Chairman of the Candler County Board of Commissioners.
- Griff Lynch serves as the President and CEO of the Georgia Ports Authority.
- Alyce Thornhill is the Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy at the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team.
- GDEcD partnered with the Candler County Industrial Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, Electric Cities of Georgia, and Georgia Quick Start on this competitive project.
- Pat Wilson serves as the Commissioner of GDEcD.
- Georgia has pursued the entire EV supply chain as the market transitions to electric vehicles.
- Georgia has created more than $25 billion in investments and 30,000 jobs since 2018 for the automotive and mobility industry.
- In fiscal year 2023, job creation in the automotive industry increased by 324 percent compared to FY21.
- Suppliers attracted by Georgia’s mobility original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) resulted in over $2 billion in investment across the state in FY23.
- DAS garnered a total revenue of approximately $900 million USD during the fiscal year of 2022.
