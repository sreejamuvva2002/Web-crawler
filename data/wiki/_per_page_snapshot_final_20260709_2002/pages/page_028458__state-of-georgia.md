# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-10-22

**Source:** [From Global to Local: International Businesses Grow Globally in Georgi | Georgia](https://georgia.org/blog/global-local-international-businesses-grow-globally-georgia)  ·  `page_028458`

## Facts

- The SelectUSA Investment Summit in 2024 featured more than 5,000 participants representing 96 global markets.
- Georgia has been ranked the No. 1 state for business for over a decade.
- In fiscal year 2024, Georgia secured more than 8,100 new jobs and $5.9 billion in investments from international companies expanding or locating in the U.S. market.
- Georgia maintains business development and trade representation in more than 12 global markets, which accounted for 29% of total new jobs and investments in fiscal year 2024.
- Georgia has built a robust international presence over the past 50 years.
- Georgia ranked as the sixth-largest state in total trade in 2024 with a value of $198.7 billion, reflecting a 6.4% year-over-year increase.
- Over the past decade, companies from more than 40 countries have invested in Georgia, and over 70 international governments maintain a presence there.
- Approximately 10% of Georgia’s population is foreign-born.
- Gwinnett County ranks among the Top 10 most diverse counties in the U.S.
- Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport, the Port of Savannah, and the Port of Brunswick provide critical logistical access to global markets for businesses in Georgia.
- Georgia maintains a low corporate tax rate of 5.19%.
- Georgia’s economic landscape includes e-mobility alongside aerospace, food processing, and agribusiness.
- Georgia offers a blend of established industries, a skilled workforce with top training programs, and strategic investments in site development and infrastructure.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development supports existing industries, empowers vibrant arts and cultural communities, and encourages tourism to Georgia’s diverse destinations.
- In 2023, Georgia’s international trade value surpassed $186.3 billion across 223 countries and territories, ranking the state seventh in the U.S.
