# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2024-02-14

**Source:** [Georgia Breaks Export Records for Third Straight Year - AllOnGeorgia](https://allongeorgia.com/georgia-business/georgia-breaks-export-records-for-third-straight-year/)  ·  `page_016718`

## Facts

- In 2023, Georgia surpassed $49.7 billion in exports, eclipsing the record-setting $47 billion set in 2022.
- The state currently ranks seventh in the U.S. for dollar value of trade and twelfth in the country for dollar value of exports.
- Georgia facilitated more than $186 billion in total trade to 222 unique countries and territories.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp joined the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) to announce the export record-breaking milestone.
- An estimated 87 percent of Georgia's exports come from small businesses.
- International trade touches every county in the state, supporting jobs in logistics, manufacturing, agriculture, and other sectors.
- Top exports for 2023 included civilian aircraft products, motor vehicles, turbojets and turbines, poultry, and chemical woodpulp products.
- Canada, Mexico, China, Germany, and Singapore were the top destinations for Georgia exports.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that international representatives in overseas markets help find homes for Georgia-made goods and play a significant role in supporting small businesses.
- The state's top five bilateral trading partners in 2023 were Mexico, South Korea, Canada, China, and Germany, with notable year-over-year growth in total trade between the State of Georgia and South Korea.
- Markets where Georgia maintains full-time international trade representation accounted for 67 percent of exports and 71 percent of bilateral trade in 2023.
- GDEcD Deputy Commissioner of International Trade Lizann Grupalo noted that an estimated one in nine jobs in Georgia is tied to the logistics industry.
- Georgia is home to Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport, described as the busiest and most efficient airport in the world.
- The Port of Savannah is the fastest growing and third-busiest container gateway in the U.S.
- The Port of Brunswick is one of the nation's busiest gateways for Roll-on/Roll-off cargo.
- Georgia offers 5,000 miles of rail and 20,000 miles of federal and state highways, providing robust rail and highway infrastructure.
- Support from Georgia’s International Trade office is a complimentary benefit available to all Georgia-based businesses.
