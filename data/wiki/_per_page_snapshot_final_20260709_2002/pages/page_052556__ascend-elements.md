# Ascend Elements

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2025-05-07

**Source:** [Ascend Elements to Construct Major Lithium Battery Facility - Investors Hangout](https://investorshangout.com/ascend-elements-to-construct-major-lithium-battery-facility-279892-/)  ·  `page_052556`

## Facts

- The Ministry of Economic Development and Technology announced a substantial cash subsidy for Ascend Elements valued at up to 1.22 billion PLN.
- The grant is equivalent to approximately USD $320 million.
- This represents one of the largest financial supports ever provided by the Polish government.
- Ascend Elements is set to construct a new manufacturing facility dedicated to sustainable precursor cathode active material (pCAM) production.
- The advanced pCAM material is critical for the creation of lithium-ion batteries utilized in electric vehicles and various industrial applications.
- The initiative aligns with the European Union's Temporary Crisis and Transition Framework, supporting Europe's shift towards a net-zero economy.
- CEO Linh Austin referred to the grant as a significant step in their European growth strategy.
- He emphasized the company's commitment to establishing a sustainable supply chain for battery materials on both sides of the Atlantic.
- The project underscores how essential battery sector initiatives are to PAIH's investment portfolio.
- Pawel Pudlowski, Vice President of PAIH, remarked that this investment would enhance Poland's footprint in the battery supply chain, fostering regional development and promoting collaboration with local educational institutions.
- The facility is indicative of Ascend Elements' commitment to technological advancement and environmental responsibility.
- At the new facility, Ascend Elements plans to utilize its proprietary Hydro-to-Cathode® technology to produce sustainable nickel, manganese, and cobalt (NMC) pCAM from recycled lithium-ion batteries.
- This method demonstrates significant efficiency improvements over traditional recycling techniques, contributing to a reduced environmental footprint.
- The innovative process has yielded impressive results in life cycle assessments, showing a dramatic reduction in carbon emissions.
- By 2030, Ascend Elements aims for an 86% reduction in CO2 emissions and a remarkable 94% decrease in particulate matter emissions through further decarbonization strategies.
- In 2024, Ascend Elements formed a joint venture with Elemental Strategic Metals, launching the AE Elemental JV battery recycling facility in Zawiercie, Poland.
- The new pCAM manufacturing plant will be entirely owned and operated by Ascend Elements.
- The company already operates one of North America's largest battery recycling facilities in Covington, Georgia, leveraging this knowledge to enhance production capabilities.
- Ascend Elements is also constructing a Hydro-to-Cathode® pCAM facility in Hopkinsville, Kentucky.
- The Kentucky facility is expected to manufacture materials for up to 750,000 electric vehicle batteries per year.
- This Kentucky facility will be recognized as North America's largest NMC pCAM manufacturer.
