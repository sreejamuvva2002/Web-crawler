# Eaton Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** power_electronics  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Eaton invests $50M+ in new Virginia facility to advance grid-to-chip AI data center solutions](https://www.eaton.com/us/en-us/company/news-insights/news-releases/2025/eaton-invests-fifty-million-dollar-in-new-virginia-facility.html)  ·  `page_019803`

## Facts

- Eaton announced it will open a new manufacturing campus in Henrico County, Virginia, for critical power distribution technologies to help meet record demand from data center customers.
- More than 50 new data centers were already permitted in Virginia this year.
- The company will more than double its footprint in Richmond.
- Eaton will increase capacity and add capabilities for manufacturing static transfer switches, power distribution units, and remote power panels.
- Production at Eaton’s new 350,000-square-foot Richmond area facility is expected to begin in 2027.
- Aidan Graham serves as senior vice president and general manager of Critical Power Solutions at Eaton.
- Eaton’s fully integrated grid-to-chip portfolio helps meet the accelerating needs of data center customers with modular and scalable systems that can be deployed globally.
- The investment in Virginia will increase regional manufacturing for higher power gray space infrastructure for North American customers.
- Eaton investments in North American manufacturing for its electrical solutions have reached more than $1.2 billion since 2023.
- McKinsey analysis anticipates that companies will invest close to $7 trillion in capital expenditures on data center infrastructure globally by 2030, with more than 40% of spending in the U.S.
- Governor Glenn Youngkin stated that Eaton’s latest investment in Henrico is proof that Virginia companies and workers are stepping up to meet growing power needs.
- Edward S. Whitlock, III serves as Chair of the Henrico EDA Board of Directors.
- Eaton will expand and consolidate its local manufacturing in the Richmond area at this new campus from three nearby facilities located within three miles of the new site.
- Existing Eaton employees in the area will transition to the new location.
- The Eaton investment is expected to create 200 additional jobs, with hiring beginning in 2026.
- The project is supported by state and local economic development incentives.
- Eaton makes products for the data center, utility, industrial, commercial, machine building, residential, aerospace, and mobility markets.
- Eaton was founded in 1911.
- Eaton had revenues of nearly $25 billion in 2024.
- Eaton serves customers in more than 160 countries.
