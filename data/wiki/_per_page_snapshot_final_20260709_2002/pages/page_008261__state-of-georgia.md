# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2021-07-01

**Source:** [Georgia achieves economic record for 2022](https://valdostatoday.com/news-2/region/2022/08/georgia-achieves-economic-record-for-2022/)  ·  `page_008261`

## Facts

- Georgia achieved a record fiscal year for 2022 in economic development investments and job creation.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp and GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson announced that Georgia achieved another record year for economic development investments and job creation during fiscal year 2022.
- Investments and job creation were 94 percent and 53 percent above prior economic development records, respectively.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated his administration prioritized bringing jobs and investment to every corner of the state and highlighted Georgia’s business-friendly environment, superior logistics network, top-ranked workforce training program, and pursuit of being the best state for Americans to live, work, and raise a family.
- Investments in expansions and new locations totaled more than $21.2 billion.
- 51,132 jobs were created in the state through 358 projects supported by GDEcD’s Global Commerce team between July 1, 2021, and June 30, 2022.
- Excluding the two largest projects in state history – Rivian and Hyundai Motor Group – Georgia jobs and investments for the year totaled 35,532 jobs and $10.7 billion, respectively.
- Rivian and Hyundai Motor Group were two of 12 automotive projects that created a total of nearly 16,000 new jobs across the state.
- Eighty-five percent of the investments and more than 30,000 new jobs came from outside the 10-county metro Atlanta region.
- Companies choosing to expand in Georgia accounted for 74 percent of total projects.
- New locations created 33,846 jobs across the state.
- Advanced manufacturing added more than 10,000 new jobs in the state.
- Both advanced manufacturing and automotive industries include manufacturers and suppliers for the electric vehicle (EV) industry.
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America’s (HMGMA) development also includes EV battery manufacturing along with affiliate suppliers on-site.
- Component recyclers Aurubis and Ascend Elements have joined HMGMA as part of Georgia’s efforts to build a sustainable, reliable supply chain for its electric mobility ecosystem.
- Lt. Governor Geoff Duncan expressed gratitude for prioritizing high-quality jobs and strengthening Georgia’s workforce to cement its reputation as the best state for business and achieve the goal of being the technology capital of the East Coast.
- Speaker David Ralston attributed Georgia’s success to strong public-private partnerships, lowering the tax burden on businesses, and incentivizing workforce development and investment.
- The logistics and distribution industry remains an economic engine for Georgia due to access to Hartsfield-Jackson International Airport, the Port of Savannah, and a robust network of highways and rail lines.
- Foreign direct investment (FDI) accounted for over $8 billion in investments created in fiscal year 2022.
- The top five foreign direct investment sources were South Korea, Germany, Japan, France, and the Netherlands.
- Korea-based projects represented the highest job creation and investment from a single country, with Germany producing the second most jobs and investments after Korea.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson led economic development missions to the United Kingdom, South Korea, and Germany in FY22.
- Meetings were held with economic development drivers like Hyundai Motor Group and SK On to discuss new opportunities for development.
- Discussions occurred regarding new opportunities for development with the German state of North Rhine-Westphalia and the Liverpool City Region Freeport.
- GDEcD offers “Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development” (GRAD) certification, which recognizes pre-qualified industrial sites for which due diligence has been completed prior to a prospect visit.
- Georgia has certified more than 60 industrial sites as ready for fast-track industrial projects through partnerships with property owners, development authorities, or economic development offices.
- Georgia’s Quick Start program provides specialized workforce training tailored specifically to business needs and has operated for 55 years.
- Since Governor Brian P. Kemp took office, more than 119,963 new jobs and over $42.9 billion in investment have been announced in communities across the state.
- Seventy-seven percent of state economic development projects, 81 percent of investments, and 57 percent of jobs since Kemp took office have gone to communities outside the 10-county metro Atlanta area.
