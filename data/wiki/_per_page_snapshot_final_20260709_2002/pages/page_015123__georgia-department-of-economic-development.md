# Georgia Department of Economic Development

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-03-31

**Source:** [2024 Economic Yearbook - Georgia Trend Magazine](https://www.georgiatrend.com/2024/03/31/2024-economic-yearbook/)  ·  `page_015123`

## Facts

- Fiscal Year 2024, which began last July, is proving to be yet another period of strong growth and development, especially in the e-mobility, clean tech and energy sectors.
- A reliable energy grid, maintained by the state’s utility companies, keeps Georgia competitive for energy-intensive sectors such as manufacturing and clean energy, according to Kristi Brigman, deputy commissioner of Global Commerce at the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD).
- The state’s investment in infrastructure and site preparation throughout its 159 counties encourages new opportunities for jobseekers and businesses alike.
- GDEcD economic development figures through December 2023 show $24 billion+ invested in facility expansions and new locations.
- GDEcD economic development figures through December 2023 report 38,400 new jobs created.
- GDEcD economic development figures through December 2023 report 426 projects announced.
- These investment and job creation figures span all industries, including aerospace, agribusiness, automotive, logistics, fintech, and film production.
- While Metro Atlanta continues to play a critical role in the state’s economy, much of the growth has occurred outside the 10-county region.
- More than $20 billion in investments have been made outside the 10-county metro Atlanta region.
- 82% of new jobs created have occurred outside the 10-county metro Atlanta region.
- Georgia has been named the No. 1 state for doing business for a record 10 consecutive years.
