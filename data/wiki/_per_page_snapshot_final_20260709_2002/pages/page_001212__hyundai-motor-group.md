# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-08-02

**Source:** [Hyundai to focus hiring on Savannah area. The local labor market is bracing for impact. - AOL](https://www.aol.com/lifestyle/hyundai-focus-hiring-savannah-area-090053276.html)  ·  `page_001212`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group is hiring locally for its automobile assembly facility and neighboring electric vehicle battery plant in the Savannah area.
- The automobile assembly facility and neighboring electric vehicle battery plant will directly employ 8,100 workers.
- Suppliers have already committed to hiring nearly 5,000 more workers to make bumpers, consoles, and other components.
- Hyundai officials are determined to hire locally as much as possible and will offer competitive wages and compensation packages to attract workers away from existing employers.
- State officials envision the initial labor cannibalization being offset in the long term as job demand coaxes Savannah young adults to stay in the local market rather than move to Atlanta or other employment centers.
- The Hyundai Metaplant site will host multiple Hyundai affiliates, including the EV assembly plant, the battery factory, and onsite subsidiaries that manufacture key parts and materials.
- Hyundai's logistics arm, Hyundai Glovis, will operate from the Metaplant site.
- The assembly facility alone will employ 2,600 workers, which includes 2,000 hourly and assembly line employees.
- Georgia House Rep. Ron Stephens represents parts of Savannah's southside, West Chatham, and South Bryan county.
- Georgia's Workforce Ready Career Pathway and Georgia Quick Start programs are well positioned to produce a significant number of local laborers over time.
- Per an incentives agreement with the state, Hyundai must create 8,100 jobs by December 2031.
- Hyundai intends to hire its first 500 employees before the end of this year and another 700 in 2024 ahead of the assembly plant's opening in early 2025.
- Existing Savannah employers are beefing up their compensation packages and offering new incentives to retain employees, according to Maria Whitfield, director of workforce for the Savannah Joint Development Authority.
- Maria Whitfield stated that laborers are free to choose where they want to work and companies cannot discriminate against applicants from other local employers like Gulfstream.
- Tal Loos, vice president of economic development at Savannah Tech, noted that companies may entice workers to stay by promoting flexible work hours or other incentives.
- Rep. Stephens, a retired pharmacist, observed that existing industries may consider additional incentives to keep talent flowing, though likely not as aggressively as past chain drugstore signing bonuses.
- Hyundai's Park hopes the company's hiring will have a limited impact on other local manufacturers like Gulfstream, noting significant differences between automobile and aviation industry expertise.
- Park stated that while they can hire some good engineers from the aviation sector, the expertise is quite different, resulting in no cannibalization between the two large companies.
- Georgia House Rep. Jesse Petrea represents Chatham's islands and coastal Bryan County.
- Rep. Petrea stated that workers developing skills and work ethic who receive new opportunities with greater economic potential are benefiting communities and society by securing good jobs.
- Small businesses are likely to feel the ripple effects of Hyundai's hiring as blue-collar workers transition to new sectors.
- Industry reports indicate that almost half of all employees working in the U.S. are employed by a small business, making them vulnerable to worker poaching.
- Workers leaving other manufacturers or warehousing roles to move up will create openings for others in hourly jobs within hospitality and retail sectors.
- Hyundai's Park stressed that hiring locally promotes a sense of community within the workforce, emphasizing that collaboration, cooperation, and close communication are vital.
- Brent Stubbs, senior manager of learning and development for the Hyundai Metaplant, is utilizing creative strategies to find talent within the state.
- Stubbs stated that efforts are 100% focused locally to ensure the community benefits from the new jobs.
