# Georgia Manufacturing Extension Partnership

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2025-07-24

**Source:** [Georgia Manufacturing Extension Partnership at Georgia Tech Celebrates 65 Years of Service | News Center](https://news.gatech.edu/news/2025/07/24/georgia-manufacturing-extension-partnership-georgia-tech-celebrates-65-years)  ·  `page_012528`

## Facts

- The Georgia Manufacturing Extension Partnership (GaMEP) is a program of the Enterprise Innovation Institute at Georgia Tech.
- GaMEP received recognition from Gov. Brian P. Kemp at the Georgia State Capitol for 65 years of service to the manufacturing industry.
- GaMEP leverages expertise and resources to advance manufacturing and economic prosperity across Georgia.
- The manufacturing industry adds $82 billion to the Georgia economy and employs 425,000 residents, according to the National Association of Manufacturers.
- By 1960, more than 4,500 manufacturers had established operations across Georgia due to strategic efforts by state leaders with economic development assistance from Georgia Tech.
- The Georgia General Assembly voted to establish the Georgia Tech Industrial Extension Service (now GaMEP) to provide technical support to manufacturers facing new challenges.
- GaMEP created statewide field offices that link industry and innovation, delivering on-site technical expertise to help manufacturers thrive.
- Tim Israel serves as GaMEP director and EI2 associate vice president for corporate engagement/firm-based programs.
- In 2024, GaMEP generated an impressive $294 in return for every state dollar allocated to manufacturing projects.
- Over the past decade, GaMEP provided assistance and education to more than 3,900 manufacturers across 144 counties.
- During that same decade, GaMEP helped manufacturers create or retain 14,500 jobs.
- Over the past decade, GaMEP helped manufacturers invest $1 billion in capital improvements.
- Over the past decade, GaMEP helped manufacturers realize $3.5 billion in sales.
- Over the past decade, GaMEP helped manufacturers save nearly $450 million in costs.
- GaMEP primarily serves small- to medium-sized manufacturers, with 75% employing fewer than 250 workers.
- GaMEP's top-served industries include fabricated metal products, food, machinery, and chemical and transportation equipment manufacturing.
- David Bridges serves as EI2 vice president.
- GaMEP's reach spans 10 regions across the state, each led by a dedicated region manager who lives and works locally.
- Lloyd Avram is the CEO and president of the Georgia Association of Manufacturers.
- In the Coastal Region (counties: Bryan, Bulloch, Camden, Chatham, Effingham, Glynn, Liberty, Long, McIntosh, Screven), GaMEP served 269 manufacturers, created/retained 283 jobs, facilitated $23,171,292 in capital improvements, saved $1,645,061 in costs, realized $13,965,000 in sales, and supported success story Roger Wood Foods.
- In the East Region (counties: Burke, Columbia, Glascock, Hancock, Jefferson, Jenkins, Lincoln, McDuffie, Richmond, Taliaferro, Warren, Washington, Wilkes), GaMEP served 169 manufacturers, created/retained 3,899 jobs, facilitated $127,754,280 in capital improvements, saved $14,771,582 in costs, realized $975,465,000 in sales, and supported success story Nutritional Resources.

- GaMEP is a member of the U.S. Department of Commerce National Institute of Standards and Technology Manufacturing Extension Partnership.
- GaMEP has advanced manufacturing and economic prosperity in Georgia since 1960.
