# Stryten Energy LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-23

**Source:** [Stryten Energy - Energy Storage Solutions](https://www.stryten.com/)  ·  `page_000928`

## Facts

- Stryten Energy is the only company with a complete offering of advanced lead, lithium and vanadium batteries.
- Stryten Energy’s Vanadium Redox Flow Battery (VRFB) is designed for medium- to long-duration energy storage from 4 to 12 hours for applications including microgrids, utility-scale storage, data centers and military bases.
- Stryten Energy offers innovative lithium-ion batteries for military and large-scale operations requiring 24/7 performance, material handling and transportation applications.
- Stryten Energy’s advanced lead battery portfolio includes Enhanced Flooded Batteries (EFB) for start-stop applications, tubular technology for material handling and Valve Regulated AGM for backup power in microgrids, utilities and data centers.
- Stryten Energy’s inCOMMAND software leverages real fleet data to optimize motive power batteries and chargers through a central dashboard for performance monitoring and management.
- Stryten Energy announced plans on March 31, 2025, to add 10 gigawatts of new U.S. energy storage manufacturing capacity to support military, long-duration energy storage, transportation and material-handling applications.
- Stryten Energy launched Storion Energy in a joint venture with Largo on December 19, 2024, to establish a domestic supply of vanadium electrolyte for long-duration energy storage via a unique leasing model.
- Stryten Energy was awarded DOE MAKE IT Prize Phase 2 funding on October 2, 2024, for its Securing America’s Vanadium Electrolyte Supply (SAVES) project to scale U.S.-based production of vanadium redox flow battery electrolyte.
- Stryten Energy secured exclusive rights to market and distribute Battle Born Batteries® for military, automotive, marine, power sports, lawn & garden and golf cart applications through a licensing agreement with Dragonfly Energy.
- Stryten Energy invested in REVKIT on October 15, 2024, to become the preferred provider of advanced automotive batteries on the platform's digital marketplace.
- Stryten Energy’s premium lead battery and charger solutions will be distributed through KION North America’s network of 200 sales and service locations nationwide.
- Stryten Energy’s M-Series Li600 lithium-ion material handling batteries were certified for fleet use and made available through Hyster-Yale as of June 17, 2025.
- Stryten Energy launched its new M-Series Li610 lithium-ion battery on April 13, 2026, expanding its motive power portfolio for material handling operations with longer cycle life.
- Stryten Energy introduced the E-Series AGM160/190 multi-terminal batteries designed for harsh environments and long-duration discharge applications in telecommunications and utility sectors.
- Stryten Energy installed a lead Battery Energy Storage System (BESS) at the VICARS Community Center in Southwest Atlanta to provide power resiliency for the community.
- Georgia Institute of Technology and Stryten Energy announced the successful installation of a lead battery energy storage system on April 23, 2025, to support advanced research and carbon-neutral goals alongside existing solar infrastructure.
- Stryten Energy hosted U.S. Senator Charles Grassley at its transportation manufacturing plant in Manchester, Iowa, which recently celebrated 50 years of battery production.
- The Kansas House of Representatives issued House Resolution No. 6036 on March 30, 2026, honoring Stryten Energy’s 50 years of manufacturing excellence at its Salina, Kansas plant.
- Stryten Energy welcomed 25 summer interns in 2025, with 18 assigned to work at its corporate headquarters in Alpharetta, GA.
- Stryten Energy opened an expanded, state-of-the-art battery testing lab at its new corporate headquarters in Alpharetta, Ga., featuring dedicated space for testing lead, lithium and vanadium batteries and materials.
- Stryten Energy’s mobile microgrid solution is designed for remote power and disaster relief scenarios, demonstrating a hybrid diesel-electric vehicle ecosystem that manages energy sources, storage and usage.
- The U.S. Department of Energy includes lead batteries among critical technologies for domestic supply chains and national security, highlighting the importance of North America’s closed-loop lead-battery recycling system.
- Stryten Energy highlighted scalable, technology-agnostic BESS solutions supporting data centers, manufacturing and EV charging at CES 2026.
- U.S. power demand is projected to reach 4,185 billion kilowatt-hours (kWh) in 2025, a record high compared to 4,067 billion kWh in 2022, driving demand for energy storage solutions.
- China’s export ban on critical minerals including antimony, gallium and germanium, effective December 3, 2024, is disrupting global battery manufacturing supply chains.
- Stryten Energy showcased advancements to its M-Series X-7 charger at MODEX 2026, highlighting updates that expand compatibility, power and flexibility for material handling applications.
- Stryten Energy introduced a new Material Handling Battery Finder tool at MODEX 2026 (Booth #B9325) to help users quickly match batteries to trucks.
- Enhanced Flooded Batteries (EFB) offer a lower-cost alternative to AGM with about 50% higher capacity and up to 52% longer cycle life for start-stop and hybrid vehicles.
- Stryten Energy’s inCOMMAND™ platform received major performance and usability enhancements on August 25, 2025, enabling more efficient fleet management for motive power batteries and chargers.
- Stryten Energy delivered battery-first technologies for commercial, industrial and utility-scale applications to BCI Flow Batteries North America as of October 27, 2025.
