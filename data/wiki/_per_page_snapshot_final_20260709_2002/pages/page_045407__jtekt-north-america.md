# JTEKT North America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [JTEKT North America expanding Upstate operations - Who's On The Move](https://whosonthemove.com/jtekt-north-america-expanding-upstate-operations-152033/)  ·  `page_045407`

## Facts

- JTEKT North America is expanding its operations in Greenville County, South Carolina.
- The company will invest $1.75 million to establish an operational headquarters at CU-ICAR.
- The expansion will create 27 new jobs in Greenville, S.C.
- JTEKT Corporation was established in January 2006 through the merger of Koyo Seiko Co., Ltd., a world-class bearing manufacturer, and Toyoda Machine Works, Ltd., a machine tool manufacturer excelling in world-leading technologies.
- JTEKT is now a trusted systems supplier of JTEKT brand automotive steering and driveline components, Koyo brand bearings, and Toyoda machine tools.
- JTEKT employs more than 44,000 workers worldwide.
- JTEKT North America employs more than 7,000 associates at 14 manufacturing facilities plus additional technical and sales centers in North America.
- The company will locate its North American operational headquarters alongside its Koyo bearing product technical center at CU-ICAR, an advanced-technology research campus for the automotive industry.
- The expansion will occupy facilities at both 1 and 7 Research Drive in Greenville, S.C.
- The expansion is expected to be fully operational in the first quarter of 2016.
- Hiring for the 27 new positions has already begun.
- The Coordinating Council for Economic Development has approved job development credits related to the project.
- Mike Davidson, COO of JTEKT North America, stated that their operational headquarters in Greenville County and CU-ICAR will provide an efficient base to support their extensive footprint in North America.
- Mike Davidson cited the availability of professional and technical talent, proximity to the majority of their production facilities, and the quality of life in the Upstate as reasons for the move.
- Greenville County Council Chairman Dr. Bob Taylor stated that seeing a company like JTEKT grow beyond their initial investment confirms they are doing things right in Greenville.
- Dr. Bob Taylor highlighted Greenville's business climate, infrastructure, and talented workforce as factors making it a globally-recognized destination for the automotive industry.
- Clemson University President James P. Clements stated that at CU-ICAR, their goal is to provide a dynamic environment where global leaders in the automotive industry can be located together and work together.
- James P. Clements noted that it is exciting to see JTEKT call the CU-ICAR campus home, calling it a win for Clemson students, faculty and staff, JTEKT, and South Carolina’s economy.
- The company's new North American Operational headquarters will be housed at 7 Research Drive, Greenville, S.C.
