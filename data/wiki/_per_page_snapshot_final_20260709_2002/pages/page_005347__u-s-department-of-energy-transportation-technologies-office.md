# U.S. Department of Energy Transportation Technologies Office

## Facts

- The Transportation Technologies Office (TTO) works with the National Laboratories and industry partners to collect and report data on vehicle performance, research projects, and the transportation sector broadly.
- TTO publishes the Transportation Energy Data Book annually in PDF and downloadable Excel formats with Oak Ridge National Laboratory, compiling information on petroleum consumption, vehicle use, transportation technologies, household vehicle characteristics, and fleet characteristics.
- TTO publishes a weekly Fact of the Week featuring facts with accompanying graphs or charts to highlight useful statistics.
- TTO’s FuelEconomy.gov and the Alternative Fuels Data Center host graphs, maps, and downloadable data sets on vehicle fuel economy, alternative fuel use, and alternative fuel vehicles and stations.
- TTO supports the Advanced Vehicle Testing Activity, which collects on-road and in-laboratory data on advanced and alternative fuel vehicles and infrastructure to provide benchmark data for technology models and future research and development.
- The Advanced Vehicle Testing Activity has tested technologies including hybrid electric, plug-in electric, neighborhood electric, start-stop hybrid, natural gas, clean diesel, and hydrogen internal combustion engine vehicles, as well as electric vehicle supply equipment and batteries.
