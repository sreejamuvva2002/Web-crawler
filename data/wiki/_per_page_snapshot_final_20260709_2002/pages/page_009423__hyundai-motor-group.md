# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** research_and_development  ·  **Published:** 2026-04-29

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Group and Georgia Tech Sign MOU to Further Collaborate on Hydrogen Mobility Development](https://www.theautochannel.com/news/2026/04/29/1662700-hyundai-motor-group-and-georgia-tech-sign-mou-to-further.html)  ·  `page_009423`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group announced a multi-year partnership on hydrogen mobility solutions with the Georgia Institute of Technology.
- The partnership includes Hyundai Motor North America donating four NEXO fuel cell electric SUVs exclusively for campus use.
- Hyundai Motor Group is donating a hydrogen electrolyzer project exclusively for campus use.
- The initiative brings NEXO hydrogen fuel cell SUVs and fueling infrastructure to the Georgia Tech campus.
- The initiative advances research, workforce development, and zero-emissions logistics.
- The donated NEXO SUVs will support campus operations and interdisciplinary research across engineering, sustainability, energy systems, and public policy.
- The partnership expansion was announced on April 29, 2026, in Seoul and Atlanta.
- Ken Ramírez, Executive Vice President and Head of Global Energy and Hydrogen Business at Hyundai Motor Group, commented on the collaboration; he is a Georgia Tech alumnus (class of 1991) and currently serves on the Georgia Tech Advisory Board.
- Randy Parker, President and CEO of Hyundai Motor North America, stated that donating NEXO fuel cell SUVs will expand the clean mobility footprint on campus and provide real-world experiences with zero-emissions transportation technology.
- Ángel Cabrera, President of the Georgia Institute of Technology, stated the expansion advances hydrogen research, reduces campus emissions, and strengthens Georgia's role in the future of clean mobility.
- The donated hydrogen electrolyzer project will be installed at Georgia Tech's North Avenue Research Area (NARA).
- Key research focus areas include engineering (exploring hydrogen-based systems and mobility solutions), sustainability (assessing environmental benefits of hydrogen technologies), energy systems (understanding integration of hydrogen fuel cells into current infrastructure), and public policy (evaluating regulatory and social implications of hydrogen adoption).
- The initiative uses the university as a living laboratory for clean transportation technologies, connecting Georgia Tech's research enterprise with campus operations.
- Faculty and students will study the real-world performance of hydrogen technology, infrastructure requirements for large-scale deployment, and environmental impacts of hydrogen energy systems.
- Insights gathered from the initiative aim to inform and accelerate the widespread use of hydrogen technology in campuses, fleets, cities, and freight corridors.
- The initiative supports Georgia Tech's strategic plan goal of expanding the use of zero-emissions vehicles powered by sustainable energy sources.
- Beyond hydrogen energy, the partnership aims to advance innovation in autonomous driving, electric vehicle (EV) batteries, charging infrastructure, materials science, and cybersecurity.
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) is located in Georgia.
- HTWO Logistics operates as a clean logistics partnership running Hyundai XCIENT fuel cell heavy-duty trucks in logistics operations near Savannah.
- The collaboration reinforces the link between education, research, and Hyundai Motor Group's long-term goal of achieving carbon neutrality by 2045.
- The partnership aims to generate workforce talent and position Georgia as a national leader in hydrogen and zero-emissions transportation.
- Additional announcements regarding the partnership's research projects, educational programs, and vehicle deployment are expected in the coming months.
- Georgia Tech conducts more than $1 billion in research annually for government, industry, and society.
