# Trenton Pressing

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-04-06

**Source:** [Trenton Pressing breaks ground on new $10 million manufacturing facility | Local News | local3news.com](https://www.local3news.com/local-news/trenton-pressing-breaks-ground-on-new-10-million-manufacturing-facility/article_392dcecc-d4a3-11ed-a75b-8bdee2a83d17.html)  ·  `page_012441`

## Facts

- Trenton Pressing, a tier one manufacturing supplier located in Dade County, Ga., broke ground today on a $10 million, 105,000-square-foot facility.
- The additional space will allow for expanded manufacturing and assembly services and significantly increases the organization’s manufacturing capacity.
- Hans Pfunder, chief operating officer for Trenton Pressing, said the company is extremely grateful for Dade County’s support throughout this expansion process.
- In just over two years, Trenton Pressing purchased a struggling manufacturing company and completely turned it around through a reorganization of management, technology and equipment upgrades and personnel training.
- The expanded facility will allow the company to better service its growth and meet the manufacturing needs of its clients which include BMW, Club Car, ZF and more.
- Pfunder noted that it was exciting to see the tremendous growth over the past two years after the reorganization process, stating that the achievement speaks to the dedication and commitment of employees.
- Trenton Pressing is currently one of the top five employers in Dade County with 220 employees.
- The company plans to add 20 more jobs with the facility expansion.
- Trenton Pressing prides itself on building an empowering, transparent and safe working environment for its employees.
- Dade County Executive Ted Rumley stated that Trenton Pressing has been a great community partner since it began operations in the county in 2021, providing jobs and supporting schools and the environment.
- Construction on the new facility is expected to be completed by December of 2023.
- Trenton Mayor Alex Case stated that Trenton Pressing has been a great asset to the city since arriving just over two years ago, providing needed jobs and helping improve the quality of life for citizens.
- Trenton Pressing provides metal stamping processes, welding and assembly processes, warehousing and shipping.
- Jeff Mullis, president and CEO of the Northwest Georgia Joint Development Agency and retired Georgia state senator, congratulated Trenton Pressing on its expansion efforts and commended it for adding jobs in the Northwest Georgia area.
