# Apple Inc.

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2021-02-05

**Source:** [Apple Car production: Reports of talks with Japanese companies - 9to5Mac](https://9to5mac.com/2021/02/05/apple-car-production-reports/)  ·  `page_009151`

## Facts

- Two reports on claimed Apple Car production plans have emerged today.
- One report suggests progress on a rumored $3.6B Hyundai deal to make up to 100,000 cars per year at a Kia plant in Georgia.
- Other manufacturers remain potential partners for Apple's EV project.
- Apple is reportedly in talks with at least six carmakers.
- Japanese manufacturers saw their share prices rise following reports of discussions with Apple.
- The WSJ reported that Hyundai is ramping up preparation for Apple Car production.
- Hyundai has discussed investing more than $3 billion with Apple to have its subsidiary Kia build cars under Apple's brand as soon as 2024.
- Under the potential agreement, up to 100,000 vehicles could be assembled in the first year at Kia's Georgia factory.
- Hyundai-Kia officials have been reaching out to potential partners in preparation for a deal.
- A person briefed on the matter in Georgia stated, "We are hearing encouraging things."
- Bloomberg reported increased speculation about Apple talking with Japanese car companies.
- Speculation that Apple is seeking an EV partner swept through South Korea and Japan, causing shares of major regional car companies to climb.
- The Nikkei newspaper reported that Apple was in discussions with at least six automakers.
- Tatsuo Yoshida, a senior analyst at Bloomberg Intelligence, noted Japanese automakers are typically too busy with internal development and sales to partner with Apple.
- Yoshida suggested Nissan Motor Co. or Mitsubishi Motors Corp. might sign up because they "don’t have much work, and are somewhat idle."
- When approached for comment, Mitsubishi and Subaru appeared to rule themselves out, while other companies offered no comment.
- Ming-Chi Kuo suggested Apple would use Hyundai’s E-GMP chassis, which supports an 80% charge in 18 minutes and a 60-mile range boost from a five-minute charge.
- Bloomberg suggests Nissan has a similar chassis that may be suitable for Apple Car production.
- Among Japanese carmakers, Nissan likely has the right solution for a non-automaker entering the EV market due to its common EV platform developed with French partner Renault SA.
- A CNBC report claimed the first version of the Apple car will "not be designed to have a driver."
- The article notes that targeting a 2024 launch for a fully autonomous car would be extremely ambitious technically and legislatively, casting doubt on the claim's credibility.
