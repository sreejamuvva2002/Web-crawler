# State of Georgia Government

**Type:** policy_or_incentive  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-05-11

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp Signs Legislation Lowering Taxes and Supporting Economic Growth | Governor Brian P. Kemp Office of the Governor](https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases/2026-05-11/gov-kemp-signs-legislation-lowering-taxes-and-supporting-economic-growth)  ·  `page_003110`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp signed nine pieces of legislation into law on May 11, 2026, to lower taxes and support economic growth in Georgia.
- HB 463 lowers Georgia's state income tax rate from 5.19% to 4.99%, beginning January 1, 2026.
- HB 463 includes provisions for further annual reductions of the state income tax rate as well as increases of the standard deduction.
- HB 463 raises the retirement income exclusion to $70,000 beginning in 2027.
- HB 463 introduces temporary tax exclusions for qualified overtime compensation and cash tips through 2028.
- HB 463 repeals some tax credits and sales and use tax exemptions while fulfilling Governor Kemp's promise to accelerate the largest state income tax cut in state history, reaching a rate below 5% three years ahead of schedule.
- HB 1129 creates guardrails for state-designated enterprise zones.
- HB 1185 modernizes complex corporate litigation policies and revises some standards around the Georgia Statewide Business Court.
- SB 33 enacts broad property tax reform by establishing a new Local Homestead Option Sales Tax (LHOST) aimed at providing homeowner tax relief and improving local fiscal management.
- HB 445 expands the right of business owners in Georgia to appeal tax reassessments of certain high-value tangible personal property to a hearing officer, and establishes new qualifications and procedures for officers handling appeals.
- HB 1209 establishes a limited sales and use tax exemption for construction materials used in specific developments near certain state-owned convention facilities.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Georgia has remained the No. 1 state for business for a historic 12 consecutive years because of its commitment to growing opportunity and budgeting conservatively.
- Lt. Governor Burt Jones noted that Republican leadership cut taxes every year he served as Lt. Governor and expanded the GOAL scholarship tax credit to support school choice.
- Speaker of the House Jon Burns highlighted measures delivering results including historic income tax rebates, rate reductions, property tax relief, and lower costs at the pump.
