# U.S. DRIVE Partnership

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** research_and_development  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [U.S. DRIVE Partnership Plan, Roadmaps, and Accomplishments | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/cmei/vehicles/us-drive-partnership-plan-roadmaps-and-accomplishments)  ·  `page_002861`

## Facts

- The U.S. DRIVE Partnership provides access to its Partnership Plan, Fuels Working Group Reports, and various Technical Team Roadmaps for reference.
- Technical team roadmaps cover Electric Drive (2024), Advanced Combustion and Emissions Control (2018), Electrical and Electronics (2017), Electrochemical Energy Storage (2017), Fuel Cells (2017), Hydrogen Production, Delivery, and Codes & Standards (all 2017), Materials (2017), Onboard Hydrogen Storage (2017), Integrated Systems Analysis (2023), Vehicle-Mobility Systems Analysis (2023), and Net-Zero Carbon Fuels (2021).
- The Partnership publishes annual Highlights of Technical Accomplishments Reports, selecting highlights from hundreds of DOE-funded projects each year.
- These accomplishments reports are available for the years 2006 through 2023.
- The compendium represents significant progress in advanced automotive and infrastructure technologies as evaluated by DOE and industry partners.
