# HL-GA Battery Company

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-08

**Source:** [After 475 arrests at Georgia jobsite, feds vow more raids | Construction Dive](https://www.constructiondive.com/news/georgia-hyundai-jobsite-raid-ice-construction/759550/)  ·  `page_001150`

## Facts

- Federal agents arrested 475 workers at a construction site in Georgia on Sept. 5, the Department of Justice announced.
- The Ellabell, Georgia, facility is the future home of a $4.3 billion electric vehicle battery facility co-owned by Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution.
- The vast majority of the arrested workers were South Korean.
- White House border czar Tom Homan told CNN that the administration will continue to ramp up raids on U.S. workplaces.
- Estimates place the total share of undocumented construction workers at about 23%.
- Immigration enforcement has affected about one in three construction firms, according to a survey released Aug. 28 by the Associated General Contractors of America and the National Center for Construction Education and Research.
- Roughly nine in ten construction firms report having openings for craft construction workers.
- The lack of skilled workforce has been the largest cause of project delays, per the AGC survey.
- Hyundai and LG invested $4.3 billion to establish the HL-GA Battery Company plant.
- Plans call for beginning production of EV batteries in early 2026, per the project website.
- Both Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution are based in South Korea.
- No Hyundai employees were arrested at the site.
- Forty-seven LG employees were detained during the raid.
- Most of the arrested workers were employed by subcontractors.
- Hyundai stated it has a “zero tolerance” policy for those who do not follow the law and expects the same from project partners.
- Hyundai is reviewing its processes to ensure all parties maintain high standards of legal compliance, including thorough vetting of employment practices by contractors and subcontractors.
- LG is working closely with the Korean government regarding detained employees and is working for their release.
- LG stated it will do its utmost to swiftly resolve the issue and ensure the safe and prompt return of its employees and partner company members.
- Negotiations to return workers to South Korea moved quickly, per Reuters.
- Three hundred detained workers will be processed, released, and returned via a chartered plane, according to the South Korean presidential office on Sunday.
- The megaproject is an example of an onshoring push to benefit investments by foreign companies on U.S. soil.
- Tom Homan claimed employers hire undocumented workers to work them harder, pay them less, and undercut competition that hires U.S. citizen employees, driving wages down.
- President Donald Trump posted on Truth Social encouraging foreign companies to continue planning projects in the U.S. legally, bring technical talent, and hire and train American workers.
