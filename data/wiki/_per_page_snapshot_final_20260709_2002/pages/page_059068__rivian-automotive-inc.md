# Rivian Automotive Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-05-03

**Source:** [Rivian Ramps Up Illinois EV Production with $827 Million State](https://hoodline.com/2024/05/rivian-ramps-up-illinois-ev-production-with-827-million-state-incentive-package-foregoing-georgia-facility-plans/)  ·  `page_059068`

## Facts

- Rivian Automotive Inc. secured $827 million in state incentives to quickly rev up its electric-vehicle manufacturing capabilities in Illinois.
- The incentive package caused Rivian to cancel plans to build a plant in Georgia.
- The funds largely comprise tax credits under the state's Reimagining Energy and Vehicles in Illinois (REV Illinois) program.
- The deal promises to deliver over 550 jobs and a $1.5 billion investment from Rivian.
- Rivian already employs 7,000 assembly workers at its Normal facility.
- Rivian cancelled its proposed $5 billion facility in Georgia.
- The decision is expected to save Rivian $2.25 billion in capital expenses by building its new midsize R2 SUV alongside its existing R1 lineup in Illinois.
- Over 68,000 preorders for the R2 took place within the first 24 hours of its unveiling.
- Illinois Governor J.B. Pritzker pushed for the incentives, stating, 'They're growing now and they had the opportunity to grow in Georgia or in Illinois.'
- The state's strategy to keep Rivian expansions local aims to turn Illinois into an EV industry hub.
- The capital investment will expand the Normal plant's production capacity to 215,000 vehicles annually.
- Rivian lost over $1.5 billion in the fourth quarter of 2023.
- Rivian saw its shares tumble from post-IPO highs before the incentive news sent shares up nearly 7% at the close.
- Rivian CEO and founder R.J. Scaringe stated the support aims to ensure the new model can efficiently come to market, providing 'even greater consumer choice for EVs.'
- The incentive package requires Rivian to keep at least 6,000 jobs.
- Illinois is investing in human capital by funding a new manufacturing training academy featuring an apprenticeship program for local high schools.
