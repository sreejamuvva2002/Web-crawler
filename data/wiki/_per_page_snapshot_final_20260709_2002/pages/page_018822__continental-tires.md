## Facts

- Continental is one of the world’s largest tire manufacturers, delivering top performance for more than 150 years.
- Continental produces tires at 19 locations in 16 countries globally.
- Continental operates manufacturing facilities in Clinton (MS, USA), Mount Vernon (IL, USA), Plymouth (IN, USA), and Sumter (SC, USA).
- A modern car tire typically consists of 25 components and 12 different rubber compounds.
- Continental covers around 25 million test kilometers annually with new tire models during development and quality control.
- In 2024, Continental conducted more digital tire simulations and virtual calculations than physical tests for the first time.
- Virtual simulations save an average of 400 km in real-world test driving per test program.
- Since March 2025, seven self-driving autonomous mobile robots (AMRs) have been transporting tires within the ContiLifeCycle plant in Hanover-Stöcken.
- Continental uses materials like recycled PET bottles in ContiRe.Tex technology and silica from rice husks to increase renewable and recycled raw material shares.
- Continental has over 2,000 R&D employees around the world.
