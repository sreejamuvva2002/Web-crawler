# YKK USA Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2014-10-14

**Source:** [Four decades of zippers, and much more, from Macon’s YKK  | Macon Telegraph](https://www.macon.com/news/business/article30148497.html)  ·  `page_016211`

## Facts

- The YKK zipper plant in Macon celebrated its grand opening on October 18, 1974, with a $15 million investment, though it actually began operating in the spring of that year.
- The Macon fastener plant currently employs about 600 workers and is located on Ocmulgee East Industrial Park in Bibb County.
- During its peak employment period of 2000-2001, YKK employed approximately 1,100 workers at the Macon facility, making it the largest zipper manufacturer in the world at that time.
- The company originally required 11 acres for a small plant but was persuaded by Bibb County to purchase a site of more than 50 acres and commit to employing 500 to 700 workers.
- Bibb County provided incentives including deeding land to the Macon-Bibb County Industrial Authority for an industrial park and increasing water and sewerage capacity to 1 million gallons a day.
- The Macon-Bibb County Industrial Authority and the state of Georgia have invested in YKK through grants and tax credits to encourage its growth and stability.
- Five million zipper sliders are manufactured in Macon every day, with 2.5 million exported globally and 2.5 million consumed in North and Central America.
- The Macon facility is one of only two YKK brass wire plants in the world, alongside a plant in Japan, and ships to 12 different countries and 14 different factories.
- YKK manufactures primarily zippers, as well as snaps, buttons, hook-and-loop fasteners, and plastic hardware for various industries including automotive, healthcare, military, and aerospace.
- The company developed silent zippers for soldiers, fire-retardant zippers for firefighters, and air-tight/water-tight zippers used in applications ranging from outer space to the bottom of the ocean.
- YKK manufactures its own machinery for zipper production and sells continuous chains to customers so they do not need to inventory different sizes.
- The company adopted 3-D printing technology to produce slider tab samples in minutes for 50 cents, replacing a process that previously took weeks and cost hundreds of dollars.
- YKK AP America began producing architectural products like aluminum doors and windows at a $45 million plant on 202 acres in Dublin, Georgia, which opened in 1992.
- In 2007, YKK began production of residential architectural products on its Macon campus, utilizing space no longer needed by the zipper plant.
- In July 2014, YKK AP announced plans to double its production capacity at the Macon plant by January 2015, employing about 100 people.
- The Macon facility currently has 2.5 million square feet of manufacturing space, with approximately 25 percent expected to be empty after the YKK AP expansion is complete.
- YKK operates more than 108 companies in over 570 locations across 71 countries, employing more than 40,700 workers worldwide under the leadership of founder Tadao Yoshida's son, Tadahiro "Tad" Yoshida.
- The company's mission is based on the founder's philosophy of the "cycle of goodness," which dictates that no one prospers without rendering benefit to others.
- YKK remains committed to Macon and Georgia despite industry downturns, with leadership publicly stating that Macon is their home and they will always be a friend of the community.
- The company has been involved in numerous community activities including the Cherry Blossom Festival, Sister Cities program, education, civic activities, and the Greater Macon Chamber of Commerce, employing thousands of Georgians and paying millions in wages and taxes.
- YKK's restructuring and downsizing were attributed to retailers buying less expensive garments globally, leading to the disappearance of many U.S. brand-name jean manufacturers that were previously its biggest customers.
- To remain competitive against lower labor costs abroad, YKK relies on innovation, standardization of products worldwide, and developing new products in Macon for production in other countries.
- Interstate 16 was being built from Savannah’s port to Macon at the time of the plant's selection, providing critical logistics infrastructure.
- YKK El Salvador performs the same manufacturing functions as YKK Macon, making Macon a regional hub that serves much larger areas than originally intended.
- Middle Georgia State College economics professor Greg George noted that economic activity in the country has become increasingly concentrated over the past 30 years with a slow but steady decline in manufacturing.
