# Archer Aviation Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-12

**Source:** [Archer Completes Construction of High-Volume eVTOL Manufacturing Facility in GA - EVinfo.net](https://evinfo.net/2024/12/archer-completes-construction-of-high-volume-evtol-manufacturing-facility-in-ga/)  ·  `page_017069`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation Inc. announced on December 19, 2024, that it has received the certificate of occupancy for its high-volume manufacturing facility, ARC.
- The ARC facility is located at the Covington Municipal Airport in Georgia.
- Stellantis and Archer are finalizing an agreement in which Stellantis would work with Archer as its exclusive contract manufacturer to mass produce Archer’s Midnight eVTOL aircraft at this facility.
- Stellantis plans to contribute capital, advanced manufacturing technology, expertise and experienced personnel, with a goal of scaling the facility to 650 aircraft annually by 2030.
- Archer’s team is focused on the start of production planned for early next year following the completion of construction.
- Adam Goldstein, Archer’s founder and CEO, stated that construction was completed fast and on budget and expressed pride in the Archer team and partners.
- A ribbon-cutting ceremony was held to celebrate the completion of construction on the ARC facility.
- The ribbon-cutting ceremony was attended by Covington Mayor Fleeta Baggett, State Representative Tim Fleming, Newton County Development President Serra Hall, Newton County Chamber President Debbie Harper, Covington City Manager Tres Thomas, Newton County Commissioner Lanier Sims, and Archer’s Vice President of Operations, Glen Burks.
- The facility is creating valuable jobs for local residents and contributing to the local economy.
- Stellantis Chief Manufacturing and Supply Chain Officer Arnaud Deboeuf stated that supporting Archer’s electric aircraft growth with deep manufacturing expertise is a great opportunity for both companies.
- On December 12, 2024, Anduril Industries Inc. and Archer Aviation Inc. announced an exclusive agreement to jointly develop a next-generation hybrid-propulsion, vertical-take-off-and-landing (VTOL) aircraft for defense applications.
- The first product of the Anduril-Archer partnership is planned to target a potential program of record from the United States Department of Defense (DOD).
- Archer raised $430 million in additional equity capital to support the development of its defense initiatives and other corporate purposes.
- The $430 million funding includes participation from existing strategic partners Stellantis and United Airlines, along with new institutional investors Wellington Management and 2PointZero, a subsidiary of the UAE’s largest listed entity IHC.
- In August, the U.S. Department of Defense (DOD) accepted the military airworthiness assessment of Archer’s Midnight aircraft.
- Archer Aviation delivered its first Midnight aircraft to the US Air Force (USAF) to evaluate as part of its AFWERX Agility Prime contract valued at up to $142M.
- Archer’s Midnight is an all-electric eVTOL aircraft designed for urban air mobility applications, such as air taxis.
- The Midnight has a passenger capacity of four and a range of approximately 100 miles on a single charge.
