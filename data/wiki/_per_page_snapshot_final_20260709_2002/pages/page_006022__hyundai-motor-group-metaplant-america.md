# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-03-26

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America Celebrates Grand Opening, Powering US Economic Growth | Press Releases & Insights | Worldwide | HTWO](https://www.htwo.hyundai.com/en/worldwide/press-releases/article/Hyundai_Motor_Group_Metaplant_America_Celebrates_Grand_Opening,_Powering_US_Economic_Growth)  ·  `page_006022`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) hosted its Grand Opening celebration on March 26, 2025.
- The ceremony marks the completion of the largest economic development project in Georgia’s history, occurring two and a half years after breaking ground.
- Euisun Chung, Executive Chair of Hyundai Motor Group, stated that HMGMA represents advanced manufacturing capabilities, commitment to innovation, and investment in relationships with partners and communities in Georgia.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp attended the grand opening ceremony alongside U.S. Representative Buddy Carter, Hyundai Motor Group Vice Chair Jaehoon Chang, Hyundai Motor Company President and CEO José Muñoz, Kia Corporation President and CEO Ho Sung Song, company officials, state and local leaders, HMGMA Meta Pro employees, and other distinguished guests.
- Governor Kemp called HMGMA a once-in-a-generation opportunity made possible by working with job creators to build fruitful partnerships for decades.
- Kemp highlighted collaborations between HMGMA and Georgia’s universities and technical colleges, including Georgia Quick Start’s on-site training facility, which are preparing Georgians for high-quality jobs.
- HMG entered into a landmark agreement with the State of Georgia in May 2022 to establish an electric and hybrid vehicle production and battery manufacturing facility in Ellabell, Georgia.
- The facility is located 20 miles outside of historic Savannah on a 2,906-acre Megasite.
- HMGMA was officially formed by June 2022, with groundbreaking occurring within four months of formation.
- On October 3, 2024, less than two years after breaking ground, the first vehicle rolled off the assembly line at HMGMA.
- The first vehicle produced was a Hyundai IONIQ 5.
- Hyundai’s IONIQ 9 three-row electric SUV is also now in production at HMGMA.
- The first Kia model to be built at the facility will join production in 2026.
- The plant manufactures electric vehicles for Hyundai, Genesis, and Kia, and is equipped to produce hybrid vehicles.
- HMGMA initially has the capacity to produce 300,000 vehicles annually.
- The Group's investment in Georgia related to HMGMA and joint battery ventures with LG Energy Solution and SK On is $12.6 billion.
- This represents the largest single investment in Georgia's history and includes a total of 8,500 jobs at HMGMA by 2031.
- The Group’s total investments in Georgia are expected to create nearly 40,000 direct and indirect jobs and generate $4.6 billion in individual earnings every year, according to the Center for Automotive Research.
- As of July 2024, more than $2.5 billion in capital investment and approximately 6,900 jobs have been announced by HMGMA suppliers across 12 counties in Georgia.
- This supplier investment more than doubles the additional $1 billion investment originally anticipated.
- To date, Hyundai Motor Group has invested more than $20.5 billion in the U.S., creating or supporting over 570,000 jobs nationwide.
- The Group announced a significant investment of $21 billion in the U.S. from 2025 to 2028.
- Of this $21 billion commitment, $9 billion will be invested to establish an annual production capacity in the U.S. of 1.2 million vehicles.
- The Group plans to expand HMGMA's annual production capacity to a total of 500,000 units, an increase of 200,000 units from the original plan.
- Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Alabama and Kia Autoland Georgia will also see improvements in production facilities as part of this expansion.
- A total of $6 billion will be allocated to enhance the Group’s parts and logistics business and establish an Electric Arc Furnace-based integrated steel mill in the U.S. through its affiliate, Hyundai Steel.
- The new steel mill will have an annual production capacity of 2.7 million tons and will supply high-quality automotive steel plates to HMGMA when commercial production begins in 2029.
- An additional $6 billion will be invested to drive innovation and expand strategic partnerships with U.S. companies in autonomous driving, robotics, artificial intelligence (AI), and advanced air mobility (AAM).
- Through the $21 billion commitment, the Group anticipates creating 14,000 new direct full-time jobs in the U.S. by 2028.
- The overall economic impact of this commitment is expected to generate more than 100,000 direct and indirect job opportunities across related industries.
- HMGMA’s architecture blends advanced technology with design elements reflecting Hyundai’s brand identity and the natural beauty of the Savannah area.
- HMGMA integrated proof-of-concept manufacturing technologies and systems from Hyundai Motor Group Innovation Center Singapore (HMGICS), scaling them to accommodate larger production volume.
- All HMGMA processes, including order collection, procurement, logistics, and production, are optimized using AI and real-time data.
- Cutting-edge robotics and vision systems ensure high-quality vehicle production at the facility.
- HMGMA expands its mixed-model production capability with various robot processes to enhance automation, including automated guided vehicles for logistics, Boston Dynamic’s Spot for exterior quality inspection in the weld shop, and parking robots in the assembly shop.
- Automation in HMGMA’s assembly shop is nearly double that of the Group’s existing facilities.
- Oscar Kwon, CEO of HMGMA, stated that creating a comfortable and welcoming work environment for Meta Pros is as important as building an energy-efficient and technologically advanced plant.
- All work zones from office to production areas feature open layouts with ample daylight, indoor landscaping, and team centers with skylights to encourage communication and collaboration.
- The 3,050-foot-long front of the HMGMA building’s exterior is lit in two parallel lines inspired by a stream, allowing interstate travelers to view vehicles moving from paint to general assembly.
- A solar parking lot at the front of the site will generate 5.2 megawatts of energy, accounting for roughly five percent of the Metaplant’s total power consumption.
- The solar panels are installed above 1,878 parking spaces, providing shade while producing electricity.
- A 41-acre ecological park featuring trails, running tracks, sports fields, and picnic areas is under construction in front of the Metaplant for employee use.
- HMGMA has deployed Hyundai Motor XCIENT heavy-duty hydrogen fuel-cell electric trucks for clean logistics operations.
- A total of 21 XCIENT trucks are currently in operation, representing more than a third of the Glovis America truck fleet at HMGMA.
- These trucks transport vehicle parts from suppliers across the region to the Megasite daily.
- Since 2022, Hyundai and Genesis, along with various social responsibility initiatives and nonprofit organizations, have donated nearly $4.5 million to local organizations in Georgia.
- Kia's Autoland Georgia factory in West Point has provided over $22 million in support of Georgia communities since 2009, including more than $6 million for educational programs like the THINC College & Career Academy and SAE’s “A World in Motion.”
