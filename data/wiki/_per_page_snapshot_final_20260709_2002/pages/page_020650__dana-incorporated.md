# Dana Incorporated

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Dana To Combine With Eaton's Mobility Business](https://pulse2.com/dana-to-combine-with-eatons-mobility-business/)  ·  `page_020650`

## Facts

- Dana Incorporated announced a definitive agreement to combine with Eaton’s Mobility business in a transaction valued at approximately $5.1 billion.
- The combination creates a global powertrain systems company with an enterprise value exceeding $10 billion.
- The combined entity has estimated 2026 pro forma revenue of approximately $11 billion.
- The transaction is structured as a Reverse Morris Trust.
- Eaton shareholders will own at least 50.1% of the combined company following the close.
- Dana shareholders will own approximately 49.9% of the combined company following the close.
- The combined company will continue operating under the Dana Incorporated name after the transaction closes.
- The combined company will remain listed on the New York Stock Exchange.
- The deal is expected to close in the first quarter of 2027, subject to shareholder and regulatory approvals.
- The combination brings together Dana’s powertrain, thermal, and sealing technologies with Eaton Mobility’s commercial vehicle transmissions, engine and emissions systems, and electrification technologies.
- The expanded portfolio will serve commercial and light vehicle manufacturers as well as aftermarket customers.
- On a fully synergized basis, the combined company is expected to generate approximately $1.7 billion in adjusted EBITDA in 2026.
- Management expects an adjusted EBITDA margin of about 15% for the combined company in 2026.
- Management expects to achieve $250 million in annual run-rate synergies within 24 months after closing.
- Synergies will be achieved through purchasing efficiencies, manufacturing optimization, engineering improvements, and lower structural costs.
- Dana’s updated 2030 revenue target is now $14 billion to $15 billion.
- Dana’s updated 2030 adjusted EBITDA margin target is approximately 18%.
- Dana’s updated 2030 adjusted free cash flow margin target is 8% to 9%.
- These updated financial targets are significantly above the company’s previous targets.
- R. Bruce McDonald will serve as Executive Chairman of the combined company, overseeing integration and synergy realization.
- Byron Foster will become Chief Executive Officer of the combined company.
- Timothy Kraus will remain Chief Financial Officer of the combined company.
- The board of directors will include all current Dana directors along with three representatives designated by Eaton.
- Following completion, the combined company is expected to maintain a strong balance sheet with approximately 1.2 times net leverage on a fully synergized 2026 basis.
- Byron Foster, Incoming Chief Executive Officer of Dana Incorporated, stated that the transaction marks an important milestone in Dana's transformation and positions it as a leading provider of powertrain solutions.
- Byron Foster noted that expanding presence in core markets with new products and complementary technologies enhances the ability to deliver greater value to customers while strengthening margins through a more balanced portfolio and meaningful synergies.
- Byron Foster emphasized bringing together highly skilled teams whose expertise will drive future success and accelerating the execution of the Dana 2030 strategy.
- Paulo Ruiz, Chief Executive Officer of Eaton Corporation, stated that they are pleased to have reached the agreement, which delivers significant value to Eaton and its shareholders.
- Paulo Ruiz noted the agreement further aligns their existing portfolio with powerful megatrends and supports Eaton’s 2030 growth strategy to lead, invest, and execute for growth.
- Paulo Ruiz expressed confidence that the highly complementary combination will drive meaningful value for customers, employees, and shareholders alike.
- Timothy Kraus, Chief Financial Officer of Dana Incorporated, stated that the transaction meaningfully enhances Dana's long-term financial outlook and enables significantly increasing the Dana 2030 targets.
- Timothy Kraus noted that prior targets included approximately $10 billion in sales, 14% to 15% adjusted EBITDA margins, and a 6% adjusted free cash flow margin.
- Timothy Kraus stated that after funding the approximately $1.1 billion cash distribution to Eaton, they expect to maintain a strong balance sheet with approximately 1.2x net leverage on a pro forma 2026 estimated basis.
