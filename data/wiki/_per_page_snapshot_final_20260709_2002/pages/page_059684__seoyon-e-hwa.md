# Seoyon E-HWA

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hyundai Supplier Plans $76M Georgia Plant, Hiring 500 – Business Manager](https://bmhrmagazine.com/hyundai-supplier-plans-76m-georgia-plant-hiring-500/)  ·  `page_059684`

## Facts

- Seoyon E-HWA announced plans to invest $76 million in a new plant near Savannah.
- The new plant will manufacture interior and exterior parts for Hyundai's upcoming assembly plant in nearby Ellabell.
- Parts to be manufactured include door trim, headlining, and tailgate trim.
- Seoyon E-HWA plans to begin production at the new Georgia plant in October 2024.
- The company will hire more than 500 workers for the new facility.
- Seoyon E-HWA currently operates a plant with 630 employees in LaGrange, Georgia.
- The LaGrange plant is located near Hyundai's plant in Montgomery, Alabama, and Kia's plant in West Point.
- Seoyon E-Hwa operates 32 manufacturing units worldwide.
- Seoyon E-HWA is the fourth Hyundai supplier to announce plans for a southeast Georgia plant since May.
- Hyundai announced in May that it would build a $5.5 billion plant to assemble electric vehicles and batteries.
- Hyundai's upcoming Georgia plant could grow to employ 8,100 workers.
- Hyundai's Georgia plant is supposed to begin turning out vehicles in 2025.
- Hyundai Mobis announced plans for a $926 million powertrain plant in Richmond Hill that will employ 1,500 workers.
- Joon Georgia announced plans for a $317 million plant near Statesboro that will employ 630 workers.
- Ecoplastic announced plans for a $205 million plant employing 456 workers in Register.
- Governor Brian Kemp has proclaimed his goal for his second term is to make Georgia the leading state for electric-powered vehicles.
- Trip Tollison, president and CEO of the Savannah Economic Development Authority, stated the new jobs would be "well paying".
- Trip Tollison did not disclose specific wage details for the new positions.
- Seoyon E-HWA already imports parts through Savannah's port to supply its LaGrange plant.
- The state of Georgia will pay to train workers for the new facility.
- The total incentive package from state and local governments was not immediately clear at the time of the announcement.
- Seoyon E-HWA could qualify for more than $3.1 million in state income tax credits.
- The tax credits are valued at $1,250 per job over five years.
- To qualify for the tax credits, workers must make at least $31,300 a year.
