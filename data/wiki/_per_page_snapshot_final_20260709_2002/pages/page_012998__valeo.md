# Valeo

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-04-20

**Source:** [Valeo gives cautious 2022 outlook amid global uncertainties By Reuters](https://www.investing.com/news/stock-market-news/car-parts-maker-valeo-sees-lower-core-profit-margin-in-2022-2772070)  ·  `page_012998`

## Facts

- French car parts maker Valeo expects to report lower core profit margin in 2022 amid geopolitical tensions and inflationary pressures, following a 2021 margin that missed the group's target.
- RBC analyst Tom Narayan noted that Valeo's 2022 guidance was well below consensus expectations, consistent with cautious outlooks from Renault, Michelin, Faurecia, and Stellantis due to the global chip shortage.
- Valeo shares fell 10% in midday trade, heading for its worst day since March 2020.
- The company forecasts a 2022 annual core profit (EBITDA) margin between 11.8% and 12.3%, compared to 13.4% in 2021.
- Valeo projects 2022 sales between 19.2 billion and 20.0 billion euros, representing 11-16% growth year-over-year.
- The financial outlook excludes potential effects from the Ukraine-Russia crisis but includes a negative impact from the integration of Valeo Siemens eAutomotive.
- CEO Christophe Perillat stated the direct impact of the Ukraine-Russia crisis should be minimal, as the company has no sales in Ukraine and less than 1% of total sales in Russia.
- Valeo plans to increase original equipment sales by approximately 13% in the medium term, targeting 27.5 billion euros ($30.73 billion) in revenue by 2025 and 40 billion euros by 2030.
- The company specializes in the design, production, and sale of components and services for the automotive sector.
