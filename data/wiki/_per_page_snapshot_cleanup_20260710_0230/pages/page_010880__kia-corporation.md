# Kia Corporation

## Facts

- Kia Corporation is a South Korean multinational automobile manufacturer headquartered in Seoul, South Korea.
- It is South Korea's second-largest automobile manufacturer, after its parent company, Hyundai Motor Company, with sales of over 2.8 million vehicles in 2019.
- Kia is owned by Hyundai, which holds a 33.88% stake valued at just over US$6 billion.
- In October 2006, Kia Motors America broke ground for Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia in West Point, Georgia, representing a US$1 billion investment for the company.
- Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia opened in February 2010.
- In November 2009, Kia started production at the first U.S. Kia Motors plant, Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia, in West Point.
- The facility initially assembled only the Kia Sorento crossover vehicle.
- The facility expanded its production lines to include the Kia Optima mid-size sedan, now sold as the K5, in 2011.
- The facility added the Kia Telluride crossover SUV to its production lines in January 2019.
- As of September 2019, the Georgia location had successfully built over 3 million units of these three models altogether.
- The facility celebrated reaching its 10th year of production in November 2019.
- Currently, the facility has a production capacity of 340,000 vehicles per year.
- Production at the Georgia facility is largely dedicated to the Telluride and Sorento crossover SUVs.
- The facility distributes assembled vehicles to hundreds of dealerships in the U.S. and Canada.
- The facility fulfills shipments across North America and even overseas.
- Kia Motors America (KMA), now known as Kia America, Inc., was incorporated in California on October 21, 1992, and became the American sales, marketing, and distribution arm of Kia Corporation.
- Kia America is based in Irvine, California.
- Kia Canada has increasingly focused on selling electric vehicles and plug-in hybrids.
- Starting in 2005, Kia identified design as its "core future growth engine," leading to the hiring of Peter Schreyer in 2006 as chief design officer.
- Kia started using an angular "KIA" wordmark logo and removed "Motors" from its corporate name to become Kia Corporation from January 2021 onwards.
- Kia Motors has specialized in the production of military vehicles with variants and other transportation equipment since 1976.
