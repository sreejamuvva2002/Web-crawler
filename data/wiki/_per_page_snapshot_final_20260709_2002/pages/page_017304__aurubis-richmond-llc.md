# Aurubis Richmond LLC

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2024-09-20

**Source:** [We pursue growth - Aurubis Annual Report 2023/24](https://annualreport2023-24.aurubis.com/we-pursue-growth)  ·  `page_017304`

## Facts

- Aurubis Richmond in Georgia is the first recycling plant specialized in multimetal recycling in the US.
- Full operations have started at Aurubis Richmond LLC in Augusta, Georgia, with more than 160 people currently at work.
- Around 300 guests attended the symbolic Ribbon-Cutting Ceremony on September 20, 2024.
- Guests at the ribbon-cutting included Governor of Georgia Brian Kemp and Mayor of Augusta Garnett L. Johnson.
- The plant is a central building block in the Aurubis Metals for Progress: Driving Sustainable Growth company strategy.
- The US was identified as an attractive growth market for recycling during the development of the company strategy.
- Aurubis Richmond contributes to sustainability and supply chain security in the US economy by recovering valuable raw materials, primarily copper, which is on the Critical Materials List in the US.
- After the second stage is completed, Aurubis Richmond will be able to process more than 180,000 t of e-waste and other complex recycling materials.
- Inge Hofkens serves as COO Multimetal Recycling and closely supervised the project.
- Aurubis Richmond is the first greenfield project in 110 years of company history.
- Project Manager Hans Rosenstock emphasized the complete support from the Executive Board, Supervisory Board, and local contacts shaped the entire project.
- In the first half of 2020, a small core team started developing the technical concept and defining equipment needs for supplier bids.
- Site selection criteria included logistics for suppliers, a close port to link to Europe, secure energy supply at competitive prices, and availability of sufficient recycling materials.
- David Schultheis serves as President & Managing Director Aurubis Richmond.
- The COVID-19 lockdown presented challenges for potential site tours and establishing relations on the ground.
- American decision-makers showed keen interest in sustainable industrial value creation, economic growth, job creation, educational opportunities, raw material security, and climate protection, particularly in Richmond County, Georgia.
- The plans for Aurubis Richmond were presented to the Supervisory Board for approval on November 10, 2021.
- The company was officially founded as Aurubis Richmond LLC (limited liability company) with framework conditions negotiated with local authorities.
- Contracts were signed in Hamburg immediately after the Supervisory Board meeting, with equipment orders following a few days later in cooperation with manufacturer SMS.
- Groundbreaking occurred in June 2022 for construction on a greenfield site roughly 20 km southeast of Augusta.
- A completely new organization was established covering Occupational Safety, Plant Security, Finance, Purchasing, and Sustainability.
- The plant offers valuable educational opportunities and scholarships to train experts of tomorrow.
- A community benefit agreement was concluded with Augusta Technical College, Aiken Technical College, and the Richmond County School system for scholarships and vocational training programs.
- A recruiting event held in spring attracted 350 participants.
- The Supervisory Board approved the second stage in December 2022, initiating expansion planning while the first stage was under construction.
- In July 2023, project and operations were separated, with Dirk Wouters from Beerse managing construction and David Schultheis overseeing the operating team.
- The shredder facility and smelter furnace were commissioned along with the first deliveries of recycling materials.
- US First Lady Dr. Jill Biden visited the new plant in November 2023, and the topping out was celebrated.
- The plant is outfitted with cutting-edge technology complying with environmental standards set by the State of Georgia and federal authorities.
- Aurubis Richmond was planned as the first zero-discharge plant, capturing, cleaning, and returning all process water and rainwater to the cycle.
- By February 2024, the workforce grew from five employees to more than 100 in one year, increasing to 160 by the September ribbon-cutting.
- The company fosters a company culture through in-house and external employee events, team and charity events, blood drives, local Christmas parade participation, and college events.
- Aurubis CEO Dr. Toralf Haag stated the new site recovers strategically important metals for the American market, bolstering the independence of local supply chains.
- Smelting operations will gradually come online in a ramp-up curve following the commissioning of equipment in Module 1.
- While ramping up the plant step by step, the expansion Module 2 is being built.
- The site's unique scalability allows production to adjust to market demand, with additional components that can be added for a custom fit.
- The investment in Augusta amounts to around €740 million.
- Aurubis is targeting becoming carbon-neutral well before 2050 and produces blister copper.
- Inge Hofkens noted there is a growing market in the US for recovering valuable metals for the circular economy.
- David Schultheis highlighted that megatrends like electric vehicles and sustainability are highly relevant to the team's purpose.
- With the new site, Aurubis is now the largest multimetal recycling provider in the United States.
- The top blown rotary converter (TBRC) is the core plant technology used for processing complex recycling materials and blister copper.
- Aurubis expects an increase in electric and hybrid vehicle batteries to drive a growth market in recycling over the long term.
- Aurubis developed and tested a patented process for recovering metals from black mass, which contains lithium, nickel, cobalt, and manganese from disassembled end-of-life batteries.
- In its smelter network, Aurubis recovers around 95% of battery metals from black mass, surpassing EU guidelines for minimum recycling efficiencies.
- A demonstration plant for testing a subprocess on an industrial scale was set up at the Hamburg site in calendar year 2024, with its main unit 50 times larger than the pilot plant.
- Aurubis entered into a partnership with Talga Group Ltd., an Australian battery material and technology company, to extend Talga technology to all Aurubis graphite products.
- The hydrometallurgical recycling technique for black mass is innovative and patented, using ozone to process black mass without nickel by recovering lithium first.
- The hydrometallurgical process allows flexible use of raw materials, separation of graphite as an intermediate, and modularity compatible with other refining steps.
