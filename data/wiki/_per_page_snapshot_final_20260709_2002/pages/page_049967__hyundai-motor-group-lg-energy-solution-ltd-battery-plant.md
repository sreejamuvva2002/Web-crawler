## Facts

- ICE agents detained 450 illegal migrants at a construction site in Georgia on September 4.
- The detained group included hundreds of fly-in Koreans who likely misused B-1/B-2 business tourist visas to take jobs from American construction workers.
- More than 30o Koreans were arrested in the sweep, Korean news sites said.
- A giant Korean-owned battery factory is being built at the construction site in Ellabell, Georgia.
- The battery factory in Ellabell, Georgia, is being built for two Korean companies, Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution Ltd.
- More than 1,400 people are working at the site.
- South Korea’s government filed a sharp complaint regarding the detention sweep because of the ongoing battery factory construction.
- South Korean ministry spokesperson Lee Jae-woong stated that the economic activities of companies investing in the U.S. and the rights and interests of their nationals must not be unfairly violated, and conveyed concern through the U.S. Embassy in Seoul.
- Immigration expert Jay Palmer labeled Hyundai a "repeat offender" for using staffing companies to legally shield executives from immigration liability while flying migrants to job sites.
- Palmer noted that other companies, including Mercedes, Kia, and Tesla, also use staffing agencies and master service agreements to avoid responsibility for workers' immigration status.
- Palmer explained the economic motive behind hiring foreign workers at $5, $6, $7, $8 an hour without insurance instead of American workers at $20, $25, $30 an hour.
- The corporate outsourcing strategy clashes with President Donald Trump’s push to hire blue-collar Americans for skilled trades.
- Palmer warned that the Georgia raid will cost Hyundai a lot of money via construction delays.
- In 2020, Fox News reported on the widespread use of B-1/B-2 visas by Korean workers at another Georgia battery factory for SK Battery America.
- David Cagle with Georgia Local Union 72 complained that Georgians were told nothing was available when applying for construction jobs to build the SKI battery plant.
- Cagle stated that about 500 people out of work could have performed generic construction tasks like electrical work, ductwork, pipework, pipe fitting, and pipe welding at the plant.
- The recent detentions were made by ICE, with support from the FBI, ATF, and the Georgia State Patrol.
- In July, South Korea pledged $350 billion in U.S. investment to lower President Donald Trump’s threatened tariffs on its products, which he ended up setting at 15%.
- In March, Hyundai said it would invest $21 billion in U.S. onshoring from 2025 to 2028, a number it said last month had increased to $26 billion.
- Hyundai’s investment initiatives include a new $5.8 billion steel plant in Louisiana, expanded U.S. auto production capacity, and a state-of-the-art robotics facility.
- These Hyundai initiatives are expected to create about 25,000 new direct jobs in the U.S. over the next four years.
- Breitbart News has closely covered the illegal use of B-1 visas for foreign labor inflows into American trucking, technology, and construction industries.
- Foreign governments quietly back this labor travel activity to extract U.S. dollars in taxes and remittances from workers who enter legally but work illegally.
- Trump’s deputies have begun efforts to curb the misuse of B-1 visas, which are intended for tourists and visiting business executives.
