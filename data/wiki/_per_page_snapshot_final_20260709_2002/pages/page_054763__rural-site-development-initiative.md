# Rural Site Development Initiative

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp Announces $7.8M in Rural Site Development Grants Across Georgia - Expansion Solutions](https://www.expansionsolutionsmagazine.com/gov-kemp-announces-7-8-million-in-rural-site-development-grants-across-georgia/)  ·  `page_054763`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced the fourth round of grants from the Rural Site Development Initiative on June 30, 2026.
- The fourth round awards $7.8 million through the OneGeorgia Authority to support ten local projects across Georgia.
- Since the program launched in fiscal year 2025, Georgia has invested a total of $28 million in rural site development projects.
- Grants are designed to help communities prepare sites for future business investment, job creation, and long-term economic growth.
- Grant funds may be used for site improvement projects or to assist with costs related to Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) certification.
- Communities provide matching funds at various levels based on program criteria.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that over 64 percent of new jobs created have gone to parts of the state outside the metro Atlanta region since he took office.
- Governor Brian Kemp credited the General Assembly for funding and promoting the program and expressed hope for more jobs and investment in rural areas.
- Lt. Governor Burt Jones described the grants as a key component of Georgia’s economic development strategy, essential to growth, productivity, and sustainability.
- Lt. Governor Burt Jones commended Governor Kemp for leading the charge to fund and promote the program.
- Speaker Jon Burns emphasized the importance of the funding for rural communities, particularly in Southeast Georgia as the region responds to changes in the forestry industry.
- Speaker Jon Burns stated that the House is proud to support Rural Site Development Grants to bring good-paying jobs to hardworking Georgians across the state.
- Speaker Jon Burns noted pride in investments being made in Southeast Georgia as it navigates unprecedented changes in the forestry industry.
- Georgia Department of Community Affairs Commissioner Christopher Nunn said GRAD sites are important to local strategies for attracting employers and investment in Georgia’s communities.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that shovel-ready sites remain a major advantage in the state’s economic development portfolio.
- Commissioner Pat Wilson reported that over the last several years, GRAD sites have helped attract an estimated 10,000 jobs to communities across the state.
- The Effingham County Development Authority was awarded $1.8 million for site readiness improvements at the GRAD-certified Savannah Portside International Park.
- Effingham County's project includes construction of a sewer pump station with total estimated costs of $2.5 million.
- The Development Authority of Columbus was awarded $2 million for site improvement work at the GRAD-certified Muscogee Technology Park.
- Planned improvements for the Columbus-Muscogee County project include clearing and grading, with total estimated costs of $10 million.
- The McIntosh County Industrial Development Authority was awarded $1.5 million for road, water, and sewer infrastructure improvements at the GRAD-certified Tidewaters Industrial Park.
- Total estimated costs for the McIntosh County project are $2.8 million.
- This marks McIntosh County’s second site development award, following a previous GRAD-certification scholarship.
- The Thomaston-Upson County Industrial Development Authority was awarded $2 million to support water, sewer, and transportation infrastructure at the GRAD-certified Central Georgia Business and Technology Park.
- Total estimated costs for the Thomaston-Upson County project are $2.3 million.
