# UCB, Inc.

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2026-03-24

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp: UCB to Invest $2 Billion in Georgia, Establish First U.S. Manufacturing Facility | Governor Brian P. Kemp Office of the Governor](https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases/2026-03-24/gov-kemp-ucb-invest-2-billion-georgia-establish-first-us-manufacturing)  ·  `page_002487`

## Facts

- Global biopharmaceutical giant UCB, Inc. is planning a significant investment of $2 billion in Georgia to establish its first U.S. pharmaceutical biologics manufacturing facility.
- The investment will generate 330 new jobs over the next several years at the Rowen Foundation's state-of-the-art, 2,000-acre science and learning campus in Gwinnett County.
- Governor Brian Kemp met with UCB leadership earlier this year in Belgium to discuss how Georgia would be the right partner for their visionary plans in the U.S.
- The announcement represents one of the largest investments in state history and establishes both the Rowen facility and Georgia as a true hub of innovation in the life sciences field.
- UCB is a global biopharmaceutical company based in Brussels, Belgium, with North American headquarters located in Smyrna that currently support more than 400 jobs.
- UCB's expertise spans neurology and immunology.
- Jean-Christophe Tellier, CEO of UCB, stated the decision reflects confidence in UCB's long-term growth and deep-rooted commitment to the United States.
- By investing in Georgia, where its U.S. headquarters have been based for more than three decades, UCB is strengthening its biologics manufacturing capabilities and supporting its innovation pipeline.
- The project is expected to generate approximately $5 billion in total economic impact, reflecting the broader value it will create for the region and its communities.
- UCB's new manufacturing footprint will be located at Rowen, serving as an anchor tenant for this 2,000-acre planned community in metro Atlanta designed to foster collaboration, knowledge sharing, and innovation.
- The cutting-edge campus will use a digital-first approach by leveraging AI, robotics, and automation while prioritizing efficiency in the use of any natural resources.
- Nicole Love Hendrickson, Chairwoman of the Gwinnett County Board of Commissioners, stated an investment of this magnitude was exactly what they envisioned when committing to establish Rowen as a hub for collaboration and discovery.
- Gwinnett County connects UCB to a highly skilled, globally connected talent pool and is recognized as a partner in progress committed to world-class infrastructure and premier services.
- Katie Kirkpatrick, President and CEO of the Metro Atlanta Chamber, stated metro Atlanta is not just a hub for life sciences today but a place where breakthroughs of tomorrow are taking shape.
- Project Director EJane Caraway represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development's (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project in partnership with Partnership Gwinnett, Metro Atlanta Chamber, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia Power.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson highlighted that Georgia's growing life sciences ecosystem and collaborative approach to economic development connect companies with world-class partners in research, education, logistics, and infrastructure.
- UCB has been a leader in biopharmaceutical innovation for more than a century and is focused on the discovery and development of innovative medicines for severe diseases of the immune system or central nervous system.
- The company employs approximately 9,000 people across approximately 40 countries and generated revenue of €7.7 billion in 2025.
- UCB is listed on Euronext Brussels under the symbol UCB.
