# Rivian Georgia Manufacturing Facility

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-04-30

**Source:** [Rivian Georgia Plant Expansion 2026: US EV Boost - VFuture Media](https://vfuturemedia.com/electric-vehicles/rivian-georgia-plant-expansion-2026-ev-production/)  ·  `page_017592`

## Facts

- On April 30, 2026, Rivian announced its future manufacturing facility in Stanton Springs North, Georgia, will have an optimized Phase 1 production capacity of 300,000 vehicles annually.
- The updated Phase 1 capacity represents a 50% increase from the original 200,000-unit plan.
- The expansion comes alongside a revised $4.5 billion DOE loan, down from an original $6.6 billion figure.
- The DOE financing consists of a principal amount of $4.006 billion plus interest.
- Funds from the DOE loan will be drawable starting in early 2027.
- Vertical construction at the Georgia site is scheduled to begin in 2026.
- Vehicle production at the facility is targeted for late 2028.
- Groundbreaking for the facility occurred in 2025.
- Site preparation is currently ongoing.
- The location is situated in Stanton Springs North, near Social Circle and Atlanta.
- CEO RJ Scaringe emphasized bolstering US manufacturing jobs and maintaining American tech leadership regarding the expansion.
- The Georgia facility will focus on Rivian’s midsize and compact vehicle platforms.
- Primary models for Phase 1 production include the R2 SUV, R3 crossover, and R3X crossover.
- Powertrain options for the vehicles will include single, dual, or tri-motor configurations.
- Range targets reach up to 345+ miles on select R2 variants.
- The R2 model pricing strategy starts around $45,000–$57,990.
- The plant is expected to support up to 50,000 R2-based robotaxis through a partnership with Uber.
- Advanced manufacturing techniques from Rivian’s Normal, IL plant will be applied, emphasizing modularity, battery integration, and quality control.
- The project is expected to create thousands of direct manufacturing jobs plus thousands more in construction and supply chain roles.
- Estimates point to 7,500+ long-term positions in Georgia by 2030.
- Local suppliers for batteries, wiring, and components will benefit from the expansion.
- The move strengthens domestic supply chains and reduces reliance on Asian imports.
- Expanding manufacturing in Georgia hedges against tariffs, geopolitical risks, and logistics disruptions.
- Newer Rivian models feature native NACS ports for seamless access to the Tesla Supercharger network.
- The Rivian Adventure Network has expanded to over 960 chargers across 145+ sites.
- Adapters are available for CCS connectors and broader charging network compatibility.
- Home EV charger installation is eligible for a federal 30% tax credit.
- Federal EV tax credits remain up to $7,500 for buyers.
- R2 production is currently ramping at Rivian’s Normal, IL plant, with first deliveries underway or imminent as of 2026–2027.
- The Georgia facility coming online in 2028 could help Rivian achieve positive cash flow and margin improvements through scale.
- Potential for a full Phase 2 expansion, additional models, and greater robotaxi output exists in the longer term.
- Increased competition from Rivian’s expansion is expected to push Tesla, Ford, GM, and legacy automakers to accelerate affordable EV offerings.
