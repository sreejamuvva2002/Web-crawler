# Georgia Economic Development Announcements

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2025-10-26

**Source:** [Economic Development Around the State - Georgia Trend Magazine](https://www.georgiatrend.com/2025/10/26/economic-development-around-the-state-96/)  ·  `page_049650`

## Facts

- Georgia has been named the No. 1 state for business by Area Development for the 12th year in a row.
- During the fiscal year, there were more than 420 facility expansions and new locations, representing more than $26.3 billion in investment, a new record.
- Rivian officially broke ground in September on its $5 billion EV manufacturing facility.
- The Rivian facility will create more than 2,000 construction jobs and 7,500 permanent jobs by 2030.
- The Rivian facility will be developed in two phases with the start of production anticipated for 2028.
- When fully completed, the Rivian facility will have the capacity to manufacture up to 400,000 R2 SUV and R3 crossover vehicles a year.
- JS Link America Inc. is investing $223 million to establish a manufacturing facility in Columbus.
- The JS Link America facility will create more than 520 new jobs.
- The Korean biotechnology company’s 130,000-square-foot facility will make permanent magnets.
- Permanent magnets are a critical component for products in industries from automobiles and wind turbines to elevators and the medical industry.
- JS Link America operation will begin in late 2027.
- Koon Chun (USA), which makes condiments like soy sauce, is investing $70 million to open a facility in Hall County.
- The Koon Chun facility will create up to 50 jobs.
- The 41-acre site is expected to be operational in 2028.
- Sewon America is expanding in Effingham County.
- The Hyundai supplier makes electric vehicle body parts.
- The $50 million investment will add almost 100,000 square feet to its existing facility.
- The expansion will create 100 new jobs.
- Anheuser-Busch is investing $9.2 million to expand its Cartersville brewery.
- The expansion will increase production of Michelob Ultra, the country’s fastest-growing beer brand.
- The facility currently produces 48 different products.
- Global logistics and supply chain solutions provider MEDLOG recently celebrated the grand opening of its 300,000-square-foot cold storage facility in Rincon.
- The facility has rail siding capacity for 18 rail cars.
- The rail siding opens new markets in the Midwest for frozen beef and pork.
- The $65 million project will create more than 100 new jobs.
