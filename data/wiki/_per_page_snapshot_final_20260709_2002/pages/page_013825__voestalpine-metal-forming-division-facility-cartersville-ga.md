# voestalpine Metal Forming Division Facility (Cartersville, GA)

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- voestalpine is a global steel-based technology and capital goods group supplying high-performance metal solutions to automotive and industrial markets.
- The Georgia-based voestalpine Metal Forming Division facility in Cartersville manufactures ultra-high-strength automotive body-in-white components using precision metal forming, stamping, and assembly processes.
- The operation supports just-in-time delivery to automotive OEMs.
- The facility operates as a Tier 1 manufacturing environment focused on metal processing, operational performance, and manufacturing systems.
- Visitors observe roll forming, stamping, press operations, and die handling in action as high-strength steels are transformed into automotive structural components.
- Embedded quality checkpoints, measurement systems, and inspection protocols are used to validate parts against tight automotive specifications.
- Material flow, inventory staging, and takt-based work sequences are structured to support continuous throughput with minimal non-value-added time.
- The event targets Continuous Improvement/Lean Leaders, Operations/Plant Leadership, Manufacturing Engineers, Quality and Production Leaders, Supply Chain/Material Flow Analysts, and Metals Processing and Industrial Engineering Professionals.
- Practical examples of standardized work in a mixed-model press and forming environment are demonstrated as lean takeaways.
- Visual controls and error proofing are utilized as part of quality assurance routines.
- Flow sequencing is tied to production scheduling and Just-In-Time delivery.
- Techniques for integrating inspection into cell-level work without disrupting throughput are showcased.
- The tour agenda schedules registration and networking from 10:00–10:20 am, a host presentation and safety briefing from 10:20–10:40 am, the plant tour from 10:40–11:40 am, and a wrap-up from 11:40 am–12:00 pm.
