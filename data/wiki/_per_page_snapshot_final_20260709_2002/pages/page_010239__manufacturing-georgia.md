# Manufacturing Georgia

**Type:** workforce_training  ·  **Role:** workforce_training  ·  **Published:** 2025-02-27

**Source:** [GAM and Grenzebach Partner with Unmudl to Launch Innovative Digital Platform: Manufacturing Georgia](https://www.grenzebach.com/en/company/news-press/detail/gam-and-grenzebach-partner-with-unmudl-to-launch-innovative-digital-platform-manufacturing-georgia/)  ·  `page_010239`

## Facts

- The Georgia Association of Manufacturers (GAM) announced the development of “Manufacturing Georgia” on February 27, 2025.
- Manufacturing Georgia is the first-ever digital platform designed to help Georgia manufacturers engage directly with the young generation.
- GAM is developing the platform through a multi-year strategic partnership with Unmudl, a leading Skills-to-Jobs® Marketplace.
- The platform will feature dynamic, social media-like capabilities to connect manufacturers with future workers, especially younger audiences.
- The initiative's goals are improving public awareness and understanding of modern manufacturing and increasing the availability of skilled manufacturing workers within the next five years.
- Manufacturing Georgia will be available to all manufacturers in the state.
- Lloyd Avram serves as GAM’s President and CEO.
- GAM formed a focus group in late 2023 to brainstorm ways to engage the public, especially the younger generation.
- Unmudl was selected as the platform partner after a competitive review process.
- Stephanie Scearce is GAM’s Vice President of Workforce Innovation.
- Jim Lechleitner is Head of Strategy at Unmudl.
- The platform will feature real-time job postings, youth apprenticeships, work-based learning opportunities, training programs, and insights into modern manufacturing.
- Patrick Lenz, President and COO of Toyo Tires NA Manufacturing, voiced support for the initiative.
- Stuart Countess, President and CEO of Kia Georgia in West Point, expressed gratitude for GAM’s commitment to combating the stigma that manufacturing is dull, dirty, dangerous, and dead end.
- Brian Cooksey, Director of Workforce Development at Shaw Industries in Dalton, stated hope that the platform will engage young people with accurate information about advanced manufacturing career opportunities.
- Smaller manufacturers make up approximately 67 percent of Georgia’s manufacturing sector.
- Grenzebach emphasized that its team members are its greatest asset and that connecting with the next generation is key to manufacturers in Georgia.
- The platform will launch in phases, starting with a pre-screening event for stakeholders in March.
- A soft rollout to select manufacturers and education institutions will occur in April.
- Manufacturing Georgia will publicly launch in July.
- The initiative is supported by the Manufacturers Education Foundation and contributions from Toyo Tires, Kia Georgia, Shaw Industries, and Grenzebach.
- Manufacturers across the state will have free access to the platform in 2025.
- An affordable subscription fee for the platform will start in 2026, with users enrolling for free.
