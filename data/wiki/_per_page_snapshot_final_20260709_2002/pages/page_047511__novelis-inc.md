# Novelis Inc

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2024-01-01

**Source:** [Hindalco shares rally 5% as US subsidiary Novelis files for IPO, but not everyone is excited - CNBC TV18](https://www.cnbctv18.com/market/hindalco-share-price-rally-as-us-subsidiary-novelis-files-for-ipo-but-not-everyone-is-excited-19097731.htm)  ·  `page_047511`

## Facts

- Novelis Inc filed for an initial public offering (IPO) with the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC).
- The proposed IPO shares are expected to be offered by Novelis' sole shareholder, AV Minerals (Netherlands), a 100% subsidiary of Hindalco Industries.
- Novelis would not receive any proceeds from the sale of shares by its sole shareholder.
- Novelis expects to complete the public offering after the SEC completes its review process, subject to market and other conditions.
- The proposed Novelis IPO is unlikely to bring a re-rating for Hindalco Industries stock due to delays and an 65% upward revision in Novelis Bay Minette project capex and also a one-year delay.
- Domestic brokerage firm Nuvama Institutional Equities maintains a 'Hold' rating on Hindalco with a target price of ₹508.
- According to Nuvama, the money received may reside in Hindalco's 100% overseas subsidiary, AV Minerals (Netherlands) NV.
- It is still unclear how many shares the promoter shall sell along with the timeline too, but Nuvama believes it would take at least six months if everything goes smoothly.
- The usage of money received post share sale is unclear; it can either be used to grow inorganically or pay a one-time dividend and capital repatriation to Hindalco depending on tax implications.
- Hindalco already has net cash of ₹3,400 crore at the end of December quarter, and future capex can be met via internal accruals.
- At the end of the third quarter of this fiscal, Novelis had a net debt of $ 4.6 billion.
- With its ongoing capex, net debt is likely to increase to $5 billion by FY26-end.
- Hindalco’s consolidated net debt stood at ₹34,840 crore at the end of December.
- Nuvama assigns 6.5 times EV/EBITDA to value Novelis on FY26 earnings while its derived market cap is $9.6 billion (₹361 per share of Hindalco).
- Novelis’s closest competitor, Constellium, accounts for 40% of Novelis’s EBITDA and is listed on the New York exchange, trading at 5.3 times 2025 earnings per Bloomberg consensus.
- The brokerage states that as it has already provided a higher valuation multiple to Novelis, this event may not provide any case for a re-rating, and the listing of shares may attract a Holdco discount in the future.
- Nuvama added that the event can be sentimentally positive only.
