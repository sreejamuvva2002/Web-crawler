# JAC-GreenTech Automotive Partnership

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2013-04-22

**Source:** [China's JAC will launch its first electric car in the US](https://www.torquenews.com/1083/chinas-jac-will-launch-its-first-electric-car-us-partnership-greentech-automotive)  ·  `page_056428`

## Facts

- Anhui Jianghuai Automobile Co., Ltd (JAC) is seeking a US partner to bring an electric car to the United States.
- JAC partnered with GreenTech Automotive, a maker of neighborhood electric vehicles (NEVs) led by a group of politicians and political advisers.
- GreenTech Automotive describes itself as a "U.S.-based automotive manufacturer dedicated to developing and producing environmentally friendly, energy-efficient vehicles."
- GreenTech currently does not manufacture vehicles for US roads and highways; it only produces a limited-use, 2-seat NEV.
- GreenTech was founded by former Democratic National Committee Chairman Terry McAuliffe.
- Terry McAuliffe co-chaired Bill Clinton’s presidential run and chaired Hillary Clinton’s 2008 presidential run, and is running for Governor of Virginia.
- JAC has been in business since 1964 and claims the capacity to build 700,000 vehicles annually.
- The partnership aims to produce an affordable, 5-passenger electric sedan for sale in the US.
- The base vehicle for the new sedan will be the JAC Rejoice, which JAC calls "An electric car platform."
- The sedan's drivetrain will use GreenTech’s electric drive system featuring a 19kWh battery pack.
- The vehicle will have an approximate range of 100 miles.
- Charles Wang, Chairman and CEO of GreenTech Automotive, stated that JAC is recognized worldwide for manufacturing caliber and quality, and that GTA's innovative electric powertrain fits JAC's EV platform.
- Marianne McInerney, executive vice president of sales and marketing at GTA, commented that the five-passenger sedan complements their two-passenger MyCar product line.
- Marianne McInerney is a former Bush administration press officer for the Peace Corps, past President of the American International Automobile Dealers Association, and former VP of Sales at another EV company.
- Cerberus Capital Management, the former US based owner of Chrysler, included former US Vice President Dan Quayle as chairman of its Global Investments Division.
- US President Bill Clinton has been an advisor to GreenTech Automotive.
- GreenTech lists green technology, affordability, and job creation as its core values.
- The new vehicle is planned to be assembled at GreenTech’s Horn Lake, Mississippi facility.
- The partnership was announced at the Shanghai International Automobile Industry Exhibition.
- The car was slated for sale in late 2013.
- The timeline is considered ambitious given US testing and regulatory requirements for vehicles sold in the marketplace.
- The vehicle may be a Chinese-made car with an electric drive from GreenTech and a battery from a supplier, possibly Flux Power.
- If realized, the collaboration would be the first production Chinese EV sold in North America.
