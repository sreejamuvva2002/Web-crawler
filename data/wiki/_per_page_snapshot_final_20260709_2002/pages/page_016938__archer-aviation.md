## Facts

- Archer Aviation is scaling production activities across a 700,000 square foot footprint split between its San Jose facility in California and an upcoming manufacturing campus in Covington, Georgia.
- The Covington, Georgia manufacturing campus is being developed in partnership with Stellantis.
- The Georgia facility is expected to serve as the company's primary production site once fully operational.
- The Georgia facility will feature advanced automation and volume manufacturing capabilities designed to support mid-decade scale-up.
- Archer Aviation aims to reach a production goal of 50 Midnight eVTOL units annually by 2026, aligning its manufacturing footprint across California and Georgia.
- As of the third quarter of 2025, six Midnight units were under assembly with three nearing final buildout.
- Archer Aviation ended the third quarter of 2025 with more than $450 million in cash and short-term investments.
- The company's financial runway includes funds from strategic partners Stellantis and United Airlines.
- United Airlines has placed a conditional order for up to 200 Midnight aircraft, reinforcing long-term commercial demand.
- Archer Aviation is working closely with the Federal Aviation Administration to achieve type certification for the Midnight aircraft by late 2025 or early 2026.
- The company must deploy ground operations and training protocols for pilots, maintenance crews, and customer service before its 2028 target date for full network launch.
