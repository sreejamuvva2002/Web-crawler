# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-03

**Source:** [HMGMA and Kia America Celebrate Production of Kia Sportage Hybrid - HMGMA](https://www.hmgma.com/2026/06/03/hmgma-and-kia-america-celebrate-production-of-kia-sportage-hybrid/)  ·  `page_010041`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) and Kia America celebrated the start of production for the Kia Sportage Hybrid in Ellabell, GA.
- The Kia Sportage Hybrid production marks three major milestones for the facility: the first Kia model, the first hybrid electric vehicle (HEV), and the third vehicle to be produced at the site.
- The Kia Sportage Hybrid now joins HMGMA’s lineup alongside award-winning Hyundai IONIQ 5 and IONIQ 9.
- Production flexibility was built into HMGMA’s original design, enabling seamless integration of different powertrains with minimal modifications.
- The plant has the ability to produce up to 10 different vehicles.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp stated that mutual success is the direct result of a proven partnership approach creating long-lasting relationships between Kia America, HMG’s Metaplant, the local community, and hardworking Georgians.
- Kia North America and Kia America President and CEO Sean Yoon said the event marks a very important moment for Kia in Georgia and for customers across the US.
- Sean Yoon noted that Metaplant in Savannah is Kia’s second major investment in Georgia and testifies to confidence in the state’s future as an automotive powerhouse.
- Sean Yoon added that Metaplant will continue the strong momentum the Sportage Hybrid has achieved since being updated last year to quickly become one of Kia’s highest volume models.
- HMGMA President and CEO Tony Heo stated that through the dedication of Meta Pros and a flexible manufacturing system, they successfully prepared to produce their first hybrid vehicle and first Kia model.
- Tony Heo said today represents an important next step in building the future of mobility through world-class manufacturing in Georgia.
- The event expands Kia America’s nearly two-decade commitment to produce award-winning vehicles in the state of Georgia.
- Kia’s West Point factory currently assembles the all-new 2nd generation Telluride, all-electric EV9 and EV6, and internal combustion powered versions of the Sportage and Sorento SUVs.
- The celebration was attended by approximately 200 Meta Pros who shared in the milestone after extensive preparation for the new powertrain.
- Many team members traveled to Kia facilities in West Point, Georgia and South Korea to learn best practices for Sportage Hybrid production.
- Kia’s West Point facility currently employs more than 3,200 team members.
- HMGMA adds nearly 2,000 Meta Pros, with a very high percentage coming from the state of Georgia.
- Distinguished guests at the celebration included Georgia Governor Brian Kemp, Kia North America and Kia America President and CEO Seungkyu (Sean) Yoon, and Kia Georgia President and CEO Stuart Countess.
- During the ceremony, the first Kia Sportage Hybrid assembled at HMGMA debuted on an Autonomous Mobile Robot (AMR).
- Gov. Kemp rode in the vehicle as it was presented to highlight the innovation and technology embedded throughout the Metaplant.
- The vehicle was signed by special guests following the reveal.
- The addition of the Kia Sportage Hybrid expands HMGMA’s production portfolio across Hyundai Motor Group’s three brands: Kia, Hyundai, and Genesis.
- HMGMA continues to ramp up to a production goal of up to 500,000 vehicles annually.
- Future model plans for the facility are not being released at this time.
