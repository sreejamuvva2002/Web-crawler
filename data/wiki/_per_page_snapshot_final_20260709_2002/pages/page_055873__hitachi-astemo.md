# Hitachi Astemo

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2024-06-28

**Source:** [Hitachi Astemo is Developing 360-degree Camera, Semi-Active Suspension and Steer-by-Wire Technologies for EVs and Autonomous Vehicles | MOTOR](https://www.motor.com/2024/06/hitachi-astemo-is-developing-360-degree-camera-semi-active-suspension-and-steer-by-wire-technologies-for-evs-and-autonomous-vehicles/)  ·  `page_055873`

## Facts

- Hitachi Astemo is developing 360-degree camera, semi-active suspension and steer-by-wire technologies to improve electric- and autonomous-vehicle performance.
- Senior engineering and product-development executives reviewed Hitachi Astemo’s mobility-technology vision during a press briefing at the company’s headquarters for the Americas in Farmington Hills, Mich.
- John Nunneley serves as senior vice president of Design, Engineering and IT at Hitachi Astemo.
- Software-defined EVs and autonomous vehicles within the next five to 10 years will depend on the technologies currently under development at Hitachi Astemo, according to John Nunneley.
- Hitachi Astemo’s 360-degree Stereo Vision Camera System provides LiDAR-like results to support autonomous driving functions at one-tenth the cost, according to Harsha Badarinarayan, vice president of Advanced Engineering.
- While LiDAR has gained prominence in recent years, camera technology has steadily and quietly evolved to offset LiDAR’s advantages, including nighttime operation and the ability to sense the surrounding environment with greater fidelity, noted Harsha Badarinarayan.
- Carmakers like Tesla are committed to camera technology, according to Hitachi Astemo.
- The company’s vision system processes data from seven to 10 cameras to create a 3D view of the area surrounding a vehicle.
- Camera placement can vary according to a vehicle’s design using existing mounting points such as mirror, windshield and grille locations.
- In addition to autonomous driving applications, the camera system could have other uses including self-parking or the recording of a vehicle in operation.
- Hitachi Astemo’s next generation G5 Semi-Active Suspension improves primary ride balance while maintaining a focus on secondary ride comfort.
- The G5 suspension system consists of Astemo’s external-valve semi-active dampers at four corners, five accelerometers and Astemo’s internally developed Vehicle Dynamics Module (VDM) ECU.
- The latest-generation G5 system increases damping force capacity to ensure vehicles today and in the future have a high level of handling stability.
- The G5 system controls production-spec dampers based on sprung and unsprung inputs calculated using five G-sensors.
- Bi-Linear Quadratic control logic with piston speed dependent damping coefficient limitation can coordinate roll and pitch by independently controlling damping force to induce or resist pitch motion in turns.
- Astemo’s drivability control can coordinate roll and pitch by independently controlling damping forces to induce or resist pitch motion during turns, according to Harsha Badarinarayan.
- Hitachi Astemo’s Smart Steer-by-Wire System replaces the steering wheel with a hand-held device set in a front seat armrest.
- The steer-by-wire device makes turns with a natural turn of the wrist and is able to adjust wheel slip during adverse weather conditions and compensate when the vehicle goes off-center.
- Steer-by-wire technology allows Hitachi Astemo to literally reinvent the steering function by eliminating the steering wheel altogether, according to Harsha Badarinarayan.
- The steer-by-wire system is especially responsive to driver input with little or no lag from input to steering response, according to Harsha Badarinarayan.
