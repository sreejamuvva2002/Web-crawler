# Hyundai Glovis Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2001-02-22

**Source:** [Hyundai Glovis — Grokipedia](https://grokipedia.com/page/Hyundai_Glovis)  ·  `page_001198`

## Facts

- Hyundai Glovis is pursuing emerging ventures in waste electric vehicle battery handling.
- The company operates a subsidiary named Hyundai Glovis Georgia.
- Its rail network efforts include adaptations for EV battery and component handling.
- In December 2024, Glovis America supported the deployment of 21 Hyundai XCIENT heavy-duty hydrogen fuel-cell trucks at the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) in Georgia for inbound logistics.
- These trucks transported vehicle parts from regional suppliers to the on-site consolidation center and eventually the assembly line.
- In 2019, the company's PCTC MV Golden Ray capsized off Georgia, United States.
- The incident incurred extensive salvage efforts, environmental remediation, and regulatory fines exceeding $3 million due to improper stability calculations and cargo handling errors.
- The MV Golden Ray capsized on September 8, 2019, in St. Simons Sound near Brunswick, Georgia, USA.
- The vessel was a 200-meter-long roll-on/roll-off car carrier that departed from Colonel's Island Terminal carrying approximately 4,200 vehicles primarily from Hyundai and Kia.
- At the Glovis America facility in West Point, Georgia, 33-year-old worker Seth Dylan Holmes suffered a catastrophic fall on November 22, 2024.
- Holmes succumbed to his injuries days later on Thanksgiving.
- In early 2025, two workers died in separate preventable accidents at the Hyundai megasite in Ellabell, Georgia, within a span of less than three months.
- GLOVIS EV Logistics America LLC, a Hyundai Glovis subsidiary, was implicated as the employer in at least one case involving a conveyor installation mishap that severely injured a worker via crushing and internal trauma.
- The fatalities were linked to inadequate safeguards during high-pressure construction phases for the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America project.
- OSHA reports cited 12 injuries at the site since January 2023, some involving foreign workers.
- On September 4, 2025, an ICE raid at the Georgia Hyundai facility detained over 300 Korean workers, including those affiliated with Glovis operations.
- The detention occurred amid allegations of visa fraud, forced labor, and exposure to hazardous conditions that contributed to fatalities.
- Federal claims highlighted systemic safety lapses, with at least three companies tied to the project—including Glovis-linked entities—under scrutiny for a worker death probe.
- This raised questions about oversight in subcontracted logistics roles.
