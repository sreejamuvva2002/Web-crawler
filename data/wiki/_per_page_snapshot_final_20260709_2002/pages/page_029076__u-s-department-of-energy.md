# U.S. Department of Energy

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** research_and_development  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [DOE Announces $60 Million to Accelerate Advanced Vehicle Technologies Research | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/cmei/vehicles/articles/doe-announces-60-million-accelerate-advanced-vehicle-technologies-research)  ·  `page_029076`

## Facts

- The DOE Vehicle Technologies Office announced a $60 million Funding Opportunity Announcement (FOA) for batteries and electrification, new mobility systems, and other advanced vehicle technologies.
- The U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) announced up to $60 million in new and innovative advanced vehicle technologies research funding on December 10, 2020.
- The funding supports research aimed at developing more affordable, efficient, and secure transportation energy.
- The funding is provided through the Office of Energy Efficiency and Renewable Energy (EERE).
- Up to $35 million is allocated for Batteries and Electrification priorities.
- Research topics under Batteries and Electrification include advanced liquid electrolytes for lithium-ion cells under extreme conditions like extreme fast charging, mechanical, thermal, or electrical abuse.
- Research topics under Batteries and Electrification include novel liquid electrolytes for lithium-sulfur cells to improve overall cell stability and performance.
- Research topics under Batteries and Electrification include lithium-sulfur and lithium-air battery cell development.
- Research topics under Batteries and Electrification include high-power-density traction inverters for light-, medium-, or heavy-duty vehicle applications.
- Up to $5 million is allocated for Advanced Combustion Engines and Fuels priorities.
- Research topics under Advanced Combustion Engines and Fuels include developing simulation tools that couple engine combustion with aftertreatment systems to optimize near-zero exhaust emissions while maintaining or improving engine efficiency.
- Up to $11.5 million is allocated for Materials Technology priorities.
- Research topics under Materials Technology include the production demonstration of lightweight multi-material passenger vehicle glider systems.
- Up to $17.5 million is allocated for New Mobility Systems priorities.
- Research topics under New Mobility Systems include cooperative driving automation in vehicles enabled by low-cost infrastructure upgrades or novel applications.
- Research topics under New Mobility Systems include demonstrating new mobility systems technologies or practices in real-world transportation systems.
- Up to $1.2 million is allocated for Transportation and Energy Analysis.
- Some funded topics support the DOE Energy Storage Grand Challenge, leveraging DOE National Laboratories, universities, and industry to accelerate energy-storage technology development and sustain American global leadership.
- The application process consists of two phases: a concept paper and a full application.
- Concept papers are due on February 5, 2021, and full applications are due on April 7, 2021.
