# Brian Kemp

**Type:** person  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- Governor Brian Kemp has championed policies supporting charging stations and battery recycling as part of his clean-energy economy agenda.
- Hours before his second-term inauguration, Korean conglomerate Hanwha announced plans for a massive solar-panel facility in Georgia.
- The Hanwha announcement is the latest in a multibillion-dollar series of economic development trophies Governor Kemp has claimed in the energy sector.
- These investments include immense EV supply chain investments from companies like Hyundai, Rivian, and SK Battery.
- In his Thursday inaugural address, Kemp vowed that by the end of his new term, Georgia would be “the electric mobility capital of America.”
- Kemp plans to attend the World Economic Forum in Switzerland to promote Georgia's economic trajectory and electric-mobility prowess globally.
- Kemp states his agenda is focused on fulfilling a promise to create good-paying jobs for the state.
- He claims there are “a lot of conservatives that are driving electric vehicles.”
- He points to Ford’s F-150 Lightning electric pickup, predicting: “You’re gonna have a lot of Republicans driving that truck.”
- Kemp is critical of California's plan to end sales of gas-powered cars by 2035.
- Republican governors are deploying billions of dollars in state incentives to lure green-manufacturing business, and getting help from tax benefits and made-in-America rules enacted by President Biden and his party in the Inflation Reduction Act.
- Kemp criticizes federal incentives for “manipulating the market” and compares Biden’s manufacturing agenda unfavorably to Donald Trump’s use of tariffs.
- When challenged on his own subsidies, Kemp argues Georgia offers development deals within a balanced state budget, whereas Biden finances his by “running up the country's debt and deficits.”
- Kemp campaigned for reelection using a message of fighting crime, cutting taxes, and growing the economy with help from the electric car.
- He recently addressed a conference in Sea Island, Georgia, hosted by Heritage Action, arguing that Republicans “have to be for something.”
- Indiana Governor Eric Holcomb attended the COP 27 summit in Egypt to compete for green auto jobs.
- Ohio Governor Mike DeWine cheered Honda’s announcement of a $3.5 billion battery plant.
- Tennessee Governor Bill Lee unveiled plans for a cathode manufacturing facility backed by $3.2 billion from LG Chem.
- Kemp stated his intention is to serve his full second term as governor.
