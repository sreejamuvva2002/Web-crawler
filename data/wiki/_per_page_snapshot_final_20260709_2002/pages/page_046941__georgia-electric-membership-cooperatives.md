# Georgia Electric Membership Cooperatives

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [EMCs Receive $130 Million for Broadband Expansion - Georgia Electric Membership Corporation](https://georgiaemc.com/news/594945/EMCs-Receive-130-Million-for-Broadband-Expansion.htm)  ·  `page_046941`

## Facts

- A number of electric membership cooperatives (EMCs) in Georgia were awarded millions in funding for broadband expansion through the America Rescue Plan Act (ARPA), part of federal coronavirus relief funding allocated to Georgia.
- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp announced almost $408 million in preliminary awards to 49 recipients, including seven EMCs, to provide fast and reliable internet service to communities, households, and businesses in 70 counties in rural parts of the state.
- Collectively, seven EMCs will receive approximately $130 million to provide broadband service to approximately 65,000 customers across rural Georgia.
- Altamaha EMC received a $5.9 million grant with a total investment of $10 million to serve 2,171 customers in portions of Toombs and Montgomery Counties.
- Central Georgia EMC received a $25 million grant with a total investment of $150 million to serve 19,996 unserved customers across its 14-county service territory in partnership with Conexon Connect, with an estimated project completion in summer 2024.
- Diverse Power received a $25 million grant with a total investment of $39 million to serve 4,769 customers in portions of Harris, Troup, Meriwether, Quitman, and Coweta Counties as a subsidiary of Kudzu Networks, with an estimated project completion in 36 to 48 months.
- Flint Energies received a $25 million grant to serve 10,659 customers across portions of nine counties in its 17-county service area, with an estimated project completion in three to four years.
- Ocmulgee EMC received a $19 million grant to serve 9,000 customers in portions of Bleckley, Dodge, Laurens, Pulaski, and Telfair Counties as an LLC, with an estimated project completion in two to four years.
- Southern Rivers Energy received a $15.6 million grant with a total investment of $56 million to serve 10,674 customers in portions of Bibb, Crawford, Lamar, Monroe, Pike, Spalding, and Upson counties in partnership with Conexon Connect, with an estimated project completion in the first quarter of 2023.
- Washington EMC received a $13.2 million grant with a total investment of $52 million to serve 6,500 customers in portions of Glascock, Hancock, Warren, and Washington counties in partnership with Conexon Connect, with an estimated project completion in mid-2024.
- Georgia EMC President/CEO Dennis Chastain stated that the funding is a great day for EMC members and highlighted that rural communities have long waited for essential broadband service to compete in the digital world, with the pandemic shining a light on access disparities.
- Five additional EMCs supported 12 applications that led to an additional $172 million in funding and 62,000 customers gaining access to high-speed internet in rural Georgia.
- Amicalola EMC, along with Ellijay Telephone Company, supported the Gilmer/Pickens Development Authority application, securing a $21.4 million grant for 4,259 customers in Gilmer and Pickens Counties.
- Colquitt EMC supported applications from Berrien, Brooks, Colquitt, Cook, and Lowndes Counties in partnership with Windstream, securing grants of $15.2 million (4,737 customers), $13.8 million (3,749 customers), $22.5 million (7,448 customers), $10.9 million (3,723 customers), and $22.5 million (18,218 customers) respectively.
- Grady EMC supported Thomas County's application in partnership with Windstream, securing a $6.8 million grant for 2,098 customers.
- Oconee EMC supported Twiggs County's application in partnership with Windstream, securing a $4.6 million grant for 2,034 customers.
- Sumter EMC supported applications from Lee, Stewart, and Terrell Counties in partnership with Windstream, securing grants of $12.5 million (4,234 customers), $6.6 million (2,729 unserved customers), and $9.6 million (2,270 customers) respectively.
- Sumter EMC also supported the One Sumter Economic Development Foundation application, securing a $25.3 million grant for 5,726 customers with an estimated project completion in one to three years.
- Applications were reviewed by the Georgia Jobs and Infrastructure Committee created by Governor Kemp.
- Most recipients included local governments, non-profit organizations, and service providers ranging from large companies, EMCs, and local Georgia-based telecommunications companies.
- Since 2019, 19 EMCs have made broadband announcements with plans to provide approximately 250,000 EMC members with high-speed internet service in 86 Georgia counties through six affiliates and 14 partnership agreements.
- In just two years, EMCs combined have invested more than $680 million to expand broadband in their service areas.
- Georgia EMC is the statewide trade association representing the state's 41 EMCs, Oglethorpe Power Corp., Georgia Transmission Corp., and Georgia System Operations Corp.
- Collectively, Georgia's customer-owned EMCs provide electricity and related services to approximately 4.4 million people, nearly half of Georgia's population, across 73 percent of the state's land area.
