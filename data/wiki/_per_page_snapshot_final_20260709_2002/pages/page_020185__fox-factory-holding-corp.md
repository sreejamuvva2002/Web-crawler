# Fox Factory Holding Corp.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Fox Factory - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fox_Factory)  ·  `page_020185`

## Facts

- Fox Factory Holding Corp. is an American company headquartered in Duluth, Georgia, U.S.
- The company was founded in 1977 in California.
- Fox Factory is a public company.
- The company reported revenue of US$1.464 billion in 2023 and $205,278,000 in 2022.
- Fox Factory had 2,600 employees as of 2019.
- The company is best known for its FOX brand of off-road racing suspension components.
- Fox Factory produces suspension components for motorcycles, automobiles, all-terrain vehicles, side-by-sides, trophy trucks, snowmobiles, and mountain bikes.
- In 2008, the company was bought by a private equity firm, Compass Diversified Holdings.
- The company went public in 2013.
- In 2014, Fox Factory acquired Sport Truck USA in Coldwater, MI, including brands BDS Suspension, Zone Offroad, and JKS Manufacturing.
- In 2014, Fox Factory acquired Race Face and Easton in Vancouver, B.C., which manufacture and distribute high performance cycling components and apparel.
- In 2015, Fox Factory acquired certain assets of Marzocchi dedicated to mountain bike suspension.
- In 2017, Fox Factory acquired a majority share in Tuscany Motors, an Elkhart, IN vehicle upfitter focused on full-size trucks on Ford, Ram, and General Motors platforms.
- In 2019, Fox Factory acquired RideTech in Jasper, IN, a manufacturer of traditional, coilover, and air suspension systems for muscle cars, trucks, and hot rods.
- In 2020, Fox Factory agreed to acquire SCA Performance in Trussville, AL, a vehicle upfitter focused on full-size trucks on the Chevrolet, Ford, and RAM platforms.
- In 2021, Fox Factory acquired Outside Van in Portland, OR, a custom converter of adventure vans on the Mercedes Sprinter platform.
- In November 2023, Fox Factory acquired Marucci Sports in Baton Rouge, LA, a sports apparel manufacturer primarily known as a bat supplier to Major League Baseball, for $572 million.
- In 2016, sales of mountain bike related equipment accounted for 56% of the company's total revenue.
- As of January 2025, Fox Factory offers fork models (Fox 32, 34, 36, 38, and 40) with travel ranges from 40mm to 203mm depending on the model and damper options like Grip/Grip2, Fit GRIP/Fit4, and GripX/X2.
- As of January 2025, Fox Factory offers rear shock models including Fox Float SL, Float, Float X, DHX, Float X2, and DHX2 for various use cases from cross country to gravity riding.
