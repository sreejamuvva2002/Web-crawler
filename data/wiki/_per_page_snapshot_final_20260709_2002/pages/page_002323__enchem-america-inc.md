# Enchem America Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Governor Lee, Commissioner McWhorter Announce Enchem America Inc. to Locate US Manufacturing Operations in Brownsville – Haywood County, Tennessee](https://haywoodtn.gov/governor-lee-commissioner-mcwhorter-announce-enchem-america-inc-to-locate-us-manufacturing-operations-in-brownsville/)  ·  `page_002323`

## Facts

- Enchem America Inc. is a leading electrolyte manufacturer selecting Tennessee for its second U.S. facility.
- The company will invest $152.5 million to establish electrolyte manufacturing operations in Brownsville, Tennessee.
- The new facility is located at the I-40 Advantage Industrial Park, a Select Tennessee Certified Site.
- The project will create 190 new jobs in Tier 4, At-Risk Haywood County.
- The Brownsville facility will operate similarly to Enchem’s U.S. headquarters in Commerce, Georgia.
- The facility will manufacture electrolytes for electric vehicle (EV) batteries as well as other EV battery-related services.
- Enchem America Inc. is a subsidiary of Enchem Co., Ltd., which is based in South Korea.
- The company specializes in the development and manufacturing of electrolytes and other additives for rechargeable batteries.
- With the additional Tennessee operations, Enchem will be able to further support its EV partners across the Southeast.
- Since 2019, TNECD has supported more than 50 economic development projects in the Southwest Tennessee region, resulting in nearly 9,300 job commitments and $7.2 billion in capital investment.
- Gov. Bill Lee stated that Enchem’s decision to invest more than $150 million in Tennessee is a testament to the state's unmatched business climate, highly skilled workforce, and position as a leader in the automotive industry.
- TNECD Commissioner Stuart McWhorter noted that South Korea is one of Tennessee’s top 10 foreign direct investment partners and that Enchem is boosting the EV supply chain across Tennessee.
- Enchem Co. Ltd. CEO and founder Jung Kang Oh expressed gratitude to Haywood County, the City of Brownsville, and professionals for assisting Enchem's expansion in North America.
- Jung Kang Oh stated that the growth of the EV market has been remarkable and indications suggest it will continue at an accelerated pace over the coming decade.
- Jung Kang Oh expressed confidence that Enchem is well positioned to capitalize on EV market growth and look forward to exploring innovation and partnership opportunities with Haywood County and the City of Brownsville.
- Haywood County Mayor David Livingston thanked Enchem for picking Haywood County and Brownsville as home to its newest location.
- Mayor Livingston stated the economic opportunity will help transform Haywood County and all West Tennessee with great, new high-paying jobs.
- Brownsville Mayor Bill Rawls welcomed Enchem America to the City of Brownsville.
- Mayor Rawls stated the announcement brings to fruition years of work and investment in I-40 Advantage Industrial Park fulfilling the goal of bringing high-quality job opportunities to the city.
- TVA senior vice president of economic development John Bradley congratulated Enchem America on its decision to establish operations in Haywood County.
- John Bradley stated TVA is proud to partner with HTL Advantage, Brownsville-Haywood County Industrial Development Corporation, City of Brownsville, and the Tennessee Department of Economic and Community Development to support companies creating jobs and investment in the region.
- Sen. Page Walley (R-Savannah) welcomed Enchem America to Haywood County.
- Sen. Walley stated the investment will bring many jobs, boost the regional economy, and further establish the community as a hub of innovation at the forefront of this rapidly growing industry.
- Rep. Johnny Shaw (D-Bolivar) noted that the prospect of a more than $152 million investment is another example of unprecedented growth in Brownsville, Haywood County, and West Tennessee.
- Rep. Shaw stated that BlueOval City lit the fuse for what continues to be an economic explosion for the area.
