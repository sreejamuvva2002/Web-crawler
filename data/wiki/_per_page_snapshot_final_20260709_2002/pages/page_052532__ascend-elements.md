# Ascend Elements

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-06

**Source:** [Ascend Elements secures $300 million in funding, began building plant for EV sustainable battery materials](https://evmagz.com/ascend-elements-secures-300-million-in-funding-began-building-plant-for-ev-sustainable-battery-materials/)  ·  `page_052532`

## Facts

- Ascend Elements, a U.S.-based engineered materials and lithium-ion battery recycling company, secured $300 million in equity and debt financing.
- The funding includes $200 million in Series C equity investments from an international group of strategic and financial investors.
- The funding round was led by Fifth Wall Climate and joined by SK ecoplant, the environmental unit of South Korean conglomerate SK Group.
- Other new investors include Oman Investment Authority, Lithium Americas Corporation, GLy Capital Management’s New Mobility Fund, Mirae Asset Capital & LS and Shinhan GIB.
- Previous investors participating in the round include Hitachi Ventures, Jaguar Land Rover’s InMotion Ventures, TDK Ventures, Orbia Ventures, At One Ventures, TRUMPF Venture, and Doral Energy-Tech Ventures.
- The funding is in addition to two recently awarded grants totaling $480 million from the Department of Energy.
- Ascend Elements plans to invest up to $1 billion to build a sustainable lithium-ion battery materials facility in Hopkinsville, Ky.
- The first-of-its-kind manufacturing facility, named “Apex 1”, will produce enough lithium-ion battery pCAM and sustainable CAM to equip up to 250,000 electric vehicles per year.
- The funding will accelerate commercialization of Ascend Elements’ proprietary Hydro-to-Cathode™ direct precursor synthesis process, establishing a closed-loop, EV battery materials supply chain in North America.
- Ascend Elements is already generating revenue and processing end-of-life batteries and manufacturing scrap at its “Base 1” facility in Georgia.
- The Georgia Base 1 facility will have 30,000 metric tons of EV battery recycling capacity by the end of 2022.
- The Georgia facility will ultimately feed Apex 1 in Kentucky.
- Ascend Elements expects to recycle more than 150,000 metric tons of lithium-ion batteries per year globally by 2026.
- The company transforms recycled batteries into high-performance, customized, EV battery cathode active material precursor (pCAM) and cathode active materials (CAM) that meet or exceed top-tier battery manufacturer requirements.
- CEO Mike O’Kronley stated the company’s patented closed-loop process goes beyond simple recycling to produce sustainable, high-performance cathode active materials for new EV batteries.
- Fifth Wall Partner Peter Gajdoš said securing recyclability of critical metals is essential to achieve energy independence from fossil fuels and supports creating sustainable batteries for automotive and stationary storage.
- SK ecoplant CEO Park Kyung-il stated the investment continues their strategic partnership and lays the foundation for SK ecoplant as the first shareholder to dominate the global battery recycling industry.
- Oman Investment Authority Director Ibrahim Al Eisri stated the partnership aligns with OIA’s strategy and Oman’s Vision 2040 priorities to transform the lithium-ion battery recycling industry globally.
