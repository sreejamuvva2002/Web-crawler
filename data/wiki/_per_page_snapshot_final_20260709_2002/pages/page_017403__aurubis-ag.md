# Aurubis AG

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2022-06-17

**Source:** [WebDisclosure](https://www.webdisclosure.com/finance/stocks/norddeutsche-affinerie-ag/news/1083389.html)  ·  `page_017403`

## Facts

- On June 17, 2022, 150-year-old German-based Aurubis AG broke ground on the United States' first large secondary smelter for multimetal recycling in Augusta, Georgia.
- Total investment for the new facility in Richmond County, Georgia, is estimated at €300 million (approx. $320 million).
- The plant is expected to be fully operational by mid-2024 or the first half of 2024.
- Once fully on-line, Aurubis Richmond will have the capacity to process about 90,000 tons of complex recycling materials annually.
- The processed materials include printed circuit boards, copper cables, and other recycling materials containing metals such as copper, nickel, tin, and other industrial and precious metals.
- The facility will create over 120 new jobs directly in the greater Augusta area.
- Aurubis AG CEO Roland Harings stated that the US currently produces about 6 million tons of recycling materials containing valuable metals, which the company aims to capture for local reuse instead of shipping them to Asia and Europe or trucking them to landfills.
- Roland Harings noted that current metal supply cannot meet demand, particularly for copper needed for cars, mobile phones, batteries, cables, and computers, making Aurubis' Augusta facility a game changer for manufacturers and the transition to a renewable economy.
- The new plant will reduce waste and direct key metals to industries including solar, wind, electric vehicle, and battery manufacturers.
- Aurubis is a leading global provider of non-ferrous metals that processes complex metal concentrates, scrap metals, organic and inorganic metal-bearing recycling materials, and industrial residues into high-quality metals.
- Globally, Aurubis produces more than 1 million tons of copper cathodes annually, along with other metals including precious metals, selenium, lead, nickel, tin, and zinc.
- The facility's state-of-the-art environmental technology meets or exceeds the highest State of Georgia and federal government environmental standards.
- Aurubis Richmond will have a very low impact on regional air quality, accounting for less than 0.2 percent of dust emissions in Richmond county.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp called the project one of the biggest international investments in state history and the largest single investment made by a German company in Georgia history.
- Aurubis AG employs approximately 7,200 people worldwide and operates production sites in Europe and the US.
