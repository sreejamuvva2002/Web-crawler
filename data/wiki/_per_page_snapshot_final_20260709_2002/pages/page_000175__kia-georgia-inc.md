# Kia Georgia, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-07-12

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp: Kia to Invest Over $200 Million in EV9 Production Expansion | Governor Brian P. Kemp Office of the Governor](https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases/2023-07-12/gov-kemp-kia-invest-over-200-million-ev9-production-expansion)  ·  `page_000175`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced on July 12, 2023 that Kia will invest more than $200 million at its West Point plant.
- The investment will create nearly 200 new jobs at the West Point plant.
- The funding accommodates assembly of the company’s all-electric 2024 EV9 three-row SUV.
- EV9 assembly is expected to begin in the second quarter of 2024.
- The EV9 will become the fifth model assembled at Kia Georgia, joining the Telluride, Sorento and Sportage SUVs, and the K5 mid-size sedan.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Georgia’s longstanding partnership with Kia has led to generational job creation and growth for the West Point area.
- Governor Kemp noted the project will help propel the state toward its goal of becoming the e-mobility capital.
- Currently, more than 40 percent of Kia vehicles sold in the U.S. are manufactured in West Point.
- The addition of the EV9 to the lineup will further increase the facility’s output.
- The EV9 is the brand’s first three-row EV SUV and the first Kia EV to be assembled in North America.
- The EV9 serves as the flagship vehicle for Kia’s global Plan S strategy focused on sustainable mobility leadership.
- Sean Yoon, President & CEO of Kia Motors North America and Kia Motors America, stated the EV9 has the potential to be another change catalyst for Kia.
- Sean Yoon described the EV9 as the most innovative vehicle Kia has ever built and a standout in the EV market.
- Kia Georgia is a certified Georgia Made manufacturer that will utilize services from Georgia Quick Start, the nation’s top-ranked workforce development program.
- Georgia Quick Start operates the Kia Georgia Training Center to provide comprehensive, customized training for new employees on the new assembly line.
- Stuart Countess, President & CEO of Kia Georgia, stated the team looks forward to beginning production of its first EV.
- Stuart Countess highlighted partnerships with the Governor’s Office, the Department of Economic Development, Georgia QuickStart, the City of West Point, and Troup County.
- City of West Point Mayor Steve Tramell stated Kia has been an outstanding corporate citizen and community partner for seventeen years.
- Mayor Tramell noted Kia has improved livelihoods in West Point through employment opportunities and economic growth.
- Amanda Fields, Senior Regional Project Manager, represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s Global Commerce team on the project.
- The project was executed in partnership with the West Point Development Authority, the City of West Point, Troup County, Georgia Center of Innovation’s Manufacturing and Energy teams, and Georgia Quick Start.
- Pat Wilson, Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner, stated Kia has exceeded expectations since being recruited by Georgia in 2006.
- Commissioner Wilson thanked Kia and partners for meeting the needs of today and tomorrow’s leading industries.
- To date, Kia has invested more than $1.9 billion in the state of Georgia.
- Kia Georgia is responsible for more than 14,000 plant and supplier jobs in the region.
- Governor Kemp and state lawmakers recognized January 31 as “Kia Day” earlier this year to commend Kia’s vehicle manufacturing and impact.
- The 2006 announcement of a state-of-the-art vehicle assembly facility in West Point was one of the biggest single foreign investments in Georgia’s history.
- From Georgia, Kia ships vehicles to sales markets globally, including the United States, Canada, Mexico, the Caribbean, Middle East and Pacific nations.
- Since 2020, Georgia has pursued job creation along the entire EV supply chain, resulting in more than $22.7 billion in investment.
- This state-wide supply chain effort has created over 28,400 jobs since 2020.
- Kia Georgia, Inc. is the first manufacturing site in North America for Seoul-based Kia Corporation.
- Kia Georgia has an annual capacity of 340,000 units and is located on 2,200 acres in West Point, Georgia.
- The facility began mass production on November 16, 2009.
- Kia America is headquartered in Irvine, California.
- Kia offers a range of gasoline, hybrid, plug-in hybrid and electrified vehicles sold through over 750 dealers in the U.S.
- The K5, Sportage, Sorento, and Telluride (excludes HEV and PHEV models) are assembled in the United States from U.S. and globally sourced parts.
- The EV9 is expected to be assembled in the United States from U.S. and globally sourced parts.
