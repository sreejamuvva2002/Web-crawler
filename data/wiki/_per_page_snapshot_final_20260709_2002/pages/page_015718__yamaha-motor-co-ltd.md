# Yamaha Motor Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-03

**Source:** [Yamaha to Move HQ to Georgia - Savannah CEO](https://savannahceo.com/news/2026/03/yamaha-move-hq-georgia/)  ·  `page_015718`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp welcomed the announcement that Yamaha Motor Co., Ltd. has officially decided to move its U.S. headquarters to Kennesaw, Georgia.
- The company is relocating its U.S. headquarters out of California following roughly 50 years of operation there.
- The relocation decision followed a meeting between Governor Brian P. Kemp and Yamaha executives in October 2025 at the Southeast U.S./Japan (SEUS-Japan) annual gathering in Tokyo.
- Yamaha Motor Co., Ltd. manufactures ATVs, boat engines, personal watercraft, and other motorized products.
- The company currently employs over 2,300 Georgians across its operations in the state.
- The relocation of the Yamaha Motor Corp. U.S. headquarters will begin in 2026 and is expected to continue through 2028.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated, "After many years of great partnership, we are honored and proud to welcome Yamaha's American headquarters to the No. 1 state for business."
- Governor Brian Kemp noted, "This is another loud and clear testament to what we offer job creators from around the world."
- The 1.3-million-square-foot Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation of America factory in Newnan opened in 1986.
- The Newnan factory currently employs roughly 2,000 Georgians.
- Yamaha relocated its marine division to Kennesaw in 1999.
- The company expanded its Kennesaw facility with a 75,000-square-foot Marine Innovation Center in 2023.
- In 2019, the company moved its motorsports operations to Georgia to benefit from close proximity to its existing facilities.
