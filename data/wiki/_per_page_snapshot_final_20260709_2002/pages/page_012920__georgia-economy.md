# Georgia Economy

**Source:** [Georgia economy expected to grow next year, but at a slower rate – Albany Herald](https://albanyherald.com/news/georgia-economy-expected-to-grow-next-year-but-at-a-slower-rate/)  ·  `page_012920`

## Facts

- Georgia’s economy is projected to grow by 2.4% in 2025, a slowdown from the 3.1% the state saw in 2024.
- The state’s GDP is projected to grow faster than the nation’s, which forecasters project to grow by 1.6% in 2025.
- The major driver of Georgia’s economic slowdown is the Federal Reserve’s 2022 move to constrain lending to control inflation.
- Inflation successfully shrunk from 8% in 2022 to 3% in 2024 and is projected at 3% again in 2025.
- The Federal Reserve began reducing monetary policy restraint by cutting policy interest rates in September, with additional rate cuts expected in 2025.
- Quantitative tightening is expected to end as credit and money supply expand moderately.
- The probability Georgia will slip into recession is 25%.
- The number of jobs in Georgia will rise by 1% in 2025, slower than the 1.5% gain estimated for 2024 but higher than the national job growth rate of 0.6%.
- Georgia’s unemployment rate is poised to increase from 3.7% to 4%, lower than the 4.3% expected at the U.S. level, due to slowed hiring rather than layoffs.
- Health care and housing will lead Georgia’s economic growth in 2025.
- Homebuilders will ramp up construction of single-family homes in 2025 by 9% due to slightly lower mortgage rates, favorable demographic trends, and a post-pandemic housing shortage.
- Home prices, which have increased 65% since before the COVID-19 pandemic, are expected to hold steady.
- Georgia is likely to see job losses in retail and information jobs due to advances in technology and competition from online retailers.
- Georgia has broken records in the number of new jobs and industries attracted to the state, though this will slow this year due to the national and global economic slowdown.
- With the build-out of existing projects, Georgia is poised to see thousands of new jobs created over the next few years.
- Georgia’s advantages for economic development include a favorable mix of industries, supportive demographics, excellent transportation and logistics infrastructure, a competitive tax climate, and low costs of living and doing business.
