# Pirelli & C SpA

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [A stronger grip on the market | Feature | Automotive Logistics](https://www.automotivelogistics.media/supply-chain/a-stronger-grip-on-the-market/212766)  ·  `page_048548`

## Facts

- Pirelli & C SpA is based in Milan, Italy and is the world’s fifth largest tyre manufacturer.
- The company operates 22 factories in 13 countries around the world.
- North America is one of its fast growing markets for both OEM and replacement tyre sales.
- Pirelli increased production of tyres within North America to improve customer service rates and reduce logistics costs.
- John Godfrey serves as vice-president of logistics and purchasing at Pirelli Tire North America.
- The company shifted to more land-based transport, especially intermodal, while noting a need to expand logistics capacity from truck drivers to warehouses and rail transport.
- Pirelli re-engineered its logistics services around direct deliveries and faster response times for retail customers in the US and Canada.
- There is higher demand for smaller and more frequent deliveries, including next day and second-day delivery, for US and Canadian retail customers.
- Increased sourcing within North America improved logistics cost and delivery performance.
- An increase in Mexican production capacity and exports to the US decreased overseas container ocean imports relative to overland imports.
- The reduction of transit time increased replacement tyre customer fill rates from 60% to 85%.
- Within the NAFTA region, Pirelli has a tyre plant in Rome, Georgia – near Atlanta.
- Pirelli serves two primary distribution channels for finished products: OEMs for vehicle production and the replacement tyre segment for the aftermarket.
- The company saw strong sales growth in both retail and OEM segments in North America in recent years.
- Pirelli expects close to double digit growth compared to 2013 sales.
- Pirelli’s tyre sales of approximately $871.4m in North America last year accounted for 11% of its global sales.
- Despite regional production expansion, Pirelli still imports most of its tyres from overseas, about double the amount compared to regional production.
- Most finished tyre imports arrive in the NAFTA region by ocean container from Brazil, China, Romania, and Germany.
- The company has a large share of raw material suppliers in Europe and Asia.
- For the Silao plant, the majority of rubber used in production is from Southeast Asia.
- Pirelli is working to develop more regional suppliers, including natural rubber in Mexico and Guatemala, and carbon black from Mexico and China.
- The company's desire is to have everything in North America.
- Potential re-introduction of tariffs on Chinese tyre imports to the United States could reach as high as 90%, making it very expensive to import Chinese tyres and likely requiring a change in sourcing.
- Both North American and imported finished tyres currently move through seven distribution centres in North America.
- In the US, Pirelli has distribution centres in Rome and McDonough, Georgia, and Fontana, California.
- Two of the US distribution centres are operated by Kuehne + Nagel.
- In Canada, Pirelli has warehouses in Brampton, Ontario and Vancouver, British Columbia, operated by Kuehne + Nagel and DB Schenker respectively.
- In Mexico, facilities are located in Silao and Mexico City.
- An upcoming tender may result in another warehouse in the US midwest or northeast.
- For customers in the US and Canada, Pirelli ships mainly by full truckload from its in-country distribution warehouses.
- Outbound OEM shipments go to vehicle assembly plants, fitters that attach tyres to wheels, or sequencers.
- For Ford’s Kansas City plant, Pirelli ships tyres to Ceva Logistics, which sequences them for the carmaker.
- From the Rome, Georgia plant, approximately 60% of finished tyres are destined to OEM plants.
- The majority of tyres from the Rome plant go to Mercedes-Benz and BMW, as well as Chrysler and Ford.
- From Mexico to the US, Pirelli mainly uses Kansas City Southern Railway through Texas, then ships to its warehouse in McDonough.
- Ocean freight coming to McDonough arrives at the ports of Savannah, Georgia and Charleston, South Carolina.
- For the Fontana warehouse, shipments use the ports of Los Angeles and Long Beach.
- For Brampton, shipments use Montreal, Vancouver, and Philadelphia primarily.
- Pirelli aims to maximize direct delivery volumes to North American customers.
- The company ships directly from its Rome plant to OEM customers whenever possible.
- In August, Pirelli began shipping directly from its Silao plant to Ford’s Flat Rock, Michigan plant.
- Pirelli does not ship directly to Mercedes-Benz in Alabama or BMW in South Carolina from its plant; many of their tyres come from the UK, Germany, Romania, and Mexico.
- The company builds trucks with multiple sequenced SKUs at its McDonough warehouse for these OEMs.
- Some replacement tyre volume ships from overseas factories directly to US customers’ distribution centres.
- Pirelli strives to balance costs with service levels, particularly for retail customers demanding shorter transit times compared to wholesalers.
- The company is pushing more business to retailers rather than wholesalers for its aftermarket business due to shorter cycle times.
- Pirelli delivers to four national crossdocks operated by Exel for its largest replacement tyre customer, Discount Tire, shipping one or two trucks per day.
- In 2012, Pirelli introduced a ‘Tier 1’ programme, a make-to-order system for delivering replacement tyres to Dealer Tire and Tire Rack.
- The Tier 1 programme includes a collaborative planning forecasting and replenishment system to reduce warehouse and pipeline inventories.
- Pirelli hopes to expand the Tier 1 initiative to other North American customers, requiring sufficient volume such as one container per month.
- Production and OEM customer growth in Mexico led to a higher share of intermodal transport from Mexico to the US, currently about 75% by intermodal container and 25% by truck.
- Passenger and light truck tyres require special pallets or racks for transport.
- Pirelli is investing in new pallets in the NAFTA region and designing an optimal pallet using specialised steel and plastic globally.
- The product portfolio has shifted toward premium tyres with larger diameters, requiring effective racking accommodations.
- In Canada, some deliveries use racks that fit into 53ft trailers.
- Intermodal transportation is half the cost of truck transportation, with a target intermodal percentage of 80-90%.
- Mexico’s growth increased the number of intermodal carriers used along with Mexico’s two Class-A railways: Ferromex and Kansas City Southern Mexico.
- Transport in Mexico presents challenges ranging from capacity to security issues.
- Across North America, there is a general driver shortage and gaps in transport capacity, partly attributed to increased US government regulations over hours of service and monitoring of driver safety records.
- Labour disputes with the longshoremen union have threatened strikes at west coast ports, making long distance just-in-time shipments more difficult.
- There is a shortage of team drivers available for expedited shipments.
- Pirelli monitors shipments to OEM customers around the clock and limits the number of carriers handling just-in-time business to ensure quality service.
- The company is organizing a 3PL tender and network re-engineering for its US and Canadian warehousing and distribution system.
- Network re-engineering may demonstrate that a third US warehouse in the midwest or northeast could be more efficient and enable better customer service.
- Pirelli generally consolidates to a single drayage carrier for each distribution centre.
- For replacement tyre shipments, preferred drayage companies transfer containers from ports.
- Threats of west coast port strikes and congestion at the port of Los Angeles caused problems picking up containers, leading to delays in direct deliveries to some customers in southern California.
- Pirelli foresees more direct deliveries from Mexican production and overseas imports to OEMs and wholesale customers in North America.
- The company is arranging more direct deliveries and switching to building more make-to-order tyres with total cycle times of around 90 days.
- In the United States, making-to-order saves Pirelli millions of dollars per year.
- Pirelli delivers more small shipments to tyre retailers using a special order management system based on larger retailers' systems, operating on a no-backorder policy with demands for delivery within 48 hours.
- The company is seeing an evolution between using distributors to handle finished products and using its own distribution centres.
- Distributors can bundle Pirelli tyres with those of other manufacturers for faster delivery within a few hours.
- In Mexico, Pirelli switched from using distributors to opening its own facility in Mexico City four months ago due to delays, stocking it with 1,000 tyres and replenishing it three times per week.
- The increase in local production and sourcing supports the company's aim for speedier, more direct delivery while cutting inventory costs.
