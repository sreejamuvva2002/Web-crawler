# Daechang Seat Corp.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-10-23

**Source:** [South Korean auto parts maker plans $72.5M plant near new Hyundai facility in Georgia, hiring 500 | Georgia | local3news.com](https://www.local3news.com/regional-national/ga/south-korean-auto-parts-maker-plans-72-5m-plant-near-new-hyundai-facility-in-georgia/article_c78d0b7a-71c3-11ee-bc46-3fad5ed6feed.html)  ·  `page_018957`

## Facts

- Daechang Seat Corp., a South Korean company that makes automotive seat frames, will open a factory in Georgia to supply a new Hyundai Motor Group plant.
- Daechang Seat Corp. is based in Gyeongju, South Korea.
- The company announced it would invest $72.5 million in a plant in Savannah.
- The Savannah plant will hire more than 500 people.
- The new facility will supply Hyundai’s $7.6 billion plant to assemble electric vehicles and batteries in Ellabell, near Savannah.
- Hyundai’s Ellabell plant was announced last year.
- Hyundai’s Ellabell plant could grow to 8,500 employees.
- Hyundai’s Ellabell plant could begin producing vehicles as early as 2024.
- Suppliers to the Hyundai plant have pledged to invest nearly $2.3 billion.
- Suppliers to the Hyundai plant have pledged to hire nearly 5,800 people.
- Daechang Seat opened a factory in Phenix City, Alabama, in 2019.
- The company announced a $23.4 million expansion in Phenix City last year, saying it would build a new building and hire an additional 300 people.
- The company employs more than 5,000 people in seven countries.
- Jinsuk Lee is the CEO of Daechang Seat Savannah Corp.
- Jinsuk Lee stated that the company's primary objective is to drive the growth of DSC through facility expansions, ultimately transforming DSC into a global entity.
- Workers will make an average wage of more than $18 an hour, not including bonuses and benefits.
- Angela Hendrix is a spokesperson for the Savannah Economic Development Authority.
- The state will pay to train workers for the new facility.
- Daechang Seat could qualify for $10 million in state income tax credits, at $4,000 per job over five years.
- To qualify for the $10 million in state income tax credits, workers must make at least $31,300 a year.
- Local officials have granted a 15-year property tax reduction that's projected to save the company $5.8 million.
- Local officials are selling land for the plant at a reduced rate.
