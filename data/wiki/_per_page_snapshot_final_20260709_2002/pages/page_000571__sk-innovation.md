## Facts

- A South Korean battery manufacturer filed a federal lawsuit and a trade complaint alleging theft of trade secrets against a rival company planning a sprawling factory in northeast Georgia.
- The facility is planned to employ 2,000 workers.
- The dispute pits two of South Korea's largest conglomerates against each other in U.S. court and before a top U.S. trade body.
- LG Chem alleges SK Innovation stole their technology.
- LG Chem requests that a federal judge in Delaware and the U.S. International Trade Commission step in to block SK from importing lithium-ion batteries into the U.S.
- LG Chem also requests blocking SK from importing equipment for manufacturing and testing batteries into the U.S.
- SK Innovation's CEO Lee Jae-hyun said GL was trying to block SK from importing batteries and equipment for manufacturing and testing.
- SK Innovation is confident that the U.S. International Trade Commission will find in their favor and protect them from unfair competition.
- SK Innovation, a South Korean conglomerate worth $17.1 billion in 2025, is planning a sprawling factory in northeast Georgia that will employ up to 2,000 workers.
- The project is part of the state's effort to become a global leader in battery manufacturing and attract automotive companies.
- The state has invested $50 million in workforce training and infrastructure for the project.
- Georgia announced a Joint-Construction Contract for funding and tax credits to support the project.
- The contract is not yet approved, but work is ongoing.
