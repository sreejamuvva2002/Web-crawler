# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [State of the Brand: Investment in America. Hyundai’s commitment to U.S. manufacturing - Automotive News](https://www.autonews.com/sponsored/state-of-the-brand-investment-in-america-hyundais-commitment-to-us-manufacturing/)  ·  `page_009436`

## Facts

- Hyundai celebrated the grand opening of its Metaplant in Bryan County, Georgia.
- The facility is known officially as Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA).
- HMGMA is a highly connected, intelligent manufacturing and assembly plant that serves as a centerpiece of Hyundai’s North American manufacturing strategy.
- The plant operates under the “Hyundai Way,” the company’s new mid- to long-term strategy focused on agility via a unique flexible response system.
- The flexible response system allows Hyundai to quickly produce electric vehicles, hybrids, extended-range EVs, hydrogen fuel cell vehicles, or internal combustion vehicles based on consumer demand.
- Hyundai Motor Company President and CEO José Muñoz states that Hyundai is maintaining its long-term vision for electrification while leveraging powertrain technology and manufacturing flexibility to respond to market changes.
- The Metaplant features a connected, automated, and flexible manufacturing system where all production processes from order collection to procurement, logistics, and production are optimized using artificial intelligence and data.
- The innovative manufacturing system is designed to create a human-centered work environment with robotics assisting human workers.
- Hyundai announced plans to invest $21 billion to expand U.S. manufacturing capabilities, advance future technologies, and enhance energy infrastructure over the next three years.
- Of the total investment, $9 billion will establish an annual production capacity of 1.2 million vehicles across the Hyundai Motor, Kia, and Genesis brands.
- The company plans to invest $6 billion to increase the localization rate of automotive components, including core parts for electric vehicles, to ensure robust supply chains.
- An additional $6 billion will be invested to drive innovation and expand strategic partnerships with U.S.-based companies in autonomous driving, robotics, artificial intelligence, and advanced air mobility.
- This $6 billion investment also includes energy infrastructure projects to contribute to sustainable energy generation development.
- These investments are expected to create 14,000 new direct full-time jobs by 2028.
- The investments will also create more than 100,000 direct and indirect job opportunities in related industries.
- Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Alabama (HMMA), the automaker’s first U.S. assembly and manufacturing plant, will celebrate its 20th anniversary in May.
- HMMA is a 3.4-million-square-foot plant in Montgomery that serves as the largest manufacturer in the region.
- The Alabama plant assembles six models: Hyundai Tucson, Santa Fe, Sante Fe Hybrid, Santa Cruz, Genesis GV70, and Electrified GV70.
- HMMA’s press factory topped the Harbor Report’s North American Automaker Productivity Survey for six consecutive years.
- Its engine and assembly factory topped the same survey for five consecutive years.
- Almost 390,000 vehicles roll off the HMMA line each year.
- The Alabama plant directly employs 4,200 team members.
- HMMA supports 38,000 direct and indirect jobs and generates a $5 billion annual economic impact.
- To date, 72 suppliers have located in Alabama and North America to support HMMA operations.
- The plant invests $750,000 annually in regional programs covering education, cultural arts, diversity, environment, and health and fitness.
- Hyundai’s Metaplant began production in the fourth quarter with the 2025 Hyundai IONIQ 5 electric SUV.
- Production recently began on the plant’s second vehicle, the all-electric 2026 IONIQ 9 three-row SUV, which will be the first all-new Hyundai model assembled at the facility.
- In addition to Hyundai models, the Metaplant will produce Genesis and Kia vehicles.
- At full production, the Georgia plant will build more than 500,000 [BF2] vehicles per year.
- Hyundai and its partners are investing $12.6 billion in the assembly plant and two battery joint ventures.
- The Metaplant will directly employ 8,500 team members, while the battery plants will employ an additional 3,500.
- A recent Center for Automotive Research study projects Hyundai’s Georgia investments will create nearly 40,000 jobs and $4.6 billion in individual earnings annually.
- Hyundai has invested in regional educational systems through various STEM initiatives.
- The company partnered with the SAE Foundation to deliver the A World in Motion PreK-12 STEM education program in the Bryan County School District.
- Hyundai partnered with the H2GP Foundation to implement its Hydrogen STEM program to foster curiosity and innovation in sustainable energy.
- Hyundai is partnering with Georgia Southern University and Ogeechee Technical College to address regional workforce needs and bridge academia with industry for skilled talent pipelines.
- For the past two years, Hyundai has awarded scholarships to Georgia State University undergraduate students pursuing STEM degrees.
- Hyundai recently announced a partnership with Georgia Tech to collaborate on research supporting sustainable mobility, including hydrogen economy development, workforce development, and smart city initiatives.
- Hyundai reported an all-time annual sales record in 2024 with total and retail sales up 4%, driven by U.S. production capabilities.
- The automaker has shifted its manufacturing focus from sedans to SUVs to meet market demand.
