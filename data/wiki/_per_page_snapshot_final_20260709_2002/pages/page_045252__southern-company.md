## Facts

- Georgia Power requested certification for approximately $16.7 billion in new utility-owned projects through 2030.
- The requested $16.7 billion portfolio includes generation, battery storage, and transmission projects.
- Georgia Power has signed 8 GW of data center contracts since 2023.
- Weather-adjusted commercial kilowatt-hour sales at Georgia Power increased 3.7% year-to-date due to surging demand from new data centers.
- Southern Company's retail electric revenues grew 9% year-to-date to $15.1 billion.
- Southern Company's net income attributable to the holding company grew minimally by 1.5% year-to-date to $3.93 billion.
- Southern Company's total debt load increased by $8.7 billion to $72.2 billion.
- Southern Company's year-to-date interest expense surged 14% to $2.34 billion, including a $129 million loss on the extinguishment of debt.
- Southern Power's net income declined 47% year-to-date to $141 million.
- The decline in Southern Power's earnings was primarily due to an $181 million non-cash charge for accelerated depreciation related to wind repowering projects.
- Southern Power's wind repowering strategy aims to extend asset life and secure 96% power purchase agreement coverage through 2029.
- Alabama Power and Georgia Power claimed a combined $373 million in zero-emission nuclear Production Tax Credits (PTCs).
- The $373 million nuclear PTC claim is deferred as a regulatory liability pending IRS acceptance of the prevailing wage multiplier and state PSC direction.
- Southern Company's consolidated cash and cash equivalents increased by $2.3 billion to $3.3 billion year-to-date.
- Southern Company faces a maximum potential collateral requirement of $3.8 billion if the credit ratings of any power pool participant fall to non-investment grade.
- Georgia Power's construction work in progress (CWIP) increased by $2.4 billion over nine months.
- Southern Company Gas property, plant, and equipment increased by $821 million year-to-date.
- The Georgia Public Service Commission approved the extension of the 2022 ARP through 2028.
- Southern Company generated $10.3 billion in new debt proceeds year-to-date to fund capital-intensive construction programs.
- Net cash provided by operating activities decreased by $410 million year-to-date to $7.2 billion, primarily due to adverse timing of vendor payments and decreased retail fuel cost recovery.
