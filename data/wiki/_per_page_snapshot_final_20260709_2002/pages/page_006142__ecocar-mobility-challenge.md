# EcoCAR Mobility Challenge

**Type:** event  ·  **Role:** workforce_training  ·  **Published:** 2022-09-21

**Source:** [Alternative Fuels Data Center: College Students Engineer Next-Generation Vehicles in EcoCAR Mobility Challenge](https://afdc.energy.gov/case/3116?text)  ·  `page_006142`

## Facts

- The EcoCAR Mobility Challenge is a 4-year competition in which eleven North American college teams were challenged to reduce the environmental impact of a Chevrolet Blazer SUV while retaining or enhancing its utility, safety, and performance.
- After a year of modeling and simulation, teams received their vehicles for year two to implement their designs.
- Students had to consider real-world packaging for component fit and finish, drivability, and consumer acceptance.
- Headline sponsor General Motors provided the vehicles and encouraged teams to mimic GM's global vehicle design process for prototype development.
- General Motors hosted all eleven schools for the finals at its proving grounds in Yuma, Arizona.
- The competition showcased finished projects in motion at Phoenix Raceway to media, sponsors, and local students.
- One team implemented a one-pedal like driving regenerative mode found in hybrid vehicles, noting that calibration was difficult and they were only allowed one mode of operation.
- The US Department of Energy provided expertise and organizational support, with engineers from Argonne National Lab managing the competition.
- Judging each year measures progress in emissions, economy, component integration, and pure performance.
- The Department of Energy has sponsored these student competitions for more than 30 years to ensure teams work on alternative fuels or advanced technologies like connected and automated vehicles.
- DOE aims to train future engineers who will eventually work for car companies, national labs, and the DOE through hands-on training with an OEM vehicle.
- Georgia Tech claimed top team honors for 2022.
- The Georgia Tech EcoCAR team converted its originally six-cylinder Blazer into a four-cylinder hybrid vehicle equipped with adaptive cruise control.
- Georgia Tech's vehicle features vehicle-to-infrastructure communication technology that allows it to communicate with stoplights and adjust speed for optimization.
- Ohio State placed 2nd and the University of Alabama placed 3rd in the competition.
- Teams utilized varying engines, battery pack sizes, motors, and wheels, with one team using 18-inch wheels and maintaining the most cargo space.
- Another team equipped its vehicle with six radars (three front, one on each side, one rear) and a Mobileye windshield camera to provide a 360-degree field of view for driving decisions, reporting that all systems passed testing.
- All eleven teams produced drivable, nearly-production-ready vehicles imagined, engineered, and built solely by college students.
- The competition provides invaluable knowledge and experience for the next generation of automotive innovators.
