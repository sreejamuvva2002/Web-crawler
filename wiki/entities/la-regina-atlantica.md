# La Regina Atlantica

**Type:** Company  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[unknown|Uncategorized]]  ·  **Location:** Alma, Georgia, Bacon County, Georgia, United States

## Overview

Family-owned manufacturer of Rao’s homestyle pasta sauce that opened its first U.S. production facility in Alma, Georgia and is expanding the plant with a workforce training partnership involving Georgia Quick Start and Coastal Pines Technical College.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Indirect - workforce development for manufacturing adds skilled labor to the broader Georgia supply-chain ecosystem that supports EV and other advanced industries.

## Workforce & partners

- [[georgia-quick-start|Georgia Quick Start]] — State workforce training program partner delivering customized safety and job-specific training. Provides safety training and instruction on specialized sauce-production machines for La Regina’s expansion.
- Coastal Pines Technical College — Long-term training partner offering ongoing education and a pipeline of qualified graduates. Will continue to provide training support after the initial rollout.

## Key facts

- La Regina Atlantica has grown its Alma, GA facility to 103 employees and plans to hire up to 50 more.
- The training agreement involves three partners: Georgia Quick Start, La Regina Atlantica, and Coastal Pines Technical College.
- Georgia Quick Start will provide safety training and job-specific instruction on specialized sauce-production machines.
- Emidio Rinaldi said the training support will enable growth while maintaining safety standards and recipe quality.
- Scott McMurray highlighted that La Regina chose Georgia for its first U.S. production facility.
- Lonnie Roberts emphasized the shared commitment to building a skilled workforce in Georgia’s food industry.
- Bacon County Development Authority and GDEcD worked with partners to qualify La Regina for training support.
- Training is scheduled to begin in early 2026.
