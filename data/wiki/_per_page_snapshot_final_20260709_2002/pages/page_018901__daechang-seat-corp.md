# Daechang Seat Corp.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-10-24

**Source:** [New Korean Supplier to Land in Savannah, Investing $72M and Hiring 500 - Global Atlanta](https://www.globalatlanta.com/new-korean-supplier-to-land-in-savannah-investing-72m-and-hiring-500/)  ·  `page_018901`

## Facts

- A new Korean supplier is set to invest $72.5 million in Savannah.
- The facility will hire more than 500 workers.
- The facility will make seat frames for cars with an automated process outfitted with robotic welding machines.
- Daechang Seat Corp. will set up shop in the Savannah Chatham Manufacturing Center.
- The location is near the Hyundai Meta Plant, where the South Korean auto maker is constructing its first dedicated electric vehicle factory in the United States.
- Another two suppliers have already located in the industrial park.
- Hyundai will soon mark one year since it broke ground on the 2,900-acre site in Bryan County.
- Daechang works already with key suppliers Hyundai Mobis and Hyundai Transys.
- Jinsuk Lee, CEO of the local operation, Daechang Seat Savannah Corp., stated the company hopes to work on "even more projects in the future."
- Daechang is now one of the largest seat frame producers globally.
- Daechang started in the city of Gyeongju in the Gyeongchang-buk province of Korea in 1979, where it still has factories.
- It now employs more than 5,000 people across Korea and seven other countries, including China, India, Turkey, Poland, Indonesia, Czech Republic and Slovakia.
- Along with Korean OEMs Kia and Hyundai, Daechang counts as customers global automakers like Toyota, Ford, Chrysler, Volvo and Volkswagen.
- The corporate headquarters is in Dongtan, south of Seoul.
- In 2022, the company had 1.4 trillion won in sales, about $1 billion.
- In the U.S., the company known as DSC has a factory in Phenix City, Ala., just across the state line from Columbus, Ga.
- The Phenix City facility was established in 2020.
- It was a latecomer to a troop of Korean firms that set up shop in the western part of Georgia or eastern Alabama to serve the Hyundai and Kia plants in Montgomery, Ala., and West Point, Ga., respectively.
- Daechang’s initial Phenix City investment was $9 million.
- The company followed with a reported $40 million expansion in 2022.
- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp said suppliers for the Hyundai Meta Plant are present in nine counties.
- The Hyundai Meta Plant represents a $5.5 billion investment along Interstate 16.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development was recently recognized by The Korea Society for its prowess in welcoming Korean firms, particularly those participating in the energy transition in the United States.
- The Southeast U.S.-Korea Chamber of Commerce on Nov. 2 will host The Next Generation Workforce Development Forum in partnership with the Georgia Automotive Manufacturers Association, or GAMA, at the Kia plant in West Point.
