# FOX Factory Headquarters

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [New FOX Factory Headquarters - DBIA](https://dbia.org/project/new-fox-factory-headquarters/)  ·  `page_020228`

## Facts

- FOX Factory decided to move its headquarters and plant from California to Georgia.
- The new facility is FOX’s first “vertically integrated” factory.
- The building layout allows products to move linearly from materials arriving on one side to finished products and shipping on the other.
- The facility was designed to be energy efficient and make optimal use of the site topography.
- Productivity increased by more than 25% within the first two months of the new facility’s operation.
- The linear flow of materials and product through the building enhances predictability, planning, and worker safety.
- The plant will support current and future local employees, with a capacity of up to 800 workers.
- FOX Factory served as the client/owner for the project.
- Carroll Daniel Construction Co. acted as the design-build firm.
- BRPH Architects-Engineers, Inc. served as the architect.
- Rochester & Associates, Inc. served as the engineer.
- Specialty contractors for the project included Lawson HVAC, Simco Interiors, Simpson Grading, T&M Tilt Up, and Titan Electric – Georgia.
- The construction duration for the new headquarters was 16 months.
- The total project cost was $47,172,103.00.
