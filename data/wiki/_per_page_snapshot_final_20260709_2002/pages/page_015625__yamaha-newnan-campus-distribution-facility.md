# Yamaha Newnan Campus Distribution Facility

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-03

**Source:** [DHL to build new distribution facility on Yamaha campus | News | times-herald.com](https://www.times-herald.com/news/dhl-to-build-new-distribution-facility-on-yamaha-campus/article_ce01ac09-2e29-47bc-af08-a0640538793a.html)  ·  `page_015625`

## Facts

- Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation and DHL Supply Chain are moving forward with plans that could bring a new $60 million to $90 million distribution facility to Yamaha’s Newnan campus.
- The proposed 784,700-square-foot building would be dedicated solely to Yamaha operations and marks a major investment in both logistics and the local community.
- If approved, the facility would consolidate freight operations that currently run between Yamaha’s main plant and off-site centers, traffic that contributes to congestion along Highway 34 and surrounding intersections.
- By relocating distribution onto the Yamaha campus, the project is expected to reduce truck traffic on local roads, improving both safety and traffic flow without cost to taxpayers.
- Bob Brown, president of Yamaha Motor Manufacturing, stated: “This opportunity is about more than logistics. It’s about strengthening our supply chain while also being a good neighbor. We are working carefully through the necessary studies and permitting to ensure this project would benefit both Yamaha and the community for years to come.”
- Company officials said the design includes sustainability features such as stormwater management systems, tree buffers and pervious surfaces to limit environmental impact.
- Plans also call for roadway improvements, including new turn lanes and upgraded intersections.
- Construction could begin as early as 2026, with operations starting in the first quarter of 2028 if the project receives required approvals.
- Dave Rumler, president of automotive engineering and manufacturing at DHL Supply Chain North America, stated: “As North America’s leading contract logistics provider, DHL Supply Chain is committed to delivering integrated, end-to-end solutions that drive efficiency and resilience for our partners. We’re proud to collaborate with Yamaha on a project that not only enhances supply chain performance but also supports infrastructure improvements that benefit the Newnan community.”
- Yamaha employs more than 2,000 metro Atlanta residents at its Coweta County facility, where it designs and builds golf cars, Side-by-Sides, WaveRunners and ATVs.
