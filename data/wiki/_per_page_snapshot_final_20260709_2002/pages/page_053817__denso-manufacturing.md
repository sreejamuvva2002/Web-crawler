# DENSO Manufacturing

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2018-02-28

**Source:** [Denso manufacturing to add 320 new jobs to Athens plant](https://newschannel9.com/news/local/denso-manufacturing-to-add-320-new-jobs-to-athens-plant)  ·  `page_053817`

## Facts

- DENSO Manufacturing is expanding its Athens, Tennessee facility with a $190 million investment.
- The expansion will add 320 new jobs to the Athens plant.
- Officials from DENSO and Tennessee Governor Bill Haslam made the announcement on Wednesday morning (February 28, 2018).
- DENSO’s Athens facility manufactures fuel injectors, fuel pumps, oxygen sensors, ignition coils, monolithic carriers and spark plugs.
- The investment will add four production lines in Athens, increasing the company’s ability to produce and deliver key parts for automakers across North America.
- The 320 new jobs will span production, facility operations, engineering, administration and other skilled-traded positions.
- DENSO will introduce a new Gasoline Direct Injection (GDI) production line, one low-pressure fuel pump line and two high-pressure fuel pump lines.
- This expansion is part of DENSO’s strategic investments in North America and its commitment to make Tennessee a primary manufacturing center.
- In 2017, DENSO announced a $1 billion investment in its Maryville facility, creating 1,000 jobs focused on electrification and safety systems.
- Tennessee Governor Bill Haslam stated that DENSO is one of Tennessee’s largest employers and has been a valuable part of the state for 30 years.
- Community Development Commissioner Bob Rolfe noted that DENSO employs approximately 1,375 people in Athens, more than any business in McMinn County.
- Hugh Cantrell, director of Administrative Services for DENSO Manufacturing Athens Tennessee, stated that increasing manufacturing capacity closer to North American customers will continue to power DENSO’s growth in the region.
- DENSO has its global headquarters in Japan and has had a presence in Tennessee for three decades.
- DENSO operates three locations in Tennessee: Maryville, Athens and Jackson, employing roughly 4,500 people across the state.
- Prior to this expansion, DENSO has invested approximately $3 billion in Tennessee.
- McMinn County Mayor John M. Gentry stated that the expansion will improve the quality of life for several hundred McMinn County families and have a lasting impact on the local economy.
- City of Athens Mayor Chuck Burris stated that the announcement exemplifies the success of Athens' development strategies to help existing companies grow and praised DENSO as a standard bearer for corporate citizenry in Athens.
