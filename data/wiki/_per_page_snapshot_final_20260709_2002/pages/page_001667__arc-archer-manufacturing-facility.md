# ARC (Archer Manufacturing Facility)

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Archer Aviation - Archer Completes Construction of High-Volume Manufacturing Facility](https://investors.archer.com/news/news-details/2024/Archer-Completes-Construction-of-High-Volume-Manufacturing-Facility/default.aspx)  ·  `page_001667`

## Facts

- Archer received the certificate of occupancy for its ~400,000 square foot aircraft manufacturing facility—referred to as ARC—in Covington, GA.
- Archer held a commemorative ribbon cutting ceremony for the facility.
- Tooling load-in is already underway ahead of the planned start of initial production beginning in early 2025.
- The goal is to ramp production to a rate of two aircraft per month by the end of the year.
- Stellantis and Archer are working to finalize an agreement pursuant to which Stellantis would work with Archer as its exclusive contract manufacturer to mass produce Archer’s Midnight eVTOL aircraft at this facility.
- Stellantis is contributing capital, advanced manufacturing technology and expertise and experienced personnel to the partnership.
- The goal of the partnership is scaling the facility to 650 aircraft annually by 2030.
- ARC is connected to the Covington Municipal Airport in Georgia.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp commented that the choice to build the new manufacturing facility in Newton County marks the next step in an incredible relationship and reaffirms Georgia’s commitment to creating quality jobs and opportunities for years to come.
- Congressman Mike Collins of Georgia, who sits on the House Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure and Committee on Science, Space, and Technology, stated that the completion highlights Georgia’s leadership in innovation, advanced manufacturing and job creation.
- Archer founder and CEO Adam Goldstein commented that the completion is a testament to the industry shifting from R&D into commercialization and praised the team for getting construction done fast and on budget.
- Stellantis Chief Manufacturing and Supply Chain Officer Arnaud Deboeuf stated that supporting Archer’s electric aircraft growth with deep manufacturing expertise is a great opportunity for both companies.
- ARC is where Archer plans to produce its all-electric vertical take-off and landing aircraft, Midnight.
- The Midnight aircraft is designed to offer passengers a sustainable, low-noise and safe alternative to ground transportation in congested cities around the world.
