# Novelis Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-12-12

**Source:** [Novelis Inc. "Lighter, Greener, Simpler - Aluminum Drives New Automotive Design Dynamic" :: Novelis Inc.](https://investors.novelis.com/news-events/press-releases/detail/13/novelis-inc-lighter-greener-simpler---aluminum-drives-new-automotive-design-dynamic)  ·  `page_047337`

## Facts

- Novelis Inc. is a leading sustainable aluminum solutions provider and world leader in aluminum rolling and recycling.
- The press release features thought leadership by Jamie Zinser (Vice President, Global Automotive), Xavier Varone (Global Technical Director), and Don Whitacre (Director, Automotive Technical Development) of Novelis Inc.
- Making vehicles lighter, greener, and simpler to manufacture is critical to improving the features, functionality, and sustainability of internal combustion engine models while driving growth in the battery electric vehicle market.
- Aluminum is the fastest-growing automotive material according to the Ducker Carlisle 2023 North American Light Vehicle Aluminum Content and Outlook report.
- Novelis has been a leading global supplier of aluminum sheet to the auto industry for more than 40 years, with automotive operations on all three auto-producing continents: Asia, Europe, and North America.
- Novelis' automotive product portfolio includes high-performing alloys featured in more than 315 models on the road today, including the Ford F-150 series, Jeep Wrangler, and Land Rover Defender.
- Transportation makes up roughly 16% of total global greenhouse emissions according to Climate Watch and the World Resources Institute (2020).
- Passenger cars, trucks, buses, and other commercial and recreational vehicles constitute more than three quarters (77%) of all transportation emissions according to a 2023 Statista report.
- Life cycle assessments show the greatest potential to reduce vehicle environmental impact is addressing the use phase.
- An Oakridge National Lab study shows the use phase of an ICE vehicle accounts for up to 90% of carbon emissions generated over its total lifecycle.
- The use phase of a BEV contributes to approximately 70% of its lifetime carbon emissions according to a recently published Alumobility Last Mile Delivery Vehicles study.
- Aluminum has a material density that is one third of steel while delivering greater specific strength, energy absorbing capacity, and longevity.
- Lightweighting via aluminum typically generates a body-in-white weight savings of more than 40% compared to a steel body-in-white.
- Replacing 1 kilogram of steel with 0.6 kilograms of aluminum results in no reduction in performance.
- Primary mass reduction from aluminum generates secondary mass reduction opportunities for motors, motor controllers, batteries, wheels, tires, and brakes.
- Secondary weight reduction can often exceed 50% of the primary weight reduction.
- Ducker Carlisle identified key growth areas for aluminum in BEVs from 2022 to 2030: battery housings (+37 pounds per vehicle), electric motor housings (+34 ppv), doors (+8 ppv), and sills/rockers (+4 ppv).
- Novelis participated in an Alumobility consortium study modeling an aluminum body for a fully electric last mile delivery van.
- The study showed a compounded 550kg weight savings with aluminum versus a steel baseline.
- This weight savings triggered a battery size reduction from 69kWh to 63kWh, representing a net saving of $770 per vehicle.
- Alumobility studies on converting existing steel bodies into aluminum showed potential to reduce part counts by 10% and the number of joints required by 20%.
- Aluminum content in automobiles grew from less than 130kg per vehicle at the beginning of this century to close to 220kg per vehicle today.
- Aluminum content is expected to grow at a compound annual growth rate of 6.4% from 2022 to 2026, driven by the rise of BEVs according to Ducker Carlisle.
- Reducing an ICE vehicle's weight by 220 pounds (100kg) decreases fuel consumption by as much as 5% over its entire life.
- The same 220-pound weight reduction for a BEV translates to a reduction in energy needed for the same range by approximately 3.2kWh according to NREL's FASTSim simulator.
- A weight savings of 450kg in a full-body aluminum automobile saves owners more than 6,000kWh of energy over a typical 140,000-mile life compared to steel bodies.
- These energy savings amount to 60% of the average annual electricity consumption for a U.S. residential utility customer.
- Lighter vehicles create less wear and tear on sub-systems like brakes and tires, resulting in lower maintenance costs.
- Carbon emission reductions and consumer maintenance benefits can be achieved by using aluminum for hoods, doors, and battery enclosures.
- Energy savings and corresponding carbon emission reductions can reach up to 20% if aluminum use is expanded to the full vehicle body according to the EDAG Silverado Body Lightweighting Final LCA Report.
- Novelis operates automotive Customer Solution Centers in Stuttgart, Germany; Shanghai, China; and Detroit, Michigan.
- The centers are designed to speed development of innovative aluminum solutions by collaborating with OEM customers and Tier 1 suppliers from early vehicle concept and design stages.
- The Metro Detroit CSC features a hot stamping press, computer-aided design software, and advanced visualization technologies for digitally modeling vehicle performance and safety scenarios.
- The Detroit CSC contains a roll forming development line for exploring solutions for battery enclosure frames, rockers, front longitudinal members, and various cross members.
- This capability enables Novelis researchers to work with auto OEMs to develop and test alloys and processes as an emerging alternative to extruded parts.
- Since launching its first CSC in 2018, Novelis has advanced efficient door designs using high-forming aluminum alloys.
- Novelis has developed critical strength-dominated structural components to compete with ultra-high-strength steel alloys.
- Novelis demonstrated optimized tempers for energy absorbing crash members that save weight and cost versus current extruded aluminum crash rails.
- Novelis designed and prototyped aluminum-intensive battery enclosure solutions for electric vehicles.
- Novelis works hand-in-hand with automotive customers globally on innovative design, roll forming research, sustainable alloys, and BEV efficiency.
