# Archer Aviation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Electric air taxi company Archer to build Georgia factory, hire 1,000](https://www.bignewsnetwork.com/news/273104191/electric-air-taxi-company-archer-to-build-georgia-factory-hire-1000)  ·  `page_017061`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation announced it will invest $118 million to construct a plant near Atlanta, Georgia.
- The company plans to eventually hire up to 1,000 people for the facility.
- The aircraft plant will be built adjoining an airport in Covington, Georgia.
- Archer Aviation is developing a battery-powered vertical takeoff and landing (eVTOL) craft with six propellers that holds four passengers and a pilot.
- The eVTOL craft is designed for short flights, particularly in urban areas.
- United Airlines announced it would fly the craft from downtown Manhattan to Newark Liberty International Airport in New Jersey.
- The United Airlines route would cut a trip that takes an hour by car in congested traffic down to 10 minutes.
- The U.S. Air Force is evaluating Archer's aircraft for possible use.
- Archer Aviation is currently seeking Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) certification for its aircraft.
- The company states it could receive FAA certification by 2024.
- Archer aims to produce 650 aircraft annually at the Georgia site initially.
- Annual production capacity at the Georgia site has the potential to rise to 2,300 aircraft per year.
- The company expects to receive an incentive package worth some $40 million from Georgia and local governments.
- The incentive package includes the donation of 96 acres of land at Covington Municipal Airport.
- The incentive package also includes a cash grant from the state and other tax incentives.
- Archer chose Georgia due to the availability of local workers, the ability to conduct test flights, affordable construction costs, and good highway, rail, and air connections.
- Archer CEO Adam Goldstein stated that their eVTOL technology can transform how urban and rural communities live and commute, and the factory can create pathways to highly skilled manufacturing hubs and other ladders of social and economic opportunity.
- Georgia Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson noted that economic recruiters specializing in aerospace had worked with Archer.
- Pat Wilson stated that technology and innovation continue to drive change across strategic industries.
