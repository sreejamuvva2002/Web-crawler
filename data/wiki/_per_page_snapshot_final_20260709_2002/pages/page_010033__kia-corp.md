# Kia Corp.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-07-13

**Source:** [Kia to invest $200M in Georgia plant to begin building electric SUV there, hiring 200](https://newschannel9.com/news/local/kia-to-invest-200m-in-georgia-plant-to-begin-building-electric-suv-there-hiring-200)  ·  `page_010033`

## Facts

- Kia will invest $200 million in its Georgia factory to begin producing an electric-powered SUV.
- Kia announced it would hire an additional 200 workers to begin producing its large EV9 vehicle in West Point by early 2024.
- The company will expand overall production at its plant located near the Alabama state line southwest of Atlanta.
- The West Point plant currently produces 340,000 vehicles a year.
- Current plant production includes Telluride, Sorrento, and Sportage SUVs, as well as K5 sedans.
- Kia currently employs 3,000 workers at the West Point plant.
- Spokesperson Patrick Sands stated that the EV9 will be built on the same assembly line as other vehicles.
- Kia is investing in the plant to assemble electric powertrains.
- Hyundai Motor Group, Kia's parent company, is aggressively expanding its electric vehicle portfolio.
- Hyundai is working to complete a $5.5 billion plant to assemble electric vehicles and batteries in Ellabell, Georgia, near Savannah.
- Hyundai also invested $300 million in its Montgomery, Alabama plant to begin manufacturing electric vehicles, starting with the Genesis Electrified GV70 SUV.
- Hyundai hired additional workers for the Montgomery, Alabama plant investment.
- Kia leaders hope the three-row EV9 will boost company sales similarly to how the Telluride boosted the brand after starting production in West Point in 2019.
- Kia began delivering EV9 vehicles to customers in South Korea one month prior to the announcement.
- Sean Yoon, President and CEO of Kia Motors America, stated that the EV9 has the potential to be another change catalyst for Kia, similar to the Telluride.
- Sean Yoon described the EV9 as the most innovative vehicle Kia has ever built and a standout in the EV market.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp and Hyundai have complained that federal EV tax credits are unfair to Kia because its vehicles are only eligible for credits when leased.
- The Inflation Reduction Act's domestic content standards require the vehicle, battery, and key raw materials to be made in the U.S. for EV purchase tax incentives to apply.
- Hyundai has announced plans to partner on a battery factory in Cartersville, northwest of Atlanta.
- Since 2020, Georgia has seen over 40 EV-related projects pledge $22.9 billion in investment and create 28,600 jobs.
- Governor Kemp claims credit for the state's EV investment boom, while Senator Jon Ossoff attributes it to President Biden's policies.
- The state of Georgia will fund worker training for the Kia plant expansion.
- Kia states that production workers at the plant start with an annual salary of $38,000, plus pay raises and extra compensation for evening shifts.
- Kia could qualify for $1.75 million in state income tax credits, calculated at $1,750 per job over five years, contingent on workers earning at least $31,300 annually.
- Kia may also qualify for additional incentives, including property tax breaks from the city of West Point and Troup County.
- Kia previously received over $450 million in incentives for its West Point plant, which opened in 2010.
- In 2019, Kia, its affiliates, and suppliers received $2.8 million in property tax breaks from West Point alone.
