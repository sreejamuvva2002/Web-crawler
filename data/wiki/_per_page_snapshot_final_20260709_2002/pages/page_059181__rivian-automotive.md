# Rivian Automotive

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-10-05

**Source:** [Rivian applies for federal loan to restart Georgia EV factory construction, ETManufacturing](https://manufacturing.economictimes.indiatimes.com/news/automotive/rivian-applies-for-federal-loan-to-restart-georgia-ev-factory-construction/113958803)  ·  `page_059181`

## Facts

- Rivian Automotive has applied for a federal loan to enable the construction of its electric vehicle factory in Georgia, according to a filing by the U.S. Department of Energy.
- The U.S. Department of Energy has not made a final decision on the loan application, and the filing did not disclose the requested amount or terms.
- The Georgia facility is scheduled to begin partial operations in the third quarter of 2027.
- The first production capacity block at the Georgia facility is scheduled to reach full operation in 2028.
- Rivian had previously paused construction of its planned $5 billion factory in Georgia to prioritize speeding up the production of vehicles on the R2 midsize platform and conserving cash.
- Aiming to save $2.25 billion and speed up production, Rivian shifted the manufacturing of its R2 midsize SUV (set to compete with Tesla's Model Y) to its Illinois factory.
- Production at the Illinois factory for the R2 midsize SUV is slated to begin in 2026.
- In 2022, Rivian secured $1.5 billion in state and local incentives for the Georgia facility.
- Rivian lowered its annual production forecast and missed third-quarter deliveries expectations due to a parts shortage and a slowdown in EV demand.
- At the end of June, Rivian had cash and cash equivalents of $5.76 billion and a long-term debt of $5.53 billion.
- In June, Volkswagen said it would invest $5 billion in Rivian as part of a new joint venture to share EV architecture and software.
- Rivian received an $827 million incentive package from the State of Illinois to expand operations at its Normal facility, where it also makes electric delivery vans for Amazon.com.
