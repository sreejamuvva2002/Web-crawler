# Honda Development & Manufacturing of America, LLC (HDMA)

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2021-05-12

**Source:** [Honda Transmission Plant in Georgia Celebrates Milestone, Steps Toward Electrified Future – Gradick Communications](https://gradickcommunications.com/honda-transmission-plant-in-georgia-celebrates-milestone-steps-toward-electrified-future/)  ·  `page_008777`

## Facts

- Honda celebrated 15 years of U.S. automobile transmission production at its Tallapoosa, Georgia plant on May 12, 2021.
- Transmission production in Georgia began in 2006 with 179 associates.
- The Georgia operation has a total investment of more than $465 million.
- The facility currently employs more than 500 associates.
- The plant produces 375,000 transmissions annually for the Honda Odyssey, Accord, Acura MDX, RDX, and TLX.
- The facility was formerly known as Honda Precision Parts of Georgia (HPPG).
- Production initially began with 5-speed automatic transmissions.
- The plant added 6-speed automatic transmissions in 2012.
- A second assembly line was added to the Georgia plant in 2017.
- The Georgia facility became the first Honda plant globally to produce Honda's advanced 10-speed automatic transmission (10AT).
- In early 2021, the plant began replacing its original production line to increase 10AT production.
- Gerald Bruch, vice president of Honda Development and Manufacturing of America, LLC (HDMA), stated that the Georgia and Ohio plants form a transmission powerhouse creating advanced technology components for Honda and Acura products.
- Honda's first dedicated U.S. transmission plant in Russells Point, Ohio, will mark its 25th anniversary in July 2021.
- Honda has manufactured over 21 million transmissions in America since starting automatic transmission production in Ohio in 1989.
- The Ohio transmission plant has a total investment of more than $1 billion and spans 1.1 million square feet.
- The Ohio facility produces over 850,000 transmissions per year, along with gear sets, transfer cases, and differentials for four-wheel drive vehicles.
- Both the Georgia and Ohio plants are taking on new responsibilities as Honda begins electrifying its vehicle lineup.
- Production will include Honda's innovative two-motor hybrid system used in the Accord Hybrid, CR-V Hybrid, and Insight.
- Honda began U.S. transmission production at its Anna Engine Plant in Ohio in 1989.
- In 1996, transmission manufacturing lines were relocated from the Anna Engine Plant to Bellemar Parts Industries in Russells Point, Ohio.
- Bellemar was incorporated as Honda Transmission of America in 1997.
- In spring 2021, both Honda transmission plant locations were combined under a new company named Honda Development & Manufacturing of America, LLC (HDMA).
- HDMA encompasses all Honda automobile manufacturing facilities in the U.S. related to frame, engine, transmission, and related engineering and purchasing operations.
