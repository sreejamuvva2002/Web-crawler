# HEXPOL Compounding

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [HEXPOL Compounding invest in devulcanization - Cable Technology News](https://www.cabletechnologynews.co.uk/hexpol-compounding-invest-in-devulcanization/)  ·  `page_020934`

## Facts

- HEXPOL Compounding, the rubber compounding arm of the HEXPOL Group, announced an investment in a devulcanization line in Europe.
- The investment enables in-house mechanical devulcanization of rubber to support the transition to a circular economy.
- For years, HEXPOL Compounding has worked on utilizing cured compound scrap and waste generated as unavoidable side products of rubber part production.
- Previously, the converter paid for disposal of rubber waste that often ended in landfills or was incinerated, or was ground and reused mainly as a filler in low-quality applications.
- Devulcanization transforms cured rubber parts back into an uncured state while retaining the original polymer, carbon black or other fillers, and plasticizers.
- The process creates a raw material base for producing new rubber parts, demonstrating that rubber can be recycled.
- Carsten Rüter serves as President of HEXPOL Rubber Compounding Europe & Asia.
- HEXPOL's target is to reprocess cured rubber from post-industrial sources and reuse it for typical applications in the automotive or building and construction industries.
- The company supports customers in transforming production offcuts into new raw material with a reduced product carbon footprint instead of charging for disposal.
- The devulcanization process adds no additional chemicals, relying solely on mechanical forces like shear, compression, and temperature via a specific extruder to break down crosslinking bonds.
- The investment will enable HEXPOL Compounding in Europe to produce several thousand tons of compounds with devulcanized rubber content per year.
- Ralph Wolkener serves as President of HEXPOL Compounding Europe & Asia.
- HEXPOL already offers a variety of compound solutions with recycled content and will soon add rubber recycling to its services.
- Customers act as business partners in the process by collecting and sorting production side products until HEXPOL transforms them into devulcanized raw materials.
- HEXPOL believes the devulcanization process could potentially be extended to post-consumer rubber recycling.
- The new extruder will be installed at the HEXPOL Compounding site in the Czech Republic by year-end 2024.
- The Czech facility will provide devulcanization services to other European HEXPOL Compounding sites to ensure a broad customer base can participate.
- Norbert Niemand serves as Managing Director at HEXPOL Lesina.
- Adjusting compound recipes based on devulcanized rubber and other renewable raw materials to achieve performance matching standard compounds is part of HEXPOL's service.
