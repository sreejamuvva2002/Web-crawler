# Hyundai Motor Company - EV Plant, Savannah, Georgia

**Type:** Company  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[vehicle-manufacturing|Vehicle Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** West of Savannah, Georgia, Georgia, United States

## Overview

Hyundai is constructing a multibillion-dollar electric vehicle manufacturing facility west of Savannah, Georgia. The plant will span roughly 17 million square feet-about two and a half times the size of Disneyland-and is projected to create more than 8,000 jobs.

## EV supply-chain relevance

The facility will produce electric vehicles in Georgia, directly expanding the state’s EV manufacturing supply chain.

## Related organizations & people

- [[sk-on|SK On]] — Battery plant partner with Hyundai. Co-developer of a $5 billion battery plant announced after the IRA passed.
- Freyr Battery U.S. — Competitor battery manufacturer in Georgia. Building a $2.5 billion battery factory near Atlanta; EVP Jeremy Bezdek quoted on IRA impact.
- Hanwha Qcells — Solar panel manufacturer expanding in Georgia. Opened first Georgia factory in 2019 and is adding a second $2.5 billion plant in Cartersville.
- Governor Brian Kemp — State official promoting clean-energy manufacturing. Has said he wants Georgia to be the “electric mobility capital of America.”
- Senator Jon Ossoff — U.S. Senator, advocate for IRA provisions benefiting Georgia. Quoted saying Georgia benefits more than any state; helped draft solar manufacturing tax credits.
- Senator Rafael Warnock — U.S. Senator, co-advocate with Ossoff for EV and battery priorities in IRA
- E2 (Environmental Entrepreneurs) — Industry tracking organization. Tracks nearly 20 major clean-energy projects totaling >$12 billion in Georgia.
- Aaron Brickman — Senior principal at RMI, quoted on Georgia’s economic-development infrastructure
- Bob Keefe — Executive Director of E2, quoted on job quality and local impact

## Workforce & partners

- Technical College System of Georgia training program — Workforce training partner for new factories in Georgia. Designed to quickly train new employees while a factory is being built.

## Key facts

- Georgia leads the United States in new clean-energy investments a year after the Inflation Reduction Act passed.
- The Hyundai EV plant will be about 2.5 times larger than Disneyland, spanning 17 million square feet.
- Hyundai announced plans to build a $5 billion battery plant with SK On following the IRA passage.
- Freyr Battery’s $2.5 billion battery factory near Atlanta is expected to begin production later this year.
- Hanwha Qcells is expanding its solar panel manufacturing footprint in Georgia with a second $2.5 billion plant in Cartersville.
- Senator Jon Ossoff says Georgia benefits more than any other state from the IRA’s clean-energy tax credits.
- Governor Brian Kemp has promoted Georgia as the “electric mobility capital of America.”
- Technical College System of Georgia provides rapid workforce training programs to support new factory construction.
