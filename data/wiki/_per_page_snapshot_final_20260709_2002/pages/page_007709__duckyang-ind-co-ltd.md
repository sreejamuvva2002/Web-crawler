# Duckyang Ind. Co. Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2021-05-20

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp announces Korean auto parts supplier to invest $10 million facility in Braselton - Georgia Asian Times](https://gasiantimes.com/metro-asian-news/2021/05/20/gov-kemp-announces-korean-auto-parts-supplier-to-invest-10-million-facility-in-braselton/)  ·  `page_007709`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced on May 20, 2021, that Duckyang will invest $10 million in opening its first U.S. manufacturing facility in Jackson County.
- Duckyang is an electric mobility parts supplier of automotive battery modules and energy storage systems to SK Battery America (SKBA).
- The new facility will create 285 jobs in Braselton, Georgia.
- Governor Kemp stated that it is great to see Duckyang create high-paying, advanced automotive manufacturing jobs for Georgians in Jackson County.
- Governor Kemp noted that Georgia is home to the best workforce training programs in the nation and has remained the number one state for business for the past eight years.
- Duckyang is headquartered in Ulsan, Korea.
- Duckyang supplies cockpit modules to customers around the world including Hyundai, Kia, Renault Samsung Motors, Genesis, and Mobis.
- Cockpit modules are integrated auto parts comprised of more than 130 subcomponents found near the driver’s seat of a vehicle.
- By opening a facility in Georgia, Duckyang will be able to better serve several of its automotive customers within the region.
- Dong-in Son, CEO of Duckyang Ind. Co. Ltd., stated that entering the U.S. electric vehicle battery market with SKBA provides an opportunity to cooperate with other automotive companies like Hyundai and Kia Motors that already have a U.S. manufacturing base.
- Dong-in Son expected business expansion opportunities to supply electric vehicle parts, interior parts including cockpit modules and door trim panel modules, and other flagship parts to global automakers based in the United States.
- Duckyang’s new facility will be 230,000-square-foot.
- The facility will be located at 984 Broadway Avenue in Braselton.
- The company will be hiring for positions in production, maintenance, logistics, and warehouse management.
- Town Manager and Clerk of Braselton Jennifer Scott stated that the town looks forward to welcoming Duckyang and is delighted to see a new manufacturing facility located in their town.
- John Scott, Director of Economic Development for the Jackson County Area Chamber of Commerce, stated that companies like Duckyang are helping Jackson County and Georgia secure a place in the automotive industry's future as it moves toward electric vehicles.
- John Scott expressed excitement about welcoming Duckyang to the community.
- Director of Korean Investment Yoonie Kim represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce division on this project.
- The project was in partnership with the Jackson County Industrial Development Authority, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia EMC.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Georgia is focused on growing the entire electric mobility supply chain.
- Pat Wilson noted that strong relationships with SK Battery America and other Korean companies like Duckyang are helping grow the supply chain.
- Pat Wilson highlighted that the project is an example of how Georgia helps companies bring their suppliers to the state to secure a local supply of needed parts.
- Pat Wilson appreciated partners in Jackson County for their support and looked forward to seeing new opportunities created locally and for the U.S. mobility sector.
