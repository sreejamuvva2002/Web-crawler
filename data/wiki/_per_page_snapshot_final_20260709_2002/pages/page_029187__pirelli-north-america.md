# Pirelli North America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-05-15

**Source:** [PIRELLI NORTH AMERICA LAUNCHES THE GROUP'S FIRST CLOSED-LOOP RECYCLING INITIATIVE, TURNING SCRAP TIRES INTO NEW TIRES](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/pirelli-north-america-launches-the-groups-first-closedloop-recycling-initiative-turning-scrap-tires-into-new-tires-302773022.html)  ·  `page_029187`

## Facts

- Pirelli North America launched its first closed-loop circular recycling initiative to increase the use of recycled and bio-based materials in its products.
- The project is executed in collaboration with Bolder Industries.
- The initiative focuses on recovering scrap tires generated during the manufacturing process in North America.
- Scrap tire materials are collected and processed by Bolder Industries using pyrolysis technology.
- The processing produces ISCC PLUS-certified BolderBlack® recovered carbon black (rCB).
- Pirelli reuses the recovered carbon black in the North America production of new tires.
- The project is part of a broader Pirelli plan that will involve additional facilities to create industrial ecosystems capable of valorizing waste into recovered materials for reintroduction across the Group's production network.
- BolderBlack can partially replace virgin carbon black in tire production, contributing to more efficient resource use.
- The initiative was recognized by the Tire Recycling Foundation with a Circle of Change Award in the Value Chain Collaboration category.
- Claudio Zanardo, CEO of Pirelli North America, stated that the Rome plant is one of the most technologically advanced manufacturing facilities in Pirelli.
- Zanardo noted the initiative reflects an approach focused on increasing recovered materials within existing production processes while maintaining consistency in performance and quality.
- Tony Wibbeler, CEO of Bolder Industries, stated that the collaboration demonstrates a traceable, mass-balance approach to tire-to-tire circularity that is achievable and ready to scale inside a premium manufacturing environment.
- The initiative aligns with Pirelli's broader target to reach over 80% bio-based and recycled materials in its best products available on the market by 2030.
- Pirelli also targets reaching 40% bio-based and recycled materials in its total production by 2030.
- The project centers on Pirelli's manufacturing facility located in Rome, Ga.
