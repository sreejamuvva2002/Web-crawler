# Daimler Truck North America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-03-03

**Source:** [Daimler’s "RIZON" Expands in Canada: More Payload and a New Giant in the Background - Truckstop Canada](https://truckstopcanada.ca/2026/03/03/daimlers-rizon-expands-in-canada-more-payload-and-a-new-giant-in-the-background/)  ·  `page_053600`

## Facts

- Daimler Truck North America (DTNA) is expanding its all-electric RIZON brand in British Columbia and Quebec in 2026.
- RIZON trucks (specifically Class 4 and 5) are designed for last-mile urban delivery, reefers, and flatbeds.
- Early adopters in 2024 requested more capacity, prompting chassis upgrades for the 2026 model year.
- The latest 2026 RIZON models feature higher payload capacity to make switching from diesel to electric financially viable for fleets moving heavy pallets.
- Fleet owners in Canada can utilize the federal iMHZEV program (Incentives for Medium- and Heavy-Duty Zero-Emission Vehicles) to offset purchase prices, often stacking it with provincial rebates in BC and Quebec.
- RIZON is technically based on the proven eCanter platform from Mitsubishi Fuso, a Daimler subsidiary.
- In April 2026, Mitsubishi Fuso will merge with Hino Motors (part of the Toyota Group) to form a new global holding company called ARCHION.
- The combined focus of Archion is explicitly on electrification, hydrogen, and autonomous driving.
- For Canadian RIZON customers, the merger ensures long-term parts availability and innovation dominance backed by two of the world's largest truck manufacturers (Daimler & Toyota Group).
- RIZON trucks have a range of up to 250 kilometres on a single charge, depending on the battery pack configuration.
- RIZON supports both Level 2 AC and DC Fast Charging, offering flexibility for overnight depot charging or quick top-ups.
- RIZON comes standard with Daimler’s active safety suite, including Active Brake Assist and Lane Departure Warning.
- With federal incentives still in place and improved payload specs for 2026, RIZON is positioning itself as the leader in zero-emission city logistics.
