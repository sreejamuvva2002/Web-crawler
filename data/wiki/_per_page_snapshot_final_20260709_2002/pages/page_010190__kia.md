# Kia

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-30

**Source:** [Which 2025 Kia Electric Cars Qualify for the $7,500 Tax Credit? A Practical Guide](https://carinterior.alibaba.com/buyingguides/2025-kia-evs-$7.5k-tax-credit-eligibility)  ·  `page_010190`

## Facts

- Kia has shifted its U.S. electric lineup focus from imported EVs to North American production.
- Only select battery electric vehicles (BEVs) qualify for the full $7,500 federal tax credit under current guidance.
- The qualifying status of each Kia model depends on final assembly location, MSRP limits, and compliance with critical mineral and battery component requirements.
- Only vehicles assembled at the West Point, Georgia plant shared with Hyundai are eligible for the full $7,500 credit.
- The 2025 Kia EV6 is available in Wind, GT-Line, and GT trims, but only those built in Georgia qualify for the credit.
- The 2025 Kia EV6 delivers up to 310 miles of EPA-estimated range and supports ultra-fast DC charging via 800V architecture.
- The 800V fast-charging architecture enables adding 200 miles of range in about 18 minutes under ideal conditions.
- The EV6 and EV9 support 10–80% charges in under 20 minutes at compatible stations.
- SUVs must have a final MSRP under $80,000 to qualify for the full credit, while sedans and crossovers must be under $55,000.
- The 2026 Kia EV9 is Kia’s first three-row electric SUV launching in late 2025, with only lower- and mid-tier trims built in Georgia qualifying for the credit.
- Higher trims of the 2026 Kia EV9 are priced above the $80,000 limit for SUVs and are ineligible for the full credit.
- The 2025 Kia Niro EV is a compact crossover with current production being mixed, meaning only future North American builds will qualify for the credit.
- The Kia Sorento PHEV offers a modest electric range of approximately 32 miles and is eligible only for a partial $3,750 credit.
- The federal tax credit expires on September 30, 2025, unless extended by Congress, with no grace period or retroactive eligibility.
- Purchasers must have finalized ownership or lease agreements before September 30, 2025, to claim the credit.
- Income limits do not apply to the federal tax credit itself, but the taxpayer's annual tax liability must equal or exceed $7,500 to claim the full amount.
- Leased vehicles may qualify for the credit regardless of assembly location because the fleet owner claims it and typically passes the benefit to lessees through lower payments.
- The 2025 Kia EV6 starts around $45,000 for the Wind RWD trim.
- The 2026 Kia EV9 starts near $59,000, with top trims exceeding $75,000.
- The 2025 Kia Niro EV starts under $43,000.
- Dealer markups may apply on high-demand trims, especially Georgia-built versions.
- Manufacturer rebates or state-level credits may stack with the federal benefit, though local rules vary by region.
- The 2025 EV6 remains Kia’s top-selling electric model in the U.S., competing directly with the Tesla Model Y, Ford Mustang Mach-E, and Hyundai Ioniq 5.
- Kia offers advanced warranty coverage including an 8-year/100k mile battery warranty.
- The EV9 earned a Top Safety Pick+ rating from IIHS in early 2025.
- Recommended service intervals for Kia EVs are every 12 months or 7,500 miles, focusing on brakes, tires, and battery health checks.
- All qualifying Kia EV models include advanced safety suites featuring forward collision warning, lane keep assist, automatic emergency braking, and adaptive cruise control.
- The EV6 supports bidirectional charging, allowing owners to power tools during outages.
- Availability of Georgia-built trims is limited, creating tight trim selection due to MSRP caps.
- Buyers are advised to ask dealers specifically for 'Georgia-built' or 'North America assembled' VINs and use dealer ordering systems instead of relying on lot stock.
