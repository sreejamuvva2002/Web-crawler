# Hyundai Motor Company

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-06-26

**Source:** [Hyundai’s Georgia supplier network expands to 9 manufacturers | WardsAuto](https://www.automotivedive.com/news/hyundai-suppliers-hanon-systems-woory-industrial-georgia/653718/)  ·  `page_001399`

## Facts

- Two additional Hyundai suppliers are establishing manufacturing sites in Georgia, following the automaker's decision to operate in the state.
- Auto thermal systems manufacturer Hanon Systems will invest over $40 million in a new manufacturing facility.
- Hanon Systems' new plant will be located near Statesboro and will assemble automotive thermal solutions.
- Hanon Systems aims to open its Georgia site in May 2024, creating 160 jobs.
- South Korea-based Woory Industrial Co. is investing $18 million to establish its first U.S. manufacturing facility in Georgia.
- Woory Industrial's new plant will be located in Dublin and will produce electrical heaters, control units, and actuators for EV and internal combustion automakers in North America.
- The Woory Industrial site will create more than 130 jobs, with operations expected to commence in November.
- Woory Industrial CEO Jungwoo Kim stated that the transition to electric vehicles is the future and the U.S. EV market is the key market among all automotive markets.
- Jungwoo Kim added that Georgia will be a good opportunity for Woory Industrial to advance as part of the vanguard of EV development.
- The two new supplier factories follow Hyundai's announcement of a $5.5 billion manufacturing campus in Ellabell, which is the largest economic development project in Georgia's history.
- Georgia officials originally predicted that Hyundai's Metaplant would attract approximately $1 billion to the state via offsite supplier investments.
- As of April, officials stated that Hyundai's suppliers have outpaced that estimate with plans to invest nearly $2 billion in the state.
- Angela Hendrix, senior vice president of marketing and public relations for the Savannah Economic Development Authority, said more supplier announcements are anticipated.
- Hyundai continues to expand its own direct investment in Georgia.
- In late May, Hyundai and LG Energy Solution struck a joint venture deal to build a $4.3 billion battery cell plant adjacent to Hyundai's EV campus.
- Hyundai and LG Energy Solution will each take a 50% stake in the venture, where Hyundai will assemble battery packs using cells from the plant for its U.S. manufacturing sites.
