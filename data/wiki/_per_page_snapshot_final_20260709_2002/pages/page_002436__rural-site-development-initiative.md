# Rural Site Development Initiative

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-29

**Source:** [Kemp Announces Fourth Round of Rural Site Development Grants | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-releases/kemp-announces-fourth-round-rural-site-development-grants)  ·  `page_002436`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced the fourth round of grants from the Rural Site Development Initiative (RSDI) within the OneGeorgia Authority on June 29, 2026.
- The fourth round of RSDI grants totals $7.8 million for ten local projects across Georgia.
- Since the program's launch in FY 2025, the State of Georgia has invested a total of $28 million into rural site development projects.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that over 64 percent of new jobs created since he took office have gone to areas outside the metro Atlanta region, with RSDI being part of this broader effort to bring opportunity to all parts of Georgia.
- Lt. Governor Burt Jones commended Governor Kemp for leading the program, which has awarded $28 million for critical projects in rural communities across Georgia.
- House Speaker Jon Burns highlighted that the grants support good-paying jobs in hardworking Georgians, with specific investments noted in Southeast Georgia navigating unprecedented changes in the forestry industry.
- RSDI grants are awarded for site improvements or to assist with costs of seeking Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) certification on approved projects critical to local economic development.
- Communities receiving grants must match state funding at various levels based on program criteria.
- Georgia Department of Community Affairs Commissioner Christopher Nunn stated that GRAD sites are important to local strategies for attracting employers and investment in Georgia’s communities.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson noted that GRAD sites have helped attract an estimated 10,000 jobs to communities across the state over the last several years.
- Effingham County Development Authority was awarded $1.8 million for site readiness improvements, including a sewer pump station, at the GRAD-certified Savannah Portside International Park, with total project costs of $2.5 million.
- Development Authority of Columbus was awarded $2 million for clearing and grading site improvement work at the GRAD-certified Muscogee Technology Park, with total project costs of $10 million.
- McIntosh County Industrial Development Authority was awarded $1.5 million for road, water, and sewer infrastructure improvements at the GRAD-certified Tidewaters Industrial Park, with total project costs of $2.8 million.
- This is the second site development award for McIntosh County, which previously received a GRAD-Certification Scholarship for this grant.
- Thomaston-Upson County Industrial Development Authority was awarded $2 million to construct water, sewer, and transportation infrastructure at the GRAD-certified Central Georgia Business and Technology Park, with total project costs of $2.3 million.
- Albany-Dougherty Economic Development Commission was awarded $135,000 to certify the Pecan Grove Industrial Park, with total project costs of $180,000.
- Glynn County Development Authority was awarded $107,500 for surveys and research to prepare the Mallard Industrial Park for GRAD certification, with total project costs of $119,500.
- Development Authority of Harris County was awarded $54,400 to certify the largest remaining tract of the Northwest Harris Business Park, with total project costs of $77,500.
- Development Authority of Jeffersonville & Twiggs County was awarded $22,500 for surveys and studies required to certify the Hillandale Tract, with total project costs of $25,000.
- Screven County Development Authority was awarded $121,500 for studies and surveys necessary for the Screven Industrial Park to receive GRAD certification, with total project costs of $135,000.
- Seminole County Development Authority was awarded $47,250 for assessments and surveys to GRAD certify the East Industrial Park, with total project costs of $52,500.
- Eligible activities for grant funds include site studies, land grading, and other essential community infrastructure efforts supporting site development.
- Grantees must demonstrate substantive plans for industrial site development while meeting OneGeorgia Authority eligibility requirements and applicable state laws and regulations.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) serves as the state’s sales and marketing arm and lead agency for attracting new business investment, encouraging industry expansion, and promoting the state for economic development.
- The Georgia Department of Community Affairs (DCA) partners with communities to build strong commercial and industrial areas through community and economic development planning and local government assistance.
