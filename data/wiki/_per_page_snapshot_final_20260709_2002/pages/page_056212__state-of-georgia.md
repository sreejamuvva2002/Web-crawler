# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2025-10-22

**Source:** [Georgia governor to thank Hyundai, LG, SK, CJ during 3-day visit - The Korea Times](https://www.koreatimes.co.kr/business/companies/20251022/georgia-governor-to-thank-hyundai-lg-sk-cj-during-3-day-visit)  ·  `page_056212`

## Facts

- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp will meet high-level executives from Hyundai Motor, LG Energy Solution (LGES), SK On and CJ Foodville during his three-day visit to Korea this week.
- He will express gratitude for their investments in Georgia.
- The visit is an apparent attempt to address negative public sentiment stemming from the mass detention of Korean workers in early September at a construction site in the state.
- Kemp is scheduled to join interviews with Korean-language newspapers but does not plan to meet any government officials.
- Kemp will arrive in Korea on Thursday morning and leave Saturday morning.
- This is his third visit to Korea since his inauguration in 2019.
- The trip was initially arranged to celebrate the 40th anniversary of Georgia's economic development office in Seoul.
- A weeklong detention of more than 300 Korean workers sparked calls for Kemp to offer an apology during his visit.
- The detentions followed a major immigration raid on Sept. 4 at the construction site of a plant jointly operated by Hyundai Motor and LGES.
- Kemp will meet Hyundai Motor Group Vice Chair Chang Jae-hoon on Thursday afternoon to discuss the aftermath of the raid and plant construction progress.
- He will have dinner with Chang Jae-hoon on Friday.
- LGES CEO Kim Dong-myung and SK On CEO Lee Seok-hee will reportedly meet the governor on Friday.
- Kemp stated: “Georgia has enjoyed a strong and friendly relationship with the Republic of Korea for 40 years now.”
- Kemp added: “This visit reflects our commitment to deepening those ties even further, through the creation of opportunities that benefit both our people and our economies.”
- Kemp's office said it has worked with the federal government on addressing visa issues and advocating for clarity in these areas.
- Rep. Kim Joon-hyung of the minor opposition Rebuilding Korea Party said Kemp must offer a sincere apology, provide incentives to Korean investors and ensure safety for Korean workers.
- Rep. Kim Joon-hyung is a former chancellor of the Korea National Diplomatic Academy.
- Industry officials expect the governor will apologize or express regret over the immigration raid incident.
- Skepticism remains that there is little a state government can do in response to federal government policies.
- Georgia repeatedly emphasized the importance of Korean investment in the state before the visit.
- Kemp remarked that the raid highlighted the need for U.S. visa system reform.
- Rep. Lee Un-ju of the ruling Democratic Party of Korea led a delegation of lawmakers to Texas earlier this week.
- Kemp made an unexpected appearance at the Texas venue and promised to submit a white paper to the White House to ensure Korean investors and engineers return home safely after finishing their work in Georgia.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development reaffirmed its long-standing relationship with Korea at the National Day of the Republic of Korea event on Oct. 8 at the Consulate General of the Republic of Korea in Atlanta.
- The department posted on social media: “As Governor Brian Kemp prepares for his mission to Korea later this month, Georgia stands committed to strengthening cultural and business ties, especially during this festive time of Chuseok," Together, we look forward to a future of continued collaboration and success.”
- Montana Gov. Greg Gianforte visited Korea earlier this week to meet with industry leaders and government officials, including Prime Minister Kim Min-seok and Trade Minister Yeo Han-koo.
- Tennessee Gov. Bill Lee will also travel to Korea this week to meet representatives from Hyosung, LG Chem and SK On.
- This occurs amid intensifying competition among U.S. states to retain Korean investors after the Georgia immigration raid.
