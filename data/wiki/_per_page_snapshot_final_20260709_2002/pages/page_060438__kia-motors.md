# Kia Motors

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2020-06-30

**Source:** [Kia unveils new K5 in attempt to halt skidding sedan sales](https://www.cnbc.com/2020/06/30/kia-unveils-new-k5-sedan-in-attempt-to-halt-skidding-sales.html)  ·  `page_060438`

## Facts

- Kia Motors unveiled a new midsize sedan called the K5 to replace the outgoing Optima.
- The K5 will arrive in U.S. dealerships this summer.
- Kia Motors America stated that nearly a decade after the 2011 Kia Optima paved the way for significant growth, the reveal of the all-new K5 resets expectations.
- The 2021 K5 features more standard and available safety features, additional technology, and a new design with elements similar to Kia's Stinger performance sedan.
- Exterior design changes include a new interpretation of Kia's "tiger face" front, a sleeker grille flowing into the headlights, and "heart beat" daytime running lights extending to the side.
- The redesigned interior includes an optional 10.25 inch infotainment touchscreen and wireless Apple CarPlay and Android Auto that mirror phone functions without a cord.
- The K5 is powered by a 1.6 liter turbocharged GDI four-cylinder engine producing 180 horsepower and 195 pound-feet of torque, managed by an all-new eight-speed automatic transmission.
- A GT model with a 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine making 290 horsepower and 311 pound-feet of torque will be available in the fall.
- All-wheel-drive versions of the car will be available in late 2020.
- The K5 will be built at Kia's plant in West Point, Georgia.
- Pricing for the K5 was not announced, while the Optima currently starts at about $24,000.
- After being introduced in 2010, the Optima quickly became Kia's best-selling vehicle in the U.S., with sales surging to nearly 160,000 units in 2015 before declining 39.4% through 2019 and another 16% through the first five months of 2020.
- Kia's overall U.S. sales last year were up 4.4% to more than 615,000 vehicles compared to 2018, while sales this year are down about 13% through May compared to the same period in 2019.
