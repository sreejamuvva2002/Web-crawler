# OneGeorgia Authority's Rural Site Development Initiative

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2025-06-18

**Source:** [Governor Kemp Announces Second Round of Rural Site Development Grants | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-release/governor-kemp-announces-second-round-rural-site-development-grants)  ·  `page_003955`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced the second round of grants in the OneGeorgia Authority's Rural Site Development Initiative on June 18, 2025.
- The program enables Georgia’s rural communities to identify, assess, and develop new, ready-for-development industrial sites.
- Eligible activities for grant funds include site studies, land grading, and other essential community infrastructure efforts that support site development.
- Total funding for this round of grants is $8 million.
- More than $18 million in grants have been distributed to rural site development since the program’s launch.
- Grantees demonstrated substantive plans for industrial site development while meeting OneGeorgia Authority eligibility requirements.
- Applicants leveraged other funding sources to demonstrate community commitment.
- Use of funds is subject to all applicable state laws and regulations, as well as policies and requirements of the OneGeorgia Authority and Department of Community Affairs.
- OneGeorgia funds must be expended within a timeframe specified in grant award documentation, generally two years from the date of the award.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated Georgia offers workforce, connectivity, and business-friendly environment for companies to succeed statewide, noting it has been named top state for business for over a decade.
- Governor Kemp said the initiative helps maintain competitive edge by empowering communities to build on success and turn fields into good-paying jobs for rural Georgians.
- Governor Kemp congratulated receiving communities and thanked legislative partners for strategic funding that made the program possible.
- Speaker Jon Burns stated Georgia's historic economic development success is a direct result of leadership's decades-long dedication to strengthening workforce, investing in infrastructure, and building a better future for Georgians.
- Speaker Burns noted the grants represent the state's commitment to bringing new economic opportunities statewide and looks forward to their impact on rural communities for generations.
- Lt. Governor Burt Jones said the initiative is critical for rural communities where a business-friendly environment is growing at a record pace.
- Lt. Governor Jones stated approved funding will help current projects grow and future projects begin, while keeping Georgia’s business environment a model nationally.
- Department of Community Affairs Commissioner Christopher Nunn stated Georgia’s economic development success results from strong leadership and strategic investment.
- Commissioner Nunn noted the tool enables communities to remain attractive for future prospects and achieve their vision of prosperity.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson said the initiative empowers communities to proactively shape economic growth aligning with long-term goals.
- Commissioner Wilson stated GRAD-certified sites offer speed-to-market advantages for companies and strengthen Georgia’s competitive site portfolio.
- Commissioner Wilson noted investing in new site development ensures sustaining and accelerating economic success momentum.
- Development Authority of the City of Milledgeville and Baldwin County was awarded $2 million for site readiness improvements, including entry road and other road improvements at the 1,500-acre, GRAD-certified Sibley Place.
- Total project costs for the Milledgeville and Baldwin County site readiness improvements are $2,193,982.
- Development Authority of Bulloch County received a grant of $1,518,920 for site grading and sewer improvements at the 69-acre, GRAD-certified Cannady Site.
- The Development Authority of Bulloch County is contributing to the total infrastructure cost of $2,024,960 for the Cannady Site project.
- Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority was awarded a grant of $2 million for sewage and drainage improvements at the 250-acre, “GRAD Select” Georgia Highway 257 Rail Site.
- The Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority is contributing to the total infrastructure cost of $6,039,671 for the rail site project.
- Worth County Economic Development Authority received a grant of $2 million for water, drainage, road, and other infrastructure improvements at the 196-acre, “GRAD Select” Worth County Industrial Complex.
- The total expected project cost for the Worth County Industrial Complex improvements is $2,248,723.
- Development Authority of Burke County was awarded $35,278 to assist with GRAD certification costs for the 150-acre Waynesboro-Burke Industrial Park.
- Total project costs for the Burke County GRAD certification are $39,198.
- Douglas-Coffee County Economic Development Authority was awarded $75,000 to assist with GRAD certification costs for the 72-acre Douglas Aviation Industrial Park.
- Total project costs for the Douglas-Coffee County GRAD certification are $90,050.
- Development Authority of Jefferson County was awarded $75,000 to assist with GRAD certification costs for the 230-acre Louisville Airport Industrial Park.
- Total project costs for the Jefferson County GRAD certification are $82,500.
- McIntosh County Industrial Development Authority was awarded $192,745 to assist with GRAD certification costs for the 247-acre Tidewater East Industrial Park.
- Total project costs for the McIntosh County GRAD certification are $260,345.
- Pulaski County-Hawkinsville Development Authority was awarded $31,455 to assist with GRAD certification costs for the 170-acre Hawkinsville-Pulaski County Industrial Park.
- Total project costs for the Pulaski County-Hawkinsville GRAD certification are $34,950.
- Washington-Wilkes Payroll Development Authority was awarded $142,450 to assist with GRAD certification costs for the 455-acre Washington-Wilkes Industrial Park Expansion.
- Total project costs for the Washington-Wilkes GRAD certification are $167,450.
- The OneGeorgia Authority administers the initiative and sets eligibility requirements for site improvement grants.
- The Department of Community Affairs partners with communities to build strong neighborhoods and commercial/industrial areas through economic development and local government assistance.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) serves as the state’s sales and marketing arm, lead agency for attracting new business investment, encouraging industry expansion, locating markets, promoting tourism/arts/film/digital entertainment, and mobilizing state resources for economic development.
