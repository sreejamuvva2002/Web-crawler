# Anovion Battery Materials

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2023-05-18

**Source:** [Anovion’s $800 Million Georgia Plant Aimed at Securing U.S. Graphite Supply - Global Atlanta](https://www.globalatlanta.com/anovions-800-million-georgia-plant-aimed-at-securing-u-s-graphite-supply/)  ·  `page_001495`

## Facts

- Anovion is the only commercial producer of synthetic graphite in North America.
- The company announced an $800 million investment to build a plant in Bainbridge, Georgia.
- The 1.5 million-square-foot plant will employ 400 people.
- Anovion is scaling up from an existing single plant near Niagara Falls, New York, which is one of the only U.S. facilities producing synthetic graphite commercially today.
- The company was selected for a $117 million Department of Energy grant funded by the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law to support the Georgia project.
- The investment aligns with automakers' efforts to secure battery minerals and components in the U.S. or free-trade partner countries to comply with the Inflation Reduction Act.
- Under current rules, half of the $7,500 EV tax incentive depends on at least 40 percent of critical minerals like graphite being sourced from compliant countries.
- The critical mineral sourcing threshold for the EV tax incentive increases by 10 percent annually, reaching 80 percent in 2027.
- Synthetic graphite can be produced from a petroleum byproduct called needle coke.
- Graphite is a key anode material and represents the most plentiful material in a lithium-ion battery.
- Anovion CEO Eric Stopka stated that graphite demand is expected to outstrip supply by more than 1.2 million tons by 2030 based on roughly one terawatt of American battery production already announced.
- Mr. Stopka noted that today there is almost a negligible amount of synthetic graphite being produced in the United States.
- Once operational, the Bainbridge plant will produce 40,000 metric tons of graphite per year.
- Georgia already has one operational EV battery plant: SK On’s $2.6 billion investment in Commerce.
- A joint-venture plant between SK On and Hyundai Motor is currently being built in Cartersville, Georgia.
- Hyundai Motor is building an EV plant near Savannah, known as "Meta Plant America," which will start production in 2025 and create 8,000 jobs.
- Rivian plans to manufacture its electric trucks in Covington, Georgia.
- CEO Eric Stopka declined to name specific customer prospects but confirmed discussions have been held with automotive OEMs and niche users like the Department of Defense.
- The company's primary growth focus is on the electric vehicle space and grid storage systems to support renewable energy.
- Anovion's corporate motto is "to secure the supply chain of the electrified future."
- Most global graphite processing currently resides in China and uses older methods that do not meet modern stakeholder demands for reduced carbon footprints.
- Anovion was launched in March 2022 as a spinoff from Pyrotek Inc. and Amsted Graphite Materials.
- The company utilizes proprietary furnace technology and controls to avoid outdated incumbent technology used in China, which controls over 90 percent of global graphite processing and production.
- Internal lifecycle assessments indicate that Anovion's materials have lower emissions intensity compared to both mined graphite and rival producers.
- Most graphite used for U.S. cell production is currently shipped from Asia, and locating in Bainbridge will drastically reduce logistics-related emissions by shortening delivery times to U.S. customers.
- Anovion gathered site proposals from 15 states toward the end of the previous year before narrowing them to 10 and finally four prime candidates visited in person.
- Bainbridge was selected for its available land for the 1.5 million square foot plant, good rail lines and barge access, and a workforce described as "technically capable, trainable or upskill-able."
- The company identified sufficient available workers in Decatur County who are currently seeking employment outside the county, allowing Anovion to provide local job opportunities.
- Most plant jobs require computer literacy and skills in operating and maintaining technical equipment, with a long-term goal of building a sustainable employment pipeline through partnerships with local high schools and technical colleges.
- Georgia's expanding clean-energy infrastructure, including new nuclear reactors in Augusta and large-scale solar farms in the south, influenced the company's decision.
- CEO Eric Stopka noted Georgia's grid decarbonization efforts were impressive and important for the company's commitment to climate tech processes.
- Anovion has long-term plans to become carbon neutral.
- The local community and the Georgia Department of Economic Development provided strong support and made the company feel welcome during site visits.
- State officials were well-represented at every visit to Bainbridge, demonstrating strong cross-level government support for the project.
- Governor Brian Kemp welcomed the company, citing his goal to make Georgia the electric mobility capital of the U.S.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated in a release that job creators recognize Georgia's statewide resources and workforce, and he looks forward to partnering with Decatur County residents.
- Anovion is simultaneously expanding its Center of Excellence and hydro-powered facility in New York to scale up production and meet demand.
