# Troup County

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia selected West Point in Troup County for a $1.1 billion assembly plant in 2006, attracted by available land, interstate access via I-85, and state incentives including tax abatements.
- The Kia facility opened in 2009, creating 2,700 direct jobs and spurring over 15,000 indirect positions in suppliers and services.
- The plant's expansions include a 2023 $200 million investment for electric vehicle production like the EV9, adding nearly 200 jobs.
- Kia Georgia (now Hyundai) assembly plant has attracted approximately 30 related supplier projects since 2007, bolstering automotive and component manufacturing.
- Manufacturing accounts for approximately 30% of private-sector employment in Troup County as of early 2025, with 6,754 workers based on 2023 data.
- Kia Motors Manufacturing Georgia is the state's sole dedicated automotive assembly plant, directly employing over 3,000 workers and generating more than 15,000 regional jobs including suppliers.
- Other significant manufacturing subsectors include batteries, films, and chemicals, supported by companies like Duracell and Milliken.
- Hyundai TRANSYS announced a $240 million transmission facility in 2020, operational by 2022, adding 300 jobs.
- Lee Kum Kee's $288 million sauce manufacturing plant (2024) projects 267 positions.
- Walmart and UPS distribution centers are major logistics employers leveraging I-85 access.
- Caterpillar has a heavy equipment production facility in the county.
- A March 2025 proposal for Project West, a $9.7 billion data center campus spanning up to 1.5 million square feet near LaGrange, is expected to generate hundreds of high-wage positions.
- County-wide economic development initiatives have exceeded $800 million in commitments, leveraging industrial parks like the Greater LaGrange Industrial Business Park.
- The county's labor market featured an annual average unemployment rate of 3.2% in 2023, rising to 3.6% in 2024 from historic lows of 2.4% in 2023.
- Building a robust workforce pipeline remains critical due to gaps in talent development hindering scaling for high-tech projects.
- Challenges persist in workforce training and adapting to automation at the Kia plant expansions.
- Gross domestic product increased from $3.68 billion in 2020 to $4.77 billion in 2023, underscoring resilient growth amid national disruptions.
- The county's economy transitioned from textiles to automotive manufacturing following the Kia plant opening, with sustained economic momentum despite housing supply deficits and labor market pressures.
