# Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Joint Corridor Development Authority

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2015-04-24

**Source:** [1,900-acre manufacturing facility sought for I-16 megasite - Coastal Courier](https://coastalcourier.com/news/1900-acre-manufacturing-facility-sought-for-i-16-megasite/)  ·  `page_014419`

## Facts

- The Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Joint Corridor Development Authority filed an application with the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers for a 1,904.5-acre project site to construct a mega-site manufacturing facility at Highway 280 and I-16.
- The site application requests authorizations from both the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers and the state of Georgia.
- The application is believed to be part of a bid to secure Volvo’s first U.S. auto plant.
- Volvo, described as a Swedish-based, Chinese-owned car maker, announced in late March that it would spend $500 million to build a manufacturing facility in the U.S.
- The Atlanta Journal-Constitution reported that Volvo is considering both the Georgia coast and South Carolina for the site.
- Last month, Georgia lawmakers approved more than $17 million to build a manufacturing training center in Pooler.
- The application to the Savannah District of the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers was filed by Joint Development Authority representative Hugh “Trip” Tollison through agent Alton Brown Jr. of Resource and Land Consultants.
- Bryan County Development Authority Chairman Derrick Smith declined to comment on the application or whether Volvo is considering Bryan County.
- Derrick Smith serves on the regional authority alongside County Commission Chairman Jimmy Burnsed.
- The joint corridor development authority includes members from Bryan, Bulloch, Chatham and Effingham counties.
- The authority was formed in the wake of the announced deepening of the Savannah Harbor.
- The authority's reported aim is to work on projects of a regional nature too big for any single county-sized authority to handle.
- A spokesman for the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers stated there is no timeline on permit approval.
