# Hyundai Motor Company Georgia EV Facility

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [South Koreans detained in ICE raid at Hyundai electric vehicle site in Georgia](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cj6xe5d6103o)  ·  `page_009185`

## Facts

- Almost 500 people were arrested at a Hyundai factory in Georgia by immigration authorities during the largest workplace raid of President Donald Trump's second term.
- The 3,000-acre site was built by the Korean company to manufacture electric vehicles and had been operational for one year at the time of the raid.
- A majority of those detained at the facility are Korean nationals.
- South Korea expressed 'concern and regret' over the operation and urged the US government to respect the rights of its citizens.
- The Department of Homeland Security stated agents executed a search warrant due to allegations of 'unlawful employment practices and other serious federal crimes'.
- Steve Schrank, special agent in charge of Homeland Security Investigations in Atlanta, said at a Friday news conference that it was not an immigration operation but a multi-month criminal investigation where evidence, interviews, and documents were gathered for a judicial search warrant.
- Schrank described the raid as 'the largest single-site enforcement operation in the history of homeland security investigations'.
- The raid highlights a tension between President Trump's priorities of building US manufacturing and cracking down on illegal immigration, potentially stressing relations with a key ally.
- President Trump stated in the Oval Office on Friday: 'They were illegal aliens and ICE was just doing its job.'
- Regarding Seoul's reaction, Trump told reporters: 'Well, we want to get along with other countries, and we want to have a great, stable workforce. And we have, as I understand it, a lot of illegal aliens, some not the best of people, but we had a lot of illegal aliens working there.'
- Trump added that the detained workers 'came through with Biden' and 'came through illegally'.
- Immigration officials reported that 475 people who were in the country illegally or working unlawfully were detained.
- The detainees were held at a US Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) facility in Folkston, Georgia, pending further decisions on their placement.
- Of the detained individuals, 300 are reported to be Korean nationals.
- Hyundai Motor Company issued a statement saying it was 'closely monitoring the situation and working to understand the specific circumstances'.
- Hyundai stated that as of the report date, none of those detained are directly employed by the company.
- Reuters reported that Hyundai's electric vehicle production at the site was not affected by the raid.
- LG Energy Solutions, Hyundai's partner in a battery joint venture at the site, paused construction work following the raid.
- South Korea dispatched diplomats to the site and contacted the US embassy in Seoul, urging the US 'to exercise extreme caution' regarding the rights of Korean citizens.
- The South Korean foreign ministry stated that 'the economic activities of Korean investment companies and the rights and interests of Korean citizens must not be unfairly infringed upon during US law enforcement operations'.
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp had previously touted Hyundai's new electric vehicle operation as the biggest economic development project in the state's history, noting it employs 1,200 people.
- South Korean companies have promised to invest billions of dollars in key US industries in the coming years, partly to avoid tariffs levied by Trump.
- The raid occurs amid Trump's broader post-return-to-office effort to round up individuals thought to be in the US illegally, hold them in detention facilities, and frequently deport them.
