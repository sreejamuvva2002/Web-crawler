# Georgia Department of Economic Development

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-15

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp: Economic Development Investments Break New Record | Governor Brian P. Kemp Office of the Governor](https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases/2025-09-15/gov-kemp-economic-development-investments-break-new-record)  ·  `page_003571`

## Facts

- Between July 1, 2024, and June 30, 2025 (Fiscal Year 2025), GDEcD’s Global Commerce team supported 423 facility expansions and new locations.
- These FY25 commitments represent more than $26.3 billion in investment, a new record for the state.
- The projects will create 23,200 new private-sector jobs over the next few years.
- Expansions at existing Georgia companies accounted for 74% of FY25 projects, building on an estimated 50,000 jobs already employing Georgians.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated that Georgia has been the No. 1 state for business for over a decade due to its unmatched ability to attract new companies and support existing employers.
- Lt. Governor Burt Jones highlighted ongoing investments in workforce programs with the University System of Georgia, the Technical College System of Georgia, and College Career Academies to ensure a steady talent pipeline for current and future industry partners.
- 77% of all FY25 expansions and new locations occurred in communities outside the 10-county Atlanta region.
- Metro Atlanta remains one of the Southeast’s top business hubs, with companies like TriNet, A.I.G., CRH, Duracell, and Mercedes-Benz establishing or expanding key corporate functions in the 10-county region.
- Georgia attracted more than 6,500 new jobs from international companies expanding or locating in the U.S., with the Republic of Korea, Japan, and Canada as top investment sources.
- International companies committed to more than $3 billion in investment, with 64% slated for expansions within the state.
- Notable international investments include Irving Tissue’s expansion in Macon, GreenBox Systems’ new location in Butts County, and KISS USA’s expansion in Bryan County.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson noted that Georgia’s stable, reliable business climate gives companies confidence to plan for the long term.
- Georgia is investing over $18 million to help rural communities prepare new industrial sites through the Rural Site Development Initiative.
- These grants support site improvements and add locations to the “Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development” (GRAD) Program, which now includes more than 70 certified sites.
- Deputy Commissioner of Global Commerce Misti Martin stated that programs like the Rural Site Development Initiative and site-readiness certification help communities attract new jobs by offering speed-to-market solutions.
- Georgia remains the No. 1 state for business according to Area Development magazine, a title held for over a decade.
