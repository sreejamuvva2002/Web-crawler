# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-09-19

**Source:** [Georgia Tech and Hyundai Motor Group Sign MoU for Future Mobility Collaboration](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/georgia-tech-and-hyundai-motor-group-sign-mou-for-future-mobility-collaboration-301932726.html)  ·  `page_002787`

## Facts

- Georgia Tech and Hyundai Motor Group signed a memorandum of understanding on Sept. 19, 2023, to collaborate on research and applications for sustainable mobility.
- The MoU partnership focuses on developing a hydrogen economy, workforce development, and making cities smarter and more sustainable.
- Hyundai Motor Group is investing more than $12 billion in EV manufacturing and battery production in Georgia.
- Hyundai's economic impact in Georgia will be tens of billions of dollars in the years to come.
- The partnership will help create nearly 40,000 jobs.
- The collaboration builds upon Hyundai Motor Group's long history in Georgia.
- The Group is investing $12.6 billion in new dedicated electric vehicle and battery manufacturing facilities in Georgia.
- The investment includes the $7.59 billion Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) site in Bryan County, Georgia.
- The HMGMA facility will create more than 8,500 direct jobs.
- The investment includes a $5 billion battery manufacturing facility joint venture with SK On in Bartow County, Georgia.
- The SK On joint venture facility will employ approximately 3,500 people.
- Proximity to Georgia Tech was one of the important considerations in deciding the locations for these facilities.
- Kia Georgia's West Point assembly plant began vehicle production in 2009 under the leadership of Honorary Chairman Mong-Koo Chung and Executive Chair Euisun Chung.
- University System of Georgia Chancellor Sonny Perdue participated in the HMG-Georgia Tech MOU ceremony.
- Sonny Perdue was the Governor of Georgia at the time of the Kia Georgia plant's opening.
- A recent study by the Center for Automotive Research reveals that Hyundai Motor's automotive operations contributed $20.1 billion and 190,000 jobs to the U.S. economy in 2021 alone.
- The study states Hyundai's investments in Georgia are expected to create nearly 40,000 jobs and $4.6 billion in individual earnings every year.
- Georgia Tech President Ángel Cabrera said Hyundai is a global brand synonymous with quality, innovation, and a commitment to advancing technology to make a positive difference in the world.
- Cabrera noted that the alignment of values between Georgia Tech and Hyundai has become obvious as they have gotten to know each other.
- Cabrera expressed gratitude for the transformative investments Hyundai is making in the state.
- Cabrera stated he is proud that the Hyundai brand will feature prominently on the campus.
- Cabrera looks forward to working with Hyundai leaders to deepen the partnership, develop exceptional leaders, and produce new ideas that will shape the automotive industry and advance mobility.
- Jay Chang, President and CEO of Hyundai Motor Company, said Georgia is like a second home to the company.
- Chang described Hyundai as a mission-driven organization with a compelling point of view about the future.
- Chang stated the company feels a great responsibility to contribute to a sustainable future where all humankind can have a safe and healthy life while enjoying freedom of movement.
- Chang expressed thrill to create a partnership with Georgia Tech including research and applications for sustainable mobility, hydrogen economy, workforce development, and smart cities.
- Chang stated the partnership will last for decades and was one of the reasons they chose Georgia for EV investments.
- José Muñoz, President and Global COO of Hyundai Motor Company and President and CEO of Hyundai and Genesis Motor North America, said Hyundai's investments in Georgia will create tens of thousands of jobs and bring tens of billions of dollars in economic impact for decades to come.
- Muñoz stated proximity to institutions like Georgia Tech was one of the many reasons Hyundai selected Georgia for their new EV manufacturing facility.
- Hyundai Motor Group is a global enterprise that has created a value chain based on mobility, steel and construction, logistics, finance, IT, and service.
- The company has about 250,000 employees worldwide.
- Hyundai Motor Group's mobility brands include Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis.
