# Butler, Covington, Crenshaw & Lowndes Counties

**Type:** unknown  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-04-29

**Source:** [Spotlight on Butler, Covington, Crenshaw & Lowndes Counties: Economic Engines](https://businessalabama.com/spotlight-on-butler-covington-crenshaw-lowndes-counties-economic-engines/)  ·  `page_056038`

## Facts

- The automotive sector is the largest industry in Butler County, employing more than 1,100 workers.
- Butler County is home to three companies in the supply chain for Hyundai and Kia: Hwashin America Corp., Hyundai Steel, and DL/A.
- Hwashin America is a Tier 1 automotive chassis and body parts supplier that has undergone several expansions at its Greenville facility, adding jobs and investing millions of dollars.
- In November 2025, Hwashin America announced it will create 40 more jobs.
- The expansion is slated to open in October 2027.
- In November 2025, Hwashin America Corp. announced it will begin construction of a new manufacturing facility in the area that will involve an investment of more than $60 million.
- Connector Manufacturing Co., a Hubbell brand, is a manufacturer of machined metal parts in Greenville.
- After a recent expansion, Hubbell Corp. is now the third largest employer in Butler County, with approximately 280 employees.
- Over the past two years, the company has invested more than $25 million in the Greenville community.
- David Hutchison serves as executive director of the Butler County Commission for Economic Development.
- Tracy Salter is the executive director of the Greenville Area Chamber of Commerce.
- In April 2025, Four Star Freightliner opened a new location in Georgiana, marking the company’s eighth dealership.
- The new facility, strategically located off I-65, provides drivers and fleet owners with a convenient, full-service location for repairs, maintenance and parts.
- To open the location, Four Star Freightliner invested $200,000 in the community and added 10 new jobs.
- In Crenshaw County, ITAC Alabama, formerly known as SMART, makes automotive frames for Hyundai vehicles.
- In late 2025, ITAC Alabama announced a 37,000-square-foot expansion at its current facility.
- When complete, the expansion is expected to create 100 jobs.
- Dongwon Autopart Technology Alabama makes automotive door parts, inner assemblies and more in Luverne.
- In Lowndes County, Daehan Solutions Alabama makes interior sound solutions components and employs almost 300 workers.
- Many of the products from Daehan Solutions Alabama are headed for the Hyundai plant in Montgomery.
- Sejong Alabama LLC, located in Fort Deposit, makes mufflers and exhaust systems.
- Many of the products from Sejong Alabama LLC are also headed for the Hyundai plant in Montgomery.
- In August 2025, the Busy Ant Travel Center broke ground on an $8 million facility in Georgiana, just off Interstate 65.
- The facility will include a 12,000-square-foot truck stop featuring several stores including Subway, an electric vehicle charging station and fueling stations.
- The project is estimated to bring 30 new jobs to the community.
- Jacob Morgan serves as president and CEO of the Covington County Economic Development Commission.
