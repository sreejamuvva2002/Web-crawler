## Facts

- WIKA USA has implemented a succession planning initiative called the Professional Development Program (PDP), which was formerly known as the Graduate Training Program.
- Eugenys Contreras was the first participant of the PDP, completing the program in July 2021 and immediately transitioning to a business analyst role in the IT group’s operations systems division.
- The PDP involves department rotations, participation in high-level meetings, and mentorship from company executives.
- WIKA USA oversees its Americas operations from its Lawrenceville headquarters.
- WIKA operates divisions and locations in Houston (Diaphragm Seal Division), Ohio (WIKA Sensor Technology), Pennsylvania (WIKA Mobile Control), as well as subsidiaries in Canada, Brazil, Argentina, and other countries.
- Eugenys Contreras is based out of WIKA’s Houston branch, traveling there for one week each month.
- Contreras led her first Manufacturing Systems process team meeting for all of the Americas, utilizing a shared ERP system across distant locations.
- WIKA USA participates in community service initiatives, including sponsoring the Angel Tree for Georgia CASA (Court Appointed Special Advocates) in the greater Atlanta area, where 57 kids were sponsored within one day with an employee waitlist.
- Other PDP participants include Justin Savage in the Lawrenceville office, who has a background in supply chain management and recently accepted a role as inventory controller after his first rotation; Shelby Becker in the Houston office, who has a degree in mechanical engineering and is on her second rotation; and Elijah Mattox-Davis in the Lawrenceville office, who studied industrial and systems engineering and joined the PDP in September.
- Brent Shadix serves as vice president of Human Resources and co-chair of the PDP.
- Felix Brockmeyer serves as vice president of Industrial Instrumentation and the other co-chair of the PDP.
- WIKA USA is currently accepting applications for PDP project specialists and other open positions.
