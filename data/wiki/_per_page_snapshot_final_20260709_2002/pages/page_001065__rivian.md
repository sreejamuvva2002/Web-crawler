# Rivian

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-05-05

## Facts

- Rivian is accelerating production at its upcoming plant in Georgia.
- Production will see a 20% increase during the first year of operations to 'build out its supply chain and manufacturing footprint,' according to Rivian.
- Rivian is aiming to reach 150,000 vehicles annually by bringing down the cost of its next generation of $25,000 and $35,000 electric vehicles.
- Rivian is increasing its production capacity to 150,000 vehicles annually for its full-size manufacturing facility in Georgia.
- The 150,000 vehicle annual capacity represents a 20% increase over the initially planned capacity of 125,000 units.
- The increased capacity is intended to facilitate a lower cost vehicle in later years.
- Rivian and Gov. Brian Kemp are accelerating plans for a new plant at the Chattahoochee Hills site between Columbus and Atlanta.
- Gov. Brian Kemp said they are excited to work with the Georgia Department of Economic Development to grow their manufacturing footprint in Georgia.
- Kemp stated the project directly expands workforce opportunities and will create thousands of jobs in addition to Rivian’s growth as a global leader in innovation and technology.
- Rivian anticipates construction to begin next year after the agreement was first announced by Rivian CEO RJ Scaringe in 2024.
- The State of Georgia brokered the deal for Rivian to build a $15 billion plant in the Chattahoochee Hills, which included more than 50,000 jobs in two phases.
