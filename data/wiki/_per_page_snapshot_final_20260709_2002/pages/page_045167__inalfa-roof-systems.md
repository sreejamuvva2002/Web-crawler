# Inalfa Roof Systems

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Inalfa - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inalfa)  ·  `page_045167`

## Facts

- Inalfa Roof Systems is a tier-one automotive supplier serving over 40 vehicle brands globally.
- Its customer list includes Daimler Benz, BMW, Chrysler, Ford, General Motors, Volkswagen, Audi, Volvo, Hyundai, Kia, Honda, Land Rover, and Nissan.
- The company delivers more than 2.5 million roof modules annually.
- It specializes in complex automotive sunroof and reconfigurable roof systems.
- Its operations include designing, developing, manufacturing, prototyping, testing, assembling, and supplying sunroof modules.
- The company is headquartered in Venray, Netherlands.
- It operates as a wholly owned subsidiary of Beijing Hainachuan Automotive Parts Co., Ltd. (BHAP).
- BHAP is headquartered in Beijing, China.
- Inalfa was established by J.A.M. Burgman in Utrecht, Netherlands in 1946.
- Initially, the company manufactured aluminium household supplies including knitting needles, stair rods, and curtain rods.
- The company moved to Venray in 1956 and began manufacturing metal profiles, stamped metal parts, and oil furnaces.
- In 1974, the company began manufacturing automotive sunroofs, initially for Jaguar and Rover.
- By 2011, the company had been owned by two Northern European mid-market buyout firms: AAC Capital Partners and its co-shareholder Parcom Capital.
- In 2011, it became a wholly owned subdivision of Beijing Hiananchuan Automotive Parts (BHAP).
- Inalfa is known for marketing complex roofing solutions, including the highly reconfigurable roofing of the Citroën C3 Pluriel.
- It supplied the sliding rear cargo roof of the GMC Envoy XUV.
- It supplied the fully retractable, all-glass panoramic roof of the second generation Lincoln MKZ.
- Inalfa operates production facilities in the United States, Japan, Mexico, Brazil, South-Korea, China, and Poland.
- Its global headquarters remains in Venray.
