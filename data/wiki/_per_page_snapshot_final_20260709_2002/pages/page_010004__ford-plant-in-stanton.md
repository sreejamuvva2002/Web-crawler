# Ford Plant in Stanton

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-01-27

**Source:** [FORD: Don’t Get The Memphis Benefit – Memphis Corporate Community Leadership Measured](https://mcclmeasured.net/dontget/)  ·  `page_010004`

## Facts

- The Ford plant will be located in Stanton, Haywood County, TN, approximately 50 minutes away from Memphis.
- Discussions are beginning about a light rail connection from Memphis to Stanton to facilitate workforce transportation.
- The author questions whether the proposed light rail would only serve as a temporary need before workers move closer to Stanton after sustaining higher incomes.
- The Ford plant deployment is compared to Kia's plant in West Point, Troup County, GA, located near the larger population center of Columbus, Muscogee County, GA.
- Kia opened its plant in West Point, GA in 2006 and employs approximately 2,700 workers.
- The Ford plant will employ 6,000 workers, drawing from a larger population base in Shelby County than the Georgia region referenced for Kia.
- A proportional similarity exists between projected occupational demands and regional populations in both the TN and GA regions based on their respective demographics.
- BLS sourced data chart analysis compares Kia's economic impact on county total wage growth, mapping West Point’s Troup County to Haywood County, Columbus’ Muscogee County to Shelby County, and Georgia to Tennessee.
- In 2006, when Kia production started, Troup County emerged from negative total wage growth when benchmarked against 2001 total wages.
- In 2006, the population center of the region in Columbus GA’s Muscogee County experienced accelerated growth that briefly fell off at the beginning of the Great Recession alongside the State of Georgia and Troup County.
- In 2009, as visibility began to clear from the Great Recession, Muscogee County’s percentage total wage growth exceeded the State of GA, while Troup County’s percentage total wage growth exceeded both the state and Muscogee County.
- From 2012 to 2019, Troup County’s total wage growth sustained exceeding the percentage wage growth of Muscogee County, decelerating below the State of GA in 2015 but remaining competitive with the state through 2019.
- Columbus GA’s Muscogee County and its population center were not competitive with GA statewide percentage total wage growth, raising questions about the future economic impact on Memphis and Shelby County from the Ford plant deployment in remote Haywood County.
- The author questions whether a Memphis/Shelby County economic development plan exists to help ensure that Shelby County has competitive percentage total wage growth compared to the State of Tennessee.
- The author concludes that a local economic development plan emphasizing Memphis and Shelby County small business and workforce development is needed instead of relying on the Ford plant in Haywood County.
