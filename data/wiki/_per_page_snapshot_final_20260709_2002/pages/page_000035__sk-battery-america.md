# SK Battery America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-01-30

**Source:** [SK Battery America Exceeds Hiring Goal, On Track to Reach 3,000 Worker | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-releases/2023/sk-battery-america-exceeds-hiring-goal-track-reach-3000-workers)  ·  `page_000035`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced on January 30, 2023, that SK Battery America (SKBA) has exceeded its hiring goal of 2,600 employees two years ahead of schedule for its two lithium-ion battery manufacturing facilities in Commerce, Georgia.
- The company met its initial hiring goal at the end of 2022.
- SKBA is expanding its workforce to approximately 3,000 over the next year to meet growing demand for electric vehicles.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that SK Battery America and the State of Georgia share a commitment to reaching the next milestone and thanked SKBA for going above and beyond in delivering on their commitments.
- SK Battery America announced its first project in Georgia in 2018, with an initial planned investment of $1.6 billion.
- Since 2019, the company has invested $2.6 billion to build two battery manufacturing facilities in Jackson County.
- The facilities supply batteries for electric vehicles including the Ford F-150 Lightning and the Volkswagen ID.4.
- SKBA started mass production in early 2022.
- SK Battery America CEO Timothy Jeong credited the state’s Quick Start program for helping attract and train workers with speed and scale.
- More than 300,000 new electric vehicles a year will be on American roads because of the work being done at the Georgia facilities.
- SKBA has worked with Georgia Quick Start, the state’s top-ranked job training program through the Technical College System of Georgia, since announcing its first project in 2018.
- In July 2022, SKBA announced a partnership with the Georgia National Guard’s Work for Warriors Georgia program to focus on hiring veterans, servicemembers, retirees, reservists, and their families.
- The company has held numerous career fairs, visited schools, and held community events in recent months to attract talent.
- To attract the highest-quality workforce, SKBA has consistently improved wages, benefits, and its overall work environment.
- Tom Crow, Chairman of the Jackson County Board of Commissioners, expressed appreciation for SKBA’s commitment to providing high quality jobs and its substantial financial investment in the county.
- The company is still hiring for jobs in production, quality, utility, logistics, ERT, maintenance, and other areas.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson praised SKBA’s relentless pursuit of hiring Georgians for high-skilled, high-paying jobs and its commitment to meeting standing commitments and aggressive hiring needs.
- SK On is the parent company of SK Battery America.
- SK Battery America produces one of the world’s safest, fastest charging and longest-lasting batteries, currently installed in the Ford F-150 Lightning and Volkswagen ID.4.
- The two facilities will have a combined capacity to make 22 GWh of battery cells a year.
- This production capacity is enough to power the equivalent of about 300,000 EVs annually.
- Since 2019, the company has invested a total of $2.6 billion to build EV battery plants in Commerce, Georgia, creating 2,600 high-skilled jobs.
- Governor Kemp and General Carden previously announced SK Battery America becoming a ‘Work for Warriors’ partner.
- SK Innovation announced an additional investment of $940 million for a Jackson County expansion that will create 600 jobs.
- SK Innovation announced it would begin construction on its second Georgia battery facility in July.
