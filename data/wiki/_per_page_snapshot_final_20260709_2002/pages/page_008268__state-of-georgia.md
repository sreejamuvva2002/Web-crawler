# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2023-07-01

**Source:** [Kemp, GDEcD announces Georgia job creation remains strong](https://valdostatoday.com/news-2/region/2024/09/kemp-gdecd-announces-georgia-job-creation-remains-strong/)  ·  `page_008268`

## Facts

- Between July 1, 2023, and June 30 of Fiscal Year 2024, GDEcD’s Global Commerce team supported 429 facility expansions and new locations statewide.
- These FY2024 facility expansions and new locations resulted in more than $20.3 billion in investment and the commitment of 26,900 new private sector jobs across Georgia.
- 83% of FY2024 facility expansions and new locations are located in communities outside the 10-county Atlanta region, representing more than 18,300 new jobs and $12.5 billion in investment.
- Metro Atlanta attracted nearly $7.8 billion in investments during FY2024 as a logistics and business hub.
- Statewide, new locations accounted for $12.8 billion in investments, while expansions were responsible for 9,800 new jobs.
- Manufacturing, including the aerospace, automotive, and e-mobility sectors, accounted for over 50% of facility expansions or new locations in FY2024.
- Kia Georgia added the EV9 to its lineup at its facility in Troup County during FY2024.
- Toyota Industries Corporation established a campus in Jackson County during FY2024.
- StandardAero expanded operations in Richmond County during FY2024.
- International life sciences company Gerresheimer announced its most recent expansion in Fayette County during FY2024.
- Suppliers for Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America announced new locations across Bryan, Candler, Chatham, Coweta, Effingham, Harris, and Laurens Counties.
- SOLARCYCLE, an innovator in solar panel recycling, announced plans for a new location in Polk County to support solar panel manufacturer Qcells’ operations.
- Governor Brian Kemp led economic development missions to Korea and Switzerland during FY2024, focusing on strengthening relationships with Hyundai Motor Group and its supplier network.
- During the prior fiscal year, Governor Kemp led similar economic development missions to Israel, France, and Switzerland.
- Georgia maintained its position as a global gateway for business, landing more than 8,100 new jobs and $5.9 billion in investments from international companies expanding or locating in the U.S. market in FY2024.
- Lt. Governor Burt Jones highlighted that the Technical College System of Georgia, University System of Georgia, College and Career Academies, and private-public partnerships are building Georgia’s workforce pipeline through workforce training programs.
- Speaker of the House Jon Burns noted that the state has delivered historic investments in education, workforce development, and infrastructure alongside tax cuts and regulatory relief.
- The State of Georgia celebrated more than 50 years of continuous economic representation in Europe and Japan during FY2024, which remain among the top sources of foreign direct investment.
- The coming fiscal year will mark 40 years of continuous economic representation in Korea, a partnership that earned GDEcD the James A. Van Fleet Award from the Korea Society.
- All markets where Georgia maintains business and trade representation accounted for 29% of total new jobs and investments in FY2024.
- Investments in food processing and agribusiness totaled more than $1.3 billion in FY2024.
- Japanese probiotic beverage company Yakult located in Bartow County during FY2024.
- Korean bakery CJ Foodville located in Hall County during FY2024.
- Walmart will source milk from local dairy farmers at a future dairy processing facility in Lowndes County.
- Cargill Corporation is establishing a new office hub in the City of Atlanta, creating at least 4,400 new jobs as part of FY2024 wins.
- U.S. Soccer’s future headquarters and national training center in Fayette County reinforces Georgia’s position within entertainment, sports, and tourism.
- Georgia earned the rank of No. 1 state for business from Area Development magazine for 10 consecutive years.
- Georgia earned the No. 1 spot in Site Selection magazine’s annual Prosperity Cup rankings for successful investment recruitment in 2023.
- Georgia was awarded a Gold Shovel by Area Development for achieving significant success in attracting new business investment and job creation in 2023.
- Business Facilities magazine ranked Georgia as the No. 1 state for EV investments, as well as a top 10 state for food processing, cybersecurity, aerospace and defense, and AI growth hubs.
