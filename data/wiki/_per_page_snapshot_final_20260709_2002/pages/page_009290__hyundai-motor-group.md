# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor is unveiling its first electric three-row SUV, IONIQ 7, at the Busan International Motor Show in June.
- The IONIQ 7 is built on Hyundai Motor's unique E-GMP platform.
- Hyundai's IONIQ 5 and IONIQ 6 models are favored by consumers for their excellent performance and design.
- According to S&P Global, Hyundai ranked fourth in electric vehicle sales in the U.S. market last year, accounting for 4.5% of the market.
- Unlike rivals such as Ford and General Motors, which have slowed down electric vehicle production, Hyundai has shown strong momentum.
- Hyundai Motor will open its first large-scale electric vehicle and battery factory in Georgia this year to further consolidate its position in the electric vehicle field.
- Hyundai Motor America CEO Jose Munoz said the company is working hard to qualify for electric vehicle tax credits to reduce costs and improve competitiveness.
- The US$7.6 billion Georgia factory is expected to be put into operation in October this year.
- The facility is expected to provide IONIQ tax credits for Class 7 models.
- The IONIQ 7 will reportedly be paired with Kia's electric three-row SUV, the EV9, sharing some components and having 8 inches of ground clearance.
- The IONIQ 7 is expected to offer multiple battery capacity options and support up to 350 kW charging capacity for fast charging in a short time.
- Hyundai Motor plans to start producing the IONIQ 7 at its Asan plant in South Korea in July 2024.
- Hyundai Motor plans to introduce the IONIQ 7 to its new plant GA (Georgia) for production next year.
- The IONIQ 7 and Kia EV9 share the same E-GMP platform but have obvious differences in appearance and performance.
- The front face design of the IONIQ 7 features a majestic look with a sloping A-pillar, giving it a smoother, more fashionable, and dynamic appearance compared to the EV9.
- Hyundai Motor announced a groundbreaking ceremony for a new electric vehicle-specific factory in Ulsan, with an investment of 2 trillion won (approximately 11.08 billion yuan).
- Full construction of the Ulsan factory will begin in the fourth quarter of 2023, with mass production starting in the first quarter of 2026.
- The new Ulsan factory covers an area of 548,000 m2 and has an annual production capacity of approximately 200,000 vehicles.
- The Genesis luxury electric SUV will be the first model produced in the new Ulsan factory.
