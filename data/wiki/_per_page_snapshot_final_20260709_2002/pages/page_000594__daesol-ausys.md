# Daesol Ausys

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [GA: Automotive Supplier Daesol Ausys Georgia to Invest $72 Million in Harris County | Trade and Industry Development](https://www.tradeandindustrydev.com/region/georgia/news/ga-automotive-supplier-daesol-ausys-georgia-invest-32293)  ·  `page_000594`

## Facts

- Georgia Gov. Brian P. Kemp announced that Daesol Ausys Georgia will establish a new manufacturing facility in West Point.
- The facility will create more than 140 new jobs and $72 million in investment in Harris County.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that West Point has become an automotive capital in its own right, and he is excited that Daesol Ausys will join other innovators in Harris County.
- Governor Brian Kemp noted that Georgia's automotive industry employs over 50,000 hardworking Georgians and will continue to grow as e-mobility suppliers choose all corners of the state for the jobs of the future.
- Daesol Ausys was established in Korea in 2017.
- The company specializes in designing and manufacturing motor and electric vehicle interior components and accessories.
- It is a key supplier for Kia Georgia, Hyundai Motor Group, and General Motors.
- Min Ho Kwon, CEO of Daesol Ausys, stated the company is committed to delivering world-class services that exceed its clients’ requirements and expectations.
- Min Ho Kwon expressed excitement about this project in the Northwest Harris Business Park and looking forward to continuing the partnership with the State of Georgia and Harris County.
- Daesol Ausys’ new facility will produce luggage boards and luggage covers.
- Operations are expected to begin in December 2024.
- The company will be hiring for positions in production, purchasing, quality control, maintenance, human resources, and management.
- Robert Grant, Chairman of the Harris County Commission, stated that Daesol Ausys’ decision to locate in the Northwest Harris Business Park represents a commitment to Harris County.
- Robert Grant noted that West Point and Harris County have a wonderful relationship with Daesol Ausys’ sister companies Daehan Solutions and Daesol Materials, and they have been working closely with company officials to get this new venture in West Point.
- Craig Greenhaw, Chairman of the Harris County Development Authority, extended a gracious welcome to Daesol Ausys and expressed looking forward to a long and mutually beneficial partnership with their newest corporate citizen.
- Craig Greenhaw stated that West Point and Harris County continue to work well together, calling this venture another successful one that brings jobs and capital investment into the region.
- Alyce Thornhill, Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy, represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project in partnership with the Harris County Commission, Harris County Development Authority, City of West Point, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that economic development is a team sport that is driven by the local level, and congratulated Harris County and community partners for helping bring Daesol Ausys to West Georgia.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson noted that the automotive industry is providing generational change in the region and across the state, and expressed excitement for the continuation of this growth and the opportunities that Daesol Ausys will provide to the people of Harris County.
- For over a century, Georgia has fostered healthy industry practices, encouraged collaboration and innovation, and positioned the state as a leader in developing and harnessing emerging technologies for the evolving automotive and mobility industry.
- As the market transitions to electric vehicles, Georgia has pursued the entire supply chain, creating more than $25 billion in investments and 30,000 jobs since 2018.
- In fiscal year 2023, job creation in the automotive industry increased by 324 percent when compared to FY21.
- Suppliers attracted by Georgia’s mobility original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) resulted in over $2 billion in investment across the state in FY23.
