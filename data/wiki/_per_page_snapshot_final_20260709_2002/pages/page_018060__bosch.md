## Facts

- German automotive supplier Bosch plans to lay off 5,000 employees.
- Some 3,800 of the job cuts are to be made in Germany.
- The exact number of layoffs will be negotiated in talks with workers' representatives.
- Bosch stated it is making significant investments in new technologies.
- Bosch manager Stephan Hölzl said, 'We must adapt our structures to the changing market environment and reduce costs sustainably to strengthen our competitiveness.'
- Global vehicle production will stagnate this year at around 93 million units, if not decline slightly compared to the previous year.
- Manufacturers need significantly fewer parts to make electric vehicles, also making the process less labor-intensive.
- Bosch plans to cut up to 1,300 jobs between 2027 and 2030 at its division that makes steering systems for cars and trucks, which is based in Swäbisch Gmünd in the southwestern German state of Baden Württemberg.
- The firm had already announced plans in December 2023 to lay off 1,500 workers.
- Frank Sell, head of the workers' council for Bosch's automotive division in Germany, called the planned layoffs a 'slap in the face' and vowed resistance to the move.
- The crisis has hit a number of other German manufacturers, with ZF, Continental and Webasto also announcing layoffs.
- Volkswagen (VW) has said it plans wide-reaching plant closures and layoffs in order to reduce labor costs, citing difficulties breaking into the competitive electric vehicle market.
- Workers' representatives urged VW to strike a deal and threatened potential industrial action starting in December if talks fall through.
- The European Union increased tariffs on Chinese-imported electric vehicles to as much as 45.3%.
