# Georgian Energy Sector

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Russian Capital in Georgian Energy sector - GeorgianJournal](https://georgianjournal.ge/business/31331-russian-capital-in-georgian-energy-sector.html)  ·  `page_051960`

## Facts

- Russian capital is growing in Georgia across communication, water supply, mineral waters, banking, and energy sectors.
- Inter Rao owns a lion’s share of Georgian energy facilities including JSC Telasi (75 percent of shares), Mtkvari Energy LLC, Khrami HPP-1, Khrami HPP-2, and Enguri HPP.
- All shareholders of Inter Rao are companies affiliated with the Russian government.
- Igor Sechin serves as the board chairman of Inter Rao and is one of Vladimir Putin's closest friends.
- Russian citizen Mevlud (Evgeny) Bliadze possesses 70 percent of shares of Energy LLC, which owns Dariali HPP, Larsi HPP, and Shilda HPP.
- According to the National Statistics Office of Georgia, the largest portion of investments made by Russia into Georgia in 2011 can be credited precisely to Bliadze.
- Energy expert Eugene Kogan states that having a big share of Russian capital in Georgia means Russia maintains economic and financial leverage over Georgia.
- Several other hydropower objects owned by Russians include Avani HPP, Algeti HPP, and Skurdidi HPP.
- SakRusEnergo, which runs the country’s electricity transmission system, is jointly owned by the Georgian and Russian side.
- Research author Nata Dzvelishvili notes that the sale of strategic energy facilities to Russians started in the 2000s when Inter Rao entered the market.
- After the August War in 2008, Enguri HPP was given to Inter Rao, followed by Khrami HPP-1 and Khrami HPP-2.
- Both Khrami-1 and Khrami-2 were sold directly without allowing other investors an opportunity to participate in a competition.
- Eugene Kogan says Russia wishes to maintain control over Georgia not only politically but also economically and socially.
- Kogan claims that Russian investment does not provide the promised extra funds or extra employment for Georgia.
- The current Georgian government is described as being dead scared of the influx of Russian capital but maintains silence and is too timid to undertake anti-Russian measures.
- Liza Tavdumadze, head of Investment Projects Department of Ministry of Energy, stated that the ministry does not see the threat of any country becoming an energy monopolist in Georgia.
- Kogan suggests Russia intends to get a hold of the majority of energy companies to dictate its own terms, mainly concerning price regulations.
- The price of electricity in Armenia is currently set by Russia.
