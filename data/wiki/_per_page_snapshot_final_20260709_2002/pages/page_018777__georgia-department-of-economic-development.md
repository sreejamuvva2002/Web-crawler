# Georgia Department of Economic Development

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2022-08-09

**Source:** [Georgia Shatters Investment and Job Records in FY22 | Development Authority of Peach County](https://peachcountydevelopment.com/news/article/georgia-shatters-investment-and-job-records-in-fy222)  ·  `page_018777`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp and GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson announced that Georgia achieved a record year for economic development investments and job creation in fiscal year 2022 on August 10, 2022.
- Investments during FY22 were 94 percent above prior economic development records.
- Job creation during FY22 was 53 percent above prior economic development records.
- Total investments in expansions and new locations during FY22 (July 1, 2021 – June 30, 2022) totaled more than $21.2 billion.
- A total of 51,132 jobs were created statewide through 358 projects supported by GDEcD’s Global Commerce team during FY22.
- Excluding the two largest projects in state history – Rivian and Hyundai Motor Group – Georgia jobs for the year totaled 35,532.
- Excluding the two largest projects in state history – Rivian and Hyundai Motor Group – Georgia investments for the year totaled $10.7 billion.
- The Rivian and Hyundai Motor Group projects were part of 12 automotive projects that created a total of nearly 16,000 new jobs across the state.
- Eighty-five percent of the investments came from outside the 10-county metro Atlanta region.
- More than 30,000 new jobs came from outside the 10-county metro Atlanta region.
- Companies choosing to expand in Georgia accounted for 74 percent of total projects.
- New locations created 33,846 jobs across the state.
- The advanced manufacturing sector added more than 10,000 new jobs in the state.
- Advanced manufacturing includes manufacturers and suppliers for the electric vehicle (EV) industry.
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America’s (HMGMA) development includes EV battery manufacturing along with affiliate suppliers on-site.
- Component recyclers Aurubis and Ascend Elements have joined HMGMA as part of Georgia’s efforts to build a sustainable, reliable supply chain for its electric mobility ecosystem.
- Foreign direct investment (FDI) accounted for over $8 billion in investments created in fiscal year 2022.
- The top five FDI sources were South Korea, Germany, Japan, France, and the Netherlands.
- Korea-based projects represented the highest job creation and investment from a single country.
- Germany produced the second most jobs and investments after Korea.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson led economic development missions to the United Kingdom, South Korea, and Germany in FY22.
- Meetings were held with economic development drivers like Hyundai Motor Group and SK On.
- GDEcD offers "Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development" (GRAD) certification, which recognizes pre-qualified industrial sites where due diligence is completed prior to a prospect visit.
- Georgia has certified more than 60 industrial sites as ready for fast-track industrial projects through the GRAD program.
- Georgia’s Quick Start program provides specialized workforce training tailored specifically to business needs and has operated for 55 years.
- Logistics and distribution capabilities are supported by access to Hartsfield-Jackson International Airport, the Port of Savannah, and a robust network of highways and rail lines.
