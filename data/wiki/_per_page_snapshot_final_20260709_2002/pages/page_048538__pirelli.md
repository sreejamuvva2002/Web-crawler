# Pirelli

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-07

**Source:** [Pirelli Plans Up to $1.2 Billion U.S. Investment, Expanding Focus on Rome Plant - Coosa Valley News](https://coosavalleynews.com/2026/07/pirelli-plans-up-to-1-2-billion-u-s-investment-expanding-focus-on-rome-plant/)  ·  `page_048538`

## Facts

- Tire manufacturer Pirelli is planning to invest between $1 billion and $1.2 billion in its U.S. operations over the coming years.
- The investment is expected to further strengthen production at Pirelli's Rome manufacturing facility.
- Pirelli's newly appointed board of directors has been informed of the multi-year investment plan.
- The investment plan will be presented for formal approval at an upcoming board meeting.
- The investment aims to increase Pirelli's manufacturing capacity in the United States.
- The investment includes expanding production of Pirelli's Cyber Tyres.
- In May, Pirelli announced that Cyber Tyres would be produced at its Rome, Georgia, plant.
- The Rome, Georgia, facility is positioned at the forefront of the company's next generation of tire technology.
- Cyber Tyre technology integrates sensors embedded inside the tire with software capable of transmitting real-time data to vehicles.
- The Cyber Tyre system can monitor information such as tire pressure, temperature, and road conditions.
- The system provides drivers and vehicle systems with enhanced safety and performance data.
- The planned investment underscores the strategic importance of Pirelli's U.S. manufacturing operations as demand grows for advanced tire technologies.
- Earlier this year, the Italian government stepped in to limit the influence of Pirelli's largest shareholder, Chinese state-owned chemical company Sinochem.
- The Italian government cited concerns that Sinochem's ownership structure could affect Pirelli's business opportunities in the United States.
- Shareholders approved a new board of directors last week that is controlled by Italian investor Camfin.
- Camfin is the investment company led by longtime Pirelli executive Marco Tronchetti Provera.
- Camfin owns a 26.2% stake in Pirelli, while Sinochem remains the company's largest shareholder with a 34.1% stake.
- Under the new board structure, Sinochem secured just three seats on the 15-member board.
- On Tuesday, the board confirmed Andrea Casaluci as Pirelli's chief executive officer.
- The board appointed Marco Tronchetti Provera as executive chairman after he previously served as executive vice chairman.
- Pirelli has operated one of its largest North American manufacturing facilities in Rome for decades.
- The investment signals continued confidence in the Rome plant's future.
- The company's decision earlier this year to manufacture Cyber Tyres in Rome places the local facility at the center of Pirelli's U.S. growth strategy.
- The Rome facility is positioned to play a key role as the automotive industry increasingly adopts connected vehicle technologies.
