# Hyundai Motor Group & SK On Battery Manufacturing Facility

**Type:** Facility  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[battery-cell-manufacturing|Battery Cell Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** Bartow Centre, Highway 411, Bartow County, Georgia, United States

## Overview

A planned electric vehicle battery manufacturing facility in Bartow County, Georgia, jointly developed by Hyundai Motor Group and SK On to supply Hyundai's U.S. plants.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Battery manufacturing for EVs that will supply Hyundai Motor Group’s plants in the United States.

- **$4-5 billion** — Construction of new EV battery manufacturing facility in Bartow County

## Jobs

## Timeline

- **2021** — SK On established as lithium-ion battery subsidiary of SK Innovation.
- **July** — SK Battery America announced partnership with Work for Warriors Georgia program to hire veterans.
- **2025** — Planned start of operations for the Hyundai & SK On battery manufacturing facility.

## Key facts

- The project is one of the largest economic development projects in Georgia state history.
- Estimated to create more than 3,500 new jobs through a $4-5 billion investment.
- The new facility will supply Hyundai Motor Group’s plants in the United States.
- Facility aims to begin operations in 2025.
- SK On employs more than 2,000 Georgians at its SK Battery America facility in Commerce.
- Hyundai and SK On have signed a memorandum of understanding (MOU) for the partnership, with details still under development.
- The project will provide regional benefits for job seekers and establish Bartow County in Georgia’s EV ecosystem.
- Georgia’s prime location, major ports, extensive infrastructure, skilled workforce, and pro-business climate attract EV industry investment.
- Since 2020, EV-related projects announced in the state total approximately $17 billion in investment and more than 22,800 new jobs.
- "Hyundai Motor Group and SK On are valued partners and key players in our state’s ever-growing automotive industry," said Governor Brian Kemp.
