# SL Alabama LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2019-10-25

**Source:** [Workers, Kia Supplier Reach $1.2 Million Trafficking Settlement](https://news.bloomberglaw.com/daily-labor-report/workers-kia-supplier-reach-1-2-million-trafficking-settlement)  ·  `page_010222`

## Facts

- A group of migrant workers and auto part suppliers for Kia and Hyundai are seeking court approval of a $1.2 million settlement to resolve class action claims of labor trafficking and racketeering.
- Mexican engineers and technicians alleged that auto parts manufacturer SL Alabama LLC, staffing agency Allswell, and recruiting firm SPJ Connect Inc. operated a “bait and switch” scheme to recruit high-skilled workers on NAFTA professional visas and place them in low-paid, manual labor jobs.
- TN visa holders recruited by Allswell or SPJ and employed at SL production facilities between Oct. 25, 2019 to the present will be eligible for the settlement fund.
- The class members in exchange will release the defendants from any claims related to their recruitment or employment.
- The lawsuit is one of several in the Southeast in which auto manufacturers have been accused of abusing the TN visa program, which allows professional workers from Mexico and Canada to fill jobs in the US.
- Logistics companies Hyundai Glovis and GFA Alabama were accused in a June complaint in the Northern District of Georgia of exploiting the TN visas for cheap labor.
- The TN visa program was established as part of the North American Free Trade Agreement and continued in the same form after being replaced by the United States-Mexico-Canada Agreement in 2020.
- Plaintiffs alleged they were paid less and required to work longer hours than US workers and non-Hispanic employees.
- Plaintiffs claimed they were threatened with deportation in response to complaints about their employment and pay.
- The case is Peregrina v. SL Alabama, LLC, filed in the US District Court for the Northern District of Georgia as No. 3:23-cv-00206, with a settlement proposed on July 19, 2024.
