# Duckyang Industrial Co. Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2021-05-21

**Source:** [Korean Automotive Manufacturer Duckyang to Open Company's First U.S. Facility in Georgia - AllOnGeorgia](https://allongeorgia.com/georgia-business/korean-automotive-manufacturer-duckyang-to-open-companys-first-u-s-facility-in-georgia/)  ·  `page_001302`

## Facts

- Duckyang, an electric mobility parts supplier of automotive battery modules and energy storage systems to SK Battery America (SKBA), will invest $10 million in opening its first U.S. manufacturing facility in Jackson County.
- The new facility will create 285 jobs in Braselton, Georgia.
- Duckyang’s new facility will be 230,000 square feet and located at 984 Broadway Avenue in Braselton.
- The company will be hiring for positions in production, maintenance, logistics, and warehouse management.
- Headquartered in Ulsan, Korea, Duckyang supplies cockpit modules to customers around the world including Hyundai, Kia, Renault Samsung Motors, Genesis, and Mobis.
- Cockpit modules are integrated auto parts comprised of more than 130 subcomponents found near the driver’s seat of a vehicle.
- By entering the U.S. electric vehicle battery market with SKBA, Duckyang will cooperate with other automotive companies like Hyundai and Kia Motors that already have a manufacturing base in the U.S.
- Duckyang expects business expansion opportunities to supply not only electric vehicle parts but also interior parts, including cockpit modules, door trim panel modules, and other flagship parts to global automakers based in the United States.
- Governor Kemp stated that Duckyang is creating high-paying, advanced automotive manufacturing jobs for Georgians in Jackson County.
- Georgia has remained the number one state for business for the past eight years.
- Town Manager and Clerk of Braselton Jennifer Scott welcomed Duckyang to the community as a new manufacturing facility.
- John Scott, Director of Economic Development for the Jackson County Area Chamber of Commerce, stated that companies like Duckyang are helping Jackson County and Georgia secure a place in the industry's future as the automotive industry moves toward electric.
- Director of Korean Investment Yoonie Kim represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce division on this project in partnership with the Jackson County Industrial Development Authority, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia EMC.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Georgia is focused on growing the entire electric mobility supply chain and uses relationships with SK Battery America and Duckyang to help companies secure local supply of needed parts.
- The State of Georgia facilitated more than $1.3 billion in investment and more than 2,700 jobs from South Korean companies during 2020 alone.
- Duckyang Industrial was established in July 1977 and was the first company to develop and produce automobile cockpit modules in Korea.
- Duckyang solidified its position in the automobile business through a strategic alliance with Visteon, an American global automotive supplier spun off from Ford in 1999.
- Duckyang is continuously promoting research and development on new technologies to play a role as a leader in the global automotive market and plans to expand production capabilities for interior automotive parts in addition to electronics in the future.
