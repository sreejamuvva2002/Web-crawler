# Wheelabrator Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2014-03-13

**Source:** [Wheelabrator Tallies 1 Million Safety Hours](https://www.marinelink.com/news/wheelabrator-tallies367428)  ·  `page_014909`

## Facts

- Wheelabrator Group reached a major milestone of 1 million hours without a lost time incident during the first quarter of 2014.
- More than 200 Wheelabrator employees and guests marked the event during a safety luncheon at their LaGrange facility on March 13, 2014.
- Robert E. Joyce served as President & CEO of Norican Group, the parent company of Wheelabrator and DISA.
- Poul Erik Arre attended as Director of HS&E for Norican Group from Denmark.
- Pierre Tanguay attended as VP Supply Chain for Wheelabrator Group.
- Judith Carr attended as a Member of Chairman’s Board for the Georgia State Board Workers Compensation.
- Jim Thornton, Mayor of the City of LaGrange, was a featured speaker at the luncheon.
- Kathleen Parsloe attended as CPCU, ARM Senior VP for Marsh USA, Inc.
- An American Flag flew over the Washington D.C. Capitol on March 13, 2014, in recognition of Wheelabrator's 1 million work hours without a lost time incident.
- The flag was sent to Wheelabrator with a certificate and will be displayed in the LaGrange, Georgia Wheelabrator lobby.
- Wheelabrator is a surface preparation company that offers a complete range of equipment, services, and parts.
- Companies in the foundry, automotive, aerospace, energy, marine, rail, construction, and many other industries have used Wheelabrator Group’s products and services for over 100 years.
- Wheelabrator has active customers in nearly 100 countries.
- Wheelabrator manufactures a full range of wheel-type shot blast machines, mass finishing equipment dedicated to industry demands, and automated airblast solutions.
- Wheelabrator Plus professionals inspect current blasting equipment and conduct thorough assessments to determine if productivity, cost savings, safety, or environmental improvements can be realized through upgrading to newer technology.
