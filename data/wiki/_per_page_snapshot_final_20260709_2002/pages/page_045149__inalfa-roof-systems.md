# Inalfa Roof Systems

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2013-06

**Source:** [Inalfa Roof Systems Investing $17M in Georgia, Creating 300 Manufacturing Jobs - Middle Georgia CEO](https://middlegeorgiaceo.com/news/2013/06/inalfa-roof-systems-investing-17m-georgia-creating-300-manufacturing-jobs/)  ·  `page_045149`

## Facts

- Gov. Nathan Deal announced that Inalfa Roof Systems, a leading global supplier of vehicle roofing systems, will open a manufacturing plant in Cherokee County.
- The plant represents a $17.1 million investment and will create 300 jobs for Georgians.
- Gov. Nathan Deal stated that Georgia's auto industry has been cultivated for more than 100 years, and the state's central location, deep workforce resources, and logistics infrastructure have attracted well over 300 companies and give the state a competitive advantage in this manufacturing sector.
- Inalfa Roof Systems supplies sunroofs and open roofing systems to automotive manufacturers including Hyundai Kia, Ford, General Motors, Volvo, Chrysler Group, and BMW Group.
- The facility is strategically located off Interstate 75 at Cherokee 75 Corporate Park, in close proximity to automotive manufacturers throughout the Southeast.
- Danny Colaris, vice president of North American Operations for Inalfa Roof Systems, stated that after a four-year search for a location in the South, Cherokee County was found to be the ideal site.
- Danny Colaris noted that state and local economic development teams, as well as Georgia Power, were extremely helpful in introducing the company to the strategic location and business advantages in Georgia.
- The Cherokee County facility will be Inalfa's fourth plant in the U.S. and its first in the Southeast.
- Inalfa Roof Systems has its global headquarters in Venray, The Netherlands, and a global workforce of 3,000 employees.
- Inalfa’s North American headquarters is located in Michigan, and it operates a total of four plants in Michigan and Mexico.
- Worldwide, Inalfa Roof Systems also has facilities in Slovakia, South Korea, China, Brazil, and Japan, and is currently expanding its footprint in Poland.
- Building plans and preparation for construction for the Cherokee County facility have begun.
- The plant is projected to begin operating in January 2014.
- The new Georgia facility will provide job opportunities in engineering, operations and supply chain, purchasing, and manufacturing associates.
- Hiring for these positions will begin in the fourth quarter of 2013 and continue through the second quarter of 2015 until all lines are installed and the plant is fully operational.
- Marshall Day, chairman of the Cherokee Office of Economic Development, stated that Inalfa Roof Systems is the first business to locate in the Cherokee 75 Corporate Park.
- Marshall Day thanked project partners at CBRE, Rooker, GDEcD, and Georgia Power for their work in making the project a reality.
- Marshall Day noted that the new investment and jobs created will have a significant impact on Cherokee and the northwest Atlanta region.
- Wylly Harrison is the project manager for the Georgia Department of Economic Development, which partnered with the Cherokee Office of Economic Development on the project location.
- Chris Cummiskey, Commissioner of the Georgia Department of Economic Development, stated that investment by international companies creates jobs and contributes to the state’s economy.
- Chris Cummiskey noted that commitments from top-notch businesses like Inalfa help other investors understand how Georgia can help them become successful in the U.S. market.
