# Seoyon E-Hwa Interior Systems

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2020-03-11

**Source:** [Auto supplier Seoyon E-Hwa plans expansion, creating 60 jobs in Selma - Yellowhammer News](https://yellowhammernews.com/auto-supplier-seoyon-e-hwa-plans-expansion-creating-60-jobs-in-selma/)  ·  `page_059737`

## Facts

- Seoyon E-Hwa Interior Systems is a Tier 1 supplier to Alabama’s Hyundai assembly plant.
- The company announced plans Tuesday to invest more than $5 million to expand its industrial operation in Dallas County.
- Seoyon E-Hwa will add 60 jobs to its current workforce of 436 in Selma.
- The expansion makes the company one of Dallas County’s largest employers.
- Wayne Vardaman, executive director of the Selma & Dallas County Economic Development Authority, stated the company has grown from 125 jobs in 2005 to currently 436.
- The expansion project is expected to get underway in April with a potential completion by the end of September.
- The company’s total investment for the expansion will be $5.14 million.
- Seoyon E-Hwa’s facility at Selma’s Craig Industrial Park produces several molded plastic interior trim parts for vehicles assembled at Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Alabama in Montgomery.
- This expansion is in conjunction with new vehicle production beginning later this year at HMMA’s plant.
- Based in South Korea, Seoyon E-Hwa’s roots date to the early 1970s and the beginning of that nation’s auto industry.
- The company has plants around the world and serves automakers including Kia, Volkswagen, Ford, and Nissan.
- The expansion project was formally announced Tuesday during an event at the Selma-Dallas County Public Library.
- State Sen. Malika Sanders-Fortier expressed excitement about the company's presence in Selma and Dallas County, noting that 60 people will have the opportunity to be employed.
- Vardaman expects additional growth projects to take shape in Dallas County.
