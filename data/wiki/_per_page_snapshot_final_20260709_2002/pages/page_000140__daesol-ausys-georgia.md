# Daesol Ausys Georgia

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-29

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp: Automotive Supplier Daesol Ausys Georgia to Invest $72 Million in Harris County - Harris County, Georgia](https://www.harriscountyga.gov/gov-kemp-automotive-supplier-daesol-ausys-georgia-to-invest-72-million-in-harris-county/)  ·  `page_000140`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Daesol Ausys Georgia will establish a new manufacturing facility in West Point.
- The facility will create more than 140 new jobs.
- The project represents $72 million in investment in Harris County.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that West Point has become an automotive capital in its own right.
- Governor Brian Kemp expressed excitement that Daesol Ausys will join other innovators in Harris County.
- Georgia’s automotive industry currently employs over 50,000 hardworking Georgians.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that the state’s automotive industry will continue to grow as e-mobility suppliers choose all corners of the state for future jobs.
- Daesol Ausys was established in Korea in 2017.
- The company specializes in designing and manufacturing motor and electric vehicle interior components and accessories.
- Daesol Ausys is a key supplier for Kia Georgia, Hyundai Motor Group, and General Motors.
- Min Ho Kwon serves as the CEO of Daesol Ausys.
- Min Ho Kwon stated the company is committed to delivering world-class services that exceed client requirements and expectations.
- The new facility will be located in the Northwest Harris Business Park.
- Min Ho Kwon expressed excitement about the project and looks forward to continuing the partnership with the State of Georgia and Harris County.
- Operations at the new facility are expected to begin in December 2024.
- The facility will produce luggage boards and luggage covers.
- The company will be hiring for positions in production, purchasing, quality control, maintenance, human resources, and management.
- Robert Grant serves as the Chairman of the Harris County Commission.
- Robert Grant stated that Daesol Ausys’ decision to locate in the Northwest Harris Business Park represents a commitment to Harris County.
- West Point and Harris County have a wonderful relationship with Daesol Ausys’ sister companies Daehan Solutions and Daesol Materials.
- Local officials have been working closely with company officials to bring the new venture to West Point.
- Craig Greenhaw serves as the Chairman of the Harris County Development Authority.
- Craig Greenhaw extended a welcome to Daesol Ausys and expressed excitement over a long and mutually beneficial partnership with the company.
- West Point and Harris County continue to work well together on successful ventures that bring jobs and capital investment into the region.
- Alyce Thornhill is the Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy at the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team.
- The project was a competitive effort developed in partnership with the Harris County Commission, Harris County Development Authority, City of West Point, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that economic development is a team sport driven by the local level.
- Pat Wilson congratulated Harris County and community partners for helping bring Daesol Ausys to West Georgia.
- The automotive industry is providing generational change in the region and across the state.
- Pat Wilson expressed excitement over the continuation of growth and the opportunities Daesol Ausys will provide to the people of Harris County.
- For over a century, Georgia has fostered healthy industry practices, encouraged collaboration and innovation, and positioned the state as a leader in developing and harnessing emerging technologies for the evolving automotive and mobility industry.
- As the market transitions to electric vehicles, Georgia has pursued the entire supply chain, creating more than $25 billion in investments and 30,000 jobs since 2018.
- In fiscal year 2023, job creation in the automotive industry increased by 324 percent when compared to FY21.
- Suppliers attracted by Georgia’s mobility original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) resulted in over $2 billion in investment across the state in FY23.
- Daesol Ausys Georgia is a leading manufacturer of automotive components and a key supplier of luggage boards and luggage covers.
- The company is committed to exceeding customer expectations with award-winning products and services through innovation and efficiency.
- The company has a long history as one of the top suppliers to Kia and Hyundai worldwide.
- Coach Robert (Rob) M. Grant Sr. serves as a Harris Co. Commissioner for District 2.
