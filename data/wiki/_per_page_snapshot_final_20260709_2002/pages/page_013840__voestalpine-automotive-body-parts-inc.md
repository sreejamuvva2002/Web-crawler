# voestalpine Automotive Body Parts Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2016-08-17

**Source:** [Leading automotive supplier to create 150 jobs in Bartow County - Expansion Solutions](https://www.expansionsolutionsmagazine.com/leading-automotive-supplier-to-create-150-jobs-in-bartow-county/)  ·  `page_013840`

## Facts

- Governor Nathan Deal announced that voestalpine Automotive Body Parts Inc. will expand its existing operation in Bartow County, creating 150 jobs and investing $50 million in the local community.
- voestalpine Automotive Body Parts Inc. is the supply base for the voestalpine Group, which produces ultra-high strength body-in-white components for premium car manufacturers.
- Governor Nathan Deal stated that voestalpine’s decision to expand operations in Bartow County strengthens Georgia’s growing automotive industry and highlights the state’s extensive manufacturing capabilities.
- Governor Nathan Deal noted that when he met with voestalpine executives in Germany, they discussed the benefits that Georgia can continue to offer the growing company.
- Governor Nathan Deal said the business environment in Bartow County and throughout the state will help voestalpine maintain its position as a leader in automotive supply, while bolstering Georgia’s reputation as a top state for business.
- The newly created jobs will range from advanced manufacturing to hot-forming and assembly positions.
- The company currently employs approximately 100 Georgians at the facility.
- The company offers extensive training opportunities, including an apprenticeship program with the students of the Bartow County College & Career Academy in Cartersville.
- Philipp Schulz, managing director of voestalpine Automotive Body Parts Inc., stated that this investment is an important step in the growth strategy of their company in the U.S. and positions them well in the center of the southern automotive hub.
- Philipp Schulz thanked state and local officials as well as the Georgia Department of Economic Development for their assistance in this project.
- Philipp Schulz expressed excitement about expanding operations in Cartersville, where the company has found great partners for its operations.
- voestalpine Automotive Body Parts Inc. is a subsidiary company of the Metal Forming Division of the German-based voestalpine Group.
- The Metal Forming Division produces highly refined sections, tubes and precision strip steel products, as well as ready-to-install components made of pressed, stamped and roll formed parts.
- Bartow County Commissioner Steve Taylor stated they are proud and thankful for voestalpine’s investment in Bartow County.
- Bartow County Commissioner Steve Taylor noted that voestalpine was the first company to build in Highland 75 and their multiple additional phases, continued investment and creation of jobs shows that Bartow is a great location to do business.
- Bartow County Commissioner Steve Taylor said the success of voestalpine and Highland 75 is a testament to the collaboration between Bartow County, the City of Cartersville and all their state and corporate partners.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) Senior Project Manager Wylly Harrison and Regional Project Manager Stephanie Scearce represented the Global Commerce Division in partnership with the Cartersville-Bartow County Department of Economic Development and Georgia Power.
- GDEcD Commissioner Chris Carr stated that when global manufacturing companies like voestalpine choose to locate or expand their existing presence in Georgia, it is a testament to the international reputation of their top-ranked business climate.
- GDEcD Commissioner Chris Carr expressed confidence that as the state’s automotive industry grows and manufacturing companies continue to invest, voestalpine will find the support it needs to successfully serve its customers around the world and remain competitive in the marketplace.
- The voestalpine Group operates globally with around 500 Group companies and locations in more than 50 countries on all five continents.
- The voestalpine Group has been listed on the Vienna Stock Exchange since 1995.
- The voestalpine Group is one of the leading partners to the automotive and consumer goods industries in Europe and to the oil and gas industries worldwide using steel and other metals.
- The voestalpine Group is the world market leader in turnout technology, special rails, tool steel, and special sections.
