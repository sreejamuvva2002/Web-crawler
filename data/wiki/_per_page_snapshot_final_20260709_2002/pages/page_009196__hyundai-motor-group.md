# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Paid Program: Inside Hyundai’s EV Strategy to Electrify America](https://partners.wsj.com/hyundai/miles-that-define-us/inside-hyundais-ev-strategy-to-electrify-america/)  ·  `page_009196`

## Facts

- In 1991, Hyundai introduced its first electric vehicle to the world—a Sonata prototype that showed the promise of zero-emissions mobility.
- The 1991 Sonata prototype was never mass produced.
- Over the next three decades, Hyundai has been bullish on its electrification roadmap.
- According to Hyundai Motors—consisting of Hyundai and Kia—its revenue has doubled since 2019.
- Hyundai now represents more than 1 in 10 new vehicles sold in the U.S.
- A significant portion of the company’s gains has come from EVs, which accounted for nearly 8% of U.S. sales in 2023.
- Hybrids accounted for 10% of Hyundai's U.S. sales.
- In the first quarter of 2024, Hyundai Motors became America’s second-largest seller of EVs.
- José Muñoz is president and global COO of Hyundai Motor Company and CEO of Hyundai and Genesis Motor North America.
- At an investor event in August, Hyundai revealed it was doubling the number of hybrid variants it offers from seven to 14.
- Hyundai is developing extended range electric vehicle (EREV) technology to give customers an added bridge from internal combustion engines to pure EVs.
- EREV technology combines the advantages of internal combustion engines and EVs to provide an all-electric driving range of over 500 miles, with the engine being used only for battery charging.
- Hyundai will begin producing the new EREVs at the soon-to-open $7.6 billion Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America facility in Bryan County, Georgia.
- The Bryan County, Georgia facility had been originally slated to exclusively manufacture battery EVs.
- Production of the new EREVs is scheduled by the end of 2026, with sales starting in 2027.
- Hyundai plans to introduce 21 new EVs by 2030, from affordable to luxury to high-performance Hyundai and Genesis models.
- Globally, Hyundai is investing $28 billion over the next decade.
- Hyundai is targeting 2 million EV sales by 2030.
- Hyundai’s electrification strategy really started to take shape in 2016 with the introduction of the award-winning IONIQ brand.
- The company launched IONIQ with a battery electric model, a hybrid model and a plug-in hybrid model.
- Hyundai continues to sell hybrids across its lineup, including the popular Santa Fe, Tucson, Kona, Elantra and Sonata models.
- Hyundai has spent the past few years perfecting Hyundai Home Marketplace, which offers complimentary home EV chargers for owners, among other electrification needs.
- Hyundai has taken a leadership role in building out the EV infrastructure across America by helping to found IONNA, a joint venture among several of the world’s largest automakers for the purpose of expanding a universal charging network.
- By 2030, the goal for IONNA is to have more than 30,000 chargers in urban areas, along highway corridors and on vacation routes.
- Hyundai is committed to achieving company-wide carbon neutrality by 2045.
