# SK Battery America Plant

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2021-07-27

**Source:** [Here's how Georgia is creating jobs, economic growth across the state](https://www.augustachronicle.com/story/news/2021/07/27/jobs-in-georgia-economic-development-sk-battery-america-athens-ga-tax-incentives-amazon-wayfair/5351242001/)  ·  `page_049963`

## Facts

- The SK Battery America plant is located in Commerce, Georgia.
- The $1.6 billion project was expected to employ 2,000 people and was finalized in early January 2019.
- The project required investment from state and local government to be built in Georgia.
- SK Battery was looking at several other states, especially in the southeastern U.S., with final two sites between Commerce and somewhere in Tennessee, according to John Scott, director of economic development for Jackson County.
- Georgia is regularly touted as being the No. 1 state for business in the country, although there is some disagreement on this ranking.
- Beating out competitors for major industrial projects brings in potentially thousands of jobs at a time and can include significant investments from local governments.
- Some experts say state and local developers in Georgia are careful to ensure these projects are net economic gains, while others raise concerns about transparency and democratic processes.
- USA TODAY Network-Georgia examined six major economic development projects across Georgia using open records requests, state-provided data, and interviews with officials.
- All of the examined projects created 500 new jobs or more and received government support ranging from tax breaks and leases to outright grants.
- The examined projects included the SK Battery plant, the Hanwha Q Cells solar manufacturing facility in Whitfield County, Amazon facilities in Bibb County, Wayfair facilities in Athens and Savannah, and a warehouse development project still under development in Savannah.
- Cal Wray, president of the Augusta Economic Development Authority, acknowledges there are not many 500-job projects locally but notes food, chemical, and advanced manufacturing companies draw national and international competition.
- Each of these local projects might employ a few hundred people, alongside non-industrial cyber and office space projects.
- Cyber is considered equally important to industrial development outside of the industrial piece, according to Wray.
- Amazon came to Columbia County in 2020 with a fulfillment center that will employ 800 people, receiving state and local grants, infrastructure work, and some property tax breaks while still paying school taxes.
- Earlier this year, Amazon announced a new sortation facility with no incentives that will create several hundred additional jobs.
- Robbie Bennett, executive director of the Development Authority of Columbia County, stated the county's only input on the second facility was selling 47 acres for $37,000.
- Bennett noted Amazon enjoyed doing business with Columbia County and liked the site access.
- The original Amazon project brought 500 construction workers who stayed in Columbia County hotels during the COVID hospitality slumps.
- Georgia competed against South Carolina, Alabama, Tennessee, and North Carolina for these projects, occasionally facing bids from cities as far away as Chicago, Dallas, and Denver, according to Scott McMurray, deputy commissioner for Global Commerce for the Georgia Department of Economic Development.
- Michael Toma, Fuller E. Callaway Professor of Economics at Georgia Southern University, noted a new project creating 1,000 jobs can have a huge impact, while 500 jobs is significant for medium-sized cities.
- Job quality remains a matter of debate, though state and local officials emphasize they look for jobs that pay well.
- Christina Cummings, vice president of operations for the Partnership for Southern Equity, emphasized the need to dig deeper to determine what success looks like when bringing development to a community.
- The SK Battery plant agreement between SK Battery, the city of Commerce, Jackson County, and Jackson County Industrial Development Authority requires the plant to employ 2,000 people and invest $1.6 billion by 2027.
- Parties to the first agreement are working on an amendment to cover a second phase that will bring an additional $940 million investment, according to McMurray.
- The SK Battery project was the reason behind a huge spike in state investment to the Athens area economic development region.
- Common deal elements across Georgia projects include: local development authority owning land and leasing it to the company; PILOT agreements for reduced property tax liability; requirements that 80% of job and investment goals be met by a deadline; counting jobs at 110% and investment at 100% when calculating the 80% requirement; definitions of full-time jobs sometimes including minimum wage or salary; and sometimes one or more state grants for specific aspects.
- To receive a grant, a project must be competitive, meaning it would have been located outside the state without the grants.
- McMurray stated incentives can't make a bad deal good, they can only make a good deal better.
- Toma noted Georgia is particularly careful about ensuring incentives are proportional to project benefits and tends to be more conservative in offering them compared to other states.
- To land the SK Battery plant, the state provided a $5 million grant and a separate $18.75 million grant for rough grading of the 283-acre site, and the Industrial Development Authority issued a $1.6 billion bond to cover construction on authority-owned land.
- Because of land ownership, SK Battery owed no property taxes but agreed to a PILOT schedule through 2039.
- If SK Battery missed hiring and investing targets, it would owe penalties based on PILOT savings and proximity to targets.
- Agreements with local industrial development authorities usually include side agreements like assistance with permitting or technical matters, but do not usually include statewide incentives like job tax credits.
- McMurray stated governors are 100% behind efforts to develop the workforce and education.
- Georgia has an unusually diverse economic base that helps bring in a wide range of industrial development projects, including growth in electric vehicle manufacturing, warehouses, and food processing.
- McMurray highlighted Georgia's presence in the cybersecurity space with a cyber security center in Augusta doing fantastic work.
- Major manufacturers like SK Battery bring their supply chain with them, creating further growth.
- McMurray stated economic development efforts work across the entire state, all 159 counties, rather than just Atlanta.
