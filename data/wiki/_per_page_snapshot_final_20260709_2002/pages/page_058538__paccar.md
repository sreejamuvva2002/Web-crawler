# Paccar

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-10-23

**Source:** [Paccar expects boost after seeing U.S. truck tariff details - Truck News](https://www.trucknews.com/transportation/paccar-expects-boost-after-seeing-u-s-truck-tariff-details/1003203237/)  ·  `page_058538`

## Facts

- Paccar executives expressed optimism that U.S. planned tariffs on new trucks and truck parts will boost sales and provide market certainty.
- CEO Preston Feight applauded Commerce and the White House for a clear document outlining tariff plans during an Oct. 21 earnings call with analysts.
- Paccar reported third-quarter net income of $590 million ($1.12 per share) and revenue of $6.67 billion (all USD).
- The White House clarified that 25% tariffs on Classes 3-8 trucks and truck parts will begin on Nov. 1.
- Trucks qualifying for preferential treatment under the USMCA will only be subject to tariffs on the value of non-U.S. content.
- Paccar produces more than 90% of its Kenworth and Peterbilt trucks sold in the United States at facilities in Texas, Ohio, and Washington.
- Feight stated the tariffs will benefit customers by reducing tariff costs and bringing clarity to the market.
- Paccar expects tariff impacts to peak during the current quarter, with uncertainty moderating after an adjustment period for truck parts manufactured in Mexico and Canada.
- Feight said the situation should improve Paccar's competitive position heading into next year.
- Paccar develops integrated powertrains and manufactures trucks in Europe under the DAF brand nameplate.
- CFO Brice Poplawski noted that bonus depreciation measures for new equipment in the recently signed "Big, Beautiful Bill" will help spur new truck demand during the fourth quarter.
- Feight indicated that tariff clarity will allow Paccar to shift discussions away from price increases back to standard truck pricing.
- Paccar estimates total U.S. and Canadian Class 8 sales for the current year in a range of 230,000-245,000 trucks.
- Paccar projects next year's (2026) Class 8 sales to range between 230,000-270,000 trucks.
- Feight noted the 2026 total could end up higher than current year projections if clarity is realized around tariffs, emissions policy, and potential freight market improvements.
