# Archer Aviation Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [ACHR Press Release: Archer Aviation Selects Site in Georgia Adjac...](https://marketchameleon.com/PressReleases/i/1585765/ACHR/archer-aviation-selects-site-in-georgia-adjacent)  ·  `page_017096`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation Inc. announced plans to locate its manufacturing facility in Covington, Georgia at a site adjacent to the Covington Municipal Airport.
- The company plans to initially build out a 350,000 square-foot facility on a 96-acre site.
- The planned facility will be capable of producing up to 650 aircraft per year.
- The manufacturing facility is expected to create over 1,000 jobs over the long term.
- Construction is expected to be completed in the first half of 2024.
- Initial production is expected to begin in the second half of 2024.
- The facility will be capable of being expanded by an additional 550,000 square feet.
- The expansion is estimated to support production of up to 2,300 aircraft per year.
- Archer completed a comprehensive survey of potential sites across the United States to select the location for its first manufacturing facility.
- Key site selection considerations included local labor market talent availability, utility availability, ability to conduct seamless flight test operations, construction costs, and logistics.
- Archer will receive an incentive package that includes land conveyance, tax incentives, and a Georgia REBA grant.
- The company anticipates that financing for the project will be provided by Synovus, the largest bank headquartered in Georgia.
- CEO Adam Goldstein stated that Georgia played a vital role in the U.S. aerospace industry and that the factory can create pathways to highly skilled manufacturing jobs and other ladders of social and economic opportunity.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp commented that Georgia's established leadership in the aerospace industry was essential to landing Archer Aviation in Newton County.
- Governor Kemp noted that Archer can take advantage of Georgia's signature workforce training program Quick Start to help fill over 1,000 jobs in Covington long-term.
- Georgia is home to more than 800 aerospace companies and maintains a robust aerospace education pipeline.
- The state's education pipeline includes a dozen high schools offering training in the field, five technical colleges with aviation programs, and several universities offering degrees in aerospace engineering.
- The Georgia Center of Innovation for Aerospace, a strategic arm of the Georgia Department of Economic Development, has been cultivating an ecosystem for innovative companies like Archer for more than 10 years.
- Archer is designing and developing electric vertical takeoff and landing (eVTOL) aircraft for use in urban air mobility networks.
