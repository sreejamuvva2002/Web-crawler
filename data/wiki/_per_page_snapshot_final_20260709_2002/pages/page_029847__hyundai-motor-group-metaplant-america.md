# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-10

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp, Hyundai Motor Group Execs Break Ground at Georgia’s Bryan County Megasite – Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority](https://savannahjda.com/2022/10/post-with-thumbnail/)  ·  `page_029847`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp, Hyundai Motor Group Executive Chair Euisun Chung, company officials, and Savannah JDA representatives officially broke ground on Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America’s new manufacturing plant at the Bryan County Megasite.
- The $5.54 billion investment for this facility by the company and its affiliate suppliers was announced in May of this year.
- Plans include producing a diverse range of innovative, fully electric vehicles (EV) and a new battery manufacturing facility.
- HMGMA will create more than 8,100 jobs over the next few years as it establishes a stable supply chain for EV battery and associated components in the U.S. market.
- The new smart factory is expected to begin commercial production in the first half of 2025.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that record jobs and investment are coming to all parts of Georgia, highlighting award-winning workforce development programs and infrastructure as drivers of the state's economic growth.
- Hyundai Motor Group Executive Chair Euisun Chung said Hyundai and Georgia share respect for histories, ingenuity, creativity, and determination, and that Hyundai EVs are recognized as best in class.
- Hyundai aims to become the global leader in electrification, safety, quality, and sustainability through this partnership.
- The groundbreaking celebration included a morning event on-site followed by an expo and program at Enmarket Arena in Savannah featuring exhibits highlighting advanced technology and human achievement for hundreds of attendees.
- HMG announced corporate social responsibility programs totaling $1.54 million to enrich local communities.
- Hyundai Hope and Genesis Gives collectively committed $120,000 to roughly ten organizations supporting local communities in and around Bryan County.
- Hyundai donated $120,000 to Georgia Tech for STEM education scholarships.
- Hyundai Hope On Wheels presented $500,000 in Hyundai Scholar Hope and Young Investigator Grants to Children’s Healthcare of Atlanta.
- Genesis Inspiration Foundation presented donations totaling $200,000 to Georgia-based nonprofits Drawchange and Paint Love.
- Kia’s “Accelerate the Good” program donated $600,000 to help local youth with gifts to Family Promise of the Coastal Empire, America’s Second Harvest of Coastal Georgia, St. Jude Children’s Research Hospital, and Inspiredu.
- Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority Chairman Carter Infinger noted that beyond the investment and jobs, Hyundai is already impacting the community through nonprofit contributions.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Hyundai placed a bet on Georgia sixteen years ago and has since become a Georgia company that changes lives across the state.
- The Bryan County facility will directly create future jobs for the Savannah area and support opportunities in the EV revolution statewide.
- The Bryan County Megasite was selected partly due to its close proximity to the Port of Savannah.
- Under Griff Lynch and the Georgia Ports Authority, the Ports of Savannah and Brunswick have expanded to handle record cargo volumes.
- Hyundai will access global markets quickly via rail and highway connections to the port.
- Non-affiliated HMGMA suppliers connected to the project will invest approximately $1 billion in other locations around Georgia.
- Hyundai Motor Group is a global enterprise with about 250,000 employees worldwide and mobility brands including Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis.
