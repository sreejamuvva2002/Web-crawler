# Fouts Brothers, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-06

**Source:** [Fouts Bros showcases its impact in community, economy | The Baldwin Bulletin](https://www.bbnews.today/news/fouts-bros-showcases-its-impact-community-economy)  ·  `page_020130`

## Facts

- Fouts Bros, Inc. hosted a "Home Day" open house event on Nov. 7 at its corporate headquarters on Roberson Mill Road in Milledgeville, Baldwin County, Georgia.
- The company relocated its operations from Cobb County to Milledgeville in December 2019.
- Fouts Brothers is a family-owned company dedicated to the manufacturing and upfitting of American work trucks for private and public sectors.
- The company's products are sold through dealerships across the U.S., with its headquarters located in a 700,000-square-foot factory in Milledgeville.
- Fouts Brothers operates a second facility in La Grange, Texas.
- Attendees at the open house included Georgia’s District 8 Congressman Austin Scott, Georgia College & State University President Cathy Cox, Baldwin County Commissioner Henry Craig, Baldwin County Manager Carlos Tobar, and Baldwin County Fire Rescue Chief Victor Young.
- The company currently employs 410 people organization-wide.
- Sixty-three employees currently work across five dedicated production lines for building fire apparatus at the Milledgeville facility.
- The company plans to expand its manufacturing capacity to eight production lines by April 1st, increasing the floor workforce to up to 85 employees.
- It takes six weeks to build a single fire apparatus from start to finish.
- Finished trucks undergo quality control testing conducted by a certified third party.
- In addition to fire trucks, the company's manufacturing line includes bucket, crane, box, utility, tow, and flatbed trucks.
- The founding brothers, C.J. Fouts and Jerry Fouts, started the company in 1952 with a Sinclair Service Station before operating car and truck dealerships across the Metro Atlanta area for 41 years.
- Commercial vehicle body manufacturing began through their dealerships, eventually expanding to include fire trucks based on C.J. Fouts' hobby.
- The open house event was held in connection with the company's membership in the Fire Apparatus Manufacturers Association (FAMA).
- Presentations at the event highlighted federal and state-level funding available for fire departments, emergency service agencies, and first responder training facilities.
- Federal grants are available to assist fire departments, as explained by CEO Scott Edens during the event.
- The company participates in annual Capitol Hill advocacy efforts known as "Hill Day" in Washington D.C. through its membership in FAMA.
- CEO Scott Edens stated that the event showcased the company's progress over seven decades as a family-owned business.
- Scott Edens serves as President & CEO of Fouts Brothers, while his wife Heather Edens serves as executive vice president.
- John Slawson serves as the Executive Vice President of Sales & Business Development for the company.
- Cody Jacobs serves as the Director of Operations and oversees the manufacturing line tours.
