- Electric vehicle manufacturers rely heavily on extruded aluminum for battery housings, structural reinforcements, and lightweight body parts that improve efficiency and performance.
- Between 2025 and 2034, the aluminum extrusion industry is expected to experience accelerated growth, particularly in high-margin sectors such as construction, electronics and automotive.
- Increasing adoption of electric vehicles is expanding demand for lightweight structural components, battery housings and thermal management solutions made from extruded aluminum.
- Shift toward sustainable building materials in residential and commercial projects across the Asia Pacific, Europe and North America is strengthening demand.
- Global expansion is driven by strategic acquisitions, capacity increases and new production facilities across North America, Europe and Asia Pacific.
- Norsk Hydro ASA recently opened a new aluminum recycling plant in Michigan, United States, to support the North American market with high-recycled-content extrusions and improved supply-chain resilience.
- Major investors including Hindalco Industries Limited, Alcoa Corporation, Norsk Hydro ASA, and Constellium N.V. are investing in modern extrusion presses, upgraded die technologies, automation, and high-speed finishing lines to improve precision and output.

- Fastest growing region is North America, driven by growing development of fuel-efficient vehicles and rise in adoption of electric vehicles.
- Automakers in North America are increasingly using lightweight aluminum components to improve performance, reduce emissions, and extend driving range, strengthening need for extruded parts in frames, battery housings, and structural components.
- United States is a key contributor to the aluminum extrusion sector due to growing expansion of the transportation sector and rise in electric vehicles.
- Growing production of building materials such as structural elements, window frames, and curtain walls requires aluminum extrusion, with construction companies relying on it for energy-efficient building systems.
- Europe is growing rapidly in the market due to strong focus on reducing vehicle emissions and rise in electric vehicles, with automakers replacing heavier steel components with lightweight aluminum parts to meet emissions targets and improve energy efficiency.
- Demand for extruded profiles used in engine systems, battery structures, and body frames is strengthened by the European EV trend.
- Germany is growing rapidly due to growing production of vehicles and rise of electric mobility, with German automakers focusing on lightweight engineering and energy efficiency.
- Extruded aluminum components are increasingly used in battery housings, safety structures, and interior support systems in Germany.
- Innovations such as automated processes and die design supported by advanced machinery, improved tooling, and digital process control help German manufacturers enhance product quality, reduce waste, and increase production efficiency.

- Renewable energy installations depend on extruded aluminum for mounting structures, support beams, electrical housings, and frame systems due to durability and ease of fabrication.
