# Wabash National Corp.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2021-02-04

**Source:** [Wabash National Refocuses Business On Cold Chain And e-commerce](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/wabash-national-refocuses-business-cold-154436503.html)  ·  `page_014777`

## Facts

- Trailer builder Wabash National Corp. (NYSE: WNC) is refocusing its business on cold chain, home delivery, and parts and service, pushing resources to refrigerated vans needed to move vaccines and food.
- Wabash anticipates that growth in home delivery will have staying power even as more Americans receive COVID-19 inoculations.
- CEO Brent Yeagy stated on the company's fourth-quarter earnings call that new technology and products to keep goods refrigerated present significant organic growth prospects.
- CEO Brent Yeagy said, "We've never been faced with a market that has this much potential," and noted that all potential growth avenues are currently on the table.
- Potential growth avenues include expanding upfitting operations and acquiring technology or another manufacturing platform.
- Wabash streamlined its portfolio by selling assets that do not offer strong strategic fit and is setting the stage to backfill by diversifying with expanded and new revenue streams in transportation, logistics, and distribution markets.
- Wabash sold its Beall brand of tank trailer to MAC Trailer Enterprises in December.
- Wabash is retaining its other tank trailer brands.
- CEO Brent Yeagy stated the company is repositioning resources to accelerate organic efforts to create additional revenue in unprecedented ways for Wabash National.
- Wabash anticipates expanded use of molded structural composite (MSC) technology beyond the 53-foot refrigerated van market.
- MSC technology weighs less and offers better thermal protection than other trailer construction materials.
- Wabash conducted an MSC pilot project with C&S Wholesale Grocers in 2020.
- Using MSC technology, Wabash can make a 48-foot trailer 30% more thermally efficient than a traditional refrigerated trailer.
- The improved thermal efficiency allows refrigeration units to run fewer hours, requiring fewer batteries for all-electric units.
- CEO Brent Yeagy stated that MSC technology offers unique value through durability, reduced weight, and improved thermal efficiency, with consistent customer feedback highlighting benefits not found elsewhere.
- Wabash is manufacturing Gruau refrigerated inserts for the Ford Transit van to serve the rapidly expanding food and grocery home delivery market.
- Traditionally constructed refrigerated cargo vans use spray foam insulation that can release gas and allow mold intrusion.
- Gruau inserts fit specific van models and provide 30% to 50% greater thermal efficiency.
- CEO Brent Yeagy noted that participating in this space positions Wabash to better serve the smaller light-duty upfit market compared to its larger traditional truck body products.
- CEO Brent Yeagy stated that President Joe Biden's green agenda, the lingering pandemic, climate change, and technology advances combine to create opportunity.
- Yeagy stated that over the next four years, these factors open opportunities for Wabash to leverage innovation and existing technology to bring sustainable solutions to these markets.
- Traditional truck bodies remain the main catalyst funding Wabash's expansion.
- The value of Wabash's trailer order backlog rose to $1.5 billion at the end of Q4, up from $1 billion at the end of Q3.
- Most of the backlog consists of dry vans, which dominate the Commercial Trailer Products division.
- The average selling price for trailers was $27,000, remaining flat compared to the prior year.
- CEO Brent Yeagy stated that Q4 shipments were in line with expectations and sees no structural impediments to moving forward in 2021.
- Wabash shipped 10,610 trailers in Q4, down from 14,950 in the same period of the prior year.
- Despite earlier concerns about finding workers during the pandemic, Wabash added 600 hourly employees during the October-December period.
- CFO Mike Pettit stated the company is seeking 900 additional new hires for the current quarter.
- Net sales for Q4 2020 were $404.1 million.
- Operating income was $10 million, representing 2.5% of net sales.
- Total revenue for the full year 2020 totaled $1.5 billion.
- Full-year 2020 operating income was $22.5 million.
- Q4 net income was $5.5 million, or 10 cents per diluted share.
- Full-year 2020 net income was $7.8 million, or 15 cents per share.
- Q4 operating EBITDA was $25.2 million, or 6.2% of net sales.
- Full-year operating EBITDA was $73.6 million, or 5% of net sales.
