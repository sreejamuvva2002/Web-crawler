# Q CELLS

**Type:** Company  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[charging-infrastructure|Charging Infrastructure]]  ·  **Location:** Dalton, Georgia, Georgia, United States

## Overview

Q CELLS is a global solar-panel manufacturer with German roots (founded in 1999) that was acquired by South Korean conglomerate Hanwha Group in 2012. Manufacturing is split across South Korea, Malaysia and a facility in Dalton, Georgia, which produces the Q.PEAK DUO series panels used in residential systems.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Provides domestically manufactured solar panels that can qualify for the Inflation Reduction Act domestic content adder, supporting EV charging infrastructure in Georgia.

## Key facts

- The Dalton plant produces Q.PEAK DUO series panels that qualify for the full 30% federal Investment Tax Credit under the Inflation Reduction Act’s domestic content adder.
- Q CELLS offers a 25-year product warranty and a 25-year linear performance warranty with no more than 2% degradation in year one and 0.54% per year thereafter.
- Panels have a temperature coefficient of -0.34%/°C, better than the industry average of -0.38%/°C.
- Q CELLS panels are PID-resistant per IEC 62804 testing.
- The Q.PEAK DUO series carries UL 61730 and IEC 61215 certifications required by most US utilities.
- Field failure rate data from EnergySage’s 2024 installer survey places Q CELLS in the lower-failure-rate tier with defect rates under 0.1% over 5 years.
- Q CELLS has been classified as Bloomberg NEF Tier 1 since 2013, indicating bankability and manufacturing quality.
- The flagship residential line in 2026 (Q.PEAK DUO BLK ML-G10+) offers efficiencies between 20.4% and 21.6% and power outputs from 395W to 430W.
