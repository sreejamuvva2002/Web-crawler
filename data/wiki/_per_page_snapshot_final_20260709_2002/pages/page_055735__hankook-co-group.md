# Hankook & Co. Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hankook outlines plans for Hanon Systems | European Rubber Journal](https://www.european-rubber-journal.com/article/2095511/hankook-details-plans-for-hanon-systems-acquisition)  ·  `page_055735`

## Facts

- Hankook & Co. Group is targeting sales of KRW 30 trillion (€20.283 billion) by 2030 through expanding additional businesses based on next-generation technology beyond the automotive industry.
- Hyunbum Cho, chairman of Hankook & Co. Group, confirmed the Korean group’s plan to acquire a majority-shareholding in Hanon Systems.
- By acquiring management rights in Hanon Systems, Hankook will become a provider of crucial technology for the electric vehicle era, including TMS (thermal management systems) and tires.
- The Korean group expects to further strengthen its business portfolio by working with TMS solutions alongside its tire products.
- The €1.1-billion transaction will see Hankook increase its stake in the automotive supplier from an existing 19.49% to 50.53%.
- Upon finalisation of the deal, expected by year-end, Hankook's total global assets are forecast to reach around KRW26 trillion.
- This asset forecast positions Hankook among South Korea’s top 30 companies.
- Hanon Systems is considered the world’s second-largest company in TMS for electric vehicles.
- Hanon Systems offers services ranging from complete design to parts supply.
- The acquired business is set for high growth through technology advances in TMS areas, including compressors and integrated coolant modules.
- Hanon Systems operates 53 production locations across 21 countries.
- Hanon Systems reported sales of KRW9.6 trillion last year.
- Key clients of Hanon Systems include Hyundai Motor Group, Ford, Volkswagen, BMW, and Mercedes-Benz.
