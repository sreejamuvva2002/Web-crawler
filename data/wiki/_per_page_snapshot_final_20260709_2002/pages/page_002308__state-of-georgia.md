# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-09-13

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp Celebrates Top State for Business Ranking | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-releases/2024/gov-kemp-celebrates-top-state-business-ranking-workforce-summit)  ·  `page_002308`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp delivered the keynote address at the second annual Governor's Workforce Summit on September 13, 2024.
- Georgia was named the No. 1 state for business for the 11th consecutive year by Area Development magazine.
- The summit put forth the goal of making Georgia the Top State for Talent.
- Georgia Quick Start earned a No. 1 ranking for the 15th year in a row for its nationally recognized workforce development program.
- Area Development’s annual poll considers approximately 50 leading site consulting firms from across the U.S. and evaluates 14 different factors to determine the Top State for Business.
- The second annual Governor's Workforce Summit was hosted at the Georgia World Congress Center in Atlanta.
- Georgia MATCH is described as the largest direct college admission program in the nation.
- Georgia MATCH sent a letter to every high school senior in the state informing them of spots at University System of Georgia (USG) and Technical College System of Georgia (TCSG) institutions.
- TCSG saw a preliminary year-over-year enrollment increase of 9.3% this fall following last year's program rollout.
- USG saw a preliminary year-over-year enrollment increase of 6.2% this fall following last year's program rollout.
- The enrollment increases occurred despite challenges from the federal government's mishandling of the FAFSA rollout.
- Dennis Shea, Publisher of Area Development, stated that Georgia has maintained top 10 rankings in survey subcategories for well over a decade, including available workforce, cost of doing business, cooperative and responsive state government, energy availability and costs, logistics and infrastructure, and site readiness programs.
- Site selectors recognize that Georgia provides the whole package for companies choosing to grow or expand in the state.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Georgia does economic development differently than anywhere else by prioritizing partnerships and investments in infrastructure, education, and workforce development.
- Between July 1, 2023, and June 30, 2024, GDEcD’s Global Commerce team supported 429 facility expansions and new locations.
- These supported expansions and new locations totaled more than $20.3 billion in investment.
- The investments resulted in plans for 26,900 new jobs statewide.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) serves as the state’s sales and marketing arm and lead agency for attracting new business investment and encouraging industry expansion.
