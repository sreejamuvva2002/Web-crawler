# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Group chairman outline strategic growth vision for 2021 and beyond | Autocar Professional](https://www.autocarpro.in/news-international/hyundai-motor-group-chairman-sees-2021-as-inflection-point-for-future-growth’-78118)  ·  `page_009400`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group Chairman Euisun Chung outlined the company’s strategic direction and commitments for 2021 and beyond in a New Year’s message to global employees.
- The Group aims to transform into a new growth engine by expanding market share in the eco-friendly market, securing future technology capabilities, and strengthening business competitiveness.
- Chairman Chung emphasized making "quality and safety" a top priority for all staff and business partners.
- 2021 is positioned as an inflection point and decisive year for long-term sustainable investments and innovations in the post-pandemic era.
- The Group’s vision includes stressing eco-friendliness, future technology, and business competitiveness; reinforcing electrification leadership with E-GMP based EV models; striving for carbon neutrality via hydrogen fuel cell technology; realizing the safest and most innovative mobility technologies; expanding new mobility through investments in new growth fields; and rationalizing its business portfolio.
- Hyundai Motor Group plans to launch dedicated electric vehicles on the E-GMP (Electric-Global Modular Platform), including Hyundai Motor’s IONIQ 5, Kia’s crossover EV, and Genesis luxury brand’s crossover EVs.
- The E-GMP platform offers a multi-charging system allowing both 800V and 400V charging capabilities.
- E-GMP technology is the world’s first patented system to operate the motor and inverter to boost 400V to 800V for stable charging compatibility.
- Battery electric vehicles based on E-GMP can achieve a maximum range of over 500 kilometres (WLTP standard) and high-speed charge up to 80 percent in 18 minutes, adding 100 kilometers of range in five minutes.
- Hyundai Motor Group plans to expand its battery electric vehicle lineup from eight models to 23 by 2025.
- The Group aims to sell 1 million EV units annually in global markets.
- Hyundai Motor Group will set up 20 high-speed charging stations in Korea by 2021 and expand its charging network with energy companies.
- Overseas, the Group plans to optimize EV charging infrastructure with partners including IONITY, Europe's leading high-power charging network in which it made a strategic investment.
- The Group will accelerate the creation of a hydrogen energy ecosystem by expanding the supply of hydrogen fuel cell systems for ships, generators, trains, and global automakers.
- Hyundai Motor Group recently introduced a dedicated HTWO brand for its hydrogen fuel cell systems.
- The Group will realize the safest and most innovative mobility technology by strengthening autonomous driving, connectivity, and software capabilities.
- Hyundai Motor Group will invest in new growth fields such as urban air mobility (UAM) and robotics to expand the realm of new mobility.
- The Group plans to apply Level 3 autonomous driving technology to mass production models in 2022.
- Hyundai Motor Group will push for commercialization projects for autonomous driving technologies, such as robotaxi services, in 2023.
- Motional Inc., a joint venture between Hyundai Motor Group and Aptiv, plans to conduct Level 4 driverless tests on public roads in Nevada, U.S.
- In 2023, Motional will work with U.S. car-sharing provider Lyft to launch autonomous driving commercialization services in major U.S. regions.
- The Group will focus on core next-generation software technologies including vehicle software for driving assistance, precision map-linked navigation, and connected infotainment services.
- Hyundai Motor Group recently signed a deal to acquire the controlling stake in Boston Dynamics.
- Hyundai’s wearable robot technology and industrial/logistics automation technology are expected to maximize synergies with Boston Dynamics’ capabilities.
- Robotics technology will be applied to autonomous driving, UAM, and PBV (Purpose Built Vehicle) to help establish a leading position in mobility areas.
- Hyundai Motor Group affiliates will enhance future business capabilities through electrification and self-driving core components and software specialization, while exploring smart city development and renewable energy projects.
