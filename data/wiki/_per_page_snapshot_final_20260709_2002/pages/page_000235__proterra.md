# Proterra

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2018-08-22

**Source:** [PROTERRA SELECTED IN ELECTRIC BUS CONTRACT BY GEORGIA DEPARTMENT OF ADMINISTRATIVE SERVICES](https://www.theautochannel.com/news/2018/08/21/624807-more-byd-to-become-electric-vehicle-supplier-to-state-georgia.html)  ·  `page_000235`

## Facts

- Proterra was selected by the Georgia Department of Administrative Services as a vendor to supply zero-emission battery-electric buses and charging stations for its Statewide Contract for Public Mass Transit & Transportation.
- The state of Georgia currently operates over 1,182 buses that service customers with nearly 150 million trips per year.
- Proterra was chosen by the State of Georgia as part of a “pool of providers” alongside other manufacturers to streamline local transit agencies’ access to vendors and vehicles.
- Local transit agencies can now purchase Proterra zero-emission buses and charging stations through the state’s pre-established contract, bypassing future RFPs.
- The contract represents Proterra’s growing foothold across the southeast region.
- Prior to this award, Proterra had already signed contracts with transit agencies in Alabama, Florida, South Carolina, North Carolina, and Tennessee.
- Proterra’s configurable EV platform includes adaptable battery and charging options suited for a wide range of transit and campus routes.
- The Proterra Catalyst E2 series achieves an efficiency rating of 22 MPGe.
- Conventional diesel buses average 3.86 MPG, compared to the Proterra Catalyst E2 series.
- Proterra utilizes a lightweight carbon-fiber-reinforced composite bus body instead of industry-standard steel frames to maximize vehicle life and minimize repairs and maintenance.
- Proterra offers a suite of high power DC fast chargers that comply with EV industry technology standards including J1772 CCS, J3105, and OCPP 1.6.
- Proterra’s charging systems support bi-directional, vehicle-to-grid (V2G) power flow to maximize energy management capabilities and help transit agencies achieve 100 percent electric bus fleets.
- Ryan Popple, CEO of Proterra, stated that implementing this transportation initiative is a huge win for Proterra, Georgia state institutions, and EV adoption in the U.S.
- Ryan Popple also expressed eagerness to support the state of Georgia’s efforts to accelerate the adoption of emission-free mass transit.
- Proterra designs and manufactures zero-emission heavy-duty vehicles to help bus fleet operators reduce operating costs while delivering clean transportation.
- The company has sold more than 645 vehicles to 70 different municipal, university, airport, federal, and commercial transit agencies across 33 U.S. states and Canadian provinces.
- Proterra’s configurable Catalyst platform is capable of serving the full daily mileage needs of nearly every transit route on a single charge.
- Proterra products are designed, engineered, and manufactured in America.
- Proterra maintains offices in Silicon Valley, South Carolina, and Los Angeles.
- The 2018 market shift in cost and performance for electric buses enabled municipal, airport, university, federal, commercial, and utility transit agencies to adopt electric solutions.
- Proterra's electric buses outperform internal combustion diesel or CNG engines in efficiency, reliability, acceleration, hill climb, and passenger-carrying power.
