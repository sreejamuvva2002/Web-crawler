# Georgia Department of Economic Development

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2020-09-03

**Source:** [GDEcD expresses gratitude to PPE providers during pandemic](https://valdostatoday.com/business-2/2020/09/gdecd-expresses-gratitude-to-ppe-providers-during-pandemic/)  ·  `page_012388`

## Facts

- The Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) and Governor Brian P. Kemp thanked companies across the state for supplying personal protective equipment (PPE) and health care supplies to fight COVID-19.
- Governor Kemp signed legislation encouraging additional PPE production in Georgia and aiding businesses whose job numbers were harmed during the pandemic.
- Georgia reported roughly $574 million in new investments and 3,629 new jobs created in July prior to the immediate pandemic response.
- On June 30, Governor Kemp signed legislation offering economic relief to companies that created new jobs while making PPE and other critical health care supplies.
- The new legislation generated strong interest from industry.
- Governor Kemp expressed gratitude for manufacturers' resilience during the crisis and commended the General Assembly for supporting measures to secure Georgia's future.
- Georgia businesses adjusted operations, retooled machines, and pivoted to manufacture critical health care supplies during COVID-19.
- Some companies tapped assistance from GDEcD’s Centers of Innovation and Global Commerce divisions and their networks.
- The Atlanta Business Chronicle highlighted these manufacturing efforts in a recent article titled “Manufacturing companies create products to fight Covid-19”.
- The Georgia legislature, with support from Governor Kemp, Lieutenant Governor Geoff Duncan, and House Speaker David Ralston, passed a tax credit to aid PPE manufacturers, including those who did not traditionally manufacture PPE or hand sanitizer.
- The new credit aims to incentivize more PPE and hand sanitizer production in Georgia to enhance its position as a national leader in advanced manufacturing.
- Georgia’s GDP was $64.7 billion in 2019 due to strength across multiple manufacturing sectors.
- The Georgia Department of Labor estimated there were 387,400 manufacturing jobs in the state as of June 2020.
- Changes were made to existing job tax credit programs to give relief to companies whose recently created jobs were impacted by the pandemic.
- Existing Georgia companies that claimed Job Tax Credits or Quality Jobs Tax Credits in 2019 may carry over their 2019 job creation numbers to 2020 and/or 2021.
- This change ensures impacted businesses can continue receiving tax credits at 2019 levels while returning employment to pre-COVID levels.
- The credit expires on Jan. 1, 2025.
- The Georgia Department of Community Affairs’ Board of Directors updated its policies to account for the new legislation.
- GDEcD led efforts to support businesses, identify companies restructuring operations for PPE production, and coordinate with existing producers.
- The Georgia Suppliers Interactive Map and List, compiled by GDEcD’s Centers of Innovation, includes more than 320 Georgia companies providing protective supplies.
- The interactive map displays business locations and allows sorting supplies by category.
- The free tool allows Georgia businesses to support each other while protecting employees and patrons, keeping valuable dollars in the state.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson appreciated Governor Kemp and the legislature for incentivizing PPE production and safeguarding job creators.
- Wilson stated these pro-business measures will help ensure Georgia’s future is bright as they continue to create good jobs and prepare for public health challenges.
- H.B. 846 covers the PPE credit and carryover option legislation.
