# Volvo Cars

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-05-26

## Facts

- The United States government has granted Volvo permission to continue importing and selling its connected vehicles in America under new federal supply-chain security rules.
- Volvo received a “specific authorization” from the Office of Information and Communications Technology and Services as part of the US Commerce Department’s “Securing the Information and Communications Technology and Services Supply Chain: Connected Vehicles” regulation.
- The rule requires automakers to undergo federal review of their connected-car software, data security, and foreign supply chains, particularly those linked to China.
- Volvo Cars is majority owned by China’s Zhejiang Geely Holding Group, which bought the company from Ford in 2010.
- Volvo received approval after discussions with US officials regarding its governance, technology, and data security measures.
- Bloomberg reported the authorization allows Volvo to avoid restrictions tied to a crackdown on Chinese vehicle technology.
- The approval removes a potential hurdle for Volvo's goal of expanding in the United States.
- Volvo operates an assembly plant in Charleston, South Carolina.
- Volvo has invested more than $1.3 billion at its Charleston, South Carolina plant.
- The Charleston plant has created more than 2,000 jobs.
- Last year, Volvo announced plans to add two more vehicles to production at the Charleston plant before 2030.
- CEO Håkan Samuelsson suggested earlier this year that the company could consider building Geely-developed EVs in South Carolina if regulations allowed it.
- Volvo Car USA is headquartered in New Jersey.
- Volvo Car USA currently operates 281 dealerships across 48 states.
- Volvo Car USA supports roughly 11,500 jobs nationwide.
