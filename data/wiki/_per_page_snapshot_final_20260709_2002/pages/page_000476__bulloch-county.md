# Bulloch County

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2023-07-08

**Source:** [Bulloch County deal will supply water to Hyundai plant - Statesboro Herald](https://www.statesboroherald.com/local/bulloch-county-deal-will-supply-water-hyundai-plant/)  ·  `page_000476`

## Facts

- Bulloch County Board of Commissioners contracted to buy two acres of land on Old Highway 46 as the proposed site for one of four large wells intended to supply water to Hyundai Motor Group’s Meta Plant America.
- Bulloch County is expected to own two wells and Bryan County will own the other two wells to furnish water to the $5.54 billion electric vehicle and battery manufacturing complex.
- The Hyundai plant is projected to open in the first half of 2025 and eventually employ 8,100 people.
- The plant is under construction in northern Bryan County a little over five miles from the Bulloch County line.
- Bulloch is expected to host all four wells, including the two that Bryan County will own.
- Bulloch County Manager Tom Couch stated this is still an “unwritten agreement in principle,” part of arrangements by the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority, its four member counties (Bryan, Bulloch, Chatham and Effingham), and Georgia’s state government to lure Hyundai.
- Bulloch commissioners voted 6-0 on July 5 to approve a purchase contract offering current owners Randall T. Neal, Janet N. Reid and Connie N. Robinson exactly $60,000 for the two-acre site in southeastern Bulloch County.
- The sale is not final, but the contract predicts that the county and current owners will close on or before Sept. 1 if conditions are met.
- Tom Couch confirmed in an email that the land is intended for one of the well sites serving Hyundai and noted the two acres would not allow co-locating other facilities.
- County officials have suggested at least one well site might host a Bulloch County Fire Department, EMS, or Sheriff’s Office substation, or a park, but Couch said 3-5 acres or more would be ideal for that purpose.
- This is the first identified site for one of the two wells Bulloch will own to provide water across the county line to the Hyundai plant.
- Couch and other staff proposed these wells will supply the beginnings of Bulloch’s first county-operated water system, serving future residential subdivisions, commercial businesses, and Hyundai suppliers in southeastern Bulloch County.
- A development strategy using county water and sewer systems to concentrate development was codified in an amendment to the Smart Bulloch 2040 Comprehensive Plan and Future Development Map approved by commissioners in early June.
- The plan stakes out a new “suburban neighborhood” character area and narrower “suburban corridors” in the southern end of Southeast Bulloch.
- A rewrite of zoning regulations created a new R-8 single-family residential classification allowed only for subdivisions served by public water and sewer systems in these areas.
- In June, commissioners approved rezoning to “HI” heavy industrial for a Hyundai supplier to build an injection-molded plastic parts factory on a 22-acre parcel on Georgia Highway 119 South in southeastern Bulloch.
- The project summary stated the supplier plant could use 50,000 gallons of water per day and that the county anticipates supplying it eventually with its water system.
- Before closing, the county must pay for a boundary survey and sellers must give access for an environmental audit if chosen by the county, with a 30-day notice to correct problems; similar conditions apply to title encumbrances.
- Bryan County communications manager Matthew Kent said he also understands Bryan will own two wells within Bulloch County, with Bulloch owning the other two.
- Couch commented that Bryan County officials may be prospecting for their own well sites.
- The regulatory reason for Bryan’s wells in Bulloch is coastal saltwater intrusions into the Floridan aquifer, which has led the Georgia Environmental Protection Division to include Bryan County in a “yellow zone” with restrictions on new wells and goals to reduce groundwater withdrawals.
- Unrelated to Hyundai, the city of Pembroke in northern Bryan County already owns a well in southern Bulloch under an intergovernmental agreement first signed in 2017.
- The four wells owned by Bulloch and Bryan counties are expected to supply 1.5 million gallons of water per day to the Hyundai plant.
- Last summer, Couch and staff projected $22.6 million in infrastructure spending to provide water and sewer service to future homes in southeastern Bulloch as well as the Hyundai plant.
- County officials earmarked federal American Rescue Plan Act (ARPA) money for about half of these costs.
- Hyundai and other customers will pay for the water they use, and Couch suggested Bryan County would pay a “host fee” for its wells in Bulloch.
