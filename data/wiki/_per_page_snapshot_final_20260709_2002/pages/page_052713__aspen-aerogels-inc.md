## Facts

- Aspen Aerogels, Inc. received a conditional commitment from the U.S. Department of Energy ("DOE") for a proposed loan of up to $670.6 million under the Advanced Technology Vehicles Manufacturing loan program within DOE's Loan Programs Office ("LPO").
- The loan is intended to finance the construction of Aspen's planned second aerogel manufacturing facility, called the Register Plant, in Register, Georgia.
- Aspen Aerogels Georgia, LLC, a subsidiary of Aspen Aerogels, Inc., will be the borrower of the proposed loan.
- The loan will be structured as project financing with repayment commencing upon project commissioning.
- The interest rate for the loan will be the applicable U.S. Treasury Rate at the time of each cash draw.
- The Register Plant is designed to manufacture PyroThin® aerogel blankets.
- Aspen's PyroThin platform offers ultrathin and lightweight thermal barriers designed to help prevent thermal runaway propagation in batteries.
- Thermal runaway is an event in which a battery cell overheats and triggers neighboring cells, possibly resulting in a sudden fire.
- PyroThin is designed to offer a unique combination of thermal management, mechanical performance, and fire protection properties.
- Aerogel thermal barriers have the potential to increase energy density of EV battery systems when compared to alternative thermal barrier products.
- The Register Plant has an initial estimated revenue capacity of $1.2B - $1.6B, depending on product mix.
- The project is expected to create up to 550 construction jobs.
- The project is expected to create 255 permanent, full-time operations jobs.
- Donald R. Young, Aspen's President and CEO, stated the proposed loan would fully fund the required CAPEX for the second aerogel manufacturing facility.
- Thermal Barrier segment revenues increased to $232 million over the trailing twelve months ended June 30, 2024, representing a 277% year-over-year increase compared to the same period in 2023.
- Aspen secured additional EV production contracts, bringing its total to six major OEMs in the United States and Europe.
- Aspen won a prestigious Automotive News Pace Award.
- The DOE and the Company must satisfy certain technical, legal, environmental, and financial conditions before entering into definitive financing documents and funding the loan.
- Q3 2024 preliminary quarterly revenue was approximately $117 million.
- Q3 2024 preliminary quarterly net loss was approximately $13 million, including a $27.5 million one-time charge from the extinguishment of a convertible note on August 19, 2024.
- Q3 2024 preliminary quarterly Adjusted EBITDA was approximately $25 million.
- Cash and cash equivalents were approximately $113 million as of September 30, 2024.
- Aspen's Battery Materials initiative seeks to increase the performance of lithium-ion battery cells to enable EV manufacturers to extend driving range and reduce EV costs.
- The company is headquartered in Northborough, Mass.
