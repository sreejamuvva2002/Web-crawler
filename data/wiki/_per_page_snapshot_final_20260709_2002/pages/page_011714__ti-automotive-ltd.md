# TI Automotive Ltd.

## Facts

- Global fuel systems supplier TI Automotive Ltd. has agreed to be acquired by private equity firm Bain Capital LLC.
- Sources told Bloomberg the acquisition deal is valued at $2.4 billion.
- Bain will pay about six times earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation and amortization (EBITDA).
- TI’s EBITDA is approximately $400 million, giving the company a sale price of around $2.4 billion.
- Talks between Bain and TI have been ongoing for more than a year.
- The two sides previously tried to finalize a deal last January valuing TI at more than $2 billion.
- Hedge funds owning TI delayed a vote on the sale to Bain last year to weigh a rival bid of roughly $2 billion from Pamplona Capital Management LLP.
- The hedge funds that own TI, led by Oaktree and Duquesne Capital Management LLC, voted against a sale last year because they wanted a higher price.
- The existing management team, including CEO Bill Kozyra, will continue to lead the company with no operational changes.
- Frank Buscemi serves as the global communications director for TI Automotive and is based in suburban Detroit.
- A source told Crain’s Detroit Business that CEO Bill Kozyra had a troubled relationship with Oaktree and other owners on the board, and the deal is likely a sigh of relief for him.
- The deal is subject to approval by TI Automotive shareholders.
- Closing is expected in mid-2015.
- CEO Bill Kozyra stated that Bain Capital will invest in TI Automotive to grow the business and enable the team to focus solely on manufacturing high quality, safety critical fluid systems for customers.
- Kozyra noted that Bain Capital brings a deep understanding of the business and a well-known track record of global success in the automotive industry.
- A substantial majority of investors have pledged to support the deal, which is still pending regulatory review and other customary closing conditions.
- TI traces its roots to 1919 and once supplied fuel lines to Ford Motor Co.’s Model T.
- The company is chartered in Britain with a headquarters in Auburn Hills, Mich., and was bought by Oaktree and Duquesne Capital in a 2007 debt restructuring.
- TI generated about $3 billion in revenue in 2013, with more than one-fourth coming from North America.
- Bain manages about $80 billion in assets.
- Bain's investments include Texas-based specialty retailer Michaels Stores Inc. and German auto-parts maker FTE Automotive GmbH.
