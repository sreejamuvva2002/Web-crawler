# FY23 AMMTO Battery Manufacturing Lab Call

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** research_and_development  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [Funding Selections: FY23 AMMTO Battery Manufacturing Lab Call | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/cmei/ammto/funding-selections-fy23-ammto-battery-manufacturing-lab-call)  ·  `page_029456`

## Facts

- The Office of Advanced Materials & Manufacturing Technologies (AMMTO) announced the selection of five projects for the FY23 AMMTO Battery Manufacturing Lab Call.
- Total funding awarded across all selected projects is $16 million.
- Topic 1 covers solid-state battery projects, comprising 3 awards totaling $11 million.
- Proposals in Topic 1 address barriers for domestic, large-scale solid-state battery production.
- The DOE is focusing on translating fundamental solid-state electrolyte R&D into large format/high-volume manufacturing RD&D.
- The DOE is also focusing on enhancing precision processing and fabrication of solid-state batteries in large format cells.
- The DOE is conducting verification and validation of solid-state battery scalability.
- Project 1 focuses on manufacturing large format, high-energy density, and long durability solid-state batteries with oxide electrolyte.
- Award amount for the oxide electrolyte project is $4 million.
- Oak Ridge National Laboratory leads the oxide electrolyte project.
- The project location is Oak Ridge, Tennessee.
- Industry partners for the oxide electrolyte project are Intecells and High-T Tech.
- ORNL and its partners will develop a scalable process to manufacture solid-state batteries with LiNixMnyCo1-x-yO2 (x≥0.6, NMC) cathode and oxide-based electrolytes.
- The oxide-based electrolyte used in the project is intrinsically nonflammable.
- The project will transform the current batch process for fabricating and sintering SSB oxide-based electrolytes into a continuous process.
- The project will integrate the cathode, electrolyte, and anode into full cells.
- The project aims to scale up cells from ≤10 mAh button cells to ≥ 1 Ah cells.
- Target performance for the scaled-up cells includes ≥350 Wh/kg energy density and 500 cycles.
- Project 2 focuses on solventless processing for precise, reliable, and scalable solid-state battery manufacturing.
- Award amount for the solventless processing project is $4 million.
- The National Renewable Energy Laboratory leads the solventless processing project.
- The project location is Golden, Colorado.
- Partners for this project include ORNL, SLAC National Accelerator Laboratory, Solid Power, SkyNano, Epic Advanced Materials, and Mott Corporation.
- The multi-lab and industrial team will advance next-generation SSB technology through novel dry process electrode manufacturing.
- The effort will combine state-of-the-art modelling, advanced characterization, new material formulation, novel high-throughput processing, and a unique three-dimensional cell architecture.
- The project aims to demonstrate high energy (>400 Wh/kg), large-area (> 100cm2) all solid-state batteries with commercially relevant, low failure rate manufacturing processes.
- Project 3 focuses on advancing solid-state battery manufacturing through scalable synthesis, processing, and analysis.
- Award amount for the scalable synthesis project is $3 million.
- Argonne National Laboratory leads the scalable synthesis project.
- The project location is Lemont, Illinois.
- Partners for this project include ORNL, Ampcera, Arkema, Forge Nano, Ion Storage Systems, Koura Global, NEI Corp., and Soelect.
- ANL and ORNL will partner with industry to explore SSB manufacturing through a comprehensive examination of slurry-based and dry processing methods for roll-to-roll (R2R) manufacturing.
- The primary aim is to optimize solid-state electrolytes for use in safer and higher-capacity batteries.
- The project seeks to enhance ionic conductivity, area-specific resistance, mechanical properties, and stability of SSEs.
- The project will investigate the integration of innovative R2R techniques into commercial-scale SSB production.
- Expected outcomes include refined manufacturing methodologies, a comprehensive understanding of slurry-based and dry processes, and practical guidelines for implementing R2R processes in SSB production.
- Topic 2 covers developing innovative flow battery manufacturing capabilities, comprising 2 awards totaling $5 million.
- Proposals in Topic 2 address technical and manufacturing challenges in U.S. flow battery production.
- The DOE is targeting optimization of flow batteries across commercial, industrial, and utility applications.
- The DOE emphasizes creating new or enhanced cell/reactor architectures and configurations for flow batteries.
- The DOE also emphasizes developing manufacturing and process standards for flow batteries.
- Project 4 focuses on standard electrolyte preparation and cell stack design for redox flow batteries.
- Award amount for the redox flow battery project is $2.9 million.
- Pacific Northwest National Laboratory leads the redox flow battery project.
- The project location is Richland, Washington.
- Partners for this project include ESS, Inc., Otoro Energy Inc., and the Chemours Company.
- PNNL and its partners will develop standardized redox flow battery electrolytes and stacks.
- The goal is to reduce the cost of manufacturing redox flow battery components and systems by implementing standardized designs and processes.
- Project 5 focuses on ultra-high volumetric power density bundled microtubular membrane-enabled flow batteries.
- Award amount for the microtubular flow battery project is $2.1 million.
- Oak Ridge National Laboratory leads the microtubular flow battery project.
- The project location is Oak Ridge, Tennessee.
- Partners for this project include Kraton Corp., Stryten Energy, Perma Pure, and the Georgia Institute of Technology.
- ORNL will collaborate with partners to develop a novel RFB architecture specifically the co-axial bundled microtubular RFB.
- The 3-year goals include developing a prototype based on the new RFB architecture that can deliver >10 kWh power.
- The 3-year goals also include standardizing material processing and cell fabrication protocol for this innovative RFB architecture.
