# SK On and Hyundai Motor Group Joint Venture EV Battery Facility

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-12-08

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Group and SK On To Build EV Battery Facility in Bartow | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-release/hyundai-motor-group-and-sk-build-ev-battery-facility-bartow-county)  ·  `page_003308`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Hyundai Motor Group (HMG) and SK On have selected a site in Bartow County for a new electric vehicle (EV) battery manufacturing facility that will supply Hyundai Motor Group’s plants in the U.S.
- Stakeholders estimate the project will create more than 3,500 new jobs through approximately $4-5 billion of investment in Bartow County.
- Hyundai Motor Group and SK On recently signed a memorandum of understanding (MOU) regarding the partnership for a new EV battery facility in the U.S., with details of the partnership still in development.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Hyundai Motor Group and SK On are valued partners and key players in our state's ever-growing automotive industry, and expressed pride in their investment in the No. 1 state for business.
- Established in 2021, SK On is the lithium-ion battery subsidiary branch of SK Innovation and currently employs more than 2,000 Georgians at its SK Battery America facility in Commerce.
- In July, SK Battery America announced it would partner with the Work for Warriors Georgia program to hire veterans, servicemembers, and their families at its Georgia operations.
- Subject to execution of relevant agreements and HMG and SK On's final board decisions, the new facility will be located at Bartow Centre, a zoned manufacturing and industrial site located on Highway 411, aiming to begin operations in 2025.
- Bartow County Sole Commissioner Steve Taylor expressed pleasure with the decision, thanked the owners of the former Bartow Centre Industrial property, and noted the project establishes Bartow in Georgia’s EV ecosystem while leveraging the area's advanced manufacturing talent base.
- Director of Korean Investment Yoonie Kim represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project in partnership with Georgia EMC, Development Authority of Bartow County, the Cartersville-Bartow County Department of Economic Development, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson noted that HMG and SK have been pioneering partners for Georgia for decades, driving the state's automotive renaissance, and highlighted efforts to create a fully integrated supply chain connecting battery manufacturers with recyclers.
- Georgia is positioned as a hub for the electric transportation industry due to its prime location, major ports system, extensive infrastructure, skilled workforce, and pro-business climate.
- Excluding this announcement, EV-related projects announced in the state since 2020 total approximately $17 billion in investment and more than 22,800 new jobs in Georgia.
- Hyundai Motor Group separately announced in May its plans to invest $5.54 billion in Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America, an EV manufacturing facility in Bryan County, breaking ground on the facility in October with Governor Kemp and other officials.
- Hyundai Motor Group is a global enterprise with about 250,000 employees worldwide, creating a value chain based on mobility, steel, construction, logistics, finance, IT, and service, with mobility brands including Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis.
- SK On was launched as an independent company in 2021 after SK Innovation split off its battery business, aims to become a world leader in the clean energy industry leveraging global production and R&D capabilities, is headquartered in Seoul, South Korea, and has battery plants operating or under construction across the U.S., Europe, and Asia.
