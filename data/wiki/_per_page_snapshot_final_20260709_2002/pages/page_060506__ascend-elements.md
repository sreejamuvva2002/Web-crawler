# Ascend Elements

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2022-10-19

**Source:** [Ascend Elements | Ascend Elements Awarded $480M in Grants from U.S. Department of Energy to Manufacture Battery Cathode Active Materials](https://ascendelements.com/ascend-elements-awarded-480m-in-grants-from-u-s-department-of-energy-to-manufacture-battery-cathode-active-materials/)  ·  `page_060506`

## Facts

- Ascend Elements received two grants from the DOE totaling $480m for the production of lithium-ion battery cathode materials made from recycled battery feedstock.
- Grants will support the construction of the planned $1B Apex facility in Hopkinsville, Ky.
- The Apex facility is expected to yield over $4.4B in positive economic impact for the region.
- Apex will be the first facility of its kind, utilizing the company’s patented Hydro-to-Cathode™ technology to produce cathode precursors (pCAM) and cathode active materials (CAM) from spent lithium-ion batteries.
- The awards were granted from the U.S. Department of Energy’s Bipartisan Infrastructure Law (BIL) Battery Materials Processing and Battery Manufacturing Initiative.
- The awards total $480M in matching funds to support construction of the Ascend Elements ‘Apex’ manufacturing facility in southwest Kentucky.
- The project aims to establish lithium-ion battery supply chain infrastructure that previously did not exist in the United States.
- Mike O’Kronley, CEO of Ascend Elements, stated: “These awards are transformational for the U.S. lithium-ion battery industry.”
- With Department of Energy support, the Apex facility will be the first and largest battery material production facility of its kind in the U.S., improving security of supply of critical materials for the domestic electric vehicle industry.
- The facility will enhance the United States’ competitiveness in the lithium-ion battery industry on a global scale.
- The grant accelerates achievement of a closed-loop lithium-ion battery supply chain.
- The Apex facility will be a commercial-scale deployment of the company’s Hydro-to-Cathode direct precursor synthesis technology which had been running at pilot scale in Massachusetts for the last few years.
- This technology enables manufacturing of lithium-ion battery cathode active material and precursors directly from a recycled battery feedstock known as black mass.
- Construction at the 140-acre site, located in Hopkinsville Ky., will begin this month (October 2022).
- Once completed, Apex will produce enough material annually to power over 250,000 electric vehicles.
- The project will generate approximately $4.4B in total economic impact during its construction phase and the first ten years of operation.
- The facility will create over 400 jobs in the Hopkinsville area.
- The Apex project will additionally seek to establish community benefits programs including job training and workforce development, affordable childcare, and affordable transportation initiatives.
- Ascend Elements is based in Westborough, Mass.
- Ascend Elements is the leading provider of closed-loop battery material solutions.
- Its Hydro-to-Cathode™ direct precursor synthesis technology produces new CAM from spent lithium-ion cells more efficiently than traditional methods.
- The process results in reduced cost, improved performance, and lowered GHG emissions.
- With fewer batteries going to landfill and a cleaner manufacturing process, Ascend Elements is taking the lithium-ion battery industry to a higher level of efficiency.
