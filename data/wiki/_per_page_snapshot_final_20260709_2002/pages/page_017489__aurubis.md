# Aurubis

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Aurubis looks to boost US sales in absence of tariffs - MINING.COM](https://www.mining.com/web/aurubis-posts-half-year-core-profit-above-estimates-confirms-guidance/)  ·  `page_017489`

## Facts

- Europe’s largest copper producer Aurubis is looking to increase its sales to the US while there are no import tariffs on the metal.
- CEO Toralf Haag told analysts that the company would ramp up its copper recycling smelter in the US this year, with potential for further investment in coming years.
- The company’s only US operation is a copper recycling smelter located in Richmond County, Georgia.
- CFO Steffen Hoffmann stated that the Richmond multi-metal recycling plant should contribute a lower double-digit million euro amount to the company’s earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation and amortization next year.
- Higher ramp-up costs for strategic projects, such as its multi-metal recycling plant in the United States, had a negative effect on operating EBT.
- Copper was not included in a list of reciprocal tariffs announced by the US in April.
- US President Donald Trump ordered a probe into copper imports in February, with findings expected by November 2025 at the latest.
- Haag stated that current sales to the US are limited but the operation aims to play a role in the US copper market.
- The company confirmed its forecast for the 2024/25 financial year, expecting full-year operating EBT around the midpoint of a 300 million to 400 million euros range.
- Half-year core profit beat market expectations on Thursday, helped by higher prices of sulphuric acid and precious metals.
- Operating earnings before tax (EBT) fell 5.8% to 229 million euros ($259.2 million) in the six months through March 2025, from 243 million euros a year earlier.
- The half-year operating EBT beat analysts’ expectations of 221 million euros.
