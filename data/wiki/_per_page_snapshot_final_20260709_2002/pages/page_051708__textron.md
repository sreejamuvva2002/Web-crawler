# Textron

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-05-05

**Source:** [Textron to shed industrial unit](https://www.aerotime.aero/articles/textron-shed-industrial-unit-cessna-beechcraft-bell)  ·  `page_051708`

## Facts

- Textron plans to separate its Industrial segment as it moves to sharpen its focus on aerospace and defense businesses led by Textron Aviation, Bell and Textron Systems.
- The company intends to shed its Industrial unit, which includes Kautex and Textron Specialized Vehicles, either through a sale or a tax-free separation into a standalone publicly traded company.
- The planned separation would leave the remaining company as a standalone aerospace and defense business built around Cessna and Beechcraft, Bell military and commercial rotorcraft, and Textron Systems’ defense and aerospace products.
- The company referred to the post-separation aerospace and defense business as “New Textron,” though it did not confirm the final company name.
- CEO Lisa Atherton stated that the planned separation creates greater clarity and focus for both businesses, positioning New Textron as a pure-play aerospace and defense company for higher growth while giving Industrial independence to pursue distinct strategies.
- The separation is intended to give each business more flexibility to pursue its own capital allocation strategy, product development priorities, and growth opportunities.
- The aerospace and defense business is expected to generate more than $12 billion in 2026 revenue and hold a $19 billion backlog after the separation.
- Textron expects the separation to improve its revenue growth profile and operating margins while continuing to maintain a strong balance sheet and invest in research and development and capital expenditures.
- The Industrial segment is expected to generate more than $3 billion in 2026 revenue.
- Kautex supplies plastic fuel systems, battery enclosures, and clear-vision systems for the automotive industry.
- Textron Specialized Vehicles brands include E-Z-GO, PACE Technologies, Jacobsen, and TUG Technologies.
- Executive Chairman Scott Donnelly stated that separating Industrial would sharpen Textron’s strategic focus and support long-term shareholder value creation.
- Textron is targeting completion of the separation within 12 to 18 months.
- The timing and structure of the separation remain subject to customary conditions, regulatory approvals, and final board approval.
