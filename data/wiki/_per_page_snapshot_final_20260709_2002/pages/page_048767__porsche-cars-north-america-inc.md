# Porsche Cars North America, Inc.

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Porsche Expanding North American HQ In Georgia - Automotive - Business Facilities Magazine](https://businessfacilities.com/porsche-expanding-north-american-headquarters-in-georgia/)  ·  `page_048767`

## Facts

- Porsche Cars North America, Inc. (PCNA) has announced a new multi-million dollar investment to further develop its North American headquarters and U.S. footprint in Georgia.
- The new 33-acre development is located at the Northeast corner of Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport.
- The development adds to the company’s existing 27-acre headquarters.
- Construction for the first stage of the development starts this month.
- The first stage will be a standalone Porsche Service Center, independently developed, owned and operated jointly by Hennessy Porsche North Atlanta and Porsche Atlanta Perimeter.
- Over the next three years, PCNA will invest in a new Porsche Classic Factory Restoration facility as well as a track extension for the Porsche Experience Center Atlanta.
- Kjell Gruner, newly appointed President and CEO of PCNA, stated that Porsche is committed to the United States and its American hometown of Atlanta.
- The expansion aims to enhance the customer experience by expanding the campus for faster service at a unique location next to the world’s busiest airport.
- This development starts a longer-term expansion of the successful Porsche Experience Center Atlanta, which has already welcomed over 270,000 visitors since opening in 2015.
- In partnership with two Atlanta dealers who will operate the service center, PCNA announced this next phase of development to lay the groundwork for additional economic activity in the region.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated it is a pleasure to see Porsche grow their U.S. footprint in Georgia in partnership with their two Atlanta area dealers.
- Georgia’s automotive industry is a $3 billion economic driver in the state, and Porsche is an important part of its success story.
- Governor Kemp thanked Porsche for their continued commitment to Georgia and looks forward to seeing the opportunities this multi-million dollar expansion will create for Georgians.
- City of Hapeville Mayor Alan Hallman expressed excitement for PCNA launching the expansion of its North American headquarters within the Hapeville community.
- The project symbolizes tremendous growth and opportunities for the City of Hapeville, according to Mayor Hallman.
- Atlanta Mayor Keisha Lance Bottoms thanked Porsche for continuing to believe, choose, and invest in Atlanta.
- The Atlanta Administration looks forward to working with Porsche’s leadership to strengthen the local economy through highly skilled and middle-wage opportunities.
- The headquarters campus expansion near Hartsfield-Jackson International Airport further cements Atlanta’s reputation as a premiere international hub for commerce, according to Mayor Bottoms.
- The Porsche Service Center South Atlanta will be a 20,815 square foot facility on 2.5 acres of land.
- The new service center facility will include 13 lift bays to service customer vehicles, a Porsche Driver’s Selection boutique, as well as a reception area and waiting lounge for customers.
- The Porsche Service Center South Atlanta is one of two standalone service centers for Porsche in the U.S.
- The service center will offer greater service capacity and additional convenience for customers in Atlanta and surrounding cities.
- The expected completion date for the service center is the third quarter of 2021.
- Phase two of development is expected to begin in 2021 with construction of a parking structure.
- Phase two will be followed by construction of the new Porsche Classic Factory Restoration facility and Porsche Experience Center Atlanta track extension.
- This phase of the development is expected to be completed in mid-2023.
