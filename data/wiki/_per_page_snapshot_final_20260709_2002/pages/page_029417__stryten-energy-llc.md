# Stryten Energy LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-01-19

**Source:** [Stryten Energy Enters the Long-Duration Energy Storage](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2022/01/19/2369455/0/en/Stryten-Energy-Enters-the-Long-Duration-Energy-Storage-Market-with-Acquisition-of-Storion-Energy-s-Vanadium-Redox-Flow-Battery-Technology.html)  ·  `page_029417`

## Facts

- Stryten Energy LLC, a U.S.-based energy storage solutions provider headquartered in Alpharetta, Georgia, announced on Jan. 19, 2022 that it purchased the assets of Storion Energy Inc.
- Storion’s vanadium redox flow battery (VRFB) technology is designed for applications requiring more than four hours of storage capacity to deploy clean energy from renewable sources such as solar and wind.
- Target applications for the VRFB technology include utility-scale storage, microgrids, and military bases.
- The technology solves the energy-to-power tradeoff by allowing batteries to operate for many hours without sacrificing power or release speed through the addition of more electrolytes.
- Key features of Storion’s proprietary VRFB design include domestically sourced vanadium that is easily recycled, increased power density, reduced battery downtime, potential limitless cycle life with proper maintenance, high-temperature ratings, thermal safety, and scalability for varying energy storage needs.
- Tim Vargo, Chief Executive Officer of Stryten Energy, stated the acquisition expands Stryten’s capabilities to solve current and future energy challenges and positions the company for success in the long-duration energy storage market.
- The acquisition adds a flow battery technology R&D facility to complement Stryten’s four North American R&D centers focused on advanced lead and lithium energy storage solutions.
- Stryten’s combined resources include a team of 2,500 employees and more than two million square feet of manufacturing space in the U.S.
- Mike Judd, President and Chief Operating Officer of Stryten Energy, noted the combination provides industrial resources to establish a reliable supplier of long-duration batteries engineered and produced in the U.S. for essential power, motive power, transportation, military, and government customers.
- Mohan Misra, Founder, Chairman and CEO of Storion Energy, highlighted leveraging Stryten’s U.S.-based manufacturing footprint and distribution network to accelerate commercial deployment of VRFB technology.
- Storion’s VRFB technology will expand the portfolio of Stryten’s newly formed Essential Power division, which provides advanced lead and lithium battery technologies, services, chargers, and racking systems.
- The Essential Power division serves backup power for military and government, telecommunications, uninterruptible power supply (UPS), railway, utility, microgrid, and renewable markets.
- Storion Energy Inc. was formed in 2015 as a commercial spinoff from ITN Energy Systems following a decade of fuel cell research.
- An ARPA-E-funded redox flow battery development program for the technology began in 2010.
- Storion’s solutions optimize battery storage performance to stabilize inconsistent renewable power, manage peak load demand and frequency regulation, and help investors achieve ROI expectations.
