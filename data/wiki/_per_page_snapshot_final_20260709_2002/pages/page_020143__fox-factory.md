# Fox Factory

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Fox Factory](https://www.carrolldaniel.com/case-study/fox-factory/)  ·  `page_020143`

## Facts

- Fox Factory is a manufacturer of performance shock absorbers and racing suspension products.
- Fox Factory selected Carroll Daniel to build its relocated assembly facilities in Georgia inside the Gainesville Industrial Park.
- The creation of a campus environment provides an opportunity to foster increased innovation and collaboration between Fox's teams and customers.
- The building will house functions necessary to utilize material from a raw material standpoint all the way through manufacturing, anodizing, pre-assembly, full-assembly, and shipping within the same building.
- Consolidating these steps reduces the need to ship product to other facilities to complete necessary steps prior to packaging.
- This building will be the first for the Fox family that will house anodizing and clean room facilities within a building owned by the company itself.
- Owning the facility eliminates the need to outsource activities in their overall shock production process.
- Site challenges required creative solutions, including using a Sikorsky helicopter to successfully set 33 HVAC units in place on the roof due to limited crane access.
