# Right to Work Georgia

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Recap: Peanuts, Guns, and Metal for Right to Work Georgia! | NILRR](https://nilrr.org/recap-peanuts-guns-and-metal-for-right-to-work-georgia/)  ·  `page_017466`

## Facts

- Businesses investing in Right to Work Georgia include Aurubis (a metal recycling company), American Peanut Growers, and Remington.
- Aurubis is adding a new location soon in Augusta.
- Remington is locating its headquarters in LaGrange.
- American Peanut Growers is expanding in Donalsonville.
- Altogether, these three investments will create 1,071 new jobs for the state.
- Aurubis is investing $340 million to add a new location in Right to Work Augusta, Georgia.
- The Aurubis investment will create 125 new jobs.
- Aurubis states that with this investment, they are setting a clear example of sustainable growth and will become a forerunner for multi-metal recycling in the U.S., a role they already hold in Europe.
- The Georgia Ports Authority is playing a role in bringing jobs and investment to Georgia.
- Exports produced by the Aurubis recycling operation will strengthen the state’s global position via Savannah, which is already one of the nation’s most balanced ports in terms of import-export trade.
- Aurubis sought site size, full utility services, and workforce; the corporate park and workforce were positives in Augusta’s favor.
- American Peanut Growers Group is investing $85 million to expand in Right to Work Donalsonville, Georgia.
- The American Peanut Growers expansion will create 90 new jobs.
- Agriculture is Georgia’s oldest and largest industry and continues to be a major driver of economic prosperity in every corner of the state.
- Georgia is perennially the number one state in the nation in the production of peanuts.
- Partners at the Development Authority of Seminole County and Donalsonville supported American Peanut Growers Group's investment.
- Remington is adding headquarters and manufacturing in Right to Work LaGrange, Georgia.
- The Remington investment totals $100 million.
- The Remington investment will create 856 new jobs.
- Georgia’s firearms industry is responsible for thousands of jobs and millions of dollars of investment in communities.
- A state official welcomes Remington to the Peach State, noting they are a proud owner of some of Remington’s first-class products.
- The official looks forward to seeing the oldest firearms manufacturer in America thrive in Georgia’s pro-business environment.
- For more than two centuries, Remington Arms has been part of the fabric of the country.
- A trusted brand like Remington has been interwoven into the lives of many who grew up with a connection to the outdoors and outdoor sports.
- Remington Firearms will locate their global headquarters and advanced manufacturing operations in Georgia, joining a strong community of great companies in the state.
