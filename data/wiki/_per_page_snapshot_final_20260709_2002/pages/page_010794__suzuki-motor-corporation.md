## Facts

- Suzuki Manufacturing of America Corp. (SMAC) opened its only U.S. manufacturing facility in Rome, Georgia, in 2002 and began producing the Eiger series of all-terrain vehicles (ATVs).
- In February 2011, SMAC celebrated the 10th anniversary of its Rome, Georgia, plant, reporting $1.4 billion in sales over the past decade.
- Suzuki Motor Corp. and Intelligent Energy of Loughborough in the UK announced a joint venture to accelerate the commercialization of zero-emission vehicles using hydrogen-powered fuel cells.
- Suzuki manufactures automobiles, engines, motorcycles, ATVs, outboard marine engines, wheelchairs, and small internal combustion engines.
- Suzuki has over 45,000 employees.
- Suzuki operates 35 production facilities in 23 countries and 133 distributors in 192 countries.
- In 2016, Suzuki was the eleventh biggest automaker by production worldwide.
- Suzuki's production output reached 3,900,000 units in 2021.
- In November 2012, American Suzuki Motor Corp. filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy protection and announced plans to discontinue building automobiles for the U.S. market.
