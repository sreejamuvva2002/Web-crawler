# U.S. Department of Energy

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [Energy Department Announces Largest Loan in Department History, Delivering Over $7 Billion in Electricity Cost Savings for Georgia and Alabama Customers | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/articles/energy-department-announces-largest-loan-department-history-delivering-over-7-billion)  ·  `page_028359`

## Facts

- U.S. Secretary of Energy Chris Wright announced the Department of Energy’s Office of Energy Dominance Financing (EDF) has closed a historic $26.5 billion loan package.
- The loan package will deliver over $7 billion in electricity cost savings to millions of customers in Georgia and Alabama.
- The investment is funded under President Trump’s Working Families Tax Cut and aligns with the Executive Order "Unleashing American Energy".
- The loans support two wholly owned subsidiaries of Southern Company.
- The investment aims to lower American energy costs, create thousands of jobs, and increase grid reliability in Georgia and Alabama.
- Secretary Wright stated that the Energy Department is lowering energy costs and ensuring access to affordable, reliable, and secure energy for decades to come.
- Secretary Wright noted the President's directive to reverse past administrations' "energy subtraction agenda" and add more reliable power generation to the electrical grid.
- The two loans will build or upgrade over 16 gigawatts (GW) of firm reliable power to the electrical grid.
- The power mix includes 5 GW of new gas generation, 6 GW in nuclear improved through uprates and license renewals, hydropower modernization, battery energy storage systems, and over 1,300 miles of transmission and grid enhancement projects.
- These loans represent the largest government investment aimed at directly lowering consumer energy costs and increasing grid reliability.
- Once all funds are received through the program, the loans are estimated to reduce Southern Company’s interest expenses by over $300 million per year.
- The reduced interest expenses will help expedite lower electricity costs for customers.
- Southern Company is among the first utilities working with the DOE and the Trump Administration to restore American energy dominance through common-sense energy investments.
- In 2025, Southern Company announced plans to enact multiyear rate freezes.
- The DOE remains committed to setting a new standard for government energy financing to ensure loans deliver affordable, reliable, and secure energy.
