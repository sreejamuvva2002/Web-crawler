# Kia Georgia

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-05-31

**Source:** [Kia Georgia unveils flagship electric vehicle, the 2025 EV9 SUV](https://www.wtvm.com/2024/05/31/kia-georgia-unveils-flagship-electric-vehicle-2025-ev9-suv/)  ·  `page_001079`

## Facts

- Workers at Kia Georgia’s assembly plant in West Point gathered on Thursday to celebrate the start of production for the 2025 EV9 SUV.
- The 2025 EV9 SUV serves as Kia’s flagship electric vehicle.
- The EV9 is the first saleable electric vehicle assembled in Georgia, according to Kia.
- Governor Brian Kemp and Kia Georgia’s vice president James Watson drove the EV9 off the assembly line.
- Governor Brian Kemp congratulated the Kia Georgia team on the achievement and thanked them for choosing Georgia.
- Governor Kemp stated that state and local partners working with Kia demonstrates the value of such collaboration.
- Georgia is working to become the "e-mobility capital of the nation."
- The EV9 joins existing SUVs like the Telluride, Sorento, and Sportage being assembled at Kia Georgia.
- An executive named Yoon stated that the launch reflects Kia’s commitment to the U.S. market and the state of Georgia.
- Through "Plan S," Kia aims to be a leader in the e-mobility sector for years to come.
- The EV9 has won accolades including World Car of the Year, World EV of the Year, and North American Utility Vehicle of the Year.
- Nearly 15 years have passed since the first Kia vehicle rolled off the assembly line at the West Point facility.
- Production of the EV9 SUV has resulted in approximately 200 additional jobs at the plant.
- The West Point facility now has the flexibility to assemble both EVs and traditional products on the same assembly line.
- Stuart Countess serves as the president and CEO of Kia Georgia.
- Stuart Countess stated that the Kia Georgia team continues a mindset of turning challenges into opportunities.
- Kia Georgia is adding its flagship EV to the facility and entering a new era focused on assembling high-quality vehicles in West Point.
