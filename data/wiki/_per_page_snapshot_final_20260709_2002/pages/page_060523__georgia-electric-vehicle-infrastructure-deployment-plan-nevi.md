# Georgia Electric Vehicle Infrastructure Deployment Plan (NEVI Program)

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** charging_infrastructure  ·  **Published:** 2024-05-12

**Source:** [Georgia NEVI Program - All You Need To Know](https://getevgas.com/georgia-nevi/)  ·  `page_060523`

## Facts

- Georgia released its Electric Vehicle Infrastructure Deployment Plan in August 2023.
- The plan outlines an investment of over $135 million from the Federal National Electric Vehicle Infrastructure (NEVI) Program.
- The strategy was developed by the Georgia Department of Transportation (GDOT).
- It is a comprehensive five-year strategy to build out a network of DC fast chargers across designated Alternative Fuel Corridors.
- Input from regional planning commissions, local governments, utilities, site hosts, EV charging companies, labor organizations, freight carriers, and advocates for disadvantaged communities shaped the plan's approach to charger siting, procurement, operations, and equitable access.
- Initial funds will focus on deploying NEVI-compliant stations spaced no more than 50 miles apart along approved corridors.
- GDOT acknowledges that additional infrastructure beyond the current plan will be required to meet Georgia’s charging needs as EV adoption grows.
- The agency plans to use lessons from similar programs in neighboring states to maximize the program's impact.
- Multiple state agencies are aligned behind GDOT’s coordination efforts to support transportation electrification, economic development, and environmental goals.
- Utilities are assessing grid capacity and strategies for “make-ready” infrastructure to energize charging sites.
- Ongoing collaboration aims to optimize deployments and accelerate the energy transition.
- Equity remains a top priority for the program.
- Strategies to engage and benefit underserved communities were informed by discussions with advocates.
- Performance measures will track progress toward increasing affordable mobility options for all Georgians.
- Training initiatives aim to create green jobs through fair labor practices.
- GDOT will keep stakeholders engaged annually through the implementation phase to adapt the plan as needs evolve.
- The first chargers are scheduled to come online in 2023, positioning Georgia to lead the Southeast in developing the backbone for nationwide EV adoption.
