# U.S. Department of Energy

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [Energy Department Announces $11 Million in Awards to Develop HALEU Transportation Packages | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/articles/energy-department-announces-11-million-awards-develop-haleu-transportation-packages)  ·  `page_004272`

## Facts

- The U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) announced $11 million in awards to five U.S. companies to develop and license new or modified transportation packages for high-assay low-enriched uranium (HALEU).
- The announcement was made on December 8, 2025, during Secretary of Energy Chris Wright’s visit to Idaho National Laboratory (INL) in Idaho Falls, ID.
- The visit marked the final stop in Secretary Wright’s ongoing tour of all 17 DOE National Laboratories.
- The selections advance President Trump’s executive orders and commitment to rebuild the Nation’s nuclear fuel cycle.
- The initiative aims to strengthen domestic enrichment and fabrication capabilities.
- The initiative aims to accelerate the deployment of advanced reactors to usher in a new American nuclear renaissance.
- Secretary Chris Wright stated: “From critical minerals to nuclear fuel, the Trump administration is fully committed to restoring the supply chains needed to secure America’s future.”
- Secretary Chris Wright stated: “Thanks to President Trump, the Energy Department is operating at record speeds to unleash the next American Nuclear Renaissance and to deliver more affordable, reliable, and secure energy for American families and businesses.”
- DOE’s $11 million in awards will support industry-led efforts to design, modify, and license transportation packages through the U.S. Nuclear Regulatory Commission (NRC).
- The investments will help establish long-term, economical HALEU transport capabilities that better serve domestic reactor developers.
- The investments aim to strengthen the U.S. nuclear supply chain.
- Five companies were selected for Topic Area 1 to develop new package designs that can be licensed by the NRC: NAC International, Westinghouse Electric Company, Container Technologies Industries, LLC, American Centrifuge Operating, and Paragon D&E.
- NAC International was also selected for Topic Area 2 to modify existing design packages for NRC certification.
- Projects under Topic Area 1 will have performance periods of up to three years.
- The Topic Area 2 project will have a performance period of up to two years.
- Funding is provided through DOE’s HALEU Availability Program, established under the Energy Act.
- The program supports civilian research, development, demonstration, and commercial use of HALEU.
