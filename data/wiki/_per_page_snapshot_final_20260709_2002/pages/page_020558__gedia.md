# GEDIA

## Facts

- GEDIA has existed for over a century.
- Since 1955, the company has produced for the automotive industry.
- Today, GEDIA employs over 4,700 professionals across nine production plants globally.
- The company is involved in joint ventures and research projects worldwide.
- In the 1950s, GEDIA entered plastic handle production and began parallel production of pressing, stamping, and deep-drawing parts for automobiles.
- In the late 1990s, production plants were established in Nowa Sól (Poland) and Sta. Margarida (Spain).
- Production facilities were later added in Hungary, a second plant in Poland, and new plants in China and Mexico.
- Engineering centers were established in France, the US, and Sweden.
- In 2003, GEDIA sold its handle division to focus on developing lightweight technologies and automotive chassis components.
- In 2016, the GEDIA Automotive group expanded with new headquarters in Michigan (USA) and Pune (India).
- Construction of the "Askay II" production plant began in June 2018 and became operational in 2019.
- In 2022, a new production plant in Georgia (USA) was integrated into GEDIA's production network.
- The Georgia plant supplies body components to both German and American OEMs.
