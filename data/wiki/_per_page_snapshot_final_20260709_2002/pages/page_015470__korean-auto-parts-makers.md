# Korean auto parts makers

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Korean auto parts players to shine as carmakers go all in on EVs - KED Global](https://www.kedglobal.com/ev-parts-makers/newsView/ked202102080004)  ·  `page_015470`

## Facts

- Korean auto parts makers were historically regarded by stock investors as mere subcontractors with performance largely dependent on local carmakers.
- Nearly all major global automakers and tech giants are going all in on electric vehicles, bringing Korean auto parts players into the spotlight due to their technology and capacity for EV components.
- Analysts say Korean companies are well positioned in the global EV value chain as Hyundai Motor Co. and Kia Corp. are among the leading automakers shifting to EVs and future mobility.
- Hyundai has set a goal of supplying 8-10% of the world’s EVs by 2025.
- Hyundai plans to introduce more than 12 battery-powered electric cars built on the E-GMP platform.
- Among major global carmakers, only four have EV-dedicated platforms ready to roll out battery-powered cars: General Motors Co., Volkswagen Group, Hyundai Motor, and China’s Geely.
- Japan’s Toyota Motor Co. has developed its own EV platform architecture called e-TNGA but has yet to unveil an electric car based on it.
- Global auto parts players previously outperformed Korean peers in combustion-engine components but were relatively late preparing for the EV era.
- German parts makers benefited from gasoline and diesel car popularity in China, while Japanese auto parts makers focused on hybrid cars.
- Korean auto parts makers have shifted their focus to EV components.
- LG Chem Ltd., Samsung SDI Co., and SK Innovation Co. have supplied battery components to Volkswagen, which also procures other parts from Korean suppliers.
- EV tires are supplied by Hankook Tire & Technology Co. and automotive compressors by Hanon Systems Co.
- GM gets batteries for its own EV platform from LG Chem, motor converters and inverters from LG Electronics Inc., headlamps from SL Corp., and tires from Hankook Tire.
- Automotive parts used in EVs are five times more expensive per unit compared to combustion-engine cars, according to Samsung Securities auto analyst Im Eun-young.
- Stock investors can take advantage of expected gains by investing in suppliers advancing into the US EV market jointly with Hyundai and Kia, or buying shares of parts makers with business tie-ups with global automakers.
- Hyundai and Kia are widely expected to provide the E-GMP platform to other carmakers and tech giants entering the electric car market.
- Analysts expect Korean automakers to produce more EVs in the US than their Chinese rivals starting from 2022.
- SL Corp. entered the US market by supplying headlamps to Hyundai and Kia there.
- SL Corp. is forecast to see its US revenue rise 50% this year, with global sales estimated at 3 trillion won ($2.7 billion), up 20% from 2020.
- Korean suppliers Hanon, Mando Corp., Woory Industrial Co., and Pyeong Hwa Automotive Co. count Chinese EV automakers NIO Co. and Xiaopeng Motors Co. as new clients, in addition to existing customers Tesla Inc. and Volkswagen.
- Mando, Woory, and Pyeong Hwa also supply components to US EV startups Canoo Inc. and Rivian Co.
- The price-to-earnings ratios (PER) of Mando and Hanon are hovering around a multiple of 30 times.
- The PE multiple of Hyundai Mobis Co., an affiliate of Hyundai Motor, stands at 15 times, signaling further upside potential.
- NH Investment & Securities analyst Cho Soo-hong named Hyundai Mobis one of their top picks among auto parts plays as Hyundai Motor Group’s E-GMP is set to take off soon.
