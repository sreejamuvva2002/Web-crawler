# Aurubis

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_material  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [A conversation with our Executive Board - Aurubis Annual Report 2024/25](https://annualreport2024-25.aurubis.com/a-conversation-with-our-executive-board)  ·  `page_017415`

## Facts

- Aurubis is setting a course to capitalize on megatrends through its revised strategy, "Aurubis Performance 2030. Forging resilience. Leading in multimetal."
- The company is strengthening safety and plant security while prioritizing its core business and leveraging efficiencies in its smelter network.
- Aurubis has a strong footprint in Europe and North America with a clear focus on responsible concentrate processing and recycling.
- Electrification, energy infrastructure, defense, artificial intelligence, and data centers require high volumes of copper and other metals produced by Aurubis.
- By 2035, more than 200,000 new wind turbines will have been built with up to 40 t of copper each.
- Over 1,000 new hyperscale data centers are expected to require as much as 30,000 t of copper apiece for electricity and cooling by 2035.
- Rising defense spending worldwide will flow into industries that rely on Aurubis's metals.
- Market analysts anticipate a demand increase over the next ten years of around 26% for gold, 40% for tin, and 82% for tellurium.
- Demand for copper is expected to double by 2050.
- Aurubis has already commissioned key strategic investment projects, with others following in the current fiscal year and next.
- The company deployed more than 75% of the around €1.7 billion planned for concrete projects by the end of September.
- Aurubis is systematically developing all sites in its smelter network while continuously optimizing material streams and investing in state-of-the-art technology to maximize metal recovery.
- Aurubis's new site in the United States stands out in terms of investment volume, providing unique capacities with the first secondary smelter for complex recycling materials.
- The US needs about two million t of copper per year, half of which is imported, creating high dependence on foreign countries, especially South America.
- Aurubis's new site in the United States is positioned to reduce this import dependence and benefit from the attractive US market.
- An innovative recycling plant will be commissioned in Hamburg in 2026 to process significantly more complex recycling materials.
- Aurubis has expertise in recovering 20 metals and elements, developed over nearly 160 years of handling complex raw materials.
- The company aims to achieve the highest recovery rates to convert all input materials into marketable products, ensuring exceptional product quality and supply security for customers.
- Aurubis's copper production has a carbon footprint more than 60% below the global average of all copper smelters.
- Aurubis invested in plant facilities, processes, and know-how, including carrying out the largest scheduled maintenance shutdowns in its history at its Hamburg and Bulgaria sites.
- New anode furnaces in Hamburg are hydrogen-ready for the first time and built for the hydrogen age.
- The company consistently eliminates bottlenecks in production steps and uses state-of-the-art technologies such as digital twins to optimize production control and planning.
- Aurubis has more than doubled the number of cameras at its Hamburg site, increased security staffing levels, intensified employee training, relies on drones with infrared cameras for perimeter security, and invested in a state-of-the-art automated sampling system.
- These security measures are being rolled out to other sites.
- Aurubis invests extensively in decarbonization and environmental protection, having spent more than €1 billion since 2000.
- Recycled content in Aurubis's cathodes is high, at around 45%.
- Nearly all of Aurubis's sites are certified by the Copper Mark.
- Aurubis halted further investment in battery recycling because the market is developing much more slowly than expected with stark regional differences, despite recovery rates for valuable battery metals like lithium being at about 95%.
- The company decided to concentrate its resources on processing primary and secondary materials and producing copper and other metals of the future.
- Demand for copper products is good, with momentum seen in the copper premium.
- Aurubis is forecasting an earnings corridor between €300 and €400 million for the fiscal year, on par with the previous year's level.
- The company is targeting positive free cash flow.
- Aurubis aims to continue growing strategically while allowing shareholders to participate in its success.
- For the 2025/26 fiscal year, Aurubis targets a dividend payout amounting to 30% of adjusted consolidated earnings.
- The company has a strong balance sheet with very low debt and an equity ratio of over 50%, which opens the door on possible acquisitions, though that is not the current focus.
