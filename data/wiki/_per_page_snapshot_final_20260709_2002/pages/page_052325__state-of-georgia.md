# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-05

**Source:** [Georgia Scores Top Spot in Competitive Economic Rankings - Valdosta CEO](https://valdostaceo.com/features/2024/05/georgia-scores-top-spot-competitive-economic-rankings/)  ·  `page_052325`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp and the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) announced that Georgia earned the No. 1 spot in Site Selection’s annual Prosperity Cup rankings for successful investment recruitment in 2023.
- The Prosperity Cup ranking criteria include the total values of new and expanded facilities in the state, business-climate attractiveness, and workforce readiness.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that the award adds to a decade of evidence that Georgia's business environment is working for job creators.
- Georgia provides access to global markets through logistics assets including Hartsfield-Jackson International Airport and some of the fastest-growing ports in the nation.
- In 2023, Georgia attracted 318 new and expanded facilities to secure its No. 1 ranking.
- The Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) project represents an expanded scope and is the largest project in state history.
- HMGMA announced an additional 400 new jobs and $2 billion in investment in 2023, bringing its total investment to $7.59 billion.
- The HMGMA project will net 8,500 new jobs for Georgians.
- Anovion Technologies selected southwest Georgia in May for an $800 million, 400-job synthetic graphite manufacturing facility.
- Anovion Technologies broke ground on its synthetic graphite manufacturing facility in southwest Georgia in June of last year (2023).
- Meissner Corporation plans to create more than 1,700 new jobs at a new campus in northeast Georgia that will more than double the company’s U.S. manufacturing footprint.
- Site Selection’s Mark Arend stated that the Prosperity Cup recognizes state-level economic development competitiveness and that Georgia consistently delivers location attributes required by capital investors.
- Following HMGMA's groundbreaking in October 2022, Georgia welcomed more than 10 Hyundai and automotive suppliers to middle Georgia and beyond in 2023.
- These Hyundai and automotive suppliers will ultimately create over 4,300 new jobs and bring $1.2 billion in combined investment.
- Kia’s facility in West Point announced an expansion during 2023.
- Toyota’s campus in Pendergrass announced an expansion during 2023.
- The Kia and Toyota expansions created a combined 440 new jobs and $286 million in investment.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Georgia's economic development team is collaborative, working with sister state agencies, utility partners, and local economic developers to match companies with communities.
- The top Prosperity Cup spot reaffirms the effectiveness of Georgia's decades-long partnerships and consistent pro-business policies in intentional economic development.
