# Solarcycle

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2024-02-16

**Source:** [Arizona company plans $344 million Georgia factory](https://ktar.com/arizona-news/arizona-company-plans-344-million-georgia-factory-to-make-recycled-glass-for-solar-panels/5562971/)  ·  `page_020318`

## Facts

- Arizona-based company Solarcycle announced plans to build a $344 million factory in Cedartown, Georgia.
- The new facility will be the first time the company expands into making new glass for solar panels.
- Solarcycle plans to hire more than 600 workers at the Cedartown factory.
- The factory is located in northwest Georgia, about 50 miles (80 kilometers) northwest of Atlanta.
- The factory is scheduled to open in 2026.
- Solarcycle was founded in 2022.
- The company opened its first recycling facility in Odessa, Texas.
- Earlier this month, the company opened a headquarters, research lab, and second recycling facility in Mesa, Arizona.
- The Mesa expansion hired more than 100 people.
- Solarcycle's automated recycling process can extract materials worth 95% of a solar panel’s value, including silver, silicon, copper, and aluminum.
- The Cedartown facility will be able to recycle 1 million solar panels.
- The company plans to make enough glass to produce solar panels that could generate 5 gigawatts of electricity per year, using a combination of recycled glass and raw material.
- Solarcycle intends to sell the manufactured glass to companies that make solar panels in the United States.
- South Korean-owned Qcells, which makes solar panels in nearby Dalton, contracted with Solarcycle last week to recycle decommissioned Qcells panels in the United States.
- Solarcycle has similar recycling contracts with more than 40 other solar energy companies.
- Spokesperson Brooke Havlik stated the company chose Cedartown to be close to domestic solar panel makers and noted the location offers rail and shipping infrastructure and workers.
- Solarcycle has raised tens of millions of dollars from private investors for expansion.
- The Cedartown factory will largely be funded through private investment, according to Havlik.
- The company received $1.5 million from the U.S. Department of Energy to fund research and development.
- Havlik stated that companies buying Solarcycle’s glass are expanding, “largely driven by incentives and tailwinds” created by Biden administration actions.
- Democratic U.S. Sen. Raphael Warnock credited President Joe Biden’s clean energy and healthcare law, the Inflation Reduction Act, with spurring Solarcycle’s investment, saying Georgians continue “to reap its benefits.”
- Gov. Brian Kemp argued that Georgia’s business environment deserves credit for attracting companies like Solarcycle and Qcells.
- Georgia Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson said the company approached state economic recruiters at a trade show.
- Wilson stated in a quote: “Solarcycle provides a critical piece to the integrated solar supply chain we are building in Georgia.”
- Solarcycle did not disclose specific worker wages, only describing pay and benefits as “competitive.”
- The company could qualify for $9 million in state income tax credits, calculated at $3,000 per job over five years.
- To qualify for the tax credits, workers must make at least $31,300 a year.
- Chris Thomas, president and CEO of the Development Authority of Polk County, confirmed Solarcycle will receive property tax breaks from Cedartown and Polk County but did not provide an estimate.
- Georgia will pay to train workers for the facility, according to Solarcycle.
