# Archer Aviation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-05-19

**Source:** [Archer Aviation named as official air taxi provider of 2028 Olympic Games - The Covington News](https://www.covnews.com/news/business/archer-aviation-named-official-air-taxi-2028-olympic-games/)  ·  `page_016975`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation was named the official air taxi provider of the 2028 Los Angeles Olympic and Paralympic Games.
- Archer Aviation will also serve as the air taxi provider for Team USA at the LA28 Games.
- The Midnight aircraft, which is partially being manufactured at the Covington “ARC” facility in Covington, Georgia, will be the primary aircraft used for the partnership.
- Adam Goldstein, CEO and founder of Archer Aviation, expressed excitement about the partnership in a released statement.
- Goldstein stated that Archer wants to transform transportation in Los Angeles and leave a legacy shaping the future of transportation in America during the LA28 Games.
- The Midnight aircraft will fly passengers over Los Angeles emblazoned with the Team USA logo and the Olympic Rings and Paralympic Agitos.
- Archer labels the Midnight aircraft as an affordable alternative for medium-distance drives.
- Chief People and Partnerships Officer Dr. Tosha Perkins previously called the Midnight “the uber of the skies” in an interview with The Covington News.
- Perkins explained that users will be able to book routes via the Archer app, reducing travel times from 60-90 minutes by car to 10-15 minutes in the aircraft.
- The Midnight aircraft can host up to four passengers along with carry-on luggage and is operated by a licensed pilot.
- Travel speeds for the Midnight are expected to pace up to 150 mph with minimal charge times between back-to-back flights.
- Flight prices for the Midnight are expected to be in the range of “20% higher than an Uber black.”
- The Midnight is currently in its preliminary production phase following the completion of the ARC facility in December 2024.
- Archer expects a full grand scale rollout of the Midnight by 2026.
