# State of Georgia

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Georgia's Top Spot: Investment Recruitment Success in 2023](https://ciiadvisors.com/georgia-earns-top-spot-in-site-selection-ranking/)  ·  `page_052326`

## Facts

- Georgia scored the top spot in Site Selection’s annual Prosperity Cup ranking of state-level successful investment recruiting in the previous year.
- The headline on Site Selection regarding the award is “Georgia’s Long Game Secures Its Claim to the Cup.”
- Georgia unseated previous #1 North Carolina, which had claimed the top spot in the ranking for the past three years.
- Press releases celebrating the award were issued by the Governor’s Office and the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD).
- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp stated that job creators choosing Georgia get access to global markets through logistics assets like Hartsfield-Jackson International Airport and some of the fastest-growing ports in the nation, along with state and local partners.
- Georgia attracted 318 new & expanded facilities in 2023.
- A top criterion for the Prosperity Cup is a state’s ability to attract new and expanded facilities in the previous year.
- Georgia led all states with 318 new and expanded projects, including the expanded scope of the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant.
- The Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant is described as the largest project in the history of Georgia.
- The Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant was originally announced in 2022.
- The South Korean automaker plans to produce a diverse range of innovative Hyundai, Genesis and Kia electric vehicles (EVs) at the facility.
- Hyundai also plans a new battery manufacturing facility northwest of Atlanta in Bartow County.
- Hyundai Motor Group is investing $7.59 billion to develop HMGMA.
- The Hyundai project will create 8,500 direct jobs for Georgians.
- Georgia received a boost in the Prosperity Cup ranking when Hyundai expanded its commitment in 2023 by an additional $2 billion and 400 new jobs.
- Anovion Technologies announced plans for an $800-million, 400-job synthetic graphite manufacturing facility in Decatur County.
- Meissner Corporation plans to double its Georgia manufacturing footprint with a new $250 million facility in Athens scheduled to open in 2026.
- GDEcD Commission Pat Wilson credited the state’s pro-business environment as a solid contributing factor to Georgia's top ranking.
- Pat Wilson stated that taking the top spot reaffirms that the Georgia Way works, citing decades of partnerships, intentional economic development groundwork, and consistent pro-business policies.
- Florida, North Carolina, and South Carolina earned spots behind Georgia in Site Selection’s ranking of “South Atlantic” states.
- North Carolina earned the #2 spot in the “South Atlantic” ranking for attracting new and expanded facilities.
- South Carolina earned the #3 spot in the “South Atlantic” ranking.
- Florida earned the #4 spot in the “South Atlantic” ranking.
- Other states earning the #1 spot for attracting new investment in different regions included Ohio (East North Central), Texas (South Central), Pennsylvania (Northeast), Kansas (West North Central), and Arizona (Mountain).
