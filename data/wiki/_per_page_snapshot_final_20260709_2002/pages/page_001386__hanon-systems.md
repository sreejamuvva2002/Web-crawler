# Hanon Systems

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** thermal_management  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hanon Systems, which shares the global market for thermal management components for vehicles with Ja.. - MK](https://www.mk.co.kr/en/business/10942697)  ·  `page_001386`

## Facts

- Hanon Systems shares the global market for thermal management components for vehicles with Japan's Denso.
- The company recorded KRW 2.4625 trillion in sales and KRW 53.2 billion in operating profit in the fourth quarter of last year (consolidated).
- Fourth-quarter sales rose 5% from the same period last year, while operating profit fell 48.9%.
- Based on annual performance, Hanon System's sales last year rose 10.8% from the previous year to 9.5593 trillion won, and its operating profit rose 8.1% to 277.2 billion won.
- The company is investing $40 million (about 53 billion won) in Georgia to build a new factory.
- The company is investing $170 million (about 227 billion won) in Tennessee to build a new factory.
- Hanon Systems invested 443 billion won in R&D last year, up 14% from the previous year.
- Over the past five years, the amount of money spent on R&D has reached 1.87 trillion won.
- Last year, the depreciation cost of Hanon System totaled 598 billion won, which is 2.2 times the annual operating profit.
- Hanon Systems acquired Magna International's hydraulic control business unit for $1.23 billion (about 1.64 trillion won) in 2019.
- Depreciation costs from the Magna acquisition alone amount to about 30 billion won per year.
- EV investment is expected to begin to pay off in earnest in the second half of this year.
- Hyundai Motor Group will begin mass production of electric vehicles at a plant dedicated to electric vehicles (HMGMA) in the U.S. in the second half of this year.
- Sales of thermal management parts will begin at Hanon System's new plant in Georgia when Hyundai begins production.
- Hyundai Motor Group will introduce electric vehicles using next-generation platforms starting next year, and Hanon System will supply parts for next-generation thermal management systems.
- Mercedes-Benz and BMW are expanding their heat pumps to next-generation electric vehicle models.
- The heat pump technology recycles heat generated from a battery for indoor heating and minimizes the degradation of battery performance in winter when installed in an electric vehicle.
- As of the end of last year, the order balance of Hanon System totaled $51 billion (about 68 trillion won), of which about 70% were electric vehicle parts.
- Currently, electric vehicle parts account for 26% of the total sales of Hanon System.
- Sales of electric vehicle parts increased 17% in a year to 2.48 trillion won last year.
- Hanon Systems supplied 3 million electric air compressors (e-compressors) to automakers last year.
- The e-compressor maintains the battery pack at a safe operating temperature by circulating refrigerant in electric vehicles.
- Rolls-Royce electric vehicle 'Spector' uses an e-compressor from Hanon System.
- Hanon Systems presented KRW 10 trillion in sales and KRW 400 billion in operating profit as its earnings forecast for this year.
- Compared to last year's performance, the projected sales and operating profit increased by 4.6 percent and 44.3 percent respectively.
- Hanon System presented 11 trillion won in sales and 600 billion won in operating profit as next year's forecast.
- The company expressed confidence that it can increase its operating profit ratio by 40-50% for the second consecutive year.
