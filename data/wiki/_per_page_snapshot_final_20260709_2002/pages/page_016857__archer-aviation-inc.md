# Archer Aviation Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-12-31

**Source:** [Archer Aviation - Archer Announces Fourth Quarter & Full Year 2024 Results, Sets Plans For Midnight Commercial Deployments Ahead Of FAA Certification](https://investors.archer.com/news/news-details/2025/Archer-Announces-Fourth-Quarter--Full-Year-2024-Results-Sets-Plans-For-Midnight-Commercial-Deployments-Ahead-Of-FAA-Certification/)  ·  `page_016857`

## Facts

- Archer is starting production of its first Midnight aircraft at its ARC facility in Covington, GA.
- Archer plans to build up to 10 Midnight aircraft this year to support ongoing certification testing programs and deployments with key partners.
- Archer unveiled a "Launch Edition" commercialization program for the Midnight aircraft to deploy commercially to dozens of early adopter markets ahead of FAA type certification.
- Abu Dhabi Aviation (ADA) is Archer's first Launch Edition customer, with plans to deploy an initial fleet of Midnight aircraft beginning later this year.
- Archer will provide ADA with a team of pilots, technicians, and engineers to support the initial operational ramp.
- In December, Archer announced an exclusive partnership with Anduril Industries Inc. to jointly develop a hybrid vertical take off and landing (VTOL) aircraft for critical defense applications.
- Archer believes potential demand for the hybrid defense aircraft is stronger than expected and expects sizable programs of record without needing FAA certification.
- Archer's total liquidity is over $1 billion, including a Q4 2024 ending cash balance of $835 million and $302 million raised through an equity offering in February.
- Archer's spending in Q4 2024 remained within its guided range and stayed nearly flat for the second straight quarter.
- Archer reported a net loss of $536.8 million for fiscal year 2024.
- Archer's total operating expenses were $509.7 million for fiscal year 2024.
- Adam Goldstein serves as Archer's founder and CEO.
- Jeff Greenwood serves as Archer's Chief Pilot.
- The company is designing and developing key enabling technologies and aircraft necessary to power the future of aviation.
