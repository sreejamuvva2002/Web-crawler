## Facts

- Counties in East Central Georgia look to two powerful economic engines: the long-established metro of Augusta to the north and an emerging megasite to the southeast where automaker Hyundai Motor Group is building an electric vehicle and battery plant.
- The Hyundai facility investment is $5.5 billion.
- The Hyundai facility is projected to open by 2025.
- Benjy Thompson is CEO of the Development Authority of Bulloch County.
- Four counties collaborated to bring Hyundai to Bryan County.
- Benjy Thompson describes the Hyundai project as "the largest project in the history of the state to the coastal region."
- Suppliers and service providers are racing to set up their own operations before the first EV rolls off the assembly line.
- A Bulloch location for suppliers puts them about 30 miles away from the plant.
- Joon Georgia Inc. announced it will build a new manufacturing facility in Bryan County.
- The Joon Georgia plant will make frames for EVs.
- The Joon Georgia project is expected to create more than 600 jobs.
- The Joon Georgia project involves a $317 million investment.
- Ecoplastic is a Korean-based auto parts manufacturer investing $205 million into a manufacturing facility in Bryan County.
- Ecoplastic produces consoles, bumpers, and other parts.
- Ecoplastic is a tier-1 supplier for Hyundai.
- The Ecoplastic project will add more than 450 jobs.
- Since February 2022, Bulloch County has added 1,400 jobs and $900 million in capital investments.
