# Hyundai-LG Energy Solution Battery Plant

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-05

**Source:** [Immigration raid hits Biden-touted plant construction site in Georgia | Fox News](https://www.foxnews.com/politics/massive-dhs-raid-hits-georgia-hyundai-factory-biden-touted-major-victory-new-american-jobs)  ·  `page_020176`

## Facts

- A Hyundai factory under construction near Savannah, Georgia, was previously touted by former President Joe Biden and other politicians as a win for manufacturing jobs in the United States.
- Homeland Security Investigations arrested 475 people who were in the country illegally, primarily from South Korea, at the under-construction battery plant.
- Hyundai claimed that none of the detained individuals were "directly employed" by the company.
- The incident brings renewed scrutiny on construction sites of large projects as the Trump administration continues to investigate illegal worksite practices.
- Former President Joe Biden announced in May 2022 that the project involves more than $10 billion in new investment in American manufacturing, including a $5 billion commitment for advanced automotive technology and a $5.5 billion investment to open a new factory near Savannah, Georgia, which is going to create more than 8,000 new American jobs.
- Biden stated that his administration is setting ambitious standards to cut pollution in cars and trucks and boost fuel economy standards for those continuing to operate on gasoline.
- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp touted the project in February in an X post praising the "momentum" of the project.
- The effort is a partnership between Hyundai and LG Energy Solution to bolster EV development in the U.S.
- HSI reported that some of the arrested workers were employed by subcontractors on the construction site, which has since been paused.
- An EV manufacturing plant on the campus was not affected by the raid.
- ICE and other law enforcement agencies were part of the operation at the site.
- Hyundai stated that it prioritizes the safety and well-being of everyone working at the site and complies with all laws and regulations wherever it operates.
- LG Energy Solution stated that it will fully cooperate with the relevant authorities.
- Operations at worksites have become a noteworthy element of the Trump administration’s crackdown on illegal immigration.
- A man wearing an HSI vest at the site on Thursday announced via video that he had a warrant and ordered construction to cease immediately, stating "We need all work to end on the site right now."
- A DHS spokesperson described the operation as a "complex case" that required help from numerous federal agencies and the Georgia State Patrol.
