# Toyota Industries Electric Systems North America (TIESNA)

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-11-15

**Source:** [Toyota campus in Pendergrass, Georgia expanding](https://www.onlineathens.com/story/business/2023/11/15/toyota-campus-pendergrass-georgia/71590370007/)  ·  `page_012367`

## Facts

- Toyota's footprint in northeast Georgia is growing.
- Toyota Industries Electric Systems North America (TIESNA), a subsidiary of Toyota that manufactures air conditioning compressors and other vehicle electronics, broke ground on a new facility.
- The expansion adds 250 employees to the existing Jackson County campus.
- County Commission Chair Tom Crow welcomed the third Toyota Industries related company to Jackson County and pledged support for its success.
- TIESNA is making a $69 million investment in the new facility.
- The facility will produce DC-to-DC converters.
- Operations at the new facility are expected to begin in 2025.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Georgia has distinguished itself as a leader in the automotive industry and thanked Toyota for choosing to expand its footprint there.
- TD Automotive Compressor Georgia moved to Pendergrass in 2004.
- Toyota Industries Compressor Parts America, Co. (TICA) joined the Jackson County campus in 2012.
- The TIESNA investment is part of a string of automotive manufacturing additions throughout Georgia.
- Rivian Automotive recently secured property for its $5 billion EV plant in Morgan and Walton counties.
- Hyundai is building a massive manufacturing facility near Savannah, scheduled to begin production in the coming months.
- Hwashin Co. is committing $176 million for a plant in Dublin that will supply the Hyundai facility.
- SK Battery America, which operates an EV battery plant in Jackson County, recently announced furloughs despite Georgia's growing automotive manufacturing industry.
