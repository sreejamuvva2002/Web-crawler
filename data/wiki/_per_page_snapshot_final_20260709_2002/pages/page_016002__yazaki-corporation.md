## Facts

- Yazaki Corporation, headquartered in Minato-ku, Tokyo with President Riku Yazaki, has won the Automotive News PACE Award 2024.
- The award was sponsored by Automotive News (America) and given for the flexible printed circuit (FPC) type busbar module developed by the company.
- The Automotive News PACE Award is given to suppliers who develop innovative technologies and manufacturing processes in the automotive industry and contribute to its development in a game-changing way.
- Yazaki Corporation received a double award last fiscal year and has now won an award two years running.
- Yazaki stated it will continue striving for the development of products that put customers first and work on improving the safety and comfort of mobility society.
- The spread of hybrid EVs and battery EVs is advancing rapidly in the midst of the transformation surrounding the automotive industry.
- As the need for extended range increases with these vehicles, it is expected that battery capacity will become increasingly large.
- In association with greater battery capacity, components need to be smaller to improve mounting efficiency and have a lower profile to secure cabin space.
- Ensuring the safety of high-voltage batteries is also necessary alongside size reduction.
- Yazaki developed the FPC type busbar module to contribute to the reduction of battery size and weight and securing safety.
- The product replaces the normal electric wires of a voltage detection circuit with an FPC on busbar modules.
- The busbar modules have functions that detect cell voltage and temperature while simultaneously connecting multiple cells in series in the high voltage battery of an electric vehicle.
- The adoption of FPC eliminates the excess space required by wire-type modules.
- The busbar module on its own is about 50% lighter than a wire-type.
