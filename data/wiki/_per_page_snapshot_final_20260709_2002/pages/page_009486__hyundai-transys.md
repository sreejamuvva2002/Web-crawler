# Hyundai Transys

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2020-12-02

**Source:** [Hyundai affiliate plans $240M transmission plant in Georgia | FOX 5 Atlanta](https://www.fox5atlanta.com/news/hyundai-affiliate-plans-240m-transmission-plant-in-georgia)  ·  `page_009486`

## Facts

- A South Korean auto parts supplier will invest more than $240 million to build a transmission factory in western Georgia.
- The company plans to hire 678 workers for the new facility.
- Hyundai Transys announced plans on Tuesday for the new factory in West Point.
- The new factory will adjoin an existing Hyundai Transys factory and a Kia Motors Corp factory.
- Both facilities are part of Hyundai Motor Group, the Korean auto conglomerate that sells cars under the Kia and Hyundai brands.
- The new plant will manufacture eight-speed transmissions.
- The facility will be 620,000 square feet (58,000 square meters) in size.
- The plant will be located on the Kia campus just off Interstate 85 in West Point near the state line with Alabama.
- Hyundai Transys was created by a merger of two Hyundai affiliates last year.
- The company also manufactures axles and vehicle seats.
- The company has operations in South Korea, the United States, and seven other countries.
- The investment will create an additional 165 jobs.
- Once the new plant is complete, Hyundai Transys's total employees in Georgia will rise above 1,700.
- West Point Mayor Steve Tramell stated he is thrilled about the long-term investment and the new jobs being created.
- Many supplier plants are clustered near the Hyundai plant in Montgomery, Alabama, and the Kia plant in West Point.
- Kia, its affiliates, and suppliers had more than 4,300 employees in Georgia’s Troup County in 2018.
- This is the largest industrial announcement by investment value made so far in 2020 in Georgia.
- Hyundai TRANSYS Georgia Powertrain President Sangkil Jung cited years of trust built through capital investment, employment, training support, and unmatched opportunities as reasons Georgia was a natural choice.
- Final incentives for Hyundai Transys have not been agreed upon yet, according to Marie Hodge Gordon, spokesperson for the Georgia Economic Development Department.
- The company will qualify for a state income tax credit allowing it to deduct $3,500 per job from its Georgia income taxes for five years.
- If Hyundai Transys does not incur $2.37 million in state income taxes each year, it can use the credit to receive cash payments redirected from money withheld from employee paychecks for state income taxes.
- The plant's location is classified by the state as a less-developed area.
- Workers must earn at least $28,000 annually for Hyundai Transys to qualify for the tax credits.
- The state will pay to train Hyundai Transys workers.
- The state could also contribute funding to pay for infrastructure.
- Hyundai Transys will automatically qualify for tax breaks that will save it millions in sales and use taxes on machinery.
- Local officials could also abate property taxes.
- West Point’s financial statements show that in 2018, Kia, its affiliates, and suppliers were receiving $2.85 million in property tax breaks from the city alone.
- Kia received subsidies and tax breaks worth more than $450 million to locate its plant in West Point, which opened in 2009.
- The state aims to revitalize a region previously hit hard by the closure of textile and apparel plants.
- Because the plant is only miles from the Alabama state line, a significant number of its workers live in Alabama.
