## Facts

- Rivian has already begun public deliveries of its lower-priced R2 mid-size SUV from its Normal, Illinois plant.
- Rivian is extending its AT&T partnership to add built-in 5G connectivity.
- Rivian is planning additional capacity at a future Georgia facility.
- This move marks Rivian's push beyond the premium niche into the mass-market EV segment.
- Management is targeting gross margin positive R2 units.
- Rivian is leveraging software-enabled features to support longer-term financial resilience.
- The immediate catalyst for investors is clear execution on R2 deliveries and margins.
- The biggest risk remains high cash use and the possibility of further capital raises.
- Rivian plans to add up to 300,000 units of annual capacity at its future Georgia plant starting in 2028.
- This expansion could reinforce the R2 platform as the center of Rivian's growth story.
- The expansion amplifies execution and capital intensity risks if demand, pricing, or regulatory support fall short of expectations.
- Rivian projects $19.1 billion revenue and $478.9 million earnings by 2029.
