## Facts

- Hyundai has not canceled plans to complete the electric vehicle battery plant in Georgia but has recommitted to the project and broadened its agreement.
- At its CEO Investor Day and a prior press release, Hyundai announced a sweeping expansion to production and an overall investment of $26 million over three years.
- CEO José Muñoz outlined Hyundai Motor's goals to grow in the global market while speaking in New York.
- Hyundai announced a $5 billion battery joint venture in Cartersville, Georgia.
- Hyundai plans expansions to the Kia Corp. plant in West Point, Georgia.
- Hyundai is building a steel mill in Donaldsonville, Louisiana.
- Hyundai is establishing a robotics hub and expanding its plant in Montgomery, Alabama.
- In a press release ahead of Investor Day, Hyundai announced a reduction to its 2025 budget citing a current tariff rate of 25%.
- Hyundai's new operating profit margin will be approximately 7%, with a goal to raise it back to approximately 9% by 2030.
- The 25% tariff is directly affecting Hyundai's profitability by raising costs on cars and components not made domestically, as only about 40% of its U.S. sales are currently made in the U.S.
- Hyundai plans rapid growth at its Ellabell Georgia "Metaplant" with a target of producing 500,000 vehicles a year, including hybrids and EVs.
- Hyundai plans to introduce a midsize truck for the first time ever.
- Hyundai plans to offer 18 hybrid models globally.
- Hyundai plans to launch extended-range EVs in 2027.
- Hyundai plans to release several US specific offerings.
- Hyundai will have a 10 model lineup at the Georgia facility.
- For Phase 1 in 2025, Hyundai raised its revenue growth target and adjusted operating profit.
- For Phase 1 in 2025, Hyundai announced a KRW 77.3 trillion ( $55.66 billion in USD) investment by 2030.
- For Phase 1 in 2025, Hyundai plans to roll out a Software-Defined Vehicle (SDV) platform and High-Performance Vehicle Computer.
- For Phase 1 in 2026, Hyundai's Genesis brand will introduce hybrid models.
- For Phase 1 in 2026, Hyundai will launch an all-new Palisade Hybrid.
- For Phase 1 in 2026, Hyundai will launch a cloud-based battery management system.
- For Phase 1 in 2026, Hyundai will open a plant in Saudi Arabia.
- For Phase 1 in 2027, Hyundai plans the debut of its first Extended Range electric vehicle (EREV).
- For Phase 2 in 2028, Hyundai will expand at Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA).
- For Phase 2 in 2028, Hyundai will add 200,000 units of annual capacity.
- For Phase 2 in 2028, Hyundai will create 3,000 new jobs.
- For Phase 2 in 2028, Hyundai will reach 500,000 a year hybrid and EV vehicles.
- For Phase 2 in 2030, Hyundai aims to reach a target of 55.5 million vehicles sold globally.
