# Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation of America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2021-08-12

**Source:** [Yamaha Celebrates 4 Million Units Assembled in the USA](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2021/08/12/2280046/0/en/Yamaha-Celebrates-4-Million-Units-Assembled-in-the-USA.html)  ·  `page_004460`

## Facts

- Yamaha Motor Manufacturing Corporation of America (YMMC) and Yamaha Motor Corp., USA (YMUS) celebrated the assembly of a new 2021 Wolverine RMAX2 1000 Side-by-Side, marking a historic milestone of 4 million vehicles produced in the U.S.
- The celebration took place at YMMC’s state-of-the-art manufacturing facility in Newnan, Georgia, on August 12, 2021, to recognize employee dedication and manufacturing quality.
- YMMC serves as Yamaha’s hub for 4-wheel off-highway vehicle manufacturing, producing all of the company’s Side-by-Sides, full-size ATVs, WaveRunners, and Golf Cars for worldwide distribution.
- Over the past 20 years prior to August 2021, Yamaha invested more than $380 million into U.S. manufacturing to establish the world-class Newnan facility.
- Taka Imanishi, president of YMMC, stated that the company maintains high quality and environmental standards while supporting community programs through corporate social responsibility.
- Since its founding in 1988, the YMMC facility has expanded to cover 280 acres with more than 1.3 million square feet of manufacturing space in Newnan.
- Yamaha employs over 2,000 people in Georgia across the YMMC facility and YMUS corporate offices in Marietta and Kennesaw, accounting for more than half of the company’s 4,000+ U.S. employees.
- The city of Newnan designated May 18, 2011, as “Yamaha Day” to celebrate the company’s positive economic impacts and ongoing job creation in the area.
- Yamaha’s Georgia operations and workforce were featured behind-the-scenes on the season 19 premiere of “Modern Marvels: Machines” on the HISTORY Channel.
- Mike Martinez, YMUS vice president of motorsports, noted that Yamaha continues to add personnel and strengthen Georgia operations as demand for outdoor recreation grows and new Side-by-Side models are introduced.
- The YMMC facility holds ISO9001 Quality Management System and ISO14001 Environmental Management System certifications for quality, safety, and environmental manufacturing standards.
- YMUS maintains corporate offices in California, two in Georgia (Marietta and Kennesaw), facilities in Wisconsin and Alabama, and factory operations in Tennessee and Georgia.
- Additional U.S.-based subsidiaries include Yamaha Marine Systems Company, Skeeter Boats, and Yamaha Precision Propeller, alongside a nationwide network of distributors and dealers for YMUS products.
