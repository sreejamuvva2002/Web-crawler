# SK On / Hyundai Motor Group Joint Venture EV Battery Plant

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-12-08

**Source:** [EV's Are Good For American Workers:Hyundai Motor Group and SK On To Build EV Battery Facility in Bartow County](https://www.theautochannel.com/news/2022/12/08/1228908-hyundai-motor-group-and-sk-to-build-ev-battery-facility.html)  ·  `page_050122`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Hyundai Motor Group (HMG) and SK On selected a site in Bartow County for a new electric vehicle (EV) battery manufacturing facility.
- The new facility will supply Hyundai Motor Group's plants in the U.S.
- Stakeholders estimate the project will create more than 3,500 new jobs through approximately $4-5 billion of investment in Bartow County.
- Hyundai Motor Group and SK On recently signed a memorandum of understanding (MOU) regarding the partnership for a new EV battery facility in the U.S., with details still in development.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Hyundai Motor Group and SK On are valued partners and key players in Georgia's ever-growing automotive industry.
- SK On was established in 2021 as the lithium-ion battery subsidiary branch of SK Innovation.
- SK On currently employs more than 2,000 Georgians at its SK Battery America facility in Commerce.
- In July, SK Battery America announced it would partner with the Work for Warriors Georgia program to hire veterans, servicemembers, and their families at their Georgia operations.
- The new facility is subject to execution of relevant agreements and HMG and SK On's final board decisions.
- The facility will be located at Bartow Centre, a zoned manufacturing and industrial site located on Highway 411.
- The project is aiming to begin operations in 2025.
- Bartow County Sole Commissioner Steve Taylor expressed pleasure with the decision of Hyundai Motor Group and SK On.
- Taylor thanked the owners of the former Bartow Centre Industrial property and noted the project will establish Bartow in Georgia's EV ecosystem in a significant way.
- Director of Korean Investment Yoonie Kim represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development's (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The project was pursued in partnership with Georgia EMC, Development Authority of Bartow County, the Cartersville-Bartow County Department of Economic Development, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that HMG and SK have been pioneering partners for Georgia for decades as major drivers for Georgia's automotive renaissance and the first Korean manufacturer to locate in the state, respectively.
- Wilson noted that supporting cooperation across the growing EV ecosystem is creating a fully integrated supply chain for automotive OEMs while connecting battery manufacturers with recyclers to close the loop on battery manufacturing.
- Georgia is positioned as a hub for the electric transportation industry due to its prime location, major ports system, extensive infrastructure, skilled workforce, and pro-business climate.
- Excluding this announcement, EV-related projects announced in the state since 2020 total approximately $17 billion in investment and more than 22,800 new jobs in Georgia.
- In May, Hyundai Motor Group separately announced plans to invest $5.54 billion in Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America, an EV manufacturing facility in Bryan County.
- Hyundai Motor Group broke ground on the Metaplant America facility in October with Governor Kemp and other state, local, and federal officials.
- Hyundai Motor Group is a global enterprise with about 250,000 employees worldwide and mobility brands including Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis.
- SK On was launched as an independent company in 2021 after SK Innovation split off its battery business.
- SK On aims to become a world leader in the clean energy industry by leveraging its global production base and R&D capabilities.
- SK On is headquartered in Seoul, South Korea, and has battery plants currently operating or in construction across the United States, Europe, and Asia.
