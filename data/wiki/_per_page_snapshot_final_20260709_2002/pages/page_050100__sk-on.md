# SK On

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-03-20

**Source:** [Ford EV Partner SK On Signs Battery Supply Agreement With Nissan](https://fordauthority.com/2025/03/ford-ev-partner-sk-on-signs-battery-supply-agreement-with-nissan/)  ·  `page_050100`

## Facts

- Ford and South Korean EV battery manufacturer SK On have a long relationship that extends beyond a simple customer-supplier dynamic.
- The joint venture between Ford and SK On, known as BlueOval SK, is preparing to begin production of EV batteries at the under-construction BlueOval SK Battery Park in Kentucky very soon.
- SK On has signed a battery supply agreement with Nissan.
- Under the agreement, SK On will supply Nissan with roughly 100 GWh of EV batteries between 2028 and 2033.
- All batteries supplied under the agreement will be built in the U.S.
- The batteries utilize high-nickel pouch cells.
- The batteries will power Nissan's next-generation EVs.
- Nissan's next-generation EVs will be built at the company's plant in Canton, Mississippi starting in 2028.
- This agreement marks SK On's first partnership with a Japanese automaker.
- SK On's other automotive partners include Ford, Hyundai, and Ferrari.
- SK On currently operates two battery plants in the U.S.
- SK On is building four additional battery plants with its partners in the U.S.
- Once completed and fully operational, SK On's projected annual production capacity in the U.S. will be more than 180 GWh.
- Seok-hee Lee, SK On President and CEO, stated that the agreement underscores the strength of their battery technology and growing presence in the North American market.
- Seok-hee Lee added that leveraging their production footprint and expertise, they are committed to supporting Nissan’s electrification strategy and the broader transition to sustainable mobility.
- Christian Meunier, Chairman of Nissan Americas, stated that the agreement is a significant milestone for Nissan’s electrification journey in North America and supports further investment in their U.S. production footprint.
- Christian Meunier noted that through this partnership with SK On, Nissan can leverage their growing U.S. production capacity to deliver innovative, high-quality electric vehicles that meet customer needs.
