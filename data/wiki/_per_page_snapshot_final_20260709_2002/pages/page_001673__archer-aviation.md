# Archer Aviation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-01-02

**Source:** [Archer Aviation completes Georgia air taxi factory | Manufacturing Dive](https://www.manufacturingdive.com/news/archer-aviation-completes-georgia-factory-manufacture-air-taxis/736311/)  ·  `page_001673`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation received the certificate of occupancy for its approximately 400,000-square-foot manufacturing facility in Covington, Georgia.
- The company plans to begin initial production at the facility in early 2025.
- Archer broke ground on the Covington facility in March 2023.
- In December, Archer announced it is finalizing an agreement with global automaker Stellantis to serve as its exclusive contract manufacturer.
- Archer aims to manufacture two aircraft per month by the end of next year (2026).
- By 2030, Archer plans to produce 650 electric takeoff and landing aircraft annually.
- During a November 2024 investor call, Founder and CEO Adam Goldstein named New York City, Los Angeles, the San Francisco Bay Area, and Miami as the company’s first U.S. markets.
- Archer has received interest from the United Arab Emirates and Japan for its air taxi services.
- The company plans to deploy a network of air taxis in Southern California in time for the 2026 FIFA World Cup in Los Angeles.
- Archer worked closely with Stellantis to construct the Covington facility.
- The facility will produce Archer’s Midnight aircraft, which is optimized to fly routes of 20 to 50 miles at up to 150 mph with a quick recharge between flights.
- The Midnight air taxi can carry up to four passengers and their carry-on luggage, plus a pilot.
- Stellantis Chief Manufacturing and Supply Chain Officer Arnaud Deboeuf stated that supporting Archer’s electric aircraft growth with deep manufacturing expertise is a great opportunity for both companies.
- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp issued a statement saying Archer’s announcement reaffirms the state’s commitment to working with innovative companies to create quality jobs and opportunities for years to come.
- President-elect Donald Trump expressed support for advanced air mobility technology such as air taxis in a 2023 campaign speech, indicating potential regulatory friendliness from the incoming administration.
