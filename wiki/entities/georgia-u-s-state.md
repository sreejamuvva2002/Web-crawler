# Georgia (U.S. state)

**Type:** Government Program  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[government-support|Government Support]]  ·  **Location:** Georgia, United States

## Overview

Georgia currently leads the United States in committed investments and permanent jobs in electric-vehicle and battery manufacturing, with billions of dollars announced and tens of thousands of new jobs since 2021.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Leads nation in EV and battery manufacturing investments and permanent jobs.

## Key facts

- Georgia leads the nation in committed EV and battery manufacturing investments and permanent jobs.
- $22 billion and nearly 24,000 new private-sector EV and battery manufacturing jobs have been announced for Georgia since November 2021.
- Blue Bird’s Fort Valley facility employs 2,000 workers in a town of only 9,000 residents.
- Blue Bird plans to ramp up production from two electric buses per day to 20.
- Hyundai’s Ellabell plant is projected to build 300,000 EVs annually and create upward of 8,500 permanent jobs.
- Rivian’s second Georgia facility is anticipated to employ 7,500 workers by 2030.
- FREYR chose the Atlanta metro area for a $2.6 billion battery plant because of strong logistics connections and an engineering workforce trained at schools like Georgia Tech.
- Governor Brian Kemp visited the Kia plant in West Point and said, "We are working to become the e-mobility capital of the nation."
- 97 percent of the nearly 24,000 EV and battery manufacturing jobs announced for the state are in congressional districts represented by Republicans.
- The Clean School Bus program helps shift production away from polluting diesel engines toward cleaner electric buses.
