# Lehigh Technologies Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2013-11-15

**Source:** [Lehigh Technologies Adds Industry Vets To Its Ranks - AftermarketNews](https://www.aftermarketnews.com/lehigh-technologies-adds-industry-vets-to-its-ranks/)  ·  `page_057097`

## Facts

- Lehigh Technologies Inc. announced that Jim MacMaster has joined its board of directors.
- Jim MacMaster is the former executive vice president and chief operating officer at Yokohama Tire Corp. North America.
- Lehigh Technologies appointed Adel Halasa, a polymer scientist formerly with Goodyear Tire & Rubber Co., to its advisory board.
- The appointments coincide with Lehigh’s growth and its continued drive to adopt micronized rubber powder (MRP) material in high-value applications.
- Lehigh Technologies is expanding its presence in Asia and Europe.
- CEO Alan Barton stated that MacMaster's experience in key market segments will help deliver the compelling value proposition of MRP to the tire industry.
- MacMaster began his career with Yokohama in 1981 in sales and rose through multiple commercial roles to become vice president of the company’s North American Commercial Tire Division.
- In 2001, MacMaster was named executive vice president at Yokohama.
- As a new advisory board member, Halasa will provide guidance to Lehigh’s technical team.
- Halasa served as a research and development fellow at Goodyear from 1983 to 2009.
- Halasa has since served as an adjunct professor at the University of Akron.
- Halasa has been listed on more than 300 patents.
- Halasa was recognized in 1997 with the Charles Goodyear Medal for his work developing anionic solution polymerization of styrene-butadiene rubber (SBR).
- Tom Rosenmayer, vice president of technology at Lehigh Technologies, stated the company's technology roadmap for the tire industry focuses on driving higher loading levels of MRP and developing MRPs with chemical reactivity.
- Halasa’s depth of experience in polymers and structure-property relationships will help Lehigh develop new products faster, which is a key part of the company's growth strategy.
