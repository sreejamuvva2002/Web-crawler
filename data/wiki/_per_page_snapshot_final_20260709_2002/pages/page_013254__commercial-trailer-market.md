# Commercial Trailer Market

**Type:** unknown  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2011-09-05

**Source:** [July Trailer Orders at 13,200, Down for 4th Month in a Row - TT](https://www.ttnews.com/articles/july-trailer-orders-13200-down-4th-month-row)  ·  `page_013254`

## Facts

- Truck fleets ordered 13,200 new commercial trailers during July.
- July orders represented a 20% increase over July 2010 but marked the fourth straight month of declining figures.
- Trailer manufacturers attributed the declines to a typical summer slowdown.
- Two officials reported seeing a rebound in preliminary August orders.
- Glenn Harney, chief sales officer of Hyundai Translead in San Diego, stated that their July orders mirrored ACT Research's data but noted an exceptionally strong August.
- Harney noted that fleets are still replacing aging trailers and getting plans together for 2012.
- Through July, there were 132,300 net trailer orders this year compared to 82,900 in the first seven months of 2010.
- Monthly order totals remained above 20,000 through March, peaking at 28,705 before declining to 19,579 in April, 13,600 in May, and 13,428 in June.
- Frank Maly, ACT’s director of commercial vehicle transportation and analysis, reported that net orders were up 60% year-over-year through July.
- The existing trailer backlog was almost double that of a year ago according to ACT.
- The 2011 build backlog stood at 93,600 at the end of July, up 97% from the July 2010 backlog of 47,400.
- Charles Mudd, president of Vanguard National Trailer Corp. in Monon, Ind., stated there was no drop in activity in July and that they had started accepting orders for the first quarter of next year.
- Vanguard’s factory was maintaining maximum capacity.
- Mudd noted that many big fleets have not yet bought new trailers and does not detect stronger growth for 2012 than currently, but enough to support current manufacturing levels.
- Craig Bennett, senior vice president of sales and marketing for Utility Trailer Manufacturing Co. in the City of Industry, Calif., said new orders slowed during July like a normal summer slowdown but remained fairly strong overall.
- Utility’s backlog returned to 2011 beginning-of-the-year levels, averaging around three-and-a-half months.
- ACT’s Maly explained that total new orders were exactly the same as June, with the difference coming from a 2% cancellation rate below normal.
- Chris Hammond, vice president of dealer sales at Great Dane Trailers in Savannah, Ga., agreed with the summer lull assessment but expressed uncertainty about the second half of the year.
- Hammond stated that trailer fleet averages are over eight years old, which will continue to drive the trailer business into 2012.
- Dry-van sales led the early-year surge, but flatbed and reefer sales are now bursting at the seams according to Hammond.
- Hammond noted economic uncertainty could impact the third and fourth quarters.
- David Giesen, vice president of sales and marketing at Stoughton Trailers in Stoughton, Wis., reported customers are buying new trailers to replace old ones rather than expand fleets.
- Giesen stated that more replacement is needed and growth will continue even if fleets do not expand.
- June and July order levels were typical of summer slowdowns, with Stoughton busy building orders from earlier in the year.
- Some potential customers were apprehensive about the slow economy, but August saw an uptick in the latter part of the month.
- Hyundai’s Harney stated the company just started planning production expansion after being sold out for the year.
- Hyundai’s trailer plant is located in Tijuana, Mexico.
- In 2009, new trailer orders totaled 81,300, the lowest since 1975.
- In 2010, orders quickened in the second half of the year, rising from about 11,000 in July to 21,314 in November.
