## Facts

- Georgia governor’s office announced February 4 that Dongwon Autopart Technology Co. will build a new auto parts factory near Swainsboro, Georgia, in Emanuel County.
- The Korean auto parts manufacturer will spend $30 million on the plant and create 200 new jobs in the coming years.
- Hiring for the site is expected to begin this year, but no timeline was given for when the plant would become optional.
- The new factory will expand Dongwon’s auto components business, which produces door frames, seat frames, and battery frames for automotive OEMs.
- Dongwon’s new factory will add capacity alongside the company’s existing site in Hogansville, Georgia.
- The project adds to the company’s existing Southeastern workforce of about 500 employees.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development’s Global Commerce team helped secure the factory.
- The Emanuel County Development Authority helped secure the factory.
- The Georgia Ports Authority helped secure the factory.
- Georgia Power helped secure the factory.
- Georgia Quick Start helped secure the factory.
- Heather Hollstein, Human Resources Manager at Dongwon Autopart Technology, stated the company has been in the United States since 2003 and is proud to be part of growing the state’s automotive industry.
- Heather Hollstein thanked Emanuel County for welcoming the company and affirmed commitment to supplying internal and external customers with high-quality products and service.

- Governor Brian Kemp expressed excitement for the project to bring new jobs to Emanuel County and impact the community for years to come.
