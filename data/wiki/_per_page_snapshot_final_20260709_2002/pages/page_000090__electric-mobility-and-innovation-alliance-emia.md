# Electric Mobility and Innovation Alliance (EMIA)

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2021-07-20

**Source:** [Gov. Kemp Announces Statewide Initiative to Accelerate Georgia’s Electric Mobility Industry | Governor Brian P. Kemp Office of the Governor](https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases/2021-07-20/gov-kemp-announces-statewide-initiative-accelerate-georgias-electric)  ·  `page_000090`

## Facts

- On July 20, 2021, Governor Brian P. Kemp announced a statewide initiative to strengthen Georgia's position as a national leader in the electric mobility industry.
- The Electric Mobility and Innovation Alliance (EMIA) is driven by the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD).
- EMIA includes partners from the private and public sectors ranging from government, industry, electric utilities, education, nonprofits, and other stakeholders.
- The initiative focuses on advancing Georgia’s role in developing the electric mobility industry and its supply chain.
- In conjunction with the initiative, GDEcD launched new website assets for the program.
- Governor Kemp stated that Georgia has a proven record of investing early in the resources and infrastructure needed to connect it to the world and develop jobs of the future.
- Governor Kemp noted that the Electric Mobility and Innovation Alliance will ensure the state is positioned to continue leading the nation in the rapidly growing electric mobility industry.
- The announcement cited nearly $2.6 billion in SK Innovation electric vehicle (EV) battery manufacturing facilities located in Jackson County as an existing investment.
- Georgia has attracted numerous recent international investments, including Dutch e-mobility charging systems leader Heliox.
- Georgia has attracted Turkish EV-parts manufacturer TEKLAS as a recent international investment.
- Georgia has attracted German-owned lightweight automotive-body parts manufacturer GEDIA as a recent international investment.
- Georgia has attracted SK-supplier EnChem of Korea as a recent international investment.
- In 2018, Georgia Made school bus manufacturer Blue Bird introduced their all-electric buses.
- Blue Bird recently reported its 500th delivery or sale of electric buses.
- The Electric Mobility and Innovation Alliance will offer policy recommendations intended to further enhance the state’s attractiveness to the electric mobility industry.
- EMIA aims to foster innovation in related fields including drones, autonomous vehicles, connected vehicles, battery technology, and more.
- EMIA consists of five committees that will develop a multi-step approach setting objectives for Supply Chain, Infrastructure, Workforce, Innovation, and Policy/Initiatives.
- The Carl Vinson Institute at the University of Georgia will host EMIA’s committee meetings and integrated research forums.
- According to a report by the Alliance for Automotive Innovation, automakers are planning to invest $250 billion in electrification by 2023.
- IHS Markit predicts there will be 130 EV models available in the U.S. by 2026.
- Georgia is positioned as the center of the southeast automotive corridor with close proximity to the region’s automotive original equipment manufacturers.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that expanding on Georgia’s growing e-mobility ecosystem, EMIA will give Georgia a greater, more comprehensive strategy for the wave of electrification across industry sectors.
- Commissioner Wilson noted that by bringing together industry leaders and public and private sector partners, they are proactively developing a roadmap to keep Georgia at the forefront of electric mobility and innovation.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) serves as the state’s sales and marketing arm and the lead agency for attracting new business investment.
- GDEcD's responsibilities include encouraging the expansion of existing industry and small businesses, locating new markets for Georgia products, attracting tourists, promoting the state for arts and entertainment projects, and planning and mobilizing state resources for economic development.
