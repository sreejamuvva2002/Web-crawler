# Ecoplastic Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-01-05

**Source:** [Metaplant America supplier to build manufacturing facility in Bulloch Co.](https://www.wtoc.com/2023/01/05/metaplant-america-supplier-build-manufacturing-facility-bulloch-co/)  ·  `page_019861`

## Facts

- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp announced that a manufacturing facility will be built in Bulloch County to help provide supplies to the new Hyundai plant in Bryan County.
- Automotive parts manufacturer Ecoplastic Corporation will create 465 new jobs.
- The company will invest over $205 million in the facility.
- The company will produce auto parts like bumpers, consoles, trim and molding.
- The facility will be located on Highway 301 in south Register, near Statesboro.
- The facility is expected to begin operations by Oct. 2024.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated the company will serve the Hyundai Metaplant well from Statesboro due to its close proximity to Georgia's ports system.
- Ecoplastic Corporation is the second Hyundai supplier to choose Bulloch County for their production facility.
- County development leaders have been working with different supply companies since soon after Hyundai’s announcement in May.
- Benjy Thompson of the Development Authority of Bulloch County stated that the location is close enough to the mega site to be a good place for suppliers to locate, but far enough away to be attractive to them.
- Developers have worked with the company for months to find them the right place, including both the land and the community to support them.
- Billy Allen of the Development Authority of Bulloch County stated they have all the right components to attract companies and expect the same success with employees.
- The announcement brings the number of supply jobs coming to Bulloch County to more than 1,000 so far.
- More than 1,400 new industrial jobs have been created in Bulloch County in the past 11 months.
