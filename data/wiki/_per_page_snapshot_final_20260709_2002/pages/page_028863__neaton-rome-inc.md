# Neaton Rome Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [U.S. Department of Labor Investigation Results in Auto Parts Manufacturer Reinstating, Paying Employee for Family and Medical Leave Act Violations | U.S. Department of Labor](https://www.dol.gov/newsroom/releases/whd/whd20190912)  ·  `page_028863`

## Facts

- Neaton Rome Inc. is an automobile parts manufacturer based in Rome, Georgia.
- A U.S. Department of Labor's Wage and Hour Division (WHD) investigation found that Neaton Rome terminated an employee in violation of the Family and Medical Leave Act (FMLA).
- The employer failed to provide required notification about whether the employee's absence would qualify for leave after she requested time off for an FMLA-qualifying health condition and provided supporting documentation.
- The employer erroneously failed to designate the leave as FMLA-protected before terminating the employee wrongfully after assessing disciplinary attendance points for missed work.
- Neaton Rome Inc. will reinstate the employee and pay her $13,133 in lost wages.
- The company will reinstate the employee's health benefits dating back to the day of termination.
- The employer will grant the employee 160 hours of vacation time she would have earned on her work anniversary date.
- The company will increase the employee's hourly wage to reflect a raise she would have received on her anniversary.
- Neaton Rome Inc. will remove disciplinary attendance points erroneously assessed due to the employee's FMLA-covered absence.
- Wage and Hour Division District Director Eric Williams stated that many Americans rely on the Family and Medical Leave Act for critically needed workplace flexibility.
- Eric Williams noted that the U.S. Department of Labor is committed to ensuring employees' rights under the FMLA are protected and encouraged employers to review tools and resources regarding their rights and obligations under the law.
- WHD's mission is to promote and achieve compliance with labor standards to protect and enhance the welfare of the Nation's workforce.
- WHD enforces Federal minimum wage, overtime pay, recordkeeping, and child labor requirements of the Fair Labor Standards Act.
- WHD also enforces the Migrant and Seasonal Agricultural Worker Protection Act, the Employee Polygraph Protection Act, the Family and Medical Leave Act, wage garnishment provisions of the Consumer Credit Protection Act, and employment standards in immigration statutes.
- Additionally, WHD administers and enforces prevailing wage requirements of the Davis Bacon Act and the Service Contract Act for Federal construction and goods/services contracts.
- The Department of Labor's mission is to foster, promote, and develop the welfare of wage earners, job seekers, and retirees; improve working conditions; advance opportunities for profitable employment; and assure work-related benefits and rights.
