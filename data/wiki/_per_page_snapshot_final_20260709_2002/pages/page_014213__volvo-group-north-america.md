# Volvo Group North America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Volvo Laying Off 550-800 Employees At Plants In MD, PA, VA | Daily Voice](https://dailyvoice.com/virginia/arlington/volvo-slashing-up-to-350-virginia-jobs-as-trumps-tariffs-low-demand-rattle-automakers/)  ·  `page_014213`

## Facts

- Volvo Group North America will lay off 550 to 800 employees, a company spokesperson confirmed on Friday, April 18.
- The layoffs will occur at plants in Maryland, Pennsylvania, and Virginia.
- The Swedish automaker cited the need to adjust to worsening economic conditions.
- A spokesperson stated that heavy-duty truck orders continue to be negatively affected by market uncertainty about freight rates and demand, possible regulatory changes, and the impact of tariffs.
- Between 250 and 350 employees will lose their jobs at Mack Trucks Lehigh Valley Operations in Macungie, PA.
- The Macungie, PA facility employs about 3,050 workers.
- Another 250 to 350 jobs are being cut at Volvo Trucks New River Valley Operations in Dublin, VA.
- The Dublin, VA plant has a workforce of around 3,400.
- A smaller round of 50 to 100 layoffs is hitting Volvo Group Powertrain Operations in Hagerstown, MD.
- About 1,800 people work at the Hagerstown, MD facility.
- Volvo's layoffs come amid rising fears about the state of auto manufacturing under Trump's sweeping tariffs.
- Trump has imposed 25% duties on imported cars, light trucks, aluminum, and steel, along with a 145% tariff on Chinese goods.
- Vehicle data company Cox Automotive warned that panic buying has dramatically lowered the US supply of new and used vehicles.
- New-vehicle stock fell to 2.69 million units as of Tuesday, April 1, down 10.2% from the start of March.
- Volvo said the layoffs were fueled by fewer purchases of its cars and trucks.
- A spokesperson stated: "We regret having to take this action, but we need to align production with reduced demand for our vehicles."
- Other automakers have cut North American jobs since Trump's tariff announcement.
- Stellantis has temporarily laid off about 900 US workers while halting production at some Canadian and Mexican plants.
- General Motors said about 200 temporary layoffs have happened at an all-electric vehicle plant in Detroit to align with market dynamics.
- Another GM plant in Ontario will pause manufacturing, with a union leader saying there will be an indefinite layoff of nearly 500 workers once production resumes.
- Volvo's website states it employs more than 19,600 workers in North America.
