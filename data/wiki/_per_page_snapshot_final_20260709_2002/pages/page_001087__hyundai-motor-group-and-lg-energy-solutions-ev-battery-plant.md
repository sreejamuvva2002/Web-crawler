# Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solutions EV Battery Plant Joint Venture

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hyundai, LG Partner To Build $4.3 Billion EV Battery Plant In Georgia: What To Know](https://www.ibtimes.com.au/hyundai-lg-partner-build-43-billion-ev-battery-plant-georgia-what-know-1845715)  ·  `page_001087`

## Facts

- South Korea's Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solutions announced plans late Thursday to spend $4.3 billion in building an electric vehicle (EV) battery plant in southeast Georgia.
- The two companies are making efforts to comply with President Joe Biden's clean energy tax law.
- Currently, no Hyundai electric vehicles are eligible for the tax credit.
- Both companies signed a memorandum of understanding during a ceremony at LG's headquarters in Seoul.
- Both companies will 50/50 jointly invest in the EV battery plant to be set up in Bryan County, south of Savannah.
- Jaehoon Chang, president and chief executive of Hyundai Motor Company, stated that Hyundai Motor Group is focusing on its electrification efforts to secure a leadership position in the global auto industry.
- The plant will be built adjacent to Hyundai's "Metaplant" manufacturing facility in Georgia.
- In May 2022, Hyundai announced plans to invest $5.5 billion in a massive EV plant in Ellabell, west of Savannah.
- Garrison Douglas, a spokesperson for Republican Gov. Brian Kemp, stated the new plant will foster 3,000 jobs as part of the previously estimated 8,100 roles in the state by the end of 2025.
- The Metaplant facility will produce Hyundai, Genesis and Kia electric vehicles.
- The Hyundai/LG plant is likely to break ground later this year.
- The Hyundai/LG plant is set to begin battery production toward the end of 2025.
- The plant will work toward supplying batteries for 300,000 electric vehicles annually.
- Battery supply capacity will later be expanded to support 500,000 electric vehicles.
- Gov. Brian Kemp stated the project is a milestone in Georgia's path to becoming the EV capital of the nation.
- The announcement comes amid an ongoing rush of electric vehicle and battery makers across the country due to the U.S. Inflation Reduction Act.
- Under the IRA, EV makers can qualify for a $7,500 tax credit if they assemble vehicles in North America or meet a certain percentage of battery parts and minerals from North America or a U.S. free trade partner.
- As of now, no Hyundai electric vehicles, along with its Kia and Genesis models, are eligible for the tax credit because they don't meet domestic manufacturing requirements.
- The state of Georgia pledged $1.8 billion in tax breaks and other incentives for an automobile plant, representing the largest subsidy package offered by a U.S. state.
- Hyundai is the world's third-largest automaker by vehicle sales.
- Hyundai is already assembling electric vehicles at its plant in Montgomery, Alabama.
- Hyundai announced it would start assembling its electric Kia EV9 large SUV at the Kia plant in West Point, Georgia.
- Last month, Hyundai announced a partnership with SK On to build a huge plant in Cartersville, Bartow County.
- The Hyundai/SK On partnership involves a $5 billion investment to create 3,500 jobs.
- Through the joint venture, both parties plan to establish an EV battery cell plant in Bartow County, Georgia, close to the Group's U.S. production facilities including Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Alabama, Kia Georgia and Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- The new plant is expected to start manufacturing battery cells in the second half of 2025 with an annual production capacity of 35 GWh.
- The 35 GWh capacity is sufficient to support the production of 300,000 EVs.
- LG noted it would be its seventh battery plant in operation or under construction in the U.S.
- LG's focus is on expanding EV production in the country.
- LGES CEO Youngsoo Kwon stated that two strong leaders in the auto and battery industries have joined hands to drive the EV transition in America.
