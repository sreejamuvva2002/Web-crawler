# Freyr Battery

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-02-10

**Source:** [Freyr cancels $2.6 billion gigafactory in Georgia, sells site - Energy Storage](https://www.ess-news.com/2025/02/10/freyr-cancels-2-6-billion-gigafactory-in-georgia-sells-site/)  ·  `page_001609`

## Facts

- Freyr Battery decided to abandon its plans for a $2.6 billion lithium-ion battery plant in Coweta County, Georgia.
- The canceled plant would have created more than 700 jobs.
- The cancellation decision was communicated in a letter to county leaders.
- Reasons cited for the withdrawal include rising interest rates, falling battery prices, changes in management, and a realignment of the company’s near-term strategic goals.
- Freyr will now focus on its newly-opened, 5 GW solar cell fab that it bought for $340 million from Trina Solar in November 2024.
- This cancellation marks the second gigafactory plan in the U.S. shelved within a week.
- A couple of days earlier, Kore Power abandoned efforts to build a $1.2 billion battery plant in Buckeye, Arizona.
- The cancellation highlights the difficulty in breaking into a highly-competitive, low-margin business such as battery manufacturing.
- Globally, 2024 saw the highest gigafactory capacity cancellations on record with more than 312 GWh of projects worldwide shelved by the end of November.
- These global cancellations have been primarily from less experienced Tier 3 manufacturers in China.
- Plans for Freyr’s Giga America plant were initially laid down in November 2022.
- The initial plan called for a $1.7 billion investment to build the manufacturing facility with an annual output of 34 GWh.
- At the time of that announcement, the Coweta County project was the second-largest battery factory in planning worldwide.
- By 2029, the projected investment was expected to surpass $2.6 billion, with subsequent expansion of annual manufacturing capacity.
- Giga America was expected to produce lithium-ion cells for both electric vehicles and energy storage applications.
- Construction on the 368-acre (149-hectare) site never began.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development conveyed a $7 million grant to buy a site for Freyr in Newnan.
- Freyr is now expected to repay the $7 million state grant.
- Freyr was founded in 2008 and had an ambitious 200 GWh production target by 2030 across gigafactory projects in Europe and the U.S.
- In 2023, Freyr decided to minimize all investments in its flagship Giga Arctic project in Mo I Rana, Norway, as well as other European projects to focus only on scaling in the U.S.
- To maximize eligibility for U.S. tax benefits of the Inflation Reduction Act (IRA), Freyr moved its corporate headquarters from Norway to Newnan.
- The company announced Austin, Texas, as the location for its new global headquarters to geographically align its workforce with operations and strategy for providing solar modules made in the United States.
- Freyr entered into a definitive agreement to sell its 368-acre site in Coweta County, Georgia, to an undisclosed party for gross sales proceeds of $50 million.
- The site sale transaction is expected to close on February 15, 2024.
- Estimated net proceeds to Freyr from the sale are expected to total $22.5 million following repayment of previously received state and local grants.
