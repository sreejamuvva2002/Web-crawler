# Georgia Department of Economic Development

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2022-03-31

**Source:** [GA: Georgia Economic Development Surpasses 2021 FY Totals, with $13B in Investment, 35K Jobs | Trade and Industry Development](https://www.tradeandindustrydev.com/region/georgia/ga-georgia-economic-development-surpasses-2021-fy-30226)  ·  `page_008247`

## Facts

- Georgia Gov. Brian P. Kemp and the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) announced that through Q3 of FY 2022 (July 1, 2021 – March 31, 2022), job creation and investments from economic development projects surpassed FY 2021 year-end totals.
- GDEcD’s Global Commerce team supported 251 project locations that will result in the creation of more than 35,400 new jobs and $12.9 billion in investment for the state.
- There was a recorded 48% increase in jobs compared to three-quarters through the previous fiscal year and a 6% increase in jobs compared to the prior year’s overall total.
- There was a recorded 54% increase in total investment compared to the same time period last year.
- Nearly 78% of both projects and investments were outside the 10-county metro Atlanta area, and over half of the jobs created were outside that area.
- Key industry sectors supported include automotive, advanced manufacturing, logistics and distribution, software and technology, and food processing.
- Georgia’s electric vehicle (EV) and sustainability ecosystems have grown tremendously over the past year.
- Georgia is continuing to recruit the entire EV supply chain to ensure an effective, efficient, and affordable environment for EV production.
- Rivian plans to develop a $5 billion campus at Stanton Springs North and an electric vehicle service center in Atlanta that will provide training on EVs.
- SK Battery America partnered with American lithium-ion battery recycling and engineered materials startup Ascend Elements to recycle both cell and module lithium-ion battery manufacturing scrap from SK’s Jackson County facility.
- Ascend Elements announced its first commercial-scale battery recycling plant in Covington in January.
- Aurubis will invest $340 million in a ‘first-of-its-kind in the U.S.’ state-of-the-art recycling and secondary smelting facility for multi-metal recycling in Augusta.
- Aspen Aerogels announced plans to locate a new 500,000-square-foot advanced manufacturing facility in Statesboro.
- Aspen Aerogels will produce PyroThin materials that provide thermal management, mechanical stability, and fire protection properties to EV battery systems at its new Statesboro facility.
- Georgia’s No. 1-ranking workforce training program, Georgia Quick Start, helped secure numerous projects including Pepsi Co.’s expansion in DeKalb County, Remington Firearms’ R&D center in LaGrange, and Titan Steel Doors’ expansion in Hart County.
- The Pepsi Co., Remington Firearms, and Titan Steel Doors projects alone represent 1,082 new jobs and $373 million in investment.
- Georgia Quick Start has provided specialized training tailored to business needs for more than 40 years and is administered through the Technical College System of Georgia.
- The Georgia Ports Authority (GPA) deepwater and regional ports projects played a key role in securing diverse projects including Serena & Lily Inc.’s new facility in Effingham County.
- The GPA has been reporting record-breaking numbers for 19 consecutive months as it expedites plans for long-term growth.
- The Savannah Harbor Expansion Project (SHEP) was recently completed, deepening a 40-mile stretch between the port and the Atlantic Ocean to accommodate super-sized freighters from the expanded Panama Canal.
- The Appalachian Regional Port drove Roper Corporation’s decision to invest $118 million in expanding its facilities in Walker County.
- Hartsfield-Jackson International Airport and Georgia’s extensive rail network are assets driving the state’s growth.
- The Georgia Department of Transportation maintains more than 1,200 miles of highway as part of the state’s highway and interstate system, connecting companies to 80% of the country in two days or less of driving time.
- This transportation system was integral to landing Ryder System Inc.’s new facility in Henry County that will create more than 250 new jobs.
