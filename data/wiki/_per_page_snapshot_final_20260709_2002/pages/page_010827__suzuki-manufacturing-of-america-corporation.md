# Suzuki Manufacturing of America Corporation

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-08-05

**Source:** [Suzuki Manufacturing of America now subsidiary of Suzuki Motor](https://powersportsbusiness.com/latest-news/2024/03/06/suzuki-manufacturing-of-america-now-subsidiary-of-suzuki-motor/?share=facebook)  ·  `page_010827`

## Facts

- As of March 1, Suzuki Manufacturing of America Corporation (SMAC) became a 100 percent subsidiary of Suzuki Motor USA, LLC (SMO).
- SMAC was founded in 2001 and is based in Rome, Georgia.
- SMAC serves as Suzuki’s ATV manufacturing facility where all Suzuki adult KingQuad ATVs have been manufactured for over two decades.
- Prior to the change, SMAC stock was owned by Suzuki Motor Corporation (SMC) at 20 percent and Suzuki Motor of America, Inc. (SMAI) at 80 percent.
- SMAI is owned by SMC and acts as the holding company for Suzuki companies operating in the United States.
- The organizational change consolidates ATV sales and production arms to strengthen Suzuki’s overall ATV business in the United States and globally.
- A primary goal of the change is to expand commitment to the growth of Suzuki’s entire powersports business, according to Masami Haga, president of SMO.
- The move aims to build on relationships with Suzuki dealers and SMAC vendor partners.
- Strengthening the supply chain and purchasing policies for locally sourced parts are objectives of the organizational change.
- Integrating production and sales functions is expected to improve responsiveness to the ATV market by more accurately linking production to sales demand.
- SMO aims to improve inventory management for both the company and its dealer body.
- Improved inventory management will help protect sales margins, flooring costs, and profitability for both groups.
- ATV industry sales are generally steady throughout the calendar year with demand typically increasing in the fall months.
- The change enables more direct communication between SMO and SMAC, resulting in improved staff interaction between the two companies.
- Suzuki will create a more collaborative environment between production and sales groups while fostering better connection between Rome-based and Brea-based teams.
- Suzuki’s United States distribution headquarters are located in Brea, California.
- Suzuki relocated its US headquarters to Brea in 1981 after first coming to the United States in 1963.
- Suzuki introduced the first four-wheel ATV in 1982 with the Suzuki LT125, creating an all-new category of off-road recreational and utility vehicles.
- Suzuki currently offers a full line of ATV vehicles with the KingQuad 750AXi as its flagship model.
- SMAC becoming a subsidiary sets the stage for new Suzuki ATV offerings and enhancements to existing products in the future.
