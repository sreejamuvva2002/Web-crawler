# Hyundai Mobis

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** thermal_management  ·  **Published:** 2024-12-22

**Source:** [Hyundai Mobis tackles EV battery overheating with ‘pulsating heat pipes’](https://www.yahoo.com/news/hyundai-mobis-tackles-ev-battery-150753239.html)  ·  `page_021336`

## Facts

- South Korea-based automotive component supplier Hyundai Mobis has introduced new battery cooling technology using pulsating heat pipes to prevent EV batteries from overheating during ultra-fast charging.
- The new insulation material is known as a pulsating heat pipe (PHP) and is made of aluminum alloy and coolant.
- Heat pipes are also used in devices like computer CPUs and smartphones, where the metal tube-shaped thermal conductors improve heat transfer efficiency and serve as high-heat-dissipation materials.
- Battery systems (BSA) are usually made by adding battery management systems (BMS), cooling fans, and other electronics to multiple battery modules (BMA).
- The BMA generates electrical energy and consists of stacked battery cells, making it essential to optimize the cooling structure to prevent overheating.
- Hyundai Mobis's system differs from traditional thermal management by placing pulsating heat pipes between battery cells to rapidly transfer heat to cooling blocks.
- This placement ensures stable temperature control at the module level and is expected to significantly reduce electric vehicle charging time by effectively withstanding heat during ultra-fast charging.
- Pulsating heat pipes work by distributing heat through the vibration and circulation of coolant, maintaining efficiency with minimal gravity impact even in fast-moving vehicles.
- The PHPs have over ten times the heat transfer capacity of standard aluminum and swiftly dissipate heat from overheated battery cells to the vehicle's exterior.
- Hyundai Mobis will employ a molding process to build the system, enabling large-scale continuous production and streamlining the PHP manufacturing process.
- The new molding process aims to lower production costs for the thermal management system.
- To ease installation on vehicle batteries, the company produced PHPs with a thickness of just 0.8 mm.
- The 0.8 mm PHPs are significantly thinner than standard heat pipes, which are around 6 mm thick, offering a larger surface area to support wider EV adoption.
- The company states its approach directly addresses two key consumer concerns: charging time and safety, both crucial for widespread electric vehicle adoption.
- Hyundai Mobis is also known for producing various components for electrification, brakes, chassis and suspension, steering, airbags, lighting, and automotive electronics.
- Last year, Hyundai Mobis introduced its e-Corner System, which allows all four wheels to turn 90 degrees.
- The e-Corner System helps cars perform parallel parking in tight spaces by rotating front wheels inward and rear wheels outward.
- The e-Corner System supports a 360-degree idle turn and a 180-degree maneuver to reverse from a dead end, offering improved maneuverability for parking and navigating narrow areas.
