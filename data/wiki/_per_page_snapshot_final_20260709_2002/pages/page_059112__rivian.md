# Rivian

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-07-06

**Source:** [Rivian Expands Georgia EV Plant Plans With DOE Loan and Volkswagen Backing](https://evmagz.com/rivian-expands-georgia-ev-plant-plans-with-doe-loan-and-volkswagen-backing/)  ·  `page_059112`

## Facts

- Rivian is expanding plans for its second U.S. production facility in Georgia, increasing the site’s planned annual production capacity to 300,000 vehicles from an earlier target of 200,000.
- The California-based company partners with Volkswagen and currently operates a manufacturing plant in Normal, Illinois that produces the EDV electric delivery van, R1 models, and the recently introduced R2 midsized electric SUV.
- The Illinois facility has a total annual production capacity of 215,000 vehicles.
- Rivian broke ground on the Georgia site near Atlanta in September, with early work focused on infrastructure and civil engineering rather than building construction.
- A project delay enabled Rivian to redesign the Georgia facility for larger-scale production.
- The expansion is supported by a $4.5 billion loan package from the U.S. Department of Energy, consisting of $4.006 billion in financing and $494 million in capitalized interest to be repaid after construction.
- RJ Scaringe, founder and CEO of Rivian, stated excitement about partnering with the DOE to grow Rivian's manufacturing footprint in Georgia.
- Scaringe noted that the R2 model dramatically expands market opportunity and emphasized that thousands of workers at the Georgia plant will be instrumental to scaling American manufacturing and maintaining U.S. leadership in innovation and technology.
- The Georgia facility is expected to produce the R2 midsized electric SUV and the upcoming R3 compact SUV, which Rivian positions as a more affordable offering.
- Rivian plans to manufacture 50,000 robotaxis for ride-hailing partner Uber at the facility.
- Production at the Georgia site is scheduled to begin by the end of 2028.
- Rivian reported first-quarter 2026 financial results showing 11% revenue growth to $1.38 billion, based on the production of 10,236 vehicles and 10,365 deliveries.
- Automotive revenue declined 2% to $908 million, attributed by the company to lower sales of regulatory credits and a larger share of commercial vehicle deliveries.
- Vehicle deliveries rose 20% year-on-year.
- The software and services segment grew 49% to $473 million, driven largely by the development partnership with Volkswagen.
- Following successful winter testing of a zonal vehicle architecture platform, Volkswagen invested an additional $1 billion in Rivian via equity, increasing its stake from 12.3% to approximately 16%.
- The increased ownership likely makes Volkswagen Rivian's largest shareholder, surpassing Amazon, another key strategic partner.
- Rivian reported a quarterly operating loss of $881 million (compared to $655 million a year earlier), citing lower gross margins and higher operating costs.
- Net loss narrowed 23% year-on-year to $416 million.
