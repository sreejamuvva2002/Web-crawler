# Robert Bosch GmbH

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2024-01-01

**Source:** [Mobility in the software age: Bosch to realign its automotive-supply business for further growth - Bosch Media Service](https://www.bosch-presse.de/pressportal/de/en/mobility-in-the-software-age-bosch-to-realign-its-automotive-supply-business-for-further-growth-253638.html)  ·  `page_049287`

## Facts

- Bosch sees itself as a mobility software company and is changing its organizational structure to reflect customer requirements and open up further growth.
- Dr. Markus Heyn is a member of the Bosch board of management and the future chairman of the five-strong Bosch Mobility sector board.
- Some of the business sector's individual divisions will be redrawn effective January 1, 2024.
- All divisions will be given horizontal, cross-divisional responsibilities.
- Bosch aims for its mobility solutions to generate sales revenue of more than 80 billion euros worldwide by 2029.
- The market for automotive software is driven by the trend toward software-defined vehicles and is likely to reach a volume of significantly more than 200 billion euros by 2030.
- The projected 2030 automotive software market volume will be three times more than in 2020.
- Bosch's current growth in the automotive software market is in double digits.
- The entire automotive industry is putting a stronger focus on software development, with its share in development cost expected to be roughly 30 percent by 2030.
- At Bosch, the share of software in development cost is already higher than the 30 percent industry projection.
- More than 50 percent of all R&D associates working for Bosch Mobility are software engineers.
- Software-defined vehicles offer speed of development and decoupling of software and hardware development as outstanding advantages.
- Implementing new functions in existing systems will take days instead of years due to software-defined vehicle architecture.
- Cars will feel like new for longer thanks to continuous software updates.
- Drivers across the globe want to seamlessly integrate their vehicles into their digital world, similar to smartphones.
- From 2025, Bosch expects that software-defined vehicles will be introduced on a broad scale.
- Vehicle motion management serves as an example of how reorganization and intensified cross-divisional collaboration support new automotive engineering trends.
- A new control concept stabilizes vehicles faster and more accurately by intervening in the braking system, electrical powertrain, and electric steering system.
- The new control system helps shorten braking distance and requires considerably less corrective action from the driver.
- The technical basis for the new control system is ESP10, the latest generation of the Bosch braking control system, and the Vehicle Dynamics Control 2.0 software function.
- The new control system can be integrated in the central vehicle computer or available as a standalone software package instead of forming part of the ESP control unit.
- Bosch anticipates a threefold increase in software applications and a tenfold increase in applications that access the cloud.
- Software-defined vehicles require a new, centralized electrical and electronic (E/E) architecture.
- More than 100 control units are installed in the latest premium-class vehicles.
- Compact-class vehicles feature between 30 and 50 control units.
- Bosch's focus is on making the complexity of electronic systems controllable and as reliable as possible.
- The number of control units will be significantly reduced by using powerful computers for various vehicle domains such as cockpit and connectivity functions, driver assistance systems, automated driving, and powertrain.
- Bosch is developing a uniform IT architecture for the entire vehicle, spanning from the cloud to the central vehicle computer to individual control units.
- Bosch pursues a technology-neutral approach with fuel cells, batteries, and hydrogen engines while operating equally in software and hardware domains.
- Bosch develops and manufactures key components of modern vehicles under one roof.
- Bosch produces more than 250 million control units each year, which are configured with the company's proprietary software.
- Bosch specializes in integrating software from diverse sources and resulting from collaboration between the automotive and IT industries.
- The Bosch Mobility sector board defined the business sector's new configuration in consultation with associates and executives from individual divisions.
- The reorganization is subject to observance of employee representatives' participatory rights.
- The business sector will comprise seven divisions following the realignment.
- The Electrified Motion division will cover everything relating to electric motors, from the Bosch e-axle to seat adjusters.
- The Vehicle Motion division will deal with vehicle dynamics, from ABS and ESP to steering.
- The Power Solutions division will handle combustion-engine technology, mobile and stationary fuel cells, electrolyzers, and hydrogen engines.
- Cross-Domain Computing Solutions will develop solutions for areas ranging from automatic parking to automated driving.
- Mobility Electronics will drive forward the development of control units and promote in-house semiconductor activities at Bosch.
- Mobility Aftermarket will deal with the spare-parts market and the Bosch Car Service workshop franchise.
- E-Bike Systems will supply systems solutions comprising drive units, rechargeable batteries, ABS, and connected displays for e-bikes.
- The Bosch subsidiary ETAS will be given horizontal responsibility for hardware-agnostic software for operating systems and engineering tools.
