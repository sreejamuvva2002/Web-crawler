# Panasonic Automotive Systems Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [New Generation Wireless Smartphone Charger Developed for Toyota Motor Corporation New Business Expansion for Existing Vehicles | Panasonic Automotive Systems Co., Ltd.](https://automotive.panasonic.com/en/newsroom/wireless-charger)  ·  `page_047956`

## Facts

- In June 2023, Panasonic Automotive Systems’ new generation wireless charger was installed as standard equipment in all grades of Toyota’s fully remodeled Alphard/Vellfire.
- The charger provides highly efficient charging performance using a proprietary moving coil method and algorithms that support smartphones of various shapes and sizes.
- Maximum charging output increased by 1.5 times from the previous 10 W to 15 W for products compliant with Ver. 1.3.2 of the Qi standard.
- The charger is compatible with iPhone 7.5 W charging and Galaxy fast charging specifications (10 W).
- A unique safety function stops charging if a foreign object between the charger and smartphone generates heat.
- The chargeable range has been expanded by 3.6 times compared to the previous model to ensure highly efficient charging even if the smartphone is misaligned.
- Panasonic Automotive made aggressive technical appeals to Toyota’s development managers to highlight its Moving Coil technology, which initially was not recognized by Toyota.
- Following initial standard installation in the Alphard/Vellfire, the charger's installation rate expanded to other Toyota models.
- Sales increased through the KINTO FACTORY service, a vehicle update service for cars already on the road, in cooperation with Toyota-affiliated KINTO Corporation.
- As of May 2024, the charger is available as a retrofit option for a total of five Toyota- and LEXUS-brand models.
- Panasonic participated in an offline meeting of LEXUS and RAV4 users organized by an SNS influencer to raise product awareness and demonstrate its capabilities.
- End-user feedback highlighted that wireless charging often fails when phones are out of position, which the moving coil technology successfully addresses during demonstrations.
- Demonstrations at KINTO FACTORY booths effectively attracted customers and generated more orders than expected for LEXUS NX since the service launch.
- Future activities will focus on increasing orders at KINTO FACTORY, updating existing vehicles, and expanding factory-installed options in Japan and overseas.
- Panasonic plans to actively participate in offline meetings to allow end-users to test the product with their own smartphones and increase interest.
- User feedback will be reflected in future product planning and leveraged in proposals to car manufacturers.
- The engineering team aims to evolve charging algorithms to achieve higher efficiency and keep pace with smartphone evolution.
