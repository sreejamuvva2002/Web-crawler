# Pat Wilson

**Type:** person  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2022-12-31

**Source:** [2023 Georgian of the Year: Successfully Selling the State - Georgia Trend Magazine](https://www.georgiatrend.com/2022/12/31/2023-georgian-of-the-year-successfully-selling-the-state/)  ·  `page_017249`

## Facts

- Pat Wilson serves as Commissioner of the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) and was named the 2023 Georgian of the Year.
- Wilson previously served as director of government affairs for Gov. Sonny Perdue for three years starting in 2010, promoting the legislative agenda of the first Republican elected governor in Georgia since Reconstruction.
- Wilson worked for Perdue in D.C. as federal affairs director and previously in the offices of U.S. Rep. Nathan Deal and the late Sen. Paul Coverdell.
- Gov. Perdue encouraged Wilson to join GDEcD, telling him it would be great if he went to sell something he loves – the state of Georgia.
- Wilson joined GDEcD as deputy commissioner for global commerce, later served as chief operating officer from 2011 to 2016, and was appointed commissioner by then-Gov. Nathan Deal.
- Heidi Green, managing director of policy and government relations at Deloitte, was GDEcD commissioner in 2010 and noted Wilson's talent for building relationships and team culture.
- Perdue, now chancellor of the University System of Georgia, credits Wilson with much of Georgia being named one of the best places to grow a business by communicating the governor and legislature's achievements.
- During Wilson's six years leading GDEcD, Georgia has been named the No. 1 state to do business for nine consecutive years.
- Georgia posted more than $21.2 billion in investments and 51,132 new jobs in FY2022.
- Lee Thomas, deputy commissioner for film, music and digital entertainment at GDEcD (and Georgian of the Year in 2019), credits Wilson's institutional knowledge for helping the department.
- Hyundai Motor Group’s new electric vehicle plant is located in Bryan County.
- Rivian’s EV factory is located in Newton and Morgan counties.
- Wilson grew up on his grandfather’s peach farm; his parents still live in Franklin County and his father serves on the City Council of Franklin Springs (population about 1,000).
- In 2010, Wilson pitched a Caterpillar project to Gov. Perdue looking at Griffin for 300 jobs.
- Kia operates a plant in West Point, Georgia, where the state continues to help with workforce needs as the facility expands.
- The relationship leading to Hyundai's Georgia plant traces back to Kia’s first U.S. plant in West Point, announced in 2006.
- SK Battery announced a production facility in Jackson County in 2018, giving Georgia a head start in the EV industry.
- Gov. Brian Kemp took a 2020 trade trip to Stuttgart, Germany, where he met with executives from Mercedes-Benz and Porsche regarding their EV plans.
- Both Mercedes-Benz and Porsche have their North American headquarters in Atlanta.
- After the Stuttgart trip, Gov. Kemp told Wilson's team: “You guys really need to be focused on electrification.”
- The site in Newton and Morgan counties was previously pitched to Toyota-Mazda and Tesla, but both companies chose other locations.
- Georgia was initially in Rivian’s top three for its EV factory but was bumped down the list before being brought back onto the short list.
- The Rivian deal was sealed during a meeting at the proposed site between Rivian CEO RJ Scaringe and Gov. Kemp.
- Hyundai came to GDEcD in January and made its decision by April.
- Hyundai Motor North America president José Muñoz wore a Hyundai backpack to identify himself during early project discussions, later telling Savannah Economic Development Authority president Trip Tollison he did it intentionally to spur the team.
- Hyundai chair Chung Euisun (previously Kia’s president) recognized Georgia's Quick Start workforce education program, stating: “Governor, you don’t have to talk to me about workforce. We know Quick Start. We know the state of Georgia is going to live up to its promises in workforce solutions.”
- GDEcD uses a team approach to recruiting new business, likened to a quarterback bringing in utilities, workforce solutions teams, and community leaders as part of the sales pitch.
- Wilson balances his demanding travel schedule with attending his children’s activities, including baseball games, choral concerts, and symphonies.
- Wilson attended the HOPE Scholarship program during his upbringing in Georgia.
- Wilson thinks the COVID pandemic sped up industry timelines as companies came out of shutdown ready to invest and make up for lost time.
- Wilson flew Delta Air Lines transatlantic flights during the pandemic to build relationships with international companies since they could not visit Georgia.
- Wilson says: “On so many of these projects, you’re playing a long game. The company may choose to not do business with you today, but many times if they had a good experience in Georgia, things come back around.”
- After hosting one prospect, a site consultant noted the team enjoyed dinner together regardless of outcome, highlighting Georgia's special collaborative recruiting approach.
