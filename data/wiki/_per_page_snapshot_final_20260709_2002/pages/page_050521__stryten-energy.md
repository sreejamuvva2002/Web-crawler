# Stryten Energy

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-08-09

**Source:** [Snapping Shoals unveils new battery system demo with Stryten Energy - The Covington News](https://www.covnews.com/news/county/snapping-shoals-unveils-new-battery-system-demo-stryten-energy/)  ·  `page_050521`

## Facts

- On August 2, 2023, Stryten Energy and Snapping Shoals EMC celebrated the installation of a vanadium redox flow battery (VRFB) system.
- A ribbon-cutting ceremony for the installation was held at Snapping Shoals EMC's office on Brown Bridge Road in Newton County, Georgia.
- The installed VRFB system is the first of its kind in Georgia.
- The partnership between Stryten Energy and Snapping Shoals EMC will demonstrate the VRFB’s energy storage capabilities and evaluate other uses for its long-duration technology.
- The battery system promotes clean, renewable energy and holds a capacity of 100 kilowatt hours, which equates to 26,000 AA batteries.
- Ceremony attendees included U.S. Rep. Mike Collins, representatives from the Georgia General Assembly, a representative from Senator Jon Ossoff’s office, Georgia Public Service Commissioner Tim Echols, and leaders from both Stryten Energy and Snapping Shoals EMC.
- Georgia Public Service Commissioner Tim Echols expressed enthusiasm for clean energy, comparing the benefits of the VRFB to bacon.
- Tim Echols stated that devices like the VRFB represent a "very high tech time" in the energy world that improves solar, resiliency, and grid performance.
- Tim Echols quoted the VRFB as being "bacon in a box" that makes solar, resiliency, and the Snapping Shoals grid better.
- The VRFB system is currently operating as a pilot project but will directly impact Snapping Shoals EMC customers.
- The battery is connected to Snapping Shoals EMC's utility grid but has a limited transmission range due to its small demo size.
- Snapping Shoals EMC Vice President of Engineering and Developing Technologies Robbie Young stated that the VRFB's effectiveness will be assessed using monitoring devices that provide energy values for engineer analysis.
- Robbie Young aims to gain a better understanding of the battery through this installation to improve it for future applications.
- Robbie Young expressed hope to learn significantly about the VRFB technology itself.
- Snapping Shoals EMC engineers will monitor the system with the goal of increasing reliability and efficiency to make it cost-effective for larger-scale use.
- Stryten Energy designed the VRFB over an 18-month period focusing on safety, reliability, and recyclability.
- Stryten Energy Vice President of Essential Power Scott Childers expressed hope regarding the project's future and community benefits.
- Scott Childers noted that Snapping Shoals operates in an active daily energy trading market.
- Childers explained that Snapping Shoals' daily buying and selling of energy allows them to test the VRFB's performance and economic viability.
- Scott Childers stated the project has far-reaching impacts ranging from reducing electric bills to driving energy independence and national security.
- Childers described the joint relationship between Stryten Energy and Snapping Shoals EMC as a "one of a kind scenario" that enables solving major energy challenges together.
