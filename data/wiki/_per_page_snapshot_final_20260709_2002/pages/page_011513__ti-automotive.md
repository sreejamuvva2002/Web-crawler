# TI Automotive

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2025-08-07

## Facts

- Canadian automotive parts supplier ABC Technologies recently completed its acquisition of TI Fluid Systems, forming a new global entity known as TI Automotive.
- The newly formed TI Automotive has a combined annual revenue of $5.4 billion.
- The merged company will have a workforce of 34,600 employees across 26 countries around the world.
- Announced in November 2024, the $2.37B CAD deal has now been made official and includes the full share capital of the UK-based TI Fluid Systems.
- The newly merged company, TI Automotive, will be headquartered in Auburn Hills, Michigan.
- TI Automotive will be led by ABC Technologies President and CEO Terry Campbell.
- The firm will operate under a regional operating model that’s been designed to streamline delivery and scale operations across continents.
- The merger signals a strategic consolidation with an aim to expand product offerings and enhance supply chain integration for fluid handling systems.
- Operations will now span 27 countries with a customer base that includes all major automotive manufacturers.
- TI Automotive is positioned to serve a broader range of mobility solutions, especially in EV thermal management, fuel delivery and advanced materials.
- Ownership of the new entity rests primarily with Apollo Global Management, which also holds a significant stake in ABC Technologies.
- Oaktree Capital Management maintains a minority equity interest in TI Automotive.
- The acquisition strengthens Canada’s role in next-generation mobility solutions and reinforces its presence in the global automotive supply chain.
- TI Automotive president and CEO Terry Campbell stated: “The combination creates exciting opportunities to drive innovation and manufacturing excellence, while establishing the foundations for our future growth.”
