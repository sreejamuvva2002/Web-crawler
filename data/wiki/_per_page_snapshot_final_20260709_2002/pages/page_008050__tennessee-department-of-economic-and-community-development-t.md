# Tennessee Department of Economic and Community Development (TNECD)

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Tennessee Tops for FDI from Korea - Area Development](https://www.areadevelopment.com/contributedcontent/q1-2024/tennessee-tops-for-fdi-from-korea.shtml)  ·  `page_008050`

## Facts

- Tennessee is home to nearly 1,050 foreign-based establishments that employ roughly 165,000 people across the state.
- Much of Tennessee's FDI growth is attributed to the Tennessee Department of Economic and Community Development’s (TNECD) well-earned reputation for building relationships, understanding cultures, and solving problems.
- Karel Abboud serves as global director of FDI and trade for TNECD.
- TNECD employs a team of five in-country global FDI directors who recruit across 12 countries in Europe and Asia.
- Since 2016, TNECD has landed nearly 20 projects by Korean-owned companies to create 4,600 new jobs and invest over $4.9 billion in capital.
- South Korea ranks among the top 10 countries for FDI in Tennessee, much of which is invested in automotive operations.
- According to fDi Markets, an online Financial Times product, Tennessee ranks second in the nation for job commitments from Korean companies over the last five years and fifth for capital investment.
- Heejung Shin serves as TNECD’s in-country FDI representative in South Korea.
- Shin states that key factors for building a long-term relationship with Koreans are sincerity, honesty, and consistency.
- Shin's networking extends to lawyers, accountants, architects, and various association members in both South Korea and the U.S. to help companies expand successfully.
- Shin emphasizes that Korean companies prioritize unwavering local-level support, such as permitting assistance during construction and ongoing operational support, over incentives alone.
- Clarksville has become a thriving manufacturing cluster anchored by several South Korean companies including Hankook, LG Electronics, Shinhung Global, Hankook & Company, LG Chem, and Dongwha Electrolyte.
- Hankook Tire located in Clarksville in 2013 and later assisted LG Electronics during its site search in 2015/2016, influencing LGE's decision to locate there as well.
- Wonbae Kim is the COO and senior director of Dongwha Electrolyte.
- Kim praises Shin for her agile approach, prompt follow-up, and commitment to consistency and transparency in client communication.
- Korean companies have a strong presence in Tennessee's manufacturing sectors including automotive, electronics, chemicals, electric vehicles (EV), food, and biotechnology.
- In 2022, LG Chem announced a $3.2 billion cathode materials plant in Montgomery County for EV batteries, creating 1,000 jobs.
- In 2023, Dongwha Electrolyte USA announced a $70 million facility in Clarksville to produce electrolytes for EV lithium batteries, creating 68 jobs.
- In 2023, Enchem America announced a $152 million electrolyte manufacturing plant in Brownsville, creating 190 new jobs.
- In 2023, Hanon Systems announced a $170 million manufacturing facility in Loudon to produce thermal solutions for the EV industry, creating 600 jobs.
- In 2024, Unitech North America announced a $30 million first U.S. facility in Morrison to produce thermal adhesives and battery compression pads for EV batteries, creating 75 jobs.
