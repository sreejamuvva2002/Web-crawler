# Suzuki Manufacturing of America Corp.

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Suzuki Plant Celebrates Milestone | ATV.com](https://www.atv.com/news/suzuki-plant-celebrates-milestone-738.html)  ·  `page_010937`

## Facts

- Suzuki’s ATV manufacturing facility in Rome, Ga. celebrated the production of its 250,000th ATV on March 31.
- The year 2008 marks the 25th anniversary of Suzuki's production of the industry’s first four-wheeled ATV, the 1983 Suzuki LT-125.
- Anniversary events are scheduled throughout the summer to commemorate the landmark.
- Suzuki Manufacturing of America Corp. (SMAC) opened its Rome plant in May of 2002 as the company’s only US-based manufacturing facility to produce the Eiger series of ATVs.
- The facility employs 300 SMAC employees who build ATV frames, mold plastic, and assemble KingQuad 400s, 450s, and 750s.
- More than 200 units are assembled in an eight-hour shift at the Rome plant.
- Nearly 60,000 ATVs were produced at the facility in 2007.
- Factory workers, local dignitaries, media, and the Suzuki factory ATV race team (Team Rockstar/MSR/Suzuki/Yoshimura/Pro Taper) attended a commemorative ceremony.
- SMAC’s ATV sales manager Alan Horne stated that the milestone commemorates the success of Suzuki’s ATV production over the last six years and the cooperation with the community.
- Rome mayor Wright Bagby stated that for the last ten years, local efforts have fought to save manufacturing jobs, and with help from Suzuki employees, they saved jobs and maintained a higher percentage of manufacturing jobs in the community than most places in the South or probably in the country.
- Current AMA ATV Motocross Series points leader Dustin Wimmer rode the historic quad off the production line.
- Team Suzuki personnel, factory staff, mayor Bagby, and other local government officials added their signatures to the red KingQuad 450 AXI 4X4.
- Mel Harris, vice president of American Suzuki’s motorcycle/ATV division, congratulated the city of Rome, Ga., and SMAC employees for believing in the project, noting that producing a quarter of a million ATVs in six years demonstrates Suzuki’s commitment to the ATV market.
