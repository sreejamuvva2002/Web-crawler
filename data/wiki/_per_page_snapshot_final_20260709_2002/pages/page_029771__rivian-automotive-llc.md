# Rivian Automotive, LLC

## Facts

- Rivian determined that certain MY 2022 Rivian EDV motor vehicles do not fully comply with paragraph S4.5.1(b)(5)(i) of FMVSS No. 208, Occupant Crash Protection (49 CFR 571.208).
- Rivian filed a noncompliance report dated October 24, 2022, and amended the report on November 14, 2022, pursuant to 49 CFR part 573.
- Rivian petitioned NHTSA on November 15, 2022, for an exemption from notification and remedy requirements of 49 U.S.C. Chapter 301 based on inconsequential noncompliance regarding motor vehicle safety.
- Notice of receipt of Rivian's petition was published with a 30-day public comment period on May 19, 2023, in the Federal Register (88 FR 32275), and no comments were received.
- Approximately 1,278 MY 2022 Rivian EDV motor vehicles, manufactured between December 10, 2021, and September 27, 2022, were reported by the manufacturer.
- A label displaying the subject vehicle's clearance height is affixed to the same side of the sun visor containing the air bag warning label, causing noncompliance with FMVSS No. 208.
- Paragraph S4.5.1(b)(5)(i) of FMVSS No. 208 requires that except for specific labels, no other information shall appear on the same side of the sun visor to which the sun visor air bag warning label is affixed.
- Rivian states the air bag warning label meets FMVSS No. 208 content requirements and is displayed as intended by the standard.
- Rivian contends the noncompliance is inconsequential because the subject vehicles are exclusively used in a single fleet of delivery vehicles driven by professional drivers.
- The subject vehicles are walk-in vans with only the driver's seat designated, and drivers are required to wear seat belts when operating them.
- Rivian believes the clearance height label does not affect air bag warning label efficacy due to the vehicle's intended usage.
- Rivian contends the labeling requirement aims to mitigate risks for children placed in seating positions equipped with air bags.
- Rivian states there is no expectation of children being present in or around the subject vehicles.
- If a child occupied the driver's seat, the brake transmission shift interlock required by FMVSS No. 114 would prevent operation, so air bags could not deploy.
- Drivers are required to use seat belts at all times, and the air bag warning label is visible, easy to read, and not positioned near the clearance height label.
- Rivian believes NHTSA's rule aims to maximize label effectiveness through consistent and uniform messaging to consumers.
- Rivian quotes NHTSA's decision on a Maserati petition regarding the purpose of FMVSS No. 208 air bag warning labels.
- Rivian contends NHTSA has previously determined that vehicle usage bears upon inconsequentiality, granting exemptions for vocational vehicles where children are unlikely to be in front passenger areas.
- Rivian adds NHTSA granted prior petitions for deviations in advanced air bag warning label language, and the rationale applies here.
- The subject noncompliance has been corrected for vehicles in production.
- Rivian is not aware of any crash, death, injury, field report, or claims related to the subject noncompliance.
- NHTSA focuses on safety risk to individuals experiencing events against which a recall would otherwise protect.
- NHTSA generally does not consider absence of complaints or injuries when determining inconsequentiality.
- Each petition is evaluated on its own facts, and prior determinations are not binding precedent.
- The purpose of the labeling requirement is to reduce adverse effects of air bags by attracting occupants' attention to the warning label.
- Rivian provided evidence that the FMVSS No. 208 required Figure 1 label is present, unobstructed, and within clear view despite the clearance height information.
- The vehicle's clearance height could be frequently used information for delivery van drivers.
- NHTSA determined the noncompliance is inconsequential to motor vehicle safety given the nature and intended use of the vocational vehicles.
- NHTSA agrees it would be unlikely for children to be placed in the front passenger seating area of these vehicles.
- NHTSA finds Rivian met its burden of persuasion that the FMVSS No. 208 noncompliance is inconsequential to motor vehicle safety.
- Rivian's petition is granted, exempting it from notification and free remedy obligations under 49 U.S.C. 30118 and 30120.
- The decision only applies to subject vehicles Rivian no longer controlled at the time of determining noncompliance.
- The petition granting does not relieve distributors and dealers of prohibitions on sale or delivery of noncompliant vehicles under their control after Rivian notified them.
- Otto G. Matheke III is the Director of the Office of Vehicle Safety Compliance who signed the decision.
