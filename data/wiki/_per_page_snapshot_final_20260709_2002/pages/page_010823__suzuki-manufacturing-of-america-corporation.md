## Facts

- Suzuki opened its factory in Rome, Georgia, more than two decades ago.
- The Rome, Georgia facility recently produced its 500,000th model.
- The milestone vehicle was a KingQuad 750AXi, which is Suzuki’s flagship model.
- Masami Haga serves as the president of Suzuki Manufacturing of America Corporation (SMAC).
- Masami Haga stated, “We’re proud of this accomplishment and the significance of having built a half-million ATVs in the United States.”
- North America represents the largest ATV market in the world.
- The company emphasized the importance of manufacturing these products using skilled American workers.
- Suzuki acknowledged and praised its supply chain, specifically noting its suppliers located in the United States.
- Suzuki was the first manufacturer to build a four-wheel ATV in 1982.
- Suzuki currently operates more than 700 franchised dealerships in the US.
- The ATVs produced at the Rome, Georgia facility are sold in almost 25 countries.
- The company plans to display the milestone KingQuad 750AXi at its US headquarters in Brea, California.
- Yamaha Outdoors operates a manufacturing facility in Georgia.

- Polaris and Arctic Cat assemble side-by-sides in Minnesota.
