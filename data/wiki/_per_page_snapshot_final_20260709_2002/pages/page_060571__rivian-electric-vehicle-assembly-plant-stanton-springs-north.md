# Rivian Electric Vehicle Assembly Plant (Stanton Springs North)

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-01-15

**Source:** [Rivian Restarts Site-Prep at Georgia Plant; Vertical Construction Set for 2026](https://constructionreviewonline.com/rivian-chooses-construction-firm-to-build-their-planned-5b-ev-factory-in-georgia/)  ·  `page_060571`

## Facts

- Rivian resumed site-preparation work at its Stanton Springs North site in Social Circle, Ga., in August 2025.
- Site preparation activities include installing utilities and carrying out grading and other preparatory work.
- The company hosted a community event on Sept. 14, 2025.
- Rivian plans an invitation-only ceremonial groundbreaking on Sept. 16, 2025.
- Current on-site activity is focused on site prep and utility installation rather than full structural work.
- Vertical construction, defined as the main structural build including foundations and building shells, is slated to begin in 2026.
- The restart of work follows a pause in construction that occurred in March 2024.
- Local officials and company representatives will attend the Sept. 16 ceremony to highlight expected economic and job benefits for the region.
- The Georgia factory project was first announced in December 2021 when Rivian unveiled plans for a $5 billion electric vehicle assembly plant at Stanton Springs North.
- On Jan. 15, 2025, the U.S. Department of Energy announced a $6.57 billion loan package to Rivian to support construction of an EV manufacturing facility in Georgia.
- Rivian confirmed on Jan. 16, 2025 that it had closed a loan agreement with the DOE for up to $6.6 billion.
- The loan funds will help finance the Stanton Springs plant and expand Rivian's U.S. production capacity.
- Approximately $5.98 billion of the loan covers principal interest, with an additional $592 million covering capitalized interest.
- This funding aligns with the federal government’s strategic push to strengthen the domestic supply chain for clean energy manufacturing and electric vehicles.
- Rivian is among automakers benefiting from the DOE’s Advanced Technology Vehicles Manufacturing loan program.
- Previous recipients of the DOE loan program include Ford Motor Company ($5.9 billion in 2009), Nissan North America ($1.45 billion in 2010), and Tesla Motors ($465 million in 2010).
- Rivian announced on December 19, 2023, that it selected a construction company to build its planned manufacturing factory in Georgia.
- Clayco, a Chicago-based company, was selected to build the facility.
- The factory will be approximately 16 million square feet and sit on an expansive 1,800-acre site spanning Morgan and Walton counties in Georgia.
- The project is expected to create approximately 7,500 direct jobs once manufacturing begins.
- Rivian is headquartered in California and currently operates a factory in Illinois that transforms a former Mitsubishi facility to manufacture its R1T truck and R1S SUV.
- The new custom-built Georgia facility will focus on producing Rivian’s upcoming vehicle lineup, including the R2 crossover model.
- In November 2024, the U.S. Department of Energy issued a conditional commitment for a multi-billion-dollar loan to support the project.
- On Sept. 14, 2025, Rivian hosted a public community celebration at the site that offered vehicle demos and family activities.
- The Sept. 16, 2025 ceremonial groundbreaking included state and local officials and signaled the formal restart of the project.
- Vertical construction of the plant’s main structures is scheduled to begin in early 2026 (Q1 2026), covering foundations, building shells, and major structures.
- Rivian targets a production start at the Georgia facility around 2028, with commissioning and ramp-up expected around that time.
- The fully built plant will have a capacity of up to 400,000 vehicles per year.
