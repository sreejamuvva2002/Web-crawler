# Woory Industrial Co.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-06-07

**Source:** [EV parts supplier to build plant in Georgia - The Augusta Press](https://theaugustapress.com/ev-parts-supplier-to-build-plant-in-georgia/)  ·  `page_015401`

## Facts

- A South Korea-based auto parts maker will build its first U.S. manufacturing plant in Georgia.
- Woory Industrial Co. will invest an estimated $18 million in the facility.
- The facility will be located in Dublin, Georgia.
- The project will create more than 130 jobs.
- The company specializes in heating, ventilation, and air conditioning components for both electric and internal combustion vehicles.
- Pioneering development by Woory has made the company a leader in EV heating technology.
- Unlike internal combustion engines, EVs do not have a heating source, making cabin heaters necessary to maintain battery temperature.
- The company is working to develop other new products for EVs as well as hydrogen-fueled vehicles.
- Gov. Brian Kemp announced the project on Wednesday, June 7, 2023.
- Gov. Brian Kemp stated that Woory Industrial is helping Georgia become the e-mobility capital of the nation.
- Gov. Brian Kemp noted that the emerging industry continues to bring historic levels of new jobs and investment to communities across the state thanks to a highly skilled workforce and reliable infrastructure network.
- Jungwoo Kim, Woory’s CEO, said the automobile industry is facing a revolution for the first time in 100 years.
- Jungwoo Kim stated that the transition to electric vehicles is the future and the U.S. EV market is the key market among all automobile markets.
- The Georgia Department of Economic Development’s Global Commerce team worked with the Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority, Georgia Power, and the Technical College System of Georgia’s Quick Start program to land the project.
- Woory will be hiring for positions from all fields necessary for an automotive parts plant.
- The new plant is expected to begin operations in November.
