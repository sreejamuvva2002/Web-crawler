# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA)

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-02-27

**Source:** [State Leaders Celebrate Hyundai Motor Group's Historic Impact on Georgia - Southeast Regional Center](https://srceb-5.com/state-leaders-celebrate-hyundai-motor-groups-historic-impact-on-georgia/)  ·  `page_009179`

## Facts

- Governor Brian Kemp and First Lady Marty Kemp joined President and Global COO of Hyundai Motor Company and President and CEO of Hyundai and Genesis Motor North America José Muñoz to celebrate Hyundai Motor Group’s historic impact on Georgia and provide an update on Metaplant America progress.
- The facility will officially start vehicle production in the fourth quarter of this year.
- Hyundai has been a partner to Georgia for decades, helping recruit businesses, giving back to communities, and working with world-class colleges and universities.
- Hyundai and Genesis electric vehicles including the IONIQ 5, IONIQ 6, GV60, and Electrified GV70 were displayed outside the State Capitol.
- The hydrogen-powered XCIENT Fuel Cell Truck was displayed and will be used for logistics needs between Hyundai’s manufacturing facilities and suppliers.
- Governor Kemp officially declared February 26 as Hyundai Day throughout the state.
- The Georgia General Assembly passed a Resolution commending Hyundai Motor Group’s dedication to the state and recognizing February 26 as Hyundai Day at the State Capitol.
- Hyundai’s investments in Georgia will create tens of thousands of great paying American jobs and bring tens of billions of dollars in economic impact for decades to come.
- The Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) project was announced in May 2022.
- LG Energy Solution was named as the joint venture partner in the manufacture of battery components.
- An additional expansion of the battery facility was announced alongside the joint venture.
- Hyundai and SK On are building an EV battery manufacturing facility in Bartow County that will supply Hyundai Motor Group’s U.S. plants.
- Hyundai Motor Group’s planned investment in Georgia has reached more than $12.6 billion.
- The planned investment brings with it the creation of 12,000 direct, long-term jobs.
- Lieutenant Governor Burt Jones, House Speaker Jon Burns, legislative leaders, GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson, and officials from the Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority joined the celebration.
- Lt. Governor Burt Jones praised Governor Kemp, state agencies, and the University System of Georgia, especially Georgia Tech, for their partnership with Hyundai.
- Up to 300,000 Hyundai, Genesis, and Kia EVs are expected to be produced annually at the Metaplant.
- 17 HMGMA suppliers have announced projects across 10 Georgia counties spanning beyond southeast Georgia.
- Further supplier announcements are expected.
- Supplier facilities are expected to create more than 6,700 new jobs and more than $2.5 billion in investment.
- Supplier facilities will be operational prior to the opening of HMGMA.
- Speaker Jon Burns credited Governor Kemp and the General Assembly for sound policy direction that enabled Hyundai’s Southeast Georgia investment.
- The Metaplant site is nearly 3,000 acres.
- Four building exteriors are complete at the site with outfitting for internal operations underway.
- The Metaplant’s architecture incorporates technologically advanced elements, design features representing the Savannah area, Hyundai’s brand identity, nature, and clean energy.
- HMGMA is hiring full-time employees known as Meta Pros rapidly for training and skills on machinery, office and clerical work, and numerous other employment roles.
- HMGMA currently has 566 full-time employees.
- Approximately 75 percent of the current workforce are from the Greater Savannah area.
- An additional 37 employees are from Georgia but are outside the local area.
- Those interested in applying to be a Meta Pro can apply online.
- Georgia Quick Start broke ground on its customized training center at the Metaplant site on February 8, 2024.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Georgians will be trained in high-skill careers as Meta Pros at one of the most advanced vehicle assembly and battery plants in the world.
- HMGMA partnered with local technical colleges to prepare students for entry-level employment at the Metaplant.
- Electrical Vehicle Professional Technical Certificate of Credit (EVP TCC) training is available at Coastal Pines Technical College’s Baxley and Waycross campuses, Southeastern Technical College’s Vidalia campus, Savannah Technical College, and Ogeechee Technical College.
- Technical College System of Georgia Commissioner Greg Dozier highlighted the partnership as a commitment to innovation, excellence, and cultivating a highly skilled workforce for the EV industry.
- In September 2023, Georgia Institute of Technology (Georgia Tech) and Hyundai Motor Company signed a memorandum of understanding to collaborate on research and applications for sustainable mobility.
- The Georgia Tech collaboration includes developing a hydrogen economy, workforce development, and making cities smarter and more sustainable.
- U.S. News & World Report ranked Georgia Tech as the top public university and No. 3 nationally in energy and fuel research.
- Georgia Tech President Ángel Cabrera stated the strategic partnership represents one of their most significant corporate relationships to advance science and produce the next-generation workforce.
- Since 1998, Hyundai has directed over $6 million towards local Georgia communities through philanthropic programs and partnerships.
- Hyundai is promoting STEM programs in local K-12 schools and hosting educational programs on alternative energy sources.
- Hyundai supports local organizations dedicated to affordable homeownership and focuses on environmental stewardship.
- Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority’s Trip Tollison noted Hyundai has given more than $1 million to regional nonprofits and formed key partnerships with local educational institutions.
- Trip Tollison stated Hyundai committed to $7.59 billion in capital investment in HMGMA and will create 8,500 jobs.
- More than 550 Meta Pros have already been hired, with 75 percent coming from the Savannah region.
- Beginning with mass production, electricity used to power the Metaplant will come from 100 percent clean power sources.
- The clean power initiative aligns with Hyundai’s commitment to carbon neutrality by 2045.
- A 41-acre park will be featured in front of the plant including trails, running tracks, environmental art displays, sports fields, and amenities for employee use.
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America is Hyundai Motor Group’s first dedicated Electric Vehicle mass-production plant located in Bryan County, Georgia.
- The plant features a highly connected, automated, and flexible manufacturing system that organically connects all elements of the EV ecosystem.
- The facility will operate as an intelligent manufacturing plant optimizing order collection, procurement, logistics, and production utilizing AI and data.
- The innovative manufacturing system creates a human-centered work environment with robots assisting human workers.
- Hyundai Motor Group is a global enterprise with about 250,000 employees worldwide and mobility brands including Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis.
