# La Regina Atlantica

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** workforce_training  ·  **Published:** 2026-01-13

**Source:** [La Regina Agreement Signing - Georgia Quick Start](https://www.georgiaquickstart.org/la-regina-agreement-signing/)  ·  `page_000120`

## Facts

- La Regina Atlantica hosted a training plan signing on January 13, 2026 at 10 a.m. to commemorate its partnership with Georgia Quick Start and Coastal Pines Technical College.
- Over the past six years, the family-owned company has grown its Georgia facility to 103 employees.
- La Regina Atlantica plans to hire up to 50 more employees in the coming years.
- The company manufactures Rao’s homestyle pasta sauce at its manufacturing facility in Alma, Georgia.
- Georgia Quick Start is the state’s top-ranked workforce training program and will provide customized training for the manufacturer.
- La Regina Atlantica announced its Georgia facility in Alma in 2020.
- Scott McMurray, Deputy Commissioner at Georgia Quick Start, stated they are honored to support a company that chose Georgia for its first-ever US production facility.
- The partnership extends all the way from Italy to South Georgia to the shelves of local supermarkets.
- In addition to safety training, Quick Start will provide job-specific training on how to operate specialized machines that combine fresh ingredients to create sauces.
- Emidio Rinaldi, COO of La Regina Atlantica, stated they look forward to beginning training with Quick Start and that the support will ensure growth while maintaining safety standards and family brand recipe standards.
- The training program involves three distinct partners: Georgia Quick Start, La Regina Atlantica, and Coastal Pines Technical College.
- Coastal Pines Technical College will serve as a long-term partner for ongoing training and provide a pipeline of qualified graduates to meet future workforce needs.
- Lonnie Roberts, President of Coastal Pines Technical College, stated they are eager to partner with companies committed to growing Georgia’s workforce and that the collaboration reflects a shared commitment to quality through skilled people.
- The Bacon County Development Authority and the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) worked with Coastal Pines Technical College and Georgia Quick Start to qualify La Regina Atlantica for training support.
- Quick Start training is scheduled to begin in early 2026.
