# Georgia Department of Economic Development

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2023-09-21

**Source:** [Governor Kemp Accepts James A. Van Fleet Award from The Korea Society | Georgia](https://georgia.org/press-release/governor-kemp-accepts-james-van-fleet-award-korea-society)  ·  `page_028969`

## Facts

- On September 21, 2023, Governor Brian P. Kemp accepted the James A. Van Fleet Award at The Korea Society’s 2023 Annual Dinner in New York, NY on behalf of the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD).
- The General James A. Van Fleet Award was established in 1992 and is awarded annually to recognize outstanding contributions to U.S.-Korea relations, being one of the most prestigious awards in the field.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that his first economic development mission as governor was to Korea.
- Georgia is the first state to receive this honor alongside former U.S. presidents, diplomats, and business icons who have been presented with the award in previous years.
- Alongside GDEcD, the Korea International Trade Association (KITA) also received a 2023 Van Fleet Award.
- Previous awardees include former U.S. Presidents George W. Bush, George H.W. Bush, and Jimmy Carter; former South Korean President Kim Dae-Jung; former Secretaries of State Colin Powell and Henry Kissinger; General Raymond G. Davis; organizations including The Korea Chamber of Commerce and Industry, LG, and General Motors; and corporate leaders like Hyundai Chairman Mong-Koo Chung, Samsung Chairman Kun-Hee Lee, SK Chairman Tae-won Chey, and CJ Group Chairman Kyung-Shik Sohn.
- The Korea Society is a nonprofit organization that has promoted greater awareness, understanding, and cooperation between the U.S. and Korea for over 65 years.
- Thomas Byrne, President and CEO of The Korea Society, stated that both awardees are serving to enhance trade and investment between the two countries, creating high-paying jobs in American communities and securing supply chains in critical technologies.
- To advance statewide business development opportunities, GDEcD opened an office in Korea in 1985.
- At least 144 facilities with Korean parent companies operate in the state of Georgia.
- For the last three years prior to the announcement, Korea has committed to creating more jobs in Georgia than any other country through foreign direct investment.
- Over the last decade, Korean investment has exceeded $23.6 billion through economic development projects in Georgia.
- Total trade between Georgia and Korea reached $13 billion in 2022.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Georgia’s long-term commitment to diplomatic, cultural, and business engagement in Korea has supported transformative growth over decades, providing economic benefits for both countries.
- Governor Kemp and the State of Georgia were introduced at the event by José Muñoz, President and COO of Hyundai Motor Company and President and CEO of Hyundai and Genesis Motor North America, as well as Stuart Countess, Kia Georgia President and CEO.
- Billions of additional investment dollars and tens of thousands of Georgia jobs in the mobility industry are a result of investments from Hyundai and Kia.
- Firms from Korea that operate in Georgia have helped to create one of the most advanced statewide manufacturing hubs in the U.S.
- Korean companies operating in Georgia span diverse industries, including SK Group, CJ Foodville, Kumho Tire, LG, POSCO, and Hanwha Qcells.
- Korea has maintained a Consular presence in the State of Georgia since 1976.
- Three Georgia communities have sister city agreements with South Korea: Atlanta with Daegu; Cobb County with Seongdong-Gu; and Gwinnett County with Gangnam-Gu.
- Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport offers 12 weekly direct flights from Atlanta to Seoul, South Korea through Delta Air Lines and Korean Air.
- There are six international cargo flights per week between Incheon, South Korea, and Atlanta.
- The Georgia Ports Authority (GPA) in Savannah has an established relationship with ports of Korea and offers seven regular shipping services to the Port of Busan.
- GPA’s Brunswick Port offers shipping services to Incheon, Masan, Pyongtaek, and Kunsan.
- The Van Fleet Award is named for General James A. Van Fleet, commander of the U.S. Eighth Army at the height of the Korean War in 1951.
- Beginning in 1957, General Van Fleet served as the first president of The Korea Society.
- GDEcD serves as the state’s sales and marketing arm and the lead agency for attracting new business investment, encouraging expansion of existing industry, locating new markets, attracting tourists, promoting arts/film/digital entertainment, and planning/mobilizing state resources for economic development.
