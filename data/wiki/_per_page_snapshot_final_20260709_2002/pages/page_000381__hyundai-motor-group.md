# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-05-20

**Source:** [Hyundai to Establish Its First Dedicated EV Plant and Battery Manufacturing Facility in the U.S.](https://www.theautochannel.com/news/2022/05/20/1145972-hyundai-to-establish-first-dedicated-ev-plant-and-battery-manufacturing.html)  ·  `page_000381`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group entered an agreement with the State of Georgia to build full electric vehicle and battery manufacturing facilities in Bryan County.
- The new facilities represent an investment of approximately USD 5.54 billion.
- Commercial production at the new facility is expected to begin in the first half of 2025.
- The facility will have an annual capacity of 300,000 units.
- Hyundai Motor Group plans to produce a wide range of innovative full electric vehicle models for U.S. customers at the new Georgia plant.
- Details regarding specific production models will be shared at later dates.
- The local EV production aims to increase U.S. consumer accessibility to the Group’s innovative EVs.
- The battery manufacturing facility will be established through a strategic partnership, with details to be disclosed at a later stage.
- Through the battery manufacturing facility, the Group aims to establish a stable supply chain and build a healthy EV ecosystem in the U.S.
- The EV and battery manufacturing plant will be located on a dedicated 2,923-acre site in Bryan County, Georgia.
- The site has immediate access to I-95 and I-16 highways, providing easy access to 250 major metro areas.
- The site is located less than 50 kilometers from the Port of Savannah, which is the single-largest and fastest-growing container terminal in the U.S.
- Two Class I rail facilities are on-site at the port, provided by Georgia Central Railway.
- West Point, Georgia is already home to the Group’s Kia manufacturing hub in the U.S.
- Hyundai Motor Group selected Georgia due to favorable business conditions including speed-to-market, a talented workforce, and an existing network of Group affiliates and suppliers.
- Georgia is the Southeastern leader for EV registration per 1,000 registered automobiles.
- Georgia already offers more publicly available EV charging outlets per capita than anywhere else in the Southeast.
- The project is expected to create about 8,100 new jobs.
- This investment is part of the Group’s 2021 announcement to invest USD 7.4 billion by 2025 to foster future mobility in the U.S., including EV production and smart mobility solutions.
- Hyundai Motor Group aims to become a leader in the U.S. auto market’s EV transition with the new facility.
- The Group has a global target to sell 3.23 million full electric vehicles annually by 2030.
- To realize this goal, the Group plans to establish a global EV production network to ensure a stable supply of EVs worldwide.
- With additional EV and battery production capabilities in the U.S., the Group aims to become one of the top three EV providers in the U.S. by 2026.
- The Group plans to lead the EV market in sales, design, technologies, and mobility solutions optimized for the electric mobility era.
- The investment aligns with the U.S. government’s roadmap to accelerate electrification as global OEMs expand local U.S. EV production.
- Last year, the U.S. government announced an initiative requiring zero-emission vehicle sales to account for at least half of OEM new vehicle sales by 2030.
- The new plant will feature a highly connected, automated, and flexible manufacturing system that connects all elements of the EV ecosystem.
- The Group plans to implement advanced intelligent manufacturing technologies currently under test at its innovation hub, the Hyundai Motor Group Innovation Center in Singapore (HMGICS).
- The Georgia facility will operate as an intelligent manufacturing plant where order collection, procurement, logistics, and production processes are optimized using AI and data.
- The innovative manufacturing system aims to create a human-centered work environment with robots assisting human workers.
- As part of its sustainability commitment, the plant will mainly rely on renewable energy sources and use emission-reduction technologies to meet RE100 requirements.
- Hyundai Motor Group Executive Chair Euisun Chung joined the official signing ceremony virtually in Bryan County.
- Georgia Governor Brian P. Kemp attended the signing ceremony along with other Georgia officials.
- Hyundai Motor Company President and CEO Jaehoon Chang attended the signing ceremony.
- Hyundai Motor President and Chief Operating Officer José Muñoz attended the signing ceremony.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that the project is the largest in the state’s history and delivers high-quality jobs on the leading edge of mobility to hardworking Georgians.
- Governor Brian Kemp noted it is incredible that within a year of Georgia’s investment in the Bryan County Megasite, they are locating a world-class project with a company with great Georgia history.
- Euisun Chung stated that the U.S. holds an important place in the Group’s global strategy and expressed excitement to partner with the State of Georgia for electrified mobility and sustainability.
- Jaehoon Chang stated that America embraces change and drives innovation, making it the right location for their first dedicated EV plant in the U.S.
- Jaehoon Chang added that the new EV plant is the future of their business and will help meet growing demands from U.S. customers for leading-edge design, safe, zero-emissions vehicles.
- Hyundai Motor Group is a global enterprise with a value chain based on mobility, steel, construction, logistics, finance, IT, and service.
- The Group has about 250,000 employees worldwide and its mobility brands include Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis.
