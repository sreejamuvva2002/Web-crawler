# Kia Georgia

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Georgia's Journey Towards a Just, Green Economy](https://blog.drawdownga.org/georgias-journey-towards-a-just-green-economy)  ·  `page_010035`

## Facts

- Kia Georgia currently employs approximately 3,000 team members and produces upwards of 350,000 vehicles annually at its manufacturing site in West Point, Georgia.
- Earlier this year, Kia introduced the EV9, which is the company’s first U.S.-assembled electric vehicle (EV).
- As Kia Georgia’s West Point manufacturing site grows and evolves, the company is converting and diversifying the skillsets of its existing workforce to meet the unique demands of EV production.
- Kia Georgia is employing creative tactics and new partnerships to attract and retain new talent in a challenging labor market.
- Kia Georgia is investing in workforce development through education partnerships around new curriculums for EV manufacturing, focusing on unique components and safety issues.
- These curriculum and educational resource partnerships are helping Kia Georgia upskill current employees and create a pipeline of new workers ready for future EV manufacturing positions.
- Kia Georgia has partnered with high schools in Troup County, including THINC College & Career Academy, to create work-based learning opportunities exposing students to manufacturing.
- Through the THINC partnership, students can participate in plant internships across production, engineering, or administrative areas to experience a manufacturing environment.
- Kia Georgia supports an interactive summer camp called Students Learning About Manufacturing (SLAM), which invites students to tour local industries, manufacturers, and suppliers, and participate in team robotic competitions.
- Kia Georgia supports the LaGrange Industrial Fellowship for Teachers (LIFT), a program designed to expose teachers and guidance counselors to manufacturing careers to help them educate students about manufacturing pathways.
- MaKara Rumley, an environmental justice expert and founder of the Hummingbird Firm, moderated the Drawdown Georgia panel on advancing equity and economic opportunities in the state.
- Latesa Bailey, senior manager and head of department for human resources at Kia Georgia, provided background on Kia’s presence and workforce investments in Georgia.
- Garry Harris, managing director of the Center for Sustainable Communities, outlined current clean energy investment and job growth statistics for Georgia.
- Georgia ranked 14th in the country for solar jobs in 2022, with solar-related jobs in the state expected to increase by 10.6% in 2023.
- In 2022, there were 80,710 jobs in Georgia that could be aligned with clean energy opportunities.
- Since 2018, electric vehicle manufacturers and suppliers have invested $22.7 billion in more than 40 projects in Georgia, creating 28,400 jobs.
- The Bipartisan Infrastructure Law is providing more than $800 million for workforce development in growing sectors like clean energy.
- Since the introduction of the Inflation Reduction Act, the energy sector has added nearly 300,000 jobs nationwide, with 114,000 of them in clean energy technologies like renewables and zero-emission vehicles.
- Georgia is the state with the second highest number of new private sector clean energy investments, featuring 22 projects that are creating 16,678 jobs.
- Garry Harris stated: “It's a good time to be in Georgia. And it's a good time to be part of the renewable energy movement.”
- The convergence of clean energy, workforce development, sustainable industries, and equitable access to fair wages and benefits is positioned to pave the way for lasting economic success in Georgia.
