# Pirelli

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Pirelli capacity plan 'could mitigate US tariffs' | European Rubber Journal](https://www.european-rubber-journal.com/article/2097002/pirelli-capacity-plan-could-mitigate-us-tariffs)  ·  `page_048542`

## Facts

- Executives stated on a 2024 financial results call that Pirelli could potentially expand its tire production capacity in the US to offset the impact of the escalating North American trade war.
- Pirelli has a contingency plan to address the US imposition of 25% tariffs on goods from Canada and Mexico, effective from 4 March.
- On a 27 February call, CFO Andrea Casaluci explained that while Pirelli's US business contributed more than 20% of group sales, the level of tire production there is limited.
- The group's plant in Georgia has a capacity of around 400,000 high-tech tires per year.
- To meet US market demand, Pirelli imports more than 50% of its US supply from Mexico and about 40% from Brazil and Europe.
- To mitigate tariff impacts, Pirelli has a contingency plan to increase imports from Brazil and increase production capacity in the US.
- Pirelli has already started a technological upgrade of the automated MIRS manufacturing processes at its Georgia facility.
- The finance chief noted that the technological upgrade will be the basis for the first step of capacity expansion in the US.
- Pirelli's commercial policy will be reviewed based on the 'inflationary scenario'.
- CFO Casaluci pointed out that since 30% of overall US tire demand is imported, some inflationary pressure is to be expected following the tariffs.
- Pirelli's contingency plan includes cost-reduction measures and €150 million of savings in 2025, as envisaged under its industrial plan.
