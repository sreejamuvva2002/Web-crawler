- Valeo announced it will lay off 1,150 people.
- The wave of layoffs continues as the cold wind of late winter blows towards the automobile industry.
- Reasons for layoffs at these three century-old auto suppliers can be summed up in three points: economic situation, financial situation, and electrification.
- Companies such as Bosch, Valeo, and ZF are in good financial condition and maintain a steady growth trend that may exceed expected growth targets despite the sluggish economic environment.
- The round of layoffs may be attributed to the electric transformation of the automotive industry.
- Giants have made adjustments in organizational structure, business, and product research and development directions.
- Bosch complies with the trend of "software-defined cars" and integrates its automotive departments to improve customer docking efficiency.
- Valeo focuses on core areas of electric vehicles such as assisted driving, thermal systems, and motors.
- ZF is integrating business departments to deal with electric vehicles development needs.
- Musk mentioned that the future of electric vehicles is inevitable and that over time, electric vehicles will gradually replace traditional fuel vehicles.
- At the beginning of 2024, the three major traditional auto parts suppliers announced layoffs.
- On January 19, Bosch said it plans to lay off about 1,200 people in its software and electronics divisions by the end of 2026.
- Of Bosch's planned layoffs, 950 (about 80%) will be in Germany.
- On January 18, Valeo announced that it would lay off 1,150 employees worldwide.
- Valeo is merging its hybrid and electric vehicle parts manufacturing divisions.
- A spokesperson for Valeo said: "We hope to strengthen our competitiveness by having a more agile, coherent and complete organization."
- On January 19, ZF announced that it expected to lay off 12,000 people in Germany over the next six years.
- ZF's planned layoffs are equivalent to almost a quarter of all ZF jobs in Germany.
- The direct reason for Bosch's layoffs is that the development of fully autonomous driving is slower than expected.
- Bosch attributed the layoffs to a weak economy and high inflation.
- Bosch stated in an official statement: "Economic weakness and high inflation resulting from, inter alia, increased energy and commodity costs are currently slowing down the transition."
- There are no public data and reports on the business performance of the Bosch Group's automotive division in 2023.
- Bosch's automotive business sales in 2022 were 52.6 billion euros (approximately RMB 408.7 billion), a year-on-year increase of 16%.
- Bosch's automotive business profit margin was only the lowest among all businesses, at 3.4%.
- Bosch's automotive business underwent adjustments in 2023, which may bring new growth.
- Valeo stated the reason for layoffs is to improve the group's competitiveness and efficiency in the context of automobile electrification.
- An article on Valeo's official website shows that the company's sales in the first half of 2023 reached 11.2 billion euros (approximately RMB 87 billion), a year-on-year increase of 19%.
- Valeo's operating profit margin in the first half of 2023 reached 3.2%, which is higher than the same period in 2022.
- Valeo's financial performance in the second half of the year is expected to improve.
- This layoff may be an early layout and preparation for electric transformation.

- A ZF spokesperson said that the company does not want to lay off employees, but the transition to electrification will inevitably involve the elimination of some positions.
- ZF's financial report shows the company achieved sales of 23.3 billion euros (approximately RMB 181.1 billion) in the first half of 2023.
