## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group has grown to become one of the global top three automakers in the U.S. auto market.
- A CNBC broadcast report analyzed how Hyundai Motor Group closed the gap with leading electric vehicle companies like Tesla in the EV market.
- Hyundai Motor Group continues to invest in robotics, autonomous driving, and future air mobility areas that competitors are giving up.
- In January, Hyundai Motor Group ranked fourth in the U.S. electric vehicle market, following General Motors, Toyota, and Ford.
- Hyundai Motor Group's electric vehicles feature high-level EV technology and lead the market through innovative technology such as the E-GMP-based ultra-fast charging system.
- Guidehouse Insights analyst Sam Abuelsamid stated that Hyundai Motor Group’s EV is recognized as a leading vehicle and its E-GMP-based electric automobiles and future models are recognized for product competitiveness.
- AutoPacific senior analyst Ed Kim evaluated that Hyundai’s Ioniq 5 is perceived as a superior product compared to Toyota’s equivalent model, the bZ4X, by general standards.
- In 1986, Hyundai Motor started its challenge as a car manufacturer in the U.S. by selling finished cars through the Excel model, a successor to the Pony designed by Giorgetto Giugiaro.
- Hyundai Motor quickly grew in the U.S. by achieving synergy based on the credibility of Asian brands built by Toyota.
- In 1998, Hyundai Motor faced difficulties after being criticized for not having the same quality as Japanese brands.
- Hyundai Motor Group executed a “fast follower” strategy, borrowing best practices from earlier U.S. market entrants like Toyota and Honda.
- Through proactive efforts to regain quality reliability, Hyundai Motor Group reached a level comparable to competitors from the 2000s.
- Yonsei University professor Lee Moo Won stated that the quality level of Hyundai Motor Group in the early 2000s rose to a level similar to that of the U.S. Big 3 and Japanese automakers.
- Ed Kim emphasized that Hyundai Motor Group’s sincerity about quality has been conveyed to consumers through a radical warranty policy, and it is no longer a manufacturer that makes cheap cars.
- Hyundai Motor Group faces various difficulties in the U.S. market such as recalls and vehicle theft but is receiving continuous praise for improved vehicle quality and actively responding to vehicle theft issues.
- Hyundai Motor Group has shown innovative models such as Nexo and N Vision 74 by continuously developing hydrogen fuel cell technology.
- Hyundai Motor Group is constantly challenging and innovating in autonomous driving and online vehicle sales, quickly responding to changes and pioneering the market as a leader in the automotive industry.
