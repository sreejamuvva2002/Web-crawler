# Stryten Energy LLC

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-10-26

**Source:** [Stryten Energy to Emphasize Increasing Market Opportunities](https://www.globenewswire.com/en/news-release/2023/10/26/2767589/0/en/Stryten-Energy-to-Emphasize-Increasing-Market-Opportunities-for-Enhanced-Flooded-Battery-Technology-at-AAPEX-2023.html)  ·  `page_029422`

## Facts

- Stryten Energy LLC is a U.S.-based energy storage solutions provider headquartered in Alpharetta, Georgia.
- The company will feature its Enhanced Flooded Battery (EFB) technology along with its full lineup of energy storage solutions for the automotive aftermarket at AAPEX 2023.
- AAPEX 2023 takes place October 31 to November 2 at the Venetian Expo in Las Vegas, Nevada.
- According to the EPA, half of new vehicles produced in 2021 included start-stop technology.
- Nearly 30% of new vehicles sold in the U.S. with start-stop technology now come equipped with EFB, a trend projected to increase year over year.
- EFB technology provides energy to run modern vehicles' electrical load demands repeatedly without significantly impacting the battery’s performance.
- For start-stop systems, the battery provides the electrical energy to support vehicle functions during the stop portion of the drive cycle.
- The battery must be resilient during partial state of charge cycling and have an enhanced charge acceptance capability for this system to work properly.
- Bill Nonnamaker, Vice President of Sales, Transportation for Stryten Energy, stated that batteries need more energy to support start-stop technology alongside conveniences and advanced safety features in vehicles today.
- As vehicle power demands continue to evolve, choosing the right battery will become even more important, according to Nonnamaker.
- The Stryten EFB is specifically designed to resist cycling-related failures associated with increased electrical demands of modern vehicles.
- Independent third-party testing found that Stryten Energy’s EFB offers three times the cycle life and twice the charge acceptance compared to standard flooded batteries.
- Additional tests showed Stryten Energy’s EFB yields more than two times the cycle life of a competing premium flooded battery.
- The EFB contains a more robust alloy for higher heat tolerance at a lower grid thickness.
- The EFB has an average capacity output 50% greater than Absorbed Glass Matt (AGM) for an equally sized battery.
- The EFB outperforms AGM in under-hood applications for vehicles equipped with a flooded battery from the factory due to greater energy availability, increased battery life, and high heat tolerance.
- Stryten Energy will make a $10 donation (up to $10,000 total) to the University of the Aftermarket Foundation for every selfie taken with the Stryten Jeep at its AAPEX booth.
- The University of the Aftermarket Foundation funds scholarships and educational grants to help talented individuals maximize their career potential in the automotive aftermarket industry.
- Bob Egan, MAAP, chairman of the University of the Aftermarket Foundation, praised Stryten Energy's support and emphasized that education and training are critical for developing and servicing new technologies.
- As part of its sustainability pledge for AAPEX 2023, Stryten Energy implemented LED lighting and will power down the booth during non-show hours.
- QR Codes will be used in place of printed literature to minimize paper waste, and branded plastic water bottles have been replaced with a water cooler and recycled cups at the booth.
- Stryten Energy provides energy storage solutions across the Essential Power, Motive Power, Transportation, Military, and Government sectors.
- The company partners with recognized global companies to meet demand for reliable and sustainable energy storage capacity.
- Stryten's stored energy technologies power submarines to subcompacts, microgrids, warehouses, distribution centers, cars, trains, and trucks.
- Stryten's technology portfolio includes advanced lead, lithium, and vanadium redox flow batteries, intelligent chargers, and energy performance management software.
- The independent third-party battery testing studies evaluated EFB performance against other technologies in the North American replacement market using standard industry test protocols.
