# Freudenberg-NOK

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2015-01-16

**Source:** [Freudenberg-NOK's Mexico Plant Shifts Into High Gear With New Oil Seals Portfolio - AftermarketNews](https://www.aftermarketnews.com/freudenberg-noks-mexico-plant-shifts-high-gear-new-oil-seals-portfolio/)  ·  `page_020419`

## Facts

- The Queretaro facility supplies several Japanese engine customers and major automakers with front-wheel drive transmission parts.
- Freudenberg-NOK provides oil seals through this business line, which began production in 2014.
- Sales for the new business line are expected to grow rapidly alongside overall Mexican auto production.
- By the end of 2015, industry sources estimate that Mexico will surpass Japan and Canada to become the top source of imported cars by the U.S.
- Gary VanWambeke, general manager of the Global Transmission and Driveline Lead Center at Freudenberg-NOK, stated that increased customer requests for production in Mexico prompted the company to expand its manufacturing there.
- Six months after installing machinery for oil seal production, the facility operated 58 pieces of equipment and delivered 2.4 million parts.
- During its first year of operation, the Queretaro plant achieved a perfect on-time-delivery score while remaining free from accidents and customer complaints.
- Plant manager Luis Pardo credited the facility's success to associates who adapted well to change, highlighting the team's passion, stable performance, quick turnaround times, and incredible morale.
- The plant introduced several new operational concepts and a "one company, one team" motto to maintain employee spirit.
- Freudenberg-NOK relocated a portion of its automotive sealing production to Mexico based on a global business philosophy to establish a local manufacturing presence and integrate into the host country's business community.
- Torsten Maschke, president of automotive sales and marketing at Freudenberg Sealing Technologies, stated that the company does not base regional operations on low-cost labor opportunities.
- Instead, the company pursues investments where it can become a local supplier focused on local customer needs and integrate into a country's manufacturing fabric.
- Freudenberg-NOK's Queretaro facility has become one of the company's fastest-growing locations due to successful integration into the Mexican industry.
- Production numbers at the facility are expected to continue growing as Mexico increases its exporting trend and automakers move production to the country.
