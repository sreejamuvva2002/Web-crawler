# Orbia-Solvay Joint Venture

**Type:** Joint Venture  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[battery-materials|Battery Materials]]  ·  **Location:** Augusta, Georgia, Richmond County, Georgia, United States

## Overview

A joint venture between Orbia’s Fluorinated Solutions business and Solvay to build the largest polyvinylidene fluoride (PVDF) production capacity in North America for lithium-ion battery applications.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Provides IRA-compliant PVDF binder material essential for EV battery performance, range, lifespan and safety.

- **Orbia-Solvay PVDF Finished Products Facility** — Facility for production of finished suspension-grade PVDF used as binder and separator coating in EV batteries.
- **Orbia-Solvay PVDF Raw Material Conversion Plant** — Plant to convert raw materials into intermediates needed for PVDF production.

## Timeline

- **November 2, 2023** — Joint venture agreement signed between Orbia’s Fluorinated Solutions business and Solvay in Boston.
- **2026** — Both the Augusta, GA finished-product facility and the St. Gabriel, LA raw-material plant are expected to become operational.

## Key facts

- The JV will create the largest PVDF production facilities for battery materials in North America.
- PVDF is a lithium-ion binder and separator coating that allows electric vehicles to go further on each charge, extends battery life and improves safety.
- Materials produced will be Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) compliant, supporting U.S. EV supply-chain security.
- "We are proud of this important project..." - Ilham Kadri, CEO of Solvay, highlighting clean energy job creation.
