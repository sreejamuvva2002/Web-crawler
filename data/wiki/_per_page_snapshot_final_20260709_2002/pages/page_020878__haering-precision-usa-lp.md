## Facts

- Haering Precision USA LP hosted its inaugural Skills Invitational in collaboration with North Georgia Technical College.
- The Skills Invitational aimed to ignite interest in machining and metals jobs among high school students while providing them a platform to explore diverse career opportunities within the manufacturing industry.
- Participants from local high schools teamed up with senior precision machining students from North Georgia Tech in a proam style contest.
- The event featured challenges designed to test technical skills, creativity, and teamwork.
- Throughout the day-long event, students faced hands-on challenges simulating real-world scenarios encountered in precision machining and manufacturing.
- Challenges included interpreting technical drawings and programming CNC machines.
- Judges evaluated teams based on criteria such as accuracy, efficiency, innovation, and collaborative problem-solving.
- Winners received various prizes, including tools and paid internship opportunities at Haering's US facility.
- Franklin County High School senior Logan Turner took first place in the contest.
- The event recognized participating high schools, students, faculty, and industry partners from Franklin, Hart, Stephens, and Union counties for their active involvement and commitment to advancing manufacturing education and workforce development initiatives.
- Haering plans to expand opportunities for students to explore and pursue careers in precision machining and manufacturing following the success of the inaugural Skills Invitational.
- The company looks forward to hosting next year's competition.
