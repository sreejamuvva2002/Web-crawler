# Murata Electronics North America Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [All-in-one Bluetooth module - EDN](https://www.edn.com/all-in-one-bluetooth-module/)  ·  `page_047222`

## Facts

- Murata Electronics North America Inc. is located in Smyrna, Ga.
- The company developed a Bluetooth Module using Low Temperature Co-Fired Ceramics technology.
- The module integrates both active devices (ICs) and passive components onto a single surface-mount ceramic chip that supports Universal Serial Bus, UART and pulse-code modulation interfaces.
- Applications for the module include cellular phones, PDAs, notebook and desktop PCs, wireless printers, mouses and keyboards.
- The module measures 14.5 x 11.9 x 2.3 mm.
- Murata integrated the passives and matching circuits within the substrate of the chip, making it both a carrier of the IC and a functional device.
- Scott Klettke serves as Murata's marketing-segment manager.
- Sourcing and inventory costs can be reduced because the all-in-one device eliminates the line item cost of managing several part numbers on a bill of materials.
- Approximately 60 components are embedded in the module.
- The module includes a radio-frequency/baseband IC that is mounted in a cavity on the bottom surface of the substrate.
- Murata mounts a power amplifier to increase the output power and a low-noise amplifier to improve sensitivity on the surface of the substrate.
- Several parts and functions are integrated within the substrate, such as a bandpass filter, a balun and other passives.
- The module has achieved Logo Certification of Version 1.0b plus CE.
- The module will acquire version 1.1 certification shortly.
- The module is available for sampling at $100 each.
