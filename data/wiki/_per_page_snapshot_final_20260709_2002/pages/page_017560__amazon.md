## Facts

- Amazon plans to spend at least $11 billion on new data center capacity in metro Atlanta.
- The investment responds to huge demand for computing power driven by the rise of generative artificial intelligence.
- Amazon Web Services (AWS) will make the investments in facilities located in Butts County and Douglas County.
- Butts County is located south of Atlanta, and Douglas County is located west of Atlanta.
- Combined, the two data center projects would likely rank as the largest corporate investment in Georgia's history by dollar amount.
- The combined investment will exceed the capital expenditure of the state’s largest economic development project to date, which is the $7.6 billion Hyundai Motor Group electric vehicle factory near Savannah.
- AWS announced it will procure power to the grid with renewable energy, including solar and wind.
- Amazon has committed to purchasing 100% renewable electricity for its operations worldwide.
- The Georgia Data Center in Atlanta is described as a hub for sustainable and efficient computing infrastructure.
- John Jackson, Douglas County Commissioner, commented on the projects.
