# Georgia Department of Economic Development

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2022-12-31

**Source:** [Key Industries Like Film Sustain Momentum for State’s Economic Development - Film Columbus GA News](https://www.filmcolumbusganews.com/2023/02/16/key-industries-like-film-sustain-momentum-for-states-economic-development/)  ·  `page_019326`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp and GDEcD announced Georgia sustained economic development momentum through key industries during H1 FY2023 (July 1 through December 31, 2022).
- The 218 locations or expansions supported by GDEcD’s Global Commerce division are expected to create approximately 17,500 new jobs and more than $13 billion in investment for the state.
- Both the number of locations and total investment amount increased over the same period last year.
- Areas outside the 10-county metro Atlanta region accounted for 85 percent of jobs created and 92 percent of investments from economic development projects during the period.
- Four of Georgia’s largest industries – advanced manufacturing, automotive, aerospace, and food processing – accounted for nearly 75 percent of new jobs and 84 percent of investments.
- Planned investments resulting from these projects increased by more than 38 percent compared to the same time last year.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson cited proven solutions such as the Georgia Quick Start job training program and GRAD certified sites that support accelerated project timelines.
- The automotive industry was the second-largest source of new jobs and investment, after advanced manufacturing.
- In October, state and local officials joined company representatives including Korean Ambassador to the U.S. Tae-yong Cho for the groundbreaking of the new Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) EV facility.
- The HMGMA EV facility will support more than 8,100 direct jobs in Bryan, Bulloch, Chatham and Effingham Counties.
- Three off-site HMGMA suppliers announced locations following the groundbreaking, swiftly surpassing the initial $1 billion in investment predicted to come from such additions.
- International companies have been valued partners in creating a closed-loop electric mobility supply chain and accounted for 23 percent of state projects since July 1, 2022.
- The partnership between SK On and Hyundai Motor Group in Bartow County is a historic project example of international investment.
- Next-generation developer FREYR Battery announced a facility in Coweta County as another historic project example.
- Both the SK On/Hyundai and FREYR Battery projects are in the top five largest projects by investment in the state’s history.
- Battery recyclers Sung-Il Hitech and Denkai America joined Georgia’s growing electric mobility ecosystem.
- The aerospace and defense industries continued to benefit from Georgia’s logistics infrastructure and engineering talent pool, creating more than 1,500 new jobs.
- Aerospace company Archer Aviation Inc. advancing sustainable urban air mobility announced facilities near airports.
- Defense technology company Anduril Industries also announced facilities near airports.
- Both Archer Aviation and Anduril Industries will benefit from nearby top-ranked technical colleges and four-year universities.
- Advanced manufacturing is a cornerstone industry sector in Georgia’s middle and coastal regions.
- YKK AP announced a major investment or expansion in Macon during FY 2023.
- Beretta Holding S.A. announced a new facility in Bryan County through its subsidiary Norma Precision Inc. during FY 2023.
- The advanced manufacturing sector has announced more than 7,700 new jobs in the state since July 1, 2022.
- Germany-based Haering Precision celebrated the grand opening of its Lavonia facility in November with a two-day event including a “Future Day” for approximately 400 local high school students and community members.
- Supported by agricultural strength and logistics capabilities, the food processing industry continued to choose Georgia for growth.
- Companies in the food processing field announced more than $695 million in investments, largely through expansions.
- King’s Hawaiian® announced another expansion of its Oakwood facility, marking its second expansion in less than a year.
- GDEcD Deputy Commissioner for Global Commerce Kristi Brigman noted that companies connect with community partners to solve workforce challenges and utilize the Georgia Quick Start program to build talent from the ground up.
- These programs help Georgians upskill, including those who are part of Georgia’s strong inbound domestic migration trends.
- Life sciences and film industries welcomed additional facilities that will support industry breakthroughs and learning opportunities for students.
- Georgia Tech hosted a groundbreaking for its Science Square purpose-built district in August.
- The Rowen Foundation’s 2,000-acre science and learning campus broke ground in early December.
- Athena Studios announced it will add an additional 350,000 square feet to its Athens facility.
- The Athena Studios expansion includes a structure dedicated to training the local workforce through a partnership with the University of Georgia and Georgia Film Academy.
- BlueStar Studios at the former Fort Gillem outside Atlanta announced more than 600,000 square feet of new stage space.
- BlueStar Studios will also add an additional 45,000 square feet dedicated to technical resources and amenities, which will include refurbished buildings that preserve the base’s historic architecture.
