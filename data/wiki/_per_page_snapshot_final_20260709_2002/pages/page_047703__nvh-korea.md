# NVH Korea

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-06-22

**Source:** [EV Parts Supplier NVH Korea to Create Over 160 Jobs, Invest $72M in Henry County - AllOnGeorgia](https://allongeorgia.com/georgia-business/ev-parts-supplier-nvh-korea-to-create-over-160-jobs-invest-72m-in-henry-county/)  ·  `page_047703`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that automotive parts manufacturer NVH Korea will establish a new facility in Locust Grove.
- The project will create over 160 new jobs.
- The project will bring $72 million in investment to Henry County.
- This is the second economic development project Governor Kemp has announced this week alone.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated that Georgia has a reliable infrastructure network connecting partners all over the world and a great workforce.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp noted that Georgia is experiencing incredible growth in the e-mobility and battery industries.
- NVH Korea is an automotive parts manufacturer based in the City of Ulsan.
- The company was established in 1984.
- The company specializes in automotive noise, vibration, and heat-control parts.
- The company currently produces these parts in Korea, Europe, and India.
- The company operates a manufacturing facility that produces floor mats, cargo mats, and cargo trays in Georgia.
- The new facility will supply battery parts for electric vehicles (EVs) in the United States.
- Customers and partners include Hyundai Mobis, Kia Georgia, and Hyundai Motor Group.
- Ja Kyum Koo, Chairman and CEO of NVH Korea, stated that Georgia is the company's first EV battery component manufacturing site.
- Ja Kyum Koo noted that cooperation between state agencies and company staff helped guide the project.
- The new facility will be located at Gardner Logistics Park on Colvin Drive in Locust Grove.
- The facility will produce EV battery system components that protect, connect, and sense the performance of battery cells.
- Operations are expected to begin in the second quarter of 2024.
- Mayor Robert Price of the City of Locust Grove stated that NVH's investment will increase the manufacturing jobs base in Locust Grove.
- Carlotta Harrell, Chair of the Henry County Board of Commissioners, stated that the announcement will add quality manufacturing jobs for Henry County citizens.
- Project Manager Jacob Lee represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- Partners include the Henry County Development Authority, Georgia EMC, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that NVH Korea is establishing its first U.S. or e-mobility manufacturing presence.
- From July 1 through December 31, 2022, the automotive industry was the second-largest source of new jobs and investment in Georgia.
- Since 2020, Georgia has pursued job creation along the entire EV supply chain, resulting in more than $22.7 billion in investments and the creation of over 28,400 jobs.
