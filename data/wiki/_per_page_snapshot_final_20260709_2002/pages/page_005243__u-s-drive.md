# U.S. DRIVE

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** research_and_development  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-30

**Source:** [U.S. DRIVE | Department of Energy](https://www.energy.gov/cmei/vehicles/us-drive)  ·  `page_005243`

## Facts

- U.S. DRIVE is a non-binding and voluntary government-industry partnership focused on advanced automotive and related energy infrastructure technology research and development (R&D).
- The Partnership serves as a forum for pre-competitive technical information exchange among partners to discuss R&D needs, develop joint goals and technology roadmaps, and evaluate R&D progress.
- The Partnership accelerates technical progress by enabling peers in the technical community to discuss pre-competitive, technology-specific R&D needs and challenges, identify possible solutions, and evaluate progress toward jointly-developed technical goals.
- The Partnership helps avoid duplication of efforts and ensures that publicly-funded research delivers high-value results and overcomes high-risk barriers to technology commercialization.
- Government-industry collaboration benefits from multiple perspectives and unequivocal technical expertise to keep efforts at the forefront of the changing world of automotive and related energy infrastructure technology development.
- The Automobile industry partner is U.S. Council for Automotive Research LLC (USCAR), which operates as the cooperative research organization for Ford Motor Company, General Motors Company, and Stellantis.
- Electric utility industry partners include DTE Energy Company, Southern California Edison Company, Electric Power Research Institute, American Electric Power, and Duke Energy.
- The Federal government partner is the U.S. Department of Energy.
- Fuels industry partners include BP America, Chevron Corporation, Phillips 66 Company, ExxonMobil Corporation, and Shell Oil Products US.
- Technical focus areas for Vehicles include advanced combustion and emission control, electric drive and power electronics, electrochemical energy storage, fuel cells, and lightweight materials.
- Technical focus areas for Fuels include hydrogen production, hydrogen delivery, hydrogen fuel pathway integration, low carbon fuels for future high-efficiency engines, or other mobility fuel as agreed to by the Partnership.
- Cross-cutting technical focus areas include analysis, energy efficient mobility systems, hydrogen codes and standards, hydrogen storage, and vehicle-grid interaction.
- Information about the Partnership's vision, mission, scope, organizational structure, and operating principles is documented in the U.S. DRIVE Partnership Plan.
- Highlights of technical accomplishments are available in the 2023 Technical Accomplishments Report or past years' technical reports.
- The Fuels Working Group published 2019 High-Octane reports detailing its work.
