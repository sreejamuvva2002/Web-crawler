# Hyundai Motor Group

## Facts

- Professor Juanita (Babet) Villena-Alvarez presented an outstanding USCB-OLLI course on Hyundai's growth from founding in 1967 to becoming a huge conglomerate.
- Hyundai has made massive investments in southeast Georgia, located only about an hour drive from Hilton Head.
- A local course aimed to educate citizens on the cultural changes and infrastructure challenges accompanying rapid growth from Korean direct investment.
- Georgia Power’s Plant Vogtle is now the largest nuclear generating plant in the U.S. and is completed within 100 miles of the HMG Metaplant.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that in a single year, they broke ground on the largest project in state history.
- Multiple suppliers across the state were landed for Hyundai’s Metaplant.
- LGES was welcomed to Bryan County.
- Georgia is being positioned as the e-mobility capital of the nation due to these major investments.
- Major investments are expected to provide hardworking Georgians with paychecks, improved schools, and infrastructure.
- Hyundai Motor Group (HMG) is just one division of a much larger conglomerate that also produces steel and shipping services.
- South Korean firms including Samsung, LG, and SK-Battery have invested over $200 Billion in plants in the U.S.
- Hyundai manufactures Hyundai, KIA, and Genesis autos.
- Over 8,100 jobs will be created by HMG in Georgia.
- Over 14,300 jobs will be created including those from supply-chain providers of components.
- The Panamax capable Port of Savannah will be enlarged with additional new Ship to Shore Cranes.
- Savannah is already one of the busiest ports in the U.S.
- The Talmadge Bridge over the Savannah River will be raised to accommodate Panamax Ships.
- Hyundai is now the #3 world auto manufacturer, after Toyota and VW.
- HMG plans to build 500,000 vehicles per year at the new GA MetaPlant.
- BMW produced 412,000 vehicles in 2022 as a production comparison.
- The Hyundai Meta Plant covers almost 3,000 acres off of I-16.
- Hyundai is partnering with Georgia Tech to collaborate on the use of Hydrogen for future Hydrogen Fuel Cell Vehicles.
- Hyundai pulled out of its large plant in China in August 2023.
- Hyundai was founded in 1967 by Chung Ju Yung, who was born in North Korea.
- SK Battery and Hyundai formed a partnership to create 3,500 jobs plus $5 billion more investment in Georgia as of March 2023.
- Total related investments for the project have reached $12 billion.
- St. Joseph's/Candler is opening a medical center near the new Hyundai Metaplant in Georgia.
- Hyundai is partnering with local colleges to expand student opportunities.
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America and Savannah Technical College signed a memorandum of understanding for a training partnership.
- A 2022 Reuters article announced a $5.5 billion investment by Hyundai for an EV plant in Georgia.
- An AJC Politics article provided a first look inside Hyundai's $7.6 billion Meta Plant.
- The Port of Savannah added four heavy lift, ship to shore cranes in April 2023.
- The Port received its first shipment for the Metaplant in August 2023.
- A plan moved forward in January 2023 to raise the Talmadge Bridge clearance and replace cables.
- Samsung announced plans to invest $200 billion in the U.S.
- Automotive News reported on Hyundai unloading its China assembly plant in January 2024.
