# City of Griffin

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Griffin (Spalding County, GA) city directory 1957 including Cherokee Heights, East Griffin, Highland Mills and Experiment, GA - Digital Library of Georgia](https://dlg.usg.edu/record/zgr-frrls-gr_grifcd_dir-griffin1957)  ·  `page_048043`

## Facts

- Griffin had a population of 20,472 with a metropolitan population of approximately 27,000 in 1957.
- The directory lists a total population of 29,666 for the area.
- Two-thirds of non-agricultural workers were employed in textile mills, making it primarily a textile city.
- Food processing was the second largest employment category, including Pomona Products Company, described as the largest pimiento pepper canning operation in the world.
- The first textile mill opened in Griffin in 1883.
- In 1900, there were 1,000 industrial wage earners in Griffin with annual wages less than a quarter of a million dollars.
- By 1957, approximately 8,000 workers were on industrial payrolls with more than $17,000,000 as an annual payroll.
- The Georgia Experiment Station on the outskirts of town developed new plants including Empire Cotton, Truehart Perfection Pimientos, Hunt muscadines, and Georgia 101 hybrid corn.
- The station improved food processing which created new industries for Georgia.
- City limits cover 6.7 square miles while Spalding County comprises 128,640 acres (201 square miles).
- Approximately 50 manufacturing plants are located in the outlying areas of Griffin.
- Dundee Mills, manufacturer of Dundee Towels, is the second largest towel producer in the world.
- The consolidated schools of Griffin and Spalding County offer vocational training alongside required and elective courses.
- A school expansion program exceeding $1,000,000 was completed with a second $1,250,000 building program underway.
- The school system includes 23 grammar schools, one junior high school, and two senior high schools serving approximately 7,543 students under 250 teachers.
- Georgia Tech, Emory University, Agnes Scott, Wesleyan College, and Mercer University are within a 50-mile radius of Griffin.
- Electric power is supplied to the area by the Georgia Power Company and distributed in the Metropolitan area by a city-owned power system.
- The power distribution system includes three transmission lines with 110,000 volts and two with 44,000 volts.
- The document states that power supply is no problem for any industrial expansion plant.
- The Griffin water supply comes from the Flint River, which originates near East Point, Georgia, forty miles north of Griffin.
- The Flint River is capable of supplying 15 million gallons of water per day, with the present filtered system providing 7,000,000 gallons per day.
- Griffin is located approximately 35 miles south of the Atlanta Municipal Air Field and is joined to it by a four-lane super highway.
- Atlanta serves as a hub for twelve major air routes across the nation with 214 mail, passenger, and cargo planes scheduled daily.
- Five major motor express lines handle interstate and intrastate freight shipments from Griffin, including Akers Motor Lines, Georgia Highway Express, Great Southern Trucking Company, Perkins Freight Lines, and Johnson Transfer Company.
- The city has had a city management form of municipal government since 1918, composed of a city manager appointed by three commissioners.
- Both city and county governments work closely together and are active in projects to secure new industry.
- Housing in the vicinity is considered adequate for current needs with approximately 200 low-rent public housing units available.
