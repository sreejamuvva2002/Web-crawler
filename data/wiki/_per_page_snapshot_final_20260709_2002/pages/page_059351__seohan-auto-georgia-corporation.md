# Seohan Auto Georgia Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [GA: Parts Maker Seohan Auto Georgia to Create 180 New Jobs with New Facility in Liberty County | Trade and Industry Development](https://www.tradeandindustrydev.com/region/georgia/news/ga-parts-maker-seohan-auto-georgia-create-180-new-31645)  ·  `page_059351`

## Facts

- Georgia Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Seohan Auto Georgia will create at least 180 new jobs.
- Seohan Auto Georgia will invest more than $72 million in a new manufacturing facility.
- The new manufacturing facility is located in Liberty County, Georgia.
- Seohan Auto Georgia is a developer and manufacturer of automobile parts.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that the Hyundai Metaplant serves as an economic driver for the region, creating jobs in communities like Liberty County.
- Governor Brian Kemp welcomed Seohan Auto Georgia as the latest supplier investing in Georgia's fast-emerging EV sector.
- Seohan Auto Georgia Corporation operates as a Tier-1 automotive parts supplier for Hyundai Motor Group America.
- Seohan Auto Georgia Corporation is headquartered in South Korea.
- The company operates production facilities globally, including multiple locations in the United States.
- CEO Jung Kee Koo cited sustained EV market growth as driving accelerated changes in the automotive industry.
- Jung Kee Koo stated that Seohan Auto Georgia believes Georgia will become the center of the EV industry and a new frontier for the company's future.
- Jung Kee Koo expressed pleasure in collaborating with Georgia on the company's expansion.
- Jung Kee Koo noted Seohan's history of creating innovative solutions and its determination to advance in the EV industry through its Liberty County project.
- Jung Kee Koo expressed confidence that Seohan's experience and technological capabilities will benefit the local community.
- Jung Kee Koo thanked Georgia and Liberty County for their continuous support and trust during the project process.
- Seohan's new Georgia facility is scheduled to begin production in late 2024.
- The facility will be located at the Dunwoody Court Site within the Tradeport East Business Center.
- The Tradeport East Business Center is a Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development (GRAD) Certified site.
- The new facility will manufacture H-shafts, axles, and brake systems for original equipment manufacturers (OEMs).
- One of the primary customers for the new facility's output is Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America.
- Liberty County Development Authority Chairman Allen Brown described Seohan Auto Georgia and its global affiliates as responsible, thorough, and progressive organizations advancing innovative technology.
- Allen Brown expressed pleasure at Liberty County being selected as Seohan's location and looked forward to supporting the advanced manufacturing project.
- GDEcD Director of Project Implementation and Supplier Strategy Alyce Thornhill represented the state's Global Commerce team on the project.
- The project involved partnerships with the Liberty County Industrial Development Authority, Georgia Ports Authority, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia Power.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that the transition to e-mobility and sustainable technology creates opportunities for forward-looking companies and states.
- Pat Wilson noted that automotive industry innovation is advancing rapidly, requiring companies to keep up with new technology and market demand.
- Pat Wilson highlighted that communities investing in speed-to-market solutions, such as Liberty County's Tradeport East Business Center, give Georgia a competitive advantage.
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America officially broke ground on its first fully dedicated EV and battery manufacturing facility in neighboring Bryan County on October 25.
- Offsite suppliers connected to the Hyundai Metaplant project were initially predicted to invest approximately $1 billion across Georgia.
- Total announced projects in the region have now exceeded the initial $1 billion supplier investment prediction by nearly $1 billion.
- Georgia is positioning itself as a hub for the electric mobility industry by leveraging its existing automotive industry assets.
- Since 2020, over 35 EV-related projects in Georgia have announced more than $21 billion in total investment.
- These projects have collectively announced the creation of 27,800 jobs in Georgia since 2020.
