# T1 Energy (formerly Freyr Battery)

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [T1 Energy's Solar Supply Chain Transformation: A Strategic Bet on U.S. Energy Independence](https://www.ainvest.com/news/t1-energy-solar-supply-chain-transformation-strategic-bet-energy-independence-2508/)  ·  `page_055010`

## Facts

- T1 Energy is a rebranded entity formerly known as Freyr Battery that has pivoted from battery storage to solar manufacturing.
- T1 Energy formed a partnership with Corning involving a $1.5 billion investment in polysilicon and wafer production.
- The partnership secures hyper-pure polysilicon and solar wafers from Corning's Michigan campus.
- Polysilicon is a foundational material for solar cells that has historically been sourced overseas, exposing projects to geopolitical risks and trade tariffs.
- The partnership ensures raw materials, wafers, cells, and modules are all produced domestically, creating a closed-loop system.
- The closed-loop supply chain aligns with the Inflation Reduction Act's (IRA) domestic content requirements.
- Analysts project that the streamlined supply chain could reduce solar module costs by 15–20% by 2030.
- Cost reduction drivers include economies of scale and the elimination of intermediaries.
- T1 Energy's G2_Austin facility is set to produce 5 GW of advanced TOPCon solar cells annually.
- T1 Energy's G1_Dallas module plant is already generating 443 MW in Q1 2025.
- Trump-era tariffs on foreign solar imports have inadvertently accelerated domestic manufacturing.
- Avoiding reliance on Chinese components ensures eligibility for the IRA's 30% Investment Tax Credit (ITC).
- The IRA's domestic content bonus could add up to 10% to the credit.
- The One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBBA) mandates 10-year recapture rules for ITC claims.
- T1's partnership with Corning includes wafer production at Hemlock Semiconductor, a U.S. polysilicon leader.
- T1 Energy made an $850 million investment in Austin.
- T1 Energy made a $340 million acquisition of the Dallas module plant.
- The company abandoned its Georgia battery project, which was a $2.6 billion venture.
- The U.S. Department of Energy emphasizes solar as a “low-hanging fruit” for rapid decarbonization.
- T1 Energy maintains a diversified energy portfolio in Europe.
- The U.S. solar market is projected to grow at a compound annual rate of 12% through 2030.
- Market growth drivers include corporate sustainability mandates and the AI-driven energy demand surge.
- By 2026, T1's Austin facility will supply 5 GW of cells annually.
- Corning's Michigan plant will produce enough wafers to support 10 GW of module production.
- The partnership has job creation potential for nearly 6,000 roles across Michigan and Texas.
- Job creation aligns with the Biden administration's industrial policy goals.
- Success of the Austin facility hinges on timely construction and operational efficiency.
- The solar market's post-2028 outlook depends on sustained demand and regulatory stability.
