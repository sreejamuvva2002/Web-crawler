# LG Energy Solution

## Facts

- South Korean battery maker LG Energy Solution suggested that it could build a factory in the US state of Georgia to manufacture batteries for electric vehicles.
- An official at LG Energy Solution made the suggestion on Saturday.
- LG Energy Solution CEO Kim Jong-hyun sent a letter to US Senator Raphael Warnock.
- In the letter, Kim Jong-hyun said that "the company is prepared to do whatever we can help the people and workers of Georgia."
- The Atlanta Journal-Constitution reported on the contents of the letter.
- LG Chem's wholly owned battery division, LG Energy Solution, confirmed it recently sent the letter to the senator but declined to confirm details.
- Kim Jong-hyun said in the letter that if an outside investor acquires a separate SK Innovation plant in Georgia, LG could partner with it to run the facility.
- The Atlanta Journal-Constitution also reported on the potential partnership with the SK Innovation plant.
- An official at LG Energy Solution told Reuters that the letter was intended to clearly address the current situation caused by SK's misappropriation of trade secrets and to alleviate concerns about jobs in Georgia.
- An official at SK Innovation rejected the idea of LG taking over its plant.
- The SK Innovation official stated that automakers do not allow contracts and suppliers to change separately, making it unreasonable for LG to say it could take over the plant and replace them.
- Georgia's governor Brian Kemp repeated his request to US President Joe Biden on Friday to overturn the US International Trade Commission (ITC)'s ruling against SK Innovation.
- Governor Kemp's request aims to save thousands of jobs in the state directly tied to SK's battery factory.
- LG Energy Solution has been in a legal feud with SK Innovation over allegations that SK stole trade secrets.
- The ITC sided with LG Chem in February and issued a 10-year order prohibiting most US imports of SK lithium-ion batteries.
- SK Innovation has lobbied the White House to overturn the decision.
- The ITC ruling could also be negated if SK and LG reach an independent settlement.
- On Thursday, LG announced its plans to invest more than $4.5 billion in its US battery production business over the next four years.
- LG's investment announcement includes plans to build at least two new plants.
