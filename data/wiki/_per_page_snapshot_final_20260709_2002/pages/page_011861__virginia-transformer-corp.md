# Virginia Transformer Corp.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** power_electronics  ·  **Published:** 2025-11

**Source:** [Virginia Transformer to create 400 jobs in Georgia expansion](https://valdostatoday.com/news-2/region/2025/11/virginia-transformer-to-create-400-jobs-in-georgia-expansion/)  ·  `page_011861`

## Facts

- Governor Brian Kemp announced that Virginia Transformer will create 400 jobs in a new Georgia expansion.
- Virginia Transformer Corp. will invest $40 million to expand its existing manufacturing facility near Rincon, creating over 400 new jobs in Effingham County.
- The Rincon facility operates under the name Georgia Transformer.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated: “Along with state and local leaders in the coastal region, we’re proud to celebrate this major expansion and Virginia Transformer’s decision to double down on Georgia,” and congratulated the company on the announcement.
- The Georgia Transformer facility in Rincon specializes in manufacturing custom-designed large power transformers for utilities, data centers, and renewable energy sectors.
- The 250,000-square-foot facility produces transformers ranging from 30 MegaVolt-Amperes (MVA) to 500 MVA at up to 525 kilovolts.
- This expansion marks the latest in a series of investments at this facility since it became part of Virginia Transformer Corp. in 2015.
- Speaker Jon Burns joined Governor Kemp and Southeast Georgia leaders to applaud the expansion, noting it arrives as the region recovers from mill closures and job losses.
- Virginia Transformer is the largest U.S.-owned producer of power transformers in North America.
- CEO Prabhat Jain stated the company has grown by focusing on delivering for customers and is known for engineering power transformers with the shortest lead times.
- The Georgia Transformer production facility is located at 2789 GA-21 in Effingham County.
- Construction on the expansion is expected to begin in January 2026.
- Applications for positions in operations, maintenance, and assembly are currently being accepted.
- Chairman Matt Saxon of the Effingham County Industrial Development Authority praised the investment as a powerful endorsement of the community’s skilled workforce and strategic location.
- The project will retain more than 800 existing jobs while creating 400 new positions.
- District 5 Effingham County Commissioner Phil Kieffer congratulated the company on the milestone and commended the ICDA for fostering a supportive business environment.
- Senior Regional Project Manager Dorie Bacon represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The project is supported in partnership with the Effingham County Industrial Development Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson noted that demand for utility and commercial scale is at an all-time high and attracting energy grid sectors ensures business growth in Georgia.
- Virginia Transformer Corp. manufactures custom-designed power transformers for a diverse clientele across utilities, renewable energy, data centers, industrial, mining, oil and gas, commercial, institutional, specialty, and export markets.
