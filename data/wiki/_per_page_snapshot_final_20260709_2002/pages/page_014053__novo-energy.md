# Novo Energy

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Volvo shutters EV battery cell manufacturer Novo Energy](https://www.evinfrastructurenews.com/ev-battery/volvo-cars-shutters-ev-battery-cell-manufacturing-effort-novo-energy-as-it-struggles-to-secure-tech-partner)  ·  `page_014053`

## Facts

- Novo Energy is the battery cell manufacturing subsidiary of Volvo Cars.
- Novo Energy has announced layoffs as its operations are put on hold.
- The decision to pause plans was described as necessary during the current situation and not taken lightly.
- Volvo Cars continues searching for an external battery technology partner while operations are paused.
- Novo Energy was founded with the ambition to produce batteries for Volvo's electric cars in Gothenburg, Sweden.
- Novo Energy was established as a joint venture between Volvo Cars and European battery company Northvolt to develop sustainable battery solutions.
- Following Northvolt's bankruptcy filing, Volvo Cars took on full ownership of Novo Energy.
- Novo Energy was scheduled to begin production of battery cells at the Gothenburg facility this year.
- The Gothenburg facility was planned to produce up to 50GWh of battery cells annually.
- Until a new technology partner is secured, Novo Energy can no longer proceed with its operations as previously planned.
- All positions at Novo Energy have been laid off while Volvo continues its search for a partner.
- Novo Energy had a total of 75 staff members who were laid off.
- The company stated it is currently not possible to share details on when battery production could start.
- It is also not possible to share under what ownership structure future production might occur.
- Novo Energy was originally intended to produce cells for Volvo vehicles and potentially other Geely group vehicles at the Gothenburg facility.
- The Gothenburg facility was pegged to create 3,000 jobs when Novo was founded.
- A report by transport research firm NewAutomotive noted Northvolt’s insolvency as evidence of financial and execution risks in the capital-intensive battery sector.
- Europe remains heavily reliant on imports for midstream refining and precursor/anode material production.
- Securing reliable sources of critical raw materials such as lithium, cobalt, nickel, and graphite is considered of utmost importance.
- China has long held a competitive advantage in EV and energy storage battery manufacturing due to strong national supply chains.
- Global trade tensions have led China to restrict exports of some components critical to battery production.
- These trade tensions have also impacted the stationary battery energy storage market.
- The energy storage technology has benefited from significant private and government investment in Western regions due to its economic and logistical value for achieving net zero.
- The quick return on investment (ROI) of energy storage deployment has caused many formerly EV-focused initiatives to switch to storage.
- Volvo’s difficulty recruiting a technology partner may stem from an increasing reluctance to invest in the EV sector due to legislative backtracking on ICE vehicle transitions.
- All European-founded battery companies have struggled recently due to softened EV demand, competition from China and the US, and a lack of expertise in battery cell manufacturing.
- Many battery tech firms are currently occupied with or seeking cell manufacturing supply deals with the storage sector, which offers more certain long-term investment.
