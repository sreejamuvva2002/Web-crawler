# Aurubis Richmond Facility

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-24

**Source:** [Aurubis Richmond: First U.S. Multimetal Recycling Plant Starts Production - Expansion Solutions](https://www.expansionsolutionsmagazine.com/aurubis-richmond-first-u-s-multimetal-recycling-plant-starts-production/)  ·  `page_017280`

## Facts

- Aurubis AG announced the official launch of production at its new Aurubis Richmond facility in Richmond County, Georgia on Sept. 24, 2025.
- The facility is the first multimetal recycling plant in the United States.
- Total investment for the facility is $800 million.
- The plant will create more than 240 jobs in the Augusta area.
- The site will process up to 180,000 tonnes of complex recycling material annually.
- Additional expansion at the facility is planned for 2026.
- The facility will recover copper, nickel, tin, and precious metals from complex recycling materials, including printed circuit boards and copper cables.
- Recovered metals will help strengthen domestic supply for industries such as energy infrastructure, data centers, AI applications, and the defense sector.
- Dr. Toralf Haag, CEO of Aurubis AG, stated that with Aurubis Richmond, the company is the frontrunner in multimetal recycling and processing in the U.S., a position it already holds in Europe.
- Dr. Toralf Haag noted that strong availability of recycling material and favorable conditions in Georgia offer tremendous opportunities for Aurubis in the U.S. market.
- Georgia Gov. Brian P. Kemp stated in a video message that Aurubis Richmond is now a significant employer for Georgia and plays a major role in the American multimetal supply chain.
- Georgia Gov. Brian P. Kemp added that the facility stands as a testament to American manufacturing, independence, and resiliency.
- U.S. Rep. Rick W. Allen (GA-12) stated that Aurubis Richmond is a strategic site that reinforces America’s ability to supply itself with metals essential to national security, infrastructure, and technology leadership.
- U.S. Rep. Rick W. Allen noted that the facility exemplifies how international partnerships strengthen America’s economic independence and industrial foundation.
- David Schultheis, President and Managing Director of Aurubis Richmond, stated that the facility is now an important player in the North American multimetal supply chain.
- David Schultheis expressed pride in his team’s achievement in building the state-of-the-art facility and gratitude to partners in Georgia for enabling a smooth approval process and strong community support.
- Aurubis AG is headquartered in Hamburg, Germany, and operates as a leading global provider of non-ferrous metals and one of the world’s largest copper recyclers.
- Aurubis AG produces and recycles copper, nickel, tin, precious metals, and other key industrial materials through an international network of production sites and service centers dedicated to sustainable resource management and supply chain resiliency.
