# Valeo

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Valeo reveals third-gen LiDAR for autonomous mobility | Autocar Professional](https://www.autocarpro.in/news-international/valeo-reveals-thirdgen-lidar-for-autonomous-mobility-80525?trk=article-ssr-frontend-pulse_little-text-block)  ·  `page_013060`

## Facts

- Valeo revealed its third-generation scanning LiDAR.
- The third-gen LiDAR is set to make its market debut in 2024.
- It reconstructs a 3D real-time image of the vehicle's surroundings at a rate of 4.5 million pixels and 25 frames per second.
- Geoffrey Bouquot serves as Valeo's Senior Vice-President, R&D and Strategy.
- Bouquot stated that the third-generation LiDAR is a major technological advance toward autonomous vehicles.
- The upgrade strengthens Valeo's technological and industrial leadership in the field.
- Valeo was already the only supplier on the market currently series-producing an automotive-grade LiDAR scanner.
- Bouquot said the number one goal with the device remains saving lives on the road.
- Compared to the previous generation, resolution has increased 12-fold, range 3-fold, and viewing angle 2.5-fold.
- The LiDAR can see things that humans, cameras, and radars cannot.
- It enables driving delegation to the vehicle in many situations, including Level 2 automation and above.
- It supports highway driving at speeds of up to 130kph.
- Vehicles fitted with the third-gen scanning LiDAR can manage emergency situations autonomously.
- The scanning LiDAR detects, recognizes, and classifies all objects located around the car.
- If objects are moving, it measures their speed and direction.
- It adapts to all light conditions, whether dazzlingly bright or pitch black.
- It measures the density of raindrops to calculate the right braking distance.
- It tracks nearby vehicles even when they are no longer in the driver's line of sight.
- It uses algorithms to anticipate trajectories and trigger necessary safety maneuvers.
- The LiDAR protects people inside the car and those around it, including pedestrians and cyclists.
- Beyond equipped vehicles, it will alert other vehicles of road hazards via the Cloud.
- Valeo designs and manufactures the entire system, including hardware, software, and associated artificial intelligence.
- The AI combines collected data to enable the vehicle to instantly make the right decision.
- Software automatically adapts to the environment and improves performance over time through regular updates.
- LiDARs are produced in Germany at Valeo's Wemding plant in Bavaria.
- Components are assembled with a micron level of precision at the plant.
- Production lines draw on Valeo's expertise in optics, mechanics, and photonics.
- 300 engineers at Valeo are dedicated solely to this technology.
- Over 500 patents have already been filed for this technology.
- Valeo is the first and only company to produce a scanning LiDAR on an industrial scale.
- Valeo has already produced over 150,000 units of scanning LiDAR.
- 99% of cars equipped with a scanning LiDAR scanner worldwide are equipped with a Valeo scanning LiDAR.
- Up to 30% of premium new vehicles are set to reach level 3 automation by 2030.
- Level 3 automation requires vehicles to be equipped with LiDAR technology.
- Autonomous shuttles, robotaxis, delivery droids, autonomous trucks, and agriculture, mining, and infrastructure sectors will need LiDARs.
- The LiDAR market is expected to represent more than $50 billion by 2030.
- Valeo says its new third-generation LiDAR will be a key contributor to this change in scale.
