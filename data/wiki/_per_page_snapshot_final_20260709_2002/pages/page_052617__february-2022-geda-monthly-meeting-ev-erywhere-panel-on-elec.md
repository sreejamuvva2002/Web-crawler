# February 2022 GEDA Monthly Meeting - EV-erywhere Panel on Electric Mobility Movement

**Type:** event  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [February 2022 GEDA Monthly Meeting - EV-erywhere - Georgia Economic Developers Association](https://www.geda.org/events/february-2022-geda-monthly-meeting---ev-erywhere)  ·  `page_052617`

## Facts

- GEDA assembled a knowledgeable panel of experts to offer perspective on drivers for the electric mobility business and how communities can be ready to accommodate it, covering utility and workforce needs as well as infrastructure and charging stations.
- Panelist Thomas Frey serves as Global Director of Communications for Ascend Elements, Inc., a vertically integrated lithium-ion battery recycling and engineered-materials company previously known as Battery Resourcers.
- Thomas Frey organized the announcement of North America’s largest lithium-ion battery recycling facility to be opened in Covington, Ga.
- Panelist Jeff Pratt is President of Green Power EMC, responsible for day-to-day management of a renewable energy portfolio that provides energy to 38 Electric Membership Corporations in Georgia.
- Jeff Pratt leads efforts to source, evaluate, and contract for new renewable energy projects.
- Georgia’s Electric Membership Corporations have about 1,200 MW of renewable energy in operation or under construction.
- Green Power EMC provides education materials and programs on renewable energy that Georgia’s Cooperatives employ for the benefit of their end-use members.
- Panelist Virginia Sengewald is a Project Manager on the Logistics, Energy, Agribusiness, and Food Processing (LEAF) industry team within the Global Commerce Division of the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD).
- Virginia Sengewald assists companies throughout their statewide site selection process and recruits new jobs and investment to the State of Georgia.
- Virginia Sengewald focuses on German-speaking projects in addition to her industry focus on renewable energy and the battery supply chain.
- Moderator Matt Colvin is Director of Economic Development for the University of Georgia (UGA) and serves as a top advisor to university leadership on economic development matters.
- Matt Colvin represents UGA with the Georgia Department of Economic Development and its key partners for business recruitment efforts, marketing the strengths of UGA’s talent and research pipeline across the state to potential companies looking to locate or expand operations in Georgia.
- The event was held at the Georgia Tech Hotel & Conference Center located at 800 Spring Street Northwest Atlanta, GA 30308-1015.
- Registration for the event closed at 4:00 PM on Friday, February 11, 2022.
- The event agenda included registration from 10:45 AM to 12:00 PM, networking from 11:00 AM to 12:00 PM, and a lunch and program from 12:00 PM to 1:30 PM on February 14.
- The event was sponsored by Matt Colvin (Director of Economic Development at the UGA Carl Vinson Institute of Government) and the UGA College of Engineering.
