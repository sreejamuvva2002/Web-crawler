# Wabash National Corp.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2018-11-05

**Source:** [Trucking Is Booming, So Why Is Wall Street Punishing This Stock? | The Motley Fool](https://www.fool.com/investing/2018/11/05/trucking-is-booming-so-why-is-wall-street-punishin.aspx)  ·  `page_014523`

## Facts

- Wabash National Corp. warned investors that its third-quarter 2018 financial results would come in lower than originally anticipated.
- Wabash's stock price was down 27% so far in the year of the report.
- Q3 2018 revenue was $553.1 million, compared to $612.7 million in Q2 2018 and $425.1 million in Q3 2017.
- Q3 2018 operating income was $16.5 million, down from $46.0 million in Q2 2018 and $26.5 million in Q3 2017.
- Q3 2018 diluted EPS was $0.08, compared to $0.54 in Q2 2018 and $0.30 in Q3 2017.
- Q3 2018 free cash flow was $8.5 million, down from $54.3 million in Q2 2018 and ($340 million) in Q3 2017.
- Management attributed the bottom-line decline to operating issues rather than sales problems.
- Customers were unable to pick up new trailer units because their trucks were too busy.
- Wabash experienced difficulty obtaining chassis from suppliers for its final mile segment.
- Higher commodity costs, driven by tariffs and raw material inflation, ate into margins.
- The diversified products and final mile segments posted losses in Q3 2018.
- Working capital grew significantly due to a surge in product inventories and accounts receivable.
- Wabash acquired its final mile business unit in the third quarter of 2017, which caused a large decline in free cash flow that quarter.
- Q3 2018 revenue represented the second-highest quarterly revenue in company history.
- Customer order volume boosted Wabash's backlog by 80% to $1.3 billion.
- Demand for Wabash's products is so high that neither its suppliers nor its customers can keep up with delivery timelines.
- CEO Brent Yeagy highlighted the sales boom and steps to address higher input costs due to tariffs.
- Management expects to make up for Q3 results in the fourth quarter of 2018.
- Continued strong market demand drove backlog to a new record level of $1.3 billion, an increase of 80 percent compared to the prior year quarter.
- Trailer and truck body demand remains strong, supporting belief in growing secular demand in e-commerce and home delivery.
- The company is taking steps to reduce margin impact from U.S. tariff policy, raw material inflation, supply base disruptions, and a very tight labor market for the remainder of 2018 and into 2019.
- Management expects sequential improvement in fourth quarter operating results as it manages business challenges.
- The core trailer business expects stronger pricing and a more favorable sales mix.
- Wabash maintained full-year guidance for 2018 new trailer shipments to 60,000 to 62,000 units.
- Full-year 2018 GAAP earnings guidance is set at $1.41 to $1.46 per diluted share.
- Full-year 2018 non-GAAP earnings guidance is set at $1.50 to $1.55 per diluted share.
- Wabash takes orders for products a couple of quarters in advance, meaning rising material costs after booking can eat into margins.
- The company is working on passing along rising costs to customers.
- Strong overall economic conditions and growth of e-commerce provide tailwinds for the trucking industry.
