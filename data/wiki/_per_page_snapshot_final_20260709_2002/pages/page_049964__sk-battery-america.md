# SK Battery America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-03-06

**Source:** [SK Battery Cuts 37% of Georgia Workforce - The EV Report](https://theevreport.com/sk-battery-cuts-37-of-georgia-workforce)  ·  `page_049964`

## Facts

- Nearly 1,000 workers at SK Battery America’s Commerce, Georgia, plant lost their jobs on March 6.
- The layoffs were the direct consequence of a Ford cancellation, vanishing federal incentives, and a domestic EV market that has stalled well short of industry projections.
- The layoffs are among the most visible signs yet that the U.S. battery supply chain built during the Biden era is now contracting under a very different policy environment.
- SK Battery America filed a Worker Adjustment and Retraining Notification covering 958 employees, roughly 37% of its Commerce workforce.
- The affected workers’ last day was March 6, with pay and benefits continuing through May 6.
- Ford announced in December that it was dissolving the BlueOval SK battery joint venture and canceling the fully electric F-150 Lightning due to sluggish sales.
- The dissolution is expected to generate a $19.5 billion write-down for Ford.
- SK Battery America opened the $2.6 billion Commerce plant in January 2022.
- As recently as 2023, SK Battery America was expanding its workforce by several hundred employees to keep pace with anticipated EV demand.
- Congress has eliminated consumer EV tax credits of up to $7,500.
- The Trump administration has moved toward more relaxed automotive emissions standards, creating significant uncertainty across the EV supply chain.
- EVs represented approximately 8% of U.S. new vehicle sales in 2025, roughly flat compared to the prior year.
- Major OEMs including Ford, GM, and Stellantis have all pulled back on pure BEV timelines, redirecting investment toward hybrids and more affordable entry-level EVs.
- SK and Hyundai are still jointly building a $5 billion battery factory near Cartersville, Georgia.
- Rivian’s $5 billion facility and Hyundai’s $7.6 billion manufacturing complex represent ongoing state investments that have not been pulled back.
- A company spokesperson said SK Battery America is pursuing new customers in the Battery Energy Storage System arena.
- The pivot toward stationary grid storage means demand drivers are less dependent on consumer purchase behavior and federal EV policy.
- A law firm has announced an investigation into whether SK Battery complied with WARN Act notification requirements, adding legal uncertainty to the transition.
