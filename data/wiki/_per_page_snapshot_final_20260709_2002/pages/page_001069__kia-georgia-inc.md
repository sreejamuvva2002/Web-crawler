# Kia Georgia, Inc.

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-02-24

**Source:** [Kia Georgia celebrates trio of major milestones, including Start of Production for 2027 Kia Telluride](https://www.kiamedia.com/us/en/media/pressreleases/24356/kia-georgia-celebrates-trio-of-major-milestones-including-start-of-production-for-2027-kia-telluride)  ·  `page_001069`

## Facts

- Kia Georgia marked the start of production for the all-new 2027 Kia Telluride at its West Point facility on Feb. 24, 2026.
- The ceremonial vehicle represented the first hybrid electric vehicle (HEV) assembled in the state of Georgia.
- The same vehicle marked the 5 millionth vehicle produced at Kia Georgia.
- Georgia Governor Brian P. Kemp attended the ceremony alongside team members and state/community leaders.
- Sean Yoon, President and CEO of Kia North America and Kia America, stated that Kia Georgia is the only plant in the world that builds the Telluride.
- Reaching five million vehicles and introducing Kia's first US-assembled HEV reflects Kia's commitment to building the future of mobility in Georgia, according to Sean Yoon.
- Since beginning production in 2009, Kia Georgia has grown into one of the region’s largest employers and a cornerstone of automotive manufacturing in the Southeast.
- The Telluride has been exclusively Georgia-made since its introduction in 2019.
- Governor Brian Kemp credited the vehicle's success to a proven partnership approach between Kia Georgia, the local community, and the state.
- Stuart Countess, Kia Georgia’s President and CEO, stated that producing world-class gasoline-powered vehicles, EVs, and HEVs demonstrates the plant's versatility and talent.
- Kia Georgia, Inc. is the first manufacturing site in North America for Seoul-based Kia Corporation.
- The facility has an annual production capacity of 350,000 units.
- Kia Georgia is located on 2,200 acres in West Point, Ga.
- Kia Georgia began mass production on Nov. 16, 2009.
- The plant currently produces the Telluride, Sorento (excludes HEV and PHEV models), Sportage (excludes HEV and PHEV models) SUVs, and the all-electric EV9 and EV6.
