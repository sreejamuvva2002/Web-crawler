# GeoDigital International Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** research_and_development  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [GA: GeoDigital to Move from Canada to Atlanta, Hire 50 for High-Tech Jobs | Trade and Industry Development](https://www.tradeandindustrydev.com/industry/technologyrd/news/ga-geodigital-move-canada-atlanta-hire-50-high-10569)  ·  `page_060624`

## Facts

- GeoDigital International Inc. will move its North American headquarters from Canada to Atlanta.
- The relocation will create approximately 50 new high-tech jobs in Fulton County over the next year.
- GeoDigital has been based in Ontario since its founding in 2005.
- The company will occupy an 11,200-square-foot, state-of-the-art office space in Atlantic Station.
- GDEcD Commissioner Chris Carr stated that Georgia is a hub for headquarter relocations, especially for technology companies.
- Since the beginning of the fiscal year (which concluded not even three weeks ago), GDEcD has announced ten corporate headquarter relocations and more than 4,200 technology jobs in Georgia.
- The newly created jobs will encompass executive management, information technology, research and development, sales, marketing, project management, finance and accounting, and human resources functions.
- GeoDigital plans to work with Kennesaw State University and the Georgia Institute of Technology to create apprenticeship programs to help fill future employment opportunities.
- GeoDigital operates at the forefront of the fast-growing autonomous, or self-driving, vehicle industry.
- The company uses precision technology called LiDAR, a kind of ‘laser range finder’, combined with high-definition imagery to create the industry’s most detailed 3D maps of the entire North American highway system.
- These ‘digital roads’ include data on topography, road surfaces, lanes, and other information critical to the safe and smooth driving experience of autonomous cars.
- GeoDigital is providing these mapping services to a major automaker, as well as several Tier 1 suppliers.
- The company is also using this technology to help efficiently inspect thousands of miles of power lines for asset and vegetation issues.
- GeoDigital serves the vast majority of the country’s largest utility companies, including PPL, Xcel Energy, Tennessee Valley Authority, and many Southern Company entities.
- By digitizing vegetation management, utility companies can better predict and respond to areas at risk of causing outages and better inform their crews while keeping them safer.
- GeoDigital solutions assist with compliancy with recent federal regulation to protect the nation’s power grid against vegetation outages.
- Chris Warrington, president and CEO of GeoDigital, stated that Atlanta meets their needs for greater access to high-tech talent and international travel routes due to the company's growth and market potential.
- Shirlynn Brownell, GDEcD project manager, assisted GeoDigital on behalf of Georgia and partnered with the Metro Atlanta Chamber and Georgia Power.
- Brian P. McGowan, executive vice president and chief operating officer for the Metro Atlanta Chamber, welcomed GeoDigital to the growing base of international companies choosing Atlanta as an operating hub.
- Founded in 2005, GeoDigital operates in the United States, Canada, and Australia.
- Its investors include NGP Energy Technology Partners, North Sky Capital, EnerTech Capital and Emerald Technology Ventures.
