# Motherson Yachiyo Automotive Systems Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-07-04

**Source:** [Motherson Yachiyo Automotive Systems Co., Ltd. (Formerly Yachiyo Industry Co., Ltd.) - MarkLines Automotive Industry Portal](https://www.marklines.com/en/top500/motherson-yachiyo-automotive-systems?rf=mail)  ·  `page_015558`

## Facts

- The company develops and manufactures fuel tanks and sunroofs.
- Honda announced on July 4, 2023, that it will sell its stake in consolidated subsidiary Yachiyo Industry to the Motherson Group.
- Honda will first make Yachiyo Industry Kogyo a wholly owned subsidiary through a takeover bid before transferring 81% of its voting rights to SMRC Automotive Holdings Netherlands B.V., a group company under Samvardhana Motherson International.
- The tender offer is scheduled to commence around October 2023.
- Honda aims to optimize its business portfolio and make the Motherson Group a strategic partner to expand sales channels in existing business and expand its plastic products business.
- Honda will acquire all shares of Goshi Giken Kogyo held by Yachiyo Kogyo, holding 95% of the voting rights in the company.
- Yachiyo Industry will concentrate management resources on its automotive business while Honda aims for future business growth in the motorcycle business.
- The company was delisted on January 10, 2024.
- Effective October 1, 2024, the company changed its name to Motherson Yachiyo Automotive Systems Co., Ltd.
- After Honda transfers stock to the Motherson Group, the Motherson Group's voting rights are forecast to be 81%, while Honda's are forecast to be 19%.
- Product lines include sunroofs, plastic fuel tanks, steel fuel tanks, hydrogen tanks for FCVs, plastic parts, bumpers, instrument panels, and CFRP products.
- Eiichi Ohtake established Ohtake Paint Shop in Tokyo in April 1947 to paint machine parts, later painting motorcycle components alongside Honda's expansion.
- Yachiyo Painting Co., Ltd. was established in August 1953 for baking finish business, setting up the Narimasu Plant in Saitama (closed June 1962) as a plant for Honda.
- The Suzuka Plant was established in Mie Prefecture in May 1960 as a buffing and painting shop for motorcycle parts.
- The company name changed to Yachiyo Industry Co., Ltd. in September 1968.
- The Kashiwabara Plant was established in Saitama in August 1972, starting assembly of Honda's mini-cars on an outsourcing basis and making the company a Honda affiliate.
- Yachiyo Press Industry Co., Ltd. merged with Shimizu Seisakusho in April 1973.
- Goshi Giken Co., Ltd. was established as a joint venture with Honda and Sankei Giken Kougyou Co., Ltd. in November 1974.
- The company started producing buggies for Honda at the Kashiwabara Plant in February 1976.
- Fuel tank production as automotive parts began in February 1977.
- Head office relocated to Toshima Ward, Tokyo, in July 1981.
- Yokkaichi Factory was established in Mie Prefecture in June 1983 for stamping and welding operations.
- Facilities at Yokkaichi Factory were enhanced in August 1985 to produce trucks for Honda on an outsourcing basis.
- The Tochigi R&D Center was established in Tochigi Prefecture in January 1986.
- Sunroof production started as part of the automotive-parts range in June 1986.
- Yachiyo of Ontario Mfg., Inc., a consolidated subsidiary, was established in Canada in January 1989.
- Contract production of Honda's mini passenger cars started on a subcontracted basis in May 1996.
- Yachiyo Industry (U.K.) Ltd. and Unipart Yachiyo Technology Ltd. were established in the UK in June 1996, with the latter being a joint venture with several local companies.
- Siam Yachiyo Co., Ltd., a consolidated subsidiary, was established in Thailand in January 1997.
- Head office relocated to Sayama, Saitama Prefecture, in May 1997.
- Yachiyo of America Inc. (consolidated subsidiary) was established in the U.S.A. in September 1997.
- AY Mfg., Ltd. (consolidated subsidiary) was established in the U.S.A. through its local subsidiary in October 1997.
- US Yachiyo Inc. (consolidated subsidiary) was established in the U.S.A. in January 1999.
- Yachiyo Manufacturing of Alabama LLC was established in the USA in September 2000.
- Yachiyo Zhongshan Mfg. Co., Ltd. was established in China in April 2002.
- The company purchased shares of Goshi Giken Co., Ltd. in August 2002, making it a subsidiary and bringing its subsidiaries under Yachiyo.
- The company cancelled its registration with the Japan Securities Dealers Association for over-the-counter trading and listed shares on the JASDAC Stock Exchange in December 2004.
- Yachiyo Wuhan Mfg. Co., Ltd. (consolidated subsidiary) was established in China in July 2005.
- The company acquired equities of AY Mfg. and Ltd. in the United States in October 2005, making it a consolidated subsidiary.
- Honda Motor Co., Ltd. became the Company's parent company in December 2006 after purchasing shares through a takeover bid.
- YG Tech Co., Ltd. (consolidated subsidiary) was established in Mie Prefecture, Japan, in April 2008 through a joint-investment pooled by Group companies.
- Yachiyo India Manufacturing Private Limited (consolidated subsidiary) was established in India in April 2008.
- Goshi India Auto Parts Private Limited (consolidated subsidiary) started operations in India in December 2008.
- Yachiyo de Brasil Industria e Comercio de Pecas Ltda. (consolidated subsidiary) was established in Brazil in September 2009.
- Yachiyo Mexico Manufacturing S.A. de C.V. (consolidated subsidiary) was established in Guanajuato, Mexico, in February 2012.
- The Saitama R&D Center was established in Sayama, Saitama Prefecture, in April 2012.
- P.T. Yachiyo Trimitra Indonesia was established in May 2012.
- Yachiyo of Ontario Manufacturing, Inc. in Canada transferred its sheet-metal business to Matcor-Matsu Group of Companies in June 2014.
- Stamping operations of Yachiyo Manufacturing of Alabama, LLC (U.S.A) were acquired by Unipres in October 2014.
- In October 2014, plastic fuel tank operations were transferred to a new production plant in Georgia.
- The company adopted the new name Yachiyo Manufacturing of America, LLC in October 2014.
- Yachiyo Germany GmbH was established in December 2014.
- A basic agreement was reached with Topre in May 2015 to start discussions for Yachiyo to transfer stamping operations in Japan to Topre.
- All operations with H-One at UYT Ltd. (an affiliate company in the U.K.) were transferred to N Press Assembly Limited in June 2015.
- The transfer of business operations of YG Tech was completed to Topre in January 2016, ending the Company’s operations in the sheet-metal business.
- Yachiyo Industries Yokkaichi Works Co., Ltd. was consigned the vehicle-assembly business and started operations in March 2018.
- All stock shares of Yachiyo Industries Yokkaichi Works Co., Ltd. were transferred to Honda Motor Co. in April 2018, ending its consignment of assembling light vehicles.
- The company announced in July 2023 that it will sell Yachiyo Industry, a consolidated subsidiary, to the Motherson Group based in India.
- The company was renamed Motherson Yachiyo Automotive Systems Co., Ltd. in October 2024.
