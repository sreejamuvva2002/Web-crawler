# FREYR Battery

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [FREYR Battery to open landmark assembly plant in Atlanta - FactoryFuture](https://www.factoryfuture.com/freyr-battery-to-open-landmark-assembly-plant-in-atlanta/)  ·  `page_054971`

## Facts

- FREYR Battery has announced that it has chosen and completed the purchase of a site in Coweta County, Georgia for its planned Giga America battery plant.
- The company selected the Bridgeport Industrial Park site after a rigorous evaluation process based on key operational, logistical, and financial criteria, including the feasibility of co-locating modular upstream and downstream projects with the Giga America cell manufacturing facility.
- The initial phase of the Giga America project is set to be a cell production module of approximately 34 GWh based on the next-generation of 24M’s U.S.-based SemiSolidTM production platform at an initial projected capital cost of $1.7 billion.
- The company is carrying out an assessment of value accretive upstream and downstream modules as well as additional cell production lines which are expected to bring total capital investments to more than $2.6 billion through 2029.
- When all of the contemplated construction phases are fully completed, the Giga America complex is expected to be one of the largest battery cell manufacturing developments in the world.
- FREYR’s Co-Founder and Chief Executive Officer Tom Einar Jensen stated that the landmark announcement underscores FREYR’s ambition to develop a very strong and near-term operational footprint in the United States.
- Expanding into the U.S. has been a foundational aspect of FREYR’s long-term strategy from its inception, and with the recent passage of the Inflation Reduction Act, the company expects U.S. demand for ESS, passenger EV and other electric mobility applications to grow rapidly over the next decade.
- Governor Brian Kemp welcomed the deal, noting that job creators and innovators bring operations to Georgia because they know they will have success there.
- Governor Brian Kemp expressed pride in welcoming FREYR to the Peach State as the latest company to bring transformational investments and opportunity to communities, calling them a valued addition to the No. 1 state for business and looking forward to their growing impact on Coweta and the surrounding region.
- Coweta County Board of Commissioners Chairman Bob Blackburn welcomed FREYR Battery to the community, stating that Coweta County is quickly becoming a premier destination for top-tier manufacturing industries that support the U.S. economy and beyond.
- In accordance with ambitions to accelerate development of Giga America in parallel with the ongoing construction of Giga Arctic in Mo i Rana, Norway, the company is intensifying and broadening its financing efforts.
- The total package of financial incentives for Giga America includes robust assistance collectively provided by The State of Georgia and Coweta County in addition to expected production tax credits associated with the recently passed Inflation Reduction Act.
- FREYR intends to apply with the U.S. Department of Energy for packages that could include a grant and/or direct loans to assist with the development of Giga America.
- The U.S. energy storage market is already the largest in the world and is expected to experience exponential growth over the next several years.
- According to Rystad Energy, to satisfy a 1.6-degree scenario, cumulative collective storage demand of 4.5 TWh from the U.S. and Europe will be required through 2030.
- Under the same scenario, Rystad expects annual collective storage demand in the U.S. and Europe to reach 1.4 TWh by 2030, representing a 34-fold increase from projected corresponding annual 2022 demand.
- FREYR is deeply committed to decarbonizing the transportation and energy sectors and expects to make meaningful investments to spur job creation and develop localized, decarbonized supply chains in the U.S. to enhance energy security and economic activity.
- The Giga America project will be based on the SemiSolidTM technology of FREYR’s U.S.-based licensing partner, 24M.
- The SemiSolidTM platform enables capital and energy efficient production of lithium-ion batteries at scale.
- FREYR is currently evaluating clean power supply solutions for the Giga America project with U.S. Independent Power Producers (IPPs).
- The company’s long-term ambition is to replicate the strategy employed at Giga Arctic to produce next-generation batteries that are among the cleanest anywhere in the world.
