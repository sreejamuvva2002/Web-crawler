# NVH Korea

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-06-22

**Source:** [Auto Parts Manufacturer NVH Korea to Locate in Locust Grove](https://henryga.news/2023/06/22/auto-parts-manufacturer-nvh-korea-to-locate-in-locust-grove/)  ·  `page_047588`

## Facts

- Governor Kemp’s office announced on Thursday morning, June 22, that auto parts manufacturer NVH Korea will be locating in Locust Grove.
- The company produces battery parts for electric vehicles.
- The announcement represents Henry County’s first addition in EV manufacturing.
- Electric vehicle production is a growing sector of new manufacturing in Georgia.
- Multiple new vehicle plants are coming to the state.
- NVH Korea will invest $72 million dollars and create over 160 new jobs in Locust Grove.
- The facility will be located at Gardner Logistics Park off Colvin Drive.
- The facility expects to begin operations in the second quarter of 2024.
- The new facility will supply battery parts for electric vehicles in the United States for manufacturers such as KIA and Hyundai Motor Group.
- The facility will produce EV battery system components that protect, connect and sense the performance of battery cells.
- NVH Korea is an automotive parts manufacturer based in the City of Ulsan and was established in 1984.
- The company specializes in automotive noise, vibration, and heat-control parts.
- The company has current production facilities in Korea, Europe, and India.
- The company operates a manufacturing facility in Columbus that produces floor mats, cargo mats, and cargo trays.
- Chairman and CEO Ja Kyum Koo stated that Georgia was chosen as the company’s first EV battery component manufacturing site and that cooperation between state agencies and company staff helped guide the project.
- Chairman and CEO Ja Kyum Koo said the project is the first step in contributing to the electrification of the mobility industry and expressed a desire to join the community of Locust Grove and Henry County.
- The announcement by NVH Korea is the latest in a series of electric vehicle manufacturing projects coming to Georgia.
- Since 2020, Georgia has attracted more than $22.7 billion in investments and created over 28,400 new jobs in the EV sector.
- Two new vehicle plants headlining recent announcements are Rivian near Social Circle and Hyundai near Savannah.
- Each of the Rivian and Hyundai plants represents several billion dollars in investment and thousands of jobs.
- Several new battery plants are also building in various parts of the state.
