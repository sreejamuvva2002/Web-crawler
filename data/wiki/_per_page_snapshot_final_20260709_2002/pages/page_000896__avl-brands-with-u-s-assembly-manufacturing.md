# AVL Brands with U.S. Assembly & Manufacturing

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-07-15

**Source:** [AVL Brands with U.S. Assembly & Manufacturing](https://www.solarinsure.com/avl-brands-us)  ·  `page_000896`

## Facts

- In response to evolving tariff regulations and growing demand for domestically sourced equipment, a curated list of AVL brands has been compiled whose products are either assembled in the U.S. or fully manufactured in the U.S.
- The 'Manufactured in the U.S.' designation means products are designed, built, and produced in the U.S. with a majority of U.S.-sourced components.
- The 'Assembled in the U.S.' designation means final product assembly, testing, and quality control are conducted in U.S.-based facilities, though some components may be globally sourced.
- Generac designs and manufactures its battery systems and power electronics in Wisconsin.
- Generac maintains full U.S. production oversight to ensure quality and performance for its durable energy solutions.
- Tesla manufactures its Powerwall and other energy storage products at Gigafactory 1 in Nevada.
- Tesla utilizes a vertically integrated process to produce cells, modules, and packs domestically.
- Qcells operates one of the largest solar module manufacturing plants in the Western Hemisphere.
- Qcells has end-to-end solar panel production based in Dalton, Georgia.
- Sonnen manufactures its advanced home battery systems at its InnovationHub in Georgia.
- The Sonnen facility handles full production, including system integration and testing.
- Fortress Power assembles its lithium iron phosphate battery systems in Pennsylvania.
- Fortress Power combines imported cells with in-house quality control and system integration.
- HomeGrid’s energy storage systems are assembled in Nevada using a modular, stackable design.
- U.S. assembly of HomeGrid products allows for shorter lead times and greater supply chain stability.
- Mission Solar Energy assembles high-performance solar panels at its facility in South San Antonio’s Brooks campus.
- The Mission Solar facility spans 246,000 sq ft.
