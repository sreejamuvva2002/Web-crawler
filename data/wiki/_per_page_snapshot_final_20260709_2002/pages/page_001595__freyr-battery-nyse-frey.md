# FREYR Battery (NYSE: FREY)

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-02-10

**Source:** [FREYR kills plans to build a $2.6 billion battery factory in Georgia | Electrek](https://electrek.co/2025/02/10/freyr-battery-factory-georgia/)  ·  `page_001595`

## Facts

- FREYR Battery has scrapped plans to build a $2.6 billion battery energy storage system factory in Coweta County, Georgia.
- The company told officials in the Atlanta suburb of Newnan on Thursday that it would not build the “Giga America” battery factory in Georgia.
- The planned Georgia factory was expected to employ more than 700 people.
- FREYR had been incentivized by the Biden administration’s Inflation Reduction Act tax credits and robust renewables growth.
- The company moved its headquarters from Norway to Georgia and announced the battery factory in 2023.
- FREYR cited climbing interest rates, falling battery prices, and a change in leadership as reasons for canceling the project, or stated it was a “realignment of near-term strategic goals” in a letter to the county authority.
- The company entered into a definitive agreement to sell the Georgia factory’s 368-acre site to an undisclosed party for gross sales proceeds of $50 million.
- The site sale transaction is expected to close on February 15.
- Estimated net proceeds to FREYR from the sale are expected to total $22.5 million following repayment of previously received state and local grants.
- In August, then-CEO Tom Einar Jensen told investors that a surplus of cheap Chinese batteries had made it harder to raise money to manufacture batteries.
- The company switched its strategic focus from battery manufacturing to solar panel manufacturing.
- On December 24, FREYR announced it had closed its acquisition of Trina Solar’s 5-gigawatt (GW), 1.35 million-square-foot solar panel factory in Wilmer, Texas.
- The Texas acquisition solidified FREYR’s transformation from a European battery company into a US solar company.
- The company announced it is moving its global corporate headquarters to Austin.
