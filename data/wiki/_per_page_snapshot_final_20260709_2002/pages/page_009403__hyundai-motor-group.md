# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Georgia Governor to Visit Korea, Requests Meeting with Hyundai Amid Detention Incident - The Asia Business Daily](https://www.asiae.co.kr/en/article/2025092209475489972)  ·  `page_009403`

## Facts

- Georgia Governor Brian Kemp will visit South Korea soon and has requested a meeting with Hyundai Motor Company officials.
- The governor's office sent an email in Korean and English on September 8 emphasizing that Hyundai Motor Company is an important investor and partner in Georgia.
- The email was sent four days after U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) conducted a raid on September 4 at the Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution battery joint venture construction site in Ellabell, Georgia.
- The ICE raid arrested a total of 475 people, including 317 Koreans.
- The detained employees returned to Korea on a chartered flight on September 12.
- Interviews with the repatriated employees have been released, leading to deteriorating public sentiment in Korea.
- President Donald Trump initially stated that ICE's actions were 'legally conducted.'
- President Trump asserted the need for skilled foreign professionals via Truth Social on September 14.
- Governor Kemp's office clarified that the visit to South Korea had been under discussion before the September 4 ICE arrests.
- The stated purpose of the visit is to strengthen ties with Georgia's economic, educational, and cultural partners.
- Trip Tollison, President and CEO of the Savannah Economic Development Authority, is part of the delegation to Korea.
- Tollison stated that they have been working to strengthen the relationship between Georgia and Hyundai and that this visit will be no different.
- The specific schedule for the visit has not been disclosed but is expected around October.
- An international conference involving governors from other southern U.S. states is scheduled in Japan on October 28-29, and Governor Kemp is highly likely to attend.
- On September 16, Governor Kemp stated that the incident is not just Hyundai's problem and that the U.S. visa system needs a thorough review.
- This marked Governor Kemp's first public comment since the ICE raid.
- Kemp added that Georgia is currently discussing the visa issue with the White House.
- Kemp emphasized that the incident will not discourage foreign companies from investing in Georgia.
- In an interview with the Savannah Morning News on September 17, Kemp said, 'We rely on Koreans, and we want them to return in time for the plant schedule.'
- Philip Reiner, spokesperson for the Economic Development Authority, stated that detained LG employees were in the U.S. temporarily to install equipment, provide support, and train staff.
- Reiner stressed that the detained LG employees are skilled technicians with professional expertise.
- Hyundai Motor Group announced its intention to continue investing in the U.S. despite recent incidents.
- On September 18, Hyundai Motor Company announced at an event in New York that it plans to invest 77.3 trillion won over the next five years.
- The planned investment includes expanding production capacity at plants in Georgia and Alabama and increasing the number of hybrid models in the U.S. market.
- Hyundai Motor Group expects construction of its electric vehicle battery plant in Georgia to be delayed by at least two to three months due to the ICE crackdown.
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) plans to hold a public recruitment event at Savannah Technical College campus in Georgia on September 30.
- This recruitment event is HMGMA's first public recruitment since the September 4 ICE raid.
- A local American employee highlighted Hyundai Motor Company's contribution to local employment and the economy in an opinion piece published on September 18.
- The employee explained that foreign professionals help with the initial production process through equipment installation and staff training.
- Hyundai Motor Group Chairman Chung Euisun took a commemorative photo with attendees at the completion ceremony of HMGMA on March 26 in Ellabel, Georgia.
- Attendees at the March 26 ceremony included former Georgia Governor Sonny Perdue, current Georgia Governor Brian Kemp, and Jose Munoz, President and CEO of Hyundai Motor Company.
