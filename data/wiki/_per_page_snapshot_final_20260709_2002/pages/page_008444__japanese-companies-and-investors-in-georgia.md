# Japanese Companies and Investors in Georgia

**Type:** event  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** 2024-01-24

**Source:** ['Japan Day' at Capitol Celebrates Impact of Japanese Investors, Consulate’s 50 Years in Georgia - Global Atlanta](https://www.globalatlanta.com/japan-day-at-capitol-celebrates-impact-of-japanese-investors-consulates-50-years-in-georgia/)  ·  `page_008444`

## Facts

- Georgia hosted a blockbuster reception in Tokyo in October to mark a half-century of engagement through its economic development office there.
- Japan Day at the Capitol was a half-day celebration held on Tuesday of the economic and cultural impact of Japanese companies in Georgia.
- Japanese companies are responsible for an estimated 40,000 jobs in Georgia.
- The Consulate General of Japan opened in Atlanta in 1974.
- Then-Gov. Jimmy Carter decided to put a physical presence in Japan in response to budding investment from companies like YKK and Murata.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson highlighted how Japanese firms have spread to "every single corner" of Georgia.
- Current Gov. Brian Kemp praised Consul General Mio Maeda and his team for their persistent presence in the state.
- Gov. Kemp presented the country’s top diplomat in the Southeast U.S. with a proclamation.
- International investment accounted for 47 percent of the jobs and 65 percent of the capital pledged to Georgia in the latest fiscal year, according to Gov. Kemp.
- Georgia broke its previous record for total trade in 2022 by some $5 billion, reaching $196 billion in imports and exports.
- Consul General Mio Maeda was presented with resolutions in both the House and Senate celebrating the 50th anniversary of the consulate.
- The event was part of activities organized by the Japanese community in partnership with the Georgia-Japan Legislative Caucus.
- The event included exhibition tables featuring products and brochures from Georgia-invested Japanese companies.
- Guests received bottles of made-in-Georgia Marukan rice vinegar and Yakult yogurt drinks, which will soon be manufactured in a $300 million-plus factory in Cartersville.
- An orange Kubota vehicle was displayed outside the Capitol.
- An Indy Car sponsored by Hitachi Astemo and a Yamaha side-by-side were displayed under a white tent.
- Commissioner Pat Wilson noted that each job created represents hope and opportunity for a family in the state.
- Consul General Mio Maeda credited the companies and the Japanese community they’ve helped build for the staying power of the consulate.
- Consul General Mio Maeda offered condolences to victims of the earthquake in western Japan’s Ishikawa prefecture that killed more than 200 and left more than 20,000 people displaced on New Year’s day.
- Gov. Brian Kemp echoed the sentiment regarding the earthquake.
- Key members of the Georgia-Japan Legislative Caucus offered remarks, including State Rep. Teri Anulewicz (D-Smyrna), State Sen. Kay Kirkpatrick (R-Cobb) and State Rep. David Knight (R-Griffin), who chairs the group.
- Students from the International Charter Academy of Georgia, a state charter school in Gwinnett that offers dual immersion in Japanese and English, closed the day with a song on the outside steps of the capitol building.
- Former Gov. Nathan Deal was on hand for the event and was set to be honored during a conferment ceremony for his Order of the Rising Sun designation during a reception at Consul General Maeda’s residence later in the evening.
- The Order of the Rising Sun decoration, which recognizes contributions to the U.S.-Japan relationship, was announced in December.
- Japanese companies with a presence at the Capitol included Marukan Vinegar Inc., Miura America Co., Ltd., Mizuno USA, MUFG Bank, TOTO USA, Asahi Biosciences, Inc., Canon Solutions America, Inc., Hitachi Astemo Americas, Inc., Hoshizaki America, Kubota Manufacturing of America Corp., Shimizu America, Yakult USA, Yamaha Motor Corp. U.S.A, Yamato Transport U.S.A., Inc., and YKK.
