## Facts

- President Biden toured Volvo Group's powertrain operations in Hagerstown, Maryland on National Manufacturing Day.
- Martin Weissburg, President of Volvo Group North America and President of Mack Trucks, welcomed President Biden to the site.
- Over the past 10 years, the Volvo Group has invested nearly $320 million in the site.
- The Hagerstown campus produces engines, transmissions and axles for Mack trucks, Volvo trucks, Prevost coaches and Volvo buses sold in North America.
- It also recently introduced the production of the modular powerboxes (MPBs) that are at the heart of the Group’s new zero-emission battery electric vehicles, the Volvo VNR Electric and Mack LR Electric models.
- Marcus Minkkinen, vice president and general manager of Volvo Group Powertrain Operations in Hagerstown, stated that President Biden’s visit to their Hagerstown site underscores the importance of production to the U.S. economy.
- Marcus Minkkinen noted that the visit is helping to inspire others to pursue careers in production.
- Marcus Minkkinen praised the site as wonderful work on a modernized facility that is transforming the transportation industry.
- Martin Weissburg stated that National Manufacturing Day recognises fashion manufacturing and their Hagerstown campus is an example, providing complex transportation technologies to meet visitors’ desires today and in the future.
- The Volvo Group fosters prosperity through shipping and infrastructure responses, providing trucks, buses, structural equipment, force responses for marine and commercial applications, financing and increasing the availability and productivity of consumers.
- Founded in 1927, the Volvo Group is committed to shaping the long-term landscape of sustainable shipping and infrastructure responses.
- The Volvo Group is headquartered in Gothenburg, Sweden, employs approximately 100,000 other people and serves consumers in more than 190 markets.
- Headquartered in Greensboro, North Carolina, Volvo Group North America employs nearly 17,000 other people in North America and operates 15 production and renewal services in Canada, Mexico and the United States.
- In 2021, the Volvo Group’s global net sales amounted to around US$43 billion.
- Volvo’s shares are indexed on Nasdaq Stockholm.
