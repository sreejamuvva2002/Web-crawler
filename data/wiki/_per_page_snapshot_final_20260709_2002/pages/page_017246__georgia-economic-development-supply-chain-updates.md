# Georgia Economic Development & Supply Chain Updates

**Type:** unknown  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2026-04-30

**Source:** [Economic Development Around the State - Georgia Trend Magazine](https://www.georgiatrend.com/2026/04/30/new-businesses-in-georgia-expanding-businesses/)  ·  `page_017246`

## Facts

- Georgia surpassed $60.2 billion in exports in calendar year 2025, marking the fifth consecutive year of increases and a 68.9% increase over the past decade.
- Georgia goods are exported to 213 unique countries and territories, with Canada, Mexico, Singapore, Germany, and the Netherlands as top destinations.
- Civilian aircraft and ancillary parts remain the state’s No. 1 export category, followed by computers, hardware and storage units; motor vehicles; network communications equipment; and medical devices.
- Yamaha Motor Co. Ltd. is relocating its U.S. headquarters from California to Kennesaw, with the shift beginning in 2026 and continuing through 2028.
- Yamaha produces motorized products including ATVs, motorcycles, boat engines, and personal watercraft, and already employs more than 2,300 people across several facilities throughout Georgia.
- The Georgia Ports Authority opened a new $134 million inland port in Gainesville with an annual capacity of 200,000 containers.
- The Gainesville inland port will feature direct rail service five days a week between Northeast Georgia and Savannah, providing an alternative to the 600-mile roundtrip truck route and reducing the number of trucks on Georgia’s highways.
- Swiss company PFIFFNER Group is investing $18.5 million to open a manufacturing facility in Heard County, creating up to 60 new jobs over 10 years.
- The PFIFFNER facility will be operational in 2027 and will supply instrument transformers for high-voltage substations and transformer bushings for manufacturers and utilities.
