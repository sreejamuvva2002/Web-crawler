# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-10-25

**Source:** [Hyundai breaks ground on $5.5 billion, 8,100-job factory in Bryan County | Georgia Public Broadcasting](https://www.gpb.org/news/2022/10/25/hyundai-breaks-ground-on-55-billion-8100-job-factory-in-bryan-county)  ·  `page_009478`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group officially broke ground Tuesday on its planned $5.5 billion electric vehicle factory in Bryan County.
- The project is poised to become the largest economic development project in Georgia history.
- It will be the South Korean automaker's first fully dedicated facility for EV manufacturing.
- Hyundai global president and chief operating officer José Muñoz stated, “We heard the clarion call of this administration to hasten the adoption of new electric vehicles and reduce carbon emissions — so, we took action.”
- The factory is dubbed by Hyundai as the “Metaplant.”
- The company says it will create more than 8,100 jobs in Bryan County.
- The site is located just west of Savannah.
- Dozens of business leaders and government officials attended the groundbreaking ceremony.
- U.S. Sen. Raphael Warnock and Gov. Brian Kemp attended the event.
- Gov. Brian Kemp remarked, “This project is just incredibly monumental in our state,” citing low unemployment and record job creation and investment in Georgia.
- Warnock was not included in the list of speakers at the ceremony.
- South Korea's ambassador to the U.S., Taeyong Cho, spoke at the event.
- Ambassador Cho noted that when President Biden visited South Korea in May, Hyundai Chairman Chong promised to build electric vehicle and battery plants in the United States.
- Cho stated, “Today, Hyundai is delivering on that commitment.”
- Cho expressed support for President Biden's efforts to address climate change through the Inflation Reduction Act.
- Cho criticized a provision of the Inflation Reduction Act that limits electric vehicle consumer tax credits to only EV models made in North America.
- Cho warned that Korean companies are at risk of being disadvantaged by this tax credit provision.
- Cho stated, “I believe it is not good for [the] Korea-U.S. partnership, for the state of Georgia, and not even for the common cause of climate change by limiting the consumer choices.”
- Cho said South Korea and the U.S. are working very hard to find a workable solution regarding the tax credits.
- The two governments will exert maximum efforts in the next few months to address the issue.
- Hyundai plans to manufacture about 300,000 vehicles per year at its Metaplant in Bryan County.
- The automaker does not currently create any EVs in North America.
- Warnock voted for the Inflation Reduction Act in August.
- Warnock has since introduced legislation that would effectively extend the tax credits to Hyundai.
