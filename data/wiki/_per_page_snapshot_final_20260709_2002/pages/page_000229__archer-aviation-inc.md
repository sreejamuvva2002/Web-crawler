# Archer Aviation Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Archer Aviation Showcases Midnight Aircraft In Atlanta As It Nears Completion Of Its High-Volume Manufacturing Facility In Covington, Georgia](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20240731140056/en/Archer-Aviation-Showcases-Midnight-Aircraft-In-Atlanta-As-It-Nears-Completion-Of-Its-High-Volume-Manufacturing-Facility-In-Covington-Georgia)  ·  `page_000229`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation Inc. (NYSE: ACHR) is constructing a high-volume manufacturing facility in Covington, Georgia.
- The facility's buildout spans nearly 400,000 square feet.
- Construction remains on track to be completed by the end of 2024.
- Archer completed the first build phase earlier this year, which included grading and foundation work.
- The company is nearing completion of phase two, involving the erection of exterior walls and roof.
- Phase three (interior fit out) and phase four (equipment load in and power on) are on track to be completed in the coming months.
- Once complete, the facility will support production of up to 650 Midnight aircraft annually in partnership with Stellantis.
- This production volume makes it one of the largest manufacturing facilities by volume in the aviation industry.
- Archer recently showcased its Midnight aircraft at Atlantic Aviation’s new terminal at DeKalb–Peachtree Municipal Airport in Atlanta.
- Guests at the showcase included government officials from Georgia’s Office of the Governor, Georgia’s Congressional Offices, the City of Atlanta, Georgia’s State Legislature, the Federal Aviation Administration, and other local governments.
- Archer’s buildout is taking place in close partnership with Stellantis to mass-produce the Midnight aircraft together.
- Adam Goldstein, CEO and founder of Archer, stated that the company aims to be the first in the eVTOL industry to achieve scaled manufacturing.
- The Midnight aircraft is built around Archer's proprietary electric powertrain.
- It is designed to offer passengers a sustainable, low-noise, and safe alternative to ground transportation.
- The aircraft travels at speeds up to 150 mph.
- It is designed for back-to-back flights of 20 to 50 miles with minimal charge time in between.
- The Midnight is 100 times quieter than a helicopter at cruising altitudes and nearly inaudible from the streets below.
- The aircraft features redundant systems, allowing Archer to target safety levels similar to commercial airliners.
- It is designed to carry a pilot, up to four passengers, and carry-on luggage.
