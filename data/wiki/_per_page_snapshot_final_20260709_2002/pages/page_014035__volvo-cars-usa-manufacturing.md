# Volvo Cars USA Manufacturing

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-24

**Source:** [Volvo Expands Operations at its Charleston Plant and Updates the 2026 EX90 — CleanTechnica Field Trip - CleanTechnica](https://cleantechnica.com/2025/09/24/volvo-expands-operations-at-its-charleston-plant-and-updates-the-2026-ex90/)  ·  `page_014035`

## Facts

- Volvo’s manufacturing plant is located just outside Charleston, South Carolina.
- The facility is Volvo’s first manufacturing plant in the US.
- The factory exclusively produces the fully electric EX90 and the Polestar 3.
- The factory opened in 2015, with Volvo investing $1.3 billion in the plant over the last decade.
- Volvo announced plans to add capacity for production of the Volvo XC60 at the Charleston plant.
- A “next generation hybrid” vehicle will be added to production by 2030.
- The plant was built with an initial capacity plan of 150,000 vehicles per year.
- Production of the XC60 will make use of existing floor space in the factory.
- In the first 8 months of 2025, Volvo sold 27,000 XC60s in the US market.
- Producing vehicles for the domestic market in South Carolina optimizes Volvo’s logistics footprint, lowers logistics costs, reduces emissions, and repurposes existing factory space.
- Volvo did not share the specific model name, planned investment amount, or expected number of jobs for the next-generation hybrid.
- Production of the next-generation hybrid will be online by 2030.
- Volvo CEO Håkan Samuelsson stated the next-generation hybrid would be a range-extended electric vehicle.
- The hybrid vehicle will behave like an electric car with very fast acceleration, agility, and quiet operation, but will have an onboard charger to extend its range.
- The powertrain relies solely on the electric motor for propulsion until the initial battery charge is depleted, making it more of an electric car with a backup engine than a combustion car with batteries.
- Volvo stated the new model is designed to meet specific US market demands in line with its increased focus on ensuring each region has needed products.
- The vehicle will run as a true zero-emission vehicle until the initial battery charge is depleted, helping customers familiarize themselves with EVs and reinforcing Volvo's commitment to zero-emission vehicles despite federal government undermining of EVs.
- The electric-first design makes it easier to ditch the range extender in the future.
- Volvo used the event to highlight updates to the 2026 Volvo EX90, including an upgrade to 800-volt battery architecture and an updated core computer.
- Volvo will upgrade the main Nvidia Orin-based system-on-chip processor to Nvidia’s latest Drive ATX platform.
- Moving to 800-volt architecture enables faster charging, quicker 0-60 times, allows use of less copper in the high-voltage system, and enables compatibility with increasing numbers of 800-volt and 1000-volt public fast chargers.
- The EX90 is getting two processing upgrades for 2026: upgrading to Nvidia’s latest Drive ATX SoC and adding a second, redundant chip.
- The compute upgrade results in more than doubling in compute power, with an expected processing capacity of 500 TOPS.
- Volvo Chief Technology and Engineering Officer Anders Bell explained the extra compute enables a modular, vertical ADAS stack.
- New ADAS features enabled by the upgrade include emergency stop assist (monitors driver attention and safely pulls over after alerts) and an automatic e-call feature to contact authorities if the driver doesn't respond.
- Volvo is teasing the big reveal of its next fully electric model, the EX60, coming on January 21, 2026.
- There is a possibility of a completely new modular architecture being introduced alongside the EX60.
