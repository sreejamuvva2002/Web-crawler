# Adient

## Facts

- Adient is a global leader in automotive seating systems, generating revenue by designing, manufacturing, and supplying complete seats and components to major automakers worldwide.
- The company's business is structured around three geographic segments: Americas, EMEA (Europe, Middle East, Africa), and Asia, with substantial joint venture operations in China.
- Q1 2026 consolidated sales increased 4% year-over-year to $3.6 billion, driven by FX tailwinds and robust China growth offsetting North American production headwinds.
- Adjusted EBITDA rose 6% year-over-year to $207 million in Q1 2026, with margin improving to 5.7%.
- Adient's booked and pending U.S. onshoring and conquest wins now total $500 million in incremental revenue opportunity.
- Management expects $300 million of the $500 million onshoring pipeline to impact fiscal year 2027, with the remaining portion impacting fiscal year 2028.
- The onshoring pipeline includes both direct onshoring (production moving into the U.S.) and indirect business, as well as a major conquest win.
- Adient leverages its established just-in-time supply network and strong OEM relationships to position itself as a net beneficiary of the North American manufacturing reshoring trend.
- Modutech, Adient's modular seat architecture, enables higher automation, significant labor and freight cost reductions, and up to 20% total value chain savings.
- The modular seat architecture supports long-distance just-in-time delivery and can reduce floor space by 15%, enhancing supply chain resilience.
- Americas operations are focused on executing key launches including Kia Telluride and Rivian R2, and expanding automation.
- EMEA operations underperformed due to customer mix and deliberate portfolio actions, with new business wins and restructuring expected to gradually restore margins by fiscal year 2027.
- Asia's growth is led by China, where Adient is deepening ties with domestic OEMs and launching innovative programs, though new business mix in the region is margin-dilutive in the near term.
- Restructuring spend remains elevated in Europe for fiscal year 2026 but is expected to decline in fiscal year 2027 as higher-margin programs ramp.
- Adient achieved a 42% reduction in Scope 1 and 2 emissions since 2019 and sources 30% of electricity from renewables.
- The company has assessed 80% of its suppliers for sustainability, aligning with OEM responsible sourcing priorities.
- Q1 2026 free cash flow was $15 million, exceeding internal expectations due to working capital management and commercial settlement timing.
- Full-year 2026 sales guidance was raised to $14.6 billion from a prior estimate of $14.4 billion.
- Full-year 2026 adjusted EBITDA guidance was raised to $880 million from a prior estimate of $845 million.
- Full-year 2026 free cash flow guidance was raised to $125 million from a prior estimate of $90 million.
- Q2 2026 EBITDA is expected to be similar to Q1, with earnings and cash flow ramping in the second half of the year.
- Production schedules are normalizing, especially in North America, supporting volume-driven upside for the full year.
- Adient maintains a robust liquidity position of $1.7 billion and net leverage at 1.7x.
- The company executed $25 million in share repurchases during Q1 2026.
- Jerome Adams serves as President & Chief Executive Officer of Adient.
- Mark serves as Chief Financial Officer of Adient.
- Key risks include execution of complex launches, particularly in EMEA, and the pace of margin recovery in China as new business ramps with lower initial profitability.
- Prolonged European volume weakness and continued Chinese import penetration could delay restructuring benefits.
- Timing mismatches in commercial recoveries and working capital swings add cash flow volatility.
- Macroeconomic or trade policy shifts could disrupt onshoring momentum and customer production outlooks.
