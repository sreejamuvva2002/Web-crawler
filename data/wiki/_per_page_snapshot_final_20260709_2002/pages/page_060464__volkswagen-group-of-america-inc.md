# Volkswagen Group of America, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2022-07-12

**Source:** [Volkswagen Group taps Redwood Materials for battery recycling in US](https://electrek.co/2022/07/12/volkswagen-group-redwood-materials-battery-recycling/)  ·  `page_060464`

## Facts

- Volkswagen Group of America (VWGoA) announced a collaboration with Redwood Materials, Inc. to implement a battery-recycling supply chain in the United States for Volkswagen- and Audi-branded EVs.
- The collaboration represents VW’s commitment to sustainability in the United States and beyond while simultaneously expanding Redwood’s closed-loop battery supply chain.
- Volkswagen Group of America, Inc. (VWGoA) is a wholly owned subsidiary of Volkswagen AG, whose electrification strategy includes the introduction of over 25 BEVs in the United States before the end of the decade.
- VW delivered strong Q1 numbers despite global supply chain issues, leading some to believe the brand will be the EV sales leader by mid-decade.
- VWGoA is bolstering its production to support incoming EV models like the ID. Buzz and eventually a production version of its ID. Aero sedan concept.
- Volkswagen is considering a second US production facility near its current footprint in Chattanooga.
- The potential expansion would increase VW’s US production capacity to 600,000 vehicles annually.
- Plans for the new facilities also include a potential battery plant near the Chattanooga locations.
- Redwood Materials is a battery recycling and manufacturing company founded by Tesla cofounder JB Straubel in 2017.
- VW implemented a recycling agreement with Redwood Materials to create a holistic EV battery supply chain in the United States, representing “a crucial step in growing North America’s domestic EV industry.”
- The battery-recycling collaboration will initially be carried out by VWGoA’s network of over 1,000 dealers selling Volkswagen- and Audi-branded EVs.
- Redwood Materials will work directly with these dealers and VW’s US facilities to help identify end-of-life batteries and their materials.
- Identified batteries will be safely packaged up before being transported to Redwood’s own recycling facilities in Nevada.
- Redwood founder and CEO JB Straubel stated that the transition to electric transportation is coming, presenting an incredible opportunity as more batteries reach end-of-life each year.
- Straubel noted that Redwood and VWGoA share a vision to create a domestic, circular supply chain for batteries to improve the environmental footprint of lithium-ion batteries, decrease cost, and increase EV access and adoption.
- Redwood Materials currently recycles more than 6 GWh of lithium-ion batteries, equivalent to 60,000 EV batteries, each year.
- The company extracts raw materials like cobalt, copper, nickel, and lithium, then refines and remanufactures them into new battery components before delivering them back to US battery-cell manufacturers like Panasonic.
- VWGoA joins a growing list of EV manufacturers in the United States tapping Redwood Materials for battery recycling, including Nissan and Ford.
- The collaboration will also help expedite and implement prototype batteries from VW’s Battery Engineering Lab (BEL) in Chattanooga.
