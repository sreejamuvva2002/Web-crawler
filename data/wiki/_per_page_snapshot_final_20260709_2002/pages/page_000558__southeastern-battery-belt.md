# Southeastern Battery Belt

## Facts

- The Biden administration is betting big that renewing the American industrial machine will speed progress toward decarbonizing the U.S. electric grid by 2035.
- The White House wants to use clean electricity to power a mass transition to electric vehicles to tackle greenhouse gases from transportation.
- Spurred by incentives in the Inflation Reduction Act, a new Battery Belt is emerging in Michigan, Indiana, Ohio, Kentucky, Tennessee, Georgia, and the Carolinas.
- Georgia leads the country in post-IRA clean energy factory investments with over $17 billion, according to a Canary Media analysis.
- South Carolina comes in second with $9.3 billion in clean energy factory investments, followed by Michigan with $8.1 billion.
- The Southeastern Battery Belt is a quickly growing cluster of factories spanning lithium processing, battery fabrication, auto assembly, and battery recycling.
- Kings Mountain mine and nearby operations historically produced more hard-rock lithium than almost anywhere else in the world before shutting down and shipping mining jobs overseas at the close of the 20th century.
- The Foote Mineral Company opened the Kings Mountain Mine in 1938, tapping the Carolina Tin-Spodumene Belt accessible across Cleveland and Gaston counties.
- Lithium from the Kings Mountain mine was initially used for ceramics glazing and bipolar disorder pharmaceuticals before World War II.
- During World War II, scientists used white metal from the Carolina Tin-Spodumene Belt for thermonuclear weapons research as part of the Manhattan Project.
- Cold War arms race demands sustained the Kings Mountain mining operations for about half a century.
- The Kings Mountain lithium mine shut down in 1988.
- Livent’s Hallman-Beam Mine in Bessemer City shuttered in 1996.
- Albemarle, which now owns the Kings Mountain mine, has been conducting economic-feasibility studies to determine whether to revive the dormant mine.
- Newcomer Piedmont Lithium hopes to open a brand-new mine in the area, but its initial public outreach campaign failed due to neighbor concerns about mining explosions and potential destabilization of nearby water wells.
- Energy Secretary Jennifer Granholm stated that new mining operations need reforms to ensure better environmental practices and respect for local residents, especially tribal communities.
- South Korea’s SK Corporation opened a $2.6 billion battery factory in Commerce, Georgia, in early 2022.
- The SK Georgia factory ships batteries to Volkswagen’s electric ID.4 production site in Chattanooga, Tennessee, and supplies Ford’s F-150 Lightning.
- SK makes enough batteries at the Georgia facility to power 300,000 EVs annually, according to company statements.
- Republican Governor Brian Kemp’s office praised SK in January for hitting employment targets two years ahead of schedule, with 2,600 people already working there and expectations to hit 3,000 by year’s end.
- The SK Georgia facility is one of the largest single-site employers in the state.
- Norwegian company Freyr opted to fast-track development of a second factory in Coweta County, southwest of Atlanta, to take advantage of Inflation Reduction Act benefits for U.S.-based battery production.
- Freyr is making a $2.6 billion investment in Georgia in partnership with Koch Industries.
- Freyr expects to make 2.5 gigawatt-hours’ worth of batteries in 2025, ramping to 38 gigawatt-hours shortly thereafter.
- The Freyr facility should employ more than 700 people.
- Freyr licensed battery technology developed by MIT spinout 24M, which advertises a streamlined production technique that lowers factory construction costs.
- SK will team up with Hyundai to build a $5 billion factory in northern Georgia.
- Georgia appointed an economic development lead specifically dedicated to attracting business from South Korea years ago.
- Yoonie Kim started the South Korea-focused economic development job in 2006 and helped facilitate $11.5 billion of Korean investment in Georgia in 2022 alone, according to Site Selection magazine.
- The Kemp administration uses tax incentives alongside Georgia’s Quick Start worker training program to prepare workers for jobs at new factories.
- Volkswagen planted a sapling at its Chattanooga plant in July 2022 to commemorate the start of electric vehicle production there.
- The ID.4, Volkswagen’s electric SUV flagship, is largely sourced from within the U.S., with steel from Alabama and Ohio, interior parts from Indiana and South Carolina, electronics from Kentucky and North Carolina, and batteries from SK’s Georgia base.
- When VW ramped up ID.4 assembly last year, it hired 1,000 new employees and added a third shift at its Chattanooga plant.
- Another 500 hires are coming this year at the Volkswagen facility.
- Tennessee is home to some 900 auto parts suppliers.
- Roughly half the U.S. population lives within 650 miles of Nashville, per the city’s Chamber of Commerce.
- Memphis serves as a logistics hub halfway up the Mississippi River, functioning as a railroad, trucking, and global air freight hub thanks to FedEx.
- GM makes electric Cadillacs at a facility in Spring Hill, Tennessee.
- BMW has added EV production to its plant in Spartanburg, South Carolina.
- Hyundai and Kia are building new sites in Georgia.
- Electric truck startup Rivian looked to be setting up shop outside Atlanta, but the state’s incentive package has been tied up in court due to a case brought by residents opposing the project.
- Central North Carolina has attracted new factories from Toyota and Vietnamese firm VinFast.
- The Southeastern EV sector region is solidly right to work, meaning workers do not have to pay union dues even if represented by a union, a policy used to weaken union power and give automakers leverage.
- Tennessee just passed a constitutional amendment to perpetuate right-to-work status, with voters approving it by a nearly 40-point margin.
- United Auto Workers President Shawn Fain has spoken about the need to ensure union representation at new EV plants popping up in the region.
- Ascend Elements opened this spring in Covington, Georgia, as the first operational battery recycler in the region.
- The Ascend facility uses a two-story-tall wet shredder to process used battery modules that still contain flammable liquid electrolyte.
- To prevent sparking from high-speed blades grinding through metal, Ascend drops batteries through an airlock into an oxygen-purged space.
- Chopped battery bits run through a room-sized rotating kiln to cook off the liquid electrolyte, which is condensed, cleaned, and sold back to battery makers.
- Remnants are separated by density and particle size into aluminum chunks, copper, magnetic bits, and black mass powder containing electrode metals.
- Ascend churns out black mass by the 1,000-kilogram sack.
- A parallel processing line takes in scrap materials from nearby battery factories, including SK’s, to produce the same powdery output.
- Scrap currently drives most inputs at Ascend’s plant, but the balance is expected to flip as more batteries reach retirement age.
- Ascend sells its black mass back to battery manufacturers for proprietary processing.
- Ascend is breaking ground on a $1 billion factory in southern Kentucky that will use a hydro-to-cathode technique to pull exact element ratios for each battery maker.
- Ascend just announced a $1 billion deal to supply an unnamed U.S. battery manufacturer.
- Redwood Materials, launched by Tesla co-founder JB Straubel, is building a $3.5 billion recycling plant near Charleston.
- The Redwood plant will receive materials from Battery Belt factories and import metals through the Port of Charleston.
- By 2025, Redwood plans to produce 100 gigawatt-hours per year of cathode active materials and anode foil annually across its U.S. operations, supplying approximately 1 million electric vehicles.
- Nearly all anode and cathode production supplying U.S. battery cell manufacturers currently occurs overseas in Asia.
- Former Tennessee Governor Phil Bredesen’s economic development lead Matt Kisber stated that the Southeast is very knowledgeable and productive around advanced manufacturing, with a legacy that battery storage combines.
- Lithium markets expert Joe Lowry stated that regional mines could complement hard-rock mining in Canada and extraction from brines and clay formations elsewhere to create a diversified North American supply chain.
- Former Charlotte Mayor Jennifer Roberts noted that state regulators often lack resources to police industrial polluters but emphasized that community engagement can make mining operations work responsibly.
- Representative Sean Casten stated he wants high-value-add manufacturing in America because that is where the competitive advantage lies.
