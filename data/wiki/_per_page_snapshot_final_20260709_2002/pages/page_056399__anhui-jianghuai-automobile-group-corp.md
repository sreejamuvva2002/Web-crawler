# Anhui Jianghuai Automobile Group Corp

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-03-24

**Source:** [JAC sets up shop in Australia | GoAuto](https://www.goauto.com.au/news/jac/t9/jac-sets-up-shop-in-australia/2024-03-24/93369.html)  ·  `page_056399`

## Facts

- State-owned JAC (Anhui Jianghuai Automobile Group Corp) has returned to the Australian market with a dual-cab 4x4 ute and plans for battery electric and plug-in hybrid models.
- The company has manufactured products since the 1960s but only began vehicle production in 2007.
- JAC sold 592,500 vehicles globally last year, an increase of 18 percent over 2022.
- Export sales accounted for more than 250,000 units in 2023, a rise of 60 percent year-on-year.
- JAC exports to more than 132 sales regions and operates six overseas subsidiaries alongside 19 factories overseas.
- Group chair Xiang Xingchu identified Australia as a strategic expansion test-case export market for the brand.
- Xiang Xingchu stated that Chinese auto brands will have increased involvement in the Australian market and JAC aims to become a mainstream automotive brand there.
- Xiang Xingchu observed that post-epidemic consumer preferences now prioritize affordable pricing, five-star safety ratings, intelligent features, richer configurations, and reliable quality.
- Danny Lenartic, deputy managing director of Australian distributor LTS Auto, described the new vehicle as having kerbside appeal, excellent features, and approaching European fit and finish.
- JAC must navigate the upcoming National Vehicle Emissions Standard, expected to take effect on January 1, 2025, which targets single-model sellers with CO2 emissions above 200 grams per kilometre.
- Lenartic expressed confidence that JAC will not face issues with the new emissions legislation.
- Jiang stated that JAC plans to introduce the T9 EV 4x4 and T9 PHEV 4x4 in the future.
- Distributor LTS Auto has invested more than $40 million in setup costs, including assembling an Australian management team and establishing aftersales operations.
- The T9 dual-cab ute is scheduled to arrive at 53 dealerships (and counting) during April.
- The Northern Territory is currently the only region without JAC representation, with major dealers like Eagers handling sales elsewhere.
- A dedicated JAC parts and accessories warehouse has been established in NSW’s Lane Cove to guarantee parts availability for business owners.
- Lenartic stated the company's mission is to align its policies to be the easiest OEM to deal with in the industry.
- Lenartic emphasized focusing on process fundamentals, marketing investment, team building, customer support, and product quality rather than dictating specific sales targets.
- Lenartic noted that starting from scratch without a legacy allows the company to establish correct practices from the beginning.
