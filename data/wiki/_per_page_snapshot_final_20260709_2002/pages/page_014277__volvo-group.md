# Volvo Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-07-17

**Source:** [Volvo Q2 Profit Halves on US Truck, Battery-Electric Weakness - TT](https://www.ttnews.com/articles/volvo-earnings-q2-2025)  ·  `page_014277`

## Facts

- Volvo Group posted a profit of $771 million in Q2 2025, less than half its $1.6 billion profit in the year-ago period.
- Parent company revenue decreased 12% year over year to $12.59 billion from $14.36 billion a year earlier.
- Truck sales revenue declined 7% to $8.37 billion in the three months ending June 30, down 14% from 9.74 billion krona in the year-ago period.
- Operating margin was 8.1% in Q2 2025, compared with 14.5% in Q2 2024.
- CEO Martin Lundstedt stated that net sales decreased by 5% adjusted for currency due to general stabilization in Europe and uncertainty among customers in North America.
- The societal transformation to zero-emission vehicles is slower than anticipated, causing costs of $460 million related to compensation for lower battery volume commitments and impairment of battery-electric assets to negatively impact reported operating income.
- Renegotiated battery delivery contracts required Volvo to pay suppliers $297 million in compensation, with $195 million taken against Q2 earnings.
- The company took a $164 million charge against earnings on the value of its battery-electric assets.
- CEO Martin Lundstedt warned that enabling conditions like charging infrastructure and demand stimulus have not yet been put in place, urging policymakers, state leaders, and stakeholders to take action.
- Volvo sold 52,764 trucks globally in Q2, down 10% from 58,935 trucks in the same period a year earlier.
- Volvo sold 101,597 trucks in the first six months of 2025, a decrease of 11% from 114,405 trucks a year earlier.
- Weak demand in North America is due to uncertainty surrounding tariffs and EPA 2027 emissions regulations, prompting production capacity reductions to adapt to lower demand.
- VTNA and Mack announced on April 23 that they would lay off 1,000 staff at manufacturing plants in the U.S.
- Volvo peers Traton Group and Daimler Truck have also laid off or are about to lay off employees in North America due to soft demand for Class 8 on-highway vehicles.
- Daimler Truck North America will temporarily lay off 2,000 production staff, with return contingent on an upturn in orders.
- Parent company of Freightliner and Western Star sold 38,580 trucks and buses in Q2, down 20% from 48,246 a year earlier.
- North America’s over-the-road freight market is in recession due to lower freight volume and rates, though the vocational market is relatively better.
- Overall industrywide heavy-duty truck retail sales in North America through end of June were down 6% at 135,723, compared with 145,056 in the first half of 2024.
- Volvo’s sales in North America decreased by 20% to 12,981 trucks from 16,234.
- VTNA sales dived 43% year over year to 4,988 trucks from 8,779 in Q2 2024.
- Mack sales rose 6% to 7,940 trucks from 7,475 in the year-ago period.
- Order intake in North America across VTNA and Mack decreased by 16% to 8,243 trucks due to carriers' wait-and-see stance.
- VTNA’s orders rose 29% to 4,131 trucks from 3,193 in the year-ago period, while Mack’s orders totaled 4,080 trucks, a 38% slump from 6,556 a year earlier.
- VTNA’s North American heavy-duty truck market share was 7.6% in Q2, down from 9.5%, as it revamped its on-highway lineup.
- Mack’s market share rose to 7.2% from 6.1% due to an improved supply chain and good vocational demand.
- Volvo Group paid $40 million in October 2024 to bring all of Mack’s cab assembly in-house after buying Commercial Vehicle Group’s Kings Mountain, N.C., plant.
- VTNA’s on-highway lineup saw a redesign of its flagship VNL tractor and the regional haul VNR tractor.
- Mack launched a new flagship on-highway tractor called the Pioneer and repositioned its Anthem predecessor to the regional haul market.
- At Volvo Group’s 2024 Capital Markets Day in Dublin, Va., executives outlined plans for 25% North American heavy-duty truck market share by 2030.
