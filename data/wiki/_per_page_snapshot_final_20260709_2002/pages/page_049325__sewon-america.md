# Sewon America

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-02-22

**Source:** [Hyundai supplier Sewon America to bring more than 700 jobs to Effingham County](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/hyundai-supplier-sewon-america-bring-182745188.html)  ·  `page_049325`

## Facts

- Bryan, Bulloch, and Chatham Counties each have a Hyundai supplier committed to building a facility in their respective counties.
- Gov. Kemp announced Sewon America will invest more than $300 million into a new manufacturing facility in Rincon.
- The project will bring more than 700 jobs to Effingham County.
- Gov. Brian Kemp stated the announcement celebrates delivering on their promise to create jobs in all four counties in the JDA and highlights the growth of a valued partner.
- Gov. Kemp recalled his first international trip as governor to Korea, where he congratulated Chairman Kim on the company's then-recent expansion and discussed continuing to work together.
- Sewon America first entered the U.S. market in 2008 by establishing operations in Lagrange, Ga., and has since doubled in size.
- The company operates one of the largest auto body parts plants in the U.S.
- Sewon's newest facility will be located at the Grande View industrial park in Rincon.
- The new facility will produce electric vehicle body parts.
- Production at the new facility is slated to begin in 2025.
- Rincon Mayor Ken Lee welcomed Sewon America to the community and commended partners at the Effingham County Industrial Development Authority for their efforts.
- Mayor Ken Lee noted the project will be the largest employment and private investment project in Rincon's history.
- Mayor Ken Lee expressed anticipation for working with Sewon to ensure their long-term success in the community.
- Georgia Speaker of the House Jon Burns worked alongside Sewon America executives to secure the project.
- Jon Burns celebrated the creation of 740 new jobs in Effingham County and coastal Georgia, thanking Sewon America, Governor Kemp, and public/private partners.
- Nine months prior to this announcement, the Savannah Joint Development Authority, Gov. Kemp, and local officials celebrated Hyundai's $5.5 billion investment into an EV battery plant.
- Last fall, Hyundai held a groundbreaking ceremony for its plant in Bryan County, followed by a community celebration at Enmarket Arena.
- Effingham County Industrial Development Authority Chairman Troy Smith expressed thrill that Sewon America selected Grande View industrial park as the best location for significant future growth investment.
- Troy Smith noted the combined investment will provide current and future generations with fulfilling, well-paying careers without leaving Effingham County.
- The announcement marks Effingham County's first Hyundai supplier for the Bryan County megasite supply chain.
