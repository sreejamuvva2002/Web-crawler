# Ascend Elements

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [US battery belt grows with $1 billion Kentucky cathode deal - MINING.COM](https://www.mining.com/web/us-battery-belt-grows-with-1-billion-kentucky-cathode-deal/)  ·  `page_000336`

## Facts

- Ascend Elements is a Massachusetts-based maker of battery materials that announced a deal to reprocess old batteries into cathode material.
- The company reached a billion-dollar deal with an unnamed automaker to supply recycled cathode material.
- Cathode material accounts for more than one third of the expense of a finished EV battery pack.
- Ascend is building a new cathode plant in Hopkinsville, Kentucky.
- The contract covers cathode for at least 40 gigawatt hours (GWh) of batteries per year.
- The contracted volume is enough to power 750,000 electric cars.
- In 2021, the contracted volume would have satisfied the entire US EV industry.
- BloombergNEF data indicates five times as much cathode will be needed by 2025.
- This contract marks the second major deal for US-made cathode.
- The deal follows a similar agreement between Nevada-based Redwood Materials Inc. and Panasonic Holdings Corp. in November.
- Most of the world’s cathode currently comes from China.
- Demand for US-sourced battery materials is increasing due to Inflation Reduction Act requirements that EV batteries be built in North America using materials processed by close US trading partners.
- Those incentives and others pushed by the Biden administration have spurred more than $70 billion of investments since last August along America’s Battery Belt.
- The Battery Belt stretches roughly from Michigan to Georgia.
- Ascend CEO Mike O’Kronley stated that the level of manufacturing investment in the US is unprecedented in decades.
- O’Kronley said this represents one of the very first commercial realizations of what the US is trying to do nationally.
- Ascend was founded in 2015 by two battery researchers at Worcester Polytechnic Institute.
- The founders developed a process for extracting complex cathode chemistries directly from shredded battery materials without individually extracting each mineral.
- Ascend operates a recycling plant in Covington, Georgia.
- The Georgia plant recycles battery materials out of discarded consumer electronics and manufacturing scrap from plants run by Honda Motor Co., Hitachi Ltd., and others.
- Ascend plans to begin installing manufacturing equipment at its Kentucky facility by the end of the year.
- Cathode deliveries will start in the fourth quarter of next year.
- Full-volume production is scheduled for 2025.
- The contract is for a minimum of $1 billion of cathode delivered over no more than five years.
- The buyer has options to expand purchases to $5 billion of cathode under similar terms.
- Ascend said the unnamed automaker requested not to be named.
- O’Kronley worked in auto manufacturing for 30 years before becoming CEO at Ascend in 2020.
- In 2016, O’Kronley served as head of corporate strategy at A123 Systems LLC, a producer of lithium-ion batteries.
- While at A123, O’Kronley received early samples of recycled cathode from Ascend and oversaw testing against a leading supplier.
- The testing showed that Ascend’s material performed better than the material A123 was buying.
- Following successful testing, A123 became an investor in Ascend.
- O’Kronley joined Ascend’s board after the investment.
- Ascend plans to close a Series D funding round this summer to finance additional factory expansion.
- It has not yet been decided whether additional operations will be built in Kentucky or elsewhere.
- The company’s growth was assisted by $480 million in matching grants from the US infrastructure law.
- O’Kronley described the grants as key to proving the company could build a factory and translate into commercial contracts.
