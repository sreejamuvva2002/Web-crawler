# Aurubis Richmond

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2022-06-18

**Source:** [Аурубис започва строителството на завод за рециклиране на мултиметали на стойност 300 млн. евро (около 320 млн. долара) в Огъста, Джорджия](https://www.aurubis.com/en/bulgaria/media/press-releases/aurubis-ag-starts-construction-of-a-300-million-multimetal-recycling-plant-in-august-georgia)  ·  `page_017369`

## Facts

- Aurubis AG broke ground on its first large secondary smelter for multimetal recycling in the United States in Augusta, Georgia.
- The total investment for the facility in Augusta, Richmond County, Georgia, is estimated at €300 million (approximately $320 million).
- Construction of the plant began on Saturday, June 18, 2022.
- The facility is expected to be fully operational in the first half of 2024.
- Once operational, Aurubis Richmond will create over 120 new direct jobs.
- The plant will have the capacity to process about 90,000 tons of complex recycling materials annually.
- Recycled materials processed at the facility include printed circuit boards, copper cables, and other materials containing metals.
- Aurubis is a 150-year-old, German-based company and one of the largest copper recyclers worldwide.
- Roland Harings, CEO of Aurubis AG, stated that the company is doubling down on its commitment to recycle valuable materials containing copper, nickel, tin, and other industrial and precious metals locally in the US.
- Harings noted that the US currently produces about 6 million tons of recycling materials containing valuable metals, which the company aims to reuse as critical input materials for many US industries.
- Harings emphasized that current metal supply cannot meet demand, particularly noting a lack of recycling capacities in the US, and described the Augusta facility as a game changer for the environment, manufacturers, and the transition to a renewable economy.
- The facility will reduce waste and direct recovered key metals to industries including solar and wind renewables, as well as electric vehicle and battery manufacturers.
- Aurubis highlighted that copper is 100-percent recyclable without any loss in performance, but industrial-scale secondary smelter recycling requires significant long-term investment.
- The plant's state-of-the-art environmental technology meets or exceeds the highest State of Georgia and federal government environmental standards.
- Aurubis Richmond will have a very low impact on regional air quality, contributing less than 0.2 percent of dust emissions in Richmond County.
- Governor Brian Kemp called the project the largest single investment made by a German company in Georgia history and welcomed Aurubis as one of over 500 German companies operating in the state.
- The facility is expected to become a major player in the North American supply chain and a significant employer in greater Augusta, GA.
- Aurubis Richmond will further elevate the company's leadership in multi-metal recycling and environmental stewardship globally.
