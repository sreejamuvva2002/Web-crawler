# Czech Automotive Trade Mission to South Carolina and Georgia

**Type:** event  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Czech Auto Experts and Managers Visit South Carolina and Georgia | Embassy of the Czech Republic in Washington, D.C.](https://mzv.gov.cz/washington/en/economy_trade/news/czech_auto_experts_and_managers_visit.html)  ·  `page_029571`

## Facts

- A trade mission from the Czech automotive industry to the United States was led by the Embassy of the Czech Republic in Washington, DC, and CzechInvest on October 17-20, 2016.
- The purpose of the visit was to gather information about the US automotive industry, compare education and training levels for automotive professions against their actual job application, make business and academic contacts, and find opportunities for Czech automotive exporters.
- The Ministry of Foreign Affairs of the Czech Republic financially supported the visit as part of its economic diplomacy efforts.
- In South Carolina, the trade mission visited the Clemson University International Center for Automotive Research (CU-ICAR), an advanced-technology research, innovation, and educational campus advancing the automotive sector.
- Czech university representatives expressed interest in Clemson’s student project Deep Orange, which has been running for eight years.
- The Deep Orange initiative gives students the opportunity to work with a chosen car manufacturer to create a prototype vehicle within a two-year program.
- The delegation held meetings with companies in Charleston, Atlanta, and Gainesville, focusing on foreign firms’ conditions for success on the American market and understanding differences between European and American automotive industries.
- The delegation met with ALBAform Inc., which is owned by its Czech parent company ALBA-METAL.
- ALBAform has been designing, developing, and manufacturing precision metal components focused on the automotive industry in Georgia since 2011.
- ALBAform operates as a family-run branch that has grown alongside its volume of customer orders and shared its experiences with the delegation.
- Ambassador of the Czech Republic Petr Gandalovic noted that Honorary Consuls often have local contacts hard to obtain from Washington and appreciated the assistance and organizational support of Honorary Consul for North and South Carolina Petr Vasicko and Honorary Consul for Georgia George Novak.
- Head of the Economic Section at the Czech Embassy Tomas Hart stated that the mission took the first step in making new contacts and is now evaluating next steps.
- Tomas Hart noted that from ALBAform’s example, it is evident that small and middle-sized companies are able to take on the American market.
