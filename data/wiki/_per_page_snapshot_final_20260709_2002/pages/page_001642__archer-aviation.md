# Archer Aviation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Future Flight: Archer Plans eVTOL Taxis for U.S. Cities by 2027 - News](https://eepower.com/news/future-flight-archer-plans-evtol-taxis-for-u.s-cities-by-2027/)  ·  `page_001642`

## Facts

- California-based Archer Aviation may soon have electric air taxis operating in cities across California, Florida, Texas, New York, and Georgia.
- The company has proposed an exclusive slot with Huntington Beach for Los Angeles-area operations ahead of the 2028 Olympic Games.
- Archer has filed multiple applications to pilot its Midnight electric air taxis under the federal eVTOL Integration Pilot Program (eIPP).
- The eIPP is a public-private initiative that will include at least five projects involving piloted and uncrewed short-range air taxis, longer-range fixed-wing aircraft, cargo, logistics, and safety systems.
- Selected cities and operators for the eIPP are expected to be named by mid-2026, with first flights following later in the year.
- Archer's Midnight aircraft has been tested at altitudes reaching 10,000 feet, exceeding the typical 1,500 to 5,000 feet operational range of many eVTOLs.
- The Midnight is designed for high-frequency, short-range urban routes typically spanning 20 to 50 miles and flown back-to-back throughout the day.
- The aircraft uses a 12-rotor lift-plus-cruise configuration with six tilting propellers on the front wing for vertical lift and forward thrust, and six fixed rotors on the rear wing for takeoff and landing.
- This rotor architecture provides redundancy in case of motor or rotor failure and enables significantly lower noise levels than helicopters during forward flight.
- The Midnight carries a pilot and four passengers, travels up to 150 miles per hour, and is designed for rapid turnaround charging between flights.
- The electric engine is powered by six battery packs containing cylindrical lithium-ion cells.
- Archer states the Midnight can recharge from 20% to 80% in about 10 minutes with minimal downtime.
- Archer is currently assembling its first Midnight fleet for early commercial deployments or certification testing.
- The company plans to increase annual production up to 50 aircraft across 700,000 square feet of manufacturing and testing sites located in California and Georgia.
- Archer acquired the Hawthorne Airport in Los Angeles as a strategic air taxi network hub and plans to upgrade the 80-acre site with charging, maintenance, and storage facilities alongside redeveloped hangar space.
- The Hawthorne Airport will serve as a testbed for AI-powered airspace integration tools including smart sensor-equipped runways, autonomous ground vehicles, advanced security systems, and AI-assisted air traffic and surface management.
- At CES 2026, Archer and Nvidia unveiled a partnership to integrate Nvidia’s IGX Thor modules into future onboard computing systems for AI-assisted flight planning and safety awareness.
- The partnership will also focus on merging IGX Thor with Archer's control software to support future autonomous or semi-autonomous aircraft.
- If the eIPP pilot projects proceed on schedule, 2026 could mark the first time U.S. cities see electric air taxis operating across an early network.
- Establishing long-term urban service in the U.S. will require local operations teams, new or upgraded infrastructure, and coordination with local emergency response and safety agencies.
- Securing municipal partnerships could provide a competitive advantage as the Federal Aviation Administration and Department of Transportation (DOT) consider new airspace rules, vertiport standards, grid connections, and emergency planning needs.
- The DOT’s Advanced Air Mobility (AAM) National Strategy aims to integrate eVTOL into national airspace and existing air traffic control workflows, laying out 40 recommendations covering vertiport infrastructure, security vetting requirements, and workforce needs.
- By 2030, the DOT aims to enable short VTOL and powered-lift flights with accessible vertiport infrastructure and a new AAM-ready Air Traffic Control system designed to manage low-altitude traffic for both piloted aircraft and uncrewed drones.
