# Rivian

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-16

**Source:** [Rivian American Manufacturing Digging in Social Circle Georgia with 7.5K Jobs | auto connected car news](https://www.autoconnectedcar.com/2025/09/rivian-american-manufacturing-digging-in-social-circle-georgia-with-7-5-jobs/)  ·  `page_059153`

## Facts

- Rivian broke ground on what it calls a generational investment in American manufacturing at the site of its future production plant outside Social Circle, Georgia.
- The electric vehicle maker is known for adventure-oriented pickup trucks and SUVs.
- Groundbreaking ceremony was attended by state leaders including Gov. Brian Kemp, First Lady Marty Kemp, and House Speaker Jon Burns.
- Officials described the project as one of the most significant economic development projects in Georgia’s history.
- The new facility represents a multibillion-dollar investment in the company’s future and U.S.-based vehicle manufacturing.
- The plant is expected to generate 7,500 direct jobs by 2030.
- Construction alone will create an additional 2,000 positions.
- Analysts project nearly 8,000 indirect jobs across suppliers, vendors, and small businesses in Jasper, Morgan, Newton, and Walton counties.
- Once operating at full capacity, the plant is projected to produce an estimated $1 billion in annual labor income for the region.
- Rivian plans to build the Georgia plant in two phases.
- First phase construction is slated to begin in 2026.
- Customer vehicle production is expected by 2028.
- The site will initially focus on producing Rivian’s midsize R2 SUV and its companion R3 crossover.
- Upon completion of the second phase, the plant will be capable of producing up to 400,000 vehicles a year.
- Rivian founder and CEO RJ Scaringe stated the facility will cement the company's future in Georgia and help ensure America maintains its technology leadership and excellence in automobile manufacturing.
- Scaringe added that the Georgia facility will support global expansion and provide scale to get millions of future drivers into all-electric vehicles.
- State leaders positioned the project as a local economic boost and a signal of Georgia’s growing role in the clean-energy economy.
- Gov. Brian Kemp stated the groundbreaking delivers on Rivian's commitment to Georgians and will provide generational benefits for families of thousands of hardworking Americans who will work at the facility.
- House Speaker Jon Burns called the plant a milestone accomplishment that lays the foundation for billions in long-term economic benefits and strengthens families and communities for decades.
- The nearly 2,000-acre campus will use modern construction techniques and advanced environmental management systems.
- Plans for the site include recreational trails and a Rivian-branded experience path.
- Rivian has already started building partnerships with Georgia universities, technical colleges, and workforce institutions to prepare skilled workers for the EV industry.
- Officials from the Joint Development Authority of Jasper, Morgan, Newton, and Walton counties described the project as the realization of a decades-old vision for high-tech, good-paying jobs in the region.
- Jerry Silvio, chairman of the authority, stated that seeing a Rivian vehicle on Georgia roads represents community pride.
- Rivian hosted a community event on September 14 to welcome nearly 1,000 residents and local businesses to the Stanton Springs site.
- The company has faced production and financial hurdles as it seeks to scale up operations.
- Company officials argue the Georgia facility will provide the capacity and stability needed to compete with established automakers while advancing sustainable transportation.
