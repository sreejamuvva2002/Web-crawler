# Lehigh Technologies

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2018-06

**Source:** [Lehigh Technologies: European MRP production beginning this summer - Tyrepress](https://www.tyrepress.com/2018/06/lehigh-technologies-european-mrp-production-beginning-this-summer/)  ·  `page_057117`

## Facts

- Lehigh Technologies has been owned by Michelin since October 2017.
- Lehigh Technologies is now part of Michelin’s High Technology Materials business unit.
- Lehigh Spain is setting up a Micronised Rubber Powders (MRP) production facility in Murrillo del Fruto, Spain.
- The new Spanish MRP production facility will begin operations in summer 2018.
- The Spanish facility is the product of a joint venture project between Lehigh Technologies and Hera Holding.
- Lehigh Technologies was founded in 2003.
- Lehigh Technologies is the market leader for Micronised Rubber Powders (MRP).
- MRP reduces feedstock costs by up to 50 percent while delivering performance across a wide range of applications.
- MRP replaces oil- and rubber-based feedstocks in high performance tyres, plastics, consumer goods, coatings, sealants, construction materials, and asphalt.
- Lehigh technical experts collaborate with customers to optimize products for each application.
- The Lehigh facility in Tucker, Georgia (USA) has an annual capacity of 54,000 tonnes.
- The Lehigh facility in Tucker, Georgia is the world’s largest MRP manufacturing plant.
- Lehigh’s Application & Development Center is located in Tucker, Georgia.
- The Tucker, Georgia center serves as an innovation hub where Michelin conducts research and formulates MRPs in collaboration with customers.
- Michelin has developed five MRP product ranges: PolyDyne, MicroDyne, EkoDyne, Rheopave, and Zenoflex.
- The range of solutions in core markets continues to expand.
