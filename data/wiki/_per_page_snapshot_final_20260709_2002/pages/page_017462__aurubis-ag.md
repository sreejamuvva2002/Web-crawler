# Aurubis AG

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_recycling  ·  **Published:** 2021-11-11

**Source:** [German company plans $347 million smelter in Georgia, hiring 125, ETHRWorld](https://hr.economictimes.indiatimes.com/news/industry/german-company-plans-347-million-smelter-in-georgia-hiring-125/87638258)  ·  `page_017462`

## Facts

- Hamburg-based Aurubis AG announced plans for a $347 million (300 million euro) smelter in Georgia.
- The facility will hire 125 workers.
- Construction on the Augusta smelter is scheduled to start next year.
- The plant is expected to begin operations in 2024.
- Aurubis stated that much copper is currently sent from the United States to Asia for recycling, making local recycling a sound business decision.
- Aurubis CEO Roland Harings stated in a statement: "The need for modern, sustainable, local processing capacities is very strong. Today, these aren't at all sufficient to cover the high demand, so large volumes of valuable feed materials have to be exported."
- Georgia Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson called it "one of the largest German investments in our state."
- Company spokesperson Meino Hauschildt said the plant will recycle circuit boards, copper cable, and other materials including nickel, tin, zinc, gold, silver, and platinum.
- The company expects up to 6.6 million tons (6 million metric tons) of metal-bearing materials to be collected for recycling in the U.S. in coming years.
- The amount of metal-bearing materials available for recycling is expected to grow at a rate of 5% annually.
- Aurubis said the smelter supports environmental goals by reducing the need for new copper to be mined and allows recovery of other materials combined with copper.
- The smelter will be powered with electricity and natural gas.
- The plant will be designed to the latest environmental standards with water reused on site.
- Dust and air emissions from the plant will be low.
- The company expects the plant to add about $93 million (80 million euros) to its profits before interest, taxes, depreciation and amortization beginning in the 2025-2026 fiscal year.
- Aurubis could claim state tax breaks including an income tax credit allowing it to annually deduct $4,000 per job from state income taxes.
- The income tax credit is capped at up to $2.5 million over five years.
- To qualify for the income tax credit, workers must make at least $28,000 a year.
- The state of Georgia will pay for job training for the new hires.
- Company officials expect to receive property tax abatements and aid with infrastructure for the project.
