# IONNA EV Charging Joint Venture

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** charging_infrastructure  ·  **Published:** 2025-02-12

**Source:** [New EV Charging Schemes Thwart Trump NEVI Squeeze](https://cleantechnica.com/2025/02/12/new-ev-charging-station-schemes-thwart-trumps-nevi-squeeze/)  ·  `page_060537`

## Facts

- President Trump has frozen a $5 billion federal program aimed at installing new public EV fast charging stations in all 50 states, Washington DC, and Puerto Rico.
- The NEVI EV Charging Station Program is funded with a total of $7.5 billion carved out of the 2021 Bipartisan Infrastructure Law.
- As of July 2024, 39 states have released solicitations for their NEVI programs and eight states have opened their first NEVI-funded stations (61 ports in total).
- Additional stations are in the pipeline with more than 2,500 additional ports having been awarded or conditionally awarded by the states.
- The midstream halt of NEVI funds jeopardizes millions in state and private investments.
- IONNA is an EV charging joint venture combining BMW, General Motors, Honda, Hyundai, Kia, Mercedes-Benz, Stellantis, and Toyota.
- On February 4, IONNA announced that it has contracted for more than 100 EV charging station sites across the US.
- IONNA aims to bring 1,000 more charging bays online before the end of this year.
- IONNA plans to build 30,000 charging bays over the next 5 years, representing new jobs across the nation and cutting-edge technological innovation.
- The IONNA 'Rechargery' model features a lounge-style experience for drivers waiting to charge.
- Rechargeries will offer seamless charging across all automakers, including reservations, route optimization, and in-car payments.
- Rechargeries will feature an AI-assisted, automated grab-and-go payment system for snacks and refreshments.
- The first IONNA Rechargery is installed at a site in Garner, North Carolina.
- Electrify America remains active in the EV charging market alongside new players.
- The New York Green Bank announced its first-ever EV charging station transaction, enabling Revel to install 267 charging ports spread among five public locations by 2027.
- Illinois utility ComEd announced a $100 million rebate program for home EV chargers, business and commercial fleet rebates, and upgrades for non-residential ratepayers.
- Ford is extending its 'Ford Power Promise' complimentary home charger and standard installation through March 31, 2025.
- Ford announced the extension on January 3 following record electric vehicle year-over-year sales in 2024.
- On February 12, Texas-based CLEAResult announced the launch of its new 'ChooseEV' assessment tool to help utilities and fleet owners transition to EVs.
- CLEAResult launched in 2003 and bills itself as North America’s largest energy efficiency, energy transition and energy sustainability services provider, with 2,700 employees stationed in 60 US and Canadian cities.
- Divakar Jandhyala serves as CLEAResult's Chief Product & Technology Officer.
