# Saft

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-09-10

**Source:** [Saft expands battery production in the US to meet local demand - Energy Storage](https://www.ess-news.com/2024/09/10/saft-expands-battery-production-in-the-us-to-meet-local-demand/)  ·  `page_059217`

## Facts

- Saft, a subsidiary of TotalEnergies, has commissioned a new line at its Jacksonville factory in Florida to produce lithium-ion battery containers.
- The expansion aims to meet booming US demand for energy storage projects by offering a solution with domestic content that qualifies for the Income Tax Credit (ITC).
- Saft stated the investment will create direct and indirect job opportunities and strengthen the national supply chain.
- Hervé Amossé, Saft EVP for Energy Storage Systems, stated the company's strategy is to expand Jacksonville’s capacity to reach more than 5 GWh in 2027 to enhance its capability to serve US customers faster and reduce its environmental footprint.
- Saft aims to significantly increase local content in its products to meet Inflation Reduction Act 2022 requirements with its 5.1 MWh containers by 2026.
- The company launched its latest-generation product at ess Europe 2024 in Munich and revealed plans to boost the energy density of its containers from the current 3.3 MWh to more than 5 MWh in 2026.
- Saft plans to add a new AI algorithm to its I-Sight monitoring platform to improve the reliability and availability of energy storage installations.
- The company announced it would start deliveries of battery storage systems produced at its US plant in Jacksonville from the second half of 2024.
- The Jacksonville plant opened in 2011 after receiving a $95.5 million grant from the US Department of Energy.
- Saft later received another $20.2 million in state and city financial incentives to build the 235,000-square-foot plant.
- In 2018, Saft was asked to repay more than $320,000 after failing to create 279 jobs by the end of 2017, as stipulated in a 2009 economic development agreement.
