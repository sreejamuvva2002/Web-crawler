# Meritor Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2020-03-01

**Source:** [Meritor, Wabash Stay Focused on Now and When - TT](https://www.ttnews.com/articles/meritor-wabash-stay-focused-now-and-when)  ·  `page_014605`

## Facts

- Key executives from global commercial vehicle supplier Meritor Inc. and trailer manufacturer Wabash National Corp. discussed industry evolution and revolution at American Trucking Associations’ Technology & Maintenance annual meeting in late February in Atlanta.
- John Nelligan, Meritor’s vice president of global sales and service, stated the company is spending more on research and development than ever before to evolve current products and develop new ones.
- Nelligan emphasized balancing near-term customer needs with future innovation to avoid losing today's customers or risking the future.
- During its fiscal 2020 first quarter ended Dec. 31, Meritor reported introducing two electric powertrains, one electric suspension, and seven core products focused on fuel efficiency and reduced maintenance.
- Meritor’s axles are currently 4% more efficient compared with 10 years ago, with a half-percent gain in axle efficiency worth $500 in fuel savings.
- Meritor will introduce a lighter, single-piston air disc brake designed to compete more effectively with drum brakes in linehaul operations and reduce pad changes over the component's life.
- Nelligan envisions electrification taking hold in intracity distribution, day cabs for shorter distance regional distribution, and port drayage applications.
- He expressed optimism about longer-range shifts including oil companies funding electric-vehicle charging stations and electric batteries that can eventually be composted safely after their useful life.
- Hydrogen is expected to power fuel cells on electric heavy-duty trucks in the near future.
- Meritor announced the new Drivetrain On Demand program, which boosts uptime with 24-hour shipping for replacement driveshaft and differential assemblies.
- Nikola Corp. announced in 2018 that Meritor would supply an advanced Class 8 independent suspension.
- Nikola intends to bring hydrogen commercial vehicles with necessary fueling infrastructure to market in 2023, along with heavy-duty, battery-electric trucks in 2021.
- Nelligan noted that increased industry investment will lead to faster breakthroughs and change.
- Wabash National Corp. is focusing on meeting demand for diverse trailer applications used to deliver freight and consumer goods.
- Dustin Smith, group president of commercial trailer products for Wabash, stated that customers trust Wabash to invest the right resources to find solutions for moving freight.
- Wabash showcased a medium-duty Isuzu NRR model truck with a refrigerated truck body on the TMC show floor, potentially supporting e-commerce and urban deliveries, marking a new business area for the company a year prior.
- Wabash’s DuraPlate HD dry van now features DuraPlate cell core technology, where composite panels reduce trailer weight by 300 pounds per 53-foot trailer.
- Trailer orders slid in 2019 compared to the all-time record level in 2018 and started slow in 2020 at 15,000 units.
- Smith noted that regarding electrification, many companies are scrambling to introduce the right technology.
- Wabash displayed a 53-foot trailer with a Carrier Corp. refrigeration unit that operates without a typical diesel engine, instead using a module feeding electricity from under-trailer batteries to perform as if plugged in at a freight terminal.
- Solar panels from eNow assist with charging the trailer's batteries during daylight hours, while shore power handles battery recharging at night.
- The trailer’s molded structural composite walls increased thermal efficiency, reducing the number of required batteries.
- Smith stated the electrified refrigerated trailer would go into service shortly after the show.
