# EnviroSpark

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** charging_infrastructure  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [EnviroSpark to Build DC Fast Chargers at Georgia Shell Station, Waffle House - CStore Decisions](https://cstoredecisions.com/envirospark-to-build-dc-fast-chargers-at-georgia-shell-station-waffle-house/)  ·  `page_060540`

## Facts

- Georgia-based EnviroSpark announced that it will build DC fast chargers at a Shell station in Metter, Ga.
- EnviroSpark will also build DC fast chargers at a Waffle House site in Tifton.
- EnviroSpark is using National Electric Vehicle Infrastructure (NEVI) funding that was awarded by the Georgia Department of Transportation (GDOT).
- EnviroSpark won two of the five open Phase 1 eligible sites.
- EnviroSpark was the only company selected to build more than one location in the first round of funding of Georgia’s NEVI deployment.
- EnviroSpark will host all eight stations on its national EnviroSpark charging network.
- The new Waffle House charging stations mark the second collaboration between EnviroSpark and Waffle House.
- In February, the Tennessee Department of Transportation selected EnviroSpark to build stations at a Lakeland, Tenn., Waffle House in the state’s first round of NEVI funding awards.
- EnviroSpark Co-Founder Stephanie Luque, who also leads the company’s federal initiatives, stated: “We are honored to once again partner with Waffle House — a fellow Georgia-based business committed to customer satisfaction and community engagement — on such a pivotal project.”
- Stephanie Luque stated: “Collaborating with Waffle House, the Metter Shell station and GDOT exemplifies all parties’ shared commitment to driving innovation and sustainability in ways that benefit drivers and communities. NEVI-funded projects like these represent huge steps toward reducing drivers’ range anxiety and moving us closer to mass EV adoption.”
- According to GDOT, Georgia is among the top 10 states for EV sales.
- Three of the top five largest investments in the EV supply chain are in Georgia.
- Georgia has approximately 1,193 publicly available EV charging stations.
- Only 14 stations have DCFC ports that meet both the distance and power requirements under federal rules.
- Andrew Heath, Georgia DOT Deputy Chief Engineer, stated: “The Georgia NEVI Deployment Program outlines the state’s approach to using our federal funding to address EV infrastructure gaps in our state’s Alternative Fuel Corridors.”
- Andrew Heath stated: “In order for us to be successful, we are turning to the private sector to deliver this innovative infrastructure at strategic locations across our state through public-private partnerships.”
- The Georgia NEVI Deployment Program is funded by the 2021 Bipartisan Infrastructure Law enacted as the Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act (IIJA).
- The program provides nearly $5 billion nationally over five years, which started in the fall of 2022.
- Georgia’s allotment from the formula program is approximately $135 million to develop its portion of the national network.
- EnviroSpark will begin construction and installation of the eight NEVI-compliant DC fast chargers later this year.
- EnviroSpark was founded in 2014.
- EnviroSpark is a leader in the turnkey design, installation and operation of EV charging solutions.
- EnviroSpark is responsible for more than 7,800 installations of charging plugs across North America to date.
