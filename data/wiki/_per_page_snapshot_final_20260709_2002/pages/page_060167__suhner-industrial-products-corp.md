# SUHNER Industrial Products Corp.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [suhner-manufacturing Archives - Bernard and Company](https://bernardandcompany.com/tag/suhner-manufacturing/)  ·  `page_060167`

## Facts

- Suhner has moved its marketing and sales office from Buffalo Grove to Barrington, Illinois.
- The new Barrington office features sample models of tapping and drilling units along with real-world application examples on the walls.
- Mike Ricketts serves as the regional manager of the machining division at the Barrington office.
- Tom Dunn is the national account representative for the transmission division and specializes in orthopedic surgical tools.
- Suhner operates two divisions: the machining division led by Mike Ricketts and the transmission division led by Tom Dunn.
- Suhner’s flex shafts have particular advances in the medical industry and its primary automotive market, which includes power windows, seats, side view mirrors, and roof retraction.
- The Barrington office is located 30 minutes from O’Hare International Airport to better serve current and future customers.
- Suhner has partnered with Velocity Products to maintain the SU-matic line at the highest level of quality and functionality.
- Velocity Products is a premier distributor of tooling for Okuma and innovative machine tools throughout North America.
- SU-matic tooling is designed and manufactured by SU-matic, which collaborates closely with engineers from Okuma to ensure flawless performance on lathes, multi-tasking machines, and machine tools.
- Velocity’s Walter Scheppe has over 30 years of experience maintaining and repairing lathe tooling, angle heads, multi-spindle heads, and specialty tooling.
- The Velocity/SU-matic Preventive Maintenance Program offers a 72-hour inspection turnaround, inspection of radial and axial play, and a six-month guarantee on repairs.
- Velocity/SU-matic tooling carries a two-year guarantee, with options to send tools to the repair center for inspection and return to original factory specifications.
- The repair center is located at Suhner’s new location in Rome, Georgia.
- Bob Bauer, Vice President of Velocity Products, stated that the partnership allows them to provide timely and high-quality service to Okuma.
- Suhner has opened a new North American service center immediately next to its existing repair center on its campus in Rome, Georgia.
- The new North American center features 40% more workspace due to better storage and materials handling operations.
- The facility’s repair area has increased by 50%, with new repair function areas and equipment.
- The Rome, Georgia campus includes improved training facilities, a test area, substantial room for expansion, vertical storage shelving (18’ racking), industrial cabinets, and dedicated stock areas.
- Suhner has hired additional staff to provide onsite training in operations and maintenance of automated tooling equipment.
- The service center repairs spindles, multiple spindle heads, slides, live tooling, and will soon service rotary index tables for CNC machines.
- Suhner also repairs its own line of abrasive power tools and competitive brands of spindles, multi-heads, and live tooling.
- SUHNER introduced the SUNmic 60, an innovative grinding, milling, and polishing system for tool and mold making, aircraft, engine, and motor building.
- The SUNmic 60 features an intelligent control unit with a touch panel, glass display, rotation speed regulator, and large digital display.
- It is driven by powerful brushless BL/DC micro motors with 280 W power, operating at 1000–60,000 rpm with high torque.
- The system operates on a 90–240 V source for global use and connects to two BL/DC motors and an AC motor.
- Chuck diameters for the SUNmic 60 range from 2.35, 3.0, and 6.0 mm to 1/8 and 1/4 inch.
- SUHNER provides free demo services via a mobile vehicle carrying tools, attachments, and abrasives for onsite field expertise.
- SUHNER introduced the ASC 22, a battery-operated straight Die-grinder with a rotation speed of 22,000 rpm.
- The ASC 22 is Li-Ion Battery powered with a 4.0 Ah charge capacity and 18 V voltage, featuring intelligent battery management to prevent overheating or overloading.
- The tool weighs 1.9 kg including its battery pack and includes two battery packs and a charger with four connector cables in a plastic case.
- Suhner introduced the TurboTrim, a complete unit for cutting and trimming beef, pork, and poultry, featuring stainless steel blades, a coated housing, and a patented adjustment-free housing.
- The TurboTrim has a stainless steel motor assembly with a slide-out bracket for easy removal and a quick change assembly for flexible shaft replacement.
- Suhner introduced the Rotomax flexible shaft machine, which functions as an angle grinder, straight grinder, drum polisher, belt sander, tube polisher, or die grinder via interchangeable hand pieces.
- The Rotomax offers power from 2.0-4.7HP with electronically-controlled speeds ranging from 500-15,000 RPM in increments of 100 RPM.
- The machine is available as a hanging model, on a basic stand, or on a specially-designed stable trolley (STM) for portability.
- Suhner Standard Flexible Shafts offer plug-and-play solutions with standard female couplings, set screws, and bi-directional application options.
- K and Ka type plastic casing flex shafts are used throughout the automotive OEM market for engine control, power transmission, and interior controls on power seats and steering wheel adjustments.
- Suhner handles speeds up to 50,000 rpm with high accuracy for applications ranging from printing presses to speedometers and power seat actuation.
