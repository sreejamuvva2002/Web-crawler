## Facts

- Rivian will raise nearly $15 billion in debt for an EV plant in Georgia.
- Rivian Automotive intends to raise around $15 billion in debt to assist in the construction of an electric car manufacturing facility in Georgia.
- According to a November 9 agreement, taxable bonds for the project will be issued by the Georgia Department of Economic Development and the Joint Development Authority of Jasper, Morgan, Newton, and Walton Counties.
- Rivian has committed to buying bonds as they are issued.
- The company has committed to paying property taxes totaling at least $300 million until 2047.
- Property tax payments will go up if the automaker makes more money than its $5 billion investment.
- In 2021, Rivian announced its intentions to establish the Georgia factory and stated that it would be operational by 2024.
- The new facility will eventually produce 400,000 cars annually and employ over 7,500 people.
