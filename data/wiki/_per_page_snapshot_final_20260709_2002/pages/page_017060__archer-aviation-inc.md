# Archer Aviation Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-11-15

**Source:** [Electric Vertical Aircraft Manufacturing Company Archer to Create 1,000 Jobs in Covington - Location Georgia](https://www.locationgeorgia.com/electric-vertical-aircraft-company-archer-to-create-1000-jobs-in-covington-november-2022/)  ·  `page_017060`

## Facts

- Archer Aviation Inc., an aerospace company advancing sustainable urban air mobility, will invest $118 million over 10 years in a new state-of-the-art electric vertical takeoff and landing aircraft (eVTOL) manufacturing facility.
- The new facility will be located alongside the Covington Municipal Airport in Newton County.
- Archer’s new facility at the Covington Municipal Airport will consist of three buildings across more than 96 acres.
- Construction is expected to begin in December 2022 and follow a phased buildout of eVTOL aircraft production.
- The facility will have an eventual capacity able to produce up to 2,300 aircraft per year.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Archer Aviation Inc. will create 1,000 jobs and invest $118 million over 10 years in the new facility.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Georgia has made fostering innovation a priority to bring the jobs of the future to the No. 1 state for business.
- Governor Brian Kemp noted that Georgia’s established leadership in the aerospace industry was essential to landing Archer Aviation in Newton County.
- Archer is based in Santa Clara, CA.
- Archer is currently developing and manufacturing eVTOL aircraft for use in Urban Air Mobility networks.
- Adam Goldstein, Archer’s Founder and CEO, stated that after many months of discussion and evaluation, they are excited to announce the decision to locate its first manufacturing facility in Covington as they progress towards commercialization.
- Adam Goldstein noted that creating Urban Air Mobility networks across major cities nationwide requires ramping production of their aircraft.
- Glen Burks, Archer’s Vice President of Manufacturing, stated that the new manufacturing facility is a crucial next step in bringing their vision for eVTOL travel to life.
- Glen Burks expressed happiness with Covington as a location and partner for this significant next step in the company’s development and growth.
- Archer plans to construct the facility adjacent to the Covington Municipal Airport in Newton County, which was established in 1963 as part of a long-term airport master plan.
- The site is strategically accessible by Georgia’s robust interstate system via I-20.
- Large adjacent parcels of land were set aside for future development, and the new Archer facility will use one of these adjacent parcels.
- Hiring is expected to begin in late 2023.
- The facility is slated to open in the first half of 2024.
- Mayor Steve Horton stated that City of Covington Council Members and himself are very excited for Archer Aviation to join their business community.
- Mayor Steve Horton noted that the Covington Municipal Airport is one of many points of pride for Covington, and Archer’s presence within the airport plan is another distinction.
- Mayor Steve Horton stated that Covington has always been on the cutting edge for quality jobs and new technology.
- Lanier Sims, Chairman of the Newton County Industrial Development Authority, stated they could not be more excited for Archer to join their community.
- Lanier Sims noted that Archer fits in perfectly with their current industry mix and will be an incredible asset to the beautiful airport in Covington.
- Lanier Sims stated that the Development Authority is committed to ensuring their workforce is prepared for the community’s future and ready for the outstanding jobs to come from Archer Aviation.
- Lanier Sims expressed gratitude to Governor Kemp and the Georgia Department of Economic Development for their leadership and continuation of keeping Georgia the number one state for business.
- Katie Kirkpatrick, Metro Atlanta Chamber President and CEO, stated they welcome Archer Aviation to metro Atlanta where the strength of their community will help ensure the success of their new facility.
- Katie Kirkpatrick noted that Metro Atlanta’s expertise in the aviation industry coupled with support from academic, business, civic and government partners makes the region the perfect place to invest in a new facility.
- The Global Commerce Industrial Projects team represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) on this competitive project.
- GDEcD partnered with the Newton County Industrial Development Authority, Metro Atlanta Chamber of Commerce, the Georgia Center of Innovation’s Aerospace industry team, Georgia EMC, Georgia Quick Start, and Georgia Tech.
- Pat Wilson, GDEcD Commissioner, stated that technology and innovation continue to drive change across strategic industries, and Archer Aviation is on the forefront of a technological evolution in the aviation industry.
- Pat Wilson noted that utilizing an innovative, state-of-the art eVTOL aircraft design, the company will look to sustainably tackle mobility issues in urban centers.
- Pat Wilson stated that GDEcD’s Center of Innovation’s specialized Aerospace team has been uniquely involved in this project and is proud of their continued efforts to foster Georgia’s aerospace and defense industries.
- Home to more than 800 aerospace companies, Georgia maintains a robust aerospace education pipeline.
- The pipeline includes a dozen high schools that offer training in the field, five technical colleges with aviation programs, and several universities offering degrees in aerospace engineering.
- The Georgia Center of Innovation for Aerospace is a strategic arm of the Georgia Department of Economic Development.
- The Georgia Center of Innovation for Aerospace has been cultivating an ecosystem of opportunities for innovative companies like Archer Aviation for more than 10 years.
- The center builds and connects Unmanned Aerial Systems (UAS) networks for the commercialization of Advanced Air Mobility and UAS Systems.
- Archer (NYSE: ACHR) is designing and developing electric vertical takeoff and landing aircraft for use in urban air mobility networks.
- Archer’s mission is to unlock the skies, freeing everyone to reimagine how they move and spend time.
- Archer’s team is based in Santa Clara, CA.
