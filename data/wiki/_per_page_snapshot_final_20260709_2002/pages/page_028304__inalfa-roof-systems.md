## Facts

- Inalfa opened its initial 300-job operation in Cherokee County during a surge in the Southeast automotive industry.
- The company announced a 100,000-square-foot expansion to its Acworth location.
- The expansion adds 100 more jobs and space for additional high-volume production lines.
- The new building is utilized to encapsulate glass panels via injection molding and steel reinforcement bonding.
- Inalfa has a large customer base in the Southeast.
- Quick Start has been Inalfa’s training partner since the beginning of its operations.
- Quick Start produced company-specific training videos on topics such as glass and steel reinforcement bonding processes, glass encapsulation and Inalfa’s TP10-BMW F15 Line for the BMW X5.
- The TP10-BMW F15 Line is described as the longest and most complex production line.
- Everyone on the production floor went through Quick Start’s pre-hire assessment process.
- Michael Adoyo, Inalfa’s production trainer of operations, stated that “[The pre-hire] gives them an awareness of what it’s like and prepares them mentally before they actually hit the floor.”
- Inalfa is headquartered in Venray, The Netherlands.
- Inalfa's vision is to become the first-choice OEM partner in roof systems and integrated technologies in the changing automotive industry landscape — a partner that is trusted to deliver.
