# Honda Motor Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-03-14

**Source:** [Honda Announces Next Steps in Preparation for U.S. EV Production | Honda Global Corporate Website](https://global.honda/en/newsroom/news/2023/c230315aeng.html)  ·  `page_009106`

## Facts

- Honda announced key next steps in the establishment of its EV Hub in Ohio to lead its journey to an electrified future in North America.
- In October 2022, Honda announced it would invest $700 million to re-tool several existing auto and powertrain plants to establish the new EV Hub in Ohio.
- The investment aims to prepare for the production of battery electric vehicles in 2026.
- The Marysville Auto Plant (MAP), where Honda began auto production in America in 1982, will be Honda’s first auto plant in the U.S. to transition to making EVs.
- MAP will consolidate two assembly lines currently making internal combustion engine and hybrid-electric vehicles to retool for EV and EV component production.
- Accord production will be transferred from MAP to the Indiana Auto Plant as MAP retools for EV production.
- Honda Transmission Plant in Georgia (TMP-G) will dedicate one production line to e-axle production, an essential EV component combining the electric motor, gearing components, and power electronics.
- TMP-G will partner with a Honda supplier to install a new e-axle line in space currently occupied by a non-operational transmission production line; the supplier will install, own, and operate the new line.
- Anna Engine Plant (AEP) will shift production of some engine components to the Honda engine plant in Alabama to prepare for battery case production for EV models.
- AEP will transfer component production for two different generations of engines to the Alabama Auto Plant (AAP).
- Machining of the engine head for the existing V6 engine that powers many Honda light trucks, which ended at AEP in February, will be added to AAP operations beginning in March 2023.
- Engine block casting for the all-new V6 engine introduced in the 2023 Honda Pilot will begin at AAP in August 2023.
- These moves will allow AEP to re-allocate space for future IPU Case production, which will be combined with battery modules from a new joint venture facility in Ohio with LG Energy Solution (LGES).
- EV batteries from the JV will power EVs produced at MAP and East Liberty Auto Plant (ELP).
- Honda expects to maintain stable employment across all locations during these transition steps.
- MAP will begin preparing for EV production as early as January 2024 by consolidating production to one line to build infrastructure necessary for EV production.
- Every MAP associate will have a position as part of this transition.
- Starting later in 2023, MAP associates will begin training programs designed to prepare them for skills required for EV production.
- Production of the Honda Accord will transfer to the Indiana Auto Plant (IAP) in 2025 to maintain core model volume while enabling MAP's transition.
- As part of the EV Hub, Honda will transform MAP, ELP, and AEP to start EV production in North America, developing knowledge and expertise to be shared across its entire North American auto production network.
- Honda and LGES recently held an official groundbreaking ceremony for a new joint venture EV battery plant in Fayette County, near Jeffersonville, Ohio.
- The two companies committed to invest $3.5 billion in the JV facility, which will be over 2 million square feet and scheduled for completion by the end of 2024.
- The JV facility aims for approximately 40GWh of annual production capacity, with overall investment projected to reach $4.4 billion.
- Honda aims to achieve carbon neutrality for all products and corporate activities by 2050, with a vision to make battery-electric and fuel cell electric vehicles represent 100% of its vehicle sales by 2040.
- Honda plans to sustain current ICE and hybrid-electric vehicle production through 2030 and beyond to meet customer demand and support investment in its electrified future.
- Honda has been producing automobiles in America for over 40 years, beginning in November 1982 at MAP.
- In 2022, more than two-thirds of all Honda and Acura automobiles sold in the U.S. were made in America using domestic and globally sourced parts.
- Honda’s cumulative auto production in America now exceeds 30 million vehicles.
- Honda established manufacturing operations in America in 1979 with motorcycle production in Marysville, Ohio, and today has one of the largest and most diverse U.S. manufacturing footprints of any international company.
- The company’s 12 plants employ more than 22,000 associates in America, supporting operations with an annual capacity of more than 1.27 million automobiles, 1.52 million engines, 500,000 power equipment products, and 300,000 powersports products, as well as the HondaJet advanced light jet and GE Honda HF120 turbofan engines.
- Cumulatively, Honda has invested $19 billion in its U.S. manufacturing operations, including more than $3.2 billion over the past five years alone.
- The company works with over 600 U.S. original equipment suppliers, with U.S.-sourced parts purchases of approximately $470 billion since 1979.
