# Hyundai Mobis

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2025-12-09

**Source:** [Hyundai Mobis to Unveil 30+ New Technologies at CES 2026, Aiming to Strengthen Global OEM Partnerships](https://www.bastillepost.com/global/article/5440035-hyundai-mobis-to-unveil-30-new-technologies-at-ces-2026-aiming-to-strengthen-global-oem-partnerships)  ·  `page_021337`

## Facts

- Hyundai Mobis will present more than 30 mobility convergence technologies at CES 2026 in Las Vegas from January 6 to 9.
- The company will host an invitation-only private booth exclusively for pre-invited customers to focus on new business opportunities.
- Hyundai Mobis is specifically targeting key clients from across North America for its CES booth.
- Senior executives from multiple major global automakers, including heads of purchasing and technology, have confirmed visits to the booth.
- A dedicated meeting room on the third floor of the West Hall at the Las Vegas Convention Center will be used to facilitate deeper discussions with visiting executives.
- The CES showcase highlights include the updated M.VICS 7.0 cockpit integrated solution and the X-by-Wire electronic control technology.
- M.VICS 7.0 integrates advanced infotainment technologies featuring a holographic windshield display, a vertically expandable 18.1-inch large display, and a redesigned center-console interface.
- The X-by-Wire integrated solution enables electronic steering and braking control without mechanical connections via a single controller for optimized driving dynamics and built-in dual safety.
- Additional showcased technologies include an augmented-reality head-up display (AR-HUD), low-power display solutions, and cost-efficient, high-performance EV drive systems.
- The Holographic Windshield Display (HWD) featured in M.VICS 7.0 won the CES 2026 Innovation Award from the Consumer Technology Association (CTA).
- HWD is developed in collaboration with German optics specialist Zeiss and is the world's first system to utilize holographic film to transform the entire front windshield into an ultra-large display surface.
- The HWD technology allows drivers to view key information directly on the windshield while enabling passengers to watch separate content like videos or games without distracting the driver.
- Hyundai Mobis is currently co-developing the HWD technology with major global customers and aims to begin mass production in 2029.
- The company plans to ramp up promotional efforts across North America and Europe through CES, international exhibitions, and roadshows.
- Hyundai Mobis aims to increase its share of global OEM sales in its core component business to 40% by 2033.
- Hyundai Mobis is ranked as the global no. 6 automotive supplier and is headquartered in Seoul, South Korea.
- The company possesses expertise in sensors, sensor fusion in ECUs, software development for safety control, and manufactures components for electrification, brakes, chassis, suspension, steering, airbags, lighting, and automotive electronics.
- Hyundai Mobis operates its R&D headquarters in Korea along with four technology centers located in the United States, Germany, China, and India.
