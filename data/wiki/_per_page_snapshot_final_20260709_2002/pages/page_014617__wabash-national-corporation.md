# Wabash National Corporation

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [J.B. Hunt orders 6,000 containers from Wabash National | FleetOwner](https://www.fleetowner.com/news/article/21677032/jb-hunt-orders-6000-containers-from-wabash-national)  ·  `page_014617`

## Facts

- Wabash National Corp. has received orders exceeding $70 million from J.B. Hunt Transport Services Inc. for 6,000 53-foot DuraPlate domestic containers.
- The order includes a smaller number of container chassis in addition to the domestic containers.
- The 53-foot domestic containers feature a new design utilizing Wabash National's proprietary DuraPlate technology.
- The containers have a stackable, high-cube design with an uninterrupted sidewall and interior dimensions resembling conventional trailers.
- These will be the first 53-foot units in the J.B. Hunt fleet with sufficient strength to permit positioning on the bottom of a "double-stack" on the railcar.
- Richard E. Dessimoz, acting CEO of Wabash, stated that J.B. Hunt selected the DuraPlate container for intermodal operations and the DuraPlate trailer for over-the-road operations.
- Dessimoz expressed confidence that the DuraPlate material will prove to be an excellent performer in this new application.
- Wabash said the containers incorporate features designed to reduce maintenance costs and increase durability.
- These containers will be the first production domestic containers to use the DuraPlate sidewall material.
- The DuraPlate material consists of a polyethylene plastic core sandwiched between two sheets of pre-painted high-strength galvanized steel.
- Wabash claims the resulting panel is efficient, lightweight, and incredibly strong, offering advantages over aluminum plate and traditional sheet/post sidewall designs.
- Kirk Thompson, president & CEO of J.B. Hunt Transportation Services, stated satisfaction with the performance and quality of their large fleet of DuraPlate van trailers in their truck business unit.
- Thompson noted the opportunity to convert J.B. Hunt's intermodal container fleet to exclusively 53-foot equipment by replacing 48-foot equipment with stackable 53-foot DuraPlate containers.
- Thompson stated that J.B. Hunt’s intermodal customers will have access to North America's largest fleet of exclusively 53-foot containers.
- Rail partners will have the option to stack the new containers on top in traditional configurations or on the bottom of 53-foot rail cars.
- With this addition, Wabash now offers a complete line of intermodal equipment including domestic containers, piggyback trailers, and bi-modal equipment.
