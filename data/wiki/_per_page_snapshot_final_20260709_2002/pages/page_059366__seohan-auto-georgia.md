# Seohan Auto Georgia

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2024-09

**Source:** [Seohan Auto Georgia is Open for Business in Liberty County - Augusta CEO](https://augustaceo.com/news/2024/09/seohan-auto-georgia-open-business-liberty-county-news/)  ·  `page_059366`

## Facts

- Seohan Auto Georgia held its Grand Opening Ceremony on Friday, September 6th, 2024.
- The new manufacturing facility is located in the Tradeport East Business Center in Liberty County, Georgia.
- The facility will produce H/shafts, axles, and brake systems for Original Equipment Manufacturers (OEMs).
- Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America is identified as an OEM customer for the produced components.
- Operations at the new facility will create at least 180 new jobs.
- The project represents a total investment of more than $72 million in Liberty County.
- The site selection process to locate the manufacturing facility in Liberty County began in November 2022.
- Liberty County won the competitive site selection against other considered locations through efforts by the Authority staff, board members, and community/state partners.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that economic development does not happen by accident and highlighted the community's investment in the Tradeport East Business Center.
- Pat Wilson noted that local infrastructure investments allowed Seohan Auto Georgia to meet a challenging timeline to get up and running.
- State Representative Al Williams, LCDA Board Chairman, welcomed Seohan to Liberty County following the competitive location process that started in November 2022.
- Opening ceremony attendees included Seohan Group Chairman Yong Suk Kim, Seohan Auto Georgia CEO Jung Kee Koo, Seohan Auto USA President Myon Han, Seohan Group Vice Chairman Woon Dae Paek, GDEcD COO Sydne Smith, GDEcD Deputy Commissioner Kristi Brigman, GDECD Commissioner Pat Wilson, Mayor Karl Riles, Liberty County Commission Chairman Donald Lovett, and LCDA Board members Luke Moses, Melissa Carter Ray, Willa Lewis, Marcus Sack, and State Representative Al Williams.
