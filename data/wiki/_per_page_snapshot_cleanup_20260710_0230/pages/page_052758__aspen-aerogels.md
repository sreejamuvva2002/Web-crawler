# Aspen Aerogels

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** thermal_management  ·  **Published:** 2026-04-20

**Source:** [Aspen Aerogels at Oppenheimer Conference: Strategic Restructuring and Growth By Investing.com](https://www.investing.com/news/transcripts/aspen-aerogels-at-oppenheimer-conference-strategic-restructuring-and-growth-93CH-4183495)  ·  `page_052758`

## Facts

- Aspen Aerogels presented at the Oppenheimer 28th Annual Technology, Internet & Communications Conference on Monday, 11 August 2025, outlining strategic restructuring and growth plans for its EV thermal barrier business.
- The company removed approximately $65 million in fixed costs to improve profitability and targets gross margins above 35% and EBITDA margins above 25%.
- Aspen Aerogels reported below 70% capacity utilization in Q1 and Q2 2024, with an EBIT breakeven point reduced to around $280 million in revenues.
- The lion's share of the company's EV thermal barrier revenue currently comes from General Motors, whose Equinox is the number one non-Tesla selling EV in the US.
- ACC (Stellantis) volumes are set to ramp up in 2026, with a revenue target of over $15 million for next year.
- Daimler is expected to bring volumes comparable to Stellantis starting in 2027, with potential additional volumes from new launches.
- Potential deals with Audi, Scania, and Porsche are anticipated, pending their decisions to switch cell suppliers to Samsung.
- Test vehicles for the Porsche Cayman boxer platform are already rolling with Aspen Aerogels' thermal barrier parts, though Porsche is rethinking its US EV launch timing.
- The company's flexible aerogel blanket provides passive thermal protection in EV batteries, replacing older polyurethane foams and allowing OEMs to push battery cells closer to their limits for faster charging and better cold/hot weather performance.
- Aspen Aerogels contemplated building a second manufacturing plant in Statesboro, Georgia, but pivoted in late 2023 to a variable supply model with supplemental manufacturing in China instead of proceeding with Plant 2.
- The original Plant 2 concept was designed to bring on $1.6 billion of revenue capacity in one phase, but the company shifted to smaller incremental supply increments to better fit demand curves.
- Significant production capacity for auto customers remains at the East Providence, Rhode Island plant, supplemented by external manufacturing relationships in China.
- The company delayered its organization in Q1 and Q2 2024 to streamline operations and reduce complexity after years of rapid growth.
- Aspen Aerogels maintains a strong balance sheet and requires minimal capital expenditures at this point to support future growth without building substantial new capacity.
- The company has historically defended its intellectual property aggressively against Chinese impersonators across the US, Europe, and Asia, including having patents validated in China.
- Don Young serves as President and CEO of Aspen Aerogels, while Ricardo Rodriguez is the CFO and Neil Baranovsky handles investor relations.
