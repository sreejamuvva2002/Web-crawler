# DENSO Manufacturing Arkansas, Inc.

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2019-09-09

**Source:** [DENSO Arkansas Facility Celebrates 7M Production Hours Without Lost Time](https://www.aftermarketnews.com/denso-arkansas-facility-celebrates-7m-production-hours-without-lost-time/)  ·  `page_053858`

## Facts

- DENSO Manufacturing Arkansas, Inc. is located in Osceola, Arkansas.
- The facility achieved more than seven million production hours without a lost-time accident.
- The Arkansas Department of Labor honored the facility for this milestone on Sept. 5.
- DENSO Manufacturing Arkansas hosted a celebration of the milestone as part of “Safety Day.”
- Lost time accidents are defined as job-related incidents where an employee is absent from the workplace for at least one day.
- The facility's last lost-time accident occurred in 2013.
- Since 2013, the facility has gone more than 2,240 days without an incident.
- The facility employs nearly 500 workers who have worked approximately 7.2 million hours.
- Over the last five years, the facility has maximized productivity while prioritizing employee safety.
- George Harguess serves as president of DENSO Manufacturing Arkansas.
- Harguess stated that everyone from junior employees to management fully supports the facility's culture of safety.
- The facility has developed various safety measures to protect employees since its founding over 15 years ago.
- In early 2016, the facility established the DENSO Manufacturing Arkansas Safety Committee.
- The Safety Committee is comprised of production employees who monitor existing safety policies.
- The committee educates employees on safety best practices and creates or updates safety initiatives.
- Michael Edwards works as a basic skills training sub leader and is a member of the DENSO Manufacturing Arkansas Safety Committee.
- Edwards stated that his daily priorities are productivity/efficiency and ensuring coworker safety.
- Kenichiro Ito serves as a senior executive officer for DENSO Corp. and CEO of DENSO’s North American headquarters.
- Ito emphasized that manufacturing practices must evolve to keep employees safe and that promoting strong safety policies is crucial for the company's success.
