# T1 Energy

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-03-20

**Source:** [T1 Energy to establish $850M solar cell facility in Texas | Manufacturing Dive](https://www.manufacturingdive.com/news/t1-energy-850m-solar-cell-facility-rockdale-texas-g2-austin-us-supply-chain/742986/)  ·  `page_001622`

## Facts

- T1 Energy, formerly known as Freyr Battery, announced plans to establish a $850 million, 5-gigawatt solar cell facility in Rockdale, Texas.
- The upcoming factory will employ up to 1,800 manufacturing jobs.
- The site will house eight lines with a mix of 210RN and 210N silicon wafers for the solar cells.
- T1 Energy aims to begin construction in Q2 or Q3.
- Chairman and CEO Daniel Barcelo said production is anticipated to begin in Q4 2026.
- The Rockdale plant advances T1 Energy’s new strategy to shift focus to solar energy, launched in November 2024 alongside the initial announcement of the facility.
- Chairman and CEO Daniel Barcelo stated that demand for these solutions in the U.S. is expected to continue growing.
- Barcelo noted that the emergence of power-intensive industries such as AI and cryptocurrency, and the electrification of the transportation sector will necessitate significant investment in the U.S. grid.
- He added that solar and battery storage is emerging as a preferred solution to provide low-carbon reliable, cost-competitive power while enhancing grid reliability.
- At the time of the strategy launch, the company operated as Freyr Battery and was mid-construction on a $2.57 billion battery cell manufacturing plant in Newnan, Georgia.
- The company axed those plans last month and sold the land for net proceeds of $22.5 million.
- Around the same time, the manufacturer underwent a full makeover with a new name, logo and ticker symbol as T1 Energy.
- The changes include relocating the company’s headquarters, for the second time in just over a year.
- The company was originally based in Luxembourg and moved its headquarters to Newnan, Georgia, in January 2024.
- Now, the company has made Austin, Texas, its home, according to a Feb. 10 press release.
- Barcelo stated that their corporate transformation positions them to build on their U.S. solar manufacturing position and leverage their team’s expertise in the battery market.
- He added that solar and battery storage developments are among the fastest-growing resources of electricity supply in the U.S. based on declining costs and the speed with which customers can bring projects online.
- Texas has been heavily involved in T1 Energy’s shifting focus on the U.S. solar and battery storage sector.
- Barcelo said the Rockdale plant is expected to be T1 Energy’s earnings and cash flow engine.
- He added that it’s “very important” that T1 Energy begins construction on the facility midway through the year to begin production by the end of 2026.
- The site is the company’s “second piece” of its “American-made supply chain.”
- The first piece is a 5-GW solar module manufacturing facility in Wilmer, Texas, which T1 Energy acquired last year from Trina Solar.
- Barcelo stated that when they have domestic cells and modules, the combination will enable them to be a very important part of the U.S. supply chain for solar.
- Jaime Gualy, EVP of corporate development, said the Wilmer plant gives T1 Energy an “American manufacturing platform” to maximize domestic production, with the Rockdale plant as a key step to achieving that goal.
- While the Rockdale site is being built, the company plans to pursue U.S.-based component suppliers in an effort to help customers qualify for Inflation Reduction Act tax incentives.
- The Wilmer plant’s output production is ahead of schedule.
- For the first two months of 2025, the facility exceeded T1 Energy’s forecast by nearly 50%.
- Barcelo stated that with four lines already on production and construction installation activities expected to be complete during the first half of 2025, they are on track to achieve their full year 2025 production target of 3.4 gigawatts at the Wilmer facility.
