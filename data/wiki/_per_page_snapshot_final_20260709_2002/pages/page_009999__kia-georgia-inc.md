# Kia Georgia, Inc.

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-07

**Source:** [Kia will invest over $200M to expand production in Georgia](https://valdostatoday.com/news-2/region/2023/07/kia-will-invest-over-200m-to-expand-production-in-georgia/)  ·  `page_009999`

## Facts

- Kia plans to invest over $200 million to expand production for the EV9 SUV creating almost 200 new jobs.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Kia will invest more than $200 million and create nearly 200 new jobs at its West Point plant.
- The investment is to accommodate assembly of the company’s highly anticipated all-electric 2024 EV9 three-row SUV.
- Through this production line transformation, the EV9 will become the fifth model to be assembled at Kia Georgia.
- The EV9 joins the Telluride, Sorento and Sportage SUVs, and the K5 mid-size sedan in assembly at the facility.
- EV9 assembly is expected to begin in the second quarter of 2024.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Georgia’s longstanding partnership with Kia has led to generational job creation and growth for the West Point area.
- Kemp noted that the project will bring more opportunity to hardworking Georgians and help propel the state toward its goal of becoming the e-mobility capital.
- Currently, more than 40 percent of Kia vehicles sold in the U.S. are manufactured in West Point.
- The addition of the EV9 to the lineup will further increase the facility’s output.
- The EV9 is the brand’s first three-row EV SUV and the first Kia EV to be assembled in North America.
- The EV9 is the flagship vehicle for the company’s global Plan S strategy focused on sustainable mobility leadership.
- Sean Yoon, President & CEO of Kia Motors North America and Kia Motors America, stated that like Telluride, the EV9 has the potential to be another change catalyst for Kia.
- Yoon described the EV9 as the most innovative vehicle they have ever built and a standout in the EV market and on the road.
- Kia Georgia is a certified Georgia Made manufacturer.
- The facility will continue to utilize the services of Georgia Quick Start, the nation’s top-ranked workforce development program.
- Georgia Quick Start operates the Kia Georgia Training Center.
- Quick Start’s comprehensive, customized training services will prepare new employees with the skills required to perform successfully on the new assembly line.
- Stuart Countess is President & CEO of Kia Georgia.
- Countess highlighted partnerships developed statewide with the Governor’s Office, the Department of Economic Development, and Georgia QuickStart.
- He also noted local partnerships with the City of West Point and Troup County.
- City of West Point Mayor Steve Tramell stated that Kia has been an outstanding corporate citizen and community partner for seventeen years.
- Tramell noted that Kia has helped improve the livelihoods of many people in West Point by providing employment opportunities and fostering economic growth.
- Senior Regional Project Manager Amanda Fields represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The project was a partnership with the West Point Development Authority, the City of West Point, Troup County, Georgia Center of Innovation’s Manufacturing and Energy teams, and Georgia Quick Start.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that since Georgia recruited Kia in 2006 to create long-lasting opportunities for West Georgia, Kia has exceeded expectations as a partner by giving back to its adopted community.
- Wilson noted that with EV9 now joining Georgia’s growing electrified lineup, they want to thank and congratulate Kia, partners in West Point, and countless other state and local agencies working to meet the needs of today and tomorrow’s leading industries.
- To date, Kia has invested more than $1.9 billion in the state of Georgia.
- Kia Georgia is responsible for more than 14,000 plant and supplier jobs in the region.
- Earlier this year, Governor Kemp and state lawmakers recognized January 31 as “Kia Day,” commending Kia’s vehicle manufacturing and its impact to the state.
- The recognition followed the 2006 announcement that a state-of-the-art vehicle assembly facility would be established in West Point, which was one of the biggest single foreign investments in Georgia’s history.
- From Georgia, Kia ships vehicles to sales markets around the globe, including the United States, Canada, Mexico, the Caribbean, Middle East and Pacific nations.
- As the emerging electric vehicle market continues to grow, Georgia has pursued job creation along the entire supply chain.
- This pursuit has resulted in more than $22.7 billion in investment and the creation of over 28,400 jobs since 2020.
- Kia Georgia, Inc. is the first manufacturing site in North America for Kia Corporation based in Seoul, Korea.
- With an annual capacity of 340,000 units, Kia Georgia is located on 2,200 acres in West Point, Georgia.
- The facility began mass production on November 16, 2009.
- Kia America is headquartered in Irvine, California.
- Kia America serves as the “Official Automotive Partner” of the NBA.
- Kia America offers a range of gasoline, hybrid, plug-in hybrid and electrified vehicles sold through a network of over 750 dealers in the U.S.
