## Facts

- Nidec Elesys Americas is based in Suwanee, Georgia.
- The company is a prominent player in the automotive electronics manufacturing sector.
- Nidec Elesys Americas has a reported annual revenue of $80.8 million.
- The company is part of the globally recognized Nidec Group.
- Elesys Americas focuses on designing and manufacturing sensors and modules for advanced driver-assistance systems (ADAS).
- Its facility in Suwanee, Georgia, is a critical part of a global supply chain that includes R&D functions, testing labs, and logistic centers.
- The company's product line includes occupant detection systems crucial for airbag deployment accuracy.
- The company's product line includes seatbelt reminder systems.
- The company's product line includes electronic control units (ECUs) that run safety diagnostics.
- The company's product line includes weight sensors to detect passenger size and position.
- The company's product line includes battery monitoring systems for electric vehicles.
- As of 2023, NHTSA regulations are pushing more stringent rules around driver-assistance and passenger protection systems.
- According to a 2022 NHTSA report, seatbelts saved over 14,000 lives in the U.S. alone.
- Roughly 2,500 people still die annually because they weren't buckled up.
- Elesys offers smart battery management ECU solutions that monitor real-time battery performance to ensure longer lifespan and safer operations for hybrid and electric vehicles.
- Direct clients often include Tier 1 suppliers and original equipment manufacturers (OEMs).
- Components must meet global safety standards like ISO 26262 and IATF 16949.
- Elesys components can be mass-produced while maintaining strict tolerances.
- Their ECUs and sensors integrate seamlessly with other systems due to modular design.
- Backed by the Nidec brand, Elesys parts are tested for consistent performance across different geographies and climates.
- Elesys works closely with automotive partners to tweak existing technologies for use in future patterns, like autonomous driving.
- In Suwanee, the company hosts one of the region's most well-regarded testing labs for passive safety technologies.
- Engineers carefully simulate crash scenarios, occupant movements, and sensor reliability under various conditions.
- Elesys conducts vibration testing to multi-year component lifecycle stress simulations.
- This significantly reduces failure rates and ensures compliance with automaker expectations and government safety standards.
- Although part of the global Nidec family headquartered in Japan, Elesys Americas operates semi-independently to better respond to North American market needs.
- Their Suwanee HQ handles not just manufacturing but also sales and regional support.
- This nearshore model helps reduce lead times, enhance communication with U.S. clients, and quickly adapt designs to meet changes in American laws or consumer preferences.
- Elesys engineers in Georgia worked directly with a Michigan-based EV maker to incorporate customized battery sensor modules.
- The result was a successful launch within a shorter development cycle—months ahead of competitor timelines.
- The company aligns itself closely with the Japanese philosophy of Kaizen or continuous improvement.
- Employees undergo routine training on new testing techniques, industry best practices, and customer requirements.
- The company invests heavily in machine upgrades, automation, and quality management systems.
- Elesys's defect rate is among the lowest in the sector according to Auto Alliance benchmarks.
- Demand for autonomous vehicles (AVs) is rising and EV sales are climbing.
- Elesys is preparing for the transition by investing in sensor fusion technologies that combine LiDAR, radar, and camera input.
- Elesys is developing redundant safety systems for Level 4 and Level 5 autonomy.
- Elesys is forming partnerships with AI firms to integrate predictive analytics into safety modules.
