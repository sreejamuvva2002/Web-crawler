# Honda Transmission Manufacturing Plant in Georgia

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2006-05-12

**Source:** [Honda Transmission Plant in Georgia Celebrates 20 Years of Operation  - Greater Haralson Chamber](https://business.haralson.org/haralson-latest-news/Details/honda-transmission-plant-in-georgia-celebrates-20-years-of-operation-332160)  ·  `page_009116`

## Facts

- Honda Transmission Manufacturing Plant in Georgia (TMP-G) began production in Tallapoosa in 2006.
- The plant started production of 5-speed transmissions on May 12, 2006.
- Since its opening, Honda associates in Georgia have built more than five million transmissions.
- The Georgia plant is the exclusive source of the advanced 10-speed automatic transmission for Honda and Acura vehicles built in the U.S.
- Honda has grown its workforce at the Georgia facility to nearly 500 associates.
- The Tallapoosa facility employs about 480 associates.
- The plant has a total investment of more than $485 million.
- The facility has an annual capacity to build 375,000 transmissions.
- TMP-G was formerly called Honda Precision Parts of Georgia (HPPG).
- The plant produced 6-speed automatic transmissions before adding a second assembly line in 2017.
- TMP-G is the first Honda plant in the world to produce the advanced 10-speed automatic transmission (10AT).
- Five years ago, the facility revamped its original production line to increase production of the 10AT.
- The 10AT transmission is featured in the Honda Pilot, Passport, Odyssey, and the Acura MDX.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that since its grand opening, the facility has provided hundreds of jobs for the community while serving as a strategic location for Honda's global production process.
- Arturo Valdes, TMP-G Plant Lead, noted the facility will play an important role in the future supporting North American production.
- Last year relative to the article, Honda awarded more than $950,000 in funding to non-profit community-based organizations in Georgia.
- TMP-G associates volunteered over 3,200 hours at numerous events across the region last year.
- The Tallapoosa facility is one of two Honda automobile transmission plants and one of 12 major Honda manufacturing facilities in America.
- The plant serves as the lead global facility for production of the company’s state-of-the-art 10-speed automatic transmission.
- The plant uses domestic and globally made parts for its transmissions.
- Over the past 20 years, the plant added the casting and machining of transmission cases to its initial assembly operations.
- The facility spans 575,000 square feet.
