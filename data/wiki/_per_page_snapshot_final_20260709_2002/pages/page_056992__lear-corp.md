# Lear Corp.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2021-10-29

**Source:** [Lear Corp. Acquires Interior Comfort Systems Unit for $203M - DBusiness Magazine](https://www.dbusiness.com/daily-news/lear-corp-acquires-interior-comfort-systems-unit-for-203m/)  ·  `page_056992`

## Facts

- Lear Corp., based in Southfield, is a global leader in automotive seating and e-systems.
- Lear entered into a definitive agreement to acquire substantially all of Kongsberg Automotive’s interior comfort systems (ICS) business unit for $203.4 million.
- Kongsberg Automotive is based in Switzerland and has operations in Novi.
- The ICS business unit specializes in comfort seating solutions, with a leadership position in seating product areas experiencing growth within the industry, including massage, lumbar, seat heat, and ventilation.
- Ray Scott, president and CEO of Lear, stated that integrating Kongsberg Automotive’s Interior Comfort Systems business unit will build on strengths in design, engineering, and operational excellence to advance their strategy of offering a complete suite of premier comfort seat systems to customers and consumers.
- The acquisition represents an opportunity for Lear to capture more content-per-vehicle by providing an optimized solution with a better value proposition in terms of cost, quality, and performance.
- Lear looks forward to welcoming the talented ICS workforce to its team.
- The purchase seeks to advance Lear’s vertical integration and expand product offerings into specialized comfort seating solutions that improve vehicle performance and packaging.
- Automakers are looking for greater product differentiation, increased efficiency, and improved performance, especially in the luxury, SUV, and electric vehicle segments.
- Frank Orsini, executive vice president and president of seating at Lear, stated that Lear is integrating seating comfort with more features to meet automaker demands and that the acquisition complements their strategic portfolio of vertically integrated design solutions and comfort capabilities.
- The Kongsberg ICS business unit brings operational leadership personnel and more than 3,800 employees globally from its production, engineering, and sales facilities throughout Asia, Europe, and North America.
- The ICS business unit is expected to generate approximately $300 million in revenue this year.
- Joerg Buchheim, president and CEO of Kongsberg Automotive, stated that the transaction provides room for additional financial maneuvering and allows Kongsberg to fully concentrate on its path towards highly focused and profitable growth.
- The transaction is subject to regulatory approvals and other customary closing conditions and adjustments.
- The acquisition is expected to close in the first quarter of 2022.
