# National Electric Vehicle Infrastructure (NEVI) Formula Program

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** government_support  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Alternative Fuels Data Center: National Electric Vehicle Infrastructure (NEVI) Formula Program](https://afdc.energy.gov/laws/12744)  ·  `page_003972`

## Facts

- The U.S. Department of Transportation’s Federal Highway Administration NEVI Formula Program provides funding to states to strategically deploy electric vehicle charging infrastructure.
- Funding is available for up to 80% of eligible project costs.
- Eligible costs include the acquisition, installation, and network connection of EV chargers to facilitate data collection, access, and reliability.
- Eligible costs include proper operation and maintenance of EV chargers.
- Eligible costs include long-term EV charger data sharing.
- EV chargers must be non-proprietary and allow for open-access payment methods.
- EV chargers must be publicly available or available to authorized commercial motor vehicle operators from more than one company.
- EV chargers must be located along designated FHWA Alternative Fuel Corridors.
- If a state and their FHWA Division Office determine that all Alternative Fuel Corridors in the state have been fully built out, the state can propose alternative public locations and roads for EV charger installation.
- FHWA must distribute NEVI Formula Program funds made available each fiscal year through FY 2026.
- Each state receives an amount equal to the state FHWA funding formula determined by 23 U.S. Code 104.
- To receive funding, states must submit updated plans to their FHWA Division Office for review and publicly post them annually.
- State plans must describe how the state intends to distribute NEVI funds and related physical and cybersecurity strategies.
- State plans must include a Community Engagement Outcomes Report.
- Federal law requires that 10% of NEVI Formula funding be set aside each fiscal year for DOT to provide grants for states and localities requiring additional assistance to strategically deploy EV charging infrastructure.
- The FY 2022 10% NEVI set aside funding was used for the EV Charger Reliability and Accessibility Accelerator Program, which provided grants focused on repairing or replacing non-operational EV chargers.
- The remainder of set-aside funds were allocated to the Charging and Fueling Infrastructure Round 1B awards.
- The program is classified as an Incentive under Federal jurisdiction.
- The program was enacted on Nov 15, 2021.
- The program supports EVs and PHEVs technologies.
