# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-04-14

## Facts

- Professor Juanita (Babet) Villena-Alvarez presented an incredible story of Hyundai's growth from its founding in 1967 to becoming a huge conglomerate during a two-part USCB-OLLI course titled “THE KOREAN WAVE 2024 – CHANGING DEMOGRAPHICS OF THE LOWCOUNTRY”.
- The course covered the massive investments by Hyundai in southeast Georgia, located only about an hour drive from Hilton Head.
- Georgia Power’s Plant Vogtle, now the largest nuclear generating plant in the U.S., is completed and within 100 miles of the HMG Metaplant.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that in a single year, Georgia broke ground on the largest project in state history.
- Georgia landed multiple suppliers across the state for Hyundai’s Metaplant.
- LGES was welcomed to Bryan County.
- Georgia is continuing to build on success to make itself the e-mobility capital of the nation.
- Major investments ultimately go to hardworking Georgians in the form of paychecks, improved schools and infrastructure.
- Hyundai Motor Group (HMG) is just one division of the larger Hyundai conglomerate.
- South Korean firms including Sam Sung, LG, and SK-Battery have invested over $200 Billion in plants in the U.S.
- Hyundai manufactures Hyundai, KIA, and Genesis autos, as well as steel and shipping.
- Over 8,100 jobs will be created by HMG in Georgia.
- Over 14,300 jobs will be created including supply-chain providers of components.
- The Panamax capable Port of Savannah will be enlarged with additional new Ship to Shore Cranes.
- Savannah is already one of the busiest ports in the U.S.
- The Talmadge Bridge over the Savannah River will be raised to accommodate Panamax Ships.
- Hyundai is now the #3 world auto manufacturer, after Toyota and VW.
- HMG plans to build 500,000 vehicles/year at the new GA MetaPlant.
- BMW produced 412,000 vehicles in 2022 as a comparison point mentioned by the professor.
- The Hyundai Meta Plant covers almost 3,000 acres off of I-16.
- Hyundai is partnering with Georgia Tech to collaborate on the use of Hydrogen for future Hydrogen Fuel Cell Vehicles.
- In August 2023, Hyundai pulled out of its large plant in China.
- Hyundai was founded in 1967 by Chung Ju Yung, who was born in North Korea.
- A majority of South Koreans are Christians and share the values of western civilization.
