# Kia

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Kia Expands Georgia Plant to Produce Electric EV9 SUV With $200M Investment - World-Energy](https://www.world-energy.org/article/34459.html)  ·  `page_010068`

## Facts

- Kia is expanding its only manufacturing plant in the US to make room for its first electric three-row SUV.
- The Kia EV9 will be the automaker’s first EV assembled in North America.
- Assembly of the EV9 will begin at Kia's West Point, Georgia, plant next year.
- Kia announced an over $200 million expansion Wednesday.
- The expansion is creating nearly 200 additional jobs in the area.
- The EV9 will be the fifth model assembled at Kia's Georgia facility but the first all-electric vehicle to join the lineup.
- Sean Yoon, president and CEO of Kia North America, said the “EV9 has the potential to be another ‘game changer’ for Kia.”
- Sean Yoon added that the EV9 will be the most sophisticated vehicle Kia has ever built, a standout in the EV market and on the road, and assembled in West Point, Georgia.
- The EV9 is the largest electric car from Kia so far and its first three-row electric SUV.
- Kia says the EV9 is reshaping the company with a “bold vision for the new era of electrification.”
- The EV9 includes some of the latest in-vehicle technology, including Kia’s most advanced Highway Driving Pilot, OTA updates, onboard power generator functionality (V2L), and ultra-wideband-based Digital Key.
- Kia claims the EV9 offers “true SUV capabilities,” including 7.8-inch ground clearance, up to 81.9 cubic feet of cargo space behind the first row, up to 5,000 lbs towing capacity, and available torque-vectoring dual-motor AWD.
- The EV9 is based on the Hyundai Motor Group’s 800-volt E-GMP platform (the same one used to power the IONIQ 5, IONIQ 6, and Kia EV6).
- The EV9 includes fast-charging capabilities, designed to go from 10% to 80% charge (adding 148 miles of driving range) in under 25 minutes.
- The EV9 will be available in two powertrain options.
- The standard powertrain is a 76.1 kWh battery (fourth-generation) with a 215 hp (160 kW) motor.
- A 99.8 kWh battery pack will be offered with a single motor, targeting 300 miles range, or a dual-motor 379 hp torque-vectoring AWD configuration with 516 lb-ft of torque.
- Kia is expected to begin assembling the EV9 at its West Point, Georgia, plant starting in the second quarter of 2024.
- The automaker began delivering the electric SUV to customers in South Korea last month, gathering 13,000 preorders in its first eight days on the market.
- Although Kia has yet to release prices for the US market, it is expected that, based on the first European prices, the EV9 will start around $65,000 to $70,000 for the larger battery pack and around $60,000 for the standard model.
