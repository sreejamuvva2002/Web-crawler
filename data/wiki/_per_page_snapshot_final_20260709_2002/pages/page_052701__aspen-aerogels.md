# Aspen Aerogels

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Aspen Aerogels stock jumps on DOE loan commitment for new plant By Investing.com](https://in.investing.com/news/stock-market-news/aspen-aerogels-stock-jumps-on-doe-loan-commitment-for-new-plant-4474283)  ·  `page_052701`

## Facts

- Aspen Aerogels shares surged more than 13% following the announcement of a major funding development and strong preliminary Q3 2024 results.
- The company secured a conditional commitment from the U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) for a loan of up to $670.6 million.
- The funding is part of the Advanced Technology Vehicles Manufacturing loan program and will finance the construction of Aspen's second aerogel manufacturing facility in Register, Georgia.
- The new plant will provide a revenue capacity between $1.2 billion and $1.6 billion.
- The facility will produce PyroThin thermal barriers designed to enhance battery safety for electric vehicles (EVs).
- Aspen CEO Donald Young stated that the proposed loan would fully fund the required CAPEX for the second aerogel manufacturing facility, playing a key role in scaling PyroThin supply for the Thermal Barrier business.
- Aspen's PyroThin product has attracted six major OEM contracts in the U.S. and Europe.
- The PyroThin product contributed to a 277% year-over-year growth in thermal barrier revenues.
- Aspen reported preliminary Q3 revenue of $117 million.
- Aspen reported preliminary Q3 adjusted EBITDA of $25 million.
- Aspen posted a preliminary Q3 net loss of $13 million, impacted by a one-time $27.5 million charge from the extinguishment of a convertible note.
- CFO Ricardo Rodriguez stated that if finalized, the loan's terms will provide an opportunity to optimize capital structure and capture long-term opportunities.
- The Register Plant project is expected to create 255 permanent jobs.
- The project aligns with the DOE's goal of supporting domestic manufacturing for zero-emissions vehicles.
