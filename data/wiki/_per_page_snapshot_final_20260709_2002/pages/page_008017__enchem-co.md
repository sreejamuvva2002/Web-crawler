# Enchem Co.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Enchem to more than double electrolyte production in US - KED Global](https://www.kedglobal.com/batteries/newsView/ked202211280020)  ·  `page_008017`

## Facts

- South Korea’s electrolyte maker Enchem Co. aims to expand its annual production capacity in the US to 300,000 tons by 2026.
- The expansion aims to meet increasing demand for electrolytes from the US as well as Korean electric vehicle battery giants LG Energy Solution Ltd. and SK On Co.
- Enchem plans to increase the annual manufacturing capacity of its existing plant in Georgia to 140,000 tons by 2024.
- Enchem plans to start producing up to 160,000 tons of electrolyte in 2026 at factories under construction in four other states.
- Enchem aims to manufacture 60,000 tons and 20,000 tons of the key battery material at Michigan-based and Ohio-based factories, respectively.
- The Michigan and Ohio plants are jointly invested in by LG Energy Solution and General Motors Co.
- Enchem plans to produce 40,000 tons of electrolytes from Kentucky-based facilities.
- The Kentucky facilities are invested in by SK On and Ford Motor Co.
- Enchem plans to produce another 40,000 tons from three Tennessee-based factories.
- Each of the three Tennessee factories is jointly invested by SK On-Ford, LG Energy Solution-GM, and Ford-Volkswagen.
- The Korean firm will apply for licenses to produce electrolytes from the four states once the plants are constructed.
- Enchem is forecast to rank fourth in the global electrolyte production market this year.
- Enchem's global market share is forecast to make up 6% with 50,000 tons this year.
- The company aims to increase its annual production capacity to 100,000 tons next year.
- This next year's capacity will account for 10% of the global market.
- The company is currently operating six factories in Korea, Poland, China and the US.
- The company is planning to set up six new plants in Hungary, China and the US.
