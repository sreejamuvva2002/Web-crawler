# Blue Bird Corp.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-05-26

**Source:** [Georgia School Bus Manufacturer Blue Bird Ratifies Historic First](https://hoodline.com/2024/05/georgia-school-bus-manufacturer-blue-bird-ratifies-historic-first-union-contract-over-1-500-workers-to-see-pay-increase/)  ·  `page_052918`

## Facts

- Workers at Blue Bird Corp., a notable Georgia school bus manufacturer, voted in favor to ratify their first union contract, concluding a year-long effort that began when they opted to unionize.
- The agreement between United Steelworkers and Blue Bird spans three years and promises a minimum of 12% pay raise for the 1,500 workers covered.
- Certain employees, particularly those on the lower end of the pay scale, will see their incomes increase by over 40%.
- The company has agreed to contribute to a retirement plan for workers, share profits, and enhance health and safety standards under the new contract.
- Blue Bird received a substantial sum of $40 million in federal aid focused on manufacturing electric school buses.
- President Joe Biden lauded the agreement, stating, "Congratulations to members of the United Steelworkers and to Blue Bird for proving once again that meeting our clean energy goals is an opportunity to create good-paying union jobs for American workers."
- Despite Blue Bird's initial opposition to unionization, CEO Phil Horlock described the contract negotiations as "very collaborative" and expressed optimism about the future.
- CEO Phil Horlock stated in a statement obtained by WABE: "We reached an agreement which provides positive outcomes for all parties involved and will continue to drive our One Team, high-performance culture."
- The new terms are expected to enhance Blue Bird's ability to attract quality workers by offering competitive pay and developmental opportunities.
- This marks a critical achievement for the United Steelworkers in a region typically resistant to organized labor.
- Dan Flippo, United Steelworkers District 9 Director, emphasized the importance of worker representation, especially when federal investments are at stake, stating: "Federal investments like these must come with a seat at the table for workers."
- Blue Bird is the largest private employer in Peach County, Georgia.
- Georgia has a low union membership rate compared to the national average, with only 4.4% of workers belonging to unions versus the national rate that dipped to 10% last year.
- The passage of this union contract at Blue Bird could signal a change in the tide for the labor movement within the state and potentially beyond.
