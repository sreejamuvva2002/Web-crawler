# Hyundai Motor Group and SK On Joint Venture for Bartow County EV Battery Facility

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-12-08

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Group and SK On To Build EV Battery Facility in Bartow County](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/hyundai-motor-group-and-sk-on-to-build-ev-battery-facility-in-bartow-county-301698015.html)  ·  `page_000150`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Hyundai Motor Group (HMG) and SK On have selected a site in Bartow County for a new electric vehicle (EV) battery manufacturing facility.
- The new facility will supply Hyundai Motor Group's plants in the U.S.
- Stakeholders estimate the project will create more than 3,500 new jobs through approximately $4-5 billion of investment in Bartow County.
- Hyundai Motor Group and SK On recently signed a memorandum of understanding (MOU) regarding the partnership for a new EV battery facility in the U.S.
- Details of the partnership are still in development.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Hyundai Motor Group and SK On are valued partners and key players in Georgia's ever-growing automotive industry.
- Governor Brian Kemp noted his administration has focused on bringing jobs and opportunity to overlooked communities and expressed pride in the companies investing further in the No. 1 state for business.
- SK On was established in 2021 as the lithium-ion battery subsidiary branch of SK Innovation.
- SK On currently employs more than 2,000 Georgians at its SK Battery America facility in Commerce.
- In July, SK Battery America announced a partnership with the Work for Warriors Georgia program to hire veterans, servicemembers, and their families at its Georgia operations.
- The new facility's construction is subject to execution of relevant agreements and final board decisions by HMG and SK On.
- The facility will be located at Bartow Centre, a zoned manufacturing and industrial site on Highway 411.
- The companies are aiming to begin operations in 2025.
- Bartow County Sole Commissioner Steve Taylor expressed pleasure with the decision and thanked the owners of the former Bartow Centre Industrial property.
- Commissioner Steve Taylor stated the project will have regional benefit for job seekers and establish Bartow in Georgia's EV ecosystem, noting Bartow's strong history and talent base in advanced manufacturing and innovation.
- Director of Korean Investment Yoonie Kim represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development's (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The project involved partnerships with Georgia EMC, Development Authority of Bartow County, Cartersville-Bartow County Department of Economic Development, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated HMG and SK have been pioneering partners for Georgia for decades as major drivers for the state's automotive renaissance and as the first Korean manufacturer to locate in the state, respectively.
- Commissioner Pat Wilson noted that supporting cooperation across the EV ecosystem is creating a fully integrated supply chain for automotive OEMs while connecting battery manufacturers with recyclers to close the loop on battery manufacturing.
- Commissioner Pat Wilson expressed excitement for the future jobs this will create for Bartow County and northwest Georgia, and gratitude for community and utility partner support.
- Georgia's prime location, major ports system, extensive infrastructure, skilled workforce, and pro-business climate have made it attractive for sustainable industries.
- Georgia is positioned as a hub for the electric transportation industry.
- Excluding this announcement, EV-related projects announced in Georgia since 2020 total approximately $17 billion in investment and more than 22,800 new jobs.
- Hyundai Motor Group separately announced in May plans to invest $5.54 billion in Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America, an EV manufacturing facility in Bryan County.
- Hyundai Motor Group broke ground on the Metaplant America facility in October with Governor Kemp, other state leaders, and local and federal officials.
- Hyundai Motor Group is a global enterprise that has created a value chain based on mobility, steel, construction, logistics, finance, IT, and service.
- Hyundai Motor Group has about 250,000 employees worldwide.
- Hyundai Motor Group's mobility brands include Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis.
- SK On is a global leading electric vehicle (EV) battery developer, manufacturer, and solutions provider.
- SK On aims to become a world leader in the clean energy industry by leveraging its global production base and R&D capabilities, as well as production and quality management know-how.
- SK On was launched as an independent company in 2021 after SK Innovation, South Korea's largest energy company, split off its battery business.
- SK On is headquartered in Seoul, South Korea, with a worldwide presence of battery plants currently operating or under construction across the United States, Europe, and Asia.
