# Circular City Forum on Sustainability & Clean Energy

**Type:** event  ·  **Role:** research_and_development  ·  **Published:** 2025-10-21

**Source:** [No Quick Fixes for Atlanta Sustainability Startups, Mobility Giants Tackling Systemic Global Problems  - Global Atlanta](https://www.globalatlanta.com/no-quick-fixes-for-atlanta-sustainability-startups-mobility-giants-tackling-systemic-global-problems/)  ·  `page_049976`

## Facts

- The Circular City forum, sponsored by Atlanta International School, was held at its new Sandy Springs campus along the Chattahoochee River to spotlight innovators creating green technology models for trade, investment, or educational exchange.
- Joulea provides an operating system that helps building managers uncover energy leakages and direct investments to fix them, using drones and AI to crunch usage data and make real-time recommendations.
- Ramtin Motahar of Joulea noted that data centers already consume 4 percent of U.S. electricity, with the Department of Energy projecting this proportion to rise to 12 percent by 2028.
- Motahar stated that buildings consume about 40 percent of energy across residential and commercial sectors, making them the largest consumption of energy across all industries.
- Joulea is currently being entertained for pilots in places like Canada and Europe to help portfolio managers cut consumption and reduce costs.
- Emrgy is an 11-year-old Atlanta startup that assembles hydroelectric turbines into modules and deploys them in irrigation canals, securing long-term contracts and power-purchase agreements with utilities.
- Emrgy CEO Emily Morris explained that the company makes more economic value by selling power on 30-year contracts than by selling hardware one time.
- Emrgy's technology aids in water conservation by modernizing outdated infrastructure, addressing water waste spent on cooling massive server farms for data centers.
- Morris noted that half of the water diverted from places like the Chattahoochee to deliver to battery factories or automotive and food/bev sectors is lost along the way through seepage or cracks in 100-year-old water infrastructure.
- XiQ co-founder and CEO Karlos Walkes introduced a patented FleetQi device that replaces keyed ignitions in golf carts, enabling zero-emissions vehicle tracking and data collection.
- XiQ's carbon-reduction strategy bets that more capable zero-emissions vehicles will encourage consumers to forego gas-guzzling cars or get rid of them altogether.
- The company is eyeing a United Arab Emirates distributor to capitalize on rapid development with little zoning restriction in the Middle East.
- Michelin vice president Olivier Brauen stated that ninety percent of a tire's environmental impact comes from its time installed on the car, making efficiency improvements more critical than production or recycling efforts.
- The France-based tire giant operates a factory in South Carolina and boasts 30,000 employees around the U.S.
- Michelin has invested some $400 million in metro Atlanta and instituted net-zero goals while buying a subsidiary to recover key materials from recycled tires.
- Brauen noted that disparate environmental regulations across 50 states create considerable balkanization that is a real problem for scaling circularity in the U.S.
- SK Battery America operates a Commerce, Ga.-based factory reporting to South Korea's SK On and produces electric vehicle batteries for automakers.
- SK Battery America government affairs and communications director Christian Bokich stated the company is recycling scrap metal shavings into its supply chain while shifting battery chemistry away from nickel cadmium toward lithium iron phosphate to reduce strategic exposure and environmental risk.
- SK has landed deals that will see its Commerce plant pivot to making battery energy storage systems to offset declining electric vehicle demand.
- Bokich emphasized the growing need for designing batteries with recycling in mind and transitioning original materials sourcing to countries that are not controversial.
- Emrgy's CEO Morris highlighted that all clean energy subsidies for hydro are preserved through 2033 under the current administration, positioning the company well despite potential future policy swings.
- Panelists noted that corporate sustainability efforts are being buoyed by consumer preferences and the demands of a younger workforce bringing environmental idealism into their companies.
