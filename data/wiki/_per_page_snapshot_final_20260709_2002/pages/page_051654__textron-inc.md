# Textron Inc.

**Source:** [Textron Announces Intent to Separate its Industrial Segment, Enhancing Strategic Focus as a Pure-Play Aerospace and Defense Company | INN](https://investingnews.com/textron-announces-intent-to-separate-its-industrial-segment-enhancing-strategic-focus-as-a-pure-play-aerospace-and-defense-company/)  ·  `page_051654`

## Facts

- Textron Inc. announced its intent to separate its Industrial segment from its core aerospace and defense businesses to enhance strategic focus and drive long-term stakeholder value.
- The separation will result in New Textron becoming a pure-play aerospace and defense company aligned with Textron Aviation, Bell, and Textron Systems.
- Textron intends to explore multiple paths for the separation, including a sale of the Industrial businesses or a tax-free separation into a standalone publicly traded company.
- Lisa M. Atherton serves as Chief Executive Officer of Textron.
- The planned separation creates greater clarity and focus for both New Textron and Industrial, which operate in distinct markets with unique business opportunities and investment requirements.
- As separate companies, each is expected to benefit from enhanced agility and focus, tailored capital allocation strategies, increased strategic flexibility, compelling investment profiles, and distinct management teams.
- Scott C. Donnelly serves as Textron's Executive Chairman.
- The Board of Directors concluded that pursuing the separation is the right approach to sharpen strategic focus and support long-term value creation for shareholders.
- New Textron expects over $12 billion in expected 2026 revenues and $19 billion in backlog.
- New Textron will be a premier pure-play aerospace & defense company with world-class engineering capabilities, a long track record of innovation, and proven manufacturing and support operations.
- New Textron's core franchises include general aviation under the Cessna and Beechcraft brands, military and commercial rotorcraft under the Bell brand, and Textron Systems' differentiated suite of aerospace and defense products and services.
- New Textron expects a successful separation to increase its revenue growth profile and operating margins while maintaining a strong balance sheet and consistent capital allocation priorities, including investments in research and development and capital expenditures.
- Industrial is expected to have over $3 billion in expected 2026 revenues.
- Industrial is composed of Kautex and Textron Specialized Vehicles.
- Kautex is a leader in plastic fuel systems, battery enclosures, and clear-vision systems for the automotive industry.
- Textron Specialized Vehicles is a manufacturer of specialized vehicles and equipment with globally recognized brands such as E-Z-GO, PACE Technologies, Jacobsen, and TUG Technologies.
- Atherton stated confidence that the separation will enable Industrial to build on its strong foundation and deliver enhanced value for employees, customers, and shareholders.
- The Company is targeting completion of the separation within 12 to 18 months, subject to customary conditions including regulatory approvals and final Board approval.
- There can be no assurance regarding the ultimate timing or structure of the proposed separation or that a transaction will be completed.
- Textron will continue to operate its Industrial businesses in alignment with its current strategy during the separation pursuit, including continued investments in growth, margin improvement, and innovation.
- Textron announced its first-quarter results in a separate press release issued today.
- Goldman Sachs & Co. LLC is serving as financial advisor to Textron.
- Latham & Watkins LLP is serving as legal advisor to Textron.
- The press release contains forward-looking statements regarding the separation, expected revenues, timing, benefits, and performance, which involve known and unknown risks and uncertainties.
- Factors that could cause actual results to differ materially include board approval, regulatory approvals (including IRS rulings), closing conditions, execution risks, costs or disruptions from the separation, and potential delays in receiving consents or approvals.
