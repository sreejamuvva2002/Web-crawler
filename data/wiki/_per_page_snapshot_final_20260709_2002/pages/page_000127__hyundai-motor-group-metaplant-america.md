# Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America Ushers in the Era of Future Mobility | Hyundai Motor Group](https://www.hyundaimotorgroup.com/en/story/CONT0000000000174940)  ·  `page_000127`

## Facts

- Hyundai Motor Group celebrated the grand opening of Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) in Ellabell, Georgia, on March 26.
- HMGMA is the Group’s third production hub in the United States, following Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Alabama and Kia Autoland Georgia.
- HMGMA operates as a Software Defined Factory (SDF) that integrates AI and robotics-powered automation.
- The Group's investment in Georgia related to HMGMA and joint battery ventures with LG Energy Solution and SK On is $12.6 billion.
- This $12.6 billion investment represents the largest single investment in Georgia's history.
- The investment includes a total of 8,500 jobs at HMGMA by 2031.
- Group’s total investments in Georgia are expected to create nearly 40,000 direct and indirect jobs.
- These investments are expected to generate $4.6 billion in individual earnings every year, according to the Center for Automotive Research.
- As of the end of 2024, more than $2.5 billion in capital investment has been announced by HMGMA suppliers across 12 counties in Georgia.
- Approximately 6,900 jobs have been announced by HMGMA suppliers in those 12 counties as of the end of 2024.
- The $2.5 billion supplier investment more than doubles the additional $1 billion investment originally anticipated.
- Prior to its grand opening, HMGMA began production of the IONIQ 5 in October of last year.
- In March, the plant commenced mass production of Hyundai Motor Company’s electrified flagship SUV, the IONIQ 9.
- Kia models are slated to join the HMGMA production line next year.
- Genesis vehicles are expected to join production at HMGMA in the near future.
- The plant leverages an integrated mixed-model production system and is equipped to manufacture hybrid vehicles.
- Grand opening attendees included Executive Chair Euisun Chung, Vice Chair Jaehoon Chang, Hyundai Motor Company President and CEO José Muñoz, Kia President and CEO Ho Sung Song, Governor Brian P. Kemp, U.S. Representative Buddy Carter, Georgia Institute of Technology President Ángel Cabrera, and Korean Ambassador Hyundong Cho.
- Executive Chair Euisun Chung declared: “With HMGMA, we are building the future of mobility together with America, in America.”
- Governor Brian P. Kemp stated that HMGMA is a once-in-a-generation opportunity made possible by partnerships with job creators and highlighted collaborations with Georgia’s universities, technical colleges, and Georgia Quick Start’s on-site training facility.
- Governor Kemp noted that Georgia's award-winning infrastructure, such as its ports, connects companies to markets around the nation and globe.
- Executive Chair Chung and Governor Kemp signed the hood of the first IONIQ 5 to roll off the HMGMA production line.
- HMGMA has an annual production capacity of 300,000 units.
- The plant scaled up advanced manufacturing technologies originally developed and validated at the Hyundai Motor Group Innovation Center Singapore (HMGICS).
- HMGMA features an automated production system connecting order collection, procurement, logistics, and assembly through AI, IT, robotics, and data technologies.
- The plant includes a 41-acre ecological park with walking trails, sports fields, and picnic areas.
- The plant will pilot Boston Dynamics’ humanoid robot, ‘All-New Atlas,’ to support its workforce.
- Hyundai Mobis established its largest global production facility at HMGMA to manufacture 300,000 battery systems annually along with cockpit, front-end, chassis, and PE component modules.
- Hyundai Glovis operates a Consolidation Center (CC) and Vehicle Processing Center (VPC) within HMGMA, using autonomous drones with AI-powered forecasting for just-in-time parts delivery.
- Hyundai Steel processes ultra-high-strength automotive steel plates at the Georgia Steel Service Center (SSC) within HMGMA, marking its first use of aluminum-steel hybrid processing equipment.
- Hyundai Transys established a seat production facility at HMGMA with an annual output of 420,000 units using advanced automation technologies.
- Hyundai WIA deploys autonomous robots for logistics automation at HMGMA, while Hyundai Rotem supplied equipment for the press shop.
- Construction is nearing completion on a joint battery cell plant between Hyundai Motor Group and LG Energy Solution targeting an annual capacity of 30 GWh.
- The LG Energy Solution joint venture is scheduled to open next year and will produce enough batteries for approximately 360,000 vehicles per year based on the IONIQ 5's 84 kWh pack.
- Battery cells from the joint venture will be processed at Hyundai Mobis before being supplied to HMGMA, Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Alabama, and Kia Autoland Georgia.
- Seventeen key suppliers are either expanding or establishing new operations near HMGMA.
- HMGMA's completion brings Hyundai Motor Group's total U.S. production network capacity to 1 million units annually.
- The network includes Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Alabama (completed in 2005 with a capacity of 360,000 units) and Kia Autoland Georgia (completed in 2010 with a capacity of 340,000 units).
- HMG is expected to surpass 30 million cumulative vehicle sales in the U.S. this year, nearly 40 years after launching the Hyundai Excel in 1986.
- The Group plans to expand HMGMA’s annual production capacity to 500,000 units and establish an integrated U.S. production system capable of manufacturing 1.2 million vehicles annually.
- Executive Chair Euisun Chung announced a commitment to invest $21 billion in the U.S. over the next four years during a White House visit on March 24.
- In Louisiana, HMG is preparing to establish an Electric Arc Furnace-based integrated steel mill with an annual production capacity of 2.7 million tons.
- HMG plans to accelerate commercialization of U.S.-based affiliates Boston Dynamics, Supernal, and Motional, while strengthening collaboration in autonomous driving, robotics, AI, and advanced air mobility (AAM).
- Additional investments will support nuclear and renewable energy, as well as the expansion of EV charging infrastructure.
