# Proterra

**Type:** Company  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[vehicle-manufacturing|Vehicle Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** Georgia, United States

## Overview

Proterra is a leading designer and manufacturer of zero-emission heavy-duty electric buses and charging systems. In August 2018 the company was selected by the Georgia Department of Administrative Services to supply battery-electric buses and charging stations under the Statewide Contract for Public Mass Transit & Transportation.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Supplier of zero-emission battery-electric buses and charging infrastructure to the state of Georgia's public transit agencies.

## Key facts

- Proterra was selected by the Georgia Department of Administrative Services as a vendor to supply zero-emission battery-electric buses and charging stations for the Statewide Contract.
- The contract allows Georgia transit agencies to purchase Proterra buses and chargers through a pre-established statewide agreement, streamlining procurement.
- Proterra’s Catalyst E2 series achieves 22 MPGe, far surpassing conventional diesel buses that average 3.86 MPG.
- Buses feature lightweight carbon-fiber-reinforced composite bodies that maximize vehicle life and reduce repairs and maintenance costs.
- Proterra charging systems are high-power DC fast chargers compliant with J1772 CCS, J3105, and OCPP 1.6 standards and support bi-directional V2G capability.
- The selection expands Proterra’s foothold across the Southeast after contracts in Alabama, Florida, South Carolina, North Carolina, and Tennessee.
- CEO Ryan Popple said the initiative is a huge win for Proterra, Georgia state institutions, and EV adoption in the United States.
- Proterra is part of a "pool of providers" for the State, allowing agencies to bypass future RFPs when purchasing zero-emission buses.
- By 2018 Proterra had sold more than 645 vehicles to 70 municipal, university, airport, federal and commercial transit agencies across 33 U.S. states and Canadian provinces.
- Proterra’s electric buses outperform internal combustion diesel or CNG engines in efficiency, reliability, acceleration, hill climb, and passenger-carrying power.
