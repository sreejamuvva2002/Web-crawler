# Archer Aviation Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2024-08-08

**Source:** [Archer Announces Key Terms Of Contract Manufacturing Relationship With Stellantis–Representing Up To ~$400M To Help Scale Midnight Production To 650 Aircraft Annually–Secures $230M In Additional Equity Capital From Strategic & Institutional Investors | Nasdaq](https://www.nasdaq.com/press-release/archer-announces-key-terms-contract-manufacturing-relationship-stellantis)  ·  `page_001672`

## Facts

- Archer reached an agreement in principle on key terms of a contract manufacturing relationship with Stellantis.
- Stellantis will contribute up to approximately $400 million to help scale Archer’s Midnight manufacturing to 650 aircraft annually.
- The commitment includes an obligation to cover manufacturing labor costs and certain capital expenditures at Archer’s Georgia manufacturing facility through 2030.
- This contribution is in addition to Stellantis’ $55 million equity investment in Archer received on July 1, 2024.
- Stellantis will fund up to approximately $370 million of the anticipated manufacturing labor costs necessary to support the planned production ramp to 650 aircraft annually.
- In exchange for funding labor costs, Stellantis will receive Archer shares on a rolling quarterly basis based on total labor costs incurred and Archer’s then-current future stock price.
- Stellantis will contribute identified initial incremental manufacturing capital expenditures for the initial ramp of Midnight production, estimated to be up to $20 million.
- Archer intends to issue Stellantis $30 million of performance warrants that will vest based on Stellantis' achievement of certain performance milestones under the contract manufacturing relationship.
- Archer’s issuance of any equity to Stellantis pursuant to this relationship is expected to be subject to approval by Archer’s stockholders.
- Archer secured $230 million in additional equity capital since the end of Q2 2024.
- The new equity capital includes a $175 million investment from strategic and institutional investors, including United.
- Moelis & Company LLC acted as exclusive placement agent for the equity investment.
- Archer unveiled plans for a Los Angeles air taxi network with take-off and landing locations at LAX, USC, Orange County, Santa Monica, Hollywood Burbank, Long Beach, and Van Nuys.
- Archer is coordinating with the Los Angeles Rams and Hollywood Park, a 300-acre district centered around SoFi Stadium, for a planned exclusive vertiport.
- Archer’s goal is to begin its Los Angeles network operations by as early as 2026.
- The U.S. Department of Defense accepted the military airworthiness assessment of Archer’s Midnight aircraft this month.
- Archer delivered its first Midnight aircraft to the U.S. Air Force as part of an AFWERX Agility Prime contract valued at up to $142 million.
- Future Flight Global plans to purchase up to 116 Midnight aircraft worth up to $580 million, bringing Archer’s indicative order book to nearly $6 billion.
- The planned aircraft purchase supports the joint goal of launching electric vertical take-off and landing aircraft operations in key global markets in Europe and Asia.
- Archer’s Q2 2024 total operating expenses were $121.2 million on a GAAP basis and $96.4 million on a Non-GAAP basis, with a net loss of $106.9 million and cash and cash equivalents of $360.4 million.
- Archer’s Q3 2024 financial estimates project non-GAAP total operating expenses between $90 million and $100 million.
- CEO Adam Goldstein stated that Archer is working to enable commercial operations worldwide and aims for commercialization as early as next year.
