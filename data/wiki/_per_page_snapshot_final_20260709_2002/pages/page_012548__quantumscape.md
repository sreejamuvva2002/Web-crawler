# QuantumScape

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2020-12-08

**Source:** [A new EV battery shows big promise | Trellis](https://trellis.net/article/new-ev-battery-shows-big-promise/)  ·  `page_012548`

## Facts

- QuantumScape is a decade-old startup that has been in stealth while developing a solid-state, lithium-metal battery for electric vehicles.
- The company was founded by serial entrepreneur Jagdeep Singh and Stanford scientists Fritz Prinz and Tim Holme.
- QuantumScape has raised $1.5 billion in capital through funding from Volkswagen and public funding via a special purpose acquisition company (SPAC).
- The company is trading on the New York Stock Exchange under the symbol QS, closing at $42.50 per share.
- Early investors include Breakthrough Energy Ventures, formed by Bill Gates, John Doerr, and Vinod Khosla, as well as Lightspeed Ventures.
- Tesla’s former CTO JB Straubel sits on QuantumScape’s board alongside John Doerr.
- The battery eliminates the liquid electrolyte and traditional anode found in lithium-ion batteries, using a unique solid ceramic separator instead.
- It uses an industry-standard mixed-metal cathode but creates a thin layer of lithium-metal anode when charged and discharged.
- QuantumScape describes the chemical composition and manufacturing process of its separator as a trade secret.
- A car using QuantumScape batteries could charge to 80 percent capacity in just 15 minutes, compared to potentially hour-plus charging times for current EVs.
- Vehicles equipped with these batteries could achieve an 80 percent longer range than those using today’s lithium-ion batteries.
- The battery technology is described as more compact, more energy-dense, safer to operate, and boasting longer lifespans.
- QuantumScape and Volkswagen have created a 50/50 joint venture to produce the first battery factory for this technology.
- The company must still create a commercial-scale factory and undergo a year of pilot testing with automakers like Volkswagen.
- CEO Jagdeep Singh stated that it will be 2024 before cars with these batteries are seen on the road.
- QuantumScape is competing against lithium-ion batteries, which are expected to drop in cost and increase in energy density over the next four years.
- The company revealed its performance data during a virtual panel featuring battery experts including Nobel Prize winner Stan Wittingham and Paul Albertus, former head of the US DOE ARPA-E IONCS solid-state battery program.
