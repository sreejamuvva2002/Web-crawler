# Capacitor Market & Component Suppliers

**Type:** supplier_relationship  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** ?

**Source:** [Pointing The Spotlight On Customer Needs - EE Times](https://www.eetimes.com/pointing-the-spotlight-on-customer-needs/)  ·  `page_047150`

## Facts

- Pricing competition has created uncertainty in the capacitor market.
- Customers expect reliable parts, excellent service, short lead times, and competitive pricing.
- Michael Foester is marketing manager for tantalum capacitors and surface-mount for North America at Siemens Passive Electronic Devices Inc. in Iselin, N.J.
- Siemens is working on programs together with customers and main suppliers to cut costs and stay competitive.
- Large customers are looking for vendors that can supply a wide range of products anywhere in the world.
- John Denslinger is vice president of sales at Murata Electronics North America Inc. in Smyrna, Ga.
- Murata plans to add a number of stocking locations in 1999 without specifying where.
- Joint venture Siemens Matsushita Components recently opened a facility in Evora, Portugal for tantalum-chip capacitor production.
- Capacity at the $70 million plant in Portugal will ramp to 1 billion components a year.
- Japan-based Elna Co. Ltd will establish a new factory in the Far East in 1999 with an unidentified site.
- Elna America Inc. in Cypress, Calif. added national distributors including Bell Microproducts, Future Electronics, and Newark Electronics.
- Loren Pleet is national sales and marketing manager at Elna America Inc.
- Keith Thomas is vice president of Kyocera Developed Products at AVX Corp. in Myrtle Beach, S.C.
- AVX will launch a pilot Internet program for Marshall Industries to track inventory and orders from the Web.
- Willie King is director of product marketing at AVX.
- Kemet Corp. opened distribution hubs in Guadalajara, Mexico, and Sao Paulo, Brazil in 1998.
- Sandy Beck is vice president of marketing at Kemet in Greenville, S.C.
- Mark Richey is product marketing manager at Rohm Electronics in San Diego.
- Vishay Intertechnology Inc. saw average selling prices of capacitors drop 10% to 15%.
- Glyndwr Smith is assistant to the chief executive and senior vice president of marketing intelligence at Malvern, Pa.-based Vishay.
- Dennis Zogbi is an analyst at the Paumanok Group in Apex, N.C.
- Prices of all fixed capacitors dropped almost 9% last year.
- Unit shipments will continue to grow with gradual improvement in demand expected as financial conditions in Asia improve.
- Worldwide revenue of fixed capacitors dropped 4.2% last year while unit shipments rose 5.9%.
- The worldwide market for fixed capacitors reached $9.5 billion in 1998, with aluminum at $2.9 billion, multilayer at $2.8 billion, tantalum at $1.7 billion, DC film at $1.4 billion, and single-layer at $700 million.
- Tantalum capacitors are starting to eat into aluminum's market share while ceramic capacitors offer similar values as some tantalums.
- Philips Components in San Jose is developing ceramic surface-mount capacitors with capacitance values up to 33 microfarads available in the first half of 1999.
- AVX predicts releasing double-layer supercapacitors in mid-1999 using patented Rechargeable Hydrogen-Ion Solid-State technology for wireless and rechargeable secondary power applications.
- Palladium accounts for most raw-material cost in multilayer ceramic capacitors.
- Palladium prices soared to $248 per troy ounce in mid-1997 and hovered near $300 in 1998.
- Rohm plans to have base-metal electrode caps in 0805 and 0603 packages in the higher range of capacitance values in summer 1999.
- Kemet is offering limited base-metal-electrode products in X7R dielectric and will work on Y5E products in early 1999.
- Murata plans to shift completely to nickel or copper electrodes, with half its products currently using base-metal electrodes.
- Philips has moved to base-metal electrodes in Y5V and Z5U products and will offer X7R devices using them early this year.
- AVX expects to reach 100 microfarads for multilayer ceramic capacitors by end of 1999, directing all development toward 100% nickel.
- Murata is shipping 47-microfarads product and offering prototype samples of 100-microfarads devices for production quantities this year.
- Vishay plans to produce low-ESR polymer-tantalum capacitors in the coming year for high-frequency applications.
- Philips will introduce high-frequency microwave-range surface-mount ceramic capacitors in 0603 and smaller cases in second half of 1999.
- AVX announced its Z Chip, a discrete impedance-matching series resistor/capacitor chip in October, available in 0603 package.
- Integrated passive devices (IPDs) can contain multiple passive and active components in one package to replace several discrete components on a printed-circuit board.
- Passives vendors estimate chip-array prices declined 30% to 35% in past 18 months, while IPDs using thin-film on silicon fell 20% in past two quarters.
- Marty Peck is vice president of Bradford, Pa.-based resistor maker KOA Spear Electronics Inc.
- Manufacturers predict 0402 and 0201 sizes of ceramic capacitors will be available to market in 1999.
- Philips plans to introduce 0201-size surface-mount capacitors in third quarter of 1999.
- Murata's Denslinger noted only a couple of North American accounts currently use 0201 devices, with placement ability dictating full-scale acceptance.
- Initial 0201 offerings will be in tape-and-reel packaging, with bulk packaging developments expected in early 2000.
