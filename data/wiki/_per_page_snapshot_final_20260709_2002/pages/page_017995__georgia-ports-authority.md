# Georgia Ports Authority

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2024-03

**Source:** [Georgia Ports Highlights Site Selection Opportunities - Columbus CEO](https://thecolumbusceo.com/features/2024/03/georgia-ports-highlights-site-selection-opportunities/)  ·  `page_017995`

## Facts

- Georgia Ports Authority links importers and exporters to global markets via 37 weekly container ship calls.
- Georgia’s ports are a key factor in the decision for automakers, their suppliers, other manufacturers, and logistics operations to bring jobs to Georgia.
- Griff Lynch serves as President and CEO of the Georgia Ports Authority.
- In partnership with the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD), the state announced 350 projects from potential port users in Fiscal Year 2023.
- The Fiscal Year 2023 announcements will bring more than 33,000 manufacturing and logistics jobs to 96 counties across Georgia.
- The Fiscal Year 2023 announcements will bring $21 billion in investment to 96 counties across Georgia.
- Griff Lynch stated that driving the success of the entire state is a mission to deliver greater economic opportunity for Georgians in large and small communities.
- GPA, GDEcD, and the Georgia Department of Transportation are working together to foster responsible growth that delivers good jobs while preserving quality of life.
- Georgia Ports is investing $29 million in an overpass that will route trucks leaving the Ocean Terminal in Savannah directly onto U.S. 17, avoiding community streets.
- Announcements associated with the Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America have located in more than 10 counties across Georgia.
- Pat Wilson is a Commissioner at the Georgia Department of Economic Development.
- Pat Wilson stated that companies, especially in e-mobility, are prioritizing speed-to-market solutions.
- Pat Wilson noted that investing in site development and “Georgia Ready for Accelerated Development” certification are ways communities can prepare for new opportunities.
- Anovion Technologies, a synthetic graphite manufacturer, is creating 400 jobs for Southwest Georgia in Decatur County.
- Chip Dunn serves as Executive Chairman of Anovion Technologies.
- Chip Dunn described the state’s recruitment effort that swayed Anovion’s decision to locate in rural Georgia, including meetings with Gov. Brian Kemp and factors such as GPA’s inland port system and access to waterway and land transport.
- The GPA inland port system includes the Appalachian Regional Port in Crandall, Ga., and the Blue Ridge Connector rail yard now under construction near Gainesville, Ga.
- The inland port system supports the movement of large, heavy products from centrally located communities to the state’s deepwater ports.
- The inventory of industrial property in the Savannah area totals more than 124 million square feet.
- More than 14 million square feet of industrial property in the Savannah area was under construction at the close of Calendar Year 2023.
- Stephen Ezelle is a broker for the industrial real estate firm Cushman & Wakefield.
- Stephen Ezelle stated that the Savannah regional economy continues to experience expansion and strong absorption in the industrial sector despite a slowing economy across most U.S. markets.
- Projections indicate Savannah will eclipse 8 million TEUs of throughput by 2030.
- The operational efficiency offered by GPA and the shift in import cargo from the West Coast and NY/NJ are key drivers in the growth of Savannah’s industrial market.
- Savannah is expected to continue a balanced expansion thanks to the Hyundai announcement and GPA’s growing national market share.
- Stephen Ezelle predicts continued absorption of well-located light industrial warehouse space.
- Savannah’s industrial sector ended 2023 with an overall vacancy rate of 7 percent.
- Stacy Watson serves as director of economic and industrial development at GPA.
- Stacy Watson stated that being within 30 miles of the port is great but not required to be competitive in drayage, lease rates, and overall cost of doing business.
- Locations 40 or 50 miles away from Savannah are an easy drive due to the port’s location upriver of the Savannah population center and Georgia DOT efforts reducing truck route congestion.
- Direct truck routes from the Port of Savannah connect to Interstates 95 and 16.
- On-terminal service at the marine terminal is provided by two Class I railroads to expedite cargo movement.
- Available industrial properties in Bryan, Liberty, and McIntosh counties as well as points west offer interstate and rail links to the port and thriving inland markets.
- Augusta is a short drive from Savannah, and a majority of manufacturers in Augusta are port users.
- Cal Wray serves as president of the Development Authority of Augusta, Ga.
- Cal Wray stated that Augusta-Richmond County provides an ideal location for port users due to available, affordable industrial land, over 350 existing manufacturers, and easy rail and highway connections to the port.
- Aurubis Richmond, a metal smelting operation in Augusta, is bringing 250 jobs and $600 million in investment.
- Denkai America, a copper film producer in Augusta, is providing 250 jobs and $440 million in investment.
- Purecycle Technologies, which recycles plastic into like-new pellets for reuse in Augusta, is creating 100 jobs and $400 million in investment.
- An electric vehicle manufacturing ecosystem exists in Augusta with EZGo Textron and Club Car and their related suppliers located in Richmond and Columbia counties.
- Metals and chemical manufacturing are two other important industries in the Augusta region.
- GPA supports the efficient operation of existing industries and local economic development efforts, serving as a key partner in ongoing recruitment of new industry.
- GPA is a resource to existing manufacturers who have invested billions in the region and many of which have announced significant further investments in the last five years.
