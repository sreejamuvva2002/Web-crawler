# Dana Incorporated

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2026-06-11

**Source:** [Dana Incorporated Announces Agreement to Combine with Eaton's Mobility Business, Strengthens Dana's Position as a Leading Global Powertrain Systems Provider | Morningstar](https://www.morningstar.com/news/pr-newswire/20260611cl81314/dana-incorporated-announces-agreement-to-combine-with-eatons-mobility-business-strengthens-danas-position-as-a-leading-global-powertrain-systems-provider)  ·  `page_020633`

## Facts

- Dana Incorporated announced a definitive agreement to combine with Eaton Corporation plc's Mobility business in a transaction valued at approximately $5.1 billion.
- The combined company is expected to have approximately $11 billion in sales and approximately $1.7 billion adjusted EBITDA (approximately 15% adjusted EBITDA margin) on a fully synergized, pro forma 2026 estimated basis.
- The transaction values Eaton Mobility at approximately $5.1 billion on a cash-free, debt-free basis, representing approximately 8.3x estimated 2026 pro forma adjusted EBITDA before synergies, or approximately 5.9x including run-rate synergies.
- The combined company is expected to achieve $250 million of annual run-rate synergies within 24 months following closing.
- Synergies will be driven by reduced structural costs, purchasing scale, manufacturing optimization, and engineering efficiencies.
- The transaction is structured as a Reverse Morris Trust transaction resulting in Eaton shareholders owning at least 50.1% and Dana shareholders owning approximately 49.9% of the combined company at close.
- Under the agreement terms, Eaton will receive a cash distribution of approximately $1.1 billion (subject to adjustments for cash and indebtedness), funded through new debt raised prior to closing.
- R. Bruce McDonald, Dana's Chairman, will serve as Executive Chairman of the combined company with responsibility for integration and synergy realization.
- Byron Foster will serve as Chief Executive Officer of the combined company, assuming his role on July 1, 2026.
- Timothy Kraus will continue as Chief Financial Officer of the combined company.
- Erin Rowse, Eaton's Senior Vice President Human Resources for Industrial, will serve as Chief Human Resources Officer at closing.
- The combined company's Board of Directors will be comprised of all members of Dana's Board of Directors and three Eaton designees.
- The combination integrates Dana's global powertrain, thermal, and sealing technologies with Eaton Mobility's commercial vehicle transmissions, engine and emissions products, and advanced electrification capabilities.
- Byron Foster stated the transaction marks an important milestone in Dana's transformation and positions it as a leading, scaled provider of powertrain solutions.
- Byron Foster noted that expanding presence in core markets with new products and complementary technologies enhances the ability to deliver greater value to customers while strengthening margins through a more balanced portfolio and meaningful synergies.
- Paulo Ruiz, Eaton Chief Executive Officer, stated the combination creates a leading global engineering solutions partner well positioned to serve commercial vehicle and light vehicle markets worldwide.
- Timothy Kraus stated the addition of Eaton Mobility increases Dana's 2030 strategy targets to $14–$15 billion in sales, approximately 18% adjusted EBITDA margins, and an 8%-9% adjusted free cash flow margin by 2030.
- Prior to this announcement, Dana's 2030 targets included approximately $10 billion in sales, 14% to 15% adjusted EBITDA margins, and a 6% adjusted free cash flow margin.
- After funding the approximately $1.1 billion cash distribution to Eaton, Dana expects to maintain a strong balance sheet with approximately 1.2x net leverage on a pro forma 2026 estimated basis.
- The combined company will continue to operate under the Dana Incorporated name and retain its listing on the New York Stock Exchange.
- The transaction is expected to be tax-free to Dana and Eaton shareholders for U.S. federal income tax purposes.
- The transaction is expected to close in the first quarter of 2027, subject to approval by Dana shareholders, receipt of regulatory approvals, and other customary closing conditions.
- Goldman Sachs & Co. LLC is serving as exclusive financial advisor to the combined company.
- Kirkland & Ellis LLP is serving as legal counsel to Dana.
- Ernst and Young LLP is serving as transaction advisor.
- Goldman Sachs Bank USA is serving as sole underwriter of the committed financing for the transaction.
- Dana Incorporated is headquartered in Maumee, Ohio, USA.
- Dana reported sales of $7.5 billion in 2025.
- Dana employs 27,000 people in 24 countries across six continents.
- Dana supplies leading vehicle manufacturers and related aftermarkets with drive systems, electrodynamic technologies, and thermal and sealing solutions.
