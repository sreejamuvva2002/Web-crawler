# Aspen Aerogels

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2025-02-14

**Source:** [Aspen Aerogels axes plans for Georgia electric-vehicle part manufacturing plant](https://wbjournal.com/article/aspen-aerogels-axes-plans-for-georgia-electric-vehicle-part-manufacturing-plant/)  ·  `page_052681`

## Facts

- Northborough-based Aspen Aerogels has cancelled its efforts to construct a manufacturing facility in Statesboro, Georgia.
- The company is pivoting to increasing its manufacturing capacity at its existing facility in East Providence and external manufacturing facilities due to anticipated changes in electric vehicle demand.
- Aspen Aerogels is evaluating options for the partially constructed facility, including using equipment at the site to upgrade its East Providence location.
- The company manufactures a special protective layer for EV batteries.
- CFO and treasurer Ricardo Rodriguez stated that in early 2023, pre-empting a reset in EV demand expectations, the company decided to right-time construction of its planned second aerogel manufacturing facility in Statesboro, Georgia, and ramped up external manufacturing capacity for its Energy Industrial business.
- The canceled Statesboro plant was originally expected to create more than 250 jobs and be completed in 2023, according to a February 2022 press release from the State of Georgia.
- Aspen Aerogels secured $575 million in private funds for the facility through a loan from General Motors Holdings and a sale of more than $25 million in shares of stock.
- The company had been approved for $671 million in funding from the U.S. Department of Energy for the site in October.
- Aspen Aerogels did not disclose what will happen to the various funds earmarked for the facility.
- Aspen Aerogels' fourth-quarter revenue was $124.1 million, up from $84.3 million in the fourth quarter of 2023.
- The firm's net income was $11.4 million, compared to a net loss of $500,000 in the same period last year.
- The company ended the year with cash and equivalents of $220.9 million.
- Aspen Aerogels opened a thermal barrier research center in Marlborough in 2023.
- Following the announcement of the Georgia plant cancellation, Aspen Aerogels stock price on the New York Stock Exchange dropped from $12.00 per share on Wednesday to $8.52 on Friday morning, a decrease of 29%.
- President Donald Trump has sought to cut funding of federal programs for electric vehicles, including a buildout of the national charging infrastructure, since taking office in January.
