# Hyundai Mobis EV Battery Module Plant

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-10-27

**Source:** [$205 million EV battery plant planned for Montgomery](https://businessalabama.com/205-million-battery-plant-planned-for-montgomery/)  ·  `page_010107`

## Facts

- A $205 million EV battery module plant is planned for Montgomery.
- The facility will supply more than 200,000 EV batteries annually to Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Alabama in Montgomery and the Kia Georgia plant.
- The facility will be 450,000 square feet.
- The facility is being built for Hyundai Mobis.
- Gov. Kay Ivey announced that the project would create 400 jobs.
- Gov. Kay Ivey stated that Alabama’s auto manufacturing sector is evolving rapidly to capitalize on the EV revolution sweeping the industry, and that this new Hyundai Mobis battery plant represents another milestone in that transition.
- Gov. Kay Ivey expressed excitement about the company’s new investment and what it represents for the next chapter of automaking in Alabama.
- H.S. Oh, vice president of the electric powertrain business for Hyundai Mobis, stated the new Alabama EV battery assembly facility will play a critical role in the company’s plans to expand its production network around the world.
- H.S. Oh noted that as the EV market continues to grow, having strong production capacity will be key to allowing Hyundai Mobis to see continued growth in the market.
- H.S. Oh stated he believes the new facility in Montgomery will be an important step in that process and expressed excitement for the project to be underway.
- Construction on the new facility is expected to begin as early as December.
- The facility is located at the Hyundai Motor Manufacturing Alabama campus off I-65 in Montgomery.
- Production of EV battery assemblies is projected to begin in 2024.
- Earlier this year, Hyundai announced plans to begin EV production at its Montgomery manufacturing plant through a $300 million investment that will create 200 jobs.
- Hyundai’s Alabama workforce will produce the all-electric Genesis GV70 luxury SUV and a hybrid electric version of the Santa Fe SUV.
