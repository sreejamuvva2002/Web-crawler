# Volvo Car USA

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2026-03-16

**Source:** [Volvo is killing its smallest EV in the US, but the rest of the world can still get it](https://electrek.co/2026/03/16/volvo-is-killing-its-smallest-ev-in-the-us-but-the-rest-of-the-world-can-still-get-it/)  ·  `page_014136`

## Facts

- Volvo will pull its smallest, most-affordable EV from the US market at the end of this model year (2026).
- The Volvo EX30 is one of the most affordable and second-smallest EVs in America.
- The EX30 is the quickest car Volvo has ever made.
- A software update will add V2L capability to all EX30s via over-the-air update soon.
- Volvo announced a new lower-priced trim for the EX30, though it did not specify if this trim would come to the US.
- Volvo told dealers on Friday that the EX30 and EX30 Cross Country off-road variant will be pulled from the US market at the end of the 2026 model year.
- Dealers have until the end of this week (Friday) to place orders for the EX30 and EX30 Cross Country.
- Volvo Car USA stated it is ending sales of the EX30 and EX30 Cross Country in the US market after the 2026 model year.
- The EX30 will remain available in other global markets, including Canada and Mexico.
- Volvo cited "market conditions and financial factors" for the withdrawal decision.
- Volvo confirmed that all current US owners will continue to receive support, service, parts, warranty, and software updates regardless of whether their model is still on sale.
- Production of the EX30 was shifted from China to Volvo’s factory in Ghent, Belgium, in part to avoid tariffs.
- The EX30 originally launched globally in the 2024 model year but arrived in the US in the 2025 model year due to US tariffs on EVs from China.
- The US will only receive two total model years of the EX30.
- Volvo will continue selling its other EVs in the US, including the EX40 SUV (formerly XC40), the EX90 three-row electric SUV, and the upcoming EX60.
- The article attributes the withdrawal decision to Republicans inflating EV prices by $7,500, which disproportionately hit lower-end EVs.
- The price inflation caused a pull-forward of demand before the hike, depressing subsequent EV sales.
- Automakers are reacting quickly to depressed EV sales and market conditions.
- The article notes that smaller, more efficient EVs like the EX30 could help address affordability crises and reduce oil dependency amid rising gas prices.
- The EX30 is 166 inches long and offers a usable amount of space for a family despite its small size.
- Competitors or alternatives mentioned include the Kia EV4, Rivian R3 (161-inch-long), and Chevy Bolt (169-inch-long).
