# National Grid

## Facts

- National Grid is an investor-owned utility (IOU) that operates in multiple states.
- The page provides a summary of the types of incentives provided by the utility related to alternative fuels and vehicles.
- Residential fuel price incentives include an EV Time-of-Use (TOU) Rate.
- Residential infrastructure incentives include an EV Level 2 Charging Station Purchase Rebate and a Make-Ready or Pre-Wiring Rebate for EV Chargers.
- No residential vehicle incentives are currently offered.
- Non-residential fuel prices have no incentives currently offered.
- Non-residential infrastructure incentives include an EV Level 2 Charging Station Installation Rebate, an EV Direct Current (DC) Fast Charging Station Installation Rebate, and a Make-Ready or Pre-Wiring Rebate for EV Chargers.
- A Fleet Electrification Assessment is offered as a non-residential vehicle incentive.
- National Grid operates in the states of Massachusetts, New York, and Rhode Island.
- National Grid serves the Boston-Cambridge-Newton, Buffalo-Cheektowaga, and Providence-Warwick Metropolitan Statistical Areas.
