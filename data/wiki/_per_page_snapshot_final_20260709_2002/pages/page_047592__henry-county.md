# Henry County

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2024-08-27

**Source:** [McDonough | Henry County: More Than Meets the Eye - Georgia Trend Magazine](https://www.georgiatrend.com/2024/08/27/mcdonough-henry-county-more-than-meets-the-eye/)  ·  `page_047592`

## Facts

- Henry County comprises Hampton, Locust Grove, Stockbridge, and McDonough (county seat).
- As of 2023, Henry County population was 254,613, representing a 20% increase from 2010's 203,922.
- Since 2019, the county has tracked over $2 billion in investments and over 8,000 new jobs, with recent wins heavily concentrated in manufacturing.
- In January 2023, Andersen Corporation announced Renewal by Andersen would create 900 new jobs and invest more than $420 million in a new manufacturing and logistics facility in Locust Grove, marking its first Southeast facility.
- NewCold completed a $333 million temperature-controlled warehousing and distribution warehouse in McDonough employing 170 workers, described as the largest foreign direct investment in the community's history.
- In 2020, The Home Depot invested $108 million to build a 657,600-square-foot warehouse in Locust Grove and hired 600 associates.
- Ken’s Foods spent $103 million and created 70 new jobs to expand its manufacturing and distribution operations in McDonough.
- Mattress manufacturer Purple established a manufacturing facility in Henry County in 2020 creating 300 jobs, followed by a 2021 call center in McDonough adding 500 employees for a total investment of $46 million.
- In 2020, Zinus USA announced its first North American advanced manufacturing facility in McDonough, committing to invest $108 million and create 804 jobs.
- In summer 2023, Governor Brian Kemp announced EV parts supplier NVH Korea would establish a new facility in Locust Grove with a $72 million investment creating 160 new jobs.
- Henry County is beginning to develop the data center industry, with announcements expected before the end of 2024.
- Local manufacturing produces well-known brands including Sweet Baby Ray’s BBQ Sauce, Behr Paint, Curry & Echols Hot & Salty Pickles, Shane’s Rib Shack BBQ Sauce, and De Wafelbakkers frozen pancakes.
- The Henry County Chamber of Commerce established a business resource center in 2023 to support small businesses, serving 435 businesses in its first year with SBA counseling, certification classes, and leadership programs.
- The chamber reports 10% to 20% consistent business growth over the past five years, with logistics, transportation, advanced manufacturing, and healthcare as the biggest growth areas.
- In fall 2023, Piedmont Henry Hospital announced a $215 million project to add a new tower expected to complete in fall 2026, adding 95 inpatient rooms to bring total census to 355 beds and three additional operating room suites for a total of 14.
- Piedmont Henry Hospital is on track for 93,000 emergency department visits this year and operates as a Level III trauma center driving population growth.
- Piedmont plans to open physical locations with expanded medical services in every Henry County city (Stockbridge, McDonough, Locust Grove, Hampton) within the next two years, including physicians' offices in Hampton.
- Locust Grove is implementing a new Bethlehem Road interchange for I-75 access, connecting Peeksville to Francis Ford to relieve Bill Gardner Parkway congestion, and adding a lane to Highway 42, with T-SPLOST funding supporting these efforts.
- There are currently 20 transportation projects underway throughout Henry County, primarily road widenings, addressing congestion from the county's quarter-million population using I-75 as a main thoroughfare.
- Atlanta Motor Speedway in Hampton hosts over 500 event days annually generating approximately $250 million in regional economic impact, employs 50 full-time staff (up to 3,500 for NASCAR events), and continues hosting Henry County graduations with social distancing protocols.
