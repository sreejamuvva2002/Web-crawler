# Addionics

**Type:** Company  ·  **Supply-chain role:** [[battery-cell-manufacturing|Battery Cell Manufacturing]]  ·  **Location:** Georgia, United States

## Overview

Addionics provides battery-cell technology that increases active material loading, delivering longer driving range and faster charging for electric vehicles. The blog explains why automotive OEMs are moving toward becoming battery makers and highlights Addionics’ role in enabling emerging chemistries such as silicon and solid-state.

## EV supply-chain relevance

Addionics’ technology improves EV battery performance, range, and charging time, addressing key cost and design challenges in the electric-vehicle supply chain.

## Timeline

- **2014** — Tesla signed an agreement with Panasonic investing $1.5-2 billion in Gigafactory 1, its first lithium-ion battery factory.
- **2019** — Mercedes-Benz invested in Sila Nanotechnologies, later announcing silicon-anode cells for the electric G-Class.
- **Early 2023** — Construction began on Hyundai’s EV and battery manufacturing facility in Savannah, Georgia.
- **First half of 2025** — Production is expected to start at Hyundai’s Savannah site, targeting 300,000 vehicles per year.

## Key facts

- Batteries account for roughly 30% of the average EV transaction price of $56,437.
- OEMs are rethinking strategy to become battery makers due to supply-chain, design, and performance challenges.
- Addionics’ technology can be added drop-in to any manufacturing process, increasing active material loading while lowering internal resistance.
- Hyundai’s Savannah plant will create over 8,000 new jobs in Georgia.
- GM estimates its Ultium proprietary cells can deliver up to 724 km range and accelerate from 0-96 km/h in three seconds.
