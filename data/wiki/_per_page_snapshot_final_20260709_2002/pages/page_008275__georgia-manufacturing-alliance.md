# Georgia Manufacturing Alliance

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** logistics  ·  **Published:** 2020-07-01

**Source:** [Georgia Manufacturing Alliance Featured in Georgia Trend Magazine -  Georgia Manufacturing Alliance](https://www.georgiamanufacturingalliance.com/news/georgia-manufacturing-alliance-featured-in-georgia-trend-magazine)  ·  `page_008275`

## Facts

- 2019 Georgia exports reached an all-time high of $41.2 billion, with total trade at $143.3 billion.
- Demand for Georgia exports grew even after the first phase of U.S.-China tariffs went into effect.
- Georgia's agriculture exporters make up 10% of its exports, or about $4.2 billion.
- Automotive-related exports generate another $3.2 billion, mostly to Canada, Mexico, the United Arab Emirates and Germany.
- Aerospace companies generated $10.8 billion in exports last year, making it the state's top export industry and Georgia the nation's fifth-largest exporter in the space.
- Canton-based J&S Chemical is an in-process specialty chemical manufacturer for metal car parts that exports to nearly 30 countries.
- J&S Chemical faced a raw material shortage three years ago, followed by the trade war with China and the pandemic.
- Kia Motors began making face shields in its West Point facility while auto production resumed.
- Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport experienced 4% growth in cargo operations since 2015 until volume dropped from nearly 700,000 metric tons in 2018 to 640,000 metric tons in 2019 due to the trade war with China.
- Air cargo volume at Hartsfield-Jackson fell 9.6% in January 2020 but grew by almost 1% over 2019 in February 2020 due to an influx of air cargo charter flights for medical and PPE supplies.
- February 2020 saw an 11.23% increase in air cargo volume from January 2020 at Hartsfield-Jackson.
- In March 2017, Hartsfield-Jackson introduced TruckPass, a staging system to alleviate roadway congestion in the South Cargo area and digitize freight loading and unloading processes.
- Hartsfield-Jackson expanded warehouse capacity with Cargo Building C, a 120,000-square-foot warehouse with 2,500 square feet of temperature-controlled space for perishable goods and pharmaceuticals.
- Worldwide Flight Services was the first lessee of Cargo Building C at Hartsfield-Jackson and began operations in late 2019.
- The Georgia Ports Authority is committed to investing in strategic projects, including the first phase of the Mason Mega Rail project which will double the Port of Savannah’s rail capacity and is scheduled to come online at the end of summer 2020.
- At the Ocean Terminal, the first phase of renovations to partially convert the terminal to handle containers is expected to be completed at the end of 2020.
- A berth renovation project at the main Garden City Terminal continues at full speed to allow more high volume ships.
- Hartsfield-Jackson plans to launch partnership agreements with airports throughout the world to increase cargo volume and passenger numbers, with Amsterdam’s Schiphol Airport partnering in June 2019.
- Hartsfield-Jackson is on track to start development of a public-private partnership for an air cargo terminal that will support the growth of pharmaceutical, aerospace and automotive industries.
- GMA organized weekly roundtable calls to help Georgia businesses connect supplies with demand during the pandemic.
- J&S Chemical sold more than 700 gallons of sanitizing solution to the Bobby Dodd Institute as of press time after joining a GDEcD representative's recommended call.
- The GDEcD’s International Trade Division launched a free online tool called the Export Georgia, USA Directory in February to serve as an online platform for international buyers to search for Georgia suppliers and services.
- Georgia Ports Authority Executive Director Griff Lynch stated that global trade was projected to have another record year before the pandemic hit.
- Mary Waters, deputy commissioner of international trade for the Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD), noted that underlying diverse markets and business environment fundamentals will allow Georgia to turn a corner after the pandemic.
- Georgia Manufacturing Alliance CEO and founder Jason Moss emphasized that supply chain redundancy is critical because missing components like nuts or bolts can stop production lines, especially in aerospace and automotive markets relying on imported parts.
- J&S Chemical director of global sales and marketing Thomas Smith explained that the company had to tighten communication with suppliers across multiple stops along the supply chain as the pandemic spread from China to European suppliers.
- Hartsfield-Jackson director of air service development Elliott Paige highlighted the need to build resiliency and efficiency in operations and capacity to handle future industry changes, cyclical market shifts, or pandemics.
