# Hyundai Motor Group

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** vehicle_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2022-07-22

**Source:** [Hyundai! Hyundai! Hyundai! Savannah, Chatham leaders celebrate new auto plant in Bryan County](https://www.yahoo.com/news/hyundai-hyundai-hyundai-savannah-chatham-100104436.html)  ·  `page_009426`

## Facts

- Georgia state and local leaders officially welcomed South-Korean automaker Hyundai Motor Group to Bryan County on Friday.
- The company will construct a $5.5 billion manufacturing facility.
- The facility will be located on the nearly 2,300 acre Bryan County mega site, which sits adjacent to Interstate 16 near the U.S. 280 interchange.
- The site will employ 8,100 workers between an electric vehicle assembly plant and an adjacent EV battery factory.
- The project makes it the largest single economic development project in Georgia history.
- Georgia Gov. Brian Kemp said the announcement marked a historic day in the state's ongoing success story.
- Gov. Kemp cited Georgia's 3.1% unemployment rate, record breaking number of Georgians working, and record breaking trade and commerce numbers as metrics of the state's success.
- Gov. Kemp stated, "And today, we are doing it yet again."
- In 2006, former Georgia Gov. Sonny Perdue inked a deal with Hyundai officials for a new Kia assembly plant in West Point, which helped lay the groundwork for this week's announcement.
- Gov. Kemp said, "It is clear companies recognize that in Georgia, we do not rest on our laurels, and we will always continue to work towards our future."
- Gov. Kemp stated, "And after today, no one can doubt that Georgia is not only leading the great recovery as a whole, but we're also the unrivaled leader in the nation's emerging electric mobility industry."
- Hyundai Motor Group Executive Chair Euisun Chung said Friday was a momentous occasion in the history and the future of Hyundai Motor Group.
- Chung announced the company's plan to open its first dedicated full EV and battery manufacturing facility in the U.S.
- Chung added that the U.S. has always held an important place in the group's global strategy.
- Chung stated, "We are proud to partner with the state of Georgia to achieve a shared goal towards electrified mobility and sustainability. We are looking forward to continuing our success story here. Let us enjoy this electrifying ride together."
- Hyundai Motor Company President and CEO Jaehoon "Jay" Chang told the crowd that the manufacturing facility represents a critical next step in the evolution of the Ho Motor Group.
- Chang said, "When he decided to build our first fully dedicated EV plant outside of Korea, we chose the United States because America embraces changes and drives innovation."
- As temperatures hovered around 90 degrees, Hyundai Motor President and COO José Muñoz proposed a toast to the new development.
- During the event, attendees chanted "Hyundai! Hyundai! Hyundai!" in response to a question from Muñoz about the best global auto group in the world.
- State Sen. Ben Watson, who represents Bryan County, said the impact of the development stretched far beyond county lines.
- Sen. Watson noted that the project is a joint effort with Bulloch County, Effingham County, Chatham County, and especially Bryan County.
- Sen. Watson said he heard from several South Korean officials that they chose the area because it was "such a well oiled machine and a great package that they had put together."
- Savannah native U.S. Senator Raphael Warnock shared on Twitter that the deal would "help bring more clean jobs to our state."
- U.S. Congressman Buddy Carter said the thousands of jobs created by the facility is a huge win not only for area residents, but Hyundai, too.
- Rep. Carter stated, "The people who work here, they are going to just love this company. It's a great company. And this company is going to love the people of this area."
- Rep. Carter added, "They have made a very wise decision, they will find that the people here are dedicated hard working people, salt of the earth people. So it's just a win-win situation for everyone."
- U.S. Senator Jon Ossoff issued a statement crediting months of behind-the-scenes work in getting the deal done for Georgians.
- Friday's announcement will help build an already strong partnership with the Port of Savannah.
- Georgia Ports Authority Executive Director Griff Lynch noted that Kia, located up in west central Georgia, has been an amazing partner for many years and just celebrated 10 years in Georgia and a million cars.
- Lynch stated, "to have the Hyundai Group invest again, in Georgia speaks volumes about the relationship they have with the state, the ports and everyone else and so we're just so excited to continue to grow that partnership."
