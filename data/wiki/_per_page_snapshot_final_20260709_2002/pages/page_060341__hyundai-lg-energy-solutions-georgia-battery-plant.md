# Hyundai-LG Energy Solutions Georgia Battery Plant

**Type:** facility  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2025-09-11

**Source:** [Hyundai CEO says immigration raid will delay Georgia battery plant's construction - CBS News](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/hyundai-ceo-georgia-battery-plant-raid-construction-delay/)  ·  `page_060341`

## Facts

- Work on Hyundai's Georgia battery plant will be delayed by up to three months due to an immigration raid.
- Hyundai operates the Georgia battery plant jointly with LG Energy Solutions.
- CEO José Muñoz stated the enforcement action leaves the plant short of workers and will cause a minimum two to three month delay.
- Muñoz explained that filling the vacant positions will be difficult because most detained workers are not currently in the U.S.
- U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) detained approximately 475 people suspected of illegally living and working at the Georgia plant earlier this month.
- More than 300 of the detained individuals were South Korean nationals, according to South Korea's Foreign Minister Cho Hyun.
- Following a deal between the U.S. and South Korea, the detained South Korean nationals were set to be repatriated on a charter flight.
- A poll in South Korea found that almost 60% of respondents were disappointed by the U.S. crackdown, calling the enforcement action 'excessive.'
- Roughly 31% of poll respondents called the ICE action 'inevitable' and said they understood why it took place.
- South Korean officials warned that the immigration enforcement action could chill South Korea's investments in the U.S.
