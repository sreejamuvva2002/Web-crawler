# TI Fluid Systems

**Source:** [TI Fluid Systems makes several executive appointments  - Sharecast.com](https://www.sharecast.com/news/news-and-announcements/ti-fluid-systems-makes-several-executive-appointments--8108789.html)  ·  `page_011642`

## Facts

- TI Fluid Systems is an automotive fluid technology company.
- The company announced three executive-level appointments on Tuesday.
- Hans Dieltjens will assume the role of president and chief executive officer on 1 October.
- Dieltjens will succeed William Kozyra, who will retire on 1 October and step down from the board of directors.
- The company stated Dieltjens would assume leadership at a "strategically-important time" as it accelerates its electric vehicle strategy.
- TI Fluid Systems is reinforcing its position as a provider of thermal solutions for the automotive industry.
- Hans Dieltjens holds a master's degree in electromechanical engineering with 25 years of automotive experience in Asia, Europe and the Americas.
- Dieltjens joined TI Fluid Systems in 1996, gaining commercial and operational experience through divisional leadership positions.
- Among his successes, Dieltjens led the global fuel tank and delivery systems division in developing and manufacturing products to enhance vehicle performance and safety while exceeding strict emissions regulations to preserve the environment.
- Earlier in the year, Dieltjens was appointed chief operating officer in preparation for his succession to president and CEO.
- TI Fluid Systems is expanding its executive committee with the appointment of Stephanie Jett as chief commercial officer.
- Johannes Helmich was appointed chief technology officer.
- Mark Sullivan was appointed chief operating officer.
- Stephanie Jett will join the company on 1 October, based at corporate headquarters in Auburn Hills, Michigan.
- Jett brings 20 years of automotive industry experience, having worked in senior commercial roles at Valeo, Visteon, Metaldyne, and most recently as global senior vice-president of sales for Autoliv.
- Jett will be responsible for sales and commercial activities, including an increased focus on the company's electric vehicle product portfolio.
- Johannes Helmich joined the company on 1 September as chief technology officer, based at its technology center in Rastatt, Germany.
- Helmich will play a "crucial role" in designing and developing TI's product portfolio, with a specific focus on accelerating electric vehicle product development as part of the 'Fluid Evolution' strategy.
- Helmich has more than 25 years of engineering experience, having held high-level executive positions at international tier-1 automotive suppliers such as Magna, Brose, Bosch, and Valeo.
- Mark Sullivan is based in Auburn Hills, Michigan, and joined the company earlier in the year from Plastic Omnium Group.
- At Plastic Omnium Group, Sullivan held various positions including global president of its fuel tank business Inergy and its plastics exteriors business.
- Sullivan has more than 35 years in the automotive industry, having lived and worked in the US, France and Germany.
- TI's chair Manfred Wennemer welcomed the new executives and congratulated Hans on his promotion to chief executive officer.
- Wennemer stated the additions will strengthen the company's ability to execute its electric vehicle strategy and deliver on near-term objectives.
