# Woory Industrial Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-06

**Source:** [Woory to open EV parts manufacturing, R&D facility in Laurens County – Savannah Harbor-Interstate 16 Corridor Joint Development Authority](https://savannahjda.com/2023/06/woory-to-open-ev-parts-manufacturing-rd-facility-in-laurens-county/)  ·  `page_004412`

## Facts

- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that Woory Industrial Co., Ltd. will establish a new manufacturing facility in Dublin, Georgia.
- The facility will create more than 130 new jobs and an estimated $18 million investment in Dublin-Laurens County.
- Woory Industrial Company, known as Woory, was established in 1989 and specializes in heating, ventilation, and air conditioning components and systems for the global automotive industry.
- The company is concentrating on the development of new products for electric vehicles (EV) and hydrogen-fueled cars.
- Jungwoo Kim, CEO of Woory Industrial Company, stated that the transition to electric vehicles is the future and the U.S. EV market is the key market among all automotive markets.
- Over the past 10 years, Woory has successfully grown into a specialized EV component company through continuous innovation.
- The new facility will be located at 404 Kellam Road in Dublin and will be the company’s first U.S. manufacturing location.
- The facility will produce electrical heaters, control units, and actuators for EV and internal combustion carmakers in North America.
- Operations are expected to begin in November 2023.
- Unlike internal combustion engine vehicles, electric vehicles do not have a heating source, so heaters for EVs are required for cabin heating and maintaining battery temperature.
- Woory’s pioneering development of electric heaters over the last 10 years has made it a leader in this growing market segment.
- Woory will be hiring for positions in personnel from all fields necessary for an automotive parts manufacturing plant.
- Dublin Mayor Joshua Kight stated that Woory’s commitment to place an existing manufacturing building back into service helps promote infill development and creates new career paths for the community.
- Dr. Fred Williams, Chair of the Board of Directors of the Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority, noted that Dublin and Laurens County have a rapidly growing automotive sector due to their geographic location on Interstate 16 and access to a skilled workforce.
- The partnership with area education systems and training providers will ensure training programs are in place to fill Woory’s talent pipeline for years to come.
- Project Manager Jacob Lee represented the Georgia Department of Economic Development’s (GDEcD) Global Commerce team on this competitive project.
- The project is a partnership with the Dublin-Laurens County Development Authority, Georgia Power, and Georgia Quick Start.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Georgia has invested for decades to match competitive projects with communities and sites that are prepared for development.
- From July 1 through December 31, 2022, the automotive industry was the second-largest source of new jobs and investment in Georgia.
- Since 2020, Georgia’s pursuit of job creation opportunities along the entire EV supply chain has resulted in over $22.7 billion in investment and the creation of more than 28,400 jobs.
- Woory began operating a small factory producing fuel senders in Suwon, South Korea almost 35 years ago.
- The company now employs over 1,300 people across production plants around the world.
- Woory has been nominated as a supplier for Tesla, Rivian, Lucid, and Canoo.
- Woory is currently supplying the HV heater system used to heat the cabins of EVs.
