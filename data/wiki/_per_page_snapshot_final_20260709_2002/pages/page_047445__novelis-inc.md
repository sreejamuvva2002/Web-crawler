# Novelis Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2018-11-29

**Source:** [Novelis to Invest $175M in Brazil Aluminum Recycling and Production Plant  - Global Atlanta](https://www.globalatlanta.com/novelis-to-invest-175m-in-brazil-aluminum-recycling-and-production-plant/)  ·  `page_047445`

## Facts

- Novelis Inc. is set to invest $175 million to upgrade its already massive plant between Rio de Janeiro and Sao Paulo, Brazil.
- The facility collects and recycles scrap aluminum into ingots that are flattened into rolled products.
- The project at the remote Pindamonhangaba plant is to create more than 50 new jobs.
- Novelis is adding 17 percent more aluminum sheet capacity and 13.3 percent more recycling capacity, up to 680 kilotonnes and 450 kilotonnes, respectively.
- The investment includes the purchase of 90 acres of land for expansion.
- The project also includes a new water sourcing system.
- Novelis officials stated in a Nov. 13 news release that these initiatives show their commitment to sustainability.
- The company won the support of Investe SP, the state investment promotion agency for the state of Sao Paulo.
- Investe SP director Sergio Costa stated: “It’s very important for the state of Sao Paulo to contribute to this high value-added project. Sao Paulo needs initiatives like this that promote the economic, social and environmental development of the state.”
- No incentives were disclosed in the news release regarding the project.
- The plant is a major driver of economic growth in the state.
- The facility was established in 1977.
- The plant currently employs more than 1,100 people.
- Atlanta-based Novelis is a subsidiary of India’s Aditya Birla Group.
- Novelis posted revenues of about $11.5 billion in 2017.
- Novelis's Atlanta headquarters is located just across the driveway from the Brazilian consulate in Buckhead’s Alliance Center development.
