# Hanwha Advanced Materials Georgia

**Type:** investment_announcement  ·  **Role:** battery_materials  ·  **Published:** 2023-03-22

**Source:** [Qcells Supplier Expands Supply Chain in Georgia - Qcells North America](https://us.qcells.com/blog/qcells-supplier-expands-supply-chain-investment-in-georgia/)  ·  `page_001580`

## Facts

- Hanwha Advanced Materials Georgia (HAGA) will build a new advanced materials manufacturing facility in Bartow County, Georgia.
- HAGA will supply Qcells with Encapsulant film, materials used to encapsulate solar cells and ensure long-term panel durability.
- This $147 million investment is expected to create more than 160 new jobs.
- The investment is critical to supporting Qcells’ plans to develop a fully integrated solar supply chain in America.
- HAGA will be the only company in the United States manufacturing solar EVA.
- Inhwan Kim, CEO of Hanwha Advanced Materials, stated that their products are an important piece of the clean energy supply chain puzzle and building cutting-edge advanced materials in Georgia will create new careers in solar and help bring more affordable, reliable clean energy to customers across the country.
- Justin Lee, CEO of Qcells, stated that Qcells is doubling down on building a complete, domestic solar supply chain and this recent investment is critical to making that happen.
- Justin Lee noted that working with Hanwha Advanced Materials will allow customers to confidently know that the solar they buy from Qcells was made right here in America.
- Governor Brian Kemp stated that Georgia is leading the nation in attracting next generation jobs.
- Governor Kemp noted that since welcoming Qcells to Georgia in 2018, the state has announced more than 4,000 related jobs for Georgians.
- Governor Kemp expressed pride that Hanwha Advanced Materials is adding to that growing number as it becomes a valued member of the Bartow County community.
- Senator Jon Ossoff stated that his solar manufacturing law continues to deliver jobs, investment, and wealth for Georgians.
- Senator Ossoff called this announcement a continuation of the largest clean energy manufacturing investment in American history.
- U.S. Senator Reverend Warnock stated that Georgians are reaping the economic benefits of the Inflation Reduction Act that has helped pave the way for advanced manufacturing companies like Qcells to invest and expand in Georgia.
- Senator Warnock expressed looking forward to continuing to partner with Qcells as Georgia becomes the premier destination for 21st century jobs.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that Hanwha Advanced Materials provides key components for the solar supply chain in Georgia, supporting the state’s commitment to making energy solutions more affordable.
- Commissioner Wilson noted that over the past five years, clean-tech jobs have been one of the fastest growing manufacturing sectors in Georgia.
- Commissioner Wilson expressed excitement that Hanwha Advanced Materials will add another crucial part of the solar supply chain to Georgia’s robust ecosystem.
- Cartersville Mayor Matt Santini stated that the community is thrilled to be adding another Hanwha affiliate to its growing family of industries.
- Mayor Santini noted their investment brings quality jobs to the community and diversifies the local mix of industries in Cartersville and Bartow County.
- Mayor Santini mentioned that recruiting world-class companies was a goal from the beginning, which this helps accomplish.
- Mayor Santini noted it is important to highlight that this is another clean energy company coming to the community, illustrating the growth and importance of that market.
- This announcement comes only two months after Qcells revealed plans to invest an historic $2.5 billion into building ingot, wafers, and cells as well as expanding solar panel manufacturing in Georgia.
- Qcells recently announced plans to buy polysilicon from REC Silicon, which is using clean energy to power its facility in Moses Lake, Washington.
- Soon, Qcells products, from polysilicon to panel, will be sustainably made in America.
- This investment comes after years of partnership with leaders in Georgia and following the passage of the Solar Energy Manufacturing for America Act (SEMA) within the Inflation Reduction Act.
- The demand for American-made solar panels is increasing rapidly driven by efforts to ensure energy independence, lower energy costs, and create new careers in energy.
- Qcells’ domestic manufacturing expansion will fulfill the growing need for these clean energy solutions and strengthen Georgia’s position as a global leader in solar.
- HAGA will hire more than 160 full time jobs such as engineers, line operators before starting production in June 2024.
