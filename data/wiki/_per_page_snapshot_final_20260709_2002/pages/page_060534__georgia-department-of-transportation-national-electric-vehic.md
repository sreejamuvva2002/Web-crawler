# Georgia Department of Transportation National Electric Vehicle Infrastructure (NEVI) Program

**Type:** government_program  ·  **Role:** charging_infrastructure  ·  **Published:** 2023-11-27

**Source:** [GA NEVI Program from Georgia DOT](https://getevgas.com/ga-nevi-program-from-georgia-dot/)  ·  `page_060534`

## Facts

- The Georgia Department of Transportation (GDOT) is implementing the National Electric Vehicle Infrastructure (NEVI) program in the state.
- This program aims to establish a network of electric vehicle charging stations along designated corridors.
- Round 1 had an open period from July 25, 2023, to November 27, 2023.
- A Request for Qualified Companies for Round 2 was open through July 1, 2024.
- The Round 2 Request for Proposals (RFP) opening date is July 1, 2024.
- The Round 2 RFP closing date is September 30, 2024.
- The rebate program is currently out of funding.
- Round 2 Request for Proposals is open from July 1, 2024, to September 30, 2024.
- The program covers state-wide geography.
- The rebate type is custom.
- Rebate amounts cover up to 80% of project cost.
- There are 33 charging sites selected through a competitive bid process.
- Chargers must be located within one travel mile of designated EV corridors.
- Charging stations must be spaced no more than 50 miles apart.
- Charger ports must be compatible with all electric vehicles sold in the United States.
- Projects must provide information on publicly available electric vehicle charging infrastructure.
- All projects must follow Buy America standards.
- Each proposal must include at least four 150kW capable DC Fast Chargers with CCS ports.
- Proposals must meet all federal NEVI guidelines.
- A map of eligible locations is available through the GDOT NEVI Program Map.
- The rebate program officially closed on October 7, 2024.
- The Round 2 RFP opened on July 9, 2024, with a closing date of September 30, 2024.
- The Request for Qualified Companies for Round 2 opened on May 9, 2024.
- The GA NEVI Program is part of a larger national initiative to create robust electric vehicle charging infrastructure across the United States.
- By strategically placing charging stations along key corridors, the program aims to reduce range anxiety and promote the adoption of electric vehicles.
- Applicants are required to carefully review all federal NEVI guidelines and Georgia-specific requirements before submitting proposals.
- The competitive bidding process ensures that the most qualified and cost-effective projects will be selected to create a comprehensive charging network across the state.
