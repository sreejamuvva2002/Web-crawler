# TEKLAS

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2020-11

**Source:** [Electric Vehicle Manufacturer TEKLAS to Open North American Headquarters in Georgia, Create 120 Jobs in Calhoun - Newnan CEO](https://newnanceo.com/news/2020/11/electric-vehicle-manufacturer-teklas-open-north-american-headquarters-georgia-create-120-jobs-calhoun/)  ·  `page_051246`

## Facts

- The investment and job creation were announced on Wednesday, November 11th, 2020.
- Governor Brian P. Kemp announced that TEKLAS, a Turkish advanced research and development, manufacturing company and supplier of electric vehicle (EV) parts, will invest $6.5 million in opening their first North American facility and headquarters in Gordon County.
- The project will deliver 120 jobs to Calhoun.
- TEKLAS was founded in Turkey in 1971.
- TEKLAS designs, develops, and manufactures fluid circulation systems for cooling and heating applications, which includes hoses and pipes from rubber, plastic, and metal for the global automotive industry.
- TEKLAS uses highly innovative and automated manufacturing processes using advanced water molding technology.
- TEKLAS USA will include assembly lines to serve the most prominent original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) in the industry with the start of its operations in the spring of 2021.
- Regional Director Arcan Ergur stated that the Calhoun, Georgia location will help the company be closer to its main customers.
- The 200,000-square-foot facility and headquarters will be located at 320 South Industrial Boulevard in Calhoun, Georgia.
- TEKLAS will be hiring for positions at a variety of skill levels, including positions in advanced manufacturing, management, and supervision.
- Larry Roye, chairman of the Development Authority of Gordon County, applauded the efforts of state and local partners who helped encourage the company to come to this decision.
- Gordon County Chamber of Commerce President and CEO Kathy Johnson commended TEKLAS for their research and advancements in electric vehicle manufacturing and for their contribution to the expansion of the automotive sector of the community's manufacturing base.
- Georgia Department of Economic Development (GDEcD) Project Manager Fernanda Kirchner represented the Global Commerce division on this competitive project.
- The project was completed in partnership with the Economic Development Authority of Gordon County and Georgia Power.
- The Center of Innovation for Logistics, a division of GDEcD, provided freight analysis to support the project and company planning.
- GDEcD Commissioner Pat Wilson stated that the State of Georgia is focused on attracting and growing the jobs of the future, and welcomed TEKLAS to the diverse family of EV suppliers that call Georgia home.
- TEKLAS currently has locations in Bulgaria, Serbia, Mexico, and China, along with sales offices in Germany and France.
