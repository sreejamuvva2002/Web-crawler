# Motherson Yachiyo Automotive Systems Co., Ltd.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** automotive_components  ·  **Published:** 2023-07-04

**Source:** [Motherson Yachiyo Automotive Systems Co., Ltd. (Formerly Yachiyo Industry Co., Ltd.) - MarkLines Automotive Industry Portal](https://www.marklines.com/en/top500/motherson-yachiyo-automotive-systems?sitesearchKey=ホンダ)  ·  `page_015615`

## Facts

- The company develops and manufactures fuel tanks and sunroofs.
- Honda announced on July 4, 2023, that it will sell its stake in consolidated subsidiary Yachiyo Industry to the Motherson Group.
- Honda will first make Yachiyo Industry Kogyo a wholly owned subsidiary through a takeover bid before transferring 81% of voting rights to SMRC Automotive Holdings Netherlands B.V., a group company under Samvardhana Motherson International.
- The tender offer for the stake transfer is scheduled to commence around October 2023.
- Honda aims to optimize its business portfolio and make the Motherson Group a strategic partner to expand sales channels in existing businesses and expand its plastic products business.
- Honda will acquire all shares of Goshi Giken Kogyo held by Yachiyo Kogyo, holding 95% of voting rights, allowing Yachiyo Industry to concentrate management resources on its automotive business while Honda focuses on motorcycle business growth.
- The company was delisted on January 10, 2024.
- Effective October 1, 2024, the company changed its name to Motherson Yachiyo Automotive Systems Co., Ltd.
- After Honda transfers its stock to the Motherson Group, voting rights are forecast to be 81% for the Motherson Group and 19% for Honda.
- The product portfolio includes sunroofs, plastic fuel tanks, steel fuel tanks, hydrogen tanks for FCVs, plastic parts, bumpers, instrument panels, and CFRP products.
- In October 2014, stamping operations of Yachiyo Manufacturing of Alabama, LLC (U.S.A) were acquired by Unipres.
- In October 2014, plastic fuel tank operations were transferred to a new production plant in Georgia.
- Following the October 2014 operational changes, the company adopted the new name Yachiyo Manufacturing of America, LLC.
- The company was originally established as Ohtake Paint Shop by Eiichi Ohtake in April 1947 at 7, Fujimi-cho, Itabashi-ku, Tokyo, starting with painting machine parts.
- In August 1953, Yachiyo Painting Co., Ltd. was established to start a baking finish business and set up the Narimasu Plant in Daiwa-cho, Saitama (currently Wako-shi), which closed in June 1962.
- The Suzuka Plant in Suzuka City, Mie, was established in May 1960 as a buffing and painting shop for motorcycle parts.
- The company changed its name to Yachiyo Industry Co., Ltd. in September 1968.
- In August 1972, the Kashiwabara Plant was established in Saitama, starting assembly of Honda's mini-cars on an outsourcing basis, at which point Honda invested and made it an affiliate.
- Yachiyo Press Industry Co., Ltd. merged with Shimizu Seisakusho in April 1973.
- Goshi Giken Co., Ltd. was established as a joint venture with Honda and Sankei Giken Kougyou Co., Ltd. in November 1974.
- Production of buggies for Honda started at the Kashiwabara Plant on an outsourcing basis in February 1976.
- The company started producing fuel tanks as automotive parts in February 1977.
- The head office was relocated to Toshima Ward in Tokyo in July 1981.
- Yokkaichi Factory in Mie Prefecture was established in June 1983 for stamping and welding operations.
- Facilities at Yokkaichi Factory were enhanced in August 1985 to produce trucks for Honda on an outsourcing basis.
- The Tochigi R&D Center was established in Tochigi Prefecture in January 1986.
- Production of sunroofs as part of its automotive-parts range started in June 1986.
- Yachiyo of Ontario Mfg., Inc., a consolidated subsidiary, was established in Canada in January 1989.
- Contract production of Honda's mini passenger cars started on a subcontracted basis in May 1996.
- Yachiyo Industry (U.K.) Ltd. was established in June 1996 and is currently a consolidated subsidiary.
- Unipart Yachiyo Technology Ltd., a joint venture with several local companies, was established in the UK in June 1996.
- Siam Yachiyo Co., Ltd., a consolidated subsidiary, was established in Thailand in January 1997.
- The head office was relocated to Sayama, Saitama Prefecture in May 1997.
- Yachiyo of America Inc., a consolidated subsidiary, was established in the U.S.A. in September 1997.
- AY Mfg., Ltd., a consolidated subsidiary, was established in the U.S.A. through its local subsidiary in October 1997.
- US Yachiyo Inc., a consolidated subsidiary, was established in the U.S.A. in January 1999.
- Yachiyo Manufacturing of Alabama Llc was established in the USA in September 2000.
- Yachiyo Zhongshan Mfg. Co., Ltd. was established in China in April 2002.
- In August 2002, shares of Goshi Giken Co., Ltd. were purchased to make it a subsidiary, bringing Uemura Tech, Shisui Tech, Goshi Tech, Siam Goshi Mfg. Co., Ltd. (Thailand), and Goshi-Thanglong Auto-Parts Co., Ltd (Vietnam) under Yachiyo's subsidiaries.
- The company cancelled its registration with the Japan Securities Dealers Association for over-the-counter trading and listed its shares on the JASDAC Stock Exchange in December 2004.
- Yachiyo Wuhan Mfg. Co., Ltd., a consolidated subsidiary, was established in China in July 2005.
- Equities of AY Mfg. and Ltd. in the United States were acquired in October 2005 to make it a consolidated subsidiary.
- Honda Motor Co., Ltd. became the Company's parent company in December 2006 after purchasing shares through a takeover bid.
- YG Tech Co., Ltd., a consolidated subsidiary, was established in Mie Prefecture, Japan through a joint-investment pooled by Group companies in April 2008.
- Yachiyo India Manufacturing Private Limited, a consolidated subsidiary, was established in India in April 2008.
- Goshi India Auto Parts Private Limited, a consolidated subsidiary, started operations in India in December 2008.
- Yachiyo de Brasil Industria e Comercio de Pecas Ltda., a consolidated subsidiary, was established in Brazil in September 2009.
- Yachiyo Mexico Manufacturing S.A. de C.V., a consolidated subsidiary, was established in Guanajuato, Mexico in February 2012.
- The Saitama R&D Center was established in Sayama, Saitama Prefecture in April 2012.
- P.T. Yachiyo Trimitra Indonesia was established in May 2012.
- In June 2014, Yachiyo of Ontario Manufacturing, Inc. in Canada transferred its sheet-metal business to Matcor-Matsu Group of Companies.
- In May 2015, a basic agreement was reached with Topre to start discussions for Yachiyo to transfer stamping operations in Japan to Topre.
- In June 2015, all operations with H-One at UYT Ltd. (an affiliate company in the U.K.) were transferred to N Press Assembly Limited.
- In January 2016, the transfer of business operations of YG Tech was completed to Topre, ending the Company’s operations in the sheet-metal business.
- Yachiyo Industries Yokkaichi Works Co., Ltd. was consigned the vehicle-assembly business and started operations in March 2018.
- All stock shares of Yachiyo Industries Yokkaichi Works Co., Ltd. were transferred to Honda Motor Co. in April 2018, ending its consignment of assembling light vehicles.
- In July 2023, the company announced it will sell Yachiyo Industry, a consolidated subsidiary, to the Motherson Group based in India.
