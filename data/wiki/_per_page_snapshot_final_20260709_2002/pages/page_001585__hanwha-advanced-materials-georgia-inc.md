# Hanwha Advanced Materials Georgia, Inc.

**Type:** company  ·  **Role:** unknown  ·  **Published:** 2023-04-03

**Source:** [Qcells supplier to build $147M Georgia factory | Manufacturing Dive](https://www.manufacturingdive.com/news/qcells-supplier-to-build-147m-georgia-factory/646453/)  ·  `page_001585`

## Facts

- An advanced materials manufacturer will spend upwards of $147 million to build a new facility in Cartersville, Georgia.
- The new facility will supply Qcells’ solar manufacturing plant located next door.
- The investment was announced by the governor’s office last week (relative to April 3, 2023).
- Hanwha Advanced Materials Georgia, Inc. will be the only company in the U.S. to make solar encapsulant film.
- Solar encapsulant film protects cells against environmental stressors.
- CEO Inhwan Kim stated that the products they make are an important piece of the supply chain puzzle.
- HAGA secured a Georgia site certified for accelerated development.
- The facility is expected to come online in summer of 2024.
- The investment by Hanwha Advanced Materials is central to Qcells’ $2.5 billion expansion plans in Georgia.
- Qcells North America President HG Park stated that Qcells is doubling down on building a complete, domestic solar supply chain and that this recent investment is critical to making this happen.
- HG Park added that working with Hanwha Advanced Materials will allow customers to confidently know that the solar they buy from them was made right here in America.
- Many solar companies are announcing plans to set up or expand operations in the U.S., sparked by clean energy production incentives in the Inflation Reduction Act.
- Solar investments have largely focused on panel assembly, leaving a need for the parts that go into them.
- Hanwha, the parent company of HAGA and Qcells, is upping its investment across the solar supply chain.
- Hanwha recently became a leading shareholder in REC Silicon.
- REC Silicon produces polysilicon, which is a key feedstock for solar.
