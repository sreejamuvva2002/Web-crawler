# L-H Battery Company, Inc.

**Type:** joint_venture  ·  **Role:** battery_cell_manufacturing  ·  **Published:** 2023-01-13

**Source:** [FORM 6-K](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/715153/000119312523007540/d418796d6k.htm)  ·  `page_002713`

## Facts

- Honda Motor Co., Ltd. completed procedures on January 13, 2023, to establish a joint venture with LG Energy Solution for EV battery production in the U.S., as previously announced on August 29, 2022.
- The new joint venture will operate under the tentative name L-H Battery Company, Inc.
- The JV is located in Jeffersonville, Ohio, U.S.A., approximately 40 miles southwest of Columbus in Fayette County.
- Initial capital for the JV is $210 million USD, with a plan for incremental investment to about $3.5 billion USD.
- Ownership split is 51% LG Energy Solution and 49% Honda Motor Co., Ltd.
- The JV will begin construction of a new battery plant early in 2023, with a goal of completion by the end of 2024.
- Mass production of advanced lithium-ion battery cells is scheduled to start by the end of 2025.
- The new plant aims for an annual production capacity of approximately 40GWh.
- LG Energy Solution and Honda have committed to creating 2,200 jobs at the new production facility.
- The companies’ overall investment related to the JV is projected to reach $4.4 billion.
- All batteries produced by the JV will be supplied exclusively to Honda plants in North America to power battery-electric vehicles sold in North America.
- Robert H. Lee serves as Director and CEO of the JV, currently holding the title of Executive Vice President and North America Regional Group Leader at LGES.
- Rick Riggle serves as Director and COO of the JV, previously Plant Lead of Honda's Anna Engine Plant.
- LGES board members appointed to the JV include Hyung Sik Kim, Se Joong Oh, and Jae Wook Jung.
- Honda board members appointed to the JV include Kensuke Oe and Arata Ichinose.
- Robert H. Lee stated that LG Energy Solution possesses financial stability, quality, competitiveness, and production capacity with global operational expertise to ensure the JV's success.
- Lee added that combining both companies' expertise will provide top-quality batteries for Honda EVs in North America and create high-value jobs in Ohio.
- Rick Riggle noted that establishing the JV in Ohio is a major step toward an electrified future and expressed excitement about partnering with LGES and building community relationships in Fayette County.
- The JV is accounted for using the equity method as an affiliate of Honda and is not anticipated to materially impact Honda's consolidated financial results for the fiscal year ending March 31, 2023.
- LG Energy Solution is a split-off from LG Chem and holds over 25,000 battery-related patents globally.
- LGES aims to achieve carbon neutral operations by 2050 and maintains battery manufacturing JV facilities with General Motors, Stellantis N.V., Hyundai Motor Group, and Honda.
- Honda Motor Co., Ltd. delivers over 30 million products annually across automobiles, motorcycles, power products, and aviation lines.
- Honda operates more than 60 manufacturing plants in 27 countries, employing approximately 220,000 associates globally.
- Honda aims to achieve carbon neutrality for all products and corporate activities by 2050, alongside zero traffic collision fatalities involving its vehicles.
